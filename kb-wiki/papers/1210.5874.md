# Properties of the outer crust of strongly magnetized neutron stars from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov atomic mass models

> The equilibrium properties of the outer crust of cold nonaccreting magnetars (i.e. neutron stars endowed with very strong magnetic fields) are studied using the latest experimental atomic mass data complemented with a microscopic atomic mass model based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method. The Landau quantization of electron motion caused by the strong magnetic field is found to have a significant impact on the composition and the equation of state of crustal matter. It is also shown that the outer crust of magnetars could be much more massive than that of ordinary neutron stars.

## Key claim

The outer crust of strongly magnetized neutron stars (magnetars) can be significantly more massive than that of ordinary neutron stars, but only under the condition that the magnetic field is strong enough to cause Landau quantization of electron motion, with the equilibrium composition and equation of state being substantially altered only when the field exceeds the critical value \(B_c \simeq 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G and especially in the strongly quantizing regime where only the lowest Landau level is filled.

## Method

The equilibrium composition of each layer of the outer crust at a given pressure was determined by minimizing the Gibbs free energy per nucleon, assuming cold catalyzed matter at zero temperature in a uniform magnetic field, with fully ionized atoms arranged in a body centered cubic lattice. The authors used the latest experimental atomic mass data from a preliminary version of the updated Atomic Mass Evaluation, complemented by the microscopic HFB-21 atomic mass model based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with a generalized Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, and treated electrons as a relativistic Fermi gas with Landau quantization.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field: \(B_c = m_e^2 c^3 / (e \hbar) \simeq 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G.
- For \(B_\star = B/B_c = 0\), the maximum density of \(^{56}\)Fe is \(4.93 \times 10^{-9}\) fm\(^{-3}\); for \(B_\star = 1\), it rises to \(5.60 \times 10^{-9}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Neutron-drip pressure increases from \(4.88 \times 10^{-4}\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) for \(B_\star = 0\) to \(1.15 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) for \(B_\star = 2000\).
- The equilibrium nuclide at the neutron-drip point is \(^{124}\)Sr in all cases, with electron chemical potential \(\mu_e^{\rm drip} \simeq 26\) MeV.
- The strongly quantizing regime (only lowest Landau level filled) applies when \(B_\star > 1304\).
- Surface density estimate: \(n_s \simeq 2.5 \times 10^{-10} B_\star^{6/5}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The HFB-21 mass model has an rms deviation of 0.58 MeV relative to 2149 measured masses.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Lai and Shapiro (1991) on the outer crust of magnetized neutron stars, using more recent experimental atomic mass data and the HFB-21 microscopic mass model instead of the older mass models used in that prior work. The results show that the sequence of equilibrium nuclides changes for strong fields \(B_\star \gg 1\), with some nuclides appearing or disappearing at specific field strengths (e.g., \(^{88}\)Sr appears at \(B_\star = 859\), \(^{58}\)Fe disappears at \(B_\star = 9\)), and that the outer crust can be much more massive than in ordinary neutron stars. This enables more accurate modeling of magnetar crust structure and seismic properties relevant to interpreting quasiperiodic oscillations observed in giant flares from soft-gamma ray repeaters.
