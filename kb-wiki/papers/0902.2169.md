# Ab initio shell-model calculation for ^{18}O in a restricted no-core model space

> We perform an ab initio shell-model calculation for ^{18}O in a restricted no-core model space, microscopically deriving a two-body effective interaction and introducing a minimal refinement of one-body energies in the spsd or spsdpf model space. Low-lying energy levels, except for the experimental 0_{2}^{+} and 2_{3}^{+} states, are better described in the spsdpf space than in the spsd space. The structure of low-lying energy levels is discussed with an emphasis on many-particle many-hole states beyond the four-particle two-hole configuration.

# Ab initio shell-model calculation for \(^{18}\)O in a restricted no-core model space

## Key claim
Low-lying energy levels of \(^{18}\)O, except for the experimental \(0_{2}^{+}\) and \(2_{3}^{+}\) states, are better described in the \(spsdpf\) model space than in the \(spsd\) space, but the calculation fails to reproduce those two specific states even when four-particle two-hole (4p2h) configurations are included, suggesting that a larger model space or more sophisticated treatment is required.

## Method
The authors start from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential and perform a two-step decoupling: first, a two-body effective interaction is derived in a large model space \(P_{1}\) (defined by a boundary number \(\rho_{1}=6\) in harmonic-oscillator quantum numbers) by solving the decoupling equation between \(P_{1}\) and its complement \(Q_{1}\); second, a further decoupling is performed between a smaller model space \(P_{2}\) (the \(spsd\) or \(spsdpf\) space) and its complement \(Q_{2}\) using the previously derived effective interaction, introducing a self-consistent one-body potential for \(^{16}\)O. A minimal refinement of the neutron one-body energies in the \(sd\) shell is then applied, using single-particle energies from the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) to reproduce the binding energies of the lowest \(5/2^{+}\), \(1/2^{+}\), and \(3/2^{+}\) states in \(^{17}\)O.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of \(^{18}\)O in the 4p2h \(spsdpf\) space at the optimal \(\hbar\Omega = 15\) MeV is about \(-120\) MeV, which is less attractive than the experimental value by about 20 MeV.
- Minimal refinements for the 4p2h \(spsdpf\) case: \(\Delta \epsilon^{n}(0d_{5/2}) = 0.99\) MeV, \(\Delta \epsilon^{n}(1s_{1/2}) = -0.42\) MeV, \(\Delta \epsilon^{n}(0d_{3/2}) = -1.04\) MeV.
- Minimal refinements for the 4p2h \(spsd\) case: \(\Delta \epsilon^{n}(0d_{5/2}) = 2.12\) MeV, \(\Delta \epsilon^{n}(1s_{1/2}) = -3.14\) MeV, \(\Delta \epsilon^{n}(0d_{3/2}) = -1.81\) MeV.
- Minimal refinements for the 6p4h \(spsd\) case: \(\Delta \epsilon^{n}(0d_{5/2}) = 2.42\) MeV, \(\Delta \epsilon^{n}(1s_{1/2}) = -2.59\) MeV, \(\Delta \epsilon^{n}(0d_{3/2}) = -1.30\) MeV.
- Calculated excitation energies in the 4p2h \(spsdpf\) case (dressed) for selected states: \(3^{+}\) at 4.94 MeV (expt. 5.38 MeV), \(0^{+}\) at 5.28 MeV (expt. 5.34 MeV), \(2^{+}\) at 3.70 MeV (expt. 3.92 MeV), \(4^{+}\) at 2.64 MeV (expt. 3.55 MeV), \(2^{+}\) at 1.31 MeV (expt. 1.98 MeV).
- In the \(spsd\) space, the lowest 4p2h-dominated \(0^{+}\) state appears at excitation energies of 41.75 MeV (4p2h), 31.50 MeV (6p4h), and 30.29 MeV (8p6h), far above the experimental \(0_{2}^{+}\) at 3.63 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the restricted no-core shell-model approach previously applied to neutron-rich carbon isotopes around \(^{16}\)C by Fujii (2007), bridging the gap between the full no-core shell model (NCSM) of Navratil (2000, 2002) and conventional shell-model calculations with empirical interactions such as those of Kuo and Brown (1965) and Coraggio (2009). The method enables simultaneous description of ground and excited states in \(^{18}\)O without using experimental energy-level information, but the failure to reproduce the \(0_{2}^{+}\) and \(2_{3}^{+}\) states, previously treated as deformed 4p2h states by Hofmann (1974) or as \(^{14}\)C+\(\alpha\) cluster states by Alhassid (1982) and Gai (1983), highlights the need for including higher configurations (e.g., 6p4h) in larger model spaces to reveal the real structure of these states.
