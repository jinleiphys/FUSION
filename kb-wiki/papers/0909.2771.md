# The sign problem across the QCD phase transition

> The average phase factor of the QCD fermion determinant signals the strength of the QCD sign problem. We compute the average phase factor as a function of temperature and baryon chemical potential using a two-flavor NJL model. This allows us to study the strength of the sign problem at and above the chiral transition. It is discussed how the $U_A(1)$ anomaly affects the sign problem. Finally, we study the interplay between the sign problem and the endpoint of the chiral transition.

# The sign problem across the QCD phase transition

## Key claim

The average phase factor of the QCD fermion determinant, which measures the severity of the sign problem, can actually increase (indicating a milder sign problem) as one approaches the critical endpoint of the chiral phase transition, because the massless sigma mode at the endpoint induces a pole in the prefactor of the mean-field expression for the average phase factor.

## Method

The authors compute the average phase factor of the QCD fermion determinant as the ratio of the partition functions of ordinary two-flavor QCD (1+1 theory) and phase-quenched two-flavor QCD (1+1* theory) using a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at mean-field level. The partition functions are evaluated via a saddle-point approximation that includes both the mean-field free energy density and the Hessian determinant (prefactor) describing fluctuations around the saddle point. The model parameters are fixed to reproduce the pion mass m_pi = 139 MeV, the pion decay constant f_pi = 94 MeV, and a chiral condensate of -(250 MeV)^3 in vacuum, with a sharp ultraviolet cutoff Lambda = 651 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass is set to m_pi = 139 MeV, the pion decay constant to f_pi = 94 MeV, and the chiral condensate to -(250 MeV)^3 in vacuum.
- The sharp ultraviolet cutoff is Lambda = 651 MeV, the bare quark mass is m_0 = 5.5 MeV, and the coupling constant is G = 2.12/Lambda^2.
- The three-volume is set to V = L^3 with L = 4/T, giving a four-volume beta V = 64/T^4.
- For low temperatures, the prefactor F follows the chiral perturbation theory prediction F = 1 - (2 mu_q / m_pi)^2.
- The average phase factor is computed for temperatures T = m_pi, T = 2 m_pi, T = 3 m_pi, and T = 4 m_pi, with and without U_A(1) symmetry breaking.
- The U_A(1) breaking parameter is modeled as alpha = (1/2) exp(-mu_q^2 / mu_0^2) with mu_0 = m_pi.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the average phase factor from chiral perturbation theory (phase-long, 1loop, BW), which is only valid below the critical temperature, to temperatures at and above the chiral phase transition. The NJL model approach complements lattice QCD studies (gattringer) and chiral random matrix model results (Han). The authors discuss how the U_A(1) anomaly, known to affect the order of the chiral transition (PW) and the location of the critical endpoint (fukushima), influences the sign problem. The interplay between the sign problem and the critical endpoint is relevant for lattice QCD efforts to locate the endpoint using methods such as multi-parameter reweighting (fodor1, fodor2).
