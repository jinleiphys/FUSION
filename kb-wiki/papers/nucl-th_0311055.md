# Hen Process in Effective Field Theory

> An effective field theory technique that combines the standard nuclear physics approach and chiral perturbation theory is applied to the $hen$ process, ${}^{3}{He}+n\to {}^{4}{He}+ γ$. For the initial and final nuclear states, high-precision wave functions are generated via the variational Monte Carlo method using the Argonne $v_{14}$ potential and Urbana VIII trinucleon interactions, while the relevant transition operators are calculated up to ${\cal O}(Q^4)$ in HB$χ$PT. The imposition of the renormalization condition that the magnetic moments of ${}^{3}{He}$ and ${}^{3}{H}$ be reproduced allows us to carry out a parameter-free calculation of the $hen$ cross section. The result, $σ=(60\pm 3\pm 1) μb$, is in reasonable agreement with the experimental values, $(54\pm 6) μb$ and $(55\pm 3) μb$. This agreement demonstrates the validity of the calculational method previously used for estimating the reaction rate of the solar $hep$ process.

# Hen Process in Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

The authors apply the "MEEFT" approach (a hybrid of standard nuclear physics wave functions and chiral perturbation theory) to the *hen* process and obtain a cross section of σ = (60 ± 3 ± 1) μb, which agrees with experimental values; this agreement validates the same method previously used for the solar *hep* process, though the *hen* process exhibits a much larger suppression of the one-body contribution (ratio of two-body to one-body contributions about -4) compared to *hep* (ratio about -0.6).

## Method

The authors generate high-precision wave functions for ³He, ³H, and ⁴He using the variational Monte Carlo method with the Argonne v₁₄ two-nucleon potential and Urbana VIII trinucleon interactions. The electromagnetic transition operators are derived up to O(Q⁴) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT), including one-body currents, one-pion-exchange currents, two-pion-exchange currents, and contact counter-term contributions. The two independent low-energy constants (LECs) in the contact terms are fixed by requiring that the magnetic moments of ³He and ³H be reproduced, making the *hen* cross section calculation parameter-free.

## Key numbers

- Threshold *hen* cross section: σ = (60.1 ± 3.2 ± 1.0) μb (first error from cutoff dependence, second from Monte Carlo statistics)
- Experimental values: (54 ± 6) μb (Wolfs) and (55 ± 3) μb (Wervelman)
- Photon energy: ω ≈ 20.521 MeV
- ³He+n scattering length: a_n^exp = 3.278(53) fm (Zimmer)
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: V₀ = 8.63637 MeV, R₀ = 4.14371 fm, d₀ = 0.8 fm
- LEC values for Λ = 600 MeV: {g₄s, g₄v} = {1.277(12), 0.981(7)}
- One-body matrix element: -1.76(1) × 10⁻³ fm³/² (for Λ = 600 MeV)
- Two-body total matrix element: 7.14(3) × 10⁻³ fm³/² (for Λ = 600 MeV)
- Cross section values for different cutoffs: 56.9(3) μb (Λ = 500 MeV), 59.2(5) μb (Λ = 600 MeV), 63.2(10) μb (Λ = 800 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the MEEFT approach previously applied to the solar *hep* process by Park et al. (2003) to the closely related *hen* process, which shares the feature of strong suppression of the leading one-body contribution due to pseudo-orthogonality of initial and final wave functions. Earlier SNPA calculations by Carlson et al. and Schiavilla et al. gave cross sections of 112 μb and 86 μb respectively, far above the experimental values, demonstrating that the problem lay in the many-body currents rather than the wave functions. The successful application to *hen* provides strong support for the reliability of the *hep* S-factor calculation and demonstrates the general validity of the MEEFT strategy for four-body processes where exchange currents dominate.
