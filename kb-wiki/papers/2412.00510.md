# Production of Light Nuclei in Au+Au Collisions with the STAR BES-II Program

> The yields and ratios of light nuclei in heavy-ion collisions offer a method to distinguish between the thermal and coalescence models. Ratios such as $\rm N_t \times N_p/N_d^2$ and $\rm N_{^3He} \times N_p/N_d^2$ are suggested as potential probes to investigate critical phenomena within the QCD phase diagram. The significantly larger datasets from STAR BES-II compared to BES-I, combined with enhanced detector capabilities, allow for more precise measurements. In this proceeding, we present the centrality and energy dependence of transverse momentum spectra and particle yields of (anti-)proton, (anti-)deuteron, and $\rm ^3He$ at BES-II energies ($\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = $7.7 - 27$ GeV), as well as the light nuclei to proton yield ratios and coalescence parameters $(B_2(\rm d)$ and $B_3(\rm ^3He))$.

# Production of Light Nuclei in Au+Au Collisions with the STAR BES-II Program

## Key claim
The yields and ratios of light nuclei in heavy-ion collisions can distinguish between thermal and coalescence models, with specific ratios such as N_t × N_p/N_d^2 and N_^3He × N_p/N_d^2 suggested as potential probes for critical phenomena in the QCD phase diagram. However, the thermal model overestimates the N_t/N_p and N_^3He/N_p ratios by about a factor of 2, likely due to hadronic re-scattering effects during the hadronic expansion stage.

## Method
Particles were identified using the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) at low transverse momentum (p_T), with Time of Flight (TOF) added at higher p_T. After signal extraction, spectra were corrected for energy loss, TPC tracking efficiency, TOF matching efficiency, and knock-out effects (specifically for protons and deuterons), with feed-down contributions from weak decays of strange baryons also applied. Point-to-point systematic uncertainties were estimated by varying track selection and analysis cuts, and systematic uncertainties of dN/dy were assessed by comparing Blast-Wave model fits with double-p_T function fits.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy range: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 27 GeV for Au+Au collisions at RHIC STAR BES-II
- The N_d/N_p and N_^3He/N_p ratios decrease monotonically with increasing collision energy in the most central 0-10% collisions
- The thermal model overestimates N_t/N_p and N_^3He/N_p ratios by approximately a factor of 2
- BES-II datasets contain about ten times more data than BES-I
- Transverse momentum acceptance was enhanced by the inner TPC (iTPC) upgrade
- Coalescence parameters B_2 (for deuteron) and B_3 (for ^3He) were measured across centrality bins 0-5%, 5-10%, 10-20%, 20-40%, and 40-80% at sqrt(s_NN) = 14.6 and 19.6 GeV
- Particle yields were measured at mid-rapidity with |y| < 0.3 for deuteron and |y| < 0.5 for ^3He

## Context
This work extends the STAR BES-I measurements of light nuclei production in Au+Au collisions (BES-I_d, BES_I_t, 3GeV) with significantly larger datasets and enhanced detector capabilities from the BES-II program. The results test the thermal model predictions for light nuclei yields and the coalescence model framework, where the coalescence parameter B_A reflects the probability of nucleon coalescence and relates to local nucleon density. The analysis of BES-II data at 9.2, 11.5, and 17.3 GeV is currently underway, promising further insights into the energy-dependent behavior of the system and potential critical phenomena in the QCD phase diagram.
