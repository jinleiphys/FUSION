# Transport coefficients of heavy mesons in a thermal medium

> We have investigated the many-body equations of $D$ and $\bar{B}$ mesons in a thermal medium by applying an effective field theory based on chiral and heavy-quark symmetries. Exploiting these symmetries within kinetic theory, we have derived an off-shell Fokker-Planck equation which incorporates information of the full spectral functions of these states. In this contribution we present our latest results on heavy-flavor transport coefficients below the chiral restoration temperature, in both charm and bottom sectors. The calculation incorporating temperature-dependent spectral functions and interactions, together with off-shell effects, allows for an improved matching to the state-of-the-art calculations above the chiral transition temperature.

# Transport coefficients of heavy mesons in a thermal medium

## Key claim
The off-shell Fokker-Planck equation incorporating temperature-dependent spectral functions and interactions yields heavy-flavor transport coefficients that improve the matching to state-of-the-art calculations above the chiral transition temperature, but only for temperatures up to 150 MeV where off-shell effects increase transport coefficients by a factor of 2-3 relative to vacuum interaction calculations.

## Method
The authors solved a self-consistent set of equations at finite temperature using the imaginary-time formalism: the unitarized T-matrix from Bethe-Salpeter equation, the heavy-meson self-energy, and the heavy-meson propagator. From this equilibrium solution they derived an off-shell Fokker-Planck equation via the Kadanoff-Baym formalism in the T-matrix approximation, expanding for small exchanged momentum. The transport coefficients were then evaluated in the static limit at the quasiparticle peak energy.

## Key numbers
- At T = 150 MeV, off-shell effects make the transport coefficients increase by a factor of 2-3 with respect to their values using vacuum interactions.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient D_s(T) is computed as D_s(T) = T^2 / B_0(k^0 = E_k, k → 0; T).
- The crossover temperature is T_c ≈ 156 MeV.
- The non-relativistic kinetic theory predicts D_s ∝ sqrt(1 + m_l/m_H), which decreases with increasing heavy meson mass m_H.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach of Tolos (2013), Montana (2020), and Montana (2020) for heavy mesons interacting with light mesons at low energies, now incorporating finite-temperature spectral functions and off-shell effects. The results enable improved matching to lattice-QCD calculations by Aarts (2022) and to high-temperature quasiparticle models by Das (2016) and Bayesian analyses by Ke (2018) around the chiral crossover temperature. The formalism supersedes earlier calculations that used only vacuum interactions.
