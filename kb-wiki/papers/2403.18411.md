# Role of hidden-color components in the tetraquark mixing model

> Multiquarks can have two-hadron components and hidden-color components in their wave functions. The presence of two-hadron components in multiquarks introduces a potential source of confusion, particularly with respect to their resemblance to hadronic molecules. On the other hand, hidden-color components are essential for distinguishing between multiquarks and hadronic molecules. In this work, we study the hidden-color components in the wave functions of the tetraquark mixing model, a model that has been proposed as a suitable framework for describing the properties of two nonets in the $J^P=0^+$ channel: the light nonet [$a_0 (980)$, $K_0^* (700)$, $f_0 (500)$, $f_0 (980)$] and the heavy nonet [$a_0 (1450)$, $K_0^* (1430)$, $f_0 (1370)$, $f_0 (1500)$]. Our analysis reveals a substantial presence of hidden-color components within the tetraquark wave functions. To elucidate the impact of hidden-color components on physical quantities, we conduct computations of the hyperfine masses, $\langle V_{CS}\rangle$, for the two nonets, considering scenarios involving only the two-meson components and those incorporating the hidden-color components. We demonstrate that the hidden-color components constitute an important part of the hyperfine masses, such that the mass difference formula, $ΔM\approx Δ\langle V_{CS}\rangle$, which has been successful for the two nonets, cannot be achieved without the hidden-color contributions. This can provide another evidence supporting the tetraquark nature of the two nonets.

## Key claim

The hidden-color components in the tetraquark mixing model wave functions constitute an essential part of the hyperfine masses for both the light and heavy nonets, such that the successful mass difference formula ΔM ≈ Δ⟨V_CS⟩ cannot be achieved without their contributions, providing further evidence for the tetraquark nature of these two nonets.

## Method

The authors decompose the two tetraquark types (Type1 and Type2) used in the tetraquark mixing model into two-meson components and hidden-color components by rearranging the diquark-antidiquark configuration (q₁q₂)(q̄₃q̄₄) into quark-antiquark pairs (q₁q̄₃)(q₂q̄₄) in both spin and color space. They then apply this decomposition to the physical wave functions of the heavy nonet and light nonet, which are linear combinations of the two tetraquark types with mixing parameters α ≈ √(2/3) and β ≈ √(1/3), and compute the hyperfine masses ⟨V_CS⟩ with and without the hidden-color contributions.

## Key numbers

- The hidden-color component constitutes approximately 5/9 of the heavy nonet wave function and approximately 4/9 of the light nonet wave function, based on the mixing parameters α ≈ √(2/3) and β ≈ √(1/3)
- In the color decomposition of Type1 (|1_c 3̄_c 3_c⟩), the hidden-color component constitutes two-thirds of the configuration
- In the color decomposition of Type2 (|1_c 6_c 6̄_c⟩), the hidden-color component constitutes one-third of the configuration
- The mixing parameters are approximately α ≈ √(2/3) and β ≈ √(1/3), with slight variations depending on the isospin channel

## Context

This work extends the tetraquark mixing model previously developed by Kim and collaborators (2016-2023) for describing the light nonet [a₀(980), K₀*(700), f₀(500), f₀(980)] and heavy nonet [a₀(1450), K₀*(1430), f₀(1370), f₀(1500)] in the Jᴾ = 0⁺ channel. The study directly addresses the confusion between tetraquarks and hadronic molecules, where alternative descriptions treat some nonet members as meson-molecular states as proposed by Ahmed (2020) and Molina (2008). By demonstrating that hidden-color components, which are unique to tetraquarks and absent in hadronic molecules, are essential for reproducing the hyperfine mass splitting between the two nonets, this work enables a clearer distinction between tetraquark and molecular interpretations of these scalar mesons.
