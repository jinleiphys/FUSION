# Effects of Rescattering in (e,e'p) Reactions within a Semiclassical Model

> The contribution of rescattering to final state interactions in (e,e'p) cross sections is studied for medium and high missing energies using a semiclassical model. This approach considers two-step processes that lead to the emission of both nucleons. The effects of nuclear transparency are accounted for in a Glauber inspired approach and the dispersion effects of the medium at low energies are included. It is found that rescattering is strongly reduced in parallel kinematics. At high missing energy and momenta, the distortion of the short-range correlated tail of the spectral function is dominated by a rearrangement of that strength itself. In perpendicular kinematics, a further enhancement of the experimental yield is due to strength that is originally in the mean field region. This contribution becomes negligible at large missing momenta.

## Key claim

Rescattering contributions to (e,e'p) cross sections are strongly reduced in parallel kinematics, but in perpendicular kinematics they can enhance the experimental yield by moving spectral strength from the mean field region into the short-range correlated tail region, submerging the direct signal in background noise.

## Method

The authors developed a semiclassical model that computes two-step rescattering processes: an electron knocks out a nucleon via the electromagnetic current, which then scatters from a second nucleon in the medium, producing the detected proton. The model incorporates nuclear transparency via a Glauber-inspired approach with pair distribution functions, accounts for Pauli blocking and Fermi spreading, and includes in-medium dispersion effects through a Dirac effective mass from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations at low energies.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear transparency T = 0.37 for 208Pb with outgoing proton kinetic energy ~161 MeV (p_f ~570 MeV/c)
- Nuclear transparency T = 0.63 for 12C and T = 0.29 for 197Au with outgoing proton energy ~1.8 GeV
- Rescattering yield is one to two orders of magnitude smaller than the direct signal for 208Pb in parallel kinematics, except at missing energies above 60 MeV where it gives a correction of about 20%
- In medium elastic pN cross sections computed at normal nuclear matter density (0.16 fm^-3) for energies up to 3 GeV show Pauli blocking produces a constant reduction at high energies
- Parameters for high-energy NN scattering: sigma_tot_pp = 44.0 mb, sigma_tot_pn = 41.1 mb, epsilon_pp = epsilon_pn = -0.48

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approach of VjPi (1999) and the analysis of Marcel (2004) for 208Pb to high missing energies relevant for short-range correlation studies. It directly addresses the kinematics of the E97-006 experiment at Jefferson Lab (Daniela, 2004; Frick, 2004) and the NIKHEF 208Pb experiment (Marcel, 2004). The results support the suggestion from E97-006 proposal that parallel kinematics minimize final state interactions, enabling cleaner extraction of short-range correlation strength from (e,e'p) data.
