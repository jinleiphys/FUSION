# Dynamics near QCD critical point by dynamic renormalization group

> We work out the basic analysis of dynamics near QCD critical point (CP) by dynamic renormalization group (RG). In addition to the RG analysis by coarse graining, we construct the nonlinear Langevin equation as a basic equation for the critical dynamics. Our construction is based on the generalized Langevin theory and the relativistic hydrodynamics. Applying the dynamic RG to the constructed equation, we derive the RG equation for the transport coefficients and analyze their critical behaviors. We find that the resulting RG equation turns out to be the same as that for the liquid-gas CP except for an insignificant constant. Therefore, the bulk viscosity and the thermal conductivity strongly diverge at the QCD CP. We also show that the thermal and viscous diffusion modes exhibit critical slowing down with the dynamic critical exponents $z_{\rm thermal}\sim 3$ and $z_{\rm viscous}\sim 2$, respectively. In contrast, the sound propagating mode shows critical speeding up with the negative exponent $z_{\rm sound}\sim -0.8$.

## Key claim

The dynamic universality class of the QCD critical point is not automatically the same as that of the liquid-gas critical point (model H) because the Poisson-bracket relations depend on the microscopic expressions of the variables; however, the renormalization group equations for the transport coefficients turn out to be identical to those for the liquid-gas CP except for an insignificant constant, despite the underlying Langevin equations being different.

## Method

The author constructed a nonlinear Langevin equation for the QCD critical point using the generalized Langevin theory of Mori and relativistic hydrodynamics (the Landau equation), identifying the slow variables as long-wavelength fluctuations of the conserved densities (baryon number, energy, and momentum). The thermodynamic potential for the slow variables was taken from the 3d Ising model because the QCD CP shares the same static universality class. The streaming terms were determined from continuity equations and the potential condition, while the bare kinetic coefficients were read from the relativistic hydrodynamic equations. The dynamic renormalization group was then applied to this Langevin equation to derive RG equations for the transport coefficients and analyze their critical behavior.

## Key numbers

- Dynamic critical exponent for the thermal diffusion mode: \(z_{\rm thermal}\sim 3\)
- Dynamic critical exponent for the viscous diffusion mode: \(z_{\rm viscous}\sim 2\)
- Dynamic critical exponent for the sound propagating mode: \(z_{\rm sound}\sim -0.8\) (negative, indicating critical speeding up)
- The bulk viscosity and thermal conductivity are predicted to strongly diverge at the QCD CP, while the shear viscosity is less important

## Context

This work extends the dynamic renormalization group approach, previously applied to condensed matter systems such as the liquid-gas critical point (model H by Kawasaki and by Halperin, Hohenberg, and Siggia), to the QCD critical point for the first time. It addresses the controversy over whether bulk viscosity diverges at the QCD CP, where lattice QCD results (Karsch) suggested divergence but microscopic calculations using the relativistic Boltzmann equation (Sasaki) found finite values. The analysis enables future studies of critical dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions near the QCD critical point, providing the RG equations for transport coefficients that can be used to compute their scaling behavior.
