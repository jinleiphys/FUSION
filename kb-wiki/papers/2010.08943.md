# Study of Decay Modes in Transfermium Isotopes

> In the unknown territory of transfermium nuclei, the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory has been applied to probe decay modes which include $α$-decay, spontaneous fission (SF), and a less explored weak-decay. These decay modes are analyzed on equal footing for 101$\leq$Z$\leq$109 and as a consequence, the half-lives for weak-decay are indeed found comparable for several isotopes. Our prediction of decay modes and half-lives are found in excellent agreement with available experimental decay modes and half-lives along with the results of some other theories. Out of $α$, $β^+$/EC, $β^-$, and SF, the most probable decay mode is anticipated along with its half-life over a wide range of odd and even nuclei to frame a novel sight into terra incognita.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, combined with empirical formulas for alpha-decay, beta-decay, and spontaneous fission, can predict decay modes and half-lives for transfermium isotopes (Z=101 to 109) with accuracy comparable to experimental data and other theoretical models, but the predictions are model-dependent and the TMA parameter of RMF is found more appropriate than the NL3* parameter in this region.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the NL3* and TMA parameter sets with an axially deformed harmonic-oscillator basis and a delta-function pairing force. They calculated Q-values for alpha-decay, beta-minus decay, beta-plus decay, and electron capture from the RMF binding energies, then used the modified Royer formula for alpha-decay half-lives, the Karpov formula for spontaneous fission half-lives, and the Fiset-Nix empirical formula for beta-decay and electron capture half-lives. The most probable decay mode for each nucleus was determined by comparing the calculated half-lives of all competing decay channels.

## Key numbers

- Root mean square error (RMSE) for Q-alpha values using the TMA parameter ranges from 0.27 MeV (for Lr) to 0.72 MeV (for Bh) across isotopic chains
- Root mean square error (RMSE) for Q-beta-minus values using the TMA parameter ranges from 0.21 MeV (for Db) to 0.67 MeV (for Sg)
- Root mean square error (RMSE) for Q-beta-plus/EC values using the TMA parameter ranges from 0.21 MeV (for Sg) to 0.67 MeV (for Bh)
- For most nuclei, the difference between experimental and calculated Q-values is less than 1.0 MeV
- Predicted half-life for Md-245 is 3.02 seconds (alpha decay) compared to experimental (0.90 plus or minus 0.25) milliseconds
- Predicted half-life for No-250 is 1.36 seconds (alpha/beta-plus/EC) compared to experimental (4.2 plus 1.2 minus 0.9) microseconds
- Predicted half-life for Rf-253 is 1.71 seconds (alpha) compared to experimental (48 plus 17 minus 10) microseconds

## Context

This work extends systematic investigations of decay modes in superheavy nuclei, building on prior studies of beta-decay in superheavy nuclei by Heenen (2015), Karpov (2012), and Moller (2019), as well as the Fiset-Nix (1972) empirical formula for beta-decay half-lives. The study covers a wider range of odd and even transfermium isotopes (Z=101 to 109) than previous work and puts weak-decay on equal footing with alpha-decay and spontaneous fission. The predictions enable experimental planning for synthesis and detection of undiscovered transfermium nuclei, particularly those where beta-decay or electron capture may compete with or dominate over alpha-decay and spontaneous fission.
