# Meson Scattering in a Pion Superfluid

> Instead of the fermion-fermion scattering which identifies the BCS-BEC crossover in cold atom systems, boson-boson scattering is measurable and characterizes the BCS-BEC crossover at quark level. We study $π$-$π$ scattering in a pion superfluid described by the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. We found that the scattering amplitude drops down monotonically with decreasing isospin density and finally vanishes at the boundary of the phase transition. This indicates a BCS-BEC crossover in the pion superfluid.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the meson-meson (π-π) scattering amplitude, rather than fermion-fermion scattering, can serve as a sensitive observable to identify the BCS-BEC crossover at quark level in a pion superfluid, with the amplitude vanishing at the phase transition boundary.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at finite temperature and isospin chemical potential to describe the pion superfluid. They calculate the π-π scattering amplitude in the random phase approximation (RPA) to lowest order in 1/N_c expansion, considering only box diagrams (σ-exchange diagrams vanish in the superfluid). The calculation is performed at threshold (√s = 2M_π̄, t = u = 0) using a hard three-momentum cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

- Critical isospin chemical potential μ_I^c = 134 MeV at T = 0 and 142 MeV at T = 100 MeV (with baryon chemical potential μ_B = 0)
- Model parameters: current quark mass m_0 = 5 MeV, coupling constant G = 4.93 GeV^{-2}, cutoff Λ = 653 MeV, yielding vacuum pion mass m_π = 134 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV, and effective quark mass M_q = 310 MeV
- At μ_I = 400 MeV (BCS state), the scattering amplitude vanishes at critical temperature T_c = 188 MeV
- At μ_I = 160 MeV (BEC state), the scattering amplitude vanishes at critical temperature T_c = 136 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of BCS-BEC crossover from cold atom systems (where fermion-fermion scattering is the observable, as in Courteille (1998), Greiner (2003), Zwierlein (2003), and Bourdel (2004)) to quark matter, where quarks are unobservable. The authors build on prior NJL model calculations of π-π scattering in vacuum by Schulze (1996), Quack (1995), and Huang (2001), and on theoretical descriptions of BCS-BEC crossover in pion superfluid by Matsuo (2001), Margueron (2002), Mao (2009), Sun (2007), and Mu (2010). The result that the scattering amplitude approaches zero in the BEC limit is consistent with the general fermion gas work by He (2012) and qualitatively supported by lattice QCD results at finite isospin density from Detmold (2012).
