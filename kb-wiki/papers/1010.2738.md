# Heavy-particle formalism with Foldy-Wouthuysen representation

> Utilizing the Foldy-Wouthuysen representation, we use a bottom-up approach to construct heavy-baryon Lagrangian terms, without employing a relativistic Lagrangian as the starting point. The couplings obtained this way feature a straightforward $1/m$ expansion, which ensures Lorentz invariance order by order in effective field theories. We illustrate possible applications with two examples in the context of chiral effective field theory: the pion-nucleon coupling, which reproduces the results in the literature, and the pion-nucleon-delta coupling, which does not employ the Rarita-Schwinger field for describing the delta isobar, and hence does not invoke any spurious degrees of freedom. In particular, we point out that one of the subleading $πN Δ$ couplings used in the literature is, in fact, redundant, and discuss the implications of this. We also show that this redundant term should be dropped if one wants to use low-energy constants fitted from $πN$ scattering in calculations of $NN\to NNπ$ reactions.

## Key claim

The authors present a bottom-up construction of heavy-baryon chiral effective field theory (HBChET) Lagrangians using the Foldy-Wouthuysen representation, which does not require a relativistic Lagrangian as a starting point. They claim that one of the subleading πNΔ couplings used in the literature is redundant and should be dropped when using low-energy constants fitted from πN scattering in calculations of NN → NNπ reactions.

## Method

The authors construct Lorentz-covariant bilinears from Foldy-Wouthuysen fields for spin-1/2 (nucleon) and spin-3/2 (delta isobar) particles, then expand these bilinears in powers of 1/m_N to obtain the heavy-baryon Lagrangian terms order by order. They apply this method to derive the πNN coupling and the πNΔ coupling, the latter without using the Rarita-Schwinger field for the delta isobar, thereby avoiding spurious spin-1/2 degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass m_N ≃ 940 MeV, cited as the lightest baryon mass
- Delta-nucleon mass splitting δ ≡ m_Δ − m_N, defined but not numerically specified
- Underlying chiral EFT scale set by m_σ ∼ 600 MeV, the mass of the lightest non-Goldstone boson
- Pion decay constant f_π ≃ 93 MeV, used in the πNN coupling expression
- The πNN coupling expansion reproduces the well-known result from the literature, with the leading term g_A N^† τ^a σ N · (∇π^a)/(2f_π)

## Context

This work extends the heavy-particle formalism used in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChET), as developed by Mannel (1992) and Bernard (1992), by providing a systematic bottom-up construction that does not rely on a relativistic starting Lagrangian. The approach enables treatment of the delta isobar without the Rarita-Schwinger field, avoiding spurious degrees of freedom discussed by Tang (1996), Pascalutsa (1998), and Krebs (2008, 2009). The finding that one subleading πNΔ coupling is redundant directly affects calculations of πN scattering and NN → NNπ reactions, and the method enables future construction of multi-baryon operators important for few-nucleon systems.
