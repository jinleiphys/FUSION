# Deconfinement energy threshold: analysis of hadron yields at 11.6 A GeV

> We analyze the hadron yields obtained at the AGS in the range 11--11.6 A GeV and discuss strategies to identify possible deconfinement at this energy scale. These include consideration of chemical nonequilibrium at hadronization, and the study of (multi)strange hadrons. We find that the totality of experimental results available favors the interpretation as hadron freeze-out at the phase boundary between confined and deconfined phase.

## Key claim

The totality of available AGS hadron yield data favors hadron freeze-out at the phase boundary between confined and deconfined matter, but the present data sample is not sufficient to argue decisively for or against QGP presence at this energy scale; the conclusion depends on allowing chemical non-equilibrium parameters (γ_q, γ_s) to vary rather than fixing them to unity.

## Method

The authors applied the Statistical Hadronization (SH) model using the SHARE program suite to fit up to 10 experimental data points (particle yields and ratios) from central Au–Au collisions at √s_NN = 4.8 GeV (11.6 A GeV/c fixed target). They allowed seven free parameters: temperature T, baryochemical potential μ_B, light-quark fugacity λ_q, strange-quark fugacity λ_s, isospin fugacity λ_I, light-quark phase-space occupancy γ_q, and strangeness phase-space occupancy γ_s, plus the fireball volume V. They evaluated fit quality via statistical significance (P-value) rather than χ² alone, and compared results with and without enforcing strangeness conservation and with and without including the φ/K⁺ ratio.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit hadronization temperature: T = 142 ± 3 MeV (with φ/K⁺ included)
- Best-fit baryochemical potential: μ_B = 708 ± 60 MeV (with φ/K⁺ included)
- Best-fit light-quark occupancy: γ_q = 0.35 ± 0.27
- Best-fit strangeness occupancy: γ_s = 0.23 ± 0.18
- Entropy per baryon: S/(B − B̄) = 14 ± 2
- Strangeness per baryon: s/(B − B̄) = 0.008 ± 0.001 at γ_q ≈ 0.4
- Valence strange quark pairs per baryon: s̄/(B − B̄) = 0.12 ± 0.02 ± 15% for 0.2 < γ_q < 1
- φ/K⁺ ratio (4π extrapolated): 0.025 ± 0.006
- K⁺/K⁻ ratio: 6.32 ± 0.65
- K⁺/π⁺ ratio: 0.197 ± 0.013
- p/π⁺ ratio: 1.23 ± 0.13
- Λ yield: 18.1 ± 1.9
- Λ̄ yield: 0.017 ± 0.005
- Total baryon number (B − B̄): 363 ± 10
- Total charge Q: 142 ± 5

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization analysis methodology previously applied to SPS data (Torrieri et al., 2003) and RHIC data (Florkowski et al., 2002) down to AGS energies, directly comparing with the chemical-equilibrium analysis of Becattini et al. (2004). The inclusion of the φ/K⁺ ratio from the E917 collaboration shifts the best fit toward chemical non-equilibrium (γ_q < 1, γ_s < 1), suggesting that the hadronization temperature lies near the deconfinement phase boundary rather than well below it. The results indicate that systematic exploration of the baryon-rich phase transition may be possible with beams in the 10 A GeV/c range, and that measurements of multistrange hadrons and strange antibaryons are needed to confirm the interpretation.
