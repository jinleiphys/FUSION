# Random Phase Approximation and Extensions Applied to a Bosonic Field Theory

> An application of a self-consistent version of RPA to quantum field theory with broken symmetry is presented. Although our approach can be applied to any bosonic field theory, we specifically study the $ϕ^4$ theory in 1+1 dimensions. We show that standard RPA approach leads to an instability which can be removed when going to a superior version,i.e. the renormalized RPA. We present a method based on the so-called charging formula of the many electron problem to calculate the correlation energy and the RPA effective potential.

## Key claim

Standard RPA applied to the $\lambda \varphi^4$ theory in 1+1 dimensions with broken symmetry produces an instability in the broken phase, which can be removed by using the renormalized RPA (r-RPA) that incorporates partial ground-state correlations through self-consistent occupation numbers.

## Method

The authors start from the Gaussian (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov) mean-field approximation for the $\varphi^4$ theory in 1+1 dimensions, then construct the RPA excitation spectrum using the equation-of-motion method with one-body and two-body boson operators. They derive the RPA Green's functions via a Dyson equation approach, obtaining the single-particle propagator and the two-particle propagators, and adapt the "charging formula" from the many-electron problem to compute the correlation energy and effective potential in both standard and renormalized RPA.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian approximation yields a first-order phase transition at a critical dimensionless coupling $p_c = 2.57$, where the deformed (broken) phase becomes stable.
- The renormalized mass gap equation in 1+1 dimensions gives $\langle\Delta\phi^2\rangle_\mu = -\frac{1}{4\pi} \ln\frac{m^2}{\mu^2}$, which is cutoff-independent.
- The dimensionless coupling constant is defined as $p = b/24\mu^2$, where $b = \lambda/6$ is the bare coupling.

## Context

This work extends the application of nuclear many-body techniques (RPA, renormalized RPA) to bosonic quantum field theory, specifically addressing the violation of Goldstone's theorem in the Gaussian/HFB approximation noted by Davesne et al. (1996). It builds on prior work by H\"auser et al. (1995) using cluster expansions of Green's functions, and by Amore et al. (1996, 1997) who showed RPA fluctuations can generate the Goldstone mode. The method enables a tractable, physically interpretable non-perturbative approach for field theories with broken symmetry, with future applications to chiral and gauge theories in view.
