# Photoproduction of hidden-charm states in the $γp \to \bar{D}^{*0} Λ^+_c$ reaction near threshold

> We report on a theoretical study of the hidden charm $N^*_{c \bar{c}}$ states in the $γp \to \bar{D}^{*0} Λ^+_c$ reaction near threshold within an effective Lagrangian approach. In addition to the contributions from the $s$-channel nucleon pole, the $t$-channel $D^0$ exchange, the $u$-channel $Λ^+_c$ exchange and the contact term, we study the contributions from the $N^*_{c \bar{c}}$ states with spin-parity $J^P = 1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$. The total and differential cross sections of the $γp \to \bar{D}^{*0} Lambda^+_c$ reaction are predicted. It is found that the contributions of these $N^*_{c \bar{c}}$ states give clear peak structures in the total cross sections. Thus, this reaction is another new platform to study the hidden-charm states. It is expected that our model calculation may be tested by the future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the $\gamma p \to \bar{D}^{*0} \Lambda^+_c$ reaction near threshold will show clear peak structures in the total cross section due to hidden-charm $N^*_{c \bar{c}}$ resonances, but they note that the free parameters in their effective Lagrangian model give it "some uncontrollable uncertainties" and that their calculation only considers selected tree-diagram contributions.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach to compute tree-level Feynman diagrams for the $\gamma p \to \bar{D}^{*0} \Lambda^+_c$ reaction, including contributions from the $s$-channel nucleon pole, $t$-channel $D^0$ exchange, $u$-channel $\Lambda^+_c$ exchange, a contact term, and $s$-channel $N^*_{c \bar{c}}$ resonances with spin-parity $J^P = 1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$. The coupling constants for the $N^*_{c \bar{c}}$ states to $J/\psi p$ and $\bar{D}^* \Lambda_c$ channels were taken from the coupled-channel unitary approach of Xiao (2013), and the electromagnetic couplings were obtained via vector meson dominance.

## Key numbers

- The six $N^*_{c \bar{c}}$ resonances considered have masses ranging from 4262 MeV to 4481 MeV and widths from 8.22 MeV to 58.9 MeV, as predicted by Xiao (2013).
- The coupling constant $g_{D^{*} D \gamma} = 2.0$ GeV$^{-1}$ was obtained from the radiative decay width $\Gamma_{D^{*0} \to D^{0} \gamma} = 26$ keV.
- The total cross section from $t$-channel $D^0$ exchange reduces by a factor of 10 when the cutoff parameter $\Lambda_{D^0}$ decreases from 2.5 GeV to 2.2 GeV.
- The coupling constants $g_{N^*_{c\bar{c}}\bar{D}^* \Lambda_c}$ for the six resonances range from 0.11 to 0.50, and the $g_{N^{*}_{\bar{c}c}NJ/\psi}$ values range from 0.53 to 1.44.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of hidden-charm pentaquark states following the LHCb discovery of $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ in $\Lambda_b^0 \to J/\psi p K^-$ decays (Aaij, 2015). It builds on the dynamically generated $N^*_{c \bar{c}}$ resonances predicted by Wu (2010) and Xiao (2013) from coupled-channel interactions of charmed mesons and baryons. The authors propose that the $\gamma p \to \bar{D}^{*0} \Lambda^+_c$ reaction can distinguish between genuine resonant states and triangle singularity enhancements, since the triangle singularity mechanism proposed for the $P_c$ states by Guo (2015) and Liu (2015) cannot be satisfied in this photoproduction reaction.
