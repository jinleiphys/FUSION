# The QCD phase diagram and Beam Energy Scan physics: a theory overview

> We review recent theoretical developments relevant to heavy-ion experiments carried out within the Beam Energy Scan program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Our main focus is on the description of the dynamics of systems created in heavy-ion collisions and establishing the necessary connection between the experimental observables and the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim
This theory review argues that non-monotonic fluctuations of conserved quantities (especially higher-order cumulants) as a function of collision energy are the primary signature of a QCD critical point, but the observed non-monotonicity in STAR data (excluded monotonic dependence at 3.1 sigma) is not yet a definitive indication of the critical point, motivating further analysis with higher statistics from BES-II.

## Method
The authors review the theoretical framework connecting equilibrium thermodynamic fluctuations to experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions, focusing on two intermediate steps: (1) the dynamical evolution of fluctuations in a hydrodynamically expanding medium, described by stochastic or deterministic approaches to hydrodynamic fluctuations; and (2) the mapping of hydrodynamic fluctuations onto particle multiplicity cumulants measured in momentum space via freeze-out. They present the confluent formalism for relativistic flow, which defines equal-time correlators in the local rest frame using a confluent derivative and Wigner transforms, and derive evolution equations for the two-point and higher-point correlators of the specific entropy fluctuation field.

## Key numbers
- The singular contributions to the cubic cumulant of the order parameter scale as \(\xi^{4.5}\) and the quartic cumulant as \(\xi^{7}\), where \(\xi\) is the correlation length.
- STAR experimental measurements exclude a monotonic dependence of quartic cumulants on collision energy at the 3.1\(\sigma\) level.
- The mapping of the QCD critical region to the Ising model uses 6 parameters: \(T_c\), \(\mu_{Bc}\), \(w\), \(\rho\), \(\alpha_1\) and \(\alpha_2\).

## Context
This review extends the theoretical framework for critical point searches in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, building on the foundational work of Stephanov (1998, 1999) on critical fluctuations as signatures and the Ising-to-QCD mapping of Parotto (2018). It addresses the gap between equilibrium thermodynamic predictions and experimental observables by incorporating recent advances in stochastic hydrodynamics (Landau-Lifshitz theory, Kapusta 2011) and the deterministic confluent formalism (An 2019, 2020, 2022) for evolving fluctuations in relativistic flow. The work enables quantitative comparison with STAR cumulant measurements and guides the analysis of higher-statistics BES-II data.
