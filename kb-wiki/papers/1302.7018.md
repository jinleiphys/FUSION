# Comparison of the Color Glass Condensate to di-hadron correlations in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions

> We perform a detailed comparison of long range rapidity correlations in the Color Glass Condensate framework to high multiplicity di-hadron data in proton-proton and proton-lead collisions from the CMS, ALICE and ATLAS experiments at the LHC. The overall good agreement thus far of the non-trivial systematics of theory with data is strongly suggestive of gluon saturation and the presence of subtle quantum interference effects between rapidity separated gluons. In particular, the yield of pairs collimated in their relative azimuthal angle $Δϕ\sim 0$, is sensitive to the shape of unintegrated gluon distributions in the hadrons that are renormalization group evolved in rapidity from the beam rapidities to those of the measured hadrons. We present estimates for the collimated di-hadron yield expected in central deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, combining "Glasma graph" and BFKL contributions, can simultaneously describe the long-range rapidity correlations observed in high-multiplicity proton-proton and proton-lead collisions at the LHC, provided that the initial saturation scales in both projectile and target are varied to reflect increasingly rare Fock state configurations at central impact parameters.

## Method

The authors compute double-inclusive di-hadron yields using the CGC framework, which includes two classes of QCD graphs: Glasma graphs (dominant for nearside collimation at Δφ≈0) and back-to-back BFKL graphs (dominant for awayside collimation at Δφ≈π). The unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDs) for both projectile and target are evolved using the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation, with initial saturation scales Q₀² varied in integer multiples of 0.168 GeV² to model different centrality classes. The collimated associated yield per trigger is extracted using the Zero-Yield-at-Minimum (ZYAM) procedure, and results are compared to CMS, ALICE, and ATLAS data for p+p at √s=7 TeV and p+Pb at √s=5.02 TeV/nucleon, with predictions for d+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Initial saturation scale for minimum-bias proton: Q₀² = 0.168 GeV², corresponding to Q_S ≈ 0.7 GeV in the adjoint representation at the initial rapidity
- Glasma graphs are enhanced by α_S⁻⁸, a factor of ~10⁵ for typical values of the QCD fine structure constant α_S
- Non-perturbative constant ζ = 1/6, specifying correction to k_T factorized UGD description due to soft multigluon interactions
- K-factors: K_Glasma = K_BFKL = 1.5 for p+p collisions; K_Glasma = K_BFKL = 1 for p+Pb collisions
- For minimum bias p+p collisions (Q₀²=0.168 GeV² in both protons), the charged track multiplicity N_trk = 14
- BFKL NLLx corrections to collimated ⟨cos(Δφ)⟩ and ⟨cos(2Δφ)⟩ moments are 10-30%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CGC framework (Dusling 2012, Dusling 2012) that described CMS p+p ridge data, now incorporating comparisons to CMS, ALICE, and ATLAS p+Pb data as well as PHENIX d+Au preliminary results. The Glasma graph formalism was first developed by Dumitru (2008) and Gelis (2008), and the BFKL back-to-back contribution uses the Green's function approach of Colferai (2010). The framework contradicts PYTHIA-8 and Quasi-Multi-Regge-Kinematics 2→4 QCD graphs, which overestimate the awayside yield, especially at larger momenta. This work enables systematic tests of gluon saturation across different collision systems and detector acceptances, with the key open question being the role of fragmentation function uncertainties and the need for Monte Carlo simulations of interference graphs at lower multiplicities.
