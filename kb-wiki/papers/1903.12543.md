# High Density with Elliptic Flows

> The elliptic flow of emitted particles and fragments observed in heavy-ion reactions at high energy has become an important observable reflecting the pressure generated in the dense collision zone. More recently, the strength of the nuclear symmetry energy has been investigated by measuring the ratios or differences of the elliptic flows exhibited by neutrons and charged particles in 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon incident energy at the GSI laboratory. A moderately soft to linear dependence on density was deduced for a range of densities shown to reach beyond twice the saturation value in these experiments. The known sources of uncertainties and possible model dependencies were thoroughly studied with transport models of the UrQMD and Tuebingen QMD type. A new source of information on the nuclear equation of state at high density has opened up with the observation of the first LIGO and Virgo GW170817 gravitational wave signal from a neutron star merger. The quantitative comparison of terrestrial and celestial results on the basis of measured or inferred neutron star radii or core pressures, including those obtained from X-ray observations, reveals a rather satisfactory agreement. Depending on the precision that can be achieved with future measurements and observations, it will thus become possible to assess the validity of the applied models and methods. The perspectives for improved experiments at FAIR using the NeuLAND and KRAB detection systems are outlined.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow ratios and differences of neutrons and charged particles in 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon provide constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy at densities reaching beyond twice saturation, yielding a moderately soft to linear density dependence; however, the deduced slope parameter L and power-law coefficient gamma depend on the chosen parametrization of the symmetry energy and on the specific transport model used for interpretation.

## Method

The elliptic flows of neutrons and light charged particles were measured in 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon incident energy at GSI, using the LAND neutron detector and the FOPI, KRATTA, AToF, CHIMERA, and Microball detector systems for charged particles and event characterization. The ratios and differences of the elliptic flow coefficients v2 for neutrons and charged particles were compared with predictions from the UrQMD transport model using 21 selected Skyrme forces and a power-law parametrization of the symmetry energy, as well as with the Tuebingen QMD model using the MDI parametrization with stiffness parameter x.

## Key numbers

- Power-law coefficient gamma = 0.72 ± 0.19 for the potential part of the symmetry energy from the ASY-EOS experiment, with acceptance-integrated elliptic-flow ratio
- Slope parameter L = 72 ± 13 MeV from the ASY-EOS data
- Power-law coefficient gamma = 0.75 ± 0.10 with purely statistical error from the ASY-EOS experiment
- Symmetry pressure at saturation p0 = 3.8 ± 0.7 MeV/fm^3, corresponding to rho0 L/3
- Pressure in neutron-star matter at saturation density: 3.4 ± 0.6 MeV/fm^3 for asymmetry delta = 0.9
- Pressure in neutron-star matter at 2 rho0: p(2rho0) = 23 ± 3 MeV/fm^3, equal to 3.7 ± 0.5 × 10^34 dyn/cm^2
- Incompressibility parameter K0 = 200 ± 30 MeV from combined IQMD and UrQMD analyses of FOPI data
- Curvature term Ksym in the range -70 to -40 MeV from the ASY-EOS result
- Slope parameter L = 63 ± 11 MeV when using alternative parametrization with E_sym^pot(rho0) = 19 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier FOPI-LAND experiment results (Russotto 2011) which yielded gamma = 0.9 ± 0.4, improving the statistical accuracy by a factor of four. The results are compared with astrophysical constraints from the GW170817 gravitational wave event (Abbott 2018), X-ray observations of neutron stars (Steiner 2013), and neutron star radius measurements (Lattimer and Prakash 2001, 2007, 2016). The analysis enables quantitative comparison of terrestrial heavy-ion results with celestial observations in the density range 1-2 rho0, and outlines perspectives for improved experiments at FAIR using the NeuLAND and KRAB detection systems.
