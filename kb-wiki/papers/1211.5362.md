# Some exact results on the QCD critical point

> We show, in a model-independent manner, that the QCD critical point can appear only inside the pion condensation phase of the phase-quenched QCD as long as the contribution of flavor-disconnected diagrams is negligible. The sign problem is known to be maximally severe in this region, implying that the QCD critical point is reachable by the present lattice QCD techniques only if there is an enhancement of the flavor-disconnected contribution at finite baryon chemical potential.

# Some exact results on the QCD critical point

## Key claim

The QCD critical point can appear only inside the pion condensation phase of the phase-quenched QCD, provided the contribution of flavor-disconnected diagrams is negligible. If the critical point exists in a region accessible by present lattice QCD techniques, the flavor-disconnected contribution must be enhanced at finite baryon chemical potential.

## Method

The authors prove a theorem using QCD inequalities derived from the Dirac operator properties at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential. They first establish that pions are the lightest mesonic modes under the conditions that flavor-disconnected diagrams are negligible and the system is outside the critical line of phase-quenched QCD, then show that the sigma meson mass cannot vanish under these conditions, ruling out a critical point. The result is extended from finite isospin chemical potential to finite baryon chemical potential using the exactness of phase quenching via large-N_c orbifold equivalence.

## Key numbers

- The bound m_σ ≥ m_π > 0 is derived for the sigma meson mass relative to the pion mass under conditions (i) and (ii)
- The pion condensation phase boundary occurs at μ > m_π/2 at T=0, where m_π is the pion mass
- The equivalence ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩_{μ_B=3μ} = ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩_{μ_I=2μ} holds outside the pion condensation phase under condition (i)
- The exactness of phase quenching holds to O(N_f/N_c) using large-N_c orbifold equivalence

## Context

This work provides a model-independent explanation for why previous model calculations, including the random matrix model (Han 2008), Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Andersen 2009), and Polyakov-loop extended NJL model (Sakai 2010), all found critical points only inside the region where the sign problem is maximally severe. It extends the earlier observation by Splittorff (2006) that the critical points found by Fodor and Katz (2001) using reweighting methods lie on the critical line of phase-quenched QCD. The result implies that accessing the QCD critical point with present lattice techniques requires an enhancement of flavor-disconnected contributions at finite baryon chemical potential, and that nuclear matter at T=0 lies inside the pion condensation phase independently of quark mass (Cohen 2003).
