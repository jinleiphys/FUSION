# Dihyperon in Chiral Colour Dielectric Model

> The mass of dihyperon with spin, parity $J^π=0^{+}$ and isospin $I = 0$ is calculated in the framework of Chiral colour dielectric model. The wave function of the dihyperon is expressed as a product of two colour-singlet baryon clusters. Thus the quark wave functions within the cluster are antisymmetric. Appropriate operators are then used to antisymmetrize inter-cluster quark wave functions. The radial part of the quark wavefunctions are obtained by solving the the quark and dielectric field equations of motion obtained in the Colour dielectric model. The mass of the dihyperon is computed by including the colour magnetic energy as well as the energy due to meson interaction. The recoil correction to the dihyperon mass is incorporated by Peierls-Yoccoz technique. We find that the mass of the dihyperon is smaller than the $Λ-Λ$ threshold by over 100 MeV. The implications of our results on the present day relativistic heavy ion experiments is discussed.

## Key claim

The dihyperon mass is found to be smaller than the Lambda-Lambda threshold by over 100 MeV, but this result depends strongly on the glueball mass parameter, with the binding energy varying between 160 MeV and 90 MeV as the glueball mass increases from 800 MeV to 1250 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a six-quark dihyperon wavefunction as a product of two colour-singlet baryon clusters, then antisymmetrize inter-cluster quark wavefunctions. They solve coupled quark and dielectric field equations in the chiral colour dielectric (CCD) model to obtain radial quark wavefunctions, compute the bare mass with center-of-mass correction via the Peierls-Yoccoz technique, and add colour magnetic energy and pseudoscalar meson self-energy contributions. The meson self-energy is computed by coupling the pseudoscalar meson octet to the dihyperon, inducing transitions to a dibaryon octet with spin 1.

## Key numbers

- Dihyperon mass ranges from 2071 MeV to 2140 MeV across the parameter sets shown in Table 1.
- Binding energy relative to the Lambda-Lambda threshold varies from 160 MeV (for glueball mass of 800 MeV) to 90 MeV (for glueball mass of 1250 MeV).
- The u and d quark masses used are approximately 100-135 MeV, and the strange quark mass is approximately 209-215 MeV.
- The strong coupling constant alpha_s ranges from 0.215 to 0.581 across the parameter sets.

## Context

This work extends Jaffe's (1977) original MIT bag model prediction of a dihyperon mass around 2150 MeV, and includes pseudoscalar meson coupling corrections that earlier calculations by Liu and Wong (1982), Rosenthal (1986), and Aerts and Dover (1984) had not fully incorporated. The CCD model differs from the bag model by achieving confinement dynamically through a colour-dielectric field rather than by hand, and uses non-zero u and d quark masses closer to constituent quark masses. The results suggest the dihyperon is stable against strong decays, which has implications for ongoing searches for strangelets in relativistic heavy ion experiments, particularly given the E886 collaboration's null result (Rusek, 1995) and the more recent but inconclusive detection by Long and collaborators.
