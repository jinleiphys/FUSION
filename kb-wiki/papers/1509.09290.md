# Symmetry energy of cold nucleonic matter within a relativistic mean field model encapsulating effects of high momentum nucleons induced by short-range correlations

> Significant progress has been made recently in constraining the isospin-dependent parameters characterizing the SRC (short-range correlation)-modified single-nucleon momentum distribution in neutron-rich nucleonic matter using both experimental data and microscopic model calculations. Using the constrained single-nucleon momentum distribution in a nonlinear relativistic mean field (RMF) model, we study the equation of state (EOS) of asymmetric nucleonic matter (ANM), especially the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$. Firstly, as a test of the model, the average nucleon kinetic energy extracted recently from electron-nucleus scattering experiments using a neutron-proton dominance model is well reproduced by the RMF model incorporating effects of the SRC-induced high momentum nucleons, while it is significantly under predicted by the RMF model using a step function for the single-nucleon momentum distribution as in free Fermi gas (FFG) models. Secondly, the kinetic symmetry energy of quasi-nucleons is found to be $E^{\rm{kin}}_{\rm{sym}}(ρ_0)=-16.94\pm13.66\,\rm{MeV}$ which is dramatically different from the prediction of $E^{\rm{kin}}_{\rm{sym}}(ρ_0)\approx 12.5$ MeV by FFG models at nuclear matter saturation density $ρ_0=0.16\,\rm{fm}^{-3}$. Thirdly, comparing the RMF calculations with and without the high momentum nucleons using two sets of model parameters both reproducing identically all empirically constraints on the EOS of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and the symmetry energy of ANM at $ρ_0$, the SRC-modified single-nucleon momentum distribution is found to make the $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$ more concave around $ρ_0$ by softening it significantly at both sub-saturation and supra-saturation densities, leading to an isospin-dependent incompressibility of ANM in better agreement with existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The SRC-modified single-nucleon momentum distribution, when incorporated into a nonlinear relativistic mean field (RMF) model, makes the nuclear symmetry energy \(E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho)\) more concave around saturation density by softening it at both sub-saturation and supra-saturation densities, but this result depends on using two sets of model parameters that both reproduce all empirical constraints on symmetric nuclear matter and the symmetry energy at \(\rho_0\).

## Method

The authors used a nonlinear RMF model with a Lagrangian including couplings between nucleons and \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) mesons, plus a \(\sigma\) self-interaction and \(\omega\)-\(\rho\) coupling. They replaced the traditional free Fermi gas (FFG) step-function momentum distribution with a phenomenological SRC-modified distribution featuring a high-momentum tail (HMT) of the form \(C_J (k_{\rm{F}}^J/|\mathbf{k}|)^4\) for momenta above the Fermi surface, with isospin-dependent parameters \(C_J\), \(\phi_J\), and \(\Delta_J\) constrained by electron-nucleus scattering data and microscopic many-body calculations. The model parameters were then readjusted to reproduce empirical values of the saturation density, binding energy, Dirac effective mass, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and its slope at \(\rho_0\).

## Key numbers

* Kinetic symmetry energy at saturation density: \(E^{\rm{kin}}_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0) = -16.94 \pm 13.66\) MeV (with \(M_0^{\ast}/M = 0.6\)), compared to the FFG prediction of approximately 12.5 MeV.
* Saturation density used: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* High-momentum tail amplitude in symmetric nuclear matter: \(C_0 \approx 0.161 \pm 0.015\).
* High-momentum tail cutoff parameter in symmetric nuclear matter: \(\phi_0 = 2.38 \pm 0.56\).
* Percentage of nucleons in the high-momentum tail in symmetric nuclear matter: \(x^{\rm{HMT}}_{\rm{SNM}} = 28\% \pm 4\%\).
* Percentage of nucleons in the high-momentum tail in pure neutron matter: \(x^{\rm{HMT}}_{\rm{PNM}} = 1.5\% \pm 0.5\%\).
* High-momentum tail cutoff parameter for pure neutron matter: \(\phi_{\rm{n}}^{\rm{PNM}} = 1.04 \pm 0.02\).
* Isospin dependence parameter of the HMT amplitude: \(C_1 = -0.25 \pm 0.07\).
* Isospin dependence parameter of the HMT cutoff: \(\phi_1 = -0.56 \pm 0.10\).
* Binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at \(\rho_0\): \(E_0(\rho_0) = -16.0\) MeV.
* Incompressibility coefficient of symmetric nuclear matter: \(K_0 = 230.0\) MeV.
* Total symmetry energy at \(\rho_0\): \(E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0) = 31.6\) MeV.
* Slope parameter of the symmetry energy: \(L = 58.9\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic studies (e.g., Cai (2015), Hen (2015b)) that found SRC-induced high-momentum nucleons reduce the kinetic symmetry energy to negative values, by confirming this effect within a relativistic mean field framework. It also builds on experimental constraints from electron-nucleus scattering (Hen (2014, 2015), Wei (2015)) and microscopic many-body calculations (Sch (2005), Gezerlis (2010, 2013)) that established the isospin-dependent parameters of the SRC-modified momentum distribution. The results enable more accurate modeling of the symmetry energy at both sub-saturation and supra-saturation densities, which is relevant for heavy-ion reaction simulations and neutron star structure calculations, including the formation of \(\Delta(1232)\) resonances as studied by Cai (2015a) and Drago (2014).
