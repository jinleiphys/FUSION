# Electromagnetic Properties of Few-Body Systems Within a Point-Form Approach

> We use a Poincare-invariant coupled-channel approach based on point-form relativistic quantum mechanics to investigate the electromagnetic properties of two-body bound systems with spin 0 and spin 1. Elastic scattering of an electron by the bound state is treated as a two-channel problem for a Bakamjian-Thomas-type mass operator. In this way retardation effects in the photon-exchange interaction are fully taken into account. The electromagnetic bound-state current is extracted from the one-photon-exchange optical potential. Wrong cluster properties, inherent in the Bakamjian-Thomas framework for more than 2 particles, are seen to cause spurious (unphysical) contributions in the current, which are associated with a dependence on the sum of the electron momenta. The Lorentz structure of our current resembles the one obtained from an explicitly covariant light-front approach, where spurious contributions also show up and are associated with a four-vector describing the orientation of the light front. For spin-0 systems, like a charged pion, the spurious contributions can be eliminated by choosing the total invariant mass of the electron-bound-state system large enough. In this case equivalence with the usual front-form expression for the form factor, resulting from a spectator current in the q^+=0 reference frame, is established. For spin-1 systems, like a charged rho meson or the deuteron, some spurious contributions cannot be completely eliminated by solely choosing an infinitely large invariant mass. Nevertheless, there is an unambiguous way how to separate them from the physical contributions such that one is left with a physical bound-state current with the required properties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Poincaré-invariant coupled-channel approach based on point-form relativistic quantum mechanics can extract electromagnetic bound-state currents for spin-0 and spin-1 two-body systems, but the Bakamjian-Thomas framework introduces spurious contributions due to wrong cluster properties for systems with more than two particles. For spin-0 systems these spurious contributions can be eliminated by taking the total invariant mass of the electron-bound-state system large enough, whereas for spin-1 systems some spurious contributions persist even in that limit.

## Method

The author treats elastic electron scattering off a two-body bound state as a two-channel problem for a Bakamjian-Thomas-type mass operator, with the electron-bound-state and electron-bound-state-photon channels coupled by electromagnetic vertex interactions motivated from quantum field theory. The electromagnetic bound-state current is extracted from the one-photon-exchange optical potential in the velocity-state representation, which is the appropriate multiparticle basis for the point form. The bound-state wave functions are obtained from instantaneous interactions between constituents: a harmonic-oscillator confining potential for mesons (pion and rho meson) and a meson-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction inspired by the Walecka model for the deuteron.

## Key numbers

* Pion form factor results show perfect numerical agreement with the standard front-form approach of Chung (1988) when using the same model wave function and neglecting quark spins.
* For spin-0 systems, the equivalence with the usual front-form expression for the form factor is established by taking the total invariant mass of the electron-bound-state system large enough, corresponding to the q^+=0 reference frame.
* For spin-1 systems (charged rho meson or deuteron), some spurious contributions cannot be completely eliminated by choosing an infinitely large invariant mass, but an unambiguous separation from physical contributions remains possible.

## Context

This work extends the point-form relativistic quantum mechanics approach previously applied to mass calculations (Glozman 1997, Melde 2008), strong decays (Melde 2005, Sengl 2007), and strong form factors (Melde 2008). It builds on a multi-channel framework proposed by Klink (2000) that had been applied to vector-meson masses (Krassnigg 2003), the hydrogen atom (Lechner 2003), and pion-nucleon scattering (Girlanda 2007). The paper establishes notable similarities with the covariant light-front approach of Karmanov (1994) and Carbonell (1998), and it contributes to a benchmark calculation of deuteron form factors aimed at defining relativistic effects across different few-body approaches. The formalism enables future extensions to include dynamical particle-exchange interactions between constituents beyond the instantaneous approximation used here.
