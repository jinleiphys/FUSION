# Constraints on $Ξ^-$ nuclear interactions from capture events in emulsion

> Five $Ξ^- p\to ΛΛ$ two-body capture events in $^{12}$C and $^{14}$N emulsion nuclei, in which a pair of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei is formed and identified by their weak decay, have been observed in $(K^-,K^+)$ emulsion exposures at KEK and J-PARC. Applying a $Ξ^-$-nucleus optical potential methodology to study atomic and nuclear transitions, we confirm that these capture events occur from Coulomb assisted $1p_{Ξ^-}$ nuclear states. Long-range $ΞN$ shell-model correlations are found essential to achieve consistency between the $^{12}$C and $^{14}$N events. The resulting $Ξ$-nuclear interaction is strongly attractive, with $Ξ$ potential depth in nuclear matter $V_Ξ\gtrsim 20$ MeV. Implications to multi-strangeness features of dense matter are outlined.

# Constraints on Ξ⁻ nuclear interactions from capture events in emulsion

## Key claim
The five observed Ξ⁻p → ΛΛ two-body capture events in ¹²C and ¹⁴N emulsion nuclei are consistent with capture from Coulomb-assisted 1p_Ξ⁻ nuclear states bound by approximately 1 MeV, but only when long-range ΞN shell-model correlations are included to reconcile the ¹⁴N events with the ¹²C events; the resulting Ξ-nuclear interaction is strongly attractive with potential depth V_Ξ ≳ 20 MeV, though this value substantially exceeds predictions from recent lattice QCD and effective field theory calculations.

## Method
The authors applied a Ξ⁻-nucleus optical potential methodology using a Klein-Gordon wave equation with a tρ form for the strong-interaction potential, fitting the strength parameter Re b₀ to reproduce the 0.82 ± 0.15 MeV binding energy of the 1p_Ξ⁻ state in ¹²C. They then introduced a Pauli correlation term to account for density dependence, and used shell-model quadrupole-quadrupole residual interactions plus spin-orbit and spin-spin splittings to describe the ¹⁴N g.s. + 1p_Ξ⁻ triplet of states, matching the observed binding energies of the three ¹⁴N capture events.

## Key numbers
- Ξ⁻ binding energy B_Ξ⁻ for ¹²C events: 0.82 ± 0.17 MeV (event 10-09-06) and 0.82 ± 0.14 MeV (event 13-11-14)
- Ξ⁻ binding energy B_Ξ⁻ for ¹⁴N events: 1.18 ± 0.22 MeV (event 14-03-35), 1.03 ± 0.18 MeV (KISO), and 1.27 ± 0.21 MeV (IBUKI)
- Fitted optical potential strength parameter Re b₀ = 0.32 ± 0.01 fm for the tρ potential, increasing to 0.527 fm when Pauli correlations are included
- Nuclear-matter Ξ potential depth V_Ξ = 24.3 ± 0.8 MeV from tρ potential, reduced to 21.9 ± 0.7 MeV with Pauli correlations
- Calculated 1s_Ξ⁻ binding energies: 9.8 MeV in ¹²C, 11.5 MeV in ¹⁴N, 2.0 MeV in ⁴He
- Radiative E1 transition rate from Ξ⁻ atomic 3D state to 1p_Ξ nuclear state: 7.6% of total 3D width Γ_3D = 3.93 eV
- Quadrupole-quadrupole Slater integral F^(2)_ΞN = -3 MeV, smaller than the ΛN value of -3.7 MeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of emulsion capture events from KEK E176, KEK E373, and J-PARC E07 experiments, providing the first self-consistent optical potential treatment of all five two-body Ξ⁻ capture events. The derived V_Ξ ≈ 22 MeV is consistent with the old analysis by Dover and Gal (1983) giving 21-24 MeV, but substantially exceeds values from recent HAL-QCD lattice calculations (HAL-QCD 2019, 2020) and chiral EFT models (Haidenbauer and Meißner 2019, Kohno 2019) which yield V_Ξ ≲ 10 MeV. The results imply that 1s_Ξ⁻ bound states exist down to ⁴He, with implications for the role of Ξ hyperons in multi-strange dense matter and neutron stars, though the authors note that ΞNN three-body attractive contributions would be needed to reconcile their findings with modern microscopic calculations.
