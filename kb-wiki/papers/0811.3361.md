# Quark matter under strong magnetic fields in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Model

> In the present work we use the large-$N_c$ approximation to investigate quark matter described by the SU(2) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model subject to a strong magnetic field. The Landau levels are filled in such a way that usual kinks appear in the effective mass and other related quantities. $β$-equilibrium is also considered and the macroscopic properties of a magnetar described by this quark matter is obtained. Our study shows that the magnetar masses and radii are larger if the magnetic field increases but only very large fields ($\ge 10^{18}$ G) affect the EoS in a non negligible way.

## Key claim

Using the large-\(N_c\) approximation of the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, the authors find that magnetar masses and radii increase with magnetic field strength, but only fields of at least \(10^{18}\) G produce a non-negligible effect on the equation of state.

## Method

The authors evaluate the thermodynamic potential for two-flavor quark matter in a constant external magnetic field using the large-\(N_c\) (mean-field) approximation, summing over Landau levels and regularizing divergent vacuum integrals with a sharp non-covariant cutoff \(\Lambda = 587.9\) MeV. They then impose \(\beta\)-equilibrium and charge neutrality by including leptons (electrons and muons) as a free Fermi gas in the magnetic field, and solve the gap equation for the effective quark mass self-consistently. The resulting equation of state is used to compute the mass-radius relation of magnetars via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

* Quark effective mass at zero density and zero field: \(M_0 \simeq 400\) MeV.
* First-order chiral transition occurs at critical chemical potential \(\mu_c \simeq 360\) MeV for \(B=0\).
* Bag constant obtained dynamically: \(\mathcal{B} = (181\ \text{MeV})^4\).
* For symmetric quark matter at \(B=0\), the energy per baryon has an absolute minimum at baryon density \(\rho_B \simeq 2 \rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is nuclear saturation density).
* Magnetic fields of order \(2 \times 10^{19}\) G shift the minimum of the energy per baryon to lower densities and change the concavity of the curve.
* Magnetar masses and radii increase only for fields \(\ge 10^{18}\) G.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of magnetized quark matter in the NJL model by Klimenko (2003), who investigated stability of two-flavor quark matter under magnetic fields, and by Ghosh (2005), who showed that magnetic fields spontaneously break chiral symmetry. It also complements hadronic magnetar studies using the nonlinear Walecka model by Prakash (1997) and density-dependent hadronic models by Providencia (2008). The results enable more realistic modeling of quark stars (self-bound by the strong interaction) as opposed to conventional neutron stars, and may also be relevant for understanding the quark-gluon plasma produced in non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where magnetic fields of about \(10^{19}\) G can arise.
