# Convergence properties of $T'$-Expansion Scheme: Hadron Resonance Gas and Cluster Expansion Model

> In this study, we assess the effectiveness and robustness of the recently proposed $T'$-expansion scheme for expanding the equation of state of strongly interacting matter to finite density, by comparing its performance relative to the conventional Taylor expansion method in various effective QCD models. We use baryon number density and its susceptibilities to calculate the expansion coefficients in the $T'$-expansion scheme with and without the Stefan-Boltzmann limit correction. Our methodology involves comparing truncation orders to exact solutions to assess the scheme's accuracy. We utilize Ideal, Excluded Volume, and van der Waals formulations of the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model at low temperatures, and the Cluster Expansion Model at higher temperatures. Our findings indicate that the $T'$-expansion scheme offers superior convergence properties near and above the chiral crossover temperature, where the chiral-criticality-inspired scaling $(\partial/ \partial T)_{μ_B} \sim (\partial^2/\partial μ_B^2)_T$ holds. However, it shows limited improvement in the HRG models, indicating that it may not be the most suitable choice for describing the hadronic phase.

## Key claim

The T'-expansion scheme offers superior convergence properties near and above the chiral crossover temperature where the chiral-criticality-inspired scaling (∂/∂T) at fixed μ_B ∼ (∂²/∂μ_B²) at fixed T holds, but shows limited improvement in the Hadron Resonance Gas models, indicating it may not be the most suitable choice for describing the hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors compared the T'-expansion scheme (with and without Stefan-Boltzmann limit correction) against the conventional Taylor expansion method for expanding the equation of state to finite baryon chemical potential. They applied all three expansion schemes to three variants of the Hadron Resonance Gas model (Ideal, Excluded Volume, and van der Waals) at low temperatures and to the Cluster Expansion Model at higher temperatures, comparing truncation orders up to O(μ_B¹⁰) against exact solutions for the scaled baryon density n_B/T³.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 155 MeV used for all HRG model comparisons
- Excluded Volume HRG model used repulsive core parameter b = 1 fm³
- van der Waals HRG model used parameters a = 329 MeV·fm³ and b = 3.42 fm³ to reproduce nuclear liquid-gas transition properties
- Cluster Expansion Model evaluated at T = 200 MeV, where the Roberge-Weiss radius of convergence for Taylor expansion is μ_B/T ≈ π
- CEM comparisons performed at μ_B/T values of 1.5, 2.5, 3.0, and 3.5
- vdW-HRG critical temperature T_c = 8a/(27b) ≈ 28.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the T'-expansion scheme originally developed by the Wuppertal-Budapest lattice QCD collaboration (Borsanyi 2021, Borsanyi 2022) by testing its convergence properties in effective QCD models rather than lattice data directly. A similar analysis was performed recently by Wen (2024) in the context of the functional renormalization group approach. The results indicate the T'-expansion scheme is most advantageous when applied to systems exhibiting the chiral-criticality scaling ∂/∂T ∼ ∂²/∂μ_B², which is preserved in the Cluster Expansion Model but not in HRG models, guiding future applications of the scheme to lattice QCD data at finite density.
