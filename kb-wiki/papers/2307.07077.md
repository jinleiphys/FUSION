# Level Structures of $^{56,58}$Ca Cast Doubt on a doubly magic $^{60}$Ca

> Gamma decays were observed in $^{56}$Ca and $^{58}$Ca following quasi-free one-proton knockout reactions from $^{57,59}$Sc beams at $\approx 200$ MeV/nucleon. For $^{56}$Ca, a $γ$ ray transition was measured to be 1456(12) keV, while for $^{58}$Ca an indication for a transition was observed at 1115(34) keV. Both transitions were tentatively assigned as the $2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_{gs}$ decays, and were compared to results from ab initio and conventional shell-model approaches. A shell-model calculation in a wide model space with a marginally modified effective nucleon-nucleon interaction depicts excellent agreement with experiment for $2^+_1$ level energies, two-neutron separation energies, and reaction cross sections, corroborating the formation of a new nuclear shell above the $N$ = 34 shell. Its constituents, the $0f_{5/2}$ and $0g_{9/2}$ orbitals, are almost degenerate. This degeneracy precludes the possibility for a doubly magic $^{60}$Ca and potentially drives the dripline of Ca isotopes to $^{70}$Ca or even beyond.

## Key claim

The measured excitation energies of the first 2+ states in 56Ca (1456 keV) and 58Ca (1115 keV) show a decrease from N=36 to N=38 that contradicts the expectation of particle-hole symmetry for an isolated 0f5/2 orbital, implying that the 0f5/2 orbital is coupled to higher orbitals (specifically 0g9/2) and thus precluding the possibility of a doubly magic 60Ca.

## Method

Gamma decays were observed following quasi-free one-proton knockout reactions from 57,59Sc beams at approximately 200 MeV/nucleon on a liquid-hydrogen target at the Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory. The reaction residues (56,58Ca) were identified by the SAMURAI spectrometer, and de-excitation gamma rays were measured using the DALI2+ detector array with Doppler corrections performed using reaction vertex information from the MINOS time projection chamber.

## Key numbers

- 56Ca: gamma-ray transition measured at 1456(12) keV, tentatively assigned as the 2+1 → 0+gs decay
- 58Ca: gamma-ray transition measured at 1115(34) keV, tentatively assigned as the 2+1 → 0+gs decay (significance of 2.8 sigma)
- Inclusive cross section for 57Sc(p,2p)56Ca: 1.23(5) mbarn
- Inclusive cross section for 59Sc(p,2p)58Ca: 1.14(15) mbarn
- Partial cross section to the 2+1 state in 56Ca: 0.43(4) mbarn
- Partial cross section to the 2+1 state in 58Ca: 0.47(19) mbarn

## Context

This work provides the first measurement of excitation energies in 56Ca and 58Ca, extending the systematics of 2+1 energies in Ca isotopes beyond the established N=34 magic number (Steppenbeck 2013, Michimasa 2018, Chen 2019). The results challenge predictions of a doubly magic 60Ca from shell-model calculations using the GXPF1Bs Hamiltonian and from ab initio approaches (VS-IMSRG and coupled-cluster theory), which all predicted a flat behavior of 2+1 energies from N=36 to N=38. The revised A3DA-t shell-model interaction, with a marginally modified effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, reproduces the experimental data and suggests the dripline of Ca isotopes may extend to 70Ca or beyond, consistent with some predictions (Koura 2005, Erler 2012) but beyond others (Hergert 2020).
