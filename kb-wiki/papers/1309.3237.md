# Anisotropic flow of identified particles in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} =$ 2.76 TeV with the ALICE detector

> The measurement of anisotropic flow of identified particles, including charged pion, kaon, (anti-)proton as well as strange and multi-strange particles (${\rm K_{S}^{0}}$, ${\rm Λ}$, ${\rm Ξ}$ and ${\rm Ω}$) are measured in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV with the ALICE detector. The results are compared to measurements at RHIC and hydrodynamic model calculations. The mass splitting and the scaling with the number of constituent quarks of the anisotropic flow are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling of elliptic flow, which was observed at RHIC and taken as evidence for partonic collectivity, is only approximate at LHC energies (within 20% at pT/nq ~ 1.2 GeV/c) and that the KET/nq scaling is violated over the full pT range, in contrast to the RHIC results.

## Method

The ALICE detector measured anisotropic flow coefficients v2 and v3 for charged pions, kaons, (anti-)protons, and strange/multi-strange particles (KS0, Lambda, Xi, Omega) in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV using approximately 16 million minimum-bias events. Particle identification was performed via specific ionization energy loss in the Time Projection Chamber and time-of-flight measurements, while strange particles were reconstructed through their weak decay channels. Flow was measured using the scalar product and event plane methods with large pseudorapidity gaps (|Delta eta| > 1 for SP, > 2 for EP) to suppress non-flow correlations.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 16 million Pb-Pb events at sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV were analyzed with a minimum-bias trigger
- NCQ scaling of v2 holds only within 20% at pT/nq ~ 1.2 GeV/c
- Viscous hydrodynamic model calculations (VISH2+1) with Color Glass Condensate initial conditions and eta/s = 0.20 quantitatively reproduce the mass splitting of v2 for most particles
- For v3, hydrodynamic calculations could not quantitatively reproduce results using either CGC initial conditions with eta/s = 0.20 or Glauber initial conditions with eta/s = 0.08
- The KET/nq scaling for v3 works better than for v2 but remains only approximate

## Context

This work extends RHIC measurements of identified particle anisotropic flow (Adams 2003, Adare 2006) to LHC energies, comparing results to PHENIX data at sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV. The measurements test hydrodynamic model calculations by Heinz (2011) and the quark coalescence mechanism for hadron production. The results enable further constraints on initial conditions and shear viscosity over entropy density (eta/s) in hydrodynamic calculations, while the breakdown of NCQ scaling at LHC energies raises questions about the universality of partonic collectivity observed at RHIC.
