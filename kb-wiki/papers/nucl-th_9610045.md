# Neutron-Proton Correlations in an Exactly Solvable Model

> We examine isovector and isoscalar neutron-proton correlations in an exactly solvable model based on the algebra SO(8). We look particularly closely at Gamow-Teller strength and double beta decay, both to isolate the effects of the two kinds of pairing and to test two approximation schemes: the renormalized neutron-proton QRPA (RQRPA) and generalized BCS theory. When isoscalar pairing correlations become strong enough a phase transition occurs and the dependence of the Gamow-Teller beta+ strength on isospin changes in a dramatic and unfamiliar way, actually increasing as neutrons are added to an N=Z core. Renormalization eliminates the well-known instabilities that plague the QRPA as the phase transition is approached, but only by unnaturally suppressing the isoscalar correlations. Generalized BCS theory, on the other hand, reproduces the Gamow-Teller strength more accurately in the isoscalar phase than in the usual isovector phase, even though its predictions for energies are equally good everywhere. It also mixes T=0 and T=1 pairing, but only on the isoscalar side of the phase transition.

## Key claim

When isoscalar neutron-proton pairing correlations become strong enough, a phase transition occurs and the Gamow-Teller beta-plus strength increases as neutrons are added to an N=Z core, a counterintuitive behavior that the abstract notes but does not fully qualify as occurring only in the isoscalar phase and being correlated with the transfer of protons from pp pairs to isoscalar np pairs.

## Method

The authors constructed an exactly solvable model based on the algebra SO(8) with degenerate single-particle orbitals, where the Hamiltonian depends on three parameters controlling isovector spin-0 pairing, isoscalar spin-1 pairing, and a particle-hole force. They obtained exact analytic solutions for energies and Gamow-Teller matrix elements in the limiting cases of pure isovector pairing (x=1) and pure isoscalar pairing (x=-1), and performed numerical diagonalization in the SU(4) basis for intermediate cases. They then tested two approximation schemes: the renormalized quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) and generalized BCS theory, comparing their predictions for Gamow-Teller strengths and double-beta decay matrix elements against the exact results.

## Key numbers

- For Omega=12, N=10, the beta-plus strength S(beta+) in the isovector phase follows the formula S(beta+) = (N-T)(T+1)(2 Omega-N-T) / [(2T/3+1)(Omega+1/2)], where T = (N-Z)/2
- For Omega=12, N=10, the beta-plus strength in the isoscalar phase follows S(beta+) = (N-T)(T+1)(2 Omega-N-T) / (Omega-T+1/2)
- The Ikeda sum rule gives S(beta-) - S(beta+) = 3(N-Z)
- For the double-beta decay calculation, Omega=12, N=12, T=4, and g_ph = 1.5 g were used
- In the generalized BCS theory, the beta-plus strength for solution A (pure pp and nn pairing) is S(beta+) = 3Z - 3NZ/(2 Omega)
- For solution C (mixed pairing), S(beta+) = 3Z - 3NZ/(2 Omega) + 4 Omega^2 rho_0^2 - 2 Omega (t_0)^2

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the SO(8) model by Pang (1969) and Dussell et al. (1981) that focused primarily on energy levels, shifting emphasis to charge-changing decay operators. The paper directly tests the renormalized QRPA approach of Toivanen and Suhonen (1995) and Schwieger, Simkovic, and Faessler (1996), finding that while RQRPA eliminates the instability of standard QRPA near the phase transition, it fails to capture the physics of the isoscalar phase. The generalized BCS theory, reviewed by Goodman (1979), reproduces ground-state energies well in both phases and actually performs better for beta-decay strengths in the isoscalar phase than in the isovector phase. The results enable future work on a "generalized QRPA" starting from the generalized quasiparticle vacuum, and suggest that projection methods or the Lipkin-Nogami method, as tested by Satula and Wyss (1996), may be needed to properly mix isoscalar and isovector pairing on both sides of the phase transition.
