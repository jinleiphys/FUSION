# Dynamical Model of Weak Pion Production Reactions

> The dynamical model of pion electroproduction has been extended to investigate the weak pion production reactions. The predicted cross sections of neutrino-induced pion production reactions are in good agreement with the existing data. We show that the renormalized(dressed) axial N-$Δ$ form factor contains large dynamical pion cloud effects and this renormalization effects are crucial in getting agreement with the data. We conclude that the N-$Δ$ transitions predicted by the constituent quark model are consistent with the existing neutrino induced pion production data in the $Δ$ region.

## Key claim

The authors extend their dynamical model of pion electroproduction to weak pion production reactions and show that, contrary to previous observations, the N-Δ transition strengths predicted by the constituent quark model are consistent with existing neutrino-induced pion production data in the Δ region, provided that dynamical pion cloud effects are included to renormalize the bare axial N-Δ form factor.

## Method

The authors construct weak vector currents from electromagnetic currents via isospin rotations using the Conserved Vector Current (CVC) hypothesis, and build axial vector currents guided by effective chiral Lagrangian methods with no adjustable parameters. The bare N-Δ transitions at Q²→0 are taken from constituent quark model calculations by Hemmert, Holstein, and Mukhopadhyay (1995), and their Q²-dependence is assumed identical to that determined in the SL model of pion electroproduction. The dynamical pion cloud effects are then incorporated by renormalizing these bare form factors through the non-resonant π production mechanisms within the coupled-channel dynamical framework.

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark model prediction for the axial N-Δ transition strength is about 30% lower than what was previously extracted from data (Hemmert, Holstein, and Mukhopadhyay, 1995; data from Kitagaki et al., 1990).
- The nucleon axial coupling constant is taken as g_A = 1.26, and the axial dipole mass parameter is M_A = 1.02 GeV from Meissner (1988).
- The pion decay constant is F = 93 MeV, the Fermi constant is G_F = 1.16637 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻², and cos θ_c = 0.974.
- The bare axial N-Δ form factor at Q²₀ = (m_Δ - m_N)² is given by g_A*(Q²₀) = (1/√2)(6/5)g_A.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model of Sato and Lee (2001) from pion electroproduction to weak pion production reactions, providing a framework that contrasts with the earlier dispersion relation approach of Adler (1968) which did not include vector meson exchanges and neglected imaginary parts of non-P₃₃ multipole amplitudes. The model enables future extraction of axial vector N-Δ transition form factors from neutrino-induced pion production data at facilities such as Fermilab, KEK, and the Japan Hadron Facility, and resolves a long-standing discrepancy between constituent quark model predictions and experimental extractions by demonstrating that dynamical pion cloud effects account for the missing strength.
