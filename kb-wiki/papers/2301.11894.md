# Next-generation multi-fluid hydrodynamic model for RHIC BES

> We have developed a next-generation hybrid event-by-event three-fluid hydrodynamic model, suitable for simulations of heavy-ion collisions in the energy range from few up to tens of GeV per colliding NN pair. At such energies the interpenetration time of the nuclei is of the same order as the lifetime of the system, however this model treats the initial phase hydrodynamically. Thanks to that it is more sensitive to the Equation of State than 1-fluid models with initial states being parametrised or generated by transport approach. Hence, our model is well designed for simulations at collision energies, at which matter in vicinity of the QCD critical endpoint is expected. The construction of the model is explained and basic observables like hadron spectra in rapidity and transverse momentum, as well as elliptic flow are calculated.

## Key claim

The authors present MUFFIN, a next-generation event-by-event three-fluid hydrodynamic model for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies (few to tens of GeV per NN pair), which treats the initial interpenetration phase hydrodynamically rather than via parametrized or transport-based initial conditions, making it more sensitive to the Equation of State near the QCD critical endpoint. The model is hybridized with the SMASH hadronic cascade for final-state interactions, but the current version lacks viscous corrections, which are planned for future work.

## Method

The model uses three coupled fluids (projectile, target, and fireball) evolved simultaneously on a hyperbolic coordinate grid using a modified vHLLE code, with friction terms governing energy-momentum exchange between fluids based on local densities and relative velocities. Initial nucleon positions are sampled from a Woods-Saxon distribution and smeared into fluid cells, with event-by-event fluctuations included. Hadronization occurs at a fixed combined energy density of 0.5 GeV/fm³ via the Cooper-Frye formula, followed by final-state interactions simulated with the SMASH transport model.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear radius parameterization: R = (1.1A^{1/3} - 0.656A^{-1/3}) fm, with diffuseness a = 0.459 fm
- Initial time τ₀ = 5 fm/c chosen for good energy conservation (less than 1% violation)
- Particlization criterion: combined energy density ε_sw = 0.5 GeV/fm³
- Simulations performed at six collision energies: √s_NN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, and 62.4 GeV
- 3000 hydrodynamic simulations per energy, with 500 oversampled hadronic events per hydrodynamic configuration
- Friction scaling parameters: ξ_h = 1.8√(2m_N/√s_pt), ξ_q = 30√(2m_N/√s_pt), ξ_fα = 0.15 m_N²/s_fα
- Energy density threshold for QGP phase: ε > 0.5 GeV/fm³
- Net energy flow through particlization hypersurface less than 5% of total outflow

## Context

This work extends the three-fluid hydrodynamic approach originally developed by Mishustin, Russkikh and Satarov (1991) and later improved by Ivanov, Russkikh and Toneev (2005), addressing shortcomings of the earlier THESEUS hybrid model (Batyuk 2016) which lacked viscous corrections, event-by-event fluctuations, and used computationally inefficient Cartesian coordinates. The model enables systematic studies of the Equation of State sensitivity in the BES energy range where the QCD critical endpoint is expected, and can be compared to results from the PHSD model (Moreau 2021) which predicts a much lower QGP fraction at low collision energies.
