# Neutron emission associated with $γγ\toγγ$ scattering in UPC of heavy ions at the LHC

> We calculate cross sections for the production of different neutron classes $0n 0n$, $0nXn+Xn0n$ and $Xn Xn$, possible to measure in Zero Degree Calorimeters (ZDCs), associated with photon-photon scattering in UPC of lead on lead. The calculations are performed for the ATLAS kinematics. Our calculations for neutron classes are tested against existing data for $ρ^0$ production in UPC. We get a good agreement for absolute cross section as well as very good results for fractional cross section which gives confidence to the analogous calculation for diphoton production, corresponding to $γγ\to γγ$ scattering. We present both absolute as well as fractional cross sections for the different neutron classes. We also show different distributions in impact parameter, photon rapidity, transverse momentum, diphoton invariant mass and photon rapidity difference. The shapes of the photon distributions depend on the neutron classes which we quantify in this paper. It would be valuable to test our predictions using the ATLAS main detector and the ATLAS ZDCs.

## Key claim

The paper calculates cross sections for different neutron emission classes (0n0n, 0nXn+Xn0n, XnXn) associated with light-by-light scattering in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at LHC energies, and finds that the fractional cross sections for these neutron classes are similar but not identical to those previously measured for rho0 meson production, providing a testable prediction for ATLAS.

## Method

The authors use the equivalent photon approximation in impact parameter space, incorporating a probability function P^c(b) for each neutron category derived from the average number of photon interactions with a nucleus. They first validate their neutron emission model by comparing calculated cross sections and fractional cross sections for rho0 photoproduction against existing STAR data at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and ALICE data at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, then apply the same formalism to gamma gamma -> gamma gamma scattering for ATLAS kinematics at sqrt(s_NN)=5.36 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For rho0 production in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (STAR), the ratio sigma(0n0n)/sigma(XnXn) = 7.1 +/- 0.3 experimentally, compared to theoretical value 6.4 for |y_V|<1
- For rho0 production in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the ratio sigma(0nXn)/sigma(XnXn) = 3.5 +/- 0.2 experimentally, compared to theoretical value 3.1 for |y_V|<1
- For rho0 production in Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV (ALICE), the XnXn cross section for |y|<0.2 is 24.4 +/- 1.3 (stat) +3.4/-2.9 (syst) mb experimentally, compared to 17.92 mb theoretical
- For gamma gamma -> gamma gamma in Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.36 TeV with ATLAS acceptance (y1,y2 in (-2.4,2.4), p_t,gamma > 2.5 GeV), the total fiducial cross section is 81.886 nb
- For gamma gamma -> gamma gamma, the 0n0n category constitutes 75.30% of the total fiducial cross section (61.679 nb), XnXn constitutes 5.20% (4.261 nb), and Xn0n+0nXn constitutes 9.50% (15.963 nb)
- The neutron emission probability correction uses neutron separation energy E_sep = 7.37 MeV and lambda = 0.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of light-by-light scattering mechanisms (Klusek-Gawenda 2016) and inelastic processes where photons couple to individual nucleons (KGS 2025). The neutron emission formalism builds on the impact parameter approach developed by Baltz (2002, 2009) and Klusek-Gawenda (2013), and the authors validate their model against STAR rho0 data (STAR 2007) and ALICE rho0 data (ALICE 2020). The predictions enable ATLAS to test the coherent contribution to gamma gamma -> gamma gamma scattering by measuring topological cross section fractions, and deviations from these predictions could signal contributions from inelastic processes.
