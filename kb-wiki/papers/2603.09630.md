# The eikonal spin-dependent Odderon and gluon Sivers function of a proton, and its small-$x$ evolution

> The matrix element in the proton of the eikonal Odderon operator, with a helicity flip, has been shown to correspond to the dipole gluon Sivers function. We employ a three quark light-front model of the proton to determine the Sivers function at moderately small $x_0 \sim 0.1$ and transverse momentum $k_\perp \lesssim 1$~GeV. The model light-cone (LC) wave function predicts the properties of $x f_{1T}^{\perp g}(x,k_\perp)$ such as its overall magnitude, the position of its peak in $k_\perp$, and its behavior at small $k_\perp$. We then compute numerically the BFKL anomalous dimension characterizing the power-law tail at $k_\perp \gtrsim 1.5$~GeV of the gluon Sivers function at small LC momentum fractions, $α_s \log x_0/x = 1$: $x f_{1T}^{\perp g}(x,k_\perp) \sim k_\perp^{-3.3}$.

## Key claim

The eikonal spin-dependent Odderon and the associated gluon Sivers function of the proton are computed from a three-quark light-cone wave function model at moderately small $x_0 \sim 0.1$, with the caveat that the model is intended to describe partonic structure only for transverse momenta $k_\perp \lesssim 1$ GeV and that the Sivers function exhibits a logarithmic divergence at low $k_\perp$ rather than a suppression $\sim k_\perp^2$.

## Method

The authors compute the $C$-odd triple-gluon coupling to the proton (the eikonal Odderon operator with helicity flip) using a light-front three-quark model of the proton, expanding the Wilson lines to cubic order in the gluon field $gA^{+a}$. They then extract the gluon Sivers function $x f_{1T}^{\perp g}(x,k_\perp)$ from the forward helicity-flip Odderon via the relation $x f_{1T}^\perp (x,k_\perp) = (N_c k_\perp M_p)/(2 \pi^3 g^2) e^{-i\phi_k} \mathcal{O}_{-+}(\mathbf{k}_\perp)$. The initial condition at $x_0$ is evolved to smaller $x$ using the linear BFKL evolution equation (Mueller's dipole approach) for the helicity-flip Odderon, solving numerically for the radial part $\mathcal{O}_S(Y,r_\perp)$.

## Key numbers

- The gluon Sivers function peaks at $k_\perp \simeq 0.5$ GeV with magnitude $(4-5) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- At $k_\perp = 0.9$ GeV the magnitude is about $10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-2}$, falling to about $10^{-4}$ GeV$^{-2}$ at $k_\perp = 1.2$ GeV.
- The BFKL-evolved power-law tail at $\alpha_s \log x_0/x = 1$ (rapidity $Y=4$) gives $x f_{1T}^{\perp g}(x,k_\perp) \sim k_\perp^{-3.3}$ for $k_\perp \gtrsim 1.5$ GeV.
- The anomalous dimension $\gamma(Y)$ decreases from $\gamma(Y=0)=1$ (perturbative three-gluon exchange) to $\gamma(Y=4)\approx 0.8$.
- The ratio of three-gluon to photon exchange cross sections for exclusive forward $\chi_{c1}$ production is $r \approx 10^{-6}$ at $x \simeq 0.1$.
- Best-fit parameters for the Odderon at $x_0$: $\mathcal{N}=0.036$ GeV$^{-2}$, $Q_b=0.331$ GeV, $Q_c=0.220$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the eikonal Odderon approach of Zhou (2013), which used an extended McLerran-Venugopalan model with cubic Casimir and high-dimensional color/spin representations, by instead constructing a model where the static sources are in the fundamental representations of both color-SU(3) and spin-SU(2). The resulting Sivers function differs from the commonly used parameterization of D'Alesio et al. (2015), which does not originate from the small-$x$ Odderon correspondence and is not suited as initial condition for BFKL evolution. The results enable predictions for single spin asymmetries at the future Electron-Ion Collider and for processes such as exclusive forward $\chi_{c1}$ production, where the small tri-gluon PDF obtained here implies transversely polarized $\chi_{c1}$ production dominates over the Primakoff process.
