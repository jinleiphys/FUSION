# Exploiting Intrinsic Triangular Geometry in Relativistic He3+Au Collisions to Disentangle Medium Properties

> Recent results in d+Au and p+Pb collisions at RHIC and the LHC provide evidence for collective expansion and flow of the created medium. We propose a control set of experiments to directly compare particle emission patterns from p+Au, d+Au, and He3+Au or t+Au collisions at the same sqrt(sNN). Using Monte Carlo Glauber we find that a He3 or triton projectile, with a realistic wavefunction description, induces a significant intrinsic triangular shape to the initial medium and that, even with viscous damping, this survives into a significant third order flow moment v3. By comparing systems with one, two, and three initial hot spots, one can disentangle the effects from the initial spatial distribution of the deposited energy and viscous damping. These are key tools to answering the question of how small a droplet of matter is necessary to form a quark-gluon plasma described by nearly inviscid hydrodynamics.

# Exploiting Intrinsic Triangular Geometry in Relativistic He3+Au Collisions to Disentangle Medium Properties

## Key claim
The paper proposes that comparing particle emission patterns from p+Au, d+Au, and He3+Au (or t+Au) collisions at the same center-of-mass energy can disentangle the effects of initial spatial energy deposition from viscous damping, provided the system lives long enough for the intrinsic triangular shape from the He3 projectile to survive into a measurable third-order flow moment v3.

## Method
The authors used Monte Carlo Glauber simulations with realistic nuclear wavefunctions, a Woods-Saxon distribution for Au, the Hulthen wavefunction for the deuteron, and Green's function Monte Carlo calculations with the AV18+UIX interaction for He3 and triton, to generate initial energy density distributions. They then evolved individual events through a boost-invariant relativistic viscous hydrodynamic code (vh2) followed by Cooper-Frye freeze-out and a hadronic cascade, calculating v2 and v3 flow coefficients as functions of transverse momentum for p+Au, d+Au, and He3+Au systems at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers
- Au nucleus Woods-Saxon parameters: radius 6.42 fm, skin thickness 0.44 fm, with a hard-core repulsive exclusion radius of 0.4 fm between nucleons.
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross sections: 42 mb at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, 70 mb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
- Each participant nucleon contributes energy with a Gaussian distribution of sigma = 0.4 fm (default) or sigma = 0.7 fm (alternative smearing).
- For He3+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with eta/s = 1/(4pi), the pion v3 at pT = 1 GeV exceeds that from d+Au and p+Au by at least 50 percent.
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the system stays in the plasma phase for only 2-3 fm/c.
- Viscous heating changes multiplicity by less than 11 percent for eta/s < 2/(4pi).
- For p+Au at pT = 2 GeV, changing from sigma = 0.4 fm to sigma = 0.7 fm changes v2 by 60 percent.

## Context
This work extends the observation of collective flow in small systems at RHIC and the LHC, specifically following results from d+Au collisions at RHIC (Adare 2013) and p+Pb collisions at the LHC (CMS 2012, ALICE 2012, ATLAS 2012) that showed flow patterns similar to those in heavy-ion collisions. The paper directly addresses the ambiguity between initial-state energy deposition and viscous damping identified by Sorensen (2011) and the role of sub-nucleonic fluctuations discussed by Bzdak (2013). The proposed control experiment with three different projectile geometries (p, d, He3) at the same energy would enable discrimination between these effects and help determine the minimum system size required for quark-gluon plasma formation with nearly inviscid hydrodynamics.
