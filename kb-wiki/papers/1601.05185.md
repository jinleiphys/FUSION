# Time-dependent Hartree-Fock Study of Octupole Vibrations in doubly magic nuclei

> Octupole vibrations are studied in some doubly magic nuclei using the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory with a Skyrme energy density functional. Through the use of the linear response theory, the energies and transition amplitudes of the low-lying vibrational modes for each of the nuclei were determined. Energies were found to be close to experimental results. However, transition amplitudes, quantified by the deformation parameter $β_3$, are underestimated by TDHF. A comparison with single-particle excitations on the Hartree-Fock ground-state shows that the collective octupole vibrations have their energy lowered due to attractive RPA residual interaction.

## Key claim

The time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory with a Skyrme energy density functional reproduces the energies of low-lying octupole vibrations in doubly magic nuclei reasonably well, but systematically underestimates the transition amplitudes (quantified by the deformation parameter β₃) compared to experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the TDHF theory with the SLy4 Skyrme functional to compute the time evolution of the octupole moment after a small initial boost in the linear regime. The strength function was obtained by Fourier transform of the damped octupole moment, and the energies and transition amplitudes of the first 3⁻ phonon were extracted from the peak areas. The calculations were performed on a three-dimensional grid with 0.8 fm mesh spacing for 10000 iterations with a time step of 1.5×10⁻²⁴ s.

## Key numbers

- **⁴⁰Ca**: Theoretical 3⁻ energy = 3.92 MeV (experimental 3.74 MeV); theoretical β₃ = 0.20 (experimental β₃ = 0.30–0.41)
- **¹⁶O**: Theoretical 3⁻ energy = 7.23 MeV (experimental 6.13 MeV); theoretical β₃ = 0.30 (experimental β₃ = 0.37–0.79)
- **⁴⁸Ca**: Theoretical 3⁻ energy = 5.74 MeV (experimental 4.51 MeV); theoretical β₃ = 0.11 (experimental β₃ = 0.25)
- **⁵⁶Ni**: Theoretical 3⁻ energy = 9.59 MeV (experimental 4.93 MeV, uncertain assignment); theoretical β₃ = 0.13
- **¹⁰⁰Sn**: Theoretical 3⁻ energy = 6.91 MeV; theoretical β₃ = 0.08
- **¹³²Sn**: Theoretical 3⁻ energy = 5.53 MeV (experimental 4.35 MeV); theoretical β₃ = 0.07
- **²⁰⁸Pb**: Theoretical 3⁻ energy = 3.4 MeV (experimental 2.61 MeV); theoretical β₃ = 0.05 (experimental β₃ = 0.11)
- **⁴⁰Ca particle-hole excitations**: Energies range from 5.51 MeV (π(1f₇/₂,1d₃/₂⁻¹)) to 12.60 MeV (ν(1f₇/₂,1d₅/₂⁻¹)), all significantly above the collective 3⁻ energy of 3.92 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of giant resonances (Bloch 1979, Chomaz 1987, Lacroix 2000, Stevenson 2004, Almehed 2005, Nakatsukasa 2005, Umar 2005, Maruhn 2005, Reinhard 2006, Stevenson 2007, Avez 2013, Scamps 2013, Scamps 2014) to the systematic study of low-lying octupole vibrations in doubly magic nuclei, which are important for understanding reaction mechanisms such as fusion (Simenel 2013c). The results enable microscopic input for coupled-channel reaction calculations (Hagino 2012) and show that TDHF captures the mass dependence of phonon characteristics, though the systematic underestimation of β₃ requires further investigation with other Skyrme parametrisations.
