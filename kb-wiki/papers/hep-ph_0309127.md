# Deep Inelastic Scattering in the Color Glass Formalism

> 5 pages, no figure. Proceedings for XIth International Workshop on Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS03), St. Petersburg, April 2003

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism can explain geometric scaling of the total cross section in deep inelastic scattering not only below the saturation scale but also up to much higher virtualities, roughly \(Q^2 \sim Q_s^4/\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2\), through the BFKL equation with a saturation boundary condition; however, the analytical formula for the dipole S-matrix presented is crude and expected to apply only in a limited kinematical regime marginally explored at HERA.

## Method

The author computes the \(\gamma^* p\) total cross section \(\sigma_{\rm total}^{\gamma^*p}\) and the \(F_2\) structure function at small \(x\) using the dipole factorization formula, where the dipole-proton cross section \(\sigma_{\rm dipole}(x,r_\perp)\) is obtained from the CGC average of Wilson lines. The dipole S-matrix is derived analytically in two regimes (weak field for small dipoles and strong field for large dipoles) using a Gaussian approximation for the weight function, with an interpolation between them. The high-energy behavior of the dipole cross section is then analyzed by modeling the growth of the black disk radius under BFKL evolution with confinement-induced exponential decrease of the gluon density at large impact parameter.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale energy dependence is \(Q_s^2(x) \propto x^{-\beta}\), consistent with the geometric scaling variable \(R_0^2(x) \propto x^{\lambda}\).
* The LO BFKL anomalous dimension is \(\gamma \simeq 0.64\), which does not change even with higher order effects included.
* The dipole cross section at high energy saturates the Froissart bound: \(\sigma_{\rm dipole}(x,r_\perp) \simeq \frac{\pi}{2} \left(\frac{\omega\bar\alpha_s}{m_\pi}\right)^2 \ln^2 \frac{1}{x}\), with \(\mu = 2m_\pi\) where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass.
* The structure function \(F_2\) in the high energy limit behaves as \(F_2(x,Q^2) \propto \ln^3 (1/x)\).
* The total cross section in the extended scaling window behaves as \(\sigma_{\rm total}^{\gamma^*p}(x,Q^2) \sim (Q_s^2(x)/Q^2)^\gamma\) for \(Q_s^2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim Q_s^4/\Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the CGC formalism developed by Iancu and collaborators and by Edmond and Raju to phenomenological predictions for DIS, building on the geometric scaling phenomenon observed by Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (2001) and the BFKL solution with saturation boundary conditions from Iancu, Itakura, and McLerran (2002). The analytical dipole cross section formula improves upon earlier parametrizations such as the GBW model (three parameters, naive \(\gamma=1\)) and the BGK model (five parameters, includes DGLAP evolution) by reproducing correct QCD behaviors in both the saturation and extended scaling regimes. The result enables future calculations of DIS structure functions at sufficiently small \(x\) that are rooted in QCD rather than purely phenomenological, with further predictions to be presented in a separate publication.
