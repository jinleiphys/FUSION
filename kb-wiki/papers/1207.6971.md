# New type of crossover physics in three-component Fermi gases

> A three-component Fermi gas near a broad Feshbach resonance does not have a universal ground state due to the Thomas collapse, while it does near a narrow Feshbach resonance. We explore its universal phase diagram in the plane of the inverse scattering length 1/akF and the resonance range RkF. For a large RkF, there exists a Lifshitz transition between superfluids with and without an unpaired Fermi surface as a function of 1/akF. With decreasing RkF, the Fermi surface coexisting with the superfluid can change smoothly from that of atoms to trimers ("atom-trimer continuity"), corresponding to the quark-hadron continuity in a dense nuclear matter. Eventually, there appears a finite window in 1/akF where the superfluid is completely depleted by the trimer Fermi gas, which gives rise to a pair of quantum critical points. The boundaries of these three quantum phases are determined in regions where controlled analyses are possible and are also evaluated based on a mean-field plus trimer model.

# New type of crossover physics in three-component Fermi gases

## Key claim
A three-component Fermi gas near a narrow Feshbach resonance exhibits a universal phase diagram with three quantum phases, including a smooth "atom-trimer continuity" where the unpaired Fermi surface coexisting with the superfluid changes from atoms to trimers, analogous to quark-hadron continuity in dense nuclear matter. This universality exists only near a narrow resonance (where the resonance range R* sets the three-body ground state), not near a broad resonance where the Thomas collapse prevents a universal ground state.

## Method
The author derives the universal phase diagram in the plane of inverse scattering length 1/a k_F and resonance range R* k_F using controlled analyses in two limits: the narrow resonance limit (R* k_F → ∞) where zero-temperature mean-field theory becomes exact, and the dilute limit (k_F → 0) where the problem reduces to few-body integral equations for dimer and trimer binding energies. These asymptotic results are interpolated using a mean-field plus trimer model that adds noninteracting trimers to the mean-field grand potential.

## Key numbers
- Trimer appears from three-atom threshold at R*/a = -0.0917249 (quantum critical point separating atomic superfluid from trimer Fermi gas)
- Trimer disappears into atom-dimer threshold at R*/a = 2.18151
- Lifshitz transition from trimer Fermi gas coexisting with dimer superfluid to dimer superfluid alone occurs at R*/a = 0.359011
- In the mean-field plus trimer model, the superfluid transition occurs at R* k_F = 0.288325 at 1/a k_F = 0, with a maximum of R* k_F = 0.381739 reached at 1/a k_F = 0.314545
- Below the critical value R* k_F = 0.381739, a finite window in 1/a k_F opens where the superfluid is completely depleted by the trimer Fermi gas

## Context
This work extends studies of two-component Fermi gas BCS-BEC crossover (Eagles 1969, Leggett 1980, Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink 1985) to three-component systems, addressing the Thomas collapse problem (Thomas 1935) that prevents universality near broad Feshbach resonances. The narrow Feshbach resonance approach follows Petrov (2004) and builds on mean-field studies of three-component Fermi gases (Bedaque 2009, Paananen 2006, He 2006, Zhai 2007). The atom-trimer continuity provides a cold-atom analog of quark-hadron continuity in quantum chromodynamics (Schafer and Wilczek 1999, Hatsuda 2006), enabling experimental investigation of this crossover using ultracold atoms such as 6Li or 173Yb.
