# The neutron skin-thickness of 208 Pb determined by electron and proton scattering

> The consistency between electron-scattering and proton-scattering is examined through the fourth moment of the charge distribution.

## Key claim

The fourth moment of the charge distribution observed in electron scattering, ⟨r⁴⟩_c, can serve as a consistency check between electron-scattering and proton-scattering analyses of the point-proton and point-neutron densities in ²⁰⁸Pb, but the neutron skin thickness δR cannot be determined from electron scattering alone because the contribution of (⟨r²⟩_n − ⟨r²⟩_p) to ⟨r⁴⟩_c is only about 0.1%.

## Method

The authors compared the moments of the charge, proton, and neutron distributions in ²⁰⁸Pb obtained from previous proton-scattering analyses (Ray 1979, Hoffmann 1980, Starodubasky and Hintz 1994, Mark 1995) with those from electron-scattering data analyzed by sum-of-Gaussians and Fourier-Bessel methods. They used the relation between the fourth moment of the charge distribution ⟨r⁴⟩_c and the moments of the point-proton and point-neutron distributions, ⟨r⁴⟩_p, ⟨r²⟩_p, and ⟨r²⟩_n, together with the known single-nucleon charge radii r_p and r_n², to test whether the values derived from proton scattering reproduce the experimental ⟨r⁴⟩_c. A least squares analysis (LSA) of non-relativistic mean-field models was also performed to obtain reference values for the moments.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ⟨r⁴⟩_c from electron scattering: 1171.981 fm⁴ (sum-of-Gaussians analysis)
- Experimental ⟨r²⟩_c from electron scattering: 30.283 fm²
- ⟨r²⟩_p from LSA of non-relativistic mean-field models: 29.738 fm²
- ⟨r²⟩_n from LSA: 31.507 fm²
- Neutron skin thickness δR from LSA: 0.160 fm (defined as √⟨r²⟩_n − √⟨r²⟩_p)
- δR from proton-scattering analysis (Mark 1995): 0.198 fm
- δR from antiproton-atom analysis (Klos 2007): 0.159 fm
- δR from coherent π⁰ production (Tarbert 2014): 0.140 fm
- ⟨r⁴⟩_c calculated from antiproton-atom parameters: 1156.047 fm⁴
- ⟨r⁴⟩_c calculated from coherent π⁰ production parameters: 1155.040 fm⁴
- Δ₂ (correction terms for second moment) from LSA: 0.612 fm²
- Δ₄ (correction terms for fourth moment) from LSA: 2.605 fm⁴

## Context

This work extends the iterative analysis approach of Ray (1979) and Hoffmann (1980), who attempted to determine point-proton and point-neutron densities consistently from combined electron and proton scattering data. The authors show that while previous proton-scattering analyses (Ray 1978, Ray 1979, Hoffmann 1980, Starodubasky and Hintz 1994, Mark 1995, Brown 1991, Sakaguchi 1998, Zenihiro 2010) all yield δR values around 0.14–0.2 fm, the ⟨r⁴⟩_c values from antiproton-atom and coherent π⁰ production analyses are much smaller than the experimental electron-scattering value, indicating that consistent analyses across probes are necessary. The fourth moment of the charge distribution is proposed as a bridge between electron and proton scattering, and the method is expected to become more important for neutron-rich unstable nuclei where neutron contributions to charge moments increase.
