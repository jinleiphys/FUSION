# Chiral Symmetry and Low Energy Pion-Nucleon Scattering

> In these lectures, I examine the effect of the meson factory $π$N data on the current algebra/PCAC program which describes chiral symmetry breaking in this system. After historical remarks on the current algebra/PCAC versus chiral Lagrangians approaches to chiral symmetry, and description of the need for $π$N amplitudes with virtual (off-mass-shell) pions in nuclear force models and other nuclear physics problems, I begin with kinematics and isospin aspects of the invariant amplitudes. A detailed introduction to the hadronic vector and axial-vector currents and the hypothesis of partially conserved axial-vector currents (PCAC) follows. I review and test against contemporary data the PCAC predictions of the Goldberger-Treiman relation, and the Adler consistency condition for a $π$N amplitude. Then comes a detailed description of the current algebra Ward-Takahashi identities in the chiral limit and a brief account of the on-shell current algebra Ward-Takahashi identities. The latter identities form the basis of so-called current algebra models of $π$N scattering. I then test these models against the contemporary empirical $π$N amplitudes extrapolated into the subthreshold region via dispersion relations. The scale and the t dependence of the "sigma term" is determined by the recent data.

## Key claim

The current algebra/PCAC program's predictions for low-energy pion-nucleon scattering, including the Goldberger-Treiman relation and the Adler consistency condition, are tested against contemporary meson factory data; the scale and t-dependence of the sigma term are determined by recent data, but the chiral perturbation theory expansion for elastic πN scattering converges slowly, with contributions at different orders remaining comparable.

## Method

The author reviews the kinematics and isospin structure of πN invariant amplitudes, then introduces the hadronic vector and axial-vector currents and the PCAC hypothesis. The PCAC predictions (Goldberger-Treiman relation, Adler consistency condition) are tested against contemporary data. Current algebra Ward-Takahashi identities are derived in the chiral limit and on-shell, forming the basis of current algebra models of πN scattering, which are then tested against empirical πN amplitudes extrapolated into the subthreshold region via dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass is small on the scale of other hadrons: m_π²/m_N² ≈ 1/45
- The threshold for physical πN scattering is s_th = (m + μ)², where m is the nucleon mass and μ is the pion mass
- For virtual pions in three-body forces, spacelike momenta satisfy q_0 ≈ 0 and |q_vec|² ≤ 10 m_π² (from Brown et al., 1969)
- At the condensation density for pion condensation, q_c² ≤ -2 m_π² (from Wilde et al.)
- The s-channel threshold value of ν = (s-u)/(4m) is ν_th = μ

## Context

This lecture paper extends the current algebra/PCAC approach to chiral symmetry, which developed in parallel with chiral Lagrangian approaches since the 1960s, and tests its predictions against modern meson factory πN data. The work builds on the foundational PCAC work of Gell-Mann and Levy (1960), the three-nucleon force analyses of Brown et al. (1968, 1969) and Coon et al. (1974), and the Tucson-Melbourne three-body force (1979). It enables improved treatment of off-shell πN amplitudes needed for nuclear force models, three-nucleon interactions, and pion condensation studies, while noting that chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg, 1979, 1990) provides a systematic alternative but faces slow convergence in elastic πN scattering.
