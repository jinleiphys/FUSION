# Universality regained: Kibble-Zurek dynamics, off-equilibrium scaling and the search for the QCD critical point

> We generalize and apply the key elements of the Kibble-Zurek framework of nonequilibrium phase transitions to study the non-equilibrium critical cumulants near the QCD critical point. We demonstrate the off-equilibrium critical cumulants are expressible as universal scaling functions. We discuss how to use off-equilibrium scaling to provide powerful model-independent guidance in searches for the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The paper argues that off-equilibrium critical cumulants near the QCD critical point can be expressed as universal scaling functions, provided the Kibble-Zurek framework is applied; this universality holds despite the fact that off-equilibrium evolution depends on non-universal inputs such as the details of trajectories in the QCD phase diagram and the mapping to the 3d Ising model.

## Method

The authors generalize the Kibble-Zurek framework of nonequilibrium phase transitions to study the real-time evolution of non-Gaussian critical cumulants near the QCD critical point. They solve rate equations for the cumulants, using the 3d Ising model static universality class (critical exponents alpha approx 0, beta approx 1/3, nu approx 2/3) and a dynamical critical exponent z approx 3, and then test a scaling hypothesis by rescaling the cumulants by powers of the Kibble-Zurek length l_KZ and time tau_KZ.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium Gaussian cumulant scales as kappa_2 ~ xi_eq^2, while the non-Gaussian cumulants scale as kappa_3 ~ xi_eq^4.5 and kappa_4 ~ xi_eq^7, where xi_eq is the equilibrium correlation length.
- The dynamical critical exponent for the QCD critical point is z approx 3, giving the relaxation time tau_eff ~ xi^z.
- The static critical exponents used are alpha approx 0, beta approx 1/3, nu approx 2/3, taken from the 3d Ising model.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of Gaussian fluctuations near the QCD critical point by Berdnikov and Rajagopal (1999) and the authors' own earlier work on non-Gaussian cumulants (Mukherjee, Venugopalan, Yin 2015). It also builds on the Kibble-Zurek framework pioneered by Kibble in cosmology and Zurek in condensed matter. The paper enables a model-independent experimental search for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions by predicting that rescaled cumulant data from different beam energies, centralities, and rapidities will collapse onto universal curves if the critical regime is probed.
