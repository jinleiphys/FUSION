# Equilibration and locality

> Experiments motivated by predictions of quantum mechanics indicate non-trivial correlations between spacelike-separated measurements. The phenomenon is referred to as a violation of strong-locality and, after Einstein, called ghostly action at a distance. An intriguing and previously unasked question is how the evolution of an assembly of particles to equilibrium-state relates to strong-locality. More specifically, whether, with this respect, indistinguishable particles differ from distinguishable ones. To address the question, we introduce a Markov-chain based framework over a finite set of microstates. For the first time, we formulate conditions needed to obey the particle transport- and strong-locality for indistinguishable particles. Models which obey transport-locality and lead to equilibrium-state are considered. We show that it is possible to construct models obeying and violating strong-locality both for indistinguishable particles and for distinguishable ones. However, we find that only for distinguishable particles strongly-local evolution to equilibrium is possible without breaking the microstate-symmetry. This is the strongest symmetry one can impose and leads to the shortest equilibration time. We hope that the results presented here may provide a new perspective on a violation of strong-locality, and the developed framework will help in future studies. Specifically they may help to interpret results on high-energy nuclear collisions indicating a fast equilibration of indistinguishable particles.

## Key claim

The authors show that it is possible to construct Markov-chain models obeying and violating strong-locality for both indistinguishable and distinguishable particles, but only for distinguishable particles is strongly-local evolution to equilibrium possible without breaking microstate-symmetry, which is the strongest symmetry one can impose and leads to the shortest equilibration time.

## Method

The authors introduce a Markov-chain framework over a finite set of microstates, called Symmetric Cell Models, in a 1+1 dimensional discrete-time system with a conserved number of particles on a ring of V cells. They formulate conditions for transport-locality (particles cannot move more than Delta cells per time step) and strong-locality (event probabilities depend only on events within the past light cone) for indistinguishable particles, then construct explicit transition matrices that obey or violate these conditions while maintaining space-translation, space-reversal, and time-reversal symmetries.

## Key numbers

* For indistinguishable particles with V=6 cells, N=4 particles, and Delta=1, the total number of microstates is W_ind = (N+V-1)!/(N!(V-1)!) = (4+6-1)!/(4!5!) = 126
* For distinguishable particles with V cells and N particles, the total number of microstates is W_dist = V^N
* In the example non-equilibrium matrix for indistinguishable particles, the probability of a transition where N particles in one cell redistribute to N-1 in the same cell and 1 in a neighboring cell is (2Delta)!N!/(N+2Delta)!
* In the large N limit, the transition X to Y (where X has all N particles in one cell and Y has N-1 in that cell and 1 in a neighboring cell) has a probability 2Delta+1 times larger than the inverse transition Y to X

## Context

This work extends the Cell Model framework introduced by Gazdzicki (2017, 2020) to study equilibration and locality, and addresses a previously unasked question about the relationship between strong-locality (violated in quantum mechanics experiments such as those by Aspect (1982) and Hensen (2015)) and the approach to equilibrium. The results may help interpret observations from high-energy nuclear collisions indicating fast equilibration of indistinguishable particles, and the framework could connect to stochastic mechanics approaches such as those by Nelson (1966, 2012).
