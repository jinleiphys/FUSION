## Key claim

The reaction cross sections for proton scattering on lead-208 that are consistent with the PREX-II neutron skin thickness of 0.283 Â± 0.071 fm are the experimental values at incident energies of 534.1, 549, and 806 MeV, as reproduced by a Love-Franey t-matrix folding model with scaled neutron densities.

## Method

The authors determined experimental neutron and matter radii for lead-208 from the known proton radius (5.444 fm from electron scattering) and the PREX-II neutron skin thickness. They then calculated proton and neutron densities using the Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (GHFB) method with angular momentum projection (AMP), scaling the neutron density to match the extracted neutron radius of 5.727 fm. The Love-Franey t-matrix folding model was applied to compute reaction cross sections for proton-lead scattering at incident energies between 400 and 900 MeV, and the results were compared with experimental data.
