# $^3P_2$ Superfluids Are Topological

> We clarify topology of $^3P_2$ superfluids which are expected to be realized in the inner cores of neutron stars and cubic odd-parity superconductors. $^3P_2$ phases include uniaxial/biaxial nematic phases and nonunitary ferromagnetic and cyclic phases. We here show that all the phases are accompanied by different types of topologically protected gapless fermions: Surface Majorana fermions in nematic phases and a quartet of (single) itinerant Majorana fermions in the cyclic (ferromagnetic) phase. Using the superfluid Fermi liquid theory, we also demonstrate that dihedral-two and -four biaxial nematic phases are thermodynamically favored in the weak coupling limit under a magnetic field. It is shown that the tricritical point exists on the phase boundary between these two phases and may be realized in the core of realistic magnetars. We unveil the intertwining of symmetry and topology behind mass acquisition of surface Majorana fermions in nematic phases.

## Key claim

All phases of \(^3P_2\) superfluids, uniaxial/biaxial nematic, cyclic, and ferromagnetic, are topologically nontrivial and host different types of topologically protected gapless fermions, but this topological protection is conditional on the preservation of specific symmetries (time-reversal symmetry for nematic phases and the combined \(P_3\) symmetry for surface Majorana fermions), which can be broken by a magnetic field component parallel to the surface normal.

## Method

The authors classify the topology of \(^3P_2\) order parameters using the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) Hamiltonian and the Altland-Zirnbauer symmetry class DIII for nematic phases. They compute the surface bound state solutions of the BdG equation under specular boundary conditions and derive the effective Majorana Hamiltonian for surface fermions. To determine thermodynamic stability under a magnetic field, they employ the superfluid Fermi liquid theory (quasiclassical Eilenberger equation) to minimize the Luttinger-Ward thermodynamic potential and construct the magnetic field–temperature phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point on the phase boundary between the \(D_2\) and \(D_4\) biaxial nematic phases occurs at \((T/T_{\rm c0}, \gamma \hbar H/\pi k_{\rm B}T_{\rm c0}) = (0.45, 0.083)\) for the Fermi liquid parameter \(F^{\rm a}_0 = -0.7\).
- The magnetic field range for realistic magnetars, \(H = 10^{13}\)–\(10^{15}\) G, corresponds to \(\gamma \hbar H/\pi k_{\rm B}T_{\rm c0} \approx 0.001\)–\(0.1\) with \(T_{\rm c0} = 0.2\) MeV.
- For \(F^{\rm a}_0 = 0\), the tricritical point lowers to \(0.15 T_{\rm c0}\).
- The one-dimensional winding number along the chiral symmetric momenta is \(w_{\rm 1d} = 2\) for the nematic phase when the \(P_3\) symmetry is unbroken.
- The surface Majorana fermion velocities are \((c_x, c_y) = (\Delta/k_{\rm F})(1, r)\) for \(\hat{\bm u} \parallel \hat{\bm z}\) and \((\Delta/k_{\rm F})(1, 1+r)\) for \(\hat{\bm u} \perp \hat{\bm z}\), where \(r\) is the nematic order parameter parameter.

## Context

This work extends topological classification, previously applied to superfluid \(^3\)He (Mizushima 2016, Volovik 2009), to the \(^3P_2\) superfluids expected in neutron star cores and cubic odd-parity superconductors (Volovik 1985, Ueda 1985, Sigrist 1991). It provides the first topological analysis of fermionic excitations in \(^3P_2\) phases, complementing prior studies of bosonic modes (Bedaque 2013, Leinson 2012) and topological defects (Masuda 2016). The results enable predictions for anisotropic surface magnetization in magnetars and suggest that the tricritical point may be observable in realistic neutron star interiors, with implications for heat capacity and cooling mechanisms.
