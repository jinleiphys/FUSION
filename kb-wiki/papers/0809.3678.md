# Electromagnetic form factor via Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in Minkowski space

> For a relativistic system of two scalar particles, we find the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in Minkowski space and use it to compute the electromagnetic form factor. The comparison with Euclidean space calculation shows that the Wick rotation in the form factor integral induces errors which increase with the momentum transfer Q^2. At JLab domain (Q^2=10 GeV^2/c^2), they are about 30%. Static approximation results in an additional and more significant error. On the contrary, the form factor calculated in light-front dynamics is almost indistinguishable from the Minkowski space one.

## Key claim
For a relativistic system of two scalar particles, the Wick rotation in the form factor integral induces errors that increase with momentum transfer Q^2; at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2/c^2 (the JLab domain) these errors are about 30%, while the static approximation produces an additional and more significant error. The form factor calculated via light-front dynamics is almost indistinguishable from the exact Minkowski-space result.

## Method
The Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equation was solved in Minkowski space using the Nakanishi integral representation, which expresses the BS amplitude in terms of a non-singular weight function g(γ,z). The electromagnetic form factor was then computed directly from the Minkowski BS amplitude via the vertex function integral, without any Wick rotation. For comparison, the form factor was also calculated using the Euclidean BS amplitude (with and without the static approximation) and via the light-front wave function derived from the Minkowski BS amplitude.

## Key numbers
- At Q^2 = 10 m^2 (the "JLab domain"), the naive Euclidean form factor (after Wick rotation) differs from the exact Minkowski-space form factor by about 30%.
- For binding energy B = 1 (with constituent mass m = 1 and exchange mass μ = 0.5), the static approximation form factor at Q^2 ≈ 10 m^2 differs from the Minkowski-space result by approximately one order of magnitude.
- The three form factors (Minkowski, Euclidean boosted, and static approximation) agree within a few percent only for Q^2 ≤ m^2.
- A spurious zero in the static approximation form factor appears at Q^2 ≈ 38 m^2, which is absent in the exact Minkowski-space result.
- The coupling constant α for the ladder+cross-ladder kernel at B = 0.01 is 1.206 (from the Minkowski method) and 1.205 (from the Euclidean method); at B = 1.00 it is 4.549 (Minkowski) and 4.551 (Euclidean).

## Context
This work extends the method for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space developed by Carbonell, Karmanov, and Mangin-Brinet (2006-2007) and applies it to compute electromagnetic form factors without the approximations inherent in Euclidean-space calculations. It directly tests the validity of the Wick rotation for form factor integrals, which had been assumed valid based on the proof by Wick (1954) for ladder kernels, and quantifies the errors introduced by the static approximation of Zuilhof and Tjon (1982). The results enable future calculations of form factors for systems with spin, where the errors are expected to be larger, and demonstrate that light-front dynamics provides a practical and accurate alternative to Euclidean-space methods.
