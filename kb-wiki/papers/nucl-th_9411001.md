# Time Reversal Invariance in the beta-Decays of A = 8 Nuclei

> We examine time reversal invariance in the beta-decays of 8B(2+) and 8Li(2+) to 8Be(2+) in detail, with particular attention to final state interactions of the two alpha particles from the decay of 8Be, and of the electron (positron) with the daughter nucleus. A R-matrix formulation is used, with the initial state described by a shell model. The R-matrix parameters are obtained by fitting the alpha-αscattering phase shifts and allowed beta-decay rates. Because the nuclear final state interaction effects on the time reversal test come from second forbidden beta-decays, they are small and can be minimized by a suitable choice of kinematic conditions. The e(+/-)-nucleus Coulomb interaction induced T-violation effects depend on the first forbidden operators.

## Key claim

The authors examine whether final state interactions between the two alpha particles from the decay of 8Be, and between the electron/positron and the daughter nucleus, could mimic a time reversal violation signal in the beta-decays of 8B and 8Li. They find that these "pseudo-TRI violation" effects are small and can be minimized by a suitable choice of kinematic conditions, rather than completely eliminating the possibility of a true TRI test.

## Method

An R-matrix formulation was used, with the initial 2+ states of 8Li and 8B described by a shell model wave function within a 1p shell model space using the Cohen-Kurath effective interaction (2BME 8-16). The R-matrix parameters were obtained by fitting alpha-alpha scattering phase shifts and allowed beta-decay rates in the A=8 system, with the resonant states of 8Be* saturated by shell model states. The differential decay rate was expanded in terms of hadronic response functions and kinematic functions, keeping terms up to order O(κ²R²), and the T-odd correlation observables were studied in this framework.

## Key numbers

- The most accurate test of TRI in nuclear beta-decay prior to this work had an upper limit of a_T ≤ 10⁻³ (Dcoef1)
- The shell model calculation placed the J^P=2+ levels of 8Be* at E=3.55, 13.37, 16.19, and 17.45 MeV (theoretically determined energies)
- The experimental resonant state corresponding to the E=3.55 MeV shell model state lies at E=3.04 MeV
- The experimental resonant state corresponding to the E=16.19 MeV shell model state lies at E=16.63 MeV
- The energy released in the transitions is approximately 3-10 MeV, much larger than the electron mass m_e
- The R-matrix parameters were constrained by fitting alpha-alpha scattering phase shifts in the energy range 0 < E < 16 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of TRI tests in nuclear beta-decay, particularly the mass-8 system for which an experimental study had been proposed by Adelberger. The authors build on the R-matrix formalism and shell model calculations (Cohen and Kurath, 1965; Haxton) to address whether final state interactions spoil TRI tests, a question raised by earlier work. The paper enables experimental searches for time reversal violation in the A=8 system by showing that pseudo-TRI effects from nuclear final state interactions (coming from second forbidden beta-decays) and e±-nucleus Coulomb interactions (depending on first forbidden operators) can be minimized through proper kinematic selection.
