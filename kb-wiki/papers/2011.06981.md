# Classification of Magnetic Vortices by Angular Momentum Conservation

> Superfluid vortices are quantum excitations carrying quantized amount of orbital angular momentum in a phase where global symmetry is spontaneously broken. We address a question of whether magnetic vortices in superconductors with dynamical gauge fields can carry nonzero orbital angular momentum or not. We discuss the angular momentum conservation in several distinct classes of examples from crossdisciplinary fields of physics across condensed matter, dense nuclear systems, and cosmology. The angular momentum carried by gauge field configurations around the magnetic vortex plays a crucial role in satisfying the principle of the conservation law. Based on various ways how the angular momentum conservation is realized, we provide a general scheme of classifying magnetic vortices in different phases of matter.

## Key claim
Magnetic vortices in systems with dynamical gauge fields can carry nonzero orbital angular momentum only when there is a compensating angular momentum stored either in a background medium (Class Ia) or at a topological boundary (Class Ib); otherwise, angular momentum conservation forces the vortex to be "spinless" (Class II), unless the vortex has an inhomogeneous profile along its axis that prevents creation by simple flux piercing (Class III).

## Method
The authors analyze static vortex solutions in several theoretical models: a nonrelativistic reduction of a U(1) Higgs model with a background charge density (for Type-II superconductors), a Chern-Simons effective theory on the surface of a topological insulator, the relativistic Nielsen-Olesen model, a non-Abelian color-flavor-locked (CFL) vortex in dense quark matter, and the charged semilocal vortex of Abraham. For each system, they compute the matter and gauge-field contributions to the angular momentum per unit vortex length and verify how angular momentum conservation is satisfied.

## Key numbers
- For the Type-II superconductor vortex with winding number ν=1, λ=1.5, and m²/m_V²=1, the background charge density required to set a(∞)=0 is ṯilq ≃ -0.985.
- The total kinetic angular momentum per unit length for the Type-II vortex is L_z^total = -ν (2πℏ) (μ/g) ∫ dr r h(r) ṯilq(r), proportional to the background charge density.
- For the topological insulator surface, the Chern-Simons level is ν=1/2, giving charge density Q = (e²/4πℏ) B_z and Hall currents j_x = (e²/4πℏ) E_y, j_y = -(e²/4πℏ) E_x.
- The canonical angular momentum of a superfluid vortex is L_z^can,matter = νℏ N, where N is the total particle number per unit length.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of vortex angular momentum beyond superfluid vortices (which carry quantized ℏ per particle) to magnetic vortices in gauge theories, addressing a gap in the literature. It corrects an erroneous statement in Appendix C of Julia and Zee (1975) regarding charged vortex angular momentum. The classification scheme unifies examples from condensed matter (Type-II superconductors, topological insulators), nuclear physics (CFL vortices in dense quark matter relevant to neutron star cores), and cosmology (Nielsen-Olesen vortices). The analysis of topological insulator vortices builds on the setup of PhysRevLett.121.227001, while the discussion of fractional angular momentum connects to the general framework of Seiberg (2016), Seiberg (2016), and Senthil (2018) for topological phases.
