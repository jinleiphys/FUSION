# Hyperon effects on the properties of $β$-stable neutron star matter

> We present results from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations for $β$-stable neutron star matter with nucleonic and hyperonic degrees of freedom employing the most recent parametrizations of the baryon-baryon interaction of the Nijmegen group. Only $Σ^-$ and $Λ$ are present up to densities $\sim 7ρ_0$. The corresponding equations of state are then used to compute properties of neutron stars such as masses and radii.

## Key claim
The presence of hyperons (specifically Σ⁻ and Λ) softens the equation of state of β-stable neutron star matter, but the degree of softening depends on whether the hyperon-hyperon interaction is included; the hyperon-hyperon interaction is attractive over the whole density range and produces an additional softening, leading to maximum neutron star masses that are barely compatible with observations of around 1.4 M⊙.

## Method
The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for β-stable neutron star matter using the most recent parametrization of the bare baryon-baryon potential for the complete baryon octet from the Nijmegen group (Stoks and Rijken, 1999). They solved the Bethe-Goldstone integral equation for the G-matrix and the single-particle potentials self-consistently, including nucleon-nucleon, hyperon-nucleon, and hyperon-hyperon interactions. The pure nucleonic part of the equation of state was replaced by the variational calculation of Akmal et al. (1998) using the Argonne V18 interaction with three-body forces and relativistic boost corrections.

## Key numbers
- Only Σ⁻ and Λ hyperons are present up to densities ∼7ρ₀, where ρ₀ ∼ 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density.
- For β-stable pure nucleonic matter, the maximum neutron star mass is 1.89 M⊙ without rotational corrections and 2.11 M⊙ with rotational corrections.
- With hyperons including hyperon-nucleon but excluding hyperon-hyperon interaction, the maximum mass is 1.47 M⊙ without rotational corrections and 1.60 M⊙ with rotational corrections.
- With hyperons including both hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions, the maximum mass is 1.34 M⊙ without rotational corrections and 1.44 M⊙ with rotational corrections.
- The composition was studied up to a density of ρ = 1.2 fm⁻³.

## Context
This work extends earlier many-body calculations by Schulze et al. (1998) that used realistic hyperon-nucleon interactions but did not consider the hyperon-hyperon interaction, which the authors show is essential once the first hyperon appears. It also contrasts with relativistic mean field models (Prakash, 1997) and parametrized effective interactions (Baldo, 1997) used by other authors. The results enable future work on whether three-body forces between nucleons and hyperons (Yamamoto, 2000) and/or relativistic effects are needed to stiffen the equation of state enough to match observed neutron star masses around 1.4 M⊙.
