# Spectroscopic calculations of the low-lying structure in exotic Os and W isotopes

> Structural evolution in neutron-rich Os and W isotopes is investigated in terms of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) Hamiltonian determined by (constrained) Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny-D1S Energy Density Functional (EDF). The interaction strengths of the IBM Hamiltonian are produced by mapping the potential energy surface (PES) of the Gogny-EDF with quadrupole degrees of freedom onto the corresponding PES of the IBM system. We examine the prolate-to-oblate shape/phase transition which is predicted to take place in this region as a function of neutron number $N$ within the considered Os and W isotopic chains. The onset of this transition is found to be more rapid compared to the neighboring Pt isotopes. The calculations also allow prediction of spectroscopic variables (excited state energies and reduced transition probabilities) which are presented for the neutron-rich $^{192,194,196}$W nuclei, for which there is only very limited experimental data available to date.

## Key claim

The prolate-to-oblate shape/phase transition in neutron-rich Os and W isotopes is predicted to occur more rapidly as a function of neutron number than in the neighboring Pt isotopes, with the N=116 isotones (192Os and 190W) exhibiting the greatest gamma-softness; however, the mapped IBM PES does not produce a triaxial minimum even where the underlying HFB PES shows shallow triaxiality.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1S Energy Density Functional (EDF) to generate potential energy surfaces (PES) in the quadrupole deformation parameters (beta, gamma) for Os and W isotopes. They then mapped these fermionic PES onto the PES of an Interacting Boson Model-2 (IBM-2) Hamiltonian using a Wavelet analysis method, fixing the IBM parameters (epsilon, kappa, chi_pi, chi_nu, and a scale factor C_beta) for each nucleus. The resulting IBM Hamiltonian was diagonalized with the NPBOS code to compute excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The calculated 2_1+ energy for 192Os is 0.280 MeV and for 190W is 0.286 MeV; for 194Os it is 0.278 MeV and for 192W it is 0.274 MeV.
- The experimental 2_1+ energies in the N=116 isotones 190W and 192Os are approximately 206 keV; in the N=118 isotones 192W and 194Os they are approximately 218 keV.
- The mapped IBM PES for 190W shows a global energy minimum at a quadrupole deformation of beta ~ 0.15 on the oblate side.
- The ratio R1 = B(E2;4_1+ -> 2_1+)/B(E2;2_1+ -> 0_1+) remains close to the O(6) and SU(3) limit value of 10/7 across the isotopic chains.
- The ratio R2 = B(E2;2_2+ -> 2_1+)/B(E2;2_1+ -> 0_1+) is close to the O(6) limit of 10/7 for N=114-118 in both Os and W, but decreases for N >= 118.

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to Pt isotopes by Nomura et al. (2010) using the Gogny-D1S EDF, and builds on mean-field calculations by Stevenson et al. and others that predicted ground-state shapes in this mass region. The results enable predictions of spectroscopic observables for neutron-rich 192,194,196W nuclei where experimental data are very limited, and provide a microscopic foundation for the phenomenological IBM studies of W isotopes by Duval and Barrett. The paper also compares with experimental data from Shi et al. (2006), Alkhomashi et al. (2009), and Lane et al. for the tungsten chain.
