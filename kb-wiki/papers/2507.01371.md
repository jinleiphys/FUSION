# Self-bound hybrid stars with strong phase transitions can relieve major compact star observation tensions

> Some recent pulsar observations cannot naturally fit into the conventional picture of neutron stars: the compact objects associated with HESS J1731-347 and XTE J1814-338 have too small radii in the low-mass regime, while the secondary component of GW190814 is too massive for neutron stars to be compatible with constraints from the GW170817 event. In this study, we demonstrate that all these anomalous observations and tensions, together with other conventional ones such as recent NICER observations of PSR J0740+6620, J0030+0451, and PSR J0437-4715, can be naturally explained simultaneously by a new general type of self-bound hybrid stars with large density discontinuities, and thus are radially stable in either the slow or rapid phase transition context. As a proof of concept, we use hybrid quark stars, inverted hybrid stars, and hybrid strangeon stars as benchmark examples to explicitly demonstrate the advantage and feasibility of self-bound hybrid stars with strong phase transitions in relieving all tensions related to compact stars' masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities.

# Self-bound hybrid stars with strong phase transitions can relieve major compact star observation tensions

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a new class of self-bound hybrid stars, featuring large density discontinuities and slow phase transitions, can simultaneously explain multiple anomalous compact object observations (HESS J1731-347, XTE J1814-338, GW190814) alongside conventional constraints from NICER and GW170817, but only if the phase transition timescale exceeds the oscillation period (slow transition) and the energy density jump is sufficiently large.

## Method
The authors construct three benchmark self-bound hybrid star models using the Maxwell construction for phase transitions: hybrid quark stars (MIT bag model crust with constant-sound-speed core), inverted hybrid stars (MIT bag model crust with polytropic hadronic core), and hybrid strangeon stars (strangeon matter crust with constant-sound-speed core). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for stellar structure, compute radial oscillation eigenfrequencies to determine stability under both slow and rapid phase transition scenarios, and calculate tidal deformabilities for comparison with gravitational wave constraints.

## Key numbers
- HESS J1731-347: mass $M=0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17}\, M_\odot$, radius $R=10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}$ km at $1\sigma$
- XTE J1814-338: mass $M=1.21^{+0.05}_{-0.05}\, M_\odot$, radius $R=7.0^{+0.4}_{-0.4}$ km at $1\sigma$
- GW190814 secondary companion: mass $2.59^{+0.08}_{-0.09}\, M_\odot$
- GW170817 tidal deformability constraint: $\Lambda_{1.4 M_\odot}<800$
- For three-flavor strange quark matter, absolute stability requires bag constant $B\lesssim(162.8\ \text{MeV})^4\approx 91\ \text{MeV/fm}^3$
- For two-flavor up-down quark matter, absolute stability requires $B\lesssim(144.38\ \text{MeV})^4\approx 56.8\ \text{MeV/fm}^3$
- Transition pressures used: $P_{\rm trans}=125$ and $255\ \text{MeV/fm}^3$ for hybrid quark stars; $P_{\rm trans}=55$ and $135\ \text{MeV/fm}^3$ for inverted hybrid stars; $P_{\rm trans}=68$ and $90\ \text{MeV/fm}^3$ for hybrid strangeon stars

## Context
This work extends the concept of slow stable hybrid stars (Pereira 2017, Lugones 2021) to self-bound configurations, combining advantages of quark stars (which naturally explain HESS J1731-347 as shown by Doroshenko 2022) with slow stable branches (which help explain XTE J1814-338 as explored by Laskos-Patkos 2024). The authors claim no previous study has simultaneously accommodated all these anomalous observations. The results enable future tests through improved tidal deformability lower bounds from kilonova analyses and more precise mass-radius measurements.
