# Contribution of $π^0$ Exchange in Elastic Muon-Proton Scattering

> The effect of the lepton's mass is significantly enhanced when the beam's energy is on the order of the lepton's mass. In the case of electrons, this corresponds to beam momenta on the order of a few MeV and is negligible in higher energy experiments. In this study, we calculate the differential cross section $dσ/ dΩ$ for the helicity-flip meson exchange interference in elastic muon-proton $(μp)$ scattering $μ^- p \rightarrow μ^- p$. In particular, we examine the $π^0$ meson exchange in the $t$-channel for a longitudinally polarized beam and a transversely polarized target. We demonstrate the contribution to be larger for muons due to the lepton mass difference. Then we construct the corresponding beam-target double-spin asymmetries for the target polarized normal and parallel to the momentum transfer in the Breit frame, and then consider the model dependence of the calculation from the estimation of the $π^0 μμ$ vertex. The contribution was found to be on the order of $\sim .15\%$ for muons in the kinematic region of the MUSE experiment.

# Contribution of π⁰ Exchange in Elastic Muon-Proton Scattering

## Key claim

The interference between single-photon exchange and single-π⁰ meson exchange in elastic muon-proton scattering produces a correction to the beam-target double-spin asymmetry on the order of ~0.15% for muons in the kinematic region of the MUSE experiment, but only when the lepton mass is retained and both beam and target are polarized; the contribution vanishes for unpolarized or massless leptons.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section for elastic μ⁻p → μ⁻p including the t-channel π⁰ exchange amplitude interfering with the single-photon exchange Born amplitude, retaining the full lepton mass dependence. They constructed the leptonic and hadronic tensors for longitudinally polarized muon beams and transversely polarized proton targets in the Breit frame, then converted to the lab frame to compute beam-target double-spin asymmetries for target polarization normal (x-direction) and parallel (z-direction) to the momentum transfer. The π⁰μμ vertex coupling f_p was estimated from the π⁰ → e⁺e⁻ decay width assuming lepton universality, and its Q² dependence was modeled with a monopole form factor using a range of cutoff parameters Λ² = 0.4–1.6 GeV²/c² based on the pion scalar radius.

## Key numbers

- The π⁰-exchange correction to the unpolarized cross section is on the order of ~0.15% for muons in the MUSE kinematic region (beam momenta 115, 153, and 210 MeV/c, Q² range 0.0016–0.0799 GeV², scattering angle range 20°–100°).
- The effective π⁰μμ coupling constant extracted from the π⁰ → e⁺e⁻ decay width is f_p = (3.08 ± 0.08) × 10⁻⁷.
- The correction in the z-direction asymmetry is ~10² times smaller than in the x-direction asymmetry.
- As Q² → 0, the ratio δA_x/δA_z approaches 2μ² (where μ is the muon magnetic moment).
- The monopole form factor cutoff was varied over Λ² ∈ [0.4, 1.6] GeV²/c², corresponding to a scalar radius range of 0.5–1.0 fm, producing variations in the correction at the level of 10⁻⁴.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of hadronic corrections to lepton-proton scattering beyond the Born approximation, specifically the scalar σ-meson exchange contribution to unpolarized μp scattering by Koshchii (2016) and the two-photon exchange (TPE) studies by Tomalak (2014, 2018). The calculation is motivated by the proton radius puzzle, where muonic hydrogen measurements by Pohl (2010) and Antognini (2013) gave proton charge radii of 0.84184(67) fm and 0.84087(39) fm respectively, discrepant with earlier ep-scattering results. The results are directly applicable to the MUSE experiment at Paul-Scherrer Institute, which will measure elastic μ±p and e±p scattering simultaneously at beam momenta of 115, 153, and 210 MeV/c. The π⁰-exchange contribution, previously omitted from published TPE calculations, should be included as an essential hadronic correction for sub-percent precision measurements of the proton charge radius and TPE effects.
