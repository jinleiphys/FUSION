# Influence of the Pauli exclusion principle on the alpha decay

> In this study, the effects of repulsive nucleon-nucleon interactions arising from the Pauli exclusion principle were examined regarding the half-lives of heavy even-even nuclei with $84\leq Z\leq92$. The Pauli exclusion principle is applied to our investigations by renormalizing the nucleon-nucleon interactions according to the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition. It is also applied to the double-folding ($DF$) formalism as a modification term by investigating the kinetic energy variation at the overlapping regions between the densities of the alpha and daughter nuclei. The standard deviation for the calculated half-lives from their corresponded experimental data is 0.340 for the renormalized DF formalism. Its value reduces to 0.309 for the DF formalism modified with the kinetic energy contribution estimated by the extended Thomas-Fermi approach. The calculated $α$ decay half-lives are more consistent with experimental data if the kinetic energy contribution is associated with the DF formalism.

## Key claim
The Pauli exclusion principle (PEP) introduces a repulsive effect in alpha decay that can be incorporated into the double-folding (DF) formalism either by renormalizing nucleon-nucleon interactions via the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition or by adding a kinetic energy correction estimated through the extended Thomas-Fermi approach; the kinetic energy correction yields better agreement with experimental half-lives than the renormalization method.

## Method
The authors calculated alpha decay half-lives for even-even nuclei with atomic numbers 84 to 92 using the double-folding model with M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interactions. They incorporated the Pauli exclusion principle in two ways: (1) by renormalizing the strength of the folded potential using the Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization condition with the Wildermuth condition for the global quantum number, and (2) by estimating the kinetic energy increase from density overlap using the extended Thomas-Fermi approach with Skyrme SLy4 parametrization and adding this term to the DF potential. Half-lives were computed using both the Gurvitz-Kalbermann method and the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation, with and without alpha preformation factors from the Cluster Formation Model (CFM).

## Key numbers
- Standard deviation (SD) for half-lives from standard M3Y potentials (WKB, P_alpha=1): 1.245
- SD for half-lives from M3Y potentials with kinetic energy modification (WKB, P_alpha=1): 0.787
- SD for half-lives from renormalized potentials via Bohr-Sommerfeld condition (Gurvitz method, P_alpha=1): 0.994
- SD for half-lives from M3Y potentials with CFM preformation factors: 0.561
- SD for half-lives from modified M3Y potentials with kinetic energy and CFM preformation factors: 0.309
- SD for half-lives from renormalized potentials with CFM preformation factors: 0.340
- SD for half-lives from simulated repulsive potential (delta-function) with CFM preformation factors: 0.298
- SD for Viola-Seaborg-Sobiczewski (VSS) formula: 0.704
- SD for Royer formula (RF): 0.385
- SD for Universal Decay Law (UDL): 0.484

## Context
This work extends previous studies on incorporating Pauli blocking into the double-folding model, which had been applied primarily to fusion reactions (e.g., for systems like alpha + Pb-208, alpha + Au-197, and C-12 + C-12) but not systematically to alpha decay. The authors compare their approach to the standard renormalization method using the Bohr-Sommerfeld condition (Wildermuth condition) and to phenomenological formulas such as the Viola-Seaborg-Sobiczewski relationship, the Royer formula, and the Universal Decay Law of Qi et al. The results enable a more accurate description of alpha decay half-lives for heavy nuclei and provide a simple simulated repulsive potential that can replace the more computationally intensive Hartree-Fock and extended Thomas-Fermi calculations.
