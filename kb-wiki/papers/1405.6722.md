# Dissecting Soft Radiation with Factorization

> An essential part of high-energy hadronic collisions is the soft hadronic activity that underlies the primary hard interaction. It includes soft radiation from the primary hard partons, secondary multiple parton interactions (MPI), and factorization-violating effects. The invariant mass spectrum of the leading jet in $Z$+jet and $H$+jet events is directly sensitive to these effects, and we use a QCD factorization theorem to predict its dependence on the jet radius $R$, jet $p_T$, jet rapidity, and partonic process for both the perturbative and nonperturbative components of primary soft radiation. We prove that the nonperturbative contributions involve only odd powers of $R$, and the linear $R$ term is universal for quark and gluon jets. The hadronization model in PYTHIA8 agrees well with these properties. The perturbative soft initial state radiation (ISR) has a contribution that depends on the jet area in the same way as the underlying event, but this degeneracy is broken by dependence on the jet $p_T$. The size of this soft ISR contribution is proportional to the color state of the initial partons, yielding the same positive contribution for $gg\to Hg$ and $gq\to Zq$, but a negative interference contribution for $q\bar q\to Z g$. Hence, measuring these dependencies allows one to separate hadronization, soft ISR, and MPI contributions in the data.

## Key claim

The paper uses a QCD factorization theorem to predict that the nonperturbative contributions to the jet mass spectrum in Z+jet and H+jet events involve only odd powers of the jet radius R, with the linear R term being universal for quark and gluon jets, and that the perturbative soft initial state radiation (ISR) contribution scales like R^4, which can be distinguished from multiple parton interactions (MPI) by its dependence on jet p_T and partonic channel.

## Method

The authors derived a factorization theorem for the jet mass spectrum in exclusive pp → Z+1-jet and pp → H+1-jet events, separating the cross section into hard, collinear, jet, and soft functions. They computed the first moment of the jet mass distribution analytically at NLL and NNLL order, and extracted nonperturbative parameters by fitting to parton shower Monte Carlo results from PYTHIA8 and Herwig++. The R-dependence of the nonperturbative shift was obtained by expanding the operator definition of the soft function for small R, and the color structure of soft ISR was computed from the interference of initial-state Wilson lines.

## Key numbers

- For quark jets, the extracted linear nonperturbative parameter is Ω_q^(1) = 1.2 GeV; for gluon jets, Ω_g^(1) = 2.2 GeV (from PYTHIA8 with ATLAS AU2 tune at √s = 7 TeV)
- The color factor for soft ISR in qg → Zq and gg → Hg is C_A/2 = 3/2; for qq̅ → Zg it is C_F − C_A/2 = −1/6
- The nonperturbative shift Ω = 2.4 GeV for qg → Zq and Ω = 2.7 GeV for qq̅ → Zg reproduces the hadronization+MPI tail in PYTHIA8
- The jet veto threshold for additional jets is p_T^cut > 50 GeV at ECM = 7 TeV

## Context

This work extends the factorization approach for jet mass developed by Stewart, Tackmann, and collaborators (2010-2013) to systematically separate perturbative soft radiation, nonperturbative hadronization, and MPI contributions. It contradicts the common assumption that MPI and soft ISR are indistinguishable in jet observables, showing they can be separated by their p_T^J dependence. The results enable experimental measurements at the LHC to disentangle these three sources of soft hadronic activity by binning in jet p_T and partonic channel, building on earlier UE measurements by ATLAS (2010-2011) and CMS (2011-2012) that used away-from-jet regions.
