# Does the Multiple-scattering Series in the Pion-deuteron Scattering Actually Converge?

> It is demonstrated that the well-known answer for the multiple-scattering series (MSS) for a light particle interacting to a pair of static nucleons, calculated in the Fixed Centers Approximation (FCA), works well for a wide region of the two-body complex scattering length $a$. However, this approach is not applicable in a narrow region surrounding the real positive $a$ half-axis, where the MSS does not converge. Simultaneously, for real positive $a$'s the 3-body system forms an infinite set of bound states.

## Key claim

The well-known Fixed Centers Approximation (FCA) expression for the multiple-scattering series (MSS) in pion-deuteron scattering works for a wide region of the complex two-body scattering length \(a\), but fails in a narrow region surrounding the real positive \(a\) half-axis where the MSS does not converge and the 3-body system forms an infinite set of bound states.

## Method

The authors analyze the convergence of the MSS for a light particle scattering from two static nucleons by comparing two representations: the standard coordinate-space FCA amplitude and a momentum-space formulation where the MSS is summed via an integral equation for the function \(R(p,p')\). They introduce a finite-range formfactor \(g(p)=M/\sqrt{M^2+p^2}\) for the elementary \(\pi N\) amplitude and solve the resulting integral equation numerically on a discretized grid of size \(N\), studying the dependence of the amplitude on the cutoff \(\Lambda\) and the formfactor mass \(M\).

## Key numbers

- The first critical grid size \(N_{crit}^{(1)}=205\) for parameters \(a=10^{-3}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\), \(p_0=10\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(L=2.5\).
- The second critical grid size \(N_{crit}^{(2)}=525\) for the same parameters.
- The critical formfactor mass \(M_0\) is determined by \(M_0 a = 1 + \varkappa/M_0\); for \(a=0.005\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\), \(M_0=222\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The distance between adjacent critical points \(N_{crit}^{(i+1)}-N_{crit}^{(i)}\) is independent of \(i\) for fixed \(a\).
- The amplitude \(^m\! F_M^{(2)}(a)\) is calculated with cutoff \(\Lambda=2500\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for various \(M\) values including 150, 220, 250, 300, and 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Baru et al., 2012) which showed that individual MSS terms diverge for negative scattering length but the divergences cancel upon resummation. The present paper addresses the positive scattering length case, finding that the FCA amplitude becomes ill-defined due to an infinite set of bound states, analogous to the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970) for three identical bosons and to the "falling to the center" phenomenon in singular attractive potentials. The results clarify the domain of applicability of the FCA for systems like \(K^-d\) scattering (Kamalov; Oset) where the imaginary part of the scattering length is comparable to the real part, enabling reliable use of the simple coordinate-space formula.
