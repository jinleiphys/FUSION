# Statistical Model Description of $K^+$ and $K^-$ Production between 1 - 10 AGeV

> The excitation functions of $K^+$ and $K^-$ mesons in heavy ion collisions are studied within a statistical model assuming chemical and thermal equilibrium with exact strangeness conservation. At low incident energies the associate production of kaons, i.e. the production of a K+ together with a hyperon and the production of a K- together with a K+, implies specific features: different threshold energies and different dependences of K+ and K- yields on baryon number density. It is shown that the experimentally observed equality of the $K^+$ and $K^-$ rates at energies $\sqrt{s} - \sqrt{s_{th}} \leq 0$ is due to a crossing of the two excitation functions. Furthermore, the independence of the $K^+$ to $K^-$ ratio on the number of participating nucleons observed at 1 and 10 $A\cdot$GeV is consistent with this model.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed equality of \(K^+\) and \(K^-\) yields at energies \(\sqrt{s} - \sqrt{s_{th}} \leq 0\) arises from a crossing of the two excitation functions, which have different slopes due to the associate production mechanisms required by exact strangeness conservation; this crossing is a consequence of the statistical model assuming chemical and thermal equilibrium, not a sign of in-medium mass modifications.

## Method

The authors applied a statistical model with exact strangeness conservation in the canonical ensemble, using freeze-out parameters (temperature \(T\) and baryo-chemical potential \(\mu_B\)) derived from a universal freeze-out curve and measured pion multiplicities. They calculated \(K^+\) and \(K^-\) multiplicities per participant nucleon as functions of \(\sqrt{s} - \sqrt{s_{th}}\) and of \(A_{part}\), using simplified analytic expressions that show the \(K^+\) yield depends on \(\mu_B\) via the associated \(\Lambda\) hyperon while the \(K^-\) yield depends on the \(K^+\) energy. The model results were compared to data from the KaoS Collaboration, FRS Group, and E866/E917 Collaboration at AGS.

## Key numbers

- The \(NN\) threshold energy for \(K^+\) production is \(\sqrt{s_{th}(K^+)} - 2m_N = 0.67\) GeV; for \(K^-\) production it is \(\sqrt{s_{th}(K^-)} - 2m_N = 0.987\) GeV.
- At AGS energies (10.8 \(A\cdot\)GeV Au+Au), the \(K^+\) and \(K^-\) multiplicities differ by a factor of five, in agreement with the model.
- The \(K^+/K^-\) ratio is independent of the number of participating nucleons \(A_{part}\) at 10.2 \(A\cdot\)GeV (Au+Au) and at 1.93 \(A\cdot\)GeV (Ni+Ni).
- At low incident energies around 1 \(A\cdot\)GeV, the \(K^+\) and \(K^-\) multiplicities scale quadratically with \(A_{part}\), while pion multiplicities scale linearly.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model with exact strangeness conservation previously developed by Cleymans et al. (1999) to describe kaon excitation functions from SIS to AGS energies. It directly addresses the puzzle raised by KaoS Collaboration data (Barth, Laue) that the \(K^+/K^-\) ratio in heavy ion collisions differs strongly from that in \(NN\) reactions, which had been interpreted as evidence for in-medium mass changes of kaons (Kaplan). The model successfully reproduces the observed independence of the \(K^+/K^-\) ratio on centrality and the crossing of excitation functions, but the authors note that transport models (Hartnack) predict a different \(A_{part}\) dependence (\(\alpha \approx 1.4-1.6\)) and that the assumption of chemical equilibrium for strange particles remains controversial (Elena, Bravina).
