# The Rarita-Schwinger spin-3/2 equation in a nonuniform, central potential

> The equations of motion for a massive spin-3/2 Rarita-Schwinger field in a finite-range, central, Lorentz scalar potential are developed. It is shown that the resulting density may not be everywhere positive definite.

# Rarita-Schwinger spin-3/2 equation in a nonuniform, central potential

## Key claim

For a massive spin-3/2 Rarita-Schwinger field in a finite-range, central, Lorentz scalar potential, the probability density derived from the classical field equations may not be everywhere positive definite, even for physically plausible potentials corresponding to the Δ resonance in a nuclear environment. The authors show that the wave functions typically cause the probability density to change sign as a function of radius, indicating either that the interpretation of ρ(r) as a probability density is invalid or that the Rarita-Schwinger formalism with a scalar potential is intrinsically unphysical.

## Method

The authors introduce a Lorentz scalar potential into the Rarita-Schwinger formalism by replacing the constant mass m₀ with a spherically symmetric function m(r) = m₀ − U(r), following the approach used for the Dirac equation. They derive the full set of eight coupled equations (Eqs. 11, 14–16) in eight unknown radial functions that constitute the Rarita-Schwinger equations in a finite, spherically symmetric system, then reduce these to four linear, first-order differential equations. Explicit numerical solutions are obtained for j = 1/2 (ℓ = 0,1) and j = 3/2 (ℓ = 1,2) using standard numerical techniques, and the probability density is evaluated from the conserved current J^μ corresponding to the Rarita-Schwinger Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- Particle mass of 1230 MeV used in numerical solutions, corresponding to the Δ resonance
- Potentials of nuclear range (a few fm) and depth (a few hundred MeV) were examined
- For j = 1/2, only the ℒ = 0 term in the density expansion is allowed; for j = 3/2, only this term contributes at cosθ = ±1/√3
- The probability density ρ(r) = (1/4πr²){(2/3)G₀²(r) − G₁²(r) − G₂²(r) + (2/3)F₀²(r) − F₁²(r) − F₂²(r)} + higher-ℒ terms was found to change sign as r varies

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts by Jasiak and Szymacha (1988) to use the Rarita-Schwinger equations for spin-3/2 particles in bag model studies of the Δ resonance, but addresses the question of whether the calculated wave functions are physically meaningful. The authors were motivated by relativistic QHD models of Walecka and collaborators (Serot and Walecka, 1986; Horowitz and Serot, 1984), which treat nucleons via the Dirac equation with mean-field scalar and vector potentials. The paper shows that the problems known to plague quantized spin-3/2 fields, such as noncausal propagation modes found by Velo and Zwanziger (1969) and the non-positive-definite canonical anticommutator found by Johnson and Sudarshan (1961), also appear in the classical field equations, suggesting fundamental difficulties for treating the Δ as an explicit degree of freedom via the Rarita-Schwinger equation rather than dynamically as a π-N resonance as done by Lin and Serot (1991).
