# Chiral Unitary Approach to the Kaon Nucleus Interaction and Kaonic Atoms

> We review recent work on various topics related to the modification of kaon properties in nuclei. After a brief exposition of the kaon-nucleon and kaon-nucleus interaction, results from the application to kaonic atoms, renormalization of the f0 and a0 scalar resonances in nuclei and phi decay in the nucleus are shown.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach, combined with standard many-body techniques, yields a kaon-nucleus optical potential that reproduces kaonic atom data to within 20-30% accuracy, but the atomic data alone cannot uniquely determine the potential's strength because deeper potentials can produce equivalent atomic shifts by introducing additional deeply bound nuclear states.

## Method

The authors compute the antikaon selfenergy in nuclear matter by first solving the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for the $\bar{K}N$ interaction using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, then including medium modifications (Pauli blocking, selfconsistent kaon selfenergy, pion selfenergy from ph and $\Delta h$ excitations, and baryon mean-field potentials) and integrating over the Fermi sea of nucleons. This selfenergy is used in the Klein-Gordon equation to obtain both atomic and deeply bound kaonic states, and also employed in the chiral unitary coupled-channel approach to study the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances and the $\phi$ meson decay in nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Attractive potential for antikaons near nuclear matter density and close to threshold: about 40 MeV (real part), with a width of about 100 MeV.
- The theoretical kaon-nucleus potential reproduces kaonic atom data at the level of 20-30% accuracy as an average.
- The $f_0(980)$ resonance width in free space is about 40 MeV; at nuclear matter density $\rho_0$ it increases to more than 150 MeV.
- The $a_0(980)$ resonance is washed away at densities around $\rho_0$.
- Total width of the $\phi$ meson at $\rho=\rho_0$: about 22 MeV, nearly an order of magnitude larger than the free width.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to meson-meson interactions by Oset et al. (1997) and to the $\bar{K}N$ interaction by Oset et al. (1998). It builds on earlier studies of kaon selfenergies by Koch (1994), Waas et al. (1996-1997), and Lutz (who introduced selfconsistency), while adding new decay channels for antikaons in nuclei. The results enable future investigations of kaon condensates in neutron stars and establish a framework for "Chiral Nuclear Physics" that can be applied to other nuclear problems.
