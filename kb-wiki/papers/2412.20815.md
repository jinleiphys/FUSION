# Constraining the $f$-mode oscillations frequency in Neutron Stars through Universal Relations in the realm of Energy-Momentum Squared Gravity

> Neutron stars (NSs), superdense objects with exceptionally strong gravitational fields, provide an ideal laboratory for probing general relativity (GR) in the high-curvature regime. They also present an exciting opportunity to explore new gravitational physics beyond the traditional framework of GR. Thus, investigating alternative theories of gravity in the context of superdense stars is intriguing and essential for advancing our understanding of gravitational phenomena in extreme environments. Energy-Momentum Squared Gravity (EMSG) is a modified theory of gravity that extends GR by including nonlinear terms involving the energy-momentum tensor $T_{μν}$. This study examines the effects of EMSG on the properties and behaviour of NSs by varying the free parameter $α$. The hydrostatic equilibrium equations in the EMSG framework are derived and solved numerically to obtain mass-radius relations for soft, stiff, and intermediate equations of state (EOS). Observational measurements of NS masses and radii are used to constrain the fundamental-mode ($f$-mode) oscillation frequency through its universal relation with the tidal Love number and compactness. Results indicate that the Stiff EOS undergoes a phase transition at the highest energy densities and pressures, followed by the Intermediate and Soft EOSs, highlighting the distinctive characteristics of these models. Additionally, the study explores the impact of EOS choice on the sound speed profile of NSs, reaffirming the physical validity of the models across varying $α$ values.

## Key claim

The study constrains the fundamental-mode (f-mode) oscillation frequency of neutron stars within Energy-Momentum Squared Gravity (EMSG) by using universal relations that connect the f-mode frequency to the tidal Love number and compactness, finding that the Stiff equation of state undergoes a phase transition at the highest energy densities and pressures, followed by the Intermediate and Soft equations of state.

## Method

The author derived the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations within the EMSG framework and solved them numerically for three equations of state (Stiff, Intermediate, and Soft) while varying the free parameter alpha. The non-radial oscillations were studied using the Cowling approximation, which neglects metric perturbations and treats the oscillations as purely harmonic with real eigenvalues. The resulting mass-radius relations and f-mode frequencies were then connected through universal relations between compactness and f-mode frequency (C-f) and between tidal Love number and f-mode frequency (f-Love), using observational constraints from neutron star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- The EMSG free parameter alpha is constrained to lie within the range -10^-38 to 10^-37 cm^3/erg from the 2 solar mass maximum neutron star constraint, while binary pulsar observations give a compatible range of -6 x 10^-38 to 10^-36 cm^3/erg
- Solar System tests yield significantly smaller constraints on alpha of -4 x 10^-27 to 10^-26 cm^3/erg
- The f-mode frequency for quadrupolar modes (l=2) was computed, with the frequency decreasing for positive alpha values and increasing for negative alpha values until the maximum stable mass is reached
- The study used the tidal deformability limit from the GW170817 event to constrain the canonical f-mode frequency for different alpha values

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron stars in EMSG by Ghosh and collaborators (2023) and Akarsu and colleagues (2018), who derived the modified field equations and constrained the alpha parameter using maximum mass and binary pulsar observations. The study also builds on universal relation frameworks developed by Yagi and Yunes (2013) and Mohanty and colleagues (2024) for isotropic and anisotropic neutron stars, applying them for the first time to the EMSG framework. The results enable future gravitational wave observations to distinguish between GR and EMSG through f-mode frequency measurements, and the universal relations provide a model-independent way to infer neutron star properties from observable quantities.
