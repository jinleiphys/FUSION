# Complete set of electromagnetic corrections to the nucleon mass in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We show how to derive the complete set of electromagnetic corrections to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of the nucleon. Our results enable an accurate estimate of the electromagnetic contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference within this model. At the same time, our procedure demonstrates the way to calculate the complete set of meson corrections to the NJL model that maintains chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper derives the complete set of electromagnetic corrections of order \(e^2\) to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of the nucleon, enabling an accurate estimate of the neutron-proton mass difference within this model, but only if the strong interaction Green function is described nonperturbatively by integral equations and the corrections are constructed via the gauging of equations method.

## Method

The authors apply a general formalism for electromagnetic corrections to the three-quark NJL model, where the nucleon is described by a three-quark Bethe-Salpeter-like equation that reduces to a quark-diquark two-body equation. They gauge the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the dressed quark propagator and the diquark propagator equation to obtain the electromagnetic currents \(d^\mu\) and \(D^\mu\), then construct the complete correction \(\Delta\) from the gauged quantities. The final expression for the mass shift \(\delta M = \bar\psi \Delta \psi / 2M\) is obtained by solving the integral equation for the electromagnetic corrections \(\delta G\) to the quark-diquark Green function.

## Key numbers

- The quark propagator solution is \(d(p)=i(\not p - m)^{-1}\), where \(m\) is a constituent quark mass that is not zero even if the bare quark mass \(m_0=0\).
- The diquark propagator is \(D(p)=4ig_s / [1 - 2ig_s \Pi(p^2)]\), with \(\Pi(p^2)\delta_{ij} = -\int d^4k/(2\pi)^4 \, \text{tr}[\gamma_5\tau_i d(p+k)\gamma_5\tau_j d(k)]\).
- The quark-diquark interaction kernel is \(K(p',p)=\gamma_5 d(p'+p)\gamma_5 \beta^a \beta^{a'}\), where \(\beta^a_{ik}=i\sqrt{3/2}\,\epsilon_{aik}\).

## Context

This work extends the general method for electromagnetic corrections developed by the same authors (Kvinikhidze and Blankleider, 1998) to the specific case of the NJL model of the nucleon. The procedure demonstrates how to calculate complete meson corrections that maintain chiral symmetry, in contrast to earlier work by Ishii (year not given) which included only a partial set of pionic corrections. The results enable future calculations of the neutron-proton mass difference within the NJL model and can be applied to constituent quark models with confinement, clarifying discussions by Glozman (year not given) and others.
