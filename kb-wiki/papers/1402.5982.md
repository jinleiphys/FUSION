# Criticality of the net-baryon number probability distribution at finite density

> We compute the probability distribution $P(N)$ of the net-baryon number at finite temperature and quark-chemical potential, $μ$, at a physical value of the pion mass in the quark-meson model within the functional renormalization group scheme. For $μ/T<1$, the model exhibits the chiral crossover transition which belongs to the universality class of the $O(4)$ spin system in three dimensions. We explore the influence of the chiral crossover transition on the properties of the net baryon number probability distribution, $P(N)$. By considering ratios of $P(N)$ to the Skellam function, with the same mean and variance, we unravel the characteristic features of the distribution that are related to $O(4)$ criticality at the chiral crossover transition. We explore the corresponding ratios for data obtained at RHIC by the STAR Collaboration and discuss their implications. We also examine $O(4)$ criticality in the context of binomial and negative-binomial distributions for the net proton number.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the ratio of the net-baryon number probability distribution to the Skellam function (with the same mean and variance) reveals a characteristic narrowing near the chiral crossover transition, which is a signature of O(4) criticality; this narrowing is present at both zero and finite chemical potential, but at finite chemical potential the distribution becomes asymmetric with suppression for positive net-baryon number and enhancement for negative net-baryon number.

## Method

The authors compute the net-baryon number probability distribution P(N) in the quark-meson model using the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach with the optimized regulator in the local potential approximation, at physical pion mass (135 MeV) and sigma mass (640 MeV). They extract P(N) via a Fourier projection of the grand partition function onto fixed net-baryon number, then construct the ratio of P(N) to the Skellam distribution that has the same mean and variance. They also compare this ratio to the efficiency-uncorrected net-proton probability distributions from the STAR Collaboration for central Au-Au collisions at several collision energies, and examine whether binomial or negative-binomial distributions can reproduce the O(4) critical behavior.

## Key numbers

- The quark-meson model parameters are fixed to reproduce the physical pion mass m_pi = 135 MeV, sigma mass m_sigma = 640 MeV, and constituent quark mass M_q(T=mu=0) = 300 MeV with Yukawa coupling g = 3.2 and pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV.
- The ultraviolet cutoff scale is set to k = Lambda = 1 GeV.
- At mu = 0, the ratio P^{FRG}(N)/P^S(N) drops below unity near the pseudocritical temperature T_pc for |delta N/N_6| less than about 1, indicating narrowing of the distribution.
- At mu = 50 MeV, the ratio shows narrowing for positive delta N and enhancement for negative delta N as T_pc is approached, with the narrowing beginning at smaller |delta N/N_6| than at mu = 0.
- The STAR data for central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV show a similar narrowing for positive delta N and early drop below unity, while data at higher energies (27, 39, 62.4, 200 GeV) show less pronounced effects.
- The negative binomial distribution gives chi_4/chi_2 > 1, while the binomial distribution gives chi_4/chi_2 < 1; neither reproduces the negative chi_6/chi_2 values near T_pc obtained from the quark-meson model.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Morita et al., 2012, 2013) of net-baryon probability distributions at zero chemical potential to finite chemical potential, and applies the method to STAR data (STAR Collaboration, 2013). The approach builds on the expectation from Friman et al. (2011) that deviations of higher-order cumulants from hadron resonance gas (HRG) results provide a signature for criticality at the phase boundary. The paper shows that binomial and negative-binomial distributions, which have been used to describe STAR data (Westfall; STAR Collaboration, 2013), cannot capture the O(4) critical behavior seen in the quark-meson model, particularly the negative sixth-order cumulant. The results suggest that high-statistics data on the net-proton probability distribution over a range allowing reliable determination of the sixth-order cumulant are needed for unambiguous identification of O(4) chiral criticality.
