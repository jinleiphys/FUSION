# Quantum Treatment of the Multiple Scattering and Collective Flow in Intensity Interferometry

> We apply the path-integral method to study the multiple scattering and collective flow in intensity interferometry in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We show that the Glauber model and eikonal approximation in an earlier quantum treatment are special examples of the more general path-integral method. The multiple scattering and collective flow lead essentially to an initial source at a shifted momentum, with a multiple collision absorption factor that depends on the pion absorption cross section and a phase factor that depends on the deviations of the in-medium particle momenta from their asymptotic values.

# Quantum Treatment of the Multiple Scattering and Collective Flow in Intensity Interferometry

## Key claim
The path-integral method provides a more general quantum framework for intensity interferometry in high-energy heavy-ion collisions than earlier treatments, with the Glauber model and eikonal approximation emerging as special cases. The multiple scattering and collective flow produce an effective source distribution close to the initial chemical freeze-out source, with only small absorption and phase shift effects, contrary to the conventional assumption that the source evolves significantly before thermal freeze-out.

## Method
The author applies the Feynman path-integral method to describe pion propagation from chemical freeze-out to thermal freeze-out, modeling the collective flow via a density-dependent long-range mean-field potential and multiple scattering via short-range π-π interactions. The two-pion correlation function is derived by evaluating path integrals over all possible trajectories, with the dominant contribution coming from the classical path that minimizes the mean-field action difference. The multiple scattering phase shift is shown to be a sum of individual collision phase shifts, analogous to the Glauber wave function in multiple scattering theory.

## Key numbers
- At phase transition temperature T ~ 180 MeV: average pion density ~ 0.3 pions/fm³, pion energy ~ 0.5 GeV, average π-π center-of-mass energy ⟨√s_ππ⟩ ~ 0.7 GeV
- ρ resonance width in free space: 150 MeV; in thermalized medium at T = 150 MeV: increases to ~ 300 MeV
- ρ meson mean lifetime in medium: ~ 0.67 fm/c (from ℏ/(300 MeV))
- ρ meson orbiting time: ~ 2.4 fm/c (from 2πr with 2r ~ 0.77 fm)
- π⁺ absorption cross section via π⁺π⁻ → π⁰π⁰: ~ 8 mb
- Mean absorption path length for π⁺ in thermalized pion medium at T = 180 MeV: ~ 12 fm

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier quantum treatment using Glauber multiple scattering theory and the optical model (Wong 2003, 2004) by providing a more general path-integral formulation. It addresses questions raised about the conventional assumption that the initial chaotic source evolves into a different source distribution at thermal freeze-out. The results are consistent with hydrodynamical calculations by Zhang (2004) and are supported qualitatively by Kapusta and Li (2004). The work notes that Cramer et al. (2005) considered produced pions in a static optical model potential but did not account for particle collisions and collective flow. The findings enable numerical calculations of hadron matter evolution to study HBT correlation effects, with predictions that R_out/R_side should be approximately close to 1 and that HBT transverse radii should be nearly independent of collision energy.
