# Efficient magnetic-field amplification due to the Kelvin-Helmholtz instability in binary neutron star mergers

> We explore magnetic-field amplification due to the Kelvin-Helmholtz instability during binary neutron star mergers. By performing high-resolution general relativistic magnetohydrodynamics simulations with a resolution of $17.5$ m for $4$--$5$ ms after the onset of the merger on the Japanese supercomputer "K", we find that an initial magnetic field of moderate maximum strength $10^{13}$ G is amplified at least by a factor of $\approx 10^3$. We also explore the saturation of the magnetic-field energy and our result shows that it is likely to be $\gtrsim 4 \times 10^{50}$ erg, which is $\gtrsim 0.1\%$ of the bulk kinetic energy of the merging binary neutron stars.

## Key claim

Using high-resolution general relativistic magnetohydrodynamics simulations with a resolution of 17.5 m, the authors find that an initial magnetic field of moderate maximum strength 10^13 G is amplified at least by a factor of approximately 10^3, and that the saturation energy of the magnetic field is likely to be greater than or equal to 4 x 10^50 erg, corresponding to greater than or equal to 0.1% of the bulk kinetic energy of the merging binary neutron stars. However, even at the highest resolution, equipartition is not achieved and the saturation is not fully reached, indicating that further amplification may be possible.

## Method

The authors performed general relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (GRMHD) simulations of equal-mass irrotational binary neutron star mergers with total mass 2.8 solar masses, using the H4 equation of state. A fixed mesh refinement algorithm was employed, with the finest grid resolution reaching 17.5 m by adding up to two additional refinement domains about 1 ms before merger. A seed magnetic field with maximum strength 10^13 G (model B13) was introduced at approximately 1 ms before merger, and additional simulations with initial strengths up to 10^15 G (models B14 and B15) were performed to study saturation.

## Key numbers

- Initial maximum magnetic-field strength set to 10^13 G, compatible with the upper observational limit from binary pulsars (surface dipole range 10^9.7 to 10^12.2 G)
- Finest grid resolution achieved: 17.5 m, a factor of 4 finer than previous simulations by Kiuchi (2014)
- Magnetic-field energy amplified by a factor of approximately 10^6 at t - t_mrg approximately 4 ms for the B13 run with 17.5 m resolution
- Saturation energy of the magnetic field likely to be greater than or equal to 4 x 10^50 erg (from B15 run with 17.5 m resolution)
- Averaged magnetic-field strength reaches approximately 10^16 G, with peak values up to approximately 10^17 G
- Kinetic energy density of turbulence-like flow: approximately 2.3 x 10^33 erg/cm^3; magnetic-field energy density: approximately 6.0 x 10^30 erg/cm^3 (at t - t_mrg = 4.4 ms for 17.5 m run)
- Shear layer thickness D approximately 130 m and flow velocity v_flow approximately 0.1c, giving KH growth timescale approximately 6 x 10^-3 ms

## Context

This work extends the previous high-resolution GRMHD simulations by Kiuchi (2014), which used magnetar-class initial fields of 10^14.5 to 10^16 G and achieved only a factor of 10 amplification. By using a more realistic initial field strength of 10^13 G and increasing resolution by a factor of 4, the authors demonstrate that the Kelvin-Helmholtz instability can amplify the magnetic field by several orders of magnitude, consistent with suggestions from local box simulations by Obergaulinger (2010) and Zrake (2013). The results enable more realistic modeling of magnetic field evolution in binary neutron star mergers, with implications for short-hard gamma-ray burst central engines and r-process nucleosynthesis, though longer simulations are needed to determine whether a large-scale dynamo becomes active in the saturated state.
