# Halo structure of $^6$He from $\textit{ab initio}$ two-nucleon spatial correlations

> We evaluate pairwise correlations using ground state wave functions for $^4$He and $^6$He obtained by $\textit{ab initio}$ no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations with the Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon interaction plus Coulomb interaction, to characterize the structures of these two systems. We demonstrate that two-nucleon spatial correlations, specifically the pair-number operator $r^0$ and the square-separation operator $r^2$ projected on two-body spin $S$ and isospin $z$-components encode important details of the halo structure of $^6$He. We also analyze the single-particle state occupancies and the two-body state occupancies for the ground state of $^4$He and $^6$He. Our results indicate that the two valence neutrons in the ground state of $^6$He dominantly form a spin-singlet configuration. The rms pair separations between core nucleons and halo neutrons of $^6$He is about 80% larger than pair separations within the swollen and off-centered "$α$ core". We show that this off-centering effect is primarily responsible for the observed increase in point-proton radius $r_p$ in $^6$He relative to $^4$He.

## Key claim

The two valence neutrons in the ground state of $^6$He dominantly form a spin-singlet configuration, and the off-centering of the swollen "alpha core" relative to the center of mass is primarily responsible for the observed increase in point-proton radius $r_p$ in $^6$He relative to $^4$He, rather than swelling of the core itself.

## Method

The authors performed _ab initio_ no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using the Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon interaction plus Coulomb interaction to obtain ground state wave functions for $^4$He and $^6$He. They constructed two-body spatial operators, the pair-number operator $r^0$ and the square-separation operator $r^2$, projected onto two-body spin $S$ and isospin $z$-components, and evaluated their expectation values using the NCSM wave functions at $N_\text{max}=16$ and $\hbar\Omega=10$ MeV. Single-particle and two-body state occupancies were also analyzed to interpret the correlation results.

## Key numbers

- The point-proton radius $r_p$ of $^6$He is 1.8891 fm, which is 25% larger than the $r_p$ of $^4$He (1.5145 fm), calculated at $N_\text{max}=16$ and $\hbar\Omega=10$ MeV.
- The point-neutron radius $r_n$ of $^6$He is 2.6132 fm, about 39% (0.7 fm) larger than its $r_p$.
- The rms pair separations between core nucleons and halo neutrons in $^6$He are about 80% larger than pair separations within the swollen and off-centered "alpha core".
- For $^4$He, the $pp$ spin-singlet pair number is 0.976772 and the $nn$ spin-singlet pair number is 0.976774, while spin-triplet contributions are 0.023228 and 0.023226 respectively.
- For $^6$He, the $nn$ spin-singlet pair number is 2.865420 and the $nn$ spin-triplet pair number is 3.134580.
- The total number of nucleon pairs sums to 6.000001 for $^4$He and 15.000000 for $^6$He, matching $A(A-1)/2$.

## Context

This work extends previous _ab initio_ NCSM studies of clustering in light nuclei, including the NCSM/RGM calculations by Romero-Redondo (2014) and Quaglioni (2013) on a $^4$He+$n$+$n$ cluster basis, and the entanglement analysis of $^4$He and $^6$He by PhysRevC.103.034325. The method of using two-body spatial operators to extract clustering information from _ab initio_ wave functions provides a new tool for characterizing halo structure beyond what one-body density profiles can reveal, particularly for Borromean systems like $^6$He. This approach can be applied to other halo nuclei and will benefit from future development of extrapolation methods for the two-body correlations.
