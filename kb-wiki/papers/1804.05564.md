# Simulation study of elliptic flow of charged hadrons produced in Au + Au collisions at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research

> Centrality and system geometry dependence of azimuthal anisotropy of charged hadrons measured in terms of the elliptic flow parameter are investigated using Au+Au event samples at incident beam energy $20$A GeV and $40$A GeV generated by Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics and A Multiphase Transport models. The Monte Carlo Glauber model is employed to estimate the eccentricity of the overlapping zone at an early stage of the collisions. Anisotropies present both in the particle multiplicity distribution and in the kinetic radial expansion are examined by using standard statistical and phenomenological methods. In the context of upcoming Compressed Baryonic Matter experiment to be held at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research, the present set of simulated results provide us not only with an opportunity to examine the expected collective behavior of hadronic matter at high baryon density and moderate temperature environment, but when compared with similar results obtained from Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and Large Hadron Collider experiments, they also allow us to investigate how anisotropy of hadronic matter may differ or agree with its low baryon density and high temperature counterpart.

## Key claim

The paper uses simulations to predict that elliptic flow of charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions at FAIR energies (20A and 40A GeV) will exhibit a centrality dependence that is largely energy-independent between these two beam energies, and that the eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow shows different patterns depending on whether standard or participant eccentricity is used, with the participant eccentricity scaling partially restoring the bell-shaped centrality dependence seen in unscaled flow.

## Method

Au+Au collisions at 20A GeV and 40A GeV were simulated using the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model and the A Multiphase Transport (AMPT) model in both default and string-melting configurations, each with 0.2 million events. The Monte Carlo Glauber (MCG) model was used to estimate the spatial eccentricity of the initial overlap zone and to determine collision centrality via the number of participating nucleons (Npart). Elliptic flow parameter v2 was computed from the azimuthal distribution of charged hadrons, and the results were scaled by both standard eccentricity (εstd) and participant eccentricity (εpart) to study the influence of initial geometry.

## Key numbers

* Charged hadron multiplicity difference between UrQMD and AMPT at highest centrality at 20A GeV: as large as 100 particles.
* Maximum v2 for UrQMD model occurs at Npart ≈ 200, corresponding to impact parameter b ≈ 5.5–6.5 fm.
* For Au+Au at 20A GeV, 0–10% centrality: ⟨Npart⟩ = 317.5 ± 0.2, ⟨εstd⟩ = 0.068 ± 0.001, ⟨εpart⟩ = 0.113 ± 0.001.
* For Au+Au at 20A GeV, 40–50% centrality: ⟨Npart⟩ = 80.4 ± 0.1, ⟨εstd⟩ = 0.373 ± 0.001, ⟨εpart⟩ = 0.429 ± 0.002.
* For Au+Au at 20A GeV, 70–80% centrality: ⟨Npart⟩ = 17.8 ± 0.1, ⟨εstd⟩ = 0.412 ± 0.003, ⟨εpart⟩ = 0.645 ± 0.002.
* Difference between εstd and εpart is about 20% in the Npart ≈ 100–300 region.
* MCG model parameters for Au: nuclear radius R = 6.38 fm, skin depth a = 0.535 fm, NN cross-section σNN = 30.5 mb at 20A GeV and 30.8 mb at 40A GeV.

## Context

This simulation study extends the investigation of elliptic flow to the high-baryon-density, moderate-temperature regime of the upcoming CBM experiment at FAIR, complementing measurements at RHIC (e.g., STAR at √sNN = 200 GeV) and LHC where the fireball has low baryon density and high temperature. The results are compared with prior observations from Alver et al. (2007) on eccentricity scaling at RHIC energies, and with hydrodynamic predictions from Kolb et al. (2000) and Bhalerao et al. (2005). The work enables predictions for the CBM experiment and provides constraints on transport models (UrQMD and AMPT) in a previously unexplored energy range.
