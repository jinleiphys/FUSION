# The Mar(e)k of QGP: Strangeness

> Strangeness signature of of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is central to the exploration of baryon-dense matter: the search for the critical point and onset of deconfinement. I report on the discovery of QGP by means of strangeness: The key historical figures and their roles in this quest are introduced and the experimental results obtained are discussed. The important role of antihyperons is emphasized. The statistical hadronization model, and sudden hadronization are described. Results of present day data analysis: strangeness and entropy content of a large fireball, and the universal hadronization condition describing key features of all explored collision systems are presented.

## Key claim

The strangeness signature of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is central to exploring baryon-dense matter and the search for the critical point and onset of deconfinement, but the paper argues that the CERN QGP discovery announcement in February 2000 was unnecessarily diluted by including consensus-building results from diverse experiments, whereas sticking to the flagship strangeness signature would have left few doubts about the experimental results.

## Method

The paper reviews the historical development of the strangeness signature of QGP, from early theoretical proposals in 1980 through experimental programs at CERN (NA35, NA36, NA49, WA85, WA94, WA97, NA57) and later at RHIC and LHC (STAR, ALICE). It describes the statistical hadronization model (SHM) used to analyze strange baryon and antibaryon production data, focusing on chemical potentials and phase space occupancy, and presents comparisons of hyperon and antihyperon yields across collision systems and energies.

## Key numbers

- The NA35 experiment observed a two-fold increase in the relative s + s̄ concentration in central S-S collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon, reflected in the K/π ratio and hyperon multiplicities (Bartke 1990).
- The NA35 measured the ratio Λ̄/p̄ ≤ 1.4 near mid-rapidity in Summer 1995, showing enhancement by a factor 3 to 5 compared to more elementary reactions (Alber 1996).
- The Ξ/φ ratio is found to be a constant at 0.281 across a wide range of collision centrality and energy, consistent with QGP breakup (Petran 2009).
- ALICE data show hyperon and antihyperon yields per participant normalized to the smallest Npart available, with enhancement up to a factor of 20 (Abelev 2013).
- ALICE pA and pp hyperon-to-pion ratios (Λ/π, Ξ/π, Ω/π) disagree with canonical phase space model predictions by up to 5 standard deviations in low charge multiplicity events (Adam 2015).

## Context

This work extends the strangeness signature of QGP first proposed by Rafelski (1980) and developed with Koch and Müller (1986), and contradicts the canonical strangeness suppression model argued by Redlich. The paper reviews the historical contributions of Marek Gaździcki, who initiated the experimental effort to discover QGP through strangeness enhancement in the NA35 and NA49 experiments, and discusses the CERN QGP discovery announcement (Heinz and Jacob 2000) and subsequent RHIC QGP discovery claims (RHIC white papers 2005). The results enable further study of the universal hadronization condition across all explored collision systems, from SPS to LHC energies.
