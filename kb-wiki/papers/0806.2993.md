# Reply to the "Comment on `Once more about the $K\bar{K}$ molecule approach to the light scalars' "

> The need to regularize loop integrals in a manner that preserves gauge invariance, for example, using the Pauli-Villars method, requires a subtraction that in the large mass limit hides its high momentum origin. This gives rise to the illusion that only nonrelativistic kaon loop momenta are relevant, when in fact this is not the case, as we show.

# Reply to "Comment on 'Once more about the K\(\bar{K}\) molecule approach to the light scalars'"

## Key claim
The authors argue that the apparent dominance of nonrelativistic kaon loop momenta in the \(\phi \to K^+K^- \to \gamma (a_0/f_0)\) amplitude is an illusion caused by the subtraction constant in Pauli-Villars regularization, which hides the high-momentum origin of the loop integrals; in reality, the kaon loop model describes relativistic physics.

## Method
The authors analyze the gauge-invariant regularized amplitude for \(\phi \to \gamma (a_0/f_0)\) using Pauli-Villars regularization with a regulator mass \(M\). They show that the regularized amplitude \(\overline{T}_{\nu\mu}(p,q)\) satisfies the gauge invariance condition \(\epsilon^\nu(\phi)k^\mu\overline{T}_{\nu\mu}(p,q)=0\) and demonstrate that at the kinematic point \(p=q\) the contributions from the kaon mass \(m_K\) and the regulator mass \(M\) cancel to give a finite subtraction constant, which in the \(M\to\infty\) limit hides its high-momentum origin.

## Key numbers
- The regulator field mass \(M\) is taken to infinity in the limit \(M\to\infty\) to recover the physical amplitude \(T^{Phys}[\phi\to\gamma(a_0/f_0)]\).
- At the kinematic point \(p=q\), the nonregularized amplitude \(\epsilon^\nu(\phi)\epsilon^\mu(\gamma)T^{m_K}_{\nu\mu}(p,p)\) and the regulator amplitude \(\epsilon^\nu(\phi)\epsilon^\mu(\gamma)T^M_{\nu\mu}(p,p)\) combine to give \((\epsilon(\phi)\epsilon(\gamma))(1-1)=0\), demonstrating the cancellation.
- The intermediate momenta in the kaon loops are at the level of a few GeV, not nonrelativistic.

## Context
This reply responds to a Comment on the authors' earlier work (Achasov 2007) criticizing the \(K\bar{K}\) molecule approach to light scalar mesons. The kaon loop model for \(\phi\) radiative decays was originally suggested by Achasov (1989) and further analyzed by Achasov (2001, 2003). The authors argue that the Comment's authors incorrectly discarded contributions representing high-momentum and charge flow distributions, distorting the physical significance of the loop model. The work enables proper treatment of gauge invariance in kaon loop calculations for light scalar meson production.
