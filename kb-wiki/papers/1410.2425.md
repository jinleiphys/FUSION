# Generalized Loop Space and Evolution of the Light-Like Wilson Loops

> Equations of motion for the light-like QCD Wilson loops are studied in the generalized loop space (GLS) setting. To this end, the classically conformal-invariant non-local variations of the cusped Wilson exponentials lying (partially) on the light-cone are formulated in terms of the Fréchet derivative. The rapidity and renormalization-group behaviour of the gauge-invariant quantum correlation functions (in particular, the three-dimensional parton densities) are demonstrated to be connected to certain geometrical properties of the Wilson loops defined in the GLS.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the rapidity evolution of correlation functions containing light-like cusped Wilson loops can be represented as a shape-transformation law in the generalized loop space (GLS), specifically that the logarithmic Frechet derivative associated with a generating vector field is equivalent to the non-local infinitesimal shape-transformation operators previously introduced for Wilson loops.

## Method

The authors formulate classically conformal-invariant non-local variations of cusped Wilson exponentials lying partially on the light-cone using the Frechet derivative in the generalized loop space. They compute the leading-order perturbative expansion of the Frechet derivative for a quadrilateral light-like Wilson loop configuration, evaluating the contributions from each side of the contour using the gluon propagator in coordinate space in the Feynman gauge. The equivalence between the shape-transformation operators and the Frechet derivative is verified by explicit calculation of the resulting integrals.

## Key numbers

* The gluon propagator in coordinate space is given as \(K_\epsilon \frac{g_{\mu\nu}\delta^{ab}}{[-(x-y)^2]^{1-\epsilon}}\) with \(K_\epsilon = \frac{(\mu^2 \pi)^\epsilon}{4\pi^2}\Gamma(1-\epsilon)\).
* The leading-order contribution from the side \(\ell_1\) yields \(\frac{1}{2} K_\epsilon \frac{S_{12}^\epsilon}{\epsilon}\), where \(S_{12} = (\ell_1 + \ell_2)^2\).
* The renormalization group equation for the Frechet derivative of the Wilson loop is \(\mu \frac{d}{d\mu} [D_V \mathcal{W}_*] = -\sum \Gamma_{\text{cusp}}\), where \(\Gamma_{\text{cusp}}\) is the light-cone cusp anomalous dimension.

## Context

This work extends the study of Wilson loop renormalization and evolution in the generalized loop space framework, building on prior work on light-like Wilson polygons and their singularities (WL_LC_basic) and on shape-transformation operators for Wilson loops (WL_UA). The formalism connects the geometrical properties of Wilson loops in the GLS to the rapidity and renormalization-group behavior of transverse-momentum dependent parton densities (TMDs), particularly in the large-Bjorken-x regime relevant for experiments at the planned Electron-Ion Collider and Jefferson Lab. The authors note that light-like cusped Wilson loops with different numbers of cusps correspond to different physical objects obeying different evolution laws, and that application to the derivation of evolution equations for TMDs on the light-cone is under ongoing investigation.
