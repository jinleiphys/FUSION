# Systematic improvement of the quantum approximate optimisation ansatz for combinatorial optimisation using quantum subspace expansion

> The quantum approximate optimisation ansatz (QAOA) is one of the flagship algorithms used to tackle combinatorial optimisation on graphs problems using a quantum computer, and is considered a strong candidate for early fault-tolerant advantage. In this work, I study the enhancement of the QAOA with a generator coordinate method (GCM), and achieve systematic performances improvements in the approximation ratio and fidelity for the maximal independent set on Erdös-Rényi graphs. The cost-to-solution of the present method and the QAOA are compared by analysing the number of logical CNOT and $T$ gates required for either algorithm. Extrapolating on the numerical results obtained, it is estimated that for this specific problem and setup, the approach surpasses QAOA for graphs of size greater than 75 using as little as eight trial states. The potential of the method for other combinatorial optimisation problems is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that combining the quantum approximate optimisation ansatz (QAOA) with a generator coordinate method (GCM) implemented as a quantum subspace expansion (QSE) systematically improves the approximation ratio and fidelity for the maximal independent set problem on Erdős-Rényi graphs, and that for graphs larger than 75 nodes, the combined approach surpasses plain QAOA using as few as eight trial states, based on extrapolation of numerical results rather than direct demonstration at that scale.

## Method

The author applies a QAOA of depth L=20 to the maximal independent set problem on Erdős-Rényi graphs with average density ρ=1/2, optimising one layer at a time using the L-BFGS-B gradient descent algorithm. After QAOA optimisation, the resulting state is used as the reference for a quantum subspace expansion, where generating states are constructed by real-time evolution under the cost Hamiltonian at equally spaced times in the interval [−π(1−1/K); π(1−1/K)]. The generalised eigenvalue problem formed by the Hamiltonian and overlap kernels is solved after discarding overlap eigenvalues below a threshold of 10⁻³, and the cost-to-solution ratio is compared by analysing the number of logical CNOT and T gates required for each algorithm.

## Key numbers

- QAOA depth is set to L=20 layers, with only one layer optimised at a time.
- Overlap eigenvalues below ε_cut = 10⁻³ are discarded during diagonalisation.
- For the G3 cube graph, QAOA yields approximately 17% fidelity for each of the two maximal independent sets; QSE with K=8 trial states reaches above 98% fidelity.
- For the K⁺₃₃ graph, QAOA yields approximately 14% fidelity for the maximal independent set; QSE with K=8 trial states exceeds 98% fidelity.
- Extrapolating from numerical results, the approach is estimated to surpass QAOA for graphs of size greater than 75 nodes using as few as eight trial states.

## Context

This work extends the application of the generator coordinate method, a standard tool in nuclear many-body physics (Ring 1980, Bender 2003), to quantum computing for combinatorial optimisation, building on prior quantum implementations for strongly correlated systems (Zheng 2022, Jamet 2022, Beaujeault-Taudière 2023). The approach contrasts with other QAOA enhancements such as warm-starting (Tate 2024), iterative layer optimisation (Lee 2023), and adaptive mixers (Zhu 2020, Herrman 2022), and enables a systematic route to improve QAOA solutions without increasing circuit depth, at the cost of additional gate operations for kernel evaluation.
