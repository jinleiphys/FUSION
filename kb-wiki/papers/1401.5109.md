# Initial Conditions for Modified DGLAP Evolution of the Modified Fragmentation Functions in Nuclear Medium

> Initial conditions are required to solve medium modified DGLAP (mDGLAP) evolution equations for modified fragmentation functions due to multiple scatterings and parton energy loss. Such initial conditions should in principle include energy loss for partons at scale $Q_0$ above which mDGLAP evolution equations can be applied. Several models for the initial condition motivated by induced gluon bremsstrahlung in perturbative QCD are used to calculate the modified fragmentation functions in nuclear medium and to extract the jet transport parameter $\hat q$ from fits to experimental data in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclei. The model with a Poisson convolution of multiple gluon emissions is found to provide the overall best $χ^2$/d.o.f. fit to the HERMES data and gives a value of $\hat q_0 \approx 0.020 \pm 0.005$ GeV$^2$/fm at the center of a large nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a "convoluted" initial condition for modified DGLAP (mDGLAP) evolution, based on a Poisson convolution of multiple gluon emissions, provides the best overall fit to HERMES data on hadron suppression in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclei, yielding a jet transport parameter of \(\hat q_0 \approx 0.020 \pm 0.005\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at the center of a large nucleus; this result is conditional on the choice of initial condition, as the vacuum and evolved initial conditions give worse \(\chi^2\)/d.o.f. fits.

## Method

The authors solve medium-modified DGLAP (mDGLAP) evolution equations for fragmentation functions in nuclear matter, using the high-twist approach to parton energy loss. They test three models for the initial conditions at scale \(Q_0 = 1\) GeV: a vacuum initial condition (no medium effects below \(Q_0\)), an evolved initial condition (evolving vacuum fragmentation functions with only medium-induced splitting from \(Q=0\) to \(Q_0\)), and a convoluted initial condition (a Poisson convolution of multiple gluon emissions, each with a spectrum from a single induced gluon emission). The modified fragmentation functions are then used to compute the hadron suppression factor \(R_A^h\) in semi-inclusive DIS off nuclei, and the jet transport parameter \(\hat q_0\) is extracted by fitting to HERMES data for pions and kaons on Ne, Kr, and Xe targets.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit value of the jet transport parameter at the center of a large nucleus is \(\hat q_0 = 0.020 \pm 0.005\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, obtained using the convoluted initial condition in fits to HERMES data.
* The initial scale for mDGLAP evolution is set to \(Q_0 = 1\) GeV, and the gluon's average intrinsic transverse momentum parameter is \(\mu_D = 0.2\) GeV.
* The vacuum initial condition requires a larger \(\hat q_0\) value to fit the data and gives the largest \(\chi^2\)/d.o.f. at the minima, while the convoluted initial condition gives the smallest \(\chi^2\)/d.o.f. at the minima among the three models.
* For comparison, the JET Collaboration (Burke 2013) found \(\hat q \approx 1.2 \pm 0.3\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at RHIC and \(1.9 \pm 0.7\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at the LHC for hot QGP, at an initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) for a 10 GeV quark jet.

## Context

This work extends the high-twist approach to parton energy loss (Guo 2000, Wang 2001) by systematically comparing three initial condition models for mDGLAP evolution, which were previously used in studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions (Wang 2009, Deng 2010). The convoluted initial condition improves upon the vacuum initial condition (Majumder 2011, 2013) and the evolved initial condition (Wang 2009, Deng 2010) by providing a better fit to HERMES data on the energy and virtuality dependence of hadron suppression. The extracted \(\hat q_0\) value for cold nuclear matter can serve as a baseline for modeling the jet transport parameter in a hadron resonance gas at finite temperature, relevant for jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions.
