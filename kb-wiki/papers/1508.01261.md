# Characteristics of hybrid compact stars with a sharp hadron-quark interface

> We describe two aspects of the physics of hybrid stars that have a sharp interface between a core of quark matter and a mantle of nuclear matter. Firstly, we analyze the mass-radius relation. We describe a generic "Constant Speed of Sound" (CSS) parameterization of the quark matter equation of state (EoS), in which the speed of sound is independent of density. In terms of the three parameters of the CSS EoS we obtain the phase diagram of possible forms of the hybrid star mass-radius relation, and we show how observational constraints on the maximum mass and typical radius of neutron stars can be expressed as constraints on the CSS parameters. Secondly, we propose a mechanism for the damping of density oscillations, including r-modes, in hybrid stars with a sharp interface. The dissipation arises from the periodic conversion between quark matter and nuclear matter induced by the pressure oscillations in the star. We find the damping grows nonlinearly with the amplitude of the oscillation and is powerful enough to saturate an r-mode at very low saturation amplitude, of order $10^{-10}$, which is compatible with currently-available observations of neutron star spin frequencies and temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for hybrid stars with a sharp hadron-quark interface, the mass-radius relation can take four distinct forms depending on the three parameters of the Constant Speed of Sound (CSS) parameterization of quark matter, and that r-mode oscillations can be damped by periodic phase conversion at the interface to a saturation amplitude of order $10^{-10}$, but this damping mechanism only applies if the surface tension is high enough to maintain a sharp interface (Maxwell construction) rather than a mixed phase.

## Method

The authors used the CSS parameterization of the quark matter equation of state, which assumes a density-independent speed of sound, characterized by three parameters: the transition pressure $\ptrans$, the energy density discontinuity $\De\ep$ at the transition, and the speed of sound in quark matter $\cQM$. They combined this with two nuclear matter equations of state (BHF and DBHF) and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to map out the phase diagram of possible mass-radius relations for hybrid stars. For the damping mechanism, they analyzed the dissipation from periodic conversion between quark matter and nuclear matter induced by pressure oscillations in the star.

## Key numbers

- The critical energy density discontinuity for destabilization is given by $\destab/\etrans = 1/2 + (3/2)(\ptrans/\etrans)$
- For $\cQMsq=1/3$, the minimum radius of a maximum-mass star is greater than 11.25 km (BHF) or 11.4 km (DBHF)
- For $\cQMsq=1/3$, the minimum radius of a $1.4\,\Msolar$ star is 12 km (BHF) or about 12.25 km (DBHF)
- For $\cQMsq=1$, the smallest stars have radii as small as 9 km, and $R_{1.4}\gtrsim 9.5$ km at large energy density discontinuities
- The r-mode saturation amplitude is of order $10^{-10}$
- The BHF nuclear EoS gives a maximum mass of 2.03 $\Msolar$ and a radius of 11.77 km for a $1.4\,\Msolar$ star
- The DBHF nuclear EoS gives a maximum mass of 2.31 $\Msolar$ and a radius of 13.41 km for a $1.4\,\Msolar$ star
- If a $2.1\,\Msolar$ star were observed, the minimum radius for $\cQMsq=1/3$ would increase to about 12.1 km (BHF) or 12.2 km (DBHF) for maximum-mass stars, and to about 12.7 km (BHF) or 13 km (DBHF) for $1.4\,\Msolar$ stars

## Context

This work extends the CSS parameterization approach from Alford (2013) and Zdunik (2012) to systematically map the phase diagram of hybrid star mass-radius relations, showing how the four possible forms (absent, connected, disconnected, both) depend on the three CSS parameters. The damping mechanism for r-modes via phase conversion at a sharp interface addresses the problem raised by Andersson (1997) that undamped r-modes could spin down neutron stars in days via gravitational radiation. The results enable observational constraints from neutron star mass measurements (such as the $2\,\Msolar$ stars reported by Antoniadis (2013) and Demorest (2010)) and future radius measurements to be translated directly into constraints on the quark matter equation of state parameters.
