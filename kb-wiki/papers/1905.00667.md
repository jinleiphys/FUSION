# Net-proton number fluctuations in the presence of the QCD critical point

> Event-by-event fluctuations of the net-proton number studied in heavy-ion collisions provide an important means in the search for the conjectured critical end point (CP) in the QCD phase diagram. We propose a phenomenological model in which the fluctuations of the chiral critical mode couple to protons and anti-protons. This allows us to study the behavior of the net-proton number fluctuations in the presence of the CP. Calculating the net-proton number cumulants, $C_n$ with n=1,2,3,4, along the phenomenological freeze-out line we show that the ratio of variance and mean $C_2/C_1$, as well as kurtosis $C_4/C_2$ resemble qualitative properties observed in data in heavy-ion collisions as a function of beam energy obtained by the STAR Collaboration at RHIC. In particular, the non-monotonic structure of the kurtosis and smooth change of the $C_2/C_1$ ratio with beam energy could be due to the CP located near the freeze-out line. The skewness, however, exhibits properties that are in contrast to the criticality expected due to the CP. The dependence of our results on the model parameters and the proximity of the chemical freeze-out to the critical point are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the non-monotonic structure of the kurtosis and the smooth change of the variance-to-mean ratio with beam energy observed by the STAR Collaboration could be due to the QCD critical point located near the freeze-out line, but only if the overlap between O(4) and Z(2) criticalities is properly accounted for; the skewness, however, exhibits properties that contrast with the criticality expected from the critical point.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological model coupling fluctuations of the chiral critical mode to protons and anti-protons within a hadron resonance gas baseline. They implement a modified scaling relation for the net-proton number variance based on the chiral relation between net-baryon number and chiral susceptibilities found in effective model calculations for O(4) and the tricritical point. The critical mode cumulants are obtained from the 3-dimensional Ising model equation of state using a parametric representation, with a linear mapping between the spin model variables and QCD temperature and baryon-chemical potential. Net-proton number cumulants C1 through C4 are calculated along the chemical freeze-out line with experimental kinematic acceptance cuts applied.

## Key numbers

- The coupling strength g between (anti-)protons and the critical mode is taken in the range 3 to 5, inferred from effective model calculations where g ≈ mp/fπ ≈ 10 in the linear sigma model and g ≈ 3–7 in quark-meson models.
- The critical point is exemplarily located at μcp = 0.39 GeV and Tcp = 0.149 GeV.
- The mapping parameters are ΔTcp = 0.02 GeV and Δμcp = 0.42 GeV.
- The normalization constants for the magnetic equation of state are M0 = 5.52 × 10⁻² GeV and H0 = 3.44 × 10⁻⁴ GeV³.
- The critical exponents used are β = 0.325 and δ = 4.8169.
- The kinematic acceptance cuts applied are: rapidity |y| ≤ 0.5, transverse momentum 0.4 GeV/c ≤ kT ≤ 2 GeV/c, and full azimuthal angle.
- The chiral crossover curvature parameter κc ranges from 0.007 to 0.059, with T_c,0 between 0.145 GeV and 0.163 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological model of Bluhm (2016) that coupled critical mode fluctuations to (anti-)protons via fluctuating masses, which produced a pronounced peak in the variance-to-mean ratio C2/C1 that contradicted STAR data. The present modification implements the chiral relation between net-baryon number and chiral susceptibilities from Sasaki (2006, 2007), which accounts for the overlap between O(4) and Z(2) criticalities near the critical point. This enables a qualitative description of the STAR Collaboration data on net-proton fluctuations without the spurious peak in C2/C1, while the non-monotonic structure in higher-order cumulants is strongly suppressed. The results depend on the location of the critical point relative to the freeze-out line and on the coupling strength, and the skewness remains problematic for the critical point interpretation.
