# alpha decay half-lives of new superheavy nuclei within a generalized liquid drop model

> The alpha decay half-lives of the recently produced isotopes of the 112, 114, 116 and 118 nuclei and decay products have been calculated in the quasi-molecular shape path using the experimental Qalpha value and a Generalized Liquid Drop Model including the proximity effects between nucleons in the neck or the gap between the nascent fragments. Reasonable estimates are obtained for the observed alpha decay half-lives. The results are compared with calculations using the Density-Dependent M3Y effective interaction and the Viola-Seaborg-Sobiczewski formulae. Generalized Liquid Drop Model predictions are provided for the alpha decay half-lives of other superheavy nuclei using the Finite Range Droplet Model Qalpha and compared with the values derived from the VSS formulae.

## Key claim
The Generalized Liquid Drop Model (GLDM), which includes proximity effects between nucleons, provides reasonable estimates for the alpha decay half-lives of recently produced superheavy nuclei (Z=112, 114, 116, 118) when using experimental Q_alpha values, though most theoretical half-lives are slightly smaller than the experimental data, possibly due to neglecting nuclear rotation and not explicitly including shell effects and pairing correlations in the barrier.

## Method
The authors calculated alpha decay half-lives using the GLDM with a quasi-molecular shape path, adding a proximity energy term to the Coulomb barrier to account for nuclear forces between nascent fragments. The barrier penetration probability was computed via the WKB approximation with an assault frequency of 10^20 s^-1, using experimental Q_alpha values and assuming the inner turning point equals the sum of the daughter and alpha particle radii. Results were compared with calculations using the Density-Dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction and the Viola-Seaborg-Sobiczewski (VSS) formulae, and predictions for unobserved superheavy nuclei were made using Q_alpha values from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM).

## Key numbers
- 294118: experimental half-life 1.8^{+75}_{-1.3} ms; GLDM gives 0.15^{+0.05}_{-0.04} ms; DDM3Y gives 0.66^{+0.23}_{-0.18} ms; VSS gives 0.64^{+0.24}_{-0.18} ms
- 293116: experimental half-life 53^{+62}_{-19} ms; GLDM gives 22.81^{+10.22}_{-7.06} ms; VSS gives 1258^{+557}_{-384} ms
- 289114: experimental half-life 2.7^{+1.4}_{-0.7} s; GLDM gives 0.52^{+0.25}_{-0.17} s; VSS gives 26.7^{+13.1}_{-8.7} s
- 285112: experimental half-life 34^{+17}_{-9} s; GLDM gives 13.22^{+7.25}_{-4.64} s; VSS gives 592^{+323}_{-207} s
- 271106: experimental half-life 2.4^{+4.3}_{-1.0} min; GLDM gives 0.33^{+0.28}_{-0.16} min; VSS gives 8.6^{+7.3}_{-3.9} min
- The proximity energy formula uses a surface width b=0.99 fm and a surface parameter gamma as the geometric mean of the two fragment surface parameters
- The assault frequency is taken as nu_0 = 10^20 s^-1

## Context
This work extends the GLDM approach previously applied to fusion (Roy85), fission (Royzb02), and light nucleus and alpha emission (Roy00, Roy04) to the newly produced superheavy isotopes of elements 112, 114, 116, and 118 reported by Oganessian et al. (2005). The results are compared with the DDM3Y effective interaction calculations of Choudhury (2006) and the VSS formulae of Sobiczewski (1989). The GLDM predictions for unobserved superheavy nuclei (Z=106 to 120) using FRDM Q_alpha values provide a narrow window between GLDM and VSS results where unknown half-lives may lie, and the calculations suggest submagic numbers at N=162 and N=176 for certain proton ranges.
