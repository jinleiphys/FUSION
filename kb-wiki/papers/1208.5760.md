# Sign problems, noise, and chiral symmetry breaking in a QCD-like theory

> The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model reduced to 2+1 dimensions has two different path integral formulations: at finite chemical potential one formulation has a severe sign problem similar to that found in QCD, while the other does not. At large N, where N is the number of flavors, one can compute the probability distributions of fermion correlators analytically in both formulations. In the former case one finds a broad distribution with small mean; in the latter one finds a heavy tailed positive distribution amenable to the cumulant expansion techniques developed in earlier work. We speculate on the implications of this model for QCD.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in 2+1 dimensions has two equivalent path integral formulations, one of which exhibits a severe sign problem at finite chemical potential similar to QCD while the other does not; the authors show that the sign problem in the QCD-like formulation is intimately connected to chiral symmetry breaking and the existence of a light pion, and that the probability distributions of fermion correlators in the two formulations differ dramatically at large N.

## Method

The authors analyze the three-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with N flavors using a 1/N expansion, studying two complementary path integral formulations: the sigma/pi formulation (with auxiliary scalar and pseudoscalar fields) and the A/V formulation (with vector and axial vector auxiliary fields). They compute the effective action, meson propagators, and fermion self-energy analytically in the large-N limit, then derive the probability distributions and cumulants of fermion correlation functions in both formulations by evaluating connected Feynman diagrams for the characteristic function.

## Key numbers

* The dynamical fermion mass M = (f/2)[1 + sqrt(1 + 4 eta)] where eta = m/f, with f being the chiral symmetry breaking vacuum expectation value and m the current quark mass
* The pion mass squared m_pi^2 = 4 f m (1 - (1/3)eta + (31/45)eta^2 - (1654/945)eta^3 + ...) in the chiral expansion
* The sigma mass m_sigma = 2 f in the chiral limit, with m_sigma > 2 M for m > 0
* The pion wavefunction renormalization Z_pi = 4 pi f (1 + (1/3)eta - (4/15)eta^2 + ...)
* The coupling constant is fixed by the renormalization condition g = -pi/f in the chiral limit
* The first cumulant kappa_{1,0} = z exp(-M tau) for the fermion propagator in the A/V formulation, where z = Tr[Gamma (1+gamma_1)/2]

## Context

This work extends the research program of Lee (2011), Endres (2011), and Endres (2011) studying probability distributions of correlators to understand the origins of sign and noise problems in lattice QCD. The authors use the three-dimensional NJL model as a simpler theory that exhibits chiral symmetry breaking without confinement, allowing analytic computation of probability distributions. The analysis builds on the Splittorff and Verbaarschot (2006, 2007) explanation of the QCD sign problem in terms of pion condensation, and on Lepage's (1989) explanation of noise in baryon correlators. The results suggest that the sigma/pi formulation, while free of a sign problem, still faces an overlap problem that may be amenable to cumulant expansion techniques, and the authors speculate on implications for finding a path integral formulation of QCD suitable for numerical study of bulk matter at finite density.
