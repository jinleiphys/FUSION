# Ground-state properties of doubly magic nuclei from the unitary-model-operator approach with the chiral two- and three-nucleon forces

> The ground-state energies and radii for $^{4}$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca are calculated with the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA). In the present study, we employ the similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolved nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) and three-nucleon ($3N$) interactions based on the chiral effective field theory. This is the first UMOA calculation with both $NN$ and $3N$ interactions. The calculated ground-state energies and radii are consistent with the recent {\it ab initio} results with the same interaction. We evaluate the expectation values with two- and three-body SRG evolved radius operators, in addition to those with the bare radius operator. With the aid of the higher-body evolution of radius operator, it is seen that the calculated radii tend to be SRG resolution-scale independent. We find that the SRG evolution gives minor modifications for the radius operator.

## Key claim

The unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) is applied for the first time with both nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions from chiral effective field theory, and the resulting ground-state energies and radii for 4He, 16O, and 40Ca agree with recent ab initio results within a few percent; however, the simultaneous reproduction of ground-state energies and radii depends strongly on the employed nuclear interactions, and further investigations are needed to specify the proper choice.

## Method

The authors start from a Hamiltonian composed of kinetic, NN, and 3N terms, then apply similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution to soften the interactions, keeping up to three-body terms in both the Hamiltonian and the radius operator. The three-body terms are reduced via the normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation using a Hartree-Fock reference state, and the UMOA unitary transformation is constructed to decouple one-particle-one-hole and two-particle-two-hole excitations. Three types of Hamiltonians are used: "NN-only" (no genuine 3N force), "NN+3N-ind" (3N force induced by SRG evolution starting without genuine 3N), and "NN+3N-full" (starting with genuine 3N interaction).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies and radii for 4He, 16O, and 40Ca are calculated; results agree with recent ab initio calculations within a few percent (no specific numerical values for energies or radii are given in the provided text).
- The SRG evolution parameter lambda_SRG is used, with initial condition lambda_SRG = infinity fm^{-1} evolved to lower values; the resolution scale parameter alpha is in units of fm^4, with lambda_SRG = alpha^{-1/4}.
- The radius operator is evolved to include two-body and three-body SRG evolved terms; with up to three-body terms included, the calculated radii tend to become SRG resolution-scale independent, and SRG evolution gives minor modifications for the radius operator.

## Context

This work extends previous UMOA calculations (Miyagi 2017) that included only NN interactions, now incorporating 3N forces for the first time in this framework. The results are compared with other ab initio methods such as the no-core shell model (NCSM) (Barrett 2013), coupled-cluster method (Hagen 2014), and in-medium similarity renormalization group (Hergert 2016), which have reached mass region A ~ 100 (Binder 2014, Tichai 2016, Morris 2018). The study demonstrates that SRG evolution of the radius operator, following the approach of Schuster (2014, 2015) for few-body systems, is necessary to reduce resolution-scale dependence, but the simultaneous reproduction of energies and radii remains a challenge that requires further investigation of the nuclear interactions.
