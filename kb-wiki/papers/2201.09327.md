# Long and short distance behavior of the imaginary part of the heavy-quark potential

> The imaginary part of the effective heavy-quark potential is related to the total in-medium decay width of heavy quark-antiquark bound states. We extract the static limit of this quantity using classical-statistical simulations of real-time Yang-Mills dynamics by measuring the temporal decay of Wilson loops. By performing the simulations on finer and larger lattices, we are able to show that the nonperturbative results follow the same form as the perturbative ones. For large quark-antiquark separations, we quantify the magnitude of the non-perturbative long-range corrections to the imaginary part of the heavy-quark potential. We present our results for a wide range of temperatures, lattice spacings, and lattice volumes. We also extract approximations for the short-distance behavior of the classical potential.

## Key claim

The nonperturbative classical-statistical Yang-Mills simulations of the imaginary part of the heavy-quark potential follow the same functional form as perturbative hard-thermal-loop (HTL) results, with the classical potential approaching the perturbative hard-classical-loop (HCL) large-beta limit from below as the lattice spacing is decreased.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice simulations of pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in real time, initializing fields in a quasi-thermal momentum-space configuration and allowing them to self-thermalize dynamically. They extracted the imaginary part of the static heavy-quark potential by measuring the temporal decay of Wilson loops on cubic lattices up to size 252^3, using lattice spacings a = {1, 0.5, 0.25, 0.2, 0.1} and temperatures corresponding to beta values between 100 and 300. The results were compared to hard-classical-loop (HCL) perturbation theory calculations on the same lattice sizes.

## Key numbers

- Simulations performed with g^2 T_0 = 0.44 for lattice spacings a = {1, 0.5, 0.25, 0.2} keeping aN = 12 fixed, and with g^2 T_0 = 0.45 for a = 0.1 with N = 252
- The short-distance behavior of the imaginary part of the classical potential follows the parametric form |Im[V_cl(r)]| ~ C_F g^2 T (m_D r)^2 log(m_D r) for m_D r less than approximately 0.5
- The functional form Im[V_cl(r)] = g^2 T A_infinity phi(B m_D r) fits the data well for m_D r less than or equal to 6, where phi(x) is defined by an integral over z from 0 to infinity
- The CYM results with beta = 136 overlap with the corresponding HCL curve at m_D r less than approximately 1

## Context

This work extends previous classical-statistical Yang-Mills simulations by Laine (2007) which used lattices of size 12^3 and 16^3, now reaching up to 252^3 lattices. The results confirm that the nonperturbative imaginary part of the heavy-quark potential follows the same functional form as leading-order HTL calculations by Laine (2006) and HCL perturbation theory. This enables improved modeling of heavy quark-antiquark bound state suppression in the quark-gluon plasma, particularly for understanding the long-range nonperturbative corrections and short-distance behavior of the in-medium potential.
