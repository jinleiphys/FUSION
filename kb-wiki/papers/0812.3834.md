# Up-to N$^3$LO heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation for the M1 properties of three-nucleon systems

> M1 properties, comprising magnetic moments and radiative capture of thermal neutron observables, are studied in two- and three-nucleon systems. We utilize meson exchange current derived up to N$^3$LO using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory a la Weinberg. Calculations have been performed for several qualitatively different realistic nuclear Hamiltonians, which permits us to analyze model dependence of our results. Our results are found to be strongly correlated with the effective range parameters such as binding energies and the scattering lengths. Taking into account such correlation, the results are in good agreement with the experimental data with small model-dependence.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that M1 properties of three-nucleon systems calculated using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory up to N$^3$LO show strong correlation with effective range parameters such as binding energies and scattering lengths; once this correlation is accounted for, the results agree well with experimental data with small model-dependence, contrary to the abstract's implication of straightforward agreement.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations in configuration space to obtain three-nucleon bound and scattering wave functions for several realistic nuclear Hamiltonians (Argonne Av18, INOY, ISUJ, chiral N$^3$LO potentials from Idaho and Bonn-Bochum groups). They employed meson exchange currents derived up to N$^3$LO in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, including one-body currents with relativistic corrections, soft one-pion exchange, vertex corrections, two-pion exchanges, and contact-term contributions with two independent low-energy constants (LECs) fixed by fitting the magnetic moments of $^3$H and $^3$He.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy experimental value: $B_3^{\rm exp} = 8.482$ MeV
- Deuteron binding energy from B1-N3LO potential: 2.215 MeV (experimental: 2.225 MeV)
- $np$ singlet scattering length from B1-N3LO potential: -23.60 fm (experimental: -23.74 fm)
- Deuteron binding energy from B2-N3LO potential: 2.218 MeV
- $np$ singlet scattering length from B2-N3LO potential: -23.72 fm
- Deuteron binding energy from B3-N3LO potential: 2.220 MeV
- $np$ singlet scattering length from B3-N3LO potential: -23.64 fm
- Pion decay constant used: $f_\pi \sim 92.4$ MeV
- Chiral scale: $\Lambda_\chi \sim 4\pi f_\pi \sim 1$ GeV
- Nucleon mass used in Faddeev equations: $\hbar^2/m = 41.471$ MeV$\cdot$fm
- LEC estimates from resonance saturation: ${\bar c}_\omega \simeq 0.1021$, ${\bar c}_\Delta \simeq 0.1667$, ${\bar N}_{WZ} \simeq 0.02395$

## Context

This work extends the up-to-N$^3$LO HBChPT description of M1 properties from two-nucleon systems (deuteron magnetic moment $\mu_d$ and $np\to d\gamma$ capture cross section $\sigma_{np}$, studied in Song et al. (2008) and earlier references) to $A=3$ systems. It builds on the MEEFT (more effective effective field theory) approach developed by the authors, which enables consistent EFT calculations on top of phenomenological wave functions by absorbing short-range model-dependence into renormalized LECs. The results enable parameter-free predictions for the $nd\to ^3$H$\gamma$ total cross section and photon polarization, and demonstrate that model-dependence in three-nucleon observables can be overcome by correlating results with binding energy predictions.
