# Chiral Symmetry Restoration from a Boundary

> The boundary of a manifold can alter the phase of a theory in the bulk. We explore the possibility of a boundary-induced phase transition for the chiral symmetry of QCD. In particular, we investigate the consequences of imposing homogeneous Dirichlet boundary conditions on the quark fields. Such boundary conditions are sometimes employed in lattice gauge theory computations, for example, when including external electromagnetic fields, or when computing quark propagators with a reduced temporal extent. Homogeneous Dirichlet boundary conditions force the chiral condensate to vanish at the boundary, and thereby obstruct the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry in the bulk. We show the restoration of chiral symmetry due to a boundary is a non-perturbative phenomenon depending upon the mechanism of spontaneous symmetry breaking, and utilize the sigma model to exemplify the issues. Within this model, we find that chiral symmetry is completely restored if the length of the compact direction is less than 2.0 fm. For lengths greater than about 4 fm, an approximately uniform chiral condensate forms centered about the midpoint of the compact direction. While the volume-averaged condensate approaches the infinite volume value as the compact direction becomes very long, the finite-size corrections are shown to be power law rather than exponential.

## Key claim

Homogeneous Dirichlet boundary conditions on quark fields can restore chiral symmetry in the bulk of a finite Euclidean manifold, but this restoration is a non-perturbative phenomenon that depends on the mechanism of spontaneous symmetry breaking rather than on the pion Compton wavelength as in periodic lattices.

## Method

The author employed the linear sigma model with an isoscalar scalar field and an isovector pseudoscalar field, which shares the same SU(2)L x SU(2)R to SU(2)V symmetry breaking pattern as QCD with two light flavors. Model parameters were fixed using phenomenological values: the pion decay constant F = 93 MeV and the sigma meson mass m_sigma = 440 MeV, giving Lambda = 2.8. The vacuum configuration was found by minimizing the action density as a functional of the sigma field subject to homogeneous Dirichlet boundary conditions in one compact direction, reducing the problem to an analytical mechanics problem solved via elliptic integrals.

## Key numbers

- Chiral symmetry is completely restored for compact direction lengths L less than 2.0 fm (derived from L < sqrt(2)*pi/m_sigma, with m_sigma = 440 MeV)
- For L greater than about 4 fm, an approximately uniform chiral condensate forms centered about the midpoint of the compact direction
- At L = 3.5 fm, the condensate is reduced by more than 25% over half the length of the compact direction, and the maximum value is 15% smaller than the infinite volume value
- The volume-averaged condensate approaches the infinite volume value with power-law corrections: <ol psi psi>_avg = <ol psi psi> * (1 - 4 log(2)/(m_sigma L) + ...), where m_sigma = 440 MeV
- The midpoint condensate approaches the infinite volume value exponentially as <ol psi psi(L/2)> = <ol psi psi> * [1 - 4 exp(-m_sigma L/2) + ...]

## Context

This work extends the study of boundary-induced phase transitions from condensed matter physics (Diehl 1996) to chiral symmetry in QCD, and complements recent work using the Gross-Neveu model to study chiral symmetry breaking and Casimir forces (Flachi 2012, 2013). The results are relevant for lattice gauge theory computations that employ homogeneous Dirichlet boundary conditions, such as studies of hadrons in external electromagnetic fields (Fiebig 1988, Lee 2005, Christensen 2004) and lattice chopping techniques (Edwards 2005, Engelhardt 2007, Hagler 2007). The finding that finite-size corrections are power law rather than exponential contrasts with the exponential finite-volume effects in periodic lattices described by chiral perturbation theory (Gasser 1987, 1988).
