# Partial-wave analysis of $\vec{p}\vec{p}\to ppπ^\circ$ data

> We present a partial-wave analysis of the polarization data for the reaction $\vec{p}\vec{p}\to ppπ^\circ$, based solely on the recent measurements at IUCF for this channel. The fit leads to a $χ^2$ per degree of freedom of 1.7. Methods for an improved analysis are discussed. We compare the extracted values to those from a meson exchange model.

## Key claim

The partial-wave analysis of the double-polarization data for $\vec{p}\vec{p}\to pp\pi^\circ$ from IUCF yields a fit with $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom of 1.7, which is not completely satisfying; this may be a consequence of the several assumptions made in the analysis, including the neglect of final-state $NN$ $D$-waves and pion $d$-waves.

## Method

The authors performed a $\chi^2$-minimization using the software Mathematica (with differential evolution, Nelder-Mead, random search, and simulated annealing) to extract 12 complex partial-wave amplitudes (with two chosen real, leaving 21 real unknowns) from 117 equations derived from the bilinear coefficients of Meyer et al. (2001). The energy dependence of the amplitudes was assumed to arise solely from centrifugal barrier factors, and the $^3P_0\to{}^1S_0,s$ amplitude ($z_1$) was extracted at each of the four bombarding energies individually. Uncertainties in the extracted amplitudes were determined from the covariance matrix of the fit.

## Key numbers

- $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom = 1.7 for the fit to the IUCF polarization data.
- 117 equations were used to determine 21 real unknowns (12 complex amplitudes, with two chosen real).
- The amplitude $z_2$ (transition $^1S_0\to{}^3P_0s$) was found to be the largest among the extracted partial-wave amplitudes.
- Bombarding energies analyzed: 325, 350, 375, and 400 MeV.
- The energy dependence of bilinears for PsPs, PsPp, and PpPp interference terms was taken as $\eta^x$ with $x=6$, $7$, and $8$ respectively, where $\eta=q_{\text{max}}/m_\pi$.

## Context

This work extends the partial-wave decomposition approach to the reaction $pp\to pp\pi^0$, using only the IUCF polarization data of Meyer et al. (2001). It directly confronts the Jülich meson-exchange model (Jülich group, 1998-2004), which had failed to reproduce the polarization observables for neutral pion production while succeeding for charged pion channels. The analysis enables future combined fits of $NN$ elastic scattering and $NN\to NN\pi$ data, as advocated by Goldberger and Watson (1964) and Hanhart (2004), and provides a quantitative testbed for effective field theory studies of near-threshold pion production.
