# Light scalar mesons and two-kaon correlation functions

> It is shown that the recent data on the $K^0_S K^+$ correlation in Pb-Pb interactions agree with the data on the $γγ\toηπ^0$ and $ϕ\toηπ^0γ$ reactions and support the four-quark model of the $a_0(980)$ meson. It is shown that the data does not contradict the validity of the Gaussian assumption. The study of two-kaon correlations could provide more information about light scalar mesons after increasing the accuracy of the experimental and theoretical descriptions.

## Key claim

The ALICE data on \(K^0_S K^+\) correlations in Pb-Pb interactions can be described simultaneously with the Belle data on \(\gamma\gamma\to\eta\pi^0\) and the KLOE data on \(\phi\to\eta\pi^0\gamma\) within the four-quark model of the \(a_0(980)\) meson, and the data do not contradict the Gaussian source assumption with correlation strength \(\lambda=1\), although the current experimental precision does not allow strict conclusions on \(\lambda\).

## Method

The authors performed a combined fit of three data sets: the Belle cross section for \(\gamma\gamma\to\eta\pi^0\), the KLOE spectrum for \(\phi\to\eta\pi^0\gamma\), and the ALICE correlation function for \(K^0_S K^+\) in Pb-Pb collisions. They used one-loop scalar propagators with good analytical properties, included the \(a_0'\) meson, and employed a Flatte-like formalism modified to incorporate the four-quark model coupling relations. Four fits were performed, varying the correlation strength \(\lambda\) and source radius \(R\), with two fits fixing \(\lambda=1\) to test the Gaussian assumption.

## Key numbers

*   \(a_0(980)\) mass: 995.1 MeV (Fit 1), 1003 MeV (Fit 2), 993.9 MeV (Fits 3 and 4).
*   Coupling constant \(g_{a_0 K^+K^-}\): 2.70 GeV (Fit 1), 2.73 GeV (Fit 2), 2.75 GeV (Fits 3 and 4).
*   Coupling constant \(g_{a_0 \eta\pi}\): 2.85 GeV (Fit 1), 2.95 GeV (Fit 2), 2.74 GeV (Fits 3 and 4).
*   Correlation strength \(\lambda\): 0.53 (Fit 1), 1.0 (Fit 2), 0.73 (Fit 3), 1.0 (Fit 4).
*   Source radius \(R\): 5.0 fm (Fit 1), 6.7 fm (Fit 2), 5.6 fm (Fit 3), 6.8 fm (Fit 4).
*   Combined \(\chi^2\) per number of degrees of freedom: 56.9/73 (Fit 1), 72.8/74 (Fit 2), 61.6/73 (Fit 3), 77.2/74 (Fit 4).
*   \(\chi^2_{corr}\) for the 29 correlation data points: 19.0 (Fit 1), 28.2 (Fit 2), 24.8 (Fit 3), 40.4 (Fit 4).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Achasov and Kiselev (2015) on the Belle and KLOE data by including the ALICE Collaboration (2017) two-kaon correlation data. It supports the four-quark model of the \(a_0(980)\) meson originally proposed by Jaffe, in contrast to the two-quark interpretation. The results enable future studies of light scalar mesons through two-kaon correlations once experimental and theoretical descriptions achieve higher accuracy, and the authors note that a similar analysis for identical kaons would require also including the \(f_0(980)\), \(f_2(1270)\), and \(\sigma(600)\) mesons.
