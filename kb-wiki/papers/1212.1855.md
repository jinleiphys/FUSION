# Quark mass dependence of the ground-state octet baryons in next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We report on a recent study of the ground-state octet baryon masses using the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with the extended-on-mass-shell renormalization scheme up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order. By adjusting the available 19 low-energy constants, a good fit of the $n_f=2+1$ lattice quantum chromodynamics results from the PACS-CS, LHPC, HSC, QCDSF-UKQCD and NPLQCD collaborations is achieved.

## Key claim

Covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N³LO) can simultaneously describe all publicly available \(n_f=2+1\) lattice QCD data from five collaborations, but only when finite-volume corrections are calculated self-consistently and the data are restricted to pion masses squared below 0.25 GeV² and \(M_\phi L > 4\).

## Method

The authors used extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalized covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory up to N³LO (order \(q^4\)) to compute the ground-state octet baryon masses. They performed a \(\chi^2\) fit of the 19 low-energy constants (LECs) to lattice QCD data from the PACS-CS, LHPC, HSC, QCDSF-UKQCD, and NPLQCD collaborations, including finite-volume corrections self-consistently. Two data sets were considered: Set-I with \(M_\pi^2 < 0.25\) GeV² and \(M_\phi L > 4\), and Set-II with \(M_\pi^2 < 0.5\) GeV², \(M_K^2 < 0.7\) GeV², and \(M_\phi L > 3\).

## Key numbers

- For the N³LO fit to lattice data Set-I and experimental octet baryon masses, \(\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 1.0\).
- For the N³LO fit to data Set-II, \(\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 1.6\).
- The baryon mass in the chiral limit from the best fit is \(m_0 = 880\) MeV.
- The NLO fit to Set-I yielded \(\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} \approx 8.6\).
- The data selection criteria for Set-I were \(M_\pi^2 < 0.25\) GeV² and \(M_\phi L > 4\) (with \(\phi = \pi, K, \eta\)); for Set-II, \(M_\pi^2 < 0.5\) GeV², \(M_K^2 < 0.7\) GeV², and \(M_\phi L > 3\).

## Context

This work extends previous baryon chiral perturbation theory studies of octet baryon masses (e.g., Jenkins 1992, Bernard 1993, Borasoy 1996, Walker-Loud 2004, Martin Camalich 2010, Young 2010, Semke 2012) by performing the first simultaneous fit of all major \(n_f=2+1\) lattice QCD data sets with self-consistent finite-volume corrections at N³LO. Unlike Young (2010) and Semke (2012), virtual decuplet contributions were not explicitly included because their effects could not be disentangled from octet contributions given the large number of unknown LECs. The successful simultaneous fit demonstrates consistency among the different lattice QCD simulations and enables future chiral extrapolations of baryon properties with controlled uncertainties.
