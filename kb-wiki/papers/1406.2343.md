# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electromagnetic transitions in 8Be with meson-exchange currents derived from chiral effective field theory

> We report quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electromagnetic transitions in 8Be. The realistic Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potentials are used to generate the ground state and nine excited states, with energies that are in excellent agreement with experiment. A dozen M1 and eight E2 transition matrix elements between these states are then evaluated. The E2 matrix elements are computed only in impulse approximation, with those transitions from broad resonant states requiring special treatment. The M1 matrix elements include two-body meson-exchange currents derived from chiral effective field theory, which typically contribute 20--30% of the total expectation value. Many of the transitions are between isospin-mixed states; the calculations are performed for isospin-pure states and then combined with empirical mixing coefficients to compare to experiment. Alternate mixings are also explored. In general, we find that transitions between states that have the same dominant spatial symmetry are in reasonable agreement with experiment, but those transitions between different spatial symmetries are often underpredicted.

## Key claim

Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electromagnetic transitions in ⁸Be, using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potentials with meson-exchange currents derived from chiral effective field theory, find that transitions between states with the same dominant spatial symmetry agree reasonably with experiment, but transitions between different spatial symmetries are often underpredicted.

## Method

The authors used variational Monte Carlo to construct trial wave functions for the ground state and nine excited states of ⁸Be, then improved them with Green's function Monte Carlo propagation in imaginary time. They evaluated a dozen M1 transition matrix elements including two-body meson-exchange currents from chiral effective field theory up to N3LO, and eight E2 transition matrix elements in impulse approximation only, with special extrapolation procedures for broad resonant states whose wave functions evolve toward separated alpha particles.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy: −56.3(1) MeV (GFMC) versus −56.50 MeV (experiment)
- Point proton radius of ground state: 2.40 fm
- (2⁺;0) state excitation: +3.2(2) MeV (GFMC) versus +3.03(1) MeV (experiment)
- (4⁺;0) state excitation: +11.2(3) MeV (GFMC) versus +11.35(15) MeV (experiment)
- (0⁺;2) state excitation: +27.7(2) MeV (GFMC) versus +27.494(2) MeV (experiment)
- E2 reduced matrix element for (2⁺;0)→(0⁺;0): 10.0(2) e fm²
- E2 reduced matrix element for (4⁺;0)→(2⁺;0): 15.6(4) e fm²
- E2 width for (4⁺;0)→(2⁺;0): 0.87(5) eV (IA) versus 0.67(7) eV (experiment)
- Two-body meson-exchange currents contribute 20–30% of total M1 expectation values
- Average squared momentum ⟨p_i²⟩ = 1.375 fm⁻² used for relativistic correction

## Context

This work extends previous quantum Monte Carlo studies of A=8 nuclei by Wiringa et al. (2000) using the older Urbana IX three-nucleon potential, and more recent calculations by Datar et al. (2013) and Wiringa et al. (2013) using the Illinois-7 potential for energies, radii, and quadrupole moments. The present calculations complement those studies by evaluating many electromagnetic transitions between low-lying states, using chiral effective field theory meson-exchange currents previously applied to A≤9 nuclei by Pastore et al. (2013). The results enable improved extraction of isospin-mixing coefficients from experimental decay widths and provide benchmarks for testing chiral effective field theory currents in a strongly clustered nucleus.
