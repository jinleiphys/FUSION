# Bayesian Inference of the Symmetry Energy of Super-Dense Neutron-Rich Matter from Future Radius Measurements of Massive Neutron Stars

> Using an explicitly isospin-dependent parametric Equation of State (EOS) for the core of neutron stars (NSs) within the Bayesian statistical approach, we infer the EOS parameters of super-dense neutron-rich nuclear matter from three sets of imagined mass-radius correlation data representing typical predictions by various nuclear many-body theories, i.e, the radius stays the same, decreases or increases with increasing NS mass within $\pm 15\%$ between 1.4 M$_{\odot}$ and 2.0 M$_{\odot}$. The corresponding average density increases quickly, slowly or slightly decreases as the NS mass increases from 1.4 M$_{\odot}$ to 2.0 M$_{\odot}$. Using the posterior probability distribution functions (PDFs) of EOS parameters inferred from GW170817 and NICER radius data for canonical NSs as references, we investigate how future radius measurements of massive NS will improve our knowledge about the EOS of super-dense neutron-rich nuclear matter, especially its symmetry energy term, compared to what people have already learned from analyzing the GW170817 and NICER data. While the EOS of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) inferred from the three data sets are approximately the same, the corresponding high-density symmetry energies at densities above about $2ρ_0$ are very different, indicating that the radii of massive NSs carry reliable information about the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy with little influence from the remaining uncertainties of the SNM EOS.

## Key claim

Future radius measurements of massive neutron stars (NSs) between 1.4 M$_{\odot}$ and 2.0 M$_{\odot}$ can tightly constrain the high-density symmetry energy above about twice the saturation density, with little influence from remaining uncertainties in the symmetric nuclear matter EOS, provided the radii are measured within a $\pm 15\%$ systematic error band.

## Method

The authors used a Bayesian statistical approach with an explicitly isospin-dependent parametric equation of state (EOS) for the core of NSs (the $npe\mu$ model), parameterizing the symmetric nuclear matter energy $E_0(\rho)$ and symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho)$ via Taylor-like expansions around saturation density $\rho_0$ with six parameters ($K_0$, $J_0$, $E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0)$, $L$, $K_{\rm{sym}}$, $J_{\rm{sym}}$). They generated three sets of imagined mass-radius correlation data (cases 1-3) representing typical predictions by nuclear many-body theories, where the radius stays constant, decreases, or increases with increasing NS mass within $\pm 15\%$ between 1.4 M$_{\odot}$ and 2.0 M$_{\odot}$, and used Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling with the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm to infer posterior probability distribution functions of the EOS parameters from these data sets, using the GW170817 and NICER radius data for canonical NSs as a reference.

## Key numbers

- Reference radius for canonical NSs: $R_{1.4} = 11.9 \pm 1.4$ km (from GW170817, assuming $M_{\rm{max}} = 1.97$ M$_{\odot}$)
- Imagined radii for case-1 (decreasing): $R_{1.6} = 11.3 \pm 1.4$ km, $R_{1.8} = 10.7 \pm 1.4$ km, $R_{2.0} = 10.2 \pm 1.4$ km
- Imagined radii for case-2 (constant): $R_{1.6} = R_{1.8} = R_{2.0} = 11.9 \pm 1.4$ km
- Imagined radii for case-3 (increasing): $R_{1.6} = 12.5 \pm 1.4$ km, $R_{1.8} = 13.1 \pm 1.4$ km, $R_{2.0} = 13.8 \pm 1.4$ km
- Prior ranges for EOS parameters: $K_0 = 220$ to $260$ MeV, $J_0 = -800$ to $400$ MeV, $K_{\rm{sym}} = -400$ to $100$ MeV, $J_{\rm{sym}} = -200$ to $800$ MeV, $L = 30$ to $90$ MeV, $E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0) = 28.5$ to $34.9$ MeV
- Fiducial values from literature: $E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0) = 31.7 \pm 3.2$ MeV, $L = 58.7 \pm 28.1$ MeV (Oertel 2017); $E_{\rm{sym}}(2\rho_0) \approx 47$ MeV (fiducial from multiple analyses)

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian inferences of the EOS from canonical NS radii (Xie and Li 2019) and direct inversions of NS radii and tidal deformability (Zhang and Li 2018, 2019) that showed canonical NS data do not constrain the symmetry energy above $2\rho_0$. It builds on the demonstration by Fattoyev (2013, 2014) that radii of NSs heavier than 1.4 M$_{\odot}$ have stronger sensitivity to high-density symmetry energy. The study uses the GW170817 radius measurement from LIGO/VIRGO (2018) and NICER data for PSR J0030+0451 (Miller 2019, Riley 2019) as reference, and investigates how future radius measurements of massive NSs (expected from NICER and proposed X-ray observatories) can break the degeneracy between symmetric matter and symmetry energy contributions at supra-saturation densities.
