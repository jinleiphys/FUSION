# Meson Production and Baryon Resonances at CLAS

> I give a brief overview of the exploration of baryon properties in meson photo- and electroproduction. These processes provide ample information for the study of electromagnetic couplings of baryon resonances and to search for states, yet to be discovered. The CLAS detector, combined with the use of energy-tagged polarized photons and polarized electrons, as well as polarized targets and the measurement of recoil polarization, provide the tools for a comprehensive nucleon resonance program. I briefly present the status of this program, prospects for the next few years, and plans for the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade.

# Meson Production and Baryon Resonances at CLAS

## Key claim

The CLAS nucleon resonance program aims to achieve complete or nearly complete measurements of meson photo- and electroproduction reactions, which can resolve ambiguities in partial wave analyses and determine transition form factors that discriminate between competing models of baryon structure; however, the interpretation of the Roper resonance as a radial excitation of the nucleon is only consistent with light-front quark models at high Q², while the low Q² behavior suggests important missing contributions from meson-baryon interactions.

## Method

The CLAS detector was used to measure differential cross sections and polarization observables for meson photoproduction and electroproduction on proton and neutron targets, including reactions such as γp → πN, ηp, K⁺Y, and γn → πN, K⁰Λ. Data were collected using energy-tagged polarized photons (circularly and linearly polarized up to 90%), polarized electron beams, and frozen spin polarized targets (FROST using butanol and HDIce using frozen HD). Transition amplitudes were extracted using two independent approaches: fixed-t dispersion relations and the unitary isobar model, applied to nearly 120,000 data points covering a large range in θπ, φπ, W, and Q².

## Key numbers

- Photon energy tagger resolution: σ(E)/E = 10⁻³
- Linearly polarized photons generated with polarization up to 90% using coherent bremsstrahlung
- The Roper resonance transverse amplitude A₁/₂ changes sign near Q² = 0.5 GeV², reaching about the same magnitude but opposite sign at Q² = 2 GeV² as at Q² = 0
- The EBAC coupled channel analysis showed the Roper resonance bare core mass of approximately 1736 MeV is shifted downward to approximately 1365 MeV by coupled channel effects
- CLAS angular coverage: polar angle range from 8° to 140°
- The Roper analysis covered the mass region from pion threshold to 1.7 GeV/c²
- Plans for CLAS12 will measure resonance transition form factors at 5 < Q² < 12 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model predictions of Isgur (1980s) and the dynamical coupled channel analyses of the EBAC group at JLab (Kamano, 2009), which demonstrated large mass shifts for the Roper resonance. The CLAS results contradict non-relativistic constituent quark model predictions for the Roper photocoupling amplitude, which has the wrong sign and predicts a strong rise at small Q² contrary to data showing a rapid drop. The measurements enable future tests of lattice QCD predictions (with pion masses of approximately 420 MeV), light-front quark models (Aznauryan, 2009), and covariant valence quark models (Ramalho, Gross), and will provide constraints for dynamically coupled-channel analyses of strangeness electroproduction.
