# Effect of anomalous magnetic moment of quarks on the phase structure and mesonic properties in the NJL model

> Employing a field dependent three-momentum cut-off regularization technique, we study the phase structure and mesonic masses using the $2$-flavour Nambu-Jona Lasinio model at finite temperature and density in presence of arbitrary external magnetic field. This approach is then applied to incorporate the effects of the anomalous magnetic moment(AMM) of quarks on constituent quark mass and thermodynamic observables as a function of temperature/baryonic density. The critical temperature for transition from chiral symmetry broken to the restored phase is observed to decrease with the external magnetic field, which can be classified as inverse magnetic catalysis, while an opposite behaviour is realized in the case of a vanishing magnetic moment, implying magnetic catalysis. These essential features are also reflected in the phase diagram. Furthermore, the properties of the low lying scalar and neutral pseudoscalar mesons are also studied in presence of a hot and dense magnetized medium including AMM of the quarks using random phase approximation. For non-zero values of magnetic field, we notice a sudden jump in the mass of the Goldstone mode at and above the Mott transition temperature which is found to decrease substantially with the increase in magnetic field when the AMM of the quarks are taken into consideration.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of quarks in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model reverses the effect of an external magnetic field on the chiral phase transition: with AMM included, the critical temperature decreases with increasing magnetic field (inverse magnetic catalysis), whereas without AMM the critical temperature increases (magnetic catalysis), contingent on the use of a field-dependent three-momentum cutoff regularization scheme.

## Method

The authors employ a two-flavor NJL model with a field-dependent three-momentum cutoff regularization to compute the thermodynamic potential and gap equation for the constituent quark mass at finite temperature, baryonic density, and arbitrary external magnetic field, including the anomalous magnetic moment of quarks. They then calculate the chiral susceptibility to locate the (pseudo-)critical temperature and use the random phase approximation (RPA) within the real-time formalism of thermal field theory to compute the masses of the scalar sigma meson and neutral pseudoscalar pion in the magnetized medium.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass at zero temperature and magnetic field: the model parameters are fixed to reproduce pion mass \( m_\pi = 135.0 \) MeV and pion decay constant \( f_\pi = 92.4 \) MeV, using cutoff \( \Lambda = 587.9 \) MeV, coupling \( G = 2.44/\Lambda^2 \), and bare quark mass \( m = 5.6 \) MeV.
* Anomalous magnetic moment values used: \( \kappa_u = 0.29 \) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \( \kappa_d = 0.36 \) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The critical temperature for the chiral transition decreases with increasing magnetic field when AMM is included (inverse magnetic catalysis), while it increases without AMM (magnetic catalysis); specific numerical values for the critical temperature shift are not provided in the truncated text.
* The Mott transition temperature for the pion decreases substantially with increasing magnetic field when AMM is included; the exact numerical values are not given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the chiral phase transition in the NJL model under magnetic fields (Klevansky 1992, Gusynin 1994, Sadooghi 2018) by incorporating the anomalous magnetic moment of quarks, which had been shown to induce inverse magnetic catalysis in nucleonic systems (Strickland 2012, Arghya 2016). The results contradict lattice QCD findings of inverse magnetic catalysis (Bali 2012) when AMM is omitted, but reproduce that behavior when AMM is included, suggesting a resolution to the discrepancy between effective model predictions and lattice data. The calculation of meson masses including AMM in a magnetized medium is presented as novel, enabling future studies of mesonic properties in heavy-ion collision conditions where strong magnetic fields are present.
