# AdS/CFT Duality User Guide

> This is the draft/updated version of a textbook on "real-world" applications of the AdS/CFT duality for beginning graduate students in particle physics and for researchers in the other fields. The aim of this book is to provide background materials such as string theory, general relativity, nuclear physics, nonequilibrium physics, and condensed-matter physics as well as some key applications of the AdS/CFT duality in a single textbook. Contents: (1) Introduction, (2) General relativity and black holes, (3) Black holes and thermodynamics, (4) Strong interaction and gauge theories, (5) The road to AdS/CFT, (6) The AdS spacetime, (7) AdS/CFT - equilibrium, (8) AdS/CFT - adding probes, (9) Basics of nonequilibrium physics, (10) AdS/CFT - nonequilibrium, (11) Other AdS spacetimes, (12) Applications to quark-gluon plasma, (13) Basics of phase transition, (14) AdS/CFT - phase transition, (15) Exercises.

## Key claim

This paper is a textbook chapter that pedagogically introduces the AdS spacetime as a constant-curvature spacetime, emphasizing that the AdS/CFT duality uses the covering space of AdS (where the timelike coordinate is unwrapped from its periodic identification) rather than the periodic AdS spacetime itself.

## Method

The chapter defines constant-curvature spaces and spacetimes through their embedding into flat ambient spacetimes of higher dimension, starting with the sphere S² and hyperbolic space H² as warm-up examples. It then presents the AdS₂ spacetime via an embedding into ℝ^{2,1} (a flat spacetime with two timelike directions), derives its metric in global, static, conformal, and Poincaré coordinate systems, and generalizes these constructions to higher-dimensional AdS_{p+2} spacetimes. The text also analyzes geodesic motion of photons and massive particles in AdS to illustrate the gravitational potential well and the need for boundary conditions at the AdS boundary.

## Key numbers

- The Ricci scalar of the sphere S² is R = 2/L², where L is the sphere radius.
- The Ricci scalar of the hyperbolic space H² is R = -2/L².
- The Ricci scalar of AdS₂ is R = -2/L².
- A photon starting from the origin reaches the AdS boundary in a finite coordinate time Δτ̃ = π/2 (in global coordinates).
- A massive particle with energy E > 1 returns to the origin after a proper time τ = π/2 and a coordinate time τ̃ = π, independent of E.
- The turning point for a massive particle is r_* = √(E² - 1).
- In Poincaré coordinates, a photon takes an infinite coordinate time t to reach r_P = 0, but a finite affine parameter time from any finite r_P = R to r_P = 0.

## Context

This chapter is part of a textbook by Natsuume (2014) that provides background for "real-world" applications of AdS/CFT duality. It extends the standard treatment of constant-curvature spaces (sphere, hyperbolic space, de Sitter spacetime) to the AdS spacetime, which is the central geometric ingredient in the AdS/CFT correspondence. The discussion of geodesic motion and the AdS boundary condition sets the stage for later chapters on equilibrium and nonequilibrium AdS/CFT applications, as well as for comparisons with AdS black hole spacetimes.
