# Infrared regularization with spin-3/2 fields

> We present a Lorentz-invariant formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory including spin-3/2 fields. Particular attention is paid to the projection on the spin-3/2 components of the delta fields. We also discuss the nucleon mass and the pion-nucleon sigma term.

# Infrared regularization with spin-3/2 fields

## Key claim

The authors extend the infrared regularization method of baryon chiral perturbation theory to include spin-3/2 (delta) fields, showing that a consistent Lorentz-invariant formulation requires explicit projection onto the spin-3/2 components of the delta propagator, and that the spin-1/2 components can be absorbed into polynomial terms. The method is claimed to allow chiral and gauge invariant inclusion of external sources, in contrast to a recent proposal by PP.

## Method

The authors separate one-loop integrals into infrared singular (I) and regular (R) parts using a Feynman parameterization technique, extending the method of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) to integrals involving the spin-projected delta propagator. They explicitly construct the spin projection operators for spin-3/2 and spin-1/2 components of the Rarita-Schwinger field, then apply the infrared regularization to the nucleon self-energy diagram with an intermediate delta, computing the mass shift and the pion-nucleon sigma term to leading (third) order in the small scale expansion.

## Key numbers

* Delta contribution to the nucleon mass shift at lowest order: 86.4 MeV (with scale lambda = nucleon mass) or 108.1 MeV (with lambda = m_delta)
* Delta contribution to the pion-nucleon sigma term at lowest order: -45.6 MeV (with lambda = nucleon mass) or -60.0 MeV (with lambda = m_delta)
* Unexpanded result with spin-3/2 propagator: mass shift 88.1 MeV (113.7 MeV with lambda = m_delta); sigma term -44.7 MeV (-61.4 MeV with lambda = m_delta)
* Input parameters: g_A = 1.267, M_pi = 139.57 MeV, F_pi = 92.4 MeV, Delta = 271 MeV, c_A = 1.34 (from SU(4) relation c_A = 3/(2*sqrt(2)) * g_A)

## Context

This work extends the infrared regularization method of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) for baryon chiral perturbation theory to include spin-3/2 fields, building on the heavy baryon approach with deltas developed by Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1998) (the "small scale expansion"). The authors note that their result for the delta loop contribution to the nucleon mass shift agrees with Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1998), and the sigma term agrees with the result of Ecker and Mojzis (2000) but differs by a polynomial contribution from the earlier work of Ecker (1994). The method enables future applications to processes where delta-pion loops are significant, such as Compton scattering.
