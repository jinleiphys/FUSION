# $η'$ production in proton-proton collisions far from the threshold

> I discuss exclusive production of the $η'$ meson in the $pp \to pη'p$ reaction far from the threshold. The contribution of diffractive component as well as that for $γ^* γ^* \to η'$ fusion are calculated. In the first case the formalism of unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDF) is used. The distributions in the Feynman $x_F$ (or rapidity), transferred four-momenta squared between initial and final protons ($t_1$, $t_2$) and azimuthal angle difference between outgoing protons ($Φ$) are calculated. The deviations from the $\sin^2(Φ)$ dependence predicted by one-step vector-vector-pseudoscalar coupling are quantified and discussed. The results are compared with the results of the WA102 collaboration at CERN. Most of the models of UGDF from the literature give too small cross section as compared to the WA102 data and predict angular distribution in relative azimuthal angle strongly asymmetric with respect to $π/2$ in disagreement with the WA102 data. This points to a different mechanism at the WA102 energy. Predictions for RHIC, Tevatron and LHC are given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that QCD-inspired models using unintegrated gluon distribution functions (UGDFs) produce cross sections for exclusive eta-prime production in proton-proton collisions that are too small compared to WA102 data at W = 29.1 GeV, and predict azimuthal angular distributions that disagree with the measured nearly perfect sin-squared dependence, suggesting a different mechanism dominates at that energy.

## Method

The author calculates the exclusive pp to p eta-prime p reaction using two mechanisms: a diffractive component described by the Kaidalov-Khoze-Martin-Ryskin formalism with skewed unintegrated gluon distribution functions, and a photon-photon fusion component. The bare QCD amplitude for the diffractive mechanism involves an integral over gluon transverse momentum with a vertex function coupling two virtual gluons to the pseudoscalar meson, and the off-diagonal UGDFs are approximated as geometric means of diagonal UGDFs multiplied by Dirac form factors. Predictions are made for RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section as a function of center-of-mass energy is shown for W values above 10 GeV, with WA102 data at W = 29.1 GeV used for comparison.
* Differential cross section d sigma / dx_F at W = 29.1 GeV is compared to WA102 experimental data for different UGDF models.
* Differential cross section d sigma / dt_{1/2} at W = 29.1 GeV is shown as a function of four-momentum transfer squared for different UGDFs.
* Differential cross section d sigma / dPhi at W = 29.1 GeV is shown as a function of azimuthal angle difference between outgoing protons, with the photon-photon fusion contribution producing a symmetric distribution around 90 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the Kaidalov-Khoze-Martin-Ryskin formalism (KMR) originally developed for exclusive Higgs production to the production of the eta-prime meson, and contrasts with earlier studies of eta-prime production near threshold at COSY (COSY11) and Saclay (DISTO) where meson exchange currents and S11 resonance mechanisms dominate. The results contradict the Regge-inspired pomeron-pomeron fusion interpretation of WA102 data by Szczurek and collaborators (1999) and point to the need for a different mechanism at WA102 energies, while providing predictions for future measurements at RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC.
