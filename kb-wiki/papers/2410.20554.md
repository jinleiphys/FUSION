# Revisiting $KN$ elastic and charge exchange reactions in search of the pentaquark $Θ^+$ baryon

> In the search for the pentaquark $Θ^+$ baryon, the world data on $K^+n\to K^+n$ and $K^+n\to K^0\,p$ reactions on the deuteron target and $K^0\,p\to K^+n$ and $K^0_L\,p\to K^0_S\,p$ reactions on the hydrogen target are revisited to study the isoscalar component of the scattering amplitudes. The determination of the $s$- and $p$-wave phase shifts for the two former channels on the deuteron target is presented in the low momentum region with the uncertainty due to the deuteron form factors at forward angles discussed. For the reactions on hydrogen targets, a similarity expected from time reversal between the charge exchange processes $K^+n\to K^0\,p$ and $K^0\,p\to K^+n$ is exploited, while we present the analysis of the $K_L^0\,p\to K_S^0\,p$ scattering based on the $s$-wave phase shift for low momenta combined with the $t$-channel $ρ^0(775)+ω(782)+ϕ(1020)$ vector meson exchange at high momenta. With the mass 1535 MeV and decay width 5 MeV the possibility of the $Θ^+$ in the Breit-Wigner (BW) form is tested in the differential cross section measured by C. J. S. Damerell {\it et\ al.} at ${\rm P_{Lab}}=434$ MeV/$c$ and polarization. Our analysis suggests measuring the polarization observable rather than the differential cross section, which has sufficient sensitivity to distinguish the $Θ^+$ baryon among all spin and parity states $J^P = 1/2^\pm$ and $3/2^\pm$.

# Revisiting $KN$ elastic and charge exchange reactions in search of the pentaquark $\Theta^+$ baryon

## Key claim

The paper suggests that measuring polarization observables, rather than differential cross sections, in $K^+n$ scattering at ${\rm P_{Lab}}=434$ MeV/$c$ has sufficient sensitivity to distinguish the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark among all spin and parity states $J^P = 1/2^\pm$ and $3/2^\pm$, but this conclusion is conditional on the assumed $\Theta^+$ mass of 1535 MeV and decay width of 5 MeV in the Breit-Wigner form, and the analysis is limited by uncertainties from deuteron form factors at forward angles.

## Method

The authors performed a partial wave analysis of $K^+n\to K^+n$ and $K^+n\to K^0p$ reactions on deuteron targets, determining $s$- and $p$-wave phase shifts for the isoscalar amplitude after fixing the isovector amplitude from $K^+p\to K^+p$ elastic scattering. For $K^0p\to K^+n$ and $K^0_Lp\to K^0_Sp$ reactions on hydrogen targets, they exploited time-reversal symmetry between charge exchange channels and modeled the $K^0_Lp\to K^0_Sp$ scattering using an $s$-wave phase shift at low momenta combined with $t$-channel $\rho^0(775)+\omega(782)+\phi(1020)$ vector meson exchange at high momenta. The $\Theta^+$ baryon was tested in Breit-Wigner form against differential cross section data from Damerell et al. at ${\rm P_{Lab}}=434$ MeV/$c$ and polarization data.

## Key numbers

- $\Theta^+$ baryon mass assumed: 1535 MeV, decay width: 5 MeV (chosen to be consistent with experiments)
- Kaon laboratory momentum for the primary test: ${\rm P_{Lab}}=434$ MeV/$c$
- Phase shift for isovector $S_{11}$ amplitude: $\delta^1_{0+}(k)=a_0+b_0 k$ with $a_0=3$ and $b_0=-107$ GeV$^{-1}$, giving a negative phase shift consistent with repulsive $K^+p$ interaction
- Cutoff mass for deuteron form factor calculations: $\Lambda=200$ MeV
- Coupling constants for Regge pole exchange: $g_{\rho KK}=2.975$, $g_{\omega KK}=0.09$, $g_{\phi KK}=2.23$, and $g^{v}_{\phi NN}=-3.8$
- The $\Theta^+$ peak in total cross section is about 2 mb for spin parity $1/2^+$ at ${\rm P_{Lab}}=434$ MeV/$c$

## Context

This work revisits $KN$ scattering experiments from the 1970s by Damerell et al. and Giacomelli et al. in light of the theoretical prediction by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov for a pentaquark $\Theta^+$ baryon with mass around 1530 MeV from the chiral soliton picture. The analysis extends the authors' previous study of $\Theta^+$ in $K^+n$ scattering to include $K^0p$ channels, and addresses the negative results from high-statistics JLab experiments that failed to confirm the pentaquark. The work enables future searches at J-PARC (proposed by Sekihara and Kahn) and the KLF experiment at JLab (planned by the Hall D Collaboration) by providing a theoretical framework that identifies polarization as the key observable to distinguish $\Theta^+$ spin-parity states.
