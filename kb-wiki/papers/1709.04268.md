# Strong and radiative decays of the low-lying $S$- and $P$-wave singly heavy baryons

> The strong and radiative decays of the low-lying $S$- and $P$-wave $Λ_{c(b)}$, $Σ_{c(b)}$, $Ξ_{c(b)}$, $Ξ_{c(b)}'$ and $Ω_{c(b)}$ baryons are systematically studied in a constituent quark model. We find that the radiative decay mode $Λ_b^0γ$ could be very useful for us to establish the missing neutral states $Σ_b^{0}$ and $Σ_b^{*0}$. Our calculation shows that most of those missing $λ$-mode $P$-wave singly heavy baryons have relatively narrow decay width of less than 30 MeV. Their dominant strong and radiative decay channels can be ideal for searching for their signals in future experiment. Configuration mixings between the $J^P=1/2^-$ states $|^2P_λ \frac{1}{2}^- \rangle$ and $|^4P_λ \frac{1}{2}^- \rangle$ of the $\mathbf{6}_F$ multiplet are discussed. The $Ξ_c(2930)^0$ state seems to favor the mixed state $|Ξ_c'~ P_λ\frac{1}{2}^-\rangle_2$ with $J^P=1/2^-$. The $Σ_c(2800)$ resonance may be assigned to $|Σ_c~ ^2P_λ \frac{3}{2}^- \rangle$ with $J^P=3/2^-$ or $|Σ_c~ ^4P_λ \frac{5}{2}^- \rangle$ with $J^P=5/2^-$. In general, the excitations of $|^2P_λ \frac{3}{2}^- \rangle$ and $|^4P_λ \frac{5}{2}^- \rangle$ of the $\mathbf{6}_F$ multiplet have similar strong decay properties. In order to to identify them, angular distributions of their decays in either strong decay modes or radiative transitions should be needed.

## Key claim

The authors systematically calculate strong and radiative decay widths for low-lying S- and P-wave singly heavy baryons using a constituent quark model, finding that most missing λ-mode P-wave states have narrow decay widths (less than 30 MeV) and that the radiative decay mode Λb0γ could be particularly useful for establishing the unobserved neutral states Σb0 and Σb*0. The assignment of Σc(2800) remains ambiguous, favoring either J^P=3/2^- or J^P=5/2^-, and the Ξc(2930)0 state appears to favor a mixed 1/2^- configuration.

## Method

The authors apply the chiral quark model (ChQM) to compute strong decay widths, using an effective chiral Lagrangian for quark-pseudoscalar-meson coupling with π, K, and η treated as Goldstone bosons, and harmonic oscillator spatial wave functions for the baryons. Radiative decay widths are computed using a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with a quark-photon electromagnetic coupling operator. The calculations assume λ-mode excitations only, with constituent quark masses mu=md=330 MeV, ms=450 MeV, mc=1480 MeV, mb=5000 MeV, and oscillator parameters αρ=400, 420, 440 MeV for different diquark systems.

## Key numbers

- Most missing λ-mode P-wave singly heavy baryons have total decay widths less than 30 MeV.
- For the radiative decay Λc(2593) 1/2^- → Λc+ γ, the predicted width is 0.26 keV (λ-mode assumption).
- For the radiative decay Λc(2625) 3/2^- → Λc+ γ, the predicted width is 0.30 keV (λ-mode assumption).
- For the radiative decay Ξc0(2790) 1/2^- → Ξc0 γ, the predicted width is 263 keV (λ-mode assumption).
- For the radiative decay Ξc0(2815) 3/2^- → Ξc0 γ, the predicted width is 292 keV (λ-mode assumption).
- For the radiative decay Λb(5912) 1/2^- → Λb0 γ, the predicted width is 50.2 keV (λ-mode assumption).
- The global parameter δ for quark-meson coupling strength is fixed at 0.557.
- The decay constants used are fπ = 132 MeV and fK = 160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous quark model studies of heavy baryon decays, such as those by Ebert (2011), Yoshida (2015), and Chen (2016), by providing systematic predictions for both strong and radiative decays of P-wave states in the sextet (6F) multiplet, which have not been experimentally established. The calculations enable future experimental searches at LHCb and Belle II by identifying dominant decay channels and narrow-width candidates, and they address the need for theoretical guidance on the quantum number assignments of states like Σc(2800) and Ξc(2930).
