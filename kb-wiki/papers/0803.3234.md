# Comparing energy loss and $p_{\perp}$-broadening in perturbative QCD with strong coupling $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM theory

> We compare medium induced energy loss and $p_{\perp}$-broadening in perturbative QCD with that of the trailing string picture of SYM theory. We consider finite and infinite extent matter as well as relativistic heavy quarks which correspond to those being produced in the medium or external to it. When expressed in terms of the appropriate saturation momentum, we find identical parametric forms for energy loss in perturbative QCD and SYM theory. We find simple correspondences between $p_{\perp}$-broadening in QCD and in SYM theory although $p_{\perp}$-broadening is radiation dominated in SYM theory and multiple scattering dominated in perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

When expressed in terms of the appropriate saturation momentum \(Q_s\), the parametric forms for medium-induced energy loss of a heavy quark in perturbative QCD and in strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory are identical, with the replacement \(\alpha N_c \leftrightarrow \sqrt{\lambda}/(2\pi)\); however, for \(p_\perp\)-broadening the SYM result is parametrically identical to a subdominant radiative term in QCD which becomes dominant in the strong-coupling limit.

## Method

The authors compare the standard perturbative QCD picture of gluon radiation from a heavy quark's wavefunction (where gluons with transverse momentum \(k_\perp \le Q_s\) are freed by the medium) with the trailing string description from AdS/CFT for a heavy quark moving at constant rapidity through a hot \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM plasma. For finite matter, they extend the comparison to both dressed quarks (fully developed gluon cloud) and bare quarks (produced without a cloud, e.g., from a hard collision or from accelerating a quark-antiquark pair with an external electric field). The analysis uses the saturation momentum \(Q_s\) as the unifying scale, with \(Q_s^2 \sim \hat{q} t_c\) in QCD and \(Q_s^2 \sim (t_c T^2)^2\) in SYM theory.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss rate in infinite matter: \(-\frac{dE}{dt} \propto \alpha_s N_c Q_s^2\) for QCD and \(-\frac{dE}{dt} \sim \sqrt{\lambda} Q_s^2\) for SYM, where \(Q_s^3 = \hat{q} \cosh\eta\) in QCD and \(Q_s^2 \sim T^2 \cosh\eta\) in SYM (Eqs. (eloss2), (eloss4), (qs), (qseta)).
- SYM energy loss rate from trailing string: \(-\frac{dE}{dt} = \frac{\pi \sqrt{\lambda}}{2} T^2 v^2 \cosh\eta \simeq \frac{\pi \sqrt{\lambda}}{2} T^2 \cosh\eta\) for \(\cosh\eta \gg 1\) (Eq. (eloss3)).
- SYM transverse momentum broadening in infinite matter: \(\frac{dp_\perp^2}{dt} = 2\pi \sqrt{\lambda} T^3 \sqrt{\cosh\eta}\) (Eq. (ptb4)).
- For finite matter of length \(L\) with dressed quarks and \(\cosh\eta \gg (LT)^2\): energy loss \(-\frac{dE}{dt} \propto \binom{\alpha N_c}{\sqrt{\lambda}} \frac{Q_s \cosh\eta}{L}\) and \(p_\perp\)-broadening \(\frac{dp_\perp^2}{dt} \propto \binom{1}{\sqrt{\lambda}} \frac{Q_s^2}{L}\), with \(Q_s \sim L T^2\) for SYM (Eqs. (eloss6), (ptb7)).
- The separation point on the trailing string is \(u_s = \pi T \sqrt{\cosh\eta}\) (Eq. (qs2)).
- For a bare heavy quark accelerated by an electric field \(E_f\), the radiated power in vacuum is \(P_{\text{radiated}} = \frac{\sqrt{\lambda}}{2\pi} \frac{E_f^2}{M^2}\) (Eq. (power2)).

## Context

This work extends the comparison of medium-induced energy loss and \(p_\perp\)-broadening between perturbative QCD and strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory, building on the trailing string picture of Herzog (2006) and Gubser (2006) and the saturation momentum approach of Hatta (2007). It provides a unified parametric description in terms of \(Q_s\) for both infinite and finite matter, including dressed and bare heavy quarks. The results enable further study of how formation times and the distinction between bare and dressed quarks affect energy loss in SYM plasmas, and they suggest that the SYM plasma can serve as a benchmark for understanding the relative roles of multiple scattering versus radiative broadening in QCD.
