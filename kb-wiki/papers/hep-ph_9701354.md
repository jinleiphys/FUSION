# Production of energetic dileptons with small invariant masses from the quark-gluon plasma

> The resummation technique of Braaten and Pisarski is used for a complete calculation of the $α^2 α_s$-contribution to the production rate of dileptons with energy E >> T and invariant mass M <~ T from a quark-gluon plasma. In particular the cancellation of the infrared singularity by medium effects is discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The authors use the Braaten-Pisarski resummation technique to perform a complete calculation of the order $\alpha^2\alpha_s$ contribution to the dilepton production rate from a quark-gluon plasma, finding that the effective quark mass $m_q^*$ (not the invariant photon mass $M$) serves as the infrared cutoff, but only under the condition that the exchanged quark's energy and momentum are individually soft rather than using a covariant separation scale.

## Method

The authors compute the dilepton production rate by relating the matrix elements to the imaginary part of the photon self energy, separating the calculation into hard and soft momentum regions using a separation scale $k_c$ with $gT \ll k_c \ll T$. For soft momenta, they use a resummed quark propagator containing the effective quark mass $m_q^* = gT/\sqrt{6}$, while for hard momenta they use bare propagators, then add both contributions to obtain a separation-scale-independent result. The real contribution comes from the continuum part of the spectral function below the light cone, while the virtual contribution comes from the pole part corresponding to collective quark modes.

## Key numbers

- The effective quark mass is given by ${m_q^*}^2 = g^2 T^2/6 = 2\pi \alpha_s T^2/3$
- For the soft real contribution, the function $A(E-p)$ ranges from $A(E-p=0) = -1.31$ to $A(E-p=m_q^*) = -1.71$
- For the virtual contribution, the function $B(E-p)$ ranges from $B(E-p=0) = 0$ to $B(E-p=m_q^*) = -0.66$
- The final rate contains a function $C$ ranging from $C(E-p=0) = -0.73$ to $C(E-p=m_q^*) = -0.47$
- The approximate analytic expression for $k_{min}^+$ is $k_{min}^+ = |E{m_q^*}^2/M^2 - M^2/(4E)|$
- Example numerical results are shown for $\alpha_s = 0.3$, $T = 300$ MeV, with $M = 300$ MeV and $E = 3$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Altherr and Ruuskanen (1992) on dilepton production from a quark-gluon plasma, who suggested that the effective quark mass could replace the invariant photon mass as an infrared cutoff when $M < m_q^*$. The authors apply the Braaten-Pisarski resummation technique, previously used for photon production by Kapusta, Lichard, and Seibert (1991) and Baier et al. (1992), to provide a complete gauge-invariant calculation of the order $\alpha^2\alpha_s$ contribution. The result enables more reliable predictions for dilepton yields as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation in relativistic heavy ion collisions, though the authors caution that extrapolation to realistic coupling constants ($\alpha_s = 0.2$ to $0.5$) may lead to unphysical negative rates if the logarithmic approximation is insufficient.
