# Pseudorapidity shape of elliptic flow as signature for fast equilibration in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at energies up to sqrt(s) = 200 GeV

> The implications of parton recombination processes on the dynamics of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion reactions are investigated. To do so, the quark-gluon string transport model has been extended for partonic recombination and fusion processes. Parton recombination leads to short equilibration times and improves significantly on the theoretical description of measured directed and elliptic flow, i.e., v_1 and v_2, distributions in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, in particular what concerns their pseudorapidity dependence. The shape of v_2(eta) is found to be closely related to fast thermalization.

## Key claim

The shape of the pseudorapidity distribution of elliptic flow \(v_2(\eta)\) is closely related to fast thermalization, and parton recombination processes are necessary to reproduce the experimentally observed peak at midrapidity and steady decrease at larger pseudorapidity, which the standard quark-gluon string model (QGSM) without recombination fails to describe.

## Method

The authors extended the Monte Carlo quark-gluon string model (QGSM) by implementing partonic recombination and fusion processes based on parton coalescence models. Hadrons are allowed to decompose into constituent partons and recombine into new hadronic states when their spatial distance in the center-of-mass frame is less than \(\Delta_x = 0.85\) fm, with a momentum-space coalescence condition given by the uncertainty principle \(\Delta_x \Delta_p = 1\). The procedure includes three-meson fusion into baryon-antibaryon pairs and quark-antiquark annihilation, with the annihilation probability adjusted to reproduce experimental charged hadron multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time in the central cell of Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV is reduced by approximately a factor of 5, from \(\sim 10\) fm/c (standard QGSM) to \(\sim 2\) fm/c (with parton recombination).
- The spatial coalescence radius for parton recombination is \(\Delta_x = 0.85\) fm, geometrically related to a maximum cross section of about 23 mb.
- The probability of the annihilation process \(3M \rightarrow 2M\) relative to \(3M \rightarrow 3M\) is set to \(P_{\rm a} = 0.04\).
- The high-momentum cutoff for recombination processes \(3H \rightarrow 3H\) is \(\Delta_{p,{\rm max}} = m_1 + m_2\), and for the fusion process \(3M \rightarrow B\bar{B}\) it is \(\Delta_{p,{\rm max}} = m_1 + m_2 + m_3 + 0.2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard QGSM (Burau 2004) which described bulk elliptic flow but produced a double-bump structure in \(v_2(\eta)\) not seen in PHOBOS data (Back 2004). The inclusion of parton recombination resolves this discrepancy and provides a microscopic model for the dynamics of a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP). The results are complementary to hydrodynamics+cascade hybrid approaches (Hirano 2005) and consistent with the AMPT model (Chen 2004), supporting the interpretation that the matter produced at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is dominated by partons and reaches thermal equilibrium on a very short time scale.
