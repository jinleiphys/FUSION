# High energy photons from passage of jets through quark gluon plasma

> We calculate the production of high energy photons from Compton scattering and annihilation of a quark jet passing through a quark gluon plasma produced in a relativistic heavy ion collision. The contributions are large and reflect the momentum distribution of the jets and the initial conditions of the plasma.

## Key claim

The authors propose that photons produced when high-energy quark jets pass through a quark-gluon plasma (via Compton scattering with thermal gluons and annihilation with thermal antiquarks) constitute a new, large source of direct photons in relativistic heavy ion collisions; this contribution is at least comparable to other direct photon sources and dominates for transverse momenta \(p_\perp \le 6\) GeV in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but the calculation relies on an approximation that the infrared divergence in the emission rate can be handled by replacing the quark mass with a thermal mass, an assumption the authors note remains to be verified.

## Method

The authors calculate the photon production rate from jet-photon conversion by inserting a decomposition of the parton phase-space distribution into a thermal component and a hard jet component (from perturbative QCD) into the Born-level expressions for annihilation and Compton scattering, then integrating over the space-time evolution of a thermally and chemically equilibrated, longitudinally expanding QGP. They use CTEQ5L parton distributions with EKS98 nuclear modifications, a K-factor of 2.5 for jet production, and model the plasma with initial temperatures and times fixed by measured particle rapidity densities for Au+Au at RHIC and Pb+Pb at LHC.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at \(\sqrt{S_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV (RHIC), the jet-photon conversion dominates direct photon production up to \(p_\perp \approx 6\) GeV.
- Initial conditions for RHIC: \(T_0 = 446\) MeV and \(\tau_0 = 0.147\) fm/\(c\); for LHC: \(T_0 = 897\) MeV and \(\tau_0 = 0.073\) fm/\(c\).
- The photon yield from jet-photon conversion is reduced by only about 40 percent when \(\tau_0\) is increased to 1 fm/\(c\) while keeping \(T_0^3 \tau_0\) fixed.
- Minijet distribution parameters at \(y=0\) for RHIC: quarks \(a = 5.0 \times 10^2\) /GeV\(^2\), \(b = 1.6\) GeV, \(c = 7.9\); antiquarks \(a = 1.3 \times 10^2\) /GeV\(^2\), \(b = 1.9\) GeV, \(c = 8.9\) (valid for 2 GeV/\(c < p_\perp < 20\) GeV/\(c\)).
- Minijet distribution parameters at \(y=0\) for LHC: quarks \(a = 1.4 \times 10^4\) /GeV\(^2\), \(b = 0.61\) GeV, \(c = 5.3\); antiquarks \(a = 1.4 \times 10^5\) /GeV\(^2\), \(b = 0.32\) GeV, \(c = 5.2\) (valid for 2 GeV/\(c < p_\perp < 20\) GeV/\(c\)).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of direct photon sources in heavy ion collisions, which included thermal photons from the QGP (Kapusta et al. 1991, Baier et al. 1992) and from hadronic gas (Xiong et al. 1992), as well as photons from initial hard scatterings (Owens 1987) and pre-equilibrium production (Strickland 1994). The proposed jet-photon conversion mechanism is distinct from the back-to-back photon-hadron correlation proposed by Wang et al. (1996) for measuring jet energy loss; the authors argue that comparing the photon spectrum (which reflects the initial jet distribution before energy loss) with the measured high-\(p_\perp\) hadron spectrum (which reflects jets after energy loss) could provide a quantitative determination of jet quenching and the energy loss mechanism described by Gyulassy and Wang (1994) and Baier et al. (1997).
