# Magnetic field-dependent electric charge transport in hadronic medium at finite temperature

> Electric charge transport of hadronic matter at finite temperature and magnetic field is studied within the linear sigma model. Anisotropic transport coefficients associated with the charge transport are estimated both in the weak and strong regimes of the magnetic field using the transport theory approach. In a weakly magnetized medium, the magnetic field effects are incorporated through the Lorentz force term in the Boltzmann equation. Strong magnetic field puts further constraints on the motion of charged particles through Landau quantization. Magnetic field-dependent thermal relaxation time is obtained from interaction rates of hadrons with the S-matrix approach by considering the Landau level kinematics of the charged hadrons. Mean-field effects are embedded in the analysis through the temperature-dependent hadron masses. Further, the hadronic medium response to a time-varying external electric field is studied in weak and strong magnetic field regimes. It is seen that electromagnetic responses of the hadronic matter have a strong dependence on the mean-field effects, sigma mass, the strength of the external fields, and its evolution in the medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the electromagnetic response of hadronic matter has a strong dependence on mean-field effects, sigma mass, the strength of external fields, and their evolution in the medium, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of the linear sigma model with temperature-dependent hadron masses and the relaxation time approximation for transport theory.

## Method

The authors employed the linear sigma model (LSM) to describe hadronic matter and solved the Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation to obtain anisotropic electrical conductivities. In the weak magnetic field regime (qB << T²), magnetic field effects were incorporated through the Lorentz force term in the Boltzmann equation, while in the strong field regime (qB >> T²), Landau level quantization was imposed with charged pions restricted to the lowest Landau level. Magnetic field-dependent thermal relaxation times were computed from hadron interaction rates using the S-matrix approach with Landau level kinematics for charged hadrons, and mean-field effects were embedded through temperature-dependent hadron masses.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV
- Vacuum pion mass: m_π = 140 MeV
- Sigma mass values considered: m_σ = {400, 500, 700} MeV
- Strong magnetic field regime defined as qB >> T²; weak field regime as qB << T²
- Magnetic field magnitude at RHIC: ~10¹⁸ G; at LHC: ~10¹⁹ G (cited from prior work)
- Landau level energy dispersion for charged bosons: E_n = sqrt(p_z² + m² + (2n+1)qB)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of electrical conductivity in the QCD medium using the Kubo formalism (Feng 2017, Hattori 2016), lattice QCD (Buividovich 2010), holographic models (Pu 2014), and transport theory (Satow 2014, Gorbar 2016) by applying the linear sigma model to the hadronic phase for the first time. The analysis builds on earlier LSM studies of transport coefficients within relaxation time approximation (Chakraborty 2010) and functional variational approaches (Heffernan 2020), and enables future magnetohydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions that require magnetic field-dependent conductivities as input parameters.
