# Potentials for soft wall AdS/QCD

> Soft-wall models in AdS/QCD generally have dilaton and scalar fields that vary with the fifth-dimension coordinate. These fields can be parameterized to yield hadron mass spectra with linear radial trajectories and to incorporate spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry. We show how to construct scalar potentials which lead to such solutions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate how to construct scalar potentials in soft-wall AdS/QCD models that yield phenomenologically desirable background field profiles (quadratic dilaton and linear scalar field at large z) while keeping the dilaton mass arbitrary, in contrast to prior work where the dilaton mass was fixed at a value not corresponding to any local gauge invariant operator in QCD.

## Method

The authors start from a five-dimensional gravity action in the Einstein frame with two scalar fields (dilaton φ and chiral field χ) and a scalar potential V(φ,χ). They impose that the string frame metric is exactly AdS₅ and that the dilaton behaves as φ ~ z² at large z to produce linear radial trajectories. Using the Einstein equations and scalar field equations, they solve for the potential that generates power-law profiles χ(z) = χ₀ zⁿ and φ(z) proportional to z²ⁿ, then extend the method to general parameterizations by expressing the potential in terms of the inverse function z(χ) and computing two integral functions ξ₁(χ) and ξ₂(χ).

## Key numbers

- The constant relating string and Einstein frames is fixed to a = 1/√6, the only value allowing simultaneous quadratic dilaton and linear scalar field at large z
- For the scalar field χ dual to the quark bilinear operator, the mass must satisfy m_χ² L² = -3, which restricts the power-law exponent to n = 1 or n = 3
- The dilaton mass squared (m_φ L)² is left arbitrary in the general potential, with the Breitenlohner-Freedman stability bound requiring m_φ² L² ≥ -4
- The cosmological constant term in the potential is -12/L², with the potential taking the form V(φ,χ) = e^{2φ/√6} Ṽ(φ,χ)/L²

## Context

This work bridges the gap between the dynamical soft-wall model of Batell and Gherghetta (2008), which derived a potential but fixed the dilaton mass at m_φ² L² = -4, and phenomenological models of chiral symmetry breaking such as Gherghetta (2009) that allow independent variation of quark mass and chiral condensate. The authors provide a general methodology to compute potentials for arbitrary parameterizations of the dilaton and scalar fields, enabling future work on perturbations to the geometry for computing transport coefficients and potentially connecting bottom-up AdS/QCD models to top-down string theory approaches.
