# The relativistic Feynman-Metropolis-Teller theory for white-dwarfs in general relativity

> The recent formulation of the relativistic Thomas-Fermi model within the Feynman-Metropolis-Teller theory for compressed atoms is applied to the study of general relativistic white dwarf equilibrium configurations. The equation of state, which takes into account the β-equilibrium, the nuclear and the Coulomb interactions between the nuclei and the surrounding electrons, is obtained as a function of the compression by considering each atom constrained in a Wigner-Seitz cell. The contribution of quantum statistics, weak, nuclear, and electromagnetic interactions is obtained by the determination of the chemical potential of the Wigner-Seitz cell. The further contribution of the general relativistic equilibrium of white dwarf matter is expressed by the simple formula $\sqrt{g_{00}}μ_{\rm ws}$= constant, which links the chemical potential of the Wigner-Seitz cell $μ_{\rm ws}$ with the general relativistic gravitational potential $g_{00}$ at each point of the configuration. The configuration outside each Wigner-Seitz cell is strictly neutral and therefore no global electric field is necessary to warranty the equilibrium of the white dwarf. These equations modify the ones used by Chandrasekhar by taking into due account the Coulomb interaction between the nuclei and the electrons as well as inverse β-decay. They also generalize the work of Salpeter by considering a unified self-consistent approach to the Coulomb interaction in each Wigner-Seitz cell. The consequences on the numerical value of the Chandrasekhar-Landau mass limit as well as on the mass-radius relation of $^4$He, $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O and $^{56}$Fe white dwarfs are presented. All these effects should be taken into account in processes requiring a precision knowledge of the white dwarf parameters.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the equilibrium of white dwarfs in general relativity, incorporating weak, electromagnetic, and gravitational interactions along with quantum statistics, reduces to the simple condition \(\sqrt{g_{00}}\mu_{\rm ws}={\rm constant}\), where \(\mu_{\rm ws}\) is the chemical potential of the Wigner-Seitz cell obtained from the relativistic Feynman-Metropolis-Teller model, and that the configuration outside each cell is strictly neutral with no global electric field, contradicting earlier claims by Olson and Bailyn (1975, 1976) that such fields were necessary.

## Method

The authors apply the recently formulated relativistic Thomas-Fermi model within the Feynman-Metropolis-Teller theory for compressed atoms to white dwarf equilibrium configurations. Each atom is constrained in a Wigner-Seitz cell, and the equation of state is obtained as a function of compression by solving the relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation numerically, incorporating beta-equilibrium, nuclear and Coulomb interactions, and a finite-sized nucleus with experimental mass and charge values. The general relativistic equilibrium is then determined by integrating the Einstein equations using the condition \(\sqrt{g_{00}}\mu_{\rm ws}={\rm constant}\), which links the cell chemical potential to the gravitational potential at each point.

## Key numbers

- The Chandrasekhar-Landau critical mass is given by \(M^{\rm Ch-L}_{\rm crit} = 2.015 \frac{\sqrt{3 \pi}}{2} \frac{M_{\rm Pl}^3}{\mu^2 m^2_n} \approx 3.09\frac{M_{\rm Pl}^3}{\mu^2 m^2_n}\), where \(M_{\rm Pl} = \sqrt{\hbar c/G} \approx 10^{-5}\) g is the Planck mass and \(m_n\) is the neutron mass.
- The Stoner critical mass is \(M^{\rm Stoner}_{\rm crit} = \frac{15}{16} \sqrt{5 \pi} \frac{M_{\rm Pl}^3}{\mu^2 m^2_n} \approx 3.72\frac{M_{\rm Pl}^3}{\mu^2 m^2_n}\).
- The Hamada and Salpeter critical mass is \(M^{\rm H\&S}_{\rm crit} = 2.015 \frac{\sqrt{3 \pi}}{2} \frac{1}{\mu^2_{\rm eff}} \frac{M_{\rm Pl}^3}{m^2_n}\), where \(\mu_{\rm eff} = \mu \left(\frac{P_{\rm S}}{P_{\rm Ch}}\right)^{-3/4}\) and \(P_{\rm S}/P_{\rm Ch} < 1\).
- The unified atomic mass is \(M_u = 1.6604\times 10^{-24}\) g.
- The nuclear radius is given by \(R_c = \Delta \lambda_\pi Z^{1/3}\), where \(\lambda_{\pi} = \hbar/(m_\pi c)\) is the pion Compton wavelength and \(\Delta \approx (r_0/\lambda_\pi)(A/Z)^{1/3}\) with \(r_0 \approx 1.2\) fm; for ordinary nuclei with \(A/Z \approx 2\), \(\Delta \approx 1\).

## Context

This work extends the classic treatments of Chandrasekhar (1931) and Landau (1932) by incorporating Coulomb interactions and inverse beta-decay that were neglected in their free-electron gas models, and generalizes the approach of Salpeter (1961) by providing a unified self-consistent solution of the relativistic Thomas-Fermi equation within each Wigner-Seitz cell. The results modify the mass-radius relations for white dwarfs composed of \({}^{4}\)He, \({}^{12}\)C, \({}^{16}\)O, and \({}^{56}\)Fe, and enable precision calculations needed for processes requiring accurate white dwarf parameters, while also establishing a framework applicable to neutron star crusts.
