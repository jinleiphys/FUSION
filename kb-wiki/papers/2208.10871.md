# Probing system size dependence at high baryon density by systematic comparison of Ag+Ag and Au+Au reactions at 1.23$A$ GeV

> We present UrQMD predictions for the comparison of the recently measured Ag+Ag and Au+Au runs at a beam energy of $E_\mathrm{lab}$ = 1.23~$A$GeV explored by the HADES experiment. To this aim, different centrality definitions are investigated: We suggest that in the case of particle production, both systems should be compared at the same number of participating nucleons, while for a comparison of the (elliptic) flow, a selection on the initial state eccentricity - as in high energy reaction - is better suited. Generally, we find good agreement between both system, if these centrality criteria are used. Specifically, the deuteron yields per participant and the pion to proton ratios are shown to scale with $A_\mathrm{part}$ except for very central Ag+Ag reactions due to stronger stopping in such reactions. The elliptic flow in both systems follows inital state eccentricity scaling, albeit with the opposite sign as compared to high energies, suggesting a strong relation between final flows and the initial state also at the low energies explored here. The observation of this scaling might then allow to obtain further information on the expansion properties (and the EoS) of matter at high baryon densities.

# Probing system size dependence at high baryon density by systematic comparison of Ag+Ag and Au+Au reactions at 1.23$A$ GeV

## Key claim
The authors use UrQMD simulations to predict that Ag+Ag and Au+Au collisions at 1.23$A$ GeV can be meaningfully compared only if different centrality criteria are applied: particle production scales with participant number $A_\mathrm{part}$, while elliptic flow requires selection on initial-state transverse overlap eccentricity. The scaling holds generally, but breaks for very central Ag+Ag reactions due to stronger nuclear stopping compared to mid-peripheral Au+Au reactions at the same participant number.

## Method
The UrQMD transport model (v3.5) with a hard Skyrme-type equation of state (parameters $\alpha = -124$ MeV, $\beta = 71$ MeV, $\gamma = 2$) was used to simulate Ag+Ag and Au+Au collisions at $E_\mathrm{lab} = 1.23A$ GeV. Event classes were defined by either fixed average number of Monte Carlo Glauber participants $\langle A_\mathrm{part}\rangle$ or fixed average transverse overlap eccentricity $\langle\varepsilon\{2\}\rangle$, with mapping between impact parameter $b$, $A_\mathrm{part}$, and $\varepsilon$ provided via polynomial fits. Deuteron production was included via a coalescence approach in the final state.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: $E_\mathrm{lab} = 1.23A$ GeV for both Ag+Ag and Au+Au systems
- For Au+Au at $b=0$ fm: $\langle A_\mathrm{part}\rangle = 377.3$, $\langle\varepsilon\{2\}\rangle = 0.078$; for Ag+Ag at $b=0$ fm: $\langle A_\mathrm{part}\rangle = 200.2$, $\langle\varepsilon\{2\}\rangle = 0.107$
- For Au+Au at $b=6$ fm: $\langle A_\mathrm{part}\rangle = 212.3$, $\langle\varepsilon\{2\}\rangle = 0.205$; for Ag+Ag at $b=6$ fm: $\langle A_\mathrm{part}\rangle = 86.3$, $\langle\varepsilon\{2\}\rangle = 0.300$
- Rapidity loss of projectile nucleons: $\langle\delta y\rangle = 0.55$ for p+Ag at $b=0.2$ fm, $\langle\delta y\rangle = 0.37$ for p+Au at $b=6.3$ fm (both corresponding to $A_\mathrm{part}=200$)
- Scaled elliptic flow parameter $\alpha$ (from $v_2 = \alpha \varepsilon$) reported by HADES: $-0.2$ to $-0.4$ at this energy
- Deviations in directed flow $v_1$ between Ag+Ag and Au+Au at fixed eccentricity: on the order of 10%

## Context
This work extends UrQMD predictions for Ag+Ag at 1.58$A$ GeV (Reichert 2021) to the same beam energy as the HADES Au+Au data (HADES 2020, HADES 2022). It directly addresses the tension between FOPI and HADES data on pion and cluster multiplicities by providing a systematic comparison within a single experimental program. The finding that elliptic flow scales with initial eccentricity (with opposite sign to high energies) enables extraction of the equation of state at high baryon densities, building on prior flow analyses by Hillmann (2018, 2019) and Mohs (2020). The work also connects to high-energy eccentricity scaling established by PHOBOS (2006) and Bhalerao (2005), while showing that spectator shadowing at low energies produces a negative $v_2$ that is system-size dependent.
