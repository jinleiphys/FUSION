# rho-omega mixing in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We show that even moderate excess of neutrons over protons in nuclear matter, such as in ${}^{208} Pb$, can lead to large $ρ$-$ω$ mixing at densities of the order of twice the nuclear saturation density and higher. The typical mixing angle is of the order of $10^o$. The mixing may result in noticeable shifts of the positions and widths of resonances. We also analyze temperature effects and find that temperatures up to 50 MeV have practically no effect on the mixing.

# ρ-ω mixing in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Even a moderate neutron excess over protons, such as that found in ²⁰⁸Pb, can produce large ρ-ω mixing at densities of twice nuclear saturation density and higher, with mixing angles of order 10°, an order of magnitude larger than the mixing angle from electromagnetic mixing. The authors note that this result depends on their choice of a simple parameterization for the diagonal parts of the correlator rather than a specific model, and that temperatures up to 50 MeV have practically no effect on the mixing.

## Method
The authors compute the off-diagonal correlator Π_{ρω}(ν) for ρ-ω mixing in asymmetric nuclear matter using the interaction of vector mesons with the Fermi sea of protons and neutrons, including tensor couplings. They parameterize the diagonal parts of the correlator with in-medium values of mass, width, and wave-function renormalization chosen to mimic results from various existing calculations, then solve the eigenvalue problem for the coupled ρ-ω system at zero three-momentum. For finite-temperature effects, they model an ideal gas of protons, neutrons, and pions in thermal equilibrium with charge conservation constraints.

## Key numbers
- Mixing angles of order 10° or larger for most sensible parameter choices at ρ_B = 2ρ_0 with asymmetry x = 44/208 (as in ²⁰⁸Pb)
- Case 1 (equal masses m*_ρ = m*_ω = 500 MeV, zero width): ideal mixing with θ₁ = θ₂ = 45°, mass splitting of 66 MeV between eigenstates
- Case 2 (m*_ρ = 550 MeV, m*_ω = 450 MeV, zero width): mixing angles 19° and 15°, additional splitting of 20 MeV from mixing
- Case 3 (m*_ρ = 650 MeV, m*_ω = 450 MeV, zero width, √(Z*_ρ Z*_ω) = 0.35): mixing angles 6° and 4°
- Cases 8-12 (finite widths, ρ_B = 2ρ_0 or 3ρ_0): mixing angles in the range 10-20°
- Temperature effects: practically no effect on Π_{ρω}(ν) for T up to 50 MeV; at T ~ 140 MeV the mixing element is reduced by about a factor of 2

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Dutt-Mazumder, Dutta-Roy and Kundu (1997), who studied ρ-ω mixing in the Walecka model and found a mixing angle of about 2° at saturation density. The present authors find much larger mixing effects at higher densities (2-3 times saturation) relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The results are relevant for interpreting dilepton spectra measured in CERES and HELIOS experiments at CERN, and for future high-accuracy experiments planned at GSI and CERN. The approach differs from that of Dutt-Mazumder et al. by using a simple parameterization of the diagonal correlators rather than a specific model, allowing the authors to explore a wide range of in-medium meson properties.
