# Nuclear Physics from Lattice QCD

> We review recent progress toward establishing lattice Quantum Chromodynamics as a predictive calculational framework for nuclear physics. A survey of the current techniques that are used to extract low-energy hadronic scattering amplitudes and interactions is followed by a review of recent two-body and few-body calculations by the NPLQCD collaboration and others. An outline of the nuclear physics that is expected to be accomplished with Lattice QCD in the next decade, along with estimates of the required computational resources, is presented.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD is emerging as a predictive framework for nuclear physics, but current calculations remain limited to unphysical quark masses (pion masses around 300 MeV), finite lattice volumes, and finite lattice spacings, requiring extrapolation via chiral effective field theories to reach physical predictions.

## Method

The authors review the lattice QCD formalism in which Euclidean-space correlation functions are computed by Monte-Carlo evaluation of the path integral on a discrete space-time lattice, using gauge field configurations generated with dynamical fermions. Two-body and few-body hadronic scattering amplitudes and interactions are extracted from finite-volume energy eigenvalues using Luscher's method, with calculations performed by the NPLQCD collaboration using mixed-action schemes (Kogut-Susskind sea quarks with domain-wall valence quarks) and anisotropic clover-improved Wilson fermions.

## Key numbers

* Pion masses in current calculations are typically around 300 MeV, compared to the physical value of 140 MeV
* Lattice volumes currently have spatial extent L greater than or approximately 2.5 fm, with lattice spacings b less than or approximately 0.12 fm
* Expected future calculations will reach physical pion masses of 140 MeV in volumes L greater than or approximately 6 fm with lattice spacings b less than or approximately 0.06 fm
* Finite-volume effects on single-particle properties are exponentially suppressed as approximately exp(-m_pi L)
* Lattice volumes of approximately 32^3 x 256 are currently practical, involving approximately 10^9 degrees of freedom

## Context

This review extends the program of connecting QCD to nuclear phenomenology that has been explored through potential models such as AV18 (Wiringa, 1994) and many-body methods like Green's function Monte Carlo (Pieper, 2007). The work enables first-principles calculations of hyperon-nucleon and kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes that are poorly constrained experimentally, which will inform the composition and equation of state of neutron star interiors (Page, 2006). The review also outlines the computational resources needed for the next decade of nuclear physics from lattice QCD.
