# Elastic scattering of twisted neutrons by nuclei

> We present a theoretical formalism for scattering of the twisted neutrons by nuclei in a kinematic regime where interference between Coulomb interaction and the strong interaction is essential. Twisted neutrons have definite quantized values of an angular momentum projection along the direction of propagation, and we show that it results in novel observable effects for the scattering cross section, spin asymmetries and polarization of the scattered neutrons. We demonstrate that additional capabilities provided by beam's orbital angular momentum enable new techniques for measuring both real and imaginary parts of the scattering amplitude. Several possible observables are considered, for which the targets may be either well-localized with respect to the spatial beam profile, or the scattering occurs incoherently on nuclei in a bulk target. The developed approach can be applied to other nuclear reactions with strongly interacting twisted particles.

## Key claim

Twisted neutrons with definite quantized total angular momentum projection produce novel observable effects in elastic scattering off nuclei, including spin asymmetries that depend on both the real and imaginary parts of the nuclear scattering amplitude, but the magnitude of these effects depends critically on whether the target is a single nucleus, a mesoscopic target, or a macroscopic bulk target, with the largest asymmetries (tens of percent) occurring for well-localized targets and the smallest (parts per million) for macroscopic targets after averaging.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical formalism for elastic scattering of twisted (Bessel) neutrons by spin-zero nuclei, using a coherent superposition of plane-wave states weighted by an amplitude that defines the Bessel state with definite longitudinal momentum, transverse momentum, and total angular momentum projection. They compute the differential cross section for three scenarios: scattering on a macroscopic target (incoherent sum over uniformly distributed nuclei), scattering on a single nucleus at a fixed transverse position, and scattering on a mesoscopic target with a Gaussian transverse density distribution. The formalism incorporates interference between the Coulomb (Schwinger) interaction from the neutron's anomalous magnetic moment and the strong nuclear interaction.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of twisted-particle scattering previously developed for photons and electrons (Allen, 1992; Bliokh, 2017) and for twisted gamma-rays and neutrons (Afanasev, 2018) to the specific case of elastic neutron-nucleus scattering in the Schwinger regime where Coulomb and strong interactions interfere. The Schwinger scattering formalism was originally developed by Schwinger (1948) and experimentally observed by Shull (1964). The paper builds on the experimental demonstration of twisted neutron beams using spiral phase plates (exp-2015) and other generation methods (exp-2018, exp-2019, Geerits, 2020). The results enable new techniques for measuring both real and imaginary parts of the nuclear scattering amplitude, complementary to neutron interferometry and neutron gravity reflectometry, and are relevant for separating parity-conserving and parity-violating mechanisms in strong interactions as studied by Gericke (2008) and Musolf (1994).
