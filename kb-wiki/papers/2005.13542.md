# Explaining $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ and the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly with a Vector Triplet

> The most statistically significant hints for new physics in the flavor sector are discrepancies between theory and experiment in $B$ decays to lepton pairs ($b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$) and a deficit in $1^{\rm st}$ row CKM unitarity (the Cabibbo Angle anomaly). We propose that these anomalies can be reconciled by a simplified model with massive gauge bosons transforming in the adjoint representation of $SU(2)_L$. After calculating the impact of this model on $B$ decays, observables testing charged current lepton flavor universality (LFU), electro-weak precision observables and LHC searches we perform a global fit to all available data. We find that our model can provide a consistent common explanation of both anomalies and that the fit to the data is more than $7\,σ$ better than the fit of the SM. The model also predicts interesting correlations between LFU violation in the charged current and $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ data which can be tested experimentally in the near future.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a simplified model with massive gauge bosons transforming as an $SU(2)_L$ triplet can simultaneously explain both the $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ anomalies and the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly, with the global fit to data being more than $7\sigma$ better than the Standard Model fit, but only under the assumption of an approximate $U(2)^3$ flavor symmetry in the quark sector to suppress dangerous flavor-changing neutral currents.

## Method

The authors extend the Standard Model with a heavy vector triplet (a $Z^\prime$ and $W^\prime$ boson) with zero hypercharge and mass $M_X$, coupling to left-handed quarks and leptons. They implement the model in HEPfit to compute modifications to electroweak precision observables, charged current lepton flavor universality tests, the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly, $B_s-\bar B_s$ mixing, LHC constraints, and parity violation bounds, then combine these with a likelihood profile from $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ data using a Bayesian Markov Chain Monte Carlo analysis with the Bayesian Analysis Toolkit.

## Key numbers

- The combined fit to all data improves over the Standard Model by more than $7\sigma$ (Information Criterion value of approximately 115 for the NP scenario versus approximately 167 for the Standard Model)
- The $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ fit alone yields a pull of $6.2\sigma$ with respect to the Standard Model hypothesis
- The Cabibbo Angle Anomaly tension in $V_{us}$ from beta decays is reduced by $2.6\sigma$, and the tension from semileptonic kaon decays into muons by $0.9\sigma$
- Best fit point at $M_X = 10$ TeV: $\sin\alpha_{ZZ^{\prime}} = (-0.7 \pm 0.3)\times 10^{-4}$, $g_{11}^\ell = 1.8 \pm 0.5$, $g_{22}^\ell = 3.7 \pm 0.7$, $g_{33}^\ell = 6.3 \pm 1.7$, $g^q = -0.8 \pm 0.4$
- The $Z^\prime$-$Z$ mixing angle $\sin\alpha_{ZZ^\prime}$ from the fit without $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ data is $(2.9 \pm 4.8)\times 10^{-5}$

## Context

This work extends the line of simplified $Z^\prime$ and $W^\prime$ models previously studied for $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ anomalies (Buras 2013, Gauld 2013, Altmannshofer 2014, Crivellin 2015) and for the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly (Belfatto 2019, Grossman 2019, Coutinho 2019, Crivellin 2020). It is the first to show that a single $SU(2)_L$ triplet vector boson can simultaneously address both anomalies while satisfying electroweak precision, LHC, and flavor constraints. The model predicts correlations between charged current lepton flavor universality violation and $b\to s\ell^+\ell^-$ data that can be tested by future measurements at PEN, BELLE II, and the LHC, as well as by $Z$-pole measurements at future colliders such as CLIC, ILC, or FCC-ee.
