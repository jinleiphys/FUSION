# Non-linear chiral magnetic waves

> The chiral magnetic wave (CMW) is a macroscopic quantum phenomenon that arises due to the mixing of the electric and chiral charge oscillations induced by the chiral anomaly. In this study we report the first quantum simulation (on classical hardware) of the real-time dynamics of CMWs in Schwinger model. Our quench protocol is the following: at $t=0$ we suddenly place an electric dipole at the middle of our lattice. Due to chiral anomaly, this dipole excites the CMW that propagates towards the edges of the lattice. In Schwinger model tuned to the conformal critical point (at $θ= π$, $m/g \simeq 0.2$), we find a gapless linear CMW that propagates with the speed of light. For massless Schwinger model ($θ=0, m=0$), we find a gapped linear CMW, in accord with previous analytical analyses. For massive Schwinger model (that is dual to strongly interacting bosonic theory), we enter the new regime of nonlinear CMWs, where we find a surprise. Specifically, for $m/g > 1$, the frequency of electric charge oscillations becomes much smaller than the frequency of the oscillations of the chiral charge. For $m/g =4$, we find a solution corresponding to a nearly static electric dipole with fast oscillations of the chiral charge confined within. We call this solution a "thumper" and study its properties in detail.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first quantum simulation (on classical hardware) of real-time chiral magnetic wave dynamics in the Schwinger model, claiming that for fermion mass \(m/g > 1\) the electric charge oscillations become much slower than chiral charge oscillations, and at \(m/g = 4\) a nearly static electric dipole with fast chiral charge oscillations confined within (called a "thumper") emerges, but the authors note they have not yet found the corresponding classical analytic solution.

## Method

The authors use exact diagonalization of the lattice-regularized Schwinger model Hamiltonian (20 staggered sites, lattice spacing \(a = 0.5/g\)) to prepare the ground state, then perform a quench by introducing an electric dipole at the lattice center at \(t=0\). They truncate the Hilbert space to the 100 lowest energy levels, evolve the state via \(|\psi\rangle_t = e^{-iHt}|\psi\rangle_{t=0}\), and measure local vector and axial charge densities as functions of time for several fermion masses: \(m = 4g, 2g, g, 0\), and the critical mass \(m^* = -0.2g\).

## Key numbers

* For the Schwinger model tuned to the conformal critical point (\(\theta = \pi\), \(m/g \simeq 0.2\)), a gapless linear CMW propagates with the speed of light.
* For the massive case \(m/g = 4\), the electric dipole is nearly static while the axial charge oscillates rapidly within the dipole, this "thumper" solution is observed.
* The lattice simulation uses \(N = 20\) staggered sites with lattice spacing \(a = 0.5/g\).
* All excited states lie above the ground state by \(E_k - E_0 \gtrsim 2m\), corresponding to fermion–anti-fermion bound states.

## Context

This work extends the analytical treatment of chiral magnetic waves in the massless Schwinger model (Kharzeev 2010) to the massive Schwinger model, which is not solvable analytically and exhibits confinement. The results enable future studies of nonlinear CMWs and "thumpers" in real systems, and suggest a possible link between topological fluctuations and confinement in (3+1) dimensions, as previously discussed by Kharzeev (2015).
