# Relativistic Effects in Simulations of the Fragmentation Process with the Microscopic Framework

> We simulate the fragmentation processes in the \CaCa collisions at the bombarding energy 1.05 GeV/u using the Lorentz covariant RQMD and the non-covariant QMD approaches, incorporated with the statistical decay model. By comparing the results of RQMD with those of QMD, we examine the relativistic effects and find that the multiplicity of the $α$ particle after the statistical decay process is sensitive to the relativistic effects. It is shown that the Lorentz covariant approach is necessary to analyze the fragmentation process even at the energy around \Elab = 1 GeV/u as long as we are concerned with the final observables of the mass distribution, particularly, the light fragments around $A = 3 \sim 4$.

## Key claim

The Lorentz covariant RQMD approach is necessary for analyzing fragmentation processes at bombarding energies around 1 GeV/u, but only when one is concerned with final observables of the mass distribution, particularly light fragments around A = 3 to 4; the relativistic effects appear not in the dynamical stage but in the internal energies of fragments, which then affect the alpha particle multiplicity after statistical decay by roughly a factor of two.

## Method

The authors simulated Ca + Ca collisions at 1.05 GeV/u using both the Lorentz covariant Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) and the non-covariant Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) approaches, each followed by a statistical decay model. For the non-covariant QMD, they tested two boosting methods: Galilei boost (QMD/G) and Lorentz boost (QMD/L). An integrated code was prepared where the wave packet prescription, ground state initialization, two-body effective interaction (Skyrme-type with hard EOS, incompressibility K = 380 MeV), and collision prescription (Cugnon parametrization with frozen-delta approximation) were shared between the two models to isolate the effects of Lorentz covariance.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energy: 1.05 GeV/u for Ca + Ca collisions.
- Incompressibility of the Skyrme-type interaction: K = 380 MeV (hard EOS).
- Symmetry energy: 25 MeV.
- The alpha multiplicity difference between RQMD and QMD/G in the middle impact parameter region (around b = 4 fm) amounts to roughly 50 percent.
- The total cross-section difference for fragments around A = 3 and 4 between RQMD and QMD/G is about a factor of two.
- For the RQMD fragment definition, the condition used was -q_ij^2 less than 16 fm^2, with results stable when varying this condition from 14 fm^2 to 18 fm^2.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of covariant and non-covariant QMD approaches previously discussed by the Tuebingen group (Lehmann, Puri and collaborators) and by the first author (Maruyama, 1995), who had earlier shown that transverse flow is influenced by Lorentz contraction and covariance. The paper shows that while the dynamical mass distributions are almost equivalent between RQMD and QMD at energies around 1 GeV/u (agreeing with the conclusion of Puri and collaborators that relativistic effects on resonance production, density, and rapidity distributions are less significant at Elab less than or equal to 2 GeV/u), the internal energies of fragments differ, making the Lorentz covariant approach necessary for final fragment observables. This enables future work to properly include other important ingredients such as explicit momentum-dependent forces and the Coulomb force, which were not considered here but are known to be important in this energy region.
