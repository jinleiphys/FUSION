# Nonequilibrium effects in dynamic symmetry breaking

> We present a dynamic model based on the linear sigma model with constituent quarks that allows for studying the explicit propagation of fluctuations at a chiral critical point and a first-order phase transition. The coupled dynamics of the sigma field and the quarks is derived selfconsistently. Hereby, the sigma field evolves according to a semiclassical Langevin equation of motion, while the evolution of the quarks is described fluid dynamically in order to model the expansion of a hot fireball in heavy-ion collisions. Dissipative processes and fluctuations in the Langevin equation are allowed under the assumption of energy-momentum conservation of the coupled system. We study the evolution of the constituent quark masses in this nonequilibrium set up.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dynamic model of chiral symmetry breaking in heavy-ion collisions that includes explicit propagation of fluctuations, but the authors hedge that the model is currently limited to zero baryochemical potential and uses tuned coupling constants that produce incorrect vacuum quark masses, focusing only on qualitative behavior.

## Method

The authors couple a semiclassical Langevin equation for the sigma field (the order parameter of chiral symmetry) to relativistic fluid dynamics for the constituent quarks, derived from the linear sigma model with constituent quarks. The sigma field evolves under dissipation and stochastic noise satisfying the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, while the quark fluid expansion provides the driving force through the phase transition. The coupled equations are solved numerically for a boost-invariant, almond-shaped initial energy density distribution in the transverse plane.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum pion decay constant is set to \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, giving the vacuum sigma expectation value.
- The quark-meson coupling constant is \(g = 3.3\) to reproduce the correct vacuum constituent quark mass at \(\mu_B = 0\).
- For a critical point scenario, the coupling is tuned to \(g = 3.63\), yielding a critical temperature \(T_c = 139.88\) MeV.
- For a first-order phase transition scenario, the coupling is tuned to \(g = 5.5\), yielding a transition temperature \(T_c = 123.27\) MeV.
- The transition temperature is crossed at approximately \(t \approx 5\) fm for the first-order case and \(t \approx 4\) fm for the critical point case.
- Reheating is observed between 5 and 10 fm during the first-order phase transition.

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral fluid dynamic models by Mishustin (1998) and Paech (2003) by including explicit dissipation and noise terms derived from the linear sigma model, enabling the study of nonequilibrium effects such as supercooling and spinodal decomposition. The model enables future studies of how the delayed growth of the constituent quark mass at a first-order phase transition couples to the confinement-deconfinement transition, with work including the Polyakov loop reported as in progress.
