# Hagedorn bag-like model with a crossover transition meets lattice QCD

> Thermodynamic functions, the (higher-order) fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges at $μ_B = 0$, and the Fourier coefficients of net-baryon density at imaginary $μ_B$, are considered in the framework of a Hagedorn bag-like model with a crossover transition. The qualitative behavior of these observables is found to be compatible with lattice QCD results. Fair quantitative description of the lattice data is obtained when quasiparticle-type quarks and gluons with non-zero masses are introduced into the bag spectrum. The equation of state of the model exhibits a smooth and wide crossover transition.

## Key claim

The Hagedorn bag-like model with a crossover transition can qualitatively reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamic functions and fluctuations of conserved charges at zero baryon chemical potential, but a fair quantitative description requires introducing quasiparticle-type quarks and gluons with non-zero masses into the bag spectrum, rather than the standard massless assumption.

## Method

The authors construct a grand canonical partition function for a multi-component system of color-neutral objects with finite eigenvolumes, using an isobaric (pressure) ensemble to handle excluded-volume corrections. The particle spectrum consists of two contributions: established hadrons from the Particle Data Group with eigenvolumes proportional to their mass, and an exponential Hagedorn spectrum of heavy quark-gluon bags described by a mass-volume density of states derived from the bag model. The pressure is determined by the farthest-right singularity of the isobaric partition function, and the model parameters are chosen to realize a crossover transition rather than a first-order phase transition at zero baryon density.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant: B^(1/4) = 250 MeV, corresponding to Hagedorn temperature T_H ≈ 165 MeV
- Quark-gluon bag lower volume cut-off: V_0 = 4 fm^3
- Pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD: T_pc ≈ 155 MeV (from Borsanyi 2010 and Bazavov 2011)
- Hagedorn temperature from early models: T_H ~ 170 MeV
- The exponent δ is varied in the range -3 ≤ δ ≤ -1/2, corresponding to exponent α in the Hagedorn mass spectrum in the range 0 ≤ α ≤ 5/2
- The non-relativistic approximation for quark-gluon bags has relative accuracy of 10% or better for m/T > 20
- Laplace's method allows evaluation of pressure with precision better than 2% for all temperature values considered

## Context

This work extends the gas of extended quark-gluon bags with a crossover transition studied by Ferroni (2008) and Gorenstein (2005), which previously only addressed qualitative features of the lattice QCD equation of state. The model is compared to lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2013) and the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2014). The key advance is the introduction of quasiparticle-type quarks and gluons with non-zero masses into the bag spectrum, which substantially improves quantitative agreement with lattice data on conserved charge susceptibilities and Fourier coefficients of net-baryon density at imaginary chemical potential. The approach enables testing and constraining effective QCD models for the equation of state at non-zero baryon density, following work by Albright (2014, 2015), Vovchenko (2016, 2017), Critelli (2017), Huovinen (2017), and Motornenko (2018).
