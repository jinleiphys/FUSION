# The Physics of Ultraperipheral Collisions at the LHC

> We discuss the physics of large impact parameter interactions at the LHC: ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs). The dominant processes in UPCs are photon-nucleon (nucleus) interactions. The current LHC detector configurations can explore small $x$ hard phenomena with nuclei and nucleons at photon-nucleon center-of-mass energies above 1 TeV, extending the $x$ range of HERA by a factor of ten. In particular, it will be possible to probe diffractive and inclusive parton densities in nuclei using several processes. The interaction of small dipoles with protons and nuclei can be investigated in elastic and quasi-elastic $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ production as well as in high $t$ $ρ^0$ production accompanied by a rapidity gap. Several of these phenomena provide clean signatures of the onset of the new high gluon density QCD regime. The LHC is in the kinematic range where nonlinear effects are several times larger than at HERA. Two-photon processes in UPCs are also studied. In addition, while UPCs play a role in limiting the maximum beam luminosity, they can also be used a luminosity monitor by measuring mutual electromagnetic dissociation of the beam nuclei. We also review similar studies at HERA and RHIC as well as describe the potential use of the LHC detectors for UPC measurements.

## Key claim

Ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) at the LHC can probe photon-nucleon center-of-mass energies above 1 TeV, extending the accessible x range of HERA by a factor of ten, but the actual reach depends on detector acceptance, beam species, and the ability to separate hadronic from electromagnetic interactions by requiring impact parameters larger than the sum of the nuclear radii.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for UPCs using the Weizsacker-Williams equivalent photon method, where the electromagnetic fields of relativistic ions are treated as fluxes of virtual photons. Cross sections for photonuclear and two-photon processes are factorized into the product of the photon flux (integrated over impact parameters larger than the nuclear radii) and the corresponding photon-nucleon or photon-photon interaction cross section. The analysis covers multiple collision systems (Pb+Pb, pPb, pp, and lighter ions) at LHC energies, comparing effective luminosities and kinematic reach for processes including dijet production, heavy quark production, exclusive vector meson production, and hard diffraction.

## Key numbers

- Photon-nucleon center-of-mass energies above 1 TeV are accessible at the LHC, extending the x range of HERA by a factor of ten
- Maximum two-photon collision energy in symmetric AA collisions at the LHC is about 200 GeV (compared to 6 GeV at RHIC)
- Photon transverse momentum cutoff from heavy ions: pT less than approximately 28 MeV/c for Pb beams and 330 MeV/c for proton beams
- Photon cutoff energy in the nuclear rest frame: about 500 GeV at RHIC and 1 PeV (1000 TeV) at the LHC
- Maximum photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy for Pb+Pb at LHC: sqrt(s_gammaN^max) = 950 GeV
- Maximum two-photon center-of-mass energy for Pb+Pb at LHC: sqrt(s_gammagamma^max) = 162 GeV
- Maximum photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy for pPb at LHC: sqrt(s_gammaN^max) = 1500 GeV (photon from Pb) and 2610 GeV (photon from p)
- Maximum two-photon center-of-mass energy for pPb at LHC: sqrt(s_gammagamma^max) = 260 GeV
- LHC Pb+Pb beam-beam luminosity: 0.42 mb^-1 s^-1
- LHC pPb beam-beam luminosity: 420 mb^-1 s^-1
- LHC pp beam-beam luminosity: 10^7 mb^-1 s^-1
- LHC two-photon luminosities for light ion beams are higher than available elsewhere for energies up to W_gammagamma approximately 500 GeV/c^2
- Nonlinear effects in UPCs at the LHC would be a factor of six higher than at HERA and a factor of two larger than at eRHIC for pT down to 5 GeV/c
- Dijet and heavy quark production can probe the gluon distribution at x approximately 5 x 10^-5 for pT greater than or equal to 6 GeV/c
- Coherent quarkonium production probes x_eff = 2.5 x 10^-3 for Upsilon and 7.5 x 10^-4 for J/psi at midrapidity

## Context

This review extends earlier comprehensive studies of UPCs by Baur and Bertulani (1988), Krauss, Greiner and Soff (1997), Baur and collaborators (2001), and Bertulani, Klein and Nystrand, as well as the LHC FELIX proposal and a UPC workshop in Erice. The paper builds on HERA findings of rapid growth of small-x parton densities, hard diffraction, and exclusive vector meson production, and shows how UPCs at the LHC can probe the onset of the black disk regime and nonlinear QCD dynamics at significantly higher energies and smaller x than previously possible. The work enables quantitative planning for UPC measurements with the LHC detectors (ATLAS, CMS, ALICE) and provides the kinematic and rate estimates needed to design experimental searches for high gluon density phenomena in both AA and pA collisions.
