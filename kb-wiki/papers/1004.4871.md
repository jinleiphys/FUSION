# Neutron star stiff equation of state derived from cooling phases of the X-ray burster 4U 1724-307

> Thermal emission during X-ray bursts is a powerful tool to determine neutron star masses and radii, if the Eddington flux and the apparent radius in the cooling tail can be measured accurately, and distances to the sources are known. We propose here an improved method of determining the basic stellar parameters using the data from the cooling phase of photospheric radius expansion bursts covering a large range of luminosities. Because at that phase the blackbody apparent radius depends only on the spectral hardening factor (color-correction), we suggest to fit the theoretical dependences of the color-correction versus flux in Eddington units to the observed variations of the inverse square root of the apparent blackbody radius with the flux. We show that spectral variations observed during a long photospheric radius expansion burst from 4U 1724-307 are entirely consistent with the theoretical expectations for the passively cooling neutron star atmospheres. Our method allows us to determine both the Eddington flux (which is found to be smaller than the touchdown flux by 15%) and the ratio of the stellar apparent radius to the distance much more reliably. We then find a lower limit on the neutron star radius of 14 km for masses below 2.2M_sun, independently of the chemical composition. These results suggest that the matter inside neutron stars is characterized by a stiff equation of state. We finally show that the apparent blackbody emitting area in the cooling tails of the short bursts from 4U 1724-307 is two times smaller than that for the long burst and their evolution does not follow the theory. This makes their usage for determination of the neutron star parameters questionable and casts serious doubts on the results of previous works that used for the analysis similar bursts from other sources. # Neutron star stiff equation of state derived from cooling phases of the X-ray burster 4U 1724-307

## Key claim

The authors propose that the spectral evolution observed during the cooling phase of a long photospheric radius expansion (PRE) burst from 4U 1724-307 is consistent with theoretical models of passively cooling neutron star atmospheres, allowing a determination of the Eddington flux that is 15% smaller than the touchdown flux. This yields a lower limit on the neutron star radius of 14 km for masses below 2.2 solar masses, independent of chemical composition, which implies a stiff equation of state for neutron star matter.

## Method

The authors fit the observed relation between the inverse square root of the blackbody normalization (K^{-1/4}) and the observed flux (F) during the cooling tail of a long PRE burst to theoretical curves of the color-correction factor (f_c) versus luminosity in Eddington units (L/L_Edd), computed from a large set of atmosphere models covering luminosities varying by three orders of magnitude. The free parameters are the Eddington flux F_Edd and the quantity A = (R_infinity/D)^{-1/2}, where R_infinity is the apparent neutron star radius and D is the distance. The best-fit parameters are then converted to constraints on neutron star mass and radius using standard general relativistic relations, assuming a distance distribution between 5.3 and 7.7 kpc.

## Key numbers

- The Eddington flux is found to be 0.525 ± 0.025 × 10^{-7} erg s^{-1} cm^{-2} for hydrogen atmosphere models, which is 15% smaller than the touchdown flux.
- The lower limit on the neutron star radius is 14 km for masses below 2.2 solar masses, independent of chemical composition.
- For a canonical neutron star mass of 1.4 solar masses, the radius is constrained to 15.2 ± 0.4 km for solar H/He composition with subsolar metals (Z=0.3 Z_sun) and 14.2 ± 0.4 km for pure hydrogen.
- The apparent blackbody emitting area in the cooling tails of short bursts from 4U 1724-307 is two times smaller than that for the long burst.
- The distance to the source is in the range 5.3 to 7.7 kpc, based on prior measurements of the globular cluster Terzan 2.

## Context

This work extends the method of using X-ray burst cooling tails to determine neutron star masses and radii, previously applied by Ozel et al. (2009) and Guver et al. (2010) using the "touchdown method" which assumes the Eddington flux coincides with the touchdown flux and uses a fixed color-correction factor of approximately 1.4 from models by Madej et al. (2004) and Majczyna and Madej (2005). The authors argue that short bursts from 4U 1724-307 and other sources analyzed in those prior works do not follow the theoretical cooling track predictions, casting doubt on those results. The new cooling tail method enables more reliable parameter determination by using the full cooling track and checking consistency with theory, and it enables future studies to select only bursts that follow the theoretical predictions for neutron star parameter estimation.
