# Isovector soft dipole mode in 6Be

> By using the 1H(6Li,6Be)n charge-exchange reaction, continuum states in 6Be were populated up to E_t=16 MeV, E_t being the 6Be energy above its three-body decay threshold. In kinematically complete measurements performed by detecting alpha+p+p coincidences, an E_t spectrum of high statistics was obtained, containing approximately ~5x10^6 events. The spectrum provides detailed correlation information about the well-known 0^+ ground state of 6Be at E_t=1.37 MeV and its 2^+ state at E_t=3.05 MeV. Moreover, a broad structure extending from 4 to 16 MeV was observed. It contains negative parity states populated by Delta L=1 angular momentum transfer without other significant contributions. This structure can be interpreted as a novel phenomenon, i.e. the isovector soft dipole mode associated with the 6Li ground state. The population of this mode in the charge-exchange reaction is a dominant phenomenon for this reaction, being responsible for about 60% of the cross section obtained in the measured energy range.

## Key claim

The broad continuum structure observed in 6Be from 4 to 16 MeV above its three-body decay threshold is interpreted as a novel isovector soft dipole mode (IVSDM) associated with the 6Li ground state, populated via Delta L=1 angular momentum transfer in the charge-exchange reaction. This interpretation is conditional on the exclusion of collective resonances below the alpha-cluster disintegration energy, meaning the observed spectra are connected solely with specific three-body cluster dynamics rather than giant dipole or spin-dipole resonances.

## Method

Continuum states in 6Be were populated up to E_T=16 MeV using the 1H(6Li,6Be)n charge-exchange reaction in inverse kinematics with a 32.5 A MeV 6Li beam on a hydrogen target. Kinematically complete measurements were performed by detecting alpha+p+p triple coincidences using two annular telescopes with position-sensitive silicon detectors and CsI(Tl) crystals, covering angular ranges of approximately 3-8 degrees and 10-24 degrees. The three-body decay of 6Be was analyzed using a theoretical model combining three-body dynamics for the alpha+p+p system with a plane-wave impulse approximation treatment of the reaction mechanism, where the continuum wave function was obtained via Green's function method.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 5 x 10^6 triple coincidence events were accumulated in the E_T spectrum
- The 0+ ground state of 6Be is at E_T = 1.37 MeV and the 2+ state at E_T = 3.05 MeV
- The broad structure extends from 4 to 16 MeV in E_T
- The IVSDM population accounts for about 60% of the cross section in the measured energy range
- The beam energy was 32.5 A MeV with an intensity of 3 x 10^7 s^-1 and energy spread smaller than 0.5%
- The target cell was 6 mm thick with hydrogen at 3 bar pressure cooled to 35 K
- Energy resolution (FWHM) follows a sqrt(E_T) dependence: 0.4 MeV at E_T=1.4 MeV, 0.6 MeV at E_T=3 MeV, 1.0 MeV at E_T=9 MeV, and 1.3 MeV at E_T=15 MeV
- The maximum of the broad hump angular distribution occurs at theta_Be approximately 35 degrees, while the 2+ state maximum is at 50-70 degrees
- Typical momentum transfer for the IVSDM peak is q ~ 100-150 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends studies of soft dipole modes in halo nuclei, previously observed in electromagnetic excitation of 6He (Aumann 2005, Nakamura 2002), to the charge-exchange channel producing the mirror nucleus 6Be. The results contradict interpretations of the dipole excitations in 6He and 6Be as part of the giant dipole resonance or spin-dipole resonance (Yang 1995, Nakayama 2000), instead connecting the phenomenon entirely to cluster degrees of freedom. The high-statistics correlation data enable detailed comparison with three-body models (Grigorenko 2009, Grigorenko 2009c) and provide a new tool for studying isovector excitations in light nuclei, with implications for understanding two-proton radioactivity and democratic decay mechanisms.
