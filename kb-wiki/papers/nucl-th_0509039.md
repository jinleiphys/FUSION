# Particle spectra and hydro-inspired models

> Several popular parameterizations of the freeze-out conditions in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are shortly reviewed. The common features of the models, responsible for the successful description of hadronic observables, are outlined.

## Key claim

This review argues that hydro-inspired freeze-out models, while using simplified parameterizations rather than full hydrodynamic evolution, successfully describe hadronic observables in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions because they capture the essential features of a locally thermalized, collectively expanding source at freeze-out; however, the author notes that the connection between these models and full hydrodynamic calculations remains inconsistent, particularly regarding the shape of the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Method

The paper reviews several parameterizations of the freeze-out stage in nucleus-nucleus collisions, all based on the Cooper-Frye formula or a modeled emission function. Each model assumes local thermal equilibrium and collective flow at freeze-out, but differs in its treatment of geometry (cylindrically symmetric vs. ellipsoidal), boost-invariance, resonance decays, and final-state interactions. The models are compared by their ability to fit experimental data from Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energies, focusing on transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii.

## Key numbers

- Blast-wave fit (BRAHMS, Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, 0-10% centrality, midrapidity): temperature = 110 MeV, average transverse flow velocity = 0.65 c
- Retiere-Lisa blast-wave fit (Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, three centrality classes 0-5%, 15-30%, 60-92%): temperatures = 107, 106, and 100 MeV; transverse flow = 0.52 c, 0.50 c, and 0.47 c
- Buda-Lund fit (Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV): T0 = 196 MeV, Te = 117 MeV, Ts = 89.7 MeV, tau0 = 5.8 fm, Delta tau = 0.9 fm
- Seattle model fit (STAR data, Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV): T = 215 MeV, tau0 = 8 fm, Delta tau = 2.7 fm, R_WS = 12 fm
- Durham model fit (PHENIX data): tau0 = 1 fm, tau_f = 19 fm, T_f = 110 MeV
- Cracow single-freeze-out model (top RHIC energies): T = 165.5 MeV, mu_B = 28.5 MeV
- Humanic's rescattering model: initial T = 300 MeV, sigma_y = 2.4, tau_had = 1 fm, freeze-out at about 30 fm (Au+Au, 130 GeV)

## Context

This review extends the line of work on thermal and hydrodynamic descriptions of heavy-ion collisions, comparing several specific models: the blast-wave model originating from Siemens and Rasmussen (1978) and developed by Schnedermann, Sollfrank and Heinz (1993); the Buda-Lund model by Csorgo and collaborators (1995, 1999); the Seattle model by Cramer and Miller (2004, 2005); the Durham model by Renk (2004); and the Cracow single-freeze-out model by Broniowski and Florkowski (2001). The paper enables future work by identifying common features (short evolution times, large transverse flow) and open problems, particularly the need to consistently include resonance decays and emission from space-like parts of the freeze-out hypersurface, as recently attempted by the Kiev-Nantes group (2005).
