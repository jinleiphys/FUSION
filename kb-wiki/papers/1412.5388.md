# $X(3872)$ production in high energy heavy ion collisions

> We show the results obtained for the cross sections of the processes $\bar D D$, $\bar D^* D$, $\bar D^* D^*\toπX(3872)$, information which is necessary to determine the $X(3872)$ abundance in heavy ion collisions. Our formalism is based on the generation of $X(3872)$ from the interaction of the hadrons $\bar D^0 D^{*0} - \textrm{c.c}$, $D^- D^{*+} - \textrm{c.c}$ and $D^-_s D^{*+}_s - \textrm{c.c}$ an the calculation of the $X\bar D^* D^*$ anomalous vertex considering $X(3872)$ as a molecule of the above hadrons channels.

## Key claim

The paper calculates cross sections for the production of the X(3872) meson via the inverse reactions of its hadronic absorption, specifically \(\bar D D\), \(\bar D^* D\), and \(\bar D^* D^* \to \pi X\), under the assumption that X(3872) is a hadron molecule composed of \(\bar D D^*\) and charged counterparts. The key hedge is that the results are obtained within a molecular model for X(3872), and the importance of the newly included anomalous vertex \(X \bar D^* D^*\) is demonstrated, but the actual impact on X(3872) abundance in heavy ion collisions is only suggested as potentially important, not quantitatively computed here.

## Method

The authors compute isospin-spin averaged cross sections in the center-of-mass frame for the three reactions using Feynman diagrams with meson exchange. The X(3872) is generated dynamically from the coupled channels \(\bar D^0 D^{*0} - \text{c.c.}\), \(D^- D^{*+} - \text{c.c.}\), and \(D^-_s D^{*+}_s - \text{c.c.}\) following the model of Refs. [Daniel2, Daniel4, Francesca2]. The novel contribution is the inclusion of the anomalous vertices \(\pi D^* D^*\) and \(X \bar D^* D^*\), which were neglected in prior work (Cho3), and the inclusion of charged \(D\) and \(D^*\) meson components coupling to X(3872). The coupling \(g_{X \bar D^* D^*}\) is estimated from the cross section results.

## Key numbers

- The inclusion of the anomalous vertex \(X \bar D^* D^*\) raises the \(\bar D^* D \to \pi X\) cross section by around a factor of 100–150.
- The inclusion of the anomalous vertex \(X \bar D^* D^*\) raises the \(\bar D^* D^* \to \pi X\) cross section by about a factor of 8–10.
- The extracted coupling constant is \(g_{X \bar D^* D^*} \sim 1.95 \pm 0.22\).
- The cut-off used to regularize loop integrals is varied in the range 700–1000 MeV, and the results are not very affected by this variation.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Cho3, which evaluated hadronic absorption cross sections for \(\pi X \to D \bar D\), \(D^* \bar D^*\) and \(\rho X \to D \bar D\), \(D \bar D^*\), \(D^* \bar D^*\) but neglected the anomalous vertices \(\pi D^* D^*\) and \(X \bar D^* D^*\). The present paper adds the inverse production reactions \(\bar D D\), \(\bar D^* D\), \(\bar D^* D^* \to \pi X\) and includes charged \(D\) and \(D^*\) meson components as in Daniel4. The results are intended to be used in kinetic equations to compute the X(3872) abundance during the hadronic phase of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the formation of a quark-gluon plasma is expected to enhance the production of exotic charmonium states.
