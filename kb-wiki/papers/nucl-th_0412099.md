# Pairing effects in low density domain of nuclear matter

> Using equations, governing np pairing correlations in S=1, T=0 pairing channel (PRC 63 (2001) 021304(R)), it is shown that at low densities equations for the energy gap in the spectrum of quasiparticles and chemical potentials of protons and neutrons allow solutions with negative chemical potential. This corresponds to appearance of Bose--Einstein condensate (BEC) of deuterons in low density region of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in low-density asymmetric nuclear matter, the equations for the energy gap and chemical potentials allow solutions with negative chemical potential, which corresponds to a smooth crossover from BCS-type np Cooper pairing to Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of deuteron-like bound states, even at finite isospin asymmetry.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled gap and number equations for np pairing in the S=1, T=0 channel, using a zero-range effective force with parameters fitted to reproduce the pairing gap from the Paris NN potential. They compute the energy gap and chemical potentials (μ₀₀, μ₀₃, μₚ, μₙ) as functions of density and temperature for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, using the Gogny D1S effective mass and a cutoff energy ε_c = 60 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The chemical potential μ₀₀ changes sign at a critical density ρ_b ≈ 3 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ in symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature.
- The zero-range force parameter v₀ = -530 MeV·fm³ is used, with η = 0 and cutoff ε_c = 60 MeV.
- The nuclear saturation density is taken as ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- As density tends to zero, the chemical potential μ₀₀ approaches the asymptotic value μ₀₀ = -ε_b/2, where ε_b is the deuteron binding energy.
- At very low densities and finite asymmetry, μ₀₃ also approaches -ε_b/2, giving μₚ → -ε_b and μₙ → 0 as ρ → 0, independent of the asymmetry parameter.

## Context

This work extends the BCS-to-BEC crossover studies in symmetric nuclear matter (Alm et al., 2000; Baldo et al., 1995; Lombardo et al., 2001) to asymmetric nuclear matter, using the formalism developed by Isayev et al. (2001) for np pairing in the S=1, T=0 channel. The results show that isospin asymmetry suppresses np pairing less efficiently at very low densities than in the weak-coupling regime, and the asymptotic chemical potentials become independent of asymmetry. This enables future studies of the Mott transition and deuteron condensation in neutron-rich environments such as neutron star crusts.
