# Jet tomography

> I summarize the recent advances in jet tomographic studies of cold and hot nuclear matter based on perturbative QCD calculations of medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung. Quantitative applications to ultrarelativistic heavy ion reactions at RHIC indicate the creation of a deconfined state of QCD with initial energy density on the order of 100 times cold nuclear matter density.

# Jet tomography

## Key claim

Based on perturbative QCD calculations of medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung, jet tomographic analysis of ultrarelativistic heavy ion reactions at RHIC indicates the creation of a deconfined state of QCD with initial energy density on the order of 100 times cold nuclear matter density, though the analysis assumes local thermal equilibrium and longitudinal Bjorken expansion.

## Method

The author reviews calculations of medium-induced non-Abelian gluon bremsstrahlung using multiple formalisms including a path integral approach, an effective 2D Schroedinger equation, the exact Reaction Operator formalism, and a twist expansion series. These calculations are applied to single inclusive hadron suppression ratios R_AB, di-hadron correlation functions, and jet shape modifications in cold nuclear matter (semi-inclusive DIS, Drell-Yan) and hot nuclear matter (SPS, RHIC, LHC heavy ion collisions).

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at RHIC: epsilon_0 = 12-20 GeV/fm^3, approximately 100 times cold nuclear matter density (epsilon_cold = 0.14 GeV/fm^3)
- Initial temperature at RHIC: T_0 = 360-410 MeV, greater than or equal to 2 T_c (where T_c = 171 +/- 4 MeV for 2-flavor QCD and T_c = 154 +/- 6 MeV for 3-flavor QCD)
- Cold nuclear matter transport coefficient from Drell-Yan analysis: mu^2/lambda_g = 0.14 +/- 0.1 GeV^2/fm
- Cold nuclear matter absorptive power for quark jets: -dE_q/dz = 0.5 GeV/fm from semi-inclusive DIS quenching
- Soft gluon rapidity density at RHIC used in calculations: dN^g/dy = 1150, comparable to the y=0 net hadron rapidity density
- QGP lifetime at RHIC: tau_tot - tau_0 = 5-7 fm
- Mean energy loss per unit length for quark jets in hot matter at RHIC: < -dE_q/dz > = 3.2-4.6 GeV/fm (for E_jet = 15 GeV in a static plasma of epsilon_0)

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on jet quenching by Wang (1994) and the LPM effect in QCD, and summarizes the quantitative applications developed by Gyulassy, Baier, Zakharov, and collaborators. The paper synthesizes results from semi-inclusive DIS measurements at HERMES (Wang, 2002), Drell-Yan analysis (Arleo, 2003), and RHIC data from PHENIX and STAR (Adler, 2003) to establish jet tomography as a quantitative tool. It enables future studies of jet cone reconstruction, di-jet correlations, and energy balance measurements at RHIC and LHC to map out QGP properties and multiparton dynamics in ultradense nuclear matter.
