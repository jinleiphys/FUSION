# Superfluid vortices in neutron stars

> A microscopic, quantum mechanical model for neutron vortices in the crust of a neutron star is presented. After a brief introduction to the Bogoliubov- de Gennes equations, which form the basis for our calculations, we present results for density distributions, vortex core sizes and vortex energies, both for an isolated neutron vortex and for the case when the vortex core overlaps with a cylindrical nucleus. Earlier results on the vortex core size are confirmed, indicating a much less dramatic variation of the vortex core size with density than predicted by the BCS formula.

# Superfluid vortices in neutron stars

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully quantum mechanical, self-consistent Bogoliubov-de Gennes model for neutron vortices in the inner crust of a neutron star, confirming that the vortex core size varies much less dramatically with density than the BCS coherence length predicts, but only under the condition that the pairing strength is treated as density-dependent and the calculations include multiple wavenumber states along the vortex axis.

## Method

The authors solve the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations self-consistently for a neutron superfluid in cylindrical geometry, expanding quasiparticle states in Bessel function bases with quantum numbers for radial index, angular momentum projection (half-odd integers), and wavenumber along the vortex axis. They treat both isolated vortices and cases where the vortex core overlaps with a cylindrical nucleus represented by a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential, using a zero-range pairing interaction with a 10 MeV cutoff and iterating until convergence of the pairing potential and density profiles.

## Key numbers

- Vortex core size defined as $\xi_2$ (intersection of tangent at origin with asymptotic gap) remains below 15 fm for essentially all densities considered, in contrast to the BCS coherence length which scales as $k_F/\Delta_{\infty}$.
- For $k_F = 0.8$ fm$^{-1}$, the pairing potential and neutron density are shown for four configurations: isolated vortex, isolated nucleus, vortex plus nucleus, and uniform matter.
- The pairing strength $|g|$ is approximately inversely proportional to the Fermi wavenumber, with $|g| k_F \approx$ constant modulo a slowly varying logarithmic factor.
- The smallest bound-state eigenvalue in the vortex core is of order $\sim \Delta_{\infty}^2/E_F$.
- The analytic estimate of vortex core size from Kramer is given as $\xi_1 = 661.3 / (k_F^2 |g|)$ in units of MeV and fm.
- The cutoff energy for the pairing sum is taken as $\hbar\Omega = 10$ MeV.
- Typical nuclear mean-field parameters: $W_0 \sim -30$ to $-40$ MeV, $R_N \sim 5$ fm, $a \sim 0.6$ fm.
- The cylindrical cell radius is typically 50-100 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Paper I, De Blasio 1999) by including fully three-dimensional calculations with multiple $k_z$ states, a density-dependent pairing force, and the Hartree mean field, going beyond the $k_z=0$ approximation used in type-II superconductor vortex calculations (Gygi, 1990). The results contradict the simple BCS coherence length scaling and support the finding that vortex cores are smaller than previously assumed, which implies larger pinning forces for the vortex-nucleus interaction relevant to the standard model of pulsar glitches (Alpar & Pines, 1985). The work enables more reliable calculations of pinning energies and forces needed for glitch models and postglitch relaxation in the vortex creep framework.
