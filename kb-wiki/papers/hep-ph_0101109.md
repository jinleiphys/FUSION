# Quantum Nucleardynamics as an SU(2)_N \times U(1)_Z Gauge Theory

> It is shown that quantum nucleardynamics (QND) as an SU(2)_N \times U(1)_Z gauge theory, which is generated from quantum chromodynamics (QCD) as an SU(3)_C gauge theory through dynamical spontaneous symmetry breaking, successfully describes nuclear phenomena at low energies. The proton and neutron assigned as a strong isospin doublet are identified as a colorspin plus weak isospin doublet. Massive gluon mediates strong interactions with the effective coupling constant G_R/\sqrt{2}= g_n^2/8 M_G^2 \approx 10 GeV^{-2} just like Fermi weak constant G_F/\sqrt{2} = g_w^2/8 M_W^2 \approx 10^{-5} GeV^{-2} in the Glashow-Weinberg-Salam model where g_n and g_w are the coupling constants and M_G and M_W are the gauge boson masses. Explicit evidences such as lifetimes and cross sections of nuclear scattering and reaction, nuclear matter and charge densities, nucleon-nucleon scattering, magnetic dipole moment, gamma decay, etc. are shown in support of QND. The baryon number conservation is the consequence of the U(1)_Z gauge theory and the proton number conservation is the consequence of the U(1)_f gauge theory.

# Quantum Nucleardynamics as an SU(2)_N × U(1)_Z Gauge Theory

## Key claim

Quantum nucleardynamics (QND) as an SU(2)_N × U(1)_Z gauge theory, generated from quantum chromodynamics through dynamical spontaneous symmetry breaking, can describe nuclear phenomena at low energies, but only if the proton and neutron are reinterpreted as a colorspin plus weak isospin doublet rather than a conventional strong isospin doublet, and if the gluon is taken as a massive gauge boson with an effective coupling constant G_R/√2 ≈ 10 GeV^{-2}.

## Method

The paper constructs QND as a gauge theory with Lagrangian density analogous to QCD but with SU(2)_N × U(1)_Z symmetry, including a Θ term for nonperturbative effects. The proton and neutron are assigned as a colorspin doublet (up/down colorspin) combined with weak isospin, and the strong interaction is mediated by massive gluons. The effective strong coupling constant G_R/√2 = g_n²/8M_G² ≈ 10 GeV^{-2} is defined in analogy with the Fermi constant, and the color mixing angle sin²θ_R = 1/4 is set analogously to the Weinberg angle. The theory is applied to compute lifetimes, cross sections, magnetic moments, and nuclear matter properties using the massive gluon exchange formalism.

## Key numbers

- Effective strong coupling constant G_R/√2 = g_n²/8M_G² ≈ 10 GeV^{-2}, compared to Fermi weak constant G_F/√2 ≈ 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}
- Σ⁰ hyperon lifetime τ ≈ 10^{-23} s, estimated from decay width Γ = 36 MeV at central mass 1385 MeV with Q-value 130 MeV
- Strong decay Δ → n + p lifetime τ ≈ 10^{-23} s, compared to weak decay Σ → n + π lifetime τ ≈ 10^{-10} s
- Strong interaction cross section σ ≈ G_R²T² ≈ 10^{-30} m², compared to weak interaction cross section σ ≈ 10^{-44} m²
- Cross section ratio σ(π + p → π + p)/σ(ν + p → ν + p) ≈ (10 mb)/(10^{-11} mb) at kinetic energy T = 1 GeV
- Gluon mass M_G ≈ 300 MeV, corresponding to interaction distance of about 1 fm
- Nuclear number density n_B ≈ 1.95 × 10^{38} cm^{-3}, with Fermi momentum k_F = 1.33 fm^{-1} and Fermi energy ε_F ≈ 37 MeV
- Nucleon mass radius r₀ ≈ 1.2 fm, from r₀ = 1/(2 m_n α_z)
- Landé spin g-factors: g_s^p = 5.59 for proton, g_s^n = -3.83 for neutron
- Neutron electric dipole moment bound Θ ≤ 10^{-9}
- Baryon asymmetry δ_B ≈ 10^{-10}
- Total baryon number B = N_B ≈ 10^{78}
- Effective electromagnetic coupling α_f^{eff} = α_s/64 ≈ 1/137 when α_s = 0.48
- Separation energy of valence nucleon m_n α_f²/2 ≈ 0.42 MeV with α_f ≈ 0.03
- NN scattering cross sections in zero-energy limit: σ_pp ≈ σ_nn ≈ 35 b, σ_pn ≈ 66 b for spin singlet, σ_pn ≈ 4 b for spin triplet

## Context

This work extends the author's previous development of QND from QCD through dynamical spontaneous symmetry breaking (Roh, 2000; Roh, 2001) and is presented in analogy with the Glashow-Weinberg-Salam model (Glashow, 1961; Weinberg, 1967; Salam, 1968) for electroweak interactions. The paper contrasts QND with conventional nuclear effective models, which the author argues are limited to narrow energy ranges and specific phenomena, whereas QND claims applicability from zero energy to the order of 10² GeV. The approach enables predictions for parity violation in baryon spectra, CP violation in the neutron electric dipole moment, and a unified treatment of nuclear scattering, magnetic moments, and nuclear matter density that the author claims is not achievable with existing effective models.
