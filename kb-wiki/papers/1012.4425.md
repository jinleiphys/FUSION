# Progress in Lattice QCD at non-zero temperature and QGP

> I review recent progress in lattice QCD at non-zero temperature whith emphasis on the calculations of equation and the deconfinement as well as the chiral aspects of the QCD transition at non-zero temperature. I also briefly discuss meson correlation functions at high temperatures.

## Key claim

The finite temperature QCD transition is an analytic crossover (not a true phase transition) for physical quark masses, and the chiral transition temperature is estimated to lie in the range (147–164) MeV, with significant systematic differences between lattice actions that are only partially resolved by finer lattices.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD calculations using improved staggered fermion actions (p4, asqtad, HISQ, stout) on lattices with temporal extents Nτ = 4, 6, 8, and 12. The equation of state is obtained by computing the trace anomaly (interaction measure) from local gluonic and fermionic operators, then integrating to obtain pressure, energy density, and entropy density. The chiral and deconfinement transition temperatures are extracted from the renormalized Polyakov loop and the subtracted chiral condensate, while meson correlation functions are studied via Euclidean time correlators and the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) for spectral function reconstruction.

## Key numbers

- The chiral transition temperature from p4 action on Nτ = 4 and 6 lattices: Tc = 192(4)(7) MeV
- The chiral transition temperature from asqtad action (preliminary): Tc = (164 ± 6) MeV
- The chiral transition temperature from stout action (renormalized susceptibility): Tc ≈ 147 MeV
- The energy density at high temperatures reaches about 90% of the ideal gas value for p4 and asqtad actions
- The pressure at the highest temperatures is about 15% below the ideal gas value for p4 and asqtad actions
- The energy density is about 20% below the ideal gas limit for the stout action
- The trace anomaly (ε-3p)/T⁴ calculated with stout action is about 50% smaller in the high temperature region compared to p4 and asqtad results
- The singlet free energy shows exponential screening at distances rT > 0.8, with screening mass mD proportional to T
- The singlet free energy saturates at distances of about 1 fm due to string breaking in the presence of dynamical quarks

## Context

This review extends lattice QCD thermodynamics calculations from early pure SU(2) gauge theory work by Kuti (1981), McLerran (1981), and Engels (1981), and from the solution of the pure gluonic equation of state by Boyd (1996). It addresses discrepancies between different improved staggered fermion actions (p4, asqtad, stout, HISQ) that have emerged in calculations by the MILC, HotQCD, and Budapest-Wuppertal collaborations. The results enable controlled continuum extrapolations of the equation of state and transition temperature, and provide lattice constraints for potential model studies of quarkonium binding and for resummed perturbative calculations of QGP thermodynamics.
