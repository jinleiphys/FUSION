# General Multipole Expansion of Polarization Observables in Deuteron Electrodisintegration

> Formal expressions are derived for the multipole expansion of the structure functions of a general polarization observable of exclusive electrodisintegration of the deuteron using a longitudinally polarized beam and/or an oriented target. This allows one to exhibit explicitly the angular dependence of the structure functions by expanding them in terms of the small rotation matrices $d^j_{m'm}(θ)$, whose coefficients are given in terms of the electromagnetic multipole matrix elements. Furthermore, explicit expressions for the coefficients of the angular distributions of the differential cross section including multipoles up to $L_{max}=3$ are listed in tabular form.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general multipole expansion for all polarization observables in exclusive deuteron electrodisintegration, valid for longitudinally polarized electron beams and/or oriented deuteron targets, but the authors note that the multipole expansion converges slowly along the quasifree ridge, limiting its practical applicability to regions away from that ridge where final-state interactions and two-body currents become significant.

## Method

The authors start from the general formalism for observables in deuteron electrodisintegration developed in earlier work (Arenhoevel et al., 1993) and express the T-matrix in a coupled-spin representation using the Blatt-Biedenharn convention for the outgoing np-scattering state. They then insert the multipole expansion of the t-matrix into the bilinear Hermitean forms that define the structure functions, using Clebsch-Gordan series for the small rotation matrices and sum rules for 3j-symbols to obtain the coefficients of the expansion in terms of reduced electric, magnetic, and charge multipole matrix elements. The final result factorizes the coefficients into a part containing the angular momentum selection rules for the electromagnetic multipoles and a part reflecting the spin structure of the final-state polarization observables.

## Key numbers

- Multipole expansion includes contributions up to \(L_{max}=3\) for the coefficients of the angular distributions of the differential cross section, listed in tabular form
- The multipole series converges rapidly for final state c.m. energy of 120 MeV and \(\vec q^{\,2} < 2\) fm\(^{-2}\), as shown in FaA76
- The coefficients vanish for \(K > 2L_{max} + I\), where \(I\) is the rank of the deuteron polarization (0, 1, or 2)
- The deuteron density matrix is characterized by vector polarization \(P_1^d\) and tensor polarization \(P_2^d\), with \(P_0^d = 1\)

## Context

This work extends the multipole expansion techniques previously applied to deuteron photodisintegration (Arenhoevel, 1988) and to polarization observables in coincidence electron scattering from nuclei (Raskin and Donnelly, 1989) to all possible polarization observables in deuteron electrodisintegration. It builds on the authors' systematic study of inclusive and exclusive deuteron electrodisintegration (Arenhoevel et al., 1993; Arenhoevel et al., 2000) and their general criterion for selecting complete sets of independent observables (Arenhoevel et al., 1998). The results enable detailed comparison between theory and experiment for testing high-precision NN-potential models, particularly in kinematic regions where final-state interactions and two-body currents are significant.
