# Equation of state constraints from the threshold binary mass for prompt collapse of neutron star mergers

> Using hydrodynamical simulations for a large set of high-density matter equations of state (EoSs) we systematically determine the threshold mass M_thres for prompt black-hole formation in equal-mass and asymmetric neutron star (NS) mergers. We devise the so far most direct, general and accurate method to determine the unknown maximum mass of nonrotating NSs from merger observations revealing M_thres. Considering hybrid EoSs with hadron-quark phase transition, we identify a new, observable signature of quark matter in NS mergers. Furthermore, our findings have direct applications in gravitational wave searches, kilonova interpretations and multi-messenger constraints on NS properties.

# Equation of state constraints from the threshold binary mass for prompt collapse of neutron star mergers

## Key claim

The threshold binary mass for prompt black-hole formation in neutron star mergers, combined with the tidal deformability measured at that threshold, provides a direct and accurate method to determine the maximum mass of nonrotating neutron stars, with the caveat that the relation holds most tightly for purely hadronic equations of state and that hybrid models with hadron-quark phase transitions systematically deviate from the hadronic fit.

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical simulations of neutron star mergers using a smooth particle hydrodynamics code with the conformal flatness approximation for gravity. They computed the threshold binary mass for prompt black-hole formation for 23 hadronic equations of state (base sample), 8 additional hadronic EoSs incompatible with GW170817 constraints, and 9 hybrid EoSs with first-order hadron-quark phase transitions, for both equal-mass mergers (q=1) and asymmetric mergers with mass ratio q=0.7. Bilinear fits were constructed relating the maximum mass of nonrotating neutron stars to the threshold binary mass and the combined tidal deformability at that threshold.

## Key numbers

- For equal-mass mergers (q=1) with hadronic EoSs, the bilinear fit M_max = 0.632 * M_thres - 0.002 * Lambda_tilde_thres + 0.802 M_sun has a maximum residual of 0.067 M_sun and an average deviation of 0.023 M_sun
- For asymmetric mergers (q=0.7) with hadronic EoSs, the bilinear fit has a maximum residual of 0.078 M_sun and an average deviation of 0.023 M_sun
- The range of combined tidal deformability at threshold for equal-mass mergers is 200 less than or similar to Lambda_tilde_thres less than or similar to 450
- The range of combined tidal deformability at threshold for asymmetric mergers (q=0.7) is 200 less than or similar to Lambda_tilde_thres less than or similar to 650
- Assuming M_thres measured within 0.05 M_sun and Lambda_tilde_thres within 5%, error propagation yields Delta M_max = 0.06 M_sun
- The maximum mass of nonrotating NSs across the base sample ranges from 1.932 M_sun (VBAG) to 2.787 M_sun (NL3)
- The threshold binary mass for equal-mass mergers ranges from 2.675 M_sun (eosAU, WFF1) to 3.775 M_sun (NL3, GS1)

## Context

This work extends the earlier relation M_thres = (-3.606 * G * M_max / (c^2 * R_1.6) + 2.38) * M_max from Bauswein (2013) by using a much larger set of equations of state, incorporating asymmetric binaries, and employing directly measurable quantities (tidal deformability rather than radius). The new bilinear fits are significantly more accurate (maximum residual 0.067 M_sun versus 0.26 M_sun for the older relation) and enable direct implementation in gravitational-wave analysis pipelines. The identification of a distinct signature for hybrid EoSs with quark matter phase transitions opens a new observational channel for probing the QCD phase diagram at neutron star densities, complementing heavy-ion experiments such as HADES (2019) and theoretical work by Blaschke (2016).
