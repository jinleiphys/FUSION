# Free magnetized knots of parity-violating deconfined matter in heavy-ion collisions

> We show that the local parity violation in the quark-gluon plasma supports existence of free (meta)stable knots of deconfined hot quark matter stabilized by superstrong magnetic fields. The magnetic field in the knots resembles the spheromak plasma state of the magnetic confinement approach to nuclear fusion. The size of the knot is quantized, being inversely proportional to the chiral conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma. The parity symmetry is broken inside the knot. Particles produced in the decays of the knots have unusual azimuthal distribution and specific flavor content. We argue that these knots may be created in noncentral heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that local parity violation in the quark-gluon plasma can support the existence of free (meta)stable knots of deconfined hot quark matter, stabilized by superstrong magnetic fields, but only if the interior of the knot is in a deconfined and chirally restored phase; the knot cannot exist if its interior is in the chirally broken phase because the chiral condensate would wash out the imbalance between left- and right-handed quarks, leading to vanishing chiral conductivity.

## Method

The author solves the static Maxwell equations in a parity-odd medium, supplementing them with the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) constitutive relation j = sigma_chi B, where sigma_chi is the chiral conductivity. This reduces the problem to the eigenvalue problem of the curl operator (nabla x B = sigma_chi B) inside a spherical domain with the boundary condition that the radial component of B vanishes at the surface. The resulting solutions are Chandrasekhar-Kendall (Taylor) states, and the author identifies the lowest-energy spheromak-like knot solution with quantized radius R_0 = kappa_0 / sigma_chi, where kappa_0 = 4.49341 is the first positive zero of the Bessel function J_{3/2}.

## Key numbers

* The lowest bound of the maximal magnetic field in noncentral Pb-Pb collisions at LHC is e B^{max}_{LHC} >= 15 m_pi^2, where m_pi ≈ 140 MeV.
* The knot radius is quantized as R_0 = kappa_0 / sigma_chi, with kappa_0 = 4.49341.
* For a qualitative estimate at T ~ 200 MeV with chiral conductivity sigma_chi ~ 80 MeV and magnetic field scale e B_0 ~ m_pi^2, the knot radius R_0 ~ 10 fm, total magnetic energy E_magn ~ 150 GeV, magnetic moment |M| ~ 2.5 x 10^4 mu_N (nuclear magneton), and minimal baryon charge Q_B ~ 2.5 x 10^3.
* The standard eccentricity of the electric currents in the knot is epsilon_j = -0.168.
* The elliptic flow coefficient of decay products is v_2 ≈ 0.2378, and the ratio v_4/v_2^2 = -2.01.
* The maximal flow of decay products is concentrated at azimuthal angle phi_max ≈ 20 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME), proposed by Kharzeev (1998) and developed in 2007-2008, by showing that the CME can stabilize magnetohydrodynamic knots in the quark-gluon plasma. The magnetic field configuration is identical to the spheromak plasma state used in nuclear fusion confinement (Taylor, 1974) and to Chandrasekhar-Kendall states (1957) previously applied to astrophysical plasmas like the Crab Nebula (Woltjer, 1958). The paper enables experimental searches for these knots at RHIC and ALICE/LHC via their distinctive decay signatures: a 1:2 ratio of u to d quarks, large elliptic flow v_2 ≈ 1/4, negative v_4/v_2^2 ratio, and nonzero sine harmonics in the azimuthal distribution that indicate parity violation.
