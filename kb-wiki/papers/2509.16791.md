# Subtracted second Tamm-Dancoff approximation in the relativistic point-coupling model

> Theoretical description of collective nuclear excitations and astrophysically relevant processes require methods going beyond the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) or Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (TDA), which are limited to one-particle-one-hole (1p-1h) configurations. In this work, we introduce the Second Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (STDA) based on the relativistic nuclear energy density functional, which also incorporates two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) configurations, providing a more comprehensive description of nuclear excitations. Within the relativistic STDA (RSTDA), we implement the subtraction method, that is essential to avoid double counting of correlations and provide realistic description of excited states. Using the DD-PC1 parametrization of the relativistic point-coupling interaction, in the first application of the RSTDA we investigate the properties of isoscalar monopole and quadrupole transitions in 16O. The analysis reveals important role of complex configurations on centroid energies, transition strength distributions, and the fine structure of nuclear response. By resolving the fragmentation and spreading of excitation strength, the RSTDA enables accurate description of experimental spectra.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the first fully self-consistent relativistic Second Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (RSTDA) with a subtraction method (RSSTDA) to avoid double counting of correlations, but the results show that the diagonal approximation (RSSTDA(d)) fails to capture the experimental low-energy structure of monopole excitations in 16O, and the centroid energies systematically overestimate experimental values by about 1.8 MeV even in the best case.

## Method

The authors implement the relativistic Second Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (RSTDA) within the relativistic nuclear energy density functional framework using the DD-PC1 point-coupling interaction, incorporating two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) configurations in addition to the standard one-particle-one-hole (1p-1h) and antiparticle-hole (1α-1h) configurations. They solve the RSTDA eigenvalue problem in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with principal quantum numbers up to N=20 for monopole and N=10 for quadrupole transitions, applying an energy cutoff for 2p-2h configurations and implementing a subtraction method (RSSTDA) to correct for double counting of correlations. The calculations include both the full form of the A22 matrix (including interactions among 2p-2h states) and a diagonal approximation (RSTDA(d) and RSSTDA(d)) where only unperturbed 2p-2h energies are used.

## Key numbers

* ISGMR centroid energy (m1/m0) for full spectrum: RTDA = 25.055 MeV, RSSTDA(d) = 27.497 MeV, RSSTDA = 28.069 MeV; within experimental window (11-40 MeV): RTDA = 23.883 MeV, RSSTDA(d) = 23.148 MeV, RSSTDA = 22.887 MeV (experimental value: 21.13 ± 0.49 MeV)
* ISGMR m1 moment increases by 12.0% from RTDA (691.180 e²MeVfm⁴) to RSSTDA (774.098 e²MeVfm⁴)
* ISGMR effective transition energy √(m1/m-1): RTDA = 24.391 MeV, RSSTDA(d) = 25.548 MeV, RSSTDA = 25.813 MeV (experimental: 19.63 ± 0.38 MeV)
* ISGMR energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) exhaustion: RTDA = 95.74%, RSSTDA(d) = 105.04%, RSSTDA = 107.22% for full spectrum; within experimental window: RTDA = 87.15%, RSSTDA(d) = 80.88%, RSSTDA = 77.09% (experimental: 48 ± 10%)
* ISGMR width Δ for full spectrum: RTDA = 7.811 MeV, RSSTDA(d) = 17.747 MeV, RSSTDA = 18.001 MeV (experimental: 8.76 ± 1.82 MeV)
* For RSSTDA with 2p-2h cutoff change from 150 to 180 MeV, peak position shifts toward lower energies by ΔE_x^{SSTDA} ≤ 150 keV and ΔE_x^{SSTDA(d)} ≤ 100 keV
* Calculated 0+ states in 16O appear at approximately 4 MeV, 8.7 MeV, 12.7 MeV, and 13.1 MeV (experimental 0+ states at 6.05 MeV, 12.05 MeV, and 14.01 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Second Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (STDA) and Second Random Phase Approximation (SRPA) previously developed by Sawicki (1962), Providencia, Yannouleas, Drozdz (1990), Papakonstantinou (2009, 2010), Gambacurta (2011, 2015, 2020), and Yang (2021, 2024) into the relativistic framework, building on a preliminary diagonal RSTDA model by Vale and Paar (2024). The subtraction method follows the approach of Tselyaev (2007, 2013) and Gambacurta (2015) to avoid double counting and infrared instabilities. The results enable future development of the full relativistic SRPA and provide a benchmark for comparing relativistic and non-relativistic treatments of 2p-2h configurations in light nuclei such as 16O, though the overestimation of centroid energies suggests that more complex configurations beyond 2p-2h may be needed for quantitative agreement with experiment.
