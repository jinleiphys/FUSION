# Revealing proton shape fluctuations with incoherent diffraction at high energy

> The differential cross section of exclusive diffractive vector meson production in electron proton collisions carries important information on the geometric structure of the proton. More specifically, the coherent cross section as a function of the transferred transverse momentum is sensitive to the size of the proton, while the incoherent, or proton dissociative cross section is sensitive to fluctuations of the gluon distribution in coordinate space. We show that at high energies the experimentally measured coherent and incoherent cross sections for the production of $J/Ψ$ mesons are very well reproduced within the color glass condensate framework when strong geometric fluctuations of the gluon distribution in the proton are included. For $ρ$ meson production we also find reasonable agreement. We study in detail the dependence of our results on various model parameters, including the average proton shape, analyze the effect of saturation scale and color charge fluctuations and constrain the degree of geometric fluctuations.

## Key claim

The experimentally measured coherent and incoherent diffractive cross sections for J/Ψ meson production at HERA are well reproduced within the color glass condensate (CGC) framework only when strong geometric fluctuations of the gluon distribution in the proton are included; for ρ meson production the agreement is reasonable but less precise.

## Method

The authors compute exclusive diffractive vector meson production in electron-proton collisions using the dipole picture within the impact parameter dependent saturation (IPsat) model and the IP-Glasma model. The coherent cross section is obtained by averaging the scattering amplitude over target configurations, while the incoherent cross section is given by the variance of the amplitude, which is sensitive to event-by-event fluctuations of the proton's gluon density profile. Geometric fluctuations are implemented using a constituent quark model where three quark positions are sampled from a Gaussian distribution, and the proton's thickness function is the sum of Gaussian profiles centered on each quark.

## Key numbers

- Proton gluonic root mean square radius from coherent HERA data fit: r_p = 0.55 fm (corresponding to B_p = 4.0 GeV^{-2} in the Gaussian profile)
- For J/Ψ photoproduction at ⟨W⟩ = 100 GeV, the corresponding xpom = 9.6 × 10^{-4} at t=0
- For J/Ψ photoproduction at ⟨W⟩ = 75 GeV, the corresponding xpom = 1.7 × 10^{-3}
- Including geometric fluctuations (B_qc = 3.3 GeV^{-2}, B_q = 0.7 GeV^{-2}) decreases F_2 at x ~ 10^{-3}, Q^2 ~ 10 GeV^2 by approximately 8%
- Charm quark mass used: m_c = 1.4 GeV
- Infrared cutoff in IP-Glasma model: m = 0.4 GeV (unless otherwise noted)
- Lattice spacing for IP-Glasma calculations: a = 0.02 fm
- Number of longitudinal slices in IP-Glasma: N_y = 100

## Context

This work extends the analysis of a recent Letter by Mantysaari and Schenke (2016) that first pointed out the importance of geometric fluctuations for describing the incoherent cross section measured at HERA. It builds on the Good-Walker picture of diffraction and the IPsat model of Kowalski, Teaney, and Venugopalan (2003), which successfully described HERA inclusive DIS data. The results enable future constraints on proton shape fluctuations from ultra-peripheral heavy ion and proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC, and provide input for hydrodynamic simulations of small collision systems that aim to explain collective phenomena observed in high-multiplicity proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions.
