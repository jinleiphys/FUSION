# Calculating Quenching Weights

> We calculate the probability (``quenching weight'') that a hard parton radiates an additional energy fraction due to scattering in spatially extended QCD matter. This study is based on an exact treatment of finite in-medium path length, it includes the case of a dynamically expanding medium, and it extends to the angular dependence of the medium-induced gluon radiation pattern. All calculations are done in the multiple soft scattering approximation (Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigné-Schiff--Zakharov ``BDMPS-Z''-formalism) and in the single hard scattering approximation (N=1 opacity approximation). By comparison, we establish a simple relation between transport coefficient, Debye screening mass and opacity, for which both approximations lead to comparable results. Together with this paper, a CPU-inexpensive numerical subroutine for calculating quenching weights is provided electronically. To illustrate its applications, we discuss the suppression of hadronic transverse momentum spectra in nucleus-nucleus collisions. Remarkably, the kinematic constraint resulting from finite in-medium path length reduces significantly the transverse momentum dependence of the nuclear modification factor, thus leading to consistency with the data measured at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

The paper calculates the probability distribution ("quenching weight") for additional medium-induced energy loss of a hard parton traversing QCD matter, finding that the kinematic constraint from finite in-medium path length significantly reduces the transverse momentum dependence of the nuclear modification factor, which leads to consistency with RHIC data, a result that depends on the specific approximations (multiple soft scattering or single hard scattering) and the finite path length.

## Method

The authors computed the medium-induced gluon energy distribution using the path-integral formalism of the BDMPS-Z framework for multiple soft scattering and the first-order opacity (N=1) approximation for single hard scattering, modeling the medium via a transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) or a Debye screening mass \(\mu\) with scattering center density \(n_0\). They then constructed the quenching weight \(P(\Delta E)\) from this energy distribution via Laplace transformation, assuming independent gluon emissions, and provided a numerical subroutine for its evaluation.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic gluon frequency in the multiple soft scattering approximation is \(\omega_c = \frac{1}{2} \hat{q} L^2\) (Equation 2.6).
- The average parton energy loss in the multiple soft scattering limit (no kinematic constraint) is \(\langle \Delta E \rangle_{R\to\infty} = \frac{\alpha_s C_R}{2} \omega_c\) (Equation 2.11).
- The characteristic gluon energy in the single hard scattering approximation is \(\bar{\omega}_c = \frac{1}{2} \mu^2 L\) (Equation 2.17).
- The average parton energy loss for a single hard scattering (no kinematic constraint) is \(\langle \Delta E \rangle^{N=1} \simeq \frac{\alpha_s C_R}{2} (n_0L) \bar{\omega}_c \log\left[ E/\bar{\omega}_c\right]\) (Equation 2.22).
- The kinematic constraint parameter for multiple soft scattering is \(R = \omega_c L\) (Equation 2.8); for single hard scattering it is \(\bar{R} = \bar{\omega}_c L\) (Equation 2.20).
- The discrete weight \(p_0\) (probability of no additional gluon emission) exceeds unity for small values \(R < 100\) in the multiple soft scattering approximation (Figure 6).
- The total additional medium-induced gluon multiplicity is \(N(\omega = 0) \leq 3\) for realistic kinematic constraints \(R < 10000\) (Figure 2 and Figure 4).
- The coupling constant is fixed at \(\alpha_s = 1/3\) for numerical results.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigné, Schiff, 1996; Zakharov, 1996) and the opacity approximation (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000; Wiedemann, 2000) by providing a complete calculation of quenching weights that includes finite in-medium path length and dynamically expanding media. The results enable quantitative modeling of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the provided numerical subroutine allows direct application to hadronic transverse momentum spectra, as illustrated by comparison to RHIC Au-Au collision data.
