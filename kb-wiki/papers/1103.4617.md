# Model independent analysis of proton structure for hydrogenic bound states

> Proton structure effects in hydrogenic bound states are analyzed using nonrelativistic QED effective field theory. Implications for the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen are discussed. Model-dependent assumptions in previous analyses are isolated, and sensitivity to poorly constrained hadronic structure in the two-photon exchange contribution is identified.

# Model independent analysis of proton structure for hydrogenic bound states

## Key claim

The Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen is sensitive to a new spin-independent, proton structure-dependent contact interaction in NRQED whose strength is *not* determined by measured on-shell form factors or inelastic structure functions; the dominant theoretical uncertainty from the two-photon exchange contribution has been underestimated by at least an order of magnitude compared to the commonly quoted uncertainty of less than 0.004 meV.

## Method

The authors construct the nonrelativistic QED (NRQED) effective field theory for the proton-electron (or proton-muon) system, matching Wilson coefficients to elastic form factors, forward Compton scattering amplitudes, and inelastic structure functions. They decompose the two-photon exchange correction into a proton pole term, a continuum term from inelastic structure functions, and a term from the poorly constrained forward Compton amplitude \(W_1(0,Q^2)\) at intermediate momentum transfers, whose small-\(Q^2\) expansion and large-\(Q^2\) asymptotic behavior are fixed by NRQED and the operator product expansion respectively.

## Key numbers

- The measured \(2S_{j=1/2}^{(F=1)}-2P_{j=3/2}^{(F=2)}\) interval in muonic hydrogen lies \(0.258(90)\,\text{meV}\) or \(0.311(63)\,\text{meV}\) above theory, depending on which proton charge radius is used (electron scattering: \(r=0.871(10)\,\text{fm}\); electronic hydrogen spectroscopy: \(r=0.8768(69)\,\text{fm}\)).
- The proton pole contribution to the muonic hydrogen \(nS\) two-photon exchange is \(\delta E^{\text{proton}}_{\mu H}(nS) \approx (8/n^3)(0.016\,\text{meV})\).
- The continuum contribution is \(\delta E^{\text{continuum}}_{\mu H}(nS) \approx (8/n^3)(-0.0127(5)\,\text{meV})\) from Carlson (2011), consistent with earlier results \(-0.014(2)\,\text{meV}\) from Pachucki (1999) and \(-0.016(3)\,\text{meV}\) from Martynenko (2005).
- In the SIFF model, the \(W_1(0,Q^2)\) contribution is \(\delta E^{W_1(0,Q^2),\text{SIFF}}_{\mu H}(nS) = (8/n^3)(-0.034\,\text{meV})\).
- The proton magnetic polarizability is \(\bar{\beta}=1.9(5)\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\) and electric polarizability is \(\bar{\alpha}=12.0(6)\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\) from Nakamura (2010).

## Context

This work extends the NRQED framework of Caswell and Lepage (1986) to systematically classify proton structure corrections in hydrogenic bound states, resolving ambiguities in earlier treatments by Pachucki (1996, 1999), Borie (2005), and Eides, Grotch, and Shelyuto (2001). The analysis identifies that the commonly used "Sticking In Form Factors" (SIFF) model for the forward Compton amplitude \(W_1(0,Q^2)\) is not derived from a local field theory and lacks the correct large-\(Q^2\) behavior, calling into question the theoretical predictions used in the muonic hydrogen measurement by Pohl et al. (2010). The formalism enables a model-independent extraction of the contact interaction coefficient \(d_2\) from the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, and can be extended to deuterium and to spin polarizabilities of the proton.
