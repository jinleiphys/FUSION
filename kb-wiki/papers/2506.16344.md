# Effects of hadronic reinteraction on jet fragmentation from small to large systems

> We investigate the impact of the hadronic phase on jet quenching in nuclear collider experiments, an open question in heavy-ion physics. Previous studies in a simplified setup suggest that hadronic interactions could have significant effects, but a systematic analysis is needed. Using the X-SCAPE event generator with the SMASH afterburner, we study the role of hadronic rescattering on jet fragmentation hadrons. Applying this framework to $e^++e^-$ collisions, we demonstrate that even in small systems with limited particle production, hadronic interactions lead to measurable modifications in final-state hadronic and jet observables by comparing scenarios with and without afterburner rescattering.

## Key claim

Hadronic rescattering in the afterburner phase produces measurable modifications in final-state hadronic and jet observables even in the dilute environment of \(e^++e^-\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=91.2\;\mathrm{GeV}\), but the magnitude of these effects depends on a free parameter (the free-streaming time before rescattering) whose variation from 0.1 fm to 1.0 fm does not significantly change the results.

## Method

The authors used the X-SCAPE 1.2 event generator with a PYTHIA 8.3 \(e^++e^-\) gun module at \(\sqrt{s}=91.2\;\mathrm{GeV}\), MATTER for parton showering, and Hybrid Hadronization (which interpolates between quark recombination and Lund string fragmentation) to produce hadrons. These hadrons were then fed into the SMASH hadronic afterburner, which solves the Boltzmann equation for hadronic states up to \(m \leq 2.35\;\mathrm{GeV}\), and three simulation setups were compared: decays only, rescattering after 0.1 fm free-streaming, and rescattering after 1.0 fm free-streaming.

## Key numbers

- \(1.5 \times 10^6\) events were simulated for each of the three setups at \(\sqrt{s}=91.2\;\mathrm{GeV}\) in \(e^++e^-\) collisions.
- Hadronic states up to mass \(m \leq 2.35\;\mathrm{GeV}\) are implemented in SMASH under isospin symmetry.
- Jets were identified with a minimum transverse momentum \(p_T \geq 10\;\mathrm{GeV}\), pseudorapidity \(|\eta| \leq 1.74\), and jet radius \(R = 0.8\).
- The hadronic \(x_p\) spectrum (defined as \(x_p \equiv 2|\vec{p}|/\sqrt{s}\)) shows diffusion of hadrons from high to low momenta when rescattering is included.
- The jet fragmentation function \(D(z)\) (with \(z \equiv p_T^{\mathrm{ch}} / p_T^{\mathrm{jet}}\)) shows high-momentum hadrons transferring momentum to lower-momentum particles.
- The jet shape \(P(\Delta r)\) demonstrates that rescatterings shift hadrons from the jet core to larger radii \(\Delta r\).

## Context

This work extends the exploratory study of Dorau (2019), which used a simplified radially expanding hadron gas with a pion beam to show measurable effects of hadronic reinteractions on high-momentum hadrons. The present study implements this physics in the full X-SCAPE framework with the SMASH afterburner, enabling systematic quantification of hadronic rescattering effects on jet observables. The results provide a foundation for future investigations of \(p+p\) and heavy-ion (\(A+A\)) collisions, and may help explain two-particle correlations observed in high-multiplicity \(e^++e^-\) events that current event generators (Chen, 2023) fail to describe.
