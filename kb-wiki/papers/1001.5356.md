# Ab-initio calculation of the ${}^6Li$ binding energy with the Hybrid Multideterminant scheme

> We perform an ab-initio calculation for the binding energy of ${}^6Li$ using the CD-Bonn 2000 NN potential renormalized with the Lee-Suzuki method. The many-body approach to the problem is the Hybrid Multideterminant method. The results indicate a binding energy of about $31 MeV$, within a few hundreds KeV uncertainty. The center of mass diagnostics are also discussed.

## Key claim

The Hybrid Multideterminant (HMD) method, in its HMD-a version, yields a binding energy for ${}^6Li$ of about 31 MeV with an uncertainty of a few hundred keV, which is closer to the experimental value than previous no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using the same CD-Bonn 2000 potential. The result depends on using the HMD-a truncation scheme because the HMD-b version strongly overbinds due to missing three-particle cluster contributions.

## Method

The authors start from the CD-Bonn 2000 nucleon-nucleon potential, renormalized via the Lee-Suzuki method with Okubo hermitization in a harmonic oscillator basis. They then apply the Hybrid Multideterminant method, which expands the nuclear wave function as a sum of many symmetry-unrestricted Slater determinants with angular momentum and parity restored by projectors, optimized variationally. Two versions are compared: HMD-a, which truncates the effective Hamiltonian to a smaller number of major harmonic oscillator shells in the lab frame, and HMD-b, which uses a larger model space; the authors adopt HMD-a because it exhibits smaller three-particle cluster effects and avoids the strong overbinding seen in HMD-b.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of ${}^6Li$ at $\hbar\Omega = 15$ MeV and $N_s=4$: $-31.22$ MeV (with 400 Slater determinants)
- Ground-state energy of ${}^6Li$ at $\hbar\Omega = 12.5$ MeV and $N_s=4$: $-31.18$ MeV (with 400 Slater determinants)
- Ground-state energy of ${}^6Li$ at $\hbar\Omega = 10$ MeV and $N_s=5$: $-30.65$ MeV (partial result with 300 Slater determinants)
- Ground-state energy of ${}^6Li$ at $\hbar\Omega = 15$ MeV and $N_s=2$: $-31.525$ MeV (well converged with 150 Slater determinants)
- Experimental binding energy of ${}^6Li$: $-31.994$ MeV (from Ref. [26])
- NCSM ground-state energy for ${}^6Li$ with CD-Bonn 2000: $-29.07$ MeV (from Refs. [18,19])
- Excitation energy of the first $3^+$ state in ${}^6Li$ at $N_s=6$ and $\hbar\Omega=12.5$ MeV: $2.9$ MeV (experimental value: $2.18$ MeV)
- For ${}^4He$ at $\hbar\Omega=20$ MeV and $N_s=7$: ground-state energy $-26.354$ MeV (uncertainty $140$ keV)
- For ${}^3H$ at $\hbar\Omega=16$ MeV and $N_s=6$ (HMD-a): ground-state energy $-8.03$ MeV (Faddeev result: $-7.998$ MeV)

## Context

This work extends the Hybrid Multideterminant method, previously applied with the Argonne v8' potential (Puddu, 2007), to the CD-Bonn 2000 interaction for ${}^6Li$. It contrasts with NCSM calculations using the same potential (Navratil et al., 2001; Caurier et al., 2002) which gave $-29.07$ MeV, further from experiment. The finding that the HMD-a version yields a binding energy closer to experiment suggests that the truncation in HMD-a partially compensates for missing three-body forces induced by the Lee-Suzuki renormalization, a result that may guide future ab-initio calculations of light nuclei and motivate studies of the $3^+$ excited state and center-of-mass contamination effects.
