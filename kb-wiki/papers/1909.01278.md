# Cooling binary neutron star remnants via nucleon-nucleon-axion bremsstrahlung

> The QCD axion is a hypothetical particle motivated by the Strong CP problem of particle physics. One of the primary ways in which its existence can be inferred is via its function as an additional cooling channel in stars, with some of the strongest constraints coming from the supernova observation SN1987A. Multimessenger observations of binary neutron star mergers (such as those of GW170817, AT2017gfo, and GRB170817A) may provide another scenario in which such constraints could be obtained. In particular, the axion could potentially alter the lifetime, the ejection of material, and the emitted gravitational wave signal of the postmerger remnant. In this article, we perform numerical relativity simulations of a binary neutron star merger, including a phenomenological description of the nucleon-nucleon-axion bremsstrahlung to quantify the effects of such a cooling channel on the dynamical evolution. While our simulations show a difference in the temperature profile of the merger remnant, the imprint of the axion via nucleon-nucleon-axion bremsstrahlung on the emitted gravitational wave signal and the ejecta mass is too small to improve constraints on the axion mass with current or future planned detectors. Whilst we consider a limited number of cases, and a simplified cooling model, these broadly represent the "best case" scenario, thus, a more thorough investigation is unlikely to change the conclusions, at least for this particular interaction channel.

## Key claim

While the simulations show a difference in the temperature profile of the merger remnant, the imprint of the axion via nucleon-nucleon-axion bremsstrahlung on the emitted gravitational wave signal and the ejecta mass is too small to improve constraints on the axion mass with current or future planned detectors; the authors note that their simplified cooling model and limited number of cases represent a "best case" scenario, so a more thorough investigation is unlikely to change the conclusions for this particular interaction channel.

## Method

The authors performed numerical relativity simulations of equal-mass, irrotational binary neutron star mergers using the BAM code with a piecewise polytropic representation of the ALF2 equation of state. They implemented a covariant cooling scheme incorporating a phenomenological description of nucleon-nucleon-axion bremsstrahlung, using the minimum of degenerate and non-degenerate energy emission rates from Brinkmann et al. (1988). Simulations were run for four different total masses and five axion masses (0, 10^-3, 10^-2, 10^-1, and 1 eV), neglecting neutrino cooling and axion reabsorption to represent the most optimistic scenario for detecting axion effects.

## Key numbers

* For the lowest total mass system (Case-4), the central density with an axion mass of 1 eV is about 7% larger than for the simulation excluding axion cooling.
* The shift in the main postmerger gravitational wave emission frequency is significantly below 50 Hz for an axion mass of 1 eV.
* The axion-nucleon coupling constant is given by g = 1.516 x 10^-7 (m_a / 1 eV).
* The degenerate energy emission rate in geometric units is 1.98 x 10^-12 (m_a / 1 eV)^2 rho^(1/3) T^6_MeV.
* The non-degenerate energy emission rate in geometric units is 8.56 x 10^-4 (m_a / 1 eV)^2 rho^2 T^3.5_MeV.
* The axion mass is related to the decay constant by m_a = (10^6 GeV / f_a) x 6 eV.

## Context

This work extends the long history of astrophysical tests for axions, particularly constraints from the supernova SN1987A observation which ruled out QCD axion masses between 10^-3 eV and 1 eV (Burrows 1988, Burrows 1990), with more recent reanalysis (Chang 2018) providing order-of-magnitude adjustments. The authors build on numerical relativity simulations of binary neutron star mergers (such as those by Paschalidis 2012) and cooling treatments from Paschalidis (2011, 2012). The results suggest that, unlike supernova cooling constraints, binary neutron star mergers cannot provide new constraints on the axion mass through the nucleon-nucleon-axion bremsstrahlung channel, though other effects such as axion-photon conversion in magnetic fields or changes in the inspiral gravitational wave signal (Brito 2017, Hook 2017, Huang 2018) may still be promising avenues for future investigation.
