# Molecular states in $D_s^{(*)+}Ξ_c^{(',*)}$ systems

> The possible hadronic molecules in $D_s^{(*)+}Ξ_c^{(',*)}$ systems with $J^P=1/2^-,3/2^-$ and $5/2^-$ are investigated with interactions described by light meson exchanges. By varying the cutoff in a phenomenologically reasonable range of $1\sim2.5$ GeV, we find ten near-threshold (bound or virtual) states in the single-channel case. After introducing the coupled-channel dynamics of $D_s^{+}Ξ_c$-$D_s^{+}Ξ_c^{'}$-$D_s^{*+}Ξ_c$-$D_s^{+}Ξ_c^{*}$-$D_s^{*+}Ξ_c^{'}$-$D_s^{*+}Ξ_c^{*}$ systems, these states, except those below the lowest channels in each $J^{P}$ sector, move into the complex energy plane and become resonances in the mass range of $4.43\sim4.76$ GeV. Their spin-parities and nearby thresholds are $1/2^-(D_s^{+}Ξ_c)$, $1/2^-(D_s^{+}Ξ_c^{'})$, $1/2^-(D_s^{*+}Ξ_c)$,$1/2^-(D_s^{*+}Ξ_c^{'})$, $1/2^-(D_s^{*+}Ξ_c^{*})$ , $3/2^-(D_s^{*+}Ξ_c)$, $3/2^-(D_s^{+}Ξ_c)$, $3/2^-(D_s^{*+}Ξ_c^{'})$, $3/2^-(D_s^{*+}Ξ_c^{*})$, and $5/2^-(D_s^{*+}Ξ_c^{*})$. The impacts of the $δ(r)$-term in the one-boson-exchange model on these states are presented. Setting $Λ=1.5$ GeV as an illustrative value, it is found that $1/2^-(D_s^{+}Ξ_c)$ is a stable bound state (becoming unstable if turning on the coupling to lower channels), $1/2^-(D_s^{*+}Ξ_c)$ and $3/2^-(D_s^{*+}Ξ_c)$ are physical resonances in both cases of including or excluding the $δ(r)$-term, while the other seven states are physical resonances or ``virtual-state-like" poles near thresholds, depending on including the $δ(r)$-term or not. In addition, the partial decay widths of the physical resonances are provided. These double-charm hidden-strangeness pentaquark states, as the partners of experimentally observed $P_c$ and $P_{cs}$ states, can be searched for in the $D^{(*)}Λ_c$ final states in future.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that ten near-threshold states in the $D_s^{(*)+}\Xi_c^{(',*)}$ systems, which appear as bound or virtual states in single-channel calculations, become resonances in the mass range of $4.43\sim4.76$ GeV when coupled-channel dynamics is introduced, with the actual existence and nature of each state depending on the treatment of the $\delta(r)$-term in the one-boson-exchange potential and the choice of cutoff parameter $\Lambda$.

## Method

The authors employed a one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential model with $\phi$, $\sigma$, and $\eta$ meson exchanges, using effective Lagrangians satisfying heavy quark spin symmetry and SU(3) flavor symmetry, to describe interactions in the $D_s^{+}\Xi_c$-$D_s^{+}\Xi_c^{'}$-$D_s^{*+}\Xi_c$-$D_s^{+}\Xi_c^{*}$-$D_s^{*+}\Xi_c^{'}$-$D_s^{*+}\Xi_c^{*}$ systems. They solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for $J^P=1/2^-$, $3/2^-$, and $5/2^-$ partial waves, varying the cutoff $\Lambda$ in the range of $1.0$ to $2.5$ GeV, and analytically continued the $S$-matrix to find poles in the complex energy plane, separately analyzing cases with ($a=0$) and without ($a=1$) the $\delta(r)$-term.

## Key numbers

- Ten near-threshold states found in single-channel analysis when cutoff varies from $1.0$ to $2.5$ GeV, with binding energies shown as functions of $\Lambda$ in Fig. 3
- Resonance masses in coupled-channel analysis range from $4.43$ to $4.76$ GeV
- Thresholds of the six channels: $D_s^{+}\Xi_c$ at $4437.76$ MeV, $D_s^{+}\Xi_c^{'}$ at $4547.14$ MeV, $D_s^{*+}\Xi_c$ at $4581.62$ MeV, $D_s^{+}\Xi_c^{*}$ at $4614.31$ MeV, $D_s^{*+}\Xi_c^{'}$ at $4691.00$ MeV, $D_s^{*+}\Xi_c^{*}$ at $4758.17$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular picture previously applied to the experimentally observed $P_c$ states (LHCb, 2015, 2019) and $P_{cs}$ states (LHCb, 2020, 2022) to double-charm hidden-strangeness pentaquark systems. It builds on the finding by Dong (2021) that $D^{(*)}\Sigma_c^{(*)}$ systems are more attractive than $\bar D^{(*)}\Sigma_c^{(*)}$ systems under vector meson dominance, and extends the study of double-charm pentaquarks to channels with hidden strangeness. The predicted states can be searched for in $D^{(*)}\Lambda_c$ final states, serving as partners of the $P_c$ and $P_{cs}$ states observed by LHCb.
