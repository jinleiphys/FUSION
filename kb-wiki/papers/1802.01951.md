# Directed, elliptic and triangular flow of protons in Au+Au reactions at 1.23 AGeV: A theoretical analysis of the recent HADES data

> Recently, the HADES experiment at GSI has provided preliminary data on the directed flow, $v_1$ elliptic flow, $v_2$ and triangular flow, $v_3$ of protons in Au+Au reactions at a beam energy of 1.23 A GeV. Here we present a theoretical discussion of these flow harmonics within the UrQMD transport approach. We show that all flow harmonics, including the triangular flow, provide a consistent picture of the expansion of the system, if potential interactions are taken into account. Investigating the dependence of the flow harmonics on the nuclear interaction potentials it is shown that especially $v_3$ can serve as a sensitive probe for the nuclear equation of state at such low energies. The triangular flow and its excitation function with respect to the reaction-plane were calculated for the first time and indicate a complex interplay of the time-evolution of the system and the initial conditions at low beam-energies. Our study also indicates a significant softening of the equation of state at beam energies above $E_{\mathrm{lab}}> 7$A GeV which can be explored by at the future FAIR facility.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model provides a good description of the HADES preliminary data on proton flow harmonics in Au+Au collisions at 1.23 A GeV only if a hard equation of state is employed, while data at beam energies above 5 A GeV consistently favor a soft equation of state, indicating a possible softening of the nuclear matter equation of state at higher energies.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model (version 3.4) to simulate mid-peripheral Au+Au collisions at impact parameters b=6-9 fm, corresponding to the 20%-30% centrality class. They compared three versions of the model: a cascade mode without potentials, and versions including Skyrme-type nuclear potentials parameterized as either a hard equation of state (EoS) or a soft EoS. The flow coefficients v1, v2, and v3 of protons at kinetic freeze-out were calculated with respect to the reaction plane and compared to preliminary HADES data and existing experimental data from other experiments across a range of beam energies.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 1.23 A GeV (fixed-target, Au+Au reactions)
- Impact parameter range for calculations: b=6-9 fm (20%-30% centrality in data)
- Hard EoS parameters: alpha = -124 MeV, beta = 71 MeV, gamma = 2.00
- Soft EoS parameters: alpha = -356 MeV, beta = 303 MeV, gamma = 1.17
- Yukawa potential parameters: V0^Y = -0.498 MeV fm, gamma_Y = 1.4 fm
- Spectator cut applied: removes particles with p_T <= 0.3 GeV and y >= 0.6
- The slope of v1 and v3 is extracted from bins at y = -0.1 +/- 0.05 and y = 0.1 +/- 0.05
- A non-zero v3 with respect to the reaction plane is observed up to beam energies of E_lab < 5 A GeV
- The softening of the EoS is indicated at beam energies above E_lab > 5 A GeV for v1 and v2, and above E_lab > 7 A GeV as mentioned in the abstract

## Context

This work extends previous transport model studies of collective flow in heavy-ion collisions at low beam energies, particularly building on the findings of Nara (2016, 2017) who observed similar EoS-dependent behavior of directed and elliptic flow. The study provides the first theoretical prediction of a finite triangular flow (v3) with respect to the reaction plane at SIS18-SIS100 energies, which contrasts with results at RHIC and LHC energies where v3 is attributed solely to initial state fluctuations uncorrelated with the reaction plane (Alver 2010, Adam 2016). The results enable future experimental tests at the FAIR facility and challenge the applicability of hybrid hydrodynamics models that assume a separation between initial and expansion stages at these low energies.
