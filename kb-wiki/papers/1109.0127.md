# Hadron Resonances Within a Constituent-Quark Model

> In order to get a more realistic description of the hadron spectrum we extend a constituent-quark model by explicit mesonic degrees of freedom. The resulting system of constituent (anti)quarks, which are subject to an instantaneous confining force, and mesons, which couple directly to the quarks, is treated by means of a relativistic coupled-channel framework. It can be formally shown that the mass-eigenvalue problem for such a system is equivalent to a hadronic eigenvalue problem in which the eigenstates of the pure confinement potential (bare hadrons) are coupled via meson loops. Following this kind of approach we have calculated hadron masses and decay widths for a simple toy model.

## Key claim

The authors extend a constituent-quark model by including explicit mesonic degrees of freedom to achieve a more realistic description of the hadron spectrum, but they only test this approach on a simple toy model that neglects spin and isospin, with parameters adjusted to approximately reproduce the masses of the ground state and first excited state of the omega meson.

## Method

The authors use the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics with the Bakamjian-Thomas construction to treat a system of constituent (anti)quarks subject to an instantaneous confining force and mesons that couple directly to the quarks. They adopt a 2-channel mass operator and eliminate the valence-(anti)quark + Goldstone-boson component via a Feshbach reduction, converting the problem into a non-linear mass-eigenvalue equation on the hadronic level where bare hadrons are coupled via Goldstone-boson loops, solved self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- The decay width of the first excited state exhibits a maximum of about 26 MeV as a function of the Goldstone-boson-(anti)quark coupling strength.
- The authors aim to achieve typical decay widths of 0.1 GeV or more for baryon resonances within the full chiral constituent-quark model.
- The toy model parameters were adjusted to approximately reproduce the masses of the ground state and first excited state of the omega meson.

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman (1995) by incorporating explicit mesonic degrees of freedom to address the problem that perturbatively calculated partial decay widths in constituent quark models come out too small compared to experiment, as noted by Melde (2006). The approach enables future calculations of hadron masses and decay widths within a Poincare-invariant framework that can be applied to more realistic models including spin and isospin degrees of freedom.
