# Isospin fractionation and isoscaling in dynamical nuclear collisions

> Isoscaling is found to hold for fragment yields in the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) simulations for collisions of calcium isotopes at 35 MeV/nucleon. This suggests the applicability of statistical considerations to the dynamical fragment emission. The observed linear relationship between the isoscaling parameters and the isospin asymmetry of fragments supports the above suggestion. The slope of this linear function yields information about the symmetry energy in low density region where multifragmentation occurs.

## Key claim

Isoscaling holds for fragment yields in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) simulations of calcium isotope collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon, suggesting that statistical considerations apply to dynamical fragment emission even though the fragments are produced in a rapidly evolving system with no easily identifiable emitting sources.

## Method

The authors performed AMD simulations of central collisions of 40Ca+40Ca, 48Ca+48Ca, and 60Ca+60Ca at 35 MeV/nucleon and zero impact parameter, using two different effective forces (Gogny and Gogny-AS) that produce different density dependences of the symmetry energy. They extracted fragment yields at t=300 fm/c, identified the liquid part as fragments with A>4, and tested whether the equilibrium-derived linear relation between the isoscaling parameter α and the fragment isospin asymmetry (Z/A)^2 holds in the dynamical simulations.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 35 MeV/nucleon for all collisions
- Isoscaling parameters for 60Ca+60Ca vs 40Ca+40Ca: α=1.82±0.06, β=-2.23±0.08 (Gogny force); α=1.64±0.05, β=-2.09±0.07 (Gogny-AS force)
- Isoscaling parameters for 48Ca+48Ca vs 40Ca+40Ca: α=1.02±0.05, β=-1.19±0.06 (Gogny force); α=0.89±0.04, β=-1.05±0.05 (Gogny-AS force)
- Slope of linear relation between α and (Z/A)^2_liq: -26.27 (Gogny force) and -21.70 (Gogny-AS force), yielding 4C/T = 26.3 and 21.7 respectively
- Symmetry energy coefficient C values of 16-20 MeV (Gogny-AS) and 22-26 MeV (Gogny) assuming temperatures T~3-4 MeV
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter for Gogny force: K=228 MeV
- Saturation density ρ0=0.16 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the experimental observation of isoscaling by Xu (2000) and statistical model explanations by Tsang (2001) to dynamical simulations, addressing whether scaling parameters can be directly related to the asymmetry term of the equation of state. The results contradict a stochastic mean field model prediction of very large scaling parameters for dynamically produced fragments. The linear relation between α and (Z/A)^2 enables extraction of the symmetry energy at low densities where multifragmentation occurs, with the ratio C(Gogny)/C(Gogny-AS) ≈ 5/4 consistent with fragmentation at ρ<ρ0.
