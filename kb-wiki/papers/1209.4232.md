# Measurement of the distributions of event-by-event flow harmonics in Pb-Pb Collisions at sqrt(s_nn)=2.76 TeV with the ATLAS detector

> The event-by-event distributions of harmonic flow coefficients v_n for n=2-4 are measured in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_nn)=2.76 TeV with the ATLAS detector, using charged particles with p_T > 0.5 GeV and |η|<2.5. The shape of the v_n distributions is consistent with Gaussian fluctuations in central collisions for v_2 and over the measured centrality range for v_3 and v_4. When these distributions are rescaled to have the same <v_n>, the resulting shapes are nearly the same for p_T>1 GeV and 0.5<p_T<1 GeV. The v_n distributions are compared with the eccentricity distributions from two initial geometry models: Glauber and the MC-KLN model. Both fail to describe the v_2 data consistently over the full centrality range

## Key claim

The event-by-event distributions of harmonic flow coefficients v_n for n=2-4 are measured in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_nn)=2.76 TeV, and while the abstract states the v_n distributions are consistent with Gaussian fluctuations for v_2 in central collisions and for v_3 and v_4 over the full centrality range, the actual condition is that the Gaussian description fails for v_2 beyond the top 2% most central collisions.

## Method

The analysis used 8 inverse microbarns of minimum bias Pb-Pb data collected by ATLAS in 2010, reconstructing charged particle tracks with p_T > 0.5 GeV and |eta| < 2.5. Two methods were employed: a Fourier expansion of the single-track azimuthal distribution per event, and a two-particle correlation method using tracks from two symmetric subevents in positive and negative eta. The observed flow vectors were unfolded using a Bayesian procedure with a response function derived from the difference of flow vectors between the two subevents, which was well described by a 2-D Gaussian.

## Key numbers

- The ratio sigma_{v_n}/<v_n> for v_2 reaches a minimum of about 0.34 at <N_part> ~ 200 (20-30% centrality range)
- For v_3 and v_4, the values of sigma_{v_n}/<v_n> are consistent with the value expected from Gaussian distributions: sqrt(4/pi - 1) approximately 0.523
- The ratio sqrt(<v_n^2>)/<v_n> for Gaussian fluctuations is sqrt(4/pi) approximately 1.13
- The analysis used charged particles with p_T > 0.5 GeV and |eta| < 2.5
- The collision energy was sqrt(s_nn) = 2.76 TeV

## Context

This work extends previous event-averaged flow measurements by providing event-by-event distributions of v_n, which were previously only accessible indirectly through cumulant methods as done by ALICE (2011). The results are compared with eccentricity distributions from two initial geometry models: the Glauber model (Miller 2007) and the MC-KLN model (Drescher 2006), both of which fail to describe the v_2 data consistently over the full centrality range. These measurements enable more detailed tests of hydrodynamic models and the nature of initial-state fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.
