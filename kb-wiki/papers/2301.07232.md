# Systematic study for relation between nuclear structure and reaction in $^{10}$Be nucleus

> We systematically investigate the relation between the nuclear structure and reaction in the $^{10}$Be nucleus using a theoretical framework. The structure of the $^{10}$Be nucleus is constructed with a cluster model based on a microscopic viewpoint. In this paper, the $^{10}$Be nucleus with different structures is prepared by manipulating the parameters of an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The nuclear structure and expectation values of physical quantities are drastically changed by the modification. We summarize such changes and show the effects on the elastic and inelastic scatterings for the proton and $^{12}$C targets in the microscopic coupled-channel calculation. Especially, we recently reported the visualization of dineutron correlation in $^{10}$Be on proton inelastic scattering in [Phys. Rev. C104, 034613 (2021)]. In this preceding work, we found that the changing the degree of dineutron correlation in $^{10}$Be leads to drastic changes of the inelastic cross section for the 2$_2^+$ state. The development (or breaking) of the dineutron correlation is governed by the strength of the spin-orbit interaction of the structure calculation. However, in the previous work, some of the realistic physical points were missing, for example, the binding energy. Therefore, we reconstruct the $^{10}$Be nucleus by adjusting the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to obtain the reasonable binding energy of the ground state. With this improvement, we again discuss the dineutron correlation in the $^{10}$Be nucleus. We reconfirm the way to measure the degree of the development (or breaking) of dineutron cluster structure; the sensitivity to the inelastic cross section of the ground state to the 2$_2^+$ state of $^{10}$Be.

## Key claim
The paper systematically investigates how the degree of dineutron correlation in $^{10}$Be, controlled by the spin-orbit interaction strength in the structure calculation, affects inelastic scattering cross sections, but now with the improvement of simultaneously adjusting the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to reproduce a realistic ground-state binding energy, which was missing in the prior work (Furumoto 2021).

## Method
The $^{10}$Be nucleus was constructed using a four-body ($\alpha$ + $\alpha$ + n + n) microscopic cluster model with stochastic multiconfiguration mixing. The parameters of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (Majorana parameter $M$, spin-orbit strength $V_{\rm LS}$, and $B=H$) were systematically varied to produce different nuclear structures. Diagonal and transition densities from these structure calculations were then used as input to microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations for elastic and inelastic scattering on proton and $^{12}$C targets at $E/A =$ 59.4 and 200 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Ground state binding energy of $^{10}$Be: experimental value is -65.07 MeV; calculated values range from -59.29 MeV ($V_{\rm LS}=0$) to -72.80 MeV ($V_{\rm LS}=4000$ MeV) with $M=0.60$, $B=H=0.08$.
- Point-proton radius of $^{10}$Be ground state: experimental value is 2.357 $\pm$ 0.018 fm; calculated values range from 2.548 fm ($V_{\rm LS}=0$) to 2.197 fm ($V_{\rm LS}=4000$ MeV).
- Point-nucleon-matter radius of $^{10}$Be ground state: experimental value is 2.39 $\pm$ 0.02 fm; calculated values range from 2.712 fm ($V_{\rm LS}=0$) to 2.266 fm ($V_{\rm LS}=4000$ MeV).
- $B$(E2: 2$_2^+$ $\to$ 0$_1^+$) transition strength: ranges from 16.14 $e^2$ fm$^4$ ($V_{\rm LS}=0$) to 0.006089 $e^2$ fm$^4$ ($V_{\rm LS}=4000$ MeV).
- $B$(IS2: 2$_2^+$ $\to$ 0$_1^+$) transition strength: ranges from 41.46 fm$^4$ ($V_{\rm LS}=0$) to 3.502 fm$^4$ ($V_{\rm LS}=4000$ MeV).
- Incident energies for scattering calculations: $E/A =$ 59.4 MeV and 200 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the prior study by Furumoto (2021) which first demonstrated that the inelastic cross section to the 2$_2^+$ state of $^{10}$Be is sensitive to dineutron correlation, but that study did not reproduce the correct ground-state binding energy. The present paper improves the structure calculation by simultaneously adjusting the Majorana parameter and spin-orbit strength to obtain a reasonable binding energy, reconfirming that the 2$_2^+$ inelastic cross section remains a sensitive probe of dineutron cluster development or breaking. This enables future experimental searches for dineutron correlation signatures in neutron-rich beryllium isotopes through inelastic scattering measurements.
