# Single Inclusive Jet Production in $pA$ Collisions at NLO in the small-$x$ regime

> We present the first complete NLO prediction with full jet algorithm implementation for the single inclusive jet production in $pA$ collisions within the CGC effective theory. Our prediction is fully differential over the final state physical kinematics, which allows the implementation of any IR safe observable including the jet clustering procedure. The NLO calculation is organized with the aid of the power counting proposed in [1] which gives rise to the novel soft contributions in the CGC factorization. We achieve the fully-differential calculation by constructing suitable subtraction terms to handle the singularities in the real corrections. The subtraction contributions can be exactly integrated analytically. We present the NLO cross section with the jets constructed using the anti-$k_T$ algorithm. The NLO calculation demonstrates explicitly the validity of the CGC factorization in jet production. Furthermore, as a byproduct of the subtraction method, we also derive the fully analytic cross section for the forward jet production in the small-$R$ limit. We show that in the small-$R$ approximation, the forward jet cross section can be factorized into a semi-hard cross section that produces a parton and the semi-inclusive jet functions. We argue that this feature holds for generic jet production and jet substructure observables in the CGC framework. Last, we show numerical analyses of the derived formula to validate our calculations. We justify when the small-$R$ approximation is appropriate. Like forward hadron production, the obtained NLO result also exhibits the negativity of the cross section in the large jet transverse regime, which signals the need for the threshold resummation. A sketch of the threshold resummation in the CGC framework is presented based on the multiple emission picture.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete next-to-leading-order (NLO) prediction for single inclusive jet production in proton-nucleus collisions that implements a full jet clustering algorithm (anti-\(k_T\)) within the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory, rather than relying on the small-cone approximation used in prior attempts. The authors note that the NLO cross section becomes negative in the large jet transverse momentum regime, signaling the need for threshold resummation, which they sketch using a multiple emission picture.

## Method

The authors organize the NLO calculation using a power-counting scheme that identifies collinear and soft modes, with the soft mode being crucial for correctly producing rapidity poles and kinematic constraints. They construct subtraction terms to handle singularities in the real corrections, which can be integrated analytically, enabling a fully differential calculation that implements the anti-\(k_T\) jet algorithm. The calculation is performed in the large \(N_C\) limit and uses a rapidity regulator to handle divergences, with the Feynman rules derived from the CGC effective theory including interactions between collinear/soft modes and the CGC shock wave via Wilson lines.

## Key numbers

- The power counting parameter \(\lambda \equiv p_{J\perp} / (\bar{n} \cdot p_i) \sim e^{-|y_J|} \ll 1\) defines the forward scattering hierarchy, where \(p_{J\perp}\) is the jet transverse momentum and \(y_J\) is the jet rapidity.
- The saturation scale \(Q_s\) is the typical scale for gluon saturation, with the condition \(Q_s \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) required for perturbative QCD applicability.
- The NLO calculation uses dimensional regularization with \(D = 4 - 2\epsilon\) for collinear and infrared divergences, and a rapidity regulator factor \(\left| \nu / (k^+ - k^-) \right|^{\eta/2}\) where \(\nu\) is a rapidity scale and \(\eta\) is a small expansion parameter.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid factorization approach for forward hadron production in \(pA\) collisions (Chirilli 2012, Liu 2019, Kang 2019) to jet production with full jet algorithm implementation, going beyond the small-cone approximation used in earlier CGC jet calculations (Caucal 2021, Roy 2019, Kolbe 2020). The results enable direct comparison with experimental jet data from RHIC and the LHC for probing gluon saturation, and the authors argue that their subtraction method can be generalized to other jet and jet-substructure observables in the CGC framework. The threshold resummation sketch builds on the multiple emission picture developed for hadron production (Liu 2020).
