# Approximate particle number projection for finite range density dependent forces

> The Lipkin-Nogami method is generalized to deal with finite range density dependent forces. New expressions are derived and realistic calculations with the Gogny force are performed for the nuclei $^{164}$Er and $^{168}$Er. The sharp phase transition predicted by the mean field approximation is washed out by the Lipkin-Nogami approach; a much better agreement with the experimental data is reached with the new approach than with the Hartree-Fock_Bogoliubov one, specially at high spins.

## Key claim

The Lipkin-Nogami method, previously formulated only for simple separable pairing interactions, is generalized to finite range density dependent forces like the Gogny force; the sharp superfluid-to-normal phase transition predicted by the mean-field approximation is washed out, yielding much better agreement with experimental data at high spins than the plain Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach.

## Method

The authors derive new expressions for the Lipkin-Nogami coefficients \(h_1\) and \(h_2\) using the Kamlah expansion, treating the density dependence of the Gogny force consistently by including derivatives of the Hamiltonian with respect to the gauge angle \(\phi\). They then perform cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the Lipkin-Nogami prescription (CHFBLN) for the nuclei \(^{164}\)Er and \(^{168}\)Er using the standard DS1 parametrization of the Gogny force, and compare results to plain cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{164}\)Er in the CHFB approach, the neutron pairing correlations are quenched at \(I \simeq 18\hbar\); in the CHFBLN approach no superfluid-to-normal phase transition is found.
- For \(^{164}\)Er in the CHFB approach, \(\gamma\) increases up to 8 degrees at \(I=18\hbar\); in the CHFBLN approach the nucleus remains axially symmetric (\(\gamma=0\)) at all spin values.
- For \(^{168}\)Er, the CHFBLN results for transition energies and moments of inertia are in excellent agreement with experiment, while CHFB results are "very low" compared to experiment.
- The experimental data for \(^{164}\)Er end at spin \(I=22\hbar\) and for \(^{168}\)Er at \(I=16\hbar\); theoretical calculations go up to \(I=26\hbar\).

## Context

This work extends the Lipkin-Nogami method, previously applied only with separable pairing interactions (Lipkin 1960, Nogami 1964, Goodfellow and Nogami 1966) and later to superdeformed nuclei with monopole pairing (Magierski et al. 1993, Gall et al. 1994), to the finite range density dependent Gogny force. The new formulation enables realistic calculations of high-spin properties in rare-earth nuclei, washing out the unphysical phase transitions of mean-field theory and providing much better agreement with experimental transition energies, moments of inertia, and \(B(E2)\) values than plain cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations.
