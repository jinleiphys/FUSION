# Pion and proton form factors in the Regge description of electroproduction $p(e,e'π^+)n$

> Electroproduction of $π^+$ above the resonance region is analyzed in the Regge model for $π+ρ$ exchanges. Importance of the roles of pion and proton form factors in the process is discussed in comparison with existing models of Kaskulov and Mosel, and of Vrancx and Rychebusch. It is shown that the present model with proton form factor of a simple dipole-type yields a better description of DESY and JLab data over those models for high $Q^2$ and $-t$ region up to 5 GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Regge model for π+ρ exchanges using a simple dipole-type proton form factor with a constant cutoff mass (Λ₁ = 1.55 GeV) can describe DESY and JLab electroproduction data up to Q² and -t of 5 GeV², performing better than the more complicated s-dependent form factors of Kaskulov and Mosel (2011) and Vrancx and Ryckebusch (2014), but only if the pion form factor cutoff Λπ is adjusted separately for different Q² ranges.

## Method

The authors model the p(e,e'π⁺)n reaction using Reggeized t-channel π and ρ exchanges plus an s-channel proton pole, with gauge invariance maintained via the Gross-Riska prescription. The proton charge form factor F₁(Q²) is taken from the standard dipole parameterization of the Sachs form factors G_E and G_M, but with a constant cutoff mass Λ₁ = 1.55 GeV instead of the on-shell value 0.84 GeV. The pion form factor is a monopole type with Λπ varied between 0.65 and 0.78 GeV depending on the Q² range.

## Key numbers

- Proton form factor cutoff mass: Λ₁ = 1.55 GeV (constant, compared to on-shell value 0.84 GeV)
- Pion form factor cutoff: Λπ = 0.78 GeV for DESY data (Q² = 0.35 and 0.7 GeV²); Λπ = 0.65 GeV for JLab data (Q² up to 4 GeV²)
- Pion charge radius: ⟨rπ²⟩ = 0.384 fm² for Λπ = 0.78 GeV; ⟨rπ²⟩ = 0.55 fm² for Λπ = 0.65 GeV
- Coupling constants: gπNN = 13.4, gρNN = 3.4, κρ = 6.1, gγρπ = 0.22 GeV⁻¹
- Regge trajectories: απ(t) = 0.7(t - mπ²), αρ(t) = 0.83t + 0.53
- Data coverage: Q² and -t up to 5 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Regge model for photoproduction (Yu et al., 2014) to electroproduction, and directly compares with the models of Kaskulov and Mosel (2011) and Vrancx and Ryckebusch (2014), which used s-dependent proton form factors. The paper shows that a simpler constant-cutoff dipole form factor can describe the same data, and suggests that the variation of Λπ with Q² reflects an increase in the measured pion charge radius at higher resolution. The results enable future applications of the model to wider kinematic ranges.
