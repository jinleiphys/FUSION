# Half-integer anomalous currents in 2D materials from a QFT viewpoint

> Charge carriers in Dirac/Weyl semi-metals exhibit a relativistic-like behavior. In this work we propose a novel type of intrinsic half-integer Quantum Hall effect in 2D materials, thereby also offering a topological protection mechanism for the current. Its existence is rooted in the 2D parity anomaly, without any need for a perpendicular magnetic field. We conjecture that it may occur in disturbed honeycomb lattices where both spin degeneracy and time reversal symmetry are broken. These configurations harbor two distinct gap-opening mechanisms that, when occurring simultaneously, drive slightly different gaps in each valley, causing a net anomalous conductivity when the chemical potential is tuned to be between the distinct gaps. Some examples of promising material setups that fulfill the prerequisites of our proposal are also listed to motivate looking for the effect at the numerical and experimental level.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel intrinsic half-integer anomalous quantum Hall effect (A-QHE) in two-dimensional materials, with a quantized conductivity of \(e^2/2h\), that arises from the 2D parity anomaly without requiring a perpendicular magnetic field. The effect is conditional on the simultaneous breaking of both spin degeneracy and time reversal symmetry in disturbed honeycomb lattices, with the chemical potential tuned between two distinct gaps in each valley.

## Method

The authors construct a continuum quantum field theory description of charge carriers in a honeycomb lattice using reduced quantum electrodynamics (RQED), incorporating two distinct gap-opening mechanisms per valley and a chemical potential. They compute the DC anomalous Hall conductivity via linear response theory (Kubo formalism), evaluating the one-loop photon self-energy at zero momentum to extract the topological Chern-Simons contribution. The result is shown to be one-loop exact and independent of the Fermi velocity, with the topological protection analyzed through Berry curvature integrals and Chern numbers.

## Key numbers

- Predicted anomalous Hall conductivity: \(\sigma_{xy} = e^2/2h\) (half-integer quantum Hall effect), arising when \(m_{k'}^2 < \mu^2 < m_k^2\) with both masses positive.
- For a single massive two-component spinor: \(\sigma = (e^2/4\pi\hbar)(m/|m|)\theta(m^2 - \mu^2)\).
- In the candidate material MnPSe\(_3\) doped with Zn: spin up (down) valley splitting of 33 (40) meV and spin splitting of 89 (93) meV at the K' (K) point, from DFT calculations with GGA+U (on-site Coulomb interaction of 5 eV applied to Mn atoms).

## Context

This work extends the Haldane model (1988) by considering both time reversal and space inversion symmetry breaking simultaneously, rather than only time reversal breaking, and does not rely on band inversion. The proposed mechanism differs from the magnetically doped (BiSb)\(_2\)(TeSe)\(_2\) materials reviewed in the A-QHE literature, as it uses a genuine two-dimensional material with two distinct gaps per valley rather than a single Dirac cone from a topological insulator surface. The result enables experimental searches for the half-integer anomalous quantum Hall effect in specific material candidates such as doped MnPSe\(_3\) and heterostructures like MnPSe\(_3\)/CrBr\(_3\), MnPSe\(_3\)/MoS\(_2\), and WS\(_2\)/\(h\)-VN, with potential applications in topologically protected qubits via proximity-induced Majorana fermions.
