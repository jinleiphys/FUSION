# Threshold cusps and triangle singularities in hadronic reactions

> The spectrum of hadrons is the manifestation of color confinement of quantum chromodynamics. Hadronic resonances correspond to poles of the S-matrix. Since 2003, lots of new hadron resonant structures were discovered in the mass regions from light mesons to hadrons containing a pair of a heavy quark and an antiquark. Many of them are candidates of exotic hadrons, and they are usually observed as peaks in invariant mass distributions. However, the S-matrix also has kinematical singularities due to the on-shellness of intermediate particles for a process, such as two-body thresholds and triangle singularities, and they can produce peaks as well. On the one hand, such singularities may be misidentified as resonances; on the other hand, they can be used as tools for precision measurements. In this paper, we review the threshold cusps and various triangle singularities in hadronic reactions, paying attention to their manifestations in phenomena related to exotic hadron candidates.

## Key claim

Threshold cusps and triangle singularities (TSs) are kinematical singularities of the S-matrix that can produce peaks in invariant mass distributions, which may be misidentified as hadronic resonances; however, under certain conditions they can also be used as tools for precision measurements.

## Method

The authors review the Landau equations that determine the locations of kinematical singularities, focusing on two-body threshold cusps (Wigner cusps) and triangle singularities (TSs) in hadronic reactions. They derive the nonrelativistic scalar triangle loop integral in terms of elementary functions, obtaining analytic expressions involving arctan and arcsin functions, and identify the condition for the TS as a logarithmic divergence at (c2-c1)^2 + 4ac1 = 0. The review catalogs numerous hadronic processes where TSs have been suggested to produce observable peaks, listing the specific triangle loop diagrams and the intermediate particles involved.

## Key numbers

* The nonrelativistic triangle loop integral yields a logarithmic divergence at the solution of (c2-c1)^2 + 4ac1 = 0, which gives the nonrelativistic version of the triangle singularity condition.
* The two-body phase space factor rho_a(s) = k_a/(8 pi sqrt(s)) theta(sqrt(s) - m_a1 - m_a2) has a square-root branch point at threshold sqrt(s) = m_a1 + m_a2.
* The characteristic energy scale for nonperturbative QCD is Lambda_QCD, of the order of a few hundred MeV.
* The triangle singularity condition involves momentum scales sqrt(|c1|) and sqrt(|c2|) that define the averaged velocity used in nonrelativistic power counting.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of Landau singularities (Landau, 1959) and the Coleman-Norton theorem (Coleman, 1965) to modern hadronic reactions, particularly those involving heavy quarks discovered since 2003. It builds on earlier work on triangle singularities from the 1960s (Peierls, 1961; Goebel, 1964; Aitchison, 1964; Schmid, 1967) and applies these concepts to contemporary exotic hadron candidates such as X(3872), Z_c(3900), and P_c(4450). The work enables future experimental and theoretical studies to distinguish genuine resonances from kinematic effects in processes at BESIII, LHCb, Belle II, and other facilities.
