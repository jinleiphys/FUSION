# Spin symmetry in the anti-nucleon spectrum

> We discuss spin and pseudo-spin symmetry in the spectrum of single nucleons and single anti-nucleons in a nucleus. As an example we use relativistic mean field theory to investigate single anti-nucleon spectra. We find a very well developed spin symmetry in single anti-neutron and single anti-proton spectra. The dominant components of the wave functions of the spin doublet are almost identical. This spin symmetry in anti-particle spectra and the pseudo-spin symmetry in particle spectra have the same origin. However it turns out that the spin symmetry in anti-nucleon spectra is much better developed than the pseudo-spin symmetry in normal nuclear single particle spectra.

# Spin symmetry in the anti-nucleon spectrum

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that spin symmetry in single anti-nucleon spectra is much better developed than pseudo-spin symmetry in normal nuclear single particle spectra, because the factor multiplying the spin-orbit term for anti-particle states is roughly 400 times smaller than for particle states. This holds for realistic nuclei, not just in the idealized limit where the scalar and vector potentials exactly cancel.

## Method
The authors use relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the NL3 parameter set to perform relativistic Hartree calculations in coordinate space for the doubly magic nuclei 16O and 208Pb. They solve the Dirac equation for both nucleon and anti-nucleon states, deriving Schrodinger-like equations for the upper and lower components to analyze the spin-orbit and pseudo-spin-orbit splittings. The spin-orbit splitting for anti-nucleon states is computed from the expectation value of the spin-orbit potential and compared with the pseudo-spin-orbit splitting for nucleon states.

## Key numbers
- For 16O, spin-orbit splittings in anti-neutron spectra are around 0.2-0.5 MeV for p states (l=1)
- For 208Pb, spin-orbit splittings are approximately 0.1 MeV for p states and less than 0.2 MeV for h states (l=5)
- The factor in front of the spin-orbit term for anti-particle states is smaller than for particle states by a factor of approximately (2|M-S(0)|/|V-(0)|)^2 ≈ 400
- Bound anti-particle energies lie in the region 0.3 GeV ≲ ε_A ≲ 1 GeV
- Pseudo-spin-orbit splittings for neutrons in 208Pb are larger than 0.4 MeV in most cases, reaching values around 4 MeV for deeply bound states (e.g., 3.106 MeV for (n+1)s1/2 and nd3/2; 3.917 MeV for (n+1)p3/2 and nf5/2)

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of pseudo-spin symmetry in nuclei, originally introduced by Arima, Harvey, and Suzuki (1969) and Hecht and Adler (1969), and later shown by Ginocchio (1997, 1999) to arise from a relativistic symmetry in the Dirac Hamiltonian when scalar and vector potentials have equal magnitude but opposite sign. The authors apply this framework to anti-nucleon spectra for the first time in realistic nuclei, building on earlier suggestions by Smith and Tassie (1971) and Bell and Ruegg (1975) that equality of vector and scalar potentials could produce spin symmetry in meson spectra. The results enable future studies of anti-baryons in nuclei, a topic of renewed interest following work by Bahaoui, Mbarek, and Sandulescu (2002).
