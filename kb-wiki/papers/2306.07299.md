# Additive Multi-Index Gaussian process modeling, with application to multi-physics surrogate modeling of the quark-gluon plasma

> The Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) is a unique phase of nuclear matter, theorized to have filled the Universe shortly after the Big Bang. A critical challenge in studying the QGP is that, to reconcile experimental observables with theoretical parameters, one requires many simulation runs of a complex physics model over a high-dimensional parameter space. Each run is computationally very expensive, requiring thousands of CPU hours, thus limiting physicists to only several hundred runs. Given limited training data for high-dimensional prediction, existing surrogate models often yield poor predictions with high predictive uncertainties, leading to imprecise scientific findings. To address this, we propose a new Additive Multi-Index Gaussian process (AdMIn-GP) model, which leverages a flexible additive structure on low-dimensional embeddings of the parameter space. This is guided by prior scientific knowledge that the QGP is dominated by multiple distinct physical phenomena (i.e., multiphysics), each involving a small number of latent parameters. The AdMIn-GP models for such embedded structures within a flexible Bayesian nonparametric framework, which facilitates efficient model fitting via a carefully constructed variational inference approach with inducing points. We show the effectiveness of the AdMIn-GP via a suite of numerical experiments and our QGP application, where we demonstrate considerably improved surrogate modeling performance over existing models.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an Additive Multi-Index Gaussian process (AdMIn-GP) model that leverages a flexible additive structure on low-dimensional embeddings of the parameter space, guided by prior scientific knowledge that the quark-gluon plasma is dominated by multiple distinct physical phenomena each involving a small number of latent parameters. The authors claim considerably improved surrogate modeling performance over existing models, but this improvement is demonstrated specifically for the QGP application and numerical experiments shown in the paper, with the hedge that existing surrogate models often yield poor predictions with high predictive uncertainties given limited training data.

## Method

The AdMIn-GP model employs a Bayesian nonparametric framework with an additive structure on low-dimensional embeddings of the high-dimensional parameter space, where each additive component corresponds to a distinct physical phenomenon in the QGP. Model fitting is performed via a carefully constructed variational inference approach with inducing points to enable efficient computation. The method is evaluated through a suite of numerical experiments and applied to surrogate modeling of QGP simulations, where each simulation run requires thousands of CPU hours and only several hundred runs are available.

- The microthruster benchmark system has n = 4,257 degrees of freedom with three design parameters, subspace dimension k = 10, and sample size l = 6
- The GPS model (related prior work by Zhang, Mak, and Dunson, 2022) achieves computational complexity of k^3 l^3 for subspace prediction and 2 k^3 l^2 for ROM computation, compared to 8 n k^2 for subspace interpolation methods

## Context

This work extends the Gaussian Process Subspace (GPS) regression framework developed by Zhang, Mak, and Dunson (2022) for model reduction, applying it to the multi-physics setting of QGP simulations. The AdMIn-GP addresses limitations of existing surrogate models that perform poorly in high-dimensional parameter spaces with limited training data, building on prior work in parametric reduced order modeling including subspace interpolation methods by Amsallem and Farhat (2008) and matrix interpolation approaches by Panzer, Mohring, Eid, and Lohmann (2010). The method enables more precise scientific inference from expensive QGP simulations by providing improved predictive accuracy with quantified uncertainties.
