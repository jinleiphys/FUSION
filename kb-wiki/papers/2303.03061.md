# Gluons, light and heavy quarks and their interactions in the instanton vacuum

> The instanton size $ρ$ and inter-instanton distance $R$ are the main parameters of Instanton Liquid Model (ILM) of the QCD vacuum. Various estimates show that $ρ\approx1/3$ fm and $R\approx1$ fm, and the corresponding packing parameter $κ=ρ^{4}/R^{4}\approx0.01$. The strength of the light quark-instanton interaction is sizable and close to that of the gluon-instanton one since the dynamical light quark mass $M_{q}$ and dynamical gluon mass $M_{g}$ are given by $M_{q}\approx M_{g}\approx 360$ MeV$\simκ^{1/2}ρ^{-1}$. On the other hand, the strength of the heavy quark-instanton interaction is weak - the direct instanton contribution to the heavy quark mass $ΔM_{Q}^{\rm dir}\approx70$ MeV$\simκρ^{-1}.$ The instantons are responsible for mutual interactions among colored particles which are crossing the field of the same instanton, like t'Hooft-like interactions of $N_{f}$ light quarks ($N_{f}$ is the light quark flavors number). The light quark propagators in the instanton field have zero modes, which give dominant contributions. Within ILM we are able to derive the light quarks determinants and the light quarks partition function. These tools perfectly describe the light quark physics and its most important and basic phenomena - the spontaneous breaking of the chiral symmetry (SBChS) in details. These one allows us to find the properties of light and heavy quarks interactions and get SBChS traces in light-heavy and heavy-heavy quarks systems. So, we need to find heavy $Q\bar{Q}$ quarkonia spectra and their wave functions. Here we have interplay of two scales: short ($\sim(M_{Q}v)^{-1}\leq0.15$ fm) perturbative QCD and large ($\sim(M_{Q}v^{2})^{-1}\sim0.5$ fm) nonperturbative QCD scales, where $v$ is the velocity in $Q\bar{Q}$. We calculate the heavy quarks correlators with perturbative corrections within ILM.

## Key claim

The Instanton Liquid Model (ILM) provides a good framework for light hadron physics calculations and can be extended to heavy-light and heavy-heavy quark systems, but the heavy quark-instanton interaction is weak (direct contribution to heavy quark mass ~70 MeV) compared to the light quark-instanton interaction (dynamical mass ~360 MeV), and there is an almost complete mutual cancellation of the direct instanton and perturbative instanton contributions to heavy quark correlators.

## Method

The author derives light quark determinants and partition functions within the ILM using zero-mode approximations for light quark propagators in instanton fields, then extends this framework to include heavy quarks by calculating heavy quark correlators with perturbative corrections. The approach involves averaging over instanton collective coordinates (position, size, color orientation) and using saddle-point approximations to determine dynamical quark masses and effective interactions, including t'Hooft-like interactions for light quarks and instanton-generated interactions for heavy-light and heavy-heavy systems.

## Key numbers

- Instanton size ρ ≈ 1/3 fm and inter-instanton distance R ≈ 1 fm, giving packing parameter κ = ρ⁴/R⁴ ≈ 0.01
- Dynamical light quark mass M_q ≈ 360 MeV and dynamical gluon mass M_g ≈ 360 MeV, both scaling as κ^(1/2) ρ^(-1)
- Direct instanton contribution to heavy quark mass ΔM_Q^(dir) ≈ 70 MeV, scaling as κ ρ^(-1)
- QCD vacuum energy density ε ≈ -500 MeV/fm³
- For the process ψ(2S) → J/ψ π⁺π⁻, a sizable correction of ~20% to the standard dipole approximation is found
- Pion decay constant at zero quark mass F_π(m=0) = 88 MeV, and quark condensate at zero quark mass ⟨q̄q(m=0)⟩ = (255 MeV)³, used as input to fix parameters (ρ, R)
- At quark mass m = 0.1 GeV, NLO corrections to F_π are about 40%

## Context

This review extends the ILM framework developed by Shuryak (1981), Diakonov and Petrov (1983), and t'Hooft (1976) for light quark physics to include heavy quarks and heavy quarkonia. The work builds on previous studies by Musakhanov (2020), Turimov (2016), and Yakhshiev (2018) to calculate instanton-generated interactions in heavy-light systems and heavy quarkonium decay processes. The results enable precise calculations of heavy quarkonium spectra and wave functions within ILM, including processes like ψ(2S) → J/ψ ππ, and provide a path toward understanding spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking traces in heavy-light and heavy-heavy quark systems.
