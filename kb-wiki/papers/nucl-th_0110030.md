# Partial Dynamical Symmetry in the Symplectic Shell Model

> We present an example of a partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) in an interacting fermion system and demonstrate the close relationship of the associated Hamiltonians with a realistic quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, thus shedding new light on this important interaction. Specifically, in the framework of the symplectic shell model of nuclei, we prove the existence of a family of fermionic Hamiltonians with partial SU(3) symmetry. We outline the construction process for the PDS eigenstates with good symmetry and give analytic expressions for the energies of these states and E2 transition strengths between them. Characteristics of both pure and mixed-symmetry PDS eigenstates are discussed and the resulting spectra and transition strengths are compared to those of real nuclei. The PDS concept is shown to be relevant to the description of prolate, oblate, as well as triaxially deformed nuclei. Similarities and differences between the fermion case and the previously established partial SU(3) symmetry in the Interacting Boson Model are considered.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a family of fermionic Hamiltonians in the symplectic shell model exhibits partial SU(3) symmetry, meaning the Hamiltonian does not commute with the SU(3) generators yet possesses a subset of solvable eigenstates that retain good SU(3) symmetry. The authors show that these PDS Hamiltonians are closely related to the realistic quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, but only when restricted to a single major oscillator shell.

## Method

The authors construct a family of Hamiltonians H(β₀, β₂) within the symplectic shell model framework, expressed in terms of SU(3) tensor operators that act within a major harmonic oscillator shell. They prove the existence of partial SU(3) symmetry by identifying specific SU(3) irreducible representations that remain unmixed in the eigenstates, and derive analytic expressions for the energies of these pure states and E2 transition strengths between them. The construction employs the Sp(6,R) ⊃ U(3) ⊃ SO(3) dynamical symmetry chain, using the Vergados orthonormal basis to handle multiplicity of angular momentum states.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C: the leading symplectic irrep is Nσ (λσ, μσ) = 24.5 (0,4), corresponding to symplectic weights σ₁ = σ₂ = 9.5, σ₃ = 5.5
- For ²⁰Ne: the leading symplectic irrep is Nσ (λσ, μσ) = 48.5 (8,0), with σ₁ = 21.5, σ₂ = σ₃ = 13.5
- For ²⁴Mg: the leading symplectic irrep is Nσ (λσ, μσ) = 62.5 (8,4), with σ₁ = 27.5, σ₂ = 19.5, σ₃ = 15.5
- The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction expansion contains terms acting within a shell (first three terms) and terms connecting states differing by ±2ħω and ±4ħω (second line)

## Context

This work extends the partial dynamical symmetry concept from bosonic systems (previously established in the Interacting Boson Model by Leviatan (1996) for ¹⁶⁸Er) to interacting fermion systems within the symplectic shell model. It builds on the earlier proof-of-principle by Escher (2000) and provides detailed analytic expressions and applications to ¹²C, ²⁰Ne, and ²⁴Mg. The PDS approach offers an intermediate structure between exact SU(3) dynamical symmetry (which cannot reproduce K-band splitting) and complete symmetry breaking, enabling analytic treatment of selected rotational bands while allowing mixing for others.
