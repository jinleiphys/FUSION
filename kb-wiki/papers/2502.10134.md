# Chiral symmetry restoration in QC_2D from effective model using the functional renormalization group

> The order of the chiral phase transition in two-color and two-flavor QC$_2$D is investigated using the functional renormalization group (FRG) technique in an effective model setting. We calculate the $β$ function of all couplings in the dimensionally reduced Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional with Pauli-Gürsey SU(4) symmetry. We compare results of the perturbative $ε$ expansion approach with those obtained via the FRG, evaluated directly in $d=3$ dimensions. The perturbative results suggest that the fixed-point structure is more intricate than that of three-color QCD, a conclusion further supported by the FRG analysis. Both methods display an infrared stable $O(6)$ fixed point at infinite axial anomaly; however, the FRG approach also reveals the existence of $U_A(1)$ anomaly-free fixed points, which can become infrared stable if the anomaly in the underlying theory vanishes at the critical temperature. These findings imply that the phase transition can be of second order, consistent with earlier findings for three colors.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition in two-color, two-flavor QC₂D can be of second order, but only if either the axial anomaly is infinitely strong (yielding an O(6) fixed point) or if the anomaly vanishes at the critical temperature (yielding anomaly-free fixed points); the epsilon expansion alone cannot see the latter possibility, while the functional renormalization group (FRG) reveals them.

## Method

The authors constructed the most general Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional for QC₂D with Pauli-Gürsey SU(4) symmetry, including all relevant and marginal operators up to sixth order in the fields. They derived the beta functions for all couplings using two approaches: a perturbative epsilon expansion around d=4 dimensions extrapolated to d=3, and a nonperturbative FRG calculation using the Wetterich equation with the optimal regulator (Litim regulator) evaluated directly in d=3 dimensions. Fixed points were identified by solving the coupled beta-function equations, and their stability was determined by diagonalizing the stability matrix.

## Key numbers

* The O(12) fixed point in the FRG calculation has dimensionless couplings \(\bar{m}^2 = -0.2964\) and \(\bar{g}_1 = 0.0314\), with 4 relevant directions (2 relevant directions when the U_A(1) anomaly is absent).
* The anomaly-free fixed point AF₁ in the FRG calculation has \(\bar{m}^2 = -0.3433\), \(\bar{g}_1 = 0.0126\), and \(\bar{g}_2 = 0.1070\), with 3 relevant directions (1 relevant direction when the U_A(1) anomaly is absent).
* The anomaly-free fixed point AF₂ in the FRG calculation has \(\bar{m}^2 = -0.2249\), \(\bar{g}_1 = 0.0541\), and \(\bar{g}_2 = -0.1406\), with 3 relevant directions (1 relevant direction when the U_A(1) anomaly is absent).
* The O(6) fixed point in the FRG calculation has \(\bar{m}^2 = -1/4\) and \(\bar{g}_1 = 27/512\), with exactly 1 relevant direction.
* In the epsilon expansion extrapolated to d=3, the O(6) fixed point has \(\bar{m}^2 = -1/7\) and \(\bar{g}_1 = 1/14\), with exactly 1 relevant direction.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of chiral phase transitions from three-color QCD (Pisarski 1984; Fejos 2022, 2024) to two-color QC₂D, where the sign problem is absent and lattice simulations are feasible at finite density. The epsilon expansion results show no infrared-stable fixed point for finite anomaly, similar to the three-color case, but the FRG reveals anomaly-free fixed points (AF₁ and AF₂) that were inaccessible in perturbation theory. The findings enable future lattice simulations of QC₂D (Boz 2013; Braguta 2016; Iida 2024) to test whether the chiral transition is second order and, if so, whether the measured critical exponents match the O(6) universality class (indicating a strong anomaly) or the AF₁ fixed point (indicating anomaly vanishing at the critical temperature).
