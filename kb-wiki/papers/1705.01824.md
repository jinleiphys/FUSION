# Exact self-duality in a modified Skyrme model

> We propose a modification of the Skyrme model that supports a self-dual sector possessing exact non-trivial finite energy solutions. The action of such a theory possesses the usual quadratic and quartic terms in field derivatives, but the couplings of the components of the Maurer-Cartan form of the Skyrme model is made by a non-constant symmetric matrix, instead of the usual Killing form of the SU(2) Lie algebra. The introduction of such a matrix make the self-duality equations conformally invariant in three space dimensions, even though it may break the global internal symmetries of the original Skyrme model. For the case where that matrix is proportional to the identity we show that the theory possesses exact self-dual Skyrmions of unity topological charges.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a modification of the Skyrme model where the usual Killing form of the SU(2) Lie algebra is replaced by a non-constant symmetric matrix \(h_{ab}\) in the couplings of the Maurer-Cartan form components; this modification supports an exact self-dual sector with non-trivial finite energy solutions, but only for the specific case where that matrix is proportional to the identity does the theory possess exact self-dual Skyrmions of unity topological charges.

## Method
The author applies a technique of splitting the integrand of the baryonic topological charge functional (the winding number of the map \(S^3 \rightarrow SU(2)\)) into two pieces, then squaring and adding them to construct a static energy functional that by construction has a BPS bound. This yields a generalized Skyrme model action containing the usual quadratic and quartic derivative terms, but with group indices contracted by a symmetric matrix \(h_{ab}\) instead of the Killing form. The self-duality equations are derived as first-order differential equations \(\epsilon_{ijk} \partial_j R^a_k = \lambda R_i^b h_{ba}\) (with \(\lambda = \eta m_0 e_0\), \(\eta = \pm 1\)), and their solutions are shown to satisfy the second-order Euler-Lagrange equations for both the SU(2) fields and, if treated as independent, the matrix \(h_{ab}\).

## Key numbers
- The topological charge of the constructed exact solution is \(Q = -\eta\), where \(\eta = \pm 1\) is the sign in the self-duality equations.
- The energy of the self-dual Skyrmion is \(E = 48\pi^2 (m_0/e_0)\).
- The energy density decays as \(1/r^6\) for radial distance \(r \rightarrow \infty\).
- The radial profile function is \(\chi(r) = 2 \arctan(r/a)\), with \(a\) an arbitrary parameter of dimension length.
- The matrix \(h_{ab}\) for the exact solution takes the form \(h_{ab} = f^2 \delta_{ab}\) with \(f^2 = 4a / (|m_0 e_0| (r^2 + a^2))\).

## Context
This work extends the technique of constructing self-dual sectors by splitting topological charge densities, previously applied to Skyrme-type models by the same author and collaborators (Selfdual, 2015; U. A. and Shnir, 2016). It contrasts with the original Skyrme model (Skyrme, 1961; Manton, 2004), whose self-duality equations with the identity matrix are known not to possess finite energy solutions (Ruback, 1988). The model is related to the BPS Skyrme model (Adam and collaborators, 2010) and to a Skyrme-type model with target space \(S^3\) (Shnir, 2016) that also yields exact self-dual Skyrmions. The conformal invariance of the self-dual sector in three space dimensions is a new feature that enables exact solutions, and the work opens the question of whether the matrix \(h_{ab}\) can be interpreted as low-energy expectation values of more fundamental fields or as independent dynamical fields requiring kinetic terms.
