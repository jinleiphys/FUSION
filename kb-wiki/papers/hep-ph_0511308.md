# How to Sum Contributions into the Total Charged-Current Neutrino-Nucleon Cross Section

> The total charged-current (anti)neutrino-nucleon cross section is usually estimated by the sum of contributions from quasi-elastic scattering (QES), single-pion production through baryon resonances (RES), and deep inelastic scattering (DIS) with an appropriate scratching the phase space of the RES and DIS contributions. However the resulting total cross section is very sensitive to the value of the cut-off in invariant mass of the final hadron system produced in RES and DIS. We examine available experimental data on the QES and total CC cross sections in order to extract the best-fit value for this cut-off. By using the same data set we attempt to adjust the poorly known values of the axial mass for QES and RES.

## Key claim

The total charged-current (anti)neutrino-nucleon cross section, conventionally estimated by summing quasi-elastic scattering (QES), resonance production (RES), and deep inelastic scattering (DIS) contributions with a phase-space cut-off in invariant mass W, is highly sensitive to the chosen cut-off value; the authors extract this cut-off and the axial masses for QES and RES simultaneously from a global fit to experimental data, finding that the resulting cross section is not independent of the cut-off as quark-hadron duality would suggest.

## Method

The authors performed a global likelihood fit to available experimental data on quasi-elastic scattering (QES) and total charged-current cross sections for muon neutrinos and antineutrinos scattering off various nuclear targets (converted to free nucleon cross sections). The fit simultaneously determined the axial masses for QES (M_A^QES) and RES (M_A^RES), and the invariant mass cut-offs W_cut^RES and W_cut^DIS, using the Rein-Sehgal model for RES and a standard dipole parametrization for the axial form factors, with DIS contributions calculated using parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit value of M_A^QES = 0.98 GeV/c^2 obtained from the global B3 fit (see Fig. 4 caption)
- PDG averaged slope values: (0.677 ± 0.014) × 10^{-38} cm^2/GeV for ν_μN and (0.334 ± 0.008) × 10^{-38} cm^2/GeV for \bar{ν}_μN (from Hagiwara 2002)
- The two-pion production threshold W_{2π} = M_N + 2m_π ≈ 1.2 GeV, noted as too small for practical DIS structure function extrapolation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Kuzmin 2004, Kuzmin 2005) on neutrino-nucleon cross section modeling by performing a simultaneous fit of both axial masses and the invariant mass cut-off, rather than treating them separately. The analysis uses data from numerous experiments including ANL (Barish 1977, Barish 1979), BNL (Baker 1981, Baker 1982), FNAL (Kitagaki 1983, Asratyan 1984b), CERN (GGM, BEBC, CDHS collaborations), and IHEP (SKAT collaboration). The results enable improved predictions for neutrino cross sections needed by oscillation experiments such as K2K (Gran 2005) and Super-Kamiokande (Ashie 2005), and highlight the need for better understanding of the transition region between resonance and deep inelastic regimes.
