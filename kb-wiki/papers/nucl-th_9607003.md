# Nuclear Clusters as a Probe for Expansion Flow in Heavy Ion Reactions at 10-15AGeV

> A phase space coalescence description based on the Wigner-function method for cluster formation in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is presented. The momentum distributions of nuclear clusters d,t and He are predicted for central Au(11.6AGeV)Au and Si(14.6AGeV)Si reactions in the framework of the RQMD transport approach. Transverse expansion leads to a strong shoulder-arm shape and different inverse slope parameters in the transverse spectra of nuclear clusters deviating markedly from thermal distributions. A clear ``bounce-off'' event shape is seen: the averaged transverse flow velocities in the reaction plane are for clusters larger than for protons. The cluster yields --particularly at low $p_t$ at midrapidities-- and the in-plane (anti)flow of clusters and pions change if suitably strong baryon potential interactions are included. This allows to study the transient pressure at high density via the event shape analysis of nucleons, nucleon clusters and other hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that transverse expansion in central Au(11.6AGeV)Au collisions produces a strong shoulder-arm shape and different inverse slope parameters in the transverse momentum spectra of nuclear clusters (d, t, He), deviating markedly from thermal distributions; these features allow the strength of collective flow to be assessed, provided that suitably strong baryon potential interactions are included, as the cluster yields and in-plane (anti)flow change when such interactions are present.

## Method

The authors combined the RQMD (Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport approach, which simulates the dynamical evolution of nucleus-nucleus collisions up to freeze-out, with a phase space coalescence description based on the Wigner-function method to compute cluster formation probabilities for deuterons, tritons, and helium isotopes. The Wigner-function overlap integral between the cluster wave function (parametrized via Hulthén wave functions for deuterons and harmonic oscillator wave functions for heavier clusters) and the single-nucleon phase space distributions at freeze-out was evaluated in a Monte Carlo formulation. Two scenarios were compared: a cascade mode with no potentials, and a scenario with density-dependent baryon quasi-potentials adjusted to nuclear matter saturation properties and previously fitted to proton spectra.

## Key numbers

* Central Au(11.6AGeV)Au collisions (impact parameter b<3fm) produce average freeze-out radii of approximately 10 fm for clusters.
* The collective transverse flow velocities for clusters saturate at approximately 0.7c for both Au+Au and Si+Si reactions.
* For Au+Au collisions, the collective transverse velocities of heavier mass clusters provide approximately 80% of their total transverse momentum.
* For Si+Si collisions at 14.6AGeV (b<1fm), the average transverse flow velocity of 4He states is approximately a factor of two smaller than for protons.
* The statistical spin-isospin correction factors used are g(d)NS(d)=3/8, g(t)NS(t)=g(3He)NS(3He)=1/12, and g(4He)NS(4He)=1/96.
* Feed-down effects from excited t and He states are expected to be small (less than 15%).

## Context

This work extends earlier coalescence studies on deuterons by Mattiello et al. (1995) to light clusters with A<=4, and contrasts with thermal model and single-nucleon momentum distribution approaches (e.g., by various authors in the 1990s) which predicted that cluster and nucleon spectra have essentially the same shape. The paper demonstrates that flow invalidates the basic assumptions of those simple models, and that the amount of "scaling violation" in cluster spectra compared to proton spectra can be used to assess the strength of collective flow. The results enable future studies of transient pressure at high density via event shape analysis of nucleons, nucleon clusters, and other hadrons, and are compared to experimental data from E802 and E866 at the BNL-AGS.
