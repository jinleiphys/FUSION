# Double logarithms resummation in exclusive processes : the surprising behavior of DVCS

> Double logarithms resummation has been much studied in inclusive as well as exclusive processes. The Sudakov mechanism has often be the crucial tool to exponentiate potentially large contributions to amplitudes or cross-sections near phase-space boundaries. We report on a recent work where a very different pattern emerges : the DVCS quark coefficient function C(x,ξ) develops, near the particular point x=ξ, a non-alternate series in α_s^n log^{2n}(x-ξ) which may be resummed in a cosh[K sqrt α_s log(x-ξ)] factor. This result is at odds with the known result for the corresponding coefficient function for the pion transition form factor near the end point C(z) although they are much related through a z -> x/ξcorrespondence.

# Double Logarithms Resummation in Exclusive Processes: The Surprising Behavior of DVCS

## Key claim

The quark coefficient function for deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) near the point x = ξ develops a non-alternating series in α_s^n log^{2n}(x-ξ) that resums into a hyperbolic cosine factor cosh[K √α_s log(x-ξ)], in contrast to the Sudakov exponential form that governs many other exclusive processes. This result contradicts the expected behavior based on the pion transition form factor coefficient function, despite the two being related through a z → x/ξ correspondence.

## Method

The authors analyze the DVCS amplitude in the collinear factorization framework, focusing on the quark coefficient function C^q(x,ξ) in the limit x → ξ. Using a light-like axial gauge p₁·A = 0, they identify that ladder diagrams dominate in this kinematic region, with strong ordering in longitudinal and transverse momenta. They compute the n-loop ladder contributions using quasi-eikonal techniques and dimensional regularization, matching the one-loop result to the known full NLO expression to fix the scale, then generalize to all orders to obtain the resummed series.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop coefficient function contains terms of order α_s log²((ξ-x)/(2ξ)) in the limit x → ξ, as shown in Eq. (C1)
- The n-loop ladder contribution K_n yields a factor of (2πi)^n/(2n)! × log^{2n}((ξ-x)/(2ξ) - iε), as given in Eq. (In-final)
- The resummed formula involves the parameter D = √(α_s C_F / (2π)), where C_F is the QCD color factor
- The matching condition for the one-loop result fixes the logarithmic argument to log((ξ-x)/(2ξ) - iε) with the scale set by 2ξ

## Context

This work extends the study of double logarithm resummation from inclusive processes and the classic Sudakov mechanism (Sudakov, 1956) to the exclusive process of DVCS, where a fundamentally different pattern emerges. The result is at odds with the known behavior of the pion transition form factor coefficient function studied by Musatov and collaborators, which was thought to exponentiate in a Sudakov form factor. The authors suggest their result may also apply to the pion transition form factor after rescaling, and that the understanding of meson form factors (Li and Sterman) may need reconsideration. This work provides a crucial step toward consistent extraction of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from experimental data.
