# Relativistic Mean-Field Treatment of Pulsar Kick from Neutrino Propagation in Magnetized Proto-Neutron

> We make a perturbative calculation of neutrino scattering and absorption in hot and dense hyperonic neutron-star matter in the presence of a strong magnetic Field. We calculate that the absorption cross-sections in a fully relativistic mean-field theory. We find that there is a remarkable angular dependence, i.e. the neutrino absorption strength is reduced in a direction parallel to the magnetic Field and enhanced in the opposite direction. This asymmetry in the neutrino absorption is estimated to be as much as 2.2 % of the entire neutrino momentum for an interior magnetic Field of 2 x 10^{17} G. The pulsar kick velocities associated with this asymmetry are shown to be comparable to observed velocities.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that neutrino absorption in hot, dense hyperonic neutron-star matter under a strong magnetic field exhibits a significant angular asymmetry, absorption is reduced parallel to the field and enhanced antiparallel, which can produce pulsar kick velocities comparable to observed values, but only if the system is not in complete thermodynamic equilibrium, as the asymmetry arises from the non-equilibrium part of the neutrino distribution.

## Method

The authors performed perturbative calculations of neutrino scattering and absorption cross-sections in magnetized proto-neutron star matter using a fully relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory that includes nucleons, Lambda hyperons, electrons, and electron neutrinos. They solved the Dirac equation for baryons in a uniform magnetic field along the z-direction, treated the magnetic field as a weak perturbation on baryons (B less than about 10^18 G), and expanded the cross-sections into field-independent and field-dependent parts, then solved the Boltzmann equation for neutrino transport in a one-dimensional model.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry in neutrino absorption is estimated to be as much as 2.2% of the entire neutrino momentum for an interior magnetic field of approximately 2 x 10^17 G.
- Calculations were performed at core temperatures of 20-40 MeV.
- The lepton fraction was fixed at Y_L = 0.4 in the calculations.
- The RMF parameter set PM1-L1 gives a binding energy per baryon of 16 MeV, a nucleon effective mass M_N*/M_N = 0.7, and an incompressibility parameter K = 200 MeV at nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3.
- Lambda particles appear at baryon densities rho_B greater than approximately 2 rho_0 and significantly affect the equation of state for rho_B greater than approximately 3 rho_0.
- The density of anti-neutrinos is less than 0.5% of the neutrino density at rho_B = rho_0 and T = 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic calculations of neutrino-nucleon scattering in magnetized neutron stars by Lai et al. (1999) to a fully relativistic framework including hyperons, and addresses the criticism by Kusenko, Segre and Vilenkin (1998) that asymmetry in scattering cross-sections does not lead to asymmetric emission in complete thermodynamic equilibrium. The authors show that absorption, rather than scattering, can produce the asymmetry, and their results enable more realistic modeling of pulsar kick mechanisms in magnetized proto-neutron stars with strange matter.
