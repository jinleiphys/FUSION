# Delta rho pi interaction leading to N* and Delta* resonances

> We have performed a calculation for the three body $Δρπ$ system by using the fixed center approximation to Faddeev equations, taking the interaction between $Δ$ and $ρ$, $Δ$ and$π$, and $ρ$ and $π$ from the chiral unitary approach. We find several peaks in the modulus squared of the three-body scattering amplitude, indicating the existence of resonances, which can be associated to known $I=1/2, 3/2$ and $J^P=1/2^+, 3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ baryon states.

## Key claim

The three-body Delta-rho-pi system, solved via the fixed-center approximation to Faddeev equations with chiral unitary two-body interactions, produces several resonance peaks that can be associated with known N* and Delta* baryon states; however, one predicted state with isospin 3/2 and mass around 2000 MeV has no clear PDG counterpart, and the dynamics preclude the formation of exotic I=5/2 states.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations using the fixed-center approximation (FCA), treating the Delta-rho pair as a bound-state cluster (either an N* or Delta* resonance) and allowing the external pion to scatter successively off the Delta and the rho within that cluster. The two-body Delta-pi and rho-pi scattering amplitudes were taken from the chiral unitary approach, and the total three-body scattering amplitude T was obtained from the coupled partition functions T1 and T2. Peaks in the modulus squared of T were interpreted as resonances.

## Key numbers

- A resonance with I(Delta-rho)=1/2, total I=1/2 appears at a mass of approximately 1850 MeV, associated with the N*(1900) (3/2+, status **).
- A resonance with I(Delta-rho)=1/2, total I=3/2 appears at approximately 1800 MeV, associated with the Delta*(1750) (1/2+, status *).
- A resonance with I(Delta-rho)=1/2, total I=3/2 appears at approximately 1900 MeV, associated with the Delta*(1905) (5/2+, mass range 1865-1915 MeV, status ****) and the Delta*(1920) (3/2+, mass range 1900-1970 MeV, status ***).
- A resonance with I(Delta-rho)=1/2, total I=3/2 appears at approximately 2200 MeV, associated with a possible Delta*(2000) candidate (5/2+, mass 2200 +/- 125 MeV from Cutkosky (1980)).
- A resonance with I(Delta-rho)=3/2, total I=1/2 appears at approximately 2000 MeV, associated with the N*(2000) (5/2+, status **).
- A resonance with I(Delta-rho)=3/2, total I=3/2 appears at approximately 2000 MeV, with no clear PDG counterpart.

## Context

This work extends the fixed-center approximation approach previously applied to multi-rho systems (describing resonances such as f2(1270) and rho3(1690) by Roca (2010)) and to K*-multi-rho states (by Yamagata-Sekihara (2010)) to the baryonic three-body system Delta-rho-pi. The results suggest that several known N* and Delta* resonances can be understood as three-body states of two mesons and one baryon, building on earlier work by Martinez Torres (2007) and Khemchandani (2008). The missing I=3/2 state around 2000 MeV provides a clear prediction for experimental searches in three-body final-state reactions.
