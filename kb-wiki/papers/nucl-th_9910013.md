# Restoration of rotational invariance of bound states on the light front

> We study bound states in a model with scalar nucleons interacting via an exchanged scalar meson using the Hamiltonian formalism on the light front. In this approach manifest rotational invariance is broken when the Fock space is truncated. By considering an effective Hamiltonian that takes into account two meson exchanges, we find that this breaking of rotational invariance is decreased from that which occurs when only one meson exchange is included. The best improvement occurs when the states are weakly bound.

## Key claim

By including two-boson-exchange (TBE) terms in the effective light-front Hamiltonian, the breaking of rotational invariance caused by Fock-space truncation is reduced compared to using only one-boson-exchange (OBE) terms, with the best restoration occurring for weakly bound states.

## Method

The authors studied bound states of two distinguishable scalar nucleons (mass M) interacting via exchange of a lighter scalar meson (mass μ = 0.15 M) using light-front Hamiltonian formalism. They constructed effective potentials from light-front time-ordered perturbation theory in the "uncrossed approximation" (which reproduces the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation), truncating at OBE and OBE+TBE orders. Spectra (coupling constant versus bound-state mass curves) were computed for the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation (manifestly covariant) and for the light-front Schrödinger equation with OBE and OBE+TBE potentials, then compared by classifying states by their dominant angular momentum contribution.

## Key numbers

- Meson mass set to μ = 0.15 M, chosen to model a toy deuterium and facilitate comparison with Schoonderwoerd, Bakker, and Karmanov (1998).
- The OBE+TBE truncation showed decreased breaking of rotational invariance compared to OBE alone, with best improvement for weakly bound states (no specific numerical binding energies or coupling constants provided in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the light-front bound-state studies of Sales et al. (1999), who focused on ground states, by examining rotational invariance of excited states. It is closely connected to the scattering study of Schoonderwoerd, Bakker, and Karmanov (1998), who found that TBE diagrams contributed much less than iterated OBE diagrams at low momenta, suggesting lightly-bound states should be well described by OBE alone. The results enable systematic improvement of light-front bound-state calculations by including higher-order exchange terms to restore rotational symmetry.
