# Renormalization of a Finite Range Inverse Cube Potential

> We study the regularization and renormalization of a finite range inverse cube potential in the two- and three-body sectors. Specifically, we compare and contrast three different regulation schemes frequently used to study few-body systems as well as the associated renormalization group flows. We also calculate bound state and scattering observables over a wide range of cutoffs, demonstrating the sufficiency of a two-body contact interaction to renormalize two- and three-body observables. We supplement these plots with quantified analyses of the observables' residual cutoff dependence.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a two-body contact interaction is sufficient to renormalize both two- and three-body observables for a finite range inverse cube potential, but the residual cutoff dependence of observables converges more slowly than expected from previous studies of the three-nucleon sector, with oscillatory behavior not captured by simple power-law expansions.

## Method

The authors study a finite range inverse cube potential of the form \(V_S(r) = -C_3 e^{-m_\pi r}/r^3\) with \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV and \(C_3 = 0.8\) fm\(^2\), tuned to produce a deuteron-like two-body bound state at \(B_2 = 2.2\) MeV. They implement and compare three regularization schemes: local regulation using a coordinate-space cutoff and counterterm, semi-local regulation with a coordinate-space regulator on the potential and a momentum-space counterterm, and nonlocal regulation where both the potential and counterterm are regulated in momentum space. Two-body observables are calculated by solving the Schrodinger equation and Lippmann-Schwinger equation, while three-body observables are obtained from Faddeev equations for bound states and scattering.

## Key numbers

* Two-body binding energy fixed at \(B_2 = 2.2\) MeV to mimic a deuteron-like state
* Pion mass \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV and coupling \(C_3 = 0.8\) fm\(^2\) used in the finite range inverse cube potential
* Two-body phase shifts begin to converge at a cutoff \(\Lambda \approx 2\) GeV
* Leading cutoff correction exponent \(n = 1.5\) extracted from two-body phase shifts using the Griesshammer method comparing cutoffs \(\Lambda = 3408\) and \(6704\) MeV
* Optimal exponent for two-body scattering length cutoff dependence \(n_{\min} = 1.7\) from fitting to Eq. (rgi_correction)
* Phase shift and cross section analyses at center-of-mass momentum 106 MeV yield \(n_{\min} \approx 1.7\)
* Oscillation frequency parameter \(h \approx 1.5\) MeV\(^{-1/3}\) from two-body scattering length analysis
* Atom-dimer scattering length analysis yields optimal exponent \(n_{\min} = 1.3\) over cutoff range \(\Lambda_{\text{lower}} = 3.1\) GeV to \(\Lambda_{\text{upper}} = 8.1\) GeV
* Three-body phase shift and inelasticity analyses at center-of-mass kinetic energies 10, 50, and 100 MeV yield \(n_{\min}\) values ranging from 1.1 to 1.3

## Context

This work extends studies of inverse cube potential renormalization from the three-nucleon sector (Nogga 2005, Song 2016) to a simpler three-boson system, removing complications from spin-dependent tensor forces. The finite range inverse cube potential models the most singular piece of one-pion exchange in the deuteron channel, as identified by Sprung (1994) and Pavon Valderrama (2005). The paper enables systematic comparison of local, semi-local, and nonlocal regularization schemes commonly used in nuclear effective field theory, and provides quantitative analysis of residual cutoff dependence that can inform power counting schemes as suggested by Griesshammer (2015).
