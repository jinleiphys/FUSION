# Cosmological Lithium Problems

> We briefly describe the cosmological lithium problems followed by a summary of our recent theoretical work on the magnitude of the effects of electron screening, the possible existence of dark matter parallel universes and the use of non-extensive (Tsallis) statistics during big bang nucleosynthesis. Solutions within nuclear physics are also discussed and recent measurements of cross-sections based on indirect experimental techniques are summarized.

# Cosmological Lithium Problems

## Key claim

The standard Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) prediction for primordial ⁷Li abundance is approximately a factor of 3 higher than observations from metal-poor halo stars, and this discrepancy cannot be resolved by improved nuclear reaction cross-section measurements alone; however, a relatively small departure from Boltzmann statistics using the Tsallis parameter in the range 1.069 < q < 1.082 can bring the calculated ⁷Li abundance into agreement with observations while leaving other light-element abundances unchanged.

## Method

The authors performed BBN calculations using an extended code based on the Wagoner code and similar to NUC123, incorporating the full nuclear reaction network of 12 key reactions listed in Table 1. They investigated three non-standard physics scenarios: (1) electron screening effects using Debye-Hückel theory, (2) the presence of multiple dark matter sectors (up to N_DM = 5) with a separate temperature T', and (3) non-extensive Tsallis statistics with proper inclusion of reaction Q-values for reverse reactions. The calculations were compared to observed primordial abundances of D, ³He, ⁴He, ⁶Li, and ⁷Li.

## Key numbers

- The standard BBN prediction for ⁷Li/H is (4.283 +0.335 -0.292) × 10⁻¹⁰, while observations from metal-poor halo stars yield ⁷Li/H = (1.58 +0.35 -0.28) × 10⁻¹⁰, a discrepancy of roughly a factor of 3.
- The observed deuterium abundance is D/H = (2.527 ± 0.03) × 10⁻⁵, compatible with 100Ω_b h² (BBN) = 2.225 ± 0.016 from CMB measurements.
- The observed ⁴He mass fraction Y_p = 0.2565 ± 0.006, compared to the BBN calculation of 0.2485 (+0.001 -0.002).
- The BBN prediction for the ⁶Li/⁷Li isotopic ratio is (1.5 ± 0.3) × 10⁻⁵, while observations report ⁶Li/⁷Li ∼ 5 × 10⁻².
- The new ⁷Be(n,α) reaction rate from THM data is lower by a factor of approximately 10 relative to the rate used by Wagoner (1969), yielding a total cosmological lithium abundance of 4.441 × 10⁻¹⁰.
- For the dark matter parallel universe scenario with N_DM = 5, observations of D, ³He, and ⁴He are compatible with T'/T ∼ 0.2-0.3, and N_DM = 1-50 is compatible with these abundances when T' = 0.3T.
- The Tsallis parameter range that solves the ⁷Li problem while preserving other abundances is 1.069 < q < 1.082.

## Context

This work reviews and extends prior investigations of the cosmological lithium problems, which have persisted since the Spite plateau observations by Spite (1982). The authors summarize their own recent theoretical work on electron screening (Wan 2011), dark matter parallel universes (Oli 2011, Ber 2013, Oli 2016), and non-extensive Tsallis statistics (Hou 2017). The paper also discusses recent experimental results using the Trojan Horse Method (THM) by Lamia (2017) for the ⁷Be(n,α) reaction and the re-analysis of the ⁴He(d,γ)⁶Li reaction by MSB (2016). The work enables future investigations into whether non-standard physics during the BBN epoch, particularly departures from Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics, can resolve the lithium abundance discrepancies without invoking new particles or interactions.
