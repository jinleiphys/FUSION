# Supersymmetric quantum mechanics to study the 16.8 MeV resonance state of $^{9}$B

> The study of a high-lying resonant state of $^{9}$B is carried out using supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SQM). The resulting isospectral potentials are very deep and narrow and the generated wave functions identify the resonance at 16.84 MeV with a width of 69 keV. The present work shows that SQM can be successfully applied for detection of high-lying resonances in unstable nuclei.

# Supersymmetric quantum mechanics to study the 16.8 MeV resonance state of $^{9}$B

## Key claim

Supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SQM) can successfully detect a high-lying resonance in $^{9}$B at 16.84 MeV with a width of 69 keV, provided that the isospectral potentials are made very deep and narrow (200–300 MeV depth) to trap the state, whereas the original shallow two-body potential (d + $^{7}$Be) alone would not reveal the resonance.

## Method

The authors model $^{9}$B as a two-body system of a $^{7}$Be core and a deuteron, using a double folding model with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction and densities from variational Monte Carlo calculations (Argonne v18 + Urbana X potentials). From the resulting microscopic potential including the centrifugal barrier, they generate a family of strictly isospectral potentials via SQM, which add a bound state in the continuum (BIC) at a selected energy. The trapping probability $C(E)$ is computed from the normalizable wave functions between the classical turning points, and the resonance energy is identified from peaks in $C(E)$; the width is obtained via the WKB method.

## Key numbers

- Resonance energy: 16.84 MeV for the $\frac{5}{2}^{+}$ state of $^{9}$B, in agreement with the experimental value of 16.800(10) MeV from Scholl et al. (2011).
- Resonance width: 69 keV (calculated via WKB), compared to the experimental width of 81(5) keV from Scholl et al. (2011).
- Isospectral potential depths: approximately 200–300 MeV for the high-lying 16.84 MeV resonance, versus 20–50 MeV for the low-lying 1.8 MeV resonance in $^{15}$Be studied earlier by Dutta et al. (2018).
- Parameter $\lambda$ values used: $1 \times 10^{-6}$, $5 \times 10^{-7}$, and $1 \times 10^{-7}$.

## Context

This work extends the SQM-based method previously applied to low-lying resonances in unstable and unbound nuclei by Dutta et al. (2014, 2018) to the challenging case of a high-lying resonance in $^{9}$B. The 16.8 MeV state in $^{9}$B has been suggested as a possible solution to the cosmological lithium problem (Coc et al. 2008, Boyd et al. 2010, Rijal et al. 2019), where resonance-enhanced destruction of $^{7}$Be via the $^{7}$Be(d,p) reaction could reduce the primordial $^{7}$Li abundance. The successful reproduction of the experimental resonance energy and width demonstrates that SQM is applicable to high-lying resonances in unstable nuclei, enabling future studies of decay properties relevant to nuclear astrophysics.
