# Reconstruction of supernova ν_μ, ν_τ, anti-ν_μ, and anti-ν_τ neutrino spectra at scintillator detectors

> We present a new technique to directly reconstruct the spectra of mu/tau neutrinos and antineutrinos from a supernova, using neutrino-proton elastic scattering events (nu+p to nu+p) at scintillator detectors. These neutrinos, unlike electron neutrinos and antineutrinos, have only neutral current interactions, which makes it very challenging, with any reaction, to detect them and measure their energies. With updated inputs from theory and experiments, we show that this channel provides a robust and sensitive measure of their spectra. Given the low yields and lack of spectral information in other neutral current channels, this is perhaps the only realistic way to extract such information. This will be indispensable for understanding flavor oscillations of SN neutrinos, as it is likely to be impossible to disentangle neutrino mixing from astrophysical uncertainties in a SN without adequate spectral coverage of all flavors. We emphasize that scintillator detectors, e.g., Borexino, KamLAND, and SNO+, have the capability to observe these events, but they must be adequately prepared with a trigger for a burst of low-energy events. We also highlight the capabilities of a larger detector like LENA.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutrino-proton elastic scattering at scintillator detectors provides a robust and sensitive way to reconstruct the spectra of muon and tau neutrinos and antineutrinos from a supernova, but this is conditional on detectors being adequately prepared with a trigger for a burst of low-energy events and on the assumption that active-sterile neutrino mixing is absent.

## Method

The authors calculate the expected event spectra from neutrino-proton elastic scattering at three scintillator detectors (Borexino, KamLAND, and SNO+) using a Keil parametrization for the supernova neutrino fluence, the differential cross section for neutrino-proton scattering, and detector-specific parameters including number of free proton targets, Birks quenching constant, and energy resolution. They then develop a non-parametric inversion method to directly extract the unknown mu/tau neutrino fluence from the measured quenched proton recoil spectrum, accounting for detector response and backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- Expected signal yield above 0.2 MeV threshold: 27 events at Borexino (0.278 kton), 66 events at KamLAND (0.697 kton), and 111 events at SNO+ (0.800 kton) for a benchmark supernova at 10 kpc with total energy output of 3x10^53 erg equipartitioned among all six flavors
- Benchmark average energies used: 12 MeV for electron neutrinos, 15 MeV for electron antineutrinos, and 18 MeV for the four other flavors (mu/tau neutrinos and antineutrinos)
- Total yield with zero threshold is about 500 events per kiloton, similar to the electron antineutrino yield from inverse beta decays at water Cherenkov detectors
- Energy resolution values: 4.5%/sqrt(T') at Borexino, 6.9%/sqrt(T') at KamLAND, and 5.0%/sqrt(T') at SNO+ (T' in MeV)
- Background events above 0.2 MeV: approximately 2 events at Borexino and 31 events at KamLAND, mostly from the 210Po peak

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Beacom, Farr, and Vogel (2002) for detecting supernova neutrinos via neutrino-proton elastic scattering, updating it with contemporary inputs from supernova simulations that suggest lower average energies and smaller flavor differences than previously assumed. The technique addresses the critical need to measure all neutrino flavors from a supernova, which has become more important due to the discovery of collective neutrino oscillations (Duan 2006, Hannestad 2006, Raffelt 2007) that couple the flavor evolution of all species and require knowledge of all initial spectra. The method enables breaking degeneracies between astrophysical emission parameters and neutrino mixing parameters that would otherwise prevent firm inferences from supernova neutrino data.
