# $Z_3$ meta-stable states in PNJL model

> We study the Z 3 meta-stable states in the Polyakov loop Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. These states exist for temperatures above T m ~ 194 MeV and can decay via bubble nucleation. We numerically solve the bounce equation to compute the nucleation rate. We speculate that, in the context of heavy-ion collisions, the likely scenario for the decay of the meta-stable states is via spinodal decomposition.

## Key claim

The PNJL model predicts that $Z_3$ meta-stable states exist only for temperatures above $T_m \sim 194$ MeV, and the authors speculate that in heavy-ion collisions the decay of these states likely proceeds via spinodal decomposition rather than bubble nucleation, because the nucleation rate is too slow at the relevant temperatures.

## Method

The authors numerically minimized the mean-field thermodynamic potential of the two-flavor PNJL model at zero baryon chemical potential to locate the stable and meta-stable Polyakov loop vacua. They then solved the coupled bounce equations for the real and imaginary parts of the Polyakov loop field numerically, keeping the chiral condensate fixed, to compute the critical bubble profile and its Euclidean action for temperatures between 199.5 MeV and 228 MeV. The nucleation rate per unit volume was obtained from the bounce action as $\exp(-S/T)$.

## Key numbers

- Meta-stable states appear above $T_m \sim 194$ MeV, with the chiral transition temperature $T_c \sim 170$ MeV.
- The Polyakov loop magnitude in the stable state is $|\Phi| = 0.79763$ at 199.5 MeV and $|\Phi| = 0.92962$ at 247 MeV.
- The bubble action in units of temperature, $S/T$, was computed for the range $T = 199.5$ MeV to 228 MeV (1.05 $T_0$ to 1.2 $T_0$), with the action decreasing as temperature increases.
- The bubble radius for the $\Phi_1$ and $\Phi_2$ fields differs, with the $\Phi_2$ radius slightly larger; radii increase with temperature.
- Including an approximate $\sigma$ profile changes the bubble action by less than 10% decrement; a $\sigma$ profile scaled like $\Phi_1$ gives less than 20% decrement.

## Context

This work extends perturbative one-loop studies of $Z_3$ meta-stable states by Gross (1980) and Weiss (1981) to a non-perturbative mean-field model near the critical temperature, where perturbative calculations break down. It contrasts with lattice QCD results by Deka (2010) which found meta-stable states become unstable below $2T_c$, and with the PQM model study by Mishra (2016) which found meta-stable states above 310 MeV. The results enable estimates of whether meta-stable Polyakov loop states can survive in the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that spinodal decomposition rather than bubble nucleation is the likely decay mechanism.
