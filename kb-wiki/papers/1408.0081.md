# Nuclear deformation at finite temperature

> Deformation, a key concept in our understanding of heavy nuclei, is based on a mean-field description that breaks the rotational invariance of the nuclear many-body Hamiltonian. We present a method to analyze nuclear deformations at finite temperature in a framework that preserves rotational invariance. The auxiliary-field Monte-Carlo method is used to generate the statistical ensemble and calculate the probability distribution associated with the quadrupole operator. Applying the technique to nuclei in the rare-earth region, we identify model-independent signatures of deformation and find that deformation effects persist to higher temperatures than the spherical-to-deformed shape phase-transition temperature of mean-field theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the probability distribution of the spectroscopic quadrupole operator in the laboratory frame, computed via auxiliary-field Monte Carlo (AFMC) while preserving rotational invariance, exhibits model-independent signatures of nuclear deformation at finite temperature; deformation effects persist to higher temperatures than the spherical-to-deformed shape phase-transition temperature predicted by mean-field theory.

## Method

The authors use the auxiliary-field Monte Carlo (AFMC) method, also called shell-model Monte Carlo (SMMC), to compute the thermal probability distribution P_β(q) of the axial mass quadrupole operator Q_20 in the laboratory frame for rare-earth nuclei. The distribution is obtained via a Fourier representation of the delta function, where the many-particle traces reduce to determinants in the single-particle space because both Q_20 and the imaginary-time propagator U_σ are one-body operators. Rotationally invariant observables (second- through fifth-order invariants of the quadrupole tensor) are extracted from the lab-frame moments of Q_20, and effective intrinsic shape parameters β, γ, and the fluctuation Δβ are derived from these invariants.

## Key numbers

* For ¹⁵⁴Sm at T=0.1 MeV, the low-temperature quadrupole distribution matches a rigid-rotor distribution with intrinsic quadrupole moment Q_0 ~ 1600 fm² (experimentally determined from in-band electric quadrupole transitions).
* The HFB shape transition temperature for ¹⁵⁴Sm is T=1.14 MeV, corresponding to an HFB excitation energy of about 25 MeV.
* Effective deformation parameters at low temperature: ¹⁵⁴Sm has β=0.232 and γ=13.4°; ¹⁴⁸Sm has β=0.137 and γ=21.6°.
* The relative fluctuation in β is Δβ/β = 0.51 for ¹⁵⁴Sm and 0.72 for ¹⁴⁸Sm, indicating the deformed nucleus is more rigid.

## Context

This work extends previous AFMC studies of deformation (Alhassid 1996) that used an ad hoc prescription to extract intrinsic-frame properties, by instead computing rotationally invariant observables directly from lab-frame moments. The method enables construction of the joint level density distribution ρ(β,E_x) as a function of deformation and excitation energy, which is an important component for calculating fission rates in excited nuclei (Bertsch 2009). The results contradict the sharp spherical-to-deformed shape phase transition predicted by finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory (Goodman 1986, Agrawal 2000, Ma 2003), showing instead a washed-out transition with deformation effects surviving well above the mean-field transition temperature.
