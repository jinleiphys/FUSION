# Quantum Mechanics on Manifolds Embedded in Euclidean Space

> Quantum particles confined to surfaces in higher dimensional spaces are acted upon by forces that exist only as a result of the surface geometry and the quantum mechanical nature of the system. The dynamics are particularly rich when confinement is implemented by forces that act normal to the surface. We review this confining potential formalism applied to the confinement of a particle to an arbitrary manifold embedded in a higher dimensional Euclidean space. We devote special attention to the geometrically induced gauge potential that appears in the effective Hamiltonian for motion on the surface. We emphasize that the gauge potential is only present when the space of states describing the degrees of freedom normal to the surface is degenerate. We also distinguish between the effects of the intrinsic and extrinsic geometry on the effective Hamiltonian and provide simple expressions for the induced scalar potential. We discuss examples including the case of a 3-dimensional manifold embedded in a 5-dimensional Euclidean space.

# Quantum Mechanics on Manifolds Embedded in Euclidean Space

## Key claim

The confining potential formalism yields a unique effective Hamiltonian for a quantum particle constrained to an arbitrary manifold embedded in higher-dimensional Euclidean space, but the geometrically induced gauge potential appears **only when the space of states describing the degrees of freedom normal to the surface is degenerate**, a condition the abstract mentions but does not emphasize as the central prerequisite for nontrivial gauge physics.

## Method

The authors derive the effective Hamiltonian for a spinless particle confined to an m-dimensional manifold M^m embedded in n-dimensional Euclidean space by introducing an adapted coordinate frame that separates tangential from normal degrees of freedom. They expand the confining potential around its minimum, rescale the normal coordinates by a small parameter epsilon, and perform a perturbative expansion in epsilon to obtain the leading-order effective Hamiltonian. The effective Hamiltonian on M^m is then obtained by projecting the full Hamiltonian onto a low-lying multiplet of normal states, typically the ground state or a degenerate subspace of the confining potential.

## Key numbers

- The effective scalar potential for a curve M^1 embedded in R^3 is given by P = -(1/8) kappa^2 I, where kappa is the curvature of the curve.
- The effective scalar potential for a surface M^2 embedded in R^3 is given by P = -(1/8)(kappa_1 - kappa_2)^2 I, where kappa_1 and kappa_2 are the principal curvatures.
- For M^3 embedded in R^4, the effective potential is P = -(1/8)[kappa_3(kappa_3 - 2(kappa_1 + kappa_2)) + (kappa_1 - kappa_2)^2] I.
- For the explicit helical embedding of M^3 in R^5 (parameterized by rho), the induced scalar potential is P = -rho^2/[8(1 + rho^2 x^2)^2] * (4 + (1/2) rho^2 x^2) I.
- The gauge potential for this helical embedding is A_mu = (0, 0, rho l / sqrt(2 + 2 rho^2 x^2)), where l is the expectation value of the normal state angular momentum operator L_{12}.
- The corresponding magnetic-like field has nonvanishing y-component B_y = rho^3 x l / [sqrt(2)(1 + rho^2 x^2)^{3/2}].

## Context

This work reviews and extends the confining potential formalism previously developed by da Costa (1981) for surfaces in three-dimensional space, and by Maraner and Destri (1992) for arbitrary embeddings. The paper generalizes these results to arbitrary m-dimensional manifolds in n-dimensional Euclidean space, with particular attention to the group-theoretic structure of the gauge fields and the role of normal-state degeneracy. The formalism enables the study of geometrically induced gauge fields in systems such as electrons in quantum Hall devices and molecular rotational spectra, and provides a framework for understanding how extrinsic geometry generates quantum effects absent in classical constrained dynamics.
