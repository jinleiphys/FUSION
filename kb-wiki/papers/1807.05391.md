# New measures of longitudinal decorrelation of harmonic flow

> Harmonic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is observed in a broad range of rapidities, and the flow at different rapidities is correlated. However, fluctuations lead to a small decorrelation of the harmonic flow magnitudes and flow angles at different rapidities. Using a hydrodynamic model with Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions we show that the flow angle decorrelation strongly depends on the flow magnitude in the event. We propose observables to measure this effect in experiment.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the decorrelation of harmonic flow angles between different rapidities is actually larger than the combined decorrelation from both flow magnitude and angle effects, which is an "inverted hierarchy" that can be explained by a strong anticorrelation between flow magnitude and torque angle: events with large harmonic flow have smaller torque angles, while events with small flow have wider torque angle distributions.

## Method

The authors used a hydrodynamic model with Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions to simulate relativistic heavy-ion collisions. They calculated separate correlators for torque angle decorrelation and magnitude decorrelation directly from the model, and proposed new observables that weight the standard three-bin correlator with different powers of the flow coefficient \(v_n^{2k}\) to experimentally measure the correlation between flow magnitude and torque angle.

## Key numbers

- The four-bin correlator \(R_n(\eta)\) measures the torque component alone, with the form \(R_n(\eta) \simeq 1 - 2 F^{\rm tor}_{n,2} \eta\)
- The three-bin correlator \(r_{n,2}(\eta)\) measures both torque and magnitude decorrelation combined, with the form \(r_{n,2}(\eta) \simeq 1 - 2 F^{\rm asy}_{n,2} \eta - 2 F^{\rm tor}_{n,2} \eta\)
- ATLAS Collaboration measurements (Aaboud 2017) found the two components are similar: \(F^{\rm tor}_{n,2} \simeq F^{\rm asy}_{n,2}\)
- The proposed new correlator series is \(r_{n|n;1}^{n;2k}(\eta) = \frac{\langle v_n^{2k}(0) q_n(-\eta) q_n^\star(\eta_{ref}) \rangle}{\langle v_n^{2k}(0) q_n(\eta) q_n^\star(\eta_{ref}) \rangle}\) for \(k=0,1,2,\dots\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of longitudinal flow decorrelation by Bozek (2010) and the CMS Collaboration (Khachatryan 2015), who introduced the three-bin correlator to reduce non-flow contributions. The paper builds on the separation of torque angle and magnitude decorrelation proposed by Jia (2014, 2017) and the ATLAS measurements (Aaboud 2017) showing both components are similar in magnitude. The results enable experimental verification of the anticorrelation between flow magnitude and torque angle using the proposed weighted correlators, and a similar inverted hierarchy was recently found in a hybrid model with AMPT initial conditions by Wu (2018).
