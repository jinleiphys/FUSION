# Multiple Mechanisms in Proton-Induced Nucleon Removal at $\sim$100 MeV/Nucleon

> We report on the first proton-induced single proton- and neutron-removal reactions from the neutron-deficient $^{14}$O nucleus with large Fermi-surface asymmetry $S_n-S_p$ = 18.6 MeV at $\sim$100 MeV/nucleon, a widely used energy regime for rare-isotope studies. The measured inclusive cross sections and parallel momentum distributions of the $^{13}$N and $^{13}$O residues are compared to the state-of-the-art reaction models, with nuclear structure inputs from many-body shell-model calculations. Our results provide the first quantitative contributions of multiple reaction mechanisms including the quasifree knockout, inelastic scattering and nucleon transfer processes. It is shown that the inelastic scattering and nucleon transfer, usually neglected at such energy regime, contribute about 50% and 30% to the loosely bound proton and deeply bound neutron removal, respectively. These multiple reaction mechanisms should be considered in analyses of inclusive one-nucleon removal cross sections measured at intermediate energies for quantitative investigation of single-particle strengths and correlations in atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first quantitative evidence that, for proton-induced nucleon removal at ~100 MeV/nucleon, inelastic scattering and nucleon transfer contribute about 50% to loosely bound proton removal and about 30% to deeply bound neutron removal, respectively; these mechanisms are usually neglected in this energy regime, and ignoring them leads to an artificially strong dependence of the reduction factor on Fermi-surface asymmetry.

## Method

The experiment used a 94 MeV/nucleon beam of the neutron-deficient nucleus 14O incident on a solid hydrogen target at the RIKEN RIBF facility. Inclusive cross sections and parallel momentum distributions of the 13N and 13O residues were measured using the SAMURAI spectrometer and compared to state-of-the-art reaction models: the Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) and the Quantum Transfer-to-the-Continuum (QTC) model for quasifree knockout, plus calculations for inelastic scattering and (p,d) transfer, with nuclear structure inputs from many-body shell-model calculations.

## Key numbers

- Fermi-surface asymmetry Sn - Sp = 18.6 MeV for 14O
- Beam energy at target center: 94 MeV/nucleon with a spread of 0.2 MeV/nucleon (sigma)
- Experimental cross section for 13N (proton removal): 10.7(16) mb
- Experimental cross section for 13O (neutron removal): 16.7(24) mb
- Inelastic scattering contributes 51% (DWIA) or 43% (QTC) to the loosely bound proton removal cross section
- (p,d) transfer contributes approximately 30% to the deeply bound neutron removal cross section, with a single-particle cross section of 3 mb (uncertainty about 1 mb)
- Reduction factor Rs slope with Delta S: -3.0(5)(5) x 10^-3 MeV^-1 (DWIA plus inelastic and transfer) and -4.6(4)(7) x 10^-3 MeV^-1 (QTC plus inelastic)
- Target thickness: 2.40(34) mm; target density: 86 mg/cm^3

## Context

This work extends studies of one-nucleon removal reactions at intermediate energies, which previously used light absorptive targets like 9Be or 12C (Gade 2008, Tostevin and Gade 2014, 2021) and found a strong dependence of the reduction factor Rs on Fermi-surface asymmetry. The results contradict the strong Delta S dependence from eikonal-model-based analyses and instead align with the weak dependence seen in low-energy transfer reactions (Flavigny 2013, Xu 2019, Manfredi 2021) and high-energy quasifree scattering (Atar 2018, Gomez-Ramos 2018). The findings enable more quantitative extraction of single-particle strengths and correlations from inclusive one-nucleon removal measurements at intermediate energies by requiring explicit treatment of inelastic scattering and nucleon transfer mechanisms.
