# Analytical and numerical assessment of accuracy of the approximated nuclear symmetry energy in the Hartree-Fock theory

> The nuclear symmetry energy is defined by the second derivative of the energy per nucleon with respect to the proton-neutron asymmetry, and is sometimes approximated by the energy difference between the neutron matter and the symmetric matter. Accuracy of this approximation is assessed analytically and numerically, within the Hartree-Fock theory using effective interactions. By decomposing the nuclear-matter energy, the relative error of each term is expressed analytically; it is constant or is a single-variable function determined by the function type. The full errors are evaluated for several effective interactions, by inserting values of the parameters. Although the errors stay within $10\%$ up to twice of the normal density irrespective of the interactions, at higher densities accuracy of the approximation significantly depends on the interactions.

## Key claim

The approximation of the nuclear symmetry energy by the energy difference between pure neutron matter and symmetric matter has relative errors within 10% up to twice normal density for all six effective interactions tested, but at higher densities the accuracy depends significantly on the interaction type and parameter set, with the function type (Gauss vs. Yukawa) playing a role in the error magnitude.

## Method

The authors decomposed the nuclear-matter energy per nucleon into kinetic, density-dependent, Hartree, and Fock terms within the Hartree-Fock theory using effective interactions (Skyrme, Gogny, and M3Y-type). They derived analytical expressions for the relative error of each term in the approximated symmetry energy, finding that some terms have constant relative errors while others depend on the ratio of Fermi momentum to range parameter. Numerical errors were then computed by inserting parameter values for six effective interactions: SkM*, SLy4, D1S, D1M, M3Y-P6, and M3Y-P7.

## Key numbers

- The relative error of the kinetic term is constant: Δ_K = 0.057 (5.7%).
- For the Skyrme interaction momentum-dependent terms, the ratio of approximated to exact symmetry energy is 0.979 for like-nucleon terms and 0.900 for unlike-nucleon terms.
- The relative error |Δ_t| < 0.1 (10%) for all interactions up to ρ = 0.32 fm^{-3} (twice normal density), and within |Δ_t| < 0.05 except for SkM*.
- At higher densities (0.32 ≤ ρ < 0.64 fm^{-3}), |Δ_t| < 0.05 holds for SLy4 and M3Y-type interactions, while |Δ_t| exceeds 0.20 for SkM* and the Gogny interactions.
- The absolute error δ_t ranges from approximately -2 MeV to +4 MeV up to twice normal density.
- For the Gogny interaction D1S, Δ_t diverges at ρ = 0.55 fm^{-3} because a_t reaches zero.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Chen et al. (using a modified Gogny interaction at saturation density), Bombaci and Lombardo (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock with the Paris potential), Zuo et al. (with AV18 plus three-nucleon force), and Drischler et al. (chiral effective field theory). It provides analytical decomposition of errors term-by-term within Hartree-Fock theory, enabling identification of which interaction components cause the approximation to fail at high density. The results enable better selection of effective interactions for neutron-rich systems and neutron star structure calculations, and suggest that fitting to microscopic neutron-matter equation of state (as done for SLy4, D1M, M3Y-P6, M3Y-P7) helps keep errors small.
