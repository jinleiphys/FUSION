# Measurement of Differential Cross Sections for $ν_μ$-Ar Charged-Current Interactions with Protons and no Pions in the Final State with the MicroBooNE Detector

> We present an analysis of MicroBooNE data with a signature of one muon, no pions, and at least one proton above a momentum threshold of 300 MeV/c (CC0$π$Np). This is the first differential cross section measurement of this topology in neutrino-argon interactions. We achieve a significantly lower proton momentum threshold than previous carbon and scintillator-based experiments. Using data collected from a total of approximately $1.6 \times 10^{20}$ protons-on-target, we measure the muon neutrino cross section for the CC0$π$Np interaction channel in argon at MicroBooNE in the Booster Neutrino Beam which has a mean energy of around 800 MeV. We present the results from a data sample with estimated efficiency of 29\% and purity of 76\% as differential cross sections in five reconstructed variables: the muon momentum and polar angle, the leading proton momentum and polar angle, and the muon-proton opening angle. We include smearing matrices that can be used to "forward-fold" theoretical predictions for comparison with these data. We compare the measured differential cross sections to a number of recent theory predictions demonstrating largely good agreement with this first-ever data set on argon.

## Key claim

The MicroBooNE collaboration presents the first differential cross section measurement of charged-current neutrino-argon interactions with one muon, no pions, and at least one proton above 300 MeV/c (CC0πNp), finding largely good agreement with recent theory predictions, though the measurement is limited to an estimated efficiency of 29% and purity of 76% and requires forward-folding of theoretical predictions using provided smearing matrices for proper comparison.

## Method

The analysis uses data from approximately 1.6 × 10^20 protons-on-target collected with the MicroBooNE liquid argon time projection chamber in the Booster Neutrino Beam (mean energy ~800 MeV). Events are selected with a muon momentum above 100 MeV/c, at least one proton with momentum between 300 and 1200 MeV/c, and no pions or other mesons in the final state. The measured differential cross sections are presented in five reconstructed variables: muon momentum and polar angle, leading proton momentum and polar angle, and muon-proton opening angle, with smearing matrices provided to enable forward-folding of theoretical predictions.

## Key numbers

- Proton momentum threshold: 300 MeV/c (significantly lower than previous carbon and scintillator-based experiments)
- Data sample efficiency: 29%
- Data sample purity: 76%
- Total protons-on-target: approximately 1.6 × 10^20
- Mean neutrino beam energy: approximately 800 MeV
- Efficiency peaks at roughly 35% for all variables
- Muon momentum threshold: 100 MeV/c
- Leading proton momentum range: 300 MeV/c to 1200 MeV/c

## Context

This measurement extends previous CC0π results from MINERvA (Betancourt 2017, Ruterbories 2018) and T2K (Abe 2018) by providing the first such measurement on an argon target, with a significantly lower proton momentum threshold than the 450 MeV/c threshold of MINERvA or the 500 MeV/c threshold of T2K. The data enable testing of nuclear models in event generators such as GENIE, particularly for the contributions from quasielastic interactions, multinucleon (2p2h) processes, and pion production with subsequent absorption, and provide crucial input for future oscillation experiments using argon targets such as DUNE and the Short-Baseline Neutrino program.
