# Zero-temperature equation of state of mass-imbalanced resonant Fermi gases

> We calculate the zero-temperature equation of state of mass-imbalanced resonant Fermi gases in an ab initio fashion, by implementing the recent proposal of imaginary-valued mass difference to bypass the sign problem in lattice Monte Carlo calculations. The fully non-perturbative results thus obtained are analytically continued to real mass imbalance to yield the physical equation of state, providing predictions for upcoming experiments with mass-imbalanced atomic Fermi gases. In addition, we present an exact relation for the rate of change of the equation of state at small mass imbalances, showing that it is fully determined by the energy of the mass-balanced system.

## Key claim

The zero-temperature equation of state of mass-imbalanced resonant Fermi gases can be computed non-perturbatively by analytically continuing from imaginary mass imbalance, but the results for large real mass imbalances depend strongly on the details of the fit function used for analytic continuation, with uncertainty of about 30% at most for the mass imbalance of a Li-K mixture.

## Method

The authors implemented a projection quantum Monte Carlo algorithm on a spacetime lattice, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to represent the interaction. To bypass the sign problem that arises from finite mass imbalance, they introduced an imaginary-valued mass difference such that the fermion determinants become complex conjugates of each other, making their product positive semidefinite. The data obtained for imaginary mass imbalances in the range i*m* = 0 to 1 were then analytically continued to real mass imbalance using a Pade approximant fit function.

## Key numbers

- The Bertsch parameter (equation of state at zero mass imbalance) in the large-volume limit: xi(m=0) = 0.420 +/- 0.007, from a fit to data at density N/N_x^3 approximately 0.05
- The curvature of the equation of state at zero mass imbalance: xi^(1) = 0.461 +/- 0.181, from a fit using data with m <= 0.4
- For a Li-K mixture (m approximately 0.74), the mean-field result overestimates the ground-state energy by a factor of about 2.0 compared to the QMC estimate in the large-volume limit
- For the smallest lattice (N_x=8, N=24), the Bertsch parameter was xi(m=0) approximately 0.449 +/- 0.002 and the curvature xi_M = 0.496 +/- 0.010

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of mass-imbalanced Fermi gases (e.g., the review by Chevy and others) which overestimate the ground-state energy by about 50% for the mass-balanced case. The authors implement a technique originally devised for lattice QCD (related to the sign problem at finite baryon density) and previously proposed by the same authors in Braun et al. (2012) and Braun (2013). The results are qualitatively consistent with a direct QMC calculation by Gezerlis (2011) for the Li-K mixture, and enable future non-perturbative calculations for mass-imbalanced systems away from unitarity, in various dimensions, at finite temperatures, and in external potentials.
