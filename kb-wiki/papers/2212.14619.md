# On the correlation functions in stable first-order relativistic hydrodynamics

> First-order relativistic conformal hydrodynamics in a general (hydrodynamic) frame is characterized by a shear viscosity coefficient and two UV-regulator parameters. Within a certain range of these parameters, the equilibrium is stable and propagation is causal. In this work we study the correlation functions of fluctuations in this theory. We first compute hydrodynamic correlation functions in the linear response regime. Then we use the linear response results to explore the analytical structure of response functions beyond the linear response. A method is developed to numerically calculate the branch cut structure from the well-known Landau equations. We apply our method to the shear channel and find the branch cuts of a certain response function, without computing the response function itself. We then solve the Landau equations analytically and find the threshold singularities of the same response function. Using these results, we achieve the leading singularity in momentum space, by which, we find the long-time tail of the correlation function. The results turn out to be in complete agreement with the loop calculations in effective field theory.

## Key claim

The long-time tail of the shear stress correlation function in stable first-order relativistic hydrodynamics (BDNK theory) is consistent with the long-time tail in conventional hydrodynamics, provided the stability and causality conditions on the UV-regulator parameters are satisfied.

## Method

The authors compute linear response correlation functions for the shear and sound channels in BDNK theory by extending the Landau-Lifshitz method of computing correlation functions to relativistic systems. For the nonlinear regime, they use the fact that correlation functions factorize under a Gaussian distribution of fluctuations, reducing to a Feynman bubble diagram. They then apply the Landau equations to find threshold singularities and branch cuts of the shear stress response function without explicitly computing the loop integral, and extract the long-time tail from the leading singularity in momentum space.

## Key numbers

* The stability and causality conditions for conformal fluids in D=4 dimensions are: \(1-\frac{3\bar{\eta}}{\bar{\theta}}-\frac{\bar{\eta}}{\bar{\pi}}>0\) and \(\bar{\pi}>4\bar{\eta}\); sufficient conditions are \(\bar{\theta}>4\bar{\eta}\) and \(\bar{\pi}>4\bar{\eta}\).
* The BDNK spectrum in the sound channel is consistent with conventional relativistic hydrodynamics for \(\qn\lesssim0.5\) when \(\theta_{\eta}=4\).
* The energy density correlation function in the small frequency limit is \(\lim_{\wn\rightarrow0}G_{T_{tt}T_{tt}}(\wn, \qn)=-\frac{6\,\bar{\eta}\,T^4\,\big(\pi_{\eta}^2\theta_{\eta}\,\qn^2+48(\pi_{\eta}-4)\big)}{\theta_{\eta}(\pi_{\eta}-4)\,\qn^2+48}\), which is negative for stable frames (\(\theta_{\eta},\pi_{\eta}>4\)).
* The transverse (shear) channel spectral function is \(\mathcal{D}_T(\wn,\qn)=\theta_{\eta}\wn^2+4 i\wn-\qn^2\), yielding two modes per half-plane in BDNK theory versus one mode per half-plane in conventional hydrodynamics.

## Context

This work extends the BDNK framework of Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2019), which introduced first-order relativistic hydrodynamics with UV-regulator parameters that ensure stability and causality, a problem that plagued earlier Eckart and Landau-Lifshitz formulations (Hiscock, 1985; Hiscock, 1987). The authors apply Landau equation methods from quantum field theory to hydrodynamic correlation functions, following the approach used by Kovtun (2003) for conventional hydrodynamics. The results enable the study of long-time tails in BDNK theory without explicit loop calculations, and confirm agreement with effective field theory loop calculations of Chen-Lin (2018) and Martinez (2018).
