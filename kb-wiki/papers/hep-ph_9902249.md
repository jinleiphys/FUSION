# A variational fit to the lattice energy of two heavy-light mesons

> Recent lattice calculations on the interaction energy of two heavy-light mesons (Q^2\bar{q}^2) are interpreted in terms of the potential of the corresponding single heavy-light meson (Q\bar{q}). This model leads to a large overestimate of the binding compared with the lattice data -- unless the basic Q\bar{q} potential is modified to become a four-quark potential.

# A variational fit to the lattice energy of two heavy-light mesons

## Key claim

The lattice interaction energy of two heavy-light mesons (Q²q̄²) in SU(3) cannot be described using only two-quark potentials derived from the single heavy-light meson (Qq̄) system, because such a model overestimates the binding energy by over a factor of three at the smallest separation; the overestimate can be corrected by introducing a four-quark form factor with a single free parameter.

## Method

The authors performed variational solutions of the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation for both the two-quark (Qq̄) and four-quark (Q²q̄²) systems, using a two-quark potential fitted to lattice data from 40 configurations on a 16³×24 lattice at β=5.7. For the four-quark system, they constructed a 2×2 Hamiltonian matrix in the basis of two color-singlet partitions, with matrix elements integrated over the positions of the two light antiquarks, and introduced a Gaussian four-quark form factor f = exp[-k_f b_s Σ r_ij²] to suppress the overbinding. The free parameter k_f was adjusted to fit the lattice binding energies of the Q²q̄² system from Refs. [cp] and [pmg].

## Key numbers

- The two-quark potential fit (χ²/dof=1.65) gave parameters: Coulomb strength 0.309(38) (dimensionless in lattice units), string coefficient 0.1649(36) (fm⁻¹), constant 0.629(25) (fm⁻¹), yielding a string energy of (445 MeV)² for lattice spacing a=0.18 fm.
- The effective light quark mass was determined to be m_q = 400 MeV from fitting spin-averaged Qq̄ energies for L=0,1,2,3.
- At R=0.18 fm, the weak-coupling model (k_f=0) overestimates the lattice binding energy by over a factor of three.
- The optimal four-quark form factor parameter was k_f ≈ 0.10 for the Q²q̄² system, compared to k_f = 0.075 for the earlier Q⁴ static system.
- The two-body kinetic energy in the four-quark system at R=0.18 fm varied from 286 MeV (k_f=0.0) to 346 MeV (k_f=0.45), with near-complete cancellation against the two-body kinetic energy of 339 MeV at the optimal k_f ≈ 0.25.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons between lattice data and many-body models for four static quarks (Q⁴) in SU(2) by Green, Koponen, and Pennanen (1998) and by the same group in Refs. [en]–[gmp], which showed that two-quark potentials alone produce a gross overestimate of binding due to the van der Waals effect. The present study improves upon those calculations by using two heavy and two light quarks (Q²q̄²) in SU(3) color, and by incorporating preliminary unquenched lattice data from the UKQCD collaboration (Allton, 1998). The results enable the construction of an effective four-quark interaction that may be applicable to meson-baryon or baryon-baryon scattering systems with more than four quarks, though the universality of the form factor remains to be tested.
