# Generating a resonance-like structure in the reaction $B_c\to B_s ππ$

> We investigate the process $B_c^+\to B_s^0π^+π^0$ via $B\bar{K}^*$ rescattering. The kinematic conditions for triangle singularities are perfectly satisfied in the rescattering diagrams. A resonance-like structure around the $B\bar{K}$ threshold, which we denote as $X(5777)$, is predicted to be present in the invariant mass distribution of $B_s^0 π^+$. Because the relative weak $B\bar{K}$ $(I=1)$ interaction does not support the existence of a dynamically generated hadronic molecule, the $X(5777)$ can be identified as a pure kinematical effect due to the triangle singularity. Its observation may help to establish a non-resonance interpretation for some $XYZ$ particles.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that a narrow resonance-like structure, denoted $X(5777)$, will appear in the $B_s^0\pi^+$ invariant mass distribution from $B_c^+\to B_s^0\pi^+\pi^0$ decay, but this structure is not a genuine particle. Because the $B\bar{K}$ $(I=1)$ interaction is too weak to support a dynamically generated hadronic molecule, the $X(5777)$ must be a pure kinematical effect arising from a triangle singularity in the rescattering amplitude, provided the kinematic conditions are perfectly satisfied.

## Method
The authors compute the decay $B_c^+\to B_s^0\pi^+\pi^0$ via triangle rescattering diagrams where $B_c^+$ decays to $B^+\bar{K}^{*0}$, the $\bar{K}^{*0}$ decays to $\bar{K}^0\pi^0$, and the $B^+\bar{K}^0$ rescatters into $B_s^0\pi^+$ through $S$-wave coupled-channel scattering. The $B\bar{K}\to B_s\pi$ amplitude is unitarized using next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory, with low-energy constants fixed by lattice QCD results for $DK$ scattering lengths. The loop integral includes a Breit-Wigner propagator for the unstable $\bar{K}^*$ and a monopole form factor with cutoff $\Lambda$. Background from $B_c^+\to B_s^0\rho^+$ is included with a relative phase $\theta$ and a $\pi\pi$ final-state interaction function.

## Key numbers
- The $X(5777)$ peak appears at approximately $5.777$ GeV, which is the $B\bar{K}$ threshold.
- The triangle singularity in $\sqrt{s_2}$ (the $B_s^0\pi^+$ invariant mass) moves from $5.849$ GeV to $5.777$ GeV as $\sqrt{s_1}$ (the $B_c^+$ mass) increases from $6.175$ GeV to $6.297$ GeV.
- The physical $B_c^+$ mass is $6.276$ GeV, which falls into the kinematic region where the triangle singularity can be present.
- The $S$-wave scattering length of the isovector $B\bar{K}$ channel is $a_{B\bar{K}}^{I=1} = 0.02 - 0.23i$ fm, indicating weak interaction.
- The $DK$ $(I=0)$ scattering length from lattice QCD is $a_{DK}^{I=0} = -0.86 \pm 0.03$ fm (Liu 2012) or $-1.33(20)$ fm (Mohler 2013), while the $DK$ $(I=1)$ scattering length is $0.07 \pm 0.03 + i(0.17^{+0.02}_{-0.01})$ fm.
- The branching ratio of $B_c^+\to B^+\bar{K}^{*0}$ is predicted to be larger than $10^{-3}$, while $B_c^+\to B_s^0\rho^+$ is larger than $1\%$.
- The cutoff $\Lambda$ is taken as 1 GeV or 3 GeV; the lineshape is insensitive to this choice.

## Context
This work extends the triangle singularity mechanism previously applied to phenomena such as $\eta(1405)\to 3\pi$, the $a_1(1420)$ production, and the $Z_c^\pm(3900)$ (Guo 2014, Liu 2015, Roca 2017). It directly addresses the controversy surrounding the $X(5568)$ reported by D0 (2016) but not confirmed by LHCb (2016); the authors argue that the $X(5568)$ interpretation as a $B\bar{K}$ molecule is questionable because chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order (Altenbuchinger 2013, Lu 2016) finds no bound state in the isovector channel. The predicted $X(5777)$ provides a clean test case: if observed, it would support the triangle singularity interpretation for some $XYZ$ particles, distinguishing kinematic effects from genuine resonances. The proposed reaction could be studied at LHCb, though the presence of a neutral pion in the final state poses experimental challenges.
