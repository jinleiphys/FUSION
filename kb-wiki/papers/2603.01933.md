# Hadronic description of nuclear matter and neutron star properties

> The composition of the neutron star is one of the most fundamental and long-standing problems in nuclear- and astro-physics. The known properties of nuclear matter, together with the astronomical observations, impose the stringent and interconnected constraints on the theoretical descriptions. In this work, by using the most general quantum hadrodynamics model including $σ, ω, ρ$ and $a_0$ in addition to nucleons, and performing a Bayesian joint analysis of experimental nuclear matter data and astrophysical observations, we point out that the nuclear matter made of only hadrons can provide a unified description of nuclear matter properties and astrophysical observations at $1 σ$-level. In addition, we find that the existence of \(σωρa_0\) interaction naturally leads to a peak structure in the speed of sound at $\sim (2-3)$ times saturation density $n_0$ which results to a small size intermediate mass neutron star and the upper bound mass $\sim 2M_\odot$. What we find here indicate that the sequential measurement of neutron star mass and radius by the next generation facilities, especially that of the intermediate mass neutron stars, is crucial for distinguishing the pure nucleonic stars from the hybrid ones.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a pure hadronic model (without exotic degrees of freedom such as quark matter) can simultaneously satisfy all existing nuclear matter constraints and neutron star astrophysical observations at the 1-sigma level, provided the model includes sigma, omega, rho, and a0 mesons with a specific mixed coupling term. The hedge is that this conclusion depends on the inclusion of the sigma-omega-rho-a0 interaction term (b8 coupling), which produces a peak in the speed of sound at intermediate densities.

## Method

The authors constructed a general quantum hadrodynamics (GQHD) model including nucleons and sigma, omega, rho, and a0 mesons with all possible interactions up to dimension-4. They performed a Bayesian joint analysis of nuclear matter properties (binding energy, pressure, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and slope of symmetry energy around saturation density) and neutron star mass-radius constraints from astronomical observations, including PSR J0614-3329. The 21-dimensional parameter space was sampled, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation was solved to obtain mass-radius relations for beta-equilibrium nuclear matter with a crust matched to the BPS equation of state below 0.5 n0.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 (reference value used in the analysis)
- Binding energy at saturation: E(n0) = -16.0 +/- 1.0 MeV (empirical constraint)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym(n0) = 30.9 +/- 1.9 MeV (empirical constraint)
- Slope of symmetry energy at saturation: L(n0) = 52.5 +/- 17.5 MeV (empirical constraint)
- Incompressibility at saturation: K(n0) = 230 +/- 30 MeV (empirical constraint)
- Pressure at 1.5 n0: P(1.5 n0) = 3.41 +/- 1.29 MeV/fm^3 (empirical constraint)
- Speed of sound peak occurs at approximately 2-3 times saturation density n0
- Maximum neutron star mass approximately 2 solar masses (2 M_sun)
- Tidal deformability at 1.4 M_sun: Lambda_1.4 = 441.6 for GQHD1 and 320.5 for GQHD2
- GQHD2 BJA score: 8.03 (compared to -4.51 for TM1, -15.3 for NL1, -11 for NL3, 3.8 for FSU-delta6.7, 4.28 for FSUGold)

## Context

This work extends the line of quantum hadrodynamics models (Serot 1984, Serot 1997) by including the a0 meson and all possible dimension-4 interactions, and applies Bayesian joint analysis to simultaneously satisfy nuclear matter constraints from heavy-ion collisions (Danielewicz 2002) and chiral effective field theory (Huth 2021) as well as neutron star observations including PSR J0614-3329 (Mauviard 2025). The finding that a peak in the speed of sound arises naturally from the sigma-omega-rho-a0 coupling without requiring a phase transition to quark matter contradicts arguments that such non-monotonic behavior necessarily signals exotic degrees of freedom (Annala 2019, Altiparmak 2022). The results enable future sequential mass-radius measurements by next-generation facilities to distinguish pure hadronic stars from hybrid stars containing quark matter.
