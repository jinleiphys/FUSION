# Relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics

> In this paper we review recent progress in relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics. We begin with a pedagogical introduction to the topic which takes into account the advances in our understanding of this topic since its inception. We consider both conformal and non-conformal systems and demonstrate how one can implement a realistic equation of state using a quasiparticle approach. We then consider the inclusion of non-spheroidal (non-ellipsoidal) corrections to leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics and present the findings of the resulting second-order viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics framework. We compare the results obtained in both the conformal and non-conformal cases with exact solutions to the Boltzmann equation and demonstrate that, in all known cases, anisotropic hydrodynamics best reproduces the exact solutions. Based on this success, we then discuss the phenomenological application of anisotropic hydrodynamics. Along these lines, we review techniques which can be used to convert a momentum-space anisotropic fluid into hadronic degrees of freedom by generalizing the original idea of Cooper-Frye freeze-out to momentum-space anisotropic systems. And, finally, we present phenomenological results of 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamic simulations and compare them to experimental data produced in 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. Our results indicate that anisotropic hydrodynamics provides a promising framework for describing the dynamics of the momentum-space anisotropic QGP created in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) as a framework that, in all known cases, best reproduces exact solutions to the Boltzmann equation compared to standard viscous hydrodynamics, but this claim is qualified by the fact that the comparisons are limited to conformal and non-conformal systems where exact solutions exist, and the framework's phenomenological success depends on the specific implementation of the equation of state (quasiparticle vs. standard approach).

## Method

The authors present a pedagogical review of relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics, beginning with leading-order formulations for conformal and non-conformal systems, then extending to second-order viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics with non-spheroidal corrections. They implement a realistic equation of state from lattice QCD using a quasiparticle approach with temperature-dependent masses and a background field for thermodynamic consistency, and derive dynamical equations from moments of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA). The framework is applied to 3+1d simulations of Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, with hadronization performed via a generalized Cooper-Frye freeze-out procedure adapted for momentum-space anisotropic systems.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on relativistic hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions, building on prior developments in anisotropic hydrodynamics by the same group (Alqahtani 2015) and others (Tinti 2015, Molnar 2016). It contrasts the quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) approach with the "standard aHydro" method (Nopoush 2015) which implements the equation of state only approximately, showing that the consistent treatment of conformal symmetry breaking in aHydroQP leads to substantially different bulk pressure evolution and particle spectra. The paper also presents the anisotropic non-equilibrium attractor (Strickland 2017) and compares it with attractors from DNMR and Mueller-Israel-Stewart frameworks, showing that aHydro provides the best approximation to the exact Boltzmann equation solution. This work enables more realistic phenomenological simulations of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC.
