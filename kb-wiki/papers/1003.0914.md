# The Heavy Quark Free Energy in QCD and in Gauge Theories with Gravity Duals

> We show that the Polyakov loop approaches its asymptotic value at high temperatures {\it from below} in any strongly-coupled gauge theory at large $N_c$ with a dual supergravity description. In these theories the Polyakov loop can be used to define a quantity that behaves as a free energy of a heavy quark in the medium. This should be compared to the recent lattice results in pure glue SU(3) theory at high temperatures where the Polyakov loop approaches its non-interacting limit {\it from above}. We show that the "heavy quark free energy" obtained from the renormalized loop computed on the lattice does not behave as a true thermodynamic free energy. We conjecture that this should always be the case in asymptotically free gauge theories.

## Key claim
The Polyakov loop approaches its asymptotic value at high temperatures *from below* in any strongly-coupled gauge theory at large \(N_c\) with a dual supergravity description, whereas in asymptotically free gauge theories like QCD the loop approaches its limit *from above* after overshooting unity. The "heavy quark free energy" derived from the renormalized Polyakov loop on the lattice does not behave as a true thermodynamic free energy, and the author conjectures this is always the case in asymptotically free gauge theories.

## Method
The author analyzes the temperature dependence of the Polyakov loop \(\ell(T)\) in two classes of gauge theories: (1) asymptotically free theories such as pure-glue \(SU(3)\) QCD, using existing lattice data and perturbative hard-thermal-loop arguments, and (2) strongly-coupled large-\(N_c\) gauge theories with supergravity duals, using the gauge/string duality to compute \(\ell(T)\) from the Nambu-Goto action of a fundamental string in a black-brane background. For the supergravity case, a high-temperature expansion of the heavy quark internal energy \(U_Q\) and specific heat \(C_Q\) is performed, imposing positivity of \(C_Q\) to derive constraints on the scaling dimension \(\Delta\) of the scalar field deforming the conformal field theory and on the coupling function \(q(\phi)\) between the string and the scalar.

## Key numbers
- In pure-glue \(SU(3)\) lattice simulations, the Polyakov loop overshoots unity around \(3T_c\) and reaches a maximum near \(10T_c\) before decreasing toward a constant value close to one (Gupta 2007).
- The high-temperature expansion of the heavy quark entropy \(S_Q\) in supergravity duals is given by \(S_Q(T) = \frac{R^2}{2\alpha'} q(0) \sum_{n=0} s_n(\Delta) d(\Delta)^n (\Lambda/(\pi T))^{(4-\Delta)n}\), where \(d(\Delta) = \Gamma(\Delta/4)^2 / [(\Delta-2)\Gamma(\Delta/2-1)]\).
- For the internal energy \(U_Q\) to remain positive at high \(T\) in supergravity duals, the scaling dimension must satisfy \(\Delta \geq 3\) if \(s_1(\Delta) \neq 0\), or \(\Delta > 3.5\) if \(s_1(\Delta)=0\).
- The asymptotic value of the Polyakov loop at the UV fixed point is \(\lim_{\Lambda/T \to 0} \ell(T) = \exp\left( \frac{R^2}{2\alpha'} q(0) \right)\), approached from below when the derived conditions are satisfied.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of the Polyakov loop's physical interpretation initiated by Pisarski (2002) and Dumitru (2005), who argued that the quantity \(F_Q = -T \ln \ell\) is not a true free energy. The paper contrasts lattice results for pure-glue \(SU(3)\) by Kaczmarek (2002) and Gupta (2007) with predictions from gauge/string duality (Maldacena 1998, Witten 1998). The analysis of supergravity duals builds on the high-temperature expansion framework of Cherman (2009) and Yarom (2009) for transport coefficients, and on the holographic computation of the Polyakov loop by Noronha (2009). The derived constraints on the scalar scaling dimension \(\Delta\) and coupling function \(q(\phi)\) provide consistency conditions for constructing effective holographic models of the quark-gluon plasma, relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
