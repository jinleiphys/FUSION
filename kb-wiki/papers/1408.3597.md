# Properties of the Faddeev-Popov operator in the Landau gauge, matter confinement and soft BRST breaking

> In light of the development of the Gribov issue for pure Euclidean gauge theories and of the recent lattice measurement of soft breaking of the BRST invariance in Yang-Mills theories in the Landau gauge, we consider non-perturbative features in the gauge-interacting matter sector and their relation with general properties of the Faddeev-Popov operator. A signature for BRST breaking in the matter sector is proposed and a local and renormalizable framework is constructed, accommodating this signature and predicting non-perturbative matter propagators that are consistent with available lattice data for adjoint scalars and quarks.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a non-vanishing correlation function involving the inverse Faddeev-Popov operator and matter fields signals soft BRST breaking in the matter sector, and that this correlation function behaves as 1/k^4 in the deep infrared, provided the matter propagator has a finite zero-momentum value and the ghost propagator is non-enhanced.

## Method

The authors generalize the operator R, previously defined for gauge fields as a convolution of the inverse Faddeev-Popov operator with the gauge field, to matter fields (scalars and quarks) in a given representation of SU(N). They construct a local and renormalizable action by adding a non-local term analogous to the horizon term to the conventional matter action, then localize it through auxiliary fields. The resulting correlation function ⟨R R⟩ is analyzed via cluster decomposition, relating its infrared behavior to the product of the squared ghost propagator and the matter propagator.

## Key numbers

- The unrenormalized adjoint scalar propagator at zero momentum takes values D(p=0) ≈ 0.028, 0.027, and 0.0073 GeV^{-2} for bare scalar masses m_bare = 0, 1, and 10 GeV, respectively.
- The renormalized adjoint scalar propagator at zero momentum takes values D_ren(p=0) ≈ 26.7, 0.94, and 0.01 GeV^{-2} for bare scalar masses m_bare = 0, 1, and 10 GeV, respectively.
- The correlation function ⟨R R⟩ in the deep infrared behaves as 1/k^4, following from a non-enhanced ghost propagator (∼ 1/k^2) and a finite zero-momentum matter propagator.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger framework, previously developed for pure Yang-Mills theories by Gribov (1978) and Zwanziger (1988-1992), and its refined version (RGZ) by Dudal et al. (2007-2011), to the matter sector. It builds on the lattice measurement of soft BRST breaking in pure gauge theories by Cucchieri et al. (2014) and proposes that the same phenomenon occurs for matter fields. The predicted matter propagators are consistent with lattice data for adjoint scalars from Maas (2010-2011) and for quarks from Furui (2006) and Parappilly (2005). This enables future direct lattice measurements of the proposed correlation function as a signature of BRST breaking in the matter sector.
