# Effects of spinor distortion and density-dependent form factors upon $^{16}O(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})$

> We propose an effective current operator for nucleon electromagnetic knockout that incorporates spinor distortion and density-dependent nucleon form factors using an effective momentum approximation. This method can be used in a coordinate-space approach with either relativistic or nonrelativistic optical potentials and overlap functions. We studied these effects for the $^{16}O(\vec{e},e' \vec{p})$ reaction at Q^2 = 0.8 (GeV/c)^2. Spinor distortion substantially enhances the left-right asymmetry while reducing the ratio between sideways and longitudinal recoil polarization for p-shell knockout by about 5% for modest missing momenta. We also find that the density dependence of nucleon form factors suggested by a quark-meson coupling model reduces the polarization ratio further. Much larger effects are obtained for the s-shell than for p-shell. However, both effects are subject to much larger Gordon ambiguities than comparable nonrelativistic calculations.

## Key claim

Spinor distortion from Dirac scalar and vector potentials significantly modifies recoil polarization observables for quasifree nucleon knockout, but these effects are subject to much larger Gordon ambiguities than comparable nonrelativistic calculations, making it difficult to unambiguously extract medium-modified nucleon form factors.

## Method

The author developed an effective current operator that incorporates spinor distortion and density-dependent nucleon form factors using an effective momentum approximation in a coordinate-space approach. Calculations were performed for the 16O(e,e'p) reaction at Q2 = 0.8 (GeV/c)2 using Dirac optical potentials (EDAD1) and Dirac-Hartree wave functions, with the current evaluated using three different vertex functions (Gamma1, Gamma2, Gamma3) related by the Gordon identity. Density-dependent form factors from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model were evaluated at the local nuclear density and incorporated into the coordinate-space matrix element.

## Key numbers

- Q2 = 0.8 (GeV/c)2 for the 16O(e,e'p) reaction studied
- Beam energy was 2.445 GeV with omega = 445 MeV and q = 1000 MeV
- Spinor distortion reduces the polarization ratio rxz = P'x/P'z for p-shell knockout by about 5% with a ±5% Gordon ambiguity at missing momenta of 85 and 140 MeV/c
- For p-shell knockout at pm ≈ 85 MeV/c, density-dependent form factors produce an additional reduction factor of about 0.92 in rxz, giving a net reduction of about 0.88 relative to standard nonrelativistic DWIA
- For s-shell knockout at pm ≈ 85 MeV/c, the net suppression of rxz is about 0.6, and at 140 MeV/c about 0.25
- The Darwin nonlocality factor S-V/2m ≈ -0.4 for the bound state in the interior

## Context

This work extends the effective momentum approximation approach of Kelly (1999) to include spinor distortion effects first investigated by Picklesimer, van Orden, and Wallace (1985, 1989) using momentum-space methods, and by Hedayati-Poor et al. (1995) who demonstrated the importance of potentials in the effective current operator. The density-dependent form factors are based on the quark-meson coupling model calculations of Lu et al. (1998). The results suggest that upcoming experiments on 4He(e,e'p) should be able to distinguish density dependence from relativistic effects by measuring both the left-right asymmetry ALT and the polarization ratio rxz symmetrically about quasifree kinematics.
