# High-spin states in boson models with applications to actinide nuclei

> We use the 1/$N$ expansion formalism in a systematic study of high-spin states in the $sd$ and $sdg$ boson models with emphasis on spin dependence of moment of inertia and E2 transitions. The results are applied to the high-spin states in the actinide nuclei $^{232}$Th, $^{234-238}$U, where the need for $g$ bosons is especially acute but until now, no realistic calculation existed. We find that the $d$-boson energy plays a crucial role in description of the high-spin data.

## Key claim

The perceived problems with the sd-IBM in describing spin-dependent quantities (moment of inertia and E2 transitions) are not due to the lack of higher-spin bosons but rather to the energy surface not being soft enough; inclusion of the d-boson energy, together with extension to sdg space, can resolve these problems, while g bosons alone play a marginal role in ground band dynamics.

## Method

The authors used the 1/N expansion formalism with angular momentum projected mean field theory to compute ground band energies and E2 transition matrix elements in both the sd and sdg interacting boson models. They derived energy expressions up to order \(\bar L^3/N^6\) and applied variation after projection to determine mean fields, then systematically studied the spin dependence of moment of inertia and boson cutoff effects as functions of the quadrupole parameter \(q\) and boson energy parameters \(\eta_d\) and \(\eta_g\). Fits were performed to the ground band energies and E2 transitions of the actinide nuclei \(^{232}\)Th, \(^{234-238}\)U.

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(E_\gamma/N e_1\) is around 4–5 in the rare-earth region and increases to 8–10 in the actinides.
- The ratio \(N^2 e_2/e_1\) ranges from about –0.2 in the rare-earth region to –0.1 in the actinides.
- The ratio \(N^4 e_3/e_1\) in the actinides is about 0.01.
- For the sdg model with realistic \(\eta_g\) values (range 3–6), the reduction of the boson cutoff ratio \(N^2 m_2/m_1\) from the sd-IBM result (with \(\eta_d=0\)) is less than 40\%.
- Ground band energies for \(^{232}\)Th, \(^{234-238}\)U are described with relative errors of about 1\%.

## Context

This work extends the 1/N expansion formalism (Kuyucak 1988, 1995) to systematically compare sd and sdg boson models for high-spin states, addressing criticisms by Bohr and Mottelson (1982) regarding the moment of inertia problem in the sd-IBM. It contradicts earlier assumptions that g bosons alone would resolve the boson cutoff and moment of inertia issues (Raj 1982, Wu 1982, Akiyama 1987), and shows that the d-boson energy is crucial. The results enable realistic IBM calculations for actinide nuclei where previously only the SU(3) limit (Casten 1988) or truncated diagonalizations were available, and open the way for applications to rare-earth nuclei in a longer paper (Kuyucak 1996).
