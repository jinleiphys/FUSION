# A systematic study of the initial state in heavy ion collisions based on the quark participant assumption

> We investigate the initial state geometric quantities of heavy ion collisions based on the quark participant assumption in the Glauber multiple scattering approach. A systematic comparison to the nucleon participant assumption has been presented and confronted with the charged multiplicity measurements in various collision systems. It is found that the quark participant based assumption can be important to understand the data in multiplicity production and the initial spatial eccentricity in small systems.

# A systematic study of the initial state in heavy ion collisions based on the quark participant assumption

## Key claim

The quark participant assumption in the Glauber model can better describe charged multiplicity data across different collision systems than the standard nucleon participant assumption, particularly for small systems, but the improvement is most evident at high collision energies and the quark picture does not fully resolve discrepancies at lower energies.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Glauber calculations where nucleon positions were sampled from a three-parameter Fermi distribution, then three quarks per nucleon were sampled using an empirical radial distribution derived from the proton form factor. Inelastic collisions between constituents were determined by a distance criterion based on the energy-dependent elementary cross section, with the quark-quark cross section calibrated to reproduce the known nucleon-nucleon cross section. The resulting numbers of participants and binary collisions were compared between the quark and nucleon frameworks and confronted with experimental charged multiplicity data from pp, pA, and AA collisions at various energies.

## Key numbers

- The quark-quark inelastic cross section ranges from 5 to 18 mb as the nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section varies from 30 to 70 mb over the energy range studied.
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the number of participants N_part scales with the nuclear mass number A for both nucleon and quark participants in minimum bias events.
- The geometric scaling N_coll proportional to N_part^(4/3) holds for both nucleon and quark participants in CuCu and AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- In pp collisions, the ratio of quark participant number to binary collision number approaches two at very small sigma_qq_inel and becomes less than two for large sigma_qq_inel.
- The double ratio of pp-normalized N_part between quark and nucleon pictures shows enhancement for larger collision systems, converging to unity in very peripheral collisions.
- The double ratio of pp-normalized N_coll shows a 30% deviation from unity in peripheral collisions for all systems at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- Participant eccentricity in pPb and pAu collisions shows around 60% deviation from unity in the most central collisions when comparing quark to nucleon participants.
- The RMS charge radius of the proton used in the quark distribution is r_m = 0.81 fm, giving a = 4.27 fm^(-1).

## Context

This work extends the wounded quark concept originally proposed by Bialas (1977) and applied by Eremin (2003) and others to charged hadron density and multiplicity studies. It systematically compares the quark participant assumption to the standard wounded nucleon model of Bialas (1976) across multiple collision systems and energies. The results enable improved modeling of initial state fluctuations in small systems such as pPb and pAu, which is relevant for extracting shear viscosity from hydrodynamic calculations as discussed by Noronha-Hostler (2015) and Yan (2014), and may help explain elliptic anisotropies observed in high-multiplicity pp collisions by ATLAS (2015).
