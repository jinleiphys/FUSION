# Simple functional forms for total cross sections from neutron-nucleus collisions

> Neutron scattering total cross sections have been estimated from nuclei ranging in mass 6 to 238 and for neutron energies 10 MeV to 600 MeV using a simple function of three parameters. These total cross sections have also been calculated using coordinate space optical potentials formed by full folding effective two-nucleon (NN) interactions with one body density matrix elements (OBDME) of the nuclear ground states. Adjusting the theoretically defined parameter values has enabled us to fit the actual measured data. The simple functional parameter values vary smoothly with mass and energy.

## Key claim

Neutron-nucleus total scattering cross sections for energies from 10 MeV to 600 MeV and target masses from 6 to 238 can be reproduced by a simple three-parameter function for partial total cross sections, with the parameters varying smoothly with energy and mass; however, the parameter values are initially determined by fitting to partial cross sections from g-folding optical model calculations, and then adjusted to match experimental data, meaning the functional form is not derived from first principles but rather provides a convenient interpolation tool.

## Method

The authors used a three-parameter function (Eq. Fnform) to describe partial total cross sections for neutron scattering, where the parameters are the cutoff angular momentum l0, the diffuseness a, and a correction epsilon. They first calculated partial total cross sections using coordinate-space optical potentials formed by full folding effective two-nucleon interactions with one-body density matrix elements of nuclear ground states for nine nuclei (Li-6, C-12, F-19, Ca-40, Y-89, W-184, Au-197, Pb-208, U-238). The parameters a and epsilon were then expressed as parabolic functions of energy (Eq. Eps) with no mass dependence, while l0 values were adjusted to fit experimental total cross-section data from Abfalterer et al. (2001) and Finlay et al. (1993).

## Key numbers

- Energy range considered: 10 MeV to 600 MeV for neutron scattering
- Target mass range: from mass 6 (Li-6) to mass 238 (U-238)
- Parameter a expressed as: a = 1.29 + 0.00250 E - 1.76 x 10^-6 E^2, where E is in MeV
- Parameter epsilon expressed as: epsilon = -1.47 - 0.00234 E + 4.16 x 10^-6 E^2, where E is in MeV
- For Pb-208, the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model (SKM*) gave a neutron skin thickness of 0.17 fm

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to proton total reaction cross sections by Amos (2002) and Deb (2003) to neutron total scattering cross sections. The functional form reproduces experimental data more accurately than the g-folding optical potential calculations, which fail for energies above 300 MeV and show systematic deviations at lower energies for many nuclei. The results enable prediction of neutron total cross sections for nuclei and energies not yet measured by interpolation on a parameter database, which is needed for applications in astrophysics, nuclear waste disposal, and medical radiation therapy.
