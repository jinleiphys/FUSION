# Multi chiral-doublets in one single nucleus

> Adiabatic and configuration-fixed constraint triaxial relativistic mean field (RMF) approaches are developed for the first time and a new phenomenon, the existence of multi chiral-doublets (M$χ$D), i.e., more than one pairs of chiral doublets bands in one single nucleus, is suggested for nuclei in A~100 region, typically for $^{106}$Rh, based on the triaxial deformations together with their corresponding proton and neutron configurations.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that more than one pair of chiral doublet bands (multi-chiral-doublets, MχD) can exist in a single nucleus, specifically in ¹⁰⁶Rh, based on triaxial deformations and their corresponding proton and neutron configurations; the claim is conditional on the existence of multiple shape-coexisting states with suitable high-j particle and hole configurations.

## Method

Adiabatic and configuration-fixed constraint triaxial relativistic mean field (RMF) approaches were developed and applied to ¹⁰⁶Rh. The configuration-fixed method constrains the occupation of single-particle orbits during the constraint calculation, yielding smooth energy surfaces as a function of deformation β and revealing local minima. The proton and neutron configurations of these minima were then extracted from single-particle level diagrams to identify high-j proton holes and high-j neutron particles required for chirality.

## Key numbers

- Ground state (state A) of ¹⁰⁶Rh: binding energy E = 903.92 MeV (experimental data 906.72 MeV), deformation β = 0.27, γ = 24.7°.
- Excited state B: E = 903.82 MeV, β = 0.25, γ = 23.3°.
- Excited state C: E = 903.28 MeV, β = 0.30, γ = 22.9°.
- Excited state D: E = 902.69 MeV, β = 0.22, γ = 30.8°.
- All four states (A, B, C, D) lie within 1.3 MeV of each other.
- The RMF calculation used a spherical basis with 12 major oscillator shells for fermions and 10 shells for bosons, giving an error less than 0.1% for the binding energy.
- The oscillator frequency used was ħω₀ = 41 A⁻¹/³ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the concept of nuclear chirality, first suggested by Frauendorf (1997) and experimentally observed by Starosta (2001), from single chiral doublet bands to multiple such bands in one nucleus. It builds on prior particle-rotor model (PRM) and tilted axis cranking (TAC) approaches by Frauendorf (1997) and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock cranking approach by Olbratowski (2004), but introduces a self-consistent microscopic RMF framework that can handle multi-particle and multi-hole configurations. The prediction of MχD in ¹⁰⁶Rh, where one pair of chiral doublet bands had already been observed by Joshi (2004), opens the possibility of searching for additional chiral doublet bands in this nucleus and in other isotopes such as ¹⁰²,¹⁰⁸,¹¹⁰Rh, ¹⁰⁸-¹¹²Ag, and ¹¹²In.
