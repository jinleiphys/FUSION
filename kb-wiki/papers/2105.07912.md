# Azimuthal dependence of two-particle transverse momentum current correlations

> Two-particle transverse momentum correlation functions are a powerful technique for understanding the dynamics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Among these, the transverse momentum correlator $G_{2}\left(Δη,Δφ\right)$ is of particular interest for its potential sensitivity to the shear viscosity per unit of entropy density $η/s$ of the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions. We use the UrQMD, AMPT, and EPOS models for Au--Au at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200~GeV and Pb--Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2760~GeV to investigate the long range azimuthal dependence of $G_{2}\left(Δη,Δφ\right)$, and explore its utility to constrain $η/s$ based on charged particle correlations. We find that the three models yield quantitatively distinct transverse momentum Fourier harmonics coefficients $a^{p_{\rm T}}_{\rm n}$. We also observe these coefficients exhibit a significant dependence on $η/s$ in the context of the AMPT model. These observations suggest that exhaustive measurements of the dependence of $G_{2}\left(Δφ\right)$ with collision energy, system size, collision centrality, in particular, offer the potential to distinguish between different theoretical models and their underlying assumptions. Exhaustive analyses of $G_{2}\left(Δφ\right)$ obtained in large and small systems should also be instrumental in establishing new constraints for precise extraction of $η/s$.

## Key claim

The azimuthal dependence of the two-particle transverse momentum correlator \(G_{2}(\Delta\varphi)\) and its Fourier harmonics \(a^{p_{\rm T}}_{\rm n}\) show quantitative differences between the UrQMD, AMPT, and EPOS models, and exhibit significant sensitivity to the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) within the AMPT framework, suggesting that detailed measurements of \(G_{2}(\Delta\varphi)\) could provide discriminatory power between theoretical models and help constrain \(\eta/s\) extraction, but only if exhaustive measurements across collision energy, system size, and centrality are performed.

## Method

The authors simulated minimum-bias Au–Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV and Pb–Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2760\) GeV using the UrQMD (versions 3.3 and 3.4), AMPT (v2.26t9b and v1.26t7-v2.26t7), and EPOS event generators. They computed the \(G_{2}(\Delta\eta,\Delta\varphi)\) correlator for charged particles with \(0.2 < p_{\rm T} < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\) and \(|\eta| < 1.0\) (RHIC) or \(|\eta| < 0.8\) (LHC), applied a pseudorapidity gap \(|\Delta\eta| > 0.7\) to suppress short-range correlations, and projected onto \(\Delta\varphi\) to extract Fourier harmonics \(a^{p_{\rm T}}_{\rm n}\) via fits to \(f(\Delta\varphi) = a^{p_{\rm T}}_{0} + 2\sum_{n=1}^{6} A^{p_{\rm T}}_{\rm n} \cos(n\Delta\varphi)\). Flow coefficients \(v_{2}\) and \(v_{3}\) were computed using the two-particle cumulant sub-event method with the same \(\eta\)-gap.

## Key numbers

- For Au–Au at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV, AMPT and EPOS quantitatively reproduce the magnitude and centrality evolution of \(v_{2}\) and \(v_{3}\) reported by STAR, while UrQMD (version 3.3) grossly underestimates both coefficients.
- For Pb–Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2760\) GeV, AMPT best reproduces the ALICE \(v_{2}\) and \(v_{3}\) magnitudes; UrQMD (hybrid version 3.4) overestimates \(v_{n}\) by approximately 25% and EPOS by approximately 30% over the entire centrality range.
- In AMPT simulations of Au–Au at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV, varying \(\eta/s\) from 0.1 to 0.3 (by tuning \(\mu\) from 2.26 to 4.2 fm\(^{-1}\) at fixed \(\alpha_{s} = 0.47\) and \(T_{i} = 378\) MeV) produces a significant dependence of both \(v_{n}\) and \(a^{p_{\rm T}}_{\rm n}\) coefficients on \(\eta/s\).
- The slope \({\rm d}a^{p_{\rm T}}_{2}/{\rm d}q_{2}\) is considerably smaller than the slope \({\rm d}v_{2}/{\rm d}q_{2}\) in 20–30% central Au–Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV, as shown via event-shape engineering using \(q_{2}\) selections.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the \(G_{2}\) correlator's longitudinal width sensitivity to \(\eta/s\) (Gavin 2006, Sharma 2008) and earlier model comparisons of number and transverse momentum correlators \(R_{2}\) and \(P_{2}\) in Pb–Pb collisions at 2760 GeV (Basu 2020). The results confirm that standard anisotropic flow measurements (\(v_{2}\), \(v_{3}\)) alone cannot distinguish between UrQMD, AMPT, and EPOS models, as all three can describe the data with different initial conditions and \(\eta/s\) values. The demonstrated sensitivity of \(G_{2}(\Delta\varphi)\) harmonics to model details and to \(\eta/s\) in AMPT enables future experimental measurements to provide new constraints for precise extraction of \(\eta/s\) and to discriminate between theoretical models, particularly when combined with event-shape engineering techniques.
