# Extending Israel and Stewart hydrodynamics to relativistic superfluids via Carter's multifluid approach

> We construct a relativistic model for bulk viscosity and heat conduction in a superfluid. Building on the principles of Unified Extended Irreversible Thermodynamics, the model is derived from Carter's multifluid approach for a theory with 3 four-currents: particles, entropy, and quasi-particles. Dissipation arises directly from the fact that the quasi-particle four-current is an independent degree of freedom that does not necessarily comove with the entropy. For small deviations from local thermodynamic equilibrium, the model provides an extension of the Israel-Stewart theory to superfluid systems. It can, therefore, be made hyperbolic, causal and stable if the microscopic input is accurate. The non-dissipative limit of the model is the relativistic two-fluid model of Carter, Khalatnikov and Gusakov. The Newtonian limit of the model is an Extended-Irreversible-Thermodynamic extension of Landau's two-fluid model. The model predicts the existence of four bulk viscosity coefficients and accounts for their microscopic origin, providing their exact formulas in terms of the quasi-particle creation rate. Furthermore, when fast oscillations of small amplitude around the equilibrium are considered, the relaxation-time term in the telegraph-type equations for the bulk viscosities accounts directly for their expected dependence on the frequency.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a relativistic model for bulk viscosity and heat conduction in a superfluid by promoting the quasi-particle four-current to an independent degree of freedom, which provides an extension of Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics to superfluid systems for small deviations from local thermodynamic equilibrium.

## Method

The model is derived from Carter's multifluid variational approach using three independent four-currents: particles, entropy, and quasi-particles. The constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor and the canonical momenta are obtained from a generating function Lambda that depends on all local Lorentz scalars formed from the three currents. The dissipative model is then translated into its Israel-Stewart counterpart through a change of variables from currents to dissipative fluxes, with a perturbative expansion near local thermodynamic equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- The model predicts the existence of four bulk viscosity coefficients, with exact formulas provided in terms of the quasi-particle creation rate.
- The non-dissipative limit of the model is the relativistic two-fluid model of Carter, Khalatnikov and Gusakov.
- The Newtonian limit of the model is an Extended-Irreversible-Thermodynamic extension of Landau's two-fluid model.
- The infrared Eckart-type limit of the three-fluid hydrodynamics is the superfluid model of Gusakov (2007).

## Context

This work extends the Israel-Stewart theory (Israel and Stewart, 1979) to superfluid systems by exploiting the connection with Carter's multifluid formalism, which was developed for conductive media like superfluids (Carter, 1989). The model builds on recent advancements regarding heat conduction (Lopez-Monsalvo and Andersson, 2011), bulk viscosity (Gavassino et al., 2020), and multifluid thermodynamics (Gavassino et al., 2021), and enables causal and stable numerical simulations of dissipative superfluid hydrodynamics, particularly for neutron star applications.
