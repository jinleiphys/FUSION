# Unified description of superconductivity in neutron stars

> In this paper, I study the location and symmetry of superconducting protons. Solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations based on the unified Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid equation of state (BCPM EoS) and on the pairing gap calculations by Lim and Holt [1], I find that roughly 500 meters of the liquid core (with isotropic and continuous symmetry) and roughly 100-150 meters of the core-crust interface (with anisotropic symmetry) are superconducting, while the rest of the star is normal. To specify whether the superconducting symmetry is discreet in the pasta phase, I study the coexistence of the saturated nuclear and the pure neutron matter using EoS based on the chiral effective field theory (ChEFT). I find that the maximum pressure at coexistence is $P_{*}\simeq0.5\;{\rm MeV\,fm^{-3}}$. To verify the precision of the coexistence calculations I evaluate the surface and the Coulomb corrections using the compressible liquid drop model. I calculate the proton tunneling rate in the perfectly ordered slab region of the pasta phase and conclude that for the chosen EoS, the proton supercurrent tunneling between the adjacent slabs is negligible and the slab region should be described as a discreet symmetry system of quasi two-dimensional layers.

## Key claim

The paper finds that superconducting protons in neutron stars are confined to a roughly 500-meter liquid core region (with isotropic, continuous symmetry) and a 100-150 meter core-crust interface region (with anisotropic symmetry), while the rest of the star is normal; however, this result depends on the specific choice of the Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid equation of state (BCPM EoS) and the pairing gap calculations by Lim and Holt (2021), and the slab region of the pasta phase should be described as a discreet symmetry system of quasi two-dimensional layers because proton supercurrent tunneling between adjacent slabs is negligible.

## Method

The author solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations using the unified BCPM EoS and three representative chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) based EoS with polytropic extensions (soft, intermediate, stiff) to obtain the density profile of a static, spherically symmetric neutron star. The spatial profile of superconducting matter was then determined by combining the density profile with the proton pairing gap energy as a function of proton Fermi wavenumber from Lim and Holt (2021). To study the symmetry in the pasta phase, the author used the compressible liquid drop model to calculate the coexistence of saturated nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, and computed the proton tunneling rate between adjacent slabs in the perfectly ordered slab region.

## Key numbers

* Roughly 500 meters of the liquid core and roughly 100-150 meters of the core-crust interface are superconducting.
* The maximum pressure at coexistence of saturated nuclear and pure neutron matter is \(P_{*}\simeq0.5\;{\rm MeV\,fm^{-3}}\).
* The reference energy density of saturated nuclear matter is \(\rho_0=2.68\times10^{14}\;{\rm g\;cm^{-3}}\).
* The baryon number density at nuclear saturation is \(n_0=0.16\;{\rm fm}^{-3}\).
* The interaction energy per nucleon at saturation is \(\omega_0=16\;{\rm MeV}\).
* The BCPM EoS predicts slabs in the density range between 0.076 and 0.082 \({\rm fm}^{-3}\).
* The maximum deviation between the ChEFT EoS and the Baym-Bethe-Pethick energy surface is 2.23 MeV at \((n,x)=(1.1n_0,0)\) for parameter set 1, and 4.3 MeV at the same point for parameter set 2.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies by Kobyakov (2018) and Kobyakov and Pethick (2018), who showed that the effective symmetry of superconducting protons at the core-crust boundary might be anisotropic and discreet, and by Lim and Holt (2021), who demonstrated that the superconductor gap energy closes at higher densities. The paper uses the BCPM EoS from Sharma, Centelles, Vinas, Baldo and Burgio (2015) and the ChEFT-based EoS from Hebeler, Lattimer, Pethick and Schwenk (2013), and it enables future work on the role of superconductivity in neutron star magnetism, quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) in magnetar flares, and the force associated with superfluid-superconducting velocity lag in the pasta phases.
