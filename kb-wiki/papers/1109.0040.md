# 4-point correlators in finite-temperature AdS/CFT: jet quenching correlations

> There has been recent progress on computing real-time equilibrium 3-point functions in finite-temperature strongly-coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills (SYM). In this paper, we show an example of how to carry out a similar analysis for a 4-point function. We look at the stopping of high-energy "jets" in such strongly-coupled plasmas and relate the question of whether, on an event-by-event basis, each jet deposits its net charge over a narrow (~ 1/T) or wide (>> 1/T) spatial region. We relate this question to the calculation of a 4-point equilibrium correlator.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, on an event-by-event basis, each high-energy "jet" in strongly-coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma deposits its net charge over a narrow spatial region (~1/T) rather than a wide region (>>1/T), by showing that the suitably-defined charge deposition correlation vanishes for positions separated by much more than 1/T.

## Method

The authors compute a 4-point equilibrium retarded Green function (specifically G_aarr) in the gravity dual of finite-temperature N=4 SYM, using AdS/CFT correspondence with AdS5-Schwarzschild geometry. They analyze Witten diagrams where two bulk vertices are connected by a bulk-to-bulk propagator near the horizon, exploiting the simplification that the near-horizon propagator has a simple analytic form, and using WKB approximations for high-momentum source propagators and hydrodynamic approximations for low-momentum measurement propagators.

## Key numbers

- The R-charge diffusion constant D = 1/(2πT) for N=4 SYM at strong coupling
- The source momentum is chosen with frequency ω >> T (specifically light-like with k^μ = (ω,0,0,ω))
- The envelope function localizes the source to a space-time region of size L, where L should be large compared to 1/ω but small compared to the stopping distance
- The typical stopping distance scales as (EL)^{1/4}/T, where E is the jet energy

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of 3-point functions for jet stopping (Arnold and Vaman, 2011) to 4-point correlators, building on the framework of real-time finite-temperature Green functions in AdS/CFT. The calculation uses the near-horizon bulk-to-bulk propagator formalism developed by Caron-Huot, Chesler, and Teaney (2011). The result confirms the interpretation from earlier work (Arnold and Vaman, 2011) that the average charge deposition distribution represents a probability distribution for individual jet stopping positions, and enables future studies of event-by-event fluctuations in jet quenching at strong coupling.
