# Dissecting the moat regime at low energies I: Renormalization and the phase structure

> Dense QCD matter can feature a moat regime, where the static energy of mesons is minimal at nonzero momentum. Valuable insights into this regime can be gained using low-energy models. This, however, requires a careful assessment of model artifacts. We therefore study the effects of renormalization and in-medium modifications of quark-meson interaction on the moat regime. To capture the main effects, we use a two-flavor quark-meson model at finite temperature and baryon density in the random phase approximation. We put forward a convenient renormalization scheme to account for the nontrivial momentum dependence of meson self-energies and discuss the role of renormalization conditions for renormalization group consistent results on the moat regime. In addition, we demonstrate and that its extent in the phase diagram critically depends on the interaction of quarks and mesons.

## Key claim

The moat regime in dense QCD matter, where meson static energy is minimal at nonzero momentum, is studied using a two-flavor quark-meson model in the random phase approximation; the authors demonstrate that the extent of the moat regime in the phase diagram critically depends on both proper renormalization of momentum-dependent self-energies and on in-medium modifications of the quark-meson Yukawa interaction, and that the appearance of a moat regime at vanishing density found in prior work by Topfel (2024) and Cao (2025) is an approximation artifact.

## Method

The authors employ a two-flavor quark-meson model in (3+1) spacetime dimensions at finite temperature and baryon density, using the random phase approximation (RPA) where quarks are integrated out at one-loop level while meson fluctuations are neglected. They compute the effective potential and the pion two-point function, extracting the spatial pion wave function renormalization at vanishing momentum as the key probe of the moat regime. Dimensional regularization is used, and a renormalization scheme is developed that includes an additional kinetic counterterm for the momentum-dependent part of the meson self-energy, with the renormalization condition that the spatial wave function renormalization equals one in vacuum.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum parameters are fixed to: pion mass 136 MeV, sigma mass 480 MeV, constituent quark mass 300 MeV, and chiral condensate (pion decay constant) 92 MeV, with Yukawa coupling h = 6.5.
- The critical endpoint is located at mu_CEP = 290 MeV and T_CEP = 30 MeV.
- The renormalization scale M is tested at values of 300, 400, and 500 MeV, and the position of the critical endpoint remains unchanged when vacuum observables are fixed.
- The unphysical large-momentum instability in the pion self-energy sets in for momenta above approximately 900 MeV in the moat regime example shown at mu = 400 MeV and T = 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the moat regime in quark-meson models by Topfel (2024) and Cao (2025), who found a moat regime at large temperature even at vanishing density using RPA; the present paper shows that this is an artifact of neglecting in-medium modifications of the quark-meson Yukawa interaction. The study also addresses renormalization scheme dependencies noted in NJL model studies by Pannullo (2023, 2024) and Koenigstein (2023), demonstrating that proper renormalization of the momentum-dependent self-energy removes these ambiguities. The results enable more reliable model studies of inhomogeneous phases and moat regimes in dense QCD matter, and connect to functional QCD approaches by Fu (2019, 2024) where the moat regime was first identified.
