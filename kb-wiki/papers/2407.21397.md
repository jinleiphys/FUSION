# Electromagnetic $|G_E/G_M|$ ratios of hyperons at large timelike $q^2$

> In recent years, it has become possible to measure not only the magnitude of the electric ($G_E$) and magnetic ($G_M$) form factors of spin $\frac{1}{2}$ baryons, but also to measure the relative phases of those quantities in the timelike kinematic region. Aiming to interpret present $|G_E/G_M|$ data on hyperons of the baryon octet, as well as to predict future data, we present model calculations of that ratio for large invariant 4-momentum square $q^2$ in the timelike region ($q^2>0$). Without any further parameter fitting,we extend to the timelike region a covariant quark model previously developed to describe the kinematic spacelike region ($q^2 \le 0$) of the baryon octet form factors. The model takes into account both the effects of valence quarks and the excitations of the meson cloud which dresses the baryons. This application to the timelike region assumes an approximation based on unitarity and analyticity that is valid only in the large $q^2$ region. Using the recent data from BESIII we establish the regime of validity of this approximation. We report here that our results for the effective form factor (combination of $|G_E|$ and $|G_M|$) are in good agreement with the data already for $q^2$ values above 15 GeV$^2$. In addition, a more conservative onset of the validity of the approximation is provided by the newly available $|G_E/G_M|$ data which suggest that our predictions may be compared against data for $q^2 \ge $ 20 GeV$^2$. This is expected in the near future, when the range of the present measurements is expanded to the 20--50 GeV$^2$ region.

# Electromagnetic $|G_E/G_M|$ ratios of hyperons at large timelike $q^2$

## Key claim

The authors extend a covariant quark model, previously fitted only to spacelike data, to predict the ratio $|G_E/G_M|$ for hyperons in the timelike region, using an approximation based on unitarity and analyticity that is strictly valid only in the mathematical limit $q^2 \to +\infty$. The comparison with recent BESIII data suggests the approximation may become reliable for $q^2 \ge 20$ GeV$^2$, rather than the lower onset of 15 GeV$^2$ suggested by effective form factor data alone.

## Method

The covariant spectator quark model, which treats baryons as quark-diquark systems with SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking and includes both valence quark contributions and meson cloud dressing, was previously developed for the spacelike region ($q^2 \le 0$). The authors extend it to the timelike region ($q^2 > 0$) using the asymptotic reflection symmetry relation $G_\ell(q^2) = G_\ell^{\rm SL}(q^2 - 2M_B^2)$, where the shift accounts for the unphysical interval between the photon point and the baryon-antibaryon production threshold. The uncertainty is estimated by varying the symmetry point between $q^2$ and $q^2 - 4M_B^2$, producing a band of predictions.

## Key numbers

- For the $\Lambda$ hyperon, the model shows agreement with $|{\rm Re}(G_E/G_M)|$ data at the largest measured point, $q^2 \simeq 14.3$ GeV$^2$.
- For the $\Sigma^+$ hyperon, the model shows agreement with $|{\rm Re}(G_E/G_M)|$ data at the largest measured point, $q^2 \simeq 8.4$ GeV$^2$.
- The effective form factor $|G(q^2)|$ for $\Lambda$ and $\Xi^-$ shows excellent agreement with data for $q^2 > 15$ GeV$^2$.
- The authors predict that the approximation should be testable against $|G_E/G_M|$ data in the range $q^2 = 20$--50 GeV$^2$, corresponding to $\sqrt{s} = 4.5$--5.5 GeV.
- A rough lower limit for the model's applicability is estimated as $q^2 \approx 10$ GeV$^2$ or $q^2 \approx 8 M_B^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of effective form factors for hyperons (Ramalho et al., 2022) to the ratio $|G_E/G_M|$, using the same covariant spectator quark model that successfully described spacelike baryon octet data (Ramalho et al., 2013). The calculations are motivated by recent BESIII measurements of $|G_E/G_M|$ for $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma^+$ (BESIII, 2019, 2022, 2023), which for the first time allow separation of electric and magnetic form factors in the timelike region. The work enables future comparisons with upcoming BESIII and PANDA data in the 20--50 GeV$^2$ range, and may help determine where the asymptotic reflection symmetry regime sets in for hyperons, which is expected at lower $q^2$ than for the nucleon due to strange quark dynamics.
