# Astrophysics and Nuclear Physics Informed Interactions in Dense Matter: Inclusion of PSR J0437-4715

> We investigate how vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector interactions can be determined within the density regime of neutron stars (NSs), while fulfilling nuclear and astrophysics constrains. We make use of the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, a SU(3) nonlinear realization of the sigma model within the mean-field approximation, for the first time within a Bayesian analysis framework. We show that neutron-matter $χ$EFT constraints at low density are only satisfied if the vector-isovector mixed interaction term is included, e.g., a $ω^2ρ^2$ term. We also show the behavior of the model with respect to the conformal limit. We demonstrate that the CMF model is able to predict a value for the parameter $d_c$ related to the trace anomaly and its derivative takes values below 0.2 above four times saturation density within a hadronic version of the model that does not include hyperons or a phase transition to deconfined matter. We compare these effects with results from other (non-chiral) Relativistic Mean Field models to assess how different approaches to incorporating the same physical constraints affect predictions of NS properties and dense matter equations of state. We also include data from the gravitation wave event GW230529 detected by the LIGO-Virgo-Kagra collaboration and the most recent radius measurement of PSR J0437-4715 from the NASA NICER mission. Our analysis reveals that this new NICER measurement leads to an average reduction of approximately $\sim 0.1$ km radius in the posterior of the NS mass-radius relationship.

## Key claim
The Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model can simultaneously satisfy nuclear physics constraints from chiral effective field theory at low densities and astrophysical observations of neutron stars, but only if a vector-isovector mixed interaction term (omega^2 rho^2) is included; the model predicts that the conformal-limit indicator d_c falls below 0.2 above four times saturation density in a purely hadronic version without hyperons or deconfinement, suggesting that such small d_c values are not unique signatures of quark matter.

## Method
The authors performed Bayesian inference on the nucleonic Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, a SU(3) nonlinear realization of the sigma model in the mean-field approximation, varying the vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector self-interaction couplings (x for omega^2 rho^2, y for rho^4, z for omega^4). They imposed likelihoods from nuclear saturation properties, chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) neutron-matter constraints at four densities, perturbative QCD at seven times saturation density, gravitational-wave data from GW170817 and GW230529, and NICER X-ray mass-radius measurements for PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, and the new PSR J0437-4715 data.

## Key numbers
- The new NICER measurement of PSR J0437-4715 leads to an average reduction of approximately 0.1 km in the posterior of the neutron star mass-radius relationship.
- The natural logarithm of the Bayes factor for model xyz relative to model z is ln K = 6.09, indicating strong evidence against the model with only the omega^4 term.
- The symmetry energy slope at saturation for the favored sets (x, xz, xyz) is approximately 40 MeV, with curvature K_sym0 between -110 and -130 MeV.
- The incompressibility K_0 for all sets lies above 300 MeV, with values between approximately 300 and 350 MeV.
- The quantity d_c (related to the trace anomaly and its derivative) takes values below 0.2 above approximately four times saturation density (4 rho_0) for sets containing the omega^4 term.
- The speed of sound squared approaches values of approximately 0.7 at densities of about seven times saturation density for sets x and y, and approximately 0.4-0.55 for set z.

## Context
This work extends Bayesian analyses of dense matter equations of state (e.g., Malik (2023) using RMF with nonlinear mesonic interactions) by applying the chiral-invariant CMF model for the first time in a Bayesian framework. It demonstrates that the conformal-limit indicator d_c proposed by Annala (2023) as a signature of deconfined quark matter can also reach values below 0.2 in a purely hadronic model, contradicting the uniqueness of that interpretation. The inclusion of the new NICER data for PSR J0437-4715 (Choudhury, 2024) shows slight tension with older observations, suggesting the model may require additional interaction terms or new degrees of freedom.
