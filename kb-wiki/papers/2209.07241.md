# A new way of determining the Lattice QCD equation of state at a finite chemical potential

> The Taylor expansion of thermodynamic observables at a finite baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ is an oft-used method to circumvent the well-known sign problem of Lattice QCD. Owing to the associated difficulty and limitations of precision in calculating these high-ordered Taylor coefficients, it becomes essential to look for various resummation schemes which can mitigate the computational cost, besides providing trustworthy estimates of different thermodynamic observables. Recently, a way to exponentially resum the contribution of the first $N$ charge density correlation functions $D_1,\dots,D_N$ to the Taylor series to all orders in $μ_B$ was proposed in Phys. Rev. Lett. 128, 2, 022001 (2022). Since the correlation functions $D_n$ are calculated stochastically using estimates from different random volume sources, the resummation formulation gets affected by the biased estimates. These estimates can become very drastic and can radically misdirect the calculations for large values of $N$ and $μ$ and also for observables which are higher order $μ$ derivatives of free energy, specially at lower temperatures. In this work, we present a cumulant expansion procedure that allows to investigate and regulate these biased estimates at different orders in $μ$. We find that the unbiased estimates in the cumulant expansion can truly capture the genuine higher-order stochastic fluctuations of the higher order correlation functions, which got suppressed by the exponential resummation formulation. Finally, we discover an unbiased formalism of the exponential resummation, which when expanded in a series, can exactly reproduce the Taylor series upto a desired power in $μ$. We are also able to regain the knowledge of reweighting factor and many other important properties of the partition function, which got entirely lost through the implementation of cumulant expansion scheme.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the exponential resummation of Lattice QCD thermodynamic observables at finite chemical potential suffers from biased stochastic estimates of correlation functions, which can become severe at large chemical potential and low temperatures; the authors propose a cumulant expansion and a new unbiased exponential resummation formalism that reproduces the Taylor series up to a desired order in chemical potential while preserving access to the partition function and reweighting factor.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action for fermions and tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on a 32^3 × 8 lattice with 2+1 flavor QCD at physical quark masses. They collected gauge configurations at T = 135 and 157 MeV (corresponding to β = 6.245 and 6.390) using 20K configurations for both baryon and isospin chemical potentials, calculating correlation functions D_n up to D_8 stochastically with O(500) random volume sources per configuration. They introduced a cumulant expansion procedure to analyze and regulate biased estimates order-by-order in chemical potential, and developed an unbiased exponential resummation formalism in both chemical potential basis and cumulant basis.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 32^3 × 8
- Temperatures studied: T = 135 MeV and T = 157 MeV
- Gauge configurations: 20K for both baryon and isospin chemical potentials
- Random volume sources: O(500) per configuration for calculating correlation functions D_n
- Cumulant expansion truncated at M = 4 cumulants for isospin analysis
- Correlation functions calculated up to D_8 (eight derivatives)

## Context

This work extends the exponential resummation approach proposed in Phys. Rev. Lett. 128, 022001 (2022) by Mondal, Mukherjee, and Hegde, which resums contributions of the first N charge density correlation functions to all orders in chemical potential. The authors identify and address the problem of biased stochastic estimates in that formulation, which becomes severe at large chemical potential and for higher-order derivatives of the free energy. The unbiased exponential resummation formalism presented here enables access to the partition function and reweighting factor that were lost in the cumulant expansion approach, and the authors note that connections to other proposals by Dimopoulos (2021), Borsanyi (2021), Pasztor (2020), and Giordano (2020) remain to be explored.
