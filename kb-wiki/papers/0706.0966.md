# Isospin dependence of incompressibility in relativistic and non-relativistic mean field calculations

> The isospin dependence of incompressibility is investigated in the Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) and relativistic mean field (RMF) models. The correlations between the nuclear matter incompressibility and the isospin dependent term of the finite nucleus incompressibility is elucidated by using the Thomas-Fermi approximation. The Coulomb term is also studied by using various different Skyrme Hamiltonians and RMF Lagrangians. The symmetry energy coefficient of incompressibility is extracted to be K_τ=-(500\pm50) MeV from the recent experimental data of isoscalar giant monopole resonances (ISGMR) in Sn isotopes. Microscopic HF+random phase approximation (RPA) calculations are also performed with Skyrme interactions for ^{208}Pb and Sn isotopes to study the strength distributions of ISGMR. .

# Isospin dependence of incompressibility in relativistic and non-relativistic mean field calculations

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficient of finite-nucleus incompressibility is extracted as K_τ = -(500 ± 50) MeV from experimental ISGMR data in Sn isotopes, which is about 30% larger than the value reported in Umesh (2006) because the present analysis includes the mass-number dependence of the surface and Coulomb terms that were neglected in that earlier work.

## Method

The authors used the Thomas-Fermi approximation to derive analytic expressions for the volume, surface, symmetry, and Coulomb terms of the finite-nucleus incompressibility K_A in both the Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) model (14 parameter sets) and the relativistic mean field (RMF) model (7 parameter sets). They then performed microscopic HF+random phase approximation (RPA) calculations with Skyrme interactions for 208Pb and Sn isotopes (112Sn, 116Sn, 120Sn, 124Sn) to compute ISGMR strength distributions and excitation energies, comparing directly with experimental data from Texas A&M University and RCNP at Osaka University.

## Key numbers

- K_τ = -(500 ± 50) MeV extracted from the isospin dependence of ISGMR excitation energies in Sn isotopes, using the full liquid-drop expansion of K_A including surface and Coulomb terms
- For Skyrme interactions alone, K_τ = (-400 ± 100) MeV across 14 parameter sets
- For RMF Lagrangians alone, K_τ = (-620 ± 180) MeV across 7 parameter sets
- K_Coul = (-5.2 ± 0.7) MeV for Skyrme interactions
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K_∞ ≈ 230 MeV extracted from RPA analysis of 208Pb including two-body spin-orbit and Coulomb corrections (δK_∞ ~ 10 MeV)
- SkM* interaction (K_∞ = 217 MeV) gives E_c = 13.79 MeV for 208Pb, compared to experimental E_c = 14.18 ± 0.11 MeV (Texas A&M)
- For Sn isotopes with SkM*: calculated E_c ranges from 16.9 MeV (112Sn) to 16.1 MeV (124Sn); experimental E_c (Texas A&M) ranges from 15.23 MeV (112Sn) to 14.33 MeV (124Sn)
- K_A values for Sn isotopes from RPA with SkM*: 142.7 MeV (112Sn), 140.2 MeV (116Sn), 133.7 MeV (120Sn), 137.1 MeV (124Sn)

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear matter incompressibility from the well-studied 208Pb case to a systematic analysis of Sn isotopes, building on the liquid-drop expansion approach of Blaizot (1980) and the constrained HF study of Colo (2004). The authors resolve a discrepancy between the K_τ value extracted by Umesh (2006) from RCNP data (which neglected surface and Coulomb mass-number dependence) and the present more complete analysis. The results enable future experimental and theoretical work to resolve remaining differences between the Texas A&M and RCNP datasets for 112Sn and 124Sn, which currently limit the precision of K_τ determination.
