# Relating breakup and incomplete fusion of weakly-bound nuclei through a classical trajectory model with stochastic breakup

> A classical dynamical model that treats break-up stochastically is presented for low energy reactions of weakly-bound nuclei. The three-dimensional model allows a consistent calculation of breakup, incomplete and complete fusion cross sections. The model is assessed by comparing the breakup observables with CDCC quantum mechanical predictions, which are found to be in reasonable agreement. Through the model, it is demonstrated that the breakup probability of the projectile as a function of its distance from the target is of primary importance for understanding complete and incomplete fusion at energies near the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-dimensional classical dynamical model that treats breakup stochastically, enabling for the first time consistent calculations relating below-barrier no-capture breakup (NCBU) with above-barrier complete fusion (CF) and incomplete fusion (ICF); the key hedge is that the model's predictive power depends critically on the local breakup probability function, which must be determined from experimental sub-barrier breakup data or from CDCC calculations.

## Method

The model follows classical trajectories of a weakly-bound two-body projectile incident on a target, with breakup introduced stochastically at a radius sampled from a local breakup probability function. After breakup, the trajectories of the two fragments and the target are solved classically using real central two-body potentials, and events are classified as complete fusion (both fragments captured), incomplete fusion (one fragment captured), or no-capture breakup (no fragments captured). The model was validated by comparing its no-capture breakup observables with fully quantum mechanical CDCC calculations for a simplified test case of pseudo-8Be (two alpha particles bound by 0.5 MeV) incident on 208Pb at 45 MeV laboratory energy.

## Key numbers

- The CDCC-derived breakup probability function parameters: A = 1.38, α = 0.293 fm⁻¹
- The empirical breakup probability function from 9Be+208Pb sub-barrier data: A = 1.68×10⁴, α = 0.922 fm⁻¹
- CDCC total absorption cross section: 327 mb; classical model total absorption cross section: 300 mb
- CDCC total NCBU cross section: 97 mb; classical model total NCBU cross section: 93 mb
- The α-target fusion radius: R_B^(αT) = 10.6 fm
- The projectile separation energy: 0.5 MeV
- The laboratory energy for the test case: E₀ = 45 MeV
- Maximum projectile orbital angular momentum for convergence: L₀ ≤ 100 ħ
- Maximum α-α relative energy in CDCC model space: 12 MeV
- Maximum α-α relative angular momentum: 4 ħ
- Gaussian distribution of fragment separation d₁₂: centroid 1.8 fm, variance 1.0 fm

## Context

This work extends the two-dimensional classical model of Hagino (2000) by introducing a three-dimensional treatment with stochastic breakup, and addresses the limitation of quantum mechanical CDCC methods (Hagino, 1999; Alexis, 2005) which cannot separate complete and incomplete fusion contributions. The model enables quantitative predictions of CF and ICF yields for weakly-bound projectiles, and demonstrates that the breakup probability function, accessible from sub-barrier experiments (Hinde, 2002), is the key link between below-barrier breakup and above-barrier fusion suppression observed for 9Be and 6,7Li (Dasgupta, 2002).
