# Charge fluctuations and electric mass in a hot meson gas

> Net-Charge fluctuations in a hadron gas are studied using an effective hadronic interaction. The emphasis of this work is to investigate the corrections of hadronic interactions to the charge fluctuations of a non-interacting resonance gas. Several methods, such as loop, density and virial expansions are employed. The calculations are also extended to SU(3) and some resummation schemes are considered. Although the various corrections are sizable individually, they cancel to a large extent. As a consequence we find that charge fluctuations are rather well described by the free resonance gas.

## Key claim

Although individual hadronic interaction corrections to charge fluctuations are sizable, they cancel to a large extent, so that charge fluctuations in a hot meson gas are rather well described by the free resonance gas model, but this conclusion is based on a model with pions and rho mesons only, and the authors note that a complete calculation will most likely come from lattice QCD once numerically feasible.

## Method

The authors compute the charge susceptibility and electric screening mass in a hot pion gas using an effective hadronic interaction based on the hidden gauge approach, treating the rho meson as a massive gauge field. They evaluate corrections to the free resonance gas result at order e²g² using several methods: thermal loop diagrams (photon selfenergy at two-loop order), density expansions via the partition function at finite chemical potential, and a relativistic virial expansion using ππ phase shifts. The calculations are extended to SU(3) with strange degrees of freedom, and resummation schemes are considered.

## Key numbers

- For free massive pions at T = 170 MeV, D_S = 0.185; for massless pions, D_S = 0.253
- For free massive pions at T = 170 MeV, D_c = 4.52; for massless pions, D_c = 5.47
- In the classical (Boltzmann) limit at T = 170 MeV, D_S = 0.156 for massive pions and D_S = 1/6 for massless pions; D_c = 4 for all masses and temperatures
- For a QGP of massless quarks and gluons, D_S = 0.034, about a factor of five smaller than a pion gas
- The electric mass from the heavy rho limit and from dynamical rho diagrams coincide at low temperatures but show significant differences above T ~ 150 MeV
- At T = 170 MeV, the values for the electric mass of the free pion gas change by 20% when switching from Bose-Einstein to Boltzmann statistics; the two-loop diagrams change by 38%

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of net charge fluctuations in a hadron gas by Jeon and Koch (2000) and Asakawa and Heinz (2000), which were based on noninteracting particles and resonances. It also addresses the calculation of the electric screening mass by Eletsky, Ioffe, and Kogan (1993), who used chiral ππ interaction and thermal loops but found large theoretical uncertainties because thermal loops pick up energies in the resonance region where chiral perturbation theory is no longer valid. The present study provides a rough estimate of interaction effects using rho meson exchange, and the results suggest that the free resonance gas model remains a good approximation for charge fluctuations, enabling simpler phenomenological comparisons with experimental data from RHIC and LHC.
