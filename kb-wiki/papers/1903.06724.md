# Vortices in low-density neutron matter and cold Fermi gases

> Cold gas experiments can be tuned to achieve strongly-interacting regimes such as that of low-density neutron matter found in neutron-stars' crusts. We report $T$=0 diffusion Monte Carlo results (i) for the ground state of both spin-1/2 fermions with short-range interactions and low-density neutron matter in a cylindrical container, and (ii) properties of these systems with a vortex line excitation. We calculate the equation of state for cold atoms and low-density neutron matter in the bulk systems, and we contrast it to our results in the cylindrical container. We compute the vortex line excitation energy for different interaction strengths, and we find agreement between cold gases and neutron matter for very low densities. We also calculate density profiles, which allow us to determine the density depletion at the vortex core, which depends strongly on the short-ranged interaction in cold atomic gases, but it is of $\approx$ 25% for neutron matter in the density regimes studied in this work. Our results can be used to constrain neutron matter properties by using measurements from cold Fermi gases experiments.

## Key claim

The authors report that for very low densities (specifically at interaction strength $-k_Fa=1$), the vortex line excitation energy for cold Fermi gases and low-density neutron matter is comparable, but as density or interaction strength increases, the excitation energies diverge, with cold gases showing higher excitation energies than neutron matter.

## Method

The authors performed fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) simulations at zero temperature for spin-1/2 fermions with short-range interactions in a cylindrical container with hard walls and periodic boundary conditions along the axial direction. They used a modified Poschl-Teller potential for cold gases (with effective range fixed at $k_F r_e=0.05$) and two different potentials for neutron matter: the $s$-wave part of the AV18 interaction and a modified Poschl-Teller potential tuned to reproduce the neutron-neutron scattering length $a^{nn}=-18.5$ fm and effective range $r_e^{nn}=2.7$ fm. The vortex line excitation was created by coupling single-particle states with angular quantum numbers differing by one, and the excitation energy was computed as the energy difference between the vortex state and the ground state in the cylinder.

## Key numbers

* Bulk energy per particle for cold gases at $-k_Fa=1.0$: $0.7864(2) E_{FG}$ (free Fermi gas energy units)
* Bulk energy per particle for neutron matter using $s$-wave AV18 at $-k_Fa=1.0$: $0.814(5) E_{FG}$
* Bulk energy per particle for neutron matter using modified Poschl-Teller at $-k_Fa=1.0$: $0.821(4) E_{FG}$
* Bulk energy per particle for cold gases at $-k_Fa=5.0$: $0.5407(2) E_{FG}$
* Bulk energy per particle for neutron matter using $s$-wave AV18 at $-k_Fa=5.0$: $0.598(2) E_{FG}$
* Density depletion at the vortex core for neutron matter: approximately 25% for the density regimes studied
* Density depletion at the vortex core for cold gases: varies from 28% up to 47% depending on interaction strength
* Surface parameter $\lambda_S$ for cold gases at $-k_Fa=1.0$: $99(7) E_{FG} k_F^{-2}$
* Surface parameter $\lambda_S$ for neutron matter ($s$-wave AV18) at $-k_Fa=1.0$: $-78(9) E_{FG} k_F^{-2}$

## Context

This work extends previous quantum Monte Carlo comparisons between cold atom systems and low-density neutron matter by Gezerlis and Carlson (2008, 2010), which focused on the equation of state and pairing gap, to now include vortex properties. The results enable using measurements from cold Fermi gas experiments to constrain neutron matter properties in neutron-star crusts, where the density is low enough that the effective range of the neutron-neutron interaction becomes negligible compared to the interparticle distance. The authors compare their results to previous mean-field calculations using Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations by Bulgac and Yu (2003) and by Sensarma et al. (2006) for cold atoms, and by Elgaroy et al. (2001) for neutron matter.
