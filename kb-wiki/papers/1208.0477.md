# Magnetic moments of the low-lying $J^P=\,1/2^-$, $3/2^-$ $Λ$ resonances within the framework of the chiral quark model

> The magnetic moments of the low-lying spin-parity $J^P=$ $1/2^-$, $3/2^-$ $Λ$ resonances, like, for example, $Λ(1405)$ $1/2^-$, $Λ(1520)$ $3/2^-$, as well as their transition magnetic moments, are calculated using the chiral quark model. The results found are compared with those obtained from the nonrelativistic quark model and those of unitary chiral theories, where some of these states are generated through the dynamics of two hadron coupled channels and their unitarization.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of low-lying negative-parity Λ resonances are calculated within the chiral quark model, which includes contributions from valence quarks, sea quarks generated by Goldstone boson emission, and orbital angular momentum of the quark sea; however, the results differ significantly from those obtained in unitary chiral theories, indicating that the chiral quark model, while incorporating meson cloud effects, does not reproduce the same quantitative values as approaches based on hadron dynamics and unitarization.

## Method

The authors first construct wave functions for the Λ resonances as linear combinations of SU(6) quark model states (²8, ⁴8, ²1) using coefficients from Isgur and Karl (1978). They then compute magnetic moments within the nonrelativistic SU(6) quark model from spin and orbital contributions of valence quarks. In the chiral quark model, they additionally include sea quark spin contributions from Goldstone boson emission (π, K, η, η′) and the orbital angular momentum of the quark sea (Cheng-Li mechanism), using fixed parameters a=0.12, α=β=0.45, ζ=-0.15 and quark masses Mu=Md=330 MeV, Ms=510 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moment of Λ(1405) 1/2⁻: +0.081 μN in the chiral quark model, compared to +(0.2-0.5) μN from unitary chiral theories (Jido et al., 2003)
- Magnetic moment of Λ(1670) 1/2⁻: -0.575 μN in the chiral quark model, compared to approximately -0.29 μN from unitary chiral theories
- Magnetic moment of Λ(1520) 3/2⁻: -0.211 μN (J=3/2, M=1/2) and -0.634 μN (J=3/2, M=3/2) in the chiral quark model
- Magnetic moment of Λ(1690) 3/2⁻: +0.334 μN (J=3/2, M=1/2) and +1.000 μN (J=3/2, M=3/2) in the chiral quark model
- Transition magnetic moment Λ(1405)→Λ(1670): 0.075 μN in the chiral quark model, with absolute value approximately 0.023 μN from unitary chiral theories
- Quark masses used: Mu=Md=330 MeV, Ms=510 MeV; Goldstone boson masses: Mπ=137 MeV, MK=496 MeV, Mη=547 MeV, Mη′=958 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model calculations of magnetic moments, previously applied to ground-state baryons by Cheng and Li (1998) and others, to the low-lying negative-parity Λ resonances. The results are compared with those from the nonrelativistic SU(6) quark model of Isgur and Karl (1978) and with unitary chiral theory calculations by Jido et al. (2003), where Λ(1405) and Λ(1670) are generated dynamically from coupled-channel hadron interactions. The findings enable future comparisons with experimental measurements and further theoretical studies of electromagnetic properties of dynamically generated resonances.
