# Coordinate-Space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Description of Superfluid Fermi Systems

> Properties of strongly interacting, two-component finite Fermi systems are discussed within the recently developed coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) code {\hfbax}. Two illustrative examples are presented: (i) weakly bound deformed Mg isotopes, and (ii) spin-polarized atomic condensates in a strongly deformed harmonic trap.

## Key claim

The coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) code hfb-ax provides a superior description of weakly bound and/or extremely deformed superfluid Fermi systems compared to harmonic oscillator basis methods, but the differences between the two approaches increase towards the neutron drip line (e.g., 10 keV difference in total binding energy for 34Mg versus 170 keV for 40Mg).

## Method

The authors solved the HFB equations in a 2D coordinate space using B-spline techniques with box boundary conditions, employing the SLy4 Skyrme energy density functional with mixed delta pairing for nuclear calculations and the Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) for atomic systems. For the Mg isotopes, calculations used a box of ρmax=zmax=18 fm with mesh spacing h=0.6 fm and 13th-order B-splines; for the atomic gas of 1000 polarized atoms in a deformed harmonic trap, a rectangular box with ρmax=10 and zmax=50 was used with h=0.4 fm. The modified Broyden mixing method accelerated convergence of self-consistent iterations.

## Key numbers

- For 34Mg: total binding energy difference between hfb-ax and hfbtho is 10 keV (hfb-ax: -257.49 MeV, hfbtho: -257.48 MeV); quadrupole moment Q20 = +148.0 fm²; neutron chemical potential λn = -3.63 MeV
- For 40Mg: total binding energy difference between codes is 170 keV (hfb-ax: -266.96 MeV, hfbtho: -267.13 MeV); quadrupole moment Q20 = +221.6 fm²; neutron chemical potential λn = -0.45 MeV; static proton and neutron pairing vanish (Epair = 0)
- For 42Mg: quadrupole moment Q20 = -136.1 fm² (oblate deformation); neutron chemical potential λn = -0.22 MeV (unbound to two-neutron emission)
- For 44Mg: neutron chemical potential λn = +0.15 MeV (positive, indicating unbound system)
- Atomic calculations: 1000 atoms (N = N↑ + N↓ = 1000) in trap with deformation η = 10; polarization P = 0.21 corresponds to 214 unpaired particles
- Mesh spacing h = 0.4 fm and B-spline order M = 13 used for atomic calculations

## Context

This work extends the coordinate-space HFB approach of Dobaczewski (1996) and the hfbtho harmonic oscillator basis code of Stoitsov (2005) to treat weakly bound deformed Mg isotopes and spin-polarized atomic condensates. The calculations confirm that 40Mg is the last two-neutron bound Mg isotope with SLy4, consistent with the experimental observation by Baumann (2007), and predict that 42Mg could be long-lived due to shape coexistence. For atomic systems, the results show spatial separation between paired superfluid and polarized normal phases, matching experimental findings by Partridge (2006), and reveal small-amplitude oscillations in the pair density characteristic of the Fulde-Ferrel-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) phase, as discussed by Sensarma (2006) and Kinnunen (2006). The coordinate-space framework enables calculations at trap deformations (η=10) far beyond the η≤4 accessible to harmonic oscillator basis methods.
