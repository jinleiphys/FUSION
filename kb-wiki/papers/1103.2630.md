# Impact of the phonon coupling on the dipole strength and radiative neutron capture

> The E1 strength functions and radiative capture cross sections for several compound Sn isotopes, including unstable 132S n and 150S n, have been calculated using the self-consistent microscopic theory. In addition to the standard RPA or QRPA approaches, the method includes the quasiparticle-phonon coupling and the single-particle continuum. The results obtained show that the phonon contribution is very noticeable for the pygmy-dipole resonance, which, as it is known, is important for a description of the radiative neutron capture. The phonon contribution to the pygmy-dipole resonance and to the radiative neutron capture cross sections is increased with the (N-Z) difference growth. For example, in the (0-10) MeV interval the full theory gives 17% of EWSR for 150S n and 2.8% for 124S n, whereas within the continuum QRPA approach we have 5.1% and 1.7%, respectively. These facts indicate an important role of the self-consistent calculations that are of astrophysical interest for neutron-rich nuclei. The comparison with the phenomenological Generalized Lorentzian approach by Kopecky-Uhl has shown that the (Q)RPA approach gives a significant increase in the cross section by a factor of 2 for 132S n and a factor of 10 for 150S n and inclusion of the phonon coupling increases the cross sections for these nuclei even more, by a factor of 2-3.

## Key claim

The phonon coupling contribution to the pygmy dipole resonance and radiative neutron capture cross sections increases with the (N-Z) difference, and for neutron-rich isotopes like 150Sn the full theory including phonon coupling gives 17% of the energy-weighted sum rule in the (0-10) MeV interval compared to 5.1% from the continuum QRPA approach alone.

## Method

The authors used the self-consistent Extended Theory of Finite Fermi Systems in the discretized time-blocking approximation (ETFFS(DTBA)) with the SLy4 Skyrme force, which includes quasiparticle-phonon coupling and the single-particle continuum in addition to standard (Q)RPA configurations. Ground states were calculated within the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov approach using the HFBRAD code, and the residual interaction was derived as the second derivative of the Skyrme functional. Radiative neutron capture cross sections were then computed using the reaction code TALYS with the calculated gamma-ray strength functions.

## Key numbers

- For 150Sn in the (0-10) MeV interval, the full theory gives 17% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR), while the continuum QRPA approach gives 5.1%.
- For 124Sn in the (0-10) MeV interval, the full theory gives 2.8% of EWSR, while the continuum QRPA approach gives 1.7%.
- The (Q)RPA approach gives an increase in the neutron capture cross section by a factor of 2 for 132Sn and a factor of 10 for 150Sn compared to the Generalized Lorentzian approach.
- Inclusion of phonon coupling increases the cross sections for 132Sn and 150Sn by a factor of 2-3 at energies around 100 keV.
- The DTBA peak energy of 132Sn is predicted at 13.5 MeV, with the pygmy dipole resonance reproduced around 9.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent microscopic treatment of dipole strength from stable to unstable neutron-rich Sn isotopes, building on prior (Q)RPA calculations by the same group and others (e.g., refn6, refn7, refn8) that did not include phonon coupling. The results demonstrate that the phenomenological Generalized Lorentzian approach of Kopecky-Uhl (refn10, refn15) is inadequate for neutron-rich nuclei, and that self-consistent calculations including phonon coupling are necessary for reliable predictions of radiative neutron capture cross sections relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis. The approach enables more reliable estimates of electromagnetic properties for exotic nuclei where experimental data are unavailable.
