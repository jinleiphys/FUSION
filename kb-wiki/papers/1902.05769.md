# Fock contributions to nuclear symmetry energy and its slope parameter based on Lorentz-covariant decomposition of nucleon self-energies

> Using relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation, we study the effect of Fock terms on the nuclear properties not only around the saturation density, $ρ_{0}$, but also at higher densities. In particular, we investigate how the momentum dependence due to the exchange contribution affects the nuclear symmetry energy and its slope parameter, using the Lorentz-covariant decomposition of nucleon self-energies in an extended version of the RHF model, in which the exchange terms are adjusted so as to reproduce the single-nucleon potential at $ρ_{0}$. We find that the Fock contribution suppresses the kinetic term of nuclear symmetry energy at the densities around and beyond $ρ_{0}$. It is noticeable that not only the isovector-vector ($ρ$) meson but also the isoscalar mesons ($σ, ω$) and pion make significant influence on the potential term of nuclear symmetry energy through the exchange diagrams. Furthermore, the exchange contribution prevents the slope parameter from increasing monotonically at high densities.

## Key claim

The Fock (exchange) contribution suppresses the kinetic term of the nuclear symmetry energy at densities around and beyond saturation density, and prevents the slope parameter from increasing monotonically at high densities; this effect is achieved by adjusting the exchange terms to reproduce the single-nucleon potential at saturation density, and the momentum dependence from exchange diagrams is essential for this behavior.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation based on Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD), including sigma, omega, rho, and pion mesons with a dipole-type form factor at each interaction vertex. They apply the Lorentz-covariant decomposition of nucleon self-energies into scalar, time, and space components to separate the symmetry energy and its slope parameter into kinetic and potential terms, and compare results from RH (Hartree), RHF, and extended RHF (ERHF) models where the coupling constants in the exchange terms are varied.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Saturation energy: -16.0 MeV
- Incompressibility K_0 = 250 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: M_N*/M_N = 0.70
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym(rho_0) = 32.5 MeV (used to fix g_rho in some models)
- Cutoff parameters: Lambda_sigma = 2.0 GeV, Lambda_omega = 1.5 GeV, Lambda_rho = 1.3 GeV, Lambda_pi = 1.2 GeV
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: f_pi^2/(4 pi) = 0.08
- Rho-nucleon tensor-to-vector coupling ratio: f_rho/g_rho = 6.0 (empirical value)
- Meson masses: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV, m_pi = 138 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean-field (RMF) and RHF studies of nuclear symmetry energy (e.g., Sun (2008), Zhao (2014), Sun (2016), Liu (2018)) by using a Lorentz-covariant decomposition of nucleon self-energies that is more consistent with experimental analyses than the standard kinetic/potential decomposition based on expectation values of T and V. The results enable more reliable comparisons between theoretical predictions and experimental constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy from heavy-ion collisions and neutron-star observations, including the tidal deformation data from the LIGO/Virgo gravitational-wave detection (2017).
