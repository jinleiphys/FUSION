# A Schematic Model For Density-Dependent Vector Meson Masses

> A schematic two-level model consisting of a "collective" bosonic state and an "elementary" meson is constructed that provides interpolation from a hadronic description (a la Rapp/Wambach) to B/R scaling for the description of properties of vector mesons in dense medium. The development is based on a close analogy to the degenerate schematic model of Brown for giant resonances in nuclei.

# A Schematic Model For Density-Dependent Vector Meson Masses

## Key claim

The authors construct a schematic two-level model that interpolates between the hadronic description of Rapp/Wambach (valid near zero density) and the Brown/Rho scaling description (valid near the chiral phase transition density), but the interpolation is only clearly interpretable at the two endpoints, at intermediate densities the model cannot uniquely identify which state corresponds to the effective rho meson degree of freedom.

## Method

The authors build a degenerate schematic model of a "collective" bosonic state (an isobar-hole excitation \(N^*(1520)N^{-1}\)) mixing with an "elementary" vector meson, analogous to Brown's schematic model for giant resonances in nuclei. They compute the in-medium rho-meson and omega-meson dispersion relations and Z-factors (spectral weights) by solving the real part of the inverse propagator, using the Rapp/Wambach self-energy with and without the replacement of \(q_0^2/m_\rho^2\) by 1 to transition from the hadronic to the Brown/Rho scaling regime. The coupling constants are taken from empirical amplitudes of Friman, Lutz and Wolf.

## Key numbers

- In the Rapp/Wambach approach with zero width at normal nuclear density \(\rho_0\), the two rho-meson solutions are \(q_0^- \simeq 498\) MeV and \(q_0^+ \simeq 897\) MeV, with Z-factors \(Z(q_0^-) \simeq 0.16\) and \(Z(q_0^+) \simeq 0.84\).
- In the Brown/Rho approach with zero width at \(\rho_0\), the solutions are \(q_0^- = 406.7\) MeV and \(q_0^+ = 873.9\) MeV, with Z-factors \(Z(q_0^-) = 0.285\) and \(Z(q_0^+) = 0.714\).
- In the Brown/Rho approach with zero width at \(\rho = 2.5\rho_0\), the lower state mass drops to \(q_0^- = 136\) MeV with \(Z(q_0^-) = 0.356\), and the rho mass goes to zero at \(\rho_c \sim 2.75\rho_0\).
- For the omega meson in the Rapp/Wambach approach with zero width at \(\rho_0\), the solutions are \(q_0^- \simeq 395\) MeV and \(q_0^+ \simeq 1149\) MeV, with Z-factors \(Z(q_0^-) \simeq 0.155\) and \(Z(q_0^+) \simeq 0.845\).
- The omega coupling constant is taken as \(f_{\omega N^* N}^2 \approx 4.4 \times f_{\rho N^* N}^2\) from the empirical results of Friman et al.

## Context

This work extends the heuristic idea of Brown et al. (1998) that the Rapp/Wambach hadronic description of in-medium vector mesons could be reinterpreted as a density-dependent mass scaling, providing a "bottom-up" hadronic route to the Brown/Rho scaling proposed by Brown and Rho (1991). The model is built on the degenerate schematic model of Brown for giant dipole resonances and uses empirical coupling constants from Friman, Lutz and Wolf. The results enable predictions for low-mass dilepton yields in heavy-ion collisions, which can be tested by the CERES collaboration with the TPC detector, and suggest that the omega meson mass falls faster than the rho mass, potentially signaling an induced symmetry breaking phase at high density as discussed by Langfeld et al.
