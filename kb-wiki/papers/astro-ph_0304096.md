# Metastable strange matter and compact quark stars

> Strange quark matter in beta equilibrium at high densities is studied in a quark confinement model. Two equations of state are dynamically generated for the {\it same} set of model parameters used to describe the nucleon: one corresponds to a chiral restored phase with almost massless quarks and the other to a chiral broken phase. The chiral symmetric phase saturates at around five times the nuclear matter density. Using the equation of state for this phase, compact bare quark stars are obtained with radii and masses in the ranges $R\sim 5 - 8$ km and $M\sim M_\odot$. The energy per baryon number decreases very slowly from the center of the star to the periphery, remaining above the corresponding values for the iron or the nuclear matter, even at the edge. Our results point out that strange quark matter at very high densities may not be absolutely stable and the existence of an energy barrier between the two phases may prevent the compact quarks stars to decay to hybrid stars.

# Metastable strange matter and compact quark stars

## Key claim

Strange quark matter at very high densities may not be absolutely stable; the existence of an energy barrier between a chiral restored phase and a chiral broken phase may prevent compact quark stars from decaying to hybrid stars, but this metastability only occurs for the chiral symmetric phase (solution II) at high densities.

## Method

The authors used the chromodielectric model (CDM) with a quartic potential for the confining scalar field, with parameters fixed from nucleon phenomenology. They solved the mean-field gap equations for homogeneous strange quark matter in beta equilibrium with electrons, obtaining two distinct stable solutions (chiral broken phase with massive quarks and chiral restored phase with nearly massless quarks) for the same set of model parameters, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to compute stellar structure.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetric phase (solution II) saturates at around 5 times nuclear matter density (ρ ∼ 5 ρ₀) for η ∼ 0.12.
- Compact bare quark stars from solution II have radii R ∼ 5–8 km and masses M ∼ M⊙.
- For η ∼ 0.115 (B¹/⁴ ∼ 0.195 GeV), the maximum radius is R ∼ 6 km with corresponding mass M ∼ 0.9 M⊙.
- The central density of such a star is 10 ρ₀, with central energy density ε ∼ 3 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³; at the edge density drops to 5 ρ₀ with ε ∼ 1.35 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³.
- The minimum rotation period for these stars is ∼ 0.4 ms.
- For η ∼ 0.12 at ρ ∼ 5 ρ₀, the energy per baryon number for the unstable solution is ∼ 2.7 GeV, representing the energy barrier between phases.

## Context

This work extends the chromodielectric model studies of Drago et al. (2000), who used a quadratic potential and obtained hybrid stars with masses 1–2 M⊙ and radii ∼ 10 km with a hadron crust. The authors find that using a quartic potential dynamically generates two equations of state, consistent with results from perturbative QCD calculations by Fraga (2001) and with lattice QCD extrapolations by Peshier (2002), suggesting some model independence. The predicted small, dense quark stars are relevant to interpreting observations of isolated compact objects such as RX J1856.5-3574, though the revised parallax for that object gives R = 11.4 ± 2 km and M/M⊙ = 1.7 ± 0.4, which can be reproduced by many equations of state with and without strange matter.
