# Saxion Emission from SN1987A

> We study the possibility of emission of the saxion, a superpartner of the axion, from SN1987A. The fact that the observed neutrino pulse from SN1987A is in excellent agreement with the current theory of supernovae places a strong bound on the energy loss into any non-standard model channel, therefore enabling bounds to be placed on the decay constant, f_a, of a light saxion. The low-energy coupling of the saxion, which couples at high energies to the QCD gauge field strength, is expected to be enhanced from QCD scaling, making it interesting to investigate if the saxion could place stronger bounds on f_a than the axion itself. Moreover, since the properties of the saxion are determined by f_a, a constraint on this parameter can be translated into a constraint on the supersymmetry breaking scale. We find that the bound on f_a from saxion emission is comparable with the one derived from axion emission due to a cancellation of leading-order terms in the soft-radiation expansion.

## Key claim

The bound on the Peccei-Quinn scale \(f_a\) from saxion emission from SN1987A is comparable to the bound derived from axion emission, despite the saxion's low-energy coupling being enhanced by QCD scaling, because a cancellation of leading-order terms in the soft-radiation expansion suppresses the saxion emissivity relative to naive expectations.

## Method

The authors calculated the saxion emissivity from the process \(NN \to NNs\) in a supernova core using a tree-level \(SU(3)\) chiral Lagrangian with \(\pi^0\) and \(\eta\) exchange between nucleons, since the leading-order bremsstrahlung poles cancel for scalar emission. They derived the low-energy saxion-nucleon coupling by running the saxion's UV coupling to the QCD gauge field strength down to hadronic scales via the trace anomaly, obtaining an enhancement factor \(\kappa\). The resulting emissivity was compared to the Raffelt criterion (maximum emissivity \(10^{19}\) ergs/g/s) to set bounds on \(f_a\).

## Key numbers

* The enhancement factor \(\kappa\) from QCD scaling is approximately 26 for \(f_a = 10^9\) GeV, a SUSY breaking scale of \(10^4\) GeV, and \(\Lambda_\text{QCD} = 200\) MeV.
* The saxion-nucleon coupling matrix element \(\tilde{M} = 687 \pm 75\) MeV, derived from octet baryon masses and the pion-nucleon \(\Sigma\)-term.
* The ratio of saxion to axion emissivity is estimated as \(\dot{\epsilon}_s / \dot{\epsilon}_a \sim \kappa^2 \tilde{M}^2 / (256 \pi^4 M_n^2) \simeq 10^{-2}\).
* The Raffelt criterion for maximum emissivity is \(\dot{\epsilon} \le 10^{19}\) ergs/g/s, corresponding to a neutrino luminosity \(L_\nu \sim 10^{52}\) ergs/s.
* The allowed window for \(f_a\) from other constraints is \(10^9 \text{ GeV} \lesssim f_a \lesssim 10^{12}\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the standard axion emission calculation from SN1987A (Raffelt 1996) to the supersymmetric partner of the axion, the saxion, in gauge-mediated SUSY breaking models (Asaka 1998). Previous model-independent calculations for axion, neutrino, and Kaluza-Klein graviton emission (Hanhart 2000) relied on low-energy theorems that relate soft emission to on-shell NN scattering amplitudes, but such theorems cannot be constructed for saxion emission due to cancellation of the leading \(1/\omega\) poles. The result enables constraints on \(f_a\) to be translated into constraints on the supersymmetry breaking scale in gauge-mediated models, and suggests that including saxion emission could tighten the allowed PQ parameter space if the UV physics is supersymmetric.
