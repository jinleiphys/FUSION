# Relativistic Models for Quasi-Elastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering

> Two relativistic approaches to charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering are illustrated and compared: one is phenomenological and based on the superscaling behavior of electron scattering data and the other relies on the microscopic description of nuclear dynamics in relativistic mean field theory. The role of meson exchange currents in the two-particle two-hole sector is explored. The predictions of the models for differential and total cross sections are presented and compared with the MiniBooNE data.

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE CCQE cross section data can be explained without invoking an anomalously large nucleon axial mass, provided that two-particle two-hole meson exchange current contributions are included; however, the authors state that more refined calculations including correlation currents and axial-vector MEC effects are needed before final conclusions can be drawn.

## Method

Two relativistic approaches to charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering on carbon-12 were compared: the SuperScaling Approach (SuSA), which uses a phenomenological scaling function extracted from longitudinal electron scattering data, and the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model, which provides a microscopic description with relativistic scalar and vector potentials for both bound and final states. The SuSA model was extended by including 2p2h meson exchange currents (MEC) in the transverse polar vector response, and both models' predictions for flux-integrated cross sections were compared with MiniBooNE data.

## Key numbers

- The standard dipole axial mass value is \(M_A \simeq 1\) GeV/c\(^2\); the RFG model requires an ad hoc value of \(M_A \simeq 1.35\) GeV/c\(^2\) to match MiniBooNE data.
- The RMF and SuSA models yield an effective axial mass \(M_A^{\rm eff} \simeq 1.5\) GeV/c\(^2\) from best fit to MiniBooNE data.
- Values in the range \(1.35 < M_A^{\rm eff} < 1.65\) GeV/c\(^2\) produce results compatible with MiniBooNE data within experimental errors.
- The neutrino energy reaches values as high as 3 GeV in the MiniBooNE kinematics.
- The RMF model gives longitudinal and transverse scaling functions that differ by typically 20%, with the transverse one being larger.

## Context

This work extends the SuSA approach of Amaro (2004) and the RMF model previously applied to inclusive quasielastic electron scattering, addressing the puzzle raised by MiniBooNE data (Aguilar-Arévalo, 2010) that showed the Relativistic Fermi Gas model underestimates cross sections unless an unusually large axial mass is used. The results enable further investigation of whether multinucleon effects can resolve the discrepancy without modifying the nucleon axial form factor, and point to the need for including correlation diagrams and axial-vector MEC contributions as done in recent electron scattering studies by Amaro (2010).
