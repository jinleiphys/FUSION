# Comment on "New Limits on Intrinsic Charm in the Nucleon from Global Analysis of Parton Distributions"

> A Comment on the Letter by P. Jimenez-Delgado, T.J. Hobbs, J.T. Londergan, and W. Melnitchouk, Phys. Rev. Lett. 114, 082002 (2015).

## Key claim

The authors argue that the intrinsic charm momentum fraction ⟨x⟩_IC = (0.15 ± 0.09)% reported by Jimenez-Delgado et al. (2015) is not warranted, because the SLAC inclusive electron-scattering data used in the global fit cannot unambiguously identify an intrinsic charm contribution even at the 1% level due to statistical and systematic uncertainties, both experimental and theoretical.

## Method

Brodsky and Gardner critique the global QCD analysis of Jimenez-Delgado et al. (2015) by examining the SLAC e d (p) → e′ X data set used in the fit, noting that only 157 of 1021 data points have W² above the charm hadronic threshold of about 16 GeV². They identify specific theoretical issues: the higher-twist model used is isospin-independent and does not include enhancements at x ∼ 1 from hard subprocesses involving multiple quarks; the target-mass-corrected structure functions remain nonzero as x → 1; and intrinsic strange and beauty quark contributions are neglected. They also challenge the statistical methodology, arguing that the reported 4σ limit of ⟨x⟩_IC ≲ 0.5% is too severe because the tolerance criterion Δχ² = 1 at 1σ does not properly account for correlations among the many fit parameters (25 leading-twist plus 12 higher-twist parameters).

## Key numbers

- The SLAC e d (p) → e′ X data set has an overall normalization (systematic) error of ±1.7% (2.1%) and a relative normalization error of typically ±1.1% (Whitlow, 1991).
- Only 157 of 1021 SLAC data points have W² in excess of the charm hadronic threshold W²_th ≈ 16 GeV².
- Jimenez-Delgado et al. (2015) report ⟨x⟩_IC = (0.15 ± 0.09)% at 1σ (68% CL) and ⟨x⟩_IC ≲ 0.5% at 4σ.
- Typical prior determinations limit ⟨x⟩_IC ∼ O(1%) at 90% CL (Harris, 1995; Pumplin, 2007; Dulat, 2013).

## Context

This Comment challenges the specific result of Jimenez-Delgado et al. (2015), which claimed the most stringent limit on intrinsic charm from a global PDF analysis including SLAC inclusive data. The critique extends earlier concerns about the difficulty of extracting intrinsic charm from inclusive structure functions, noting that the EMC charm structure function measurements (Aubert, 1982) were excluded from the fit based on a goodness-of-fit criterion, whereas Brodsky and Gardner argue that including QCD nuclear effects (de Florian, 2011; de Florian, 2012) could improve the fit to those data. The Comment enables future work by emphasizing that unambiguous identification of intrinsic charm requires measurements beyond single-arm inclusive electron scattering, such as charm-tagged final-state observables.
