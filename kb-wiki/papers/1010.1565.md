# Electron screening and its effects on Big-Bang nucleosynthesis

> We study the effects of electron screening on nuclear reaction rates occurring during the Big Bang nucleosynthesis epoch. The sensitivity of the predicted elemental abundances on electron screening is studied in details. It is shown that electron screening does not produce noticeable results in the abundances unless the traditional Debye-Hückel model for the treatment of electron screening in stellar environments is enhanced by several orders of magnitude. The present work rules out electron screening as a relevant ingredient to Big Bang nucleosynthesis and ruling out exotic possibilities for the treatment of screening, beyond the mean-field theoretical approach.

## Key claim

Electron screening does not produce noticeable changes in Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) elemental abundances unless the traditional Debye-Hückel screening model is artificially enhanced by a factor of at least \(10^4\) relative to its standard formulation, definitively ruling out electron screening as a relevant ingredient for BBN.

## Method

The authors modified a standard BBN network code derived from Wagoner, Fowler, and Hoyle (1967) and Kawano (1988, 1992), incorporating all reactions from the standard BBN model with cross sections as published in Nollett (2004). They calculated screening enhancement factors using the Debye-Hückel model adapted for the BBN epoch, where the abundant \(e^+e^-\) pairs dominate the electron density, and then artificially rescaled the screening factor by a multiplicative factor \(w\) ranging from 1 to \(10^4\) to test the sensitivity of elemental abundances to enhanced screening.

## Key numbers

- For \(w = 1\) (standard Debye-Hückel screening), all elemental abundances are modified by less than \(10^{-5}\) percent.
- The screening enhancement factor for BBN at \(T_9 \lesssim 1\) is \(f^{BBN} = \exp(4.49 \times 10^{-8} \zeta Z_1 Z_2)\), yielding corrections of order \(1 + 4.49 \times 10^{-8} \zeta Z_1 Z_2\).
- The baryon-to-photon ratio used is \(\eta = 6.19 \times 10^{-10}\) (from WMAP data, Komatsu 2010), with \(N_\nu = 3\) neutrino families and neutron lifetime \(\tau_n = 878.5\) s.
- The Debye radius during BBN scales as \(R_D = R_D^{(0)} T_9^{-1}\) with \(R_D^{(0)} \sim 6.1 \times 10^{-5}\) cm for \(T_9 \sim 2\) and \(R_D^{(0)} \sim 3.7 \times 10^{-5}\) cm for \(T_9 \lesssim 1\).
- The number of ions inside the Debye sphere is at least of order \(10^3\), justifying the mean-field approximation.

## Context

This work reinforces the conclusions of Itoh et al. (1997), who used mean-field calculations to show that screening due to free \(e^+e^-\) pairs yields at most a 0.1% change in BBN abundances. The study extends prior sensitivity analyses of BBN parameters (e.g., Nollett 2004; Serpico 2004; Cyburt 2001, 2002, 2004) by specifically testing electron screening, and it rules out exotic screening models beyond the mean-field approach (such as those from molecular dynamics simulations by Shaviv 1996, 2000; Mao 2009; and Weiss, Fink, and Truran 2001) as relevant for BBN. The results enable future BBN studies to safely neglect electron screening when interpreting discrepancies such as the lithium problem (the observed \(^7\)Li abundance in metal-poor halo stars versus BBN predictions).
