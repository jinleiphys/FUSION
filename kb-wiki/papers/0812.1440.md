# From kinetic theory to dissipative fluid dynamics

> We present the results of deriving the Israel-Stewart equations of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics from kinetic theory via Grad's 14-moment expansion. Working consistently to second order in the Knudsen number, these equations contain several new terms which are absent in previous treatments.

## Key claim
The paper derives the full Israel-Stewart equations of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics from kinetic theory via Grad's 14-moment expansion, working consistently to second order in the Knudsen number. The key hedge is that several second-order terms in these equations were missed or neglected in the original Israel-Stewart treatment and in subsequent works by Muronga, with the authors claiming these additional terms constitute important corrections for times longer than the relaxation times.

## Method
The authors start from the Boltzmann equation and apply Grad's 14-moment expansion to derive evolution equations for the dissipative quantities (bulk viscous pressure, heat flux current, and shear stress tensor). They perform a systematic power counting in terms of the Knudsen number K = ℓ_mfp/L_hydro, identifying the Navier-Stokes equations as first-order in K and the Israel-Stewart equations as second-order in K. The derivation is not restricted to the shear stress tensor but also includes bulk viscous pressure and heat flux current, making it applicable to non-conformal systems with non-vanishing net charge density.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of dissipative quantities to energy density is proportional to the Knudsen number K, independent of the ratio of viscosity to entropy density (Eq. 18)
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s is proportional to ℓ_mfp/λ_th, where λ_th is the thermal wavelength (Eq. 13)
- The relaxation times τ_Π, τ_q, and τ_π characterize the time scales over which dissipative quantities relax to their Navier-Stokes values
- The coefficient λ_1 from kinetic theory is predicted as λ_1 ≡ τ_π η, in agreement with Baier (2008)

## Context
This work extends the original Israel-Stewart theory (Israel and Stewart, 1976) by including additional second-order terms that were missed or neglected in that treatment and in subsequent applications by Muronga (2004) and Song and Heinz (2008). The paper also connects to the work of Baier (2008) on second-order fluid dynamics for the shear stress tensor, providing a kinetic-theory derivation of the coefficient λ_1. The results enable more complete numerical implementations of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics for modeling heavy-ion collisions, particularly for systems with non-vanishing net charge density and non-conformal equations of state.
