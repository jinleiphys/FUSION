# Exploring Nucleon Structure and the Proton Mass Problem through Holographic QCD

> Understanding the internal structure of the proton-including the distributions of quarks and gluons and their contributions to proton properties such as mass-remains a central challenge in quantum chromodynamics (QCD). While quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) have been studied extensively, a unified approach that simultaneously extracts quark parton distribution functions (PDFs), gravitational form factors (GFFs), and gluon GPDs from experimental constraints is still lacking. Moreover, the role of gluons in proton mass generation, particularly through the trace anomaly mechanism, requires deeper theoretical and phenomenological exploration. In this study, we first extract quark GPDs in protons using parameterization method based on the electromagnetic form factors provided by Light-Front Holographic QCD (LFHQCD) from which we derive both quark PDFs and their GFFs. We then extend this approach to model gluon GPDs. Our calculations show consistency with experimental data and lattice QCD results and successfully reproduce soft Pomeron behavior. Furthermore, we investigate near-threshold $J/ψ$ production using gauge/string duality to quantify the contribution of the trace anomaly to the proton mass. Our results demonstrate that the parameterization method provides a consistent framework for describing both quark and gluon structure, bridging GPDs, PDFs, and GFFs. The analysis of $J/ψ$ production confirms that the trace anomaly contributes significantly ($\sim 23\%$) to the proton mass, with the calculated cross-section dependence on momentum transfer $t$ in agreement with experimental observations. This work advances the understanding of proton structure by integrating quark and gluon degrees of freedom and elucidating the origin of proton mass within QCD.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a parameterization method based on Light-Front Holographic QCD (LFHQCD) provides a consistent framework for simultaneously extracting quark and gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs), parton distribution functions (PDFs), and gravitational form factors (GFFs), and that the trace anomaly contributes approximately 23% to the proton mass, though the precise numerical value from the full text is 23.75%, and this result depends on the specific holographic model and the use of gauge/string duality for near-threshold J/psi production.

## Method

The authors first extracted quark GPDs in protons using a parameterization method based on electromagnetic form factors provided by Light-Front Holographic QCD (LFHQCD), deriving both quark PDFs and their GFFs. They then extended this approach to model gluon GPDs by assuming the same functional form as quark GPDs but with different parameters, fixing the gluon parameter c2 = -1 using the masslessness of the graviton in the dual AdS theory. Finally, they computed the cross section for exclusive near-threshold J/psi photoproduction in ep scattering using gauge/string duality, incorporating the previously obtained GFFs as input and including energy-scale dependence via the two-loop QCD beta-function and the anomalous dimension gamma_m.

## Key numbers

- The trace anomaly contributes approximately 23.75% to the proton mass, as determined from the holographic analysis of J/psi production.
- The proton mass is 938 MeV, while the quark mass contribution from the Higgs mechanism is approximately 9 MeV.
- The ground state mass of the rho meson fixes lambda = (0.548 GeV)^2 in the LFHQCD framework.
- The first excited state mass of the graviton (soft Pomeron) is M1 = (2-2.24) GeV, with lambda_g = (1-1.14) GeV^2 derived from the Pomeron slope alpha'_p = (0.20-0.25) GeV^{-2}.
- The threshold energy for J/psi production is W_tr = M + M_J/psi = 4.04 GeV, with M_J/psi = 3.10 GeV and M = 0.94 GeV.
- The hard truncation parameter z_m is fixed as z_m = 2*sqrt(2)/M_psi using the ground state mass of the J/psi meson.
- The soft Pomeron intercept is alpha_p(0) = 1.08 and slope alpha'_p = (0.20-0.25) GeV^{-2}.

## Context

This work extends the LFHQCD framework previously developed by Brodsky (2014) and Sufian (2016) for electromagnetic form factors to a unified description of both quark and gluon GPDs and GFFs, showing consistency with experimental data and lattice QCD results from Hackett (2023). The analysis of near-threshold J/psi production builds on the theoretical connection between the trace anomaly and proton mass proposed by Kharzeev (1998) and Hatta (2018), and provides a first-principles-based estimate that can serve as a benchmark for future experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).
