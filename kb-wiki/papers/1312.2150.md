# Polarized $χ_{c2}$-charmonium production in antiproton-nucleus interactions

> Starting from the Feynman diagram representation of multiple scattering we consider the polarized $χ_c$(1P)-charmonia production in antiproton-nucleus reactions close to the threshold ($p_{\rm lab}=5-7$ GeV/c). The rescattering and absorption of the incoming antiproton and outgoing charmonium on nucleons are taken into account, including the possibility of the elastic and nondiagonal (flavor-conserving) scattering $χ_{cJ} N \to χ_{cJ^\prime} N$, $J,J^\prime=0,1,2$. The elementary amplitudes of the latter processes are evaluated by expanding the physical $χ_c$-states in the Clebsch-Gordan series of the $c \bar c$ states with fixed values of internal orbital angular momentum ($L_z$) and spin projections on the $χ_c$ momentum axis. The total interaction cross sections of these $c \bar c$ states with nucleons have been calculated in previous works using the QCD factorization theorem and the nonrelativistic quarkonium model and turned out to be strongly $L_z$-dependent due to the transverse size difference. This directly leads to finite values of the $χ_c$-nucleon nondiagonal scattering amplitudes. We show that the $χ_{c0} N \to χ_{c2} N$ transitions significantly influence the $χ_{c2}$-production with helicity zero at small transverse momenta. This can serve as a signal in future experimental tests of the quark structure of $χ_c$-states by the PANDA collaboration at FAIR.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nondiagonal transitions \(\chi_{c0} N \to \chi_{c2} N\) significantly influence the production of \(\chi_{c2}\) with helicity zero at small transverse momenta in near-threshold antiproton-nucleus reactions, providing a potential signal for future experimental tests by the PANDA collaboration at FAIR, though the effect is conditional on the polarization-dependent \(\chi_c\)-nucleon cross sections derived from the transverse size difference of \(c\bar c\) states.

## Method

The authors calculate Feynman multiple scattering diagrams in the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) for \(\bar p A \to \chi_J (A-1)^*\) reactions, including the direct formation mechanism \(\bar p p \to \chi_c\) and corrections from elastic rescattering of the incoming antiproton and outgoing charmonium on nucleons, as well as nondiagonal (flavor-conserving) transitions \(\chi_{cJ} N \to \chi_{cJ^\prime} N\). The elementary amplitudes for these transitions are evaluated by expanding the physical \(\chi_c\) states in a Clebsch-Gordan series of \(c\bar c\) states with fixed internal orbital angular momentum projections (\(L_z\)), using total interaction cross sections of these \(c\bar c\) states with nucleons from prior QCD factorization and nonrelativistic quarkonium model calculations.

## Key numbers

* Beam momentum range: \(p_{\rm lab} = 5-7\) GeV/c for near-threshold antiproton-nucleus reactions.
* Mass splitting between \(\chi_c\) states: approximately 140 MeV, enabling nondiagonal transitions.
* Diffractive cross section \(\sigma(\bar p p \to \bar N^* p + \text{c.c.}) = 0.13 \pm 0.02\) mb at beam momentum 5.7 GeV/c, two orders of magnitude smaller than the elastic \(\bar p p\) cross section (approximately 15 mb) at the same momentum.

## Context

This work extends the predictions of Gerland et al. (1998) and Hufner et al. (2000) on polarization-dependent charmonium-nucleon cross sections, which were originally applied to heavy-ion collisions, to the cleaner environment of antiproton-nucleus collisions. The authors show that the nondiagonal transitions enhance the polarization signal beyond the color filtering mechanism alone, enabling future experimental tests by the PANDA collaboration at FAIR and potentially complementing studies of \(\gamma A \to J/\psi A^*\) reactions at the upgraded TJNAF facility.
