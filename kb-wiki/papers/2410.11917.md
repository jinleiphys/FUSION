# Recent theoretical developments in quarkonia production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Quarkonia are golden probes of heavy ion collisions that have attracted much attention from both experimental and theoretical perspectives. This paper will review recent theoretical studies on quarkonium thermal properties, with a particular focus on the heavy quark finite-temperature potential obtained by Lattice QCD and other approaches. Moreover, it will examine the advancements in the real-time evolution of quarkonia in heavy ion collisions.

# Recent theoretical developments in quarkonia production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
This review argues that recent lattice QCD results and other approaches indicate the heavy quark in-medium potential may exhibit no color screening or diminished screening, with a pronounced imaginary component, challenging the long-standing picture that static color screening is the primary mechanism for quarkonium dissociation in the quark-gluon plasma. The author notes that the question of how to detect this potential experimentally and which observable it is sensitive to remains unanswered.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical studies of quarkonium thermal properties, focusing on the heavy quark finite-temperature potential obtained from lattice QCD, hard thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory, and the Gribov-Zwanziger approach. It then examines real-time evolution frameworks including classical transport models (Boltzmann equation, rate equation) and open quantum system approaches (Duke, Munich-Kent, Nantes, and Osaka approaches) based on NRQCD or pNRQCD with Lindblad equations.

## Key numbers
- The heavy quark energy scale hierarchy in vacuum is given as $m_Q \gg m_Qv \gg m_Qv^2$, enabling effective field theory descriptions
- The leading order singlet potential is $V(r) = -C_F\alpha_s/r$ with $C_F = (N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)$ and number of colors $N_c = 3$
- The Cornell potential form is $V(r) = -\alpha/r + \sigma r + \text{constant}$
- The real and imaginary parts of the HQ potential are compared at $T = 205$ MeV from different lattice approaches
- Three principal time scales are identified: environment relaxation $\tau_e \sim 1/(\pi T)$, subsystem intrinsic time $\tau_s \sim 1/E_{\text{bind}}$, and subsystem relaxation $\tau_r \sim 1/\eta \approx m_Q/T^2$
- The scale hierarchy for the Quantum Brownian Motion regime is $m_Q \geq 1/a_0 \gg \pi T \sim m_D \gg E_{\text{bind}}$

## Context
This review extends the line of work on quarkonium suppression in heavy ion collisions, originally proposed by Matsui (1986) as a signature of QGP formation via color screening. It contrasts the traditional screening picture with recent lattice QCD results from the HotQCD Collaboration (Bala 2021, Bazavov 2023) and prior studies by Shi (2021), Du (2019), and Tang (2023) that find no-screening or less-screening potentials. The review enables future work connecting the quantum Brownian motion regime at high temperatures to the quantum optical limit at low temperatures, and the development of unified frameworks for both open heavy flavor and quarkonium production based on EPOS4.
