# {\bar p}p-annihilation processes in the tree approximation of SU(3) chiral effective theory

> The {\bar p}p-annihilation reactions {\bar p}p-->eta eta eta and {\bar p}p--> eta K{\bar K} at rest are considered in the tree approximation in the framework of SU(3) chiral effective theory at leading order. The calculated branchings are compared with the data. The results for neutral (eta eta eta, K^0{\bar K^0}eta) and charged (K^+ K^- eta) channels are essentially different.

## Key claim

The tree-level SU(3) chiral effective theory at leading order underestimates the branching ratios for \(\bar pp\to\eta\eta\eta\) and \(\bar pp\to\eta K^0\bar K^0\) by roughly one order of magnitude and a factor of three, respectively, while overestimating the \(\bar pp\to\eta K^+K^-\) branching by more than an order of magnitude; the authors present these results as a background model and note that missing resonance contributions and next-to-leading-order corrections may be important.

## Method

The authors computed the tree-level Feynman diagrams for \(\bar pp\to\eta\eta\eta\) and \(\bar pp\to\eta K\bar K\) at rest using the leading-order SU(3) chiral effective Lagrangian for mesons and baryons. They expanded the Lagrangian to obtain the relevant four-meson and baryon-meson vertices, wrote down the amplitudes for all contributing diagrams (including baryon-exchange and contact terms), and integrated the squared amplitudes over the three-body phase space to obtain cross sections and branching ratios. They also evaluated a simplified "threshold" approximation where final mesons are taken at rest.

## Key numbers

*   Branching ratio for \(\bar pp\to\eta\eta\eta\): theoretical (set a) \(0.424\times 10^{-4}\) (exact) vs. experimental \((4.22\pm 0.24)\times 10^{-4}\) (from \(6\gamma\) events) and \((3.93\pm 0.68)\times 10^{-4}\) (from \(10\gamma\) events).
*   Branching ratio for \(\bar pp\to\eta K^0\bar K^0\): theoretical (set a) \(1.488\times 10^{-4}\) (exact) vs. experimental \(2\times (2.2\pm 0.7)\times 10^{-4}\).
*   Branching ratio for \(\bar pp\to\eta K^+K^-\): theoretical (set a) \(182.\times 10^{-4}\) (exact); experimental value is unknown, but an upper estimate of \(< 9.4\times 10^{-4}\) is given from annihilation frequency data.
*   Total \(p\bar p\)-annihilation cross section at rest used for normalization: \(v\sigma^{\rm ann}_{p\bar p}(v\to 0)=36.5\) mb, derived from the imaginary part of the s-wave scattering length Im \(a_s = -0.69\) fm.
*   Pion decay constant used: \(f\approx 93\) MeV.
*   Two sets of axial-vector coupling constants were used: (a) \(D=0.76\), \(F=0.48\); (b) \(D=0.80\), \(F=0.46\).

## Context

This work is a first attempt to apply chiral perturbation theory to nucleon-antinucleon annihilation into mesons, extending the approach previously used for \(\pi\pi\), \(\pi N\), and few-nucleon systems (Weinberg 1979, Gasser and Leutwyler 1984). The calculated tree-level branchings are compared with experimental data from Amsler (2008) and others. The results show that the leading-order tree approximation underestimates the neutral channels (\(\eta\eta\eta\), \(K^0\bar K^0\eta\)) and overestimates the charged channel (\(K^+K^-\eta\)), suggesting that resonance production (e.g., \(f_0(1500)\), \(\phi\eta\)) and next-to-leading-order loop corrections, which are not included, may be essential. The work enables future studies that incorporate these missing contributions and test the applicability of chiral effective theory to annihilation processes.
