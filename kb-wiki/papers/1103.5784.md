# Low Q^2 measurements of the proton form factor ratio $mu_p G_E / G_M$

> We present an updated extraction of the proton electromagnetic form factor ratio, mu_p G_E/G_M, at low Q^2. The form factors are sensitive to the spatial distribution of the proton, and precise measurements can be used to constrain models of the proton. An improved selection of the elastic events and reduced background contributions yielded a small systematic reduction in the ratio mu_p G_E/G_M compared to the original analysis.

## Key claim

The updated extraction of the proton form factor ratio mu_p G_E/G_M at low Q^2 yields values typically lower by about 1% compared to the original analysis (Ron et al., 2007), with the reduction attributed to improved selection of elastic events and reduced background contamination from quasielastic scattering in the target windows.

## Method

The experiment used a polarized electron beam incident on cryogenic liquid hydrogen targets (10 cm length for 362 MeV beam energy, 15 cm for 687 MeV) in Jefferson Lab Hall A. Elastic electron-proton scattering events were identified by detecting the struck proton in one of the High Resolution Spectrometers, with the proton polarization measured in a focal plane polarimeter. The ratio of transverse to longitudinal polarization components was used to extract G_E/G_M via the polarization transfer formula. The reanalysis applied tighter cuts on reconstructed target position to remove endcap contributions (reducing them to less than 0.5%) and used dummy target measurements to subtract residual background, with a 50% systematic uncertainty assigned to the endcap correction.

## Key numbers

- The new results are typically lower by approximately 1% compared to the original analysis (Ron et al., 2007)
- Measurements were taken at eight Q^2 values: 0.215, 0.235, 0.251, 0.265, 0.308, 0.346, 0.400, and 0.474 GeV^2
- Beam energies used were 0.362 GeV and 0.687 GeV
- Beam polarization was approximately 40%, flipped pseudo-randomly at 30 Hz
- Endcap scattering contributed less than 0.5% after applying tighter cuts (compared to 3-5% in the original analysis)
- Target lengths were 10 cm for 362 MeV running and 15 cm for 687 MeV settings (corrected from the original publication which misstated 15 cm for all runs)
- Spin precession angles ranged from 91.0 to 103.0 degrees across the kinematic settings

## Context

This work extends the low-Q^2 proton form factor measurements originally reported by Ron et al. (2007), providing improved precision through better background rejection. The results are comparable in precision to the BLAST experiment measurements (Crawford et al., 2007) which covered Q^2 from 0.15 to 0.6 GeV^2, and complement the higher-statistics follow-up experiment JLab E08-007 (Zhan et al., 2011) which achieved average uncertainties below 1.2%. The improved low-Q^2 form factor data help constrain models of the proton's large-scale structure, including pion cloud contributions, and provide essential input for extracting strange quark form factors from parity-violating electron scattering measurements.
