# Femtoscopy analysis of d-Au interactions at $\sqrt{s}=200$GeV

> The femtoscopy correlation radii for d-Au collisions at $200$GeV are calculated in the hydrodynamic model and compared to PHENIX collaboration data. For asymmetric systems, such as d-Au or p-Pb collisions, the correlation radius $R_{out-long}$ is estimated and predicted to be non-zero. It appears due to different lifetimes of the parts of the fireball at positive and negative rapidities. Azimuthally sensitive Hanbury Brown-Twiss analysis with respect to the second order event plane shows a significant angular dependence of the radii. It reflects the strong azimuthal asymmetry of the d-Au source geometry and flow for central interactions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a non-zero HBT correlation radius \(R_{out-long}\) for asymmetric d-Au and p-Pb collisions, arising from the different lifetimes of the fireball at positive and negative rapidities due to the forward-backward asymmetry of the system.

## Method

The dynamics of d-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV are simulated using a 3+1-dimensional event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic model with shear and bulk viscosity, initialized with a Glauber Monte Carlo model. The HBT correlation function for identical pions is constructed from the freeze-out hypersurface and fitted with a Gaussian Bertsch-Pratt parametrization, including an additional \(R_{ol}^2\) term for asymmetric systems. Azimuthally sensitive HBT analysis is performed with respect to the second-order event plane, extracting Fourier coefficients of the radii.

## Key numbers

- For d-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the HBT radii \(R_{side}\), \(R_{out}\), and \(R_{long}\) are calculated for pion pairs with \(350\) MeV \(< k_\perp < 450\) MeV and compared to PHENIX data; the systematic uncertainty from varying the fit range \(|q|<0.1\) GeV to \(0.2\) GeV is smaller than 10%.
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio is fixed at \(0.08\) and the bulk viscosity to entropy ratio at \(0.04\).
- The freeze-out temperature is set to \(150\) MeV.
- The \(R_{ol}^2\) parameter is predicted to be non-zero for d-Au collisions at \(200\) GeV and for p-Pb collisions at \(5.02\) TeV, with values shown as a function of pion pair momentum (Fig. 3).
- For central d-Au collisions (0-5% centrality), the second-order Fourier coefficients of the HBT radii are extracted: \(R_{o,2}^2\) is negative, \(R_{s,2}^2\) is positive, and \(R_{l,2}^2\) is smaller but measurable.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic model calculations of HBT radii from symmetric Au-Au collisions to asymmetric d-Au collisions, comparing with PHENIX data (Adare 2014) and preliminary STAR data (Chajecki 2009). It builds on previous studies of collective flow in d-Au collisions (Adare 2013) and the observation of elliptic flow in such systems (Bozek 2011). The prediction of a non-zero \(R_{ol}^2\) enables experimental tests of the fireball lifetime asymmetry in d-Au and p-Pb collisions, while the azimuthally sensitive HBT analysis provides a method to distinguish between collective flow and color glass condensate explanations for angular correlations (Dusling 2013, Bozek 2011, Bzdak 2013).
