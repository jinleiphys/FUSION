# Studies of $\rm ^{144,148}Sm+α$ potential for the $p$-process nucleosynthesis

> Nucleosynthesis reaction networks leading to $p$-nuclei involve a combination of different types of photodisintegration and capture reactions, as well as $β^+$ decays or electron captures. Photodisintegration reactions involving $α$ particles present a particular interest as they serve as branching points of the reaction networks. The cross sections of these reactions depend crucially on the $α$-nucleus interaction. The $α$ optical model potential (AOMP) is determined mostly by means of experimental differential elastic scattering distributions. Several previous studies have focused on the case of $\rm ^{144}Sm$, an intriguing $p$-nucleus that is semi-magic with 82 neutrons. This work presents new experimental data on $α$ elastic and inelastic scattering on $\rm ^{148}Sm$, its closest stable isotope. Isotopic effects on the description of the AOMP are studied, as well as their consequences on the prediction of $α$-induced reaction cross sections at astrophysical energies. It is shown that the isotopic ratio for $(α,γ)$ cross sections can be multiplied up to a factor of two when these effects are included.

## Key claim

The paper shows that isotopic effects in the α-nucleus optical model potential (AOMP) can change the predicted (α,γ) cross-section ratio between ¹⁴⁴Sm and ¹⁴⁸Sm by up to a factor of two at astrophysical energies, but this conclusion is based on relative observables rather than absolute cross sections because systematic uncertainties in beam intensity prevented reliable absolute angular distributions.

## Method

The authors measured α elastic and inelastic scattering angular distributions at 20 MeV beam energy on isotopically enriched ¹⁴⁴Sm and ¹⁴⁸Sm targets using a hybrid detection system combining the Split-Pole magnetic spectrometer (22°–85°) with three silicon-detector telescopes (80°–163°) at the ALTO facility. They extracted the elastic differential cross-section ratio between the two isotopes and the inelastic-to-elastic ratio for ¹⁴⁸Sm, then compared these relative observables with predictions from nine different AOMP models implemented in the TALYS-2.0 reaction code, including the Atomki-V2 potential and custom potentials fitted to existing ¹⁴⁴Sm(α,α) data from Mohr (1997).

## Key numbers

- Elastic isotopic ratio R_el(¹⁴⁸Sm/¹⁴⁴Sm) measured at 20 MeV across 31°–145° laboratory angles, with values ranging from 0.819(126) to 1.14(11), each with statistical uncertainties up to 3% and systematic uncertainties up to 7% from target thickness
- Inelastic-to-elastic ratio for ¹⁴⁸Sm measured at 20 MeV across 80°–163° laboratory angles, with values ranging from 0.0180(23) at 80° to 0.156(17) at 163°
- ¹⁴⁴Sm target thickness: 44 ± 4 μg/cm²; ¹⁴⁸Sm target thickness: 74 ± 6 μg/cm², both on 50 μg/cm² carbon backings
- Energy resolution: ΔE detectors ~80 keV FWHM, E detectors ~20 keV FWHM
- The isotopic ratio for (α,γ) cross sections can be multiplied up to a factor of two when isotopic effects in the AOMP are included

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the α-nucleus optical potential for ¹⁴⁴Sm, a semi-magic p-nucleus (N=82) that has been extensively studied by Mohr (1997), Kiss (2022), Somorjai (1997), and Scholtz (2020), by providing new experimental data on its closest stable isotope ¹⁴⁸Sm. The study builds on the suggestion by Palumbo (2012) that isotopic effects in α elastic scattering are key observables for constraining the AOMP, and on the earlier work by Badawy (1978) who analyzed α scattering excitation functions for several samarium isotopes. The results enable improved predictions of (α,γ) and (α,n) reaction cross sections at astrophysical energies relevant to the p-process, particularly for understanding how nuclear magicity affects the AOMP and its consequences for nucleosynthesis reaction networks.
