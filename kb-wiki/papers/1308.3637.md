# Neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb from the energy of the anti-analog giant dipole resonance

> The energy of the charge-exchange Anti-analog Giant Dipole Resonance (AGDR) has been calculated for the 208Pb isotope using the state-of-the-art fully self-consistent relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation based on the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. It is shown that the AGDR centroid energy is very sensitively related to the corresponding neutron-skin thickness. The neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb has been determined very precisely by comparing the theoretical results with the available experimental data on E(AGDR). The result DR(pn)= 0.161(42) agrees nicely with the previous experimental results.

## Key claim
The centroid energy of the Anti-analog Giant Dipole Resonance (AGDR) in 208Pb is very sensitively related to the neutron-skin thickness, and by comparing theoretical calculations with experimental data on E(AGDR), the neutron-skin thickness is determined to be ΔRpn = 0.161 ± 0.042 fm, which agrees with previous experimental results.

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pn-RQRPA) calculations based on the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model for 208Pb. They employed a family of density-dependent meson-exchange (DD-ME) effective interactions with symmetry energy at saturation varied from 30 to 38 MeV, plus the DD-ME2 interaction, and computed the energy difference between the AGDR and the Isobaric Analog State (IAS) as a function of the ground-state neutron-skin thickness. The theoretical results were compared with experimental data from Sterrenburg et al. (1980), corrected for mixing with the isovector spin giant dipole resonance (IVSGDR), to extract the neutron-skin thickness.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb: ΔRpn = 0.161 ± 0.042 fm (including theoretical uncertainties)
- Experimental AGDR energy relative to IAS (corrected): E(AGDR) - E(IAS) = 9.12 ± 0.20 MeV, obtained from the original value of 8.97 ± 0.20 MeV (Sterrenburg et al., 1980) with a +0.15 MeV shift to compensate for IVSGDR mixing
- The DD-ME2 effective interaction predicts dipole polarizability αD = 20.8 fm³ for 208Pb, in agreement with the measured value αD = 20.1 ± 0.6 fm³ (Tamii et al., 2011)
- The set of effective interactions used spans symmetry energy at saturation a4 = 30, 32, 34, 36, and 38 MeV, plus DD-ME2 with a4 = 32.3 MeV

## Context
This work extends the method of using giant resonance energies to determine neutron-skin thickness, previously applied to the isovector spin giant dipole resonance (IVSGDR) by Krasznahorkay et al. (1999, 2004) and to the Gamow-Teller Resonance relative to the IAS by Vretenar et al. (2003). The result ΔRpn = 0.161 ± 0.042 fm agrees with values from electric dipole polarizability measurements (Tamii et al., 2011; Piekarewicz et al., 2012) and the PREX parity-violating electron scattering experiment (Abrahamyan et al., 2012), while providing a new method applicable to rare-isotope beams.
