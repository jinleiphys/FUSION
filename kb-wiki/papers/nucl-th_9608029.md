# Resonance model study of strangeness production in pp collisions

> Results for the energy dependence of the elementary kaon production cross sections in proton-proton collisions are reported. Calculations are performed within an extended version of the resonance model which was used for the previous studies of elementary kaon production in pion-nucleon and pion-$Δ$ collisions. Although the model treatment is within the {\it empirical} tree level (observed widths for the resonances are used), it is fully relativistic, and includes all relevant baryon resonances up to 2 GeV. One of the purposes of this study is to provide the results for the simulation codes of subthreshold kaon production in heavy ion collisions. This is the first, consistent study of the elementary kaon production reactions including both $πB$ and $B B$ ($B=N, Δ$) collisions on the same footing. Comparisons are made between the calculated results and the existing semi-empirical parametrizations which are widely used for the simulation codes, as well as the experimental data.

## Key claim

The resonance model, which treats kaons as always arising from baryon resonances decaying to hyperon and kaon channels, can simultaneously reproduce the available experimental data for the total cross sections of the reactions \(pp \to p\Sigma^+ K^0\), \(pp \to p\Sigma^0 K^+\), and \(pp \to n\Sigma^+ K^+\), but the model neglects all interference terms among the amplitudes, which could be important near the reaction threshold.

## Method

The authors extended a previously developed resonance model, which had been applied to \(\pi B \to Y K\) reactions, to the baryon-baryon channels \(B_1 B_2 \to B_3 Y K\) (specifically \(pp \to N Y K\)). The model is a fully relativistic, empirical tree-level calculation that includes all relevant baryon resonances up to 2 GeV (N(1650), N(1710), N(1720), and \(\Delta\)(1920)) and uses observed decay widths. The amplitudes for \(pp \to p\Lambda K^+\) include contributions from \(\pi\), \(\eta\), and \(\rho\)-meson exchange with intermediate resonances, but all interference terms (including those between direct and exchange amplitudes) are neglected.

## Key numbers

*   The best-fit values for the \(\rho NN\) vertex are \(g^2_{\rho NN}/4\pi = 0.74\) and a cut-off parameter \(\Lambda_\rho = 920\) MeV.
*   The coupling constant for the \(\pi NN\) vertex is \(g^2_{\pi NN}/4\pi = 14.4\) with a cut-off of 1050 MeV.
*   The coupling constant for the \(K\Lambda N(1710)\) vertex is \(g^2_{K\Lambda N(1710)}/4\pi = 3.78\) with a cut-off of 800 MeV.
*   The coupling constant for the \(K\Sigma N(1710)\) vertex is \(g^2_{K\Sigma N(1710)}/4\pi = 4.66\) with a cut-off of 800 MeV.
*   The tensor coupling ratio for the \(\rho NN\) vertex is \(\kappa = f_{\rho NN}/g_{\rho NN} = 6.1\).

## Context

This work extends the resonance model previously applied to \(\pi B \to Y K\) reactions (Tsushima et al., 1996) to the baryon-baryon channels \(pp \to N Y K\), providing the first consistent study of both \(\pi B\) and \(BB\) collisions on the same footing. The results are compared with the semi-empirical parametrizations of Randrup and Ko (1987) and Schurmann and Zwermann (1987), which show drastically different energy dependences, especially near threshold, while the present results are similar to those of Sibirtsev (1995) and Li and Ko (1995). The calculated cross sections are intended for use in simulation codes for subthreshold kaon production in heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that future work should incorporate in-medium modifications.
