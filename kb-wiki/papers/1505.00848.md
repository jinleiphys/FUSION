# Phonons, pions and quasi-long-range order in spatially modulated chiral condensates

> We investigate low-energy fluctuations in the real kink crystal phase of dense quark matter within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The modulated chiral condensate breaks both the translational symmetry and chiral symmetry spontaneously, which leads to the appearance of phonons and pions that are dominant degrees of freedom in the infrared. Using the Ginzburg-Landau expansion near the Lifshitz point, we derive elastic free energies for phonons and pions in dependence on the temperature and chemical potential. We show that the one-dimensional modulation is destroyed by thermal fluctuations of phonons at nonzero temperature, and compute the exponent that characterizes the anisotropic algebraic decay of quasicondensate correlations at long distance. We also estimate finite-volume effects on the stability of the real kink crystal and briefly discuss the possibility of its existence in neutron stars.

## Key claim

Thermal fluctuations of phonons destroy the one-dimensional spatial modulation of the chiral condensate at any nonzero temperature, leading to quasi-long-range order rather than true long-range order, with the exponent characterizing the algebraic decay of correlations depending on temperature, chemical potential, and the elastic constants of the phonon mode.

## Method

The authors perform a Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to sixth order near the Lifshitz point, where the coefficients are functions of temperature and chemical potential computed with a three-momentum cutoff regularization. They solve the Ginzburg-Landau equation analytically using the real kink crystal solution (an elliptic function) and numerically determine the elliptic parameter by minimizing the free energy per period. The phonon and pion elastic free energies are derived by expanding the Ginzburg-Landau free energy around the modulated condensate, and the phonon eigenvalue equation is solved numerically via Bloch's theorem with a Fourier decomposition.

## Key numbers

- Lifshitz point located at (T, μ) = (81.4, 276.4) MeV
- Cutoff parameter Λ = 632 MeV and coupling GΛ² = 2.173
- Period L of the real kink crystal at (T, μ) = (70, 286.0) MeV is 7.71 fm
- Period L at (T, μ) = (70, 286.5) MeV is 6.40 fm
- The exponent η_c = Q²T/(8π√(BC)) characterizes the algebraic decay of order parameter correlations, with Q the wave number and B, C the elastic coefficients

## Context

This work extends the mean-field studies of inhomogeneous chiral condensation in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Nickel (2009) and others, who established the existence of one-dimensionally modulated chiral condensates but did not include collective fluctuations. The analysis parallels prior work on fluctuations in smectic-A liquid crystals by de Gennes and in Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov phases of fermionic superfluids by Radzihovsky (2008, 2011) and Samokhin (2010, 2011). The results demonstrate that the Landau-Peierls instability applies to the real kink crystal phase, and the quasi-long-range order with algebraic decay of correlations is analogous to the Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless phase in two-dimensional systems. The findings enable future studies of whether such modulated phases can exist in neutron stars, where finite-volume effects may suppress the phonon fluctuations.
