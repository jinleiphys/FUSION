# The sigma meson in a nuclear medium through two pion photoproduction

> We present theoretical results for (gamma,pi0 pi0) and (gamma,pi+- pi0) production on nucleons and nuclei in the kinematical region where the scalar isoscalar pi pi amplitude is influenced by the sigma pole. The final state interaction of the pions modified by the nuclear medium produces a spectacular shift of strength of the pi0 pi0 invariant mass distribution induced by the moving of the sigma pole to lower masses and widths as the nuclear density increases.

# The sigma meson in a nuclear medium through two pion photoproduction

## Key claim

The shift of strength in the $\pi^0\pi^0$ invariant mass distribution toward lower masses in nuclear photoproduction provides a clear experimental signature of the modification of the $\sigma$ meson in nuclear matter, specifically a decrease of the $\sigma$ pole mass and width with increasing nuclear density. This effect is visible in the $(\gamma,\pi^0\pi^0)$ channel but not in $(\gamma,\pi^{\pm}\pi^0)$ because the latter cannot be in isospin I=0.

## Method

The authors model the elementary $(\gamma,\pi\pi)$ reaction on nucleons using tree-level diagrams with couplings to mesons, nucleons, and the resonances $\Delta(1232)$, $N^*(1440)$, $N^*(1520)$, and $\Delta(1700)$, following the approach of Nacher (2000). Final state interaction of the two pions is implemented by factorizing the on-shell tree-level $\pi N \to \pi\pi N$ and $\pi\pi \to \pi\pi$ amplitudes in loop functions, using the $\pi\pi$ scattering matrix in isospin I=0 from Chiang (1998). For nuclei, the cross section is calculated using many-body techniques with an eikonal approximation for pion distortion, absorption, and quasielastic collisions based on a well-tested model for the pion selfenergy in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The $\sigma$ pole moves to lower masses and widths as nuclear density increases, producing a shift of strength in the $\pi^0\pi^0$ invariant mass distribution for $^{12}C$ and $^{208}Pb$ targets.
- The shift in the $(\gamma,\pi^0\pi^0)$ channel is remarkably similar to the one found in the measurements of Messchendorp (2002).
- No shift is seen in the $(\gamma,\pi^{\pm}\pi^0)$ channel because $\pi^{\pm}\pi^0$ are not allowed to be in isospin I=0.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of Chiang (1998), which showed a shift of strength in the $\pi\pi$ amplitude in the $\sigma$ channel toward low $\pi\pi$ invariant masses at finite nuclear density. The $(\gamma,\pi\pi)$ reaction is better suited than the $(\pi,\pi\pi)$ reaction for investigating $\pi\pi$ modification in nuclear matter because photons are not distorted by the nucleus and can probe higher densities. The results enable experimental tests of the $\sigma$ meson's nature as a dynamically generated $\pi\pi$ resonance versus a $q\overline{q}$ state, and provide a direct comparison with the data of Messchendorp (2002).
