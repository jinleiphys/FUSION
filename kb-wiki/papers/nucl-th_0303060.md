# Limitation of energy deposition in classical N body dynamics

> Energy transfers in collisions between classical clusters are studied with Classical N Body Dynamics calculations for different entrance channels. It is shown that the energy per particle transferred to thermalised classical clusters does not exceed the energy of the least bound particle in the cluster in its ``ground state''. This limitation is observed during the whole time of the collision, except for the heaviest system.

## Key claim

Energy per particle transferred to thermalised classical clusters in Classical N Body Dynamics collisions does not exceed the energy of the least bound particle in the cluster in its ground state, with this limitation holding throughout the collision time except for the heaviest system (100+100 particles) where pre-formed clusters remain close enough together to inhibit particle escape.

## Method

Classical N Body Dynamics simulations were performed using a third-degree polynomial two-body potential with finite-range attractive and short-range repulsive parts, similar to a Lennard-Jones potential. An adaptative stepsize fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm was used to evolve Newtonian equations of motion for systems of 13+13, 18+50, 34+34, 50+50, and 100+100 particles at center-of-mass energies per particle ranging from 25 to 120 Energy Simulation Units (E.S.U.). Clusters were identified using the Minimum Spanning Tree method at final simulation time (200 T.S.U.) and the Early Cluster Recognition Algorithm for time evolution studies.

## Key numbers

- For the 13+13 system at E_cm/N = 25 E.S.U., the binding energy of the fused cluster (N=26) is -73.30 E.S.U. and the least bound particle energy is -50.86 E.S.U.
- For the 50+50 system at E_cm/N = 30 E.S.U., the binding energy of the fused cluster (N=100) is -98.93 E.S.U. and the least bound particle energy is -62.46 E.S.U.
- For the 100+100 system, the estimated least bound particle energy for the fused cluster (N=200) is -65.00 E.S.U.
- The average excitation energy per particle is limited around 40 E.S.U. for all systems except the 100+100 system, where the maximum reached is 50 E.S.U. at E_cm/N = 60 E.S.U.
- Thermalisation of clusters is reached at approximately 50 T.S.U. for the 34+34 and 50+50 systems at E_cm = 90 E.S.U., and at approximately 35 T.S.U. at E_cm = 120 E.S.U.
- The energy difference between beginning and ending simulation time is lower than 0.001%

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of maximum energy storage in equilibrated nuclei, which previously focused on critical temperatures around 16 MeV from equation of state calculations (Bonche, Levit, and Vautherin, 1984, 1986) without considering reaction mechanism limitations. The observed saturation of excitation energy in classical clusters provides a potential explanation for experimental observations of limited excitation energies in primary fragments from Xe+Sn collisions (Marie, 1998; Hudan) and the saturation of temperature values around 5 MeV using isotope ratio methods (Ma, 1998; Pochodzalla, 1995). The results contradict experimental claims of energy deposits up to 22.5 A.MeV (Borderie, 1996; Ma, 1998), suggesting that calorimetry analyses in those works may overestimate thermal energy. The study enables future investigation of whether quantum effects and Coulomb interaction modify this classical limitation when applied to nuclear physics.
