# On lightest baryon and its excitations in large-N 1+1-dimensional QCD

> We study baryons in multicolour 1+1D QCD via Rajeev's gauge-invariant reformulation as a non-linear classical theory of a bilocal meson field constrained to lie on a Grassmannian. It is known to reproduce 't Hooft's meson spectrum via small oscillations around the vacuum, while baryons arise as topological solitons. The lightest baryon has zero mass per colour in the chiral limit; we find its form factor. It moves at the speed of light through a family of massless states. To model excitations of this baryon, we linearize equations for motion in the tangent space to the Grassmannian, parameterized by a bilocal field U. A redundancy in U is removed and an approximation is made in lieu of a consistency condition on U. The baryon spectrum is given by an eigenvalue problem for a hermitian singular integral operator on such tangent vectors. Excited baryons are like bound states of the lightest one with a meson. Using a rank-1 ansatz for U in a variational formulation, we estimate the mass and form factor of the first excitation.

## Key claim

The lightest baryon in large-N 1+1D QCD has exactly zero mass per colour in the chiral limit of massless quarks, and moves at the speed of light through a continuous family of degenerate massless states; the paper derives an eigenvalue problem for excited baryons (baryon-meson bound states) but the numerical estimate for the first excitation mass is not expected to be accurate because the consistency condition from the u=0 approximation was not imposed in the variational calculation.

## Method

The author uses Rajeev's gauge-invariant reformulation of multicolour QCD_{1+1} as a classical non-linear theory of a bilocal meson field M constrained to lie on a Grassmannian phase space, where baryons arise as topological solitons. The lightest baryon ground state is found exactly in the chiral limit using a rank-1 separable ansatz M = -2 ψ ψ^†. To model excitations, the equations of motion are linearized in the tangent space to the Grassmannian around the baryon ground state, parameterized by a bilocal field U; a gauge redundancy in U is removed and the u=0 approximation (setting valence quark corrections to zero) is made to separate variables, yielding an eigenvalue problem for a hermitian singular integral operator on the remaining field U^{+-}. A rank-1 variational ansatz U^{+-} = φ η^† is then used to estimate the mass and form factor of the first excited baryon.

## Key numbers

- The lightest baryon has zero mass per colour in the chiral limit (m → 0, holding ̃g fixed).
- The valence quark momentum distribution in the lightest baryon is P^{-1} exp(-p/P) dp, where P is the mean null-momentum per colour of the baryon.
- The soliton has a size ∼ P^{-1} where P is the baryon momentum per colour.
- The estimated mass of the first excited baryon is 0.3 ̃g N (not expected to be accurate or an upper bound).
- For comparison, the first excited meson mass in the chiral limit from 't Hooft's work is about 1.4 ̃g.
- The squared masses of 't Hooft's mesons grow linearly as M_n^2 ∼ ̃g^2 n, with eigenstates approximately χ_n(ξ) ≈ i^{n-1} sin(nπ ξ) for μ^2 = 0.

## Context

This work extends the soliton picture of baryons in large-N QCD_{1+1} developed by Rajeev (2000s) and earlier work by the author (Krishnaswami, 2008) on the baryon ground state, providing a framework for excited baryons analogous to 't Hooft's meson equation (1974). The approach enables a systematic study of baryon resonances (nucleon resonances P_{11}, D_{13}, S_{11}, D_{15} etc.) in the large-N limit, though the numerical estimates here are preliminary; a more careful treatment of the consistency condition is needed for quantitative results. The paper also notes that away from the chiral limit, the baryon ground state becomes massive with mass^2 ∼ ̃g^2 √(π ν / 3) for small ν = m^2/̃g^2, containing sea quarks and antiquarks as studied by Bogoliubov (1990s).
