# Elastic proton scattering on tritium below the n-$^{3}{He}$ threshold

> Elastic proton scattering on ${}^{3}{H}$ nucleus is studied inbetween p-% ${}^{3}{H}$ and n-${}^{3}{He}$ thresholds, in the energy region where $α$-particles first excited state is inbeded in the continuum. For this aim Faddeev-Yakubovski equations are solved in configuration space, fully considering effects due to the isospin breaking as well as rigorously treating Coulomb interaction. Different realistic nuclear Hamiltonians are tested, elucidating open problems in nuclear interaction description.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that none of the six realistic nuclear Hamiltonians tested can simultaneously reproduce the shape of the near-threshold J^π=0+ resonance in p-³H elastic scattering and the ground state binding energy of the alpha particle, despite fully accounting for isospin breaking and Coulomb effects in the Faddeev-Yakubovski equations.

## Method

The four-nucleon scattering problem was solved using Faddeev-Yakubovski equations in configuration space, with the total wave function decomposed into 18 amplitudes (12 of type K for 3+1 particle channels and 6 of type H for 2+2 particle channels). The formalism included full isospin-symmetry breaking by incorporating total isospin T=1 and T=2 states in the partial wave basis, allowing separation of the mirror p-³H and n-³He channels. Six different realistic nuclear Hamiltonians were tested: AV18, INOY, ISUJ, I-N³LO, AV18+UIX, and I-N³LO+UIX*.

## Key numbers

- Proton laboratory energies studied: up to 1 MeV (below the n-³He threshold)
- INOY potential: singlet scattering length a₀ = -37.4(2) fm, triplet scattering length a₁ = 5.37(1) fm
- AV18 potential: singlet scattering length a₀ = -15.5(1) fm, triplet scattering length a₁ = 5.79(1) fm
- AV18+UIX potential: singlet scattering length a₀ = -23.6(2) fm, triplet scattering length a₁ = 5.47(1) fm
- I-N³LO potential: singlet scattering length a₀ = -19.6(2) fm, triplet scattering length a₁ = 5.85(1) fm
- I-N³LO+UIX* potential: singlet scattering length a₀ = -22.9(2) fm, triplet scattering length a₁ = 5.59(1) fm
- ISUJ potential: singlet scattering length a₀ = -35.5(2) fm, triplet scattering length a₁ = 5.39(1) fm
- Experimental ⁴He binding energy: 28.30 MeV; INOY gives 29.08 MeV, AV18 gives 24.23 MeV, AV18+UIX gives 28.47 MeV
- The resonance position relative to p-³H threshold is roughly less than 100 keV (ħ²/(2μa₀²))
- A 170 keV reduction in alpha particle binding energy (INOY to ISUJ) shifts the resonant scattering length by almost 2 fm

## Context

This work extends prior Faddeev-Yakubovski calculations by Lazauskas (2004) and Deltuva (2006, 2008) on n-³H scattering, which showed that realistic Hamiltonians underpredict elastic cross sections at resonance by about 10%. The study directly addresses the suggestion by Hofmann and Hale (2005) that the ⁴He continuum can be used to fine-tune three-nucleon interactions. The results enable future experimental tests of charge symmetry breaking in NN S-waves and isospin structure of three-nucleon forces, with new p-³H differential cross section measurements recently begun at Fudan University (Shanghai) for proton energies from 1.4 to 3.4 MeV.
