# Recent Progress in Nuclear Astrophysics

> The manuscript reviews progress achieved in recent years in various aspects of nuclear astrophysics, including stellar nucleosynthesis, nuclear aspects of supernova collapse and explosion, neutrino-induced reactions and their possible role in the supernova mechanism and nucleosynthesis, explosive hydrogen burning in binary systems, and finally the observation of $γ$-rays from supernova remnants.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes progress in nuclear astrophysics as of 1999, but explicitly notes that simulations of core collapse and supernova explosion mechanisms remain "far from being completely understood and robustly modelled" despite advances in weak interaction rates, neutrino opacities, and convective simulations.

## Method

The paper reviews recent theoretical and experimental developments in nuclear astrophysics, focusing on type II supernova physics: weak interaction rates for core collapse (electron capture and beta decay) calculated via large-scale shell model diagonalization in model spaces of 10 million or more configurations; neutrino opacities in dense matter evaluated using random phase approximation with nucleon-nucleon correlations; and r-process nucleosynthesis modeling in the neutrino-driven wind scenario. The review also discusses observational tests including supernova SN1987A lightcurve analysis and neutrino detection capabilities of Superkamiokande.

## Key numbers

- The Chandrasekhar mass limit for iron core collapse is about 1.44 solar masses
- Neutrino trapping density is rho_trap approximately 4 x 10^11 g/cm^3
- Nuclear density is rho_N approximately 10^14 g/cm^3
- Shell model electron capture rates are reduced by more than an order of magnitude compared to FFN rates for Y_e less than 0.47
- Neutrino temperatures in the delayed mechanism: T = 8 MeV for nu_x neutrinos, T approximately 5.6 MeV for anti-nu_e, T = 4 MeV for nu_e, corresponding to average energies of 25 MeV, 16 MeV, and 11 MeV respectively
- The 3-body reaction alpha+alpha+n -> Be-9 has Q-value 1.57 MeV
- SN1987A produced 0.075 solar masses of Co-56
- Neutrino-induced capture rates on waiting-point nuclei are of order 5 s^-1
- Upper bounds on neutrino fluence: F less than or equal to 0.045 at A approximately 130 and less than or equal to 0.030 at A approximately 195
- The r-process requires neutron-to-seed ratio n/s of at least 100 for successful synthesis up to A approximately 200

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Burbidge, Burbidge, Fowler and Hoyle (1957) and Cameron (1957) on stellar nucleosynthesis, and the FFN weak interaction rate compilations of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman. It updates the presupernova electron capture and beta decay rates using modern large-scale shell model calculations that reproduce measured Gamow-Teller strength distributions, correcting systematic misplacements of GT centroids in the FFN parametrization. The review enables more reliable supernova collapse simulations and tests of the neutrino-driven wind model for r-process nucleosynthesis, while noting that open questions remain about the entropy values achievable in supernova models and the underproduction of abundances near A approximately 115.
