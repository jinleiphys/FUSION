# Parametrization of polarized parton distributions

> We report the polarized parton distributions proposed by the Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration (AAC). Using parametrized distributions at Q^2=1 GeV^2 and measured A_1 data, we determine optimum polarized distributions in the leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO). We find that the polarized antiquark distribution is not well determined particularly at small x. It could lead to a rather small quark spin content in comparison with usually-quoted values of 10-30%. In our analysis, it varies from 5% to 28% depending on the small-x extrapolation. It is necessary to have small-x (<10^{-3}) data for precise determination. In addition, the large-x region should be also studied for Δq-bar, which cannot be determined solely by g_1. We propose three sets of distributions as the longitudinally polarized parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The quark spin content is not well constrained by existing inclusive spin asymmetry data, varying from 5% to 28% depending on the small-x extrapolation of the polarized antiquark distribution, which cannot be fixed by present measurements of the structure function g1 alone.

## Method

The Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration (AAC) parametrized polarized parton distributions at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 using a functional form where each polarized distribution is an unpolarized distribution multiplied by an x-dependent function with four parameters. They performed a chi-squared minimization against 375 data points of the spin asymmetry A1 (with Q^2 > 1 GeV^2) using the MINUIT subroutine, evolving distributions to experimental Q^2 points via DGLAP equations in both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in the MSbar scheme.

## Key numbers

- Minimum chi-squared values: chi^2/d.o.f. = 322.6/360 for LO and 300.4/360 for NLO, using 375 data points with Q^2 > 1 GeV^2
- Quark spin content Delta-Sigma = 0.201 (LO) and 0.051 (NLO) from the free fit
- First moments of valence distributions fixed using semi-leptonic decay constants: eta_{u_v} = 0.926 and eta_{d_v} = -0.341
- With fixed small-x parameter alpha_{bar q} = 1.0 (pQCD suggestion): chi^2 = 305.8, Delta-Sigma = 0.241 (NLO)
- With fixed small-x parameter alpha_{bar q} = 1.6 (Regge upper bound): chi^2 = 323.5, Delta-Sigma = 0.276 (NLO)
- Spin content varies from 5% to 28% depending on small-x extrapolation

## Context

This work extends the determination of polarized parton distributions by the AAC group, using inclusive A1 data prior to RHIC-Spin measurements. The analysis finds that the polarized antiquark distribution is poorly constrained compared to other recent analyses such as LSS (Leader-Sidorov-Stamenov) and SMC (Spin Muon Collaboration), particularly at small x where data exist only for x > 0.004. The results enable future Drell-Yan measurements at RHIC to clarify the large-x antiquark distribution and highlight the need for small-x (x < 10^{-3}) data to precisely determine the quark spin content.
