# Analytical structure of the binary collision integral and the ultrarelativistic limit of transport coefficients of an ideal gas

> In this paper we discuss the analytical properties of the binary collision integral for a gas of ultrarelativistic particles interacting via a constant cross-section. Starting from a near-equilibrium expansion over a complete basis of irreducible tensors in momentum space we compute the linearized collision matrices analytically. Using these results we then numerically compute all transport-coefficients of relativistic fluid dynamics with various power-counting schemes that are second-order in Knudsen and/or inverse Reynolds numbers. Furthermore, we also exactly compute the leading-order contribution with respect to the particle mass to the coefficient of bulk viscosity, the relaxation time, and other second-order transport coefficients of the bulk viscous pressure.

## Key claim
The paper analytically computes the linearized collision matrices for a gas of ultrarelativistic particles interacting via a constant isotropic cross-section, enabling numerical determination of all second-order transport coefficients with five significant digits of precision, while also providing exact leading-order mass corrections to the bulk viscosity coefficient and its relaxation time.

## Method
The authors apply a relativistic version of Grad's method of moments (DNMR formalism) to a classical gas of massless particles with hard-sphere interactions. They expand the near-equilibrium distribution function over a complete basis of irreducible tensors in momentum space, compute the linearized collision matrices analytically using a novel anisotropic decomposition of the collision integral in the center-of-momentum frame, and then numerically obtain all transport coefficients for truncation orders up to \(N_0-2=N_1-1=N_2 = 1000\), corresponding to 9014 moments included in the basis. Three different power-counting schemes (DNMR, Inverse Reynolds Dominance, and corrected DNMR) are compared for the second-order transport coefficients.

## Key numbers
- First-order transport coefficients (shear viscosity \(\eta\), bulk viscosity \(\zeta\), and heat conductivity \(\kappa\)) are computed numerically in the ultrarelativistic limit with five significant digits of precision using truncation orders \(N_0-2=N_1-1=N_2 = 1000\) (9014 moments total)
- The leading-order contribution with respect to particle mass \(m_0\) to the bulk viscosity coefficient \(\zeta\) and the relaxation time \(\tau_\Pi\) for the bulk viscous pressure is computed exactly analytically
- The collision matrices \(\mathcal{A}^{(\ell)}_{rn}\) for scalar (\(\ell=0\)), vector (\(\ell=1\)), and tensor (\(\ell=2\)) moments are computed in closed form for massless ultrarelativistic particles with constant isotropic cross-section

## Context
This work extends the hard-sphere collision model to the ultrarelativistic regime, complementing studies of the \(\lambda \phi^4\) scalar field theory collision operator by Mullins (2022) and Denicol (2022), and the Israel particles model by Israel (1963) and Polak model by Polak (1973). The results enable computation of all second-order transport coefficients in relativistic fluid dynamics with controlled precision, and the analytical collision matrices provide a benchmark for testing approximations in kinetic theory. The paper compares three power-counting schemes (DNMR from Denicol (2012), IReD from Wagner (2022), and cDNMR from Ambrus (2022)) for the treatment of non-dynamical moments.
