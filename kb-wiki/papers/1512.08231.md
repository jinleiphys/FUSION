# New insights from 3D simulations of heavy ion collisions

> Viscous relativistic hydrodynamics in 3+1 dimensions is applied to describe heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. We present calculations of observables that are sensitive to the longitudinal structure of the created system. In particular we present pseudo-rapidity correlations and demonstrate their dependence on both the initial state and short range correlations introduced via a microscopic transport description. We further demonstrate the effect of a varying temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio on rapidity dependent flow harmonics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that 3+1 dimensional viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations can describe pseudorapidity correlations and rapidity-dependent flow harmonics in heavy ion collisions, but the agreement with experimental data for the Legendre coefficients of pseudorapidity correlations requires the inclusion of short-range non-flow correlations from a microscopic transport model (UrQMD), not just hydrodynamics alone.

## Method

The authors used the 3+1 dimensional viscous relativistic hydrodynamic code Music with a Monte Carlo Glauber-type initial state that employs the Lexus model to determine fluctuating distributions of net baryon and entropy density in rapidity. They evolved the system with various temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosity to entropy density ratios, performed Cooper-Frye freeze-out at constant energy density of 0.1 GeV/fm^3, computed thermal spectra for all particles with masses up to 2 GeV, and decayed resonances. For pseudorapidity correlations, they additionally used the UrQMD microscopic transport model to describe the low-temperature evolution and include short-range correlations from resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out performed at constant energy density of 0.1 GeV/fm^3
- Thermal spectra computed for all particles with masses up to 2 GeV
- Flow harmonics v2 and v3 computed for charged hadrons at 0-40% centrality (v2) and 10-20% centrality (v3) as functions of pseudorapidity, compared to PHOBOS and STAR data respectively

## Context

This work extends event-by-event 3+1 dimensional viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations (Schenke 2010, Bozek 2011, Molnar 2014, Karpenko 2015) to study observables sensitive to longitudinal structure. It builds on the initial state model of Monnai (2015) and the earlier Music framework (Schenke 2010, 2011; Gale 2012). The pseudorapidity correlation analysis follows the ATLAS collaboration's Legendre coefficient method (ATLAS-CONF-2015-020, ATLAS-CONF-2015-051) and the non-flow treatment of Bozek (2015). The results enable constraints on the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of QCD matter, particularly at forward rapidities, and can help constrain three-dimensional fluctuating initial states when combined with precise measurements over a wide rapidity range.
