# Systematic errors in transport calculations of shear viscosity using the Green-Kubo formalism

> The purpose of this study is to provide a reproducible framework in the use of the Green-Kubo formalism to extract transport coefficients. More specifically, in the case of shear viscosity, we investigate the limitations and technical details of fitting the auto-correlation function to a decaying exponential. This fitting procedure is found to be applicable for systems interacting both through constant and energy-dependent cross-sections, although this is only true for sufficiently dilute systems in the latter case. We find that the optimal fit technique consists in simultaneously fixing the intercept of the correlation function and use a fitting interval constrained by the relative error on the correlation function. The formalism is then applied to the full hadron gas, for which we obtain the shear viscosity to entropy ratio.

## Key claim

The Green-Kubo formalism with exponential fitting of the autocorrelation function is applicable for extracting shear viscosity in systems with both constant and energy-dependent cross-sections, but only for sufficiently dilute systems in the energy-dependent case; the optimal fit technique fixes the intercept of the correlation function and uses a fitting interval constrained by the relative error on the correlation function.

## Method

The authors performed infinite-matter simulations using the SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) transport model, computing the off-diagonal component of the energy-momentum tensor to obtain the autocorrelation function \(C^{xy}(t)\) over 250 fm with timestep \(\delta t = 0.05\) fm and \(N=5000\) timesteps. They fitted the correlation function to a decaying exponential \(C^{xy}(t) = C^{xy}(0) e^{-t/\tau}\), comparing fits with fixed versus floating intercepts and varying the fitting interval cutoff based on relative statistical error (2%, 4%, 6%, 10%). The extracted shear viscosity was validated against semianalytical Chapman-Enskog calculations for simple systems (constant cross-section and energy-dependent pion cross-section with zero resonance lifetimes) before applying the method to a full hadron gas.

## Key numbers

- For a system of massive particles (\(m=138\) MeV) with constant isotropic cross-section \(\sigma=20\) mb at \(T=150\) MeV, the largest deviations between fitted shear viscosity and Chapman-Enskog calculation remained under 8% across all cutoff schemes.
- The correlation function was computed using \(N=5000\) timesteps with \(\delta t = 0.05\) fm, giving a total interval of 250 fm.
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio for the full hadron gas at \(\mu_B=0\) MeV was obtained using a cutoff corresponding to 6% relative error, which reproduced best the available analytical calculations.
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio showed a decreasing profile with temperature, eventually reaching a plateau, with very mild dependence on baryochemical potential (all results within systematic error of each other).

## Context

This work extends prior inconsistent calculations of shear viscosity in the hadron gas phase (Muronga 2003, Demir 2008, Romatschke 2014, Pratt 2016) by identifying systematic errors in the Green-Kubo fitting procedure and providing a reproducible framework. The results are consistent with the shear viscosity to entropy ratio behavior found by Demir (2008) and with the weak dependence on baryochemical potential reported by Itakura (2007). The study enables reliable extraction of transport coefficients from transport models for systems where no analytical Chapman-Enskog equivalent exists, such as the full hadron gas.
