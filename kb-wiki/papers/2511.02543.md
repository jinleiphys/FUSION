# Revisiting the three-kaon interaction and its relation with $K(1460)$

> We test the hypothesis of $K(1460)$ being a hadronic $J^P=0^-$ molecule through nonperturbative $S$-wave $K K\bar K$, $Kππ$, $Kπη$ coupled-channel dynamics in a three-body unitary isobar approach. The scalar two-body coupled-channel resonances $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$, $a_0(980)$ and $K^*_0(700)$ are generated in the subsytems in amplitudes that match phase shifts from experiment. In a first step, previous results in the limit of mass-degenerate, stable $f_0$ and $a_0$ isobars are reproduced. Once the full seven-coupled channel model is switched on, other $S$-matrix effects obscure and modify the resonance signal, including complex thresholds, a two-body cusp, and a triangle singularity at threshold.

## Key claim
The paper tests whether the $K(1460)$ resonance can be explained as a hadronic $J^P=0^-$ molecule arising from three-kaon dynamics, but finds that when the full seven-coupled-channel model is activated (including $K\pi\pi$ and $K\pi\eta$ channels), the resonance signal is obscured and modified by complex thresholds, a two-body cusp, and a triangle singularity at threshold, rather than producing a clean molecular state.

## Method
The authors employ a dynamical coupled-channel (DCC) three-body unitary isobar approach, solving Lippmann-Schwinger type equations for spectator-isobar scattering in the $S$-wave. The two-body subsystems generate scalar resonances ($f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$, $a_0(980)$, $K^*_0(700)$) from coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations with lowest-order chiral dynamics, and the three-body system couples $KK\bar K$, $K\pi\pi$, and $K\pi\eta$ channels through seven spectator-isobar channels ($Kf_0^{K\bar K}$, $Kf_0^{\pi\pi}$, $Ka_0^{K\bar K}$, $Ka_0^{\pi\eta}$, $\bar KK_2^{KK}$, $\pi\kappa^{K\pi}$, $\eta\kappa^{K\pi}$) with particle exchange ($B$-term) required by three-body unitarity.

## Key numbers
- The nominal $KK\bar K$ three-body threshold is approximately 1488 MeV.
- The $K(1460)$ mass and width from LHCb (2017) are $1482.40\pm3.58\pm15.22$ MeV and $335.60\pm6.20\pm8.65$ MeV, respectively.
- In the constituent quark model of Taboada-Nieto (2022), the $K(1460)$ mass was found to be 1512 MeV as a $2^1S_0$ excitation.
- In the quark model of Godfrey (1985) as modified by Pang (2017), the $K(1460)$ mass was determined as 1457 MeV with a two-body partial decay width of 468 MeV.
- In the Faddeev approach of Martinez Torres (2011), a bump at approximately 1420 MeV with a width of approximately 50 MeV was observed in the $T$-matrix modulus squared for total isospin $1/2$.

## Context
This work extends the three-kaon molecular hypothesis for $K(1460)$ previously studied by Martinez Torres (2011) using Faddeev equations and by Zhang (2021) using time-ordered perturbation theory with stable $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ isobars. Unlike those earlier approaches, the present model incorporates full isobar decay dynamics through coupled channels (including $f_0\to\pi\pi$ and $a_0\to\pi\eta$), which introduces additional $S$-matrix effects that obscure the resonance signal. The results enable future lattice QCD studies of $K(1460)$ decay dynamics, similar to recent finite-volume studies of $\pi(1300)$ by Yan (2025), and provide a framework for analyzing upcoming high-precision kaon spectroscopy data from the COMPASS/Amber upgrade at CERN.
