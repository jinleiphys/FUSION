# $NN$ scattering in $^3S_1-^3D_1$ channels of EFT ($\notπ$)

> The closed-form $T$ matrices in the $^3S_1$$-$$^3D_1$ channels of EFT($\not/!/!/pi$) for $NN$ scattering with the potentials truncated at order $\mathcal{O}(Q^4)$ are presented with the nonperturbative divergences parametrized in a general manner. The stringent constraints imposed by the closed form of the $T$ matrices are exploited in the underlying theory perspective and turned into virtues in the implementation of subtractions and the manifestation of power counting rules in nonperturbative regimes, leading us to the concept of EFT scenario. A number of scenarios of the EFT description of $NN$ scattering are compared with PSA data in terms of effective range expansion and $^3S_1$ phase shifts, showing that it is favorable to proceed in a scenario with conventional EFT couplings and sophisticated renormalization in order to have large $NN$ scattering lengths. The informative utilities of fine tuning are demonstrated in several examples and naturally interpreted in the underlying theory perspective. In addition, some of the approaches adopted in the recent literature are also addressed in the light of EFT scenario.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in nonperturbative EFT for NN scattering, the closed-form T matrices impose constraints that prevent conventional perturbative renormalization via local counterterms, forcing a shift to "EFT scenarios" where subtractions must be performed at the loop-integral level and residual parameters are fixed by boundary conditions rather than by cutoff independence.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the coupled ^3S_1-^3D_1 channels in EFT(π̸) with contact potentials truncated at O(Q^4), obtaining closed-form on-shell T matrices. They parametrize the divergent loop integrals I(E) in a general manner using constants J_n, then analyze the mismatch between these divergences and available counterterms from the truncated potential. The renormalization is implemented by treating the unmatched J_n parameters as physical constants to be fixed by boundary conditions, leading to the concept of an "EFT scenario" comprising both EFT couplings and complementary loop-integral parameters.

## Key numbers

* The inverse scattering length for ^3S_1 is a^{-1}(^3S_1) ≈ 36.4 MeV, and for ^1S_0 is a^{-1}(^1S_0) ≈ -8.3 MeV, used to illustrate the scale hierarchy ε ≈ 1/4 with Λ ∼ m_π.
* The upper scale for EFT(π̸) is set by the pion mass, Λ_(π̸) ∼ m_π, while for pionfull EFT the upper scale is Λ_(π) ∼ m_ρ.
* At truncation order Δ=4, the highest powers of p^2 in the polynomials are: ω̄_{D_0}=4, ω̄_{D_1}=ω̄_{N_0}=3, ω̄_{N_1}=ω̄_{D_sd}=2.
* The nonperturbative running couplings at order Δ=2 have IR fixed points C_{0;ss}^{(IR)}=α_0, C_{2;ss}^{(IR)}=α_2/2, C_{2;sd}^{(IR)}=β, and UV fixed points all zero; they blow up at finite J_{3;±} = (α_2 ± √(α_2^2+4β^2))/(2β^2).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Yang 2003, 2007, 2009, 2011) on nonperturbative renormalization in EFT(π̸) and addresses the long-standing controversy between two main approaches: one insisting on standard subtraction algorithms around a leading nonperturbative component (Kaplan-Savage-Wise 1998, van Kolck 1999), and the other following Lepage's method of nonperturbative treatment with cutoff-based renormalization. The closed-form T matrices enable a systematic comparison of different EFT scenarios with partial-wave analysis data, and the framework can be applied to other non-relativistic systems dominated by short-range interactions.
