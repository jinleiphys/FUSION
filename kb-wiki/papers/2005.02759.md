# Was GW170817 a canonical neutron star merger? Bayesian analysis with a third family of compact stars

> We investigate the possibility that GW170817 has not been the merger of two conventional neutron stars (NS) but involved at least one if not two hybrid stars with a quark matter core which might even belong to a third family of compact stars. To this end, we develop a Bayesian analysis method for selecting the most probable equation of state (EoS) under a set of constraints from compact star physics, which now also include the tidal deformability from GW170817 and the first result for the mass and radius determination for PSR J0030+0451 by NICER. We apply this method for the first time to a two-parameter family of hybrid EoS based on the DD2 model with nucleonic excluded volume for hadronic matter and the color superconducting generalized nlNJL model for quark matter. The model has a variable onset of deconfinement and can mimic the effects of pasta phases with the possibility of a third family of hybrid stars in the mass-radius ($M-R$) diagram. The main findings of this study are that: 1) the presence of multiple configurations for a given mass (twins) corresponds to a set of disconnected lines in the diagram of tidal deformabilities for binary mergers, so that merger events from the same mass range may result in a probability landscape with different peak positions; 2) the Bayesian analysis with the above observational constraints favors an early onset of the deconfinement transition, at masses of $M_{\rm onset}\le 0.8~M_\odot$ with a $M-R$ relationship that in the range of observed neutron star masses is almost indistinguishable from that of a soft hadronic APR EoS; 3) a few yet fictitious measurements of the NICER experiment with a $1σ$ range that is half of the present value and different mass and radius would change the posterior likelihood so that hybrid EoS with a phase transition onset in the range $M_{\rm onset} = 1.1 - 1.6~M_\odot $ would be favored.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the GW170817 merger event involved at least one hybrid star with a quark matter core rather than two conventional neutron stars, finding that current observational constraints favor an early onset of deconfinement at masses below 0.8 solar masses, but that more precise NICER measurements could shift the posterior likelihood to favor hybrid equations of state with a phase transition onset between 1.1 and 1.6 solar masses.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-parameter family of hybrid equations of state combining the DD2 model with nucleonic excluded volume for hadronic matter and a color superconducting generalized nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nlNJL) model for quark matter, joined by a replacement interpolation method that mimics pasta phase effects. They performed a Bayesian analysis incorporating constraints from the tidal deformability of GW170817, the mass and radius determination of PSR J0030+0451 from NICER, and the lower limit on maximum mass from PSR J0740+6620, using the onset chemical potential for deconfinement and the mixed phase pressure parameter as free variables.

## Key numbers

- The Bayesian analysis favors an onset mass for deconfinement of M_onset ≤ 0.8 solar masses under current observational constraints
- A fictitious NICER measurement with 1σ range half the present value would favor hybrid EoS with onset masses in the range M_onset = 1.1 - 1.6 solar masses
- The hadronic DD2p40 model includes a nucleonic excluded volume of 4 fm^3
- The CSS extrapolation uses a matching energy density of ε_x = 818 MeV/fm^3 for the constant speed of sound extension
- The maximum mass constraint comes from pulsars PSR J0740+6220 and PSR J0348+0432, with the latter having a precisely measured mass

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses by Ayriyan et al. (2018) that considered tidal deformability from GW170817, now incorporating the first NICER mass and radius results for PSR J0030+0451 from Miller et al. (2019) and Riley et al. (2019). The study addresses the tension between GW170817 data favoring smaller radii (e.g., R = 11.0 km from Capano et al., 2019) and NICER results suggesting larger radii (R = 13.02 km from Miller et al., 2019), proposing that mass twin solutions from a third family of compact stars could resolve this discrepancy. The work enables future analyses to determine whether upcoming NICER measurements will favor hybrid stars with quark matter cores over purely hadronic neutron stars.
