# Quark matter in Neutron Stars within the Field Correlator Method

> We discuss the appearance of quark matter in neutron star cores', focussing on the possibility that the recent observation of a very heavy neutron star could constrain free parameters of quark matter models. For that, we use the equation of state derived with the Field Correlator Method, extended to the zero temperature limit, whereas for the hadronic phase we use the equation of state obtained within both the non-relativistic and the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory. We find a strong dependence of the maximum mass both on the value of the q\bar q interaction V_1, and on the gluon condensate G_2, for which we introduce a dependence on the baryon chemical potential mu_B. We find that the maximum masses are consistent with the observational limit for not too small values of V_1.

## Key claim

The maximum mass of neutron stars containing quark matter, computed using the Field Correlator Method (FCM) equation of state, is consistent with the observational limit of 2.01 solar masses only for values of the quark-antiquark potential V1 greater than about 0.1 GeV, with the exact threshold depending on the hadronic equation of state used.

## Method

The authors used the Field Correlator Method to derive the quark matter equation of state at zero temperature and finite baryon chemical potential, incorporating a density-dependent gluon condensate G2 based on extrapolations from two-color QCD and QCD sum rules. For the hadronic phase, they employed both non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory with the Argonne v18 potential plus Urbana three-body forces, and relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) theory with the Bonn A potential. The hadron-quark phase transition was treated using the Maxwell construction, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The observed neutron star PSR J0348+0432 has mass M = 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses, used as the observational constraint
- For the BHF hadronic EoS with V1 = 0.01 GeV, the maximum mass is 1.69 solar masses (with asterisk indicating stable hybrid star with pure quark core)
- For the BHF hadronic EoS with V1 = 0.1 GeV, the maximum mass is 1.95 solar masses (no asterisk, no stable pure quark core)
- For the BHF hadronic EoS with V1 = 0.2 GeV, the maximum mass is 2.03 solar masses
- For the DBHF hadronic EoS with V1 = 0.01 GeV, the maximum mass is 1.69 solar masses (with asterisk)
- For the DBHF hadronic EoS with V1 = 0.1 GeV, the maximum mass is 1.95 solar masses (with asterisk)
- For the DBHF hadronic EoS with V1 = 0.2 GeV, the maximum mass is 2.31 solar masses
- The gluon condensate at zero chemical potential is G2 = 0.012 ± 0.006 GeV^4 from QCD sum rules
- The transition baryon chemical potential for BHF with V1 = 0.01 GeV is 0.986 GeV, with density 1.22 times saturation density
- The transition baryon chemical potential for BHF with V1 = 0.3 GeV is 2.408 GeV, with density 8.4 times saturation density
- The largest V1 producing stable neutron stars with a quark core is approximately 0.095 GeV for BHF and 0.12 GeV for DBHF

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Plumari et al., 2008) of the FCM for neutron stars by introducing a density-dependent gluon condensate G2 and systematically exploring the dependence on the quark-antiquark potential V1. The study is motivated by the observation of the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0342 (Antoniadis et al., 2013), which provides a stringent constraint on quark matter models. The FCM approach differs from other quark matter models like the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by incorporating confinement ab initio. The results enable future work to constrain the FCM parameters using neutron star observations, and suggest that the density dependence of the gluon condensate, motivated by two-color QCD studies (Zhitnitsky et al., 2002), can significantly affect the stability of hybrid stars.
