# Exactly solvable three-body Calogero-type model with translucent two-body barriers

> A new exactly solvable alternative to the Calogero three-particle model is proposed. Sharing its confining long-range part, it contains the mere zero-range two-particle barriers. Their penetrability gives rise to a tunneling, tunable via their three independent strengths. Their variability can control the removal of the degeneracy of the energy levels in an innovative, non-perturbative manner.

# Exactly solvable three-body Calogero-type model with translucent two-body barriers

## Key claim

The authors propose a new exactly solvable three-body Hamiltonian that replaces the impenetrable inverse-square two-body barriers of the Calogero model with zero-range (delta-function) barriers that are partially penetrable, allowing tunneling between particles. The model is Hermitian (unlike a prior non-Hermitian PT-symmetric approach by the same authors) and retains exact solvability while introducing three independent barrier strengths that can control energy level degeneracy in a non-perturbative manner.

## Method

Starting from the Calogero three-body Hamiltonian with harmonic confinement and inverse-square two-body repulsion, the authors regularize the singular barriers by replacing them with a Lorentzian form that becomes a delta function in the zero-width limit. They separate the three-body problem in the usual Calogero coordinates (hyperradius and angular variable), obtaining an angular Schrodinger equation on a circle with six delta-function barriers at positions determined by the two-body coincidence points. The angular equation is solved by matching free-particle solutions across each delta barrier using standard discontinuity conditions, leading to a secular determinant that implicitly defines the angular eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- Energy spectrum formula: \(E_{m,k} = \sqrt{3/2} \, \omega \, (2m+1 + \kappa_k)\), where \(m,k = 0,1,\ldots\) are radial and angular quantum numbers
- For the toy model with a single barrier of strength \(G\): the secular equation is \(\cos \kappa\pi + \sigma + (G \sin \kappa\pi)/(2\kappa) = 0\) with \(\sigma = \pm 1\)
- For three equal barriers with \(G=1\): the lowest two numerical angular eigenvalues are \(\tilde{\kappa} = 1.367840720\) and \(2.199769250\)
- In the limit \(G \to 0\) of vanishing barriers, the standard square-well degeneracy \(\delta(b) \to \delta(a)\) is recovered at every quantum number \(k\)
- In the limit \(G \to \infty\) of very strong repulsion, the Calogero-like impenetrable barrier case is recovered

## Context

This work extends the exactly solvable three-body Calogero model (Calogero, 1971) by replacing its impenetrable inverse-square barriers with penetrable delta-function barriers, enabling particle tunneling that the original model forbids. It contrasts with a prior non-Hermitian PT-symmetric approach by the same authors (Znojil and Tater, 2000) that also introduced tunneling but at the cost of Hermiticity. The construction parallels work by Gangopadhyaya and Sukhatme (2000) who introduced contact terms to preserve solvability when Calogero barriers become attractive. The model enables future phenomenological applications to few-body systems where partial barrier penetrability is desired, such as quark models or systems requiring tunable level degeneracy.
