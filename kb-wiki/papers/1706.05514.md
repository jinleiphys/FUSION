# Formation and decay of resonance state in $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B nuclei. Microscopic three-cluster model investigations

> We study nature of the low-lying resonance states in mirror nuclei $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B. Investigations are performed within a three-cluster model. The model makes use of the hyperspherical harmonics, which provides convenient description of three-cluster continuum. The dominant three-cluster configurations $α+α+n$ and $α+α+p$ in $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B, respectively, are taken into account. Dominant decay channels for all resonance states in $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B are explored. Much attention is paid to the controversial $1/2^{+}$ resonance states in both nuclei. We study effects of the Coulomb interaction on energy and width of three-cluster resonances in the mirror nuclei $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B. We also search for the Hoyle-analogue state which is a key step for alternative way of $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B syntheses in a triple collision of clusters in a stellar environment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the controversial $1/2^{+}$ resonance states in both $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B are genuine three-cluster resonances close to the $\alpha+\alpha+N$ threshold, but their exact energy and width depend strongly on the choice of nucleon-nucleon potential, and the modified Hasegawa-Nagata potential (MHNP) produces fewer and generally wider resonances than the Minnesota potential used in prior work.

## Method

The authors employed the Algebraic three-cluster Model with the Hyperspherical Harmonics Basis (AM HHB), expanding the three-cluster wave function over hyperspherical harmonics and oscillator functions to reduce the many-body Schrodinger equation to a system of linear algebraic equations. They used the modified Hasegawa-Nagata potential (MHNP) with a tuned Majorana parameter ($m=0.4389$) to reproduce the $^{9}$Be ground state binding energy, and calculated resonance parameters by imposing proper boundary conditions on the hyperradial components to extract S-matrix elements for the $\alpha+\alpha+n$ and $\alpha+\alpha+p$ configurations.

## Key numbers

* Ground state of $^{9}$Be ($3/2^{-}$): calculated energy $-1.5743$ MeV (experimental $-1.5735$ MeV).
* $1/2^{+}$ resonance in $^{9}$Be: calculated energy $0.338$ MeV, width $0.168$ MeV (experimental energy $0.111\pm7$ MeV, width $0.217\pm10$ MeV).
* $1/2^{+}$ resonance in $^{9}$B: calculated energy $0.636$ MeV, width $0.477$ MeV (experimental energy approximately $1.9$ MeV, width approximately $0.7$ MeV).
* $5/2^{-}$ resonance in $^{9}$Be: calculated energy $0.897$ MeV, width $2.363\times10^{-5}$ MeV (experimental energy $0.8559\pm1.3$ MeV, width $0.00077\pm0.15$ MeV).
* $3/2^{-}$ resonance in $^{9}$B: calculated energy $0.379$ MeV, width $1.076\times10^{-6}$ MeV (experimental energy $0.277$ MeV, width $0.00054\pm0.21$ MeV).
* Maximum hypermomentum $K_{\max}=14$ for positive parity states and $K_{\max}=13$ for negative parity states; up to $n_{\rho}\leq100$ hyperradial excitations used.
* Oscillator length $b=1.317$ fm selected to minimize the alpha particle bound state energy.

## Context

This work extends the three-cluster model studies of $^{9}$Be and $^{9}$B previously performed by Vasilevsky et al. (2014) using the Minnesota potential, now employing the modified Hasegawa-Nagata potential to improve the description of cluster-cluster interactions. The paper addresses the long-standing controversy over the $1/2^{+}$ resonance states, which have been reported at different energies or even claimed absent in some experiments, and also searches for a Hoyle-analogue state that would enable alternative nucleosynthesis pathways via triple-cluster collisions in stellar environments. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from photodisintegration reactions and provide a benchmark for testing other microscopic cluster models.
