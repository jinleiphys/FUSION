# Mode-by-mode hydrodynamics: ideas and concepts

> The main ideas, technical concepts and perspectives for a mode resolved description of the hydrodynamical regime of relativistic heavy ion collisions are discussed. A background-fluctuation splitting and a Bessel-Fourier expansion for the fluctuating part of the hydrodynamical fields allows for a complete characterization of initial conditions, the fluid dynamical propagation of single modes, the study of interaction effects between modes, the determination of the associated particle spectra and the generalization of the whole program to event-by-event correlations and probability distributions.

## Key claim

A mode-by-mode decomposition of hydrodynamic fields (using a background-fluctuation splitting and Bessel-Fourier expansion) allows for a complete characterization of initial conditions, propagation of single modes, and study of interaction effects, with the hedge that this semi-analytic approach is presented as complementary to full numerical event-by-event simulations rather than replacing them.

## Method

The author splits hydrodynamic fields (enthalpy density, fluid velocity, shear stress) into a rotationally symmetric event-averaged background and a fluctuating part, then expands the fluctuations in Bessel-Fourier modes using Bessel functions \(J_m(k_l^{(m)} r)\) for the radial dependence and Fourier modes \(e^{im\phi}\) for the azimuthal dependence. The linearized hydrodynamic equations are solved for individual modes, yielding coupled ordinary differential equations for the expansion coefficients, and the freeze-out to particle spectra is performed by expanding the logarithm of the single-event spectrum in the same mode coefficients with \(p_T\)-dependent response functions \(\theta_l^{(m)}(p_T)\).

## Key numbers

- For LHC conditions, the radial cutoff is chosen as \(R = 8\) fm for the Bessel-Fourier expansion.
- Triangular flow \(v_3(p_T)\) for charged particles from the mode-by-mode method agrees with ALICE data on the \(2\%\) most central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV.
- Convergence of the mode expansion is quick, with the dominant contribution coming from the lowest radial modes \(l=1\) and \(l=2\).
- From numerical results, fluctuation modes up to \(l_{\text{max}} \approx 5\) can be resolved.

## Context

This work extends the fluid dynamic standard model of heavy ion collisions (reviewed by Heinz (2013), Gale (2013), and Teaney (2009)) by providing a semi-analytic alternative to full numerical event-by-event simulations. It builds on prior characterizations of transverse densities using eccentricities \(\epsilon_{n,m}\) and cumulants (Teaney, 2010) and earlier Bessel-Fourier approaches (Coleman-Smith, 2012), but improves upon them by ensuring that single modes yield positive densities. The formalism enables systematic study of how initial state models and QCD transport properties can be constrained from experimental data, analogous to the program of cosmic microwave background analysis in cosmology.
