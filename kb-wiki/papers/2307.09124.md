# Radiative energy loss of heavy quark through soft gluon emission in QGP

> The Low's theorem is applied to the soft gluon emission from heavy quark scattering in quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The QGP is described by the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM) which reproduces the EoS from lQCD at finite temperature and chemical potential. We show that if the emitted gluon is soft and of long wavelength, the scattering amplitude can be factorized into the scattering part and the emission part and the Slavnov-Taylor identities are satisfied in the leading order. Imposing a proper upper limit on the emitted gluon energy, we obtain the scattering cross sections of charm quark as well as the transport coefficients (momentum drag and diffusion) in the QGP with and without gluon emission.

# Radiative energy loss of heavy quark through soft gluon emission in QGP

## Key claim
The soft gluon approximation (SGA), motivated by Low's theorem in QED, can be applied to heavy quark scattering in QGP and satisfies Slavnov-Taylor identities at leading order, but only when the emitted gluon energy is restricted by a realistic upper limit that accounts for the scattering scale (roughly \(1/\sqrt{-t}\)) rather than the purely kinematic limit.

## Method
The authors apply Low's theorem to factorize the scattering amplitude for \(c+q \rightarrow c+q+g\) and \(c+g \rightarrow c+g+g\) into an elastic 2-to-2 scattering part and a soft gluon emission part, considering only diagrams where the gluon is emitted from external partons. The QGP medium is described by the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM), which provides temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent pole masses and spectral widths for quarks and gluons that reproduce lattice QCD equation of state. The differential cross sections and transport coefficients (momentum drag \(A\) and transverse momentum transfer \(\hat{q}\)) are computed with two upper limits on the emitted gluon energy: the kinematic limit and a more restrictive realistic limit based on the momentum transfer \(t\).

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass taken as 1.5 GeV in the calculations.
- Differential cross sections for \(c+q \rightarrow c+q+g\) and \(c+g \rightarrow c+g+g\) are shown at \(\sqrt{s}=10\) GeV and temperature \(T=200\) MeV.
- The radiative differential cross sections with kinematic and realistic upper limits are almost identical except near \(\cos\theta=1\), where the realistic limit is lower due to small \(t\).
- The 2-to-3 scattering cross section scales as \(\alpha_s^3\) while the 2-to-2 scattering scales as \(\alpha_s^2\), leading to stronger temperature dependence near \(T_c\) where \(\alpha_s\) increases rapidly in DQPM.
- Transport coefficients (drag coefficient \(\eta_D = A/p_c\) and \(\hat{q}/T^3\)) are presented at \(T=200\) MeV as a function of charm quark momentum, showing negligible radiative corrections at low momentum but enhancement at high momentum.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s = \lim_{p_c\to 0} T/(M_c \eta_D)\) is barely affected by radiative corrections.

## Context
This work extends the soft photon approximation of Low (1958) and earlier work by Song (2018, 2022) to soft gluon emission in QGP, building on the DQPM framework developed by Moreau (2019) and applied to heavy quark transport by Berrehrah (2014) and Song (2016). The results agree with full calculations (without SGA) at low emitted gluon momentum, as shown in prior work by Grishmanovskii (2022). The finding that radiative corrections enhance transport coefficients at high momentum may affect the \(R_{\rm AA}\) of \(D\) mesons, which was previously well described using only elastic scattering by Song (2015). The authors note that using a more realistic \(\alpha_s\) for energetic scattering (smaller than the lattice-extracted \(\alpha_s(T)\)) could suppress this enhancement.
