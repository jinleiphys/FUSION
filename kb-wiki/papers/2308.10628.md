# Strangeness production in neutron star matter

> Based on a dynamical model on particle production, the production and fraction of exotic components in neutron star matter are analyzed. It is found that there exists a small fraction of strangeness in twice saturation density matter. For five times saturation density matter, the fraction of strange baryons can be as high as 25-50\%, depending on the equation of state used. The neutron-proton asymmetry of dense matter does not significantly impact the strangeness fraction in neutron star matter. This research provides new insights into the strange components in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that strangeness fractions in neutron star matter can reach 25-50% at five times saturation density, but this result is conditional on the equation of state (EoS) used; the fractions are significantly lower (roughly 4%) at twice saturation density and depend on whether the EoS is stiff or soft.

## Method

The author uses a pure hadronic cascade mode of the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, called AMPT-HC, which includes hadronic mean-field potentials and simulates particle production in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions. Initial nucleon positions are randomly distributed, and momenta are set by the local Thomas-Fermi approximation at zero temperature; the system evolves for 1000 fm/c with 10 test particles per nucleon and 1000 runs. Strangeness production arises from baryon-baryon, meson-baryon, and meson-meson scatterings, with mean-field potentials for baryons (including strange baryons via a quark counting rule) and kaons, but not for pions or the Omega baryon.

## Key numbers

- At twice saturation density (2ρ₀), the fraction of strange baryons is about 4% for symmetric (N = Z) and asymmetric (N/Z = 9) matter with a soft EoS.
- At twice saturation density, the strange baryon fraction increases to roughly 10% for a stiff symmetry energy (γ = 1.5) and to about 20% for a large incompressibility coefficient (k = 380 MeV).
- At five times saturation density (5ρ₀), the fraction of strange baryons is 25-50%, depending on the equation of state used.
- The cubic box used for simulations has dimensions a = b = c = 5 fm, and the time step is 0.2 fm/c with a total simulation time of 1000 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends dynamical transport model studies of strangeness production (e.g., the AMPT model of Lin et al. (2005) and the hyperon puzzle discussed by Vidaña (2018)) to static neutron star matter via box simulations, rather than relying solely on nuclear many-body or effective field theory approaches. The results suggest that nucleon-only many-body approaches may be insufficient above twice saturation density, and they call for clearer hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions to determine bulk properties of neutron star matter. The study also notes that recent measurements of Lambda hyperon and hypernuclei directed flows at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2023) are reigniting interest in hyperon-nucleon interactions.
