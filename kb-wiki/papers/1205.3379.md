# V2 Scaling in PbPb Collisions at 2.76 TeV

> We investigate scaling properties of the elliptical flow parameter v_{2} in PbPb Collisions at 2.76 TeV within a recently introduced new theoretical scheme which accounts for hydrodynamically expanding bulk matter, jets, and the interaction between the two.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that quark number scaling of the elliptic flow parameter \(v_{2}\) holds only at small transverse kinetic energy (\(KE_t\)) in PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV, and is violated at larger \(KE_t\), a result that depends on the specific treatment of fluid-jet interactions in the EPOS2.17v3 model.

## Method
The author uses the EPOS2.17v3 theoretical scheme, which models color flux tubes from hard scatterings that evolve into hydrodynamically expanding bulk matter, jets, and their interactions. String segments with sufficient energy escape as jets, while those inside matter or at the surface receive endpoint partons from the fluid, providing a "push" to transverse momentum. The \(v_{2}\) of identified hadrons (pions, kaons, protons, sigma baryons) is computed for semi-peripheral PbPb collisions (40-50% centrality) at 2.76 TeV, and the scaling representation \(v_{2}/n_{q}\) versus \(KE_t/n_{q}\) is examined.

## Key numbers
- In semi-peripheral PbPb collisions (40-50%) at 2.76 TeV, the proton \(v_{2}\) curve is shifted to the right compared to pions at low \(p_t\), and at higher \(p_t\) the proton result is 50% above the pion one.
- Transverse fluid velocities up to 0.7c are considered in the model.
- Scaling is observed at small \(KE_t\) but violated at high \(KE_t\); for protons the violation is clear, while for sigma baryons the analysis is limited to relatively small \(p_t\) due to statistics.
- Calculations without hadronic cascade show much less mass splitting at low \(p_t\) and no scaling, while high \(KE_t\) results are similar to the full calculation.

## Context
This work extends the study of quark number scaling from AuAu collisions at 200 GeV (where scaling was observed in central collisions but broken in peripheral collisions for \(KE_t > 0.7\) GeV, as shown by PHENIX) to PbPb collisions at 2.76 TeV. It uses the EPOS2.17v3 model introduced by Werner (2012) to cover the full \(p_t\) range from low to high, and contrasts with simple quark coalescence models (e.g., Coalescence 2002, Coalescence 2003). The results enable further tests of the fluid-jet interaction mechanism and its role in scaling violations at LHC energies.
