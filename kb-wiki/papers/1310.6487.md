# Infrared-Improved Soft-wall AdS/QCD Model for Mesons

> We construct and investigate an infrared-improved soft-wall AdS/QCD model for mesons. Both linear confinement and chiral symmetry breaking of low energy QCD are well characterized in such an infrared-improved soft-wall AdS/QCD model. The model enables us to obtain a more consistent numerical prediction for the mass spectra of resonance scalar, pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector mesons. In particular, the predicted mass for the lightest ground state scalar meson shows a good agreement with the experimental data. The model also provides a remarkable check for the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation and a sensible result for the space-like pion form factor.

# Infrared-Improved Soft-wall AdS/QCD Model for Mesons

## Key claim

The authors construct an infrared-improved soft-wall AdS/QCD model that simultaneously incorporates linear confinement and chiral symmetry breaking, yielding consistent mass spectra for all light resonance mesons, including the lightest ground state scalar meson, which previous IR-deformed metric models (Sui, 2009) had predicted with a much smaller mass than experiment.

## Method

The authors start from a 5D AdS action with a background dilaton field, a quartic scalar interaction term, and an IR-modified 5D conformal mass for the bulk scalar field. They parameterize the dilaton field, the z-dependent quartic coupling, and the bulk scalar vacuum expectation value (bVEV) to satisfy both UV conformal recovery and IR quadratic growth, then solve the resulting eigenvalue equations for scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector meson spectra using the shooting method. The five free parameters (quark mass, quark condensate, confinement scale, and two dilaton parameters) are fixed by fitting the pion mass, pion decay constant, rho meson mass, and the slopes of resonance trajectories.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass: m_q = 3.52 MeV, consistent with the PDG mean value of 3.50 MeV for u and d quarks
- Quark condensate: σ^(1/3) = 290 MeV
- Confinement scale parameters: μ_g = 473 MeV, μ_c = 375 MeV
- Dilaton parameter: λ_g = 1.7
- Predicted ground state scalar meson mass: 460 MeV (experimental range: 400–550 MeV)
- Predicted ground state pseudoscalar mass: 139.6 MeV (input, matches π meson)
- Predicted ground state vector meson mass: 775.5 MeV (input, matches ρ meson)
- Predicted ground state axial-vector meson mass: 1095 MeV (experimental: 1230 ± 40 MeV)
- Predicted first excited pseudoscalar: 1465 MeV (experimental: 1300 ± 100 MeV)
- Predicted first excited vector: 1354 MeV (experimental: 1460 ± 25 MeV)
- Predicted first excited scalar: 1359 MeV (experimental range: 1200–1500 MeV)
- Predicted first excited axial-vector: 1626 MeV (experimental: 1647 ± 22 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the soft-wall AdS/QCD approach of Karch et al. (2006) and the IR-deformed metric model of Sui et al. (2009), which successfully described most meson spectra but failed to predict the lightest scalar meson mass. The authors replace the IR-deformed metric with an IR-modified bulk mass and quartic coupling, avoiding the inconvenience of a non-Einstein metric for finite-temperature studies. The model enables future investigation of finite-temperature effects and provides a consistent check of the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation, as well as a sensible prediction for the space-like pion form factor that agrees with data from DESY and Jefferson Lab.
