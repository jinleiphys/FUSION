# Vacuum Properties of Mesons in a Linear Sigma Model with Vector Mesons and Global Chiral Invariance

> We present a two-flavour linear sigma model with global chiral symmetry and vector and axial-vector mesons. We calculate pion-pion scattering lengths and the decay widths of scalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons. It is demonstrated that vector and axial-vector meson degrees of freedom play an important role in these low-energy processes and that a reasonable theoretical description requires globally chirally invariant terms other than the vector meson mass term. An important question for meson vacuum phenomenology is the quark content of the physical scalar f0(600) and a0(980) mesons. We investigate this question by assigning the quark-antiquark sigma and a0 states of our model with these physical mesons. We show via a detailed comparison with experimental data that this scenario can describe all vacuum properties studied here except for the decay width of the sigma, which turns out to be too small. We also study the alternative assignment f0(1370) and a0(1450) for the scalar mesons. In this case the decay width agrees with the experimental value, but the pion-pion scattering length $a_{0}^{0}$ is too small. This indicates the necessity to extend our model by additional scalar degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a two-flavour linear sigma model with global chiral invariance and explicit vector and axial-vector meson degrees of freedom can describe vacuum meson properties, but only if the scalar quark-antiquark fields are assigned to the heavier resonances f0(1370) and a0(1450) (Scenario II) rather than the lighter f0(600) and a0(980) (Scenario I); however, even in Scenario II the pion-pion scattering length a0^0 comes out too small, indicating the need for additional scalar degrees of freedom beyond the quark-antiquark states.

## Method

The authors constructed a globally chirally invariant U(2)R x U(2)L linear sigma model for two quark flavours containing scalar (sigma, a0), pseudoscalar (pion, eta_N), vector (rho, omega), and axial-vector (a1, f1) meson fields, with all terms up to naive scaling dimension four. They computed tree-level decay widths for rho -> pi pi, a1 -> rho pi, sigma -> pi pi, a0 -> eta pi, and f1 -> a0 pi, as well as the pion-pion scattering lengths a0^0 and a0^2, then fitted the six free parameters (m_sigma, m_a0, Z, g2, h1, h2) to experimental data under two different assignments for the scalar meson fields.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV (used to fix phi/Z = f_pi)
- Experimental rho -> pi pi decay width: Gamma_rho->pi pi^(exp) = (149.1 +/- 0.8) MeV (used to fix g2 as function of Z)
- Experimental f1 -> a0 pi decay width: Gamma_f1->a0 pi^(exp) = (8.748 +/- 2.097) MeV (used to fix h2 in Scenario I)
- Experimental a0 -> eta pi decay amplitude: A_a0 eta pi^(exp) = (3330 +/- 150) MeV (from Crystal Barrel data, used as constraint)
- Input meson masses from PDG: m_pi = 139.57 MeV, m_rho = 775.49 MeV, m_a1 = 1230 MeV; m_eta_N = 716 MeV (from eta-eta' mixing with phi ~ -36 degrees)
- For Scenario II (a0 = a0(1450)), the decay f1 -> a0 pi is kinematically forbidden, so h2 cannot be fixed by that channel

## Context

This work extends the globally invariant linear sigma model approach previously presented by the same authors in Mainz (2009) and builds on earlier locally invariant linear sigma model studies by Gasiorowicz and Geffen (1969) and Koch and Rischke (2009). The paper directly addresses the ongoing debate about the quark content of the scalar mesons f0(600) and a0(980), comparing with alternative interpretations that identify these states as tetraquark or molecular configurations (refs2 in the text). The results indicate that a pure quark-antiquark assignment for the light scalars fails, motivating future extensions of the model to include glueball and tetraquark degrees of freedom, which would be necessary for a complete description of scalar meson phenomenology and for studies of hadronic matter at nonzero temperature and density.
