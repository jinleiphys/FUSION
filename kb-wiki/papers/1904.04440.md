# Studying newborn neutron stars by the transient emission after stellar collapses and compact binary mergers

> The formation of neutron stars (NSs), both from collapses of massive stars and mergers of compact objects, can be usually indicated by bright transients emitted from explosively-ejected material. In particular, if the newborn NSs can rotate at a millisecond period and have a sufficiently high magnetic field, then the spin-down of the NSs would provide a remarkable amount of energy to the emitting material. As a result, super-luminous supernovae could be produced in the massive stellar collapse cases, while some unusual fast evolving and luminous optical transients could arise from the cases of NS mergers and accretion-induced collapses of white dwarfs. In all cases, if the dipolar magnetic fields of the newborn NSs can be amplified to be as high as $10^{15}$ G, a relativistic jet could be launched and then a gamma-ray burst can be produced as the jet successfully breaks out from the surrounding nearly-isotropic ejected material.

## Key claim

Newborn neutron stars (NSs) with millisecond periods and sufficiently high magnetic fields can power a variety of transient phenomena through spin-down energy injection, but the specific observational outcome depends critically on the NS's magnetic field strength, spin period, and the mass and opacity of the surrounding ejecta.

## Method

The authors review and synthesize observational data from super-luminous supernovae (SLSNe), long-duration gamma-ray bursts (LGRBs), short-duration gamma-ray bursts (SGRBs), and fast evolving luminous optical transients (FLTs), fitting light curves to infer the spin-down luminosity, magnetic field strength, and initial spin period of the central NS. They compare NS parameters derived from 31 SLSNe with those from LGRB afterglows, and model the thermal emission from merger ejecta (mergernovae) as powered by both radioactive decays and NS spin-down, using the event AT 2017gfo as a case study.

## Key numbers

- SLSNe are about 10–100 times brighter than normal supernovae, with a total radiated energy of ~10^51 erg for a typical event.
- NSs from SLSNe have magnetic field strengths generally higher than the critical field B_c of electron Landau quantization, with spin periods ranging from 1 ms to 10 ms.
- SLSN NSs fall into two subclasses separated empirically at ~5 B_c; the light-curve width scales as Δt_10% ∝ B_p^(-0.68).
- NSs from LGRBs have magnetic fields roughly within (10–300) B_c, generally much higher than those of SLSN NSs.
- The merger ejecta mass for AT 2017gfo in a purely radioactive model is ~0.065 M_⊙; a spin-down-powered model suggests a post-merger NS mass around ~2.6 M_⊙.
- Mergernova luminosities can range from 10^41 erg s^(-1) to 10^44 erg s^(-1).
- AIC ejecta masses are ~10^(-3)–10^(-2) M_⊙.

## Context

This work extends the magnetar central-engine model for SLSNe (Ostriker & Gunn 1971; Woosley et al. 2010; Kasen et al. 2010) and LGRB afterglows (Dai & Lu 1998a, b; Zhang & Meszaros 2001) to NS mergers and white-dwarf accretion-induced collapses. It contrasts the NS spin-down model with the fallback accretion model (Dexter & Kasen 2013) and the pair-instability supernova model (Barkat et al. 1967; Heger & Woosley 2002) for SLSNe, and with the purely radioactive kilonova model (Li & Paczynski 1998; Metzger et al. 2010) for merger transients. The results enable future multi-messenger studies of newborn NSs via optical, X-ray, and gravitational-wave observations, and motivate searches for orphan mergernovae and off-axis GRB afterglows.
