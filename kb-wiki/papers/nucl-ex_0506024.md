# Formation of Light Isotopes by Protons and Deuterons of 3.65 GeV/nucleon on Separated Tin Isotopes

> We measure cross sections for residual nuclide formation in the mass range 6 < A < 97 caused by bombardment with protons and deuterons of 3.65 GeV/nucleon energy of enriched tin isotopes (112-Sn, 118-Sn, 120-Sn, 124-Sn). The experimental data are compared with calculations by the codes FLUKA, LAHET, CEM03, and LAQGSM03. Scaling behavior is observed for the whole mass region of residual nuclei, showing a possible multifragmentation mechanism for the formation of light products (6 < A < 31). Our analysis of the isoscaling dependence also shows a possible contribution of multifragmentation to the production of heavier nuclides, in the mass region 39 < A < 81.

## Key claim

The paper observes scaling behavior in the cross sections for residual nuclide formation across the entire mass range studied (7 ≤ A ≤ 96), which suggests a possible multifragmentation mechanism for light products (7 ≤ A ≤ 30) and also a possible contribution of multifragmentation to the production of heavier nuclides in the mass region 40 ≤ A ≤ 80, rather than solely spallation or evaporation processes.

## Method

Enriched tin isotopes (112Sn, 118Sn, 120Sn, 124Sn) were irradiated with protons and deuterons at 3.65 GeV/nucleon at the Nuclotron and Synchrophasotron of LHE JINR in Dubna. Cross sections for approximately 70 residual products from each target were measured using the induced-radioactivity method, identifying radioactive nuclei by characteristic gamma-rays and half-lives. The experimental data were compared with calculations from the FLUKA, LAHET, CEM03, and LAQGSM03 codes, and isoscaling analysis was performed by fitting the ratio of yields from different target pairs as a function of the isotopic-spin projection t3.

## Key numbers

- Cross section for 7Be production from p + 112Sn: 13.9 ± 1.5 mb; from p + 124Sn: 7.5 ± 0.8 mb
- Cross section for 7Be production from d + 112Sn: 31.1 ± 2.7 mb; from d + 124Sn: 23.1 ± 4.0 mb
- Cross section for 85Sr production from p + 112Sn: 21 ± 1.6 mb; from p + 124Sn: 15.2 ± 0.9 mb
- For the mass region 7 ≤ A ≤ 30, the fitting parameters for B versus ΔN are k = -0.036 ± 0.01 and d = 0.094 ± 0.016
- For the mass region 40 ≤ A ≤ 80, the fitting parameters are k = -0.0008 ± 0.0001 and d = 0.071 ± 0.005
- For the mass region A ≥ 80, the fitting parameters are k = -0.113 ± 0.060 and d = 0.033 ± 0.008
- For 93-96Tc production on the 124Sn/112Sn target pair, the B parameter is 1.07 ± 0.32 for protons and 1.10 ± 0.40 for deuterons
- For 81-86Rb production on the 124Sn/112Sn target pair, the B parameter is 0.94 ± 0.20 for protons and 1.17 ± 0.29 for deuterons
- Mean deviation factor <F> for LAQGSM03 on deuteron-induced reactions ranges from 2.04 ± 1.69 (118Sn) to 3.305 ± 3.08 (112Sn)
- Mean deviation factor <F> for FLUKA on proton-induced reactions ranges from 5.92 ± 4.18 (112Sn) to 8.87 ± 5.42 (120Sn)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Bogatin et al. (1980s) and Balabekyan et al. (2000s) on isoscaling and multifragmentation in nuclear reactions, using separated tin isotopes to probe the isospin dependence of fragment formation. The results are compared with calculations from the FLUKA, LAHET, CEM03, and LAQGSM03 codes, none of which include multifragmentation mechanisms, and the systematic underestimation of light-fragment yields by these codes supports the interpretation that multifragmentation contributes to their production. The findings enable further investigation of the liquid-gas phase transition in hot nuclear matter and the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter, and the isoscaling parameters obtained can be used to constrain models such as the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) of Botvina et al. (1990s).
