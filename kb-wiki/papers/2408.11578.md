# Strong decays of $P-$wave doubly charmed and bottom baryons

> In this work, we investigate the strong decays for $P-$wave excited states of doubly charmed and bottom baryons in the constituent quark model. Our results indicate that some $λ-$mode $Ξ_{cc/bb}(1P)$ and $Ω_{cc/bb}(1P)$ states are relatively narrow, which are very likely to be discovered by future experiments. The light meson emissions for the low-lying $ρ-$mode states are highly suppressed due to the orthogonality of wave functions between initial and final states. Moreover, the strong decay behaviors for doubly charmed and bottom baryons preserve the heavy superflavor symmetry well, where the small violation originates from the finite heavy quark masses and different phase spaces. We hope that present theoretical results for undiscovered doubly charmed and bottom baryons can provide helpful information for future experiments and help us to better understand the heavy quark symmetry.

## Key claim

Some lambda-mode P-wave excited states of doubly charmed and bottom baryons (Xi_cc/bb and Omega_cc/bb) are predicted to be relatively narrow and likely discoverable in future experiments, but this result depends on the specific spin-parity assignment (j=3/2 triplet states are narrow while j=1/2 doublet states are broad) and on the available phase space for decays, which for Omega states is severely restricted.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrodinger equation with a potential model to obtain masses and realistic wave functions for P-wave doubly charmed and bottom baryons (Xi_cc, Xi_bb, Omega_cc, Omega_bb). They then used these wave functions together with quark-chiral field interactions (Yukawa-type coupling) to calculate OZI-allowed two-body strong decays into a final doubly heavy baryon plus a pseudoscalar meson (pion or kaon). The decay amplitudes were computed in the helicity formalism, and the measured mass of Xi_cc++(3621) was used to adjust the zero-point energy of the potential model.

## Key numbers

- Xi_cc(1/2-,1/2) state (j=1/2 doublet): total decay width 296.88 MeV, dominated by Xi_cc pi channel
- Xi_cc(3/2-,1/2) state (j=1/2 doublet): total decay width 271.59 MeV, dominated by Xi_cc* pi channel
- Xi_cc(1/2-,3/2) state (j=3/2 triplet): total decay width 46.38 MeV, saturated by Xi_cc* pi channel
- Xi_cc(3/2-,3/2) state (j=3/2 triplet): total decay width 66.54 MeV, with branching ratios Br(Xi_cc pi, Xi_cc* pi) = 48.47%, 51.53%
- Xi_cc(5/2-,3/2) state (j=3/2 triplet): total decay width 82.05 MeV, with branching ratios Br(Xi_cc pi, Xi_cc* pi) = 45.55%, 54.45%
- Omega_cc(1/2-,1/2) state: total decay width 349.23 MeV, decays to Xi_cc Kbar
- Omega_cc(3/2-,1/2), Omega_cc(1/2-,3/2), Omega_cc(3/2-,3/2), Omega_cc(5/2-,3/2) states: all "Narrow" (no specific numerical widths given for these near-threshold states)
- Xi_bb(1/2-,1/2) state: total decay width 302.94 MeV
- Xi_bb(3/2-,1/2) state: total decay width 311.22 MeV
- Xi_bb(1/2-,3/2) state: total decay width 40.89 MeV
- Xi_bb(3/2-,3/2) state: total decay width 67.38 MeV
- Xi_bb(5/2-,3/2) state: total decay width 83.85 MeV
- All five lambda-mode Omega_bb(1P) states: "Narrow" (no specific numerical widths given)
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV and kaon decay constant f_K = 111 MeV used in calculations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of strong decays for doubly charmed and bottom baryons (Xiao 2017, He 2021) which used simple harmonic oscillator wave functions, and follows the same route as their recent systematic study of bottom-charmed baryons (Song 2023) that combined a potential model with quark-chiral field interactions. The results are compared with P-wave charmed mesons from the Review of Particle Physics (ParticleDataGroup 2024): the broad j=1/2 doublet corresponds to D_0*(2300) and D_1(2430), while the narrow j=3/2 triplet corresponds to D_1(2420) and D_2(2460), demonstrating heavy superflavor symmetry. The narrow Omega_cc(1P) states near the Xi_cc Kbar threshold are analogous to the charmed-strange mesons D_s0*(2137) and D_s1(2460), which have been studied with molecular and coupled-channel approaches (Hwang 2004, MartinezTorres 2011, Mohler 2013, Lang 2014, Ortega 2016, Bali 2017, Albaladejo 2018). The results enable future experimental searches for these narrow doubly heavy baryon states, particularly through isospin-broken pion emissions and radiative decays.
