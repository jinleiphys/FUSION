# Two-particle properties in nuclear matter at finite temperatures

> Correlation effects in nuclear matter at finite temperatures are studied for subnuclear densities ($ρ<ρ_0$) and medium excitation energy, where a nonrelativistic potential approach is possible. A quantum statistical approach is given, where clusters are treated under the influence of a clustered medium treated within a mean field approximation. Spectral functions, in-medium cross sections, and reaction rates are considered as well as the formation of a quantum condensate. In particluar, exploratory calculations are shown with respect to the pseudo gap in the nucleon level density close to the superfluid phase transition. Estimates of isospin singlet pairing and quartetting in nuclear binding energies are given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in subnuclear-density nuclear matter at finite temperatures, strong correlations produce in-medium effects (such as a pseudo gap in the nucleon level density and modified cross sections) that cannot be captured by a quasi-particle picture, and that the transition to superfluidity is not a sharp BCS-like gap opening but a soft crossover with precursor effects above the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors use a quantum statistical approach based on thermodynamic Green's functions and a cluster mean-field approximation. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation (ladder approximation) for the two-particle T matrix using separable representations of the Paris and Bonn potentials, treating the medium via angle-averaged Pauli blocking and single-particle potentials. From the T matrix they compute in-medium deuteron binding energies, scattering phase shifts, nucleon spectral functions, momentum distributions, and average NN cross sections; they also estimate critical temperatures for superfluidity and quartetting via pole conditions and a variational four-particle wave equation.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy drops to zero at the Mott density of approximately \(\rho_0/20\) at \(T=10\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for zero total momentum; a finite total momentum shifts the Mott effect to higher densities.
* At normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and \(T=10\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the nucleon spectral function for low momenta has a width of about \(150\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The critical temperature for the onset of superfluidity (isospin singlet pairing) in symmetric nuclear matter is \(T_c = 1.78\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(\rho = \rho_0\).
* The average in-medium NN cross section is strongly reduced with increasing density, with a parabolic density dependence; the temperature dependence is rather weak.
* The contribution of quartetting to nuclear binding energies is estimated to be approximately \(0.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for symmetric nuclei with mass number \(A \approx 100\).

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach to nuclear matter previously developed by the Rostock group (Roepke 1994/1995, DRS98) and applies it to finite temperatures and subnuclear densities. It builds on the separable potential representations of the Paris and Bonn potentials by Plessas et al. and on the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach of Schmidt, Roepke, and Schulz (1990). The results for the smooth crossover from Bose-Einstein condensation of deuterons to BCS pairing follow the framework of Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink. The paper enables improved transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions (BUU, QMD) by providing in-medium NN cross sections and reaction rates, and it motivates further work on the equation of state below the superfluid critical temperature.
