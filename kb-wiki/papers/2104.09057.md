# Born-Oppenheimer potential energy surfaces for Kohn-Sham models in the local density approximation

> We show that the Born-Oppenheimer potential energy surface in Kohn-Sham theory behaves like the corresponding one in Thomas-Fermi theory up to $o(R^{-7})$ for small nuclear separation $R$. We also prove that if a minimizing configuration exists, then the minimal distance of nuclei is larger than some constant which is independent of the nuclear charges.

## Key claim

The Born-Oppenheimer potential energy surface in Kohn-Sham theory with the local density approximation behaves like the corresponding one in Thomas-Fermi theory up to \(o(R^{-7})\) for small nuclear separation \(R\), provided the nuclear charges satisfy \(z_{\mathrm{min}} \ge \delta_0 z_{\mathrm{max}}\) for some \(\delta_0 > 0\) and \(z_{\mathrm{min}} \ge 1\).

## Method

The author compares the Kohn-Sham LDA energy with the Thomas-Fermi energy by constructing screened potentials and using Solovej's iterative bootstrap argument. The proof relies on Sommerfeld estimates for molecules, a localization procedure using a partition of unity, and semiclassical analysis via coherent states to bound the difference between the two theories. The iterative step improves the range of radii for which the screened potential difference is controlled, starting from an initial estimate at very small nuclear separations.

## Key numbers

* The difference between the Kohn-Sham and Thomas-Fermi Born-Oppenheimer potential energy surfaces satisfies \(|D(\uZ, \uR) - D^{\mathrm{TF}}(\uZ, \uR)| \le C R_{\mathrm{min}}^{-7 + \epsilon}\) for \(R_{\mathrm{min}} \in (0, 4]\) and some \(\epsilon > 0\).
* The initial semiclassical bound gives \(E_{V_{\uR}}(N) \ge \E_{V_{\uR}}^{\mathrm{TF}}(\rho^{\mathrm{TF}}) + D(\rho_0 - \rho^{\mathrm{TF}}) - C Z^{25/11}\).
* The screened potential difference on \(\partial A_r\) is bounded by \(\sup_{x \in \partial A_r} |\Phi_r^{\mathrm{TF}}(x) - \Phi_r(x)| \le C_1 Z^{49/36 - a} r^{1/12}\) with \(a = 1/198\).
* The exterior kinetic energy satisfies \(\tr(-\Delta \eta_r \gamma_0 \eta_r) \le C(r^{-7} + \lambda^{-2} r^{-5})\) for any \(\lambda \in (0, 1/2]\).
* The exterior TF density satisfies \(\int (\rho_r^{\mathrm{TF}})^{5/3} \le C r^{-7}\) for any \(r \in (0, D]\).
* The exponents \(\eta = (7 + \sqrt{73})/2 \sim 7.772\) and \(\xi = (\sqrt{73} - 7)/2 \sim 0.77\) arise from the Sommerfeld asymptotic analysis.

## Context

This work extends Samojlow's result for reduced Hartree-Fock theory (Samojlow, Ph.D. thesis) to Kohn-Sham models with the local density approximation, generalizing to \(K \ge 2\) nuclei. It builds on the Brezis-Lieb result (Brezis and Lieb, 1979) showing that the Thomas-Fermi potential energy surface scales as \(R^{-7}\) for small separation, and on Solovej's conjecture (Solovej, 2015) that the same scaling holds for the exact ground state. The result enables further study of molecular stability in Kohn-Sham theory and suggests that the minimal inter-nuclear distance in a minimizing configuration is bounded below by a constant independent of nuclear charges.
