# A Bayesian-Neural-Network Prediction for Fragment Production in Proton Induced Spallation Reaction

> Fragments productions in spallation reactions are key infrastructure data for various applications. Based on the empirical parameterizations {\sc spacs}, a Bayesian-neural-network (BNN) approach is established to predict the fragment cross sections in the proton induced spallation reactions. A systematic investigation have been performed for the measured proton induced spallation reactions of systems ranging from the intermediate to the heavy nuclei and the incident energy ranging from 168 MeV/u to 1500 MeV/u. By learning the residuals between the experimental measurements and the {\sc spacs} predictions, the BNN predicted results are in good agreement with the measured results. The established method is suggested to benefit the related researches in the nuclear astrophysics, nuclear radioactive beam source, accelerator driven systems, and proton therapy, etc.

## Key claim
A Bayesian-neural-network (BNN) approach, when combined with the empirical SPACS parameterization, can improve predictions of fragment cross sections in proton-induced spallation reactions by learning the residuals between experimental data and SPACS predictions, but the method is limited by the absence of data for reactions below 100 MeV/u and for small spallation systems (A < 20).

## Method
The BNN approach uses a multilayer perceptron with one hidden layer to reconstruct the logarithmic residuals between experimental cross sections and SPACS predictions. The network inputs are the projectile mass number, projectile charge number, bombarding energy, fragment charge number, and fragment mass number. The hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm with Gibbs sampling is used for parameter optimization, and the optimal network structure was determined to be 5-23-1 (5 inputs, 23 hidden neurons, 1 output) based on A-factor minimization.

## Key numbers
- 3,511 measured fragment cross sections from 20 different spallation reaction systems were used, with 3,211 in the learning set and 300 in the validation set.
- Incident energies ranged from 168 MeV/u to 1500 MeV/u.
- The optimal network structure was 5-23-1 (5 inputs, 23 hidden neurons in a single hidden layer, 1 output).
- The A-factor decreased with increasing hidden neurons up to H=23, beyond which it could not be further minimized.
- Systems studied include: 36Ar+p (361, 546, 765 MeV/u), 40Ar+p (352 MeV/u), 40Ca+p (356, 565, 763 MeV/u), 56Fe+p (300, 500, 750, 1000, 1500 MeV/u), 136Xe+p (168, 200, 500, 1000 MeV/u), 197Au+p (800 MeV/u), 208Pb+p (500, 1000 MeV/u), and 238U+p (1000 MeV/u).

## Context
This work extends the empirical SPACS parameterization (SPACS, 2014) by incorporating Bayesian neural network methods previously applied to nuclear mass predictions (Neal, 1996; Nmass1, Nmass2, Nmass3) and spallation reactions (spal, 2019). The BNN+SPACS approach improves upon direct BNN learning by embedding physical guidance from the SPACS model, overcoming SPACS's tendency to underestimate light fragment cross sections and overestimate cross sections for fragments near heavy spallation nuclei. The method enables more reliable predictions for applications in nuclear astrophysics, accelerator-driven systems, and proton therapy, but calls for new experimental data below 100 MeV/u and for small spallation systems (A < 20).
