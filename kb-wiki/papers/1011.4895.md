# An One Dimensional Adiabatic Model for Fusion Involving Loosely Bound and Halo Nuclei with Heavy Targets

> An one dimensional adiabatic model has been proposed for fusion of loosely bound and halo light nuclei with various heavy targets. It is shown that fusion cross sections at near and sub-barrier energies can be explained using a simple WKB tunneling through an adiabatic barrier without invoking breakup coupling explicitly. The model has been applied successfully to explain fusion cross sections for several systems including recently measured $^{6}Li+^{198}Pt$ system (Phy. Rev. Lett. 103, 232702, 2009) where data exists well above and below the barrier and $^{8}He+^{197}Au$ system (Phy. Rev. Lett. 103, 232701, 2009) where $^{8}He$ is highly neutron rich. Interestingly, the fusion of stable $^{4}He+^{197}Au$ system can not be explained on the basis of this adiabatic model as it requires normal tunneling through the sudden barrier. The requirement of adiabatic potential for the loosely bound and halo nuclei is linked to the fact that for such systems neutron flow leading to neck formation is initiated at a larger distance which modifies the sudden potential.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that fusion cross sections for loosely bound and halo nuclei with heavy targets can be explained by a simple one-dimensional WKB tunneling through an adiabatic barrier, without explicitly invoking breakup coupling, provided that a constant suppression factor (typically 0.56–0.80) is applied to the above-barrier cross sections.

## Method

The author constructs an adiabatic ion-ion potential by adding a neck-formation correction term to the standard sudden Akyuz-Winther nuclear potential. The neck potential is derived from a macroscopic cylindrical neck model and becomes active only when the internuclear distance is less than an adiabatic parameter \(R_a\), which is typically close to the Coulomb barrier radius. Fusion cross sections are then calculated using the WKB approximation for sub-barrier energies and the Hill-Wheeler formula for above-barrier energies, with the adiabatic potential replacing the sudden potential in the nuclear interior region.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{6}\text{Li}+^{198}\text{Pt}\): Coulomb barrier \(V_b = 28.4\) MeV, barrier radius \(R_b = 11.2\) fm, adiabatic parameter \(R_a = 11.0\) fm, suppression factor \(f = 0.56\) (compared to 0.58 for sudden potential).
- For \(^{8}\text{He}+^{197}\text{Au}\): \(V_b = 18.7\) MeV, \(R_b = 11.5\) fm, \(R_a = 11.5\) fm, \(\hbar\omega = 3.5\) MeV, suppression factor \(f = 0.71\) (compared to 0.80 for sudden potential).
- For \(^{4}\text{He}+^{197}\text{Au}\): no adiabatic correction needed, suppression factor \(f = 1.0\).
- For \(^{9}\text{Be}+^{208}\text{Pb}\): \(V_b = 38.5\) MeV, \(R_b = 11.6\) fm, \(R_a = 11.5\) fm, \(\hbar\omega = 4.7\) MeV, suppression factor \(f = 0.64\).
- For \(^{6}\text{Li}+^{144}\text{Sm}\): \(V_b = 25.2\) MeV, \(R_b = 9.9\) fm, \(R_a = 9.9\) fm, suppression factor \(f = 0.60\).

## Context

This work extends the barrier penetration model to loosely bound and halo nuclei, where standard coupled-channel calculations (including breakup coupling) typically require a 10%–30% energy-independent suppression factor to match above-barrier data (Canto 2006). The model successfully reproduces recent high-precision data for \(^{6}\text{Li}+^{198}\text{Pt}\) (Sri 2009) and \(^{8}\text{He}+^{197}\text{Au}\) (Lem 2009), while noting that stable \(^{4}\text{He}+^{197}\text{Au}\) requires only the sudden potential. The approach suggests that the basic ion-ion potential becomes adiabatic for halo and loosely bound nuclei due to neutron flow and neck formation initiating at larger distances, and that this adiabatic nature should be incorporated into future coupled-channel calculations.
