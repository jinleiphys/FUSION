# Expected Production of Strange Baryons and Antibaryons in Baryon-Poor QGP

> In a dynamical model of QGP at RHIC we obtain the temporal evolution of strange phase space occupancy at conditions expected to occur in 100+100A GeV nuclear collisions. We show that the sudden QGP break up model developed to describe the SPS experimental results implies dominance of both baryon and antibaryon abundances by the strange baryon and antibaryon yields.

# Expected Production of Strange Baryons and Antibaryons in Baryon-Poor QGP

## Key claim

In a dynamical model of QGP at RHIC, the authors show that the sudden QGP break-up model developed to describe SPS experimental results implies dominance of both baryon and antibaryon abundances by strange baryon and antibaryon yields, but this result depends on the assumption that the deconfined QGP is formed and that the freeze-out temperature at RHIC rises only slightly to 150±5 MeV from the SPS value of 145±5 MeV.

## Method

The authors study the temporal evolution of the strange quark phase space occupancy γ_s in an expanding QGP using a Boltzmann collision term for strangeness production via gluon fusion (GG→s\bar s) and quark-antiquark annihilation (q\bar q→s\bar s), with running QCD parameters (α_s and strange quark mass). They assume kinetic equilibrium is reached faster than chemical equilibrium, and that gluons equilibrate chemically faster than strangeness, allowing them to focus on the evolution of γ_s after an initial time τ_0. The dynamical equation is integrated numerically for a range of initial temperatures (300–600 MeV) with entropy-conserving longitudinal and transverse expansion, using a freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV extrapolated from SPS analysis.

## Key numbers

- The freeze-out temperature at RHIC is taken as T_f^RHIC ≃ 150±5 MeV, compared to T_f^SPS ≃ 145±5 MeV.
- The initial temperature range explored is 300 ≤ T_0 ≤ 600 MeV, varied in steps of 50 MeV.
- The initial strangeness occupancy is taken as γ_s(T_0) = 0.2.
- The final strangeness phase space occupancy at freeze-out falls in the narrow band 1.18 > γ_s(T_f) > 0.95, with the adopted most likely value γ_s(T_f) = 1.15.
- The strange quark mass used is m_s(1 GeV) = 220 MeV, with α_s(M_Z^0) = 0.118.
- The initial thermal time is τ_0 = 1 fm/c, with initial temperature T_0 = 0.5 GeV and longitudinal dimension d(T_0=0.5)/2 = 0.75 fm, transverse radius R_⊥ = 4.5 fm.
- The light quark fugacity is in the range 1 < λ_q < 1.1, with λ_q = 1.03±0.005 chosen to give energy per baryon E/b ≃ 100 GeV.
- The strangeness yield per participant at RHIC is 13–23 times greater than at SPS, where there are 0.75 strange quark pairs per baryon.
- The net baryon density is db/dy ≃ 15±2, assuming central rapidity proton density dp/dy|_central = 25.

## Context

This work extends the sudden QGP break-up model previously applied to SPS data by Rafelski and Letessier (1999) to RHIC conditions, using the same hadronization framework that assumes no significant hadronic gas epoch. The authors differ from other studies of chemical equilibration for RHIC conditions (Bir93, Won96, Sri97) by studying phase space occupancy rather than particle density, eliminating dynamical flow effects through entropy-conserving evolution, and using running QCD parameters. The results enable predictions for the STAR collaboration at RHIC, particularly for multi-strange (anti)baryon production, and suggest that shape identity of m_⊥ and y spectra for antibaryons (\bar p, \overline\Lambda, \overline\Xi) should be observable, with m_⊥-slopes similar to Pb–Pb 158A GeV results.
