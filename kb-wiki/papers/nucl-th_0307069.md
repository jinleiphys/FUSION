# Relativistic nuclear model with point-couplings constrained by QCD and chiral symmetry

> We derive a microscopic relativistic point-coupling model of nuclear many-body dynamics constrained by in-medium QCD sum rules and chiral symmetry. The effective Lagrangian is characterized by density dependent coupling strengths, determined by chiral one- and two-pion exchange and by QCD sum rule constraints for the large isoscalar nucleon self-energies that arise through changes of the quark condensate and the quark density at finite baryon density. This approach is tested in the analysis of the equations of state for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, and of bulk and single-nucleon properties of finite nuclei. In comparison with purely phenomenological mean-field approaches, the built-in QCD constraints and the explicit treatment of pion exchange restrict the freedom in adjusting parameters and functional forms of density dependent couplings. It is shown that chiral (two-pion exchange) fluctuations play a prominent role for nuclear binding and saturation, whereas strong scalar and vector fields of about equal magnitude and opposite sign, induced by changes of the QCD vacuum in the presence of baryonic matter, generate the large effective spin-orbit potential in finite nuclei.

# Relativistic nuclear model with point-couplings constrained by QCD and chiral symmetry

## Key claim

The authors derive a microscopic relativistic point-coupling model where nuclear binding and saturation arise primarily from chiral (pionic) fluctuations, while large scalar and vector fields of approximately equal magnitude and opposite sign originate from changes in the QCD vacuum condensates at finite baryon density; however, the leading-order QCD sum rule constraint that the ratio of scalar to vector condensate self-energies equals -1 carries an estimated uncertainty of about 20% and is relaxed in the generalized approach presented here.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic point-coupling Lagrangian with density-dependent coupling strengths for isoscalar-scalar, isoscalar-vector, isovector-scalar, and isovector-vector four-fermion interaction terms. The density dependence of the couplings is determined by combining constraints from in-medium QCD sum rules (for the condensate background contributions to nucleon self-energies) with in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculations up to three-loop order (for pion-exchange contributions). The model is then applied in the mean-field approximation to compute the equation of state for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, and to perform self-consistent calculations of ground-state properties of finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density from chiral perturbation theory alone: ρ₀ = 0.178 fm⁻³, with energy per particle Ē(k_f0) = -15.3 MeV, compression modulus K₀ = 255 MeV, and asymmetry energy A(k_f0) = 33.8 MeV at saturation density
- Single-particle potential depth at equilibrium nuclear matter density: U(0, k_f0) = -53.2 MeV, in agreement with empirical optical-model potential
- Momentum cutoff scale Λ = 0.65 GeV (646 MeV) adjusted to reproduce the saturation energy per particle, corresponding to λ ≈ 1.113
- Chiral vacuum condensate value: ⟨q̄q⟩₀ ≈ -(240 MeV)³ ≈ -1.8 fm⁻³ at renormalization scale of about 1 GeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 92.4 MeV
- Nucleon sigma term: σ_N ≈ 50 MeV; current quark masses: m_u + m_d ≈ 12 MeV at scale of 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear matter developed by Kaiser, Fritsch, and Weise (2001), which calculated the nuclear matter equation of state using in-medium chiral perturbation theory to three-loop order with a single momentum cutoff parameter. The authors previously outlined this approach in Finelli et al. (2002), and here present a generalized version that relaxes the assumption of exact cancellation between scalar and vector condensate self-energies at saturation density. The model provides a more explicit connection between low-energy QCD and nuclear phenomenology than purely phenomenological relativistic mean-field models such as those reviewed by Serot and Walecka (1997) and Ring (1996), and is related to density-dependent relativistic hadron field models of Fuchs, Lenske, and Wolter (1995) and Typel and Wolter (1999). The approach enables systematic calculations of finite nuclei properties constrained by QCD sum rules and chiral symmetry, with the potential to reduce the number of free parameters compared to phenomenological models.
