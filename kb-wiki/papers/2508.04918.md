# Acausality-driven instabilities in transient relativistic viscous hydrodynamics

> We investigate non-linear instabilities stemming from superluminal propagation of information in Israel-Stewart-like models of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics. In relativity, the characteristic speed of propagation of information, $w$, and the speed of the fluid, $v$, allow us to differentiate between regimes of the hydrodynamic equations that are acausal but stable ($w>1$), unstable ($v^{2} w^{2} \geq 1$), and covariantly ill-posed ($w^{2} \leq 0$). As an analytical benchmark, we present a new solution that illustrates these distinct regimes. We compare this analytical solution to the result of a numerical relativistic viscous fluid dynamics solver, and confirm that the analytical result can be recovered numerically in the stable regime, whether causal or acausal. The onset of numerical instabilities is further found to occur in the regime predicted by the analytical solution.

## Key claim
Acausality in Israel-Stewart-like relativistic viscous hydrodynamics does not automatically produce instabilities; instabilities arise only when the product of fluid velocity and characteristic speed exceeds unity ($v^2 w^2 \geq 1$), causing reversed dissipation, or when the characteristic speed becomes imaginary ($w^2 \leq 0$), making the system elliptic and ill-posed.

## Method
The authors derived the condition $v^2 w^2 \geq 1$ for acausality-driven instability by analyzing the acoustic cone geometry in a Lorentz-boosted frame. They constructed an analytical benchmark solution for a homogeneous bulk-viscous fluid slab with zero chemical potential, constant speed of sound $c_s^2 = 1/3$, and Israel-Stewart dynamics, solving the coupled conservation and relaxation equations to obtain $v(t)$ explicitly. They compared this analytical solution to numerical solutions from the MUSIC solver for initial conditions in the causal ("good"), acausal-stable ("bad"), and unstable ("ugly") regimes.

## Key numbers
- Critical velocity for bifurcation between stable and unstable regimes: $v_c \approx 0.838$, where $vw = 1$ for the chosen parameters $\{c_s^2, a, \tau_\Pi, \mathcal{E}/\mathcal{P}^1\} = \{1/3, 1, 1 \text{ fm}, 2\}$.
- Causality violation threshold: $w > 1$ occurs for $v > \sqrt{3} - 1 \approx 0.732$ in this system.
- For the unstable ("ugly") initial condition $v(0) = 0.9$, numerical runaway occurs at $t/\tau_\Pi \sim 0.015$, with $v(t)$ approaching 1.
- The ratio $\Pi/P$ saturates to $-4$ as $v \rightarrow 1$ in the unstable branch.

## Context
This work extends the causality analysis of Bemfica et al. (2019, 2020), who showed that Israel-Stewart theory can violate causality far from equilibrium, by demonstrating that acausality alone does not guarantee instability. It provides the first analytical benchmark solution for acausality-driven instabilities in transient relativistic hydrodynamics, enabling systematic testing of numerical solvers like MUSIC (Schenke et al., 2010, 2011; Paquet et al., 2015). The results enable future studies of acausality-driven instabilities in more realistic $(2+1)$-dimensional heavy-ion collision simulations with shear viscosity.
