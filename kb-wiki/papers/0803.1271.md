# Taming Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering

> We study recent Deeply Virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) data within a dual parameterization of the Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs). This parameterization allows to quantify the maximum amount of information, that can be extracted from DVCS data, in a ``quintessence'' function. We present a ``zero step'' model for the latter solely based on the forward quark density, providing a parameter free prediction for the imaginary part of the DVCS amplitude. It is shown that the bulk effect of the $e p \to e p γ$ beam helicity asymmetry can be understood within such a model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the bulk of the beam helicity asymmetry in deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) can be explained by a parameter-free prediction based solely on forward quark densities, but this "zero step" model fails to simultaneously describe the unpolarized cross section at all azimuthal angles, indicating that genuine non-forward effects are needed for a complete description.

## Method

The authors employed the dual parameterization of generalized parton distributions (GPDs), which expresses the GPD H(x,ξ,t) as an infinite series of forward-like functions Q_{2ν}(x,t). They isolated the contribution of the leading term Q_0(x,t), which is related to the t-dependent forward quark densities via Eq. (Q0), and used this to compute the imaginary part of the DVCS amplitude. The predictions were compared with JLab/Hall A data on polarized and unpolarized cross sections and CLAS beam spin asymmetry data, using a Regge-motivated Ansatz for the t-dependence of quark densities with slope parameter α' = 1.105 GeV^{-2}.

## Key numbers

- The polarized cross section difference (d^4 σ_+ - d^4 σ_-)/2 was compared with data at Q^2 > 2 GeV^2 and -t < 0.3 GeV^2; the parameter-free prediction using only Q_0 gave an "amazingly consistent description" of these data.
- At Φ = 0 and -t = 0.23 GeV^2, the Bethe-Heitler process makes up about 85% of the unpolarized cross section.
- At Φ = 180 deg, the Bethe-Heitler cross section is more than a factor 2 below the data.
- The slope parameter for the t-dependence of quark densities was fixed as α' = 1.105 GeV^{-2} from form factor sum rules.

## Context

This work extends the dual parameterization approach of Polyakov and Shuvaev (2002) by providing a clean separation between forward quark density contributions and genuine non-forward effects in GPDs. It contrasts with the double distribution model of Vanderhaeghen et al. (1999), which systematically overestimates the beam helicity asymmetries, and with the earlier work of Guzey (2003) that used a predefined model for the forward-like functions without discussing the zero-step separation. The results enable future extraction of t-dependent quark densities q(x,t) and e(x,t) from precise DVCS measurements in a wider kinematic region, including neutron targets for accessing the e(x,t) function.
