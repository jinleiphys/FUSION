# Near-threshold $η$ production in $pp$ collisions

> We study near-threshold $η$ meson production in $pp$ collisions within an effective Lagrangian approach combined with the isobar model, by allowing for the various intermediate nucleon resonances due to the $π$, $η$, and $ρ$-meson exchanges. It is shown that the $ρ$-meson exchange is the dominant excitation mechanism for these resonances, and the contribution from the $N^*(1720)$ is dominant. The total cross section data can be reasonably reproduced, and the anisotropic angular distributions of the emitted $η$ meson are consistent with experimental measurements. Besides, the invariant mass spectra of $pp$ and $pη$ explain the data well at excess energy of 15 MeV, and are basically consistent with the data at excess energy of 40 MeV. However, our model calculations cannot reasonably account for the two-peak structure in the $pη$ distribution at excess energies of 57 and 72 MeV, which suggests that a more complicated mechanism is needed at higher energy region.

## Key claim
The authors show that the $N^*(1720)$ resonance, excited primarily by $\rho$-meson exchange, dominates near-threshold $\eta$ production in $pp$ collisions, but their model cannot reproduce the two-peak structure in the $p\eta$ invariant mass distribution at excess energies of 57 and 72 MeV, indicating that a more complicated mechanism is needed at higher energies.

## Method
The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach combined with the isobar model, including $\pi$, $\eta$, and $\rho$-meson exchanges to excite intermediate nucleon resonances ($N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$, $N^*(1710)$, $N^*(1720)$) and the nucleon pole. They computed total cross sections, invariant mass spectra, angular distributions, and Dalitz plots via a Monte Carlo multi-particle phase space integration, including proton-proton and proton-$\eta$ final-state interactions through enhancement factors.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section data for $pp \to pp\eta$ are reproduced up to excess energy of 80 MeV.
- The $\rho$-meson exchange coupling constant for $N^*(1720)N\rho$ is $g_{\rho N N^*(1720)}^2/4\pi = 635.11$, the largest among all resonances considered.
- The $N^*(1720)$ branching ratio to $N\rho$ is 0.775 (77.5%), with a width of 0.25 GeV.
- The $N^*(1535)$ branching ratio to $N\rho$ is 0.02 (2%), with a coupling constant $g_{\rho N N^*(1535)}^2/4\pi = 5.55$.
- Cut-off parameters: $\Lambda_\pi = \Lambda_\eta = 0.8$ GeV, $\Lambda_\rho = 1.85$ GeV; for $N^*$ resonances, $\Lambda_{N^*} = 2.0$ GeV.
- The $pp$ FSI parameters: $\alpha = 0.1$ fm$^{-1}$, $\beta = 0.5$ fm$^{-1}$; the $p\eta$ FSI scattering length $a = (0.487 + i0.171)$ fm.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies on $\eta$ production in $NN$ collisions, where the dominant resonance was debated: some works (e.g., Batinic 1995, Shyam 2007) favored $N^*(1535)$, while Nakayama (2008) found $N^*(1520)$ dominant. The present result supports the importance of $\rho$-meson exchange and $N^*(1720)$, consistent with the strong $N^*(1535)N\rho$ coupling found by Xie (2013). The failure to explain the two-peak structure at higher excess energies (57 and 72 MeV) suggests that higher partial waves in the $p\eta$ final-state interaction, as noted by Petrus (2000) and Krusche (2014), may be needed. This work enables future studies to include additional resonances or more sophisticated FSI treatments.
