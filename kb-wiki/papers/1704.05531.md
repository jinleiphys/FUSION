# Quantifying the Chiral Magnetic Effect from Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics

> In this contribution we report a recently developed Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) framework, which simulates the evolution of fermion currents in QGP on top of the bulk expansion from data-validated VISHNU hydrodynamics. With reasonable estimates of initial conditions and magnetic field lifetime, the predicted CME signal is quantitatively consistent with change separation measurements in 200GeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC. We further develop the event-by-event AVFD simulations that allow direct evaluation of two-particle correlations arising from CME signal as well as the non-CME backgrounds. Finally we report predictions from AVFD simulations for the upcoming isobaric (Ru-Ru v.s. Zr-Zr ) collisions that could provide the critical test of the CME in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) framework predicts a Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) signal that is quantitatively consistent with charge separation measurements in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions at RHIC, but only when using relatively large values of the saturation scale \(Q_s^2\) (around 1 to 1.5 GeV\(^2\)) to estimate the initial axial charge density from gluonic topological fluctuations.

## Method

The authors solve fluid dynamical equations for right-handed and left-handed chiral fermion currents (for u and d quark flavors) as perturbations on top of a bulk fluid evolution provided by the data-validated VISHNU 2+1D second-order viscous hydrodynamics framework. The magnetic field is parametrized as \(B(\tau) = B_0 / (1 + (\tau / \tau_B)^2)\) with lifetime \(\tau_B = 0.6\) fm/c, and the initial axial charge density is estimated from glasma flux tube parameters depending on the saturation scale \(Q_s\). Event-by-event simulations are performed with fluctuating initial conditions and a hadronic afterburner (UrQMD) to evaluate two-particle correlation observables.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field lifetime is set to \(\tau_B = 0.6\) fm/c, comparable to the onset time of hydrodynamic evolution.
* The saturation scale range used is \(Q_s^2 \simeq 1 \sim 1.5\) GeV\(^2\) for RHIC 200 AGeV collisions, controlling the initial axial charge density.
* For event-by-event simulations of 50-60% centrality 200 GeV Au-Au collisions, three setups are used: (a) null case; (b) \(eB = 5 m_\pi^2\) and \(n_A/s = 0.1\) (corresponding to \(Q_s^2 \simeq 1\) GeV\(^2\)); (c) \(eB = 5 m_\pi^2\) and \(n_A/s = 0.2\) (corresponding to \(Q_s^2 \simeq 1.5\) GeV\(^2\)).
* For isobaric collisions, the CME signals are expected to differ by about 20% due to different magnetic field strength by about 10%, with a predicted visible shift of about 15% in the \(\gamma\)-correlation in peripheral region differentiating Ru-Ru from Zr-Zr.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical modeling of the Chiral Magnetic Effect in heavy ion collisions beyond earlier estimates by providing a full fluid-dynamical treatment of chiral fermion currents. It directly addresses the challenge raised by Bzdak et al. (2012) and Kharzeev et al. (2015) regarding separating CME signal from flow-driven background correlations. The AVFD predictions for isobaric Ru-Ru versus Zr-Zr collisions, planned at RHIC following proposals by Skokov et al. (2016), provide a quantitative benchmark for the critical experimental test that could unambiguously confirm the CME in heavy ion collisions.
