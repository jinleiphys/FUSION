# Order parameters with higher dimensionful composite fields

> We discuss the possibility of the spontaneous symmetry breaking characterized by order parameters with higher dimensionful composite fields. By analyzing general Ginzburg-Landau potential for a complex scalar field ϕ=ϕ_1 + i ϕ_2 with O(2) symmetry, we demonstrate that a phase characterized by < ϕ_1^2 - ϕ_2^2 > \neq 0 with < ϕ_1 >=< ϕ_2 >=0 is realized in a certain parameter region. To clarify the driving force to favor this phase, we study the O(2) ϕ^6 theory in three dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spontaneous symmetry breaking (SSB) characterized by higher-dimensionful composite order parameters, specifically the pattern O(2) → Z₂ with order parameter Re⟨φ²⟩ ≠ 0 while ⟨φ⟩ = 0, can be realized in a Ginzburg-Landau effective potential for a complex scalar field; however, in the specific 3d O(2) φ⁶ theory treated in the Hartree-Fock approximation, this "WFH phase" appears only as a meta-stable state rather than a global minimum.

## Method

The authors first construct a general Ginzburg-Landau effective potential for a complex scalar field φ = φ₁ + iφ₂ with O(2) symmetry, including terms up to φ⁶ order, and analyze the conditions under which the phase with ⟨φ₁² − φ₂²⟩ ≠ 0 and ⟨φ₁⟩ = ⟨φ₂⟩ = 0 (the WFH phase) is energetically favored. They then study a specific 3d O(2) φ⁶ theory using the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism in the Hartree-Fock (HF) approximation, deriving gap equations for the dynamical masses m₁ and m₂ from self-consistent two-particle irreducible (2PI) diagrams, and expanding the effective potential to identify the parameter region where the WFH phase exists.

## Key numbers

- The stability condition for the 3d O(2) φ⁶ theory at large f requires g₆ < 2π²/3 (equivalently, the rescaled coupling satisfies ḡ₆ < 1).
- The condition for the WFH phase to have at least a local minimum is a₁(b₂ + 1) < 3/4, where a₁ and b₂ are rescaled coupling constants in the effective potential.
- The stability in the m₂ direction requires 3(b₂ + 1) + 4a₁b₂(b₂ − 1) + √3 √(3 − 4a₁(b₂ + 1)) > 0.
- For the WFH phase to be a global minimum, the inequality involving the rescaled couplings a, b, c (with b = −3/2 and c = 1 + b₂ > 4 in the specific model) must satisfy Eq. (13), which the authors find cannot be met in the HF approximation.

## Context

This work extends the general discussion of SSB with unbroken discrete subgroups, previously considered in QCD contexts such as the four-quark operator for non-standard chiral symmetry breaking (Stern, 1998) and the color-flavor-locking phase in color superconductivity (Alford, 1999) where U_B(1) breaks to Z(2). The paper provides a concrete field-theoretic demonstration using the 3d O(2) φ⁶ model, which is related to the 3d effective theory describing tricritical behavior of the chiral phase transition in QCD at finite temperature and baryon density (Stephanov, 1998). The results open the question of whether such higher-dimensionful order parameter phases can exist as true ground states beyond the HF approximation, and suggest applications to gauge theories and condensed matter systems.
