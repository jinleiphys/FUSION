# Searching for charmoniumlike states with hidden $s\bar{s}$

> We investigate the processes $e^+e^-$$\to$$γJ/ψϕ$, $γJ/ψω$ and $π^0 J/ψη$ to search for the charmnium-like states with hidden $s\bar{s}$, such as $Y(4140)$, $Y(4274)$, $X(4350)$ and $X(3915)$. These processes will receive contributions from the charmed-strange meson rescatterings. When the center-of-mass energies of the $e^+e^-$ scatterings are taken around the $D_{s0}(2317)D_s^{*}$, $D_{s1}(2460)D_s$ or $D_{s1}(2460)D_s^{*}$ threshold, the anomalous triangle singularities can be present in the rescattering amplitudes, which implies a non-resonance explanation about the resonance-like structures. The positions of the anomalous triangle singularities are sensitive to the kinematics, which offers us a criterion to distinguish the kinematic singularities from genuine particles.

## Key claim

The resonance-like structures Y(4140), Y(4274), X(4350), and X(3915) may not be genuine exotic hadrons but instead kinematic artifacts arising from anomalous triangle singularities (ATS) in charmed-strange meson rescattering amplitudes, provided the center-of-mass energy of the e⁺e⁻ collision falls within specific kinematic windows near the Dₛ₀(2317)Dₛ*, Dₛ₁(2460)Dₛ, or Dₛ₁(2460)Dₛ* thresholds.

## Method

The authors computed the amplitudes for e⁺e⁻ → γJ/ψφ, γJ/ψω, and π⁰J/ψη via ψ(4415) intermediate states followed by charmed-strange meson loop rescatterings, using heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory (HHChPT) to encode heavy quark spin symmetry. Coupling constants were extracted from experimental decay widths and from matching to the quark-interchange model (Barnes-Swanson model) for meson-meson scattering. The loop integrals were evaluated to locate the anomalous triangle singularities, and the differential cross sections for the final-state invariant mass distributions were calculated at several center-of-mass energies.

## Context

This work extends the kinematic singularity approach previously applied to charmonium-like states by Liu et al. (2015) and others (Chen 2011, Bugg 2011, Wang 2011, Szczepaniak 2015), applying it specifically to candidates with hidden s̄s content. The authors argue that Y(4140), Y(4274), X(4350), and X(3915), previously interpreted as hadronic molecules (Liu 2009, Shen 2010, He 2011) or tetraquarks (Stancu 2009, Wang 2015), could instead arise from ATS effects. The work enables experimental searches at BESIII, Belle, and Belle II at specific center-of-mass energies (4.430–4.435 GeV or 4.572–4.585 GeV for γJ/ψφ; 4.428–4.443 GeV or 4.572–4.583 GeV for π⁰J/ψη) to distinguish kinematic singularities from genuine resonances by observing the movement of peak positions with changing kinematics.
