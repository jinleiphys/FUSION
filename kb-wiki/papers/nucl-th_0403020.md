# Consequences of wall stiffness for a beta-soft potential

> Modifications of the infinite square well E(5) and X(5) descriptions of transitional nuclear structure are considered. The eigenproblem for a potential with linear sloped walls is solved. The consequences of the introduction of sloped walls and of a quadratic transition operator are investigated.

## Key claim

The infinite square well approximation in the E(5) and X(5) models can be relaxed by introducing a linear sloped wall, and the resulting energy spacings and transition strengths depend sensitively on the wall stiffness parameter S, with softer walls (smaller S) reducing the overprediction of excited-band energy spacings seen in the pure square well limit.

## Method

The Bohr Hamiltonian was solved for a potential that is zero for β ≤ β_w and linear with slope C for β > β_w. The radial Schrödinger equation was separated, and analytic solutions (Bessel functions for the inner region, Airy functions for the outer region) were obtained for the α=0 (no centrifugal term) case. These α=0 solutions were used as a basis for numerical diagonalization to obtain solutions for general angular momentum, with the centrifugal term treated as a perturbation in the basis. Electromagnetic transition strengths were calculated using both linear and quadratic terms in the E2 operator.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R_{4/2} ≡ E(4⁺₁)/E(2⁺₁) decreases as the wall stiffness parameter S is reduced from infinity (square well limit).
- For S ≈ 50, the predicted energy spacing within the K^π = 0⁺₂ band becomes consistent with spacings found for N=90 transitional nuclei.
- Eigenvalues converge to within ~1.5% of their true values with a truncated basis of only 5 eigenfunctions; calculations used a basis size of 25.
- The parameter S is defined as S ≡ (2B/ℏ²) β_w³ C, and the essential parameter controlling the relative strength of linear and quadratic E2 terms is A' ≡ A₂β_w/A₁.
- For a rigid rotor estimate from simple charge distribution models, A' ≈ -0.2, giving weak constructive interference for low-lying transitions.
- In the interacting boson model, the relative contribution of the second-order term is comparable to A' ≈ -1.

## Context

This work extends the E(5) model of Iachello (2000) and X(5) model of Iachello (2001), which used infinite square well potentials to describe shape-transitional nuclei. It addresses discrepancies noted by Caprio (2002), Clark (2003), and McCutchan (2004) between X(5) predictions and observed data, particularly the overprediction of excited-band energy spacings and interband B(E2) strengths. The sloped well approach enables systematic investigation of how wall stiffness affects observables, and the inclusion of quadratic E2 operator terms (motivated by Arias (2001) and Pietralla and Gorbachenko) provides a framework for reproducing the varied spin dependences of B(E2) values observed in transitional nuclei.
