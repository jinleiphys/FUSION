# Quark Condensate and Effective Action from Dyson--Schwinger Equations

> We obtain the QCD quark condensate from consideration of unquenched quark dynamics in Dyson-Schwinger gluon vacuum. We consider the non-local extension of the condensate and determine the quark virtuality. We also obtain the condensate-driven contribution of the non-perturbative QCD to Euler--Heisenberg Lagrangian of QED in external electromagnetic fields.

## Key claim
The paper obtains the QCD quark condensate from unquenched quark dynamics in the Dyson-Schwinger gluon vacuum, but the authors caution that the results should be treated as a first qualitative estimate rather than exact predictions, because the full non-perturbative understanding of the QCD vacuum cannot be reached from Dyson-Schwinger equations alone without additional resummation procedures.

## Method
The authors solve a coupled system of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) for ghost, gluon, and quark propagators in Landau gauge, using the Newton optimization method with Tschebyschev polynomial expansions. They compute the non-local quark condensate (NLC) from the resulting propagators by relating the vacuum expectation value C_0(x) = ⟨ψ̄(x)ψ(0)⟩ to the quark propagator form factors A(p) and M(p), omitting the Wilson line. The condensate response to an external electromagnetic field is derived from a diagrammatic expansion, and the Euler-Heisenberg effective action coefficients for photon-photon scattering are extracted by comparing the full DSE-propagator diagram with the perturbative result at small photon frequencies.

## Key numbers
- The infrared fixed point of the running coupling from DSE is α(0) ≈ 3 for N_c = 3.
- The local condensate mass dependence follows a power law: c(m) = 0.2 GeV³ (m/1 GeV)^0.73 for quark masses below 500 MeV.
- Quark virtuality follows the fit: λ²_q = 0.39 GeV² (m_q/1 GeV)^1.07.
- For the u quark (m = 0.0025 GeV, charge 2/3): local condensate C(0) = 0.00239 GeV, virtuality λ²_q = 0.00066 GeV².
- For the d quark (m = 0.005 GeV, charge 1/3): C(0) = 0.0042 GeV, λ²_q = 0.0013 GeV².
- For the s quark (m = 0.105 GeV, charge 1/3): C(0) = 0.037 GeV, λ²_q = 0.039 GeV².
- For a quark with m = 0.51 GeV (charge 2/3): C(0) = 0.12 GeV, λ²_q = 0.18 GeV².
- Fields of order 10⁻¹ GeV² may destroy the local condensate, comparable to the critical field F_cr = m_π² / log 2 from Shushpanov and Smilga (1997).
- The ratio of non-perturbative to perturbative Euler-Heisenberg coefficient a/a_0 is 0.07732 for the u quark, 0.00302 for d, 0.00019 for s, and 0.00064 for c.

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach of Fischer (2003) to compute non-local quark condensates, which were first introduced by Mikhailov and Radyushkin (1986). The results contradict the standard heavy-quark condensate relation ⟨q̄q⟩ = -1/(12 m_q) ⟨α G²⟩ from Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979), and also disagree with the light-quark condensate ratio ⟨s̄s⟩ ≈ 0.8 ⟨ūu⟩ from Ioffe (2002). The mass dependence found here differs from prior DSE-based virtuality results reported by Zhou et al. (2008) (λ²_{u,d} = 0.7 GeV², λ²_s = 1.6 GeV²) and by Chernyak and Zhitnitsky (1983) (λ²_q ≈ 0.4 ± 0.1 GeV²). The work enables future studies of pion wave functions in external fields, as the non-locality of condensates is key for non-perturbative contributions to the pion form factor, as shown by Pimikov, Bakulev, and Stefanis (2009).
