# Entanglement in massive Schwinger model at finite temperature and density

> We evaluate the entanglement entropy and entropic function of massive two dimensional QED (Schwinger model) at finite temperature, density, and $θ$-angle. In the strong coupling regime, the entropic function is dominated by the boson mass for large spatial intervals, and reduces to the CFT result for small spatial intervals. We also discuss the entanglement spectrum at finite temperature and a finite $θ$-angle.

## Key claim

The entropic function (UV-finite part of spatial entanglement entropy) in the massive Schwinger model at strong coupling is dominated by the boson mass for large spatial intervals and reduces to the conformal field theory (CFT) result for small spatial intervals, with the boson mass depending on temperature and vacuum angle but not on finite density.

## Method

The authors bosonize the massive Schwinger model (QED2) at finite chemical potential, temperature, and theta-angle, then analyze the spatial entanglement entropy via the entropic function C(l) = dS_EE/d ln l. For the strong coupling regime, they derive the boson mass from the effective potential and compute the entropic function using the known asymptotic form for a free massive boson, with the boson mass shifted by temperature through the thermal chiral condensate. They also perform numerical diagonalization of a discretized chain of coupled oscillators representing the bosonized Hamiltonian to compute the entanglement entropy and spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Critical mass ratio for vacuum degeneracy at theta=pi: m/g > m*/g = 1/(2 sqrt(pi) e^gamma) ≈ 0.158
- Fermion density per length: n_F = mu/pi
- Entropic function asymptotic for large intervals: C(m_B l) ~ (m_B l/4) K_1(2 m_B l) for m_B l >> 1
- Entropic function in CFT limit (small intervals): C ~ 1/3 for fermions, C ~ 1/6 for bosons
- Entanglement entropy slope for massless open chain: 0.0108 + 0.1666 log(n), converging to continuum limit of 1/6
- High temperature limit of entropic function: C(m_S l) ~ 0.070 for l=1 in units g=1
- Low temperature behavior: m_theta^2(T) ~ m_S^2 + m_pi^2 cos(theta) (1 - sqrt(2 pi T/m_theta) e^{-m_theta/T})
- High temperature behavior: m_theta^2(T) ~ m_S^2 - (m/f^2) cos(theta) 2T e^{-pi T/m_theta}

## Context

This work extends the analysis of entanglement in the Schwinger model from Ikeda (2023) to include finite temperature, density, and theta-angle. It uses bosonization techniques following Coleman (1974) and the thermal chiral condensate results from Sachs (1991), Smilga (1992), and Steele (1994). The entropic function analysis builds on the Painleve V approach for free massive fermions and bosons from Casini (2005) and Liu (2022). The results enable disentangling chiral and U_A(1) restoration effects in QCD4 through entanglement measurements, and the authors note that extension to multiple flavors following Banuls (2016) and Funcke (2022) is a natural next step.
