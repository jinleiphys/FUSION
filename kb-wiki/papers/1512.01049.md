# Ab-initio calculations of charge symmetry breaking in the A=4 hypernuclei

> We report on ab-initio NCSM calculations of the A=4 mirror Lambda hypernuclei Lambda-4H and Lambda-4He, using the Bonn-Juelich LO chiral EFT YN potentials plus a CSB Lambda0--Sigma0 mixing vertex. In addition to reproducing rather well the 0+ (g.s.) and 1+ (exc.) binding energies, these four-body calculations demonstrate for the first time that the observed CSB splitting of mirror levels, reaching hundreds of keV for 0+ (g.s.), can be reproduced using realistic theoretical interaction models, although with a non-negligible momentum cutoff dependence. Our results are discussed in relation to recent measurements of the Lambda-4H (0+ g.s.) binding energy [MAMI A1 Collaboration, Phys. Rev. Lett. 114, 232501 (2015)] and the Lambda-4He (1+ exc.) excitation energy [J-PARC E13 Collaboration, Phys. Rev. Lett. 115, 222501 (2015)].

# Ab-initio calculations of charge symmetry breaking in the A=4 hypernuclei

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate for the first time that the observed charge symmetry breaking (CSB) splitting of mirror levels in A=4 hypernuclei, reaching hundreds of keV for the 0+ ground state, can be reproduced using realistic theoretical interaction models, although with a non-negligible momentum cutoff dependence. The CSB splitting arises from a dominant 1S0 Lambda-N ↔ Sigma-N coupling-potential contact term in the leading-order chiral EFT YN interaction, which previous Nijmegen models could not reproduce.

## Method

The authors performed ab-initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations of the mirror hypernuclei Lambda-4H and Lambda-4He, using Bonn-Juelich leading-order chiral EFT hyperon-nucleon (YN) potentials with cutoff momenta from 550 to 700 MeV, plus a CSB Lambda-Sigma0 mixing vertex derived from the strong-interaction YN potentials. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in finite four-body harmonic-oscillator bases up to Nmax=18 (for J=0) and Nmax=14 (for J=1), with extrapolation to infinite model spaces, using chiral N3LO NN and N2LO NNN interactions for the nuclear part.

## Key numbers

- CSB ground-state splitting: mean value Delta-B(J=0) = 176(+118/-146) keV across cutoffs, compared to experimental 270±95 keV from MAMI A1 Collaboration (2015)
- CSB excited-state splitting: mean value Delta-B(J=1) = -204(+32/-24) keV, with opposite sign to ground-state splitting
- Lambda separation energies for 0+ ground state in Lambda-4H: range 2.556 MeV (cutoff 550) to 2.196 MeV (cutoff 700), experimental 2.04±0.04 MeV
- Lambda separation energies for 0+ ground state in Lambda-4He: range 2.586 MeV (cutoff 550) to 2.490 MeV (cutoff 700), experimental 2.39±0.03 MeV
- Charge-symmetric averaged excitation energy: mean value 1.23(+0.35/-0.32) MeV, compared to 1.25±0.02 MeV from gamma-ray measurements
- CSB difference in excitation energies: mean value 380(+140/-180) keV, compared to 320±20 keV from experiment
- At cutoff 600 MeV, the difference Delta-B(J=0) - Delta-B(J=1) = 0.33±0.04 MeV, in agreement with the gamma-ray energy difference of 0.32±0.02 MeV
- CSB scale coefficient from Lambda-Sigma0 mass mixing: 0.0297, derived from mixing matrix element 1.14(5) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous four-body calculations using Nijmegen soft-core realistic meson exchange YN interaction models NSC97e,f (Nogga 2001, Nogga 2002, Haidenbauer 2007, Nogga 2013, Nogga 2014), which produced at most 30% of the observed CSB ground-state splitting. The authors show that the LO chiral EFT YN interaction (Polinder 2006) succeeds where Nijmegen models failed because the latter have a dominant 3S1-3D1 tensor component in the Lambda-N ↔ Sigma-N coupling, which is ineffective for CSB when the Lambda-N states are purely s-wave. The results are discussed in relation to recent measurements by the MAMI A1 Collaboration (2015) and the J-PARC E13 Collaboration (2015). The work enables future ab-initio CSB calculations using the NLO chiral EFT YN interaction (Haidenbauer 2013) and extensions to p-shell hypernuclei.
