# The Multiplicity Scaling of the Fragmentation Function

> The single-particle inclusive fragmentation function and the particle multiplicity are observables of fundamental importance in studying properties of quantum chromodynamics at colliders. It is well-known that at high energies, the multiplicity distribution satisfies KNO scaling in which all moments are proportional to powers of the mean multiplicity. We prove that, under weak assumptions, the leading dependence of the fragmentation function on multiplicity is itself a kind of KNO scaling in which all moments are inversely proportional to powers of the mean multiplicity. This scaling with multiplicity additionally accounts for the dominant dependence on collision energy in the fragmentation function. The proof relies crucially on properties of the fragmentation function conditioned on the total multiplicity and application of the Stieltjes moment problem. In the process, we construct a novel basis of the fragmentation function expressed as an overall exponential suppression times a series of Laguerre polynomials. We study this scaling of the fragmentation function in experimental electron-position collision data and observe that residual scale violations are significantly reduced.

## Key claim

The authors prove that, under weak assumptions (large multiplicity, smooth distributions, identical particles), the leading dependence of the fragmentation function on multiplicity exhibits a KNO-like scaling where all moments are inversely proportional to powers of the mean multiplicity, and this scaling accounts for the dominant dependence on collision energy in the fragmentation function.

## Method

The authors start from the fragmentation function conditioned on total multiplicity and exploit permutation symmetry and the energy conservation sum rule to derive an exponential form in the large-multiplicity limit. They construct a novel basis expansion of the conditional fragmentation function using Laguerre polynomials and apply constraints from the Stieltjes moment problem to show that the expansion coefficients cannot depend on multiplicity in the large-N limit, establishing the scaling. They validate the scaling by comparing simulated Pythia events and experimental data from TASSO, AMY, DELPHI, and OPAL at four different center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers

- The mean of the conditional fragmentation function is fixed by multiplicity: ⟨z⟩_N = 2/N.
- TASSO data at √s = 22 GeV: ⟨N_ch⟩ = 11.22 ± 0.07.
- AMY data at √s = 55 GeV: ⟨N_ch⟩ = 16.82 ± 0.22.
- DELPHI data at √s = 91 GeV: ⟨N_ch⟩ = 21.21 ± 0.2.
- OPAL data at √s = 206 GeV: ⟨N_ch⟩ = 27.75 ± 0.67.
- Simulated events were generated at √s = 20, 50, 91, 180 GeV using Pythia 8.309.

## Context

This work extends the well-known KNO scaling of the multiplicity distribution, established by Polyakov (1970) and Koba, Nielsen, and Olesen (1972), to the single-particle inclusive fragmentation function. The proof provides a model-independent relationship between multiplicity and energy fraction that has been absent despite over 50 years of fragmentation function studies, including the DGLAP evolution equations. The result enables isolation of other physical effects controlling the fragmentation function, such as the Gaussian peak's energy dependence in the distribution of log(1/z) and the transition between recombination and fragmentation in high-multiplicity heavy ion collisions.
