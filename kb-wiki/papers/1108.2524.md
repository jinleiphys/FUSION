# Transfer reactions and the dispersive optical-model

> The dispersive optical-model is applied to transfer reactions. A systematic study of $(d,p)$ reactions on closed-shell nuclei using the finite-range adiabatic reaction model is performed at several beam energies and results are compared to data as well as to predictions using a standard global optical-potential. Overall, we find that the dispersive optical-model is able to describe the angular distributions as well as or better than the global parameterization. In addition, it also constrains the overlap function. Spectroscopic factors extracted using the dispersive optical-model are generally lower than those using standard parameters, exhibit a reduced dependence on beam energy, and are more in line with results obtained from $(e,e'p)$ measurements.

# Transfer reactions and the dispersive optical-model

## Key claim

The dispersive optical-model (DOM) describes angular distributions for (d,p) transfer reactions as well as or better than the standard global CH89 parameterization, while also constraining the overlap function; however, the DOM's performance depends on the specific nucleus and beam energy, with notable discrepancies for 208Pb where DOM optical potentials move angular distributions away from the data.

## Method

The authors performed finite-range adiabatic wave approximation (FR-ADWA) calculations for (d,p) reactions on closed-shell nuclei (40Ca, 48Ca, 132Sn, 208Pb) at multiple beam energies, using three interaction models: (1) CH89 global optical potentials with Woods-Saxon bound states, (2) DOM optical potentials with Woods-Saxon bound states, and (3) DOM optical potentials with DOM overlap functions corrected for non-locality. The DOM fits from Mueller (2011) provided all nucleon-target optical potentials and neutron bound-state interactions, with spectroscopic factors extracted by normalizing theoretical cross sections to experimental data at the first peak or at backward angles for sub-barrier energies.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca(d,p)49Ca at Ed=2 MeV, normalization was performed at θ≈155° (backward angles, sub-barrier)
- For 48Ca(d,p)49Ca at Ed=19.3 MeV, normalization was at θ≈10° (first peak)
- For 48Ca(d,p)49Ca at Ed=56 MeV, normalization was at θ≈5° (forward angles)
- Spectroscopic factor for 49Ca using CH89+WS ranged from S=0.77 (at 19.3 MeV) to S=1.1 (at 56 MeV)
- Spectroscopic factor for 49Ca using DOM ranged from S=0.61 (at 13 MeV) to S=0.66 (at 2 MeV), showing reduced energy dependence
- DOM spectroscopic factor for 133Sn at Ed=9.46 MeV was S=0.72, compared to S=1.1 for CH89+WS
- DOM spectroscopic factor for 209Pb at Ed=8 MeV was S=1.2, and at Ed=20 MeV was S=0.51
- DOM theoretical spectroscopic factor (without transfer data) for 41Ca was 0.75, for 49Ca was 0.80, for 133Sn was 0.80, and for 209Pb was 0.76
- RMS radius for 49Ca DOM overlap function was 4.820 fm versus 4.606 fm for Woods-Saxon
- RMS radius for 133Sn DOM overlap function was 6.513 fm versus 6.080 fm for Woods-Saxon
- FR-ADWA accuracy relative to full Faddeev solutions for 48Ca(d,p) was 6% at 19 MeV and 3% at 56 MeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersive optical-model approach of Mahaux and Sartor (1991) and the local DOM implementations by Charity (2006, 2007) and Mueller (2011) to transfer reactions, connecting scattering and bound-state information that had previously been treated separately. The FR-ADWA method, validated against exact Faddeev solutions by Nunes (2011), provides a practical framework for testing DOM potentials. The results enable future extraction of nucleon properties in rare isotopes by providing a consistent framework where both reaction and structure information are linked, though the discrepancies for 208Pb and the sensitivity of spectroscopic factors to the particle-hole gap in DOM fits indicate areas requiring further refinement.
