# Nucleus-nucleus potential with shell-correction contribution and deep sub-barrier fusion of heavy nuclei

> It is suggested that the full nucleus-nucleus potential consists of the macroscopic and shell-correction parts. The deep sub-barrier fusion hindrance takes place in a nucleus-nucleus system with a strong negative shell-correction contribution to the full heavy-ion potential, while a strong positive shell-correction contribution to the full potential leads to weak enhancement of the deep sub-barrier fusion cross section.

# Nucleus-nucleus potential with shell-correction contribution and deep sub-barrier fusion of heavy nuclei

## Key claim
The full nucleus-nucleus potential should include both a macroscopic part and a shell-correction contribution derived from the Strutinsky prescription; the sign and magnitude of this shell-correction term determine whether deep sub-barrier fusion is hindered (negative shell-correction sum) or weakly enhanced (positive shell-correction sum), but the effect is energy-dependent and diminishes at higher collision energies via an exponential reduction factor.

## Method
The author constructs the total heavy-ion potential as the sum of a macroscopic Woods-Saxon nuclear potential plus Coulomb potential and a microscopic shell-correction term. The shell-correction contribution is modeled using a Fermi-type function that smoothly interpolates between the sum of the isolated-nuclei shell-correction energies at large separations and the shell-correction energy of the compound nucleus at R=0, with an additional exponential energy-dependent factor that suppresses the shell-correction contribution when the collision energy exceeds the barrier height. Fusion cross sections are computed using the coupled-channel code CCFULL, including couplings to low-energy surface vibrational states (2+, 3-, and mutual couplings), and compared to experimental data for three reactions.

## Key numbers
- Shell-correction energies: 16O = -5.249 MeV, 208Pb = -10.620 MeV, 48Ca = -2.648 MeV, 58Ni = -4.453 MeV, 54Fe = -3.840 MeV
- Macroscopic binding energy formula fitted to 3353 nuclei with a root mean error of 2.49 MeV
- Reduction parameter a = 0.35 MeV^-1 for the energy-dependent suppression of the shell-correction contribution
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters for 16O+208Pb: V0 = 18.462 MeV/fm, r0 = 1.2270 fm, d = 0.54592 fm, r0^sh = 1.1826 fm, d_sh = 0.21970 fm
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters for 48Ca+48Ca: V0 = 15.719 MeV/fm, r0 = 1.2307 fm, d = 0.60868 fm, r0^sh = 1.2041 fm, d_sh = 0.27687 fm
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters for 58Ni+54Fe: V0 = 19.607 MeV/fm, r0 = 1.2288 fm, d = 0.58794 fm, r0^sh = 1.2016 fm, d_sh = 0.20269 fm

## Context
This work extends the Strutinsky shell-correction method (Strutinsky, 1966-1968) to the nucleus-nucleus interaction potential, building on earlier studies of shell effects in nuclear potentials (Denisov and Reshitko, 1996; Denisov, 2013). It addresses the long-standing puzzle of deep sub-barrier fusion hindrance observed in reactions such as 16O+208Pb (Evers et al., 2011), 48Ca+48Ca (Montagnoli et al., 2012), and 58Ni+54Fe (Stefanini et al., 2010), which cannot be explained by standard coupled-channel models that work well at near-barrier energies. The approach enables predictions for systems with positive shell-correction energies, where weak enhancement rather than hindrance is expected at deep sub-barrier energies, and can be incorporated into existing reaction codes that use Woods-Saxon potentials.
