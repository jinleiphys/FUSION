# Study of electron capture rates on chromium isotopes for core-collapse simulations

> Electron capture rates on \emph{fp}-shell nuclei play a pivotal role in the dynamics of stellar evolution and core collapse. These rates play a crucial role in the gravitational collapse of the core of a massive star activating the supernova explosion. As per simulation results, capture rates on chromium isotopes have a major impact on controlling the lepton-to-baryon fraction of the stellar core during the late phases of evolution of massive stars. In this paper we calculate the electron capture rates on isotopes of chromium with mass range $42\leq A \leq 65$, including neutron-deficient and neutron-rich isotopes. For the calculation of weak rates in stellar matter, we used the pn-QRPA model with separable Gamow-Teller forces and took deformation of nucleus into consideration. A recent study proved this form of pn-QRPA to be the best for calculation of GT strength distributions amongst the pn-QRPA models. The stellar weak rates are calculated over a broad range of temperature $(0.01 \times 10^{9}-30 \times 10^{9} (K))$ and density $(10-10^{11}(g/cm^{3}))$ domain. We compare our electron capture rates with the pioneering calculation of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN) and with the large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculation. Our electron capture rates are enhanced compared to the FFN and shell model rates.

# Study of electron capture rates on chromium isotopes for core-collapse simulations

## Key claim

The authors calculate electron capture rates on 24 chromium isotopes (A=42–65) using the pn-QRPA model with separable Gamow-Teller forces and nuclear deformation, finding that their rates are enhanced compared to the earlier Fuller-Fowler-Newman (FFN) and large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations. The enhancement is attributed to the pn-QRPA model placing GT centroid energies at lower excitation energies in daughter nuclei, which translates into stronger weak rates in astrophysical environments.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with separable Gamow-Teller forces, incorporating nuclear deformation via the Nilsson model and pairing within the BCS approximation. Single-particle energies and wave functions were calculated in the Nilsson model, and the particle-hole and particle-particle interaction constants were chosen as χ = 4.2/A and κ = 0.10, respectively. The calculated GT strength distributions were quenched by a factor of (0.6)², and electron capture rates were computed over a temperature range of (0.01×10⁹ – 30×10⁹) K and density range of (10 – 10¹¹) g/cm³, including contributions from parent excited states via Boltzmann weighting.

## Key numbers

- Electron capture rates were calculated for 24 chromium isotopes in the mass range 42 ≤ A ≤ 65.
- Temperature range: (0.01×10⁹ – 30×10⁹) K; density range: (10 – 10¹¹) g/cm³.
- The pn-QRPA results were quenched by a factor of f_q² = (0.6)² for GT strength calculations.
- The particle-hole interaction constant was set to χ = 4.2/A, and the particle-particle constant to κ = 0.10.
- The Nilsson oscillator constant was taken as ħω = 41A⁻¹/³ MeV.
- Pairing gaps were set to Δ_p = Δ_n = 12/√A MeV.
- The constant D in the ft-value relation was taken as 6295 s, and g_A/g_V as -1.254.
- The re-normalized Ikeda sum rule ISR_re-norm = ΣB(GT₋) – ΣB(GT₊) ≅ 3f_q²(N–Z) was satisfied to within a few percent deviation.
- For isotopes ⁵⁶Cr and ⁵⁸Cr at low density and low temperature, the pn-QRPA and LSSM rates are enhanced by approximately seven orders of magnitude compared to FFN rates.
- At high temperatures, pn-QRPA electron capture rates are enhanced by about one order of magnitude compared to LSSM rates.
- For neutron-deficient isotopes ⁴²⁻⁴⁷Cr at T = (1, 5, 10)×10⁹ K and density 10⁷ g/cm³, positron decay rates exceed electron capture rates by 1–2 orders of magnitude.
- At high densities (10⁹ – 10¹¹) g/cm³, electron capture rates exceed positron decay rates by 1–4 orders of magnitude.
- For isotopes with N ≥ 31, calculated positron decay rates become less than 10⁻¹⁰⁰ s⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the earlier FFN calculations (Fuller, Fowler, and Newman, 1980) and the large-scale shell model calculations (Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo, 2001) for electron capture rates on chromium isotopes, which are identified as key nuclei in the Aufderheide list (Aufderheide et al., 1994) for presupernova evolution. The pn-QRPA model used here was previously shown by Sadiq et al. (2015) to be the best among six pn-QRPA models for calculating GT strength distributions in chromium isotopes. The enhanced electron capture rates reported here may significantly impact the lepton-to-baryon fraction (Y_e) during core collapse and the shock wave energetics in supernova simulations, and the authors call for testing these rates in collapse simulation codes.
