# Charge radii of potassium isotopes in the RMF(BCS)* approach

> We apply the recently proposed RMF(BCS)* ansatz to study the charge radii of the potassium isotopic chain up to $^{52}$K. It is shown that the experimental data can be reproduced rather well, qualitatively similar to the Fayans nuclear density functional theory, but with a slightly better description of the odd-even staggerings (OES). Nonetheless, both methods fail for $^{50}$K and to a lesser extent for $^{48,52}$K. It is shown that if these nuclei are deformed with a $β_{20}\approx-0.2$, then one can obtain results consistent with experiments for both charge radii and spin-parities. We argue that beyond mean field studies are needed to properly describe the charge radii of these three nuclei, particularly for $^{50}$K.

# Charge radii of potassium isotopes in the RMF(BCS)* approach

## Key claim
The RMF(BCS)* ansatz reproduces experimental charge radii of potassium isotopes qualitatively similar to the Fayans nuclear density functional theory, with slightly better description of odd-even staggerings (OES), but both methods fail for 50K and to a lesser extent for 48K and 52K. The authors argue that if these three nuclei are deformed with a quadrupole deformation parameter β20 ≈ -0.2, then results consistent with experiments for both charge radii and spin-parities can be obtained, though beyond-mean-field studies are needed for a proper description.

## Method
The authors applied the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with a BCS pairing treatment, using the NL3 parameter set and a modified charge radius formula that adds a correction term proportional to the difference of Cooper pair condensations for neutrons and protons (ΔD = |Dn - Dp|). The pairing strength was fixed at 350 MeV·fm³ with a pairing space of 24 MeV above and below the Fermi surface, and a blocking approximation was used for odd-nucleon systems. For 48K, 50K, and 52K, the authors manually assigned different single-particle orbital occupations for the last unpaired proton and neutron and computed potential energy surfaces as functions of the quadrupole deformation parameter β20.

## Key numbers
- The pairing strength used in the RMF(BCS)* approach is fixed at 350 MeV·fm³.
- The normalization constant a0 in the correction term is 0.834, obtained by fitting to experimental charge radii.
- The standard rms deviation between experimental and theoretical charge radii for the Fayans model is 0.0214 fm, while for the RMF(BCS)* approach it falls to 0.0150 fm.
- For 48K with the manually chosen (1f7/2, 2p3/2) configuration, the charge radius is 3.481 fm, agreeing with experiment.
- For 48K, 50K, and 52K with deformation restricted to β20 ≈ -0.20, the charge radii are 3.4882 fm, 3.5210 fm, and 3.5463 fm respectively, consistent with experimental data.

## Context
This work extends the RMF(BCS)* ansatz previously proposed by An et al. (2020) for calcium isotopes and nine other even-Z isotopic chains to the odd-proton potassium isotopic chain. It directly compares with the Fayans energy density functional (EDF) model results from Koszorus et al. (2020), which reproduced the strong increase in charge radii beyond N=28 but overestimated OES effects. The study is motivated by recent collinear resonance ionization spectroscopy (CRIS) measurements of potassium charge radii by PhysRevC.100.034304 and Koszorus et al. (2020), which provided the first precision measurements beyond N=32 for Z<20. The results suggest that beyond-mean-field studies incorporating configuration mixing are needed to properly describe 48K, 50K, and 52K, particularly 50K.
