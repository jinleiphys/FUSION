# Laser assisted proton collision on light nuclei at moderate energies

> We present analytic angular differential cross section model for laser assisted proton nucleon scattering on a Woods-Saxon optical potential where the nth-order photon absorption is taken into account simultaneously. As a physical example we calculate cross sections for proton - $^{12}$C collision at 49 MeV in the laboratory frame where the laser intensity is in the range of $ 10^{7} - 10^{21}$ W/cm$^2$ at optical frequencies. The upper intensity limit is slightly below the relativistic regime.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytic model for laser-assisted proton-nucleus scattering that includes simultaneous nth-order photon absorption, but the model is limited to non-relativistic dipole approximation and first Born approximation, with the upper laser intensity limit of \(10^{21}\) W/cm\(^2\) kept slightly below the relativistic regime for protons.

## Method

The authors solve the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation with minimal coupling for a proton scattering on a Woods-Saxon optical potential in a linearly polarized laser field, using Volkov wave functions for the initial and final proton states. The transition amplitude is calculated in the first Born approximation, yielding a differential cross section formula that factors into the product of the field-free Born cross section and a squared Bessel function \(J_n^2(z)\) describing nth-order photon absorption or emission. The scattering potential includes a regularized Coulomb term from a uniform charged sphere and a full Woods-Saxon optical potential with volume, surface, and spin-orbit terms, whose analytic Fourier transforms are used.

## Key numbers

- Proton energy: 49 MeV in the laboratory frame for proton-\(^{12}\)C collision.
- Laser wavelength: 800 nm (photon energy \(\hbar \omega_0 = 1.56\) eV).
- Laser intensity range: \(10^{7}\) to \(10^{21}\) W/cm\(^2\).
- Critical intensity for protons at 800 nm: \(I_{crit} = 3.91 \times 10^{21}\) W/cm\(^2\).
- Total elastic cross section (no laser): 201 mbarn, consistent with data from Abd (year not given in text).
- At \(I = 4.56 \times 10^{7}\) W/cm\(^2\): single, double, and triple photon absorption total cross sections are 2.26, 1.1, and 0.71 mbarn, respectively.
- At \(I = 4.56 \times 10^{11}\) W/cm\(^2\): single photon absorption total cross section is 0.5 mbarn.
- At \(10^{7}\) W/cm\(^2\) laser intensity, the elastic Born total cross section is a factor of 90 times higher than the single-photon absorption cross section.

## Context

This work extends the optical potential formalism, which has been successful for decades in nuclear single-particle spectra (Greiner, 1990s; Gerton, 1970s), by incorporating laser assistance into proton-nucleus scattering, a topic with no prior publications according to the authors. The analytic Fourier transform of the Woods-Saxon potential from Hlophe (2010s) is used, and the laser-assisted scattering formalism builds on prior work by Bunkin (1970s), Faisal (1970s), Bergou (1980s), and Kroll (1970s). The results are intended to stimulate experimental laser-assisted nuclear scattering at the ELI facility in Romania.
