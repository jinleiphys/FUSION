# Scaling Behavior in Soliton Models

> In the framework of chiral soliton models we study the behavior of static nucleon properties under rescaling of the parameters describing the effective meson theory. In particular we investigate the question of whether the Brown--Rho scaling laws are general features of such models. When going beyond the simple Skyrme model we find that restrictive constraints need to be imposed on the mesonic parameters in order to maintain these scaling laws. Furthermore, in the case when vector mesons are included in the model it turns out that the isoscalar form factor no longer scales according to these laws. Finally we note that, in addition to the exact scaling laws of the model, one may construct approximate {\it local scaling laws}, which depend of the particular choice of Lagrangian parameters.

# Scaling Behavior in Soliton Models

## Key claim

The Brown-Rho scaling laws, which hold exactly in the simple Skyrme model, do not automatically hold in more realistic soliton models that include vector mesons; restrictive constraints must be imposed on the mesonic parameters to maintain these scaling laws, and the isoscalar form factor no longer scales according to these laws when vector mesons are present.

## Method

The authors study static nucleon properties under rescaling of the parameters describing the effective meson theory by first constructing a universal expression for the soliton mass through field and coordinate redefinitions, absorbing as many parameters as possible. They then apply these redefinitions to examine the behavior of the nucleon mass, axial coupling constant, and isoscalar radius in both the pseudoscalar Skyrme model and a vector meson model that includes rho and omega mesons, deriving conditions under which scaling laws hold.

## Key numbers

- In the simple Skyrme model, the universal combinations are A = M/(f_pi sqrt(g_A)) = 17.2 and B = <r^2>_{I=0} f_pi^2 / g_A = 0.062.
- The classical soliton mass in the Skyrme model scales as E_cl = 72.9 f_pi / e.
- The isoscalar radius in the Skyrme model scales as <r^2>_{I=0} = 1.12 / (f_pi^2 e^2).
- The axial coupling constant in the Skyrme model scales as g_A = 18.0 / e^2.
- For the vector meson model, the vector meson coupling constant is g ≈ 5.6, with parameters tilde_h = - (2 sqrt(2)/3) gamma_1 ≈ 0.4, tilde_g_{VVphi} = g gamma_2 ≈ 1.9, and kappa = gamma_3 / gamma_2 ≈ 1.
- At nuclear density (x = x_E = 0.35) with c = -0.34, the vector meson model yields m_rho^* / m_rho ≈ 0.78, g_A^* / g_A ≈ 0.80, and M^* / M ≈ 0.60.
- The quantity B decreases by about 17% at x_E, while the modified quantity tilde_B varies by only about 5%.

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling laws (Brown and Rho, 1991, 1995) derived from the simple Skyrme model to more realistic soliton models containing vector mesons. The authors show that the scaling laws derived by Brown and Rho require additional constraints when going beyond the simplest model, particularly regarding the KSRF relation (Kawarabayashi, 1966) and the scaling of anomalous coupling constants. The study enables future tests of scaling laws at RHIC and highlights that the density dependence of coefficients in the epsilon terms remains poorly known, making confirmation of specific scaling laws difficult until the a priori scaling behavior of anomalous terms is better understood.
