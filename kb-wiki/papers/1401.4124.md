# Structure of longitudinal chromomagnetic fields in high energy collisions

> We compute expectation values of spatial Wilson loops in the forward light cone of high-energy collisions. We consider ensembles of gauge field configurations generated from a classical Gaussian effective action as well as solutions of high-energy renormalization group evolution with fixed and running coupling. The initial fields correspond to a color field condensate exhibiting domain-like structure over distance scales of order the saturation scale. At later times universal scaling emerges at large distances for all ensembles, with a nontrivial critical exponent. Finally, we compare the results for the Wilson loop to the two-point correlator of magnetic fields.

## Key claim
The paper claims that spatial Wilson loops in the glasma field exhibit universal scaling behavior at later times (τ ≳ 3/Qs) independent of the initial condition (MV model or JIMWLK-evolved ensembles), with a nontrivial critical exponent γ_IR ≈ 1.2–1.3 that gradually approaches area-law scaling (γ = 1) at late times.

## Method
The authors performed lattice simulations of classical Yang–Mills equations in 2+1D boost-invariant coordinates, starting from three different ensembles of initial gauge field configurations: the McLerran–Venugopalan (MV) model, and fixed-coupling and running-coupling JIMWLK-evolved configurations. They measured expectation values of spatial Wilson loops (square loops in the transverse plane) and two-point correlators of the longitudinal magnetic field B_z, both at initial time τ = 0 and at later proper times up to Q_s τ = 10.

## Key numbers
- For the MV model at τ = 0, the IR string tension σ/Q_s² = 0.44 (N_⊥ = 2048 lattice).
- At τ = 0, the UV string tension σ/Q_s² = 0.59 (MV), 0.55 (rcJIMWLK), 0.56 (fcJIMWLK) on N_⊥ = 1024 lattice.
- At late times Q_s τ ≳ 3, the IR exponent γ_IR ≈ 1.2–1.3, with values within 10% across all initial conditions.
- The magnetic field correlator at Q_s τ = 10 follows approximately C_B(r) ∼ r^{−1.55}, consistent with the Wilson loop exponent γ = 1.225 via a naive scaling relation.
- The Wilson loop at τ = 0 shows domain-like structure with typical area ∼ 1/Q_s²; loops of area ∼ 1.5 Q_s² are invariant under high-energy evolution.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of spatial Wilson loops in the glasma by Dumitru et al. (2013) and magnetic field correlators by Dumitru et al. (2013), which used only the MV model and found area-law scaling for radii ≳ 1/Q_s. The present paper broadens the analysis to include JIMWLK-evolved initial conditions (both fixed and running coupling) and studies the time evolution up to Q_s τ = 10. The universal scaling observed at later times mirrors a similar universality seen in the IR gluon spectrum by Lappi (2011). The results enable further understanding of the glasma phase and its role in pre-thermalization in high-multiplicity collisions, as discussed by Floerchinger and Martinez (2013).
