# Sum rules of single-particle spectral functions in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

> The neutron and proton single-particle spectral functions in asymmetric nuclear matter fulfill energy weighted sum rules. The validity of these sum rules within the self-consistent Green's function approach is investigated. The various contributions to these sum rules and their convergence as a function of energy provide information about correlations induced by the realistic interaction between the nucleons. These features are studied as a function of the asymmetry of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The energy-weighted sum rules for single-particle spectral functions are well satisfied within the self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) approach using the T-matrix approximation, but the paper's main physical finding is that the near-equality of the correlated and Hartree-Fock (HF) sum-rule values arises from a compensation: correlations make the quasi-particle energy more attractive (lowering the m1 sum-rule contribution) while simultaneously shifting spectral strength to high positive energies (raising it), so the net m1 value remains close to the HF result even in highly asymmetric matter.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson equation for the single-particle Green's function in asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature (T = 5 MeV) and fixed density (ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³) using a fully self-consistent ladder approximation (T-matrix) with the charge-dependent Bonn (CDBONN) potential. They computed the neutron and proton spectral functions A_ν(k,ω) for three proton fractions (x_p = 0.50, 0.30, 0.04) and evaluated the zeroth (m₀) and first (m₁) energy-weighted sum rules by comparing the asymptotic expansion of the Green's function with the Lehmann representation. The contributions from the hole-like part (A_ν^<) and particle-like part (A_ν^>) were separated using the Fermi-Dirac distribution.

## Key numbers

* At ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and T = 5 MeV, the m₀ sum rule is fulfilled with accuracy better than 0.1% for both neutrons and protons across all momenta.
* The m₁ sum rule is satisfied better than 1% in all cases (neutrons and protons at x_p = 0.5, 0.3, and 0.04).
* For symmetric matter (x_p = 0.50) at k = 400 MeV, the quasi-particle energies for neutrons and protons are identical: ε_qp = 36.45 MeV.
* At x_p = 0.30, the quasi-particle energies split: ε_qp^n = 41.88 MeV (neutrons) and ε_qp^p = 30.86 MeV (protons).
* At x_p = 0.04, the splitting increases: ε_qp^n = 49.35 MeV and ε_qp^p = 25.22 MeV.
* The isospin splitting of the Hartree-Fock energies at k = 0 for x_p = 0.04 is about 10 MeV, while the quasi-particle energy splitting at the same momentum is about 55 MeV.
* At x_p = 0.04 and k ~ 160 MeV, the proton m₁ contribution from A_p^> reaches almost -15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous SCGF sum-rule analyses for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature (Pol94) and finite temperature (Frick04) to the asymmetric case, using the same CDBONN potential. It builds on the SCGF calculations of hot asymmetric matter by Frick05, which provided the bulk properties and momentum distributions. The results confirm that the T-matrix approximation respects the analytic structure of the Green's function, and the compensation between attractive quasi-particle shifts and repulsive high-energy tails explains why the HF approximation gives a good estimate of m₁ even in correlated systems. The analysis enables future studies of isospin-dependent short-range correlations and provides a numerical consistency check for SCGF calculations at finite temperature and asymmetry.
