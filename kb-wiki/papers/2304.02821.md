# Asymptotic normalization coefficients of alpha-particle removal from $^{16}$O($3^-,2^+,1^-$)

> Asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) determine the overall normalization of cross sections of peripheral radiative capture reactions. In a recent paper [Blokhintsev et al., Eur. Phys. J. A 58, 257 (2022)], we considered the ANC $C_0$ for the virtual decay $^{16}$O$(0^+; 6.05$ MeV)$\to α+^{12}$C(g.s.). In the present paper, which can be regarded as a continuation of the previous, we treat the ANCs $C_l$ for the vertices $^{16}$O$(J^π)\to α+^{12}$C(g.s.) corresponding to the other three bound excited states of $^{16}$O ($J^π=3^-$, $2^+$, $1^-$, $l=J$). ANCs $C_l$ ($l=3,\,2,\,1$) are found by analytic continuation in energy of the $α^{12}$C $l$-wave partial scattering amplitudes, known from the phase-shift analysis of experimental data, to the pole corresponding to the $^{16}$O bound state and lying in the unphysical region of negative energies. To determine $C_l$, the scattering data are approximated by the sum of polynomials in energy in the physical region and then extrapolated to the pole. For a more reliable determination of the ANCs, various forms of functions expressed in terms of phase shifts were used in analytical approximation and subsequent extrapolation.

## Key claim

The asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for the virtual decays of the three bound excited states of 16O (Jπ = 3−, 2+, 1−) to α + 12C(g.s.) are determined by analytic continuation of α-12C elastic scattering phase shifts to negative energies, with the caveat that the extracted values depend on the choice of approximating function and the energy range used for the polynomial fit.

## Method

The authors use the Δ-method: they construct the function Δ̃_l(E) from the experimental α-12C elastic scattering phase shifts of Tischhauser (2009) for l = 1, 2, 3, then approximate it (or a transformed version that removes poles and zeros) by a sum of Chebyshev polynomials in the physical energy region (E > 0). The polynomial is then extrapolated to the bound-state pole at E = −ε, where ε is the binding energy of the 16O excited state in the α + 12C channel. The ANC is extracted from the residue of the renormalized Coulomb-nuclear partial-wave amplitude at that pole. For the 2+ state, the function G(E) is introduced to remove two poles and two zeros in Δ̃_2(E) before approximation.

## Key numbers

*   C3 for 16O(3−) with binding energy ε = 1.032 MeV: (2.17 ± 0.05) × 10^2 fm−1/2.
*   C2 for 16O(2+) with binding energy ε = 0.245 MeV: (1.42 ± 0.05) × 10^5 fm−1/2.
*   C1 for 16O(1−) with binding energy ε = 0.045 MeV: (2.27 ± 0.02) × 10^14 fm−1/2.
*   The polynomial approximation used 20 experimental points for laboratory α energy Eα in the range 2.61 – 6.20 MeV, with a narrower range (up to Eα = 4.31 MeV) also tested for C3 and C1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous determination of the ANC C0 for the 16O(0+; 6.05 MeV) state (Blokhintsev et al., Eur. Phys. J. A 58, 257, 2022) to the three remaining bound excited states of 16O. The results are compared with a wide spread of prior values from Brune (2002), Balhout (2007), Oulevsir (2012), Avila (2015), Orlov (2013, 2014), Sparen (2012), and Ando (2001). The new C3 value exceeds previous results, the new C2 is close to the maximum of prior values, and the new C1 is slightly above most earlier determinations. The authors propose a new, more rigorous method for analytic continuation based on subtracting a square-well background from the effective-range function, which they plan to test in future work.
