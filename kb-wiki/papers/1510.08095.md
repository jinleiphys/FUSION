# A Scaling Phenomenon in Shannon Information Uncertainty Difference of fragments in Heavy-ion Collisions

> The Shannon information-entropy uncertainty (in brief as "information uncertainty") is used to analyze the fragments in the measured 140$A$ MeV $^{40, 48}$Ca + $^{9}$Be and $^{58, 64}$Ni + $^{9}$Be reactions. A scaling phenomenon is found in the information-uncertainty difference of fragments between the reactions. The scaling phenomenon is explained in a manner of canonical ensemble theory, and is reproduced in the simulated reactions by the antisymmetric molecular dynamics (AMD) and AMD + GEMINI models. The probes based on information uncertainty, requiring no equilibrium state of reaction, can be used in the non-equilibrium system, and bridge the results of the static thermodynamics models and the evolving dynamical transport models.

## Key claim
The paper reports a scaling phenomenon in the Shannon information-entropy uncertainty difference of fragments between neutron-rich and symmetric projectile reactions at 140A MeV, where the scaling parameter \(S_{\Delta_{21}In} = \Delta_{21}In_{(I, I + m, A)}/(m/2)\) becomes approximately constant for \(m = 2, 4, 6\). The scaling is explained within canonical ensemble theory and is reproduced by antisymmetric molecular dynamics (AMD) simulations, but the quality of scaling depends on the specific reaction pair (better for \({}^{48}\)Ca/\({}^{40}\)Ca than for \({}^{64}\)Ni/\({}^{58}\)Ni).

## Method
The authors analyzed experimental fragment cross sections from 140A MeV \({}^{40,48}\)Ca + \({}^{9}\)Be and \({}^{58,64}\)Ni + \({}^{9}\)Be reactions measured at NSCL (Michigan State University) by Mocko et al. (2006). They defined the Shannon information uncertainty \(In_{(I,A)} = -\ln\sigma(I,A)\) for each fragment with neutron-excess \(I = N-Z\) and mass number \(A\), then computed differences between isobars differing by 2, 4, and 6 units in \(I\) within each reaction, and finally took the difference of those isobaric differences between two reactions (\(\Delta_{21}In\)). The scaling parameter \(S_{\Delta_{21}In}\) was defined as \(\Delta_{21}In_{(I, I + m, A)}/(m/2)\) for \(m = 2, 4, 6\). The scaling was also tested in AMD and AMD+GEMINI simulations of the \({}^{58,64}\)Ni + \({}^{9}\)Be reactions using the Gogny-g0 interaction with a coalescence radius of 5 fm at \(t = 500\) fm/c.

## Key numbers
- Plateau values of \(\Delta_{21}In_{(I, I + 2, A)}\) are \(-1.9 \pm 0.3\) for the \({}^{48}\)Ca/\({}^{40}\)Ca reactions and \(-1.2 \pm 0.4\) for the \({}^{64}\)Ni/\({}^{58}\)Ni reactions.
- The plateau of \(\Delta_{21}In_{(I, I + 4, A)}\) is approximately twice that of \(\Delta_{21}In_{(I, I + 2, A)}\).
- The scaling parameter \(S_{\Delta_{21}In}\) shows a well-scaled plateau for the \({}^{48}\)Ca/\({}^{40}\)Ca reactions across \(m = 2, 4, 6\); for the \({}^{64}\)Ni/\({}^{58}\)Ni reactions the scaling is present but with a larger range of values for isobars.
- The AMD and AMD+GEMINI simulations reproduce the scaling phenomenon and the plateau range for the \({}^{64}\)Ni/\({}^{58}\)Ni reactions.

## Context
This work extends the use of Shannon information entropy in heavy-ion collisions, previously applied to measure chaoticity in hadron decays (EntrHC) and to probe the liquid-gas phase transition (Ma, 1999). The scaling phenomenon is analogous to the isoscaling phenomenon of fragments (HShan, 1998), which has been explained using canonical ensemble theory (GrandCan, Tsang, 2007) and related to chemical potential differences and symmetry energy. The key advance is that the information-uncertainty probe does not require thermal equilibrium, unlike standard thermodynamic probes, allowing it to bridge results from static thermodynamic models and evolving dynamical transport models such as AMD.
