# Vacuum birefringence in strong magnetic fields: (I) Photon polarization tensor with all the Landau levels

> Photons propagating in strong magnetic fields are subject to a phenomenon called the "vacuum birefringence" where refractive indices of two physical modes both deviate from unity and are different from each other. We compute the vacuum polarization tensor of a photon in a static and homogeneous magnetic field by utilizing Schwinger's proper-time method, and obtain a series representation as a result of double integrals analytically performed with respect to proper-time variables. The outcome is expressed in terms of an infinite sum of known functions which is plausibly interpreted as summation over all the Landau levels of fermions. Each contribution from infinitely many Landau levels yields a kinematical condition above which the contribution has an imaginary part. This indicates decay of a sufficiently energetic photon into a fermion-antifermion pair with corresponding Landau level indices. Since we do not resort to any approximation, our result is applicable to the calculation of refractive indices in the whole kinematical region of a photon momentum and in any magnitude of the external magnetic field.

## Key claim

The authors claim to have obtained an analytic expression for the one-loop vacuum polarization tensor of a photon in a static homogeneous magnetic field, expressed as an infinite sum over all Landau levels of fermions, without resorting to any approximation, making it applicable to any photon momentum and any magnetic field strength, though the result is presented as a series representation rather than a closed form.

## Method

The authors used Schwinger's proper-time method to compute the one-loop vacuum polarization tensor with a dressed fermion propagator that includes all-order tree-level interactions with the external magnetic field. They performed the double proper-time integrals analytically by expanding the integrand into a double infinite series, yielding an expression in terms of known functions summed over Landau levels. The calculation was done for a static homogeneous magnetic field oriented along the third spatial axis, with the fermion propagator computed in the Fock-Schwinger gauge.

## Key numbers

- The critical magnetic field for electrons is defined as \(B_c = m_e^2/|e|\), where \(m_e\) is the electron mass and \(e\) is the electron charge.
- The dimensionless magnetic field strength is defined as \(\Br = B/B_c\).
- The dimensionless longitudinal and transverse photon momenta are defined as \(r_\parallel^2 = q_\parallel^2/(4m^2)\) and \(r_\perp^2 = q_\perp^2/(4m^2) = -|\mathbf{q}_\perp|^2/(4m^2)\).
- The scalar function \(\chi_1\) is real-valued when \(r_\parallel^2 \leq 1\), while \(\chi_0\) and \(\chi_2\) are real-valued when \(r_\parallel^2 \leq (1+\sqrt{1+2\Br})^2/4\).
- The overall prefactor of the polarization tensor contains \(\alpha/4\pi\) where \(\alpha = e^2/4\pi\) is the fine-structure constant.

## Context

This work extends earlier integral representations of the vacuum polarization tensor in magnetic fields by Tsai (1974), Urrutia (1978), Adler (1971), and Tsai and Erber (1974), which were limited to restricted phase-space regions or weak-field approximations. The authors' analytic series representation overcomes the difficulty of rapidly oscillating integrands that prevented precise analytic and numerical understanding, enabling calculation of refractive indices for any photon momentum and magnetic field strength. The result enables future computation of complex refractive indices including photon decay into fermion-antifermion pairs, which the authors state will be presented in a forthcoming paper (Hattori and Itakura, in preparation).
