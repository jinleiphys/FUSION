# The no-drag frame for anomalous chiral fluid

> We show that for an anomalous fluid carrying dissipationless chiral magnetic and/or vortical currents there is a frame in which a stationary obstacle experiences no drag, but energy and charge currents do not vanish, resembling superfluidity. However, unlike ordinary superfluid flow, the anomalous chiral currents do transport entropy in this frame. We show that the second law of thermodynamics completely determines the amounts of these anomalous non-dissipative currents in the "no-drag frame" as polynomials in temperature and chemical potential with known anomaly coefficients. These general results are illustrated and confirmed by a calculation in the chiral kinetic theory and quark-gluon plasma at high temperature.

# The no-drag frame for anomalous chiral fluid

## Key claim

The authors show that for an anomalous fluid carrying dissipationless chiral magnetic and/or vortical currents, there exists a frame (the "no-drag frame") in which a stationary obstacle experiences no drag, yet energy and charge currents do not vanish, resembling superfluidity. However, unlike ordinary superfluid flow, the anomalous chiral currents do transport entropy in this frame, and the second law of thermodynamics completely determines the amounts of these currents as polynomials in temperature and chemical potential with known anomaly coefficients.

## Method

The authors derive the no-drag frame by imposing the second law of thermodynamics on the hydrodynamic equations for an anomalous chiral fluid, including a drag force term from an impurity or obstacle. They use the freedom to redefine the local fluid velocity frame (via shifts proportional to vorticity and magnetic field) to set the combinations K_omega and K_B to zero, which defines the frame where drag vanishes. The anomalous transport coefficients in this frame are then solved from the second-law constraints, yielding polynomial forms in temperature T and chemical potential mu. The results are confirmed by explicit calculations in chiral kinetic theory and in a high-temperature quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

- For the chiral vortical effect (CVE) in chiral kinetic theory: xi_{J omega} = mu^2/(4 pi^2) + T^2/12 (charge current coefficient)
- For CVE in chiral kinetic theory: xi_{T omega} = mu^3/(6 pi^2) + mu T^2/6 (energy current coefficient)
- For CVE in chiral kinetic theory: xi_{S omega} = mu T/6 (entropy current coefficient)
- The anomaly coefficient C = 1/(4 pi^2) appears in the chiral magnetic effect coefficient xi_{JB} = C mu
- The mixed gauge-gravity anomaly coefficient X_B = 1/12 appears in the entropy current coefficient xi_{SB} = X_B T
- The coefficient X_omega = 0 for the chiral kinetic theory example

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic treatment of anomalous chiral fluids with chiral magnetic and vortical effects, building on the second-law constraints derived by Son and Surowka (2009) and Neiman and Oz (2010). The no-drag frame provides a physically transparent interpretation of the special frame previously identified in holographic calculations by Chapman, Goutéraux, and Myers (2012) and in the ansatz of Loganayagam (2011). The results contradict the expectation from superfluid analogy (Kalaydzhyan, 2012) that entropy flow should vanish in this frame, and generalize the holographic findings of Rajagopal, Sadofyev, and van der Schee (2015) where entropy flow vanished only in the absence of mixed gauge-gravity anomaly. The work enables phenomenological studies of anomalous transport in heavy-ion collisions and Dirac semimetals, particularly regarding the thermomechanical effects of entropy-carrying persistent currents.
