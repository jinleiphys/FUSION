# Direct detection of dark matter polarizability

> We point out that the direct detection of dark matter via its electro-magnetic polarizability is described by two new nuclear form factors, which are controlled by the 2-nucleon nuclear density. The signature manifests a peculiar dependence on the atomic and mass numbers of the target nuclei, as well as on the momentum transfer, and can differ significantly from experiment to experiment. We also discuss UV completions of our scenario.

## Key claim

The direct detection of dark matter via its electromagnetic polarizability is dominated by a two-nucleon process rather than a one-nucleon process, and the resulting signature depends on two new nuclear form factors controlled by the 2-nucleon nuclear density; the authors note that the relative size of the two-body and one-body contributions is of order \(Z Q_0 / m_N\), which is not negligible for heavy nuclei, and that the hadronic uncertainties make it impossible to precisely determine the actual relation among the Wilson coefficients.

## Method

The authors derive the non-relativistic amplitude for the two-proton process \(ppX \to ppX\) from the nucleon-level effective field theory containing the DM polarizability operator \(O_\gamma = F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}\overline{X}X\) and the contact operators \(O_N = m_N\overline{N}N\overline{X}X\). They compute the resulting DM-nucleus scattering amplitude by convolving the two-body potential with the 2-proton nuclear density matrix, obtaining a 2-proton form factor \(F_{pp}(q)\). The differential cross section is then expressed in terms of this form factor, the standard one-proton charge form factor, and the Wilson coefficients \(C_\gamma\), \(C_p\), and \(C_n\), with the relative strength of the two-body to one-body contributions parameterized by \(f_{pp}/f_p\).

## Key numbers

* The two-body amplitude coefficient is \(V_0 = -8 e^2 C_\gamma(m_N) / \Lambda^3\) for the process \(ppX \to ppX\).
* The relative size between two-body and one-body contributions is \(\sim Z Q_0 / m_N\), where \(Q_0 = 0.5(0.3 + 0.9 A^{1/3})^{-1}\) GeV and \(m_N\) is the nucleon mass.
* The inelastic process \(XT \to XT\gamma\) is suppressed by a factor \(v^2 4\pi / \alpha \sim 10^{-3}\) compared to the elastic process.
* The phenomenological 2-proton form factor at small momentum transfer expands as \(\overline{F}_{pp}^{\rm pheno} = e^{-\bar{q}^2/2} [1 + \frac{1}{4}c_1 - (\pi^{3/2} / 2\sqrt{2}) \bar{q} + (\frac{5}{3} - \frac{5}{12}c_1 + c_2) \bar{q}^2 + O(\bar{q}^3)]\), where \(\bar{q} = |\mathbf{q}|/Q_0\).
* Numerical results are presented for DM masses of \(m_X = 10\) GeV and \(m_X = 100\) GeV, with the two-body form factor parameter \(c_2\) varied over \(-1, 0, +1\).

## Context

This work extends the study of dark matter polarizability first analyzed by Pospelov and ter Veldhuis (2000) in the limit of an infinitely heavy target nucleus, and later by Weiner and Yavin (2012) who estimated the rate using an effective field theory for the nucleus. The authors provide a more rigorous treatment using a nucleon-level EFT, showing that the dominant contribution comes from a two-body process involving two protons, and that the 2-proton form factor depends on nucleon-nucleon correlations that were neglected in prior work by Frandsen, Sannino, Shoemaker, and Svendsen (2012) and by Crivellin, Haisch, and Schmidt (2014). The results enable future direct detection experiments to distinguish the polarizability signature through its unique dependence on atomic number, mass number, and momentum transfer, and through the elliptical degeneracy patterns in the parameter space of \(f_{pp}/f_p\) versus \(f_n/f_p\).
