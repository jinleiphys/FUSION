# Mass signature of supernova $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ neutrinos in SuperKamiokande

> The $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ neutrinos (and their antiparticles) from a Galactic core-collapse supernova can be observed in a water-Čerenkov detector by the neutral-current excitation of $^{16}$O. The number of events expected is several times greater than from neutral-current scattering on electrons. The observation of this signal would be a strong test that these neutrinos are produced in core-collapse supernovae, and with the right characteristics. In this paper, this signal is used as the basis for a technique of neutrino mass determination from a future Galactic supernova. The masses of the $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ neutrinos can either be measured or limited by their delay relative to the $\barν_e$ neutrinos. By comparing to the high-statistics $\barν_e$ data instead of the theoretical expectation, much of the model dependence is canceled. Numerical results are presented for a future supernova at 10 kpc as seen in the SuperKamiokande detector. Under reasonable assumptions, and in the presence of the expected counting statistics, $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ masses down to about 50 eV can be simply and robustly determined. The signal used here is more sensitive to small neutrino masses than the signal based on neutrino-electron scattering.

## Key claim

The masses of $\nu_\mu$ and $\nu_\tau$ neutrinos from a Galactic core-collapse supernova can be measured or limited down to about 50 eV by comparing their arrival-time delay relative to the high-statistics $\bar{\nu}_e$ signal, rather than comparing to theoretical expectations, which cancels much of the model dependence.

## Method

The authors model the supernova neutrino emission with a Fermi-Dirac energy spectrum (temperatures of 8 MeV for $\nu_x$, 5 MeV for $\bar{\nu}_e$, 3.5 MeV for $\nu_e$) and an exponential luminosity decay with time constant 3 s. They calculate the expected event rates in SuperKamiokande for neutral-current excitation of $^{16}$O by $\nu_x$ neutrinos (producing detectable gamma rays between 5 and 10 MeV) and for $\bar{\nu}_e$ absorption on protons. Using Monte Carlo simulations of $10^4$ supernova realizations at 10 kpc, they perform both a $\chi^2$ shape test and an average-arrival-time test to determine the smallest $\nu_x$ mass distinguishable from zero given the expected counting statistics.

## Key numbers

- For a supernova at 10 kpc in SuperKamiokande, the expected number of $\nu_x$ events from $^{16}$O excitation is 355 for $\nu_\mu$ plus $\bar{\nu}_\mu$ combined, and 355 for $\nu_\tau$ plus $\bar{\nu}_\tau$ combined (total 710 events)
- The expected number of $\bar{\nu}_e$ events from absorption on protons is 8300 total, with 530 of those having positron energy $\leq$ 10 MeV
- The $\nu_x$ mass sensitivity down to about 50 eV can be achieved under reasonable assumptions with the expected counting statistics
- The energy-dependent time delay for a neutrino of mass $m$ (in eV) and energy $E$ (in MeV) over distance $D$ (in 10 kpc) is $\Delta t(E) = 0.515 (m/E)^2 D$ seconds
- The "Gamow peak" energy for the $^{16}$O excitation cross section is approximately 60 MeV, significantly above the average $\nu_x$ energy of 25 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of $\nu_x$ mass determination using supernova neutrinos (Wolfenstein, Seckel, Krauss, Burrows, Fiorentini) by using a different detection method: inelastic scattering on $^{16}$O nuclei followed by gamma decay, as suggested by Langanke, Vogel, and Kolbe (1996). The technique enables sensitivity to $\nu_\mu$ and $\nu_\tau$ masses down to about 50 eV, which is far below existing kinematic limits ($m_{\nu_\mu} < 170$ keV and $m_{\nu_\tau} < 24$ MeV) and compatible with the cosmological bound that the sum of neutrino masses is $\lesssim 100$ eV. The signal based on $^{16}$O excitation is more sensitive to small neutrino masses than the signal based on neutrino-electron scattering.
