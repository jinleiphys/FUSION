# Cluster effective field theory and nuclear reactions

> An effective field theory (EFT) for a nuclear reaction at low energies is studied. The astrophysical $S$-factor of radiative $α$ capture on $^{12}$C at the Gamow-peak energy, $T_G=0.3$ MeV, is a fundamental quantity in nuclear-astrophysics, and we construct an EFT for the reaction. To fix parameters appearing in the effective Lagrangian, the EFT is applied to the study for three reactions: elastic $α$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies, $E1$ transition of radiative $α$ capture on $^{12}$C, and $β$ delayed $α$ emission from $^{16}$N. We report an estimate of the $S_{E1}$-factor of the reaction through the $E1$ transition at $T_G$ by employing the EFT. We also discuss applications of EFTs to nuclear reactions at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an effective field theory (EFT) for radiative α capture on ¹²C at the Gamow-peak energy TG = 0.3 MeV, but the perturbative expansion (with expansion parameter Q/ΛH ∼ 1/3) is not reliable at the higher energies (T ≈ 1–5 MeV) where experimental data used to fix parameters are measured; instead, the dressed ¹⁶O propagator is treated non-perturbatively and reaction amplitudes are expanded perturbatively around it.

## Method

The EFT is built using point-like α and ¹²C fields with composite ¹⁶O fields introduced for each partial wave (l = 0,1,2,3), and the effective Lagrangian includes terms for elastic scattering, radiative capture, and β-delayed α emission. Parameters of the dressed ¹⁶O propagator (expressed via effective range expansion) are fitted to elastic α-¹²C scattering phase shift data at T ≈ 2–5 MeV and to binding energies of excited ¹⁶O states; additional coupling constants for the E1 transition amplitude are fitted to SE1 factor data at T ≈ 1–3 MeV, and the SE1 factor is then extrapolated to TG = 0.3 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Gamow-peak energy: TG = 0.3 MeV; corresponding Gamow momentum kG = √(2μ TG) ≈ 41 MeV, giving length scale kG⁻¹ ≈ 4.8 fm
- Large momentum scale ΛH ≈ 150 MeV, set by first excited states: T(4) = 20.21 MeV for ⁴He (0⁺₂) and T(12) = 4.44 MeV for ¹²C (2⁺₁)
- Expansion parameter Q/ΛH ∼ 1/3
- Subthreshold 1₁⁻ state of ¹⁶O at T = −0.045 MeV; subthreshold 2₁⁺ state at T = −0.24 MeV
- Resonant 1₂⁻ state at T = 2.42 MeV; resonant 2₂⁺ state at T = 2.68 MeV
- Emitted photon energy Eγ′ ≈ 7.64 MeV, giving photon length scale Eγ′⁻¹ ≈ 26 fm

## Context

This work extends the application of EFTs from few-nucleon systems (e.g., proton-proton fusion, ³He(α,γ)⁷Be, ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B) to heavier nuclei in nuclear astrophysics, specifically the ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O reaction. It contrasts with conventional R-matrix and K-matrix approaches (Lane and Thomas, 1958) and potential models, where subthreshold and resonant states are combined linearly with background contributions; here a single dressed ¹⁶O propagator represents both states, and the secondary peak in β-delayed α emission from ¹⁶N arises from interference between pole and non-pole diagrams rather than level interference. The approach enables a model-independent estimate of the SE1 factor at stellar energies, addressing the need for cross-checks on the predominantly R-matrix-based determinations.
