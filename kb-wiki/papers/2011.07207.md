# Cluster effective field theory and nuclear reactions

> An effective field theory (EFT) for a nuclear reaction at low energies is studied. The astrophysical $S$-factor of radiative $Оұ$ capture on $^{12}$C at the Gamow-peak energy, $T_G=0.3$ MeV, is a fundamental quantity in nuclear-astrophysics, and we construct an EFT for the reaction. To fix parameters appearing in the effective Lagrangian, the EFT is applied to the study for three reactions: elastic $Оұ$-$^{12}$C scattering at low energies, $E1$ transition of radiative $Оұ$ capture on $^{12}$C, and $ОІ$ delayed $Оұ$ emission from $^{16}$N. We report an estimate of the $S_{E1}$-factor of the reaction through the $E1$ transition at $T_G$ by employing the EFT. We also discuss applications of EFTs to nuclear reactions at low energies.

## Key claim

## Method

The EFT is built using point-like Оұ and В№ВІC fields with composite В№вҒ¶O fields introduced for each partial wave (l = 0,1,2,3), and the effective Lagrangian includes terms for elastic scattering, radiative capture, and ОІ-delayed Оұ emission. Parameters of the dressed В№вҒ¶O propagator (expressed via effective range expansion) are fitted to elastic Оұ-В№ВІC scattering phase shift data at T вүҲ 2вҖ“5 MeV and to binding energies of excited В№вҒ¶O states; additional coupling constants for the E1 transition amplitude are fitted to SE1 factor data at T вүҲ 1вҖ“3 MeV, and the SE1 factor is then extrapolated to TG = 0.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of EFTs from few-nucleon systems (e.g., proton-proton fusion, ВіHe(Оұ,Оі)вҒ·Be, вҒ·Be(p,Оі)вҒёB) to heavier nuclei in nuclear astrophysics, specifically the В№ВІC(Оұ,Оі)В№вҒ¶O reaction. It contrasts with conventional R-matrix and K-matrix approaches (Lane and Thomas, 1958) and potential models, where subthreshold and resonant states are combined linearly with background contributions; here a single dressed В№вҒ¶O propagator represents both states, and the secondary peak in ОІ-delayed Оұ emission from В№вҒ¶N arises from interference between pole and non-pole diagrams rather than level interference. The approach enables a model-independent estimate of the SE1 factor at stellar energies, addressing the need for cross-checks on the predominantly R-matrix-based determinations.
