# Nonequilibrium effects and transverse spherocity in ultra-relativistic proton-nucleus collisions

> We investigate the effects of nonequilibrium dynamics in small colliding systems by comparing a nonequilibrium transport approach, the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD), with a (2+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model, VISHNew. Focusing on p+Pb collisions at LHC energy, we extract the initial conditions for the hydrodynamic model from PHSD, in order to reduce the impact of the early out-of-equilibrium dynamics and focus on the traces of nonequilibiurm in the ensuing medium evolution. We find that in the transport approach quantities like energy density and bulk viscous pressure are highly inhomogeneous on the transverse plane during the whole evolution, whereas the hydrodynamic simulations dissolve more efficiently the initial spatial irregularities, still keeping a high degree of inhomogeneity due to the smaller size and lifetime of the medium produced in p+Pb collisions with respect to heavy-ion reactions. As a first step that will help to identify the impact of these nonequilibrium effects on final observables in proton-nucleus collisions, we perform an analysis of the transverse spherocity, an event-shape observable able to distinguish between jetty and isotropic configurations of transverse momenta. We found that the spherocity distribution in PHSD is slightly shifted towards the isotropic limit with respect to the hydrodynamic result. Even though this dissimilarity is partially due to the difference in the final charged particle production, it mainly comes from the different description within the two frameworks of the medium produced in small colliding systems. This finding supports the idea that multi-differential measurements, such as those based on event categorization according to multiplicity and spherocity, are useful to study final-state observables in ultrarelativistic proton-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The authors find that in p+Pb collisions at LHC energy, the nonequilibrium transport approach (PHSD) maintains highly inhomogeneous energy density and large bulk viscous pressure throughout the evolution, whereas the hydrodynamic model (VISHNew) smooths these irregularities more efficiently; however, the spherocity distribution shift between the two frameworks is "partially due to the difference in the final charged particle production" but "mainly comes from the different description within the two frameworks of the medium produced in small colliding systems."

## Method

The authors simulate p+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV using the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach and compare it to the (2+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model VISHNew. To isolate the effects of nonequilibrium dynamics in the medium evolution, they extract the initial conditions for VISHNew directly from PHSD at three different initialization times (\(t_0 = 0.2\), 0.4, and 0.6 fm/\(c\)), including the initial flow and viscous corrections. They then analyze the space-time evolution of energy density, temperature, velocity, viscous corrections, and the transverse spherocity event-shape observable.

## Key numbers

*   The collision energy studied is \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV for p+Pb collisions at the LHC.
*   The cell size used for extracting initial conditions from PHSD is \(\Delta x = \Delta y = 0.3\) fm on the transverse plane.
*   The critical energy density for the deconfinement transition in PHSD is \(e_c \approx 0.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
*   In a single PHSD event, the ratio of bulk viscous pressure to equilibrium pressure (\(-\Pi/P\)) is \(\gtrsim 0.8\) in most droplets at \(t = 2\) fm/\(c\), while in the hydrodynamic simulation this ratio becomes less than 0.3 after about 1 fm/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the comparison of transport and hydrodynamic descriptions from heavy-ion collisions, previously studied by Xu (2017) and Moreau (2017) for Au+Au collisions at RHIC, to the smaller p+Pb system at LHC energies. The use of PHSD to generate initial conditions for VISHNew follows the same strategy as those earlier works. The analysis of transverse spherocity as an event-shape observable in proton-nucleus collisions is a first step, building on proposals by Banfi (2010) for its use in p+p collisions and on theoretical studies by Khuntia (2018) and experimental measurements by the ALICE collaboration (2019). This work enables future multi-differential measurements that combine multiplicity and spherocity to identify nonequilibrium effects in small colliding systems.
