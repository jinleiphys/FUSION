# Circle compactification and 't Hooft anomaly

> Anomaly matching constrains low-energy physics of strongly-coupled field theories, but it is not useful at finite temperature due to contamination from high-energy states. The known exception is an 't Hooft anomaly involving one-form symmetries as in pure $SU(N)$ Yang-Mills theory at $θ=π$. Recent development about large-$N$ volume independence, however, gives us a circumstantial evidence that 't Hooft anomalies can also remain under circle compactifications in some theories without one-form symmetries. We develop a systematic procedure for deriving an 't Hooft anomaly of the circle-compactified theory starting from the anomaly of the original uncompactified theory without one-form symmetries, where the twisted boundary condition for the compactified direction plays a pivotal role. As an application, we consider $\mathbb{Z}_N$-twisted $\mathbb{C}P^{N-1}$ sigma model and massless $\mathbb{Z}_N$-QCD, and compute their anomalies explicitly.

## Key claim

The authors develop a systematic procedure to show that 't Hooft anomalies involving only ordinary (zero-form) symmetries can survive circle compactification, provided a twisted boundary condition is imposed; this contradicts the generic expectation that thermal fluctuations destroy such anomalies at finite temperature.

## Method

The authors start with a (D+1)-dimensional quantum field theory having a mixed 't Hooft anomaly between a flavor symmetry G = SU(N)/Z_N and another symmetry H. They compactify one direction to a circle S^1 and fix a nontrivial SU(N) holonomy Omega around the circle that satisfies S Omega S^{-1} = omega Omega for some shift symmetry generator S not in the center. This intertwines the Z_N zero-form symmetry acting on the Polyakov loop with a faithful shift symmetry of the compactified theory. By embedding the D-dimensional background gauge fields (a one-form B^{(1)} for the shift symmetry and a two-form B^{(2)} for the center) into the (D+1)-dimensional two-form gauge field B = B^{(2)} + B^{(1)} wedge L^{-1} dx^{D+1}, the original anomaly descends to a D-dimensional mixed anomaly among the shift symmetry, the remaining flavor symmetry, and H.

## Key numbers

* For the Z_N-twisted CP^{N-1} sigma model on R x S^1 at theta = pi, the anomaly relation is Z_{pi,Omega}[T * B^{(1)}] = Z_{pi,Omega}[B^{(1)}] exp(-i integral B^{(1)}), where B^{(1)} is a Z_N one-form gauge field for the shift symmetry and T denotes time-reversal.
* For the SU(N) Yang-Mills theory at theta = pi compactified on M^3 x S^1, the anomaly relation becomes Z_{theta=pi}[T * B] = Z_{theta=pi}[B] exp(i N/(2pi) integral B^{(2)} wedge B^{(1)}), where B^{(2)} is a Z_N two-form gauge field and B^{(1)} a Z_N one-form gauge field.
* For even N, the two-dimensional CP^{N-1} model at theta = pi has a genuine 't Hooft anomaly because the equation 2k = 1 mod N has no integer solution k; for odd N, only a global inconsistency exists.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Gaiotto et al. (2017) that 't Hooft anomalies involving one-form symmetries survive finite temperature in pure SU(N) Yang-Mills theory at theta = pi. The authors show that the same survival occurs for theories without one-form symmetries, such as the CP^{N-1} sigma model and massless QCD with N_c = N_f, provided a Z_N-twisted boundary condition is used. This connects to the adiabatic continuity program initiated by Unsal (2007) and collaborators, where semiclassical confinement on R^3 x S^1 is argued to be continuously connected to strong-coupling confinement; the present work provides an anomaly-matching consistency check for that program. The results enable rigorous constraints on the phase diagrams of these theories under compactification, for example forbidding a trivially gapped vacuum at theta = pi in the Z_N-twisted CP^{N-1} model.
