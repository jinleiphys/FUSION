# Pion Interferometry From A Relativistic Fluid With A First Order Phase Transition In CERN-SPS 158 GeV/A Pb+Pb Collisions

> We investigate pion source sizes through the Yano-Koonin-Podgoretski\uı (YKP) parametrization for the Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) effect in the CERN-SPS 158 GeV/A central collisions. We calculate two-particle correlation functions numerically based on a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics with a first order phase transition and analyze the pair momentum dependence of the HBT radii extracted from the YKP parametrization in detail. We find that even in the case of a first order phase transition, expansion and the surface dominant freeze-out make the source in the hydrodynamical model opaque significantly. Consequently, the interpretation of the temporal radius parameter as the time duration becomes unavailable for the hydrodynamical model.

## Key claim

Even in a hydrodynamical model with a first-order phase transition, the collective expansion and surface-dominant freeze-out make the source significantly opaque, so that the YKP temporal radius parameter \(R_0\) cannot be interpreted as the emission duration.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of central 158 GeV/A Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS, including a first-order phase transition between quark-gluon plasma and hadronic gas. They computed two-pion correlation functions assuming a completely chaotic source, then extracted the Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) HBT radii \(R_\bot\), \(R_\parallel\), and \(R_0\) as functions of transverse pair momentum \(K_T\). The extracted radii were compared both to the space-time extensions calculated directly from the hydrodynamic source function and to experimental data from NA49.

## Key numbers

- The extracted \(R_\bot\) and \(R_\parallel\) from the hydrodynamic model are consistent with the NA49 experimental results (open circles in Fig. 1) across the \(K_T\) range shown.
- The calculated \(R_0\) values are a few fm smaller than the experimental results, despite the presence of a first-order phase transition.
- The source function was analyzed at \(K_T = 450\) MeV and rapidity \(Y_{\pi\pi} = 4.15\) to demonstrate the crescent-shaped opaque source (Fig. 2, left panel) versus the azimuthally symmetric source without transverse flow (Fig. 2, right panel).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of HBT interferometry in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the YKP parametrization introduced by Yano, Koonin, and Podgoretskii, and the expectation by Pratt that a first-order phase transition would produce an enlarged time duration. The authors show that even with such a phase transition, the transverse flow and surface-dominant freeze-out produce an opaque source that invalidates the simple temporal interpretation of \(R_0\). The deviations from the NA49 data suggest that including resonance decays and other effects could improve the agreement.
