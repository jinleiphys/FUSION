# Virtual Compton Scattering and Generalized Polarizabilities of the Nucleon in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The spin-independent part of the virtual Compton scattering (VCS) amplitude from the nucleon is calculated within the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT). The calculation is performed to third order in external momenta according to chiral power counting. The relation of the tree-level amplitudes to what is expected from the low-energy theorem is discussed. We relate the one-loop results to the structure coefficients of a low-energy expansion for the model-dependent part of the VCS amplitude recently defined by Fearing and Scherer. Finally we discuss the connection of our results with the generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon defined by Guichon, Liu and Thomas.

## Key claim

The spin-independent part of the virtual Compton scattering (VCS) amplitude from the nucleon is calculated to third order in external momenta using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), but the results are strictly valid only when both the outgoing photon energy and the initial photon three-momentum are smaller than the pion mass; the multipole expansion of Guichon, Liu and Thomas (1995) may not reliably parametrize the VCS amplitude in this kinematical region because contributions from higher multipoles enter at the same level as the slopes of the electromagnetic polarizabilities.

## Method

The invariant VCS amplitude was decomposed into a structure-independent part (Born diagrams plus seagull graph) and a structure-dependent part generated by one-particle irreducible loop diagrams. The loop diagrams were evaluated using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to order \(O(p^3)\) in chiral power counting, expanding the loop integrals in the small kinematic quantities \(r\) (such as \(\omega'\) and \(|\vec q|\)) relative to the pion mass \(m_\pi\). The resulting structure-dependent amplitudes were then related to the nine structure coefficients of the low-energy expansion defined by Fearing and Scherer (1996) and to the generalized polarizabilities of Guichon, Liu and Thomas (1995).

## Key numbers

- Electric polarizability of the proton: \(\alpha_0 = 12.8 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\)
- Magnetic polarizability of the proton: \(\beta_0 = 1.3 \times 10^{-4} \text{ fm}^3\)
- Generalized electric polarizability as a function of three-momentum: \(\alpha(|\vec q|) = \alpha_0 \left(1 - \frac{7}{50} \frac{|\vec q|^2}{m_\pi^2} + O\left(\frac{|\vec q|^4}{m_\pi^4}\right)\right)\)
- Generalized magnetic polarizability as a function of three-momentum: \(\beta(|\vec q|) = \beta_0 \left(1 + \frac{1}{5} \frac{|\vec q|^2}{m_\pi^2} + O\left(\frac{|\vec q|^4}{m_\pi^4}\right)\right)\)
- Structure coefficient \(g_0 = \frac{1}{192} \frac{g_A^2}{F^2} \frac{1}{\pi m_\pi}\)
- Structure coefficient \(g_{2b} = -\frac{1}{960} \frac{g_A^2}{F^2} \frac{1}{\pi m_\pi^3}\)

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculation of real Compton scattering by Bernard et al. (1991, 1995) to the virtual Compton scattering case, providing the first HBChPT prediction for the generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon. The results differ significantly from earlier predictions using a non-relativistic constituent quark model by Guichon, Liu and Thomas (1995) and a refined quark model by Guichon et al. (1996), with a larger electric polarizability and a smaller magnetic polarizability, and with slopes of opposite sign for the magnetic polarizability. The calculation enables experimental tests in the kinematical region where both \(\omega'\) and \(|\vec q|\) are smaller than the pion mass, and provides a parametrization that accounts for higher multipole contributions that the multipole expansion of Guichon, Liu and Thomas (1995) neglects.
