# First principles electromagnetic responses in medium-mass nuclei

> We review the recent progress made in the computation of electromagnetic response functions in light and medium-mass nuclei using coupled-cluster theory. We show how a many-body formulation of the Lorentz integral transform method allows to calculate the photoabsorption cross sections of $^{16, 22}$O and $^{40}$Ca. Then, we discuss electromagnetic sum rules, with particular emphasis on the electric dipole polarizability, $α_D$. By including triples corrections in coupled-cluster theory, we revisit $^{48}$Ca, for which, beside the electric dipole polarizability, we had previously investigated the neutron and proton radii, as well as the size of the neutron-skin thickness [1]. We show that correlations among these observables still hold, albeit a better agreement with experiment is obtained for $α_D$ and the prediction of a small neutron-skin thickness is further corroborated.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that merging coupled-cluster theory with the Lorentz integral transform method (LIT-CC) enables first-principles calculations of electromagnetic response functions in medium-mass nuclei, but the results are obtained within truncated approximation schemes (singles and doubles, D/D) and using only two-nucleon forces for the response functions, with three-nucleon forces included only in the later electric dipole polarizability calculations for 48Ca.

## Method

The authors combine coupled-cluster theory with the Lorentz integral transform method, where the many-body Schrodinger-like equation (H - z)|Psi_tilde> = Theta|Psi_0> is solved using a similarity-transformed Hamiltonian and electromagnetic operator within the coupled-cluster framework. The ground state is obtained from the exponential ansatz |Psi_0> = exp(T)|Phi_0> with T truncated at singles and doubles (D), and the excited-state operator R(z) is similarly truncated. The Lorentz integral transform L(omega_0,Gamma) is computed directly, then the response function R(omega) is obtained through numerical inversion using a least-squares fit with a Gamow-factor ansatz.

## Key numbers

- For 4He benchmark: the theoretical threshold with N3LO potential is omega_th = 17.54 MeV, shifted to experimental threshold of 19.82 MeV
- For 16O: experimental threshold omega_th = 12.1 MeV
- For 40Ca: the giant dipole resonance peak is located at excitation energy around 20 MeV
- Model-space truncation: up to N_max = 18 for NN-only calculations, N_max = 14 for NN+3N calculations
- 3N matrix elements truncated with E_3max <= 16
- Coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles (D) approximation captures about 90% of full correlation energy; adding triples captures about 99%
- D approximation algorithm scales as n_o^2 n_u^4, where n_o and n_u are numbers of occupied and unoccupied orbitals

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz integral transform method, previously applied to few-body systems using hyperspherical harmonics (e.g., for 4He, 6He, 7Li by Bacca et al. 2002-2004), to medium-mass nuclei by merging it with coupled-cluster theory. The LIT-CC method enables ab initio calculations of dipole response functions for nuclei up to 40Ca, showing the emergence of collective modes like giant dipole resonances from first principles. The authors also revisit correlations among electric dipole polarizability, neutron radius, proton radius, and neutron-skin thickness in 48Ca previously studied by Hagen et al. (2016), finding that including triples corrections improves agreement with experiment for alpha_D and further supports a small neutron-skin prediction.
