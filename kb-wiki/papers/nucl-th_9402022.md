# Capture of Solar and Higher-Energy Neutrinos by Iodine 127

> We discuss and improve a recent treatment of the absorption of solar neutrinos by ${}^{127}$I, in connection with a proposed solar neutrino detector. With standard-solar-model fluxes and an in-medium value of -1.0 for the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$, we obtain a ${}^8$B-neutrino cross section of 3.3$\times 10^{-42}$, about 50\% larger than in our previous work, and a ${}^7$Be cross section that is less certain but nevertheless also larger than before. We then apply the improved techniques to higher incoming energies that obtain at the LAMPF beam dump, where an experiment is underway to finalize a calibration of the ${}^{127}$I with electron neutrinos from muon decay. We find that forbidden operators, which play no role in solar-neutrino absorption, contribute nonnegligibly to the LAMPF cross section, and that the preliminary LAMPF mean value is significantly larger than our prediction.

## Key claim

The authors obtain a 50% larger cross section for solar 8B neutrino capture by 127I than in their previous work (Engel, Pittel, and Vogel, 1991), but the 7Be cross section remains less certain; they also find that forbidden operators contribute nonnegligibly to the LAMPF beam-dump cross section, and that the preliminary LAMPF mean value is significantly larger than their prediction, making a clean calibration of the iodine detector more difficult than originally hoped.

## Method

The authors used the Quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (QTDA) with a valence space of the 2s-1d-0g oscillator shell plus two 0h orbitals, employing a two-body interaction from Hosaka, Kubo, and Toki (1985) with the monopole component removed and single-particle energies taken from spectra of N=81 and Z=51 nuclei. They also incorporated three-quasiparticle configurations and spreading widths, and compared results with the number-conserving Generalized Seniority (GS) approximation. For the higher-energy LAMPF neutrinos, they evaluated matrix elements of multipole operators including forbidden corrections and treated Coulomb effects with a Fermi function for 1+ transitions and a modified factor for 2- transitions.

## Key numbers

- 8B-neutrino cross section: 3.3 × 10^{-42} cm^2 (with g_A = -1.0 and standard-solar-model fluxes), about 50% larger than the previous value of 2.2 × 10^{-42} cm^2 from Engel, Pittel, and Vogel (1991)
- 7Be cross section: 2.0 × 10^{-45} cm^2 (9.4 SNU) in the prior work; the new GS calculation gives a strength to the lowest 3/2+ state of 0.079, more than twice the previous value of 0.035
- Total GT strength below the neutron-emission threshold (7.23 MeV): 4.38 in the GS calculation and 4.13 in the QTDA
- Final 8B cross section with all improvements: 3.27 × 10^{-42} cm^2
- LAMPF total cross section prediction: 2.098 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (with g_A = -1.0) and 3.096 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (with g_A = -1.26), compared to the preliminary experimental value of 6 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- 1+ multipole contribution to LAMPF cross section: 1.017 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (g_A = -1.0); 2- contribution: 0.693 × 10^{-40} cm^2 (g_A = -1.0)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Engel, Pittel, and Vogel, 1991) of neutrino capture on 127I, which itself improved on the original proposal by Haxton (1988) for an iodine-based solar neutrino detector. The paper addresses the need for a direct calibration of the detector using the LAMPF beam-dump experiment (Cleveland, Daily, Distel, Lande, et al., 1993), but finds that forbidden contributions complicate the extraction of the Gamow-Teller strength needed for solar neutrino cross sections. The results support continued development of a calibrated iodine detector but indicate that a complete calibration may be more difficult than originally hoped, requiring better understanding of forbidden operators and their removal.
