# Crystals of gauged solitons, force free plasma and resurgence

> We show that the (3+1)-dimensional gauged non-linear sigma model minimally coupled to a U(1) gauge field possesses analytic solutions representing gauged solitons at finite Baryon density whose electromagnetic field is a Force Free Plasma. These gauged solitons manifest a crystalline structure and generate in a very natural way persistent currents able to support Force Free Plasma electromagnetic fields. The trajectories of charged test particles moving within these configurations can be characterized. Quite surprisingly, despite the non-integrable nature of the theory, some of the perturbations of these gauged solitons allow to identify a proper resurgent parameter. In particular, the perturbations of the solitons profile are related to the Lamé operator. On the other hand, the electromagnetic perturbations on the configurations satisfy a two-dimensional effective Schrodinger equation, where the soliton background interacts with the electromagnetic perturbations through an effective two-dimensional periodic potential. We studied numerically the band energy spectrum for different values of the free parameters of the theory and we found that bands-gaps are modulated by the potential strength. Finally we compare our crystal solutions with those of the (1+1)- dimesional Gross-Neveu model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that analytic solutions of the (3+1)-dimensional gauged non-linear sigma model minimally coupled to a U(1) gauge field represent gauged solitons at finite Baryon density whose electromagnetic field is a Force Free Plasma, but this result is conditional on a specific ansatz for the soliton fields and gauge potential that reduces the coupled field equations to a single ordinary differential equation for the profile function.

## Method

The authors adopt a flat metric representing a box with adimensional coordinates and use a hedgehog ansatz for the SU(2) field with profile α(r), angular function Θ = qθ, and phase Φ = p(t/L − φ), together with a gauge potential Aμ = (u, 0, 0, −L u) where u = u(r, θ). This reduces the three coupled field equations of the gauged non-linear sigma model to a single ordinary differential equation for α(r) that is solved in terms of inverse elliptic functions, while the Maxwell equations reduce to a two-dimensional linear Schrödinger-like equation for Ψ = (2L/p)u − 1 with an effective periodic potential V = 4K sin²(α) sin²(qθ). The authors then analyze perturbations of the soliton profile and electromagnetic field, study the band energy spectrum numerically, and compare the crystal solutions with those of the (1+1)-dimensional Gross-Neveu model.

## Key numbers

- The topological charge B equals −np when q is an odd integer (q = 2v + 1) and 0 when q is even, where n is determined by the boundary condition α(2π) − α(0) = nπ and p is a non-zero natural number.
- The box volume is V = 4π³ L_r L_θ L, where L_r, L_θ, and L are the side lengths in the adimensional coordinates r, θ, and φ respectively.
- The effective resurgent parameter g in the m = 0 case is given by 1/g² = L_r² q² / L_θ².
- The elliptic parameter k is defined as k = √(2 L_r² q² / (L_r² q² − 4 E_0 L_θ²)), where E_0 is an integration constant satisfying E_0 > (q²/4)(L_r²/L_θ²) + (4m²/K)L_r².

## Context

This work extends the analytic construction of exact non-linear sigma model solutions with non-trivial topological charge at finite density developed in Canfora et al. (2018) and subsequent papers on crystalline soliton configurations, which describe features of the nuclear pasta phase such as the nuclear spaghetti phase. The paper establishes a novel connection between Force Free Plasma (relevant to pulsar magnetospheres and the Blandford-Znajek process) and hadronic matter described by the low-energy limit of QCD, and it identifies a resurgent parameter in the perturbation analysis of a non-integrable system, which is unusual because resurgence applications have mostly been limited to integrable models. The comparison with the Gross-Neveu model crystals from Thies (2003-2006) and Dunne (2008) suggests a relation between (3+1)-dimensional soliton crystals and (1+1)-dimensional models.
