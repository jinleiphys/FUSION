# Phase transitions and resilience of the MDCDW phase at finite temperature and density

> We study the phase transitions of the magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW). This spatially inhomogeneous phase emerges in cold, dense QCD in the presence of a strong magnetic field. Starting from the generalized GL expansion of the free energy, we derive several analytical formulas that enable fast numerical computation of the expansion coefficients to arbitrary order, allowing high levels of precision in the determination of the physical dynamical parameters, as well as in the transition curves in the temperature vs. chemical potential plane at different magnetic fields. At magnetic fields and temperatures compatible with neutron star (NS) conditions, the MDCDW remains favored over the symmetric ground state at all densities. The phase's "resilience" manifests in (1) a region of small but nonzero remnant mass and significant modulation at intermediate densities, originating in part from the nontrivial topology of the lowest Landau level, and (2) a region of increasing condensate parameters at high densities. Our analysis suggests the MDCDW condensate remains energetically favored at densities and temperatures much higher than previously considered, opening the possibility for this phase to be a viable candidate for the matter structure of even young neutron stars produced by NS mergers.

## Key claim

The MDCDW condensate remains energetically favored over the symmetric ground state at densities and temperatures much higher than previously considered, but this resilience is conditional on the presence of a strong magnetic field (of order 10^17-10^18 G) and is limited to low temperatures and intermediate to large baryon densities ranging from 2.5 to about ten times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors used a generalized Ginzburg-Landau (GL) expansion of the thermodynamic potential for a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in an external magnetic field, deriving analytical formulas via the Euler-Maclaurin formula to compute expansion coefficients to arbitrary order. They then minimized the GL free energy to determine the condensate parameters (magnitude m and modulation b) and compared these results against exact numerical minimization of the full free energy to validate the expansion, generating phase diagrams in the temperature versus chemical potential plane at different magnetic field strengths.

## Key numbers

- At zero magnetic field and zero temperature, the inhomogeneous phase exists between chemical potentials of approximately 311 MeV and 341 MeV (Region II), with a first-order phase transition at 311 MeV and a second-order transition at 341 MeV
- At zero magnetic field, a return of the condensate occurs for chemical potentials above 491 MeV (Region IV)
- The proper-time regularization cutoff used was Lambda = 636.790 MeV with coupling constant G*Lambda^2 = 6, corresponding to a vacuum mass m_vac = 300 MeV
- Magnetic field strengths considered for neutron star conditions are in the range 10^17-10^18 G, with magnetar surface fields observed at approximately 10^15 G and core fields possibly reaching 10^17-10^18 G
- The baryon density range where the MDCDW phase is favored extends from 2.5 to about ten times nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the magnetic dual chiral density wave phase by Frolov et al. and Tatsumi et al., which established the MDCDW as a spatially inhomogeneous chiral condensate phase in dense QCD with a magnetic field. The paper builds on the finding by de la Incera et al. that the MDCDW phase is free of the Landau-Peierls instability that affects other inhomogeneous phases like the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phase studied by Nakano and the periodic real kink crystal studied by Hidaka. The results enable future calculations of the equation of state for magnetized neutron star matter and provide a framework for analyzing matter in neutron star interiors and heavy-ion collision experiments such as those at FAIR and NICA.
