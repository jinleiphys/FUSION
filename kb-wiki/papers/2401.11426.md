# Abnormal Bifurcation of the Double Binding Energy Differences and Proton-Neutron Pairing: Nuclei Close to $N=Z$ Line from Ni to Rb

> The recently observed abnormal bifurcation of the double binding energy differences $δV_{pn}$ between the odd-odd and even-even nuclei along the $N=Z$ line from Ni to Rb has challenged the nuclear theories. To solve this problem, a shell-model-like approach based on the relativistic density functional theory is established, by treating simultaneously the neutron-neutron, proton-neutron, and proton-proton pairing correlations both microscopically and self-consistently. Without any \textit{ad hoc} parameters, the calculated results well reproduce the observations, and the mechanism for this abnormal bifurcation is found to be due to the enhanced proton-neutron pairing correlations in the odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei, compared with the even-even ones. The present results provide an excellent interpretation for the abnormal $δV_{pn}$ bifurcation, and provide a clear signal for the existence of the proton-neutron pairing correlations for nuclei close to the $N=Z$ line.

## Key claim

The abnormal bifurcation of the double binding energy differences δVpn between odd-odd and even-even N=Z nuclei from Ni to Rb is caused by enhanced proton-neutron pairing correlations in the odd-odd nuclei, as demonstrated by a shell-model-like approach based on relativistic density functional theory that treats all pairing channels simultaneously without ad hoc parameters.

## Method

A shell-model-like approach (SLAP) based on relativistic density functional theory (RDFT) was developed, using the PC-PK1 functional and solving the Dirac equation in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with ten major shells. The many-body Hamiltonian includes one-body terms from the Dirac equation and a pairing Hamiltonian with neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and proton-neutron pair creation operators, with the many-particle configuration space truncated by an energy cutoff of 16 MeV and the pairing strength G=0.8 MeV determined by fitting odd-even mass differences of N=Z+2 nuclei. The wave functions are obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in the truncated configuration space, and the full framework is solved iteratively to achieve self-consistency between the densities and the scalar and vector fields.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength G = 0.8 MeV, determined from odd-even mass differences of N=Z+2 nuclei from Ni to Rb
- MPC space truncated by energy cutoff E_cut = 16 MeV
- Root-mean-square deviation for N=Z nuclei binding energies: 3.388 MeV without pairing, 1.460 MeV with nn and pp pairing, 0.832 MeV with nn, pp, and pn pairing
- Root-mean-square deviation for N≠Z nuclei binding energies: 3.380 MeV without pairing, 0.832 MeV with nn and pp pairing, 0.671 MeV with nn, pp, and pn pairing
- Nonvanishing pn pairing energy starts at about 3.5 MeV for odd-odd nucleus 66As and about 6.9 MeV for even-even nucleus 64Ge
- Changing pairing strength G by 10% changes pn pairing energy by around 1 MeV for 66As and around 0.4 MeV for 64Ge

## Context

This work resolves a puzzle from WangM (2023) who reported an abnormal bifurcation of δVpn along the N=Z line from Ni to Rb that could not be explained by macroscopic-microscopic models (Duflo 1995, Moller 1995), shell model calculations (Honma 2005), or density functional theories (Goriely 2013). The valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations by WangM (2023) suggested three-nucleon forces were important but dramatically overestimated the bifurcation amplitude. The present RDFT-SLAP approach provides the first microscopic and self-consistent treatment of all three pairing channels simultaneously, enabling future studies of proton-neutron pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei and their role in nuclear structure phenomena such as Wigner SU(4) symmetry and pseudo-SU(4) symmetry.
