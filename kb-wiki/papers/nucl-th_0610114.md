# Photoproduction of eta and etaprime mesons on the nucleon

> The isobar models eta-MAID and eta'-MAID have been used to analyze new data on quasi-free eta photoproduction on the deuteron from Bonn and recent eta' data on the proton from Jlab. In eta photoproduction on the neutron a bump around W=1700 MeV was observed which could possibly arise from a narrow P11 state that is discussed as a non-strange member of the Theta^+ anti-decuplett. In eta' photoproduction on the proton resonance contributions are found that can be attributed to missing resonances in the energy region around W=1900 MeV.

## Key claim

The bump observed in quasi-free eta photoproduction on the neutron around W=1700 MeV could arise either from a standard D15(1675) resonance with an unusually large eta-N branching ratio that violates SU(3) symmetry bounds, or alternatively from a narrow P11 pentaquark state, but the data alone cannot distinguish between these two interpretations.

## Method

The isobar models eta-MAID and eta'-MAID were used to analyze photoproduction data, constructed with a nonresonant background of nucleon Born terms and t-channel vector meson exchange (either standard single-pole or reggeized), plus s-channel nucleon resonance excitations parameterized with standard Breit-Wigner functions. For eta photoproduction, two model variants were fitted to cross section data from TAPS, GRAAL, and CLAS: one with standard vector meson poles and one with reggeized vector mesons. For eta-prime photoproduction, a fit to new CLAS data was performed using four nucleon resonances as free parameters, determining effective p(gamma,eta')p couplings for helicity 1/2 and 3/2.

## Key numbers

- In eta photoproduction on the neutron, a bump around W=1700 MeV was observed
- The D15(1675) resonance in the standard pole model (model I) has an eta-N branching ratio of 17%, which violates the SU(3) upper limit of 2.5%
- In the reggeized model (model II), the D15(1675) branching ratio drops to 0.7%
- A narrow P11 pentaquark state was considered with mass 1675 MeV, total width 10 MeV, eta-N branching ratio 40%, and photon couplings A1/2(p)=10 and A1/2(n)=30 (in units of 10^{-3}/sqrt(GeV))
- For eta-prime photoproduction on the proton, a fit to CLAS data found resonances: S11 at 1904 MeV (width 527 MeV, chi1/2=15.7), P13 at 1926 MeV (width 146 MeV, chi1/2=-1.5, chi3/2=1.0), P11 at 2083 MeV (width 51 MeV, chi1/2=2.5), and D13 at 2100 MeV (width 91 MeV, chi1/2=6.5, chi3/2=-6.5), all in units of 10^{-3}/sqrt(GeV)

## Context

This work extends the isobar model analyses of Chiang (2002) and Chiang (2003) by incorporating new quasi-free eta photoproduction data from the CB-TAPS-ELSA collaboration (Jaegle, 2006) and new eta-prime data from JLab/CLAS (Dugger, 2006). The eta photoproduction results connect to the pentaquark discussion following Diakonov, Petrov and Polyakov (1997), who predicted a non-strange member of the Theta+ antidecuplet around 1710 MeV, and to the partial wave analysis of Arndt (2004) who reported possible candidates at 1680 and 1730 MeV with widths below 30 MeV. The eta-prime results enable comparisons with quark model predictions for missing resonances from Capstick (1986), Giannini (2002), and Metsch (2001), as well as with the meson-exchange models of Sibirtsev (2004) and Nakayama and Haberzettl (2004).
