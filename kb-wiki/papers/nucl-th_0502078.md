# Properties of mesons and nucleons in chiral topological models of QCD

> The problem under consideration in this thesis has, actually, two aspects. The first one concerns the case when a nucleon is in free space that is in vacuum, while the second one studies a nucleon embedded into a nuclear environment. Both of these two aspects have been considered in the framework of chiral topological models of QCD. The whole content of the thesis may be divided into two main parts. In the first part the original Skyrme model with finite pion mass has been extended by inclusion of the light scalar - isoscalar sigma - meson. The Lagrangian has been further extended by explicit inclusion of sigma, rho and omega - mesons as well. In order to get a more complete picture of NN potential the appropriate meson - nucleon vertex form - factors are obtained. The second part of the thesis considers a nucleon immersed into a nuclear medium. For this purpose a medium modified Skyrme Lagrangian has been proposed. The Lagrangian describes well such well known medium effects as decreasing of nucleon mass and increasing of its size (swelling). We studied also a system with finite temperature also. The temperature effects are taken into account by using the method of termofield dynamics (TFD). The corresponding critical temperatures for each meson - nucleon system are calculated .

## Key claim

The thesis extends the Skyrme model to describe both free nucleons and nucleons in nuclear matter, claiming that the medium-modified Skyrme Lagrangian reproduces known medium effects such as decreasing nucleon mass and increasing nucleon size (swelling), with the caveat that the temperature dependence of meson-nucleon vertex form factors is not simple and remains nearly constant until a critical temperature before decreasing dramatically.

## Method

The first part extends the original Skyrme model (with finite pion mass) by including the light scalar-isoscalar sigma meson, then further extends the Lagrangian by explicit inclusion of sigma, rho, and omega mesons, obtaining meson-nucleon vertex form factors for a more complete nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential. The second part proposes a medium-modified Skyrme Lagrangian for a nucleon immersed in nuclear matter and studies finite-temperature effects using the method of thermofield dynamics (TFD), calculating critical temperatures for each meson-nucleon system.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant fitted to f_pi = 64.5 MeV (experimental value 93 MeV) and Skyrme parameter e = 5.45, adjusted to fit nucleon and Delta masses
- Predicted isoscalar charge radius sqrt(<r^2>_{I=0}) = 0.59 fm (experiment: 0.72 fm)
- Predicted isoscalar magnetic radius sqrt(<r^2>_{M,I=0}) = 0.92 fm (experiment: 0.81 fm)
- Predicted proton magnetic moment mu_p = 1.87 (experiment: 2.79)
- Predicted neutron magnetic moment mu_n = -1.31 (experiment: -1.91)
- Predicted axial coupling constant g_A = 0.61 (experiment: 1.23)
- Predicted pion-nucleon coupling constant g_{pi NN} = 8.9 (experiment: 13.5)

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) for baryon properties and the product ansatz for NN interactions, addressing the known shortcomings that the original Skyrme model gives a strongly repulsive spin-isospin-independent central force at medium distances and fails to account for nuclear binding. The inclusion of the scalar dilaton field follows the approach of Shehter to restore the anomalous scaling behavior of QCD at the effective Lagrangian level. The results enable studies of heavy-ion collisions, cosmology, and astrophysics by providing a framework for hadron properties in nuclear matter at finite temperature.
