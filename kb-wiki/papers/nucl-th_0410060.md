# The Short Range Mechanism of N-N interaction in the Extended Chiral SU(3) Quark Model

> We give the comparisons between the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model. The results show that the phase shifts of NN scattering are very similar. However, the short range mechanisms of nucleon-nucleon interaction are totally different. In the chiral SU(3) quark model, the short range interaction is dominantly from OGE, and in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, it is dominantly from vector meson exchanges.

## Key claim

The phase shifts of NN scattering are very similar between the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, but the short-range mechanisms of nucleon-nucleon interaction are totally different: in the chiral SU(3) quark model the short-range interaction is dominantly from one-gluon exchange (OGE), while in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model it is dominantly from vector meson exchanges.

## Method

The authors compared two quark models by calculating NN scattering phase shifts using the Resonating Group Method (RGM). In the chiral SU(3) quark model, the short-range interaction comes from OGE, while in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, vector meson exchange potentials (derived from a quark-vector meson coupling Lagrangian) are added and the OGE coupling constant is greatly reduced. The model parameters were fixed by fitting the mass difference between N and Delta, the stability condition of the nucleon, and experimental meson masses, with two parameter sets (set I and set II) explored for the extended model.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic-oscillator width parameter b_u = 0.50 fm in the chiral SU(3) quark model and b_u = 0.45 fm in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model.
- The OGE coupling constant g_u = 0.886 (alpha_s = 0.785) in the chiral SU(3) quark model, reduced to g_u = 0.293 (alpha_s = 0.086) for set I and g_u = 0.399 (alpha_s = 0.159) for set II in the extended model.
- The vector meson coupling constant g_chv = 2.351 for set I and g_chv = 1.972 for set II, with f_chv/g_chv = 0 for set I and f_chv/g_chv = 2/3 for set II.
- The sigma meson mass m_sigma = 595 MeV in the chiral SU(3) quark model, and m_sigma = 535 MeV (set I) or 547 MeV (set II) in the extended model.
- The deuteron binding energy B_deu = 2.13 MeV in the chiral SU(3) quark model, and B_deu = 2.19 MeV (set I) or 2.14 MeV (set II) in the extended model.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model of Zhang et al. by incorporating vector meson exchanges, following the approach of Riska and Glozman who applied quark-chiral field coupling to baryon structure and found that vector meson coupling could replace OGE. The results challenge the traditional Isgur model where OGE governs baryon structure, and show that on the quark level, vector meson exchanges can substitute the OGE mechanism for short-range NN repulsion, similar to the traditional one-boson-exchange (OBE) model on the baryon level. This enables further study of whether OGE, vector meson exchange, or both are the correct mechanism for short-range quark-quark interactions.
