# Cooling of neutron stars and hybrid stars with a stiff hadronic EoS

> Within the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario of neutron stars all reliably known temperature - age data, including those of the central compact objects in the supernova remnants of Cassiopeia A and XMMU-J1732, can be comfortably explained by a set of cooling curves obtained by variation of the star mass within the range of typical observed masses. The recent measurements of the high masses of the pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348-0432 on the one hand, and of the low masses for PSR J0737-3039B and the companion of PSR J1756-2251 on the other, provide independent proof for the existence of neutron stars with masses in a broad range from $\sim 1.2$ to 2 $M_\odot$. The values $M>2 M_{\odot}$ call for sufficiently stiff equations of state for neutron star matter. We investigate the response of the set of neutron star cooling curves to a stiffening of the nuclear equation of state so that maximum masses of about $2.4 M_\odot$ would be accessible and to a deconfinement phase transition from such stiff nuclear matter in the outer core to color superconducting quark matter in the inner core. Without readjustment of cooling inputs the mass range required to cover all cooling data for the stiff DD2 equation of state should include masses of $2.426 M_\odot$ for describing the fast cooling of CasA while the existence of a quark matter core accelerates the cooling so that CasA cooling data are described with a hybrid star of mass $1.674 M_\odot$.

## Key claim

Within the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario, the full set of known neutron star temperature-age data (including Cassiopeia A and XMMU-J1732) can be described using a stiff hadronic equation of state (DD2) without readjusting any cooling inputs, provided the mass range is shifted to higher values; alternatively, a hybrid star with a quark matter core can describe the same data with a much narrower mass range.

## Method

The authors computed cooling curves for neutron star sequences using the stiff DD2 hadronic equation of state (EoS) and, separately, a hybrid EoS incorporating a deconfinement phase transition to color superconducting quark matter (NJL model in the 2SC phase). All cooling inputs (neutrino emissivities, specific heat, crust properties, pairing gaps, and the density-dependent effective pion gap) were kept identical to those used in prior work with softer EoS (Blaschke 2011, Blaschke 2013). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation was integrated to generate mass-radius sequences, and the thermal evolution was computed with a simplified heat conductivity treatment using a suppression pre-factor ζκ.

## Key numbers

- The stiff DD2 hadronic EoS yields a maximum neutron star mass of about 2.4 M⊙.
- For the purely hadronic DD2 scenario, the Cassiopeia A cooling data are described by a neutron star of mass 2.426 M⊙, requiring a thermal conductivity suppression pre-factor ζκ = 0.021.
- For the hybrid star scenario (DD2 hadronic EoS with quark matter core), the Cassiopeia A cooling data are described by a star of mass 1.674 M⊙, with the same pre-factor ζκ = 0.021.
- The hot source XMMU-J1732 is explained by a neutron star of mass 1.29 M⊙ in the purely hadronic DD2 scenario.
- The black dot on the HDD reference curve at mass 1.498 M⊙ denotes the configuration that would best fit CasA cooling data with the softer HDD EoS and pairing gaps from model I.

## Context

This work extends the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario previously developed by Blaschke (2004, 2011, 2013) by replacing the softer HHJ and HDD equations of state with the stiffer DD2 EoS (Typel 2009), motivated by recent radius determinations suggesting larger neutron star radii (Bogdanov 2012) and by the existence of high-mass pulsars up to 2 M⊙ (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013). The stiff EoS enables exploration of deconfinement phase transitions to quark matter (Alford 2006, Klahn 2013) while still satisfying the 2 M⊙ mass constraint. The results enable future work to test whether a density-dependent decrease of the effective pion gap could reduce the required mass for the Cassiopeia A neutron star.
