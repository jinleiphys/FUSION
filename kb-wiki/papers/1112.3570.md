# A consistent model for πN transition distribution amplitudes and backward pion electroproduction

> The extension of the concept of generalized parton distributions leads to the introduction of baryon to meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), non-diagonal matrix elements of the nonlocal three quark operator between a nucleon and a meson state. We present a general framework for modelling nucleon to pion ($πN$) TDAs. Our main tool is the spectral representation for πN TDAs in terms of quadruple distributions. We propose a factorized Ansatz for quadruple distributions with input from the soft-pion theorem for πN TDAs. The spectral representation is complemented with a D-term like contribution from the nucleon exchange in the cross channel. We then study backward pion electroproduction in the QCD collinear factorization approach in which the non-perturbative part of the amplitude involves πN TDAs. Within our two component model for πN TDAs we update previous leading-twist estimates of the unpolarized cross section. Finally, we compute the transverse target single spin asymmetry as a function of skewness. We find it to be sizable in the valence region and sensitive to the phenomenological input of our πN TDA model.

## Key claim

The authors present a consistent model for nucleon-to-pion transition distribution amplitudes (πN TDAs) that satisfies both the polynomiality property and the soft-pion theorem, enabling updated leading-twist estimates of backward pion electroproduction cross sections and the first calculation of transverse target single spin asymmetry in this regime.

## Method

The authors construct a two-component model for πN TDAs: (1) a spectral representation in terms of quadruple distributions with a factorized Ansatz constrained by the soft-pion theorem at ξ=1, using the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky nucleon distribution amplitude as input; (2) a D-term-like contribution from u-channel nucleon exchange. They then compute the γ*N→πN amplitude in the QCD collinear factorization approach at leading order in αs and 1/Q, and evaluate the unpolarized cross section and transverse target single spin asymmetry for backward π+ and π0 electroproduction off protons.

## Key numbers

- The pion weak decay constant fπ = 93 MeV is used in the amplitude calculation.
- The dimensional nucleon wave function at the origin fN ~ 5.2·10⁻³ GeV² is used in the amplitude calculation.
- The electromagnetic fine structure constant αem ≃ 1/137 is used.
- The nucleon pole contribution to isospin-1/2 πN TDAs contains the factor (gπNN) M fπ / (Δ² - M²), where M is the nucleon mass and gπNN is the pion-nucleon phenomenological coupling.
- The leading-order amplitude scales as 1/Q⁴, with the prefactor C = -i (4παs)² √(4παem) fN² / (54 fπ).

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization approach for backward pion electroproduction, previously developed by Lansberg (2007), Pire (2010), and Pire (2011), by constructing a model for πN TDAs that satisfies both the polynomiality condition and the soft-pion theorem. The soft-pion theorem for πN TDAs was established by Pire (2011) and relates the ξ=1 limit to nucleon distribution amplitudes, analogous to how the soft-pion theorem for 2π GDAs constrains the isovector pion GPD (Polyakov, 1998). The model enables predictions for transverse target single spin asymmetry in backward kinematics, which is found to be sizable in the valence region and sensitive to the phenomenological input, opening a window for spatial imaging of the meson cloud of the nucleon as suggested by Strikman (2009).
