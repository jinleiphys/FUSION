# Three-body couplings in RMF and its effects on hyperonic star equation of state

> We develop a new relativistic mean field (RMF) model including explicit three-body couplings and apply it to hyperonic systems and neutron star matter. The recent observation of the massive mass neutron star has given us a puzzle; when strange hadrons including hyperons are taken into account, the equation of state (EOS) becomes too soft to support the observed two-solar-mass neutron star. Three-baryon repulsion is a promising ingredient to answer this puzzle. We demonstrate that it is possible to consistently explain the massive neutron star and hypernuclear data when we include three-body couplings and modify the hyperon vector couplings from the flavor SU(3) values.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that including explicit three-body couplings (at next-to-leading order in the Furnstahl-Serot-Tang expansion) in a relativistic mean field model, together with modifying the hyperon-vector meson coupling ratio from the flavor SU(3) value, can produce a neutron star equation of state stiff enough to support a two-solar-mass neutron star while still reproducing hypernuclear data.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean field Lagrangian with standard two-body couplings (n=2), meson self-energies including an omega^4 potential, and explicit three-body coupling terms (n=3) of baryon-meson-meson and three-meson types. They determine two parameter sets (TB-a and TB-b) by fitting to the density dependence of the vector potential from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations and to nuclear saturation properties, then adjust hyperon-meson couplings to reproduce Lambda separation energies and double-Lambda bond energies, exploring both the flavor SU(3) value and a modified ratio R = g_omegaY/g_omegaN = 0.8.

## Key numbers

- The observed massive neutron star mass is M = 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses, discovered using the Shapiro delay
- Without hyperons, nucleonic equations of state predict maximum neutron star masses of (1.5-2.7) solar masses; with hyperons this reduces to (1.3-1.6) solar masses
- For the TB-a parameter set: g_sigmaN = m_N/f_pi, g_omegaN = 12.116, g_sigmasigmaN = 1.50, g_omegaomegaN = -1.50, g_sigmaomegaN = 3.05, c_sigmaomegaomega = -3.0, c_omega = 4.1015
- For the TB-b parameter set: g_omegaN = 12.284, g_sigmasigmaN = 0.70, g_omegaomegaN = -0.15, g_sigmaomegaN = 3.45, c_sigmaomegaomega = -3.0, c_omega = -3.9186
- With the SU(3) Lambda-omega coupling, Lambda-scalar meson couplings are g_sigmaLambda = 3.46 (TB-a) and 3.45 (TB-b); with R=0.8, g_sigmaLambda = 4.71 (TB-a) and 4.73 (TB-b)
- The calculated maximum mass exceeds 1.97 solar masses only with TB-b and R=0.8

## Context

This work addresses the "hyperon puzzle" raised by the discovery of a two-solar-mass neutron star by Demorest (2010), where standard relativistic mean field models with hyperons produce equations of state too soft to support such masses. The authors extend their previous SCL3 RMF model (Tsubakihara, 2010) by adding explicit three-body couplings, contrasting with approaches that rely solely on omega^4 terms or density-dependent couplings. The results differ from Bednarek (2011), who found support for 2-solar-mass neutron stars using different higher-order terms with SU(6) hyperon couplings, possibly due to the absence of omega-rho coupling terms (n=4 in the FST expansion) in the present work. The findings suggest that both three-body repulsion and modified hyperon-vector couplings may be necessary ingredients for a consistent description of massive neutron stars with hyperons.
