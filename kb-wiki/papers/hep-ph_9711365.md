# Chiral nucleon dynamics

> These lectures give an introduction to baryon chiral perturbation theory. I show in detail how to construct the chiral effective pion-nucleon Lagrangian in the one loop approximation. Particular emphasis is put on the physics related to electromagnetic probes, as manifest in pion photo- and electroproduction, Compton scattering off nucleons and the electroweak form factors of the nucleon. Other topics discussed in some detail are the meaning of low-energy theorems, pion-nucleon scattering, the reaction $πN \to ππN$ and isospin violation in the pion-nucleon system. The chiral predictions are confronted with the existing data. Some remaining problems and new developments are outlined.

## Key claim

Baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) provides a systematic low-energy expansion around the chiral limit for nucleon structure and interactions, but the straightforward generalization from the meson sector fails because the baryon mass does not vanish in the chiral limit, requiring a velocity-dependent field redefinition to restore a consistent power counting.

## Method

The author constructs the chiral effective pion-nucleon Lagrangian in the one-loop approximation using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT), where the nucleon four-momentum is decomposed as \(p_\mu = m v_\mu + \ell_\mu\) with \(v \cdot \ell \ll m\) and the "small" component of the Dirac field is eliminated via equations of motion or path-integral methods. The chiral power counting is derived for single-baryon processes, showing that tree diagrams start at order \(p\) and one-loop graphs at order \(p^3\), with the chiral dimension given by \(D = 2L + 1 + \sum_d (d-2) N_d^M + \sum_d (d-1) N_d^{MB}\). A sample calculation of the nucleon mass shift from the pion cloud is performed using dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV
- Axial-vector coupling constant: \(g_A = 1.26\)
- Nucleon mass shift from the pion loop at one loop: \(\delta m = -\frac{3g_A^2}{32\pi} \frac{M_\pi^3}{F_\pi^2}\)
- Low-energy constants \(c_i\) (in GeV\(^{-1}\)) from pion-nucleon data: \(c_1 = -0.93 \pm 0.10\), \(c_2 = 3.34 \pm 0.20\), \(c_3 = -5.29 \pm 0.25\), \(c_4 = 3.63 \pm 0.10\), \(c_5 = -0.09 \pm 0.01\)
- Dimensionless LECs for anomalous magnetic moments: \(c_6 = 5.83\) (isovector), \(c_7 = -2.98\) (isoscalar)
- Kaon loop correction ratio: \((M_K / M_\pi)^3 \sim 46\)

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) from the meson sector to baryons, building on the heavy fermion formalism of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) to overcome the problem of the non-vanishing baryon mass in the chiral limit identified by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988). The lectures enable systematic one-loop calculations of pion photo- and electroproduction, Compton scattering, and nucleon electroweak form factors, and confront chiral predictions with existing data from facilities like CEBAF at Jefferson Lab. Open problems include the three-flavor sector where kaon loop corrections are large and the consistency of baryon mass and sigma-term calculations at order \(p^4\) remains unresolved.
