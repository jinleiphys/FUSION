# Theoretical descriptions of compound-nuclear reactions: open problems & challenges

> Compound-nuclear processes play an important role for nuclear physics applications and are crucial for our understanding of the nuclear many-body problem. Despite intensive interest in this area, some of the available theoretical developments have not yet been fully tested and implemented. We revisit the general theory of compound-nuclear reactions, discuss descriptions of pre-equilibrium reactions, and consider extensions that are needed in order to get cross section information from indirect measurements.

# Theoretical descriptions of compound-nuclear reactions: open problems & challenges

## Key claim

Despite the widespread use of Hauser-Feshbach theory and pre-equilibrium models for compound-nuclear reactions, many theoretical developments have not been fully tested or implemented, and the underlying assumptions may break down for nuclei far from stability, particularly weakly-bound systems where statistical assumptions become questionable and intermediate structure may require explicit treatment of doorway states.

## Method

The authors review the projection-operator formalism of Feshbach for compound-nuclear reactions, the Kawai-Kerman-McVoy (KKM) theory that eliminates compound-direct interference terms to yield an incoherent sum of optical and fluctuation cross sections, and the exciton model of pre-equilibrium reactions formulated as a time-dependent master equation. They also examine the doorway-state formalism using a third projection operator and Ericson's fluctuation analysis for overlapping resonances, and discuss hybrid reactions that use indirect measurements to extract cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Elastic enhancement factor of 2 for the fluctuation cross section in the region of overlapping resonances, verified experimentally by Kretschmer et al. (1978)
- Elastic enhancement factor of 3 for the fluctuation cross section in the opposite limit of isolated resonances (weak absorption)
- The correlation width Gamma_corr from Ericson analysis is related to transmission coefficients by 2*pi*Gamma_corr/D = sum_c P_cc, where D is the average spacing between compound-nucleus resonances
- The single-particle density of states is approximately g ≈ A/13 MeV^(-1)
- For the 3p-2h exciton configuration, equilibration among states is plausible at values of gE below about 50, corresponding to excitation energies of about 17 MeV in ^40Ca and about 3 MeV in ^208Pb
- The minimum exciton number for equilibration within a configuration scales as n_0 ≈ sqrt(0.4*gE), while the equilibrium exciton number scales as n_eq ≈ sqrt(gE)

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Bohr (1936) on the compound-nucleus hypothesis, Hauser and Feshbach (1952) on the quantum-mechanical treatment, and Kawai, Kerman, and McVoy (1973) on eliminating compound-direct interference. It addresses open challenges identified in prior work by Goriely (1997, 1998) and Chiba (2008) regarding the need for formalisms that treat direct and compound contributions on the same footing, and by Escher et al. (2012) on extracting cross sections from indirect measurements. The paper enables future development of more predictive reaction models that incorporate microscopic structure descriptions and extend validity to nuclei far from stability, particularly for applications in nuclear astrophysics and radioactive-beam experiments.
