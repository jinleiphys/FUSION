# Hadronic Interactions with lattice QCD

> 4 pages, 7 figures Talk presented on behalf of the NPLQCD Collaboration at MENU 2010, May 31-June 4, 2010. College of William and Mary, Williamsburg, Virginia, USA

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of hadronic interactions are now precise enough to post-dict known nuclear phenomena (e.g., large nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths and the deuteron), but all current fully-dynamical results are obtained at unphysical pion masses (typically m_pi ~ 300–390 MeV) and require chiral extrapolation to reach the physical pion mass; the calculations at m_pi ~ 390 MeV should be viewed only as a proof of principle.

## Method

The NPLQCD collaboration performed n_f=2+1 (fully-dynamical up, down, strange) lattice QCD calculations using a mixed-action scheme of domain-wall valence quarks on rooted staggered sea configurations, with lattice spacings b ~ 0.12–0.125 fm and volumes L ~ 2.5 fm. Two-particle energies in finite volume were extracted from Euclidean-space correlation functions and converted to scattering parameters via the Lüscher formalism; for baryon-baryon systems, the signal-to-noise degradation at large times was managed by high-statistics sampling. Multi-baryon correlation functions (e.g., triton channel) were computed at m_pi ~ 390 MeV and compared with quenched results from the PACS-CS collaboration at m_pi ~ 800 MeV.

## Key numbers

- m_pi a_pi+pi+ (I=2) = -0.04330 ± 0.00042, from NPLQCD mixed-action n_f=2+1 calculation extrapolated to physical pion mass
- m_pi a_pi+pi+ (I=2) = -0.04385 ± 0.00028 ± 0.00038, from ETM n_f=2 calculation at physical pion mass
- m_pi a_pi+pi+ (I=0) = 0.220 ± 0.005 and m_pi a_pi+pi+ (I=2) = -0.0444 ± 0.0010, from Roy-equation analysis (Colangelo 2001, Leutwyler 2008)
- Chiral perturbation theory leading-order predictions: m_pi a_pi+pi+ (I=0) = 0.1588, m_pi a_pi+pi+ (I=2) = -0.04537 (Weinberg 1966)
- Lattice parameters: lattice spacing b ≤ 0.12 fm, spatial extent L ≥ 2.5 fm, typical pion masses m_pi ~ 300–390 MeV in dynamical calculations; NPLQCD baryon-baryon calculations at m_pi ~ 390 MeV on 20^3 × 128 anisotropic clover configurations
- PACS-CS quenched calculation of triton and alpha-particle channels at m_pi ~ 800 MeV

## Context

This talk summarizes the NPLQCD collaboration’s program to compute hadronic interactions from lattice QCD, extending earlier work by Lüscher (1986, 1990) on finite-volume methods for extracting scattering amplitudes. The meson-meson results (pi+pi+, K+K+, pi+K+) show a cancellation between chiral logs and counterterms that is not fully understood, while the baryon-baryon results at unphysical pion masses (Beane 2006, Beane 2009) are consistent with quenched calculations (Aoki 2008) and provide a proof of principle for future calculations at physical pion masses. The multi-baryon calculations (Beane 2009, Yamazaki 2009) represent the first steps toward computing light-nuclei properties directly from QCD, but require substantially smaller pion masses, larger volumes, and finer lattice spacings before they can provide meaningful constraints on nuclear interactions at the physical point.
