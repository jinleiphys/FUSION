# Exploring the properties of the Hadronic Phase in Heavy-Ion Collisions at RHIC Energies via Partial Chemical Equilibrium

> The hadronic phase in heavy-ion collisions plays a crucial role in shaping the final-state hadron abundances. In this work, we study Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 7.7-200 GeV using the Hadron Resonance Gas model in Partial Chemical Equilibrium (HRG-PCE). By fitting the yields of stable hadrons and short-lived resonances such as K$^*(892)^0$, we extract both chemical and kinetic freeze-out temperatures as functions of center-of-mass energy and centrality. The analysis, performed using the Thermal-FIST package, avoids assumptions about radial flow profile or freeze-out hypersurfaces. Furthermore, we estimate the baryon annihilation freeze-out temperature from the experimentally measured $\bar{\rm p}/$p ratio, using the HRG-PCE framework extended to include $B\bar{B} \leftrightarrow nπ$ reactions. The inferred annihilation freeze-out temperature lies between the chemical and kinetic freeze-out temperatures, suggesting that baryon annihilation remains active in the early hadronic phase but ceases prior to kinetic freeze-out. These results provide a consistent picture of the sequential decoupling of hadronic processes and demonstrate that inelastic hadronic interactions significantly influence the chemical composition of the system between chemical and kinetic freeze-outs at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the hadronic phase in heavy-ion collisions undergoes a sequential decoupling of processes, with baryon-antibaryon annihilation freezing out at a temperature between chemical and kinetic freeze-out, but this ordering is model-dependent and relies on the assumption that short-lived resonances remain in relative chemical equilibrium until kinetic freeze-out.

## Method

The authors used the Hadron Resonance Gas model in Partial Chemical Equilibrium (HRG-PCE) implemented in the Thermal-FIST package to simultaneously fit mid-rapidity yields of stable hadrons (π±, K±, K_S^0, p, φ, Λ, Ξ) and the short-lived resonance K*(892)^0 measured by the STAR collaboration in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 7.7–200 GeV across multiple centrality classes. They extended the HRG-PCE framework to include baryon-antibaryon annihilation reactions (B\bar{B} ↔ nπ) and extracted the annihilation freeze-out temperature by mapping the centrality dependence of the experimentally measured \bar{p}/p ratio onto theoretical predictions.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: √s_NN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, and 200 GeV in Au+Au collisions
- Short-lived resonance threshold: decay widths Γ > 15 MeV (lifetimes τ ≲ 13.2 fm/c)
- Baryon-antibaryon annihilation cross sections: > 60 mb
- K*(892)^0 lifetime: ∼4.2 fm/c; ρ^0 lifetime: ∼1.3 fm/c
- Average pion multiplicity per nucleon-antinucleon annihilation: ⟨n_π^{N\bar{N}}⟩ = 5
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature for HRG-PCE predictions: T_kin = 100 MeV (with variation band 90–110 MeV)
- Chemical freeze-out temperature at LHC energies: T_ch = 155 MeV (fixed for Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV)

## Context

This work extends the HRG-PCE framework previously applied at LHC energies by Motornenko et al. (2019) and Vovchenko et al. (2022) to the RHIC beam energy scan range, where baryon chemical potential is non-zero. The results contradict blast-wave model analyses by STAR (2017) that yield higher kinetic freeze-out temperatures, though a separate blast-wave analysis by Melo et al. (2019) that includes resonance decay effects gives T_kin values comparable to the HRG-PCE results. The framework enables future studies of additional short-lived resonances (ρ^0, Σ*±, Δ++) as experimental data become available, and the extracted temperatures can be compared with rate-equation approaches such as those by Neidig et al. (2025).
