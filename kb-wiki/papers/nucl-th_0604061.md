# Quark Model Estimates of the Structure of the Meson-N-N*(1535,1/2-) Transition Vertices

> We address an actual problem of baryon-resonance dominated meson-exchange processes in the low GeV regime, i.e. the phase and the structure of meson-NN* transition vertices. Our starting point is a quark-diquark model for the baryons (obeying approximate covariance; the mesons are kept as elementary objects), together with the relative phases for the NN vertices, as determined from low energy NN scattering. From the explicit representation of the N and N* baryons, we exemplify the derivation of the coupling constants and form factors of the NN*(1535,1/2-)transition vertices for pseudo-scalar, scalar and vector mesons.

# Quark Model Estimates of the Structure of the Meson-N-N*(1535,1/2-) Transition Vertices

## Key claim

The relative phases of meson-NN* transition vertices for the N*(1535,1/2-) resonance, derived from a quark-diquark model, confirm the standard iγ₅ recipe for the magnetic vector meson term but find the opposite sign for the time-like (ε₀) component of the vector meson coupling compared to the naive extension from NN vertices, leading to different interference patterns among ρ, ω, scalar, and pseudoscalar meson exchanges.

## Method

The authors construct a quark-diquark representation of the nucleon and the N*(1535) resonance, treating mesons as elementary objects that couple directly to the quark-diquark system. They derive the meson-NN* vertex structures in momentum space from harmonic oscillator wave functions, evaluating overlap integrals to extract both the relative phases of coupling constants (in the limit of small momentum transfer) and the momentum dependence of form factors for scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector mesons up to momentum transfers of 1 GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Baryon size parameters are taken as a = a* = 0.7 fm, determined from a fit to the proton charge form factor compared to the standard dipole representation with Λ² = 0.71 GeV²
- The universal scaling constant for coupling constants is C = √(16/3) × (a a*/(a² + a*²))^(5/2)
- Lorentz quenching reduces the effective size parameter from a² to a²_eff = (3/4)a² for q² = M² (where M is the nucleon mass)
- The pseudoscalar NN* form factor has the structure F_PS*(q²) ~ (1 + (1/6)a²q²) exp(-a²q²/4) for a = a* = a_λ
- The ratio F((1 GeV/c)²)/F(0) is enhanced by typically a factor of 2 due to Lorentz quenching

## Context

This work addresses the problem of unknown phases and form factors in meson-NN* vertices that cause uncertainties up to one order of magnitude in near-threshold η-production calculations (as shown in prior work by the authors and others). The quark-diquark approach extends earlier non-relativistic constituent quark model analyses (Reference 16 in the paper) and provides a covariant framework that incorporates Lorentz boosts, which remain problematic in three-quark models (Reference 19). The results are intended for application in meson-exchange models at high momentum transfers up to 1 GeV/c, particularly for reactions at COSY, MAMI, ELSA, and CEBAF facilities.
