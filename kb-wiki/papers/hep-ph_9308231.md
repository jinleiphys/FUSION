# Factorial Moments in a Generalized Lattice Gas Model

> We construct a simple multicomponent lattice gas model in one dimension in which each site can either be empty or occupied by at most one particle of any one of $D$ species. Particles interact with a nearest neighbor interaction which depends on the species involved. This model is capable of reproducing the relations between factorial moments observed in high--energy scattering experiments for moderate values of $D$. The factorial moments of the negative binomial distribution can be obtained exactly in the limit as $D$ becomes large, and two suitable prescriptions involving randomly drawn nearest neighbor interactions are given. These results indicate the need for considerable care in any attempt to extract information regarding possible critical phenomena from empirical factorial moments.

## Key claim

A generalized one-dimensional lattice gas model with multiple particle species can exactly reproduce the factorial moments of the negative binomial distribution in the limit of infinitely many species, but this does not imply that empirical factorial moments indicate critical phenomena; rather, the agreement at small bin sizes is a trivial consequence of fixing the global mean and dispersion.

## Method

The authors construct a one-dimensional lattice gas model where each site can be empty or occupied by at most one particle of any of D species, with species-dependent chemical potentials and nearest-neighbor interactions. They diagonalize the (D+1)-dimensional transfer matrix to compute q-body correlation functions and factorial moments analytically in the thermodynamic limit, then impose constraints on the eigenvalues and eigenvectors to match the negative binomial factorial moments. Two prescriptions are given for randomly drawing the nearest-neighbor interaction strengths to achieve this matching.

## Key numbers

- For p-pbar scattering at 900 GeV, F2(M) grows from 1 to roughly 1.7, F3 from 1 to 4.2, F4 from 1 to 15, and F5 from 1 to 80 over the range of bin numbers M considered.
- For 200 GeV/A 16O and 32S scattering on emulsions, data is limited to the range 1 < F2 < 1.35, where the difference between lattice gas and negative binomial F3 is less than 2.8%.
- For 200 GeV p on emulsion and 900 GeV p-pbar scattering, data covers 1 < F2 ≤ 1.7; at the upper end the difference between lattice gas and negative binomial F3 grows to 6.2%, while nonlinear terms account for 24% of F3, and data uncertainties are approximately 7.2%.

## Context

This work extends the lattice gas model of Chau and Huang (1993) for factorial moments in high-energy scattering by introducing multiple particle species, showing that the negative binomial distribution's factorial moments can be reproduced exactly in the D → ∞ limit. The results caution against interpreting factorial moment scaling as evidence for critical phenomena, since the linear term in M is model-independent for any one-dimensional system with short-range correlations. The random-dynamics prescription provides a theoretical basis for why cascade model calculations (Bialas and Peschanski, 1986; Giovannini, 1988) are insensitive to detailed input parameters.
