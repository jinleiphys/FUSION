# Spin Polarized Non-Relativistic Fermions in 1+1 Dimensions

> We study by Monte Carlo methods the thermodynamics of a spin polarized gas of non-relativistic fermions in 1+1 dimensions. The main result of this work is that our action suffers no significant sign problem for any spin polarization in the region relevant for dilute degenerate fermi gases. This lack of sign problem allows us to study attractive spin polarized fermions non-perturbatively at spin polarizations not previously explored. For some parameters values we verify results previously obtained by methods which include an uncontrolled step like complex Langevin and/or analytical continuation from imaginary chemical potential. For others, larger values of the polarization, we deviate from these previous results.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that their lattice action for spin-polarized non-relativistic fermions in 1+1 dimensions suffers no significant sign problem for any spin polarization in the region relevant for dilute degenerate Fermi gases, enabling direct Monte Carlo simulations without the need for uncontrolled methods like complex Langevin or analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential.

## Method

The authors study the thermodynamics of a dilute system of non-relativistic spin-1/2 fermions interacting via an attractive s-wave contact interaction in 1+1 dimensions, using a lattice discretization with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to convert the quartic fermion term into a quadratic term coupled to a real auxiliary field. They compute the fermion determinant using Schur complement methods to reduce computational complexity, and sample configurations using both Metropolis and Hybrid Molecular Dynamics (HMC) algorithms, with improvements to the continuum time and space limits by using exponentiated and continuum dispersion relations. The sign of the determinant is included in measured observables via reweighting, but the authors report that no negative determinants were encountered in any of their simulations.

## Key numbers

* For the continuum time limit at βμ = 2.0 and βh = 2.0 with Δx = 1 and Nx = 61, the density and spin converge to the same limit using both linear and quadratic extrapolations, with the exponentiated B matrix showing faster convergence.
* For the thermodynamic limit of density at βμ = 2.0 and βh = 2.0, the thermodynamic limit is achieved with L ≥ 20 for this high density.
* For the trapping test with G = 1/√8, L = 61, β = 8, βμ = 4, βh = 4, Δx = 1, and Δt = 1/20, the density remained unchanged as the imaginary shift δ was varied, while the average phase ⟨cos(Im S)⟩ decreased significantly, confirming no trapping.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin-polarized non-relativistic fermions that relied on methods with uncontrolled steps, such as complex Langevin (Rammelmuller 2017, Loheac 2017) and analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential (PhysRevA.92.063609, 2015). The authors verify results from these prior approaches for some parameter values but find deviations for larger polarizations. The mild sign problem enables direct non-perturbative investigations of spin-polarized systems, with preliminary analysis suggesting the sign problem is also mild in higher spatial dimensions, potentially enabling future studies of atomic and nuclear systems in 2+1 and 3+1 dimensions.
