# Conservation of Isospin in Neutron-Rich Fission Fragments

> On the occasion of the $75^{th}$ anniversary of the fission phenomenon, we present a surprisingly simple result which highlights the important role of isospin and its conservation in neutron rich fission fragments. We have analysed the fission fragment mass distribution from two recent heavy-ion reactions $^{238}$U($^{18}$O,f) and $^{208}$Pb($^{18}$O,f) as well as a thermal neutron fission reaction $^{245}$Cm(n$^{th}$,f). We find that the conservation of the total isospin explains the overall trend in the observed relative yields of fragment masses in each fission pair partition. The isospin values involved are very large making the effect dramatic. The findings open the way for more precise calculations of fission fragment distributions in heavy nuclei and may have far reaching consequences for the drip line nuclei, HI fusion reactions, and calculation of decay heat in the fission phenomenon.

## Key claim

The conservation of total isospin, with the compound nucleus assigned a unique isospin value equal to the sum of the target and projectile isospins, explains the overall trend in the observed relative yields of fission fragment mass pairs in each partition, provided that the residual compound nucleus is assigned the minimum even or odd isospin value consistent with the dominant neutron emission channel.

## Method

The authors analyzed fission fragment mass distributions from two heavy-ion fusion-fission reactions, $^{238}$U($^{18}$O,f) and $^{208}$Pb($^{18}$O,f), and one thermal neutron-induced fission reaction, $^{245}$Cm(n$^{th}$,f). For each reaction, the compound nucleus isospin was fixed as the sum of the projectile and target isospins, and the isospin of the residual compound nucleus after neutron emission was assigned based on the dominant neutron multiplicity channel. The relative yields of correlated fragment pairs within each partition were then calculated from the square of the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients coupling the fragment isospins to the residual compound nucleus isospin, and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For $^{238}$U($^{18}$O,f), the compound nucleus $^{256}$Fm has $T_{CN}=28$; the 12n emission channel dominates the Sn-Sn partition ($T_{3_{RCN}}=22$), the 8n channel dominates the Ru-Ba partition ($T_{3_{RCN}}=24$), and the 10n channel dominates all other partitions ($T_{3_{RCN}}=23$).
- For $^{208}$Pb($^{18}$O,f), the compound nucleus $^{226}$Th has $|T_{CN},T_{3_{CN}}\rangle = |23,23\rangle$; the 6n emission channel dominates the Ru-Pd and Kr-Xe partitions, giving $|T_{RCN},T_{3_{RCN}}\rangle = |26,20\rangle$.
- For $^{245}$Cm(n$^{th}$,f), the compound nucleus $^{246}$Cm has $|T_{CN},T_{3_{CN}}\rangle = |27,27\rangle$; the 4n emission channel has the largest weight factor of 0.334261, with $T_{RCN}=28$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical predictions of Lane and Soper (1962) that isospin may become a good quantum number in heavy nuclei, and the arguments of Robson (1973) that neutron-rich fission fragments carry a pure isospin. It provides the first direct evidence of isospin conservation in neutron-rich heavy nuclei and their reactions, and opens the way for more precise calculations of fission fragment distributions, with potential consequences for understanding drip line nuclei, heavy-ion fusion reactions, and calculation of decay heat in fission.
