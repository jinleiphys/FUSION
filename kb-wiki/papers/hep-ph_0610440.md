# Potential Models and Lattice Gauge Current-Current Correlators

> We compare current-current correlators in lattice gauge calculations with correlators in different potential models, for a pseudoscalar charmonium in the quark-gluon plasma. An important ingredient in the evaluation of the current-current correlator in the potential model is the basic principle that out of the set of continuum states, only resonance states and Gamow states with lifetimes of sufficient magnitudes can propagate as composite objects and can contribute to the current-current correlator. When the contributions from the bound states and continuum states are properly treated, the potential model current-current correlators obtained with the potential proposed in Ref. [11] are consistent with the lattice gauge correlators. The proposed potential model thus gains support to be a useful tool to complement lattice gauge calculations for the study of $Q\bar Q$ states at high temperatures.

## Key claim

When continuum states are properly restricted to only resonance states and Gamow states with lifetimes sufficiently long compared to the propagation time, the potential model current-current correlators obtained with the linear-combination potential of Wong et al. (2005) are consistent with lattice gauge correlators, contrary to the claim of Mocsy and Petreczky (2006) that potential models cannot describe heavy quarkonia above the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors computed the pseudoscalar charmonium current-current correlator in a non-relativistic potential model by summing contributions from bound states and from continuum Gamow states with positive time delay (derived from the phase shift derivative) and with widths less than the maximum detectable width of 2T. The wave functions at the origin for bound and continuum states were obtained from the Schrödinger equation with the linear-combination potential of Wong et al. (2005), which uses a temperature-dependent mixture of the lattice gauge free energy F₁ and internal energy U₁. The resulting correlator ratio G(τ)/G_recon(τ) was compared to lattice gauge data from Datta et al. (2003).

## Key numbers

- The maximum width for continuum states detectable by the correlator measurement is Γ_max = 2T, derived from the minimum lifetime τ_min_life = β/2 = 1/(2T).
- At temperatures above approximately 1.6 T_c, S-wave continuum states with positive time delays and finite lifetimes appear and contribute to the correlator.
- The width of a continuum state is given by Γ_k = 2 / (∂δ_k/∂ε_k), where δ_k is the phase shift and ε_k is the continuum energy.

## Context

This work extends the potential model approach of Wong et al. (2005, 2006) for heavy quarkonia at high temperatures, which uses a linear combination of lattice gauge free energy F₁ and internal energy U₁. It directly contradicts the conclusion of Mocsy and Petreczky (2006) that potential models cannot describe heavy quarkonia above T_c, by showing that the disagreement arose from including free-fermion continuum states that lack the temporal and spatial correlations needed for composite propagation. The method adapts techniques from nuclear physics for calculating the density of continuum states of composite objects, as used by Tubbs (1979) and Shlomo et al. (1992, 1995, 1997). This enables potential models to serve as a complementary tool to lattice gauge calculations for studying Q\bar Q states at high temperatures.
