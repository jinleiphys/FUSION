# Elliptic flow and nearly perfect fluidity in dilute Fermi gases

> In this contribution we summarize recent progress in understanding the shear viscosity of strongly correlated dilute Fermi gases. We discuss predictions from kinetic theory, and show how these predictions can be tested using recent experimental data on elliptic flow. We find agreement between theory and experiments in the high temperature regime $T\gg T_F$, where $T_F$ is the the temperature where quantum degeneracy effects become important. In the low temperature regime, $T\sim T_F$, the strongest constraints on the shear viscosity come from experimental studies of the damping of collective modes. These experiments indicate that $η/s\lsim 0.5\hbar/k_B$, where $η$ is the shear viscosity and $s$ is the entropy density.

## Key claim
Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in dilute Fermi gases reaches a minimum near the phase transition temperature with a trap-averaged value of η/s ≲ 0.5 ℏ/k_B, but this result is a trap-averaged quantity and improved data will be needed to determine the local value.

## Method
The author reviews kinetic theory predictions for shear viscosity in dilute Fermi gases at unitarity, then compares these predictions to experimental data on elliptic flow from deformed trap release experiments and on damping of collective radial breathing modes. The elliptic flow data are analyzed using a scaling solution to the Navier-Stokes equations with shear viscosity proportional to density, while the collective mode data are analyzed using the measured damping rate of the radial breathing mode converted to η/s via published entropy per particle measurements.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity in the zero frequency limit at unitarity: η = 15 (mT)^(3/2) / (32 √π) (in units where ℏ = k_B = 1)
- Shear viscosity in the weak coupling limit a → 0: η = 15 (mT)^(3/2) / (32 √π) × 1/(3mTa²)
- Critical temperature for superfluidity: T_c ≃ 0.15 E_F
- Minimum trap-averaged η/s ≃ 0.5 ℏ/k_B near the phase transition temperature
- Kinetic theory prediction for η_0 = 15/(32√π) ≃ 0.26, compared to experimental fit η_0 = 0.33
- Frequency of radial breathing mode: ω = (10/3)^(1/2) ω_⟂
- Prandtl number in the high temperature limit from kinetic theory: Pr = 2/3

## Context
This work extends the study of nearly perfect fluidity from the quark gluon plasma at RHIC (Adler 2003, Back 2004, Adams 2004) to dilute Fermi gases, where Feshbach resonances allow tuning of interactions to the unitarity limit. The results are compared to the proposed lower bound η/s = ℏ/(4π k_B) from the AdS/CFT correspondence (Policastro 2001, Kovtun 2004). The analysis uses experimental data from O'Hara (2002) on elliptic flow and Kinast (2004) on collective mode damping, with thermodynamic input from Luo (2008). Future work requires better description of the transition from hydrodynamic to kinetic behavior in the dilute corona, and development of non-perturbative calculations of transport properties, including sum rules from Taylor and Randeria (2010) and resummed diagrammatic schemes from Enss, Haussmann and Zwerger (2010).
