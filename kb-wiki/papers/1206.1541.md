# Nuclear and partonic dynamics in the EMC effect

> It has been recently confirmed that the magnitude of the EMC effect measured in electron deep inelastic scattering is linearly related to the Short Range Correlation scaling factor obtained from electron inclusive scattering. By using a $x$-rescaling approach we are able to understand the interplay between the quark-gluon and hadronic degrees of freedom in the discussion of the EMC effect.

## Key claim

The linear correlation between the EMC effect slope and the Short Range Correlation (SRC) scale factor \(a_2(A/d)\) can be extended to a linear correlation between the EMC effect slope and the \(x\)-rescaling parameter \(\eta\), but a piecewise linear fit with a transition at carbon-12 may be more statistically appropriate than a single linear fit for both light and heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors reanalyze EMC effect data using an \(x\)-rescaling formalism where the nuclear structure function is evaluated at a rescaled Bjorken variable \(x^* = \eta x\), with \(\eta\) a free parameter per nucleus related to nucleon binding energy. They fit the EMC ratio \(R(A) = F_2^{(A)}(x^*,Q^2)/F_2^{(D)}(x,Q^2)\) to experimental data from Jefferson Lab (E03-103) and SLAC for nuclei from helium-3 to gold-197, using the Martin-Stirling-Thorne-Watt parton distribution parametrization for free nucleons. They then compare the resulting \(\eta\) values to the SRC scale factors \(a_2(A/d)\) from inclusive electron scattering, performing both single-linear and piecewise-linear fits.

## Key numbers

* The single linear fit of EMC slope \(dR/dx\) versus \(a_2(A/d)\) yields a slope of \(0.090 \pm 0.012\) with \(\tilde{\chi}^2/dof = 2.31/4\).
* The piecewise linear fit of \(dR/dx\) versus \(a_2(A/d)\) gives slopes of \(0.076 \pm 0.016\) for light nuclei and \(0.22 \pm 0.07\) for heavy nuclei, with \(\tilde{\chi}^2/dof = 0.152/3\).
* The single linear fit of \(dR/dx\) versus \(\eta\) yields a slope of \(13.11 \pm 1.7\) with \(\tilde{\chi}^2/dof = 7.65/7\).
* The piecewise linear fit of \(dR/dx\) versus \(\eta\) gives slopes of \(19.5 \pm 3.7\) for light nuclei and \(9.3 \pm 2.5\) for heavy nuclei, with \(\tilde{\chi}^2/dof = 5.22/5\).
* The transition between light and heavy nuclei occurs at \(\eta^0 = 1.015\), corresponding to carbon-12.
* The \(x\)-rescaling parameter \(\eta\) values obtained are: \(^3\)He: 1.0040, \(^4\)He: 1.0115, \(^9\)Be: 1.0125, \(^{12}\)C: 1.0160, \(^{56}\)Fe: 1.0255, \(^{197}\)Au: 1.0330.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Weinstein (2010) and Hen (2012) of a linear relation between the EMC effect and SRC scale factors, and connects it to the older \(x\)-rescaling model of Garcia Canal (1984) and Akulinichev (1985). The authors show that both the hadronic (SRC) and partonic (\(x\)-rescaling) descriptions are mathematically related, suggesting a QCD-hadron duality in the EMC effect. The piecewise behavior around carbon-12 indicates that different dynamics may dominate in light versus heavy nuclei, and the authors predict \(a_2(A/d)\) values for aluminum-27, calcium-40, and silver-108 that could be tested by future experiments.
