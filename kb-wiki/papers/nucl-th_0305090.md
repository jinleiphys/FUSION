# Ab initio shell model with a genuine three-nucleon force for the p-shell nuclei

> The ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) is extended to include a realistic three-body interaction in calculations for p-shell nuclei. The NCSM formalism is reviewed and new features needed in calculations with three-body forces are discussed in detail. We present results of first applications to 6,7Li, 6He, 7,8,10Be, 10,11,12B, 12N and 10,11,12,13C using the Argonne V8' nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential and the Tucson-Melbourne TM'(99) three-nucleon interaction (TNI). In addition to increasing the total binding energy, we observe a substantial sensitivity in the low-lying spectra to the presence of the realistic three-body force and an overall improvement in the level-ordering and level-spacing in comparison to experiment. The greatest sensitivity occurs for states where the spin-orbit interaction strength is known to play a role. In particular, with the TNI we obtain the correct ground-state spin for 10,11,12B and 12N, contrary to calculations with NN potentials only.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the Tucson-Melbourne TM'(99) three-nucleon interaction (TNI) alongside the Argonne V8' nucleon-nucleon potential in no-core shell model calculations for p-shell nuclei produces the correct ground-state spin for 10,11B, 12N, and 12B, which is not achieved with nucleon-nucleon potentials alone, though the study is limited to a single TNI and a restricted basis size (up to 6ℏΩ for A=6,7 and 4ℏΩ for heavier nuclei).

## Method

The authors extended the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) to include a realistic three-nucleon force by first solving the three-nucleon system with and without the TNI in a harmonic-oscillator basis up to Nmax(3)=30, then deriving a three-body effective interaction via the Lee-Suzuki unitary transformation. This effective interaction was transformed from Jacobi-coordinate to Cartesian-coordinate single-particle Slater-determinant basis using generalized Brody-Moshinsky brackets, and the A-nucleon Schrödinger equation was solved using the Many-Fermion Dynamics shell model code or the REDSTICK code with the Hamiltonian including both two-body and three-body effective interactions plus a Lawson projection term for center-of-mass removal.

## Key numbers

- 6Li binding energy increased by more than 2.5 MeV when TNI is included (ℏΩ=14 MeV, up to 6ℏΩ basis space)
- 8Be binding energy gain of almost 4 MeV with TNI (4ℏΩ basis space, ℏΩ=14 MeV)
- 10B binding energy gain of almost 6 MeV for the 3+1 0 state and about 4 MeV for the 1+1 0 state with TNI (4ℏΩ basis space, ℏΩ=15 MeV)
- 11B binding energy gain of about 6 MeV with TNI (4ℏΩ basis space, ℏΩ=15 MeV)
- 6He→6Li ground-state-to-ground-state Gamow-Teller matrix element of 2.283 for AV8'+TM'(99) compared to 2.305 for AV8' alone (experimental value not given in text)
- 3H binding energy reproduced within 50 keV of exact result using Nmax(3)=30 for the TNI calculation (cutoff Λ=4.7 fitted to 3H binding energy)
- 7Li calculation at 6ℏΩ used 741,823,056 three-nucleon M-scheme matrix elements and 4,555,058,857 non-zero Hamiltonian matrix elements (compared to 235,927,305 for two-body only)

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell model formalism, previously limited to realistic two-nucleon interactions (Navratil 2000), to include genuine three-nucleon forces for p-shell nuclei, building on earlier developments for three-body effective interactions (Navratil 2001) and three-nucleon system solutions (Navratil 2003). The Green's function Monte Carlo method (Pieper 2001, Wiringa 2000) was previously the only approach capable of solving light p-shell nuclei with both realistic two- and three-nucleon forces. The results enable systematic studies of three-nucleon force effects across the p-shell and provide a benchmark for constraining the still-uncertain three-nucleon interaction form and parametrization, with future work planned using a broader variety of realistic three-nucleon interactions and larger basis spaces.
