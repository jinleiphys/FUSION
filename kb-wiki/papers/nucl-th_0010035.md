# Quarks in nuclear medium

> Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model we study nuclear matter from the point of view of quark degrees of freedom. As the nucleon model we adopt the MIT bag model and the relativistic constituent quark model, where a square well and harmonic oscillator potentials are used to confine the quarks. We introduce the Lorentz-vector type confining potential as well as the Lorentz-scalar type one in order to examine how the vector confining potential contributes to the properties of the nucleon and nuclear matter. Next, we perform a re-definition of the scalar field in matter and transform the QMC model to a QHD-type model with a non-linear scalar potential. The result obtained from QMC is then compared with the potentials which are determined so as to fit various properties of finite nuclei and nuclear matter in relativistic mean-field models. The QMC model provides the parameters $κ\sim 20 - 40$ (fm$^{-1}$) and $λ\sim 80 - 400$ for the standard, non-linear scalar potential. We discuss a relationship between the QMC and QHD-type models in detail.

## Key claim

Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the paper shows that the internal quark substructure of the nucleon generates a non-linear scalar potential in the equivalent quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) description, with the specific parameters κ ∼ 20–40 fm⁻¹ and λ ∼ 80–400 for the standard non-linear potential, but only for quark models without a strong Lorentz-vector confining potential, which otherwise produces unrealistically large effective nucleon masses and weak scalar fields.

## Method

The author employs the QMC model with two nucleon descriptions: the MIT bag model (massless quarks, square well confinement) and the relativistic constituent quark model (quark mass 300 MeV, harmonic oscillator confinement). For each, both Lorentz-scalar and Lorentz-vector confining potentials are introduced, and the Dirac equation is solved analytically for quarks in uniform nuclear matter under mean-field approximation for σ and ω mesons. The resulting effective nucleon mass as a function of the scalar field is then used to perform a field re-definition that transforms the QMC model into a QHD-type model with a non-linear scalar potential, whose parameters κ and λ are extracted and compared with phenomenological relativistic mean-field models.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³; binding energy: −15.7 MeV at saturation
- Free nucleon mass: M_N = 939 MeV; free nucleon rms charge radius: r_q = 0.6 fm
- In the harmonic oscillator model for the free nucleon: c = 1.591 fm⁻³, E_s = 344.7 MeV, E = 641.8 MeV
- MIT bag model (massless quarks, bag radius 0.8 fm): B¹/⁴ = 170.3 MeV
- Nuclear compressibility from QMC models: within experimental range K ∼ 200–300 MeV
- QMC-generated non-linear potential parameters: κ ∼ 20–40 fm⁻¹ and λ ∼ 80–400 (for models without strong vector confining potential)
- In the harmonic oscillator model at saturation density: rms radius increase ∼ 10%
- ZM model effective mass ratio: M_N^⋆/M_N ≃ 0.85; compressibility ∼ 230 MeV

## Context

This work extends the QMC model originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and developed in subsequent studies, which treats nuclear matter at the quark level with non-overlapping nucleon bags coupled to mesons. The paper establishes a formal connection between QMC and QHD-type models with non-linear scalar potentials, such as those introduced by Boguta and Bodmer (1977) to improve the original QHD model's excessive nuclear compressibility (~550 MeV). The results enable future work to extract information about in-medium nucleon quark structure from experimentally determined non-linear scalar potentials, and show that the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) derivative coupling model (1989) can be exactly transformed into a QHD model with a specific non-linear potential. The paper also notes that the coupling h(φ) to gradient terms affects surface properties of finite nuclei, as studied by Muller (1997).
