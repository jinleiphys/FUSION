# Open Quantum Systems with Kadanoff-Baym Equations

> We study the temporal evolution of quantum mechanical fermionic particles exhibiting one bound state within a one-dimensional attractive square-well potential in a heat bath of bosonic particles. For this open quantum system we formulate the non-equilibrium Kadanoff-Baym equations for the system particles by taking the interactions to be elastic 2-2 scatterings with the heat-bath particles. The corresponding spatially imhomogeneous integro-differential equations for the one-particle Greens's function are solved numerically. We demonstrate how the system particles equilibrate and thermalize with the heat bath and how the off-diagonal elements of the density matrix, expressed in the one-particle energy eigenbasis, decohere, so that only the diagonal entries, i.e. the occupation numbers, survive. In addition, the time evolution of the (retarded) Green's function also determines the spectral properties of the various one-particle quantum states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Kadanoff-Baym equations, solved numerically for a one-dimensional open quantum system of fermions in an attractive square-well potential coupled to a bosonic heat bath, automatically yield thermalization to the bath temperature and decoherence of off-diagonal density-matrix elements, without requiring the ad-hoc Lindblad operators or Markovian approximations that often fail to guarantee proper thermalization.

## Method

The authors formulate non-equilibrium Kadanoff-Baym equations for the one-particle Green's function of fermionic system particles interacting via elastic 2-2 scatterings with a bosonic heat bath, using a second-order direct Born self-energy (sunset diagram) and neglecting the Hartree tadpole term. They expand the field operators in the eigenbasis of the one-particle Hamiltonian (one bound state plus quasi-free continuum states in a finite box), reducing the spatially inhomogeneous integro-differential equations to a set of ordinary integro-differential equations for matrix-valued correlation functions in the two-time plane. These equations are solved numerically using a predictor-corrector (Heun's method) on a discretized two-time grid, with MPI-OpenMP parallelization.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the bound state: \(E_0 \sim -2.23\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (for potential depth \(V_0 = 12.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\), well width \(a = 1.2\ \mathrm{fm}\))
- Bath temperature: \(T_{\mathrm{bath}} = 100\ \mathrm{MeV}\); bath chemical potential: \(\mu_{\mathrm{bath}} = 0\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Coupling constant: \(\lambda = 0.35\)
- Fermion mass: \(m_f = 938\ \mathrm{MeV}\); boson mass: \(m_b = 138\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- System box length: \(L = 20\ \mathrm{fm}\); bath box length: \(L_{\mathrm{bath}} = 55\ \mathrm{fm}\)
- Number of time steps: \(N_t = 1400\); time step: \(\delta t = 0.075\ \mathrm{fm}\)
- Fermionic mode cutoff: \(F = 25\); bosonic mode cutoff: \(B = 30\)
- Final system temperature from Fermi-Dirac fit: \(T_{\mathrm{system}} \approx 100.133\ \mathrm{MeV}\); final system chemical potential: \(\mu_{\mathrm{system}} \approx -298.125\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Spectral width of states: approximately \(250\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (decreasing for higher states)

## Context

This work extends the application of Kadanoff-Baym equations from homogeneous closed quantum systems (Juchem 2004) and inhomogeneous systems (Dahlen 2006, Dahlen 2007, Stan 2009, PhysRevA.82.033427, PhysRevB.82.155108) to open quantum systems with a heat bath, demonstrating for the first time equilibration and decoherence in this framework. It provides a numerically expensive but theoretically rigorous alternative to Markovian Lindblad master equations (Gorini 1975, 1976CMaPh..48..119L, DIOSI1993517) and the Caldeira-Leggett framework (CALDEIRA1983587), which often fail to guarantee proper thermalization (PhysRevLett.80.5702). The approach enables future studies of bound-state formation, population, and thermalization in three dimensions, relevant for deuteron formation in heavy-ion collisions (Danielewicz 1991, Vovchenko 2019, Neidig 2021, Rais 2022, Coci 2023) and heavy quarkonia (Beraudo 2010).
