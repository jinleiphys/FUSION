# Beta Decays and Non-Standard Interactions in the LHC Era

> We consider the role of precision measurements of beta decays and light meson semi-leptonic decays in probing physics beyond the Standard Model in the LHC era. We describe all low-energy charged-current processes within and beyond the Standard Model using an effective field theory framework. We first discuss the theoretical hadronic input which in these precision tests plays a crucial role in setting the baseline for new physics searches. We then review the current and upcoming constraints on the various non-standard operators from the study of decay rates, spectra, and correlations in a broad array of light-quark systems. We finally discuss the interplay with LHC searches, both within models and in an effective theory approach. Our discussion illustrates the independent yet complementary nature of precision beta decay measurements as probes of new physics, showing them to be of continuing importance throughout the LHC era.

## Key claim

Precision beta-decay measurements of neutrons, nuclei, and mesons can probe new physics at the TeV scale with sensitivities of order 10^{-3} to 10^{-4}, providing complementary constraints to LHC searches, but only if the hadronic matrix elements of Standard Model operators are known to comparable precision (10^{-3} or better) to separate new physics signals from the Standard Model background.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory framework at the quark level, parameterizing all possible dimension-six operators that contribute to low-energy charged-current processes involving light quarks (u, d, s). They then match this to hadronic matrix elements using chiral perturbation theory and lattice QCD results for meson decay constants, form factors, and nucleon form factors, and review constraints from decay rates, spectra, and correlation coefficients in beta decays of neutrons, nuclei, pions, and kaons.

## Key numbers

- The Higgs vacuum expectation value v = (2√2 G_F)^{-1/2} ≈ 174 GeV sets the scale for new physics effects of size O((v/Λ_BSM)^2)
- The Fermi constant from muon decay is G_μ = 1.166371(6) × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}
- The pion decay constant in the authors' normalization is F_π ≈ 92 MeV
- Planned measurements aim for sensitivities between 10^{-3} and 10^{-4}, corresponding to new physics scales Λ_BSM of order a few TeV
- The effective couplings ε_α are defined at the renormalization scale μ = 2 GeV in the MS-bar scheme

## Context

This review extends earlier work by Herczeg (2001), Erler and collaborators (2004), Severijns and collaborators (2006), and Ramsey-Musolf and collaborators (2006) on precision beta-decay tests of the Standard Model. It provides an updated effective field theory framework that allows direct comparison of low-energy constraints with LHC searches through the process pp → e + ν + X, showing that precision beta-decay measurements remain competitive and complementary to collider probes throughout the LHC era.
