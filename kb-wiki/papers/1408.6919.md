# QCD inequalities for hadron interactions

> We derive generalisations of the Weingarten--Witten QCD mass inequalities for particular multi-hadron systems. For systems of any number of identical pseudo-scalar mesons of maximal isospin, these inequalities prove that interactions between the constituent mesons must be repulsive and that no bound states can form in these channels. Similar constraints in less symmetric systems are also extracted. These results are compatible with experimental results (where known) and recent lattice QCD calculations, and also lead to a more stringent bound on the nucleon mass than previously derived, $m_N \ge 3/2 m_π$.

# QCD inequalities for hadron interactions

## Key claim

The paper proves that for systems of any number of identical pseudo-scalar mesons of maximal isospin, interactions between the constituent mesons must be repulsive and no bound states can form; however, for less symmetric systems (e.g., mixed pion-kaon systems), the proof is only rigorous for certain quark mass ratios, and the authors have been unable to prove the necessary relations for all mass ratios and all particle numbers.

## Method

The author uses the positivity of the QCD functional integral measure (in the absence of a theta-term or baryon chemical potential) together with an eigenvalue decomposition of multi-hadron correlation functions. For systems of identical pseudo-scalar mesons, the correlation functions are expressed as sums of products of eigenvalues of a non-negative definite Hermitian matrix constructed from quark propagators, allowing the author to bound multi-particle correlators by products of single-particle correlators via the inequality \(c_n \leq [c_1]^n\). For less symmetric systems, the eigenvalue sums are analyzed in the continuous limit using the spectral density of the Dirac operator, and positivity of the relevant integrals is checked for specific quark mass ratios.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass bound: \(m_N \ge \frac{3}{2} m_\pi\), improving on the previous bound \(m_N \ge m_\pi\) from Weingarten (1983) and Nussinov (1984).
- For the \(I=2\) \(\pi\pi\) scattering length, chiral perturbation theory gives \(m_\pi a_{\pi\pi}^{(I=2)} = -2\pi \chi \left[1+\chi\left(3 \log\chi - L_{\pi\pi}^{(I=2)}\right)\right]\) where \(\chi = [m_\pi/(4\pi f_\pi)]^2\); the experimental value from dispersive analysis is \(m_\pi a_{\pi\pi}^{(I=2)} = -0.0444(10)\).
- The eigenvalues of the matrix \(\mathbf{\Pi}_A\) in the isospin limit occupy the interval \([0, 1/m_q]\).
- The proof for \(\pi^+K^+\) systems is complete for physical quark mass ratios (as reported in Aoki (2013)), but fails for \(K^+K^+\) or \(D^+D^+\) systems where the mass ratios are such that the proof does not apply.

## Context

This work extends the QCD mass inequalities of Weingarten (1983), Witten (1983), and Nussinov (1983) from single-hadron masses to multi-hadron systems and hadronic interactions. The results are compatible with chiral perturbation theory predictions (Weinberg (1966), Gasser (1983)) and with lattice QCD calculations of \(\pi\pi\) scattering (Yamazaki (2004), Beane (2005, 2007, 2011), Feng (2009), Yagi (2011), Fu (2013)) and \(\pi K\) scattering (Beane (2006), Detmold (2011), Fu (2011), Sasaki (2013)). The new inequalities provide fundamental constraints on low-energy constants in chiral perturbation theory and on lattice QCD studies of hadron interactions.
