# Hyperon Puzzle: Hints from Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations

> The onset of hyperons in the core of neutron stars and the consequent softening of the equation of state have been questioned for a long time. Controversial theoretical predictions and recent astrophysical observations of neutron stars are the grounds for the so-called hyperon puzzle. We calculate the equation of state and the neutron star mass-radius relation of an infinite systems of neutrons and $Λ$ particles by using the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo algorithm. We find that the three-body hyperon-nucleon interaction plays a fundamental role in the softening of the equation of state and for the consequent reduction of the predicted maximum mass. We have considered two different models of three-body force that successfully describe the binding energy of medium mass hypernuclei. Our results indicate that they give dramatically different results on the maximum mass of neutron stars, not necessarily incompatible with the recent observation of very massive neutron stars. We conclude that stronger constraints on the hyperon-neutron force are necessary in order to properly assess the role of hyperons in neutron stars.

## Key claim

Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron matter containing Lambda hyperons show that the three-body hyperon-nucleon interaction dramatically affects the equation of state, and that two different parametrizations of this force which both successfully describe medium-mass hypernuclei give dramatically different predictions for the maximum neutron star mass, meaning the hyperon puzzle is not necessarily incompatible with observations of massive neutron stars.

## Method

The authors used the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) algorithm to compute the equation of state of infinite hyperneutron matter composed of neutrons and Lambda particles. The Hamiltonian included the Argonne V8' two-nucleon potential and Urbana IX three-nucleon potential, plus a phenomenological hyperon-nucleon two-body interaction and two different parametrizations of the three-body Lambda-nucleon-nucleon force. The energy per particle was parametrized as a function of baryon density and hyperon fraction, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations for neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Lambda threshold density with only two-body Lambda-nucleon interaction: 0.24(1) fm^-3
- Lambda threshold density with three-body force parametrization (I): 0.34(1) fm^-3
- Maximum neutron star mass with only two-body Lambda-nucleon interaction: 0.66(2) solar masses
- Maximum neutron star mass with three-body force parametrization (I): 1.36(5) solar masses
- Maximum neutron star mass for pure neutron matter at densities up to 3.5 times nuclear saturation density: 2.09(1) solar masses
- With three-body force parametrization (II), Lambda particles do not appear up to the maximum density studied of 0.56 fm^-3
- Nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends previous Quantum Monte Carlo studies of pure neutron matter by Gandolfi et al. (2012, 2014) to include hyperons, and builds on earlier variational and diffusion Monte Carlo studies of hypernuclei by Lonardoni et al. (2013, 2014). The results show that fitting three-body hyperon-nucleon forces to hypernuclear binding energies alone is insufficient to determine the equation of state at neutron star densities, and that additional constraints from highly asymmetric hypernuclei or chiral perturbation theory calculations are needed to resolve the hyperon puzzle.
