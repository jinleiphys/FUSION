# Impact of nuclear structure on the CME background in $^{96}_{44}$Ru + $^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr + $^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 $\sim$ 200 GeV from a multiphase transport model

> Impacts of nuclear structure on multiplicity ($N_{ch}$) and anisotropic flows ($v_{2}$ and $v_{3}$) in the isobaric collisions of $^{96}_{44}$Ru + $^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr + $^{96}_{40}$Zr at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7, 27, 62.4 and 200 GeV are investigated by using the string melting version of A MultiPhase Transport (AMPT) model. In comparison with the experimental data released recently by the STAR collaboration, it is found that the impact of quadrupole deformation $β_{2}$ on the $v_{2}$ difference is mainly manifested in the most central collisions, while the octupole deformation $β_{3}$ is in the near-central collisions, and the neutron skin effect dominates in the mid-central collisions. Viewing from the energy dependence, these effects are magnified at lower energies.

# Wiki Page: Impact of Nuclear Structure on CME Background in Isobar Collisions

## Key claim

The differences in multiplicity and anisotropic flow between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions, which constitute the background to the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME), can be attributed to distinct nuclear structure effects that dominate in different centrality ranges: quadrupole deformation (β₂) affects the most central collisions, octupole deformation (β₃) affects near-central collisions, and the neutron skin effect dominates in mid-central collisions. These effects are magnified at lower beam energies, but the exact magnitude depends on which set of Woods-Saxon parameters is used, as the true deformation values of ⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁶Zr are not accurately known.

## Method

The string melting version of the A MultiPhase Transport (AMPT) model was used to simulate ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr collisions at √s_NN = 7.7, 27, 62.4, and 200 GeV. Five different sets of Woods-Saxon parameters (Cases 1-5) were employed, varying the quadrupole deformation β₂, octupole deformation β₃, radius R₀, and surface diffuseness a for each nucleus, to investigate how nuclear structure affects charged particle multiplicity N_ch, elliptic flow v₂, triangular flow v₃, and initial eccentricity ε₂. The simulation results were compared with STAR collaboration data at √s_NN = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Multiplicity distributions were scaled by a factor of 1.21 to match STAR data at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Case-1: Ru (β₂ = 0.13, a = 0.46 fm, R₀ = 5.13 fm) and Zr (β₂ = 0.06, a = 0.46 fm, R₀ = 5.06 fm)
- Case-2: Ru (β₂ = 0.03, a = 0.46 fm, R₀ = 5.13 fm) and Zr (β₂ = 0.18, a = 0.46 fm, R₀ = 5.06 fm)
- Case-3: Ru (β₂ = 0, a = 0.5 fm, R₀ = 5.067 fm) and Zr (β₂ = 0, a = 0.556 fm, R₀ = 4.965 fm)
- Case-4: Ru (β₂ = 0.154, β₃ = 0, a = 0.485 fm, R₀ = 5.065 fm) and Zr (β₂ = 0.062, β₃ = 0.202, a = 0.544 fm, R₀ = 4.961 fm)
- Case-5: Ru (β₂ = 0.162, β₃ = 0, a = 0.46 fm, R₀ = 5.09 fm) and Zr (β₂ = 0.06, β₃ = 0.20, a = 0.52 fm, R₀ = 5.02 fm)
- Simulated event statistics: 5M events at 7.7 GeV, 2M at 27 GeV, 2M at 62.4 GeV, and 2.5M at 200 GeV for each case
- The v₂ ratio in central collisions (0-5%) shows enhanced deviation from unity at lower energies for cases with non-zero β₂ differences
- Case-2 (Δβ₂ = -0.15) shows the strongest drop in v₂ ratio in central collisions, consistent with larger quadrupole deformation difference producing larger v₂ ratio effects

## Context

This work extends the isobar collision program initiated by the STAR collaboration (Abdallah et al., 2021) to disentangle CME signals from flow-driven backgrounds. It builds on prior studies by Jia (2021) and Nijs et al. (2021) that established the centrality-dependent roles of β₂, β₃, and neutron skin effects on v₂ ratios, and on density functional theory calculations by Xu et al. (2021) for nuclear structure parameters. The study enables future beam energy scans of isobar collisions to either constrain nuclear structure parameters or isolate CME backgrounds, and suggests that extremely ultra-central collisions (0-1% centrality) could avoid the offsetting effect of β₃ on v₂ ratios to obtain stronger monotonic energy dependence.
