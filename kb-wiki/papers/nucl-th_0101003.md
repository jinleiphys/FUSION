# Reworking the Tucson-Melbourne Three-Nucleon Potential

> We introduce new values of the strength constants (i.e., $a$, $b$, $c$, and $d$ coefficients) of the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) 2$π$ exchange three nucleon potential. The new values come from contemporary dispersion relation analyses of meson factory $π$N scattering data. We make variational Monte Carlo calculations of the triton with the original and updated three-body forces to study the effects of this update. We remove a short-range -- $π$-range part of the potential due to the $c$ coefficient and discuss the effect on the triton binding energy.

## Key claim

The paper introduces updated strength constants for the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) three-nucleon potential derived from contemporary meson factory πN scattering data, and demonstrates that removing a short-range π-range part of the potential (the c-term), now identified as spurious by chiral effective field theory, makes the three-body force less sensitive to the cutoff parameter and yields similar triton binding energies regardless of whether old or new strength constants are used.

## Method

The authors performed variational Monte Carlo calculations of the triton using the Reid v₈ nucleon-nucleon potential combined with various versions of the Tucson-Melbourne two-pion-exchange three-nucleon force. They compared the original TM force (with strength constants a, b, c, d from 1981 and 1993 analyses) against the modified TM′ force (with the c-term removed, leaving coefficients a′ = a − 2μ²c, b, and d) using strength constants derived from both pre-meson factory data (KH80) and new meson factory data (SP98/KH). The calculations were performed for three values of the cutoff parameter Λ/μ = 4.1, 5.8, and 7.1.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy with Reid v₈ alone: −7.28(3) MeV (variational) compared to Faddeev eigenvalue of −7.59 MeV
- Original TM(81) coefficients (in units of SU(2) average pion mass μ = 138.0 MeV): μa′ = −0.84, μ³b = −2.49, μ³c = 0.98, μ³d = −0.72
- Updated TM(99) coefficients: μa′ = −1.12, μ³b = −2.80, μ³c = 1.25, μ³d = −0.75
- New on-shell πN amplitude values from meson factory data: F⁺(0, m²_π⁺) = −0.05 ± 0.05 m⁻¹_π⁺ and F⁺(0, 2m²_π⁺) ≈ σ/f_π² = 1.40 ± 0.25 m⁻¹_π⁺
- The a-term alone decreased triton energy by 0.11 MeV (RSC/TM(81)) and 0.13 MeV (Reid v₈/TM(81)); the c-term decreased energy by 0.72 MeV and 0.95 MeV respectively

## Context

This work extends the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force program by incorporating updated πN scattering data from meson factories (post-1980) and addresses a structural criticism by Friar et al. (1999) who showed via chiral effective field theory that the short-range π-range part of the c-term is spurious and should be removed. The paper also relates to earlier work by the Hokkaido group and the São Paulo group on contact terms in three-body forces, and enables more reliable calculations of light nuclei with three-nucleon forces that are less sensitive to the cutoff parameter.
