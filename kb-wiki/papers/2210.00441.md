# Mixed configurations and intertwined quantum phase transitions in odd-mass nuclei

> We introduce a new Bose-Fermi framework for studying spectral properties and quantum phase transitions (QPTs) in odd-mass nuclei, in the presence of configuration mixing. A detailed analysis of odd-mass Nb isotopes discloses the effects of an abrupt crossing of states in normal and intruder configurations (Type II QPT), accompanied by a gradual evolution from spherical- to deformed-core shapes within the intruder configuration (Type I QPT). The pronounced presence of both types of QPTs demonstrates, for the first time, the occurrence of intertwined QPTs in odd-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new Bose-Fermi framework (IBFM-CM) for studying quantum phase transitions in odd-mass nuclei with configuration mixing, and demonstrates for the first time that intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), an abrupt Type II configuration crossing accompanied by a gradual Type I shape evolution, occur in odd-mass nuclei, specifically in Nb isotopes.

## Method

The authors extend the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) to include configuration mixing (IBFM-CM), constructing a Hamiltonian with boson, fermion, and boson-fermion interaction terms in matrix form for normal (A) and intruder (B) configurations. They apply this framework to positive-parity states in odd-mass Nb isotopes (A=93-105) by coupling a single proton in the π(1g9/2) orbit to Zr cores, using boson parameters from prior calculations of the even-even Zr isotopes and fitting fermion parameters from BCS calculations and experimental data. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized numerically, and the resulting wave functions are analyzed for configuration mixing probabilities, while electromagnetic transition rates and moments are calculated using boson and fermion operators.

## Key numbers

- The ground state in Nb isotopes changes from 9/2+1 to 5/2+1 at neutron number 60, indicating the Type II QPT crossing point.
- For 93Nb, the center of gravity of the quintuplet built on 2+1;A in 92Zr is 0.976 MeV (calculated) versus the observed 0.935 MeV for 2+1 in 92Zr.
- For 93Nb, the observed E2 rate for 9/2+2 → 9/2+1 is 1.03(9) W.u., compared to the calculated value of 0.85 W.u.
- For 93Nb, the center of gravity of the quintuplet built on 2+2;B in 92Zr is 1.705 MeV, versus 1.847 MeV for 2+2;B.
- The boson-fermion interaction parameters include Γ0 = 1.00 MeV constant across the entire Nb chain (neutron numbers 52-64), and Λ0 ranges from 1.00 MeV (neutron numbers 52-54) to 3.80 MeV (neutron numbers 60-64).
- The fermion effective charge for E2 transitions is ef = -2.361 √W.u., determined from a fit to the ground state quadrupole moment of 93Nb.

## Context

This work extends the study of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs) from even-even Zr isotopes (Gavrielov 2019, Gavrielov 2022) to odd-mass nuclei for the first time. The IBFM-CM framework builds on the IBM-CM approach used extensively for even-even nuclei (Duval 1981, Duval 1982, Frank 2006) and the standard IBFM for odd-mass nuclei without configuration mixing (Scholten and Blasi 1982, Petrellis 2011). The results demonstrate that the intricate IQPT scenario previously observed in even-even Zr isotopes persists in the adjacent odd-even Nb isotopes, motivating further experimental non-yrast spectroscopy and new investigations of multiple QPTs in other Bose-Fermi systems.
