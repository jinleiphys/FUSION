# Simulating chiral anomalies with spin dynamics

> Considering that the chiral kinetic equations of motion (CEOM) can be derived from the spin kinetic equations of motion (SEOM) for massless particles with approximations, we simulate the chiral anomalies by using the latter in a box system with the periodic boundary condition under a uniform external magnetic field. We found that the chiral magnetic effect is weaker while the damping of the chiral magnetic wave is stronger from the SEOM compared with that from the CEOM. In addition, effects induced by chiral anomalies from the SEOM are less sensitive to the decay of the magnetic field than from the CEOM due to the spin relaxation process.

## Key claim

The spin kinetic equations of motion (SEOM) produce qualitatively similar chiral effects to the chiral kinetic equations of motion (CEOM) in box simulations, but the chiral magnetic effect is weaker and the damping of the chiral magnetic wave is stronger from the SEOM; however, due to the spin relaxation process, effects from the SEOM are less sensitive to a rapidly decaying magnetic field than those from the CEOM.

## Method

The authors performed transport simulations in a cubic box (side length 10 fm) with periodic boundary conditions under a uniform external magnetic field, comparing two scenarios: one using the SEOM (Eqs. 1-3) and one using the CEOM (Eqs. 4-5). Both scenarios included isotropic two-body scatterings with a cross section determined by a shear viscosity of ℏ/4π, and the initial momentum distribution was sampled from a Fermi-Dirac distribution at temperature 300 MeV with electric and axial chemical potentials. For the SEOM scenario, particle spins were initialized as σ = c k̂, and after each scattering the spin direction was determined by energy conservation and a maximum polarization condition relative to the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the system: T = 300 MeV
- Side length of the cubic box: 2l = 10 fm
- Time step: Δt = 0.01 fm/c
- Cell volume for collisions: (Δx)³ = 1 fm³
- Magnetic field strength used: eB_y = 0.5 GeV/fm (for the cosine angle distribution study)
- Initial axial chemical potential for CME study: μ₅ = 20 MeV
- Equilibrated electric current reached at about t = 4 fm/c, independent of magnetic field strength
- Phase velocity from CEOM is slightly higher than from SEOM, both lower than the theoretical limit v_p = α(3ℏeB)/(2π²T²) with α=1
- Diffusion constant D_L from SEOM decreases with increasing eB_y, while from CEOM it increases slightly with increasing eB_y
- Under a fast damping magnetic field with lifetime τ = 0.4 fm/c, the SEOM leads to a larger final electric quadrupole moment than the CEOM

## Context

This work extends the comparison between spin dynamics and chiral kinetic theory initiated in Zhou (2019), where the CEOM were derived from the SEOM via an ℏ expansion in the adiabatic limit. The study directly simulates the SEOM without the truncation needed for the CEOM (which removes particles with too small k, using a truncation 0.3 < √G < 1.7). The results are relevant to understanding the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral magnetic wave (CMW) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the chiral symmetry restoration of partons at high energy densities makes the massless limit approximately valid. The authors note that future work should incorporate side-jump corrections to conserve angular momentum in collisions, as discussed by Chen (2014, 2015), and account for additional quantum corrections to the dispersion relation from more recent chiral kinetic studies.
