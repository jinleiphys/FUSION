# Systematic investigation of E1 strength for the isotopes from Z = 28 to 50

> We carry out a systematic study of electric dipole mode (E1) for neutron-rich isotopes from nickel (Z=28) to tin (Z=50) using a time-dependent mean-field theory. Our time-dependent scheme is the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory which can self-consistently describe nuclear dynamics with pairing correlation. We focus our discussion on the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) and E1 polarizability. The correlation between neutron-skin thickness and PDR strongly depends on the neutron number, but the correlation between the skin thickness and E1 polarizability is much more stable.

## Key claim

The correlation between pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) strength and neutron-skin thickness is linear but strongly depends on the neutron number, whereas the correlation between E1 polarizability and neutron-skin thickness is much more stable across isotopic chains and different Skyrme energy functionals.

## Method

The authors used the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Cb-TDHFB) theory to compute the linear response of over 350 neutron-rich isotopes from nickel (Z=28) to tin (Z=50). They applied an external dipole field as a delta-function perturbation to the ground state, then performed real-time evolution and Fourier transformation to obtain the E1 strength function. The PDR ratio was defined as the fraction of the energy-weighted sum rule up to 10 MeV, and the E1 polarizability was computed from the inverse-energy-weighted sum rule up to 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Over 350 isotopes from Z=28 (nickel) to Z=50 (tin) were systematically studied.
- The cutoff energy for PDR definition was E_c = 10 MeV; the total integration energy was E_T = 100 MeV.
- The coordinate-space mesh used Δx = Δy = Δz = 1.0 fm in a sphere of radius 15 fm.
- For Sn isotopes from N=50 to 58, the slope of the PDR-skin correlation is about half that in Ge isotopes.
- The PDR ratio shows a jump for isotopes with N > 82.
- Two Skyrme functionals were compared: SkM* and SkI3, with SkI3 tuned to reproduce single-particle levels in Pb isotopes from relativistic mean-field models.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the linear relation between PDR and neutron-skin thickness (prior work from 2004 and 2005) and addresses the conflicting finding from a covariance analysis (2011) that showed weak PDR-skin correlation but strong polarizability-skin correlation. The systematic calculation over a wide isotopic range confirms that E1 polarizability provides a more robust constraint on neutron-skin thickness than PDR strength alone, supporting the experimental measurement of dipole polarizability in 206Pb at RCNP, Osaka University.
