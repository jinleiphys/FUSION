# Charge Radii of the Meson and Baryon Octets in Quenched and Partially Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We calculate the electric charge radii of the SU(3) pseudoscalar mesons and the SU(3) octet baryons in quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory. We work in the isospin limit, up to next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, and to leading order in the heavy baryon expansion. The results are necessary for the extrapolation of future lattice calculations of meson and baryon charge radii. We also derive expressions for the nucleon and pion charge radii in SU(2) flavor away from the isospin limit.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the electric charge radii of the SU(3) pseudoscalar meson and octet baryon multiplets in both quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory, working in the isospin limit up to next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion and to leading order in the heavy baryon expansion, with the caveat that the quenched results show no meson mass dependence at this order, revealing a pathologic behavior that puts into question chiral perturbation theory extrapolations to the physical pion mass.

## Method

The authors use the heavy baryon formalism of Jenkins and Manohar, treating the decuplet baryons as dynamical degrees of freedom and keeping contributions to lowest order in the heavy baryon mass. They calculate one-loop diagrams and tree-level contributions from dimension-4 and dimension-6 operators in partially quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQCPT) and quenched chiral perturbation theory (QCPT), using dimensional regularization with subtraction of the standard ultraviolet divergences. The calculations are performed in the SU(3) flavor isospin limit, with the partially quenched theory retaining three fermionic sea quarks alongside valence and ghost quarks in the graded group SU(6|3).

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is taken as f = 132 MeV
- The meson mass formula at lowest order is m_{QQ'}^2 = (4λ/f^2)(m_Q + m_{Q'})
- For the π+ in PQCPT, the form factor is G^{PQ}_{π+}(q^2) = 1 - (1/(16π^2 f^2))[2F_{uj} + F_{ur}] + (4/f^2)α_9 q^2
- For the π+ in QCPT, the form factor is G^{Q}_{π+}(q^2) = 1 + (4/f^2)α_9^Q q^2, showing no meson mass dependence
- The first derivative of the loop function at q^2=0 is 6 d/dq^2 F_{QQ'}|_{q^2=0} = log(m_{QQ'}^2/μ^2) + 1
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is taken as Λ_χ ∼ 4πf for power counting

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of octet baryon magnetic moments in quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory by Savage (2001) and Chen (2001) to the charge radii observables. The results are necessary for extrapolating future lattice QCD calculations of meson and baryon charge radii from the heavier quark masses used on the lattice (currently on the order of the strange quark mass) down to physical light quark masses. The paper shows that quenched chiral perturbation theory produces meson mass-independent charge radii at this order, in contrast to the partially quenched results which recover the QCD results of Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) in the appropriate limit, enabling physical predictions from partially quenched lattice data.
