# Two-center harmonic oscillator basis for Skyrme-DFT calculations (I): formalism and Proof of Principle

> We present a new method to solve the nuclear density functional theory (DFT) equations using a two-center harmonic oscillator for Skyrme-like functionals, incorporating pairing and Coulomb interactions. The goal is to efficiently determine the fission and fusion configurations in nuclei. The Coulomb exchange term is evaluated exactly, allowing for a novel approach to neck formation without the Slater approximation, commonly used in space coordinate-based approaches. The new method has been implemented in the code {\sc hfodd}, enabling direct comparison with standard one-center solutions. This first paper focuses on deriving and implementing a methodology based on stable, precise, and exact applications of harmonic oscillator bases for the two fragments, which can either overlap or be separated by arbitrarily large distances. The implementation is tested on two proof-of-principle examples using light nuclei, specifically, $^8$Be and $^{24}$Mg.

# Two-center harmonic oscillator basis for Skyrme-DFT calculations (I): formalism and Proof of Principle

## Key claim
The two-center harmonic oscillator basis method can solve Skyrme-DFT equations with exact Coulomb exchange (avoiding the Slater approximation) and accurately describe fission and fusion configurations, but the proof-of-principle demonstrations are limited to light nuclei (⁸Be and ²⁴Mg) and exclude pairing correlations, so the method's performance for heavy nuclei and realistic fission paths remains to be established.

## Method
The authors expand single-particle wave functions in a two-center harmonic oscillator (TCHO) basis, where each center has its own deformation, shift, and oscillator parameters. They solve the generalized eigenvalue problem arising from the non-orthogonal basis using Löwdin's canonical orthogonalization, and evaluate all matrix elements via Gauss-Hermite quadrature on five distinct integration lattices. The Coulomb interaction is treated exactly in both direct and exchange channels using a Gaussian expansion of the 1/r potential, with the number of Gaussians adjustable for accuracy at large fragment separations.

## Key numbers
- For ⁸Be ground state with UNEDF1 functional: TCHO energies lie below OCHO energies at all shell numbers; quadrupole moments converge faster and more smoothly with TCHO than OCHO.
- For ⁸Be Coulomb test with SV_T functional: direct Coulomb interaction follows 1/r behavior for separations up to 40 fm with N_C=10 Gaussians; exchange Coulomb energy shows exponential decrease after scission at Δz₀ ≈ 7.5 fm (Q₂₀ ≈ 9 b).
- For ²⁴Mg ground state with SkM* functional: TCHO total ground-state energy is -179.38 MeV versus OCHO -179.48 MeV; quadrupole moment ⟨Q̂₂₀⟩ = 1.17 b for both methods; optimal TCHO center separation is 4 fm, giving energy only 0.75 keV above OCHO result.
- For ²⁴Mg: TCHO calculation used 572 HO states (twice spherical N₀=10 basis) with norm eigenvalue cutoff ζ_cut = 10⁻⁴, discarding a few tens of states.

## Context
This work extends the two-center harmonic oscillator approach previously applied in nuclear physics to Gogny functionals (Berger and Gogny, 1980) and covariant DFT (Li, 2007; Li, 2023), but provides the first implementation for Skyrme energy density functionals with exact Coulomb exchange. The method is implemented in the hfodd code (Dobaczewski, 1997; 2021; 2024), enabling direct comparison with standard one-center solutions. Unlike prior two-center shell model calculations using Wood-Saxon potentials (Scharnweber, 1971; Chandra, 1978) or Green's function methods (Diaz-Torres, 2008), this approach treats the Coulomb interaction exactly and handles overlapping fragments properly. The method enables future studies of fission fragment angular momenta, neck formation, and fragment distributions without the Slater approximation for Coulomb exchange.
