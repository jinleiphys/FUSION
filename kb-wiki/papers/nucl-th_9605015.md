# Isospin dependence of collective flow in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> Within the framework of an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) model using initial proton and neutron densities calculated from the nonlinear relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, we compare the strength of transverse collective flow in reactions $^{48}Ca+^{58}Fe$ and $^{48}Cr+^{58}Ni$, which have the same mass number but different neutron/proton ratios. The neutron-rich system ($^{48}Ca+^{58}Fe$) is found to show significantly stronger negative deflection and consequently has a higher balance energy, especially in peripheral collisions. NOTE ADDED IN PROOF: The new phenomenon predicted in this work has just been confirmed by an experiment done by G.D. Westfall et al. using the NSCL/MSU radioactive beam facility and a spartan soccer. A paper by R. Pak et al. is submitted to PRL to report the experimental result.

# Isospin dependence of collective flow in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

## Key claim
The neutron-rich system (⁴⁸Ca+⁵⁸Fe) shows significantly stronger negative deflection and consequently a higher balance energy compared to the neutron-poor system (⁴⁸Cr+⁵⁸Ni) of the same total mass number, especially in peripheral collisions. This isospin dependence is predicted to be most easily observable at beam energies around and below the balance energy, and the effect disappears at beam energies far above the balance energy.

## Method
The authors used an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with initial proton and neutron densities calculated from the nonlinear relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory using NL-SH force parameters. They compared transverse collective flow in two reaction systems with the same mass number (48+58) but different neutron/proton ratios (1.04 for ⁴⁸Cr+⁵⁸Ni and 1.30 for ⁴⁸Ca+⁵⁸Fe), using a soft equation of state with compressibility K=200 MeV and symmetry potential strength C=32 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The balance energy in ⁴⁸Ca+⁵⁸Fe is higher than in ⁴⁸Cr+⁵⁸Ni by about 10 to 30 MeV/nucleon.
- At impact parameter b=2 fm, the flow parameter F as a function of beam energy E/A follows: F(Ca+Fe) = -32.2 + 0.55E/A and F(Cr+Ni) = -23.9 + 0.48E/A.
- At impact parameter b=5 fm, the flow parameter follows: F(Ca+Fe) = -35.9 + 0.22E/A and F(Cr+Ni) = -23.2 + 0.18E/A.
- At a beam energy of 50 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter 2 fm, the transverse flow in ⁴⁸Ca+⁵⁸Fe is still negative while that in ⁴⁸Cr+⁵⁸Ni is already positive.
- The nuclear compressibility used is K=200 MeV and the symmetry potential strength is C=32 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of collective flow and balance energy in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Klakow (year not specified), Danielewicz (1985), Molitoris and collaborators (various years), Gale and collaborators (1987), Bertsch and collaborators (1987), Tsang and collaborators (various years), Xu and collaborators, Pan and collaborators, Li (1993), Zhang and collaborators) by adding the isospin degree of freedom. The study was motivated by the availability of neutron-rich radioactive beams at facilities like NSCL/MSU (noted by Gary Westfall and collaborators, 1995) and aims to provide a new approach to extract the isospin-dependent part of the nuclear equation of state, which is vital for determining properties of neutron stars (as discussed by Toki, Pethick, and Baym). The prediction was subsequently confirmed experimentally by Westfall et al. using the NSCL/MSU radioactive beam facility.
