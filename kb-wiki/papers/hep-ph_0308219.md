# "Point-form" estimate of the pion form factor revisited

> The pion form factor calculation in the ``point-form'' of relativistic quantum mechanics is re-considered. Particular attention is given to the relation between the momentum of the system and the momentum transfer as well as to the quark current.

## Key claim
The point-form implementation of relativistic quantum mechanics, previously thought to agree well with experimental pion form factor data, actually fails when tested on a simple scalar-constituent model: the calculated form factors decrease too rapidly, especially at high momentum transfer or large binding, and the squared charge radius is an order of magnitude too large.

## Method
The authors re-derived analytic expressions for the pion form factor using Gaussian and Hulthen wave functions, correcting the relation between the Breit-frame pion momentum and the momentum transfer (removing an unnecessary rescaling introduced in earlier work) and including the correct quark current matrix element. They compared the resulting form factors to the earlier point-form results and to the expected asymptotic power law \(Q^{-2}\).

## Key numbers
- The squared charge radius obtained with the corrected point-form calculation has an "incompressible value" of order a few fm\(^2\), which is one order of magnitude larger than the experimental value.
- The analytic Gaussian form factor is given by \(F_1^G(Q^2) = \frac{(1-v^2)^2}{\sqrt{1+v^2}} \exp\left[-\frac{v^2}{1-v^2} \frac{m^2}{b^2}\right]\) with \(v^2 = Q^2/(4M_\pi^2 + Q^2)\).
- The earlier point-form result (with rescaling) is approximated by \(F_1(Q^2) = \frac{1-\tilde{v}^2}{\sqrt{1+\tilde{v}^2}}\) with \(\tilde{v}^2 = Q^2/(16 m_q^2 + Q^2)\).
- The asymptotic behavior of the corrected form factor falls off faster than the expected \(Q^{-2}\) power law, as shown up to \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context
This work extends the critique of the point-form approach first raised in Desplanques (2003) using a scalar-constituent model, and directly contradicts the earlier point-form results of Allen (2000) which appeared to agree with experiment. The authors note that Coester and Riska (2003) successfully reproduced nucleon form factors in the point-nucleon limit, but the same approximation fails for the pion due to its much smaller mass. The results are similar to those obtained in other relativistic forms under non-standard kinematics (Amghar, 2001; Simula, 1995), and the authors suggest that including two-body currents may resolve the problems, particularly the dependence on the ratio \(Q/M_\pi\) and the Goldstone boson nature of the pion.
