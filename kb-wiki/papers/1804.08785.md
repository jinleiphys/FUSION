# A comparative study of Compact Objects using 3 models: Walecka Model, PAL Model, and M.I.T. Bag Model

> Compact objects are the name used to classify the following objects: white dwarf, neutron star and black holes. In addition to them, some authors have suggested that in the core of a neutron star we may have the quarks u,d,s and c. In this work we investigate the structure of neutron star and strange star, described by the Tolman-Openheimer-Volkof equations. For the neutron star, we used the relativistic EoS from Walecka's model and the non-relativistic EoS PAL model. For the strange star, we used the M.I.T. bag model. We obtain results for the mass-radius relation and then we compared ours results with the actual pulsar data recently observed PSR J1614-2230 with a mass $1.97\pm 0.04\ M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim

The Walecka model and PAL model produce neutron star maximum masses (2.65 and 2.25 solar masses respectively) that exceed the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 solar masses), while the M.I.T. bag model for strange stars yields a maximum mass (1.82 solar masses) that does not reach this observed mass barrier, suggesting that a realistic description requires including rotation and strong magnetic fields in the TOV equations.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for static, spherically symmetric compact stars using three different equations of state: the relativistic Walecka model (nucleons coupled to sigma and omega mesons), the non-relativistic PAL model (Prakash, Ainsworth, Lattimer), and the M.I.T. bag model (with up, down, and strange quarks confined by a bag constant). The TOV equations were integrated numerically using the 4th order Runge-Kutta method to obtain mass-radius relations for each model.

## Key numbers

- Walecka model maximum mass: 2.65 solar masses, radius 11.55 km, central density 2.33 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3})
- PAL model maximum mass: 2.25 solar masses, radius 11.92 km, central density 5.70 times nuclear saturation density
- M.I.T. bag model maximum mass: 1.82 solar masses, radius 10.11 km, central density 4.92 times nuclear saturation density
- Observed pulsar PSR J1614-2230 mass: 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses
- Bag constant for M.I.T. model: B^{1/4} = 145 MeV
- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Fermi energy at saturation: E_F^0 = -16 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of compact object structure by comparing three distinct EoS models against the precise mass measurement of PSR J1614-2230 obtained via Shapiro delay. The Walecka model and PAL model both predict maximum masses above the observed pulsar mass, while the M.I.T. bag model for strange quark matter fails to reach 1.97 solar masses. The authors note that future work should incorporate rotation and strong magnetic fields into the TOV equations, as well as consider EoS that agree with all symmetric nuclear matter properties.
