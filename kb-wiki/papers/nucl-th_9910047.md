# (p,π^+-) correlations in central heavy-ion collisions at 1-2 AGeV

> The proton - charged pion correlated emission is studied in the reactions Au (1.06 AGeV) + Au, Ni (1.06 and 1.93 AGeV) + Ni and Ni (1.97 AGeV) + Cu within the BUU approach. The associated invariant mass distributions are shifted to smaller energies with respect to the free $Δ(1232)$ mass distribution due to kinematical reasons. We find that the existing and partly conflicting experimental data do not allow to draw definite conclusions on the in-medium modification of the $Δ(1232)$.

# (p,π^±) correlations in central heavy-ion collisions at 1-2 AGeV

## Key claim

The existing and partly conflicting experimental data on (p,π^±) correlated pairs do not allow to draw definite conclusions on the in-medium modification of the Δ(1232) resonance. The BUU transport calculation with selfconsistent Δ-width and medium-modified matrix elements gives good agreement with (p,π^-) data but overpredicts the peak position of (p,π^+) spectra relative to FOPI data, while agreeing reasonably with EOS-TPC data.

## Method

The authors performed BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport calculations for central collisions of Au+Au at 1.06 AGeV, Ni+Ni at 1.06 and 1.93 AGeV, and Ni+Cu at 1.97 AGeV, using a momentum-dependent mean field with compressibility modulus K=280 MeV and vacuum spectral functions for all resonances. Correlated (p,π^±) pairs were identified as those originating from the same resonance decay where neither the proton nor the pion rescatter, and the invariant mass spectra were extracted both directly and via event-mixing background subtraction. Additional calculations included a selfconsistent Δ-width accounting for Pauli blocking, ΔN→NN absorption, and ΔN→ΔN rescattering, plus a density-dependent matrix element for ΔN→NN.

## Key numbers

- The free Δ(1232) resonance pole mass is 1.232 GeV with a free-space width Γ_Δ = 0.118 GeV
- The peak of the (p,π^-) spectrum in Au+Au central collisions is shifted by about -50 MeV due to Coulomb effects; the (p,π^+) shift is about +20 MeV for the same reaction
- Average freeze-out density ⟨ρ_f.o.⟩ ≈ 0.41 ρ_0 and average freeze-out temperature ⟨T_f.o.⟩ ≈ 39 MeV, with ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- The baryon chemical potential extracted at ρ_B = 0.41 ρ_0 and T = 39 MeV is μ_B = 889 MeV
- Only 1-4 (p,π^-) and 2-6 (p,π^+) correlated pairs per event are produced on average without acceptance cuts; after FOPI CDC acceptance filtering, the number decreases by a factor of 3-4
- The Λ decay contribution creates a narrow peak at invariant mass 1.116 GeV with width σ = 6.4 MeV/c²
- About 25% of emitted pions are in correlations with protons for Au+Au central collisions at 1.06 AGeV

## Context

This work extends the BUU transport studies of Teis (1997) and the earlier work of Cassing, Moser, and collaborators, and directly addresses the experimental measurements of Hjort (1997) for Ni+Cu at 1.97 AGeV and Eskef (1998) and Pelte (1999) for Au+Au and Ni+Ni collisions. The conflicting situation between FOPI and EOS-TPC data on (p,π^+) correlations calls for new experiments. The analysis also compares with the thermodynamic model of WFN (1998) using the B-function derived from πN scattering phase shifts, which improves agreement with FOPI data but worsens agreement with EOS data.
