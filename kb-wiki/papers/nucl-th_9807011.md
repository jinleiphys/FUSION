# Relativistic Quark Physics

> We present a brief survey of the development of nuclear physics towards relativistic quark physics. This is followed by a thorough discussion of the quest for the observation of the dissolution of nuclear matter into the deconfined quark matter (QGP) in relativistic nuclear collisions. Use of strange particle signatures in search for QGP is emphasized.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the dissolution of nuclear matter into deconfined quark-gluon plasma (QGP) may be observable in relativistic nuclear collisions, but it is far from certain that QGP can or will be created in collisions lasting only 10⁻²² s, and the complex aspects of interactions within confined bound states may become irrelevant only in the high density/energy limit.

## Method

The paper presents a historical and theoretical survey of the development from nuclear physics to relativistic quark physics, followed by a detailed discussion of the QCD vacuum structure using the MIT bag model and the statistical bootstrap model of hadronic matter. Strange particle signatures are emphasized as probes for QGP formation, and the author reviews the color-magnetic hyperfine interaction formalism for hadronic mass splittings, applying permutation operator methods to compute chromo-magnetic interaction strengths in baryons.

## Key numbers

- Quark confinement radius: about 1 fm
- Light quark (u,d) masses: 3 to 15 MeV
- Bag constant lower value (fluid flavor vacuum): B_F = 0.1 GeV fm⁻³
- Bag constant upper value (perturbative vacuum): B_P = 1 GeV fm⁻³
- Glue condensate value: ⟨(α_s/π) G²⟩ = (2.3 ± 0.3) × 10⁻² GeV⁴ = [390 ± 12 MeV]⁴
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93.3 MeV
- Charged pion mass: m_π⁺ = 139.6 MeV
- Light quark mass sum at 1 GeV: (m_u + m_d)|₁ GeV = 14.7 ± 0.8 MeV
- Strange quark mass at 1 GeV: m_s|₁ GeV = 195 ± 12 MeV
- Quark condensate at 1 GeV: ½⟨ūu + d̄d⟩|₁ GeV = −[(225 ± 9) MeV]³
- QGP temperature range: kT = 150 to 250 MeV
- Nucleon mass: M_3 = 940 MeV
- Δ-mass: M_3 = 1330 MeV
- Bag model quark eigenenergy: κ = 2.04 (theoretical), κ = 1.97 (fitted)
- Fitted bag constant from hadron spectra: B_b^{1/4} = 170 MeV
- Time after Big Bang for QGP epoch: about 40 μs

## Context

This paper extends the line of work by Hagedorn (1964) on the statistical bootstrap model of hadronic matter and by the MIT bag model group (Chodos et al., 1974) on hadronic structure. It builds on the author's own prior work with Hagedorn (1981) on the phase transition between confined and deconfined matter, and updates developments reported at the previous Rio de Janeiro workshop (Rafelski, 1995). The work enables further experimental searches for QGP in heavy-ion collisions, particularly using strange particle production as a signature, and connects to lattice gauge studies of QCD and QCD sum-rule analyses by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979).
