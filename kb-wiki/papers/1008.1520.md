# Dipole responses in Nd and Sm isotopes with shape transitions

> Photoabsorption cross sections of Nd and Sm isotopes from spherical to deformed even nuclei are systematically investigated by means of the quasiparticle-random-phase approximation based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov ground states (HFB+QRPA) using the Skyrme energy density functional. The gradual onset of deformation in the ground states as increasing the neutron number leads to characteristic features of the shape phase transition. The calculation well reproduce the isotopic dependence of broadening and emergence of a double-peak structure in the cross sections without any adjustable parameter. We also find that the deformation plays a significant role for low-energy dipole strengths. The $E1$ strengths are fragmented and considerably lowered in energy. The summed $E1$ strength up to 10 MeV is enhanced by a factor of five or more.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the isotopic dependence of broadening and double-peak structure in photoabsorption cross sections of Nd and Sm isotopes can be reproduced without any adjustable parameter by the HFB+QRPA method, but this agreement relies on a smearing width of 2 MeV to simulate the missing spreading effect, and the calculation cannot fully reproduce the width increase from spherical to transitional nuclei when the HFB calculation produces a spherical ground state.

## Method

The authors performed systematic HFB+QRPA calculations using the Skyrme energy density functional (SkM*, SLy4, and SkP) for Nd and Sm isotopes from spherical to deformed shapes. They solved the HFB equations in coordinate space using cylindrical coordinates with a mesh size of 0.6 fm and a box boundary condition at (14.7, 14.4) fm, assuming axial and reflection symmetries. The QRPA matrix was constructed with a two-quasiparticle energy cutoff of 60 MeV, and the photoabsorption cross section was calculated with a smearing width of 2 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The GDR width for N=82 and 84 is approximately 4.5 MeV, gradually increasing to about 6 MeV for N=88, with splitting becoming visible for N greater than or equal to 90.
- The summed B(E1) strength up to 10 MeV in deformed nuclei is approximately 1.5 e^2 fm^2, about five times larger than in spherical nuclei.
- The summed B(E1) strength up to 10 MeV in spherical 144Sm is 0.27 e^2 fm^2, which reasonably agrees with the experimental value of 0.20 e^2 fm^2.
- The lowest K=0- state energies in 150Nd, 152Nd, 152Sm, and 154Sm are 0.97, 1.40, 1.10, and 1.37 MeV respectively; the lowest K=1- state energies are 1.80, 1.93, 1.60, and 1.49 MeV respectively.
- The QRPA calculation for 154Sm involved about 38,000 two-quasiparticle states for K=0- excitation, requiring about 390 CPU hours for matrix elements and 135 CPU hours for diagonalization.

## Context

This work extends previous separable QRPA calculations with Skyrme EDF by Neshev et al. (2006, 2008) which produced significantly larger second peaks for deformed nuclei that disagreed with experiment, and improves upon the full QRPA approach by including the residual spin-orbit interaction. The calculations enable systematic study of both high-energy giant resonances and low-energy dipole states (including the pygmy dipole resonance) across the shape phase transition, with implications for astrophysical r-process nucleosynthesis as discussed by Goriely (2002). The authors note that inclusion of higher-order mode-mode coupling beyond QRPA is needed for further improvements, particularly for spherical nuclei where the low-energy E1 strengths are calculated too high in energy.
