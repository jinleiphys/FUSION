# Is Relativistic Hydrodynamics always Symmetric-Hyperbolic in the Linear Regime?

> Close to equilibrium, the kinetic coefficients of a thermodynamic system must satisfy a set of symmetry conditions, which follow from the Onsager-Casimir principle. Here, we show that, if a system of hydrodynamic equations is analysed from the perspective of the Onsager-Casimir principle, then it is possible to impose very strong symmetry conditions also on the principal part of such equations (the part with highest derivatives). In particular, we find that, in the absence of macroscopic magnetic fields and spins, relativistic hydrodynamics should always be symmetric-hyperbolic, when linearised about equilibrium. We use these results to prove that Carter's multifluid theory and the Israel-Stewart theory in the pressure frame are both symmetric-hyperbolic in the linear regime. Connections with the GENERIC formalism are also explored.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, when linearised about equilibrium and in the absence of macroscopic magnetic fields and spins, relativistic hydrodynamics should always be symmetric-hyperbolic as a direct consequence of the Onsager-Casimir principle, rather than being an accidental mathematical property. The hedge is that this conclusion applies only to the linear regime and only when the system is analysed through the Onsager-Casimir framework, which imposes symmetry conditions on the principal part of the hydrodynamic equations.

## Method

The author decomposes the hydrodynamic fields into Fourier modes, converting the partial differential equations into a system of ordinary differential equations for the sine and cosine coefficients of each mode. The Onsager-Casimir principle is then applied to these ordinary differential equations in their canonical thermodynamic form, where the entropy perturbation matrix S_ab and the kinetic matrix L_ab are identified. The symmetry conditions from the Onsager-Casimir principle are shown to enforce the symmetric-hyperbolic structure of the principal part of the equations, and this is verified explicitly for the Israel-Stewart theory in 1+1 dimensions and for Carter's multifluid theory.

## Key numbers

- The Israel-Stewart theory in 1+1 dimensions (Eckart frame, no shear viscosity) is shown to be symmetric-hyperbolic when linearised, with the information current given by T E^0 = 1/2 [beta n_alpha (T delta alpha)^2 + 2 beta rho_alpha (T delta alpha)(beta delta T) + T rho_T (beta delta T)^2 + b_0 (delta Pi)^2 + w (delta u)^2 + 2 delta u delta q + b_1 (delta q)^2].
- The coupling coefficient a_0, which appears both in the bulk viscosity equation and the heat flux equation, is shown to be symmetric under the Onsager-Casimir principle, consistent with the reciprocal relations verified in kinetic theory by Liu (1986).
- The toy model in 1+1 dimensions with fields delta rho and delta u yields the condition sigma = +1 from the Onsager-Casimir principle, selecting the wave equation (Model 1) over the elliptic Laplace equation (Model 2).

## Context

This work extends the connection between the Onsager-Casimir principle and symmetric hyperbolicity that was previously explored for divergence-type theories by Geroch and Lindblom (1990) and for the Israel-Stewart theory in specific frames by Hiscock (1983) and Olson and Lifshitz (1990). The paper proves that Carter's multifluid theory and the Israel-Stewart theory in the pressure frame are both symmetric-hyperbolic in the linear regime, and it explores connections with the GENERIC formalism. This enables a deeper understanding of why most relativistic hydrodynamic theories with strictly non-negative entropy production are well-posed in the linear regime, and it provides a physical foundation for the mathematical structure of these theories based on time-reversal symmetry.
