# Nonmesonic Weak Decay Spectra and Three-Nucleon Emission

> We have evaluated the nonmesonic weak decay spectra within the independent-particle shell-model, and compared them with the recent measurements of: i) the single and double coincidence nucleon spectra in ${\mathrm{^{12}_ΛC}}$ performed at KEK, and ii) proton kinetic energy spectra in ${\mathrm{^{5}_ΛHe}}$, ${\mathrm{^{7}_ΛLi}}$, ${\mathrm{^{9}_ΛBe}}$, ${\mathrm{^{11}_ΛB}}$, ${\mathrm{^{12}_ΛC}}$, ${\mathrm{^{13}_ΛC}}$, ${\mathrm{^{15}_ΛN}}$ and ${\mathrm{^{16}_ΛO}}$ done by FINUDA. Based on this comparison we argue that the extraction from the data of the three-body $ΛNN \rightarrow nNN$ induced decay rate, as done in these works, could be questionable.

## Key claim
The authors argue that the extraction of the three-body ΛNN → nNN induced decay rate from KEK and FINUDA data, which both reported a branching ratio Γ₂/ΓNM ∼ 25–30%, is questionable because the theoretical overestimation of the data can be explained by uncertainties in the one-meson-exchange model and short-range correlations, without invoking the two-nucleon induced decay channel.

## Method
The authors performed independent-particle shell-model (IPSM) calculations for the one-nucleon induced nonmesonic weak decay (1N-NM) using two one-meson-exchange (OME) potentials: M1 with only π+K exchange, and M2 with full π+K+η+ρ+ω+K* exchange. They compared the calculated single-nucleon kinetic energy spectra, opening angle correlations, and center-of-mass momentum spectra with KEK data for ¹²ΛC, and proton kinetic energy spectra with FINUDA data for eight hypernuclei (⁵ΛHe, ⁷ΛLi, ⁹ΛBe, ¹¹ΛB, ¹²ΛC, ¹³ΛC, ¹⁵ΛN, and ¹⁶ΛO). The analysis used the spectroscopic factors and kinematics from the IPSM, with short-range correlations and finite nucleon size effects included as in prior work.

## Key numbers
- For ¹²ΛC, the experimental total neutron transition rate Γnt from KEK is 0.823 ± 0.016, while the IPSM with M1 gives 1.455 and with M2 gives 1.789 (Table 1).
- For ¹²ΛC, the experimental proton transition rate Γp from KEK is 0.419 ± 0.013, while the IPSM with M1 gives 0.627 and with M2 gives 0.792 (Table 1).
- For ⁵ΛHe, the FINUDA experimental Γp is 0.284, while the IPSM with M1 gives 0.466 and with M2 gives 0.360 (Table 2).
- For ¹⁶ΛO, the FINUDA experimental Γp is 0.320, while the IPSM with M1 gives 0.906 and with M2 gives 0.718 (Table 2).
- The FINUDA Gaussian fit mean peak energy for ¹²ΛC is 80.2 ± 2.1 MeV, while the IPSM calculated maximum is 67.5 MeV (Table 2).
- The KEK and FINUDA groups both reported a branching ratio Γ₂/ΓNM ∼ 25–30%, but the authors find that the FINUDA χ² fit yields Γ₂/ΓNM = 0.24 ± 0.10 in case A, 0.26 ± 0.10 in case B, and −0.40 ± 0.10 in case C (Table 3).

## Context
This work extends the independent-particle shell-model calculations for nonmesonic weak decay previously developed by the same group (Ba09, Kr10, Go11) and confronts them with the KEK experiment on ¹²ΛC (Ki09) and the FINUDA experiment on multiple hypernuclei (Ag10). The authors challenge the interpretation of both experimental groups, who attributed the discrepancy between their data and calculations to the two-nucleon induced decay channel, following the prediction by Alberico (1991) and the Fermi Gas Model calculations by Bauer and Garbarino (2009, 2011). The authors note that a previous upper limit from BNL on ⁴ΛHe (Pa07) gave Γ₂/ΓW ≤ 0.097 (95% CL), much smaller than the KEK and FINUDA claims. This work enables future development of an IPSM formalism for the three-nucleon emission channel (Ba11) and calls for triple coincidence measurements to directly measure Γ₂.
