# The Pervasive Role of the Nuclear Symmetry Energy in the Structure and Evolution of Neutron Stars

> The multifaceted role of the density dependent nuclear symmetry energy in the nuclear astrophysics involving neutron stars is highlighted. Efforts toward a model independent determination of the dense matter equation state through a deconstruction of the neutron star structure equation utilizing the masses and radii of several individual neutron stars are described. The need for observational data of both measurements for the same star is stressed.

## Key claim

The density-dependent nuclear symmetry energy pervasively influences neutron star structure and evolution, and the masses and radii of several individual neutron stars can be used to uniquely determine the dense matter equation of state through an inversion procedure, provided both quantities are measured for the same star.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations using a variable \(h\) defined through \(dh = dp/(p+\rho(p))\), where \(p\) is pressure and \(\rho\) is mass-energy density. They then apply two iterative schemes, one based on a modified Lindblom procedure and another using a Newton-Raphson approach, to reconstruct the equation of state from assumed mass and radius values for individual neutron stars. The methods are tested on both polytropic and physically motivated nucleonic equations of state (e.g., PAL31).

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium nuclear density is \(n_0 \simeq 0.16 \pm 0.01\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- The simple mean of observed neutron star masses in x-ray binaries is \(1.55\ M_\odot\); the error-weighted mean is \(1.37\ M_\odot\).
- For double neutron star binaries, the simple mean mass is \(1.32\ M_\odot\) and the error-weighted mean is \(1.41\ M_\odot\).
- For white dwarf–neutron star binaries, the simple mean mass is \(1.60\ M_\odot\) and the error-weighted mean is \(1.33\ M_\odot\).
- The symmetry energy at nuclear density \(n_0\) for the PAL31 equation of state is 30 MeV, with a nuclear matter compression modulus \(K = 240\) MeV.
- The two iterative reconstruction schemes reproduce the exact equation of state to hundredths of percent accuracy for test cases.

## Context

This work extends the inversion procedure originally proposed by Lindblom (1992) for reconstructing the dense matter equation of state from neutron star mass-radius observations. It builds on correlations established by Lattimer and Prakash (2001) between neutron star radius and the pressure of beta-equilibrated matter near nuclear density, and on the Typel-Brown (2001) and Horowitz-Piekarewicz (2001) correlations linking neutron skin thickness in nuclei to neutron star properties. The results enable future observational campaigns, such as those described by Rutledge (2008), to uniquely determine the high-density equation of state from measurements of 5 to 7 individual neutron stars.
