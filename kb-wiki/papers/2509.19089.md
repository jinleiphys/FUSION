# Improved initial colliding nuclei density profile method for QMD-type transport models

> Accurate modeling of the initial density profile is essential for studying heavy-ion collisions (HICs) with a transport model. Within the framework of the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD)-type model, a novel method for generating nuclear density distributions based on a Fourier series expansion (FSE) is proposed. In this approach, the objective density distribution is expanded into a Fourier series to construct a filter function, which is then applied to select the randomly sampled nucleon coordinates in phase space to generate a three-dimensional nuclear density distribution that matches the desired profile. This new initialization method is further incorporated into the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, and the bubble-like density distribution of $^{96}$Ru is constructed, showing good stability. Then, by simulating $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru collisions at $E_\mathrm{lab}=1500$ MeV/nucleon with different equations of state (EoS) and initialization methods, the effects of the initialization method on the final state observables and the constrained information of EoS are analyzed. It is found that the maximum system density increases when the new FSE initialization method is adopted, and results in an enhanced collective flow. Moreover, a relatively stiff EoS with $K_0>280$ MeV is favored when adopting the Woods-Saxon initialization method, whereas an EoS of $K_0$ = 200-280 MeV is supported when using the FSE initialization. These results indicate that, within QMD-like transport models, the FSE filtering method provides a reliable means to sample nuclei with exotic density profiles, offering new insight to investigate nuclear structure and dense nuclear matter EoS through HICs.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a Fourier series expansion (FSE) filtering method for generating initial nuclear density distributions in QMD-type transport models, but the method's advantage over the standard Woods-Saxon initialization is conditional: the inferred stiffness of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) depends on which initialization method is used, with the FSE method favoring a softer EoS (K0 = 200-280 MeV) while the Woods-Saxon method favors a stiffer EoS (K0 > 280 MeV) when comparing to the same FOPI experimental data.

## Method

The authors expand the target nuclear density distribution into a Fourier cosine series, then construct a filtering function by multiplying each Fourier coefficient by an exponential factor exp(c_j^2 * sigma_r^2 / 2) that accounts for the Gaussian wave-packet width in the UrQMD model. This filtering function is used to accept or reject randomly sampled nucleon coordinates via Monte Carlo rejection sampling, generating a three-dimensional density distribution that matches the desired profile. The method is implemented in the UrQMD model and tested by simulating semi-central 96Ru+96Ru collisions at Elab = 1500 MeV/nucleon with both soft (K0 = 200 MeV) and medium-hard (K0 = 280 MeV) momentum-dependent equations of state.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: Elab = 1500 MeV/nucleon for 96Ru+96Ru collisions
- Fourier series truncated at 20 terms with l = 16 fm and radial sampling interval Delta r = 0.02 fm over r in [0, 16] fm (n = 800 grid points)
- Soft EoS (SM) with incompressibility K0 = 200 MeV; medium-hard EoS (mHM) with K0 = 280 MeV
- At 10 fm/c, the RMS radius of the FSE-initialized nucleus increases by about 1.8% while the WS-initialized nucleus increases by 1.1%
- The complete geometrical overlap time of the colliding nuclei is approximately 6.2 fm/c
- A relatively stiff EoS with K0 > 280 MeV is favored when adopting the Woods-Saxon initialization method, whereas an EoS of K0 = 200-280 MeV is supported when using the FSE initialization

## Context

This work extends the line of research on nuclear equation of state constraints from heavy-ion collisions using transport models, specifically addressing the sensitivity of inferred EoS stiffness to the initial nuclear density profile. The authors build on prior work by Li (2019) who calculated bubble-like density distributions for 96Ru using extended Skyrme-Hartree-Fock methods, and on the FOPI Collaboration (2011) who provided experimental collective flow data for 96Ru+96Ru collisions. The new FSE filtering method enables QMD-type models to incorporate exotic nuclear density profiles (such as bubble structures) that deviate from the standard Woods-Saxon distribution, allowing future studies to investigate nuclear structure effects and dense nuclear matter EoS simultaneously through heavy-ion collisions.
