# Structure functions in the polarized Drell-Yan processes with spin-1/2 and spin-1 hadrons: II. parton model

> We analyze the polarized Drell-Yan processes with spin-1/2 and spin-1 hadrons in a parton model. Quark and antiquark correlation functions are expressed in terms of possible combinations of Lorentz vectors and pseudovectors with the constrains of Hermiticity, parity conservation, and time-reversal invariance. Then, we find tensor polarized distributions for a spin-1 hadron. The naive parton model predicts that there exist 19 structure functions. However, there are only four or five non-vanishing structure functions, depending on whether the cross section is integrated over the virtual-photon transverse momentum \vec Q_T or the limit Q_T->0 is taken. One of the finite structure functions is related to the tensor polarized distribution b_1, and it does not exist in the proton-proton reactions. The vanishing structure functions should be associated with higher-twist physics. The tensor distributions can be measured by the quadrupole polarization measurements. The Drell-Yan process has an advantage over the lepton reaction in the sense that the antiquark tensor polarization could be extracted rather easily.

## Key claim

The naive parton model predicts 19 structure functions for the polarized Drell-Yan process with spin-1/2 and spin-1 hadrons, but only four or five are non-vanishing at leading twist, depending on whether the cross section is integrated over the virtual-photon transverse momentum \(\vec Q_T\) or the limit \(Q_T \to 0\) is taken; the vanishing structure functions should be associated with higher-twist physics.

## Method

The authors analyzed the Drell-Yan process \(A\) (spin-1/2) + \(B\) (spin-1) \(\to \ell^+ \ell^- + X\) in a parton model by expressing quark and antiquark correlation functions in terms of Lorentz vectors and pseudovectors under constraints of Hermiticity, parity conservation, and time-reversal invariance. They derived the hadron tensor using a Fierz transformation to factorize the quark-hadron amplitudes, then identified the parton distributions contributing to the structure functions, including the tensor polarized distribution \(b_1\) and a new distribution \(c_1\) for spin-1 hadrons. The cross section was obtained by integrating over the transverse momentum of the virtual photon or taking the limit \(Q_T \to 0\).

## Key numbers

* The naive parton model predicts 19 structure functions for the polarized Drell-Yan process with spin-1/2 and spin-1 hadrons.
* After integrating over \(\vec Q_T\) or taking the limit \(Q_T \to 0\), only four or five structure functions are non-vanishing at leading twist.
* The hadron tensor \(W^{\mu\nu}\) contains 108 structure functions in general, which reduces to 22 after integration over \(\vec Q_T\) or in the limit \(Q_T \to 0\).
* The tensor distribution \(b_1(x)\) is defined by \(b_1(x) = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \mathcal{P}(x)_{\lambda=0} - \frac{\mathcal{P}(x)_{\lambda=+1} + \mathcal{P}(x)_{\lambda=-1}}{2} \right]\), where \(\lambda\) is the helicity of the spin-1 hadron.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Ralston-Soper (1979), Donohue-Gottlieb (1981), and Tangerman-Mulders (1995) for spin-1/2 hadrons to include spin-1 hadrons in the Drell-Yan process, building on the authors' previous general formalism (Hino and Kumano, 1999) that identified 108 structure functions. The paper enables future measurements of tensor polarized distributions, particularly \(b_1\), via quadrupole polarization measurements in Drell-Yan experiments, which have an advantage over lepton reactions in extracting antiquark tensor polarization. The results are relevant for planned spin-1 deuteron acceleration at RHIC and other facilities.
