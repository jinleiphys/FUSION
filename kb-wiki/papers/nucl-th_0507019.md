# Influence of Bottom Quark Jet Quenching on Single Electron Tomography of Au+Au

> High transverse momentum single (non-photonic) electrons are shown to be sensitive to the stopping power of both bottom, b, and charm, c, quarks in AA collisions. We apply the DGLV theory of radiative energy loss to predict c and b quark jet quenching and compare the FONLL and PYTHIA heavy flavor fragmentation and decay schemes. We show that single electrons in the p_T=5-10 GeV range are dominated by the decay of b quarks rather than the more strongly quenched c quarks in Au+Au collisions at sqrt{s}=200 AGeV. The {smaller} b quark energy loss, even for extreme opacities with gluon rapidity densities up to 3500, is predicted to limit the nuclear modification factor, R_{AA}, of single electrons to the range R_{AA} ~ 0.5-0.6, in contrast to previous predictions of R_{AA} ~ 0.2-0.3 based on taking only c quark jet fragmentation into account.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that, within the DGLV theory of radiative energy loss, the nuclear modification factor R_AA of single electrons in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV is limited to the range 0.5-0.6, even for extreme opacities with gluon rapidity densities up to 3500, because bottom quark decays dominate the electron spectrum in the p_T=5-10 GeV range and bottom quarks experience smaller energy loss than charm quarks. This contrasts with previous predictions of R_AA ~ 0.2-0.3 that considered only charm quark jet fragmentation.

## Method

The authors compute the single electron spectrum by convolving next-to-leading order perturbative QCD heavy quark production cross sections (using the MNR code with CTEQ6M parton densities) with the DGLV generalization of the GLV opacity expansion for heavy quark radiative energy loss, heavy quark fragmentation functions (from e+e- data and Peterson fragmentation), and measured semi-leptonic decay spectra of D and B mesons. The medium is modeled as a uniform cylinder undergoing Bjorken 1+1D expansion with an effective static medium approximation, using gluon rapidity densities dN_g/dy of 1000 and 3500 to represent default and extreme opacities.

## Key numbers

- Bottom quark mass m_b = 4.75 GeV; charm quark mass m_c = 1.2 GeV
- For dN_g/dy=1000, the crossover where bottom quarks dominate over charm quarks in the quenched quark distributions occurs at p_T ~ 9 GeV; for dN_g/dy=3500, the crossover shifts to p_T ~ 7 GeV
- For the electron decay distributions without quenching, c->e and b->e cross at p_T ~ 5.5 GeV; for dN_g/dy=3500, the crossover reduces to p_T ~ 3 GeV
- The predicted R_AA for single electrons from combined c+b decays satisfies R_AA(e) > 0.5 for p_T < 6 GeV, even at the highest opacity of dN_g/dy=3500
- For a charm quark with p_T ~ 12 GeV, R_AA(c) is predicted to be approximately 0.25-0.5 for dN_g/dy between 1000 and 3500
- Effective branching ratios used: B(B->e) = 10.86%, B(D->e) = 10.3%, B(B->D->e) = 9.6%

## Context

This work extends the DGLV theory of radiative energy loss (Djordjevic and Gyulassy, 2004) to predict the combined contribution of both charm and bottom quark jets to single electron suppression in heavy-ion collisions. It contradicts earlier estimates that considered only charm quark fragmentation and predicted R_AA ~ 0.2-0.3, and it addresses the "fragility" of light hadron tomography pointed out by Eskola et al. (2005). The paper enables future experimental tests at RHIC to distinguish between radiative energy loss and alternative mechanisms such as anomalously large elastic cross sections proposed by Molnar (2005) and Moore and Teaney (2005), noting that if preliminary PHENIX data suggesting R_AA(e) < 0.5 are confirmed, novel energy loss mechanisms beyond radiative energy loss would be required.
