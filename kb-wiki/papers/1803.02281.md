# High Precision Statistical Landau Gauge Lattice Gluon Propagator Computation vs. the Gribov-Zwanziger approach

> The compatibility of the results from the Gribov-Zwanziger tree level prediction and lattice simulations, using large statistical ensembles, for the Landau gauge gluon propagator are investigated, thereby complementing earlier work using small-scale statistics. Our results show that the data is well described by the tree level estimate only up to momenta $p \lesssim 1$ GeV while clearly favoring the so-called Refined Gribov-Zwanziger scenario, implying particular relations between certain possible $d=2$ condensates. We also provide a global fit of the lattice data which interpolates between the above scenario at low momenta and the usual continuum one-loop renormalization improved perturbation theory after introducing an infrared log-regularizing term.

## Key claim

The lattice data for the Landau gauge gluon propagator is well described by the tree level Refined Gribov-Zwanziger (RGZ) prediction only up to momenta p less than about 1 GeV, and the Very Refined Gribov-Zwanziger (VRGZ) scenario reduces to the RGZ form in this infrared region, implying a particular relation between certain d=2 condensates.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations for SU(3) gauge theory in four dimensions using two large ensembles: a 64^4 lattice with 2000 gauge configurations and an 80^4 lattice with 550 gauge configurations, both at beta = 6.0 (lattice spacing a = 0.1016 fm). The gluon propagator was computed in Landau gauge and renormalized in the MOM scheme at mu = 3 GeV. The lattice data were fitted to the tree level RGZ and VRGZ functional forms using a chi-squared minimization procedure that accounts for correlations between different momenta via the covariance matrix.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1016(25) fm, corresponding to 1/a = 1.943 GeV
- Physical volumes: (6.57 fm)^4 for the 64^4 lattice and (8.21 fm)^4 for the 80^4 lattice
- Smallest non-vanishing continuum momentum: 191 MeV for the 64^4 lattice, 153 MeV for the 80^4 lattice
- Largest physical continuum momentum considered: about 7.7 GeV
- For the 64^4 lattice with Z=1 and p_max = 1.0 GeV: M^2_1 = 2.521(28) GeV^2, M^2_2 = 0.5082(90) GeV^2, M^4_3 = 0.2795(27) GeV^4
- For the 80^4 lattice with Z=1 and p_max = 1.1 GeV: M^2_1 = 2.580(64) GeV^2, M^2_2 = 0.522(14) GeV^2, M^4_3 = 0.2845(43) GeV^4
- The RGZ functional form fits the 64^4 lattice data with chi-squared/d.o.f. less than 2 only up to p_max approximately 1 GeV
- The renormalization constants for the two lattices differ by 0.068 percent

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Dudal et al. (2010) and Cucchieri et al. (2011) that used smaller statistical ensembles (O(100) gauge configurations) to test compatibility between lattice gluon propagator data and Gribov-Zwanziger tree level predictions. The high-statistics analysis shows that the RGZ description is limited to momenta below about 1 GeV, whereas previous lower-statistics studies suggested agreement up to much higher momenta (approximately 6 GeV). The results enable future work on global fits that interpolate between the RGZ infrared behavior and one-loop renormalization group improved perturbation theory in the ultraviolet, using an infrared log-regularizing term.
