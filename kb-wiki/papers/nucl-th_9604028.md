# Self-energy of Lambda in finite nuclei

> The self--energy of the strange baryon $Λ$ in $^{17}$O is calculated using a microscopic many--body approach which accounts for correlations beyond the mean--field or Hartree--Fock approximation. The non-locality and energy-dependence of the $Λ$ self--energy is discussed and the effects on the bound and scattering states are investigated. For the nucleon--hyperon interaction, we use the potential models of the Jülich and Nijmegen groups.

## Key claim

The self-energy of the Lambda in O-17 is calculated using a microscopic many-body approach that includes correlations beyond the mean-field approximation, but the results depend strongly on which YN potential model (Juelich or Nijmegen) is used, and the finite-nucleus calculation yields a substantially less attractive optical potential than previous local-density approximation calculations.

## Method

The authors start from a nuclear matter G-matrix for the YN interaction at fixed Fermi momentum (1.4 fm^-1) and starting energy (-80 MeV), then calculate the G-matrix for the finite nucleus O-17 including second-order corrections that use harmonic oscillator states for occupied hole states and plane waves for intermediate unbound particle states. The Lambda self-energy is constructed from a Hartree-Fock term plus a 2p1h (two-particle-one-hole) dispersive contribution, with a correction term subtracted to avoid double-counting of intermediate YN states already included in the nuclear matter G-matrix. The Schrodinger equation is solved by diagonalizing the single-particle Hamiltonian in a spherical box basis of radius 15 fm or larger, with a self-consistent process where the self-energy is evaluated at the energy of each resulting eigenvalue.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter Lambda binding energy at saturation density (k_F = 1.4 fm^-1): -24.35 MeV for Nijmegen potential, -31.48 MeV for Juelich potential
- Woods-Saxon parameters reproducing the Hartree-Fock bound state: V_0 = -22.40 MeV, R = 3.15 fm, a = 0.6 fm
- Effective k-mass from Hartree-Fock non-locality: m_k,Lambda / m_Lambda = 0.8
- Total effective mass m*/m ranges from about 0.7 at low energies to 0.9 at energies above 50 MeV
- At omega = 40 MeV, the real optical potential depth is about -9 MeV (compared to -25 MeV in Ref. Bando2)
- At 40 MeV, the imaginary optical potential depth is about -0.2 MeV (compared to -2.4 MeV in Ref. Bando2)
- At 105 MeV, the imaginary part shows a center-peaked shape

## Context

This work extends the nuclear matter G-matrix calculations of Reuber et al. and Yamamoto et al. to finite nuclei, eliminating the need to choose an effective Fermi momentum for each single-particle state as done in earlier shell-model calculations by Bando and Yamamoto. The results differ substantially from the local density approximation calculations of Bando2, giving a much shallower Lambda-nucleus optical potential, and the authors conclude that microscopic calculations of the effective interaction must be combined with a reliable treatment of the finite hypernucleus. The approach enables study of both bound and scattering states of the Lambda, which will be useful for analyzing the (gamma,K+) reaction at CEBAF and (pi+,K+) reaction at KEK where quasifree hyperon production occurs.
