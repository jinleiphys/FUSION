# Characteristic form of boost-invariant and cylindrically non-symmetric hydrodynamic equations

> It is shown that the boost-invariant and cylindrically non-symmetric hydrodynamic equations for baryon-free matter may be reduced to only two coupled differential equations. In the case where the system exhibits the cross-over phase transition, the standard numerical methods may be applied to solve these equations and the proposed scheme allows for a very convenient analysis of the cylindrically non-symmetric hydrodynamic expansion.

## Key claim
The authors show that boost-invariant and cylindrically non-symmetric relativistic hydrodynamic equations for baryon-free matter can be reduced to only two coupled differential equations, provided the sound velocity satisfies the stability condition against shock formation (Equation \ref{stab1}). The abstract glosses over the condition that the method requires the sound velocity function to have a positive derivative to naturally fulfill the shock-stability criterion.

## Method
The authors start from the perfect-fluid relativistic hydrodynamic equations for baryon-free matter under boost invariance, expressed in polar coordinates (t, r, φ) in the transverse plane. They introduce the substitutions v = tanh θ and a± = exp(Φ ± θ), where Φ is a potential derived from the temperature-dependent sound velocity cs(T), to transform the three original equations into two coupled equations for the functions a(t,r,φ) and α(t,r,φ). The system is closed by using a temperature-dependent sound velocity profile obtained by matching lattice QCD results with a hadron resonance gas model, with the critical temperature Tc = 170 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature Tc = 170 MeV used for the sound velocity profile
- Initial time t0 = 1 fm for the start of hydrodynamic evolution
- Initial central temperature T0 = T(t0,0,0) = 2 Tc
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section σin = 40 mb used in the participant nucleon calculation
- Initial transverse flow parameter H0 = 0.001 fm⁻¹
- Impact parameters used: b = 3.9 fm (centrality 0-20%), b = 7.1 fm (centrality 20-40%), and b = 9.2 fm (centrality 40-60%)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for gold nucleus: ρ0 = 0.17 fm⁻³, a = 0.54 fm, A = 197

## Context
This work directly generalizes the approach of Baym, Friman, Blaizot, Soyeur, and Czyz (1983), who treated boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric systems with constant sound velocity cs² = 1/3. The authors previously extended that work to include temperature-dependent sound velocity and non-zero initial transverse flow (Chojnacki and Florkowski, 2004). The present formalism enables convenient numerical analysis of non-symmetric hydrodynamic expansion in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and future LHC experiments, and the authors plan to combine it with the statistical Monte-Carlo model THERMINATOR to calculate elliptic-flow coefficients v₂.
