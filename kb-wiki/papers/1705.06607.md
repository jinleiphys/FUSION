# Superfluid Gap in Neutron Matter from a Microscopic Effective Interaction

> Correlated Basis Function (CBF) perturbation theory and the formalism of cluster expansions have been recently employed to obtain an effective interaction from a nuclear Hamiltonian strongly constrained by phenomenology. We report the results of a study of the superfluid gap in pure neutron matter, associated with the formation of Cooper pairs in the $^1S_0$ channel. The calculations have been carried out using an improved version of the CBF effective interaction, in which three-nucleon forces are taken into account using a microscopic model. Our results show that a non-vanishing superfluid gap develops at densities in the range $2 \times 10^{-4} \lesssim ρ/ρ_0 \lesssim 0.1 $, where $ρ_0 = 2.8 \times 10^{14}$ g cm$^{-3}$ is the equilibrium density of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, corresponding mainly to the neutron star inner crust.

## Key claim

A non-vanishing superfluid gap in pure neutron matter, associated with Cooper pairing in the \(^1S_0\) channel, develops at densities in the range \(2 \times 10^{-4} \lesssim \rho/\rho_0 \lesssim 0.1\) (where \(\rho_0 = 2.8 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\)), corresponding mainly to the neutron star inner crust; the inclusion of interaction and correlation effects via the Correlated Basis Function (CBF) effective interaction leads to a significant reduction of the gap compared to calculations using the bare nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Method

The superfluid gap equation for S-wave pairing in cold pure neutron matter was solved using the algorithm of Khodel (1996), with momentum-space matrix elements of the potential computed from the CBF effective interaction derived by Lovato et al. (2011, 2013). This effective interaction was obtained from a nuclear Hamiltonian comprising the Argonne \(v_6^\prime\) two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential, using Correlated Basis Function perturbation theory and cluster expansions including three-body cluster contributions. The single-particle spectrum was computed consistently at first order in the CBF effective interaction (Hartree-Fock approximation).

## Key numbers

* The superfluid gap is nonzero in the density range \(2 \times 10^{-4} \lesssim \rho/\rho_0 \lesssim 0.1\), where \(\rho_0 = 2.8 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) is the equilibrium density of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter.
* The maximum critical temperature of the superfluid transition, estimated from the zero-temperature gap, is in the range \(T_c \sim 1-2\) MeV, corresponding to \(\sim 1-2 \times 10^{10}\) K.
* The density range extends from the neutron drip density \(\rho_{ND} \approx 4 \times 10^{11}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) to \(\rho \approx 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the CBF effective interaction approach, previously applied to transport coefficients and neutrino interactions by Benhar et al. (2007, 2010) and Lovato et al. (2011, 2013), to the calculation of the superfluid gap. The results show that using the CBF effective interaction with a Hartree-Fock spectrum yields a significantly reduced gap compared to calculations with the bare \(v_6^\prime\) potential and kinetic energy spectrum, while the range of Fermi momentum where the gap is nonzero remains about the same. This work enables future consistent calculations of neutron superfluidity in the \(^3P_2\) channel and proton superconductivity in neutron star cores, which are needed to study dissipative processes affecting rotating star stability.
