# Light Meson Spectroscopy

> We survey the current status of light meson spectroscopy. We begin with a general introduction to meson spectroscopy and and its importance in understanding the physical states of Quantum Chromo Dynamics (QCD). Phemenological models of hadron spectroscopy are described with particular emphasis on the constituent quark model and the qualitative features it predicts for the meson spectrum. We next discuss expectations for hadrons lying outside the quark model, such as hadron states with excited gluonic degrees of freedom. These states include so-called hybrids glueballs}, as well as multiquark states. The established meson states are compared to the quark model predictions and we find that most meson states are well described by the quark model. However, a number of states in the light-quark sector do not fit in well, suggesting the existence of hadronic states with additional degrees of freedom. We end with a brief description of future directions in meson spectroscopy.

## Key claim

Most observed light meson states are well described by the constituent quark model, but a number of states in the light-quark sector do not fit in well, suggesting the existence of hadronic states with additional degrees of freedom such as glueballs, hybrids, or multiquark states. However, as of this 1998 review, no observed state has unambiguously been identified as an exotic hadron.

## Method

The authors survey the current status of light meson spectroscopy by comparing the predictions of a specific "relativized" quark model (Godfrey, 1985a, 1985c, 1986) against the established meson states. They describe the theoretical framework of QCD, the constituent quark model, and expectations for hadrons lying outside the quark model (hybrids, glueballs, multiquark states), then identify discrepancies between the quark model predictions and experimental observations sector by sector.

## Key numbers

- The string tension from lattice QCD is found to be b ≈ 0.18 GeV² ≈ 0.9 GeV/fm
- The flavour mixing angle φ for the η-η' system is approximately 45°
- The u and d quark masses are approximately 5-10 MeV
- The static QCD potential is given by V(r) = br for r >> 1/Λ and V(r) ∼ (-4/3)(α_s/r) for r << 1/Λ

## Context

This review extends the line of work comparing quark model predictions to experimental meson spectra, following earlier reviews by Close (1988), Cooper (1988), Diekmann (1988), Burnett and Sharpe (1990), Tornqvist (1990), Amsler and Myhrer (1991), Konigsman (1991), Landau (1996), Amsler (1998), and Barnes (1998). The paper enables future identification of exotic hadrons by providing a detailed template of conventional quark model predictions against which candidate exotic states can be compared, and identifies specific puzzles that future experiments at facilities under construction should address.
