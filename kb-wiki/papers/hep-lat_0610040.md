# Low energy scattering parameters from the solutions of the non-relativistic Yukawa model on a 3-dimensional lattice

> The numerical solutions of the non-relativistic Yukawa model on a 3-dimensional size lattice with periodic boundary conditions are obtained. The possibility to extract the corresponding -- infinite space -- low energy parameters and bound state binding energies from eigensates computed at finite lattice size is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Luscher relation (Equation A0R0S) can reliably extract the infinite-volume scattering length and effective range from finite-lattice eigenenergies for the non-relativistic Yukawa model, but only if the effective range contribution is explicitly included; neglecting it leads to results wrong by a factor of two even at lattice sizes as large as \(aL=10\).

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic Schrodinger equation with a Yukawa potential \(U(r) = -g^2 e^{-\mu r}/(4\pi r)\) on a 3-dimensional cubic lattice of size \(L^3\) with periodic boundary conditions, using finite-difference discretization and an euclidean time-dependent propagation method. They computed the ground-state eigenenergies \(\epsilon_0(L)\) as a function of lattice size \(aL\) for several coupling strengths \(G = g^2 M/(4\pi\mu)\), both below and above the bound-state threshold. The infinite-volume scattering length \(A_0\) and effective range \(R_0\) were then extracted by applying the exact Luscher relation \(k\cot\delta_0(k) = S(\eta)/(\pi aL)\) combined with the effective-range expansion, and compared with independently computed exact values from the one-dimensional radial Schrodinger equation.

## Key numbers

* For \(G=0.40\) and lattice spacing \(a=0.20\), the zero-th order approximation \(A_0^{(0)}(L) = (aL)^3\epsilon_0(L)/(4\pi)\) at \(aL=10\) gives only 20% accuracy relative to the exact infinite-volume scattering length \(A_0 = 0.501\) (in dimensionless units).
* For \(G=1.5\) (near the bound-state pole at \(G_0=1.68\)) with \(a=0.10\), the exact scattering length is \(A_0 = -11.4\) and effective range \(R_0 = 2.47\); the full Luscher relation \(A_0^{(S)}(L)\) converges to a few percent accuracy already at \(aL \approx 5\), while neglecting \(R_0\) gives a result wrong by a factor of two even at \(aL=10\).
* The bound-state critical coupling for the first S-wave state is \(G_0 \approx 1.680\), with subsequent poles at \(G_1 = 6.44\) and \(G_2 = 14.34\).
* For \(G=0.40\) and \(a=0.20\), the exact scattering length is \(A_0 = -0.501\) and effective range \(R_0 = 10.08\) (from Table 2 with \(a=0.10\) exact column).

## Context

This work extends the Luscher formalism (Luscher 1986, 1991) for extracting infinite-volume scattering parameters from finite-volume spectra, previously applied in quenched and dynamical lattice QCD to \(\pi\pi\) and nucleon-nucleon systems, by testing it in a controlled non-relativistic Yukawa model where exact results are independently available. The study complements the approach of Beane et al. (2004) in pionless effective field theory, who assumed the Luscher equations to generate energy levels from given low-energy parameters, whereas here the dynamical model generates both the energies and the parameters, allowing direct assessment of the method's validity. The results enable reliable extraction of scattering lengths and effective ranges from lattice QCD simulations of hadron-hadron interactions, particularly for systems with large scattering lengths like the \(^1S_0\) nucleon-nucleon channel.
