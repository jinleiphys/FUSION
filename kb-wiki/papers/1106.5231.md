# Quantum Spectrum of Cherenkov Glue

> Full quantum calculation of Cherenkov gluon radiation by quark and gluon currents and a Cherenkov decay of a gluon into a pair of Cherenkov gluons in transparent media is performed. Energy losses due to Cherenkov gluon radiation in high energy nuclear collisions are calculated. The angular distribution of the energy flow due to the radiation of Cherenkov gluons is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a full quantum calculation of Cherenkov gluon radiation by quark and gluon currents and a Cherenkov decay of a gluon into a pair of Cherenkov gluons, but only within a simplified quasi-Abelian model where the chromopermittivity is taken as diagonal in color space and approximated by a step function with a constant value epsilon above 1 below a cutoff frequency omega_0.

## Method

The authors compute the differential decay rates for three processes: a quark decaying into a quark and a Cherenkov gluon, a gluon decaying into a gluon and a Cherenkov gluon (single Cherenkov decay), and a gluon decaying into two Cherenkov gluons (double Cherenkov decay). The calculations use in-medium QCD Feynman rules derived from a field-theoretical model with a chromopermittivity that is diagonal in color space and has a step-function frequency dependence. The matrix elements are computed in Coulomb gauge, and the resulting differential decay rates are integrated to obtain energy loss per unit length and angular distributions of energy flow.

## Key numbers

*   The chromopermittivity is taken as epsilon = 5 and the cutoff frequency as omega_0 = 3 GeV, obtained from fitting experimental data in Dremin et al. (2009).
*   For quark Cherenkov radiation, the differential decay rate is gamma_{q -> q g̃}(omega | E) = alpha_s (N_c^2 - 1)/(2 N_c) (1 - 1/epsilon) (1 - omega/E + (epsilon+1)/4 omega^2/E^2).
*   For gluon Cherenkov radiation, the differential decay rate gamma_{g -> g g̃}(omega | E) contains an additional factor with terms up to omega^4/E^4.
*   The double Cherenkov decay of a gluon is only kinematically allowed for gluon energies in the interval omega_0 < E < 2 omega_0, with the emitted gluon energy restricted to 1/2 - 1/(2 sqrt(epsilon)) < omega/E < 1/2 + 1/(2 sqrt(epsilon)).
*   For the double Cherenkov decay with epsilon = 5 and omega_0 = 3 GeV, the energy loss for an initial gluon with energy E = 5 GeV is approximately 35 GeV/fm.

## Context

This work extends the classical Tamm-Frank theory of Cherenkov radiation (Tamm and Frank, 1937) and earlier classical treatments of Cherenkov gluon radiation by Dremin (1979, 1981, 2006) to a full quantum field theory description based on in-medium QCD. The calculation generalizes the quantum theory of electromagnetic Cherenkov radiation developed by Ritus (1957) and Stepanov (1975) to the non-Abelian case, and includes new purely non-Abelian processes such as the double Cherenkov decay of a gluon. The results enable a more reliable theoretical picture for interpreting the two-humped structure of dihadron angular correlations observed at RHIC (STAR and PHENIX collaborations), and the authors note that a detailed comparison with data will be published separately.
