# Tracing the origin of azimuthal gluon correlations in the color glass condensate

> We examine the origins of azimuthal correlations observed in high energy proton-nucleus collisions by considering the simple example of the scattering of uncorrelated partons off color fields in a large nucleus. We demonstrate how the physics of fluctuating color fields in the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory generates these azimuthal multiparticle correlations and compute the corresponding Fourier coefficients v_n within different CGC approximation schemes. We discuss in detail the qualitative and quantitative differences between the different schemes. We will show how a recently introduced color field domain model that captures key features of the observed azimuthal correlations can be understood in the CGC effective theory as a model of non-Gaussian correlations in the target nucleus.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that azimuthal multiparticle correlations observed in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions can arise purely from initial-state color field fluctuations in the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory, without requiring final-state collective flow. However, the authors explicitly note that the kinematic region where the strongest azimuthal correlations are seen experimentally corresponds more to a "dense-dense" situation, while their analytical and numerical calculations are performed in the simpler "dilute-dense" limit.

## Method
The authors compute two-particle correlation functions for quarks scattering off a large nucleus in the dilute-dense CGC framework, where the double inclusive distribution is expressed in terms of the dipole-dipole correlator of light-like Wilson lines. They evaluate this correlator within three approximation schemes: (i) the Glasma graph approximation (two-gluon exchange with Gaussian statistics), (ii) the nonlinear Gaussian approximation (all-order resummation of multiple gluon exchanges with Gaussian statistics), and (iii) full numerical lattice simulations of the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model and after JIMWLK renormalization group evolution. The azimuthal Fourier coefficients v₂, v₃, and v₄ are extracted from the two-particle correlation function and compared across these schemes.

## Key numbers
- The connected (correlated) contribution to the dipole-dipole correlator is suppressed by a factor of 1/(Nc² - 1) relative to the disconnected (uncorrelated) contribution, where Nc = 3 for QCD.
- The antisymmetric contribution to the dipole-dipole correlator, responsible for odd Fourier moments (v₃, v₅, ...), is proportional to (Nc² - 4)/(Nc² - 1)² and vanishes identically for Nc = 2.
- The color field domains are characterized by a transverse size of order 1/Qs, where Qs is the saturation scale of the target nucleus.
- In the limit of a large probe with Qs²B ≫ 1, the two-particle correlation function in the Glasma graph approximation reduces to a form containing delta functions δ⁽²⁾(p_T + q_T) and δ⁽²⁾(p_T - q_T), with the connected contribution suppressed by 1/(B(Nc² - 1)).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of initial-state azimuthal correlations previously studied in the Glasma graph framework by Dumitru et al. (2008-2014) and Dusling et al. (2012-2013), and in the color field domain model introduced by Dumitru et al. (2014). The authors show that the color field domain model can be understood within the CGC effective theory as a model of non-Gaussian correlations in the target nucleus. The results enable systematic comparisons between different CGC approximation schemes and clarify the relationship between the phenomenological Glasma graph approach and first-principles CGC calculations, while noting that full quantitative comparisons to experimental data will require extending the calculations to the dense-dense regime where classical Yang-Mills simulations are necessary.
