# Is The Heavy Fermion Formalism Applicable to Meson Production Process?

> We consider tree diagram contributions to neutral pion production in pp ->pp pi0 using chiral perturbation theory in the relativistic and extremely non-relativistic limits. In marked difference with the results from heavy fermion formalism, the impulse and s-wave rescattering terms in the relativistic limit have equal signs and therefore add constructively, giving rise to a substantial contribution to the cross section. We argue that the power series expansion of the nucleon propagator is on the border of its convergence circle. Consequently, a finite order heavy fermion formalism does not predict nucleon pole terms correctly and therefore can not be applied to meson production.

# Is The Heavy Fermion Formalism Applicable to Meson Production Process?

## Key claim

The heavy fermion formalism (HFF) cannot be applied to meson production processes because the power series expansion of the nucleon propagator is on the border of its convergence circle for such reactions, meaning a finite-order HFF does not predict nucleon pole terms correctly. In a fully relativistic chiral perturbation theory (χPT) treatment of neutral pion production in pp → pp π⁰, the impulse and s-wave rescattering terms have equal signs and add constructively, in marked difference to HFF results where these terms have opposite signs.

## Method

The authors calculate tree diagram contributions to neutral pion production in pp → pp π⁰ using fully relativistic chiral perturbation theory, deriving the nucleon pole (impulse) and sea-gull (rescattering) amplitudes from the relativistic pion-nucleon Lagrangian. They compare these relativistic results with the corresponding expressions obtained from the extremely non-relativistic heavy fermion formalism, and analyze the convergence properties of the power series expansion of the covariant nucleon propagator.

## Key numbers

- For the off-mass-shell nucleon pole contribution, the relativistic calculation gives T_off^(s+u) = -0.9/m, while the HFF expression T_HFF^(s+u) = (g_A²)/(4MF²)(q² + k²) is always positive
- The sea-gull term off the mass shell in the relativistic approach is a factor 2-3 larger than the corresponding HFF result
- For the production process, the exchanged pion has q² ≤ -M m and q² ≥ M m, and the virtual nucleon residual momentum gives T(p⊥)/vl ≈ -1, placing the power series on the border of its convergence circle
- Cross sections calculated with different low-energy constant (LEC) parameter sets vary within a factor of 2, and all except Set 2 underestimate experimental data by a factor of 1.5-2

## Context

This work extends the debate over neutral pion production in pp → pp π⁰, where previous HFF-based χPT calculations by Park et al. (1996), Cohen et al. (1996), and Sato et al. (1997) found impulse and rescattering terms with opposite signs leading to cross sections substantially smaller than experiment. The authors note that meson-exchange models by Hernandez et al. (1995), Hanhart et al. (1995), and Gedalin et al. (1996, 1998) predict constructive interference and achieve better descriptions of data near threshold. The paper demonstrates that the HFF expansion converges slowly for production processes and that a fully relativistic χPT approach reverses the sign of the impulse term relative to HFF predictions. The authors note that the Jülich group (Meissner, 1998) has reported similar results since the preparation of this work.
