# Approaching universality in weakly-bound three-body systems

> Atom-dimer scattering below the three-body break-up threshold is studied for a system of three identical bosons. The atom-dimer scattering length and the energy of the most weakly-bound three-body state are shown to be strongly correlated. An appropriate rescaling of the observables reveals the subtlety of the correlation, and serves to identify universal trends in the unitary limit of divergent two-body scattering length. The correlation provides a new quantitative measure of the degree of universality in three-body systems with short-ranged interactions, as well as a consistency check of effective field theories and other theoretical models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a rescaling of the atom-dimer scattering length and the three-body binding energy reveals a universal, potential-independent correlation (the "modified Phillips line") for three identical bosons, but only in the limit of large two-body scattering length; deviations from universality occur for smaller scattering lengths where the correlation depends on the specific two-body potential.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for three identical bosons using a recently developed three-body code, computing atom-dimer scattering lengths and three-body bound state energies for six families of two-body short-ranged potentials (including Bargmann potentials, the TTY potential, and the MTV potential) with widely varied two-body scattering lengths and binding energies. They also solved the regularized Skornyakov/Ter-Martirosyan equations corresponding to a zero-range interaction model. The results were plotted using scaled dimensionless variables: α = a₃√(-2m₁₂E₂) for the scattering length and ω = 1/√(E₃/E₂ - 1) for the binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The universal ratio E₃/E₂ ≈ 2.54 corresponds to the point where the atom-dimer scattering length approaches zero.
- The universal ratio E₃/E₂ ≈ 6.7 corresponds to divergent negative atom-dimer scattering length.
- For the Bargmann potential with effective range r₀ = 1 a.u., the two-body scattering length was varied from 2 to 460 a.u.
- The universal part of the modified Phillips line is reproduced by the formula α = α₁/(1/ω - 1/ω₀) + ω + α₀ with α₁ = 5.5, ω₀ = 0.419, and α₀ = 4.
- A simpler empirical fit α = ω + (3/2)(1 - 1/ω²) is suitable for positive three-body scattering length.

## Context

This work extends the original Phillips line correlation between triton binding energy and neutron-deuteron scattering length, first observed by Phillips (1968) and later explained by Efimov and Tkachenko as deriving from limited sampling of scattering parameters. The authors introduce a rescaled "modified Phillips line" that reveals universal behavior in the unitary limit of divergent two-body scattering length, which is now accessible in ultracold gas experiments using Fano-Feshbach resonances. The correlation provides a quantitative test for effective field theories and other theoretical models, and can be used to check consistency between bound-state and scattering calculations for three-body systems.
