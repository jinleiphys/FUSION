# Kaon-nucleon scattering lengths from kaonic deuterium experiments revisited

> We analyse the impact of the recent measurement of kaonic hydrogen X rays by the SIDDHARTA collaboration on the allowed ranges for the kaon-deuteron scattering length in the framework of non-relativistic effective field theory. Based on data from KN scattering only, we predict the kaon-deuteron scattering length A_Kd= (-1.46 + i 1.08) fm, with an estimated uncertainty of about 25% in both the real and the imaginary part.

## Key claim

Based on the new SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurement and antikaon-nucleon scattering data, the kaon-deuteron scattering length is predicted as \(A_{Kd}=(-1.46 + i\ 1.08)\) fm with an estimated uncertainty of about 25% in both real and imaginary parts, but this prediction relies on the resummed fixed-center approximation without recoil corrections and assumes the genuine three-body term is zero.

## Method

The authors relate the elementary antikaon-nucleon scattering lengths \(a_0\) and \(a_1\) to the \(K^-p\) scattering length \(a_p\) using non-relativistic effective field theory up to next-to-leading order in isospin breaking, including the unitary cusp from neutral kaon loops. They then use the resummed fixed-center approximation (FCA) with the CD-Bonn deuteron wave function to connect the elementary scattering lengths to the \(K^-d\) scattering length \(A_{Kd}\), scanning the complex plane for allowed values where solutions for \(a_0\) and \(a_1\) exist.

## Key numbers

* Kaonic hydrogen ground-state shift from SIDDHARTA: \(\epsilon_{1s} = -283 \pm 36\ \text{(stat)} \pm 6\ \text{(syst)}\) eV
* Kaonic hydrogen ground-state width from SIDDHARTA: \(\Gamma_{1s} = 541 \pm 89\ \text{(stat)} \pm 22\ \text{(syst)}\) eV
* \(K^-p\) scattering length extracted from SIDDHARTA: \(a_p = (-0.66 + i\ 0.81)\) fm
* \(K^-p\) scattering length from combined SIDDHARTA and scattering data average: \(a_p = (-0.85 + i\ 0.78)\) fm
* Predicted kaon-deuteron scattering length: \(A_{Kd} = (-1.46 + i\ 1.08)\) fm
* Uncertainty in Re \(A_{Kd}\) and Im \(A_{Kd}\) from scattering data: approximately \(\pm 0.35\) fm each
* Uncertainty in Re \(a_p\) from SIDDHARTA: \(\Delta\,\text{Re}\, a_p = 0.10\) fm; Im \(a_p\): \(\Delta\,\text{Im}\, a_p = 0.13\) fm
* Uncertainty in Re \(a_p\) and Im \(a_p\) from scattering data: \(\sim 0.2\) fm each
* Experimental bound from COSY: Im \(A_{Kd} \le 1.3\) fm and \(|\text{Re}\, A_{Kd}| \le 1.3\) fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Meissner et al. (2006) which showed that the earlier DEAR measurement (Beer et al.) was incompatible with scattering data, leading to very restricted allowed ranges for the kaon-deuteron scattering length. The new SIDDHARTA measurement resolves that inconsistency, and the predictions now agree with older KpX results (KEK) and with scattering data from Borasoy et al. (2006). The results provide a baseline for the upcoming SIDDHARTA2 measurement of kaonic deuterium X rays, which would test chiral QCD dynamics at low energies.
