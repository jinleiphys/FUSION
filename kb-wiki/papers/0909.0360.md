# A study of nuclear effect in $F_3$ structure function in the deep inelastic $ν(\barν)$ reactions in nuclei

> We study nuclear effect in the $F^A_3(x)$ structure function in the deep inelastic neutrino reactions on iron by taking into account Fermi motion, binding, target mass correction, shadowing and anti-shadowing corrections. Calculations have been done in a local density approximation using relativistic nuclear spectral functions which include nucleon correlations for nuclear matter. Results for $F^A_3(x)$ have been compared with the results reported at NuTeV and also with some of the older experiments reported in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors find that nuclear medium effects (Fermi motion, binding, target mass correction, shadowing, and anti-shadowing) significantly modify the $F_3$ structure function in iron targets, and that their theoretical calculations using a relativistic spectral function approach show qualitative agreement with experimental data from NuTeV and CDHSW, though the abstract does not specify that the agreement is only qualitative and that the shadowing/anti-shadowing corrections are taken from an external prescription rather than derived within the same framework.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear structure function $F^A_3(x,Q^2)$ for iron using a local density approximation applied to relativistic nuclear spectral functions that include nucleon correlations, derived from the Lehmann representation of the nucleon propagator in nuclear matter. They incorporate Fermi motion and binding effects through the spectral function formalism, add target mass corrections following Schienbein et al., and include shadowing and anti-shadowing corrections using the Glauber-Gribov multiple scattering prescription of Kulagin and Petti. The nucleon parton distributions are taken from the NNLO MSTW parametrization, and QCD corrections to $F_3$ are included up to second order in $\alpha_s$ using the coefficient functions of Moch et al.

## Key numbers

- At x=0.45 and $Q^2=1-2$ GeV$^2$, the increase in $F_3(x,Q^2)$ due to shadowing/anti-shadowing is around 25-30%, reducing to 5-7% at $Q^2=5-10$ GeV$^2$.
- At x=0.65 and $Q^2=1-2$ GeV$^2$, the increase in $F_3(x,Q^2)$ is around 70-75%, reducing to 25-30% at $Q^2=5-10$ GeV$^2$ and around 5% at $Q^2=20$ GeV$^2$.
- For x=0.0001 to x=0.015, the reduction in $F_3(x,Q^2)$ due to shadowing is around 40-50% at $Q^2=1-2$ GeV$^2$, becoming 30-35% at $Q^2=3-5$ GeV$^2$, and around 10% at $Q^2=20$ GeV$^2$.
- For x=0.05, the reduction in $F_3(x,Q^2)$ is around 10% at $Q^2=1-2$ GeV$^2$, becoming 8-10% at $Q^2=3-5$ GeV$^2$, and around 3-4% at $Q^2=20$ GeV$^2$.
- For x=0.08, an enhancement of around 8% is seen at $Q^2=1-2$ GeV$^2$, becoming 2-3% at $Q^2=3-5$ GeV$^2$.
- For x=0.1-0.3, the enhancement in $F_3(x,Q^2)$ is around 10% at low $Q^2$, dying out rapidly with increasing $Q^2$.
- The ratio $R(x,Q^2)=F^A_3(x,Q^2)/AF^N_3(x,Q^2)$ shows suppression for $x \le x_{min}=0.7$, with $x_{min}$ decreasing as $Q^2$ increases.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear medium effects in deep inelastic scattering, particularly the EMC effect seen in charged lepton scattering, to the $F_3$ structure function in neutrino and antineutrino reactions on iron. The authors compare their results with experimental data from the NuTeV collaboration (Tzanov) and the CDHSW collaboration (Berge), finding qualitative agreement. The calculations use the spectral function approach of Fernandez de Cordoba and Oset (1991) and the shadowing prescription of Kulagin and Petti, and are relevant for interpreting neutrino oscillation experiments such as MINERvA and the proposed NuSOnG experiment, which require precise knowledge of neutrino-nucleus cross sections in the few-GeV energy region.
