# Contact operators in renormalization of attractive singular potentials

> We discuss renormalization of chiral nuclear forces in the 3P0 channel of N N scattering at next- to-next-to leading order (N2LO) if the one-pion exchange is treated nonperturbatively at leading order. The matrix elements of the subleading contact potentials become nearly dependent of each other for the so-called exceptional ultraviolet momentum cutoff, making it difficult to determine the strengths of those contact potentials from the empirical phase shifts, as reported in Ref. [1]. We argue that this issue can be resolved by adjusting the strategy by which the low-energy constants are deduced from the data, thus making those exceptional cutoffs amenable to chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the difficulty in determining subleading contact-potential strengths from empirical phase shifts at certain "exceptional" ultraviolet momentum cutoffs in the 3P0 channel can be resolved by slightly adjusting the input phase shift used to fix the leading-order low-energy constant, rather than indicating a fundamental failure of renormalization-group invariance in chiral effective field theory.

## Method

The authors study NN scattering in the 3P0 channel at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) with one-pion exchange treated nonperturbatively at leading order. They analyze the correlation between the two subleading contact operators (C^(2) and D^(0)) by examining the dimensionless quantity g̃(k; Λ) = Λ² χ(k)/(2φ(k)), which measures the independence of their matrix elements. Near exceptional cutoffs where g̃ vanishes, they propose varying the leading-order input phase shift δ_In by a small amount (0.5°) within narrow cutoff windows, thereby removing the correlation and enabling independent determination of the subleading low-energy constants through least-squares fits to Nijmegen partial-wave analysis phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order input momentum μ₀ = 153.216 MeV is used throughout to fix the leading-order contact strength C^(0)
- Two exceptional cutoff windows are identified: near Λ = 590 MeV and near Λ = 2710 MeV, where g̃(k; Λ) becomes vanishingly small (∼10⁻⁶ at Λ = 590 MeV, ∼10⁻⁷ at Λ = 2710.54 MeV)
- The alternative input phase shift Δδ_In = 0.5° is chosen, which is smaller than the estimated N2LO truncation error of δ_PWA(μ₀/M_hi)² ≈ 2° (with M_hi taken as the nucleon-delta mass splitting)
- The exceptional cutoff Λ_E varies by only ΔΛ_E ≈ 0.15 MeV near Λ = 2710 MeV when k changes from 0 to 300 MeV
- Phase shift fits use data up to k = 300 MeV, with cutoff Λ varied from 800 to 3200 MeV for the normal band

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of subleading chiral nuclear forces developed by Long (2007, 2011, 2012) and addresses a problem reported by Gasparyan (2022), who found that at certain "genuine exceptional cutoffs" the subleading contact operators in the 3P0 channel become dangerously correlated, making low-energy constant determination difficult. The resolution enables these exceptional cutoffs to be included in chiral effective field theory without violating renormalization-group invariance, and the approach could be applied to similar difficulties reported by Shi (2022) for the 3S1-3D1 channel.
