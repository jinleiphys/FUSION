# Hydrodynamic effective field theory and the analyticity of hydrostatic correlators

> We study one-loop corrections to retarded and symmetric hydrostatic correlation functions within the Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory framework for relativistic hydrodynamics, focusing on charge diffusion. We first consider the simplified setup with only diffusive charge density fluctuations, and then augment it with momentum fluctuations in a model where the sound modes can be ignored. We show that the loop corrections, which generically induce non-analyticities and long-range effects at finite frequency, non-trivially preserve analyticity of retarded correlation functions in spatial momentum due to the KMS constraint, as a manifestation of thermal screening. For the purposes of this analysis, we develop an interacting field theory for diffusive hydrodynamics, seen as a limit of relativistic hydrodynamics in the absence of temperature and longitudinal velocity fluctuations.

## Key claim
The paper shows that one-loop corrections to retarded hydrostatic correlation functions, which generically introduce non-analyticities in spatial momentum, are rendered analytic by the KMS (Kubo-Martin-Schwinger) symmetry condition, thereby preserving the consistency of thermal screening and the derivative expansion of the hydrostatic generating functional.

## Method
The authors develop an interacting Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory for diffusive hydrodynamics, first considering only charge density fluctuations and then augmenting with transverse momentum fluctuations while freezing sound modes. They compute one-loop corrections to retarded and symmetric density-density correlation functions, keeping the dissipative transport coefficients and stochastic fluctuation parameters independent to isolate the role of the KMS condition, which sets the stochastic parameters equal to the dissipative ones.

## Key numbers
- The tree-level retarded density correlator is \( G_{nn}^{\mathrm{R, tree}}(\omega, \mathbf{k}) = i\chi D \mathbf{k}^2 / (\omega + iD\mathbf{k}^2) \), where \(\chi\) is the static charge susceptibility and \(D = \sigma/\chi\) is the diffusion constant.
- In \(d=3\) spatial dimensions, the one-loop corrected retarded correlator at zero frequency takes the form \( G_{nn}^{\mathrm{R}}(\omega=0, \mathbf{k}) = \chi (Z_\chi + c_1^{\mathrm{R}} |\mathbf{k}| + c_2^{\mathrm{R}} |\mathbf{k}|^3 + \mathcal{O}(\mathbf{k}^4)) \), where \(c_1^{\mathrm{R}}\) and \(c_2^{\mathrm{R}}\) are non-analytic coefficients.
- Under the KMS condition (\(\tilde\sigma = \sigma\), \(\tilde\eta = \eta\)), the non-analytic coefficients vanish: \(c_1^{\mathrm{R}} \overset{\text{KMS}}{=} 0\) and \(c_2^{\mathrm{R}} \overset{\text{KMS}}{=} 0\).
- The symmetric correlator at zero frequency remains non-analytic even under KMS, with explicit expressions for \(c_1^{\mathrm{S}}\) and \(c_2^{\mathrm{S}}\) given in terms of thermodynamic and transport parameters.

## Context
This work extends the Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory framework for relativistic hydrodynamics developed by Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015) and Jain, Kovtun, Ritz, and Shukla (2020), and builds on the study of long-time tails due to density fluctuations at one-loop order by Chen-Lin, Delacretaz, and Hartnoll (2018). The results confirm that the hydrostatic generating functional approach of Banerjee, Bhattacharya, Bhattacharyya, Jain, and Minwalla (2012) and Jensen, Kaminski, Kovtun, Meyer, Ritz, and Yarom (2012) remains consistent at loop level, enabling further study of stochastic signatures in hydrodynamic correlation functions.
