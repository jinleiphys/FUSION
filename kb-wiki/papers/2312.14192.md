# iDMEu: An initiative for Dark Matter in Europe and beyond

> We introduce the initiative for Dark Matter in Europe and beyond (iDMEu), a collective effort by a group of particle and astroparticle physicists to set up an online resource meta-repository, a common discussion platform and a series of meetings on everything concerning Dark Matter. This document serves as a status report as well as a citable item concerning iDMEu.

## Key claim

The iDMEu initiative provides an online meta-repository, discussion platform, and meeting series for the diverse dark matter community, but it explicitly does not intend to replace existing efforts, instead acting as a hub and aggregator to complement them.

## Method

The proponents submitted an Expression of Interest following a call from the JENAS workshop (2019), jointly organized by ECFA, NuPECC, and APPEC. They established a light management structure of Proponents, Organizers, and Curators (including early-career researchers) to build and maintain the online meta-repository at www.idmeu.org, organize regular town-hall meetings, and collect endorsements from the community. The Organizers report to the sponsoring agencies ECFA, NuPECC, and APPEC, which set up a Task Force in 2022 to provide feedback.

## Key numbers

- The kick-off meeting (10-12 May 2021) had about 375 registered participants.
- The 1st town-hall meeting (1 September 2023) had about 100 registered participants, with about half attending in person in Vienna.
- The meta-repository tables are intended to be upgraded regularly, e.g., on a monthly basis.

## Context

This work extends the community coordination efforts initiated by the JENAS workshop (2019) and the subsequent Expression of Interest process organized by ECFA, NuPECC, and APPEC. It complements existing dark matter resources by acting as a central aggregator, and enables cross-community collaboration by providing a common platform for particle theorists, experimentalists, cosmologists, and astrophysicists working on dark matter detection, simulations, and theory. The initiative also connects to broader efforts such as the Snowmass process (2021) on dark matter complementarity.
