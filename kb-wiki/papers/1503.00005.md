# The Higgs Transverse Momentum Distribution at NNLL and its Theoretical Errors

> In this letter, we present the NNLL-NNLO transverse momentum Higgs distribution arising from gluon fusion. In the regime $p_\perp\ll m_H$ we include the resummation of the large logs at next to next-to leading order and then match on to the $α_s^2$ fixed order result near $p_\perp \sim m_h$. By utilizing the rapidity renormalization group (RRG) we are able to smoothly match between the resummed, small $p_\perp$ regime and the fixed order regime. We give a detailed discussion of the scale dependence of the result including an analysis of the rapidity scale dependence. Our central value differs from previous results, in the transition region as well as the tail, by an amount which is outside the error band. This difference is due to the fact that the RRG profile allows us to smoothly turn off the resummation.

## Key claim

The authors present a NNLL-NNLO resummed transverse momentum distribution for Higgs production via gluon fusion, claiming that their central value differs from previous results in the transition region and tail by an amount outside the error band, specifically because the rapidity renormalization group (RRG) profile allows them to smoothly turn off the resummation rather than using a fixed matching scale.

## Method

The calculation uses soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) with the rapidity renormalization group (RRG) to resum large logarithms in the regime p_perp much less than m_H, matching onto the fixed-order alpha_s^2 result near p_perp ~ m_h. The resummation is carried out in impact parameter space, and the transition between resummed and fixed-order regimes is controlled by scale profiles that smoothly vary the low matching scale from 1/b_0 to m_h. The hard function is run from m_h down to 1/b_0, while the jet function is run in rapidity space up to m_h, with the cusp anomalous dimension kept at three loops and non-cusp terms at two loops.

## Key numbers

- The resummation is performed at NNLL (next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic) accuracy, matched to NNLO (next-to-next-to-leading order) fixed-order result at alpha_s^2
- The transition region where resummation is turned off is centered at t = 35, 45, and 55 GeV for the three profile choices
- The low matching scale L is set to 2e^{-gamma_E}/b and varied by a factor of two about its central value for error estimation
- The cut-off in impact parameter space is set at b ~ 2 GeV^{-1}, with variation between 1.5 and 3 GeV^{-1} producing errors only in the fourth significant digit
- The error from the large top mass approximation is less than 1% for p_perp < m_t
- Power corrections in p_perp/M_h become relevant above p_perp > 30 GeV for ten percent accuracy
- The cross section is evaluated at center-of-mass energies of 8, 13, and 14 TeV using MSTW 2008 PDFs at NNLO with alpha_s(M_z) = 0.1184
- PDF set variation between MSTW2008 and CTEQ5 shows deviations of a few percent at 13 TeV

## Context

This work extends previous NNLL resummation calculations for the Higgs transverse momentum distribution, specifically comparing to results from Becher et al. (2012) and the CSS formalism calculations by Grazzini and collaborators. The key advance is using the RRG to smoothly turn off resummation in the tail region, which the authors argue prevents the overshoot of the cross section seen in prior work where resummation was not turned off. The formalism was previously applied to the Higgs jet veto cross section by Berger, Neill, and Stewart (2011), and the paper fills a gap by presenting the full spectrum matched onto the NNLO result. The approach enables more reliable error estimation by including rapidity scale variation alongside the usual renormalization scale variation.
