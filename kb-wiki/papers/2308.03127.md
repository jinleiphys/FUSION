# Effect of cluster transfer on neutron-rich nuclide production around N=126 in multinucleon transfer reactions

> The cluster transfer in multinucleon transfer reactions near Coulomb barrier energies is implemented into the master equations in dinuclear system model, in which the deuteron, triton, $^{3}$He and $α$ are taken into account. The effects of cluster transfer and dynamical deformation on the formation of primary and secondary fragments are systematically investigated. It is found that the inclusion of cluster transfer is favorable the fragment formation with increasing the transferring nucleons and leads to a broad mass distribution. The isotopic cross sections of elements W, Os, Rn and Fr in the reaction of $^{136}$Xe+$^{208}$Pb at the incident energy of E$_{c.m.}$ = 450 MeV are nicely consistent with the Argonne data. The new neutron-rich isotopes of wolfram and osmium are predicted with cross sections above 10 nb. The production mechanism of neutron-rich heavy nuclei around N = 126 in the reactions of $^{58,64,72}$Ni + $^{198}$Pt is investigated thoroughly. The cross sections for producing the neutron-rich isotopes of platinum, iridium, osmium and rhenium in the multinucleon transfer reactions of $^{64}$Ni + $^{198}$Pt and $^{72}$Ni + $^{198}$Pt at the center of mass energies of 220 MeV and 230 MeV are estimated and proposed for the future experiments.

## Key claim

The inclusion of cluster transfer (deuteron, triton, \(^{3}\)He, and \(\alpha\)) in the dinuclear system model broadens the mass and charge distributions of fragments in multinucleon transfer reactions, but the effect is most pronounced in the target-like (heavier fragment) region and depends on the cluster separation energy of the DNS fragment; the model reproduces Argonne data for \(^{136}\)Xe+\(^{208}\)Pb at 450 MeV but shows narrower distributions than Dubna data at 423 MeV, possibly due to neglected shape evolution.

## Method

The dinuclear system (DNS) model was extended by adding cluster transfer terms (deuteron, triton, \(^{3}\)He, \(\alpha\)) to the master equations that govern the temporal evolution of the distribution probability \(P(Z_1,N_1,E_1,\beta_1,B,t)\) for fragment 1. The transition probabilities for each cluster were derived from the single-particle Hamiltonian in valence space, with spin-isospin statistical factors (e.g., 1/96 for \(\alpha\), 3/8 for deuteron) based on the Wigner density approach. The model also couples the cluster transfer to dynamical quadrupole deformation and dissipation of relative motion energy and angular momentum, and the secondary decay of primary fragments via neutron evaporation is included to obtain final cross sections.

## Key numbers

- In \(^{136}\)Xe+\(^{208}\)Pb at \(E_{c.m.}=450\) MeV, the total capture cross sections with cluster transfer and dynamical deformation are 82 mb for \(^{136}\)Xe and 69 mb for \(^{208}\)Pb; without cluster transfer and deformation, they are 62 mb and 38 mb at 423 MeV.
- New neutron-rich isotopes of wolfram (W) and osmium (Os) are predicted with cross sections above 10 nb; for example, from 8 \(\mu\)b for \(^{194}\)W to 4.5 nb for \(^{200}\)W, and from 23.5 nb for \(^{203}\)Os to 1.8 nb for \(^{206}\)Os.
- In \(^{72}\)Ni+\(^{198}\)Pt at 220 MeV and 230 MeV, maximal yields include 27.8 mb for \(^{198}\)Pt, 1.58 mb for \(^{197}\)Ir, 3.03 mb for \(^{194}\)Os, and 0.82 mb for \(^{191}\)Re.
- For \(^{136}\)Xe+\(^{208}\)Pb at 450 MeV, calculated cross sections are 15.9 \(\mu\)b for \(^{220}\)Rn and 29.7 \(\mu\)b for \(^{220}\)Fr, compared to experimental 5.7\(\pm\)1.1 \(\mu\)b for \(^{216}\)Fr.

## Context

This work extends the dinuclear system model of Feng (2009) and others by incorporating cluster transfer, which was previously treated only via single-nucleon transfer in models such as the GRAZING model (Winther, 1994) and the multidimensional Langevin approach (Zagrebaev and Greiner, 2008). The improved model reproduces the Argonne data for \(^{136}\)Xe+\(^{208}\)Pb (Barrett et al., 2015) and predicts new neutron-rich isotopes around N=126, enabling future experiments at facilities like RIBF, SPIRAL2, FRIB, and HIAF to target specific systems such as \(^{72}\)Ni+\(^{198}\)Pt for producing neutron-rich platinum, iridium, osmium, and rhenium isotopes.
