# Inhomogeneous chiral phase in the magnetic field

> Inhomogeneous chiral phase is discussed in the presence of the magnetic field. A topological aspect is pointed out for the complex order parameter, in relation to the spectral asymmetry of the Dirac operator. It induces an anomalous baryon number and extremely extends the region of the inhomogeneous chiral phase in the QCD phase diagram. It is also shown that the novel tricritical point appears at zero chemical potential, which should be examined by the lattice QCD simulation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the presence of a magnetic field induces spectral asymmetry in the lowest Landau level for the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phase, which generates an anomalous baryon number and dramatically extends the region of the inhomogeneous chiral phase in the QCD phase diagram; a novel tricritical point appears at zero chemical potential, but the authors hedge that this prediction "should be examined by the lattice QCD simulation."

## Method

The authors study the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) configuration, characterized by a constant amplitude \(\Delta\) and a chiral angle \(\theta(z)=qz\), within the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the presence of an external magnetic field applied along the \(z\)-axis. They diagonalize the Dirac Hamiltonian in the Landau gauge, compute the energy spectrum including the lowest Landau level, and evaluate the Atiyah-Patodi-Singer \(\eta\) invariant to obtain the anomalous baryon number. A generalized Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the thermodynamic potential is performed, with coefficients derived from the quark Green function in the magnetic field, to locate the novel tricritical point.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous baryon-number density is \(\rho_{\rm anom} = \sum_f \frac{|e_f H|}{4\pi^2} q\), where \(q\) is the wave vector of the DCDW and \(e_f\) is the flavor-dependent electric charge.
- The \(\eta\) invariant for each flavor is \(\eta_{\rm H}^f/(2L) = -N_c \frac{|e_f H| q}{2\pi^2}\), independent of the dynamical mass \(m\).
- In 1+1 dimensions (without magnetic field), the topological baryon-number density is \(\rho_{\rm anom} = \frac{q}{2\pi}\) for \(m > q/2\).
- The condition for the novel tricritical point is \(\alpha_2 = \alpha_3 = 0\); \(\alpha_3 = 0\) gives \(\mu = 0\), and \(\alpha_2(H,T,\mu=0)=0\) defines a tricritical line on the \(T-H\) plane.
- The paper shows a phase diagram in the \(\mu-T\) plane for the DCDW phase in the chiral limit (\(m_c=0\)) and for \(m_c=5\) MeV (from prior work by Karasawa, 2013).

## Context

This work extends studies of inhomogeneous chiral phases in QCD, particularly the dual chiral density wave introduced by Nakano and Tatsumi (2004), by adding an external magnetic field. It builds on the concept of magnetic catalysis (Klevansky and Lemmer, 1989; Gusynin et al., 1995) and the spectral asymmetry of the Dirac operator known from the chiral bag model (Goldstone and Jaffe, 1983). The results enable future lattice QCD simulations at zero chemical potential, where the sign problem is absent, to test the predicted novel tricritical point on the \(T-H\) plane.
