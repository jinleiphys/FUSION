# Probing equilibration with respect to isospin degree of freedom in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions

> We have studied equilibration with respect to isospin degree of freedom in four 96 mass systems $^{96}Ru+^{96}Ru$, $^{96}Ru+^{96}Zr$, $^{96}Zr+^{96}Ru$, $^{96}Zr+^{96}Zr$ at 100 AMeV and 400 AMeV with isospin dependent QMD. We propose that the neutron-proton differential rapidity distribution is a sensitive probe to the degree of equilibration with respect to the isospin degree of freedom. By analyzing the average $N/Z$ ratio of emitted nucleons, light charged particles (LCP) and intermediate mass fragments (IMF), it is found that there exist memory effect in multifragmentation process. The average $N/Z$ ratio of IMF reduces largely as beam energy increases from 100 AMeV to 400 AMeV which may result from the change of the behavior of the isotope distribution of IMF charges. The isotope distribution of IMF charges does also show certain memory effect at 100 AMeV case but not at 400 AMeV case.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton differential rapidity distribution is a sensitive probe for the energy dependence of equilibration with respect to the isospin degree of freedom in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions, and the memory effect in the isotope distribution of intermediate mass fragment (IMF) charges exists at 100 AMeV but disappears at 400 AMeV.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model with a soft equation of state (K=200 MeV) and a symmetry energy of about 29 MeV to simulate four mass-96 systems: ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru, ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Zr, ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Ru, and ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr at beam energies of 100 AMeV and 400 AMeV. They analyzed the normalized proton counting R_Z, proton rapidity distributions, neutron-proton differential rapidity distributions, and the average N/Z ratios of emitted nucleons, light charged particles (LCP), and intermediate mass fragments (IMF) at projectile, central, and target rapidity regions.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry potential strength C_S was taken as 35 MeV, giving a symmetry energy of about 29 MeV.
- The soft equation of state used K=200 MeV with parameters: alpha = -356 MeV, beta = 303 MeV, gamma = 7/6, rho_0 = 0.168 fm⁻³, L = 2.1 fm, and C_Yuk = -5.5 MeV.
- For reactions Zr+Ru and Ru+Zr at 100 AMeV and 400 AMeV with b=0 fm, the absolute R_Z value goes from zero to about 0.5; at 400 AMeV with b=5 fm, it reaches about 0.75.
- The N/Z ratios of ⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁶Zr are 0.85 and 0.71, respectively.
- The average N/Z ratio of IMF reduces largely as beam energy increases from 100 AMeV to 400 AMeV for all four systems.

## Context

This work extends the "mixing experiment" performed by the FOPI collaboration (Rami et al., 2000) which used the same four mass-96 systems at 400 AMeV and found that protons were not emitted from an equilibrium source. The present study uses isospin-dependent QMD to test non-equilibrium effects at both 400 AMeV and 100 AMeV, finding that the neutron-proton differential rapidity distribution provides clearer energy-dependent information than the normalized proton counting R_Z alone. The results enable further investigation of whether multifragmentation is a statistical or dynamical emission process, particularly at lower beam energies where memory effects persist in the isotope distribution of IMF charges.
