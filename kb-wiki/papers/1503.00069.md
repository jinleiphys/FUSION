# Lorentz Coherence and the Proton Form Factor

> The dipole cutoff behavior for the proton form factor has been and still is one of the major issues in high-energy physics. It is shown that this dipole behavior comes from the coherence between the Lorentz contraction of the proton size and the decreasing wavelength of the incoming photon signal. The contraction rates are the same for both cases. This form of coherence is studied also in the momentum-energy space. The coherence effect in this space can be explained in terms of two overlapping wave functions.

# Lorentz Coherence and the Proton Form Factor

## Key claim

The dipole cutoff behavior of the proton form factor (scaling as 1/(momentum transfer)^4) arises from a coherence between the Lorentz contraction of the proton's longitudinal size and the decreasing wavelength of the incoming photon signal, where both quantities shrink at the same rate of 1/p for large momentum transfer. This coherence prevents the steeper Gaussian cutoff that would occur if the proton size remained unchanged.

## Method

The author calculates the proton electromagnetic form factor using Lorentz-covariant harmonic oscillator wave functions for bound quarks in the Breit frame, where the incoming and outgoing protons move in opposite directions with equal speed. The form factor is evaluated as a Fourier integral of the product of two Lorentz-squeezed ground-state oscillator wave functions moving in opposite directions, with the momentum transfer appearing as the Fourier conjugate variable. The calculation is then extended to the three-quark proton by including two independent oscillator modes, and the result is compared with the non-squeezed (Gaussian) case to isolate the coherence effect.

## Key numbers

- The form factor for a two-quark system decreases as 1/p^2 for large momentum transfer p, given by F(p) = [1/(1 + 2p^2)] exp[-p^2/(1 + 2p^2)] (Eq. ff07).
- For the three-quark proton with two oscillator modes, the form factor becomes F_3(p) = [1/(1 + 2p^2)]^2 exp[-p^2/(1 + 2p^2)], yielding the dipole cutoff of (1/p^2)^2.
- The Lorentz contraction factor for two wave functions moving in opposite directions with the same speed is 1/cosh(2eta) = (1 - beta^2)/(1 + beta^2), which in momentum units becomes 1/(1 + 2p^2) (Eq. contrac55, contrac66).
- Without Lorentz contraction (non-squeezed wave functions), the form factor gives a Gaussian cutoff G(p) = (1/sqrt(pi)) exp(-p^2) (Eq. gf02).

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Fujimura, Kobayashi, and Namiki (1970) and Licht and Pagnamenta (1970), who first obtained the dipole cutoff using harmonic oscillator wave functions, by providing a detailed physical mechanism for the effect. The author's previous work with Noz (1973) and the Kim-Noz-Oh paper (1979) constructed oscillator wave functions as representations of Wigner's little group, showing that the quark model and parton model are two aspects of one Lorentz-covariant entity. The present paper enables a quantitative understanding of the continuous transition between the static proton and the high-speed regime, and provides a framework for studying deviations from exact dipole behavior observed in experiments.
