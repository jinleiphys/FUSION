# Practical considerations for measuring global spin alignment of vector mesons in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Global spin alignment of vector mesons is a sensitive probe of system vorticity and particle production mechanism in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The measurement of global spin alignment is gaining increasing interest and deserves careful considerations. In this paper, we lay out a few practical issues that need to be taken care of when measuring global spin alignment of vector mesons. They are, the correction for event plane resolution, reconciling measurements made with different event planes, the correction for the effect of finite acceptance in pseudorapidity, and the consideration for measuring the azimuthal angle dependence. Insights and methodologies offered in this paper will help experiments to measure the global spin alignment properly and accurately.

## Key claim

The paper provides practical correction procedures for measuring the global spin alignment of vector mesons, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the correction for event plane resolution is not a simple scaling factor: even when the event plane resolution is zero (random angle), the observed spin alignment signal should not be expected to equal 1/3, because an object cannot be rotated into a ball around a fixed axis unless the object itself is a ball.

## Method

The authors derive an analytic formula for correcting the observed spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ for finite event plane resolution, starting from the general angular distribution of vector meson decay daughters and applying a rotation from the reaction plane to the event plane. They then establish a connection between ρ₀₀ measured with first-order and second-order event planes by factorizing the de-correlation between the two planes. For finite pseudorapidity acceptance in detectors with cylindrical symmetry, they model the acceptance effect as a multiplicative function and extract a correction parameter F from simulation. Finally, for the azimuthal angle dependence of spin alignment, they construct a response matrix that accounts for event-by-event smearing of the reaction plane angle, using a particle-level resolution defined per bin.

## Key numbers

- Initial global angular momenta in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be as large as of the order of 1000ℏ.
- The correction formula for event plane resolution is: ρ₀₀ − 1/3 = [4/(1+3R)] (ρ₀₀^{obs} − 1/3), where R is the event plane resolution defined as ⟨cos 2Δ⟩.
- The relation between ρ₀₀^{obs} and the mean value of cos 2(φ−Ψ') is: ρ₀₀^{obs} − 1/3 = −[(1+3R)/(3R)] ⟨cos 2(φ−Ψ')⟩.
- In the acceptance correction simulation, three pseudorapidity cuts are tested: |η| < 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0, for a specific pT range of [1.2 – 1.8 GeV/c].
- The azimuthal dependence simulation uses v₂ = 0.08 and χ = 0.69 for generating the event plane angle distribution.

## Context

This work extends the methodology for measuring global spin alignment of vector mesons, which was previously studied by Abelev et al. (2008) and Mohanty et al. (2017) using complicated simulation procedures for resolution correction. The paper provides analytic formulas that replace those simulations, and it addresses the relationship between measurements using first-order and second-order event planes, building on the event plane reconstruction techniques of Poskanzer and Voloshin (1998). The acceptance correction procedure responds to the observation by Lan et al. (2017) that finite pseudorapidity coverage significantly distorts the measured ρ₀₀. The azimuthal dependence correction method can also be applied to the global polarization of Λ hyperons, and the work enables differential measurements of spin alignment as a function of azimuthal angle relative to the reaction plane.
