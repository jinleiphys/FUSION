# Short-Lived Phi Mesons

> We use effective hadronic field theory to study in-medium properties of the $ϕ$ meson. The dominant decay channels $ϕ\toK^{+}K^{-}$ and $ϕ\toπρ$ are modeled using an SU(3) chiral Lagrangian with normal and abnormal parities. The $ϕ$ self-energy is approximated to one-, two-, and even three-loop order in the strong coupling. Effects of modified spectral functions for $π$, $K$, $ρ$ and $ϕ$ are also included in the calculation. This allows us to study in-medium decay of phi mesons into (in-medium) kaon-pair daughters and (in-medium) $πρ$ pairs. The results point to the possibility of rather short-lived $ϕ$'s, short enough to decay inside the fireball in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Implications relevant for the NA49 and NA50 experimental results, recently dubbed the ``$ϕ$ puzzle'', are discussed.

# Short-Lived Phi Mesons

## Key claim

The in-medium lifetime of the phi meson decreases dramatically at high temperature, dropping to roughly 5–10 fm/c near the critical temperature Tc ≈ 170 MeV, which is short enough for the phi to decay inside the hadronic fireball in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but only if both the phi and its daughter particles (kaons, pions, rho mesons) are treated as having broadened spectral functions from scattering in the medium.

## Method

The authors use an SU(3) chiral Lagrangian with normal and abnormal parity interactions to model the dominant phi decay channels ϕ → K⁺K⁻ and ϕ → πρ. The phi self-energy is computed to one-, two-, and three-loop order in the strong coupling, where higher loops correspond physically to collision broadening of the phi and its daughters via scattering processes such as ϕ + π → K + K*. In-medium spectral functions for π, K, ρ, and ϕ are then used to compute the in-medium decay rate, from which the lifetime is obtained by dividing the number of decays per unit time per unit volume by the phi number density.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum lifetime of the phi meson: 45 fm/c
- In-medium lifetime near Tc ≈ 170 MeV: roughly 10 fm/c at 150 MeV temperature, and 7 fm/c at 200 MeV temperature (solid curve including off-shell K⁺K⁻ and πρ final states)
- In-medium decay rate jumps from vacuum value of roughly 4 MeV to nearly 40 MeV at high temperature
- Collision broadening of phi spectral function near Tc: approximately 40 MeV width
- Fit to NA49 kaon data yields T = 135 MeV with radial flow β = 0.54
- Fit to NA50 dimuon data yields T = 178 MeV with radial flow β = 0.23 (with large uncertainty)
- Predicted increase in apparent dilepton branching ratio: factor of 2–5

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector meson in-medium modifications, particularly the collision broadening of rho mesons seen in CERN low-mass dilepton experiments (CERES collaboration, 1990s) and the scattering rate calculations for phi mesons by Haglin (1995) and by Vogt and Koch (2002). The paper addresses the "phi puzzle" from NA49 and NA50 experiments at CERN, where the kaon-pair momentum distribution shows an inverse slope parameter of 305 MeV while the dimuon signal shows 228 MeV, a discrepancy the authors propose to resolve by having phi mesons decay inside the fireball (producing dileptons with in-medium broadening) while later decays near freezeout produce the vacuum-like kaon pairs. The results enable quantitative predictions for the in-medium spectral shape of the dilepton signal and for an enhanced dilepton branching ratio, which the authors state is under further study.
