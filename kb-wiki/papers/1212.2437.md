# Nuclear Field Theory predictions for 11Li and 12Be: shedding light on the origin of pairing in nuclei

> Recent data resulting from studies of two-nucleon transfer reaction on 11Li, analyzed through a unified nuclear-structure-direct-reaction theory have provided strong direct as well as indirect confirmation, through the population of the first excited state of 9Li and of the observation of a strongly quenched ground state transition, of the prediction that phonon mediated pairing interaction is the main mechanism binding the neutron halo of the 8.5 ms-lived 11Li nucleus. In other words, the ground state of 11Li can be viewed as a neutron Cooper pair bound to the 9Li core, mainly through the exchange of collective vibration of the core and of the pigmy resonance arizing from the sloshing back and forth of the neutron halo against the protons of the core, the mean field leading to unbound two particle states, a situation essentially not altered by the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction acting between the halo neutrons. Two-neutron pick-up data, together with (t,p) data on 7Li, suggest the existence of a pairing vibrational band based on 9Li, whose members can be excited with the help of inverse kinematic experiments as was done in the case of 11Li(p,t)9Li reaction. The deviation from harmonicity can provide insight into the workings of medium polarization effects on Cooper pair nuclear pairing, let alone specific information concering the "rigidity" of the N=6 shell closure. Further information concerning these questions is provided by the predicted absolute differential cross sections associated with the reactions 12Be(p,t)10Be(gs) and 12Be(p,t)10Be(pv)(~10Be(p,t)8Be(gs)). In particular, concerning this last reaction, predictions of σ_{abs} can change by an order of magnitude depending on whether the halo properties associated with the d_{5/2} orbital are treated selfconsistently in calculating the ground state correlations of the (pair removal) mode, or not.

## Key claim

The paper argues that phonon-mediated pairing interaction, rather than the bare nucleon-nucleon force, is the dominant mechanism binding the neutron halo of 11Li, with the bare N-N interaction contributing only about 100 keV to the two-neutron binding energy. The authors claim that this conclusion is supported by quantitative agreement between Nuclear Field Theory predictions and experimental two-nucleon transfer data, but note that the predictions for absolute cross sections in 12Be(p,t) reactions can change by an order of magnitude depending on whether halo properties of the d5/2 orbital are treated self-consistently.

## Method

The authors applied Nuclear Field Theory (NFT) to calculate the structure of 11Li and 12Be, treating the ground states as neutron Cooper pairs coupled to 9Li and 10Be cores respectively, with pairing mediated by exchange of collective vibrations (dipole and quadrupole phonons) of the core and the pigmy resonance. They computed absolute differential cross sections for two-neutron transfer reactions (p,t) and (t,p) using a unified structure-reaction framework that includes successive, simultaneous, and non-orthogonality contributions, with spectroscopic amplitudes obtained from RPA calculations and from the wavefunction components of the halo neutron configurations.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy of 11Li: S2n ≈ 0.38 MeV
- Bare N-N interaction contributes only about 100 keV to the two-neutron binding energy of 11Li
- Differential cross section for 11Li(p,t)9Li(gs) at θ = 60°: dσ/dΩ = 0.7 mb/sr
- Differential cross section for 7Li(t,p)9Li(gs) at θ = 60°: dσ/dΩ = 3 mb/sr
- Experimental integrated cross section for 11Li(p,t)9Li(gs) over 20°-150°: σ_exp = 5.7 ± 0.9 mb
- Harmonic approximation energy of the two-phonon pairing vibrational state in 9Li: 5.7 MeV, calculated from binding energy differences B(9)-B(7) = 6.1 MeV and B(11)-B(9) = 0.4 MeV
- Harmonic approximation energy of the two-phonon pairing vibrational state in 10Be: 4.8 MeV, sum of pair removal energy W1(β=-2) = 0.5 MeV and pair addition energy W1(β=2) = 4.3 MeV
- Lowest known 0+ excited state of 10Be appears at about 6 MeV, shifted from the harmonic value of 4.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pairing vibrational model previously applied to medium-heavy nuclei like 208Pb (Flynn 1972, Broglia 1973) to the light halo nuclei 11Li and 12Be, where the bare N-N interaction provides only a minor fraction of the pairing correlation energy (about 100 keV) compared to 50-70% in heavier systems (Barranco 1999). The predictions build on earlier NFT calculations of 11Li structure (Barranco 2001, Gori 2004) and were tested against experimental data from two-neutron transfer reactions (Tanihata 2008). The results enable future inverse-kinematics experiments to search for pairing vibrational bands based on 9Li and 10Be, where deviations from harmonicity could reveal the relative contributions of bare and phonon-mediated pairing forces, and the "rigidity" of the N=6 shell closure.
