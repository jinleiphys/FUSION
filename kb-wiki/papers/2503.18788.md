# Relativistic $^3$He light-front wave function

> The relativistic light-front (LF) wave function of $^3$He is determined by the three-body LF equation for the Faddeev components in the momentum space. As an interaction, we take the one-meson exchange kernels, without the potential approximation. Within the explicitly covariant formulation of LF dynamics, we calculate the full relativistic $^3$He LF wave function, comprising 32 spin-isospin components. In the non-relativistic domain, five of these components dominate and closely resemble their non-relativistic counterparts. Relativistic effects manifest themselves in deviations in relativistic domain of these components from the non-relativistic ones and in appearance of new components.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first solution for the relativistic light-front wave function of $^3$He using the explicitly covariant LFD framework, but the numerical results are preliminary and obtained via a single iteration starting from the non-relativistic solution, meaning the reported relativistic wave functions are approximate rather than fully self-consistent solutions of the three-body equation.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body light-front Faddeev equation in momentum space using one-meson exchange kernels (pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector mesons) without the potential approximation, with parameters taken from the deuteron calculation of Carbonell (1995) and Machleidt (1987). The wave function is decomposed into 32 spin-isospin scalar functions using an orthogonal basis of Dirac spinor structures, and the integral equation is solved iteratively: starting from the non-relativistic wave function of Baru (2003), one iteration produces approximate relativistic scalar functions.

## Key numbers

- The full $^3$He LF wave function comprises **32 spin-isospin components** (16 for pair isospin t=0 and 16 for t=1)
- Each spin-isospin component depends on **five independent scalars**: $|\vec{R}_{1\perp}|$, $|\vec{R}_{2\perp}|$, $\vec{R}_{1\perp}\cdot\vec{R}_{2\perp}$, $x_1$, $x_2$
- The bound state has total angular momentum $J=1/2$, total isospin $1/2$, and positive parity
- The three-body kinetic energy is ${\cal M}^2 = (\vec{R}_{1\perp}^2 + m^2)/x_1 + (\vec{R}_{2\perp}^2 + m^2)/x_2 + (\vec{R}_{3\perp}^2 + m^2)/x_3$
- The exchanged mesons include $\pi$, $\eta$, $\delta$, $\sigma_0$, $\sigma_1$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ mesons with parameters from Carbonell (1995) and Machleidt (1987)
- In the non-relativistic domain, **five components dominate** and closely resemble their non-relativistic counterparts
- The two dominant relativistic components are $\psi_{\text{r},1}^{(1)}$ and $\psi_{\text{r},2}^{(0)}$

## Context

This work extends the explicitly covariant LFD framework previously applied to the deuteron by Carbonell (1995) to the three-body $^3$He system, providing the first relativistic wave function solution for a three-nucleon bound state in this formalism. It contrasts with the approach of Polyzou (various years) for Poincare-invariant three-body states and with the harmonic oscillator basis decomposition used by Siqi (2023) for the nucleon wave function, which does not separate covariant spin structures. The results enable future calculations of $^3$He electromagnetic form factors, momentum distributions, and other observables, and the method is stated to be applicable to any three-fermion system including the nucleon and to $^4$He.
