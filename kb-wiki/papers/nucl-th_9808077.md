# Hadron Probing of the Deuteron Structure at Short Distances in Deuteron Breakup Reactions

> We discuss deuteron $A(d,p)X$ breakup and cumulative pion production $A(d,π)X$ in the framework of constituent quark model of the deuteron. We demonstrate that consideration of the Pauli principle at the quark level, as well as multiple scattering, affect drastically cross section and polarization observables of these reactions and provide good description of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that including the Pauli principle at the constituent quark level and multiple scattering effects drastically changes cross sections and polarization observables in deuteron breakup reactions, and that these effects provide a good description of experimental data, particularly at high internal momenta in the deuteron.

## Method

The authors used a one-channel Resonating Group Method (RGM) approach where the deuteron wave function is antisymmetrized over six constituent quarks, decomposing into nucleon-nucleon, Delta-Delta, and nucleon-resonance components. Multiple scattering effects were incorporated using the Bertocchi-Treleani model, and calculations were performed with the Nijm-I potential for the deuteron distribution function.

## Key numbers

- The tensor analyzing power T20 and polarization transfer kappa0 were calculated for 0-degree inclusive 12C(d,p) breakup as a function of the relativistic internal momentum k in the deuteron, with results shown in Figures 1 and 2.
- The differential cross section I = (E/2) d^3 sigma / d^3 p and tensor analyzing power T20 were calculated for 0-degree cumulative pion production A(d,pi)X as a function of pion momentum q in the deuteron rest frame, shown in Figure 3.
- The Delta-Delta component has the largest probability among the non-nucleonic components of the deuteron, but does not contribute to (d,p) breakup.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach to deuteron structure developed by Glozman and Kuchina (1990) and the Resonating Group Method framework. It addresses the short-distance deuteron structure where quarks and gluons must be explicitly considered, building on earlier experimental data from Ableev (1983), Kuehn, Baldin, and Afanasiev. The results enable further study of P-wave components in the deuteron wave function and suggest that the Delta-Delta component provides a dominant contribution to cumulative pion production mechanisms.
