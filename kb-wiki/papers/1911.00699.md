# Beyond the charge radius: the information content of the fourth radial moment

> Measurements of atomic transitions in different isotopes offer key information on the nuclear charge radius. The anticipated high-precision experimental techniques, augmented by atomic calculations, will soon enable extraction of the higher-order radial moments of the charge density distribution. To assess the value of such measurements for nuclear structure research, we study the information content of the fourth radial moment $\rf$ by means of nuclear density functional theory and a multiple correlation analysis. We show that $\rf$ can be directly related to the surface thickness of nuclear density, a fundamental property of the atomic nucleus that is difficult to obtain for radioactive systems. Precise knowledge of these radial moments is essential to establish reliable constraints on the existence of new forces from precision isotope shift measurements .

## Key claim

The fourth radial moment ⟨r⁴⟩ of the nuclear charge distribution can be directly related to the surface thickness σ of nuclear density, but this relationship is most reliable for heavy nuclei and requires corrections for halo effects in proton-rich isotopes; the paper shows that for nuclei where precise values of the diffraction radius R, surface thickness σ, and rms charge radius r₂ are already available, the values of ⟨r⁴⟩ are fully constrained.

## Method

The authors employed nuclear density functional theory using two energy density functionals (the Skyrme parametrization SV-min and the Fayans functional Fy(Δr,HFB)) to compute charge density form parameters for isotopic chains of Ca, Sn, and Pb. They performed a multiple correlation analysis using coefficients of determination (CoD) and multiple correlation coefficients (MCC) to quantify the statistical relationships between the fourth radial moment ⟨r⁴⟩ and the standard form parameters (diffraction radius R, surface thickness σ, and rms radius r₂), and complemented this with an analytic Helm model analysis to derive closed-form expressions relating these quantities.

## Key numbers

- The adopted model uncertainties (reflecting model capability to reproduce observables) are ±0.04 fm for the diffraction radius R and surface thickness σ, and ±0.02 fm for the rms charge radius r₂.
- The combination of R and σ provides a 95% estimate of ⟨r⁴⟩ for spherical nuclei.
- The deviation between the Helm-model prediction R^(H) and the exact diffraction radius R is around 0.02 fm, smaller than the adopted error of 0.04 fm.
- The surface thickness σ is predicted from r₂ and ⟨r⁴⟩ at a 90% level for spherical nuclei.
- The ratio σ²/R² is around 0.02–0.03 for the nuclei studied.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear charge density form parameters previously studied by Mizutori (2000) and others, and builds on the Helm model originally developed by Helm (1956). The paper directly addresses the need identified by Papoulia (2016) and Ekman (2019) for higher-order radial moments to interpret precision isotope shift measurements. The results enable future experiments at radioactive ion beam facilities to extract surface thickness and diffraction radii for unstable nuclei where electron scattering is impractical, and provide constraints for improving nuclear energy density functionals.
