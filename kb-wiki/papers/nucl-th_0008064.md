# From Hadrons to Nuclei: Crossing the Border

> The study of nuclei predates by many years the theory of quantum chromodynamics. More recently, effective field theories have been used in nuclear physics to ``cross the border'' from QCD to a nuclear theory. We are now entering the second decade of efforts to develop a perturbative theory of nuclear interactions using effective field theory. This work describes the current status of these efforts.

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) can systematically describe low-energy nuclear interactions by starting from QCD symmetries, but the presence of shallow bound states like the deuteron requires a non-trivial renormalization-group fixed point and a modified power counting (Q-counting) rather than naive dimensional analysis, because the unnaturally large scattering lengths (e.g., \(a = -23.714\ \mathrm{fm}\) in the singlet channel) cannot arise from natural-sized couplings.

## Method

The authors construct an EFT for two-nucleon systems at momenta below the pion mass, using a Lagrangian with only nucleon fields and local four-nucleon contact interactions. They sum the bubble-chain of Feynman diagrams to all orders, employing Power Divergence Subtraction (PDS) to handle linear divergences and maintain a consistent power counting (Q-counting) where the subtraction scale \(\mu\) is taken of order the external momentum. The EFT is matched to the effective range expansion for \(NN\) scattering, and the same framework is extended to include pions as explicit degrees of freedom at higher energies, following Weinberg's approach of applying chiral perturbation theory to the \(NN\) potential.

## Key numbers

*   \(a = -23.714\ \mathrm{fm}\) is the scattering length in the \(^1S_0\) channel, with effective range \(r_0 = 2.73\ \mathrm{fm}\) and shape parameter \(r_1 = -0.48\ \mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   In the \(^3S_1\) channel, the deuteron binding momentum is \(\gamma = 45.7025\ \mathrm{MeV} = (4.318946\ \mathrm{fm})^{-1}\), with effective range \(\rho_d = 1.764\ \mathrm{fm}\) and shape parameter \(w_2 = 0.389\ \mathrm{fm}^3\).
*   The deuteron binding energy is \(B = 2.224644 \pm 0.000034\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
*   The pion mass is \(m_\pi \approx 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and the chiral symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda_\chi \sim 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
*   The expansion of \(|{\bf k}|\cot\delta\) converges within a radius set by the pion cut at \(|{\bf k}| = m_\pi/2\).

## Context

This work reviews and extends the program of applying effective field theory to nuclear physics, building on Bethe's Effective Range Theory (1940s) and Weinberg's pioneering efforts (1990-1992) to set up a systematic EFT for multi-nucleon systems. It addresses unresolved formal issues in cutoff EFT approaches (e.g., those of Lepage (1997) and others) by developing the PDS scheme and Q-counting, which provide a regulator-independent power counting for systems with shallow bound states. The results enable systematic, perturbative calculations of low-energy \(NN\) scattering and electroweak processes (e.g., \(np \rightarrow d\gamma\)) to \(\sim 1\%\) accuracy, and lay groundwork for extending EFT to three-nucleon systems and larger nuclei.
