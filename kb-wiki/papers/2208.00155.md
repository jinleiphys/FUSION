# Hydrodynamic simulations of directed flow for light hadrons in Au+Au and isobar collisions $\sqrt{s_{\textrm{NN}}}=$ 200 GeV

> Using a (3+1)-D hydrodynamic model CLVisc, we study the directed flow ($v_{1}$) of light hadrons produced in Au+Au, Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\textrm{NN}}}=$ 200 GeV. The evolution of tilted energy density, pressure gradient and radial flow along the $x$-direction are systematically investigated. Counter-clockwise tilt of initial fireball is shown to be a vital source of the directed flow for final light hadrons. A good description of directed flow is provided for light hadrons in central and mid-central Au+Au and isobar collisions at RHIC. Our numerical results show a clear system size dependence for light hadron $v_{1}$ across different collision systems. We further study the effect of nuclear structure on the directed flow and find that the $v_{1}$ for light hadrons is insensitive to the nuclei with quadrupole deformation.

## Key claim

Using a (3+1)-D hydrodynamic model CLVisc with a tilted initial condition, the directed flow of light hadrons in Au+Au and isobar collisions at 200 GeV is well described, but the authors find that the directed flow is insensitive to the quadrupole deformation of the nuclei, with the system size dependence being the dominant factor.

## Method

The authors employed the (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc with a tilted initial energy density profile constructed from a Woods-Saxon distribution for Au, Ru, and Zr nuclei. The initial condition included a deformation mechanism via a weight function with a tilt parameter H_t extracted from experimental data, and the hydrodynamic evolution used the lattice QCD equation of state from the Wuppertal-Budapest group (2014) with shear viscosity ratio eta_v/s = 0.08 and freeze-out temperature T_frz = 137 MeV. The Cooper-Frye mechanism was used to calculate hadron spectra on the freeze-out hypersurface, with resonance decay contributions included.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity ratio set to eta_v/s = 0.08 for all collision systems
- Freeze-out temperature set to T_frz = 137 MeV
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section sigma_NN = 42 mb at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Initial proper time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c for all collision systems
- Normalization factor K = 35.5 GeV/fm^3 for Au+Au and K = 23.0 GeV/fm^3 for both Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr
- Impact parameter b = 2.4 fm for Au+Au and b = 2.1 fm for both Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr in central collisions
- Tilt parameter H_t = 1.0 for all three systems in central collisions; for 50-80% centrality, H_t = 4.5 for Au+Au, H_t = 3.9 for Ru+Ru, and H_t = 3.1 for Zr+Zr
- Nuclear radius R_0 = 6.38 fm for Au, 5.067 fm for Ru, and 4.965 fm for Zr
- Surface diffusiveness d = 0.535 fm for Au, 0.500 fm for Ru, and 0.556 fm for Zr
- Quadrupole deformation beta_2 = 0.0 for all three nuclei in the default case
- The difference in v_1 between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr is less than 0.001 within |y| < 1.5

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of directed flow using the CLVisc model by Jiang et al. (2021, 2022) and applies the framework to isobar collisions (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr) at RHIC energies. The study is motivated by the STAR Collaboration's isobar run (2021) which found differences in particle yields and elliptic flow between Ru and Zr, and the authors investigate whether nuclear structure differences affect directed flow. The results enable future studies incorporating event-by-event fluctuations and more detailed nuclear structure parameters, and the authors note that the Monte Carlo Glauber model would enhance directed flow at large forward and backward rapidity.
