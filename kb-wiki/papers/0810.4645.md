# Light nuclei quasiparticle energy shift in hot and dense nuclear matter

> Nuclei in dense matter are influenced by the medium. In the cluster mean field approximation, an effective Schrödinger equation for the $A$-particle cluster is obtained accounting for the effects of the correlated medium such as self-energy, Pauli blocking and Bose enhancement. Similar to the single-baryon states (free neutrons and protons), the light elements ($2 \le A \le 4$, internal quantum state $ν$) are treated as quasiparticles with energies $E_{A,ν}(\vec P; T, n_n,n_p)$. These energies depend on the center of mass momentum $\vec P$, as well as temperature $T$ and the total densities $n_n,n_p$ of neutrons and protons, respectively. No $β$ equilibrium is considered so that $n_n, n_p$ (or the corresponding chemical potentials $μ_n, μ_p$) are fixed independently. For the single nucleon quasiparticle energy shift, different approximate expressions such as Skyrme or relativistic mean field approaches are well known. Treating the $A$-particle problem in appropriate approximations, results for the cluster quasiparticle shifts are given. Properties of dense nuclear matter at moderate temperatures in the subsaturation density region considered here are influenced by the composition. This in turn is determined by the cluster quasiparticle energies, in particular the formation of clusters at low densities when the temperature decreases, and their dissolution due to Pauli blocking as the density increases. Our finite-temperature Green function approach covers different limiting cases: The low-density region where the model of nuclear statistical equilibrium and virial expansions can be applied, and the saturation density region where a mean field approach is possible.

## Key claim

Light nuclei with mass numbers 2 ≤ A ≤ 4 can be treated as quasiparticles with medium-dependent energies E_{A,ν}(P; T, n_n, n_p) in hot and dense nuclear matter, but the results are obtained within a cluster mean field approximation using perturbation theory that is justified only in the low-density region, and no β-equilibrium is considered so that neutron and proton densities are fixed independently.

## Method

The author starts from a nonrelativistic Hamiltonian with a nucleon-nucleon interaction and uses a finite-temperature Green function approach to derive an in-medium Schrödinger equation for A-nucleon clusters. The cluster quasiparticle energy shifts are calculated using perturbation theory with Gaussian and Jastrow variational wave functions, incorporating self-energy shifts from single-nucleon quasiparticle energies and Pauli blocking terms from the surrounding medium. The single-nucleon quasiparticle shifts themselves are treated using either Skyrme or relativistic mean field parametrizations, while the cluster shifts are evaluated for deuteron, triton, helion, and alpha particle.

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach previously applied to the two-nucleon system (deuteron) by Röpke and collaborators (SRS, ARSKK) to light clusters with A=3 and A=4, using the cluster mean field approximation. It bridges the low-density nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) description, where bound states are treated as new species via mass action laws, and the high-density mean field approaches such as Skyrme (Vautherin, 1972) and relativistic mean field (Typel, Fuchss) that describe nucleon quasiparticles near saturation density. The results enable calculation of the nuclear matter equation of state via a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formula that includes cluster quasiparticle energies, and can be applied to describe the composition of warm dense matter in astrophysical contexts such as supernovae.
