# On dissociation of heavy mesons in a hot quark-gluon plasma

> We compare two mechanisms for the dissociation of heavy mesons in an infinite quark-gluon plasma: dynamic Debye screening and multiple scattering. Using the uncertainty principle inspired by a Schrodinger-like equation, we find that the criterion a_B\simeq1/μ\simeq 1/(α_{eff}^{1/2}T) with α_{eff}\equivα(N_c+{N_f\over2}) is parametrically true both for the dissociation of fast moving heavy mesons with a size a_B due to dynamic Debye screening as well as for mesons at rest in the medium. In contrast, we find that the criterion for the dissociation of heavy mesons due to uncorrelated multiple scattering is parametrically 1/a_B \simeq [γα_{eff}\ln{1/α_{eff}}]^{1/3}T. Therefore, multiple scattering is a more efficient mechanism for the dissociation of heavy mesons in an infinite hot plasma.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for heavy mesons in an infinite quark-gluon plasma, the dissociation criterion due to multiple scattering is parametrically \(a_B \simeq 1/[\gamma\alpha_{eff}\ln(1/\alpha_{eff})]^{1/3}T\), while the criterion due to dynamic Debye screening is \(a_B \simeq 1/\mu \simeq 1/(\alpha_{eff}^{1/2}T)\); the authors conclude that multiple scattering is a more efficient dissociation mechanism, but note that this conclusion is parametric and derived under the assumption of an infinite plasma with perturbative coupling.

## Method

The authors compare two dissociation mechanisms for heavy mesons in an infinite hot quark-gluon plasma: dynamic Debye screening and uncorrelated multiple scattering. For screening, they derive a Schrodinger-like equation in light-cone gauge from the Dirac equation, compute the effective gluon field using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation for the photon polarization vector, and apply the uncertainty principle to estimate the dissociation criterion. For multiple scattering, they identify the intrinsic time scale \(\tau_B = \gamma/E_B\) of the bound state and use the saturation momentum \(Q_s\) as the natural scale for transverse momentum broadening, setting the dissociation condition as \(Q_s \simeq 1/a_B\).

## Key numbers

- The screening dissociation criterion is parametrically \(a_B \simeq 1/\mu\) with \(\mu^2 = \frac{1}{6}g^2T^2(N_c + N_f/2)\); the numerical condition for a bound state to exist is \(\mu a_B \equiv \mu/(\alpha C_F m_a) \leq 0.84\).
- The multiple scattering dissociation criterion is parametrically \(a_B \simeq 1/[\gamma\alpha_{eff}\ln(1/\alpha_{eff})]^{1/3}T\), where \(\alpha_{eff} \equiv \alpha(N_c + N_f/2)\).
- For the charm quark, the condition to neglect the confining linear potential is \(m_a^2 \gg 0.2/(\alpha C_F)^3\) GeV\(^2\); with \(m_c^2 \simeq 1.6\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\alpha(m_c)=0.4\), this gives \(0.2/(\alpha C_F)^3 \simeq 1.3\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the bottom quark, the same condition gives \(0.2/(\alpha C_F)^3 \simeq 10.5\) GeV\(^2\) with \(m_b^2 \simeq 22\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\alpha(m_b)=0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the classic analysis of Matsui and Satz (1986) on \(J/\psi\) suppression due to Debye screening in a quark-gluon plasma, where the dissociation criterion for a meson at rest was \(r_{J/\psi} = 1.61 r_D\). It also builds on AdS/CFT calculations by Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006) showing that the screening length for a moving heavy quarkonium in strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM plasma scales as \(r_D(v,T) \sim r_D(0,T)/\sqrt{\gamma}\). The paper enables future work to determine which dissociation mechanism dominates in realistic, finite-size plasmas produced in heavy-ion collisions, and to refine the parametric estimates with full numerical solutions.
