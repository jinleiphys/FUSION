# Implementation of chromomagnetic gluons in Yang-Mills thermodynamics

> Motivated by the recent high-precision lattice data on Yang-Mills equations of state, we propose an effective theory of SU(3) gluonic matter. The theory is constructed based on the center and scale symmetries and their dynamical breaking, so that the interplay between color-electric and color-magnetic gluons is included coherently. We suggest, that the magnetic gluon condensate changes its thermal behavior qualitatively above the critical temperature, as a consequence of the matching to dimensionally-reduced magnetic theories. We consider thermodynamics in the mean field approximation and discuss the properties and interpretation of the trace anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an effective theory of SU(3) gluonic matter where the magnetic gluon condensate changes its thermal behavior qualitatively above the critical temperature, as a consequence of matching to dimensionally-reduced magnetic theories; this changeover is estimated to occur at approximately 2.4 times the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors construct an effective potential combining a dilaton field (representing the chromomagnetic sector via scale symmetry breaking), a Polyakov loop (representing the chromoelectric sector via center symmetry breaking), and a mixing term between them. They solve the coupled gap equations in the mean field approximation and impose a matching condition at high temperature where the dilaton condensate is replaced by the magnetic gluon condensate derived from three-dimensional Yang-Mills theory. The interaction measure is then computed from the resulting thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement critical temperature is set to \(T_c = 270\) MeV, reproducing lattice results.
- The bag constant is \(B = (0.368\ \text{GeV})^4\) and the vacuum glueball mass is \(M_\chi = 1.7\) GeV.
- The vacuum energy density is \(\mathcal{E} = \frac{1}{4}B = 0.6\) GeV fm\(^{-3}\).
- The dilaton vacuum expectation value parameter is \(\chi_0 = 0.16\) GeV.
- The model parameters fixed at \(T_c\) are \(a_0 = (0.184\ \text{GeV})^3\), \(c_0 = (0.244\ \text{GeV})^4\), and \(G = (0.206\ \text{GeV})^4\).
- The matching temperature between the dilaton and the magnetic condensate is \(T_{\text{match}} \sim 2.4\,T_c\).
- The magnetic condensate normalization constant is \(H_0 = (0.8\ \text{GeV})^4\).
- For SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, the spatial string tension coefficient is \(c_\sigma = 0.566\) and the magnetic gluon mass coefficient is \(c_m = 0.491\).
- The Polyakov loop expectation value at \(T_c\) is \(\langle\Phi\rangle = 0.4\).

## Context

This work extends effective descriptions of Yang-Mills thermodynamics that incorporate both center symmetry (via the Polyakov loop) and scale symmetry (via the dilaton), following the approach of Sannino (2002) and earlier dilaton models by Schechter (1980). It directly addresses the plateau in the interaction measure \(I(T)/T^2T_c^2\) observed in high-precision lattice data (Bazavov et al., 2009; Borsanyi et al., 2012), which cannot be explained by perturbative Hard Thermal Loop resummation alone. The proposed matching to three-dimensional Yang-Mills theory (Kajantie et al., 1997; Agasian, 2003) provides a mechanism for the residual non-perturbative contribution at high temperature, and the framework can be extended to include quarks for a full QCD effective description.
