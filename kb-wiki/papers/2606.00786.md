# Proton High-Order Cumulants in Au+Au Collisions at High Baryon Density from JAM with a Centrality-Independent Framework

> The event-by-event higher-order cumulants of conserved quantities such as net-baryon, net-electric charge, and net-strangeness in heavy-ion collisions have been extensively utilized in experimental searches for the QCD critical point, notably in the RHIC-STAR experiment. In this study, we conduct a systematic analysis of higher-order cumulants of proton number distributions in Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 3.2$, $3.5$, $3.9$, and $4.5$ GeV using the JAM model. We calculate cumulants, factorial cumulants, and their ratios using a novel method, Centrality-Independent Genuine Cumulant Analysis fRamework (CIGAR), which effectively eliminates initial volume fluctuations. We comprehensively compare the CIGAR method with the traditional Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC) method. In addition, the effect of spectators on cumulant is systematically investigated. Our results provide a dynamic non-critical baseline in the high-baryon-density regime which is crucial for QCD critical point searches in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The JAM model provides a non-critical baseline for proton high-order cumulants at high baryon density, but the results are conditional on the method used: the novel CIGAR framework effectively eliminates initial volume fluctuations that the traditional CBWC method fails to remove at low collision energies, and spectator nucleon contributions significantly affect cumulant ratios at lower energies and wider rapidity windows.

## Method

Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 3.2, 3.5, 3.9, and 4.5 GeV were simulated using the JAM hadronic transport model in standard cascade mode, with impact parameters from 0 to 14 fm and pseudorapidity coverage matching the RHIC-STAR fixed-target detector acceptance (-2.4 < η < 0). Proton cumulants up to fourth order, factorial cumulants, and their ratios were calculated using the CIGAR method, which reconstructs the particle number distribution via Edgeworth expansion with parameters optimized through differential evolution and Bayesian inference, and compared with results from the traditional CBWC method using both RefMult3 and N_part as centrality references.

## Key numbers

*   Statistics: 44–61 million events per energy, with 49–58 million events including spectator contributions (Table 1).
*   Proton yield in 0–5% central collisions at mid-rapidity: approximately 70 at √s_NN = 3.2 GeV, decreasing to approximately 60 at √s_NN = 4.5 GeV (Figure 2).
*   C_4 exhibits a maximum around N_part ~ 150–200 across all energies, with saturation and suppression in more central collisions (Figure 4).
*   Factorial cumulant ratios κ_2/κ_1, κ_3/κ_1, and κ_4/κ_1 are approximately an order of magnitude smaller than ordinary cumulant ratios and remain close to zero across all energies (Figure 6).
*   Spectator removal lowers C_4/C_2 in both central and peripheral collisions, with the reduction most pronounced at 3.2 GeV and diminishing to negligible levels at 4.5 GeV (Figure 6).

## Context

This work extends the non-critical baseline calculations for QCD critical point searches in heavy-ion collisions, complementing experimental results from the RHIC-STAR Collaboration (STAR, 2021; STAR, 2025) that show non-monotonic net-proton C_4/C_2 ratios at higher energies. The CIGAR method, introduced by Wang et al. (2025), is validated against the traditional CBWC method (Luo, 2013) and shown to be essential for low-energy collisions where volume fluctuations are severe. The results provide a reference for comparisons with upcoming data from the RHIC-STAR fixed-target program at √s_NN = 3.2–7.7 GeV (Sweger, 2025) and the future CBM experiment at FAIR at √s_NN = 2.4–4.9 GeV (CBM, 2023).
