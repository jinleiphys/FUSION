# Cluster structure of 20Ne: Evidence for D(3h) symmetry

> We study the cluster structure of 20Ne and show that the available experimental data can be well described by a bi-pyramidal structure with D(3h) symmetry. Strong evidence for the occurrence of this symmetry comes from the observation of all nine expected vibrational modes (3 singly degenerate and 3 doubly degenerate) and of six (singly degenerate) double vibrational modes. 20Ne appears to be another example of the simplicity in complexity program, in which simple spectroscopic features arise out of a complex many-body system.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the available experimental data on \(^{20}\)Ne can be well described by a bi-pyramidal cluster structure with \(D_{3h}\) symmetry, but they note that the rotational motion is not rigid and that the classical calculation of moments of inertia may not be appropriate, requiring a quenching factor of \(\eta = 0.76\) to match experiment.

## Method

The authors model \(^{20}\)Ne as five \(\alpha\)-particles in a bi-pyramidal configuration with \(D_{3h}\) symmetry, using three length scales (\(\beta_1\), \(\beta_2\), \(\beta_3\)) and three size scales (\(\alpha_1\), \(\alpha_2\), \(\alpha_3\)), with \(\beta_2 = \beta_3\) and \(\alpha_2 = \alpha_3\) for the symmetric case. They calculate ground state properties (moments of inertia, form factors, electromagnetic transition rates, charge radius) from the density distribution and compare them to experimental data, then analyze the vibrational spectrum using group theory to identify all nine expected normal modes and six double vibrational bands.

## Key numbers

- Elastic form factor minimum at \(q_{\min} = 1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\) used to fix \(\beta_1 = 1.80\) fm and \(\beta_2 = 3.00\) fm.
- Calculated intrinsic quadrupole moment \(Q_0 = 52.5\) efm\(^2\); experimental values are \(80.5(10.5)\) efm\(^2\) and \(57.3(12.7)\) efm\(^2\) from two different measurements.
- Calculated \(B(E2;0^+ \rightarrow 2^+) = 275\) e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) compared to experimental \(327(16)\) e\(^2\)fm\(^4\).
- Calculated r.m.s. charge radius \(\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 2.89\) fm compared to experimental \(3.004(25)\) fm.
- Rigid moments of inertia: \(I_z/m = 76.6\) fm\(^2\), \(I_x/m = I_y/m = 129.2\) fm\(^2\); experimental \(B_x = B_y = 0.212\) MeV from the \(4-0\) energy difference.
- Calculated \(B(E4;0^+ \rightarrow 4^+) = 8.6 \times 10^4\) e\(^2\)fm\(^8\) and hexadecapole moment \(H_4 = 347\) efm\(^4\) compared to experimental \(H_4 = 220(30)\) efm\(^4\).
- Calculated \(B(E3;0^+ \rightarrow 3^-) = 210.3\) e\(^2\)fm\(^6\) (no experimental value available).

## Context

This work extends the study of cluster structures in \(k\alpha\) nuclei from \(^{12}\)C (triangular \(D_{3h}\) symmetry, studied by Bijker and Iachello) and \(^{16}\)O (tetrahedral \(T_d\) symmetry) to \(^{20}\)Ne (\(k=5\)). It builds on earlier Brink-Bloch model calculations by Brink (1965) and Weiguny (1970s), and compares with shell model calculations by Wong and Hartree-Fock results. The identification of all nine vibrational modes and six double vibrational bands provides strong evidence for \(D_{3h}\) symmetry, enabling future studies of cluster structures in heavier \(sd\)-shell nuclei.
