# Constraining Hadronic Superfluidity with Neutron Star Precession

> I show that the standard picture of the neutron star core containing coexisting neutron and proton superfluids, with the proton component forming a type II superconductor threaded by flux tubes, is inconsistent with observations of long-period (~1 yr) precession in isolated pulsars. I conclude that either the two superfluids coexist nowhere in the stellar core, or the core is a type I superconductor rather than type II. Either possibility would have interesting implications for neutron star cooling and theories of spin jumps (glitches).

## Key claim

The standard picture of the neutron star core containing coexisting neutron and proton superfluids, with the proton component forming a type II superconductor threaded by flux tubes, is inconsistent with observations of long-period (~1 yr) precession in isolated pulsars; either the two superfluids coexist nowhere in the stellar core, or the core is a type I superconductor rather than type II.

## Method

The author calculates the precession dynamics of a neutron star whose core contains a neutron superfluid with quantized vortex lines entangled in a proton type II superconductor's flux tube array. The Euler equations are solved for a model where the neutron fluid's angular momentum is pinned to the charged body (crust plus charged core fluid) through vortex-flux tube interactions, and the resulting precession frequency and damping timescale are compared to observations of PSR B1828-11. The dissipation rate from vortices cutting through flux tubes is estimated using first-order perturbation theory for kelvon excitation.

## Key numbers

- Precession period of PSR B1828-11: ~1 yr, with amplitude ~3 degrees (from observations by Stairs et al. 2000, Cordes 1993, Shabanova 1995)
- Fractional distortion of the star: epsilon ~ 10^-8, implied by the measured precession period
- Vortex areal density: n_v ~ 10^4 P(s)^{-1} cm^{-2}, with average spacing l_v ~ 10^{-2} P(s) cm
- Flux tube areal density: n_Phi ~ 10^19 B_{12} cm^{-2}, with average spacing l_Phi ~ 3000 B_{12}^{-1/2} fm
- Energy per vortex-flux tube intersection: E_int ~ 5 MeV, with range ~ Lambda_p ~ 80 fm
- Pinning force per intersection: F_p ~ 0.1 MeV fm^{-1}, corresponding to force per unit length f_p ~ 3 x 10^15 B_{12}^{1/2} dyn cm^{-1}
- Characteristic cyclotron-vortex wave frequency: omega_{cv,0} ~ 10 rad s^{-1}
- Precession frequency with pinned neutron fluid: Omega_p ~ (I_n/I_1) Omega_c ~ 10 Omega_c, independent of angles
- Dissipation rate from vortex cutting: dE/dt ~ 10^41 erg s^{-1}
- Excess rotational energy: Delta E_rot ~ 2 x 10^44 erg
- Characteristic damping time: tau_d ~ 1 hr

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of periodic timing behavior in PSR B1828-11 as precession (Link and Epstein 2001, Jones and Andersson 2001, Rezania 2002) and uses it to constrain the interior composition of neutron stars. The standard picture of coexisting neutron and proton superfluids in the outer core, based on pairing calculations by Hoffberg et al. (1970), Chao et al. (1972), Amundsen and Ostgaard (1985), Wambach et al. (1993), Takatsuka and Tamagaki (1993), Elgaroy et al. (1996), and others, is shown to be incompatible with the observations. The result enables new constraints on neutron star cooling models and glitch theories, suggesting that either glitch models relying on vortex-flux tube interactions (such as Ruderman, Zhu, and Chen 1998) must be replaced by inner crust mechanisms, or that URCA reactions may be significantly enhanced in macroscopic normal regions of the core.
