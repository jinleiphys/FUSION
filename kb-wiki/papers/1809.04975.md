# Nonperturbative strange-quark sea from lattice QCD, light-front holography, and meson-baryon fluctuation models

> We demonstrate that a nonzero strangeness contribution to the spacelike electromagnetic form factor of the nucleon is evidence for a strange-antistrange asymmetry in the nucleon's light-front wave function, thus implying different nonperturbative contributions to the strange and antistrange quark distribution functions. A recent lattice QCD calculation of the nucleon strange quark form factor predicts that the strange quark distribution is more centralized in coordinate space than the antistrange quark distribution, and thus the strange quark distribution is more spread out in light-front momentum space. We show that the lattice prediction implies that the difference between the strange and antistrange parton distribution functions, $s(x)-\bar{s}(x)$, is negative at small-$x$ and positive at large-$x$. We also evaluate the strange quark form factor and $s(x)-\bar{s}(x)$ using a baryon-meson fluctuation model and a novel nonperturbative model based on light-front holographic QCD. This procedure leads to a Veneziano-like expression of the form factor, which depends exclusively on the twist of the hadron and the properties of the Regge trajectory of the vector meson which couples to the quark current in the hadron. The holographic structure of the model allows us to introduce unambiguously quark masses in the form factors and quark distributions preserving the hard scattering counting rule at large-$Q^2$ and the inclusive counting rule at large-$x$. Quark masses modify the Regge intercept which governs the small-$x$ behavior of quark distributions, therefore modifying their small-$x$ singular behavior. Both nonperturbative approaches provide descriptions of the strange-antistrange asymmetry and intrinsic strangeness in the nucleon consistent with the lattice QCD result.

## Key claim

A nonzero strangeness contribution to the nucleon's spacelike electromagnetic form factor implies a strange-antistrange asymmetry in the nucleon's light-front wave function, leading to different nonperturbative contributions to the strange and antistrange quark distribution functions; the lattice QCD result that the strange quark distribution is more centralized in coordinate space than the antistrange quark distribution implies that the difference $s(x)-\bar{s}(x)$ is negative at small-$x$ and positive at large-$x$.

## Method

The authors used lattice QCD results for the strange quark form factor $F_1^s(Q^2)$ from previous continuum-limit calculations (Sufian 2016, 2017) and compared them with two nonperturbative models: a baryon-meson fluctuation model where the proton fluctuates to a $K^+\Lambda^0$ state, and a light-front holographic QCD (LFHQCD) model based on the Veneziano-like Beta function structure of form factors. The baryon-meson fluctuation model used Brodsky-Huang-Lepage light-front wave functions with Gaussian transverse momentum dependence, while the LFHQCD approach expressed the form factor as $F_\tau(t) = B(\tau-1, 1-\alpha(t))/N_\tau$ where $\tau$ is the twist and $\alpha(t)$ is the Regge trajectory of the vector meson coupling to the quark current.

## Key numbers

- The fluctuation model fit to lattice QCD data yielded $\beta = 0.31(11)\,\text{GeV}$ and $I_s = 1.06(51)\%$ for the intrinsic strange quark number
- The original fluctuation model parameters used $m_q = 0.330\,\text{GeV}$, $m_s = 0.480\,\text{GeV}$, $m_D = 0.600\,\text{GeV}$, and $\beta = 0.330\,\text{GeV}$
- The universal momentum scale $\beta$ was constrained by extended observables to the range $0.24 < \beta < 0.37\,\text{GeV}$ (Zhang 2016)
- The fluctuation probability $I_s = 1.27\%$ from Cao (1999) was used for initial calculations
- The LFHQCD form factor at large $Q^2$ scales as $F_\tau(Q^2) = \Gamma(\tau-1)(1/\alpha' Q^2)^{\tau-1}$

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon strange quark form factor (Sufian 2016, 2017) and prior phenomenological studies of the $s(x)-\bar{s}(x)$ asymmetry using baryon-meson fluctuation models (Signal 1987, Cao 1999, Brodsky 1996, Melnitchouk 1999). The LFHQCD approach builds on the semiclassical bound state equations from holographic QCD (Brodsky 2006, de Teramond 2008, 2013) and provides a framework where quark masses can be introduced while preserving the hard scattering counting rules at large $Q^2$ and the inclusive counting rule at large $x$. The results enable constraints on the strange-antistrange asymmetry that can be tested against global PDF analyses such as NNPDF3.0 (Ball 2014), MMHT2014 (Harland-Lang 2014), and JR14 (Jimenez-Delgado 2014).
