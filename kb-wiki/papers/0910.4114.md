# Jet Propagation and Mach-Cone Formation in (3+1)-dimensional Ideal Hydrodynamics

> This thesis investigates the jet-medium interactions in a Quark-Gluon Plasma using a hydrodynamical model. It deals with the creation of Mach cones which are supposed to exhibit a characteristic structure in the measured angular particle distributions allowing for direct conclusions about the Equation of State and in particular about the speed of sound of the medium. Several different scenarios of jet energy loss are examined and different mechanisms of energy and momentum loss are analyzed, ranging from weak interactions (based on calculations from perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics, pQCD) to strong interactions (formulated using the Anti-de-Sitter/Conformal Field Theory Correspondence, AdS/CFT). Though they result in different angular particle correlations which could in principle allow to distinguish the underlying processes, it is shown that the characteristic structure observed in experimental data can be obtained due to the different contributions of several possible jet trajectories through an expanding medium. Such a structure cannot directly be connected to the Equation of State. In this context, the impact of a strong flow created behind the jet (the diffusion wake) is examined which is common to almost all jet deposition scenarios. Besides that, the transport equations for dissipative hydrodynamics are discussed which are fundamental for any numerical computation of viscous effects in a Quark-Gluon Plasma.

## Key claim

The thesis shows that the characteristic conical structure observed in experimental angular particle correlations cannot be directly connected to the Equation of State or the speed of sound of the medium, because the observed pattern arises from the superposition of several possible jet trajectories through an expanding medium rather than from a single Mach-cone signature.

## Method

The author performed (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations of jet propagation through a Quark-Gluon Plasma, implementing energy and momentum deposition from jets using two different microscopic models: weak-coupling energy loss based on perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (pQCD) and strong-coupling energy loss formulated via the Anti-de-Sitter/Conformal Field Theory Correspondence (AdS/CFT). The resulting flow patterns and angular particle correlations were analyzed for different jet trajectories in an expanding medium, with particular attention to the diffusion wake behind the jet.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies of Mach-cone formation in heavy-ion collisions by examining the role of multiple jet trajectories and comparing pQCD-based and AdS/CFT-based energy-loss mechanisms. The thesis also discusses dissipative hydrodynamics and the transport equations derived from kinetic theory via Grad's 14-moment method, building on prior work by Kolb (2003), Romatschke (2007), and others. The findings enable future studies to disentangle medium properties from geometric effects in jet-medium interactions.
