# Tetraneutron condensation in neutron rich matter

> In this work we investigate the possible condensation of tetraneutron resonant states in the lower density neutron rich gas regions inside Neutron Stars (NSs). Using a relativistic density functional approach we characterize the system containing different hadronic species including, besides tetraneutrons, nucleons and a set of light clusters ($^3$He, $α$ particles, deuterium and tritium). $σ,ω$ and $ρ$ mesonic fields provide the interaction in the nuclear system. We study how the tetraneutron presence could significantly impact the nucleon pairing fractions and the distribution of baryonic charge among species. For this we assume that they can be thermodynamically produced in an equilibrated medium and scan a range of coupling strengths to the mesonic fields from prescriptions based on isospin symmetry arguments. We find that tetraneutrons may appear over a range of densities belonging to the outer NS crust carrying a sizable amount of baryonic charge thus depleting the nucleon pairing fractions.

## Key claim
Tetraneutron resonant states may condense in the outer crust of neutron stars at densities well below nuclear saturation, but only if their in-medium decay width falls within a specific allowed interval (between a minimum and maximum critical value); outside that interval the condensation is either unphysical (multiple solutions) or absent.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic mean-field (RMF) density functional approach with σ, ω, and ρ meson fields to describe β-equilibrated, electrically neutral neutron-star matter at zero temperature. The system includes nucleons, electrons, light clusters (deuterium, tritium, ³He, α particles), and tetraneutrons treated as resonant states with a Breit-Wigner mass distribution; tetraneutron condensation is described as a Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) with an excluded-volume correction for finite size (R = 0 or 5 fm). Coupling strengths of tetraneutrons to mesonic fields are varied using two parameter sets (A and B) based on isospin symmetry arguments, and the self-consistent field equations are solved to determine species fractions and thermodynamic properties.

## Key numbers
- Tetraneutron excitation energy in vacuum: E₄ₙ = 0.83 MeV with width Γ₄ₙ = 2.6 MeV from the exchange reaction α + ⁸He → ⁸Be + ⁴n (experimental claim).
- Theoretical vacuum predictions for tetraneutron: E₄ₙ = 0.8 MeV, Γ₄ₙ = 1.4 MeV (one calculation); alternatively E₄ₙ = 7.3 MeV, Γ₄ₙ = 3.7 MeV (another calculation).
- Nuclear saturation density: n₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³; binding energy per nucleon = 16.3 MeV; symmetry energy coefficient a_sym = 32.5 MeV; incompressibility K₀ = 250 MeV; effective nucleon mass m_N* = 0.75 m_N at saturation.
- For parameter set B (x₄ₙσ/4 = 0.85, x₄ₙω/4 = 1) with pointlike tetraneutrons: onset density n_os = 0.05 n₀, dissolution density n_dis = 0.14 n₀, allowed width Γ₄ₙ ≈ 9.0 MeV.
- For parameter set A (x₄ₙσ/4 = 1, x₄ₙω/4 = 1) with pointlike tetraneutrons: onset density n_os = 0.13 n₀, dissolution density n_dis = 0.20 n₀, allowed width Γ₄ₙ ≈ 22.0 MeV.
- Tetraneutron condensation occurs in the baryon density range n_B ≈ 0.02–0.031 fm⁻³ for set A (pointlike), carrying up to about 4% of the baryonic charge.
- Symmetry energy slope: L = 91.2 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of tetraneutron resonances from vacuum conditions (experimental claim by Kisamori et al. (2016) and theoretical calculations by various groups) into the dense, neutron-rich medium of neutron-star crusts. It builds on relativistic mean-field treatments of light clusters in neutron-star matter (RMFclusters1–4) and on the hadron resonance gas model for finite-width states (HRGM1, HRGM2). The results suggest that tetraneutron condensation could deplete nucleon pairing fractions and modify the baryonic charge distribution, but the strong dependence on the uncertain in-medium width and coupling strengths calls for more refined few-body calculations (e.g., Faddeev-Yakubovsky methods) and experimental constraints on tetraneutron properties in nuclear matter.
