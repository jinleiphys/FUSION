# Can nuclear matter consist of $α$-particles?

> A sufficient condition for the spatial collapse in an infinite system of interacting Bose particles is obtained on the basis of the variational principle with the use of trial functions with the Jastrow pair correlation factors. The instability of a hypothetical infinite system of $α$-particles with respect to the spatial collapse is shown under the assumption of the Ali - Bodmer interaction potentials between such Bose particles. Thus, it becomes clear why the hypothetical nuclear matter is naturally treated with the use of at least the nucleon degrees of freedom.

# Can Nuclear Matter Consist of α-Particles?

## Key claim

An infinite system of α-particles interacting via Ali-Bodmer potentials (without Coulomb repulsion) is unstable with respect to spatial collapse, meaning nuclear matter cannot be treated as a Bose gas of α-particles and must instead be described using nucleon degrees of freedom. This conclusion depends on the assumption that the Coulomb repulsion is omitted, as including it would prevent binding entirely.

## Method

The author applies the Ritz variational principle to an infinite system of N identical Bose particles with pairwise interactions, using trial wavefunctions that include Jastrow pair correlation factors of the form f(r) = 1 − exp(−r²/r₀²) multiplied by a product of one-particle Gaussian functions. The variational energy per particle is evaluated in the limit R ≫ r₀ (where R is the system size parameter and r₀ is the correlation radius), yielding a sufficient condition for spatial collapse expressed as an inequality involving the integral of the potential weighted by the squared Jastrow factor. This criterion is then applied to six versions of the Ali-Bodmer α-α interaction potential (labeled a₀, b₀, c₀, d₀, d″₀, e₀) with parameters taken from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The α-particle binding energy is approximately 28.3 MeV
- For potentials a₀ and b₀, the collapse criterion A < 0 is satisfied already at r₀ = 0; for the remaining potentials (c₀, d₀, d″₀, e₀), the criterion is satisfied at nonzero values of r₀
- The Ali-Bodmer potential parameters include: for a₀, μₐ = 0.35 fm⁻¹, Vₐ = 30 MeV, μᵣ = 0.65 fm⁻¹, Vᵣ = 125 MeV; for b₀, μₐ = 0.42 fm⁻¹, Vₐ = 150 MeV, μᵣ = 0.55 fm⁻¹, Vᵣ = 325 MeV; for c₀, μₐ = 0.45 fm⁻¹, Vₐ = 190 MeV, μᵣ = 0.6 fm⁻¹, Vᵣ = 500 MeV; for d₀, μₐ = 0.475 fm⁻¹, Vₐ = 130 MeV, μᵣ = 0.7 fm⁻¹, Vᵣ = 500 MeV; for d″₀, μₐ = 0.475 fm⁻¹, Vₐ = 130 MeV, μᵣ = 0.8 fm⁻¹, Vᵣ = 1300 MeV; for e₀, μₐ = 0.5 fm⁻¹, Vₐ = 150 MeV, μᵣ = 0.8 fm⁻¹, Vᵣ = 1050 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier criterion for spatial collapse in Bose systems (Grinyuk, previous paper, reference R8), which used a simple product of one-particle Gaussian trial functions and gave the condition ∫V(r)dr < 0; that simpler criterion was too mild to decide unambiguously for most Ali-Bodmer potentials. The improved criterion with Jastrow factors shows that all realistic α-α potentials produce collapse, explaining why nuclear matter must be treated with nucleon degrees of freedom rather than as a system of α-particles. The result also demonstrates that the Gross-Pitaevskii equation (Gross 1961, Pitaevskii 1961) cannot be used to analyze spatial collapse in such systems, as the sign of the two-particle scattering length is not the determining factor.
