# Universal parameterization of initial-state fluctuations and its applications to event-by-event anisotropy

> We propose Elliptic Power and Power parameterizations for the probability distribution of initial state anisotropies in heavy-ion collisions. By assuming a linear eccentricity scaling, the new parameterizations can also be applied to fluctuations of harmonic flow. In particular, we analyze flow multi-particle cumulants and event-by-event distributions, both of which are recently measured at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a universal parameterization of initial-state eccentricity fluctuations, based on the constraint that eccentricity is bounded by unity, provides a significantly better description of event-by-event anisotropic flow distributions than the previously used Bessel-Gaussian distribution, especially for peripheral collisions and small systems.

## Method

The authors derive analytic "Elliptic Power" and "Power" probability distributions for the two-dimensional eccentricity vector, starting from an independent-source model of nucleon-nucleon collisions. They test these parameterizations by fitting to eccentricity distributions from Monte-Carlo Glauber and IP-Glasma simulations across all collision centralities. They then apply the rescaled distributions to experimental flow data by assuming a linear response between initial eccentricity and final anisotropic flow, fitting the ATLAS event-by-event distributions of elliptic flow v2 and triangular flow v3 in Pb-Pb collisions, and comparing cumulant ratios from CMS p-Pb data to Power distribution predictions.

## Key numbers

* The Elliptic Power distribution contains two parameters: alpha (approximately the number of independent sources N) and epsilon_0 (the event-averaged reaction-plane eccentricity).
* For azimuthally symmetric systems such as p-Pb collisions, epsilon_0 = 0 and the distribution reduces to the Power distribution P(epsilon_x, epsilon_y) = (alpha/pi)(1 - epsilon_x^2 - epsilon_y^2)^(alpha-1).
* Fits to ATLAS v2 and v3 distributions from 45-50% central Pb-Pb collisions show that Elliptic Power and Power distributions achieve better agreement with experiment than Bessel-Gaussian fits.
* The extracted flow response coefficient kappa_2 is described as a function of shear viscosity over entropy ratio eta/s by kappa_2(eta/s) = C_0 [kappa_2^ideal - (eta/s) delta kappa_2], with C_0 approximately 1.68, yielding an estimate of eta/s approximately 0.18.
* The parameter alpha decreases with centrality percentile, indicating increased initial-state fluctuations from central to peripheral collisions; epsilon_0 grows towards peripheral collisions, indicating a more elliptic average geometry.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of initial-state fluctuations beyond the Bessel-Gaussian parameterization previously used as the "state-of-art" description, which fails for larger eccentricities in peripheral collisions and small systems. The parameterizations are tested against Monte-Carlo Glauber simulations (Alver 2008) and IP-Glasma simulations (Schenke 2014). The analysis of flow cumulants from CMS p-Pb collisions (Chatrchyan 2013, CMS 2014) confirms Power distribution predictions, supporting the picture of collective expansion in p-Pb systems. The extracted flow response coefficients enable a self-contained estimate of the quark-gluon plasma shear viscosity without detailed initial-state modeling, which can be compared to hydrodynamic calculations (Teaney 2013).
