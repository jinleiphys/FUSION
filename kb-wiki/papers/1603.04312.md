# Role of scalar dibaryon and $f_0(500)$ in the isovector channel of low-energy neutron-proton scattering

> We calculate the total and the differential cross section for $np$ scattering at low energies in the isospin $I=1$ channel within the so-called extended Linear Sigma Model. This model contains conventional (pseudo)scalar and (axial--)vector mesons, as well as the nucleon and its chiral partner within the mirror assignment. In order to obtain good agreement with experimental data analysis results we need to consider two additional resonances: the lightest scalar state $f_{0}(500)$ and a dibaryon state with quantum numbers $I=1,$ $J^{P}=0^{+}$ (also known as $^{1}S_{0}$ resonance). The resonance $f_{0}(500)$ is coupled to nucleons in a chirally invariant way through the mirror assignment and is crucial for a qualitatively correct description of the shape of the differential cross section. On the other hand, the dibaryon is exchanged in the $s$--channel and is responsible of the large cross section close to threshold. We compare our results to data analysis results performed by the SAID program of the CNS Data Analysis Center (in the following "SAID results").

## Key claim

The extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) can describe low-energy neutron-proton scattering in the isospin I=1 channel only if two additional resonances are included: the scalar meson f0(500) and a scalar dibaryon state with quantum numbers I=1, J^P=0^+ (the 1S0 resonance). The f0(500) is necessary for a qualitatively correct description of the differential cross section shape, while the dibaryon exchanged in the s-channel is responsible for the large cross section near threshold.

## Method

The authors calculated total and differential cross sections for np scattering in the I=1 channel using the extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) with mirror assignment for nucleons and their chiral partner. They included meson exchange in t- and u-channels (pseudoscalar, scalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons) plus s-channel exchange of a scalar dibaryon field, with a Gaussian form factor attached to each nucleon-nucleon-meson vertex. The dibaryon propagator included an energy-dependent width from the proton-neutron loop, and the model parameters were fitted to reproduce SAID data analysis results up to nucleon center-of-momentum momentum of about 0.4 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Dibaryon coupling strength G_R = 2.13 and mass parameter D_R = m_R - m_p - m_n = 0.0015 GeV for best fit with dibaryon only
- On-shell decay width of the dibaryon: Gamma_R = 0.0135 GeV, which is much larger than D_R = 0.0015 GeV
- For the full model including f0(500): m_chi = 0.525 GeV, coupling a = 8.95, dibaryon D_R = 0.0018 GeV and G_R = 2.27
- Pole position in complex sqrt(s) plane for Lambda_R = 0.5 GeV: sqrt(s_pole) = m_p + m_n + 0.014 GeV - i 0.0774 GeV
- Pole position for Lambda_R = 0.3 GeV: sqrt(s_pole) = m_p + m_n + 0.0273 GeV - i 0.0309 GeV
- Spectral function peak located only 0.0000174 GeV above threshold for Lambda_R = 0.5 GeV
- f0(500) mass of about 0.5-0.55 GeV gives good agreement with SAID results; varying by 0.1 GeV worsens description considerably

## Context

This work extends the eLSM framework, previously applied to meson-meson and meson-nucleon interactions (Denisold, Staniold, Susannaold, Susannanew), to nucleon-nucleon scattering. It contrasts with chiral perturbation theory approaches (Kaplan, Epelbaum, Bedaquerev) where the dibaryon is treated as an auxiliary field that can be integrated out, instead treating it as a genuine resonance with a pole in the complex plane. The results enable future studies of the analogous resonance in neutron-neutron and proton-proton channels, and suggest that the f0(500) generates attraction between nucleons, consistent with its role in nuclear matter saturation (Susagiu) and nuclear binding energy (Machleidtrev).
