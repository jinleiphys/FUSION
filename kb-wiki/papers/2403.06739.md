# Energy loss of a heavy fermion in a collisional QED plasma

> We compute the energy loss of heavy fermions moving in a plasma, taking into account the modification of the photon collective modes induced by collisions using a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook collisional kernel. We include contributions from both hard and soft scatterings of the heavy fermion using a collisionally modified hard-thermal-loop resummed propagator. Using this method, one does not need to introduce a separation scale between hard- and soft-momentum exchanges. To place our calculation in context, we review other theoretical approaches to computing the collisional energy loss of fermions and discuss the systematics and results obtained in each approach compared to using a resummed propagator for both hard and soft momentum exchanges. Our final results indicate that self-consistently including the effect of collisions in the self-energies of the resummed propagator results in an increased energy loss compared to using collisionless hard-thermal-loop propagators. The effect becomes larger as the magnitude of the coupling constant and the velocity of the fermion increase.

## Key claim

Self-consistently including collisions in the photon self-energies via a Bhatnagar-Gross-Krook (BGK) collisional kernel increases the heavy fermion collisional energy loss compared to using collisionless hard-thermal-loop (HTL) propagators, with the effect growing as the coupling constant and fermion velocity increase; the calculation uses a resummed propagator for both hard and soft momentum exchanges, eliminating the need for a separation scale.

## Method

The authors compute the collisional energy loss of a heavy fermion (muon) in a hot QED plasma using the diagrammatic method with a BGK-modified HTL-resummed photon propagator for all momentum exchanges, avoiding an explicit separation scale between hard and soft processes. The BGK collisional kernel introduces a collision rate ν into the photon self-energies, modifying the transverse and longitudinal components Π_T and Π_L, which are then used in the resummed propagator to evaluate the energy loss integral derived from the interaction rate. The calculation assumes M ≫ T, v ≫ T/E, and E ≪ M²/T, and includes only t-channel Coulomb scattering, with Compton scattering contributions suppressed by (T/M)².

## Key numbers

- For a coupling α = e²/(4π) = 0.3 (consistent with QCD α_s ~ 0.3), the inclusion of collisional effects in the gauge boson propagator results in an approximately 10% increase in the heavy fermion collisional energy loss at high momentum.
- The screening mass is defined as m_γ² = e²T²/3 for QED.
- The hard contribution to energy loss from the Braaten-Thoma method becomes negative as q* → 0, where the energy loss approaches zero due to the increasingly smaller integral region in the soft contribution.

## Context

This work extends the method of Djordjevic and Gyulassy (2006), who computed collisional energy loss using collisionless HTL-resummed propagators without a separation scale, by incorporating collisional effects via the BGK kernel. It goes beyond prior studies by Han (2017), Shi (2018), Yousuf Jamal (2019), and Jamal (2020), which computed only the soft contribution to collisional energy loss using BGK-HTL self-energies, by including the hard contribution self-consistently. The results enable future extension to full QCD calculations and provide comparisons with the Braaten-Thoma (1991) and Romatschke-Strickland (2003, 2004) methods, showing that different methods agree in the weak-coupling limit but exhibit discrepancies at moderate couplings relevant to QGP physics.
