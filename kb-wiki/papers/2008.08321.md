# Charged rho superconductor in the presence of magnetic field and rotation

> In this work, we mainly explore the possibility of charged rho superconductor (CRS) in the presence of parallel magnetic field and rotation within three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasino model. By following similar schemes as in the previous studies of charged pion superfluid (CPS), the CRS is found to be favored for both choices of Schwinger phase in Minkovski and curved spaces. Due to the stability of the internal spin structure, charged rho begins to condensate at a smaller threshold of angular velocity than charged pion for the given large magnetic fields. Even the axial vector meson condensation is checked -- the conclusion is that CRS is the robust ground state at strong magnetic field and fast rotation, which actually sustains to very large angular velocity.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charged rho meson condensation (CRS) is the robust ground state in the presence of strong parallel magnetic field and fast rotation, and that it sustains to very large angular velocity; however, this conclusion depends on the choice of Schwinger phase (Minkowski or curved space) and is conditional on the rotation invalidating the Vafa-Witten theorem that would otherwise forbid such condensation in pure magnetic field.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with u, d, and s quarks as fundamental degrees of freedom, incorporating vector interaction channels and a six-fermion 't Hooft determinant term. They compute the quadratic coefficient in a Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the effective potential for charged rho meson condensation, evaluating the polarization function via fermion loop integrals with quark propagators reconstructed in the presence of a parallel magnetic field and rotation, for two choices of Schwinger phase (Minkowski space and curved space).

## Key numbers

- The threshold angular velocity for charged rho condensation is smaller than that for charged pion condensation at given large magnetic fields, due to the stability of the internal spin structure of the rho meson.
- The dimensionless energy \(\tilde{E}_{0l}^\Omega = \sqrt{2\lambda_l^0+1} - l/\sqrt{2\mathcal{N}}\) is shown for \(\mathcal{N}=15\) and \(\mathcal{N}=100\) (where \(\mathcal{N} \equiv [|qB|R^2/2]\) is the number of magnetic flux quanta), demonstrating that finite lower boundaries exist for total energies of any charged particles.
- The quasi Landau levels \(\lambda_l^n\) (n=0,1,2) are plotted as functions of orbital angular momentum \(l\) for \(\mathcal{N}=100\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charged pion superfluid (CPS) in parallel magnetic field and rotation by Liu et al. (2017) and addresses the controversy over charged rho superconductor (CRS) in pure magnetic field initiated by Chernodub (2010-2011) and debated by Braguta (2011), Hidaka (2012), Bali (2017), and others. The paper uses the same NJL model framework as Cao (2019) for charged pion condensation and Cao (2019) for rho mesons in magnetic fields, and it resolves the ambiguity of Schwinger phase definitions previously discussed by Cao (2019) and Chen (2019). The rotation term breaks the semi-positivity of the fermion determinant, freeing the study from the Vafa-Witten theorem constraint that previously argued against CRS in pure magnetic fields.
