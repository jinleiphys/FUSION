# $^4$He energies and radii by the coupled-cluster method with many-body average potential

> The reformulated coupled-cluster method (CCM), in which average many-body potentials are introduced, provides a useful framework to organize numerous terms appearing in CCM equations, which enables us to clarify the structure of the CCM theory and physical importance of various terms more easily. We explicitly apply this framework to $^4$He, retaining one-body and two-body correlations as the first illustrating attempt. Numerical results with using two modern nucleon-nucleon interactions (AV18 and CD-Bonn) and their low-momentum interactions are presented. The characters of short-range and many-body correlations are discussed. Although not considered explicitly, the expression of the ground-state energy in the presence of a three-nucleon force is given.

## Key claim

The reformulated coupled-cluster method (CCM) with many-body average potentials, when truncated to one-body and two-body correlations (CCSD), yields ground-state energies and radii for $^4$He that are almost independent of the harmonic oscillator constant, but only when the one-body amplitude $s_1$ is included; without $s_1$, the results show significant oscillator-constant dependence.

## Method

The authors applied the CCSD truncation of the reformulated CCM, which introduces average many-body potentials to organize the similarity-transformed Hamiltonian into normal-ordered form, to $^4$He in a harmonic oscillator basis with major shells up to $N_{max}=22$ and two-body partial waves up to $J=6$. They solved the nonlinear decoupling equations iteratively using the Newton method for the one-body amplitude $s_1$ and two-body amplitude $s_{12}$, using both bare nucleon-nucleon interactions (CD-Bonn and AV18) and their low-momentum equivalent interactions at three cutoff momenta ($\Lambda=4.0$, $3.0$, and $1.9$ fm$^{-1}$). The root-mean-square radius was obtained via the Feynman-Hellman theorem by solving the constrained Hamiltonian $H+\lambda Q_r$ and taking the derivative at $\lambda=0$.

## Key numbers

- CCSD ground-state energy of $^4$He with the bare CD-Bonn potential at $N_{max}=22$ and $\nu=0.56$ fm$^{-2}$ ($\hbar\omega=23.2$ MeV) is about 8 MeV above the Faddeev-Yakubovsky reference energy.
- CCSD ground-state energy of $^4$He with the bare AV18 potential at $N_{max}=22$ is about 21 MeV above the Faddeev-Yakubovsky result of -24.3 MeV.
- For the low-momentum interaction with $\Lambda=1.9$ fm$^{-1}$, the CCSD energy at $N_{max}=6$ almost reproduces the Faddeev-Yakubovsky energy with the same low-momentum interaction.
- Neglect of the two-body average potentials $u_{12}^{A,B}$ changes the ground-state energy by about 0.5 MeV (less attractive) and the radius by about 0.02 fm (smaller) for both CD-Bonn and AV18.
- The r.m.s. radius of the $^4$He reference state $|\Phi_0\rangle\equiv |(0s)^4\rangle$ is $\frac{3}{2\sqrt{2\nu}}$, where $\nu$ is the oscillator constant.
- The momentum scale estimate $\sqrt{\langle \bp^2\rangle}=3.6$ fm$^{-1}$ for $N_{max}=22$ and $\nu=0.56$ fm$^{-2}$.

## Context

This work applies the reformulated CCM with many-body average potentials, originally proposed by Suzuki (1992) and Suzuki et al. (1994), to an actual nucleus for the first time. It extends prior CCM calculations of light nuclei by Dean and Hjorth-Jensen (2004), Hagen et al. (2007), and others, while clarifying the structure of the CCM theory through the introduction of average potentials. The results demonstrate that low-momentum interactions (Bogner et al.) can be treated adequately at the CCSD level, and the framework enables future quantitative investigation of three-body correlations involving high-momentum components.
