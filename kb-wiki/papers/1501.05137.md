# Three-body recombination of two-component cold atomic gases into deep dimers in an optical model

> We consider three-body recombination into deep dimers in a mass-imbalanced two-component atomic gas. We use an optical model where a phenomenological imaginary potential is added to the lowest adiabatic hyper-spherical potential. The consequent imaginary part of the energy eigenvalue corresponds to the decay rate or recombination probability of the three-body system. The method is formulated in details and the relevant qualitative features are discussed as functions of scattering lengths and masses. We use zero-range model in analyses of recent recombination data. The dominating scattering length is usually related to the non-equal two-body systems. We account for temperature smearing which tends to wipe out the higher-lying Efimov peaks. The range and the strength of the imaginary potential determine positions and shapes of the Efimov peaks as well as the absolute value of the recombination rate. The Efimov scaling between recombination peaks is calculated and shown to depend on both scattering lengths. Recombination is predicted to be largest for heavy-heavy-light systems. Universal properties of the optical parameters are indicated. We compare to available experiments and find in general very satisfactory agreement.

# Three-body recombination of two-component cold atomic gases into deep dimers in an optical model

## Key claim

The optical model with a phenomenological imaginary square-well potential added to the lowest adiabatic hyper-spherical potential can describe three-body recombination into deep dimers for mass-imbalanced two-component atomic gases, but the model is phenomenological by nature and the range and strength of the imaginary potential determine positions, shapes, and absolute values of Efimov recombination peaks, with universal properties of the optical parameters only indicated rather than proven.

## Method

The authors solve the hyper-radial wave equation derived from the adiabatic hyper-spherical expansion method, where the angular eigenvalue ν(ρ) is obtained by solving the determinant of a 3×3 matrix of coupled Faddeev equations with zero-range two-body interactions. They add a complex square-well potential at small hyper-radii (ρ < ρ_cut) to the real adiabatic potential, regularizing the otherwise diverging −1/ρ² behavior. The recombination rate is computed either from the S-matrix element S₁₁ describing missing probability in the outgoing hyper-spherical wave, or from the imaginary part Γ of complex energy eigenvalues obtained with box boundary conditions, with both methods yielding equivalent results after proper volume normalization.

## Key numbers

- For three identical bosons, the barrier height of the real potential is 0.287 ℏ²/(2ma²) located at ρ = 1.46 a.
- The Efimov scaling factor for three identical bosons is 22.7, independent of atomic mass.
- For a Cs-Li gas with densities from experiment Pires (2014), the half-life from α₁₁₂⁽¹⁾ recombination ranges from a few milliseconds to a few nanoseconds as a₁₂ grows from −100 a₀ to −10000 a₀.
- For a Cs-Li gas with densities from experiment Pires (2014), the half-life from α₂₂₁⁽¹⁾ recombination ranges from about 150 seconds to a few milliseconds as a₁₂ grows from −100 a₀ to −10000 a₀.
- For a single-species gas with typical experimental densities from Huang (2014), the half-life varies from about half a second to a few nanoseconds as the scattering length changes from −100 a₀ to −20000 a₀.

## Context

This work extends the optical model approach previously applied to three identical bosons by Peder (2013) to the more complicated case of mass-imbalanced two-component atomic gases, where two scattering lengths and one mass ratio are necessary parameters. The method addresses the regime of negative scattering lengths where final states are deeply bound dimers unavailable in standard zero-range three-body models, complementing multiple scattering approaches such as those by Hammer (2010). The results enable comparison with recent experiments on Cs-Li and other two-component systems by Pires (2014), Tung (2014), and Ulmanis (2015), and predict that recombination is largest for heavy-heavy-light systems.
