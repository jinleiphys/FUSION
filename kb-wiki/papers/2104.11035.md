# Hydrodynamic dispersion relations at finite coupling

> By using holographic methods, the radii of convergence of the hydrodynamic shear and sound dispersion relations were previously computed in the ${\cal N} = 4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at infinite 't Hooft coupling and infinite number of colours. Here, we extend this analysis to the domain of large but finite 't Hooft coupling. To leading order in the perturbative expansion, we find that the radii grow with increasing inverse coupling, contrary to naive expectations. However, when the equations of motion are solved using a qualitative non-perturbative resummation, the dependence on the coupling becomes piecewise continuous and the initial growth is followed by a decrease. The piecewise nature of the dependence is related to the dynamics of branch point singularities of the energy-momentum tensor finite-temperature two-point functions in the complex plane of spatial momentum squared. We repeat the study using the Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity as a model where the equations can be solved fully non-perturbatively, and find the expected decrease of the radii of convergence with the effective inverse coupling which is also piecewise continuous. Finally, we provide arguments in favour of the non-perturbative approach and show that the presence of non-perturbative modes in the quasinormal spectrum can be indirectly inferred from the analysis of perturbative critical points.

## Key claim

The radii of convergence of hydrodynamic shear and sound dispersion relations in $\mathcal{N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at large but finite 't Hooft coupling grow with increasing inverse coupling when computed perturbatively, contrary to naive expectations, but become piecewise continuous and eventually decrease when a qualitative non-perturbative resummation is applied.

## Method

The authors use holographic duality to study finite-coupling corrections to the radii of convergence by treating higher-derivative terms in the dual type IIB supergravity action as perturbations parameterized by $\gamma \propto \lambda^{-3/2}$. They solve the linearized fluctuation equations for the shear and sound channels both perturbatively (expanding in $\gamma$) and non-perturbatively (treating the equations as exact in $\gamma$), then locate critical points of the spectral curve $P(\mathfrak{q}^2,\mathfrak{w})=0$ where $\partial_\mathfrak{w}P=0$ to determine branch point singularities that set the radii of convergence. The analysis is repeated in Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity as a fully solvable non-perturbative model for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Shear radius at infinite coupling: $R_{\text{shear}}^{(\infty)} \approx 2.22$
- Sound radius at infinite coupling: $R_{\text{sound}}^{(\infty)} = 2$
- Perturbative correction to shear radius: $R_{\text{shear}}(\lambda) = R_{\text{shear}}^{(\infty)} \left( 1 + 674.15 \, \lambda^{-3/2} + \cdots \right)$
- Perturbative correction to sound radius: $R_{\text{sound}}(\lambda) = R_{\text{sound}}^{(\infty)} \left( 1 + 481.68 \, \lambda^{-3/2} + \cdots \right)$
- Shear channel critical point at infinite coupling: $\mathfrak{q}_c^2 \approx 1.8906469 \pm 1.1711505i$, $\mathfrak{w}_c \approx \pm 1.4436414 - 1.0692250i$
- Sound channel critical point at infinite coupling: $\mathfrak{q}_c^2 = \pm 2i$, $\mathfrak{w}_c = \pm 1 - i$
- Entropy density correction: $s/s_0 = \frac{3}{4}(1 + 15\gamma + \cdots)$
- Shear viscosity correction: $\eta/s = \frac{1}{4\pi}(1 + 120\gamma + \cdots)$

## Context

This work extends the holographic calculation of hydrodynamic dispersion relation convergence radii from infinite coupling (Grozdanov 2019) to finite 't Hooft coupling in $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM theory. The non-monotonic, piecewise continuous coupling dependence resembles results found for the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev chain at finite coupling (Choi 2020) and for theories with finite chemical potential (Jansen 2020). The non-perturbative resummation approach follows methods used by Waeber (2015) and Grozdanov (2016), and the results enable understanding of how the domain of applicability of hydrodynamics shrinks as coupling decreases, with the non-perturbative part of the shear radius curve coinciding with the boundary of validity of hydrodynamics discussed by Grozdanov (2016).
