# Thermodynamics of $n$-$p$ condensate in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We study the neutron-proton pairing in nuclear matter as a function of isospin asymmetry at finite temperatures and the saturation density using realistic nuclear forces and Brueckner-renormalized single particle spectra. Our computation of the thermodynamic quantities shows that while the difference of the entropies of the superconducting and normal phases anomalously changes its sign as a function of temperature for arbitrary asymmetry, the grand canonical potential does not; the superconducting state is found to be stable in the whole temperature-asymmetry plane. The pairing gap completely disappears for density-asymmetries exceeding $α_c= (n_n-n_p)/n \simeq 0.11$.

# Thermodynamics of $n$-$p$ condensate in asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim
The superconducting state remains stable in the whole temperature-asymmetry plane despite an anomalous sign change in the entropy difference between superconducting and normal phases, provided a non-trivial solution to the gap equation exists. The pairing gap completely vanishes for density asymmetries exceeding $\alpha_c = (n_n-n_p)/n \simeq 0.11$.

## Method
The authors solve the coupled Dyson equations for normal and anomalous propagators in the Matsubara formalism, decoupling the isospin singlet $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ pairing channel. They use the Paris potential for the bare interaction and Brueckner-renormalized single particle spectra from the Argonne V14 potential, solving the BCS gap equation and density equations self-consistently at saturation density $\rho = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Key numbers
- Critical asymmetry for pairing disappearance: $\alpha_c = 0.11$ at saturation density $\rho = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$
- At $\alpha = 0$, the BCS relation $T_c = \Delta(T=0)/1.76$ holds; this relation breaks down for asymmetric matter
- The pairing gap vanishes at finite temperature $T \simeq 1.5$ MeV for the critical asymmetry $\alpha_c = 0.11$
- Saturation density used throughout: $\rho = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$

## Context
This work extends earlier BCS studies of neutron-proton pairing in asymmetric nuclear matter (ALM1, VONDER, BALDO1, MORTEN) by including Brueckner-renormalized single particle spectra, which previous work without self-energy corrections (SAL97, BALDO3) had shown to overestimate the pairing gap and critical asymmetries. The results are relevant for neutron star models with pion or kaon condensation (Bethe 1994), the astrophysical r-process (Kratz 1993, Chen 1995), and are analogous to spin-zero pairing in superconducting metals under magnetic fields (Larkin, Fulde, Leggett) and the B-phase of liquid $^3$He in magnetic fields (Leggett).
