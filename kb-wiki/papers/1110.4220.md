# The imprints of superstatistics in multiparticle production processes

> We provide an update of the overview of imprints of Tsallis nonextensive statistics seen in a multiparticle production processes. They reveal an ubiquitous presence of power law distributions of different variables characterized by the nonextensivity parameter q > 1. In nuclear collisions one additionally observes a q-dependence of the multiplicity fluctuations reflecting the finiteness of the hadronizing source. We present sum rules connecting parameters q obtained from an analysis of different observables, which allows us to combine different kinds of fluctuations seen in the data and analyze an ensemble in which the energy (E), temperature (T) and multiplicity (N) can all fluctuate. This results in a generalization of the so called Lindhard's thermodynamic uncertainty relation. Finally, based on the example of nucleus-nucleus collisions (treated as a quasi-superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions) we demonstrate that, for the standard Tsallis entropy with degree of nonextensivity q < 1, the corresponding standard Tsallis distribution is described by q' = 2 - q > 1.

# The imprints of superstatistics in multiparticle production processes

## Key claim

The paper argues that Tsallis statistics provides a unified description of multiparticle production processes, but the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) extracted from experimental distributions (\(q' > 1\)) is not the same as the parameter \(q\) appearing in Tsallis entropy (\(q < 1\)); instead, they are related by the duality relation \(q' = 2 - q\). This duality is demonstrated using a superposition model of nucleus-nucleus collisions treated as quasi-superpositions of nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Method

The authors derive sum rules connecting the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) obtained from different observables (rapidity distributions, transverse momentum distributions, and multiplicity distributions) by decomposing temperature fluctuations into longitudinal and transverse components. They generalize Lindhard's thermodynamic uncertainty relation to express fluctuations in energy, temperature, and multiplicity in terms of \(q\). For nucleus-nucleus collisions, they model the total entropy using Tsallis entropy with a superposition of independent nucleon-nucleon collisions, deriving relations between mean multiplicities in AA and pp collisions that require \(q < 1\) for the entropy parameter.

## Key numbers

- For longitudinal fluctuations in pp and \(\bar{p}p\) collisions: \(q_L - 1 \sim 0.1 - 0.3\), growing with collision energy.
- For transverse fluctuations: \(q_T - 1 \sim 0.01 - 0.1\), varying slowly with energy.
- The relation between multiplicity fluctuations and \(q\): \(k = 1/|q-1|\) in the negative binomial distribution \(P(N)\).
- For nucleus-nucleus collisions, the fit parameter \(c_2 = 1.7\) and \(c_1(s) = 1.0006 - 0.036 s^{-1.035}\) for energies \(\sqrt{s} > 7\) GeV, where \(c_1 > 1\) implies \(\tilde{q} < 1\).
- The generalized thermodynamic uncertainty relation: \(\left| \omega^2_N - 1/\langle N\rangle \right| = |q - 1|\), where \(\omega^2_x = Var(x)/\langle x\rangle^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous review (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2009) on Tsallis statistics in multiparticle production, building on earlier identifications of the \(q\) parameter with intrinsic fluctuations (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2000) and the connection to superstatistics (Beck and Cohen, 2003). The paper generalizes Lindhard's thermodynamic uncertainty relation (Lindhard, 1974) and addresses the \(q\) duality previously noted by Kaniadakis et al. (1996) and Beck (2001). The results enable connecting fluctuations observed in different parts of phase space and provide a framework for analyzing ensembles where energy, temperature, and multiplicity all fluctuate simultaneously.
