# Consistency of spectroscopic factors from (e,e'p) reactions at different momentum transfers

> The possibility to extract relevant information on spectroscopic factors from (e,e$'$p) reactions at high $Q^2$ is studied. Recent ${}^{16}$O(e,e$'$p) data at $Q^2 = 0.8$ (GeV/$c)^2$ are compared to a theoretical approach which includes an eikonal description of the final-state interaction of the proton, a microscopic nuclear matter calculation of the damping of this proton, and high-quality quasihole wave functions for $p$-shell nucleons in ${}^{16}{\rm O}$. Good agreement with the $Q^2 = 0.8$ (GeV/$c)^2$ data is obtained when spectroscopic factors are employed which are identical to those required to describe earlier low $Q^2$ experiments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spectroscopic factors extracted from (e,e'p) reactions on ¹⁶O at high momentum transfer (Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)²) are consistent with those obtained from low Q² experiments, provided that a theoretical framework incorporating an eikonal description of final-state interactions, microscopic nuclear matter damping, and high-quality quasihole wave functions is employed.

## Method

The authors compare recent ¹⁶O(e,e'p) data at Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)² to a theoretical model that combines an eikonal approximation for the final-state interaction of the outgoing proton, a self-consistent nuclear matter calculation of the nucleon self-energy (including short-range and tensor correlations) to describe proton absorption, and quasihole wave functions for p-shell nucleons in ¹⁶O obtained by solving the Dyson equation. The electromagnetic current operator is treated using a relativized Schrödinger framework with a relativistic current operator and spinor distortion, avoiding nonrelativistic reduction. The same quasihole wave functions and spectroscopic factors previously used to describe low Q² data from the NIKHEF experiment are applied without modification to the high Q² data.

## Key numbers

- Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)²: the momentum transfer squared for the high-Q² ¹⁶O(e,e'p) data analyzed in the paper
- Spectroscopic factors for p-shell quasihole states in ¹⁶O: identical values are used for both low Q² and high Q² (Q² = 0.8 (GeV/c)²) data, yielding good agreement with experiment
- Proton momenta below 0.5 GeV/c: the regime where conventional DWIA with partial-wave expansion (L_max < 50) has been successfully applied to (e,e'p) reactions
- p'_N ≳ 1 GeV/c: the regime where the eikonal approximation is considered reliable for the outgoing proton
- 0.6 ≲ q ≲ 1 GeV/c: the momentum range over which the eikonal approximation was tested against Schrödinger equation solutions with up to L_max = 120 partial waves
- 65-70%: the fraction of single-particle strength carried by the main peak for states near the Fermi energy
- 10-15%: the overall reduction of single-particle strength for all mean-field orbits due to short-range and tensor correlations
- 10%: the estimated uncertainty in spectroscopic factors from the optical potential treatment of proton distortion

## Context

This work addresses a challenge raised by Lapikás (2000) and Fissum et al. (2000), who reported that spectroscopic factors extracted from (e,e'p) data on ¹²C increased substantially with increasing Q² when analyzed using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock bound-state wave functions and a Glauber-type final-state interaction. The present paper extends the approach of Muther and Dickhoff (1994) for quasihole wave functions and the eikonal description developed by Brieva et al. (1999, 2000) to ¹⁶O, showing that consistency between low and high Q² data is achievable when using the same quasihole wave functions and spectroscopic factors. This enables reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors from high-Q² (e,e'p) experiments at facilities like CEBAF, provided that the theoretical framework includes proper treatment of final-state interactions and realistic bound-state wave functions.
