# Field transformations and simple models illustrating the impossibility of measuring off-shell effects

> In the context of simple models illustrating field transformations in Lagrangian field theories we discuss the impossibility of measuring off-shell effects in nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, Compton scattering, and related processes. To that end we introduce a simple phenomenological Lagrangian describing nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung and perform an appropriate change of variables leading to different off-shell behavior in the nucleon-nucleon amplitude as well as the photon-nucleon vertex. As a result we obtain a class of equivalent Lagrangians, generating identical S-matrix elements, of which the original Lagrangian is but one representative. We make use of this property in order to show that what appears as an off-shell effect in an S-matrix element for one Lagrangian may originate in a contact term from an equivalent Lagrangian. By explicit calculation we demonstrate for the case of nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung as well as nucleon Compton scattering the equivalence of observables from which we conclude that off-shell effects cannot in any unambiguous way be extracted from an S-matrix element. Finally, we also discuss some implications of introducing off-shell effects on a phenomenological basis, resulting from the requirement that the description of one process be consistent with that of other processes described by the same Lagrangian.

## Key claim

Off-shell effects in nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung and Compton scattering cannot be unambiguously extracted from S-matrix elements because field transformations can shift apparent off-shell contributions into contact terms without changing any observable. The paper demonstrates that what appears as an off-shell effect in one Lagrangian representation may originate from a contact term in an equivalent Lagrangian, making the concept of measuring off-shell behavior fundamentally ill-defined.

## Method

The authors construct a simple phenomenological Lagrangian for nucleons and photons (including a scalar contact interaction between protons and neutrons) and perform a specific field transformation on the proton field, introducing parameters that generate off-shell contributions at both the strong nucleon-nucleon vertex and the electromagnetic photon-nucleon vertex. They then calculate tree-level amplitudes for nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung and Compton scattering using both the transformed Lagrangian (which contains off-shell vertex contributions plus contact terms) and an alternative Lagrangian (which contains only contact terms), demonstrating that the two produce identical S-matrix elements. The analysis is performed for spin-1/2 particles (nucleons) to extend previous spin-zero results, and does not rely on chiral perturbation theory or any momentum expansion.

## Key numbers

- Pion-decay constant: F = 92.4 MeV (used in the introductory spin-zero example)
- Pion mass: m_pi = 135 MeV (used in the introductory spin-zero example)
- Fine-structure constant: e^2/4pi ≈ 1/137 (used throughout)
- The transformation parameters are real constants a-tilde and b-tilde (no numerical values assigned; they serve as free parameters demonstrating the equivalence)
- The off-shell contribution to the magnetic part of the photon-nucleon vertex is parameterized as F_2^-(m^2) → kappa + 8 m^2 b-tilde (in the notation of Nyman (1970))

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous demonstration for spin-zero bremsstrahlung (Fearing, 1998) to the spin-1/2 case, showing that the impossibility of measuring off-shell effects is not an artifact of spinless particles. It contradicts the long-standing assumption in medium-energy physics that nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung can distinguish between on-shell-equivalent potentials via their off-shell behavior, an approach used in many potential-model calculations (Workman, 1986; Fearing, 1987; Jetter, 1995; Eden, 1996) and motivated by experiments such as the TRIUMF measurement (Michaelian, 1990). The paper also relates to earlier field-theoretic work on equivalence theorems (Haag, 1958; Chisholm, 1961; Kamefuchi, 1961; Coleman, 1969) and to off-shell form factor calculations via dispersion relations (Nyman, 1970; Bincer, 1960) where the ambiguity was reflected in subtraction constants (Davidson, 1996). The result implies that phenomenological off-shell parameters extracted from data cannot be uniquely associated with off-shell vertex behavior, and that consistency requirements across different processes described by the same Lagrangian must be carefully considered.
