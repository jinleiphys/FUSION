# Kaon and antikaon optical potentials in isospin asymmetric hyperonic matter

> The medium modifications of the energies of kaons and antikaons in isospin asymmetric hyperonic matter are investigated using a chiral SU(3) model. The isospin dependent medium effects, are important for asymmetric heavy ion collision experiments, as well as relevant for the neutron star phenomenology as the bulk matter in the interior of the neutron star is highly isospin asymmetric. The effects of hyperons on the medium modifications of the kaons and antikaons in the strange hadronic matter are investigated in the present work and are seen to be appreciable for hadronic matter with large strangeness fractions. The study of the K-mesons in the asymmetric strange hadronic matter can be especially relevant for the compressed strange baryonic matter which can result from asymmetric heavy ion collision experiments in the future accelerator facility FAIR at GSI.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the medium modifications of kaon and antikaon optical potentials in isospin asymmetric hyperonic matter using a chiral SU(3) model, finding that hyperon contributions become appreciable for hadronic matter with large strangeness fractions, and that the isospin dependence of these potentials is significant at high densities, particularly for future compressed baryonic matter experiments at FAIR.

## Method

The authors employ a chiral SU(3) model in the mean field approximation to describe hadronic matter, including the baryon octet and scalar mesons (sigma, zeta, delta). They derive dispersion relations for kaons and antikaons from an interaction Lagrangian that includes vectorial Weinberg-Tomazawa terms, scalar exchange terms, and range terms (both isospin symmetric and isospin asymmetric), with parameters fitted to reproduce vacuum baryon masses, nuclear matter saturation properties, and low-energy kaon-nucleon scattering lengths for I=0 and I=1 channels.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-averaged KN scattering length used as input is approximately -0.255 fm, derived from a_{KN}(I=0) approximately -0.09 fm and a_{KN}(I=1) approximately -0.31 fm.
- For strangeness fraction f_s=0.1 at density rho_B=rho_0, the K+ energy drops by about 7 MeV when the isospin asymmetry parameter eta changes from 0 to 0.5, while the K0 energy increases by about 10 MeV.
- For f_s=0.5 at rho_B=rho_0, the drop (increase) in energy of K+ (K0) from the isospin symmetric case is about 9 MeV (11 MeV); at rho_B=4 rho_0 these become about 30 MeV (42 MeV).
- For antikaons with f_s=0.1 at eta=0.5, the changes in mass from the isospin symmetric case are 16 MeV (K-) and 14 MeV (anti-K0) at rho_B=rho_0, and 47 MeV (K-) and 32 MeV (anti-K0) at rho_B=4 rho_0.
- For f_s=0.5, these values become 15 MeV (K-) and 12 MeV (anti-K0) at rho_B=rho_0, and 45 MeV (K-) and 37 MeV (anti-K0) at rho_B=4 rho_0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon and antikaon properties in isospin symmetric nuclear matter (Mishra et al., 2004) and isospin asymmetric nuclear matter (Mishra et al., 2007) within the same chiral SU(3) model, by now including hyperon contributions to the strange hadronic matter. The results are relevant for interpreting observables such as K+/K0 and K-/anti-K0 ratios and collective flow in isospin asymmetric heavy ion collisions at the future CBM experiment at FAIR, and for understanding the onset of antikaon condensation in neutron star matter.
