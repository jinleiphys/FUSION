# Microscopic Study of $öÝ+N$ Bremsstrahlung from Effective and Realistic Multi-nucleon Interactions

> Based on an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, a microscopic cluster model of the nucleus-nucleus bremsstrahlung, including implicitly a part of the effects of meson-exchange currents via an extension of the Siegert theorem is applied to the $öÝ+p$ and $öÝ+n$ systems. The contributions of the $E1$ and $E2$ transitions to the bremsstrahlung cross sections are evaluated and their relative importance for the mirror systems $öÝ+p$ and $öÝ+n$ is compared. Another approach based on realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions and the No-Core Shell Model/Resonating-Group Method is also investigated. Some preliminary results for the $öÝ+p$ bremsstrahlung are displayed.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that microscopic cluster models using an extended Siegert theorem can describe öÝ+N bremsstrahlung cross sections, but the NCSM/RGM results are preliminary and require inclusion of more configurations to reproduce elastic phase shifts accurately before precise bremsstrahlung predictions can be made.

## Method
The authors applied the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) with the effective Minnesota NN potential to calculate E1 and E2 contributions to öÝ+p and öÝ+n bremsstrahlung cross sections at a photon energy of 1 MeV, using an extended Siegert theorem to partially include meson-exchange current effects without the long-wavelength approximation. They also performed preliminary NCSM/RGM calculations for the öÝ+p system using chiral effective field theory NN and NNN interactions softened by similarity renormalization group, with a harmonic oscillator basis of 13 quanta maximum and oscillator frequency of 20 MeV/ã. The Microscopic R-matrix Method enforced the correct asymptotic behavior of the scattering wave functions.

## Context
This work extends previous microscopic bremsstrahlung studies by Dohet-Eraly et al. (2013, 2014) that applied the extended Siegert theorem to nucleus-nucleus bremsstrahlung, and builds on the NCSM/RGM framework developed by Quaglioni and NavrûÀtil (2009) for describing light-ion reactions. The öÝ+p system allows direct comparison with experimental bremsstrahlung measurements from Weller et al. (1971), while the öÝ+n system is a necessary step toward modeling t(d,nö°)öÝ bremsstrahlung for fusion plasma diagnostics. The results enable future work incorporating five-nucleon NCSM states in the continuum, as in the NCSM with continuum approach by Baroni et al. (2013), to improve agreement with experimental elastic phase shifts.
