# Invariant-Mass Spectroscopy for Condensed Single- and Double-\kbar Nuclear Clusters to be Formed as Residues in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Using a phenomenological KN interaction combined with the method of Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics., we predict that few-body double-\kbar nuclei, such as ppK$^-$K$^-$ and ppnK$^-$K$^-$, as well as single-\kbar nuclei, are tightly bound compact systems with large binding energies and ultra-high nucleon densities. We point out that these \kbar nuclear clusters can be produced as residual fragments in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and that their invariant masses can be reconstructed from their decay particles.

# Invariant-Mass Spectroscopy for Condensed Single- and Double-$\bar{\rm K}$ Nuclear Clusters to be Formed as Residues in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Using a phenomenological $\bar{\rm K}$N interaction (the AY interaction) combined with Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD), the authors predict that few-body double-$\bar{\rm K}$ nuclei such as ppK$^-$K$^-$ and ppnK$^-$K$^-$ are tightly bound compact systems with large binding energies and ultra-high nucleon densities, and propose that these clusters can be produced as residual fragments in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and identified via invariant-mass reconstruction of their decay particles. The prediction is conditional on the neglect of the $\bar{\rm K}$-$\bar{\rm K}$ interaction due to lack of information, and the authors note that the clusters may actually be in a deconfined quark-gluon phase rather than a hadronic one.

## Method
The authors applied the AY $\bar{\rm K}$N interaction (which reproduces low-energy $\bar{\rm K}$N scattering data, kaonic hydrogen atom data, and the binding energy and decay width of $\Lambda$(1405)) combined with the Tamagaki potential (OPEG) as the bare NN interaction, neglecting the $\bar{\rm K}$-$\bar{\rm K}$ interaction. For ppK$^-$K$^-$, a variational calculation was performed; for ppnK$^-$K$^-$ and larger systems, the effective NN-central force and $\bar{\rm K}$N force were constructed with the $G$-matrix method and an AMD calculation was carried out. The decay width $\Gamma_{\rm K}$ was evaluated by calculating the expectation value of the imaginary potential contained in the effective AY $\bar{\rm K}$N interaction with the wave function obtained by the AMD calculation.

## Key numbers
- ppK$^-$K$^-$: binding energy $E_K = 117$ MeV, decay width $\Gamma_K = 35$ MeV, p-p rms distance reduced to 1.3 fm (compared to 1.90 fm for ppK$^-$)
- ppnK$^-$K$^-$: binding energy $E_K = 221$ MeV, decay width $\Gamma_K = 37$ MeV, central nucleon density $\rho(0) \sim 2.97$ fm$^{-3}$, rms radius $R_{\rm rms} = 0.69$ fm
- pppnK$^-$K$^-$: binding energy $E_K = 230$ MeV, decay width $\Gamma_K = 61$ MeV, central nucleon density $\rho(0) = 2.33$ fm$^{-3}$, rms radius $R_{\rm rms} = 0.73$ fm
- ppK$^-$ (single-$\bar{\rm K}$): binding energy $E_K = 48$ MeV, decay width $\Gamma_K = 61$ MeV, p-p rms distance 1.90 fm
- ppnK$^-$ (single-$\bar{\rm K}$): binding energy $E_K = 118$ MeV, decay width $\Gamma_K = 21$ MeV, central nucleon density $\rho(0) = 1.50$ fm$^{-3}$, rms radius $R_{\rm rms} = 0.72$ fm
- Normal nuclear density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$; predicted central densities are 4-9 times $\rho_0$
- For $\Gamma_K = 20$ MeV, the lifetime $\tau_K \sim 10$ fm/$c$, compared to freeze-out time $\tau_f \sim 5$ fm/$c$

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous theoretical predictions of single-$\bar{\rm K}$ bound states (Akaishi 2002, Yamazaki 2002, Dote 2002, Dote 2003) based on the AY $\bar{\rm K}$N interaction to double-$\bar{\rm K}$ systems, and proposes a new experimental method for their observation. The predicted $\bar{\rm K}$ clusters are related to the concept of density isomers proposed by Lee and Wick, and the authors suggest that the clusters may represent cold, dense strange matter possibly in a deconfined quark-gluon phase. The proposal enables experimental searches for these exotic nuclear systems using invariant-mass spectroscopy in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as GSI-SIS, RHIC, and the future GSI accelerator, and could provide empirical constraints on the equation of state for neutron stars and strange matter.
