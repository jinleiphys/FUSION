# Extrapolations of Lattice Meson Form Factors

> We use chiral perturbation theory to study the extrapolations necessary to make physical predictions from lattice QCD data for the electromagnetic form factors of pseudoscalar mesons. We focus on the quark mass, momentum, lattice spacing, and volume dependence and apply our results to simulations employing mixed actions of Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks and staggered sea quarks. To determine charge radii at quark masses on the lattices currently used, we find that all extrapolations except the one to infinite volume make significant contributions to the systematic error.

## Key claim

Using partially quenched chiral perturbation theory to analyze lattice QCD data for pseudoscalar meson electromagnetic form factors, the authors find that for the quark masses and lattice volumes currently used, all extrapolations except the one to infinite volume make significant contributions to the systematic error when determining charge radii.

## Method

The authors derived one-loop expressions for meson form factors in partially quenched chiral perturbation theory, then incorporated lattice spacing effects for mixed actions of Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks and staggered sea quarks, and finite volume corrections using Poisson re-summation. They applied these results to analyze lattice data from Bonnet et al. (2004) for the pion form factor, estimating the mixed-action low-energy constant C_mix by fixing the chiral perturbation theory parameter α_9 to the experimentally determined pion charge radius.

## Key numbers

- The lightest pion mass in the mixed-action lattice data from Bonnet et al. (2004) is approximately 300 MeV, which is "arguably within the chiral regime"
- The minimum spatial momentum on current lattice volumes is approximately |q_min| ≈ 0.5 GeV
- For the lowest q²_min = 0.18 GeV² and lightest pion mass in Bonnet et al. (2004), the deviation from linearity in the form factor is less than one percent
- For valence-sea meson masses around 350 MeV, lattice calculations will undershoot the pion charge radius by approximately 10%
- For a valence-sea pion mass of 0.35 GeV in a 2.5 fm box, finite volume effects lead to a bigger positively charged pion by only 0.5%
- The estimated value of the mixed-action low-energy constant is C_mix = 0.0064 GeV⁶, with a statistically allowed range from -0.0040 to 0.080 GeV⁶
- At the physical pion mass, volume effects are approximately 12% in a 3 fm box
- Systematic errors are dominated by higher-order chiral contributions: approximately 10% from corrections scaling as m_π²/Λ_χ², and approximately 20% from terms of order q²/Λ_χ² at the q²_min of Bonnet et al. (2004)

## Context

This work extends the earlier one-loop calculation of meson form factors in partially quenched chiral perturbation theory by Arndt (2003) using a computationally judicious choice for quark charges. It provides the theoretical framework for analyzing mixed-action lattice simulations that use Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks on staggered sea quarks, such as those by Bonnet et al. (2004) and Hashimoto et al. (2005). The results enable systematic extraction of the pion charge radius from current lattice data at approximately the 10-20% level, and demonstrate that additional lattice data at multiple valence quark masses or lattice spacings would allow better determination of the mixed-action low-energy constant C_mix.
