# Viscosity calculations from Hadron Resonance Gas model: Finite size effect

> We have attempted to review on microscopic calculation of transport coefficients like shear and bulk viscosities in the framework of hadron resonance gas model, where a special attention is explored on the effect of finite system size. The standard expressions of transport coefficients, obtained from relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory or diagrammatic Kubo-type formalism, carry mainly two temperature dependent components -- thermodynamical phase space and relaxation time of medium constituent. Owing to quantum effect of finite system size, thermodynamical phase space can be reduced as its momentum distribution will be started from some finite lower momentum cut-off instead of zero momentum. On the other hand, relaxation time of hadrons can also face finite size effect by considering only those relaxation scales, which are lower than the system size. Owing to these phenomenological issues, we have proposed a system size dependent upper bound of transport coefficients for ideal HRG model, whose qualitative technique may also be applicable in other models. This finite size prescription may guide to shorten the broad numerical band, within which earlier estimated values of transport coefficients for hadronic matter are located. It is also suspected that the hadronic matter may not be far from the (nearly) perfect fluid nature like the quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that considering a finite system size in the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model introduces a lower momentum cut-off and an upper bound on relaxation scales, which can reduce the broad numerical band of previously estimated shear and bulk viscosity values for hadronic matter, potentially bringing them closer to the nearly perfect fluid behavior observed in quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors calculate shear viscosity (η) and bulk viscosity (ζ) using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) of kinetic theory, with thermodynamic inputs (entropy density s, speed of sound c_s) obtained from the ideal HRG model partition function. Finite size effects are introduced by replacing the lower limit of momentum integration from zero to k_min = π/R, where R is the system size, and by imposing an upper bound on relaxation times such that only relaxation scales smaller than the system size are considered. The relaxation times for non-resonance hadrons (pions, kaons, nucleons) are obtained from self-energy diagrams and scattering processes, while resonance hadrons are treated separately.

## Key numbers

- Lower momentum cut-off is set as k_min = π/R, with system sizes R = 2, 2.5, 3, 4, 5, 6 fm considered, guided by experimental values from STAR (2017) for Au+Au collisions at √S_NN = 200 GeV
- For non-central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, the freeze-out size R varies from approximately 7 fm to 3 fm for N_part = 350 to 40, with temperature T varying from 0.168 GeV to 0.164 GeV and baryon chemical potential μ_B from 0.027 GeV to 0.019 GeV
- The entropy density normalized by its Stefan-Boltzmann limit s_SB = (19π²/9)T³ decreases as R decreases, with LQCD data from the WB group (Borsanyi, 2013) and Hot QCD group (2014) shown for comparison
- The speed of sound squared c_s² also decreases with decreasing R, deviating from the Stefan-Boltzmann limit of 1/3

## Context

This work extends the finite size prescription introduced in Ghosh et al. (SG_HRG_PRC, SG_HRGV_JPG) to systematically review how system size affects transport coefficients in the HRG model. It builds upon earlier microscopic calculations of η/s for hadronic matter by Prakash, Itakura, Dobado, Nicola, Weise, and others, which produced a broad numerical band of values. The paper also connects to macroscopic hydrodynamic simulations by Roy (2012) that showed centrality-dependent η/s values, and to lattice QCD thermodynamics from the WB group (Borsanyi, 2013) and Hot QCD group (2014) that agree with the ideal HRG model in the hadronic temperature range.
