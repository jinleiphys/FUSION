# Exploring the distribution and impact of bosonic dark matter in neutron stars

> The presence of dark matter (DM) within neutron stars (NSs) can be introduced by different accumulation scenarios in which DM and baryonic matter (BM) may interact only through the gravitational force. In this work, we consider asymmetric self-interacting bosonic DM which can reside as a dense core inside the NS or form an extended halo around it. It is seen that depending on the boson mass ($m_χ$), self-coupling constant ($λ$) and DM fraction ($F_χ$), the maximum mass, radius and tidal deformability of NSs with DM admixture will be altered significantly. The impact of DM causes some modifications in the observable features induced solely by the BM component. Here, we focus on the widely used nuclear matter equation of state (EoS) called DD2 for describing NS matter. We show that by involving DM in NSs, the corresponding observational parameters will be changed to be consistent with the latest multi-messenger observations of NSs. It is seen that for $m_χ\gtrsim200$ MeV and $λ\lesssim2π$, DM admixed NSs with $4\%\lesssim F_χ\lesssim20\%$ are consistent with the maximum mass and tidal deformability constraints.

## Key claim

For asymmetric self-interacting bosonic dark matter that interacts with baryonic matter only through gravity, neutron stars with a dark matter fraction between 4% and 20% can simultaneously satisfy the maximum mass and tidal deformability constraints from multi-messenger observations, provided the boson mass is greater than about 200 MeV and the self-coupling constant is less than about 2 pi.

## Method

The authors solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for a system consisting of baryonic matter described by the DD2 nuclear equation of state and a bosonic dark matter component modeled as a condensed scalar field with a quartic self-interaction. They treat the dark matter as either forming a dense core inside the neutron star or an extended halo around it, and vary the boson mass, self-coupling constant, and dark matter fraction to compute the resulting mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Boson mass threshold for consistency with observations: m_chi greater than or approximately 200 MeV
- Self-coupling constant upper bound for consistency: lambda less than or approximately 2 pi
- Dark matter fraction range consistent with constraints: F_chi between 4% and 20%
- The DD2 nuclear equation of state was used for the baryonic matter component

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter in neutron stars (e.g., by Goldman and Nussinov, and by Kouvaris) by systematically exploring the parameter space of asymmetric self-interacting bosonic dark matter and comparing to the latest multi-messenger constraints from gravitational wave events (GW170817) and NICER observations. The results provide a framework for using neutron star observations to constrain bosonic dark matter properties, and enable future work to distinguish between dark matter core and halo configurations using tidal deformability measurements.
