# Symmetry energy at subsaturation densities and the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb

> The mass-dependent symmetry energy coefficients $a_{sym}(A)$ has been extracted by analysing the heavy nuclear mass differences reducing the uncertainties as far as possible in our previous work. Taking advantage of the obtained symmetry energy coefficient $a_{sym}(A)$ and the density profiles obtained by switching off the Coulomb interaction in $^{208}\text{Pb}$, we calculated the slope parameter $L_{0.11}$ of the symmetry energy at the density of $0.11\text{fm}^{-3}$. The calculated $L_{0.11}$ ranges from 40.5 MeV to 60.3 MeV. The slope parameter $L_{0.11}$ of the symmetry energy at the density of $0.11\text{fm}^{-3}$ is also calculated directly with Skyrme interactions for nuclear matter and is found to have a fine linear relation with the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}\text{Pb}$, which is the difference of the neutron and proton rms radii of the nucleus. With the linear relation the neutron skin thickness $ ΔR_{np} $ of $^{208}\text{Pb}$ is predicted to be 0.15 - 0.21 fm.

## Key claim

The slope parameter \(L_{0.11}\) of the symmetry energy at subsaturation density \(0.11\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) is extracted from finite-nucleus properties and is linearly correlated with the neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\text{Pb}\); using this correlation the neutron skin thickness is predicted to be 0.15–0.21 fm, a range that is too small to allow the direct Urca process in a \(1.4 M_{\odot}\) neutron star.

## Method

The authors used the mass-dependent symmetry energy coefficient \(a_{sym}(A) \simeq 22.4\ \text{MeV}\) for \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) from their previous work and density profiles obtained by switching off the Coulomb interaction in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations for 30 Skyrme interactions. They applied the local density approximation to relate \(a_{sym}(A)\) to the symmetry energy \(S(\rho)\) at subsaturation density, thereby determining the slope parameter \(L_{0.11}\). A linear fit between the directly calculated \(L_{0.11}\) and the neutron skin thickness \(\Delta R_{np}\) of \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) was then used to predict \(\Delta R_{np}\).

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter \(L_{0.11}\) at density \(0.11\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) extracted from finite-nucleus properties ranges from 40.5 MeV to 60.3 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at \(0.11\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), \(S^{(6)}_{0.11}\), is 24.8–26.3 MeV.
- The curvature parameter \(K_{\text{sym}}\) at \(0.11\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) ranges from -150.1 MeV to -68.3 MeV.
- The linear correlation between \(\Delta R_{np}\) (in fm) and \(L_{0.11}\) (in MeV) is \(\Delta R_{np} = (0.0279 \pm 0.00338) + (0.00307 \pm 0.00007) L_{0.11}\) with correlation coefficient \(r = 0.984\).
- The predicted neutron skin thickness \(\Delta R_{np}\) of \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) is 0.15–0.21 fm.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Centelles et al. (2009) that connects the symmetry energy of finite nuclei to nuclear matter, and it builds on the authors’ own prior extraction of mass-dependent symmetry energy coefficients. The linear relation found between \(L_{0.11}\) and \(\Delta R_{np}\) is stronger than the correlation with the slope at saturation density \(L_0\), consistent with the conclusion of Zhang and Chen (2013). The predicted neutron skin thickness agrees with values from pygmy dipole resonance studies (0.185 ± 0.035 fm) and mean-field calculations (0.185 ± 0.035 fm), and is consistent with the robust value \(0.176 \pm 0.021\) fm obtained by Dong et al. using a correlation with \(J - a_{\text{sym}}\). The result implies that a \(1.4 M_{\odot}\) neutron star cannot cool via the direct Urca process, and it provides a constraint that can be tested by future high-precision measurements of the neutron skin, such as the PREX experiment.
