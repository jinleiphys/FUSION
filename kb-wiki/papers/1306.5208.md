# Longitudinal fluctuations of the center of mass of the participants in heavy-ion collisions

> A model for computing the probability density of event-by-event participant center-of-mass rapidity y^{c.m.} is presented. The evaluations of the y^{c.m.} distribution are performed for different collision energies and different centralities. We show that for certain conditions the rapidity distribution is described by a Gaussian with a variance determined mostly by the collision centrality. It is found that the width of the y^{c.m.} distribution increases strongly for more peripheral collisions, while it depends weakly on the collision energy. Other theoretical estimates of rapidity distribution are presented and questions of interaction and separation between spectators and participants are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model showing that event-by-event fluctuations of the participant center-of-mass rapidity in heavy-ion collisions are described by a Gaussian distribution at mid-rapidity, but only under the conditions that the average spectator and participant numbers are large enough (e.g., >5) and that initial nucleon correlations and spectator-participant interactions are neglected.

## Method

The authors compute the probability distribution of the participant center-of-mass rapidity using a Glauber-Sitenko approach to determine the single-nucleon spectator probability as a function of impact parameter, then model spectator numbers as independent binomial distributions for projectile and target nuclei. The rapidity distribution is derived analytically by approximating the binomial distributions as Gaussians and integrating over spectator numbers, yielding a closed-form expression for the rapidity distribution function. The model is evaluated for Pb+Pb collisions at various energies and centralities, and compared with results from the UrQMD microscopic transport model.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, the nucleon-nucleon cross section used is σNN = 70 mb; for SPS energies (√sNN = 6.41 GeV and 17.32 GeV), σNN = 33 mb.
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear density parameters used are R = 6.53 fm and α = 0.545 for lead nuclei (A = 208).
- The standard deviation of the participant c.m. rapidity distribution for Pb+Pb at √s = 2.76 TeV increases from near zero at b = 0 fm (central) to approximately 0.05 at b = 6 fm (mid-central) and to about 0.2 at b = 9.5 fm (peripheral), as shown in Fig. 3.
- The Gaussian approximation variance is given by δy² = p vᵢ² / [2A(1-p)], where p is the spectator probability and vᵢ is the initial nucleon velocity.
- The angular momentum of the participant system at LHC energy (√sNN = 2.76 TeV) is about two orders of magnitude larger than at SPS energy (√sNN = 17.32 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event longitudinal fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier discussions by Csernai and collaborators (Csernai 2000s) and the analysis of fireball density fluctuations by Bzdak and Teaney (2013). The model provides an analytic framework for computing participant c.m. rapidity fluctuations that can be used as input for hydrodynamic and transport models, and the authors note that recent studies by Csernai (2013) indicate a possibility of experimental measurement of these fluctuations. The comparison with UrQMD calculations shows qualitative agreement, though the UrQMD distribution is wider due to correlations neglected in the simplified model.
