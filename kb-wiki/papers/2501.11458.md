# Beta-delayed particle emission and collective rotations

> Beta-delayed proton emission in the lower half of the sd-shell will involve deformed nuclei. We derive the normalized matrix element connecting emission of one particle from an initial rotational nuclear state to another final rotating state, and we extract selection rules involving the $K$ quantum number. The initial state is approximated as having a core identical to the final nuclear state. The formalism is then directly applicable to $β^+$-delayed proton decays of even-$Z$, odd-$N$ nuclei or $β^-$-delayed neutron decays of odd-$Z$, even $N$ nuclei. These beta-decay results are compared to the outcomes of possible transfer reactions. As an example the beta-delayed proton emission of $^{21}$Mg is considered, where new quantum numbers can be assigned to several states in $^{21}$Na.

## Key claim

The paper derives a normalized matrix element for beta-delayed particle emission between two rotational nuclear states, extracting selection rules involving the \(K\) quantum number, under the approximation that the initial state has a core identical to the final nuclear state. The formalism is directly applicable to \(\beta^+\)-delayed proton decays of even-\(Z\), odd-\(N\) nuclei or \(\beta^-\)-delayed neutron decays of odd-\(Z\), even-\(N\) nuclei, but the derivation assumes strong deformation and that the intrinsic states are nearly identical except for the emitted particle.

## Method

The authors start from the general formalism for rotational wave functions in deformed nuclei, expressing the initial and final states as integrals over Euler angles of intrinsic wave functions multiplied by \(D\)-functions. They compute the overlap matrix element between these states, including a plane wave (or Coulomb wave) for the emitted particle, and then normalize the result using the assumption that the intrinsic states are strongly deformed, so that overlaps are significant only for small rotation angles. The final normalized matrix element is expressed in terms of Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and an integral over the emitted particle's wave function in the intrinsic frame, with selection rules derived from angular momentum and \(K\)-quantum number conservation.

## Key numbers

*   The deformation parameter in the region of interest is large, \(\delta \approx 0.4\).
*   For the decay of \(^{21}\)Mg, the ground state has spin-parity \(5/2^+\) and is expected to be a member of a \(K=1/2\) band with a large positive decoupling parameter.
*   The isobaric analogue state (IAS) in \(^{21}\)Na is at 8.97 MeV excitation energy.
*   The first excited state in \(^{20}\)Ne is at 1.634 MeV excitation energy.
*   The Wigner width for a level in this region is about 1.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of particle emission from deformed nuclei, building on the general rotational model of Bohr and Mottelson (1969) and the particle-rotor model of Sierk (1987). It is motivated by studies of beta-delayed particle emission in the lower sd-shell, where nuclei are well deformed, and aims to extract structure information complementary to that from transfer reactions, such as those discussed by Elbek (1969). The results are applied to the decay of \(^{21}\)Mg, building on recent experimental data from Jensen et al. (2024), and enable new quantum number assignments for states in \(^{21}\)Na, particularly for \(K'=1/2\) and \(K'=3/2\) bands.
