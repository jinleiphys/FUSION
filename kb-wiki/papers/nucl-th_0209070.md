# Cluster Interpretation of Properties of Alternating Parity Bands in Heavy Nuclei

> The properties of the states of the alternating parity bands in actinides, Ba, Ce and Nd isotopes are analyzed within a cluster model. The model is based on the assumption that cluster type shapes are produced by the collective motion of the nuclear system in the mass asymmetry coordinate. The calculated spin dependences of the parity splitting and of the electric multipole transition moments are in agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the properties of alternating parity bands in heavy nuclei (actinides, Ba, Ce, Nd isotopes) can be quantitatively explained by a cluster model based on collective oscillations in the mass asymmetry coordinate, where the reflection-asymmetric shape arises from cluster-type configurations rather than static octupole deformation. The hedge is that the model assumes cluster configurations only up to Li as the light cluster, and the single-particle degrees of freedom are not explicitly included.

## Method

The authors construct a collective Hamiltonian in the mass asymmetry coordinate η, where the potential energy is calculated as a dinuclear potential for configurations with α and Li clusters, using a double-folding procedure for the nuclear interaction and experimental binding energies. The Schrödinger equation is solved for each angular momentum I to obtain wave functions of positive and negative parity, which are then used to calculate parity splitting and electric multipole transition moments (E1, E2, E3). The mass parameter Bη and moments of inertia are determined from known systematics and fitted to reproduce ground state energies and rotational spacings.

## Key numbers

- The mass parameter Bη = 20 × 10⁴ m₀ fm² for most actinides, with a variation of 10%; for Ba, Ce, Nd region Bη = 4.5 × 10⁴ m₀ fm², with better results at Bη = 3 × 10⁴ m₀ fm².
- For light Ra isotopes (²²⁰,²²²Ra), Bη was varied in the range (10–20) × 10⁴ m₀ fm².
- The moment of inertia scaling factor c₁ = 0.85 for cluster configurations; c₂ varies between 0.1 and 0.3 for the mononucleus.
- The weight of the α-cluster configuration in ²²⁶Ra is about 5 × 10⁻², close to the known spectroscopic factor.
- The analytical formula for parity splitting uses fitting parameters b₀ = π MeV⁻¹/² and b₁ = 0.2 MeV⁻¹, with wₘ(I=0) = 0.93.
- For ²²⁴Ra and ¹⁴⁶Ba (I=7), calculated D₁₀ values are larger by a factor of four than experimental D₁₀.
- The intrinsic octupole moment for ¹⁴⁸Nd is ~2000 e fm³ (summed over ground and β bands), compared to ~1500 e fm³ for ground band transitions only.

## Context

This work extends the cluster model approach of Shneidman (2002) and the dinuclear system concept used in heavy-ion reactions to the description of alternating parity bands, previously treated with octupole deformation models (Butler, 1996; Möller, 1996; Leander, 1998) or algebraic cluster models (Iachello, 1991; Gai, 1992; Daley, 1993). The model provides a quantitative alternative to the mean-field octupole approach and the alpha-cluster model of Buck (1994, 1995), using configurations with larger mass asymmetry and smaller cluster overlap. The results enable predictions for unmeasured low-lying states and suggest that the staggering of alpha-clusterization with angular momentum could be verified by measuring the angular momentum dependence of alpha-decay widths.
