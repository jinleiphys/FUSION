# Large Mixing from Small: Pseudo-Dirac Neutrinos and the Singular Seesaw

> If the sterile neutrino mass matrix in an otherwise conventional seesaw model has a rank less than the number of flavors, it is possible to produce pseudo-Dirac neutrinos. In a two-flavor, sterile rank 1 case, we demonstrate analytic conditions for large active mixing induced by the existence of (and coupling to) the sterile neutrino components. For the three-flavor, rank 1 case, ``3+2'' scenarios with large mixing also devolve naturally as we show by numerical examples. We observe that, in this approach, small mass differences can develop naturally without any requirement that masses themselves are small. Additionally, we show that significant three channel mixing and limited experimental resolution can combine to produce extracted two channel mixing parameters at variance with the actual values.

# Large Mixing from Small: Pseudo-Dirac Neutrinos and the Singular Seesaw

## Key claim

If the sterile neutrino mass matrix in a seesaw model has rank less than the number of flavors (specifically rank 1 in a three-flavor space), then pseudo-Dirac neutrinos arise naturally, and large active-neutrino mixing can be induced even when the Dirac mass matrix itself contains no mixing, the paper demonstrates this analytically for two flavors and numerically for three flavors, with the caveat that the effect requires a specific misalignment between the sterile mass eigenvector and the Dirac mass eigenstate axes.

## Method

The authors construct a 6×6 neutrino mass matrix in block form (zero active-Majorana block, diagonal Dirac mass matrix, and a rank-1 sterile Majorana mass matrix parameterized by two angles θ and φ) and solve the secular equation analytically in the two-flavor case as a power series in 1/M, then numerically diagonalize the full 6×6 matrix for three flavors with Dirac masses m1=1, m2=2, m3=3 and sterile scale M=1000 (arbitrary units). They compute eigenvectors to extract active-component mixing ratios and examine oscillation probabilities as functions of L/E for a specific parameter set where two pseudo-Dirac pairs are nearly degenerate.

## Key numbers

- For the two-flavor analytic case, the ratio of active components in the light eigenstates is |β_i/α_i| = (m3/m1) tan(θ), correct to O(m_D²/M²) for the first two eigenstates and to O(m_D/M) for the third.
- For the three-flavor numerical example with m1=1, m2=2, m3=3, M=1000, the lower-mass pseudo-Dirac pair eigenvalues range from approximately 1.07 to 1.88 across the sampled angles (θ, φ = π/8, π/4, 3π/8).
- The upper pseudo-Dirac pair eigenvalues range from approximately 2.04 to 2.87 across the same angle set.
- The heavy see-saw state has mass approximately 1000.003–1000.008, and the light see-saw state has mass 0.001–0.007, for the same parameter choices.
- In the Table I example with m1=1, m2=1.1, m3=3, M=1000, at φ=2.25° the active-1 component of the first pseudo-Dirac eigenstate is 0.59 (compared to 0.00 at φ=0°), demonstrating large induced mixing from a small sterile-sector angle.

## Context

This work extends the singular seesaw mechanism previously discussed by the same authors (Stephenson, Goldman, McKellar, 1998) and by Chang, Chang, and Ho (2002), where large mixing was achieved via a mass hierarchy in the Dirac sector. It contrasts with the conventional seesaw assumption that the sterile mass matrix is proportional to the unit matrix, which would not produce this mixing structure. The paper enables a natural explanation of 3+2 neutrino scenarios (three active plus two light sterile states) without requiring small absolute neutrino masses, and it warns that two-channel mixing analyses of oscillation data (as used by Super-Kamiokande, SNO, and KamLAND) may extract parameters at variance with the true three-channel values, a concern also raised independently by Ernst (2004).
