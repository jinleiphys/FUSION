# Convergence of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion in relativistic kinetic theory

> We rigorously prove that, in any relativistic kinetic theory whose non-hydrodynamic sector has a finite gap, the Taylor series of all hydrodynamic dispersion relations has a finite radius of convergence. Furthermore, we prove that, for shear waves, such radius of convergence cannot be smaller than $1/2$ times the gap size. Finally, we prove that the non-hydrodynamic sector is gapped whenever the total scattering cross-section (expressed as a function of the energy) is bounded below by a positive non-zero constant. These results, combined with well-established covariant stability criteria, allow us to derive a rigorous upper bound on the shear viscosity of relativistic dilute gases.

## Key claim

The paper rigorously proves that the Taylor series for all hydrodynamic dispersion relations in relativistic kinetic theory has a finite radius of convergence, provided the non-hydrodynamic sector has a finite gap at zero wavenumber. For shear waves specifically, this radius of convergence cannot be smaller than half the gap size, and the existence of such a gap is guaranteed whenever the total scattering cross-section is bounded below by a positive constant.

## Method

The author maps the linearized Boltzmann-type equation for sinusoidal perturbations into an eigenvalue problem for a one-parameter family of operators \(I(\chi)=I+\chi v^1\), where \(I\) is the collision operator and \(v^1\) is the particle velocity. Using the theory of holomorphically-perturbed linear operators, the convergence of the eigenvalue series is established by treating the problem as analogous to quantum perturbation theory for a bounded perturbation. For shear waves, rotational symmetry is exploited to reduce the degeneracy of the zero eigenvalue, allowing application of a theorem bounding the convergence radius by half the isolation distance of the eigenvalue.

## Key numbers

* The radius of convergence for shear waves satisfies \(R_{\text{shear}} \geq (2\tau_g)^{-1}\), where \(\tau_g\) is the gap timescale defined by the non-hydrodynamic gap \(\omega_g = -i/\tau_g\) at \(k=0\).
* The total cross-section condition for a finite gap is \(\inf_{s\in \mathbb{R}^+} \int_{\mathcal{S}^2} \upvarsigma(s,\theta) d^2\Omega = \sigma_0 > 0\), where \(\sigma_0\) is a positive constant.
* The mean-free time bound yields \(1/\tau_p \geq \sigma_0 \int d^3p'/(2\pi)^3 f^{\text{eq}}_{p'} (1 - m/p'^0) \equiv 1/\tau_M > 0\), establishing a maximum relaxation timescale \(\tau_M\) independent of momentum.
* The free-energy decay for non-hydrodynamic states satisfies \(E^0(t) \leq e^{-2Gt/\tau_M} E^0(0)\), with \(G>0\) a constant from the coercivity inequality \((\phi_p, I\phi_p) \geq G (\phi_p, \tau_p^{-1}\phi_p)\).
* The non-hydrodynamic gap magnitude is bounded below by \(1/\tau_g \geq G/\tau_M > 0\).

## Context

This work generalizes and simplifies the mathematical analysis of Dudynski (1989), which found sufficient conditions for convergence of the gradient expansion in relativistic gases based on the detailed structure of the Boltzmann integral. The results extend the convergence proofs previously established for strongly coupled systems by Grozdanov et al. (2019) and for dilute gases with hard interactions by McLennan (1965) and Dudynski (1989). The paper complements the author's earlier work (Gavassino, 2024) showing that no non-hydrodynamic gap exists when the differential cross-section tends to zero at high energies, and together with covariant stability criteria enables derivation of a rigorous upper bound on the shear viscosity of relativistic dilute gases.
