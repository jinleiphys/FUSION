# Amplitudes of radiative corrections in fermion bags bound by Higgs boson exchange

> Properties of amplitudes that describe radiative corrections in a bag of heavy fermions bound by the Higgs boson exchange are studied. Classes of amplitudes, in which the large fermion mass is canceled out and hence produces no enhancement for the radiative corrections are found. For fermions with masses in the region 400< m < 1000 Gev all relevant amplitudes are found to possess this property. Correspondingly the radiative corrections for this range of masses are small. For very heavy fermions, m>1000 Gev, the processes described by diagrams with closed fermion loops are also mass-independent.

## Key claim

For fermion bags bound by Higgs exchange, radiative corrections are small for fermion masses in the range 400–1000 GeV because the large fermion mass cancels out in all relevant amplitudes; for very heavy fermions above 1000 GeV, diagrams with closed fermion loops also become mass-independent, but the one-loop approximation is not sufficient in that regime.

## Method

The author evaluates one-loop radiative corrections for fermion bags using the Yukawa interaction between heavy fermions and the Higgs field in the unitary gauge, with the Higgs field expressed as a deviation from its vacuum expectation value. Three classes of one-loop diagrams are computed: the vacuum polarization by fermions (Higgs self-energy), the Yukawa vertex formfactor, and the fermion self-energy. The calculations are performed in two kinematic regimes: for "moderately heavy" fermions (400–1000 GeV) where external fermion legs are near the mass shell and the Higgs field deviates only slightly from its VEV, and for "very heavy" fermions (above 1000 GeV) where all external momenta are taken to zero.

## Key numbers

- The critical fermion mass for binding 12 heavy fermions is approximately 320–410 GeV, with the range due to uncertainty in the Higgs mass (100–200 GeV).
- The Higgs vacuum expectation value is v = 246 GeV.
- For the polarization operator at small momentum, P(0) = -m_H^4 / (80 pi^2 v^2).
- The coefficient gamma in the Yukawa formfactor expansion at small t changes sign at m = 1.83 m_H.
- The asymptotic form of the Yukawa formfactor for large |t| >> m^2, m_H^2 is f_Y(t) - 1 ≈ (g^2 / 16 pi^2) (ln(-t/m^2 - i0) + C), where C depends on the mass ratio mu = m_H/m.
- The imaginary part of the polarization operator for t > 4m^2 is Im P(t) = -(g^2 / 8 pi) (t - 4m^2)^(3/2) / sqrt(t).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field calculations of Kuchiev (2008, 2010) on fermion bags bound by Higgs exchange, which showed that 12 top-antitop systems are unbound for realistic Higgs masses and that bag properties become independent of fermion mass for very heavy fermions. It addresses the concern from Dimopoulos (1991), Bagger (1991), and Farhi (2003) that quantum one-loop corrections might produce strong repulsion that destabilizes such bags, but finds that for the specific system of several heavy fermions in the 400–1000 GeV range, the radiative corrections are small due to mass cancellation. This enables future calculations of bag stability using the simplified amplitudes presented, and suggests that fermion bags could be observable if fourth-generation heavy fermions exist.
