# Hard Rescattering Mechanism in High Energy Photodisintegration of the Light Nuclei

> We discuss the high energy photodisintegrataion of light nuclei in which the energy of the absorbed photon is equally shared between two nucleons in the target. For these reactions we investigate the model in which photon absorption by a quark in one nucleon followed by its high momentum transfer interaction with a quark of the other nucleon leads to the production of two nucleons with high relative momentum. We sum the relevant quark rescattering diagrams, and demonstrate that the scattering amplitude can be expressed as a convolution of the large angle NN scattering amplitude, the hard photon-quark interaction vertex and the low-momentum nuclear wave function. Within this model we calculate the cross sections and polarization observables of high energy gamma + d --> pn and gamma + ^3He --> pp + n reactions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that high-energy photodisintegration of light nuclei (deuteron and helium-3) can be described by a hard rescattering mechanism where a photon absorbed by a quark in one nucleon is followed by a high-momentum-transfer interaction with a quark from a second nucleon, and that the resulting scattering amplitude can be expressed as a convolution of the experimentally measured hard nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude with the low-momentum nuclear wave function, but this model is presented as a hypothesis that requires confirmation by additional studies, particularly at photon energies above 2 GeV.

## Method

The author sums quark rescattering Feynman diagrams for the reactions γ + d → pn and γ + ³He → pp + n, assuming that the dominant contribution comes from soft vertices of the d → NN transition while quark rescattering proceeds through hard gluon exchange. The scattering amplitude is factorized into the hard NN scattering amplitude (taken from experimental data), the hard photon-quark interaction vertex, and the low-momentum nuclear wave function calculated using realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. The differential cross sections are computed by integrating over the deuteron or ³He wave function in the nonrelativistic limit, with the quark interchange mechanism assumed dominant at center-of-mass angle θ_cm = 90°.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy threshold for the model: E_γ ≥ 2 GeV, with momentum transfer −t, −u ≥ 2 GeV²
- For the ³He reaction, the final neutron is required to be very slow: p_n ≤ 100 MeV/c
- The calculation of γ d → pn cross section has approximately 25% accuracy due to experimental errors in high momentum transfer pn scattering data
- The function C(̃t/s) ≈ 1 at θ_cm = 90° and is slowly varying with angle
- The nucleon momenta entering the deuteron wave function integral are restricted to ≤ 300 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the quark counting rule predictions for γ d → pn reactions (which predicted dσ/dt ∼ s⁻¹¹ but could not calculate absolute cross sections) and addresses the failure of perturbative QCD calculations that underestimate measured cross sections by orders of magnitude, as noted by Isgur and Smith (1989) and Rady (1990). The model builds on experimental data from Jefferson Lab experiments E89-012 and NE17 showing that conventional mesonic pictures break down at E_γ ≥ 1 GeV. The approach enables predictions for polarization observables (recoil transverse P_y and longitudinal C_x, C_z) that can be tested by planned Jefferson Lab experiments, and suggests a new class of calculable hard nuclear reactions where infinite sums of quark interactions can be replaced by the hard NN scattering amplitude.
