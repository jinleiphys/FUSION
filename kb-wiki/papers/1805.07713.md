# Spin structure of heavy-quark hybrids

> A unique feature of quantum chromodynamics (QCD), the theory of strong interactions, is the possibility for gluonic degrees of freedom to participate in the construction of physical hadrons, which are color singlets, in an analogous manner to valence quarks. Hadrons with no valence quarks are called glueballs, while hadrons where both gluons and valence quarks combine to form a color singlet are called hybrids. The unambiguous identification of such states among the experimental hadron spectrum has been thus far not possible. Glueballs are particularly difficult to establish experimentally since the lowest lying ones are expected to strongly mix with conventional mesons. On the other hand, hybrids should be easier to single out because the set of quantum numbers available to their lowest excitations may be exotic, i.e., not realized in conventional quark-antiquark systems. Particularly promising for discovery appear to be heavy hybrids, which are made of gluons and a heavy-quark-antiquark pair (charm or bottom). In the heavy-quark sector systematic tools can be used that are not available in the light-quark sector. In this paper we use a nonrelativistic effective field theory to uncover for the first time the full spin structure of heavy-quark hybrids up to $1/m^2$-terms in the heavy-quark-mass expansion. We show that such terms display novel characteristics at variance with our consolidated experience on the fine and hyperfine splittings in atomic, molecular and nuclear physics. We determine the nonperturbative contributions to the matching coefficients of the effective field theory by fitting our results to lattice-QCD determinations of the charmonium hybrid spectrum and extrapolate the results to the bottomonium hybrid sector where lattice-QCD determinations are still challenging.

## Key claim
The paper derives the full spin structure of heavy-quark hybrids up to order \(1/m^2\) in the heavy-quark-mass expansion using a nonrelativistic effective field theory, but the spin splittings depend on nonperturbative matching coefficients that must be determined by fitting to lattice-QCD data for charmonium hybrids; the resulting pattern of spin splittings is opposite to that of ordinary quarkonia and can only be reconciled by including the dominant nonperturbative contribution \(V_{\rm SK}^{\rm np\,(0)}\), which is parametrically larger than the perturbative contributions.

## Method
The authors extend the Born-Oppenheimer effective field theory (BOEFT) for heavy-quark hybrids to include spin-dependent potentials at order \(1/m\) and \(1/m^2\). They compute the mass shifts from these potentials using time-independent perturbation theory (second-order for the \(1/m\) operator proportional to \(V_{\rm SK}^{\rm np\,(0)}\), first-order for all others) applied to the four lowest-lying hybrid multiplets. The eight nonperturbative matching coefficients in the spin-dependent potentials are determined by fitting the calculated charmonium hybrid spin splittings to lattice-QCD data from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Cheung 2016) with pion mass \(m_\pi \approx 240\) MeV, using a prior that enforces natural scaling of the nonperturbative potentials.

## Key numbers
- The lowest hybrid charmonium spin multiplet is identified at about \(4.3\,\text{GeV}\) containing a state with exotic quantum numbers \(J^{PC}=1^{-+}\) (from lattice QCD, cited in the paper).
- The charm quark mass used in the RS\(^\prime\) scheme is \(m_c^{\rm RS}(1\ {\rm GeV}) = 1.477\ {\rm GeV}\).
- The bottom quark mass used in the RS\(^\prime\) scheme is \(m_b^{\rm RS}(1\ {\rm GeV}) = 4.863\ {\rm GeV}\).
- The strong coupling constant is taken as \(\alpha_{\rm s}(2.6\ {\rm GeV}) = 0.26\) (four loops, three massless flavors).
- The nonperturbative matching coefficient \(V^{\rm np\,(0)}_{\rm SK}/\Lambda^2_{\rm QCD} = 1.03\) (with \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 0.5\ {\rm GeV}\)).
- The nonperturbative matching coefficient \(V^{\rm np\,(1)}_{\rm SK}/\Lambda^4_{\rm QCD} = -0.51\).
- The nonperturbative matching coefficient \(V^{\rm np\,(0)}_{{\rm SL}a}/\Lambda^3_{\rm QCD} = -1.32\).
- The nonperturbative matching coefficient \(V^{\rm np\,(0)}_{{\rm SL}b}/\Lambda^3_{\rm QCD} = 2.44\).
- The nonperturbative matching coefficient \(V^{\rm np\,(0)}_{{\rm S}^2}/\Lambda^3_{\rm QCD} = -0.33\).
- The nonperturbative matching coefficient \(V^{\rm np\,(0)}_{{\rm S_{12}}b}/\Lambda^3_{\rm QCD} = -0.39\).
- The fit to charmonium hybrid lattice data yields \(\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.} = 0.99998\) for the eight nonperturbative parameters.

## Context
This work extends the Born-Oppenheimer effective field theory for heavy-quark hybrids developed by Berwein (2015), Oncala (2017), and Brambilla (2017) by computing for the first time the full spin-dependent potentials up to order \(1/m^2\). It contradicts the naive expectation that perturbative spin splittings would dominate, finding instead that nonperturbative contributions (especially \(V_{\rm SK}^{\rm np\,(0)}\)) are parametrically larger and reverse the pattern predicted by perturbation theory. The results enable predictions for the bottomonium hybrid spin spectrum, where lattice-QCD calculations are computationally demanding, and provide a theoretical framework for interpreting exotic XYZ mesons observed at B-factories, LHC, and future experiments like PANDA@FAIR.
