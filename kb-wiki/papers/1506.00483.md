# Collective Modes in the Superfluid Inner Crust of Neutron Stars

> The neutron-star inner crust is assumed to be superfluid at relevant temperatures. The contribution of neutron quasiparticles to thermodynamic and transport properties of the crust is therefore strongly suppressed by the pairing gap. Nevertheless, the neutron gas still has low-energy excitations, namely long-wavelength collective modes. We summarize different approaches to describe the collective modes in the crystalline phases of the inner crust and present an improved model for the description of the collective modes in the pasta phases within superfluid hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that long-wavelength collective modes (Goldstone modes) of the superfluid neutron gas in the inner crust of neutron stars can be thermally excited at low temperatures and contribute to specific heat and heat conductivity, even though the contribution of individual neutron quasiparticles is exponentially suppressed by the pairing gap; however, the authors note that the electron and lattice-phonon contributions are usually dominant.

## Method

The authors develop a hydrodynamic model for collective modes in the pasta phases (specifically the lasagne phase of plates) by solving linearized superfluid hydrodynamic equations with Coulomb interaction, treating the inner crust as a mixed phase of neutron gas and neutron-proton liquid separated by a sharp interface with surface tension. They include boundary conditions allowing neutron flux across the interface and use the Young-Laplace formula for pressure differences due to surface curvature. The model is compared with Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations in uniform neutron matter and in Wigner-Seitz cells, as well as with low-energy effective theories for long wavelengths.

## Key numbers

- The baryon density in the inner crust is below approximately 0.08 fm\(^{-3}\) (mass density \(\varrho \lesssim 1.3\times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\))
- The inner crust begins at mass density \(\varrho \gtrsim 4\times 10^{11}\) g/cm\(^3\) (baryon density \(n_B \gtrsim 2.4\times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\))
- The pairing gap \(\Delta\) is approximately 1 MeV
- Temperatures of interest are 10–100 keV (corresponding to \(10^8\)–\(10^9\) K)
- A Wigner-Seitz cell calculation by Khan et al. (2005) used \(R_{WS} = 27.6\) fm containing 1750 neutrons and 50 protons (\({}^{1800}\)Sn)
- The super-giant resonance in that Wigner-Seitz cell appears at approximately 3 MeV excitation energy
- The specific heat of the collective mode is given by \(c_{v,\text{coll}} = 2\pi^2 T^3 / (15 u^3)\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective modes in neutron-star crust matter, including QRPA calculations by Martin and Urban (2014) for uniform neutron matter and by Khan et al. (2005) for Wigner-Seitz cells, as well as low-energy effective theories developed by Cirigliano et al. (2011), Chamel and Page (2013), and Kobyakov and Pethick (2013). The hydrodynamic model builds on earlier work by Di Gallo et al. (2011) and is similar to approaches by Magierski and Bulgac (2004) for calculating effective masses of clusters. The results enable improved calculations of thermodynamic and transport properties of the inner crust, particularly specific heat and heat conductivity, which are relevant for modeling neutron-star cooling curves and X-ray transients.
