# $\boldsymbol{μ^-}$- $\boldsymbol{e^+}$ conversion in upcoming LFV experiments

> We present an illustrative discussion of the physics potential of $μ^-$- $e^+$ conversion. We point out that this process, although much less frequently studied than the related but much more popular processes of $μ^-$- $e^-$ conversion and neutrinoless double beta decay, in fact is a promising alternative possibility to detect both lepton flavour and number violation. However, for this goal to be reached, a combined effort of experiments and theory, both in nuclear and particle physics, is necessary to advance. The aim of this paper is to be an "appetiser" to trigger such an initiative.

## Key claim

The paper argues that muon-to-positron conversion in nuclei, while much less studied than muon-to-electron conversion or neutrinoless double beta decay, is a promising alternative for detecting lepton flavour and number violation in the e-mu sector, but only if a combined effort of experiments and theory in both nuclear and particle physics advances.

## Method

The authors present an effective field theory treatment of short-range contributions to muon-to-positron conversion, analogous to the formalism used for neutrinoless double beta decay, using dimension-9 operators. They map several particle physics models (including doubly charged scalars, heavy right-handed Majorana neutrinos, Left-Right symmetric models, and two-loop neutrino mass models) onto the short-range operator coefficients epsilon_3. The decay rate formula is given in terms of the nuclear matrix element, the muon average probability density, and the Fermi function for the final-state positron.

## Key numbers

* The expected positron energy from muon-to-positron conversion in Al-27 is 83.9 MeV if mediated by the giant dipole resonance, compared to 104.97 MeV for muon-to-electron conversion in the same isotope.
* The giant dipole resonance in Al-27 has a mean excitation energy of 21.1 MeV and a width of 6.7 MeV, fitted from the EXFOR database.
* The only explicit nuclear matrix element value for muon-to-positron conversion mediated by heavy particles is 5.2, computed for Ti-48 in Domin (2004).
* The 2p-to-1s transition in muonic Al-27 emits a 346.8 keV photon with 79.7(6)% intensity.
* Current limits on muon-to-positron conversion from SINDRUM II on Au-197 translate to a bound on the effective parameter epsilon_3,e-mu, with the sensitivity for muon-to-electron conversion being nearly identical to that for muon-to-positron conversion up to a factor of order 1.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of lepton number violation beyond the well-studied processes of muon-to-electron conversion and neutrinoless double beta decay, which are limited to probing the ee-sector. The authors note that experiments like COMET and Mu2e, designed for muon-to-electron conversion, can search for muon-to-positron conversion with minimal modifications. The paper calls for new computations of nuclear matrix elements for isotopes other than Ti-48 and for short-range operators beyond the epsilon_3 type, as only one computation exists for Ti-48 from Domin (2004) and one for Al-27 from Divari (2002) that treated only light neutrino mediation.
