# Leading Hadron Production in $d$+Au and $^3$He+Au collisions in the PHENIX experiment

> Neutral pions have been measured in $^3$He+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}$=200 GeV up to 20 GeV/$c$ in the RHIC Year-2014 run. The nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$ was measured and compared with that from $d$+Au collisions. The integrated $R_{\rm AA}$ as a function of $N_{\rm part}$ was calculated for $d$+Au, $^3$He+Au and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}$=200 GeV, and found to converge for $N_{\rm part}>$12, while a clear system ordering $R_{\rm dAu}$$>$$R_{\rm HeAu}$$>$$R_{\rm AuAu}$ was observed for $N_{\rm part}<$12. The fractional momentum loss for the most central $^3$He+Au collisions was also estimated.

## Key claim

The nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}\) for neutral pions in \(^3\)He+Au and \(d\)+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=200\) GeV shows a convergence across all three collision systems (d+Au, \(^3\)He+Au, Au+Au) for systems with more than 12 participant nucleons, suggesting a similar hot dense medium may be created for larger systems; however, the authors note that the uncertainty needs to be improved before a concrete statement can be made.

## Method

Neutral pions were reconstructed via the \(\pi^0\rightarrow\gamma\gamma\) decay channel using the PHENIX lead-scintillator electromagnetic calorimeter (PbSc EMCal) in \(^3\)He+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=200\) GeV from the RHIC Year-2014 run. The invariant \(p_{\rm T}\) spectra were measured in four centrality classes (0–20%, 20–40%, 40–60%, 60–88%) plus minimum bias events, using both minimum bias and ERT triggers, with efficiency corrections determined from Glauber Monte Carlo simulations. The nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}\) was computed relative to \(p+p\) collisions from the RHIC Year-2005 run and compared with existing \(d\)+Au and Au+Au data.

## Key numbers

- Integrated luminosity of 25 nb\(^{-1}\) recorded in \(^3\)He+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=200\) GeV in the RHIC Year-2014 run.
- Neutral pion spectra measured up to \(p_{\rm T}=20\) GeV/\(c\) in \(^3\)He+Au collisions.
- Minimum bias trigger efficiency determined as 88% for both \(d\)+Au and \(^3\)He+Au collisions, and 94% for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV.
- Bias factors for centrality classes in \(^3\)He+Au: 0.95 (0–20%), 1.02 (20–40%), 1.02 (40–60%), 1.03 (60–88%), and 0.89 (0–100%).
- Integrated \(R_{\rm AA}\) for peripheral Au+Au collisions at \(p_{\rm T}>5\) GeV/\(c\): \(\sim\)0.78 for \(N_{\rm part}=26.7\pm3.7\), \(\sim\)0.87 for \(N_{\rm part}=13.7\pm2.5\), and \(\sim\)0.84 for \(N_{\rm part}=5.6\pm0.8\).
- Convergence of integrated \(R_{\rm AA}\) observed for \(N_{\rm part}>12\) across d+Au, \(^3\)He+Au, and Au+Au collisions.
- System ordering \(R_{\rm dAu}>R_{\rm HeAu}>R_{\rm AuAu}\) observed for \(N_{\rm part}<12\).
- Fractional momentum loss \(\delta p_{\rm T}/p_{\rm T}\sim0.03\) for the most central \(^3\)He+Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of high-\(p_{\rm T}\) hadron suppression from Au+Au collisions (Adare 2012) to small collision systems at RHIC, following the observation of ridge-like correlations in \(p\)+Pb collisions at the LHC (Aad 2013, Aad 2014) and finite \(v_2\) in \(d\)+Au collisions at RHIC (Adare 2013, Adare 2014). The finding that \(R_{\rm AA}\) converges for systems with more than 12 participant nucleons suggests a possible onset of quark-gluon plasma formation as a function of collision system size, which the authors propose can be further tested with \(p\)+Au collisions at RHIC to complete the systematic study.
