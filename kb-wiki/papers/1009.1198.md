# Liquid-gas mixed phase in nuclear matter at finite temperature

> We explore the geometrical structure of Liquid-gas (LG) mixed phase which is relevant to nuclear matter in the crust region of compact stars or supernovae. To get the equation of state (EOS) of the system, the Maxwell construction is found to be applicable to symmetric nuclear matter, where protons and neutrons behave simultaneously. For asymmetric nuclear matter, on the other hand, the phase equilibrium can be obtained by fully solving the Gibbs conditions since the components in the L and G phases are completely different. We also discuss the effects of surface and the Coulomb interaction on the mixed phase.

# Liquid-gas mixed phase in nuclear matter at finite temperature

## Key claim

The Maxwell construction is applicable to symmetric nuclear matter (proton fraction Yp ≈ 0.5) for obtaining the baryon partial pressure, but for asymmetric nuclear matter the phase equilibrium requires fully solving the Gibbs conditions because the liquid and gas phases have different compositions; at finite temperature, pasta structures still appear but with smaller size due to reduced surface tension, and the retrograde condensation predicted by bulk calculations may be washed away by finite-size effects.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with σ, ω, and ρ mesons and baryons, including the Coulomb interaction in the equations of motion, and solve the coupled field equations within Wigner-Seitz cells of spherical, cylindrical, or slab geometry using a relaxation method under global charge neutrality constraints. For finite-temperature calculations, momentum distributions follow Fermi-Dirac statistics with tabulated integrals, while meson excitations and anti-particle contributions are neglected. The cell size and geometrical shape are varied to minimize the free energy density.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter (Yp = 0.5) is approximately 16 MeV, below which the phase transition is congruent.
- At Yp = 0.3 and T = 10 MeV, the baryon partial pressure at the coexistence curve is approximately 0.04 MeV/fm³.
- At Yp = 0.1 and T = 10 MeV, the baryon partial pressure at the onset of the mixed phase is approximately 0.08 MeV/fm³, and the system exits the mixed phase at approximately 0.18 MeV/fm³ (retrograde condensation in bulk calculation).
- At T = 14 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter, no pasta structure is obtained because the baryon partial pressure of uniform matter becomes a monotonic function of density.

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature studies of pasta structures in nuclear matter by Maruyama et al. (2007, 2008) for hadron-quark transitions and kaon condensation, now investigating the liquid-gas mixed phase at finite temperatures relevant to supernovae, proto-neutron stars, and neutron star mergers. The results build on the framework developed by Maruyama et al. (2005) for pasta structures and connect to the spinodal instability analysis of Avancini et al. and Friedman et al. The paper shows that finite-temperature effects reduce surface tension and pasta structure size, and that the retrograde condensation phenomenon predicted by bulk calculations (Iosilevskiy, 2000) may be suppressed by finite-size effects in the full calculation.
