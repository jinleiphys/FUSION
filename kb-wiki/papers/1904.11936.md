# Higgs boson production in photon-photon interactions with proton, light-ion, and heavy-ion beams at current and future colliders

> The production of the Higgs boson in photon-photon interactions with proton and nucleus beams at three planned or proposed future CERN colliders --- the high-luminosity Large Hadron Collider (HL-LHC), the high-energy LHC (HE-LHC), and the Future Circular Collider (FCC) --- is studied. The cross sections for the process AA$\xrightarrow{γγ}$(A)H(A), with the ions A surviving the interaction and the Higgs scalar exclusively produced, are computed with MadGraph 5 modified to include the corresponding elastic $γ$ fluxes, for Pb-Pb, Xe-Xe, Kr-Kr, Ar-Ar, O-O, p-Pb, and p-p over the nucleon-nucleon collision energy range $\sqrt{s}\approx 3$--100 TeV. Simulations of the $γγ\to H \to b\bar{b}$ decay mode --- including realistic (mis)tagging and reconstruction efficiencies for the final-state b-jets, as well as appropriate kinematical selection criteria to reduce the similarly computed $γγ\to b\bar{b},c\bar{c},q\bar{q}$ continuum backgrounds --- have been carried out. Taking into account the expected luminosities for all systems, the yields and significances for observing the Higgs boson in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) are estimated. At the HL-LHC and HE-LHC, the colliding systems with larger Higgs significance are Ar-Ar(6.3 TeV) and Kr-Kr(12.5 TeV) respectively, but $3σ$ evidence for two-photon Higgs production would require 200 and 30 times larger integrated luminosities than those planned today at both machines. Factors of ten can be gained by running for a year, rather than the typical 1-month heavy-ion LHC operation, but the process will likely remain unobserved until a higher energy hadron collider, such as the FCC, is built. In the latter machine, the $5σ$ observation of Higgs production in UPCs is feasible in just the first nominal run of Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 39$ and 63 TeV respectively.

## Key claim

The production of the Higgs boson via photon-photon fusion in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) at the HL-LHC and HE-LHC will likely remain unobserved with currently planned luminosities, requiring 200 and 30 times larger integrated luminosities respectively for 3σ evidence; only at the Future Circular Collider (FCC) is a 5σ observation feasible within the first nominal run of Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions.

## Method

The authors used MadGraph 5 (v.2.6.5) modified to include elastic equivalent photon fluxes from the Weizsäcker-Williams approximation, computing cross sections for exclusive Higgs production via γγ→H followed by H→bb̅ decay for Pb-Pb, Xe-Xe, Kr-Kr, Ar-Ar, O-O, p-Pb, and p-p collisions over nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies √sNN ≈ 3–100 TeV. They simulated the γγ→bb̅, cc̅, qq̅ continuum backgrounds with the same generator, applied realistic b-jet tagging (70% efficiency) and mistagging probabilities (5% for c-jets, 1.5% for light-flavor jets), and imposed kinematical selection criteria including jet pseudorapidity cuts, transverse momentum requirements around mH/2, an angular cut |cosθj1j2| < 0.5, and a dijet mass window of ±1.4σjj around the Higgs mass.

## Key numbers

- At the HL-LHC, Ar-Ar collisions at √sNN = 6.3 TeV yield 0.40 Higgs bosons per month (nominal integrated luminosity 1.1 pb⁻¹); reaching 3σ evidence requires 200 times larger integrated luminosity
- At the HE-LHC, Kr-Kr collisions at √sNN = 12.5 TeV yield 2.4 Higgs bosons per month (nominal integrated luminosity 0.12 pb⁻¹); reaching 3σ evidence requires 30 times larger integrated luminosity
- At the FCC, Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 39 TeV with 110 nb⁻¹ integrated luminosity yield 200 Higgs bosons total (21.1 signal events after cuts), enabling 5σ observation by combining two experiments or two runs
- At the FCC, p-Pb collisions at √sNN = 63 TeV with 29 pb⁻¹ integrated luminosity yield 45 Higgs bosons total (4.8 signal events after cuts), requiring ~8 months of running for 5σ observation
- The γγ→H cross section for Pb-Pb at HL-LHC (√sNN = 5.5 TeV) is 15 pb; for Pb-Pb at FCC (√sNN = 39 TeV) it is 1.8 nb
- The dijet mass resolution at the Higgs peak is σjj ≈ 6 GeV, with the mass window set to 116–134 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by d'Enterria (2009) which showed Higgs boson production was unobservable in UPCs at the LHC without 300 times more luminosity than the standard 1-month heavy-ion operation. The present study includes lighter ion species (Xe-Xe, Kr-Kr, Ar-Ar, O-O) not previously considered, and evaluates prospects at the proposed HE-LHC and FCC. The results enable planning for future collider runs, showing that while the HL-LHC and HE-LHC cannot observe the process with planned luminosities, the FCC can achieve a 5σ observation of Higgs production in UPCs, providing an independent measurement of the H–γ loop-induced coupling via s-channel production rather than Higgs decay.
