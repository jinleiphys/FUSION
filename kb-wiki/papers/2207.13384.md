# Properties of glitching pulsars in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock framework

> We address the issues of crustal properties of neutron stars such as crustal mass, crustal radius, crustal fraction of moment of inertia and investigate the crustal and structural properties related to the glitching mechanism observed in pulsars. The mass, radius and crustal fraction of moment of inertia in neutron stars have been determined using $β$-equilibrated (npe$μ$) dense neutron star matter obtained using the extended Skyrme effective interactions with NRAPR and Brussels-Montreal parameter sets. The maximum mass of neutron star calculated from these sets is able to reach $\sim$2$M_\odot$ and higher, corroborating the recently observed masses of compact stars. The crustal fraction of the moment of inertia depends sensitively on the pressure and corresponding density at core-crust transition. The core-crust transition density and pressure together with the extracted minimum crustal fraction of the total moment of inertia provide a limit for the radii of pulsars. Present calculations imply that due to crustal entrainment the crustal fraction of the total moment of inertia is about 5.5$\%$.

## Key claim

The paper calculates crustal properties of neutron stars using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock interactions and finds that, when crustal entrainment is accounted for, the crustal fraction of the total moment of inertia is about 5.5%, which is close to the lower bound of about 7% required to explain glitches in canonical Vela-like pulsars.

## Method

The authors constructed a composite equation of state (EoS) for neutron star matter using the Feynman-Metropolis-Teller (FMT), Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS), and Baym-Bethe-Pethick (BBP) EoSs for the outer crust, combined with β-equilibrated npeμ dense matter from extended Skyrme effective interactions using the NRAPR and Brussels-Montreal (BSk22, BSk24, BSk26) parameter sets. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations, and the crustal fraction of moment of inertia was computed using the core-crust transition density and pressure determined by the dynamical method.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses: 1.94 M⊙ (NRAPR), 2.27 M⊙ (BSk22), 2.28 M⊙ (BSk24), 2.18 M⊙ (BSk26), with corresponding radii of 9.93 km, 11.21 km, 11.05 km, and 10.21 km respectively.
- Core-crust transition densities: 0.073 fm⁻³ (NRAPR), 0.0716068 fm⁻³ (BSk22), 0.0807555 fm⁻³ (BSk24), 0.0849477 fm⁻³ (BSk26); corresponding transition pressures: 0.413 MeV fm⁻³ (NRAPR), 0.290934 MeV fm⁻³ (BSk22), 0.267902 MeV fm⁻³ (BSk24), 0.363049 MeV fm⁻³ (BSk26).
- Crustal fraction of moment of inertia due to crustal entrainment: ~6.0% (NRAPR), ~6.3% (BSk22), ~5.4% (BSk24), ~5.3% (BSk26), with the paper stating an overall value of about 5.5%.
- Radius constraints from the minimum crustal fraction ΔI/I > 1.4%: R ≥ 3.71 + 3.78 M/M⊙ km (NRAPR), R ≥ 4.03 + 3.83 M/M⊙ km (BSk22), R ≥ 3.90 + 3.91 M/M⊙ km (BSk24), R ≥ 3.67 + 3.81 M/M⊙ km (BSk26).
- The β-equilibrium proton fraction forbids the direct URCA process for NRAPR but permits it for BSk22, BSk24, and BSk26.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron star crustal properties using Skyrme interactions, specifically building on the NRAPR parameter set of Steiner (2005) and the Brussels-Montreal parameter sets of Goriely (2013). The analysis of glitching pulsars follows the framework of Link (1999), who established that glitches require ΔI/I > 1.4% if entrainment is ignored, and of Andersson (2012) and Chamel (2013), who raised this bound to about 7% when crustal entrainment is considered. The results enable constraints on neutron star radii from glitch observations and test the consistency of different Skyrme interactions with observed pulsar masses such as PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432, and PSR J0740+6620.
