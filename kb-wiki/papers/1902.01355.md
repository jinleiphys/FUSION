# QED and Fundamental Symmetries in Positronium Decays

> We discuss positronium decays with emphasis on tests of fundamental symmetries and the constraints from measurements of other precision observables involving electrons and photons.

# QED and Fundamental Symmetries in Positronium Decays

## Key claim
Precision measurements of positronium decay rates are consistent with QED theoretical predictions, but the present experimental uncertainties (∼10⁻⁴) are very much larger than the theoretical uncertainties on the QED calculations, by a factor of 100 for ortho-positronium and by 10,000 for para-positronium, calling for increased experimental precision. Constraints from other precision observables involving electrons and photons, particularly the fine structure constant and the electron electric dipole moment, place strong upper bounds on possible new physics contributions to positronium decays.

## Method
The paper reviews the QED calculations of positronium decay rates for both ortho-positronium (o-Ps → 3γ, 5γ) and para-positronium (p-Ps → 2γ, 4γ) using non-relativistic QED formalism evaluated to two-loop (NNLO) level. It then compares these theoretical predictions with existing experimental measurements and analyzes constraints from precision determinations of the fine structure constant (via electron g-2, Cs and Rb atom interferometry, and the Quantum Hall Effect) and the electron electric dipole moment. The paper also surveys discrete symmetry tests (C, CP, CPT) in positronium decays and discusses rare and exotic decay channels including axion decays and invisible decays to mirror matter.

## Context
This paper reviews the status of positronium decay physics in the context of new experiments coming online, particularly the J-PET detector (Jagiellonian Positron Emission Tomograph) being built in Krakow, which is designed for both medical imaging and fundamental physics research. The work extends and updates earlier reviews by Cassidy (2018), Karshenboim (2005), and Gninenko (2002), while incorporating the latest precision measurements of the fine structure constant from Parker (2018) and the electron EDM from Andreev (2018). The paper enables future experimental searches for discrete symmetry violation and exotic decays by providing the theoretical framework and constraints from other precision observables, with J-PET aiming to improve CP and CPT observable accuracy to 10⁻⁵ and probe invisible decay branching ratios down to 10⁻⁸.
