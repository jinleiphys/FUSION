# Two-loop anomalous dimensions for the structure function h1

> Chiral-odd structure function h1 is expected to be measured in polarized Drell-Yan process. We calculate two-loop anomalous dimensions for h1 in the minimal subtraction scheme. Dimensional regularization and Feynman gauge are used for calculating the two-loop contributions. Our results are important in studying Q^2 dependence of h1.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the two-loop (next-to-leading-order) anomalous dimensions for the chiral-odd structure function \(h_1\) in the minimal subtraction (MS) scheme, finding that the results are exactly the same as those for the nonsinglet unpolarized case from Floratos, Ross, and Sachrajda (1977) for several diagrams, because the operator vertex factor \(\gamma_5 \Deltasl \, \Omegasl\) separates from the momentum integrals.

## Method

The authors compute two-loop anomalous dimensions for the twist-two chiral-odd operators that define the transversity distribution \(h_1\), using dimensional regularization in \(d=4-\epsilon\) dimensions and the Feynman gauge. They evaluate one-particle-irreducible Feynman diagrams contributing to the operator renormalization constant, extracting the \(1/\epsilon\) pole terms to obtain the anomalous dimensions via the relation \(\gamma_{Q^n} = -2 Z_1^{Q^n}\). The calculation follows the nonsinglet formalism of Floratos, Ross, and Sachrajda (1977), with many diagram results taken directly from that work, while remaining diagrams (e, f, i, j, k, n, o) are evaluated explicitly using integrals listed in the appendices.

## Key numbers

* The total two-loop anomalous dimension for \(h_1\) (with the factor \(g^4/(4\pi)^4\) taken out) is:
  \[
  \gamma_n^{total} = C_F^2 \left[ 32 S_1(n) \{ S_2(n) - S_2'(n/2) \} + 24 S_2(n) - 8 S_3'(n/2) + 64 \widetilde S(n) - 8 \frac{1-(-1)^n}{n(n+1)} - 3 \right]
  + C_F T_R \left[ -\frac{160}{9} S_1(n) + \frac{32}{3} S_2(n) + \frac{4}{3} \right]
  + C_F C_G \left[ \frac{536}{9} S_1(n) - \frac{88}{3} S_2(n) + 4 S_3'(n/2) - 32 \hat S(n) - 16 S_1(n) \{2 S_2(n) - S_2'(n/2)\} + 4 \frac{1-(-1)^n}{n(n+1)} - \frac{17}{3} \right]
  \]
  where \(C_F = (N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)\), \(C_G = N_c\), \(T_R = N_f/2\), and the sums are defined as \(S_j(n)=\sum_{k=1}^n 1/k^j\), \(S_j'(n/2)=[\{1+(-1)^n\} S_j(n/2) + \{1-(-1)^n\} S_j((n-1)/2)]/2\), \(\widetilde S(n)=\sum_{k=1}^n (-1)^k S_1(k)/k^2\), and \(\hat S(n)=\sum_{k=1}^n S_1(k)/k^2\).

* Diagram (d) contributes \(\gamma_n^{(2d)} = 8 g^4 C_F^2 [G_1(n) - S_1(n)]/(4\pi)^4\) with \(G_1(n)=\sum_{j=1}^n (1/j) \sum_{i=1}^j (1/i)\).

* Diagram (e) gives \(\gamma_n^{(2e)} = - g^4/(4\pi)^4 \cdot C_F T_R \cdot 16/9\).

* Diagram (f) gives \(\gamma_n^{(2f)} = + g^4/(4\pi)^4 \cdot C_F C_G \cdot 32/9\).

* Diagram (i) gives \(\gamma_n^{(2i)} = - g^4/(4\pi)^4 \cdot (C_F^2 - C_F C_G/2) \cdot 8 S_1(n)/n\).

* Diagram (j) gives \(\gamma_n^{(2j)} = 0\).

* Diagram (k) gives \(\gamma_n^{(2k)} = - g^4/(4\pi)^4 \cdot (C_F^2 - C_F C_G/2) \cdot 8 \{1-(-1)^n\} / [n(n+1)]\).

* Diagram (n) gives \(\gamma_n^{(2n)} = + g^4/(4\pi)^4 \cdot C_F C_G \cdot 4 [ (1/(2n))\{4 S_1(n) - S_1^2(n) - S_2(n)\} + S_3(n) - 2 \hat S(n) ]\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order (LO) \(Q^2\) evolution of \(h_1\) studied by Artru and Mekhfi (1990), Koike and Tanaka (1995), and Barone, Calarco, and Drago (1997) to next-to-leading order (NLO), analogous to the NLO evolution established for the polarized structure function \(g_1\) by Mertig and van Neerven (1996) and Vogelsang (1996). The results enable NLO QCD analyses of future polarized Drell-Yan data from the RHIC Spin project, where \(h_1\) is expected to be measured with transversely polarized protons. The authors note that their anomalous dimensions agree with the independent calculations by Vogelsang (1997) and by Hayashigaki, Kanazawa, and Koike (1997).
