# Chiral symmetry restoration in hadron spectra

> Invited talk given at Int. School on Nuclear Physics "Quarks in Hadrons and Nuclei", September 2002, Erice/Sicily/Italy; to appear in Progr. Part. Nucl. Phys., vol. 50; 10 pages

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry can be effectively restored in highly excited hadrons without any change to the QCD vacuum itself, because the valence quarks in these states decouple from the chiral condensates that break the symmetry in the vacuum. This "chiral symmetry restoration of the second kind" is a smooth phenomenon that becomes relevant only for states with masses above approximately 1.7 GeV in baryons and above 1.8 GeV in mesons.

## Method

The author uses operator product expansion (OPE) analysis of two-point correlation functions at large spacelike momenta \(Q^2\), combined with dispersion relations, to show that chirally nontrivial condensates (such as \(\langle \bar q q \rangle\)) are suppressed by inverse powers of \(Q^2\) and become irrelevant at asymptotically high energies. The argument is then applied to the spectral densities \(\rho(s)\) extracted from these correlators, which must become chirally symmetric at large \(s\). The author compares this theoretical prediction with experimental baryon and meson spectra from PDG tables and from partial wave analyses of \(p \bar p\) annihilation at LEAR.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon spectrum shows three almost perfect parity doublets with spins \(J=1/2, 3/2, 5/2\) starting at mass \(M \sim 1.7\) GeV, all with **** or *** PDG status.
- In the delta spectrum, parity doublets appear starting from the mass region 1.9–2 GeV.
- Meson degeneracies observed: \(\pi(1801 \pm 13)\) MeV with \(f_0(1770 \pm 12)\) MeV; \(\pi(2070 \pm 35)\) MeV with \(f_0(2040 \pm 38)\) MeV; \(\pi(2360 \pm 25)\) MeV with \(f_0(2337 \pm 14)\) MeV.
- \(a_0(2025 \pm ?)\) MeV is approximately degenerate with \(\eta(2010^{+35}_{-60})\) MeV.
- The \(f_0\) state at \(2102 \pm 13\) MeV is excluded from the analysis as a possible glueball candidate, not a \(q\bar q\) state.

## Context

This work extends the concept of chiral symmetry restoration from the thermal/density phase transition (where the vacuum itself changes) to a new mechanism where the valence quarks decouple from the vacuum condensates at high excitation energies, as first proposed by Glozman (2001). The author contrasts this with the constituent quark model, which cannot explain systematic parity doubling, and instead argues that a string description with massless quarks at the ends is compatible with the observed restoration. The analysis of meson spectra uses data from Bugg (2000, 2001) and PDG, and the baryon analysis relies on PDG classifications. The results enable a clear experimental program: searching for missing chiral partners in both nucleon and delta spectra at higher masses, and identifying the predicted \(a_0\) and \(\eta\) states around 1.8 GeV and 2.3 GeV.
