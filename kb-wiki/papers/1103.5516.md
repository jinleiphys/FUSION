# $J/־¨$-nuclear bound states

> $J/־¨$-nuclear bound state energies are calculated for a range of nuclei by solving the Proca (Klein-Gordon) equation. Critical input for the calculations, namely the medium-modified $D$ and $D^*$ meson masses, as well as the nucleon density distributions in nuclei, are obtained from the quark-meson coupling model. The attractive potential originates from the $D$ and $D^*$ meson loops in the $J/־¨$ self-energy in nuclear medium. It appears that $J/־¨$-nuclear bound states should produce a clear experimental signature provided that the $J/־¨$ meson is produced in recoilless kinematics.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that $J/\Psi$-nuclear bound states should exist for a range of nuclei from $^4$He to $^{208}$Pb, with binding energies up to about 19 MeV, but only under the condition that the $J/\Psi$ meson is produced in recoilless kinematics (nearly at rest relative to the nucleus). The bound states are predicted to be narrow enough to be clearly observed experimentally, with the strong interaction width set to zero under these kinematics.

## Method

The $J/\Psi$ scalar potential in nuclear matter was calculated from the sum of $D\bar D$, $D\bar D^*$, $D^*\bar D$, and $D^*\bar D^*$ meson loop contributions to the $J/\Psi$ self-energy, using non-gauged effective Lagrangians with coupling constants from vector meson dominance. The medium-modified $D$ and $D^*$ meson masses and the nucleon density distributions for finite nuclei were obtained consistently from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. Using a local density approximation, the $J/\Psi$-nuclear potentials were constructed, and the bound state energies were computed by solving the Klein-Gordon equation (reduced from the Proca equation) in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- $J/\Psi$ scalar potential maximum depth in finite nuclei: approximately 18 MeV to 24 MeV (for cutoff masses 1500 and 2000 MeV)
- $J/\Psi$-$^4$He 1s bound state energy: -4.19 MeV (cutoff 1500 MeV) and -5.74 MeV (cutoff 2000 MeV)
- $J/\Psi$-$^{12}$C 1s bound state energy: -9.33 MeV (cutoff 1500 MeV) and -11.21 MeV (cutoff 2000 MeV)
- $J/\Psi$-$^{40}$Ca 1s bound state energy: -14.96 MeV (cutoff 1500 MeV) and -17.24 MeV (cutoff 2000 MeV)
- $J/\Psi$-$^{208}$Pb 1s bound state energy: -16.83 MeV (cutoff 1500 MeV) and -19.10 MeV (cutoff 2000 MeV)
- Free space $J/\Psi$ natural width: approximately 93 keV (negligible for bound state calculations)
- Estimated width from $J/\Psi + N \to \eta_c + N$ process: approximately 0.8 MeV at nuclear matter density $\rho_B = \rho_0/2$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of $J/\Psi$ mass shifts in nuclear matter (Tsushima et al., 2011) to finite nuclei, using the quark-meson coupling model for consistent treatment of nuclear structure and meson in-medium masses. It contrasts with earlier estimates by Brodsky (1989) of up to 400 MeV binding in A=9 nuclei, and by Wasson (1991) of 30 MeV maximum binding, as well as with QCD sum rule estimates of 4-7 MeV mass decrease (Klingl 1998, Hayashigaki 1998, Kim 2000). The predicted binding energies (up to about 19 MeV) are comparable to the 8 MeV nuclear matter binding found by Ko (2000) but are obtained with a different mechanism (color-singlet meson loops rather than chromo-electric polarizability). The results enable experimental searches for $J/\Psi$-nuclear bound states at Jefferson Lab after the 12 GeV upgrade and at the FAIR facility, where recoilless kinematics may be achievable.
