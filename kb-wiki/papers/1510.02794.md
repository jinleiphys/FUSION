# Predictions of covariant chiral perturbation theory for nucleon polarisabilities and polarised Compton scattering

> We update the predictions of the SU(2) baryon chiral perturbation theory for the dipole polarisabilities of the proton, $\{α_{E1},\,β_{M1}\}_p=\{11.2(0.7),\,3.9(0.7)\}\times10^{-4}$fm$^3$, and obtain the corresponding predictions for the quadrupole, dispersive, and spin polarisabilities: $\{α_{E2},\,β_{M2}\}_p=\{17.3(3.9),\,-15.5(3.5)\}\times10^{-4}$fm$^5$, $\{α_{E1ν},\,β_{M1ν}\}_p=\{-1.3(1.0),\,7.1(2.5)\}\times10^{-4}$fm$^5$, and $\{γ_{E1E1},\,γ_{M1M1},\,γ_{E1M2},\,γ_{M1E2}\}_p=\{-3.3(0.8),\,2.9(1.5),\,0.2(0.2),\,1.1(0.3)\}\times10^{-4}$fm$^4$. The results for the scalar polarisabilities are in significant disagreement with semi-empirical analyses based on dispersion relations, however the results for the spin polarisabilities agree remarkably well. Results for proton Compton-scattering multipoles and polarised observables up to the Delta(1232) resonance region are presented too. The asymmetries $Σ_3$ and $Σ_{2x}$ reproduce the experimental data from LEGS and MAMI. Results for $Σ_{2z}$ agree with a recent sum rule evaluation in the forward kinematics. The asymmetry $Σ_{1z}$ near the pion production threshold shows a large sensitivity to chiral dynamics, but no data is available for this observable. We also provide the predictions for the polarisabilities of the neutron: $\{α_{E1},\,β_{M1}\}_n=\{13.7(3.1),\,4.6(2.7)\}\times10^{-4}$fm$^3$, $\{α_{E2},\,β_{M2}\}_n=\{16.2(3.7),\,-15.8(3.6)\}\times10^{-4}$fm$^5$, $\{α_{E1ν},\,β_{M1ν}\}_n=\{0.1(1.0),\,7.2(2.5)\}\times10^{-4}$fm$^5$, and $\{γ_{E1E1},\,γ_{M1M1},\,γ_{E1M2},\,γ_{M1E2}\}_n=\{-4.7(1.1),\,2.9(1.5),\,0.2(0.2),\,1.6(0.4)\}\times10^{-4}$fm$^4$. The neutron dynamical polarisabilities and multipoles are examined too. We also discuss subtleties related to matching dynamical and static polarisabilities.

## Key claim

The covariant chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) predictions for the proton scalar dipole polarisabilities (αE1, βM1) disagree significantly with semi-empirical analyses based on dispersion relations, while the spin polarisabilities agree remarkably well with those same dispersion-relation results.

## Method

The authors performed a next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) calculation of nucleon Compton scattering in manifestly-covariant SU(2) baryon chiral perturbation theory with pion, nucleon, and Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, using the δ-counting scheme where the nucleon-Delta mass difference is treated as an intermediate scale. They computed the invariant amplitudes from pion-nucleon loops, pion-Delta loops, and Delta-pole graphs, then extracted static polarisabilities by expanding the Breit-frame amplitudes in photon energy and identifying coefficients. For the Delta-resonance region, they resummed the pion-nucleon loop contributions to the Delta propagator and included leading pion-loop corrections to the γNΔ vertex.

## Key numbers

* Proton dipole polarisabilities: αE1 = 11.2(0.7) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, βM1 = 3.9(0.7) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
* Proton quadrupole polarisabilities: αE2 = 17.3(3.9) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵, βM2 = −15.5(3.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵
* Proton dispersive polarisabilities: αE1ν = −1.3(1.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵, βM1ν = 7.1(2.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵
* Proton spin polarisabilities: γE1E1 = −3.3(0.8) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, γM1M1 = 2.9(1.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, γE1M2 = 0.2(0.2) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, γM1E2 = 1.1(0.3) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
* Neutron dipole polarisabilities: αE1 = 13.7(3.1) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, βM1 = 4.6(2.7) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
* Neutron quadrupole polarisabilities: αE2 = 16.2(3.7) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵, βM2 = −15.8(3.6) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵
* Neutron dispersive polarisabilities: αE1ν = 0.1(1.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵, βM1ν = 7.2(2.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵
* Neutron spin polarisabilities: γE1E1 = −4.7(1.1) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, γM1M1 = 2.9(1.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, γE1M2 = 0.2(0.2) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, γM1E2 = 1.6(0.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴

## Context

This work extends the NNLO BχPT calculation of Lensky and Pascalutsa (2009) by providing predictions for quadrupole, dispersive, and spin polarisabilities of both proton and neutron, and by extending the calculation into the Delta(1232) resonance region. The results for scalar polarisabilities disagree with fixed-t dispersion relation extractions by Babusci et al. (1998) and Holstein et al. (1999), while the spin polarisabilities agree well with those same analyses. The predictions for polarised Compton asymmetries Σ₃ and Σ₂ₓ reproduce experimental data from LEGS and MAMI, and the asymmetry Σ₁₂ near pion threshold shows large sensitivity to chiral dynamics, offering a test for future experiments at MAMI and HIGS.
