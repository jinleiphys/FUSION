# Lepton universality test in the photoproduction of $e^- e^+$ versus $μ^- μ^+$ pairs on a proton target

> In view of the significantly different proton charge radius extracted from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements as compared to electronic hydrogen spectroscopy or electron scattering experiments, we study in this work the photoproduction of a lepton pair on a proton target in the limit of very small momentum transfer as a way to provide a test of the lepton universality when extracting the proton charge form factor. By detecting the recoiling proton in the $γp \to l^- l^+ p$ reaction, we show that a measurement of a ratio of $e^-e^+ + μ^-μ^+$ over $e^-e^+$ cross sections with a relative precision of around 2%, would allow for a test to distinguish between the two different proton charge radii currently extracted from muonic and electronic observables.

## Key claim

By measuring the ratio of the sum of electron-positron and muon-antimuon photoproduction cross sections to the electron-positron cross section alone on a proton target, with an absolute precision of 7 × 10⁻⁴, one can distinguish at the 3σ level between the two different proton charge radii currently extracted from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements versus electronic hydrogen spectroscopy or electron scattering.

## Method

The authors compute the Bethe-Heitler differential cross section dσ/dt dM²_ll for the γp → l⁻l⁺p reaction in the limit of very small momentum transfer t, where the process is dominated by the proton electric form factor G_Ep. They propose detecting only the recoiling proton's momentum and angle, which determines both t and the lepton-pair invariant mass M²_ll, and then forming the ratio R_μ/e of the cross section above μ⁻μ⁺ threshold (where both e⁻e⁺ and μ⁻μ⁺ contribute) to the cross section below μ⁻μ⁺ threshold (where only e⁻e⁺ contributes). The analysis uses a photon lab energy of E_γ = 0.5 GeV and considers momentum transfers −t = 0.01, 0.02, and 0.03 GeV².

## Key numbers

- The muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement yields R_E = 0.8409 (4) fm (Antognini 2013), while electronic hydrogen spectroscopy yields R_E = 0.8775 (51) fm (Mohr 2012); these differ by about 7 standard deviations.
- A lepton-universality-violating scenario with G^μ_Ep / G^e_Ep = 1.01 (motivated by the ~1% larger G_Ep at −t = 0.03 GeV² from the muonic radius) produces a difference in the ratio R_μ/e of about 2 × 10⁻³.
- A measurement of R_μ/e with an absolute precision of 7 × 10⁻⁴ would test this difference at the 3σ level.
- At E_γ = 0.5 GeV and −t = 0.01 GeV², the recoil proton momentum is |p'|^lab = 100 MeV/c; at −t = 0.02 GeV² it is 142 MeV/c; at −t = 0.03 GeV² it is 174 MeV/c.
- Around M²_ll = 0.06 GeV², the μ⁻μ⁺ cross section is about a factor of 10 smaller than the e⁻e⁺ cross section.
- The ratio R_μ/e varies between 1.10 and 1.14 in the kinematic range where only e⁻e⁺ and μ⁻μ⁺ channels contribute.

## Context

This work addresses the "proton radius puzzle" arising from the discrepancy between the proton charge radius extracted from muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013) and from electronic hydrogen spectroscopy (Mohr 2012) or electron-proton elastic scattering (Bernauer 2010, Bernauer 2013). It proposes a new experimental approach complementary to the MUSE experiment (Gilman 2013), which plans simultaneous μp and ep elastic scattering measurements, and to ongoing low-Q² ep scattering experiments (Mihovilovic 2014, Gasparian 2014). The key advantage is that the ratio measurement cancels overall normalization uncertainties and first-order radiative corrections, and can be performed at existing facilities such as MAMI and Jefferson Lab. The authors estimate that the dominant background from γp → π⁰γp can be controlled with 10-30% precision on that cross section, and the timelike Compton scattering background is about a factor of 5 smaller than the 1% G_Ep variation effect.
