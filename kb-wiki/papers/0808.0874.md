# Vacuum Decay Time in Strong External Fields

> We consider dynamics of vacuum decay and particle production in the context of short pulse laser experiments. We identify and evaluate the invariant "materialization time," $τ$, the timescale for the conversion of an electromagnetic field energy into particles, and we compare to the laser related time scales.

# Vacuum Decay Time in Strong External Fields

## Key claim

The paper identifies and evaluates the invariant "materialization time" τ, the timescale for conversion of electromagnetic field energy into electron-positron pairs, and finds that a field of order 0.2E₀ is sufficient for observable materialization even though the critical field E₀ is not required for a clear signal of vacuum decay processes.

## Method

The authors calculate the rate of energy transfer to electron-positron pairs by integrating the tunneling probability for pair creation over transverse momentum states, using the adiabatic Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger (EHS) switch-on limit. They define the materialization time τ as the ratio of available field energy density to the rate of electromagnetic field energy conversion into particles. The calculation is extended to include constant homogeneous magnetic fields by converting the transverse momentum integration into a sum over Landau levels.

## Key numbers

- Critical field strength: E₀ = 1.32 × 10¹⁸ V/m, corresponding to critical power intensity 4.65 × 10³³ W/m²
- Materialization time for E/E₀ = 0.2: τ = 2800 fs, with pair production rate W = 8.234 × 10¹⁵ μm⁻³ fs⁻¹
- Materialization time for E/E₀ = 1: τ = 8.85 × 10⁻³ fs, with pair production rate W = 5.903 × 10²² μm⁻³ fs⁻¹
- Materialization time for E/E₀ = 0.402: τ = 1 fs, with pair production rate W = 9.68 × 10¹⁹ μm⁻³ fs⁻¹
- Characteristic frequency ω₀ = α mc²/π²ℏ = 5.740 × 10¹⁷ s⁻¹, giving ω₀⁻¹/4 = 0.435 attoseconds
- At E/E₀ = 0.2 with 10⁻¹⁵ s pulse duration, 0.036% of field energy converts, producing approximately 280 nC of electrons per μm⁻³
- For near-critical fields in relativistic focusing, formation of electron-positron-photon plasma domains of O(50 nm³) with T ≃ 2 MeV is predicted

## Context

This work extends the study of vacuum decay from heavy ion collisions (Pieper 1969, Mueller 1972, Rafelski 1974) and cosmological models (Coleman 1977) to the context of short-pulse laser experiments. The authors build on the Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger pair production mechanism (Euler and Heisenberg 1936, Schwinger 1951) and prior work on pair production in time-dependent fields (Brezin 1970, Narozhnyi 1970, Kim 2006) and back reaction (Mueller 1975, Casher 1979, Cooper 1993, Kluger 1998, Tomaras 2000). The concept of materialization time enables experimental design for observing vacuum decay with pulsed lasers and provides a framework for studying strongly coupled QED plasmas analogous to quark-gluon plasma.
