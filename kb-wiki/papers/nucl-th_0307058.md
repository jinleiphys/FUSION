# Reevaluation of the density dependence of nucleon radius and mass in the global color symmetry model of QCD

> With the global color symmetry model (GCM) at finite chemical potential, the density dependence of the bag constant, the total energy and the radius of a nucleon in nuclear matter is investigated. A relation between the nuclear matter density and the chemical potential with the action of QCD being taken into account is obtained. A maximal nuclear matter density for the existence of the bag with three quarks confined within is given. The calculated results indicate that, before the maximal density is reached, the bag constant and the total energy of a nucleon decrease, and the radius of a nucleon increases slowly, with the increasing of the nuclear matter density. As the maximal nuclear matter density is reached, the mass of the nucleon vanishes and the radius becomes infinite suddenly. It manifests that a phase transition from nucleons to quarks takes place.

# Reevaluation of the Density Dependence of Nucleon Radius and Mass in the Global Color Symmetry Model of QCD

## Key claim

The global color symmetry model (GCM) predicts that nucleons in nuclear matter undergo a first-order phase transition to deconfined quark matter at a critical density of approximately 12 times normal nuclear matter density, but only when the self-interaction among quarks is included; without this interaction (i.e., in the Fermi gas approximation), the deconfinement transition would occur at densities only slightly above normal.

## Method

The authors extended the global color symmetry model (GCM) of QCD to finite chemical potential by transforming the quark field under a baryon-number constraint and deriving the GCM action in nuclear matter. They solved the truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations using an infrared-dominant form for the effective gluon propagator, obtained the bag constant from the vacuum configuration of the bilocal field, and derived a relation between nuclear matter density and chemical potential from the generating functional. The nucleon was treated as a nontopological soliton bag, with its total energy, radius, and bag constant evaluated as functions of density under the equilibrium condition dE/dR = 0.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density is denoted as ρ₀; the critical density for deconfinement is ρ_c ≈ 12 ρ₀, corresponding to a chemical potential μ ≈ 0.316 GeV.
- In free space (μ = 0, ρ = 0), the nucleon mass is calibrated to M₀ = 939 MeV and the nucleon radius to R₀ = 0.8 fm.
- The bag constant in free space is B₀ = (172 MeV)⁴.
- The energy-scale parameter η = 1.220 GeV and the correction parameter Z₀ = 3.303 were obtained by fitting to the free-space nucleon properties.
- The relation between nuclear matter density and chemical potential in the GCM is ρ_n = (1/π²)[μ³ + (η²/4)μ], which differs from the Fermi gas approximation ρ_n = (2/(3π²))μ³ by a linear term.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GCM calculation (Liu et al., 2001) which used a Fermi gas approximation for the density-chemical potential relation, by now including the quark self-interaction through the full GCM action. The results are qualitatively consistent with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model (as in the works of Jin and Jennings, 1997; Lu et al., 1998; and others), which introduced a phenomenological density-dependent bag constant. The finding of a critical density near 12ρ₀ provides a QCD-based foundation for the nucleon swell hypothesis used to explain the EMC effect (as reviewed by Li et al., 1994) and suggests that hybrid hadron-quark matter or pure quark matter may exist in the centers of neutron stars, similar to earlier results (Prakash, 1997).
