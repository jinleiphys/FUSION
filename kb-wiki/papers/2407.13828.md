# Anisotropic charge transport in strongly magnetized relativistic matter

> We investigate electrical charge transport in hot magnetized plasma using first-principles quantum field theoretical methods. By employing Kubo's linear response theory, we express the electrical conductivity tensor in terms of the fermion damping rate in the Landau-level representation. Utilizing leading-order results for the damping rates from a recent study within a gauge theory, we derive the transverse and longitudinal conductivities for a strongly magnetized plasma. The analytical expressions reveal drastically different mechanisms that explain the high anisotropy of charge transport in a magnetized plasma. Specifically, the transverse conductivity is suppressed, while the longitudinal conductivity is enhanced by a strong magnetic field. As in the case of zero magnetic field, longitudinal conduction is determined by the probability of charge carriers to remain in their quantum states without damping. In contrast, transverse conduction critically relies on quantum transitions between Landau levels, effectively lifting charge trapping in localized Landau orbits. We examine the temperature and magnetic field dependence of the transverse and longitudinal electrical conductivities over a wide range of parameters and investigate the effects of a nonzero chemical potential. Additionally, we extend our analysis to strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma and study the impact of the coupling constant on the anisotropy of electrical charge transport.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical expressions for the transverse and longitudinal electrical conductivities of a strongly magnetized relativistic plasma using first-principles quantum field theory, showing that the high anisotropy arises because longitudinal conduction is proportional to the inverse damping rate (and thus enhanced by strong magnetic fields) while transverse conduction is proportional to the damping rate itself (and thus suppressed), with the caveat that the leading-order approximation is valid only when the magnetic field satisfies \(|eB| \gtrsim \alpha T^2\) to ensure that one-to-two and two-to-one processes dominate over two-to-two scattering.

## Method

The authors employ Kubo's linear response theory to express the electrical conductivity tensor in terms of the retarded current-current correlation function at the one-loop level. They use a spectral representation for the fermion propagator in the Landau-level representation, replacing zero-width delta-function peaks with Lorentzian functions whose widths are given by the fermion damping rates \(\Gamma_{f,n}(k_z)\) obtained from a prior leading-order gauge theory calculation (Ghosh and Shovkovy, 2024). The transverse and longitudinal conductivities are then computed by numerically integrating the resulting expressions over energy and longitudinal momentum, using tabulated damping rate data for up to \(n_{\rm max}=50\) Landau levels and about 50 points of longitudinal momentum.

## Key numbers

- Transverse conductivity in chiral QED is approximated by the Pade approximant \(\tilde{\sigma}_{\perp, {\rm chiral}}^{\rm QED} \simeq 0.076 \frac{1 + 0.13 b^{3/2} -0.002 b^2 + 0.0026 b^{7/2}}{1 + 6.5 b + 31.6 b^2 + 13.2 b^3}\) where \(b=|eB|/T^2\), with a local minimum at \(b_{{\rm min},\perp}\approx 12\).
- Longitudinal conductivity in chiral QED is approximated by \(\tilde{\sigma}_{\parallel, {\rm chiral}}^{\rm QED} \simeq 2.47 \frac{ 1 - 0.172 b + 0.125 b^2 - 0.0058 b^3 + 8.1\times 10^{-4} b^4 - 8.6\times 10^{-6} b^5 + 2.88\times 10^{-7} b^6}{1 - 0.013 b + 5.4\times 10^{-5}b^2 - 5.6\times 10^{-8} b^3}\), with a local minimum at \(b_{{\rm min},\parallel}\approx 0.6\).
- The leading-order damping rate in a strongly magnetized plasma is of order \(\alpha |e_f B|/T\), compared to order \(\alpha^2 T\) in the absence of a magnetic field.
- Numerical data for conductivities are generated for temperatures \(15 m_e \leq T \leq 80 m_e\) and magnetic fields \((15m_e)^2 \leq |eB| \leq (200m_e)^2\), where \(m_e = 0.511\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior quantum field theoretic studies of electrical conductivity in magnetized plasmas (Hattori and collaborators, 2016; Fukushima and collaborators, 2017-2019) by using the Landau-level representation for Green's functions and the fermion damping rate derived in Ghosh and Shovkovy (2024). It goes beyond kinetic theory approaches (Kurian and collaborators, 2017; Das and collaborators, 2019; Thakur and collaborators, 2019; Dey and collaborators, 2020) and the relaxation-time approximation by providing first-principles results that reveal the distinct mechanisms for transverse and longitudinal transport. The results enable quantitative predictions for charge transport in magnetized quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions and in the magnetospheres of magnetars, and can be extended to other relativistic plasmas with minor modifications.
