# Scaling of space and timelike response of confined relativistic particles

> The response of a relativistic particle bound in a linear confining well is calculated as a function of the momentum and energy transfer, q, ν. At large values of |q| the response exhibits scaling in the variable y=ν-|q|, which is proportional to the Nachtmann variable, ξ. The approach to scaling is studied at smaller values of |q|. Scaling occurs at ν~|q| at relatively small |q|, and its validity extends over the entire ξrange as |q| increases; this behavior is observed in electron-proton scattering. About 10% of the response at large |q| is in the timelike region where ν>|q|, and it is necessary to include it to fulfill the particle number sum rule. The Gross-Llewellyn Smith and Gottfried sum rules are discussed in the context of these results.

## Key claim

The response of a relativistic particle bound in a linear confining well exhibits scaling in the variable \(\tilde{y} = \nu - |\mathbf{q}|\) (proportional to the Nachtmann variable \(\xi\)) at large momentum transfer, but about 10% of the response lies in the timelike region (\(\nu > |\mathbf{q}|\)) and must be included to satisfy the particle number sum rule, contrary to the parton model assumption that the response is entirely spacelike.

## Method

The authors model a hadron as a single light valence quark confined by a linear flux-tube potential with an infinitely massive color source, using the Hamiltonian \(H = \sqrt{|\mathbf{p}|^2 + m_q^2} + \sqrt{\sigma} \, r\) in the massless quark limit (\(m_q = 0\)). They diagonalize this Hamiltonian in a spherical basis of Bessel functions up to \(\ell \leq 100\) and 200–300 basis states per \(\ell\), compute the exact response \(R(|\mathbf{q}|, \nu)\) from Eq. (1) including all states with \(n \leq 100\) and \(\ell \leq 100\), and smooth the resulting delta functions by introducing a decay width \(\Gamma(\nu)\) parameterized with threshold \(E_t = 100\) MeV and variable \(\Gamma_0\).

## Key numbers

* The integrated response sum rule \(\int_0^\infty R(\mathbf{q}, \nu) d\nu = 1\) is satisfied to within \(< 0.02\%\) error for all momentum transfers considered.
* In the limit \(\Gamma_0 = 0\), about \(9.6\%\) of the response at large \(|\mathbf{q}|\) lies in the timelike region, independent of \(\sqrt{\sigma}\); this fraction increases to \(13.7\%\) for \(\Gamma_0 = 100\) MeV and \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 1\) GeV/fm.
* The elastic scattering strength drops from \(0.85\) at \(|\mathbf{q}| = 0.25\) GeV to \(0.00086\) at \(|\mathbf{q}| = 2.0\) GeV.
* The first inelastic peak (lowest excited state with \(n=1, \ell=1\)) lies 335 MeV above the ground state.
* The Regge trajectories from the model have slopes within \(10\%\) of the classical estimate \(4\sqrt{\sigma}\).

## Context

This work extends the many-body theory approach to deep inelastic scattering (DIS) developed by Benhar, Pandharipande, and Sick (2000), which introduced \(\tilde{y}\) scaling as an alternative to Bjorken scaling. The toy model reproduces the approach to \(\xi\) scaling observed in experimental data from Keppel (proton and deuteron) and the scaling behavior seen in electron-proton scattering. The results suggest that nonperturbative binding effects can shift a portion of the valence quark response into the timelike region, potentially explaining why the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule (measured by CCFR, 1998) and the Gottfried sum rule (measured by NMC, 1995) give values about 10% below the naive parton model expectations of 3 and 1/3, respectively.
