# Jet angularities in photoproduction at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We consider the one-parameter family of jet substructure observables known as angularities using the specific case of inclusive jets arising from photoproduction events at an Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). We perform numerical calculations at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy within perturbative QCD and compare our results to PYTHIA 6 predictions. Overall, we find good agreement and conclude that jet substructure observables are feasible at the EIC despite the relatively low jet transverse momentum and particle multiplicities. We investigate the size of subleading power corrections relevant at low energies within the Monte Carlo setup. In order to establish the validity of the Monte Carlo tune, we also perform comparisons to jet shape data at HERA. We further discuss detector requirements necessary for angularity measurements at an EIC, focusing on hadron calorimeter energy and spatial resolutions. Possible applications of precision jet substructure measurements at the EIC include the tuning of Monte Carlo event generators, the extraction of nonperturbative parameters and studies of cold nuclear matter effects.

## Key claim

Jet substructure observables, specifically jet angularities, are feasible at the Electron-Ion Collider despite the relatively low jet transverse momentum and particle multiplicities, provided that the definition of the observable is chosen to avoid large hadron mass corrections. The authors qualify that the agreement between next-to-leading logarithmic perturbative QCD calculations and PYTHIA 6 Monte Carlo predictions is good, but that power corrections become numerically more important for smaller values of the angularity parameter \(a\).

## Method

The authors performed numerical calculations of jet angularity distributions at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy (NLL') within perturbative QCD using Soft Collinear Effective Theory, including resummation of large logarithms and a shape function to model nonperturbative hadronization corrections. They compared these results to pseudo-data generated with PYTHIA 6 using the CTEQ5m proton PDF and SAS 1D-LO photon PDF, with jets reconstructed using the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm in the laboratory frame for photoproduction events at a center-of-mass energy of \(\sqrt{s}=141\) GeV. The Monte Carlo tune was validated by comparing to ZEUS jet shape data from HERA.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive jet yield at \(\sqrt{s}=141\) GeV: jets with \(p_T \sim 10\) GeV will be produced with sufficiently high statistics for \(10\) fb\(^{-1}\) of integrated luminosity.
- Jet transverse momentum cut: \(p_T > 10\) GeV.
- Photon virtuality cutoff: \(Q_{\text{max}}^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- Inelasticity cut: \(0.2 < y < 0.8\).
- Jet radii studied: \(R = 0.4\) and \(R = 0.8\).
- Beam energies: electron energy \(E_e = 20\) GeV, proton energy \(E_p = 250\) GeV.
- Center-of-mass energy: \(\sqrt{s} = 141\) GeV.
- Laboratory frame rapidity range: \(-2 < \eta_{\text{lab}} < 4\).
- Landau pole cutoff for QCD coupling: \(450\) MeV.
- Monte Carlo particle-level cut: transverse momenta greater than \(250\) MeV/\(c\) and pseudorapidity of \(\pm 4\).

## Context

This work extends the study of jet substructure observables, previously developed for proton-proton collisions at RHIC and the LHC, to the lower-energy environment of the EIC. It builds on prior calculations of inclusive jet production in photoproduction at HERA by Hinderer et al. (2015) and Abelof et al. (2016), and on the factorization framework for jet angularities in proton-proton collisions by Kang et al. (2018). The results enable future precision tests of QCD at the EIC, including tuning of Monte Carlo event generators, extraction of nonperturbative parameters, and studies of cold nuclear matter effects in electron-nucleus collisions.
