# Maximum latent heat of neutron star matter independently of General Relativity

> We establish bounds on the maximum possible specific latent heat of cold neutron-star matter derived from hadron physics alone. Existing chiral perturbation theory computations for the equation of state, together with perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics, relevant at highest densities (even if they would turn out not to be physically realizable) bind the maximum latent heat which is possible in actual neutron stars. Because these are already near gravitational collapse in General Relativity, no denser form of cold matter can exist: thus, the bounds are a generic physical limit. Even in scenarios that modify the theory of gravity, the existence of a family of latent-heat maxima is relevant to diagnose progress in the knowledge of the equation of state of neutron matter, by quantifying the maximum possible (presumed) phase transition that its error bands would allow. Thus, latent heat is a natural benchmark for the equation of state in cold QCD.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the maximum possible specific latent heat of cold neutron-star matter, derived purely from hadron physics (chiral perturbation theory and perturbative QCD) without assuming General Relativity, is bounded by about 0.7 (for the relativistic definition L|_ε) or about 1 (for the nonrelativistic definition L|_n). These bounds are generic physical limits because neutron stars are already near gravitational collapse, so no denser cold matter can exist.

## Method

The authors systematically sample the theoretically allowed equation of state (EoS) band for neutron-star matter, constructed from low-density chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) up to about 1.3 nuclear saturation density and high-density perturbative QCD (pQCD) at chemical potentials above 2.6 GeV, with intermediate densities constrained by monotony, causality (0 ≤ c_s² ≤ 1), and integral constraints from the (n, μ) plane. For each EoS in a 500² grid, they compute the maximum possible zero-slope stretch (first-order phase transition) that could be inserted without violating these constraints, then evaluate the specific latent heat using both the relativistic definition L|_ε = P_H (ε_E − ε_H)/(ε_E ε_H) and the nonrelativistic definition L|_n = ΔE/(N M_N).

## Key numbers

- Maximum L|_ε ≈ 0.7, reached at ε ≈ 5 ε_sat (where ε_sat ≈ 153 MeV/fm³), for an EoS matched to nuclear matter with maximum slope c_s² ≈ 1 at 1.3 n_sat.
- Maximum L|_n ≈ 1, reached at ε ≈ 5 ε_sat.
- The pQCD matching point is at ε = 1.5 GeV/fm³ with P ∈ (1.65, 4.7) GeV/fm³, and at μ ≥ 2.6 GeV corresponding to ε ≈ 15 GeV/fm³.
- Nuclear saturation density n_sat ≈ 0.16/fm³, with binding energy B/A ≈ 16 ± 1 MeV.
- The difference between L|_n and L|_ε remains within 15% up to ε ≈ 5.8 ε_sat for the softest EoS, and up to ε ≈ 2.6 ε_sat for the stiffest EoS.

## Context

This work extends the nEoS framework of Oter et al. (2019), which systematically maps uncertainty in the neutron-star EoS from hadron physics alone, and incorporates new integral constraints from causality in the (n, μ) plane from Komoltsev and Kurkela (2021). The latent heat bounds are compared to the Seidov limit (1971), which assumes General Relativity and a small exotic core; the hadron-physics bound is less tight than the Seidov limit for densities relevant to GR, but becomes the relevant constraint for modified gravity theories. The authors propose L|_ε(ε_H) as a diagnostic for future progress in constraining the EoS, noting that improvements from functional renormalization group methods (Leonhardt et al., 2019) could shrink the allowed band.
