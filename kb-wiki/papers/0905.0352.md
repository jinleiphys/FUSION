# Deconfinement Phase Transition in Compact Stars : Maxwell vs. Gibbs Construction of the Mixed Phase

> We study different realisations of the first order deconfinement phase transition inside a compact star by comparing the Gibbs and Maxwell construction for the mixed phase. The hadronic sector is described within the relativistic mean field model including hyperons. The quark sector is described by the MIT Bag model. We find that these two realisations lead to very different star properties, in particular, the composition of the stellar matter. We also find that for the Maxwell construction there is a sharp discontinuity in the baryon density and the electron chemical potential. We argue that a sharp jump in the elctron chemical potential should lead to the redistribution of electrons and formation of strong electric fields around the discontinuity surface.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Gibbs and Maxwell constructions for the deconfinement mixed phase in compact stars lead to very different star properties, but the Gibbs construction may be energetically too expensive due to interface and electrostatic energies, making the Maxwell construction more realistic in practice.

## Method

The hadronic phase was described using the relativistic mean field TM1 model including hyperons (p, n, Λ, Σ⁰,±, Ξ⁰,−) with additional σ* and φ meson fields for hyperon-hyperon interactions, while the quark phase was described using the MIT Bag model with three quark flavors (u, d, s). The mixed phase was constructed using both the Gibbs conditions (equal pressure, baryon chemical potential, and electric chemical potential with global charge neutrality) and the Maxwell construction (equal pressure and baryon chemical potential only, with a jump in electric chemical potential). Stellar properties were obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- For B¹/⁴ = 180 MeV with Maxwell construction: mixed phase starts at baryon density ρ_B = 0.41 fm⁻³ and ends at 0.68 fm⁻³; for Gibbs construction: starts at 0.23 fm⁻³ and ends at 0.89 fm⁻³
- For B¹/⁴ = 185 MeV with Maxwell construction: mixed phase starts at ρ_B = 0.67 fm⁻³ and ends at 0.83 fm⁻³; for Gibbs construction: starts at 0.26 fm⁻³ and ends at 1.06 fm⁻³
- Maximum stellar mass for B¹/⁴ = 180 MeV: 1.493 M_⊙ (Maxwell) vs 1.396 M_⊙ (Gibbs)
- Maximum stellar mass for B¹/⁴ = 185 MeV: 1.567 M_⊙ (Maxwell) vs 1.466 M_⊙ (Gibbs)
- For B¹/⁴ = 180 MeV Maxwell construction: electron chemical potential jumps from about 10 MeV in the quark core to about 196 MeV in the hadronic phase, giving a maximum electric field E_0 ≈ 3 MV/fm at the discontinuity surface

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the deconfinement phase transition in compact stars, particularly the Wigner-Seitz cell approach by the "pasta" group (ref. pasta in the paper) which suggested the mixed phase behaves more like the Maxwell construction. The results enable future investigation of strong electric field generation at the phase transition boundary, which could lead to phenomena such as magnetic field generation in rotating stars, building on earlier work by Alcock (1986) on electric fields at bare quark star boundaries.
