# Transverse momentum broadening at NLO and QCD evolution of $\hat q$

> Within the framework of a high-twist approach, we show the first complete next-to-leading order calculation of transverse momentum broadening in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic $e+A$ scattering and Drell-Yan dilepton production in $p+A$ collisions. We demonstrate at one-loop level how QCD factorization holds for multiple scattering in nuclear medium, and the university of the associated quark-gluon correlation function. Our calculation also identifies QCD evolution equation for the quark-gluon correlation function, which determines the QCD factorization scale and energy dependence of jet transport parameter $\hat q$.

# Transverse momentum broadening at NLO and QCD evolution of $\hat q$

## Key claim
The authors present the first complete next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation of transverse momentum broadening in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and Drell-Yan (DY) processes, demonstrating that QCD factorization holds for multiple scattering at twist-4 and that the associated quark-gluon correlation function is universal. However, the factorization is verified only at one-loop level and under the assumption that the nucleus is loosely bounded, with the evolution equation for the jet transport parameter $\hat q$ remaining unclosed except under specific kinematic limits such as large $x_B$ or an expansion around $x = x_B$.

## Method
The authors compute the $\ell_{hT}^2$-weighted differential cross section for transverse momentum broadening in SIDIS ($e+A$ scattering) and DY ($p+A$ collisions) at NLO, including both real and virtual corrections. They use dimensional regularization in $n = 4 - 2\epsilon$ dimensions to isolate collinear and soft divergences, absorb the collinear divergences into renormalized fragmentation functions and the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function $T_{qg}$ via $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme, and derive the QCD evolution equation for $T_{qg}$ and subsequently for $\hat q$.

## Key numbers
- The LO transverse momentum broadening is given by $\Delta\langle\ell_{hT}^2\rangle = \left(\frac{4\pi^2\alpha_s z_h^2}{N_c}\right) \frac{\sum_q e_q^2 T_{qg}(x_B,0,0) D_{h/q}(z_h)}{\sum_q e_q^2 f_{q/A}(x_B) D_{h/q}(z_h)}$, where $T_{qg}$ is the twist-4 quark-gluon correlation function.
- The NLO real correction contains double poles $\propto 1/\epsilon^2$ and single poles $\propto 1/\epsilon$, with the double poles canceling between real and virtual contributions.
- The virtual correction includes a term $C_F(-2/\epsilon^2 - 3/\epsilon - 8)$ in $n=4-2\epsilon$ dimensions.
- The evolution equation for $T_{qg}$ is $\mu_f^2 \frac{\partial}{\partial \mu_f^2} T_{qg}(x_B,0,0,\mu_f^2) = \frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi} \int_{x_B}^1 \frac{dx}{x} [\mathcal{P}_{qg\to qg} \otimes T_{qg} + P_{qg}(\hat x) T_{gg}(x,0,0,\mu_f^2)]$.
- In the large-$x_B$ limit ($x_B \to 1$), the formation time $\tau_f = \frac{2\hat x}{\hat z(1-\hat x)} \frac{q^-}{Q^2}$ becomes much larger than the nuclear size ($\tau_f \gg R_A$), leading to the LPM suppression and a $\mu_f$-independent $\hat q$.

## Context
This work extends the high-twist approach to multiple scattering in nuclear media, previously applied at leading order by Guo (1998) and Wang (2001), to NLO for the first time. It establishes QCD factorization for twist-4 observables, demonstrating the universality of the quark-gluon correlation function across SIDIS and DY processes, which implies that the jet transport parameter $\hat q$ is intrinsic to the medium and independent of the hard process that creates the fast parton. The derived evolution equation for $\hat q$ enables future studies of its energy and scale dependence, analogous to DGLAP evolution for parton distribution functions, and is relevant for the JET Collaboration's efforts to extract medium properties from heavy-ion collision data.
