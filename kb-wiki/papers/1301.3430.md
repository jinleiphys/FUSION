# Inhomogeneous condensates in the parity doublet model

> Within the parity doublet model coupled to the linear sigma model including vector mesons it is possible to describe vacuum properties of the low energy mesons and to achieve nuclear saturation at nonzero density. Motivated by recent studies we investigate the emergence of inhomogeneous condensation in the parity doublet model. As a first step the chiral density wave (CDW) is con- sidered, which allows for a straightforward investigation of inhomogeneous condensation. As a result it is still possible to have a homogeneous ground state of nuclear matter, but at larger baryon chemical potential the CDW is favored with respect to the homogeneous phase.

# Inhomogeneous condensates in the parity doublet model

## Key claim
Within the parity doublet model coupled to the linear sigma model including vector mesons, a homogeneous ground state of nuclear matter is still possible at nonzero density, but at larger baryon chemical potential the chiral density wave (CDW) is favored over the homogeneous phase; however, this result depends on the pion mass being physical (139 MeV), because in the chiral limit (pion mass sent to zero) the CDW is always the favored ground state and no homogeneous ground state can be found.

## Method
The parity doublet model, which includes nucleon N and its chiral partner N* with a mirror assignment, was coupled to the linear sigma model with vector mesons in the mean-field approximation. A chiral density wave Ansatz was introduced, parameterizing the sigma and neutral pion fields as spatially modulated condensates, and the grand canonical potential was extremized with respect to the condensate amplitude, the omega meson field, and the wave number f. Parameters were fixed using vacuum meson masses, the pion decay constant, and nuclear matter saturation conditions (binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV at density 0.16 fm^-3).

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter saturation conditions: binding energy per nucleon E/A - m_N = -16 MeV, saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3, corresponding to baryon chemical potential mu = 923 MeV.
- For physical pion mass m_pi = 139 MeV, the chiral condensate drops from the vacuum value f_pi to 30-60 MeV at the first-order phase transition to homogeneous nuclear matter.
- Two values of the chiral partner mass were studied: m_N* = 1200 MeV and m_N* = 1500 MeV, with the parameter m_0 varied from 600 MeV to 700 MeV to 800 MeV.
- In the homogeneous phase at intermediate mu, the condensate value ranges from 30 to 60 MeV depending on parameters.

## Context
This work extends studies of inhomogeneous condensation from quark-based models (NJL model, Gross-Neveu model) to a fully hadronized model that can describe both vacuum properties and nuclear matter saturation. It builds on the parity doublet model of Jido (1998) and Zschiesche (2006), which successfully reproduces vacuum phenomenology and finite-density physics. The chiral density wave concept originates from Migdal (1978), and the analysis follows the approach used by Nickel (2009) for the NJL model and by Schnetz (2004) and Wagner (2007) for the Gross-Neveu model. The results enable future extensions to more general inhomogeneous condensate patterns and to the extended linear sigma model including exotic matter such as tetraquarks and glueballs as studied by Gallas (2011).
