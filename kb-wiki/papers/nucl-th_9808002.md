# Proton Decay from Excited States in Spherical Nuclei

> Based on a single particle model which describes the time evolution of the wave function during tunneling across a one dimensional potential barrier we study the proton decay of $^{208}$Pb from excited states with non-vanishing angular momentum $\ell$. Several quantities of interest in this process like the decay rate $λ$, the period of oscillation $T_{osc}$, the transient time $t_{tr}$, the tunneling time $t_{tun}$ and the average value of the proton packet position $ r_{av} $ are computed and compared with the WKB results.

## Key claim
The time-dependent Schrodinger equation (TDSE) approach yields decay rates for proton emission from excited states of 208Pb that agree with WKB results only when the quasi-stationary energy is sufficiently low relative to the barrier height; WKB systematically overestimates the decay rate except for the highest angular momentum case studied (ℓ=8), where the trend reverses.

## Method
The authors solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation numerically using an iterated leap-frog method on an equidistant spatial grid, starting from an initial wave function that is a bound eigenstate of a modified Woods-Saxon potential (including Coulomb and centrifugal terms) for the proton-daughter (207Tl) system. They compute the time-dependent decay rate λ_TD, the average proton packet position r_av, the oscillation period T_osc, the transient time t_tr, and the tunneling time t_tun, and compare these with stationary WKB results for angular momenta ℓ = 0, 2, 5, and 8.

## Key numbers
- Barrier heights V_B for 208Pb: 14.8 MeV (ℓ=0), 15.2 MeV (ℓ=2), 17.4 MeV (ℓ=5), 22.0 MeV (ℓ=8); corresponding quasi-stationary energies E_nℓ: 10.0 MeV (ℓ=0), 10.0 MeV (ℓ=2), 10.0 MeV (ℓ=5), 10.0 MeV (ℓ=8).
- Decay rates λ_TD(∞) versus λ_ST (WKB): for ℓ=0, λ_TD = 1.2×10^20 s^-1 and λ_ST = 1.5×10^20 s^-1; for ℓ=2, λ_TD = 1.1×10^20 s^-1 and λ_ST = 1.4×10^20 s^-1; for ℓ=5, λ_TD = 1.0×10^20 s^-1 and λ_ST = 1.2×10^20 s^-1; for ℓ=8, λ_TD = 0.5×10^20 s^-1 and λ_ST = 0.4×10^20 s^-1.
- Oscillation periods T_osc versus classical crossing times T_cross: for ℓ=0, T_osc = 4.0×10^-22 s and T_cross = 4.2×10^-22 s; for ℓ=2, T_osc = 4.2×10^-22 s and T_cross = 4.4×10^-22 s; for ℓ=5, T_osc = 4.5×10^-22 s and T_cross = 4.7×10^-22 s; for ℓ=8, T_osc = 5.5×10^-22 s and T_cross = 5.8×10^-22 s.
- Woods-Saxon parameters: nuclear radius r_0 = 1.25 fm, diffuseness a = 0.7 fm, central potential depth V_0 = 58 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the time-dependent approach previously applied to alpha decay by the same group (Misicu and Carjan, 1994; Misicu, Carjan, and Serban, 1994) to the case of proton emission from spherical nuclei with non-zero angular momentum. It contrasts with stationary Gamow-type treatments and with semiclassical WKB calculations used by Kadmensky et al., Bugrov et al. (1992), and Aberg et al. (1997) for proton emitters beyond the drip line. The results enable future time-dependent studies of ground-state proton radioactivity and provide a benchmark for assessing the accuracy of the WKB approximation in proton decay calculations.
