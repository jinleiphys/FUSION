# Model Dependence of the 2H Electric Dipole Moment

> Background: Direct measurement of the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the neutron lies in the future; measurement of a nuclear EDM may well come first. The deuteron is one nucleus for which exact model calculations are feasible. Purpose: We explore the model dependence of deuteron EDM calculations. Methods: Using a separable potential formulation of the Hamiltonian, we examine the sensitivity of the deuteron EDM to variation in the nucleon-nucleon interaction. We write the EDM as the sum of two terms, the first depending on the target wave function with plane-wave intermediate states, and the second depending on intermediate multiple scattering in the 3P1 channel, the latter being sensitive to the off-shell behavior of the 3P1 amplitude. Results: We compare the full calculation with the plane-wave approximation result, examine the tensor force contribution to the model results, and explore the effect of short range repulsion found in realistic, contemporary potential models of the deuteron. Conclusions: Because one-pion exchange dominates the EDM calculation, separable potential model calculations will provide an adequate description of the 2H EDM until such time as a better than 10% measurement is obtained.

# Model Dependence of the ²H Electric Dipole Moment

## Key claim

Separable potential model calculations will provide an adequate description of the deuteron electric dipole moment until a measurement with better than 10% precision is obtained, because one-pion exchange dominates the calculation and the results show relatively little sensitivity to the nuclear model details.

## Method

The authors used a separable potential formulation of the Hamiltonian to examine the sensitivity of the deuteron EDM to variation in the nucleon-nucleon interaction. They wrote the EDM as the sum of two terms: a plane-wave contribution depending on the target wave function with plane-wave intermediate states, and a multiple scattering contribution depending on intermediate multiple scattering in the ³P₁ channel, the latter being sensitive to the off-shell behavior of the ³P₁ amplitude. They compared results using Yamaguchi-Yamaguchi separable potentials with 4% and 7% D-state probability and Unitary Pole Approximation representations of the Reid68 and Reid93 realistic potentials, with various ³P₁ separable potentials fitted to the Nijmegen np phase shift data.

## Key numbers

- The plane-wave contribution d_PW for the Reid93 deuteron wave function is -0.9715 A e fm, and for the Reid68 deuteron is -0.9620 A e fm.
- The total two-body deuteron EDM d_D^(2) for the Reid93 deuteron with a rank-two Case I ³P₁ potential is -0.7706 A e fm, and for the Reid68 deuteron is -0.7902 A e fm.
- The total two-body deuteron EDM d_D^(2) for the Yamaguchi-Yamaguchi 4% D-state deuteron with a rank-two Case I ³P₁ potential is -0.6234 A e fm, and for the 7% D-state deuteron is -0.6564 A e fm.
- The multiple scattering contribution d_MS for the Reid93 deuteron ranges from 0.2009 A e fm (rank-two Case I) to 0.3805 A e fm (rank-two Case III), while for the 4% YY deuteron it ranges from 0.3277 A e fm (Case IV) to 1.108 A e fm (rank-two Case III).
- The Liu and Timmermans result for the polarization component of d_D^(2) using realistic potentials is approximately -0.73 ± 0.01 A e fm.
- The Khriplovich and Korkin zero-range approximation result for d_D^(2) is -0.92 A e fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier estimate by Avishai (1985), who first calculated the two-body deuteron EDM using a Mongan separable potential and reported -0.91 A e fm, and the later calculation by Khriplovich and Korkin (2000) using a zero-range approximation in the chiral limit. It directly compares with the more recent calculations by Liu and Timmermans (2004) using the Argonne Av18, Reid93, and Nijmegen II realistic potentials, which gave values of -0.72, -0.73, and -0.74 A e fm respectively. The results enable reliable extension of the plane-wave approximation to calculations of the ³He and ³H EDMs, as the multiple scattering contribution is found to be only about 20% of the plane-wave contribution when realistic potentials with short-range repulsion are used.
