# Vector meson photoproduction in the quark model

> We present a quark model study of the $ω$ meson photoproduction near threshold. With a limited number of parameters, all the data in history are reproduced. The roles played by the {\it s}- and {\it u}-channel processes (resonance excitations and nucleon pole terms), as well as the {\it t}-channel {\it natural} (Pomeron) and {\it unnatural} parity (pion) exchanges are clarified. This approach provides a framework for systematic study of vector meson photoproduction near threshold.

# Vector meson photoproduction in the quark model

## Key claim
The quark model approach reproduces all historical data for ω meson photoproduction near threshold with a limited number of parameters, but the analysis shows that the dominant process near threshold is actually the s- and u-channel resonance and nucleon pole terms, not the t-channel exchanges that dominate at higher energies.

## Method
The model combines three processes: (i) s- and u-channel vector meson production via an effective Lagrangian at the quark level with SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry, including eight low-lying resonances (P11(1440), S11(1535), D13(1520), P13(1720), F15(1680), P11(1710), P13(1900), and F15(2000)) with quark harmonic oscillator shell n≤2; (ii) t-channel Pomeron exchange; and (iii) t-channel π0 exchange for the ω meson case. Higher mass states are treated as degenerate with n.

## Key numbers
- Differential cross sections are presented for four energy bins: Eγ = 1.225, 1.450, 1.675, and 1.915 GeV, compared with SAPHIR data
- The Pomeron exchange becomes dominant over pion exchange above Eγ ∼ 3.5 GeV
- Eight low-lying resonances with n≤2 are explicitly included: P11(1440), S11(1535), D13(1520), P13(1720), F15(1680), P11(1710), P13(1900), and F15(2000)

## Context
This work extends the nonrelativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) framework for vector meson photoproduction near threshold, motivated by the search for "missing resonances" predicted by NRCQM but not found in πN scatterings. The approach contrasts with a phenomenological model by Oh (year not specified) using the 3P0 quark-pair-creation model, which obtained quite different results for exclusive processes. The predictions are intended for comparison with forthcoming data from GRAAL and JLAB, and the measurement of forward angle parity asymmetries (Ballam, 1973) can constrain the natural and unnatural parity exchanges.
