# Central depressions in the charge density profiles of the nuclei around $^{46}$Ar

> The occurrence of the proton bubble-like structure has been studied within the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theories by exploring the bulk properties, the charge density profiles and single proton spectra of argon isotopes and $N = 28$ isotones. It is found that the RHFB calculations with PKA1 effective interaction, which can properly reproduce the charge radii of argon isotopes and the $Z=16$ proton shell nearby, do not support the occurrence of the proton bubble-like structure in argon isotopes due to the prediction of deeper bound proton orbit $\pi2s_{1/2}$ than $\pi1d_{3/2}$. For $N = 28$ isotones, $^{42}$Si and $^{40}$Mg are predicted by both RHFB and RHB models to have the proton bubble-like structure, owing to the large gap between the proton $\pi2s_{1/2}$ and $\pi1d_{5/2}$ orbits, namely the $Z=14$ proton shell. Therefore, $^{42}$Si is proposed as the potential candidate of proton bubble nucleus, which has longer life-time than $^{40}$Mg.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the proton bubble-like structure (central depression in charge density) is not supported in argon isotopes when using the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theory with the PKA1 effective interaction, which best reproduces experimental charge radii; however, for the N=28 isotones ⁴²Si and ⁴⁰Mg, both RHFB and RHB models predict the bubble structure, with ⁴²Si proposed as a longer-lived candidate than ⁴⁰Mg.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent calculations using relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theories with several effective interactions (PKA1, PKO1, PKO3, PKDD, DD-ME2) for argon isotopes and N=28 isotones. They analyzed bulk properties (binding energies, charge radii), charge density profiles (including center-of-mass and finite nucleon size corrections), and canonical proton single-particle spectra to determine the conditions for proton bubble-like structure formation, specifically examining the ordering and occupancy of the π2s₁/₂ and π1d₃/₂ orbits.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁶Ar, the RHFB calculation with PKA1 predicts the π2s₁/₂ orbit to be deeper bound than π1d₃/₂, preventing bubble formation; the root-mean-square deviation of charge radii from experimental data is smallest for PKA1 among all tested interactions
- For N=28 isotones, the binding energy per particle of ⁴²Si is 7.405 MeV (PKA1) compared to the experimental value of 7.416 MeV; for ⁴⁰Mg it is 6.625 MeV (PKA1) versus 6.621 MeV experimental
- In ⁴⁸Ca, the pseudo-spin partner splitting ΔE between π2s₁/₂ and π1d₃/₂ is -0.170 MeV for PKA1, compared to the experimental value of 0.36 MeV; other interactions give much larger splittings (PKDD: 2.154 MeV, PKO1: 1.718 MeV)
- The RHFB calculations used a spherical box size Rmax = 28 fm with NF = 44 positive energy states and ND = 12 negative energy states

## Context

This work extends the debate on proton bubble nuclei beyond earlier studies of ³⁴Si (Grasso 2009, Nakada 2013) and ⁴⁶Ar (Todd 2004, Chu 2010, Khan 2008, Wang 2011). It contradicts nonrelativistic Skyrme-HFB predictions that tensor interactions could produce bubble structure in ⁴⁶Ar (Wang 2011), and also differs from RMF calculations without tensor force that predicted central depressions in ⁴⁶Ar (Todd 2004, Chu 2010). The finding that only PKA1 reproduces the Z=16 proton shell suggested by experimental beta-decay Q values and separation energies (Kanungo 2002) provides a consistency check. The results enable future experimental searches for bubble structure in ⁴²Si and ⁴⁰Mg rather than ⁴⁶Ar, and suggest that dynamical correlations (Yao 2012, 2013; Wu 2014) may be less relevant for these lighter isotones.
