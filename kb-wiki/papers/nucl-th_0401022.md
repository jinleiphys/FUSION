# Medium effects on meson couplings and dilepton spectra

> The pi omega rho coupling constant is calculated in a relativistic hadronic framework incorporating nucleons and Delta(1232) isobars. Medium modifications are included. The vertex is analyzed in context of the omega to pi e+e- and rho to pi e+e- Dalitz decays in nuclear matter. A sizeable increase of the widths for these decays is found for invariant mass of dileptons in the range 0.2-0.5 GeV. This increase may help, among other medium effects, to explain the problem of the low-mass dilepton enhancement seen in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The medium-modified $\pi \omega \rho$ coupling constant shows a sizeable increase for dilepton invariant masses in the range 0.2–0.5 GeV, which may help, among other medium effects, to explain the low-mass dilepton enhancement seen in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the author notes that detector acceptance and efficiency must be included before comparing to experimental numbers.

## Method

The $\pi \omega \rho$ vertex was calculated in a relativistic hadronic framework using nucleon and $\Delta(1232)$ isobar degrees of freedom at leading baryon density and zero temperature. In-medium diagrams with nucleon and $\Delta$ propagators were evaluated, and the effective coupling constant $g_{\rm eff}$ was extracted for both the $\omega \to \pi^0 e^+e^-$ and $\rho^a \to \pi^a e^+e^-$ Dalitz decays. The dilepton yield was then computed by integrating the production rate over a hydrodynamically expanding fire cylinder model from Rapp and Wambach.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.17\ {\rm fm^{-3}}$
- At saturation density ($y=1$) and $M_{e^+e^-} = 0.4\ {\rm GeV}$, the enhancement of $|g_{\rm eff}/g_{\rm vac}|^2$ is about a factor of 2 for the $\omega$ meson and about a factor of 5 for the $\rho$ meson
- The medium effect remains substantial for momenta of the decaying particle lower than $150\ {\rm MeV}$
- The enhancement is observed for dilepton invariant masses in the range $0.2-0.5\ {\rm GeV}$; below $0.2\ {\rm GeV}$ the effective coupling constant is practically unchanged
- The dilepton yield calculations were performed at two temperatures: $T=210\ {\rm MeV}$ and $T=150\ {\rm MeV}$

## Context

This work extends prior studies of in-medium modifications of the $\rho \pi \pi$ coupling constant (Song and Koch; Urban, Buballa, Rapp, and Wambach; Hiller) to the $\pi \omega \rho$ vertex, which governs Dalitz decays of vector mesons. The results address the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed by the CERES collaboration in Pb-Au and S-Au collisions at 158 and 200 A GeV, where the cocktail model fails by about a factor of 6. The calculations enable more complete theoretical descriptions of dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions, and the author notes that new data from the NA60 experiment will provide further insight.
