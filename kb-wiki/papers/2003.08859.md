# New, multipole solutions of relativistic, viscous hydrodynamics

> We present a new class of exact fireball solutions of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics. We describe new exact solutions both for the relativistic Navier-Stokes and for the Israel-Stewart theory, for arbitrary shear and bulk viscosities, as well as for other dissipative coefficients. The common property of these solutions is the presence of the relativistic Hubble flow. Our results generalize the recently found first solution in these classes, for an arbitrary temperature dependent speed of sound, shear and bulk viscosity, heat conduction and fluctuating initial temperature profiles. These solutions are causal and not only stable but also asymptotically perfect. A strong and narrow peak in the kinematic bulk viscosity is shown to imitate the effects of a first order phase transition. The new class of asymptotically perfect solutions is thus found to be very rich, but at the same time mostly academic as the solutions are limited by the spherical symmetry of the Hubble flow field.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new class of exact solutions for relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics (both Navier-Stokes and Israel-Stewart theories) that are "asymptotically perfect", they approach the perfect fluid CCHK solution at late times, but the authors acknowledge these solutions are "mostly academic" because they are limited by the spherical symmetry of the Hubble flow field.

## Method

The authors reduce the partial differential equations of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics to coupled ordinary differential equations by assuming a relativistic Hubble flow velocity profile (u^μ = x^μ/τ) and a bulk viscosity proportional to pressure (ζ ∝ p). They solve these equations analytically for both the relativistic Navier-Stokes and Israel-Stewart theories, allowing for arbitrary temperature-dependent speed of sound, shear and bulk viscosities, heat conduction, and fluctuating initial temperature profiles.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic perfect fluid temperature scales as T ∼ T_A (τ₀/τ)^(d/κ₀) for τ ≫ τ₀, where d = 3 spatial dimensions and κ₀ is the constant relating energy density to pressure (ε = κp)
- The ratio of asymptotic to initial pressure is p_A/p₀ = exp[ζ₀ d²/(κ₀ p₀ τ₀)], where ζ₀ is initial bulk viscosity, p₀ is initial pressure, and τ₀ is initial proper time
- For the Israel-Stewart theory, the bulk pressure evolves as Π(τ) = Π₀ (τ₀/τ)^(d ζ₀/(τ_Π Π₀)) exp(-(τ-τ₀)/τ_Π), where τ_Π is the relaxation time parameter
- The temperature-dependent kinematic bulk viscosity peak was enhanced by a factor c = 3.6 relative to the parametrization of Bozek (2017) to illustrate the imitation of a first-order phase transition
- Example calculations use a constant speed of sound c_s² = 1/κ = 1/7 and initial temperatures of T₀ = 150, 250, 300, and 400 MeV

## Context

This work generalizes the first exact solutions of relativistic Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics reported by Csanad (2019) from constant speed of sound and homogeneous initial temperature to arbitrary temperature-dependent speed of sound, shear and bulk viscosities, heat conduction, and fluctuating initial profiles. The solutions extend the CCHK perfect fluid solutions of Csorgo (2003) to dissipative cases, and the authors show that a narrow peak in kinematic bulk viscosity can mimic the effects of a first-order phase transition as discussed by Csernai and Kapusta (1992). The main limitation is the spherical symmetry of the Hubble flow, and the authors suggest future work should seek asymptotically perfect solutions that violate this symmetry.
