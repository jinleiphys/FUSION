# Quenching of spectroscopic factors for proton removal in oxygen isotopes

> We present microscopic coupled-cluster calculations of the spectroscopic factors for proton removal from the closed-shell oxygen isotopes $^{14,16,22,24,28}$O with the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order. We include coupling-to-continuum degrees of freedom by using a Hartree-Fock basis built from a Woods-Saxon single-particle basis. This basis treats bound and continuum states on an equal footing. We find a significant quenching of spectroscopic factors in the neutron-rich oxygen isotopes, pointing to enhanced many-body correlations induced by strong coupling to the scattering continuum above the neutron emission thresholds.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the significant quenching of spectroscopic factors for proton removal in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes is primarily due to many-body correlations arising from strong coupling to the scattering continuum above the neutron emission thresholds, rather than from short-range or tensor correlations alone.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio coupled-cluster calculations at the CCSD level using the chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon interaction (cutoff Lambda=500 MeV) for the closed-shell oxygen isotopes 14,16,22,24,28O. They constructed a Hartree-Fock basis from a Woods-Saxon single-particle basis that treats bound and continuum states on equal footing, including 30 mesh points along the real momentum axis for neutron s1/2, d3/2, and d5/2 partial waves, combined with 17 major harmonic oscillator shells.

## Key numbers
- Spectroscopic factors for p1/2 and p3/2 proton removal show significant quenching in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes when continuum states are included (HF-WS basis), while calculations using a purely harmonic oscillator basis (HF-OSC) show no significant quenching.
- 28O is bound by 3.67 MeV with respect to one-neutron emission for the N3LO interaction with cutoff Lambda=500 MeV.
- For 24O, using the N3LO interaction evolved via similarity renormalization group methods, the spectroscopic factor for p1/2 proton removal varied from 0.79 to 0.75 as the cutoff changed from lambda=3.2 fm^-1 to lambda=3.6 fm^-1.
- The EOM-CC approach gave a reduction of 20-25% in spectroscopic factors for 24O over the range of cutoffs considered, compared to a 15-20% reduction when using a HF solution for the A-body system with one-hole and two-hole-one-particle excitations for the A-1 system.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of spectroscopic factors in stable nuclei from (e,e'p) experiments (Vijay 1997, Carlo 2009, Jenny 2009) and knockout reactions on neutron-rich nuclei (Alexandra 2008, Alexandra 2004) by providing microscopic ab initio calculations for the oxygen isotope chain. The results agree with the mathematical analysis of Michel (2007) showing that energy dependence of spectroscopic factors due to opening of reaction channels requires proper treatment of scattering states. The findings enable future experimental proton knockout measurements from oxygen isotopes and suggest the need for similar many-body calculations for proton-rich heavy nuclei.
