# Angular momentum non conserving symmetries in bosonic models

> The Levi-Malcev decomposition is applied to bosonic models of quantum mechanics based on unitary Lie algebras u(2), u(2)+u(2), u(3) and u(4) to clearly disentangle semisimple subalgebras. The theory of weighted Dynkin diagrams is then applied to identify conjugacy classes of relevant A_1 subalgebras allowing to introduce a complete classification of new angular momentum non conserving (AMNC) dynamical symmetries. The tensor analysis of the whole algebra based on the new "angular momentum" operators reveals unexpected spinors to occur in purely bosonic models. The new chains of subalgebra can be invoked to set up ANMC bases for diagonalization.

## Key claim

The paper shows that bosonic models based on unitary Lie algebras admit additional dynamical symmetry chains that do not conserve the usual angular momentum, but instead conserve a different operator that has the formal properties of an angular momentum; these new chains provide alternative bases for diagonalization that might simplify solving model Hamiltonians.

## Method

The authors apply the Levi-Malcev decomposition to the unitary Lie algebras u(2), u(2)⊕u(2), u(3), and u(4) to separate each algebra into a radical (maximal solvable ideal) and a semisimple Levi subalgebra. They then use the theory of weighted Dynkin diagrams to classify all conjugacy classes of A₁ subalgebras (isomorphic to su(2) ∼ so(3)) within each semisimple part, and perform a complete tensor analysis of the full algebra with respect to each distinct A₁ subalgebra to determine the tensorial character of all operators.

## Key numbers

- For u(3), the semisimple part su(3) (A₂ in Cartan notation) has two conjugacy classes of A₁ subalgebras, labeled by weighted Dynkin diagrams [1,1] and [2,2].
- For u(4), the semisimple part A₃ has four conjugacy classes of A₁ subalgebras, labeled by weighted Dynkin diagrams [101], [202], [020], and [222].
- The u(3) algebra contains nine bilinear operators: one scalar (g₁), one vector with three components (g₂,g₃,g₄), and one quadrupole tensor with five components (g₅,g₆,g₇,g₈,g₉).
- The u(4) algebra contains sixteen bilinear operators: the nine from u(3) plus seven additional ones (g₁₀ through g₁₆) involving s bosons.

## Context

This work extends the classification of subalgebra chains for bosonic models used in nuclear, molecular, and other branches of physics, building on the foundational treatments of Iachello and Arima (1987), Iachello and Levine (1995), and Iachello (2006). The identification of angular-momentum-non-conserving chains and the occurrence of spinor operators in purely bosonic models was previously noted only in specific contexts by Chen and Arima (within the Interacting Boson Model) and by Elliott (in nuclear shell model collective motion); the present paper provides a complete classification using weighted Dynkin diagrams and enables the construction of new basis states for diagonalization problems in the vibron model of diatomic molecules.
