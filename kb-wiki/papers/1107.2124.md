# Interweaving Chiral Spirals

> We elaborate how to construct interweaving chiral spirals in (2+1) dimensions, defined as a superposition of chiral spirals oriented in different directions. We divide a two-dimensional Fermi sea into distinct wedges, characterized by the opening angle 2Theta and depth Q ~ pF, where pF is the Fermi momentum. In each wedge, the energy is lowered by forming a single chiral spiral. The optimal values for Theta and Q are chosen by balancing this gain in energy versus the cost of deforming the Fermi surface (which dominates at large Theta) and patch-patch interactions (dominant at small Theta). Using a non-local four-Fermi interaction model, we estimate the gain and cost in energy by expanding in terms of 1/Nc (where Nc is the number of colors), lqcd/Q, and Theta. Due to a form factor in our non-local model, at small 1/Nc the mass gap (chiral condensate) is large, and the interaction among quarks and the condensate local in momentum space. Consequently, interactions between different patches are localized near their boundaries, and it is simple to embed many chiral spirals. We identify the dominant and subdominant terms at high density and categorize formulate an expansion in terms of lqcd/Q or Theta. The kinetic term in the transverse directions is subdominant, so that techniques from (1+1)-dimensional systems can be utilized. To leading order in 1/Nc and lqcd/Q, the total gain in energy is ~ pF lqcd^2 with Theta ~ (lqcd/pF)^{3/5}. Since Theta decreases with increasing pF, there should be phase transitions associated with the change in the wedge number. We also argue the effects of subdominant terms at lower density where the large-Nc approximation is more reliable.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at high baryon density, chiral symmetry is broken not by a homogeneous condensate but by an "interweaving" of many chiral spirals oriented in different directions, which spontaneously breaks continuous rotational symmetry to a discrete one; the optimal opening angle of each wedge scales as $\Theta \sim (\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/p_{\rm F})^{3/5}$, a result derived under the conditions that the transverse patch size is much larger than the QCD scale and that the expansion in $1/N_c$ and $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/Q$ is valid.

## Method

The authors decompose a two-dimensional Fermi sea into wedges of opening angle $2\Theta$ and depth $Q \sim p_{\rm F}$, each wedge hosting a single chiral spiral. Using a non-local four-Fermi interaction model with a form factor that mimics infrared enhancement of the gluon propagator, they expand the energy density in powers of $1/N_c$, $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/Q$, and $\Theta$, treating the kinetic cost of deforming the Fermi surface and the patch-patch interactions as competing terms against the condensation energy gain. The gap equation is solved in the large-$N_c$ limit where the particle-condensate interaction becomes local in momentum space, allowing the embedding of many spirals.

## Key numbers

* The optimal opening angle scales as $\Theta \sim (\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/p_{\rm F})^{3/5}$, where $p_{\rm F}$ is the Fermi momentum and $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ is the QCD scale.
* The total gain in energy from condensation, to leading order in $1/N_c$ and $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/Q$, is $\sim p_{\rm F} \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2$.
* The non-trivial deformation energy of the Fermi surface first appears at order $\Theta^4$, specifically as $\sim N_c (p_{\rm F}^3/3\pi) (1 + \Theta^4/30 + \dots)$ after subtracting the free Fermi gas contribution.
* The condition for the analysis to apply is $p_{\rm F} \Theta \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}$, which is self-consistently satisfied since $p_{\rm F} \Theta_0 \sim (p_{\rm F}/\Lambda_{\rm QCD})^{2/5} \Lambda_{\rm QCD}$.
* The leading corrections to the total energy are suppressed by $(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/p_{\rm F})^{2/5}$ relative to the leading condensation term.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous arguments for chiral spiral formation in Quarkyonic matter (Kojo et al., 2009; Kojo et al., 2010) by explicitly constructing a superposition of many spirals in (2+1) dimensions, going beyond the single-spiral analysis. It contrasts with earlier studies using perturbative gluon propagators (Shuster et al., 1999; Park et al., 1999) which argued that charge density waves require very large $N_c$ and did not include confinement. The results enable a description of the phase diagram of Quarkyonic matter with stair-like energy density growth as the number of patches changes with density, and suggest that techniques from (1+1)-dimensional systems can be applied to the dominant terms.
