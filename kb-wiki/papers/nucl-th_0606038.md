# A new variable in flow analysis

> We have used a simple spectrum distribution which was derived from a hydrodynamical equation\cite{Csorgo} to fit the data of the STAR group. It is found that it can fit the $v_2$ of STAR group very well. We have found that $v_2$ is sensitive to both the effective temperature of particles and the expanding velocity. We have suggested a new variable ${\bf z}$ to be used in the flow analysis. This new variable will measure the correlation of particles momentum components. We have also shown that one of the $x$ or $y$ direction in the reaction plane is the direction which has the largest variance.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) measured in heavy-ion collisions reflects both the effective temperature difference between momentum components and the collective expansion velocity, but that a newly defined variable \(\mathbf{z}\) isolates the correlation between momentum components and yields \(v_2(z)=0\) for the tested hydrodynamical model, suggesting that standard \(v_2\) measurements may conflate these effects.

## Method

The authors fit a hydrodynamically derived spectrum distribution \(P(\mathbf{p}) \propto \exp\left(-\frac{(p_x-mv_x)^2}{2mT_x} - \frac{(p_y-mv_y)^2}{2mT_y}\right)\) to STAR data. They compute \(v_2\) using three different variables: the raw momentum \(\mathbf{p}\), the shifted momentum \(\mathbf{\tilde{p}} = \mathbf{p} - \mathbf{\bar{p}}\), and a new normalized variable \(\mathbf{z} = (\tilde{p}_x / \langle \tilde{p}_x^2 \rangle, \tilde{p}_y / \langle \tilde{p}_y^2 \rangle)\). They then analyze the sphericity tensor and eigenvalue methods to relate these variables to the reaction plane determination.

## Key numbers

- \(v_2(z) = 0\) for all cases in the model, regardless of parameters.
- \(v_2(\tilde{p}) = 0\) when \(T_x = T_y\); \(v_2(\tilde{p}) \neq 0\) when \(T_x \ne T_y\).
- The asymmetry parameter \(\alpha\) for variable \(\mathbf{z}\) is given by \(\alpha(z) = |S_{12}| / \sqrt{S_{11} S_{22}}\), where \(S_{ij}\) are elements of the sphericity tensor.
- When \(\alpha(z)\) is very small, \(\alpha(\tilde{p}) \sim |1-t|/(1+t)\) where \(t = S_{11}/S_{22}\).
- The model fits STAR data well, especially in the larger \(p_T\) region (no explicit numerical fit values given in the text).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamical approach of Csorgo (2004) by applying a simple Gaussian spectrum solution to elliptic flow analysis at RHIC. It contrasts with standard methods that use the second harmonic event plane (widely used in RHIC data analysis) or pairwise azimuthal correlations (Correlation, 2004). The paper enables a new way to separate flow contributions from expansion velocity and effective temperature versus intrinsic momentum correlations, and suggests that the effective temperature for different momentum directions is nearly the same in the data.
