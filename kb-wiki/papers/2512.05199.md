# Jet Charge with Global Event Shapes: Probing Quark Flavor Dynamics

> We propose measuring the jet electric charge of jet regions, defined within the framework of global event shapes, as a probe of quark flavor dynamics within the nucleon and the hadronization process. In particular, we consider a measurement of the jet region charge while simultaneously keeping track of the energy flow throughout the event, as characterized by the global event shape. As a concrete example, we focus on the measurement of the 1-Jettiness jet charge ($Q$), the jet charge of the jet region ($J$) defined within the framework of the 1-Jettiness global event shape ($τ_1$) for the Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) process, $e^- + p \to e^- + J + X$, with unpolarized or longitudinally polarized protons. The 1-Jettiness distribution, binned according to jet charge, allows for enhanced quark flavor separation of the initial state unpolarized or polarized PDFs. On the other hand, the jet charge distribution binned by 1-Jettiness can serve as a probe of quark flavor dynamics in the final state hadronization process. We derive a factorization theorem for simultaneous measurements of $τ_1$ and $Q$ in the resummation region, $τ_1 \ll P_{J_T}$, where $P_{J_T}$ denotes the transverse momentum of the jet region. The factorization theorem contains a new universal charged jet function, generalizing the standard jet function to include a jet charge measurement. Therefore these universal functions can be extracted from a global analysis of N-jettiness and thrust at $e^+e^-$ colliders. We provide simulation studies to demonstrate the sensitivity of the 1-Jettiness jet charge observable to quark flavor dynamics in nucleon structure and explore the possibility of probing the final state hadronization process. This observable is well-suited for applications with existing HERA data and the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the jet electric charge of jet regions defined by the 1-Jettiness global event shape in deep inelastic scattering can simultaneously probe quark flavor dynamics in nucleon structure and the hadronization process, with the strongest correlation between jet region charge and struck quark charge expected in the resummation region where tau_1 is much less than P_JT.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization theorem for the simultaneous measurement of the 1-Jettiness global event shape tau_1 and the jet charge Q of the jet region in the resummation region tau_1 much less than P_JT for the neutral current deep inelastic scattering process e- + p to e- + J + X. They replace the standard jet function in the factorization formula with a new universal charged jet function that includes a jet charge measurement operator, and perform simulation studies using Pythia 8.312 to demonstrate sensitivity to quark flavor dynamics.

## Key numbers

* The resummation region is defined by tau_1 much less than P_JT, where P_JT denotes the transverse momentum of the jet region
* The default jet charge parameter kappa = 0.3 provides the best discrimination between jets initiated by different quark flavors
* The jet radius R = 1 is used with the anti-k_t algorithm to determine the jet reference vector
* The 1-Jettiness spectrum has been obtained at the N3LL + O(alpha_s^2) level of accuracy
* The center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 90.0 GeV is used for the simulation with P_JT between 20.0 and 30.0 GeV and rapidity y_J between -2.5 and 2.5

## Context

This work extends the jet charge concept from Krohn (2012) and Waalewijn (2012) by incorporating it within the global event shape framework of 1-Jettiness for deep inelastic scattering, building on the factorization formalism developed by Kang (2012-2014) and Cao (2024). It also builds on recent work by Kang (2020) that used jet charge for flavor separation of transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions. The observable is well-suited for application to existing HERA data and the future Electron-Ion Collider, and the universal charged jet function can be extracted from global analysis of N-jettiness and thrust at e+e- colliders.
