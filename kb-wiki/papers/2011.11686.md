# Deconfinement Phase Transition under Chemical Equilibrium

> In this work, we investigate how the assumption of chemical equilibrium with leptons affects the deconfinement phase transition to quark matter. This is done within the framework of the Chiral Mean Field model (CMF) allowing for non-zero net strangeness, corresponding to the conditions found in astrophysical scenarios. We build 3-dimensional QCD phase diagrams with temperature, baryon chemical potential, and either charge or isospin fraction or chemical potential to show how the deconfinement region collapses to a line in the special case of chemical equilibrium, such as the one established the interior of cold catalyzed neutron stars.

## Key claim

The deconfinement phase transition region in the QCD phase diagram, which is a 3-dimensional surface when plotted against temperature, baryon chemical potential, and charge or isospin fraction, collapses to a line when the system is constrained by chemical equilibrium with leptons, as occurs in cold catalyzed neutron stars.

## Method

The authors employed the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, a relativistic model based on a non-linear realization of the SU(3) linear sigma model that includes three light quarks and reproduces chiral symmetry restoration and quark deconfinement. For each temperature and charge or isospin fraction, they varied the Gibbs free energy per baryon until finding a discontinuity in the order parameters, then imposed chemical equilibrium conditions with leptons (requiring mu_Q = -mu_electron and Y_Q = Y_lepton) to identify the special equilibrium line.

## Key numbers

- At T=0, the baryon chemical potential on the hadronic side of the deconfinement transition in chemical equilibrium is 1343 MeV; on the quark side it is 1344 MeV.
- At T=160 MeV, the baryon chemical potential on the hadronic side in chemical equilibrium is 561 MeV; on the quark side it is 562 MeV.
- At T=0, the charge chemical potential on the hadronic side in chemical equilibrium is -253 MeV; on the quark side it is -76 MeV.
- At T=160 MeV, the charge chemical potential on the hadronic side in chemical equilibrium is -34 MeV; on the quark side it is -29 MeV.
- The difference in baryon chemical potential between fixed Y_Q=0 and Y_Q=0.5 cases on the hadronic side is 48 MeV at T=0 and 11 MeV at T=160 MeV.
- The difference in baryon chemical potential between fixed Y_I=-0.5 and Y_I=0 cases on the hadronic side is 93 MeV at T=0 and 14 MeV at T=160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the 3-dimensional QCD phase diagrams previously derived by Aryal (2020) and Dexheimer (2020) by including, for the first time, chemically equilibrated matter lines for the deconfinement phase transition. The results apply to cold catalyzed neutron star interiors where chemical equilibrium holds, but not to hot matter in core-collapse supernovae or neutron-star mergers where timescales for neutrino diffusion compete with hydrodynamic collapse, as discussed by Bethe (1979). The CMF model results are compared with expectations from lattice QCD and perturbative QCD, and the authors note that comparisons with the Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model or the Polyakov-loop-extended two-flavor quark-meson (PQM) model are limited because those works include quark matter only and are not sensitive to baryonic physics including hyperons.
