# Complex-mass scheme and resonances in EFT

> The complex-mass scheme (CMS) provides a consistent framework for dealing with unstable particles in quantum field theory and has been successfully applied to various loop calculations. As applications of the CMS in chiral effective field theory we consider the form factor of the pion in the time-like region and the magnetic moment of the Roper resonance.

# Complex-mass scheme and resonances in EFT

## Key claim
The complex-mass scheme (CMS) provides a consistent renormalization framework for including unstable particles in chiral effective field theory, enabling a satisfactory description of the pion form factor up to \(q^2 \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\) and the magnetic moment of the Roper resonance at \({\cal O}(q^3)\), though the latter has no measured value for comparison.

## Method
The authors apply the complex-mass scheme to chiral effective field theory with explicit resonance fields. For the pion form factor, they calculate tree and one-loop contributions from an effective Lagrangian of pions and \(\rho\) mesons, fitting parameters to data from \(\tau^- \rightarrow \nu_\tau \pi^- \pi^0\) decay and including \(\rho^0-\omega-A\) mixing at tree order for the \(e^+ e^- \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^-\) channel. For the Roper resonance magnetic moment, they construct a leading-order Lagrangian including nucleon, Roper, and \(\Delta\) fields, compute tree and fourteen one-loop diagrams to \({\cal O}(q^3)\), and renormalize using the CMS with a power counting that treats the width as \({\cal O}(q^1)\).

## Key numbers
- Pion form factor described up to \(q^2 \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- Roper resonance magnetic moment loop contribution: \(\kappa_R = (0.055 + 0.090\,i) - (0.223 + 0.156\,i)\,\tau_3\) (dimensionless, complex-valued due to CMS).
- Input parameters for Roper calculation: pion decay constant \(F = 0.092\) GeV, pion mass \(M = 0.140\) GeV, nucleon mass \(m = 0.940\) GeV, \(\Delta\) complex mass \(z_{\Delta \chi} = (1.210 - 0.100\,i/2)\) GeV, Roper complex mass \(z_\chi = (1.365 - 0.190\,i/2)\) GeV, renormalization scale \(\mu = 1\) GeV, coupling constants \(g_R = 1\), \(g_{\Delta R} = 1\), \(g_{NR} = 0.45\).

## Context
This work extends the complex-mass scheme, previously applied to the \(\rho\) meson and Roper resonance masses and widths by Djukanovic et al. (2009, 2010), to electromagnetic form factors and magnetic moments. It builds on chiral perturbation theory established by Weinberg (1978) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984), and on the CMS framework developed by Stuart (1990) and Denner et al. (1999). The results enable future lattice QCD extrapolations for the Roper magnetic moment, for which no experimental measurement currently exists.
