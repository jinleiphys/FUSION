# Interfering Doorway States and Giant Resonances. I: Resonance Spectrum and Multipole Strengths

> A phenomenological schematic model of multipole giant resonances (GR) is considered which treats the external interaction via common decay channels on the same footing as the coherent part of the internal residual interaction. The damping due to the coupling to the sea of complicated states is neglected. As a result, the formation of GR is governed by the interplay and competition of two kinds of collectivity, the internal and the external one. The mixing of the doorway components of a GR due to the external interaction influences significantly their multipole strengths, widths and positions in energy. In particular, a narrow resonance state with an appreciable multipole strength is formed when the doorway components strongly overlap.

## Key claim

The formation of giant resonances is governed by the interplay and competition between internal collectivity (from the residual interaction) and external collectivity (from coupling to decay channels), and when doorway components strongly overlap, a narrow resonance state with appreciable multipole strength emerges, a result that depends on neglecting the damping due to coupling to the sea of complicated states.

## Method

The authors extend the Brown-Bolsterli schematic model to open (decaying) systems by constructing a non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian that includes both a factorized internal residual interaction (dipole-dipole type) and an external interaction via common decay channels, treated on equal footing. They solve the secular equation for the complex energies of resonance states and analyze the K-matrix analytically in a doorway-state basis, then confirm the main features numerically with and without the analytical restrictions.

## Key numbers

- The mixing between the collective level and the other states is governed by the parameter κ = Δ_e / D², where Δ_e is the variance of the parental level distribution and D² = Tr(D D^T); when κ ≪ 1, the first level accumulates a dipole strength f¹ = 1 − κ².
- The collective level energy shift is ∼ D², while the other levels are shifted by distances of order the unperturbed mean level spacing, much smaller than D².
- The partial escape width of the collective state in the limit of maximal internal collectivity is Γ_coll^c = (A_d^c)², where A_d^c is the projection of the decay amplitude onto the dipole vector.
- The square length of the vector h coupling the collective level to the other states equals the variance Δ_e² = ⟨(e − ⟨e⟩)²⟩.
- The number of doorway states is N_dw = k + 1, where k is the number of open channels; the remaining N_tr = N − k − 1 states are "trapped" with no direct access to the continuum.

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Bolsterli schematic model (1959) for multipole collective nuclear excitations to open (decaying) systems, following the approach of Sokolov and Zelevinsky (1990). It generalizes earlier treatments of width collectivization and the trapping effect observed in numerical simulations by Kleinwachter and Rotter (1985) and Rotter (1988). The results enable a framework for understanding how overlapping doorway states redistribute multipole strengths and escape widths, with the following part II of the paper planned to address cross sections and the role of background complicated states.
