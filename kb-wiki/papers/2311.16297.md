# Quantum-classical simulation of quantum field theory by quantum circuit learning

> We employ quantum circuit learning to simulate quantum field theories (QFTs). Typically, when simulating QFTs with quantum computers, we encounter significant challenges due to the technical limitations of quantum devices when implementing the Hamiltonian using Pauli spin matrices. To address this challenge, we leverage quantum circuit learning, employing a compact configuration of qubits and low-depth quantum circuits to predict real-time dynamics in quantum field theories. The key advantage of this approach is that a single-qubit measurement can accurately forecast various physical parameters, including fully-connected operators. To demonstrate the effectiveness of our method, we use it to predict quench dynamics, chiral dynamics and jet production in a 1+1-dimensional model of quantum electrodynamics. We find that our predictions closely align with the results of rigorous classical calculations, exhibiting a high degree of accuracy. This hybrid quantum-classical approach illustrates the feasibility of efficiently simulating large-scale QFTs on cutting-edge quantum devices.

## Key claim

Quantum circuit learning (QCL) using only three or five qubits can accurately predict the real-time dynamics of a 1+1-dimensional quantum electrodynamics system that requires up to 18 qubits in conventional simulation, with the critical advantage that only a single-qubit measurement is needed even for fully-connected operators like the electric field.

## Method

The author discretizes the massive Schwinger model (1+1d QED) on a lattice using staggered fermions and transforms the Hamiltonian into a qubit representation via the Jordan-Wigner transformation. A quantum circuit learning approach is employed where a small parameterized quantum circuit (3 or 5 qubits) is trained on classical data from exact simulations of the N=18 system, using a cost function that compares the circuit's single-qubit measurement output to the target observable values. The trained circuit then predicts real-time evolution of observables including chiral condensate, electric field, axial charge density, and energy for quench dynamics, chiral dynamics, and jet production scenarios.

## Key numbers

- System size: N=18 lattice sites (corresponding to 18 qubits in conventional simulation) for all benchmark tasks
- QCL circuit size: 3 qubits for predicting chiral condensate and electric field dynamics; 5 qubits for predicting axial charge dynamics and jet production
- Lattice spacing: a=0.1 for jet production simulations
- Coupling constant: g=0.5/a for jet production simulations
- Fermion mass: m=0.25/a for jet production simulations; m=4.0 for axial charge dynamics with chiral potential values μ₅=0.5, 1, and 2
- Chiral potential values tested: μ₅=0.5, 1, 2 for chiral dynamics predictions

## Context

This work extends the quantum circuit learning approach to quantum field theory simulations, building on prior Schwinger model studies by Kharzeev (2020) and Ikeda (2020) that performed simulations of the chiral magnetic effect and quark-antiquark production in e⁺e⁻ annihilation. The method directly reproduces results from the jet production framework developed by Casher (1974) and later implemented in quantum simulations by Kharzeev and collaborators (2023). The approach enables efficient simulation of large-scale QFTs on near-term quantum devices by dramatically reducing qubit requirements, addressing the technical limitations of implementing Hamiltonians with Pauli spin matrices that have hindered conventional quantum simulations of QFTs.
