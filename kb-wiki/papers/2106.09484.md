# Investigations of the linear and non-linear flow harmonics using the A Multi-Phase Transport model

> The Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT) is used to study the effects of the parton-scattering cross-sections ($σ_{pp}$) and hadronic re-scattering on the linear contributions to the flow harmonic $\textit{v}_{4}$, the non-linear response coefficients, and the correlations between different order flow symmetry planes in Au+Au collisions at 200~GeV. The model results, which agree with current experimental measurements, indicate that the higher-order flow harmonics are sensitive to the $σ_{pp}$ variations. However, the non-linear response coefficients and the correlations between different order flow symmetry planes are $σ_{pp}$ independent. These results suggest that further detailed experimental measurements which span a broad range of collision systems and beam energies could serve as an additional constraint for the theoretical models' calculations.

## Key claim
The AMPT model results indicate that higher-order flow harmonics (v₄, v₅) are sensitive to variations in the parton-scattering cross-section σ_pp, but the non-linear response coefficients (χ₄,₂₂) and the correlations between different order flow symmetry planes (ρ₄,₂₂) are σ_pp independent, contrary to what the abstract's phrase "agree with current experimental measurements" might suggest about uniform sensitivity.

## Method
The study used the AMPT model (version ampt-v2.26t9b) with the string melting option both on and off, simulating Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV for charged hadrons with 0.2 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c and |η| < 1.0. Four parameter sets were employed: Set-1 (σ_pp = 6.1 mb, string melting off), Set-2 (σ_pp = 6.1 mb, string melting on), Set-3 (σ_pp = 2.7 mb, string melting on), and Set-4 (σ_pp = 1.8 mb, string melting on). The linear and non-linear contributions to v₄ were extracted using two- and multi-particle cumulant methods with a two-subevents technique (|Δη| > 0.7) to reduce nonflow correlations.

## Key numbers
- Parton-scattering cross-sections used: σ_pp = 6.1 mb, 2.7 mb, and 1.8 mb, with fixed α_s = 0.47 and screening mass μ varied from 2.26 to 4.2 fm⁻¹.
- Approximately 4.0, 5.0, 4.0, and 3.0 million events were generated for AMPT Set-1, Set-2, Set-3, and Set-4, respectively.
- The ratio v₂{4}/v₂{2} was found to be insensitive to the model setups to within approximately 2%, indicating flow fluctuations are eccentricity-driven.
- The AMPT model results for v_n{2} and the three-particle correlators C₄,₂₂ and C₅,₂₃ show sensitivity to σ_pp variations, while χ₄,₂₂ and ρ₄,₂₂ are σ_pp independent.

## Context
This work extends previous phenomenological studies using transport and hydrodynamic models (e.g., Magdy (2020), Yang (2019), Liu (2018), Bozek (2017)) that investigated linear and non-linear contributions to higher-order flow harmonics. The results suggest that further experimental measurements spanning a broad range of collision systems and beam energies could serve as additional constraints for theoretical models, particularly for extracting the temperature and baryon chemical potential dependence of η/s. The study is directly relevant to ongoing beam energy measurements at RHIC designed to constrain η/s(T, μ_B).
