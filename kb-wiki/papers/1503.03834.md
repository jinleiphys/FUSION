# High-mass twins & resolution of the reconfinement, masquerade and hyperon puzzles of compact star interiors

> We aim at contributing to the resolution of three of the fundamental puzzles related to the still unsolved problem of the structure of the dense core of compact stars (CS): (i) the hyperon puzzle: how to reconcile pulsar masses of $2\,$M$_\odot$ with the hyperon softening of the equation of state (EoS); (ii) the masquerade problem: modern EoS for cold, high density hadronic and quark matter are almost identical; and (iii) the reconfinement puzzle: what to do when after a deconfinement transition the hadronic EoS becomes favorable again? We show that taking into account the compositeness of baryons (by excluded volume and/or quark Pauli blocking) on the hadronic side and confining and stiffening effects on the quark matter side results in an early phase transition to quark matter with sufficient stiffening at high densities which removes all three present-day puzzles of CS interiors. Moreover, in this new class of EoS for hybrid CS falls the interesting case of a strong first order phase transition which results in the observable high mass twin star phenomenon, an astrophysical observation of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The authors show that accounting for the compositeness of baryons (via excluded volume and/or quark Pauli blocking) on the hadronic side, combined with confining and stiffening effects on the quark matter side, results in an early phase transition to quark matter with sufficient stiffening at high densities which removes all three present-day puzzles of compact star interiors: the hyperon puzzle, the masquerade problem, and the reconfinement puzzle.

## Method

The authors apply a Lorentz-invariant formulation of the excluded volume approximation (EVA) to account for baryon finite size effects in the hadronic equation of state, and use an advanced Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector meanfields and higher order quark interactions to describe stiff quark matter. They construct a Maxwell construction first order phase transition between the EVA-improved DD2 hadronic EoS and the stiff quark matter EoS, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius sequences for compact stars.

## Key numbers

- The latent heat (jump in energy density at the phase transition) amounts to at least 60% of the critical energy density at the transition for EoS exhibiting the high mass twin phenomenon.
- The microscopically motivated high mass twin EoS has a rather low critical pressure, below 100 MeV/fm^3.
- Statistical model analyses of chemical freeze-out in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions find indications for a universal pressure along the freeze-out line with a value of about 80 MeV/fm^3.
- Lattice QCD studies of finite temperature find the range of pressures in the crossover transition region to be 40 - 80 MeV/fm^3.
- The two known high-mass pulsars used as constraints are PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432, both with masses of about 2 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Benic et al. (2014) who first suggested that a strong first order phase transition in compact star interiors could be recognized from mass and radius measurements, leading to the high-mass twin star phenomenon. The paper addresses three long-standing puzzles: the hyperon puzzle (how to reconcile 2 solar mass pulsars with hyperon softening, as discussed by Baldo et al. 2003), the masquerade problem (modern EoS for hadronic and quark matter being almost identical, noted by Alford et al. 2004), and the reconfinement puzzle (when hadronic EoS becomes favorable again after deconfinement, studied by Lastowiecki et al. 2011 and Zdunik et al. 2012). The results enable future Bayesian analysis of compact star observations to extract the most probable parametrization of the high-density EoS and potentially identify a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.
