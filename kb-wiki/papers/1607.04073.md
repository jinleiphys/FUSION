# Chiral symmetry restoration in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> We study the effect of the chiral symmetry restoration (CSR) on heavy-ion collisions observables in the energy range $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=3-20 GeV within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. The PHSD includes the deconfinement phase transition as well as essential aspects of CSR in the dense and hot hadronic medium, which are incorporated in the Schwinger mechanism for the hadronic particle production. We adopt different parametrizations of the nuclear equation of state from the non-linear $σ-ω$ model, which enter in the computation of the quark scalar density for the CSR mechanism, in order to estimate the uncertainty in our calculations.

## Key claim

The chiral symmetry restoration (CSR) mechanism in the hadronic phase produces the steep increase of the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio up to $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7$ GeV, while the drop at higher energies is associated to the appearance of a deconfined partonic medium; the results depend on the chosen parametrization of the nuclear equation of state and the adopted value of the pion-nucleon $\Sigma$-term ($\Sigma_\pi \approx 45$ MeV).

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which includes both hadronic and partonic degrees-of-freedom and a deconfinement phase transition, to simulate heavy-ion collisions in the energy range $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3-20$ GeV. CSR was incorporated into the Schwinger mechanism for string decay by computing effective quark masses from the scalar quark condensate, which was evaluated dynamically using the non-linear $\sigma-\omega$ model with three different parameter sets (NL1, NL2, NL3) for the nuclear equation of state. The calculations were performed for central Au+Au collisions and compared to experimental data on particle abundances, rapidity spectra, transverse mass spectra, and excitation functions of strange to non-strange particle ratios.

## Key numbers

- Energy range studied: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3-20$ GeV for heavy-ion collisions
- String tension in vacuum: $\kappa \approx 0.176$ GeV$^2$
- Vacuum constituent quark masses: $m_u \approx 0.35$ GeV, $m_s \approx 0.5$ GeV
- Bare quark masses: $m_q^0 \approx 7$ MeV, $m_s^0 \approx 100$ MeV
- Pion-nucleon $\Sigma$-term adopted: $\Sigma_\pi \approx 45$ MeV
- Critical energy density for deconfinement: $\epsilon_c \approx 0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Vacuum strangeness suppression factor: $\gamma_s \approx 0.3$ (increases to 0.8-0.9 at $\epsilon \approx \epsilon_c$)
- Formation time for string decay products: $\tau_f \sim 0.8$ fm/c
- Vacuum scalar quark condensate: $\langle\bar{q} q\rangle_V \approx -3.2$ fm$^{-3}$ from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation
- Compression moduli for EoS parameter sets: $K = 380$ MeV (NL1, NL3) and $K = 210$ MeV (NL2)
- Effective nucleon mass ratios at saturation: $m^*/m = 0.83$ (NL1, NL2) and $m^*/m = 0.70$ (NL3)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Palmese et al. (2015) on CSR effects in heavy-ion collisions by systematically studying the sensitivity to different nuclear equation of state parametrizations (NL1, NL2, NL3 from the non-linear $\sigma-\omega$ model) and examining system size and centrality dependence. The PHSD approach provides a dynamical explanation for the "horn" structure in the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio previously described by statistical models (Cleymans et al., 2004; Andronic et al., 2006) and suggested as a QGP signature by Gazdzicki and Gorenstein (1999). The results enable future experimental searches for the QCD critical point in the ($T, \mu_B$)-phase diagram at facilities such as FAIR and NICA.
