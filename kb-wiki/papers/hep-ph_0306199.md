# Roper Resonance and S_{11}(1535) from Lattice QCD

> Using the constrained curve fitting method and overlap fermions with the lowest pion mass at $180 {\rm MeV}$, we observe that the masses of the first positive and negative parity excited states of the nucleon tend to cross over as the quark masses are taken to the chiral limit. Both results at the physical pion mass agree with the experimental values of the Roper resonance ($N^{1/2+}(1440)$) and $S_{11}$ ($N^{1/2-}(1535)$). This is seen for the first time in a lattice QCD calculation. These results are obtained on a quenched Iwasaki $16^3 \times 28$ lattice with $a = 0.2 {\rm fm}$. We also extract the ghost $η' N$ states (a quenched artifact) which are shown to decouple from the nucleon interpolation field above $m_π \sim 300 {\rm MeV}$. From the quark mass dependence of these states in the chiral region, we conclude that spontaneously broken chiral symmetry dictates the dynamics of light quarks in the nucleon.

## Key claim

Using the constrained curve fitting method and overlap fermions with the lowest pion mass at 180 MeV, the authors observe that the masses of the first positive and negative parity excited states of the nucleon tend to cross over as the quark masses are taken to the chiral limit, with both results at the physical pion mass agreeing with the experimental values of the Roper resonance and S11(1535). The claim is hedged by noting that more statistics are needed to clarify the crossing point and that the results are obtained in the quenched approximation.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD calculations on Iwasaki 16^3 × 28 and 12^3 × 28 lattices with lattice spacing a = 0.200(3) fm using overlap fermions. They implemented a constrained curve fitting method based on an empirical Bayes approach to extract excited state masses, fitting the nucleon correlator and its parity partner sequentially while including ghost η′ N states with a negative weight constraint to account for quenched artifacts. The volume dependence of spectral weights was used to distinguish one-particle states (Roper, S11) from two-particle scattering states (η′ N).

## Key numbers

- Lowest pion mass achieved: 180 MeV (specifically 181(8) MeV from the text)
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.200(3) fm determined from fπ(mπ)
- Lattice size: 16^3 × 28, corresponding to physical size 3.2 fm
- Extrapolated nucleon mass: 928(56) MeV (experimental: 939 MeV)
- Extrapolated Roper mass: 1462(157) MeV (experimental: ≈1440 MeV)
- Extrapolated S11 mass: 1554(65) MeV (experimental: ≈1535 MeV)
- Ghost η′ N state decouples from nucleon interpolation field above mπ ∼ 300 MeV
- Finite volume error at lowest pion mass: estimated ∼2.7%
- Repulsive interaction between would-be η′ and N in P-wave: about 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD studies of nucleon excited states (Lee 2001, Sasaki 2002, Richards 2002, Mathur 2002, and others) by reaching lower quark masses while preserving chiral symmetry via overlap fermions. It directly addresses the long-standing theoretical controversy between quark potential models based on SU(6) symmetry with color-spin interaction (which predict the Roper higher than S11) and chiral soliton models (which predict parity reversal), supporting the flavor-spin interaction picture proposed by Glozman and Riska (1996). The results enable future dynamical fermion calculations with light quarks to separate physical ηN and η′ N states from the S11 resonance.
