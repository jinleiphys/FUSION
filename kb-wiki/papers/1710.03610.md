# Simultaneous Production of Lepton Pairs at Ultra-peripheral Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We calculate the total cross sections and probabilities of electromagnetic productions of electron, muon and tauon pair productions simultaneously. At the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), the available electromagnetic energy is sufficient to produce all kind of leptons coherently. The masses of muon and tauon are large, so their Compton wavelengths are small enough to interact with the colliding nuclei. Therefore, the realistic nuclear form factors are included in the calculations of electromagnetic pair productions. The cross section calculations show that, at the LHC energies, the probabilities of simultaneous productions of all kind of leptons are increased significantly compare to the RHIC energies. Experimentally, observing this simultaneous production can give us important informations about the strong QED.

## Key claim
The authors calculate that at LHC energies, the total cross sections for simultaneous production of all three lepton pairs (electron, muon, and tauon) are nearly three orders of magnitude greater than at RHIC energies, making them potentially measurable. However, the cross sections remain very small (on the order of 10^-6 barn at LHC), and the authors note that experimental detection may be difficult due to the very low transverse momentum of the produced leptons.

## Method
The authors use a semi-classical approach with the electromagnetic field represented by classical four-vector potentials from both colliding nuclei, including realistic nuclear form factors via the two-parameter Fermi (Woods-Saxon) distribution for the charge distribution of protons. They calculate the total cross sections and impact-parameter-dependent probabilities for single lepton pair production, then use a factorization method to obtain probabilities for simultaneous production of two or three different lepton types. The nine-dimensional integrals are evaluated numerically using Monte Carlo techniques with over two million points, and the impact-parameter-dependent cross sections are obtained by fitting an exponential form to the calculated function F(q).

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for single electron-positron pair production at RHIC (100 GeV/nucleon Au+Au): 34,566 barn
- Total cross section for single electron-positron pair production at LHC (3400 GeV/nucleon Au+Au): 210,254 barn
- Total cross section for single muon pair production at RHIC: 0.2 barn; at LHC: 2.3 barn
- Total cross section for single tauon pair production at RHIC: 3.7 × 10^-6 barn; at LHC: 1.4 × 10^-3 barn
- Total cross section for simultaneous electron-muon pair production at RHIC: 0.039 barn; at LHC: 0.22 barn
- Total cross section for simultaneous electron-tauon pair production at RHIC: 7.3 × 10^-7 barn; at LHC: 9.4 × 10^-5 barn
- Total cross section for simultaneous muon-tauon pair production at RHIC: 9.6 × 10^-9 barn; at LHC: 1.2 × 10^-5 barn
- Total cross section for simultaneous electron-muon-tauon pair production at RHIC: 1.92 × 10^-9 barn; at LHC: 1.1 × 10^-6 barn
- For tauon production, the contribution to total cross section from impact parameters greater than 2000 fm is 0.5% at RHIC and 1% at LHC
- For electron-positron production, the contribution from impact parameters greater than 2000 fm is 72% at RHIC and 92% at LHC
- Maximum electric field at RHIC: ~2 × 10^23 V/m; at LHC: ~7 × 10^24 V/m
- Ratio of maximum electric field to critical field for tauon production: 0.01 at RHIC, 0.4 at LHC

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of single lepton pair production in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions to the simultaneous production of multiple lepton types, motivated by the higher available energy at the LHC compared to RHIC. The authors note their results agree with Baltz (2001) for electron-positron pair production, while their muon pair cross sections are slightly lower than those of Bhatk et al. (2001) due to the use of the more realistic Woods-Saxon form factor instead of a monopole form factor. The calculations enable experimental searches for simultaneous lepton pair production at LHC energies, which could provide tests of strong-field QED, and the authors suggest future comparison with the STARlight Monte Carlo program results.
