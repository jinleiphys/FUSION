# Elliptic flow of ϕ-meson a sensitive probe for onset of de-confinement transition in high energy heavy-ion collisions

> Elliptic flow (v_2) of ϕ-meson is shown to be a sensitive probe of the partonic collectivity using A Multi Phase Transport (AMPT) model. Within the ambit of the AMPT model with partonic interactions (string melting version), the ϕ-meson v_2 at midrapidity is found to have negligible contribution from hadronic interactions for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon. Changing the hadron cascade time in the model calculations does not change the ϕ-meson v_2, while it reduces the v_2 of proton at low transverse momentum (pT). These observations indicate that a substantial reduction in ϕ-mesons v_2 as a function of colliding beam energy would suggest the dominance of hadronic interactions over partonic interactions.

## Key claim

Elliptic flow (v₂) of the φ-meson is shown to be generated almost entirely by partonic interactions in the AMPT model, with negligible contribution from hadronic interactions; therefore, a substantial reduction in φ-meson v₂ as a function of colliding beam energy would indicate the dominance of hadronic over partonic interactions, signaling the onset of de-confinement transition.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting to simulate minimum bias (0-80% centrality) Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, generating approximately one million events per configuration. They varied the parton-parton scattering cross section (0, 3, 10, and 14 mb) and the hadronic cascade termination time (0.6, 15, and 30 fm/c) to isolate the contributions of partonic versus hadronic interactions to φ-meson and proton elliptic flow at midrapidity (|y| < 1.0). Results were compared to STAR experimental data and UrQMD model calculations.

## Key numbers

- φ-meson v₂ from AMPT with parton-parton cross section of 10-14 mb matches STAR data for pT > 1 GeV/c, while a 3 mb cross section (pQCD value) yields lower v₂.
- With parton-parton cross section set to 0 mb, φ-meson v₂ is consistent with zero; with 10 mb cross section, v₂ is non-zero and comparable to data.
- Varying hadronic cascade time from 0.6 fm/c to 30 fm/c leaves φ-meson v₂ unchanged, while proton v₂ at low pT decreases with increasing cascade time.
- The average transverse momentum ⟨pT⟩ for protons increases with hadronic cascade time; for φ-mesons the change is much smaller, for both parton-parton cross sections of 3 mb and 10 mb.

## Context

This work extends the understanding that multi-strange hadrons like the φ-meson have small hadronic interaction cross sections (vanHecke, 1998), making them clean probes of partonic collectivity. It builds on prior STAR measurements of φ-meson v₂ (Abelev, 2007) and number-of-constituent-quark scaling, and contrasts with UrQMD model results (which lack partonic interactions and give substantially smaller v₂). The findings enable the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program to use φ-meson v₂ as a signature for locating the phase boundary between partonic and hadronic matter in the QCD phase diagram.
