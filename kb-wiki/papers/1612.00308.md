# On the solution of a pairing problem in the continuum

> We present a generalized Richardson solution for fermions interacting with the pairing interaction in both discrete and continuum parts of the single particle (s.p.) spectrum. The pairing Hamiltonian is based on the rational Gaudin (RG) model which is formulated in the Berggren ensemble. We show that solutions of the generalized Richardson equations are exact in the two limiting situations: (i) in the pole approximation and (ii) in the s.p. continuum. If the s.p. spectrum contains both discrete and continuum parts, then the generalized Richardson equations provide accurate solutions for the Gamow Shell Model.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalized Richardson solution for the pairing Hamiltonian formulated in the Berggren ensemble (including bound states, resonances, and non-resonant continuum), but this solution is only exact in two limiting cases: the pole approximation (neglecting non-resonant continuum) and when the single-particle spectrum consists solely of continuum states; for mixed discrete-continuum spectra, the generalized Richardson equations provide accurate but approximate solutions for the Gamow Shell Model.

## Method

The authors formulate the rational Gaudin (RG) pairing model in the Berggren single-particle ensemble, which includes bound states, resonances, and discretized non-resonant continuum states. They derive generalized Richardson equations by replacing the exact commutator relations for continuum states with approximate ones that restore the SU(2) algebra, then solve these nonlinear coupled equations numerically using a Newton-Raphson method with initial conditions from the weak-coupling limit. The results are compared with exact Gamow Shell Model (GSM) diagonalizations of the same Hamiltonian using the Davidson method.

## Key numbers

- For bound single-particle levels at energies -5, -4, -3, -2, -1 MeV with pairing strength G = -0.01 MeV, the relative error in total energy between the generalized Richardson solution and exact GSM ranges from 7.2e-13 (2 pairs, 9 discretization points) to 2.9e-11 (5 pairs, 45 discretization points)
- For the same bound levels with G = -0.3 MeV, the relative error ranges from 1.3e-6 (2 pairs, 9 points) to 3.2e-5 (5 pairs, 45 points)
- For G = -0.5 MeV, the relative error ranges from 1.5e-5 (2 pairs, 9 points) to 3.1e-4 (5 pairs, 45 points)
- For G = -0.7 MeV, the relative error ranges from 6.9e-5 (2 pairs, 9 points) to 1.4e-3 (5 pairs, 45 points)
- The contour for discretization consists of three segments in k-space: [0.0; 0.5], [0.5; 1.0], and [1.0; 2.0] (units not specified but consistent with the momentum variable)

## Context

This work extends the exact Richardson solution (Richardson 1963, 1964) and the rational Gaudin model (Gaudin 1976, Dukelsky 2001) to include continuum effects in nuclear pairing problems. It addresses limitations of prior attempts by Hasegawa and Kaneko (2003) and Id Betan (2012) to formulate exact pairing solutions in the continuum. The generalized Richardson equations enable calculations beyond the computational limits of exact GSM diagonalization, which is restricted to systems with few active nucleons, potentially allowing treatment of pairing correlations in weakly-bound and unbound nuclear systems.
