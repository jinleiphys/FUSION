# Large-scale shell-model analysis of the neutrinoless $ββ$ decay of $^{48}$Ca

> We present the nuclear matrix element for the neutrinoless double-beta decay of $^{48}$Ca based on large-scale shell-model calculations including two harmonic oscillator shells ($sd$ and $pf$ shells). The excitation spectra of $^{48}$Ca and $^{48}$Ti, and the two-neutrino double-beta decay of $^{48}$Ca are reproduced in good agreement to experiment. We find that the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element is enhanced by about 30\% compared to $pf$-shell calculations. This reduces the decay lifetime by almost a factor of two. The matrix-element increase is mostly due to pairing correlations associated with cross-shell $sd$-$pf$ excitations. We also investigate possible implications for heavier neutrinoless double-beta decay candidates.

## Key claim

The neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element for ⁴⁸Ca is enhanced by about 30% when the shell-model configuration space is expanded from the pf shell alone to include both the sd and pf shells (sdpf space) with up to 2ħω cross-shell excitations, which reduces the predicted decay lifetime by almost a factor of two; the enhancement is attributed primarily to pairing correlations from sd-pf cross-shell excitations, and the authors note that the result provides an upper bound for the matrix element in the full sdpf space.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the M-scheme code KSHELL, including two harmonic oscillator shells (sd and pf) with up to 2ħω sd-pf cross-shell excitations, for the ⁴⁸Ca → ⁴⁸Ti 0νββ decay transition. The SDPFMU-db effective interaction was used, with the pf-shell part based on GXPF1B and the sd-pf shell gap adjusted to reproduce the observed 0⁺₂ level of ⁴⁸Ca; the largest calculation (⁴⁸Ti) had a dimension of 2.0×10⁹. The nuclear matrix element was computed in the closure approximation with closure parameter ⟨E⟩ = 0.5 MeV, using g_V = 1 and g_A = 1.27, and including Argonne- and CD-Bonn-type short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

- The 0νββ decay nuclear matrix element in the sdpf space is M⁰ν = 0.96 – 1.18 (range covering different short-range correlation treatments), compared to pf-shell values of M⁰ν = 0.78 – 0.92 from previous work (Menendez 2009, Horoi 2010, Senkov 2013).
- With the SDPFMU-db interaction and no short-range correlations, the total NME is M⁰ν = 1.118; with CD-Bonn SRC it is 1.183; with Argonne SRC it is 1.073.
- The 2νββ decay matrix element is M²ν = 0.051 MeV⁻¹ (SDPFMU-db) and 0.045 MeV⁻¹ (SDPFMU), in agreement with the experimental value M²ν = 0.046 ± 0.004 MeV⁻¹ (Barabash 2015).
- The 2ħω components in the ground states are 22% for ⁴⁸Ca and 33% for ⁴⁸Ti with SDPFMU-db (14% and 20% with SDPFMU).
- The 0⁺₂ state of ⁴⁸Ca obtained with SDPFMU-db shows 1.64 protons in the pf shell, versus 0.22 with SDPFMU.
- A seniority-zero calculation in the full sdpf space shows components beyond 2ħω excitations are less than 0.5% in both ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁸Ti.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of ⁴⁸Ca 0νββ decay that were restricted to the pf shell (Caurier 2008, Menendez 2009, Horoi 2010, Senkov 2013), and finds that including sd-pf cross-shell excitations increases the NME by about 30%, reducing the predicted lifetime by nearly a factor of two. The result is about 15% smaller than a perturbative calculation including orbitals outside the pf shell (Kwiatkowski 2014), and is considerably smaller than IBM (Barea 2015) and EDF (Vaquero 2013, Yao 2015) values, while significantly larger than QRPA (Simkovic 2013). The analysis suggests that for heavier 0νββ decay candidates, a competition between pairing-like 2p-2h excitations (which increase the NME) and 1p-1h excitations (which reduce it) will need to be evaluated precisely to obtain reliable NMEs.
