# The quarkynic phase and the Z_{Nc} symmetry

> We investigate the interplay between the Z_{Nc} symmetry and the emergence of the quarkyonic phase, adding the flavor-dependent complex chemical potentials μ_f=μ+iTθ_f with (θ_f)=(0, θ, -θ) to the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. When θ=0, the PNJL model with the μ_f agrees with the standard PNJL model with the real chemical potential μ. When θ=2π/3, meanwhile, the PNJL model with the μ_f has the Z_{Nc} symmetry exactly for any real μ, so that the quarkyonic phase exists at small T and large μ. Once θvaries from 2π/3, the quarkyonic phase exists only on a line of T=0 and μlarger than the dynamical quark mass, and the region at small T and large μis dominated by the quarkyonic-like phase in which the Polyakov loop is small but finite.

## Key claim

The quarkyonic phase (defined by zero Polyakov loop and nonzero quark-number density) exists as a true phase only when the Z_{Nc} symmetry is exact (theta = 2pi/3); once theta deviates from 2pi/3, the quarkyonic phase shrinks to a line at T=0 and mu larger than the dynamical quark mass, and the region at small T and large mu becomes a "quarkyonic-like phase" with small but finite Polyakov loop.

## Method

The authors used the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with three flavors and three colors, adding flavor-dependent complex chemical potentials mu_f = mu + i T theta_f with (theta_f) = (0, theta, -theta). They varied theta continuously from 0 (standard PNJL model with real chemical potential) to 2pi/3 (the twisted boundary condition model with exact Z_{Nc} symmetry) and computed the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation, solving for the Polyakov loop and chiral condensate as functions of temperature T and chemical potential mu.

## Key numbers

* For theta = 2pi/3, the first-order deconfinement transition occurs at T_c = 203 MeV at mu = 0.
* The dynamical quark mass M_f is 323 MeV at vacuum.
* The quarkyonic phase at theta = 2pi/3 exists at mu greater than or approximately equal to M_f = 323 MeV and small T.
* The Polyakov potential parameters are a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = -2.47, a_2 = 15.2, b_3 = -1.75, with T_0 rescaled to 195 MeV to reproduce T_c = 160 MeV from full lattice QCD.
* The NJL sector parameters are m_0 = 5.5 MeV, Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G_S Lambda^2 = 1.835, G_D Lambda^5 = 12.36.

## Context

This work extends the twisted boundary condition (TBC) model of Kouno et al. (2011), which introduced a QCD-like theory with exact Z_{Nc} symmetry, to study how the quarkyonic phase (first proposed in large N_c QCD by McLerran and Pisarski (2007)) evolves as the Z_{Nc} symmetry is explicitly broken. The results show that the quarkyonic-like phase seen in standard PNJL models (e.g., by McLerran et al. (2009) and by Abuki et al. (2008)) is a remnant of the exact quarkyonic phase present when Z_{Nc} symmetry is exact, enabling a unified understanding of the phase diagram across different values of the twisted angle.
