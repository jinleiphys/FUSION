# Dynamics of dilute nuclear matter with light clusters and in-medium effects

> We investigate the dynamics of dilute systems composed of nucleons and light clusters within a linear response approach, taking into account the in-medium Mott effects on cluster appearance, through a density-dependent momentum cut-off. We find that spinodal instabilities and associated growth rates are severely affected by the presence of light clusters and, in particular, by the treatment of in-medium effects, foreshadowing intriguing consequences for fragment formation in heavy-ion collisions and in the broader astrophysical context.

## Key claim

The presence of light clusters and, in particular, the treatment of in-medium Mott effects on cluster appearance severely affect spinodal instabilities and associated growth rates in dilute nuclear matter, with the direction of the effect depending on whether the density-dependent momentum cut-off is included in the dynamics (suppressing growth) or neglected (enhancing growth).

## Method

The authors perform a linear response analysis of the collisionless (Vlasov) limit of the Boltzmann equation for symmetric nuclear matter at temperatures above 5 MeV, including neutrons, protons, and deuterons as explicit degrees of freedom. They incorporate in-medium Mott effects through a density-dependent momentum cut-off on the deuteron kinetic energy, parameterized following microscopic calculations of Ropke (2015), and compare two dynamical scenarios: one where the cut-off adapts instantly to local density fluctuations (R_d >> R_delta_rho_b) and one where it remains constant (R_d << R_delta_rho_b). The spinodal border and growth rates are extracted from the dispersion relation obtained by solving the coupled linearized Vlasov equations.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron kinetic energy cut-off is parameterized as lambda_d = 440 MeV fm^2 * rho_b^(2/3) * [1 + tanh(1 - 2 * rho_d^Mott(T)/rho_b)], with the Mott density rho_d^Mott taken from Ropke (2011).
- For rho_b = 0.02 fm^-3 and T = 8 MeV, the wavelength of the most unstable modes deviates by approximately +/- 20% from the typical wavelength values (lambda approximately 10 fm) observed in pure nucleonic matter.
- The spinodal border shows a re-entrant behavior at densities below 0.002 fm^-3 at low temperature in both the hybrid and full in-medium cases.
- At densities above rho_0/3 (approximately 0.05 fm^-3) and temperatures below 8 MeV, light clusters do not much affect the growth of spinodal instabilities.

## Context

This work extends the linear response framework for spinodal instabilities in nuclear matter (Chomaz, 2004) by incorporating light clusters as explicit degrees of freedom, building on thermodynamic approaches that describe concurrent light cluster and heavy fragment formation (Hempel, 2012; Pais, 2019; Roepke, 2020). The results enable improved transport theories for heavy-ion collisions at Fermi/intermediate energies (Colonna, 2020; Sorensen, 2024) and for astrophysical scenarios such as supernova collapse and proto-neutron star structure, where the interplay between mean-field instabilities and in-medium cluster dissolution must be treated consistently.
