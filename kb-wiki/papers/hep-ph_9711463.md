# Effective Field Theory for Low-Energy Two-Nucleon Systems

> We illustrate how effective field theories work in nuclear physics by using an effective Lagrangian in which all other degrees of freedom than the nucleonic one have been integrated out to calculate the low-energy properties of two-nucleon systems, viz, the deuteron properties, the np 1S0 scattering amplitude and the M1 transition amplitude entering into the radiative np capture process. Exploiting a finite cut-off regularization procedure, we find all the two-nucleon low-energy properties to be accurately described with little cut-off dependence, in consistency with the general philosophy of effective field theories.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a pionless effective field theory (EFT) with a finite cut-off regularization can accurately describe low-energy two-nucleon properties, including the deuteron, np 1S0 scattering, and the M1 transition for radiative np capture, with little cut-off dependence. This claim is conditional on using a cut-off regularization rather than dimensional regularization, which the authors state would break down at a very small momentum scale for systems with large scattering length.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic EFT by integrating out all degrees of freedom except the nucleon, including the pion field, and use heavy-fermion formalism. They solve the Lippman-Schwinger equation with a separable potential up to next-to-leading order (NLO), employing a Gaussian cut-off regulator with cut-off scale Λ around 200 MeV. The five parameters (two in the 1S0 channel and three in the 3S1 channel) are fixed by fitting to the effective-range expansion parameters from the Argonne v18 potential.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length a = -23.732 fm and effective range r_e = 2.697 fm for the 1S0 channel (taken from Argonne v18 potential as "experimental")
- Scattering length a = 5.419 fm and effective range r_e = 1.753 fm for the 3S1 channel (from Argonne v18)
- Maximum cut-off Λ_Max ≈ 348.0 MeV for the 1S0 channel; Λ_Max = 304.0 MeV for the 3S1 channel
- Special cut-off Λ_{Z=1} ≈ 172.2 MeV for 1S0 channel; Λ_{Z=1} = 198.8 MeV and Λ_{r_Λ=0} = 216.1 MeV for 3S1 channel
- Deuteron binding energy B_d = 2.211 MeV at Λ = 216.1 MeV (experimental: 2.225 MeV)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment Q_d = 0.288 fm² at Λ = 216.1 MeV (experimental: 0.286 fm²)
- Deuteron D/S ratio η_d ≈ 0.025 (from Argonne v18)
- M1 transition amplitude M_{1B} = 3.99 fm at Λ = 216.1 MeV (Argonne v18 value: 3.98 fm)
- Phase shift agreement with Argonne v18 is "perfect up to p ∼ 70 MeV" for the 1S0 channel

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation calculation of radiative np capture by Park, Min and Rho (1995) by supplying the "missing link" to make it a complete first-principle calculation within EFT, rather than borrowing the single-particle M1 matrix element from phenomenological wave functions. The authors contrast their cut-off regularization approach with the dimensional regularization used by Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1996) and Luke and Manohar (1997), which they state breaks down at a very small scale for large scattering length. The results enable future incorporation of pions to extend the approach to higher energies, and demonstrate that EFTs can work in nuclear physics as they do in particle physics.
