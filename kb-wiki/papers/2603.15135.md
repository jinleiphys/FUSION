# A systematic study of global spin polarizations and correlations of hadrons with different spins in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Based on the formalism presented in [1], we make a systematic study of spin polarizations of hadrons with different spins including vector mesons, spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 hyperons and spin correlations of hadron-hadron such as hyperon-hyperon, hyperon-vector meson and vector meson-vector meson in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We present the results obtained and discuss the physical consequences in special cases. These results can be used for further numerical studies and for references of future experiments as well.

## Key claim

Based on the formalism presented in Lv (2024), the authors systematically derive analytic expressions for spin polarizations of hadrons with spins 1/2, 1, and 3/2, and for spin correlations between hadron pairs (hyperon-hyperon, hyperon-vector meson, vector meson-vector meson) in relativistic heavy ion collisions, under the assumption that hadronization proceeds via the quark combination mechanism and that the spin density matrix elements of produced hadrons are independent of the transition operator due to space rotation invariance.

## Method

The authors use the quark combination mechanism, where hadrons form from a quark matter system of quarks and antiquarks, and calculate hadron spin density matrices from effective quark spin density matrices via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, with the transition operator contribution absorbed into normalization constants. They unify the decomposition of spin density matrices for hadrons of any spin using a complete set of traceless hermitian matrices, and define hadron-hadron spin correlations via a decomposition analogous to that for quarks. Results are presented in terms of quark spin polarizations and two-quark, three-quark, and four-quark spin correlations, with special attention to longitudinal (LL) components measurable via polar angle distributions of decay products.

## Key numbers

- For spin-1/2 hyperons, the polarization of Lambda is given by \(P_{\Lambda j}(\alpha_{\Lambda}) = \bar{P}_{sj} + \delta\bar{\rho}_{\Lambda j} / \bar{C}_{\Lambda}\), where \(\bar{C}_{\Lambda} = 1 - \bar{t}^{(ud)}_{ii}\).
- For vector mesons, the tensor polarization component \(S_{LL} = (3\bar{t}^{(q_1 \bar{q}_2)}_{zz} - \bar{t}^{(q_1 \bar{q}_2)}_{ii}) / \bar{C}_V\), with normalization \(\bar{C}_V = 3 + \bar{t}_{ii}^{(q_1\bar q_2)}\).
- For spin-3/2 hyperons, the longitudinal vector polarization \(S_L = (5\sum_{j=1}^3 \bar{P}_{q_j z} + \bar{t}^{\{ q_1 q_2 q_3\}}_{zii}) / (2\bar{C}_3)\), where \(\bar{C}_3 = 3 + \bar{t}^{(q_1 q_2)}_{ii} + \bar{t}^{(q_2 q_3)}_{ii} + \bar{t}^{(q_3 q_1)}_{ii}\).
- For Lambda-anti-Lambda spin correlations, \(\bar{t}^{\Lambda \bar{\Lambda}}_{ij} = [\bar{t}^{(s \bar{s})}_{ij} - \bar{t}^{(s \bar{u} \bar{d} \bar{s})}_{illj} - \bar{t}^{(uds \bar{s})}_{llij} + \bar{t}^{(uds \bar{u} \bar{d} \bar{s})}_{llikkj}] / \bar{C}_{\Lambda \bar{\Lambda}}\), with \(\bar{C}_{\Lambda \bar{\Lambda}} = 1 - \bar{t}^{(ud)}_{ll} - \bar{t}^{(\bar{u} \bar{d})}_{ll} + \bar{t}^{(ud \bar{u} \bar{d})}_{llkk}\).
- For hyperon-vector meson spin correlations, \(\bar{t}^{\Lambda V}_{z,LL} = -[\bar{t}^{(s q_4 \bar{q}_5)}_{zii} - 3\bar{t}^{(s q_4 \bar{q}_5)}_{zzz} - \bar{t}^{(u d s q_4 \bar{q}_5)}_{iizjj} + 3\bar{t}^{(u d s q_4 \bar{q}_5)}_{iiz zz}] / \bar{C}_{\Lambda V}\), with \(\bar{C}_{\Lambda V} = 3 + \bar{t}^{(q_4 \bar{q}_5)}_{ii} - 3\bar{t}^{(ud)}_{ii} - \bar{t}^{(ud q_4 \bar{q}_5)}_{iijj}\).
- For vector meson-vector meson spin correlations, \(\bar{t}^{V_1 V_2}_{LL,LL} = [\bar{t}^{(q_1 \bar{q}_2 q_3 \bar{q}_4)}_{iijj} - 3\bar{t}^{(q_1 \bar{q}_2 q_3 \bar{q}_4)}_{iizz} - 3(\bar{t}^{(q_1 \bar{q}_2 q_3 \bar{q}_4)}_{zzii} - 3\bar{t}^{(q_1 \bar{q}_2 q_3 \bar{q}_4)}_{zzzz})] / \bar{C}_{V_1 V_2}\), with \(\bar{C}_{V_1 V_2} = 9 + 3\bar{t}^{(q_1 \bar{q}_2)}_{ii} + 3\bar{t}^{(q_3 \bar{q}_4)}_{ii} + \bar{t}^{(q_1 \bar{q}_2 q_3 \bar{q}_4)}_{iijj}\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Lv (2024) for describing quark spin polarizations and correlations to a comprehensive set of hadron observables, including vector mesons and spin-3/2 hyperons in addition to spin-1/2 hyperons. It provides the theoretical foundation for interpreting experimental measurements from STAR, ALICE, and HADES collaborations on global polarization of Lambda hyperons (STAR 2017, 2018, 2020, 2021; ALICE 2019; HADES 2022) and vector meson spin alignments (STAR 2022, 2008; ALICE 2019, 2022). The results enable future numerical studies and experimental searches for hadron-hadron spin correlations, particularly the long-range quark spin correlations probed by hyperon-vector meson and vector meson-vector meson correlations, which are predicted to vanish if long-range quark spin correlations are zero.
