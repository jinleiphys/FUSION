# Low energy monopole Modes of a Trapped atomic Fermi Gas

> We consider the low energy collective monopole modes of a trapped weakly interacting atomic Fermi gas in the collisionless regime. The spectrum is calculated for varying coupling strength and chemical potential. Using an effective Hamiltonian, we derive analytical results that agree well with numerical calculations in various regimes. The onset of superfluidity is shown to lead to effects such as the vanishing of the energy required to create a Cooper molecule at a critical coupling strength and to the emergence of pair vibration excitations. Our analysis suggests ways to experimentally detect the presence of the superfluid phase in trapped atomic Fermi gases.

## Key claim

For a trapped weakly interacting atomic Fermi gas in the collisionless regime, the onset of superfluidity can be detected via the behavior of the lowest monopole collective mode: the energy required to create a Cooper molecule vanishes at a critical coupling strength for a closed-shell configuration, and a pair vibration mode with energy approximately twice the pairing gap emerges in the superfluid phase.

## Method

The collective monopole modes were calculated using the random-phase approximation (RPA) at zero temperature, with the response matrix including anomalous correlation functions to describe superfluidity. For the intrashell regime (where Cooper pairs form only within the same harmonic oscillator shell), an effective Hamiltonian was derived and used to obtain analytical RPA solutions for both closed-shell and open-shell configurations. These analytical results were compared to exact numerical solutions of the full RPA equations.

## Key numbers

- For a closed-shell system near the critical coupling, the lowest particle-conserving monopole excitation frequency scales as \((\omega_r + \omega_a)/\omega_T = 2[2/(7\xi(3))]^{1/2} \sqrt{1/G - 1/G_c}\) as \(G \to G_c^-\), where \(\xi(3) \approx 1.202\).
- For weak coupling in the closed-shell case, \((\omega_r + \omega_a)/\omega_T = 2 - 4G\) as \(G \to 0\).
- For the superfluid closed-shell case just above the critical coupling, the pair vibration mode energy is \(\omega/\omega_T = 2\Delta/\omega_T = [2^{1/2}3/(7\xi(3))]^{1/2} \sqrt{1/G_c - 1/G}\) as \(G \to G_c^+\).
- For the open-shell superfluid case in the weak-coupling limit, \(\omega/\omega_T \simeq 2\Delta/\omega_T = 2G/[1 - 2^{5/2}\ln(e^\gamma n_F)G/3]\) as \(G \to 0_+\), with \(\gamma \approx 0.577\).
- The critical particle number separating intrashell and bulk regimes is \(N_\sigma^* = (1/6)(e/2)^7 \exp(3\pi/2k_F|a|)\); for \(k_F|a| = 0.1\) this gives \(N_\sigma^* = 4 \times 10^{20}\), and for \(k_F|a| = 0.3\) it gives \(N_\sigma^* = 9 \times 10^6\).
- Numerical calculations used \(n_F = 50\), corresponding to approximately \(2 \times 10^4\) particles in each hyperfine state.

## Context

This work extends the study of collective modes in trapped Fermi gases from the normal phase into the superfluid phase, building on the BCS theory of superfluidity in these systems (Stoof, 1999) and earlier RPA calculations (Bruun and Mottelson, 2001). The results provide experimentally testable signatures of the superfluid transition through monopole mode spectroscopy, complementing other proposed detection methods such as laser probing of the pairing energy and density profile measurements. The analysis is limited to the intrashell regime, which is argued to be the relevant regime for current experiments with \(N \sim 10^5\) particles in optical traps with trap frequencies \(1.2\ \text{kHz} \le \omega_T/2\pi \le 12\ \text{kHz}\).
