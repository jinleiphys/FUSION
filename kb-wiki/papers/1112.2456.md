# Effective range corrections from effective field theory with di-baryon fields and perturbative pions

> Contributions of perturbative pions around a non-trivial fixed point are studied by utilizing di-baryon fields. We calculate ${}^1S_0$ and ${}^3S_1$ phase shifts for the $np$ scattering at low energies up to one-pion-exchange contributions. We also calculate effective range parameters, $v_2$, $v_3$ and $v_4$ in the higher order of the effective range expansion, and obtain corrections of the effective range to the expressions previously reported by Cohen and Hansen. After the scattering length and the effective range are renormalized, we study the role of renormailzation scale parameter $μ$ and discuss the range of validity of the theory.

## Key claim

The paper calculates one-pion-exchange contributions to S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering using di-baryon fields around the non-trivial fixed point, obtaining corrections to the effective range parameters v2, v3, and v4 that were previously reported by Cohen and Hansen (1999); the authors retain the renormalization scale dependence in higher orders as a probe of optimal higher-order contributions, rather than removing it.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory with di-baryon fields for the 1S0 and 3S1 channels, treat pions perturbatively around the non-trivial fixed point, and compute the S-wave np scattering amplitude including one-pion-exchange contributions up to O(Q0). They expand the resulting p cot δ0 in powers of momentum to extract the effective range parameters v2, v3, and v4, renormalize the scattering length and effective range to empirical values, and study the dependence of the higher-order parameters on the renormalization scale μ in the power divergence subtraction scheme.

## Key numbers

- For the 1S0 channel: 1/a0 = -8.30 MeV, r0 = 2.73 fm (empirical values used for renormalization)
- For the 3S1 channel: 1/a1 = 36.4 MeV, r1 = 1.76 fm (empirical values used for renormalization)
- 1/ad(μ) vanishes for the 1S0 channel at μ = 52.6 MeV and 154.6 MeV
- With μ = mπ, 2mπ, and 3mπ, the applicable range for S-wave phase shifts is less than p ∼ 50 MeV
- The perturbative expansion of the pion-exchange contribution is reasonable for p ≤ 150 MeV based on the ratio R criterion
- For the 1S0 channel with μ = 178 MeV (fitted to reproduce PWA v2): v2 = -0.48 fm3, v3 = 4.40 fm5, v4 = -26.8 fm7
- For the 3S1 channel with μ = 167 MeV (fitted to reproduce PWA v2): v2 = 0.04 fm3, v3 = 0.75 fm5, v4 = -4.1 fm7
- For the 1S0 channel with μ = mπ: v2 = -1.74 fm3, v3 = 11.1 fm5, v4 = -68.0 fm7
- For the 3S1 channel with μ = mπ: v2 = -0.05 fm3, v3 = 1.42 fm5, v4 = -8.22 fm7

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory with di-baryon fields by incorporating perturbative one-pion-exchange contributions, building on the renormalization group analysis of Birse, McGovern, and Richardson (1999) which identified the non-trivial fixed point. The authors calculate corrections to the effective range parameters v2, v3, and v4 that were previously obtained by Cohen and Hansen (1999) using a pionful EFT without di-baryon fields, and compare with results from Fleming, Mehen, and Stewart (2000) in the KSW counting scheme. The formalism provides a linear relation among coefficients when matching to the effective range expansion, and the μ-dependence study enables assessment of the theory's predictive range and the role of higher-order corrections.
