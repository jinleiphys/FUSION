# Phenomenology of Heavy Meson Chiral Lagrangians

> The approximate symmetries of Quantum ChromoDynamics in the infinite heavy quark ($Q=c,b$) mass limit ($m_Q \to \infty$) and in the chiral limit for the light quarks ($m_q \to 0,\;q=\,u,\,d,\,s$) can be used together to build up an effective chiral lagrangian for heavy and light mesons describing strong interactions among effective meson fields as well as their couplings to electromagnetic and weak currents, including the relevant symmetry breaking terms. The effective theory includes heavy ($Q \bar q$) mesons of both negative and positive parity, light pseudoscalars, as well as light vector mesons. We summarize the estimates for the parameters entering the effective lagrangian and discuss in particular some phenomenologically important couplings, such as $g_{B^* B π}$. The hyperfine splitting of heavy mesons is discussed in detail. The effective lagrangian allows for the possibility to describe consistently weak couplings of heavy ($B,\, D$) to light ($π,\, ρ, \, K^*,\,$ etc.) mesons. The method has however its own limitations, due to the requirement that the light meson momenta should be small, and we discuss how such limitations can be circumvented through reasonable ansatz on the form factors. Flavour conserving (e. g. $B^* \to B\, γ$) and flavour changing (e. g. $B \to K^* \, γ$) radiative decays provide another field of applications of effective lagrangians; they are discussed together with their phenomenological implications. Finally we analyze effective lagrangians describing heavy charmonium- like ($\bar Q Q$) mesons and their strong and electromagnetic interactions. The role of approximate heavy quark symmetries for this case and the phenomenological tests of these models are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper systematically constructs an effective chiral Lagrangian for heavy and light mesons by simultaneously exploiting the infinite heavy quark mass limit (m_Q → ∞) and the chiral limit for light quarks (m_q → 0), but the method is limited to processes where light meson momenta remain small, requiring additional ansätze on form factors to circumvent this restriction.

## Method

The authors build an effective field theory combining Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) with chiral perturbation theory, using the CCWZ formalism to couple heavy meson fields (both negative and positive parity) to the pseudoscalar Goldstone boson octet and light vector mesons. They include symmetry-breaking terms from finite quark masses and 1/m_Q corrections, then apply the Lagrangian to compute strong couplings, hyperfine splittings, semileptonic decays, and radiative decays of heavy mesons, including one-loop chiral corrections. The analysis also extends the formalism to heavy quarkonium-like (Q̅Q) mesons.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f = 132 MeV is used in the exponential representation of the Goldstone boson matrix Σ.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ is estimated to be of order 1 GeV.
- The leading-order heavy meson propagators are i/(2v·k) for pseudoscalars and -i(g^{μν} - v^μ v^ν)/(2v·k) for vectors.
- The Wilson coefficient a_2(μ) for the chromomagnetic operator runs as [α_s(m_Q)/α_s(μ)]^{-9/(33-2N_f)} in leading-log approximation, while a_1(μ) = 1 due to reparametrization invariance.
- The hyperfine splitting M_{B*} - M_B and the ratio f_{D_s}/f_D are computed as examples of one-loop chiral corrections, though the paper does not quote specific numerical values for these quantities in the truncated text.

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on combined heavy-quark and chiral symmetry approaches by Wise (1992), Yan et al. (1992), and Burdman and Donoghue (1992), systematizing the effective Lagrangian formalism for heavy mesons including positive-parity states and light vector mesons. The paper enables future calculations of strong couplings (such as g_{B*Bπ}), semileptonic form factors, and radiative decays (B* → Bγ, B → K*γ) within a unified framework, while also discussing limitations from the low-momentum requirement and the abundance of undetermined coupling constants that must be fixed from experiment, QCD sum rules, potential models, or lattice QCD.
