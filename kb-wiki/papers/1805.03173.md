# The gluon and charm content of the deuteron

> We evaluate the frame-independent gluon and charm parton-distribution functions (PDFs) of the deuteron utilizing light-front quantization and the impulse approximation. We use a nuclear wave function obtained from solving the nonrelativistic Schroedinger equation with the realistic Argonne v18 nuclear force, which we fold with the proton PDF. The predicted gluon distribution in the deuteron (per nucleon) is a few percent smaller than that of the proton in the domain x_{bj} = Q^2 / (2 p_N \cdot q) \sim 0.4, whereas it is strongly enhanced for x_{bj} larger than 0.6. We discuss the applicability of our analysis and comment on how to extend it to the kinematic limit x_{bj} \to 2. We also analyze the charm distribution of the deuteron within the same approach by considering both the perturbatively and non-perturbatively generated (intrinsic) charm contributions. In particular, we note that the intrinsic-charm content in the deuteron will be enhanced due to 6-quark "hidden-color" QCD configurations.

## Key claim

The gluon distribution in the deuteron (per nucleon) is a few percent smaller than that of the proton near x_bj ~ 0.4, but becomes strongly enhanced for x_bj larger than 0.6 due to Fermi motion; the intrinsic-charm content of the deuteron is expected to be enhanced relative to the nucleon due to 6-quark "hidden-color" QCD configurations, though the overall normalization is currently uncertain.

## Method

The authors solved the nonrelativistic Schroedinger equation for the two-nucleon system using the Argonne v18 nuclear potential with the Gaussian expansion method to obtain the deuteron wave function. They then performed a boost-invariant light-front transformation of this wave function and folded it with the gluon parton distribution function (PDF) of the proton (using GRV98) via the impulse approximation to obtain the deuteron gluon PDF. The charm distribution was computed similarly by folding the charm PDF from CTEQ-JLAB 15 (for perturbative charm) and a model for intrinsic charm from Brodsky (1981) with the same nuclear light-front distribution.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron wave function has a D-wave component representing 6% of the total probability.
- The standard deviation of the nucleon momentum distribution in the deuteron is close to 50 MeV.
- The gluon PDF ratio G^d / G^p is within 5% of 2 for 0 < x < 0.6.
- The ratio G^d / G^p shows a minimum near x = 0.4.
- The domain of applicability of the calculation is estimated to extend up to x ~ 1.1, where the average squared nucleon velocity <v^2> is approximately 0.3.
- The intrinsic-charm distribution in the nucleon peaks at x ~ 0.2.
- The counting rules predict G^d(x) proportional to (2-x)^11 at the endpoint x ~ 2.

## Context

This work extends the impulse-approximation approach to nuclear PDFs previously applied to quark distributions (e.g., Merabet (1993), Chen (2004)) by computing the gluon and charm content of the deuteron specifically. It provides predictions relevant for future fixed-target experiments at the LHC (AFTER@LHC study group, Brodsky (2012)) and for interpreting ultra-high-energy neutrino data from IceCube (Aartsen (2014), Aartsen (2016)). The analysis enables improved extraction of the neutron gluon PDF from deuteron data and motivates measurements of charm at high x_bj to determine the normalization of intrinsic-charm and hidden-color states.
