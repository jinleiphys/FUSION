# Study of compound nucleus formation via bremsstrahlung emission in proton $α$-particle scattering

> In this paper a role of many-nucleon dynamics in formation of the compound $^{5}{\rm Li}$ nucleus in the scattering of protons off $α$-particles at the proton incident energies up to 20 MeV is investigated. We propose a bremsstrahlung model allowing to extract information about probabilities of formation of such nucleus on the basis of analysis of experimental cross-sections of the bremsstrahlung photons. In order to realize this approach, the model includes elements of microscopic theory and also probabilities of formation of the short-lived compound nucleus. Results of calculations of the bremsstrahlung spectra are in good agreement with the experimental cross-sections.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a bremsstrahlung model that includes elements of microscopic many-nucleon theory to extract information about the probability of forming the short-lived compound nucleus 5Li in proton-alpha scattering, but the model's ability to reveal such information depends on the assumption that the internal nucleon structure of the alpha particle and nucleus produces measurable corrections to the bremsstrahlung spectrum beyond the leading contribution from relative motion.

## Method

The authors generalize their existing photon emission operator for a system of a scattering proton and a nucleus composed of A nucleons, transforming it to the center-of-mass frame and expressing it in terms of relative coordinates and momenta. They construct the full nuclear wave function as an antisymmetrized product of one-nucleon space and spin-isospin functions, then compute the matrix element for photon emission, retaining only the term involving the relative momentum operator p (the second matrix element M2) while neglecting contributions from center-of-mass motion and internal nucleon deformations. The bremsstrahlung cross-sections are obtained after applying multipolar expansion for the photon wave function.

## Key numbers

* Proton incident energies up to 20 MeV are considered for the p + alpha scattering system forming 5Li.
* The model is tested against experimental bremsstrahlung data from Wolfli (1971) measured in coplanar geometry, and compared with calculations by Liu, Tang and Kanada (1990) and Dohet-Eraly (2014).
* Phase shifts of the scattering wave functions are compared with empirical data from Arndt, Roper, and Shotwell (1971).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous bremsstrahlung formalism (Maydanyuk, 2003-2016) by incorporating elements of microscopic theory to study compound nucleus formation, specifically targeting the p + alpha system where experimental data exist in coplanar geometry from Wolfli (1971). The approach contrasts with microscopic cluster models using the resonating group method (Wildermuth, 1977; Tang, 1978) and generator coordinate method (Horiuchi, 1977), which explain resonant maxima in bremsstrahlung cross-sections via compound nuclear resonances. The model enables future investigation of how many-nucleon dynamics and spinor properties of nucleons affect photon emission in nuclear reactions, and could be extended to study fusion and breakup processes in light nuclear systems.
