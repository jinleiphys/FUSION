# The Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and isospin symmetry breaking in nuclear matter

> The quark-meson coupling model which we have developed previously is extended to incorporate the $δ$ meson. It is then used to study the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and isospin symmetry breaking in nuclear matter. We find that, in combination with the {\it u-d\/} mass difference, the difference between quark scalar densities in protons and neutrons generates an effective neutron-proton mass difference of the right magnitude. Finally we find that isospin symmetry breaking in the quark condensates can be directly related to the $δ$ meson field.

# The Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and isospin symmetry breaking in nuclear matter

## Key claim
The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model extended to include the isovector scalar δ meson can account for the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly in finite nuclei, provided the u-d quark mass difference is combined with the difference between quark scalar densities in protons and neutrons. The calculated binding energy differences for mirror nuclei are of the right order of magnitude to resolve the anomaly, though a complete treatment including spin-orbit effects goes beyond the local density approximation used.

## Method
The authors extended the QMC model, in which nuclear matter consists of non-overlapping nucleon bags bound by self-consistent exchange of σ, ω, ρ and δ mesons in the mean-field approximation, to incorporate the δ meson. They solved the Dirac equation for quarks inside MIT bags interacting with these mean fields, fitted coupling constants to reproduce nuclear matter saturation (binding energy −16 MeV at density 0.17 fm⁻³) and the bulk symmetry energy (33.2 MeV), then used a local density approximation with Woods-Saxon density profiles to compute the effective neutron-proton mass difference in finite nuclei and the resulting ΔE_c for mirror nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Free neutron-proton mass difference Δₙₚ⁰ = 1.293 MeV; the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly is expressed as ΔE_c = M_{Z>} − M_{Z<} + 0.782 MeV + E_c, where 0.782 MeV is the neutron-proton atomic mass difference.
- Bare proton mass taken as 938.272 − 0.63 MeV and bare neutron mass as 939.566 + 0.13 MeV, after subtracting electromagnetic self-energies of +0.63 MeV (proton) and −0.13 MeV (neutron).
- Coupling constants for R₀ = 0.8 fm: g_σ²/4π = 20.8, g_ω²/4π = 1.26, g_ρ²/4π = 5.74, g_δ²/4π = 2.82 (fixed from Bonn potential); meson masses: m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_ρ = 770 MeV, m_δ = 983 MeV.
- Nuclear compressibility around 220 MeV; saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.
- For symmetric matter (f_p = 0.5), the isospin symmetry breaking parameter γ in the quark condensate is γ ≈ γ₀ − 1.0 × 10⁻³ (ρ_B/ρ₀) for R₀ = 0.6 fm and γ ≈ γ₀ − 1.3 × 10⁻³ (ρ_B/ρ₀) for R₀ = 1.0 fm, where γ₀ = −(6 to 9) × 10⁻³ in vacuum and |Q_u(0)| ≈ (225 MeV)³.
- Ratio g_Aⁿ/g_Aᵖ = 1 + (3/5)η with η ≈ 2 × 10⁻³ + 0.2 × 10⁻⁴ (ρ_B/ρ₀) for R₀ = 0.6 fm and η ≈ 4 × 10⁻³ + 0.6 × 10⁻⁴ (ρ_B/ρ₀) for R₀ = 1.0 fm in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Context
This work extends the QMC model developed by Saito and Thomas (1994) and Guichon (1988) to include the δ meson, addressing the long-standing Nolen-Schiffer (Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer) anomaly that conventional nuclear contributions and ρ-ω mixing explanations (e.g., Henley and Serot) had left unresolved. The approach contrasts with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model study by Henley and Krein (1989) and with QCD sum-rule investigations by Hatsuda and collaborators. The results enable future experimental searches for isospin symmetry breaking in the axial-vector neutral-current coupling constants of protons and neutrons, and provide a framework for relating isospin symmetry breaking in quark condensates directly to the δ meson field in nuclear matter.
