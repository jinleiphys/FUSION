# Bound-state formation and thermalization within the Lindblad approach

> The Lindblad equation, as one approach to open quantum systems, describes the density matrix of a particle or a chain of interacting particles, which are in contact with a thermal bath. Still, it is not fully understood yet, how arbitrary systems evolve towards a stationary distribution, which guarantees thermalization in a thermodynamical context, and how to systematically incorporate the variety of assumptions that are made in this approach in order to preserve thermal Gibbs states. Despite these shortcomings, Lindblad dynamics was successfully employed in heavy-ion physics (quarkonia) and also became of interest in quantum-computer applications. In this paper, we consider a problem borrowed from heavy-ion collisions, namely the formation of bound states, as for example the deuteron, in the non-relativistic regime by using the already well understood techniques of Lindblad dynamics. However, only recently, we were able to extend this toolbox by showing, that the position-space Lindblad equation can be reformulated in terms of a diffusion-advection equation with sources and therefore provides a hydrodynamical formulation of a dissipative quantum master equation. Making use of this advanced machinery and insights, we describe the possible formation of a bound state, which is realized by a Pöschl-Teller-like potential, of a particle in interaction with a heat bath in a 1-dim setting. We analyse the possibility of a thermalization and the time-scale of the formation, population and depopulation of the bound state. Finally, we also show an example of a much deeper potential, where we allow for three bound states, just in the spirit of quarkonia. Besides this, we discuss general aspects of open quantum systems, like decoherence, entropy production etc.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Lindblad equation, reformulated as a diffusion-advection equation with sources, can describe bound-state formation and thermalization in a one-dimensional Pöschl-Teller potential, but the thermalization time depends on the specific initial state and the bound state may not thermalize on the same timescale as the full system.

## Method

The authors reformulate the position-space Lindblad equation into a conservative advection-diffusion equation by splitting the density matrix into real and imaginary parts, then apply numerical methods from computational fluid dynamics (Kurganov-Tadmor scheme) to evolve the system. They model a single particle in a one-dimensional Pöschl-Teller-like potential (with parameters motivated by deuteron binding energy) coupled to an Ohmic heat bath via the Caldeira-Leggett model in Lindblad form, using constant diffusion coefficients D_pp = 2mγT, D_px = -γT/Ω (or Ωγ/6πT), and D_xx = 0.

## Key numbers

* The reduced mass of the deuteron is set to m = 470 MeV, with ℏ = k_B = 1.
* The computational domain is L × L = 40 × 40 fm², divided into 300 × 300 cells.
* The Pöschl-Teller potential parameters are α = 1.45 fm⁻¹ and V₀ = 16.5 MeV, yielding a bound state at energy -2.3 MeV (n=0).
* The heat bath temperature is T = 250 MeV, with cut-off frequency Ω = 4T and damping coefficient γ = 0.1 c/fm for the main simulations.
* The deuteron binding energy is approximately 2.3 MeV, which is orders of magnitude below the hadronic chemical freeze-out temperature of approximately 155 MeV.
* Heavy-ion collision energies range from √s = 2.4 GeV to 13000 GeV at LHC and RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Rais 2022) which used a simpler potential and pulse-based interactions, now employing continuous system-bath coupling via the Caldeira-Leggett model. It builds on the fluid-dynamical reformulation of the Lindblad equation (Rais 2024) and applies it to bound-state formation relevant to heavy-ion collisions, where deuteron production at energies far above the binding energy remains unexplained by coalescence models (Nagle 1996, Mattiello 1995) and n-body collision approaches (Neidig 2021, Staudenmaier 2021). The work enables quantitative study of thermalization timescales and bound-state modification in open quantum systems relevant to quarkonia and deuteron formation in heavy-ion collisions.
