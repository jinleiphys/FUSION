# Space-time Evolution of Quark-Gluon Plasma Fluid with First Order Phase Transition

> We numerically solve the (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical equation with a bag-model equation of state, which is one of the simplest models for the first order phase transition. Based on the numerical solution, we discuss the space-time evolution of the hot fluid produced in ultra relativistic nuclear collisions. Especially, the space-time structure of the two-phase co-existing region during the quark-gluon plasma-hadron phase transition is analyzed in detail. Finally, we investigate the change of the mass spectra of $J/ψ$, $η_{c}$ and $ϕ$ as possible experimental signatures of the phase transition.

# Space-time Evolution of Quark-Gluon Plasma Fluid with First Order Phase Transition

## Key claim
The authors numerically solve (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics with a bag-model equation of state and find that the isothermal region at the phase transition temperature T_c appears clearly, but the accumulated mass spectra of J/ψ, η_c, and φ do not show distinguishable double peaks at CERN SPS energies, meaning the T_c peak is indistinguishable from the hot hadronic contribution in present experiments.

## Method
The authors solve the (3+1)-dimensional relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamical equation using a bag-model equation of state with a first-order phase transition at T_c = 160 MeV, parameterizing the mixed phase via a volume fraction λ. Initial conditions are set on the τ = 1 fm hypersurface with a Gaussian entropy density distribution in both rapidity and radius, tuned to reproduce experimental hadron distributions from WA80, NA49, and WA98 for S+Au at 200 AGeV and Pb+Pb at 158 AGeV.

## Key numbers
- Phase transition temperature: T_c = 160 MeV
- Bag constant: B = 412 MeV/fm³, obtained from pressure continuity at T_c
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 140 MeV for S+Au, T_f = 138 MeV for Pb+Pb
- Initial temperature: T_0 = 209 MeV for S+Au, T_0 = 190 MeV for Pb+Pb
- Initial proper time: τ_0 = 1 fm
- Gaussian smearing width for φ spectrum: σ = 10 MeV
- η_c yield from the T = T_c region is almost the same order as the cold and thermal contributions, while J/ψ yield from T_c is several orders smaller

## Context
This work extends earlier (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical studies of QGP phase transitions, including viscous fluid calculations by Date et al. and (3+1)-dimensional expansion with phase transition by Akase et al. It contrasts with the scaling ansatz approach of Alam et al. and Kajantie et al., who focused only on transverse expansion, and with the smeared equation of state used by Sollfrank et al. The paper applies the mass-shift proposals of Hioki et al. for J/ψ and η_c and Asakawa et al. for φ to a full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical model, finding that while η_c is a better signature than J/ψ, the φ spectrum does not show distinguishable double peaks at current SPS energies, suggesting that higher precision future experiments are needed.
