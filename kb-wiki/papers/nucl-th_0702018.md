# Neutron-$^3$H potentials and the $^5$H-properties

> The continuum resonance spectrum of $^5$H ($^3$H+$n$+$n$) is investigated by use of the complex scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method. The crucial $^3$H-neutron potential is obtained by switching off the Coulomb part from successful fits to $^3$He-proton experimental data. These two-body potentials must be expressed exclusively by operators conserving the nucleon-core mean field angular momentum quantum numbers. The energies $E_R$ and widths $Γ_R$ of the $1/2^+$ ground-state resonance and the lowest two excited $5/2^+$ and $3/2^+$-resonances are found to be $(1.6,1.5)$ MeV, $(2.8,2.5)$ MeV and $(3.2,3.9)$ MeV, respectively. These results agree with most of the experimental data. The energy distributions of the fragments after decay of the resonances are predicted.

## Key claim

The authors compute the resonance spectrum of $^5$H using a neutron-$^3$H potential derived by removing the Coulomb part from a $^3$He-proton potential that reproduces experimental phase shifts, but they note that the resulting resonance energies and widths depend on whether one defines resonances as $S$-matrix poles or via the maximum of $d\delta/dE$, and that the $^4$H $S$-matrix pole energies are systematically lower than those from $R$-matrix or Breit-Wigner analyses.

## Method

The continuum resonance spectrum of $^5$H ($^3$H+$n$+$n$) was investigated using the complex scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method with Faddeev decomposition. The neutron-$^3$H two-body potential was constructed by fitting a $^3$He-proton potential of the form $V_{Nc}(r)=V_c(r)+V_{js}(r)(\bm{\ell}+\bm{s}_N)\cdot\bm{s}_c+V_{so}(r)\bm{\ell}\cdot\bm{s}_N$ to experimental $^3$He-proton phase shifts from Bel (1985), then removing the Coulomb interaction; this form conserves $\{\ell, j_N, j\}$ quantum numbers consistent with mean-field motion. The three-body resonances were obtained as poles of the $S$-matrix by solving the coupled radial equations with complex rotation of the hyperradius.

## Key numbers

- $^5$H $1/2^+$ ground-state resonance: $E_R=1.6$ MeV, $\Gamma_R=1.5$ MeV
- $^5$H $5/2^+$ first excited resonance: $E_R=2.8$ MeV, $\Gamma_R=2.5$ MeV
- $^5$H $3/2^+$ second excited resonance: $E_R=3.2$ MeV, $\Gamma_R=3.9$ MeV
- $^4$H $2^-$ state as $S$-matrix pole: $(E_R,\Gamma_R)=(1.22,3.34)$ MeV; from $d\delta/dE$ maximum: $(1.80,6.21)$ MeV
- $^4$H $1^-$ state as $S$-matrix pole: $(E_R,\Gamma_R)=(1.15,3.49)$ MeV; from $d\delta/dE$ maximum: $(1.70,6.84)$ MeV
- $^4$H $0^-$ state as $S$-matrix pole: $(E_R,\Gamma_R)=(0.77,6.72)$ MeV; from $d\delta/dE$ maximum: $(1.92,19.1)$ MeV
- $^4$H $1^-$ state (second) as $S$-matrix pole: $(E_R,\Gamma_R)=(1.15,6.38)$ MeV; from $d\delta/dE$ maximum: $(2.25,14.6)$ MeV
- Neutron-$^3$H $s$-wave singlet scattering length: 5.32 fm (experimental: $4.98\pm0.29$ fm)
- Neutron-$^3$H $s$-wave triplet scattering length: 3.01 fm (experimental: $3.13\pm0.11$ fm)

## Context

This work extends the complex scaled hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method, previously applied to $^6$He, $^6$Be, $^6$Li, $^{11}$Li, $^{12}$C, and $^{17}$Ne by Garrido (2002, 2004, 2005) and Fedorov (2003), to the $^5$H system. The authors address the controversial experimental situation for $^5$H resonances, where Korsheninnikov (2001) found a $1/2^+$ ground state at $1.7\pm0.3$ MeV with width $1.9\pm0.4$ MeV, while other measurements range from 1.8 to 5.5 MeV. The neutron-$^3$H potential construction follows the approach of Descouvemont (2001) but uses the mean-field-consistent operator form of Garrido (2003), and the results agree with the microscopic three-cluster calculation of Aoyama (2003) which gave $E_R=1.59$ MeV and $\Gamma_R=2.48$ MeV for the $1/2^+$ state. The predicted energy distributions of fragments after decay of the resonances enable future experimental tests.
