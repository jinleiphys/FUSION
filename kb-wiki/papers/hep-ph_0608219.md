# Evidence for the Absence of Gluon Orbital Angular Momentum in the Nucleon

> The Sivers mechanism for the single-spin asymmetry in unpolarized lepton scattering from a transversely polarized nucleon is driven by the orbital angular momentum carried by its quark and gluon constituents, combined with QCD final-state interactions. Both quark and gluon mechanisms can generate such a single-spin asymmetry, though only the quark mechanism can explain the small single-spin asymmetry measured by the COMPASS collaboration on the deuteron, suggesting the gluon mechanism is small relative to the quark mechanism. We detail empirical studies through which the gluon and quark orbital angular momentum contributions, quark-flavor by quark-flavor, can be elucidated.

## Key claim

The small single-spin asymmetry measured by the COMPASS collaboration on a deuteron target, combined with the isospin structure of the quark and gluon mechanisms, suggests that the gluon orbital angular momentum contribution to the nucleon spin is small relative to the quark contribution, though the conclusion is specific to the light-cone gauge decomposition used in the paper.

## Method

The authors analyze the Sivers mechanism for single-spin asymmetries (SSAs) in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic lepton scattering (SIDIS) from transversely polarized nucleons, using the light-front wave function formalism in light-cone gauge. They compare the isospin structure of quark-mediated and gluon-mediated contributions to the Sivers function, relating these to the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon through matrix elements of spin-orbit operators. The analysis uses existing experimental data from HERMES on the proton and COMPASS on the deuteron to distinguish between the two mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- The proton anomalous magnetic moment is κ_p = 1.79 and the neutron anomalous magnetic moment is κ_n = -1.91 (in units of e/2M)
- The isoscalar magnetic moment of the nucleon, κ_S ≡ (κ_n + κ_p)/2 = -0.06, is numerically very small relative to |κ_{n,p}|
- From isospin symmetry analysis: a^p_d = a^n_u = -2.03 and a^p_u = a^n_d = 1.67 for the quark-flavor contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment
- The sum a^p_u + a^n_u = a^p_d + a^n_d = -0.360, predicting cancellation in deuteron SSAs
- The gluon mechanism is estimated to be smaller than the quark mechanism by a factor of approximately 0.2, based on comparing deuteron to proton SSA data and scaling the strong phase by a factor of 2.25

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework connecting the Sivers SSA to orbital angular momentum developed by Brodsky et al. (2002) and Burkardt (2002-2005), and provides an interpretation of the COMPASS collaboration (2005) deuteron data showing SSAs consistent with zero. The paper enables future experimental tests of gluon orbital angular momentum through measurements of SSAs in reactions producing hadrons with non-valence quark content, such as K^-K^+ or φ production from photon-gluon fusion, and complements contemporary work by Anselmino et al. (2006) using p↑p → π^0 X scattering.
