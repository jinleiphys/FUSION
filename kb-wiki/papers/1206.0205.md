# Inner crust of neutron stars with mass-fitted Skyrme functionals

> The equation of state and composition of the inner crust of neutron stars at zero temperature are calculated, using the T = 0 version of the TETFSI (temperature-dependent extended Thomas-Fermi plus Strutinsky integral) method, for each of a family of three functionals based on Skyrme-type forces BSk19, BSk20 and BSk21, which are characterized by different degrees of symmetry-energy stiffness, and also for the SLy4 functional. We also solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to calculate the distribution of mass within the inner crust. Qualitatively similar results are found for all four functionals, and in particular the number of protons per Wigner-Seitz cell is in all cases equal to 40 throughout the inner crust.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for all four Skyrme functionals studied (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21, and SLy4), the number of protons per Wigner-Seitz cell is exactly 40 throughout the inner crust at zero temperature, but the authors caution that this result is "somewhat fortuitous" and that pairing effects or slightly different functionals could shift the favored value.

## Method

The authors used the zero-temperature version of the TETFSI (temperature-dependent extended Thomas-Fermi plus Strutinsky integral) method to calculate the composition and equation of state of the inner crust of neutron stars, modeling the inhomogeneous medium by spherical Wigner-Seitz cells with parametrized neutron and proton density distributions. They minimized the internal energy per nucleon at constant mean density with respect to eight cell parameters (including proton number Z and neutron number N) using the CERN routine MINUIT, for over one hundred different densities between the neutron drip point and 0.1 fm^{-3}. They also solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to determine the mass distribution within the inner crust.

## Key numbers

- The number of protons per Wigner-Seitz cell is 40 for all four functionals at all densities up to 0.06 fm^{-3}
- The drip-point density for BSk19 is 2.63464 x 10^{-4} nucleons fm^{-3}, with internal energy per nucleon e = -1.79426 MeV and pressure P = 5.072 x 10^{-4} MeV fm^{-3}
- The drip-point density for BSk20 is 2.62873 x 10^{-4} nucleons fm^{-3}, with e = -1.79451 MeV and P = 5.064 x 10^{-4} MeV fm^{-3}
- The drip-point density for BSk21 is 2.57541 x 10^{-4} nucleons fm^{-3}, with e = -1.81718 MeV and P = 4.984 x 10^{-4} MeV fm^{-3}
- The drip-point density for SLy4 is 2.45897 x 10^{-4} nucleons fm^{-3}, with e = -1.78801 MeV and P = 4.744 x 10^{-4} MeV fm^{-3}
- The energy difference per nucleon between Z = 40 and Z = 50 for BSk19 is about 10 keV at the drip density and 5 keV at 0.06 fm^{-3}
- The inner-crust code underbinds relative to the outer-crust HFB code by around 5 percent at the drip point

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of neutron-star outer crust (Pearson et al., 2011) and homogeneous core (Goriely et al., 2010) using the same family of Skyrme functionals BSk19-21, which were fitted to 2149 measured nuclear masses with an rms deviation of 0.58 MeV. The TETFSI method avoids the spurious neutron shell effects that plague Hartree-Fock calculations in the Wigner-Seitz approximation (as discussed by Chamel, 2007 and Margueron et al., 2007) while providing a more realistic treatment of spatial inhomogeneities than the compressible liquid-drop model used by Douchin and Haensel (2001). The results enable unified calculations of neutron-star structure from crust to core with functionals that simultaneously reproduce nuclear masses and neutron-matter equations of state.
