# Axial Vector Coupling Constant in Chiral Colour Dielectric Model

> The axial vector coupling constants of the $β$ decay processes of neutron and hyperon are calculated in SU(3) chiral colour dielectric model (CCDM). Using these axial coupling constants of neutron and hyperon, in CCDM we calculate the integrals of the spin dependent structure functions for proton and neutron. Our result is similar to the results obtained by MIT bag and Cloudy bag models.

# Axial Vector Coupling Constant in Chiral Colour Dielectric Model

## Key claim

The SU(3) chiral colour dielectric model (CCDM) yields axial vector coupling constants for neutron and hyperon beta decay that are smaller than experimental values, and the resulting proton spin structure function integrals are very large compared to the EMC measurement, a shortcoming shared by all quark models considered.

## Method

The authors solve the quark and dielectric field equations in mean field approximation, construct bare baryon states of three quarks using Peierls-Yoccoz momentum projection, and then include pseudoscalar meson-quark interaction perturbatively to compute mesonic effects on baryon properties. The axial coupling constants for neutron beta decay (n → p e⁻ ν̄ₑ) and hyperon beta decay (Σ⁻ → n e⁻ ν̄ₑ) are calculated from the quark part of the axial current, with meson exchange contributions evaluated via intermediate baryon states. The resulting gA values are used in the Ellis-Jaffe sum rules to compute integrals of the spin-dependent structure functions for proton and neutron.

## Key numbers

- gA for neutron beta decay (gA_np): ranges from 0.908 (Set C) to 1.180 (Set D), compared to experimental value 1.254 ± 0.006
- gA for Σ⁻ beta decay (gA_Σn): ranges from 0.185 (Set C) to 0.237 (Set D), compared to experimental value 0.340
- Proton spin structure function integral ∫g₁ᵖ(x)dx: ranges from 0.150 (Set C) to 0.197 (Set D), compared to experimental value 0.114 ± 0.012 ± 0.026
- Neutron spin structure function integral ∫g₁ⁿ(x)dx: ranges from -0.00095 (Set C) to -0.00018 (Set D), compared to experimental value -0.077 ± 0.012 ± 0.026
- F/D ratio: approximately 0.67 across all parameter sets, compared to 0.575 ± 0.016 from Close and Roberts and 0.49 ± 0.08 from Ehrnsperger et al.
- Bare nucleon probability: 0.83 for parameter Set A; bare sigma probability: 0.87 for Set A
- Meson cloud reduces gA for neutron by 17% and for Σ by 13% (Set A)

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) chiral colour dielectric model previously developed by Sahu to calculate axial coupling constants and spin structure functions, following the approach of Thomas et al. for the Cloudy bag model. The results are compared with the MIT bag model, Cloudy bag model (CBM), and non-relativistic quark model (NRQM), showing that all these quark models overestimate the proton spin structure function relative to the EMC experiment. The calculation enables further study of how meson cloud effects and strange quark polarization contribute to nucleon spin structure, though the CCDM gives only a small negative neutron spin structure function compared to experimental data.
