# Moments of the longitudinal proton structure function F_L from global data in the Q^2 range 0.75-45.0 (GeV/c)^2

> We present an extraction of the lowest three moments of the proton longitudinal structure function F_L from world data between Q^2 = 0.75 and 45 (GeV/c)^2. The availability of new F_L data at low Bjorken x from HERA and at large x from Jefferson Lab allows the first determination of these moments over a large Q^2 range, relatively free from uncertainties associated with extrapolations into unmeasured regions. The moments are found to be underestimated by leading twist structure function parameterizations, especially for the higher moments, suggesting either the presence of significant higher twist effects in F_L and/or a larger gluon distribution at high x.

## Key claim

The lowest three moments of the proton longitudinal structure function \(F_L\) are extracted from world data over \(Q^2 = 0.75\) to 45 (GeV/c)\(^2\) and are found to be underestimated by leading twist parton distribution function parameterizations, especially for the higher moments; this suggests either significant higher twist effects in \(F_L\) or a larger gluon distribution at high \(x\) than currently assumed.

## Method

The authors performed a global analysis of proton \(F_L\) data from dedicated longitudinal-transverse separations at CERN (EMC, BCDMS, NMC), SLAC (E140X, SLAC global), DESY (H1), and Jefferson Lab (E94110, E99118). Data were binned in \(Q^2\) and bin-centered to central values using phenomenological fits; gaps in \(x\) coverage were filled with renormalized fits from two different models for the resonance and deep-inelastic regions. The longitudinal Nachtmann moments \(M_L^{(n)}\) for \(n=2,4,6\) were computed by integrating the Nachtmann-weighted combination of \(F_L\) and \(F_2\) data, and uncertainties were estimated via a Monte Carlo procedure that generated 1000 pseudo-data sets per \(Q^2\) bin, with systematic errors evaluated by repeating the gap-filling with three alternative model combinations.

## Key numbers

- The extracted \(n=2\) longitudinal Nachtmann moment \(M_L^{(2)} \times 10^{-3}\) (inelastic plus elastic) ranges from \(19.7 \pm 3.3\) at \(Q^2 = 0.75\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) to \(5.4 \pm 11.0\) at \(Q^2 = 45.0\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- The \(n=4\) moment \(M_L^{(4)} \times 10^{-3}\) ranges from \(1.2 \pm 0.4\) at \(Q^2 = 0.75\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) to \(0.2 \pm 0.3\) at \(Q^2 = 45.0\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- The \(n=6\) moment \(M_L^{(6)} \times 10^{-3}\) ranges from \(0.18 \pm 0.08\) at \(Q^2 = 0.75\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) to \(0.0 \pm 0.05\) at \(Q^2 = 45.0\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- The elastic contribution is significant only for the four lowest \(Q^2\) bins (0.75, 1.75, 2.5, 3.75 (GeV/c)\(^2\)) and decreases rapidly for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- The inelastic moments show a turnover at \(Q^2 \approx 2-3\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) due to the pion threshold \(x_{\pi}\) reducing the integration limit.

## Context

This work extends previous moment analyses that relied on model-dependent estimates of the longitudinal-to-transverse structure function ratio (Ricco 1998) by using new \(F_L\) data from H1 at HERA (H1 Collaboration 2010) and from Jefferson Lab Hall C (Liang 2004) to perform the first direct extraction of \(F_L\) moments over a large \(Q^2\) range. The comparison with leading twist calculations from global PDF fits (MSTW08 by Martin 2009, ABKM09 by Alekhin 2009, and CJ by Accardi 2011) reveals discrepancies that motivate either an increased high-\(x\) gluon distribution or the inclusion of higher twist effects; the results enable future global fits to directly constrain the gluon PDF using \(F_L\) data, particularly the Jefferson Lab data at low \(Q^2 < 4\) GeV\(^2\).
