# Clustering structure effect on Hanbury-Brown-Twiss correlation in $^{12}$C + $^{197}$Au collisions at 200 GeV}

> Through $^{12}$C + $^{197}$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 200 GeV using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model, the azimuthal angle dependences of the Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii relative to the second- and third-order participant plane from $π$-$π$ correlations are discussed. Three initial geometric configurations of $^{12}$C, namely three-$α$-cluster triangle, three-$α$-cluster chain and Woods-Saxon distribution of nucleons, are taken into account, and their effects on the correlations are investigated. The ratio of the third- to the second-order HBT radii $R_{o(s),3}^2/R_{o(s),2}^2$ is shown to be a clear probe for three configurations. In addition, this work presents the hadronic rescattering time evolution of the azimuthally dependent HBT radii. From the present study, one can learn that the HBT correlation from identical particles at freeze-out is able to provide the information of different initial configurations as collective flow proposed before.

## Key claim

The ratio of the third- to the second-order HBT radii \(R_{o(s),3}^2/R_{o(s),2}^2\) from \(\pi\)-\(\pi\) correlations can distinguish between three-\(\alpha\)-cluster triangle, three-\(\alpha\)-cluster chain, and Woods-Saxon nucleon distributions in \(^{12}\)C, but this discrimination is clearer and more stable at high transverse momentum (0.8–1.2 GeV/\(c\)) than at low \(K_T\) (0.3–0.5 GeV/\(c\)).

## Method

The authors simulated \(^{12}\)C + \(^{197}\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV using the string melting version of the AMPT model. Three initial geometric configurations of \(^{12}\)C were implemented in the HIJING initialization: a three-\(\alpha\)-cluster equilateral triangle, a three-\(\alpha\)-cluster linear chain, and a spherical Woods-Saxon nucleon distribution. The azimuthal angle dependence of the HBT radii \(R_s^2\) and \(R_o^2\) relative to the second- and third-order participant planes was extracted from \(\pi\)-\(\pi\) correlations for two \(K_T\) bins and three hadronic rescattering times (0, 7, and 30 fm/\(c\)).

## Key numbers

*   RMS radius of \(^{12}\)C: \(\langle R(\text{WS}) \rangle \approx 2.46\) fm for Woods-Saxon, \(\langle R(\text{Chain}) \rangle \approx 2.47\) fm, \(\langle R(\text{Triangle}) \rangle \approx 2.47\) fm, all consistent with the experimental value of 2.47 fm (Angeli 2013).
*   For the chain configuration with \(K_T\) 0.3–0.5 GeV/\(c\) and \(t_{hs}=0\) fm/\(c\): \(R_{s,2}^2 = 0.605\) (fm\(^2\)), \(R_{o,2}^2 = -0.973\) (fm\(^2\)); for the triangle configuration: \(R_{s,2}^2 = 0.280\) (fm\(^2\)), \(R_{o,2}^2 = -0.417\) (fm\(^2\)); for Woods-Saxon: \(R_{s,2}^2 = 0.260\) (fm\(^2\)), \(R_{o,2}^2 = -0.418\) (fm\(^2\)).
*   For the triangle configuration with \(K_T\) 0.8–1.2 GeV/\(c\) and \(t_{hs}=0\) fm/\(c\): \(R_{o,3}^2 = -0.211\) (fm\(^2\)); for chain: \(R_{o,3}^2 = -0.114\) (fm\(^2\)); for Woods-Saxon: \(R_{o,3}^2 = -0.123\) (fm\(^2\)).
*   Ratio \(R_{o,3}^2/R_{o,2}^2\) for high \(K_T\): Woods-Saxon around 0.5; chain no more than 0.2; triangle always larger than 0.6 and up to 1.
*   Initial eccentricities: \(\epsilon_2^i = 0.499\) (chain), 0.227 (triangle), 0.263 (Woods-Saxon); \(\epsilon_3^i = 0.179\) (chain), 0.296 (triangle), 0.219 (Woods-Saxon).

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Broniowski et al. (2014) and Bozek et al. (2014) that collective flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can probe \(\alpha\)-clustering in light nuclei, and builds on the authors' own prior studies of elliptic and triangular flow in the same system (Zhang et al. 2017, Zhang et al. 2018). It demonstrates that azimuthally differential HBT femtoscopy, previously applied to Au+Au collisions by STAR (Adams et al. 2004, 2003), PHENIX (Adare et al. 2015), and ALICE (Acharya et al. 2018), can serve as an additional observable to distinguish nuclear clustering configurations. The results suggest that system-scan experiments around \(^{12}\)C could use both HBT correlations and collective flow to test for \(\alpha\)-cluster structures.
