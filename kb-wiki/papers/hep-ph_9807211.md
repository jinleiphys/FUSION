# Challenges of Nuclear Shadowing in DIS

> Nuclear shadowing in DIS at moderately small $x$ is suppressed by the nuclear formfactor and depends on the effective mass of a hadronic fluctuation of the virtual photon. We propose a solution to the problems (i) of how to combine a definite transverse size of the fluctuation with a definite effective mass, and (ii) of how to include the nuclear formfactor in the higher multiple scattering terms. Comparison of the numerical results with known approximations shows a substantial difference.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a solution to two problems in describing nuclear shadowing in deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) at moderately small Bjorken x: (i) how to combine a definite transverse size of a hadronic fluctuation of the virtual photon with a definite effective mass, and (ii) how to include the nuclear formfactor in higher multiple scattering terms; the authors find that numerical results from their correct quantum-mechanical treatment differ substantially from known approximations.

## Method

The authors develop a path integral (Green function) approach for the propagation of a quark-antiquark pair through the nuclear medium, solving a Schrodinger-like equation with an imaginary optical potential that accounts for absorption. They restrict the calculation to valence quark-antiquark fluctuations of the virtual photon, neglecting higher Fock components containing gluons and sea quarks, and use a dipole form for the color dipole cross section sigma(r) = C r^2 with C approximately 3. The Green function is solved analytically as a harmonic oscillator Green function with complex frequency, and nuclear shadowing is computed for calcium and lead nuclei using a uniform nuclear density.

* The double scattering approximation with proper formfactor treatment (thin solid curve) does not show saturation even at x = 0.001.
* The full calculation including higher order rescattering (thick solid curve) shows substantial deviation from the standard approach, especially for lead, and saturates at very low x at the level given by the eikonal expression (Eq. 1.2).
* Quark masses are fixed at m_q = 0.3 GeV, m_s = 0.45 GeV, and m_c = 1.5 GeV for perturbative calculation of the proton structure function F_2.
* A uniform nuclear density rho_A = 0.16 fm^{-3} is used for all nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the color dipole approach to nuclear shadowing introduced by Kopeliovich (1990) and others, addressing the specific problems of how to treat the nuclear formfactor in higher multiple scattering terms and how to define the effective mass of a quark-antiquark fluctuation with definite transverse size. The authors compare their full calculation to the standard approach of Kopeliovich (1990) and to the hadronic representation of Karmanov and Kondratyuk (1973), finding substantial differences. The path integral technique developed here can be applied to include higher Fock components containing gluons and to incorporate the x-dependence of the dipole cross section, enabling more realistic calculations for comparison with nuclear shadowing data.
