# Identifying Inconsistencies in Fission Product Yield Evaluations with Prompt Neutron Emission

> We present a self-consistency analysis of fission product yield evaluations. Anomalous yields are determined using a series of simple conservation checks and comparing charge distributions with common parameterizations. The total prompt neutron multiplicity as a function of product mass $\barν_T(A)$ is derived directly from the independent fission product yields using average charge conservation. This method is checked against Monte Carlo simulations of the de-excitation of the fission fragments in a Hauser-Feshbach statistical decay framework. The derived $\barν_T(A)$ is compared with experimental data, when available, and used to compare the prompt neutron multiplicity $\barν$ for the various evaluations. Differences in $\barν$ for each evaluation are investigated and possible sources are identified. We also identify fission reactions that are inconsistent with prompt neutron data and propose possible solutions to remedy the observed inconsistencies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that independent fission product yields (IFY) contain sufficient information to derive prompt neutron multiplicity via average charge conservation, but many evaluated yield libraries show fundamental inconsistencies with experimental prompt neutron data. The author notes that while basic conservation checks are satisfied, the more precise derivation reveals that only about half of the listed fission reactions show good agreement with experimental values in at least one evaluation library.

## Method

The author applies average charge conservation to independent fission product yields, fitting charge distributions with Gaussian parameterizations following Wahl (2002) to determine the average charge ⟨Z⟩ as a function of product mass A. This ⟨Z⟩(A) is used to infer complementary mass pairs and derive the total prompt neutron multiplicity ν̄_T(A) between each product and its complement. The method is validated against Monte Carlo Hauser-Feshbach statistical decay calculations using the CGMF code for ²³⁵U(n_th,f), ²³⁹Pu(n_th,f), and ²⁵²Cf(sf), showing reconstruction of ν̄ within 1% of the true value.

- Derived ν̄ for ²⁵²Cf(sf): 3.67 ± 0.11 (ENDF-B/VII.1), 3.66 ± 0.12 (JENDL-4.0u2), 4.64 ± 0.09 (JEFF-3.1.1), compared to experimental 3.77 ± 0.004
- Derived ν̄ for ²³⁹Pu(n_th,f): 2.83 ± 0.08 (ENDF-B/VII.1), 2.82 ± 0.08 (JENDL-4.0u2), 2.97 ± 0.05 (JEFF-3.1.1), compared to experimental 2.88 ± 0.006
- The ⟨Z⟩(A) for ²⁵²Cf(sf) is on average ∼0.4% larger in JEFF-3.1.1 than ENDF-B/VII.1
- For ²³³U(n_th,f), artificially increasing ⟨Z⟩(A) by 0.28% (JEFF-3.1.1) or 0.65% (ENDF-B/VII.1) for A_h ∈ [129,134] brings ν̄ to ∼2.49, consistent with experimental 2.49 ± 0.004
- JEFF-3.1.1 evaluation of ²³⁵U(n,f) yields ν̄ = [2.40 ± 0.05, 2.46 ± 0.04, 4.33 ± 0.08] for thermal, intermediate, and fast incident energies, agreeing with experimental [2.407 ± 0.0066, 2.461 ± 0.0028, 4.387 ± 0.0081]

## Context

This work extends consistency checks of fission yield evaluations beyond basic mass and charge conservation, which the author notes typically have errors larger than one neutron per fission. The analysis builds on the Wahl (2002) systematics for charge distributions and uses the CGMF Hauser-Feshbach code (Becker 2013) for validation. The results identify specific nuclides with anomalous yields that could be remeasured in future experiments such as those by Meierbachtol (2015) and Martin (2015), and support the need for new evaluated fission product yields as identified by the nuclear data community (Koning 2009) and demonstrated by Kawano (2013).
