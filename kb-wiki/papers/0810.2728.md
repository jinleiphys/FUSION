# Shell Model in the Complex Energy Plane

> This work reviews foundations and applications of the complex-energy continuum shell model that provides a consistent many-body description of bound states, resonances, and scattering states. The model can be considered a quasi-stationary open quantum system extension of the standard configuration interaction approach for well-bound (closed) systems.

## Key claim

This paper reviews the foundations and applications of the complex-energy continuum shell model (Gamow Shell Model, GSM), which provides a consistent many-body description of bound states, resonances, and scattering states by treating all states on the same footing through the Berggren completeness relation, rather than relying on an artificial separation of Hilbert space into bound/resonant and non-resonant scattering parts as in alternative approaches.

## Method

The authors present the mathematical framework of the GSM based on the Berggren ensemble, which replaces the real-energy scattering states in the standard completeness relation with a sum over bound and resonant poles plus a contour integral over complex-energy scattering states in the complex momentum plane. The paper discusses the regularization of divergent radial integrals through external complex scaling, the generalization of the Berggren representation to include antibound states, and the extension to proton potentials with Coulomb tails. The review covers the one-body Fock space formulation and the many-body GSM, with selected applications to drip-line nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The complex energy of a Gamow state is given by \(\tilde{E}_n = E_n - i \Gamma_n/2\), where \(\Gamma_n\) is the decay width related to the half-life by \(T_{1/2} = \hbar \ln 2 / \Gamma_n\).
- For neutron \(l=0\) poles in a square well potential with radius \(R=7\) fm, below \(V_0=1.05\) MeV there appears only one antibound state; at about \(V_0=9.5\) MeV a \(2s\) bound state emerges; at about \(V_0=26\) MeV three antibound and two bound states are present.
- The regime of particularly strong many-body correlations involving weakly bound and unbound neutrons is indicated for \(|S_n| < 2\) MeV, where the neutron chemical potential has the same magnitude as the neutron pairing gap.
- The astrophysical r-process is expected to proceed in the region of low separation energies around 2-4 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the standard configuration interaction approach for well-bound (closed) systems to open quantum systems by placing the shell model in the complex energy plane. The work contrasts with real-energy continuum shell model approaches such as the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) of Ref. [SMEC, SMEC_2p], which rely on a projected subspace separation of Hilbert space, and with the phenomenological continuum shell model of Ref. [CSM_Volya]. The GSM framework enables unified treatment of bound, resonance, and scattering states in weakly bound and unbound nuclei near the drip lines, and is mathematically grounded in the Rigged Hilbert Space formulation of quantum mechanics (Gel'fand 1961, Maurin 1968).
