# Positronium-hydrogen-atom scattering in a five-state model

> The scattering of ortho-positronium (Ps) by hydrogen atoms has been investigated in a five-state coupled-channel model allowing for Ps(1s)H(2s,2p) and Ps(2s,2p)H(1s) excitations using a recently proposed electron-exchange model potential. The higher ($n\ge 3$) excitations and ionization of the Ps atom are calculated using the first Born approximation. Calculations are reported of scattering lengths, phase shifts, elastic, Ps- and H-excitation, and total cross sections. Remarkable correlations are observed between the S-wave Ps-H binding energy and the singlet scattering length, effective range, and resonance energy obtained in various model calculations. These correlations suggest that if a Ps-H dynamical model yields the correct result for one of these four observables, it is expected to lead to the correct result for the other three. The present model, which is constructed so as to reproduce the Ps-H resonance at 4.01 eV, automatically yields a Ps-H bound state at -1.05 eV which compares well with the accurate value of -1.067 eV. The model leads to a singlet scattering length of 3.72$a_0$ and effective range of 1.67$a_0$, whereas the correlations suggest the precise values of $3.50a_0$ and 1.65$a_0$ for these observables, respectively.

## Key claim

The authors construct a five-state coupled-channel model for ortho-positronium (Ps) scattering by hydrogen atoms, tuning a free parameter in their model exchange potential to reproduce the known S-wave singlet Ps-H resonance at 4.01 eV, and find that this same model then automatically yields a Ps-H bound state at -1.05 eV, close to the accurate value of -1.067 eV, without further adjustment.

## Method

The authors solve coupled Lippmann-Schwinger scattering equations in momentum space for a five-state basis including Ps(1s)H(1s), Ps(2s)H(1s), Ps(2p)H(1s), Ps(1s)H(2s), and Ps(1s)H(2p). They use a non-local model exchange potential with a time-reversal symmetric form, and treat higher Ps excitations (n >= 3) and Ps ionization in the first Born approximation with exchange. The free parameter C in the exchange potential denominator is set to 0.784 to match the known resonance energy.

## Key numbers

- Singlet scattering length: 3.72 a0 from the five-state model; correlations suggest the precise value should be 3.50 a0
- Singlet effective range: 1.67 a0 from the five-state model; correlations suggest the precise value should be 1.65 a0
- Ps-H bound state energy: -1.05 eV from the five-state model, compared to the accurate value of -1.067 eV from Frolov and Smith
- S-wave singlet resonance energy: 4.01 eV (width 0.075 eV) from Ho, used as the tuning target
- Triplet scattering length: 1.68 a0 from the five-state model
- Elastic cross section at k=0.0: 22.36 pi a0^2 for the five-state model
- Elastic cross section at k=0.1: 21.18 pi a0^2 for the five-state model

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-state model calculations for Ps-H, Ps-He, and Ps-H2 scattering (Biswas and Adhikari, 1998) by including hydrogen excitation channels. It contradicts the 22-pseudo-state R-matrix calculations of Campbell et al. (1998), which do not reproduce the experimental trend of a broad maximum near 20 eV and a minimum near the Ps(2s) threshold. The observed correlations between binding energy, scattering length, effective range, and resonance energy mirror similar correlations found in the trinucleon system (Adhikari, Frederico, and Goldman, 1995; Phillips, 1968), and enable predictions of converged low-energy observables from models that correctly reproduce the Ps-H bound state.
