# Medium-mass nuclei from chiral nucleon-nucleon interactions

> We compute the binding energies, radii, and densities for selected medium-mass nuclei within coupled-cluster theory and employ the "bare" chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at order N3LO. We find rather well-converged results in model spaces consisting of 15 oscillator shells, and the doubly magic nuclei 40Ca, 48Ca, and the exotic 48Ni are underbound by about 1 MeV per nucleon within the CCSD approximation. The binding-energy difference between the mirror nuclei 48Ca and 48Ni is close to theoretical mass table evaluations. Our computation of the one-body density matrices and the corresponding natural orbitals and occupation numbers provides a first step to a microscopic foundation of the nuclear shell model.

## Key claim
Using the "bare" chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at order N3LO within the CCSD approximation, medium-mass nuclei are underbound by about 1 MeV per nucleon, with the binding-energy difference between the mirror nuclei 48Ca and 48Ni agreeing well with theoretical mass table evaluations; the authors note that the missing binding is mainly due to omitted three-nucleon forces and the missing triples correction.

## Method
The authors employed spherical coupled-cluster theory at the CCSD (coupled-cluster singles and doubles) level, using a single-particle basis of spherical harmonic oscillator wave functions with up to 15 oscillator shells (N=14). The Hamiltonian was first transformed to a spherical Hartree-Fock basis, then the CCSD equations were solved to compute binding energies, charge radii, and one-body density matrices for the doubly magic nuclei 4He, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, and 48Ni.

## Key numbers
- 4He: CCSD binding energy per nucleon E/A = -5.99 MeV; CCSD-T1 triples correction adds 1.3 MeV of binding; experimental value is -28.3 MeV total.
- 16O: CCSD binding energy per nucleon E/A = -6.72 MeV; convergence within about 100 keV over model space variations.
- 40Ca: CCSD binding energy per nucleon E/A = -7.72 MeV; missing about 10% of experimental binding energy of -342 MeV; increasing model space from N=13 to N=14 yields additional 0.9 MeV.
- 48Ca: CCSD binding energy per nucleon E/A = -7.40 MeV; charge radius R = 3.22 fm (experimental R_exp = 3.47 fm).
- 48Ni: CCSD binding energy per nucleon E/A = -6.02 MeV; binding-energy difference from 48Ca is 1.38 MeV per nucleon (theoretical mass table evaluations give 1.43 MeV per nucleon).
- 40Ca: binding-energy deviation from experiment ΔE/A = 0.84 MeV; charge radius R = 3.24 fm (experimental R_exp = 3.48 fm).

## Context
This work extends ab-initio coupled-cluster calculations from light nuclei (up to mass 13, as in Epelbaum (2002), Caurier (2004), Nogga (2006), and Navratil (2007)) to medium-mass nuclei using a "bare" chiral NN interaction, avoiding secondary renormalization procedures that generate additional many-body forces. It contrasts with the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo calculation of 40Ca by Gandolfi (2007) which used a potential lacking spin-orbit interaction, and with the importance truncated no-core shell model calculation by Roth and Navratil (2007) which was criticized for convergence issues and lack of size extensivity. The results provide a first microscopic foundation for the nuclear shell model and enable future studies of chiral interactions in even heavier nuclei, as well as systematic inclusion of three-nucleon forces.
