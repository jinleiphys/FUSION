# Competition between alpha-decay and beta-decay for Heavy and Superheavy Nuclei

> In this work, the $β$-stable region for Z $\geq$ 90 is proposed. The calculated $β$-stable nuclei in the $β$-stable region are in good agreement with the ones obtained by Möller \emph{et al}.. The half-lives of the nuclei close to the $β$-stable region are calculated and the competition between $α$-decay and $β$-decay is systematically investigated. The calculated half-lives and the suggested decay modes are well in line with the experimental results. The predictions for half-lives and decay modes of the nuclei with Z = 107$-$110 are presented.

## Key claim

The authors propose a defined beta-stable region for nuclei with Z ≥ 90, rather than a single beta-stable line, and claim that their calculated beta-stable nuclei are in good agreement with those obtained by Möller et al., though the comparison shows that Möller's results are not continuous in isotopic chains for odd Z and the range by Möller et al. is slightly larger than their calculated results.

## Method

The authors used a binding energy formula from Dong and Ren to calculate beta-decay energies for nuclei with Z = 90 to 126, setting the beta-minus and beta-plus decay energies to zero to find the limits of each decay mode for each isotopic chain. They then calculated alpha-decay half-lives using the unified formula of Ni et al. and beta-decay half-lives using the formulas of Zhang et al., comparing the half-lives to determine the dominant decay mode based on the ratio of alpha to beta half-lives.

## Key numbers

- The beta-stable region for Z = 90 includes mass numbers A = 224-230; for Z = 110 includes A = 282-289; for Z = 126 includes A = 332-340
- For Z = 90, the calculated alpha half-life of 218Th is 9.14×10⁻⁸ s, compared to the experimental value of 1.09×10⁻⁷ s (C = -0.08)
- For Z = 92, the calculated alpha half-life of 236U is 3.37×10¹⁵ s, but the experimental decay mode is alpha=100% with half-life 7.39×10¹⁴ s (one of six nuclei where predicted decay mode disagrees)
- For Z = 107, the calculated alpha half-life of 264Bh is 0.16 s with predicted alpha=94%, beta+=6%, compared to experimental alpha half-life of 0.9 s (D = 0.18)
- For Z = 108, the calculated alpha half-life of 266Hs is 2.72×10⁻³ s with predicted alpha=100%, compared to experimental alpha half-life of 2.3×10⁻³ s (D = 1.18)
- For Z = 110, the calculated alpha half-life of 272Ds is 1.02×10⁻⁴ s with predicted alpha=100%, compared to experimental alpha half-life of 1.7×10⁻⁴ s

## Context

This work extends the study of beta stability from the well-known region of Z < 83 into heavy and superheavy nuclei with Z ≥ 90, where most nuclei undergo both alpha and beta decay. The calculated beta-stable region is compared with the results of Möller et al., and the half-life predictions for Z = 107-110 are intended to guide future experiments on superheavy nuclei synthesis, particularly the efforts by Chinese physicists around Z = 110 as reported by Gan et al.
