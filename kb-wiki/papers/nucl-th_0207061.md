# On the Surface Structure of Strange Superheavy Nuclei

> Bound, strange, neutral superheavy nuclei, stable against strong decay, may exist. A model effective field theory calculation of the surface energy and density of such systems is carried out assuming vector meson couplings to conserved currents and scalar couplings fit to data where it exists. The non-linear relativistic mean field equations are solved assuming local baryon sources. The approach is calibrated through a successful calculation of the known nuclear surface tension.

## Key claim

Bound, neutral superheavy nuclei composed of protons, neutrons, and cascade hyperons (Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻) with strangeness-to-baryon ratio |S|/B = 1 may be stable against strong decay, but the result depends on the uncertain scalar coupling of the cascade hyperon in nuclear matter, which is varied across a range of values from -14 to -40 MeV binding energy.

## Method

The author constructs an effective Lagrangian density within quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) using hadronic degrees of freedom, with vector mesons coupled to conserved baryon currents and scalar mesons coupled to baryon scalar densities. The nonlinear relativistic mean field equations are solved using a shooting method with local density approximation for baryon sources, treating the meson fields as classical Kohn-Sham potentials within density functional theory. The approach is calibrated by calculating the surface tension of ordinary N=Z nuclei and fitting the semiempirical mass formula (SEMF) to extract bulk and surface energy coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per baryon for infinite nuclear matter (N=Z): -15.768 MeV (NLC parameter set), -16.099 MeV (Q1 parameter set), -15.758 MeV (L2 parameter set)
- Equilibrium Fermi momentum for infinite nuclear matter: 1.301 fm⁻¹ (NLC and L2), 1.299 fm⁻¹ (Q1)
- Effective mass ratio M*/M for infinite nuclear matter: 0.6313 (NLC), 0.5975 (Q1), 0.5409 (L2)
- Binding energy of a single Λ in nuclear matter: -28 MeV (used to fit g_sΛ/g_s)
- Binding energy of a single Ξ in nuclear matter: -68.9 MeV (g_sΞ/g_s = 1), -51.6 MeV (g_sΞ/g_s = 0.95), -34.3 MeV (g_sΞ/g_s = 0.9) for NLC parameter set
- Binding energy per baryon for infinite cascade-nucleon matter with g_sΞ/g_s = 1: -41 MeV (NLC parameter set)
- Equilibrium Fermi momentum for infinite cascade-nucleon matter with g_sΞ/g_s = 1: 1.363 fm⁻¹ (NLC parameter set)
- Effective mass ratio M*/M for infinite cascade-nucleon matter with g_sΞ/g_s = 1: 0.1391 (NLC parameter set)

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of strange matter by Gal et al. (1993, 2000) who studied bulk matter composed of n, p, and Λ hyperons using relativistic mean field theory and found binding energies per baryon as low as -25 MeV. It also responds to the more pessimistic results of Stoks and Lee (1999) who used baryon-baryon potential models from SU(3) extensions of Nijmegen soft-core potentials and found only slight binding (E/B ~ -3 MeV or less). The calculation enables future exploration of whether such strange superheavy nuclei could be detected through their weak decay signatures, and provides a framework for investigating the maximum allowable coupling of a Φ meson coupled to the conserved strangeness current.
