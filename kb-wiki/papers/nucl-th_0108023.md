# Faddeev Approach to the Study of One- and Two-hole Spectral Functions

> Theoretical calculations of one- and two-hole spectral functions for the O16 nucleus are still failing to describe some of the important features observed experimentally. Of critical importance for the solution of these issues is to obtain an appropriate description of the interplay between hole-hole and particle-hole excitations. A formalism is reviewed here that allows the consistent treatment of such phonons at a Random Phase approximation level. Although the application of this formalism to O16 is still in the implementation stage, some preliminary results are discussed here.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a consistent treatment of both particle-hole (ph) and hole-hole (hh) collective excitations at the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) level is necessary to resolve discrepancies between theoretical calculations and experimental data for one- and two-hole spectral functions in O16, but the application of the proposed Faddeev formalism to O16 is still in the implementation stage with only preliminary results from a simplified model.

## Method

The authors review a formalism based on self-consistent Green's function theory (SCGF) in which the irreducible self-energy is computed from a 2p1h/2h1p propagator that is expanded using Faddeev equations. The Faddeev vertices incorporate collective excitations in the particle-particle (pp) and particle-hole (ph) channels evaluated at the Dressed RPA (DRPA) level, using dressed single-particle propagators. The formalism is tested on a simplified "pairing-plus-quadrupole" model for O16, using a model space of harmonic oscillator wave functions up to four major closed shells plus a g9/2 state, with single-particle energies taken from a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation based on the Bonn-C potential.

## Key numbers

* Experimental spectroscopic strength for knockout of a proton from both the p1/2 and p3/2 shells in O16 corresponds to about 60% of the shell-model prediction.
* Variational calculations focused on short-range correlations yield about 10% strength removal before center-of-mass corrections, which raise the spectroscopic factor by about 7%, worsening agreement with data.
* Green's function calculations including 2h1p states (Ref. GeurtsO16) obtained a reduction of about 25%, still neglecting center-of-mass corrections, suggesting about 20% of the observed single-particle strength removal remains unexplained.
* The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength used in the model is g_Q = -1.14 MeV/fm^4 and the pairing strength is g_P = +15.0 MeV.
* The oscillator parameter used for quadrupole matrix elements is b = 1.76 fm.
* Single-particle energies range from -35.0 MeV (1s1/2) to 30.0 MeV (1g9/2).

## Context

This work extends the calculations of GeurtsO16, which included 2h1p states at the Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) level and achieved a 25% reduction in spectroscopic strength, but failed to describe the fragmentation of the p3/2 peak into multiple fragments and could generate only one 2+ excited state in C14 instead of the experimentally observed doublet at 7.0 and 8.32 MeV. The proposed Faddeev formalism aims to include RPA-level phonons (necessary to describe the isoscalar negative parity states observed in O16) while avoiding the double-counting and spurious pole problems that arise from naive summation of pp and ph phonon diagrams. The formalism enables future self-consistent calculations where one- and two-hole spectral functions are generated together, and can be extended to include short-range correlations via G-matrix interactions as done in geurts2h and geueepp.
