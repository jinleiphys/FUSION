# Magicity and occurrence of band with enhanced B(E2) in neutron-rich nuclei 68Ni and 90Zr

> Experimental energy spectrum and B(E2) values in 68Ni and 90Zr indicate a double-magic character in these neutron-rich nuclei with N or Z=40. The data nevertheless do not show any pronounced irregularity in two-nucleon separation energy. To understand the underlying physics, we carry out both shell-model and mean-field calculations. The shell-model calculation can well reproduce all the observations. It is understood from the mean-field results for 68Ni that the shell gap at N=40 disappears due to dynamical correlations of the isovector J=0 pairing interaction. In 90Zr, however, such a dynamic process with the J=0 pairing appears not important because of the strong contribution of the J>0 interaction. We study also level schemes in the Ni isotopes and N=50 isotones. We predict a new band built on the $0_{2}^{+}$ state in both 68Ni and 90Zr. The states of this band are dominated by two-particle-two-hole excitations from the fp-shell to the intruder g_{9/2} orbit.

# Magicity and occurrence of band with enhanced B(E2) in neutron-rich nuclei 68Ni and 90Zr

## Key claim

The authors argue that the double-magic character of 68Ni and 90Zr, suggested by large 2₁⁺ excitation energies and small B(E2,0₁⁺→2₁⁺) values, is not contradicted by the absence of irregularities in two-nucleon separation energies; in 68Ni the shell gap at N=40 is washed out by dynamical correlations from the isovector J=0 pairing interaction, while in 90Zr the J>0 interactions dominate and the J=0 pairing is not important for the smooth separation-energy behavior.

## Method

Spherical shell-model calculations were performed for Ni isotopes (using a restricted fpg-shell model space with ⁵⁶Ni as a core and the VMS effective interaction based on the Bonn-C NN potential) and for N=50 isotones (using both the VMS interaction and the JW interaction with ⁷⁸Ni as a core). Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) mean-field calculations were also carried out using the same shell-model Hamiltonians to analyze the role of the T=1, J=0 pairing interaction versus J>0 interactions in the separation energies and shell gaps.

## Key numbers

- B(E2,0₁⁺→2₁⁺) in ⁶⁸Ni: experimental value 53(12) e²fm⁴, calculated value 52 e²fm⁴ (using VMS interaction with neutron effective charge eₙ=1.0)
- B(E2,0₁⁺→2₁⁺) in ⁶⁶Ni: experimental value 120(20) e²fm⁴, calculated value 125 e²fm⁴
- B(E2,4₁⁺→2₁⁺) in ⁶⁸Ni: calculated 239 e²fm⁴ (enhanced compared to 56 e²fm⁴ in ⁶⁶Ni)
- B(E2,6₁⁺→4₁⁺) in ⁶⁸Ni: calculated 144 e²fm⁴ (compared to 13 e²fm⁴ in ⁶⁶Ni)
- B(E2,0₂⁺→2₁⁺) in ⁶⁸Ni: calculated 198 e²fm⁴ (compared to 11 e²fm⁴ in ⁶⁶Ni)
- HF single-particle energy gap between g₉/₂ and fp-shell in Ni isotopes: varies from 4 MeV at N=28 to 2.5 MeV at N=40
- Proton effective charge for N=50 isotones: eₚ=1.8 for VMS interaction, eₚ=2.0 for JW interaction

## Context

This work extends the discussion of shell quenching at N=40 in neutron-rich nuclei, following experimental findings by Broda (1995), Grzywacz (1998), Ishii (2000), and Sorlin (2002) that ⁶⁸Ni shows double-magic-like features. The authors contrast their conclusion, that T=1, J=0 pairing correlations erase the shell gap signature in separation energies, with earlier Skyrme HF and relativistic mean-field calculations by Reinhard (1995) and Grawe (2001) which attributed the disappearance of the δ₂ₙ peak to quadrupole correlations. The study also parallels the analysis for ⁹⁰Zr using recent data from Garrett (2005) and predicts a new collective band built on the 0₂⁺ state in both nuclei, dominated by 2p-2h excitations from the fp-shell to the g₉/₂ intruder orbit, enabling future experimental searches for deformed bands in these neutron-rich systems.
