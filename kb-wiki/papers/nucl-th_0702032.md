# Nucleon Mass Splitting at Finite Isospin Chemical Potential

> We investigate nucleon mass splitting at finite isospin chemical potential in the frame of two flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. It is analytically proved that, in the phase with explicit isospin symmetry breaking the proton mass decreases and the neutron mass increases linearly in the isospin chemical potential.

# Nucleon Mass Splitting at Finite Isospin Chemical Potential

## Key claim

The authors analytically prove that in the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, at zero temperature and in the normal phase with only chiral condensate (i.e., below the critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluidity), the proton mass decreases and the neutron mass increases linearly with the isospin chemical potential. This linear relation holds only in the phase with explicit isospin symmetry breaking and fails at finite temperature or in the pion superfluid phase.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor NJL model with scalar, pseudoscalar, and diquark interaction channels in mean-field approximation. They construct nucleons as quark-diquark bound states via the static approximation to the Faddeev equation, reducing it to an effective Bethe-Salpeter equation. The nucleon mass is obtained from the pole of the nucleon propagator at zero momentum, and the chemical potential dependence is extracted analytically by comparing the pole equations in matter and vacuum.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum nucleon mass: \( m_N = 940 \) MeV, fitted by setting the diquark coupling ratio \( G_d/G_s = 1.1 \), which yields a diquark mass \( M_d = 600 \) MeV.
- Vacuum quark mass: \( m = 400 \) MeV, fitted with current quark mass \( m_0 = 5 \) MeV, momentum cutoff \( \Lambda = 593 \) MeV, and scalar coupling \( G_s = 6.92 \) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- Critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluidity: \( \mu_I^c = m_\pi = 140 \) MeV.
- Proton mass in matter: \( M_p = m_N - \mu_B - \frac{1}{2}\mu_I \).
- Neutron mass in matter: \( M_n = m_N - \mu_B + \frac{1}{2}\mu_I \).

## Context

This work extends prior NJL model studies of meson masses at finite isospin chemical potential (He et al.) to the nucleon sector, where the authors find a similar linear splitting for proton and neutron masses as was previously found for charged pions. The results are relevant for understanding isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter in compact stars and heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. The linear relation is expected to break down in the pion superfluid phase (above \( \mu_I^c \)) and at finite temperature, requiring numerical treatment in future work.
