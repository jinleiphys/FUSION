# CP violation and CPT invariance in B+- decays with final state interactions

> We show that, besides the usual short distance contribution for CP violation, final state interactions together with CPT invariance can play an important role in the recent observation of CP violation in three-body charmless $B^\pm$ decays. A significant part of the observed CP asymmetry distribution in the Dalitz plot is located in a region where hadronic channels are strongly coupled. We illustrate our discussion comparing the recent observation of CP violation in the $B^\pm\to K^\pm K^+ K^-$ and $B^\pm\to K^\pm π^+ π^-$ phase space, with a calculation based on $ππ\to KK$ scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, in addition to the usual short-distance (CKM-based) contribution to CP violation, final-state interactions (FSI) together with CPT invariance can produce a significant "compound" CP asymmetry in three-body charmless \(B^\pm\) decays, particularly in the region of the Dalitz plot where hadronic channels (such as \(\pi\pi\) and \(KK\)) are strongly coupled.

## Method

The authors derive a leading-order expression for the CP asymmetry in a two-channel system (\(\alpha\) and \(\beta\)) using the CPT constraint and the S-matrix formalism for hadronic rescattering. They then apply this to the specific case of the S-wave \(\pi\pi \to KK\) scattering channel (isospin \(I=0\), \(J^P=0^+\)), using a parametrization of the \(\pi\pi\) phase shift and inelasticity from Pelaez (2005) to compute the compound contribution to the asymmetry. The result is compared to the LHCb data for \(B^\pm\to K^\pm K^+ K^-\) and \(B^\pm\to K^\pm \pi^+ \pi^-\) decays.

## Key numbers

- The compound contribution to the CP asymmetry is proportional to \(\sqrt{1-\eta^2}\), where \(\eta\) is the inelasticity parameter for the \(\pi\pi\) S-wave; \(\eta\) drops from 1 to values below 1 in the mass region around 1 GeV, due to coupling to the \(KK\) channel.
- The asymmetry is modulated by a phase-space factor \(F(M^2_{KK}) = (M^2_{K^+K^-})_{\text{max}} - (M^2_{K^+K^-})_{\text{min}}\) for the \(B^\pm\to K^\pm K^+ K^-\) decay.
- The S-wave \(\pi\pi\) elastic scattering is elastic (\(\eta=1\)) up to the \(f_0(980)\) resonance, after which \(\eta<1\) until about 1.4 GeV, then returns to 1 above 1.6 GeV.
- The P-wave \(\pi\pi\) scattering is elastic (\(\eta=1\)) until 1.4 GeV, then drops to a minimum of 0.5 due to the \(\rho(1690)\) resonance.
- The D-wave \(\pi\pi\) scattering is elastic until 1.2 GeV, after which \(\eta\) slowly decreases.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Wolfenstein (1991) and Bigi and Sanda (2000), who proposed that CPT invariance and unitarity impose a relation between CP asymmetries in coupled hadronic channels. The paper specifically applies this to the LHCb observations of CP violation in \(B^\pm\to K^\pm K^+ K^-\) and \(B^\pm\to K^\pm \pi^+ \pi^-\) decays (LHCb, 2013). The analysis suggests that the "compound" contribution from \(\pi\pi\to KK\) rescattering can explain the sign and mass-region of the observed asymmetries, and it motivates future searches for similar effects in double-charm \(B\) decays and in other three-body channels.
