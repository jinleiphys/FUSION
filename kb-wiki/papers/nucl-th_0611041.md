# A Monte Carlo approach to study neutron and fragment emission in heavy-ion reactions

> Quantum Molecular Dynamics models (QMD) are Monte Carlo approaches targeted at the description of nucleon-ion and ion-ion collisions. We have developed a QMD code, which has been used for the simulation of the fast stage of ion-ion collisions, considering a wide range of system masses and system mass asymmetries. The slow stage of the collisions has been described by statistical methods. The combination of both stages leads to final distributions of particles and fragments, which have been compared to experimental data available in literature. A few results of these comparisons, concerning neutron double-differential production cross-sections for C, Ne and Ar ions impinging on C, Cu and Pb targets at 290 - 400 MeV/A bombarding energies and fragment isotopic distributions from Xe + Al at 790 MeV/A, are shown in this paper.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their QMD code, combined with the FLUKA de-excitation module, produces an "overall reasonable agreement" with experimental neutron and fragment data, but they explicitly note that this agreement is achieved only when target geometry effects (energy loss and reinteraction) are neglected, and that discrepancies remain at forward emission angles and for neutron-rich fragment yields.

## Method

The fast stage of ion-ion collisions was simulated using a Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) code that treats nucleons as Gaussian wave packets, includes Skyrme-type potentials, Coulomb interactions, and Pauli blocking, and uses isotropic free nucleon-nucleon cross-sections. The slow de-excitation stage of hot fragments was handled by the FLUKA statistical de-excitation module, which includes evaporation (up to mass A=24), fission, Fermi break-up, and photon emission. The combined QMD+FLUKA code was run for single ion-ion collision events (neglecting target thickness effects) and compared to experimental data for neutron double-differential cross-sections and fragment isotopic distributions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron double-differential production cross-sections were simulated for C beams at 290 MeV/A and Ne and Ar beams at 400 MeV/A on C, Cu, and Pb targets.
- Fragment isotopic distributions were simulated for 129Xe + 27Al at 790 MeV/A, using about 10,000 reaction events.
- The ion-ion system evolution was followed for approximately 150-200 fm/c after the overlapping stage.
- De-excitation processes can occur on time scales up to about 10^7 fm/c.
- The equilibrium component of neutron spectra corresponds to neutron kinetic energies below 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the QMD approach of Aichelin (1991) and others by coupling it to the FLUKA de-excitation module (Ferrari et al., 2005), which was originally developed for nucleon-nucleus collisions via the PEANUT model. The results are compared to experimental data from Iwata (2001) for neutron production and from a GSI experiment (1990s) for Xe+Al fragment distributions. The authors note that their results are very similar to those obtained using the relativistic QMD code RQMD2.4 (Sorge, 1995) coupled to the same FLUKA de-excitation module, suggesting that relativistic potential effects are not critical at 790 MeV/A. The work enables future inclusion of target geometry effects and anisotropic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections to improve agreement with data.
