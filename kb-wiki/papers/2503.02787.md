# Super-enhanced Nuclear Fusion in Metal-like Systems and in Condensed Plasmas of Supernova Progenitors

> In the scheme of chemonuclear reaction, bulk of itinerant s-electrons revealing the thermodynamical liquid activity in metallic systems undergo contact interaction with atomic nuclei and therein nucleons, inducing contagiously thermodynamical liquid activity among the bulk of nuclei under the irreversible action of Nature towards the chemical potential minimum resulting in the united spontaneous, irreversible atomic and nuclear reactions. The chemonuclear reaction, moreover, is enhanced with astronomical figures besides the enhancement of few particle processes (e.g., in the electron-screened pycnonuclear fusion). In the systems of metal-like hydride/deuteride and electron donor mixtures, self-sustained H-H and D-D chemonuclear fusion may take place. In these systems, however, some unforeseen phenomena are induced, e.g., radiation-less fusion and $γ$-ray missing positron annihilation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that "chemonuclear fusion" in metal-like hydride/deuteride systems and condensed plasmas of supernova progenitors can achieve astronomical enhancement factors (over 10^20 to 10^46) through the thermodynamical liquid activity of itinerant s-electrons, but this enhancement is conditional on the presence of electron donor mixtures that lower the melting temperature of metallic hydrogen/deuterium to enable liquid-state behavior.

## Method

The author derives screened nuclear fusion rates using a formalism based on the nuclear Bohr radius, screening length, and cross-section factors S(0) for hydrogen burning reactions (Eqs. 5-11). Enhancement factors are evaluated from the chemical potential change ΔG_r for quasi-He atom formation using empirical formulas for immersion energies of impurity atoms in bulk metals (Eqs. 19-24 and 19'-24'). The analysis extends to coherent D_n-D_n fusion where the enhancement factor scales as K^n for n-fold coherent bond collapse.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement factor for D-D chemonuclear fusion via quasi-He atom formation: K(He) = 2.9×10^20 at T = 300 K (k_B T = 0.0259 eV), reducing to K(He) = 2.4×10^13 at T = 460 K
- Alternative empirical formula yields K(He) = 1.4×10^25 at T = 300 K and K(He) = 2.2×10^15 at T = 460 K for Ni hydride-Li mixtures
- Coherent D_2-D_2 chemonuclear fusion enhancement: K(He_2) = 5.7×10^26 (or 5.1×10^30 for Ni hydride-Li mixture)
- Coherent D_3-D_3 chemonuclear fusion enhancement: K(He_3) = 1.4×10^40 (or 1.2×10^46 for Ni hydride-Li mixture)
- Observed enhancement in ^7Li(d,n)^8Be→2·^4He reaction on Li-liquid target: approximately 10^10
- Observed enhancement in ^7Li(^7Li,2n)^12C reaction: close to 10^20
- Number density of conduction s-electrons in light metals: 10^22 to 10^23 cm^-3
- Melting temperature of metallic deuterium in PdD (n_D ≈ 6.3×10^28 m^-3): approximately 300 K intrinsic, reducing to about 200 K with itinerant electrons of n_e ≈ 10^26 m^-3
- D-D fusion rates in PdD: R_s = 8.9×10^-12 s^-1·m^-3; in TiD_2: R_s = 1.0×10^-21 s^-1·m^-3
- H-H fusion rates in PdH: R_s = 1.8×10^-21 s^-1·m^-3; in TiH_2: R_s = 1.7×10^-28 s^-1·m^-3

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier findings on enhanced pycnonuclear fusion in metallic Li-liquids (Ikegami, 2001) and experimental observations with R. Pettersson in Uppsala and T. Watanabe in Tokyo/Sakura. The paper argues that chemonuclear fusion in condensed matters with thermodynamical liquid activity is essentially identical to enhanced pycnonuclear fusion in astrophysical condensed plasmas of white-dwarf supernova progenitors, as previously studied by Cameron and others. The mechanism is claimed to explain the long-standing puzzle of irreproducible cold nuclear fusion experiments, and suggests that self-sustained H-H and D-D chemonuclear fusion may be achievable in metal-like hydride/electron donor mixtures such as Ni nanocrystal powder combined with LiD or complex compounds like LiAlD_4 or LiMgD_3.
