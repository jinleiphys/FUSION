# Hydrodynamic attractor and the fate of perturbative expansions in Gubser flow

> Perturbative expansions, such as the well-known gradient series and the recently proposed slow-roll expansion, have been recently used to investigate the emergence of hydrodynamic behavior in systems undergoing Bjorken flow. In this paper we determine for the first time the large order behavior of these perturbative expansions in relativistic hydrodynamics in the case of Gubser flow. While both series diverge, the slow-roll series can provide a much better overall description of the system's dynamics than the gradient expansion when both series are truncated at low orders. The truncated slow-roll series can also describe the attractor solution of Gubser flow as long as the system is sufficiently close to equilibrium near the origin (i.e., $ρ=0$) in $dS_3 \otimes \mathbb{R}$. Differently than the case of Bjorken flow, here we show that the Gubser flow attractor solution is not solely a function of the effective Knudsen number $τ_R \sqrt{σ_{μν}σ^{μν}} \sim τ_R\, \tanhρ$. Our results give further support to the idea that new \emph{resummed} constitutiv relations between dissipative currents and the gradients of conserved quantities can emerge in systems far from equilibrium that are beyond the regime of validity of the usual gradient expansion.

## Key claim

The gradient series and the slow-roll expansion both diverge in Gubser flow, but the truncated slow-roll series provides a much better overall description of the system's dynamics than the gradient expansion at low orders, provided the system is sufficiently close to equilibrium near the origin (ρ=0) in dS₃⊗ℝ; unlike Bjorken flow, the Gubser flow attractor solution is not solely a function of the effective Knudsen number τ_R√(σ_μνσ^μν) ∼ τ_R tanh ρ.

## Method

The authors study conformal Israel-Stewart theory under Gubser flow, which describes a longitudinally boost-invariant fluid with radially symmetric transverse expansion. They construct two perturbative expansions for the shear stress tensor π: a gradient expansion in powers of the relaxation time τ_R (or equivalently in powers of gradients), and a slow-roll expansion analogous to that used in cosmology. The large-order behavior of both series is determined numerically up to n=100 using recurrence relations derived from the Israel-Stewart equations, and truncations are compared to exact numerical solutions of the same equations.

## Key numbers

- The gradient expansion coefficients display factorial growth |π_n|^(1/n) ∼ n for n up to 100, indicating zero radius of convergence, for ρ = 0.1, 1, 10, and also at ρ = 0 and ρ → ∞.
- For η/s = 1/(4π), the 1st and 2nd order truncations of the gradient expansion provide a good approximation to the exact solution around ρ = 0, while the 8th order truncation is significantly worse.
- For η/s = 1, none of the gradient expansion truncations can describe the exact solution, and π(0) deviates significantly from zero.
- The first-order gradient term (Navier-Stokes) is π₁(ρ) = (4/15) τ_R tanh ρ.
- The second-order gradient term is π₂(ρ) = -4/15 + (4/45) tanh² ρ.

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic attractors and divergent perturbative expansions from Bjorken flow (Heller 2013, Heller 2015, Denicol 2017) to the more complex Gubser flow geometry (Gubser 2010). The gradient series was previously shown to diverge in Bjorken flow for strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma (Heller 2013) and in kinetic theory (Denicol 2016, Heller 2016). The slow-roll expansion was first applied to hydrodynamics in Bjorken flow by Heller (2015), and its large-order behavior was computed by Denicol (2017). The present results support the idea that resummed constitutive relations between dissipative currents and gradients can emerge far from equilibrium, beyond the regime of validity of the usual gradient expansion.
