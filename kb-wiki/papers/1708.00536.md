# High-precision $Q_{EC}$-value measurement of the superallowed $β^+$ emitter $^{22}$Mg and an evaluation of the $A=22$ isobaric triplet

> A direct $Q_{EC}$-value measurement of the superallowed $β^+$ emitter $^{22}$Mg was performed using TRIUMF's Ion Trap for Atomic and Nuclear science (TITAN). The direct ground-state to ground-state atomic mass difference between $^{22}$Mg and $^{22}$Na was determined to be $Q_{EC}=4781.40(22)$~keV, representing the most precise single measurement of this quantity to date. In a continued push towards calculating superallowed isospin-symmetry-breaking (ISB) corrections from first principles, ab-initio shell-model calculations of the $A=22$ IMME are also presented for the first time using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group formalism. With particular starting two- and three-nucleon forces, this approach demonstrates a level of agreement with the experimental data that suggests reliable ab-initio calculations of superallowed ISB corrections are now possible.

## Key claim
The direct ground-state to ground-state atomic mass difference between $^{22}$Mg and $^{22}$Na was measured as $Q_{EC}=4781.40(22)$ keV, representing the most precise single measurement of this quantity to date. The authors claim that ab-initio shell-model calculations using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) formalism with the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction reproduce the excitation energy of the isobaric analogue state in $^{22}$Na at an 8 keV level of agreement, suggesting that reliable ab-initio calculations of superallowed isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections are now possible, though the full theoretical uncertainties are likely larger than this.

## Method
Penning-trap mass spectrometry was performed at TRIUMF's TITAN facility using the Time-of-Flight Ion-Cyclotron-Resonance technique with both quadrupole and Ramsey excitation schemes, with 500 ms excitation times for singly charged $^{22}$Mg$^+$, $^{22}$Na$^+$, and $^{23}$Na$^+$ ions. The $^{22}$Mg beam was produced via spallation reactions from a 35 $\mu$A, 480-MeV proton beam on a SiC target, with selective ionization using the Ion-Guide Laser Ion Source to suppress surface-ionized $^{22}$Na contamination by nearly 6 orders of magnitude. Ab-initio shell-model calculations of the $A=22$ isobaric multiplet mass equation (IMME) coefficients were performed using the VS-IMSRG formalism starting from two different sets of chiral effective field theory two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces: NN+3N(400) and 1.8/2.0(EM).

## Key numbers
- $Q_{EC}=4781.40(22)$ keV for the superallowed $\beta^+$ decay $^{22}$Mg$\rightarrow^{22}$Na, the most precise single measurement to date
- $^{22}$Mg mass excess $\Delta_{Mg}=-400.10(22)$ keV, representing a 30% increase in precision relative to the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2016 (AME16) value of $-399.93(31)$ keV
- $^{22}$Na mass excess $\Delta_{Na}=-5181.49(22)$ keV, compared to AME16 value of $-5181.51(17)$ keV
- Updated evaluated $Q_{EC}=4781.46(16)$ keV when combined with previous measurements from the Hardy/Towner superallowed review (HT15), a 30% increase in precision
- Excitation energy of the $T=1$, $J^\pi=0^+$ isobaric analogue state in $^{22}$Na: experimentally measured as 657.00(14) keV; predicted by the 1.8/2.0(EM) VS-IMSRG calculation as 665 keV (8 keV agreement)
- Experimental IMME coefficients for the $A=22$ triplet: $a=-4524.51(22)$ keV, $b=-3812.31(11)$ keV, $c=312.10(25)$ keV
- 1.8/2.0(EM) VS-IMSRG predicted IMME coefficients: $b=-3283$ keV, $c=508$ keV
- NN+3N(400) VS-IMSRG predicted IMME coefficients: $b=-3516$ keV, $c=1397$ keV

## Context
This work extends the program of high-precision superallowed $\beta$-decay measurements that provide the most precise determination of the vector coupling strength $G_V$ and the CKM matrix element $V_{ud}$, currently dominated by the theoretical isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections of Towner and Hardy (2008, 2010). The ab-initio VS-IMSRG calculations follow first attempts in the $A=20,21$ systems using many-body perturbation theory by Gallant et al. (2014), and demonstrate that the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction reproduces experimental binding energies across the nuclear chart from the $p$ shell to the tin region as shown by Hergert et al. (2016), Simonis et al. (2017), and others. This work enables future controlled exploration of extracting isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed decays from ab-initio methods, potentially reducing the model-dependent uncertainty that currently limits tests of the Standard Model.
