# Production and anisotropic flow of thermal photons in collision of $α$-clustered carbon with heavy nuclei at relativistic energies

> The presence of $α$-clustered structure in the light nuclei produces different exotic shapes in nuclear structure studies at low energies. Recent phenomenological studies suggest that collision of heavy nuclei with $α$-clustered carbon ($^{12}$C) at relativistic energies can lead to large initial state anisotropies. This is expected to impact the final momentum anisotropies of the produced particles significantly. The emission of electromagnetic radiations is considered to be more sensitive to the initial state compared to hadronic observables and thus photon observables are expected to be affected by the initial clustered structure profoundly. In this work we estimate the production and anisotropic flow of photons from most-central collisions of triangular $α$-clustered carbon and gold at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV using an event-by-event hydrodynamic framework and compare the results with those obtained from unclustered carbon and gold collisions. We show that the thermal photon $v_3$ for most central collisions is significantly large for the clustered case compared to the case with unclustered carbon, whereas the elliptic flow parameter does not show much difference for the two cases. In addition, the ratio of anisotropic flow coefficients is found to be a potential observable to constrain the initial state produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and also to know more about the $α$-clustered structure in carbon nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in most-central collisions of triangular α-clustered carbon with gold at √sNN = 200 GeV, the triangular flow v3 of thermal photons is significantly larger than in collisions with unclustered carbon, while the elliptic flow v2 shows little difference; the ratio v2/v3 is proposed as a potential observable to constrain the initial state and identify α-clustered structure in carbon, but the results are based on an ideal (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic framework and the authors note that a (3+1)-dimensional viscous calculation would be ideal for more reliable quantitative results.

## Method

The authors used a two-component Monte Carlo Glauber model to generate initial entropy density distributions for triangular α-clustered 12C (three α-clusters at vertices of an equilateral triangle with side length 3.05 fm) and unclustered carbon, colliding with gold at 200A GeV. These initial conditions were evolved using a (2+1)-dimensional longitudinally boost-invariant ideal relativistic hydrodynamic framework with a lattice-based equation of state (L&S), initial thermalization time τ0 = 0.17 fm/c, and freeze-out temperature Tf = 160 MeV. Thermal photon production was calculated by integrating next-to-leading order emission rates from the QGP phase and parameterized rates from the hadronic phase over the space-time evolution, and anisotropic flow coefficients v1, v2, v3 were computed using the participant plane method for events with Npart > 80.

## Key numbers

- The side length of the equilateral triangle formed by the three α-clusters in carbon is 3.05 fm, and the radius of each α-cluster is 0.96 fm.
- The ratio of event-averaged initial triangular eccentricities (clustered to unclustered) varies in the range 1.6–2.0 as a function of Npart for most-central collisions, indicating more than 50% rise for the clustered case.
- The linear correlation coefficient between v3 and ε3 is approximately 0.82 for the clustered case, compared to approximately 0.65 for the unclustered case.
- The thermal photon v3 for the clustered case is found to be almost twice as large as that for the unclustered case.
- The ratio v2/v3 for the clustered case is smaller than 1 in the region pT > 1 GeV, while for the unclustered case it is about 2 at pT ≈ 1 GeV and approaches 1 above pT > 3 GeV.
- The linear correlation coefficient between pT-integrated photon v1 and ε1 is about -0.59 for the clustered case and -0.51 for the unclustered case.

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological studies by Dasgupta et al. (2020) that first studied direct photon signals in α-clustered C+Au collisions using smooth initial energy density distributions, and by Bozek et al. (2014) and others who showed effects of α-clustering on hadronic anisotropic flow. The present study uses a more realistic event-by-event hydrodynamic framework and focuses on thermal photon anisotropic flow (v1, v2, v3) from most-central collisions, finding that photon v3 is a sensitive probe of the triangular α-cluster structure. The results enable future experimental measurements of photon anisotropic flow ratios from C+Au collisions at RHIC to potentially identify α-clustered structure in carbon, and the authors note that a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic calculation would provide more reliable quantitative results.
