# The SU(2) and SU(3) chiral phase transitions within Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The SU(2) and SU(3) chiral phase transitions in a hot gas made of pions, kaons and etas are studied within the framework of Chiral Perturbation Theory. By using the meson meson scattering phase shifts in a second order virial expansion, we are able to describe the temperature dependence of the quark condensates. We have estimated the critical temperatures where the different condensates melt. In particular, the SU(3) formalism yields a lower critical temperature for the non-strange condensates than within SU(2), and also suggests that the strange condensate may melt at a somewhat higher temperature, due to the different strange and non-strange quark masses.

## Key claim

The SU(3) chiral phase transition yields a lower critical temperature for the non-strange condensates than the SU(2) case, and the strange condensate melts more slowly than the non-strange condensate due to the larger strange quark mass; these effects are clearly visible already at low temperatures where both Chiral Perturbation Theory and the virial expansion can be trusted.

## Method

The temperature dependence of the quark condensates was calculated using a second-order relativistic virial expansion for a gas of pions, kaons, and etas, with the interaction part of the second virial coefficients obtained from meson-meson scattering phase shifts. The phase shifts were taken from one-loop SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory amplitudes, and also from unitarized amplitudes using the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) to extend the description up to 1.2 GeV. The condensates were obtained from the derivative of the pressure with respect to quark masses, using the ChPT expressions for the mass dependence of the meson masses and the vacuum condensate values.

## Key numbers

- Non-strange condensate melting temperature from one-loop ChPT: T_m = 231 MeV for SU(2) and T_m = 227 MeV for SU(3) for a pure pion gas
- Non-strange condensate melting temperature from one-loop ChPT with full SU(3) gas (pions, kaons, etas with interactions): T_m = 211 (+19, -7) MeV
- Difference between SU(2) and SU(3) non-strange melting temperatures from one-loop ChPT: 21 (+14, -7) MeV
- Non-strange condensate melting temperature from unitarized IAM amplitudes: T_m = 204 (+3, -1) (+13, -5 conservative) MeV for SU(3)
- Difference between SU(2) and SU(3) non-strange melting temperatures from IAM: 31.5 (+1.2, -0.03) (+9, -8 conservative) MeV
- Strange condensate extrapolated melting temperature from IAM: T_m = 304 (+39, -25) (+120, -65 conservative) MeV
- Difference between strange and non-strange melting temperatures from IAM: 100 (+36, -29) (+120, -80 conservative) MeV
- Approximately 90% of the strange condensate remains at T = 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) ChPT treatment of Gerber and Leutwyler (1990) to SU(3), including kaons and etas as interacting particles in the hadron gas. The results agree with lattice QCD results suggesting a roughly 20 MeV decrease in the chiral critical temperature when going from two to three light flavors (lattice, 2000). The approach enables model-independent predictions for the strange condensate evolution and can be extended to Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory for studies at non-zero baryon density.
