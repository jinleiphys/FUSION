# Electromagnetic form factors of baryons in nuclear medium

> The electromagnetic structure of the baryons is modified in the nuclear medium. The modifications can be inferred from the comparison between the electromagnetic form factors in medium with the respective form factor in vacuum. Of particular interest is the ratio between the electric and magnetic form factors in medium ($G_E^*/G_M^*$) and vacuum ($G_E/G_M$) of the octet baryon. The deviation of the double ratios ($G_E^*/G_M^*)/(G_E/G_M)$ from unity measures the impact of the medium modification of the electromagnetic structure in a nuclear medium. Measurements of the double ratios $(G_E^*/G_M^*)/(G_E/G_M)$ for different nuclear densities may become available in a near future using the polarization-transfer method developed at Jefferson Lab. We present estimates of the double ratios of octet baryons based on a covariant constituent quark model, which takes into account pion cloud excitations of the baryon cores,for different nuclear densities. Our results manifest different features, namely, enhancement or quenching depending on the baryon flavor content.

## Key claim

The double ratio $(G_E^*/G_M^*)/(G_E/G_M)$ for octet baryons deviates from unity in nuclear medium, with enhancement or quenching depending on the baryon flavor content, but the effect for the proton may be small because previous measurements used the low-density $^4$He system compared to normal nuclear density $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Method

The authors used a covariant spectator quark model where baryons are described as three constituent quarks with two quarks treated as an effective diquark, combined with vector meson dominance parametrization of quark electromagnetic structure. Medium modifications were incorporated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to estimate effective baryon and meson masses as functions of nuclear density, and pion cloud contributions were included via the cloudy bag model with $SU(3)$ pion-baryon interactions. Calculations were performed for symmetric nuclear matter at densities of $0.5\rho_0$ and $\rho_0$.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$
- Double ratios calculated at two nuclear densities: $0.5\rho_0$ and $\rho_0$
- For the neutron, enhancement of the ratio predicted below 2.5 GeV$^2$
- For the proton and $\Sigma^\pm$, suppression of $G_E/G_M$ observed in nuclear medium, increasing with nuclear density
- For $\Xi^0$ and $\Xi^-$, the $Q^2$-dependence is weak, as expected from systems dominated by strange quarks

## Context

This work extends previous studies of medium modifications of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Dieterich 2001, JLab data) to all octet baryons, building on the covariant spectator quark model (CSQM) and QMC model (Saito 2007). The calculations anticipate future measurements using the polarization-transfer method at Jefferson Lab for the neutron ($^4$He($\vec{e},\vec{n}$)$^3$He reaction) and ongoing MAMI experiments on $^{14}$C. The formalism can be extended to higher densities for applications in heavy-ion collisions, neutron stars, and compact stars, and can also calculate effective electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region.
