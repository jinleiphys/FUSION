# Thermal Correlators in the ρ channel of two-flavor QCD

> We present a lattice QCD calculation with two dynamical flavors of the isovector vector correlator in the high-temperature phase. We analyze the correlator in terms of the associated spectral function, for which we review the theoretical expectations. In our main analysis, we perform a fit for the difference of the thermal and vacuum spectral functions, and we use an exact sum rule that constrains this difference. We also perform a direct fit for the thermal spectral function, and obtain good agreement between the two analyses for frequencies below the two-pion threshold. Under the assumption that the spectral function is smooth in that region, we give an estimate of the electrical conductivity.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the isovector vector correlator at high temperature and, under the assumption that the spectral function is smooth below the two-pion threshold, provides an estimate of the electrical conductivity; the analysis relies on fitting the difference between thermal and vacuum spectral functions and using an exact sum rule that constrains this difference, with the caveat that the determination remains a numerically ill-posed problem.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two dynamical, mass-degenerate O(a)-improved Wilson quark flavors on a 64^3 x 16 lattice at T ≈ 253 MeV (T/Tc ≈ 1.2) and on a matching zero-temperature 64^3 x 128 lattice. They computed the Euclidean isovector vector correlator and analyzed it by fitting the difference Δρ(ω,T) between the thermal and vacuum spectral functions, using an exact sum rule that requires the integral of Δρ(ω,T)/ω over all frequencies to vanish; they also performed a direct fit for the thermal spectral function and compared the two analyses for frequencies below the two-pion threshold.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the finite-temperature ensemble: T = 253(4) MeV
- Static isospin susceptibility: χ_s/T^2 = 0.871(1)
- Midpoint value of the difference between thermal and reconstructed correlators: ΔG(τ=β/2,T)/T^3 = 0.291(55)
- Ratio of thermal correlator to free lattice correlator at the midpoint: G_ii^(0)/G_ii^(0),free = 1.100(15)
- Ratio of second thermal moments: R^(2,0)/R^(2,0)_free = 1.089(30)
- Area under the vacuum spectral function up to 1.0 GeV: A_ρ ≈ 0.114 GeV^2
- Missing area in the weak-coupling spectral function at T = 253 MeV: A_missing ≈ 0.038 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of the vector correlator in quenched QCD by Ding et al. (2010) to the case of two dynamical quark flavors, and adopts an approach used by Meyer (2010) in the bulk channel of analyzing the difference of thermal and vacuum correlators. The results enable future determinations of the electrical conductivity and dilepton rates in QCD at temperatures accessible in heavy-ion collisions, and the sum rule derived by Bernecker and Meyer (2011) provides a crucial constraint on the thermal spectral function that must be satisfied by any theoretical model.
