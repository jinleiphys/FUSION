# Dilepton production from chirally asymmetric matter

> We evaluate the dilepton production rate (DPR) from hot and dense chirally asymmetric quark matter. The presence of a finite chiral chemical potential (CCP) in the electromagnetic spectral function results in the appearance of new cut structures signifying additional scattering processes in the medium which leads to a significant enhancement in the DPR at lower values of invariant mass. The constituent quark mass evaluated using a 3-flavour Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model is also non-trivially affected by the CCP. These are found to result in a continuous dilepton production rate as a function of the invariant mass for higher values of temperature and baryonic chemical potential.

## Key claim

The presence of a finite chiral chemical potential (CCP) in the electromagnetic spectral function introduces new cut structures that allow Landau cuts to contribute to the dilepton production rate (DPR) in the time-like region, leading to a significant enhancement at lower invariant masses, but only for kinematic regimes where the three-momentum magnitude satisfies \(|\vec{q}| < 2\mu_5\).

## Method

The authors evaluate the dilepton production rate from hot and dense chirally asymmetric quark matter using the real-time formulation of finite-temperature field theory. The constituent quark masses for u, d, and s flavors are computed self-consistently via the 3-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as functions of temperature, baryonic chemical potential, and chiral chemical potential. The imaginary part of the electromagnetic current correlator (matter tensor) is then computed analytically, including the contributions from both Unitary and Landau cuts that arise from the modified quark propagator in the presence of a finite CCP.

## Key numbers

- The Unitary-I cut threshold for dilepton production shifts from \(q_0 \ge 2M_f\) (for \(|\vec{q}| < 2\mu_5\)) to \(q_0 \ge \sqrt{(|\vec{q}|-2\mu_5)^2+4M_f^2}\) (for \(|\vec{q}| \ge 2\mu_5\)).
- The Landau cuts contribute in the kinematic region \(-|\vec{q}|-2\mu_5 \le q_0 \le |\vec{q}|+2\mu_5\), enabling positive-energy time-like dilepton production for \(|\vec{q}| < q_0 < 2\mu_5\).
- For \(\mu_5 \ge |\vec{q}|/2\), a virtual photon with invariant mass \(q^2 \ge 4(M_f^2-\mu_5^2)\) can decay into a real quark-antiquark pair below the usual threshold of \(q^2 \ge 4M_f^2\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the electromagnetic spectral function in chirally imbalanced matter performed by Ghosh et al. (2022), which revealed a rich cut structure including Unitary and Landau cuts. The present paper applies that spectral function formalism to compute the actual dilepton production rate, which is a key observable in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC. The results enable future experimental searches for signatures of local parity and charge-parity violation in the quark-gluon plasma, complementing efforts to detect the chiral magnetic effect.
