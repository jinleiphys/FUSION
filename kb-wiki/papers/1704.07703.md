# Observation of Ultra Fine Structures in Energy Levels of Prolate Nuclei

## Key claim
The authors claim that splitting in the energy levels of prolate nuclei is possible when the X(3) model is formulated in a three-dimensional non-commutative phase space, producing a term proportional to the projection of angular momentum. The effect is called "ultra fine structures" and is presented as a theoretical possibility, with the caveat that it cannot occur at the atomic scale and would be much harder to observe than the analogous Zeeman effect in atomic physics.

## Method
The authors introduced non-commutative phase space relations in three dimensions, modifying the standard commutation relations between coordinates and momenta. They then applied this formalism to the X(3) model, which describes axially symmetric prolate nuclei with a rigid gamma degree of freedom, by replacing the ordinary momentum squared operator with its non-commutative counterpart. Treating the non-commutative correction as a perturbation, they solved the resulting Schrodinger equation with an infinite square well potential in the beta collective variable, using spherical Bessel functions and evaluating the first-order energy shift from the angular-momentum-dependent perturbation term.

## Key numbers
- Energy spectrum formula: \(E = \frac{\hbar^2}{2B} \left( \frac{x_{s,\nu}}{\beta_w} \right)^2 - \frac{m \bar{\vartheta}}{2B}\), where \(x_{s,\nu}\) is the s-th zero of the spherical Bessel function \(J_\nu\), \(\beta_w\) is the well width, and \(\bar{\vartheta}\) is the non-commutativity parameter.
- For numerical illustration, the authors set \(B = \hbar = \beta_w = 1\) and \(\bar{\vartheta} = 0.1\).
- The index \(\nu\) is given by \(\nu = \sqrt{\frac{L(L+1)}{3} + \frac{1}{4}}\).
- Each excited level with angular momentum \(L\) splits into \(2L+1\) sublevels due to the dependence on the magnetic quantum number \(m\).

## Context
This work extends the X(3) model of Bonatsos et al. (2006), which is the gamma-rigid counterpart of the X(5) critical point symmetry for first-order shape phase transitions between spherical and prolate deformed nuclei. The authors introduce non-commutative phase space concepts, motivated by string theory and quantum gravity, into nuclear collective models for the first time. The result suggests a possible nuclear analogue of the Zeeman effect, but the predicted splittings are extremely small and would require experimental detection of non-commutative effects at nuclear scales, which the authors acknowledge is not straightforward.
