# The (e,e'p) Reaction and the Transition to the Eikonal Regime

> The reliability of the models describing the Final-State Interactions (FSI) in (e,e$'$p) scattering at high proton energies is an important issue in view of the experiments planned at CEBAF. One of the most popular approaches adopted, the Glauber method, involves the linearization of the wave equation for the ejected proton travelling through the residual nucleus. We have studied the consequences of such an assumption for the case of the $^{12}\mathrm{C(e,e}'\mathrm{p)}^{11}\hbox{\rm B}^*$ reaction at high proton momenta by comparing the results with the predictions obtained when the second-order differential equation for the proton wave is solved exactly for each partial wave. We find that the two methods give well-correlated angular distributions for momenta in the range 1-4 GeV/c, i.e. for kinematics relevant to the transition to the eikonal regime.

## Key claim

The Glauber (eikonal) method and the exact partial-wave solution of the Schrödinger equation produce well-correlated angular distributions for the \(^{12}\mathrm{C(e,e'p)}^{11}\mathrm{B}^*\) reaction at outgoing proton momenta between 1 and 4 GeV/c, but the abstract’s claim of “well-correlated” glosses over the fact that discrepancies appear in the diffraction minima at 1.4 GeV/c, which only smooth out as momentum increases toward 4 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for the scattering wave function of the ejected proton using two approaches: method I, which solves the full second-order differential equation exactly for each partial wave up to a maximum angular momentum \(L_{\mathrm{max}} = 120\); and method II, which linearizes the wave equation along the propagation axis (the Glauber approximation). They computed the distorted spectral density \(S^{\mathrm{D}}_{\mathrm{s}1/2}\) for the knockout of a 1s proton from \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) in perpendicular kinematics (fixed \(p\) and \(q\), varying the angle \(\gamma\) between them), using a Woods-Saxon bound state and a local optical potential with zero real part and an imaginary part scaled as \(W = p \times 50/1400\) MeV to reproduce the 40% damping observed in the NE18 experiment.

## Key numbers

- The calculations cover outgoing proton momenta \(p\) in the range 1–4 GeV/c, with momentum transfer \(q\) equal to \(p\).
- At \(p = q = 1.4\) GeV/c and missing momentum \(p_{\mathrm{m}} = 0\), the distorted spectral density shows an average 40% damping relative to the Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) result, matching the NE18 semi-inclusive (e,e'p) observation.
- The maximum angular momentum used in the partial-wave expansion is \(L_{\mathrm{max}} = 120\).
- The optical potential parameters are \(R = 1.2 \times A^{1/3}\) fm (with \(A=12\)) and \(a = 0.5\) fm; the imaginary part is set to \(W = p \times 50/1400\) MeV, producing \(W = 50\) MeV at \(p = 1.4\) GeV/c.
- The angular distributions are compared for \(p = q = 1.4\) GeV/c and \(p = q = 4\) GeV/c; at 4 GeV/c the discrepancies in the diffraction minima almost disappear.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous analysis (Bianconi and Radici, 1994) of the \(^{12}\mathrm{C(e,e'p)}^{11}\mathrm{B}_{\mathrm{s}1/2}\) reaction in parallel kinematics from \(p < 2\) GeV/c up to 4 GeV/c, now in perpendicular kinematics. It tests the validity of the Glauber approximation for final-state interactions in exclusive (e,e'p) reactions, a method widely used in proton-nucleus elastic scattering (Glauber, 1959; various authors, 1980s–1990s) but whose generalization to (e,e'p) is not straightforward. The results enable the use of the computationally simpler eikonal method for analyzing planned CEBAF experiments at high proton momenta, particularly for identifying exotic effects like Colour Transparency, which require FSI modeling accuracy within 10% at moderate missing momenta.
