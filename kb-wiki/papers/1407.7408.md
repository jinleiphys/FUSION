# Strong decays of higher excited heavy-light mesons in a chiral quark model

> The strong decay properties of the higher excited heavy-light mesons from the first radially excited states up to the first $F$-wave states are studied in a constituent quark model. It is found many missing excitations have good potentials to be found in future experiments for their narrow widths, some of them dominantly decay into the first orbital excitations rather than into ground states. In future observations, one should focus on the decay processes not only into the ground states, but also into the low-lying $P$-wave excitations with $J^P=0^+,1^+$. Furthermore, the nature of the newly observed states $D_J(3000)$, $D_J^*(3000)$ and B(5970) is discussed. It is predicted that $D_J(3000)$ seems to be a partner of $D_{sJ}(3040)$, which could be identified as the high-mass mixed state $|2{P_1}>_L$ ($J^P=1^+$) via the $2^1P_1$-$2^3P_1$ mixing. The $D_J^*(3000)$ resonance seems to favor the $1^3F_4$ state, however, the quantum numbers $J^P=0^+$ and $2^+$ can not be excluded complectly, more experimental observations are needed to determine its $J^P$ values. The B(5970) resonance is most likely to be the $1^3D_3$ with $J^P=3^-$.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that many higher excited heavy-light mesons (from first radially excited states up to first F-wave states) have narrow widths and dominantly decay into first orbital excitations rather than ground states, but these predictions depend on specific mixing angles and mass assignments that carry significant uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used a constituent chiral quark model with nonrelativistic harmonic oscillator wave functions to calculate strong decay widths of heavy-light mesons (D, D_s, B, B_s families). They employed effective Lagrangians for quark-pseudoscalar-meson and quark-vector-meson interactions, with model parameters (quark masses, harmonic oscillator parameter beta=0.40 GeV, coupling delta=0.557) fixed from previous studies of charmed baryons and heavy-light mesons. The analysis included configuration mixing between 2^3S_1-1^3D_1 states and 1^1D_2-1^3D_2 states, with mixing angles varied to match observed states.

## Key numbers

- D(2550) as 2^1S_0: predicted total width Gamma = 68 MeV (with Gamma[D*pi]/Gamma[D_0(2400)pi] ratio = 0.28), about a factor of 2 narrower than the experimental width of approximately 130 MeV
- B(2^1S_0) with mass 5886 MeV: predicted total width Gamma = 40 MeV (B*pi = 16 MeV, B(1^3P_0)pi = 24 MeV)
- B_s(2^1S_0) with mass 5985 MeV: predicted total width Gamma = 26 MeV
- D(2600) mixing angle phi = -(36 +/- 6) degrees for 2^3S_1-1^3D_1 mixing
- D_{s1}(2700) mixing angle phi = -(54 +/- 7) degrees for 2^3S_1-1^3D_1 mixing
- D(2750) as 1^1D_2-1^3D_2 mixed state: mixing angle phi_{1D} = -(51 +/- 18) degrees
- B(|(SD)_1>_L) low-mass mixed state with mass 5.9 GeV: predicted width Gamma = (150 +/- 50) MeV
- B(|(SD)_1'>_H) high-mass mixed state with mass 6.0 GeV: predicted width Gamma = (310 +/- 90) MeV
- B_s(|1D_2'>_H) with mass 6.08 GeV: predicted width Gamma = (13 to 50) MeV, with branching ratio Gamma[B*K]/Gamma_total approximately 100%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of strong decays of heavy-light mesons (Zhong 2008, Zhong 2010, Zhong 2009) to higher excitations up to F-wave states, using the same chiral quark model framework. The paper addresses the nature of newly observed states D_J(3000), D_J*(3000), and B(5970) from LHCb and CDF collaborations, and provides predictions for missing states in B and B_s meson families. The results enable experimental searches for predicted narrow states in specific decay channels (such as B*pi for B(|1D_2'>_H) and B*K for B_s(|1D_2'>_H)), and test the universality of configuration mixing patterns across heavy-light meson families. The analysis contrasts with alternative interpretations by other groups (such as Lu 2014, Godfrey 2013, Colangelo 2007, 2012) who suggest weak 2^3S_1-1^3D_1 mixing and prefer pure 2^3S_1 assignments for D(2600) and D_{s1}(2700).
