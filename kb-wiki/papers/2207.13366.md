# The study of the alpha formation probability in 10Be and 12Be within the microscopic cluster model

> The alpha clustering in the 10Be and 12Be has been studied within the framework of the real-time evolution method (REM). By using the effective interaction tuned to reproduce the charge radii and the threshold energies, we have evaluated the alpha reduced width amplitude (RWA) and spectroscopic factor (S-factor) for the ground and excited states. With several improvements made in this work, comparing with our previous calculations, larger rate of clustering results are obtained for the 10Be and 12Be with correct asymptotic at large distance.

# The study of the alpha formation probability in 10Be and 12Be within the microscopic cluster model

## Key claim
The real-time evolution method (REM) with a tuned Volkov No.2 interaction provides an upper limit for alpha cluster formation probabilities in 10Be and 12Be, while previous antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction likely provide a lower limit; the REM results reproduce correct asymptotic behavior at large inter-cluster distances for 10Be, but the more complex structure of 12Be causes deviations from the Whittaker function.

## Method
The authors used the real-time evolution method (REM) to generate basis wave functions assuming an alpha+alpha+xn cluster structure for 10Be and 12Be, then applied the generator coordinate method (GCM) with angular momentum and parity projection. They calculated the alpha reduced width amplitude (RWA) and spectroscopic factor (S-factor) using the Laplace expansion method, and compared the RWA to the Whittaker function at large distances to test asymptotic correctness. The Volkov No.2 interaction was used for the central nucleon-nucleon force with the G3RS spin-orbit interaction, with parameters tuned to reproduce charge radii and threshold energies.

## Key numbers
- For 10Be ground state: REM yields S-factor = 0.77 and R_rwa = 3.42 fm, compared to AMD values of S-factor = 0.35 and R_rwa = 3.23 fm
- For 12Be ground state: REM (set2) yields S-factor = 0.33 and R_rwa = 3.36 fm, compared to AMD values of S-factor = 0.24 and R_rwa = 4.07 fm
- The asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) for 10Be from REM is 5.3
- REM reproduces the alpha decay threshold energy for 10Be as -7.21 MeV, close to the experimental value of -7.41 MeV, while AMD gives -3.75 MeV
- For 12Be, REM (set2) gives the 6He+6He threshold energy as -10.11 MeV, matching the experimental value exactly, while AMD gives -2.63 MeV
- The charge radius of 10Be from REM is 2.55 fm (experiment: 2.34 fm, AMD: 2.62 fm); for 12Be, REM (set2) gives 2.45 fm (experiment: 2.50 fm, AMD: 2.82 fm)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous AMD calculations (Zhao 2021) that showed negative correlation between alpha clustering and neutron-skin thickness in Be and C isotopes, but which likely underestimated alpha formation probabilities due to the Gogny D1S interaction's poor description of light nuclei and insufficient model space from using only quadrupole deformation as the generator coordinate. The REM approach with Volkov No.2 interaction, previously applied to Be and C isotopes (Imai 2019, Zhou 2020, Zhao 2022), provides improved threshold energies and charge radii. The results are relevant for interpreting upcoming (p,palpha) knockout experiments on Be and C isotopes, following the experimental technique established by Yoshida (2016, 2017, 2018, 2019, 2021) and applied to Sn isotopes by Tanaka (2021), which revealed negative correlation between alpha-cluster formation and neutron-skin thickness.
