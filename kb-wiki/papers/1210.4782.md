# Jet probes of QCD matter: single jets and dijets in heavy-ion collisions

> Modifications of jets in the existence of a hot and dense QCD medium have recently attracted a lot of attentions. In this talk, we demonstrate how jet-medium interactions change the behavior of jets by offering examples of inclusive jet and dijet productions at $ {\cal O}(α_s^3)$ in heavy ion collisions including initial-state cold nuclear effects and especially the final-state parton energy loss effect. The suppression of inclusive jet spectrum varying with jet radii and a flatter dijet momentum imbalance as compared to those in hadron-hadron collisions are observed in high-energy nuclear collisions.

# Jet probes of QCD matter: single jets and dijets in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Jet-medium interactions in hot QCD matter modify inclusive jet and dijet observables at next-to-leading order (${\cal O}(\alpha_s^3)$), but the observed broadening of dijet momentum imbalance distributions in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC can only be described when collisional dissipation (parameterized by $p_T^{\min}=20$ GeV) is included alongside radiative energy loss; purely radiative models with $p_T^{\min}=0$ GeV do not produce sufficient broadening.

## Method
The authors compute medium-modified inclusive jet and dijet cross sections in heavy-ion collisions by convolving next-to-leading-order (${\cal O}(\alpha_s^3)$) partonic cross sections in p+p collisions with probability distributions $P_{q,g}(\epsilon, E_T)$ for fractional parton energy loss, including a factor $f(R, p_T^{\min})$ that accounts for energy redistributed inside the jet cone. Both initial-state cold nuclear matter effects and final-state parton energy loss are incorporated, with the coupling strength between jet and medium varied to study sensitivity.

## Key numbers
- Inclusive jet suppression in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV is larger for smaller jet radii and less pronounced for larger jet radii, with the ratio of $R_{CP}$ values between $R=0.3, 0.4, 0.5$ and $R=0.2$ jets shown in Fig. 1 (right panel).
- Dijet suppression in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV is largest along the diagonal $E_{T1}=E_{T2}$ and is "strikingly enhanced" when $E_{T1}$ is far from $E_{T2}$.
- Dijet $A_J$ distributions in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV with jet size $R=0.4$ show broadening relative to p+p; including collisional dissipation with $p_T^{\min}=20$ GeV produces significantly larger broadening than the radiative-only case ($p_T^{\min}=0$ GeV).
- Dijet $z$ distributions in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV with coupling strength $g_{med}=2.0$ are predicted to shift toward $z=0$ and decrease the average $\langle z \rangle$, with strong dependence on jet size that weakens when collisional dissipation is included.

## Context
This work extends the perturbative QCD approach of Vitev (2008, 2009) for jet modification in heavy-ion collisions to next-to-leading order, and builds on the same authors' earlier studies of jet-medium interactions (He 2011). The predicted continuous variation of single-jet suppression with jet radius was subsequently observed by the ATLAS collaboration (2012). The dijet momentum imbalance calculations provide a framework to describe ATLAS (2010) and CMS (2011) data when collisional energy loss is included, and predict results for RHIC energies that could be tested by future measurements.
