# Density dependent hadron field theory for hypernuclei

> The Density Dependent Relativistic Hadron Field (DDRH) theory, previously introduced and applied to isospin nuclei, is extended to hypernuclei by including the octet hyperons. Infinite matter Dirac-Brueckner theory for octet baryons and the derivation of in-medium DDRH baryon-meson vertices is discussed. From the properties of Dirac-Brueckner interactions it is found that hyperon and nucleon self-energies and vertices are related by the ratios of free space coupling constants. This leads to simple scaling laws for the in-medium hyperon and nucleon vertices. The model is applied in relativistic DDRH mean-field calculations to singl$Λnuclei. Free space N-Lambda T-matrix results are used for the scalar vertex. As the only free parameter the hyperon vector vertex scaling factor is adjusted to a selected set of hypernuclear data. Spectroscopic data of single Lambda hypernuclei over the full mass range are well described. The reduced Lambda spin-orbit splitting is reproduced and found to be related closely the medium dependence of scalar and vector interactions.

## Key claim

The Density Dependent Relativistic Hadron Field (DDRH) theory, previously applied only to isospin nuclei, is extended to hypernuclei by deriving scaling laws that relate hyperon and nucleon in-medium vertices through the ratios of free space coupling constants, with the hyperon vector vertex scaling factor as the only free parameter adjusted to hypernuclear data.

## Method

The authors extended the DDRH Lagrangian to include octet hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ) and derived in-medium baryon-meson vertices from infinite matter Dirac-Brueckner theory. They showed that hyperon and nucleon self-energies and vertices are related by simple scaling laws, and applied the model in relativistic DDRH mean-field calculations to single Λ nuclei, using free space NΛ T-matrix results for the scalar vertex while adjusting the hyperon vector vertex scaling factor empirically.

## Key numbers

- The effective potential for Λ hyperons is reduced to about 50% of the nucleon value, consistent with "ideal mixing" conditions where meson-hyperon coupling should be reduced by at least a factor of Rσ,ω ∼ 2/3 according to naive quark counting
- The χ² distribution for Λ single particle spectra across eight nuclei (²⁰⁸PbΛ, ⁸⁹YΛ, ⁵¹VΛ, ⁴⁰CaΛ, ²⁸SiΛ, ¹⁶OΛ, ¹²CΛ, ⁹BeΛ) shows a valley extending between (Rσ, Rω) = (0.1, 0.1) and (Rσ, Rω) = (0.8, 0.9), with the quark model value pair Rσ = Rω = 2/3 located at the ridge of this valley

## Context

This work extends the DDRH theory previously developed for isospin nuclei by Lenske and collaborators (1995) to include strangeness, providing a theoretically grounded alternative to phenomenological relativistic mean-field (RMF) approaches by Ruecker and collaborators (1990), Gloeckle and collaborators (1992), and Vretenar and collaborators (1998) that used empirically adjusted meson-hyperon vertices. The scaling laws derived here give a theoretical foundation for the phenomenological scaling ansatz used in those earlier RMF approaches, and the model enables systematic calculations of hypernuclear structure across the full mass range while reproducing the reduced Λ spin-orbit splitting.
