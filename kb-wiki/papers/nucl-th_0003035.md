# Inelastic photon scattering and the magnetic moment of the $Δ$ (1232) resonance

> The reaction $γ+p\toγ'+p+π^0$ has been suggested as a means to deduce the $Δ^+$ magnetic moment. # Inelastic photon scattering and the magnetic moment of the Δ (1232) resonance

## Key claim
The reaction γ + p → γ' + p + π⁰ yields a total cross section of only 5–10 nb, which is at the limit of present experimental capabilities, contrary to earlier estimates that suggested cross sections of several microbarns. The cross section is suppressed relative to elastic Compton scattering by a factor of roughly (Γ/2(m_Δ − m_N))³ ≈ 10⁻² due to the small phase space available for the final-state photon.

## Method
The authors calculated the cross section for γ + p → γ' + p + π⁰ using two complementary approaches: a constituent quark model with symmetrized spin-isospin wave functions and a nonrelativistic propagator for the Δ resonance, and an effective Lagrangian model with a magnetic dipole coupling for the Δ⁺ current. In the quark model, the ratio of inelastic to elastic cross sections was expressed analytically in terms of the Δ⁺ magnetic moment and the N→Δ transition strength G_{NΔ}, and the inelastic cross section was obtained by integrating over the final-state photon energy with an energy-dependent Δ width Γ(W) fixed at Γ(m_Δ) = 120 MeV. The effective Lagrangian calculation used a simple form for the Δ⁺ current with a magnetic moment parameter G_{M1}^Δ and was evaluated at several incident photon energies.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for γ + p → γ' + p + π⁰ is predicted to be of order 5–10 nb.
- The ratio of inelastic to elastic total cross section at k ≈ 300 MeV is R ≈ 6.4 × 10⁻³.
- The elastic (Compton) cross section at resonance (W = m_Δ) is σ^{el}_{tot} ≈ 1.2 μb in the quark model, compared to an experimental value of about 2.8 μb.
- The electromagnetic width of the Δ(1232) in the quark model is Γ_γ ≈ 0.46 MeV, giving a branching ratio Γ_γ/Γ ≈ 0.38%, while the experimental value is (0.56 ± 0.04)%.
- The forward Compton cross section at resonance is dσ^{el}/dΩ'(Θ=0, W=m_Δ) ≈ 125 nb/sr.
- The Thomson cross section σ_{Th} = (e²/4π m_N)² ≈ 23.6 nb.
- The effective Lagrangian calculation at k = 286 MeV yields fivefold differential cross sections of order 0.25 nb/GeV sr², about three orders of magnitude lower than the results of Machavariani et al. (1999).
- The maximum inelastic cross section is expected for W ≈ m_Δ + Γ/2, i.e., k ≈ 306 MeV and k'_{max} ≈ 200 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the earlier suggestion by Kondratyuk and Ponomarev (1968) to use radiative π⁺p scattering for measuring Δ magnetic moments, and directly addresses the discrepancy with the effective Lagrangian calculation of Machavariani et al. (1999), which predicted cross sections of several microbarns. The authors note that the A2 collaboration at MAMI with the TAPS detector observed only 3-photon events at a rate corresponding to tens of nanobarns, partly from γ + p → p + π⁰ + π⁰ (Harvey et al., 1996). The results enable a realistic assessment of the feasibility of extracting the Δ⁺ magnetic moment from inelastic photon scattering experiments, and the authors plan to include backgrounds from bremsstrahlung and nonresonant intermediate states in future work.
