# Deuteron-Deuteron Interaction and Correlation Function

> The interaction between deuterons ($d$-$d$) is pivotal for understanding the characteristics of certain light nuclei from the perspective of the deuteron cluster and achieving a precise reproduction of $d$-$d$ fusion cross sections. In this work, we construct a new set of elastic $d$-$d$ interactions by fitting the phase shifts using potentials parameterized in a Woods-Saxon shape. Then, the correlation functions are calculated with the obtained potential and compared with the recent measurements by the STAR collaboration. We find that the $d$-$d$ phase shifts and the correlation functions are internally consistent, confirming that correlation functions can provide cross-check for the $d$-$d$ interaction. In addition, both the $^1S_0$ bound state and the repulsive $^5S_2$ interaction contribute to the observed suppression in the measured correlation function. Moreover, we demonstrate that the $P$-wave contribution of the correlation functions cannot be neglected, especially in determining the source size.

# Deuteron-Deuteron Interaction and Correlation Function

## Key claim
The observed suppression in the deuteron-deuteron correlation function relative to pure Coulomb predictions arises from a combination of a bound state in the $^1S_0$ channel and a repulsive interaction in the $^5S_2$ channel, not solely from repulsive strong interactions as previously assumed. The correlation functions and phase shifts are internally consistent, confirming that femtoscopic correlation functions can serve as a cross-check for the $d$-$d$ interaction obtained from scattering data.

## Method
The authors constructed elastic $d$-$d$ interactions by fitting phase shifts from Hofmann and Hale (1996) using a Woods-Saxon potential parameterization for the $^1S_0$, $^5S_2$, $^3P_0$, $^3P_1$, and $^3P_2$ partial waves. They then computed $d$-$d$ correlation functions via the Koonin-Pratt equation with a Gaussian source function and compared them to recent STAR collaboration measurements, using the Gaussian source radius as the only free parameter.

## Key numbers
- $^1S_0$ scattering length: $-0.26$ fm (RM dataset) and $4.95$ fm (RG dataset)
- $^5S_2$ scattering length: $3.96$ fm (RM) and $3.55$ fm (RG)
- $^1S_0$ binding energy: $52.89$ MeV (RM) and $42.09$ MeV (RG)
- Woods-Saxon potential depth $V_0$ for $^1S_0$: $-56.38$ MeV (RM) and $-59.31$ MeV (RG)
- Woods-Saxon potential depth $V_0$ for $^5S_2$: $29.71$ MeV (RM) and $35.74$ MeV (RG)
- Spin-averaged scattering lengths $\bar{a}_0 = \frac{1}{6}a_0^{^1S_0} + \frac{5}{6}a_0^{^5S_2}$ show only minor variations between RM and RG datasets
- $S$-wave channels carry a combined weight of $2/3$ in the spin-averaged correlation function; $P$-wave channels carry $1/3$

## Context
This work extends the approach of Chitwood (1985), who first extracted Woods-Saxon $d$-$d$ interactions from phase shift data but attributed correlation function suppression solely to repulsive interactions. The present study uses improved phase shift analysis from Hofmann and Hale (1996), which employed refined Resonating Group Model calculations with the Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, and high-precision $d$-$d$ correlation data from the STAR collaboration (2024). The results enable future studies incorporating coupled-channel effects among proton-triton, deuteron-deuteron, and neutron-$^3$He systems, and suggest that transport models or data-driven neural networks could further refine source function descriptions.
