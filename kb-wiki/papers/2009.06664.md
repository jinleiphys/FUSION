# Measuring the surface thickness of the weak charge density of nuclei

> The present PREX-II and CREX experiments are measuring the rms radius of the weak charge density of $^{208}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca. We discuss the feasibility of a new parity violating electron scattering experiment to measure the surface thickness of the weak charge density of a heavy nucleus. Once PREX-II and CREX have constrained weak radii, an additional parity violating measurement at a momentum transfer near 0.76 fm$^{-1}$ for $^{208}$Pb or 1.28 fm$^{-1}$ for $^{48}$Ca can determine the surface thickness.

## Key claim

Once the PREX-II and CREX experiments have constrained the weak charge radii of 208Pb and 48Ca, a single additional parity violating electron scattering measurement at a specific higher momentum transfer (0.76 fm^-1 for 208Pb or 1.28 fm^-1 for 48Ca) can determine the surface thickness of the weak charge density, but the feasibility depends on the surface-to-volume ratio of the nucleus and the proximity of the kinematics to the diffraction minimum.

## Method

The authors model the weak charge density with a two-parameter Fermi function and compute the parity violating asymmetry Apv including Coulomb distortions by solving the Dirac equation numerically. They define log derivatives epsilon_a and epsilon_R to quantify the sensitivity of Apv to the surface thickness parameter a and the weak radius RW, respectively, and calculate figures of merit FOM = (dsigma/dOmega) * Apv^2 * epsilon^2 to identify optimal momentum transfers for a new measurement. The analysis uses relativistic mean field models (e.g., FSU Gold) to generate reference weak densities and Fermi function fits.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb at 950 MeV and scattering angle 4.55 degrees (PREX-II kinematics), epsilon_a = 0.091; a 2.5% measurement of Apv yields sensitivity to a of +/- 27%.
- For 48Ca at 2220 MeV and scattering angle 4.55 degrees (CREX kinematics), epsilon_a = 0.731; a 5% measurement of Apv yields sensitivity to a of about 7%.
- The optimal momentum transfer for measuring the surface thickness a is 0.76 fm^-1 for 208Pb and 1.28 fm^-1 for 48Ca.
- For 208Pb, the maximum in FOM_a near 10 degrees is about 400 times smaller than the maximum in FOM_R; for 48Ca, the local maximum in FOM_a near 17 degrees is about 1/100 the maximum in FOM_R.
- Model predictions for the surface thickness parameter a for 208Pb average about 0.60 fm with a variance of +/- 0.02 fm (3% range); for 48Ca the average is about 0.525 fm with a variance of +/- 0.025 fm (5% range).
- At MAMI with 855 MeV beam energy, a 10% measurement of the surface thickness for 208Pb at 10.35 degrees would require about 100 days at 20 microamps beam current.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of parity violating electron scattering used by the PREX and CREX collaborations (Jefferson Laboratory) to measure weak charge radii, and builds on prior theoretical work by Horowitz and collaborators on determining the full weak density distribution. The paper shows that while PREX-II is insensitive to the surface thickness (epsilon_a = 0.091), CREX is significantly sensitive (epsilon_a = 0.731), meaning the surface thickness uncertainty must be addressed in extracting RW from CREX. A new measurement at higher momentum transfer would provide a model-independent determination of the surface thickness, which is sensitive to poorly constrained isovector gradient terms in nuclear energy functionals, and would enable a sharper determination of the average interior baryon density of 208Pb, closely related to nuclear matter saturation density.
