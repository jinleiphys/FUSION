# A Brief Review of Future Lepton-Hadron and Photon-Hadron Colliders

> Options for future $lepton-proton,lepton-nucleus,γ-proton,γ-nucleus$ and FEL$γ-nucleus$ colliders are discussed. In the spirit of this content, we consider TESLAxHERA, LEPxLHC, $μ$xTEVATRON, exRHIC, LinacxLHC, $\sqrt{s}=3TeV$ $μp$ and CLIC based colliders.

# A Brief Review of Future Lepton-Hadron and Photon-Hadron Colliders

## Key claim

Linac-ring type lepton-hadron colliders, particularly those based on superconducting linacs such as TESLA, can extend the kinematic reach of HERA by roughly two orders of magnitude in both Q² and x, potentially reaching the parton saturation regime; however, the actual realization and luminosity of each proposed machine depend critically on unresolved accelerator issues such as intra-beam scattering, beam-beam tune shifts, and the feasibility of dynamic focusing schemes.

## Method

The authors survey proposed future lepton-hadron and photon-hadron collider configurations, including TESLA⊗HERA (THERA), LEP⊗LHC, μ⊗TEVATRON, e⊗RHIC, Linac⊗LHC, a √s=3 TeV μp collider, and CLIC-based designs. For each configuration, they compile or estimate luminosity using the standard linac-ring luminosity formula L_ep = (1/4π)·(P_e/E_e)·(n_p/ε_p^N)·(γ_p/β_p^*) for round transversely matched beams, and discuss limitations from beam-beam tune shift (ΔQ), intra-beam scattering (IBS), and the dynamic focusing scheme proposed by Brinkmann and Dohlus (1995). Photon-hadron options are evaluated using Compton backscattering of laser light off electron beams, following the method of Telnov (1990) and Ciftci et al. (1995).

## Key numbers

- TESLA⊗HERA ep collider: L_ep = 4.1×10³⁰ cm⁻²s⁻¹ with E_e = 250 GeV and E_p = 1 TeV; alternative versions yield L_ep = 2.5×10³¹ cm⁻²s⁻¹ at E_e = E_p = 500 GeV and L_ep = 1.6×10³¹ cm⁻²s⁻¹ at E_e = E_p = 800 GeV.
- TESLA⊗HERA γp collider: L_γp ≈ 2×L_ep at z=0 (conversion region to interaction point distance), decreasing by factor ~1/2 at z=10 m.
- TESLA⊗HERA eC (electron-Carbon) collider: L_eC = 10²⁹ cm⁻²s⁻¹ for 250 GeV electrons and Carbon beam with n_C = 2.5×10⁹, corresponding to integrated luminosity L_int·A ≈ 10 pb⁻¹ per working year (10⁷ s).
- TESLA⊗HERA γC collider: L_γC = 0.6×10²⁹ cm⁻²s⁻¹ at z=5 m.
- LEP⊗LHC ep collider: estimated L_ep = 1.2×10³² cm⁻²s⁻¹; e-Pb luminosity more realistically L_e-Pb ≈ 10²⁸ cm⁻²s⁻¹.
- μ⊗TEVATRON: L_μp = 1.7×10³³ cm⁻²s⁻¹ estimated from 200 GeV muon beam parameters of the Muon Collider Collaboration (1999).
- e⊗RHIC: electron-gold luminosity expected as 6.4×10³⁰ cm⁻²s⁻¹ with 360 bunches or 45×10³⁰ cm⁻²s⁻¹ with 2520 bunches; linac case gives 5.6×10³⁰ cm⁻²s⁻¹.
- Linac⊗LHC ep collider: √s = 5.29 TeV, L_ep = 8×10³¹ cm⁻²s⁻¹ with E_e = 1 TeV and E_p = 7 TeV; γp option yields L_γp > 10³² cm⁻²s⁻¹.
- √s=3 TeV μp collider: L_μp = 10³² cm⁻²s⁻¹ with n_p = n_μ = 2×10¹².
- CLIC-based ep collider: L_ep ≈ 5×10³¹ cm⁻²s⁻¹ estimated from L_ee = 10³⁵ cm⁻²s⁻¹ scaled by m_e/m_p.

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on linac-ring colliders by Tigner, Wiik, and Willeke (1991) and Wiik (1993), and the γp collider concept of Alekhin et al. (1991). It directly compares the proposed TESLA⊗HERA complex (THERA) with the LEP⊗LHC option discussed by Ellis, Keil, and Rolandi (1998) and Keil (1997), and with μp collider estimates from the Muon Collider Collaboration (1999). The paper enables future design studies by identifying the critical accelerator challenges, particularly intra-beam scattering for heavy nuclei, beam-beam tune shift limitations, and the need for dynamic focusing and cooling methods, that must be solved before any of these machines can be realized.
