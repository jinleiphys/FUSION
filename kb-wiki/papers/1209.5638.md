# Dynamical Structure Factors in Quantum Many-Body Systems from Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations

> An ab-initio method for determining the dynamical structure function of an interacting many--body quantum system has been devised by combining a generalized integral transform method with Quantum Monte Carlo methods. As a first application, the coherent and, separately, the incoherent excitation spectrum of bulk atomic 4He has been computed, both in the low and intermediate momentum range. The peculiar form of the kernel in the integral transform of the dynamical structure function allows to predict, without using any model, both position and width of the collective excitations in the maxon--roton region, as well as the second collective peak. A prediction of the dispersion of the single--particle modes described by the incoherent part is also presented.

## Key claim

By combining a generalized integral transform method (using a Sumudu-based kernel) with Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) techniques, the authors claim to extract both the coherent and incoherent dynamical structure factor of bulk atomic 4He without any model input, but the resolution is ultimately limited by the available computer power and the method requires careful choice of kernel parameters (P=2, a=1, b=2) and long imaginary-time propagation to achieve narrow kernel widths.

## Method

The authors compute the dynamical structure factor S(q,omega) by first evaluating an integral transform of S with a Sumudu-based kernel K(sigma,omega) that reduces to a linear combination of imaginary-time propagators. They then perform Reptation Monte Carlo (RMC) simulations of 64 or 125 4He atoms in a periodic box at the experimental saturation density, using the HFDHE2 pair potential, to obtain the imaginary-time correlation functions. Finally, they invert the transform using either the Entropy Maximization Maximum Likelihood (EMML) or the Simultaneous Algebraic Reconstruction Technique (SMART) to recover the real-frequency spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy per particle: epsilon_0^RMC = -7.23 +/- 0.01 K, compared to experimental value epsilon_0^exp = -7.17 K
- Momentum range studied: q approximately 0.3 to 3.5 A^-1 (low momentum phonon-maxon-roton region)
- Simulation box side length: L = 14.306 A for the 64-atom system
- Experimental saturation density: n_0 = 0.02186 A^-3
- Kernel parameters used: P=2, a=1, b=2
- Imaginary-time steps: Delta tau = 0.002 K^-1 and 0.001 K^-1, giving mesh separation less than 0.5 K up to about 40 K
- For q <= 2.4 A^-1, both inversion methods (EMML and SMART) converge to the same solution
- Intrinsic peak width estimate for collective excitation: Delta omega approximately 5 x 10^-4 K (from Mezei)
- Lower energy single-particle branch velocity relative to superfluid critical velocity: ve/vc approximately 1.57

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method, previously limited to few-body systems (up to N=6 in BaM02 and N=7 in BaA04), to many-body systems by implementing it within QMC algorithms. It directly addresses the inability of standard QMC methods to treat dynamical properties due to their imaginary-time formulation, and improves upon the Maximum Entropy Method (Ja63, SiS90) which could not resolve the double-peaked structure of S(q,omega) in superfluid 4He. The method enables future calculations of dynamical structure factors for a wide range of quantum many-body systems, including extensions to finite temperature via Path Integral Monte Carlo methods.
