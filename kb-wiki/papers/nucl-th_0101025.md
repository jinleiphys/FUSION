# The Strange Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Strangeness flavor has turned out to be a very effective diagnostic tool of relativistic heavy ion physics. The absolute yield provides information about conditions arising in first instants of the interaction. Strange hadrons are abundant allowing a precise study of the chemical freeze-out conditions in the dense matter fireball. The thermal and chemical freeze-out universality seen in strange hadrons confirms sudden hadronization as the breakup mechanism. A plausible cause of sudden fireball breakup is the mechanical instability arising when a quark-gluon plasma phase supercools deeply in its adiabatic expansion. Applying these ideas, we interpret CERN-SPS and the first results on hadronic particle ratios obtained at RHI

## Key claim

Strangeness flavor is a practical diagnostic tool for quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but the claim that observed strangeness enhancement confirms QGP formation is conditional on the absence of any consistent alternative interpretation of the full set of experimental data on strange hadron production.

## Method

The authors model the QGP fireball using a partition function that includes interacting gluons, light quarks, strange quarks with reduced effective number, and a vacuum energy density term (bag constant). They compute the QGP equation of state as a function of temperature and baryochemical potential, incorporating running coupling constant from two-loop renormalization group equations and lattice-QCD results. They then perform a statistical model analysis of hadron yields from 158 A GeV Pb-Pb collisions at CERN-SPS, fitting chemical freeze-out parameters (temperature, flow velocity, quark fugacities, and phase space occupancies) to all measured hadron species except Omega and anti-Omega.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature from Pb-Pb data analysis: T_f = 142 ± 3 MeV (unconstrained fit)
- Collective flow velocity at sudden breakup: v_c = 0.54 ± 0.04
- Light quark fugacity: λ_q = 1.61 ± 0.02
- Strange quark fugacity: λ_s = 1.09 ± 0.02
- Ratio of strange to light quark phase space occupancy: γ_s/γ_q = 0.79 ± 0.05
- Specific entropy per baryon at freeze-out: S_f/B = 42 ± 3
- Specific strangeness per baryon: s_f/B = 0.69 ± 0.04
- Energy per baryon in the flowing frame: E_f/B = 7.8 ± 0.5 GeV
- Equilibrium phase transition temperature at zero baryochemical potential: T_pt ≈ 172 MeV (point hadrons), T_fp ≈ 166 MeV (with excluded volume correction)
- Supercooled zero-pressure baryon-free QGP temperature: T_sc = 157 MeV
- Deep supercooled temperature for expanding fireball instability: T_dsc ≈ 147 MeV
- Bag constant: B = 0.19 GeV/fm³

## Context

This work extends the strangeness signature approach proposed by Rafelski (1982) and developed in collaboration with Müller (1982) and Danielewicz (1983), which predicted enhanced multi-strange baryon production from QGP. The authors build on their own prior statistical model analysis (Letessier et al., 2000) and earlier QGP fireball modeling (Acta Physica Polonica, 1996). The mechanical instability mechanism for sudden hadronization was suggested by Csörgő and Csernai (1994) and is here connected to the condition of vanishing surface pressure in a supercooled expanding QGP fireball. The results enable quantitative comparison with first RHIC strangeness data and predictions for strangeness production as a function of collision energy and system size.
