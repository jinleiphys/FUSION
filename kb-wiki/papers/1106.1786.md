# Study of $ππ$ correlations at LHC and RHIC energies in $pp$ collisions within the quark-gluon string model

> The Quark Gluon String Model (QGSM) reproduces well the global characteristics of the $pp$ collisions at RHIC and LHC, e.g., the pseudorapidity and transverse momenta distributions at different centralities. The main goal of this work is to employ the Monte Carlo QGSM for description of femtoscopic characteristics in $pp$ collisions at RHIC and LHC. The study is concentrated on the low multiplicity and multiplicity averaged events, where no collective effects are expected. The different procedures for fitting the one-dimensional correlation functions of pions are studied and compared with the space-time distributions extracted directly from the model. Particularly, it is shown that the double Gaussian fit reveals the contributions coming separately from resonances and from directly produced particles. The comparison of model results with the experimental data favors decrease of particle formation time with rising collision energy.

## Key claim

The Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) can reproduce femtoscopic correlation functions in low-multiplicity and multiplicity-averaged pp collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, but only if the string tension scaling parameter alpha is increased from 1.0 to 1.5 (for 200 GeV) or 3.0 (for 900 GeV), implying a decrease of particle formation time with rising collision energy.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo version of the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) to simulate pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV and 900 GeV, generating space-time coordinates of pions via the Lund string fragmentation scheme with a constituent formation time definition. They constructed one-dimensional and three-dimensional two-pion correlation functions using both ideal (same-event reference) and realistic (mixed-event reference) procedures, fitting them with single-Gaussian and double-Gaussian parametrizations to extract correlation radii R_inv, R_out, R_side, and R_long. The string tension was scaled by a factor alpha (1.0, 1.5, or 3.0) to study the effect of formation time on the correlation functions, and results were compared directly to STAR and ALICE experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, best agreement with STAR data was obtained with alpha = 1.5 (string tension 1.32 GeV/fm)
- For pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 900 GeV, best agreement with ALICE data was obtained with alpha = 3.0 (string tension 2.64 GeV/fm)
- The fraction of direct pions decreases from 46.9% at 200 GeV to 37.5% at 900 GeV; pions from rho decays increase from 37.1% to 40.7%, from omega decays from 11.2% to 15.9%, and from K* decays from 4.2% to 5.5%
- The single-Gaussian fit correlation radius R_inv for the lowest k_T bin (0.1-0.25 GeV/c) at 900 GeV with alpha=3 was 1.26 fm; for the highest k_T bin (0.7-1.0 GeV/c) it was 0.71 fm
- The double-Gaussian fit at 900 GeV with alpha=3 gave a first radius of 1.23 fm (KT1) decreasing to 0.71 fm (KT5), and a second radius of 5.04 fm (KT1) increasing to 13.97 fm (KT5)
- The path length l* of omega mesons is 28.1 fm, of K* mesons is 8.0 fm, and of rho mesons is 3.3 fm

## Context

This work extends the application of the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM), previously validated for global observables like pseudorapidity and transverse momentum distributions, to femtoscopic correlations in pp collisions. It directly compares with experimental correlation functions from the STAR Collaboration (2006) at RHIC and the ALICE Collaboration (2010) at LHC, and addresses the puzzle raised by ALICE regarding the absence of k_T dependence in correlation radii when using PYTHIA/PHOJET baselines. The study enables future use of QGSM as a reference model for interpreting femtoscopic data in pp collisions and for disentangling contributions from direct pions versus resonance decays using double-Gaussian fitting procedures.
