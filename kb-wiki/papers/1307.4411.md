# First Observation of the $Λ(1405)$ Line Shape in Electroproduction

> We report the first observation of the line shape of the $Λ(1405)$ from electroproduction, and show that it is not a simple Breit-Wigner resonance. Electroproduction of $K^+ Λ(1405)$ off the proton was studied by using data from CLAS at Jefferson Lab in the range $1.0<Q^2<3.0$ (GeV/c)$^2$. The analysis utilized the decay channels $Σ^+ π^-$ of the $Λ(1405)$ and $p π^0$ of the $Σ^+$. Neither the standard (PDG) resonance parameters, nor free parameters fitting to a single Breit-Wigner resonance represent the line shape. In our fits, the line shape corresponds approximately to predictions of a two-pole meson-baryon picture of the $Λ(1405)$, with a lower mass pole near 1368 MeV/c$^2$ and a higher mass pole near 1423 MeV/c$^2$. Furthermore, with increasing photon virtuality the mass distribution shifts toward the higher mass pole.

## Key claim

The line shape of the Λ(1405) observed in electroproduction is not a simple Breit-Wigner resonance; the data are best described by two poles near 1368 and 1423 MeV/c², and the mass distribution shifts toward the higher mass pole with increasing photon virtuality (Q²), but the limited statistics prevent more quantitative statements.

## Method

The CLAS spectrometer at Jefferson Lab used a 5.5 GeV electron beam on a liquid hydrogen target to study electroproduction of K⁺Λ(1405) in the range 1.0 < Q² < 3.0 (GeV/c)². The analysis selected events with exactly four detected charged particles (e⁻, K⁺, p, π⁻) and reconstructed the Λ(1405) via its decay chain Λ(1405) → Σ⁺π⁻ followed by Σ⁺ → pπ⁰, with the π⁰ missing. Backgrounds from non-resonant K⁺Σ⁺π⁻ and K*⁰Σ⁺ were simulated using GEANT-based Monte Carlo and subtracted, and acceptance corrections were applied using three-body phase space simulations.

## Key numbers

- Best fit two-pole positions from the higher Q² range (1.5 to 3.0 (GeV/c)²): m₀^(high) = 1.423 ± 0.002 GeV/c² and m₀^(low) = 1.368 ± 0.004 GeV/c², with χ² per degree of freedom = 1.31
- Combined fit over seven Q² ranges (1.0 to 3.0 (GeV/c)²) gave m₀^(high) = 1.422 ± 0.002 GeV/c² and m₀^(low) = 1.365 ± 0.002 GeV/c², with χ² per degree of freedom = 1.44
- Single Breit-Wigner fit with PDG parameters gave χ² per degree of freedom = 3.12; free single Breit-Wigner fit gave χ² per degree of freedom = 2.97
- Q² range of significance after event selection: 1.0 to 3.0 (GeV/c)²
- The t range covered: 0.5 to 4.5 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Λ(1405) line shape from real photoproduction by CLAS/JLab (Moriya, Schumacher 2013) and LEPS/SPring-8 (Ahn, Niiyama), as well as hadronic beam experiments (Hemingway, Braun 1977, Thomas). The results support the two-pole chiral unitary picture of the Λ(1405) proposed by Jido (2003) and Oller (2001), where the lower mass pole couples more strongly to Σπ and the higher mass pole to N̄K. The observed Q² dependence of the relative pole strengths is a new phenomenon not predicted by any existing theoretical work, and the paper calls for higher statistics electroproduction data to enable more quantitative comparisons with models.
