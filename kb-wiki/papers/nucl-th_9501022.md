# Meson Exchange Currents in Pion Double Charge Exchange at High Energies

> In this letter we study the high energy behavior of the forward differential cross section for the $^{18}O(π^+,π^-)^{18}Ne$ double charge exchange reaction. We have evaluated the sequential and the meson exchange current mechanisms. The meson exchange current contribution shows a very weak energy dependence and becomes relevant at incident pion kinetic energies above 600 MeV.

# Meson Exchange Currents in Pion Double Charge Exchange at High Energies

## Key claim

The meson exchange current (MEC) contribution to the forward differential cross section for the $^{18}O(\pi^+,\pi^-)^{18}Ne$ double charge exchange reaction shows a very weak energy dependence and becomes relevant at incident pion kinetic energies above 600 MeV, but the MEC amplitude is too small to produce a very significant enhancement in the cross section on its own; interference effects between the sequential and MEC mechanisms can produce strong cancellation or addition depending on energy.

## Method

The authors calculated the forward differential cross section for the $^{18}O(\pi^+,\pi^-)^{18}Ne$ double charge exchange reaction using the Glauber model of multiple scattering, assuming the DCX process occurs in the valence neutrons while the $^{16}O$ core is responsible only for pion wave distortion. They evaluated two mechanisms: the sequential mechanism (SEQ), involving two successive single charge exchange scatterings using isovector $\pi N$ amplitudes from the SAID code with partial waves up to $l=6$, and the meson exchange current mechanism (MEC), computed in chiral perturbation theory at tree level with baryons, including both a pion pole term and a contact term with a cutoff parameter $\Lambda = 1.3$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- The SEQ cross section shows a first dip at 700 MeV and a second, more pronounced dip at 1100 MeV incident pion kinetic energy.
- In the region of the first dip (around 700 MeV), the cross section including both MEC and SEQ mechanisms is bigger than the SEQ-only cross section by a factor of 2.
- At 1030 MeV and 1200 MeV, the SEQ and MEC mechanisms are comparable in magnitude; at 1030 MeV interference causes strong cancellation, while at 1200 MeV it is additive.
- The harmonic oscillator wave function parameter used for the valence neutrons is $\alpha^2 = 0.32$ fm$^{-2}$.
- The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, and the $\pi N$ coupling constant is $f = 1.02$.

## Context

This work extends the sequential-only DCX calculation of Oset et al. (1995) for incident pion energies up to 1.4 GeV, which showed that the forward-angle cross section decreases above 600 MeV with dips at 700 MeV and 1300 MeV. The present calculation adds MEC contributions first studied by Germond, Robilotta and Wilkin (1980s) in the $\Delta$ resonance region, where they were found to be small compared to the sequential mechanism. The results suggest that at high energies above 600 MeV, MEC processes can produce observable changes in the cross section, and the authors note that additional MEC mechanisms involving the $\rho$ meson could be important, as well as higher-order chiral perturbation theory contributions and explicit inclusion of resonances such as the $\rho(770)$.
