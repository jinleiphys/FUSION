# Equation of state and effective mass of the unitary Fermi gas in a 1D periodic potential

> By solving the Bogoliubov -- de Gennes equations at zero temperature, we study the effects of a one-dimensional optical lattice on the behavior of a superfluid Fermi gas at unitarity. We show that, due to the lattice, at low densities the gas becomes highly compressible and the effective mass is large, with a consequent significant reduction of the sound velocity. We discuss the role played by the lattice in the formation of molecules and the emergence of two-dimensional effects in the equation of state. Predictions for the density profiles and for the frequency of the collective oscillations in the presence of harmonic trapping are also given.

## Key claim

The Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) mean-field theory predicts that a one-dimensional optical lattice at unitarity causes the gas to become highly compressible with a large effective mass at low densities, but the authors note that the BdG predictions are approximate and that the universal parameter for the uniform gas, β ≈ -0.41, differs from the Monte Carlo result β ≈ -0.58.

## Method

The authors solved the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations at zero temperature for a two-component superfluid Fermi gas at unitarity in a one-dimensional optical lattice potential V_ext(z) = s E_R sin²(q_B z). They calculated the chemical potential, inverse compressibility κ⁻¹, and effective mass m* from the energy density, using two regularization schemes (a cut-off method and the method of Bruun and Bulgac) to handle ultraviolet divergences. The results were then used within a local density approximation to predict density profiles and collective mode frequencies for harmonically trapped gases.

## Key numbers

- For the uniform gas (s=0), the BdG equations predict the universal parameter β ≈ -0.41, compared to the Monte Carlo result β ≈ -0.58.
- For s=1, the estimated dipole frequency along the lattice direction satisfies 0.89 ≤ ω_D/ω_z ≤ 0.96; for s=2.5, 0.59 ≤ ω_D/ω_z ≤ 0.66; for s=5, 0.26 ≤ ω_D/ω_z ≤ 0.28.
- In the low-density limit (E_F/E_R → 0), the effective mass m* for a given s lies between the effective mass of a single atom of mass m in the same lattice and the effective mass of a point-like molecule of mass 2m (e.g., for s=2.5, the single-atom value is m*/m = 1.20 and the point-like molecule value is m*/m = 5.53).
- The sound velocity c_s is significantly reduced compared to the uniform system, with results shown for s=1, 2.5, and 5 as functions of E_F/E_R.
- For a trapped gas with s=5, the central density is n(0) = 0.0869 q_B³, corresponding to E_F/E_R = 1.88.

## Context

This work extends the study of Fermi superfluids in one-dimensional optical lattices, following experiments by Miller et al. that measured critical velocities along the BCS-BEC crossover. The BdG approach captures basic features of the unitary regime where prior ab initio Monte Carlo simulations (QMC) exist for uniform systems, and the authors compare their results to the ideal Fermi gas in the same lattice and to bosonic atoms in a lattice (Ref. boson). The predictions enable experimental tests via density profile measurements and collective oscillation frequencies, and the authors note that further work on finite quasi-momenta and 2D effects at large laser intensities is in progress.
