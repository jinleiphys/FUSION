# OMR-NPA: Optimized Matrix Representation of Nucleon Pair Approximation

> We optimize the matrix representation of the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA) of the nuclear shell model. The NPA is a widely adopted truncation approach of the nuclear shell model and proves to be effective in describing low-lying states of medium-heavy and heavy nuclei. Due to simplified (yet flexible) commutators and absolute elimination of angular momentum coupling, the matrix representation provides a formalism for the $M$-scheme NPA more efficient than others as far as we know. It also enables the practicable organization and storage design for intermediate results, including generated collective pairs, matrix products, and matrix traces, so that further optimization is achieved by reducing repetitive matrix operations, which are the most time-consuming procedures in the matrix-represented $M$-scheme NPA. We also describe optimizations specified for the $M$-scheme NPA, realized by invoking the Wigner-Eckart theorem, time-reversal symmetry, and conjugate operation of spherical tensors. Our optimization makes the combination of matrix representation and NPA more profitable. Such an implementation denoted by optimized matrix representation of NPA (OMR-NPA) is publicly released with open source. Its performance is analyzed and compared against unoptimized NPA codes.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their optimized matrix representation of the nucleon-pair approximation (OMR-NPA) provides a formalism for the M-scheme NPA that is more efficient than other approaches, but this efficiency gain is conditional on the use of simplified commutators, elimination of angular momentum coupling, and a storage/retrieval scheme that reduces repetitive matrix operations, which are the most time-consuming procedures.

## Method

The authors encode collective pair structural matrices and single-particle vectors as unsigned char arrays representing matrix products, enabling string-matching to identify repetitive matrix multiplications. They implement a binary tree data structure to store and retrieve intermediate matrix products, using the array length as a criterion to decide which products to retain. Additional optimizations exploit the Wigner-Eckart theorem, time-reversal symmetry, conjugate operations of spherical tensors, and the one-hot property of single-particle vectors in odd-nucleon systems to convert trace calculations into inner products or single matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The matrix representation of a collective pair requires only a few bytes when encoded as an unsigned char array, compared to 8N^2 bytes for conventional double-float storage, where N is the number of single-particle orbits (N >= 20 for usual NPA calculations).
- Trace calculation with a symmetric or anti-symmetric matrix can be reduced to an inner product of two vectors with O(n^2) time complexity, instead of O(n^3) for matrix multiplication.
- The upper limit L_max is set as the maximum array length for stored matrix products, with products having shorter arrays (e.g., length 4 for p1^n1 p2^n2) being more frequently involved than longer ones (e.g., length 6 for p1^n1 p2^n2 p3^n3).

## Context

This work extends the matrix representation approach previously developed by Lei and collaborators for collective pair condensate variation (Lei, 2020) and later applied to M-scheme NPA (Lei and Lu, 2021; Luo, 2020). It contrasts with traditional J-scheme NPA, which requires angular momentum recoupling and heavy computation of angular momentum algebra, and with the seniority scheme (Racah, 1943; Talmi, 1952; de-Shalit, 1953) and Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer theory (1957) that limit two-body degrees of freedom or violate particle-number conservation. The OMR-NPA enables more efficient calculations of low-lying states in medium-heavy and heavy nuclei by making the combination of matrix representation and NPA more profitable through dynamic programming of intermediate results.
