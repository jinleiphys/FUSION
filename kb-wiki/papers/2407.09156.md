# High-precision analysis of the critical point in heavy-quark QCD at $N_t=6$

> Binder-cumulant analysis of the critical point in the heavy-quark region of QCD is performed by Monte-Carlo simulations with the hopping-parameter expansion at $N_t=6$. We extend our previous analysis at $N_t=4$ to finer lattices and perform high-precision analyses on large spatial volumes up to the aspect ratio $LT=N_s/N_t=18$. Higher order terms in the hopping-parameter expansion are incorporated effectively up to 14th order. The numerical results show that the violation of the finite-size scaling becomes more prominent on the finer lattice at a given aspect ratio.

## Key claim

The Binder-cumulant analysis of the critical point in the heavy-quark region of QCD at a finer lattice spacing (temporal extent \(N_t=6\)) shows that the violation of finite-size scaling becomes more prominent than on coarser lattices (\(N_t=4\)) at the same aspect ratio, and that the critical point is located at \(\lambda_c \simeq 0.0007\) after incorporating higher-order hopping-parameter expansion terms up to 14th order.

## Method

Monte-Carlo simulations were performed using the hopping-parameter expansion (HPE) for the quark determinant, with gauge configurations generated by a pseudo-heat-bath algorithm for the leading-order action \(S_{g+\text{LO}}\). Higher-order terms in the HPE (up to 14th order) were incorporated effectively by reweighting, using linear correlations between Wilson loops and Polyakov-loop-type loops to approximate the action as \(S_{\text{eff [NLO]}}^{(n_W,n_L)}\) with \((n_W,n_L) = (10,14)\). The Binder cumulant \(B_4\) of the real part of the Polyakov loop was measured on lattices with temporal extent \(N_t=6\) and spatial volumes up to aspect ratio \(LT = N_s/N_t = 18\), and its minimum as a function of the gauge coupling was used to locate the transition point.

## Key numbers

- The critical point is located at \(\lambda_c = 7.047(38)(3) \times 10^{-4}\) from a fit using Eq. (4) with \(LT = 18, 15, 12, 10\) (first error statistical, second systematic).
- The critical exponent \(\nu\) from the same fit is \(0.621(11)(3)\), consistent with the \(Z(2)\) universality class value \(\nu = 0.630\).
- The universal Binder cumulant value at the critical point is \(b_4 = 1.6295(55)(5)\) from the fit with \(LT = 18, 15, 12, 10\), compared to the \(Z(2)\) value \(b_4 = 1.604\).
- Simulations were performed on lattices with aspect ratios \(LT = 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 12, 15, 18\) and spatial sizes up to \(N_s = 108\) (since \(N_t=6\) and \(LT=18\) gives \(N_s = 108\)).
- The hopping-parameter expansion coefficients were determined from 100 gauge configurations on a \(36^3 \times 6\) lattice near the critical point, with 5,000 noise vectors for measurements.

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Kiyohara et al. (2021) at \(N_t=4\) to a finer lattice with \(N_t=6\), using larger spatial volumes up to \(LT=18\) (compared to \(LT=12\) in the earlier study). It employs a method proposed by Wakabayashi et al. (2021) to incorporate higher-order HPE terms effectively, which is necessary because the HPE convergence worsens as the continuum limit is approached. The results show that finite-size scaling violations are more severe on finer lattices, which may explain difficulties in reproducing the expected \(Z(2)\) scaling behavior seen in earlier work by Cuteri et al. (2020) and Kiyohara et al. (2021). This enables high-precision determination of the critical point location and tests of universality in the heavy-quark region of QCD.
