# Highlights of Recent Results with Clas

> Recent results on the study of the electromagnetic structure of nucleon resonances, the spin structure of proton and neutrons at small and intermediate photon virtualities, and the search for exotic pentaquark baryons are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents recent CLAS results on nucleon resonance structure, spin structure, and pentaquark searches, but the pentaquark evidence remains inconclusive: a peak at 1555 ± 10 MeV is observed in one channel but with limited statistics and sizeable background, and dedicated higher-statistics experiments are still underway.

## Method

The CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab measured inclusive and semi-inclusive polarized electron scattering on hydrogen and deuterium targets at beam energies from 1.6 to 5.75 GeV to extract spin structure functions and their moments. For nucleon resonances, differential cross sections and beam asymmetries for single-pion and two-pion electroproduction were analyzed using unitary isobar models and dispersion relation approaches to extract transition form factors and helicity amplitudes. Pentaquark searches used exclusive final-state reconstruction in photoproduction reactions on hydrogen and deuterium targets at photon energies from 3 to 5.75 GeV.

## Key numbers

- First moment Γ₁ᵖ(Q²) shows a zero crossing near Q² = 0.3 GeV², with strong Q² dependence below Q² = 1.5 GeV²
- Bjorken integral Γ₁^{p-n} determined in the range Q² = 0.06 – 2.5 GeV²
- R_EM = E₁₊/M₁₊ for the N→Δ(1232) transition remains negative and small over Q² = 0.4 – 2 GeV², with systematic uncertainties currently ±2% absolute
- The Roper P₁₁(1440) transverse photocoupling amplitude A₁/₂(Q²) changes sign near Q² = 0.5 GeV²
- A peak in the nK⁺ invariant mass distribution is observed at 1555 ± 10 MeV in γp → π⁺K⁺K⁻n at photon energies 3 – 5.4 GeV
- Electroproduction total cross section for pπ⁺π⁻ shows a discrepancy near W = 1.7 GeV at Q² values of 0.65, 0.95, and 1.3 GeV²

## Context

This work extends measurements of nucleon spin structure from the deep inelastic regime into the transition region where resonance effects and higher twists dominate, connecting to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule at Q²=0 and to leading-twist pQCD at higher Q². The N→Δ transition form factor results agree with lattice QCD calculations by Alexandrou (2003, 2004) and dynamical models, indicating a small oblate deformation of the Δ(1232). The Roper P₁₁(1440) results challenge non-relativistic quark models and support a picture with a small quark core and large meson cloud as in Cano (1998). The pentaquark search follows the chiral soliton model prediction by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and aims to confirm or refute the Θ⁺(1540) with dedicated higher-statistics experiments g10, g11, g12, and eg3, which will enable determination of production mechanisms, spin, and possible excited states.
