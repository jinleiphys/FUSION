# Effects of Multiple Scattering in Cold Nuclear Matter on J/psi Suppression and <p_T^2> in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Coherent multiple scatterings of c and cbar quark pairs in the environment of heavy ion collisions have been used in a previous work by Qiu et al. [Qiu:1998rz] to study J/psi suppression. That model suggests that heavy quark re-scatterings in a cold nuclear medium can completely explain the centrality dependence of the observed J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS [Abreu:2001kd]. Their calculations also revealed significant differences under the assumptions of a color singlet or color octet production mechanism. A more recent analytic calculation [Fujii:2003fq], which includes incoherent final-state re-scatterings with explicit momentum transfer fluctuations in three dimensions, indicates much less suppression and little sensitivity to the production mechanism. In this article, we study simultaneously both the J/psi suppression and p_T modifications, at SPS and RHIC energies. We mainly focus on incoherent momentum transfer fluctuations in two dimensions, which is more appropriate for the heavy-ion collision kinematics. Our analytic and Monte-Carlo calculations reinforce the analytic results in [Fujii:2003fq]. Additionally, we find that the experimental J/psi suppression and <p_T^2> from nucleus-nucleus collisions at the SPS or RHIC cannot simultaneously be described in this incoherent multiple scattering framework for any value of the fluctuation strength parameter <k_T^2>.

# Effects of Multiple Scattering in Cold Nuclear Matter on J/psi Suppression and <p_T^2> in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that an incoherent multiple scattering model with two-dimensional Gaussian momentum transfer fluctuations cannot simultaneously describe both the measured J/psi suppression and the increase in mean transverse momentum squared (<p_T^2>) in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS or RHIC energies, for any value of the fluctuation strength parameter <k_T^2>. This contradicts the earlier claim by Qiu et al. (1998) that coherent multiple scatterings in cold nuclear matter could fully explain the centrality dependence of J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte-Carlo Glauber model that generates c-cbar pairs from PYTHIA events at the appropriate collision energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 17.2 GeV for SPS and 200 GeV for RHIC), then simulates incoherent Gaussian momentum kicks in two dimensions transverse to the beam axis for each quark independently as they traverse cold nuclear matter. The pathlength L through nuclear matter is calculated by integrating over the nuclear density distribution, and final-state scatterings are modeled via a 2D Gaussian random walk where the number of scatterings is quantized in terms of binary collisions. The effective formation probability for J/psi is computed using both color singlet and color octet transition probability parameterizations from Qiu et al. (1998), and the results for suppression and <p_T^2> are compared to NA50 data at SPS and PHENIX data at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.2 GeV, the relationship between <k_T^2> and epsilon^2 (average increase in q^2 per unit nuclear pathlength) is tabulated: <k_T^2> = 0.1 GeV^2 corresponds to epsilon^2 = 0.09 GeV^2/fm; <k_T^2> = 0.3 GeV^2 corresponds to epsilon^2 = 0.27 GeV^2/fm; <k_T^2> = 0.4 GeV^2 corresponds to epsilon^2 = 0.36 GeV^2/fm.

- For d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the model with <k_T^2> = 0.3 GeV^2 gives R_dAu = 0.84 and Delta<p_T^2> = 2.4 GeV^2; with <k_T^2> = 0.075 GeV^2 gives R_dAu = 0.95 and Delta<p_T^2> = 0.6 GeV^2.

- The PHENIX collaboration reports for minimum bias d+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: Delta<p_T^2> = 1.77 +/- 0.37 (backward), 1.12 +/- 0.33 (forward), -1.28 +/- 0.94 (midrapidity); R_dAu = 1.18 +/- 0.12 (backward), 0.79 +/- 0.06 (forward), 0.95 +/- 0.1 (midrapidity).

- For central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with <k_T^2> = 0.3 GeV^2, the model gives R_AA approximately 0.7 and Delta<p_T^2> approximately 5 GeV^2.

- The constant shift approximation gives suppression factor R_psi = exp[-L epsilon^2/(2 alpha_F^2)], while the partial fluctuation approximation gives R_psi = 2 alpha_F^2/(2 alpha_F^2 + L epsilon^2), where alpha_F = 1.15 for the color singlet case.

## Context

This work extends the analytic calculations of Fujii (2003), who studied incoherent final-state re-scatterings with three-dimensional momentum transfer fluctuations and found much less suppression than the coherent scattering model of Qiu, Vary, and Zhang (1998). The authors improve upon Fujii's work by using a more realistic two-dimensional random walk (appropriate for heavy-ion collision kinematics) and by simultaneously studying both J/psi suppression and p_T modifications, which the earlier calculations did not address. The results contradict the Qiu et al. (1998) claim that cold nuclear matter effects alone could explain the anomalous J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS, and instead suggest that hot medium effects must be invoked. The authors note that their framework could be extended to study nuclear effects on other heavy-flavor mesons such as bottomonium states and open charm and bottom.
