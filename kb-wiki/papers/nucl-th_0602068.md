# Effects of Multiple Scattering in Cold Nuclear Matter on J/psi Suppression and <p_T^2> in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Coherent multiple scatterings of c and cbar quark pairs in the environment of heavy ion collisions have been used in a previous work by Qiu et al. [Qiu:1998rz] to study J/psi suppression. That model suggests that heavy quark re-scatterings in a cold nuclear medium can completely explain the centrality dependence of the observed J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS [Abreu:2001kd]. Their calculations also revealed significant differences under the assumptions of a color singlet or color octet production mechanism. A more recent analytic calculation [Fujii:2003fq], which includes incoherent final-state re-scatterings with explicit momentum transfer fluctuations in three dimensions, indicates much less suppression and little sensitivity to the production mechanism. In this article, we study simultaneously both the J/psi suppression and p_T modifications, at SPS and RHIC energies. We mainly focus on incoherent momentum transfer fluctuations in two dimensions, which is more appropriate for the heavy-ion collision kinematics. Our analytic and Monte-Carlo calculations reinforce the analytic results in [Fujii:2003fq]. Additionally, we find that the experimental J/psi suppression and <p_T^2> from nucleus-nucleus collisions at the SPS or RHIC cannot simultaneously be described in this incoherent multiple scattering framework for any value of the fluctuation strength parameter <k_T^2>.

# Effects of Multiple Scattering in Cold Nuclear Matter on J/psi Suppression and <p_T^2> in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that an incoherent multiple scattering model with two-dimensional Gaussian momentum transfer fluctuations cannot simultaneously describe both the measured J/psi suppression and the mean transverse momentum squared (<p_T^2>) in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS or RHIC energies, for any value of the fluctuation strength parameter <k_T^2>. This contradicts the earlier coherent scattering model by Qiu, Vary, and Zhang (1998) which could explain the centrality dependence of J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS using a constant shift in q^2.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte-Carlo Glauber model that generates c-cbar pairs from PYTHIA events at the appropriate collision energies, then simulates incoherent Gaussian momentum kicks in two dimensions transverse to the beam axis for each quark independently as they traverse cold nuclear matter. The model calculates both the J/psi suppression factor (R_AA) and the change in mean transverse momentum squared (Delta <p_T^2>) as functions of centrality for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.2 GeV and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, using color octet transition probabilities and varying the average momentum transfer per scattering <k_T^2>.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.2 GeV, the conversion between <k_T^2> and epsilon^2 (average increase in q^2 per unit pathlength) is tabulated: <k_T^2> = 0.1 GeV^2 corresponds to epsilon^2 = 0.09 GeV^2/fm; <k_T^2> = 0.3 GeV^2 corresponds to epsilon^2 = 0.27 GeV^2/fm; <k_T^2> = 0.4 GeV^2 corresponds to epsilon^2 = 0.36 GeV^2/fm

- For d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the model with <k_T^2> = 0.3 GeV^2 gives R_dAu = 0.84 and Delta <p_T^2> = 2.4 GeV^2; with <k_T^2> = 0.075 GeV^2 gives R_dAu = 0.95 and Delta <p_T^2> = 0.6 GeV^2

- For central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with <k_T^2> = 0.3 GeV^2, the model predicts R_AA approximately 0.7 and Delta <p_T^2> approximately 5 GeV^2

- The FermiLab data on dijet momentum imbalance constrains epsilon^2 to approximately 0.2-0.5 GeV^2/fm

- For the color singlet case with the constant shift approximation, the suppression factor is R_jpsi = exp[-L epsilon^2/(2 alpha_F^2)]; with partial fluctuations it becomes R_jpsi = 2 alpha_F^2 / (2 alpha_F^2 + L epsilon^2)

## Context

This work extends the analytic calculations by Fujii (2003) which considered incoherent final-state re-scatterings with three-dimensional momentum transfer fluctuations, finding much less suppression than the coherent model of Qiu, Vary, and Zhang (1998). The authors improve upon Fujii's work by using two-dimensional fluctuations (more appropriate for heavy-ion collision kinematics) and by simultaneously studying both J/psi suppression and p_T modifications. The results contradict the earlier QVZ model which could explain the NA50 data on anomalous J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS using a constant q^2 shift. The paper enables future work incorporating hot medium effects through parton cascade models, and could be extended to study nuclear effects on other heavy-flavor mesons such as b-bbar states and open charm and bottom.
