# Open charm production and $Λ_{c}^{+}/D^{0}$ ratio in pp and Au+Au collisions at the RHIC

> We study open charm hadrons production in pp and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200$~GeV using an improved a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model. Specifically, we show the transverse-momentum spectra and nuclear modification factors $R_{\mathrm{AA}}$ of $D^{0}$ mesons and $Λ_{c}^{+}$ baryons, as well as the $Λ_{c}^{+}/D^{0}$ ratio in pp and Au+Au collisions. The results obtained from the AMPT model simulations are compared with the STAR experimental data and found to be consistent. We further investigate the $Λ_{c}^{+}/D^{0}$ ratio by evaluating contributions from coalescence, fragmentation, and the combined coalescence+fragmentation mechanisms, and we find that fragmentation alone underestimates the pronounced enhancement in Au+Au relative to pp at low and intermediate $p_{\mathrm{T}}$, whereas the coalescence+fragmentation mechanism reproduces the observed trend significantly better. These results indicate that coalescence plays a key role in charm baryon productions and helps constrain the relative importance of different hadronization mechanisms in the ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of the $\Lambda_{c}^{+}/D^{0}$ ratio in Au+Au collisions relative to pp at low and intermediate transverse momentum is driven by charm-light quark coalescence, but the quantitative agreement with data depends on a transverse-mass threshold selection that differs between pp and Au+Au systems, meaning the hadronization parameters are not uniquely constrained.

## Method

The authors used an improved string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, where charm-anticharm pairs are extracted directly from HIJING initial hard scatterings rather than from the generic string-melting procedure, and an initial-state Cronin momentum broadening is applied. Charm quarks propagate through the deconfined medium via Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) and then hadronize through a hybrid scheme combining spatial quark coalescence (with constraints on relative distance and invariant mass) and independent fragmentation using the Peterson fragmentation function, with a transverse-mass threshold selection to separate the two regimes.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200$ GeV for both pp and Au+Au collisions
- Coalescence distance parameter: $p_{\mathrm{r}} = 0.5$ fm for both pp and Au+Au
- Coalescence invariant mass parameter: $p_{\mathrm{m}} = 0.5$ for both pp and Au+Au
- Transverse-mass threshold: $m_{\mathrm{T}} = 2.0$ for pp and $m_{\mathrm{T}} = 3.3$ for Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200$ GeV
- Heavy flavor baryon-to-meson ratio parameter: $r_{\mathrm{BM}}^{\mathrm{HQ}} = 2$ for charm
- Peterson fragmentation parameter: $\epsilon_{\mathrm{Q}} = 0.05$ for charm quarks
- $\Lambda_{c}^{+}/D^{0}$ ratio in 10-80% Au+Au collisions: values of order 0.5-0.8 in the range $3 \lesssim p_{\mathrm{T}} \lesssim 6$ GeV/$c$
- Cronin effect parameter: $\delta = 1$ constrained from $D^{0}$ spectra in d+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends the string-melting AMPT model framework previously developed by Lin (2004) and improved by Zhang (2022, 2024) to include a hybrid coalescence-fragmentation hadronization scheme for charm quarks, building on the coalescence paradigm established in the light-flavor sector. The results are compared with STAR experimental data for $D^{0}$ mesons (STAR, 2012, 2014, 2018) and $\Lambda_{c}^{+}$ baryons (STAR, 2019) at RHIC, and the finding that coalescence drives the baryon enhancement at intermediate $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ contrasts with pure fragmentation models like FONLL (Cacciari, 2005) which underestimate the $\Lambda_{c}^{+}/D^{0}$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions. Future measurements of the $\Lambda_{c}^{+}$ nuclear modification factor and its centrality dependence at RHIC energies will provide a direct test of the coalescence-dominated hadronization scenario proposed here.
