# Back-to-back azimuthal correlations in Z+jet events at high transverse momentum in the TMD parton branching method at next-to-leading order

> Azimuthal correlations in Z+jet production at large transverse momenta are computed by matching Parton - Branching (PB) TMD parton distributions and showers with NLO calculations via MCatNLO. The predictions are compared with those for dijet production in the same kinematic range. The azimuthal correlations $Δϕ$ between the Z boson and the leading jet are steeper compared to those in dijet production at transverse momenta ${\cal O}(100)$ GeV, while they become similar for very high transverse momenta ${\cal O}(1000)$ GeV. The different patterns of Z+jet and dijet azimuthal correlations can be used to search for potential {\it factorization - breaking} effects in the back-to-back region, which depend on the different color and spin structure of the final states and their interferences with the initial states. In order to investigate these effects experimentally, we propose to measure the ratio of the distributions in $Δϕ$ for Z+jet - and multijet production at low and at high transverse momenta, and compare the results to predictions obtained assuming factorization. We examine the role of theoretical uncertainties by performing variations of the factorization scale, renormalization scale and matching scale. In particular, we present a comparative study of matching scale uncertainties in the cases of PB-TMD and collinear parton showers.

## Key claim
The paper shows that azimuthal correlations between Z bosons and leading jets are steeper (more strongly correlated) than dijet correlations at transverse momenta of order 100 GeV, but become similar at very high transverse momenta of order 1000 GeV, with the caveat that this behavior arises primarily from differences in initial-state parton flavor composition rather than from final-state effects.

## Method
The authors match Parton-Branching (PB) transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton distributions and showers with next-to-leading order (NLO) matrix elements via the MCatNLO framework, using the PB-NLO-2018-Set2 TMD PDFs. Events are processed through the Cascade3 Monte Carlo for TMD initial-state showering, with jets reconstructed using the anti-kt algorithm with distance parameter R=0.4 in the rapidity range |y|<2.4. The matching scale, factorization scale, and renormalization scale are varied to assess theoretical uncertainties, and comparisons are made between predictions using Cascade3 and Pythia8 parton showers.

## Key numbers
- Azimuthal correlations are compared at leading transverse momenta ptmax > 200 GeV and ptmax > 1000 GeV
- The strong coupling is set to alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.118
- Jets are reconstructed with distance parameter R=0.4 and rapidity range |y|<2.4
- Matching scale variations of a factor of two up and down produce approximately 5% changes in predictions
- The transverse momentum imbalance scale in the back-to-back region at high pt is of order several tens of GeV, compared to a few GeV at lower pt

## Context
This work extends the PB-TMD method previously applied to Drell-Yan transverse momentum spectra (Martinez 2019, Bermudez Martinez 2020) and dijet azimuthal correlations (Abdulhamid 2021) to Z+jet production. The predictions are compared with CMS measurements of dijet correlations (Sirunyan 2017, Sirunyan 2019). The study enables experimental searches for factorization-breaking effects by comparing Z+jet and multijet azimuthal correlations, as proposed theoretically by Rogers (2013) and studied in TMD factorization approaches by Chien (2022). The authors propose measuring the ratio of Delta-phi distributions between Z+jet and multijet production at low and high transverse momenta to test factorization assumptions.
