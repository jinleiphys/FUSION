# Hadron production in deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering

> Predictions for semi-inclusive deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering are presented. Both the effects of gluon radiation by the struck quark and the absorption of the produced hadron are considered. The gluon radiation covers a larger window in virtuality $Q^2$ because of the increased deconfinement of quarks inside nuclei. The absorption of hadrons formed inside the nucleus is described with a flavor dependent cross section. Calculations for rescaled fragmentation functions and nuclear absorption are compared with the EMC and HERMES data for N, Cu and Kr targets with respect to the deuteron target. Predictions for Ne and Xe targets in the HERMES kinematic regime are given.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the modification of fragmentation functions in nuclei due to gluon radiation, combined with nuclear absorption of produced hadrons, can explain semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering data, but only when using a "partial deconfinement" rescaling model rather than a maximal one; the partial deconfinement model overestimates data at low energy transfer and low momentum fraction, requiring additional absorption effects.

## Method

The authors compute hadron multiplicity ratios between nuclear targets and deuterium using leading-order perturbative QCD with rescaled parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions, where the rescaling factor depends on an assumed deconfinement scale in the nucleus. They then supplement this with a Lund model description of hadron formation lengths and a flavor-dependent nuclear absorption cross section for the produced hadrons and prehadronic states, comparing the combined calculation to EMC and HERMES data for nitrogen, copper, and krypton targets.

## Key numbers

- The partial deconfinement (PD) model gives a scale factor sqrt(xi_A) at Q=1.5 GeV of 1.164 for iron-56 and 1.195 for xenon-131; the maximal deconfinement (MD) model gives 8.47 for iron-56 and 12.3 for xenon-131 at the same Q.
- The deconfinement ratio lambda_A/lambda_0 in the PD model is 1.100 for iron-56 and 1.118 for xenon-131; in the MD model it is 4.42 for iron-56 and 5.68 for xenon-131.
- The string tension in the nucleus is related to the vacuum value by kappa_A * lambda_A^2 = kappa * lambda_0^2, with kappa approximately 1 GeV/fm in vacuum.
- The Lund model parameter C is set to 0.3 for the prehadron formation length distribution.
- The average formation length at z approaching 1 behaves as (1-z)*nu/kappa.

## Context

This work extends the rescaling models previously applied to nuclear structure functions by Close et al. (1984), Date et al. (1986), and Nachtmann and Pirner (1984, 1987) to also modify fragmentation functions in nuclei. It contrasts with the maximal deconfinement model of Nachtmann and Pirner (1984) by adopting a reduced deconfinement scale based on the analysis of Sick et al. (2002), which found an upper limit of about 10% deconfinement on iron. The absorption treatment builds on the work of Bialas and Gyulassy (1987) and Bialas and Chmaj (1983), and the paper provides predictions for neon and xenon targets in the HERMES kinematic regime that can be tested by future experiments.
