# MesonNet Workshop on Meson Transition Form Factors

> The mini-proceedings of the Workshop on Meson Transition Form Factors held in Cracow from May 29th to 30th, 2012 introduce the meson transition form factor project with special emphasis on the interrelations between the various form factors (on-shell, single off-shell, double off-shell). Short summaries of the talks presented at the workshop follow.

## Key claim

The workshop focused on the interrelations between on-shell, single off-shell, and double off-shell meson transition form factors, with the goal of improving the Standard Model prediction for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, where the current uncertainty of approximately $3.9 \times 10^{-10}$ will soon limit the precision of the entire hadronic contribution.

## Method

The workshop compiled and compared experimental data and theoretical predictions for transition form factors of pseudoscalar mesons (pi0, eta, eta') and vector mesons (omega, phi, rho), including Dalitz decays, two-photon production, and e+e- annihilation processes. The naive Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model was used as a baseline for comparison, with the form factor expressed as a sum over vector meson propagators weighted by SU(3) flavor couplings. Experimental slope parameters b_P(q^2) were extracted from single off-shell form factor data using a single-pole approximation.

## Key numbers

- Hadronic light-by-light contribution to muon g-2: a_mu^{LbL,had} = (10.5 +/- 2.6) x 10^{-10} from Prades (2009), and (11.6 +/- 3.9) x 10^{-10} from Jegerlehner (2009)
- Pion transition form factor slope at q^2=0: b_pi0(0) = 1.79 +/- 0.14 GeV^{-2} from CELLO (Behrend 1990)
- Pion transition form factor slope at q^2=0.613 GeV^2: b_pi0(0.613) = 2.241 +/- 0.025 +/- 0.028 GeV^{-2} from NA60 (Usai 2011)
- Eta transition form factor slope at q^2=0: b_eta(0) = 1.950 +/- 0.059 +/- 0.042 GeV^{-2} from NA60 (Usai 2011)
- Branching fraction of pi0 -> e+e-: (7.48 +/- 0.29 +/- 0.25) x 10^{-8} from KTeV, compared to theoretical predictions of (6.33 +/- 0.19) x 10^{-8} from Ametller (1993) and (6.2 +/- 0.1) x 10^{-8} from Dorokhov, showing a more than 3 sigma discrepancy
- Omega -> pi0 mu+ mu- branching fraction: (1.73 +/- 0.25 +/- 0.14) x 10^{-4} from NA60 (Arnaldi 2009), compared to VMD predictions of (1.16 +/- 0.07) x 10^{-4} from Terschluesen (2010)

## Context

This workshop extended the ongoing discussion of meson transition form factors motivated by the muon g-2 discrepancy, building on earlier work by Hayakawa (1997), Bijnens (2007), and Jegerlehner (2009) on hadronic light-by-light scattering. The meeting highlighted tensions between experimental data and naive VMD predictions, particularly in omega -> pi0 mu+ mu- decays (Arnaldi 2009) and the pi0 -> e+e- branching fraction, and identified the need for improved theoretical calculations using dispersion theory (Schneider 2012) and lattice QCD (Feng 2011) to resolve these discrepancies and enable more precise Standard Model predictions.
