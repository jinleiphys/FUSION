# Reduced transition probabilities for the gamma decay of the 7.8 eV isomer in $^{229}$Th

> The reduced magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole transition probabilities for the radiative decay of the $^{229}$Th 7.8 eV isomer to the ground state are predicted within a detailed nuclear-structure model approach. We show that the presence and decay of this isomer can only be accounted for by the Coriolis mixing emerging from a remarkably fine interplay between the coherent quadrupole-octupole motion of the nuclear core and the single-nucleon motion within a reflection-asymmetric deformed potential. We find that the magnetic dipole transition probability which determines the radiative lifetime of the isomer is considerably smaller than presently estimated. The so-far disregarded electric quadrupole component may have non-negligible contributions to the internal conversion channel. These findings support new directions in the experimental search of the $^{229}$Th transition frequency for the development of a future nuclear frequency standard.

## Key claim

The magnetic dipole transition probability for the 7.8 eV isomer decay in $^{229}$Th is predicted to be considerably smaller than previously estimated, with the Coriolis interaction, not previously considered in this context, being the mechanism that allows the otherwise forbidden M1 and E2 inter-band isomeric transition to occur.

## Method

The authors applied a coherent quadrupole-octupole model (CQOM) coupled to a deformed shell model (DSM) with a reflection-asymmetric Woods-Saxon potential, pairing correlations of Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer type, and a fully microscopic treatment of the Coriolis interaction via perturbation theory. The model was fitted to the low-lying experimental spectrum of $^{229}$Th below 400 keV, including positive- and negative-parity levels, to constrain six CQOM parameters. The reduced transition probabilities were then calculated using Coriolis-perturbed wave functions that include first-order and second-order K-mixing effects.

## Key numbers

- $B(M1) = 0.0076$ W.u. (Th1) and $0.0061$ W.u. (Th2) for the $3/2^{+}_{\text{ex}} \rightarrow 5/2^{+}_{\text{yr}}$ isomeric transition, about one order of magnitude lower than the 0.048 W.u. value from Dyk98 and a factor of two lower than the 0.014 W.u. from QPM (Ruch06)
- $B(E2) = 27.04$ W.u. (Th1) and $23.05$ W.u. (Th2) for the same transition, approximately a factor of 2 smaller than the only other available prediction of 54 W.u. from Ruch06
- The predicted $B(E2)$ and $B(M1)$ values vary within stable limits of 20–30 W.u. and 0.006–0.008 W.u., respectively, across different model parameter combinations
- The theoretical $3/2^{+}$ isomer energy was obtained as 0.4 keV, with root mean square deviations of 39.9 keV for the yrast band and 26 keV for the isomer-based band (total rms = 34 keV)
- For the neighboring isotope $^{231}$Th, the model achieved an energy rms value of 26 keV using the same six parameter values

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical efforts on the $^{229}$Th isomer decay, which relied on branching ratios from Alaga rules (Alaga55, Dyk98, Tkalya15) and the quasiparticle-plus-phonon model (QPM) (Gulda02, Ruch06). The present approach differs fundamentally from QPM by treating quadrupole and octupole modes on the same footing in a reflection-asymmetric deformed potential, rather than assuming a reflection-symmetric mean field with octupole phonon admixtures. The results contradict earlier claims (Dyk98, Tkalya15) that Coriolis mixing is weak, and instead support the role of Coriolis mixing assumed in QPM calculations. The predicted lower $B(M1)$ value may explain experimental difficulties in observing the radiative decay (Jeet15, Yamaguchi, LarsThesis2016), while the $E2$ component could become dominant over $M1$ for internal conversion involving $7p$, $6d$, or $5f$ electronic orbitals, supporting experimental efforts focused on IC electron observation (Seiferle2017).
