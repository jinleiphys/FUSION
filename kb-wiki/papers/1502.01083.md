# Emergence of rotational bands in ab initio no-core configuration interaction calculations

> Rotational bands have been observed to emerge in ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for p-shell nuclei, as evidenced by rotational patterns for excitation energies, electromagnetic moments, and electromagnetic transitions. We investigate the ab initio emergence of nuclear rotation in the Be isotopes, focusing on 9Be for illustration, and make use of basis extrapolation methods to obtain ab initio predictions of rotational band parameters for comparison with experiment. We find robust signatures for rotational motion, which reproduce both qualitative and quantitative features of the experimentally observed bands.

## Key claim

Rotational bands robustly emerge in ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for p-shell nuclei, even in incompletely converged calculations, and reproduce both qualitative and quantitative features of experimentally observed bands, though the excitation energies converge at rates that vary considerably among bands and remain too strongly dependent on basis truncation to immediately determine how close converged predictions will lie to experimental values.

## Method

The authors performed NCCI calculations for Be isotopes (focusing on 9Be) using the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the Coulomb interaction omitted to ensure exact isospin conservation. Calculations were carried out in a harmonic oscillator basis truncated to a maximum number Nmax of oscillator excitations (Nmax=2 to 10 for natural parity, Nmax=7 to 11 for unnatural parity) at fixed oscillator energy hw=22.5 MeV. An exponential basis extrapolation scheme was applied to estimate converged energy eigenvalues from calculations at three successive Nmax values.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 9Be shows a variational minimum in the range hw ≈ 20 MeV to 25 MeV for each Nmax truncation
- At Nmax=10, changes in the variational minimum energy for 9Be are still at the MeV scale
- The rotational parameter A varies by a factor of approximately 2 across the different calculated bands
- Basis extrapolation was performed using three successive Nmax values for each fixed hw to determine the three parameters (a_hw, b_hw, and E_infinity) in the exponential convergence formula

## Context

This work extends previous observations of rotational band emergence in ab initio NCCI calculations for p-shell nuclei (Maris 2012, Caprio 2013, Maris 2015) by systematically investigating the Be isotopic chain and applying basis extrapolation methods. The results build on earlier demonstrations of collective phenomena in ab initio calculations (Wiringa 2000, Dytrych 2007, Neff 2008, Shimizu 2012, Dytrych 2013). The paper enables future investigation of the physical origin of the rotation, with prospective contributors including multishell SU(3) symmetry, symplectic symmetry (Rowe 1985, Dytrych 2007), and alpha-alpha molecular rotation (Kanada-En'yo 1999).
