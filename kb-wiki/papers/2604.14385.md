# Measurement of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integrand for proton and deuteron from 200 to 1400 MeV

> New data for the total inclusive helicity-dependent cross section for the proton and deuteron were obtained in the photon energy interval 200-1400 MeV. The experiment was performed at the A2 tagged-photon facility of the Mainz Microtron (MAMI) using a circularly polarized photon beam and longitudinally polarized proton and deuteron targets. The reaction products were detected using the large-acceptance Crystal Ball/TAPS calorimeter, which covers 97% of the full solid angle. These new results, obtained with fine energy binning, significantly expand both the quantity and the quality of the available data for these observables and enable a detailed comparison with state-of-the-art theoretical calculations. From the combination of the results for the deuteron and the proton, important information could also be extracted for the free neutron. Based on these data, and using existing models to evaluate the missing contributions from unmeasured photon energy regions, the validity of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule has been verified for the proton, the neutron, and the deuteron. These new data provide a precise experimental benchmark for theoretical models used to study nucleons, both in their free state and when embedded in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule is verified for the proton, neutron, and deuteron using new high-precision data, but only after employing existing models to estimate the contributions from photon energy regions not covered by the measurement (200–1400 MeV).

## Method

The experiment used a circularly polarized photon beam from the A2 tagged-photon facility at MAMI, incident on longitudinally polarized proton (butanol) and deuteron (deuterated butanol) frozen-spin targets. Reaction products were detected inclusively using the Crystal Ball/TAPS calorimeter covering 97% of the full solid angle, and the helicity-dependent cross-section difference Δσ = (σ_P − σ_A) was extracted for both targets from 200 to 1400 MeV photon energy. Model-dependent acceptance corrections, evaluated via GEANT4 simulations using SAID-SM22 and Fix-Arenhövel models for Nπ and Nππ channels, were applied to account for events where all final-state particles escaped detection.

## Key numbers

- The GDH sum rule integral for the proton is 205 μb (theoretical value); the new data, combined with model estimates for unmeasured regions, yield a result consistent with this value within experimental uncertainties.
- The systematic uncertainty on target polarization is ±10% for both proton and deuteron targets.
- The absolute systematic uncertainty on the photon flux is 4%.
- The extrapolation correction uncertainty for the proton starts at approximately 6 μb at E_γ = 200 MeV and decreases quadratically to less than 1 μb for E_γ ≥ 300 MeV.
- The extrapolation correction uncertainty for the deuteron is about twice that estimated for the proton.
- The detector covers 97% of the full solid angle, with a detection efficiency of ≥99% for both charged hadrons and photons from neutral meson decays.
- Data were collected at electron beam energies of 450 MeV and 1557 MeV, with photon energy bin widths of approximately 2 MeV below 400 MeV and 7–9 MeV above 400 MeV for the proton.

## Context

This work extends the earlier GDH collaboration measurements at MAMI (200–800 MeV) and ELSA (700–2900 MeV) reported by Ahr (2000), Dutz (2003, 2004, 2005), and Ahr (2006, 2009), providing significantly improved statistical precision and finer energy binning, especially in the Δ(1232) resonance region. The new data enable detailed comparison with state-of-the-art theoretical calculations including SAID-SM22, BnGa-2019, Jülich-Bonn (JuBo-2025), and the updated 2-PION-MAID framework, and serve as a precise experimental benchmark for studying nucleon properties in free space and in the nuclear medium, with implications for ongoing JLab programs on the GDH integral and helicity-dependent photoabsorption on nuclei.
