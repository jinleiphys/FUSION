# The role of the chiral anomaly in polarized deeply inelastic scattering III: Wess-Zumino-Witten contributions and chiral Ward identities for finite quark mass

> We extend our prior results on the worldline computation of the axial vector-vector-vector (AVV) triangle anomaly in polarized deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) to the finite mass case by computing in addition the pseudoscalar-vector-vector (PVV) triangle graph. For the well-studied QED case, we show explicitly how the off-forward AVV pole exactly cancels an identical PVV pole. We then demonstrate the dramatic difference in QCD due to the chiral condensate, which qualitatively modifies anomalous Ward identities. As in the massless case, the anomaly pole in QCD is canceled by the dynamics of a primordial isosinglet pseudoscalar $\bar η$-meson, whose Wess-Zumino-Witten coupling to the topological charge density shifts the pole to the physical $η^\prime$ mass, with the finite quark mass contribution differing by $O(10\%)$ from the Witten-Veneziano formula. We obtain a compact analytic expression for the finite mass corrections to Shore and Veneziano's result that the proton's net quark helicity $ΔΣ\propto \sqrt{χ_{\rm QCD}' |_{m=0}(0)}$, the forward slope of the topological susceptibility in the chiral limit, and show they are of the order of a few percent. Our prior prediction that the polarized DIS structure function $g_1$ is quenched by sphaleron-like topological transitions at small $x$ is unaffected by quark mass effects. Our results illustrate how worldline computations of anomalous processes, in synergy with lattice computations and nonet chiral perturbation theory, can uncover novel nonperturbative features of QCD at the Electron-Ion collider.

## Key claim

The paper extends prior worldline computations of the axial vector-vector-vector (AVV) triangle anomaly in polarized deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) to the case of finite quark mass, showing that while the AVV pole exactly cancels the pseudoscalar-vector-vector (PVV) pole in QED, this cancellation fails in QCD due to the chiral condensate, which qualitatively modifies the anomalous Ward identities; the finite quark mass corrections to the proton's net quark helicity ΔΣ are of order a few percent, and the prior prediction that the polarized DIS structure function g₁ is quenched by sphaleron-like topological transitions at small x remains unaffected.

## Method

The authors perform worldline computations of the AVV and PVV triangle graphs in the presence of external scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector background fields, deriving the imaginary part of the worldline effective action that captures the chiral anomaly. They then relate these worldline results to the Wess-Zumino-Witten effective action and to chiral Ward identities for two-point correlators of the axial vector current with isosinglet pseudoscalar fields and with the topological charge density, extracting nonperturbative information about the η′ mass and ΔΣ for finite quark mass.

## Key numbers

- The finite quark mass contribution to the η′ mass differs by O(10%) from the Witten-Veneziano formula.
- The finite mass corrections to Shore and Veneziano's result that ΔΣ ∝ √(χ′QCD|m=0(0)) are of the order of a few percent.
- The primordial η decay constant Fη̄ ∝ √Nc, where Nc is the number of colors.
- The η′ mass squared in the chiral limit is given by m̃²η′ = −(2Nf/F²η̄) χYM(0), where χYM(0) is the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility at zero momentum.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior results on the worldline computation of the AVV triangle anomaly in polarized DIS (Tarasov and Venugopalan, 2020, 2021) to finite quark mass, building on the Shore-Veneziano framework (Shore and Veneziano, 1990, 1991, 1997) that relates the proton's net quark helicity to the topological susceptibility of the QCD vacuum. The results enable quantitative predictions for the Electron-Ion Collider and demonstrate how worldline computations can work in synergy with lattice QCD and nonet chiral perturbation theory to uncover novel nonperturbative features of QCD.
