# Muon pairs from In+In collision at SPS energy

> NA60 collaboration has extracted the inverse slope parameters, T_{eff} of the dimuon spectra originating from the In+In collisions at root(s_NN)=17.3 GeV for various invariant mass region. They have observed that the inverse slope parameter as a function of invariant mass of the lepton pair drops beyond the rho-peak. In the present work, first we reproduce the observed invariant mass and transverse momentum spectra of the muon pairs. Then show that the slope parameters extracted from the transverse momentum distributions for various invariant mass region windows can be explained by assuming formation of a partonic phase initially which reverts to hadronic phase through a weak first order phase transition at a temperature T_c ~ 175 MeV. It is observed that a scenario without the formation of a partonic phase does not reproduce the non-monotonic behaviour of the inverse slope parameter non typical of radial flow.

## Key claim

The non-monotonic behavior of the inverse slope parameter \(T_{\mathrm{eff}}\) as a function of dimuon invariant mass, which drops beyond the \(\rho\)-peak, can be explained only by assuming an initial partonic phase that reverts to a hadronic phase through a weak first-order phase transition at \(T_c \sim 175\) MeV; a scenario without partonic phase formation fails to reproduce this behavior.

## Method

The authors used ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry to model the space-time evolution of the fireball. They computed thermal dimuon emission rates from both the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase (via \(q\bar{q}\) annihilation with QCD corrections) and the hadronic phase (using vector meson dominance with in-medium \(\rho\) spectral functions broadened by interactions with mesons and baryons). The theoretical transverse mass and invariant mass spectra were convoluted over the hydrodynamic evolution and compared with NA60 data for semi-central In+In collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV, testing two scenarios: (i) initial QGP transitioning through a mixed phase to hadrons, and (ii) purely hadronic initial state.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature: \(T_c \sim 175\) MeV (assumed for the weak first-order phase transition).
- Initial energy density: \(\epsilon_i = 5.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- Thermalization time: \(\tau_i = 0.6\) fm.
- Freeze-out temperature: \(T_f = 130\) MeV (reproduces the slope of the \(\phi\) spectra from NA60).
- Beam energy: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV (158 A GeV at CERN-SPS).
- Surface thickness parameter: \(\delta = 0.5\) fm.
- For the purely hadronic scenario, average squared sound speed: \(c_s^2 = 0.16\).

## Context

This work extends theoretical efforts to interpret high-quality dimuon data from the NA60 collaboration (2006-2008) for In+In collisions, following earlier studies by Hees (2008), Ruppert (2008), Renk (2008), and Dusling (2008) that analyzed the same data. The key advance is demonstrating that the non-monotonic \(T_{\mathrm{eff}}\) versus \(M\) trend, which is atypical of pure radial flow, requires an initial partonic phase, thereby providing a chronometer of the collision evolution. The results enable future constraints on the QCD transition temperature and the in-medium modification of vector meson spectral functions.
