# DN interaction from the Juelich meson-exchange model

> The $DN$ interaction is studied in close analogy to the meson-exchange $\bar KN$ potential of the Juelich group using SU(4) symmetry constraints. The model generates the $Λ_c$(2595) resonance dynamically as a $DN$ quasi-bound state. Results for $DN$ scattering lengths and cross sections are presented and compared with predictions based on the Weinberg-Tomozawa term. Some features of the $Λ_c$(2595) resonance are also discussed emphasizing the role of the near-by $πΣ_c$ threshold.

## Key claim

The Juelich meson-exchange model for the DN interaction, extended via SU(4) symmetry, dynamically generates the Lambda_c(2595) resonance as a DN quasi-bound state, but the model's description of the resonance's decay into pi+Sigma_c0 and pi-Sigma_c++ channels leaves unexplained events below the nominal thresholds, especially for the CLEO experiment.

## Method

The authors constructed a DN interaction potential in close analogy to the Juelich group's meson-exchange Kbar N model, using SU(4) symmetry constraints and G-parity conservation to fix direct DN contributions and coupled-channel transitions. They included vector meson (rho, omega) exchange and higher-order box diagrams for the DN channel, coupled to pi Lambda_c(2285) and pi Sigma_c(2455) channels via vector-meson exchange, and solved a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation to obtain scattering amplitudes and observables.

## Key numbers

- DN scattering length for isospin I=0: a = -0.41 + i 0.04 fm
- DN scattering length for isospin I=1: a = -2.07 + i 0.57 fm
- The model generates a P_{01} partial wave state at 2804 MeV, just below the DN threshold, tentatively identified with the Lambda_c(2765) resonance
- The SU(4) WT model of Mizutani (2006) obtains an S_{11} state at 2694 MeV with width Gamma = 153 MeV that strongly couples to the DN channel
- The width of Sigma_c+ is roughly 4 MeV; the widths of Sigma_c0 and Sigma_c++ are just 2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Juelich group's meson-exchange Kbar N model (Mueller-Groeling, 1990) to the charm sector using SU(4) symmetry, building on prior coupled-channel meson-baryon models in the charm sector by Tolos (2004), Lutz (2004), Hofmann (2005), Mizutani (2006), and Garcia-Recio (2009). The results are compared with predictions from the leading-order SU(4) Weinberg-Tomozawa term (Mizutani, 2006; Tolos, 2008) and an SU(8) spin-flavor scheme (Garcia-Recio, 2009; Tolos, 2010). The model enables future experimental guidance for PANDA and CBM experiments at FAIR and further studies of D-mesic nuclei and J/psi dissociation in matter.
