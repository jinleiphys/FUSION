# Finding, and not finding, "higher harmonic flows"

> Certain analysis methods have emerged recently that claim to reveal "higher harmonic flows" in more-central åcollisions at the RHIC and LHC. But pQCD calculations describe the same structures quantitatively.

# Finding, and not finding, "higher harmonic flows"

## Key claim

The structures interpreted as "higher harmonic flows" in more-central A-A collisions at RHIC and LHC are quantitatively described by pQCD calculations of jet-related structure, specifically the same-side (SS) 2D peak from dijet fragmentation, rather than by hydrodynamic flow; the claim depends on whether one projects 2D angular correlations onto 1D azimuth and fits with a Fourier series, which necessarily decomposes any azimuthal structure including jet peaks into harmonic terms.

## Method

The author applies a two-component model of p_t spectra and angular correlations (soft component from projectile nucleon dissociation, hard component from parton fragmentation) to 200 GeV p-p and Au-Au collisions, extracting SS 2D peak, away-side (AS) 1D peak, and nonjet (NJ) azimuth quadrupole amplitudes via 2D model fits. The SS 2D peak properties are compared with pQCD-calculated fragment distributions (FDs) from a parton spectrum folded with fragmentation function ensembles described by beta distributions, and the dijet frequency from pQCD is combined with fragment multiplicity to predict hard-component yields versus centrality.

## Key numbers

- The NJ quadrupole amplitude is described by A_Q{2D}(b) = 0.0045 N_bin R(sqrt(s_NN)) epsilon_opt^2, with R a log(sqrt(s_NN)) factor.
- The SS 2D peak volume within acceptance Delta eta = 2 is shown as a function of centrality; the pQCD predicted dijet frequency within Delta eta is shown as a function of centrality.
- For 0-12% central 200 GeV Au-Au collisions, the measured spectrum hard component for pions shows a five-fold suppression at y_t = 5 (p_t approximately 10 GeV/c) relative to the Glauber linear superposition (GLS) reference, described by pQCD with a single parameter Delta q approximately 1 adjusted in the fragmentation function.
- The pQCD description of hadron production at RHIC implies that one third of final-state hadrons in central Au-Au collisions are contained within resolved jets.
- The more-peripheral 50% of the Au-Au total cross section corresponds to transparent collisions; over that transparency interval the NJ azimuth quadrupole increases to 2/3 of its maximum value.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior two-component model studies of p-p spectra and correlations (Trainor, 2009; Porter and Trainor, 2005) and Au-Au spectra and correlations (Trainor, 2008; Trainor, 2010) to directly confront the "higher harmonic flow" interpretation popularized by Fourier series analyses of 1D azimuth projections (e.g., Alver and Roland, 2010; Luzum and Ollitrault, 2011). The paper enables a quantitative test: the predicted v_2{2} from summing the NJ quadrupole and SS 2D jet peak contributions accurately describes published RHIC data from Adams et al. (2004) and LHC data from Aamodt et al. (2011) with an overall factor 1.3 increase, demonstrating that jet and nonjet-quadrupole trends are similar at both energies.
