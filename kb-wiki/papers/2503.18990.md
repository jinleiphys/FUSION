# Tables of Neutron Thermal Cross Sections, Westcott Factors, Resonance Integrals, Maxwellian Averaged Cross Sections, Astrophysical Reaction Rates, and r-process Abundances Calculated from Evaluated Nuclear Data Libraries

> We present calculations of neutron thermal cross sections, Westcott factors, resonance integrals, Maxwellian-averaged cross sections, astrophysical reaction rates, and solar system $r$-process abundances using the latest data from the major evaluated nuclear libraries for 849 ENDF target materials. The recent release of ENDF/B-VIII.1 library, progress in $^{252}$Cf(SF) evaluation, extensive analysis of newly-evaluated neutron reaction cross sections, neutron covariances, and improvements in data processing techniques motivated us to calculate the nuclear industry and neutron physics parameters, produce $s$-process Maxwellian-averaged cross sections and astrophysical reaction rates, extract $r$-process abundances, systematically calculate uncertainties, and provide additional insights on currently available neutron-induced reaction data.

## Key claim
This paper presents comprehensive calculations of neutron thermal cross sections, Westcott factors, resonance integrals, Maxwellian-averaged cross sections, astrophysical reaction rates, and solar system r-process abundances for 849 ENDF target materials, using the latest evaluated nuclear data libraries including the recent ENDF/B-VIII.1 release, with systematic uncertainty quantification.

## Method
The author processed evaluated nuclear data from five major libraries (ENDF/B-VIII.1, JEFF-3.3, JENDL-5.0, CENDL-3.2, and BROND-3.1) to compute neutron-induced reaction parameters for 849 target materials. Calculations included thermal cross sections at 0.0253 eV, Westcott factors, resonance integrals, Maxwellian-averaged capture cross sections at kT=1420 keV, astrophysical reaction rates, and solar system r-process abundances, with uncertainties propagated from the evaluated covariance data.

## Key numbers
- Maxwellian-averaged capture cross sections at kT=1420 keV tabulated for 849 target materials from five evaluated libraries (ENDF/B-VIII.1, JEFF-3.3, JENDL-5.0, CENDL-3.2, BROND-3.1) and compared with EXFOR experimental data
- For 1-H-1: thermal capture cross section of 3.910E-5 barns with uncertainty 8.167E-6 barns from ENDF/B-VIII.1
- For 27-Co-59: Maxwellian-averaged capture cross section of 3.387E-3 barns from ENDF/B-VIII.1, compared with EXFOR value of 6.970E-3 barns with uncertainty 3.400E-4 barns
- For 63-Eu-151: Maxwellian-averaged capture cross section of 2.268E-1 barns from ENDF/B-VIII.1, compared with EXFOR value of 3.700E-1 barns with uncertainty 2.500E-2 barns
- For 55-Cs-133: Maxwellian-averaged capture cross section of 3.853E-2 barns with uncertainty 7.765E-3 barns from ENDF/B-VIII.1

## Context
This work extends the systematic calculations of neutron reaction parameters previously performed using earlier evaluated nuclear data libraries, now incorporating the ENDF/B-VIII.1 release and progress in 252Cf(SF) evaluation. The comprehensive tabulation of Maxwellian-averaged cross sections and astrophysical reaction rates enables improved modeling of s-process nucleosynthesis and extraction of r-process abundances, while the systematic uncertainty quantification provides additional insights for nuclear industry applications and neutron physics parameter calculations.
