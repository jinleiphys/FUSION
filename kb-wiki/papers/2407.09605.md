# Soft-gluon exchange matters: isotropic screening in QCD kinetic theory

> QCD kinetic theory simulations are a prominent tool for studying the nonequilibrium initial stages in heavy-ion collisions. Despite their success, all implementations rely on approximations of the hard thermal loop (HTL) screened matrix elements in the collision terms. In this paper, we present our results for different isotropic screening prescriptions in the elastic collision term for gluons. In particular, we go beyond the simple Debye-like screening form that is used in all current implementations and apply the isotropic HTL matrix element instead. For isotropic systems, the evolution is nearly unchanged, but when studying the equilibration process in a Bjorken expanding plasma, we find qualitative and quantitative differences in a range of moments of the distribution function, such as a decrease in the maximum pressure anisotropy by up to 50%. In contrast, we find no significant qualitative impact on the jet quenching parameter or on the late-time hydrodynamization dynamics of anisotropic plasmas but observe systematically smaller values of $η/s$ by about 10% that coincide with perturbative calculations at small couplings. Our study reveals that the choice of the screening prescription can lead to large corrections at early times, but is less important close to equilibrium.

## Key claim
While all current QCD kinetic theory implementations use a simple Debye-like screening approximation for elastic collision terms, replacing it with the full isotropic hard thermal loop (HTL) matrix element leaves isotropic equilibration nearly unchanged but causes qualitative and quantitative differences in Bjorken-expanding plasmas, including a decrease in maximum pressure anisotropy by up to 50% and systematically smaller values of shear viscosity over entropy density by about 10% that match perturbative calculations at small couplings.

## Method
The authors performed gluonic QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT) simulations of the Boltzmann equation, comparing three screening prescriptions in the elastic 2↔2 collision term: the standard Debye-like screened propagator with a screening factor S(q)=q²/(q²+ξ_g² m_D²) where ξ_g=e^{5/6}/√8, the full isotropic HTL retarded propagator in Coulomb gauge, and alternative Debye-like forms that differ at subleading order in |t|/s. They evolved both isotropic systems (overoccupied and underoccupied initial conditions) and longitudinally expanding plasmas using the Bjorken expansion term, computing observables including pressure anisotropy, energy-momentum tensor components, the jet quenching parameter, and shear viscosity over entropy density.

## Key numbers
- Maximum pressure anisotropy in Bjorken-expanding plasma decreases by up to 50% when using isoHTL screening compared to Debye-like screening (for coupling λ=0.5)
- Shear viscosity over entropy density η/s is systematically smaller by about 10% with isoHTL screening, coinciding with perturbative calculations at small couplings
- Thermalization times for overoccupied isotropic systems at λ=0.5: 69.7 (Debye-like) vs 67.0 (isoHTL) in units of λ²T t_therm
- Thermalization times for underoccupied isotropic systems at λ=0.5: 1029 (Debye-like) vs 1022 (isoHTL) in units of λ²T t_therm
- For λ=10, thermalization times for overoccupied systems: 93 (both prescriptions); for underoccupied: 1245 (Debye-like) vs 1216 (isoHTL)

## Context
This work extends the systematic study of screening approximations in QCD kinetic theory, which was originally formulated by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002) and has been implemented numerically by Kurkela and collaborators (2015, 2018) and Du and collaborators (2020) using a Debye-like screening prescription from Abraao York and collaborators (2014). The authors previously found (Boguslavski and Lindenbauer, 2023) that Debye-like screening cannot simultaneously reproduce both longitudinal and transverse momentum diffusion for the jet quenching parameter. This study enables improved far-from-equilibrium simulations of the bottom-up thermalization process in heavy-ion collisions and provides a benchmark for assessing the accuracy of simpler screening approximations used in all current EKT implementations.
