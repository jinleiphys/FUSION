# Transversity studies with protons and light nuclei

> A general formalism to evaluate time-reversal odd transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (T-odd TMDS) is reviewed and applied to the calculation of the leading-twist quantities, i.e., the Sivers and the Boer-Mulders functions. Two different models of the proton structure, namely a non relativistic constituent quark model and the MIT bag model, have been used. The results obtained in both frameworks fulfill the Burkardt sum rule to a large extent. The calculation of nuclear effects in the extraction of neutron single spin asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off transversely polarized $^3$He is also illustrated. It is found that the nuclear effects described by an Impulse Approximation approach are under theoretical control.

## Key claim

The paper reviews a general formalism for calculating T-odd transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) in quark models, finding that the Burkardt sum rule is fulfilled to within a few percent in the non-relativistic constituent quark model and to within about 5% in the MIT bag model, but only when the spin-flip of the spectator quark is properly included.

## Method

The author evaluates the Sivers and Boer-Mulders functions using two quark models: a non-relativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) and the MIT bag model, both assuming SU(6) symmetry for the proton. Final state interactions are introduced through a one-gluon-exchange mechanism, and the T-odd TMDs are computed from the imaginary part of the relevant matrix elements. The formalism is then applied to calculate nuclear effects in the extraction of neutron single spin asymmetries from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off transversely polarized ³He, using an impulse approximation with realistic nuclear wave functions from the AV18 interaction.

## Key numbers

- The Burkardt sum rule is fulfilled with a ratio r ≈ 0.02 in the NRCQM, corresponding to ⟨k_x^u⟩ = 10.85 MeV and ⟨k_x^d⟩ = -11.25 MeV.
- In the MIT bag model, the Burkardt sum rule is violated by about 5% when the spectator quark spin-flip is included, compared to about 60% violation without it.
- The strong coupling constant is fixed at α_s(μ₀²)/(4π) ≈ 0.13, with μ₀² ≈ 0.1 GeV² and Λ_QCD^(NLO) ≈ 0.24 GeV.
- Nuclear effects in the extraction of neutron asymmetries from ³He are found to be around 10-15% when using the simple dilution formula, but reduce to a few percent when using the full extraction scheme with effective nucleon polarizations.

## Context

This work extends earlier quark model calculations of T-odd TMDs by Courtoy et al. (2008, 2009) and improves upon a previous bag model calculation by Yuan (2004) that neglected spectator quark spin-flip and found 60% violation of the Burkardt sum rule. The nuclear part builds on the analysis of Scopetta (2009) for the planned Jefferson Lab experiments at 6 GeV (completed) and after the 12 GeV upgrade. The results enable reliable extraction of neutron single spin asymmetries from ³He data, using the effective polarization method of the inclusive DIS analysis, now validated for the semi-inclusive case in the specific JLab kinematics.
