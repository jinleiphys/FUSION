# Baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We provide an introduction to the power-counting issue in baryon chiral perturbation theory and discuss some recent developments in the manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of the one-nucleon sector. As explicit applications we consider the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass, its convergence properties, and calculations of the electromagnetic and axial form factors of the nucleon.

# Baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory can be reconciled with a systematic power counting through the choice of a suitable renormalization condition, such as the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme or infrared regularization, contrary to the long-held belief that such a power counting was impossible in the relativistic formulation.

## Method

The paper reviews the power-counting problem in baryon ChPT by analyzing the one-loop nucleon self-energy diagram and showing that the modified minimal subtraction scheme (MS-tilde) produces terms of order q^2 rather than the expected q^1. Two solutions are presented: the heavy-baryon approach, which expands in 1/m, and manifestly Lorentz-invariant methods including infrared regularization (Becher and Leutwyler 1999) and the extended on-mass-shell scheme (Gegelia 1999, Fuchs 2003), which subtract power-counting-violating analytic terms through finite renormalization.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass in the chiral limit: m = (883 ± 4) MeV, obtained from m_N = 938.3 MeV with Δm = (55.5 ± 4) MeV (Fuchs 2003)
- The coefficient k_5 for the M^5 ln(M/μ) term in the nucleon mass expansion at O(q^5) is k_5 = (3 g_A^2)/(1024 π^3 F^4) × (16 g_A^2 − 3), evaluated with g_A = 1.2695 and F_π = 92.42 MeV gives k_5 M^5 ln(M/m_N) = −4.8 MeV, approximately 31% of the leading non-analytic term k_2 M^3
- At M ≈ 360 MeV, the k_5 M^5 ln(M/m_N) term becomes as large as the leading non-analytic term k_2 M^3, indicating convergence issues at unphysical pion masses
- The iterated self-energy contribution from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term gives δm = −0.00233530 MeV, with the leading perturbative term contributing −0.00230219 MeV and higher-order corrections suppressed
- The EOMS scheme and infrared regularization produce identical non-analytic terms (k_2 and k_3) in the nucleon mass expansion but differ in the analytic k_4 term

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on mesonic chiral perturbation theory by Weinberg (1978) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1983) into the baryon sector, addressing the power-counting problem first identified by Gasser (1987). It contrasts the heavy-baryon approach of Jenkins (1990) and Bernard (1992) with the manifestly Lorentz-invariant infrared regularization of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) and the EOMS scheme of Gegelia (1999) and Fuchs (2003). The results enable chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data to physical quark masses and provide a framework for including vector mesons (as in the work of Kubis 2000) and axial-vector mesons as explicit degrees of freedom, improving the description of electromagnetic and axial form factors beyond Q^2 ≈ 0.1 GeV^2.
