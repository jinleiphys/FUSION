# Short-range correlations effects on the deformability of neutron stars

> In the present work, we investigate the effects of short-range correlations (SRC) on the dimensionless deformability of the binary neutron system related to the GW170817 event. We implemented phenomenological SRC in a relativistic mean-field model in which the bulk parameters, namely, incompressibility ($K_0$), effective nucleon mass ratio ($m^*$), symmetry energy ($J$) and its slope ($J_0$), are independently controlled. Our results point out that the SRC favor the model to pass through the constraints, established by the LIGO/Virgo Collaboration, on the values of $Λ_{1.4}$ and on the $Λ_1\timesΛ_2$ region. We also found a clear linear correlation between $Λ_{1.4}$ with $K_0$ and $L_0$ (increasing dependence), and with $m^*$ and $J$ (decreasing dependence). Finally, we also obtained compatible numbers for $R_{1.4}$ (model with and without SRC) in comparison with recent data from the neutron star interior composition explorer mission.

## Key claim
Short-range correlations (SRC) favor the relativistic mean-field model to pass the LIGO/Virgo constraints on the dimensionless tidal deformability of the GW170817 binary neutron star system, but this effect is conditional on the specific values of the bulk nuclear parameters and is most pronounced for parametrizations with higher effective nucleon mass ratios.

## Method
The authors implemented phenomenological short-range correlations via a high-momentum tail in the nucleon momentum distribution within a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. They generated multiple parametrizations by independently varying the bulk nuclear parameters (incompressibility K0, effective mass ratio m*, symmetry energy J, and its slope L0) while keeping saturation density and binding energy fixed, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron star mass: 2.05 solar masses with SRC, 1.96 solar masses without SRC (both compatible with observed 2-solar-mass pulsars)
- Reference model bulk parameters: rho0 = 0.15 fm^-3, B0 = -16.0 MeV, m* = 0.60, K0 = 230 MeV, J = 31.6 MeV, L0 = 58.9 MeV
- Radius range for 1.4-solar-mass star: 12.45 km to 13.71 km (RMF) and 11.51 km to 13.61 km (RMF-SRC)
- From linear correlations with Lambda_1.4 = 190^{+390}_{-120}: K0 maximum = 230 MeV (RMF) and 280 MeV (RMF-SRC); L0 maximum = 58 MeV (RMF) and 74 MeV (RMF-SRC)
- Effective mass range from Lambda_1.4 constraints: 0.60 to 0.88 (RMF) and 0.58 to 0.79 (RMF-SRC)

## Context
This work extends the study of short-range correlations in neutron star matter by Cai (2018) to the specific case of tidal deformability from the GW170817 event. The results show that SRC systematically reduce Lambda_1.4 and shift the Lambda_1-Lambda_2 curves toward the LIGO/Virgo constraints, consistent with the increasing effect on maximum mass reported by Cai (2018). The linear correlations found between Lambda_1.4 and bulk parameters (increasing with K0 and L0, decreasing with m* and J) agree with patterns observed in density-dependent van der Waals models (for K0) and in nonrelativistic Gogny and MDI models (for L0), though the J dependence shows opposite behavior compared to some other models. The radius predictions are compatible with NICER mission results for PSR J0030+0451.
