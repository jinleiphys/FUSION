# The QCD phase diagram from analytic continuation

> We present the crossover line between the quark gluon plasma and the hadron gas phases for small real chemical potentials. First we determine the effect of imaginary values of the chemical potential on the transition temperature using lattice QCD simulations. Then we use various formulas to perform an analytic continuation to real values of the baryo-chemical potential. Our data set maintains strangeness neutrality to match the conditions of heavy ion physics. The systematic errors are under control up to $μ_B\approx 300$ MeV. For the curvature of the transition line we find that there is an approximate agreement between values from three different observables: the chiral susceptibility, chiral condensate and strange quark susceptibility. The continuum extrapolation is based on $N_t=$ 10, 12 and 16 lattices. By combining the analysis for these three observables we find, for the curvature, the value $κ= 0.0149 \pm 0.0021$.

## Key claim

The curvature of the QCD crossover line between the quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas phases, determined via analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potentials, is κ = 0.0149 ± 0.0021 under strangeness-neutral conditions, with systematic errors controlled up to μB ≈ 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1+1 flavors of stout-smeared staggered fermions on Nt = 8, 10, 12, and 16 lattices, using imaginary baryochemical potentials μB^I/T = jπ/8 for j = 1–6. They tuned the strange chemical potential to enforce strangeness neutrality at each imaginary μB value, then extracted the transition temperature shift from three observables: chiral susceptibility, chiral condensate, and strange quark susceptibility. The curvature κ was obtained by fitting ΔTc versus μB^2 in the range 1.2 ≲ μB^I/T ≲ 2, with continuum extrapolation from Nt = 10, 12, and 16 lattices, and systematic errors estimated from histograms over multiple analysis choices.

## Key numbers

- Curvature from chiral susceptibility: κ = 0.0158 ± 0.0013
- Curvature from chiral condensate: κ = 0.0138 ± 0.0011
- Curvature from strange susceptibility: κ = 0.0149 ± 0.0021
- Combined curvature from all three observables: κ = 0.0149 ± 0.0021
- Curvature for Z = 0.4A (lead/gold ions): κ = 0.0149 ± 0.0017
- Transition temperature at μB = 0: Tc = 157 MeV
- Lattice spacings used: a = 0.2 to 0.063 fm
- Lattice volumes: 32³×8, 40³×10, 48³×12, and 64³×16
- Temperature range simulated: 135 to 210 MeV
- Imaginary chemical potential values: μB^I/T = jπ/8 for j = 1,2,3,4,5,6

## Context

This work extends the imaginary-μ approach of deForcrand and Philipsen (2002) and D'Elia and Lombardo (2002) by using finer lattices (up to Nt=16), enforcing strangeness neutrality to match heavy-ion collision conditions, and comparing three different observables. The result shows a stronger curvature than the earlier Taylor-expansion result from Endrodi et al. (2011), which used μs=0 and only statistical uncertainties; the present analysis includes systematic uncertainties and uses strangeness neutrality. The curvature enables mapping the QCD phase diagram for small real chemical potentials relevant to heavy-ion experiments, with the range of validity limited to μB ≲ 300 MeV due to higher-order uncertainties.
