# Analysis of exchange terms in a projected ERPA Theory applied to the quasi-elastic (e,e') reaction

> A systematic study of the influence of exchange terms in the longitudinal and transverse nuclear response to quasi-elastic (e,e') reactions is presented. The study is performed within the framework of the extended random phase approximation (ERPA), which in conjuction with a projection method permits a separation of various contributions tied to different physical processes. The calculations are performed in nuclear matter up to second order in the residual interaction for which we take a (pi+rho)-model with the addition of the Landau-Migdal g'-parameter. Exchange terms are found to be important only for the RPA-type contributions around the quasielastic peak.

## Key claim

Exchange terms in the nuclear response to quasi-elastic (e,e') reactions are important only for the RPA-type contributions around the quasielastic peak, while they can be neglected for ground state correlations and self-energy insertions, provided the direct diagrams are already included.

## Method

The authors applied a projection method within the extended random phase approximation (ERPA) to separate contributions to the longitudinal and transverse nuclear response into three channels: S_PP (final state correlations), S_QP (interference), and S_QQ (ground state correlations). Calculations were performed in infinite nuclear matter at normal saturation density (Fermi momentum k_F = 1.36 fm^-1) up to second order in the residual interaction, using a (pi+rho)-exchange model with a Landau-Migdal g'-parameter of 0.7 and an effective mass m*/m = 0.85, with explicit antisymmetric matrix elements for exchange terms.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum: k_F = 1.36 fm^-1 (nuclear matter at normal saturation density)
- Effective mass: m*/m = 0.85 (momentum-independent, used to dress nucleon lines)
- Landau-Migdal parameter: g' = 0.7 (standard value, not redefined for exchange)
- Pion-nucleon vertex cutoff: Lambda_pi = 1.3 GeV
- Rho-nucleon vertex cutoff: Lambda_rho = 2.0 GeV
- Rho coupling constant: C_rho = 2.18
- Nucleon magnetic moments: mu_s = 0.88, mu_v = 4.70 (related to proton and neutron magnetic moments)
- Dipole electromagnetic form factor parameter: 839 MeV (in the denominator of G_E)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous projection method study (Bauer 1995) which neglected exchange terms, and builds on the ERPA formalism developed by Takayanagi (1988, 1989). The results contradict the common practice of using an effective g' parameter to account for exchange contributions, as previously noted by Bauer (1996) for the RPA series; the present work shows that such redefinition fails for other correlation types. The findings enable future calculations to safely restrict exchange terms to RPA-type correlations only, significantly reducing numerical complexity in nuclear response calculations.
