# Signatures for tetraquark mixing from partial decay widths of the two light-meson nonets

> In this talk, we present successful aspects of the tetraquark mixing model for the two light-meson nonets in the $J^{PC} = 0^{++}$ channel, the light nonet [$a_0(980)$, $K_0^*(700)$, $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$] and the heavy nonet [$a_0(1450)$, $K_0^*(1430)$, $f_0(1370)$, $f_0(1500)$]. In particular, we focus on how their experimental partial decay widths extracted from Particle Data Group (PDG) can support this mixing model. Currently, the experimental data exhibit an unnatural tendency that partial widths of the light nonet are consistently larger than those of the heavy nonet. This unnatural tendency can be explained if the coupling into two pseudoscalar mesons is enhanced in the light nonet and suppressed in the heavy nonet as predicted by the tetraquark mixing model. Therefore, this could be strong evidence to support for the tetraquark mixing model.

## Key claim

The tetraquark mixing model predicts that the coupling strength of the light nonet decaying into two pseudoscalar mesons is much larger than that of the heavy nonet, which can explain the experimentally observed unnatural tendency that partial decay widths of the light nonet are consistently larger than those of the heavy nonet, even though kinematical phase space favors the heavy nonet.

## Method

The authors calculated coupling strengths for each decay mode of the two nonets by recombining the tetraquark wave functions (mixtures of the two tetraquark types |000⟩ and |011⟩) into quark-antiquark pairs in color, spin, and flavor space. They then compared these predicted coupling strengths with experimental partial decay widths extracted from the Particle Data Group (PDG) 2022 compilation, using the relation Γ_partial = (coupling strength)² × Γ_kin.

## Key numbers

- Mixing parameters: α ≈ √(2/3), β ≈ 1/√3, fixed by diagonalization of the color-spin interaction.
- Light nonet coupling strengths (G): a₀(980)→πη = 0.6076, a₀(980)→KK̅ = 0.7441, K₀*(700)→πK = 0.5253, f₀(500)→ππ = −0.3310, f₀(980)→ππ = −0.1690, f₀(980)→KK̅ = −0.4685.
- Heavy nonet coupling strengths (G′): a₀(1450)→πη = 0.1406, a₀(1450)→KK̅ = 0.1722, K₀*(1430)→πK = 0.1251, f₀(1370)→ππ = −0.0785, f₀(1500)→ππ = −0.0394, f₀(1500)→KK̅ = −0.1093.
- Experimental partial widths (MeV): a₀(980)→πη = 60, a₀(980)→KK̅ = 10.6, K₀*(700)→πK = 468, f₀(980)→ππ = 50, f₀(980)→KK̅ = 9.5–46.2; a₀(1450)→πη = 15.4–20.5, a₀(1450)→KK̅ = 13.5–18.0, K₀*(1430)→πK = 251.1, f₀(1500)→ππ = 38.1, f₀(1500)→KK̅ = 9.5.
- Hyperfine mass for light nonet: approximately −500 MeV; for heavy nonet: approximately −20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the tetraquark mixing model previously proposed by Kim and collaborators (2016–2022) for the two scalar meson nonets in the J^PC = 0⁺⁺ channel. It contrasts with non-tetraquark models such as the meson molecular picture (Weinstein, 1990) and the qq̅ picture with hadronic intermediate states (Törnqvist, 1995; Boglione, 2002), which typically apply only to some members of the two nonets. The results enable future experimental verification of the tetraquark nature of these mesons through systematic measurements of partial decay widths, particularly for the f₀(500) and f₀(1370) ππ modes where PDG data remain inconclusive.
