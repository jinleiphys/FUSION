# Axial-vector mesons in a relativistic point-form approach

> The Poincare invariant coupled-channel formalism for two-particle systems interacting via one-particle exchange, which has been developed and applied to vector mesons in Ref. [1] is applied to axial vector mesons. We thereby extend the previous study of a dynamical treatment of the Goldstone-boson exchange by comparison with the commonly used instantaneous approximation to the case of orbital angular momentum l=1. Effects in the mass shifts show more variations than for the vector-meson case. Results for the decay widths are sizable, but comparison with sparse experimental data is inconclusive.

# Axial-vector mesons in a relativistic point-form approach

## Key claim
The Poincaré invariant coupled-channel formalism, previously applied to vector mesons, is extended to axial-vector mesons with orbital angular momentum l=1; the dynamical treatment of Goldstone-boson exchange produces mass shifts that differ significantly in both magnitude and sign from the instantaneous approximation, but comparison with experimental decay data remains inconclusive because only one branching ratio is available for direct comparison.

## Method
The coupled-channel mass operator is defined on the direct sum of quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-pseudoscalar meson Hilbert spaces, with confinement modeled by a harmonic oscillator in M². The eigenvalue equation is reduced to the quark-antiquark channel, producing an effective interaction term that contains the exchange of a pseudoscalar meson; loop contributions are not treated explicitly, and Wigner rotations are neglected based on their small effect compared to boost effects in nucleon form factor calculations.

## Key numbers
- Confinement strength a = 312 MeV and constant V₀ = -1.04115 GeV, taken from the vector-meson sector without readjustment
- For the b₁(1235) meson, the calculated decay width is 33 MeV with the vertex form factor and 42 MeV without it, compared to the experimental value of 142 ± 9 MeV (underestimated by approximately a factor of 4)
- Calculated decay widths for axial-vector mesons are generally in the range of approximately 20 to 90 MeV
- The harmonic oscillator mass operator formula used is M_{nl} = sqrt(8 a² (2n + l + 3/2) + V₀ + 4 m̅²)

## Context
This work extends the Poincaré invariant coupled-channel formalism developed by Krassnigg et al. (2003) for vector mesons to the axial-vector sector, where the quark-antiquark pair has orbital angular momentum l=1 rather than the predominantly l=0 case of vector mesons. The results show that the dynamical treatment produces mass shifts with different signs and magnitudes compared to the instantaneous approximation, particularly in isoscalar channels where one-pion exchange dominates. The study enables calculation of hadronic decay widths within the model's restrictions and suggests analogous investigations of baryon systems using a Faddeev-type approach, as the stochastic variational method used previously in the Goldstone-boson exchange constituent quark model by Glozman et al. (1998) appears unsuitable due to the high-dimensional numerical integrations required.
