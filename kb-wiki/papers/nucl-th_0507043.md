# A Colorful Wake for Gerhard Soff

> We calculate the wake induced in a hot QCD plasma by a fast parton in the framework of linear response theory. We discuss two scenarios: ($i$) a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma described by hard-thermal loop perturbation theory and ($ii$) a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma which resembles a quantum liquid. We show that a Mach cone can appear in the second scenario, but not in the first one

# A Colorful Wake for Gerhard Soff

## Key claim

A Mach cone can appear in the wake induced by a fast parton traversing a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) described as a quantum liquid, but not in a weakly coupled plasma described by hard-thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory, provided the parton's velocity exceeds the speed of plasmon propagation and the collective plasma mode's dispersion relation extends into the space-like region of the ω–k plane.

## Method

The authors apply linear response theory to calculate the induced charge and current densities behind a fast color charge moving through a QCD plasma, using chromo-dielectric functions ε_L and ε_T that are scalars in color space. They consider two scenarios: (i) a weakly coupled plasma where the dielectric functions are taken from the HTL approximation at leading order in the high-temperature expansion, and (ii) a strongly coupled plasma modeled by a modified Bloch-type dielectric function that yields a longitudinal plasmon dispersion relation ω_L = √(u²k² + ω_p²) with a constant plasmon speed u, allowing the mode to enter the space-like region. The Fourier integrals are cut off at a critical momentum k_c to separate collective from single-particle excitation regimes.

## Key numbers

- The suppression factor R_AA in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC reaches about 1/5 (0.2) for high-p_T hadrons.
- The induced charge density ρ^a_ind is proportional to m_g³, where m_g is the gluon thermal mass.
- For the strongly coupled plasma scenario, the plasmon speed is assumed to be u/c = 1/√3 ≈ 0.577.
- The critical momentum where the plasmon dispersion curve intersects the light cone is k_s = ω_p / √(c² − u²).
- The Mach angle is given by φ_M = arccos(u/v); for v=0.99c and u=c/√3, this yields a specific opening angle.
- The Fourier integration cutoff is set to k_c = 2 ω_p for the strongly coupled case.
- In Au+Au collisions, two maxima in the angular distribution of secondary hadrons are located at Δφ ≈ π ± 1.1 radians.

## Context

This work extends the study of electromagnetic wakes in electron plasmas of metal foils, pioneered by Groeneveld and collaborators at Frankfurt and calculated by Schäfer (1980), to the QCD plasma context. It contrasts with the HTL-based treatment of Thoma (1990) for weakly coupled plasmas, where no Mach cone appears. The paper is motivated by RHIC experimental results on collective flow suggesting the sQGP behaves as a nearly ideal fluid with very low viscosity (Teaney, 2003), and by the suggestion of Stöcker (2004) that fast partons could excite Mach waves in such a plasma. The results enable interpretation of preliminary RHIC data (Rak, 2005; Adler, 2005) showing a double-peaked away-side jet correlation at Δφ ≠ π as possible evidence for Mach cone formation, and open the question of whether the shock front is excited by collective plasma modes, the "thunder" effect (Casalderrey-Solana and Shuryak, 2004), or elastic knock-on processes (Lokhtin, 1998).
