# Conserved charge fluctuations at vanishing net-baryon density from Lattice QCD

> We present here continuum extrapolated results for all 2nd order cumulants using the most recent results obtained by the HotQCD collaboration in (2+1)-flavor QCD. We constrain the applicability of various HRG models by presenting a detailed comparison of our results based on different sets of hadron spectra as well as with virial expansion based model calculations. A comparison with our lattice QCD results for 2nd order cumulants with models that parametrize repulsive interactions among baryons and anti-baryons in a hadron resonance gas through a single excluded volume parameter (EVHRG) is also shown.

## Key claim

The paper claims that hadron resonance gas (HRG) models, even when augmented with additional resonances or repulsive interactions via a single excluded volume parameter, cannot simultaneously describe all second-order cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations from lattice QCD; at least two independent excluded volume parameters for strange and non-strange baryons would be needed, and all HRG-based calculations fail for temperatures above the pseudo-critical transition temperature.

## Method

The HotQCD collaboration performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations using highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) with two degenerate light quarks and one strange quark, computing second-order cumulants of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness fluctuations at vanishing chemical potential. The authors performed continuum extrapolations from lattices with different temporal extents and compared these results to several HRG model variants: a point-like non-interacting model using only established PDG resonances (PDGHRG), an extended model including less established resonances and quark model states (QMHRG2020), an excluded volume HRG (EVHRG) with a single hard-sphere parameter, and a second-order virial expansion approach.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical transition temperature of QCD is \(T_{pc}=156.5(1.5)\) MeV.
- QMHRG2020 describes the lattice data for \(\chi_{11}^{BS}\) and \(\chi_{11}^{QS}\) quantitatively better than 1% in the temperature range \(T < 156\) MeV.
- QMHRG2020 provides better than 5% level agreement for \(\chi_{11}^{BQ}\) in the temperature range \(T \leq 150\) MeV, but exhibits an almost 20% deviation close to \(T_{pc}\).
- Electric charge fluctuations \(\chi_{2}^{Q}\) deviate about 10% close to \(T_{pc}\) from HRG model calculations.
- The excluded volume HRG calculations used a hard sphere radius parameter \(b=1\) fm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations by the HotQCD collaboration and provides a systematic comparison with HRG models, including the QMHRG2020 spectrum detailed by Bollweg (2021). The results constrain the applicability of HRG models used to determine freeze-out conditions in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, particularly the ALICE collaboration measurements. The paper shows that while point-like non-interacting HRG models work reasonably well up to \(T_{pc}\), a single excluded volume parameter as used by Vovchenko (2016) and Karthein (2021) is insufficient, and higher-order virial expansions beyond the second-order calculations of Lo (2017) and Fernandez-Ramirez (2018) are needed for quantitative agreement with lattice QCD.
