# Strangeness: Theoretical Status

> Strangeness plays an important role in the study of quark matter since it indicates the rate at which new particles are being produced and therefore provides information about the degree of chemical equilibration reached in heavy ion collisions. It is shown that ratios provide an excellent method for determining the temperature and the chemical potential since many theoretical uncertainties cancel out. All indications are that particle abundances are very close to chemical equilibrium. The amount of strangeness present in $S-S$ collisions is very close to the one corresponding to chemical equilibrium. Deviations are at the 30% level.

# Strangeness: Theoretical Status

## Key claim

Particle ratios integrated over transverse momentum provide a robust method for determining freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential because many theoretical uncertainties (excluded volume corrections, superposition of fireballs, transverse flow, and Bjorken expansion with transverse flow) cancel out in the ratio. The abstract's claim that "deviations are at the 30% level" refers specifically to strangeness abundance in S-S collisions relative to chemical equilibrium, not to all particle species or collision systems.

## Method

The paper demonstrates analytically, using Boltzmann statistics for a hadronic gas model including all hadronic resonances with known branching ratios, that corrections from excluded volume effects, superposition of fireballs along rapidity, instantaneous freeze-out with arbitrary transverse velocity profiles, and Bjorken longitudinal expansion with transverse flow all cancel in hadronic yield ratios when integrated over transverse momentum. The formalism uses chemical potentials for baryon number, strangeness, and charge, with strangeness chemical potential tuned to enforce overall strangeness neutrality.

## Key numbers

- In S-S collisions at CERN, the amount of strangeness present deviates from chemical equilibrium at the 30% level.
- For p-p collisions, the strangeness suppression factor gamma_S is approximately 0.5 (from Becattini, 1990s).
- For relativistic heavy ion collisions, gamma_S estimates range from 0.7 to 1.0.
- At a temperature of T = 200 MeV, the ratio of thermal pions to all pions (including those from resonance decays) is down to the 10% level.
- AGS (Brookhaven) data yield T approximately 100-120 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B = 540 MeV.
- CERN data yield T approximately 180 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B approximately 200-300 MeV.

## Context

This talk extends the hadronic gas model approach previously applied by Stachel (1990s), Becattini (1990s), Rafelski (1990s), and others to heavy ion collisions at CERN and BNL. The key advance is the systematic demonstration that many corrections cancel in ratios, justifying the use of simple thermal fits. The paper enables the interpretation of particle abundance data from S-S, Pb-Pb, Si-Au, and Au-Au collisions in terms of freeze-out temperature and chemical potential, and sets the stage for the subsequent finding that chemical equilibrium is approximately reached in heavy ion collisions, in contrast to the strangeness deficiency quantified by Wroblewski (1980s) for p-p collisions.
