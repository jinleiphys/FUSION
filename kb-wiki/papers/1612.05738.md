# Trimer and Tetramer Bound States in Heteronuclear Systems

> The Efimov effect in heteronuclear cold atomic systems is experimentally more easily accessible than the Efimov effect for identical atoms, because of the potentially smaller scaling factor. We focus on the case of two or three heavy identical bosons and another atom. The former case was recently observed in a mixture of 133Cs and 6Li atoms. We employ the Gaussian Expansion Method as developed by Hiyama, Kino et al.. This is a variational method that uses Gaussians that are distributed geometrically over a chosen range. Supplemental calculations are performed using the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation. Blume et al. previously investigated the scaling properties of heteronuclear systems in the unitary limit and at the three-body breakup threshold. We have completed this picture by calculating the behaviour on the positive scattering length side of the Efimov plot, focussing on the dimer threshold.

## Key claim

The paper completes the picture of heteronuclear Efimov physics by calculating the behavior of trimer and tetramer bound states on the positive scattering length side of the Efimov plot, focusing on the region near the dimer threshold, whereas prior work by Blume and Yan (2014) had focused on the negative scattering length side.

## Method

The authors employed the Gaussian Expansion Method, a variational approach using Gaussian basis functions distributed geometrically over a chosen range, with multiple rearrangement channels in Jacobi coordinates to cover different angular momentum couplings. For the four-body system, random sampling within broad ranges was used to optimize basis functions due to the rapid increase in parameter space. Supplemental calculations were performed using the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) equation for effective three-body treatment near the dimer threshold.

## Key numbers

- For the $^7$Li-$^6$Li mixture (mass ratio M/m = 7/6), the trimer and tetramer approach the dimer threshold very slowly, with a small difference $c_d$ between the vanishing points.
- For the $^{133}$Cs-$^6$Li mixture (mass ratio M/m = 133/6), the states approach the dimer threshold with a steeper slope, and the difference $c_d$ between trimer and tetramer vanishing points is much larger than for the 7/6 case.
- The scattering length between the HL dimer and H atom near the dimer threshold is very large, approximately $a_{AD}/r_0 \approx 10^7$.
- The STM equation fitted to reproduce the 133/6 data point could not simultaneously reproduce the 87/7 data point, suggesting possible non-universal behavior or limitations of the effective three-body treatment.

## Context

This work extends the study of universal Efimov physics in heteronuclear cold atomic systems, building on the theoretical framework of Efimov (1970) and the prior investigation of scaling properties by Blume and Yan (2014), who studied the negative scattering length side. The results enable future experimental investigations on the positive scattering length side, where experiments are complicated by the existence of the shallow dimer, and suggest the need for further study of the dependence of $c_d$ on potential shape and three-body force strength to rule out non-universal behavior.
