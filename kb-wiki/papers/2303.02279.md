# Dynamical mechanisms for deuteron production at mid-rapidity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions from SIS to RHIC energies

> The understanding of the mechanisms for the production of weakly bound clusters, such as a deuteron $d$, in heavy-ion reactions at mid-rapidity is presently one of the challenging problems which is also known as the "ice in a fire" puzzle. In this study we investigate the dynamical formation of deuterons within the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) microscopic transport approach and advance two microscopic production mechanisms to describe deuterons in heavy-ion collisions from SIS to RHIC energies: kinetic production by hadronic reactions and potential production by the attractive potential between nucleons. Differently to other studies, for the "kinetic" deuterons we employ the full isospin decomposition of the various $πNN\leftrightarrow πd$, $NNN\leftrightarrow N d$ channels and take into account the finite size properties of the deuteron by means of an excluded volume condition in coordinate space and by the projection onto the deuteron wave function in momentum space. We find that considering the quantum nature of the deuteron in coordinate and momentum space reduces substantially the kinetic deuteron production in a dense medium as encountered in heavy-ion collisions. If we add the "potential" deuterons by applying an advanced Minimum Spanning Tree (aMST) procedure, we obtain good agreement with the available experimental data from SIS energies up to the top RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that deuteron production in heavy-ion collisions from SIS to RHIC energies can be described by combining two dynamical mechanisms, kinetic production via hadronic reactions and potential production via attractive nucleon-nucleon interactions, but only when the quantum nature of the deuteron (finite size in coordinate space and projection onto the deuteron wave function in momentum space) is explicitly accounted for, which substantially reduces the kinetic production rate in dense medium.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) transport approach, which combines Quantum Molecular Dynamics for baryon propagation with the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics framework for hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom. For kinetic deuteron production, they implemented the full isospin decomposition of the πNN ↔ πd and NNN ↔ Nd channels using a covariant rate formalism, incorporating an excluded volume condition in coordinate space and projection onto the deuteron wave function in momentum space. For potential deuterons, they applied an advanced Minimum Spanning Tree (aMST) procedure that identifies bound clusters with negative binding energy and stabilizes them by freezing internal degrees of freedom when the cluster is no longer interacting with other hadrons.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy |E_B| ≈ 2 MeV, used to characterize the fragility of deuterons compared to the surrounding hadronic medium with kinetic freeze-out temperature T ≈ 100–150 MeV
* At SIS beam energies, between 30% and 50% of protons are bound in deuterons, tritons, and ³He, decreasing to around 1% at LHC energies
* The Gaussian width parameter L = 8.66 fm² used in the PHQMD single-particle Wigner density
* The critical energy density for QGP formation set at ε_c ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm³
* The MST cluster radius r_clus = 4 fm, corresponding roughly to the range of the attractive NN potential
* The saturation density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ and nuclear matter binding energy E(T=0, ρ/ρ_0=1) = -16 MeV used to tune the Skyrme potential parameters
* The study covers collision energies from √s_NN = 2.52 GeV (SIS) up to √s_NN = 200 GeV (top RHIC)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical treatment of deuteron production beyond the statistical model (Andronic 2010) and coalescence approaches (Scheibl 1998, Zhu 2015), and improves upon earlier transport studies using SMASH (Oliinychenko 2018, 2020; Staudenmaier 2021) by including full isospin decomposition and quantum finite-size effects. The aMST procedure resolves the time-selection ambiguity present in earlier PHQMD studies (Aichelin 2019, Glassel 2021) by producing stable cluster yields independent of the analysis time. The combined approach enables a unified description of deuteron observables across three orders of magnitude in collision energy, from SIS through AGS and SPS to RHIC.
