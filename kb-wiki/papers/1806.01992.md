# $K^0Λ$ photoproduction off the neutron with nucleon resonances

> We investigate kaon photoproduction off the neutron target, i.e., $γn \to K^0 Λ$, focusing on the role of nucleon resonances given in the Review of Particle Data Group in the range of $\sqrt{s} \approx 1600 - 2200$ MeV. We employ an effective Lagrangian method and a Regge approach. The strong couplings of nucleon resonances with $KΛ$ vertices are constrained by quark model predictions. The numerical results of the total and differential cross sections are found to be in qualitative agreement with the recent CLAS and FOREST experimental data. We discuss the effects of the narrow nucleon resonance $N(1685,1/2^+)$ on both the total and differential cross sections near the threshold energy. In addition, we present the results of the beam asymmetry as a prediction.

## Key claim

The narrow nucleon resonance \(N(1685,1/2^+)\) has only a marginal effect on the total cross section of \(\gamma n \to K^0 \Lambda\), but it is essential to explain the differential cross section data in the photon energy range \(1037\ \mathrm{MeV} \le E_\gamma \le 1062\ \mathrm{MeV}\), particularly improving agreement with FOREST and CLAS data in forward and backward angles.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level Feynman diagrams including \(s\)-channel nucleon resonance exchanges, \(t\)-channel \(K^*\) Reggeon exchange, and \(u\)-channel \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma\) hyperon exchanges. Sixteen nucleon resonances from the Particle Data Group (PDG) in the range \(\sqrt{s} \approx 1600-2200\) MeV were included, plus the narrow \(N(1685,1/2^+)\) resonance; strong couplings at the \(K\Lambda\) vertices were constrained by quark model predictions from Capstick (1998) and PDG branching ratios, while electromagnetic couplings were taken from PDG helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section for \(\gamma n \to K^0 \Lambda\) is computed over \(\sqrt{s} \approx 1600-2200\) MeV, with \(N^*\) contributions dominant below \(W \lesssim 2.2\) GeV and \(K^*\) Reggeon exchange taking over at higher energies.
- The \(K^*\) Regge trajectory is \(\alpha(t) = 0.83 t + 0.25\), with slope \(\alpha' = 0.83\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\) and energy scale \(s_0 = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- The \(K\Lambda N(1685)\) coupling constant is fitted to \(g_{K \Lambda N(1685,1/2^+)} = -(0.8 - 1.1)\), corresponding to a branching ratio \(\mathrm{Br}(N(1685,1/2^+) \to K\Lambda) = (0.5 - 1.0)\%\) with \(\Gamma_{N(1685,1/2^+)} = 30\) MeV, giving a partial decay width \(\Gamma_{N^*(1685) \to K\Lambda} = (0.15 - 0.30)\) MeV.
- The cutoff masses for baryon and resonance form factors are fixed to \(\Lambda_{B(N,\Lambda,\Sigma),N^*} = 0.9\) GeV.
- The \(N(1650,1/2^-)\) and \(N(1720,3/2^+)\) are the most dominant resonances in the total cross section; nine resonances have sizable effects, while the \(N(1685,1/2^+)\) effect is marginal on the total cross section.
- Differential cross sections are compared with FOREST data at photon energies from \(E_\gamma = 937.5\) MeV to \(1137.5\) MeV and with CLAS data from \(E_\gamma = 0.97\) GeV to \(2.45\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of the "neutron anomaly" in \(\eta\) photoproduction (Kuznetsov, 2006; Miyahara, 2007; Jaegle, 2008; Werthmuller, 2013, 2014; Witthauer, 2017) to the \(\gamma n \to K^0 \Lambda\) channel, where the narrow \(N(1685,1/2^+)\) resonance was previously predicted by the chiral quark-soliton model (Diakonov, 1997; Polyakov, 2003; Kim, 2005; Yang, 2013). The results are qualitatively similar to the Bonn-Gatchina coupled-channel partial-wave analysis (Anisovich, 2017) regarding dominant partial waves, but the inclusion of \(N(1685,1/2^+)\) improves agreement with recent CLAS (Compton, 2017) and FOREST (Tsuchikawa, 2016, 2017) experimental data on differential cross sections. The work provides predictions for beam asymmetry that can be tested by future experiments.
