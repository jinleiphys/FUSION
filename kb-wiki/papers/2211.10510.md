# How does dark matter affect compact star properties and high density constraints of strongly interacting matter

> We study the impact of asymmetric bosonic dark matter on neutron star properties, including possible changes of tidal deformability, maximum mass, radius, and matter distribution inside the star. The conditions at which dark matter particles tend to condensate in the star's core or create an extended halo are presented. We show that dark matter condensed in a core leads to a decrease of the total gravitational mass and tidal deformability compared to a pure baryonic star, which we will perceive as an effective softening of the equation of state. On the other hand, the presence of a dark matter halo increases those observable quantities. Thus, observational data on compact stars could be affected by accumulated dark matter and, consequently, constraints we put on strongly interacting matter at high densities. To confirm the presence of dark matter in the compact star's interior, and to break the degeneracy between the effect of accumulated dark matter and strongly interacting matter properties at high densities, several astrophysical and GW tests are proposed.

## Key claim
The presence of asymmetric bosonic dark matter in neutron stars can mimic either a softening or stiffening of the baryonic equation of state at high densities, creating a degeneracy that affects constraints on strongly interacting matter. The actual effect depends on whether dark matter forms a core (decreasing mass and tidal deformability) or a halo (increasing those quantities), and the authors propose astrophysical and gravitational-wave tests to break this degeneracy.

## Method
The authors model dark matter as a complex scalar field forming a Bose-Einstein Condensate at zero temperature, with repulsive self-interaction mediated by a vector field, and treat it as a separate fluid coupled only gravitationally to baryonic matter. They solve two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for three different hadronic equations of state (IST EoS, DD2 EoS with and without Λ hyperons) and vary the dark matter particle mass (0.1 GeV and 1 GeV), interaction strength, and dark matter fraction (1%, 3%, 5%) to compute mass-radius relations, energy density profiles, and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers
- Dark matter particle masses considered: \(m_\chi = 1\) GeV (heavy, forms core) and \(m_\chi = 0.1\) GeV (light, forms halo).
- For heavy DM (\(m_\chi = 1\) GeV, \(m_I = 1\) GeV), the DM core radius is \(R_D \sim 2.5\) km, inside which DM energy density is an order of magnitude higher than baryonic energy density.
- For light DM (\(m_\chi = 0.1\) GeV, \(m_I = 0.25\) GeV), halo configurations can produce total maximum gravitational masses up to \(5 M_\odot\) for DM fractions above 95%.
- The tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 is \(\Lambda(1.4 M_\odot) \leq 800\) at 90% confidence level, which the authors use to disfavor extended DM halos in the host galaxy NGC 4993.
- The four heaviest pulsars used for mass constraints: PSR J0348+0432 at \(2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\), PSR J0740+6620 at \(2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} M_\odot\), PSR J1810+1744 at \(2.13 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\), and PSR J0952-0607 at \(2.35 \pm 0.17 M_\odot\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Ivanytskyi et al. (2020) and others on dark matter effects in neutron stars, specifically addressing the degeneracy between dark matter accumulation and changes in the baryonic equation of state. The authors show that the same observational signatures (e.g., reduced mass and radius) could arise from either dark matter cores or from new degrees of freedom such as Λ hyperons or quark-gluon plasma, as studied by Ivanytskyi et al. (2022). The results enable future tests using correlations of neutron star properties with distance from the Galactic center, gravitational-wave signatures from neutron star mergers, and pulsar pulse profile modifications due to dark halos.
