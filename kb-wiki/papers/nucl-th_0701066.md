# Analysis of (pi^{+-},K^+) and (K^-,K^+) hypernuclear production spectra in distorted wave impulse approximation

> We study the hyperon-nucleus potential with distorted wave impulse wave approximation (DWIA) using Green's function method. In order to include the nucleon and hyperon potential effects in Fermi averaging, we introduce the local optimal momentum approximation of target nucleons. We can describe the quasi free Lambda, Sigma and Xi production spectra in a better way than in the standard Fermi averaged t-matrix treatments.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their Local Optimal Fermi Averaging t-matrix (LOFAt) treatment within the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) describes quasi-free hyperon production spectra better than standard Fermi averaged t-matrix treatments, but the improvement depends on including nucleon and hyperon potential effects in the on-shell kinematics of the elementary process.

## Method

The authors use the Green's function method within DWIA to calculate hypernuclear production cross sections for \((\pi^\pm,K^+)\) and \((K^-,K^+)\) reactions. They introduce a Local Optimal Fermi Averaging t-matrix (LOFAt) that incorporates nucleon and hyperon potential effects through effective masses, where the energy of each baryon in the nuclear medium is defined as \(E_B(r)=\sqrt{\mathbf{p}_B^2+m_B^2+2m_B V_B(r)}\). The distorted meson waves are evaluated in the eikonal approximation, and the hyperon-nucleus optical potential is taken as a Woods-Saxon form with real and imaginary volume terms, a spin-orbit term, and a Coulomb term.

## Key numbers

- \(\Lambda\)-nucleus potential depth of \(V_0^\Lambda = -32\ \mathrm{MeV}\) with spin-orbit strength \(V_{ls}^\Lambda = 4\ \mathrm{MeV}\), radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.1\ \mathrm{fm}\) and diffuseness \(d = 0.6\ \mathrm{fm}\) reproduces the \({}^{28}\mathrm{Si}(\pi^+,K^+)\) spectrum at \(p_\pi = 1.20\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\) and \(\theta = 6^\circ\) in both quasi-free and bound state regions.
- For \(\Sigma^-\) production on \({}^{28}\mathrm{Si}\) at \(p_\pi = 1.2\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\), the LOFAt + DWIA calculation with a Woods-Saxon potential of small repulsion (0 to +10 MeV) and imaginary part fixed at \(-20\ \mathrm{MeV}\) reproduces the experimental data; the Batty density-dependent potential also gives good agreement.
- For \(\Xi^-\) production, a potential depth of \((V_0^\Xi, W_0^\Xi) = (-15\ \mathrm{MeV}, -1\ \mathrm{MeV})\) reproduces the quasi-free spectra on C, Al, Cu, Ag, and Pb targets at \(p_\pi = 1.65\ \mathrm{GeV}/c\) and \(\theta = 6^\circ\) with an assumed experimental resolution of 10 MeV.
- Isospin averaged cross sections used for meson distortion: \(\bar{\sigma}_{N\pi^\pm} = 34\ \mathrm{mb}\), \(\bar{\sigma}_{NK^+} = 18\ \mathrm{mb}\) for \((\pi^\pm,K^+)\) at 1.20 GeV/\(c\); \(\bar{\sigma}_{NK^-} = 40\ \mathrm{mb}\), \(\bar{\sigma}_{NK^+} = 30\ \mathrm{mb}\) for \((K^-,K^+)\) at 1.65 GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the DWIA analysis of hypernuclear production spectra by incorporating potential effects into the Fermi averaging procedure, building on the on-shell optimal Fermi averaging approach of Harada and Hirabayashi (2005) and the Semi Classical Distorted Wave analysis of Kohno et al. (2006). The authors find that their LOFAt treatment prefers a less repulsive \(\Sigma^-\) potential than suggested by earlier DWIA analyses of the KEK \((\pi^-,K^+)\) data (Noumi et al., 2002), which had required a repulsive real potential of 90 MeV or more. The method enables simultaneous description of both quasi-free and bound state regions for \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), and \(\Xi\) production, and the authors suggest that bound state peaks in \(\Xi^-\) production could be identified if experimental resolution is improved to around 2 MeV.
