# Anomaly inflow and thermal equilibrium

> Using the anomaly inflow mechanism, we compute the flavor/Lorentz non-invariant contribution to the partition function in a background with a U(1) isometry. This contribution is a local functional of the background fields. By identifying the U(1) isometry with Euclidean time we obtain a contribution of the anomaly to the thermodynamic partition function from which hydrostatic correlators can be efficiently computed. Our result is in line with, and an extension of, previous studies on the role of anomalies in a hydrodynamic setting. Along the way we find simplified expressions for Bardeen-Zumino polynomials and various transgression formulae

## Key claim

The authors compute the flavor/Lorentz non-invariant contribution to the partition function in a background with a U(1) isometry, obtaining a local functional of background fields; this result extends previous studies on anomalies in hydrodynamics but is restricted to global anomalies (those anomalous only in the presence of external background fields) and excludes anomalies associated with gauge symmetries such as the Axial-Vector-Vector anomaly of the standard model.

## Method

The authors construct the anomalous contribution to the hydrostatic generating functional \(W_{anom}\) using the anomaly inflow mechanism, starting from the anomaly polynomial \(\mathcal{P}\) of a \(2n\)-dimensional theory and its associated Chern-Simons form \(I_{CS}\). They define hatted connections \(\hat{A} = A + \mu u\) and \(\hat{\Gamma}^\mu{}_\nu = \Gamma^\mu{}_\nu + (\mu_R)^\mu{}_\nu u\) using the velocity field \(u^\mu\), temperature \(T\), chemical potential \(\mu\), and a spin chemical potential \(\mu_R\), then obtain \(W_{anom}\) as the integral of \(-\frac{u}{2\omega} \wedge (I_{CS} - \hat{I}_{CS})\), where \(\omega\) is the vorticity two-form. They also derive a second construction using a formal \(2n+1\)-form \(V_\mathcal{P} = \frac{u}{2\omega} \wedge (\mathcal{P} - \hat{\mathcal{P}})\) from which the covariant anomaly-induced current, heat current, and stress tensor can be obtained by differentiation.

## Key numbers

- For a \(3+1\) dimensional theory with a \(U(1)^3\) anomaly coefficient \(c_A\) and mixed gauge-gravitational anomaly coefficient \(c_m\), the zero-frequency two-point function of the covariant current and stress tensor is \(\langle J_{cov}^i(\vec{k}) T_{cov}^{0j}(-\vec{k})\rangle = i (8 \pi^2 c_m T^2 + 3 c_A \mu^2) \epsilon^{ijk} k_k + O(k^2)\) in the small momentum limit.
- For a left Weyl fermion with positive unit charge, the conventions give \(c_A = 1/(24 \pi^2)\) and \(c_m = 1/(192 \pi^2)\).
- The thermal helicity per unit volume of a 4d quantum field theory is given by \(-6T(8 \pi^2 c_m T^2 \mu + c_A \mu^3)\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the role of anomalies in a hydrodynamic setting, particularly the computation of anomaly-induced transport such as the chiral magnetic effect and chiral vortical effect studied by Kharzeev (2010) and others. The authors build on the hydrostatic generating functional approach developed by Banerjee (2012) and Jensen (2012), and provide a unified framework for computing anomalous contributions to the partition function in arbitrary dimensions. The results enable efficient computation of hydrostatic correlators and clarify the distinction between contributions fixed by anomalies (\(W_{anom}\) and \(W_{transcendental}\)) and non-anomalous contributions, with the explicit evaluation of \(W_{transcendental}\) left to future work.
