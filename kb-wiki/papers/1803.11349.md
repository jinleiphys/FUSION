# Resonance in the $Y_{c}N$ potential model

> We calculate two-body $J^π=0^{+}, 1^{+}$, and $J^π=2^{+}$ resonance states of $Y_{c}$ ($= Λ_{c}$, $Σ_{c}$, or $Σ_{c}^{*}$) and $N$ using the complex scaling method. We employ the $Y_{c}N$-CTNN potentials, which were proposed in our previous study, and obtain four resonances near $Σ_{c}N$ and $Σ_{c}^{*}N$ thresholds. From the analysis by the binding energies of partial channel systems, we conclude that these resonance states are Feshbach resonances. We compare the results with the $Y_{c}N$ resonance states in the heavy quark limit, where the $Σ_{c}N$ and $Σ_{c}^{*}N$ thresholds are degenerate, and find that they form two pairs of the heavy-quark doublets in agreement with the heavy quark spin symmetry.

## Key claim

Using the complex scaling method with the YcN-CTNN potentials, the authors identify four Feshbach resonances near the ΣcN and Σc*N thresholds; the abstract's claim of "four resonances" is qualified by the fact that these states only appear when the ΛcN channel is omitted in the partial-channel calculation, and their properties depend on the specific parameter set (d-parameter being the most attractive).

## Method

The authors solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for the YcN two-body system (Λc, Σc, Σc* with a nucleon) using the complex scaling method with Gaussian expansion basis. The YcN-CTNN potentials include pion-exchange, sigma-exchange, and quark-exchange-model short-range repulsion, with the d-parameter set (b=0.5 fm, Cσ=-70.68) used primarily. Resonance energies and widths were extracted from the complex eigenvalues that remain stable as the scaling angle θ is varied from 10° to 20°.

## Key numbers

- Jπ=0+ resonance near ΣcN threshold: resonance energy 163 MeV (4 MeV below threshold), width 1 MeV
- Jπ=1+ resonance near ΣcN threshold: resonance energy 144 MeV (23 MeV below threshold), width 12 MeV
- Jπ=1+ resonance near Σc*N threshold: resonance energy 225 MeV (7 MeV below threshold), width 2 MeV
- Jπ=2+ resonance near Σc*N threshold: resonance energy 206 MeV (25 MeV below threshold), width 14 MeV
- In the heavy quark limit with degenerate threshold (188.6 MeV), the narrow-width pair (Jπ=0+ and Jπ=1+) both appear at 184 MeV (5 MeV below threshold) with width 1 MeV
- In the heavy quark limit, the broad-width pair (Jπ=1+ and Jπ=2+) appear at 162 MeV (27 MeV below threshold) with width 13 MeV and 161 MeV (28 MeV below threshold) with width 14 MeV respectively

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous YcN potential model study (Maeda, Oka, Liu, 2016) from bound states below the ΛcN threshold to resonance states above it, using the same CTNN potentials. The results are compared with the heavy quark spin symmetry prediction that ΣcN and Σc*N states form degenerate doublets in the heavy quark limit. The paper notes that lattice QCD calculations (ref-hal1, ref-hal2) and constituent quark model studies (ref-02, ref-022) also suggest existence of resonance states, and that recent lattice calculations predict less attractive potentials that may not support ΛcN bound states, making the search for these ΣcN and Σc*N resonances experimentally relevant even if no ΛcN bound state exists.
