# Analysis of the pp-->pp, pid-->pid, and pid-->pp Scattering Data

> A combined analysis of the main reactions of the two-baryon system (pp-->pp, pid-->pid, and pid-->pp) over the sqrt{s} interval from pion threshold to 2.4 GeV has been completed. The overall phase in pid-->pp has now been determined. The combined analysis has resulted in an improved fit to the pid elastic and pid-->pp databases.

# Analysis of the pp→pp, πd→πd, and πd→pp Scattering Data

## Key claim

The combined K-matrix analysis of the coupled two-baryon reactions (pp→pp, πd→πd, and πd→pp) over the sqrt(s) interval from pion threshold to 2.4 GeV has resolved the previously undetermined overall phase in πd→pp, finding it to be about 60 degrees; however, the most noticeable improvements over separate analyses appear only at higher energies where existing data are sparse, and much additional data above 300 MeV pion kinetic energy will be required before a stable solution to 500 MeV can be expected.

## Method

The authors constructed a 4x4 K-matrix formalism coupling the pp, πd, and NΔ channels, with the NΔ channel added to account for all other channels and ensure unitarity. The pp partial waves (J ≤ 7) were held fixed from a prior separate analysis, while the πd elastic and πd→pp partial waves (J ≤ 5) were determined from a fit to the combined πd elastic and πd→pp databases. The K-matrix parameters were determined solely from fits to available databases without using results from outside analyses or model approaches as constraints.

## Key numbers

- The combined analysis (C500) covers the sqrt(s) interval from pion threshold to 2.4 GeV, corresponding to Tπ = 0–500 MeV or Tp = 288–1290 MeV
- For πd→πd, the combined analysis achieved χ²/Data = 2418/1362, compared to 2745/1362 in the separate analysis (SM94)
- For πd→pp, the combined analysis achieved χ²/Data = 7570/4787, compared to 7716/4787 in the separate analysis (SP96)
- The overall phase in πd→pp was determined to be about 60 degrees in the combined analysis
- The pp→pp fit remained unchanged with χ²/Data = 17380/10496
- Significant differences between combined and separate analyses begin to appear above a pion laboratory kinetic energy of 300 MeV or 2.3 GeV in sqrt(s)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous separate analyses of pp→pp (WI96 solution, Arndt et al.), πd→πd (SM94 solution), and πd→pp (SP96 solution) by unifying them in a coupled-channel K-matrix formalism that respects unitarity. The combined analysis provides a unified description of resonancelike behavior previously noted in separate analyses, which has been variously described as "resonant" (due to dibaryon resonances) or "pseudo-resonant" (due to the NΔ intermediate state). The results enable future combined analyses to be extended to sqrt(s) = 3 GeV, provided additional πd elastic and πd→pp data above 300 MeV become available.
