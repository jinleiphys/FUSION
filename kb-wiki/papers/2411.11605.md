# Fermionic Neural Networks through the lens of Group Theory

> We present an overview of the method of Neural Quantum States applied to the many-body problem of atomic nuclei. Through the lens of group representation theory, we focus on the problem of constructing neural-network ansätze that respect physical symmetries. We explicitly prove that determinants, which are among the most common methods to build antisymmetric neural-network wave functions, can be understood as the result of a group convolution. We also identify the reason why this construction is so efficient in practice compared to other group convolutional operations. We conclude that group representation theory is a promising avenue to incorporate explicitly symmetries in Neural Quantum States.

# Fermionic Neural Networks through the lens of Group Theory

## Key claim
The paper proves that Slater determinants, the standard method for building antisymmetric neural-network wave functions, can be understood as a special case of group convolution under the symmetric group S_N. The efficiency of this construction arises because the alternating irreducible representation of S_N admits determinant algorithms with O(N^3) complexity, unlike other group convolutional operations that would scale with the group size.

## Method
The authors apply group representation theory and Schur's lemma to show that antisymmetric wave functions must transform under the alternating irreducible representation of the symmetric group S_N. They construct an intertwiner between the regular representation and the alternating representation of S_N using a group convolution, and demonstrate that this intertwiner reduces to the Leibniz formula for a Slater determinant when the input consists of single-particle orbitals. The analysis extends to backflow-enhanced orbitals and Pfaffians as generalizations of this group-convolutional construction.

## Key numbers
- The group convolution sum over S_N involves N! terms, but the determinant form allows evaluation with O(N^3) time complexity using standard algorithms.
- The prefactor 1/N! appears in the group convolution expression for the intertwiner (Eq. 4), though the authors note this does not affect equivariance properties.

## Context
This work extends the Neural Quantum States (NQS) method introduced by Carleo and Troyer (2017) for solving quantum many-body problems, which has been applied to quantum chemistry by Pfau et al. (2020) and to nuclear structure by Keeble et al. (2020), Adams et al. (2021), and Gnech et al. (2023). The paper builds on group-convolutional neural networks (G-CNNs) developed by Cohen et al. (2016) for general symmetry groups, and on the proof by Kondor and Cohen (2018) that any equivariant layer must be group-convolutional. The work enables systematic construction of neural-network ansätze that simultaneously respect multiple symmetries (e.g., antisymmetry, spin, isospin, rotational symmetry) for nuclear physics applications, potentially leading to more cost-effective energy minimization.
