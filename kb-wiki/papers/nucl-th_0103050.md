# Gluon self-energy in a two-flavor color superconductor

> The energy and momentum dependence of the gluon self-energy is investigated in a color superconductor with two flavors of massless quarks. The presence of a color-superconducting quark-quark condensate modifies the gluon self-energy for energies which are of the order of the gap parameter. For gluon energies much larger than the gap, the self-energy assumes the form given by the standard hard-dense loop approximation. It is shown that this modification of the gluon self-energy does not affect the magnitude of the gap to leading and subleading order in the weak-coupling limit.

## Key claim

The modification of the gluon self-energy in a two-flavor color superconductor (compared to the standard hard-dense-loop approximation) does not affect the magnitude of the gap parameter to leading and subleading order in the weak-coupling limit, but the paper computes the self-energy for the full range of gluon energies and momenta relevant to the gap equation to verify this claim.

## Method

The author computed the real and imaginary parts of the electric (00-component) and magnetic (transverse) components of the gluon self-energy for gluons of adjoint colors 1, 2, 3, and 8 in a two-flavor color superconductor at zero temperature, using the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism in pure Coulomb gauge. The calculation was performed for gluon energies and momenta much smaller than the quark chemical potential, with the gap function taken to be constant near the Fermi surface. The results were expressed in terms of elliptic integrals and compared to the standard hard-dense-loop (HDL) self-energies.

## Key numbers

* The imaginary part of the gluon self-energy vanishes below the threshold \(p_0 = 2\phi\) for gluons of color 1, where \(\phi\) is the gap parameter, because quasiparticle-quasihole excitations require at least this energy.
* For gluon energies \(p_0 \gg \phi\), the imaginary parts of all self-energy components approach the corresponding HDL expressions, which for the electric self-energy are \({\rm Im}\, \Pi^{00}_0(P) \simeq -\pi \frac{3}{2} m_g^2 \frac{p_0}{p} \theta(p-p_0)\) and for the magnetic self-energy are \({\rm Im}\, \Pi^t_0(P) \simeq -\pi \frac{3}{4} m_g^2 \frac{p_0}{p} (1 - p_0^2/p^2) \theta(p-p_0)\), where \(m_g = \sqrt{N_f/(6\pi^2)} \, g\mu\) is the gluon mass.
* The gluon self-energy for color 8 is given by \(\Pi^{\mu\nu}_{88}(P) = \frac{2}{3} \Pi^{\mu\nu}_0(P) + \frac{1}{3} \tilde{\Pi}^{\mu\nu}(P)\), where \(\Pi^{\mu\nu}_0\) is the standard HDL self-energy and \(\tilde{\Pi}^{\mu\nu}\) contains the superconducting modifications.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculations of the gluon self-energy in the limit of vanishing energy and momentum (Rischke 2000), which yielded the Debye and Meissner masses in a color superconductor. The paper addresses a question raised by earlier work (Son 1999; Rischke 2000) about whether the modification of the gluon propagator in the superconducting phase could change the leading-order coefficient \(c = 3\pi^2/\sqrt{2}\) in the gap equation solution \(\phi = b\mu \exp(-c/g)\). The results confirm that the self-energy approaches the HDL form for energies much larger than the gap, supporting the conclusion that the leading and subleading order gap is unaffected, and provide the necessary input for future calculations of sub-subleading contributions.
