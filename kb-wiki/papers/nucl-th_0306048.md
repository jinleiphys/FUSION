# DWBA analysis of the 13C(6Li,d)17O reaction at 10 MeV/nucleon and its astrophysical implications

> The value of the alpha spectroscopic factor (S_alpha) of the 6.356 MeV 1/2+ state of 17O is believed to have significant astrophysical implications due to the importance of the 13C(alpha,n)16O reaction as a possible source of neutron production for the s process. To further study this effect, an accurate measurement of the 13C(6Li,d)17O reaction at E_lab = 60 MeV has been performed recently by Kubono et al., who found a new value for the spectroscopic factor of the 6.356 MeV 1/2+ state of 17O based on a distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) analysis of these data. This new value, S_alpha approximately = 0.011, is surprisingly much smaller than those used previously in astrophysical calculations (S_alpha approximately = 0.3-0.7) and thus poses a serious question as to the role of the 13C(alpha,n)16O reaction as a source of neutron production. In this work we perform a detailed analysis of the same 13C(6Li,d)17O data within the DWBA as well as the coupled reaction channel (CRC) formalism. Our analysis yields an S_alpha value of over an order of magnitude larger than that of Kubono et al. for the 6.356 MeV 1/2+ state of 17O.

# DWBA analysis of the 13C(6Li,d)17O reaction at 10 MeV/nucleon and its astrophysical implications

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that the alpha spectroscopic factor (S_alpha) for the 6.356 MeV 1/2+ state of 17O is over an order of magnitude larger than the value of approximately 0.011 reported by Kubono et al. (2003), and that the transfer data are consistent with S_alpha values in the range 0.3-0.7 used in previous astrophysical calculations, contrary to the earlier claim that the small S_alpha posed a serious question about the role of the 13C(alpha,n)16O reaction as a neutron source for the s process.

## Method
The authors performed distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) and coupled reaction channel (CRC) analyses of the 13C(6Li,d)17O reaction at E_lab = 60 MeV using the code FRESCO, employing the same optical potentials as Kubono et al. (2003) as well as a new folding model plus dynamic polarisation potential (DPP) for the 6Li+13C entrance channel. They included the full complex remnant term in the DWBA calculations and tested the sensitivity of extracted S_alpha values to the choice of entrance channel optical potential, the assumed alpha+13C cluster structure (2p-1h versus 4p-3h configurations), and the inclusion of couplings between states in 17O.

## Key numbers
- S_alpha for the 6.356 MeV 1/2+ state of 17O obtained from DWBA with Kubono et al.'s set 1 potential: 0.49 (with full complex remnant term) or 0.24 (no remnant term)
- After normalisation to S_alpha = 0.25 for the 3.055 MeV 1/2- state, the normalised S_alpha for the 6.356 MeV 1/2+ state is 0.15 (with remnant) or 0.19 (without remnant), compared to Kubono et al.'s value of 0.011
- Using the folding + DPP optical potential, the absolute S_alpha for the 6.356 MeV 1/2+ state is 0.36 from DWBA and 0.40 from CRC calculations
- Reduced alpha width gamma_alpha^2 for the 6.356 MeV 1/2+ state: 184 keV (using set 1 potential) or 136 keV (using folding + DPP potential), compared to 7.4 keV reported by Kubono et al.
- The folding + DPP potential gives a volume integral J_V = -329 MeV fm^3, consistent with the global systematics value of approximately -330 MeV fm^3 for 10 MeV/nucleon

## Context
This work reanalyses the same 13C(6Li,d)17O transfer data measured by Kubono et al. (2003) at 60 MeV, which had yielded a surprisingly small S_alpha of 0.011 for the 6.356 MeV 1/2+ state. The present analysis shows that the original DWBA analysis was flawed and that the data are compatible with S_alpha values (0.3-0.7) used in earlier astrophysical calculations by Drotleff et al. (1993) and Hale et al. (1994), which suggested a rapid increase of the astrophysical S factor for the 13C(alpha,n)16O reaction at low energies. The results support the role of the 6.356 MeV 1/2+ sub-threshold resonance in enhancing the neutron production for the s process in AGB stars, consistent with the models of Gallino et al. (1998). The work also demonstrates the importance of using realistic entrance channel optical potentials and highlights the need for elastic scattering data over a wide angular range to obtain reliable spectroscopic factors from alpha transfer reactions.
