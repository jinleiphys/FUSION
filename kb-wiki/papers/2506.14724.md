# Quantitative predictions of alpha-charmonium correlation functions in high-energy collisions

> Two-body $ ^{4}\textrm{He}\left(α\right)$-charmonium $ \left(c\bar{c}\right) $ potentials in the single-folding potential (SFP) approach are built by using a first principles HAL QCD low-energy $ NJ/ψ$ and $ Nη_{c} $ interactions. The $N\textrm{-}c\bar{c}$ potentials are observed to exhibit an attractive nature across all distances, accompanied by a characteristic long-range tail. It is found that the $ α\textrm{-}J/ψ$ system appears to be loosely bound with the central binding energy in the range of 0.1-0.6 MeV, while for spin-$ 1/2 $ $α\textrm{-}η_{c}$, no bound or resonance state (with respect to the $ α\textrm{-} c\bar{c} $ threshold) was found. The $ α\textrm{-}c\bar{c} $ correlation function in high-energy collisions is examined to explore the $ N\textrm{-}c\bar{c} $ interaction. The analysis revealed that variations in spin-dependent $α\textrm{-}c\bar{c}$ interactions-spin-$3/2$ $α\textrm{-}J/ψ$, spin-$1/2$ $α\textrm{-}J/ψ$, spin-$1/2$ $α\textrm{-}η_c$, and the spin-averaged $α\textrm{-}J/ψ$-produce noticeable differences in the $α\textrm{-}c\bar{c}$ correlation function, especially when the source size is around $ 3 $ fm. It is found that different results are produced by the Lednicky-Lyuboshits formula at small source sizes. This indicates that a relatively long-range interaction exists for the $ α\textrm{-}c\bar{c} $ system. Furthermore, a comparison has been conducted between two density functions of $ ^{4}\textrm{He}$ the central depression (CD) and the simple single Gaussian (SG) density-both of which share an identical rms radius of 1.56 fm. Although the $α\textrm{-}J/ψ$ binding energies for the two models are nearly indistinguishable, their corresponding correlation functions demonstrate markedly different behaviors.

## Key claim

The alpha-J/psi system is predicted to be loosely bound with a central binding energy in the range of 0.1-0.6 MeV, but this result depends on the choice of nucleon density distribution for the alpha particle and the spin channel; the alpha-eta_c system shows no bound or resonance state relative to the alpha-charmonium threshold.

## Method

The authors constructed two-body alpha-charmonium potentials using the single-folding potential approach, folding the HAL QCD lattice-derived N-J/psi and N-eta_c interactions with nucleon density distributions of the alpha particle. They used both a central depression density and a simple single Gaussian density for the alpha particle, each with root-mean-square radii of 1.56 fm, and also considered Gaussian densities with radii of 1.70 and 1.84 fm. The resulting potentials were fitted to a Woods-Saxon form, and the Schrodinger equation was solved to obtain binding energies, scattering parameters, and momentum correlation functions via the Koonin-Pratt formula.

## Key numbers

*   The alpha-J/psi binding energy ranges from 0.1 to 0.6 MeV depending on the spin channel and density model; the highest value of 0.6 MeV occurs for the spin-1/2 alpha-J/psi system with the simple single Gaussian density at rms radius 1.56 fm.
*   The scattering lengths for the alpha-J/psi systems range from 5.5 fm (spin-1/2, SG density, rms 1.56 fm) to 35.2 fm (spin-3/2, rms 1.84 fm).
*   The effective ranges for the alpha-J/psi systems range from 1.74 fm (spin-1/2, SG density, rms 1.56 fm) to 2.45 fm (spin-3/2, rms 1.84 fm).
*   The N-J/psi scattering lengths from HAL QCD are 0.30(2) fm for spin-3/2, 0.38(4) fm for spin-1/2, and 0.21(2) fm for N-eta_c.
*   The rms matter radius of the alpha particle used for the central depression and simple single Gaussian densities is 1.56 fm, with additional calculations at 1.70 and 1.84 fm.
*   The source size where spin-dependent differences in the correlation function become noticeable is around 3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the application of HAL QCD lattice-derived N-charmonium interactions (Yan 2022) to alpha-charmonium systems, building on previous theoretical studies of hyperon-alpha correlations such as Lambda-alpha (Jinno 2024), Xi-alpha (Kamiya 2024), Omega-alpha (Etminan 2024), and phi-alpha (Etminan 2025). The study enables future femtoscopic measurements of alpha-charmonium correlations in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, which could provide independent constraints on the N-charmonium interaction and the nuclear matter distribution of the alpha particle. The comparison of central depression and simple single Gaussian density models shows that correlation functions are more sensitive to the density shape than binding energies, offering a potential tool to probe the alpha particle structure.
