# In-medium modification and decay asymmetry of omega mesons in cold nuclear matter

> We discuss an asymmetry of the decay $ω->e^+e^-$ in nuclear matter with respect to the electron and positron energies. This asymmetry is sensitive to the properties of the $ω$ meson self-energy and, in particular, it has a non-trivial dependence on the $ω$ energy and momentum. Therefore, this asymmetry may serve as a powerful tool in studying the properties of the $ω$ meson in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The asymmetry of the ω → e⁺e⁻ decay with respect to electron and positron energies is sensitive to the properties of the ω meson self-energy in nuclear matter and has a non-trivial dependence on the ω energy and momentum, but the analysis is performed using a simplified resonance model that serves as an illustration rather than a definitive prediction.

## Method

The authors calculate the current-current correlation function for the ω meson in cold nuclear matter using the vector dominance model and the low-density theorem, expressing the ω meson self-energy as a sum of its vacuum part and a contribution from the Compton ωN scattering amplitude. The ωN scattering amplitude is evaluated via a resonance model with tree-level s-channel diagrams for nucleon resonance excitations (including D₁₃(1520), S₁₁(1535), F₁₅(1680), D₁₃(1700), P₁₁(1710), and D₁₃(2080)), where the imaginary part is computed using Cutkosky cutting rules and the real part is obtained from a resonant amplitude ansatz. The asymmetry A^TL = (W^T - W^L)/(W^T + W^L) is then extracted from the longitudinal and transverse parts of the current-current correlation function.

## Key numbers

- At |q| = 0.75 GeV/c, the model predicts an upward shift of the pole position of ΔM = 20 MeV for the longitudinal part and ΔM = 40 MeV for the transverse part of the ω meson self-energy.
- The asymmetry A^TL is zero at |q| = 0 and becomes non-zero at finite |q|, with strong dependence on both the ω meson mass M and momentum |q| in the range |q| ≈ 0.75 GeV/c.
- For the alternative resonance model dominated by P₁₁(1710), the ωNN* coupling constant is g_{ωNN*} = 10.32, which is 4.9 times larger than the value used in the authors' primary model.
- The asymmetry shows a non-monotonic behavior at M ∼ M_ω (approximately 0.78 GeV) for |q| = 0.75 GeV/c, determined by the difference in peak positions between W^T and W^L.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of ω meson in-medium modifications (Caillon 1995, Klingl 1997, Zschocke 2002, Post 2000, Lutz 2001) that gave conflicting predictions for the sign and magnitude of the mass shift, by introducing a new observable, the decay asymmetry, that is sensitive to the dynamics of the ωN interaction. The asymmetry can discriminate between different resonance models, such as those emphasizing F₁₅(1680) versus P₁₁(1710) contributions, and the authors note their results are qualitatively similar to the coupled-channel approach of Mosel (2006). The work enables experimental studies at facilities like KEK and HADES at GSI to use the asymmetry as a tool for determining the ω meson self-energy structure in nuclear matter.
