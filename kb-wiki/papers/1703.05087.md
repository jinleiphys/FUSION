# Calculation of Gamow-Teller and Two-Neutrino Double-Beta Decay Properties for 130Te and 136Xe with a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential

> We report on the calculation of Gamow-Teller and double-beta decay properties for nuclei around 132Sn within the framework of the realistic shell model. The effective shell-model Hamiltonian and Gamow-Teller transition operator are derived by way of many-body perturbation theory, without resorting to empirical effective quenching factor for the Gamow-Teller operator. The results are then compared with the available experimental data, in order to establish the reliability of our approach. This is a mandatory step, before we apply the same methodology, in forthcoming studies, to the calculation of the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element for nuclei that are currently considered among the best candidates for the detection of this process.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a realistic shell-model approach using a free nucleon-nucleon potential (CD-Bonn) and many-body perturbation theory can reproduce experimental Gamow-Teller strengths and two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements for 130Te and 136Xe without resorting to an empirical quenching factor for the Gamow-Teller operator, though the effective operator derived from many-body theory naturally produces quenching factors ranging from 0.50 to 0.69 for different orbital transitions.

## Method

The authors start from the high-precision CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, renormalized via the V-low-k approach with a cutoff momentum of 2.6 fm^-1, and derive an effective shell-model Hamiltonian and effective Gamow-Teller transition operator using the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded-diagram expansion through third order in perturbation theory. The model space consists of five proton and neutron orbitals (0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, 0h11/2) outside the doubly-closed 100Sn core. Calculations are performed with both theoretical and empirical single-particle energies to test sensitivity, and the Lanczos strength function method is used to compute the two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For 130Te, the theoretical total GT- strength up to 3 MeV excitation energy is 0.842 (with single-particle energies I) and 0.873 (with single-particle energies II), compared to the experimental value of 0.746 ± 0.045
- The two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix element for 130Te → 130Xe is 0.044 MeV^-1 (single-particle energies I) and 0.046 MeV^-1 (single-particle energies II), compared to the experimental value of 0.034 ± 0.003 MeV^-1
- For 136Xe, the theoretical total GT- strength up to 4.5 MeV excitation energy is 0.94 (single-particle energies I) and 1.13 (single-particle energies II), compared to the experimental value of 1.33 ± 0.07
- The two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix element for 136Xe → 136Ba is 0.0285 MeV^-1 (single-particle energies I) and 0.0287 MeV^-1 (single-particle energies II), compared to the experimental value of 0.0218 ± 0.0003 MeV^-1
- The bare GT operator (without many-body renormalization) gives matrix elements of 0.131 MeV^-1 (I) and 0.137 MeV^-1 (II) for 130Te, far from the experimental value
- The B(E2; 2+ → 0+) for 130Te is calculated as 430 e^2fm^4 (I) and 420 e^2fm^4 (II), compared to the experimental value of 580 ± 20 e^2fm^4
- The B(E2; 2+ → 0+) for 136Xe is calculated as 300 e^2fm^4 (both I and II), compared to the experimental value of 420 ± 20 e^2fm^4

## Context

This work extends the realistic shell-model approach previously applied by the same group to nuclei around 132Sn, and provides a necessary validation step before calculating neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements for 130Te and 136Xe, which are targets of the CUORE, EXO-200, and KamLAND-Zen experiments. The results are compared with recent shell-model calculations by Caurier (2012) using empirically modified realistic potentials (which gave 0.043 MeV^-1 for 130Te with a quenching factor of 0.57) and by Neacsu (2015) (which gave 0.0328 MeV^-1 for 130Te with a quenching factor of 0.74), as well as with calculations by Horoi (2013) using a different realistic potential (N3LO) that gave 0.062 MeV^-1 for 136Xe. The approach enables future calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements without the need for empirical quenching factors, addressing the open question of whether the same quenching factor should apply to all multipole channels in the neutrinoless decay.
