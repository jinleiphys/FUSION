# Towards finite density QCD with Taylor expansions

> Convergence properties of Taylor expansions of observables, which are also used in lattice QCD calculations at non-zero chemical potential, are analyzed in an effective N_f = 2+1 flavor Polyakov-quark-meson model. A recently developed algorithmic technique allows the calculation of higher-order Taylor expansion coefficients in functional approaches. This novel technique is for the first time applied to an effective N_f = 2+1 flavor Polyakov-quark-meson model and the findings are compared with the full model solution at finite densities. The results are used to discuss prospects for locating the QCD phase boundary and a possible critical endpoint in the phase diagram.

## Key claim

The convergence properties of Taylor expansions of thermodynamic observables around zero chemical potential, as used in lattice QCD, are tested in an effective Polyakov-quark-meson model; the radius of convergence estimators from the pressure and quark number susceptibility series converge to the same limit only in the infinite-order limit, and at finite order the susceptibility-based estimator may be numerically closer to the true radius.

## Method

The authors compute Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure and quark number susceptibility up to 22nd order in the N_f = 2+1 flavor Polyakov-quark-meson model using algorithmic differentiation, a technique that handles implicit mu-dependence of order parameters with high numerical precision. The model is solved in mean-field approximation, and the Taylor series results are compared with the full model solution at finite chemical potential. Padé resummation is also applied to improve convergence and characterize singularities.

## Key numbers

- The chiral crossover at vanishing chemical potential occurs at T_chi ~ 206 MeV.
- In mean-field approximation, the critical endpoint (CEP) is located at (T_c, mu_c) ~ (185, 167) MeV, corresponding to T_c ~ 0.9 T_chi and mu_c/T_c ~ 0.9.
- Taylor coefficients for n > 4 show rapid oscillations near the transition temperature T_chi, within a 5% temperature interval around T_chi (0.95 < T/T_chi < 1.05).
- The estimator for the radius of convergence from the susceptibility series, r_{2n}^chi, is related to the pressure series estimator by r_{2n}^chi = r_{2n+2} (1 - 1/n + O(n^{-2})) for large n.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Taylor expansion convergence properties, previously limited by the difficulty of obtaining higher-order coefficients in lattice QCD (Allton 2002, Gavai 2003), by applying a new algorithmic differentiation technique (Wagner 2009) to an effective model. The results enable a systematic comparison of different estimators for the radius of convergence and the phase boundary, and they inform strategies for locating a possible critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram using lattice QCD data. The Polyakov-quark-meson model results are compared with full model mean-field solutions (Schaefer 2009, Wambach 2009).
