# Antiproton Production in p+d Reaction at Subthreshold Energies

> An enhancement of antiprotons produced in p+d reaction in comparison with ones in p+p elementary reaction is investigated. In the neighborhood of subthreshold energy the enhancement is caused by the difference of available energies for antiproton production. The cross section in p+d reaction, on the other hand, becomes just twice of the one in elementary p+p reaction at the incident energy far from the threshold energy when non-nucleonic components in deuteron target are not considered.

## Key claim

The enhancement of antiproton production in p+d reactions compared to p+p reactions near subthreshold energies can be explained solely by the difference in available energy for antiproton production, without invoking non-nucleonic components in the deuteron wave function; at incident energies far above threshold, the p+d cross section becomes exactly twice the p+p cross section when non-nucleonic components are not considered.

## Method

The authors calculated the total cross section for antiproton production in p+p → p̅ppp using a constant invariant matrix element fitted to data near threshold, assuming the cross section is proportional to the four-body phase space. They then extended this to p+d reactions by treating the deuteron target non-relativistically with a Reid soft core potential wave function parameterized as a sum of three Gaussians, considering two processes: deuteron bound (p+d → p̅ppd) and deuteron breakup (p+d → p̅pppn). The cross section ratio σ(p+d)/σ(p+p) was computed as a function of incident energy.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron wave function parameters are: a₁ = 540 (GeV)⁻³/², d₁ = 0.058 GeV; a₂ = 72, d₂ = 0.158 GeV; a₃ = 4.4, d₃ = 0.58 GeV.
- The available energy for p+p reaction is 7M (where M is the proton mass), while for p+d reaction it is 5M + ε (where ε is the deuteron binding energy), defining the subthreshold energy for p̅ production in p+d.
- At incident energies far above threshold, the p+d cross section becomes exactly twice the p+p cross section when the small binding energy of the deuteron is neglected.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of subthreshold antiproton production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions, particularly following the surprising enhancement observed in d+A reactions at KEK (Chiba et al., 1993). The authors address the suggestion that non-nucleonic components in the deuteron wave function might explain this enhancement, as explored by the BUU approach group (Cassing, Lykasov and Teis, 1994). The present calculation shows that the enhancement near threshold can be explained by available energy differences alone, and the ratio approaches a factor of two at higher energies, providing a baseline for future calculations that include non-nucleonic components.
