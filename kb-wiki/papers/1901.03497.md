# Effects of strong magnetic field on the formation of wakes in thermal QCD

> We have investigated how the wakes in the induced charge density and in the potential due to the passage of highly energetic partons through a thermal QCD medium get affected by the presence of strong magnetic field. For that purpose, we wish to analyze first the dielectric responses of the medium both in presence and absence of strong magnetic field. We found that for slow moving partons, the real part of dielectric function is not affected by the magnetic field whereas for fast moving partons, for small $|\textbf{k}|$, it becomes very large and approaches towards its counterpart at $B=0$, for large $|\textbf{k}|$. On the other hand the imaginary part is decreased for both slow and fast moving partons, due to the fact that the imaginary contribution due to quark-loop vanishes. With these ingredients, we found that the oscillation in the (scaled) induced charge density, due to the very fast partons becomes less pronounced in the presence of strong magnetic field whereas for smaller parton velocity, no significant change is observed. For the (scaled) wake potential along the motion of fast moving partons (which is of Lennard-Jones (LJ) type), the depth of negative minimum in the backward region gets reduced drastically, resulting in the reduction of the amplitude of oscillation. On the other hand in the forward region, it remains as the screened Coulomb one, except the screening now becomes much stronger for higher parton velocity. Similarly for the wake potential transverse to the motion of partons in both forward and backward regions, the depth of LJ potential for fast moving partons gets decreased severely, but still retains the forward-backward symmetry. However, for lower parton velocity, the magnetic field does not affect it significantly.

## Key claim

The paper claims that strong magnetic fields (SMF) significantly modify the wakes in induced charge density and wake potential created by fast-moving partons in a thermal QCD medium, but only for parton velocities close to the speed of light; for slower partons (v=0.55) the magnetic field has minimal effect. The hedge is that the imaginary part of the quark-loop contribution to the gluon self-energy vanishes except for the unphysical case v=1, so the magnetic field's main influence comes through the real part of the quark-loop contribution.

## Method

The authors compute the longitudinal component of the gluon self-energy tensor in the strong magnetic field limit (|q_f B| >> T^2 and |q_f B| >> m_f^2) by treating quark and gluon loops separately: quarks are restricted to the lowest Landau level (LLL), reducing their dynamics to (1+1) dimensions, while gluons remain unaffected by the magnetic field and are treated via hard thermal loop (HTL) perturbation theory. The real and imaginary parts of the dielectric function are then obtained from the self-energy, and the induced charge density and wake potential are calculated in coordinate space using linear response theory for parton velocities v=0.55 and v=0.99.

## Key numbers

- Strong magnetic field condition: |q_f B| >> T^2 and |q_f B| >> m_f^2 (no specific numerical B value given, but the scale is set by the magnetic field dominating over temperature and quark masses)
- Parton velocities studied: v = 0.55 (slow) and v = 0.99 (fast)
- The real part of the quark-loop contribution to the self-energy in SMF: Re Π^{q,∥}_V = (g^2 / 4π^2) Σ_f |q_f B| (k_z^2 / k_∥^2)
- The imaginary part of the quark-loop contribution in SMF: Im Π^{q,∥}_{n^2} = -(g^2 Σ_f |q_f B| / 8π) k_0 [δ(k_0 - k_z) + δ(k_0 + k_z)]
- Gluon-loop contribution (HTL): Re Π^{g,L} = g'^2 T^2 [(k_0 / 2|k|) ln|(k_0 + |k|)/(k_0 - |k|)| - 1]; Im Π^{g,L} = -g'^2 T^2 (π k_0 / 2|k|)
- Running coupling parameters: μ_0 = 1.1 GeV, Λ_V = 0.385 GeV, M_B ~ 1 GeV, string tension σ = 0.18 GeV^2, Λ_QCD ~ 0.200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of wakes in thermal QCD, previously investigated by Ruppert et al. (2005) and Chakraborty et al. (2006) using HTL calculations, by incorporating the effects of a strong magnetic field. It also builds on the gluon self-energy formalism in magnetic fields developed by Hattori et al. (2013, 2018) and the bosonization technique applied to QCD in strong magnetic fields by Fukushima et al. (2016). The results suggest that the modification of wake potentials could serve as a signal for the existence of strong magnetic fields in noncentral heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, potentially observable through correlations between high-p_T secondary particles.
