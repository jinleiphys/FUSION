# Supernova Preshock Neutronization Burst as a Probe of Non-Standard Neutrino Interactions

> Core-collapse supernova (CCSN) provides a unique astrophysical site for studying neutrino-matter interactions. Prior to the shock-breakout neutrino burst during the collapse of the iron core, a preshock $ν_e$ burst arises from the electron capture of nuclei and it is sensitive to the low-energy coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) which dominates the neutrino opacity. Since the CE$ν$NS depends strongly on the non-standard neutrino interactions (NSI) which are completely beyond the standard model and yet to be determined, the detection of the preshock burst thus provides a clean way to extract the NSI information. Within the spherically symmetric general-relativistic hydrodynamic simulation for the CCSN, we investigate the NSI effects on the preshock burst. We find that the NSI can maximally enhance the peak luminosity of the preshock burst almost by a factor of three, reaching a value comparable with that of the shock-breakout burst. Future detection of the preshock burst will have critical implications on astrophysics, neutrino physics and physics beyond the standard model.

## Key claim

The preshock neutronization burst in core-collapse supernovae can serve as a clean probe of the largely unknown non-standard neutrino interaction (NSI) parameters εee^{uV} and εee^{dV}, with the caveat that the peak luminosity enhancement depends on the specific values of these parameters and can reach up to a factor of three relative to the standard model case.

## Method

The authors performed spherically symmetric general-relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of a 15 solar mass solar-metallicity progenitor star using the GR1D code, with the SFHo equation of state as default. They modified the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS) cross sections in the NuLib neutrino interaction library by replacing the standard model neutral-current vector couplings with NSI-modified couplings for ν_e scattering on nuclei, nucleons, and alpha particles. The preshock ν_e burst was then extracted from the simulated time evolution of neutrino luminosities during the infall phase and early neutronization stage.

## Key numbers

- Peak number luminosity of the preshock burst reaches ~2.1 × 10^58 s^{-1} for ε^u = 0.2 (ε^d = 0), compared to ~0.86 × 10^58 s^{-1} for the standard model (ε^u = 0)
- Peak energy luminosity of the preshock burst reaches ~3.9 × 10^53 erg·s^{-1} for ε^u = 0.2, compared to ~1.3 × 10^53 erg·s^{-1} for the standard model
- The shock-breakout burst peak luminosity is ~2.5 × 10^58 s^{-1} (~6.0 × 10^53 erg·s^{-1}), comparable to the NSI-enhanced preshock burst
- Average ν_e energy of the preshock burst is ~10 MeV; shock-breakout burst average energies are ~14 MeV for ν_e, ~15 MeV for heavy-flavor neutrinos, and ~10 MeV for anti-ν_e
- The central electron fraction after bounce is reduced from 0.281 to 0.247 as ε^u varies from 0 to 0.2
- The discovery region for ε^u at 3σ significance with Hyper-Kamiokande via electron scattering is [0.015, 0.388] combined with [0.415, 0.5] for no neutrino oscillation

## Context

This work extends previous studies of NSI effects in supernovae, particularly the estimate by Stapleford et al. (2021) on NSI effects on neutrino-nucleon scattering in the post-bounce core, by focusing on the earlier preshock phase where CEνNS dominates the opacity. The preshock burst was previously identified in simulations by Liebendoerfer (2003), Kachelriess et al. (2005), and Walk et al. (2016), but this work is the first to demonstrate its sensitivity to the poorly constrained vector NSI parameters ε_{ee}^{uV} and ε_{ee}^{dV}. The results enable future detection of the preshock burst by large-scale detectors such as Hyper-Kamiokande and JUNO to constrain NSI parameters, potentially breaking degeneracies that remain from combined analyses of oscillation experiments and COHERENT data.
