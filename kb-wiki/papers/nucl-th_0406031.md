# Signals of spinodal phase decomposition in high-energy nuclear collisions

> High-energy nuclear collisions produce quark-gluon plasmas that expand and hadronize. If the associated phase transition is of first order then the hadronization should proceed through a spinodal phase separation. We explore here the possibility of identifying the associated clumping by analysis of suitable N-particle momentum correlations.

## Key claim

If the quark-gluon plasma to hadron gas phase transition is of first order, then hadronization should proceed through spinodal phase separation, and the resulting transient clumpiness may be identifiable through analysis of suitable N-particle momentum correlations, though the paper acknowledges that lattice calculations are not yet sufficiently accurate to predict the critical chemical potential with confidence and that no microscopic dynamical model is yet available for the phase coexistence region.

## Method

The author constructs schematic scenarios where a clumpy system is represented as a number of distinct thermal sources (plasma blobs) that hadronize independently according to thermal distributions, with collective expansion causing the spatial configuration of blobs to become reflected in their motions. For these scenarios, the author calculates two-particle rapidity correlation functions in one dimension (longitudinal rapidity) and three dimensions (relative rapidity), as well as N-body correlation functions based on internal kinetic energy per particle, comparing correlated distributions to uncorrelated reference distributions obtained by event mixing.

## Key numbers

- Thermal rapidity dispersion at T=170 MeV: σ_T = 0.69 for π, 0.48 for K, 0.38 for N, 0.35 for Λ
- Thermal rapidity dispersion at T=130 MeV: σ_T = 0.65 for π, 0.44 for K, 0.34 for N, 0.32 for Λ
- For the standard 3D scenario with N_φ=4, Δy=1, y_T=0.6, σ_∥=0.3, σ_⊥=0.3, the relative rapidity between neighboring sources is approximately 1.20
- The N-body correlation signal strength approximately doubles for each additional particle added (N=2,3,4)
- C_N(κ) falls off approximately as ~exp(κ/T_N) with T_N ≈ (1-N^{-1})

## Context

This work extends the study of spinodal decomposition from intermediate-energy nuclear collisions (where the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition causes multifragmentation, as studied by Chomaz (2004) and Borderie (2001)) to high-energy nuclear collisions producing quark-gluon plasmas. The analysis builds on earlier schematic growth rate calculations by Randrup (2003) and fluid dynamical studies by Harri, and suggests that experimental data from RHIC, SPS, or the future GSI facility could be analyzed for evidence of spinodal clumping, which would provide constraints on the equation of state and the character of the phase transition in strongly interacting matter.
