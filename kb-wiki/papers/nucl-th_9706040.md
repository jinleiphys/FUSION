# Kaon Productions Off Nucleons And The Structure of Baryon Resonances

> The recent Investigations in the chiral quark model show that kaon productions of nucleon play an important role in understanding the structure of baryons. The evidences of a third $S_{11}$ resonance in the second resonance region and two narrow states around 2 GeV suggest a set of the molecular type baryons with hidden strangeness. Confirming these states requires further theoretical and experimental studies of the strangeness production.

# Kaon Productions Off Nucleons And The Structure of Baryon Resonances

## Key claim
The paper argues that circumstantial evidence suggests a third S11 resonance with mass 1.7–1.8 GeV and width around 0.2 GeV exists in the second resonance region, and that two narrow states X(2000) and X(2050) observed by the SPHINX collaboration may be molecular-type baryons with hidden strangeness. However, the author qualifies that confirming these states requires further theoretical and experimental studies of strangeness production, and that a pure KΛ or KΣ configuration for S11(1535) is inconsistent with existing electromagnetic form factor data.

## Method
The author reviews evidence from η photoproduction data (particularly from MAMI) and from the SPHINX collaboration’s p+C coherent diffractive production data, analyzing them within the chiral quark model framework for meson photoproductions. The analysis compares the masses of candidate resonances to the threshold energies for kaon and K* production, and discusses how the quark model approach relates photoproduction data to the spin-flavor structure of baryon resonances. The author also examines preliminary data from ELSA for γp → K+Λ and γp → K+Σ0 reactions.

## Key numbers
- Third S11 resonance candidate: mass 1.7–1.8 GeV, width around 0.2 GeV
- X(2000) → Σ0K+: mass = 1996 ± 7 MeV, width = 99 ± 21 MeV
- X(2050) → Σ*(1385)K+: mass = 2052 ± 6 MeV, width = 35 ± 29 MeV
- Threshold energy for K*Λ production: 2007 MeV
- Threshold energy for K*Σ production: 2084 MeV
- S11(1535) resonance is enhanced in η photoproduction in the threshold region
- S11(1650) is enhanced in the threshold region of γn → K−p

## Context
This work extends the chiral quark model approach developed by Li (1997) for meson photoproductions, and builds on earlier findings by Li (1996) that the enhancement of S11(1535) and suppression of S11(1650) in the ηN channel cannot be explained in the constituent quark model. The paper references the KΛ or KΣ quasi-bound state proposal for S11(1535) by Kaiser and collaborators, and the K*Λ and K*Σ interpretation for X(2000) and X(2050) by Shuryak and collaborators. The results suggest that forthcoming kaon production data from TJNAF and ELSA could test the existence of molecular-type baryons with hidden strangeness and help resolve the puzzle of S11 resonances in the second resonance region.
