# Centrality Dependence of Directed and Elliptic Flow at the SPS

> New data with a minimum bias trigger for 158 GeV/nucleon Pb + Pb have been analyzed. Directed and elliptic flow as a function of rapidity of the particles and centrality of the collision are presented. The centrality dependence of the ratio of elliptic flow to the initial space elliptic anisotropy is compared to models.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of elliptic flow divided by the initial spatial elliptic anisotropy (\(v_2/\varepsilon\)) for 158 GeV/nucleon Pb+Pb collisions is presented as preliminary data, with the caveat that only part of the available data has been analyzed and no systematic errors have been included yet.

## Method

The NA49 collaboration analyzed minimum bias trigger data for 158 GeV/nucleon Pb+Pb collisions, combining tracks from the Main and Vertex Time Projection Chambers to achieve full forward hemisphere coverage. Events were sorted into six centrality bins using the Zero Degree Calorimeter, with impact parameter values estimated from the number of participants obtained by integrating particle yields. Directed flow (\(v_1\)) and elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) were extracted from the Fourier decomposition of the azimuthal distribution of particles, and the elliptic flow was divided by the calculated initial spatial elliptic anisotropy (\(\varepsilon\)) for comparison with model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Pion elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) values increase continuously as the reaction becomes more peripheral, with data integrated over rapidity up to \(y = 6\).
- The experimental \(v_2/\varepsilon\) values lie below hydrodynamical model predictions (Kolb 1999), indicating a lack of complete equilibration in the reaction.
- The data are above the RQMD v2.3 resonance gas model and close to the RQMD v3.0 calculation that includes a phase transition.
- The analysis covers impact parameters up to about 11 fm, with the most peripheral bin (cen6) corresponding to the largest impact parameters.

## Context

This work extends the NA49 collaboration's earlier flow results from medium impact parameter triggered data (NA49 1997, 1998) by providing a minimum bias trigger dataset that enables systematic study of centrality dependence. The ratio \(v_2/\varepsilon\) is compared to hydrodynamical calculations by Ollitrault (1992) and Kolb (1999), as well as to RQMD v2.3 and v3.0 simulations by Sorge (1997, 1999), with the latter including a phase transition. The results enable further investigation of the degree of thermalization and the possible onset of a phase transition in nuclear matter at SPS energies, though the authors note that final conclusions require processing the full NA49 dataset.
