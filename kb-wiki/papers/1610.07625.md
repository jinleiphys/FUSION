# Consistent Chiral Kinetic Theory in Weyl Materials: Chiral Magnetic Plasmons

> We argue that the correct definition of the electric current in the chiral kinetic theory for Weyl materials should include the Chern-Simons contribution that makes the theory consistent with the local conservation of the electric charge in electromagnetic and strain-induced pseudoelectromagnetic fields. By making use of such a kinetic theory, we study the plasma frequencies of collective modes in Weyl materials in constant magnetic and pseudomagnetic fields, taking into account the effects of dynamical electromagnetism. We show that the collective modes are chiral plasmons. While the plasma frequency of the longitudinal collective mode coincides with the Langmuir one, this mode is unusual because it is characterized not only by oscillations of the electric current density, but also by oscillations of the chiral current density. The latter are triggered by a dynamical version of the chiral electric separation effect. We also find that the plasma frequencies of the transverse modes split up in a magnetic field. This finding suggests an efficient means of extracting the chiral shift parameter from the measurement of the plasma frequencies in Weyl materials.

# Consistent Chiral Kinetic Theory in Weyl Materials: Chiral Magnetic Plasmons

## Key claim

The correct definition of the electric current in chiral kinetic theory for Weyl materials must include the Chern-Simons (Bardeen-Zumino) topological contribution to restore local electric charge conservation in electromagnetic and strain-induced pseudoelectromagnetic fields; the collective modes in such materials are chiral magnetic plasmons whose transverse mode frequencies split in a magnetic field, providing a means to extract the chiral shift parameter.

## Method

The authors developed a consistent chiral kinetic theory by adding the Bardeen-Zumino topological term to the electric current, then solved the collisionless kinetic equations for chiral fermions with Berry curvature in constant background magnetic and pseudomagnetic fields. Using linear response theory at zero wave vector, they derived the polarization tensor and solved the characteristic equation from Maxwell's equations to obtain the dispersion relations for collective modes, including the oscillating chiral current density from a dynamical chiral electric separation effect.

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory framework of Stephanov and Son (2015) by incorporating the Bardeen-Zumino polynomial to resolve the electric charge nonconservation problem identified in earlier treatments by Pikulin (2016) and Grushin-Vishwanath (2016). The results show that the chiral magnetic wave proposed by Kharzeev (2010) becomes a longitudinal plasmon when dynamical electromagnetism is included, and the predicted splitting of transverse plasmon frequencies enables experimental extraction of the chiral shift parameter in Weyl materials such as TaAs and NbP, building on band theory calculations by Franz (2015) and anomalous Hall effect studies by Grushin (2012).
