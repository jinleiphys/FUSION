# New Geiger-Nuttall law for proton radioactivity

> In the present work considering the contributions of the daughter nuclear charge and the orbital angular momentum taken away by the emitted proton, we propose a two-parameter formula of new Geiger-Nuttall law for proton radioactivity. A set of universal parameters of this law is obtained by fitting 44 experimental data of proton emitters in the ground state and isomeric state. The calculated results can reproduce the experimental data well. For a comparison, the calculations performed using other theoretical methods, such as UDLP proposed by Qi, et al. [https://journals.aps.org/prc/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevC.85.011303], the CPPM-Guo2013 analyzed by our previous work [Deng, et al., https://link.springer.com/article/10.1140/epja/i2019-12728-0] and the modified Gamow-like model proposed by us [Chen, et al., https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1361-6471/ab1a56] are also included. Meanwhile, we extend this new Geiger-Nuttall law to predict the proton radioactivity half-lives for $51 \leq Z \leq 91$ nuclei, whose proton radioactivity is energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified in NUBASE2016.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new two-parameter Geiger-Nuttall law for proton radioactivity that incorporates both the daughter nuclear charge and the orbital angular momentum taken away by the emitted proton, claiming it reproduces experimental half-lives with a standard deviation of 0.397, which is a 7.0% improvement over the Universal Decay Law for Proton emission (UDLP) proposed by Qi et al. (2012).

## Method

The authors fit a two-parameter formula, log₁₀T₁/₂(s) = a(Z_d⁰·⁸ + l)Q_p⁻¹/² + b, to 44 experimental half-lives of proton emitters in ground and isomeric states, where Z_d is the daughter charge number, l is the orbital angular momentum, and Q_p is the decay energy. The optimal exponent for the orbital angular momentum term was determined by varying β from 0.5 to 1.5 and selecting the value giving the smallest standard deviation. The resulting parameters a = 0.843 and b = -27.194 were then used to calculate half-lives and to predict values for 23 nuclei with Z between 51 and 91 that are energetically allowed for proton emission but not yet quantified in NUBASE2016.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation σ = 0.397 between calculated and experimental log₁₀T₁/₂ values for the new formula, corresponding to an average deviation factor of 2.49 from experiment.
- The new formula improves upon UDLP (σ = 0.427) by 7.0%, and also outperforms CPPM-Guo2013 (σ = 0.472) and the modified Gamow-like model (σ = 0.501).
- The optimal exponent for the orbital angular momentum term was found to be β = 1.0, determined by scanning β from 0.5 to 1.5.
- The formula parameters are a = 0.843 and b = -27.194, obtained by fitting 44 experimental data points.

## Context

This work extends the classic Geiger-Nuttall law, originally formulated for alpha decay, to proton radioactivity by incorporating the daughter charge and orbital angular momentum, following the approach of Brown (1992) for alpha decay and the empirical formulas of Budaca (2017) and Sreeja et al. (2018) for proton emission. The new formula provides a simpler two-parameter alternative to the UDLP of Qi et al. (2012) and to more computationally intensive models such as the Coulomb and proximity potential model (CPPM-Guo2013) analyzed by Deng et al. (2019) and the modified Gamow-like model of Chen et al. (2019). The predictions for 23 unmeasured proton emitters in the range 51 ≤ Z ≤ 91 can guide future experimental searches.
