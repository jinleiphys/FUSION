# Signatures of QCD Matter at RHIC

> We discuss possible experimental signatures of forming a Quark-Gluon plasma in high energy nuclear collisions. In first order phase transitions such as the chiral symmetry restoration supercooling may lead to density fluctuations such as droplet formation and hot spots. These will lead to rapidity fluctuations event-by-event and triggering on such fluctuations will have distinct effects for HBT radii.

## Key claim

In a first-order phase transition such as chiral symmetry restoration, supercooling may produce density fluctuations (droplets of dense chiral-symmetric matter) that lead to event-by-event rapidity fluctuations; triggering on these fluctuations yields distinct effects on HBT radii that are opposite to predictions from cascade and hydrodynamic calculations, providing a clean signal of a first-order transition.

## Method

The authors model the QCD phase diagram using a random matrix theory (RMT) effective thermodynamic potential with a ϕ⁶ form, which predicts a tricritical point and spinodal lines. They then parametrize droplets of supercooled chiral-symmetric matter as spatial and temporal Gaussians of size R_d and duration t_d, embedded in a larger nuclear overlap region of size R_A, and compute the two-particle correlation function C₂(q) from Bose-Einstein interference for both non-expanding and expanding sources. The resulting HBT radii (R_s, R_o, R_l) are compared for the case of many overlapping droplets versus the case where droplets are separated in rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Tricritical point in the RMT model: T₃/T_c = ½√(√2 + 1) and μ₃/μ₀ ≈ 0.610
- Discontinuity in density at (T=0, μ₀): Δn ≈ 2.5 n₀, where n₀ is the equilibrium density of nuclear matter
- Chiral transition temperature at μ=0: T_c ≈ 140 MeV
- Typical heavy ion source size: R ∼ 5 fm, so interference occurs when q ≲ ħ/R ∼ 40 MeV/c
- Typical particle momenta: k_i ≳ K ∼ 300 MeV/c
- Reduction factor λ in HBT fits: λ ∼ 0.5 for pions, λ ∼ 0.9 for kaons
- Droplet rapidity separation condition: |η_i − η_j| ≳ √(T/m_⊥); with Δz ∼ 1 fm and τ₀ ≲ 1 fm/c, this condition is satisfied even for light pions
- Nuclear size scaling: R_A(b) ≃ √(R_A(0)² − b²), where R_A(0) ≃ 1.2 A^{1/3} fm

## Context

This work extends the discussion of QCD phase transition signatures at RHIC beyond established signals such as J/ψ suppression, strangeness enhancement, and η' enhancement (Shuryak). It builds on lattice QCD results suggesting a first-order transition at zero chemical potential for sufficiently light strange quarks, and on random matrix theory work (Jackson) providing an effective thermodynamic potential. The paper proposes that triggering on rapidity fluctuations and measuring the centrality dependence of HBT radii can distinguish droplet formation from the predictions of cascade and hydrodynamic calculations (Rischke), enabling experimental identification of a first-order phase transition at RHIC.
