# Ab initio construction of the energy density functional for electron systems with the functional-renormalization-group-aided density functional theory

> We show an $\textit{ab initio}$ construction of the energy density functional (EDF) for electron systems using the functional renormalization group. The correlation energies of the homogeneous electron gas given in our framework reproduce the exact behavior at high density and agree with the Monte-Carlo data in a wide range of densities. Our analytic technique enables us to get the correlation energies efficiently for various densities, which realizes the determination of EDF in the local density approximation (LDA) without any fitting for physically relevant densities. Applied to the Kohn-Sham calculation for the noble gas atoms, our EDF shows comparable results to those of other conventional ones in LDA.

## Key claim
The authors present the first numerical application of functional-renormalization-group-aided density functional theory (FRG-DFT) to (3+1)-dimensional electron systems, constructing a correlation energy functional for the homogeneous electron gas that reproduces the exact high-density limit from Gell-Mann–Brueckner resummation and agrees with diffusion Monte-Carlo data across a wide density range, without any fitting for physically relevant densities.

## Method
The authors apply the functional renormalization group to the (3+1)-dimensional homogeneous electron gas, using a second-order vertex expansion of the flow equation for the effective action. They derive an analytic expression for the correlation energy per particle as a function of the Wigner-Seitz radius, and reduce the multi-dimensional momentum integrals to a double integral using cylindrical coordinates and analytic Bessel function identities. The resulting correlation energies are computed on 65536 grid points over a logarithmic mesh from 10⁻⁶ a.u. to 100 a.u., and used to construct a local density approximation (LDA) functional via linear interpolation, with extrapolation beyond 100 a.u. using a fitted function.

## Key numbers
- Correlation energy deviations from Ceperley and Alder (1980) DMC results: about 2.0% at r_s = 1 a.u., 6.7% at r_s = 50 a.u., and 17% at r_s = 100 a.u.
- FRG-PZ fitting parameters: C = 0.00173055, D = -0.0100569, gamma = -0.175617, beta_1 = 1.67669, beta_2 = 0.348219 (all in Hartree atomic units)
- FRG-NT extrapolation parameters for r_s >= 100 a.u.: gamma = 0.0378052, beta_1 = -0.801035, beta_2 = -0.0306778
- Computation performed on 65536 grid points over r_s in [10⁻⁶ a.u., 100 a.u.)

## Context
This work extends the FRG-DFT formalism previously applied to lower-dimensional systems (Kemler 2013, 2017; Liao 2018; Yokota 2018, 2019) to realistic three-dimensional electron systems for the first time. It provides a non-empirical alternative to conventional LDA functionals such as VWN (Vosko, Wilk, Nusair 1980), PZ81 (Perdew, Zunger 1981), and PW92 (Perdew, Wang 1992), which rely on fitting a few DMC data points from Ceperley and Alder (1980). The approach enables systematic construction of EDFs without empirical parameters and can be extended beyond LDA to include gradient effects and spin polarization.
