# Chiral Magnetic Wave

> We consider a relativistic plasma containing charged chiral fermions in an external magnetic field, e.g a chirally symmetric quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We show that triangle anomalies imply the existence of a new type of collective gapless excitation in this system that stems from the coupling between the density waves of the electric and chiral charges; we call it "the Chiral Magnetic Wave" (CMW). The CMW exists even in a neutral plasma, i.e. in the absence of the axial and vector chemical potentials. We demonstrate the existence of CMW and study its properties using three different approaches: i) relativistic magnetohydrodynamics; ii) dimensional reduction to $(1+1)$ Sine-Gordon model, appropriate in a strong magnetic field; and iii) holographic QCD (Sakai-Sugimoto model), appropriate at strong coupling. We also briefly discuss the phenomenological implications of the CMW for heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new type of collective gapless excitation, called the Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW), exists in a relativistic plasma containing charged chiral fermions in an external magnetic field, even when the plasma is neutral with no axial or vector chemical potentials. The existence of the CMW is a direct consequence of the coupling between the Chiral Magnetic Effect and the Chiral Separation Effect, which are both rooted in triangle anomalies.

## Method

The authors demonstrate the existence and properties of the CMW using three complementary approaches: (i) relativistic magnetohydrodynamics, where they derive a hydrodynamic dispersion relation for the wave; (ii) dimensional reduction to a (1+1) dimensional Sine-Gordon model, appropriate in the limit of a strong magnetic field; and (iii) holographic QCD using the Sakai-Sugimoto model, which is appropriate at strong coupling. In each approach, they analyze the linearized fluctuations of electric and chiral charge densities and currents along the direction of the applied magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* The velocity of the CMW in the hydrodynamic regime is given by \( v_\chi = N_c e B \alpha / (2\pi^2) \), where \(\alpha\) is the susceptibility of the charge densities.
* In the limit of a strong magnetic field (\( eB \gg T^2 \)), the CMW velocity reaches the speed of light: \( v_\chi = 1 \).
* The dispersion relation for the CMW is \( \omega = \mp v_\chi k - i D_L k^2 + \cdots \), where \( D_L \) is the longitudinal diffusion constant.
* The density of left-handed fermions in a strong magnetic field is given by \( j^0_L = N_c (eB/(2\pi)) (\mu_L/(2\pi)) \).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of anomalous transport in relativistic plasmas, building directly on the previously identified Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and Chiral Separation Effect (CSE). The CME, which describes electric charge separation along a magnetic field due to an axial chemical potential, was introduced by Kharzeev (2004) and others, while the CSE, describing axial current generation from a vector chemical potential, was established by Son and others (2004). The key insight is that the coupling of these two effects produces a propagating wave mode, even in a neutral plasma, which was not previously recognized. The existence of the CMW has implications for the phenomenology of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, as it may induce observable charge asymmetries that do not vanish when averaged over many collision events, unlike the purely fluctuating CME signal.
