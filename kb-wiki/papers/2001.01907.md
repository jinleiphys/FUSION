# Nuclear magnetic moments of francium 207-213 from precision hyperfine comparisons

> We report a fourfold improvement in the determination of nuclear magnetic moments for neutron-deficient isotopes of francium-207--213, reducing the uncertainties from 2% for most isotopes to 0.5%. These are found by comparing our high-precision calculations of hyperfine structure constants for the ground states with experimental values. In particular, we show the importance of a careful modeling of the Bohr-Weisskopf effect, which arises due to the finite nuclear magnetization distribution. This effect is particularly large in Fr and until now has not been modeled with sufficiently high accuracy. An improved understanding of the nuclear magnetic moments and Bohr-Weisskopf effect are crucial for benchmarking the atomic theory required in precision tests of the standard model, in particular atomic parity violation studies, that are underway in francium.

## Key claim

The authors report a fourfold improvement in the determination of nuclear magnetic moments for neutron-deficient francium isotopes 207-213, reducing uncertainties from 2% to 0.5%, but this precision depends critically on using a single-particle nuclear model for the Bohr-Weisskopf effect rather than the standard uniform-magnetization ball model, which overestimates the correction by about a factor of two.

## Method

The authors performed high-precision all-orders correlation potential calculations of the magnetic hyperfine constants A for the ground 7s states of 207-213Fr, incorporating the Bohr-Weisskopf effect through a nuclear single-particle model that accounts for the magnetization distribution of the unpaired nucleon. They extracted improved nuclear magnetic moments by comparing their calculated hyperfine constants with experimental values, using the relation mu = (A_expt / A_theory) * mu_input, where mu_input are the literature values used as inputs in the calculations.

## Key numbers

- Uncertainty in extracted nuclear magnetic moments reduced from 2% to 0.5% for most francium isotopes 207-213
- Relative Bohr-Weisskopf corrections for 7s states range from -1.26% (207Fr) to -1.77% (212Fr); for 7p1/2 states from -0.37% (207Fr) to -0.53% (212Fr)
- The ball model overestimates the Bohr-Weisskopf correction by about a factor of two; the difference between models amounts to 1.4% for 211Fr s-states
- Calculated hyperfine constant for 211Fr 7s state: 8899(46) MHz (assuming mu = 4.0 mu_N)
- Extracted magnetic moments: 207Fr = 3.81(2) mu_N, 208Fr = 4.67(2) mu_N, 209Fr = 3.87(2) mu_N, 210Fr = 4.33(2) mu_N, 211Fr = 3.92(2) mu_N, 212Fr = 4.55(2) mu_N, 213Fr = 3.94(2) mu_N
- For H-like 209Bi82+, the calculated single-particle Bohr-Weisskopf correction is -1.07%, in excellent agreement with the experimental value of -1.03(5)%
- For H-like 207Pb81+, the calculated single-particle Bohr-Weisskopf correction is -3.55%, differing from the experimental value of -3.85(4)% by 8%

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of francium nuclear magnetic moments from Ekstrom (1986), which provided values with 2% uncertainty for most isotopes, and from Gomez (2008) and Voss (2015), which used less accurate treatments of the Bohr-Weisskopf effect and omitted quantum electrodynamics corrections. The improved moments are crucial for ongoing atomic parity violation experiments at TRIUMF (Aubin 2013, Tandecki 2013), which require accurate atomic wavefunction benchmarking through hyperfine comparisons. The single-particle model for the Bohr-Weisskopf effect should replace the standard ball model in future high-precision calculations for heavy atoms.
