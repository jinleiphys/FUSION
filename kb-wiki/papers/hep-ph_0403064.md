# Meson loops and the $g_{D^* D π}$ coupling

> The $D^*Dπ$ form factor is evaluated at low and moderate $Q^2$ in a hadronic loop model, for off-shell $D$ mesons. The results contain arbitrary constants, which are fixed so as to match previous QCD sum rule calculations valid at higher $Q^2$. The form factor obtained in this way was used to extract the coupling constant, which is in very good agreement with the experimental value.

## Key claim

The authors combine a hadronic loop model (valid when the D meson is not too far off-shell) with QCD sum rule results (valid in the deep Euclidean region) to determine the \(D^*D\pi\) form factor, obtaining a coupling constant \(g_{D^*D\pi}=17.5\pm 1.5\) that agrees with the CLEO experimental value of \(17.9\pm 0.3\pm 1.9\), unlike earlier QCD sum rule predictions which were significantly lower.

## Method

The authors compute the \(D^*D\pi\) vertex function for an off-shell D meson using an effective Lagrangian with SU(2) flavor and chiral symmetry, evaluating one-loop diagrams with pion, rho, D, and D* intermediate states. The resulting form factor contains three unknown constants (K, \(C_\pi\), \(C_\rho\)) that absorb bare couplings and renormalization parameters; these constants are fixed by fitting the loop calculation to QCD sum rule results from a prior work (Nielsen et al., 2004) at higher \(Q^2\). The same procedure is then applied to the \(B^*B\pi\) vertex by replacing charmed mesons with bottom mesons.

## Key numbers

- \(g_{D^*D\pi} = 17.5 \pm 1.5\) (extracted from the matched form factor at \(p^2 = m_D^2\))
- \(g_{B^*B\pi} = 44.7 \pm 1.0\) (extracted from the matched form factor at \(p^2 = m_B^2\))
- For the \(D^*D\pi\) form factor parametrized as a mixed monopole-dipole form: \(C = 8.7\), \(\Lambda_1 = 5.1\) GeV, \(\Lambda_2 = 2.9\) GeV
- For the \(B^*B\pi\) form factor parametrized as a mixed monopole-dipole form: \(C = 22.4\), \(\Lambda_1 = 7.8\) GeV, \(\Lambda_2 = 7.1\) GeV
- The fit to the QCD sum rule points yields \(\chi^2 \sim 10^{-3}\) for the loop calculation

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between QCD sum rule predictions for \(g_{D^*D\pi}\) (which gave values as low as \(5.7\pm 0.4\) in Nielsen et al. (2004) and at most 13.5 in Khodjamirian et al. (2000)) and the CLEO experimental measurement of \(17.9 \pm 0.3 \pm 1.9\). The method extends the approach of Nielsen et al. (2004) for the \(DD\rho\) vertex, where double parametrization and matching at the on-shell point gave \(g_{D^*D\pi}=14.0\pm 1.5\), by replacing arbitrary analytical extrapolations with a physically motivated hadronic loop calculation. The success for both \(D^*D\pi\) and \(B^*B\pi\) vertices suggests this matching procedure can improve the reliability of coupling constant extractions from QCD sum rules.
