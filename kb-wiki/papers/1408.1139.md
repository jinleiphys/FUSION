# Four-quark structure of the excited states of heavy mesons

> We propose a four-quark structure for some of the excited states of heavy mesons containing a single charm or bottom quark. The four-quark wave functions are constructed based on a diquark-antidiquark form under the constraint that they form an antitriplet $\bar{\bf{3}}_f$ in $\mbox{SU(3)}_f$, which seems to be realized in some of the excited states listed in Particle Data Group. Depending on the structure of antidiquark, we construct two possible models for its wave functions: Model I) the antidiquark is symmetric in flavor ($\bar{\bf{6}}_f$) and antisymmetric in color ($\bf{3}_c$) and Model II) the antidiquark is antisymmetric in flavor ($\bf{3}_f$) and symmetric in color ($\bar{\bf{6}}_c$). To test phenomenological relevance of these wave functions, we calculate the mass differences among the excited states of spin $J=0,1,2$ using color-spin interactions. The four-quark wave functions based on Model~I is found to reproduce the observed mass of the excited states of heavy mesons. Also, our four-quark model provides an interesting phenomenology relating to the decay widths of the excited states. To further pursue the possibility of the four-quark structure, we make a few predictions for open charm and open bottom states that may be discovered in future experiments. Most of them are expected to have broad widths, which would make them difficult to be identified experimentally. However, one resonance with $J=1$ containing bottom and strange quarks is expected to appear as a sharp peak with its mass around $B^{\bar s}_{1N} \sim 5753$~MeV. Confirmation of the existence of such states in future experiments will shed light on our understanding of the structure of heavy meson excited states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that many of the positive-parity excited states of open-charm and open-bottom mesons listed by the Particle Data Group may be four-quark states (diquark-antidiquark systems) rather than conventional two-quark states, but this interpretation is explicitly limited to those states that form an SU(3) flavor antitriplet and is not claimed to apply to all excited states; the authors note that the J^P=0^+ channel in particular shows puzzling features that may require mixing of configurations beyond their model.

## Method

The authors construct four-quark wave functions in a diquark-antidiquark form, where the diquark contains one heavy quark (c or b) and one light quark, and the antidiquark contains two light antiquarks. Two models are built depending on the flavor symmetry of the antidiquark: Model I uses a flavor-symmetric antidiquark (color antisymmetric, spin 1 only) and Model II uses a flavor-antisymmetric antidiquark (color symmetric, spin 1 only). The mass differences among the J=0,1,2 excited states are then calculated using color-spin interactions, with the overall coupling strength v_0 fitted from the Delta-N baryon mass difference, and compared to experimental data from PDG.

## Key numbers

* The mass of the predicted J=1 resonance containing bottom and strange quarks (B^{bar s}_{1N}) is around 5753 MeV.
* The mass splitting between the I=1/2 and I=0 members of the J^P=2^+ channel in the D/D_s family is about 108 MeV (using D_2*±(2464), D_2*0(2463), and D_s2*±(2572)).
* The mass splitting between the I=1/2 and I=0 members of the J^P=1^+ channel in the D/D_s family (using D_1±(2423), D_1^0(2421), and D_s1*±(2535)) is about 113 MeV.
* The mass splitting between B_1^0(5724) and B_s1^0(5829) in the J^P=1^+ channel is about 106 MeV.
* The mass splitting between B_2*0(5743) and B_s2*0(5840) in the J^P=2^+ channel is about 97 MeV.
* The constituent quark masses used are: m_u = m_d = 330 MeV, m_s = 500 MeV, m_c = 1500 MeV, m_b = 4700 MeV.
* The fitted coupling strength v_0 from the Delta-N mass difference is approximately (-199.6)^3 MeV^3.

## Context

This work extends the diquark-antidiquark model originally advocated by Jaffe (1977) for light scalar mesons (a0(980), f0(980), sigma(600), kappa(800)) to the heavy meson sector, specifically the excited states of D, D_s, B, and B_s mesons. It contrasts with the conventional two-quark picture where positive-parity states arise from orbital excitation (l=1) of the Q-anti-q system, and with mixing configurations between two-quark and four-quark states proposed by other authors (e.g., Vilnius-Florence-Vilnius group, 2006). The results enable future experimental searches for predicted open-charm and open-bottom four-quark states, particularly a sharp J=1 resonance near 5753 MeV containing bottom and strange quarks, while noting that most predicted states would have broad widths making them difficult to identify.
