# Density Matrix Functional Theory with account of pairing correlations

> The extension of the density functional theory (DFT) to include pairing correlations without formal violation of the particle-number conservation condition is described. This version of the theory can be considered as a foundation of the application of existing DFT plus pairing approaches to atoms, molecules, ultracooled and magnetically trapped atomic Fermi gases, and atomic nuclei where the number of particles is exactly conserved. The connection with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory is discussed. The method of the quasilocal reduction of the nonlocal theory is described. This quasilocal reduction allows to obtain equations of motion which are much more simple for the numerical solution than the equations corresponding to the nonlocal case.

## Key claim

The paper presents a rigorous formulation of density functional theory (DFT) extended to include pairing correlations while exactly conserving particle number, in contrast to earlier DFT-plus-pairing approaches that formally violate particle-number conservation. The authors prove that for any arbitrary nonlocal single-particle density matrix of an interacting time-reversal invariant fermion system with fixed particle number, there exists a quasiparticle-vacuum wave function that reproduces that density matrix, generalizing the Lieb theorem.

## Method

The authors construct an extended density matrix (EDM) in a doubled single-particle space that is idempotent and contains the normal density matrix as a block, using the Bloch-Messiah theorem to express the EDM in canonical basis form. They define an effective many-body Hamiltonian and a residual correlation energy functional, then apply a variational principle to derive equations of motion that have the same matrix form as the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations but with a more general energy functional. Finally, they perform a quasilocal reduction by introducing local particle, kinetic-energy, spin, and anomalous densities, obtaining simplified equations of motion with local single-particle potentials.

## Key numbers

* The eigenvalues of the normal density matrix satisfy \(0 \leqslant v_c \leqslant 1\) for the canonical basis expansion \(\rho(x,x') = \sum_{c,s} v^2_c \phi_{c,s}(x) \phi^*_{c,s}(x')\)
* The quasiparticle energies \(E_\lambda\) appear as Lagrange multipliers in the equations of motion, with the condition \(E_\lambda > 0\) (if negative, the permutation \(\psi_{\lambda;+} \leftrightarrow \psi_{\lambda;-}\) is made)
* The chemical potentials \(\mu_q\) for neutrons and protons are introduced as Lagrange multipliers to enforce particle-number conservation: \(\sum_\sigma \int d\mathbf{r}\, \rho(\mathbf{r},\sigma,q; \mathbf{r},\sigma,q) = N_q\)

## Context

This work extends the quasilocal density functional theory developed by Soubbotin, Tselyaev, and Viñas (2003) to include pairing correlations, and generalizes the Lieb theorem (Lieb, 1983) from local particle densities to nonlocal density matrices. The approach provides a rigorous foundation for existing DFT-plus-pairing methods applied to atoms, molecules, ultracold Fermi gases, and atomic nuclei, and establishes the connection to Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory as a phenomenological realization. The quasilocal reduction enables numerical solutions that are much simpler than the full nonlocal equations, making the theory applicable to nuclear structure calculations with effective masses and spin-orbit potentials.
