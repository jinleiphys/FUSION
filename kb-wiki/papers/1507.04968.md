# Probing the properties of event-by-event distributions in Hanbury-Brown--Twiss radii

> Hanbury-Brown--Twiss interferometry is a technique which yields effective widths (i.e., "HBT radii") of homogeneity regions in the fireballs produced in heavy ion collisions. Because the initial conditions of these collisions are stochastically fluctuating, the measured HBT radii also exhibit variation on an event-by-event basis. However, HBT measurements have, to date, been performed only on an ensemble-averaged basis, due to inherent limitations of finite particle statistics. In this paper, we show that experimental measurements to date are best characterized theoretically as weighted averages of the event-by-event HBT radii, and we propose a new method for extracting experimentally both the arithmetic mean and the variance of the event-by-event distribution of HBT radii. We demonstrate the extraction of the mean and variance of this distribution for a particular ensemble of numerically generated events, and offer some ideas to extend and generalize the method to enable measurement of higher moments of the HBT distribution as well.

## Key claim
Experimental HBT radii measurements are best characterized theoretically as weighted averages of event-by-event HBT radii, where the weights are proportional to the square of the event-by-event particle yield at a given momentum; the arithmetic mean and variance of the event-by-event distribution of HBT radii can be extracted experimentally using a proposed method based on repeated sub-ensemble averaging.

## Method
The authors performed event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions at 0-10% centrality using the iEBE-VISHNU package with MC-Glauber initial conditions and boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamics (η/s = 0). They computed HBT radii via the source variance method (Eq. 7) for each event, then compared four different theoretical ensemble-averaging procedures: single-shot hydrodynamics, ensemble-averaged emission function, ensemble-averaged correlation function, and direct ensemble average. They derived analytic relations showing that the experimentally measured ensemble-averaged correlation function yields HBT radii that are weighted averages of event-by-event radii weighted by N²(K), and proposed a method to extract the arithmetic mean and variance of the event-by-event HBT radius distribution by constructing sub-ensembles and measuring fluctuations of the weighted averages.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of HBT interferometry in heavy-ion collisions by clarifying that experimental measurements correspond to weighted (not arithmetic) averages of event-by-event HBT radii, correcting a common assumption in the field. It builds on numerical studies by Bozek (2014) showing that mean HBT radii from fluctuating correlation functions are almost indistinguishable from those from ensemble-averaged emission functions, and on event-by-event simulations by Plumberg and Heinz (2015) showing that individual-event HBT radii fluctuate by 10-15%. The proposed method enables experimental access to the variance of the HBT radius distribution, which could provide sensitivity to different functional forms of the temperature dependence of η/s, and is generalizable to any event-by-event observable.
