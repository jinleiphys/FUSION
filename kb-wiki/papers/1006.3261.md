# Langevin diffusion of heavy quarks in non-conformal holographic backgrounds

> The Langevin diffusion process of a relativistic heavy quark in a non-conformal holographic setup is discussed. The bulk geometry is a general, five-dimensional asymptotically AdS black hole. The heavy quark is described by a trailing string attached to a flavor brane, moving at constant velocity. From the equations describing linearized fluctuations of the string world-sheet, the correlation functions defining a generalized Langevin process are constructed via the AdS/CFT prescription. In the local limit, analytic expressions for the Langevin diffusion and friction coefficients are obtained in terms of the bulk string metric. Modified Einstein relations between these quantities are also derived. The spectral densities associated to the Langevin correlators are analyzed, and simple analytic expressions are obtained in the small and large frequency limits. Finally, a numerical analysis of the jet-quenching parameter, and a comparison to RHIC phenomenology are performed in the case of Improved Holographic QCD.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized Langevin diffusion process for a relativistic heavy quark moving through a non-conformal holographic plasma, showing that the fluctuation-dissipation relation involves a velocity-dependent world-sheet temperature \(T_s\) that is typically smaller than the heat bath temperature \(T\), and that the local (Markovian) Langevin description breaks down for charm quarks at momenta above approximately 5–10 GeV for temperatures above about 400 MeV.

## Method

The authors consider a five-dimensional asymptotically AdS black hole background with a dilaton field, representing a non-conformal holographic dual of a strongly coupled plasma. A heavy quark is modeled by a trailing Nambu-Goto string whose endpoint moves at constant velocity \(v\) on a flavor brane in the UV. Linearized fluctuations around the classical string profile are solved, and the holographic prescription (Schwinger-Keldysh formalism) is used to extract the retarded and symmetric correlators of the world-sheet fluctuations, which define the generalized Langevin equation. Analytic expressions for the diffusion and friction coefficients are obtained in the local (low-frequency) limit in terms of the bulk metric, and the full frequency-dependent spectral densities are computed numerically for the Improved Holographic QCD model.

## Key numbers

* The world-sheet Hawking temperature in the conformal case is \(T_s = T (1-v^2)^{1/4}\), which is always \(\leq T\); in the non-conformal model, \(T_s \leq T_{\mathrm{s,conf}} \leq T\) for all velocities.
* The transverse diffusion constant \(\kappa_\perp\) is related to the jet-quenching parameter by \(\hat{q}_\perp = 2 \kappa_\perp / v\).
* For temperatures above \(\sim 400\) MeV, the local Langevin description breaks down for charm quarks with momenta above \(\sim 5\)–\(10\) GeV.
* The jet-quenching parameter \(\hat{q}_\perp\) increases approximately as \(\sim T^3\) as temperature rises.
* The longitudinal diffusion constant always dominates the transverse one: \(\kappa_\parallel \ge \kappa_\perp\).
* In the large-frequency limit, for finite quark mass the spectral densities grow linearly with \(\omega\); in the infinite-mass limit they grow as \(\omega^3\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Langevin analysis of heavy quarks from the conformal \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills case (Iancu, 2008) to general non-conformal holographic backgrounds, including the Improved Holographic QCD model (Gursoy, Kiritsis, Mazzanti, Nitti, 2009) which quantitatively matches lattice Yang-Mills thermodynamics. The results contradict the rotationally-invariant Langevin equations used in phenomenological studies (Rapp, 2009; Lan, 2010) by showing that the diffusion is anisotropic and the Einstein relation is modified. The full frequency-dependent correlators computed here enable modeling of non-Markovian dynamics at LHC energies, where the local jet-quenching parameter \(\hat{q}_\perp\) alone is insufficient for charm quarks.
