# GSI anomaly and spin-rotation coupling

> We propose a model in which a recently reported modulation in the decay of the hydrogenlike ions ${}^{140}$Pr$^{\, 58 +}$, ${}^{142}$Pm$^{\, 60 +}$ and ${}^{122}$I$^{\, 52 +}$ arises from the coupling of rotation to the spin of electron and nucleus. The model shows that the spin-spin coupling of electron and nucleus does not contribute to the modulation and predicts that the anomaly cannot be observed if the motion of the ions is rectilinear, or if the ions are stopped in a target. It also supports the notion that the modulation frequency is proportional to the inverse of the atomic mass.

## Key claim

The observed modulation in the electron-capture decay of hydrogenlike ions at GSI arises from the spin-dependent part of the Thomas precession, not from the spin-spin coupling of electron and nucleus; the modulation frequency is proportional to the inverse of the atomic mass and no modulation is expected for beta-plus decay.

## Method

The authors construct a semiclassical Hamiltonian for a hydrogenlike ion moving in the magnetic field of a storage ring, including the Thomas precession of both electron and nucleus, the magnetic-moment coupling, and the cyclotron frequency. They diagonalize the hyperfine-interaction Hamiltonian in the basis of total angular momentum states F=3/2 and F=1/2, compute the energy differences between the two F=1/2 eigenstates, and derive the modulation frequency of the electron-capture decay probability from quantum beats between those states. The unknown Lorentz factor of the bound electron relative to the nucleus is then solved by matching the predicted modulation frequency to the observed GSI values.

## Key numbers

* Modulation periods observed in the laboratory frame: 7.069(8) s for 140Pr58+, 7.10(22) s for 142Pm60+, and 6.1 s for 122I52+.
* Magnetic field of the GSI storage ring: B = 1.197 T.
* Derived Lorentz factors of the bound electron relative to the nucleus: gamma_e|n(Pr) ~ 1.07904, gamma_e|n(Pm) ~ 1.08435, gamma_e|n(I) ~ 1.05902.
* Binding energies derived from the model: E(Pr) ~ -54.4 keV, E(Pm) ~ -58.2 keV, E(I) ~ -46.3 keV.
* Experimental binding energies from the Dirac equation: E_R(Pr) = -49.5 keV, E_R(Pm) = -53.1 keV, E_R(I) = -39.6 keV.
* Modulation amplitudes from GSI experiments: a(Pr) = 0.18(3), a(Pm) = 0.23(4), a(I) = 0.22(2).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the GSI anomaly first reported by the GSI collaboration (2008) and previously modeled by Giunti (2009) and Merle (2010), but differs from an earlier attempt by Lambiase (2008) by including bound-state kinematics, dragging effects, Thomas precessions of both nucleus and electron, and QED corrections. The model avoids criticisms raised by Faber (2009) and Fastermann (2010) because the Hamiltonians are essentially different, and it is consistent with experiments on electron-capture decays of neutral atoms in solid environments by Vetter (2008) that showed no modulations. The work enables future tests by predicting that the modulation disappears for rectilinear motion, for ions stopped in a target, and for beta-plus decay.
