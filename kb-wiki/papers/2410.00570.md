# Two-dimensional bosonic droplets in a harmonic trap

> We investigate a system of bosons in a two-dimensional harmonic trap. In the limit of strong attractive interactions, the bosons make a droplet insensitive to external confinement. For weak interactions, in contrast, the ground state is given by the harmonic trap. In this work, we conduct a variational study of the transition between these two limits. We find that this transition occurs abruptly at the critical interaction strength whose value is universal if scaled appropriately with the number of particles. To connect the abrupt change in the properties of the system to the classical description of phase transitions, we analyze the static response of the Bose gas related to the isothermal compressibility. Finally, we perform numerically exact calculations for a few particles to demonstrate the effects of finite range interactions on this transition. We conclude that finite range effects wash out the point of transition.

## Key claim

The transition between a trap-dominated harmonic oscillator ground state and an interaction-dominated universal many-body bound state occurs abruptly at a critical interaction strength whose value is universal when scaled as \(\ln(B_2/\omega)/N\), but finite-range interactions wash out this sharp transition point.

## Method

The authors performed a variational study using an ansatz that superposes the harmonic oscillator ground state and the universal many-body bound state shape from Hammer (2004), minimizing the expectation value of the Hamiltonian with a renormalization-group-improved running coupling constant. For finite-range effects, they employed the configuration interaction method with a Gaussian interaction potential for \(N=3\) and \(N=4\) particles, calculating the two-body binding energy numerically from the Schrödinger equation.

## Key numbers

- For the universal many-body bound state in free space, the energy ratio \(E_{N+1}/E_N = 8.567\) and the size ratio \(R_{N+1}/R_N = 0.3417\) from Hammer (2004)
- The transition point for large particle numbers occurs at \(\ln(B_2/\omega)/N = -2.148 + \mathcal{O}(1/N)\)
- For weak interactions, the static response per particle is \(\chi\omega^3/N = 1\); for strong interactions, \(\chi\omega^3/N = 0\)
- The ratio of mean-square radii for strong interactions is \(r^2_{N+1}/r^2_N = 0.116\), matching the universal prediction from Hammer (2004)
- For the trap-dominated regime, the ratio of mean-square radii is \(r^2_{N+1}/r^2_N = 1\)

## Context

This work extends the universal many-body bound state predicted by Hammer and Son (2004) for attractively interacting bosons in two dimensions without external confinement, confirmed by Blume (2005), Lee (2006), and Bazak (2018), to the experimentally relevant case with a harmonic trapping potential. The study connects to work by Tononi (2023) on bosonic droplets on a sphere, which found an analogous first-order phase transition between a homogeneous state and a localized droplet. The results enable experimental studies of universal two-dimensional bosonic droplets by tuning either the scattering length via Feshbach resonances or the trapping frequency, with the static response serving as a measurable indicator of the transition.
