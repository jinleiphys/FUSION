# Resonance saturation for four-nucleon operators

> In the modern description of nuclear forces based on chiral effective field theory, four-nucleon operators with unknown coupling constants appear. These couplings can be fixed by a fit to the low partial waves of neutron-proton scattering. We show that the so determined numerical values can be understood on the basis of phenomenological one-boson-exchange models. We also extract these values from various modern high accuracy nucleon-nucleon potentials and demonstrate their consistency and remarkable agreement with the values in the chiral effective field theory approach. This paves the way for estimating the low-energy constants of operators with more nucleon fields and/or external probes.

# Resonance saturation for four-nucleon operators

## Key claim
The low-energy constants (LECs) of four-nucleon contact operators in chiral effective field theory, previously determined by fitting to neutron-proton scattering data, can be understood quantitatively from the resonance parameters of phenomenological one-boson-exchange models; this agreement holds provided the delta-resonance contribution to the pion-nucleon LECs is subtracted at next-to-next-to-leading order to avoid unphysical deeply bound states that would prevent a direct comparison with boson-exchange potentials.

## Method
The authors determined the spectroscopic LECs for S-wave and P-wave four-nucleon contact interactions at NLO and NNLO in chiral EFT, including the charged-to-neutral pion mass difference in one-pion exchange and using an exponential regulator with cutoff between 500 and 600 MeV. They then expanded the short-range parts of several one-boson-exchange potentials (Bonn-B, Nijmegen 93) and high-precision phenomenological potentials (CD-Bonn 2000, Nijmegen I/II, Argonne V18) in terms of local four-nucleon operators with zero and two derivatives, projecting onto the same partial waves. The resulting LECs from each potential were compared with the EFT values.

## Key numbers
- The chiral EFT LECs at NLO and NNLO were obtained for cutoff values between 500 and 600 MeV; the NNLO fit used modified pion-nucleon LECs: c1 = -0.81 GeV^{-1}, c3 = -1.15 GeV^{-1}, c4 = 1.20 GeV^{-1}.
- For the Bonn-B potential, the scalar meson contributions to the LECs (in appropriate units) include: sigma meson gives C_S contribution of 4.1 fm^2, C_T contribution of -0.3 fm^2; rho meson gives C_S of -0.2 fm^2, C_T of 0.2 fm^2.
- The dimensionless naturalness coefficients c_{200} and c_{202} for the LECs from the EFT fits range between 0.3 and 3.5, with the exception of f_pi^2 * C_T which is much smaller than one.
- The S-wave LECs from all six potential models (Bonn-B, Nijmegen 93, CD-Bonn 2000, Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II, Argonne V18) fall within a band that overlaps with the EFT values; the theoretical uncertainties from the EFT determination are smaller than the spread across the potential models.

## Context
This work extends the concept of resonance saturation, previously established for the meson-sector LECs of chiral perturbation theory by Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) and for pion-nucleon LECs by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1997), to the four-nucleon operators that appear in Weinberg's chiral EFT approach to nuclear forces. The authors build on their own prior determination of the NLO LECs from neutron-proton scattering (Epelbaum et al., 2000) and show that these constants can be understood from the same meson exchanges (sigma, rho, omega, delta) that appear in traditional boson-exchange models. This enables future estimation of LECs for three-nucleon forces and operators involving external probes, where direct fitting to data would be computationally expensive.
