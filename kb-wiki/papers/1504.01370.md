# Hydrodynamics dual to Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity: all-order gradient resummation

> Relativistic hydrodynamics dual to Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity in asymptotic $\textrm{AdS}_5$ space is under study. To linear order in the amplitude of the fluid velocity and temperature, we derive the fluid's stress-energy tensor via an all-order resummation of the derivative terms. Each order is accompanied by new transport coefficients, which all together could be compactly absorbed into two functions of momenta, referred to as viscosity functions. Via inverse Fourier transform, these viscosities appear as memory functions in the constitutive relation between components of the stress-energy tensor.

## Key claim
The paper derives an all-order resummation of derivative terms in the stress-energy tensor of relativistic hydrodynamics dual to Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet gravity in asymptotic AdS₅ space, working to linear order in the amplitude of fluid velocity and temperature, and to linear order in the Gauss-Bonnet coupling α. The authors find that the Gauss-Bonnet correction does not introduce causality violation in the resummed hydrodynamics for small α, contrary to an earlier erroneous claim in the first version of the preprint.

## Method
The authors linearize the fluid velocity and temperature around equilibrium, then solve the Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet field equations in the bulk for metric perturbations in Fourier space, using a decomposition of the metric corrections into basis functions of the fluid velocity. The viscosity functions η(ω,q²) and ζ(ω,q²) are extracted from the near-boundary behavior of the metric corrections, computed both analytically in a hydrodynamic expansion (low momenta) and numerically for generic frequencies and momenta via a shooting method from the black hole horizon to the boundary.

## Key numbers
- The Gauss-Bonnet coupling α is constrained to the range −7/72 ≤ α ≤ 9/200 from causality requirements in the dual CFT (bounds from prior work).
- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density is η₀/s = (1/4π)(1 − 8α), violating the KSS bound for positive α.
- In units πT = 1, the hydrodynamic expansion of the shear viscosity function begins as η(ω,q²) = (1 − 5α) + (1/2)[(2 − ln2) − (21 − 5ln2)α] iω − [ (1/48)(6π − π² + 24 − 36ln2 + 12ln²2) − 6.53(280)α ] ω² − (1/8 − 2.11(320)α) q² + …
- The ζ viscosity function begins as ζ(ω,q²) = (1/12)[(5 − π − 2ln2) + (15π − 87 + 30ln2)α] + …
- The shear wave dispersion relation at low q is ω = −(i/4)(1 − 8α) q² − (i/32)[1 − log2 + (8#₁ − 40 + 16log2)α] q⁴ + …, with #₁ = 6.53(280).
- The sound wave dispersion relation at low q is ω = ±q/√3 − (i/6)(1 − 8α) q² ± (1/(24√3))[3 − 2log2 + (16log2 − 38)α] q³ + …

## Context
This work extends the all-order gradient resummation approach previously applied to pure Einstein gravity by Bu, Lublinsky, and Sharon (2014, 2015) to the Einstein-Gauss-Bonnet case, which introduces higher-curvature corrections dual to finite-N or finite-λ effects in the gauge theory. It builds on prior calculations of first-order (shear viscosity) and second-order transport coefficients in Gauss-Bonnet gravity by Brigante et al. (2008) and Grozdanov and Starinets (2015), and provides new third-order transport coefficients. The results enable the study of causality in resummed hydrodynamics beyond the Einstein gravity limit and constrain the allowed values of the Gauss-Bonnet coupling through the requirement that memory functions vanish at negative times.
