# Mass suppression effect in QCD radiation and hadron angular distribution in jet

> The finite mass of the heavy quark suppresses the collimated radiations, which is generally referred to as the dead cone effect. In this paper, we study the distribution of hadron multiplicity over the hadron opening angle with respect to the jet axis in various flavors of jets. The corresponding measurement can be the most straightforward and simplest to explore the dynamical evolution of the radiations in the corresponding jet, which can expose the mass effect. We also propose the transverse energy-weighted angular distribution which sheds light on the interplay between perturbative and nonperturbative effects in the radiation. With Monte-Carlo simulations, our calculation shows that the dead cone effect can be clearly seen by taking the ratio between the b jet and the light-quark (inclusive) jet, promising to be measured at the LHC in the future.

## Key claim

The dead cone effect from heavy quark mass can be clearly observed by taking the ratio of the hadron multiplicity angular distribution between b jets and light-quark (inclusive) jets, but the observable is not infrared safe and the mass suppression is not removed by increasing the quark energy.

## Method

The authors performed Monte-Carlo simulations using PYTHIA 8.306 for dijet production at 13 TeV LHC, with multiparton interaction switched off. Jets were reconstructed with the anti-kT algorithm (radius R=0.4) using charged tracks with pT>1 GeV and |η|<2.0, and b jets were identified by requiring at least one B hadron among their constituents. They computed the averaged charged hadron multiplicity distribution as a function of the opening angle between each hadron and the jet axis, and also a transverse-energy-weighted version with a free parameter κ that interpolates between the multiplicity distribution (κ=0) and the infrared-safe jet shape (κ=1).

## Key numbers

- Jet radius R=0.4 used for anti-kT jet reconstruction
- Charged tracks selected with pT>1 GeV and |η|<2.0
- Simulations performed for 13 TeV LHC dijet production
- Jet transverse momentum intervals studied include 30<pT,j<40 GeV and 600<pT,j<800 GeV
- The dead cone suppression is expected when the typical splitting scale pT,j θ z(1-z) ≤ pT,j θ/4 is close to the b quark mass mb

## Context

This work extends the study of the dead cone effect first described by Dokshitzer (1991) and Schumm (1992), which has recently been measured by ALICE (2021, 2022) using charm jet substructure. The authors propose a simpler observable than groomed jet substructure approaches used by Chang (2017), Li (2017), and Caletti (2023), and introduce a κ-weighted distribution that bridges the non-perturbative multiplicity distribution with the infrared-safe jet shape observable from Ellis (1992). The results enable future LHC measurements of heavy flavor jet evolution and can be used to test or tune hadronization models in Monte-Carlo event generators.
