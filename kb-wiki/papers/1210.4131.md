# Pair condensation in a Finite Trapped Fermi Gas

> Superfluidity in the cold atomic two-species Fermi gas system in the unitary limit of infinite scattering length remains incompletely understood. In particular, a pseudogap phase has been proposed to exist above the superfluid critical temperature. Here we apply the auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo method to perform the first ab initio calculations of the temperature dependence of three quantities -- the energy-staggering pairing gap, the condensate fraction and the heat capacity -- in a trapped finite-size cold atom system. As the calculations of the energy-staggering pairing gap require the use of the canonical ensemble, we employ a novel algorithm for the stabilization of particle-number projection that is essential for reaching convergence in the size of the model space. We observe clear signatures of the superfluid phase transition in all three quantities, including a signature of the recently measured lambda peak in the heat capacity, but find no evidence of a pseudogap effect in the energy-staggering pairing gap.

## Key claim

The authors perform the first *ab initio* calculations of the energy-staggering pairing gap, condensate fraction, and heat capacity in a finite-size trapped Fermi gas at unitarity, finding clear signatures of the superfluid phase transition in all three quantities but no evidence of a pseudogap effect in the energy-staggering pairing gap. The claim is qualified by the finite system size (N=20 atoms), which prevents sharp phase transitions but allows smooth signatures.

## Method

The authors applied the auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) method within a configuration-interaction shell model space spanned by single-particle eigenstates of the spherical harmonic trap, truncated to Nmax oscillator shells. They performed calculations in the canonical ensemble using a novel algorithm for stabilizing particle-number projection via diagonalization of a cyclic permutation of the matrix decomposition, enabling convergence in model spaces up to Nmax=11 (364 single-particle states). The energy-staggering pairing gap was computed by particle-number reprojection, the condensate fraction from the largest eigenvalue of the L=0 pair correlation matrix, and the heat capacity by numerical differentiation of the energy inside the path integral.

## Key numbers

- System: N=20 trapped spin-balanced Fermi gas (N_up=N_down=10) at unitarity; zero-temperature pairing gap Gap = 0.271(32) epsilon_F, where epsilon_F = 4.0 hbar omega
- Pairing gap ratio for different system sizes: Gap(10,10)/Gap(5,5) = 0.92(13), compared to expected scaling of 0.866 for large N
- Upper bound temperature for condensation: T_ub/T_F approximately 0.175, where T_F = epsilon_F/k_B
- Gap at T_ub: Gap(T_ub) = 0.028(2) epsilon_F, an insignificant fraction of its T=0 value
- Model space convergence: pairing gap well-converged by Nmax=9, condensate fraction by Nmax=11, heat capacity by Nmax=11
- Imaginary time discretization: linear extrapolation to Delta beta -> 0 using Delta beta <= 1/32

## Context

This work extends previous quantum Monte Carlo studies of the unitary Fermi gas, which had conflicting predictions about pseudogap effects: Magierski (2009, 2011) found a large pairing gap above T_c in the uniform gas using spectral weight fitting, while Nascimbene (2010, 2011) and Su (2010) did not. The authors' finite-size trapped system results contrast with those uniform-gas calculations, and their heat capacity shows a smoothed signature of the lambda peak recently observed experimentally by Ku (2012). The method enables future studies of finite-size scaling and can be applied on either side of the BEC-BCS crossover.
