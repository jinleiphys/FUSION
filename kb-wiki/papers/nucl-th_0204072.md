# Perturbative Effective Theory in an Oscillator Basis?

> The effective interaction/operator problem in nuclear physics is believed to be highly nonperturbative, requiring extended high-momentum spaces for accurate solution. We trace this to difficulties that arise at both short and long distances when the included space is defined in terms of a basis of harmonic oscillator Slater determinants. We show, in the simplest case of the deuteron, that both difficulties can be circumvented, yielding highly perturbative results in the potential even for modest (~6hw) included spaces.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the conventional belief that effective interactions in a harmonic oscillator basis are highly nonperturbative can be circumvented for the deuteron by resumming the kinetic energy to all orders and optimizing the oscillator length, yielding perturbative convergence even for modest (~6 ℏω) included spaces.

## Method

The authors start from the Bloch-Horowitz equation for the effective Hamiltonian in a harmonic oscillator basis and expand the excluded-space contribution using a Green's function expansion. They identify two sources of nonperturbative behavior: a long-distance problem from the incorrect asymptotic falloff of HO wave functions, and a short-distance problem from the hard core of the NN potential. They reformulate the Bloch-Horowitz equation so that the Green's function is sandwiched between potential terms rather than kinetic terms, which resums the kinetic energy to all orders and cures the long-distance problem; the short-distance problem is then largely absorbed by optimizing the oscillator size parameter b to minimize the 0th-order energy.

## Key numbers

* For a 10 ℏω included space, the 0th-order approximation to the reformulated equation (Eq. 4) yields a binding energy error of 21 keV; for 8 ℏω the error is 36 keV; for 6 ℏω the error is 52 keV.
* The 1st-order correction V(E-QT)^{-1}QV for 6, 8, and 10 ℏω calculations yields binding energies accurate to ≤3 keV.
* At 3rd order, even the 4 ℏω calculation achieves an accuracy of 1.2 keV.
* For Λ_SM = 70, the total excluded-space contribution accounts for only 20 keV of binding energy, with 85% generated by the first term in the expansion.
* The optimal oscillator parameter b for the reformulated equation lies at 0.4-0.5 fm for 6-10 ℏω spaces.

## Context

This work extends the exact effective theory approach of Haxton and Song (2000) for the deuteron and 3He, which used direct summation of high-momentum states up to Λ_∞ ~ 140 for 1 keV accuracy. The paper addresses the poor convergence found in earlier systematic treatments such as the third-order calculations of Barrett and Kirson (1970) and the toy-model demonstrations of nonperturbative intruder states by Shucan and Weidenmuller (1972-1973). The results enable perturbative calculations of effective interactions in light p-shell nuclei using existing Jacobi coordinate shell-model codes, with the hierarchy of three-body, four-body, etc. contributions to the effective potential expected to diminish rapidly.
