# Strangeness at finite temperature from Lattice QCD

> The precision reached by recent lattice QCD results allows for the first time to investigate whether the measured hadronic spectrum is missing some additional strange states, which are predicted by the Quark Model but have not yet been detected. This can be done by comparing some sensitive thermodynamic observables from lattice QCD to the predictions of the Hadron Resonance Gas model (with the inclusion of decays [3]). We propose a set of specific observables, defined as linear combinations of conserved charge fluctuations, which allow to investigate this issue for baryons containing one or more strange quarks separately. Applications of these observables to isolate the multiplicity fluctuations of kaons from lattice QCD, and their comparison with the experimental results, are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the tension between light and strange hadron yields in heavy-ion collisions may be resolved either by including additional strange resonances predicted by the Quark Model but not yet observed, or by a difference in chemical freeze-out temperatures for light and strange hadrons; however, the current error bars on kaon fluctuation data from STAR are too large to decisively extract the strange freeze-out temperature from Lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors compare thermodynamic observables from Lattice QCD to predictions of the Hadron Resonance Gas model, using linear combinations of conserved charge fluctuations to isolate contributions from baryons containing one or more strange quarks. They implement partial pressures for charged strange mesons to extract kaon multiplicity fluctuations from Lattice QCD, and compare these to preliminary STAR data for net-kaon fluctuations at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. Additionally, they incorporate Quark Model states with extrapolated branching ratios to test whether missing resonances affect freeze-out parameters extracted from net-proton and net-charge fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The inflection point for light susceptibility is around \(T\sim 150\) MeV, while the inflection point for strangeness susceptibility is around \(T\sim 165\) MeV, indicating a \(\sim 15\) MeV difference between light and strange hadrons.
- Comparison of charged strange mesons from Lattice QCD via partial pressure to preliminary STAR kaon fluctuations is shown for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.
- The ratio \(\frac{\langle |K^{+/-}|^2\rangle}{\langle K^{+/-}\rangle}\) from Lattice QCD is compared to preliminary STAR data for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends comparisons between Lattice QCD and the Hadron Resonance Gas model (Bazavov 2014) that suggested missing strange hadrons from Quark Model states (Capstick 1986, Ebert 2009) could account for tensions in thermal fits at LHC (Abelev 2012). The paper also builds on the observation (Bellwied 2013) that the inflection points of light and strange susceptibilities differ by about 15 MeV, and on prior studies of chemical freeze-out using ratios of susceptibilities (Alba 2014, Karsch 2012, Borsanyi 2014). The proposed method of extracting charged strange susceptibilities from Lattice QCD via partial pressures (Noronha-Hostler 2016) enables future decisive tests of whether light and strange hadrons reach chemical equilibrium at different temperatures, once experimental error bars on kaon fluctuations are reduced.
