# Enhanced dynamics in fusion of neutron-rich oxygen nuclei at above-barrier energies

> Above-barrier fusion cross-sections for an isotopic chain of oxygen isotopes with A=16-19 incident on a $^{12}$C target are presented. Experimental data are compared with both static and dynamical microscopic calculations. These calculations are unable to explain the $\sim$37\% increase in the average above-barrier fusion cross-section observed for $^{19}$O as compared to $β$-stable oxygen isotopes. This result suggests that for neutron-rich nuclei existing time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations underpredict the role of dynamics at near-barrier energies. High-quality measurement of above-barrier fusion for an isotopic chain of increasingly neutron-rich nuclei provides an effective means to probe this fusion dynamics.

## Key claim

The measured above-barrier fusion cross-section for $^{19}$O on $^{12}$C is approximately 37% larger than for $\beta$-stable oxygen isotopes, and this enhancement cannot be explained by either static density models or time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations that include dynamics, suggesting that existing TDHF calculations underpredict the role of extremely large-amplitude collective motion at near-barrier energies.

## Method

Fusion excitation functions were measured for $^{16,17,18,19}$O ions incident on a $^{12}$C target at above-barrier energies using an energy/time-of-flight approach with annular double-sided silicon detectors, with improved calibration yielding two- to four-fold better statistics for $^{19}$O. The experimental cross-sections were compared to static calculations using the Sao Paulo model with relativistic mean-field densities (RMF-SP), an analytical fusion model, coupled-channels calculations with CCFULL, frozen Hartree-Fock (FHF) calculations, and full TDHF calculations on a 3D Cartesian grid with an impact parameter precision of 0.02 fm.

## Key numbers

- The average above-barrier fusion cross-section for $^{19}$O is 1178 mb, compared to 831 mb for $^{16}$O and 858 mb for $^{18}$O, representing a ~37% increase with the addition of a single neutron to $^{18}$O.
- The ratio $\sigma(^{19}\text{O})/\sigma(^{18}\text{O})$ in the above-barrier regime is approximately 1.22.
- To reproduce the $^{19}$O excitation function with CCFULL, the Woods-Saxon radius parameter had to be increased from 0.74 fm to 0.85 fm, a 15% increase.
- The interaction cross-section for $^{18,19}$O on carbon at ~900 A.MeV shows only a modest increase from 1032 $\pm$ 26 mb to 1066 $\pm$ 9 mb, indicating the fusion enhancement is not due to static size.
- The Q-value for single neutron removal from $^{19}$O ($^{19}\text{O} \rightarrow ^{18}\text{O} + n$) is -3.96 MeV, comparable to -4.14 MeV for $^{17}\text{O} \rightarrow ^{16}\text{O} + n$.

## Context

This work extends prior measurements of fusion for oxygen isotopes (Kovar 1979, Eyal 1976, Heusch 1982, Steinbach 2014) and revises earlier $^{19}$O data (Singh 2017) with improved calibration. The observed enhancement contradicts the predictions of static models (RMF-SP, FHF) and TDHF calculations, which normally overpredict fusion for light systems due to the absence of breakup. This result suggests that extremely large-amplitude collective motion, not captured by standard TDHF or coupled-channels approaches, plays a crucial role in fusion of neutron-rich nuclei, and enables future measurements of the most neutron-rich light nuclei to probe these dynamics, with implications for understanding heat sources in X-ray superbursts in accreting neutron stars (Horowitz 2008).
