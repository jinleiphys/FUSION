# Hypernuclei with Neural Network Quantum States

> Leveraging complementary machine-learning-based approaches, we compute properties of $s$- and $p$-shell $Λ$ hypernuclei - including binding energies, single-particle densities, and radii - starting from the individual interactions among their constituents. These interactions are modeled using an improved leading-order pionless effective field theory expansion, with coefficients determined via a Gaussian Process framework anchored on virtually exact few-body techniques. We solve the many-body Schrödinger equation using a variational Monte Carlo method based on neural network quantum states, extending it for the first time to include $Λ$ particles alongside protons and neutrons. The predicted binding energies show remarkably good agreement with experimental results, given the simplicity of the input Hamiltonian. We also confirm the experimentally observed shrinkage of the proton radius in $^7_Λ$Li compared to its parent nucleus, $^6$Li. This work paves the way for an ab initio description of medium-mass and heavy hypernuclei, as well as for understanding the onset of strange degrees of freedom in the core of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors compute ground-state properties of s- and p-shell Lambda hypernuclei using a variational Monte Carlo method based on neural network quantum states extended to include Lambda particles, with interactions modeled by a leading-order pionless effective field theory whose low-energy constants are fixed to finite regulator values rather than taken to infinity, thereby sacrificing formal renormalization group invariance to avoid instabilities in many-body systems.

## Method

The nuclear and hypernuclear interactions are modeled using a leading-order pionless effective field theory with Gaussian-regulated contact interactions, where the two-body low-energy constants and regulator cutoffs are fitted to reproduce experimental scattering lengths and effective ranges in the np and p-Lambda channels. Three-body nucleon-nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon-Lambda forces are calibrated by fitting to experimental binding energies of 3H, 3He, 4He, and 16O for the nuclear sector, and to Lambda separation energies of 3LambdaH, 4LambdaH (spin-0 and spin-1), 5LambdaHe, and 16LambdaO for the hypernuclear sector, using Gaussian Process interpolation and Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to explore parameter space. The many-body Schrodinger equation is solved with a variational Monte Carlo approach using a Pfaffian-Jastrow neural network quantum state ansatz, where the total wave function for single-Lambda hypernuclei factorizes into a Jastrow factor, a Lambda single-particle orbital, and a Pfaffian antisymmetrizing the nucleonic part.

## Context

This work extends the neural network quantum state variational Monte Carlo method, previously applied to nuclear systems by Adams (2020), Lovato (2022), Kim (2023), and Fore (2024), to include Lambda hyperons alongside protons and neutrons. It builds upon the pionless effective field theory approach to hypernuclei developed by Contessi (2018) and the strategy of fixing regulator cutoffs to finite values introduced by Lu (2018) and Schiavilla (2021), which avoids the instability of multi-fermion states at leading order. The results enable future ab initio calculations of medium-mass and heavy hypernuclei, and provide a pathway toward understanding the role of strange degrees of freedom in neutron star cores, potentially addressing the hyperon puzzle identified by Lonardoni (2014) and Haidenbauer (2016).
