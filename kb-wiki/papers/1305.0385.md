# Differential HBT Method to Analyze Rotation

> Two particle correlations are studied in the reaction plane of peripheral relativistic heavy ion reactions where the initial state has substantial angular momentum. The earlier predicted rotation effect and Kelvin Helmholtz Instability, leads to space-time momentum correlations among the emitted particles. A specific combination of two particle correlation measurements is proposed, which can sensitively detect the rotation of the emitting system. Here the method is presented in simple few source models where the symmetries and the possibilities of the detection can be demonstrated in a transparent way.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a Differential Hanbury Brown and Twiss (DHBT) method to detect rotation in peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions, but the method is demonstrated only in simplified few-source models where symmetries and detection possibilities can be shown transparently, rather than in full-scale fluid dynamical calculations.

## Method

The authors construct a two-particle correlation function using a source function based on a Jüttner (relativistic Boltzmann) distribution for pions emitted from a freeze-out layer, with the emission function parametrized in the Buda-Lund model. They then evaluate the correlation function analytically for idealized configurations of one or two Gaussian fluid cells at rest or moving in opposite directions, computing the momentum-dependent correlation function C(k,q) from the Fourier transform of the source distribution.

## Key numbers

- Single static Gaussian source with radius R = 0.25 fm yields a correlation function C(k,q) = 1 + exp(-R²q²) that extends to large relative momentum q (Figure 1)
- Two static sources separated by distance 2d = 5 fm produce zero points in the correlation function at q_x = π/(2d) ≈ 0.628 fm⁻¹ and q_x = 3π/(2d) ≈ 1.885 fm⁻¹ (Figure 3)
- For two sources with separation 2d = 2.5 fm, the cosine modulation in the correlation function becomes apparent when the distance between sources is comparable to the source radius (Figure 3)
- The fluid cell size in high-resolution 3+1D fluid dynamical calculations is (0.3 fm)³

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions of rotation and Kelvin-Helmholtz instability in peripheral heavy ion collisions from fluid dynamical estimates (Csernai and collaborators, 2011-2013) and the observation that increased rotation could be observable via v₁ flow. The authors note that a detailed two-particle correlation study of flow rotation had not been performed previously (Csernai, L.P., S. Velle, and D. Wang, 2013). The method is intended to enable detection of rotation and determination of vorticity or circulation strength in future full-scale fluid dynamical calculations, complementing the weak v₁ signal at high energies.
