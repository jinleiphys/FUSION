# Effect of medium dependent binding energies on inferring the temperatures and freeze-out density of disassembling hot nuclear matter from cluster yields

> We explore the abundance of light clusters in asymmetric nuclear matter at subsaturation density. With increasing density, binding energies and wave functions are modified due to medium effects. The method of Albergo, Costa, Costanzo and Rubbino (ACCR) for determining the temperature and free nucleon density of a disassembling hot nuclear source from fragment yields is modified to include, in addition to Coulomb effects and flow, also effects of medium modifications of cluster properties, which become of importance when the nuclear matter density is above 10$^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$. We show how the analysis of cluster yields, to infer temperature and nucleon densities, is modified if the shifts in binding energies of in medium clusters are included. While, at low densities, the temperature calculated from given yields changes only modestly if medium effects are taken into account, larger discrepancies are observed when the nucleon densities are determined from measured yields.

## Key claim

The standard Albergo-Costa-Costanzo-Rubbino (ACCR) method for extracting temperature and free nucleon density from cluster yields must be modified to include medium-dependent binding energy shifts when the nuclear matter density exceeds 10^-3 fm^-3; while the temperature changes only modestly at low densities, the inferred nucleon densities show larger discrepancies when medium effects are included.

## Method

The authors start with a quantum statistical approach to nuclear matter, using a cluster decomposition of the self-energy to derive an in-medium Schrodinger equation that includes single-nucleon quasiparticle shifts and Pauli blocking terms. They calculate the Pauli blocking shifts for deuterons, tritons, 3He, and alpha particles as linear functions of the nucleon densities and temperature, then use these medium-modified binding energies to compute cluster yields in the quasiparticle approximation. By comparing the yields predicted with medium effects to those that would be inferred using the standard ACCR relations (which neglect medium effects), they quantify the deviations in extracted temperature and density.

## Key numbers

- The medium-dependent binding energy shifts become important when baryonic density n_B is above 10^-3 fm^-3 (approximately 10^-3 times nuclear saturation density).
- The ACCR temperature formula uses a constant 14.325 MeV (the difference in ground-state binding energies: -2.225 - 28.3 + 8.482 + 7.718 MeV) and a prefactor 1.591 (9/sqrt(32)).
- The Pauli blocking shift for deuterons is given by: Delta E_d^Pauli = {38384/(1+22.52/T)^(1/2) - 0.39402 exp[0.049418(1+22.52/T)] Erfc[0.2223(1+22.52/T)^(1/2)]} * (n_p + n_n)/T^(3/2).
- The Pauli blocking shift for tritons is: Delta E_t^Pauli = 3389.7 * [1+0.13347 T]^(-3/2) * (2/3 n_p + 4/3 n_n).
- The Pauli blocking shift for 3He is: Delta E_h^Pauli = 3901.5 * [1+0.16455 T]^(-3/2) * (4/3 n_p + 2/3 n_n).
- The Pauli blocking shift for alpha particles is: Delta E_alpha^Pauli = 4716.0 * [1+0.09372 T]^(-3/2) * (n_p + n_n).
- The chemical potential formulas without medium effects give mu_p^(a) = -19.8 MeV + T^(a) ln[(3^(3/2)/4) Y_alpha/Y_t] and mu_n^(a) = -20.6 MeV + T^(a) ln[(3^(3/2)/4) Y_alpha/Y_h].
- The corrected chemical potential formulas with medium effects give mu_p = -19.818 MeV + Delta E_alpha^qu - Delta E_t^qu + T ln[(3^(3/2)/4) Y_alpha/Y_t] and mu_n = -20.582 MeV + Delta E_alpha^qu - Delta E_h^qu + T ln[(3^(3/2)/4) Y_alpha/Y_h].

## Context

This work extends the ACCR method (Albergo et al., 1985) for extracting temperatures from isotope ratios by incorporating medium modifications of cluster binding energies, building on earlier quantum statistical approaches (Roepke, 1983; Roepke, 2008). Previous modifications to the ACCR method included Coulomb screening using the Wigner-Seitz approximation (Kolomietz et al., 1997), which gave temperature corrections less than 20%, and radial collective flow effects (Shlomo et al., 1997), which had small effects on temperature but significant effects on freeze-out density. The present work enables more accurate extraction of thermodynamic parameters from heavy-ion collision data at densities above 10^-3 fm^-3, and suggests that self-consistent iteration or precomputed tables should be used to account for density-dependent binding energies when inferring freeze-out conditions.
