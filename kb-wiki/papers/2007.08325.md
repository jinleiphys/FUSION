# Possibilities of direct production of superheavy nuclei with Z=112--118 in different evaporation channels

> The production cross sections of heaviest isotopes of superheavy nuclei with charge numbers 112--118 are predicted in the $xn$--, $pxn$--, and $αxn$--evaporation channels of the $^{48}$Ca-induced complete fusion reactions for future experiments. The estimates of synthesis capabilities are based on a uniform and consistent set of input nuclear data. Nuclear masses, deformations, shell corrections, fission barriers, and decay energies are calculated within the macroscopic-microscopic approach for even-even, odd-Z, and odd-N nuclei. For odd systems, the blocking procedure is used. To find, the ground states via minimization and saddle points using Immersion Water flow technique, multidimensional deformation spaces, containing non-axially are used. As shown, current calculations based on a new set of mass and barriers, agree very well with experimentally known cross-sections, especially in the $3n$--evaporation channel. The dependencies of these predictions on the mass/fission barriers tables and fusion models are discussed. A way is shown to produce directly unknown superheavy isotopes in the $1n$-- or $2n$--evaporation channels. The synthesis of new superheavy isotopes unattainable in reactions with emission of neutrons is proposed in the promising channels with emission of protons ($σ_{pxn} \simeq 10-200$ fb) and alphas ($σ_{αxn} \simeq 5-500$ fb).

# Possibilities of direct production of superheavy nuclei with Z=112--118 in different evaporation channels

## Key claim

The production cross sections for superheavy nuclei with charge numbers 112-118 in $^{48}$Ca-induced complete fusion reactions are predicted using a consistent set of nuclear data from a single macroscopic-microscopic model, with the claim that the $pxn$- and $\alpha xn$-evaporation channels can produce isotopes unreachable via neutron evaporation channels. The hedge is that these predictions carry an estimated uncertainty within a factor of 2-4 near the maxima of excitation functions, and the cross sections depend sensitively on the choice of mass/fission barrier tables and fusion models.

## Method

The authors calculated evaporation residue cross sections using a factorized approach combining the capture cross section, the probability of compound nucleus formation (described within the dinuclear system model), and the survival probability against fission during de-excitation. Nuclear masses, deformations, shell corrections, fission barriers, and decay energies were computed within the macroscopic-microscopic approach using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, with ground states found by minimization over axially-symmetric shapes and saddle points searched in a five-dimensional deformation space (including triaxial shapes) using the Imaginary Water Flow technique. For odd systems, the blocking procedure was employed, and the de-excitation of the compound nucleus was treated with the statistical model using Fermi-gas level densities.

## Key numbers

- Predicted cross sections in $pxn$-evaporation channels: approximately 10-200 fb for production of isotopes such as $^{287-290}$Nh, $^{291-293}$Mc, and $^{295-296}$Ts
- Predicted cross sections in $\alpha xn$-evaporation channels: approximately 50-500 fb for production of isotopes such as $^{286,287}$Cn, $^{286}$Nh, $^{290,291}$Fl, $^{291,292}$Mc, and $^{294}$Lv
- The rms deviation in calculated masses for 252 heavy nuclei is about 400 keV with the blocking scenario
- For 88 measured $Q_{\alpha}$ values in superheavy nuclei, the rms deviation is about 250 keV
- The average discrepancy and rms deviation for inner and outer fission barrier heights compared with experimental estimates for 75 actinides are not larger than 0.82 MeV and 0.94 MeV, respectively
- The damping parameter for shell effects is set to $E_d = 25$ MeV
- The asymptotic level-density parameter for neutrons is $a_n = A/10$ MeV$^{-1}$, with $a_f = 0.98a$, $a_p = 0.96a$, and $a_\alpha = 1.15a$
- The Coulomb barrier parameter $r_\alpha$ is set to 1.57 fm and $r_p$ to 1.7 fm, giving $V_\alpha$ and $V_p$ values about 2.5 MeV and 1.5 MeV larger than those used in prior work by Swiatecki et al.
- The $Q_\alpha$ value for $^{294}$Og is calculated as 12.09 MeV compared to the experimental value of 11.81 MeV; for $^{288}$Fl, 10.32 MeV calculated versus 10.07 MeV experimental

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Hong, Adamian, and Antonenko (2017) and Swiatecki, Kowal, and collaborators (2017-2018) by employing a new mass table from Kowal, Jachimowicz, and collaborators (2014, 2017) based on the macroscopic-microscopic method with blocking for odd nuclei, rather than the mass table of Moller (1995) or the quasiparticle method used in the Fusion-by-Diffusion model. The calculations enable predictions for producing neutron-rich superheavy isotopes closer to the center of the predicted "island of stability" at $N=184$, including isotopes such as $^{295}$Ts and $^{295-297}$Og that are presently unknown and cannot be reached through neutron evaporation channels alone. The results also show that the $pxn$-evaporation channels appear more effective for approaching $N=184$ than the $\alpha xn$-channels, and that reactions with even-$Z$ targets produce more neutron-rich isotopes than those with odd-$Z$ targets.
