# Effect of higher orbital angular momenta in the baryon spectrum

> We have performed a Faddeev calculation of the baryon spectrum for the chiral constituent quark model including higher orbital angular momentum states. We have found that the effect of these states is important, although a description of the baryon spectrum of the same quality as the one given by including only the lowest-order configurations can be obtained. We have studied the effect of the pseudoscalar quark-quark interaction on the relative position of the positive- and negative-parity excitations of the nucleon as well as the effect of varying the strength of the color-magnetic interaction.

## Key claim

The authors show that including higher orbital angular momentum components (up to ℓ and λ = 5) in a Faddeev calculation of the baryon spectrum for the chiral constituent quark model does not qualitatively change the conclusions of their earlier restricted-basis calculation, although the higher components shift the masses of the N, N(1440), and N(1535) by approximately 100 MeV while the Δ shifts by only 5 MeV. The correct ordering of positive- and negative-parity nucleon excitations cannot be achieved without sacrificing consistency with the two-nucleon system.

## Method

The authors performed a non-relativistic Faddeev calculation of the nonstrange baryon spectrum using the chiral constituent quark model, which includes a confining interaction, perturbative one-gluon exchange (OGE), and pseudoscalar and scalar boson exchanges between quarks. They extended their previous calculation by including all configurations (ℓ, λ, s, t) with orbital angular momenta ℓ and λ up to 5, and studied convergence by comparing results with increasing numbers of configurations. They also varied the strong coupling constant α_s of the OGE and the cutoff parameter Λ_π of the one-pion exchange (OPE) to investigate the relative positions of the N(1440) and N(1535) states.

## Key numbers

- The Δ mass shifts by only 5 MeV when going from 1 configuration (423 MeV) to 3 configurations (418 MeV) relative to the nucleon ground state.
- The N(1535) mass shifts from 643 MeV (4 configurations) to 540 MeV (20 configurations), a change of approximately 100 MeV.
- The N ground state mass shifts from 115 MeV (2 configurations) to 0 MeV (12 configurations, converged reference), a change of approximately 100 MeV.
- The N(1440) mass shifts from 641 MeV (2 configurations) to 558 MeV (12 configurations), a change of approximately 80 MeV.
- The confining potential slope a_c is 67.0 MeV fm^{-1} for one parameter set and 60.12 MeV fm^{-1} for another.
- The strong coupling constant α_s is 0.485 for one parameter set and 0.75 for another.
- The OPE cutoff parameter Λ_π is 4.2 fm^{-1} for both parameter sets; inversion of the N(1440) and N(1535) ordering requires Λ_π around 7 fm^{-1}, which yields a N-Δ mass difference of approximately 955 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev calculation of Garcilazo, Valcarce, and Fernandez (2001) by including higher orbital angular momentum components that were omitted in their earlier restricted-basis study. The chiral constituent quark model used here was developed by Fernandez, Valcarce, Straub, and Faessler (1993) and Valcarce, Buchmann, Fernandez, and Faessler (1994) for baryon-baryon interactions, and previously applied to the baryon spectrum by Valcarce, Gonzalez, Fernandez, and Vento (1996) and Fernandez, Gonzalez, and Valcarce (1999). The results confirm that the model combining OGE with Goldstone-boson exchanges can describe the baryon spectrum, but the incorrect ordering of positive- and negative-parity nucleon excitations remains a problem that cannot be fixed without breaking consistency with the two-nucleon system, as also noted by Nakamoto and Toki (1998). The work enables future studies to explore other mechanisms responsible for the correct level ordering.
