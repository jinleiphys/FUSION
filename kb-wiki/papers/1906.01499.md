# Diffusion of charm quarks in jets in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> The radial distribution of $D^0$ mesons in jets probes the diffusion of charm quark relative to the jet axis and provides a new perspective to study the interaction mechanisms between heavy quarks and the medium in the nucleus-nucleus collisions. The in-medium parton propagations are described by a Monte Carlo transport model which uses the next-to-leading order (NLO) plus parton shower (PS) event generator SHERPA as input and includes elastic (collisional) and inelastic (radiative) interaction for heavy quarks as well as light partons. At low $D^0$ meson $p_T$, the radial distribution significantly shifts to larger radius indicating a strong diffusion effect which is consistent with the recent experimental data. We demonstrate that the angular deviation of charm quarks declines with $p_T$ and is very sensitive to the collisional more than radiative interaction at $p_T<5$~GeV. As predictions, we present the $D^0$ meson radial distribution in jets in p+p and $0-10\%$ Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$~GeV at the RHIC, and also estimate the nuclear modification factor of charm jet in central Au+Au collisions at 200~GeV at the RHIC and central Pb+Pb collisions at $5.02$~TeV at the LHC.

## Key claim

The radial distribution of low-\(p_T\) \(D^0\) mesons in jets is sensitive to the diffusion of charm quarks relative to the jet axis, and the angular deviation of charm quarks is more sensitive to collisional (elastic) interactions than to radiative (inelastic) interactions at \(p_T < 5\) GeV, providing a way to disentangle these two energy-loss mechanisms.

## Method

The authors use a Monte Carlo transport model that takes next-to-leading order (NLO) plus parton shower (PS) events from the SHERPA generator as input for p+p collisions. In-medium parton propagation is simulated using modified Langevin equations for elastic (collisional) interactions and a higher-twist formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation (inelastic interactions), with the background QGP evolution provided by the iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic code. The charm quark hadronization into \(D^0\) mesons is performed using Peterson fragmentation functions, and jets are reconstructed with the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm.

## Key numbers

* The spatial diffusion coefficient is set to \(2\pi T D_s = 4\), based on Lattice QCD calculations.
* The jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}\) is taken as \(q_0 = 1.2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm for Pb+Pb at LHC energy and \(q_0 = 0.5\) GeV\(^2\)/fm for Au+Au at RHIC energy.
* The predicted nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for charm jets in \(0-10\%\) Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV is approximately 0.4, with weak \(p_T\) dependence.
* The predicted \(R_{AA}\) for charm jets in \(0-10\%\) Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV varies from 0.4 to 0.6 as \(p_T\) increases from 50 to 400 GeV.
* The angular deviation \(\Delta r\) of charm quarks is dominated by collisional interactions at \(p_T < 5\) GeV, while radiative interactions give a non-zero contribution even at \(p_T \sim 50\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of heavy-flavor jet quenching, such as measurements of \(b\)-jets by Chatrchyan (2013) and \(b\bar{b}\) dijets by Sirunyan (2018), and the CMS measurement of the \(D^0\) meson radial profile in jets (CMS, 2018). It provides a theoretical framework to disentangle collisional and radiative energy-loss mechanisms for heavy quarks, which is a key open question in understanding the properties of the quark-gluon plasma. The predictions for the \(D^0\) meson radial distribution in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and the nuclear modification factor of charm jets at both RHIC and LHC are intended to guide future experimental measurements.
