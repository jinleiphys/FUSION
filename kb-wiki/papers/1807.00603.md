# A Fixed-Target Programme at the LHC: Physics Case and Projected Performances for Heavy-Ion, Hadron, Spin and Astroparticle Studies

> We review the context, the motivations and the expected performances of a comprehensive and ambitious fixed-target program using the multi-TeV proton and ion LHC beams. We also provide a detailed account of the different possible technical implementations ranging from an internal wire target to a full dedicated beam line extracted with a bent crystal. The possibilities offered by the use of the ALICE and LHCb detectors in the fixed-target mode are also reviewed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a fixed-target program using the multi-TeV LHC beams can provide competitive or superior physics reach for heavy-ion, hadron, spin, and astroparticle studies compared to collider-mode experiments, but the actual achievable luminosities and physics output depend critically on the specific technical implementation (internal wire target, gas jet, or bent-crystal extracted beam) and on the detector used (ALICE or LHCb), with the projected rates limited by detector occupancy and readout capabilities.

## Method

The authors reviewed the technical feasibility of several fixed-target implementations at the LHC, including internal wire targets, gas-jet targets, and bent-crystal beam extraction, and evaluated the projected luminosities and detector acceptances for each. They generated pseudo-data for the ALICE and LHCb detectors in fixed-target mode, using the EPOS event generator to compute charged-particle multiplicities and inelastic rates for proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions at center-of-mass energies per nucleon pair of 115 GeV and 72 GeV. The projected physics performance was assessed for three main topics: high-x parton distribution functions, spin physics with polarized targets, and heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- For proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 115 GeV in fixed-target mode, the instantaneous luminosity can reach up to 10^33 cm^-2 s^-1, yielding an inelastic rate of 36 MHz.
- For proton-xenon collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 115 GeV, the instantaneous luminosity can reach up to 2 x 10^32 cm^-2 s^-1, yielding an inelastic rate of 300 MHz.
- For lead-xenon collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 72 GeV, the instantaneous luminosity can reach up to 3 x 10^28 cm^-2 s^-1, yielding an inelastic rate of 190 kHz.
- The rapidity shift for a fixed-target experiment is Delta y = 4.2 for a beam energy per nucleon of 2.76 TeV and Delta y = 4.8 for 7 TeV.
- In the most central 10% of PbXe and PbW collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 72 GeV, the maximum charged-particle multiplicity is dN_ch/d eta ~ 600-700 at eta_lab ~ 4.2.
- The ALICE detector in fixed-target mode can achieve a transverse momentum resolution of 0.8% to 2% for p_T = 1 to 10 GeV using the ITS and TPC.
- The LHCb VELO achieves a primary vertex resolution of 13 microns in the x-y plane and 71 microns in the z direction for 25 tracks.

## Context

This work extends the concept of fixed-target experiments at colliders, previously demonstrated at RHIC with the H-jet polarized target (Zelenski, 2005) and at the LHC with the LHCb SMOG system, by proposing a comprehensive program using the full LHC beam intensity. It enables measurements at very large negative center-of-mass rapidities (high x) that are inaccessible in collider mode, and allows for polarized target studies of transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions and single-spin asymmetries. The paper provides the projected performance needed to motivate technical design studies for integrating fixed-target systems into ALICE and LHCb during LHC Long Shutdowns 2 and 3.
