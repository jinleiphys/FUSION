# Toward an estimate of the amplitude $X(3872)\toŽĆ^0Žć_{c1}(1P)$

> The well-known model of the triangle diagrams with $D^*\bar DD^*$ and $\bar D^*D\bar D^*$ mesons in the loops is compared with the modern data on the amplitude of the $X(3872)\toŽĆ^0Žć_{c1}(1P)$ decay. Considering the $X(3872)$ object as a $Žć_{c1}(2P)$ charmonium state, we introduce a parameter $╬Š$ characterizing the scale of the isotopic symmetry violation in this decay and find a lower limit of $╬Š\simeq0.0916$. The model incorporates the only fitted parameter associated with the form factor. We analyze in detail the influence of the form factor on the amplitude $X(3872)\toŽĆ^0 Žć_{c1}(1P)$ and on the parameter $╬Š$. As the suppression of the amplitude by the form factor increases, $╬Š$ increases. Due to the fact that the $X(3872)$ resonance is located practically at the threshold of the $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ channel, the amplitude of $X(3872) \toŽĆ^0Žć_{c1}(1P)$ turns out to be proportional to $\sqrt{m_d -m_u}$. Using the estimating values for the coupling constants $g_{XD\bar D^*}$, $g_{Žć_{ c1}D\bar D^*}$, and $g_{D^{*0}D^{*0}ŽĆ^0}$, we show that the model of the triangle loop diagrams is in reasonable agreement with the available data. Apart from the difference in the masses of neutral and charged charmed mesons, any additional exotic sources of isospin violation in $X(3872)\toŽĆ^0Žć_{c1}(1P)$ (such as a significant difference between the coupling constants $g_{XD^0\bar D^{*0}}$ and $g_{XD^+ D^{*-}}$) are not required to interpret the data. This indirectly confirms the isotopic neutrality of the $X(3872)$, which is naturally realized for the $c\bar c$ state $Žć_{c1}(2P)$.

## Key claim

The triangle-loop model with charmed mesons can explain the measured amplitude of $X(3872)\to\pi^0\chi_{c1}(1P)$ without invoking exotic isospin-violation mechanisms, provided the form-factor cutoff parameter $\alpha$ lies in the range 1.487 to 2.565; the isospin-violation parameter $\xi$ has a lower limit of about 0.0916 at the $X(3872)$ mass when no form factor is applied, but increases to about 0.15 when the form factor is included.

## Method

The authors compute the $X(3872)\to\pi^0\chi_{c1}(1P)$ amplitude using triangle-loop diagrams with $D^*\bar D D^*$ and $\bar D^* D \bar D^*$ intermediate states, treating $X(3872)$ as the $\chi_{c1}(2P)$ charmonium state with equal couplings to neutral and charged $D\bar D^*$ channels. They introduce a monopole form factor $\mathcal{F}(q^2,m_{D^*}^2)=(\Lambda^2-m_{D^*}^2)/(\Lambda^2-q^2)$ with $\Lambda=m_{D^*}+\alpha\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ ($\Lambda_{\rm QCD}=220$ MeV) as the only fitted parameter, and compare the resulting invariant amplitude $G_{\pi^0}(m_X^2)$ to the experimental value extracted from branching-fraction data.

## Key numbers

- Lower limit of isospin-violation parameter $\xi\simeq0.0916$ at $\sqrt{s}=m_X=3871.65$ MeV when no form factor is applied ($\alpha=\infty$)
- Experimental branching fraction $\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\to\pi^0\chi_{c1}(1P))=(3.4\pm1.6)\%$ from PDG (2023)
- Experimental absolute width $\Gamma(X(3872)\to\pi^0\chi_{c1}(1P);m_X^2)=(0.04\pm0.02)$ MeV
- Extracted invariant amplitude $|G_{\pi^0}(m_X^2)|=(0.216\pm0.054)$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Product of coupling constants $|g_X g_{\chi_{c1}} g_{\pi^0}|/(16\pi)=\left(15.67^{+24.29}_{-3.14}\right)$ GeV from heavy-quark effective theory estimates
- Form-factor parameter range consistent with data: $\alpha$ from 1.487 to 2.565, with central value $\alpha=1.98$
- Average isospin-violation parameter in the consistent $\alpha$ range: $\xi\simeq0.15$
- Threshold difference $|\rho^n(s)-\rho^c(s)|\simeq0.0652$ between neutral and charged $D\bar D^*$ channels

## Context

This work extends the triangle-loop mechanism previously applied to $X(3872)$ decays by Me07 and Wu21, treating $X(3872)$ as the conventional $\chi_{c1}(2P)$ charmonium state (Achasov-Ramazashvili 2014, 2015, 2016) rather than a molecular or tetraquark state. The analysis shows that the data do not require the significant difference between $g_{XD^0\bar D^{*0}}$ and $g_{XD^+ D^{*-}}$ invoked in molecular models (Wu21), and that the isospin violation arises naturally from the $m_d-m_u$ mass difference via threshold effects. The result enables future high-statistics measurements of $X(3872)$ decays to distinguish between the charmonium interpretation and alternative models such as the molecular picture (Swanson 2004, Zhang 2014) or tetraquark states (Maiani 2005).
