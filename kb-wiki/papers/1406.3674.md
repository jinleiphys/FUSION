# In-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-sections with non-spherical Pauli blocking

> We present a formalism to solve the Bethe-Goldstone scattering equation without the use of partial wave expansion which is alternative to the one we developed in a previous work. The present approach is more suitable for the calculation of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, which are the focal point of this paper. The impact of removing the spherical approximation on the angle and energy dependence of, particularly, in-medium proton-proton and proton-neutron differential cross sections is discussed along with its potential implication.

## Key claim

The paper presents an alternative three-dimensional formalism for solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation without partial wave expansion, and finds that removing the spherical (angle-averaged) approximation on the Pauli blocking operator produces only moderate changes in in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, with slightly larger sensitivity in asymmetric nuclear matter and in spin-dependent observables.

## Method

The authors solve the Thompson and Bethe-Goldstone scattering equations in a helicity basis without partial wave decomposition, using a new method to integrate out the azimuthal degree of freedom that preserves non-zero matrix elements when the initial momentum is along the quantization axis. They obtain six partially coupled integral equations for the independent amplitudes, solve them numerically, and construct in-medium differential cross sections and depolarization parameters for proton-proton and proton-neutron scattering in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. The results are compared with calculations using the conventional angle-averaged Pauli operator.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter at Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.4\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\): 612.8 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass at \(k_F = 1.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\): 718.3 MeV
- Free-space incident energies considered: 50, 100, and 200 MeV
- Fermi momenta used for asymmetric matter: \(k_{F1} = 1.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), \(k_{F2} = 1.4\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)
- Density relation: \(\rho = 2k_F^3 / (3\pi^2)\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-dimensional approach to the Bethe-Goldstone equation (White 2013) by developing an alternative azimuthal integration method and applying it to calculate in-medium cross sections. It contrasts with earlier microscopic predictions from Li and Machleidt (2005), the work of Pandharipande and collaborators, and the calculations of Persram and Gale, which all used the angle-averaged Pauli operator and did not include medium asymmetries. The results enable more accurate modeling of in-medium NN scattering for applications in heavy-ion collision transport equations (such as the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation) and for calculating nucleon mean-free paths in nuclear matter.
