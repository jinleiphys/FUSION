# Multi-Collinear Splitting Kernels for Track Function Evolution

> Jets and their substructure play a central role in many analyses at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). To improve the precision of measurements, as well as to enable measurement of jet substructure at increasingly small angular scales, tracking information is often used due to its superior angular resolution and robustness to pile-up. Calculations of track-based observables involve non-perturbative track functions, that absorb infrared divergences in perturbative calculations and describe the transition to charged hadrons. The infrared divergences are directly related to the renormalization group evolution (RGE), and can be systematically computed in perturbation theory. Unlike the standard DGLAP evolution, the RGE of the track functions is non-linear, encoding correlations in the fragmentation process. We compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) evolution of the track functions, which involves in its kernel the full $1\rightarrow3$ splitting function. We discuss in detail how how we implement the evolution equation numerically, and illustrate the size of the NLO corrections. We also show that our equation can be viewed as a master equation for collinear evolution at NLO, by illustrating that by integrating out specific terms, one can derive the evolution for any $N$-hadron fragmentation function. Our results provide a crucial ingredient for obtaining track-based predictions for generic measurements at the LHC, and for improving the description of the collinear dynamics of jets.

## Key claim

The authors compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) renormalization group evolution kernels for track functions, which are non-linear unlike standard DGLAP evolution, and show that this evolution equation can serve as a master equation from which the NLO evolution for any N-hadron fragmentation function can be derived by integrating out specific terms.

## Method

The authors extract the NLO evolution kernels from the infrared poles of renormalized track jet functions, which are differential in both the invariant mass of all particles and the momentum fraction of the subset of charged hadrons. They use sector decomposition to handle overlapping soft divergences in the 1→3 splitting phase space, dividing the momentum fraction space into six sectors and applying coordinate transformations that map each sector to a unit square. The calculation requires both tree-level 1→3 splitting functions and one-loop 1→2 splitting functions in dimensional regularization, with results obtained for both N=4 super Yang-Mills theory and full QCD with quark and gluon jets.

## Key numbers

- The LO evolution kernels K^(0) were computed in Chang (2013); the NLO kernels K^(1) are the new results presented in this work
- The NLO evolution involves 1→3 splitting functions at order α_s^2, requiring calculation of the track jet function at O(α_s^2)
- The sector decomposition divides the 3-parton phase space into 6 sectors, each mapping to a unit square with coordinates 0 ≤ z ≤ 1 and 0 ≤ t ≤ 1
- The collinear phase-space measure for 1→3 splittings includes a factor 1/(4π)^(5-2ε)Γ(1-2ε) and the Δ function involving momentum fractions and invariant masses

## Context

This work extends the leading-order track function formalism developed by Chang (2013) to next-to-leading order, providing the first complete NLO evolution equations for track functions in QCD. The results enable track-based predictions for generic LHC measurements and improve the description of collinear jet dynamics, complementing recent NLO calculations of track function moments for energy correlators by Li (2021) and Jaarsma (2022). The evolution equations can be reduced to standard DGLAP equations or multi-hadron fragmentation functions (previously unknown at NLO), and the kernels are directly related to infrared poles that must be subtracted in fixed-order calculations of track-based observables.
