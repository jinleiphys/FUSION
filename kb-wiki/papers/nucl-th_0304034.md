# A Possible Form of the Orthogonal Set in Six Kinds of Boson Operators --In Relation to the su(2)- and its Relevant Algebras--

> With the aim of constructing coherent states for many-body systems consisting of six kinds of boson operators, a possible form of the orthogonal set is presented in terms of monomial with respect to state generating operators. In connection with the su(3)-, the su(2)-, the su(2,1)- and the su(1,1)-algebras, four types of the orthogonal set are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a monomial form of the orthogonal set for six kinds of boson operators, which is claimed to be useful for constructing coherent states for many-body systems; however, the orthogonal set is not orthogonal in its raw form and requires appropriate linear combinations (via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients) to achieve orthogonality, and the construction is limited to the specific case of M=1 and N=2 in the (M+1)(N+1) boson framework.

## Method

The authors construct the orthogonal set by first defining six kinds of boson operators (â_i, â_i^*) and (b̂_i, b̂_i^*) for i=1,2,3, then building su(2)-, su(3)-, su(2,1)-, and su(1,1)-algebras from bilinear combinations of these operators. The intrinsic state is determined by applying raising operators R_+(1), R_+(2), T_+(1), and T_+(2) to a vacuum state built from b̂_3^*, and the orthogonal set is obtained by forming spherical tensors with rank k and l and combining them via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, ultimately expressing the state as a monomial in the state-generating operators S_+, M_+, Q_+, T_+(2), and R_+(2).

## Key numbers

- The orthogonal set is specified by six quantum numbers: s, s_0, m, m_0, k, and l, where s = 0, 1/2, 1, 3/2, ... and s_0 = -s, -s+1, ..., s-1, s.
- The eigenvalues for the Casimir operator R̂² are given as (1/3)(2s+l)(2s+l+3) + l(l+1).
- The eigenvalue for the operator R̂_0 is 3k - (2s+l).
- The quantum numbers satisfy the inequalities: |s_0| ≤ s, |m_0| ≤ m, |k-l| ≤ m ≤ k+l, and m ≤ 2s+l-k.
- In the conventional su(2)-spin language, the spin magnitudes are s_1 = (1/2)(k+l+m_0), s_2 = (1/2)(k+l-m_0), s_3 = l-k+s, and s_{12} = m.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formalism for two, three, and four kinds of boson operators (Kuriyama et al., Refs. 9-11) to the case of six kinds, building on the (M+1)(N+1) boson framework developed in Ref. 8. The orthogonal set is intended to enable construction of coherent states for describing statistically mixed states of many-body systems consisting of two kinds of bosons interacting with an external harmonic oscillator, such as the single-level shell model under pairing correlation and the two-level shell model under particle-hole interaction (Lipkin model), as well as thermal effects in the three-level shell model.
