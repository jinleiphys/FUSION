# Neutron in Strong Magnetic Fields

> Relativistic world-line Hamiltonian for strongly interacting 3q systems in magnetic field is derived from the path integral for the corresponding Green's function. The neutral baryon Hamiltonian in magnetic field obeys the pseudomomentum conservation and allows a factorization of the c.m. and internal motion. The resulting expression for the baryon mass in magnetic field is written explicitly with the account of hyperfine, OPE and OGE (color Coulomb) interaction. The neutron mass is fast decreasing with magnetic field, losing 1/2 of its value at eB~0.25 GeV^2 and is nearly zero at eB~0.5 GeV^2. Possible physical consequences of the calculated mass trajectory of the neutron, M_n(B), are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The neutron mass decreases rapidly in strong magnetic fields, losing half its value at \(eB \sim 0.25\) GeV\(^2\) and becoming nearly zero at \(eB \sim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\), but the authors note that a theorem forbids the mass from actually vanishing, implying higher-order effects keep it finite though small.

## Method

The authors derive a relativistic world-line Hamiltonian for a three-quark (3q) system in a magnetic field from the Feynman-Schwinger path integral for the Green's function. For the neutral neutron, they exploit pseudomomentum conservation to factorize center-of-mass and internal motion, then compute the baryon mass by minimizing the Hamiltonian with respect to variational parameters \(\omega_i\) and \(\gamma\), including confinement approximated by a quadratic form, color Coulomb (one-gluon exchange) interaction with quark-loop screening, hyperfine splitting, and one-pion exchange contributions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron mass loses 1/2 of its value at \(eB \sim 0.25\) GeV\(^2\).
- Neutron mass is nearly zero at \(eB \sim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The dynamical mass \(\bar{M}_0\) (without gluon exchange and hyperfine interaction) at \(eB = 0\) for \(m_q = 0\) is \(\bar{M}_0 = 6\sqrt{\sigma}\).
- The magnetic field scale \(eB \sim \Lambda^2_{\text{QCD}} \sim 10^{19}\) G corresponds to \(eB \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\) (with GeV\(^2 \simeq 5.12 \cdot 10^{19}\) G).
- The Landau radius \(r_H = (eB)^{-1/2}\) equals 0.6 fm at \(eB \simeq 5 \cdot 10^{18}\) G.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytical studies of mesons in strong magnetic fields (Andreichikov et al., 2013; Orlovsky, 2013) to the three-quark baryon case, using the same world-line Hamiltonian formalism developed by Simonov (2000s) for baryons without magnetic fields. The results agree with lattice QCD data (Hidaka, 2012; Braguta, 2011) in the region \(eB \leq 5\) GeV\(^2\). The paper enables future studies of neutron star structure and possible neutron-to-quark matter transitions in strong magnetic fields, and addresses the "magnetic collapse" phenomenon previously seen in mesons in the large-\(N_c\) limit.
