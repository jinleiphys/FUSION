# Causal Viscous Hydrodynamics for Central Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study causal viscous hydrodynamics in the context of central relativistic heavy-ion collisions and provide details of a straightforward numerical algorithm to solve the hydrodynamic equations. It is shown that correlation functions of fluctuations provide stringent test cases for any such numerical algorithm. Passing these tests, we study the effects of viscosity on the temperature profile in central heavy-ion collisions. Also, we find that it is possible to counter-act the effects of viscosity to some extent by re-adjusting the initial conditions. However, viscous corrections are strongest for high-mass particles, signaling the breakdown of hydrodynamic descriptions for large eta/s.

## Key claim

Causal viscous hydrodynamics can be implemented numerically for central heavy-ion collisions, but viscous corrections are strongest for high-mass particles, signaling the breakdown of hydrodynamic descriptions for large values of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s).

## Method

The authors solve the Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamic equations for a geometry that is longitudinally expanding, space-time rapidity independent, and radially symmetric, using a finite-difference algorithm on a discretized space-time lattice. They test their numerical code by comparing correlation functions of fluctuations to analytic solutions of linearized hydrodynamics, both for ideal hydrodynamics (eta/s = 10^-4) and for viscous hydrodynamics (eta/s = 0.1 and 0.3), finding good agreement for the position and width of sonic peaks.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T_0 = 0.2 GeV at r=0 for ideal hydrodynamic tests, with nuclear radius R = 6.4 fm and sigma = 0.02 fm for the energy density profile
- For viscous hydrodynamic studies, initial temperature T_0 = 0.36 GeV at tau_0 = 1 fm/c, with R_0 = 6.4 fm and sigma = 0.54 fm
- Lattice spacing a = 0.25 GeV^-1 with N = 8192 sites for ideal tests, and N = 4096 sites for viscous tests
- Temporal time step delta tau = 0.005 a, with violation of the constraint sqrt(|(u^tau)^2 - (u^r)^2 - 1|) summed over all lattice sites staying below one percent
- Speed of sound squared c_s^2 = 1/3 used throughout

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of viscous corrections to ideal hydrodynamics by Teaney (2004) and provides a dynamical implementation of Israel-Stewart theory for central collisions at RHIC and the LHC, going beyond the acausal relativistic Navier-Stokes equations studied by Hiscock (1983). The authors note that fluctuation measurements may help constrain eta/s from RHIC data, as suggested by Gavin (2006), and that the initial conditions for the shear tensor can be chosen to match the Color-Glass-Condensate model of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) or its extensions.
