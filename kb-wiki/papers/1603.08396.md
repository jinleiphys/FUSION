# Inclusive electron scattering within the SuSAv2-MEC approach

> We present our recent progress on the relativistic modeling of electron-nucleus reactions and compare our predictions with inclusive $^{12}$C ($e,e'$) experimental data in a wide kinematical region. The model, originally based on the superscaling phenomenon shown by electron-nucleus scattering data, has recently been improved through the inclusion of Relativistic Mean Field theory effects that take into account the enhancement of the transverse scaling function compared with its longitudinal counterpart. We also discuss the impact of meson-exchange currents (MEC) through the analysis of two-particle two-hole longitudinal and transverse contributions to electromagnetic response functions evaluated within the framework of the relativistic Fermi gas. The formalism is also extended to include the complete inelastic spectrum -- resonant, non-resonant and deep inelastic scattering (DIS). The results show quite good agreement with data over the whole range of energy transfer, including the dip region between the quasielastic peak and the $Δ$ resonance.

## Key claim

The SuSAv2-MEC model, which combines relativistic mean field (RMF) and relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) descriptions with meson-exchange current (MEC) contributions, reproduces inclusive electron-carbon scattering data across a wide kinematic range, including the dip region between the quasielastic peak and the Delta resonance, but only after introducing a momentum-transfer-dependent blending parameter q0 that is tuned separately for the quasielastic and inelastic regimes.

## Method

The authors extended the SuSAv2 superscaling model by incorporating RMF effects that enhance the transverse scaling function relative to the longitudinal one, then blended these with RPWIA scaling functions using a q-dependent transition parameter determined by chi-squared fits to experimental data. They added 2p-2h MEC contributions computed within a fully relativistic Fermi gas framework, including for the first time both longitudinal and transverse channels, and parametrized these responses for efficient implementation. The inelastic spectrum was included using phenomenological fits to single-nucleon inelastic structure functions from Bosted and Christy, covering resonant, non-resonant, and deep inelastic scattering regions.

## Key numbers

* Fermi momentum for carbon-12: k_F = 228 MeV/c, taken from Chiara et al.
* RMF/RPWIA transition width parameter: omega_0 = 200 MeV (fixed)
* Quasielastic transition parameter q0^QE parametrized piecewise: for q < 700 MeV/c, q0^QE = 377.629 + 0.407 q (MeV/c); for q > 700 MeV/c, q0^QE = -5.322 + 0.968 q (MeV/c)
* Inelastic transition parameter q0^inel = 494.439 + 0.706 q (MeV/c)
* 2p-2h MEC contributions to the cross section are approximately 10-15% of the total
* At kinematics q_QE = 917 MeV/c (theta_e = 37.5 degrees), the model overestimates data by about 10% at the quasielastic peak and in the dip region
* At kinematics q_QE = 792 MeV/c (theta_e = 37.5 degrees), the model overestimates data by about 5% at the quasielastic peak
* Momentum transfer range explored: q = 239-3432 MeV/c
* Incident electron energies: E_i = 280-4045 MeV
* Scattering angles: theta_e = 12-145 degrees

## Context

This work extends the SuSAv2 model, previously restricted to the quasielastic domain by Gonzalez-Jimenez et al. (2014), to cover the full inelastic spectrum including resonant and deep inelastic scattering regions. It improves upon earlier superscaling analyses by Maieron et al. (2009) that assumed equal longitudinal and transverse scaling functions (scaling of zeroth kind), by incorporating the RMF-induced transverse enhancement and a q-dependent transition to RPWIA at higher momentum transfers. The model enables application to neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments where all kinematic regions contribute, particularly addressing the 2p-2h MEC effects that Martini et al. (2009) and Nieves et al. (2011) have shown to be essential for reproducing neutrino cross sections.
