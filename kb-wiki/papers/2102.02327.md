# Two- and three-nucleon contact interactions and ground-state energies of light- and medium-mass nuclei

> Classes of two-nucleon ($2N$) contact interactions are developed in configuration space at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) by fitting the experimental singlet $np$ scattering length and deuteron binding energy at LO, and $np$ and $pp$ scattering data in the laboratory-energy range 0--15 MeV at NLO and 0--25 MeV at N3LO. These interactions are regularized by including two Gaussian cutoffs, one for $T\,$=$\,0$ and the other for $T\,$=$\,1$ channels. The cutoffs are taken to vary in the ranges $R_0\,$=$(1.5$--2.3) fm and $R_1\,$=$(1.5$--3.0) fm. The 780 (1,100) data points up to 15 (25) MeV energy, primarily differential cross sections, are fitted by the NLO (N3LO) models with a $χ^2$/datum about 1.7 or less (well below 1.5), when harder cutoff values are adopted. As a first application, we report results for the binding energies of nuclei with mass numbers $A\,$=$\,3$--6 and 16 obtained with selected LO and NLO $2N$ models both by themselves as well as in combination with a LO three-nucleon ($3N$) contact interaction. The latter is characterized by a single low-energy constant that is fixed to reproduce the experimental $^3$H binding energy. The inclusion of the $3N$ interaction largely removes the sensitivity to cutoff variations in the few-nucleon systems and leads to predictions for the $^3$He and $^4$He binding energies that cluster around 7.8 MeV and 30 MeV, respectively. However, in $^{16}$O this cutoff sensitivity remains rather strong. Finally, predictions at LO only are also reported for medium-mass nuclei with $A\,$=$\,40$, 48, and 90.

## Key claim

The paper constructs coordinate-space two-nucleon contact interactions (pionless EFT) at LO, NLO, and N3LO, fitted to low-energy scattering data, and finds that while adding a LO three-nucleon contact interaction removes cutoff sensitivity in few-nucleon systems (A=3–6), the cutoff sensitivity remains rather strong in the medium-mass nucleus 16O, indicating that the simple contact-only framework may be insufficient for heavier systems without higher-order or pionful contributions.

## Method

The authors developed two-nucleon contact interactions in configuration space at LO, NLO, and N3LO by fitting to experimental np and pp scattering data from the Granada database (0–15 MeV at NLO, 0–25 MeV at N3LO) and to the deuteron binding energy, using two independent Gaussian cutoffs (R0 for T=0 channels, R1 for T=1 channels) with values ranging from 1.5–2.3 fm and 1.5–3.0 fm respectively. They then combined selected LO and NLO two-nucleon models with a LO three-nucleon contact interaction (single low-energy constant cE fixed to reproduce the 3H binding energy) and computed ground-state energies for nuclei with A=3–6 and 16 using hyperspherical-harmonics methods, and for A=40, 48, and 90 at LO only using auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo.

## Key numbers

- The NLO models fit 780 data points up to 15 MeV with χ²/datum about 1.7 or less (model o: 1.17); the N3LO models fit about 1,100 data points up to 25 MeV with χ²/datum well below 1.5 (model o: 1.19).
- Cutoff ranges: R0 = 1.5–2.3 fm, R1 = 1.5–3.0 fm, corresponding to momentum-space cutoffs Λ0 = 172–263 MeV and Λ1 = 132–263 MeV.
- With the 3N interaction included, predicted 3He binding energies cluster around 7.8 MeV and 4He binding energies around 30 MeV.
- The LO model o (optimized cutoffs R0=1.5459 fm, R1=1.8304 fm) achieved χ²/datum of 2.16 for np data in 0–1 MeV, compared to 5.54–41.2 for models a–d.
- The deuteron binding energy was fitted exactly at 2.2246 MeV; the asymptotic D/S ratio η ranged from 0.0169 to 0.0373 across models at NLO/N3LO (experimental value: 0.0256(4)).
- The singlet np scattering length was reproduced exactly at LO for models a–d (−23.740 fm); at NLO and N3LO all models gave values within about 0.05 fm of the experimental −23.740(20) fm.

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT (slashed-piEFT) approach of Chen (1999) and Bedaque (2002) by constructing coordinate-space contact interactions with two independent cutoffs, addressing fine-tuning issues noted in Weinberg counting. It contrasts with chiral EFT interactions (e.g., Entem 2003, Epelbaum 2004, Piarulli 2014) that include explicit pion exchange, and with the single-cutoff approach that the authors found inadequate (producing poor deuteron binding energies and large χ²). The results enable systematic testing of whether pionless contact interactions can describe nuclear structure beyond the few-body sector, with the strong cutoff sensitivity in 16O motivating future inclusion of higher-order three-nucleon forces or explicit pion degrees of freedom.
