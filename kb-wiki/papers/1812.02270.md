# On generation of the Bargmann-Moshinsky basis of SU(3) group

> An efficient procedure of orthonormalisation of the Bargmann-Moshinsky (BM) basis is examined using analytical formulas of the overlap integrals of the BM basis. Calculations of components of the quadrupole operator between the both BM and the orthonormalised bases needed for construction of the nuclear models are tested. The proposed procedure is also implemented as the Fortran program.

## Key claim

The paper presents an efficient procedure for orthonormalising the Bargmann-Moshinsky (BM) basis of SU(3) using analytical formulas for overlap integrals, but the orthonormalisation is achieved through an adapted Gram-Schmidt procedure that avoids square root operations, enabling large-scale symbolic calculations; the method is tested for SU(3) irreducible representations with quantum numbers up to lambda=10 and mu=10.

## Method

The authors construct BM basis states as polynomials of vector-boson creation operators acting on a vacuum state, then expand these states in an orthonormal six-dimensional boson basis to compute overlap integrals analytically. They implement an adapted Gram-Schmidt orthonormalisation procedure that uses determinants of Gramian matrices to recursively construct orthogonal vectors without square root operations, followed by normalisation. The action of the zero component of the quadrupole operator Q0 is calculated on both the non-orthogonal BM basis and the orthonormalised basis using the Wigner-Eckart theorem and inverse transformation matrices.

## Key numbers

- Overlap integrals for lambda=0,...,10 and mu=1,...,10 (with lambda >= mu) were calculated and coincide up to 10 digits with results from a symbolic algorithm in Wolfram Mathematica
- The orthonormalisation coefficients for lambda,mu=0,...,10 (lambda >= mu) coincide up to 10 digits with results from the symbolic algorithm
- The dimension of an SU(3) irreducible representation is given by D_lambda_mu = (1/2)(lambda+1)(mu+1)(lambda+mu+2)
- The Casimir operator eigenvalue is C2(SU(3)) = 4(lambda^2 + mu^2 + lambda*mu + 3*lambda + 3*mu)

## Context

This work extends the analytical construction of the Bargmann-Moshinsky basis originally developed by Bargmann and Moshinsky (1961) and Moshinsky et al. (1975), and the overlap integral formulas from Ališauskas and Raychev (1981). The orthonormalisation procedure enables construction of nuclear model Hamiltonians containing quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, rotational terms, and higher-order interactions that can simulate tetrahedral or octahedral nuclear symmetries as considered by Dudek (2002). The Fortran implementation allows calculations for larger irreducible representations (lambda,mu > 5) than previously feasible with symbolic algebra systems alone.
