# Studying short-range correlations and momentum distributions with unitarily transformed operators

> Short-range correlations in 4He are investigated using many-body wave functions obtained in the no-core shell model. The similarity renormalization group (SRG) is used to evolve the Argonne V8' interaction and the density operators. The effects of short-range correlations are reflected in the two-body densities in coordinate space as a function of the distance between two nucleons, or alternatively in in momentum space as function of the relative momentum between two nucleons. The SRG transformation is performed in two-body approximation. The importance of missing three-body and higher-body contributions is investigated by comparing results obtained for different flow parameters and by comparing to exact results with the bare Argonne V8' interaction obtained in the correlated Gaussian approach.

# Studying short-range correlations and momentum distributions with unitarily transformed operators

## Key claim

Short-range correlations in 4He can be recovered from SRG-softened wave functions by applying the same unitary transformation to the density operators as to the Hamiltonian, but only in the two-body approximation; the accuracy of this recovery depends on the SRG flow parameter and is limited by missing three-body and higher-body contributions, particularly in odd spin-isospin channels and intermediate momentum regions.

## Method

The authors solve the SRG flow equation in two-body space for the Argonne V8' interaction to obtain a transformed Hamiltonian and transformation matrix, using relative momenta up to kmax = 15 fm^-1. No-core shell model (NCSM) calculations with Nmax = 16 are performed using the SRG-evolved Hamiltonian to obtain many-body wave functions for 4He. Two-body densities in coordinate and momentum space are then computed both with bare density operators and with SRG-transformed density operators (in two-body approximation), and compared to exact results from the correlated Gaussian method using the bare interaction.

## Key numbers

- Relative momentum cutoff for SRG evolution: kmax = 15 fm^-1
- NCSM model space size: Nmax = 16
- SRG flow parameters studied: α = 0 (bare), α = 0.01 fm^4, α = 0.04 fm^4, and α = 0.20 fm^4
- Fermi momentum in 4He: approximately 1.5 fm^-1
- High-momentum region where SRG-transformed operators reproduce bare results: relative momentum greater than about 3 fm^-1
- Intermediate momentum region with strong α-dependence in S=0,T=1 channel: between 1.5 and 3 fm^-1
- Number of pairs in S=1,T=0 channel is larger by about a factor of ten compared to S=0,T=1 channel in the intermediate momentum region

## Context

This work extends the SRG approach of Bogner (2003, 2010) and the UCOM method of Neff and Feldmeier (2003, 2010) by explicitly transforming density operators alongside the Hamiltonian, following the two-body system study of Anderson (2010). It enables systematic study of short-range correlations in any nucleus accessible to NCSM calculations, including analysis of two-body densities as functions of pair position or pair momentum. The results confirm the dominance of tensor correlations in the S=1,T=0 channel observed in JLAB knockout experiments by Subedi (2008), and show that three-body correlations in the transformed Hamiltonian are needed to fully reproduce the bare interaction results in odd channels and intermediate momentum regions.
