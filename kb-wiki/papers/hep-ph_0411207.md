# Phenomenological approach to the critical dynamics of the QCD phase transition revisited

> The phenomenological dynamics of the QCD critical phenomena is revisited. Recently, Son and Stephanov claimed that the dynamical universality class of the QCD phase transition belongs to model H. In their discussion, they employed a time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau equation for the net baryon number density, which is a conserved quantity. We derive the Langevin equation for the net baryon number density, i.e., the Cahn-Hilliard equation. Furthermore, they discussed the mode coupling induced through the {\it irreversible} current. Here, we show the {\it reversible} coupling can play a dominant role for describing the QCD critical dynamics and that the dynamical universality class does not necessarily belong to model H.

## Key claim

The dynamical universality class of the QCD phase transition does not necessarily belong to model H as claimed by Son and Stephanov, because the reversible coupling between the chiral condensate and the chiral current can play a dominant role over the irreversible coupling considered in their analysis, and the reduction of the chiral condensate and net baryon number density into a single gross variable is not clear when critical slowing down is taken into account.

## Method

The author derives Langevin equations for the chiral condensate (a non-conserved quantity) using the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau (TDGL) equation and for the net baryon number density (a conserved quantity) using the Cahn-Hilliard (CH) equation, with thermodynamic forces defined by derivatives of a Ginzburg-Landau free energy. The irreversible coupling is analyzed via eigenmode decomposition of the coupled linearized equations in the limit of vanishing momentum, and the reversible coupling is derived using the projection operator method to identify operators that yield non-vanishing Poisson brackets with the chiral condensate. The effect of the reversible coupling on the relaxation of the chiral condensate is then estimated using mode coupling theory with a decoupling approximation and Markov approximation.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time of the chiral condensate at vanishing momentum, when coupling to the net baryon number density is ignored, is given by \((\gamma_{ss}A)^{-1}\), where \(\gamma_{ss}\) is the Onsager coefficient and \(A\) is a parameter in the free energy potential.
- The two eigenfrequencies of the coupled system, up to second order in momentum \(q\), are \(\omega_1 = -i \frac{\gamma_{nn} (AC-B^2)}{A} q^2\) and \(\omega_2 = -i \gamma_{ss} A + i (\gamma_{nn} C - \gamma_{ss} a) q^2 + i \frac{\gamma_{nn} (AB-C^2)}{A} q^2\), where \(A,B,C,a,b,c\) are parameters of the Ginzburg-Landau free energy and \(\gamma_{nn}\) is the Onsager coefficient for the net baryon number density.
- The renormalized damping rate of the chiral condensate including the reversible coupling is \(L^R = i\omega_2 + \frac{R}{V} \langle i\mathbf{q}\cdot\mathbf{v}_\sigma(\mathbf{0},t) \rangle + \frac{R^2 q^2}{3V^2} \sum_{\mathbf{k}} \frac{\langle \mathbf{v}_\sigma(\mathbf{k})\cdot\mathbf{v}^*_\sigma(\mathbf{k}) \rangle}{-\nu \mathbf{k}^2 - i\omega_2}\), where \(R\) is the coefficient of the reversible coupling, \(\mathbf{v}_\sigma\) is the velocity field associated with the chiral current, and \(\nu\) is the diffusion constant of the velocity field.

## Context

This paper revisits the phenomenological dynamics of QCD critical phenomena previously discussed by Son and Stephanov (2004), who claimed the dynamical universality class belongs to model H in the classification of Hohenberg and Halperin (1977). The author argues that Son and Stephanov only considered irreversible (dissipative) mode couplings, whereas in standard critical dynamics of systems like the liquid-gas phase transition and superfluid helium, the dominant mode couplings arise from reversible currents. The analysis suggests the dynamical universality class may instead be similar to model G (the isotropic antiferromagnet), though with a four-component order parameter rather than three, and opens the question of whether additional gross variables (chiral current, net baryon number current, energy density, momentum density) must be included, requiring microscopic calculations as in the work of Koide and collaborators (2003, 2004).
