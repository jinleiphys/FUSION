# Electromagnetic form factors of neutron and neutral stange hyperons in the oscillating view of point

> Based on the recently precise measurements of the electron-positron annihilation reactions into a neutron and anti-neutron pair by BESIII collaboration, the effective form factor of neutron was determined in the time like region, and it was found that the effective form factor of neutron is smaller than the ones of proton. The effective form factors of neutron shows a periodic behaviour, similar to the case of proton. Here, a compared analysis for $Λ$, $Σ^0$ and $Ξ^0$ is performed. Fits of the available data on the effective form factors of $Λ$, $Σ^0$ and $Ξ^0$ with charge zero, allow to show a universal phenomenon of the oscillation behavior in their effective form factors. However, this needs to be confirmed by future precise experiments. Both theoretical and experimental investigations of this phenomenon can shed light on the reaction mechanisms of the electron-positron annihilation process.

## Key claim

The effective form factors of the neutral strange hyperons Λ, Σ⁰, and Ξ⁰, when extracted from existing data on electron-positron annihilation, show an oscillatory behavior after subtraction of a dipole function that is qualitatively similar to the oscillation previously observed for the neutron and proton; however, the authors state that this universal phenomenon "needs to be confirmed by future precise experiments" because the current hyperon data have large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed χ² fits of a dipole function G_D(s) = c₀/(1-γs)² to the experimental effective form factor data for the neutron, Λ, Σ⁰, and Ξ⁰ from BaBar and BESIII collaborations. After subtracting this dipole part, they fitted the residual oscillation with a cosine function G_osc(s) = [c₀/(1-γs)²] A cos(C√s + D) to extract the oscillation parameters A, C, and D for the neutron, Λ, and Σ⁰. For the Ξ⁰, the data errors were too large to obtain a reliable oscillation fit.

## Key numbers

- For the neutron dipole fit, γ was fixed to 1.41 GeV⁻² (same as for the proton) and c₀ = 3.48 ± 0.06, with χ²/dof = 4.3.
- For the Λ dipole fit: γ = 0.34 ± 0.08 GeV⁻², c₀ = 0.11 ± 0.01, χ²/dof = 2.4.
- For the Σ⁰ dipole fit: γ = 0.26 ± 0.01 GeV⁻², c₀ = 0.033 ± 0.007, χ²/dof = 1.1.
- For the Ξ⁰ dipole fit: γ = 0.21 ± 0.02 GeV⁻², c₀ = 0.023 ± 0.008, χ²/dof = 3.0.
- For the neutron oscillation fit: A = 0.176 ± 0.027, C = 12.16 ± 0.61 GeV⁻¹, D = 1.66 ± 1.41.
- For the Λ oscillation fit: A = 0.235 ± 0.033, C = 3.99 ± 0.14 GeV⁻¹, D = 4.61 ± 0.40.
- For the Σ⁰ oscillation fit: A = 0.199 ± 0.102, C = 11.94 ± 1.13 GeV⁻¹, D = 0.99 ± 3.13.
- For Λ, the dipole form factor G_D(s) drops from 0.172 at √s = 2.3 GeV to 0.015 at √s = 3.3 GeV; for the neutron, it drops from 0.162 at √s = 2.0 GeV to 0.025 at √s = 3.0 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the observation of an oscillatory behavior in the effective form factor of the neutron, recently reported by BESIII (2021) from precise e⁺e⁻ → n n̅ measurements, to the neutral strange hyperons Λ, Σ⁰, and Ξ⁰. The analysis uses data from BaBar (2007) and BESIII (2017, 2019, 2021) and follows the phenomenological approach of Bianconi (2015) and Tomasi-Gustafsson (2020), who modeled the nucleon form factor as a dipole plus a cosine oscillation. The results suggest a universal oscillation pattern across neutral baryons, which future precise measurements at BESIII could confirm and which may provide insight into the reaction mechanism of baryon-antibaryon production in electron-positron annihilation.
