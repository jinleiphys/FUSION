# The Isgur-Karl model revisited

> We show how to match the Isgur-Karl model to the spin-flavor quark operator expansion used in the 1/Nc studies of the non-strange negative parity L=1 excited baryons. Using the transformation properties of states and interactions under the permutation group S_3 we are able to express the operator coefficients as overlap integrals, without making any assumption on the spatial dependence of the quark wave functions. The general mass operator leads to parameter free mass relations and constraints on the mixing angles that are valid beyond the usual harmonic oscillator approximation. The Isgur-Karl model with harmonic oscillator wave functions provides a simple counterexample that demonstrates explicitly that the alternative operator basis for the 1/Nc expansion for excited baryons recently proposed by Matagne and Stancu is incomplete.

## Key claim

The authors show that the Isgur-Karl model for non-strange negative parity L=1 excited baryons can be matched to the spin-flavor quark operator expansion used in 1/N_c studies, and that this matching reveals the operator basis proposed by Matagne and Stancu (2006) is incomplete, using the harmonic oscillator version of the model as a counterexample.

## Method

The authors express the mass operator of the Isgur-Karl model in terms of spin-flavor operators using transformation properties under the permutation group S_3, without assuming any specific spatial dependence of the quark wave functions. They compute reduced matrix elements of the orbital operators (contact spin-spin and tensor interactions) as overlap integrals, and evaluate the resulting mass matrices for the J=1/2, 3/2, and 5/2 nucleon and Delta states. For the harmonic oscillator case, they compute the reduced matrix elements explicitly using the rho-lambda basis.

## Key numbers

- The spin-weighted average masses are: <Delta> = 1683.3 +/- 28.5 MeV, <N_{1/2}> = 1596.5 +/- 8.2 MeV, <N_{3/2}> = 1610.0 +/- 25.1 MeV
- The relation <Delta> - N_{5/2} = (2/9)(<N_{3/2}> - <N_{1/2}>) gives experimental values 8.3 +/- 28.9 MeV = 3.0 +/- 5.9 MeV, well satisfied within errors
- Best fit coefficients for the general IK-V(r) model: c_0 = 1368 +/- 11 MeV, a = 155 +/- 8 MeV, b = -4^{+9}_{-10} MeV, c = -8^{+11}_{-12} MeV, with chi-squared per degree of freedom = 1.7
- In the harmonic oscillator model, the mixing angles are theta_{N1} = 31.7 degrees and theta_{N3} = 173.6 degrees
- The Delta_{1/2} and Delta_{3/2} masses are predicted equal; experimentally they are split by 80 +/- 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the method of Pirjol (2007) for matching quark models to the 1/N_c expansion, and provides a concrete counterexample to the alternative operator basis proposed by Matagne and Stancu (2006) for excited baryons. The results enable parameter-free mass relations and mixing angle constraints valid beyond the harmonic oscillator approximation, and connect the Isgur-Karl model to the systematic 1/N_c expansion framework of Carlson (1998) and Goity (1996). The analysis of mixing angles from strong decays by Hey (1974) and Goity (2004) provides experimental context, while the one-gluon exchange model results of Pirjol (2008) offer a comparison for the spin-orbit effects neglected here.
