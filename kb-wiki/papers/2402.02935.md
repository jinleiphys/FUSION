# Nuclear mass table in deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum, II: Even-$Z$ nuclei

> The mass table in the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 density functional has been established for even-$Z$ nuclei with $8\le Z\le120$, extended from the previous work for even-even nuclei [Zhang $\it{et.~al.}$ (DRHBc Mass Table Collaboration), At. Data Nucl. Data Tables 144, 101488 (2022)]. The calculated binding energies, two-nucleon and one-neutron separation energies, root-mean-square (rms) radii of neutron, proton, matter, and charge distributions, quadrupole deformations, and neutron and proton Fermi surfaces are tabulated and compared with available experimental data. A total of 4829 even-$Z$ nuclei are predicted to be bound, with an rms deviation of 1.477 MeV from the 1244 mass data. Good agreement with the available experimental odd-even mass differences, $α$ decay energies, and charge radii is also achieved. The description accuracy for nuclear masses and nucleon separation energies as well as the prediction for drip lines is compared with the results obtained from other relativistic and nonrelativistic density functional. The comparison shows that the DRHBc theory with PC-PK1 provides an excellent microscopic description for the masses of even-$Z$ nuclei. The systematics of the nucleon separation energies, odd-even mass differences, pairing energies, two-nucleon gaps, $α$ decay energies, rms radii, quadrupole deformations, potential energy curves, neutron density distributions, and neutron mean-field potentials are discussed.

## Key claim

The DRHBc theory with PC-PK1 predicts 4829 bound even-Z nuclei from oxygen (Z=8) to Z=120 with an rms deviation of 1.433 MeV from 1244 experimental mass data, though this agreement is achieved only after including rotational correction energies and using the equal filling approximation for odd-N nuclei.

## Method

The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) was solved in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with the PC-PK1 point-coupling density functional, using a density-dependent zero-range pairing force with strength V0 = -325 MeV fm^3 and saturation density ρ_sat = 0.152 fm^-3. For odd-N nuclei, the blocking effect of the unpaired nucleon was treated with the equal filling approximation, and an automatic blocking procedure was implemented to determine the ground state. The Legendre expansion for axially deformed potentials was truncated at λ_max = 6, 8, or 10 depending on the proton number range, and the angular momentum cutoff for the basis was J_max = 23/2 ħ.

## Key numbers

- 4829 even-Z nuclei predicted to be bound, comprising 2584 even-even and 2245 even-odd nuclei
- RMS deviation of 1.433 MeV from 1244 experimental mass data (634 even-even and 610 even-odd nuclei)
- RMS deviation for even-even nuclei alone was 1.518 MeV (from previous work by Zhang et al., 2022)
- Pairing strength V0 = -325 MeV fm^3 and saturation density ρ_sat = 0.152 fm^-3 with a 100 MeV pairing window
- Energy cutoff for the Dirac Woods-Saxon basis in the Fermi sea: E^+_cut = 300 MeV
- 278 weakly bound even-Z nuclei with S_2n ≤ 1 MeV, of which 253 are even-even and 25 are even-odd
- 34 even-even nuclei at Z=108,110,112 form a peninsula of stability, with 17 additional even-odd nuclei predicted

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc mass table from even-even nuclei (Zhang et al., 2022, Atomic Data and Nuclear Data Tables 144, 101488) to all even-Z nuclei including odd-N species, using the odd-nucleon formalism developed by Pan et al. (2022, Physical Review C). The results are compared with other relativistic and nonrelativistic density functionals including RCHB with PC-PK1 (Xia et al., 2018), RHB with PC-PK1 and NL3* (Yang et al., 2021; Agbemava et al., 2014), RMF+BCS with TMA (Geng et al., 2005), HFB with UNEDF1 (Erler et al., 2012), FRDM(2012) (Moller et al., 2016), and WS4 (Wang et al., 2014). The tabulated binding energies, separation energies, radii, deformations, and Fermi surfaces enable systematic studies of drip lines, halo phenomena, and superheavy element stability.
