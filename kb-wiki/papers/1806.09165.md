# {\it Ab initio} perturbation calculations of realistic effective interactions in the Hartree--Fock basis

> We perform two types of {\it ab initio} perturbation calculations of effective interactions in the Hartree--Fock (HF) basis instead of the harmonic-oscillator basis: one is called the Brillouin--Wigner (BW) perturbation and another is called the Rayleigh--Schrödinger (RS) perturbation. It is shown that the HF basis provides good convergences. We also benchmark the perturbation calculations with the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) which is a nonperturbative method. In the HF basis some-type perturbation diagrams can be cancelled out, while the cancellation does not happen in the harmonic-oscillator basis. We have investigated the {\it sd} shell using the chiral N$^3$LO potential softened by $V_{\text{low-}k}$. With the low-momentum N$^3$LO potential, we first perform the spherical HF calculations for the $^{16}$O core of the {\it sd} shell, and the realistic effective two-body interactions for the HF {\it sd}-shell space can be derived by the perturbation calculations. The calculations give simultaneously effective single-particle energies and excitation spectra of two valence particle systems (i.e., $^{18}$O, $^{18}$F, and $^{18}$Ne in the {\it sd} shell). Convergences have been analyzed order by order. The perturbation calculations are in fairly good agreement with nonperturbative method IM-SRG. We find that the HF RS perturbation gives even better results compared with the BW perturbation. The HF realistic effective interactions derived by the perturbations can be used for further shell-model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that *ab initio* perturbation calculations of effective interactions in the Hartree-Fock (HF) basis converge well and agree fairly well with the nonperturbative in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) method, but the agreement is conditional: the Rayleigh-Schrodinger (RS) perturbation gives even better results than the Brillouin-Wigner (BW) perturbation, and the HF basis provides cancellation of some perturbation diagrams that does not occur in the harmonic-oscillator basis.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock calculations for the 16O core in a harmonic-oscillator basis, then derived realistic effective two-body interactions for the sd-shell valence space using two types of perturbation theory: Brillouin-Wigner (BW) perturbation, where the Q-box was calculated up to third order and the energy-dependent secular equation solved by iteration, and Rayleigh-Schrodinger (RS) perturbation, calculated up to second order without folded diagrams. The chiral N3LO potential softened by V-low-k with a cutoff of 2.5 fm^-1 was used, with the harmonic-oscillator basis parameter hbar-omega = 22 MeV and basis truncation N_max = 10. The perturbation results were benchmarked against valence-space IM-SRG calculations using the same input Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* Effective single-particle energies for neutron 0d5/2: HF = 1.89 MeV, RS-2nd = -4.73 MeV, BW-3rd = -4.55 MeV, IM-SRG = -4.89 MeV, experimental = -4.14 MeV
* Effective single-particle energies for neutron 1s1/2: HF = 1.75 MeV, RS-2nd = -3.30 MeV, BW-3rd = -2.95 MeV, IM-SRG = -3.23 MeV, experimental = -3.27 MeV
* Effective single-particle energies for neutron 0d3/2: HF = 7.38 MeV, RS-2nd = 1.83 MeV, BW-3rd = 2.53 MeV, IM-SRG = 2.53 MeV, experimental = 0.94 MeV
* Effective single-particle energies for proton 0d5/2: HF = 5.45 MeV, RS-2nd = -0.80 MeV, BW-3rd = -0.57 MeV, IM-SRG = -0.93 MeV, experimental = -0.60 MeV
* Effective single-particle energies for proton 1s1/2: HF = 4.96 MeV, RS-2nd = 0.31 MeV, BW-3rd = 0.67 MeV, IM-SRG = 0.40 MeV, experimental = -0.10 MeV
* Effective single-particle energies for proton 0d3/2: HF = 10.48 MeV, RS-2nd = 5.39 MeV, BW-3rd = 6.06 MeV, IM-SRG = 6.04 MeV, experimental = 4.40 MeV
* BW-3rd calculated single-particle energies are within 0.3 MeV of IM-SRG calculations
* Discrepancies between BW-3rd and IM-SRG in spectra for 18O and 18Ne are within 0.6 MeV; for 18F the discrepancy can reach 1.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative calculations of effective shell-model interactions using many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) in the harmonic-oscillator basis, as done by Brandow (1967), Kuo (1974), and Hjorth-Jensen (1995), by instead using the self-consistent Hartree-Fock basis which provides improved convergence and diagram cancellation. It builds on prior HF-basis MBPT calculations for the p-shell by Coraggio (2005) and for 18O by Tsukiyama (2012), and benchmarks against the nonperturbative IM-SRG method of Hergert (2016). The derived effective interactions and single-particle energies can be used as input for shell-model calculations of other sd-shell nuclei.
