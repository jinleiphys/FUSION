# Unveiling the sea: universality of the transverse momentum dependent quark distributions at small $x$

> Within the Colour Glass Condensate effective theory, we demonstrate that back-to-back dijet correlations in dilute-dense collisions involving a small-$x$ quark from the nuclear target can be factorised in terms of universal transverse momentum dependent distributions (TMDs) for the sea quarks. Two building blocks are needed to construct all these TMDs at the operator level: the sea quark TMD operator which appears in semi-inclusive Deep-Inelastic Scattering (SIDIS) or in the Drell-Yan process and the elastic $S$-matrix for a quark-antiquark dipole. Compared to SIDIS, the saturation effects are stronger for dijet production in forward proton-nucleus collisions, due to additional scattering in the initial and final state, effectively resulting in a larger value for the nuclear saturation momentum.

## Key claim
Within the Colour Glass Condensate effective theory, back-to-back dijet correlations in dilute-dense collisions involving a small-x quark from the nuclear target can be factorised in terms of universal transverse momentum dependent distributions for the sea quarks, but the saturation effects are stronger for dijet production in forward proton-nucleus collisions than in semi-inclusive Deep-Inelastic Scattering due to additional initial-state and final-state scattering, effectively resulting in a larger value for the nuclear saturation momentum.

## Method
The authors computed dijet cross-sections to leading order in the strong coupling and to leading power in the small ratios of transverse momentum imbalance to hard scale, using the Colour Glass Condensate effective theory with the projectile described by light-cone wavefunctions and the target as a classical colour field. They isolated the leading-power contributions in the back-to-back limit by integrating over the kinematics of the unmeasured soft antiquark or quark, and demonstrated the correspondence between CGC diagrams and target-picture diagrams for quark-gluon dijet production in electron-nucleus deep inelastic scattering and photon-gluon jet production in proton-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers
- The sea quark transverse momentum dependent distribution is proportional to the gluon distribution with a proportionality constant of order alpha_s, the QCD coupling.
- The hard factor for quark-gluon dijet production in DIS behaves like 1/P_perp^4 when P_perp^2 is greater than or comparable to Q^2, showing the result counts to leading-twist order.
- The longitudinal momentum fraction of the unmeasured antiquark scales as z ~ K_perp^2 / P_perp^2, where K_perp is the dijet momentum imbalance and P_perp is the relative transverse momentum.
- The transverse size of the hard dijet pair is r ~ 1/P_perp, much smaller than 1/Q_s, while the effective quark-antiquark dipole size is R ~ 1/K_perp, much larger than r.

## Context
This work extends pioneering studies of sea quark TMD factorisation for selected processes in electron-nucleus DIS at small x, including SIDIS by Marquet (2009) and Xiao (2017), lepton-jet correlations by Tong (2022), Drell-Yan by Kang (2012) and Gelis (2006), and diffractive production by Hatta (2022) and Hauksson (2024). It fills the gap of a systematic understanding of quark TMD factorisation at small x analogous to that already existing for gluons, and opens a new window for saturation studies at the Electron-Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider through the quark channel.
