# Nuclear limits on gravitational waves from elliptically deformed pulsars

> Gravitational radiation is a fundamental prediction of General Relativity. Elliptically deformed pulsars are among the possible sources emitting gravitational waves (GWs) with a strain-amplitude dependent upon the star's quadrupole moment, rotational frequency, and distance from the detector. We show that the gravitational wave strain amplitude $h_0$ depends strongly on the equation of state of neutron-rich stellar matter. Applying an equation of state with symmetry energy constrained by recent nuclear laboratory data, we set an upper limit on the strain-amplitude of GWs produced by elliptically deformed pulsars. Depending on details of the EOS, for several millisecond pulsars at distances $0.18kpc$ to $0.35kpc$ from Earth, the {\it maximal} $h_0$ is found to be in the range of $\sim[0.4-1.5]\times 10^{-24}$. This prediction serves as the first {\it direct} nuclear constraint on the gravitational radiation. Its implications are discussed.

## Key claim

The gravitational wave strain amplitude $h_0$ from elliptically deformed pulsars depends strongly on the equation of state of neutron-rich stellar matter; applying an equation of state with symmetry energy constrained by recent nuclear laboratory data sets an upper limit on $h_0$, but this limit assumes a breaking strain of the neutron star crust of $\sigma=10^{-2}$ which "might be too optimistic" and the results should be regarded as upper limits because the true breaking strain could be as low as $10^{-5}$.

## Method

The authors calculate the gravitational wave strain amplitude $h_0$ for several millisecond pulsars using the quadrupole approximation, where $h_0$ depends on the quadrupole moment $\Phi_{22}$, rotational frequency $\nu$, and distance to Earth $r$. They compute the quadrupole moment using an empirical relation for slowly rotating neutron stars and apply several nucleonic equations of state (EOS), focusing on the MDI interaction whose symmetry energy is constrained by isospin-diffusion laboratory data and measurements of the $^{208}$Pb skin thickness, considering the limiting cases $x=0$ and $x=-1$ as lower and upper bounds.

## Key numbers

- For several millisecond pulsars at distances $0.18$ kpc to $0.35$ kpc from Earth, the maximal $h_0$ is in the range $\sim[0.4-1.5]\times 10^{-24}$.
- The maximum ellipticity found is $\epsilon_{max}\approx 2.4\times 10^{-6}$, corresponding to the largest crust "mountain" expected on a neutron star assuming breaking strain $\sigma=10^{-2}$.
- The quadrupole moment relation used is $\Phi_{22,max}=2.4\times 10^{38}\text{ g cm}^2 (\sigma/10^{-2})(R/10\text{ km})^{6.26}(1.4M_\odot/M)^{1.2}$.
- The breaking strain of the neutron star crust lies in the range $\sigma=[10^{-5}-10^{-2}]$.
- The three pulsars studied have rotational frequencies: PSR J2124-3358 at 202.79 Hz, PSR J1024-0719 at 193.72 Hz, and PSR J0437-4715 at 173.91 Hz.
- PSR J0437-4715 has mass $1.3\pm 0.2 M_\odot$ and distance $0.18$ kpc; PSR J2124-3358 is at $0.25$ kpc; PSR J1024-0719 is at $0.35$ kpc.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of gravitational wave strain from rotating neutron stars by incorporating nuclear equation of state constraints from terrestrial laboratory data, specifically the MDI interaction with symmetry energy constrained by isospin-diffusion experiments (Li and Chen, 2005; Li and Steiner, 2005) and the flow-constrained symmetric matter EOS from Danielewicz, Lacey and Lynch (2002). The results provide the first direct nuclear constraint on gravitational radiation from elliptically deformed pulsars, and the predicted strain amplitudes are compared with the current detection limits of LIGO (Abbott, 2004). The work enables future studies of rapidly rotating neutron stars using exact numerical solutions of the Einstein field equations, as previously done by Laarakkers and Poisson (1999).
