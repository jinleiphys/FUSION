# $J/ψ$ suppression in the threshold model and QGP formation time

> In the QGP motivated threshold model, in addition to the normal nuclear absorption, $J/ψ$'s are subjected to an additional "anomalous" suppression. We have analysed the recently published PHENIX data on the participant number dependence of the nuclear modification factor for $J/ψ$'s in Au+Au collisions and extracted the anomalous suppression required to explain the data. At mid rapidity $J/ψ$'s are anomalously suppressed only above a threshold density $n_c$=3.73 fm$^{-2}$. The forward rapidity data on the otherhand require that $J/ψ$'s are continuously "anomalously" suppressed. The analysis strongly indicate that in mid rapidity $J/ψ$'s are suppressed in a deconfined medium. Using the PHENIX data on the participant number dependence of the Bjorken energy density, we have also estimated the QGP formation time. For critical temperature $T_c$=192 MeV, estimated QGP formation time ranges between 0.06-0.08 fm/c.

## Key claim

The threshold model can explain the PHENIX mid-rapidity data with a step-like anomalous suppression above a threshold density, but the forward-rapidity data require continuous anomalous suppression that defeats the model's core assumption of sudden melting in a deconfined medium; the QGP formation time is estimated to be 0.06-0.08 fm/c only if one assumes a critical temperature of 192 MeV.

## Method

The author fitted the PHENIX data on the centrality (participant number) dependence of the nuclear modification factor for J/psi in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV using the Blaizot threshold model, which adds an anomalous suppression factor (a step function or smeared step function of local transverse density) on top of conventional nuclear absorption described by a Glauber model with a Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile. The J/psi-nucleon absorption cross-section was first extracted from peripheral collisions (up to Npart=150), then the threshold density and its smearing parameter were fitted separately for mid-rapidity (|y|<0.35) and forward-rapidity (1.2<|y|<2.2) data. The extracted threshold density was then converted to a threshold energy density using PHENIX data on the Bjorken energy density times formation time, and the QGP formation time was inferred by requiring the critical temperature to match lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- J/psi-nucleon absorption cross-section extracted from peripheral Au+Au collisions: sigma_abs = 4.39 +/- 0.74 mb
- Mid-rapidity threshold density: n_c = 3.73 +/- 0.29 fm^{-2}, with smearing parameter lambda = 8.96 +/- 9.72 fm^2
- Forward-rapidity threshold density: n_c = 2.96 +/- 0.42 fm^{-2}, with smearing parameter lambda = 0.99 +/- 0.94 fm^2
- The threshold density n_c = 3.73 fm^{-2} corresponds to Au+Au collisions with participant number N_part = 199.6 (+68.5/-56.2)
- For N_part = 199.6, the Bjorken energy density times formation time is tau * epsilon_TH = 3.98 (+1.02/-1.48) GeV fm^{-2}
- For a critical temperature T_c = 192 MeV, the estimated QGP formation time ranges between 0.06-0.08 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the threshold model of Blaizot et al. (2000), originally developed to explain NA50 data on anomalous J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, to RHIC-energy Au+Au collisions. It contradicts the expectation from Thews et al. (2000) and Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (2000) that J/psi production would be enhanced at RHIC due to c-cbar recombination, as PHENIX data show suppression instead. The analysis enables extraction of the QGP formation time from the threshold density, finding it to be considerably smaller than the thermal equilibration time of about 0.6 fm/c estimated by Kolb and Heinz (2003).
