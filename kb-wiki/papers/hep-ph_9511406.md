# Some Aspects of Nuclear Deep Inelastic Scattering

> Nuclear deep inelastic scattering is considered in the framework of a model in which the current operator explicitly satisfies Poincare invariance and current conservation. The results considerably differ from the standard ones at small values of the Bjorken variable $x$. In particular, it is impossible to extract the neutron structure functions from the deuteron data at x<0.01 and we predict that the behavior of the deuteron structure functions at low x and large momentum transfer considerably differs from the behavior of the nucleon structure functions at such conditions. We also argue that for heavier nuclei the effect of the final state interaction is important even in the Bjorken limit.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a model where the current operator satisfies Poincare invariance and current conservation, the standard impulse approximation for nuclear deep inelastic scattering is inconsistent except in the point form of dynamics, and that at Bjorken x below about 0.01 the neutron structure functions cannot be extracted from deuteron data.

## Method

The author constructs a relativistic model of nuclear deep inelastic scattering using the point form of dynamics, where the Lorentz boost generators are free of interactions and the current operator at the origin can be taken as a sum of nucleon current operators. The deuteron structure functions are derived from the hadronic tensor by expressing the deuteron wave function in terms of internal momentum variables and integrating over the spectator nucleon phase space, yielding explicit convolution formulas for the structure functions F1, F3, g1, and FL in terms of nucleon structure functions.

## Key numbers

- The condition for impossibility of extracting neutron structure functions from deuteron data is given as x less than about 0.01 (stated as "x <~ 0.01" in the abstract).
- The Bjorken variable for the struck nucleon in the deuteron is given by tilde x = [M_0(k) - m_d(1-x)] / [omega(k) + k^z], where m_d is the deuteron mass and M_0(k) = 2 omega(k).
- The lower limit of integration over the internal variable xi is xi_min = (m^2 + k_perp^2) / (m^2 + k_perp^2 + m_d^2 (1-x)^2).
- The normalization condition for the deuteron wave function is integral [phi_0(k)^2 + phi_2(k)^2] d^3 k / [(2 pi)^3 omega(k)] = 1.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of the EMC effect (first reported by the EMC collaboration) by addressing the fundamental incompatibility of the impulse approximation with Poincare invariance in most forms of relativistic dynamics. It contradicts the standard convolution approach used by authors such as Akulinichev (1985) and Ciofi degli Atti (1990s), which neglect the off-shellness of the struck nucleon, and it builds on the point-form formalism developed by Sokolov (1970s) and earlier work by Dirac (1949) on forms of relativistic dynamics. The results enable a reformulation of nuclear DIS calculations that respects current conservation and Poincare covariance, but also impose strict limits on extracting free nucleon structure functions from nuclear targets.
