# Rotational Brownian motion and heavy quark polarization in QCD medium

> We consider the rotational Brownian motion of heavy quark in QCD medium and provide analytical results for polarization of open heavy-flavor hadrons. We calculate expressions for vector and tensor polarization, corresponding to baryon spin polarization and vector meson spin alignment, respectively. Assuming that heavy quarks are initially fully spin polarized along the direction of magnetic field, we compare our results with recent experimental data from ALICE collaboration for $D^{*+}$ meson and provide predictions for spin polarization of open charm baryons. We propose that the transverse momentum dependence of heavy quark polarization may serve as a distinctive signature of the intense initial magnetic field generated in off-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the observed increase in heavy quark spin polarization with transverse momentum in relativistic heavy-ion collisions serves as a distinctive signature of the intense initial magnetic field, under the assumption that heavy quarks are initially fully spin polarized along the magnetic field direction and that the spin relaxation time is significantly larger than the magnetic field decay time.

## Method

The authors model heavy quark spin evolution in the QCD medium using a stochastic Landau-Lifshitz-Gilbert equation for rotational Brownian motion, which they convert into a Fokker-Planck equation for the spin orientation probability distribution. They solve this equation perturbatively in the parameter alpha (ratio of magnetic to thermal effects) for an exponentially decaying magnetic field, then take the limit where the spin relaxation time tau_s is much larger than the magnetic field decay time tau_B to obtain simplified expressions for vector and tensor polarization.

## Key numbers

- The perturbation parameter alpha evaluates to 0.034 for charm quarks and -0.007 for bottom quarks at momentum 50 GeV, assuming an average QGP temperature of 300 MeV
- Best fit to ALICE D*+ meson data yields tau_s = 1.31 fm with reduced chi-squared = 0.27
- The initial magnetic field in the lab frame typically reduces to eB_Lab less than or equal to 0.1 m_pi^2 by the time QGP is formed
- The average path length L traversed by a heavy quark in the fireball is assumed to be approximately 10 fm

## Context

This work extends the theory of rotational Brownian motion, originally developed by Debye (1929) and later refined by Perrin (1934), Landau (1935), and others, to the novel context of heavy quark spin dynamics in QGP. It provides the first formulation of stochastic spin dynamics for heavy quarks in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions and offers a theoretical explanation for recent ALICE collaboration measurements of D*+ meson spin alignment (ALICE 2025). The framework enables future work incorporating realistic hydrodynamic evolution of the fireball, collisional and radiative energy loss of heavy quarks, and field-theoretic calculations of the spin relaxation time.
