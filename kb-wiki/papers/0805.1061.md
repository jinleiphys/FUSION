# A thermodynamic approach to the relaxation of viscosity and thermal conductivity

> A novel higher order theory of relaxation of heat and viscosity is proposed based on corrections to the traditional treatment of the relativistic energy density. In the framework of generalized Bjorken scaling solution to accelerating longitudinal flow we point out that the energy flux can be consequently set to zero in the stationary case, independently of the choice of a specific local rest frame, like the Landau-Lifshitz or Eckart one. We investigate and compare several cooling and re-heating scenarios for the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) within this approach.

# A thermodynamic approach to the relaxation of viscosity and thermal conductivity

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel higher-order theory of relaxation of heat and viscosity based on correcting the traditional treatment of relativistic energy density by using the absolute value of the local rest frame energy vector, $\tilde e = \sqrt{u_a T^a_b T^b_c u^c}$, rather than the standard internal energy density. They claim that within this framework, the energy flux can be set to zero in the stationary Bjorken flow case independently of the choice of local rest frame (Landau-Lifshitz or Eckart), but this result depends on the specific assumption of a stationary flow with vanishing flow velocity.

## Method

The authors construct a fiducial energy density scalar $L = e^2 + q_i q^i - 3(p+\Pi)^2 - \pi_{ij}\pi^{ij}$ from Lorentz scalars of the energy-momentum tensor, then define the entropy density as a function of this scalar and the baryon density. From the entropy production derived via the energy-momentum conservation and the Gibbs relation, they obtain linear response equations for heat flux, bulk viscosity, and shear viscosity, each containing relaxation terms. These relaxation equations are then applied to a generalized Bjorken scaling solution with accelerating longitudinal flow, and the resulting coupled differential equations for energy density, bulk pressure, and shear pressure are solved numerically for a quark-gluon plasma with radiation equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c
- Initial energy density: $e_0 = \ep_0 = 30$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Initial viscosity to entropy density ratio: $\eta_0 = 0.4$
- Initial shear stress: $\pi_0 = 0$
- Stefan-Boltzmann coefficient for QGP with 3 massless quarks and 16 gluonic degrees of freedom: $\sigma = 47.5\pi^2/30$
- Relaxation time for heat conduction: $\tau_q = \lambda T/e$
- Relaxation time for bulk viscosity: $\tau_{\Pi} = 3\zeta/e$
- Relaxation time for shear viscosity: $\tau_{\pi} = 2\eta/e$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Biro and Van, 2008) of instabilities in first-order Eckart theory by constructing a hyperbolic (second-order) extension that maintains stability without additional restrictive conditions. The approach is contrasted with the Israel-Stewart theory (Israel and Stewart, 1976), where the coefficients $\beta_0$, $\beta_1$, and $\beta_2$ for quadratic dissipative terms in the entropy four-current are fixed by the construction rather than being free parameters. The paper enables further investigation of cooling and re-heating scenarios in quark-gluon plasma evolution, particularly addressing the discrepancy between perfect fluid dynamical calculations and elliptic flow data from RHIC (Heinz and Hirano, 2005; Hirano, 2005).
