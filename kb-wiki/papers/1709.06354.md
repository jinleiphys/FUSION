# Elliptic Flow in Pb+Pb Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV at the LHC Using Boltzmann Transport Equation with Non-extensive Statistics

> Elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions is an important signature of a possible de-confinement transition from hadronic phase to partonic phase. In the present work, we use non-extensive statistics, which has been used for transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) distribution in proton+proton ($p+p$) collisions, as the initial particle distribution function in Boltzmann Transport Equation (BTE). A Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave (BGBW) function is taken as an equilibrium function to get the final distribution to describe the particle production in heavy-ion collisions. In this formalism, we try to estimate the elliptic flow in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV at the LHC for different centralities. The elliptic flow ($v_2$) of identified particles seems to be described quite well in the available $p_{\rm T}$ range. An approach, which combines the non-extensive nature of particle production in $p+p$ collisions through an evolution in kinetic theory using BTE, with BGBW equilibrium distribution is successful in describing the spectra and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that combining a non-extensive Tsallis distribution (which describes particle production in proton-proton collisions) as the initial condition in the Boltzmann Transport Equation with a Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave equilibrium distribution can successfully describe the elliptic flow of identified hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, but only when the full formalism is used; the BGBW formalism alone fails beyond transverse momentum of about 1.5 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann Transport Equation in the relaxation time approximation, using a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis non-extensive distribution as the initial particle distribution and a Boltzmann-Gibbs Blast Wave function as the equilibrium distribution. The final distribution is then used to compute the elliptic flow coefficient v2 at mid-rapidity for identified hadrons (pions, kaons, K_S^0, protons, phi mesons, and Lambda hyperons) in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for two centrality classes (0-5% and 50-60%). The model parameters are obtained by fitting the transverse momentum spectra of identified particles and then used to predict v2.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic freeze-out temperature fixed at 0.095 GeV for central collisions and 0.11 GeV for peripheral collisions.
- The formalism describes v2 data for identified hadrons up to p_T = 5 GeV/c.
- The ratio t_f/tau (freeze-out time to relaxation time) increases with particle mass, with a steeper slope in central collisions compared to peripheral collisions.
- The non-extensive parameter q decreases with increasing particle mass, and for a given mass, q values are higher for peripheral collisions than for central collisions.
- The radial flow parameter <beta_r> is higher for central collisions than for peripheral collisions.

## Context

This work extends prior studies that used Tsallis statistics to explain particle spectra and the nuclear modification factor R_AA in heavy-ion collisions (Tripathy 2016, Tripathy 2017). It contrasts with earlier calculations based on transport equations (Kolb 2000, Das 2017) and phenomenological models (Younus 2012) that explained v2 without connecting to non-extensive statistics. The paper claims to make the first direct connection between particle production in proton-proton collisions and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions using non-extensive statistics in the Boltzmann Transport Equation. This enables future investigations into the interplay between thermalization and freeze-out dynamics, and the centrality dependence of equilibration.
