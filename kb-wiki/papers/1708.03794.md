# Elementary excitations in homogeneous superfluid neutron star matter: role of the neutron-proton coupling

> The thermal evolution of Neutron Stars is affected by the elementary excitations that characterize the stellar matter. In particular, the low-energy excitations, with a spectrum linear in momentum, can play a major role in the emission and propagation of neutrinos. In this paper, we focus on the elementary modes in the region of proton superfluidity, where the neutron component is expected to have a very small or zero pairing gap. We study the overall spectral functions of protons, neutrons and electrons on the basis of the Coulomb and nuclear interactions. This study is performed in the framework of the Random Phase Approximation, generalized in order to describe the response of a superfluid system. The formalism we use ensures that the Generalized Ward's Identities are satisfied. Despite their relative small fraction, the protons turn out to modify the neutron spectral function as a consequence of the nuclear neutron-proton interaction. This effect is particularly evident at the lower density, just below the crust for a density close to the saturation value, while at increasing density the neutrons and the protons are mainly decoupled. The proton spectral function is characterized by a pseudo-Goldstone mode below $ 2Δ$, twice the pairing gap, and a pair-breaking mode above $ 2Δ$. The latter merges in the sound mode of the normal phase at higher momenta. The neutron spectral function develops a collective sound mode only at the higher density. The electrons have a strong screening effect on the proton-proton interaction at the lower momenta, and decouple from the protons at higher momenta.

## Key claim

Despite the small proton fraction (a few percent), the neutron-proton nuclear coupling substantially modifies the neutron spectral function at densities near saturation, contradicting the expectation that the two components are largely independent; this effect is strongest at lower density and essentially disappears at twice saturation density.

## Method

The authors used a generalized Random Phase Approximation (RPA) in the Landau monopole approximation to compute the density-density response functions for neutrons, protons, and electrons in homogeneous superfluid neutron star matter. The nuclear effective interactions were derived from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations including three-body forces, with the proton pairing gap treated as a parameter and the pairing interaction strength fixed consistently via the gap equation; two Skyrme functionals (SLy230a and NRAPR) were also used for comparison.

## Key numbers

- At density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, the proton fraction is 3.7%, and the pairing gap is assumed to be 1 MeV.
- The electron Thomas-Fermi screening length is about 18 fm at saturation density and about 11 fm at twice saturation density.
- At the lower density (0.16 fm⁻³) and momentum q = 0.025 fm⁻¹, the proton pseudo-Goldstone mode appears below 2Δ (2 MeV), and the neutron spectral function is divided by 15 for display due to its larger magnitude.
- At twice saturation density, the proton fraction increases to about 8%, but neutrons and protons become essentially decoupled.
- For a small pairing gap of 0.002 MeV, the system behaves nearly as a normal system, with a sharp electron plasmon peak appearing at low momentum.

## Context

This work extends the study of Ducoin (2015) on superfluid proton matter, which neglected the neutron-proton coupling, by including that interaction. It builds on earlier RPA calculations of spectral functions in normal neutron star matter by Ducoin (2011) and Ducoin (2012), and on studies of superfluid excitations by Reddy (2000), Kundu (2002), Leinson (2002), and others. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutrino emission and mean free paths in neutron star crusts, and show that Skyrme functionals (SLy230a, NRAPR) give substantially weaker neutron-proton coupling than the microscopic BHF approach.
