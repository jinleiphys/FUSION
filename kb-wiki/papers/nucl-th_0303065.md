# Bound state kinetics in high-energy nuclear collisions

> A Lorentz covariant kinetic equation for bound states and their constituents is presented and solved exactly in closed form. It describes in a unified way dynamical formation and dissociation of states such as quarkonia and (anti)-deuterons in the excited medium formed with a high-energy heavy-ion collision.

## Key claim

A Lorentz-covariant kinetic equation for bound states (quarkonia and anti-deuterons) and their constituents is presented and solved exactly in closed form, providing a unified description of dynamical formation and dissociation in the excited medium of a high-energy heavy-ion collision. The solution is constructed for two different ways of specifying the initial condition (global time or proper time), and the author notes that the full solution requires sufficiently realistic numerical studies with detailed knowledge of medium constituents and transition probabilities.

## Method

The author postulates a kinetic equation for the phase-space density of a bound state B, with a collision term consisting of a formation part C_F and a dissociation part C_D, based on processes B(p) + n(k) ↔ c1(q1) + c2(q2) where n is a fourth degree of freedom. The equation is solved exactly in closed form by combining partial solutions for free streaming, dissociation-only, and formation-only cases, first for initialization at a fixed global time t0 and then for initialization at a fixed proper time τ0 using momentum rapidity y and space-time rapidity η as longitudinal variables. A simplified analytical example is worked out by neglecting all dependencies on y, p⊥, η, and r⊥, reducing the problem to a first-order ordinary differential equation.

## Key numbers

- The simplified solution for the final number of bound states at τf is N^f_B = (P_F N_{c1} N_{c2})/(P_D N_D) * [1 - (τ0/τf)^{P_D N_D}], which becomes proportional to N_{c1} N_{c2} / N_D when P_D N_D ≫ 1.
- The formation and dissociation rates in the simplified model are taken as Λ_F(τ) = P_F N_{c1} N_{c2} / τ and Λ_D(τ) = P_D N_D / τ, where P_F and P_D are probabilities and N_{c1}, N_{c2}, N_D are particle numbers.
- The invariant spectrum of bound states is computed via the Cooper-Frye formula with integration over a hyper-surface τf = τf(η, r⊥), using the invariant volume measure dσ_μ p^μ.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of charmonium suppression via dissociation-only kinetics from Blaizot and Ollitrault (1989) to include both formation and dissociation in a unified framework. It provides a tool for comprehensive analysis of rapidity and transverse momentum dependencies of medium effects on quarkonia, as studied by Kharzeev, Pisarski, and Henley (2001) and by Hüfner and Zhu (2002), and for studying (anti-)deuteron formation via coalescence at freeze-out, previously addressed by Danielewicz and Bertsch (1991) for low and intermediate energies. The solution enables future numerical studies of quarkonium formation in a quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC energies, as suggested by Thews, Schroedter, and Rafelski (2001), and of collective flow patterns in (anti-)deuteron spectra as probes of freeze-out properties.
