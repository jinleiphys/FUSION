# Production of one and two $c \bar c$ pairs at LHC

> We report on charm production at LHC. The production of single $c \bar c$ pairs is calculated in the $k_t$-factorization approach with different unintegrated gluon distributions. Examples of transverse momentum distributions for charmed mesons are presented and compared to recent experimental results from LHC. Some missing strength is observed for most of UGDFs. Furthermore we discuss production of two $c \bar c$ pairs within double-parton scattering (DPS) and single-parton scattering (SPS) mechanisms. Surprisingly large cross sections, comparable to single $c \bar c$ pair production are predicted. We discuss first experimental results from LHCb collaboration on production of pairs of $D$ mesons of the same flavour.

## Key claim

The production of two \(c \bar c\) pairs at LHC energies may have cross sections comparable to single \(c \bar c\) pair production, but this result depends on the specific unintegrated gluon distribution used and is most pronounced at high center-of-mass energies where double-parton scattering dominates.

## Method

The authors calculated single \(c \bar c\) pair production using the \(k_t\)-factorization approach with several different unintegrated gluon distributions (UGDFs), then compared the resulting transverse momentum distributions of charmed mesons to ALICE and LHCb data. For two \(c \bar c\) pair production, they separately computed double-parton scattering (DPS) contributions using a factorized formula with an effective cross section parameter, and single-parton scattering (SPS) contributions using a high-energy extension of the \(k_t\)-factorization formalism.

## Key numbers

- Single \(c \bar c\) pair production cross sections at LHC energies are very large, but the paper does not quote a specific numerical value for the total cross section.
- The SPS contribution to \(c \bar c c \bar c\) production is more than two orders of magnitude smaller than single \(c \bar c\) production at low energies.
- At high energies, the DPS contribution to \(c \bar c c \bar c\) production approaches the single \(c \bar c\) production cross section and the total proton-proton cross section.
- For \(D^{0}D^{0}\) production within LHCb acceptance, the experimental cross section is \(690 \pm 40 \pm 70\) nb; the KMR UGDF gives 256 nb, Jung setA0+ gives 101 nb, and KMS gives 100 nb.
- For \(D^{+}D^{+}\) production within LHCb acceptance, the experimental cross section is \(80 \pm 10 \pm 10\) nb; the KMR UGDF gives 41 nb, Jung setA0+ gives 16 nb, and KMS gives 16 nb.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous \(k_t\)-factorization studies of charm production at the Tevatron (Szczurek and collaborators, 2006) and nonphotonic electron production at RHIC (Szczurek and collaborators, 2009, 2011). It also builds on their first estimates for two \(c \bar c\) pair production (Szczurek and collaborators, 2011; Szczurek and Maciula, 2012) and compares with recent LHCb measurements of same-flavor \(D\) meson pairs. The results suggest that double-parton scattering effects can be identified through correlation observables such as rapidity differences between same-flavor quarks, enabling future experimental studies to isolate DPS contributions from SPS backgrounds.
