# Isospin and Z$^{1/3}$ Dependence of the Nuclear Charge Radii

> Based on the systematic investigation of the data available for $A \geq 40$, a $Z^{1/3}$ dependence for the nuclear charge radii is shown to be superior to the generally accepted $A^{1/3}$ law. A delicate scattering of data around $R_c/Z^{1/3}$ is infered as owing to the isospin effect and a linear dependence of $R_c/Z^{1/3}$ on $N/Z$ (or $(N-Z)/2$) is found. This inference is well supported by the microscopic Relativistic Continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculation conducted for the proton magic Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn and Pb isotopes including the exotic nuclei close to the neutron drip line. With the linear isospin dependence provided by the data and RCHB theory, a new isospin dependent $Z^{1/3}$ formula for the nuclear charge radii is proposed.

# Isospin and Z^{1/3} Dependence of the Nuclear Charge Radii

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Z^{1/3} dependence describes nuclear charge radii for nuclei with A ≥ 40 significantly better than the traditional A^{1/3} law, but only after adding a linear isospin correction term proportional to N/Z. The abstract's claim of superiority is qualified by the fact that the Z^{1/3} dependence alone still shows residual scattering due to isospin, which the proposed formula corrects.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental root-mean-square charge radii for 536 nuclei with A ≥ 40 from muonic atom spectroscopy, optical isotope shift, and electron scattering data. They compared fits using the A^{1/3} law and the Z^{1/3} law, applying a deformation correction factor (1 + 5β²/8π) for rare-earth deformed nuclei. To test the isospin dependence for exotic nuclei far from stability where no data exist, they performed fully self-consistent Relativistic Continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculations for even-even Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, and Pb isotopes out to the neutron drip line, using the NL-SH parameter set and a density-dependent delta force in the pairing channel.

## Key numbers

- The Z^{1/3} coefficient for stable nuclei is r_{Zd} = 1.631(11) fm, compared to r_A ≈ 1.31 fm for A ∼ 40 and r_A ≈ 1.20 fm for very heavy nuclei under the A^{1/3} law.
- The relative rms deviation σ for the Z^{1/3} dependence is 7.57 × 10⁻³ for stable nuclei and 1.00 × 10⁻² for all 536 nuclei, versus σ = 1.90 × 10⁻² and 1.63 × 10⁻² respectively for the A^{1/3} law.
- The isospin slope parameter in the proposed formula R_c = a Z^{1/3}[1 + b(η − η*)] is b = 0.062(9), obtained from least squares fitting.
- The RCHB calculations reproduce experimental charge radii within 1.5% and binding energies within 1% for the isotopic chains studied.
- Neutron drip-line nuclei are predicted at ⁷²Ca, ⁹⁸Ni, ¹⁴⁰Zr, and ¹⁷⁶Sn.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Z^{1/3} dependence proposed by Zeng (1957) and the isospin-dependent A^{1/3} formulas of Nerlo-Pomorska and Pomorski (1993) and Warda, Nerlo-Pomorska, and Pomorski (1998). The new formula reduces the rms deviation by about 40% compared to the simple Z^{1/3} law. The RCHB calculations, which previously reproduced halo phenomena in ¹¹Li (Meng and Ring, 1996) and predicted giant halos (Meng and Ring, 1998), provide microscopic support for the isospin dependence in regions where experimental data are unavailable. The proposed formula is intended to help extract information about proton-neutron decoupling in exotic nuclei and to constrain effective nucleon-nucleon interactions.
