# Octupole deformation properties in the actinides region using Fayans functionals

> In this first-of-its-kind survey conducted on heavy and deformed nuclei in the actinide region of the nuclear chart, we have charted nuclear ground state properties predicted by Fayans energy density functionals (EDFs), focusing in particularly on octupole deformability. Compared to earlier studies with Skyrme-based EDFs, we found similar region of octupole deformed nuclei. Moreover, Fayans EDFs were found to provide accurate predictions for various ground state properties, when compared to experimental data. Comparison to Skyrme-based EDF shows similar trends in various nuclear properties.

## Key claim

Fayans energy density functionals (EDFs) predict a region of octupole-deformed nuclei in the actinides that coincides in location, shape, and magnitude with earlier Skyrme-based EDF surveys, but the Fayans functionals also show a strong responsiveness to octupole deformation effects on charge radii and pairing gaps, with the Fy(Δr,HFB) parametrization predicting slightly stronger octupole deformation than Fy(std).

## Method

The authors performed systematic constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the HFBTHO program with two Fayans EDF parametrizations (Fy(std) and Fy(Δr,HFB)) for even-even nuclei in the region Z=82 to Z=100 and N=126 to N=142, spanning quadrupole moments from Q2=-25 b to Q2=35 b and octupole moments from Q3=0 b^(3/2) to Q3=10 b^(3/2). For odd-A nuclei, they used the equal filling approximation with quasiparticle blocking, selecting the lowest-energy blocked state from unconstrained calculations. In total, 1,040,416 independent self-consistent constrained calculations were performed across 416 isotopes.

## Key numbers

* Octupole deformation lowers ground state energy by 0.2 MeV for lesser-impacted isotopes up to 1.25 MeV for the most sensitive isotopes in the middle of the isotopic chains.
* The octupole-deformed cluster spans Z=84 to Z=108 and N=120 to N=150, centered around 226U.
* Thorium isotopic chain shows non-zero octupole deformation up to N=142, while Plutonium continues until N=148.
* The threshold for significant octupole deformation impact was estimated at β3 > 0.02.
* Fy(Δr,HFB) predicts approximately 0.2 MeV lower deformation energy compared to Fy(std) for the cluster center.
* Constrained calculations spanned Q2 from -25 b to 35 b in steps of 1 b and Q3 from 0 b^(3/2) to 10 b^(3/2) in steps of 0.25 b^(3/2).

## Context

This work extends previous surveys of octupole deformation using Skyrme-based EDFs (Cao 2020) to the Fayans functional framework, which has shown superior accuracy for nuclear charge radius predictions (Reinhard 2017, Wang 2024). The results confirm that Fayans EDFs reproduce the same octupole-deformed region as Skyrme-based UNEDF0 calculations while also capturing local variations in charge radii that Skyrme functionals struggle with (Kortelainen 2022, Rong 2025). This enables systematic comparisons between the two functional families for octupole-related phenomena including fission fragment mass distributions and nuclear Schiff moments.
