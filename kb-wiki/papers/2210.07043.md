# Connections between many-body perturbation and coupled-cluster theories

> Here, we build on the works of Scuseria (et al.) http://dx.doi.org/10.1063/1.3043729 and Berkelbach https://doi.org/10.1063/1.5032314 to show connections between the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) formalism combined with the $GW$ approximation from many-body perturbation theory and coupled-cluster (CC) theory at the ground- and excited-state levels. In particular, we show how to recast the $GW$ and Bethe-Salpeter equations as non-linear CC-like equations. Similitudes between BSE@$GW$ and the similarity-transformed equation-of-motion CC method introduced by Nooijen are also put forward. The present work allows to easily transfer key developments and general knowledge gathered in CC theory to many-body perturbation theory. In particular, it may provide a path for the computation of ground- and excited-state properties (such as nuclear gradients) within the $GW$ and BSE frameworks.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) formalism combined with the $GW$ approximation can be exactly recast as non-linear coupled-cluster (CC) equations, and that the $GW$ equations themselves can be rewritten as a set of CC-like amplitude equations, but only under the assumption that the inverse of the eigenvector matrix exists and that one restricts to the principal (quasiparticle) solutions.

## Method

The authors start from the linear eigenvalue problems of RPA with exchange (RPAx) and BSE, introduce the matrix $\bT{}{} = \bY{}{} \cdot \bX{}{-1}$, and derive Riccati equations that match the ring CCD (rCCD) amplitude equations. For $GW$, they recast the quasiparticle equation as a larger linear eigenvalue problem coupling the 1h, 2h1p, and 2p1h sectors, then derive two coupled Riccati equations that become CC-like residual equations for the 2h1p and 2p1h amplitudes. The resulting equations are solved with $\order*{N^6}$ cost using quasi-Newton iterative updates.

* The $GW$ CC-like residual equations (Eqs. 31-32) also scale as $\order*{N^6}$ cost by defining judicious intermediates.
* The trace formula for the RPAx correlation energy is $E_c^\text{RPAx} = \frac{1}{4} \Tr(\bOm{}{} - \bA{}{})$, which matches the rCCD correlation energy $E_c^\text{rCCD} = \frac{1}{4} \sum_{ijab} \dbERI{ij}{ab} t_{ij}^{ab}$.
* The BSE correlation energy via the trace formula is $E_c^\text{BSE} = \frac{1}{4} \Tr(\bOm{}{\BSE} - \bA{}{\BSE}) = \frac{1}{4} \sum_{ijab} \wERI{ij}{ab} \tilde{t}_{ij}^{ab}$.

## Context

This work builds directly on Scuseria et al. (2008), who proved ring CCD equivalence to RPAx for correlation energies, and Berkelbach (2018), who extended the connection to excitation energies via equation-of-motion rCCD. The paper extends these connections to the BSE formalism and the $GW$ approximation, showing that BSE@$GW$ resembles the similarity-transformed equation-of-motion CC method of Nooijen (1997). This enables transferring CC developments (such as nuclear gradient computation) to the $GW$ and BSE frameworks, potentially broadening their applicability in computational photochemistry.
