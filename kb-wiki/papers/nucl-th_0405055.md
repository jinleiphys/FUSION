# Neutrino scattering off pair-breaking and collective excitations in superfluid neutron matter and in color-flavor locked quark matter

> We calculate the correlation functions needed to describe the linear response of superfluid matter, and go on to calculate the differential cross section for neutral-current neutrino scattering in superfluid neutron matter and in color-flavor locked quark matter (CFL). We report the first calculation of scattering rates that includes neutrino interactions with both pair-breaking excitations and low-lying collective excitations (Goldstone modes). Our results apply both above and below the critical temperature, allowing use in simulations of neutrino transport in supernovae and neutron stars.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first calculation of neutral-current neutrino scattering rates in superfluid neutron matter and color-flavor locked quark matter that includes both pair-breaking fermionic excitations and low-lying collective excitations (Goldstone modes), with results valid both above and below the critical temperature. The abstract's claim of applicability to supernova and neutron star simulations is qualified by the use of a simplified contact interaction model and the Random Phase Approximation, which introduces screening corrections that become important at moderate coupling strengths.

## Method
The authors computed the quark polarization tensor (current-current correlation function) using Nambu-Gor'kov propagators in the mean-field approximation for BCS pairing, then applied the Random Phase Approximation to incorporate the Goldstone mode associated with spontaneous breaking of U(1)_B symmetry, ensuring current conservation via the generalized Ward identity. For non-relativistic superfluid neutron matter, they solved the vertex equation analytically for the dressed vertex function; for relativistic CFL quark matter, they summed the geometric series of RPA diagrams to obtain the collective response. The differential cross section was then obtained from the imaginary part of the polarization tensor contracted with the neutrino tensor.

## Key numbers
- For superfluid neutron matter with chemical potential μ = 30 MeV and zero-temperature gap Δ = 5 MeV (critical temperature T_c = 2.8 MeV): at T = 0.5 T_c, the RPA differential cross section integrated over energy transfer is 0.5 × 10^{-5} m^{-1} compared to 0.1 × 10^{-5} m^{-1} for the quasi-free response alone; at T = 0.85 T_c, RPA gives 4.9 × 10^{-5} m^{-1} versus 2.6 × 10^{-5} m^{-1} for quasi-free; at T = 1.1 T_c, RPA gives 12.9 × 10^{-5} m^{-1} versus 12.8 × 10^{-5} m^{-1} for quasi-free.
- The Goldstone mode dispersion in non-relativistic neutron matter has sound velocity c_s = p_F/(√3 M) ≈ 0.15 for the parameters used.
- For CFL quark matter, the gap Δ is estimated to be 10–100 MeV for quark chemical potential μ ≈ 300–500 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of neutrino interactions in dense matter from earlier calculations that considered only Goldstone boson interactions in CFL quark matter (Reddy, 2002) or only pair-breaking processes (Flowers, 1976), by providing a unified RPA treatment that includes both channels and satisfies current conservation. The results enable more realistic simulations of neutrino transport in proto-neutron stars and supernovae, where temperatures approach the critical temperature for superfluidity, and provide a microscopic foundation for effective field theory descriptions of the Goldstone mode (Bedaque, 2003). The formalism also applies to neutrino pair production and emissivity calculations in superfluid matter.
