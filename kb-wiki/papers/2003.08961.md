# Quasifission dynamics in microscopic theories

> In the search for superheavy elements quasifission reactions represent one of the reaction pathways that curtail the formation of an evaporation residue. In addition to its importance in these searches quasifission is also an interesting dynamic process that could assist our understanding of many-body dynamical shell effects and energy dissipation thus forming a gateway between deep-inelastic reactions and fission. This manuscript gives a summary of recent progress in microscopic calculations of quasifission employing time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory and its extensions.

## Key claim

Quasifission dynamics, as studied via time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory and its extensions, can reveal many-body dynamical shell effects and energy dissipation that bridge deep-inelastic reactions and fission, but the paper emphasizes that TDHF calculations vastly under-predict the widths of fragment distributions, requiring beyond-mean-field extensions (such as TDRPA or SMF) to obtain experimentally comparable results.

## Method

The authors review microscopic calculations of quasifission using TDHF theory, where the time-evolution of nuclear densities is computed for given initial conditions (energy, impact parameter, orientation). Extensions including the time-dependent random phase approximation (TDRPA) and the stochastic mean-field (SMF) method are employed to calculate fluctuations and correlations of neutron and proton transfer beyond the mean-field level. For the \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{249}\mathrm{Bk}\) system, 150 TDHF collisions were cataloged by varying the orientation of the deformed \(^{249}\mathrm{Bk}\) in \(15^{\circ}\) steps and orbital angular momentum \(L\) in units of \(10\hbar\) from 0 to quasielastic collisions.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{249}\mathrm{Bk}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=234\) MeV and \(L/\hbar=60\) with \(^{249}\mathrm{Bk}\) orientation \(\beta=135^{\circ}\), TDHF predicts final fragments \(^{203}\)Au and \(^{94}\)Sr.
- The mass-angle distribution for \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{249}\mathrm{Bk}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=234\) MeV shows yields strongly peaked at mass ratio \(M_R \sim 0.33\) and \(0.67\), with a full width at half maximum \(\mathrm{FWHM} \simeq 0.1\) corresponding to a standard deviation \(\sigma_{M_R} \simeq 0.042\).
- For the \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{249}\mathrm{Bk}\) system at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=234\) MeV, mass equilibration times between \(8-10\) zs (\(1\) zs \(=10^{-21}\) s) are obtained from fits of mass ratio versus contact time.
- The TDRPA calculation for \(^{176}\mathrm{Yb}+{}^{176}\mathrm{Yb}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=880\) MeV in side-side orientation predicts mass-angle and mass-energy distributions in units of millibarns per degree and millibarns per MeV, respectively.
- For the \(^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{238}\mathrm{U}\) system, the potential energy surface (PES) is calculated at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=211\) MeV; for \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{238}\mathrm{U}\), the PES is calculated at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=203\) MeV, both with equatorial orientation of \(^{238}\)U.

## Context

This review extends prior TDHF studies of quasifission (e.g., Kedziora 2010, Wakhle 2014, Oberacker 2014, Goddard 2015, Hammerton 2015, Umar 2015a, Umar 2015c, Umar 2016, Prasad 2016, Wang 2016, Sekizawa 2016, Godbey 2019) and builds on experimental work by Toke (1985), Shen (1987), Hinde (1992-2008), Itkis (2004, 2011), and others. The paper shows that shell effects similar to those in fission (e.g., \(Z=82\) and \(^{100}\)Zr region) drive quasifission fragment formation, as confirmed experimentally by Morjean (2017). It enables future calibration of the compound nucleus formation probability \(P_\mathrm{CN}\) by comparing theoretical angular distributions to experiment, and may help distinguish quasifission from fusion-fission in superheavy element searches.
