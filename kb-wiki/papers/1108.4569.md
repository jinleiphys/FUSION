# Fine-Grid Calculations for Stellar Electron and Positron Capture Rates on Fe-Isotopes

> The acquisition of precise and reliable nuclear data is a prerequisite to success for stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis studies. Core-collapse simulators find it challenging to generate an explosion from the collapse of the core of massive stars. It is believed that a better understanding of the microphysics of core-collapse can lead to successful results. The weak interaction processes are able to trigger the collapse and control the lepton-to-baryon ratio ($Y_{e}$) of the core material. It is suggested that the temporal variation of $Y_{e}$ within the core of a massive star has a pivotal role to play in the stellar evolution and a fine-tuning of this parameter at various stages of presupernova evolution is the key to generate an explosion. During the presupernova evolution of massive stars, isotopes of iron, mainly $^{54,55,56}$Fe, are considered to be key players in controlling $Y_{e}$ ratio via electron capture on these nuclide. Recently an improved microscopic calculation of weak interaction mediated rates for iron isotopes was introduced using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) theory. The pn-QRPA theory allows a microscopic \textit{state-by-state} calculation of stellar capture rates which greatly increases the reliability of calculated rates. The results were suggestive of some fine-tuning of the $Y_{e}$ ratio during various phases of stellar evolution. Here we present for the first time the fine-grid calculation of the electron and positron capture rates on $^{54,55,56}$Fe. Core-collapse simulators may find this calculation suitable for interpolation purposes and for necessary incorporation in the stellar evolution codes.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fine-grid calculation of stellar electron and positron capture rates on the iron isotopes 54Fe, 55Fe, and 56Fe using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model, with the claim that these rates are suitable for interpolation in core-collapse simulation codes; however, the positron capture rates are orders of magnitude smaller than the electron capture rates and are described as "fragile functions of the available phase space" that can change appreciably with a 0.5 MeV change in phase space calculations.

## Method

The authors used the pn-QRPA model to perform a microscopic state-by-state calculation of Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions for 246 parent excited states in 54Fe, 297 states in 55Fe, and 266 states in 56Fe, with each parent state transitioning to 150 daughter excited states covering excitation energies of 15-20 MeV. Experimental deformation parameters were incorporated for 54Fe and 56Fe from Raman et al. (1987), while the deformation for 55Fe was calculated from the electric quadrupole moment. The calculated excitation energies and log ft values were replaced with experimental values when they agreed within 0.5 MeV, and the rates were tabulated on a fine grid of stellar temperatures (T9 from 0.5 to 30.0 in units of 10^9 K) and densities (log ρYe from 0.5 to 1.0 in g cm^-3).

## Key numbers

- Electron capture rates on 54Fe at T9 = 0.50 and log ρYe = 0.5: log10(λec) = -24.273 sec^-1; at T9 = 30.00 and same density: log10(λec) = 1.699 sec^-1
- Positron capture rates on 54Fe at T9 = 0.50 and log ρYe = 0.5: log10(λpc) = -92.182 sec^-1; at T9 = 30.00 and same density: log10(λpc) = 0.762 sec^-1
- Electron capture rates on 55Fe at T9 = 0.50 and log ρYe = 0.5: log10(λec) = -11.945 sec^-1; at T9 = 30.00: log10(λec) = 1.770 sec^-1
- Electron capture rates on 56Fe at T9 = 0.50 and log ρYe = 0.5: log10(λec) = -47.582 sec^-1; at T9 = 30.00: log10(λec) = 1.453 sec^-1
- pn-QRPA electron capture rates on 54Fe are about three times larger than large-scale shell model (LSSM) rates during oxygen and silicon core burning phases
- FFN (Fuller, Fowler, Newman) electron capture rates are larger than pn-QRPA rates by about one order of magnitude
- TQRPA (thermal QRPA) electron capture rates on 54Fe are smaller by two orders of magnitude at T9 = 1 and log ρYe = 10^7 g cm^-3, and by six orders of magnitude at log ρYe = 10^8 g cm^-3, compared to pn-QRPA and LSSM rates
- Positron capture rates on 54Fe from previous calculations are 3-5 orders of magnitude larger than the pn-QRPA rates

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of Nabi (2009) by providing a fine temperature-density grid suitable for direct interpolation in stellar evolution codes, addressing the need identified by Fuller et al. (1982) for finer grids to reduce interpolation uncertainty. The results are compared with the large-scale shell model calculations of Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2000), the earlier FFN rates of Fuller et al. (1982), and the thermal QRPA approach of Dzhioev et al. (2010), with the pn-QRPA rates showing better agreement with LSSM than with FFN or TQRPA. The fine-grid tables enable core-collapse simulators to incorporate these rates directly into simulation codes for studying the lepton-to-baryon ratio (Ye) evolution during presupernova phases.
