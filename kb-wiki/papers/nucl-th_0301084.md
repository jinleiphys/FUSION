# Inclusive electron scattering in a relativistic Green function approach

> A relativistic Green function approach to the inclusive quasielastic (e,e') scattering is presented. The single particle Green function is expanded in terms of the eigenfunctions of the nonhermitian optical potential. This allows one to treat final state interactions consistently in the inclusive and in the exclusive reactions. Numerical results for the response functions and the cross sections for different target nuclei and in a wide range of kinematics are presented and discussed in comparison with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic Green function approach to inclusive quasielastic (e,e') scattering that treats final state interactions consistently by expanding the single particle Green function in terms of eigenfunctions of the nonhermitian optical potential, thereby preserving flux conservation in the inclusive reaction even though the optical potential's imaginary part causes absorption in exclusive channels.

## Method

The hadron tensor for inclusive electron scattering is expressed in terms of the single particle optical model Green function, which is expanded using a biorthogonal basis of eigenfunctions of the nonhermitian relativistic optical potential and its Hermitian conjugate. The approach follows the same steps as the nonrelativistic treatment of Capuzzi et al. (previous work), but extends the formalism to include Dirac matrix structure and relativistic kinematics. The interference term between different channels is approximated by replacing the Green function with an effective Green function that includes factors of the square root of one minus the energy derivative of the optical potential.

## Key numbers

- The energy sum rule for the incoherent hadron tensor is given by Eq. (13): ∫ dω W^{μμ}(ω,q) = (A+1) ⟨Ψ₀| j^{μ†}(q) j^{μ}(q) |Ψ₀⟩, where A+1 is the total number of nucleons.
- The spectral strength λ_n for each channel n satisfies ∑_n λ_n ≃ A + 1, where A is the mass number of the target nucleus.
- The threshold of the continuum for the single particle Hamiltonian h(E) is the nucleon mass M, used as the lower limit of integration in the spectral representation.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic Green function approach of Capuzzi et al. (previous paper) to a relativistic framework, enabling calculations at higher momentum transfers where nonrelativistic treatments become unreliable. The method treats final state interactions consistently in both inclusive and exclusive reactions, addressing the longstanding problem of flux conservation that arises when using optical potentials with imaginary parts. The approach enables comparison with experimental data for different target nuclei across a wide range of kinematics, and can be combined with treatments of meson exchange currents and correlations (Sluys, Cenni, Amaro, Fabrocini, Co) to potentially resolve the discrepancies between theory and experiment in the longitudinal and transverse response functions.
