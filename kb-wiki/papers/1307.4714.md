# Explaining the fine hierarchy in pion and kaon suppression at LHC: Importance of fragmentation functions

> We here concentrate on available $π^\pm$ and $K^\pm$ ALICE preliminary $R_{AA}$ data in central 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. These data show an interesting fine resolution hierarchy, i.e. the measured $K^\pm$ data have consistently lower suppression compared to $π^\pm$ measurements. We here ask whether theoretical predictions based on energy loss in dynamical QCD medium can quantitatively and qualitatively explain such fine resolution. While our suppression calculations agree well with the data, we find that qualitatively explaining the fine hierarchy critically depends on the choice of fragmentation functions. While the most widely used fragmentation functions lead to the reversal of the observed hierarchy, a more recent version correctly reproduce the experimental data. We here point to the reasons behind such discrepancy in the predictions. Our results argue that accuracy of the theoretical predictions reached a point where comparison with fine resolution data at LHC can generate useful understanding.

## Key claim

The observed fine hierarchy where kaon suppression is consistently lower than pion suppression in central 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at LHC can be qualitatively explained by theoretical predictions based on energy loss in a dynamical QCD medium, but only if the more recent DSS fragmentation functions are used; the widely used KKP fragmentation functions lead to a reversal of the observed hierarchy.

## Method

The authors computed the quenched hadron spectra using a pQCD convolution of initial parton spectra with an energy loss probability and fragmentation functions. The energy loss probability included both radiative and collisional energy loss in a finite size dynamical QCD medium, incorporating multi-gluon fluctuations, path-length fluctuations for 0-5% most central collisions, finite magnetic mass effects, and running coupling. The theoretical predictions for pion and kaon nuclear modification factors (R_AA) were compared with ALICE preliminary data using two different sets of fragmentation functions: KKP and DSS.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the QGP was taken as T = 304 MeV, as extracted by ALICE
- Effective number of light quark flavors n_f = 2.5
- Perturbative QCD scale Lambda_QCD = 0.2 GeV
- Debye mass mu_E approximately 0.9 GeV, obtained by self-consistently solving Eq. (3) from the paper
- Magnetic mass mu_M taken in the range 0.4 mu_E < mu_M < 0.6 mu_E
- The ratio of gluon to light quark contribution in initial distributions of pions and kaons was computed as a function of momentum for both KKP and DSS fragmentation functions, showing opposite trends between the two sets

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dynamical energy loss formalism (Djordjevic 2009, Djordjevic and Heinz 2008) to test fine resolution between pion and kaon suppression data from ALICE (Velasquez 2013). The study builds on earlier work by Vitev (2006) showing that fragmentation functions can affect the hierarchy of suppression predictions for different particle species. The results demonstrate that theoretical predictions have reached sufficient precision to discriminate between different fragmentation function sets (KKP from Kniehl, Kramer and Potter 2000 versus DSS from de Florian, Sassot and Stratmann 2007), enabling more stringent tests of QCD medium properties through comparison with fine resolution data.
