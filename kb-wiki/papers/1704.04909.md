# Properties and uses of factorial cumulants in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We discuss properties and applications of factorial cumulants of various particle numbers and for their mixed channels measured by the event-by-event analysis in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. After defining the factorial cumulants for systems with multi-particle species, their properties are elucidated. The uses of the factorial cumulants in the study of critical fluctuations are discussed. We point out that factorial cumulants play useful roles in understanding fluctuation observables when they have underlying physics approximately described by the binomial distribution. As examples, we suggest novel utilization methods of the factorial cumulants in the study of the momentum cut and rapidity window dependences of fluctuation observables.

## Key claim

Factorial cumulants are useful for analyzing fluctuation observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly when the underlying physics is approximately described by the binomial distribution, but they lack the direct connection to the grand partition function that ordinary cumulants of conserved charges possess.

## Method

The authors define factorial cumulants and factorial moments for systems with multiple particle species and for linear combinations of stochastic variables representing conserved charges such as net-baryon number. They derive explicit relations between cumulants and factorial cumulants up to third order for multi-variable cases, showing that factorial cumulants of net-particle numbers contain contributions from total particle numbers. They then apply these formal results to analyze the momentum cut dependence, rapidity window dependence, and efficiency correction of fluctuation observables using the binomial model.

## Key numbers

- For Poisson distribution, factorial cumulants vanish for orders m ≥ 2: ⟨n^m⟩_fc,Poisson = 0 (for m ≥ 2)
- The second-order factorial cumulant of a linear combination q_(a) is given by: ⟨q_(a)^2⟩_fc = ⟨q_(a)^2⟩_c - ⟨q_(a^2)⟩_c
- For net-baryon number N_(net) = N - N̄, the second factorial cumulant is: ⟨N_(net)^2⟩_fc = ⟨N_(net)^2⟩_c - ⟨N_(tot)⟩_c, where N_(tot) = N + N̄
- The third factorial cumulant of net-baryon number is: ⟨N_(net)^3⟩_fc = ⟨N_(net)^3⟩_c - 3⟨N_(net)N_(tot)⟩_c + 2⟨N_(net)⟩_c

## Context

This work extends previous studies on factorial cumulants in heavy-ion collisions by Kitazawa (2013), Kitazawa (2015), Ling (2015), Bzdak (2016), and Nonaka (2017), which focused on single-particle-species factorial cumulants. The authors generalize the formalism to multi-particle species and mixed channels, and clarify that factorial cumulants cannot be regarded as identical to ordinary cumulants except in the very vicinity of the QCD critical point as suggested by Ling (2015). The paper enables new methods for studying momentum cut and rapidity window dependences of fluctuations, and for reconstructing early-time fluctuations from final-state observables using the binomial model.
