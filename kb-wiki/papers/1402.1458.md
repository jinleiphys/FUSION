# Thermalization of Hadrons via Hagedorn States

> Hagedorn states are characterized by being very massive hadron-like resonances and by not being limited to quantum numbers of known hadrons. To generate such a zoo of different Hagedorn states, a covariantly formulated bootstrap equation is solved by ensuring energy conservation and conservation of baryon number $B$, strangeness $S$ and electric charge $Q$. The numerical solution of this equation provides Hagedorn spectra, which enable to obtain the decay width for Hagedorn states needed in cascading decay simulations. A single (heavy) Hagedorn state cascades by various two-body decay channels subsequently into final stable hadrons. All final hadronic observables like masses, spectral functions and decay branching ratios for hadronic feed down are taken from the hadronic transport model UrQMD. Strikingly, the final energy spectra of resulting hadrons are exponential showing a thermal-like distribution with the characteristic Hagedorn temperature.

## Key claim

The final energy spectra of hadrons produced by the cascading decay of a single heavy Hagedorn state follow an exponential, thermal-like Boltzmann distribution with a temperature exactly equal to the Hagedorn temperature, even though no temperature parameter was introduced in the underlying bootstrap formalism.

## Method

A covariantly formulated bootstrap equation enforcing conservation of energy, baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge was solved numerically to generate Hagedorn spectra up to masses of 8 GeV. Using these spectra and the principle of detailed balance, total decay widths and two-body branching ratios for Hagedorn states were derived, and cascading decay simulations were performed with hadronic properties taken from the UrQMD transport model.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperature extracted from the exponential slope of the spectrum: \(T_H = 0.145\) GeV for radius \(R = 1.0\) fm, and \(T_H = 0.162\) GeV for \(R = 0.8\) fm.
- The numerical bootstrap solution was limited to masses \(m \le 8\) GeV due to computation time scaling as \(m^8\).
- The thermal temperature of the final hadron energy distributions was \(T_{th} = 0.162\) GeV, matching the Hagedorn temperature for \(R = 0.8\) fm.
- Particle multiplicity ratios for an uncharged Hagedorn state of mass 8 GeV: \(K^-/\pi^- = 0.210\), \(\overline p/\pi^- = 0.066\), \(\Lambda/\pi^- = 0.038\), \(\Xi^-/\pi^- = 0.0066\), \(\Omega^-/\pi^- = 5.60 \times 10^{-4}\).
- The probability for a Hagedorn state to decay into two particles is 69%, into three particles 24%.

## Context

This work extends the statistical bootstrap model of Hagedorn (1965) and the covariant bootstrap formulation of Byckling and Kajantie (1973) by providing a numerical solution that includes known hadron spectral functions from UrQMD, avoiding ad hoc assumptions about the low-mass spectrum used in earlier approaches such as Broniowski (2004). The results enable future implementation of Hagedorn state production and decay into the UrQMD transport framework, offering a consistent microscopic description of hadronization and thermalization near the deconfinement transition in heavy ion collisions, and potentially explaining the faster chemical equilibration times (1-2 fm/c) found in earlier work by Noronha-Hostler (2007, 2009).
