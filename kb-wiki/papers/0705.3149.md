# Deuteron-Proton Elastic Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> The deuteron-proton elastic scattering has been studied in the multiple scattering expansion formalism. The essential attention has been given to such relativistic problem as a deuteron wave function in a moving frame and transformation of spin states due to Wigner rotation. Parameterization of the nucleon-nucleon $t$-matrix has been used to take the off-energy shell effects into account. The vector, $A_y,$ and tensor, $A_{yy}$, analyzing powers of the deuteron have been calculated at two deuteron kinetic energies: 395 MeV and 1200 MeV. The obtained results are compared with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a semi-phenomenological multiple scattering expansion, using a nonrelativistic CD-Bonn deuteron wave function transformed into the deuteron Breit frame and a parameterized Love-Franey nucleon-nucleon t-matrix extended off-energy-shell, can reproduce the vector and tensor analyzing powers of deuteron-proton elastic scattering at 395 MeV and 1200 MeV, though the agreement is qualitative rather than precise at the higher energy.

## Method

The deuteron-proton elastic scattering amplitude was constructed from three contributions (one nucleon exchange, single scattering, and double scattering) within the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formalism. The deuteron wave function was transformed from the deuteron rest frame to the Breit frame using Lorentz transformations and Wigner rotation operators, allowing the use of a nonrelativistic CD-Bonn wave function. The nucleon-nucleon interaction was described by the Love-Franey parameterization of the on-shell t-matrix, which was extended to off-energy-shell cases using a kinematical factor, and the resulting amplitude was decomposed into 12 spin-dependent amplitudes to compute the vector analyzing power A_y and tensor analyzing power A_yy.

## Key numbers

* Vector analyzing power A_y and tensor analyzing power A_yy were calculated at deuteron kinetic energy 395 MeV and compared with Saclay experimental data.
* Vector analyzing power A_y and tensor analyzing power A_yy were calculated at deuteron kinetic energy 1200 MeV and compared with Saclay and Argonne experimental data.
* The nucleon-nucleon t-matrix parameterization used is valid for nucleon kinetic energies up to 1 GeV, as the Love-Franey model covers this range.

## Context

This work extends the semi-phenomenological approach of Alberi (year not specified) by including a proper deuteron wave function transformation via Wigner rotation and using a parameterized nucleon-nucleon t-matrix (Love and Franey, year not specified) instead of phase shift analysis data, which loses energy dependence. The method is intended to describe deuteron-proton elastic scattering at intermediate energies (400-1200 MeV) where Faddeev equation approaches using realistic potentials like Bonn, CD-Bonn, Paris, Nijmegen, or AV18 are limited to below 300-350 MeV laboratory kinetic energy. Future work plans to apply this formalism to other polarization observables in the same energy range.
