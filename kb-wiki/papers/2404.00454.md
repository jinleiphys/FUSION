# Revisiting the 3α reaction rates in helium burning stars

> Helium burning is one of the most fundamental steps of stellar nucleosynthesis, as it describes the formation of life-determining element of carbon, while it plays a key role in the evolution of Red Giant, accreting White Dwarfs and Neutron Stars. In this work we develop a generalized statistical theory for the 3α reaction, which is based on the use of the Imaginary Time Method, along with the semi-classical Hybrid α-Clustering (HαC) and Neck Model (NM) frameworks. The results compared to the methodology and data of the NACRE collaboration, following in several orders of magnitude discrepancies, especially at low temperatures. This may be crucial for the early dynamics of helium burning stars.

# Revisiting the 3α reaction rates in helium burning stars

## Key claim

The standard NACRE compilation overestimates the triple-alpha reaction rate by 20-30 orders of magnitude at temperatures around 10^7 K, primarily because it integrates reaction rates from zero energy rather than from the 92.08 keV threshold for 8Be formation and uses an incorrect width ratio (Γγ/Γα instead of Γγ/Γ) in the cross section formula for the second step.

## Method

The authors develop a generalized statistical theory for the 3α reaction using the Imaginary Time Method (ITM) to compute quantum tunneling probabilities, coupled with two semi-classical models: the Hybrid α-Clustering (HαC) model, which treats α particles as dynamical degrees of freedom, and the Neck Model (NM), a macroscopic description based on Wigner transforms of Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock densities. The fusion cross sections are calculated for l=0 partial waves, and the α widths are derived from the imaginary-time action without free parameters, while the γ widths are computed using a new energy-dependent reduced matrix element proportional to the α width, rather than the constant matrix element approximation used in NACRE.

## Key numbers

- The 8Be ground state threshold energy is E_8Be = 92.08 keV, below which the first step (α+α → 8Be) cannot occur; NACRE incorrectly integrates from 0 MeV.
- The Hoyle state resonance energy is E_H = 7.654 MeV, with α width Γα,H(0) = 9.3 eV and γ width Γγ,H(0) = 5.1 meV.
- The original NACRE prescription overestimates the reaction rate by 20-30 orders of magnitude at temperatures T ∼ 10^7 K compared to the corrected calculations.
- An astrophysical upper limit from Suda et al. (2011) constrains the reaction rate at T ∼ 10^7.8 K to be no larger than 10^(-29) cm^6 s^(-1) mol^(-2).
- The temperature dependence ν must satisfy ν ≳ 10 in the region T ∼ 10^8 - 1.2×10^8 K to ensure AGB stars experience helium shell flashes consistent with observations.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of the triple-alpha process beyond the NACRE compilation (Angulo 1999, NACRE II 2013) by correcting two key approximations: the integration threshold and the width ratio in the cross section formula. The results are consistent with earlier calculations by Ogata (2010), Ishikawa (2013), Nguyen (2013), and Garrido (2011), which also found lower rates than NACRE at low temperatures. The corrected rates satisfy astrophysical constraints from Suda et al. (2011) regarding helium ignition in First Asymptotic Giant Branch stars and third dredge-up events in AGB stars. The authors propose that future experimental investigations using the Trojan Horse Method (Tumino 2018) could provide more stringent constraints on the cross sections by bypassing the short lifetime of 8Be and Coulomb suppression.
