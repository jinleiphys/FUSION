# Linear response theory in asymmetric nuclear matter for Skyrme functionals including spin-orbit and tensor terms

> The formalism of linear response theory for a Skyrme functional including spin-orbit and tensor terms is generalized to the case of infinite nuclear matter with arbitrary isospin asymmetry. Response functions are obtained by solving an algebraic system of equations, which is explicitly given. Spin-isospin strength functions are analyzed varying the conditions of density, momentum transfer, asymmetry and temperature. The presence of instabilities, including the spinodal one, is studied by means of the static susceptibility.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the linear response formalism for Skyrme functionals including spin-orbit and tensor terms to asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM) with arbitrary isospin asymmetry, but restricts the analysis to isospin excitations induced by the operator τ₃, leaving charge-exchange processes for future study.

## Method

The authors start from a general Skyrme energy density functional (EDF) including central, spin-orbit, tensor, and density-dependent terms, and derive the residual particle-hole (ph) interaction matrix elements via second functional derivatives of the total energy. They then solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the RPA ph propagator in asymmetric nuclear matter, obtaining a closed algebraic system of equations (6 equations for S=0, 8 for S=1 per isospin subsystem) that is solved numerically in (q,ω)-space. The response functions are obtained by averaging the RPA propagator over hole momenta, and the resulting strength functions are analyzed for different densities, momentum transfers, asymmetry parameters, and temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Calculations performed at densities ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter) and ρ = 0.08 fm⁻³ (surface/crust density)
- Asymmetry parameters Y = 0 (SNM), 0.21 (roughly corresponding to ²⁰⁸Pb), 0.5 (β-equilibrium condition), and 1 (PNM)
- Momentum transfers studied: q = 0.1, 0.5, and 1.34 fm⁻¹
- Temperature T = 16 MeV used for thermal effects study
- Critical densities for S=1 instabilities: for q less than approximately 1.2-1.4 fm⁻¹, critical density exceeds 0.16 fm⁻³; for higher q, critical density falls below 0.16 fm⁻³
- Spinodal instability contours mapped in the (ρ_p, ρ_n) plane at T=0 and T=10 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous linear response calculations for Skyrme functionals from symmetric nuclear matter (Gar92, Mar06, Dav09) and pure neutron matter (Pas12, Pas12b) to the general asymmetric case, including spin-orbit and tensor terms that were absent in earlier ANM treatments (Her97, Her97a). The formalism enables systematic detection of finite-size instabilities at nonzero momentum transfer, complementing the Landau-Migdal approach valid only in the long-wavelength limit (Mar02, Cao10, Nav13). The results can be used to constrain Skyrme EDF coupling constants during fitting procedures (Kor10, Kor13) and are relevant for describing phenomena in compact stars (Gal11, Cha13, Gul13).
