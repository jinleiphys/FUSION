# Isospin violation in the vector form factors of the nucleon

> A quantitative understanding of isospin violation is an increasingly important ingredient for the extraction of the nucleon's strange vector form factors from experimental data. We calculate the isospin violating electric and magnetic form factors in chiral perturbation theory to leading and next-to-leading order respectively, and we extract the low-energy constants from resonance saturation. Uncertainties are dominated largely by limitations in the current knowledge of some vector meson couplings. The resulting bounds on isospin violation are sufficiently precise to be of value to on-going experimental studies of the strange form factors.

## Key claim
The isospin-violating electric and magnetic form factors of the nucleon are calculated in chiral perturbation theory to leading and next-to-leading order respectively, with the low-energy constants estimated from resonance saturation via ρ–ω mixing. The resulting bounds are sufficiently precise to be of value for ongoing experimental studies of strange form factors, but uncertainties remain dominated by limitations in current knowledge of some vector meson couplings.

## Method
The authors performed one-pion-loop calculations in both manifestly covariant (infrared regularization) and heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, cross-checking the two formalisms to identify and correct errors in prior work. The lone combination of low-energy constants appearing in the chiral expressions was estimated via resonance saturation, specifically by computing ρ–ω mixing contributions using vector meson couplings extracted from recent dispersive analyses of nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers
- The dimensionless prefactor C = g_A² m_N Δm / (4π F_π)² ≈ 1.4 × 10⁻³, where Δm = m_n – m_p is the proton-neutron mass difference.
- The ρ–ω mixing amplitude Θ_ρω = (−3.75 ± 0.36) × 10⁻³ GeV², obtained from the latest experimental analysis.
- The leading-order chiral loop prediction for the magnetic radius term ρ^{u,d}_M = (2C/3M_π²) [1 – 7π M_π/(4m_N)], where the next-to-leading-order correction reduces the leading prediction by approximately 80%.
- The ρ–nucleon coupling constants extracted from dispersive analyses yield g_ρ values ranging from 4.0 to 6.2 and κ_ρ values from 5.1 to 6.8, depending on the treatment of the two-pion continuum.

## Context
This work extends prior chiral perturbation theory studies of isospin violation in nucleon form factors by Lewis and Mobed (2004), correcting errors in that earlier calculation and providing the first resonance-saturation estimate of the unknown low-energy constants. The results enable more precise extraction of strange vector form factors from experiments by the SAMPLE, A4, HAPPEX, and G0 Collaborations, complementing earlier quark model estimates by Pollock (1990) and Miller (1996) that found isospin violation below 1%. The formalism also allows comparison with the light-cone baryon model of Ma (2003) and the quark model approach of Gardner (1999), though matching these models to consistent chiral perturbation theory remains problematic.
