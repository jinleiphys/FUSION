# alpha-cluster states in intermediate mass nuclei

> Properties of intermediate mass nuclei have been investigated within the framework of the alpha-cluster model in combination with systematic double-folding potentials. Previously, this alpha-cluster model has been widely applied to light nuclei, in particular to 8Be = alpha \otimes alpha, 20Ne = 16O \otimes alpha, and 44Ti = 40Ca \otimes alpha, and to heavy nuclei, in particular to 212Po = 208Pb \otimes alpha. In the present work a wide range of nuclei is investigated with the magic neutron number N = 50 in the mass range around A \approx 80 - 100: (A+4,N=52) = (A,N=50) \otimes alpha. It is found that excitation energies, decay properties, and transition strengths can be described successfully within this model. The smooth and small variation of the underlying parameters of the alpha-nucleus potential may be used for extrapolations to predict experimentally unknown properties in the nuclei under study.

# alpha-cluster states in intermediate mass nuclei

## Key claim

The alpha-cluster model with systematic double-folding potentials can successfully describe excitation energies, decay properties, and transition strengths for a wide range of nuclei with magic neutron number N=50 in the mass range A ≈ 80-100, provided that the potential strength parameter λ is adjusted slightly (by less than 1% between neighboring levels) for each state of the rotational band; the model fails when using Woods-Saxon potentials, which produce an inversion of the level ordering.

## Method

The author calculated bound and quasi-bound states of (A+4, N=52) = (A, N=50) ⊗ alpha systems using double-folding potentials derived from the DDM3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and nuclear densities from electron scattering data, scaled by a strength parameter λ. The Pauli principle was incorporated via the Wildermuth condition with oscillator quanta Q=16 for nuclei above ⁹⁴Mo and Q=14 for nuclei below ⁹²Zr, and the potential strength was readjusted for each angular momentum state to reproduce experimental excitation energies of the ground state rotational bands.

## Key numbers

- The potential strength parameter λ for the ground state of ⁹⁶Ru = ⁹²Mo ⊗ alpha was λ = 1.1965 to reproduce the binding energy of -1692 keV
- For the Woods-Saxon potential, V₀ = 142.97 MeV (with r₀ = 1.3 fm, a = 0.7 fm) or V₀ = 162.38 MeV (with r₀ = 1.2 fm, a = 0.7 fm) were required for the same binding energy
- The calculated excitation energies for the rotational band in ⁹⁶Ru using the folding potential were: 31 keV (2⁺), 123 keV (4⁺), 255 keV (6⁺), 417 keV (8⁺), 601 keV (10⁺), 816 keV (12⁺), 1088 keV (14⁺), and 1453 keV (16⁺)
- The variation parameter c(L=2) for the first excited 2⁺ state averaged c = 0.0057 ± 0.0007 for nuclei between Z=32 and Z=46, with an exceptional value of c = 0.0102 for Z=48 (¹⁰²Sn)
- Calculated B(E2) values for 2⁺ → 0⁺ transitions ranged from 7.1 W.u. (⁸²Zn) to 10.1 W.u. (¹⁰⁴Te)
- Experimental B(E2) values for ⁹⁰Sr: 8.4(24) W.u. (2⁺ → 0⁺) and 5.1(9) W.u. (4⁺ → 2⁺)
- Experimental B(E2) values for ⁹⁴Mo: 16.0(4) W.u. (2⁺ → 0⁺) and 26(4) W.u. (4⁺ → 2⁺)
- Predicted excitation energy for the first 2⁺ state in ⁸⁴Ge: E_x = 877 keV with uncertainty of about 100 keV
- Predicted excitation energy for the first 2⁺ state in ⁸²Zn: E_x = 880 keV with uncertainty of about 100 keV
- Revised prediction for the first 2⁺ state in ¹⁰⁴Te: E_x = 807 keV
- Higher-nodal band heads in ⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁴Mo: E_x ≈ 7 MeV for Q=17 (1⁻) bands and E_x ≈ 11 MeV for Q=18 (0⁺) bands

## Context

This work extends earlier alpha-cluster model studies that focused on light nuclei such as ⁸Be, ²⁰Ne, and ⁴⁴Ti, and heavy nuclei such as ²¹²Po, as well as previous work on ⁹⁴Mo = ⁹⁰Zr ⊗ alpha by Mic98, Ohk95, and Buck95. The study demonstrates that the proton subshell closure at Z=40 is not important for alpha-cluster properties, contrary to earlier assumptions, and that the smooth variation of potential parameters enables predictions for experimentally unknown nuclei. The results enable extrapolations to unstable N=50 nuclei such as ⁸²Zn and ⁸⁴Ge, and provide a foundation for extending the model to even-odd nuclei as demonstrated in the companion study by Kiss08 for ⁹³Nb = ⁸⁹Y ⊗ alpha.
