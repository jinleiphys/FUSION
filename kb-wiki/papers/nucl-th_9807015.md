# Shadowing and Antishadowing Effects in a Model for the n+d Total Cross Section

> Based on the multiple scattering series incorporated in the Faddeev scheme the high energy limit of the total n+d cross section is evaluated in a nonrelativistic model system where spins are neglected. In contrast to the naive expectation that the total n+d scattering cross section is the sum of two NN cross sections we find two additional effects resulting from rescattering processes. These additional terms have different signs (shadowing and antishadowing) and a different behavior as function of the energy. Our derivation of these results which are already known from Glauber theory is based on the analytical evaluation of elastic transition amplitudes in the high energy limit. It does not depend on the diffraction type assumptions connected with Glauber theory. In this model of spinless Yukawa type forces (with no absorption) the total n+d cross section does not approach twice the NN total cross section in the high energy limit but rather approaches the total NN cross section multiplied by a number larger than two. Therefore, the enhancement effect resulting from rescattering is larger than the shadowing effect, which decreases faster with energy.

## Key claim

In a nonrelativistic model of spinless bosons interacting via Yukawa-type forces (with no absorption), the total n+d cross section does not approach twice the NN total cross section in the high energy limit; instead it approaches the total NN cross section multiplied by a number larger than two, because the antishadowing (enhancement) effect from rescattering is larger than the shadowing effect, which decreases faster with energy.

## Method

The authors start from the multiple scattering series embedded in the Faddeev framework for three identical spinless bosons, evaluate the elastic forward scattering amplitude analytically in the high energy limit, and use the optical theorem to obtain the total n+d cross section. They compute the first and second order terms in the NN t-matrix expansion, explicitly evaluating the permutation operators and momentum-space integrals to extract the leading energy-dependent contributions without relying on diffraction-type assumptions of Glauber theory.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of shadowing and antishadowing effects previously known from Glauber theory (Glauber, 1959; Joachain, 1983; Henley, 1984; Abers, 1966) by deriving them from the Faddeev multiple scattering series without diffraction-type approximations. The paper uses a simplified spinless boson model to isolate the basic mechanism, noting that including spin and isospin degrees of freedom (to be addressed in a forthcoming article) will modify the results due to interference effects. This analytical framework enables future studies of how spin-dependent NN interactions alter the balance between shadowing and antishadowing in realistic n+d scattering.
