# The isospin dependent nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section in the nuclear medium

> The calculation of the energy-, density-, and isospin-dependent $Δ$ production cross sections in nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering $σ^*_{NN\rightarrow NΔ}$ has been performed within the framework of the relativistic BUU approach. The N$Δ$ cross sections are calculated in Born approximation taking into account the effective mass splitting of the nucleons and $Δ$s in asymmetric matter. Due to the different mass splitting for neutron, proton and differently charged $Δ$s, it is shown that, similar to the NN elastic ones, the reductions of N$Δ$ inelastic cross sections in isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium are different from each other for all the individual channels and the effect is largest and of opposite sign for the $Δ^{++}$ and $Δ^{-}$ states. This approach is also compared to calculations without effective mass splitting and with splitting derived from Dirac-Brueckerner (DB) calculations. The isospin dependence of the N$Δ$ cross sections is expected to influence the production of $π^+$ and $π^-$ mesons as well as their yield ratio, and thus affect the use of the latter quantity as a probe of the stiffness of the symmetry energy at supranormal densities.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the isospin asymmetry of the nuclear medium causes the in-medium cross sections for the production of \(\Delta^{++}\) and \(\Delta^{-}\) resonances in nucleon-nucleon scattering to be affected in opposite directions and with the largest magnitude among all six production channels, while the other four channels show only weak isospin dependence. This effect arises from the mass splitting of nucleons and \(\Delta\) isobars when the \(\delta\) meson exchange is included, and the authors caution that this may influence the \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) yield ratio used as a probe of the symmetry energy at supranormal densities.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium cross sections for the six individual channels of the \(NN \rightarrow N\Delta\) process using the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) approach, with the cross sections calculated in the Born approximation. The effective Lagrangian includes \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\delta\), \(\rho\), and \(\pi\) mesons coupled to nucleons and \(\Delta(1232)\) resonances via density-dependent coupling coefficients derived from Dirac-Brueckner calculations, and the effective masses of protons, neutrons, and the four charged states of the \(\Delta\) are split by the isovector scalar \(\delta\) meson field in asymmetric matter.

## Key numbers

* At a kinetic energy \(E_K = 1\) GeV and zero density (\(u=0\)), the isospin-averaged in-medium cross section \(\sigma^*_{NN\rightarrow N\Delta}\) is 19.5 mb, comparable to the experimental value of 18 mb and the previous calculation of 17.5 mb.
* At normal nuclear density (\(u=1\)) and \(E_K = 1\) GeV, the isospin-averaged cross section is about 10 mb, comparable to the Dirac-Brueckner result of 11-12 mb.
* At an isospin asymmetry \(\alpha = 0.5\) and \(u=1\) with \(E_K = 1\) GeV, the ratio \(R(\alpha) = \sigma^*(\alpha)/\sigma^*(\alpha=0)\) reaches 1.74 for the \(pp \rightarrow n\Delta^{++}\) channel and 0.73 for the \(nn \rightarrow p\Delta^{-}\) channel.
* At the same conditions, the ratio \(R(\alpha)\) for the \(\Delta^{+}\) and \(\Delta^{0}\) production channels remains within the interval 0.88 to 1.15.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections by Li (2003) and of isospin-averaged inelastic cross sections by Mao (1994), and it directly addresses the isospin dependence omitted in the earlier DB-based calculation by Prassa (2007). The results are intended to be implemented in microscopic transport models such as IBUU, LQMD, or UrQMD to improve the interpretation of pion yield ratios from heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies, particularly to resolve the ongoing controversy regarding the stiffness of the symmetry energy at supranormal densities that has been debated by Xiao (2008), Feng (2009), Trautmann (2009), and others.
