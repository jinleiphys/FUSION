# Thermal suppression of moving heavy quark pairs in strongly coupled plasma

> The imaginary part of the heavy quark-antiquark potential experienced by moving heavy quarkonia in strongly coupled plasmas dual to theories of gravity is computed by considering thermal worldsheet fluctuations of the holographic Nambu-Goto string. General results for a wide class of gravity duals are presented and an explicit formula for $\mathrm{Im} \, V_{Q\bar{Q}}$ is found in the case where the axis of the moving $\bar{Q}Q$ pair has an arbitrary orientation with respect to its velocity in the plasma. These results are applied to the study of heavy quarkonia propagating through a strongly coupled $\mathcal{N} = 4$ SYM plasma. Our results indicate that the onset of $\mathrm{Im} \, V_{Q\bar{Q}}$ decreases with increasing rapidity (though our analysis is limited to slowly moving quarkonia) and that, in general, a $Q\bar{Q}$ pair is more strongly bound if its axis is aligned with its direction of motion through the strongly coupled plasma.

## Key claim

The imaginary part of the heavy quark-antiquark potential for moving quarkonia in strongly coupled plasmas is computed using thermal worldsheet fluctuations of the holographic Nambu-Goto string, though the analysis is limited to slowly moving quarkonia because the saddle point approximation used breaks down at large rapidities.

## Method

The authors boost from the frame where the plasma is at rest and the quark-antiquark dipole is moving to a frame where the dipole is at rest and the plasma moves past it as a hot wind. They then compute the real part of the potential by solving the classical Nambu-Goto string equations in the boosted black brane metric, and obtain the imaginary part by considering thermal fluctuations of the string worldsheet near the bottom of the string configuration, following the method developed in Noronha and Dumitru (2009) and Finazzo and Noronha (2013).

## Key numbers

- For the perpendicular dipole case in strongly coupled N=4 SYM, the separation length times temperature is given by \(LT = \frac{2 \sqrt{2 \pi} y_c}{\Gamma(1/4)^2} \sqrt{1- y_c^4 \cosh^2 \eta} \, {}_2 F_1 \left(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{4}, \frac{5}{4}, y_c^4 \right)\), where \(y_c = U_h/U_c\) ranges from 0 to 1 and \(\eta\) is the rapidity.
- The real part of the potential is \(\frac{\mathrm{Re}\,V_{Q\bar{Q}}}{T} = - \frac{\sqrt{\lambda}}{y_c} \frac{\sqrt{2 \pi^3}}{\Gamma(1/4)^2} \, \left[ {}_2 F_1 \left(\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{4}, \frac{1}{4}, y_c \right) + y_c^4 \cosh^2 \eta \, {}_2 F_1 \left(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{4}, \frac{5}{4}, y_c^4 \right) \right]\), where \(\lambda\) is the 't Hooft coupling.
- The imaginary part formula for the perpendicular case is \(\mathrm{Im} \, V_{Q\bar{Q}} = -\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2} \alpha'} \sqrt{\tilde{M}_c} \left[ \frac{\tilde{V}'_c}{2 \tilde{V}''_c} - \frac{\tilde{V}_c}{\tilde{V}'_c} \right]\), valid when \(\tilde{M}(U_c) > 0\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic calculation of the imaginary part of the static heavy quark potential from Noronha and Dumitru (2009) and Finazzo and Noronha (2013) to the case of moving quarkonia, complementing earlier studies of the real part for moving pairs by Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006, 2007) and Caceres, Natsuume, and Okamura (2006). The results are consistent with the findings of Faulkner and Liu (2009) that meson widths become large for rapidly moving mesons, and with the general arguments from Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2007) and Ejaz, Faulkner, Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2008) that dissociation temperature decreases with rapidity. The method enables future calculations of thermal widths for moving quarkonia in strongly coupled plasmas, though it is limited to slowly moving pairs due to the saddle point approximation.
