# Kinematical relativistic effects in $(\vec{e},e'\vec{N})$ reactions

> Kinematical relativistic effects are analyzed within the plane-wave impulse approximation for outgoing nucleon polarized responses in coincidence electron scattering. Following recent approaches for non-relativistic reductions of the electromagnetic current operator, here an exact treatment of the problem for the transferred energy and momentum is presented. Rather than employing existing non-relativistic expressions for the single-nucleon matrix elements, in this work the response functions are expanded directly. Results are compared for different kinematical situations with the fully-relativistic PWIA calculation as well as with previous non-relativistic expansions. Off- and `on-shell' prescriptions are also studied in detail, and a systematic analysis of the various kinematical variables involved in the process is presented.

## Key claim

Kinematical relativistic effects in coincidence electron scattering can be treated exactly for the transferred energy and momentum by expanding the response functions directly in the variable \(\chi \equiv (p/M_N)\sin\theta\) (where \(p\) is missing momentum, \(M_N\) nucleon mass, and \(\theta\) the angle between missing momentum and momentum transfer), rather than expanding the single-nucleon current matrix elements as in prior semi-relativistic approaches. The accuracy of this semi-relativistic reduction depends on the kinematic regime and on which class of response (classified by leading order \(\chi^n\)) is considered, with off-shell prescriptions introducing additional uncertainty.

## Method

The authors derived fully-relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) expressions for the polarized and unpolarized single-nucleon response functions in \(A(\vec{e},e'\vec{N})B\) reactions, then performed a semi-relativistic expansion directly on these responses using the variable \(\chi = (p/M_N)\sin\theta\) while treating the transferred energy \(\omega\) and momentum \(q\) exactly. They compared the expanded results with fully-relativistic PWIA calculations and with previous non-relativistic expansions for various kinematic conditions, and also examined different off-shell prescriptions and an 'on-shell' prescription where the bound nucleon is forced onto its mass shell.

## Key numbers

- The expansion variable is \(\chi \equiv \frac{p}{M_N}\sin\theta\), where \(p\) is missing momentum, \(M_N\) is the nucleon mass, and \(\theta\) is the angle between missing momentum \(\mathbf{p}\) and momentum transfer \(\mathbf{q}\).
- For the example of \(^{16}\text{O}(e,e'p)^{15}\text{N}\), two kinematic cases are shown: \(q = 0.5\) GeV/c with \(\omega = 55.0\) MeV (below the quasielastic peak \(\omega_{QEP}\)) and \(\omega = 240.6\) MeV (above \(\omega_{QEP}\)).
- The quasielastic peak value for the energy transfer is given by \(\omega_{QEP} = \sqrt{q^2 + M_N^2} + M_B - M_A\).
- The energy transfer as a function of \(q\) and the missing momentum variable \(y\) is \(\omega(q,y) = \sqrt{M_N^2 + (q+y)^2} + \sqrt{y^2 + M_B^2} - M_A\), with \(\omega = \omega_{QEP}\) occurring at \(y=0\).

## Context

This work extends the semi-relativistic approach of Amaro et al. (1996, 1998), who expanded the single-nucleon current matrix elements in missing momentum over nucleon mass while treating \(q/M_N\) and \(\omega/M_N\) exactly, by instead expanding the response functions directly. It complements parallel studies of dynamical relativistic effects in RPWIA by Caballero et al. (1998a, 1998b) and Crispino et al. (2001), and provides a foundation for future work incorporating relativistic final-state interactions (RDWIA) by Crispino et al. (in progress). The analysis enables systematic classification of responses by their leading-order behavior in \(\chi\) and establishes the kinematic conditions under which semi-relativistic reductions remain reliable for polarized observables.
