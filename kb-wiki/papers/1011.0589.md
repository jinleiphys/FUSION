# Field theory on the light-front

> We present a general framework to study relativistic compound systems in a Hamiltonian formalism. This formalism is based on the explicitly covariant formulation of light-front dynamics, with a decomposition of the state vector in Fock components. In order to be able to make definite predictions order by order in the truncation of the Fock expansion, we use an appropriate Fock sector dependent renormalization scheme. Our covariant formulation enables us to have a strict control of any violation of rotational invariance due to the choice of a given orientation of the light-front plane. This is mandatory in order to define the necessary renormalization conditions, and hence to be able to calculate physical observables. We emphasize the role played by antiparticle degrees of freedom in order to control, order by order in the Fock expansion, the scale invariance of physical observables. This nonperturbative framework demands also to use an appropriate regularization scheme. We show why the recently proposed Taylor-Lagrange regularization scheme is a very adequate scheme since it can be implemented very naturally, and from a systematic point of view, in light-front dynamics. As a direct application of this general framework, we settle the basis for a new formulation of chiral effective field theory in the baryonic sector based on light-front dynamics and a Fock decomposition of the state vector.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general nonperturbative framework for studying relativistic compound systems in light-front dynamics, claiming that the combination of covariant light-front dynamics, Fock sector dependent renormalization, and Taylor-Lagrange regularization enables controlled predictions order by order in the Fock expansion. The hedge is that this framework demands careful control of rotational invariance violations and antiparticle degrees of freedom, and that the residual scale dependence seen at higher coupling constants (e.g., α = 0.5) indicates the need for antiparticle contributions.

## Method

The author develops a covariant formulation of light-front dynamics (CLFD) where the state vector is defined on a plane ω·x = σ with ω an arbitrary light-like four-vector, enabling explicit separation of ω-dependent (rotational invariance violating) terms from physical contributions. The Fock sector dependent renormalization (FSDR) scheme assigns distinct mass counterterms δm^(n) and bare coupling constants g_0^(n) to each Fock sector with n bosons in flight, with renormalization conditions fixed by demanding the on-shell two-body vertex function Γ_2(s_2=M^2) be independent of both ω and the longitudinal momentum fraction x. The Taylor-Lagrange regularization scheme (TLRS) treats fields as operator-valued distributions with super regular test functions that are partitions of unity with running boundary conditions, yielding finite extensions of singular distributions without introducing ad-hoc Pauli-Villars particles.

## Key numbers

- Anomalous magnetic moment in the Yukawa model calculated in three-body Fock state truncation for coupling constant α = 0.2 shows "rather nice scale invariance" as a function of regularization mass μ₁ (Pauli-Villars boson mass)
- Anomalous magnetic moment for α = 0.5 shows "onset of scale dependence" attributed to missing antiparticle degrees of freedom
- The two-body vertex function components b₁ and b₂ at s₂=M² in perturbation theory yield b₁^(pert) = constant and b₂^(pert) = 0 when antifermion contributions are included
- The extension of the singular distribution T(X)=1/(X+a) under TLRS yields Ṯ(X) = 1/(X+a) - 1/(X+η²), where η² is an arbitrary dimensionless scale

## Context

This work extends the covariant light-front dynamics formalism originally developed by Dirac (1949) and later by Karmanov, Carbonell, and collaborators, addressing the long-standing problem of controlling rotational invariance violations in truncated Fock space calculations. The Fock sector dependent renormalization scheme builds on earlier work by Wilson and collaborators, while the Taylor-Lagrange regularization scheme provides a mathematically rigorous alternative to Pauli-Villars regularization that avoids infinite mass limits. The proposed light-front chiral effective field theory (LFχEFT) offers a nonperturbative alternative to heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Manohar, 1990s) and infrared regularization schemes, enabling systematic calculations of nucleon properties without relying on momentum expansions or infinite nucleon mass approximations.
