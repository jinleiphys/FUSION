# q- Deformed Boson Expansions

> A deformed boson mapping of the Marumori type is derived for an underlying $su(2)$ algebra. As an example, we bosonize a pairing hamiltonian in a two level space, for which an exact treatment is possible. Comparisons are then made between the exact result, our q- deformed boson expansion and the usual non - deformed expansion.

## Key claim

A q-deformed (quantum algebra) Marumori-type boson mapping can reproduce the exact ground-state energy of a pairing Hamiltonian in a two-level space using only a low-order truncated expansion, provided the deformation parameter q is tuned appropriately; the abstract does not specify that the deformation parameter must be chosen to force convergence of the truncated series.

## Method

The authors construct a deformed boson mapping of the Marumori type for an underlying su(2) algebra, mapping fermionic states onto q-deformed boson states defined by the commutation relation \([b,b^\dagger]_q = 1\). They apply this mapping to a pairing Hamiltonian in a two-level space with degeneracy \(\Omega\) and interaction strength parameter \(x = 2G\Omega/\epsilon\), deriving explicit second-order and fourth-order boson Hamiltonians. The ground-state energies from these truncated expansions are compared with the exact diagonalization of the original fermionic Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Degeneracy \(\Omega = 20\) and interaction strength \(x = 1.0\) are used for all calculations.
- For the second-order deformed expansion, setting \(q = 0.862\) makes the truncated result converge to the exact ground-state energy.
- For the fourth-order deformed expansion, setting \(q = 0.810\) makes the truncated result converge to the exact ground-state energy.

## Context

This work extends the standard Marumori boson expansion method (Marumori, 1964) by incorporating quantum algebra deformation, following earlier studies of deformed su(2) algebras in pairing models (Avancini et al., 1995). The authors show that a tuned deformation parameter can compensate for truncation of the infinite boson series, which is not possible in the usual non-deformed expansion. This suggests that quantum algebras may provide a practical shortcut for boson mapping calculations, though the physical meaning of the deformation parameter remains an open question.
