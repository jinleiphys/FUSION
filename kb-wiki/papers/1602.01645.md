# Speed of Sound in Hadronic matter using Non-extensive Tsallis Statistics

> The speed of sound ($c_s$) is studied to understand the hydrodynamical evolution of the matter created in heavy-ion collisions. The quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in heavy-ion collisions evolves from an initial QGP to the hadronic phase via a possible mixed phase. Due to the system expansion in a first order phase transition scenario, the speed of sound reduces to zero as the specific heat diverges. We study the speed of sound for systems, which deviate from a thermalized Boltzmann distribution using non-extensive Tsallis statistics. In the present work, we calculate the speed of sound as a function of temperature for different $q$-values for a hadron resonance gas. We observe a similar mass cut-off behaviour in non-extensive case for $c^{2}_s$ by including heavier particles, as is observed in the case of a hadron resonance gas following equilibrium statistics. Also, we explicitly present that the temperature where the mass cut-off starts, varies with the $q$-parameter which hints at a relation between the degree of non-equilibrium and the limiting temperature of the system. It is shown that for values of $q$ above approximately 1.13 all criticality disappear in the speed of sound, i.e. the decrease in the value of the speed of sound, observed at lower values of $q$, disappears completely.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a hadron resonance gas described by non-extensive Tsallis statistics, the critical behavior in the speed of sound (a decrease followed by a minimum) disappears completely for values of the non-extensivity parameter \(q\) above approximately 1.13, suggesting that a phase transition is no longer present for sufficiently large deviations from thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors calculate the speed of sound \(c_s^2 = (\partial P / \partial \epsilon)_V\) for a hadron resonance gas using Tsallis non-extensive statistics, where the parameter \(q\) quantifies temperature fluctuations and deviation from Boltzmann-Gibbs equilibrium. They compute energy density, pressure, and specific heat at constant volume from the Tsallis-modified Bose-Einstein and Fermi-Dirac distributions (with zero chemical potential) for a resonance gas including mesons and baryons, and study \(c_s^2\) as a function of temperature for different \(q\)-values and different upper mass cut-offs on the hadron spectrum.

## Key numbers

* For \(q = 1.005\) (near equilibrium), the speed of sound in a hadron resonance gas shows the same mass cut-off behavior as in the equilibrium case, reproducing results from Castorina et al.
* For \(q\) values above approximately 1.13, all criticality in the speed of sound disappears; the decrease in \(c_s^2\) observed at lower \(q\) vanishes completely.
* At a fixed temperature of 170 MeV (chosen as the Hagedorn limiting temperature and lattice QCD critical temperature), the speed of sound decreases monotonically with increasing upper mass cut-off, and increases systematically with increasing \(q\)-value.
* For \(q = 1.15\), there is no distinction between Tsallis-Boltzmann and Tsallis-Bose-Einstein distributions for a pion gas.

## Context

This work extends the study of the speed of sound in a hadron resonance gas by Castorina et al. (using equilibrium Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics) to systems with temperature fluctuations described by Tsallis non-extensive statistics. The non-extensive parameter \(q\) is related to temperature fluctuations as discussed by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (1999), and the range \(1 < q < 1.2\) is motivated by analyses of transverse momentum spectra in high-energy collisions by Beck (2000). The results enable future studies of event-by-event temperature fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies, particularly for systems that stay away from thermal equilibrium.
