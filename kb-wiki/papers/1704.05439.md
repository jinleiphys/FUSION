# $P$-wave $ππ$ scattering and the $ρ$ resonance from lattice QCD

> We calculate the parameters describing elastic $I=1$, $P$-wave $ππ$ scattering using lattice QCD with $2+1$ flavors of clover fermions. Our calculation is performed with a pion mass of $m_π\approx 320\:\:{\rm MeV}$ and a lattice size of $L\approx 3.6$ fm. We construct the two-point correlation matrices with both quark-antiquark and two-hadron interpolating fields using a combination of smeared forward, sequential and stochastic propagators. The spectra in all relevant irreducible representations for total momenta $|\vec{P}| \leq \sqrt{3} \frac{2π}{L}$ are extracted with two alternative methods: a variational analysis as well as multi-exponential matrix fits. We perform an analysis using Lüscher's formalism for the energies below the inelastic thresholds, and investigate several phase shift models, including possible nonresonant contributions. We find that our data are well described by the minimal Breit-Wigner form, with no statistically significant nonresonant component. In determining the $ρ$ resonance mass and coupling we compare two different approaches: fitting the individually extracted phase shifts versus fitting the $t$-matrix model directly to the energy spectrum. We find that both methods give consistent results, and at a pion mass of $am_π=0.18295(36)_{stat}$ obtain $g_{ρππ} = 5.69(13)_{stat}(16)_{sys}$, $am_ρ= 0.4609(16)_{stat}(14)_{sys}$, and $am_ρ/am_{N} = 0.7476(38)_{stat}(23)_{sys} $, where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second is the systematic uncertainty due to the choice of fit ranges.

## Key claim
The lattice QCD calculation finds that elastic I=1, P-wave ππ scattering at a pion mass of approximately 320 MeV is well described by a minimal Breit-Wigner form with no statistically significant nonresonant component, but the authors caution that this conclusion depends on the specific fit ranges and models considered, and they quantify the systematic uncertainty from these choices.

## Method
The authors used 2+1 flavors of clover-improved Wilson fermions on a 32³ × 96 lattice with lattice spacing a ≈ 0.114 fm and volume (3.6 fm)³. They constructed two-point correlation matrices using both quark-antiquark and two-pion interpolating fields, employing smeared forward, sequential, and stochastic propagators. Energy spectra were extracted for all relevant irreducible representations with total momenta |P| ≤ √3 × (2π/L) using both a variational (GEVP) analysis and multi-exponential matrix fits, then analyzed via Lüscher's formalism to relate finite-volume energies to infinite-volume scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: am_π = 0.18295(36)_stat, corresponding to m_π ≈ 320 MeV
- Lattice size: L ≈ 3.6 fm (specifically 3.649(25) fm)
- ρ resonance mass: am_ρ = 0.4609(16)_stat(14)_sys
- ρ → ππ coupling: g_{ρππ} = 5.69(13)_stat(16)_sys
- Ratio of ρ mass to nucleon mass: am_ρ/am_N = 0.7476(38)_stat(23)_sys
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.11403(77) fm, determined from the Υ(2S)-Υ(1S) splitting
- Pion speed of light from dispersion relation: c² = 1.0195(86), consistent with relativistic dispersion within 2%

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the ρ resonance (Gottlieb 1985, McNeile 2002, Aoki 2007, Gockeler 2008, Jansen 2009, Feng 2010, Frison 2010, Lang 2011, Aoki 2011, Pelissier 2012, Dudek 2012, Wilson 2015, Bali 2015, Bulava 2016, Hu 2016, Guo 2016, Fu 2016) by using N_f = 2+1 clover fermions at a single pion mass of 320 MeV with a large volume of (3.6 fm)³. The calculation enables future determinations of matrix elements involving the ρ resonance, such as πγ → ρ(→ ππ) and B → ρ(→ ππ)ℓν̄_ℓ, and provides a comparison of two analysis methods (fitting individual phase shifts versus fitting the t-matrix directly to the energy spectrum) that yield consistent results.
