# Non-perturbative Heavy-Flavor Transport at RHIC and LHC

> We calculate open heavy-flavor (HF) transport in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by applying a strong-coupling treatment in both macro- and microscopic dynamics (hydrodynamics and non-perturbative diffusion interactions). The hydrodynamic medium evolution is quantitatively constrained by bulk and multi-strange hadron spectra and elliptic flow. The heavy quark (HQ) transport coefficient is evaluated from a non-perturbative $T$-matrix approach in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) which, close to the critical temperature, leads to resonance formation and feeds into the recombination of heavy quarks on a hydrodynamic hypersurface. In the hadronic phase, the diffusion of HF mesons is obtained from effective hadronic theory. We compute observables at RHIC and LHC for non-photonic electrons and HF mesons, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a non-perturbative treatment of heavy-quark transport, using a thermodynamic T-matrix approach that produces resonant states near the critical temperature, can simultaneously describe heavy-flavor observables at both RHIC and LHC energies, but the authors note that their D-meson nuclear suppression factor appears systematically slightly too high and that the underprediction of elliptic flow data may indicate a missing radiative energy-loss contribution.

## Method

The authors implemented relativistic Langevin simulations of heavy-quark diffusion in a Quark-Gluon Plasma background modeled by boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamics, using transport coefficients from a non-perturbative T-matrix approach for heavy-light quark and heavy-gluon scattering. Heavy quarks were hadronized at the critical temperature (Tc = 170 MeV) using resonance recombination with thermal light quarks on the hydrodynamic hypersurface, with remaining heavy quarks treated by delta-function fragmentation. After hadronization, D and B mesons continued to diffuse in the hadronic phase until kinetic freezeout at Tkin = 110 MeV, with D-meson thermal relaxation rates from effective hadronic theory and B-meson rates obtained by mass scaling.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: mc = 1.3 GeV; bottom quark mass: mb = 4.2 GeV
- In-medium masses at Tc: mq = 0.3 GeV, mc = 1.7 GeV, mb = 5.2 GeV, mD = 2.1 GeV, mB = 5.6 GeV
- D- and B-meson widths: Gamma_D,B = 0.1 GeV
- Critical temperature for hadronization: Tc = 170 MeV; kinetic freezeout temperature: Tkin = 110 MeV
- T-matrix resonant states accelerate HQ thermal relaxation by a factor of ~3-5 for HQ-light quark scattering and ~2 for HQ-gluon scattering over leading-order pQCD results, at temperatures T ~ 1-1.5 Tc
- Charm-electron RAA reaches 0.2-0.3 at pT^e ~ 5 GeV in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Charm-electron elliptic flow reaches ~8% at pT^e ~ 2 GeV at RHIC
- B-meson RAA ~ 0.4 at pT > 10 GeV in Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- B-meson v2 reaches up to 5% at LHC
- Suppression of non-prompt J/psi from CMS: ~0.37 +/- 0.08 at average pT^J/psi ~ 9.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative heavy-flavor transport framework previously developed by He, Fries, and Rapp (2011) to LHC energies and flavor-separated electron observables at RHIC. It contrasts with perturbative QCD approaches by Armesto (2005), Gossiaux (2008), Alberico (2011), and Uphoff (2011), which struggled to explain the large suppression and substantial elliptic flow of non-photonic electrons measured at RHIC by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations. The results enable predictions for upcoming RHIC data on charm- and bottom-separated electron spectra and provide a baseline for assessing the need for radiative energy-loss contributions in heavy-quark transport.
