# Covariant chiral nucleon-nucleon contact Lagrangian up to order $\mathcal{O}(q^4)$

> We adopt a covariant version of the naive dimensional analysis and construct the chiral two-nucleon contact Lagrangian constrained by Lorentz, parity, charge conjugation, hermitian conjugation, and chiral symmetries. We show that at $\mathcal{O}(q^0)$, $\mathcal{O}(q^2)$, $\mathcal{O}(q^4)$, where $q$ denotes a generic small quantity, there are 4, 13, and 23 terms, respectively. We find that by performing $1/m_N$ expansions, the covariant Lagrangian reduces to the conventional non-relativistic one, which includes 2, 7, and 15 terms at each corresponding order.

## Key claim

The authors construct a complete set of covariant chiral nucleon-nucleon contact Lagrangian terms up to order O(q^4), finding 4, 13, and 23 independent terms at O(q^0), O(q^2), and O(q^4) respectively, which reduces to the conventional non-relativistic set of 2, 7, and 15 terms upon performing a 1/m_N expansion. The abstract does not emphasize that the counting depends on using the equation of motion to eliminate redundant terms and that the power counting requires subtracting lower-order contributions from nominally higher-order terms to avoid power-counting-breaking contributions.

## Method

The authors adopt a covariant version of naive dimensional analysis to construct the chiral two-nucleon contact Lagrangian, constrained by Lorentz, parity, charge conjugation, hermitian conjugation, and chiral symmetries. They use the Dirac equation of motion to derive linear relations that eliminate redundant terms, and apply four simplification rules to obtain a minimal set of independent operators. The resulting covariant Lagrangian is then expanded in 1/m_N to perform a non-relativistic reduction and compare with the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory results of Epelbaum (2004).

## Key numbers

- At O(q^0): 4 independent covariant terms (compared to 2 in the non-relativistic limit)
- At O(q^2): 13 independent covariant terms (compared to 7 in the non-relativistic limit)
- At O(q^4): 23 independent covariant terms (compared to 15 in the non-relativistic limit)
- The total Lagrangian contains 40 terms fulfilling the specified power counting, with some terms defined as differences (e.g., O_{14} = (1/4m^2)(\bar{\psi} i\overleftrightarrow{\partial}^\mu \psi)(\bar{\psi} i\overleftrightarrow{\partial}_\mu \psi) - (\bar{\psi} \psi)(\bar{\psi} \psi)) to restore proper power counting

## Context

This work extends the relativistic NN contact Lagrangian construction of Girlanda (2010, 2011) up to O(q^4) and provides an alternative basis compared to Petschauer (2013) for the SU(3) case. The covariant Lagrangian enables relativistic chiral effective field theory studies of nucleon-nucleon scattering beyond leading order, building on the leading-order relativistic calculations of Ren (2016) and the hyperon-nucleon work of Li (2016). The non-relativistic reduction confirms consistency with the established heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework of Epelbaum (2004).
