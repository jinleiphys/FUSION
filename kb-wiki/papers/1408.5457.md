# Anomalous magnetic moment of hot quarks, inverse magnetic catalysis and reentrance of chiral symmetry broken phase

> The effect of anomalous magnetic moment of quarks on thermodynamic properties of the chiral condensate is studied, using of a two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model at finite temperature $T$, chemical potential $μ$, and in the presence of a uniform magnetic field $eB$. To this purpose, the Schwinger linear-in-$B$ ansatz for the quark anomalous magnetic moment in term of the nonperturbative Bohr magneton is considered. In a two-dimensional flavor space, it leads to the correction $\hat{T}_{Sch}=\hatκ\hat{Q} eB$ in the energy dispersion relation of quarks. Here, $\hat{Q}$ is the quark charge matrix. We consider three different sets for $\hatκ$, and numerically determine the dependence of the constituent quark mass on $T,μ$ and $eB$ for fixed $\hatκ$. By exploring the complete phase portrait of this model in $T$-$μ$, $μ$-$eB$, and $T$-$eB$ phase spaces for various fixed $eB$, $T$, $μ$ and $\hatκ$, we observe that inverse magnetic catalysis occurs for large enough $\hatκ$. Moreover, in the regime of weak magnetic fields, the phenomenon of reentrance of chiral symmetry broken and restored phases occurs for $T, μ$ and $eB$ dependent $\hatκ$.

## Key claim

Including a sufficiently large anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) for quarks, parameterized by the Schwinger linear-in-\(B\) ansatz with a nonperturbative Bohr magneton, can produce inverse magnetic catalysis (decrease of the critical temperature or chemical potential with increasing magnetic field) in a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and can also cause reentrance of the chiral symmetry broken phase in the weak-field regime when the AMM coupling is made temperature-, chemical-potential-, and magnetic-field-dependent.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at finite temperature \(T\), chemical potential \(\mu\), and uniform magnetic field \(eB\), adding a quark AMM term \(\frac{1}{2}\hat{a}\sigma^{\mu\nu}F_{\mu\nu}\) to the Lagrangian. The AMM is implemented via the Schwinger linear-in-\(B\) ansatz \(T_f = \kappa_f q_f eB\) in the quark energy dispersion, where \(\kappa_f = \alpha_f/(2m)\) and \(m\) is the dynamically generated constituent quark mass. They consider three sets of \(\kappa_f\): two constant sets derived from the constituent quark model (one large, one small) and one set that includes a \(T\)-dependent one-loop perturbative correction. The one-loop effective potential is minimized numerically to determine the constituent quark mass as a function of \(T\), \(\mu\), and \(eB\) for each fixed \(\kappa_f\), and the complete phase portraits in \(T\)-\(\mu\), \(\mu\)-\(eB\), and \(T\)-\(eB\) spaces are mapped.

## Key numbers

- For the large constant AMM set: \(\kappa_u^{(1)} = 0.29016\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(\kappa_d^{(1)} = 0.35986\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- For the small constant AMM set: \(\kappa_u^{(2)} = 0.00995\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(\kappa_d^{(2)} = 0.07975\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- A threshold magnetic field \(eB_t \sim 0.5\ \text{GeV}^{2}\) separates the LLL-dominated regime (above this value) from the regime where de Haas-van Alphen oscillations occur (below this value).
- At RHIC, estimated magnetic field strength \(eB \sim 0.03\ \text{GeV}^{2}\); at LHC, \(eB \sim 0.3\ \text{GeV}^{2}\).
- The constituent quark model yields fixed ratios \(I_u \sim 338\ \text{MeV}\) for the up quark and \(I_d \sim 322\ \text{MeV}\) for the down quark, used to determine constant \(\kappa_f\) values.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the magnetized two-flavor NJL model without quark AMM (Fayazbakhsh and Sadooghi, 2014), which reported magnetic catalysis, de Haas-van Alphen oscillations, and reentrant phases. It is motivated by lattice QCD results from Bali et al. (2011) showing a decrease of the chiral transition temperature with increasing magnetic field (inverse magnetic catalysis), and by the suggestion from Blaizot et al. (2012) that the deviation of the Lande \(g\)-factor from 2 may produce sizable corrections to QCD pressure. The approach contrasts with that of Ferrer et al. (2013, 2014), who dynamically generate the quark AMM in a one-flavor NJL model and find that AMM alone cannot explain inverse magnetic catalysis. The present results enable further study of how quark AMM modifies the QCD phase diagram in the presence of strong magnetic fields relevant to heavy ion collisions and magnetars.
