# Exploring highly-deformed ground states involving the second intruder orbit in Z>50 even-even nuclei

> We present a systematic survey of even-even nuclei with $Z>50$ to identify where a very large prolate configuration driven by the second intruder orbit emerges. Within the energy density functional theory framework, we find in representative cases a pronounced prolate minimum at quadrupole deformation $β_2\approx$ 0.3--0.4. A characteristic feature of these minima is a local enhancement of the hexadecapole ($β_4$) component relative to nearby deformations, which is a clear fingerprint of the $β_2$--$β_4$ coupling expected for the second intruder orbit. Representative comparisons among three Skyrme interactions show a similar appearance of the highly deformed minimum and a local enhancement of $β_4$ at the prolate minimum, indicating qualitative robustness with respect to the interaction. The resulting maps highlight specific heavy nuclei where highly deformed ground states are anticipated.

# Exploring highly-deformed ground states involving the second intruder orbit in Z>50 even-even nuclei

## Key claim
The authors systematically identify where highly-deformed prolate ground states driven by occupation of the second intruder orbit emerge in even-even nuclei with Z>50, finding that the appearance is qualitatively robust across three different Skyrme interactions (SkM*, SLy4, UNEDF1) but caution that for very heavy nuclei like 272No, octupole and basis-size effects could change the energy ordering of competing minima.

## Method
The authors performed systematic axial Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the HFBTHO solver for even-even nuclei from Te (Z=52) to Hg (Z=80) isotopes. Single-particle wave functions were expanded in an axially deformed harmonic-oscillator basis truncated at Nmax=14, with the quadrupole moment Q20 constrained by a standard quadratic term while other multipoles remained unconstrained. Three Skyrme interactions (SkM*, SLy4, UNEDF1) were employed with a standard mixed-type pairing interaction to check interaction dependence.

## Key numbers
- The pronounced prolate minimum appears at quadrupole deformation β2 ≈ 0.3–0.4 for representative cases
- 166Nd has its energy minimum at β2 = 0.33 with β4 = 0.11, and the second intruder orbit [651]1/2 from the spherical 1g9/2 orbit is occupied with occupation probability 0.48
- 138Gd has its energy minimum at β2 = 0.33 with β4 = 0.05, and the second intruder neutron orbit from the spherical 1f7/2 orbit is occupied with occupation probability 0.73
- 272No shows a prolate minimum near β2 ≈ 0.4 connected to the second intruder
- All three Skyrme interactions (SkM*, SLy4, UNEDF1) produce a common large prolate minimum at β2 ≈ 0.35 for 166Nd
- Kinks in β2 and β4 appear at N=106 and 108 for Nd and Sm isotopes, and at N=70 for the proton-rich region

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis of 112Zr (Horiuchi 2023), which demonstrated that partial occupancy of the second intruder orbit can stabilize a very large prolate shape within a self-consistent EDF framework. The systematic survey covers the region Z>50 where the second intruder mechanism may materialize, complementing beyond-mean-field studies in the rare-earth region (Kumar 2023) and heavy nuclei (Guzman 2025) that report stable (β2,β4) trends under reasonable interaction changes. The results provide maps highlighting specific heavy nuclei where highly deformed ground states are anticipated, enabling future experimental measurements of total reaction cross sections and diffuseness parameters to detect the structure change.
