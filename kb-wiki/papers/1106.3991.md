# Chiral Phase Transitions around Black Holes

> In this paper we discuss the possibility that chiral phase transitions, analogous to those of QCD, occur in the vicinity of a black hole. If the black hole is surrounded by a gas of strongly interacting particles, an inhomogeneous condensate will form. We demonstrate this by explicitly constructing self-consistent solutions.

## Key claim
Chiral phase transitions analogous to QCD can occur around a Schwarzschild black hole, producing an inhomogeneous condensate that forms a bubble of restored symmetry near the horizon, but only under the condition of thermal equilibrium described by the Hartle-Hawking vacuum state.

## Method
The authors model strongly interacting fermions using a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type effective field theory in a Schwarzschild black hole background, computing the finite-temperature effective action in the large-N approximation via zeta regularization and heat-kernel expansion. They conformally transform the metric to an ultrastatic form, evaluate the effective action in the rescaled spacetime, add a cocycle correction term, and derive a nonlinear Schrodinger-like equation for the condensate. Numerical solutions are obtained by matching a resummed small-condensate form near the horizon to a truncated large-condensate form at larger radii.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: \( T_c = \sqrt{3} \lambda^{-1/2} \), where \(\lambda\) is the coupling constant.
- Bubble radius estimate: \( r_{\text{bubble}} \sim r_s / \left( 1 - T_{BH}^2 / T_c^2 \right) \), where \( r_s = 2m \) is the Schwarzschild radius and \( T_{BH} = (8\pi m)^{-1} \) is the black hole temperature.

## Context
This work extends the study of phase transitions around black holes from the Higgs model (Hawking 1980, Moss 1984) to strongly interacting fermionic systems analogous to QCD. It builds on the mapping of inhomogeneous chiral phases in flat-space Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Nickel 2009) and on the heat-kernel method for effective actions in curved space (Flachi 2010). The results suggest that a symmetry-restored bubble can form around primordial black holes, potentially affecting the chromosphere debate between Heckler (1995) and MacGibbon (2007), and open the door to studying confinement/deconfinement transitions by coupling to the Polyakov loop (Fukushima 2003).
