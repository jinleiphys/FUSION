# Vector meson dominance and the rho meson

> We discuss the properties of vector mesons, in particular the rho^0, in the context of the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) model. This provides a unified framework to study several aspects of the low energy QCD sector. Firstly, we show that in the HLS model the physical photon is massless, without requiring off field diagonalization. We then demonstrate the equivalence of HLS and the two existing representations of vector meson dominance, VMD1 and VMD2, at both tree level and one loop order. Finally the S matrix pole position is shown to provide a model and process independent means of specifying the rho mass and width, in contrast to the real axis prescription currently used in the Particle Data Group tables.

# Vector meson dominance and the rho meson

## Key claim

The Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) model provides a unified framework that demonstrates the equivalence of the two existing representations of vector meson dominance (VMD1 and VMD2) at both tree level and one-loop order, provided loop-induced photon-vector meson mixings are consistently included; the S-matrix pole position offers a model-independent and process-independent means of specifying the rho mass and width, in contrast to the real-axis prescription used in the Particle Data Group tables.

## Method

The authors construct the HLS Lagrangian with a hidden local symmetry parameter a, generating vector meson masses via the Higgs-Kibble mechanism, and derive the VMD2_a and VMD1_a Lagrangians. They compute the dressed vector propagators using a matrix Dyson-Schwinger equation for the photon-rho system, then evaluate pion and kaon form-factors at tree level and one-loop order, including loop-induced photon-vector meson mixings and vector meson mixing such as rho-omega mixing.

## Key numbers

- The Higgs-Kibble mass for vector mesons is given by M^2 = a g^2 f_pi^2, where f_pi is the pion decay constant and a is the HLS parameter.
- The effective rho-omega mixing function A_{omega pi pi} = -3500 +/- 300 MeV^2.
- The Bando-Kugo-Yamawaki relation m_omega m_phi = m_{K*}^2 holds to 0.1-0.4% for charged or neutral K*, while observed rho and omega masses differ by a few percent despite identical Higgs-Kibble masses.
- For the standard VMD1 form factor fit, an additional term (1+epsilon) = 1.167 +/- 0.008 was needed; the VMD2_a type fit found a/2 = 1.182 +/- 0.008.

## Context

This work extends the Hidden Local Symmetry model of Bando, Kugo, and Yamawaki (1985) and the vector meson dominance framework reviewed by O'Connell, Pearce, Thomas, and Williams. It reconciles the VMD1 and VMD2 representations previously studied by Kuraev, L'vov, and Zhalov, and by Benayoun, Eidelman, O'Connell, and Williams (1998). The demonstration that the S-matrix pole provides a model-independent rho mass and width, analogous to the Z^0 analysis by Hung and Sakurai, enables improved extraction of rho parameters from experimental data and has implications for studies of chiral symmetry restoration in heavy ion collisions.
