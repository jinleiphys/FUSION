# Pairing correlations in N~Z pf-shell nuclei

> We perform Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations to study pair correlations in the ground states of $N=Z$ nuclei with masses A=48-60. We find that $T=1$, $J^π=0^+$ proton-neutron correlations play an important, and even dominant role, in the ground states of odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei, in agreement with experiment. By studying pairing in the ground states of $^{52-58}$Fe, we observe that the isovector proton-neutron correlations decrease rapidly with increasing neutron excess. In contrast, both the proton, and trivially the neutron correlations increase as neutrons are added. We also study the thermal properties and the temperature dependence of pair correlations for $^{50}$Mn and $^{52}$Fe as exemplars of odd-odd and even-even $N=Z$ nuclei. While for $^{52}$Fe results are similar to those obtained for other even-even nuclei in this mass range, the properties of $^{50}$Mn at low temperatures are strongly influenced by isovector neutron-proton pairing. In coexistence with these isovector pair correlations, our calculations also indicate an excess of isoscalar proton-neutron pairing over the mean-field values. The isovector neutron-proton correlations rapidly decrease with temperatures and vanish for temperatures above $T=700$ keV, while the isovector correlations among like nucleons persist to higher temperatures. Related to the quenching of the isovector proton-neutron correlations, the average isospin decreases from 1, appropriate for the ground state, to 0 as the temperature increases.

## Key claim

Shell Model Monte Carlo calculations show that isovector (T=1, Jπ=0+) proton-neutron pairing dominates the ground states of odd-odd N=Z nuclei in the pf-shell (A=48-60), but this dominance is conditional on the absence of neutron excess; the isovector proton-neutron correlations decrease rapidly with increasing neutron number and vanish by 56,58Fe.

## Method

The authors performed Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations within the complete pf-shell model space using the modified Kuo-Brown KB3 residual interaction, with canonical ensemble projections to guarantee correct proton and neutron numbers. Pairing correlations were quantified by the trace of the pair matrix M^J for J=0 (isovector) and J=1 (isoscalar) channels, comparing SMMC results to mean-field values derived from occupation numbers. Ground-state properties were studied at T=0.5 MeV for even-even nuclei and T=0.4 MeV for odd-odd nuclei, while thermal properties of 50Mn and 52Fe were examined at temperatures up to T=2 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The excess of total isovector J=0 pairing over mean-field is 0.42 ± 0.1 in 56Ni (about 13% of total), compared to 2.1 ± 0.1 in 48Cr (about 350% of total).
- Isovector proton-neutron correlations vanish for temperatures above T=700 keV.
- The level density parameter for 52Fe is a(T) ≈ 5.3 ± 1.2 MeV⁻¹ at T ≥ 1 MeV, compared to the empirical value of 6.5 MeV⁻¹.
- The internal excitation energy of 50Mn at T=1.6 MeV is about 8.6 MeV.
- The heat capacity of 50Mn is C(T) ≈ 5.4 ± 1 MeV⁻¹, with level density parameter decreasing as a(T) ~ T⁻¹ between 0.4 and 1.5 MeV.
- The mean-field Gamow-Teller strength for 50Mn increases from 11.1 at T=0.4 MeV to 11.6 at T=2 MeV.
- The mean-field B(M1) strength for 50Mn is 33.6 μN² at T=2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends SMMC studies previously performed for even-even iron isotopes (Dean et al., 1995; Langanke et al., 1996) to N=Z nuclei in the A=50-60 range, and contradicts earlier Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Wolter et al. (1970) and Goodman (1979) which found isoscalar T=0 pairing to dominate over isovector T=1 pairing in these nuclei. The results enable systematic studies of pairing competition in proton-rich nuclei accessible at radioactive ion-beam facilities, and provide benchmarks for simpler pairing models such as the seniority-like isospin-invariant pairing Hamiltonian of Engel et al. (1996).
