# A Consistent Treatment of Final-State Interactions in NuWro Quasielastic Channel

> In this proceeding, I present a modified treatment of final-state interactions (FSI) in quasielastic (QE) lepton-nucleus scattering within the spectra function (SF) framework of the NuWro Monte Carlo generator. Our approach establishes a consistent correspondence between inclusive cross-section calculations and exclusive descriptions of hadron-propagation by combining a convolution-based formalism at the cross-section level with an event-level implementation in which interactions are classified as transparent or non-transparent within the NuWro intranuclear cascade. This unified framework enables realization of FSI effects across inclusive observables and exclusive final states. We demonstrate the impact of this implementation by comparing predictions to both inclusive electron-scattering data and exclusive MicroBooNE measurements of CCQE-dominated observable, showing that the inclusion of FSI leads to a significant improvement in agreement with the data.

# A Consistent Treatment of Final-State Interactions in NuWro Quasielastic Channel

## Key claim

The paper presents a modified treatment of final-state interactions (FSI) in quasielastic lepton-nucleus scattering within the spectral function framework of the NuWro Monte Carlo generator, establishing a consistent correspondence between inclusive cross-section calculations and exclusive descriptions of hadron propagation. The improvement in agreement with data is demonstrated but the deficit at higher transverse momentum suggests the intranuclear cascade in NuWro might need to be stronger.

## Method

The authors combine a convolution-based formalism at the cross-section level with an event-level implementation in which interactions are classified as transparent or non-transparent within the NuWro intranuclear cascade. For transparent events, the struck nucleon propagates without any rescattering; for non-transparent events, the struck nucleon is forced to undergo at least one intranuclear collision by redrawing its propagation and reducing the mean free path until an interaction occurs. The folding function incorporates nuclear transparency and a finite-width function for broadening, along with a shift from the real part of the optical potential.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear transparency profiles and real parts of the optical potentials are implemented in NuWro as functions of nucleon's kinetic energy (presented in figures, no explicit numerical values given in text)
- The fixed step size for hadron propagation in the intranuclear cascade is 0.2 fm
- The spectral function approach is available in NuWro version 25.11 for carbon, oxygen, argon, and iron
- For argon, NuWro employs spectral functions extracted from Jefferson Laboratory E12-14-012 measurements

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach in the NuWro generator (Sobczyk, 2005) by establishing a direct mapping between the inclusive description of FSI, formulated in terms of a folding function (Benhar, 1991), and its realization through the intranuclear cascade model (PhysRevC.86.015505). The framework is tested against inclusive electron-scattering data from Barreau et al. (1983) and exclusive MicroBooNE measurements of CC1p0pi interactions (PhysRevLett.131.101802), enabling consistent treatment of FSI effects across both inclusive observables and exclusive final states for future neutrino-oscillation experiments such as Hyper-Kamiokande and DUNE.
