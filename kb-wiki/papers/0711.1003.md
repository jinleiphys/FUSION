# The ΣΣ interactions in finite-density QCD sum rules

## Key claim

The ΣΣ nuclear potential U_Σ is most likely strongly attractive, possibly about -50 MeV or even more attractive at normal nuclear density, but this prediction depends strongly on the undetermined four-quark condensate parameter f and the strangeness content y of the nucleon, with the potential ranging from approximately -296 MeV to -174 MeV for one parameter set and approximately -(50 ± 70) MeV for another.

## Method

The authors applied the finite-density quantum chromodynamics sum rule (QCDSR) approach to pure Σ matter, extending previous work on Σ hyperons in nuclear matter by substituting the in-medium quark and gluon condensates appropriate for Σ matter. They considered condensates up to dimension 4 and dimension-6 four-quark condensates, with the leading order in-medium condensates derived from chiral perturbation theory, and introduced a parameter f to describe the density dependence of the scalar-scalar four-quark condensate. The baryon scalar self-energy Σ_s and vector self-energy Σ_v were extracted by minimizing a logarithmic measure δ(M^2) over the Borel mass range 1.1 ≤ M^2 ≤ 1.6 GeV^2, and the ΣΣ potential was obtained as U_Σ = Σ_s + Σ_v.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density ρ_0 = (110 MeV)^3, with σ_{πN} = 56 MeV and y = 0.5, the effective mass M*_Σ ranges from 0.551 M_Σ to 0.658 M_Σ for 0 ≤ f ≤ 0.5.
- At normal nuclear density with σ_{πN} = 56 MeV and y = 0.5, the vector self-energy Σ_v ranges from 0.153 M_Σ to 0.173 M_Σ for 0 ≤ f ≤ 0.5.
- At normal nuclear density with σ_{πN} = 56 MeV and y = 0.5, the ΣΣ potential U_Σ ranges from -296 MeV to -174 MeV for 0 ≤ f ≤ 0.5.
- At normal nuclear density with σ_{πN} = 45 MeV and y = 0.2, the effective mass M*_Σ ranges from 0.763 M_Σ to 0.863 M_Σ for 0 ≤ f ≤ 0.5.
- At normal nuclear density with σ_{πN} = 45 MeV and y = 0.2, the vector self-energy Σ_v ranges from 0.140 M_Σ to 0.146 M_Σ for 0 ≤ f ≤ 0.5.
- At normal nuclear density with σ_{πN} = 45 MeV and y = 0.2, the ΣΣ potential U_Σ is -(50 ± 70) MeV.
- The optimal Borel mass region was found to be 1.1 ≤ M^2 ≤ 1.6 GeV^2, with the central value M^2 = 1.4 GeV^2 used in calculations.

## Context

This work extends the finite-density QCDSR approach previously applied to nucleons, Λ hyperons, and Σ hyperons in nuclear matter by Jin and Nielsen (2000s) and the authors' own study of ΛΛ interactions (Li et al., 2007). The predicted strongly attractive ΣΣ potential is consistent with earlier meson-exchange model results (Stoks, 1999) and SU(6) quark model calculations (Nakamoto, 1997), and is found to be much stronger than the ΛΛ potential predicted in the same framework. The results suggest that ΣΣ interactions should play crucial roles in strange nuclear matter with multiple Σ hyperons and that a bound state of double-Σ may exist, though the strong parameter dependence on the four-quark condensate and strangeness content calls for further study of these quantities.
