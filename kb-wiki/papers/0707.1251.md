# Differential elliptic flow prediction at the LHC from parton transport

> We predict how differential elliptic flow v2(pT) changes from RHIC to LHC collision energies (Pb+Pb at 5.5 TeV/nucleon), ASSUMING that the quark-gluon system created has a ``minimal'' shear viscosity eta/s = 1/(4*pi) at both RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) will be **reduced** at the LHC relative to RHIC at a given transverse momentum, assuming the quark-gluon system at both energies has a "minimal" shear viscosity \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\). The hedge is that this prediction relies on the assumption that the system is near the viscosity bound and that the main difference between LHC and RHIC is the typical momentum scale \(T_0\), leading to an approximate scaling \(v_2^{LHC}(p_T) \approx v_2^{RHIC}(p_T T_0^{LHC}/T_0^{RHIC})\).

## Method
The author uses Lorentz-covariant, on-shell Boltzmann transport theory with a \(2\to 2\) scattering rate, applied to massless partons with an \(\epsilon = 3p\) equation of state. The transport is evolved near its local equilibrium limit, with the shear viscosity fixed to \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) by requiring a time-dependent transport cross section \(\sigma_{tr}(\tau) = \sigma_{0,tr} (\tau/0.1\ \text{fm})^{2/3}\) that grows with proper time to maintain constant \(\eta/s\) under longitudinal Bjorken expansion. Initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at impact parameter \(b = 8\) fm are taken from a class of models parameterized by parton density \(dN/d\eta\), formation time \(\tau_0\), and effective temperature \(T_0\), with the ratio of LHC to RHIC temperature scales \(r \equiv T_0^{LHC}/T_0^{RHIC} \approx 1.3-1.5\) estimated from gluon saturation physics via the Gribov-Levin-Rishkin formula.

## Key numbers
- The minimal shear viscosity assumed is \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\), corresponding to a "most perfect fluid" limit.
- At RHIC, with \(dN/d\eta(b=0) = 1000\), the transport cross section at formation time is \(\sigma_{0,tr} \approx 2.7\) mb.
- The ratio of LHC to RHIC temperature scales at \(b = 8\) fm is estimated as \(r \equiv T_0^{LHC}/T_0^{RHIC} \approx 1.3-1.5\).
- The scaling factor in \(v_2(p_T)\) may shift from \(r\) toward \(r^{2/3} \approx 1.2-1.3\) due to earlier thermalization at the LHC.

## Context
This work extends parton transport calculations from RHIC to LHC energies (Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s}=5.5\) TeV per nucleon), contrasting with ideal hydrodynamic expectations that predicted an increase in \(v_2(p_T)\) with energy, as was observed from SPS to RHIC. The prediction of a reduction in \(v_2(p_T)\) at the LHC provides a test of both the minimal viscosity hypothesis and gluon saturation models (e.g., Gribov-Levin-Rishkin formula as applied by Azfar (2001)). The result enables experimental determination of the scaling factor \(r\) to further constrain saturation physics.
