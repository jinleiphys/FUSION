# A note on nonperturbative renormalization of effective field theory

> Within the realm of contact potentials, the key structures intrinsic of nonperturbative renormalization of $T$-matrices are unraveled using rigorous solutions and an inverse form of algebraic Lippmann-schwinger equation. The intrinsic mismatches between effective field theory power counting and nonperturbative divergence structures are shown for the first time to preclude the conventional counterterm algorithm from working in the renormalization of EFT for $NN$ scattering in nonperturbative regimes.

## Key claim

The paper shows for the first time that, beyond leading order in pionless effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon scattering, the conventional counterterm algorithm cannot renormalize the theory in nonperturbative regimes because of intrinsic mismatches between the power-counting constraints on the inverse potential matrix and the divergence structure of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Method

The author works with contact (pionless) EFT potentials for uncoupled and coupled partial-wave channels, converting the Lippmann-Schwinger equation into an algebraic matrix equation using a polynomial expansion of the potentials and T-matrices. The divergent loop integrals are parametrized in a general regularization scheme, and the inverse algebraic Lippmann-Schwinger equation \(\tau^{-1} = \lambda^{-1} - \mathcal{I}\) is used to analyze the renormalization of the T-matrix, focusing on the constraints imposed by EFT power counting on the entries of \(\lambda^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

- For the \(^1S_0\) channel at \(\Delta=2\) (next-to-leading order), the inverse on-shell T-matrix is \(\frac{1}{T} = \mathcal{I}_0 + \frac{N_0}{D_{0;0} + D_{0;1}p^2}\), with \(N_0 = (1 - C_2 J_3)^2\), \(D_{0;0} = C_0 + C_2^2 J_5\), and \(D_{0;1} = C_2(2 - C_2 J_3)\).
- For the \(^1S_0\) channel at \(\Delta=4\), the inverse on-shell T-matrix contains terms \(N_2 = C_4^2 J_3^2\) and \(D_3 = -C_4^2 J_3\), leading to the ratio \(N_2/D_3 = -J_3\) which contains a divergence that cannot be renormalized by counterterms from couplings.
- In the \(^1S_0\) channel at \(\Delta=2\), the inverse potential matrix is \(\lambda^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & C_2^{-1} \\ C_2^{-1} & -C_0 C_2^{-2} \end{pmatrix}\), while the loop integral matrix is \(\mathcal{I} = \begin{pmatrix} -\mathcal{I}_0 & J_3 - \mathcal{I}_0 p^2 \\ J_3 - \mathcal{I}_0 p^2 & J_5 + J_3 p^2 - \mathcal{I}_0 p^4 \end{pmatrix}\), showing that the \((0,0)\) entry of \(\lambda^{-1}\) is zero and cannot absorb the divergence in \(\mathcal{I}_{0,0}\) (i.e., \(J_0\)).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nonperturbative renormalization in pionless EFT, building on earlier studies by Phillips (1997) and by Kaplan (1996) who noted that perturbative counterterm analysis can be misleading. The paper contradicts the expectation that the standard counterterm algorithm from perturbative field theory can be straightforwardly applied to nonperturbative \(NN\) scattering beyond leading order, and it explains why approaches that keep the cutoff finite (e.g., Epelbaum and Meissner) or treat higher-order potentials perturbatively (e.g., Beane, Kaplan, and van Kolck) are pragmatic alternatives. The results enable a clearer understanding of why the constant \(J_0\) (related to the scattering length) must be treated as a universal physical parameter in nonperturbative renormalization.
