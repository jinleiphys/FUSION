# Tortuous ways to the extraction of neutron observables from inclusive lepton scattering

> We analyze new JLAB data for inclusive electron scattering on various targets. Computed and measured total inclusive cross sections in the range $0.3\lesssim x\lesssim 0.95$ show on a logarithmic scale reasonable agreement for all targets. However, closer inspection of the Quasi-Elastic components bares serious discrepancies. EMC ratios which may contain less systematic errors fare the same. The above observations for the new data do not enable the extraction of the magnetic form factor (FF) $G_M^n$ and the Structure Function (SFs) $F_2^n$ of the neutron, although the application of exactly the same analysis to older data had been successful. We add to the above analysis older CLAS collaboration on $F_2^D$. Removing some scattered points, it appears possible to obtain the above mentioned neutron information. We compare our results with others from alternative sources. Particular attention is paid to the A=3 iso-doublet. Present data exist only for $^3$He, but the available input and charge symmetry also enable computations for $^3$H. Their average is the computed iso-scalar part and is compared with the empirical modification of $^3$He towards a fictitious A=3 iso-singlet.

## Key claim

The new JLab data (E03-102, E02-90) for inclusive electron scattering on various targets do not enable extraction of the neutron magnetic form factor $G_M^n$ and structure function $F_2^n$, although the same analysis applied to older data had been successful; however, by incorporating older CLAS data on $F_2^D$ and removing some scattered points, extraction of these neutron observables becomes possible.

## Method

The authors applied a non-perturbative GRS (Gurvitz-Rinat-Schieber) approach using generalized convolution formulas linking nuclear structure functions $F_k^A$ to nucleon structure functions $F_k^{p,n}$, with distribution functions $f^A(x,Q^2)$ computed from spectral functions and including final-state interactions (FSI) via a two-term GRS expansion. For the $A=3$ iso-doublet, separate treatment of proton and neutron distribution functions was performed using spectral functions from the Rome-Pisa group with AV18 and AV18+UR9 interactions, and charge symmetry was exploited to relate $^3$He and $^3$H. The analysis compared computed total inclusive cross sections and EMC ratios with new JLab data (E03-102, E02-90) and older CLAS data on $F_2^D$, applying a criterion requiring a continuous $x$-range in the quasi-elastic region where the ratio $K^{A;NE}/f^A$ varies by less than 10%.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy for new JLab experiments: 5.76 GeV unpolarized electrons scattered over angles $\theta=18^{\circ}, 22^{\circ}, 26^{\circ}, 32^{\circ}, 40^{\circ}, 50^{\circ}$
- Kinematic range analyzed: $0.3\lesssim x\lesssim 0.95$ for total inclusive cross sections
- For $^3$He distribution functions at $Q^2=2.5$ GeV$^2$, B2 proton peak value: 4.041; neutron peak value: 3.483; at $Q^2=10.0$ GeV$^2$, B2 proton peak: 5.394; neutron peak: 4.648
- B2+B3 peak values approximately 4% lower than B2 values for $^3$He
- For $\theta=40^{\circ}$ on $^3$He, $Q^2$ range covered: $2.80\lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 6.12$ GeV$^2$
- EMC ratio crossover $x_{co}\approx (0.2-0.3)$ for most nuclei, but new data show intercept at $x\approx 0.33$
- CLAS data assigned $(2-3)\%$ systematic errors for extraction attempts
- For older data (OD) at $E=4.045$ GeV, empirical inelastic NI parts exceed computed NI by less than 15% in the critical region between QEP and first resonance

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GRS-based analyses of older SLAC and JLab E89-008 data (Rinat et al., 2000s) to new JLab experiments E03-102 and E02-90, finding that the new data produce serious discrepancies in quasi-elastic components that prevent neutron observable extraction, unlike the successful application to older data. The analysis enables future comparisons with the $A=3$ iso-doublet, particularly for $^3$H where no data yet exist, and provides computed predictions for $^3$H structure functions and EMC ratios that can be tested when such data become available (Petratos, Pace et al.). The work also contrasts with the empirical modification approach of Seely et al. (2009) for constructing a fictitious $A=3$ iso-singlet EMC ratio.
