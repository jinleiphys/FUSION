# Indirect observation of an in-medium eta' mass reduction in sqrt(s_{NN})=200 GeV Au+Au collisions

> PHENIX and STAR data on the intercept parameter of the two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation functions in sqrt(s_{NN}) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions are analyzed in terms of different models of hadronic multiplicities. To describe this combined PHENIX and STAR dataset, an in-medium eta' mass reduction of at least 200 MeV is needed, at the 99.9 % confidence level in the considered model class. Such a significant eta' mass modification may indicate the restoration of the U_A(1) symmetry in a hot and dense hadronic matter and the return of the 9th "prodigal" Goldstone boson.

# Indirect observation of an in-medium eta' mass reduction in sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV Au+Au collisions

## Key claim

An in-medium reduction of the eta'(958) meson mass by at least 200 MeV is required at the 99.9% confidence level to describe the combined PHENIX and STAR two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation data, but only within the considered class of models; the claim is indirect, relying on the enhanced production of low-transverse-momentum pions from eta' decays rather than direct observation of the mass shift.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the transverse mass dependence of long-lived resonance multiplicities, incorporating an enhanced eta' production at low transverse momentum due to a possible in-medium eta' mass modification. They fitted the simulated relative intercept parameter lambda*(m_T)/lambda*^max of charged pion Bose-Einstein correlations simultaneously to PHENIX and STAR data from sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV central Au+Au collisions, using an improved Hagedorn formula for the eta' enhancement factor and scanning over model parameters including the in-medium eta' mass m_eta* and the inverse slope parameter B^{-1} for trapped eta' mesons.

## Key numbers

- In-medium eta' mass reduction of at least 200 MeV needed at the 99.9% confidence level (more than 5-sigma effect) to describe both STAR 0-5% central and PHENIX 0-30% central Au+Au data at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- Best fit in-medium eta' mass: 340 +50/-60 +280/-140 +/- 45 MeV (vacuum value is 958 MeV), with the first error statistical, second from resonance model choice and parameters, third from centrality and acceptance systematics
- Best fit in-medium eta' mass values for different resonance models: ALCOR 490 +60/-50 MeV, Kaneta 530 +50/-50 MeV, Letessier 340 +50/-60 MeV, Stachel 340 +50/-60 MeV, UrQMD 400 +50/-40 MeV
- Integrated eta' enhancement factors f_eta' range from 25.6 (Kaneta model) to 67.6 (Letessier and Stachel models)
- Fitted inverse slope parameters B^{-1} range from 42 to 86 MeV across all models
- AMPT 2.11 simulation without eta' mass modification yields chi^2/ndf = 102/13, corresponding to CL = 6.8 x 10^{-16}
- Upper limit of 2% on possible effect of exotic high mass resonances, estimated by switching phi(1020) meson contribution on and off

## Context

This work extends the approach of Vance (2001) who first proposed the observable lambda*(m_T) as sensitive to enhanced eta' multiplicity, and applies it to combined PHENIX and STAR data from RHIC at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. The analysis uses thermal resonance production models from Kaneta (2005), Letessier (2003), Stachel (2004), and the hadronic cascade UrQMD, while excluding AMPT and FRITIOF as unable to describe the data. The results support the prediction by Kapusta (1996) of a reduced eta' mass in the range sqrt(1/3(2 m_K^2 + m_pi^2)) to sqrt(2 m_K^2 - m_pi^2), and are above the Weinberg (1975) lower limit of sqrt(3) m_pi. The observed low-mass dilepton enhancement in PHENIX data (Adare 2009) may be explained by the enhanced eta' and eta production reported here. Future measurements at lower RHIC energies (7.7, 9.2, 11.5, 39, 62.4 GeV) and of the dilepton spectrum could provide further constraints on the onset of partial U_A(1) symmetry restoration.
