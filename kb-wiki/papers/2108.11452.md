# Phenomenological study on correlation between flow harmonics and mean transverse momentum in nuclear collisions

> To assess the properties of the quark-gluon plasma formed in nuclear collisions, the Pearson correlation coefficient between flow harmonics and mean transverse momentum, $ρ\left(v_{n}^{2},\left[p_{\mathrm{T}}\right]\right)$, reflecting the overlapped geometry of colliding atomic nuclei, is measured. $ρ\left(v_{2}^{2},\left[p_{\mathrm{T}}\right]\right)$ was found to be particularly sensitive to the quadrupole deformation of the nuclei. We study the influence of the nuclear quadrupole deformation on $ρ\left(v_{n}^{2},\left[p_{\mathrm{T}}\right]\right)$ in $\rm{Au+Au}$ and $\rm{U+U}$ collisions at RHIC energy using $\rm{AMPT}$ transport model, and show that the $ρ\left(v_{2}^{2},\left[p_{\mathrm{T}}\right]\right)$ is reduced by the prolate deformation $β_2$ and turns to change sign in ultra-central collisions (UCC).

## Key claim

The Pearson correlation coefficient between elliptic flow and mean transverse momentum, ρ(v₂²,[pT]), is reduced by the prolate quadrupole deformation β₂ and can change sign in ultra-central collisions (UCC), while the abstract does not specify that this sign change occurs only for large positive β₂ values.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT transport model (v2.26t5) with string-melting mode and a partonic cross section of 3.0 mb to simulate Au+Au and U+U collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They calculated ρ(vₙ²,[pT]) via a three-particle correlator using all particles within |η| < 2 and 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV/c, with centrality defined by the number of participants Npart. The nuclear quadrupole deformation β₂ was varied to study its influence on the correlation.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au and U+U collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, ρ(v₂²,[pT]) shows the strongest sensitivity to β₂ in the ultra-central collision (UCC) region, where a large positive β₂ leads to a negative ρ(v₂²,[pT]).
- In mid-central and peripheral regions, the magnitudes of ρ(v₂²,[pT]) always decrease with increasing magnitude of β₂.
- ρ(v₃²,[pT]) remains positive for both collision systems, as triangular flow v₃ is purely fluctuation driven and insensitive to nuclear geometric effects.

## Context

This work extends phenomenological studies of nuclear deformation effects on flow observables, following proposals by Bozek (2020) and Giacalone (2020) that ρ(vₙ²,[pT]) is sensitive to nuclear deformation. It directly addresses the significantly negative correlation observed in central U+U collisions by the STAR Collaboration, attributed to the quadrupole deformation β₂. The results enable detailed comparison with experimental data from STAR in Au+Au and U+U collisions to constrain the β₂ values of the uranium nucleus, and complement measurements by ATLAS (2019) and ALICE (2021) in pp, p+Pb, Xe+Xe, and Pb+Pb systems.
