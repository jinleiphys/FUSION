# Directed Flow Indicates a Crossover Deconfinement Transition in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Analysis of directed flow ($v_1$) of protons, antiprotons and pions in heavy-ion collisions is performed in the range of incident energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.7--27 GeV. Simulations have been done within a three-fluid model employing a purely hadronic equation of state (EoS) and two versions of the EoS involving deconfinement transitions: a first-order phase transition and a smooth crossover transition. High sensitivity of the directed flow, especially the proton one, to the EoS is found. The crossover EoS is favored by the most part of considered experimental data. A strong wiggle in the excitation function of the proton $v_1$ slope at the midrapidity obtained with the first-order-phase-transition EoS and a smooth proton $v_1$ with positive midrapidity slope, within the hadronic EoS unambiguously disagree with the data. The pion and antiproton $v_1$ also definitely testify in favor of the crossover EoS. The results obtained with deconfinement EoS's apparently indicate that these EoS's in the quark-gluon sector should be stiffer at high baryon densities than those used in the calculation.

## Key claim

The directed flow of protons, antiprotons, and pions in heavy-ion collisions is highly sensitive to the nuclear equation of state (EoS), and a smooth crossover deconfinement transition is favored by most experimental data, though the crossover EoS fails to reproduce proton directed flow at both low (below 5 GeV) and high (above 20 GeV) collision energies, suggesting the QGP sector of the EoS should be stiffer at high baryon densities than the versions used.

## Method

The authors performed simulations using a three-fluid (3FD) hydrodynamic model for mid-central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at center-of-mass energies from 2.7 to 27 GeV per nucleon pair, employing three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma, and an EoS with a smooth crossover transition. The directed flow coefficient v1(y) was computed as a function of rapidity for protons, antiprotons, and pions with a transverse momentum acceptance of pT < 2 GeV/c, and results were compared to experimental data from the STAR, NA49, and E895 collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Energy range studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.7–27 GeV for Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions
- Impact parameter: b = 6 fm for Au+Au collisions, b = 6.5 fm for Pb+Pb collisions
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV, the calculated proton dv1/dy with crossover EoS was -0.077, compared to the experimental value of -0.0048
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, antiproton multiplicity in mid-central collisions was approximately 1 within deconfinement scenarios and approximately 3 within the hadronic scenario
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV, approximately 80% of antiprotons near midrapidity originate from the baryon-free (fireball) fluid
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV, near midrapidity approximately 50% of pions come from baryon-rich fluids and 50% from the baryon-free fluid

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Konchakovski et al. (2014) within the 3FD model, adding results for a first-order phase transition EoS and including AGS and SPS data alongside new STAR beam energy scan results. The study contrasts with the hybrid approach of the Frankfurt group (2014), which found no sensitivity of directed flow to the EoS because the initial interpenetration stage was described with hadronic UrQMD dynamics for all scenarios. The results enable further refinement of the QGP sector of equations of state at finite baryon densities, as the directed flow data indicate the QGP EoS should be stiffer than the versions used in this calculation.
