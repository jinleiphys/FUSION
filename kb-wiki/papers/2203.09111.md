# Electromagnetic moments of scandium isotopes and $N=28$ isotones in the distinctive $0f_{7/2}$ orbit

> The electric quadrupole moment of $^{49}$Sc was measured by collinear laser spectroscopy at CERN-ISOLDE to be $Q_{\rm s}=-0.159(8)$ $e$b, and a nearly tenfold improvement in precision was reached for the electromagnetic moments of $^{47,49}$Sc. The single-particle behavior and nucleon-nucleon correlations are investigated with the electromagnetic moments of $Z=21$ isotopes and $N=28$ isotones as valence neutrons and protons fill the distinctive $0f_{7/2}$ orbit, respectively, located between magic numbers, 20 and 28. The experimental data are interpreted with shell-model calculations using an effective interaction, and ab-initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations based on chiral interactions. These results highlight the sensitivity of nuclear electromagnetic moments to different types of nucleon-nucleon correlations, and establish an important benchmark for further developments of theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The electric quadrupole moment of 49Sc was measured for the first time, and the precision of electromagnetic moments for 47,49Sc was improved nearly tenfold; however, the theoretical calculations (both shell-model and ab-initio VS-IMSRG) systematically underestimate magnetic moments along the Z=21 isotopic chain and for N=28 isotones, and the quadrupole moment trends are only partially reproduced, indicating that the N=28 shell gap may be too small in the GXPF1A interaction and that missing E2 correlations exist in the ab-initio calculations.

## Method

Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed at CERN-ISOLDE using two independent setups (COLLAPS and CRIS) to measure hyperfine structures of Sc isotopes. The COLLAPS setup used a frequency-doubled continuous-wave Ti:Sapphire laser at 364.3 nm to excite an ionic transition (3d4s 3D1 to 3d4p 3F2), while the CRIS setup used pulsed lasers at 246.8 nm and 532 nm for atomic excitation and ionization. Magnetic moments were extracted from A parameters using the reference 45Sc, and quadrupole moments were obtained from B(3F2) parameters using the recommended Qs(45Sc). The experimental results were compared with shell-model calculations using the GXPF1A effective interaction and with ab-initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations based on chiral interactions (NNLOsat and DeltaNNLOGO).

## Key numbers

- Electric quadrupole moment of 49Sc: Qs = -0.159(8) e b (first measurement)
- Magnetic moment of 47Sc: mu = +5.209(2) mu_N (COLLAPS, improved precision)
- Magnetic moment of 49Sc: mu = +5.539(4) mu_N (COLLAPS) and +5.540(11) mu_N (CRIS)
- Magnetic moment of 46Sc: mu = +3.035(2) mu_N (COLLAPS) and +3.036(6) mu_N (CRIS)
- Quadrupole moment of 47Sc: Qs = -0.200(6) b (COLLAPS)
- Quadrupole moment of 46Sc: Qs = +0.124(5) b (COLLAPS)
- Reference quadrupole moment of 45Sc: Qs = -0.220(2) b
- Reference magnetic moment of 45Sc: mu = +4.75400(8) mu_N

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of electromagnetic moments in the f7/2 orbit region, following earlier measurements on Sc isotopes at IGISOL (Avgoulea 2011) and ABMR/NMR experiments (47Sc, 49Sc references). The results provide a textbook confirmation of the seniority scheme for N=28 isotones, where quadrupole moments follow a linear trend crossing zero at half-filling of the pi f7/2 orbit. The data serve as benchmarks for ab-initio VS-IMSRG calculations, which had previously been applied mainly in the sd shell (e.g., for Ca and Al isotopes) and are here extended to the pf shell for the first time. The discrepancies between experiment and theory motivate further development of effective interactions for the calcium region in the sdpf model space and improvements to many-body methods including three-body operator truncations and meson-exchange currents.
