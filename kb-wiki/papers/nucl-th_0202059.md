# Anti-Baryon Puzzle in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The evolution of (non-strange) antibaryon abundances in the hadronic phase of central heavy-ion collisions is studied within a thermal equilibrium framework, based on the well-established picture of subsequent chemical and thermal freezeout. Due to large annihilation cross sections, antiprotons are, a priori, not expected to comply with this scheme. However, we show that a significant regeneration of their abundance occurs upon inclusion of the inverse reaction of multipion fusion, $n_ππ\to p\bar p$ (with $n_π$=5-6), necessary to ensure detailed balance. Especially at SPS energies, the build-up of large pion-chemical potentials between chemical and thermal freezeout reinforces this mechanism, rendering the $\bar p/p$ ratio in reasonable agreement with the observed one (reflecting chemical freezeout). Explicit solutions of the pertinent rate equation, which account for chemical off-equilibrium effects, corroborate this explanation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed antiproton-to-proton ratio in central heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies, which appears consistent with chemical freezeout at high temperature, can be reconciled with the hadronic phase despite large annihilation cross sections, provided one includes the inverse reaction of multipion fusion (5-6 pions) and accounts for nonzero pion-chemical potentials that build up between chemical and thermal freezeout.

## Method

The authors formulate a thermal rate equation for antiproton density in an expanding hadron gas, incorporating detailed balance between antiproton annihilation on baryons and the inverse reaction of multipion fusion (n_π π → p̄p). They use experimental data on pion multiplicities from p̄p annihilation at rest (mean of 5.06 pions, width 0.9) and the energy dependence of the annihilation cross section, combined with temperature-dependent pion- and nucleon-chemical potentials extracted from fireball evolution models at SPS energies. The rate equation is solved explicitly for the antiproton density evolution from chemical to thermal freezeout, including volume expansion effects.

## Key numbers

- Measured p̄/p ratio in Pb(158 AGeV)+Pb collisions: (5.5 ± 1)% (NA44 experiment)
- Chemical freezeout parameters at SPS: (T_ch, μ_B^ch) ≈ (170, 260) MeV
- Thermal freezeout parameters at SPS: (T_th, μ_N^th) ≈ (120, 400) MeV
- Naive p̄/p ratio using thermal freezeout conditions: exp(-2μ_N^th/T_th) ≈ 0.1%, a factor ~50 below the measured value
- Average pion multiplicity in p̄p annihilation at rest: ⟨n_π⟩ = 5.06 with width δn_π = 0.9
- Pion-chemical potentials at thermal freezeout: μ_π^th ≈ 60-80 MeV
- Chemical relaxation time for antiprotons at T=150 MeV: about 3 fm/c
- Effective chemical freezeout temperature for antiprotons from off-equilibrium solution: close to 130 MeV

## Context

This work extends the sequential freezeout picture of hadronic matter in heavy-ion collisions, previously established by studies of hadro-chemical equilibration (e.g., Braun-Munzinger et al., 1999; Becattini et al., 2000) and thermal freezeout with collective flow (e.g., Stock, 1999). It directly addresses the "antiproton puzzle" that arose from transport simulations (e.g., ARC and UrQMD collaborations, 1997-2000), which required ad-hoc assumptions such as in-medium suppression of annihilation or enhanced production via increased string tension. The regeneration mechanism via multipion fusion with finite pion-chemical potentials, first proposed by Rapp and Shuryak (2001), is here corroborated by explicit rate-equation solutions. The work enables future application to RHIC conditions where baryon and antibaryon densities become comparable, and suggests the balance function technique (Bass, Danielewicz, Pratt, 2001) as a test of the regeneration mechanism.
