# Spin Asymmetries in Electron-jet Production at the EIC

> We investigate all the possible spin asymmetries that can occur in back-to-back electron-jet production with hadron observed inside a jet in electron-proton collisions. We derive the factorization formalism for all spin asymmetries and perform phenomenological studies for the future electron ion collider kinematics. We illustrate that the back-to-back electron-jet production opens up new opportunities to study transverse momentum dependent fragmentation functions and distribution functions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that back-to-back electron-jet production with a hadron observed inside the jet at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) can separately constrain transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) and transverse momentum dependent fragmentation functions (TMDFFs) by simultaneously measuring the electron-jet transverse momentum imbalance and the hadron transverse momentum relative to the jet axis, which is not possible in standard semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering where the two functions are always convolved.

## Method

The authors derive the factorization formalism for all possible spin asymmetries in the process \(p(S_A) + e(\lambda_e) \rightarrow e + (\text{jet } h(z_h, \boldsymbol{j}_\perp, \boldsymbol{S}_{h\perp})) + X\) at leading twist, where the incoming proton and electron and the outgoing hadron inside the jet can have general polarizations. They perform phenomenological studies for EIC kinematics by computing the relevant structure functions and asymmetries using existing extractions of the Boer-Mulders function \(h_1^\perp\) from Barone (2009), the Collins fragmentation function \(H_1^\perp\) from Kang (2015), and the polarizing TMDFF \(D_{1T}^{\perp}\) from Callos (2020), incorporating transverse momentum dependent (TMD) evolution.

## Key numbers

*   For unpolarized \(\pi^+\) production in the jet, the \(\cos(\phi_q - \hat{\phi}_h)\) spin asymmetry \(A_{UU,U}^{\cos(\phi_q - \hat{\phi}_h)}\) is negative with magnitude approximately 1%.
*   For unpolarized \(\pi^-\) production in the jet, the \(\cos(\phi_q - \hat{\phi}_h)\) spin asymmetry is positive with magnitude approximately 3%.
*   For transversely polarized \(\Lambda\) production in the jet, the \(\sin(\hat{\phi}_\Lambda - \hat{\phi}_{S_\Lambda})\) asymmetry \(A_{UU,T}^{\sin(\hat{\phi}_\Lambda - \hat{\phi}_{S_\Lambda})}\) increases up to 3% at \(j_\perp = 0.4\) GeV and then gradually drops to around 2.5% for the EIC kinematics with \(\sqrt{s}=89\) GeV, \(0.1 < y < 0.9\), and \(0.15 < x < 0.20\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for back-to-back electron-jet production at the EIC, previously studied by Liu (2018) and Arratia (2020), to include all possible spin asymmetries with hadron-in-jet measurements. It enables independent extraction of TMDPDFs and TMDFFs, which in standard semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) as formulated by Bacchetta (2006) requires auxiliary processes like dihadron production in electron-positron annihilation and Drell-Yan production in proton-proton collisions for separate constraints.
