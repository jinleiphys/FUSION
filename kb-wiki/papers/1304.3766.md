# Simultaneous quadrupole and octupole shape phase transitions in Thorium

> The evolution of quadrupole and octupole shapes in Th isotopes is studied in the framework of nuclear Density Functional Theory. Constrained energy maps and observables calculated with microscopic collective Hamiltonians indicate the occurrence of a simultaneous quantum shape phase transition between spherical and quadrupole-deformed prolate shapes, and between non-octupole and octupole-deformed shapes, as functions of the neutron number. The nucleus $^{224}$Th is closest to the critical point of a double phase transition. A microscopic mechanism of this phenomenon is discussed in terms of the evolution of single-nucleon orbitals with deformation.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first microscopic study of a simultaneous double quantum shape phase transition in Thorium isotopes, where both a spherical-to-prolate quadrupole transition and a non-octupole-to-octupole transition occur as functions of neutron number, with the nucleus \(^{224}\)Th being closest to the critical point of this double phase transition.

## Method

The authors employed nuclear covariant density functional theory (CDFT) using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) implementation with the PC-PK1 functional and BCS pairing. They performed constrained RMF+BCS calculations to generate potential energy surfaces (PESs) as functions of both quadrupole and octupole deformation parameters. The self-consistent solutions were then used to determine the parameters of a five-dimensional (5D) quadrupole collective Hamiltonian and a two-dimensional (2D) axially-symmetric quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian, which were diagonalized to obtain excitation spectra and collective wave functions for calculating observables.

## Key numbers

- The energy ratio \(E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)\) in \(^{224}\)Th is experimentally close to the value of 2.9 predicted by the X(5) model of quadrupole quantum phase transitions.
- The PES contours in Fig. 1 are plotted with a separation of 0.5 MeV between neighboring contours.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta_2\) at the equilibrium minimum for \(^{224}\)Th is 0.16.
- The octupole deformation parameter \(\beta_3\) is varied from 0 to 0.3 along the constant \(\beta_2 = 0.16\) path in the single-nucleon level analysis.
- The flat prolate minimum in \(^{224}\)Th extends in the interval \(0 \leq \beta \leq 0.2\) of the axial deformation parameter.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear shape phase transitions, which have mostly focused on quadrupole degrees of freedom (e.g., the X(5) model by Iachello (2001) for spherical-to-prolate transitions), and applies them to the less-studied case of simultaneous quadrupole and octupole transitions. The results are consistent with earlier phenomenological geometric models by Bonatsos et al. (2005), Lenis et al. (2006), and Bizzeti and Bizzeti-Sona (2004, 2008), as well as with experimental systematics of octupole correlations in the actinide region (Butler, 1996). The study enables future investigations of double shape quantum phase transitions in other finite quantum systems with quadrupole and octupole collective degrees of freedom.
