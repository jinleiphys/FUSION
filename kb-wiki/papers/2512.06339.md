# The influence of heavy quark potential on quarkonium production in quark-gluon plasma

> Remler formalism is the Wigner projection of a two particle state to bound states, which is carried out when the bound state begins to exist or when one of the two particles scatters in medium. This method has been successfully applied to quarkonium production in box simulations and in heavy-ion collisions. In this study this method is extended to strongly bound states with considerable binding energies by taking into account the potential energy between heavy quark pairs in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). We find that an attractive heavy-quark potential enhances quarkonium production and the results are consistent with those from the statistical model in box simulations, if a proper spatial cutoff is introduced to the potential to mimic quantum effects.

## Key claim

Extending the Remler formalism to include an attractive heavy-quark potential enhances quarkonium production in box simulations, and the results become consistent with the statistical model only when a proper spatial cutoff is introduced to the potential to mimic quantum effects; without this cutoff, the Wigner projection overestimates the number density at low temperatures (e.g., by a factor of 1.5 for J/psi and 3.0 for Upsilon(1S) at T_c).

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation with a lattice-QCD-based free-energy potential to obtain the wavefunction, binding energy, and Wigner function for J/psi and Upsilon(1S) as functions of temperature. They then compute the ratio of quarkonium number density from the Wigner projection (Remler formalism) to that from the statistical model, both with and without including the potential energy term exp[-V(r)/T] in the heavy-quark pair phase-space distribution, using a lower cutoff at r = 1/mu to avoid infrared divergence from the Coulomb-like potential.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of Wigner projection to statistical model for J/psi at T_c drops to 0.22 without the potential, but rises to 1.5 when the potential is included.
- The ratio for Upsilon(1S) at T_c drops to 3% without the potential, but rises to 3.0 when the potential is included.
- The charm quark effective mass required for smooth density across the phase transition is m_c approximately 1.8 GeV at T_c approximately 0.16 GeV; using m_c = 1.55 GeV instead increases the open charm number by a factor of 4.
- The probability for the ground state of a 1D simple harmonic oscillator from the Wigner projection at T=0 is 2 (nonphysical), while quantum statistics gives 1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous applications of the Remler formalism to quarkonium production in box simulations (Song 2023) and heavy-ion collisions (Song 2017, Zhao 2023, Song 2023), which assumed free heavy quarks without potential. It also builds on studies by Villar (2022) that included heavy quark potential but did not systematically treat color combinations. The results show that including the potential compensates for the underestimate from binding energy (the factor e^{-epsilon_0/T}), but the semi-classical approach overestimates bound-state yields at low temperature due to neglect of the uncertainty principle, motivating the introduction of a spatial cutoff. This enables future application of the Remler formalism with potential to realistic heavy-ion collision simulations.
