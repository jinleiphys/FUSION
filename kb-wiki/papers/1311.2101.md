# Kurtoses and high order cumulants: Insights from resummed perturbation theory

> Cumulants of conserved charges provide important information about the physics of the quark-gluon plasma around the phase transition region, as they are by construction sensitive to changes in the degrees of freedom of the system. In this brief proceedings contribution, I report on recent results for two such quantities from two different improved perturbative frameworks, as well as discuss their relevance for heavy ion experiments.

# Kurtoses and high order cumulants: Insights from resummed perturbation theory

## Key claim
Resummed perturbation theory (dimensional reduction and hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory) can describe lattice data for kurtoses of light quark and baryon numbers down to 2-3 times the pseudo-critical temperature, but sixth order cumulants remain challenging because their weak coupling expansions only start at order g^3, and the two frameworks give opposite signs for these quantities.

## Method
The author computed kurtoses (ratios of fourth to second order cumulants) and sixth order cumulants for light quark and baryon numbers using two resummed perturbative frameworks: dimensional reduction (DR) at four-loop level, where the EQCD parameters are not expanded in the gauge coupling g, and hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) at one-loop level (with a three-loop HTLpt result also shown for baryon kurtosis). The renormalization scale was set to approximately 2πT and varied by a factor of two, with the Fastest Apparent Convergence scheme applied to the EQCD gauge coupling, and Λ_QCD fixed using the lattice result α_s(1.5 GeV)=0.326.

## Key numbers
- Pseudo-critical temperature of the deconfinement transition: T_c = 154 ± 9 MeV
- For three flavors, Λ_QCD = 176 MeV (one-loop) and 283 MeV (two-loop); for two flavors, Λ_QCD = 204 MeV (one-loop) and 324 MeV (two-loop)
- Central values for the FAC scale: 1.445 × 2πT (two flavors) and 1.291 × 2πT (three flavors)
- Lattice data for kurtoses at T ≈ 300-400 MeV agree with one-loop HTLpt band, while the DR result reproduces the overall trend of lattice data better across the full temperature range
- Sixth order cumulants: DR predictions are positive for all displayed temperatures, while HTLpt results are consistently negative; lattice data from SimonTwoFlavors and Peterchi6 appear to favor negative values above the transition region

## Context
This work extends the application of resummed perturbation theory to cumulants of conserved charges, which are sensitive probes of changes in degrees of freedom across the QCD phase transition. The DR approach builds on dimensional reduction (Braaten and Nieto, 1996; Kajantie et al., 1997) and earlier work on the pressure at zero chemical potential (Mikko and York, 2008), while HTLpt follows from the hard-thermal-loop effective action (Frenkel and Taylor, 1990; Braaten and Pisarski, 1990) and its application to the QCD pressure (Jens, Mike, and Braaten, 2000). The results are compared with lattice data from BNL-B and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations. The findings suggest that a weakly interacting quasiparticle picture works well for the fermionic sector down to 2-3 T_c, and these quantities can inform analysis of heavy ion collision data from RHIC, LHC, and future FAIR and NICA experiments.
