# Phases of spin- and mass-imbalanced ultracold Fermi gases in harmonic traps

> We analyze the phase structure of mass- and spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases in harmonic traps. To this end, we employ Density Functional Theory in the local density approximation. Depending on the values of the control parameters measuring mass and spin imbalance, we observe that three regions exist in the trap, namely: a superfluid region at the center, surrounded by a mixed region of resonantly interacting spin-up and spin-down fermions, and finally a fully polarized phase surrounding the previous two regions. We also find regimes in the phase diagram where the existence of a superfluid region at the center of the trap is not energetically favored. We point out the limitations of our approach at the present stage, and call for more detailed (ab initio) studies of the equation of state of uniform, mass-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases.

## Key claim

Using Density Functional Theory in the local density approximation, the authors find that a superfluid region exists at the center of the trap for certain values of mass and spin imbalance, but this region disappears for mass-imbalance parameters below approximately -0.08, and the authors caution that their results rely on an approximate equation of state and call for more detailed ab initio studies.

## Method

The authors constructed an energy density functional in the local density approximation (LDA) following the approach of Ref. [RLS], using a mean-field-inspired Bertsch parameter for the superfluid phase and a variational ansatz for the normal phase equation of state parameterized by an energy gain parameter eta, an effective mass m*, and a quasiparticle interaction parameter B. They minimized the functional with respect to the densities and the superfluid core radius to determine density profiles and the critical polarization at which the superfluid core disappears, solving the resulting equations for various values of the mass-imbalance parameter m-bar and trap-asymmetry parameter alpha.

## Key numbers

- For the mass-balanced case (m-bar = 0), the ratio of spin-down to spin-up density at the superfluid boundary is x(R_S) approximately 0.54, and the critical polarization P_c is approximately 0.68.
- For m-bar = 0, using the alternative effective mass m*/m = 1.09 from Monte Carlo studies yields P_c approximately 0.77, consistent with Ref. [RLS] and the experimental value of approximately 0.75 from MIT experiments (Zwierlein et al., 2006; Schunck et al., 2008).
- The critical mass-imbalance parameter below which no superfluid core exists is m-bar_{c,<} approximately -0.08 for B = 0.14, and approximately -0.001 for B = 0.
- The Bertsch parameter used in the superfluid equation of state is xi_S approximately 0.42, based on earlier Monte Carlo results (Carlson et al., 2003; Astrakharchik et al., 2004), while more recent estimates place it at about 0.375 (Endres et al., 2012).
- For m-bar = 0, the energy gain parameter eta is approximately -0.60 and the effective mass ratio m*/m is approximately 1.17 from the variational approach of Ref. [CRLC].

## Context

This work extends the LDA-based DFT approach of Ref. [RLS] for spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases to include mass imbalance, and complements mean-field studies of mass-imbalanced systems (e.g., Baarsma and Stoof; Roscher et al., 2013). The results provide predictions for the phase diagram of trapped mass- and spin-imbalanced Fermi gases that could guide future experiments (e.g., with Li-6 and K-40 mixtures) and motivate improved DFT functionals beyond LDA, as well as ab initio Monte Carlo studies that currently suffer from the sign problem in this regime (Drut, 2008; Braun et al., 2012; Roscher et al., 2013).
