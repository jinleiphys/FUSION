# Transverse-Mass Effective Temperature in Heavy-Ion Collisions from AGS to SPS

> Transverse-mass spectra in Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions in incident energy range from 2A to 160A GeV are analyzed within the model of 3-fluid dynamics. It is shown that dynamical description of freeze-out, accepted in this model, naturally explains the incident energy behavior of inverse-slope parameters of these spectra observed in experiment. Simultaneous reproduction of the inverse-slopes of all considered particles (protons, pions and kaons) suggests that these particles belong to the same hydrodynamic flow at the instant of their freeze-out.

## Key claim

The "step-like" behavior of the inverse-slope parameter (effective temperature) of kaon transverse-mass spectra as a function of incident energy, previously interpreted as a signal of deconfinement, can be explained purely by the dynamics of freeze-out within a 3-fluid hydrodynamic model using a single constant freeze-out energy density parameter, without requiring a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors analyzed central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions from 2A to 160A GeV using the 3-fluid dynamics (3FD) model with a simple hadronic equation of state (EoS) involving a density-dependent mean field. They calculated transverse-mass spectra of protons, pions, and kaons at mid-rapidity and extracted inverse-slope parameters T by fitting the spectra with an exponential function (m_T)^λ exp(-m_T/T), using λ=0 for a purely exponential fit and λ=-1 for pions and λ=1 for protons to match experimental conventions. The freeze-out was implemented via a critical energy density criterion (ε < ε_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm^3) with an anti-bubble prescription that removes matter from the surface of the expanding system.

## Key numbers

- The critical freeze-out energy density parameter was set to ε_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm^3 for all incident energies above 2A GeV, with lower values of 0.3 GeV/fm^3 at 2A GeV and 0.2 GeV/fm^3 at 1A GeV.
- The actual average freeze-out energy density ⟨ε_out⟩ saturates at approximately ε_frz/2 = 0.2 GeV/fm^3 at SPS energies.
- The freeze-out baryon density n_frz exhibits a maximum at incident energies of E_lab = 10A–30A GeV.
- At the top SPS energy (160A GeV), the 3FD code required 7.5 GB of RAM memory for central Pb+Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic simulations by Hama (2004) that reproduced the kaon effective temperature behavior but required an incident-energy-dependent freeze-out temperature, effectively translating the puzzle into one of freeze-out temperatures. It contradicts the assumption by Gorenstein (2003) and Mohanty (2003) that the saturation of kaon inverse slopes is directly associated with the deconfinement phase transition, and it explains why microscopic transport models such as those by Bratkovskaya and Wagner failed to reproduce the observed behavior. The results enable further investigation into whether the freeze-out parameter ε_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm^3 itself has a physical interpretation related to the phase transition, and they support the view that heavy nuclear systems exhibit hydrodynamic motion during expansion, consistent with recent success of the GiBUU model by Larionov (2007) that included three-body interactions.
