# Nucleon Polarisabilities from Deuteron Compton Scattering, and Its Lessons for Chiral Power Counting

> A brief review of Compton scattering off the deuteron in Chiral Effective Field Theory, including gauge invariance and the correct Thomson limit. We model-independently determine the iso-scalar, spin-independent dipole polarisabilities of the nucleon at zero energy from all Compton data below 100 MeV as α_{E1}^s=11.3+-0.7_{stat}\+-0.6_{Baldin}+-1_{theory}, β_{M1}^s=3.2-+0.7_{stat}+-0.6_{Baldin}+-1_{theory}, each in 10^{-4} fm^3. Proton and neutron polarisabilities are therefore identical within (predominantly statistical) errors.

## Key claim

The paper model-independently determines the iso-scalar, spin-independent dipole polarisabilities of the nucleon from all deuteron Compton scattering data below 100 MeV, finding that proton and neutron polarisabilities are identical within predominantly statistical errors, but with a theoretical uncertainty of ±1 (in 10⁻⁴ fm³) that is comparable to the statistical precision.

## Method

The analysis uses Chiral Effective Field Theory (ChiEFT) with explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom to describe elastic deuteron Compton scattering γd → γd for photon energies between zero and 130 MeV. A Green's function approach embeds all NN-rescattering processes between photon absorption and emission to guarantee the correct Thomson limit, with only two energy-independent parameters from short-distance physics as unknowns up to next-to-leading order. The iso-scalar Baldin sum rule serves as input to constrain the fit.

## Key numbers

- Iso-scalar electric dipole polarisability: α_{E1}^s = 11.3 ± 0.7_stat ± 0.6_Baldin ± 1_theory (in 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Iso-scalar magnetic dipole polarisability: β_{M1}^s = 3.2 ∓ 0.7_stat ± 0.6_Baldin ± 1_theory (in 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Proton electric dipole polarisability from same method: α_{E1}^p = 11.0 ± 1.4_stat ± 0.4_Baldin ± 1_theory (in 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Proton magnetic dipole polarisability: β_{M1}^p = 2.8 ∓ 1.4_stat ± 0.4_Baldin ± 1_theory (in 10⁻⁴ fm³)
- Analysis covers photon energies from zero to 130 MeV, with data below 100 MeV used for the fit

## Context

This work extends previous ChiEFT analyses of nucleon polarisabilities by including explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom, which resolves the "SAL-puzzle" where earlier analyses of SAL data at 95 MeV extracted vastly varying nucleon polarisabilities. The approach contrasts with a ChiEFT fit at O(p⁴) by Judith (2006) which gave α^s_{E1}=11.5, β^s_{M1}=0.3 (in 10⁻⁴ fm³), showing the importance of the Δ(1232) contribution. The results enable future more precise extractions from upcoming MAXlab (Lund) data to probe proton-neutron differences.
