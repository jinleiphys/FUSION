# The Relativistic Three-Body Bound State in a 3D Formulation

> Background: The relativistic three-body problem has a long tradition in few-nucleon physics. Calculations of the triton binding energy based on the solution of the relativistic Faddeev equation in general lead to a weaker binding than the corresponding non-relativistic calculation. Purpose: In this work we solve for the three-body binding energy as well as the wave function and its momentum distribution. The effect of the different relativistic ingredients are studied in detail. Method: Relativistic invariance is incorporated within the framework of Poincar{é} invariant quantum mechanics. The relativistic momentum-space Faddeev equation is formulated and directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial-wave decomposition. Results: The relativistic calculation gives a three-body binding energy which is about 3% smaller than its non-relativistic counterpart. In the wave function, relativistic effects are manifested in the Fermi motion of the spectator particle. Conclusions: Our calculations show that though the overall relativistic effects in the three-body bound state are small, individual effects by themselves are not necessarily small and must be taken into account consistently.

## Key claim

The relativistic three-body binding energy is about 3% smaller than its non-relativistic counterpart, but this small net effect arises from individual relativistic contributions that are not themselves small and must be treated consistently.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic momentum-space Faddeev equation for three identical nucleons using direct integration in terms of momentum vectors without partial-wave decomposition. Relativistic invariance was incorporated within Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics, with the relativistic two-body interaction constructed to be phase-equivalent to the non-relativistic Malfliet-Tjon V potential. The Faddeev equation was solved as an eigenvalue problem by varying the three-body mass eigenvalue until the eigenvalue of the kernel reached unity.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic three-body binding energy is about 3% smaller than the non-relativistic binding energy for the Malfliet-Tjon V potential.
- The non-relativistic two-body interaction used is the Malfliet-Tjon V potential, with parameters: repulsive strength V_R, attractive strength V_A, repulsive range mu_R, and attractive range mu_A (specific numerical values of these parameters are not given in the truncated text).
- The relativistic Faddeev equation was solved in a coordinate system where the spectator momentum vector is parallel to the z-axis and the Jacobi momentum is in the x-z plane, with integration over the magnitude of the spectator momentum from 0 to infinity.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic three-body calculations of Glöckle, Coester, and Lee (1986), who used the same Malfliet-Tjon V interaction but with an approximate relativistic interaction and partial-wave expansion truncated to s-waves, finding a small decrease in binding energy. It also relates to the fully converged partial-wave calculations of Kamada et al. (2008) with realistic interactions. The direct integration method used here, previously applied to scattering calculations by Lin et al. (2007, 2008), enables future few-GeV scale scattering calculations with realistic interactions and provides the first direct comparison of relativistic and non-relativistic bound-state wave functions.
