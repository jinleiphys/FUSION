# The unexpected role of D waves in low-energy neutral pion photoproduction

> It has been commonly assumed that low-energy neutral pion photoproduction from the proton can be described accounting only for S and P waves, and that higher partial waves are irrelevant. We have found that this assumption is not correct and that the inclusion of D waves is necessary to obtain a reliable extraction of the $E_{0+}$ multipole from experimental data. This is due in large measure to the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry in QCD which leads to very small S-wave contributions. This makes the usual partial wave expansion less accurate and although D waves are small, their contribution is enhanced through the interference with P waves, which compromises the S-wave extraction from data if D waves are not taken into account. In our work we have used Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory to one loop, and up to ${\cal O}(q^4)$, to account for the S and P waves, while D waves are added in an almost model-independent way using standard Born terms and vector mesons. We also show that higher partial waves do not play an important role.

## Key claim

The common assumption that low-energy neutral pion photoproduction from the proton can be described using only S and P waves is incorrect; D waves must be included to reliably extract the \(E_{0+}\) multipole from experimental data, because the S-wave contribution is very small due to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, and D-wave interference with P waves is enhanced.

## Method

The authors used Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBCHPT) to one loop and up to \(\mathcal{O}(q^4)\) to compute the S and P waves, while D waves were added using standard Born terms and vector meson (\(\omega\) and \(\rho\)) exchange in an almost model-independent way. They performed fits to 171 differential cross sections and 7 photon asymmetries from MAMI data (energy range from threshold up to 166 MeV) using either only S and P waves (SP fit) or S, P, and D waves (SPD fit), employing a hybrid optimization code based on a genetic algorithm.

## Key numbers

- The D-wave/P-wave interference contribution \(\frac{27}{5}M_{2+}\) is of the order of 6% of the \(E_{0+}\) multipole above the \(\pi^+\) production threshold.
- At the charged pion threshold, the impact of D waves on the extracted \(E_{0+}\) multipole is nearly a 20% effect.
- The SP fit yields \(a_+ = 6.48^{+0.82}_{-0.93}\) (70% C.L.) and the SPD fit yields \(a_+ = 6.57^{+0.83}_{-0.92}\) (70% C.L.), where \(a_+ = a_1 + a_2\) is the sum of the Low Energy Constants associated with the \(E_{0+}\) counter-term.
- The P waves from SP and SPD fits agree within a 1% level.

## Context

This work extends the standard approach to neutral pion photoproduction, previously assumed to require only S and P waves (Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner, 2001; Schmidt, 2001), by demonstrating that D waves are necessary for accurate multipole extraction. The finding is a direct consequence of chiral symmetry and the Nambu-Goldstone nature of the pion, which makes the S wave very small. This enables future experiments at MAMI (Bernstein, 2009; Hornidge, 2009) to extract the electromagnetic multipoles with higher accuracy, including the real and imaginary parts of the S wave and the D-wave contribution via asymmetry measurements.
