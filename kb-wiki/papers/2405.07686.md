# Pole trajectories of the $Λ(1405)$ helps establish its dynamical nature

> The $Λ(1405)$ has been one of the most controversial exotic baryons. If the $Λ(1405)$ possesses a two-pole molecular structure, these poles are expected to evolve differently towards the SU(3) limit. From an analysis of a recent LQCD simulation on the $πΣ-\bar{K}N$ scattering for $I=0$ and the study of the quark mass dependence of the octet baryon masses, we determine for the first time the trajectories of these poles towards the symmetric point over the $\mathrm{Tr}[M]=C$ trajectory accurately. At $m_π\simeq 200$ MeV, our results are consistent with the lattice simulations, and the extrapolations to the physical point, based on the NLO chiral Lagrangians, agree well with existing experimental analyses. We predict qualitatively similar trajectories at LO and up to NLO, consistent with the LO interaction's dominance. At the SU(3) symmetric point of this trajectory, both poles are on the physical sheet, and the lower pole is located at $E^{(1)}=1573(6)(6)$ MeV, becoming a SU(3) singlet, while the higher pole at $E^{(8a)}=1589(7)(5)$ MeV couples to the octet representation. Moreover, we make predictions in $I=1$ for the $Σ^*$ resonance. We find a resonance pole that evolves into a bound state around $m_π=415$ MeV in this sector. The results presented here are crucial to shed light on the molecular nature of exotic strange baryon resonances and can be tested in future LQCD simulations.

## Key claim
The two-pole molecular structure of the Λ(1405) is confirmed by showing that the poles evolve differently toward the SU(3) symmetric limit along the Tr[M]=C trajectory, with the lower pole becoming a singlet and the higher pole coupling to the octet representation, but only when the analysis includes both the recent LQCD energy levels for πΣ–K̄N scattering and the quark mass dependence of octet baryon masses from the CLS ensembles.

## Method
The authors performed a combined fit of the NLO chiral Lagrangian to LQCD energy levels for I=0 πΣ–K̄N scattering at mπ≈200 MeV from Bulava (2023) and to the quark mass dependence of octet baryon masses from RQCD (2022) along the Tr[M]=C, ms=ms,phy, and symmetric trajectories. The finite-volume scattering amplitude was computed using the s-wave projection of the NLO interaction kernel, with the loop function evaluated via dimensional regularization and a cutoff scheme, and the LECs bi and di were determined by minimizing χ² with the covariance matrix of the LQCD energy levels. Pole trajectories were then extrapolated from the physical point to the SU(3) symmetric point by varying the pion mass along the Tr[M]=C curve.

## Key numbers
- At the SU(3) symmetric point along the Tr[M]=C trajectory, the lower pole (singlet) is located at E^(1)=1573(6)(6) MeV and the higher pole (octet) at E^(8a)=1589(7)(5) MeV.
- At mπ≈200 MeV, the two poles from the two-coupled-channel analysis are consistent with LQCD: z1=1392(9)(2)(16) MeV and z2=1455(13)(2)(17)−i11.5(4.4)(4)(0.1) MeV.
- The ratio of couplings at mπ≈200 MeV is |gπΣ^(1)/gK̄N^(1)|=1.9(4)_st(6)_md and |gπΣ^(2)/gK̄N^(2)|=0.53(9)_st(10)_md.
- In the I=1 sector, a resonance pole evolves into a bound state around mπ=415 MeV.
- The baryon mass in the chiral limit is m0=805(40)(40) MeV, and the SU(3) limit is reached at mπ=423 MeV with baryon mass mB=1182(7)(5) MeV.
- The cutoff qmax obtained from the fit is 623(23)(23) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary approach studies of the Λ(1405) two-pole structure (Jido 2003, Guo 2023, Xie 2023, Ren 2024) by performing the first combined analysis of the LQCD energy levels from Bulava (2023) and the baryon mass data from RQCD (2022) for the CLS ensembles. It enables future LQCD simulations to test the predicted pole trajectories along the Tr[M]=C and ms=ms,phy paths, and establishes consistency between LQCD, experimental cross sections, and the chiral unitary approach for the first time.
