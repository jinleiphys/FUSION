# Pole trajectories of the $Œõ(1405)$ helps establish its dynamical nature

> The $Œõ(1405)$ has been one of the most controversial exotic baryons. If the $Œõ(1405)$ possesses a two-pole molecular structure, these poles are expected to evolve differently towards the SU(3) limit. At $m_œÄ\simeq 200$ MeV, our results are consistent with the lattice simulations, and the extrapolations to the physical point, based on the NLO chiral Lagrangians, agree well with existing experimental analyses. The results presented here are crucial to shed light on the molecular nature of exotic strange baryon resonances and can be tested in future LQCD simulations.

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary approach studies of the Œõ(1405) two-pole structure (Jido 2003, Guo 2023, Xie 2023, Ren 2024) by performing the first combined analysis of the LQCD energy levels from Bulava (2023) and the baryon mass data from RQCD (2022) for the CLS ensembles. It enables future LQCD simulations to test the predicted pole trajectories along the Tr[M]=C and ms=ms,phy paths, and establishes consistency between LQCD, experimental cross sections, and the chiral unitary approach for the first time.
