# Hyperon halo structure of C and B isotopes

> We study the hypernuclei of C and B isotopes by Hartree-Fock model with Skyrme-type nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon interactions. The calculated $Λ$ binding energies agree well with the available experiment data. We found halo structure in the hyperon $1p$-state with extended wave function beyond nuclear surface in the light C and B isotopes. We also found the enhanced electric dipole transition between $1p$- and $1s$-hyperon states, which could be the evidence for this hyperon halo structure.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hyperon halo structure exists in the $\Lambda$ $1p$-state of light C and B isotopes, with the halo being most pronounced in weakly bound or quasi-bound states where the $\Lambda$ binding energy is less than about 1 MeV relative to the core+$\Lambda$ threshold, specifically in $^{12-14}_{\Lambda}$C and $^{12-13}_{\Lambda}$B.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations for $\Lambda$ hypernuclei of C and B isotopes using three different Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interactions (SIII, SLy4, SkM*) combined with several Skyrme-type $\Lambda N$ interactions (YBZ1, LY1, LY5, LY5r). The LY5r interaction was constructed with a reduced $\Lambda N$ spin-orbit strength $W^{\Lambda}_0=4.7$ MeV fm$^5$ to reproduce the experimentally observed spin-orbit splitting of $0.152$ MeV in $^{13}_{\Lambda}$C. The filling approximation was adopted for occupation probabilities, and the center of mass correction was applied by scaling the mass terms.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit splitting of the $\Lambda$ $1p$ states in $^{13}_{\Lambda}$C is calculated as $0.155$ MeV with SkM*+LY5r, consistent with the experimental value of $0.152$ MeV from Kohri (2002)
- The rms radius of the $\Lambda(1p_{1/2})$ state in $^{13}_{\Lambda}$C is $3.464$ fm, compared to $2.144$ fm for the $\Lambda(1s_{1/2})$ state, representing an enhancement of about 60%
- The $\Lambda$ binding energy of the $1p_{1/2}$ state in $^{13}_{\Lambda}$C is $0.312$ MeV (SIII+LY5r), $0.324$ MeV (SLy4+LY5r), and $0.273$ MeV (SkM*+LY5r)
- The $B(E1)$ value for the transition from $\Lambda(1p_{1/2})$ to $\Lambda(1s_{1/2})$ in $^{13}_{\Lambda}$C is $1.030\times 10^{-1}$ $e^2$fm$^2$
- In $^{12}_{\Lambda}$C, the $\Lambda(1p_{1/2})$ state is unbound with $B_{\Lambda}=-0.385$ MeV (SIII+LY5r) and has an rms radius of $3.679$ fm
- The $\Lambda(1p_{1/2})$ rms radius in $^{12}_{\Lambda}$B is $3.674$ fm with $B_{\Lambda}=-0.386$ MeV (SkM*+LY5r)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hyperon halo structure, which had been focused on lighter hypernuclei with $A\leq 7$ such as $^3_{\Lambda}$H (Miyagawa, 1995) and $^6_{\Lambda}$He, $^7_{\Lambda}$He, $^7_{\Lambda}$Li (Hiyama, 1996), to heavier systems with $A\geq 8$ in C and B isotopes. The study uses the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with $\Lambda N$ interactions derived from G-matrix calculations (Lanskoy, 1997) and is the first systematic investigation of $p$-wave $\Lambda$ halo structure in these mass regions using this framework. The enhanced $B(E1)$ transitions predicted between $\Lambda(1p)$ and $\Lambda(1s)$ states could provide experimental evidence for the halo structure, enabling future measurements at hypernuclear facilities.
