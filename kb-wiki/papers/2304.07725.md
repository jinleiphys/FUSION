# Double-strangeness exchange reactions in a hybrid Regge-plus-resonance approach

> We investigate double-strangeness exchange reactions, $K^-p\to K^+╬Ş^-$ and $K^-p\to K^0╬Ş^0$, using an effective Lagrangian approach based on a hybrid Regge-plus-resonance model involving rescattering diagrams. We consider the background processes that include $╬ø$, $╬ú$, and $╬ú(1385)$ Regge trajectories in the $u$ channel and the $s$-channel Born-term diagrams. The $s$-channel hyperon resonances account for the bump structures in the total cross sections. The $s$-channel $╬ø(2100,7/2^-)$ and $╬ú(2030,7/2^+)$ resonances contribute significantly, and the $╬ú(2250)$ resonance favors $J^P = 7/2^-$. The interference pattern between the $╬ø(2100,7/2^-)$ and $╬ú(2030,7/2^+)$ amplitudes is essential for describing forward differential cross sections in the resonance region $2.07 < \sqrt{s} < 2.15$ GeV. The absence of the $S=-2$ meson leads to the inclusion of the meson-baryon rescattering diagrams to describe the $K^- p \to K╬Ş$ reaction data, proving the significant contribution of the $(¤ò,¤ü,¤ë)$--$(╬ø,╬ú)$ rescattering diagrams.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a hybrid Regge-plus-resonance model, which includes meson-baryon rescattering diagrams, can describe the double-strangeness exchange reactions $K^-p\to K^+\Xi^-$ and $K^-p\to K^0\Xi^0$, but the description relies on fitting several unknown coupling constants and branching ratios from experimental data, particularly for the $s$-channel hyperon resonances where the branching fractions to $K\Xi$ are not well known.

## Method

The authors employed a tree-level effective Lagrangian approach with a hybrid Regge-plus-resonance model that includes Reggeized $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Sigma(1385)$ hyperon trajectories in the $u$ channel, $s$-channel Born-term diagrams, and $s$-channel hyperon resonances with spin-parities up to $J^P = 7/2^\pm$. Because no $S=-2$ meson exists for $t$-channel exchange, the model incorporates meson-baryon rescattering diagrams mediated by $(\phi,\rho,\omega)$ and $(\pi,\eta)$ mesons with $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ ground states, exchanging $K$ and $K^\ast$ mesons, using coupling constants from SU(3) flavor symmetry relations and the Nijmegen potential.

## Key numbers

- The Regge trajectories are given by $\alpha_\Lambda (u) = -0.65 + 0.94 u$, $\alpha_\Sigma (u) = -0.79 + 0.87 u$, and $\alpha_{\Sigma(1385)} (u) = -0.27 + 0.9 u$, with energy-scale parameters $s_{Y,\Sigma^\ast} = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$
- The SU(3) coupling constants used are $g_{KN\Lambda} = -13.24$, $g_{K\Xi\Lambda} = 3.52$, $g_{KN\Sigma} = 3.58$, $g_{K\Xi\Sigma} = -13.26$, $g_{KN\Sigma(1385)} = -3.22$, and $g_{K\Xi\Sigma(1385)} = -3.22$, with $f/d = 0.575$
- The extracted branching ratios for $Y^\ast \to K\Xi$ are: $\Lambda(1890)$ at 0.23%, $\Lambda(2100)$ at 0.73%, $\Sigma(2030)$ at 0.88%, $\Sigma(2230)$ at 2.0%, and $\Sigma(2250)$ at 1.0%
- The $s$-channel resonance region of interest is $2.07 < \sqrt{s} < 2.15$ GeV, where interference between $\Lambda(2100,7/2^-)$ and $\Sigma(2030,7/2^+)$ is essential
- The mass of $\Sigma(2250)$ was assumed to be 2290 MeV to fit the bump structure near $\sqrt{s} \approx 2.3$ GeV
- The cutoff energies for rescattering diagrams were fixed at $\Lambda_{VB} = 0.85$ GeV and $\Lambda_{PB} = 0.50$ GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical efforts on $K^-p \to K\Xi$ reactions including effective Lagrangian approaches (Sharov 2011, Shyam 2011), unitarized chiral perturbation theory (Feijoo 2015), and coupled-channel analyses from Argonne-Osaka (Kamano 2014), Julich-Bonn-Washington (Landay 2018), and Bonn-Gatchina (Matveev 2019). The hybrid Regge-plus-resonance model with rescattering diagrams enables description of old bubble-chamber data from the 1960s and 1970s and provides a framework for analyzing new high-statistics data from J-PARC experiments E05 and E42, as well as future measurements at GSI-FAIR with the PANDA detector.
