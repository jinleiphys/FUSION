# Unexpected impact of D waves in low-energy neutral pion photoproduction from the proton and the extraction of multipoles

> Contributions of $D$ waves to physical observables for neutral pion photoproduction from the proton in the near-threshold region are studied and means to isolate them are proposed. Various approaches to describe the multipoles are employed --a phenomenological one, a unitary one, and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The results of these approaches are compared and found to yield essentially the same answers. $D$ waves are seen to enter together with $S$ waves in a way that any means which attempt to obtain the $E_{0+}$ multipole accurately must rely on knowledge of $D$ waves and that consequently the latter cannot be dismissed in analyses of low-energy pion photoproduction. It is shown that $D$ waves have a significant impact on double-polarization observables that can be measured. This importance of $D$ waves is due to the soft nature of the $S$ wave and is a direct consequence of chiral symmetry and the Nambu--Goldstone nature of the pion. $F$-wave contributions are shown to be negligible in the near-threshold region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to the standard assumption in low-energy neutral pion photoproduction, \(D\)-wave contributions cannot be neglected when extracting the \(S\)-wave multipole \(E_{0+}\) from data, because the \(S\) wave is itself small (a consequence of chiral symmetry) and \(D\) waves interfere significantly with the dominant \(P\) waves, especially in the coefficient \(T_1\) of the Legendre expansion of the differential cross section.

## Method

The authors employed three complementary approaches to describe the electromagnetic multipoles: a phenomenological parameterization, heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) to one loop and \(\mathcal{O}(q_\pi^4)\), and a hybrid unitary fit that uses HBCHPT for the \(P\) waves but a unitary parameterization for the \(S\) wave. For all three approaches, the \(D\) waves were computed using Born terms plus vector-meson exchange (\(\omega\) and \(\rho\)). Fits to 171 differential cross sections and 7 photon asymmetries from MAMI (threshold to 166 MeV) were performed using a genetic algorithm, both with and without \(D\) waves (SP vs. SPD fits).

## Key numbers

- The real part of \(E_{0+}\) at the \(\pi^0\) threshold is \(-1.090 \times 10^{-3}/m_{\pi^+}\) for the HBCHPT SPD fit and \(-1.196 \times 10^{-3}/m_{\pi^+}\) for the Unitary SPD fit.
- At the \(\pi^+\) threshold, the real part of \(E_{0+}\) is \(-0.393 \times 10^{-3}/m_{\pi^+}\) for HBCHPT SPD and \(-0.336 \times 10^{-3}/m_{\pi^+}\) for Unitary SPD.
- The unitary cusp parameter \(\beta\) is determined to be \((3.43 \pm 0.08) \times 10^{-3}/m_{\pi^+}\) from experimental values of the \(\pi N\) charge-exchange scattering length and the charged-pion photoproduction multipole.
- The ratio of \(\text{Re} E_{0+}\) between SP and SPD fits changes by almost 20% at the unitary cusp and reaches a 35% change at 165 MeV.
- The \(\chi^2/\text{DOF}\) for all fits (SP and SPD, both approaches) lies between 1.23 and 1.25, all compatible within a 70% confidence level.

## Context

This work extends the preliminary findings of Fernandez-Ramirez, Bernstein, and Donnelly (2009) by systematically comparing phenomenological, unitary, and HBCHPT approaches to demonstrate that the \(D\)-wave effect on \(E_{0+}\) extraction is model independent. It contradicts the standard practice in earlier analyses by Schmidt et al. (2001) and in HBCHPT studies by Bernard et al. (1996, 2001), which assumed that only \(S\) and \(P\) waves were sufficient near threshold. The results enable more reliable extraction of the \(E_{0+}\) multipole and its associated low-energy constants, and they identify the \(T\) asymmetry as a promising observable to test unitarity and determine the imaginary part of \(E_{0+}\), with measurements proposed at HI\(\gamma\)S and MAMI.
