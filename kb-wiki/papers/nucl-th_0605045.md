# Failure of the Standard Coupled-Channels Method in Describing the Inelastic Reaction Data: On the Use of a New Shape for the Coupling Potential

> We present the failure of the standard coupled-channels method in explaining the inelastic scattering together with other observables such as elastic scattering, excitation function and fusion data. We use both microscopic double-folding and phenomenological deep potentials with shallow imaginary components. We argue that the solution of the problems for the inelastic scattering data is not related to the central nuclear potential, but to the coupling potential between excited states. We present that these problems can be addressed in a systematic way by using a different shape for the coupling potential instead of the usual one based on Taylor expansion.

# Failure of the Standard Coupled-Channels Method in Describing the Inelastic Reaction Data: On the Use of a New Shape for the Coupling Potential

## Key claim
The standard coupled-channels method, using either microscopic double-folding or phenomenological deep potentials, systematically underestimates the magnitude of the mutual-2⁺ state cross-section in the ¹²C+¹²C reaction by a factor of 3 to 10. The authors argue that this failure is not due to the central nuclear potential but to the coupling potential between excited states, and that replacing the usual first-derivative Taylor-expansion coupling potential with a second-derivative Woods-Saxon shape resolves the discrepancy across all measured energies.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-channels calculations for the ¹²C+¹²C reaction at laboratory energies of 74.2 MeV, 93.8 MeV, and 126.7 MeV, using both a microscopic double-folding potential (with M3Y nucleon-nucleon effective interaction) and a phenomenological Woods-Saxon squared real potential with a shallow Woods-Saxon volume imaginary component. They compared the standard coupling potential (first derivative of the optical potential via Taylor expansion) with a new coupling potential having a second-derivative Woods-Saxon shape multiplied by the diffuseness parameter, and fitted the resulting cross-sections to experimental data for the ground state, single-2⁺ state, and mutual-2⁺ state.

## Key numbers
- The standard coupled-channels model underestimates the mutual-2⁺ state cross-section magnitude by a factor of 3 to 10.
- The deformation parameter β for ¹²C is -0.6.
- The double-folding matter density parameters for the projectile are ρ₀P = 0.1644 fm⁻³, γ = 0.4988 fm⁻², ξ = 0.3741 fm⁻²; for the target, ρ₀T = 0.207 fm⁻³, c = 2.1545 fm, a = 0.425 fm.
- The M3Y effective interaction uses coefficients 7999 and 2134 with ranges 4 fm⁻¹ and 2.5 fm⁻¹, and J₀₀(E) = 276(1 − 0.005 E/Ap).
- Calculations were performed at three laboratory energies: 74.2 MeV, 93.8 MeV, and 126.7 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Boztosun (2001) and Stokstad et al. (1979) that identified the magnitude problem for the mutual-2⁺ state in the ¹²C+¹²C system, and is consistent with prior failures reported by Fry (1997), Rae (1997), Sakaguchi (1999), and Wolf (1982) using standard deformation procedures. The new coupling potential, with its repulsive part at short distances and attractive part at large distances, provides a systematic solution to a long-standing problem and suggests that the standard Taylor-expansion-based deformation formalism is inadequate for highly deformed nuclei such as ¹²C. This enables future coupled-channels studies to adopt non-standard coupling shapes for strongly deformed systems.
