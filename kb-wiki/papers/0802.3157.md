# Dynamics study of $Z^+(4430)$ and X(3872) in molecular picture

> In this talk, we review our recent work about the dynamical studies of $Z^+(4430)$ and X(3872). $Z^+(4430)$ can not be explained as a $D_1'D^*$ or $D_1D^*$ molecular state only considering one pion exchange potential without the cutoff, which needs to be confirmed by introducing sigma exchange potential and adding the cutoff in the effective potential. One also excludes the possibility of X(3872) as a $DD^*$ molecular state by one pion and one sigma exchanges with the cutoff. Fortunately there exists an S-wave $BB^*$ bound state with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$. we suggest future experiment to search this state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Z⁺(4430) cannot be explained as a D₁'D* or D₁D* molecular state when only one-pion exchange potential is considered without a cutoff, but this conclusion requires confirmation by introducing sigma exchange and a cutoff; similarly, X(3872) cannot be a DD* molecular state even when both one-pion and one-sigma exchanges with a cutoff are included, yet an S-wave BB* bound state with J^PC = 1⁺⁺ is predicted to exist.

## Method

The authors derived effective potentials for heavy meson pairs using a chiral Lagrangian in the heavy quark and chiral limits, applying the Breit approximation to relate scattering amplitudes to coordinate-space potentials. They used variational methods with three trial wave functions to search for loosely bound molecular states in the D₁'D*, D₁D*, and DD* systems, introducing a monopole form factor cutoff to regulate short-distance singularities in the potential. For the DD* system, both one-pion and one-sigma exchange potentials were included, and the analysis was extended to the BB* system.

## Key numbers

- Z⁺(4430) experimental mass: m = 4433 ± 4 (stat) ± 1 (syst) MeV; width: Γ = 44⁺¹⁷₋₁₃ (stat)⁺³⁰₋₁₁ (syst) MeV
- For the DD* system with g = 0.59 and g_σ = 0.76, no bound state was found at cutoff Λ = 1 GeV; bound states appeared only at larger cutoffs, e.g., Λ = 6.0 GeV gave binding energy E₀ = -1.3 MeV with r_rms = 2.8 fm, and Λ = 6.1 GeV gave E₀ = -4.9 MeV with r_rms = 1.5 fm
- For the BB* system with g = 0.59 and g_σ = 0.76, a bound state was found at Λ = 2.5 GeV with E₀ = -0.5 MeV and r_rms = 2.7 fm, and at Λ = 2.6 GeV with E₀ = -2.5 MeV and r_rms = 1.2 fm
- Coupling constants used: g = 0.59 ± 0.07 ± 0.01 (from D* width), |g'| = |g|/3, |g''| = |g|, h = -0.56 ± 0.28, h' = 0.55 GeV⁻¹, g_σ = 0.76; meson masses: m_D* = 2007 MeV, m_D₁' = 2430 MeV, m_D₁ = 2420 MeV, m_B* = 5325 MeV, m_B₁' = 5732 MeV, m_B₁ = 5725 MeV, m_π = 135 MeV, f_π = 132 MeV

## Context

This work extends dynamical studies of heavy meson molecules following the experimental discoveries of Z⁺(4430) by Belle and X(3872), and addresses contradictions in the literature: Suzuki (no year given) argued against X(3872) as a DD* molecule using only one-pion exchange, while Swanson (no year given) and Wong (no year given) found binding via pion and quark exchange or quark model calculations. The paper shows that including sigma exchange and a cutoff still rules out X(3872) as a DD* molecule, but predicts a BB* bound state that could be searched for experimentally, motivating further work with improved potentials.
