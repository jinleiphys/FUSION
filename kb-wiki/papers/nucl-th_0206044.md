# Parity nonconservation effects in the photodesintegration of polarized deuterons

> P-odd correlations in the deuteron photodesintegration are considered. The $π$-meson exchange is not operative in the case of unpolarized deuterons. For polarized deuterons a P-odd correlation due to the $π$-meson exchange is about $3 \times 10^{-9}$. Short-distance P-odd contributions exceed essentially than the contribution of the $π$-meson exchange.

## Key claim

For polarized deuterons, the parity-violating correlation due to π-meson exchange in photodisintegration is about 3 × 10⁻⁹, but short-distance (vector meson) contributions exceed this and dominate the asymmetry; the π-meson exchange is not operative for unpolarized deuterons.

## Method

The author calculates parity-nonconserving (P-odd) correlations in deuteron photodisintegration using a potential model with the deuteron wave function in the zero-range approximation. The weak π-meson exchange potential is combined with electric dipole (E1) and magnetic dipole (M1) transition operators to compute amplitudes, differential cross sections, and total cross sections for polarized deuterons. Vector meson (ρ, ω) exchange contributions are estimated using a Jastrow repulsion model and perturbative admixtures to the deuteron wave function, with weak coupling constants taken from the "best values" supported by the ¹³³Cs anapole moment experiment.

## Key numbers

- P-odd correlation due to π-meson exchange for polarized deuterons: about 3 × 10⁻⁹.
- Weak πNN constant used: g̅ = 3.3 × 10⁻⁷.
- Vector meson contribution to the regular E1 transition exceeds the π-meson contribution by a factor of 2 in the main energy region.
- Maximal relative magnitude Δσ/σ for vector meson contribution to the magnetic dipole regular transition: about 4 × 10⁻⁸.
- Accuracy of the π-meson contribution estimate: 20%.
- Accuracy of the vector meson contribution estimate: about 40% for given weak constants.
- λₛ = −0.16 × 10⁻⁷ m_π⁻¹; λₜ = −0.37 × 10⁻⁷ m_π⁻¹ (from best values of weak constants).

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of parity nonconservation in the deuteron, such as those by Khriplovich and Korkin (2001) and others cited in the paper, which found that π-meson exchange vanishes for unpolarized deuterons. The calculation addresses the contradiction between atomic experiments on ¹³³Cs (Wood et al., 1997) and other nuclear experiments by considering polarized deuterons, where π-meson exchange becomes operative. The results enable future experimental searches for parity violation in deuteron photodisintegration with polarized beams, though the vector meson contributions, while dominant, suffer from larger theoretical uncertainties due to short-distance effects.
