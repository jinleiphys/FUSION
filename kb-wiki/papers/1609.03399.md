# Determination of the $\boldsymbol{η^3{\rm He}}$ threshold structure from the low energy $\boldsymbol{pd \to η^3{\rm He}}$ reaction

> We analyze the data on cross sections and asymmetries for the $pd (dp) \to η^3{\rm He}$ reaction close to threshold and look for bound states of the $η^3 {\rm He}$ system. Rather than parameterizing the scattering matrix, as is usually done, we develop a framework in which the $η^3 {\rm He}$ optical potential is the key ingredient, and its strength, together with some production parameters, are fitted to the available experimental data. The relationship of the scattering matrix to the optical potential is established using the Bethe-Salpeter equation and the $η^3 {\rm He}$ loop function incorporates the range of the interaction given by the empirical $^3 {\rm He}$ density. We find a local Breit Wigner form of the $η^3$He amplitude $T$ below threshold with a clear peak in $|T|^2$, which corresponds to an $η^3 {\rm He}$ binding of about 0.3 MeV and a width of about 3 MeV. By fitting the potential we can also evaluate the $η^3 {\rm He}$ scattering length, including its sign, thus resolving the ambiguity in the former analyses.

## Key claim

The data on the $pd (dp) \to \eta^3{\rm He}$ reaction close to threshold are consistent with a very weakly bound $\eta^3{\rm He}$ state, but the potential derived from the fit does not produce a pole below threshold in the conventional sense; instead, the amplitude shows a clear Breit-Wigner-like peak below threshold with a binding energy of about 0.3 MeV and a width of about 3 MeV.

## Method

The authors fit an $\eta^3{\rm He}$ optical potential, rather than a parameterized scattering matrix, to the total cross section and asymmetry data for $pd (dp) \to \eta^3{\rm He}$ from the ANKE collaboration (Mersmann et al.) and COSY-11 (Smyrski et al.). The scattering amplitude $T$ is obtained from the potential using the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the loop function $G$ incorporating the range of the interaction via the empirical $^3{\rm He}$ density (Gaussian form factor with $\beta^2 = 13.7~{\rm GeV}^{-2}$). The fit includes $s$-wave and $p$-wave production amplitudes, and the resulting $T$ matrix is examined below threshold for a bound-state signature.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the $\eta^3{\rm He}$ state: $B_E = (0.30 \pm 0.10 \pm 0.08)~{\rm MeV}$ (statistical and systematic uncertainties).
- Width of the $\eta^3{\rm He}$ state: $\Gamma = (3.0 \pm 0.5 \pm 0.7)~{\rm MeV}$.
- $\eta^3{\rm He}$ scattering length: $a_{\eta^3{\rm He}} = [(2.23 \pm 1.29) - i(4.89 \pm 0.57)]~{\rm fm}$.
- Effective $\eta N$ scattering length derived from the fit: $a'_{\eta N} = [-(0.48 \pm 0.05) - i(0.18 \pm 0.02)]~{\rm fm}$.
- Fitted optical potential parameters: ${\rm Re}(V) = (-14.57 \pm 0.42) \times 10^{-2}~{\rm MeV}^{-1}$, ${\rm Im}(V) = (-5.36 \pm 0.14) \times 10^{-2}~{\rm MeV}^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of the $pd \to \eta^3{\rm He}$ reaction by Mersmann et al. and Colin et al., which used parameterized scattering matrices and suggested a pole with binding around 0.3 MeV but with very small width. The present approach resolves the sign ambiguity in the real part of the $\eta^3{\rm He}$ scattering length present in those earlier analyses. The results are consistent with theoretical expectations from chiral unitary approaches (e.g., Inoue et al., Garcia-Recio et al.) that $\eta$-nucleus bound states have widths larger than their binding energies, and with the specific calculations for $\eta^3{\rm He}$ by Barnea et al. (2015) which predicted binding of about 1 MeV or less and width of 15 MeV. The finding that the potential does not produce a pole below threshold, despite the clear peak in $|T|^2$, highlights a subtlety in defining bound states in systems with strong absorption.
