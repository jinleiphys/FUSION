# Summary of the NuSTEC Workshop on Shallow- and Deep-Inelastic Scattering

> The NuSTEC workshop (https://indico.cern.ch/event/727283) held at L'Aquila in October 2018 was devoted to neutrino-nucleus scattering in the kinematic region where hadronic systems with invariant masses above the $Δ(1232)$ resonance are produced: the so-called shallow- and deep-inelastic scattering regime. Not only is the physics in this kinematic region quite intriguing, it is also important for current and future oscillation experiments with accelerator and atmospheric neutrinos. For the benefit of the community, links to the presentations are accompanied by annotations from the speakers.

## Key claim

The workshop summary asserts that the shallow- and deep-inelastic scattering regime (hadronic invariant mass W above the Δ(1232) resonance) is both theoretically intriguing and important for current and future neutrino oscillation experiments, but it hedges that this kinematic region has been "hardly covered either experimentally or theoretically" for neutrino-nucleus interactions, in contrast to the extensive study via electron/muon scattering.

## Method

The workshop gathered presentations from experts on neutrino-nucleus scattering in the SIS (1.4 GeV < W < 2.0 GeV) and DIS (W > 2.0 GeV, Q² > 1.0 GeV²) regimes. Presentations covered generator implementations (GENIE, NEUT, NuWro, GiBUU), comparisons of their predictions for kinematic variables and particle multiplicities, sensitivity of oscillation experiments (NOvA, atmospheric neutrino experiments like Super-Kamiokande and Hyper-Kamiokande) to SIS/DIS modeling, and theoretical topics including quark-hadron duality, nuclear parton distribution functions, and hadronization. The summary provides annotated links to each presentation rather than formal proceedings.

## Key numbers

- The SIS region is defined as 1.4 GeV < W < 2.0 GeV; the DIS region as W > 2.0 GeV and Q² > 1.0 GeV².
- DUNE expects "order 50% of their events to have W > M_Δ" (the Δ(1232) resonance mass).
- NOvA's neutrino spectrum peaks at 2 GeV with bulk exposure from 1-3 GeV.
- GENIE uses a W cutoff of 1.7 GeV for the transition between resonance and Bodek-Yang models in version 2 and earlier.
- NEUT uses a transition at W = 2.0 GeV/c² between its low-W and high-W DIS models.
- NOvA applies a 50% systematic uncertainty on DIS normalization for 1 and 2 pion final states with W < 1.7 GeV, extended up to W = 3 GeV.
- NOvA reduced the normalization of GENIE non-resonant single pion production with W < 1.7 GeV by 57% for neutrinos (not antineutrinos).
- The GENIE tune used nine parameters: M_A^RES, M_A^QE, R-vp-CC-m2, R-vn-CC-m2, R-vp-CC-m3, R-vn-CC-m3, DIS-XSecScale, RES-XSecScale, and W_cut.

## Context

This workshop summary extends the work of the NuSTEC community's previous workshops on neutrino-nucleus scattering, focusing specifically on the SIS/DIS region that had been comparatively neglected. It documents the current status of generator implementations (Rein-Sehgal model from 1981, Berger-Sehgal model from 2007, Bodek-Yang model from 2003) and identifies tensions between inclusive and exclusive data fits, as seen in the GENIE tune where fitting exclusive data decreased the inclusive cross section in the 1-10 GeV energy region. The summary enables future work by documenting the need for improved resonance models, proper treatment of resonant-nonresonant interference, better hadronization models, and resolution of the apparent non-universality of nuclear parton distribution functions when extracted from neutrino versus electron scattering data.
