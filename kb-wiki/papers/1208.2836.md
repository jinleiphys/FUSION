# Statistical Spectroscopy for Neutron-rich sd-Shell Nuclei

> Statistical spectroscopic results using the spectral distribution theory are obtained for the structure of neutron-rich light nuclei going towards the drip line and compared to experimental values available. These results will be useful for nuclear astrophysics problems where often averaged nuclear properties are adequate.

## Key claim

Statistical spectroscopy using spectral distribution theory can reproduce binding energies of neutron-rich sd-shell nuclei with an average deviation of 0.07 MeV and RMS deviation of 1.92 MeV compared to experiment, but this agreement requires an additional isospin-dependent phenomenological correction term of 0.3 T^2 and excludes five nuclei near the neutron drip line where the sd-shell space is insufficient.

## Method

The author applied spectral distribution theory using the Ratcliff prescription to extract ground-state binding energies from the smoothed Gaussian level density, with corrections from excited-state energies, Cornish-Fisher expansion for skewness and excess, and an isospin-dependent term. Orbit occupancies and isoscalar E2 sum rule strengths were computed from configuration-averaged expectation values using the correlation coefficient between the operator and Hamiltonian in configuration-isospin space. All calculations used the Universal-sd two-body interaction for nuclei with neutron number equal to or greater than proton number within the sd-shell.

## Key numbers

- Average deviation of SDM binding energies from experimental values for 70 nuclei: 0.07 MeV (SDM slightly overbinds)
- RMS deviation for all 70 nuclei: 1.92 MeV
- RMS deviation excluding five nuclei near neutron number 20 (28F, 29Ne, 31Na, 32Mg, 33Al): 1.65 MeV
- Correlation coefficient for isoscalar E2 sum rule operator with Universal-sd interaction: -0.48 for 22Na (T=0) and -0.36 for 30Na (T=4)
- Isospin-dependent phenomenological correction term: 0.3 T^2

## Context

This work extends earlier spectral distribution method calculations for binding energies in the sd-shell by Chang (1971) and Sarkar (1987) from stable isotopes to neutron-rich nuclei approaching the drip line, and compares with the shell model calculations of Brown (2001). The results are intended for nuclear astrophysics applications such as r-process nucleosynthesis, where averaged nuclear properties are often adequate. The author notes that for nuclei with neutron number near 20, the sd-shell space must be enlarged to include fp-shell orbits (particularly f7/2), and plans to extend the method to heavier nuclei and other excitation operators.
