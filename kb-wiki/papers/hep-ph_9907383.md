# Neutrino magnetic moments, flavor mixing, and the SuperKamiokande solar data

> We find that magnetic neutrino-electron scattering is unaffected by oscillations for vacuum mixing of Dirac neutrinos with only diagonal moments and for Majorana neutrinos with two flavors. For MSW mixing, these cases again obtain, though the effective moments can depend on the neutrino energy. Thus, e.g., the magnetic moments measured with $\barν_e$ from a reactor and $ν_e$ from the Sun could be different. With minimal assumptions, we find a new limit on $μ_ν$ using the 825-days SuperKamiokande solar neutrino data: $|μ_ν| \le 1.5\times 10^{-10} μ_B$ at 90% CL, comparable to the existing reactor limit.

# Neutrino magnetic moments, flavor mixing, and the SuperKamiokande solar data

## Key claim

The effective magnetic moment measured in neutrino-electron scattering can be defined for flavor eigenstates and is non-oscillating (independent of distance) for Dirac neutrinos with only diagonal moments and for two-flavor Majorana neutrinos, but under MSW mixing the effective moment can depend on neutrino energy, meaning that the magnetic moment measured with reactor antineutrinos and solar neutrinos could be different. The new limit derived from the SuperKamiokande 825-day data is comparable to the existing reactor limit, but the authors note that the limit is obtained by assuming a fitted value of zero for the magnetic moment squared, making it slightly weaker than what the data naively imply but more robust.

## Method

The authors analyze the shape of the electron recoil spectrum from neutrino-electron scattering at SuperKamiokande, folding the differential cross section (weak plus magnetic scattering) with the 8B neutrino spectrum and the detector energy resolution. They perform a chi-squared fit to the binned data (0.5 MeV bins in total electron energy) with two free parameters: an overall normalization factor alpha and the magnetic moment squared, using the relative errors published by SuperKamiokande. To account for statistical fluctuations and the constraint that the magnetic moment squared must be non-negative, they generate simulated data sets with reference values and construct confidence intervals from the distribution of fitted parameters.

## Key numbers

* New limit on the solar neutrino magnetic moment from 825-day SuperKamiokande data: |mu_e^sol| <= 1.5 x 10^-10 mu_B at 90% CL
* Existing reactor limit (Derbin 1993): 1.8 x 10^-10 mu_B at 90% CL
* For 504-day data set (I): |mu_e^sol| <= 2.0 x 10^-10 mu_B at 90% CL
* For 708-day data set: |mu_e^sol| <= 1.6 x 10^-10 mu_B at 90% CL
* The Standard Model loop-induced magnetic moment is approximately 3 x 10^-19 mu_B for a 1 eV neutrino mass
* The correlation coefficient between fitted alpha and mu_nu^2 is approximately -0.9

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of how neutrino magnetic moments behave in the presence of flavor mixing, clarifying that for Dirac neutrinos with only diagonal moments and for two-flavor Majorana neutrinos the effective magnetic moment does not oscillate, contrary to the claim of Gninenko (1999). The analysis uses the SuperKamiokande data (Fukuda 1999) and the 8B neutrino spectrum from Bahcall (1996). The result enables future comparisons with reactor experiments and with the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory, which will independently check the spectral shape and total flux of the electron neutrino component.
