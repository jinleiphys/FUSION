# Introduction to Nonequilibrium Statistical Mechanics with Quantum Field

> In this article, we present a concise and self-contained introduction to nonequilibrium statistical mechanics with quantum field theory by considering an ensemble of interacting identical bosons or fermions as an example. Readers are assumed to be familiar with the Matsubara formalism of equilibrium statistical mechanics such as Feynman diagrams, the proper self-energy, and Dyson's equation. The aims are threefold: (i) to explain the fundamentals of nonequilibrium quantum field theory as simple as possible on the basis of the knowledge of the equilibrium counterpart; (ii) to elucidate the hierarchy in describing nonequilibrium systems from Dyson's equation on the Keldysh contour to the Navier-Stokes equation in fluid mechanics via quantum transport equations and the Boltzmann equation; (iii) to derive an expression of nonequilibrium entropy that evolves with time. In stage (i), we introduce nonequilibrium Green's function and the self-energy uniquely on the round-trip Keldysh contour, thereby avoiding possible confusions that may arise from defining multiple Green's functions at the very beginning. We try to present the Feynman rules for the perturbation expansion as simple as possible. In particular, we focus on the self-consistent perturbation expansion with the Luttinger-Ward thermodynamic functional, i.e., Baym's Phi-derivable approximation, which has a crucial property for nonequilibrium systems of obeying various conservation laws automatically. We also show how the two-particle correlations can be calculated within the Phi-derivable approximation, i.e., an issue of how to handle the "Bogoliubov-Born-Green-Kirkwood-Yvons (BBGKY) hierarchy".

## Key claim

This pedagogical review claims that nonequilibrium statistical mechanics for interacting bosons or fermions can be formulated concisely using quantum field theory on the Keldysh contour, with the self-consistent Phi-derivable approximation ensuring automatic conservation laws, and that a microscopic nonequilibrium entropy can be derived from quantum transport equations so as to be compatible with the law of increase in entropy.

## Method

The author introduces nonequilibrium Green's functions and self-energies uniquely on the round-trip Keldysh contour, avoiding multiple definitions at the outset, and presents Feynman rules for perturbation expansion with an alternative summation rule. The paper focuses on the self-consistent perturbation expansion using the Luttinger-Ward thermodynamic functional (Baym's Phi-derivable approximation), derives the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-particle correlations within this scheme, and performs successive reductions via Wigner transformation, gradient expansion with the Groenewold-Moyal product, and Enskog's expansion to connect Dyson's equation to quantum transport equations, the Boltzmann equation, and finally the Navier-Stokes equation.

## Context

This review extends the equilibrium Matsubara formalism (Matsubara, 1955) and the Keldysh contour approach (Keldysh, 1964) to provide a unified pedagogical treatment of nonequilibrium quantum field theory. It specifically builds on the Phi-derivable approximation of Baym (1962) and the Luttinger-Ward thermodynamic functional (Luttinger and Ward, 1960), and it connects to earlier work by Kadanoff and Baym (1962) on Green's functions for transport. The paper enables microscopic first-principles treatments of nonequilibrium phenomena including fluctuations, turbulence, and pattern formation, and it provides a derivation of nonequilibrium entropy following Ivanov et al. (2000) that generalizes the Boltzmann entropy for dilute classical gases (Boltzmann, 1872).
