# Spherical and Deformed Shell Effect Competition in Quasifission of Superheavy Nuclei

> Quasifission, along with fusion-fission, represent the two most likely reaction outcomes to occur post-capture in collisions leading to superheavy nuclei. As such, understanding these mechanisms and how they relate to one another is key to understanding the intricate dynamics that drive the formation (or dissociation) of the nascent compound nuclei formed in fusion reactions. This understanding directly translates to a more informed picture of suitable reaction partners and can provide vital information for experimental efforts to study the physics and chemistry of superheavy elements. In this work we report results from time-dependent simulations of $^{48}$Ca + $^{238}$U and $^{50}$Ti + $^{236}$Th reactions at incident energies just above the Coulomb barrier with a focus on the quasifission process that prevent the formation of a fully equilibrated $^{286}$Cn compound nucleus. We study these reactions systematically and consider a wide range of initial configurations to extract a robust estimate of primary fragment yields for the quasifission process. Multiple preferred exit channels are observed, with both spherical and deformed shell effects in the heavy and light fragments driving contributions to the production yields depending on the initial configuration of the system. Orientation effects of the deformed actinide targets are found to be a primary driver of which exit channels are populated. Furthermore, the impact of moving away from a doubly-magic projectile is explored with implications towards the reactions considered for current and future superheavy searches.

# Spherical and Deformed Shell Effect Competition in Quasifission of Superheavy Nuclei

## Key claim

Time-dependent Hartree-Fock simulations of $^{48}$Ca + $^{238}$U and $^{50}$Ti + $^{236}$Th reactions show that quasifission fragment production is driven by a competition between spherical shell effects (near $N=126$ in the heavy fragment) and deformed shell effects (near $N=56$ in the light fragment), with the initial orientation of the deformed actinide target being the primary determinant of which exit channel is populated, though the authors note that TDHF results should be interpreted as the most probable outcome and cannot capture the full distribution of observables.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the VU-TDHF3D code with the Skyrme SLy4d interaction, simulating collisions of $^{48}$Ca + $^{238}$U and $^{50}$Ti + $^{236}$Th at incident energies 5% above the Coulomb barrier (203.53 MeV and 219.27 MeV respectively). For each system, they systematically varied the initial orbital angular momentum from $l=0$ to $100 \, \hbar$ in steps of $10 \, \hbar$ and the Euler angle $\beta$ of the deformed actinide target from $0^\circ$ to $165^\circ$ in $15^\circ$ intervals, yielding 132 trajectories per target-projectile combination.

## Key numbers

- $^{48}$Ca + $^{238}$U center-of-mass energy: $E_{CM} = 203.53$ MeV; $^{50}$Ti + $^{236}$Th center-of-mass energy: $E_{CM} = 219.27$ MeV
- Angular momentum range examined: $l = 0$ to $100 \, \hbar$ at intervals of $10 \, \hbar$
- Contact time defined as interval when total density exceeds $\rho = 0.08$ nucleons/fm$^3$ (roughly half nuclear saturation density)
- For tip-like collisions ($\beta < 30^\circ$ and $\beta > 150^\circ$), contact times $\tau \leq 10$ zeptoseconds (zs) with heavy fragments near $N \approx 126$
- For side-like collisions ($30^\circ \leq \beta \leq 150^\circ$), contact times exceeded 20 zs (6000 fm/c) without scission for central collisions
- Minimum quasifission contact time for both systems: $\tau \approx 4.0$ zs
- Critical angular momentum for quasifission in $^{48}$Ca + $^{238}$U at $\beta = 90^\circ$: $l_c \sim 50 \, \hbar$
- Charge yields peaked around mercury ($Z=80$) and germanium ($Z=32$) for tip-like collisions; side-like collisions produced fragments near zirconium ($Z=40$)

## Context

This work extends systematic TDHF studies of heavy-ion reactions involving deformed actinide targets, consistent with prior experimental results from Hinde (1995) on orientation dependence of contact times and previous TDHF studies by Umar (2016) and Kohley (2015) on $^{238}$U collisions. The results show similarities to a quasifission study of $^{48}$Ca + $^{249}$Bk by Godbey (2020), though that system showed only a single peak in charge yields without the spreading seen here. The comparison between $^{48}$Ca and $^{50}$Ti projectiles is motivated by recent experimental successes at LBNL with $^{50}$Ti beams for superheavy synthesis reported by Gates (2024). The findings enable future exploration of "inverse quasifission" as a potential route to produce neutron-rich nuclei far from stability, building on concepts discussed by Kozulin (2014) and Zagrebaev (2005).
