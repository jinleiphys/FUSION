# Measuring the saturation scale in nuclei

> The saturation momentum seeing in the nuclear infinite momentum frame is directly related to transverse momentum broadening of partons propagating through the medium in the nuclear rest frame. Calculation of broadening within the color dipole approach including the effects of saturation in the nucleus, gives rise to an equation which describes well data on broadening in Drell-Yan reaction and heavy quarkonium production.

## Key claim

The saturation momentum in the nuclear infinite momentum frame is directly equal to the transverse momentum broadening of partons propagating through the nucleus in the rest frame, but this equality holds only when the dipole cross section is evaluated at a characteristic transverse separation set by the saturation scale itself, and the calculation must include the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression of gluon radiation to avoid divergent results.

## Method

The authors calculate transverse momentum broadening within the color dipole approach, using a saturated parametrization of the dipole-nucleon cross section fitted to pion-nucleon total cross sections, photoproduction of vector mesons, and DIS data at moderate Q^2. They derive an equation for the gluon saturation momentum modified by gluon shadowing (LPM effect), where the nuclear modification factor depends on the same saturation momentum, making the treatment self-consistent. The resulting saturation scale is then compared to experimental data on broadening in Drell-Yan reactions and heavy quarkonium production.

## Key numbers

- Quark saturation momentum in a nucleon: Q_{qN}(E) = 0.19 GeV × (E/1 GeV)^{0.14}
- Pion-proton total cross section parametrization: σ^{πp}_{tot}(E) = 14.35 mb × (E/1 GeV)^{0.08}
- Pion charge radius squared: ⟨r_{ch}^2⟩_π = 0.44 fm^2
- Effective gluon mass (mean gluon transverse momentum): m_g ≈ 0.65 GeV
- The two terms in the broadening factor C_q(E) become equal at energy about 100 GeV; above this energy broadening rises as E^{0.36}
- In the limit of very large nuclear thickness T_A, the modified saturation scale becomes independent of T_A and impact parameter b

## Context

This work extends the color dipole approach of Kopeliovich, Lapidus, and Zamolodchikov (1981) and the broadening formalism of Dolejsi, Hufner, and Kopeliovich (1993) and Johnson, Kopeliovich, and Tarasov (2001). It directly relates the saturation momentum to measurable transverse momentum broadening, in contrast to the color glass condensate framework of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and Iancu, Leonidov, and McLerran (2001), which defines saturation in the infinite momentum frame. The result enables experimental extraction of the saturation scale from existing Drell-Yan and quarkonium production data, and provides a self-consistent treatment of gluon shadowing that predicts the saturation scale becomes independent of atomic number for very heavy nuclei.
