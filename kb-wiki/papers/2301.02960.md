# Directed flow and global polarization in Au+Au collisions across energies covered by the beam energy scan at RHIC

> We study the directed flow of identified particles in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=7.7$ to 62.4 GeV. The Glauber model is extended to include both a tilted deformation of the QGP fireball with respect to the longitudinal direction and a non-zero longitudinal flow velocity gradient in the initial state. By combining this improved initial condition with a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model calculation, we obtain a satisfactory description of the transverse momentum spectra and the rapidity dependent directed flow coefficient of different hadron species. Our calculation indicates the sensitivity of the hadron directed flow, especially its splitting between protons and antiprotons, to both the initial geometry and the initial longitudinal flow velocity. Therefore, the combination of directed flow of different hadrons can provide a tight constraint on the initial condition of nuclear matter created in heavy-ion collisions. The initial condition extracted from the directed flow is further tested with the global polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ within the same theoretical framework, where we obtain a reasonable description of these hyperon polarization observed at different collision energies at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simultaneous description of the directed flow splitting between protons and antiprotons across RHIC BES energies (7.7 to 62.4 GeV) requires both a tilted deformation of the energy and baryon density profiles and a non-zero longitudinal flow velocity gradient in the initial state, with the baryon density tilt needing to be stronger than the energy density tilt at the lowest energies (11.5 and 7.7 GeV) to match data.

## Method

The authors extended the optical Glauber model to include a counterclockwise tilt of the QGP fireball in the reaction plane via a rapidity-dependent wounded nucleon distribution, and introduced a non-zero longitudinal fluid velocity beyond the Bjorken approximation parameterized by a fractional momentum transfer factor. These initial conditions were evolved using the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc with the NEOS-BQS equation of state, and the Cooper-Frye formalism was applied at freeze-out to obtain hadron spectra and flow coefficients. The global polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons was computed from the same hydrodynamic fields to test the consistency of the extracted initial conditions.

## Key numbers

*   The model describes Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies: 62.4, 39, 27, 19.6, 14.5, 11.5, and 7.7 GeV.
*   The tilt strength parameter H_t increases from 10.0 at 62.4 GeV to 28.0 at 7.7 GeV.
*   The fractional longitudinal momentum parameter f_v increases from 0.18 at 62.4 GeV to 0.26 at 7.7 GeV.
*   The specific shear viscosity was set to C_eta_v = 0.08 and the baryon diffusion coefficient parameter C_B = 0.4 for all collision energies.
*   The freeze-out energy density was set to e_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm^3.
*   At the two lowest energies (11.5 and 7.7 GeV), the baryon density tilt was assumed to be 1.2 times H_t (i.e., 1.2 * 22.0 = 26.4 and 1.2 * 28.0 = 33.6) to describe the proton v_1.

## Context

This work extends the tilted fireball approach of Bozek (2011) and the authors' earlier study (Jiang 2021) by incorporating both energy and baryon density tilts along with a longitudinal flow velocity gradient, following the methodology of Shen (2020) and Ryu (2021). It addresses the challenge of describing the proton-antiproton v_1 splitting observed by STAR (2014) at RHIC-BES energies, which earlier hydrodynamic and transport models (Chen 2009, Steinheimer 2014, Nara 2016) could not quantitatively reproduce. The extracted initial conditions are further tested against global polarization data, enabling a combined constraint on the initial geometry and flow of nuclear matter created in heavy-ion collisions.
