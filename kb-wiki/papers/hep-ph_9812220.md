# Nonequilibrium Photons as a Signature of Quark-hadron Phase Transition

> We study the nonequilibrium photon production in the quark-hadron phase transition, using the Friedberg-Lee type solitons as a working model for quark-hadron physics. We propose that to search for nonequilibrium photons in the direct photon measurements of heavy-ion collisions may be a characteristic test of the transition from the quark-gluon to hadronic phases.

## Key claim

Nonequilibrium photons produced via parametric amplification of the oscillating sigma field during the quark-hadron phase transition may provide a characteristic test of quark-gluon plasma formation, but the results depend on the "quench" approximation and the choice of Friedberg-Lee model parameters, and a more realistic dynamical treatment is needed before comparison with experimental data.

## Method

The authors model the quark-hadron phase transition using the Friedberg-Lee nontopological soliton model with a weakly first-order transition. They adopt a "quench" approximation in which a thermalized quark-gluon plasma is instantaneously cooled to zero temperature, then compute the nonequilibrium photon production from the oscillating sigma mean field using the closed time path formalism with Hartree factorization for the sigma self-interactions. The photon production arises from effective couplings sigma-F^2 and (partial sigma)^2-F^2 derived by integrating out quark and vector meson fields at one-loop level.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant g = 2.6 x 10^-3 for the sigma-F^2 interaction
- Coupling constant h = 1.8 x 10^-2 for the (partial sigma)^2-F^2 interaction, assuming vector meson mass m_V = 2 GeV and |Psi_V(0)|^2 = 0.1 fm^-3
- Sigma field oscillation frequency omega_phi = 8.8 fm^-1, with mean field oscillating about 1.1 fm^-1 (compared to classical vacuum value sigma_c = 0.36 fm^-1)
- Photon production rate peaks at two momenta: k = 4.4 fm^-1 (from sigma-F^2 coupling) and k = 8.8 fm^-1 (from (partial sigma)^2-F^2 coupling)
- The 8.8 fm^-1 peak is approximately two orders of magnitude larger than thermal photon production rates from quark-gluon plasma or hadron gas
- Nonequilibrium dynamics occurs over a time scale of about 10 fm

## Context

This work extends prior studies of single photon spectra from quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas by Srivastava and Sinha (1994), Sollfrank et al. (1997), and Li and Brown (1998), which found that distinguishing between the two phases using thermal photons alone is difficult. The authors apply the nonequilibrium field theory formalism developed by Boyanovsky, de Vega, Holman, and collaborators to the quark-hadron phase transition problem. The Friedberg-Lee model had previously been used to fit hadronic properties by Wilets (1989) and to study the quark-hadron phase transition in the early Universe by Ng and Sze (1991) and Cottingham et al. (1994). The results suggest that high-energy nonequilibrium photons around 2 GeV could serve as a distinct signature of quark-gluon plasma formation in heavy-ion collisions, but require convolution with hydrodynamic expansion models for quantitative comparison with experiments.
