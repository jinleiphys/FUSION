# Microscopic particle-rotor model for low-lying spectrum of Lambda hypernuclei

> We propose a novel method for low-lying states of hypernuclei based on the particle-rotor model, in which hypernuclear states are constructed by coupling the hyperon to low-lying states of the core nucleus. In contrast to the conventional particle-rotor model, we employ a microscopic approach for the core states, that is, the generator coordinate method (GCM) with the particle number and angular momentum projections. We apply this microscopic particle-rotor model to $^9_Λ$Be employing a point-coupling version of the relativistic mean-field Lagrangian. A reasonable agreement with the experimental data for the low-spin spectrum is achieved using the $ΛN$ coupling strengths determined to reproduce the binding energy of the $Λ$ particle.

## Key claim

The authors propose a microscopic particle-rotor model for hypernuclei that, unlike the conventional rigid-rotor approach, constructs core states via the generator coordinate method with particle-number and angular-momentum projections, achieving reasonable agreement with experimental low-spin data for $^9_\Lambda$Be only after tuning the $\Lambda N$ coupling strengths to reproduce the $\Lambda$ binding energy.

## Method

The method couples a $\Lambda$ hyperon, described by a Dirac equation with scalar and vector contact interactions, to core-nucleus states obtained from a relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS calculation. The core wave functions are built by superposing particle-number and angular-momentum projected RMF+BCS states with different quadrupole deformations $\beta$, solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation (GCM+PN1DAMP). The coupled-channels equations for the hyperon radial wave functions are then solved, using transition densities from the core, to yield the hypernuclear spectrum and $B(E2)$ strengths.

## Key numbers

- The experimental $\Lambda$ binding energy of $^9_\Lambda$Be is $B^{\rm(exp.)}_{\Lambda}=6.71 \pm 0.04$ MeV, which is used to fix the $\Lambda N$ coupling strengths.
- The fitted coupling strengths are $\alpha_S^{N\Lambda} = -4.2377 \times 10^{-5}$ MeV$^{-2}$ and $\alpha_V^{N\Lambda} = 1.2756 \times 10^{-5}$ MeV$^{-2}$.
- For $^8$Be, the $B(E2: 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)$ is $24.99$ $e^2$fm$^4$ and $B(E2: 4^+_1 \to 2^+_1)$ is $47.28$ $e^2$fm$^4$.
- For $^9_\Lambda$Be, the $cB(E2)$ values for the $2^+ \to 0^+$ transition (via $3/2^+_1 \to 1/2^+_1$ and $5/2^+_1 \to 1/2^+_1$) are $22.55$ and $22.57$ $e^2$fm$^4$, respectively.
- The $cB(E2)$ for the $4^+ \to 2^+$ transition (via $9/2^+_1 \to 5/2^+_1$) is $41.55$ $e^2$fm$^4$.
- The $1/2^-_1$ state lies at $6.276$ MeV and the $3/2^-_1$ state at $6.249$ MeV, with a spin-orbit splitting of $0.03$ MeV between the $p_{1/2}$ and $p_{3/2}$ $\Lambda$ orbitals.
- The measured state at $5.80(13)$ MeV is interpreted as a mixture of $3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$ states; the state at $9.52(13)$ MeV is a mixture of $9/2^+, 7/2^+, 7/2^-, 5/2^-$, and $1/2^+$ states.

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field methods for hypernuclei, which previously relied on cluster models (Motoba 1983, Hiyama 1999) or shell models (Dalitz 1978, Gal 1971, Millener), and addresses the limitation of pure mean-field approaches (Zhou 2007, Win 2008) that do not yield spectra. It builds on the authors' earlier five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian study of $^{24}$Mg (Yao 2011) and on the conventional particle-rotor model of Bohr and Mottelson (1975). The method enables systematic studies of hypernuclear spectra across mass regions, including vibrational-to-rotational transitions, and can be extended to triaxial cores (e.g., $^{25}_\Lambda$Mg) and to odd-mass ordinary nuclei.
