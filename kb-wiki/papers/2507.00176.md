# Entanglement, Trace Anomaly and Confinement in QCD

> We formulate confinement in QCD as an entropic surface phenomenon. Quark and gluon quantum information is localized on a transverse entangling two-sphere of radius $R_{EE}$; at this radius the QCD vacuum -- partitioned by a hadron into interior and exterior regions -- reaches its maximal entanglement entropy. Lattice-QCD determinations of the scalar (trace) gravitational form factors fix both $R_{EE}$ and the transverse trace-anomaly density $ρ_h(R_{EE})$, yielding a parameter-free slope $c_h = 8π^2 R_{EE}^2\,ρ_h(R_{EE})$ and a mechanical entropy $S_{EE}(y)=c_h\,y$ that grows linearly with rapidity $y$. The entropy gradient $\partial_R S_{EE}$ changes sign at $R_{EE}$: it pushes colored degrees of freedom outward for $r<R_{EE}$ and pulls them inward for $r>R_{EE}$, thereby localizing them on the codimension-2 entangling two-sphere $Σ_\perp = S^2_{R_{EE}}$ (which, in the infinite-momentum frame, projects onto the transverse plane), the 'information wall'. This provides a high-energy (large-$y$) entropic confinement diagnostic that complements -- rather than replaces -- Wilson's area-law criterion, which probes long-distance dynamics near the rest frame ($y\to 0$). Imposing unitarity on an entropic ansatz for the amplitude yields $σ(s)\propto y^δ$. World data favor $δ=2$ for elastic $pp\,(p\bar p)$ scattering and heavy-quark photoproduction, whereas $ϕ$ photoproduction favors a softer $δ=0.387$. All extracted cross sections remain well below the Froissart--Martin bound. These results provide a confinement criterion quantified directly from non-perturbative QCD inputs, unifying the trace anomaly, entanglement entropy, and high-energy scattering within a single quantitative framework.

# Entanglement, Trace Anomaly and Confinement in QCD

## Key claim

Confinement in QCD is formulated as an entropic surface phenomenon where colored degrees of freedom are localized on a transverse entangling two-sphere of radius \(R_{EE}\), with the entropy gradient changing sign at that radius to push partons outward for \(r < R_{EE}\) and pull them inward for \(r > R_{EE}\). This provides a high-energy (large-rapidity) entropic confinement diagnostic that complements, rather than replaces, Wilson's area-law criterion, which probes long-distance dynamics near the rest frame.

## Method

The author uses percent-level lattice QCD determinations of scalar (trace) gravitational form factors (GFFs) from Shanahan (2018), Hackett (2023), and Wang (2024) to fix both the maximal-entanglement radius \(R_{EE}\) and the transverse trace-anomaly density \(\rho_h(R_{EE})\) for hadrons and nuclei. The universal logarithmic contribution to the von Neumann entropy of the reduced state is controlled by the QCD trace anomaly on the entangling two-sphere, yielding a mechanical entropy \(S_{EE}(y) = c_h y\) that grows linearly with rapidity \(y\), where the slope \(c_h = 8\pi^2 R_{EE}^2 \rho_h(R_{EE})\) is parameter-free. An entropic ansatz relating elastic \(2 \to 2\) cross sections to the mechanical entropy of the color field is then constrained by unitarity, producing a power-law scaling \(\sigma(s) \propto y^\delta\) that is fit to world data for elastic \(pp\) scattering and vector-meson photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- Maximum-entropy radius for the nucleon: \(R_{EE} = 0.67(8)\) fm, with specific entropy \(S_{EE}/(Ay) = 7.17(36)\)
- Maximum-entropy radius for the pion: \(R_{EE} = 0.98(6)\) fm, with specific entropy \(S_{EE}/(Ay) = 12(4)\)
- Maximum-entropy radius for \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\): \(R_{EE} = 13.9(1.6)\) fm, with specific entropy \(S_{EE}/(Ay) = 8.94(45)\)
- Entropy per nucleon saturates at \(S_{EE}/(A y) \simeq 8.94\) for large nuclei
- Elastic \(pp\) scattering exponent: \(\delta = 2\), with normalization \(\mathcal{N}_{pp}^2 = 0.324\) mb
- \(J/\psi\) photoproduction exponent: \(\delta = 2\), with normalization \(\mathcal{N}_{J/\psi}^2 = 2.1 \times 10^{-6}\) mb
- \(\Upsilon(1S)\) photoproduction exponent: \(\delta = 2\), with normalization \(\mathcal{N}_{\Upsilon}^2 = 1.3 \times 10^{-8}\) mb
- \(\phi\) photoproduction exponent: \(\delta = 0.387\), with normalization \(\mathcal{N}_{\phi}^2 = 3.3 \times 10^{-4}\) mb
- All extracted cross sections remain below the Froissart-Martin bound \(\pi/m_\pi^2 \simeq 62.8\) mb

## Context

This work extends the entropic confinement framework of Kharzeev (2017), Liu (2018), and Gursoy (2023) from 1+1D CFT derivations to a 3+1D QCD realization using nonperturbative lattice inputs. It contrasts with the approach of Dosch (2023), who used an exponential entropic ansatz \(\sigma \propto e^{\delta y}\) that fails near threshold and overshoots the Froissart-Martin bound, whereas the power-law ansatz \(\sigma \propto y^\delta\) describes both threshold and high-energy data with a single exponent. The results enable a quantitative baseline for future measurements at HL-LHC, Jefferson Lab (CLAS12, GlueX, SoLID), and the Electron-Ion Collider, against which deviations from odderon exchange, Regge exchanges, or exotic multiquark resonances can be identified.
