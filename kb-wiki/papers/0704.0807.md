# Nuclear forces from chiral effective field theory

> 54 pages, 12 figures; lectures presented at the DAE-BRNS workshop on physics and astrophysics of hadrons and hadronic matter, Visva Bharati University, Santiniketan, West Begal, India, November 2006

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) provides a systematic, model-independent framework for deriving nuclear forces from low-energy QCD, where the hierarchy of contributions is controlled by a power-counting expansion in small momenta over the chiral symmetry breaking scale (≈1 GeV). The approach is not a new fundamental derivation from QCD itself, but rather a controlled effective theory that respects all relevant symmetries of QCD, with the crucial hedge that the method relies on an organizational scheme (power counting) to distinguish more from less important contributions, and the low-energy constants must be fitted to data.

## Method

The authors construct the most general Lagrangian consistent with the (broken) chiral symmetry of QCD, using pions and nucleons as effective degrees of freedom while heavy mesons and nucleon resonances are integrated out. The Lagrangian is organized by chiral dimension (derivatives and pion-mass insertions), and Feynman diagrams are evaluated order by order in the low-momentum expansion (chiral perturbation theory) using dimensional regularization and heavy-baryon formalism. Two-nucleon forces are derived from contact terms and pion-exchange diagrams, with the power ν = 2L + ΣΔ_i determining the order of each contribution, where L is the number of loops and Δ_i depends on derivatives and nucleon fields at each vertex.

## Key numbers

- Up quark mass: m_u = 2 ± 1 MeV; down quark mass: m_d = 5 ± 2 MeV; strange quark mass: m_s = 95 ± 25 MeV (from PDG)
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Λ_χ ≈ 1 GeV
- ρ-meson mass: m_ρ = 0.78 GeV ≈ 1 GeV (used as typical hadronic scale)
- ρ-meson mass: 776 MeV; a_1 meson mass: 1230 MeV (evidence for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking)
- Pion decay constant f_π and axial-vector coupling constant g_A appear in the leading-order Lagrangians (numerical values not given in the text)
- Leading-order one-pion exchange potential: V_{1π} = -(g_A²/4f_π²) τ₁·τ₂ (σ₁·q σ₂·q)/(q² + m_π²)
- Two-pion exchange at NLO: W_C = -L(q)/(384π² f_π⁴) [4m_π²(5g_A⁴ - 4g_A² - 1) + q²(23g_A⁴ - 10g_A² - 1) + 48g_A⁴ m_π⁴/w²]; V_T = -1/q² V_S = -3g_A⁴ L(q)/(64π² f_π⁴)
- L(q) ≡ (w/q) ln[(w+q)/(2m_π)]; w ≡ √(4m_π² + q²)

## Context

This lecture series reviews the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces, which extends and supersedes earlier meson-exchange models such as the one-boson-exchange model (OBE) and the Stony Brook (1975), Paris (1979, 1980), and Bonn (1989, 1987) potentials. The work builds on the foundational ideas of Weinberg (1979, 1990) and the pioneering coordinate-space NN potential of Ordóñez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994, 1999). The ChEFT framework enables systematic derivation of two-nucleon, three-nucleon, and four-nucleon forces on the same footing, with three-nucleon forces appearing first at NNLO and four-nucleon forces at N³LO, allowing for controlled improvements in nuclear structure and reaction calculations.
