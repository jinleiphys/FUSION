# Photoproduction of $η^\prime$ mesons off nuclei and their properties in the nuclear medium

> We study the photoproduction of $η^\prime$ mesons from nuclei near the threshold within the collision model based on the nuclear spectral function. The model takes properly into account both primary photon-nucleon and secondary pion-nucleon $η^\prime$ production processes as well as the effect of the nuclear $η^\prime$ mean-field potential on these processes. We find that the secondary pion-induced reaction channel $πN \to {η^\prime}N$ plays an insignificant role in the $η^\prime$ photoproduction off nuclei. We calculate the transparency ratio for $eta^\prime$ mesons and compare it to the available experimental data. The comparison indicates an inelastic ${η^\prime}N$ cross section of the order of 6-10 mb. We show that the existing transparency ratio measurements do not allow one to distinguish between two adopted $η^\prime$ in-medium modification scenarios. Our studies also demonstrate that the momentum distribution and excitation function for $η^\prime$ production in $γA$ reactions reveal some sensitivity to these scenarios, which means that such observables may be an important tool to get a valuable information on the $η^\prime$ in-medium properties.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the existing transparency ratio measurements for eta-prime mesons photoproduced off nuclei cannot distinguish between two adopted scenarios for eta-prime in-medium modification (with or without a mass shift of approximately 50 MeV), but that the momentum distribution and excitation function for eta-prime production in gamma+A reactions do show sensitivity to these scenarios, potentially providing valuable information on eta-prime in-medium properties.

## Method

The author employed a collision model based on the nuclear spectral function approach to calculate inclusive eta-prime meson photoproduction from nuclei (C, Al, Ca, Nb, Pb, U) near threshold for incident photon energies up to approximately 2.2 GeV. The model accounted for primary photon-nucleon (gamma+N -> eta-prime+N) and secondary pion-nucleon (pi+N -> eta-prime+N) production processes, included the effect of a nuclear eta-prime mean-field potential with a depth of V0 approximately -100 MeV at saturation density, and described eta-prime final-state absorption using an inelastic eta-prime+N cross section. The author compared calculated transparency ratios, momentum distributions, and excitation functions with experimental data from the CBELSA/TAPS Collaboration (2012).

## Key numbers

- The eta-prime mass shift adopted in the medium modification scenario is approximately 50 MeV, derived from a potential depth V0 approximately -100 MeV at saturation density rho_0 and an average nucleon density <rho_N> = rho_0/2.
- The inelastic eta-prime+N cross section extracted from comparison with transparency ratio data is of the order of 6-10 mb.
- The free production threshold for the elementary gamma+N -> eta-prime+N reaction is approximately 1.446 GeV.
- The nuclear radii used for target nuclei are: 4.0 fm for C-12, 5.259 fm for Al-27, 5.763 fm for Ca-40, 7.128 fm for Nb-93, 8.871 fm for Pb-208, and 9.214 fm for U-238.
- The parametrization for the pi- + p -> eta-prime + n total cross section uses a threshold energy sqrt(s_0) = m_eta-prime + m_N, with cross section values given by 223.5*(sqrt(s_1)-sqrt(s_0))^0.352 microbarns for 0 < sqrt(s_1)-sqrt(s_0) <= 0.354 GeV, and 35.6/(sqrt(s_1)-sqrt(s_0))^1.416 microbarns for sqrt(s_1)-sqrt(s_0) > 0.354 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of eta-prime meson photoproduction from nuclei, building on the spectral function approach previously applied to vector mesons by the author and collaborators (Paryev, 2000-2012). The study directly addresses experimental measurements by the CBELSA/TAPS Collaboration (2012), who reported transparency ratios and momentum distributions for eta-prime production off C, Ca, Nb, and Pb targets and extracted an eta-prime in-medium width of 15-25 MeV at normal nuclear matter density. The paper demonstrates that while transparency ratios alone cannot distinguish between different in-medium modification scenarios, momentum distributions and excitation functions may serve as more sensitive observables, potentially enabling future experiments to determine whether the eta-prime mass is reduced by approximately 50 MeV in nuclear matter as predicted by chiral symmetry restoration models (Jido et al., 2012; Nagahiro et al., 2006).
