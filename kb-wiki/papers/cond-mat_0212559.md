# Exactly solvable model of three interacting particles in an external magnetic field

> The quantum mechanical problem of three identical particles, moving in a plane and interacting pairwise via a spring potential, is solved exactly in the presence of a magnetic field. Calculations of the pair--correlation function, mean distance and the cluster area show a quantization of these parameters. Especially the pair-correlation function exhibits a certain number of maxima given by a quantum number. We obtain Jastrow pre-factors which lead to an exchange correlation hole of liquid type, even in the presence of the attractive interaction between the identical electrons.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact solution for three identical particles in a plane interacting via a pairwise spring potential in a constant magnetic field, showing that the wave function acquires a Jastrow prefactor even for attractive interactions. The authors find that the exchange correlation hole at small distances shows Wigner crystal behavior (proportional to \(\xi^2\)) from the fully antisymmetrized wave function, but that neglecting certain crossed terms yields the Laughlin-type behavior (proportional to \(\xi^{2|m_2|}\)) instead.

## Method

The Hamiltonian for three identical particles with effective mass \(m^*\) interacting via a spring potential of strength \(\kappa = m^*\omega_0^2/3\) in a perpendicular magnetic field \(B\) (symmetric gauge) is decoupled into center-of-mass and two relative coordinates (\(\boldsymbol{\xi}\) and \(\boldsymbol{\eta}\)). Each decoupled Hamiltonian is solved exactly in polar coordinates, yielding wave functions expressed in terms of generalized Laguerre polynomials. The total antisymmetric wave function is constructed by multiplying spatial wave functions with appropriate spin wave functions for quartet states (total spin \(S=3/2\)) and doublet states (\(S=1/2\)), with the spatial part antisymmetrized via permutations of particle coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Energy spectrum: \(E(n_0,n,m) = \hbar \omega_B (n_0 + \frac{1}{2}) + \frac{\hbar \omega_B}{2} \sqrt{1 + \frac{4\omega_0^2}{\omega_B^2}} (n+2) + \frac{\hbar \omega_B}{2} m\), where \(\omega_B = eB/m^*c\) is the cyclotron frequency.
- The number of maxima in the pair correlation function is equal to \(n_2 + 1\), where \(n_2\) is a quantum number.
- Mean distance between particles (analytical approximation): \(\langle (\mathbf{r}_1 - \mathbf{r}_2)^2/2 \rangle = \frac{3 C^2 l_B^2}{\sqrt{1+4\omega_0^2/\omega_B^2}} [(1+2n_2+|m_2|) + (1+2n_3+|m_3|)]\).
- Mean cluster area (analytical approximation): \(\langle \frac{1}{4} |(\mathbf{r}_2 - \mathbf{r}_1) \times (\mathbf{r}_3 - \mathbf{r}_1)|^2 \rangle = \frac{9}{4} \frac{C^2 l_B^4}{1+4\omega_0^2/\omega_B^2} [(1+2n_2+|m_2|)(1+2n_3+|m_3|)]\).
- Small-distance expansion of the pair correlation function: \(g(|\xi^*|) = a |\xi^*|^{2m_2} + (b + c |\xi^*|^{2m_2})|\xi^*|^2 + o(|\xi^*|^3)\), with \(a = 2 (n_2+m_2)!/(m_2!^2 n_2!)\).
- For quartet states, only odd values of \(m_2\) are permitted; for \(m_2\) even the antisymmetric wave function vanishes.

## Context

This work extends the class of exactly solvable three-particle problems, which are rare and mostly limited to one-dimensional systems (Dodd 1970, Hinds and Thacker 1974, McGuire and Batchelor 1978) or three-dimensional spinless particles (Faddeev et al. 1980, Belyaev and Mikhajlov 1992, 1994). The model provides an exact benchmark for testing approximate methods such as Faddeev equations (Faddeev 1965). The results connect to the fractional quantum Hall effect by showing that the Jastrow prefactor characteristic of Laughlin's wave function (Laughlin 1983, 1987) arises from antisymmetrization even for attractive interactions, and the quantum number \(|m_2|\) is identified as analogous to the filling factor. The work enables further study of three-particle clustering in systems like Si-MOSFET structures and GaAs/AlGaAs heterojunctions, where effective three-particle attractive interactions were previously predicted (Nakhmedov and Morawetz 2001).
