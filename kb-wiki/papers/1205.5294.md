# Partial-wave analysis of π-p {\to} ηn and π-p {\to} K0Λ reactions

> We investigate the hadronic reactions πN {\to} ηN and πN {\to} KΛ via single-energy partial-wave analyses in the c.m. energy range 1080 to 2100 MeV. Our results for the KΛ channel are consistent with prior works; however, for the ηN channel our results differ significantly from previous energy-dependent partial-wave analyses that violate the S-matrix unitarity. We present the first (new) results of ηN and KΛ partial-wave amplitudes constrained by a unitary energy-dependent model. We obtain excellent predictions of integrated cross sections for the two reactions from a global energy-dependent solution. Our results imply that the region just above S11 (1535) has a major contribution from P11 (1710) for π-p {\to} ηn, whereas the large peak near 1700 MeV in π-p {\to} K0Λ is dominated by contributions from both S11 (1650) and P11 (1710).

## Key claim

The authors present the first partial-wave amplitudes for π⁻p → ηn and π⁻p → K⁰Λ that are constrained by a unitary energy-dependent model, finding that for the ηN channel their results differ significantly from previous energy-dependent analyses that violated S-matrix unitarity. The region just above S₁₁(1535) has a major contribution from P₁₁(1710) for π⁻p → ηn, while the large peak near 1700 MeV in π⁻p → K⁰Λ is dominated by contributions from both S₁₁(1650) and P₁₁(1710).

## Method

Single-energy partial-wave analyses were performed separately for π⁻p → ηn and π⁻p → K⁰Λ in the center-of-mass energy range 1080 to 2100 MeV, using energy bins of 30 MeV width. Within each bin, partial-wave amplitudes were approximated as complex constants, and all available differential cross section, polarization, polarized cross section, and spin-rotation data were fitted simultaneously. The single-energy solutions were then iterated against a global multichannel energy-dependent fit that enforced two-body S-matrix unitarity, with the S₁₁ amplitude for ηN below the KΛ threshold held fixed at values from the GWU solution (SP06) and the overall phase ambiguity resolved by requiring the G₁₇ amplitude to have the same phase as the elastic G₁₇ amplitude for ηN, and by rotating the KΛ amplitudes so that the S₁₁ amplitude had a resonant phase consistent with prior determinations of the S₁₁(1650) mass and width.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range of analysis: 1080 to 2100 MeV
- Energy bin width: 30 MeV
- For π⁻p → ηn at W = 1530 MeV: 89 differential cross section data points fitted (no polarization data)
- For π⁻p → ηn at W = 2070 MeV: 20 differential cross section and 7 polarization data points fitted
- For π⁻p → K⁰Λ at W = 1678 MeV: 170 differential cross section, 10 polarization, and 80 polarized cross section data points fitted
- For π⁻p → K⁰Λ at W = 1858 MeV: 10 differential cross section, 14 polarization, and 11 spin-rotation parameter data points fitted
- S₁₁ amplitude for π⁻p → ηn at W = 1620 MeV: Re = -0.200 ± 0.033, Im = 0.156 ± 0.040
- S₁₁ amplitude for π⁻p → K⁰Λ at W = 1678 MeV: Re = 0.028 ± 0.003, Im = -0.186 ± 0.017
- P₁₁ amplitude for π⁻p → ηn at W = 1710 MeV: Re = 0.039 ± 0.049, Im = -0.197 ± 0.036
- P₁₁ amplitude for π⁻p → K⁰Λ at W = 1708 MeV: Re = 0.152 ± 0.011, Im = 0.025 ± 0.016

## Context

This work extends prior partial-wave analyses of π⁻p → ηn by Feltesse (1975) and Baker (1979) and of π⁻p → K⁰Λ by Knasel (1975), Baker (1978), Saxon (1980), and Bell (1983), all of which violated S-matrix unitarity by representing amplitudes as simple sums of resonant and background terms. The results for the KΛ channel are consistent with prior works, particularly Bell (1983) and the Bonn-Gatchina group (Sarantsev, 2011), but the ηN results differ significantly from the GWU analysis (Arndt, 2006) which rejects the existence of P₁₁(1710). The D₁₃ amplitude was found to be negligible for both reactions, consistent with prior work showing its inelasticity is saturated by ππN channels (Manley, 1992). These results enable improved extraction of N* resonance parameters from a unitary multichannel energy-dependent analysis that includes both hadronic and photoproduction channels.
