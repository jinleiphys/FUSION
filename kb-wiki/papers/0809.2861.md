# A particle-hole model approach for hypernuclei

> A particle-hole model is developed to describe the excitation spectrum of single lambda hypernuclei and the possible presence of collective effects is explored by making a comparison with the mean-field calculations. Results for the spectra of 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 90Zr and 208Pb single lambda hypernuclei are shown. The comparison with the available experimental data is satisfactory. We find that collective phenomena are much less important in hypernuclei than in ordinary nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors develop a particle-hole model for single-lambda hypernuclei that goes beyond the mean-field approximation, but they find that collective effects are much less important in hypernuclei than in ordinary nuclei, with the residual interaction modifying mean-field energies by only about 4% on average.

## Method

The authors construct an RPA-like model where the hypernucleus excited state is formed by transforming a nucleon below the Fermi surface into a lambda hyperon, with the excited state described as a linear combination of various particle-hole excitations. They solve the secular equations in angular-momentum-coupled form using a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential for both nucleons and the lambda, and test three different parameterizations of the lambda-nucleon residual interaction (a zero-range force and two finite-range gaussian forces) on five doubly-closed shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For 16O_lambda, the mean-field energies are already within about 4% of experimental values, and the RPA-like results change them by about 4% on average
- The 1^-_2 state in 16O_lambda is calculated at 6.55-6.58 MeV with the three interactions, compared to the experimental value of 6.562 MeV
- The 2^- state in 16O_lambda is calculated at 6.76-6.79 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 6.784 MeV
- The 2^+ state in 16O_lambda is calculated at 10.33-10.43 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 10.57 MeV
- For 16O_lambda, the RPA-like and TDA energy differences are smaller than 10 keV for states below 10 MeV
- The configuration space includes 54 single particle levels for the lambda hyperon up to the 5p_{1/2} level, corresponding to 10 major harmonic oscillator shells
- The lambda mean-field potential uses V^Lambda_0 = 29.0 MeV and V^Lambda_ls = 2.0 MeV for all hypernuclei, with radii varying from 2.50 fm for 12C_lambda to 7.60 fm for 208Pb_lambda

## Context

This work extends earlier Tamm-Dancoff Approximation studies of hypernuclei by Auerbach (1977) and Lopes (1995) to include ground-state correlations through an RPA-like formalism, while also comparing with mean-field calculations. The model enables calculation of transition form factors and electromagnetic transition probabilities between hypernuclear states, which can be compared with future gamma-ray spectroscopy experiments. The authors note that their effective interactions cannot be directly compared with more microscopic G-matrix interactions like the YNG interaction from Yamamoto (1985) and Bando (1990), as those produce spectra very different from experiment when used in this framework.
