# Nonlocalized Clustering: A New Concept in Nuclear Cluster Structure Physics

> We investigate the $α$+\oo\ cluster structure in the inversion-doublet band ($K^π=0_{1}^\pm$) states of \nene\ with an angular-momentum-projected version of the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function, which was successful "in its original form" for the description of, e.g., the famous Hoyle state. In contrast with the traditional view on clusters as localized objects, especially in inversion doublets, we find that these {\it single} THSR wave functions, which are based on the concept of nonlocalized clustering, can well describe the $K^π=0_1^-$ band and the $K^π=0_1^+$ band. For instance, they have 99.98% and 99.87% squared overlaps for $1^-$ and $3^- $ states (99.29%, 98.79% and 97.75% for $0^+, 2^+$ and $4^+$ states), respectively, with the corresponding exact solution of the $α$+\oo\ resonating group method. These astounding results shed a completely new light on the physics of low energy nuclear cluster states in nuclei: The clusters are nonlocalized and move around in the whole nuclear volume, only avoiding mutual overlap due to the Pauli blocking effect.

# Nonlocalized Clustering: A New Concept in Nuclear Cluster Structure Physics

## Key claim
The authors claim that the inversion-doublet bands (K^π = 0_1^±) in ²⁰Ne, traditionally interpreted as evidence for localized α + ¹⁶O clustering with a fixed intercluster distance, can instead be described by single THSR wave functions based on nonlocalized clustering, with the key qualifier that the hybrid wave function's energy minimum occurs at zero intercluster separation parameter (S_z = 0) for both positive- and negative-parity states.

## Method
The authors constructed a hybrid Brink-THSR wave function for the α + ¹⁶O system in ²⁰Ne, introducing both a size parameter β and a shift parameter S_z. They performed angular-momentum-projected variational calculations using the Volkov No.1 force with oscillator parameter b = 1.46 fm, mapping energy surfaces in the two-parameter space (S_z, β) for each J^π state. The resulting single hybrid wave functions at the energy minima were compared via squared overlaps with the full GCM Brink wave functions (equivalent to the exact RGM solution).

## Key numbers
- Squared overlap of the single hybrid wave function with the GCM Brink wave function for the 1⁻ state: 99.98%
- Squared overlap for the 3⁻ state: 99.87%
- Squared overlaps for positive-parity states: 99.29% (0⁺), 98.79% (2⁺), and 97.75% (4⁺)
- Minimum energy for the 1⁻ state: -155.38 MeV at β_x = β_y = β_z = 2.4 fm and S_z = 0
- Minimum energy for the 0⁺ state: -159.66 MeV at β_x = β_y = β_z = 1.8 fm and S_z = 0
- In the Brink model (β = 0), the minimum for the 1⁻ state occurs at intercluster distance S_z = 3.9 fm with energy -153.87 MeV

## Context
This work extends the nonlocalized clustering concept previously applied to the Hoyle state in ¹²C (Tohsaki et al., 2001) and to the ground-state band of ²⁰Ne (Zhou et al., 2012) by now addressing the negative-parity inversion-doublet band, which had been considered the strongest evidence for localized clustering since the work of Horiuchi (1970) and the Brink model (1966). The finding that single THSR wave functions achieve near-perfect overlap with the full GCM solution for both parity bands enables a reinterpretation of cluster states as nonlocalized motion constrained only by Pauli blocking, and opens the question of how rotational bands emerge from such delocalized intrinsic states, which the authors indicate will be addressed in a forthcoming paper.
