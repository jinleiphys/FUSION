# Lambda hypernuclei and neutron star matter in a chiral SU(3) relativistic mean field model with a logarithmic potential

> We develop a chiral SU(3) symmetric relativistic mean field (RMF) model with a logarithmic potential of scalar condensates. Experimental and empirical data of symmetric nuclear matter saturation properties, bulk properties of normal nuclei, and separation energies of single- and double-$Λ$ hypernuclei are well explained. The nuclear matter equation of state (EOS) is found to be softened by $σζ$ mixing which comes from determinant interaction. The neutron star matter EOS is further softened by $Λ$ hyperons.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) relativistic mean field model with a logarithmic potential can simultaneously explain symmetric nuclear matter saturation, bulk properties of normal nuclei, and separation energies of single- and double-Lambda hypernuclei, but only after the sigma-zeta mixing from the determinant interaction softens the nuclear matter equation of state and the inclusion of Lambda hyperons further softens the neutron star matter equation of state.

## Method

The authors extended a chiral SU(2) relativistic mean field model (SCL2 RMF) to SU(3) flavor symmetry (SCL3 RMF) by incorporating a logarithmic chiral potential derived from the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD, including scalar mesons with hidden strangeness (zeta) and the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant interaction. Model parameters were constrained by chiral symmetry through hadron masses and vacuum condensates, then fitted to nuclear matter saturation properties, finite nuclei binding energies and charge radii, and Lambda hypernuclear separation energies, with meson-Lambda coupling constants determined from SU(3) symmetric relations and hypernuclear data.

## Key numbers

* Saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter: rho_0 = 0.150 fm^{-3}, E_0/A = -16.3 MeV per nucleon
* Incompressibility K = 211.0 MeV, comparable to the empirical value K = 210 +/- 30 MeV
* Sigma meson mass m_sigma = 690 MeV, chosen to reproduce bulk properties of normal nuclei
* Pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV, kaon mass m_K = 496 MeV, zeta meson mass M_zeta = 980 MeV (used as input constraints)
* Vacuum condensates: f_pi = 92.4 MeV, f_zeta = 94.5 MeV
* Omega-nucleon coupling g_omegaN = 11.95, omega self-coupling c_omega = 294.9, rho-nucleon coupling g_rhoN = 4.54
* Sigma-Lambda coupling g_sigmaLambda = 3.40, zeta-Lambda coupling g_zetaLambda = 5.17
* Lambda potential depth in nuclear matter: U_Lambda approximately -30 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(2) RMF model (SCL2 RMF) developed by Tsubakihara and Ohnishi to SU(3) flavor symmetry, enabling the description of hypernuclear systems. The model reproduces the Lambda separation energies from hypernuclear data and the Lambda-Lambda bond energy in double-Lambda hypernucleus He-6 observed by Nagara and collaborators. The resulting equation of state is softer than those from the TM1 model and the SCL2 model, and agrees with the variational calculations by Friedman and Pandharipande at around saturation density. The pressure is consistent with the range inferred from heavy-ion collision flow data analyzed by Danielewicz, Lacey, and Lynch. This framework enables predictions of neutron star matter equation of state and maximum neutron star mass using a model that simultaneously respects chiral symmetry and hypernuclear experimental data.
