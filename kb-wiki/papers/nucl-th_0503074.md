# Bulk and single-particle properties of hyperonic matter at finite temperature

> Bulk and single-particle properties of hot hyperonic matter are studied within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation extended to finite temperature. The bare interaction in the nucleon sector is the Argonne V18 potential supplemented with an effective three-body force to reproduce the saturating properties of nuclear matter. The modern Nijmegen NSC97e potential is employed for the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions. The effect of the temperature on the in-medium effective interaction is found to be, in general, very small and the single-particle potentials differ by at most 25% for temperatures in the range from 0 to 60 MeV. The bulk properties of infinite matter of baryons, either nuclear isospin symmetric or a beta-stable composition which includes a non-zero fraction of hyperons, are obtained. It is found that the presence of hyperons can modify the thermodynamical properties of the system in a non-negligible way.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first microscopic calculation of the equation of state at finite temperatures including baryonic degrees of freedom beyond nucleons, finding that the effect of temperature on the in-medium effective interaction is generally very small, with single-particle potentials differing by at most 25% for temperatures from 0 to 60 MeV, but that the presence of hyperons can modify the thermodynamical properties of the system in a non-negligible way.

## Method

The authors extended the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation of the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory to finite temperature using the "naive" finite temperature BBG expansion, which replaces zero-temperature step functions with Fermi-Dirac distributions in the Pauli operator and occupation numbers. The bare nucleon-nucleon interaction was the Argonne V18 potential supplemented with an effective three-body force, while the Nijmegen NSC97e potential was used for hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions. Self-consistent solutions were obtained for the G-matrices, single-particle potentials, and chemical potentials at each density and temperature.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle potentials differ by at most 25% for temperatures in the range from 0 to 60 MeV
- At T=60 MeV, the interaction energy is only about 15 MeV higher than at T=0 across the density range explored
- The non-interacting part of the internal energy increases by between 50 and 90 MeV over the same density range at T=60 MeV
- The liquid-gas transition temperature T_C is close to 15-20 MeV
- Higher order contributions would increase the free energy per particle by less than 5 MeV at T=60 MeV
- At T=0 and the highest density, rearrangement effects in the chemical potential are of order 50 MeV; at T=60 MeV they amount to at most 35 MeV
- The hyperonic attractive contribution to the internal energy at zero temperature reaches -6 MeV at ρ=0.5 fm⁻³
- At twice normal nuclear matter density and T=60 MeV, hyperonic interacting terms represent about half of the hyperonic kinetic energy contribution

## Context

This work extends microscopic calculations of hyperonic matter, previously performed at zero temperature by Vidana (2000) and others, to finite temperatures for the first time. It builds on the finite-temperature Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach developed by Baldo and Ferreira (1999), who validated the "naive" finite temperature expansion for temperatures up to about 30 MeV. The results enable improved modeling of proto-neutron star formation and supernova dynamics, where matter reaches densities beyond nuclear matter saturation density (0.17 fm⁻³) and temperatures of several tens of MeV, conditions that demand the inclusion of hyperonic degrees of freedom beyond nucleons.
