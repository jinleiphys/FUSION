# d+³He Elastic Backward Scattering and the ³He Structure at Short Distances

## Key claim

The d+³He backward elastic scattering cross section is primarily determined by the one-nucleon-exchange (ONE) mechanism, which connects the reaction to the momentum distribution of deuterons in ³He; however, the deuteron-deuteron rescattering (ddS) mechanism becomes comparable to ONE only at deuteron kinetic energies near 1 GeV, and the calculations assume a maximal spectroscopic factor of S_dp = 1, whereas realistic three-body calculations give lower values.

## Method

The author performed relativistic calculations in the infinite momentum frame (IMF) using a "minimal relativization" scheme for the ³He wave function, considering only the d+p channel. The differential cross section for 180° elastic d+³He scattering was derived in the ONE approximation, and an additional contribution from elastic deuteron-deuteron rescattering (ddS) was estimated using a pure imaginary amplitude with a total dd cross section approximated as 150 mb.

## Key numbers

- The ddS mechanism is not significant at the energy scale of RIKEN data (T_d = 140, 200, and 270 MeV), but becomes comparable with ONE at T_d ~ 1 GeV.
- The total dd cross section used in calculations is σ^tot_dd = 2 σ^tot_pd ≈ 150 mb.
- The spectroscopic factor is taken as S_dp = 1 in the calculations, but three-body calculations with the Argon potential give S_dp = 0.69 (Schiavilla, year not specified in text).

## Context

This work extends the one-neutron-exchange analysis of dp elastic scattering (Kobushkin, 1986) to the d+³He system, following the approach used for p+³He backward scattering (Kobushkin, 1993) where empirical momentum distributions from ³He breakup data (Dubna group, year not specified) were employed. The results are compared with recent RIKEN data (Tanifuji, year not specified) at T_d = 140, 200, and 270 MeV, and the paper identifies the ddS mechanism as a potential competitor at higher energies, similar to a mechanism previously considered for pd backward scattering (Gurvitz, year not specified).
