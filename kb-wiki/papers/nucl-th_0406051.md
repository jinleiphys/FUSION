# Does Σ-Σ-αForm a Quasi-Bound State?

> We have investigated the possible existence of a quasi-bound state for the Σ-Σ-αsystem in the framework of Faddeev calculations. We are particularly interested in the state of total iso-spin T=2, since for an inert αparticle there is no strong conversion to Ξ-N-αor Λ-Λ-αpossible. A Σ-αoptical potential based on Nijmegen model D and original Σ-Σinteractions of the series of Nijmegen potentials NSC97 as well a simulated Gaussian type versions thereof are used. Our investigation of the Σ-Σ-αsystem leads to a quasi bound state where, depending on the potential parameters, the energy ranges between -1.4 and -2.4 MeV and the level width is about 0.2MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Σ-Σ-α system in the total isospin T=2 state likely forms a quasi-bound state, but this result depends critically on the specific Nijmegen Σ-Σ potentials used, which themselves predict a bound Σ-Σ two-body state that may be unrealistic.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Σ-Σ-α system using Faddeev equations in momentum space with partial wave decomposition. They employed a complex Σ-α optical potential derived from the Nijmegen model D interaction in a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework, and used either the original meson-exchange Nijmegen potentials NSC97a,c,e or simulated Gaussian versions for the Σ-Σ interaction in the T=2 channel. The complex energy eigenvalues were found by tracing trajectories in the complex energy plane, starting from an enhanced real potential and gradually introducing the imaginary part.

## Key numbers

- The quasi-bound state energy ranges between -1.4 and -2.4 MeV depending on the potential parameters, with a level width of about 0.2 MeV.
- For the original Nijmegen potentials, the complex energies found were: NSC97a: -1.418 - i0.202 MeV; NSC97c: -2.34 - i0.014 MeV; NSC97e: -2.376 - i0.191 MeV.
- For the simulated Gaussian potentials, the complex energies were: NSC97a: -1.492 - i0.218 MeV; NSC97c: -2.323 - i0.017 MeV; NSC97e: -2.354 - i0.211 MeV.
- When the Σ-Σ potential strength was reduced by a factor 0.9, the three-body energy became -0.68 - i0.21 MeV; with a factor 0.8, it became -0.279 - i2.0×10^{-2} MeV.
- The two-body Σ-Σ binding energies for the original Nijmegen potentials were: NSC97a: -2.250 MeV; NSC97c: -2.437 MeV; NSC97e: -3.122 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of S=-2 hypernuclei, specifically the Λ-Λ-α system (the nucleus ⁶_ΛΛHe) which had been investigated by Filikhin et al. using Faddeev equations in configuration space and by Myint et al. using a variational Gaussian expansion method. The authors benchmarked their momentum-space Faddeev code against these prior calculations, achieving excellent agreement. The existence of a Σ-Σ-α quasi-bound state would provide new information on baryon-baryon forces in the S=-2 sector, where only three Λ-Λ hypernuclei (¹⁰_ΛΛBe, ⁶_ΛΛHe, and ¹³_ΛΛB) have been identified experimentally. The authors suggest a possible experimental production route via a two-step (K⁻,K⁺) reaction on a ⁶Li target.
