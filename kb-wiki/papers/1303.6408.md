# Medium effects on the viscosities of a pion gas

> The bulk and shear viscosities of a pion gas is obtained by solving the relativistic transport equation in the Chapman-Enskog approximation. In-medium effects are introduced in the $ππ$ cross-section through one-loop self-energies in the propagator of the exchanged $ρ$ and $σ$ mesons. The effect of early chemical freeze-out in heavy ion collisions is implemented through a temperature dependent pion chemical potential. These are found to affect the temperature dependence of the bulk and shear viscosities significantly.

## Key claim

The bulk and shear viscosities of a pion gas are significantly affected by in-medium modifications of the ππ cross-section through one-loop self-energies in the ρ and σ meson propagators, and by the inclusion of a temperature-dependent pion chemical potential that models early chemical freeze-out in heavy ion collisions; the abstract's claim of "significant" effects is qualified by the paper's focus on temperatures below the crossover temperature and on elastic scattering only.

## Method

The relativistic transport equation (Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation) was solved in the first Chapman-Enskog approximation to obtain the bulk and shear viscosities of a pion gas. The ππ cross-section was constructed from an effective Lagrangian with ρ and σ meson exchange, and medium effects were introduced by replacing vacuum propagators with in-medium propagators obtained from one-loop self-energy diagrams (ππ loops for the σ, and πh loops with h = π, ω, h₁, a₁ for the ρ) using the real-time formulation of thermal field theory. A temperature-dependent pion chemical potential from the partial chemical equilibrium scenario of Bebie et al. was implemented to account for early chemical freeze-out in heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The ρ meson mass used in the vacuum cross-section parametrization is m_ρ = 5.53 m_π, and the σ meson mass is m_σ = 5.8 m_π.
- The coupling constants used in the effective Lagrangian are g_ρ = 6.05 and g_σ = 2.5.
- The pion decay constant used for entropy density corrections is f_π = 93 MeV.
- The entropy density correction from interactions is approximately 1-2% for the values of μ_π and T considered.
- The η/s values remain well above the quantum bound of 1/4π in all cases considered.
- The temperature-dependent pion chemical potential is taken from Hirano (2002), starting from T ~ 170 MeV where μ_π = 0 at chemical freeze-out, down to T ~ 100 MeV at kinetic freeze-out where μ_π ~ 85 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of shear viscosity at vanishing chemical potential (Mitra and Sarkar, 2013) by including a temperature-dependent pion chemical potential, and extends earlier kinetic theory calculations of pion gas viscosities by Prakash et al. (1993), Chen et al. (2007), Davesne (1996), and Itakura et al. (2008) that used phenomenological or chiral perturbation theory cross-sections. The medium-modified cross-section approach enables more realistic input for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions, particularly for improving descriptions of pT spectra and elliptic flow as argued by Dusling and Schäfer (2012).
