# Self-consistent Green's function approaches

> We present the fundamental techniques and working equations of many-body Green's function theory for calculating ground state properties and the spectral strength. Green's function methods closely relate to other polynomial scaling approaches discussed in chapters 8 and 10. However, here we aim directly at a global view of the many-fermion structure. We derive the working equations for calculating many-body propagators, using both the Algebraic Diagrammatic Construction technique and the self-consistent formalism at finite temperature. Their implementation is discussed, as well as the inclusion of three-nucleon interactions. The self-consistency feature is essential to guarantee thermodynamic consistency. The pairing and neutron matter models introduced in previous chapters are solved and compared with the other methods in this book.

## Key claim

This chapter presents the fundamental techniques of many-body Green's function theory for calculating ground state properties and spectral strength, emphasizing that full self-consistency in the Green's function calculation is essential to guarantee thermodynamic consistency, though in practical applications this scheme may not be achievable exactly and self-consistency is implemented only partially for the most important contributions.

## Method

The authors derive the working equations for calculating many-body propagators using both the Algebraic Diagrammatic Construction (ADC) technique and the self-consistent formalism at finite temperature. The ADC method, originally devised for quantum chemistry, is applied to discrete bases for finite nuclei and can also be applied to fermion gases in a box with periodic boundary conditions. The alternative approach solves the nucleon-nucleon ladder scattering matrix for dressed particles in the medium using a finite temperature formalism, requiring iterative self-consistent calculations where the computed spectral function is fed back into the working equations until convergence is achieved.

## Key numbers

- The error from approximating the three-body interaction expectation value using three correlated density matrices has been estimated not to exceed 250 keV for total binding energies for 16O and 24O (Cipollone 2013)
- The Koltun sum-rule allows calculating the total energy of the system using the exact one-body propagator alone, and is exact for Hamiltonians containing at most one- and two-body interactions

## Context

This work extends the polynomial-scaling many-body methods discussed in chapters 8 (coupled cluster), 9 (Monte Carlo), and 10 (in-medium similarity renormalization group) of the same book, providing a complementary approach that focuses on spectral information rather than direct ground state energy evaluation. The self-consistent Green's function method enables calculation of one-nucleon addition and removal energies, spectroscopic factors, and the optical potential for elastic scattering. Recent advances in extending SCGF theory to the Gorkov-Nambu formalism for particle number symmetry breaking (Soma 2011, Idini 2012) have opened the possibility of studying large sets of semi-magic nuclei previously beyond the reach of ab initio theory.
