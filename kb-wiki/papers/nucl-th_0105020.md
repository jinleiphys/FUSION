# Effective hadron masses and couplings in nuclear matter and incompressibility

> The role of effective hadron masses and effective couplings in nuclear matter is studied using a generalized effective Lagrangian for sigma-omega model. A simple relation among the effective masses, the effective couplings and the incompressibility K is derived. Using the relation, it is found that the effective repulsive and the effective attractive forces are almost canceled to each other at the normal density. Inversely, if this cancellation is almost complete, K should be 250-350MeV.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general relation between the incompressibility of nuclear matter and the effective hadron masses and couplings, showing that the effective repulsive and attractive forces nearly cancel at normal nuclear density; if this cancellation is complete, the incompressibility K must lie in the range 250–350 MeV, a condition that is consistent with known nuclear compressional properties.

## Method

The authors start from a generalized effective Lagrangian for the sigma-omega model that includes a limitless number of effective couplings and meson self-interactions, then apply the mean-field approximation to uniform nuclear matter. They derive the equations of motion for the meson mean fields and the meson self-energies at vanishing external momentum, and from these obtain a compact expression for the incompressibility K in terms of the effective nucleon mass m*, the effective meson masses (m_sigma*, m_omega*), and the effective meson-nucleon couplings (g_sigma*, g_omega*). The relation is then evaluated at saturation density for several well-known parameter sets (NL1, NL-SH, TM1) and for four specially constructed parameter sets (A–D) that explore different combinations of density-dependent couplings and self-interactions.

## Key numbers

* For the NL1 parameter set: K = 211 MeV, m*/m = 0.573, and the cancellation quantity -g*(M*^2)^{-1}g* = -12.420 GeV^{-2} at normal density.
* For the NL-SH parameter set: K = 355 MeV, m*/m = 0.597, and the cancellation quantity = 3.0916 GeV^{-2} at normal density.
* For the TM1 parameter set: K = 281 MeV, m*/m = 0.634, and the cancellation quantity = -2.4926 GeV^{-2} at normal density.
* If the cancellation quantity is exactly zero at normal density, the incompressibility K is predicted to be 250–350 MeV.
* The normal density is taken as rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}, the nucleon mass m = 939 MeV, and the binding energy per nucleon a_1 = 16.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier relation of Iwasaki et al. (unreferenced in text by name) by incorporating density-dependent effective couplings and effective omega-meson mass, going beyond the original Walecka model (Walecka, 1974), the relativistic Hartree approximation (Chin, 1977), and nonlinear sigma-omega models with sigma-meson self-interactions (Boguta and Bodmer, 1977; Reinhard et al., 1986; Waldhauser et al., 1988; Sharma et al., 1993) and omega-meson self-interactions (Bodmer, 1991; Sugahara and Toki, 1994; Kouno et al., 1995). The derived relation enables systematic study of how density-dependent effective masses and couplings affect the equation of state at high density, with implications for asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron star structure.
