# Is there model-independent evidence of the two-photon-exchange effect in the electron-proton elastic scattering cross section?

> We re-analyze the data of the elastic electron proton scattering to look for model-independent evidence of the two-photon-exchange (TPE) effect. In contrast to previous analyses, TPE effect is parametrized in forms which are free of kinematical-singularity, in addition to being consistent with the constraint derived from crossing symmetry and the charge conjugation. Moreover, we fix the value of $R=G_E/G_M$ as determined from the data of the polarization transfer experiment. We find that, at high $Q^2 \geq 2 GeV^2$ values, the contribution of the TPE effect to the slope of $σ_R$ vs. $ε$ is large and comparable with that arising from $G_{E}$. It also behaves quasi-linearly in the region of current data, namely, in the range of $0.2 < ε< 0.95$. Hence the fact that the current elastic $ep$ cross section data shows little nonlinearity with respect to $ε$ can not be used to exclude the presence of the TPE effect. More precise data at extreme angles will be crucial for a model-independent extraction of the TPE effect.

## Key claim

The authors find that the two-photon-exchange (TPE) effect contributes significantly to the slope of the reduced cross section at high $Q^2 \geq 2$ GeV$^2$, and that this contribution behaves quasi-linearly in the current data region ($0.2 < \varepsilon < 0.95$), meaning the observed linearity of Rosenbluth plots cannot rule out a large TPE effect.

## Method

The authors re-analyze elastic electron-proton scattering data from Andivahis (1994) using a parametrization of the TPE effect that is analytic at $y=0$ (where $y=\sqrt{(1-\varepsilon)/(1+\varepsilon)}$) and respects crossing symmetry and charge conjugation constraints. They fix the ratio $R=G_E/G_M$ to the values determined from polarization transfer experiments (Jones 2000, Gayou 2002) and perform fits to $\sigma_R(\varepsilon)$ using either a polynomial in $y$ (fit II: $F = A y + B y^3$) or a polynomial plus logarithmic term (fit III: $F = \hat{A} y + \hat{B} y (\ln|y|)^2$), with the coefficients parametrized by a dipole form factor $G_D^2(Q^2)$.

## Key numbers

- At $Q^2 = 5.0$ GeV$^2$, the ratio $C_1/(G_E^2/\tau)$ (TPE contribution to the slope relative to the OPE contribution) reaches approximately 4–6, indicating the TPE effect dominates the slope at high $Q^2$.
- The values of $G_M$ from fits II and III are enhanced by about $2\%$ compared to the Rosenbluth fit.
- The curvature parameter $P_2$ (coefficient of $(\varepsilon-1/2)^2$) is around $-3.15\%$ to $-3.69\%$ in fit II and $-0.99\%$ to $-1.14\%$ in fit III across $Q^2 = 1.75$ to $5.0$ GeV$^2$.
- The ratio $R^{\pm} = \sigma(e^+p)/\sigma(e^-p)$ is predicted to exceed $10\%$ at moderate $Q^2 \geq 1.75$ GeV$^2$ for $\varepsilon < 0.4$.
- In fit II, the coefficients are $\alpha = -0.221$ and $\beta = -0.28$ for $A(Q^2)=\alpha G_D^2$ and $B(Q^2)=\beta G_D^2$.
- In fit III, the coefficients are $\hat{\alpha} = -0.614$ and $\hat{\beta} = -0.205$ for $\hat{A}(Q^2)=\hat{\alpha} G_D^2$ and $\hat{B}(Q^2)=\hat{\beta} G_D^2$.

## Context

This work addresses the discrepancy between the Rosenbluth method and polarization transfer experiments (Jones 2000, Gayou 2002) for extracting the proton form factor ratio $R=G_E/G_M$, which had been proposed to arise from two-photon-exchange effects (Guichon 2003, Blunden 2003, Chen 2004). Previous data analyses (Tomasi-Gustafsson 2005, Tvaskis 2006) claimed no conclusive evidence for TPE nonlinearity in $\sigma_R(\varepsilon)$, but the authors show that quasi-linear TPE behavior can explain both the past success of Rosenbluth separation and the observed discrepancy. The results motivate future positron-proton scattering experiments (Arrington 2004) and more precise data at extreme angles ($\varepsilon < 0.2$ or $\varepsilon > 0.95$) to distinguish between different TPE parametrizations.
