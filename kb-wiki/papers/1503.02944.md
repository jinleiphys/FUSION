# Effects of deformation on the beta-decay patterns of light even-even and odd-mass Hg and Pt isotopes

> Bulk and decay properties, including deformation energy curves, charge mean square radii, Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions, and beta-decay half-lives, are studied in neutron-deficient even-even and odd-A Hg and Pt isotopes. The nuclear structure is described microscopically from deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation calculations with residual interactions in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels, performed on top of a self-consistent deformed quasiparticle Skyrme Hartree-Fock basis. The observed sensitivity of the, not yet measured, GT strength distributions to deformation is proposed as an additional complementary signature of the nuclear shape. The $β$-decay half-lives resulting from these distributions are compared to experiment to demonstrate the ability of the method.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions in neutron-deficient Hg and Pt isotopes are sensitive to nuclear deformation, and proposes that these not-yet-measured distributions could serve as an additional complementary signature of nuclear shape, but the exact isotope where the shape transition occurs depends sensitively on small details of the calculation because the competing shapes are practically degenerate in the transitional region.

## Method

The authors performed deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations with residual interactions in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels, built on a self-consistent deformed quasiparticle Skyrme Hartree-Fock basis with BCS pairing correlations, using the SLy4 interaction primarily. Deformation energy curves were obtained from constrained Hartree-Fock calculations for even-even and odd-A Hg and Pt isotopes, and GT strength distributions and beta-decay half-lives were computed at the equilibrium deformations of each nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Shape transition in Hg isotopes occurs around A=184 for SLy4, where two coexisting shapes are at the same energy; for SGII the transition occurs around A=186 and for Sk3 around A=188
- Shape transition in Pt isotopes occurs around A=188-190 for SLy4, with prolate ground states in lighter isotopes and oblate in heavier ones (A=190,192)
- Coupling strengths used in the QRPA: χ^{ph}_{GT}=0.08 MeV and κ^{pp}_{GT}=0.02 MeV for the particle-hole and particle-particle channels, respectively
- The quenching factor used for half-life calculations is (g_A/g_V)_eff = 0.77(g_A/g_V)_free, with D=6200 s
- Experimental Q_EC values from Audi (2012) were used in half-life calculations
- The lowest excitation energy of 3qp type in odd-A nuclei is of the order of twice the neutron pairing gap, so strength below typically 2-3 MeV corresponds to 1qp transitions

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Sarriguren et al. (2001, 2005) and Moreno et al. (2006) on the decay properties of even-even neutron-deficient Pb, Po, and Hg isotopes, now including odd-A isotopes and discussing sensitivity to spin-parity of the decaying nucleus. The calculations are compared with experimental half-lives and with shape coexistence studies using Gogny forces by Nomura et al. (2013) and Rayner et al. (2010), as well as beyond-mean-field calculations by Yao et al. (2013). The results are timely because measurements of GT strength distributions in neutron-deficient isotopes in the lead region using total absorption spectroscopy techniques are being carried out at ISOLDE/CERN, with data already analyzed for 188,190,192Pb and measurements performed for 182,183,184,186Hg currently being analyzed.
