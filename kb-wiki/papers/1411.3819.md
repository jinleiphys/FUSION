# Description of nuclei around N=20 starting from the Argonne V18 interaction

> Using the Argonne V18 interaction, renormalized with the Lee-Suzuki method, we study nuclei around the $N=20$ island of inversion. We include 5 major oscillator shells, in a no-core approach, using the Hybrid Multi-Determinant method reaching up to few hundreds Slater determinants. Although qualitatively in agreement with the experimental levels, the calculated BE2 do not show the same amount of collectivity seen experimentally.

## Key claim

Using the Argonne V18 interaction renormalized with the Lee-Suzuki method to 5 major harmonic oscillator shells, the calculated BE2 values are systematically too small compared to experiment, indicating insufficient collectivity for Ne and Mg isotopes, although the qualitative trend of the 2+ excitation energies across the N=20 island of inversion is reproduced.

## Method

The bare Argonne V18 interaction was renormalized via the Lee-Suzuki (unitary-model-operator) method at the cluster-2 approximation, retaining only induced two-body interactions. The nuclear wave functions were expanded as linear combinations of up to 250 angular-momentum and parity projected Slater determinants using the Hybrid Multi-Determinant (HMD) variational method, with a spherical harmonic oscillator basis of 5 major shells at a single oscillator frequency of ℏω = 12 MeV. The Hamiltonian included a center-of-mass term to suppress spurious excitations, and the final energies and transition probabilities were obtained by reprojecting with an exact three-dimensional angular momentum projector.

## Key numbers

- For 28Ne: calculated E(2+1) = 1.733 MeV (experimental 1.304 MeV); calculated BE2 = 62.1 e²fm⁴ (experimental 136(23) e²fm⁴)
- For 30Ne: calculated E(2+1) = 1.250 MeV (experimental 0.792 MeV); calculated BE2 = 48.0 e²fm⁴ (experimental 226(35) e²fm⁴)
- For 32Mg: calculated E(2+1) = 1.171 MeV (experimental 0.8853 MeV); calculated BE2 = 57 e²fm⁴ (experimental 434(52) e²fm⁴)
- For 34Mg: calculated E(2+1) = 1.160 MeV (experimental 0.660 MeV); calculated BE2 = 93.2 e²fm⁴ (experimental 573(79) e²fm⁴)
- For 34Si: calculated E(2+1) = 2.316 MeV (experimental 3.327 MeV); calculated BE2 = 37.9 e²fm⁴ (experimental 85(33) e²fm⁴)
- For 36Si: calculated E(2+1) = 1.445 MeV (experimental 1.408 MeV); calculated BE2 = 20.5 e²fm⁴ (experimental 193(59) e²fm⁴)
- For 33Mg, the calculation suggests ground state spin 3/2⁻ with a first excited 7/2⁻ state at 0.1 MeV excitation energy and a 1/2⁻ state at 0.49 MeV

## Context

This work extends no-core shell model techniques to the N=20 island of inversion using a realistic bare NN interaction for the first time, contrasting with traditional shell model calculations that assume an inert 16O core and use effective interactions adapted to the sd-pf space (as in the Monte Carlo Shell Model of Otsuka et al., ref. [5]). The insufficient collectivity and the occupation numbers differing from those found with realistic effective interactions point to the need for larger model spaces (6 or 7 major shells) and possibly alternative single-particle bases such as the Coulomb-Sturmian basis proposed by Caprio et al. (ref. [21]) to improve the description of radii and quadrupole moments. The author plans to apply the same method to the CDBonn2000 and chiral N3LO interactions with different renormalization prescriptions.
