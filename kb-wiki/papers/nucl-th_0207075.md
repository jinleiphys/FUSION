# Explicitly covariant light-front dynamics and some its applications

> The light-front dynamics is an efficient approach to study of field theory and of relativistic composite systems (nuclei at relativistic relative nucleon momenta, hadrons in the quark models). The explicitly covariant version of this approach is briefly reviewed and illustrated by some applications.

## Key claim

The explicitly covariant light-front dynamics (CLFD) provides a satisfactory framework for describing relativistic composite systems, with the deuteron electromagnetic form factors calculated without any fitting parameters showing good agreement with experimental data; however, for three-boson systems with zero-range interaction, the Thomas collapse is avoided but replaced by a "relativistic collapse" where the three-body mass squared becomes negative for two-body masses below a critical value.

## Method

The authors review the explicitly covariant version of light-front dynamics, where the state vector is defined on the light-front plane ω·x=0 with ω²=0, and the wave function depends on both the relative momentum k and the unit vector n (the direction of ω in the center-of-mass system). They present the two-body wave function equation, relate the light-front wave function to the Bethe-Salpeter function via a projection integral, and apply the formalism to calculate the deuteron wave function (with six invariant functions) and its electromagnetic form factors, as well as to solve the three-boson bound state problem with zero-range interaction using Faddeev-type equations.

## Key numbers

- For the three-boson system with zero-range interaction at the zero two-body binding limit (B₂→0), the three-body binding energy is B₃ ≈ 0.012 m, where m is the constituent mass set to 1
- The three-body bound state exists for two-body mass values in the range M_c ≈ 1.43 m ≤ M₂ ≤ 2 m
- When M₂ → 0, the three-body mass squared M₃² tends to approximately -11.6 (in units of m²)
- The critical two-body mass below which the three-body system no longer exists is M_c ≈ 1.43 m
- The deuteron relativistic component f₅ dominates at k > 500 MeV/c

## Context

This review extends the light-front dynamics approach originally formulated by Dirac (1949) and the explicitly covariant version developed by Karmanov (1976). The deuteron calculations are compared to experimental data from CEBAF/TJNAF (1999-2000), and the three-boson results extend prior work by Noyes (1971) and Tobias (1995) on relativistic three-body systems with zero-range interaction, showing that the Thomas collapse (Thomas, 1935) is avoided but replaced by a relativistic collapse at a critical two-body mass. The approach enables further applications to deuteron structure, heavier nuclei, and quark models of hadrons.
