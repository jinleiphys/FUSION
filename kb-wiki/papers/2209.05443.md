# Gluon Transverse-Momentum-Dependent Distributions from Large-Momentum Effective Theory

> We demonstrate that gluon transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) can be extracted from lattice calculations of appropriate Euclidean correlations in large-momentum effective theory (LaMET). Based on perturbative calculations of gluon unpolarized and helicity TMDPDFs, we present a matching formula connecting them and their LaMET counterparts, where the latter are renormalized in a scheme facilitating lattice calculations and converted to the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme. The hard matching kernel is given up to one-loop level. We also show that the perturbative result is independent of the prescription used for the pinch-pole singularity in the relevant correlations. Our results offer a guidance for the extraction of gluon TMDPDFs from lattice simulations, and have the potential to greatly facilitate perturbative calculations of the hard matching kernel.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that gluon transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) can be extracted from lattice QCD calculations via large-momentum effective theory (LaMET), providing a one-loop matching kernel that connects the lattice-calculable quasi-TMDPDFs to the standard TMDPDFs, with the caveat that the perturbative result is independent of the prescription used for the pinch-pole singularity.

## Method

The authors performed one-loop perturbative calculations of both the standard gluon unpolarized and helicity TMDPDFs and their LaMET counterparts (quasi-TMDPDFs) in the Feynman gauge using dimensional regularization, with the delta-regulator for rapidity divergences in the TMDPDF case and finite-length spatial gauge links for the quasi-TMDPDF case. They derived the hard matching kernel by factorizing the renormalized quasi-TMDPDF into the standard TMDPDF, a Collins-Soper evolution part, and a reduced soft function, and verified that the result is independent of the pinch-pole singularity regulator by comparing the delta-prescription with the finite-link-length prescription.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop unpolarized gluon TMDPDF in b-perp space contains the gluon-gluon splitting kernel P_gg = 2C_A[x/(1-x)_+ + (1-x)/x + x(1-x)] + (beta_0/2)delta(1-x), where beta_0 = (11C_A - 4T_F n_f)/3.
* The renormalized subtracted gluon TMDPDF at one-loop includes a term proportional to C_A[ln(mu^2 b_perp^2 e^{2gamma_E}/4)(beta_0/(2C_A) + ln(mu^2/zeta)) - (1/2)ln^2(mu^2 b_perp^2 e^{2gamma_E}/4) - pi^2/12]delta(1-x).
* The polarized gluon TMDPDF g_{1L}^g uses the polarized splitting kernel Delta P_gg = 2C_A[2(1-x) + x/(1-x)_+] + (beta_0/2)delta(1-x).
* The gluon self-energy contribution to the unpolarized TMDPDF at one-loop gives (alpha_s/pi)delta(1-x)delta^{(2)}(k_perp)(5C_A/12 - T_F n_f/3)(1/epsilon_UV - 1/epsilon_IR + ln(mu_UV^2/mu_IR^2)).

## Context

This work extends the LaMET framework previously developed for quark TMDPDFs (Ji 2014, Ji 2019, Ebert 2019) to the gluon case, where experimental knowledge is much more limited due to the dominance of quark-initiated processes in SIDIS and Drell-Yan measurements. The results provide guidance for first-principles lattice QCD calculations of gluon TMDPDFs, which are needed as inputs for describing multi-scale observables at the LHC and for the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider, and the observation of pinch-pole singularity independence simplifies future higher-order calculations of the hard matching kernel.
