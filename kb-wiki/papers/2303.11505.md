# Structure of Nucleons and Their Interaction in the Concept of Nonperturbative QCD as a Pressing Issue of 21st-Century Physics

> The problem of the structure of nucleons and their interaction in the concept of nonperturbative QCD is discussed as an approach to studying the transformation of current quarks into constituent ones and the search for the mechanism of such a transformation, creating the bulk of the nucleon mass. Attention is drawn to the possibilities offered by studying central nucleon-nucleon collisions in this aspect.

# Structure of Nucleons and Their Interaction in the Concept of Nonperturbative QCD as a Pressing Issue of 21st-Century Physics

## Key claim
The Higgs mechanism generates only the masses of leptons and current quarks, not the mass of constituent quarks that form nucleons; at least 80% of the mass of the visible Universe comes from the unknown mechanism that transforms a light (5 MeV) current quark into a heavy (300 MeV) constituent quark. Studying central nucleon-nucleon collisions at the NICA facility's Spin Physics Detector (SPD) in the energy range of 4–28 GeV may reveal this transition, which is expected to occur at a center-of-mass energy near 12 GeV.

## Method
The paper reviews the conceptual and experimental case for using central nucleon-nucleon collisions (impact parameter less than the quark-core radius of about 0.4 fm) to study the nonperturbative QCD regime where constituent quarks transition to current quarks. It identifies the reaction p + p → d(90°) + M (where M is a meson system) as a key channel, and notes that the SPD at NICA is designed to cover the relevant energy range (4–28 GeV) and to perform polarization measurements. The author estimates cross sections for central collisions and for deuteron emission at 90° as experimentally accessible benchmarks.

## Key numbers
- The mass of a current quark is approximately 5 MeV, while a constituent quark mass is approximately 300 MeV.
- The radius of the quark region of nucleons (central collision criterion) is r_c ≈ 0.4 fm.
- The total inelastic cross section for central nucleon-nucleon collisions is σ^inel_cNN = π r_c^2 = 5 mb.
- The differential cross section for isotropic proton emission at 90° from central collisions is about 0.4 mb/sr.
- The ratio of deuteron to proton emission in a wide range of processes is about 3 × 10^{-3}, giving a differential cross section for deuteron emission at 90° of approximately 1 μb/sr.
- The characteristic impulse of spontaneous chirality breaking is P_χ ≈ 1.2 GeV/c.
- The total energy required to destroy all six colliding constituent quarks in central NN collisions is √s_NN ≈ 12 GeV.
- The overlapping energy of the quark content of nucleons begins at √s_NN ≈ 3 GeV.
- The SPD energy range is 4–28 GeV.
- Dibaryon resonances are discussed with masses of 2.1–2.2 GeV, 2.38 GeV, 2.65 GeV, and 3.0 GeV.

## Context
This paper extends the long-standing problem of nucleon mass generation beyond the Higgs mechanism, contrasting with perturbative QCD approaches that yield one-dimensional parton distributions far from the low-energy three-dimensional structure of nucleons. It builds on the chiral model of constituent quarks (cite4 in the text), which reproduces baryon spectra and baryon-baryon interactions, and on prior work by Feynman (1969) on constant high-multiplicity cross sections, as well as experimental observations of dibaryon resonances (cite16–cite21). The proposed program at NICA's SPD would enable a new experimental field for nonperturbative QCD studies, potentially leading to a rigorous nonperturbative QCD theory of baryons, and complements existing high-energy studies at hundreds of GeV and TeV scales that are too energetic to observe the constituent-to-current quark transition.
