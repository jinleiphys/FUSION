# The potential of effective field theory in NN scattering

> We study an effective field theory of interacting nucleons at distances much greater than the pion's Compton wavelength. In this regime the NN potential is conjectured to be the sum of a delta function and its derivatives. The question we address is whether this sum can be consistently truncated at a given order in the derivative expansion, and systematically improved by going to higher orders. Regularizing the Lippmann-Schwinger equation using a cutoff we find that the cutoff can be taken to infinity only if the effective range is negative. A positive effective range---which occurs in nature---requires that the cutoff be kept finite and below the scale of the physics which has been integrated out, i.e. O(m_π). Comparison of cutoff schemes and dimensional regularization reveals that the physical scattering amplitude is sensitive to the choice of regulator. Moreover, we show that the presence of some regulator scale, a feature absent in dimensional regularization, is essential if the effective field theory of NN scattering is to be useful. We also show that one can define a procedure where finite cutoff dependence in the scattering amplitude is removed order by order in the effective potential. However, the characteristic momentum in the problem is given by the cutoff, and not by the external momentum. It follows that in the presence of a finite cutoff there is no small parameter in the effective potential, and consequently no systematic truncation of the derivative expansion can be made. We conclude that there is no effective field theory of NN scattering with nucleons alone.

# The potential of effective field theory in NN scattering

## Key claim

An effective field theory (EFT) of nucleon-nucleon scattering with only nucleons as explicit degrees of freedom cannot be systematically improved by truncating the derivative expansion of the potential, because when a finite cutoff is required to produce a positive effective range (as occurs in nature), the characteristic momentum in the problem is set by the cutoff rather than the external momentum, leaving no small parameter for the expansion.

## Method

The authors studied the $^1S_0$ channel of NN scattering using an EFT with contact interactions only, valid at momenta $k \ll m_\pi$ where pions are integrated out. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a potential consisting of a delta function and its derivatives, regularized with a sharp cutoff, and compared results to dimensional regularization. The scattering amplitude was renormalized by fitting to the experimental scattering length and effective range, and the behavior of the cutoff dependence was analyzed order by order in the derivative expansion.

## Key numbers

- Experimental scattering length: $a = -23.714 \pm 0.013$ fm
- Experimental effective range: $r_e = 2.73 \pm 0.03$ fm
- In the separable potential model, the choices $\Lambda = 152$ MeV and $g = 0.95$ reproduce the experimental $a$ and $r_e$
- The Wigner bound gives $r_e \leq 2[R - R^2/a + R^3/(3a^2)]$, which for zero-range forces ($R \to 0$) forces $r_e \leq 0$

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach to NN scattering proposed by Weinberg (1990, 1991), who argued that power counting should be applied to the effective potential rather than the scattering amplitude. The authors show that the naive EFT with only nucleons fails because of the presence of unnaturally low-energy bound states, contradicting the expectation that such an EFT could be systematically improved. The result that a finite cutoff is required for a positive effective range follows from an old theorem by Wigner (1955) based on causality and unitarity. This work motivates the introduction of explicit dibaryon or other degrees of freedom to represent the low-energy bound states, as pursued by Kaplan (1997).
