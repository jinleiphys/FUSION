# Strongly paired fermions: Cold atoms and neutron matter

> Experiments with cold Fermi atoms can be tuned to probe strongly interacting fluids that are very similar to the low-density neutron matter found in the crusts of neutron stars. In contrast to traditional superfluids and superconductors, matter in this regime is very strongly paired, with gaps of the order of the Fermi energy. We compute the T=0 equation of state and pairing gap for cold atoms and low-density neutron matter as a function of the Fermi momentum times the scattering length. Results of quantum Monte Carlo calculations show that the equations of state are very similar. The neutron matter pairing gap at low densities is found to be very large but, except at the smallest densities, significantly suppressed relative to cold atoms because of the finite effective range in the neutron-neutron interaction.

## Key claim

The zero-temperature equation of state for cold atoms and low-density neutron matter are very similar, but the neutron matter pairing gap is significantly suppressed relative to cold atoms except at the smallest densities, due to the finite effective range in the neutron-neutron interaction.

## Method

Fixed-node quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations were performed for both cold atoms and low-density neutron matter using Jastrow-BCS trial wave functions with fixed particle number and periodic boundary conditions. For cold atoms, the interaction was a simple attractive cosh potential tuned to produce a specific scattering length with nearly zero effective range; for neutron matter, the s-wave part of the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction was used, which correctly reproduces the scattering length of approximately -18.5 fm and effective range of approximately 2.7 fm. The pairing gap was extracted from the odd-even energy staggering formula Δ = E(N+1) - (E(N) + E(N+2))/2 for even particle numbers N.

## Key numbers

- Neutron matter scattering length: approximately -18.5 fm, with effective range r_e approximately 2.7 fm, giving |r_e / a| approximately 0.15
- At unitarity in cold atoms, BCS calculations give a pairing gap of 0.69 E_F while the QMC result is 0.50(3) E_F
- For cold atoms at unitarity, the ratio ξ of the unitary gas energy to the free Fermi gas energy E_FG is 0.42(1) from previous calculations, with extrapolations suggesting ξ = 0.40(1)
- At k_F a = -2.5, where k_F r_e approximately 0.35 for neutron matter, the pairing gaps for cold atoms and neutron matter are similar
- For neutron matter at k_F a = -10, the pairing gap from QMC calculations for N=66-92 particles is consistent within statistical errors and considerably smaller than BCS results

## Context

This work extends previous QMC calculations of the cold atom equation of state by Giorgini (2004) and Chang (2004) to larger system sizes and better optimizations, yielding slightly lower energies (up to approximately 10% in some cases). The neutron matter results are compatible with previous calculations at higher densities (k_F ≥ 1 fm^{-1}) by Akmal (1998), Carlson and Morales (2003), and Schwenk (2005). The pairing gap results are significantly larger than those from diagrammatic approaches by Chen, Wambach, and Schulze, and from renormalization group approaches by Schwenk, but significantly smaller than the AFDMC results of Fabrocini. The work enables cold atom experiments to directly constrain the neutron matter equation of state and pairing gap, which is important for determining neutron star crust structure and the neutron skin thickness of nuclei.
