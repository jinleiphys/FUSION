# Why does the sign problem occur in evaluating the overlap of HFB wave functions?

> For the overlap matrix element between Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states, there are two analytically different formulae: one with the square root of the determinant (the Onishi formula) and the other with the Pfaffian (Robledo's Pfaffian formula). The former formula is two-valued as a complex function, hence it leaves the sign of the norm overlap undetermined (i.e., the so-called sign problem of the Onishi formula). On the other hand, the latter formula does not suffer from the sign problem. The derivations for these two formulae are so different that the reasons are obscured why the resultant formulae possess different analytical properties. In this paper, we discuss the reason why the difference occurs by means of the consistent framework, which is based on the linked cluster theorem and the product-sum identity for the Pfaffian. Through this discussion, we elucidate the source of the sign problem in the Onishi formula. We also point out that different summation methods of series expansions may result in analytically different formulae.

## Key claim

The sign problem in the Onishi formula arises because the linked cluster theorem leads to an infinite series expansion that produces a square root function with two-valued complex logarithm, whereas the overlap matrix element is actually a finite polynomial that is single-valued; the different summation methods (finite series via Pfaffian product-sum versus infinite series via linked cluster expansion) yield analytically different formulae.

## Method

The authors expand the HFB wave functions in the Thouless representation as a finite series of creation operators, then express the overlap matrix element as a sum over Pfaffian products of submatrices of the Thouless matrices Z^0* and Z^1. They apply the product-sum identity for Pfaffians to sum this finite series into Robledo's single Pfaffian formula. Separately, they evaluate the same overlap using Wick contractions and the linked cluster theorem, which produces an infinite series of connected diagrams that sums to a logarithm, yielding the Onishi formula with its square root.

## Key numbers

- The dimension of the Fermion single-particle space is N, and the expansion of the HFB wave function terminates at order N/2 because the number of single-particle states is N.
- The overlap formula with product-sum of Pfaffians involves a summation over 2^(N-1) elements of the index set I^N_{2t}.
- The eigenvalues of the Y = Z^0* Z^1 matrix are doubly degenerate, with pairs {e_i, e_i} for i = 1, ..., N/2, which cancels the 1/2 factor in the logarithm.
- The connected term for k-th order is given by Tr(Y^k)/(-2k), and the infinite series sum over k=1 to infinity of these terms yields the logarithm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Onishi formula (Onishi and Yoshida, 1966) and Robledo's Pfaffian formula (Robledo, 2009) by providing an algebraic derivation that clarifies why the sign problem appears. The linked cluster theorem approach (Bloch and Messiah, 1963) is used to derive the Onishi formula, while the product-sum identity for Pfaffians yields Robledo's formula without Grassmann integrals. The paper enables a deeper understanding of the sign ambiguity in angular momentum projection calculations and explains the origin of the "nodal line" singularities reported by Oi and Tazaki (2005).
