# QCD Phenomenology of Static Sources

> We discuss the spectrum of open string and point particle excitations in QCD with various source representations. Some general relations are introduced and lattice results presented. In particular we discuss the short-distance behaviour, relate this to perturbation theory expectations and comment on the matching between low energy matrix elements and high energy Wilson coefficients, within the framework of effective field theories.

## Key claim

The paper argues that static energies in QCD (such as the binding energy of a static-light meson and gluelump masses) are scheme- and scale-dependent and contain renormalon ambiguities, but that these ambiguities cancel in physical observables; the lattice regularisation with a hard cut-off reveals this structure through power divergences that are indicative of the renormalon structure in dimensional regularisation.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD results for static potentials, static-light meson binding energies, and hybrid excitations, comparing them with perturbative predictions in the RS (renormalon-subtracted) scheme. Correlation functions for static sources in the fundamental and adjoint representations are computed on the lattice, and energy levels are extracted from the large Euclidean time limit. The short-distance behaviour is related to perturbation theory via pNRQCD (potential Non-Relativistic QCD), and the matching between lattice data and continuum schemes is performed by subtracting power divergences and converting to the RS scheme.

## Key numbers

* String breaking in QCD with two flavours of sea quarks occurs at \(r_c \approx 1.27\) fm for light quark masses similar to the strange quark; extrapolation to physical quark masses yields \(r_c \approx 1.16\) fm.
* The energy gap between the two lowest states in the string breaking region is \(\Delta E \approx 50\) MeV, resolved with 10 standard deviations.
* The \(b\) quark mass in the \(\overline{MS}\) scheme is extracted as \(m_{b,\overline{MS}}(m_{b,\overline{MS}}) = [4191 \pm 29(\text{stat.}) \pm 47(\text{th.}) \pm 1 (\Lambda_{\overline{MS}})]\) MeV, with an additional \(O(100\) MeV) quenching uncertainty.
* The gluelump energy in the RS scheme is \(\Lambda_B^{\text{RS}}(1\,\text{GeV}) = [887 \pm 39(\text{latt.}) \pm 83(\text{th.}) \pm 32(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}})]\) MeV.
* The second lightest gluelump (electric, \(1^{--}\)) is about 350–400 MeV heavier than the magnetic gluelump.

## Context

This work reviews and synthesises lattice results on static QCD potentials, extending earlier studies of string breaking in toy models (Jersak 1988) and SU(2)-Higgs models (Philipsen 1998, Knechtli 1998) to full QCD with sea quarks (Bali 2004). It also connects the static-light binding energy to the determination of the \(b\) quark mass via the RS scheme (Bali 2003), and compares hybrid potentials with gluelump spectra (Foster and Michael, lattice short-distance studies). The results enable improved matching between low-energy non-perturbative matrix elements and high-energy Wilson coefficients in effective field theories such as HQET and pNRQCD.
