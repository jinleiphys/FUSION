# Bottomonium above deconfinement in lattice nonrelativistic QCD

> We study the temperature dependence of bottomonium for temperatures in the range $0.4 T_c < T < 2.1 T_c$, using nonrelativistic dynamics for the bottom quark and full relativistic lattice QCD simulations for $N_f=2$ light flavors on a highly anisotropic lattice. We find that the $Υ$ is insensitive to the temperature in this range, while the $χ_b$ propagators show a crossover from the exponential decay characterizing the hadronic phase to a power-law behaviour consistent with nearly-free dynamics at $T \simeq 2 T_c$.

## Key claim
The Upsilon (Υ) meson shows no significant temperature dependence up to 2.09 T_c, while the chi_b (χ_b) propagators exhibit a crossover from exponential decay (bound states) to power-law decay consistent with nearly-free dynamics at T ≃ 2 T_c, based on lattice simulations using nonrelativistic QCD for the bottom quark.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with N_f=2 dynamical light quark flavors on highly anisotropic lattices (ξ = a_s/a_τ = 6) of size 12^3 × N_τ, using nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) for the bottom quark with a mean-field improved action including terms up to O(v^4). They computed bottomonium propagators at four temperatures in the range 0.42 T_c < T < 2.09 T_c, using point and extended sources, and analyzed effective masses and effective power exponents to distinguish exponential decay (bound states) from power-law decay (quasi-free behavior).

## Key numbers
- Temperature range studied: 0.42 T_c to 2.09 T_c, corresponding to 90 MeV to 441 MeV
- Lattice spacing in temporal direction: a_τ^{-1} = 7.35 GeV at lowest temperature, 7.06 GeV at higher temperatures
- Bare bottom quark mass: a_s m_b chosen such that m_b ≃ 5 GeV
- Zero-temperature Υ mass set to 9460 MeV (from Particle Data Book) to fix the scale
- Zero-temperature hyperfine splitting: a_τ ΔE = 0.118(1) for η_b (1^1S_0) and 0.121(1) for Υ (1^3S_1)
- At T = 2.09 T_c, χ_b propagator power-law fit: G(τ) = c τ^{-d} with d = 2.605(1), close to the continuum noninteracting value of 5/2
- Number of configurations: 74 at T = 0.42 T_c, 500 at each higher temperature

## Context
This work extends earlier pioneering NRQCD studies of bottomonium at nonzero temperature by Fingberg (1997) on quenched backgrounds, now using full dynamical light quark configurations. It also builds on the authors' previous relativistic charmonium studies (Aarts 2007, Morrin 2006) using the same anisotropic lattice action. The results enable future extraction of spectral functions via the Maximum Entropy Method and provide a benchmark for comparing NRQCD with relativistic treatments of bottom quarks, as well as for analytic calculations within effective field theory frameworks at nonzero temperature (Laine 2006, Brambilla 2008).
