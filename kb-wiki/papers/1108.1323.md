# Sub-barrier capture with quantum diffusion approach

> With the quantum diffusion approach the behavior of capture cross sections and mean-square angular momenta of captured systems are revealed in the reactions with deformed and spherical nuclei at sub-barrier energies. With decreasing bombarding energy under the barrier the external turning point of the nucleus-nucleus potential leaves the region of short-range nuclear interaction and action of friction. Because of this change of the regime of interaction, an unexpected enhancement of the capture cross section is found at bombarding energies far below the Coulomb barrier. This effect is shown its worth in the dependence of mean-square angular momentum on the bombarding energy. From the comparison of calculated capture cross sections and experimental capture or fusion cross sections the importance of quasifission near the entrance channel is demonstrated for the actinidebased reactions and reactions with medium-heavy nuclei at extreme sub-barrier energies.

## Key claim

The quantum diffusion approach predicts an unexpected enhancement of the capture cross section at bombarding energies far below the Coulomb barrier, because the external turning point of the nucleus-nucleus potential leaves the region of short-range nuclear interaction and friction, changing the regime of interaction. This effect is most pronounced in collisions of spherical nuclei, where the regime change occurs at energies about 3.5–5 MeV below the barrier.

## Method

The authors apply a quantum diffusion approach based on a quantum master-equation for the reduced density matrix, treating the relative distance between colliding nuclei as a single collective variable. The model incorporates fluctuation and dissipation effects to simulate coupling with various channels. Calculated capture cross sections and mean-square angular momenta are compared with experimental capture or fusion cross sections for reactions involving both spherical and deformed nuclei at sub-barrier energies.

## Key numbers

* For the reaction 16O+208Pb, capture cross sections are calculated at center-of-mass energies of 65, 69.5, 73, 83, and 100 MeV (Fig. 1).
* For reactions with deformed nuclei, the regime of interaction changes when the bombarding energy is about 3–18 MeV below the Coulomb barrier.
* For collisions of spherical nuclei, the regime change occurs at energies about 3.5–5 MeV below the Coulomb barrier.
* For the reactions 16O+232Th and 16O+238U, the minima in mean-square angular momentum occur about 7–8 MeV below the corresponding Coulomb barriers.
* The static quadrupole deformation parameters used include: β2(238U)=0.286, β2(232Th)=0.261, β2(34S)=0.125, and β2(64Ni)=0.087.
* The barrier distribution for 16O+238U is calculated with energy increments of 0.2 MeV and 1.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously suggested by the same group (EPJSub) to describe capture processes, and addresses the failure of conventional coupled-channel approaches to describe fusion cross sections below the Coulomb barrier, as noted by Dg. The paper demonstrates that quasifission near the entrance channel is important for actinide-based reactions leading to superheavy nuclei, building on earlier work by Volkov, nasha, and Avaz on the competition between complete fusion and quasifission. The results enable future experiments to search for the predicted changes in capture cross section slope and mean-square angular momentum behavior, and suggest that binary decay events observed in reactions like 58Ni+124Sn by Henning may be related to quasifission rather than deep-inelastic scattering.
