# Particle-hole level densities in deformed nuclei

> Microscopic Combinatorial approach is used to calculate the state and level densities with fixed exciton numbers, in some actinide nuclei. Deformed Saxon-Woods shell model was used as a basis from which all posible configurations were generated. The pairing interaction was taken into account by applying the BCS theory to each configurations. Both the spin and parity distributions were obtained,considering the deformation of the nucleus. Relevance of the result to parity nonconservation studies involving epithermal neutrons on $^{238}$U and $^{232}$Th is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a microscopic combinatorial approach using a deformed Saxon-Woods potential with BCS pairing, applied to actinide nuclei, yields particle-hole level densities that differ significantly from the equidistant-spacing Williams formula, especially for low angular momenta and low exciton numbers; this difference is critical for understanding parity nonconservation (PNC) sign correlations in epithermal neutron reactions on \(^{238}\)U and \(^{232}\)Th, where the standard spherical Gaussian spin factor underestimates the level density by nearly two orders of magnitude for low J.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic combinatorial method to generate all possible particle-hole configurations within a deformed Saxon-Woods single-particle basis, with axial deformation described by Cassini ovaloid parametrization and quadrupole plus hexadecapole deformation parameters. Pairing was treated by applying BCS theory to each configuration via the blocking method, and the spin distribution was obtained using a rotational-band formula with parallel and perpendicular spin-cutoff factors calculated from the single-particle spectrum and moment of inertia, rather than the spherical Gaussian-Wigner factor. The calculations were performed for \(^{233}\)Th and \(^{239}\)U compound nuclei using the ICAR and CONV codes, with single-particle spectra from the BARRIER code using Chepurnov potential parameters.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{233}\)Th and \(^{239}\)U, the 2p-1h level density for J=1/2 from the deformed microscopic calculation can be up to 3 times larger than the Williams formula prediction using standard parameters.
* The spin-cutoff parameter \(\sigma_{\parallel}^2\) shows marked differences between \(^{233}\)Th and \(^{239}\)U as a function of excitation energy, while \(\sigma_{\perp}^2\) behaves similarly for both isotopes due to similar ground-state moments of inertia.
* For low J (e.g., J=1/2) at low excitation energy, the spherical Gaussian spin factor underestimates the level density by nearly two orders of magnitude compared to the deformed rotational-band treatment.
* The parity asymmetry ratio A(U) shows strong oscillatory character up to 15 MeV excitation energy, with the largest fluctuations at lower energies, contradicting the Ericson equal-parity hypothesis.

## Context

This work extends the combinatorial calculations of Herman and Reffo (1987, 1988) from spherical and some deformed nuclei to a systematic treatment of actinide nuclei, incorporating rotational degrees of freedom via the Bjornholm-Bohr-Mottelson spin factor (1973) and using a deformed Saxon-Woods potential with Chepurnov parameters rather than the harmonic oscillator basis of Seeger and Howard (1975). The results are directly relevant to the Feshbach-Hussein-Kerman (1997) doorway-state explanation of sign correlations in PNC epithermal neutron scattering on \(^{232}\)Th and \(^{238}\)U, as the 2p-1h level density is a key input for determining how isolated p-wave resonances are. The work enables more accurate pre-equilibrium reaction calculations for deformed nuclei and provides a framework for reevaluating the parameters of the Williams formula for actinide targets.
