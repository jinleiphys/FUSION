# Isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed Fermi beta decay: Formalism and schematic models

> We study the formalism to include isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections when extracting the up-down Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element from superallowed 0+ -> 0+ nuclear beta decay. We show that there are no first order isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to the relevant nuclear matrix elements. We find corrections to the treatment of Towner and Hardy, and assess these using schematic models of increasing complexity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the isospin-symmetry-breaking (ISB) corrections used by Towner and Hardy (2008) are based on a formalism that does not use the Standard Model isospin operator, and that there are no first-order ISB corrections to the Fermi matrix elements for superallowed beta decay, with corrections starting only at second order.

## Method

The authors derive an exact formalism for ISB corrections using the correct isospin operator \(\tau_+ = \sum_\alpha a_\alpha^\dagger b_\alpha\) and expand the exact initial and final eigenstates of the full Hamiltonian using projection operators. They then compare this exact formalism to the Towner-Hardy treatment by evaluating schematic models of increasing complexity: a one-body problem, a one-body problem with a single core excitation, two interacting nucleons outside an inert core, and two nucleons with a single core excitation.

## Key numbers

- For a single particle outside a \(Z=20\) core with \(l=3\), the simple estimate gives \(\delta_C(3) = 0.17\%\), and with the Macfarlane-French sum rule factor of 3.33, \(\delta_C = 0.57\%\), in qualitative agreement with the Towner-Hardy contribution of \(0.45\%\) for \(^{46}\)V.
- The Towner-Hardy radial correction \(\delta_{C2}\) for \(^{46}\)V is \(0.58\%\) (from their Table I).
- The Towner-Hardy final values for \(\delta_{C2}\) range between \(0.17\%\) and \(1.50\%\), increasing with mass number.
- The Towner-Hardy values for \(\delta_{C1}\) range between \(0.01\%\) and \(0.35\%\), also increasing with mass number.
- For the harmonic oscillator estimate with \(R = 1.1\,\text{fm}\,A^{1/3}\), the scaling \(\delta_C \sim Z^2\) is found, with specific values: \(\delta_C(0) = 0.0020\%\,Z^2\), \(\delta_C(1) = 0.0013\%\,Z^2\), \(\delta_C(2) = 0.00071\%\,Z^2\), \(\delta_C(3) = 0.00043\%\,Z^2\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Towner and Hardy (2008, 2002) for ISB corrections in superallowed Fermi beta decay, which are used to extract the up-down CKM matrix element \(V_{ud}\). The authors identify formal problems with the Towner-Hardy treatment, specifically that they do not use the Standard Model isospin operator and that their separation into \(\delta_{C1}\) and \(\delta_{C2}\) is model-dependent. The theorems derived are more general versions of the Behrends-Sirlin theorem for CVC in nucleons and the Ademollo-Gatto theorem for weak kaon decays. This work enables future systematic improvements to ISB corrections using charge-dependent effective interactions and modern nuclear theory methods.
