# Thermal Dileptons at LHC

> We predict dilepton invariant-mass spectra for central 5.5 ATeV Pb-Pb collisions at LHC. Hadronic emission in the low-mass region is calculated using in-medium spectral functions of light vector mesons within hadronic many-body theory. In the intermediate-mass region thermal radiation from the Quark-Gluon Plasma, evaluated perturbatively with hard-thermal loop corrections, takes over. An important source over the entire mass range are decays of correlated open-charm hadrons, rendering the nuclear modification of charm and bottom spectra a critical ingredient.

# Thermal Dileptons at LHC

## Key claim
Thermal dilepton spectra in central 5.5 ATeV Pb-Pb collisions at LHC are predicted, but the yield from correlated open-charm decays is comparable to hadronic emission already at low mass and dominant at intermediate mass, meaning the thermal signal will be difficult to isolate without accounting for the nuclear modification of heavy-quark spectra in the QGP.

## Method
The authors compute thermal dilepton emission rates using in-medium spectral functions of light vector mesons from hadronic many-body theory for the low-mass region, and hard-thermal loop improved perturbative QCD for quark-gluon plasma radiation in the intermediate-mass region. These rates are evolved over a cylindrical homogeneous thermal fireball with isentropic expansion, using an ideal-gas equation of state for the QGP and a resonance gas for the hadronic phase, with initial conditions at tau_0 = 0.17 fm/c and T_0 = 560 MeV. Non-thermal sources include primordial Drell-Yan annihilation and correlated open-charm decays scaled from RHIC with an anticipated LHC charm cross-section.

## Key numbers
- Central Pb-Pb collisions at 5.5 ATeV (nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair)
- Charged particle multiplicity estimated as dN_ch/dy = 1400
- Initial temperature T_0 = 560 MeV at initial time tau_0 = 0.17 fm/c
- Critical temperature T_c = 180 MeV for the phase transition
- Chemical freezeout at (mu_B^c, T_c) = (2, 180) MeV
- Thermal freezeout temperature T_fo = 100 MeV
- Total fireball lifetime tau_fb = 18 fm/c
- QGP contribution takes over from hadronic radiation at M > 1.1 GeV

## Context
This work extends the approach of Rapp (1999) for in-medium vector-meson spectral functions and van Hees (2006) for four-pion type annihilation with chiral mixing, applying it to LHC energies for the first time. The predictions enable experimental searches for chiral symmetry restoration signals at LHC, but require improved understanding of heavy-quark energy loss in the QGP to disentangle thermal radiation from correlated open-charm and bottom decays.
