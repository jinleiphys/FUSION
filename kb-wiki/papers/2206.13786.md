# Understanding the QCD medium by the diffusion of charm quarks using a Color String Percolation Model

> We study the drag and diffusion coefficients of the charm quark in the deconfined matter produced in the ultra-relativistic collisions by taking the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM) approach. CSPM, being a QCD-inspired model, can give us essential information about the hot and dense system produced in ultra-relativistic collisions. With the information on initial percolation temperature and percolation density, we estimate the relaxation time ($τ_{c}$), drag coefficient ($γ$), transverse momentum diffusion coefficient ($B_{0}$), and spatial diffusion coefficient ($D_{s}$) of charm quark inside a deconfined medium. Finally, we compare the obtained results with lattice QCD and with various other theoretical models. A good agreement can be observed between the results obtained from CSPM and lattice QCD.

# Understanding the QCD medium by the diffusion of charm quarks using a Color String Percolation Model

## Key claim
The Color String Percolation Model (CSPM) can reproduce the drag and diffusion coefficients of charm quarks in deconfined matter, showing good agreement with lattice QCD results, but only under the assumption that the charm quark is non-relativistic and the transferred energy in collisions is small, which the authors justify via the NRQCD framework.

## Method
The authors use the Color String Percolation Model to compute the initial percolation temperature and density of the deconfined medium formed in ultra-relativistic collisions. From these inputs, they estimate the relaxation time of charm quarks using the mean free path formalism, where the relaxation time is given by τ_c = (m_c/T) × L/(1−e^(−ξ)), with L = 1 fm as the longitudinal string extension. The drag coefficient γ is taken as the inverse of the relaxation time, the transverse momentum diffusion coefficient B_0 is obtained from the Einstein relation B_0 = γ T m_c, and the spatial diffusion coefficient D_s is computed as D_s = T/(m_c γ). All results are compared with lattice QCD, perturbative QCD (pQCD), quasi-particle model, T-matrix calculations, and hadron gas diffusion results.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass: m_c ≃ 1.275 GeV
- Single string radius: r_0 ≃ 0.2 fm
- Critical temperature: T_c = 167.7 ± 2.8 MeV
- Critical percolation density: ξ_c ∼ 1.2
- Single string squared average transverse momentum: √⟨p_T²⟩₁ = 207.2 ± 3.3 MeV
- Charm quark relaxation time at T near T_c: ∼ 10 fm/c
- Charm quark relaxation time at T = 300 MeV: ∼ 4 fm/c
- Spatial diffusion coefficient dimensionless product D_s(2πT) approaches a minimum near T_c, consistent with the AdS/CFT bound of 2πT D_s = 1

## Context
This work extends the Color String Percolation Model, previously used to estimate thermodynamic and transport properties of the QGP (Sahu 2020, Mishra 2021, Sahu 2022), to heavy flavor dynamics for the first time. The results are compared with pQCD calculations by vanHees (2004), quasi-particle model by Scardina (2017), lattice QCD by Banerjee (2011) and Ding (2012), T-matrix calculations by Riek (2010), and D meson diffusion in hadron gas by Ozvenchuk (2014) and Tolos (2013). The work enables future studies of heavy flavor dynamics in the high-luminosity ALICE RUN-3 era, particularly for understanding charm and bottom hadron production across a broad multiplicity range.
