# Collective Modes in a Superfluid Neutron Gas within the Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation

> We study collective excitations in a superfluid neutron gas at zero temperature within the quasiparticle random phase approximation. The particle-hole residual interaction is obtained from a Skyrme functional, while a separable interaction is used in the pairing channel which gives a realistic density dependence of the pairing gap. In accordance with the Goldstone theorem, we find an ungapped collective mode (analogous to the Bogoliubov-Anderson mode). At low momentum, its dispersion relation is approximately linear and its slope coincides with the hydrodynamic speed of sound calculated with the Skyrme equation of state. The response functions are compared with those obtained within the Landau approximation. We also compute the contribution of the collective mode to the specific heat of the neutron gas, which is relevant for the thermodynamic properties of the inner crust of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a superfluid neutron gas at zero temperature, the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) predicts an ungapped collective mode (the Bogoliubov-Anderson sound) whose low-momentum dispersion matches the hydrodynamic speed of sound, but only when the same Skyrme interaction is used consistently for both the ground state and the residual particle-hole interaction, and when the pairing interaction is chosen to reproduce a realistic density-dependent pairing gap.

## Method

The authors solve the QRPA equations for uniform neutron matter at zero temperature, using the SLy4 Skyrme energy-density functional to generate the mean field and the residual particle-hole interaction, and a separable interaction (fitted to a low-momentum effective interaction \(V_{\text{low-}k}\)) for the pairing channel. They linearize the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations to obtain the QRPA response functions, and search for poles of the response to identify the collective mode dispersion. The results are compared with the Landau approximation (keeping only \(F_0\) and \(F_1\) parameters) and with hydrodynamic sound speeds computed from the Skyrme equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Maximum pairing gap \(\Delta_{k_F} \sim 2.7\) MeV at Fermi momentum \(k_F \sim 0.8\) fm\(^{-1}\) (density \(\rho \sim 0.017\) fm\(^{-3}\)), obtained with the separable pairing interaction (parameters \(g = 856\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(k_0 = 1.367\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
- At density \(\rho = 0.016\) fm\(^{-3}\), the undamped collective mode carries 71.3% of the total strength at \(q = 0.5\,k_F\), 25.2% at \(q = k_F\), and 9.5% at \(q = 1.3\,k_F\).
- The collective mode enters the two-quasiparticle continuum above \(q \sim 1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\) at \(\rho = 0.016\) fm\(^{-3}\) and above \(q \sim 0.9\) fm\(^{-1}\) at \(\rho = 0.04\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The low-momentum slope \(d\omega/dq\) at \(q=0\) agrees with the hydrodynamic speed of sound calculated from the Skyrme equation of state.

## Context

This work extends previous RPA calculations of collective excitations in neutron matter (Garcia et al., Pastore et al.) by including pairing via the QRPA, and goes beyond the Landau approximation used in earlier superfluid neutron matter studies (Keller et al., Baldo and Ducoin, 2011). The results are relevant for the thermodynamic properties of the inner crust of neutron stars, where the contribution of acoustic phonons to the specific heat (previously studied by Khan and Sandulescu, 2005; Di Gallo et al.) can dominate over that of thermally excited quasiparticles. The paper enables more accurate calculations of the specific heat and neutrino mean-free path in neutron-star crusts by providing the full QRPA response including the coupling of the Goldstone mode to the two-quasiparticle continuum.
