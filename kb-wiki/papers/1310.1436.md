# How learn the branching ratio X(3872)\to D^{*0}\bar D^0 + c.c

> Enfant terrible of charmonium spectroscopy, the resonance $X(3872)$, generated a stream of interpretations and ushered in a new exotic $XYZ$ spectroscopy. In th meantime, many (if not all) characteristics of $X(3872)$ are rather ambiguous. We construct spectra of decays of the resonance $X(3872)$ with good analytical and unitary properties which allows to define the branching ratio of the $X(3872) \to D^{*0}\bar D^0 + c.c.$ decay studying only one more decay, for example, the $X(3872)\toπ^+π^- J/ψ(1S)$ decay. We next define the range of values of the coupling constant of the $X(3872)$ resonance with the $D^{*0}\bar D^0$ system. Finally, we show that our spectra are effective means of selection of models for the resonance $X(3872)$.

## Key claim

The authors construct decay spectra for the X(3872) resonance with good analytical and unitary properties that allow determination of the branching ratio for X(3872) → D*0D̅0 + c.c. by studying only one additional decay channel, such as X(3872) → π+π− J/ψ(1S), without needing to know the branching ratio of that second channel.

## Method

The authors model the X(3872) as a 1++ axial vector and describe the S-wave X(3872) → D*0D̅0 decay via an effective Lagrangian with coupling constant gA. They construct mass spectra for both the D*0D̅0 channel and non-D*0D̅0 channels using a propagator D_X(m) that includes the D*0D̅0 self-energy function Π_X(m), ensuring unitarity. They fit their theoretical spectra to Belle data on the π+π−J/ψ(1S) and D*0D̅0 invariant mass distributions, extracting the coupling constant gA and the total width Γ of decays into non-D*0D̅0 channels, from which the branching ratio BR(X → D*0D̅0 + c.c.) follows.

## Key numbers

- The fitted total width for non-D*0D̅0 decays is Γ = 1.2−0.467 MeV (Table I, central fit without D*+D− channel).
- The fitted coupling constant squared divided by 8π is gA2/8π = 0.857−0.481+3.614 GeV2 (Table I).
- The resulting branching ratio BR(X → D*0D̅0 + c.c.) = 0.795−0.224+0.19 (Table I).
- The branching ratio for the seen part of the D*0D̅0 spectrum (m ≤ 3891.84 MeV) is BRseen = 0.486−0.29+0.061 (Table I).
- When including the D*+D− channel, the fitted gA2/8π = 1.36−0.95+4.85 GeV2, with BR(X → D*0D̅0 + c.c.) = 0.586−0.101+0.025 and BR(X → D*+D− + c.c.) = 0.315−0.16+0.132 (Table II).
- For the model of Maiani et al. with mX = 3871.68 MeV and Γ = 1.2 MeV, gA2/8π = 0.1 GeV2 gives BR(X → D*0D̅0 + c.c.) ≈ 0.189 and BR(X → Others) = 0.792 (Table III).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Achasov and collaborators (prior papers on scalar meson spectra with good analytical and unitary properties) to the exotic X(3872) resonance, which was the first discovered member of the XYZ spectroscopy. The method provides a tool to discriminate between theoretical models for the X(3872); for example, the model of Maiani et al. (2005) predicts a small branching ratio to D*0D̅0 (≈0.2) implying dominant unknown decays, while other models with larger coupling would give a larger D*0D̅0 branching ratio. The approach enables determination of the D*0D̅0 branching ratio from any single non-D*0D̅0 decay channel once high-statistics data become available.
