# Causal-Horizon Scaling of Quarkonium Suppression in Strong QCD Fields

> The simultaneous observation of strong sequential suppression and small azimuthal anisotropy for bottomonium states provides a stringent constraint on the time scale and geometry of the suppression mechanism. We propose an early-time geometric survival mechanism in which the strong pre-equilibrium color field induces a local proper acceleration and an associated Unruh causal scale. The survival probability is modeled by a WKB-motivated exponential controlled by the ratio of the quarkonium radius to the causal horizon. Combined with CGC-inspired centrality and energy scaling, the framework gives a compact description of the $Υ(1S, 2S, 3S)$ suppression pattern and predicts a definite RHIC/LHC energy dependence. Because the suppression acts as a local scalar factor before hydrodynamic response develops, it naturally produces little additional quarkonium momentum anisotropy.

# Causal-Horizon Scaling of Quarkonium Suppression in Strong QCD Fields

## Key claim
The observed sequential suppression and small azimuthal anisotropy of bottomonium states can be explained by an early-time geometric survival mechanism based on a causal horizon scale, without requiring late-stage transport through quark-gluon plasma. The survival probability is modeled by a WKB-motivated exponential controlled by the ratio of the quarkonium radius to the causal horizon, and the framework predicts a definite energy dependence between RHIC and LHC that is consistent with current data within uncertainties.

## Method
The authors model the strong pre-equilibrium color field (Glasma) as inducing a local proper acceleration on heavy quarks, which generates an Unruh causal horizon at distance r_H = 1/a. The survival probability for each bottomonium state is taken as exp(-r_nS / r_H), where r_nS is the spectroscopic vacuum radius. The acceleration scale is anchored to the QCD pseudocritical temperature T_c in central collisions and scaled with centrality via N_part^(1/3) and with collision energy via a CGC-inspired power law (sqrt(s_NN))^λ with λ ≈ 0.2-0.3.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum radii used: r_1S ≈ 0.28 fm, r_2S ≈ 0.56 fm, r_3S ≈ 0.78 fm
- Central acceleration scale κ = 0.63 fm^(-1), anchored to T_c ≈ 140-150 MeV with N_part,max ≈ 380
- Energy scaling exponent λ ∈ [0.2, 0.3] for the CGC saturation scale evolution
- RHIC energy sqrt(s_RHIC) = 200 GeV compared to LHC energy sqrt(s_LHC) = 5.02 TeV
- The suppression acts at timescales τ ≲ 0.1 fm/c, before hydrodynamic response develops

## Context
This work extends the conceptual framework of Unruh-like thermal phenomena in accelerated systems (Castorina 2007, Hawking 1975) to heavy quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with macroscopic transport models and open quantum system frameworks (Rapp 2017, Du 2017, Brambilla 2020, Akamatsu 2020, Strickland 2011, Yao 2020) that accumulate suppression over the QGP lifetime and tend to generate positive elliptic flow. The approach also differs from conventional nuclear absorption or comover interaction models (Gerschel 1998, Ferreiro 2018) and Debye screening (Matsui 1986, Mocsy 2013) by acting as a local scalar factor before hydrodynamic response develops. The framework enables a compact description of the CMS, ALICE, ATLAS, and STAR bottomonium data and provides a testable prediction for the energy dependence of suppression between RHIC and LHC.
