# Helium-3 production from Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies with the UrQMD model and the traditional coalescence afterburner

> A potential version of the UrQMD (UrQMD/M) transport model and a traditional coalescence model are combined to calculate the production of $^3$He fragments in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies 20-80 GeV/nucleon. It is found that the Lorentz transformation in the afterburner influences visibly the $^3$He yield and should be considered in calculations. The rapidity distribution of $^3$He multiplicities (including the concave shape) can be described well with UrQMD/M when it stops during t$_{\rm cut}$=100$\pm$25 fm$/c$ and the coalescence afterburner with one parameter set of ($R_0$,$P_0$)=(3.8 fm, 0.3 GeV$/$c) is taken into use afterwards.

## Key claim

The UrQMD/M transport model combined with a traditional phase-space coalescence afterburner can describe the rapidity distribution and integrated multiplicity of 3He fragments in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies 20-80 GeV/nucleon, but only when the transport model is stopped at a specific time window of t_cut = 100 ± 25 fm/c and the Lorentz transformation in the afterburner is taken into account.

## Method

The authors used the potential version of the UrQMD model (UrQMD/M) to simulate central Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies of 20, 30, 40, 80 GeV/nucleon, stopping the transport at various times t_cut between 25 and 125 fm/c. They then applied a traditional phase-space coalescence afterburner with fixed parameters (R_0 = 3.8 fm, P_0 = 0.3 GeV/c) to construct 3He clusters from baryons, explicitly including the Lorentz transformation when computing relative distances and momenta in the center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- The coalescence afterburner parameter set used is (R_0, P_0) = (3.8 fm, 0.3 GeV/c), the same set previously used for proton production.
- The UrQMD/M transport model is stopped at t_cut = 100 ± 25 fm/c to achieve agreement with NA49 data.
- At t_cut = 25 fm/c, the calculated 3He multiplicities are at least one order of magnitude larger than experimental data.
- The rapidity-integrated multiplicity (|y| < 1.7) of 3He fragments is calculated as a function of beam energy from 20 to 80 GeV/nucleon and compared to NA49 data from Blume (2007).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Li (2015), who used the same UrQMD/M model and coalescence parameters to describe proton rapidity distributions at AGS and SPS energies. The present study applies the method to light fragment (3He) production, finding that the Lorentz transformation in the afterburner has a larger visible effect on 3He yields than on free nucleon yields. The results support the coalescence mechanism for light fragment production and indicate that proper treatment of the time evolution and sequential decay of excited fragments is essential for describing experimental data from the NA49 collaboration (Blume, 2007; Kolesnikov, 2007).
