# A See-Saw Mechanism with light sterile neutrinos

> The usual see-saw mechanism for the generation of light neutrino masses is based on the assumption that all of the flavours of right-handed (more properly, sterile) neutrinos are heavy. If the sterile Majorana mass matrix is singular, one or more of the sterile neutrinos will have zero mass before mixing with the active (left-handed) neutrinos and be light after that mixing is introduced. In particular, a rank 1 sterile mass matrix leads naturally to two pseudo-Dirac pairs, one very light active Majorana neutrino and one heavy sterile Majorana neutrino. For any pattern of Dirac masses, there exists a region of parameter space in which the two pseudo-Dirac pairs are nearly degenerate in mass. This, in turn, leads to large amplitude mixing of active states as well as mixing into sterile states.

## Key claim

If the sterile Majorana mass matrix is singular (specifically rank 1), the usual see-saw assumption that all sterile neutrinos are heavy can be relaxed, producing two pseudo-Dirac pairs and one very light active Majorana neutrino plus one heavy sterile Majorana neutrino; for any pattern of Dirac masses there exists a region of parameter space where the two pseudo-Dirac pairs are nearly degenerate, leading to large-amplitude mixing of active states as well as mixing into sterile states.

## Method

The authors construct a 6×6 mass matrix in block form (zero active Majorana block, Dirac mass matrix M_D, and a rank-1 sterile Majorana mass matrix M_S parameterized by two angles θ and φ with a single mass scale M). They solve the secular equation analytically for special alignments and numerically for general angles, using Dirac masses m_1=1, m_2=2, m_3=3 (in arbitrary units) and M=1000. They then specialize to a two-flavor subspace to obtain analytic expansions in powers of 1/M, and finally examine the case where one Dirac mass (m_2=1.1) is tuned to be nearly degenerate with the pseudo-Dirac pair by choosing θ=9.324° and varying φ.

## Key numbers

- For the numerical example with m_1=1, m_2=2, m_3=3, M=1000: the two close pseudo-Dirac pairs have eigenvalues between 1 and 3 (e.g., at θ=π/8, φ=π/8: 1.398125 and 1.394934 for the smaller pair; 2.038992 and 2.038729 for the next pair).
- The light Majorana eigenvalue is of order 10^{-3} (e.g., 0.00444 at θ=π/8, φ=π/8) and the heavy sterile eigenvalue is of order 10^{3} (e.g., 1000.008 at θ=π/8, φ=π/8).
- For the nearly degenerate case with m_1=1, m_2=1.1, m_3=3, M=1000, θ=9.324078°, φ=0: the pseudo-Dirac pair masses are 1.099328 and 1.100680, bracketing the Dirac mass m_2=1.100000; the light Majorana mass is 0.007438; the heavy sterile mass is 1000.008789.
- At φ=2.25°: the four nearly degenerate masses become 1.095953, 1.096608, 1.103359, 1.104056, with significant mixing between active states 1 and 2.

## Context

This work extends the standard see-saw mechanism (see-saw, 1979) by relaxing the assumption that all sterile neutrinos are heavy, following earlier theoretical suggestions of pseudo-Dirac neutrinos (GSMcK, KC). It contrasts with early discussions (Wolfenstein, 1978) that implicitly assumed a unit matrix for the sterile sector. The results are motivated by Super-Kamiokande atmospheric neutrino data (SuperK, 1998) which require nearly maximal mixing, though pure mixing to sterile states is disfavored (atmos). The paper enables a framework where large active-sterile mixing can arise naturally from a rank-1 sterile mass matrix, and suggests further exploration of parameter space for oscillation phenomenology.
