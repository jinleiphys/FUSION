# Absence of strong CP violation

> Quantum Chromodynamics admits a CP-violating contribution to the action, the $θ$ term, which is expected to give rise to a nonvanishing electric dipole moment of the neutron. Despite intensive search, no CP violations have been found in the strong interaction. This puzzle is referred to as the strong CP problem. There is evidence that CP is conserved in the confining theory, to the extent that color charges are totally screened for $θ> 0$ at large distances. It is not immediately obvious that this implies a vanishing dipole moment though. With this Letter I will close the gap. It is shown that in the infinite volume hadron correlation functions decouple from the topological charge, expressed in terms of the zero modes. The reason is that hadrons have a limited range of interaction, while the density of zero modes vanishes with the inverse root of the volume, thus reducing the probability of finding a zero mode in the vicinity to zero. This implies that CP is conserved in the strong interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that strong CP violation vanishes in the infinite volume limit because hadron correlation functions decouple from topological charge: the density of zero modes of the Dirac operator scales as the inverse square root of the volume, so the probability of finding a zero mode within the interaction range of a hadron goes to zero, implying that CP is conserved in the strong interaction for any value of the theta parameter that keeps quarks bound.

## Method

The author expresses the topological charge Q in terms of zero-mode eigenvectors of the overlap Dirac operator, using the Atiyah-Singer index theorem and the "vanishing theorem" which states that gauge configurations of charge Q have exactly |Q| zero modes of a single chirality. The electric dipole moment of the neutron is written as a correlation function involving the topological charge and the electromagnetic current, and the probability of finding a zero mode within a reference volume of about (2.5 fm)^4 is computed from the topological susceptibility and the volume scaling of the zero-mode density.

## Key numbers

- Topological susceptibility for N_f=2+1 flavors at the physical point: chi_t = (79 MeV)^4, derived from the quenched result chi_t^{YM} = (179 MeV)^4 using chiral perturbation theory.
- On a (5 fm)^4 lattice volume, the fraction of zero modes in a 1 fm^4 subvolume is shown; increasing volume to (10 fm)^4 and (20 fm)^4 shrinks this fraction by factors of 4 and 16 respectively.
- On a 64^3 x 128 lattice at a=0.08 fm (total volume ~ (5.12 fm)^4), the probability of finding a zero mode within the reference volume V_0 is approximately 15%; on a 128^3 x 256 lattice it drops to approximately 4%.
- The dipole moment vanishes in the infinite volume as |d_n| proportional to sqrt(chi_t / V) times |theta|.
- The CP-violating gluon condensate (1/V)langle Qrangle scales as -i theta sqrt(2/pi) sqrt(chi_t / V) to leading order in theta.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous argument (Nakamura and Schierholz, 2019, 2021) that confinement implies CP invariance, closing a loophole about whether CP violation could still arise from correlations of the theta term with hadronic currents. It contradicts the dilute instanton gas approximation of the QCD vacuum, which assumes mixed instanton-anti-instanton states incompatible with the vanishing theorem. The result is consistent with lattice calculations of the neutron electric dipole moment: calculations on smaller volumes (Liang et al., 2023) find nonzero values, while calculations on larger volumes (Alexandrou et al., 2020) obtain values consistent with zero. The paper also references the earlier observation by Leutwyler and Smilga (1992) that the number of zero modes per unit volume tends to zero as V tends to infinity.
