# Spectra of Identified Hadrons in Pb-Pb Collisions at LHC

> The transverse momentum distributions of identified hadrons produced in Pb-Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are studied in the low and intermediate range for $p_T<5$ GeV/c. All four spectra ($π, K, p, Λ$) can be well reproduced in the recombination model based on a common thermal parton distribution of light and strange quarks and on shower partons emitted in hard and semihard jets. Two essential parameters are adjusted to fit the data, one being the inverse slope of the thermal distribution and the other revealing the degree of momentum degradation in the medium. Various combinations of thermal and shower parton components are calculated, showing the dominance of minijets. The effect of minijets is to produce harder baryons than mesons resulting in their ratio to peak at around $p_T \sim 3$ GeV/c. Substantial portion of the jet energy is found to be lost to the dense medium before the partons emerge at the surface to undergo hadronization by recombination.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, protons, and lambdas in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC can be reproduced by the recombination model using a common thermal parton distribution for light and strange quarks combined with shower partons from minijets, with only two adjustable parameters: the inverse slope of the thermal distribution and a parameter describing momentum degradation in the medium. The abstract's claim of "well reproduced" spectra depends on the specific values of these two parameters (T = 0.38 GeV and κ = 2.6) and applies only for pT < 5 GeV/c, with the pion spectrum requiring additional resonance contributions below 1.5 GeV/c that are not calculated within the formalism.

## Method

The authors used the recombination model to compute hadron spectra from thermal partons (exponential distribution with inverse slope T) and shower partons from hard and semihard gluon jets, where the shower parton distributions were derived from fragmentation functions. The effect of the dense medium on parton energy loss was parametrized through a momentum degradation factor κ, such that the average parton momentum at the medium surface is q = k/κ, where k is the initial parton momentum. The model was applied to central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, with the parton creation distributions at 2.76 TeV obtained by logarithmic interpolation between values at 0.2 TeV and 5.5 TeV from prior work (Hwa and Yang, 2008).

## Key numbers

- Thermal inverse slope parameter: T = 0.38 GeV, determined from fitting all four hadron spectra (compared to T = 0.32 GeV at RHIC)
- Momentum degradation parameter: κ = 2.6, implying approximately 60% of initial parton energy is lost to the medium (1 - 1/κ)
- Thermal parton normalization constant: C = 23.2 (GeV/c)^{-1}, taken from the RHIC value
- Gluon jet creation distribution parameters at 2.76 TeV: A = 6.2 × 10^4 GeV^{-2}, B = 0.98 GeV, n = 6.22
- The p/π ratio peaks at pT ≈ 3 GeV/c, and the Λ/K ratio shows a peak at pT = 3 GeV/c exceeding 1.5 at 0-5% centrality
- The TS (thermal-shower) component for pions is larger than the TT (thermal-thermal) component for all pT > 0.5 GeV/c at LHC, whereas at RHIC TT dominates TS for pT < 3 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the recombination model previously applied to RHIC data (Hwa and Yang, 2004; Hwa and Yang, 2005) to the higher energy LHC regime, where minijets are much more copious. The approach contrasts with hydrodynamical descriptions that invoke mass-dependent flow effects (PHENIX Collaboration, 2005), instead explaining the harder baryon spectra through the larger phase space for three-quark recombination involving harder shower partons. The results enable future study of hadron production at higher pT (> 5 GeV/c) and provide a framework for understanding the transition from thermal to jet-dominated hadronization without relying on perturbative QCD or hydrodynamics.
