# Backward DVCS in a Sullivan process

> Mesons' internal structure and dynamics may be accessed through hard exclusive electroproduction processes such as deeply virtual Compton scattering in both near forward and near backward kinematics. With the help of the Sullivan process which allows us to use a nucleon target as a quasi-real $π$ meson emitter, we study backward scattering in the framework of collinear QCD factorization where pion-to-photon transition distribution amplitudes describe the photon content of the $π$ meson. We present a model of these TDAs based on the overlap of lightfront wave functions primarily developed for generalized parton distributions, using a previously developed pion lightfront wave function and deriving a new model for the lightfront wave functions of the photon. This leads us to an estimate of the cross-sections for JLab energies. We conclude that deeply virtual $ep\rightarrow eγM n$ processes, in backward kinematics, may be experimentally discovered in the near future.

## Key claim

The authors model pion-to-photon transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) using lightfront wave functions and estimate cross-sections for backward deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) on a virtual pion target via the Sullivan process, concluding that such processes may be experimentally observable at JLab energies, though the vector TDA model shows a roughly 50% discrepancy with the experimental vector transition form factor at zero momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors constructed models for axial and vector pion-to-photon TDAs using the overlap of two-body lightfront wave functions for the pion and the photon, deriving the DGLAP region from the overlap representation and extending to the ERBL region via a covariant extension method based on double distributions. They then computed the amplitudes for the electroproduction processes \(\gamma^{\ast}(q)+\pi^{+}(p_\pi) \rightarrow \gamma (q') + \pi^{+}(p'_{\pi})\) and \(\gamma^{\ast}(q)+\pi^{+}(p_{\pi}) \rightarrow \gamma(q') + \rho^{+}(p'_{\rho})\) in backward kinematics, evaluating cross-sections at JLab 22 kinematics energies.

## Key numbers

- The pion lightfront wave function mass scale is \(M = 318\ \text{MeV}\), fitted to experimental data for the \(\pi^{+}\) electromagnetic form factor.
- The axial transition form factor at zero momentum transfer from the model is \(F_{A}^{\pi\gamma}(0) = 0.0113\), compared to the PDG 2024 value of \(0.0119 \pm 0.0001\) (a 5% discrepancy).
- The vector transition form factor at zero momentum transfer from the model is \(F_{V}^{\pi\gamma}(0) = 0.039\), compared to the PDG 2024 value of \(0.0254 \pm 0.0017\) (roughly 50% discrepancy).
- The pion decay constant used is \(f_{\pi} = 130.02 \pm 1.2\ \text{MeV}\) and the pion mass is \(m_{\pi} = 139.57039 \pm 0.00018\ \text{MeV}\), from the Particle Data Group (2024).
- The covariant extension for the axial TDA used a mesh with maximum element-area of 0.09, resulting in \(n_{e} = 14\) triangles sampled with \(12 n_{e}\) DGLAP lines; for the vector TDA, \(n_{e} = 59\) elements with maximum element-area of 0.025 were sampled with \(30 n_{e}\) lines.

## Context

This work extends the study of backward DVCS processes, previously focused on nucleon-to-meson or nucleon-to-photon TDAs (Pire 2004, Pire 2021), to meson-to-photon TDAs accessed via the Sullivan process (Sullivan 1971). The Sullivan process has been used to extract pion form factors (NA7 1986, JeffersonLab 2008), parton distribution functions (Barry 2018, Barry 2021), and transverse momentum dependent PDFs (Barry 2023), and this work enables future experimental searches for backward DVCS on virtual pion targets at JLab, complementing forward DVCS studies on virtual pions (Amrath 2008, Chavez 2021). The TDA model builds on lightfront wave function approaches developed for generalized parton distributions (Mezrag 2016, Chouika 2017) and the covariant extension method (Chouika 2017, Chavez 2021, DallOlio 2024).
