# On the thermodynamically consistent quasiparticle model of quark gluon plasma

> We give the alternative formulation of quasiparticle model of quark gluon plasma with medium dependent dispersion relation. The model is thermodynamically consistent provided the medium dependent contribution to the energy density is taken in to account. We establish the connection of our model with other variants of quasiparticle models which are thermodynamically consistent. We test the model by comparing the equation of state with the lattice gauge theory simulations of SU(3) pure gluodynamics .

## Key claim

The paper presents an alternative formulation of the quasiparticle model for quark gluon plasma where thermodynamic consistency is achieved by adding an extra medium-dependent term to the energy density rather than to the pressure, provided one uses the statistical mechanics definition of energy density based on derivatives of the partition function rather than the direct thermal average of the Hamiltonian.

## Method

The author derives expressions for pressure and energy density starting from the grand canonical partition function for a system with a medium-dependent dispersion relation, where the quasiparticle mass depends on temperature. The model is tested by comparing the resulting equation of state for pure SU(3) gluodynamics and for two-flavor quark gluon plasma against lattice QCD simulation data, using a running coupling ansatz with two fitting parameters (T_s/T_c and T_c/lambda) and an adjusted gluon degeneracy factor.

## Key numbers

- For pure SU(3) gluodynamics, the fitting parameters are lambda = 5.7, T_s/T_c = -0.4, and gluon degeneracy d_g = 18.
- For two-flavor (N_f = 2) quark gluon matter, the fitting parameters are lambda = 2.5, T_s/T_c = -0.8, and gluon degeneracy d_g = 16.
- The quasiparticle mass squared for gluons in the hard thermal loop approximation is given by m*^2 = (1/6) * {(N_c + 0.5*N_f)*T^2 + (3/(2*pi^2))*sum_q(mu_q^2)} * G^2(T,mu), where N_c is the number of color charges and N_f is the number of flavors.
- The effective coupling ansatz is G^2(T) = 48*pi^2 / [(11*N_c - 2*N_f) * ln(lambda*(T+T_s)/T_c)^2].

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle model approach initiated by Goloviznin and Satz (1993) and addresses the thermodynamic consistency issue first raised by Gorenstein and Yang (1995), who introduced a medium-dependent vacuum energy term to restore consistency. The model is an alternative to the q-potential formulation of Bannur (2007), where the pressure rather than the energy density acquires an extra term; both approaches avoid explicit introduction of a vacuum energy term. The model enables further studies of transport coefficients and photon/dilepton production rates in heavy-ion collisions using a thermodynamically consistent framework.
