# Electroweak processes of the deuteron in effective field theory

> We review our recent calculations of electroweak processes involving the deuteron, based on pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields. These calculations are concerned with neutron-neutron fusion and np -> d gamma at BBN energies.

# Electroweak processes of the deuteron in effective field theory

## Key claim
Pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields (dEFT) can describe neutron-neutron fusion and np → dγ at big-bang nucleosynthesis energies, but the nn-fusion cross section carries a ∼4% uncertainty dominated by current experimental errors in the neutron-neutron scattering length and effective range, while the np → dγ cross section is reliable to within 1% when combined with a Markov Chain Monte Carlo analysis of experimental data.

## Method
The author applies pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields (dEFT) to calculate amplitudes for nn-fusion and np → dγ up to next-to-leading order (NLO). For nn-fusion, the Fermi function and α-order radiative corrections for the one-body interaction are included to ensure better than 1% accuracy in the cross section. For np → dγ, a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) analysis is performed on low-energy np scattering data (2124 data points at energies ≤5 MeV), np → dγ cross sections, dγ → np cross sections, and photon analyzing power data to determine the low-energy constants a₀, r₀, ρ_d, and l₁.

## Key numbers
- nn-fusion total cross section for ultra-cold neutrons (v ≈ 10 m/s): σ = (38.6 ± 1.5) × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²
- Maximum electron energy from nn-fusion: E_e^max ≈ 3.52 MeV (deuteron binding energy B plus neutron-proton mass difference δ_N = 1.29 MeV)
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a₀^nn = −18.5 ± 0.4 fm
- Neutron-neutron effective range: r₀^nn = 2.80 ± 0.11 fm
- Low-energy constant from neutron β-decay: (α/2π) e_V^R = (2.01 ± 0.40) × 10⁻²
- Low-energy constant l₁A = −0.33 ± 0.03 (from pionful EFT)
- np → dγ cross section at thermal energy (E = 1.265 × 10⁻⁸ MeV): 333.8(4) mb (dEFT with MCMC)
- np → dγ cross section at E = 0.100 MeV: 0.06341(7) mb (dEFT with MCMC)
- np → dγ cross section at E = 1.00 MeV: 0.03502(10) mb (dEFT with MCMC)

## Context
This work extends pionless EFT calculations of electroweak processes involving the deuteron, following earlier studies of neutron β-decay by Ando et al. (2004) and the development of dibaryon EFT by Ando and Hyun (2005). For np → dγ, the results are compared with the N⁴LO pionless EFT calculation by Rupak (2000), a high-precision potential model with meson-exchange currents by Nakamura, and an R-matrix analysis by Hale. The nn-fusion calculation highlights that the dominant uncertainty comes from the neutron-neutron scattering parameters a₀^nn and r₀^nn, motivating improved measurements at facilities such as Oak Ridge National Laboratory and J-PARC. The combination of dEFT with MCMC analysis provides a systematic method to extract reliable cross sections for few-body processes relevant to big-bang nucleosynthesis.
