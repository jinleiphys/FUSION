# Comment on "Lattice determination of $Σ$-$Λ$ mixing"

> A recent lattice QCD (LQCD) calculation of $Σ$-$Λ$ mixing by the QCDSF-UKQCD Collaboration [Phys. Rev. D 91, 074512 (2015)] finds a mixing angle about half of that found from the Dalitz-von Hippel (DvH) flavor SU(3) mass formula which relates the $Σ$-$Λ$ mixing matrix element to known octet baryon mass differences and which has been used widely to evaluate charge symmetry breaking effects in $Λ$ hypernuclei. We show that the LQCD-calculated $Σ$-$Λ$ mixing matrix element and octet baryon masses satisfy the DvH mass formula, concluding thereby that a good LQCD evaluation of $Σ$-$Λ$ mixing requires an equally good reproduction of octet baryon mass differences which is yet to be demonstrated.

# Comment on "Lattice determination of Σ-Λ mixing"

## Key claim

The LQCD-calculated Σ-Λ mixing matrix element and octet baryon masses satisfy the Dalitz-von Hippel (DvH) SU(3) mass formula, but the LQCD calculation's reproduction of the individual octet baryon mass differences that enter the formula is still far from PDG values, so the reliability of the LQCD mixing angle is questionable until those mass differences are accurately reproduced.

## Method

The author evaluates the DvH mass formula relating the Σ-Λ mixing matrix element to isospin-breaking mass differences within the baryon octet: ⟨Σ|δM|Λ⟩ = (1/√3)[(M_Σ⁰ − M_Σ⁺) − (M_n − M_p)]. The LQCD-calculated octet baryon masses from the QCDSF-UKQCD Collaboration are inserted into this formula and compared to the PDG-based result, and the Coleman-Glashow mass formula is also checked for consistency.

## Key numbers

- PDG input masses yield ⟨Σ|δM|Λ⟩ = 1.14(05) MeV and mixing angle α = 0.015(1)
- LQCD input masses yield ⟨Σ|δM|Λ⟩ = 0.52(23) MeV and mixing angle α = 0.007(3)
- The LQCD-calculated mixing angle α_LQCD = 0.006(3) agrees with the value 0.007(3) obtained from the DvH formula using LQCD masses
- The electromagnetic contribution to the DvH mass formula left-hand side is estimated as order 0.1 MeV

## Context

This comment extends the analysis of the QCDSF-UKQCD Collaboration (2015) lattice calculation of Σ-Λ mixing, which found a mixing angle about half the value from the Dalitz-von Hippel (1964) flavor SU(3) mass formula. The DvH formula has been widely used to evaluate charge symmetry breaking effects in Λ hypernuclei, as referenced in recent experimental and theoretical work by MAMI (2015) and Gal (2015). The comment also notes that the BMW Collaboration (2015) has calculated the relevant baryon mass differences including electromagnetic effects, showing good agreement with PDG values, but their calculation does not include the Σ⁰−Σ⁺ mass difference needed for the DvH formula. The result raises questions for reconciling a small LQCD mixing angle with the large charge symmetry breaking splitting observed in the A=4 hypernuclei ⁴_ΛH–⁴_ΛHe.
