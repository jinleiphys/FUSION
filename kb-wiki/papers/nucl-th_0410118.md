# The interparticle interaction and noncommutativity of conjugate operators in quantum mechanics II. Lightest nuclei

> A new model for calculating the structure of bound states of interacting particles is considered. The model takes into account the noncommutativity of the space and impulse operators plus the correlation equations for the indeterminacy of these quantities. The efficiency of the model is demonstrated by specific calculations for some lightest nuclei.

# The interparticle interaction and noncommutativity of conjugate operators in quantum mechanics II. Lightest nuclei

## Key claim

The NOCE model (noncommutativity of operators and correlation equations) can be applied to light nuclei, yielding binding energy corrections on the order of 4-8% relative to conventional quantum mechanics, but the results are qualitative because they depend sensitively on the choice of nucleon-nucleon potential and the correlation factor γ₀, which must be fixed using experimental data.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation with a modified nucleon mass m' = m/β², where β is a noncommutativity parameter determined iteratively from the matrix element of the interparticle force f₀. For the deuteron, a variational calculation with a Yukawa potential is performed using a single-parameter trial wave function, and the resulting cubic equation for the variational parameter is solved graphically. For ³H and ³He, the stochastic variational method (SVM) with a Gaussian basis is employed, using the central exchange Volkov potential, and the generalized eigenvalue problem is solved with the IMSL library.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron experimental binding energy: |E_D^exp| = 2.22457 MeV; the NOCE model yields values from 2.2019 MeV (γ₀ = 0.00001) up to 2.3826 MeV (γ₀ = 1.00), a maximum increase of ~8% over conventional SE
- For the deuteron at the experimental binding energy: γ₀ = 0.13523, f₀ = 20.0812 MeV/fm, β = 0.998765, corresponding to a nucleon mass increase Δm = 0.00247516 m
- For ³H with γ₀ = 0.6: theoretical binding energy E^theor = -8.819 MeV (experimental: -8.482 MeV, conventional quantum mechanics: -8.464 MeV), f₀ = 8.2483085 MeV/fm, β = 0.995498
- For ³He with γ₀ = 0.6: theoretical binding energy E^theor = -8.113 MeV (experimental: -7.718 MeV, conventional quantum mechanics: -7.759 MeV), f₀ = 8.4056275 MeV/fm, β = 0.995412
- Volkov potential parameters: first component V_31 = V_13 = 144.86 MeV, V_33 = V_11 = -28.972 MeV, μ₁ = 0.82 fm; second component V_31 = V_13 = -83.34 MeV, V_33 = V_11 = 16.668 MeV, μ₂ = 1.60 fm

## Context

This work extends the NOCE model previously applied to hydrogen-like atoms (Steshenko, 2004) to nuclear systems, specifically the deuteron, tritium, and helium-3. The model introduces noncommutativity between coordinate and momentum operators of different particles, parameterized by β and β₀, which modifies the effective particle mass. The results are qualitative because the nucleon-nucleon potential (Volkov potential from Volkov, 1965) is not uniquely determined, unlike the Coulomb potential in atomic calculations. The work enables future studies of heavier nuclei within the NOCE framework, though the corrections depend strongly on the chosen NN-potential and the correlation factor γ₀.
