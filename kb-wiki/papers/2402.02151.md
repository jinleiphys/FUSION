# The relativistic three-body scattering and the $D^0D^{*+}-D^+D^{*0}$ system

> Scattering amplitudes involving three-particle scattering processes are investigated within the isobar approximation which respects constraints from two- and three-body unitarity. The particular system considered is the $D^0D^{*+}-D^+D^{*0}$, where the $D^{*+}$ $(D^{*0})$ enters as a $p$-wave $D^+π^0$ or $D^0π^+$ ($D^0π^0$ or $D^+π^-$) resonance. The interaction potentials in the coupled-channel $D^0D^{*+}-D^+D^{*0}$ system contain the $σ$, $ρ$, $ω$ and $π$-exchange. The analytic continuation of the amplitudes across the three-body unitary cuts is investigated to search for poles on the unphysical Riemann sheets. Associated with an unstable particle $D^{*+}$ $(D^{*0})$ is a complex two-body unitarity cut, through which one can further analytically continue into another unphysical Riemann sheet. Dynamical singularities emerged from the $π$-exchange potential are stressed. The pole generated from the $D^0D^{*+}-D^+D^{*0}$ interaction and its line shape in $D^0D^0π^+$ break-up production are in agreement with double-charmed tetraquark $T_{cc}^+$ observed by the LHCb Collaboration.

## Key claim
The pole generated from the coupled-channel $D^0D^{*+}-D^+D^{*0}$ interaction, including the effects of three-body dynamics and dynamical singularities from $\pi$-exchange, produces a line shape in $D^0D^0\pi^+$ break-up production that agrees with the double-charmed tetraquark $T_{cc}^+$ observed by the LHCb Collaboration, provided the analytic continuation across three-body and complex two-body unitarity cuts is performed correctly.

## Method
The scattering amplitude for the $D^0D^{*+}-D^+D^{*0}$ system is solved using a relativistic three-body equation within the isobar approximation, which respects two- and three-body unitarity constraints. The interaction potentials include $\sigma$, $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\pi$-exchange, and the $D^{*+}$ and $D^{*0}$ are treated as $p$-wave resonances in the $D\pi$ subsystem. The amplitude is analytically continued into the unphysical Riemann sheets via complex contour deformation of the spectator momentum and self-energy integrals to search for poles.

## Key numbers
* The pole position of the $D^{*+}$ resonance, evaluated with the $D^0\pi^+$ and $D^+\pi^0$ channels on their unphysical Riemann sheets, is at ${\rm Re}\,m_{D^{*+}}^{p}=m_{D^{*+}}$ and ${\rm Im}\,m_{D^{*+}}^{p}=-43.5$ keV.
* The pole position of the $D^{*0}$ resonance, evaluated with the $D^0\pi^0$ and $D^+\pi^-$ channels on their unphysical Riemann sheets, is at ${\rm Re}\,m_{D^{*0}}^{p}=m_{D^{*0}}$ and ${\rm Im}\,m_{D^{*0}}^{p}=-32.5$ keV.
* The masses used for the mesons are $m_{D^0}=1.86484$ GeV, $m_{D^{*+}}=2.01026$ GeV, $m_{D^+}=1.86966$ GeV, and $m_{D^{*0}}=2.00685$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of three-body dynamics in exotic hadrons, specifically the $T_{cc}^+$ and $X(3872)$ systems, following earlier investigations by Du (2021) and Qiu (2023) on the effects of three-body $D^0D^0\pi^+$ dynamics. It also builds on the analytic continuation techniques for three-body scattering developed by Glockle (1978), Pearce (1984), and Afnan (1991) for systems like $\pi d$-$N\Delta$ and three-neutron interactions. The results enable a more rigorous extraction of the $T_{cc}^+$ pole position from experimental data and lattice QCD simulations by properly treating left-hand cuts from $\pi$-exchange, as discussed by Du (2023) and Hansen (2024).
