# Radial distribution of charm quarks in jets in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Heavy flavor physics in high-energy heavy-ion collisions is a promising and active area to study the mass dependence of the "jet quenching" effects both at the RHIC and the LHC. In this talk, we present the first theoretical study on the $D^0$ meson radial distributions relative to the jet axis both in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, where a nice agreement of our results with experimental data is observed. The in-medium parton propagations are described by a Monte Carlo transport model which uses the next-to-leading order (NLO) plus parton shower (PS) event generator SHERPA as input and includes elastic (collisional) and inelastic (radiative) in-medium interaction of heavy flavor jet. We find that, at low $D^0$ meson $p_T$, the radial distribution significantly shifts to larger radius indicating a strong diffusion effect, and the diffusion effects decrease quickly with $p_T$ ,which is consistent with the recent CMS measurements. We demonstrate that the angular deviation of charm quarks is sensitive to $D_s$ but not $\hat{q}$, which may provide new constrains on the collisional and radiative heavy quark energy loss.

## Key claim

This work presents the first theoretical calculation of the radial distribution of D0 mesons relative to the jet axis in both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, finding that the observed diffusion effect at low D0 meson transverse momentum is dominated by collisional interactions while at higher transverse momentum the main contribution comes from medium-induced gluon radiation.

## Method

The authors use the SHERPA event generator with next-to-leading order (NLO) matrix elements matched with parton shower to generate initial charm-jet events in p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. In-medium parton propagation is described by a modified Langevin equation incorporating elastic (collisional) interactions and a higher-twist approach for inelastic (radiative) gluon radiation, with the medium evolution provided by the iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic model. The Peterson fragmentation functions are used for hadronization of charm quarks into D0 mesons, and jets are reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with FastJet.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV for both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions
- Jet selection: |eta^jet| < 1.6 and p_T^jet > 60 GeV
- D0 meson kinematic regions: 4 GeV < p_T^D < 20 GeV and p_T^D > 20 GeV
- Spatial diffusion coefficient fixed at 2*pi*T*D_s = 4 based on lattice QCD calculations
- Centrality bins studied: 0-10%, 10-30%, and 30-100% for Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the study of jet quenching effects to heavy flavor mesons, specifically addressing the CMS measurements of D0 meson radial distributions in jets (Sirunyan 2019). The results provide new constraints on the relative contributions of collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms for heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma, and the authors note that similar studies for b-quarks in jets are underway (Wang 2020). The approach combines established frameworks including the higher-twist formalism for radiative energy loss (Guo 2000, Zhang 2003, Majumder 2009) and Langevin dynamics for heavy quark diffusion (Moore 2004, Cao 2013).
