# Hadronic parity violation in effective field theory

> The application of effective field theories to hadronic parity violation in two- and three-nucleon systems is described. These methods provide several important advantages over the traditionally used meson-exchange models, e.g., model independence and the possibility to reliably estimate theoretical errors. We focus on the so-called "pionless" theory and describe calculations of various two- and three-nucleon observables. The role of parity-violating three-nucleon interactions is also discussed.

# Hadronic parity violation in effective field theory

## Key claim
Pionless effective field theory (EFT) provides a model-independent framework for describing hadronic parity violation in two- and three-nucleon systems, with the key advantage that up to next-to-leading order, parity-violating three-nucleon interactions are not required, so observables can be parameterized in terms of only five low-energy constants (LECs) from two-nucleon interactions.

## Method
The author applies pionless effective field theory (EFT($\pi\!\!/$)) to parity-violating observables in two- and three-nucleon systems, using a Lagrangian written in terms of dibaryon fields that contains five S-P wave transition operators at leading order. Calculations are performed for longitudinal asymmetries in $\vec{N}N$ scattering, spin rotation angles in $np$ and $nd$ systems, and photon asymmetries and polarizations in $np \leftrightarrow d\gamma$ reactions, with results adjusted to a consistent dibaryon formalism.

## Key numbers
- Longitudinal asymmetry in $\vec{n}n$ scattering: $A_L^{nn} = - \sqrt{32 M/\pi}\,p \left( g_B - g_C + g_D \right)$ at leading order
- Longitudinal asymmetry in $\vec{p}p$ scattering: $A_L^{pp} = - \sqrt{32 M/\pi}\,p \left( g_B + g_C + g_D \right)$ at leading order, with Coulomb corrections of approximately 3% at the lowest experimentally considered energy
- Photon angular asymmetry in $\vec{n}p \to d\gamma$: $A_\gamma = (4/3)\sqrt{2/\pi}\, M^{3/2}/[\kappa_1(1-\gamma_t a^{(^1S_0)})]\, g_E$ at leading order
- Circular photon polarization in $np \to d\vec{\gamma}$: $P_\gamma = -2\sqrt{2/\pi}\, M^{3/2}/[\kappa_1(1-\gamma_t a^{(^1S_0)})] \left[ (1-2\gamma_t a^{(^1S_0)}/3) g_A + (\gamma_t a^{(^1S_0)}/3)(g_B-2g_D) \right]$ at leading order
- Neutron-deuteron spin rotation angle per unit length at NLO: $(1/N)\, d\phi_{PV}^{nd}/dl = [16 \pm 1.6]g_A + [34\pm 3.4]g_E + [4.6 \pm 1.0](3g_B - 2g_D)$

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to hadronic parity violation proposed by Zhu et al. (2004) and earlier EFT applications by Savage (1998-2000), providing a systematic alternative to the traditional meson-exchange model of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1979, referred to as DDH). The DDH model used $\pi$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ mesons with parity-violating couplings determined from quark models and symmetry arguments, but its interpretation is complicated by model assumptions and many-body physics. The pionless EFT approach enables consistent treatment of two- and three-nucleon interactions with reliable theoretical error estimates, and the results enable extraction of the five parity-violating LECs from upcoming experiments including the $A_\gamma$ measurement at Oak Ridge's Spallation Neutron Source and possible $A_L^\gamma$ measurements at the HIGS facility.
