# Systematics of strong nuclear amplification of gluon saturation from exclusive vector meson production in high energy electron-nucleus collisions

> We show that gluon saturation gives rise to a strong modification of the scaling in both the nuclear mass number $A$ and the virtuality $Q^2$ of the vector meson production cross-section in exclusive deep-inelastic scattering off nuclei. We present qualitative analytic expressions for how the scaling exponents are modified as well as quantitative predictions that can be tested at an Electron-Ion Collider.

# Systematics of strong nuclear amplification of gluon saturation from exclusive vector meson production in high energy electron-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

Gluon saturation produces a strong modification of the nuclear mass number $A$ and virtuality $Q^2$ scaling of exclusive vector meson production cross-sections in deep-inelastic scattering off nuclei, but the asymptotic scaling laws (e.g., $A^{4/3}$ at low $Q^2$ or $A^2$ at high $Q^2$) are not fully reached in the kinematics accessible at a future Electron-Ion Collider; the predicted scaling exponents are only approximate for realistic nuclei and energies.

## Method

The authors compute exclusive $J/\Psi$ and $\rho$ vector meson production cross-sections in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory using the dipole picture, where the virtual photon splits into a quark-antiquark dipole that scatters elastically off the target. The dipole-proton cross-section is described by the IPsat model, which includes eikonalized DGLAP-evolved gluon distributions fitted to HERA inclusive DIS data, and is generalized to nuclear targets using the Woods-Saxon density profile. Analytical estimates are derived in the simpler Golec-Biernat–Wusthoff (GBW) model to capture asymptotic scaling behavior, while numerical results are obtained in the IPsat model for kinematics relevant to the EIC and LHeC.

## Key numbers

- For Gold at $x=0.01$, the quark saturation scale is $Q_{s,A}^2 = 0.85\ \text{GeV}^2$; at $x=0.001$, it is $Q_{s,A}^2 = 1.2\ \text{GeV}^2$.
- For Iron at $x=0.01$, the quark saturation scale is $Q_{s,A}^2 = 0.54\ \text{GeV}^2$; at $x=0.001$, it is $Q_{s,A}^2 = 0.75\ \text{GeV}^2$.
- At high $Q^2$ ($Q^2 \gg Q_{s,A}^2$), the forward ($t=0$) exclusive cross-section for longitudinal polarization scales as $Q^{-6}$, and for transverse polarization as $Q^{-8}$.
- At low $Q^2$ ($Q^2 \ll Q_{s,A}^2$), the forward cross-section for longitudinal polarization scales as $Q^2$, and for transverse polarization as $Q^0$ (independent of $Q^2$).
- The total coherent cross-section at high $Q^2$ scales as $A^{4/3}$; at low $Q^2$ it scales as $A^{2/3}$ in asymptotic kinematics, but numerically for $\rho$ production at low $Q^2$ the $A$ scaling exponent is approximately $1.7$ rather than $4/3$.
- For $J/\Psi$ at low $Q^2$, the $A$ scaling exponent for the forward cross-section is far from the asymptotic $A^{4/3}$ due to the large charm quark mass.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of exclusive vector meson production in the saturation picture by Goncalves (2004), Kowalski (2006), Caldwell (2009), Lappi (2010), and Toll (2012), and builds on the IPsat model fitted to HERA data by Rezaeian (2012). It provides quantitative predictions for the EIC, complementing studies of ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC where photon virtuality is limited to $Q^2 \sim 1/R_A^2$. The results enable experimental searches for gluon saturation through the systematic variation of $A$, $Q^2$, and polarization in exclusive vector meson production, and can be refined by future next-to-leading order calculations such as those by Boussarie (2016).
