# Multi-task deep neural network for predicting both nuclear fission yields and their experimental errors in peak-shaped data

> The fission product yield (FPY) is crucially important information for numerous nuclear applications. However, the peak-shaped characteristics of FPY data present important challenges for predicting unobservable FPY data. To address these challenges, after applying Multi-task learning models to fission product yield data and their experimental error estimates, we introduce a novel loss function along with incorporation of the odd even effect. Our approach is intended to predict unknown fission yields and the associated experimental error. To demonstrate the effectiveness of our proposed method, we compared our proposed method with conventional methods that learn each dataset independently. Our findings demonstrate that the proposed methods can predict peak shaped data with experimental error estimates more effectively than earlier methods can.

## Key claim
The proposed Multi-gate Mixture-of-Experts (MMoE) multi-task deep neural network, combined with a novel weighted loss function and odd-even effect encoding, can predict both fission product yields and their experimental errors more effectively than conventional single-task methods, but only when the weighted loss and odd-even features are used together; the improvement is most pronounced for peak-shaped data at thermal neutron energies.

## Method
The authors trained an MMoE architecture with three expert feedforward networks and task-specific gates to simultaneously predict FPY values and FPY errors from JENDL-5 data. They introduced a weighted loss function that assigns higher weights to data points with normalized FPY values above a threshold r, and incorporated an odd-even parity indicator O_i as an additional input feature (1 for even mass number, 0 for odd, 0.5 for non-peak data). The model was trained on four data sets with different training/testing splits of actinides (U, Pu, Cm, Cf, Am, Np, Pa, Es, Th, Fm), using input features (Z_i, N_i, A_i, E_i) and evaluated at three neutron energies.

## Key numbers
- The best performing model (Multi-task DNN with weighted loss function + odd-even) achieved an average total χ² of 3.36 × 10⁻⁶ for FPY prediction at 2.53 × 10⁻⁸ MeV (thermal energy), compared to 4.66 × 10⁻⁶ for BNN and 7.09 × 10⁻⁶ for DNN (MSE).
- At thermal energy, the same model achieved an average peak χ² of 4.26 × 10⁻⁶ for FPY and an FPY error average peak χ² of 9.87 × 10⁻⁶, both the lowest among all methods.
- The tuning parameter α for the multi-task loss balance was set to 0.9 after grid search over {0.5, 0.6, 0.7, 0.8, 0.9}.
- The number of experts m was set to 3 after grid search over {1, 2, 3, 4, 5}.
- Training data without augmentation contained 6,039 samples; with data augmentation it increased to 16,713 samples.

## Context
This work extends prior machine learning approaches to fission yield prediction, particularly Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) used by Wang et al. (2021) and Qiao et al. (2021), which struggled with peak-shaped FPY distributions due to Gaussian-based uncertainty quantification. The multi-task learning framework is inspired by MMoE (Ma et al., 2018) and its successful application to correlated physics observables such as nuclear masses and deformations (Bai et al., 2021; Wu et al., 2022). The results enable more accurate prediction of unmeasured fission yields and their experimental uncertainties for fast reactor applications, where JENDL-5 currently lacks complete data.
