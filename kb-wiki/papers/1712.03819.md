# Effect of channel coupling on the elastic scattering of lithium isotopes

> Herein, we investigated the channel coupling (CC) effect on the elastic scatterings of lithium (Li) isotopes ($A =$ 6--9) for the $^{12}$C and $^{28}$Si targets at $E/A =$ 50--60 MeV. The wave functions of the Li isotopes were obtained using the stochastic multi-configuration mixing (SMCM) method based on the microscopic-cluster model. The proton radii of the $^{7}$Li, $^{8}$Li, and $^{9}$Li nuclei became smaller as the number of valence neutrons increased. The valence neutrons in the $^{8}$Li and $^{9}$Li nuclei exhibited a glue-like behavior, thereby attracting the $α$ and $t$ clusters. Based on the transition densities derived from these microscopic wave functions, the elastic-scattering cross section was calculated using a microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) method with a complex $G$-matrix interaction. The existing experimental data for the elastic scatterings of the Li isotopes and $^{10}$Be nuclei were well reproduced. The Li isotope elastic cross sections were demonstrated for the $^{12}$C and $^{28}$Si targets at $E/A$ =53 MeV. The glue-like effect of the valence neutrons on the Li isotope was clearly demonstrated by the CC effect on elastic scattering. Finally, we realize that the valence neutrons stabilized the bindings of the core parts and the CC effect related to core excitation was indeed reduced.

## Key claim

The channel coupling (CC) effect on elastic scattering decreases for lithium isotopes with more valence neutrons (⁸Li and ⁹Li) because the valence neutrons exhibit a glue-like behavior that attracts the α and t clusters, reducing the core excitation and thus the CC effect, but this conclusion depends on fixing the renormalization factor to 0.7 for all isotopes.

## Method

The authors combined microscopic nuclear structure and reaction calculations: they obtained wave functions of ⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁸Li, and ⁹Li using the stochastic multi-configuration mixing (SMCM) method based on a microscopic cluster model with α+p+n configurations, then calculated transition densities from these wave functions. They applied these densities to microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations with a complex G-matrix interaction (MPa) to compute elastic-scattering cross sections for Li isotopes on ¹²C and ²⁸Si targets at E/A = 53 MeV, considering both projectile and target excitations.

## Key numbers

- Proton radii decreased with added valence neutrons: calculated charged proton radii were 2.662 fm (⁶Li), 2.636 fm (⁷Li), 2.530 fm (⁸Li), and 2.393 fm (⁹Li), compared to experimental values of 2.54(3) fm, 2.43(3) fm, 2.41(3) fm, and 2.30(2) fm respectively (Tanihata 1985)
- Total energies from SMCM: ⁶Li at -29.66 MeV, ⁷Li at -37.64 MeV, ⁸Li at -38.35 MeV, and ⁹Li at -41.04 MeV
- Transition strengths B(IS2) from ground state to first excited state: ⁷Li (g.s. → 1/2⁻₁) at 54.47 fm⁴, ⁸Li (g.s. → 1⁺₁) at 16.24 fm⁴, ⁹Li (g.s. → 1/2⁻₁) at 19.26 fm⁴
- Renormalization factor N_W = 0.7 reproduced experimental data for ⁶Li + ¹²C and ⁶Li + ²⁸Si at E/A = 53 MeV; N_W = 0.65–0.7 for ⁷Li + ¹²C and ⁷Li + ²⁸Si at E/A = 50 MeV; N_W = 0.8 for ⁹Li + ¹²C at E/A = 60 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of channel coupling effects in light-ion scattering, particularly the breakup effects of ⁶Li and ⁷Li into α+d and α+t systems investigated by Sakuragi (1986, 1987) and Watanabe (2015). It also builds on the authors' earlier work (Furumoto 2013) using the same SMCM+MCC framework. The results enable systematic comparison of the glue-like role of valence neutrons across the lithium isotopic chain, showing that the CC effect from core excitation is reduced for ⁸Li and ⁹Li compared to ⁷Li, consistent with the smaller transition strengths and radii calculated for the neutron-richer isotopes.
