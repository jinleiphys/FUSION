# Total reaction cross section on a deuteron target and the eclipse effect of the constituent neutron and proton

> Background: Eclipse effect of the neutron and proton in a deuteron target is essential to correctly describe high-energy deuteron scattering. The nucleus-deuteron scattering needs information not only on the nucleus-proton but also the nucleus-neutron interaction, for which no direct measurement of the nucleus-neutron cross sections is available for unstable nuclei. Purpose: We systematically evaluated the total reaction cross sections by a deuteron target to explore the feasibility of extracting the nucleus-neutron interaction from measurable cross sections. Methods: High-energy nucleus-deuteron collision is described by the Glauber model, in which the proton and neutron configuration of the deuteron is explicitly taken into account. Results: Our calculation reproduces available experimental total reaction cross section data on the nucleus-deuteron scattering. The possibility of extracting the nucleus-neutron total reaction cross section from nucleus-deuteron and nucleus-proton total reaction cross sections is explored. The total reaction cross sections of a nucleus by proton, neutron, and deuteron targets can be expressed, to good accuracy, in terms of the nuclear matter radius and neutron skin thickness. Incident-energy dependence of the total reaction cross sections is examined. Conclusions: The total reaction cross section on a deuteron target includes information on both the nucleus-neutron and nucleus-proton profile functions. Measuring the cross sections by deuteron and proton targets is a promising tool to extract the nuclear size properties.

# Total reaction cross section on a deuteron target and the eclipse effect of the constituent neutron and proton

## Key claim

The total reaction cross section on a deuteron target is always significantly smaller than the sum of the nucleus-neutron and nucleus-proton total reaction cross sections, with the cross-section defect (eclipse effect) reaching 10–30% of the sum, and this defect can be exploited to extract the unknown nucleus-neutron interaction from combined deuteron-target and proton-target measurements.

## Method

The authors used the Glauber model to describe high-energy nucleus-deuteron scattering, explicitly incorporating the spatial configuration of the proton and neutron in the deuteron via the AV8' potential ground-state wave function. The total reaction cross sections for projectile nuclei on deuteron, proton, and neutron targets were calculated using the optical-limit approximation for the nucleus-nucleon phase-shift functions, with nucleon-nucleon profile function parameters taken from Ibrahim (2008). The cross-section defect was defined as the difference between the sum of the proton-target and neutron-target cross sections and the deuteron-target cross section.

## Key numbers

- For 12C on deuteron at 50 MeV/nucleon: calculated total reaction cross section = 638 mb (642 mb with Coulomb breakup), experimental value = 600 ± 17 mb at 48.7 ± 0.15 MeV/nucleon (Auce, 1996)
- For 16O on deuteron at 50 MeV/nucleon: calculated = 735 mb (741 mb with Coulomb breakup), experimental = 726 ± 21 mb
- For 40Ca on deuteron at 50 MeV/nucleon: calculated = 1220 mb (1260 mb with Coulomb breakup), experimental = 1260 ± 30 mb
- The expectation value of the inverse square of the neutron-proton distance in the deuteron ground state: ⟨1/r²⟩_d = 0.294 fm⁻² = 0.0294 mb⁻¹
- The cross-section defect δσ reaches a minimum at approximately 200–500 MeV/nucleon, where the nucleon-nucleon total cross section also reaches its minimum
- For 30Ne, the extracted matter radius r_m = 3.34 fm and neutron skin thickness δr = 0.47 fm from two-energy fitting, in exact agreement with the Hartree-Fock values (3.34 fm and 0.47 fm)
- For 60Ca, extracted r_m = 3.98 fm and δr = 0.46 fm, compared to Hartree-Fock values of 4.00 fm and 0.46 fm
- The difference Δ₀σ between total reaction and interaction cross sections for 16O at 80 MeV/nucleon is 60–70 mb, about 10% of σ_d

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-Franceo eclipse effect analysis from antiproton-deuteron scattering (Glauber, 1959; Franco, 1966) to nucleus-deuteron scattering for stable and unstable nuclei. It builds on prior studies of total reaction cross sections on proton and carbon targets that established relationships between cross sections and nuclear radii (Horiuchi, 2014; Horiuchi, 2016). The results enable extraction of the nucleus-neutron total reaction cross section, for which no direct measurements exist for unstable nuclei, from combined deuteron-target and proton-target measurements, and provide a method to determine matter radii and neutron skin thickness of exotic nuclei. The work also quantifies the difference between total reaction cross sections and experimentally measured interaction cross sections for deuteron targets, which is essential for interpreting future inverse-kinematics experiments with unstable beams.
