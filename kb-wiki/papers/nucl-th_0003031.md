# Hot nuclear matter in the modified quark-meson coupling model with quark-quark correlations

> Short-range quark-quark correlations in hot nuclear matter are examined within the modified quark-meson coupling model (MQMC) by adding repulsive scalar and vector quark-quark interactions. Without these correlations, the bag radius increases with the baryon density. However when the correlations are introduced the bag size shrinks as the bags overlap. Also as the strength of the scalar quark-quark correlation is increased, the decrease of the effective nucleon mass $M^{*}_N$ with the baryonic density is slowed down and tends to saturate at high densities. Within this model we study the phase transition from the baryon-meson phase to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase with the latter modeled as an ideal gas of quarks and gluons inside a bag. Two models for the QGP bag parameter are considered. In one case, the bag is taken to be medium-independent and the phase transition from the hadron phase to QGP is found to occur at 5-8 times ordinary nuclear matter density for temperatures less than 60 MeV. For lower densities, the transition takes place at higher temperature reaching up to 130 MeV at zero density. In the second case, the QGP bag parameter is considered medium-dependent as in the MQMC model for the hadronic phase. In this case, it is found that the phase transition occurs at much lower densities.

## Key claim

Short-range quark-quark correlations, introduced via repulsive scalar and vector contact interactions, cure the problem of the bag radius increasing with density in the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model; instead, the bag size shrinks when bags overlap, and the effective nucleon mass saturates at high densities. The phase transition to quark-gluon plasma (QGP) occurs at 5-8 times normal nuclear matter density for temperatures below 60 MeV only when the QGP bag parameter is taken as medium-independent.

## Method

The authors extend the MQMC model by adding repulsive scalar and vector contact interactions between quarks in overlapping nucleon bags, with the overlap probability estimated geometrically using a critical rigid-ball nucleonic radius. The Dirac equation for quarks inside a nucleon bag is solved in mean-field approximation with scalar and vector mean fields coupled to the quarks, and the bag radius is obtained by minimizing the effective nucleon mass. The QGP phase is modeled as an ideal gas of noninteracting quarks and gluons inside a bag, and the phase transition is determined by equalizing pressure and baryon chemical potential between the hadronic and QGP phases for a given temperature.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Free nucleon mass: \(M_N = 939\ \text{MeV}\) (experimental value used for fitting)
- Free bag radius: \(R_0 = 0.60\ \text{fm}\)
- Scalar correlation strength parameter \(\alpha\) values considered: 0, 5, 7, 9
- Vector correlation strength parameter \(\beta\) values considered: 0, 30, 60 \(\text{GeV}^{-2}\)
- For medium-independent QGP bag parameter (Case I): phase transition occurs at 5-8 times \(\rho_0\) for temperatures less than 60 MeV; at zero density the transition temperature reaches up to 130 MeV
- For cold nuclear matter with medium-independent QGP bag parameter, correlations reduce the baryon chemical potential at transition from 1850 MeV to 1450 MeV
- For medium-dependent QGP bag parameter (Case II): phase transition at low temperatures occurs at \(\mu_B = 950\ \text{MeV}\) and density \(\rho_B / \rho_0 = 1.35\)

## Context

This work extends the MQMC model previously applied to cold nuclear matter by Jin and Jennings (1996, 1997) and to hot nuclear matter by Zakout and Jaqaman (1999), which suffered from the problem that the bag radius increases with density. The authors follow the approach of Saito, Tsushima, and Thomas (1999) for introducing short-range quark-quark correlations in a simple geometrical way. The results enable future studies of the QGP phase transition in heavy ion collisions, suggesting that medium-dependent bag parameters (Case II) could allow QGP production at much lower compressions in very heavy nuclear systems.
