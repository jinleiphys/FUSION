# Scattering states in the four nucleons system

> The Faddeev-Yakubowski equations have been solved in configuration space for the four nucleons system. Results for bound and scattering states in the isospin and S-wave approximation for different (T,S) channels are presented. The n-t elastic cross section has been also calculated with realistic interactions. A special interest was devoted to the description of the observed resonant structure at $T_{lab}\approx 3$ MeV which is well reproduced only by the simple MT I-III potential.

# Scattering states in the four nucleons system

## Key claim

The Faddeev-Yakubowski equations in configuration space can be solved for four-nucleon scattering states below the first inelastic threshold, but the observed resonant structure at approximately 3 MeV laboratory energy is well reproduced only by the simple MT I-III potential, not by realistic interactions like AV14 or Nijmegen II.

## Method

The authors solved the 18 coupled Faddeev-Yakubowski equations in configuration space for identical fermions, reducing them to two independent amplitudes (K and H) via permutation operators. The wavefunction was expanded in generalized tripolar harmonics with angular momentum, spin, and isospin quantum numbers, and the resulting linear system was solved using Hermite spline expansion, orthogonal collocation, and the tensor trick. Scattering observables were extracted from the logarithmic derivative of the K amplitude in the asymptotic region, where the solution factorizes into a 3N bound state and a plane wave.

## Key numbers

- n-t scattering lengths with MT I-III (S-wave approximation): a = 14.75 fm (T=0, S=0), 3.25 fm (T=0, S=1), 4.13 fm (T=1, S=0), 3.73 fm (T=1, S=1); effective ranges: r0 = 6.75 fm, 1.82 fm, 2.01 fm, 1.87 fm respectively
- 4He binding energy with MT I-III (S-wave approximation): B = 30.30 MeV, r.m.s. radius = 1.44 fm
- n-t zero-energy cross section with MT I-III (including higher partial waves): sigma(0) = 177.0 fm^2, with scattering lengths a0 = 4.10 fm, a1 = 3.63 fm
- n-t zero-energy cross section with AV14: sigma(0) = 193.7 fm^2, with a0 = 4.31 fm, a1 = 3.79 fm; 4He binding energy B4 = 23.34 MeV, r.m.s. = 1.56 fm
- n-t zero-energy cross section with Nijmegen II: sigma(0) = 191.6 fm^2, with a0 = 4.31 fm, a1 = 3.76 fm; B4 = 23.39 MeV, r.m.s. = 1.54 fm
- Experimental zero-energy n-t cross section: sigma(0) = 170.2 ± 9.7 fm^2
- With a phenomenological three-nucleon interaction (Wr = 500 MeV, Wa = 174 MeV, mu = 2.0 fm^-1): B3 = 8.48 MeV, B4 = 28.56 MeV, a0 = 4.0 fm, a1 = 3.6 fm, sigma(0) = 172.4 fm^2
- P-wave n-t phase shifts at E = 3 MeV with AV14 (S and D waves only): 21.2 degrees (0-), 26.6 degrees (1-), 47.8 degrees (1-), 35.4 degrees (2-); with added P waves: 24.1, 20.0, 37.5, 32.7 degrees respectively
- P-wave n-t phase shifts at E = 3 MeV with MT I-III: 44.1 degrees (0-), 30.3 degrees (1-), 44.1 degrees (1-), 44.1 degrees (2-)

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-Yakubowski approach in configuration space from bound states to scattering states in the four-nucleon system, building on prior calculations by the same group and others (SSK 1992, KG 1992). The results for n-t scattering with realistic potentials (AV14, Nijmegen II) show similar deficiencies as in the three-nucleon case, suggesting the need for three-nucleon forces. The successful reproduction of the 3 MeV resonance by the simple MT I-III potential raises questions about whether effective two-body potentials should be used from the start in low-energy few-nucleon physics, and enables future work with more FY components, higher partial waves, and realistic three-nucleon interactions.
