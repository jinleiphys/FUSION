# High energy pA collisions in the color glass condensate approach II. Quark production

> We compute the production of quark-antiquark pairs in high energy collisions between a small and a large projectile, as in proton-nucleus collisions, in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate. We derive a general expression for quark pair-production, which is not k_t-factorizable. However, k_t-factorization is recovered in the limit of large mass pairs or large quark--anti-quark momenta. Our results are amenable to a simple interpretation and suggest how multi-parton correlations at small x can be quantified in high-energy proton/deuteron-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quark-antiquark pair production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions, computed in the Color Glass Condensate framework, is not $k_\perp$-factorizable in general, contrary to gluon production; $k_\perp$-factorization is recovered only in the limit of large quark pair masses or large transverse momenta of the pair.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in covariant gauge to first order in the dilute proton source and to all orders in the dense nuclear source, then compute the time-ordered quark propagator in the resulting classical background field to obtain the pair production amplitude. The amplitude is expressed in terms of Wilson lines in both the fundamental and adjoint representations of SU(N), and the cross-section is obtained by averaging over color source configurations with the weight functionals $W_p$ and $W_{A}$.

## Key numbers

- The gauge field of the nucleus alone is given by $A_{_A}^\mu(q)=2\pi g\delta^{\mu-}\delta(q^+) \rho_{A}(\mathbf{q}_\perp)/q_\perp^2$, linear in the nuclear source $\rho_{A}$.
- The condition for $k_\perp$-factorization to hold is $\rho_p/k_\perp^2, \rho_{A}/k_\perp^2 \ll 1$, where $\rho_p$ and $\rho_{A}$ are the number of parton sources per unit area in projectile and target respectively.
- In the limit $k_{1\perp}\to 0$, the two terms in the complete amplitude cancel, ensuring collinear factorizability on the proton side.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate formalism developed in the accompanying paper (Blaizot, Gelis, Venugopalan, 2004, referred to as Paper I) from gluon production to quark-antiquark pair production in proton-nucleus collisions. It builds on prior work by Collins and Ellis (1991) and Catani, Ciafaloni, and Hautmann (1991) on $k_\perp$-factorization for large mass pairs, and by Gelis and Venugopalan (2002) who showed that $k_\perp$-factorization fails when parton densities become large. The results enable quantitative tests of the JIMWLK evolution equations and multi-parton correlations at small $x$ in high-energy proton/deuteron-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
