# Exact analytical solutions of second-order conformal hydrodynamics

> We present some exact solutions of relativistic second-order hydrodynamic equations in theories with conformal symmetry. Starting from a spherically expanding solution in ideal hydrodynamics, we take into account general conformal second-order corrections, and construct, for the first time, fully analytical axisymmetric exact solutions including the case with nonzero vorticity. These solutions are time-reversible despite having a nonvanishing shear stress tensor and provide a useful quantitative measure of the second-order effects in relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first fully analytical axisymmetric exact solutions to general second-order conformal hydrodynamic equations, including cases with nonzero vorticity. These solutions are time-reversible despite having a nonvanishing shear stress tensor, which contradicts the usual expectation that viscous effects break time-reversal symmetry.

## Method
The authors start from a spherically expanding solution in ideal hydrodynamics and use a conformal mapping from Minkowski space to \(AdS_3 \times S^1\) space, following the approach of Gubser et al. (2010). In the global coordinates of \(AdS_3\), they consider a hydrostatic fluid with vanishing shear tensor and expansion rate, then solve the second-order constitutive equation for the shear stress tensor \(\hat{\pi}^{\mu\nu}\) as an independent variable, rather than using the Navier-Stokes approximation. The solutions are then conformally mapped back to Minkowski space to obtain explicit expressions for the energy density and flow velocity.

## Key numbers
- The energy density in Minkowski space for the three non-rotating solutions is proportional to expressions involving factors like \(\left(\frac{4L^2 x_\perp^2}{(L^2+(t+r)^2)(L^2+(t-r)^2)}\right)^{\frac{9}{2(\lambda_1-3)}}\), where \(L\) is the \(AdS_3\) radius parameter and \(\lambda_1\) is a dimensionless transport coefficient.
- For the rotating solution with nonzero vorticity, the energy density in Minkowski space is \(\epsilon \propto \frac{1}{((L^2+t^2+r^2)^2-4r^2t^2 -4\omega^2 L^2 x_\perp^2)^2}\), where \(\omega\) is the rotation parameter with \(1 \ge \omega \ge 0\).
- The special rotating solution with \(b = -\frac{4}{21}\) gives \(A = \frac{7\lambda_3 \omega^2}{4\left(\frac{4}{21}\lambda_1 - 1\right)}\), where \(\lambda_3\) is another dimensionless transport coefficient.
- The ideal hydro limit corresponds to \(\lambda_1 \to \infty\), and consistency requires \(\lambda_1 > 3\) for finite total energy in the first two non-rotating solutions.

## Context
This work extends the known exact solutions of relativistic ideal hydrodynamics, such as those by Bjorken (1983), Csorgo et al. (2006), and Nagy (2009), to include second-order conformal corrections. It generalizes the approach of Gubser et al. (2010, 2011) and Marrochio et al. (2013) for constructing exact solutions via conformal mapping. The solutions provide a quantitative benchmark for testing numerical codes used in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, and they enable further study of nonlinear second-order effects and vorticity in relativistic hydrodynamics.
