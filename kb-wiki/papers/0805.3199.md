# Hot QCD equations of state and RHIC

> We show how hot QCD equations of states can be adapted to make definite predictions for quark-gluon plasma at RHIC. We consider equations of state up to $O(g^5)$ and $O[g^6(ln(1/g)+δ)]$. Our method involves the extraction of equilibrium distribution functions for gluons and quarks from these equations of state by capturing the the interaction effects entirely in the effective chemical potentials. We further utilize these distribution functions to study the screening length in hot QCD and dissociation phenomenon of heavy quarkonia states by combining this understanding with the semi-classical transport theory.

## Key claim

The paper shows that perturbative QCD equations of state up to $O(g^5)$ and $O[g^6(\ln(1/g)+\delta)]$ can be adapted to make predictions for quark-gluon plasma at RHIC, but the agreement with lattice results on screening length is only qualitative and becomes better only at higher temperatures ($T \gtrsim 3T_c$), with the free parameter $\delta$ in the $O(g^6)$ equation of state needing to be fixed by matching to lattice data.

## Method

The authors extract equilibrium distribution functions for gluons and quarks from hot QCD equations of state by capturing all interaction effects in effective fugacities (chemical potentials), expanding the grand canonical partition functions as a Taylor series in the dimensionless chemical potentials up to cubic order. They then use these distribution functions to compute the Debye screening mass and combine the quasi-particle model with semi-classical transport theory to obtain the chromo-electric permittivity, which modifies the Cornell potential and allows determination of heavy quarkonia dissociation temperatures.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle approach previously developed by Chandra and collaborators (2007) for extracting distribution functions from QCD equations of state, and builds on the semi-classical transport theory of Ravishankar and Ranjan (2004) for computing response functions. The results on dissociation temperatures are consistent with other theoretical works on quarkonia dissociation, and the approach can be generalized to finite quark-chemical potential for studying thermodynamic and transport properties of matter at RHIC and LHC, where temperatures of $2T_c$ to $3T_c$ are expected.
