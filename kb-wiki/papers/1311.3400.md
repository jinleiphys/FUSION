# New look at the QCD ground state in a magnetic field

> We explore chiral symmetry breaking in a magnetic field within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of interacting massless quarks including tensor channels. We show that the new interaction channels open up via Fierz identities due to the explicit breaking of the rotational symmetry by the magnetic field. We demonstrate that the magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking leads to the generation of two independent condensates, the conventional chiral condensate and a spin-one condensate. While the chiral condensate generates a dynamical fermion mass, the new condensate gives rise to a dynamical anomalous magnetic moment for the fermions. As a consequence, the spectrum of the excitations in all Landau levels, except the lowest one, exhibits Zeeman splitting. Since the pair, formed by a quark and an antiquark with opposite spins, possesses a resultant magnetic moment, an external magnetic field can align it giving rise to a net magnetic moment for the ground state. This is the physical interpretation of the spin-one condensate. Our results show that the magnetically catalyzed ground state in QCD is actually richer than previously thought. The two condensates contribute to the effective mass of the LLL quasiparticles in such a way that the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration becomes enhanced.

## Key claim

The magnetically catalyzed ground state in QCD under a strong magnetic field generates not only the conventional chiral condensate but also a spin-one condensate, which gives quarks a dynamical anomalous magnetic moment; the two condensates together significantly increase the effective mass of lowest-Landau-level quasiparticles and thereby enhance the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration, contrary to lattice QCD results that show a decreasing critical temperature.

## Method

The authors use a one-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of massless quarks in a constant magnetic field, extending the standard scalar-pseudoscalar four-fermion interactions with a tensor channel that emerges from Fierz identities when rotational symmetry is broken by the magnetic field. They compute the mean-field effective potential using the Ritus transformation to diagonalize the fermion propagator in momentum space with explicit Landau-level dependence, then solve the gap equations in the lowest-Landau-level (LLL) approximation for magnetic fields \(qB \sim \Lambda^2\), and extend the analysis to finite temperature via Matsubara sums to obtain the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

* The zero-temperature dynamical mass of LLL quasiparticles including both condensates is \(M_\xi = \overline{\sigma} + \overline{\xi} = 2\Lambda \exp\left[-\frac{2\pi^2}{(G+G')N_c qB}\right]\), where \(\Lambda = 602.3\ \text{MeV}\) and \(G\Lambda^2 = 1.835\).
* The logarithm of the mass ratio between the model with and without the spin-one condensate is \(\ln(M_\xi / M_{\xi=0}) = \frac{2\pi^2}{G N_c qB} \left(\frac{\eta}{1+\eta}\right)\), which for \(\eta \simeq 1\) and \(q = |e|/3 \simeq 0.1\) gives \(\pi^2/(G N_c qB) \simeq 1.8\), corresponding to a sixfold increase in dynamical mass.
* The condensate \(\xi\) vanishes at a critical temperature \(T \sim 0.6 M_\xi\), where \(M_\xi\) is the zero-temperature dynamical mass.

## Context

This work extends the magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking (MC\(\chi\)SB) framework, previously studied in QED by Ferrer and de la Incera (2010), to QCD using an NJL model that includes tensor channels. It contradicts lattice QCD results (QCD-latticeB, 2013) which found that the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration decreases with magnetic field, whereas this model predicts an enhanced critical temperature due to the additional spin-one condensate. The results enable further studies of the QCD phase diagram under strong magnetic fields relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and to magnetar interiors.
