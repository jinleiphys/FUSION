# Extended Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick Hamiltonian

> The Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model was devised to test the validity of different approximate formalisms to treat many-particle systems. The model was constructed to be exactly solvable and yet non-trivial, in order to capture some of the main features of real physical systems. In the present contribution, we explicitly review the fact that different many-body approximations commonly used in different fields in physics clearly fail to describe the exact LMG solution. With similar assumptions as those adopted for the LMG model, we propose a new Hamiltonian based on a general two-body interaction. The new model (Extended LMG) is not only more general than the original LMG model and, therefore, with a potentially larger spectrum of applicability, but also the physics behind its exact solution can be much better captured by common many-body approximations. At the basis of this improvement lies a new term in the Hamiltonian that depends on the number of constituents and polarizes the system; the associated symmetry breaking is discussed, together with some implications for the study of more realistic systems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an Extended Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (ELMG) Hamiltonian that, unlike the original LMG model, includes a term proportional to \(G(N-1)\) that originates from a general two-body interaction but acts as a one-body term; this new term explicitly breaks symmetry and makes common many-body approximations (Hartree-Fock, Random Phase Approximation, second RPA) capture the exact solution much more accurately than they do for the original LMG model.

## Method

The authors start from the most general two-body Hamiltonian for fermions distributed on two \(M\)-fold degenerate levels separated by energy \(\varepsilon\), with a monopole interaction that does not change the quantum number \(p\). They write the Hamiltonian in quasi-spin SU(2) operators and, under mild assumptions on the matrix elements, derive a compact form containing coupling constants \(V\), \(W\), \(G\), and \(F\). For a fixed particle number \(N\), the \(F\) and constant terms are dropped, yielding the ELMG Hamiltonian: \(\mathcal{H} = \varepsilon J_z - \frac{V}{2}(J_+^2+J_-^2) - \frac{W}{2}(J_+J_-+J_-J_+) - G(J_++J_-)(N-1)\). The exact solution is obtained via angular momentum representation, and the authors compare exact results for \(N=20\) particles with Hartree-Fock (HF), 1-particle-1-hole Random Phase Approximation (RPA), and 2-particle-2-hole RPA (SRPA) calculations.

## Key numbers

- For the ELMG model with \(N=20\) particles and parameters \(v \equiv V(N-1)/\varepsilon\), \(w \equiv W(N-1)/\varepsilon\), \(g \equiv G(N-1)/\varepsilon\) set to \(v=w=g\), the deviation of HF and RPA ground-state energies from the exact result is below 0.05% (compared to several percent for the LMG model).
- In the LMG model, the HF ground-state deviation from the exact solution reaches a maximum around the border \(v+w \approx 1\), where the ground state transitions from non-degenerate (\(v+w<1\)) to degenerate (\(v+w>1\)).
- For the ELMG model at \(v=0.2\) (with \(v=w=g\)), the exact ground state shows the largest difference from the HF ground state, and RPA ground-state correlations reach a maximum at that value.

## Context

This work extends the original Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (Lipkin, 1965) by adding a term that arises from a general two-body interaction but reduces to a one-body term, which was not previously studied. The ELMG model overcomes a limitation of the LMG model: in SRPA calculations, the LMG model lacks coupling between 1-particle-1-hole and 2-particle-2-hole states, whereas the ELMG model includes such coupling. The paper shows that deformed systems (those with \(\alpha \neq 0\)) are more amenable to mean-field descriptions, and the ELMG model provides a pedagogical tool for understanding why mean-field methods work better for deformed nuclei. The new term \(G\) can be seen as a proxy for the interaction with nucleons outside closed shells that polarize the system.
