# Properties of single-particle states in a fully self-consistent particle-vibration coupling approach

> The properties of single-particle states in the magic nuclei $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, in particular the energies, spectroscopic factors and the effective mass, have been studied in a fully self-consistent particle-vibration coupling (PVC) approach within the framework of Skyrme energy density functional theory. All selected phonons are obtained by the Random Phase Approximation and the same Skyrme interaction is also used in the PVC vertex. We focus on the effect of the non-central two-body spin-orbit and tensor interactions on the single-particle properties. It has been found that the contributions of those terms are important to improve the results of $^{208}$Pb. The calculated single-particle energies and spectroscopic factors are compared to available experimental data. The single-particle level density around the Fermi surface is significantly increased due to the effect of PVC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including the non-central two-body spin-orbit and tensor interactions in the particle-vibration coupling (PVC) vertex, within a fully self-consistent Skyrme energy density functional framework, significantly improves the description of single-particle properties in $^{208}$Pb, but the overall agreement with experimental single-particle energies remains limited, with root-mean-square deviations actually increasing when these terms are added, suggesting a need for re-fitting the Skyrme parameters.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations using the Skyrme interactions SLy5 and T44 for the magic nuclei $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, then applied second-order perturbation theory to compute the particle-vibration coupling self-energy. The PVC vertex was constructed using the same Skyrme interaction as the mean field and RPA, progressively including central terms only, central plus spin-orbit terms, and finally central plus spin-orbit plus tensor terms, to isolate the effect of each non-central component on single-particle energies, spectroscopic factors, and effective masses.

## Key numbers

- For $^{40}$Ca with SLy5, the maximum energy shift from PVC with only central terms is -1.95 MeV for the $2p_{1/2}$ state and -1.88 MeV for the $2p_{3/2}$ state.
- For $^{40}$Ca with T44, the maximum energy shift from PVC with only central terms is -2.78 MeV for the $2p_{3/2}$ state and -2.68 MeV for the $2p_{1/2}$ state.
- The root-mean-square deviation $\sigma$ between theoretical and experimental single-particle energies in $^{40}$Ca with SLy5 increases from 1.026 MeV (HF) to 2.393 MeV (full PVC with all terms); with T44 it increases from 1.578 MeV (HF) to 3.010 MeV (full PVC).
- The effective $k$-mass around the Fermi surface in $^{40}$Ca is 0.852 (SLy5) and 0.857 (T44) in HF; the total effective mass $m^*/m$ from full PVC is 0.999 (SLy5) and 1.155 (T44).
- For $^{208}$Pb with SLy5, the $2f_{7/2}$ state shows a positive energy shift of +1.58 MeV in the full PVC calculation, moving from -12.07 MeV (HF) to -10.49 MeV (full PVC), compared to the experimental value of -9.81 MeV.
- The spectroscopic factor for the $1j_{15/2}$ state in $^{208}$Pb with SLy5 is 0.656 (theory) versus 0.58 (experiment); with T44 it is 0.583 (theory) versus 0.58 (experiment).

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent PVC calculations by Colo et al. (2010) and Mizuyama et al. (2012) that included only the central terms of the Skyrme force in the PVC vertex, by now incorporating the two-body spin-orbit and tensor interactions at the vertex for the first time. The study is motivated by recent work on tensor forces in Skyrme functionals by Brown et al. (2006), Colo et al. (2007), and others, which showed that tensor terms affect single-particle level evolution in exotic nuclei at the mean-field level. The results enable future re-fitting of Skyrme interaction parameters to account for PVC effects, and provide a benchmark for comparing fully self-consistent PVC calculations against experimental single-particle energies and spectroscopic factors in doubly magic nuclei.
