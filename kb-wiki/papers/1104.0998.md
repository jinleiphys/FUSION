# Anomaly-induced Quadrupole Moment of the Neutron in Magnetic Field

> The neutrons cannot possess a quadrupole moment in the vacuum. Nevertheless, we show that in the presence of an external magnetic field the neutrons acquire a new type of quadrupole moment $Q^{ij}= χ\,σ^i B^j$ involving the components of spin and magnetic field. This "chiral magnetic" quadrupole moment arises from the interplay of the chiral anomaly and the magnetic field; we estimate its value for the neutron in the static limit, and find $χ\simeq 1.35\cdot10^{-2}\,{\rm fm}^4$. The detection of the quadrupole moment of the neutron would provide a novel test of the role of the chiral anomaly in low-energy QCD and can be possible in the presence of both magnetic and inhomogeneous electric fields. The quadrupole moment of the neutron may affect e.g. the properties of neutron stars and magnetars.

## Key claim

Neutrons, which cannot possess a quadrupole moment in vacuum, acquire a new type of "chiral magnetic" quadrupole moment \(Q^{ij}= \chi\,\sigma^i B^j\) when placed in an external magnetic field, arising from the interplay of the chiral anomaly and the magnetic field; the authors estimate its value in the static limit only, noting that non-static chiral corrections are expected to be small but have not been fully evaluated.

## Method

The authors compute the expectation value of the Abelian chiral anomaly Lagrangian \(\mathcal{L}_1 = \frac{N_c e^2}{12\pi^2} \frac{\pi^0}{f_\pi} \vec{E}\cdot\vec{B}\) over a neutron state in the static limit. They use the pion field sourced by the neutron through the Klein-Gordon equation with a pseudoscalar coupling, perform a non-relativistic reduction of the neutron wave function, and extract the quadrupole moment from the resulting effective Hamiltonian by functional differentiation with respect to \(\nabla^i E^j\). The Goldberger-Treiman relation is used to express the result in terms of the axial charge \(g_A\).

## Key numbers

* The chiral magnetic quadrupole moment of the neutron is \(\chi \simeq 1.35 \cdot 10^{-2}\,\mathrm{fm}^4\), defined by \(Q^{ij} = \chi\,\sigma^i B^j\).
* The numerical estimate uses \(g_A = 1.23\), \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, and \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV.
* The calculation is performed in the static limit; non-static corrections are argued to be small but not numerically quantified.

## Context

This work extends the study of anomaly-induced effects on electric charge distribution inside nucleons by Eto (2011) and the finding of anomaly-induced quadrupole deformation of quark-gluon plasma in a magnetic field by Burnier (2011). The resulting operator also appears in the low-energy expansion of polarized Compton scattering at order \(\omega^3\), where the authors' evaluation corresponds to the pion pole part of the spin-polarizability of the neutron, which dominates over higher-order chiral loops and \(\Delta\pi\) contributions as discussed by L'vov (1998) and Holstein (1999). The result enables a novel test of the chiral anomaly in low-energy QCD and may affect the properties of neutron stars and magnetars, potentially leading to macroscopic precession of angular momentum in anisotropic \(^3P_2\) neutron superfluids as suggested by Leggett.
