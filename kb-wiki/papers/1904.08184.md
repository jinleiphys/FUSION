# Real-time dynamics of axion particle production due to spontaneous decay of a coherent axion field

> We show that the coherent axion field spontaneously decays by emitting axion particles via quantum tunneling if axion potential has more than one minimum. By employing the $\hbar$-expansion and mean field approximation, we develop a formalism to trace the real-time dynamics of the axion particle production including its backreaction to the coherent axion field. We also present numerical results for the time evolution of various physical quantities including the strength of the coherent axion field, phase-space density of produced axion particles, energy density, and pressure. Phenomenological implications of our results are also discussed.

## Key claim

The coherent axion field spontaneously decays by emitting axion particles via quantum tunneling, but only if the axion potential has more than one minimum; for a single-minimum potential, quantum fluctuations cannot grow and the coherent field remains stable.

## Method

The authors separate the total axion field into a classical coherent field (vacuum expectation value) and a quantum fluctuation field, then apply an $\hbar$-expansion and mean field approximation to derive coupled equations of motion. They solve these equations as an initial value problem using the adiabatic particle picture, where the mode function is approximated by the lowest-order WKB solution, and compute the in-vacuum expectation value of the number operator via Bogoliubov transformation to quantify axion particle production including backreaction.

## Key numbers

* The axion self-coupling constant is estimated as $g \sim \chi/f_a^4 \lesssim 10^{-40}$, where $\chi \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^4$ is the topological susceptibility of QCD.
* The $\hbar$-expansion is justified when the condition $gf \lesssim 100$ is satisfied, where $f$ is the phase-space density; the expansion becomes invalid if $f \gg 100/g \gtrsim 10^{42}$.
* The typical momentum scale of produced axions is given by the axion mass scale $m$, with $\int d^3{\bm p} \sim (4\pi/3)m^3 \sim m^3$.
* The phase-space density of produced axions is estimated as $f \equiv (2\pi)^3 d^6N/d{\bm x}^3 d{\bm p}^3 \sim 10^2 \times N/(m^3 V)$.

## Context

This work proposes a novel axion particle production mechanism via spontaneous decay of a coherent axion field through quantum tunneling, which is distinct from previously studied mechanisms such as the Primakoff effect (Dic78, Fuk82a, Fuk82b, Raf86), thermal production in the early Universe (Tur87, Mas02, Gra11, Sal14), and parametric resonance (Pat99, Ray18, Sod18, Kia18, Hay19). The formalism builds on field-theoretical studies of real-time particle production with backreaction developed for the Schwinger mechanism (Coo89, Klu91, Klu92, Klu93, Tan09), and the mean field approximation is justified because the axion self-coupling is extremely small ($g \lesssim 10^{-40}$), making scattering effects negligible unless the phase-space density exceeds $10^{40}$. The results enable quantitative predictions for axion production from coherent fields seeded by the misalignment mechanism (Pre83, Abb83, Mic83), QCD sphalerons, or classical electromagnetic fields with nonvanishing ${\bm E}\cdot{\bm B}$.
