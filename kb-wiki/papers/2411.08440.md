# Bayesian evaluation of hadron-quark phase transition models through neutron star observables in light of nuclear and astrophysics data

> We investigate the role of hybrid and nucleonic equations of state (EOSs) within neutron star (NS) interiors using Bayesian inference to evaluate their alignment with recent observational data from NICER and LIGO-Virgo (LV) collaborations. We find that smooth hybrid EOSs are slightly favoured in explaining NS mass-radius relations, particularly for pulsars such as PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620. However, this preference is not definitive, as gravitational wave (GW) data does not significantly differentiate between our hybrid and nucleonic models. Our analysis also reveals tensions between older NICER data and recent measurements for PSR J0437-4715, highlighting the need for more flexible EOS models. Through two sampling approaches - one fixing the hadronic EOS set and the other without fixing the same, we demonstrate that the hybrid EOS model can incorporate stiffer EOSs, resulting in a better agreement with NICER data but leading to higher tidal deformability, which is less consistent with GW observations. In some recent publications a parameter $d_c$, related to the trace anomaly and its derivative, is used to indicate the presence of deconfined quark matter. We find that our hadronic model, which does not include phase transition to deconfined matter, under the influence of imposed constraints, is able to predict values below 0.2 for $d_c$ at around five times saturation density. The hybrid model goes below this threshold at lower densities under the same conditions.

## Key claim

Smooth hybrid equations of state (EOSs) incorporating hadron-quark phase transitions are slightly favored over purely nucleonic EOSs in explaining neutron star mass-radius relations from NICER data, but this preference is not definitive because gravitational wave data does not significantly differentiate between the two model classes.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid EOSs by combining a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model for hadronic matter with a Mean Field Theory of Quantum Chromodynamics (MFTQCD) model for quark matter, using a smooth crossover transition controlled by parameters δ0 and μC. They performed Bayesian inference with nested sampling (PyMultiNest, 2000 live points, ~10,000 samples per set) under two approaches: a Fixed Hadronic (FH) scenario where the hadronic EOS distribution was held fixed while sampling quark parameters, and a Combined Parameter Sampling (CPS) scenario where all hadronic and quark parameters were varied simultaneously. Constraints included χEFT calculations at low densities, nuclear saturation properties, NICER mass-radius posteriors for PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J0437-4715, tidal deformability from GW170817, and pQCD constraints at 7 times saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Median maximum mass for purely nucleonic Set0: 2.07 M⊙
- Median radius at 1.4 M⊙ for Set0: 12.26 km; for Set1 CPS: 12.51 km
- Median tidal deformability at 1.4 M⊙ for Set0: 389; for Set1 CPS: 447
- Median f-mode frequency at 1.4 M⊙ for Set0: 1.812 kHz; for Set1 CPS: 1.712 kHz
- Median GW damping time at 1.4 M⊙ for Set0: 0.111 s; for Set1 CPS: 0.124 s
- Maximum mass for Set1 CPS: 2.21 M⊙; for Set2 CPS: similar to Set1 FH at 2.01 M⊙
- Pearson correlation coefficient between radii at 1.34 M⊙ and 2.07 M⊙: 0.77 for Set0, 0.70 for Set1 CPS, 0.45 for Set1 FH
- The purely nucleonic model predicts dc values below 0.2 at around 5 times saturation density (ρB,0 = 0.16 fm-3); the hybrid model crosses this threshold at lower densities

## Context

This work extends Bayesian analyses of neutron star EOSs such as those by Essick (2023) and Lin (2023), who studied radius correlations as signatures of phase transitions, and Guha Roy (2024), who analyzed selected hybrid EOSs from the CompOSE database. The study directly compares nucleonic and hybrid EOSs using the same Bayesian framework, finding that while hybrid models incorporating stiff hadronic EOSs better fit NICER data, they produce higher tidal deformabilities less consistent with GW170817 constraints from LIGO-Virgo. The analysis of the conformality parameter dc follows the approach of Annala (2023), who proposed dc < 0.2 as an indicator of deconfined quark matter, but finds that even purely nucleonic models can reach this threshold at high densities. The results highlight tensions between older NICER data and recent PSR J0437-4715 measurements, motivating more flexible EOS models.
