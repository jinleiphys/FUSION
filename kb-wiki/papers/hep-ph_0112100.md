# Quasiquarks in two stream system

> We study the collective quark excitations in an extremely anisotropic system of two interpenetrating streams of the quark-gluon plasma. In contrast to the gluon modes, all quark ones appear to be stable in such a system. Even more, the quark modes in the two-stream system are very similar to those in the isotropic plasma.

# Quasiquarks in two stream system

## Key claim
All quark collective excitations in an extremely anisotropic two-stream quark-gluon plasma are stable, unlike the unstable gluon modes; the quark modes are very similar to those in isotropic plasma, but the paper's claim is hedged by the fact that the analysis uses a highly simplified delta-function distribution for two interpenetrating streams, and the similarity holds most strongly in the long-wavelength limit.

## Method
The quark self-energy was computed at one-loop level within the Hard Loop approximation for an arbitrary but symmetric parton momentum distribution. The dispersion relations for quasiquarks were then derived by solving the pole equation of the quark propagator for the specific case of two interpenetrating streams with delta-function momentum distributions along the z-axis. The resulting modes were compared analytically to those in isotropic plasma using the long-wavelength approximation.

## Key numbers
- The effective mass parameter for the two-stream system is defined as \( m^2 \equiv g^2 C_F \rho /(8|\mathbf{q}|) \), where \( \rho \) is the parton density in each stream and \( \mathbf{q} \) is the stream momentum.
- The dispersion relations for the two-stream system are \( \omega_{\pm}^2(k_T,k_z) = m^2 + k_z^2 + \frac{1}{2} k_T^2 \pm \sqrt{m^2 k_T^2 + \frac{1}{4}k_T^4} \), which for \( m \gg k_T \) approximates to \( m^2 + k_z^2 + \frac{1}{2} k_T^2 \pm m k_T \).
- For the isotropic plasma, the long-wavelength (\( \omega \gg k \)) dispersion is \( \omega_{\pm}^2(k) \cong M^2 + \frac{1}{2} k^2 \pm M k \), where \( M^2 \equiv \frac{g^2}{32 \pi^2}C_F \int_0^{\infty} dp \, p \, f(p) \).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies of unstable gluon modes in anisotropic parton systems (Mrowczynski 1988, 1993, 1994) by examining quark modes for the first time. It contrasts with the gluon instabilities found in two-stream systems by Pokrovsky (1988, 1990) and Pavlenko (1992). The results enable further study of Cherenkov radiation from quasiquarks in the nonequilibrium plasma produced at the early stage of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the author argues the two-stream results approximately hold for any strongly elongated momentum distribution.
