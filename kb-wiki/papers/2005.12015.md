# Isovector parton distribution functions of the proton on a superfine lattice

> We study isovector unpolarized and helicity parton distribution functions (PDF) of the proton within the framework of Large Momentum Effective Theory. We use a gauge ensemble, generated by the MILC Collaboration, with a superfine lattice spacing of $0.042$ fm and a pion mass of $310$ MeV, enabling us to simultaneously reach sub-fermi spatial separations and larger nucleon momenta. We compare the spatial dependence of quasi-PDF matrix elements in different renormalization schemes with the corresponding results of the global fits, obtained using 1-loop perturbative matching. We present determinations of the first four moments of the unpolarized and helicity PDFs of proton from the Ioffe-time dependence of the isovector matrix elements, obtained by employing a ratio-based renormalization scheme.

## Key claim
Using a superfine lattice with spacing 0.042 fm (at least twice smaller than any previous nucleon PDF lattice calculation) and pion mass 310 MeV, the authors demonstrate that 1-loop perturbative matching can describe the spatial dependence of quasi-PDF matrix elements when compared to global fits, but only for spatial separations where z Λ_QCD ≪ 1 and nucleon momenta up to 2.77 GeV.

## Method
The authors computed isovector unpolarized (Γ=γ_t) and helicity (Γ=γ_z γ_5) quasi-PDF matrix elements on a 64³×192 MILC HISQ ensemble with a=0.042 fm and M_π≈310 MeV, using clover valence fermions with HYP smearing and Gaussian momentum-smeared sources. They extracted bare matrix elements from three-point to two-point function ratios at source-sink separations t_s=16a, 18a, 20a (0.67–0.84 fm) using two- and three-state fits, then applied nonperturbative RI-MOM renormalization and 1-loop perturbative matching to compare with phenomenological PDFs.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a=0.042 fm; spatial volume: 64³×192; pion mass: M_π≈310 MeV
- Nucleon momenta: P_z = n_z × 2π/L with n_z=0–6, corresponding to 0.46, 0.92, 1.38, 1.84, 2.31, and 2.77 GeV in physical units
- Three-point functions computed at P_z=1.84 GeV (n_z=4) and 2.31 GeV (n_z=5) for source-sink separations t_s=16a, 18a, 20a (≈0.67, 0.76, 0.84 fm)
- Total statistics: 3258 measurements from 6 sources per configuration on 543 gauge configurations
- RI-MOM renormalization constants shown at μ_R=4 GeV for z=16a≈0.67 fm and p_z^R values of 0.93 GeV and 2.3 GeV

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD calculations of nucleon PDFs (Liu 2018, Lin 2018, Alexandrou 2019, Joo 2019) to a superfine lattice spacing a=0.042 fm, which is at least twice as fine as all previous nucleon PDF lattice studies (which used a>0.08 fm). The fine lattice enables simultaneous access to sub-fermi spatial separations and larger nucleon momenta (up to 2.77 GeV) while keeping aP_z≪1, reducing discretization and higher-twist corrections. This enables systematic comparison of lattice quasi-PDF matrix elements with global fits (de Florian 2014) and tests the applicability of 1-loop perturbative matching, paving the way for future calculations at physical pion mass and with higher-order matching.
