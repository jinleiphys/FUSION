# Vorticity and magnetic field production in relativistic ideal fluids

> In the framework of relativistic ideal hydrodynamics, we study the production mechanism for vorticity and magnetic field in relativistic ideal fluids. It is demonstrated that in the uncharged fluids the thermal vorticity will always satisfy the Kelvin's theorem and the circulation must be conserved. However, in the charged fluids, the vorticity and magnetic field can be produced by the interaction between the entropy gradients and the fluid velocity gradients. Especially, in the multiple charged fluids, the vorticity and magnetic field can be produced by the interaction between the inhomogenous charge density ratio and the fluid velocity gradients even if the entropy distribution is homogeneous, which provides another mechanism for the production of vorticity and magnetic field in relativistic plasmas or in the early universe.

# Wiki Page: Vorticity and Magnetic Field Production in Relativistic Ideal Fluids

## Key claim
In relativistic ideal fluids with multiple conserved charges, vorticity and magnetic fields can be produced even when the entropy distribution is homogeneous, through the interaction between inhomogeneous charge density ratios and fluid velocity gradients. This new mechanism operates alongside the previously known baroclinic and relativistic baroclinic effects, and is distinct from the case of uncharged fluids where thermal vorticity always satisfies Kelvin's circulation theorem and cannot emerge from zero initial value.

## Method
The authors derive the relativistic Kelvin circulation theorem for uncharged ideal fluids using the thermal vorticity tensor defined from the temperature current \(T u^\mu\). For charged fluids with multiple conserved currents (one electric current from local gauge symmetry and additional currents from global symmetries such as baryon number), they construct a generalized thermal vorticity tensor incorporating the electromagnetic field tensor and the enthalpy-per-charge function \(f = (\varepsilon+P)/n\). The circulation equation is then evaluated for synchronic loops in the lab frame, yielding source terms proportional to gradients of thermodynamic quantities.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of the pure relativistic source term to the baroclinic source term scales as \(|S_r|/|S_b| \approx v^2/(1-v^2)\) for comparable velocity and entropy gradients.
- The ratio of the new relativistic charge-density-ratio term to the corresponding nonrelativistic term scales as \(|S_{nr}|/|S_{n\mu}| \approx v^2/(1-v^2)\) for comparable velocity and chemical potential gradients.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic vorticity production mechanisms of Mahajan (2010, 2011), which considered only single conserved charges, to systems with multiple conserved currents. It also treats the uncharged fluid case separately, which cannot be obtained as a limit of the charged case due to the \(1/n\) singularity. The results are relevant to vorticity and magnetic field generation in relativistic plasmas, the early universe, and quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The new mechanism involving inhomogeneous charge density ratios could affect interpretations of the chiral vortical effect observed by STAR (Xu, 2014) and the Bjorken scaling scenario (Bjorken, 1982) where initial vorticity is zero.
