# Gauge-invariant meson photoproduction with extended nucleons

> The general gauge-invariant photoproduction formalism given by Haberzettl is applied to kaon photoproduction off the nucleon at the tree level, with form factors describing composite nucleons. Numerical results show that this gauge-invariance procedure, when compared to Ohta's, leads to a much improved description of experimental data. Predictions for the new Bonn SAPHIR data for $p(γ,K^+)Λ$ are given.

## Key claim

The gauge-invariant photoproduction formalism of Haberzettl, when applied to kaon photoproduction off the nucleon at tree level with composite-nucleon form factors, yields a much improved description of experimental data compared to Ohta's gauge-invariance procedure. The improvement is conditional on the choice of the contact-current function \(\widehat{F}\) as a linear combination of all form factors, rather than Ohta's constant value.

## Method

The authors applied the general gauge-invariant photoproduction formalism of Haberzettl (1997) to the reactions \(\gamma p \rightarrow \Lambda K^+\) and \(\gamma p \rightarrow \Sigma^0 K^+\) at tree level, using form factors to describe composite nucleons. They compared two prescriptions for restoring gauge invariance: Ohta's method, which sets the contact-current function \(\widehat{F}\) to a constant (the same as for point-like nucleons), and Haberzettl's method, which allows \(\widehat{F}\) to be a linear combination of the \(s\)-, \(u\)-, and \(t\)-channel form factors with coefficients summing to unity. Numerical fits were performed using the resonance model of Mart and Bennhold (1995) and the coupled-channels \(K\)-matrix model of Feuster and Mosel (1998).

## Key numbers

* For the reaction \(\gamma p \rightarrow \Lambda K^+\), the \(\chi^2\) per data point obtained with Haberzettl's method is better than Ohta's by at least a factor of two, across a range of the leading Born coupling constant \(g_{K \Lambda N} / \sqrt{4 \pi}\).
* In Haberzettl's method, the form factor cutoff \(\Lambda\) decreases with increasing \(g_{K \Lambda N}\) coupling constant, leaving the effective coupling (coupling constant times form factor) roughly constant; in Ohta's method, the cutoff remains insensitive to the coupling constant.
* Predictions for differential cross sections of \(p(\gamma, K^+)\Lambda\) are shown at four energies for which new Bonn SAPHIR data exist (the data were not included in the fit).

## Context

This work extends the general gauge-invariant photoproduction formalism of Haberzettl (1997) to kaon photoproduction, contrasting it with the earlier approach of Ohta (1989) which uses a constant contact current. The results show that Ohta's method is too restrictive to account for hadronic structure while maintaining gauge invariance, whereas Haberzettl's method provides the needed flexibility. The findings are corroborated by the coupled-channels work of Feuster and Mosel (1998), and the predictions for the new Bonn SAPHIR data enable future tests of the formalism.
