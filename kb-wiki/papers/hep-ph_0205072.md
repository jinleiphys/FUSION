# SU(2)_L * SU(2)_R and U(1)_A restorations high in the hadron spectrum and what it tells us about

> Recent data for highly excited mesons suggest that not only the chiral $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R$ symmetry of QCD is restored high in the spectrum but also the $ U(1)_A$ symmetry. This means that it is not a confining interaction in QCD which triggers the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry. The restoration of the $U(2)_L\times U(2)_R$ symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian implies the appearance of multiplets of this group high in the hadron spectra. Such type of multiplets is naturally explained within the string picture of confinement. It also supports the scenario that the $U(1)_A$ breaking is related to instantons and not to the gluonic interaction responsible for confinement.

## Key claim

Recent data from partial wave analysis of proton-antiproton annihilation at LEAR suggest that not only the chiral SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R symmetry of QCD is restored high in the hadron spectrum, but also the U(1)_A symmetry, which would imply that the confining interaction in QCD is not responsible for triggering spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The author presents this as a preliminary conclusion that would be strongly supported by discovery of missing a_0 and eta mesons in specific mass regions.

## Method

The author analyzed meson masses from partial wave analysis of p-bar p annihilation at LEAR, focusing on scalar (f_0, a_0) and pseudoscalar (pi, eta) mesons in the mass range 1.8-2.4 GeV. The masses of these states were compared to identify degeneracy patterns predicted by chiral and U(1)_A symmetry restoration, specifically looking for parity doublets and full U(2)_L x U(2)_R multiplets. The analysis was limited to the two-flavor (u,d) sector, excluding one f_0 state at 2102 +/- 13 MeV that was identified as a glueball candidate rather than a q-bar-q state.

## Key numbers

- pi(1801 +/- 13) MeV and f_0(1770 +/- 12) MeV are approximately degenerate, indicating chiral symmetry restoration
- pi(2070 +/- 35) MeV and f_0(2040 +/- 38) MeV are approximately degenerate
- pi(2360 +/- 25) MeV and f_0(2337 +/- 14) MeV are approximately degenerate
- a_0(2025 +/- ?) MeV and eta(2010 +35/-60) MeV are approximately degenerate, suggesting U(1)_A restoration
- The lowest pseudoscalar and scalar mesons show strong symmetry breaking: m_pi ~ 140 MeV, m_f0 ~ 400-1200 MeV, m_a0 ~ 985 MeV, m_eta-bar ~ 782 MeV
- The unmixed eta-bar mass is approximately 782 MeV, extracted from physical eta and eta-prime masses

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Cohen and Glozman (2002) on effective chiral symmetry restoration high in hadron spectra, and contrasts with the nonrelativistic potential model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985), which fails to predict the observed high-lying states. The results support the instanton-based solution to the U(1)_A problem proposed by 't Hooft (1976) over the Witten-Veneziano mechanism, and argue against scenarios where confinement itself causes chiral or U(1)_A breaking. The findings enable further experimental searches for missing a_0 and eta states around 1.8 GeV and 2.3 GeV to confirm full U(2)_L x U(2)_R symmetry restoration.
