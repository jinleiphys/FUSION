# The impact of charge symmetry and charge independence breaking on the properties of neutrons and protons in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

> We investigate the effects of charge independence and charge symmetry breaking in neutron-rich matter. We consider neutron and proton properties in isospin-asymmetric matter at normal densities as well as the high-density neutron matter equation of state and the bulk properties of neutron stars. We find charge symmetry and charge independence breaking effects to be very small.

# The impact of charge symmetry and charge independence breaking on the properties of neutrons and protons in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim

Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) and charge independence breaking (CIB) effects on neutron and proton properties in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter are very small, even at high neutron relative concentrations and high densities; however, the symmetry potential shows small but noticeable sensitivity at low momentum, and the neutron star maximum mass increases by only about 1.3% due to CIB.

## Method

The authors used the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) description of nuclear matter with the charge-dependent CD-Bonn potential, which provides separate potentials for nn, pp, and np pairs, to compute single-particle potentials, effective masses, and the symmetry potential in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter as a function of density and neutron excess parameter. For neutron star properties, they compared predictions from three models: no charge dependence, CIB/CSB via the CD-Bonn mass-splitting mechanism, and CSB via rho-omega mixing with a mixing matrix element extracted from e+e- to pi+pi- annihilation.

## Key numbers

- nn scattering length: a_nn = -18.9 ± 0.4 fm; pp scattering length: a_pp = -17.3 ± 0.4 fm; np scattering length: a_np = -23.74 ± 0.02 fm (experimental values from literature)
- CD-Bonn predictions: a_nn = -18.9680 fm, a_pp = -17.4602 fm, a_np = -23.7380 fm
- rho-omega mixing matrix element: <rho^0|H_m|omega> = -0.00452 ± 0.0006 GeV^2
- Neutron star maximum mass changes from 1.58 to 1.6 solar masses due to CIB, a variation of 1.3%
- Effective masses and single-particle potentials computed at Fermi momenta of 1.4 fm^-1 and 2 fm^-1, with neutron excess parameters of 0.5 and nearly 1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the nuclear equation of state (Sammarruca, 2010) by incorporating charge-dependent effects into isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter calculations. It contrasts with earlier work by Ishihara et al. (2000), who found that CSB could reduce neutron star mass by as much as 35% using a density-dependent rho-omega mixing mechanism through baryon loops, whereas the present study attributes rho-omega mixing to electromagnetic coupling and finds much smaller effects. The results enable future investigations of relativistic effects on CIB and CSB in stellar matter, and the authors plan to develop a charge-dependent relativistic potential for Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations.
