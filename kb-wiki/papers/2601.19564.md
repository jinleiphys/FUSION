# Octet baryon electroweak form factors in dense nuclear matter

> Motivated by the necessity of developing theoretical models for studying the electroweak structure of baryons in a nuclear medium, we apply a covariant quark model to study interactions of baryons with nuclear matter. The electromagnetic and axial form factors of the octet baryons are determined by combining a covariant quark model that takes into account the meson cloud dressing of the baryon cores, developed for free space, with the quark-meson coupling model in the extension to the nuclear medium. We discuss the medium modifications on the electroweak form factors of octet baryons for the range of densities from $ρ=0$ up to $ρ=2 ρ_0$, where $ρ_0= 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$ is the normal nuclear matter density. We also study how the shape of the form factors is modified in finite nuclei due to the profile of the nuclear density distributions compared with calculations using the average density of the nucleus

## Key claim

The paper claims that a covariant spectator quark model combined with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model can describe the medium modifications of octet baryon electroweak form factors for densities up to \(2\rho_0\), but the results are conditional on a smooth extension of chiral perturbation theory expressions for the in-medium pion decay constant at densities above \(\rho_0\).

## Method

The authors combine a covariant spectator quark model (which includes valence quarks and meson cloud dressing in free space) with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to compute electromagnetic and axial form factors of octet baryons in nuclear matter. In-medium coupling constants are determined from axial couplings, baryon masses, and the in-medium pion decay constant \(f_\pi^*\). For finite nuclei, effective in-nucleus form factors are obtained by integrating the local-density-dependent form factors weighted by the nuclear density profile.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Form factors calculated for constant densities \(\rho = 0\), \(\rho = 0.5\rho_0\), \(\rho = \rho_0\), and \(\rho = 2\rho_0\)
- For \(^{12}\)C, calculations using the nuclear density profile and the average density agree within a few percent below \(Q^2 = 0.2\) GeV\(^2\)
- In progress: calculations of proton double ratios \((G_E^*/G_M^*)/(G_E/G_M)\) for nuclei \(^{12}\)C, \(^{16}\)O, and \(^{40}\)Ca

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nucleon form factors in medium by Ramalho, Tsushima, and collaborators (Medium1, Medium2, Medium3) to higher densities and to finite nuclei. The results are consistent with suppression of the proton \(G_E/G_M\) ratio measured at MAMI and JLab (Dieterich 2001, Strauch 2003). The formalism enables calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections and studies of bremsstrahlung from nuclei (Maydanyuk 2026), with future applications to astrophysical dense matter up to \(3\rho_0\).
