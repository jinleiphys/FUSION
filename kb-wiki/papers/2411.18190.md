# The Equation of State and Multiparticle Production

> We discuss the distribution of fireballs produced in heavy-ion collisions in the net-baryon number and argue that neither the Free-Quark Model (FQM) nor the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model can provide a comprehensive explanation of the distribution observed at the LHC. The concept of net-baryon number freezeout temperature is suggested and the role of sea quarks as a possible source of net-baryon number fluctuations is emphasized.

# Wiki Page: The Equation of State and Multiparticle Production

## Key claim

Neither the Free-Quark Model (FQM) nor the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model can provide a comprehensive explanation of the net-baryon number distribution observed at the LHC, because the HRG model cannot account for the suppression of net-baryon transport at midrapidity at LHC energies, while the FQM predictions disagree with experimental cumulant ratios even after including finite-volume and acceptance corrections.

## Method

The author analyzes the relation between the equation of state of fireball matter and the probability distribution of fireballs in net-baryon number \(B\), using the fugacity expansion of the grand canonical partition function. Finite-volume corrections and the effect of a transverse momentum cutoff in the FQM are estimated by direct computation of the partition function for a zero-triality gas of massless free quarks in a cube of volume \(V\), dividing the fireball into approximately 40 independent cubes. The net-baryon number rapidity distribution and the baryon chemical potential at LHC energies are examined to argue that net-baryon number freezeout occurs at a temperature \(T_{Bf}\) significantly higher than the pseudocritical temperature \(T_{pc}\).

## Key numbers

- LHC lead-lead collision energy: \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 5\) TeV per nucleon-nucleon pair.
- Freezeout temperature: \(T_f \approx 155\) MeV, approximately equal to the pseudocritical temperature \(T_{pc}\) of the chiral crossover.
- Fireball radius: \(R = 7\) fm; time when net-baryon number ceases to fluctuate: \(\tau \sim 2\) fm/\(c\).
- Dimensionless volume parameter for each cube: \(\nu \sim 30\) at \(T \sim 300\) MeV.
- Direct computation of FQM with \(\nu = 30\): \(\kappa_2 = 6.68(2)\), \(\kappa_4/\kappa_2 = 0.0657(2)\), \(\kappa_6/\kappa_2 = 0.00139(2)\).
- FQM with \(\nu = 30\) and transverse momentum cutoff \(p_\perp > 300\) MeV: \(\kappa_2 = 3.62(1)\), \(\kappa_4/\kappa_2 = 0.0857(2)\), \(\kappa_6/\kappa_2 = 0.00202(3)\).
- Textbook FQM formula (infinite volume, no cutoff): \(\kappa_2 = 6.667\), \(\kappa_4/\kappa_2 = 0.0056\), \(\kappa_6/\kappa_2 = 0.00000\).
- FQM cumulant ratio: \(\chi_4/\chi_2 = 1/(2\pi^2 N_c^2)\); HRG cumulant ratio: \(\chi_{2n}/\chi_2 = 1\) for all \(n\).
- Roberge-Weiss temperature: \(T_{RW} \sim 200\) MeV; lattice QCD indicates FQM behavior for \(T > 200\)–\(230\) MeV.
- Baryon chemical potential at LHC: \(\mu < 1\) MeV (ALICE, 2023).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of net-baryon number fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, building on lattice QCD studies by Bazavov (2017) and Bornyakov (2016, 2022) that show the FQM equation of state applies at high temperatures. The author contrasts the HRG model predictions, which agree with ALICE data (Behera, 2018), with the FQM predictions that disagree. The paper introduces the concept of a net-baryon number freezeout temperature \(T_{Bf} \gtrsim 300\) MeV, distinct from the hadronic freezeout temperature, and suggests that sea quarks from colliding nuclei may be a source of net-baryon number fluctuations at early stages, a possibility requiring further study. This challenges the standard assumption that HRG model agreement with data at LHC energies provides a complete explanation.
