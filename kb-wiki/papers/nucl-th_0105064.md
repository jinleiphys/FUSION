# New Magic Numbers and New Islands of Stability in Drip-Line Regions

## Key claim

The systematics of local energies and two-neutron separation energies from the infinite nuclear matter (INM) model mass predictions provide strong evidence for new neutron magic numbers 100, 152, and 164, a new proton magic number 78, and new islands of stability in drip-line regions, where the classical magic numbers are found to be no longer valid. The evidence is based on the behavior of the local energy η and S₂n isolines in the model's predictions, not on direct experimental observation.

## Method

The author used the infinite nuclear matter (INM) model, which treats the ground-state energy of a nucleus as the sum of the energy of a perfect sphere of infinite nuclear matter plus a residual local energy η that captures shell, deformation, and diffuseness effects. The model was fitted to 5652 data points (binding energies plus neutron and proton separation energies) for 1884 known nuclei to determine five global parameters, then extrapolated to predict masses of 7208 nuclei. The systematics of the local energy η and two-neutron separation energies S₂n were examined as functions of neutron and proton numbers to identify signatures of shell closures (Gaussian peaks in η and sharp bending in S₂n isolines) in drip-line regions.

## Key numbers

- The mass prediction achieved an r.m.s. deviation of 401 keV and a mean deviation of 9 keV for 7208 nuclei.
- New neutron magic number 100 identified from η Gaussian peaks for Z = 58, 60, and 62, with corresponding S₂n bending at N = 100.
- New neutron magic number 152 identified from η Gaussian peaks for Z = 78, 80, 82, and 84, with the peak for Z = 78 around N = 152 described as "as good and even better than the peak for Z = 82 around N = 126 in the valley of stability."
- New neutron magic number 164 identified from a Gaussian peak for Z = 90 and S₂n bending for Z = 88 and 90 at N = 164.
- New proton magic number 78 identified from the η peak at Z = 78 around N = 152.
- New island of stability identified around N = 100, Z ≈ 62.
- New island of stability identified around N = 152, Z = 78.
- New island of stability identified around N = 164, Z ≈ 90.

## Context

This work extends the INM model mass predictions previously developed by Satpathy and Nayak (1999) and the demonstration by Nayak (1999) that η systematics show vanishing of shells in drip-line regions. It contradicts the expectation that classical magic numbers (such as N = 82, 126 and Z = 50, 82) remain valid far from stability, showing instead that they undergo shell quenching in drip-line regions. The prediction of N = 164 as a magic number had been anticipated theoretically by Nazarewicz et al. (1989) and Ragnarsson et al. (1998), and this work provides empirical support from the INM model. The results enable experimental searches for these new islands of stability using radioactive ion beam facilities, and have implications for r-process nucleosynthesis and stellar evolution.
