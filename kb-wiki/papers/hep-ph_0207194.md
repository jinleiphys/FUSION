# Equivalence of Classical Skyrmions and Coherent States of Baryons I. Constrained Quantization on the SU(2) and SO(3) manifolds

> In the Skyrme model, the Lagrangian can be quantized in several ways using the collective coordinate approach. Not all of which produce quantum states that can be interpreted as physical particles. For example the SU(2) collective coordinate approach produces both integral and half-integral spin and isospin states. Only half of these are the physical baryons. Less well known is the fact that it is equally possible to quantize the system using the SO(3) collective coordinates. This produces only unphysical integral spin and isospin states. To fulfill the goal of being able to express a classical skyrmion as a coherent state of baryons directly in terms of the baryon states, surprisingly a combination of both collective coordinate approaches is required. To prepare for the subsequent application to skyrmion formation through disoriented chiral condensates in heavy ion collisions, the Skyrme model is rigorously quantized using the Dirac prescription for constrained systems. This is shown for both the SU(2) as well as the SO(3) collective coordinate approach.

## Key claim

The paper argues that to represent a classical skyrmion as a coherent state of baryons, one must use a combination of both SU(2) and SO(3) collective coordinate quantization approaches, because the SU(2) approach alone produces both physical baryon states (half-integral spin and isospin) and unphysical states (integral spin and isospin), while the SO(3) approach produces only unphysical integral spin and isospin states.

## Method

The Skyrme model Lagrangian is quantized using the Dirac prescription for constrained systems, treating the collective coordinates as living on the S^3 manifold. The Dirac brackets are constructed from the second-class constraints arising from the SU(2) unitarity condition, and these brackets are then promoted to quantum commutators. Differential representations for the momentum operators and angular momentum operators are derived, and the Schrodinger equation is solved on the S^3 manifold to obtain eigenstates labeled by energy, spin, and isospin quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- The Hamiltonian energy eigenvalues are given by E_l = (1/8 lambda) l(l+2) + M, where l is the degree of the polynomial wavefunction
- The moment of inertia parameter is lambda = (8 pi)/(3 g^3 f_pi) * Lambda, where Lambda = integral of dimensionless radial functions involving the skyrmion profile F(r)
- The Laplacian on S^3 satisfies -nabla^2 (a_b + i a_c)^l = l(l+2) (a_b + i a_c)^l for integral power l
- The Hamiltonian can be expressed as H = (1/4 lambda) (J^2 + I^2) + M = (1/2 lambda) J^2 + M = (1/2 lambda) I^2 + M

## Context

This work extends the collective coordinate quantization approach of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) by applying the rigorous Dirac constraint quantization procedure to both SU(2) and SO(3) collective coordinate manifolds. The paper prepares for constructing coherent states of baryons directly from the Skyrme model, which will be done in a subsequent paper, and for applications to skyrmion formation through disoriented chiral condensates in heavy ion collisions, building on earlier work by Kapusta and collaborators. The approach differs from previous attempts to identify skyrmions with baryon superpositions, such as those using the linear sigma model by Birse and Banerjee (1984) or the U(4) group theoretic coherent state method by Aitchison and collaborators.
