# $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ Pairing in Dense Neutron Matter: The Spectrum of Solutions

> The $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ pairing model is generally considered to provide an adequate description of the superfluid states of neutron matter at densities some 2-3 times that of saturated symmetrical nuclear matter. The problem of solving the system of BCS gap equations expressing the $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ model is attacked with the aid of the separation approach. This method, developed originally for quantitative study of S-wave pairing in the presence of strong short-range repulsions, serves effectively to reduce the coupled, singular, nonlinear BCS integral equations to a set of coupled algebraic equations. For the first time, sufficient precision becomes accessible to resolve small energy splittings between the different pairing states. Adopting a perturbative strategy, we are able to identify and characterize the full repertoire of real solutions of the $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ pairing model, in the limiting regime of small tensor-coupling strength. The P-F channel coupling is seen to lift the striking parametric degeneracies revealed by a earlier separation treatment of the pure, uncoupled $^3P_2$ pairing problem. Remarkably, incisive and robust results are obtained solely on the basis of analytic arguments. Unlike the traditional Ginzburg-Landau approach, the analysis is not restricted to the immediate vicinity of the critical temperature, but is equally reliable at zero temperature. Interesting connections and contrasts are drawn between triplet pairing in dense neutron matter and triplet pairing in liquid $^3$He.

## Key claim

The paper identifies and characterizes the full set of real solutions of the coupled \(^3\)P\(_2\)-\(^3\)F\(_2\) BCS gap equations in dense neutron matter, but only in the limiting regime of small tensor-coupling strength \(\eta\) and under the assumption that the central components of the in-vacuum \(nn\) interaction nearly compensate each other, a feature the authors assume is maintained by the effective interaction within neutron matter.

## Method

The authors apply the separation approach, originally developed for S-wave pairing with strong short-range repulsions, to reduce the coupled, singular, nonlinear BCS integral equations for the \(^3\)P\(_2\)-\(^3\)F\(_2\) pairing model to a set of coupled algebraic equations. They adopt a perturbative strategy treating the tensor-coupling parameter \(\eta = -\langle p_F| V_{13}^2 |p_F \rangle / v_F\) as small, and solve the resulting algebraic system analytically to find all real solutions for the gap amplitude ratios \(\lambda_1\) and \(\lambda_2\). The analysis is performed at zero temperature but is stated to be equally reliable at any temperature.

## Key numbers

* The tensor-coupling parameter \(\eta = -\langle p_F| V_{13}^2 |p_F \rangle / v_F\) is the small parameter used in the perturbative treatment; the ratio of tensor to spin-orbit force strength varies around 0.3 in the density interval \(\rho_0 < \rho < 3\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is saturation density of symmetrical nuclear matter).
* The pure \(^3\)P\(_2\) pairing problem exhibits parametric degeneracy along curves \(\lambda_1(\lambda_2)\) in the \((\lambda_1,\lambda_2)\) plane; the \(^3\)P\(_2\)-\(^3\)F\(_2\) coupling reduces this to isolated points, including the surviving solution \(\lambda_2 = -1\) at any \(\lambda_1\).
* The discrete set of two-component solutions for the coupled problem includes \(\lambda_1 = 0\) with \(\lambda_2 = \pm 1, \pm 3\).
* The gap function \(D_0^2(\mathbf{n})\) for the pure \(^3\)P\(_2\) problem is given by an expression involving \(\Delta_F^2\), \(\lambda_1\), and \(\lambda_2\) (Eq.~(\ref{do})), with \(\Delta_F\) defined as the angle-averaged gap measure.

## Context

This work extends the earlier separation-method treatment of the pure \(^3\)P\(_2\) pairing problem by Zverev, Clark, and Khodel (2002) and by Khodel, Khodel, and Clark (1998), which revealed striking parametric degeneracies in the uncoupled case. The present paper shows that coupling to the \(^3\)F\(_2\) channel lifts those degeneracies, yielding a discrete spectrum of real solutions. The analysis is contrasted with the traditional Ginzburg-Landau approach, which is restricted to the vicinity of the critical temperature and thus has little relevance to neutron stars, which cool to temperatures far below \(T_c\). The results enable future determination of the relative stability of different pairing states and construction of the superfluid phase diagram of neutron matter, and the authors draw connections and contrasts with triplet pairing in liquid \(^3\)He as studied by Vollhardt and Wolfle (1990).
