# Thermodynamic approach to proton number fluctuations in baryon-rich heavy-ion matter created at moderate collision energies

> We develop a framework to relate proton number cumulants measured in heavy-ion collisions within a momentum space acceptance to the susceptibilities of baryon number, assuming that particles are emitted from a fireball with uniform distribution of temperature and baryochemical potential, superimposed on a hydrodynamic flow velocity profile. The rapidity acceptance dependence of proton cumulants measured by the HADES Collaboration in $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.4$ GeV Au-Au appears to be consistent with thermal emission of nucleons from a grand-canonical heat bath, with the extracted baryon number susceptibilities exhibiting an hierarchy $χ_4^B \gg -χ_3^B \gg χ_2^B \gg χ_1^B$. Naively, this could indicate large non-Gaussian fluctuations that might point to the presence of the QCD critical point close to the chemical freeze-out at $T \sim 70$ MeV and $μ_B \sim 850-900$ MeV. However, the description of the experimental data at large rapidity acceptances becomes challenging once the effect of exact baryon number conservation is incorporated, suggesting that more theoretical and experimental studies are needed to reach a firm conclusion.

# Thermodynamic approach to proton number fluctuations in baryon-rich heavy-ion matter created at moderate collision energies

## Key claim

The rapidity acceptance dependence of proton cumulants measured by HADES in Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 GeV is consistent with thermal emission from a grand-canonical heat bath, and the extracted baryon number susceptibilities exhibit a hierarchy that could naively indicate the presence of the QCD critical point near chemical freeze-out at T ~ 70 MeV and mu_B ~ 850-900 MeV; however, the description of data at large rapidity acceptances becomes challenging once exact baryon number conservation is incorporated, so more theoretical and experimental studies are needed for a firm conclusion.

## Method

The authors develop a framework relating proton number cumulants measured within a momentum-space acceptance to grand-canonical baryon number susceptibilities, assuming emission from a fireball with uniform temperature and baryochemical potential superimposed on a spherically-symmetric Hubble-like flow velocity profile (Siemens-Rasmussen blast-wave model). The Cooper-Frye formalism is generalized to describe cumulants of multiplicity distributions, and the effect of the experimental acceptance is modeled by convoluting the cumulants from each hypersurface element with binomial distributions. The extracted susceptibilities are then used to compute proton cumulant ratios as a function of rapidity acceptance, and the effect of exact baryon number conservation is incorporated via the subensemble acceptance method (SAM-1.0 and SAM-2.0).

## Key numbers

* Extracted baryon number susceptibility ratios from HADES data at y_cut = 0.1-0.2: chi_2^B/chi_1^B ~ 9.17 +/- 0.21, chi_3^B/chi_2^B ~ -33.1 +/- 0.8, chi_4^B/chi_2^B ~ 691 +/- 50
* Chemical freeze-out temperature T ~ 70 MeV and baryochemical potential mu_B ~ 750-800 MeV (from statistical hadronization model fits by Harabasz (2020) and Motornenko (2021))
* Estimated isothermal speed of sound: c_T^2 ~ (9.8 +/- 0.2) x 10^{-3}
* Logarithmic derivative of speed of sound with respect to baryon density: d(ln c_T^2)/d(ln n_B) ~ 4.6 +/- 0.1
* Fireball parameters: R = 6.1 fm, H = 0.097 fm^{-1}
* Fraction of measured protons from all baryons: q ~ 0.25 (from isospin factor q^iso ~ 0.4 and nucleon binding fraction q^nucl ~ 0.375)
* Fraction alpha = <N_p^acc>/<N_B^tot> varies from ~0.03 (y_cut = 0.1) to ~0.13 (y_cut = 0.5)

## Context

This work extends the generalized Cooper-Frye formalism for cumulants developed by Vovchenko (2021) to low collision energies, specifically the HADES energy regime at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 GeV. The analysis builds on the HADES Collaboration's measurements of proton number cumulants (HADES, 2020) and on blast-wave fits to hadron spectra by Harabasz (2020). The extracted susceptibilities are compared to lattice QCD results (HotQCD, 2018; Borsanyi, 2021) which are limited to mu_B/T less than 2-3.5, and to holographic QCD model predictions (Critelli, 2017; Grefa, 2021). The results also improve upon the simplified estimates of the speed of sound from cumulants by Sorensen (2021). The work identifies a conundrum: the data at large rapidity acceptances are better described without baryon conservation than with it, contrasting with the behavior seen at higher energies (sqrt_s_NN >= 20 GeV) where baryon conservation drives the acceptance dependence (Vovchenko, 2021).
