# Mach cones in heavy ion collisions

> We study the fate of the energy deposited by a jet in a heavy ion collision assuming that the medium created is opaque (jets quickly lose energy) and its viscosity is so low that the energy lost by the jet is quickly thermalized. The expectation is that under these conditions the energy deposited gives rise to a Mach cone. We argue that, in general, the behavior of the system is different from the naive expectation and it depends strongly on the assumptions made about the energy and momentum deposited by the jet into the medium. We compare our phenomenological hydrodynamic calculations performed in a static medium for a variety of energy-momentum sources (including a pQCD-based calculation) with the exact strong coupling limit obtained within the AdS/CFT correspondence. We also discuss the observability of hydrodynamical features triggered by jets in experimentally measured two-particle correlations at RHIC.

# Mach cones in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors argue that the appearance of a Mach cone signal in heavy ion collisions is not guaranteed even under the assumption of an opaque, low-viscosity medium where jet energy is quickly thermalized; the observable signal depends crucially on how energy and momentum are deposited by the jet into the medium, and in many scenarios the strongest signals come from non-conical contributions such as the diffusion wake rather than from the Mach cone itself.

## Method

The authors perform three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the SHASTA code in a static thermal background medium, comparing a variety of energy-momentum deposition schemes including a pQCD-based calculation. They also analyze the energy-momentum tensor of a heavy quark moving through a strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma at finite temperature using the AdS/CFT correspondence, computing metric fluctuations due to a string hanging from the boundary of an AdS-Schwarzschild background. The freeze-out is modeled via the Cooper-Frye formula to obtain final particle distributions.

## Key numbers

- The sound wave attenuation length is given by Gamma = (4/3) * eta / (epsilon + p), relating shear viscosity to energy density and pressure
- The Mach cone angle satisfies cos(phi_M) = c_s / v, where for an ideal gas c_s = 1/sqrt(3)
- In the AdS/CFT calculation, the energy-momentum tensor perturbation scales as Delta T ~ alpha * gamma * sqrt(lambda) * T^2 / (distance)^3, where alpha = gamma * sqrt(lambda) * T^2
- For phenomenologically relevant parameters N_c = 3, lambda = 3*pi (alpha_s = 0.25), and gamma = 10, the parameter xi > 30, setting a minimum distance of 5/(pi * T) from the quark for the linearized approximation to hold

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies of Mach cones in heavy ion collisions (Stoecker, 2005; Casalderrey-Solana, 2005; Shuryak, 2005) and addresses the debate surrounding experimental observations at RHIC of conical structures in two-particle correlations (PHENIX, STAR collaborations). The paper compares phenomenological hydrodynamic calculations with the exact strong coupling limit from AdS/CFT (Friess, 2006; Yarom, 2007; Chesler, 2007; Gubser, 2007) and shows that the cone-plus-diffusion-wake picture is remarkably independent of the energy-deposition scenario. The results enable future studies using three-particle correlations (Abelev, 2008) and dependence of the Mach cone angle on various variables (Winter, 2008; Sickles, 2008) to distinguish between competing interpretations.
