# Gauge invariance in two-particle scattering

> It is shown how gauge invariance is obtained for the coupling of a photon to a two-body state described by the solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. This is illustrated both for a complex scalar field theory and for interaction kernels derived from chiral effective Lagrangians.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that gauge invariance in the coupling of a photon to a two-body state described by the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) is achieved only when the photon is attached at all possible places in the bubble chain (external legs, intermediate propagators, and interaction vertices), not merely to the external legs as is often done in chiral unitary approaches.

## Method

The authors first illustrate the gauge invariance procedure using a complex scalar field theory with a contact interaction, showing that the Ward-Takahashi identities for the propagators together with the BSE structure guarantee current conservation. They then extend the proof to meson-baryon scattering within chiral effective field theory, using the Weinberg-Tomozawa term as the leading-order interaction kernel and generalizing to higher-order contact interactions of the form \(\bar{B} C_{\mu_1\cdots\mu_n} (D^{\mu_1}\ldots D^{\mu_l} \tilde{\phi})(D^{\mu_{l+1}}\ldots D^{\mu_m} \phi^\dagger)(D^{\mu_{m+1}}\ldots D^{\mu_n} \tilde{B})\). The proof relies on the Ward-Takahashi identities for the propagators and a derived relation for the interaction kernel, without requiring explicit evaluation of the BSE or specification of the regularization scheme.

## Key numbers

* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda_\chi \simeq 4\pi F_\pi \approx 1.2\) GeV, above which the chiral series is expected to break down.
* The scalar loop integral \(G\) satisfies \(\Im T^{-1} = -\Im G = |\boldsymbol{q}_{cm}|/(8\pi\sqrt{s})\,\theta(s-(m+M)^2)\) from unitarity of the amplitude \(T\).
* For the scalar field theory, the photon momentum satisfies \(k^2 < 4\,\text{min}(m^2,M^2)\) for physical and virtual photons, ensuring that cuts through propagators directly connected to the photon do not yield imaginary values.

## Context

This work extends the "gauging of equations" method of Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1999) to coupled channels and to interaction kernels derived from chiral effective Lagrangians, addressing a gap in chiral unitary approaches such as those of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and Oset and Ramos (1998), which did not discuss gauge invariance. The results enable consistent inclusion of electromagnetic processes (e.g., photoproduction) in non-perturbative chiral frameworks that dynamically generate resonances like the \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(S_{11}(1535)\), and are applicable to radiative decays such as \(\phi,\rho\to\gamma\) and \(\eta,\eta'\to\gamma\gamma\).
