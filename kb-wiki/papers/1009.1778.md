# Light and heavy at the end of the funnel

> We show that, by taking a bare mass spectrum with constant spacings for the quark-antiquark propagators, which is subject to considerable mass shifts from meson loops, one adequately describes a large variety of mesonic resonances, from the light scalars to the b-bbar states. All our results indicate that a harmonic-oscilator spectrum with universal frequency, in combination with coupled-channel effects, does a much better job than the q-barq spectrum of the funnel potential.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a harmonic-oscillator (HO) confining potential with a universal frequency, combined with mass shifts from meson loops (coupled-channel effects), describes a wide range of mesonic resonances from light scalars to bottomonium much better than the standard funnel-type (Coulomb-plus-linear) potential, but this conclusion depends on interpreting the available experimental data as showing larger S-D splittings than funnel-potential models predict.

## Method

The authors compare the predictions of their "HORSE" model (HO confinement plus meson loops, using three-meson vertices from ZPC21p291) against the funnel-potential model of PRD72p054026 for the JPC=1-- charmonium spectrum. They analyze published experimental data on e+e- annihilation into open-charm channels, identifying resonance candidates by their pattern of mass shifts and threshold enhancements rather than assuming a smooth background.

## Key numbers

- HO radial level splitting of 380 MeV for all flavor combinations, with effective quark masses determining absolute positions (e.g., mc=1.562 GeV, ω=0.190 GeV for charmonium)
- For vector charmonium, the dominantly S-wave combination shifts 100–200 MeV downward from its bare HO level due to meson loops, while the dominantly D-wave combination shifts at most a few tens of MeV
- The S-D splitting for higher vector charmonium states (e.g., 6D-7S) is estimated from data to be roughly five to ten times larger than the ~20 MeV predicted by the funnel-potential model of PRD72p054026
- The ρ(2S) central mass is predicted at about 1.27 GeV, consistent with observations of a ρ-type resonance at 1.25–1.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' long-standing program (van Beveren and Rupp, PRD21p772, PRD27p1527) of using HO confinement derived from anti-De Sitter (AdS) conformal invariance, in contrast to the Cornell potential (PRL34p369) and its semirelativistic variants (PRD32p189) that have dominated quarkonium spectroscopy for three decades. The paper directly contradicts the funnel-potential predictions of PRD72p054026 for charmonium spin-orbit splittings, and argues that many states (light scalars, Ds0(2317), Ds1(2460)) previously assigned to tetraquarks, molecules, hybrids, or glueballs are instead dynamically generated resonances from meson loops coupled to HO confinement. The analysis enables a systematic classification of higher vector charmonium states (ψ(5S), ψ(4D), ψ(6S), ψ(5D), and beyond) from existing e+e- data, and predicts that the light scalar mesons f0(600), K0(800), f0(980), and a0(980) are dynamically generated rather than conventional qqbar states.
