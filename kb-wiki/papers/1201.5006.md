# High-pt triggered dihadron correlations with vn background subtraction by STAR

> STAR measurements of dihadron correlations with high-pt trigger partcles in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV are presented with subtraction of v2, v3, and v4 backgrounds. The vn azimuthal anisotropies were measured by the two-particle cumulant method with η gap to reduce nonflow. The dihadron correlations relative to the v2 as well as the v3 harmonic planes are also presented. Implications of the results are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that after subtracting not only \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) but also \(v_3\) backgrounds from high-\(p_T\) triggered dihadron correlations in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, a near-side ridge signal may still remain in non-peripheral collisions, and the away-side double-peak structure becomes weaker than previously reported; however, the author explicitly states that the talk does not provide a complete answer to how much nonflow contaminates the subtracted \(v_n\) backgrounds.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) azimuthal anisotropies using the two-particle cumulant method with an \(\eta\) gap (\(|\Delta\eta|>1\)) to reduce nonflow contributions. Dihadron correlations with high-\(p_T\) trigger particles (3–6 GeV/\(c\)) and associated particles (2–3 GeV/\(c\)) were then constructed, and the measured \(v_n\) backgrounds were subtracted. The analysis was also performed relative to the \(v_2\) harmonic event plane and the \(v_3\) harmonic plane, with additional subtraction of the uncorrelated portions of \(v_3\) and \(v_4\).

## Key numbers

- \(v_2\{2\}\) and \(v_3\{2\}\) were measured in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV for centrality bins from 80% to top 5%, with \(p_T\) ranges 0–2 GeV/\(c\), 1–2 GeV/\(c\), and 3–4 GeV/\(c\).
- At large \(p_T\) in central collisions, \(v_3\) is comparable to \(v_2\).
- Dihadron correlations used trigger \(p_T^{\rm trig}=3\)–6 GeV/\(c\) and associated \(p_T^{\rm assoc}=2\)–3 GeV/\(c\), with \(|\Delta\eta|>1\).
- For event-plane dependent correlations, trigger \(p_T^{\rm trig}=3\)–4 GeV/\(c\) and associated \(p_T^{\rm assoc}=1\)–2 GeV/\(c\) (or 1–1.5 GeV/\(c\)) were used, with \(|\Delta\eta|>0.7\).
- The \(v_4\) correlated to \(\psi_2\) was parameterized as \(v_4\{\psi_2\}=1.15 v_2\{2\}^2\).

## Context

This work extends prior STAR measurements of dihadron correlations that subtracted only \(v_2\) and \(v_4\) backgrounds, assuming vanishing triangular flow (Alver 2007; ALICE Collaboration 2011; Sorensen 2011). The inclusion of \(v_3\) subtraction addresses recent model studies showing that odd harmonics do not vanish due to initial geometry fluctuations. The results enable further investigation of the near-side long-range pseudorapidity ridge and the away-side conical emission, while highlighting the need to separate flow from nonflow contributions in \(v_n\) measurements.
