# From meson- and photon-nucleon scattering to vector mesons in nuclear matter

> We present a relativistic and unitary approach to pion- and photon-nucleon scattering taking into account the $πN$, $ρN$, $ωN$, $ηN$, $πΔ$, $K Λ$ and $K Σ$ channels. Our scheme dynamically generates the s- and d-wave nucleon resonances N(1535), N(1650), N(1520) and N(1700) and isobar resonances $Δ(1620)$ and $Δ(1700)$ in terms of quasi-local two-body interaction terms. We obtain a fair description of the experimental data relevant for slow vector-meson propagation in nuclear matter. The s-wave $ρ$- and $ω$-meson nucleon scattering amplitudes, which define the leading density modification of the $ρ$- and $ω$-meson spectral functions in nuclear matter, are predicted.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the s-wave scattering amplitudes for ρ- and ω-mesons off nucleons, which define the leading density modification of the vector-meson spectral functions in nuclear matter, but the authors note that the results are only qualitatively correct at normal nuclear matter density and may change when higher-order terms in the density expansion are included.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic, unitary coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach for meson-baryon scattering, including the πN, ρN, ωN, ηN, πΔ, KΛ, and KΣ channels. They constrained the model parameters by fitting 1156 data points from pion-nucleon phase shifts and pion- and photon-induced production cross sections in the energy window 1.4 GeV < √s < 1.8 GeV, using energy-independent coupling matrices. The resulting vector-meson nucleon scattering amplitudes were then applied via the low-density theorem to compute the in-medium ρ and ω propagators at nuclear saturation density ρ₀ and 2ρ₀.

## Key numbers

- The analysis includes N = 1156 data points from pion- and photon-induced reactions.
- The energy window for the fit is 1.4 GeV ≤ √s ≤ 1.8 GeV.
- The s-wave ωN scattering lengths are a_ωN^(1/2) = (-0.45 + i 0.31) fm and a_ωN^(3/2) = (-0.43 + i 0.15) fm.
- At nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the center-of-gravity of the ρ-meson spectral function is shifted down in energy by about 3%.
- At ρ₀, the center-of-gravity of the ω-meson spectral function is shifted down by about 4%.
- The low-lying modes of the ω-meson carry about 15% of the energy-weighted sum rule.
- The subleading correction term (order k_F⁴) for the ω meson implies a further repulsive mass shift of 1 MeV and an increase of the decay width of 2 MeV at nuclear saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the longstanding and controversial discussion about vector meson properties in dense nuclear matter (Sakurai; Brown and Rho; Hatsuda, Fukushima, and Nemoto; Wambach) by using constraints from pion- and photon-nucleon scattering data to determine the vector-meson nucleon scattering amplitudes, which were previously not directly constrained by data. The results are relevant for the experimental program at GSI (HADES detector) and complementary programs at MAMI and KEK, and the authors suggest that a self-consistent calculation (Lutz and Korpa) would be needed to improve quantitative accuracy.
