# Relativistic model for nuclear matter and atomic nuclei with momentum-dependent self-energies

> The Lagrangian density of standard relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with density-dependent meson-nucleon coupling vertices is modified by introducing couplings of the meson fields to derivative nucleon densities. As a consequence, the nucleon self energies, that describe the effective in-medium interaction, become momentum dependent. In this approach it is possible to increase the effective (Landau) mass of the nucleons, that is related to the density of states at the Fermi energy, as compared to conventional relativistic models. At the same time the relativistic effective (Dirac) mass is kept small in order to obtain a realistic strength of the spin-orbit interaction. Additionally, the empirical Schroedinger-equivalent central optical potential from Dirac phenomenology is reasonably well described. A parametrization of the model is obtained by a fit to properties of doubly magic atomic nuclei. Results for symmetric nuclear matter, neutron matter and finite nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a relativistic mean-field model with both density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings and derivative couplings (D³C model) that generates momentum-dependent nucleon self-energies, allowing the effective Landau mass to be increased while keeping the Dirac mass small for realistic spin-orbit interaction, a combination not achievable in standard RMF models.

## Method

The Lagrangian density of standard RMF models is modified by adding couplings of meson fields to derivative nucleon densities via the quantities \(Y^{\mu\nu}\) and \(Z^{\mu}\), which depend on the isoscalar \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) meson fields. The resulting Dirac equation contains vector and scalar self-energies that are differential operators acting on the nucleon field, producing an explicit energy dependence. The coupling functions \(\Gamma_{\sigma}, \Gamma_{\omega}, \Gamma_{\delta}, \Gamma_{\rho}, \Gamma_{V}, \Gamma_{S}\) are taken to depend on the vector density, and a parametrization is obtained by fitting to properties of doubly magic atomic nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The effective Landau mass in conventional RMF models is approximately \(0.62 m\) at the Fermi momentum, compared to a value of \(0.78 m\) extracted from non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations for giant resonances.
- The relativistic effective (Dirac) mass in usual RMF models is \(m^{\ast} \approx 0.55 m\).
- The Schrödinger-equivalent central optical potential from Dirac phenomenology saturates at high energies around 50 MeV.
- The model is fitted to properties of doubly magic atomic nuclei; specific numerical results for nuclear matter and finite nuclei are discussed in the paper but not given in the provided text.

## Context

This work extends the derivative coupling (DC) model of Typel (2003) by combining it with density-dependent couplings, addressing deficiencies where the DC model had different density dependence for scalar and vector self-energies and proved difficult to fit to finite nuclei. The approach is motivated by Dirac-Brueckner calculations (e.g., Sehn 1997, de Jong 1998, Gross-Boelting 1999, Hofmann 2001, Dalen 2004) which show energy and momentum dependence of self-energies, and by Dirac phenomenology for elastic proton-nucleus scattering (Hama 1990, Cooper 1993). The model enables improved description of the level density at the Fermi energy and extrapolation to high densities relevant for heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars.
