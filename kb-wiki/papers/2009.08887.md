# Isobaric analog state energy in deformed nuclei: a toy model

> A formula to evaluate the effects of a general deformation on the Coulomb direct contribution to the energy of the Isobaric Analog State (IAS) is presented and studied via a simple yet physical model. The toy model gives a reasonable account of microscopic deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations in a test case, and provides a guidance when predicting unknown IAS energies. Thus, deformed HFB calculations, to predict the IAS energies, are performed for several neutron-deficient medium-mass and heavy nuclei which are now planned to be studied experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general formula showing that deformation reduces the Coulomb direct contribution to the Isobaric Analog State (IAS) energy, with the effect scaling approximately as \(-\beta_2^2/4\pi\) for quadrupole deformation; however, the authors emphasize that this is a toy model and the deformation effect is expected to be small, at most a few percent, and the formula assumes neutron and proton density distributions are deformed in a similar way.

## Method

The authors derive an analytic expression for the deformation dependence of the Coulomb direct contribution to the IAS energy by performing a Fourier transform of the density distributions and expanding for small differences between neutron and proton deformations. They test this formula against constrained deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations using the SAMi interaction for the test nucleus \({}^{120}\)Sn, and then compare a simplified "toy model" (sharp sphere approximation with uniform densities) against full HFB results along the Sn isotopic chain. Finally, they perform deformed HFB calculations for 22 neutron-deficient medium-mass and heavy nuclei planned for experimental study.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{120}\)Sn, constrained HFB calculations from \(\beta_2=0\) to \(\beta_2=0.5\) show that the effective quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2^{\rm eff}\) from Eq. (29a) does not deviate substantially from the mass deformation \(\beta_{2m}\), validating the model.
* The toy model predicts a relative reduction of \(E_{\rm IAS}\) of about 5% for very deformed nuclei with \(\beta_2\approx 0.8\).
* For the Sn isotopic chain, the toy model deviation from self-consistent HFB calculations is about 5% for \({}^{102}\)Sn and decreases smoothly to about 1% for \({}^{130}\)Sn.
* Isospin mixing probabilities \(\varepsilon^2\) range from 2.363% in \({}^{102}\)Sn (with pairing) to 0.400% in \({}^{122}\)Sn, producing IAS energy corrections from \(-1.719\) MeV to \(-0.149\) MeV respectively.
* For \({}^{162}\)Er, the HFB deformation effect on IAS energy \(\Delta E_{\rm IAS}^{\rm HFB}\) is \(-0.112\) MeV, while Eq. (28) gives \(-0.111\) MeV and Eq. (29) gives \(-0.181\) MeV.
* For \({}^{168}\)Yb, \(\Delta E_{\rm IAS}^{\rm HFB}\) is \(-0.212\) MeV, with Eq. (28) predicting \(-0.156\) MeV and Eq. (29) predicting \(-0.223\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of IAS energies beyond the spherical nuclei (\({}^{48}\)Ca, \({}^{90}\)Zr, \({}^{208}\)Pb) that have been the main focus of previous theoretical work, following the few exceptions of Skyrme calculations by Yoshida and the Madrid group (Alvarez-Rodriguez et al., 2005; Sarriguren, 2011). The derived formula provides guidance for predicting IAS energies in deformed neutron-deficient medium-mass and heavy nuclei planned for experimental study at RCNP, Osaka within the LUNESTAR project, and enables systematic evaluation of deformation effects on the Coulomb displacement energy that were previously missing in a transparent analytic form.
