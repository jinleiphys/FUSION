# In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy of 35Mg and 33Na

> Excited states in the very neutron-rich nuclei 35Mg and 33Na were populated in the fragmentation of a 38Si projectile beam on a Be target at 83 MeV/u beam energy. We report on the first observation of gamma-ray transitions in 35Mg, the odd-N neighbor of 34Mg and 36Mg, which are known to be part of the "Island of Inversion" around N = 20. The results are discussed in the framework of large- scale shell-model calculations. For the A = 3Z nucleus 33Na, a new gamma-ray transition was observed that is suggested to complete the gamma-ray cascade 7/2+ --> 5/2+ --> 3/2+ gs connecting three neutron 2p-2h intruder states that are predicted to form a close-to-ideal K = 3/2 rotational band in the strong-coupling limit.

# In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy of 35Mg and 33Na

## Key claim
The first observation of gamma-ray transitions in 35Mg and a new transition in 33Na are reported, with the interpretation that all low-lying states in both nuclei are intruder configurations placing them inside the "Island of Inversion" around N=20. However, the assignment of the 446 keV transition in 35Mg to a specific decay path remains ambiguous because the predicted near-degenerate 5/2- ground state and 3/2- first excited state at 30 keV separation could not be resolved due to the detector energy threshold of approximately 80 keV.

## Method
Excited states in 35Mg and 33Na were populated via fragmentation of a 38Si projectile beam at 83 MeV/u mid-target energy on a 376(4) mg/cm2 thick 9Be target at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. Reaction residues were identified using the S800 spectrograph with time-of-flight and energy-loss measurements, while gamma rays were detected with the SeGA array of 32-fold segmented high-purity germanium detectors and Doppler-reconstructed event-by-event. The results were compared to Monte Carlo Shell-Model calculations using the SDPF-M effective interaction and to conventional shell-model calculations with the SDPF-U effective interaction.

## Key numbers
- 35Mg: gamma-ray transition observed at 446 keV, with possible indications of transitions at 621 keV and 670 keV
- 35Mg: neutron separation energy reported as Sn = 1020(200) keV from Jur07 and estimated as Sn = 730(460) keV from Audi et al. (2003)
- 33Na: gamma-ray transitions measured at 429(5) keV and 688(6) keV
- 33Na: MCSM predicts first excited 5/2+ state at 390 keV and second excited 7/2+ state at Ex = 1.16 MeV
- 33Na: production cross section from 38Si fragmentation is sigma(33Na) greater than or approximately 0.03 mb
- 35Mg: production cross section from 38Si fragmentation is sigma(35Mg) greater than or approximately 0.05 mb
- 33Na: energy ratio E(7/2+)-E(3/2+) divided by E(5/2+)-E(3/2+) measured as 2.6, compared to ideal K=3/2 rotational band value of 2.4
- 33Na: MCSM calculated B(E2; 7/2+ to 5/2+) = 99.75 e2fm4 and B(M1; 7/2+ to 5/2+) = 0.59 mu_N2
- 33Na: intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0 approximately 70 fm2 extracted from MCSM calculations assuming K=3/2 rotational band
- 34Mg (N=22 isotone): MCSM calculates B(E2; 0+ to 2+1) = 552 e2fm4 and Q0 = 75 fm2
- 32Mg (N=20): MCSM calculates B(E2; 0+ to 2+1) = 447 e2fm4 and |Q0| = 67 fm2

## Context
This work extends studies of the "Island of Inversion" region around N=20, where neutron-rich Ne, Na, and Mg isotopes exhibit breakdown of the N=20 magic number due to neutron intruder configurations. Prior work on even-even nuclei such as 32Ne and 36Mg by Mot95, Pri99, Iwa01, Yan03, Chu05, Nie05, Det79, Yon01, Gad07, and Doo09 established collective properties well described by Monte Carlo Shell-Model calculations. However, odd-A neighbors including 31,33Mg (Num01, Pri02, Ney05, Yor07, Tri08, Mil09, Yor10, Tri10) and 30Na (Ett08) posed challenges for these calculations. The results for 33Na at N=22 show closer agreement with an ideal K=3/2 rotational band than previously observed in 31Na at N=20 by Doo10. Future Coulomb excitation or excited-state lifetime measurements are needed to confirm the degree of deformation and rotational character.
