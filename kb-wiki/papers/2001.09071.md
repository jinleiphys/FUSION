# Nuclear effects on jet substructure observables at the LHC

> Using a pQCD picture for jet evolution in a dense QCD medium, in which medium-induced parton branchings are factorized from vacuum-like emissions, we study two jet substructure observables: the $z_g$ and the Soft Drop multiplicity distributions. We compute the respective nuclear modification factors using a Monte-Carlo implementation of the parton showers. Our results are in qualitative agreement with LHC data for Pb+Pb collisions. We identify the physical mechanisms explaining our results: incoherent jet energy loss, semi-hard medium-induced emissions and the bias introduced by the steeply falling jet spectrum.

## Key claim

The authors show that nuclear modification factors for the $z_g$ and Soft Drop multiplicity distributions in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC can be qualitatively reproduced using a pQCD picture where medium-induced parton branchings are factorized from vacuum-like emissions, but caution that the steeply falling jet spectrum strongly suppresses the enhancement from intrajet medium-induced emissions, particularly for the $\nSD$ distribution with $\beta=0$.

## Method

The authors implement a Monte-Carlo parton shower that factorizes vacuum-like emissions (VLEs) from medium-induced emissions (MIEs) in a dense QCD medium, incorporating a vetoed region for VLEs in phase space and possible violation of angular ordering via colour decoherence. They compute the $z_g$ and Soft Drop multiplicity ($\nSD$) distributions for jets in Pb+Pb collisions, first for a monochromatic gluon with $p_{T0}=200$ GeV and then using a Born-level dijet spectrum, and compare the medium/vacuum ratios to identify the dominant physical mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- The coherence angle $\theta_c = 2/\sqrt{\hat{q} L^3} \simeq 0.04$ marks the transition where the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ changes drastically between jets with large $\theta_g$ and small $\theta_g$.
- For the monochromatic case with $\beta=0$, $\zcut=0.1$, $\thetacut=0.1$, the average number of SD splittings from primary MIEs follows a Poisson distribution with mean $2\alpha_s C_R (A_0 + A_m)/\pi$, where $A_m \sim (\zcut p_{T0}/\omega_c)^{-1/2}$ when $\zcut p_{T0} \ll \omega_c$.
- The energy scale $\omega_c \equiv \hat{q} L^2/2$ is the largest energy of a medium-induced emission, and the condition $\zcut p_{T0} \thetacut \gg Q_s$ (with $Q_s \equiv \sqrt{\hat{q} L}$) makes substructure observables insensitive to intrajet MIEs.

## Context

This work extends the pQCD factorization picture for jet fragmentation in dense QCD media developed by Caucal, Iancu, and Soyez (2018, 2019), and provides a theoretical framework to interpret LHC measurements of jet substructure in Pb+Pb collisions by CMS (Sirunyan 2017) and ALICE (Acharya 2019). The results enable quantitative predictions for the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ binned in $\theta_g$ or $z_g$, which could discriminate between different mechanisms of jet quenching, and highlight that the $\nSD$ distribution with $\beta=0$ is not a clean probe of intrajet MIEs due to strong suppression from the steeply falling jet spectrum.
