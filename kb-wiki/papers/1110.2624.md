# Quantization on Space-Time Hyperboloids

> We quantize a relativistic massive complex spin-0 field and a relativistic massive spin-1/2 field on a space-time hyperboloid. We call this procedure point-form canonical quantization. Lorentz invariance of the hyperboloid implies that the 4 generators for translations become dynamic and interaction dependent, whereas the 6 generators for Lorentz transformations remain kinematic and interaction free. We expand the fields in terms of usual plane waves and prove the equivalence to equal-time quantization by representing the Poincare generators in a momentum basis. We formulate a generalized scattering theory for interacting fields by considering evolution of the system generated by the interaction dependent four-momentum operator. Finally we expand our generalized scattering operator in powers of the interaction and show its equivalence to the Dyson expansion of usual time-ordered perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quantizing a relativistic massive complex spin-0 field and a massive spin-1/2 field on a space-time hyperboloid (point-form canonical quantization) is equivalent to standard equal-time quantization, provided one expands the fields in usual plane waves and uses the Wigner representation of the Poincaré group rather than hyperbolic coordinates.

## Method

The author imposes Lorentz-invariant canonical (anti)commutation relations on the forward hyperboloid \(x_\lambda x^\lambda = \tau^2\) for free complex scalar and Dirac spinor fields, expanding the fields in terms of ordinary plane-wave creation and annihilation operators. The Poincaré generators are represented in the Wigner momentum basis, where the four-momentum operator becomes diagonal, and the equivalence to equal-time quantization is proven by comparing the generator representations. A generalized scattering theory is then formulated using the interaction-dependent four-momentum operator as the generator of evolution, and the scattering operator is expanded in powers of the interaction to show its equivalence to the Dyson expansion of time-ordered perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The hyperboloid is defined by \(x_\lambda x^\lambda = \tau^2\) with \(\tau\) arbitrary but fixed.
- The Poincaré group has 10 generators: 4 translation generators \(P^\mu\) and 6 Lorentz generators \(M^{\mu\nu}\).
- The mass-shell condition for a free particle is \(p_\lambda p^\lambda = m^2\).
- The action for a free relativistic particle is \(S = -m \int_{s_1}^{s_2} ds\) for timelike separated points with \((y_1 - y_2)_\lambda (y_1 - y_2)^\lambda > 0\).

## Context

This work extends Dirac's (1949) classification of forms of relativistic dynamics, instant, front, and point form, by providing a concrete quantum field theory implementation of the point form. It addresses limitations of earlier point-form approaches by Fubini (1972), Sommerfield (1974), Gromes (1974), and DiSessa (1974), which used hyperbolic coordinates and Hankel functions, making them cumbersome for massive theories and scattering descriptions. The paper enables a manifestly covariant formulation of scattering in point-form quantum field theory, which can serve as a foundation for constructing interacting point-form quantum field theories and as a special case of field quantization in curved space-time with a classical gravitational background.
