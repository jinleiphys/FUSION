# Non-adiabatic corrections to elastic scattering of halo nuclei

> We derive the formalism for the leading order corrections to the adiabatic approximation to the scattering of composite projectiles. Assuming a two-body projectile of core plus loosely-bound valence particle and a model (the core recoil model) in which the interaction of the valence particle and the target can be neglected, we derive the non-adiabatic correction terms both exactly, using a partial wave analysis, and using the eikonal approximation. Along with the expected energy dependence of the corrections, there is also a strong dependence on the valence-to-core mass ratio and on the strength of the imaginary potential for the core-target interaction, which relates to absorption of the core in its scattering by the target. The strength and diffuseness of the core-target potential also determine the size of the corrections. The first order non-adiabatic corrections were found to be smaller than qualitative estimates would expect. The large absorption associated with the core-target interaction in such halo nuclei as Be11 kills off most of the non-adiabatic corrections. We give an improved estimate for the range of validity of the adiabatic approximation when the valence-target interaction is neglected, which includes the effect of core absorption. Some consideration was given to the validity of the eikonal approximation in our calculations.

## Key claim

The first-order non-adiabatic corrections to the adiabatic approximation in the core recoil model are smaller than qualitative estimates would predict, because strong absorption of the core by the target (characterized by a large imaginary component in the core-target potential) suppresses the corrections at small impact parameters where they would otherwise be largest.

## Method

The authors derived the leading-order non-adiabatic correction to the elastic T-matrix for a two-body projectile (core plus valence particle) scattering from a target, within the core recoil model where the valence-target interaction is neglected. They evaluated the correction both exactly, using a partial wave expansion and solving inhomogeneous radial Schrodinger equations, and approximately, using the eikonal approximation. The formalism was applied to elastic scattering of 11Be and 6He from a 12C target at 10 MeV/nucleon, comparing adiabatic and CDCC calculations.

## Key numbers

- For 11Be + 12C at 10 MeV/nucleon, the qualitative validity parameter lambda = 0.14, suggesting the adiabatic approximation should be reasonable.
- For 6He + 12C at 10 MeV/nucleon, lambda = 5, suggesting the adiabatic approximation should be poor.
- The maximum value of the correction term tilde{chi} for 11Be + 12C at 10 MeV/nucleon is approximately 0.6.
- The maximum overlap |S * tilde{chi}| for 11Be + 12C at 10 MeV/nucleon is 0.015, indicating small non-adiabatic corrections.
- The 11Be ground state has a separation energy of 0.503 MeV, with a core rms radius of 2.28 fm and composite rms radius of 2.90 fm.
- The 6He two-body di-neutron model has a binding energy of 0.975 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the core recoil model formalism of Summers et al. (2002) and the adiabatic approximation of the authors' earlier work, providing the first quantitative calculation of non-adiabatic corrections for halo nuclei. The results explain why the adiabatic approximation works better than expected from simple time-ratio estimates, as previously noted in comparisons of CDCC and adiabatic methods for deuteron reactions. The findings enable improved estimates of the validity range for the adiabatic approximation in reactions involving weakly-bound projectiles, and provide justification for using the eikonal approximation in more complete calculations that include the valence-target interaction.
