# Predictions for multiplicities and flow harmonics in 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> We present the next-to-leading-order event-by-event EKRT model predictions for the centrality dependence of the charged hadron multiplicity in the pseudorapidity interval $|η|\le 0.5$, and for the centrality dependence of the charged hadron flow harmonics $v_n\{2\}$ obtained from 2-particle cumulants, in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.44$ TeV Xe+Xe collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider. Our prediction for the 0-5 \% central charged multiplicity is $dN_{\rm ch}/dη=1218\pm 46$. We also predict $v_n\{2\}$ in Xe+Xe collisions to increase more slowly from central towards peripheral collisions than those in a Pb+Pb system. We find that at $10 \dots 50$\% centralities $v_2\{2\}$ is smaller and $v_3\{2\}$ is larger than in the Pb+Pb system while $v_4\{2\}$ is of the same magnitude in both systems. We also find that the ratio of flow harmonics in Xe+Xe collisions and in Pb+Pb collisions shows a slight sensitivity to the temperature dependence of the shear-viscosity-to-entropy ratio. As we discuss here, the new nuclear mass-number systematics especially in the flow harmonics serves as a welcome further constraint for describing the space-time evolution of a heavy-ion system and for determining the shear viscosity and other transport properties of strongly interacting matter.

## Key claim
The NLO EbyE EKRT model predicts that in 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions, the flow harmonics \(v_n\{2\}\) increase more slowly from central to peripheral collisions than in Pb+Pb collisions, with the ratio of flow harmonics between the two systems showing a slight sensitivity to the temperature dependence of the shear-viscosity-to-entropy ratio (\(\eta/s(T)\)). The prediction is conditional on using the two best-fitting \(\eta/s(T)\) parametrizations from the authors' prior work on Pb+Pb collisions.

## Method
The authors computed initial energy densities event-by-event using the NLO EKRT saturation model, which determines a local saturation scale \(p_{\rm sat}\) from the product of nuclear thickness functions \(T_A\) via a saturation criterion. These initial states were then evolved with 2+1 D second-order dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics, using the s95p-PCE-v1 equation of state and two parametrizations of the temperature-dependent shear-viscosity-to-entropy ratio \(\eta/s(T)\). The charged hadron multiplicity and 2-particle cumulant flow harmonics \(v_n\{2\}\) were computed for centrality classes in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.44\) TeV Xe+Xe collisions, using a Woods-Saxon nuclear density for \(^{129}\)Xe with radius parameter \(R_A = 1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3}\) and diffuseness \(d=0.54\) fm.

## Key numbers
- Central charged multiplicity in 0-5% most central collisions: \(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta = 1218 \pm 46\) for \(|\eta| \le 0.5\).
- The computed centralmost multiplicities for 5.44 TeV are 1172 for \(\eta/s=0.2\) and 1199 for the \(\eta/s\) parametrization called param1.
- The ALICE 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb measurement used for normalization is \(1601 \pm 60\) in 0-5% centrality.
- The saturation scale parametrization is valid for \(K_{\rm sat} \in [0.4, 2.0]\) and \(\beta < 0.9\), with a minimum saturation momentum \(p_{\rm sat}^{\rm min} = 1\) GeV corresponding to a starting time \(\tau_0 = 0.2\) fm.
- The nucleon-nucleon cross section used is \(\sigma_{NN} = 70.53\) mb.

## Context
This work extends the NLO EbyE EKRT model predictions, previously validated for 2.76 and 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Niemi 2015, Eskola 2017), to the new nuclear mass-number systematics provided by the 2017 LHC Xe+Xe run. The predictions enable further constraints on the temperature dependence of the shear-viscosity-to-entropy ratio \(\eta/s(T)\) and the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, complementing Bayesian global analyses such as those by Bernhard (2016) and Bass (2017). The results are intended to be compared with upcoming ALICE measurements to test the saturation-based initial-state description.
