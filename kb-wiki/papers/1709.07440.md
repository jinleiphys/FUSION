# Muonic hydrogen and the proton size

> We reexamine the structure of the $n=2$ levels of muonic hydrogen using a two-body potential that includes all relativistic, recoil and one loop corrections. The potential was originally derived from QED to describe the muonium atom and accounts for all contributions to order $α^5$. Since one loop corrections are included, the anomalous magnetic moment contributions of the muon can be identified and replaced by the proton anomalous magnetic moment to describe muonic hydrogen with a point-like proton. This serves as a convenient starting point to include the dominant electron vacuum polarization corrections to the spectrum and extract the proton's mean squared radius $r_p=\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle}$. Our results are consistent with other theoretical calculations that find that the muonic hydrogen value for $r_p$ is smaller than the result obtained from electron scattering.

## Key claim

The authors reexamine the $n=2$ levels of muonic hydrogen using a two-body potential derived from QED for muonium, modified to include the proton's anomalous magnetic moment, and find a Lamb shift value of 205.980 meV that is about 0.07 meV smaller than other theoretical calculations, leading to extracted proton radii that are consistent with the muonic hydrogen value but lower than the electron scattering result.

## Method

The authors start from a two-body potential originally derived for muonium (GRS potential) that includes all relativistic, recoil, and one-loop corrections to order $\alpha^5$, then replace the muon anomalous magnetic moment with the proton anomalous magnetic moment $\kappa = 1.79285$ to describe muonic hydrogen with a point-like proton. They add the dominant electron vacuum polarization corrections (one-loop, two-loop, and three-loop) using dispersion relations and the Kallen-Sabry formalism, compute second-order nonrelativistic perturbation corrections using Coulomb Green's functions, and include spin-independent and spin-dependent contributions from the potential to calculate the $2P-2S$ splitting and hyperfine structure.

## Key numbers

* One-loop electron vacuum polarization contribution to the $2P-2S$ splitting: 205.007 meV
* Two-loop vacuum polarization contribution: 1.508 meV
* Three-loop vacuum polarization contribution: 0.004431 meV
* Second-order nonrelativistic perturbation correction from one-loop vacuum polarization: 0.1509 meV
* Spin-independent order $\alpha^4$ contribution to $2P-2S$: 5.610 meV
* Spin-independent order $\alpha^5$ contribution to $2P-2S$: -0.7073 meV
* Fine structure splitting between $P_{3/2}$ and $P_{1/2}$: 8.347 meV
* Hyperfine splitting for $S_{1/2}$ states: 22.817 meV
* Lamb shift (point-like proton): 205.980 meV
* Extracted proton radius from $2^3S_{1/2} \leftrightarrow 2^5P_{3/2}$ transition: $r_p = 0.8276$ fm (0.8302 fm with polarizability correction)
* Extracted proton radius from $2^1S_{1/2} \leftrightarrow 2^3P_{3/2}$ transition: $r_p = 0.8428$ fm (0.8452 fm with polarizability correction)
* Leading proton size correction coefficient: $5.1975\ \langle r^2 \rangle$ meV/fm$^2$ for $n=2$ $s$-states
* Total proton size correction coefficient including vacuum polarization corrections: $5.2257\ \langle r^2 \rangle$ meV/fm$^2$

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Borie (2012), Pachucki (1996), and Pohl (2010) by using a different starting point (the GRS muonium potential) to calculate the muonic hydrogen spectrum, finding a Lamb shift about 0.07 meV smaller than those calculations. The results confirm the well-known proton radius puzzle: the muonic hydrogen value for $r_p$ is systematically smaller than the CODATA value of 0.8775(51) fm from electron-proton scattering (Mohr, Taylor, and Newell, 2012), and the authors note that the PRad experiment at Jefferson Laboratory is designed to address this discrepancy. The calculation enables improved extraction of the proton radius from the measured $2S-2P$ transitions in muonic hydrogen.
