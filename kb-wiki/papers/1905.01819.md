# Integer and half-integer channel spins for elastic scattering cross sections

> We present the analytical expressions for the differential cross sections and independent partial amplitudes for elastic scattering of nuclear particles for channels with a spin value of 1/2, 1, 3/2, 2 and 5/2. The independent partial amplitudes are presented for arbitrary orbital angular momentum $l$ and taking into account spin-orbit splitting. The analytical expressions allow one to carry out full phase shift analyses using experimental data for differential cross sections for processes with channel spins 1/2, 1, 3/2, 2 and 5/2.

## Key claim

The paper provides fully analytical expressions for differential cross sections and independent partial amplitudes for elastic scattering of nuclear particles with channel spins 1/2, 1, 3/2, 2, and 5/2, for arbitrary orbital angular momentum \(l\) and including spin-orbit splitting, which the abstract presents as a complete solution but which in practice applies only to non-identical particles and requires the user to supply the scattering matrix elements \(U_{Sl}^{J}\) from experimental data or a model.

## Method

The authors start from the general expression for the scattering \(M\)-matrix in the channel-spin representation, using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, Wigner \(6j\)-symbols, and spherical harmonics to recouple angular momenta. They then derive the differential cross section as a sum over channel spin \(S\) and a summation index \(\kappa\), with the partial amplitudes \(A_{Jl}^{S}\) containing the Coulomb amplitude and the scattering matrix \(U_{Sl}^{J}\). For each specific combination of projectile and target spins (1/2+3/2, 1/2+1, 1/2+2, and 1+3/2), they write out the \(M\)-matrix explicitly, reduce it using parity conservation to independent amplitudes, and present the final cross-section formulas in terms of those independent amplitudes, whose analytical expressions are given in appendices.

## Key numbers

* The differential cross section for spin-1/2 + spin-3/2 scattering is given by \(\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega} = \frac{3}{8}\frac{d\sigma_{\mathcal{T}}}{d\Omega} + \frac{5}{8}\frac{d\sigma_{\mathfrak{Q}}}{d\Omega}\), where the triplet and quintet contributions are weighted by statistical factors 3/8 and 5/8.
* For spin-1/2 + spin-1 scattering, the cross section is \(\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega} = \frac{1}{3}\frac{d\sigma_{\mathcal{D}}}{d\Omega} + \frac{2}{3}\frac{d\sigma_{\mathcal{Q}}}{d\Omega}\), with doublet and quartet weights 1/3 and 2/3.
* The triplet state (S=1) cross section for spin-1/2+spin-3/2 requires 5 independent partial amplitudes: \(\mathcal{T}_{\ 0}^{10}, \mathcal{T}_{\ 1}^{11}, \mathcal{T}_{\ 1}^{10}, \mathcal{T}_{\ 0}^{11}, \mathcal{T}_{\ 1}^{1-1}\).
* The quintet state (S=2) cross section for the same system requires 13 independent partial amplitudes, listed as \(\mathfrak{Q}_{\ 0}^{20}, \mathfrak{Q}_{\ 1}^{21}, \mathfrak{Q}_{\ 2}^{22}, \mathfrak{Q}_{\ 1}^{20}, \mathfrak{Q}_{\ 0}^{21}, \mathfrak{Q}_{\ 1}^{22}, \mathfrak{Q}_{\ 2}^{21}, \mathfrak{Q}_{\ 1}^{2-1}, \mathfrak{Q}_{\ 2}^{20}, \mathfrak{Q}_{\ 0}^{22}, \mathfrak{Q}_{-2}^{21}, \mathfrak{Q}_{-1}^{22}, \mathfrak{Q}_{\ 2}^{2-2}\).
* For spin-1/2+spin-1 scattering, the doublet state (S=1/2) requires 2 independent amplitudes, and the quartet state (S=3/2) requires 8 independent amplitudes.

## Context

This work extends earlier results for channel spins up to 1/2 (e.g., for \(N\)-\(^{4}\)He scattering described in Hodgson (1963) and Dubovichenko (2015)) and for specific high-spin cases such as 1/2+1 and 1/2+3/2 given by Seyler (1969) and Brown (1973), respectively. The authors provide for the first time fully analytical expressions for channel spins 1, 2, 3/2, and 5/2, which enable full phase-shift analyses of elastic scattering data for reactions relevant to nucleosynthesis of light elements, such as those involving \(^{6}\)Li and \(^{7}\)Li. The formulas are intended to be used with a small number of partial waves at astrophysical energies, allowing extraction of nuclear potentials from experimental cross sections.
