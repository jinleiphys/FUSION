# Can one-loop corrections to the one-gluon exchange potential adequately describe the charmed meson spectrum?

> We investigate the charmed meson spectrum using a constituent quark model (CQM) with one-loop corrections applied to the one-gluon exchange (OGE) potential. The study aims to understand if the modified version of our CQM sufficiently account for the charmed meson spectrum observed experimentally, without invoking exotic quark and gluon configurations such as hybrid mesons or tetraquarks. Within this model, charmed mesons' masses are computed, comparing theoretical predictions to experimental data. The results, within uncertainties, suggest that our theoretical framework generally reproduces mass splittings and level ordering observed for charmed mesons. Particularly, large discrepancies between theory and experiment found in $P$-wave states are, at least, significantly ameliorated by incorporating higher-order interaction terms. Therefore, the findings emphasize that while the traditional quark model is limited in fully describing charmed mesons, enhanced potential terms may bridge the gap with experimental observations. The study contributes a framework for predicting excited charmed meson states for future experimental validation.

## Key claim

The authors claim that incorporating one-loop corrections to the one-gluon exchange potential within a constituent quark model can adequately describe the charmed meson spectrum without invoking exotic configurations such as hybrid mesons or tetraquarks, but they acknowledge that the model parameters were fine-tuned for the heavy-light meson sector and that theoretical uncertainties of 10-20% in meson masses remain.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic constituent quark model with a Gaussian expansion method to solve the Schrodinger equation for charmed meson bound states. They computed masses at leading order (O(alpha_s)) using the standard one-gluon exchange potential and then added next-to-leading order (O(alpha_s^2)) one-loop corrections to the OGE potential as derived by Gupta and Radford (1981), which include spin-dependent terms that affect mesons with different flavor quarks. Model parameters were initially constrained from prior hadron phenomenology studies, then fine-tuned for the confinement potential parameters to better describe the heavy-light meson sector.

## Key numbers

* Ground state D meson (0-): theoretical mass 1868 MeV at both O(alpha_s) and O(alpha_s^2), compared to experimental 1867.95 +/- 0.27 MeV
* Ground state D* meson (1-): theoretical mass 1982 MeV at O(alpha_s) and 1977 MeV at O(alpha_s^2), compared to experimental 2009.12 +/- 0.04 MeV
* D0* (0+) P-wave state: theoretical mass improved from 2445 MeV at O(alpha_s) to 2281 MeV at O(alpha_s^2), compared to experimental 2343 +/- 10 MeV
* D1 (1+) P-wave state: theoretical mass 2410 MeV at O(alpha_s) and 2438 MeV at O(alpha_s^2), compared to experimental 2422.1 +/- 0.8 MeV
* D2* (2+) P-wave state: theoretical mass 2452 MeV at O(alpha_s) and 2483 MeV at O(alpha_s^2), compared to experimental 2461.1 +/- 0.7 MeV
* D3* (3-) D-wave state: theoretical mass 2767 MeV at O(alpha_s) and 2783 MeV at O(alpha_s^2), compared to experimental 2763.1 +/- 3.2 MeV
* The scale-dependent effective strong coupling constant alpha_s is 0.43 in the D meson sector, implying O(alpha_s^2) corrections provide approximately 20% mass correction, with NNLO corrections estimated at 8% additional variation

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model originally proposed by Vijande et al. (2004) and applies one-loop OGE corrections following the approach of Lakhina et al. (2006) and the potential derivation of Gupta and Radford (1981). The study addresses longstanding discrepancies between naive quark model predictions and experimental masses for P-wave charmed mesons like D0*(2300) and D1(2420), which had motivated alternative explanations involving tetraquark or molecular structures. The results suggest that before invoking exotic configurations, one should first explore refined potential terms within the conventional quark-antiquark picture, though the authors note that states like Tcc, Zc(b), and X(3872) with unconventional quantum numbers or proximity to thresholds still require multiquark descriptions.
