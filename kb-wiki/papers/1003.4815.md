# Hardon-quark hybrid stars constructed by the nonlinear $Žā$-$Žē$-$Žü$ mean-field model and MIT-bag model

> Density-dependent relations among saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter and hyperonic matter, properties of hadron-(strange) quark hybrid stars are discussed by applying the conserving nonlinear $Žā$-$Žē$-$Žü$ hadronic mean-field theory. Nonlinear interactions that will be renormalized as effective coupling constants, effective masses and sources of equations of motion are constructed self-consistently by maintaining thermodynamic consistency to the mean-field approximation. The coupling constants expected from the hadronic mean-field model and SU(6) quark model for the vector coupling constants are compared; the coupling constants exhibit different density-dependent results for effective masses and binding energies of hyperons, properties of hadron and hadron-quark stars. The nonlinear $Žā$-$Žē$-$Žü$ hadronic mean-field approximation with or without vacuum fluctuation corrections and strange quark matter defined by MIT-bag model are employed to examine properties of hadron-(strange) quark hybrid stars. We have found that hadron-(strange) quark hybrid stars become more stable in high density compared to pure hadronic and strange quark stars.

## Key claim

The authors find that hadron-(strange) quark hybrid stars become more stable at high density than either pure hadronic stars or pure strange quark stars, but this result depends on using hyperon coupling ratios \(r^{\omega}_{HN} \gtrsim 1.0\) from the hadronic mean-field model rather than the SU(6) quark model value \(r^{\omega}_{HN} = 2/3\), which produces maximum masses too small to support observed neutron stars.

## Method

The authors employed the conserving nonlinear \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) mean-field approximation with and without vacuum fluctuation corrections (VFC) for hadronic matter, and the MIT-bag model for strange quark matter. Hadron-quark phase transitions were treated as first-order using Maxwell construction, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation was solved to compute stellar masses and central densities. Hyperon coupling ratios were compared between the hadronic model and the SU(6) quark model for vector coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: \(-15.75\) MeV at Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.30\) fm\(^{-1}\) (saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.148\) fm\(^{-3}\)) with symmetry energy \(a_4 = 30.0\) MeV.
- Maximum mass of pure hadronic neutron stars in \(\beta\)-equilibrium (\(n,p,e\) matter): \(M_{max} = 2.50\) \(M_{\odot}\) (solar mass \(M_{\odot} \sim 1.989 \times 10^{30}\) kg).
- Maximum mass of hadronic stars including \(\Sigma^{-}\) and \(\Lambda\) hyperons with coupling ratios \(r^{\omega}_{\Lambda N}=1.0, r^{\omega}_{\Sigma N}=1.0\): \(M_{max}(n,p,\Sigma^{-},\Lambda) = 1.98\) \(M_{\odot}\).
- Maximum mass of hadronic stars including \(\Sigma^{-}\) and \(\Lambda\) hyperons with SU(6) quark model coupling ratios \(r^{\omega}_{\Lambda N}=2/3, r^{\omega}_{\Sigma N}=2/3\): \(M_{max}(n,p,\Sigma^{-},\Lambda) = 1.33\) \(M_{\odot}\).
- Incompressibility for (\(n,p,e\)) matter without VFC: \(K \sim 320\) MeV; with VFC: \(K \sim 350\) MeV.
- Maximum mass for (\(n,p,e\)) matter with VFC: \(M_{max} = 2.33\) \(M_{\odot}\).
- \(\Lambda\)-hyperon onset density in (\(n,p,e\))-(\(n,p,\Lambda,e\)) matter: \(\rho_{\Lambda}/\rho_0 \sim 2.2\); when \(\Lambda\) is generated after \(\Sigma^{-}\), onset density is pushed to \(\rho_{\Lambda}/\rho_0 \sim 4.2\).

## Context

This work extends the conserving nonlinear \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) mean-field approximation developed by Uechi and collaborators (2008-2010) to include hyperonic matter and hadron-quark phase transitions. It compares hyperon coupling constants with those from the SU(6) quark model used by Jaffe (1977) and others, finding significant discrepancies. The results enable future comparison with astronomical observations of neutron star masses and radii to constrain the bag constant \(B\) and strong coupling constant \(\alpha_c\) in the MIT-bag model, as discussed by Glendenning (2000) and others.
