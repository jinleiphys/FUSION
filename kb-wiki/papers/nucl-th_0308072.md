# Radii of weakly bound three-body systems: halo nuclei and molecules

> A renormalized three-body model with zero-range potential is used to estimate the mean-square radii of three-body halo nuclei and molecular systems. The halo nuclei ($^6$He, $^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be and $^{20}$C) are described as point-like inert cores and two neutrons. The molecular systems, with two helium atoms, are of the type $^4$He$_2-$X, where X$=^4$He, $^6$Li, $^7$Li, or $^{23}$Na. The estimations are compared with experimental data and realistic results.

# Radii of weakly bound three-body systems: halo nuclei and molecules

## Key claim
The authors show that a renormalized three-body model with zero-range (Dirac-delta) interactions can produce universal scaling functions for root-mean-square radii that apply equally to halo nuclei and weakly bound molecular trimers, provided the two-body scattering length is much larger than the effective range. The model systematically underestimates experimental neutron-neutron radii for halo nuclei, though the results remain in reasonable agreement within uncertainties.

## Method
The authors solve a renormalized three-body model with pairwise zero-range (Dirac-delta) potentials in the scaling limit, where observables depend only on the three-body energy and two-body scales. Halo nuclei are treated as point-like inert cores plus two neutrons, and molecular systems as two helium atoms plus a third particle (He, Li, or Na). Dimensionless products of radii with the square root of the three-body energy are computed as functions of the core-to-neutron mass ratio, and absolute radii are obtained by inserting experimental or realistic three-body and two-body energies.

## Key numbers
- For $^6$He ($^4$He core + n + n): predicted $\sqrt{\langle r_{nn}^2\rangle} = 5.1$ fm, compared to experimental $5.9 \pm 1.2$ fm.
- For $^{11}$Li ($^9$Li core + n + n): predicted $\sqrt{\langle r_{nn}^2\rangle} = 5.9$ fm, compared to experimental $6.6 \pm 1.5$ fm.
- For $^{14}$Be ($^{12}$Be core + n + n): predicted $\sqrt{\langle r_{nn}^2\rangle} = 4.4$ fm, compared to experimental $5.4 \pm 1.0$ fm.
- For $^{20}$C ($^{18}$C core + n + n): predicted $\sqrt{\langle r_{nn}^2\rangle} = 3.0$ fm (no experimental value given).
- For the $^4$He$_3$ trimer: $\sqrt{\langle r_{\alpha\alpha}^2\rangle} = 9.45$ Å, $\sqrt{\langle r_\alpha^2\rangle} = 5.55$ Å, with three-body energy $E_3^{(0)} = 106.0$ mK.
- For $^4$He$_2$–$^6$Li: $\sqrt{\langle r_{\alpha\beta}^2\rangle} = 16.38$ Å, $\sqrt{\langle r_\beta^2\rangle} = 8.14$ Å, with $E_3^{(0)} = 31.4$ mK.
- For $^4$He$_2$–$^7$Li: $\sqrt{\langle r_{\alpha\beta}^2\rangle} = 13.88$ Å, $\sqrt{\langle r_\beta^2\rangle} = 6.31$ Å, with $E_3^{(0)} = 45.7$ mK.
- For $^4$He$_2$–$^{23}$Na: $\sqrt{\langle r_{\alpha\beta}^2\rangle} = 9.54$ Å, $\sqrt{\langle r_\beta^2\rangle} = 1.94$ Å, with $E_3^{(0)} = 103.1$ mK.
- The $^4$He$_3$ trimer results deviate by about 14% from the realistic calculations of Bressanini and coworkers (2000).

## Context
This work extends the universal scaling approach previously developed by the authors (Yamashita et al., 2002; Amorim et al., 1997) for three-body systems with short-range interactions. It applies the same zero-range renormalized model to both nuclear halo systems (He, Li, Be, C isotopes) and molecular trimers (He-based systems), showing that the dimensionless radius-energy products are universal functions of the mass ratio. The results for halo nuclei are compared to experimental data from Marques et al. (2000), and the molecular results are compared to realistic calculations by Bressanini and coworkers (2000). The model enables systematic estimation of radii for weakly bound three-body systems where only the three-body energy and two-body scattering lengths are known, without detailed knowledge of the interaction potential.
