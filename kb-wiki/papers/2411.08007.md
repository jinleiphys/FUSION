# Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass models on a 3D mesh: IV. Improved description of the isospin dependence of pairing

> Providing reliable data on the properties of atomic nuclei and infinite nuclear matter to astrophysical applications remains extremely challenging, especially when treating both properties coherently within the same framework. Methods based on energy density functionals (EDFs) enable manageable calculations of nuclear structure throughout the entire nuclear chart and of the properties of infinite nuclear matter across a wide range of densities and asymmetries. To address these challenges, we present BSkG4, the latest Brussels-Skyrme-on-a-Grid model. It is based on an EDF of the extended Skyrme type with terms that are both momentum and density-dependent, and refines the treatment of $^1S_0$ nucleon pairing gaps in asymmetric nuclear matter as inspired by more advanced many-body calculations. The newest model maintains the accuracy of earlier BSkGs for known atomic masses, radii and fission barriers with rms deviations of 0.633 MeV w.r.t. 2457 atomic masses, 0.0246 fm w.r.t. 810 charge radii, and 0.36 MeV w.r.t 45 primary fission barriers of actinides. It also improves some specific pairing-related properties, such as the $^1S_0$ pairing gaps in asymmetric nuclear matter, neutron separation energies, $Q_β$ values, and moments of inertia of finite nuclei. This improvement is particularly relevant for describing the $r$-process nucleosynthesis as well as various astrophysical phenomena related to the rotational evolution of neutron stars, their oscillations, and their cooling.

## Key claim

BSkG4 improves the description of $^1S_0$ pairing gaps in asymmetric infinite nuclear matter by introducing a new interpolation formula, while maintaining the same overall accuracy for known atomic masses, radii, and fission barriers as its predecessor BSkG3, with the caveat that the improvement is specifically in pairing-related properties rather than overall mass precision.

## Method

The authors constructed BSkG4 using the same extended Skyrme energy density functional and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework as BSkG3, with 29 parameters refitted using the same large-scale fitting protocol. The key novelty is a new interpolation ansatz for the $^1S_0$ nucleon pairing gaps at arbitrary isospin asymmetries, derived from the isospin splitting of the effective mass, which replaces the earlier interpolation schemes of Eqs. (GCP09) and (GRSGC23) and is calibrated to the Extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock predictions of Cao et al. (2006) for pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

* RMS deviation of 0.633 MeV for 2457 atomic masses (AME2020, nuclei with Z,N >= 8)
* RMS deviation of 0.0246 fm for 810 charge radii (Angeli 2013)
* RMS deviation of 0.36 MeV for 45 primary fission barriers of actinides (RIPL-3 database)
* RMS deviation of 0.402 MeV for 2309 neutron separation energies (improved from 0.442 MeV for BSkG3)
* RMS deviation of 0.493 MeV for 2173 beta-decay energies (improved from 0.534 MeV for BSkG3)
* RMS deviation of 0.290 MeV for 1784 five-point neutron gaps (improved from 0.300 MeV for BSkG3)
* Pairing cutoff energy E_cut = 7.919 MeV (compared to 7.961 MeV for BSkG3)

## Context

This work extends the Brussels-Skyrme-on-a-Grid series (BSkG1-3) developed by Scamps et al. (2021), Ryssens et al. (2022), and Grams et al. (2023), correcting the interpolation scheme for pairing gaps in asymmetric matter that was previously found inadequate when compared to the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer calculations of Zhang, Cao et al. (2010) using the Argonne AV18 potential. The improved pairing description enables more reliable predictions for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations and for modeling neutron star phenomena such as pulsar glitches, cooling, and oscillations, and can be used with the recently released toolkit of Pecak et al. (2024) for simulating local superfluid dynamics in neutron stars.
