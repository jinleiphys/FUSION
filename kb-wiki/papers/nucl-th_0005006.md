# Electromagnetic moments of relativistic higher spin baryons

> We point out a source of ambiguities in the measurements of the electromagnetic moments of spin-3/2 baryons which rely on relativistic Lagrangian models. An anambiguous relation between the parameters of the spin-3/2 electromagnetic Lagrangian and the electromagnetic moments of the spin-3/2 particle exits in general only for on-shell situation, while the measurements are done on ``virtual'' baryons.

# Electromagnetic moments of relativistic higher spin baryons

## Key claim
The paper identifies a fundamental ambiguity in extracting electromagnetic moments of spin-3/2 baryons from experimental data: a unique relation between Lagrangian parameters and the magnetic moment exists only for on-shell (real) particles, whereas measurements necessarily involve virtual baryons, making the extraction model-dependent.

## Method
The author constructs a general effective Lagrangian for spin-3/2 baryons using the Rarita-Schwinger formalism, including minimal coupling for electric charge and nonminimal terms for the magnetic dipole interaction. By imposing antisymmetry of the vertex to maintain the correct number of spin degrees-of-freedom, the couplings reduce to three dimensionless parameters (L1, L2, L3). The on-shell electromagnetic vertex and forward Compton amplitude are then matched to soft-photon theorems to derive an unambiguous relation between the gyromagnetic ratio g and the parameter L1, valid only for on-shell particles.

## Key numbers
- The gyromagnetic ratio for a spin-3/2 baryon is given by g = (2/3)(1 - 2 L1), where L1 is a dimensionless parameter of the nonminimal electromagnetic Lagrangian.
- The magnetic moment μ is related to the gyromagnetic ratio by μ = g (e/2m), where m is the baryon mass.
- The forward Compton amplitude to first order in photon frequency ω contains a term proportional to (g - 2)^2.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of off-shell ambiguities in the covariant Rarita-Schwinger description of spin-3/2 fields, which generally does not exclude unphysical spin-1/2 contributions for virtual particles. The author notes that Machavariani et al. (1999) claimed to eliminate these ambiguities by writing the spin-3/2 propagator in terms of on-shell vector-spinors, but at the cost of losing relativistic covariance, as discussed by Fainberg and Tyutin (1984). The paper suggests that using "consistent" πNΔ and γNΔ interactions, which decouple the spin-1/2 sector, may resolve the ambiguity in practical calculations of radiative pion-nucleon scattering and pion photoproduction, with work in progress by Tiemeijer and Pascalutsa (2000).
