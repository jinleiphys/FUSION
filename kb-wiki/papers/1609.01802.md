# Probing nuclear bubble structure via neutron star asteroseismology

> We consider torsional oscillations that are trapped in a layer of spherical-hole (bubble) nuclear structure, which is expected to occur in the deepest region of the inner crust of a neutron star. Because this layer intervenes between the phase of slab nuclei and the outer core of uniform nuclear matter, torsional oscillations in the bubble phase can be excited separately from usual crustal torsional oscillations. We find from eigenmode analyses for various models of the equation of state of uniform nuclear matter that the fundamental frequencies of such oscillations are almost independent of the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter, but strongly depend on the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy $L$. Although the frequencies are also sensitive to the entrainment effect, i.e., what portion of nucleons outside bubbles contribute to the oscillations, by having such a portion fixed, we can successfully fit the calculated fundamental frequencies of torsional oscillations in the bubble phase inside a star of specific mass and radius as a function of $L$. By comparing the resultant fitting formula to the frequencies of quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) observed from the soft-gamma repeaters, we find that each of the observed low-frequency QPOs can be identified either as a torsional oscillation in the bubble phase or as a usual crustal oscillation, given generally accepted values of $L$ for all the stellar models considered here.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the 26 Hz quasi-periodic oscillation (QPO) observed from soft-gamma repeater SGR 1806-20 can be identified as a torsional oscillation trapped in the bubble (spherical-hole) phase of the inner crust, rather than as a usual crustal oscillation, provided the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy \(L\) takes generally accepted values (around 54–73.5 MeV) and the participant ratio (entrainment effect) is tuned to about 50–70%.

## Method
The authors constructed equilibrium neutron star crust models using an extended Thomas-Fermi theory for nuclear matter, with the equation of state parameterized by the incompressibility \(K_0\) and the slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy. They solved the linearized perturbation equation for torsional oscillations in the bubble phase under the relativistic Cowling approximation, applying zero-traction boundary conditions at the inner and outer boundaries of the bubble layer. The shear modulus was taken from the standard formula for a body-centered cubic lattice, reinterpreted for bubbles with an effective charge number, and the enthalpy density was varied between maximal (all matter participates) and minimal (only neutrons inside bubbles participate) extremes to account for the entrainment effect.

## Key numbers
- The \(\ell=2\) fundamental frequency of torsional oscillations in the bubble phase for a star with \(M=1.4M_\odot\) and \(R=12\) km is fitted as a function of \(L\) via \({}_0t_2 = d_2^{(0)} / L + d_2^{(1)} + d_2^{(2)}L\), valid for \(L \lesssim 75\) MeV.
- For the stellar model with \(M=1.4M_\odot\) and \(R=12\) km, the optimal value of \(L\) for explaining the observed QPOs (18, 30, 57, 92.5 Hz) as crustal torsional oscillations is \(L = 73.5\) MeV; the 26 Hz QPO is then identified as the bubble-phase fundamental with a participant ratio \(\tilde{H}/H \approx 50\%\).
- For the stellar model with \(M=1.8M_\odot\) and \(R=14\) km, the optimal \(L\) is \(\sim 54\) MeV and the participant ratio is \(\sim 70\%\).
- The maximum value of \(L\) for the bubble structure to appear in neutron stars is predicted to be \(L \simeq 75\) MeV; for \((K_0, L) = (360, 76.4)\) MeV no pasta structures appear, and for \((360, 146.1)\) MeV only the bubble phase is absent.
- The thickness of the bubble phase is at most \(\sim 10\) m for typical neutron star models.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of crustal torsional oscillations in the spherical-nuclei phase (Sotani et al., 2012; 2013a; 2013b; 2016) by including the bubble phase, which had been neglected in most prior calculations. It addresses a persistent problem: fitting all low-lying QPOs from SGR 1806-20 with crustal oscillations alone required an unreasonably large \(L\) (over 100 MeV), inconsistent with nuclear experiments (Tsang et al., 2012). By invoking the bubble phase, the authors show that the 26 Hz QPO can be explained with a reasonable \(L\) (54–73.5 MeV), enabling future band-structure calculations of the entrainment effect in the bubble phase to further constrain neutron star mass and radius.
