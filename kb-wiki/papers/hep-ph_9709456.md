# Generalized polarizabilities and the chiral structure of the nucleon

> 5 pages, LaTex file, 1 postscript figure, uses ``espcrc1.sty'', talk given by S. Scherer at the 15th International Conference on Few Body Problems in Physics, Groningen, The Netherlands, 22-26 July 1997, to appear in the proceedings (Nucl. Phys. A)

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of the ten generalized polarizabilities (GPs) of the nucleon in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) at order \(O(p^3)\), showing that at this order all GPs are determined solely by the pion mass, axial coupling constant, and pion decay constant, with no free parameters.

## Method

The authors split the virtual Compton scattering amplitude into a pole piece (calculated using Dirac and Pauli form factors) and a residual piece containing the structure-dependent terms. They then computed the ten generalized polarizabilities, functions of the three-momentum transfer \(\bar{q}^2\), by evaluating nine one-loop diagrams and the \(\pi^0\)-exchange \(t\)-channel pole graph in HBChPT at \(O(p^3)\), using only the leading-order Lagrangians for pion-nucleon and pion-pion interactions plus the Wess-Zumino-Witten Lagrangian for the \(\pi^0\gamma\gamma^\ast\) vertex.

## Key numbers

- The generalized electric polarizability at zero momentum transfer is predicted as \(\bar{\alpha}_E = 12.8 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\), which agrees with the experimental value from real Compton scattering of \((12.1 \pm 0.8 \pm 0.5)\times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\) (MacGibbon 1995).
- The \(\bar{q}^2\) dependence of the generalized electric polarizability is given by \(\bar{\alpha}_E(\bar{q}^2)/\bar{\alpha}_E = 1 - (7/50)(\bar{q}^2/m_\pi^2) + (81/2800)(\bar{q}^4/m_\pi^4) + O(\bar{q}^6/m_\pi^6)\).
- The spin-dependent GP \(P^{(11,11)1}(\bar{q}^2)\) is expressed as a series in \(\bar{q}^2/m_\pi^2\) with separate isoscalar (pionic loop) and isovector (\(\pi^0\)-exchange) contributions, and satisfies \(P^{(11,11)1}(0)=0\) as required by charge-conjugation invariance.

## Context

This work extends the low-energy theorem for virtual Compton scattering (Guichon 1995, Scherer 1996) by providing the first chiral perturbation theory calculation of all ten generalized polarizabilities, which parametrize the model-dependent part of the VCS amplitude beyond the leading-order terms. The results contradict the constituent quark model (Guichon 1995) in two ways: the generalized electric polarizability decreases much faster with \(\bar{q}^2\) in ChPT, and the spin-dependent GP \(P^{(11,11)1}\) vanishes at \(\bar{q}^2=0\) in ChPT (due to C-invariance, Drechsel 1997a, 1997b) whereas it does not in the quark model. The predictions enable future VCS experiments to test chiral dynamics and distinguish between different models of nucleon structure.
