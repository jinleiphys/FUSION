# The Gamow-state description of the decay energy spectrum of neutron-unbound $^{25}$O

> We show the feasibility of calculating the decay energy spectrum of neutron emitting nuclei within the Gamow-state description of resonances by obtaining the decay energy spectrum of $^{25}$O. We model this nucleus as a valence neutron interacting with an $^{24}$O inert core, and we obtain the resulting resonant energies, widths and decay energy spectra for the ground and first excited states. We also discuss the similarities and differences between the decay energy spectrum of a Gamow state and the Breit-Wigner distribution with energy-dependent width.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Gamow-state formalism can produce decay energy spectra for neutron-unbound nuclei, using $^{25}$O as a test case, but the simple two-body model (valence neutron plus inert $^{24}$O core) cannot reproduce the experimentally observed $1/2^+$ first excited state, yielding instead a $7/2^-$ state as the first excited state.

## Method

The authors model $^{25}$O as a valence neutron interacting with an inert $^{24}$O core via a Woods-Saxon potential plus spin-orbit interaction. They solve the radial Schrodinger equation with purely outgoing boundary conditions to obtain Gamow (resonant) eigenfunctions and their complex energies, and use free scattering eigenfunctions to compute the decay energy spectrum via an expression analogous to Fermi's Golden Rule that includes a Lorentzian broadening factor. The numerical procedure is validated against exact results for a delta-shell potential and against the energy density of scattering eigenfunctions.

## Key numbers

* Ground state ($d_{3/2}=3/2^+$) complex energy: $z_{d_{3/2}} = (0.766 - i\,0.034)$ MeV, giving a pole width $\Gamma_{R,d_{3/2}} = 68$ keV
* First excited state ($f_{7/2}=7/2^-$) complex energy: $z_{f_{7/2}} = (5.588 - i\,0.697)$ MeV
* Decay width for ground state: $\overline{\Gamma}_{d_{3/2}} = 0.133$ MeV
* Decay width for first excited state: $\overline{\Gamma}_{f_{7/2}} = 2.424$ MeV
* Dimensionless decay constant for ground state: $\Gamma_{d_{3/2}} = 7.824$
* Dimensionless decay constant for first excited state: $\Gamma_{f_{7/2}} = 6.956$
* Woods-Saxon parameters: $V_0 = 57.7$ MeV, $V_{so} = 15.32$ MeV-fm$^2$, $a = 0.65$ fm, $r_0 = 1.06$ fm

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by the same authors in earlier papers (Betan and de la Madrid, 2015, 2017) for calculating decay energy spectra of resonances, applying it to the specific case of $^{25}$O which has been studied experimentally by Hoffman (2008), Caesar (2013), Kondo (2016), and Jones (2017). The results are compared with Continuum Shell Model calculations by Volya (2014) and Gamow Shell Model calculations by Fossez (2017) and Jones (2017). The paper enables future application of the Gamow-state approach to multi-channel decay problems and to more sophisticated many-body calculations using the Gamow Shell Model.
