# Reaction mechanisms in transport theories: a test of the nuclear effective interaction

> We review recent results concerning collective excitations in neutron-rich systems and reactions between charge asymmetric systems at Fermi energies. Solving numerically self-consistent transport equations for neutrons and protons with specific initial conditions, we explore the structure of the different dipole vibrations in the $^{132}Sn$ system and investigate their dependence on the symmetry energy. We evidence the existence of a distinctive collective mode, that can be associated with the Pygmy Dipole Resonance, with an energy well below the standard Giant Dipole Resonance and isoscalar-like character, i.e. very weakly dependent on the isovector part of the nuclear effective interaction. At variance, the corresponding strength is rather sensitive to the behavior of the symmetry energy below saturation, which rules the number of excess neutrons in the nuclear surface. In reactions between charge asymmetric systems at Fermi energies, we investigate the interplay between dissipation mechanisms and isospin effects. Observables sensitive to the isospin dependent part of nuclear interaction are discussed, providing information on the symmetry energy density dependence below saturation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) in neutron-rich nuclei is an isoscalar-like collective mode whose energy is nearly independent of the isovector part of the nuclear effective interaction, but whose strength is sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy below saturation, specifically to the slope parameter L that governs the neutron skin thickness.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann-Langevin transport equation using the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) model for the neutron-rich nucleus 132Sn, employing a Skyrme-like (SKM*) mean-field parametrization with three different density dependences of the symmetry energy (asysoft, asystiff, asysuperstiff). They excited the system with pygmy-like initial conditions (boosting excess neutrons against core nucleons) and GDR-like initial conditions (boosting all neutrons against all protons), then followed the time evolution of dipole moments for 600 fm/c to extract power spectra. For reactions at Fermi energies, they simulated Sn+Sn collisions at 35 and 50 MeV/u and calculated isospin transport ratios as a function of energy loss.

## Key numbers

- The PDR centroid energy is located at approximately 8.5 MeV in 132Sn, well below the GDR centroid which follows the systematics of 80 A^(-1/3) MeV
- The neutron skin thickness ΔR_np ranges from 0.25 fm (asysoft, L=14.4 MeV) to 0.31 fm (asysuperstiff, L=96.6 MeV)
- The integrated cross section over the PDR region represents 2.7% of the total for asysoft, 4.4% for asystiff, and 4.5% for asysuperstiff EOS
- The symmetry energy at saturation is 29.9 MeV for asysoft and 28.3 MeV for both asystiff and asysuperstiff
- The compressibility modulus K=201 MeV for the symmetric matter parametrization
- The isospin transport ratio shows a systematic effect of the symmetry energy of about 20 percent between stiff and soft EOS

## Context

This work extends semi-classical transport studies of collective excitations in neutron-rich systems, building on earlier Vlasov equation calculations by Urban (2011) that identified a pygmy mode around 8.6 MeV, and on random-phase approximation studies by Colo (2001) linking the PDR to neutron skin properties. The results connect to experimental efforts by the Chimera Collaboration (2012) on Sn+Ni reactions at 35 MeV/u, which favor a stiff symmetry energy with L approximately 70 MeV. The findings enable constraints on the low-density behavior of the symmetry energy from both nuclear structure (PDR strength) and reaction dynamics (isospin transport ratios) observables.
