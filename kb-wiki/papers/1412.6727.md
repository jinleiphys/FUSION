# The QCD equation of state to $\mathcal{O}(μ_B^4)$

> We present results from an ongoing calculation of the QCD equation of state at finite baryon chemical potential $μ_B$. We use the method of Taylor expansions to circumvent the sign problem and calculate the expansion coefficients to sixth order using HISQ fermions. We work at two lattice spacings, namely $N_τ=6$ and 8 and, though we do not take the continuum limit, demonstrate that cutoff effects remain under control. We also use our results to construct an equation of state along the freeze-out curve. Using our sixth-order results as a cross-check, we demonstrate that our fourth-order equation of state is suitable for the modeling of dense matter created in heavy ion collisions with center-of-mass energies down to $s_{NN}^{1/2}\sim20$ GeV.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a fourth-order Taylor expansion of the QCD equation of state in baryon chemical potential, cross-checked against sixth-order results, is reliable for modeling heavy-ion collisions down to center-of-mass energies of about 20 GeV, but only when the expansion is carried out to fourth order (not just second order) for the freeze-out curve to remain flat down to that energy.

## Method
The authors used lattice QCD with HISQ (Highly Improved Staggered Quark) fermions on lattices with temporal extents N_tau = 6 and 8, computing Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure in powers of mu_B/T up to sixth order. They imposed constraints of zero net strangeness and a fixed electric charge-to-baryon number ratio (r = 0.4) to determine mu_Q and mu_S as functions of mu_B. Spline fits to the coefficients c_2 and c_4 were performed over the temperature range 145-220 MeV, and thermodynamic quantities were obtained by taking temperature derivatives of the fitted coefficients.

## Key numbers
- The fourth-order equation of state is suitable for modeling heavy-ion collisions down to center-of-mass energies s_NN^(1/2) ~ 20 GeV.
- Reliable results for pressure and energy density are obtained up to mu_B/T ~ 2.0 for temperatures at and above the chiral crossover (T_c = 154(9) MeV).
- The coefficient q_1 is negative and only about 1% of s_1 across the entire temperature range considered.
- Both s_3/s_1 and q_3/q_1 are around 10% in magnitude at T ~ 150 MeV and not more than 1-2% for T > 170 MeV.
- The freeze-out temperature parametrization uses T^f_infinity = 154 MeV.
- The baryon chemical potential at freeze-out is parametrized as mu_B^f = 1.303/(1 + 0.286 x) GeV, where x = s_NN^(1/2) in GeV.

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD equation of state calculations at zero baryon chemical potential (Bazavov et al. 2014, Borsanyi et al. 2014) to finite mu_B using the Taylor expansion method (Gavai and Gupta 2001, Allton et al. 2002). It provides an equation of state along the freeze-out curve using the parametrization of Cleymans and Redlich (1999), which is directly applicable to the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC. The results enable hydrodynamic modeling of dense matter created in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate beam energies, with the next step being improved statistics for sixth-order susceptibilities to extend reliability below 20 GeV.
