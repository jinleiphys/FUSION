# Holographic estimate of the meson cloud contribution to nucleon axial form factor

> We use light-front Holography to estimate the valence quark and the meson cloud contributions to the nucleon axial form factor. The free couplings of the holographic model are determined by the empirical data and by the information extracted from lattice QCD. The holographic model provides a good description of the empirical data when we consider a meson cloud mixture of about 30\% in the physical nucleon state. The estimate of the valence quark contribution to the nucleon axial form factor compares well with the lattice QCD data for small pion masses. Our estimate of the meson cloud contribution to the nucleon axial form factor has a slower falloff with the square momentum transfer compared to typical estimates from quark models with meson cloud dressing.

## Key claim

The holographic model can describe the empirical nucleon axial form factor data well, but only when a meson cloud mixture of about 30% is included in the physical nucleon state; without additional constraints from lattice QCD, a fit with no meson cloud contribution also reproduces the data, showing that the meson cloud fraction cannot be determined from experimental data alone.

## Method

The author uses a bottom-up soft-wall holographic QCD model with a confining potential, representing the nucleon axial form factor as a sum of contributions from the three-quark valence state (τ=3) and the five-parton (qqq)q\(\bar q\) meson cloud state (τ=5). The free parameters of the holographic model, the quark axial coupling \(g_A^0\), the induced pseudoscalar coupling \(\eta_A\), and the Fock state weight \(c_3\), are determined by fitting to the empirical parametrization of \(G_A\) and to lattice QCD data for the bare contribution \(G_A^{\rm B}\) extrapolated to the physical limit using the covariant spectator quark model of AxialFF.

## Key numbers

- The holographic mass scale is \(\kappa = 0.385\) GeV, chosen to reproduce the \(\rho\) meson mass \(m_\rho \simeq 770\) MeV.
- The experimental value of the axial form factor at zero momentum transfer is \(G_A(0) = 1.2723\) from neutron \(\beta\) decay (PDG 2014).
- The empirical parametrization uses a dipole form with \(M_A\) between 1.0 GeV and 1.1 GeV, with central value \(M_A \simeq 1.05\) GeV, and a precision parameter \(\delta = 0.03\).
- The bare contribution \(G_A^{\rm B}\) extrapolated from lattice QCD has a \(P\)-state mixture of 25% and an average error of 5%.
- The meson cloud parametrization from the spectator model has \(G_A^{{\rm MC}0} = 1.68\) and \(\Lambda = 1.05\) GeV.
- A fit constrained by lattice QCD information yields parameters \(g_A^0 = 1.200\), \(\eta_A = 1.083\), and \(c_3 = 0.721\), with \(\chi^2 = 2.06\) per data point for \(G_A^{\rm B}\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach previously applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Gutsche 2012a, Gutsche 2013a) to the axial structure of the nucleon. It uses the covariant spectator quark model of AxialFF to extract the bare valence quark contribution from lattice QCD data, enabling a separation of valence and meson cloud effects. The results enable future comparisons with quark models that include explicit meson cloud dressing (e.g., Thomas 1984, Bernard 2002) and provide a holographic estimate of the meson cloud contribution that falls off more slowly with \(Q^2\) than typical quark-model estimates.
