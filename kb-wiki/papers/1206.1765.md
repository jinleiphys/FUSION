# Benchmark calculations for elastic fermion-dimer scattering

> We present continuum and lattice calculations for elastic scattering between a fermion and a bound dimer in the shallow binding limit. For the continuum calculation we use the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equation to determine the scattering length and effective range parameter to high precision. For the lattice calculation we use the finite-volume method of Lüscher. We take into account topological finite-volume corrections to the dimer binding energy which depend on the momentum of the dimer. After subtracting these effects, we find from the lattice calculation kappa a_fd = 1.174(9) and kappa r_fd = -0.029(13). These results agree well with the continuum values kappa a_fd = 1.17907(1) and kappa r_fd = -0.0383(3) obtained from the STM equation. We discuss applications to cold atomic Fermi gases, deuteron-neutron scattering in the spin-quartet channel, and lattice calculations of scattering for nuclei and hadronic molecules at finite volume.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first benchmark of finite-volume lattice methods for low-energy scattering of composite objects, demonstrating that after subtracting topological finite-volume corrections to the dimer binding energy in a moving frame, lattice calculations of the fermion-dimer scattering length and effective range parameter agree well with high-precision continuum results from the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equation.

## Method

The authors performed continuum calculations using the STM integral equation to determine fermion-dimer scattering parameters to high precision. For the lattice calculations, they used two different Hamiltonian formulations (single-site interaction H1 and contact-plus-nearest-neighbor interaction H2) on periodic cubic lattices, applied the finite-volume method of Luscher to extract phase shifts, and accounted for topological finite-volume corrections to the dimer binding energy that depend on the dimer's momentum. The interaction coefficients were tuned to produce dimer binding energies from -1.5 MeV to -4.0 MeV in large volume (L=80), with m=939 MeV and inverse lattice spacing 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Lattice calculation after topological corrections: kappa a_fd = 1.174(9) and kappa r_fd = -0.029(13)
* Continuum STM calculation: kappa a_fd = 1.17907(1) and kappa r_fd = -0.0383(3)
* Experimental neutron-deuteron quartet scattering length: 4a_nd = 6.35(2) fm, corresponding to kappa 4a_nd = 1.47(1)
* Ratio of neutron-deuteron to triplet proton-neutron scattering length: 4a_nd / 3a_np = 1.17(1)
* Correlated Gaussian expansion result from vonStecher (2008): kappa a_fd approximately 1.18(1) and kappa r_fd approximately 0.08(1)

## Context

This work extends previous integral equation studies of fermion-dimer scattering by Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian (1957) and later calculations by Petrov (2003, 2005), Levinsen (2008, 2011), and Tan (2008), while providing the first lattice benchmark for such composite scattering. The results enable lattice calculations of elastic neutron-nucleus scattering and have applications to cold atomic Fermi gases, deuteron-neutron scattering in the spin-quartet channel, and lattice studies of scattering for nuclei and hadronic molecules at finite volume.
