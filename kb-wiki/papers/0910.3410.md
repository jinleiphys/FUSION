# Stabilized-jellium description of neutral and multiply charged fullerenes

> A description of neutral and multiply charged fullerenes is proposed based on a stabilized jellium (structureless pseudopotential) approximation for the ionic background and the local density approximation for the sigma and pi valence electrons. A recently developed shell-correction method is used to calculate total energies and properties of both the neutral and multiply charged anionic and cationic fullerenes. The effect of the icosahedral symmetry is included perturbatively. The calculated single-particle energy level spectrum of C_60 is in good correspondence with experimentally measured ones and previous self-consistent local-density-approximation calculations. For the multiply charged fullerenes, we calculate microscopically the charging energies for up to 12 excess charges. A semiclassical interpretation of these results is developed, which views the fullerenes as Coulomb islands possessing a classical capacitance. The calculated values for the first ionization potential and the first electron affinity agree well with the experimental ones. Our calculations support the results from charge transfer bracketing experiments and from direct ionization experiments through electron impact. The doubly charged negative ion is found to be a very long-lived metastable species, in agreement with observations.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a stabilized-jellium approximation combined with a shell-correction method can describe neutral and multiply charged fullerenes, including metastable anionic species, without requiring self-consistent solution of the Kohn-Sham equations; the method's validity is conditional on treating icosahedral symmetry as a perturbative correction and using a Wigner-Seitz radius of 1.23 a.u. determined by comparison with prior pseudopotential LDA calculations.

## Method

The authors used a local density approximation (LDA) with a stabilized-jellium (structureless pseudopotential) background for the ionic cores, employing an extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) variational procedure with a parametrized electron density profile appropriate for the hollow cage structure of C60. Shell corrections were incorporated via a generalized shell-correction method (GSCM) that replaces the ETF kinetic energy with a sum over single-particle energies from the ETF effective potential, and icosahedral symmetry effects were added perturbatively using crystal field theory with radial parameters adjusted to reproduce prior pseudopotential LDA calculations. Total energies for charged species were calculated by imposing constraints on the total electronic charge (240 ± x electrons) and optimizing the density profile parameters for each charge state.

## Key numbers

- First ionization potential (I1): 7.40 eV (shell-corrected, icosahedral), compared to experimental value of 7.54 eV
- First electron affinity (A1): 2.75 eV (shell-corrected, icosahedral), compared to experimental value of 2.75 eV
- Second electron affinity (A2): −0.09 eV (shell-corrected, icosahedral), indicating metastability of C60^2−
- Second ionization potential (I2): 10.31 eV (shell-corrected, icosahedral), compared to experimental values of 9.7 eV (McElvany et al.) and 10.3 eV (Sai Baba et al.)
- Appearance energy of C60^3+ (sum of I1+I2+I3): 30.99 eV (shell-corrected, icosahedral), compared to experimental value of 31.2±1 eV (Sai Baba et al.)
- Classical capacitance C: 8.84 a.u. from ETF calculation, compared to 8.32 a.u. for a classical conductor with outer jellium radius
- Wigner-Seitz radius for jellium background: 1.23 a.u.
- Fullerene cage radius: 6.7 a.u.
- Difference potential ⟨δυ⟩_WS: −9.61 eV
- Gap between t1u and t1g shells: approximately 1.23 eV
- Gap between hu and gg shells: 1.75 eV

## Context

This work extends the shell-correction method previously developed by the same authors for metal microclusters (Yannouleas and Landman, 1992) to carbon fullerenes, adapting it with a stabilized-jellium background (Perdew, 1988) and a hollow-cage density profile. The results agree well with self-consistent LDA pseudopotential calculations (Troullier and Martins, 1992; Rösch et al., 1993; Ye et al., 1993) and with experimental measurements from charge-transfer bracketing (McElvany et al., 1992) and electron impact ionization (Sai Baba et al., 1993). The method enables microscopic calculation of charging energies for multiply charged fullerenes up to 12 excess charges, including metastable anionic species where standard Kohn-Sham LDA is known to fail, and provides a semiclassical interpretation of fullerenes as Coulomb islands with a classical capacitance.
