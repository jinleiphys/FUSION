# Probing gluon saturation and nuclear structure in photon-nucleus collisions

> We calculate exclusive vector meson photoproduction within the Color Glass Condensate framework in high-energy photon-nucleus scattering probed experimentally in ultra peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and at the LHC. When the free parameters are constrained by the $γ+p$ data from HERA, we predict significant nuclear suppression for both the coherent and incoherent photoproduction cross section in the TeV range. Our results indicate that the LHC data prefers even stronger saturation effects at the highest collision energies. Furthermore, we demonstrate how the linear polarization of photons in ulra peripheral collisions generates azimuthal modulations in the decay products of the exclusively produced vector meson. We show how these measurements can probe details of the nuclear geometry, specifically the deformed structure of the uranium nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that exclusive vector meson photoproduction in ultra peripheral collisions shows significant nuclear suppression due to gluon saturation, but the LHC data prefers even stronger saturation effects than predicted by the applied leading-order Color Glass Condensate calculations.

## Method

The authors calculate exclusive J/psi photoproduction cross sections within the Color Glass Condensate framework using the dipole picture, where the scattering amplitude depends on the dipole-target scattering amplitude described by Wilson lines evolved via the JIMWLK equation. Free parameters for the proton saturation scale, geometry fluctuations, and infrared regulators are fixed by HERA gamma+p to J/psi+p data, with no free parameters added when generalizing to nuclei except for the standard Woods-Saxon geometry. The coherent cross section (target intact) is obtained from the average scattering amplitude, while the incoherent cross section (target dissociates) is obtained from the variance of the amplitude over target configurations.

## Key numbers

- Coherent J/psi photoproduction cross section in gamma+Pb is well reproduced for W < 100 GeV, but data at higher energies requires even stronger saturation effects than predicted
- Nuclear modification factor S_coh for coherent J/psi photoproduction shows strong suppression at high energies due to gluon saturation
- Incoherent J/psi photoproduction cross section predictions are presented as a function of photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy W, with and without nucleon substructure fluctuations
- Azimuthal modulation coefficient 2<cos(2 Delta Phi)> for rho meson decay products in U+U collisions is sensitive to the nuclear deformation parameter beta_2

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework calculations of Mantysaari (2022) and Mantysaari (2023) to confront new high-energy data from ALICE, CMS, and STAR collaborations at the LHC and RHIC. The method of separating the two indistinguishable contributions in UPCs (which nucleus emits the photon and which is the target) follows the approach developed by Guzey (2013), relying on forward neutron multiplicity measurements. The results enable future measurements of the energy-dependent incoherent cross section to further constrain saturation models, and demonstrate that azimuthal modulations in vector meson decay products can probe nuclear geometry, specifically the deformed structure of uranium nuclei.
