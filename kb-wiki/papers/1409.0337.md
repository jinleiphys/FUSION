# Calculation of multichannel reactions in the four-nucleon system above breakup threshold

> Exact four-body equations of Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas are solved for neutron-${}^3\mathrm{He}$ and proton-${}^3\mathrm{H}$ scattering in the energy regime above the four-nucleon breakup threshold. Cross sections and spin observables for elastic, transfer, charge-exchange, and breakup reactions are calculated using realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction models, including the one with effective many-nucleon forces due to explicit $Δ$-isobar excitation. The experimental data are described reasonably well with only few exceptions such as vector analyzing powers.

## Key claim

The authors solve exact four-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for neutron-${}^3\mathrm{He}$ and proton-${}^3\mathrm{H}$ scattering above the four-nucleon breakup threshold, obtaining cross sections and spin observables that describe experimental data reasonably well with only few exceptions such as vector analyzing powers; the calculations are fully converged with respect to numerical integration, angular momentum decomposition, and Coulomb screening.

## Method

The symmetrized AGS integral equations for four-particle transition operators were solved in momentum space at complex energies with finite imaginary part $\varepsilon$, using a special integration method to treat quasisingularities. The limit $\varepsilon \to +0$ was obtained by extrapolation using both the point method and cubic spline extrapolation, with Coulomb interaction treated via screening and renormalization using screening radii from 10 to 16 fm. Calculations employed realistic high-precision two-baryon potentials: the purely nucleonic INOY04 potential and the CD Bonn + $\Delta$ coupled-channel potential that includes effective three- and four-nucleon forces through explicit $\Delta$-isobar excitation.

## Key numbers

- ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ binding energy: 7.53 MeV (CD Bonn + $\Delta$), 7.73 MeV (INOY04), experimental 7.72 MeV
- ${}^3\mathrm{H}$ binding energy: 8.28 MeV (CD Bonn + $\Delta$), 8.49 MeV (INOY04), experimental 8.48 MeV
- Neutron beam energy range for $\nHe$ reactions: 0 to 24 MeV
- Proton beam energies for $\pH$ elastic scattering: 13.6 and 19.5 MeV
- Proton beam energies for $\Hh(p,n)\He$ charge exchange: 7, 9, 13.6, and 18 MeV
- Imaginary energy parameter $\varepsilon$: 1 to 2 MeV at lowest energies, 2 to 4 MeV at highest energies
- Coulomb screening radius $R$: 10 to 16 fm, sufficient for convergence
- Partial-wave truncation: $2N$ orbital angular momentum $l_x \leq 5$, $3N$ spectator orbital angular momentum $l_y \leq 5$, $4N$ orbital angular momentum $l_z \leq 6$, yielding up to about 21000 channels for fixed total angular momentum and parity
- Differences between INOY04 and CD Bonn + $\Delta$ for $\Hh(p,n)\He$ differential cross section: about 10% at forward angles and 5% at backward angles at 13.6 MeV
- Differences between INOY04 and CD Bonn + $\Delta$ for spin observables in charge exchange: up to 10-15%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations below breakup threshold (Deltuva and Fonseca, 2007) to the energy regime above breakup, where all two-, three-, and four-cluster channels are open simultaneously. It builds on earlier coordinate-space calculations limited to single-channel reactions below inelastic threshold (Viviani et al., 2001; Kievsky et al., 2008; Lazauskas, 2004, 2009) and on complex-scaling methods with semirealistic S-wave interactions (Lazauskas, 2012). The results enable future calculations of deuteron-deuteron scattering reactions and provide a benchmark for approximate methods in direct nuclear reaction theory, as well as applications to four-atom recombination in cold-atom physics.
