# New treatment of the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model

> We perform a study of infinite hadronic matter, finite nuclei and hypernuclei with an improved method of calculating the effective baryon mass. A detailed study of the predictions of the model is made in comparison with the available data and the level of agreement is generally very good. Comparison with an earlier treatment shows relatively minor differences at or below normal nuclear matter density, while at high density the improved calculation is quite different. In particular, we find no phase transition corresponding to chiral symmetry restoration in high density nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using a linear definition of the effective baryon mass (instead of the earlier square-root ansatz) removes the first-order phase transition to chiral symmetry restoration at high density, while producing only minor differences at or below normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors employ the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model, where quarks are confined in baryons by an effective potential and interact with scalar and vector mesons based on SU(3) chiral symmetry. They replace the previous square-root ansatz for the effective baryon mass (which subtracted center-of-mass motion via \(M_j^*=\sqrt{E_j^{*2}- \langle p_{j\,cm}^{*2}\rangle}\)) with a linear definition \(M_j^*=\sum_i n_{ij}e_i^*-E_j^0\), where \(E_j^0\) is a constant adjusted to fit free baryon masses. The model is then applied to infinite hadronic matter, finite nuclei, and hypernuclei by solving coupled equations for quark wave functions, baryon Dirac equations, and meson mean-field equations iteratively.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) with binding energy \(\varepsilon/\rho - M_N = -16\) MeV for both treatments
- Effective nucleon mass ratio at saturation: \(M^*_N/M_N = 0.603\) (square-root) vs. \(0.742\) (linear definition)
- Compression modulus: \(K = 225\) MeV (square-root) vs. \(303\) MeV (linear)
- \(\Lambda\) potential in nuclear matter at saturation: \(U_\Lambda^{(N)} = -28.5\) MeV (square-root) vs. \(-28.0\) MeV (linear)
- \(\Xi\) potential in nuclear matter at saturation: \(U_\Xi^{(N)} = -14.3\) MeV (square-root) vs. \(8.0\) MeV (linear)
- Maximum binding energy of strange hadronic matter: \(\sim 108\) MeV at strangeness fraction \(f_s \approx 1.97\) (linear), compared to \(\sim 70\) MeV at \(f_s \approx 1.5\) (square-root)
- Proton spin-orbit splitting in \(^{16}\)O: \(1p_{1/2}-1p_{3/2} \approx 5.5\) MeV (square-root, experimental 6.7 MeV)
- Double-\(\Lambda\) binding energy for \(^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}\)He: \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 11.45\) MeV (linear) vs. \(15.21\) MeV (square-root), experimental \(10.9 \pm 0.8\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model previously developed by Wang et al. (2003, 2004) by adopting an improved treatment of center-of-mass corrections inspired by Guichon et al. (1996), who found that the c.m. correction is only weakly density-dependent. The new linear definition eliminates the first-order phase transition to chiral symmetry restoration that appeared in the earlier square-root treatment, bringing the model into closer agreement with the behavior of the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988). The results enable more reliable predictions for high-density hadronic matter, finite nuclei, and hypernuclei, and provide a framework for future studies of strange hadronic matter and neutron star interiors.
