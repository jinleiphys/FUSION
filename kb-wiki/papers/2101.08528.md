# Electric dipole polarizability in neutron-rich Sn isotopes as a probe of nuclear isovector properties

> The determination of nuclear symmetry energy, and in particular, its density dependence, is a long-standing problem for nuclear physics community. Previous studies have found that the product of electric dipole polarizability $α_D$ and symmetry energy at saturation density $J$ has a strong linear correlation with $L$, the slope parameter of symmetry energy. However, current uncertainty of $J$ hinders the precise constraint on $L$. We investigate the correlations between electric dipole polarizability $α_D$ (or times symmetry energy at saturation density $J$) in Sn isotopes and the slope parameter of symmetry energy $L$ using the quasiparticle random-phase approximation based on Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov. A strong and model-independent linear correlation between $α_D$ and $L$ is found in neutron-rich Sn isotopes where pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) gives a considerable contribution to $α_D$, attributed to the pairing correlations playing important roles through PDR. This newly discovered linear correlation would help one to constrain $L$ and neutron-skin thickness $ΔR_\textnormal{np}$ stiffly if $α_D$ is measured with high resolution in neutron-rich nuclei. Besides, a linear correlation between $α_D J$ in a nucleus around $β$-stability line and $α_D$ in a neutron-rich nucleus can be used to assess $α_D$ in neutron-rich nuclei.

# Electric dipole polarizability in neutron-rich Sn isotopes as a probe of nuclear isovector properties

## Key claim
A strong and model-independent linear correlation between electric dipole polarizability α_D and the slope parameter of symmetry energy L is found in neutron-rich Sn isotopes (A ≥ 140), but only when pairing correlations are included because they enhance the pygmy dipole resonance contribution. This correlation could constrain L and neutron-skin thickness stiffly if α_D is measured with high resolution in neutron-rich nuclei, circumventing the problem that the previously known α_D J versus L correlation is limited by the uncertainty in J.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations on top of Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory for even-even Sn isotopes from ¹⁰⁰Sn to ¹⁶⁴Sn, using 19 different Skyrme density functionals (SIII, SIV, SV, SVI, SLy230a, SLy230b, SLy4, SLy5, SLy8, SAMi, SAMi-J30 through SAMi-J33, SGI, SGII, SkM, SkM*, Ska). A density-dependent zero-range surface pairing force was implemented, with the strength adjusted by fitting neutron pairing gaps of ¹¹⁶–¹³⁰Sn. Linear correlations were evaluated by least-square regression analysis with Pearson's coefficient r.

## Key numbers
- Pearson's coefficient r between α_D and L in ¹⁶⁰Sn reaches 0.99, compared to r ≈ 0.8 in stable ¹²⁰Sn
- For ¹⁴⁰Sn, an uncertainty of ±0.5 fm³ in α_D constrains L within ±10 MeV; for ¹⁶⁰Sn, the same uncertainty constrains L within ±6 MeV
- The predicted α_D for ¹⁵⁰Sn from correlations with ²⁰⁸Pb and ¹²⁴Sn experimental data is 14.13–16.25 fm³
- The slope k of the α_D versus L regression line increases from 0.050 fm³/MeV in ¹⁴⁰Sn to 0.082 fm³/MeV in ¹⁶⁰Sn
- Pearson's coefficient r between α_D J and L remains above 0.93 for all Sn isotopes from ¹⁰⁰Sn to ¹⁶⁰Sn
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, using the α_D J versus L correlation gives L = 39.45 ± 34.15 MeV with J = 31.7 ± 3.2 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Roca-Maza (2013) and Roca-Maza (2015) that found linear correlations between α_D J and L in stable nuclei like ²⁰⁸Pb, but were limited by the uncertainty in J. The authors show that in neutron-rich Sn isotopes beyond ¹³²Sn, where pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) contributes significantly, a direct α_D versus L correlation emerges that is model-independent across different Skyrme functional families. This enables future constraints on L and neutron-skin thickness once experimental α_D data become available for neutron-rich nuclei, and provides predictions for α_D in ¹⁴⁰Sn through ¹⁶⁰Sn using correlations with measured α_D in ²⁰⁸Pb and ¹²⁴Sn.
