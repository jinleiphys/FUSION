# Modified lepton couplings and the Cabibbo-angle anomaly

> Significant discrepancies between the different determinations of the Cabibbo angle have been observed. Here, we point out that this "Cabibbo-angle anomaly" can be explained by lepton flavour universality (LFU) violating New Physics (NP) in the neutrino sector. However, modified neutrino couplings to Standard Model gauge bosons also affect many other observables sensitive to LFU violation, which have to be taken into account in order to assess the viability of this explanation. Therefore, we perform a model-independent Bayesian global analysis and find that non-zero modifications of electron and muon neutrino couplings are preferred at more than $99.99\%\,{\rm C.L.}$ (corresponding to more than $4\,σ$). Our results show that constructive effects in the muon sector are necessary, meaning simple models with right-handed neutrinos are discarded and more sophisticated NP models required.

## Key claim

The Cabibbo-angle anomaly can be explained by lepton flavour universality violating new physics in the neutrino sector, but only if the modifications to muon neutrino couplings are constructive; simple models with right-handed neutrinos are discarded because they produce destructive interference that does not fit the data.

## Method

The authors performed a model-independent Bayesian global analysis using the HEPfit package, fitting modified neutrino couplings to W and Z bosons (parametrized as εee and εμμ) against electroweak observables from LEP, Tevatron, and LHC, low-energy lepton flavour universality tests from kaon, pion, and tau decays, and the four main determinations of Vus (from K→πℓν, K→ℓν/π→ℓν, super-allowed nuclear beta decay, and tau decays). The analysis included two scenarios for the nuclear corrections in beta decays (with and without the new nuclear corrections, NNC).

## Key numbers

- Non-zero modifications of electron and muon neutrino couplings are preferred at more than 99.99% C.L. (more than 4σ).
- The Information Criterion for the Standard Model is ICSM ≈ 93, compared to ICNP ≈ 76 for the new physics scenario (with or without NNC), constituting "very strong" evidence against the SM.
- Vus from K→πℓν is 0.2232(11); from K→ℓν/π→ℓν using CKM unitarity it is 0.22534(44); from super-allowed beta decay it is 0.22805(64) without NNC and 0.2280(14) with NNC; from tau decays it is 0.2221(13).
- The inconsistency between Vus determinations is quantified at 3–5σ without NNC, or 1.7–3σ with NNC.

## Context

This work extends the study of the Cabibbo-angle anomaly (previously examined in the context of first-row CKM unitarity violation by Belfatto (2019) and Cheung (2020)) by proposing lepton flavour universality violation as an alternative explanation. The results connect the anomaly to other hints of new physics in semi-leptonic B decays and the anomalous magnetic moments of the muon and electron, and they rule out simple right-handed neutrino models (Mohapatra, 1986; Bernabeu, 1987; Branco, 1989; Buchmuller, 1991; Pilaftsis, 1992; Malinsky, 2005; Dev, 2012; Antusch, 2014; Coy, 2018) while motivating more sophisticated models such as those presented by Capdevila (2020) and Crivellin (2020).
