# Constraints on narrow exotic states from K+p and K0_Lp scattering data

> We consider the effect of exotic S=+1 resonances Theta+ and Theta++ on K+p elastic scattering data (total cross section) and the process K0_Lp-->K0_Sp. Data near the observed Theta+(1540) are examined for evidence of additional states. The width limit for a Theta++ state is reconsidered and shown to be much less than 1 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper finds no conclusive evidence for exotic S=+1 resonances (Theta+ and Theta++) in existing K+p and K0_Lp scattering data, but quantifies that if a Theta++ state exists and decays primarily to K+p, its width must be significantly less than 1 MeV, much narrower than the approximately 1-2 MeV width limit associated with the Theta+(1540).

## Method

The authors analyzed K+p elastic total cross section data from 1500 MeV to 1700 MeV and integrated cross section data for the reaction K0_Lp → K0_Sp, searching for narrow resonance structures. For the K+p channel, they added a resonance peak to the partial-wave fit while accounting for the beam momentum spread (30 MeV/c FWHM), assuming the Theta++ to be an elastic resonance in the P13 partial wave. For the K0_Lp → K0_Sp channel, they compared existing data from multiple experiments to predictions based on published amplitudes, with and without an added Theta+ resonance at 1600 MeV with a width of 2 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The beam momentum spread for the K+p scattering data was 30 MeV/c (FWHM), which is crucial when resonance width is comparable to or less than the momentum resolution.
- For a Theta++ candidate at 1600 MeV with a width of 1 MeV added to the P13 partial wave, the existing K+p total cross section data would have shown a visible structure if the width were comparable to the 1-2 MeV limit associated with the Theta+.
- The ITEP experiment gave a width limit for the Theta+(1540) of Gamma < 9 MeV, while a reanalysis by Cahn and Trilling resulted in a finite width of approximately 1 MeV.
- The most precise K0_Lp → K0_Sp measurements from Cameron et al. begin at the Theta+ mass and show no sign of a structure around 1600 MeV, though a less pronounced bump could be supported by data from another reference.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous search for exotic S=+1 resonances (Workman et al., 2003) by applying a more appropriate method that accounts for beam momentum resolution, and by examining the K0_Lp → K0_Sp reaction in addition to K+p scattering. The study was motivated by the claimed observation of the Theta+(1540) at SPring-8 and other facilities, and by the subsequent mixed results from high-energy facilities that produced negative results. The paper enables future searches to set more stringent width limits for Theta++ states and highlights the importance of accounting for beam momentum resolution when searching for narrow resonances in kaon-nucleon scattering.
