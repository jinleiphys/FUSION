# Quantum Nucleardynamics as an SU(2)_N x U(1)_Z Gauge Theory

> It is illustrated that quantum nucleardynamics (QND) as an SU(2)_N x U(1)_Z gauge theory, which is generated from quantum chromodynamics (QCD) as an SU(3)_C gauge theory through dynamical spontaneous symmetry breaking, successfully describes nuclear phenomena at low energies. The proton and neutron assigned as a strong isospin doublet are identified as a colorspin plus weak isospin doublet. Massive gluon mediates strong interactions with the effective coupling constant G_R/\sqrt{2} = g_n^2/8 M_G^2 \simeq 10 GeV^{-2} just like Fermi weak constant G_F/\sqrt{2} = g_w^2/8 M_W^2 \simeq 10^{-5} GeV^{-2} in the Glashow-Weinberg-Salam model where g_n and g_w are the coupling constants and M_G and M_W are the gauge boson masses. Several explicit evidences such as cross sections, lifetimes, nucleon-nucleon scattering, magnetic dipole moment, nuclear potential, gamma decay, etc. are shown in support of QND. The baryon number conservation is the consequence of the U(1)_Z gauge theory and the proton number conservation is the consequence of the U(1)_f gauge theory.

# Quantum Nucleardynamics as an SU(2)_N x U(1)_Z Gauge Theory

## Key claim

The paper proposes that quantum nucleardynamics (QND) as an SU(2)_N x U(1)_Z gauge theory, dynamically generated from QCD through spontaneous symmetry breaking, can describe nuclear phenomena at low energies, but this claim is conditional on the introduction of a new "colorspin" concept and the identification of nucleons as a colorspin plus weak isospin doublet rather than simply a strong isospin doublet.

## Method

The author derives QND from QCD via dynamical spontaneous symmetry breaking (DSSB) through the chain SU(3)_C → SU(2)_N x U(1)_Z → U(1)_f, analogous to the Glashow-Weinberg-Salam model for weak interactions. Massive gluons mediate strong interactions with an effective coupling constant G_R/√2 = g_n²/8M_G², and the proton and neutron are assigned as a colorspin doublet governed by local SU(2)_N x U(1)_Z gauge symmetry. The paper then applies this framework to compute cross sections, lifetimes, magnetic dipole moments, nuclear potentials, and decay processes, comparing results with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Effective strong coupling constant: G_R/√2 ≈ 10 GeV⁻² (compared to Fermi constant G_F/√2 ≈ 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²)
- Color mixing angle: sin²θ_R = 1/4 (analogous to Weinberg angle sin²θ_W = 1/4)
- Strong decay lifetime: τ(Δ → n + p) ≈ 10⁻²³ s (compared to weak decay τ(Σ → n + π) ≈ 10⁻¹⁰ s)
- Strong cross section: σ ≈ G_R²T² ≈ 10⁻³⁰ m² (compared to weak cross section σ ≈ 10⁻⁴⁴ m²)
- Gluon mass: approximately 300 MeV for SU(2)_N gauge theory or 140 MeV for U(1)_Z gauge theory
- Linearly increasing potential coefficient: α ≈ Λ_QCD²/2 ≈ 0.05 GeV² with Λ_QCD ≈ 0.3 GeV
- Harmonic oscillator potential coefficient: β = Λ_QCD³/3 ≈ 0.01 GeV³
- Decay rate for quark-antiquark annihilation: Γ = 135 MeV, lifetime τ = 3.6 × 10⁻²⁴ s (using α_s = 0.48, m_q = 310 MeV, |ψ(0)|² = (148 MeV)³)
- Σ⁰ resonance: central mass 1385 MeV, decay width Γ = 36 MeV, lifetime τ ≈ 10⁻²³ s
- Separation energy of valence nucleon: m_n α_f²/2 ≈ 0.42 MeV with α_f ≈ 0.03
- Colorspin-orbit coupling energy: m_n α_f⁴ ≈ 0.8 KeV
- Nuclear Lamb shift: m_n α_f⁵ ≈ 0.02 KeV
- Effective electromagnetic coupling: α_f^eff = α_s/64 ≈ 1/137 when α_s ≈ 0.48
- Nuclear number density: n_n ≈ 1.95 × 10³⁸ cm⁻³
- Baryon asymmetry: δ_B ≈ 10⁻¹⁰
- Gluon number density: n_G = Λ_QCD³ ≈ 10⁻² GeV³ ≈ 10³⁹ cm⁻³
- Total gluon number: N_G ≈ 10⁹¹
- Total baryon number: B = N_B ≈ 10⁷⁸

## Context

This work extends the author's previous proposal (Roh, 1999) of colorspin as a new quantum number and attempts to provide a gauge-theoretic foundation for nuclear forces analogous to the Glashow-Weinberg-Salam model for weak interactions. The paper contrasts with effective nuclear models that are limited to specific phenomena, claiming QND can address multiple nuclear issues including Lande's spin g-factor, constant nucleon density, nucleon-nucleon scattering, and baryon number conservation. It enables a unified treatment of strong and electromagnetic nuclear interactions through the SU(2)_N x U(1)_Z → U(1)_f symmetry breaking chain, though the framework introduces many free parameters and speculative concepts that would require further experimental validation.
