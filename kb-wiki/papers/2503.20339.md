# Effect of $α$-clusters on particle production in O$-$O and p$-$O collisions at LHC energies

> In the present work, O$-$O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7 TeV and p$-$O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 9.9 TeV are studied using PYTHIA8/Angantyr model for heavy-ion collisions. The theoretically predicted $α$-cluster structure of oxygen nucleus is implemented in the model to investigate the effect of initial configuration of oxygen nucleus on final state observables. The results obtained from $α$-cluster structure are compared with those obtained from Woods-Saxon nuclear charge density distribution. The Angantyr model simulation showed that the radial distribution of oxygen nucleus in $α$-cluster configuration is more compact in comparison to the Woods-Saxon distribution. The results on charged and identified particle pseudorapidity distribution is obtained in the two initial state configuration of the oxygen nucleus. The results demonstrated that the effect of initial geometrical configuration is more distinct in the non-central collisions in comparison to the central collisions for both O$-$O and p$-$O collisions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the initial geometrical configuration of the oxygen nucleus (tetrahedral alpha-cluster versus Woods-Saxon distribution) affects final-state particle production in O-O and p-O collisions, but this effect is **more distinct in non-central collisions than in central collisions**, contrary to some prior transport and hydrodynamic simulations that found larger differences in central collisions.

## Method
The authors implemented a tetrahedral alpha-cluster configuration of the oxygen-16 nucleus (four alpha particles at vertices of a regular tetrahedron with side length 3.42 fm) into the PYTHIA8/Angantyr event generator, which uses the Glauber-Gribov formalism without hydrodynamic evolution. Nucleon positions within each alpha cluster were sampled from a three-parameter Fermi (3pF) Woods-Saxon distribution with parameters R=0.964 fm, a=0.322 fm, w=0.517 fm. They compared results to the standard 3pF Woods-Saxon distribution for the whole oxygen nucleus (R=2.608 fm, a=0.513 fm, w=-0.051 fm) for O-O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV and p-O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=9.9 TeV across multiple centrality intervals.

## Key numbers
- O-O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV; p-O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=9.9 TeV
- Tetrahedral alpha-cluster side length: 3.42 fm; rms radius from EQMD: 2.699 fm (agrees with experimental 2.6991 fm)
- Alpha-cluster rms radius obtained in Angantyr: 2.257 +/- 0.098 fm (smaller than EQMD prediction)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for oxygen: R=2.608 fm, a=0.513 fm, w=-0.051 fm
- Alpha-cluster 3pF parameters: R=0.964 fm, a=0.322 fm, w=0.517 fm
- For O-O 0-5% central: average number of participating nucleons <N_part> = 26.14 +/- 2.53 (Woods-Saxon) vs 27.37 +/- 2.34 (alpha-cluster)
- For O-O 60-80% peripheral: <N_part> = 4.66 +/- 2.58 (Woods-Saxon) vs 7.71 +/- 3.47 (alpha-cluster)
- For p-O 0-5% central: <N_part> = 5.44 +/- 2.64 (Woods-Saxon) vs 5.47 +/- 2.66 (alpha-cluster)
- For p-O 60-80% peripheral: <N_part> = 2.32 +/- 1.24 (Woods-Saxon) vs 2.58 +/- 1.38 (alpha-cluster)
- Minimum distance between nucleons inside same oxygen nucleus: >0.9 fm (default hard core radius in Angantyr)

## Context
This work extends studies of initial geometry effects in heavy-ion collisions, previously established for deformed nuclei like uranium-238 and xenon-129 (Sharma et al. references), to the oxygen nucleus using the alpha-cluster hypothesis originating from Gamow (1930). The results contradict findings from relativistic transport models and relativistic hydrodynamics simulations (references 44, 46) which observed larger differences in central collisions and almost no dependence at forward pseudorapidities; the Angantyr model shows significant differences at forward rapidities for non-central collisions. The work enables future studies incorporating QCD-based color reconnection and rope hadronization mechanisms, and suggests that forward-rapidity measurements of J/psi, Upsilon, and beam remnants in LHC Run-3 O-O and p-O collisions could probe the oxygen nuclear structure and gluon saturation.
