# The bosonic algebraic approach applied to the $[QQ][\bar{Q}\bar{Q}]$ tetraquarks

> The exact eigenenergies of the $T_{4c}=[cc][\bar{c}\bar{c}]$, $T_{4b}=[bb][\bar{b}\bar{b}]$, and $T_{2[bc]}=[bc][\bar{b}\bar{c}]$ tetraquarks are calculated within the extended transitional Hamiltonian approach, in which the so-called Bethe \emph{ansatz} within an infinite-dimensional Lie algebra is used. We fit the parameters appearing in the transitional region from phenomenology associated with potential candidates of tetraquarks. The rotation and vibration transitional theory seems to provide a better description of heavy tetraquarks than other attempts within the same formalism. Our results indicate that the pairing strengths are large enough to provide binding; an extended comparison with the current literature is also performed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the bosonic algebraic approach, using an infinite-dimensional Lie algebra and Bethe ansatz within an extended transitional Hamiltonian, can provide mass spectra of fully-heavy tetraquarks that agree fairly with other theoretical approaches, but the results are conditional on fitting phase parameters from meson-meson thresholds and the control parameters cannot all be set to 1 for heavy antiquarks except in the T4b case.

## Method

The authors apply an affine SU(1,1) algebraic technique within an sl-boson system to describe tetraquarks as diquark-antidiquark pairs, using a Hamiltonian constructed from Casimir operators of the U(9) to SO(10) transitional chain. They solve the eigenvalue problem via a Bethe ansatz wave function expressed as a Fourier-Laurent expansion, where the c-number parameters are determined through a set of Gaudin-like equations. The phase parameters c_Q_i are extracted by fitting to meson-meson thresholds for each tetraquark system, with values ranging between 0 and 1 to interpolate between vibrational (U(9)) and rotational (SO(10)) limits.

## Key numbers

* T4c = [cc][cbar cbar] tetraquark: ground state masses are 5.978 GeV (0++), 6.155 GeV (1+-), and 6.263 GeV (2++), with phase parameters c_Q1=0.92, c_Q2=1, c_Q3bar=c_Q4bar=0.
* T4b = [bb][bbar bbar] tetraquark: ground state masses are 18.752 GeV (0++), 18.805 GeV (1+-), and 18.920 GeV (2++), with phase parameters c_Q1=0.97, c_Q2=1, c_Q3bar=1, c_Q4bar=0.
* T2bc = [bc][bbar cbar] tetraquark: masses include 12.359 GeV (0++ scalar diquark), 12.503 GeV (0++ axial-vector diquark), 12.016 GeV (1+-), 12.155 GeV (1++), 12.896 GeV (1+-), and 12.897 GeV (2++), with phase parameters c_Q1=c_Q2=1, c_Q3bar=c_Q4bar=0.
* The T4c 0++ state lies 0.01 GeV above the eta_c(1S)eta_c(1S) threshold and 0.216 GeV below the J/psi(1S)J/psi(1S) threshold.
* The T4b 0++ state lies 0.045 GeV below the eta_b(1S)eta_b(1S) threshold and 0.168 GeV below the Upsilon(1S)Upsilon(1S) threshold.
* The boson number N is taken to be 100, and the parameter alpha is set to 1.5.

## Context

This work extends the interacting boson model originally developed by Arima and Iachello (1975) for nuclear structure, and the U(4) model for mesons proposed by Pan et al. (2006), to fully-heavy tetraquark systems. The results are compared with numerous prior calculations including potential models (e.g., Karliner and Rosner 2017, Anwar et al. 2017), QCD sum rules (e.g., Chen et al. 2016, Wang et al. 2017), and lattice QCD (Hughes et al. 2017), showing that the algebraic approach can reproduce masses in the range of other theoretical predictions. The method enables future application to more general multiquark systems and could help guide experimental searches for fully-heavy tetraquarks at LHCb and CMS.
