# Magnetic effects of QCD parameters from finite energy sum rules

> One of the advantages of the finite energy sum rules is the fact that every operator in the operator product expansion series can be selected individually by the use of an appropriate kernel function which removes other operator poles. This characteristic is maintained by QCD systems in the presence of external homogeneous magnetic field, providing interesting information about the magnetic evolution of QCD and hadronic parameters. In this work finite energy sum rules are applied on QCD in the light quark sector, combining axial and pseudoscalar channels in the presence of an external homogeneous magnetic field, obtaining the magnetic evolution of the light quark masses, pion mass, the pion decay constant, the gluon condensate and the continuum hadronic threshold.

## Key claim

Finite energy sum rules (FESR) can be extended to QCD in the presence of an external homogeneous magnetic field, allowing the magnetic evolution of hadronic and QCD parameters to be extracted individually by selecting appropriate kernel functions, but the results depend on which additional input (e.g., NJL-model pion mass, constant quark mass, or constant \(m_q/m_\pi^2\)) is chosen to close the system of four equations with six unknowns.

## Method

The authors apply FESR to three current correlators in the light-quark sector: the axial-vector correlator \(\Pi_{\mu\nu}^A\), the axial-pseudoscalar correlator \(\Pi_{5\nu}\), and the pseudoscalar correlator \(\psi_5\). They use kernels \(K(s)=s^{N-1}\) with \(N=1,2\) to isolate specific terms in the operator product expansion (OPE), and expand quark and pion propagators in powers of the magnetic field \(B\) up to order \(B^2\). The FESR contour integrals are evaluated in the frame \(p_\perp=0\), and infrared divergences from magnetic contributions are controlled by keeping finite quark masses and adjusting Feynman parameter integration limits via the condition \(\mathcal{M}^2 < s_0\).

## Key numbers

* The vacuum hadronic threshold is \(\sqrt{s_0(0)} = 0.82\) GeV; it rises to \(\sqrt{s_0(B)} \approx 1.2\) GeV at \(eB \approx 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) for the NJL pion mass input or the constant \(m_q/m_\pi^2\) condition.
* The maximum magnetic field strength for the constant quark mass scenario is \(eB \approx 0.35\) GeV\(^2\).
* The gluon condensate from the constant \(m_q/m_\pi^2\) condition deviates from lattice QCD results by less than 15% at the maximum allowed field \(eB \approx 0.5\) GeV\(^2\).
* The FESR equations include magnetic corrections proportional to \((eB)^2\) with logarithmic terms such as \(\frac{2}{9}(eB)^2[10\ln(s_0/m_q^2)-27]\) in the second sum rule.

## Context

This work extends the FESR approach of Dominguez (2018) to include external magnetic fields, building on earlier FESR studies in the chiral limit by Ayala (2015). The results are compared with Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model calculations by Coppola (2018) and with lattice QCD simulations by Bali (2012), as well as with chiral perturbation theory results by Andersen (2012). The method enables future exploration of additional condensates such as the chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic components of the gluon condensate and the polarization condensate \(\langle \bar q\sigma_{12} q\rangle\).
