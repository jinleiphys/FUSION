# Solving the $\hat{q}$ puzzle with $x$ and scale dependence

> We present here a possible resolution of the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ puzzle by exploring the momentum fraction $x$ and scale $Q^2$ dependence of $\hat{q}$, in addition to temperature dependence. We explore the momentum broadening of the jet due to Glauber gluon exchange with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). This has led us to define the momentum fraction $x$ for the partons in the QGP. We also show here, for the first time, possible forms of the parton distribution function (PDF) of the QGP. The QGP-PDF as input in $\hat{q}$ has been used to fit the PHENIX and CMS data simultaneously. We observe that the scale evolution of the QGP-PDF and the energy of the jet are the missing ingredients responsible for the enhancement of $\hat{q}$ at the same temperature in RHIC collisions compared to LHC collisions.

## Key claim

The jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ puzzle, where $\hat{q}/T^3$ appears larger at RHIC than at LHC for the same temperature, can be resolved by introducing an integrated momentum fraction $x$ dependence and scale $Q^2$ dependence in $\hat{q}$, without invoking any non-trivial temperature dependence in $\hat{q}/T^3$.

## Method

The authors calculate the hadronic tensor for a hard quark produced by a virtual photon striking a nucleon in a large nucleus, then propagating through the medium and undergoing a single Glauber gluon exchange with a second nucleon. They extract $\hat{q}$ from this calculation and identify that it inherits the $x$ and $Q^2$ dependence of the nucleon parton distribution function (PDF) of the target nucleon. They generalize this to a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium by defining a momentum fraction $x$ using the QGP mass degree of freedom $m_{QGP} \sim gT$, with an upper bound set by the nucleon mass $M_N \approx 1$ GeV. The QGP-PDF is parameterized in Feynman-Field form $G(x_N, Q^2=1\text{ GeV}^2) = N x_N^a (1-x_N)^b$, and the parameters $N, a, b$ are fit to simultaneously reproduce the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ data from PHENIX (0-10% centrality) and CMS (0-5% centrality) using a higher-twist multiple scattering, multiple emission formalism.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon mass $M_N = 1$ GeV is used as the upper bound to define the momentum fraction $x$ for partons inside a QGP degree of freedom.
* The input QGP-PDF is parameterized at scale $Q^2 = 1$ GeV$^2$ in the form $G(x_N, Q^2=1\text{ GeV}^2) = N x_N^a (1-x_N)^b$, where $N, a, b$ are fit parameters.
* Combined chi-square fits to $R_{AA}$ data at RHIC and LHC simultaneously yield a band of QGP-PDFs with $\chi^2_{dof} < 7.0$ that reproduce the data.
* The calculation of azimuthal anisotropy $v_2$ uses no further parameter tuning beyond the $R_{AA}$ fit, and shows fair agreement with experimental data at both RHIC and LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends the JET Collaboration (2014) phenomenological studies that found the free parameter in $\hat{q}/T^3$ is larger at RHIC than at LHC, known as the $\hat{q}$ puzzle. It contrasts with the approach of Gyulassy and collaborators (2015), who suggested a non-trivial upward cusp-like temperature dependence, and with Carlota and collaborators (2016), who argued the free parameter is sensitive to center-of-mass energy rather than local temperature. The resolution enables simultaneous description of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ at both RHIC and LHC using a single QGP-PDF input, without separate normalizations of $\hat{q}$ at different collision energies.
