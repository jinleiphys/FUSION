# Mapping the $N = 40$ Island of Inversion: Precision Mass Measurements of Neutron-rich Fe Isotopes

> Nuclear properties across the chart of nuclides are key to improving and validating our understanding of the strong interaction in nuclear physics. We present high-precision mass measurements of neutron-rich Fe isotopes performed at the TITAN facility. The multiple-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometer (MR-ToF-MS), achieving a resolving power greater than $600\,000$ for the first time, enabled the measurement of $^{63-70}$Fe, including first-time high-precision direct measurements ($δm/m \sim 10^{-7}$) of $^{68-70}$Fe, as well as the discovery of a long-lived isomeric state in $^{69}$Fe. These measurements are accompanied by both mean-field and ab initio calculations using the most recent realizations which enable theoretical assignment of the spin-parities of the $^{69}$Fe ground and isomeric states. Together with mean-field calculations of quadrupole deformation parameters for the Fe isotope chain, these results benchmark a maximum of deformation in the $N = 40$ island of inversion in Fe, and shed light on trends in level densities indicated in the newly-refined mass surface.

## Key claim

High-precision mass measurements of neutron-rich Fe isotopes at the TITAN facility, combined with mean-field and ab initio calculations, benchmark a maximum of deformation in the N = 40 island of inversion for Fe and reveal a long-lived isomeric state in 69Fe, though the spin-parity assignments for the 69Fe ground and isomeric states remain tentative and are proposed based on theoretical interpretation rather than direct experimental determination.

## Method

Mass measurements of 63-70Fe were performed using the TITAN multiple-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometer (MR-ToF-MS) at TRIUMF's ISAC facility, operating at a resolving power greater than 600,000 for the first time. Fe isotopes were produced via a 480 MeV proton beam on a UCx target and selectively ionized using TRIUMF's resonant ionization laser ion source (TRILIS) with a two-step resonant laser excitation scheme. Time-of-flight spectra were converted to mass spectra using time-resolved calibration, and peak centroids were determined by fitting hyper-EMG functions, with systematic uncertainties totaling approximately 8.6 x 10^-8.

## Key numbers

* Mass excess of 67Fe measured as -46014.8(8.7) keV, deviating from the JYFLTRAP Penning trap measurement by approximately 32 sigma (306.3(9.5) keV difference)
* First high-precision direct measurements of 68Fe, 69Fe, and 70Fe with relative mass precision delta m/m ~ 10^-7: mass excess values of -44100.9(5.6) keV, -39504(11) keV, and -37053(12) keV respectively
* Discovery of a long-lived isomeric state in 69Fe with excitation energy Ex = 222(14) keV, with an estimated half-life on the order of 100-200 ms
* Mass resolution of the MR-ToF-MS exceeding R > 600,000, achieved with a total time-of-flight of approximately 16 ms over 974 to 1008 isochronous turns
* Systematic uncertainty of the MR-ToF-MS system totals approximately 8.6 x 10^-8, dominated by non-ideal switching of mirrors (7.0 x 10^-8), time-resolved calibrations (3.7 x 10^-8), and ion-ion interactions (3.3 x 10^-8 per detected ion)

## Context

This work extends previous mass measurements of neutron-rich Fe isotopes, which had lower precision compared to neighboring isotope chains, and provides the first high-precision direct measurements of 68-70Fe at an ISOL facility. The results contradict a recent Penning trap measurement of 67Fe by Canete (2020) and show a smooth reduction in the odd-even staggering of the neutron pairing gap as N = 42 is approached, in contrast to previous results from Canete (2020) and Meisel (2020). The measurements enable benchmarking of mean-field calculations using the universal Woods-Saxon Hamiltonian from Gaamouci (2021) and ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations, and together with the discovered isomer in 69Fe, shed light on the structure of the N = 40 island of inversion in the Fe isotope chain.
