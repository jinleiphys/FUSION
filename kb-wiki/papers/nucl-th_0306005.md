# Microscopic HFB+QRPA predictions of dipole strength for astrophysics applications

> Large-scale QRPA calculations of the E1 strength are performed on top of HFB calculations in order to derive the radiative neutron capture cross sections for the whole nuclear chart. The spreading width of the GDR is taken into account by analogy with the second-RPA (SRPA) method. The accuracy of HFB+QRPA model based on various Skyrme forces with different pairing prescription and parameterization is analyzed. It is shown that the present model allows to constrain the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with the GDR data and to provide quantitative predictions of dipole strengths.

## Key claim
The HFB+QRPA model, when combined with a second-RPA (SRPA) treatment of the spreading width, can provide quantitative predictions of E1-strength functions across the nuclear chart, but the accuracy depends critically on the choice of Skyrme force: forces with a low isoscalar effective nucleon mass (M*_s/M ≈ 0.8) give the best agreement with experimental GDR data, while the density dependence of the pairing interaction has only a minor impact.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale QRPA calculations of the E1 dipole strength on top of HFB ground states for approximately 8300 nuclei between the proton and neutron drip lines. The spreading width of the giant dipole resonance was treated microscopically using the second-RPA (SRPA) framework, where the QRPA strength is folded with a Lorentzian function representing the self-energy operator, with an interference factor C_ST adjusted to reproduce experimental data. The residual interaction was derived self-consistently from the Skyrme effective interaction used in the HFB calculation, and the calculations assumed spherical symmetry with deformation effects added phenomenologically for deformed nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The HFB+QRPA model with the BSk7 Skyrme force reproduces experimental masses with an rms deviation of 0.676 MeV for the full set of 2135 known masses.
- For the 48 spherical nuclei studied, the BSk7 force gives an rms deviation of 0.445 MeV for the GDR peak energy and 1.169 MeV for the full width at half maximum.
- The peak cross section is reproduced with an rms deviation factor f_rms = 1.18 for the 48 spherical nuclei using the BSk7 force.
- The SRPA shifts the GDR peak energy by approximately Δ(E) ≈ 5 × (1 + C_ST) MeV upwards at energies around E ≈ 15 MeV.
- For the low-energy E1-strength function (4-8 MeV range) compared with 62 experimental data points, the T-dependent formula (with α=3) gives an rms deviation factor f_rms = 2.1, while the T-independent predictions give f_rms = 2.6 and underpredict the strength by an average factor of 1.6.
- The interference factor C_ST adopted for BSk7 is -0.63, and for SLy4 is -0.76.

## Context
This work extends the previous HFBCS+QRPA calculations by Goriely et al. (2002) by replacing the BCS pairing treatment with the more rigorous HFB framework and replacing the phenomenological Lorentzian damping of the QRPA strength with a microscopic SRPA treatment of the spreading width. The paper demonstrates that Skyrme forces fitted exclusively to ground-state masses (the BSk series from Samyn et al., 2002-2003) can also be constrained by GDR excitation data, enabling quantitative predictions of dipole strengths for exotic neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis calculations. The results enable more reliable extrapolations of radiative neutron capture cross sections for the approximately 8300 nuclei between the proton and neutron drip lines.
