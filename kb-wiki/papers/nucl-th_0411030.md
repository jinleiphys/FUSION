# Polarized Proton Pionic Capture in Deuterium as a Probe of 3N Dynamics

> The proton analyzing power Ay in pion production reaction pd --> pi0 3He has been calculated including one- and two-body meson production mechanisms with a proper treatment of the three-nucleon dynamics and an accurate solution of the 3N bound-state problem for phenomenological two-nucleon potentials. In the region around the Delta resonance, the structure of the analyzing power can be understood once interference effects among amplitudes describing intermediate Delta N formation in different orbital states are considered along with the additional interference with the S-wave pion production amplitudes. Then, the inclusion of three-nucleon dynamics in the initial state produces the structure of the analyzing power that has been observed experimentally.

## Key claim

The structure of the proton analyzing power \(A_y\) in the \(\overrightarrow{p} d \rightarrow \pi^0 {}^{3}\mathrm{He}\) reaction around the \(\Delta\) resonance can be reproduced theoretically only when three-nucleon dynamics in the initial proton-deuteron state is included, together with interference effects among \(\Delta N\) partial waves up to \(l_\Delta = 2\) and \(S\)-wave pion production amplitudes.

## Method

The authors calculated the reaction amplitude using a model that includes one-body (\(\pi NN\) vertex) and two-body meson production mechanisms, with explicit \(\Delta\) excitation via \(\pi\)- and \(\rho\)-exchange, and \(S\)-wave two-body pion rescattering. The three-nucleon bound-state wave functions were obtained from phenomenological two-nucleon potentials (Bonn-A, Bonn-B, Paris), and the initial-state interaction (ISI) was treated via the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) scheme with realistic nucleon-nucleon transition matrices. The matrix elements were evaluated in a large partial-wave basis of 464 three-nucleon states, with total angular momenta up to \(7/2\) for both parities.

## Key numbers

- The isovector coupling constant used was \(\lambda_V / 4\pi = 0.045\).
- The \(\pi NN\) coupling constant was \(f_{\pi NN}^2 / 4\pi = 0.0735\).
- The \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling constant was \(f_{\pi N\Delta}^2 / 4\pi = 0.32\).
- The maximum orbital momentum in the \(\Delta N\) subsystem required to describe the analyzing power was \(l_\Delta = 2\); calculations with \(l_\Delta = 0\) were insufficient, and convergence was reached at \(l_\Delta = 2\) (no need to go to \(l_\Delta = 4\)).
- The additional peak in \(A_y\) appears at \(\Theta_{\mathrm{c.m.}} \simeq 100^\circ\) when the proton lab energy increases from \(E_{\mathrm{lab}} = 350\) MeV toward higher energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Canton and Schad (2000) and others that treated \(\Delta\) dynamics in the three-nucleon system, but which did not extensively include the three-nucleon dynamics in the initial state. It addresses a long-standing challenge: the experimental structure of \(A_y\) measured by Cameron (1984) and others had not been theoretically interpreted before. The results enable future studies of spin observables near threshold to clarify the origin of the scalar-isoscalar \(\pi N\) interaction, and provide a foundation for understanding more complex processes such as double-pion or heavier meson production in few-nucleon systems.
