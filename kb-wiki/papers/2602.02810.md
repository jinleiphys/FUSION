# Corrections to the Smoothness and On-Shell Approximations in Femtoscopy and Coalescence

> Relativistic heavy-ion collisions produce femtometer-scale sources whose space-time structure can be constrained using two-particle femtoscopic correlations. Standard implementations rely on the smoothness and on-shell approximations, which effectively remove the relative momentum dependence of the particle emission function. We explore the validity of these approximations by deriving model-independent expansions that quantify the leading corrections for femtoscopy and coalescence with arbitrary sources and final-state interactions. The resulting first- and second-order correction terms can be evaluated with essentially the same numerical complexity as the usual Koonin-Pratt expressions; for angle-averaged correlations the first-order contributions vanish by symmetry. We illustrate the framework with explicit calculations in a blast-wave source model. For parameter sets representative of pp and PbPb fits at LHC energies, the corrections are at or below the percent level for pp correlations and deuteron coalescence.

## Key claim

The smoothness and on-shell approximations in femtoscopy and coalescence, which remove the relative momentum dependence of the particle emission function, introduce corrections that are at or below the percent level for proton-proton correlations and deuteron coalescence at LHC energies, but the paper derives model-independent expansions showing these corrections can be evaluated with the same numerical complexity as the standard Koonin-Pratt expressions.

## Method

The authors derive a Taylor expansion of the source function in relative momentum \(q\) (the smoothness expansion) and a kinematic expansion for the on-shell approximation, keeping terms up to second order. They apply these expansions to the Koonin-Pratt formula for femtoscopic correlations and the analogous coalescence formula, expressing the corrections in terms of kernels derived from scattering wavefunctions and source function moments. The framework is then tested numerically using a blast-wave source model with parameters representative of pp and PbPb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- For parameter sets representative of pp and PbPb fits at LHC energies, the corrections are at or below the percent level for pp correlations and deuteron coalescence.
- The first-order corrections in the smoothness expansion vanish by symmetry for angle-averaged correlations.
- For free identical particles, the smoothness correction to the angle-averaged correlation function is \(C(q) = C_s(q) + \frac{q^2}{3}\mathrm{tr}(\varepsilon)(1-C_s(q)) + \mathcal{O}(q^3\varepsilon^3)\), where \(\mathrm{tr}(\varepsilon)\) is the trace of the second-order source moment.
- The on-shell corrections begin at order \(q^2\) and are purely kinematic, with the leading correction involving a boost of order \(\beta_i = -\frac{\kappa q^2}{2(p^0)^3}p_i\) where \(\kappa = (m_1+m_2)^2/(4m_1m_2)\).

## Context

This work extends prior assessments of the smoothness approximation validity by Pratt (1997) and corrections for free bosonic correlations by Alt et al. (2008), providing a general expansion applicable to arbitrary sources and final-state interactions. The derived framework enables model-independent quantification of approximation errors in femtoscopic analyses of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and can be used to assess the reliability of the coalescence-femtoscopy relation proposed by Blum and Takimoto (2019) and Bellini and Blum (2023).
