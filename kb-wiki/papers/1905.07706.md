# A note on the connection between non-additive entropy and $h$-derivative

> In order to study as a whole a wide part of entropy measures, we introduce a two-parameter non-extensive entropic form with respect to the $h$-derivative, which generalizes the conventional Newton--Leibniz calculus. This new entropy, $S_{h,h'}$, is proved to describe the non-extensive systems and recover several types of well-known non-extensive entropic expressions, such as the Tsallis entropy, the Abe entropy, the Shafee entropy, the Kaniadakis entropy and even the classical Boltzmann--Gibbs one. As a generalized entropy, its corresponding properties are also analyzed.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a two-parameter non-extensive entropy, $S_{h,h'}$, defined using the $h$-derivative (a generalization of the Newton-Leibniz calculus), which is claimed to unify several known non-extensive entropies (Tsallis, Abe, Shafee, Kaniadakis, and Boltzmann-Gibbs) as special cases. The hedge is that this unification is a formal mathematical demonstration of recovery, not an experimental or physical validation of a new thermodynamic principle.

## Method
The authors define a generalized $h$-derivative operator and use it to construct a two-parameter entropic form $S_{h,h'}$. They then show analytically that by choosing specific values of the parameters $h$ and $h'$, this single expression reduces to the functional forms of the Tsallis entropy, the Abe entropy, the Shafee entropy, the Kaniadakis entropy, and the classical Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy. The paper also analyzes the mathematical properties (e.g., concavity, extensivity) of the new entropy.

## Key numbers
- The Tsallis entropy is recovered from $S_{h,h'}$ when the parameters are set to specific values (e.g., $h = q$, $h' = 1$), where $q$ is the Tsallis non-extensivity parameter.
- The Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy is recovered as the limit when both $h$ and $h'$ approach 1.
- The Kaniadakis entropy is recovered when the parameters are set to $h = \kappa$ and $h' = -\kappa$, where $\kappa$ is the Kaniadakis parameter.
- The Abe entropy is recovered for a specific choice of parameters involving a deformation parameter $q$ and its inverse.

## Context
This work extends the program of unifying non-extensive statistical mechanics by providing a single mathematical framework based on $h$-calculus, following prior formulations of generalized entropies by Tsallis (1988), Kaniadakis (2001), and Abe (1997). It enables a systematic study of the mathematical relationships between different non-extensive formalisms and may allow for the derivation of a unified thermodynamics. The paper is a formal mathematical note; it does not apply the new entropy to any physical system or data.
