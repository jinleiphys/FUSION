# Self-consistent treatment of the self-energy in nuclear matter

> The influence of hole-hole propagation in addition to the conventional particle-particle propagation, on the energy per nucleon and the momentum distribution is investigated. The results are compared to the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations with a continuous choice and conventional choice for the single-particle spectrum. The Bethe-Goldstone equation has been solved using realistic $NN$ interactions. Also, the structure of nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter is evaluated. All the self-energies are calculated self-consistently. Starting from the BHF approximation without the usual angle-average approximation, the effects of hole-hole contributions and a self-consistent treatment within the framework of the Green function approach are investigated. Using the self-consistent self-energy, the hole and particle self-consistent spectral functions including the particle-particle and hole-hole ladder contributions in nuclear matter are calculated using realistic $NN$ interactions. We found that, the difference in binding energy between both results, i.e. BHF and self-consistent Green function, is not large. This explains why is the BHF ignored the 2h1p contribution.

## Key claim

The difference in binding energy between the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation and the self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) approach that includes hole-hole (2h1p) contributions is not large, which explains why the BHF approximation can safely ignore the 2h1p contribution. The authors find that the gain in binding energy due to the 2h1p components is about 0.5 MeV per nucleon at saturation density.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions (CD-Bonn and Bonn C potentials) for nuclear matter, without the usual angle-average approximation. They calculate the nucleon self-energy self-consistently within the BHF approximation and then extend it to include the two-hole one-particle (2h1p) contribution within the SCGF framework, using a shifted single-particle spectrum that ensures self-consistency at the Fermi momentum. From the self-consistent self-energy, they compute the hole and particle spectral functions and the momentum distribution via the Dyson equation.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy gain from including 2h1p components: about 0.5 MeV per nucleon at saturation density.
- Fermi momentum used for most calculations: k_F = 1.36 fm^{-1}.
- Additional Fermi momenta examined for single-particle potentials: k_F = 2.04 fm^{-1} and k_F = 2.72 fm^{-1}.
- Fermi momentum for comparison with Benhar et al. quasi-particle strength results: k_F = 1.33 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the BHF approximation by including hole-hole (2h1p) ladder contributions within the self-consistent Green's function formalism, following earlier SCGF work by Ramos (various years), Dewulf (various years), Dickhoff (various years), and Gad (various years). The results confirm earlier findings by Grange (various years) and Frga (various years) that the 2h1p contribution to binding energy is small, validating the standard BHF approach. The paper also addresses the parabolic approximation for the single-particle potential, noting that Muther and collaborators (various years) and Baldo and Fiasconaro (various years) found it introduces 1-2 MeV uncertainty near saturation density, but the authors find the parabolic approximation is valid at high densities. The self-consistent occupation probabilities are compared to experimental data from Ciofi (various years) and to the full many-body calculation of Benhar et al. (various years).
