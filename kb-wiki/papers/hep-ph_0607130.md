# Backward DVCS and Proton to Photon Transition Distribution Amplitudes

> We analyse deeply-virtual Compton scattering on a proton target, gamma* P -> P' gamma in the backward region and in the scaling regime. We define the transition distribution amplitudes which describe the proton to photon transition. Model-independent predictions are given to test this description, for current or planned experiments at JLab or by Hermes.

## Key claim

The paper defines proton-to-photon Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) for deeply-virtual Compton scattering in the backward region, but the factorization and scaling predictions are strictly valid only in the limit of zero transverse momentum transfer (\(\Delta_T=0\)) and at leading twist; the full 16 TDAs are reduced to 4 in this limit, and the amplitude scaling as \((\alpha_S(Q^2))^2/Q^4\) is a model-independent result only under that condition.

## Method

The authors perform a leading-twist factorization of the amplitude for \(\gamma^* P \to P' \gamma\) in the backward kinematics (small \(u = (p_\gamma - p_1)^2\)), using light-cone operator product expansion. They define the 16 independent proton-to-photon TDAs from the Fourier transform of the three-quark matrix element between a proton and a photon state, then impose gauge invariance to reduce the number to 16 (from 24) and further specialize to the \(\Delta_T=0\) limit where only 4 TDAs survive. The hard-scattering amplitude is computed at leading order in \(\alpha_S\) with the final-state proton described by its distribution amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The amplitude for \(\gamma^* P \to P' \gamma\) scales as \(\frac{(\alpha_S(Q^2))^2}{Q^4}\) at leading twist in the backward region (up to logarithmic corrections from TDA and DA evolution).
* At \(\Delta_T=0\), only 4 independent TDAs survive from the full set of 16, corresponding to the helicity-conserving processes \(P(\uparrow) \to uud(\uparrow \downarrow \downarrow) + \gamma(\uparrow)\) and \(P(\uparrow) \to uud(\uparrow \uparrow \uparrow) + \gamma(\downarrow)\).
* The momentum fractions \(x_i\) satisfy \(\sum_i x_i = 2\xi\) with support within \([-1+\xi, 1+\xi]\) for \(\xi > 0\).

## Context

This work extends the TDA formalism previously applied to proton-to-pion transitions (Lansberg, Pire, Szymanowski, 2005) and to meson-to-photon transitions (Pire, Szymanowski, 2006) to the case of a proton target emitting a real photon. It provides a framework complementary to the forward DVCS studies using generalized parton distributions (GPDs) pioneered by Mueller et al. (1994) and Radyushkin (1996). The results enable experimental tests at JLab and Hermes, where the absence of Bethe-Heitler background in the backward region allows direct access to the TDAs, and also apply to \(P \bar P \to \gamma^* \gamma\) reactions planned at GSI with PANDA and PAX.
