# Formation of bremsstrahlung in an absorptive QED/QCD medium

> The radiative energy loss of a relativistic charge in a dense, absorptive medium can be affected significantly by damping phenomena. The effect is more pronounced for large energies of the charge and/or large damping of the radiation. This can be understood in terms of a competition between the formation time of bremsstrahlung and a damping time scale. We discuss this competition in detail for the absorptive QED and QCD medium, focusing on the case in which the mass of the charge is large compared to the in-medium mass of the radiation quanta. We identify the regions in energy and parameter space, in which either coherence or damping effects are of major importance for the radiative energy loss spectrum. We show that damping phenomena can lead to a stronger suppression of the spectrum than coherence effects.

## Key claim

The paper shows that damping phenomena in an absorptive QED/QCD medium can suppress the radiative energy loss spectrum more strongly than coherence effects (the LPM effect), but this suppression is conditional on the competition between the radiation formation time and a damping time scale, with the effect being most pronounced for large charge energies and/or large damping rates.

## Method

The authors model the absorptive medium using a complex squared index of refraction \(n^2(\omega)=1-m^2/\omega^2+2i\Gamma/\omega\), where \(m\) is the in-medium mass and \(\Gamma\) the damping rate of radiation quanta. They derive the radiative energy loss spectrum per unit length for a relativistic charge in linear response theory, then analyze the competition between the photon formation time \(t_f\) (from single or multiple scatterings) and the damping time \(t_d\simeq 1/\Gamma\) by examining limiting expressions of the spectrum ratio relative to the Bethe-Heitler result. The analysis is performed for the case where the charge mass \(M\) is large compared to the in-medium mass \(m\) of the radiation quanta.

## Key numbers

* The Bethe-Heitler bremsstrahlung spectrum result is \(-\frac{d^2W_{BH}}{dzd\omega} = \frac{\alpha\hat{q}}{3\pi M^2}\), where \(\hat{q}\) is the mean accumulated transverse momentum squared per unit time.
* The relative deviation from the BH result is less than 10% for \(\mathcal{A} < 0.026\), where \(\mathcal{A} = \hat{q}E^2/(3\omega M^4)\).
* The critical Lorentz factor for the onset of multiple scattering dominance is \(\gamma_c^{(1)} \sim mM^2/\hat{q}\), and \(\gamma_c^{(2)} \sim M^3/\hat{q}\).
* The damping time scale is \(t_d \simeq (\omega|n_i|\beta)^{-1}\), which for \(m \ll xE\) and \(\Gamma \ll xE\) becomes \(t_d \simeq 1/\Gamma\).
* The boundary between damping-dominated and coherence-dominated regimes occurs at \(\Gamma \sim \hat{q}/M^2\): for \(\Gamma < \hat{q}/M^2\) coherence effects can appear, while for \(\Gamma > \hat{q}/M^2\) damping effects dominate and coherence plays no role.

## Context

This work extends the classic analysis of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect (Landau 1953, Migdal 1956) and the Ter-Mikaelian (TM) effect (Ter-Mikaelian) on bremsstrahlung in dense media by incorporating damping phenomena. The authors build on their own previous work (Bluhm 2011) on radiative energy loss in absorptive plasmas and provide a semi-quantitative framework using time-scale competition that can be applied to gluon bremsstrahlung in hot QCD plasma. The analysis enables identification of parameter regions where damping rather than coherence determines the suppression of the radiation spectrum, which is relevant for understanding jet quenching in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
