# Bayesian Analysis and Analytic Continuation of Scattering Amplitudes from Lattice QCD

> We present a novel procedure for analyzing the lattice-QCD spectrum via the finite-volume formalism to obtain constraints on multi-hadron scattering amplitudes at both real and complex energies. This approach combines a Bayesian reconstruction of the scattering amplitude on the real axis with Nevanlinna interpolation for analytic continuation to complex-valued energies. The method is non-parametric, inherently accounting for parametrization dependence within the uncertainty. We demonstrate the applicability of this approach using both toy data and real lattice-QCD data in resonant systems from the HadSpec and BaSc collaborations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-parametric Bayesian method combined with Nevanlinna–Pick interpolation to extract scattering amplitudes from lattice-QCD finite-volume spectra, claiming that this approach inherently accounts for parametrization dependence within the uncertainty, but the method requires that the conformal maps correctly bound the codomain of the inverse scattering amplitude for the rigorous Nevanlinna bounds to apply, and the Bayesian uncertainty quantification depends on the choice of prior hyperparameters such as correlation length and prior width.

## Method

The authors first use a Bayesian framework with a Gaussian process prior (radial basis function kernel) to constrain the inverse K-matrix at real energies from finite-volume energy levels, sampling the posterior via reweighting from a linearized approximation or Hybrid Monte Carlo. They then apply Nevanlinna–Pick interpolation, mapping the problem to the unit disk via conformal maps and using the Schur algorithm to construct analytic functions that interpolate the scattering amplitude, thereby bounding the possible locations of resonance poles in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- For the toy model inspired by the ρ resonance, the method is demonstrated using both toy data and real lattice-QCD data from the HadSpec and BaSc collaborations.
- The Bayesian analysis uses a prior covariance kernel with correlation length ℓ_c ~ ΔE, where ΔE is the typical energy spacing between adjacent nodes.
- The Monte Carlo integration uses O(100) samples, with the statistical uncertainty from sampling reported as negligible compared to the posterior width.
- The Bayes factor for comparing different priors is computed via the average reweighting factor ⟨w_*⟩_*, requiring the prior and approximate posterior densities to be normalized Gaussian distributions.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism of Lüscher (1986, 1990) and subsequent developments by introducing a non-parametric alternative to standard parametric fits (e.g., effective-range expansion, polynomial or rational-function models) that have been used by the HadSpec and BaSc collaborations. The method is inspired by Bayesian reconstruction of inverse problems using Gaussian processes (Horak 2021, Del Debbio 2021) and by Nevanlinna–Pick interpolation for analytic continuation (PhysRevLett.126.056402, Bergamaschi 2023). It enables controlled uncertainty quantification for resonance pole positions in systems such as the σ, the ρ, and the Λ(1405), and can be applied to multi-channel scattering where standard parametric approaches suffer from model dependence.
