# Molecular components in $\mathbf{D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)}$ and $\mathbf{D_{s1}(2460)}$ mesons

> Different experiments have confirmed that the $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ mesons are very narrow states located, respectively, below the $DK$ and $D^{\ast}K$ thresholds. This is markedly in contrast with the expectations of naive quark models and heavy quark symmetry. We address the mass shifts of the $c\bar{s}$ ground states with quantum numbers $J^{P}=0^{+}$ ($D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$) and $J^{P}=1^{+}$ ($D_{s1}(2460)$) using a nonrelativistic constituent quark model in which quark-antiquark and meson-meson degrees of freedom are incorporated. The quark model has been applied to a wide range of hadronic observables and thus the model parameters are completely constrained. We observe that the coupling of the $0^{+}$ $(1^{+})$ meson sector to the $DK$ $(D^{\ast}K)$ threshold is a key feature in lowering the masses of the corresponding $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ states predicted by the naive quark model, but also in describing the $D_{s1}(2536)$ meson as the $1^{+}$ state of the $j_{q}^{P}=3/2^{+}$ doublet predicted by heavy quark symmetry and thus reproducing its strong decay properties. Two features of our formalism cannot be address nowadays by other approaches: the coupling of the $D$-wave $D^{\ast}K$ threshold in the $J^{P}=1^{+}$ $c\bar{s}$ channel and the computation of the probabilities associated with different Fock components in the physical state.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the experimentally observed masses of the \(D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)\) and \(D_{s1}(2460)\) mesons can be explained by coupling the naive quark-model \(c\bar{s}\) states to the \(DK\) and \(D^{\ast}K\) thresholds, respectively, rather than requiring an exotic interpretation; the authors hedge that their model's parameters are completely constrained by prior hadronic observables, so the mass shifts are not fitted but emerge from the dynamics.

## Method
The authors used a nonrelativistic constituent quark model (CQM) that incorporates both quark-antiquark (\(c\bar{s}\)) and meson-meson (\(DK\), \(D^{\ast}K\)) degrees of freedom in a coupled-channel formalism. They computed the mass shifts of the \(J^{P}=0^{+}\) and \(J^{P}=1^{+}\)\(c\bar{s}\) ground states by coupling them to the \(DK\) and \(D^{\ast}K\) thresholds, including both \(S\)-wave and \(D\)-wave couplings for the \(1^{+}\) channel, and extracted the probabilities of each Fock component in the physical states.

## Key numbers
- The naive quark-model splitting \(m_{D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)} - m_{\overline{1S}}\) (where \(m_{\overline{1S}} = \frac{1}{4}(m_{D_s} + 3m_{D_s^{\ast}})\)) is 437 MeV without one-loop OGE corrections, dropping to 309 MeV with \(\alpha_s^2\) corrections, compared to the experimental value of about 230 MeV.
- After coupling the \(0^{+}\)\(c\bar{s}\) state to the \(DK\) threshold, the splitting becomes 249.6 MeV, in good agreement with experiment.
- The probability composition of the \(D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)\) physical state is 66% \(q\bar{q}\) and 34% \(DK\).
- For the \(J^{P}=1^{+}\) sector, when the \(D^{\ast}K\) threshold is coupled, the meson-meson component is around 50% for both the \(D_{s1}(2460)\) and \(D_{s1}(2536)\) mesons.

## Context
This work extends the suggestion by van Beveren (2003) that coupling \(c\bar{s}\) states to \(DK\) and \(D^{\ast}K\) thresholds dynamically lowers their masses, and it agrees with lattice QCD results from Lang (2014) that found similar mass shifts when including meson-meson interpolating fields. The authors' approach uniquely includes the \(D\)-wave coupling of the \(D^{\ast}K\) threshold in the \(J^{P}=1^{+}\) channel, which lattice QCD has not yet computed, and it enables future studies of the Fock-component probabilities in other exotic candidates.
