# Reaction rate weighted multilayer nuclear reaction network

> Nuclear reaction rate ($λ$) is a significant factor in the process of nucleosynthesis. A multi-layer directed-weighted nuclear reaction network in which the reaction rate as the weight, and neutron, proton, $^4$He and the remainder nuclei as the criterion for different reaction-layers is for the first time built based on all thermonuclear reactions in the JINA REACLIB database. Our results show that with the increase of the stellar temperature ($T_{9}$), the distribution of nuclear reaction rates on the $R$-layer network demonstrates a transition from unimodal to bimodal distributions. Nuclei on the $R$-layer in the region of $λ= [1,2.5\times10^{1}]$ have a more complicated out-going degree distribution than the one in the region of $λ= [10^{11},10^{13}]$, and the number of involved nuclei at $T_{9} = 1$ is very different from the one at $T_{9} = 3$. The redundant nuclei in the region of $λ= [1, 2.5\times10^{1}]$ at $T_{9} = 3$ prefer $(γ,p)$ and $({γ,α})$ reactions to the ones at $T_{9}=1$, which produce nuclei around the $β$ stable line. This work offers a novel way to the big-data analysis on nuclear reaction network at stellar temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a multi-layer directed-weighted nuclear reaction network using reaction rates as edge weights for the first time, and finds that the distribution of reaction rates on the R-layer network transitions from unimodal to bimodal as stellar temperature increases, with the second peak at high reaction rates corresponding to photodisintegration reactions that become important at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors built a four-layer directed-weighted nuclear reaction network (N-layer for neutron, P-layer for proton, H-layer for ⁴He, and R-layer for remainder nuclei) using all thermonuclear reactions from the JINA REACLIB V2.0 database, which contains 8048 nuclei and 82851 nuclear reactions. Reaction rates were calculated using the seven-parameter REACLIB formula at different stellar temperatures T₉, and the R-layer network was analyzed for its topological properties including out-going degree distributions and reaction type classification at T₉ = 0.1, 1, and 3.

## Key numbers

- The REACLIB V2.0 database contains 8048 nuclei and 82851 nuclear reactions.
- Reaction rates in the R-layer network range from 10⁻¹⁰⁰ to 10⁵⁰.
- At T₉ = 1, the single peak of reaction rate distribution is centered at λ = 10⁰·⁶⁴⁵⁴.
- At T₉ = 3, the bimodal distribution has peaks at λ = 10⁰·⁶⁷¹⁴ and λ = 10¹¹·⁴¹⁸.
- In the λ = [10⁰, 2.5×10¹] region at T₉ = 1, there are 7331 total reactions, including 243 (γ,n), 35 (γ,p), 83 (γ,α), 162 β⁺, and 2487 β⁻ reactions.
- In the λ = [10⁰, 2.5×10¹] region at T₉ = 3, there are 5781 total reactions, including 144 (γ,n), 77 (γ,p), 162 (γ,α), 111 β⁺, and 2232 β⁻ reactions.
- In the λ = [10¹¹, 10¹³] region at T₉ = 3, there are 2546 total reactions, including 2324 (γ,n), 221 (γ,p), and 1 (γ,α) reaction, with no β decay reactions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-weighted multi-layer nuclear reaction network study (Liu et al., 2016) by incorporating reaction rates as edge weights, enabling analysis of reaction path probabilities rather than just topological connectivity. The approach provides a novel big-data statistical method for analyzing nuclear reaction networks at stellar temperatures, complementing traditional precise calculations of individual reaction rates (e.g., NACRE database by Xu et al.; REACLIB by Cyburt et al.). The method enables future studies of how reaction rate distributions change across different stellar burning stages (He-burning at T₉ ≈ 0.15-0.23, Ne-burning at T₉ ≈ 1.4-1.7, Si-burning at T₉ ≈ 3.0) and can be improved as more exotic nuclear reaction data become available from radioactive ion beam facilities.
