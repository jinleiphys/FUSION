# Forward trijet production in proton-nucleus collisions: gluon initiated channel

> In this paper, we present the results for the forward trijet production differential cross section in the gluon initiated channel at leading order in proton-nucleus collisions. The calculations are carried out within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, and in the dilute-dense approximation, using effective vertices for the quark and gluon propagators interacting with the small-$x$ background gluon field. We employ the covariant perturbation theory approach and disentangle the amplitudes into regular and instantaneous contributions. Our results are expressed as convolutions of multiparton color correlators of light-like Wilson lines and perturbative impact factors, organized in compact expressions in terms of the ``bare" topologies of the contributing diagrams. The gluon initiated channel receives contributions from a $q\bar{q}g$ and a $ggg$ final state. Interestingly, when considering the $ggg$ final state, we observe, for the first time, that the four-gluon vertex topology follows a structure similar to the instantaneous contributions. Furthermore, when integrating (one of) the real gluon(s) in the final state, we identify that: i) the rapidity divergence contributes to the real part of JIMWLK of the leading-order color correlator; and ii) the collinear divergence contribute to the evolution of initial-state gluon parton distribution function, and final state fragmentation functions. These results validate the dilute-dense hybrid formalism at one-loop order, and are key ingredients towards the complete next-to-leading order calculation of dijet/dihadron production in proton--nucleus collisions.

## Key claim
This paper presents the first complete calculation of the forward trijet production differential cross section in the gluon-initiated channel at leading order in proton-nucleus collisions within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, without imposing constraints on the transverse momenta of the final-state particles, as long as the jets are forward to justify small-x methods. The results validate the dilute-dense hybrid formalism at one-loop order and are key ingredients toward the complete next-to-leading order calculation of dijet/dihadron production in proton-nucleus collisions.

## Method
The authors computed the trijet production amplitudes for both the \(q\bar{q}g\) and \(ggg\) final states using covariant perturbation theory within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, employing effective vertices for quark and gluon propagators interacting with the small-x background gluon field in the dilute-dense approximation. They disentangled the amplitudes into regular and instantaneous contributions, expressed the results as convolutions of multiparton color correlators of light-like Wilson lines and perturbative impact factors, and organized them in compact expressions in terms of the "bare" topologies of the contributing diagrams. The authors then studied the logarithmic rapidity divergence arising from integrating one of the final-state gluons, verifying consistency with the real part of JIMWLK evolution of the leading-order cross section, and also examined the DGLAP regime associated with initial- and final-state collinear divergences.

## Key numbers
- The leading-order dijet cross section kernel for the \(g \to q\bar{q}\) channel is \(\mathcal{K}_{q\bar{q},LO}(z_q,z_{\bar q},\boldsymbol{r}_{xy}, \boldsymbol{r}_{x'y'}) = z_qz_{\bar{q}} (z_q^2 + z_{\bar{q}}^2) \frac{\boldsymbol{r}_{xy}\cdot \boldsymbol{r}_{x'y'}}{r^2_{xy}r^2_{x'y'}}\), where \(z_q\) and \(z_{\bar q}\) are longitudinal momentum fractions of the outgoing quark and antiquark.
- The leading-order dijet cross section kernel for the \(g \to gg\) channel is \(\mathcal{K}_{gg,LO}(z_1,z_2,\boldsymbol{r}_{xy}, \boldsymbol{r}_{x'y'}) = 4(z_1^2+z_2^2+z_1z_2)^2\frac{\boldsymbol{r}_{xy} \cdot \boldsymbol{r}_{x'y'}}{r^2_{xy}r^2_{x'y'}}\), where \(z_1\) and \(z_2\) are longitudinal momentum fractions of the outgoing gluons.
- The \(g\to q\bar{q}\) splitting function squared and summed over polarizations and helicities gives \(\sum_{\lambda,\sigma}\,[\Gamma^{\sigma,\lambda}_{g\rightarrow q \bar{q}}(z, 1-z)]^2 = 4 P_{g\to q\bar{q}}(z)\) where \(P_{g\to q\bar{q}}(z) \equiv \frac{z^2 + (1-z)^2}{2}\).

## Context
This work extends previous leading-order calculations of dijet production in proton-nucleus collisions within the Color Glass Condensate framework, which were performed by Baier (2005), Marquet (2007), Dominguez (2011), and Iancu (2013). It builds upon the real corrections for the quark-initiated channel computed by Iancu (2018, 2021) and the covariant perturbation theory approach used by Roy (2019) and Caucal (2021). The results enable the completion of the full next-to-leading order calculation of dijet and dihadron production in proton-nucleus collisions, which is essential for reliably separating saturation effects from perturbative radiation and hard scattering dynamics in experimental data from forward rapidity measurements at colliders.
