# Nuclear response for the Skyrme effective interaction with zero range tensor terms

> The effects of a zero-range tensor component of the effective interaction on nuclear response functions are determined in the so-called RPA approach. Explicit formula in the case of symmetric homogeneous isotropic nuclear matter are given for each spin-isospin excitation channel. It is shown for a typical interaction with tensor couplings that the effects are quantitatively important, mainly in vector channels.

## Key claim

The paper derives the full linear response (RPA) formulas for symmetric nuclear matter including both zero-range tensor and spin-orbit terms in the Skyrme effective interaction, showing that tensor effects are quantitatively important mainly in vector channels, but the actual impact depends on the specific linear combinations of the tensor coupling constants \(t_e\) and \(t_o\).

## Method

The authors start from the Skyrme effective interaction containing central, spin-orbit, and zero-range tensor terms, then compute the particle-hole (p-h) matrix elements for each component. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the RPA framework for symmetric homogeneous isotropic nuclear matter at zero temperature, obtaining closed-form expressions for the response function in each spin-isospin channel \((S,I,M)\). The tensor interaction couples vector channels with different spin projections, while the spin-orbit interaction couples scalar (\(S=0\)) and vector (\(S=1\)) channels, leading to a system of coupled equations that is reduced to three systems of four coupled equations each.

## Key numbers

* The dynamical structure functions \(S^{(\alpha)}(q,\omega)\) are calculated at the saturation density \(\rho = \rho_\mathrm{sat} = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and for transferred momentum \(q = k_F\).
* The calculations use the T44 parametrization of the Skyrme interaction from Lesinski et al. (2007), with parameters given in Table I of the paper.
* The response functions are plotted as a function of \(\omega\) (in MeV) and are given in units of \(\mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the linear response formalism of Garcia-Recio et al. (1992) and Navarro et al. (1999) for central Skyrme forces, and the spin-orbit treatment of Margueron et al. (2006), by adding the zero-range tensor terms proposed by Skyrme (1956, 1959). It enables the systematic identification of parameter regions that lead to finite-size instabilities in nuclei, which is crucial for fitting new Skyrme energy density functionals with tensor couplings, as discussed by Lesinski et al. (2007) and Zalewski et al. (2008).
