# Positronium on the light front

> Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ) is a newly developed nonperturbative approach, aiming at solving relativistic bound systems based on the Hamiltonian formalism of light-front dynamics. In this work, we introduce its application to the positronium system at strong coupling, $α= 0.3$, with a dynamical photon mediating the interaction between the positron and the electron. Nonperturbative mass renormalization is needed to cancel the fermion self-energy divergence. Here, we present the resulting mass spectrum, light-front wave functions (LFWFs), and the photon distribution inside positronium.

## Key claim

The authors apply Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ) to positronium at a strong coupling of α = 0.3, retaining a dynamical photon as an explicit degree of freedom, and demonstrate that sector-dependent renormalization successfully cancels fermion self-energy divergences to yield a reasonable mass spectrum and light-front wave functions.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front Hamiltonian eigenvalue equation for positronium in a truncated Fock space containing the |e⁺e⁻⟩ and |e⁺e⁻γ⟩ sectors. The basis uses a two-dimensional harmonic oscillator for transverse momentum and discretized plane waves with periodic/antiperiodic boundary conditions for longitudinal momentum, with truncation parameters N_max and K controlling the ultraviolet and infrared cutoffs. Nonperturbative mass renormalization via Fock-sector-dependent counterterms is applied to cancel fermion self-energy divergences, and the resulting Hamiltonian matrix is diagonalized numerically.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant: α = 0.3 (strong coupling regime)
- Basis truncation parameters: N_max = K − 1 = 32
- The lowest eight states identified in the M_J = 0 column, ordered from lowest to highest: 1¹S₀, 1³S₁, 2¹S₀, 2³S₁, 2³P₀, 2³P₁, 2¹P₁, 2³P₂
- The spectrum and LFWFs of the |e⁺e⁻⟩ sector reasonably agree with those from the effective one-photon-exchange calculation of PhysRevD.91.105009

## Context

This work extends prior BLFQ applications to positronium that used an effective one-photon-exchange interaction (PhysRevD.91.105009) by now including a dynamical photon as an explicit degree of freedom, enabling direct access to the photon content inside the bound state. The approach is a test case for including higher Fock sectors in QED before extending to QCD systems such as heavy quarkonium and light mesons, where an effective confining potential between fermions would be added. The results enable future studies of photon distribution functions and the dependence of observables on the regulators of the theory.
