# Three-dimensional femtoscopy for proton pairs in heavy-ion collisions

> Femtoscopy, a technique of measuring the size and the dynamics of the system created in heavy-ion collisions is used extensively in experiments at RHIC, LHC, SPS, and the FAIR/GSI. Analysis for pairs of pions is most common, due to their large abundance in the collisions, as well as well-understood theoretical formalism and advanced experimental methodology. It is usually performed in three dimensions in longitudinally co-moving reference frame of the pair, to maximize the amount of information obtained about the source. Similar three-dimensional analysis has also been performed for charged and neutral kaons. In contrast analyses for all other pair types -- either for pairs of identical protons, or for pairs of non-identical particles are only one-dimensional. The abundance of heavier particles is lower than for pions, while the theoretical formalism for such correlations, as well as practical experimental methodology is limited to one-dimensional case up to now. This work identifies specific challenges arising in the analysis of three-dimensional proton-proton femtoscopy and proposes a theoretical formalism to analyze them, as well as specific experimental methods to extract and fit correlations. A test and validation of these methods is performed on correlations simulated in the LHyquid+Therminator2 model, and demonstrates the feasibility of such analysis. The caveats in the theoretical interpretation of the results are discussed, by comparing the fitted values of femtscopic radii with the true characteristics of the source extracted directly from the model.

## Key claim

This work claims that a full three-dimensional femtoscopic analysis for proton pairs is feasible, but only when using a spherical harmonics decomposition combined with numerical fitting methods, and the extracted femtoscopic radii must be interpreted with caveats because they do not directly correspond to the true source characteristics extracted from the model.

## Method

The author simulates proton-proton correlations using the LHyquid3D hydrodynamic model combined with Therminator2 hadronization code, tuned to Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. The correlation functions are constructed directly in spherical harmonics representation without intermediate three-dimensional histograms, using the full proton-proton wave function including quantum statistics, Coulomb, and strong final state interactions with singlet and triplet components. The CorrFit package is then used to perform numerical fitting of the spherical harmonics components C_0^0, C_2^0, and C_2^2 to extract three independent Gaussian source radii in the Bertsch-Pratt decomposition in LCMS.

## Key numbers

- The Bohr radius for a proton pair is 57.6 fm
- Scattering length and effective radius for singlet state: f_0^S = 7.77 fm, d_0^S = 2.77 fm
- Scattering length and effective radius for triplet state: f_0^T = -5.4 fm, d_0^T = 1.7 fm
- The fitting range for all three radius parameters is from 1.4 fm up to 4.25 fm
- Five centrality ranges are used: 0-10%, 10-20%, 20-30%, 30-40%, and 40-50%
- Four pair transverse momentum ranges are used: 0.4-0.8 GeV/c, 0.8-1.0 GeV/c, 1.0-1.2 GeV/c, and 1.2-1.6 GeV/c
- The characteristic peak in the proton-proton correlation function appears around k* = 20 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional femtoscopic methodology previously applied only to pions and kaons (ALICE 2015, ALICE 2017) to the case of identical protons, where final state interactions are dominant rather than a small correction. It builds on the spherical harmonics decomposition approach developed by Kisiel (2009) and the CorrFit package (Kisiel 2004), and enables experiments at RHIC, LHC, SPS, and FAIR/GSI to perform full three-dimensional analysis of proton sources, which is particularly relevant for studying baryonic degrees of freedom in the QCD phase diagram and for the CBM experiment at FAIR and upgraded ALICE at LHC.
