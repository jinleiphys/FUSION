# Sequential deconfinement of quark flavors in neutron stars

> We suggest a scenario where the three light quark flavors are sequentially deconfined under increasing pressure in cold asymmetric nuclear matter as found, e.g., in neutron stars. The basis for our analysis is a chiral quark matter model of Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type with diquark pairing in the spin-1 single flavor (CSL), spin-0 two flavor (2SC) and three flavor (CFL) channels. We find that nucleon dissociation sets in at about the saturation density, n_0, when the down-quark Fermi sea is populated (d-quark dripline) due to the flavor asymmetry induced by beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. At about 3n_0 u-quarks appear and a two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase is formed. The s-quark Fermi sea is populated only at still higher baryon density, when the quark chemical potential is of the order of the dynamically generated strange quark mass. We construct two different hybrid equations of state (EoS) using the Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach and the EoS by Shen et al. in the nuclear matter sector. The corresponding hybrid star sequences have maximum masses of, respectively, 2.1 and 2.0 M_sun. Two- and three-flavor quark-matter phases exist only in gravitationally unstable hybrid star solutions in the DBHF case, while the Shen-based EoS produce stable configurations with a 2SC phase-component in the core of massive stars. Nucleon dissociation via d-quark drip could act as a deep crustal heating process, which apparently is required to explain superbusts and cooling of X-ray transients.

## Key claim
The paper suggests that in cold asymmetric nuclear matter (as found in neutron stars), the three light quark flavors are sequentially deconfined under increasing pressure, with down quarks appearing first at about saturation density, followed by up quarks at about three times saturation density, and strange quarks only at still higher densities. This scenario depends on the flavor asymmetry induced by beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, and the authors note that the existence of the single-flavor d-quark CSL phase may be ruled out if the U(1)_A symmetry is broken with a six-point 't Hooft interaction, depending on the parametrization of the NJL model and the nuclear matter model used.

## Method
The authors use a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type chiral quark matter model with diquark pairing in the spin-1 single flavor (CSL), spin-0 two flavor (2SC), and three flavor (CFL) channels. They construct two different hybrid equations of state by combining this quark model with two nuclear matter models: the Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach with the relativistic Bonn A potential, and the relativistic mean-field based equation of state by Shen et al. For a given baryon chemical potential, they compare three phase constructions: homogeneous nuclear matter, homogeneous quark matter, and charge-neutral mixtures of nuclear matter with quark matter phases, selecting the thermodynamically favored solution. They then solve the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for hydrostatic equilibrium to calculate compact star sequences.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon dissociation (d-quark dripline) sets in at about the saturation density, n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Up quarks appear at about 3 n_0, where a two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase forms
- Maximum masses of hybrid star sequences: 2.1 M_sun for the DBHF-based equation of state, and 2.0 M_sun for the Shen-based equation of state
- The transition to the nearly symmetric three-flavor CFL phase occurs at mu_B approximately 1600 MeV
- Estimated energy release from partial conversion of nuclear matter to DBHF+CSL hybrid matter at the crust-core boundary: 0.6 to 6 MeV per accreted nucleon, based on a d-quark admixture fraction chi between 0.001 and 0.01 and a jump of the d-quark mass gap by 300 MeV at the chiral transition

## Context
This work extends earlier hybrid star models by Klahn et al. (2006) and Blaschke et al. (2005) that used DBHF nuclear matter with three-flavor chiral quark models, but introduces the novel concept of sequential flavor deconfinement. The scenario contrasts with commonly used bag-model equations of state, which are flavor blind and cannot address sequential deconfinement. The paper also builds on the superburst mechanism proposed by Page et al. (2005) for strange stars with CFL quark matter cores, and suggests that the d-quark drip effect at the crust-core boundary could serve as an alternative deep crustal heating mechanism, potentially explaining superbursts and cooling of X-ray transients without requiring a strange matter core. The work enables further investigation of whether the single-flavor d-CSL phase is realized in nature, and what observable signatures (such as modified cooling behavior or r-mode stability) might distinguish hybrid stars with sequential deconfinement from ordinary neutron stars.
