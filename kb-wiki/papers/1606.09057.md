# First Measurement of Collectivity of Coexisting Shapes based on Type II Shell Evolution: The Case of $^{96}$Zr

> Background: Type II shell evolution has recently been identified as a microscopic cause for nuclear shape coexistence. Purpose: Establish a low-lying rotational band in 96-Zr. Methods: High-resolution inelastic electron scattering and a relative analysis of transition strengths are used. Results: The B(E2; 0_1^+ -> 2_2^+) value is measured and electromagnetic decay strengths of the secdond 2^+ state are deduced. Conclusions: Shape coexistence is established for 96-Zr. Type II shell evolution provides a systematic and quantitative mechanism to understand deformation at low excitation energies.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the low-lying $0_2^+$ and $2_2^+$ states in $^{96}$Zr form a deformed rotational band coexisting with a nearly spherical ground state, and identifies type II shell evolution driven by the tensor force as the stabilizing mechanism for this shape coexistence. The claim is hedged by the fact that the shell model calculation reproduces the dominant components of the wave functions but cannot describe the weak mixing (on the order of a few percent) between the spherical and deformed configurations, which is instead inferred from a two-state model analysis.

## Method

High-resolution inelastic electron scattering was performed at the S-DALINAC using a 43 MeV (and one measurement at 69 MeV) electron beam on a $^{96}$Zr-enriched target, with data taken at scattering angles of 81, 93, 117, and 141 degrees. The peak areas for the $0_1^+ \rightarrow 2_2^+$ transition were extracted relative to the $0_1^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+$ transition using a Plane Wave Born Approximation (PWBA) analysis, where Coulomb corrections cancel to better than 1% over the momentum transfer range. The ratio of $B(E2)$ values was determined by a chi-squared minimization of the PWBA expression, and combined with literature branching ratios and multipole mixing ratios to deduce all electromagnetic decay strengths of the $2_2^+$ state.

## Key numbers

* $B(E2; 2_2^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = 7.4(2.3)~e^2\text{fm}^4 = 0.26(8)$ W.u. for $^{96}$Zr, measured via electron scattering at momentum transfers $q = 0.28, 0.31, 0.40, 0.59~\text{fm}^{-1}$.
* $B(E2; 2_2^+ \rightarrow 0_2^+) = 36(11)$ W.u., establishing the $2_2^+$ state as a collective excitation on top of the deformed $0_2^+$ state.
* Quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2 \approx 0.24$ for the deformed $0_2^+$ and $2_2^+$ states, estimated from the $B(E2; 0_2^+ \rightarrow 2_2^+)$ value assuming a rigid axial symmetric rotor.
* Ratio $T(2_2^+,2_1^+) = \sqrt{B(E2;2_2^+\rightarrow 0_1^+)/B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow 0_1^+)} = 0.34^{+0.05}_{-0.04}$.
* $B(M1; 2_2^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+) = 0.14(5)~\mu_N^2$, indicating a small but finite mixing between spherical and deformed configurations.
* Mixing amplitudes from two-state model: $\alpha^2 = 99.8\%$ (spherical component in $0_1^+$), $\beta^2 = 0.2\%$ (deformed component in $0_1^+$), $\gamma^2 = 97.5\%$ (spherical component in $2_1^+$), $\delta^2 = 2.5\%$ (deformed component in $2_1^+$), with mixing matrix element $V_{mix} = 76$ keV.

## Context

This work extends the study of shape coexistence in Zr isotopes, which show a rapid shape phase transition from spherical ground states in $^{90-98}$Zr to deformed ground states in $^{100}$Zr and heavier isotopes (Federman, 1979). Shape coexistence had been suggested in $^{98}$Zr (Wu, 1994; Heyde, 2011) and reported in $^{94}$Zr (Chakraborty, 2013), but with considerable mixing of coexisting structures. The present measurement provides the first electromagnetic observables that quantitatively support type II shell evolution (Otsuka, 2014) as the stabilizing mechanism for shape coexistence in $^{96}$Zr, enabling future studies of configuration-dependent shell evolution in other mass regions.
