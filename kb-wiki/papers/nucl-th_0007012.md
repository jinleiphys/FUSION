# Nucleon form factors in the canonically quantized Skyrme model

> The explicit expressions for the electric, magnetic, axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors of the nucleons are derived in the {\it ab initio} quantized Skyrme model. The canonical quantization procedure ensures the existence of stable soliton solutions with good quantum numbers. The form factors are derived for representations of arbitrary dimension of the SU(2) group. After fixing the two parameters of the model, $f_π$ and $e$, by the empirical mass and electric mean square radius of the proton, the calculated electric and magnetic form factors are fairly close to the empirical ones, whereas the the axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors fall off too slowly with momentum transfer.

# Nucleon form factors in the canonically quantized Skyrme model

## Key claim
The canonically quantized Skyrme model yields well-defined nucleon form factors for arbitrary SU(2) representations, but while the calculated electric and magnetic form factors are fairly close to empirical data after fixing the two model parameters to the proton mass and electric radius, the axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors fall off too slowly with momentum transfer.

## Method
The authors canonically quantize the Skyrme model using collective coordinates that separate time and spatial variables, then derive explicit expressions for the Noether currents of the model. The chiral angle F(r) is determined by self-consistent numerical variation of the energy expression, and the form factors are computed as Fourier transforms of the charge and current densities for representations with j = 1/2, 1, 3/2 and the reducible representation 1 ⊕ 1/2 ⊕ 1/2 ⊕ 0. Relativistic boost corrections are applied to all form factors.

## Key numbers
- The two model parameters f_π and e are fixed by reproducing the empirical proton mass (939 MeV) and electric mean square radius (0.735 fm²) for each representation.
- For the reducible representation (1 ⊕ 1/2 ⊕ 1/2 ⊕ 0): f_π = 60.3 MeV, e = 4.31; for j = 1/2: f_π = 64.8 MeV, e = 4.76; for j = 1: f_π = 59.4 MeV, e = 4.19; for j = 3/2: f_π = 57.5 MeV, e = 3.86.
- Neutron electric mean square radius: -0.269 fm² (reducible), -0.368 fm² (j = 1/2), -0.249 fm² (j = 1), -0.210 fm² (j = 3/2); experimental value is -0.114 fm².
- Proton magnetic moment: 2.32 (reducible), 1.96 (j = 1/2), 2.39 (j = 1), 2.54 (j = 3/2); experimental value is 2.79.
- Neutron magnetic moment: -1.73 (reducible), -1.37 (j = 1/2), -1.81 (j = 1), -1.99 (j = 3/2); experimental value is -1.91.
- Axial coupling constant g_A: 0.84 (reducible), 0.73 (j = 1/2), 0.87 (j = 1), 0.95 (j = 3/2); experimental value is 1.26.
- Effective pion mass from quantization: 171 MeV (reducible), 110 MeV (j = 1/2), 191 MeV (j = 1), 246 MeV (j = 3/2); experimental pion mass is 138 MeV.
- Proton magnetic mean square radius: 0.594 fm² (reducible), 0.618 fm² (j = 1/2); experimental value is 0.719 fm².
- Neutron magnetic mean square radius: 0.609 fm² (reducible), 0.687 fm² (j = 1/2); experimental value is 0.637 fm².

## Context
This work extends the semiclassical Skyrme model calculations of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) and the systematic form factor study of Braaten, Tse, and Willcox (1986) by implementing canonical quantization, which yields stable soliton solutions with good quantum numbers and generates a finite effective pion mass without explicit chiral symmetry breaking terms. The results enable comparison of different SU(2) representations as phenomenological models, with the reducible representation (corresponding to the SU(3) octet restricted to SU(2)) giving the best agreement with experimental form factor data, and open the question of whether expanded versions including vector meson fields could achieve closer agreement with the axial and pseudoscalar form factors.
