# Inelastic electron scattering-induced nuclear excitation rates and dynamics in $^{229}$Th

> In the present work, we investigate the excitation rates and population dynamics of $^{229}$Th nuclei induced by inelastic electron scattering, focusing on how electron energy, flux, and ionic charge state influence the excitation process of the nuclei. Using the Dirac Hartree-Fock-Slater method, we calculate cross sections for both the isomeric state (8.36 eV) and the second-excited state (29.19 keV) of $^{229}$Th over a wide range of ionic charge states and electron energies. Our results demonstrate that these factors significantly impact the nuclear excitation efficiency. The effect of indirect excitation through the second-excited state on enhancing the accumulation of nuclei in the isomeric state cannot be ignored. By applying rate equations to model the temporal evolution of nuclear populations, we show that under optimal conditions, up to 10\% of $^{229}$Th$^{4+}$ ions can be accumulated in the isomeric state. These findings provide important insights for optimizing electron-nucleus interactions, contributing to the development of $^{229}$Th-based nuclear clocks and relevant precision measurement applications.

## Key claim

The authors show that under optimal low-energy electron beam conditions (8 eV central energy, 1 eV energy spread), up to 10% of Th$^{4+}$ ions can be accumulated in the isomeric state, but this result depends on the specific choice of ionic charge state (Th$^{4+}$) and the assumption that internal conversion is suppressed in highly charged ions, extending the isomeric state half-life to 1740 seconds.

## Method

The authors used the Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater (DHFS) method to calculate self-consistent potentials and radial wavefunctions for neutral Th and various Th ions (Th$^{1+}$, Th$^{4+}$, Th$^{50+}$, Th$^{90+}$). They computed inelastic electron scattering cross sections for excitation to both the isomeric state (8.36 eV) and the second-excited state (29.19 keV) over an incident electron energy range from threshold to 2 MeV. Rate equations were then applied to model the temporal evolution of nuclear state populations, incorporating direct excitation to the isomeric state, indirect excitation via the second-excited state (with a 90% branching ratio), and decay processes.

## Key numbers

- Isomeric state excitation energy: 8.36 eV; second-excited state energy: 29.19 keV
- For neutral Th, the isomeric state excitation cross section peaks at $4.17 \times 10^{-27}$ cm$^2$ at 10.8 eV incident electron energy, rising from $1.10 \times 10^{-27}$ cm$^2$ at 8.4 eV
- For neutral Th, the isomeric state excitation cross section reaches a minimum of $5.17 \times 10^{-31}$ cm$^2$ around 300 keV
- For Th$^{4+}$ under low-energy excitation ($E_r = 8$ eV, $\epsilon_e = 1$ eV), the effective excitation rate $\lambda_{\mathrm{eff}}$ reaches $3.24 \times 10^{-5}$ s$^{-1}$
- For Th$^{4+}$ at $E_r = 262$ keV, $\lambda_{\mathrm{eff}}$ drops to $1.93 \times 10^{-8}$ s$^{-1}$
- Under low-energy excitation (30 eV), Th$^{4+}$ reaches a steady-state isomeric population of approximately $1.85 \times 10^3$ out of $2.36 \times 10^4$ target nuclei (about 10%)
- Under high-energy excitation (30 keV), Th$^{4+}$ reaches a steady-state isomeric population of 3.64
- Neutral Th isomeric state half-life: 7 microseconds; Th$^{4+}$ isomeric state half-life: 1740 seconds
- Second-excited state half-life: 82.2 picoseconds; branching ratio from second-excited state to isomeric state: approximately 90%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Nuclear Excitation by Inelastic Electron Scattering (NEIES) in $^{229}$Th, which had primarily focused on specific energy ranges or direct excitation to the isomeric state (PhysRevC.106.044604, PhysRevC.106.064604). The authors provide a systematic analysis across a broader energy range and multiple charge states, emphasizing the role of the second-excited state in indirect population of the isomeric state. The results enable optimization of electron beam parameters for $^{229}$Th-based nuclear clock applications and also identify low-excitation parameter regions (e.g., around 262 keV) that could reduce NEIES interference in future searches for Nuclear Excitation by Electron Capture (NEEC), which has not yet been experimentally observed (PhysRevLett.130.112501).
