# Effective field theory in the harmonic oscillator basis

> We develop interactions from chiral effective field theory (EFT) that are tailored to the harmonic oscillator basis. As a consequence, ultraviolet convergence with respect to the model space is implemented by construction and infrared convergence can be achieved by enlarging the model space for the kinetic energy. In oscillator EFT, matrix elements of EFTs formulated for continuous momenta are evaluated at the discrete momenta that stem from the diagonalization of the kinetic energy in the finite oscillator space. By fitting to realistic phase shifts and deuteron data we construct an effective interaction from chiral EFT at next-to-leading order. Many-body coupled-cluster calculations of nuclei up to 132Sn exhibit a fast convergence of ground-state energies and radii in feasible model spaces.

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory (EFT) directly in the harmonic oscillator basis, where the interaction matrix elements are evaluated at the discrete momentum eigenvalues of the kinetic energy in a fixed finite oscillator space, and the low-energy coefficients are re-fitted to scattering data; this guarantees ultraviolet convergence by construction, while infrared convergence is achieved by enlarging the model space for the kinetic energy only, without changing the interaction.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-leading order (NLO) by evaluating momentum-space EFT matrix elements at the discrete momenta that are the roots of Laguerre polynomials (the eigenvalues of the squared momentum operator in a finite oscillator space). They fit the low-energy coefficients to reproduce realistic nucleon-nucleon phase shifts (computed via the J-matrix approach in the finite oscillator basis) and deuteron properties. The resulting interaction is then used in coupled-cluster many-body calculations for nuclei up to 132Sn.

## Key numbers

- The oscillator basis truncation is defined by a maximum energy (N_max + 3/2) ℏω; for a given partial wave with angular momentum l, the number of oscillator shells is N = [(N_max - l)/2].
- The UV cutoff of the oscillator-EFT interaction is approximately Λ_UV ≈ √(2(N_max + 7/2))/b, where b = √(ℏ/(mω)) is the oscillator length.
- For the chiral regulator, a high-momentum cutoff Λ_χ = 500 MeV is used with n = 3 in the super-Gaussian form f(q) = exp[-(q/Λ_χ)^(2n)].
- Many-body coupled-cluster calculations of nuclei up to 132Sn converge for ground-state energies and radii in model spaces with N_max = 10 to 14, without further renormalization.
- The error in the Gauss-Laguerre quadrature approximation for matrix elements scales as O((k/Λ)^(2N+l+4)), where k is a typical momentum and Λ is the high-momentum cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the concept of lattice EFT (Lee, 2009) to the harmonic oscillator basis, where the UV cutoff of the interaction is fixed once, analogous to fixing the lattice spacing. It differs from the oscillator-based effective theory (HOBET) of Haxton et al. (2000, 2002, 2007, 2008), which uses a contact-gradient expansion and the Bloch-Horowitz formalism, and from the pion-less EFT in the oscillator basis developed by Stetcu et al. (2007, 2010) and Rotureau et al. (2012), which also employs a contact-gradient expansion with running coupling constants. The approach enables fast convergence in many-body calculations for medium-mass and heavy nuclei without additional renormalization, and it allows the use of existing computational infrastructure for nuclear structure calculations.
