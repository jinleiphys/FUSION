# Perturbative Yukawa theory at finite density: the role of masses and renormalization group flow at two loops

> Yukawa theory at vanishing temperature provides (one of the ingredients for) an effective description of the thermodynamics of a variety of cold and dense fermionic systems. We study the role of masses and the renormalization group flow in the calculation of the equation of state up to two loops within the MSbar scheme. Two-loop integrals are computed analytically for arbitrary fermion and scalar masses, and expressed in terms of well-known special functions. The dependence of the renormalization group flow on the number of fermion flavors is also discussed.

# Perturbative Yukawa theory at finite density: the role of masses and renormalization group flow at two loops

## Key claim

The two-loop thermodynamic potential for cold and dense Yukawa theory can be computed analytically for arbitrary fermion and scalar masses, but the quantitative results depend strongly on the renormalization scale, and the perturbative treatment is only valid when the Landau pole is not reached within the region of investigation.

## Method

The authors computed the thermodynamic potential up to two-loop order in the MSbar scheme using the imaginary time formalism of finite-temperature field theory, then took the zero-temperature limit. Two-loop momentum integrals were evaluated analytically for arbitrary fermion mass m and scalar mass m_phi, with results expressed in terms of dilogarithms and inverse trigonometric functions. Renormalization was implemented via counterterms for the fermion self-energy and boson self-energy evaluated on the mass shell.

## Key numbers

- For a fixed coupling alpha_Y ~ 0.72 and (mu - m) = 0.1 Lambda_MSbar, the two-loop pressure is raised by ~50% compared to the free case.
- For mu = 0.6 Lambda_MSbar, fermions with mass m = 0.4 Lambda_MSbar lower the pressure by a factor of ~1/5 compared to the massless case.
- The sign of the boson mass effect on the pressure inverts at m_phi/m ~ 15 for mu = 4m/3 and Lambda = 10 m.
- At large chemical potentials ((mu - m) ~ 0.25 Lambda_MSbar), interaction effects reduce the pressure by less than ~20%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' preliminary results (Palhares 2007) on the two-loop correction for pressure with one massive fermion flavor and a massless scalar, and their earlier study of RG running for N_F flavors. The analytic results for arbitrary masses go beyond previous calculations that were limited to at least one vanishing mass, as was typical in gauge theories (Kapusta 1979, Freedman 1976, Baluni 1977). The findings enable more realistic effective model calculations for cold dense fermionic systems such as compact stars and color superconducting phases, and can complement lattice simulations of Yukawa theory (Lee 1989, Gerhold 2007) and Higgs-Yukawa models.
