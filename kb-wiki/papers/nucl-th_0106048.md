# Anomalous Spectral Statistics of the Asymmetric Rotor Model

> We investigate the spectral statistics of the asymmetric rotor model (triaxial rigid rotator). The asymmetric top is classically integrable and, according to the Berry-Tabor theory, its spectral statistics should be Poissonian. Surprisingly, our numerical results show that the nearest neighbor spacing distribution $P(s)$ and the spectral rigidity $Δ_3(L)$ do not follow Poisson statistics.

## Key claim

The asymmetric rotor model, although classically integrable and satisfying the Berry-Tabor condition for Poissonian spectral statistics, surprisingly does not exhibit Poisson statistics in its nearest neighbor spacing distribution or spectral rigidity, making it a pathological case like the harmonic oscillator but more interesting because it satisfies the Berry-Tabor conditions.

## Method

The quantum Hamiltonian of the triaxial rigid rotator was split into four submatrices corresponding to different symmetry classes (even/odd parity of quantum number k and symmetric/antisymmetric states). Each truncated submatrix was numerically diagonalized using a Lanczos algorithm with LAPAC code in double precision for fixed angular momentum values J = 1000, 2000, and 4000. The unfolded level spectra were then used to compute the nearest neighbor spacing distribution P(s) and the spectral rigidity Δ₃(L).

## Key numbers

- For J = 1000, the nearest neighbor spacing distribution P(s) shows a peak near s = 1 and nothing elsewhere, deviating from the Poisson prediction P(s) = exp(-s)
- For J = 1000, the spectral rigidity Δ₃(L) follows the Poisson prediction Δ₃(L) = L/15 for small L but saturates to a constant mean value with large fluctuations for larger L, in contrast with the Berry prediction
- The density of levels ρ(E) for J = 1000 displays a high peak at the left-center of the energy interval and a long tail for large energy values
- The behavior of ρ(E), P(s), and Δ₃(L) does not change when increasing J to 2000 or 4000
- Parameters used: a = 1, b = √2, c = √5, and ℏ = 1

## Context

This work extends the known exceptions to the Berry-Tabor theory, which predicts Poissonian spectral statistics for classically integrable systems, with the harmonic oscillator being the most famous pathological case discussed in detail by Berry and Tabor. Unlike the harmonic oscillator, the asymmetric rotor satisfies the Berry-Tabor condition that the Hessian of the Hamiltonian in action variables is non-zero, making this anomaly more significant. The results suggest the presence of a hidden symmetry in the system that has not yet been identified, and the model has been used extensively in describing low-lying states of even-even atomic nuclei.
