# Gamow shell model description of the radiative capture reaction $^8$Li$(n,γ)$$^9$Li

> The $^8$Li$(n,γ)$$^9$Li reaction plays a critical role in several reaction chains leading to the nucleosynthesis of $A>12$ nuclei. Due to unstable nature of $^8$Li and the unavailability of neutron targets, direct measurements of this reaction are exceedingly difficult. Only upper limits of this cross section, provided by the indirect experiments, have been obtained so far. In this work, we use the Gamow shell model (GSM) in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) to study the properties of $^9$Li and the radiative capture reaction $^8$Li$(n,γ)$$^9$Li. In GSM-CC calculations, a translationally invariant Hamiltonian is used with a finite-range two-body interaction tuned to reproduce the low-energy spectra of $^{8-9}$Li. In the calculation of $^8$Li$(n,γ)$$^9$Li cross section, all relevant E1, M1, and E2 transitions from the initial continuum states to the final bound states ${3/2}_1^-$, ${1/2}_1^-$ and the resonance ${5/2}_1^-$ of $^9$Li are included. The GSM-CC approach reproduces the experimental low-energy spectrum, neutron emission threshold, and spectroscopic factors in $^9$Li. The calculated reaction rate is consistent with the experimental upper limit of the reaction rate obtained in the indirect measurements at stellar energies. The GSM-CC calculations suggest that the $^8$Li$(n,γ)$$^9$Li reaction can reduce heavy-element production via the main chain $^7$Li($n,γ$)$^8$Li($α,n$)$^{11}$B($n,γ$)$^{12}$B($β^+$)$^{12}$C. Major contribution to the calculated cross section is given by the direct E1 transition to the ground state of $^8$Li. The contribution of excited states to the reaction rate does not exceed $\sim$18% of the total reaction rate.

## Key claim

The Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) reproduces the experimental low-energy spectrum, neutron emission threshold, and spectroscopic factors in $^9$Li, and the calculated reaction rate is consistent with the experimental upper limit from indirect measurements; however, the GSM-CC calculations suggest that the $^8$Li$(n,\gamma)$$^9$Li reaction can reduce heavy-element production via the main chain $^7$Li($n,\gamma$)$^8$Li($\alpha,n$)$^{11}$B($n,\gamma$)$^{12}$B($\beta^+$)$^{12}$C, with the major contribution coming from direct E1 transition to the ground state of $^8$Li.

## Method

The authors used the Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) with a translationally invariant Hamiltonian and a finite-range Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) two-body interaction tuned to reproduce the low-energy spectra of $^{8-9}$Li. Reaction channels were built by coupling wave functions of the $^8$Li ground state $2_1^+$, first excited state $1^+_1$, and first resonance state $3^+_1$ with neutron projectile partial waves $s_{1/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, $p_{3/2}$, $d_{3/2}$, and $d_{5/2}$, and the cross section included all relevant E1, M1, and E2 transitions from initial continuum states to final bound states ${3/2}_1^-$, ${1/2}_1^-$ and the resonance ${5/2}_1^-$ of $^9$Li.

## Key numbers

* The calculated neutron separation energy in $^9$Li is $S_n^{(\text{th})} = 3.945$ MeV, agreeing with the experimental value $S_n^{(\text{exp})} = 4.062$ MeV.
* The GSM-CC direct neutron radiative capture cross section to the ground state $3/2^-$ of $^9$Li at representative energy $\tilde{E}_n = 0.25$ MeV is 15.23 $\mu$b, compared to experimental upper limits of 19.1$\pm$10.4 $\mu$b (U target) and 17.8$\pm$11.9 $\mu$b (Pb target) from Zecher (1998).
* At $\tilde{E}_n = 0.75$ MeV, the GSM-CC cross section to the ground state is 5.24 $\mu$b, compared to experimental upper limits of 7.9$\pm$5.5 $\mu$b (U target) and 8.1$\pm$6.4 $\mu$b (Pb target).
* The contribution of excited states to the reaction rate does not exceed $\sim$18% of the total reaction rate.
* The calculated ground-state energy of $^8$Li relative to $^4$He is -13.159 MeV, close to the experimental value -12.982 MeV from Tilley (2004).
* The calculated energy of the first resonance in $^8$Li is -11.004 MeV with width 70.1 keV, compared to experimental resonance energy -10.727 MeV and width 33(6) keV from Tilley (2004).
* GSM spectroscopic factors for $^9$Li states: ${3/2}^-_1$ = 0.93, ${1/2}^-_1$ = 0.42, ${5/2}^-_1$ = 0.80, compared to experimental values from Wuosmaa (2005) of 0.90(13), 0.73(15), and 0.93(20), respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical efforts to determine the $^8$Li$(n,\gamma)$$^9$Li cross section, which is critical for inhomogeneous big-bang nucleosynthesis and r-process nucleosynthesis. Prior calculations using the potential model (Mohr 2003), the microscopic cluster model (Descouvemont 1993), the modified potential cluster model (Dubovichenko 2016), and the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC, 2021) have yielded results differing by more than an order of magnitude, with most predictions below the experimental upper limit from Zecher (1998). The GSM-CC approach enables a unified description of both nuclear structure and reaction cross sections, and its results suggest that the $^8$Li$(n,\gamma)$$^9$Li reaction can reduce heavy-element production via the main chain, providing a more reliable theoretical basis for nucleosynthesis network calculations.
