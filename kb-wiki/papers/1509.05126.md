# Quenching of inclusive and tagged b-jets at the LHC

> We present theoretical predictions for the nuclear-induced attenuation of the differential cross sections for inclusive and tagged b-jet production in heavy ion collisions at the LHC. We find that for inclusive b-jet production at high transverse momentum the mass effects are negligible, and that the attenuation is comparable to the one observed for light jets. On the other hand, for isolated-photon and B-meson-tagged b-jets the sample of events with heavy quarks produced at the early stages of the collision is greatly enhanced. Thus, these tagged b-jet final-states have a much more direct connection to the physics of b-quark energy loss. We present theoretical predictions for the quenching of such tagged b-jet events at the LHC and the QGP-induced modification of the related momentum imbalance and asymmetry. We demonstrate that these tagged processes can be used to accurately study the physics of heavy quark production and propagation in dense QCD matter.

## Key claim

The observed similarity between b-jet and light-jet suppression at high transverse momentum does not directly reflect b-quark energy loss, because inclusive b-jets are dominated by gluon splitting contributions (not prompt b-quarks), and heavy quark mass effects become negligible at high \(p_T\). Tagged b-jet processes (photon-tagged or B-meson-tagged) greatly enhance the fraction of b-jets originating from prompt b-quarks, providing a more direct probe of b-quark energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors used Pythia 8 to simulate inclusive and tagged b-jet production in p+p collisions at LHC energies, with b-jets defined via the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm requiring at least one b-quark inside the jet cone. Medium modification in Pb+Pb collisions was calculated using the GLV energy loss formalism, including both radiative energy loss and collisional dissipation, with a probability distribution \(P_{(s)}(\epsilon)\) for energy redistribution and a phase-space Jacobian \(|J_{(s)}(\epsilon)|\) to account for energy retained inside the jet cone. The fractional contributions from different subprocesses (gluon splitting, prompt b-quarks, light flavor generation) were identified through \(2 \to 2\) hard partonic scattering in Pythia.

## Key numbers

- In inclusive b-jet production at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV p+p collisions, the prompt b-quark contribution is only about \(10-20\%\) across a wide \(p_T\) range, while gluon splitting dominates.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for inclusive b-jets in minimum-bias Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV agrees with CMS data within a jet-medium coupling strength \(g^{\rm med} = 1.8 - 2.2\).
- In photon-tagged b-jet production at \(\sqrt{s}=5.1\) TeV, the prompt b-quark fraction reaches about \(50\%\) when the b-jet transverse momentum is below 100 GeV.
- In B-meson-tagged b-jet production at \(\sqrt{s}=5.1\) TeV, the b-quark fraction can reach up to \(70\%\), with \(b+\bar b\) contribution dominating the entire kinematic region.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of heavy flavor suppression in heavy ion collisions, addressing the discrepancy between the dead-cone effect prediction (smaller b-quark energy loss) and experimental data from CMS and STAR showing similar suppression for b-jets and light jets. The authors build on earlier work by Dokshitzer (2001) on the dead-cone effect, Vitev (2005, 2008) on medium-induced parton showers, and their own prior studies (Huang 2013, 2015) on flavor origin of b-jets and tagged processes. The proposed tagged b-jet observables enable future experimental measurements at the LHC to directly constrain heavy-quark energy loss mechanisms in the quark-gluon plasma.
