# Strange hadron production in heavy ion collisions from SPS to RHIC

> Strange particles have been a very important observable in the search for a deconfined state of strongly interacting matter, the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), which is expected to be formed in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. We review the main experimental observations made at the Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) at CERN, Geneva, and at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL). The large amount of recently collected data allows for a comprehensive study of strangeness production as a function of energy and system size. We review results on yields, transverse mass and rapidity spectra, as well as elliptic flow. The measurements are interpreted in the context of various theoretical concepts and their implications are discussed. Of particular interest is the question whether strange particles are in any way sensitive to a partonic phase. Finally, a compilation of experimental data is provided.

# Strange hadron production in heavy ion collisions from SPS to RHIC

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of multi-strange baryon yields in heavy ion collisions relative to proton-proton collisions is consistent with the formation of a quark-gluon plasma, but the original argument based on ideal gas partonic equilibration times is insufficient; the data can also be explained by hadronic mechanisms such as multi-meson fusion or Hagedorn states near the phase boundary, and the interpretation remains ambiguous because transport models without partonic degrees of freedom fail to reproduce multi-strange yields unless artificial modifications (e.g., reduced quark masses or increased string tension) are introduced.

## Method

The authors review experimental data on strange particle yields, transverse mass spectra, rapidity distributions, and elliptic flow from the SPS (CERN) and RHIC (BNL) programs, covering collision systems from p+p to central Pb+Pb and Au+Au at center-of-mass energies per nucleon pair from 17.3 GeV (SPS) to 200 GeV (RHIC). They compare the measurements to predictions from hadronic transport models (UrQMD, HSD, RQMD), the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) model that includes partonic degrees of freedom, and statistical (thermal) models in grand-canonical and canonical formulations, evaluating the degree of chemical equilibration and the role of a possible partonic phase.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the QCD phase transition at zero baryochemical potential is reported as 146–170 MeV (Aoki et al.) or 180–200 MeV (Bazavov et al.), depending on the order parameter used.
- In a partonic scenario at T = 200 MeV, equilibration times for strangeness are theoretically about 10 fm/c, while in a hadron gas the equilibration time for kaons and Lambda is about 30 fm/c, and for multi-strange hyperons an order of magnitude longer.
- At top SPS energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV), around 1500 partons are created in the early phase of a central Pb+Pb collision according to the PHSD model.
- The number of anti-strange quarks contained in anti-baryons at SPS energies increases by roughly a factor of two in PHSD compared to the purely hadronic HSD model.
- In statistical model fits, the strange quark fugacity gamma_s is typically found to be larger than 0.6–0.7, indicating strangeness production is not far from chemical equilibrium.
- For the Omega baryon at 158 AGeV, there is an order of magnitude difference between measured yields and default UrQMD transport model results.

## Context

This review synthesizes the SPS and RHIC experimental programs on strangeness, extending the original QGP signature proposal by Rafelski and Muller (1982) and Koch et al. (1986). It contrasts the early ideal-gas partonic equilibration arguments with modern lattice QCD results showing strong correlations above T_c (Shuryak, 2004; Datta et al., 2004; Asakawa et al., 2006) and with hadronic transport models (UrQMD, HSD) that fail to describe multi-strange baryon enhancement without invoking color ropes or reduced quark masses (NA49, WA97 collaborations). The work enables systematic comparisons between purely hadronic scenarios and models with partonic phases (PHSD), and provides a comprehensive data compilation for testing statistical hadronization models (Becattini et al., 2004; Braun-Munzinger et al., 2004; Andronic et al., 2009) and coalescence approaches for intermediate transverse momentum phenomena at RHIC.
