# Three-Nucleon Continuum by means of the Hyperspherical Adiabatic Method

> This paper investigates the possible use of the Hyperspherical Adiabatic basis in the description of scattering states of a three-body system. In particular, we analyze a 1+2 collision process below the three-body breakup. The convergence patterns for the observables of interest are analyzed by comparison to a unitary equivalent Hyperspherical Harmonic expansion. Furthermore, we compare and discuss two different possible choices for describing the asymptotic configurations of the system, related to the use of Jacobi or hyperspherical coordinates. In order to illustrate the difficulties and advantages of the approach two simple numerical applications are shown in the case of neutron-deuteron scattering at low energies using s-wave interactions. We found that the optimization driven by the Hyperspherical Adiabatic basis is not as efficient for scattering states as in bound state applications.

## Key claim

The Hyperspherical Adiabatic (HA) basis can be used to describe three-body scattering states below the breakup threshold, but the optimization that makes the HA basis efficient for bound states is not as effective for continuum states, requiring careful treatment of asymptotic boundary conditions and convergence analysis.

## Method

The authors expand the three-body wavefunction in an HA basis, where the hyperangular basis functions are eigenfunctions of the Hamiltonian at fixed hyperradius ρ, and the hyperradial functions are obtained from coupled one-dimensional differential equations. They compare convergence with an equivalent Hyperspherical Harmonic (HH) expansion using a unitary transformation, and implement the Kohn Variational Principle with two different asymptotic forms for the scattering wavefunction: one using Jacobi-coordinate-based asymptotic functions (Method 1) and one using hyperradial asymptotic functions (Method 2). Numerical applications are performed for neutron-deuteron scattering at low energies using s-wave interactions with a Gaussian potential and the MT-III potential.

## Key numbers

- The two-body potential supports only one dimer bound state of energy E^(2b); scattering is considered for energies E^(2b) ≤ E < 0
- The wavenumber k_ρ is defined from E = E^(2B) + (ħ²/(2m)) k_ρ²
- The asymptotic hyperradial function u_1 behaves as sin(k_ρ ρ)/ρ^(5/2) + tanδ cos(k_ρ ρ)/ρ^(5/2)
- The regularization factor in Method 2 uses η = 4 for the term (1 − exp[−γρ])^η

## Context

This work extends the HA method, previously applied to three-nucleon bound states by Delfino et al. (1982), Beiner et al. (1982), and Fonseca et al. (1988), into the continuum regime for 1+2 collisions below breakup. It builds on the asymptotic treatment of the adiabatic Hamiltonian developed by Nielsen and co-workers, and compares with the HH expansion approach used for scattering by Kievsky et al. (1994, 1997). The paper addresses a suggestion by Fabre de la Ripelle about truncating the adiabatic expansion to a single term, and analyzes the convergence problems that arise from the slow decay of asymptotic terms in the hyperradial expansion. The results enable future applications of the HA method to three-body continuum problems, particularly for systems where shallow bound states or Efimov states are present.
