# The sigma meson and chiral restoration in nuclear medium

> After giving brief discussions on the chiral condensate in hot and/or dense hadronic matter and the significance of the $σ$ meson in QCD, we discuss the importance of experiments using nuclear targets including ones with electro-magnetic probes for obtaining clearer confirmation of the existence of the $σ$ meson and for exploring the possible restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The sigma meson, as the quantum fluctuation of the chiral order parameter, may become a soft mode in nuclear medium, and the near-2m_pi threshold enhancement in the spectral function observed by the CHAOS collaboration could be an experimental signature of partial chiral restoration, but further measurements with various probes are necessary to confirm this interpretation.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical calculations of the quark condensate in hot and dense matter using the Feynman-Hellman theorem applied to the free energy of hadronic systems, and discusses the spectral function in the sigma channel computed with a linear sigma model at finite baryon density. The paper also describes how a nonlinear chiral Lagrangian with a heavy scalar field can be integrated out to yield an effective Lagrangian that produces a new 4pi-N-N vertex, which leads to a reduction of the pion decay constant in medium and consequent softening of the sigma channel spectral function.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}, the quark condensate is reduced by about 30% relative to the vacuum value.
- The pi-N sigma term Sigma_{pi N} is approximately 40-50 MeV.
- The scalar charge of the pion is given as <bar{q}_i q_i>_pi = 6.25 (for i = u, d).
- The scalar charge of the nucleon <bar{u}u + bar{d}d>_N is approximately 8-10, assuming (m_u + m_d)/2 ~ 5.5 MeV, compared to a naive value of 3.
- The sigma meson mass is predicted to be m_sigma = 400-800 MeV with width Gamma ~ m_sigma.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear medium, building on earlier studies of the quark condensate at finite temperature and density using the Feynman-Hellman theorem (Kunihiro and collaborators, 1990s). The paper discusses calculations by Chiku and Hatsuda (1990s) and Volkov et al. (1990s) of the spectral function in the sigma channel, and references the CHAOS collaboration experiment on A(pi+, pi+ pi- )A' reactions that observed near-threshold enhancement. The analysis by Jido et al. (2000) is presented as showing that the spectral softening can be obtained in nonlinear chiral models when the reduction of the pion decay constant in medium is taken into account, contradicting earlier conventional reaction theory calculations using nonlinear chiral Lagrangians without chiral restoration that failed to reproduce sufficient enhancement.
