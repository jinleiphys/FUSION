# Microscopic mass estimations

> The quest to build a mass formula which have in it the most relevant microscopic contributions is analyzed. Inspired in the successful Duflo-Zuker mass description, the challenges to describe the shell closures in a more transparent but equally powerful formalism are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper explores whether shell closures in nuclear masses can be described using master terms built from the Extruder-Intruder (EI) single-particle scheme rather than the harmonic oscillator basis, but concludes that adding a single master term with its asymptotic behavior removed is more promising than replacing the volume and surface terms of the Liquid Drop Model with full master terms.

## Method

The authors fit a macroscopic Liquid Drop Model (LDM) with volume, surface, Coulomb, asymmetry, surface asymmetry, and pairing terms to 2149 nuclei from the AME03 database with N,Z ≥ 8. They then add a microscopic term proportional to MA - MA_a, where MA is a master term built from weighted sums over occupied EI major shells with degeneracies D = D_p + 2, and MA_a is its asymptotic harmonic oscillator form. They also test replacing the LDM volume and surface terms entirely with MA and MA/r.

## Key numbers

- RMS of the pure LDM fit: 2.41 MeV (2149 nuclei with N,Z ≥ 8)
- RMS after adding the single term MA - MA_a: 1.46 MeV, a significant reduction for a single term
- RMS when replacing volume and surface terms with MA and MA/r: 3.33 MeV
- The fitted coefficient for the MA - MA_a term is 5.18889 MeV
- Maximum shell effects reduced from about 12 MeV (pure LDM) to about 4 MeV (LDM + MA - MA_a)

## Context

This work extends the Duflo-Zuker (DZ) mass formula approach (Duf94, Zuk94, Duf95) by attempting to build shell closures directly from the Extruder-Intruder single-particle scheme rather than the harmonic oscillator basis used in the original DZ model. The DZ model achieved an RMS around 0.3 MeV, setting the standard for mass predictions. The paper contrasts with fully microscopic Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations (Gor01, Gor09) which have broken the 1 MeV barrier, and with the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) (Mol95). The approach enables future work incorporating two-, three-, and four-body terms and deformation effects through different occupation numbers, as successfully done in the DZ model.
