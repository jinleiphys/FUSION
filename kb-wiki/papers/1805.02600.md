# Alpha and cluster decay half lives in Tungsten isotopes: A microscopic analysis

> Alpha and cluster decay half lives for W isotopes in the range between 2p drip line and beta stability line are studied. The sensitivity of different Skyrme parametrizations in predicting the alpha decay and probable cluster decay modes from W isotopes have been analysed. The half lives are calculated using UDL. Predicted half lives are compared with ELDM and also with available experimental values. The study also revealed the role of neutron shell closure in cluster decay process.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory, combined with the Universal Decay Law (UDL), can provide a qualitative description of alpha and cluster decay half-lives for tungsten isotopes, but the predicted half-lives are sensitive to the choice of Skyrme parametrization and systematically overestimate experimental or phenomenological values except for the SkM* force.

## Method

The authors solved Skyrme HFB equations using a cylindrical deformed harmonic oscillator (HO) and transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis with 20 oscillator shells and a 60 MeV cut-off energy. They employed six different Skyrme interactions (SIII, SkP, SLy5, SkM*, UNEDF0, UNEDF1) in the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent delta interaction for pairing. Q-values for alpha and cluster decays (8Be, 12C, 16O, 20Ne) were computed from the binding energies, and half-lives were calculated using the Universal Decay Law (UDL) with constants a=0.4314, b=-0.4087, c=-25.7725.

## Key numbers

- Alpha decay Q-values for 158W to 180W range from 2.4365 MeV (SIII, 180W) to 8.3707 MeV (SLy5, 158W); experimental Q-values for 162W, 164W, and 180W are 5.53 MeV, 5.153 MeV, and 2.516 MeV respectively
- Standard deviation of alpha decay half-lives relative to experiment ranges from 0.6540 (SIII, THO basis) to 2.0293 (SkM*, HO basis)
- For 8Be decay, the minimum half-life occurs for 160W leading to daughter 152Yb (N=82); for 12C, 16O, and 20Ne decays, minimum half-lives lead to daughters 150Er, 148Dy, and 146Gd respectively (all N=82)
- Geiger-Nuttall slopes for alpha decay range from 119.353 (SkM*) to 121.191 (SIII); for 20Ne decay slopes range from 1131.420 (UNEDF1) to 1206.770 (SkP)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HFB study of decay modes in osmium isotopes (Ashok and Joseph, 2017) to tungsten isotopes, and compares results with the phenomenological Effective Liquid Drop Model (ELDM) and available experimental data. The study demonstrates that neutron shell closure at N=82 enhances cluster decay rates, consistent with the established role of magicity in cluster radioactivity first predicted by Sandulescu (1980) and experimentally observed by Rose and Jones (1984). The results provide systematic predictions for future experimental searches, particularly for decays leading to magic daughter nuclei.
