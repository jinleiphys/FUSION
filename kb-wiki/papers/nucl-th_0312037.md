# Approach to Vibrational to Axially Rotational Shape Phase Transition Along the Yrast Line

> With the energy surface of the nucleus in U(5) symmetry being analyzed in the framework of thermodynamics, the vibration and rotation phase diagram in terms of the angular momentum and deformation parameter is given. Together with examining the energy spectrum, we propose a theoretical approach to describe the vibrational to axially rotational phase transition along the yrast line. By analyzing the available experimental data we show that the vibrational to rotational shape phase transition along the yrast line takes place in many nuclei.

## Key claim

The U(5) dynamical symmetry of the interacting boson model (IBM), with a specific choice of parameters (particularly \(A < 0\)), can describe a first-order vibrational to axially rotational shape phase transition along the yrast line of a single nucleus, not just across isotopic chains. The transition is controlled by angular momentum rather than neutron number, and the critical angular momentum \(L_c\) marks the point where the stable phase changes from vibrational to rotational.

## Method

The authors analyzed the energy surface of the U(5) Hamiltonian in the IBM using the coherent state formalism, treating the deformation parameter \(\beta\) as an order parameter and angular momentum \(L\) as the control quantity in a Landau-type free energy expansion. They derived conditions on the Hamiltonian parameters (e.g., \(A < 0\), \(B < 0\)) under which the free energy exhibits two minima, indicating a first-order phase transition. They then fitted the U(5) energy spectrum formula to experimental yrast-state energies of many even-even nuclei to identify the critical angular momentum and the transition.

## Key numbers

* Critical angular momentum \(L_c = 8 \hbar\) for a model system with total boson number \(N=15\) and parameters \(\varepsilon_d = 0.80\) MeV, \(A = -0.025\) MeV, \(B = -0.01\) MeV, \(C = 0.004\) MeV.
* The critical angular momentum formula: \(L_c = 2 n_d^{(c)} = -\frac{2(\varepsilon_d + 4A + 3B)}{A + B} - 2N_0\), where \(N_0 = N\) is the total boson number.
* The phase transition is identified in nuclei with atomic numbers \(30 \le Z \le 100\), including specific examples such as \(^{72}\)Se, \(^{90}\)Zr, \(^{92}\)Zr, \(^{98}\)Pd, \(^{108}\)Sn, \(^{116}\)Sn, \(^{136}\)Te, \(^{146}\)Gd, \(^{148}\)Gd, \(^{148}\)Dy, \(^{150}\)Dy, \(^{186}\)Hg, \(^{194}\)Pb, \(^{196}\)Pb, \(^{198}\)Pb, \(^{200}\)Po, \(^{202}\)Po, \(^{206}\)Po, \(^{214}\)Rn (vibrational frequency decreasing with angular momentum) and \(^{66}\)Zn, \(^{66}\)Ge, \(^{68}\)Se, \(^{82}\)Sr, \(^{84}\)Sr, \(^{86}\)Mo, \(^{114}\)Pd, \(^{116}\)Pd, \(^{116}\)Cd, \(^{118}\)Cd, \(^{120}\)Cd, \(^{112}\)Te, \(^{114}\)Te, \(^{116}\)Te, \(^{116}\)Xe, \(^{118}\)Xe, \(^{124}\)Xe, \(^{128}\)Xe, \(^{130}\)Xe, \(^{124}\)Ba, \(^{130}\)Ba, \(^{148}\)Sm, \(^{142}\)Gd, \(^{144}\)Dy, \(^{156}\)Er, \(^{158}\)Yb, \(^{160}\)Yb, \(^{160}\)Hf, \(^{162}\)Hf, \(^{164}\)W, \(^{170}\)Os, \(^{188}\)Pt, \(^{192}\)Pt, \(^{194}\)Pt, \(^{184}\)Hg (vibrational frequency increasing with angular momentum).

## Context

This work extends the concept of shape phase transition, previously studied across isotopic chains using the IBM (Iachello 1987; Cejnar 2003) and at critical points (Iachello 2000, 2001; Leviatan and Ginocchio 2003), to the yrast line of a single nucleus. It directly builds on the experimental observation by Regan (2003) of vibrational-to-rotational evolution along the yrast line in the \(A \sim 110\) mass region, providing a theoretical framework (U(5) symmetry with \(A < 0\)) to describe such transitions and identifying many more candidate nuclei. The approach also connects to the cranked shell model description of band crossing (Bengtsson, Ragnarsson, and Moller 1986) and to the vibron model with random interactions (Bao and Feng 2002).
