# Productions of high energy neutrons by interactions between deuteron beam and thick target

> The cross sections of high energy neutron-induced spallation is useful for studying the transmutation of long-life fission products. However, due to the difficulty of obtaining high-energy neutrons, the experimental data are still scarce. The present work studies the possibility to produce high energy neutrons by interactions between deuteron beam and thick target. The Geant4 toolkit is applied to simulate the interaction between the deuteron beam and thick target. An analytical method is also developed to calculate the neutron yields emitted in the interaction between the deuteron beam and thick target. The input cross section data is not only taken from the TEDNL-2017 library but also calculated by the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model. It is indicated that it is possible to produce high energy neutron by deuteron beam interaction with matter. If one wants to get high energy neutrons, low-Z matter, thin target, and small emission angle may be considered.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that interactions between a deuteron beam and a thick target can produce high-energy neutrons, but the authors explicitly hedge that the uncertainty in the required cross-section data remains "huge" and that the results depend strongly on the choice of input cross-section model (TENDL-2017 database versus the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model).

## Method

The authors used the Geant4 Monte Carlo toolkit (version G4.10.05p.01) to simulate deuteron beam interactions with various thick targets, and independently developed an analytical method based on the Bethe formula for specific energy loss and integral equations for neutron yield. Input cross-section data were taken from the TENDL-2017 library and also calculated using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, which was first validated against experimental data for proton-induced reactions at 256 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For p + 12C at 256 MeV and 7.5°, the experimental double differential cross section for 100 MeV neutrons is 0.1 mb/MeV/Sr, while the TENDL-2017 prediction is 0.4 mb/MeV/Sr.
- For d + 12C at 200 MeV and 10 MeV neutron energy, TENDL-2017 predicts a cross section of 6 mb/MeV/Sr, which is twice the IQMD model calculation.
- For d + 12C at 200 MeV, the TENDL-2017 peak value decreases from 4.5 to 2.5 mb/MeV/Sr as the emission angle increases from 0° to 30°.
- For d + 12C at 200 MeV with a thin target (0.1 cm), the neutron spectrum peaks at approximately 95-100 MeV, which is half the incident deuteron energy.
- For d + 12C at 800 MeV, the highest neutron yield at 0-10° is obtained with a carbon target thickness of 30 cm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finding (Sun et al., prior work) that neutron production cross sections in deuteron-induced spallation are large near the incident energy, and addresses the scarcity of experimental high-energy neutron data noted for LLFPs such as 129I, 135Cs, and 107Pd (only data below 1 MeV available). The study enables future design of neutron sources for transmutation experiments, particularly by showing that low-Z targets (e.g., carbon) and small emission angles (0-10°) are optimal, and that thin targets produce quasi-monoenergetic neutrons at half the deuteron beam energy.
