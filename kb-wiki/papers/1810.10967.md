# Tidal deformability with sharp phase transitions in (binary) neutron stars

> The neutron star tidal deformability is a critical parameter which determines the pre-merger gravitational-wave signal in a neutron star merger. In this article, we show how neutron star tidal deformabilities behave in the presence of one or two sharp phase transition(s). We characterize how the tidal deformability changes when the properties of these phase transitions are modified in dense matter equation of state (EoS). Sharp phase transitions lead to the smallest possible tidal deformabilities and also induce discontinuities in the relation between tidal deformability and gravitational mass. These results are qualitatively unmodified by a modest softening of the phase transition. Finally, we test two universal relations involving the tidal deformability and show that their accuracy is limited by sharp phase transitions.

## Key claim

Sharp first-order phase transitions in neutron star interiors produce the smallest possible tidal deformabilities and introduce discontinuities in the tidal deformability-mass relation, but these results are qualitatively unchanged by modest smoothing of the phase transition into a crossover.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state using the constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) parametrization, combining nuclear matter EoSs (SFHo and DBHF) with quark matter described by three parameters: transition pressure, energy density discontinuity, and sound speed in quark matter (fixed to unity for maximal stiffness). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations simultaneously with the differential equation for the tidal Love number, applying a matching condition at the phase boundary to handle the energy density discontinuity. For comparison, they also smooth the phase transition into a crossover using a hyperbolic tangent function and examine the effect of two sequential phase transitions representing 2SC and CFL quark matter.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass constraint from pulsar observations requires M_max ≥ 2 M_sun; all 23 hybrid EoS parameterizations satisfy this constraint
- For the SFHo nuclear EoS, the maximum mass of a purely-hadronic star is 2.06 M_sun; for DBHF it is 2.31 M_sun
- The radius of a 1.4 M_sun purely-hadronic star is 11.97 km for SFHo and 13.41 km for DBHF
- The updated LIGO constraint on the dimensionless tidal deformability for a 1.4 M_sun star is 70 ≤ Λ_1.4 ≤ 580 (90% confidence, low spin priors)
- Self-bound strange quark stars yield Λ_1.4 values ranging from 60 to 500, depending on the stiffness parameter c_QM^2
- For sequential nuclear → 2SC → CFL phase transitions, the Love number k_2 at 1.4 M_sun drops to 0.03–0.04
- The weighted-average tidal deformability for DBHF purely-hadronic EoS at chirp mass 1.188 M_sun is in the range 809.8–816.3, exceeding the LIGO constraint of ≤ 800

## Context

This work extends the CSS parametrization framework of Alford (2013) to study tidal deformabilities, building on the tidal Love number formalism developed by Hinderer (2007) and Damour & Nagar (2009). The authors apply the matching condition for energy density discontinuities first discussed by Postnikov (2010) for crust/core transitions. The results show that sharp phase transitions can produce tidal deformabilities below those of purely-hadronic stars, potentially explaining the relatively low tidal deformability inferred from GW170817 (LIGO-Virgo 2017). Future gravitational-wave observations of multiple binary neutron star mergers could distinguish between hadronic, hybrid, and strange quark star scenarios and constrain the phase transition parameters.
