# Hydrodynamic Short-Range Correlations from Boltzmann-Langevin Equation

> We investigate hydrodynamic contributions to short-range two-particle correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions using the Boltzmann-Langevin equation. We derive and solve the transport equation for equal-time two-point correlations, obtaining both local and non-local contributions that scale with transport coefficients. The non-local correlations emerging from 2-to-2 scattering dynamics provide a hydrodynamic signature in short-range correlation measurements.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic fluctuations can generate significant short-range two-particle correlations through the conversion of thermal fluctuations in the fluid to correlated particle emissions, providing a hydrodynamic signature in a region conventionally attributed entirely to non-flow effects such as jet fragmentation.

## Method

The authors derive and solve the deterministic transport equation for the equal-time two-point correlation function of distribution function fluctuations, starting from the Boltzmann-Langevin equation with a stochastic noise term. They obtain the leading-order dissipative correction to the two-point correlator in two frameworks: the relaxation time approximation (RTA), which yields an analytical local solution, and a scalar \(\lambda\phi^4\) theory with 2-to-2 scatterings, where the solution is decomposed into a singular (local) part solved analytically and a regular (non-local) part solved numerically via Legendre expansion.

## Key numbers

- In the RTA, the leading-order correction to the two-point correlator is \(\Lambda^{(1)}_{p_1 p_2} = -\frac{\tau_R}{2} \frac{n'_p}{TE_p}(\chi^{\text{dif}}_{p} + \chi^{\text{vis}}_{p})\delta_{p_1 p_2}\delta(\bm{y})\), proportional to transport coefficients such as \(\eta/s\) and \(D/T^2\).
- For the scalar \(\lambda\phi^4\) theory, the shear viscosity scales as \(\eta = C_\eta \frac{T^3}{\lambda^2}\) and the conductivity as \(\sigma = C_\sigma \frac{T}{\lambda^2}\), where \(C_\eta\) and \(C_\sigma\) are constants to be determined.
- The equilibrium two-point correlator is \(\Lambda^{\text{eq}}_{p_1 p_2}(\bm{x}_1, \bm{x}_2, t) = (2\pi)^3 n'_{p_1} \delta_{p_1 p_2} \delta(\bm{x}_1 - \bm{x}_2)\), yielding equilibrium correlations such as \(\{\delta e \delta e\}_{\text{eq}} = \frac{T^2 s}{c_s^2} \delta_{xx'}\) and \(\{\delta n \delta n\}_{\text{eq}} = T\chi_n \delta_{xx'}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic fluctuations (Kapusta 2011) and their conversion to particle correlations during freeze-out, building on the Boltzmann-Langevin approach (Calzetta 1999) and prior work on dissipative corrections to correlation functions (Teaney 2013). It challenges the conventional assumption that short-range correlations arise solely from non-flow effects like jet fragmentation, and provides a framework for extracting transport coefficients from short-range correlation measurements at RHIC and LHC.
