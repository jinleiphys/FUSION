# Phase diagram and nucleation in the Polyakov-loop-extended Quark-Meson truncation of QCD with the unquenched Polyakov-loop potential

> Unquenching of the Polyakov-loop potential showed to be an important improvement for the description of the phase structure and thermodynamics of strongly-interacting matter at zero quark chemical potentials with Polyakov-loop extended chiral models. This work constitutes the first application of the quark backreaction on the Polyakov-loop potential at nonzero density. The observation is that it links the chiral and deconfinement phase transition also at small temperatures and large quark chemical potentials. The build up of the surface tension in the Polyakov-loop extended Quark-Meson model is explored by investigating the two and 2+1-flavour Quark-Meson model and analysing the impact of the Polyakov-loop extension. In general, the order of magnitude of the surface tension is given by the chiral phase transition. The coupling of the chiral and deconfinement transition with the unquenched Polyakov-loop potential leads to the fact that the Polyakov-loop contributes at all temperatures.

## Key claim

This work constitutes the first application of the quark backreaction on the Polyakov-loop potential at nonzero density, and the observation is that it links the chiral and deconfinement phase transitions also at small temperatures and large quark chemical potentials, although the quantitative magnitude of this effect remains in question because the model at large chemical potentials probes the form of the Polyakov-loop potential far away from its minimum.

## Method

The authors perform an extended mean-field analysis of the 2+1-flavour Polyakov-loop-extended Quark-Meson (PQM) model, using an unquenched Polyakov-loop potential that includes backreaction effects from quarks by relating the reduced temperature scales of the pure gauge and glue potentials via the relation t_YM ≈ 0.57 t_glue. The grand-canonical potential is computed as a function of the chiral condensates and Polyakov-loop variables, with thermal fluctuations of pions added to thermodynamics using in-medium pion masses. The sign problem at nonzero chemical potential is circumvented by neglecting the imaginary part of the effective potential, setting the imaginary part of the Polyakov loop to zero.

## Key numbers

* Pseudocritical temperatures at zero chemical potential: T_c = 175 MeV (Log parametrisation) and T_c = 179 MeV (Poly-Log parametrisation) for the chiral transition, with deconfinement transition temperatures T_d = 152 MeV and T_d = 165 MeV respectively, using T_cr^glue = 240 MeV and m_sigma = 400 MeV.
* Critical endpoint coordinates: (T, mu)_CEP = (65, 276) MeV with the unquenched Polyakov-loop potential and (94, 283) MeV with the Yang-Mills potential, showing the main effect of unquenching is to lower the temperature of the CEP at a similar chemical potential.
* The relation between pure gauge and glue temperature scales is t_YM(t_glue) ≈ 0.57 t_glue, minimising the difference between the two potentials.
* Input vacuum constants include f_pi = 92 MeV, f_K = 110 MeV, m_pi = 138 MeV, m_K = 495 MeV, m_eta = 548 MeV, m_eta' = 958 MeV, m_sigma = 400 MeV, and constituent light quark mass m_u,d = 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the unquenched Polyakov-loop potential at zero density (Haas 2013, Herbst 2013) to nonzero net quark density for the first time, and builds on the framework developed in Stiele (2013) for nonzero isospin. The observation that unquenching links chiral and deconfinement transitions at large chemical potentials is a feature also seen in functional renormalization group (FRG) improvements of the PQM model using the Yang-Mills Polyakov-loop potential (Herbst 2010, Herbst 2013). The results enable future work including baryonic degrees of freedom via Fadeev and Bethe-Salpeter equations, and the inclusion of mesonic fluctuations in the equations of motion as a next step.
