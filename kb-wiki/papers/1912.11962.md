# Exclusive Hard Processes for Studying Hadron Structures at J-PARC

> With an appropriate hard scale, exclusive hadronic processes could provide novel information of the internal quark-gluon configurations of hadrons. The availability of 10-20 GeV secondary meson beam in the coming high-momentum beam line of Hadron Hall at J-PARC offers an unique opportunity to carry out the measurements of the exclusive hard processes. We address this interesting approach by two possibilities: (a) $π^- p \to K^0 Λ(1405)$ for the constituent quark structure of $Λ(1405)$, and (b) exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process $π^- p \to γ^* n \to l^+ l^- n$ for the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of nucleons. Realization of such measurements at J-PARC will open up a new way of accessing the internal quark structure of exotic hadrons and also the nucleon GPDs based on a solid theoretical foundation of perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The paper argues that exclusive hard processes using the upcoming 10–20 GeV secondary meson beam at J-PARC can determine the constituent quark configuration of the exotic hadron Λ(1405) and access nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs), but the feasibility depends on achieving sufficient integrated luminosity (2–4 fb⁻¹) and the actual discrimination between 3-quark and 5-quark interpretations of Λ(1405) relies on cross-section predictions that differ by orders of magnitude at J-PARC energies.

## Method

The author reviews two proposed measurements for the J-PARC high-momentum beam line: (a) the exclusive reaction π⁻ p → K⁰ Λ(1405) at large momentum transfer, where the energy scaling of the differential cross section dσ/dt ∝ s^(2−n) f(θ_CM) is used to extract the constituent-counting factor n; and (b) the exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process π⁻ p → γ* n → μ⁺ μ⁻ n, where the leading-twist cross section is expressed in terms of convolution integrals of nucleon GPDs and the pion distribution amplitude. A feasibility study is performed using Monte Carlo simulations with the J-PARC E50 spectrometer configuration plus a muon identification system, assuming an integrated luminosity of 2–4 fb⁻¹ over 50 days.

## Key numbers

- The secondary π⁻ beam at J-PARC will have momenta of 10–20 GeV with typical intensities of 10⁷–10⁸/sec and a beam momentum resolution better than 0.1%.
- For π⁻ p → K⁰ Λ(1405) at √s = 4.5 GeV, the predicted cross section at 90° CM angle is either ~100 pb (for a 3-quark Λ(1405)) or a few pb (for a 5-quark Λ(1405)), based on scaling from a measured cross section of dσ/dΩ = 1.09 ± 0.21 μb/sr at √s = 2.02 GeV.
- For the exclusive Drell-Yan process with dimuon mass M(μ⁺μ⁻) > 1.5 GeV and |t − t₀| < 0.5 GeV², the estimated total cross section is 10–20 pb, while the inclusive Drell-Yan cross section is 2–3 nb and the J/ψ production cross section is 1–3 nb.
- The total hadronic interaction cross section for π⁻ p at 10–20 GeV beam momentum is about 20–30 mb.

## Context

This work extends the constituent-counting rule approach previously applied to photoproduction of hyperons (Chang, 2015) to pion-induced reactions at J-PARC, and builds on theoretical predictions by Kawamura et al. (2013) for Λ(1405) production. The exclusive Drell-Yan proposal follows the factorization framework of Berger et al. (2001) and Goloskokov et al. (2015) for accessing nucleon GPDs via timelike processes, complementing spacelike measurements from DVCS and DVMP at JLab, HERMES, and COMPASS. The feasibility study is based on the J-PARC E50 experiment (stage-1 approved in 2014), originally designed for charmed-baryon spectroscopy, and a letter of intent for the GPD program was submitted to the J-PARC PAC in 2019.
