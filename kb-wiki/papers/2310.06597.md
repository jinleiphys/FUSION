# Systematic calculations of cluster radioactivity half-lives with a screened electrostatic barrier

> In the present work, based on Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin theory, we systematically study the cluster radioactivity half-lives of 22 nuclei ranging from $^{221}$$\rm{Fr}$ to $^{242}$$\rm{Cm}$ by using a phenomenological model, which considers the screened electrostatic effect of Coulomb potential. In this model, there are two adjustable parameters i.e. the parameter $t$ and $g$, which are related to the screened electrostatic barrier and the strength of spectroscopic factor, respectively. The calculated results indicate this model can well reproduce the experimental data while the corresponding root-mean-square (rms) deviation is 0.660. In addition, we extend this model to predict the half-lives of possible cluster radioactive candidates whose cluster radioactivity are energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified in the evaluated nuclear properties table NUBASE2020. The predicted results are consistent with the ones obtained by using other theoretical models and/or empirical formulae including the universal decay law (UDL) proposed by Qi \textit{et al.} [Phys. Rev. C 80, 044326 (2009)], a semi-empirical model for both $α$ decay and cluster radioactivity proposed by Santhosh \textit{et al.} [J. Phys. G 35, 085102 (2008)] and a unified formula of half-lives for $α$ decay and cluster radioactivity proposed by Ni \textit{et al.} [Phys. Rev. C 78, 044310 (2008)].

## Key claim

The authors claim that a phenomenological model based on Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin theory, which includes a screened electrostatic (Hulthen-type) Coulomb potential, can reproduce experimental cluster radioactivity half-lives for 22 nuclei with a root-mean-square deviation of 0.660, which is better than the original one-parameter model (OPM) but worse than Ni's empirical formula (0.456).

## Method

The authors modified the one-parameter model (OPM) by replacing the standard Coulomb potential with a Hulthen-type screened electrostatic potential characterized by a screening parameter t, and introduced a parameter g related to the spectroscopic factor in the overlapping region. They calculated the cluster radioactivity half-lives for 22 nuclei from 221Fr to 242Cm using the WKB approximation, fitting the two adjustable parameters (t = 9.176915 x 10^-3, g = 0.416617) via a genetic algorithm that minimized the standard deviation against experimental data. The model was then extended to predict half-lives for additional cluster radioactive candidates from NUBASE2020 that are energetically allowed but not yet quantified.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation between calculated and experimental log10 half-lives for 22 nuclei: 0.660 (this model), compared to 0.904 (OPM), 1.237 (UDL), 2.470 (Santhosh semi-empirical formula), and 0.456 (Ni formula)
- The sigma value dropped by 26.99% relative to the OPM: (0.904 - 0.660)/0.904 = 0.2699
- Optimal screening parameter t = 9.176915 x 10^-3; optimal spectroscopic factor parameter g = 0.416617
- For 231Pa -> 208Pb + 23F, the Santhosh formula discrepancy reached 4.44 in log10(T1/2); for 242Cm -> 208Pb + 34Si, the discrepancy reached 5.59

## Context

This work extends the one-parameter model (OPM) previously applied to alpha decay (Tavares et al., 2005) and proton radioactivity (Zou et al., 2022) to cluster radioactivity, incorporating a screened electrostatic barrier following the approach of Xu et al. (2022) for alpha decay of uranium isotopes. The results are compared against the universal decay law (UDL) proposed by Qi et al. (2009), the semi-empirical model of Santhosh et al. (2008), and the unified formula of Ni et al. (2008). The predicted half-lives for unmeasured cluster radioactivity candidates may guide future experimental searches for new cluster emitters.
