# Bose-Einstein condensation and non-extensive statistics

> We study the Bose-Einstein condensation in non-extensive statistics for a free gas of bosons, and extend the results to the non-relativistic case as well. We present results for the dependence of the critical temperature and the condensate fraction on the entropic index, q, and show that the condensate can exist only for a limited range of q in both relativistic and non-relativistic systems. We provide numerical results for other thermodynamics quantities like the internal energy, specific heat and number fluctuations. We discuss the implications for high energy physics and hadron physics. The results for the non-relativistic case can be of interest in cold-atom systems.

# Bose-Einstein condensation and non-extensive statistics

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Bose-Einstein condensation in Tsallis non-extensive statistics is possible only for entropic index values q < 3/2, and that the total energy remains finite only for q < 4/3, meaning systems with q > 4/3 are unphysical despite having a finite number of particles. The condensate fraction follows the same cubic temperature dependence as the Boltzmann-Gibbs case, but the critical temperature and transition sharpness depend strongly on q.

## Method

The authors generalize the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs treatment of a free relativistic boson gas to Tsallis non-extensive statistics by replacing the exponential occupation factor with the q-exponential function e_q^{(+)}(x) = [1+(q-1)x]^{1/(q-1)}. They derive the q-generalized number density, critical temperature, condensate fraction, total energy, and specific heat using the non-extensive thermodynamic potential for an ideal quantum gas. Numerical calculations are performed for both continuum and discretized energy levels in a cubic box, with the entropic index q varied across the allowed range.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature in non-extensive statistics is T_c = (N_q/V)^{1/3} * pi / [pi * zeta_q(0)]^{1/3}, where zeta_q(0) generalizes the Riemann zeta function zeta(3) = 1.20206
- The condensate fraction follows N_q^0/N_q = 1 - (T/T_c)^3, identical in form to the relativistic Boltzmann-Gibbs case
- Bose-Einstein condensation exists only for q < 3/2; the integral for excited-state particles diverges at q = 3/2
- The total energy U_q converges only for q < 4/3; for q > 4/3 the energy diverges despite finite particle number
- The peak in condensate fraction as a function of q occurs at q_max = 1.14 for N = 409 particles, asymptotically approaching q_c = 3/2 as N increases
- For q = 0.8 and q = 1.2, the specific heat at constant volume times T^{-3} is approximately constant below T_c and decreases above T_c

## Context

This work extends the study of Bose-Einstein condensation from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (Bagnato, Pritchard, and Kleppner, 1987; Ketterle and van Druten, 1996) to Tsallis non-extensive statistics, which has been applied to hadronic systems and quark-gluon plasma (Deppman, 2016; Deppman, Megias, and Menezes, 2020). The results provide constraints for BEC formation in high-energy physics and hadron physics, where the critical temperature of the confinement-deconfinement phase transition is associated with condensate formation, and in cold-atom systems for the non-relativistic limit. The analysis of the first excited state population and the q-dependent sharpness of the phase transition enables future experimental tests in systems where non-extensive effects may be present.
