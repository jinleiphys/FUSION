# Fusion rates in nuclear plasmas

> Energy and momentum of the elementary excitations become independent variables in medium: energy and momentum statistical distributions are not identical. The momentum distribution and not the energy distribution is relevant for barrier penetration. The deviations of the momentum distribution from the Maxwell-Boltzmann energy distribution can be expressed in terms of the imaginary part of the self-energy of the quasi-particle. It is possible to obtain an effective Tsallis' distribution for the kinetic energy. These effects are different from static or dynamical screening and can have important consequences for reaction rates in stars.

# Fusion rates in nuclear plasmas

## Key claim
Even when ions in a nuclear plasma have a Maxwell-Boltzmann energy distribution, the finite lifetime of quasi-particles (nonzero imaginary part of the self-energy) causes the momentum distribution to deviate from the energy distribution; since barrier penetration depends on momentum rather than energy, reaction rates can be enhanced, and this effect can be described by a Tsallis distribution with entropic parameter q > 1.

## Method
The authors derive the momentum distribution from the energy distribution by integrating over the spectral function F(E, p²) that relates energy and momentum, using a Maxwellian energy distribution as input. They introduce a one-parameter generalization of the delta-function (parameter ν) to model the broadening of the dispersion relation caused by the imaginary part of the quasi-particle self-energy Σ_I, and show that this yields a Tsallis distribution for the kinetic energy. They also compare with a Lorentzian spectral function from Green's function theory in dense media.

## Key numbers
- The inverse solar plasma frequency is t_pl = ω⁻¹_pl = √(m/4πne²) ≈ 10⁻¹⁷ s, of the same order as the collision time t_coll = f⁻¹ = ⟨nσv⟩.
- The Debye screening length R_D is approximately equal to the interparticle distance a in the solar core.
- The plasma parameter Γ = e²/(R_D kT) appears in the real part of the self-energy shift Δ ≈ kT Γ/2.
- For large momenta, the non-Maxwellian tail from the Lorentzian case is F_Q(p) = F_M(p) + (hf/2π)(kT/ε_p²) e^{μ/kT}, where hf is the collision frequency times Planck's constant.
- The relation between the Tsallis parameter q and the imaginary part of the self-energy is q - 1 = C (Σ_I/ε_p)², where C is a constant depending on the precise definition of the quasi-particle limit.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of non-ideal plasma effects on nuclear reaction rates beyond static and dynamical screening, focusing on the distinction between momentum and energy distributions in the solar core. It builds on prior work by Savchenko (1999) and Starostin (2000) on quantum uncertainty effects, and by Tsallis on nonextensive statistics. The results are relevant to solar neutrino problems, brown dwarf physics, and dark matter distribution, and the authors note that a recent calculation of the He³ + p reaction by the hep collaboration predicts an S-factor five times larger than used in standard solar models.
