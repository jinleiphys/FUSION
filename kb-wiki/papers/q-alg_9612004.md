# Quasi-Continuous Symmetries of Non-Lie Type

> We introduce a smooth mapping of some discrete space-time symmetries into quasi-continuous ones. Such transformations are related with q-deformations of the dilations of the Euclidean space and with the non-commutative space. We work out two examples of Hamiltonian invariance under such symmetries. The Schrodinger equation for a free particle is investigated in such a non-commutative plane and a connection with anyonic statistics is found.

# Quasi-Continuous Symmetries of Non-Lie Type

## Key claim

The authors introduce a smooth mapping of discrete space-time symmetries (such as parity inversions and reflections) into quasi-continuous transformations using q-deformed algebras, but this mapping is only exact for discrete values of the deformation parameter s in a partition of the interval [0,π], becoming "very close to a continuous one" only for higher partition numbers N. The claimed connection with anyonic statistics arises from the non-commutative space structure, not from any explicit anyon model.

## Method

The authors construct q-deformed operators acting on Euclidean space R₃, using the dilation generator x_i ∂_{x_i} and a complex deformation parameter q = e^{is} with s ∈ [0,π]. They define deformed coordinate and momentum operators that interpolate between the identity (s=0) and space inversions (s=π), and derive the conditions for Hamiltonian invariance under these transformations. The free-particle Schrödinger equation is then solved perturbatively in the non-commutative plane defined by the q-deformed commutation relations, and the resulting wavefunctions are compared to those of particles in a magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The deformation parameter q = e^{is} with s ∈ [0,π]; the identity corresponds to q=1 (s=0) and inversion corresponds to q=-1 (s=π).
- For a potential of the form V(x)=x^n, the invariance condition requires q^n=1, giving n roots of unity as admissible discrete symmetries.
- In the partition Δ_N of [0,π] with points s_{n,N} = (n/N)π for n=0,1,...,N, the quasi-continuous symmetry is exact for each discrete s value.
- The non-commutative plane commutation relation is xy = qyx, with deformed momentum operators p̂_x = -iq²∂_x and p̂_y = -iq∂_y.
- In the limit q→-1, the wavefunction solution for the free particle in the non-commutative plane is Ψ(x,y) = e^{-αx²}√y I_{1/4}(αy²/2) e^{-αy²/2}, where α is an arbitrary real parameter and I_{1/4} is a Bessel function.
- The associated magnetic field from the non-commutative space is B_k ≈ -(ħc/e) s k_j k_l, where (k,j,l) is a permutation of (1,2,3).

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum groups and q-deformed algebras (Drinfeld 1985, Jimbo 1985) by applying them to the problem of continuously connecting discrete space-time symmetries, which cannot be achieved through standard Lie group representations. The authors build on prior work on q-deformed calculus and non-commutative spaces (Manin 1988, Wess and Zumino 1990) and draw an analogy between the q-deformed momentum operators and the kinetic momentum of a charged particle in a magnetic field. The results suggest a possible connection to anyon statistics (Wilczek 1982) and the fractional quantum Hall effect, though the magnetic field in this model is spread across the entire plane rather than localized near particles.
