# Critical Line for H-Superfluidity in Strange Quark Matter?

## Key claim

Based on an analogy to the electroweak phase diagram, there might exist a line of second-order phase transitions in the plane of chemical potential and temperature for QCD with 2+1 flavors, where the order parameter has the quantum numbers of the H-dibaryon but the transition is driven by color superconductivity. The author states explicitly that all arguments are qualitative and, on occasion, speculative, and that the existence of such a critical line is a conjecture rather than a proven result.

## Method

The author constructs effective three-dimensional theories for the phase transition to color superconductivity with 2+1 flavors of quarks (up, down, strange), using order parameters for color superconducting condensates (color anti-triplet field φ and color sextet field χ) and chiral symmetry breaking fields (color singlet ψ and color adjoint ψ̃). The analysis incorporates instanton-induced terms, including a term coupling two φ fields and one ψ field (ℒ^I_{φψ}) and a term involving the determinant of φ fields (ℒ^I_H) that transforms with the quantum numbers of the H-dibaryon, then draws on known results from numerical lattice simulations of the electroweak phase diagram to conjecture the phase diagram for color superconductivity.

## Key numbers

- Current quark masses: m_u ∼ 4 MeV, m_d ∼ 8 MeV, m_s ∼ 100 MeV
- Ratio of strange to up/down quark masses: m_s/m_{u,d} ∼ 20, which determines the relative strength of instanton effects on different condensates
- Quark chemical potential threshold for Fermi sea: μ > 313 MeV (one third of the nucleon mass)
- Nuclear binding energy: ∼ 16 MeV, cited as small relative to other QCD scales
- Scale of hadronic superfluidity: ≤ 3 MeV
- Color superconducting gaps from effective models: ∼ 100 MeV, cited as approximately two orders of magnitude larger than hadronic gaps

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the zero-temperature phase transition to color superconductivity previously studied by the same author (Pisarski, 1999) and others (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek; Rapp, Schäfer, Shuryak; etc.), who predicted a first-order transition at zero temperature for 2+1 flavors. The paper draws on numerical lattice simulations of the electroweak phase diagram (Kajantie, Laine, Rummukainen, Shaposhnikov; Philipsen, etc.), particularly the phase diagram for adjoint scalars coupled to an SU(3) gauge field, to conjecture the existence of a critical line at nonzero temperature. If confirmed, this critical line for H-superfluidity would be of experimental interest for heavy ion collisions and quark stars, analogous to the critical end point for the chiral phase transition proposed by Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak.
