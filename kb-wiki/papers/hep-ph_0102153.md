# Closed-Time Path Integral Formalism and Medium Effects of Non-Equilibrium QCD Matter

> We apply the closed-time path integral formalism to study the medium effects of non-equilibrium gluon matter. We derive the medium modified resummed gluon propagator to the one loop level in non-equilibrium in the covariant gauge. The gluon propagator we derive can be used to remove the infrared divergences in the secondary parton collisions to study thermalization of minijet parton plasma at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper derives a medium-modified resummed gluon propagator in non-equilibrium QCD matter using the closed-time path integral formalism, claiming that this propagator is free from infrared divergences in both the electric and magnetic sectors for non-isotropic gluon distribution functions, unlike the equilibrium case where the magnetic screening mass vanishes at one-loop level.

## Method

The authors apply the closed-time path integral formalism to SU(3) pure gauge theory (gluons only) in the covariant gauge, using the Landshoff-Rebhan frozen ghost formalism to restrict the initial state to physical transverse gluon polarizations. They derive the resummed gluon propagator to one-loop order by truncating the Schwinger-Dyson equation at the one-loop self-energy level, decomposing the propagator into transverse, longitudinal, and gauge-fixing components using a tensor basis appropriate for a cylindrically symmetric 1+1 dimension expanding system with flow velocity. The self-energy contributions from gluon loop and tadpole diagrams are computed for arbitrary non-equilibrium gluon distribution functions, and the hard momentum loop approximation is applied to obtain gauge-parameter-independent results.

## Key numbers

- Energy density of produced jets and minijets at RHIC: larger than 50 GeV/fm³
- Energy density of produced jets and minijets at LHC: larger than 1000 GeV/fm³
- Magnetic screening mass at one-loop level at RHIC: 257 MeV, obtained using non-equilibrium gluon-minijet distribution function at initial time
- Magnetic screening mass at one-loop level at LHC: 330 MeV, obtained using non-equilibrium gluon-minijet distribution function at initial time
- RHIC collision energy: Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV
- LHC collision energy: Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV

## Context

This work extends finite-temperature QCD calculations of medium-modified propagators, which are valid only for equilibrium systems with isotropic distribution functions, to the non-equilibrium anisotropic conditions present in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The authors note that prior equilibrium calculations (e.g., by St�n, Weldon, and others) yield a vanishing magnetic screening mass at one-loop level for isotropic distributions, but their non-equilibrium result gives non-zero magnetic screening masses of 257 MeV at RHIC and 330 MeV at LHC. The derived propagator is intended to remove infrared divergences in secondary parton collision terms for the gg → gg process, enabling solution of the relativistic transport equation without ad-hoc momentum transfer cutoffs, which the authors plan to report in future work.
