# Heavy Quarks in the initial stages of Proton-Ion Collisions

> Collisions among heavy ions, like Pb or Au, are a great tool to study the theory of strong interactions, that is Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). In particular, these experiments are able to give insights on all the complex phases of matter that the theory of QCD allows. In this PhD Thesis we have investigated the initial stages of proton-ion collisions: in particular, we will focus on the first $\sim 0.4$ fm/c ($\sim 10^{-24}$ s) after the collision, which are dominated by very intense gluon fields, in a state called glasma. We investigated the effect of such fields on the dynamics of heavy quarks (charm and beauty) which are created and evolve in this medium. The effect of the initial gluon fields on heavy quarks is quite substantial, in particular we observe that the glasma provokes a $50\%$ dissociation rate on quark-antiquark pairs. Moreover, glasma fields have a large momentum anisotropy, and transmit a large part of such anisotropy to the heavy quarks which evolve in this medium. Finally, we have generalized our study to a non-boost invariant medium, and shown that fluctuations in rapidity do not lead to significant isotropization within glasma timescales.

## Key claim

The glasma fields in the first ~0.4 fm/c of proton-ion collisions cause a substantial dissociation of heavy quark-antiquark pairs and transmit a large part of their own momentum anisotropy to heavy quarks, but rapidity-dependent fluctuations do not lead to significant isotropization within glasma timescales.

## Method

The author employed classical Yang-Mills field theory within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory to simulate the glasma state produced in proton-ion collisions. Heavy quark dynamics were studied by solving their equations of motion in the background of the classical gluon fields, with the quark-antiquark color correlator used to quantify pair dissociation. The study was extended beyond boost-invariant glasma to include rapidity-dependent fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The glasma provokes a 50% dissociation rate on quark-antiquark pairs (charm and beauty) during the first ~0.4 fm/c (~10^{-24} s) after the collision.
- Charm quark mass: 1.27 GeV; beauty quark mass: 4.18 GeV.
- Up quark mass: 2.16 MeV; down quark mass: 4.70 MeV; strange quark mass: 93.5 MeV; top quark mass: 172 GeV.
- The QCD scale parameter Λ_QCD is of order 200 MeV.
- α_s(Q^2) ≃ 0.1 at Q = 100 GeV.
- N_c = 3 colors; N_f = 6 flavors; 8 gluon color states (adjoint representation of SU(3)).

## Context

This PhD thesis extends the study of glasma dynamics beyond the standard boost-invariant approximation, building on the CGC framework developed by Gelis (2010), Iancu (2003, 2012), and Lappi (2006). The work is based on three papers by Parisi, Greco, Ruggieri, and collaborators (2025-2026). The results enable quantitative predictions for heavy quark observables (dissociation rates, elliptic flow v_2) in the pre-equilibrium stage of proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC, providing initial conditions for subsequent hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma.
