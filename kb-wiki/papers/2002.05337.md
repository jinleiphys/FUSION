# Revisiting Bayesian constraints on the transport coefficients of QCD

> Multistage models based on relativistic viscous hydrodynamics have proven successful in describing hadron measurements from relativistic nuclear collisions. These measurements are sensitive to the shear and the bulk viscosities of QCD and provide a unique opportunity to constrain these transport coefficients. Bayesian analyses can be used to obtain systematic constraints on the viscosities of QCD, through methodical model-to-data comparisons. In this manuscript, we discuss recent developments in Bayesian analyses of heavy ion collision data. We highlight the essential role of closure tests in validating a Bayesian analysis before comparison with measurements. We discuss the role of the emulator that is used as proxy for the multistage theoretical model. We use an ongoing Bayesian analysis of soft hadron measurements by the JETSCAPE Collaboration as context for the discussion.

## Key claim

Bayesian analyses can provide systematic constraints on the shear and bulk viscosities of QCD through methodical model-to-data comparisons, but closure tests are essential to validate the entire Bayesian analysis framework before any comparison with experimental measurements, and the resulting constraints on viscosities are expected to be broad due to emulator and experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The JETSCAPE Collaboration performed a Bayesian analysis using a multistage model of heavy ion collisions that includes a pre-equilibrium phase, relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, and hadronic transport. They trained Gaussian process emulators on approximately 10^3 parameter samples from a 17-dimensional parameter space (including 4 parameters each for the temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosity to entropy density ratios) using Latin hypercube sampling, then used Markov chain Monte Carlo with approximately 10^6 steps to probe the likelihood. The analysis was validated through closure tests where hadronic observables were calculated from a known set of model parameters and the Bayesian analysis was tested on its ability to recover those parameters.

## Key numbers

- The JETSCAPE analysis contains 17 model parameters: 7 for the pre-equilibrium stage, 4 for (η/s)(T), and 4 for (ζ/s)(T)
- Approximately 10^3 parameter samples are used to train the emulators
- Approximately 10^6 Markov steps are used to probe the 17-dimensional parameter space
- Emulator uncertainty is generally 5-10% when training with approximately 10^3 parameter samples
- The quark-gluon plasma expands, cools, and reconfines into hadrons within approximately 10 fm/c
- The hydrodynamic regime starts at a time τ ~ 0.1-1 fm/c

## Context

This work extends pioneering Bayesian analyses in heavy ion physics by Bernhard (2019), Sangaline (2015), and Novak (2013), focusing on the essential role of closure tests and emulator validation before comparison with experimental data. The ongoing JETSCAPE Collaboration analysis aims to constrain quark-gluon plasma properties using soft hadron measurements from multiple collision systems, enabling future quantitative studies of hard observables and eventual combined Bayesian analyses of soft and hard probes.
