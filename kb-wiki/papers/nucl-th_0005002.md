# The Half-Life and the Nuclear Matrix Element of the $2νββ$ Decay in $^{76}Ge$

> We apply the shell-model wave-functions of $^{76}Ge$ to calculate the nuclear matrix elements and the half-life of the $2νββ$ decay in $^{76}Ge$. Our result is comparable to the recent observed $2νββ$-decay half-life of $^{76}Ge$. Furthemore it provides the upper limit of the effective neutrino mass is about 0.4 eV from the recent experimental data of the $0νββ$-decay half-life.

# The Half-Life and the Nuclear Matrix Element of the $2\nu\beta\beta$ Decay in $^{76}Ge

## Key claim
The shell-model wave-functions for $^{76}Ge$ and $^{76}Se$ produce a calculated $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay half-life that is comparable to the observed experimental value, but only with a specific choice of the modified surface delta interaction (MSDI) parameters; the second parameter set yields a half-life of $2.82 \times 10^{21}$ years, which is closer to the experimental value than the first set.

## Method
The authors employed shell-model wave-functions for $^{76}Ge$ and $^{76}Se$ using $^{56}Ni$ as an inert core and a model space consisting of four single-particle orbits: $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, and $0g_{9/2}$. They used the modified surface delta interaction (MSDI) as the two-body residual interaction, with two different parameter sets, and applied the closure approximation to compute the Gamow-Teller nuclear matrix element $M_{GT}$ and the corresponding half-life for the $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay.

## Key numbers
- The calculated $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay half-life for $^{76}Ge$ using the first MSDI parameter set is $5.17 \times 10^{21}$ years, with a nuclear matrix element $M_{GT} = 0.353$.
- The calculated $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay half-life for $^{76}Ge$ using the second MSDI parameter set is $2.82 \times 10^{21}$ years, with a nuclear matrix element $M_{GT} = 0.479$.
- The experimental $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay half-life for $^{76}Ge$ is $(1.77^{+0.01+0.13}_{-0.01-0.11}) \times 10^{21}$ years (Heidelberg-Moscow collaboration).
- The experimental lower limit for the $0\nu\beta\beta$-decay half-life of $^{76}Ge$ is $> 5.7 \times 10^{25}$ years.
- The total kinetic energy of the outgoing electrons $T_0$ for $^{76}Ge$ is 2.045 MeV, and the average intermediate energy $\bar{E}_m - E_i = 7.88$ MeV.
- The phase space correction factor $\xi_{2\nu}$ for $^{76}Ge$ is 1.65.
- The derived upper limit on the effective neutrino mass from the $0\nu\beta\beta$-decay data is about 0.4 eV.

## Context
This work extends the shell-model calculations previously applied to the $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay of $^{82}Se$ by the same authors (Wu and Huang, earlier work) to the case of $^{76}Ge$, using wave-functions discussed in prior publications (Wu and Huang, previous papers). The calculated results are compared directly to the Heidelberg-Moscow experimental measurement for $^{76}Ge$, and the agreement with the second parameter set supports the reliability of the wave-functions for extracting neutrino mass limits. The derived upper limit of 0.4 eV on the effective neutrino mass is noted to be higher than the limit around $10^{-2}$ eV from neutrino oscillation experiments, motivating improved experimental limits on the $0\nu\beta\beta$-decay half-life and further theoretical work on nuclear matrix elements.
