# Constraining the strength of $U(1)_A$ symmetry breaking using 2-flavour non-local NJL model

> In presence of magnetic field we have done a systematic analysis to constrain the strength of 't Hooft determinant term using LQCD data within a non-local version of two flavour NJL model. Topological susceptibility, being related to the axial symmetry, have also been calculated and compared with LQCD results to further validate our calculation.

# Constraining the strength of $U(1)_A$ symmetry breaking using 2-flavour non-local NJL model

## Key claim

The strength of the 't Hooft determinant term (the $U(1)_A$ symmetry breaking coupling $G_2$) can be constrained by fitting to lattice QCD data on the condensate difference between up and down quarks in a magnetic field, yielding a best-fit value of the parameter $c = 0.06$ (where $c=1/2$ corresponds to the standard NJL model) at zero temperature, with the caveat that this value may acquire non-trivial temperature and magnetic field dependence once lattice QCD data for topological susceptibility at finite $eB$ become available.

## Method

The authors use a non-local version of the two-flavour Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a Gaussian form factor $g(p^2) = \exp(-p^2/\Lambda^2)$ and parameterize the $U(1)_A$ symmetric and breaking couplings as $G_1 = (1-c)G_0/2$ and $G_2 = cG_0/2$. They fit the three model parameters (current quark mass $m_0$, coupling $G_0$, and cutoff $\Lambda$) to reproduce lattice QCD values of the chiral condensate, pion mass, and pion decay constant from Brandt et al. (2013). The parameter $c$ is then determined by a $\chi^2$ fit to the lattice QCD condensate difference data from Bali et al. as a function of magnetic field at $T=0$. The topological susceptibility is computed from the second derivative of the free energy with respect to the axion field $a/f_a$.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit value of the $U(1)_A$ breaking parameter: $c = 0.06$, with $\chi^2$/dof $= 1.5$ from fitting to the condensate difference at $T=0$.
- Lattice QCD input values used for parameter fitting: $\langle\bar{\psi}_f\psi_f\rangle^{1/3} = 261(13)(1)$ MeV, $m_\pi = 135$ MeV, and $F_\pi = 90(8)(2)$ MeV from Brandt et al. (2013).
- Topological susceptibility is computed at $eB = 0$ for various $c$ values and at $eB = 0.2$, $0.6$, and $1.0$ GeV$^2$ for the central parameter set, compared with lattice QCD bands from Borsanyi et al. and Petreczky et al.

## Context

This work extends previous non-local NJL model studies by Gomez et al. and Pagura et al., which showed that the non-local formulation can reproduce inverse magnetic catalysis automatically. The authors specifically constrain the $U(1)_A$ breaking strength, which is usually assumed equal to the symmetric term in both local and non-local versions. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD topological susceptibility data at finite magnetic field, which could reveal whether the $U(1)_A$ breaking strength has non-trivial temperature and magnetic field dependence. The paper also presents the first exploration of the role of the pion decay constant $F_\pi$ on the phase diagram in an effective model.
