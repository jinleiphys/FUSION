# Mean Field and Collisional Dynamics of Interacting Fermion-Boson systems in a Soluble Model

> A general time-dependent projection technique is applied to the study of the dynamics of quantum correlations in a system consisting of interacting fermionic and bosonic subsystems, described by the Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian. The amplitude modulation of the Rabi oscillations which occur for a strong, coherent initial bosonic field is obtained from the spin intrinsic depolarization resulting from collisional corrections to the mean-field approximation.

# Mean Field and Collisional Dynamics of Interacting Fermion-Boson Systems in a Soluble Model

## Key claim

The double mean-field approximation (isoentropic, factorized gaussian densities) fails to capture the amplitude modulation of Rabi oscillations in the Jaynes-Cummings model; the paper shows that including collisional corrections via a time-dependent projection technique produces the correct depolarization and amplitude modulation, but only for times comparable to the depolarization time, beyond which quantitative reliability degrades.

## Method

The authors apply a time-dependent projection operator formalism (Nemes and Toledo Piza, 1986) to decompose the full density matrix into a factorized gaussian part (double mean-field) and a correlation remainder. They derive closed equations of motion for the gaussian observables (shifted boson amplitude, Bogoliubov transformation coefficients, occupation numbers) by approximating the memory integrals in lowest order, setting the Green's function to the identity, and assuming initially uncorrelated states. The resulting integro-differential equations are solved numerically for the Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian and compared to the exact solution.

## Key numbers

- Mean photon number of the initial coherent bosonic field: 25 (dimensionless, squared amplitude).
- Hamiltonian parameters: fermion level splitting epsilon = 1, boson frequency omega = 1, coupling constant lambda = 0.5 (all in arbitrary units).
- Initial fermion occupation: excited state (p1(0)=1, p_{-1}(0)=0); initial boson state: vacuum of displaced annihilation operator (x_{t=0}=1, y_{t=0}=0, nu(0)=0).
- The collisional approximation is quantitatively adequate up to times comparable with the depolarization time, covering at least several Rabi periods.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent projection technique developed by Nemes and Toledo Piza (1986) and the gaussian density approach of Lin (1990) to a soluble fermion-boson model (Jaynes-Cummings, 1963). It directly contradicts the isoentropic mean-field approximation, which predicts undamped Rabi oscillations with constant amplitude. The results enable quantitative study of decoherence and depolarization in more realistic systems, including the relativistic scalar plasma (Kalinowski, 1984) in 3+1 dimensions, where analogous procedures apply with momentum expansion and counterterm renormalization.
