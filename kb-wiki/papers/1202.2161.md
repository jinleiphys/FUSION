# The Chern-Simons diffusion rate in strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma in an external magnetic field

> We calculate the Chern-Simons diffusion rate in a strongly coupled N=4 SUSY Yang-Mills plasma in the presence of a constant external $U(1)_R$ magnetic flux via the holographic correspondence. Due to the strong interactions between the charged fields and non-Abelian gauge fields, the external Abelian magnetic field affects the thermal Yang-Mills dynamics and increases the diffusion rate, regardless of its strength. We obtain the analytic results for the Chern-Simons diffusion rate both in the weak and strong magnetic field limits. In the latter limit, we show that the diffusion rate scales as $B\times T^2$ and this can be understood as a result of a dynamical dimensional reduction.

## Key claim

The Chern-Simons diffusion rate in strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma increases monotonically with an external U(1)_R magnetic field regardless of its strength, with the rate scaling as B × T^2 in the strong-field limit due to dynamical dimensional reduction; the abstract's claim of "increases the diffusion rate" is qualified by the result that the effect is negligible (0.17% change) for magnetic fields comparable to the temperature squared, as relevant for heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors use the holographic AdS/CFT correspondence to compute the Chern-Simons diffusion rate, defined as the zero-frequency and zero-momentum limit of the symmetrized Wightman correlator of the topological charge operator. The dual gravity is the five-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell system with a negative cosmological constant and a constant magnetic flux, solved numerically as an asymptotically AdS_5 magnetic brane solution following D'Hoker (2009). The retarded Green's function for the topological charge is obtained from the in-falling boundary conditions of the dual axion field near the black brane horizon, using Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates and expanding to linear order in frequency.

## Key numbers

- Zero magnetic field diffusion rate: Γ_0 = (g^2 N)^2 / (256 π^3) T^4 (strong coupling, large N)
- Weak magnetic field (B << T^2) expansion: Γ_CS = Γ_0 × [1 + (1/(6 π^4)) × (B^2/T^4) + O(B^4/T^8)]
- Strong magnetic field (B >> T^2) limit: Γ(B,T) = (g^2 N)^2 / (384 √3 π^5) × B × T^2
- For B = T^2, the relative change in rate is approximately 0.0017 (0.17%)
- The perturbative weak-field approximation differs from the exact numerical result by only 2.5% for B ~ 10 T^2

## Context

This work extends the holographic calculation of the Chern-Simons diffusion rate in N=4 SYM plasma by Son (2002) to include an external U(1)_R magnetic field, using the magnetic brane solution of D'Hoker (2009). The result parallels the finding by Comelli (1999) that the electroweak sphaleron rate in the Higgs phase increases with an external magnetic field, though through a different mechanism. The strong-field scaling B × T^2 is interpreted as dimensional reduction to (2+1) dimensions, described by a BTZ black hole geometry, and the result enables quantitative estimates of magnetic field effects on topological transitions in contexts such as electroweak baryogenesis and the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions.
