# Causality constraints on radiative transfer

> The standard formula, due to Spiegel, for the smoothing of temperature fluctuations by radiative transfer is unstable in relativity. This is due to the fact that Spiegel neglected the transit time of light, thereby allowing the transport coefficients to move outside the convex geometry compatible with causality (the "hydrohedron"). Here, we fix this pathology. First, we prove that the linearized radiative transfer equations are causal and covariantly stable by construction. Then, we repeat Spiegel's calculation accounting for the finite speed of photons. We find that the full transfer problem can be solved analytically. All the infinite (exact) transport coefficients arising from it fall inside the hydrohedron. Our analysis also accounts for isotropic scattering.

## Key claim

Spiegel's quasi-static formula for radiative transfer smoothing of temperature fluctuations is always unstable under Lorentz boosts because it neglects the finite transit time of light, causing the transport coefficients to fall outside the "hydrohedron", the convex set of coefficients compatible with causality and covariant stability. The full linearized radiative transfer equations are causal and stable by construction, and when the calculation is repeated accounting for finite photon speed, all resulting transport coefficients satisfy the causality constraints.

## Method

The author linearizes the radiative transfer equations around equilibrium, coupling the matter temperature field to the photon distribution function via absorption and isotropic scattering terms. The dispersion relation is derived analytically by solving the coupled system for plane-wave perturbations, accounting for the finite speed of photons through the full Boltzmann equation rather than the quasi-static approximation used by Spiegel (1957). The resulting transport coefficients are then checked against the hydrohedron bounds derived by Heller et al. (2023), which require the inequality Im(omega) ≤ |Im(k)| for all wavenumbers within the radius of convergence of the gradient expansion.

## Key numbers

- The first hydrohedron bound on the diffusion coefficient is 0 ≤ D^(1)/τ ≤ 16/(3π), where τ is the UV scale (mean free path of photons).
- The second hydrohedron bound gives -64/(15π) ≤ D^(3)/τ^3 ≤ [256 + 15π(8 - 3π D^(1)/τ)D^(1)/τ]/(90π).
- Spiegel's model gives transport coefficients D^(2a-1)/τ^(2a-1) = (3/(1+2a)) * D^(1)/τ, which fall outside the hydrohedron for any positive D^(1)/τ.
- The instability manifests as a temperature runaway with growth rate Γ' → +∞ as the boost parameter χ → 1⁻, where χ = γΓ'v and v is the boost velocity relative to the medium.

## Context

This work extends the hydrohedron framework of Heller et al. (2023) to radiative transfer, correcting the quasi-static approximation of Spiegel (1957) which neglected photon transit time and produced acausal behavior. The proof that the full radiative transfer equations are causal and stable relies on the Gibbs stability criterion and stability-causality correspondence developed by Gavassino (2021, 2022). The analysis enables future work on relativistic radiative hydrodynamics with guaranteed causality, and provides an explicit example of how the hydrohedron bounds constrain transport coefficients in a realistic physical system.
