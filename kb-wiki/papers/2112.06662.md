# Computation of QCD meson screening masses at high temperature

> We compute flavor non-singlet meson screening masses in the chiral limit of QCD with $N_f=3$ quarks. The calculation is carried out at 12 temperatures from $T\approx 1$ GeV up to the electroweak scale. For each temperature we simulated several lattice spacings, so as to be able to perform the continuum limit extrapolation with confidence at a few permille accuracy. In the entire range of temperatures explored, the meson screening masses deviate from the free theory result $2πT$ by at most a few percent. Their values, however, cannot be explained by one-loop perturbation theory up to the electroweak scale, where the pseudoscalar and the vector screening masses are still significantly different within our precision. Chiral symmetry restoration manifests itself through the degeneracy of the pseudoscalar and the scalar channels and of the vector and the axial ones.

## Key claim

The paper reports that flavor non-singlet meson screening masses in QCD with three massless quarks deviate from the free theory result \(2\pi T\) by at most a few percent across a temperature range from 1 GeV to 160 GeV, but the one-loop perturbative prediction cannot explain the data even at the electroweak scale because the pseudoscalar and vector screening masses remain significantly different within the achieved permille-level precision.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with \(N_f=3\) massless (chiral-limit) quarks using the \(O(a)\)-improved Wilson-Dirac operator and either the Wilson plaquette action or the tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action. They simulated 12 temperatures from \(T\approx 1\) GeV to 160 GeV, with 2–4 lattice spacings per temperature, and used shifted boundary conditions in the temporal direction. The continuum limit was taken by extrapolating tree-level improved screening masses linearly in \((a/L_0)^2\), and finite-volume effects were checked by comparing results on spatial volumes with \(L/a=288\) against smaller volumes.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range: 12 values from \(T\approx 1\) GeV up to 160 GeV (the electroweak scale).
- Lattice spacings: at temperatures \(T_1\) through \(T_8\), 4 spacings (\(L_0/a = 4,6,8,10\)); at \(T_9\) through \(T_{11}\), 3 spacings (\(L_0/a = 4,6,8\)); at the highest temperature \(T_0\approx 160\) GeV, 2 spacings (\(L_0/a = 4,6\)).
- Spatial volume: \(L/a = 288\) in each spatial direction, giving \(LT\) between 20 and 50.
- Pseudoscalar screening mass deviates from \(2\pi T\) by at most 2% positive deviation.
- Vector screening mass deviates from \(2\pi T\) by at most 4% positive deviation.
- The one-loop perturbative prediction for the screening mass is \(m^{\rm PT}_{\mathcal{O}} = 2\pi T \left(1 + 0.032739961 \cdot g^2\right)\).
- The two-loop renormalized coupling in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme uses \(\Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}} = 0.341\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of QCD screening masses, which were previously restricted to temperatures below about 1 GeV (e.g., Bazavov and collaborators), up to the electroweak scale. It adapts a strategy previously developed for the pure \(SU(3)\) gauge theory by Giusti and Pepe (2016) to include dynamical quarks. The results enable non-perturbative benchmarks for perturbative calculations at high temperature and pave the way for future studies of other thermal QCD observables, such as the equation of state, across a wide temperature range.
