# Deformation properties with a finite range simple effective interaction

> Deformed and spherical even-even nuclei are studied using a finite range simple effective interaction within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean field approach. Different parameter sets of the interaction, corresponding to different incompressibility, are constructed by varying the exponent gamma of the density in the traditional density-dependent term. Ten of the twelve parameters of these interactions are determined from properties of asymmetric nuclear matter and spin polarized pure neutron matter. The two remaining parameters are fitted to reproduce the experimental binding energies known in 620 even-even nuclei using several variants of the rotational energy correction. The rms deviations for the binding energy depend on the value of gamma and the way the rotational energy correction is treated but they can be as low as 1.56 MeV, a value competitive with other renowned effective interactions of Skyrme and Gogny type. Charge radii are compared to the experimental values of 313 even-even nuclei and the rms deviation is again comparable and even superior to the one of popular Skyrme and Gogny forces. Emphasis is given to the deformation properties predicted with these interactions by analyzing the Potential Energy Surfaces for several well deformed nuclei and the fission barriers of some nuclei. Comparison of the results with the experimental information, where available, as well as with the results of the Gogny D1S force shows satisfactory agreement.

## Key claim

The finite range simple effective interaction (SEI), with ten of its twelve parameters fixed from nuclear and neutron matter properties and the remaining two fitted to binding energies of 620 even-even nuclei, can reproduce experimental binding energies and charge radii with rms deviations competitive with Skyrme and Gogny forces, but only when a restricted variation after projection (RVAP) rotational energy correction is applied; without this correction, the rms deviation is significantly larger.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for 620 even-even nuclei using the SEI, which contains a single Gaussian finite-range term plus two delta-function terms and a density-dependent term with exponent gamma. Two parameter sets (gamma=1/3 and gamma=1/2, corresponding to nuclear matter incompressibilities of 226 MeV and 245 MeV) were constructed, with the two free parameters t0 and W0 fitted to minimize the rms deviation of binding energies under three treatments of rotational energy correction: pure HFB, projection after variation (PAV), and restricted variation after projection (RVAP). Charge radii were computed for 313 even-even nuclei using a proton radius of 0.8 fm.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy rms deviation for gamma=1/2 with RVAP correction: 1.561 MeV (620 even-even nuclei)
* Binding energy rms deviation for gamma=1/3 with RVAP correction: 1.742 MeV (620 even-even nuclei)
* Binding energy rms deviation for gamma=1/2 without rotational correction: 1.958 MeV (620 even-even nuclei)
* Binding energy rms deviation for gamma=1/3 without rotational correction: 1.873 MeV (620 even-even nuclei)
* Charge radius rms deviation for gamma=1/2: 0.0252 fm (313 even-even nuclei)
* Charge radius rms deviation for gamma=1/3: 0.0253 fm (313 even-even nuclei)
* Nuclear matter incompressibility for gamma=1/3: 226 MeV; for gamma=1/2: 245 MeV
* Saturation density for gamma=1/3: 0.1597 fm^-3; for gamma=1/2: 0.1571 fm^-3
* Symmetry energy at saturation for both gamma values: 35.0 MeV
* Effective mass at saturation for gamma=1/3: 0.710; for gamma=1/2: 0.711
* For comparison, Gogny D1S binding energy rms deviation at pure HFB level: 3.48 MeV; with rotational correction: 2.15 MeV
* For comparison, Gogny D1S charge radius rms deviation: 0.037 fm; Gogny D1M: 0.028 fm

## Context

This work extends previous SEI studies by Behera, Routray, and collaborators (2013, 2015) from spherical nuclei to deformed nuclei using full HFB calculations, whereas earlier work used a quasi-local energy density functional (QLEDF) with an Extended Thomas-Fermi approach. The results are compared with Gogny D1S, D1N, and D1M forces, which serve as benchmarks for deformed nuclei and fission barriers. The SEI predictions for potential energy surfaces and fission barriers of 240Pu and 256Fm show satisfactory agreement with Gogny D1S, though the spherical barriers are lower for SEI, and the work enables future dynamical calculations using the Generator Coordinate Method.
