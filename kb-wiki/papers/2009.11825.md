# Two-nucleon S-wave interactions at the $SU(3)$ flavor-symmetric point with $m_{ud}\simeq m_s^{\rm phys}$: a first lattice QCD calculation with the stochastic Laplacian Heaviside method

> We report on the first application of the stochastic Laplacian Heaviside method for computing multi-particle interactions with lattice QCD to the two-nucleon system. Like the Laplacian Heaviside method, this method allows for the construction of interpolating operators which can be used to construct a positive definite set of two-nucleon correlation functions, unlike nearly all other applications of lattice QCD to two nucleons in the literature. It also allows for a variational analysis in which optimal linear combinations of the interpolating operators are formed that couple predominantly to the eigenstates of the system. Utilizing such methods has become of paramount importance in order to help resolve the discrepancy in the literature on whether two nucleons in either isospin channel form a bound state at pion masses heavier than physical, with the discrepancy persisting even in the $SU(3)$-flavor symmetric point with all quark masses near the physical strange quark mass. This is the first in a series of papers aimed at resolving this discrepancy. In the present work, we employ the stochastic Laplacian Heaviside method without a hexaquark operator in the basis at a lattice spacing of $a\sim0.086$~fm, lattice volume of $L=48a\simeq4.1$~fm and pion mass $m_π\simeq714$ MeV. With this setup, the observed spectrum of two-nucleon energy levels strongly disfavors the presence of a bound state in either the deuteron or dineutron channel.

## Key claim

This first application of the stochastic Laplacian Heaviside method to the two-nucleon system, performed at a single lattice spacing and volume with pion mass approximately 714 MeV, strongly disfavors the presence of a bound state in either the deuteron or dineutron channel, but the authors note this is only the first in a series of papers aimed at resolving the long-standing discrepancy in the literature.

## Method

The authors employed the stochastic Laplacian Heaviside (sLapH) method on an isotropic clover-Wilson action with N_f=2+1 dynamical fermions at the SU(3) flavor-symmetric point where all quark masses are approximately equal to the physical strange quark mass. They constructed a variational basis of two-nucleon interpolating operators with definite isospin and little-group irreps, enabling a positive-definite correlation matrix, and extracted the energy spectrum by fitting the ratio of two-nucleon to single-nucleon correlation functions using a Bayesian constrained analysis with explicit parametrization of excited-state contributions.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a approximately 0.086 fm
- Lattice volume: L = 48a approximately 4.1 fm
- Pion mass: m_pi approximately 714 MeV (0.310810(95) in lattice units)
- Number of configurations used: 802 configurations from two replicas, with 4 time-sources each, totaling 3208 time-sources
- Number of LapH eigenvector projectors: N_ev = 384, with full spin dilution and 12 LapH eigenvector projectors giving N_dil = 48
- Number of noise source permutations used for two-nucleon correlation functions: 180

## Context

This work directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between lattice QCD calculations using the Luscher method, which typically observe bound deuteron and dineutron states at pion masses above approximately 300 MeV (Beane 2012, Yamazaki 2012, Berkowitz 2015, Wagman 2017), and the HAL QCD potential method, which concludes no bound states exist in either channel (HAL QCD Collaboration 2012, Inoue 2011). The use of a positive-definite correlation matrix with a variational basis, similar to recent work by the Mainz group on the H-dibaryon and dineutron (Francis 2018, Hanlon 2018), provides a new approach to test whether previous results were affected by false plateaus arising from non-positive-definite correlation functions, as speculated by Iritani (2016). This calculation enables future work with larger operator bases including hexaquark operators and at lighter pion masses to definitively resolve the discrepancy.
