# Pion Deuteron Scattering and Chiral Expansions

> We discuss convergence issues as well as short distance constraints on the deuteron wave functions based on chiral perturbation theory relevant to pion deuteron scattering. Non-analytical terms arise in the multiple scattering series for the pion-deuteron scattering length limiting the accuracy of the calculations. This result resembles similar findings in the structure of the NN interaction. The effects are found not to be numerically large. The iso-scalar $πN $ scattering length from the iso-vector one and the $πd$ scattering length yields values compatible with the experimental number and with much smaller errors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the multiple scattering expansion for the pion-deuteron scattering length, while widely used, suffers from non-analyticities that limit its accuracy; however, these effects are numerically small, and the iso-scalar πN scattering length extracted from the iso-vector one and the πd scattering length yields values compatible with experiment with much smaller errors.

## Method

The authors compute the pion-deuteron scattering length using the Deloff formula, which sums multiple scattering effects to all orders for zero-range πN interactions, and evaluate it with deuteron wave functions obtained by fully iterating chiral one-pion exchange (OPE) and two-pion exchange (TPE) potentials in the Schrödinger equation. They analyze the convergence of inverse moments of the deuteron wave function (⟨r⁻ⁿ⟩) and the multiple scattering series, using coordinate-space renormalization with a short-distance cutoff to handle singular potentials. The iso-scalar πN scattering length is then extracted from the πd scattering length and the known iso-vector πN scattering length.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron wave number: γ = 0.231605 fm⁻¹.
- Recoil parameter: κ = 0.117261 fm⁻¹.
- Experimental π⁻d scattering length: a_{π⁻d} = [-252 ± 5 (stat.) ± 5 (syst.)] × 10⁻⁴ m_π⁻¹.
- Experimental πN scattering lengths: b₀ = -(0.22 ± 0.43) × 10⁻² m_π⁻¹, b₁ = -(9.05 ± 0.42) × 10⁻² m_π⁻¹.
- For the OPE potential, the first inverse moment ⟨r⁻¹⟩ = 0.4782(7) fm⁻¹ and ⟨r⁻²⟩ = 0.4208(10) fm⁻²; ⟨r⁻³⟩ diverges.
- For the TPE potential (Set IV), ⟨r⁻¹⟩ = 0.447(5) fm⁻¹, ⟨r⁻²⟩ = 0.284(8) fm⁻², and ⟨r⁻³⟩ = 0.276(13) fm⁻³.
- Using the full Deloff formula with OPE potential: a_{πd}^{OPE} = -2.873(3) × 10⁻² m_π⁻¹ (without recoil and binding corrections).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on the NN interaction using chiral potentials (Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola, 2003–2005) and addresses convergence issues in the multiple scattering series for πd scattering, previously noted by Deloff (2001) and Beane et al. (2002). It contradicts the assumption of a strict power counting in chiral perturbation theory for such matrix elements, showing that non-analyticities arise from singular potentials, similar to findings in the NN interaction (Nogga and Hanhart, 2005; Platter and Phillips, 2006). The results enable a more reliable extraction of the iso-scalar πN scattering length from πd data, with reduced theoretical uncertainties.
