# Insights into nuclear saturation density from parity violating electron scattering

> The saturation density of nuclear matter $ρ_0$ is a fundamental nuclear physics property that is difficult to predict from fundamental principles. The saturation density is closely related to the interior density of a heavy nucleus, such as $^{208}$Pb. We use parity violating electron scattering to determine the average interior weak charge and baryon densities in $^{208}$Pb. This requires not only measuring the weak radius $R_{\rm wk}$ but also determining the surface thickness of the weak charge density $a$. We obtain $ρ_0=0.150\pm0.010$ fm$^{-3}$, where the 7\% error has contributions form the PREX error on the weak radius, an assumed 10\% uncertainty in the surface thickness $a$, and from the extrapolation to infinite nuclear matter. These errors can be improved with the upcoming PREX II results and with a new parity violating electron scattering experiment, at a somewhat higher momentum transfer, to determine $a$.

# Insights into nuclear saturation density from parity violating electron scattering

## Key claim
The saturation density of nuclear matter can be inferred from the average interior baryon density of 208Pb measured via parity violating electron scattering, yielding ρ0 = 0.150 ± 0.010 fm−3 with a 7% error that includes contributions from the PREX weak radius measurement, an assumed 10% theoretical uncertainty in the unmeasured surface thickness a, and the extrapolation to infinite nuclear matter.

## Method
The authors model the weak charge density of 208Pb using a symmetrized two-parameter Fermi function, whose parameters are the weak radius Rwk and surface thickness a. They combine the average interior weak charge density derived from this model with the known experimental charge density to obtain the average interior baryon density ρb0, then extrapolate to infinite nuclear matter using an extrapolation factor fex = 1.02 ± 0.03 determined from a variety of relativistic and nonrelativistic energy density functionals.

## Key numbers
- ρ0 = 0.150 ± 0.010 fm−3 (total 7% uncertainty) for the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter, derived from PREX data and theoretical assumptions
- Rwk = 5.826 ± 0.181 fm from the PREX experiment for the weak charge radius of 208Pb
- a = 0.616 ± 0.062 fm (10% uncertainty) for the surface thickness of the weak charge density, based on the UNEDF0 functional with a conservative error covering the range 0.58–0.632 fm from all considered EDFs
- ρb0 = 0.1473 ± 0.0084 ± 0.0030 fm−3 for the average interior baryon density of 208Pb, with first error from PREX and second from the surface thickness uncertainty
- fex = 1.02 ± 0.03 for the extrapolation factor from interior baryon density of 208Pb to infinite nuclear matter saturation density
- The charge density fit parameters for 208Pb: cch = 6.6658 fm, ach = 0.51219 fm, Rch = 5.5031 fm, ρch0 = 0.06246 fm−3

## Context
This work extends parity violating electron scattering measurements beyond the weak radius determination, using the PREX result (Abrahamyan et al., 2012; Horowitz et al., 2012) to extract the saturation density. It contrasts with chiral effective field theory calculations (e.g., Drischler et al., 2019) that cannot yet predict ρ0 with sharp precision, and with phenomenological energy density functionals that give varying values (e.g., 0.164 ± 0.007 fm−3 from some selected functionals versus 0.151 ± 0.001 fm−3 from a relativistic EDF calibrated to physical observables). The approach enables a future accurate determination of ρ0 using upcoming PREX II results (targeting 1% precision on Rwk) and a proposed new parity violating experiment at higher momentum transfer to measure the surface thickness a.
