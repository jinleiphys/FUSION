# Compton Scattering on the Deuteron in Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Compton scattering on the deuteron is studied in the framework of baryon chiral perturbation theory to third order in small momenta, for photon energies of order the pion mass. The scattering amplitude is a sum of one- and two-nucleon mechanisms with no undetermined parameters. Our results are in good agreement with existing experimental data, and a prediction is made for higher-energy data being analyzed at SAL.

## Key claim

Compton scattering on the deuteron is computed to third order in small momenta using baryon chiral perturbation theory in the Weinberg formulation, with the irreducible kernel sewn to external deuteron wavefunctions; the calculation contains no undetermined parameters but is valid only for photon energies of order the pion mass (approximately 100 MeV), not in the low-energy regime where the Thomson limit applies.

## Method

The irreducible kernel for the process γNN → γNN was computed to order Q³ in baryon chiral perturbation theory using the heavy-baryon formalism, including one- and two-nucleon mechanisms from the effective Lagrangian up to Δ=2. This kernel was then combined with deuteron wavefunctions generated from two modern nucleon-nucleon potentials (rather than wavefunctions computed in chiral perturbation theory) to obtain the full matrix element for Compton scattering on the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- Proton and neutron electric polarizabilities at order Q³: αp = αn = 12.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (using gA=1.26, fπ=93 MeV)
- Proton and neutron magnetic polarizabilities at order Q³: βp = βn = 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Experimental proton polarizability sum: αp + βp = 13.23 ± 0.86⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀·₄₉ × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Experimental proton polarizability difference: αp − βp = 10.11 ± 1.74⁺¹·²²₋₀·₈₆ × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Neutron polarizability sum from dispersion sum rule: αn + βn = (14.40 ± 0.66) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Neutron electric polarizability from heavy-nucleus scattering (Ref. neutpol1): αn = (12.6 ± 1.5 ± 2.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Neutron electric polarizability from alternative experiment (Ref. neutpol2): αn = (0.6 ± 5.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Neutron electric polarizability from quasi-free Compton scattering on deuteron (Ref. neutpol3): αn = (10.7⁺³·³₋₁₀·₇) × 10⁻⁴ fm³

## Context

This work extends the baryon chiral perturbation theory treatment of nucleon Compton scattering (Beane et al., 1999; Ulf1, 1999) to the two-nucleon system, providing a consistent framework that treats one- and two-nucleon mechanisms to the same order. It contrasts with conventional potential model calculations (Wilbois, 1999; Levchuk, 1999; Jerry, 1999) that do not treat nucleon polarizabilities and two-nucleon contributions consistently, and with the Kaplan-Savage-Wise formulation calculation (Martin et al., 1999) which uses a different power counting scheme valid for lower energies. The results enable extraction of neutron polarizabilities from coherent deuteron Compton scattering data at Illinois (49 and 69 MeV), Saskatoon (84.2-104.5 MeV, under analysis), and Lund (approximately 60 MeV, under analysis).
