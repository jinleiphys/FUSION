# Heavy resonance production in high energy nuclear collisions

> We estimate freezeout conditions for $s$, $c$, and $b$ quarks in high energy nuclear collisions. Freezeout is due either to loss of thermal contact, or to particles ``wandering'' out of the region of hot matter. We then develop a thermal recombination model in which both single-particle (quark and antiquark) and two-particle (quark-antiquark) densities are conserved. Conservation of two-particle densities is necessary because quarks and antiquarks are always produced in coincidence, so that the local two-particle density can be much larger than the product of the single-particle densities. We use the freezeout conditions and recombination model to discuss heavy resonance production at zero baryon density in high energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper estimates freezeout conditions for strange, charm, and bottom quarks in high energy nuclear collisions, finding that freezeout typically occurs during the two-phase coexistence region or in hadronic matter rather than above the QCD transition temperature, with the exact scenario depending strongly on the entropy density ratio between phases and the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors developed a thermal recombination model that conserves both single-particle (quark and antiquark) and two-particle (quark-antiquark) densities, introducing a pair chemical potential to account for the fact that quarks and antiquarks are always produced in coincidence. They estimated freezeout conditions using two mechanisms: loss of thermal contact (setting mean free path equal to mean distance to leave hot matter) and emission via random walk ("wandering"), applying these to strange, charm, and bottom quarks in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS (sqrt(s)=20 GeV/nucleon), RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV/nucleon), and LHC (sqrt(s)=7 TeV/nucleon) energies.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: m_s = 0.2 GeV, m_c = 1.5 GeV, m_b = 5.0 GeV
- QCD transition temperature range considered: T_c = 150-200 MeV
- Entropy density ratios considered: nu = 5 and nu = 10
- For S+S collisions at SPS (A=32, dN/dy=85), s quarks freeze out at T_f = 150-200 MeV with tau_f = 1.3-2.6 fm/c
- For Au+Au collisions at LHC (A=197, dN/dy=3500), b quarks freeze out at T_f = 117-173 MeV with tau_f = 75-121 fm/c
- Mean number of collisions per quark n_f for Au+Au at SPS: 3-6 for s quarks, 3-6 for c quarks, 4-7 for b quarks
- For S+S collisions at SPS, the ratio rho^(2)_(Q anti-Q) / (rho_Q rho_anti-Q) for c quarks is 30-45, indicating local pair density much larger than product of single-particle densities
- For Au+Au at LHC (dN/dy=3500), the same ratio for c quarks is approximately 1.0, indicating local and global densities are nearly equal

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy resonance production as a potential signal of Quark-Gluon Plasma formation, building on earlier proposals for enhanced strangeness production (Rafelski and Muller, 1982) and J/psi suppression (Matsui and Satz, 1986). The thermal recombination model differs from those used by Redlich et al. (1994), Letessier et al. (1992), and Levai and Zimanyi (1993) by introducing a pair chemical potential to account for the local quark-antiquark pair density being potentially much larger than the product of single-particle densities. The results enable predictions for the fractions of charm and bottom quarks that form charmonium and bottomonium mesons, which could help constrain the entropy density ratio and critical temperature of the QCD phase transition.
