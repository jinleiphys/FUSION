# Hadronic few-body systems in chiral dynamics, -- Few-body systems in hadron physics --

> Hadronic composite states are introduced as few-body systems in hadron physics. The $Λ(1405)$ resonance is a good example of the hadronic few-body systems. It has turned out that $Λ(1405)$ can be described by hadronic dynamics in a modern technology which incorporates coupled channel unitarity framework and chiral dynamics. The idea of the hadronic $\bar KN$ composite state of $Λ(1405)$ is extended to kaonic few-body states. It is concluded that, due to the fact that $K$ and $N$ have similar interaction nature in s-wave $\bar K$ couplings, there are few-body quasibound states with kaons systematically just below the break-up thresholds, like $\bar KNN$, $\bar KKN$ and $\bar KKK$, as well as $Λ(1405)$ as a $\bar KN$ quasibound state and $f_{0}(980)$ and $a_{0}(980)$ as $\bar KK$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Λ(1405) resonance is not a single state but a superposition of two distinct pole states, and that this hadronic composite picture can be systematically extended to predict a family of kaonic few-body quasibound states (such as \(\bar KNN\), \(\bar KKN\), and \(\bar KKK\)) due to the similar s-wave interaction nature of kaons and nucleons in chiral dynamics.

## Method

The author reviews the chiral unitary approach, which solves the coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation \(T = V + VGT\) using the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction as the driving force and a loop function that enforces unitarity. For the Λ(1405), the approach includes coupled channels such as \(\bar KN\) and \(\pi\Sigma\) within flavor SU(3). For kaonic few-body systems, the analysis extends to three-body systems using both single-channel non-relativistic potential models and coupled-channel Faddeev calculations.

## Key numbers

- The Λ(1405) resonance has a mass around 1405 MeV and a decay width of about 50 MeV to \(\pi\Sigma\), sitting between the \(\bar KN\) threshold (1435 MeV) and the \(\pi\Sigma\) threshold (1331 MeV).
- In the two-pole structure, one pole is located at 1426 MeV with a 32 MeV width coupling dominantly to \(\bar KN\); the other pole is at 1390 MeV with a broader width coupling strongly to \(\pi\Sigma\).
- The \(\bar KKN\) system was found to be bound with 20 MeV binding energy in a single-channel non-relativistic potential model.
- The \(\bar KKK\) state was found as a resonance at 1420 MeV in a three-body Faddeev calculation, while a potential model suggested a quasibound state with a binding energy of 20 MeV.
- The root-mean-squared radii of the \(\bar KKN\) and \(\bar KKK\) systems were found to be as large as 1.7 fm, similar to the radius of \(^{4}\)He.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing interpretation of Λ(1405) as a \(\bar KN\) quasibound state, originally proposed by Dalitz (1959, 1960), and builds on modern chiral unitary coupled-channel calculations initiated by Kaiser (1995). The paper systematically applies the same hadronic composite picture to predict new kaonic few-body states (\(\bar KNN\), \(\bar KKN\), \(\bar KKK\)), which were studied in potential models by Jido (2008) and Kanada-Enyo (2008) and in Faddeev calculations by Martinez Torres (2008, 2010). The results enable experimental searches for these states, such as the proposed \(K^{-}d \to \Lambda(1405)n\) reaction at J-PARC, and suggest that production rates in heavy ion collisions could distinguish hadronic molecular states from compact multiquark systems.
