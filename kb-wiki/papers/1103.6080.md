# Coherent states in real parameterization up to SU(5) and classical dynamics of spin systems

> In this paper, we develop the formulation of the spin coherent state in real parameterization up to SU(5). The path integral in this representation of coherent state and its classical consequence are investigated. Using the resolution of unity of the coherent state, we derive a path integral expression for transition amplitude and in the classical limit we derive the classical equation of motion.

# Coherent states in real parameterization up to SU(5) and classical dynamics of spin systems

## Key claim

The paper constructs explicit generalized spin coherent states in real parameterization for the groups SU(2), SU(3), SU(4), and SU(5), and derives classical equations of motion from the path integral formalism; however, the derivation assumes the classical limit where the action is large compared to Planck's constant, and the resulting equations are only valid for spin systems where the Hamiltonian can be expanded as a finite polynomial of the group's infinitesimal operators.

## Method

The authors construct coherent states for each SU(n) group by applying group operators (including Wigner rotation matrices and exponential operators of multipole moments) to a highest-weight reference state. They insert resolutions of unity into the transition amplitude path integral, compute inner products between neighboring coherent states in the continuous time limit, and extract the Lagrangian. From the Lagrangian, they derive classical equations of motion via the Euler-Lagrange equations under the condition that the action is stationary.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2): average spin components are $\langle S^+ \rangle = e^{i\phi} \sin\theta$, $\langle S^- \rangle = e^{-i\phi} \sin\theta$, $\langle S^z \rangle = \cos\theta$; Lagrangian is $L = \frac{j\hbar}{2}\cos\theta\,\phi_t - H_j$
- For SU(3): average spin components are $S^+ = e^{i\phi}\cos(2g)\sin\theta$, $S^- = e^{-i\phi}\cos(2g)\sin\theta$, $S^z = \cos(2g)\cos\theta$; Lagrangian is $L = \hbar\cos(2g)(\cos\theta\,\phi_t + \gamma_t) - H$
- For SU(4): average spin components are $S^+ = \frac{3}{2}e^{i\phi}(1-4\cos^2 g)\cos(2k)\sin\theta$, $S^z = \frac{3}{2}(1-4\cos^2 g)\cos(2k)\cos\theta$; Lagrangian is $L = \cos(2k)\cos^2 g(3\cos^2 g\,\beta_t + \cos\theta\,\phi_t + \gamma_t) - H$
- For SU(5): average spin components are $S^+ = 2e^{i\phi}\cos(\sqrt{2}g)(1-4\cos^2 k)\cos(2n)\sin\theta$, $S^z = 2\cos(\sqrt{2}g)(1-4\cos^2 k)\cos(2n)\cos\theta$; Lagrangian is $L = 2\hbar\cos(2n)\cos^2 k\cos^2 g(3\cos^2 k\cos^2 g\,\beta_t + 3\cos^2 k\,m_t + \cos\theta\,\phi_t + \gamma_t) - H$

## Context

This work extends the group-theoretic approach to coherent states pioneered by Perelomov and Gilmore for the Heisenberg-Weyl group to higher SU(n) groups up to SU(5), providing explicit real parameterizations. The authors note that for spin systems with $S \geq 1$, the Landau-Lifshitz equation (which describes constant-length magnetization dynamics) is insufficient, and multipole spin dynamics must be included; the constructed coherent states provide the necessary classical variables for describing such systems. The formalism enables the study of solitons and nonlinear dynamics in low-dimensional magnets with higher spin, where the isotropic Heisenberg Hamiltonian can include higher invariants $(J_n \vec{S}_1 \cdot \vec{S}_2)^n$ with n up to 2S, and connects to earlier theoretical work by Dzyaloshinskii on quadrupole electric fields in antiferromagnets.
