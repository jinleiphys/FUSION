# Isospectral scattering for relativistic equivalent Hamiltonians on a coarse momentum grid

> The scattering phase-shifts are invariant under unitary transformations of the Hamiltonian. However, the numerical solution of the scattering problem that requires to discretize the continuum violates this phase-shift invariance among unitarily equivalent Hamiltonians. We extend a newly found prescription for the calculation of phase shifts which relies only on the eigenvalues of a relativistic Hamiltonian and its corresponding Chebyshev angle shift. We illustrate this procedure numerically considering $ππ$, $πN$ and $NN$ elastic interactions which turns out to be competitive even for small number of grid points.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously developed non-relativistic prescription for calculating scattering phase-shifts from eigenvalues alone to the relativistic case, showing that the method preserves phase-shift invariance under unitary transformations of the Hamiltonian even on coarse momentum grids where standard discretized scattering equations violate this invariance.

## Method

The authors discretize the Kadyshevsky equation, a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, on a Gauss-Chebyshev momentum grid with a re-scaling that splits the integration range into two subintervals to handle long-tailed hadronic interactions. They compute phase-shifts from the eigenvalues of the discretized relativistic Hamiltonian using three spectral shift formulas (momentum-shift, energy-shift, and phi-shift) and compare these to results from standard discretized reaction matrix methods (K1, K2, K3 prescriptions) for separable potential models of ππ, πN, and NN elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- For ππ scattering in the 00 partial wave with N=25 grid points, the phi-shift method reproduces the continuum phase-shift accurately, while standard discretized methods show deviations that only disappear as N increases toward 100 points.
- The momentum grid uses a parameter Λ_{1/2} that places half of the N grid points in the interval [0, Λ_{1/2}] and the other half in [Λ_{1/2}, ∞), with grid points defined as p_n = (1+z_n)/(1-z_n) where z_n = -cos[π/N (n-1/2)].
- The lowest grid momentum is p_min = p_1 = [1-cos(π/(2N))]/[1+cos(π/(2N))] and the highest is p_max = p_N = [1-cos(π(1-1/(2N)))]/[1+cos(π(1-1/(2N)))].

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-relativistic isospectral phase-shift method (Gomez-Rocha and Ruiz Arriola, 2019) to relativistic scattering described by the Kadyshevsky equation, which was originally proposed by Kadyshevsky (1967) and has been applied to ππ, πN, and NN scattering with separable potentials by Mathelitsch and collaborators (1986). The method provides an alternative to standard discretization approaches such as the Haftel-Tabakin procedure (1970) and the earlier box-quantization methods of DeWitt (1956) and Fukuda and Newton (1956), enabling accurate phase-shift calculations from a single Hamiltonian diagonalization even on coarse grids where unitary equivalence is otherwise violated.
