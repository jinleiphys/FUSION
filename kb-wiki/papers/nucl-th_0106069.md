# Fluctuations of the quark densities in nuclei

> We study the static scalar susceptibility of the nuclear medium, i.e., the change of the quark condensate for a small modification of the quark mass. In the linear sigma model it is linked to the in-medium sigma propagator. At ordinary nuclear densities, when chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken, the scalar susceptibility is distinct from the pseudoscalar one, which is linked to the fluctuations of the quark pseudoscalar density. We show that the pseudoscalar one, which is large in the vacuum, owing to the smallness of the pion mass, follows the density evolution of the quark condensate and thus decreases. The scalar one instead increases due to the mixing with the softer modes of the nucleon-hole excitations. At normal nuclear matter density the two susceptibilities become much closer, a partial chiral symmetry restoration effect as they become equal when the full restoration is achieved.

## Key claim

The scalar and pseudoscalar susceptibilities of the nuclear medium, which measure fluctuations of the quark scalar and pseudoscalar densities respectively, become appreciably closer at normal nuclear density than in the vacuum, signaling partial chiral symmetry restoration; however, the scalar susceptibility increases by a factor of about 6 at normal density due to mixing with nucleon-hole excitations, while the pseudoscalar susceptibility decreases following the quark condensate evolution.

## Method

The authors compute the static scalar susceptibility of nuclear matter using the linear sigma model, where the quark scalar density fluctuations are carried by the sigma field. They incorporate the mixing of the sigma meson with nucleon-hole (NN⁻¹) excitations via an RPA-type propagator, constrained by the nuclear matter incompressibility K, and also account for the density-dependent sigma-nucleon coupling from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The pseudoscalar susceptibility is derived from the pion propagator at zero momentum and energy, and linked to the in-medium quark condensate through the nuclear sigma commutator and PCAC relations.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density ρ₀, the scalar susceptibility enhancement factor relative to vacuum is χ_S(ρ₀)/χ_{S,vac} = 6.2, using a bag radius of 0.8 fm, g_S⟨σ(ρ₀)⟩ ≈ 200 MeV, and incompressibility K = 280 MeV.
- The scalar susceptibility enhancement factor is 5.2 at 1.5ρ₀ and 4.8 at 2ρ₀.
- The pseudoscalar susceptibility decreases by approximately 35% at normal nuclear matter density, following the condensate evolution.
- The free Fermi gas polarization propagator at zero momentum is Re Π⁰(q=0, ω=0) = -2 M_N k_F / π².
- The incompressibility of nuclear matter is taken as K = 230 MeV (free Fermi gas value) or K = 280 MeV (QMC model value).
- The sigma mass squared increases by 18% at normal density due to the bag scalar susceptibility effect.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of in-medium four-quark condensates (CSSS95, DZCW00) and sigma meson propagation (Hatsuda et al. 1999, ARGCW95, WAS01) by focusing on the low-energy region below particle-hole excitation energies. The authors connect their results to the nuclear sigma commutator and pion self-energy studies by Delorme et al. (1996) and to the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988) and Guichon et al. (1996). The convergence of the two susceptibilities at normal density provides a quantitative signal of partial chiral symmetry restoration, and the framework enables future studies of the scalar susceptibility at higher densities and inclusion of two-pion mixing effects.
