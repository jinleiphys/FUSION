# Generalization of Bantilan-Ishi-Romatschke flow to Magnetohydrodynamics

> We present a generalization of the Bantilan-Ishi-Romatschke (BIR) solution of relativistic hydrodynamics to relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD). Using the symmetries of the boundary of the Kerr-AdS5 black hole, and certain simplifying assumptions we solve the equations of RMHD on this boundary for a highly conductive fluid. We then transform the resulting solution to the flat spacetime. Furthermore, we show that the force-free condition causes the magnetic field to become singular at particular points and propose a regularization process for removing the singularities. The regularization process reveals the importance of non-vanishing electrical current in RMHD.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the Bantilan-Ishi-Romatschke (BIR) solution of relativistic hydrodynamics to ideal relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD) on the boundary of a Kerr-AdS5 black hole, under the assumptions of a force-free condition and local electrical neutrality, which together lead to a current-free solution where the magnetic field becomes singular at the poles and equator unless one of the two rotation parameters vanishes.

## Method

The equations of RMHD are solved on the \(S^3 \times \mathbb{R}\) boundary of a Kerr-AdS5 black hole using the symmetries generated by the Killing vectors \(\partial_{t'}\), \(\partial_{\phi'}\), and \(\partial_{\chi'}\). The ideal MHD limit (\(u^a F_{ab}=0\)) is applied, and the force-free condition (\(\Delta_{cb} \nabla_a T^{ab}_{\text{fluid}}=0\)) together with local electrical neutrality (\(\rho_e=0\)) are imposed to determine the field tensor components \(F_{12}\) and \(F_{31}\) as functions of \(\theta'\). The resulting solution is then transformed to flat spacetime via a Weyl transformation and expressed in spherical and Milne coordinates.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field magnitude on \(S^3 \times \mathbb{R}\) is \(B = \frac{2 T_0 \gamma}{L \sin 2\theta'} \Sigma_0 \sqrt{\omega_1^2 \sin^2\theta + \omega_2^2 \cos^2\theta - \omega_1^2 \omega_2^2}\), where \(T_0\) is a constant temperature scale, \(L\) is the AdS radius, \(\Sigma_0\) is an integration constant, and \(\omega_1, \omega_2\) are rotation parameters.
- In the limit \(\omega_1=0\), the magnetic field at the equator (\(\theta' \to 0\)) is finite: \(\lim_{\theta'\to 0} B|_{\omega_1=0} = \frac{T_0 \Sigma_0}{L} \omega_2\).
- In the limit \(\omega_2=0\), the magnetic field at the pole (\(\theta' \to \pi/2\)) is finite: \(\lim_{\theta'\to \pi/2} B|_{\omega_2=0} = \frac{T_0 \Sigma_0}{L} \omega_1\).
- The energy density in flat spacetime (Milne coordinates) is \(\epsilon_{\mathbb{R}^{3,1}} = \frac{16 \gamma^4 L^8 T_0^4}{\left(L^4 + 2 L^2 (\tau^2 \cosh(2\eta) + \mathbf{x}_\perp^2) + (\tau^2 - \mathbf{x}_\perp^2)^2\right)^2}\), with \(\mathbf{x}_\perp^2 = x^2 + y^2\).

## Context

This work extends the BIR solution of relativistic hydrodynamics (Bantilan, Ishi, Romatschke, 2012) to include magnetic fields, following prior generalizations of Bjorken flow to ideal MHD (RPRR-Bjorken-iMHD) and Gubser flow to ideal MHD (cmhd). The solution provides an analytical framework for studying the evolution of electromagnetic fields in a highly conductive quark-gluon plasma, enabling future numerical investigations of resistive RMHD and the chiral magnetic effect, which remain missing in the QGP context.
