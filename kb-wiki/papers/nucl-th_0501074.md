# Charm production in antiproton-nucleus collisions at the $J/ψ$ and the $ψ'$ thresholds

> We discuss the production of charmonium states in antiproton-nucleus collisions at the $ψ'$ threshold. It is explained that measurements in $\bar p A$ collisions will allow to get new information about the strengths of the inelastic $J/ψN, ψ'N$ interaction, on the production of $Λ_c$ and $\bar{D}$ in charmonium-nucleon interactions and for the first time about nondiagonal transitions $ψ' N\to J/ψN$. The inelastic $J/ψ$-nucleon cross section is extracted from the comparison of hadron-nucleus collisions with hadron-nucleon collisions. We extract the total $J/ψ$ nucleon cross section from photon-nucleon collisions by accounting for the color transparency phenomenon within the frame of the GVDM (Generalized Vector meson Dominance Model). We evaluate also within the GVDM the inelastic $ψ'$-nucleon cross section as well as the cross section for the nondiagonal transitions. Predictions for the ratio of $J/ψ$ to $ψ'$ yields in antiproton-nucleus scatterings close to the threshold of $ψ'$ production for different nuclear targets are presented.

# Charm production in antiproton-nucleus collisions at the J/ψ and the ψ' thresholds

## Key claim

The ratio of elastic to total J/ψ-nucleon cross section is approximately 5% to 6.5%, which is about 2.5 times larger than the previous VDM-based estimate of 2% from Ioffe (1975), but only when color transparency phenomena within the Generalized Vector meson Dominance Model (GVDM) are accounted for rather than the simpler VDM approach.

## Method

The authors extract the total J/ψ-nucleon cross section from photoproduction data using the GVDM framework, which incorporates color transparency effects and a two-gluon form factor for the charmonium states. They then evaluate the inelastic ψ'-nucleon cross section and the nondiagonal transition cross section ψ'N → J/ψN within the same GVDM approach. Finally, they apply a semiclassical Glauber model to compute the ratio of J/ψ to ψ' yields in antiproton-nucleus collisions at the ψ' threshold for various nuclear targets, using five different parameter sets for the inelastic and nondiagonal cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of elastic to total J/ψ-nucleon cross section: approximately 5% to 6.5%, compared to 2% from the earlier VDM analysis by Ioffe (1975).
- Total J/ψ-nucleon cross section parametrization: σ_tot(J/ψ N) = 3.2 mb (s/s₀)^0.08 + 0.3 mb (s/s₀)^0.2, with s₀ = 39.9 GeV².
- Effective momentum transfer from two-gluon form factor integration: |t_eff| = 0.3 GeV² after including the J/ψ elastic form factor, compared to 0.4 GeV² from the two-gluon form factor alone and 1/6 GeV² used in the earlier VDM analysis.
- Two-gluon form factor mass parameter: m²_2g ≈ 1.1 GeV².
- Threshold for inelastic nondiffractive channels (Λ_c + D̄): √s > 4.15 GeV, corresponding to ω > 3.61 GeV in the nucleon rest frame.
- Momentum of charmonium in the final state: 5 GeV/c in the rest frame of the nuclear target.
- Real part contribution to elastic cross section: approximately 2% in the discussed energy range.
- Nondiagonal cross section values used in parameter sets: 0.1 mb (small), 0.2 mb (normal), and 0.4 mb (large).
- Inelastic ψ' cross section values used: 7.5 mb (normal) and 15 mb (large).
- Inelastic J/ψ cross section values used: 0 mb (normal) and 3.1 mb (with ψ-absorption).
- Inelastic antiproton-nucleon cross section: 50 mb.
- Momentum shift in center-of-mass system for J/ψ from nondiagonal transitions: Δp_cms = 0.6 GeV.
- Laboratory momentum of J/ψ produced at y_cms = 0: 6.1 GeV; J/ψ from nondiagonal transitions: average momentum at most 4.2 GeV.
- ψ' decay branching fractions relevant for background: ψ' → γ χ_c1 (8.4%) and ψ' → γ χ_c2 (6.4%), with χ_c1 → γ J/ψ (31.6%) and χ_c2 → γ J/ψ (20.2%), giving total 3.9% for this cascade.

## Context

This work extends the earlier VDM analysis by Ioffe (1975) for J/ψ-nucleon cross sections by incorporating color transparency effects and the two-gluon form factor from Frankfurt and Strikman (2002). The authors provide predictions for the planned antiproton-nucleus scattering program at GSI, as outlined in the review by Brodsky (2004). The results enable experimental determination of the inelastic J/ψN and ψ'N cross sections, the nondiagonal transition ψ'N → J/ψN (which has never been measured), and the production of Λ_c and D̄ in charmonium-nucleon interactions. The approach is complementary to photoproduction measurements at Jefferson Lab at 12 GeV, which could together distinguish absorption effects from medium modifications of nuclear gluon distributions.
