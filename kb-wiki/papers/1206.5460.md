# Hidden Local Symmetry and Infinite Tower of Vector Mesons for Baryons

> In an effort to access dense baryonic matter relevant for compact stars in a unified framework that handles both single baryon and multibaryon systems on the same footing, we first address a holographic dual action for a single baryon focusing on the role of the infinite tower of vector mesons deconstructed from five dimensions. To leading order in 't Hooft coupling $λ=N_c g_{\rm YM}^2$, one has the Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) Skyrmion that results when the warping of the bulk background and the Chern-Simons term in the Sakai-Sugimoto D4/D8-${\bar{\rm D8}}$ model are ignored. The infinite tower was found by Sutcliffe to induce flow to a conformal theory, i.e., the BPS. We compare this structure to that of the SS model consisting of a 5D Yang-Mills action in warped space and the Chern-Simons term in which higher vector mesons are integrated out while preserving hidden local symmetry and valid to $O(λ^0)$ and $O(p^4)$ in the chiral counting. We point out the surprisingly important role of the $ω$ meson that figures in the Chern-Simons term that encodes chiral anomaly in the baryon structure and that may be closely tied to short-range repulsion in nuclear interactions.

## Key claim

The infinite tower of vector mesons drives the Skyrmion toward a conformal (BPS) theory when only the leading-order 't Hooft coupling terms are kept, but the warping of the holographic background and the Chern-Simons term (both subleading in λ) introduce deviations from BPS that encode nontrivial physics, with the ω meson playing a surprisingly important role in blocking the flow to conformality and generating short-range repulsion.

## Method

The authors compare the BPS Skyrmion obtained from a 5D Yang-Mills action in flat space (ignoring warping and the Chern-Simons term) with the full Sakai-Sugimoto (SS) holographic model that includes warped space and the Chern-Simons term. They integrate out all vector mesons except the lowest-lying ρ and ω mesons while preserving hidden local symmetry, producing a Lagrangian valid to O(λ⁰) and O(p⁴) in chiral counting. The soliton mass and Δ-N mass splitting are then computed for three truncations: pions only, pions plus ρ, and pions plus ρ and ω.

## Key numbers

- BPS Skyrmion with only pions: E⁽⁰⁾ = 1.235 × (8π²B)
- BPS Skyrmion with lowest ρ meson added: E⁽¹⁾ = 1.071 × (8π²B)
- BPS Skyrmion with ρ and a₁ mesons: E⁽²⁾ = 1.048 × (8π²B)
- Instanton mass in full SS model (infinite tower, warped space, Chern-Simons term): M_instanton ≈ 1800 MeV
- Δ-N mass difference in full SS model: ΔM ≈ 570 MeV
- HLS₁ Skyrmion with π, ρ, ω: M ≈ 1184 MeV, ΔM ≈ 448 MeV
- HLS₁ Skyrmion with π, ρ only: M ≈ 835 MeV, ΔM ≈ 1707 MeV
- HLS₁ Skyrmion with π only (Skyrme model): M ≈ 922 MeV, ΔM ≈ 1014 MeV
- KK mass scale: M_KK ≈ 950 MeV (reducing to ~500 MeV gives soliton mass ~950 MeV and ΔM ~300 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the Skyrmion approach of Sutcliffe (2011) on BPS Skyrmions and the holographic Sakai-Sugimoto model (2004-2005) to include both warping and the Chern-Simons term at subleading order. It contrasts with previous truncated HLS models by providing a parameter-free calculation with all O(p⁴) terms included. The results enable future application to dense baryonic matter relevant for compact stars, where the interplay between ω repulsion and scalar attraction (analogous to mean-field nuclear matter models) is conjectured to produce the small nuclear binding energy of ~16 MeV.
