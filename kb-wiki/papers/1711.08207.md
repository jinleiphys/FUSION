# One-loop corrections to light cone wave functions: the dipole picture DIS cross section

> We develop methods needed to perform loop calculations in light cone perturbation theory using a helicity basis, refining the method introduced in our earlier work. In particular this includes implementing a consistent way to contract the four-dimensional tensor structures from the helicity vectors with d-dimensional tensors arising from loop integrals, in a way that can be fully automatized. We demonstrate this explicitly by calculating the one-loop correction to the virtual photon to quark-antiquark dipole light cone wave function. This allows us to calculate the deep inelastic scattering cross section in the dipole formalism to next-to-leading order accuracy. Our results, obtained using the four dimensional helicity scheme, agree with the recent calculation by Beuf using conventional dimensional regularization, confirming the regularization scheme independence of this cross section.

## Key claim

The authors develop a systematic method for performing one-loop calculations in light cone perturbation theory using a helicity basis with the four-dimensional helicity (FDH) scheme, and demonstrate that the resulting next-to-leading order deep inelastic scattering cross section in the dipole formalism agrees with the recent calculation by Beuf (2016, 2017) using conventional dimensional regularization, confirming regularization scheme independence of this cross section.

## Method

The authors decompose elementary light cone vertices (gluon emission/absorption from quarks and antiquarks, gluon splitting into quark-antiquark pairs) into symmetric and antisymmetric parts in terms of helicity-dependent tensor structures, using a helicity basis where quark spin states are labeled by h = ±1. They evaluate loop integrals in d = 4 − 2ε dimensions while keeping polarization sums in four dimensions (FDH scheme), contracting d-dimensional tensors from loop integrals with four-dimensional helicity structures; for cases with more than two antisymmetric tensors, they use the full Dirac matrix definitions rather than the Fierz identity to avoid ambiguity. The one-loop correction to the virtual photon to quark-antiquark dipole light cone wave function is calculated explicitly, and combined with eikonal scattering off a color field target to obtain the NLO DIS cross section.

## Key numbers

* The number of transverse dimensions is parametrized as d⊥ ≡ d − 2 = 2 − 2ε, where d = 4 − 2ε.
* The helicity sum for the quark propagator correction diagram yields num₁ = (1 − z/2)²(d − 2) + (z/2)²(d − 2)(d_s − 3), where d_s is the dimensionality of internal particle spins.
* In the FDH scheme, d_s = 4 and d = 4 − 2ε; in the CDR scheme, d_s = d = 4 − 2ε.
* The gluon emission vertex from a quark is expressed as proportional to −2gtᵃ_βα / (z√(1−z)) times a combination of δⁱʲδ_h',h and (z/4) times the antisymmetric tensor structure.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier method introduced in Lappi (2016) for loop calculations in light cone perturbation theory, correcting a partially incorrect formulation used in that paper. It provides a more systematical and economical alternative to the manual calculation by Beuf (2016, 2017) for the NLO DIS cross section in the dipole formalism, and enables future automated calculations of NLO corrections to other processes in the Color Glass Condensate framework, such as exclusive processes and particle production in proton-nucleus collisions.
