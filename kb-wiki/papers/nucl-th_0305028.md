# Modern nuclear force predictions for the neutron-deuteron scattering lengths

> The nd doublet and quartet scattering lengths have been calculated based on the modern NN and 3N interactions. We also studied the effect of the electromagnetic interactions in the form introduced in AV18. Switching them off for the various nuclear force models leads to shifts of up to +0.04 fm for doublet scattering length, which is significant for present day standards. The electromagnetic effects have also a noticeable effect on quartet scattering length, which otherwise is extremely stable under the exchange of the nuclear forces. For the current nuclear force models there is a strong scatter of the 3H binding energy and the doublet scattering length values around an averaged straight line (Phillips line). This allows to use doublet scattering length and the 3H binding energy as independent low energy observables.

## Key claim

The paper finds that no combination of modern high-precision nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces and three-nucleon (3N) forces, when electromagnetic interactions are included, reproduces the experimental value of the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length, despite adjusting the 3N force parameters to fit the triton binding energy. The authors conclude that the doublet scattering length and the triton binding energy are independent low-energy observables, contradicting the strict Phillips line correlation assumed in earlier work.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev integral equation for neutron-deuteron scattering at zero incident energy, using a partial-wave decomposition in momentum space. They employed the high-precision NN potentials CD Bonn 2000, AV18, Nijmegen I, II, and 93, alone and in combination with the three-nucleon forces Tucson-Melbourne (TM), TM99, and Urbana IX. The effects of electromagnetic interactions (magnetic moment interactions and Coulomb terms) were studied by switching them on and off, and charge-symmetry breaking was investigated by replacing strong nn forces with strong pp forces.

## Key numbers

*   The experimental doublet scattering length is \(^2a_{nd} = (0.65 \pm 0.04)\) fm.
*   The experimental quartet scattering length is \(^4a_{nd} = (6.35 \pm 0.02)\) fm.
*   The experimental coherent scattering length is \(b_{nd} = (6.669 \pm 0.003)\) fm.
*   Switching off electromagnetic interactions shifts the doublet scattering length by up to +0.04 fm.
*   The quartet scattering length with electromagnetic interactions included is about 6.34 fm for all force models, increasing by about 0.02 fm when electromagnetic interactions are included.
*   The triton binding energy shifts by up to 40 keV less binding when electromagnetic interactions are included.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the Phillips line correlation between the triton binding energy and the doublet scattering length, which were performed by Friar, Gibson, and Payne (1984) and Kievsky, Viviani, and Rosati (1994) using older NN forces. The results contradict the strict correlation found in those earlier studies and instead support the independence of these observables, as previously shown in effective field theory approaches by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998) and Epelbaum, Nogga, Gloeckle, Kamada, Meissner, and Witala (2002). The failure to reproduce the doublet scattering length motivates a new, more precise measurement of this quantity and calls for more flexible three-nucleon force models in conventional approaches.
