# Low-momentum interactions in three- and four-nucleon scattering

> Low momentum two-nucleon interactions obtained with the renormalization group method and the similarity renormalization group method are used to study the cutoff dependence of low energy 3N and 4N scattering observables. The residual cutoff dependence arises from omitted short-ranged 3N (and higher) forces that are induced by the renormalization group transformations, and may help to estimate the sensitivity of various 3N and 4N scattering observables to short-ranged many-body forces.

## Key claim

Low-momentum two-nucleon interactions evolved via RG and SRG methods show that the residual cutoff dependence in three-nucleon scattering observables (neutron-deuteron analyzing power and space star breakup cross section) is too weak to resolve long-standing discrepancies with data, implying short-range three-nucleon forces are unlikely to fix these problems; in contrast, four-nucleon scattering observables (total n-³H cross section and analyzing power) exhibit stronger cutoff dependence, suggesting greater sensitivity to omitted short-range many-body forces.

## Method

The authors solved symmetrized Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for three-nucleon and four-nucleon scattering using low-momentum two-nucleon potentials derived from CD-Bonn, AV18, and N3LO chiral EFT interactions via both the renormalization group (RG) method with a smooth regulator exp(-(k²/Λ²)⁸) and the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method. They computed neutron-deuteron elastic analyzing power A_y at E_n = 3 MeV, the nd breakup differential cross section in the space star configuration at E_n = 13 MeV, and for four-nucleon systems the total n-³H cross section and neutron analyzing power at E_n = 3.5 MeV, all as functions of the RG cutoff Λ or SRG parameter λ.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy ε_t varies by about 1 MeV over the cutoff range for CD-Bonn, AV18, and N3LO based low-momentum potentials, consistent with earlier bound-state calculations by Nogga et al. (2004)
- Neutron-deuteron analyzing power A_y at E_n = 3 MeV and θ_cm = 104 deg shows net variation smaller than the ~20% discrepancy from the experimental value of 10² A_y = 5.86 (McAninch et al., 1993)
- Total n-³H cross section σ_t at E_n = 3.5 MeV shows stronger cutoff dependence, with experimental value σ_t = 2.45 b (Phillips et al., 1980); RG cutoff Λ ≈ 1.25 fm⁻¹ or SRG parameter λ ≈ 1.8 fm⁻¹ can describe the cross section over a wide energy range
- Maximum neutron analyzing power A_y for n-³H scattering at E_n = 3.5 MeV increases by a factor of 1.13 (N3LO) to 1.21 (AV18) at cutoff values yielding experimental or deepest binding, but the experimental p-³He A_y discrepancy is larger by a factor of 1.45 (CD-Bonn) to 1.55 (AV18)

## Context

This work extends the cutoff-dependence study of Nogga et al. (2004) from three- and four-nucleon bound states to scattering observables, using both RG and SRG evolved interactions. The weak cutoff dependence of nd A_y and space star breakup confirms earlier findings that adding two-pion-exchange three-nucleon forces (Witala et al., 2001; Kievsky et al., 2001; Kuros-Zolnierczuk et al., 2002) or explicit Δ-isobar excitation (Deltuva et al., 2003, 2005) does not resolve these discrepancies, though subleading long-range chiral EFT three-nucleon forces may be promising. The stronger cutoff dependence in four-nucleon scattering, particularly the P-wave contributions to the total n-³H cross section, indicates these observables may be sensitive to missing short-range three-nucleon forces, in contrast to the p-³He A_y puzzle (Deltuva et al., 2007).
