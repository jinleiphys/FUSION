# Relativistic correction of the Coulomb interaction in the local density approximation for energies and radii in doubly-magic nuclei

> Effects of the relativistic correction of the Coulomb interaction on doubly-magic nuclei are discussed with Skyrme Hartree--Fock calculations. The relativistic correction is treated by using the local density approximation. It is found that the correction to the total energy is about $ 2.4 \, \mathrm{MeV} $ for $ {}^{208} \mathrm{Pb} $, while proton and neutron radii do not change significantly. This difference is larger than the difference of the Coulomb exchange (Fock) energy calculated with the local density (Hartree--Fock--Slater) approximation and that with the exact treatment and the neutron finite-size effect. Effects of the correction are also compared to the correction due to the vacuum polarization. It is shown that the two contributions to the total energy are comparable in light nuclei, but the latter dominates in heavy nuclei, while the contribution of the relativistic correction to the total energy is non-negligible compared to the target accuracy of the DFT calculation.

## Key claim

The relativistic correction to the Coulomb interaction in doubly-magic nuclei, treated via a local density approximation (LDA) within Skyrme Hartree–Fock calculations, contributes about 2.4 MeV to the total energy of ²⁰⁸Pb, which is non-negligible compared to the target accuracy of density functional theory (DFT) calculations (O(100 keV)), but proton and neutron radii change by less than 0.002 fm and can be safely neglected.

## Method

The author constructed an energy density functional (EDF) for the relativistic correction (the Breit correction adapted for nucleons) in the local density approximation, including only the retardation and Darwin terms (spin-dependent terms vanish in LDA). This EDF was implemented in the self-consistent Skyrme Hartree–Fock code `skyrme_rpa` using the SAMi EDF for the nuclear part, with spherical symmetry and no pairing, for a set of doubly-magic nuclei. Calculations were performed in a 15 fm box with 0.1 fm mesh.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic correction to the total energy is about 2.37 MeV for ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The relativistic correction to the total energy is about 0.42 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca.
- The vacuum polarization contribution to the total energy is about 0.4 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca and about 3.7 MeV for ²⁰⁸Pb.
- Proton and neutron radii change by less than 0.002 fm due to the relativistic correction and vacuum polarization.
- The fitted Z-dependence of the relativistic correction is E_Rel^tot ≈ 0.01660 Z^{1.12} MeV.
- The fitted Z-dependence of the vacuum polarization is E_VP ≈ 0.00398 Z^{1.55} MeV.
- The analytical estimate for the relativistic correction in the hard-sphere model gives E_Rel^tot ≈ 0.032 Z MeV for R_p < R_n.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of relativistic corrections (Breit-type) from electronic systems (Kenny 1996, Naito 2020 J.Phys.B) to nuclear DFT, using the correspondence between electron and nucleon interactions. It compares the relativistic correction to the vacuum polarization (Uehling potential, previously applied by Naito 2020 Phys.Rev.C) and finds that while both are comparable in light nuclei, vacuum polarization dominates in heavy nuclei. The correction is also compared to the gradient correction (GGA) of the Coulomb exchange energy (Roca-Maza 2016, Naito 2019), showing that the relativistic correction is comparable in magnitude but opposite in sign, leaving an open question for a first-principles relativistic Coulomb GGA EDF.
