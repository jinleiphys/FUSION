# Overview of nucleon structure

> The quark-gluon properties of the nucleon are probed by a host of recent and planned experiments. These involve elastic, deep-inelastic, semi-inclusive deep-inelastic (SIDIS), and deeply-virtual Compton scattering. A light-front description is naturally applied to interpret all of these processes. The advantages of this approach will be discussed The talk will then focus on the transverse charge and magnetization densities of the nucleons and the use of SIDIS to reveal the non-spherical shape of the nucleon. We find that the central charge density of the neutron is negative, that the magnetization density of the proton extends further in space than the charge distribution, and that the shape of the nucleon, as measured by the spin-dependent density, is not spherical.

## Key claim

The talk argues that model-independent transverse charge densities, obtained from the two-dimensional Fourier transform of the Dirac form factor \(F_1\), reveal that the central charge density of the neutron is negative, contradicting older model expectations of a positive central density from pion-cloud or one-gluon-exchange pictures.

## Method

The analysis uses light-front coordinates in the infinite-momentum frame, where the transverse charge density \(\rho(b)\) is defined as the two-dimensional Fourier transform of the Dirac form factor \(F_1(Q^2 = \mathbf{q}^2)\). The magnetization density is similarly obtained from the Pauli form factor \(F_2\). The spin-dependent density, which reveals the nucleon’s shape, is related to the transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution \(h_{1T}^\perp\), measurable in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) with polarized electrons.

## Key numbers

- The central charge density of the neutron is negative, as seen from the negative-definite nature of the measured \(F_1(Q^2)\) values.
- The mean square transverse magnetization radius \(\langle b^2\rangle_M\) exceeds the mean square transverse charge radius \(\langle b^2\rangle_{Ch}\) by approximately \(\mu / M^2 > 0\), where \(\mu\) is the anomalous magnetic moment and \(M\) the nucleon mass.
- The ratio \(QF_2/F_1\) of proton form factors falls rapidly with increasing \(Q^2\), as predicted by a 1995 light-front quark model (Frank:1995pv) that includes Dirac spinors with orbital angular momentum.

## Context

This work extends the light-front interpretation of elastic form factors pioneered by Hofstadter (1956) and the deep-inelastic scattering discoveries of Bloom (1969). It contradicts the older Fermi picture and cloudy-bag model (Thomas:1981vc) that predicted a positive central charge density for the neutron. The results enable future SIDIS experiments to measure the spin-dependent density \(h_{1T}^\perp\) and lattice QCD calculations (Schierholz:2009xx) to confirm the non-spherical shape of the proton.
