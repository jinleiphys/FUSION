# Exploring continuum structures with a pseudo-state basis

> The ability of a recently developed square-integrable discrete basis to represent the properties of the continuum of a two-body system is investigated. The basis is obtained performing a simple analytic local scale transformation to the harmonic oscillator basis. Scattering phase-shifts and the electric transition probabilities B(E1) and B(E2) have been evaluated for several potentials using the proposed basis. Both quantities are found to be in excellent agreement with the exact values calculated from the true scattering states. The basis has been applied to describe the projectile continuum in the 6He scattering by 12C and 208Pb targets at 240 MeV/nucleon and the 11Be scattering by 12C at 67 MeV/nucleon. The calculated breakup differential cross sections are found to be in very good agreement with the available experimental data for these reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis, obtained via a local scale transformation of the harmonic oscillator basis, can accurately represent continuum properties of a two-body system, including scattering phase-shifts and electric transition probabilities, provided the basis is sufficiently large and the transformation parameters are appropriately chosen.

## Method

The authors constructed a square-integrable pseudo-state basis by applying a parametric local scale transformation to the harmonic oscillator basis, then diagonalized the two-body Hamiltonian in this truncated basis. They extracted scattering phase-shifts from the pseudo-states using the Hazi-Taylor stabilization method, and computed electric transition probabilities B(E1) and B(E2) using both discrete approximations and a folded approximation that inserts the pseudo-state identity into the exact continuum expression. The basis was applied within the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method to calculate breakup differential cross sections for reactions involving weakly-bound projectiles.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of the deuteron (p-n system with Poeschl-Teller potential): -2.2245 MeV, reproduced exactly with the THO basis using N=30 states (b=1.5 fm, gamma=2.48 fm^(1/2))
- Total B(E1) for deuteron: 0.87136 e^2 fm^2 (exact value), converged to within 0.00007 e^2 fm^2 with N=30 THO states
- Total B(E2) for deuteron: 20.8297 e^2 fm^4 (exact value), converged to within 0.0000 e^2 fm^4 with N=30 THO states
- Polarizability for deuteron: 0.6209 fm^3 (converged THO value), consistent with experimental value 0.61 +/- 0.04 from Friar (1983)
- Energy-weighted B(E1) sum rule for deuteron: 7.433 e^2 fm^2 MeV (converged THO value)
- The THO basis with N=30 states reproduced exact phase-shifts for s, p, and d waves across the full energy range
- The HO basis with N=30 states gave ground state energy -2.2237 MeV and total B(E1) 0.86926 e^2 fm^2, showing slower convergence than THO

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-state method based on the Transformed Harmonic Oscillator (THO) basis, previously introduced by Moro et al. (2009) for CDCC calculations, by demonstrating that the basis also accurately reproduces fundamental continuum properties such as scattering phase-shifts and electric transition probabilities. The approach provides an alternative to the standard binning method of Rawitscher (1974) and Austern (1987) for continuum discretization, and improves upon the harmonic oscillator basis which shows poor convergence for continuum properties due to its incorrect Gaussian asymptotic behavior. The method enables efficient CDCC calculations of breakup reactions for halo nuclei such as 6He and 11Be, as demonstrated by the agreement with experimental data for 6He + 12C and 6He + 208Pb at 240 MeV/nucleon and 11Be + 12C at 67 MeV/nucleon.
