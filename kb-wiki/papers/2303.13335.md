# Nuclear polarizability effects in $^3$He$^+$ hyperfine splitting

> The nuclear polarizability effects in hyperfine splitting of light atomic systems are not well known. The only system for which they were previously calculated is the hydrogen atom, where these effects were shown to contribute about 5\% of the total nuclear correction. One generally expects the polarizability effects to become more pronounced for composite nuclei. In the present work we determine the nuclear polarizability correction to the hyperfine splitting in He$^+$ by comparing the effective Zemach radius deduced from the experimental hyperfine splitting with the Zemach radius obtained from the electron scattering. We obtain a surprising result that the nuclear polarizability of the helion yields just 3\% of the total nuclear correction, which is smaller than for the proton.

## Key claim

The nuclear polarizability correction to the hyperfine splitting in He⁺ is surprisingly small, yielding just 3% of the total nuclear correction, which is even smaller than the 5% contribution previously found for the proton in hydrogen, contrary to the general expectation that polarizability effects become more pronounced for composite nuclei.

## Method

The authors determined the nuclear polarizability correction to the hyperfine splitting in He⁺ by comparing the effective Zemach radius (deduced from the experimental hyperfine splitting) with the elastic Zemach radius obtained from electron-scattering data. They calculated all known QED, recoil, and higher-order nuclear corrections to the hyperfine splitting, subtracted them from the experimental value to isolate the leading nuclear-structure correction δ⁽¹⁾_nuc, and then extracted the effective Zemach radius from that correction. The difference between the effective Zemach radius and the elastic Zemach radius from Sick (2014) was interpreted as the nuclear polarizability contribution.

## Key numbers

- The effective Zemach radius determined in this work is 2.600(8) fm.
- The elastic Zemach radius from electron-scattering data (Sick, 2014) is 2.528(16) fm.
- The difference between the effective and elastic Zemach radii is 0.072(18) fm, interpreted as the nuclear polarizability effect.
- The nuclear polarizability yields approximately 3% of the total nuclear correction to the hyperfine splitting.
- The leading nuclear-structure correction δ⁽¹⁾_nuc is -0.00019649(63), corresponding to 1701.0(5.5) kHz.
- The experimental hyperfine splitting from Blaum (2022) is 1.00105377 times the Fermi energy, or -8,665,649.86577(26) kHz.
- The recoil correction δ⁽¹⁾_rec is -1,217(60) × 10⁻⁸.
- The higher-order nuclear correction δ⁽²⁺⁾_nuc,rel is -49.1(5) × 10⁻⁸.
- The radiative finite nuclear size correction δ⁽²⁾_nuc,rad is -240.1(2.4) × 10⁻⁸.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear polarizability effects in hyperfine splitting from hydrogen (Tomalak, 2019), where the inelastic contribution was about 5% of the elastic nuclear correction, to the composite nucleus of He⁺. The surprising result that the helion polarizability is smaller than the proton's contradicts expectations and highlights the lack of a comprehensive theory for inelastic polarizability corrections in atomic hyperfine splitting, as noted for the discrepancies in muonic deuterium (Kalinowski, 2018) and ⁶Li (Sun, 2023). The effective Zemach radius determined here enables more accurate theoretical predictions for the hyperfine splitting of neutral helium in future work.
