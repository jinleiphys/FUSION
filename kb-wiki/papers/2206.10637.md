# QED medium effects in (anti)neutrino-nucleus and electron-nucleus scattering: elastic scattering on nucleons

> Interpretation of current and future neutrino oscillation and electron scattering experiments requires knowledge of lepton-nucleon and lepton-nucleus interactions at the percent level. We study the exchange of photons between charged particles and the nuclear medium for (anti)neutrino-, electron-, and muon-induced reactions inside a large nucleus. While quantum electrodynamics (QED)-medium contributions are formally suppressed by two powers of the electromagnetic coupling constant $α$ when compared to the leading-order cross sections, low-energy modes and the nuclear size enhance the effect by orders of magnitude. They require a proper infrared regularization, which we implement as a screening of the electromagnetic interactions at atomic length scales or above. We provide approximate analytic expressions for the distortion of (anti)neutrino-nucleus and charged lepton-nucleus cross sections and evaluate the QED-medium effects for realistic values of the screening scale on the example of elastic scattering with nucleons inside the nucleus. We find new permille- to percent-level effects, which were not considered in either (anti)neutrino-nucleus or electron-nucleus scattering.

# QED medium effects in (anti)neutrino-nucleus and electron-nucleus scattering: elastic scattering on nucleons

## Key claim

While QED-medium contributions are formally suppressed by two powers of the electromagnetic coupling constant alpha relative to leading-order cross sections, low-energy modes and the nuclear size enhance the effect by orders of magnitude, requiring proper infrared regularization via screening at atomic length scales. The authors find new permille- to percent-level effects that were not previously considered in either (anti)neutrino-nucleus or electron-nucleus scattering, but note that for electron scattering the correction is not well defined for momentum transfers below approximately 0.005-0.008 GeV^2 due to overlap between hard and Glauber regions.

## Method

The authors derive O(alpha^2) QED nuclear medium corrections to (anti)neutrino-nucleus and charged lepton-nucleus scattering cross sections using a soft-collinear effective field theory (SCET_G) with Glauber photon exchanges between charged leptons and the nuclear medium, and also perform full QED calculations in the light-cone basis without the effective field theory approximations. They regularize the infrared divergence of the Coulomb potential by screening at atomic distances, using a Woods-Saxon charge distribution for nuclei with parameters from measured charge radii, and evaluate the corrections for elastic scattering on nucleons inside various nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Relative cross-section corrections reach permille to percent level for (anti)neutrino and electron scattering on nuclei at GeV beam energies
- For neutrino scattering at E_nu = 2 GeV, medium effects are negative at low Q^2 (up to a few permille) and cross zero to positive values at larger Q^2
- For antineutrino scattering at E_nu = 2 GeV, corrections increase monotonically at low Q^2 and are larger than for neutrino scattering due to more rapid falloff of the tree-level cross section
- For electron scattering at E_e = 2 GeV, the correction is positive definite and monotonically decreasing with Q^2, but not well defined for Q^2 less than approximately 0.005-0.008 GeV^2
- At 600 MeV antineutrino beam energy, corrections can reach percent level at backward angles
- The correction at zero momentum transfer can be estimated as 2*alpha^2*Z^(4/3)/(M*R_0)^2 * (1 + E'_ell/M)^2 * ln(zeta/E'_ell), which is at permille level or below for E_nu less than approximately 3 GeV
- Nuclear radii used: ^2H (2.1421 fm), ^12C (2.4702 fm), ^16O (2.6991 fm), ^40Ar (3.4274 fm), ^56Fe (3.7377 fm), ^208Pb (5.5012 fm)
- Regularization parameter zeta varied between 10^{-10} GeV and the atomic screening scale zeta = m_e*Z^(1/3)/192

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed for QCD medium effects in parton scattering in large nuclei (Moliere 1947, Gyulassy 2002, Qiu 2003, Idilbi 2008, Ovanesyan 2011) to QED applications for electron, muon, and (anti)neutrino scattering off large nuclei. The authors note that previous work on radiative corrections for neutrino reactions (Day 2012, Tomalak 2021, Tomalak 2022) did not account for photon exchange with the nuclear medium. The results point to the need for including these medium effects in precision analyses of neutrino oscillation experiments (T2K, Hyper-Kamiokande, DUNE) and electron scattering experiments, and suggest that applying this correction to elastic electron-proton scattering data could reduce the extracted proton charge radius, potentially helping to resolve the discrepancy between the A1@MAMI result (0.879 +/- 0.008 fm) and the PRad measurement (0.838 fm) and muonic hydrogen Lamb shift extraction (0.84087 +/- 0.00039 fm).
