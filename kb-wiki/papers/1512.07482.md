# Double vector meson production in $γγ$ interactions at hadronic colliders

> In this paper we revisit the double vector meson production in $γγ$ interactions at heavy ion collisions and present, by the first time, predictions for the $ρρ$ and $J/ΨJ/Ψ$ production in proton -- nucleus and proton -- proton collisions. In order to obtain realistic predictions for rapidity distributions and total cross sections for the double vector production in ultra peripheral hadronic collisions we take into account of the description of $γγ\rightarrow VV$ cross section at low energies as well as its behaviour at large energies, associated to the gluonic interaction between the color dipoles. Our results demonstrate that the double $ρ$ production is dominated by the low energy behaviour of the $γγ\rightarrow VV$ cross section. In contrast, for the double $J/Ψ$ production, the contribution associated to the description of the QCD dynamics at high energies contributes significantly, mainly in $pp$ collisions. Predictions for the RHIC, LHC, FCC and CEPC - SPPC energies are shown.

# Double vector meson production in gamma-gamma interactions at hadronic colliders

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that double J/Psi production in gamma-gamma interactions at hadronic colliders, particularly in proton-proton collisions, receives a significant contribution from high-energy QCD dynamics (gluonic dipole-dipole interactions), making it a viable probe of QCD dynamics at high energies; however, double rho production remains dominated by low-energy mechanisms across all collision systems.

## Method

The authors combine a low-energy description of the gamma-gamma to VV cross section (using a fit to experimental data for rho-rho and box diagrams for J/Psi-J/Psi) with a high-energy contribution calculated in the color dipole formalism using the IIM-S model for the forward scattering amplitude, which incorporates non-linear QCD effects. The total cross section for hadronic collisions is obtained by folding the gamma-gamma cross section with equivalent photon spectra from the colliding hadrons, using the Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA) with an absorption factor to select only ultra-peripheral collisions. Predictions are generated for pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions at RHIC, LHC, FCC, and CEPC-SPPC energies.

## Key numbers

- For double rho production in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV: total cross section = 1.73 million nb (low energy only) vs 1.97 million nb (low energy + IIM-S)
- For double J/Psi production in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV: total cross section = 217,019 pb (low energy only) vs 658,589 pb (low energy + IIM-S), an enhancement factor of approximately 3 at Y=0
- For double J/Psi production in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=14 TeV: total cross section = 0.030 pb (low energy only) vs 0.48 pb (low energy + IIM-S), an enhancement factor of approximately 40 at Y=0
- For double rho production in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=14 TeV: total cross section = 0.17 nb (low energy only) vs 0.24 nb (low energy + IIM-S)
- Maximum gamma-gamma invariant mass probed: W less than or equal to 160 GeV for PbPb at sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV, W less than or equal to 4500 GeV for pp at sqrt(s)=14 TeV
- Slope parameters used: B_rho_rho = 10 GeV^-2, B_psi_psi = 0.44 GeV^-2

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of double vector meson production in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions by Goncalves et al. (vicmagvv1, vicmagvv2, vicmagvv3) which focused only on the high-energy regime, and by Antoni et al. (antonirhorho, antonipsipsi) which considered low-energy mechanisms but disregarded QCD dynamics at high energies. The authors combine both approaches and present first predictions for pp and pPb collisions. The results enable future experimental searches at the LHC, FCC, and CEPC-SPPC to constrain QCD dynamics at high energies through double J/Psi production, while confirming that double rho production is not sensitive to high-energy QCD effects.
