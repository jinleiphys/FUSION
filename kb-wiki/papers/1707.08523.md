# Hydrodynamical corrections to electromagnetic emissivities in QCD

> We provide a general framework for the derivation of the hydrodynamical corrections to the QCD electromagnetic emissivities in a viscous fluid. Assuming that the emission times are short in comparison to the fluid evolution time, we show that the leading corrections in the fluid gradients are controlled by the bulk and shear tensors times pertinent response functions involving the energy-momentum tensor. In a hadronic fluid phase, we explicit these contributions using spectral functions. Using the vector dominance approximation, we show that the bulk viscosity correction to the photon rate is sizable, while the shear viscosity is negligible for about all frequencies. In the partonic phase near the transition temperature we provide an assessment of the viscous corrections to the photon and dilepton emissions, using a non-perturbative quark-gluon plasma with soft thermal gluonic corrections in the form of operators of leading mass dimension. Again, the thermal bulk viscosity corrections are found to be larger than the thermal shear viscosity corrections at all energies for both the photon and dilepton in the partonic phase.

## Key claim

The paper provides a general, non-perturbative framework for deriving hydrodynamical corrections to QCD electromagnetic emissivities, showing that the leading gradient corrections are controlled by bulk and shear tensors times Kubo-like response functions; the key hedge is that this framework assumes emission times are short compared to the fluid evolution time, and the numerical estimates rely on the vector dominance approximation (VDM) for the hadronic phase and a non-perturbative operator expansion for the partonic phase.

## Method

The authors organize the electromagnetic emissivities in a near-equilibrium fluid by expanding the density matrix in fluid gradients, yielding corrections proportional to the bulk scalar θ = ∂_m β_m /3 and shear tensor σ_ij times three-point correlation functions involving the energy-momentum tensor. In the hadronic phase, these response functions are evaluated using spectral functions and the vector dominance model (VDM) to saturate the axial-vector spectral function Im Π_A. In the partonic phase near the transition temperature, the viscous corrections are assessed using a non-perturbative quark-gluon plasma with soft thermal gluonic corrections treated as operators of leading mass dimension.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity correction to the hadronic photon rate is about 30% across all frequencies for a hadronic fluid with size L ≈ 10 fm and temperature T ≈ 200 MeV (gradients θ ≈ σ ≈ 1/10)
- Shear viscosity correction to the hadronic photon rate is negligible for about all frequencies in the VDM approximation

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electromagnetic emissivities from thermal equilibrium (Larry, Steele) by incorporating viscous hydrodynamical corrections without relying on weakly coupled kinetic theory, which was the approach used in prior viscous corrections (VISCO). It also contrasts with holographic calculations for N=4 SUSY (Hong, Baier). The framework enables non-perturbative assessments of viscous corrections to photon and dilepton rates in both hadronic and partonic phases, which can be checked against current hydrodynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
