# First-order Phase Transition and Resulted Expansion in Momentum Space

> We point out, according to the principle of entropy growth, that the volume occupied by the system in the momentum space should expand during the first-order phase transition. Such an expansion is visualized by the simulation using the transport model based on a NJL-typed Lagrangian, and quantified by several observables, some of which, proposed for the first time in this article, could be the probes of the first-order phase transition either between the quark gluon plasma and the hadronic phase taking place in the heavy-ion collisions or of the other expanding systems.

## Key claim
Based on the principle of entropy growth, the volume occupied by a system in momentum space must expand during a first-order phase transition to compensate for spatial clustering, and this expansion can be quantified by several observables that may serve as probes of the QGP-to-hadron phase transition in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The author performed transport model simulations based on a SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type Lagrangian, using a 20 fm x 20 fm x 20 fm box with periodic boundary conditions initially filled with uniformly and thermally distributed quarks at T = 20 MeV and net quark density n_q = 0.5 fm^{-3}. The system was initially spinodally unstable and underwent phase decomposition, during which the momentum-space expansion was visualized and quantified through several observables including the quadratic mean of momenta, average densities in coordinate and momentum space, scaled density moments, and spatial and momentum entropies.

## Key numbers
- Initial simulation conditions: box size 20 fm x 20 fm x 20 fm, temperature T = 20 MeV, net quark density n_q = 0.5 fm^{-3}
- For entropy calculations, unit volumes were chosen as v_x = (0.5 fm)^3 in spatial space and v_p = (12 MeV)^3 in momentum space
- The thermal expectation value of momentum entropy is set to <S_p>_th = ln(alpha), where alpha is a constant coefficient
- The quark mass at hadronization was assumed to be 0.1 GeV for calculating the unit volume v_p^{(alpha)}

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of first-order phase transitions in quantum chromodynamics, where lattice QCD calculations (Bernard 2004, Aoki 2006, Bazavov 2012) show a crossover at small baryon chemical potentials but the fermion sign problem prevents calculations at large chemical potentials. The paper builds on numerous phenomenological models (Stephanov 2004, Stephanov 2007, Fukushima 2008, Baym 2017) that predict a first-order phase transition at large baryon chemical potentials, and on prior work proposing observables such as light nuclei yield ratios (Sun 2017, Sun 2018, Sun 2020) as indirect signals. The proposed momentum-space observables could be tested in beam energy scan experiments (Bzdak 2019) and may also apply to other expanding systems such as nuclear matter undergoing liquid-gas transitions.
