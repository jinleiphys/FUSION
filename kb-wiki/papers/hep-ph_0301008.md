# The Vector Manifestation and Effective Degrees of Freedom At Chiral Restoration

> The role of effective degrees of freedom on the vector and axial-vector susceptibilities and the pion velocity at chiral restoration is analyzed. We consider two possible scenarios, one in which pions are considered to be the only low-lying degrees of freedom -- that we shall refer to as "standard" -- and the other in which pions, vector mesons and constituent quarks (or quasiquarks in short) are the relevant low-lying degrees of freedom -- that we shall refer to as "vector manifestation (VM)." We show at one-loop order in chiral perturbation theory with hidden local symmetry Lagrangian that while in the standard scenario, the pion velocity vanishes at the chiral transition, it instead approaches unity in the VM scenario. If the VM is realized in nature, the chiral phase structure of hadronic matter can be much richer than that in the standard one and the phase transition will be a smooth crossover: Sharp vector and scalar excitations are expected in the vicinity of the critical point. Some indirect indications that lend support to the VM scenario, and in consequence to BR scaling, are discussed.

# The Vector Manifestation and Effective Degrees of Freedom At Chiral Restoration

## Key claim

At one-loop order in chiral perturbation theory with hidden local symmetry, the pion velocity approaches unity (rather than vanishing) at the chiral restoration temperature if vector mesons and constituent quarks are included as relevant low-lying degrees of freedom in the vector manifestation scenario, whereas in the standard scenario where only pions are considered, the pion velocity vanishes at the transition. The author notes that these are one-loop results and that higher-order corrections could potentially restore the standard result of a vanishing pion velocity.

## Method

The author analyzes two scenarios for the effective degrees of freedom near chiral restoration: the "standard" scenario using a linear sigma model with only pions and a scalar meson, and the "vector manifestation" (VM) scenario using a hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian that includes pions, light-quark vector mesons, and constituent quarks (quasiquarks). For the three-flavor case in the chiral limit, the HLS Lagrangian is matched to QCD at a scale below heavy meson masses but above the vector meson mass, and one-loop calculations of the vector and axial-vector susceptibilities and the pion velocity are performed using renormalization group flow toward the VM fixed point where the gauge coupling g approaches zero and the ratio a = F_sigma/F_pi approaches 1.

## Key numbers

- At the chiral restoration temperature T_c in the VM scenario, the pion velocity v_pi = 1 (approaches unity)
- At T = T_c in the VM scenario, both the temporal and spatial components of the pion decay constant vanish: f_pi^t = f_pi^s = 0
- At T = T_c in the VM scenario, the vector and axial-vector susceptibilities are equal: chi_A = chi_V = 2 N_f [(N_f/12) T_c^2 + (N_c/6) T_c^2]
- From deeply bound pionic atom data, the in-medium pion decay constant squared at nuclear matter density n_0 is (f_pi^*(n_0)/f_pi)^2_exp = 0.65 ± 0.05
- The theoretical prediction from BR scaling for the same quantity is (f_pi^*(n_0)/f_pi)^2_th = 0.61, corresponding to Phi(n_0) = 0.78 with ~10% uncertainty

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation framework developed by Harada and Yamawaki (2000-2003) for chiral restoration, contrasting it with the standard picture by Son and Stephanov (2002) where only pions are relevant and the pion velocity vanishes at the critical point. The VM scenario predicts a richer phase structure with sharp vector and scalar excitations near the critical point and a smooth crossover transition, in contrast to the standard O(4) universality class picture. The author discusses how the VM connects to BR scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) and to the Fermi-liquid fixed point at nuclear matter density, and notes that the CF unlocking mechanism proposed by Wetterich (2000) may resolve the issue of chiral partners for transverse vector mesons at the VM point.
