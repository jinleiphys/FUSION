# An overview of (selected) recent results in finite-temperature lattice QCD

> I discuss recent results on lattice QCD calculations with the main emphasis on the thermodynamics of the crossover region, restoration of the chiral symmetry and fluctuations of conserved charges as indicator of deconfinement, that may also be used to determine the chemical freeze-out conditions in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in full QCD with physical quark masses, there is no genuine phase transition but a rapid crossover, and that different observables (chiral condensate, Polyakov loop, fluctuations of conserved charges) show changes in their temperature dependence at somewhat different temperatures, so no single temperature can be assigned to deconfinement.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD calculations using staggered fermions (asqtad and HISQ/tree actions) on lattices with temporal extent Nτ=6, 8, and 12, with light-to-strange quark mass ratio ml=ms/20 (close to the physical ml=ms/27). The chiral condensate and its disconnected susceptibility are computed and fitted to O(N) scaling functions to extract the pseudo-critical temperature. Fluctuations of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness are calculated from derivatives of the pressure with respect to chemical potentials and extrapolated to the continuum limit.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature from chiral susceptibility in the continuum limit at physical light quark mass: Tpc = 154(9) MeV (Bazavov 2011)
- Budapest-Wuppertal collaboration results for Tpc range from 147 to 157 MeV, depending on the chiral observable used (Borsanyi 2010)
- Pure gauge SU(3) first-order phase transition temperature: Tc ≈ 260 MeV (Boyd 1996)
- Hadron Resonance Gas model agrees with lattice data for fluctuations of conserved charges up to T ∼ 150-160 MeV
- Gold-gold and lead-lead collisions have charge-to-baryon ratio r ≈ 0.4

## Context

This review extends the established finding that QCD at physical quark masses and zero chemical potential exhibits a crossover rather than a phase transition (Bernard 2004, Cheng 2006, Aoki 2006). The work builds on earlier scaling analyses by Ejiri (2009) and provides comparisons between staggered fermion results and those from Wilson fermions at mπ=545 MeV (Borsanyi 2012), overlap fermions at mπ=350 MeV (Borsanyi 2012), and domain-wall fermions at mπ=200 MeV (HotQCD 2012). The results enable determination of chemical freeze-out conditions in heavy-ion collisions by comparing lattice calculations of cumulant ratios (R31^Q and R12^Q) with experimental data, as demonstrated in Bazavov (2012).
