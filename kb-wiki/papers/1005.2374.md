# On Gamow-Teller strength distributions for $ββ$-decaying nuclei within continuum-QRPA

> An isospin-selfconsistent pn-continuum-QRPA approach is formulated and applied to describe the Gamow-Teller strength distributions for $ββ$-decaying open-shell nuclei. The calculation results obtained for the pairs of nuclei $^{76}$Ge-Se, $^{100}$Mo-Ru, $^{116}$Cd-Sn, and $^{130}$Te-Xe are compared with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper formulates an isospin-selfconsistent proton-neutron continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-cQRPA) approach that exactly treats the single-particle continuum in the particle-hole channel, and applies it to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions for four pairs of double-beta-decaying nuclei, finding that the calculated strength distributions and the resulting two-neutrino double-beta decay amplitudes show sensitivity to the ratio of triplet to singlet particle-particle interaction strengths, with the amplitude decomposable into a term that vanishes when spin-isospin SU(4) symmetry is restored in the particle-particle sector.

## Method

The authors transform the discrete pn-QRPA equations into coordinate representation and then replace the sum over discrete single-particle states in the continuum by the radial single-particle Green function, yielding a continuum version (pn-cQRPA) that treats the particle-hole channel exactly. The model Hamiltonian uses a partially selfconsistent mean field (isoscalar part from a Woods-Saxon parametrization, isovector part from the isospin-selfconsistency condition relating the symmetry potential to the neutron-excess density via the Landau-Migdal interaction), zero-range Landau-Migdal forces in the particle-hole channel, and zero-range forces in the particle-particle channel with separate strengths for spin-singlet and spin-triplet channels. The pairing problem is solved within BCS theory on a basis of bound and quasibound single-particle states, and the resulting equations for the effective fields are solved to obtain the Gamow-Teller strength functions and the two-neutrino double-beta decay amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the pn-continuum-QRPA approach initially proposed in Ref. [5] for single-open-shell nuclei and further developed in Ref. [6] for double-beta decay matrix elements, by using a modern version of the phenomenological isoscalar mean field deduced in Ref. [7] from isospin-selfconsistent analysis of experimental single-particle spectra in doubly-closed-shell nuclei. The approach addresses the well-known sensitivity of the two-neutrino double-beta decay amplitude to the ratio g_pp of triplet to singlet particle-particle interaction strengths, which was analyzed in Ref. [41] in terms of SU(4) symmetry violation. The results enable more reliable evaluation of double-beta decay amplitudes by providing a framework that exactly treats the single-particle continuum in the particle-hole channel while maintaining isospin selfconsistency.
