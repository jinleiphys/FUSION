# Femtoscopy with unlike particles

> The possibilities of unlike particle correlations for a study of space--time characteristics of particle production are demonstrated. The correlation data from heavy ion collisions is discussed, particularly - in terms of the transport RQMD and hydrodynamic models. An attention is paid to the relative space-time asymmetries in the production of different particle species, measured as a ratio of the correlation functions corresponding to different directional selections of the relative momentum vector. Being sensitive to the relative time delays and to the intensity of collective flows, these asymmetries can become a promising tool in a study of the deconfinement transition and the formation of quark-gluon plasma.

# Femtoscopy with unlike particles

## Key claim

Unlike particle correlations can serve as a complementary femtoscopy tool to identical particle interferometry, but their interpretation requires careful treatment of the Coulomb final-state interaction's sensitivity to the tail of the relative distance distribution, which can lead to inconsistencies in simple Gaussian parametrizations.

## Method

The two-particle correlation function is defined as the ratio of the differential two-particle production cross section to a reference distribution constructed by mixing particles from different events, normalized to unity at large relative velocities. The correlation function is calculated by averaging the squared, properly symmetrized Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (approximated by the stationary scattering solution at equal emission times in the pair center-of-mass system) over the emitter four-coordinates and total spin. For the NA49 data analysis, events simulated with the RQMD v.2.3 transport code were used, weighting simulated pairs by squares of the corresponding wave functions, with space-time coordinates of emission points scaled by factors of 0.7, 0.8, and 1.

## Key numbers

- For $\pi^+\pi^-$, $\pi^+p$, and $\pi^-p$ systems from central Pb+Pb 158 AGeV collisions (NA49): purity = 0.77 ± 0.01, 0.76 ± 0.03, 0.74 ± 0.04; scale = 0.78 ± 0.01, 0.87 ± 0.03, 0.88 ± 0.04 (based on 900k events)
- Bohr radii for various pairs: $\pi\pi$ = 387.5 fm, $\pi K$ = 248.6 fm, $\pi p$ = 222.5 fm, $KK$ = 109.6 fm, $K p$ = 83.6 fm, $pp$ = 57.6 fm
- For $\pi^+\pi^-$ system, RQMD model yields mean $r^*$ of 21 fm and 29 fm truncated at 50 fm and 500 fm respectively; scaling by 0.78 gives experimental truncated means of 16.4 fm and 22.6 fm for $r^* <$ 39 fm and 390 fm respectively
- For $p\Lambda$ correlations from Au+Au at AGS (E985): singlet and triplet s-wave scattering lengths of 2.3 fm and 1.8 fm; effective radii 3.0 fm and 3.2 fm; fit gives $\lambda = 0.5 \pm 0.2$, Gaussian radius $r_0 = 4.5 \pm 0.7$ fm
- For $\pi^+\pi^-$ system with strong interaction scale parameter: purity = 0.81 ± 0.01, scale = 0.72 ± 0.02, sisca = 0.63 ± 0.08 (indicating scattering length ~30% lower than table value)
- For $\Lambda\Lambda$ correlations (NA49): $\lambda = 0.9 \pm 0.6$, $r_0 = 1.5 \pm 0.3$ fm, $f_0 = 0.1 \pm 0.5$ fm; fixing $\lambda = 0.5$ gives $r_0 = 1.8 \pm 0.3$ fm, $f_0 = -2.6 \pm 2.6$ fm
- For $\pi^\pm p$ systems in central Pb+Pb at SPS (RQMD): $\langle\Delta x\rangle = -6.2$ fm, $\langle\Delta t\rangle = -0.5$ fm/c, $\langle\Delta x^*\rangle = -7.9$ fm (central rapidity); $\langle\Delta x\rangle = -5.2$ fm, $\langle\Delta t\rangle = 2.9$ fm/c, $\langle\Delta x^*\rangle = -8.5$ fm (NA49 acceptance)
- Hydrodynamic model for $\pi^\pm p$ systems: $\langle \Delta x\rangle = -3$ fm, $\langle \Delta x^*\rangle = -4$ fm

## Context

This work extends the Kopylov-Podgoretsky formalism for identical particle correlations (KP, 1972) to unlike particle systems, building on earlier work by Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (1981) on final-state interactions. The paper discusses correlation data from heavy ion collisions at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, comparing with predictions from the RQMD transport code (version 2.3) and hydrodynamic models. The directional asymmetry method, originally proposed by Lednicky, Lyuboshitz, Erazmus, and Nouais (1995), enables access to relative space-time asymmetries in particle emission, which can become a tool for studying the deconfinement transition and quark-gluon plasma formation. The analysis of $\pi^+\pi^-$ scattering length from correlation data shows consistency with chiral perturbation theory two-loop calculations (Colangelo, 2000) and recent $K_{l4}$ decay data.
