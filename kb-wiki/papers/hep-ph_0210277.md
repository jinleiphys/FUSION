# On baryon-antibaryon coupling to two photons

> We discuss recent claims that $p\bar{p} \to γγ$ may be described by a generalized parton picture. We propose that quark-hadron duality provides a justification for the effective dominance of the "handbag" diagram assumed in recent literature, and that handbag diagrams may dominate phenomena in kinematic regions far more extensive than that might be expected from pQCD alone.

# On baryon-antibaryon coupling to two photons

## Key claim
Quark-hadron duality provides a justification for the effective dominance of the "handbag" diagram in $p\bar{p} \to \gamma\gamma$ over a wide kinematic range, but contrary to prior claims, there is no dynamical reason to require the dominance of $x \to 1$ configurations; the handbag dominance arises from destructive interference among parity-even and parity-odd resonance states when averaged over $s$.

## Method
The authors apply the formalism of quark-hadron duality, previously developed for Compton scattering and deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS), to the crossed channel process $p\bar{p} \to \gamma\gamma$. They analyze the interference structure of coherent intermediate resonance contributions, showing that terms proportional to $\sum_{i \ne j} e_i e_j$ are suppressed by destructive interference in the $t$-channel, while terms proportional to $\sum_i e_i^2$ survive. This is extended from the $\gamma N \to \gamma N$ and $\gamma\gamma \to M\bar{M}$ cases to the baryon-antibaryon annihilation channel.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of cross sections for $p\bar{p}$ to $n\bar{n}$ annihilation into two photons is predicted to be $\sigma(p\bar{p})/\sigma(n\bar{n}) = (2e_u^2 + e_d^2)/(2e_d^2 + e_u^2) = 3/2$, corresponding to a fragmentation parameter $\rho = 1/2$.
- For the non-forward proton structure function, the factorization is given by $F_2(x,\xi,t) = \sum_i e_i^2 \frac{(x-\xi)(x+\xi)}{x^2} F_2(x) F_{el}(t)$, where $F_{el}(t=0)=1$.
- The vector form factor for $p\bar{p}\to\gamma\gamma$ is $R_V(s) \sim \sum_i e_i^2 F_{el}(s)$, with a dipole form $F_{el}(s) = 1/(1+s/\lambda^2)^2$.
- For $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-$ and $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^0\pi^0$, the duality argument implies $\sigma(\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-) \simeq \sigma(\gamma\gamma\to\pi^0\pi^0)$ in the hadronic continuum.

## Context
This paper extends the quark-hadron duality framework of Close, Gilman, and collaborators to justify the handbag diagram dominance assumed in the generalized parton picture of Freund, Weiss, Diehl, and Kroll (1999-2002) for $p\bar{p} \to \gamma\gamma$. It builds on earlier work showing that duality suppresses coherent (exotic) exchange contributions in real Compton scattering at $t=0$ (Close and Gilman) and in non-forward Compton scattering (Close and Zhao, 2002). The results enable the application of handbag phenomenology to baryon-antibaryon annihilation without requiring the strict $x \to 1$ kinematic limit, and predict specific charge-ratio relations that can be tested against future data.
