# Relativistic quark models

> The application of relativistic constituent quark models to the evaluation of the electromagnetic properties of the nucleon and its resonances is addressed. The role of the pair creation process in the Feynmann triangle diagram is discussed and the importance both of choosing the light-front formalism and of using a Breit frame where the plus component of the four-momentum transfer is vanishing, is stressed. The nucleon elastic form factors are calculated free of spurious effects related to the loss of rotational covariance. The effect of a finite constituent size is considered and the parameters describing the constituent form factors are fixed using only the nucleon data up to Q**2 ~ 1 (GeV/c)**2; a constituent charge radius of ~ 0.45 fm is obtained in this way. Above Q**2 ~ 1 (GeV/c)**2 our parameter-free predictions are in good agreement with the experimental data, showing that soft physics may be dominant up to Q**2 ~ 10 (GeV/c)**2. Our new results for the longitudinal and transverse helicity amplitudes of the N - P_{11}(1440) transition are presented.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a light-front (LF) relativistic constituent quark model, using a Breit frame with vanishing plus component of the four-momentum transfer, can reproduce nucleon elastic form factors up to Q² ~ 10 (GeV/c)² without free parameters above Q² ~ 1 (GeV/c)², provided that the constituent quark form factors are first fitted to low-Q² data. The key hedge is that the model’s success is limited to “soft physics” and that the neutron charge radius is underestimated by about 50%, suggesting missing many-body currents.

## Method
The author applies a light-front (LF) relativistic constituent quark model to compute the nucleon elastic form factors and the N – P₁₁(1440) transition helicity amplitudes. The one-body electromagnetic current is evaluated in a Breit frame with q⁺ = 0, which suppresses the pair-creation (Z-graph) contribution and avoids spurious effects from loss of rotational covariance. Constituent quark form factors are introduced to account for finite quark size, and their parameters are fitted to nucleon data up to Q² ~ 1 (GeV/c)²; above that scale the predictions are parameter-free.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark charge radius: ~ 0.45 fm, obtained from fitting nucleon data up to Q² ~ 1 (GeV/c)².
- The model predicts that soft physics may dominate up to Q² ~ 10 (GeV/c)².
- The neutron charge radius is underestimated by ~ 50% compared to experiment.
- The proton charge radius from the model with point-like constituent quarks is ~ 0.6 fm after including boost effects, still below the experimental value of ~ 0.8 fm.
- The electric-to-magnetic proton ratio μₚ G_E^p(Q²) / G_M^p(Q²) shows a sharp suppression above Q² ~ 1 (GeV/c)², in agreement with JLAB data.

## Context
This work extends earlier relativistic constituent quark model calculations by the same author (Simula, 2001) and others (e.g., Cardarelli et al., 1999) by removing spurious effects from loss of rotational covariance. It contrasts with non-relativistic and relativized models (Isgur, 1999; Glozman, 1999) that predicted a proton size of ~ 0.3 fm. The results enable parameter-free predictions for nucleon form factors up to high Q² and provide new calculations for the N – P₁₁(1440) transition, which is relevant for identifying possible hybrid components in the Roper resonance.
