# The Equation of State of Dense Matter : from Nuclear Collisions to Neutron Stars

> The Equation of State (EoS) of dense matter represents a central issue in the study of compact astrophysical objects and heavy ion reactions at intermediate and relativistic energies. We have derived a nuclear EoS with nucleons and hyperons within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, and joined it with quark matter EoS. For that, we have employed the MIT bag model, as well as the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) and the Color Dielectric (CD) models, and found that the NS maximum masses are not larger than 1.7 solar masses. A comparison with available data supports the idea that dense matter EoS should be soft at low density and quite stiff at high density.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the maximum mass of neutron stars is not larger than 1.7 solar masses when both hyperonic degrees of freedom and a transition to quark matter are included, but this result depends on the specific quark matter model used and the observational constraint that a very heavy neutron star (above 1.7 solar masses) would indicate problems with current theoretical modeling of high-density nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors derived a nuclear equation of state (EoS) using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with nucleons and hyperons (Lambda and Sigma-), employing both phenomenological (Urbana IX) and microscopic meson-exchange three-body forces (TBF). They then joined this hadronic EoS with quark matter EoS using three different models, the MIT bag model, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, and the Color Dielectric (CD) model, via the Maxwell construction for a sharp phase transition, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass with hyperons only: less than 1.3 solar masses for all nuclear TBF considered
- Maximum neutron star mass with hyperons and quark matter: about 1.5 solar masses (MIT bag model case)
- Maximum neutron star mass with microscopic TBF and no hyperons: about 2.3 solar masses
- Maximum neutron star mass with phenomenological TBF and no hyperons: about 1.9 solar masses
- Hyperon onset density: about 2-3 times normal nuclear matter density (approximately 0.32-0.48 fm^-3)
- Phase transition density in extended MIT bag model: approximately 0.6 fm^-3
- Phase transition density in Color Dielectric model: approximately 0.05 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy at saturation point: approximately 30 MeV
- Incompressibility at saturation: approximately 210 MeV
- Measured mass of PSR1913+16: 1.44 solar masses
- Reported mass of PSR J0751+1807: 2.1 +/- 0.2 solar masses (at 2-sigma confidence, at least 1.6 solar masses allowed)
- Hybrid neutron star radius reduction: about 3 km smaller than purely hadronic stars, to typically 9 km

## Context

This work extends the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach to include hyperons (following earlier work by the same group on hyperonic matter, cited as hypmat and hypns) and compares results with variational many-body calculations by Pandharipande and collaborators (1997). The paper addresses the tension between the soft EoS required by heavy-ion collision flow data (Danielewicz et al., 2002) and the stiff EoS needed to support observed high pulsar masses like PSR J0751+1807 (Nice et al., 2005). The results enable future work to test whether additional repulsive contributions in quark matter EoS or modifications to the hadronic sector can reconcile neutron star observations with theoretical predictions.
