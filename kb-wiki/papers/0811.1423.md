# Relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation. II. Pygmy dipole resonance in neutron-rich nuclei

> Theoretical studies of low-lying dipole strength in even-even spherical nuclei within the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) are presented. The RQTBA developed recently as an extension of the self-consistent relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) enables one to investigate effects of coupling of two-quasiparticle excitations to collective vibrations within a fully consistent calculation scheme based on covariant energy density functional theory. Dipole spectra of even-even $^{130}$Sn -- $^{140}$Sn and $^{68}$Ni -- $^{78}$Ni isotopes calculated within both RQRPA and RQTBA show two well separated collective structures: the higher-lying giant dipole resonance (GDR) and the lower-lying pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) which can be identified by a different behavior of the transition densities of states in these regions.

## Key claim

The relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) predicts that the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in neutron-rich nuclei is not a pure mode but always couples to surface vibrations, and that this coupling is the main mechanism bringing dipole strength to energies far below the neutron threshold, whereas the self-consistent relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) alone yields no dipole strength in that low-energy region for stable nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the nuclear response function in the doubled quasiparticle space, using a residual interaction that contains both a static part from covariant energy density functional theory (with the NL3 parameter set) and an energy-dependent part derived from quasiparticle-phonon coupling within the quasiparticle time blocking approximation. Pairing correlations are treated in the BCS approximation. The dipole strength function and photoabsorption cross section are computed for even-even spherical tin isotopes from 130Sn to 140Sn and nickel isotopes from 68Ni to 78Ni, with the RQTBA results compared to RQRPA calculations and available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The GDR width in doubly magic 132Sn is reduced to 3.4 MeV in the RQTBA calculations, compared to typical values of 5-5.5 MeV in open-shell nuclei.
- The RQRPA predicts the first 2+ state at 0.6 MeV in 140Sn, indicating a trend toward static quadrupole deformation.
- The PDR peak in 116Sn appears at 8.94 MeV in the RQRPA, while the lowest GDR peak is at 11.78 MeV.
- In 140Sn, the PDR peak is at 7.18 MeV and the lowest GDR peak at 10.94 MeV in the RQRPA.
- The GDR mean energy follows the systematic rule ⟨E⟩ ≈ 80 A^(-1/3).

## Context

This work extends the RQTBA developed in Litvinova, Ring, Tselyaev, and Langanke (2008) to very neutron-rich tin isotopes beyond 130Sn, building on self-consistent RQRPA calculations by Paar, Niksic, Vretenar, and Ring (2003). The results confirm the picture from Litvinova, Ring, and Tselyaev (2007) that the PDR arises from resonant oscillation of the neutron skin against the isospin-saturated core, but show that pairing correlations in open-shell nuclei cause much stronger fragmentation than in magic nuclei. The calculations enable future statistical model studies comparing microscopically derived dipole strengths with the standard Lorentzian approximation for neutron capture cross sections relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis.
