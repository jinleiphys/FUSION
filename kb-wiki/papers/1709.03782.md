# Charge-exchange quasi-elastic process $nd\to p(nn)$ under $0^\circ$ in the frame of elastic $np\to np$ scattering to $180^\circ$

> It is considered the problem of spin physics related with the difference of representation of the elastic interaction between the neutron and proton. In the first case the charge-exchange reaction $np\to pn$ under the angle $θ$ is supposed, in the second --- the simple elastic scattering of $np\to np$, when the neutron is going in opposite direction $π-θ$. The transition from one representation to another is provided by the Majorana operator. In the framework of impulse approximation it is twice calculated the quasi-elastic charge-exchange reaction of a neutron on a deuteron. In the frame of $nd\to p(nn)$ scattering of proton to the angle $θ$ it gives the well-known Dean formula. Using other representation $nd\to (nn)p$ as a neutron elastic scattering under the angle $π-θ$ (together with neutron-spectator in $nn$-pair) the alternative formula is presented.

## Key claim

The charge-exchange quasi-elastic reaction \(nd\to p(nn)\) at \(0^\circ\) can be equivalently represented as elastic \(np\to np\) scattering at \(180^\circ\) via the Majorana operator, yielding an alternative formula to the well-known Dean formula within the impulse approximation.

## Method

The authors apply the impulse approximation to the quasi-elastic charge-exchange reaction of a neutron on a deuteron, calculating the process twice using two different representations. In the first representation, the reaction \(nd\to p(nn)\) is treated as proton scattering to angle \(\theta\), which reproduces the Dean formula. In the second representation, the process is viewed as neutron elastic scattering at angle \(\pi-\theta\) (with a neutron-spectator forming an \(nn\)-pair), leading to an alternative formula. The transition between representations is mediated by the Majorana operator \(\widehat{P}_M\), and the formalism uses the Goldberger-Watson scattering matrix with five complex amplitudes \((a,b,c,e,f)\) for each isotopic state.

## Key numbers

- \(R_{dp}(0)\) values at neutron kinetic energies \(T_n\) from 0.55 to 2.0 GeV: ranges from 0.546 \(\pm\) 0.024 (at 0.8 GeV) to 0.608 \(\pm\) 0.035 (at 0.55 GeV), measured in the Delta Sigma experiment (2003-2007).
- \(r^{nfl/fl}_{np\to pn}(0)\) values at the same energies: ranges from 0.097 \(\pm\) 0.062 (at 0.55 GeV) to 0.222 \(\pm\) 0.053 (at 0.8 GeV).
- World data on \(R_{dp}(0)\) at higher kinetic energies: 0.397 \(\pm\) 0.044 at 90 MeV (UCRL, 1951); 0.587 \(\pm\) 0.029 at 96 MeV (Harwell, 1967); 0.553 \(\pm\) 0.030 at 200 MeV (JINR LNP, 1962); 0.600 \(\pm\) 0.080 at 647 MeV (LAMPF, 1976); 0.550 \(\pm\) 0.080 at 997 MeV (JINR LHE, 2002).
- World data on \(r^{nfl/fl}_{np\to pn}(0)\): 0.679 \(\pm\) 0.186 at 90 MeV; 0.136 \(\pm\) 0.056 at 96 MeV; 0.205 \(\pm\) 0.065 at 200 MeV; 0.111 \(\pm\) 0.148 at 647 MeV; 0.212 \(\pm\) 0.176 at 997 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of charge-exchange reactions on deuterons, building on the Pomeranchuk-Chew hypothesis (Chew, 1950s) and the Migdal idea (Migdal, 1950s) that treat the \(nd\to p(nn)\) reaction using free-nucleon scattering. The paper provides a unitary transformation between the charge-exchange representation and the elastic scattering representation, generalizing earlier amplitude transformations by Lehar and collaborators that were limited to symmetry rules with angle replacement \(\theta\to\pi-\theta\). The results enable consistent comparison of experimental \(R_{dp}\) and \(r^{nfl/fl}\) ratios across a wide energy range from 0.55 GeV to 997 MeV, using data from multiple laboratories including UCRL, Harvard, Harwell, JINR, LAMPF, and LRL.
