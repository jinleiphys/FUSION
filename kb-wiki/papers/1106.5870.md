# Can transport peak explain the low-mass enhancement of dileptons at RHIC?

> We propose a novel relation between the low-mass enhancement of dielectrons observed at PHENIX and transport coefficients of QGP such as the charge diffusion constant $D$ and the relaxation time $τ_{\rm J}$. We parameterize the transport peak in the spectral function using the second-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics by Israel and Stewart. Combining the spectral function and the full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical evolution with the lattice EoS, theoretical dielectron spectra and the experimental data are compared. Detailed analysis suggests that the low-mass dilepton enhancement originates mainly from the high-temperature QGP phase where there is a large electric charge fluctuation as obtained from lattice QCD simulations.

# Can transport peak explain the low-mass enhancement of dileptons at RHIC?

## Key claim
The low-mass dielectron enhancement observed by PHENIX at RHIC can be explained by the transport peak in the QGP spectral function, parameterized via second-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics, but only if the charge diffusion constant satisfies D ≥ 2/T and the extracted parameter sets (DT, τ_JT) lie far from both pQCD and AdS/CFT predictions.

## Method
The authors parameterized the transport peak in the electromagnetic spectral function using Israel-Stewart theory for causal dissipative hydrodynamics, introducing three transport coefficients: the charge diffusion constant D, the conductivity σ, and the relaxation time τ_J. They combined this spectral function with a full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic model using a lattice QCD equation of state to compute dielectron spectra, then compared these spectra to PHENIX data for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The diffusion constant must satisfy D ≥ 2/T to explain the data; with τ_J = 0, data cannot be explained for D < 2/T.
- A parameter set (DT, τ_JT) = (10, 0.5) shows good agreement with the experimental data.
- pQCD estimates give (DT, τ_JT) = (4, 15); AdS/CFT estimates give (DT, τ_JT) = (1/(2π), ln2/(2π)).
- The susceptibility is parameterized as χ(T) = 0.28 T² [1 + tanh((T[GeV] - 0.155)/0.023)] from lattice data.
- Dielectrons are emitted dominantly from matter in the temperature range 0.2 ≤ T ≤ 0.3 GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous phenomenological studies of dilepton production at SPS and RHIC by Rapp (2010), which considered hadronic many-body theory and perturbative QGP spectral functions but did not include non-perturbative transport processes in the QGP phase and crossover region. The results enable future extraction of transport coefficients from experimental data and motivate further study of the transverse momentum dependence of dielectron spectra. The extracted parameter sets differ significantly from both weak-coupling pQCD calculations by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000, 2003) and Hong (2010) and strong-coupling AdS/CFT calculations by Natsuume (2007).
