# Pairing in the continuum: the quadrupole response of the Borromean nucleus 6He

> The ground state and low-lying continuum states of 6He are found within a shell model scheme, in a basis of two-particle states built out of continuum p-states of the unbound 5He nucleus, using a simple pairing contact-delta interaction. This accounts for the Borromean character of the bound ground state, revealing its composition. We investigate the quadrupole response of the system and we put our calculations into perspective with the latest experimental results. The calculated quadrupole strength distribution reproduces the narrow 2+ resonance, while a second wider peak is found at about 3.9 MeV above the g.s. energy.

## Key claim

The Borromean bound ground state of ⁶He emerges from coupling two unbound p-wave continuum states of ⁵He via a simple contact-delta pairing interaction, and the calculated quadrupole strength distribution reproduces the narrow 2⁺ resonance while a second wider peak is found at about 3.9 MeV above the ground state energy, though this second peak does not match the recently identified 2⁺ strength at 2.6 MeV from experiment.

## Method

The authors construct a basis of two-particle states from continuum p-states of the unbound ⁵He nucleus, using Woods-Saxon potentials to generate the single-particle continuum wavefunctions with energies from 0.0 to 10.0 MeV. They discretize the continuum with energy spacings down to 0.1 MeV (basis size N=100), calculate matrix elements of an attractive pairing contact-delta interaction, and diagonalize the resulting matrices for J=0 and J=2. The quadrupole response is computed as B(E2) values to the resulting eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- The ⁵He p₃/₂ resonance is at 0.789 MeV above neutron separation threshold with width 0.648 MeV; the p₁/₂ resonance is at 1.27 MeV with width 5.57 MeV
- The ⁶He ground state is bound by 0.973 MeV; the narrow 2⁺ resonance is at 1.797 MeV ± 25 keV above ground state with width 113 ± 20 keV
- A second resonance in ⁶He is found at about 5.6 ± 0.3 MeV with width 12.1 ± 1.1 MeV (uncertain spin-parity)
- Recent experimental observations (Moug et al.) report a 2⁺ state at 2.6(3) MeV with Γ=1.6(4) MeV and a 1⁽⁺,⁻⁾ state at 5.3(3) MeV with Γ=2(1) MeV
- The calculated quadrupole strength distribution shows a first peak at E=0.76 MeV with Γ∼0.2 MeV and a second peak at E=2.91 MeV with Γ∼1.8 MeV (3.88 MeV above ground state)
- Total integrated quadrupole strength is about 8.8 e²fm⁴, with about 3/4 in the first peak
- The ground state decomposition shows (p₃/₂)² components sum to 97.2%

## Context

This work extends the Migdal (year not given) argument for Borromean stability and the Hansen and Jonson (year not given) dineutron cluster model by explicitly treating the continuum of ⁵He rather than using bound or box-discretized states as in earlier shell model approaches (Mei and van Isacker, year not given). The calculations confirm the quadrupole response prediction of Lay (year not given) and highlight a discrepancy with experimental data from Moug et al. (year not given) regarding the position of the second 2⁺ resonance, calling for further experimental and theoretical work on the structure of ⁶He low-lying resonances.
