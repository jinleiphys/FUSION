# Long-Range Rapidity Correlations in Heavy Ion Collisions at Strong Coupling from AdS/CFT

> We use AdS/CFT correspondence to study two-particle correlations in heavy ion collisions at strong coupling. Modeling the colliding heavy ions by shock waves on the gravity side, we observe that at early times after the collision there are long-range rapidity correlations present in the two-point functions for the glueball and the energy-momentum tensor operators. We estimate rapidity correlations at later times by assuming that the evolution of the system is governed by ideal Bjorken hydrodynamics, and find that glueball correlations in this state are suppressed at large rapidity intervals, suggesting that late-time medium dynamics can not "wash out" the long-range rapidity correlations that were formed at early times. These results may provide an insight on the nature of the "ridge" correlations observed in heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, and in proton-proton collisions at LHC.

## Key claim

Using AdS/CFT correspondence to model heavy ion collisions as shock waves in AdS$_5$, the authors find that long-range rapidity correlations are present in two-point functions at early times after the collision, but these correlations exhibit a growth with rapidity interval that is stronger than observed experimentally, suggesting that higher-order corrections may be needed to flatten this dependence.

## Method

The authors model the colliding heavy ions as two shock waves in AdS$_5$ with delta-function profiles, and solve Einstein equations perturbatively to order $\mu_1 \mu_2$ in the shock wave strengths. They compute the two-point correlation function of the glueball operator $J(x) = \frac{1}{2} \tr[F_{\mu\nu} F^{\mu\nu}]$ by evaluating the dual massless scalar field action in the perturbed metric, and also compute the correlator of the energy-momentum tensor $T^1_2$. For late-time estimates, they use the Janik-Peschanski metric dual to ideal Bjorken hydrodynamics and perform a perturbative evaluation of glueball correlations in that background.

## Key numbers

* The two-glueball correlation function scales as $C(k_1, k_2) \sim \cosh(4 \, \Delta y)$ with the rapidity interval $\Delta y$ between the two particles, indicating exponential growth at large rapidity separations.
* The momentum transfer between the two particles is $q^2 = 4 m_\perp^2 \sinh^2(\Delta y/2)$, which for $k_\perp^2 \gg m^2$ and $\Delta y \gg 1$ gives $q^2 \approx k_\perp^2 e^{\Delta y}$.
* The shock wave strengths are parameterized as $\mu_1 \sim p_1^+ \Lambda_1^2 A_1^{1/3}$ and $\mu_2 \sim p_2^- \Lambda_2^2 A_2^{1/3}$, where $p_1^+, p_2^-$ are light-cone momenta per nucleon, $A_1, A_2$ are atomic numbers, and $\Lambda_1, \Lambda_2$ are typical transverse momentum scales.

## Context

This work extends the program of using AdS/CFT to study heavy ion collisions at strong coupling, following earlier studies of shock wave collisions by Grumiller and Romatschke (2008), Albacete et al. (2008, 2009), and others. The causality argument that long-range rapidity correlations must arise from early-time dynamics was previously made by Gavin, McLerran, and collaborators (2008) and by Dumitru et al. (2008) in the perturbative Color Glass Condensate framework. The authors note that their result of correlations growing with rapidity interval contradicts experimental data from RHIC (Adams et al., 2005; Adare et al., 2008) and LHC (Khachatryan et al., 2010), and suggest that inclusion of higher-order corrections along the lines of Albacete et al. (2009) should flatten this growth. The late-time analysis using the Janik-Peschanski metric (2005) confirms that only short-range rapidity correlations survive after thermalization, supporting the analogy with the cosmic microwave background horizon problem.
