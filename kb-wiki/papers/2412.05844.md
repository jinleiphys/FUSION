# Large-scale shell-model study of 2$ν$ECEC process in $^{78}$Kr

> In this work, we present the systematic study of $2ν$ECEC process in the $^{78}$Kr using large-scale shell-model calculations with the GWBXG effective interaction. We first validate the efficiency of the utilized interaction by comparing the theoretical low-lying energy spectra, the kinematic moment of inertia, and reduced transition probabilities with the experimental data for both the parent and grand-daughter nuclei $^{78}$Kr and $^{78}$Se, respectively. Additionally, we examine the shell-model level densities of the $1^+$ states in the intermediate nucleus $^{78}$Br, comparing them with the predictions from the Back-shifted Fermi gas model. We analyze the variation of cumulative nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for the $2ν$ECEC process in $^{78}$Kr as a function of $1^+$ state energies in the intermediate nucleus $^{78}$Br up to the saturation level. Our estimated half-life for $^{78}$Kr, extracted from the shell-model predicted NMEs, shows good agreement with the experimental value. The Gamow-Teller transitions from the lowest $1^+$ state of $^{78}$Br via both the EC$+β^+$ and $β^-$-channels are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that large-scale shell-model calculations using the GWBXG effective interaction can reproduce the experimental half-life of the 2νECEC process in ⁷⁸Kr, but only when a range of effective axial-vector coupling constants (g_A^eff from 1.00 to 1.27) is considered, and the calculated nuclear matrix element (NME) of 0.1997 is about 1.6 times smaller than the NME inferred from recent experimental half-life measurements.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the GWBXG effective interaction with a truncated model space: protons in the 0f_5/2, 1p, and 0g_9/2 orbitals and neutrons restricted to the 1p_1/2 and 0g_9/2 orbitals only. They diagonalized the Hamiltonian using the NuShellX code, considering 5000 1⁺ states in the intermediate nucleus ⁷⁸Br to compute the cumulative nuclear matrix element for the 2νECEC process. The theoretical spectroscopic properties (energy spectra, kinematic moment of inertia, B(E2) strengths) of the parent ⁷⁸Kr and grand-daughter ⁷⁸Se were first validated against experimental data before calculating the NME and half-life.

## Key numbers

- The shell-model calculated 2νECEC nuclear matrix element |M_2ν| for ⁷⁸Kr is 0.1997.
- The calculated half-life for ⁷⁸Kr ranges from 1.85×10²² yr (using g_A^eff = 1.27) to 4.82×10²² yr (using g_A^eff = 1.00).
- The experimental half-life values are [9.2^{+5.5}_{-2.6}(stat)±1.3(syst)]×10²¹ yr (Gavrilyuk) and [1.9^{+1.3}_{-0.7}(stat)±0.3(syst)]×10²² yr (Ratkevich).
- The lowest 1⁺ state in ⁷⁸Br contributes 9.95% to the final NME; the cumulative NME saturates near 8 MeV excitation energy.
- The shell-model level density of 1⁺ states in ⁷⁸Br matches the Back-shifted Fermi gas model predictions up to approximately 9 MeV.
- For the lowest 1⁺ state of ⁷⁸Br, the calculated total half-life is 56.34 min (g_A^eff = 1.00) and 34.93 min (g_A^free = 1.27), compared to the experimental value of 6.45(4) min.
- The branching ratios for the ⁷⁸Br(1⁺_1) transitions are: 96.56% to ⁷⁸Se(0⁺_g.s.), 3.02% to ⁷⁸Se(2⁺_1), and 0.42% to ⁷⁸Kr(2⁺_1) (with g_A^eff = 1.00).

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of double-beta decay processes, which have focused primarily on β⁻β⁻ decay (Patel, Kostensalo, Horoi, Caurier, Brown, Coraggio, Shimizu), to the less-explored 2νECEC process in ⁷⁸Kr. The study uses the GWBXG effective interaction, previously employed for nuclear structure studies (Dey, Kumbartzki, Stuchbery) and for 2νβ⁻β⁻ decay (Patel), and compares its level density predictions with the Back-shifted Fermi gas model (Bethe, Gilbert). The results enable future work with larger model spaces and advanced computational resources to achieve more accurate NMEs for ⁷⁸Kr and other 2νECEC candidates such as ¹²⁴Xe and ¹³⁰Ba.
