# Pions in magnetic field at finite temperature

> Pions in external magnetic field are investigated in the frame of a Pauli-Villars regularized Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The meson propagators in terms of quark bubbles in Ritus and Schwinger schemes are analytically derived, and pion masses are numerically calculated in the Ritus scheme. For neutral and charged pions at finite temperature, there exist respectively one and three mass jumps at the corresponding Mott transition points, due to the discrete energy levels of the two constituent quarks in magnetic field.

# Pions in magnetic field at finite temperature

## Key claim
Neutral pions exhibit one mass jump and charged pions exhibit three mass jumps at their respective Mott transition temperatures in a magnetic field, but these jumps are a direct consequence of the discrete Landau energy levels of constituent quarks and survive even when inverse magnetic catalysis is introduced via a magnetic-field-dependent coupling.

## Method
The author uses a Pauli-Villars regularized Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with SU(2) flavor, treating quarks at mean-field level and mesons as quantum fluctuations via the random phase approximation (RPA). Meson propagators are analytically derived in both the Ritus scheme (using conserved Ritus momentum eigenfunctions of the magnetized Klein-Gordon equation) and the Schwinger scheme (using Schwinger phases), with numerical calculations of pion masses performed in the Ritus scheme at finite temperature and magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- At zero temperature, neutral pion mass squared follows \( m_{\pi_0}^2(B) \simeq m_\pi^2 - 0.02|eB| \), while charged pion mass squared follows \( m_{\pi_\pm}^2(B) \simeq m_\pi^2 + 2|eB| \).
- Model parameters: current quark mass \( m_0 = 5 \) MeV, coupling constant \( G = 3.44 \) GeV\(^{-2}\), Pauli-Villars cutoff \( \Lambda = 1127 \) MeV, fitted to vacuum chiral condensate \( \langle\bar\psi\psi\rangle = -(250\ \text{MeV})^3 \), pion mass \( m_\pi = 134 \) MeV, and pion decay constant \( f_\pi = 93 \) MeV.
- At \( eB/m_\pi^2 = 0, 10, 20 \), the chiral pseudo-critical temperatures are \( T_c = 156, 164, 180 \) MeV, and the neutral pion Mott transition temperatures are \( T_m = 167, 160, 147 \) MeV.
- For charged pions at \( eB/m_\pi^2 = 10 \), the three jump temperatures are \( T^+ = 157, 171, 197 \) MeV; at \( eB/m_\pi^2 = 20 \), they are \( 160, 172, 187 \) MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous NJL model studies of neutral mesons in magnetic fields (Mao 2016; other NJL references) and addresses the long-standing difficulty of treating charged mesons where translational invariance is broken. It contrasts with hadron-level approaches such as chiral perturbation theory and the linear sigma model (various authors), which predict continuous temperature dependence of pion masses, and with bosonized NJL treatments (Wang 2018) that succeeded at low temperature but failed at high temperature. The results enable future extension to other charged mesons like kaons and rho mesons, and provide a framework for incorporating inverse magnetic catalysis effects from lattice QCD (Lattice collaborations 2010–2014).
