# Fission properties for r-process nuclei

> We present a systematics of fission barriers and fission lifetimes for the whole landscape of super-heavy elements (SHE), i.e. nuclei with Z>100. The fission lifetimes are also compared with the alpha-decay half-lives. The survey is based on a self-consistent description in terms of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach. Results for various different SHF parameterizations are compared to explore the robustness of the predictions. The fission path is computed by quadrupole constrained SHF. The computation of fission lifetimes takes care of the crucial ingredients of the large-amplitude collective dynamics along the fission path, as self-consistent collective mass and proper quantum corrections. We discuss the different topologies of fission landscapes which occur in the realm of SHE (symmetric versus asymmetric fission, regions of triaxial fission, bi-modal fission, and the impact of asymmetric ground states). The explored region is extended deep into the regime of very neutron-rich isotopes as they are expected to be produced in the astrophysical r process.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic survey of fission barriers and lifetimes for super-heavy elements (Z >= 100) based on Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations, but the authors qualify that the results provide an upper limit for barriers and lifetimes because triaxial shapes, which can lower barriers by 0.5-2 MeV, are not included in the axially symmetric calculations.

## Method

The authors computed fission paths using quadrupole-constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock with BCS pairing, then calculated collective masses by self-consistent cranking (ATDHF cranking) and applied zero-point energy corrections. Fission lifetimes were obtained via the WKB approximation using the quantum-corrected potential energy surface and collective mass, with the collective ground state energy computed fully quantum mechanically from a three-dimensional collective Hamiltonian restricted to axially symmetric shapes. Alpha-decay half-lives were evaluated using the Viola-Seaborg relationship with Q_alpha values from binding energy differences.

## Key numbers

- Fission barriers range from 0 to 12 MeV across the super-heavy element landscape (Z=82-120, N=120-260), depending on the Skyrme parameterization
- Fission lifetimes span from almost immediate decay to 10^12 s and longer, translating from the barrier height range
- For the parameterizations SV-min and SV-bas (effective masses m*/m = 0.95 and 0.9), fission lifetimes for No, Rf, Sg, and Hs isotopes show acceptable agreement with experimental data within an order of magnitude
- The stabilized pairing functional uses a cutoff energy E_cutp = 0.6 MeV
- The Viola-Seaborg parameters used for alpha-decay half-lives are: a = 1.66175, b = -0.5166, c = -0.20228, d = -33.9069, with h_log = 0 for even-even nuclei
- The experimental binding energy of He-4 used in Q_alpha calculations is 28.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent mean-field studies of fission in super-heavy elements, particularly building on the method presented in Schunck et al. (2009) for fully self-consistent fission lifetime calculations. The results are compared with experimental data from Oganessian et al. (2006) and others, and with predictions from other theoretical models including the finite-range liquid-drop model (FRDM) of Moller et al. (2009), the Thomas-Fermi model of Myers and Swiatecki (1999), and the HFB-14 parameterization. The survey extends into very neutron-rich isotopes relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis, enabling future studies of how fission affects the production of nuclei beyond the N=184 shell closure in astrophysical environments.
