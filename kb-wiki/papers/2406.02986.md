# Effects of pairing strength on the nuclear structure and double-$β$ decay predictions within the mapped interacting boson model

> The low-energy nuclear structure and two-neutrino double-$β$ ($2νββ$) decay are studied within the interacting boson model (IBM) that is based on the nuclear energy density functional (EDF). The IBM Hamiltonian describing the initial and final even-even nuclei, and the interacting boson fermion-fermion Hamiltonian producing the intermediate states of the neighboring odd-odd nuclei are determined by the microscopic inputs provided by the self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculations employing a relativistic EDF and a separable pairing force. Sensitivities of the low-lying structure and $2νββ$-decay properties to the pairing strength are specifically analyzed. It is shown that the SCMF calculations with decreased and increased pairing strengths lead to quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strengths in the IBM that are, respectively, significantly enhanced and reduced in magnitude. When the increased pairing is adopted, in particular, the energy levels of the excited $0^+$ states are lowered, and the predicted $2νββ$-decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) increase in magnitude systematically. The mapped IBM employing the increased pairing force generates effective NMEs and half-lives that are in a reasonable agreement with the experimental data for the $^{76}$Ge$\to^{76}$Se, $^{82}$Se$\to^{82}$Kr, and $^{100}$Mo$\to^{100}$Ru decays in particular, whereas the calculation with the standard pairing strength is adequate to provide an overall good description of the effective NMEs in agreement with data.

# Effects of pairing strength on the nuclear structure and double-β decay predictions within the mapped interacting boson model

## Key claim
The sensitivity of two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements and low-lying nuclear structure to the pairing strength in self-consistent mean-field calculations is systematically analyzed, showing that increasing the pairing strength by 15% leads to systematically larger NMEs and lower excited 0+ state energies, while the standard pairing strength provides an overall good description of effective NMEs in agreement with data for most decays.

## Method
The interacting boson model (IBM-2) Hamiltonians for initial and final even-even nuclei, and the interacting boson fermion-fermion (IBFFM-2) Hamiltonians for intermediate odd-odd nuclei, were determined by mapping triaxial quadrupole potential energy surfaces from constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) self-consistent mean-field calculations using the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force. Three pairing strengths were employed: the default value V=728 MeV fm³, a reduced value 0.9V=655 MeV fm³, and an increased value 1.15V=837 MeV fm³, with the Gaussian range parameter a=0.644 fm kept constant.

## Key numbers
- Pairing strengths used: V=728 MeV fm³ (default), 0.9V=655 MeV fm³ (reduced), and 1.15V=837 MeV fm³ (increased)
- The increased pairing strength (1.15V) produced effective NMEs and half-lives in reasonable agreement with experimental data for the ⁷⁶Ge→⁷⁶Se, ⁸²Se→⁸²Kr, and ¹⁰⁰Mo→¹⁰⁰Ru decays
- The deformation energy E_def (difference between mean-field energies at origin and global minimum) is reduced by a few MeV when the increased 1.15V pairing strength is used
- The sum over intermediate states in NME calculations includes all 1⁺ and 0⁺ states from the IBFFM-2 Hamiltonian below excitation energy of 30 MeV
- The axial vector coupling constant g_A=1.269 and vector coupling constant g_V=1 are used in the NME formula

## Context
This work extends the mapped IBM-2 study of Nomura (2022) on two-neutrino double-beta decay NMEs for candidate nuclei from ⁴⁸Ca to ¹⁵⁰Nd, where mass-dependent quenching factors were needed and varied among decay processes. The present study specifically investigates how varying the pairing strength in the underlying RHB-SCMF calculations affects the IBM-2 predictions, motivated by earlier work showing that increased pairing (15% above default) improved the description of ¹⁶⁸Er energy spectra in the mapped IBM (Nomura, 2021) and that global fits of separable pairing strength suggest scaling factors typically within 0.9 to 1.2 for medium-mass and heavy nuclei (Teeti, 2021). The results enable systematic refinement of double-beta decay NME predictions by tuning the pairing strength in the mean-field input, and suggest that the increased pairing scenario may be preferable for specific decay chains while the standard pairing works overall.
