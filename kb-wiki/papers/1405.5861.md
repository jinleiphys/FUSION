# Phenomenology of charmed mesons in the extended Linear Sigma Model

> We study the so-called extended linear sigma model for the case of four quark flavors. This model is based on global chiral symmetry and dilatation invariance and includes, besides scalar and pseudoscalar mesons, vector and axial-vector mesons. Most of the parameters of the model have been determined in previous work by fitting properties of mesons with three quark flavors. Only three new parameters, all related to the current charm quark mass, appear when introducing charmed mesons. Surprisingly, within the accuracy expected from our approach, the masses of open charmed mesons turn out to be in quantitative agreement with experimental data. On the other hand, with the exception of $J/ψ$, the masses of charmonia are underpredicted by about 10\%. It is remarkable that our approach correctly predicts (within errors) the mass splitting between spin-0 and spin-1 negative-parity open charm states. This indicates that, although the charm quark mass breaks chiral symmetry quite strongly explicitly, this symmetry still seems to have some influence on the properties of charmed mesons.

## Key claim
The extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM), extended from three to four quark flavors with only three new parameters related to the charm quark mass, predicts open charmed meson masses in quantitative agreement with experimental data (deviations of about 5%), but underpredicts charmonium masses (except J/psi) by about 10%. The model correctly predicts the mass splitting between spin-0 and spin-1 negative-parity open charm states, suggesting that chiral symmetry still influences charmed meson properties despite the large explicit breaking from the charm quark mass.

## Method
The authors extended the eLSM from three to four quark flavors by promoting meson fields from 3x3 to 4x4 matrices, adding charmed degrees of freedom to the scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector sectors. They retained all parameters previously fitted to three-flavor meson properties (from Dick et al., 2013) and introduced three new parameters (phi_C, epsilon_C, delta_C) related to the charm quark mass and condensate. These three parameters were fitted to twelve experimental charmed meson masses from the Particle Data Group using a chi-squared minimization with a 7% artificial error on experimental masses.

## Context
This work extends the eLSM from the three-flavor case studied by Dick et al. (2013), which successfully described nonstrange and strange meson masses and decay widths, to include charmed mesons. The approach contrasts with heavy-quark effective theories (such as those by Bardeen et al. and Nowak et al.) and refined potential models that typically dominate charm physics, showing that a chiral symmetry-based model can still provide reasonable predictions for open charm states. The results enable further study of OZI-dominant strong decays of charmed mesons into light mesons, acting as a bridge between high-energy and low-energy strong interaction sectors, and inform the interpretation of enigmatic states like D_S0*(2317) and D_S1(2460).
