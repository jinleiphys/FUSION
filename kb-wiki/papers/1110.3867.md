# Determination of the structure of $^{31}$Ne by full-microscopic framework

> We perform the first quantitative analysis of the reaction cross sections of $^{28-32}$Ne by $^{12}$C at 240 MeV/nucleon, using the double-folding model (DFM) with the Melbourne $g$-matrix and the deformed projectile density calculated by the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). To describe the tail of the last neutron of $^{31}$Ne, we adopt the resonating group method (RGM) combined with AMD. The theoretical prediction excellently reproduce the measured cross sections of $^{28-32}$Ne with no adjustable parameters. The ground state properties of $^{31}$Ne, i.e., strong deformation and a halo structure with spin-parity $3/2_{}^-$, are clarified.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ground state of $^{31}$Ne is a strongly deformed p-wave halo nucleus with spin-parity $3/2^-$, but this conclusion depends on the use of the resonating group method (RGM) to properly describe the tail of the last neutron wave function; without RGM, the AMD-only calculation fails to reproduce the measured reaction cross section.

## Method

The authors calculated total reaction cross sections for $^{28-32}$Ne on a $^{12}$C target at 240 MeV/nucleon using the double-folding model (DFM) with the Melbourne $g$-matrix as the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. Projectile densities were obtained from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) wave functions, and for $^{31}$Ne the resonating group method (RGM) was combined with AMD to generate a proper asymptotic behavior of the last neutron wave function. The target density was taken from electron scattering data on $^{12}$C, and the calculation contained no adjustable parameters except for a 1.8% reduction factor calibrated on $^{12}$C-$^{12}$C scattering.

## Key numbers

- Reaction cross sections for $^{28-32}$Ne + $^{12}$C at 240 MeV/nucleon were reproduced with no adjustable parameters; the AMD-RGM result for $^{31}$Ne agrees with experimental data, while the AMD-only result (triangle in Fig. 4) cannot explain the very large experimental value.
- The one-neutron separation energy $S_n$ of $^{31}$Ne is approximately 250 keV from AMD and 450 keV from AMD-RGM, consistent with the measured value $S_n = 0.29 \pm 1.64$ MeV.
- The root mean square radius of the $^{31}$Ne density is 3.617 fm (AMD-RGM) versus 3.490 fm (AMD).
- The deformation parameter $\beta$ for $^{31}$Ne is 0.41, with $\gamma = 0^\circ$, as calculated by AMD.
- The dominant configuration of $^{31}$Ne from AMD-RGM is $^{30}$Ne($0^+$) $\otimes$ $1p_{3/2}$ at 56% amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Takechi et al. (2011) and Nakamura et al. (2009) by providing the first fully microscopic calculation of reaction cross sections for neutron-rich Ne isotopes, directly incorporating AMD wave functions into the DFM. It improves upon the earlier analysis by Minomo et al. (2010) that used deformed Woods-Saxon densities, which could not explain the large difference in cross sections between $^{30}$Ne, $^{31}$Ne, and $^{32}$Ne. The results enable future application of the same framework to Mg isotopes for further exploration of the "island of inversion" and open the question of pairing anti-halo effects as discussed by Hagino (2011).
