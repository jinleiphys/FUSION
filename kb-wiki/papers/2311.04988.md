# Theoretical Developments for Jets in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is an exotic phase of matter, composed of deconfined quarks and gluons and is briefly created in heavy-ion collisions (HIC) at the LHC and at the RHIC. High-energy, self-collimated structures of final-state particles also created in HIC, called jets, probe the QGP, piercing through it on their way to the particle detector. Quantum field theory at finite temperature or thermal field theory, is then an extremely powerful tool, capable of analytically quantifying how such a high-energy object interacts with a weakly coupled thermal bath. In this thesis, we work towards the computation of corrections to two quantities, which dictate how jets are quenched by the QGP. The first being the transverse momentum broadening coefficient, which describes how the jet diffuses in transverse momentum space through its interaction with the medium. We focus on the computation of logarithmically enhanced corrections, carefully showing how the thermal scale affects the logarithmic phase space. The second is the asymptotic mass, which can be thought of as a shift in the jet dispersion relation as it undergoes forward scattering with the medium. We complete a matching calculation, which rids the mass' classical corrections of any unphysical divergences, while also beginning the completion of its full two-loop, quantum corrections.

## Key claim

This thesis works towards computing higher-order corrections to two quantities that govern how jets are quenched by the quark-gluon plasma: the transverse momentum broadening coefficient and the asymptotic mass. The author focuses on the matching calculation that removes unphysical divergences from the classical corrections to the asymptotic mass and begins the completion of its full two-loop quantum corrections, with the caveat that the calculation is performed in Feynman gauge and that many diagrams vanish under this choice.

## Method

The author computes the asymptotic mass \(m_{\infty}\) for a jet particle in a thermal medium using perturbative finite-temperature QCD and its effective theory, EQCD (electrostatic QCD). The calculation involves evaluating two-loop Feynman diagrams for the operator \(Z_g\) in the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, showing that diagrams \((f)\) and \((g)\) cancel in Feynman gauge, leaving only diagram \((c)\) to contribute at next-to-leading order. The author then performs a matching calculation between full QCD and EQCD by extracting the zero-mode contributions from diagram \((c)\) and showing they cancel against UV divergences from the EQCD side.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order contribution to \(Z_g\) from diagram \((a)\) is \(Z_g^{(a)} = T^2/6\) (Eq. (a)).
* The gluonic zero-mode contribution from the \(k^{-2}\) piece of diagram \((c)\) yields an IR pole: \(Z^{(c)}_{g\,k^{-2}\,G\,n=0} = \frac{g^2 C_A T^2}{32\pi^2}\left[-\frac{3}{\epsilon} - \left(7 + 6\ln\frac{\bar{\mu}}{4T}\right)\right]\) (Eq. (IRpoleqmsquare)).
* The gluonic zero-mode contribution from the \(\Pi_L - \Pi_T\) piece of diagram \((c)\) yields: \(Z^{(c)}_{g\,L-T\,G\,n=0} = \frac{g^2 C_A T^2}{32\pi^2}\left(-\frac{1}{\epsilon} + 4 - \ln\frac{\bar{\mu}^2 e^{\gamma_E}}{4T^2} - \psi\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)\right)\) (Eq. (LmTzeromodediv)).

## Context

This work extends the program of computing jet quenching parameters in thermal field theory, building on the EQCD framework developed by Moore (2020) and Ghiglieri (2021) for non-perturbative lattice determinations of the asymptotic mass. The matching calculation presented here removes UV divergences from the classical corrections to \(m_{\infty}\), enabling future lattice computations of the full quantum-corrected asymptotic mass. The author also references prior work by Kalashnikov (1979) and Weldon (1982) for the leading-order result, and by Ghiglieri (2023) for the matching calculation presented in this thesis.
