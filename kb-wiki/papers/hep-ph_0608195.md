# Ordinary muon capture on a proton in manifestly Lorentz invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> The amplitude for ordinary muon capture on the proton is evaluated, through the first four orders in the expansion parameter, in a manifestly Lorentz invariant form of baryon chiral perturbation theory. Expressions for the low energy constants in terms of physical quantities are obtained in each of the several renormalization schemes which have been proposed for forcing the relativistic approach to obey the same counting rules as obtained in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The advantages and disadvantages of these schemes are discussed, using the muon capture results as an example, with the aim of gaining insight as to which scheme is preferable for practical calculations.

# Ordinary muon capture on a proton in manifestly Lorentz invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The amplitude for ordinary muon capture on a proton is evaluated through fourth order in the expansion parameter using a manifestly Lorentz invariant form of baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the calculation serves primarily as a way to determine low energy constants (LECs) rather than providing a new numerical prediction for the capture rate, because the only unused information after LEC determination reproduces the known expression for the induced pseudoscalar form factor G_P in terms of the pion-nucleon coupling and axial radius.

## Method

The authors evaluate the weak nucleon-nucleon vertex for ordinary muon capture by summing tree-level diagrams from chiral Lagrangians through order p^4, one-loop diagrams with vertices from the lowest-order Lagrangian, and one-loop diagrams containing one vertex from the second-order Lagrangian, plus explicit mass insertion diagrams on internal nucleon lines. They work consistently to one loop and to O(p^4), expressing all amplitudes in terms of loop integrals which are regularized dimensionally, then apply two renormalization schemes, the Infrared Renormalization (IR) scheme of Becher and Leutwyler and the Extended On Mass Shell (EOMS) scheme of the Mainz group, to remove terms that do not obey the counting rules of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The four-momentum transfer squared for ordinary muon capture on the proton is q^2 = -0.88 m_mu^2, where m_mu is the physical muon mass.
- The nucleon mass used is the average of physical neutron and proton masses, m_N = (m_n + m_p)/2.
- The pion decay constant F_0 and the axial coupling g_A appear as bare parameters in the lowest-order Lagrangian, with g_A being the unrenormalized value of G_A(0).
- The loop integrals carry the factor 1/(4 pi F)^2, and terms with higher powers of this factor (e.g., 1/(4 pi F)^4) are dropped as consistent with the neglect of multi-loop diagrams.

## Context

This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) calculations of muon capture by Fearing (1997), Ando, Min, and Rho (2001), and Bernard, Hemmert, and Meissner (2001) to a manifestly Lorentz invariant framework, addressing the problem identified by Gasser et al. that relativistic loop diagrams do not obey the same counting rules as HBChPT. The calculation is intended as a basis for future consistent applications to processes such as pi p -> n gamma, where HBChPT produces unnaturally large LECs, and to radiative muon capture, where unresolved problems remain in extracting G_P.
