# Is chiral symmetry manifested in nuclear structure?

> Spontaneously broken chiral symmetry is an established property of low-energy quantum chromodynamics, but finding direct evidence for it from nuclear structure data is a difficult challenge. Indeed, phenomenologically successful energy-density functional approaches do not even have explicit pions. Are there smoking guns for chiral symmetry in nuclei?

## Key claim

The paper argues that while chiral symmetry is a fundamental property of low-energy QCD, finding direct, necessary evidence for it in nuclear structure data (as opposed to merely sufficient descriptions) remains an open challenge; the authors specifically seek "smoking guns" where data forces the inclusion of explicit pionic degrees of freedom, noting that phenomenologically successful energy-density functional approaches do not even include explicit pions.

## Method

The authors review the conceptual challenge by contrasting two extremes: pionless effective field theory (EFT) describing few-body scattering at very low energies and binding up to the alpha particle, and energy-density functional approaches (such as Skyrme) that achieve wide phenomenological success without explicit pions. They then examine the prototype case of NN partial-wave analyses by the Nijmegen group, where pion masses and couplings were extracted as fit parameters from scattering data, and discuss opportunities for analogous searches in nuclear structure through no-core shell model, coupled cluster, lattice EFT, and shell-model calculations that include pion physics.

## Key numbers

- The extracted charged and neutral pion masses from NN partial-wave analyses agreed with experiment within estimated one percent errors (the paper does not give the absolute mass values).
- The $pp\pi^0$ coupling was determined from each individual partial wave except for $^1$S$_0$ in the Nijmegen analysis (Stoks 1992).
- Fitting the pion mass as a parameter in the two-pion exchange (TPE) potential gave agreement with experiment at the ten percent level (Rentmeester 1999, 2003).
- The nuclear Fermi momentum is comparable to $2m_\pi$ (the paper does not give a numerical value for the Fermi momentum).
- The pion mass squared is much less than the rho meson mass squared ($m_\pi^2 \ll m_\rho^2$), manifesting the approximate Goldstone boson nature of the pion.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of chiral symmetry from meson spectra and $\pi$–$\pi$/$\pi$–$N$ scattering (Gasser 1983, Bernard 2006) to the more subtle question of nuclear structure, building on the prototype NN partial-wave analyses of Stoks (1992) and Rentmeester (1999, 2003) where pion parameters were extracted directly from data. The paper challenges the nuclear structure community to find direct evidence for chiral symmetry in nuclei, which could unify descriptions of strong-interaction phenomena, guide microscopic calculations, and connect to lattice QCD calculations of light nuclei; it also raises the alternative question of where pionless EFTs (Bedaque 2002, Platter 2009) are applicable.
