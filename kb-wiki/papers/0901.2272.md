# Viscosity and dissipation - early stages

> A very early start up time of the hydrodynamic evolution is needed in order to reproduce observations from relativistic heavy-ion collisions experiments. At such early times the systems is still not locally equilibrated. Another source of deviations from local equilibrium is the viscosity of the fluid. We study these effects at very early times to obtain a dynamical prescription for the transition from an early 2-dimensional expansion to a nearly equilibrated 3-dimensional expansion at latter stages. The role of viscosity at latter stages of the evolution is also illustrated.

# Viscosity and dissipation - early stages

## Key claim

A very early hydrodynamic start time (τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c) is needed to reproduce experimental observables, but at such early times the system is not locally equilibrated and the initial pressure anisotropy is large; the paper proposes an effective dynamical description for the transition from an anisotropic 2-dimensional expansion to a nearly equilibrated 3-dimensional expansion, noting that after retuning initial conditions to account for entropy production from early dissipation, most of the effect on final observables is canceled except for harder transverse momentum spectra.

## Method

The author uses a numerical solution of relativistic hydrodynamic equations with a stress tensor that includes a dissipative correction π quantifying pressure anisotropy between transverse and longitudinal directions. For the early stage where the stress correction is too large for the Israel-Stewart formalism to be reliable, an effective exponential relaxation equation π(τ) = π(τ₀) exp[-(τ-τ₀)/τ_π] is employed. At later stages, full second-order viscous hydrodynamics with shear viscosity η/s = 1/4π is solved in a boost-invariant geometry with azimuthally asymmetric transverse expansion, using initial time τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c and initial stress corrections π^{zz}(τ₀)/2 = π^{xx}(τ₀) = π^{yy}(τ₀) = p/2.

## Key numbers

- Initial hydrodynamic start time τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c is required to reproduce experimental soft observables
- Up to 30% increase of entropy is possible in the early phase, depending on the ratio τ_π/τ₀
- Shear viscosity is set to η/s = 1/4π in the viscous hydrodynamic calculations
- Initial stress corrections are set as π^{zz}(τ₀)/2 = π^{xx}(τ₀) = π^{yy}(τ₀) = p/2

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic calculations by Chojnacki (2007) and Broniowski (2008) that required very early start times to reproduce experimental data, and builds on the author's own earlier work (Bozek 2007) on early pressure anisotropy. The paper addresses the tension between the need for early hydrodynamic start times and the fact that the system is not yet equilibrated, proposing a practical dynamical prescription. It enables more realistic modeling of the full evolution from the pre-equilibrium stage through the viscous hydrodynamic phase, showing that the combination of initial pressure anisotropy and later shear viscosity produces harder transverse momentum spectra and reduced elliptic flow compared to perfect fluid calculations.
