# Nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung of dark gauge bosons and revised supernova constraints

> We calculate the rate of production of hypothetical light vector bosons (LVBs) from nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung reactions in the soft radiation limit directly in terms of the measured nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections. We use these results and the observation of neutrinos from supernova SN1987a to deduce constraints on the couplings of vector bosons with masses $\lesssim 200$ MeV to either electric charge (dark photons) or to baryon number. We establish for the first time strong constraints on LVB that couple only to baryon number, and revise earlier constraints on the dark photon. For the latter, we find that the excluded region of parameter space is diminished by about a factor of 10.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the production rate of hypothetical light vector bosons (LVBs) from nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung using the soft radiation approximation directly tied to measured elastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections, rather than relying on model-dependent one-pion-exchange potentials. They find that earlier constraints on the dark photon are relaxed by about a factor of 10, and they establish for the first time strong supernova cooling constraints on LVBs that couple only to baryon number.

## Method

The authors apply Low's soft-photon theorem to relate the bremsstrahlung emission amplitude for LVBs to the measured elastic nucleon-nucleon differential cross sections, retaining only the leading-order terms in an expansion in the ratio of radiated energy to center-of-mass energy. They compute the emissivity for dipole radiation (for dark photons from neutron-proton collisions) and quadrupole radiation (for baryon-coupled LVBs from all nucleon-nucleon channels) using phase shifts up to orbital angular momentum L=5 to reconstruct the elastic cross sections. The supernova cooling constraint is applied using Raffelt's criterion at fiducial density rho = 3 x 10^14 g/cm^3 and temperature T = 30 MeV, and trapping constraints are estimated from the mean free path for inverse bremsstrahlung absorption.

## Key numbers

- The excluded region of parameter space for the dark photon is diminished by about a factor of 10 compared to earlier work by Dent et al. (2012).
- The supernova cooling constraint is applied at fiducial conditions: density rho = 3 x 10^14 g/cm^3, temperature T = 30 MeV, and proton fraction Y_p = 0.3.
- Raffelt's criterion threshold for energy loss rate per gram is dot_E_Raffelt = 10^19 erg/(g s).
- The total luminosity limit corresponding to Raffelt's criterion is approximately L < 2 x 10^52 (M_core / M_sun) erg/s.
- The LVB masses considered range from m = 1 eV to 200 MeV.
- The gravitational binding energy of the neutron star is approximately 3-5 x 10^53 ergs.
- The typical nucleon velocity in the supernova core is v less than or approximately 1/3.
- The temperature at which neutrinos decouple is approximately T_nu/2 approximately 3 MeV.
- The core density is approximately 3 x 10^14 g/cm^3 and core temperature is approximately 30 MeV.
- The nucleon number density at saturation is n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the soft radiation approximation method previously applied to neutrino and axion production by Hanhart et al. (2000) and to Kaluza-Klein graviton production by Hanhart et al. (2001). It directly contradicts the earlier dark photon constraints derived by Dent et al. (2012), which used a one-pion-exchange potential in the Born approximation and overestimated the bremsstrahlung rate by about a factor of 10 for quadrupole radiation. The results enable refined experimental searches for leptophobic gauge bosons in the mass range from a few eV to MeV, where neutron scattering constraints from Barbieri (1975) and neutron optics constraints from Leeb (1992) currently provide the strongest limits.
