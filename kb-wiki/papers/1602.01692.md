# New Astrophysical Reaction Rate for the $^{12}\textrm{C}(α,γ)^{16}\textrm{O}$ Reaction

> A new astrophysical reaction rate for $^{12}$C($α,γ$)$^{16}$O has been evaluated on the basis of a global R-matrix fitting to the available experimental data. The reaction rates of $^{12}$C($α,γ$)$^{16}$O for stellar temperatures between 0.04 $\leq$ $T_9$ $\leq$ 10 are provided in a tabular form and by an analytical fitting expression. At $T_9$ = 0.2, the reaction rate is (7.83 $\pm$ 0.35)$\times10^{15}$ $\rm{cm^3 mol^{-1} s^{-1}}$, where stellar helium burning occurs.

# New Astrophysical Reaction Rate for the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}\) Reaction

## Key claim
The authors present a new reaction rate for \(^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}\) based on a global R-matrix fit to complementary experimental data on the \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) compound nucleus, claiming reduced uncertainties compared to prior compilations. However, the result remains a theoretical extrapolation from data above 0.9 MeV to the astrophysically relevant energy of 0.3 MeV, and the authors note that important factors not included in their model may exist.

## Method
A multilevel, multichannel R-matrix analysis was performed on differential cross section and angle-integrated cross section data for the \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) compound nucleus, including data from the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}\) reaction and complementary channels. The total S-factor \(S_{\mathrm{tot}}(E)\) was extrapolated to the Gamow window around 0.3 MeV, and the reaction rate \(N_{\mathrm{A}}\langle\sigma v\rangle\) was computed by integrating the S-factor over the Gamow energy distribution for stellar temperatures \(0.04 \leq T_9 \leq 10\). Error propagation followed the formalism of Smith (1991).

## Key numbers
- Extrapolated total S-factor at 0.3 MeV: \(S_{\mathrm{tot}}(0.3\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 162.7 \pm 7.3\ \mathrm{keV\ b}\)
- Ground-state capture components at 0.3 MeV: \(S_{E1}(0.3\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 98.0 \pm 7.0\ \mathrm{keV\ b}\) and \(S_{E2}(0.3\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 56.0 \pm 4.1\ \mathrm{keV\ b}\)
- Cascade capture components at 0.3 MeV: \(S_{\mathrm{casc}}(0.3\ \mathrm{MeV}) = 8.7 \pm 1.8\ \mathrm{keV\ b}\), with \(S_{6.05} = 4.91 \pm 1.11\ \mathrm{keV\ b}\) and \(S_{6.13} = 0.16 \pm 0.26\ \mathrm{keV\ b}\)
- Reaction rate at helium-burning temperature \(T_9 = 0.2\): \((7.83 \pm 0.35) \times 10^{15}\ \mathrm{cm^3\ mol^{-1}\ s^{-1}}\)
- Analytical fit valid for \(0.04 \leq T_9 \leq 10\) with maximum deviation of 4.0% from tabulated values; in the range \(T_9 = 0.1-0.3\) the maximal deviation is 1%

## Context
This work extends the R-matrix analysis previously reported by An et al. (2015) and provides an updated reaction rate that is compared to prior compilations including CF88 (Caughlan and Fowler 1988), Buchmann (1996), NACRE (Angulo et al. 1999), Kunz et al. (2002), and NACRE II (Xu et al. 2013). At \(T_9 = 0.2\), the new rate is about 10% larger than NACRE II and Buchmann (1996), about 16% lower than NACRE, and consistent with Kunz et al. (2002). The reduced uncertainty enables more precise modeling of stellar helium burning, the carbon-to-oxygen ratio in massive stars, and the production of radioactive nuclei such as \(^{26}\mathrm{Al}\), \(^{44}\mathrm{Ti}\), and \(^{60}\mathrm{Fe}\). The authors identify the need for further measurements of cascade transitions, asymptotic normalization coefficients, and direct \((\gamma,\alpha)\) cross sections using high-intensity gamma-ray beams.
