# Neutrino radiation from dense matter

> This article provides a concise review of the problem of neutrino radiation from dense matter. The subjects addressed include quantum kinetic equations for neutrino transport, collision integrals describing neutrino radiation through charged and neutral current interactions, radiation rates from pair-correlated baryonic and color superconducting quark matter.

## Key claim

This paper provides a concise review of neutrino radiation from dense matter, covering quantum kinetic equations, collision integrals, and radiation rates from pair-correlated baryonic and color superconducting quark matter, with the caveat that the treatment focuses on the neutrino cooling era of neutron stars (temperatures from ~0.1 MeV down to ~0.01 MeV) rather than the initial rapid cooling phase.

## Method

The review derives neutrino transport equations using real-time Green's functions (RTG) techniques, starting from the Dyson equation for neutrinos on a real-time contour and taking the quasiparticle limit to obtain a Boltzmann equation. The collision integrals are expressed in terms of neutrino self-energies expanded to second order in the weak coupling constant, with the central problem being the computation of polarization tensors of nucleonic or quark matter using Matsubara finite-temperature Green's functions. For superfluid matter, the formalism incorporates pairing correlations through normal and anomalous propagators, with vertex corrections included via summation of infinite series of particle-hole loops.

## Key numbers

- Neutron star core cooling from initial temperatures T ~ 50 MeV down to 0.1 MeV occurs over weeks to years, then evolves slowly over 10^3-10^4 yr down to T ~ 0.01 MeV
- The Urca process in nucleonic matter is kinematically allowed under β-equilibrium if the proton fraction Y_p ≥ 11-14%
- The direct Urca emissivity in unpaired matter is given by ε_0^Urca = (1+3g_A^2)(3G̃² m_n* m_p* p_Fe T^6)/(2π⁵) where g_A = 1.26 is the axial coupling constant
- The neutral current pair-breaking emissivity per neutrino flavor is ε_{νν̄} = (G² c_V²)/(240π³) ν(p_F) T⁷ I₁(ζ) where ζ = 2Δ(T)/T
- The direct Urca emissivity in interacting quark matter to first order in α_c is ε_0 = (914/315) G̃_F² α_c μ_d μ_u μ_e T⁶
- CPBF processes are efficient in the temperature domain T* ≤ T ≤ T_c where T* ~ 0.2 T_c, and are suppressed asymptotically at low temperatures as exp(-Δ(0)/T)
- The Cabibbo angle cos θ_C = 0.973

## Context

This review extends the work of Voskresensky and Senatorov (1987) on neutrino emissivities computed via the S-matrix and optical theorem, and the work of Lattimer et al. (1981) on the zero-temperature Urca process. It builds on the classification of weak reactions in neutron stars dating back to Gamow (1941) and the modified Urca process, and addresses the effects of Cooper pair breaking and formation (CPBF) as developed by Schaab et al. and Flowers et al. The review enables further work on neutron star cooling simulations incorporating superfluid and superconducting phases, including the 2SC phase of color superconducting quark matter and alternative realizations such as spin-1 and crystalline color superconductivity.
