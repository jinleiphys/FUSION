# Relativistic Effects and the Role of Heavy Meson Exchange in Deuteron Photodisintegration

> Relativistic effects and the role of heavy meson exchange in deuteron photodisintegration are studied systematically for photon energies below the pion production threshold. In a (p/M)-expansion, all leading order relativistic one-body and pi-exchange as well as all static heavy meson exchange currents consistent with the Bonn OBEPQ model are included. In addition, one- and two-body boost effects have been investigated. Sizeable effects from the various two-body contributions beyond pi-exchange have been found in almost every observable considered, i.e., differential cross section and single polarization observables.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects beyond the leading spin-orbit current and heavy meson exchange currents beyond pion exchange produce sizeable contributions to deuteron photodisintegration observables below pion production threshold, but the various heavy meson contributions (delta, omega, sigma, eta) exhibit strong destructive interference so that their net effect is often small, with the rho meson being the most important heavy meson contributor.

## Method

The authors used an equations-of-motion method within a (p/M)-expansion to derive consistent electromagnetic current operators for the Bonn OBEPQ potential models, including all leading-order relativistic one-body and pion-exchange currents, all static heavy meson exchange currents (rho, delta, omega, sigma, eta), and one- and two-body boost effects. They calculated the differential cross section and all single polarization observables (photon asymmetry, target asymmetries T11, T20, T21, T22, and final nucleon polarizations) at four photon energies (4.5, 40, 100, and 140 MeV) for the three Bonn OBEPQ versions (A, B, C).

## Key numbers

- At 4.5 MeV, the nonrelativistic pion MEC gives a 10 percent enhancement to the differential cross section relative to the nonrelativistic one-body current
- At 100 and 140 MeV, the relativistic one-body current produces a sizeable reduction of the differential cross section at forward and backward angles, mainly from the dominant spin-orbit current
- The Dirac rho current contribution is roughly the same size as the Pauli rho current contribution, despite coupling constants suggesting suppression by a factor of about 50
- Individual heavy meson contributions beyond rho are surprisingly big (up to about 5 percent) in the differential cross section
- Boost contributions (both one-body and two-body) turned out to be totally negligible at all energies studied

## Context

This work extends the consistent treatment of relativistic effects and meson exchange currents from a pure one-pion-exchange model (Goller and Arenhoevel, 1992) to the realistic Bonn OBEPQ potential models, and complements the authors' earlier study of deuteron electrodisintegration (Ritz et al., 1997). It contrasts with the nonrelativistic calculation by Levchuk (1995) for the Bonn OBEPR model, which included heavy meson exchange currents but neglected relativistic contributions. The results enable future work incorporating isobar contributions (particularly from the Delta resonance) and proper treatment of pion retardation above pion production threshold, which the authors identify as more crucial than heavy meson exchange effects.
