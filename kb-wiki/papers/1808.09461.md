# Mass-dependence of pseudoscalar meson elastic form factors

> A continuum approach to quark-antiquark bound-states is used to determine the electromagnetic form factors of pion-like mesons with masses $m_{0^-}/{\rm GeV}=0.14$, $0.47$, $0.69$, $0.83$ on a spacelike domain that extends to $Q^2 \lesssim 10\,$GeV$^2$. The results enable direct comparisons with contemporary lattice-QCD calculations of heavy-pion form factors at large values of momentum transfer and aid in understanding them. They also reveal, inter alia, that the form factor of the physical pion provides the best opportunity for verification of the factorised hard-scattering formula relevant to this class of exclusive processes and that this capacity diminishes steadily as the meson mass increases.

## Key claim

The continuum Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) calculation shows that the physical pion's elastic form factor provides the best opportunity to verify the factorised hard-scattering formula for exclusive processes, but this capacity diminishes steadily as the meson mass increases; the lower bound \(Q_0\) of the domain where the formula is valid increases quickly with growing meson mass.

## Method

The authors used rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, a symmetry-preserving continuum approach to quark-antiquark bound states, to compute the electromagnetic form factors of pion-like mesons. They solved the dressed-quark gap equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation for four current-quark masses, then evaluated the form factor integral in Eq. (RLFpi) on a spacelike domain up to \(Q^2 \lesssim 10\) GeV\(^2\), using a fitted parametrisation to extend beyond the domain where direct numerical integration fails. The meson parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) were obtained from the leading nontrivial Mellin moment \(\langle \xi^2 \rangle\).

## Key numbers

* Physical pion (\(m_{0^-}=0.14\) GeV): computed charge radius \(r_\pi = 0.66\) fm (with \(\omega=0.5\) GeV), compared to empirical \(r_\pi = 0.672(8)\) fm; decay constant \(f_\pi = 0.094\) GeV (empirical \(f_\pi = 0.092\) GeV).
* Meson with \(m_{0^-}=0.47\) GeV: computed \(f_{0^-}=0.115\) GeV, \(r_{0^-}=0.55\) fm; lQCD results from Chambers (2017) give \(f_{0^-}=0.111(2)\) GeV, \(r_{0^-}=0.56(1)\) fm.
* Meson with \(m_{0^-}=0.69\) GeV: computed \(f_{0^-}=0.133\) GeV, \(r_{0^-}=0.49\) fm; lQCD results from Koponen (2017) give \(f_{0^-}=0.128\) GeV, \(r_{0^-}=0.498(4)\) fm.
* Meson with \(m_{0^-}=0.83\) GeV: computed \(f_{0^-}=0.145\) GeV, \(r_{0^-}=0.45\) fm.
* The product \(f_{0^-} r_{0^-}\) is nearly constant over the mass range; with \(\omega=0.5\) GeV, the integrated relative difference between the computed \(m_{0^-}\)-dependence and the mean value of \(f_{0^-} w_{0^-}\) is just 3%.
* The conformal-limit prediction \(Q^2 F_\pi(Q^2) \approx 0.15\) at \(Q^2=4\) GeV\(^2\) is a factor of 2.7 smaller than the empirical value \(0.41^{+0.04}_{-0.03}\) at \(Q^2=2.45\) GeV\(^2\) from Huber (2008).

## Context

This work extends the continuum DSE calculation of the pion form factor by Chang (2013) to heavier pseudoscalar mesons, enabling direct comparison with lattice-QCD results from Chambers (2017) at \(m_{0^-}=0.47\) GeV and Koponen (2017) at \(m_{0^-}=0.69\) GeV. The results reveal that the lQCD data from Chambers (2017) are consistent with the continuum predictions at large \(Q^2\), while those from Koponen (2017) appear inconsistent with the mass trend. The study enables future experimental tests at Jefferson Lab (JLab 12) aiming for precision measurements of \(F_\pi(Q^2)\) up to \(Q^2=6\) GeV\(^2\) and potentially \(Q^2\approx 8.5\) GeV\(^2\).
