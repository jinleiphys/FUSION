# Weak asymptotics of wave function for N-particle system and asymptotic filtering

> Asymptotic representations for large values of the hyperradius are constructed for the scattering wave function of a system of $ N $ particles considered as a generalized function of angular variable coordinates. The coefficients of the asymptotic representations are expressed in terms of the $N$-particle scattering matrix. The phenomenon of asymptotic filtration is discovered, which consists in the fact that only scattering processes contribute to the leading terms of such an asymptotic representation, in which all particles are free both before and after interaction. The obtained representations are used to construct the correct asymptotics of the partial components of the wave function of $N$ particles in the hyperspherical representation.

## Key claim

The paper constructs asymptotic representations for the N-particle scattering wave function considered as a generalized function (distribution) of angular variables, discovering that only scattering processes where all particles are free both before and after interaction contribute to the leading terms of this weak asymptotics, a phenomenon termed "asymptotic filtration."

## Method

The authors use the Fourier transform method, expressing the coordinate-space wave function as a Fourier integral of the momentum-space wave function, whose singularities are described using the Faddeev-Yakubovsky integral equations. The weak asymptotics is obtained by integrating the wave function against smooth test functions on the unit sphere in the d-dimensional configuration space (d = 3(N-1)), then evaluating the resulting integrals as the hyperradius tends to infinity using Lemma 2 (stationary phase) and contour integration. The structure of singularities in the N-particle T-matrix is analyzed recursively via connected parts of operators for partitions a_l, with explicit formulas given for the three-particle case.

## Key numbers

- The configuration space dimension is d = 3(N-1) for N particles.
- The total number of distinct A_2 chains (complete partition chains from 2 to N-1) is n_N = 2^(1-N) N! (N-1)!.
- The spherical waves in R^d have amplitude proportional to |X|^(-(d-1)/2), with phase factor exp{±i|P||X| ∓ iπ(d-3)/4}.
- The coefficient N_d(|P|) in the weak asymptotics is i (2π)^(-1/2) |P|^(-(d-1)/2).
- The interaction potentials decay as O(|x|^(-β)) with β > 1.

## Context

This work extends the asymptotic analysis of scattering wave functions from the well-studied three-particle case (Merkuriev, 1971; Yakovlev, 2016) and four-particle case (Yakovlev, 1990) to general N-particle systems, where the asymptotic behavior in the complete configuration space had remained insufficiently studied for N > 4. The weak asymptotics obtained enables the construction of correct asymptotic boundary conditions for the N-particle scattering problem in the hyperspherical representation, and the asymptotic filtration phenomenon shows that only the fully free-to-free scattering channel contributes to the leading-order terms, simplifying the treatment of more complex reaction processes.
