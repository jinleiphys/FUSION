# Comment on ``Determination of pion-baryon coupling constants from QCD sum rules''

> In this comment, we propose possible errors in constructing the continuum contribution in the sum rule studied by M. C. Birse and B. Krippa, Phys. Rev. C. {\bf 54} (1996) 3240.

# Comment on "Determination of pion-baryon coupling constants from QCD sum rules"

## Key claim
The continuum contribution in the sum rule studied by Birse and Krippa (1996) was constructed incorrectly; if the continuum is treated properly using the conventional single-dispersion-relation method, the factors \(E_2\), \(E_1\), and \(E_0\) in their equations should be replaced by \(E_1\), \(E_0\), and \(1\), respectively, which significantly modifies their extracted coupling constants.

## Method
The author re-examines the two-point correlation function for \(\pi NN\), \(\pi \Sigma \Sigma\), and \(\pi \Sigma \Lambda\) couplings used by Birse and Krippa (1996), focusing on the structure \(\not\!k \gamma_5\) at leading order in the pion momentum. He compares the conventional QCD sum rule approach, where the continuum spectral density is modeled as a step function \(\rho_c^{phen} \equiv \rho^{ope}(p^2) \theta(p^2 - S_{\pi})\), with the double-dispersion-relation method employed by Birse and Krippa. By explicitly performing the Borel-transformed integrals for terms like \(C \ln(-p^2)\) and \(1/p^2\), he shows that the continuum factors in the original work are one order too high (e.g., \(E_1\) instead of \(E_0\)).

## Key numbers
- After the replacement \(E_2 \rightarrow E_1\), the dashed line in Figure 1 of Birse and Krippa (1996) varies from 13 to 30 (no units given in text; the quantity is a coupling constant or sum-rule output).
- After the replacement, the solid line in Figure 1 varies from 7.5 to 5 (no units given in text).
- The continuum threshold is denoted \(S_{\pi}\) (no numerical value provided in the comment).

## Context
This comment corrects the continuum treatment in the QCD sum rule calculation of pion-baryon coupling constants by Birse and Krippa (1996). The author argues that the double-dispersion-relation method used by Birse and Krippa introduces an unphysical pole at the continuum threshold \(p^2 = S_{\pi}\), which must be subtracted to satisfy QCD duality. The corrected factors lead to substantially different results for the \(\pi NN\), \(\pi \Sigma \Sigma\), and \(\pi \Sigma \Lambda\) couplings. Further discussion of the correct continuum treatment is given in a separate work by the same author (Kim, 1999, referenced as "hung" in the text).
