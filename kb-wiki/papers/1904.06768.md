# On the two-body decay processes of the predicted three-body $K^*(4307)$ resonance

> In a recent theoretical work, Phys. Lett. B785, 112 (2018), we proposed that a $K^*$ resonance with hidden charm content arises from the $KD\bar D^*$ dynamics, where the $D\bar D^*$ system is treated as a $Z_c(3900)$ or $X(3872)$. With the motivation of determining its further properties, which can be observed in experiments, we now present a calculation of the decay processes of this $K^*$, namely $K^*(4307)$, to two-body channels. Particularly, we consider the decay channels $J/ψK^*(892)$, $\bar{D}D_s$, $\bar{D}D_s^*$ and $\bar{D}^* D_s^*$. The mechanisms of the decay to these channels involve triangular loops and are a consequence of the internal structure of the state. Thus, the values found for the decay widths of the proposed $K^*(4307)$ are related to its nature and should be valuable for carrying on an experimental study of the $K^*(4307)$. A $K^*$ state with such a mass (in the charmonium region) and quantum numbers is a clear manifestation of an exotic meson, since, having hidden charm (i.e., a $c\bar c$ pair), its mass and quantum numbers can not be explained within a quark-antiquark description.

# On the two-body decay processes of the predicted three-body $K^*(4307)$ resonance

## Key claim

The predicted $K^*(4307)$ resonance, which arises from $KD\bar D^*$ dynamics where the $D\bar D^*$ system forms a $Z_c(3900)$ or $X(3872)$ cluster, can decay to two-body channels through triangular loop mechanisms that are a direct consequence of its internal structure; the calculated decay widths are therefore linked to the nature of the state and should guide experimental searches, though the contribution from the $X(3872)$ component is negligible compared to that from the $Z_c(3900)$ component due to the coupling of $K^*_R$ to $KZ_c$ being about four times larger than to $KX$.

## Method

The authors calculated the decay widths of the predicted $K^*(4307)$ resonance (denoted $K^*_R$) to the two-body channels $J/\psi K^*(892)$, $\bar D D_s$, $\bar D D_s^*$, and $\bar D^* D_s^*$ using triangular loop diagrams. The vertices for the $K^*_R\to KZ_c$ coupling were obtained from the residue of the isospin 1/2 scattering matrix at the pole position, while the $Z_c$ couplings to $J/\psi\pi$ and $\bar D D^*$ were taken from the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach of Aceti et al. (2014). The remaining vertices involving pseudoscalar and vector mesons were determined using effective Lagrangians based on chiral and heavy quark spin symmetries, with the tensor loop integrals evaluated via Passarino-Veltman decomposition and regularized with a cutoff of 700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The $K^*(4307)$ resonance has a mass of $(4307\pm 2)$ MeV and a width of $(9\pm 2)$ MeV from the three-body dynamics, with an additional width of about 18 MeV when considering the $Z_c(3900)$ width of 28 MeV.
- The coupling of $K^*_R$ to the isospin 1/2 $KZ_c$ channel is $|g_{K_R^*\to (KZ_c)_{\frac{1}{2}}}| \simeq 22143$ MeV.
- The coupling of $Z_c$ to $J/\psi\pi$ in isospin 1 is $|g_{Z_c\to (J/\psi\pi)_{1}}| \simeq 3715$ MeV.
- The coupling of $Z_c$ to the $\bar D D^*$ system in isospin 1 is $|g_{Z_c\to \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}[(\bar D D^*)_{1}+\text{c.c.}]}| \simeq 8149$ MeV.
- The effective coupling $g$ in the $PPV$ Lagrangian is $g = m_V/(2f_\pi) \simeq 4.41$ for light mesons, with $m_V \simeq 815$ MeV and $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV; for heavy mesons, $g \simeq 9.9$ based on heavy quark symmetry.
- The cutoff used to regularize divergent loop integrals is $\Lambda = 700$ MeV, matching the value used in Ren et al. (2018) to generate the $K^*_R$ state.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction of a hidden-charm $K^*$ resonance with strangeness from Ren et al. (2018, Phys. Lett. B785, 112), where the state was obtained by solving Faddeev equations for the $KD\bar D^*$ system under the fixed center approximation. The present calculation provides decay widths to two-body channels that are experimentally accessible, complementing the three-body decay modes expected from the internal structure. A similar bound state with mass $4317.92^{+3.66}_{-4.32}$ MeV was also predicted by Ma et al. (2018) from solving the Schrodinger equation for the $DD^*K$ system with pion exchange. The results should enable experimental searches at current facilities, as the $K^*(4307)$ would constitute a manifestly exotic meson requiring at least a $c\bar c$ pair plus an $s$ quark and a light antiquark, with no known $q\bar q$ explanation possible.
