# Comment on "Direct Observation of Proton Emission in $^{11}$Be"

> We argue that conclusions of [PRL 123, 082501 (2019)] are incorrect. The authors present the direct observation of beta-delayed proton emission in the beta decay of 11Be. From the determined branching ratio for this process and from the energy spectrum of emitted protons the existence of a so far unobserved narrow resonance in 11Be was deduced. The given beta strength for the transition to this state is however wrong. In addition, we show that the combination of peak position and branching ratio is in strong disagreement with models considered by the authors. Furthermore, we identify several deficiencies in the analysis, and we provide possible sources of background, that could explain the error.

## Key claim

The beta-delayed proton branching ratio and resonance energy reported by Ayyad et al. (2019) are mutually inconsistent with any single-nucleon model, because the implied Gamow-Teller strength of about 4.8 exceeds the maximum possible value of 3; the claimed log(ft) value of 4.8(4) is incorrect and should be approximately 2.9.

## Method

The authors recalculated the log(ft) value from the reported resonance position (11.425 MeV) and branching ratio (1.3 × 10⁻⁵) using standard beta-decay formulae, finding a value of 2.9 for a sharp resonance. They then compared the resulting Gamow-Teller strength with single-nucleon model limits. They also examined the reported proton energy spectrum and identified that the observed peak width (42 keV FWHM) is inconsistent with the quoted energy resolution (15 keV) and the fitted Breit-Wigner width (15 keV). Finally, they extrapolated the known beta-delayed alpha spectrum from Refsgaard et al. to estimate the number of low-energy alpha particles that could contaminate the proton signal.

## Key numbers

- The reported branching ratio of 1.3(3) × 10⁻⁵ and resonance energy of 11.425(2) MeV imply a log(ft) value of approximately 2.9, not 4.8(4) as claimed by Ayyad et al. (2019).
- The implied Gamow-Teller strength ⟨GT⟩² would be about 4.8, which exceeds the maximum possible value of 3 in any single-nucleon model.
- The width of the proton peak shown in Ayyad et al. (2019) is 42 keV (FWHM), inconsistent with the quoted energy resolution of 15 keV and the fitted Breit-Wigner width of 15 keV.
- Extrapolation of the beta-delayed alpha spectrum from Refsgaard et al. indicates about 150 alpha particles could be expected below 250 keV, amounting to 40% of the reported branching ratio.

## Context

This Comment directly challenges the conclusions of Ayyad et al. (2019), who claimed the first direct observation of beta-delayed proton emission in ¹¹Be. The work extends earlier indirect observations by Riisager et al., who reported a branching ratio of (8.3 ± 0.9) × 10⁻⁶ using AMS techniques, and builds on the known beta-delayed alpha emission branch from ¹¹Be studied by Refsgaard et al. with a total branching of 3.30(10)%. The Comment identifies specific deficiencies in the event classification and background subtraction of Ayyad et al. (2019), and suggests that low-energy alpha particles and ⁷Li recoils from the much larger beta-alpha branch are likely sources of contamination that could explain the erroneous result.
