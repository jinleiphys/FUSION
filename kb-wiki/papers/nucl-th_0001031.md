# Spin-orbit interaction in Hartree-Fock calculations

> The contribution of the spin-orbit interaction in Hartree-Fock calculations for closed shell nuclei is studied. We obtain explicit expressions for the finite range spin-orbit force. New terms with respect to the traditional spin-orbit expressions are found. The importance of the finite-range is analyzed. Results obtained with spin-orbit terms taken from realistic interactions are presented. The effect of the spin-orbit isospin dependent terms is evaluated.

# Spin-orbit interaction in Hartree-Fock calculations

## Key claim

The authors derive explicit expressions for finite-range spin-orbit forces in Hartree-Fock calculations and show that, while the dominant contribution comes from the traditional shell-model direct term, the exchange (Fock-Dirac) term modifies spin-orbit splittings by up to 15% in a way that cannot be simulated by simply reparameterizing the force. The spin-orbit isospin-dependent terms produce very small effects, but the finite range of the interaction is essential: zero-range spin-orbit forces produce no splitting in the authors' formalism unless artificially modified.

## Method

The authors extend the Hartree-Fock formalism for closed-shell nuclei to include finite-range spin-orbit interactions of the form L·S and L·S τ₁·τ₂, expanding the interaction in multipoles and deriving explicit expressions for both the direct (Hartree) and exchange (Fock-Dirac) terms in spherical coordinates. They add various spin-orbit terms to a fixed central interaction (B1a, a two-Gaussian force fitted to reproduce the binding energy of ⁴He) and perform HF calculations for the doubly magic nuclei ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb, comparing results from finite-range Gaussian spin-orbit forces, zero-range spin-orbit forces, and spin-orbit terms taken from the realistic Urbana V14, Argonne V14, and Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

- The splitting between the proton 1p levels in ¹⁶O was used to fix the Gaussian spin-orbit force parameters; the fitted strength A₇ values were -108.75 MeV (c calculation, only the 𝒰_C term), -107.50 MeV (d calculation, full direct term), and -97.86 MeV (so calculation, all spin-orbit terms), with the range parameter b₇ fixed to 1.2 fm⁻².
- The exchange term modifies spin-orbit splittings by up to 10-15% relative to calculations with only the direct term, with the sign of the modification varying depending on the orbital angular momentum state (reduced for p states, increased for f, g, h states).
- For ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb, the zero-range spin-orbit interaction produced splittings much larger than empirical values, while finite-range results were comparable to those from Skyrme III and the realistic interactions.
- The maximum absolute differences between splittings calculated with and without the isospin-dependent spin-orbit term (p=8) were larger in ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb than in ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca, but remained small overall.

## Context

This work extends the Hartree-Fock formalism of Co (1998) by adding finite-range spin-orbit terms, and contrasts with the standard zero-range Skyrme-type spin-orbit interactions (Bell 1956) and the Gogny interaction (Gogny 1980) which uses a Skyrme-like expression for the spin-orbit term. The authors compare their results with Skyrme III (Beiner 1975) and with spin-orbit terms from realistic interactions (Lagaris 1981, Wiringa 1984, Wiringa 1995). The study enables future investigations of whether the medium modifies the spin-orbit term of realistic interactions, and identifies that going beyond mean-field theory is likely necessary to reproduce empirical spin-orbit splittings, which are systematically smaller than the HF results except for ¹²C.
