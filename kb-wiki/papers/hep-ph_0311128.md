# Chiral Perturbation Theory in a Nuclear Background

> We propose a novel way to formulate Chiral Perturbation Theory in a nuclear background, characterized by a static, non-uniform distribution of the baryon number that describes the finite nucleus. In the limiting case of a uniform distribution, the theory reduces to the well-known zero-temperature in-medium ChPT. The proposed approach is used to calculate the self-energy of the charged pion in the background of the heavy nucleus at O(p^5) in the chiral expansion, and to derive the leading terms of the pion-nucleus optical potential.

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel formulation of Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) for finite nuclei, extending the standard in-medium ChPT (which assumes uniform baryon density) to systems with non-uniform, finite-size baryon distributions. The approach is used to calculate the charged pion self-energy at O(p^5) in the chiral expansion, but the authors explicitly note that rescattering and absorption terms of O(p^6) and higher orders are quantitatively important and are deferred to future work.

## Method

The authors construct an S-operator formalism using external c-number sources and functional integrals, ensuring chiral symmetry is preserved at every step and avoiding off-shell ambiguities. They expand the generating functional in powers of nucleon sources, then define an operator Ŝ(j) whose matrix elements between free-particle states yield the desired scattering amplitudes. For the nuclear background, they assume the static limit (A → ∞), spin-saturation, and that the nucleus contains no free pions or photons, parametrizing nuclear structure through expectation values of normal-ordered products of free nucleon fields. The pion self-energy operator Π(E; q′, q) is then extracted from the two-point function of pseudoscalar densities sandwiched between nuclear states, and the bound-state energy is identified with the pole position of this two-point function.

## Key numbers

- The pion self-energy is calculated at O(p^5) in the chiral expansion, which generates the complete set of leading terms in the pion-nucleus optical potential that are linear in the proton and neutron densities ρ_p(r) and ρ_n(r).
- The approach reduces to standard in-medium ChPT in the limit of a uniform baryon number distribution.
- The O(p^2) πN electromagnetic low-energy constant f₁ enters the in-medium mass shift of the charged pion at lowest order, independently from other unknown LECs.
- The authors state that 4-fermion interactions do not contribute to the pion-nucleus optical potential at O(p^5).

## Context

This work extends the in-medium ChPT framework of Oller (2000) and the Annals of Physics approach (2002) from uniform nuclear matter to finite nuclei with non-uniform density distributions, addressing a limitation noted in those prior works. It also provides a systematic alternative to the phenomenological potential analyses of Ericson and Weise (1988) and the local density approximation used by Kolomeitsev et al. (2003) for calculating pionic atom level shifts. The formalism enables future systematic calculations of pion-nucleus bound states (pionic atoms) at higher chiral orders, and may allow experimental constraints on the electromagnetic low-energy constant f₁ from pionic atom data, which is currently the dominant uncertainty in relating the pionic hydrogen ground-state shift to πN scattering lengths.
