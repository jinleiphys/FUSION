# RMF models with $σ$-scaled hadron masses and couplings for description of heavy-ion collisions below 2A GeV

> Within the relativistic mean-field framework with hadron masses and coupling constants dependent on the mean scalar field we study properties of nuclear matter at finite temperatures, baryon densities and isospin asymmetries relevant for heavy-ion collisions at laboratory energies below 2$A$ GeV. Previously constructed (KVORcut-based and MKVOR-based) models for the description of the cold hadron matter, which differ mainly by the density dependence of the nucleon effective mass and symmetry energy, are extended for finite temperatures. The baryon equation of state, which includes nucleons and $Δ$ resonances is supplemented by the contribution of the pion gas described either by the vacuum dispersion relation or with taking into account the $s$-wave pion-baryon interaction. Distribution of the charge between components is found. Thermodynamical characteristics on $T-n$ plane are considered. The energy-density and entropy-density isotherms are constructed and a dynamical trajectory of the hadron system formed in heavy-ion collisions is described. The effects of taking into account the $Δ$ isobars and the $s$-wave pion-nucleon interaction on pion differential cross sections, pion to proton and $π^-/π^+$ ratios are studied. The liquid-gas first-order phase transition is studied within the same models in isospin-symmetric and asymmetric systems. We demonstrate that our models yield thermodynamic characteristics of the phase transition compatible with available experimental results. In addition, we discuss the scaled variance of baryon and electric charge in the phase transition region. Effect of the non-zero surface tension on spatial redistribution of the electric charge is considered for a possible application to heavy-ion collisions at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper extends previously constructed cold-hadron-matter RMF models (KVORcut-based and MKVOR-based) to finite temperatures and isospin asymmetries relevant for heavy-ion collisions below 2A GeV, demonstrating that the resulting equations of state yield thermodynamic characteristics of the liquid-gas phase transition compatible with available experimental results, but only for baryon densities up to about 3 times nuclear saturation density and temperatures below the pion mass.

## Method

The authors generalize relativistic mean-field models with σ-scaled hadron masses and coupling constants to finite temperature, including nucleons and Δ resonances as baryonic degrees of freedom and a pion gas described either by the vacuum dispersion relation or with s-wave pion-baryon interactions. They solve the mean-field equations for given baryon density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry to obtain thermodynamic quantities, then apply a simplified expanding fireball framework to describe the dynamical trajectory of the hadron system formed in heavy-ion collisions. The liquid-gas first-order phase transition is studied in both isospin-symmetric and asymmetric systems, first with zero surface tension and then including non-zero surface tension effects.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- KVORcut03 model: binding energy per nucleon E₀ = -16 MeV, incompressibility K = 275 MeV, nucleon effective mass m_N*(n₀) = 0.805 m_N, symmetry energy J = 32 MeV, slope parameter L = 71 MeV
- MKVOR model: binding energy per nucleon E₀ = -16 MeV, incompressibility K = 240 MeV, nucleon effective mass m_N*(n₀) = 0.730 m_N, symmetry energy J = 30 MeV, slope parameter L = 41 MeV
- For the MKVOR*Δπ_free model at U_Δ(n₀) = -50 MeV, the first-order phase transition to Δ resonance matter occurs at n_c,Δ ≈ 3.6 n₀ for T = 55 MeV and n_c,Δ ≈ 3.3 n₀ for T ≈ 140 MeV
- The binding energy per baryon for isospin-asymmetric matter: E_bind(Y_Z=0.4) ≈ -14.7 MeV
- Pion weak decay constant: f_π ≈ 92.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the KVORcut-based and MKVOR-based RMF models previously developed for cold baryon matter by Maslov, Kolomeitsev, and Voskresensky (2015-2017) to finite temperatures and isospin asymmetries relevant for heavy-ion collisions. It builds upon earlier expanding fireball frameworks by Siemens and Rasmussen (1978), Friedman and Pandharipande (1981), and Mishustin et al. (1983), as well as the modified Walecka model studies by Voskresensky (1989-1993), but now uses models that simultaneously satisfy modern constraints from both heavy-ion flow data and the 2-solar-mass neutron star observations. The work enables future hydrodynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions below 2A GeV with these realistic equations of state, and provides a framework for studying the liquid-gas phase transition with isospin dependence and surface tension effects that could produce pasta-like structures.
