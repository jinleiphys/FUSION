# Effective interactions and shell model studies of heavy tin isotopes

> We present results from large-scale shell-model calculations of even and odd tin isotopes from 134Sn to 142}Sn with a shell-model space defined by the 1f7/2,2p3/2,0h9/2,2p1/2,1f5/2,0i13/2 single-particle orbits. An effective two-body interaction based on modern nucleon-nucleon interactions is employed. The shell-model results are in turn analyzed for their pairing content using a generalized seniority approach. Our results indicate that a pairing-model picture captures a great deal of the structure and the correlations of the lowest lying states for even and odd isotopes.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a generalized seniority pairing model captures a great deal of the structure and correlations of the lowest-lying states in even and odd tin isotopes from 134Sn to 142Sn, but this conclusion is qualified by the observation that for some states (such as the 4+1 to 2+1 transition in 138Sn) the naive seniority picture breaks down due to cancellations from other contributions.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations for even and odd tin isotopes from 134Sn to 142Sn using a model space defined by the 1f7/2, 2p3/2, 0h9/2, 2p1/2, 1f5/2, and 0i13/2 single-particle orbits, with 132Sn as the closed-shell core. An effective two-body neutron-neutron interaction was derived from the charge-dependent Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential via a G-matrix approach followed by a perturbative many-body scheme (the Q-box method) including non-folded diagrams through third order. The resulting shell-model wave functions were then analyzed for their pairing content using the generalized seniority approach of Talmi, constructing seniority v=0, v=2, and v=1 states from the two-particle amplitudes of 134Sn and computing overlaps with the full shell-model states.

## Key numbers

- The neutron shell gap at 132Sn is 4.84 MeV, defined as the difference between the 1f7/2 and 0g7/2 single-particle energies.
- The first excited negative parity state in 132Sn has excitation energy 4.351 MeV with Jpi = 3-.
- The 0i13/2 single-particle orbital is estimated at 2.6940 +/- 0.2 MeV above the 132Sn + n threshold.
- The 0+1 to 2+1 spacing in even tin isotopes remains nearly constant at approximately 0.73-0.80 MeV across the isotopic chain, with a small increase at 140Sn (0.8028 MeV).
- For 134Sn, the calculated 2+1 energy is 0.7748 MeV compared to the experimental value of 0.7256 MeV; the 4+1 energy is 1.1161 MeV compared to 1.0734 MeV; the 6+1 energy is 1.2582 MeV compared to 1.2474 MeV; the 8+1 energy is 2.4634 MeV compared to 2.5089 MeV.
- The B(E2: 2+1 to 0+1) values (in W.u. with e_eff = 0.64e) are: 1.50 for 134Sn, 2.94 for 136Sn, 4.19 for 138Sn, 5.72 for 140Sn, and 5.55 for 142Sn.
- The B(E2: 4+1 to 2+1) value for 138Sn is 0.006 W.u., nearly vanishing.
- The B(E2: 6+1 to 4+1) for 134Sn is 0.83 W.u., with experimental value 0.88 +/- 0.17 W.u.
- The B(E2: 0+1 to 2+1) for 134Sn is approximately 7.24 W.u. experimentally, reproduced with e_eff = 0.62e.
- The effective neutron charge used for all calculated transitions is e_eff = 0.64e, averaged from values of 0.66e and 0.62e obtained from the two available experimental transitions in 134Sn.

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies of neutron-rich tin isotopes beyond 135Sn, where experimental data are sparse, providing predictive spectroscopic information for nuclei up to 142Sn. The calculations build on earlier mean-field investigations by Hoffman and Lenske and by Dobaczewski et al. (1996), and on a study of two and three valence particles by Coraggio et al. (2002). The results enable future experimental comparisons with decay studies at CERN/Isolde and transition strength measurements at HRIBF, and the generalized seniority analysis provides a framework for understanding pairing correlations as the neutron drip line is approached (predicted at A ~ 160).
