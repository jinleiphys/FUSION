# Neutron skins of $^{208}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca from pionic probes

> The neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb has received considerable attention in recent years. A variety of strongly-interacting probes depict a rather consistent picture but pionic probes have not been referred to in this context. We present here neutron-skin values from pionic atoms and from total reaction cross sections of $π^+$ between 0.7 and 2 GeV/c which fit well into the picture. In addition we show that a neutron skin for $^{48}$Ca can be obtained from existing data on pionic atoms and the result agrees with pion scattering experiments and with the scattering of $α$ particles.

# Neutron skins of $^{208}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca from pionic probes

## Key claim
Pionic probes (pionic atoms and total reaction cross sections of $\pi^+$ between 0.7 and 2 GeV/c) yield neutron skin values for $^{208}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca that agree with the majority of other strong-interaction probe results, but the uncertainties are relatively large and the pionic atom fits for $^{208}$Pb show a disappointing $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom of 5.5 compared to the global fit value of 1.9.

## Method
For $^{208}$Pb, the author analyzed strong interaction level shifts and widths of pionic atoms using an optical potential from global fits to 100 data points across the periodic table, varying radial parameters of the neutron distribution with two extreme shapes (skin and halo). Separately, total reaction cross sections for $\pi^+$ on C, Ca, and Pb at six momenta between 0.71 and 2.00 GeV/c were analyzed using a $t\rho$-type optical potential constructed from forward pion-proton scattering amplitudes, with calibration on C and Ca where the neutron skin vanishes. For $^{48}$Ca, pionic atom data for the $2p$ level shift and width were analyzed using the same global fit potentials.

## Key numbers
- For $^{208}$Pb from pionic atoms: $\delta R_{np} = 0.15 \pm 0.08$ fm for the skin shape and $0.14 \pm 0.10$ fm for the halo shape
- For $^{208}$Pb from $\pi^+$ reaction cross sections: weighted average $\delta R_{np} = 0.11 \pm 0.06$ fm across six momenta (0.71, 0.84, 1.00, 1.36, 1.58, 2.00 GeV/c)
- Individual $\delta R_{np}$ values from $\pi^+$ reaction cross sections range from $0.073 \pm 0.057$ fm at 1.00 GeV/c to $0.162 \pm 0.066$ fm at 0.84 GeV/c
- For $^{48}$Ca from pionic atoms: $\delta R_{np} = 0.13 \pm 0.06$ fm for the skin version and $0.16 \pm 0.07$ fm for the halo version
- Previous weighted average from Garcia-Recio et al. (1992) for $^{208}$Pb: $\delta R_{np} = 0.18 \pm 0.05$ fm
- Gibbs and Dedonder (1992) value for $^{48}$Ca from pion scattering across the (3,3) resonance: $\delta R_{np} = 0.11 \pm 0.04$ fm
- Alpha particle scattering result for $^{48}$Ca from Gils et al. (1984): $\delta R_{np} = 0.17 \pm 0.05$ fm

## Context
This work extends the study of neutron skins using strong-interaction probes, which had previously included protons, alpha particles, and other hadrons but had not systematically employed pionic probes. The results for $^{208}$Pb are consistent with the accepted values compiled by Jastrzebski et al. (2004) and with later polarized proton measurements by Zegers et al. (2012). For $^{48}$Ca, the pionic atom results agree with pion scattering analyses by Gibbs and Dedonder (1992) and with alpha particle scattering by Gils et al. (1984). The work enables compilation of strong-interaction neutron skin values for $^{48}$Ca in anticipation of the C-REX parity-violating electron scattering experiment at Jlab., analogous to the PREX experiment for $^{208}$Pb.
