# Elastic electron deuteron scattering with consistent meson exchange and relativistic contributions of leading order

> The influence of relativistic contributions to elastic electron deuteron scattering is studied systematically at low and intermediate momentum transfers ($Q^2\leq 30$ fm$^{-2}$). In a $(p/M)$-expansion, all leading order relativistic $π$-exchange contributions consistent with the Bonn OBEPQ models are included. In addition, static heavy meson exchange currents including boost terms and lowest order $ρπγ$-currents are considered. Sizeable effects from the various relativistic two-body contributions, mainly from $π$-exchange, have been found in form factors, structure functions and the tensor polarization $T_{20}$. Furthermore, static properties, viz. magnetic dipole and charge quadrupole moments and the mean square charge radius are evaluated.

# Elastic electron deuteron scattering with consistent meson exchange and relativistic contributions of leading order

## Key claim
Relativistic contributions of leading order in a (p/M)-expansion, consistently included for all one- and two-body currents and boost terms within the Bonn OBEPQ potential models, produce sizeable effects in form factors, structure functions, and the tensor polarization T20, but the theoretical description still shows systematic deviations from experimental data, overshooting B(Q2) by up to a factor of two at Q2 = 25 fm−2, indicating that the approach reaches its limit near Q2 ∼ 25 fm−2.

## Method
The authors used the equation-of-motion method with the FST (Fukuda-Sawada-Taketani) procedure to eliminate explicit meson degrees of freedom, then applied a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation to obtain nonrelativistic reduction including leading order relativistic contributions. They evaluated elastic electron-deuteron scattering form factors, structure functions A(Q2) and B(Q2), and tensor polarization T20 for momentum transfers Q2 ≤ 30 fm−2 using the three Bonn OBEPQ potential models (A, B, C), incorporating relativistic one-body currents, one-body boost terms, relativistic π-exchange currents including retardation and two-body boost, static heavy meson exchange currents (ρ, η, ω, σ, δ), and the lowest order ρπγ-current.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic dipole moment μd: total theoretical value 0.8875 μN, compared to experimental 0.8574 μN (3.5% higher)
- Electric quadrupole moment Qd: total enhancement of 3.8% over impulse approximation, experimental value 0.2860(15) fm2
- Mean square charge radius rdch: total theoretical value 2.1121 fm, compared to experimental 2.127(7) fm (about 1% difference)
- Relativistic one-body contributions reduce the magnetic moment by 0.7% relative to nonrelativistic impulse approximation
- Relativistic π-exchange currents enhance the magnetic moment by about 1.8%, with retardation reducing it by 0.6%
- ρ-exchange contributes 1.7%, other heavy mesons 0.8%, and ρπγ-current 0.5% to the magnetic moment
- Pion exchange charge contribution increases the quadrupole moment by 4.9%, while relativistic one-body terms decrease it by 0.7%
- For B(Q2) at Q2 = 25 fm−2, the deviation between theory and experiment reaches a factor of two
- The zero of GC(Q2) shifts with potential model: increasing D-state probability Pd shifts the zero downward
- D-state percentages Pd for OBEPQ models: model A smallest, model C largest (specific values not given in truncated text)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous systematic studies of relativistic contributions in deuteron photo- and electrodisintegration (Ritz et al., 1997; Ritz and Arenhoevel, 1998) to elastic electron-deuteron scattering. It provides a more consistent treatment than earlier approaches: Mosconi and Ricci (1989) used the Paris potential without heavier mesons; Schiavilla and Riska (1991) omitted boost contributions; Wiringa et al. (1995) used the Argonne v18 potential also without boost terms; Tamura et al. (1992) performed the most extensive previous treatment but with a less sophisticated one-boson-exchange potential. The present work enables comparison with the recent covariant three-dimensional relativistic framework of Phillips et al. (1999) and identifies the need for further studies to resolve the systematic overprediction of magnetic observables, particularly B(Q2).
