# A three-body calculation of incoherent $π^0$ photoproduction on a deuteron

> Incoherent $π^0$ photoproduction on a deuteron in the $Δ(1232)$ region is treated in a three-body scattering approach using separable two-body interactions. Results are presented for total and differential cross sections. It turns out that the role of higher order terms beyond the first order in the multiple scattering series is insignificant, and their inclusion cannot explain the existing discrepancy between theory and experiment.

# Wiki Page: Three-body calculation of incoherent π⁰ photoproduction on a deuteron

## Key claim

Higher-order multiple scattering terms beyond first-order rescattering contribute only 1–2% to the cross section in the Δ(1232) region, and therefore cannot resolve the persistent 15% overestimate of the experimental total cross section at the Δ peak. The authors conclude that the discrepancy likely originates from an overestimate of the free-neutron cross section in existing multipole analyses, not from neglected three-body dynamics.

## Method

The authors solve coupled three-body Faddeev-type integral equations for the final π⁰np state, using separable two-body interactions for the πN (Δ-resonance channel) and NN (³S₁ and ¹S₀ channels) subsystems. The electromagnetic transition γN → Δ is treated in the dominant M1 multipole, with parameters fitted to the M⁽⁺⁾₁₊ multipole data from threshold to 450 MeV. The coupled one-dimensional integral equations are solved by matrix inversion with contour rotation into the complex momentum plane, including partial waves up to J_max = 7 and L_max = 9.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section at the Δ(1232) peak is overestimated by about 15% compared to experimental data from Krusche (1999) and Siodlaczek (2001).
- The 2⁺ partial wave contributes approximately 45% of the total impulse-approximation cross section in the Δ region; inclusion of final-state interactions reduces σ(2⁺) by a factor of 2–3.
- The full three-body calculation changes the cross section by only 1–2% relative to the first-order rescattering calculation in the Δ resonance region.
- The bare Δ mass is fitted as M⁰_Δ = 1306 MeV, with coupling f²_πNΔ/4π = 0.8113 and cutoff β_Δ = 295 MeV.
- The first-order np rescattering reduces the differential cross section at forward pion angles significantly; πN rescattering adds only 0.5–1% correction.
- True pion absorption corrections are estimated at about 0.5% using a bound-state picture.
- To bring theory into agreement with data, the free-neutron cross section would need to be decreased by about 25%.

## Context

This work extends the first-order rescattering calculations of Fix and Arenhövel (2005) by solving the full three-body equations for γd → π⁰np, confirming that higher-order terms are negligible. The results contradict the conjecture that ΔN interactions could broaden the Δ resonance and resolve the discrepancy with data from Krusche (1999) and Siodlaczek (2001). The paper also tests the three-body formalism against π⁺d → π⁺np data from Khandaker (1996), finding reasonable agreement. The unresolved discrepancy points to the need for improved elementary γN → πN amplitudes, as the existing MAID and SAID multipole analyses may overestimate the neutron cross section.
