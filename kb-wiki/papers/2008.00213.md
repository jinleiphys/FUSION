# Proto-neutron stars with heavy baryons and universal relations

> We use covariant density functional theory to obtain the equation of state (EoS) of matter in compact stars at non-zero temperature, including the full baryon octet as well as the $Δ(1232)$ resonance states. Global properties of hot $Δ$-admixed hypernuclear stars are computed for fixed values of entropy per baryon ($S/A$) and lepton fraction ($Y_L$). Universal relations between the moment of inertia, quadrupole moment, tidal deformability, and compactness of compact stars are established for fixed values of $S/A$ and $Y_L$ that are analogous to those known for cold catalyzed compact stars. We also verify that the $I$-Love-$Q$ relations hold at finite temperature for constant values of $S/A$ and $Y_L$.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that universal relations (I-Love-Q and moment of inertia/quadrupole moment/tidal deformability versus compactness) hold for hot proto-neutron star matter at fixed entropy per baryon and lepton fraction, with accuracy comparable to cold stars, contrary to earlier claims that thermal effects break universality.

## Method

The authors used covariant density functional theory with the DDME2 parameterization extended to include the full baryon octet (hyperons) and Δ(1232) resonance states. They computed global properties of hot, rigidly rotating compact stars for fixed values of entropy per baryon (S/A) and lepton fraction (Y_L), solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for non-rotating configurations and using slow-rotation and tidal perturbation formalisms for rotating and tidally deformed stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass for cold β-equilibrated stars: 2.113 M⊙ (DDME2Y model) and 2.111 M⊙ (DDME2YΔ model)
- Central baryon density at maximum mass: 0.93 fm⁻³ (DDME2Y) and 0.96 fm⁻³ (DDME2YΔ)
- Radius of a 1.44 M⊙ star: 13.25 km (DDME2Y) and 13.09 km (DDME2YΔ)
- Tidal deformability for a 1.4 M⊙ star: Λ₁.₄ = 712 (DDME2Y) and Λ₁.₄ = 653 (DDME2YΔ)
- Δ⁻ resonance appears as first heavy baryon at threshold density 0.28 fm⁻³ with corresponding gravitational mass 0.96 M⊙
- Λ hyperon threshold in DDME2YΔ model: 0.36 fm⁻³ at mass 1.33 M⊙
- Nuclear saturation density n_s = 0.152 fm⁻³, binding energy per nucleon E_s = -16.1 MeV, compression modulus K_inf = 250.9 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32.3 MeV, slope L = 51.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the cold-star universal relations established by Yagi and collaborators (2013) to finite-temperature proto-neutron star matter, contradicting earlier findings by Martinon (2014), Marques (2017), and Lenka (2019) who reported deviations from universality at finite temperature. The DDME2Y parameterization follows the hyperonic extension by Fortin (2016) and includes Δ resonances as studied by Drago (2014), Li (2018), and others. The results enable interpretation of gravitational wave signals from binary neutron star mergers and proto-neutron star evolution without requiring detailed knowledge of the hot equation of state.
