# Phase diagram of chiral quark matter: color and electrically neutral Fulde-Ferrell phase

> The phase diagram of charge and color neutral two-flavor color superconducting quark matter is studied including the homogeneous two-flavor superconductor (2SC) and the inhomogeneous Fulde-Ferrell (FF) phases within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The low-temperature domain T < 5 MeV of the phase diagram contains the FF phase, which borders at high temperatures to the 2SC phase. The critical temperature of phase transition from the 2SC to the unpaired state is in the range 20-30 MeV. We derive the equation of state of matter and its composition and show that matter in mature compact stars should be in the inhomogeneous FF-like superconducting state. We briefly discuss the astrophysical implications of such a phase in compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in charge- and color-neutral two-flavor quark matter under compact-star conditions, the homogeneous 2SC phase is replaced at low temperatures (T < 5 MeV) by the inhomogeneous Fulde-Ferrell (FF) phase, but this conclusion is an upper bound on the ground-state energy because only a single-plane-wave order parameter was considered, and more complicated crystalline order parameters could lower the energy further.

## Method

The authors solve the mean-field thermodynamic potential of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for two-flavor quark matter, imposing both charge neutrality and color neutrality via chemical potentials μ_e and μ_8. They simultaneously solve four coupled integral equations: the gap equation for the pairing gap Δ, the equation for the total momentum |Q| of the FF condensate, and the two neutrality conditions ∂Ω/∂μ_e = 0 and ∂Ω/∂μ_8 = 0. The calculation is performed for baryon densities up to three times nuclear saturation density (3ρ_0) and temperatures up to the critical temperature of the 2SC-to-unpaired transition.

## Key numbers

- The FF phase occupies the low-temperature domain T ≤ 5 MeV of the phase diagram.
- The critical temperature for the 2SC-to-unpaired phase transition lies in the range 20–30 MeV.
- At T = 1 MeV and n_B = 0.32 fm⁻³, the pairing gap Δ = 38.0 MeV and the condensate momentum |Q| = 0.21 fm⁻¹.
- At T = 1 MeV and n_B = 0.48 fm⁻³, Δ = 70.2 MeV and |Q| = 0.53 fm⁻¹.
- At T = 5 MeV and n_B = 0.32 fm⁻³, Δ = 37.8 MeV and |Q| = 0.00 fm⁻¹ (homogeneous 2SC phase).
- The baryonic density range extends up to 3ρ_0, where ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density.
- The coupling constants used are G_d = 3.11 GeV⁻² and G_d Λ² = 1.31.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Sedrakian (2009) by imposing charge and color neutrality on the FF phase, which was not done previously. It contradicts the assumption that the homogeneous 2SC phase persists to low temperatures in neutral matter, showing instead that the FF phase is favored below 5 MeV. The results enable construction of complete equations of state for hybrid compact stars via matching procedures (e.g., Maxwell construction) and open the possibility of studying astrophysical signatures such as gravitational waves from crystalline quark cores (Knippel, 2009) and latent heat release from the first-order BCS-FF phase transition powering burst phenomena.
