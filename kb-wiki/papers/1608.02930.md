# Selection Rules for ^48Cr

## Key claim

In the single j shell (f_{7/2}) model space, ^48Cr is the first even-even nucleus where T=0 (isoscalar) J=1^+ states and T=1 J=0^+ states appear, and the quantity S = (-1)^((v_p + v_n)/2), involving the seniorities of protons and neutrons separately, is a good quantum number despite seniority itself not being conserved, leading to selection rules for electromagnetic transitions and static moments.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations in both the single j (f_{7/2}) space and the full f-p model space using the NUSHELLX code with the MBZE interaction. They analyzed wave functions in terms of proton and neutron angular momentum couplings D^{I,T}(J_p, J_n) and assigned the quantum number S based on the sum of proton and neutron seniorities. They computed B(M1) and B(E2) transition strengths and static quadrupole moments, comparing results between the restricted single j space and the larger configuration space.

## Key numbers

- B(E2, 0_1^+ → 2_1^+) = 1225.5 e^2 fm^4 in the large space, compared to 452.6 e^2 fm^4 in the single j space
- B(E2, 0_1^+ → 2_2^+) = 2.367 e^2 fm^4 in the large space, compared to 0 in the single j space (vanishing due to selection rule)
- Static quadrupole moment Q(2^+) = -35.42 e fm^4 in the large space, with intrinsic quadrupole moment Q_0 = 123.97 e fm^4 from the quadrupole moment and 125.30 e fm^4 from B(E2)
- B(M1) from lowest J=1^+ T=1 state to lowest J=0^+ T=0 state = 1.101 μ_N^2 (orbital contribution 0.3046 μ_N^2, spin contribution 0.2475 μ_N^2)
- Lowest J=0^+ T=1 state excitation energy = 8.89 MeV; lowest J=1^+ T=0 state excitation energy = 4.81 MeV
- Largest B(E2) from J=1^+ states to J=2^+ states = 25.32 e^2 fm^4 (considerably smaller than the collective 0^+ → 2^+ transition of 1225.5 e^2 fm^4)

## Context

This work extends the earlier findings of Escuderos and Zamick (2015) and Bayman (1966) regarding the S quantum number in midshell nuclei, with analytical proof by Neergaard (2015). The selection rules generalize results for identical particles from Racah (1943) and Lawson's textbook to the case of both protons and neutrons, replacing single-particle seniority v with (v_p + v_n). The paper enables future experimental searches for scissors mode excitations in ^48Cr, which is unstable and cannot be directly excited by electrons, but may be studied through deexcitation experiments following the approach of recent work on other nuclei.
