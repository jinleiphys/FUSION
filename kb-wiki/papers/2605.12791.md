# Jet Momentum Broadening in Viscous QCD Matter: A Moment Expansion Approach

> We formulate out-of-equilibrium jet momentum broadening in QCD effective kinetic theory through a moment expansion of the medium distribution function, a method traditionally used to derive relativistic viscous hydrodynamics from kinetic theory. We explicitly compute the leading near-equilibrium contribution to the spatial jet broadening tensor $\hat q^{ij}$ within the 14-moment approximation, and show that it is controlled by the medium shear-stress tensor. This provides a direct map from QCD effective kinetic theory to event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations, converting local shear-stress fields into anisotropic corrections to jet broadening in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The leading near-equilibrium correction to the jet momentum broadening tensor \(\hat q^{ij}\) is controlled by the medium shear-stress tensor \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\), providing a direct map from QCD effective kinetic theory to event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations. This result is derived within the 14-moment approximation and is valid for small deviations from local equilibrium.

## Method

The authors expand the non-equilibrium part of the medium distribution function \(\delta f_b(X,K)\) in a complete orthogonal basis of irreducible tensors constructed from the particle momentum \(K^\mu\), the fluid 4-velocity \(U^\mu\), and the metric, following the standard moment expansion used to derive relativistic viscous hydrodynamics from kinetic theory. They truncate the expansion at the 14-moment approximation, retaining only scalar, vector, and rank-two moments, and for a massless system at zero baryon chemical potential the only remaining dissipative contribution is the shear-stress tensor \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\). The non-equilibrium correction \(\delta \hat q^{ij}\) is then computed in linear response to \(\pi^{ij}\) using the QCD scattering matrix elements within the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation with a screened \(t\)-channel gluon exchange.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium transverse jet broadening coefficient is \(\hat q^{\rm eq}_\perp = \frac{g^2}{4\pi}C_A T m_D^2 \ln\left(1+\frac{\Lambda_\perp^2}{m_D^2}\right)\), where \(m_D\) is the Debye mass, \(T\) is the temperature, and \(\Lambda_\perp\) is the transverse momentum cutoff.
- The equilibrium longitudinal jet broadening coefficient is \(\hat q^{\rm eq}_\parallel = \frac{g^2 C_A T M_\infty^2}{4\pi} \ln\left(1 + \frac{\mu_{\tilde q_\perp}^2}{M_\infty^2}\right)\), with \(M_\infty^2 = \frac{g^2 C_A T^2}{6}\).
- For a purely gluonic medium with screened \(t\)-channel exchange, the coefficients \(\alpha\), \(\beta\), and \(\gamma\) are computed as functions of \(\Lambda_\perp/T\) for two values of the screening mass: \(m_D = T\) and \(m_D = 3T\).
- The coefficients satisfy \(\alpha > 0\), \(\beta > 0\), and \(\gamma < 0\) for the cases shown in the paper.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet momentum broadening in non-equilibrium systems using QCD kinetic theory (Schlichting 2020, Boguslavski 2023, Barata 2025) by establishing a direct connection to the dissipative currents of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics. Prior approaches modeled the medium using ad hoc anisotropy parameters, whereas this framework expresses non-equilibrium corrections in terms of the shear-stress tensor \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\), which is a standard output of hydrodynamic simulations (Romatschke 2017). This enables event-by-event incorporation of shear-induced corrections into phenomenological studies of jet momentum broadening in heavy-ion collisions, and the tensor decomposition of \(\delta \hat q^{ij}\) is model-independent, depending only on rotational symmetry and linearity in \(\pi^{ij}\).
