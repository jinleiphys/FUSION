# Quantitative extraction of the jet transport parameter from combined data at RHIC and LHC

> Using theoretical tools developed by the JET Collaboration in which one employes 2+1D or 3+1D hydrodynamic models for the bulk medium evolution and jet quenching models, the combined data on suppression of single inclusive hadron spectra at both RHIC and LHC are systematically analyzed with five different approaches to the parton energy loss. The jet transport parameter is extracted from the best fits to the data with values of $\hat q \approx 1.2\pm 0.3$ and $1.9\pm 0.7$ GeV$^2$/fm in the center of the most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.67$ TeV, respectively at an initial time $τ_0=0.6$ fm/$c$ for a quark jet with an initial energy of 10 GeV/$c$.

## Key claim

The jet transport parameter \(\hat q\) is extracted from combined RHIC and LHC data using five different parton energy-loss models, yielding values of \(1.2\pm 0.3\) and \(1.9\pm 0.7\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at the highest temperatures reached in central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions, but these values carry theoretical uncertainties from the model spread and are quoted for a specific initial time \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm/\(c\) and a quark jet of initial energy 10 GeV/\(c\).

## Method

The JET Collaboration combined five parton energy-loss approaches (GLV/CUJET, high-twist HT-BW and HT-M, MARTINI, and McGill-AMY) with 2+1D or 3+1D hydrodynamic models for the bulk medium evolution. A \(\chi^2\) fit to the suppression ratios of single inclusive hadron spectra in the most central (0-5%) Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV was performed within each approach to extract the model parameters and the corresponding jet transport parameter \(\hat q\).

## Key numbers

- \(\hat q \approx 1.2\pm 0.3\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at the center of most central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, at initial time \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm/\(c\) for a quark jet with initial energy 10 GeV/\(c\).
- \(\hat q \approx 1.9\pm 0.7\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at the center of most central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, at initial time \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm/\(c\) for a quark jet with initial energy 10 GeV/\(c\).
- \(\hat q/T^3 \approx 4.6\pm 1.2\) at RHIC and \(3.7\pm 1.4\) at LHC at the highest temperatures reached in the most central collisions.
- Initial temperatures from the hydrodynamic models: \(T_0=346-373\) MeV at RHIC and \(T_0=447-486\) MeV at LHC.
- In cold nuclei from DIS experiments, \(\hat q_N \approx 0.02\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, an order of magnitude smaller than in A+A collisions.

## Context

This work extends the systematic extraction of the jet transport parameter from single hadron suppression data at both RHIC and LHC, building on earlier jet quenching studies such as Wang (1991) and the first RHIC observations by Adcox (2001). The combined analysis rules out earlier models that gave unrealistically large \(\hat q\) values and provides a benchmark for future studies of dihadron and gamma-hadron correlations, which can further constrain \(\hat q\). The results enable comparisons to next-to-leading order calculations of parton energy loss and to the QCD evolution of the twist-four matrix elements related to \(\hat q\), as discussed by Kang (2013) and Iancu.
