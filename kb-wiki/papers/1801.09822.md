# $N^*$ Production from $e^+e^-$ Annihilations

> Up to now, the $N^*$ production from $e^+e^-$ annihilations has been studied only around charmonium region. Charmonium decays to $N^*$s are analogous to (time-like) EM form factors in that the charm quark annihilation provides a nearly pointlike (ggg) current. Complementary to other sources, such as $πN$, $eN$ and $γN$ reactions, this new source for $N^*$ spectroscopy has a few advantages, such as an isospin filter and a low spin filter. The experimental results on $N^*$ from $e^+e^-$ annihilations and their phenomenological implications are reviewed. Possible new sources on $N^*$ production from $e^+e^-$ annihilations are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how charmonium decays from $e^+e^-$ annihilations provide a complementary source for $N^*$ spectroscopy, with the specific advantage that the three-gluon annihilation process acts as an isospin filter (selecting only isospin 1/2 states) and a low-spin filter, but the review notes that this source has only been studied around the charmonium region so far.

## Method

The author reviews experimental results from BES/BESII/BESIII collaborations on $N^*$ production from $J/\psi$, $\psi(2S)$, $\psi(3770)$, and $\chi_{cJ}$ decays into final states such as $\bar pp\eta$, $p\bar n\pi^-$, $p\bar p\pi^0$, $pK^-\bar\Lambda$, and $\bar pp\omega$. Invariant mass spectra and Dalitz plots from these decays are compared with phase space distributions, and partial wave analyses are used to extract $N^*$ resonance parameters.

## Key numbers

- Three new $N^*$ resonances above 2 GeV were found: $N^*(2040)$ with $3/2^+$, $N^*(2300)$ with $1/2^+$, and $N^*(2570)$ with $5/2^-$.
- The $N^*(1440)$ peak is produced much stronger from $\psi(2S)$ than from $J/\psi$ decays.
- A clear $\Sigma^*$ peak around 1580 MeV was observed in $\psi(2S) \to \Lambda\bar\Sigma^{\pm}\pi^{\mp}$, consistent with the 1-star $\Sigma(1580) 3/2^-$ resonance.
- A very narrow $\Lambda^*$ peak around 1670 MeV was observed by Belle in $\Lambda^{+}_{c} \to p K^{-} \pi^{+}$.
- Super-heavy $N^*$ with hidden charm were predicted around 4.3 GeV, and with hidden beauty around 11 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the study of $N^*$ spectroscopy beyond traditional $\pi N$, $eN$, and $\gamma N$ reactions (reviewed by Klempt (2009), Aznauryan (2011), and Crede (2013)) by focusing on $e^+e^-$ annihilation data from BES collaborations. The results address two outstanding problems in the classical 3-quark constituent quark model: the reversed mass ordering of the lowest excited states (e.g., $N^*(1535)$ heavier than $N^*(1440)$) and the "missing $N^*$ states" around 2 GeV/$c^2$ predicted by Capstick. The work enables future searches for super-heavy $N^*$ and hyperon resonances with hidden charm and beauty at super-B or super-Z factories, and motivates further studies of radiative decays of $\Lambda^*$, $\Sigma^*$, and $\Xi^*$ at BESIII.
