# Featuring the structure functions geometry

> We consider geometrical properties of the polarized and unpolarized structure functions and provide definition for the $b$--dependent structure functions. It is shown that unitarity does not allow factorized form of the structure functions over the $x$ and $b$ variables. We conclude that the spin of constituent quark has a significant orbital angular momentum component.

## Key claim

The paper argues that unitarity forbids a factorized form of structure functions over the Bjorken \(x\) and impact parameter \(b\) variables at small \(x\), and that the spin of the constituent quark contains a significant orbital angular momentum component, which is nonperturbative in origin and not due to perturbative QCD evolution.

## Method

The authors define \(b\)-dependent structure functions \(q(x,b)\), \(\Delta q(x,b)\), and \(\delta q(x,b)\) via the Fourier-Bessel transforms of helicity amplitudes for elastic quark-hadron scattering, using the aligned-jet mechanism. They impose unitarity through the \(U\)-matrix representation for the helicity amplitudes in impact-parameter space, and derive explicit expressions for these functions at small \(x\) using a model where the hadron consists of constituent quarks embedded in a nonperturbative vacuum.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of strange quark distribution radius to light quark distribution radius is given by \(R_s(x)/R_q(x) \simeq (1 + \Delta m / (4 m_Q))^{-1}\), where \(\Delta m = m_S - m_Q\) is the difference between strange and light constituent quark masses.
- The radius of the quark matter distribution is \(R_q(x) \simeq (1/M) \ln(Q^2 / x)\), where \(M\) is the total mass of the \(n+1\) quarks in the quark-hadron system.
- The transverse spin distribution \(\delta q(x,b)\) is suppressed relative to the longitudinal spin distribution \(\Delta q(x,b)\) by factors \(\sqrt{x}\) and \(\exp(-\gamma b)\), as shown in the relation \(\delta q(x,b) = c(Q^2) (\sqrt{x}/Q) \exp(-\gamma b) \Delta q(x,b)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model (Troshin and Tyurin, 1999) of elastic quark-hadron scattering to the geometry of structure functions, and contrasts with perturbative QCD approaches that assume factorization of \(x\) and \(b\) dependencies. It provides a nonperturbative framework for interpreting spatial distributions of partons and spin in deep-inelastic scattering, and suggests that experimental signatures of peripheral spin structure functions could be observed at Jefferson Lab (Burkert, 1999) and in exclusive electroproduction.
