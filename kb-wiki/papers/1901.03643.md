# Efimov universality with Coulomb interaction

> The universal properties of charged particles are modified by the presence of a long-range Coulomb interaction. We investigate the modification of Efimov universality as a function of the Coulomb strength using the Gaussian expansion method. The resonant short-range interaction is described by Gaussian potentials to which a Coulomb potential is added. We calculate binding energies and root mean square radii for the three- and four-body systems of charged particles and present our results in a generalised Efimov plot. We find that universal features can still be discerned for weak Coulomb interaction, but break down for strong Coulomb interaction. The root-mean-square radius plateaus at increasingly smaller values for strong Coulomb interaction and the probablity distributions of the states become more concentrated inside the Coulomb barrier. As an example, we apply our universal model to nuclei with an alpha-cluster substructure. Our results point to strong non-universal contributions in that sector.

## Key claim

Efimov universality and discrete scale invariance are progressively broken as the Coulomb interaction strength increases, with universal features still discernible for weak Coulomb forces but breaking down for strong Coulomb forces; the authors find that the scaling factor between consecutive trimer states changes from approximately 23 in the no-Coulomb case to 32-55 for weak Coulomb interaction, and becomes ill-defined for stronger Coulomb interaction.

## Method

The authors used the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) to solve the Schrodinger equation for systems of N identical charged bosons with a Hamiltonian consisting of a Gaussian short-range potential plus a long-range Coulomb potential. They calculated binding energies and root mean square radii for two-, three-, and four-body systems as a function of the Coulomb strength parameter \(\tilde c\), presenting results in generalized Efimov plots using the Coulomb-modified scattering length \(a_C\) as the relevant two-body observable.

## Key numbers

- The scaling factor between consecutive trimer states changes from \(18 \pm 1\) (at three-body threshold) and \(23 \pm 1\) (at unitarity) for no Coulomb interaction to \(55 \pm 5\) and \(32 \pm 3\) respectively for weak Coulomb interaction with \(\tilde c = 0.007\)
- For the ratio \(a^-\sqrt{mB_3}/\hbar\), the universal value is \(-1.5077(1)\); without Coulomb interaction the authors obtain \(2.1(1)\) for the trimer ground state and \(1.6(1)\) for the excited state, while for \(\tilde c = 0.007\) they obtain \(2.3(1)\) for the ground state and \(4(1)\) for the excited trimer
- The scaling factor between ground state tetramer and trimer changes from \(2.3 \pm 0.1\) (threshold) and \(2.4 \pm 0.1\) (unitarity) without Coulomb to \(2.2 \pm 0.1\) and \(2.3 \pm 0.1\) for \(\tilde c = 0.007\), then to \(2 \pm 0.1\) and \(2.1 \pm 0.1\) for \(\tilde c = 0.07\), and finally to \(1.7 \pm 0.1\) for \(\tilde c = 0.7\)
- The ratio \(B_4^0/B_3\) without Coulomb interaction is \(5.8 \pm 0.5\), compared to the universal value of \(4.610(1)\)

## Context

This work extends the study of Efimov physics, originally formulated for neutral particles by Efimov (1970), to systems with long-range Coulomb interactions. It fills a gap between previous calculations on three-body halo nuclei with Coulomb interaction by Fedorov (1994) and the effective field theory approach of Konig et al. (2016) which treated Coulomb forces as subleading in an expansion around the unitary limit. The results enable systematic understanding of when universal Efimov features survive in charged systems such as alpha-cluster nuclei, though the authors find strong non-universal contributions in that sector.
