# η- and η'-mesic nuclei and U_A(1) anomaly at finite density

> We discuss theoretically the possibility of observing the bound states of the ηand η'(958) mesons in nuclei. We apply the NJL model to study the ηand η' meson properties at finite density and calculate the formation cross sections of the ηand η' bound states with the Green function method for (γ,p) reaction. We also discuss the experimental feasibility at photon facilities like SPring-8. The contributions due to the ωmeson production are also included to obtain the realistic (γ,p) spectra. We conclude that we can expect to observe resonance peaks in (γ,p) spectra for the formation of meson bound states and we can deduce new information on ηand η' properties at finite density. These observations are believed to be essential to know the possible mass shift of η' and deduce new information on the effective restoration of the U_A(1) anomaly in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The authors predict that resonance peaks from η- and η'-mesic nuclei should be observable in (γ,p) missing-mass spectra at facilities like SPring-8, but the sign and strength of the η'-nucleus optical potential depend strongly on the density dependence of the U_A(1) anomaly strength g_D(ρ), meaning that observation could distinguish between different scenarios for anomaly restoration in medium.

## Method

The authors used the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) determinant interaction to calculate quark condensates and meson masses (π, η, η') in SU(2) symmetric nuclear matter as functions of nucleon density. They then translated the calculated in-medium mass shifts into real parts of the η- and η'-nucleus optical potentials via a local density approximation, and computed formation cross sections for the ¹²C(γ,p) reaction using the Green function method, including contributions from ω meson production and empirical imaginary potentials.

## Key numbers

- The NJL parameter set yields vacuum masses: m_π = 135.0 MeV, m_η = 514.8 MeV (about 6% smaller than the observed 547.75 MeV), m_η' = 957.7 MeV, and f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- Normal nuclear density is taken as ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.
- The elementary cross section for both η' and η production in the (γ,p) reaction is taken as 150 nb/sr at the laboratory frame.
- The incident photon energy chosen for the calculation is E_γ = 2.7 GeV, accessible at SPring-8.
- The imaginary part of the η'-nucleus optical potential is estimated to lie in the range -5 MeV to -20 MeV at ρ₀, based on analysis of γp → η'p data.
- For the η meson, the imaginary potential strength is taken as W(r) = -40 (ρ(r)/ρ₀) MeV, assuming N*(1535) dominance.
- In the SU(2) symmetric matter with constant g_D (case a), the η' mass decreases rapidly with density while π and η masses gradually increase; in case (c) with density-dependent g_D, both η and η' masses decrease up to around ρ = ρ₀.
- Differences between SU(2) and SU(3) symmetric matter results are less than 10% at normal nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of η-mesic nuclei by Haider and Liu and by Chiang, Oset and Liu, and the authors' own previous work (2005) where in-medium η' properties were estimated more roughly. It also builds on the deeply bound pionic atom experiments by K. Suzuki et al. (2004) that quantitatively determined the in-medium pion decay constant f_π. The calculations are compared with results from the Quark-Meson Coupling model (2006), which predicted mass reduction for both η and η' at normal nuclear density, in qualitative agreement with the authors' case (c) but with significantly different magnitudes. The work aims to enable experimental determination of the η' mass shift in medium, which would provide new information on the effective restoration of the U_A(1) anomaly in nuclear matter.
