# Medium Modifications of Hadron Properties and Partonic Processes

> Chiral symmetry is one of the most fundamental symmetries in QCD. It is closely connected to hadron properties in the nuclear medium via the reduction of the quark condensate <bar{q}q>, manifesting the partial restoration of chiral symmetry. To better understand this important issue, a number of Jefferson Lab experiments over the past decade have focused on understanding properties of mesons and nucleons in the nuclear medium, often benefiting from the high polarization and luminosity of the CEBAF accelerator. In particular, a novel, accurate, polarization transfer measurement technique revealed for the first time a strong indication that the bound proton electromagnetic form factors in 4He may be modified compared to those in the vacuum. Second, the photoproduction of vector mesons on various nuclei has been measured via their decay to e+e- to study possible in-medium effects on the properties of the rho meson. In this experiment, no significant mass shift and some broadening consistent with expected collisional broadening for the rho meson has been observed, providing tight constraints on model calculations. Finally, processes involving in-medium parton propagation have been studied. The medium modifications of the quark fragmentation functions have been extracted with much higher statistical accuracy than previously possible.

## Key claim

The paper reports that polarization transfer measurements in quasi-elastic electron scattering on 4He provide a strong indication that bound proton electromagnetic form factors may be modified compared to free-space values, but this interpretation remains model-dependent because alternative calculations using free form factors with strong charge-exchange final-state interactions can also describe the data.

## Method

The authors review three classes of Jefferson Lab experiments: (1) polarization transfer measurements in the A(e,e'p) reaction on 2H and 4He to extract the double ratio R = (P'_x/P'_z)_A / (P'_x/P'_z)_1H, which is sensitive to the proton electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio; (2) photoproduction of vector mesons on various nuclei via their decay to e+e- to study in-medium modifications of the rho meson spectral function; and (3) semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering on nuclei to extract medium-modified quark fragmentation functions and study parton energy loss.

## Key numbers

* Polarization transfer double ratio R for 4He at Q2 = 0.8 and 1.3 (GeV/c)2 from experiment E03-104 shows a suppression of approximately 6% relative to RDWIA calculations using free form factors.
* The Madrid RDWIA calculation overestimates the 4He polarization transfer data by about 6% when using free form factors; including density-dependent medium-modified form factors from the QMC or CQS models brings the calculation into good agreement.
* For the iron nucleus, the nucleon root-mean-square radius cannot vary by more than 3% from y-scaling analysis, with the corresponding limit on G_E being nearer 10%.
* The Coulomb sum rule analysis of medium-weight and heavy nuclei (40Ca, 48Ca, 56Fe, 197Au, 208Pb, 238U) shows a quenching corresponding to a relative change of the proton charge radius of 13 ± 4%.
* The QMC model predicts a 5.5% increase in G^p_E at nuclear saturation density ρ0, and less than 0.8% increase in the root-mean-square radius of the magnetic form factors.
* Experiment E03-103 measured inclusive electron scattering from 1H, 2H, 3He, 4He, Be, C, Al, Cu, and Au over 0.3 < x < 1.0 up to Q2 ≈ 8.0 GeV2, finding that the nuclear dependence of the cross section is comparable for 4He, 9Be, and 12C but clearly smaller for 3He.
* The Jefferson Lab Coulomb sum rule experiment E05-110 covered momentum transfers 0.55 GeV/c ≤ q ≤ 0.9 GeV/c on 4He, 12C, 56Fe, and 208Pb.

## Context

This work reviews a decade of Jefferson Lab experiments that extend earlier studies of the EMC effect (European Muon Collaboration, 1983) and in-medium hadron properties. The polarization transfer measurements on 4He build on earlier work at MAMI (Dieterich, 2001) and Jefferson Lab experiment E93-049 (Strauch, 2003), and are compared to calculations by the Madrid group (Udias, 1998; 2000) using the RDWIA framework and by Schiavilla (2005) including meson exchange currents. The vector meson photoproduction results (experiment E01-112) provide tight constraints on model calculations of in-medium rho meson spectral functions. The EMC-type measurements in experiment E03-103 extend the work of Gomez (1994) at SLAC to few-body nuclei and larger x, suggesting that the nuclear dependence of quark distributions may depend on the local nuclear environment rather than average nuclear density. The Coulomb sum rule experiment E05-110 will provide precision data to resolve the controversy from earlier work by Morgenstern (2001) on medium-weight and heavy nuclei.
