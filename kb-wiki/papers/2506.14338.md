# Phenomenological refinement of $p$-$d$ elastic scattering descriptions towards the 3NF study in nuclei via the ($p,pd$) reaction

> The ($p,pd$) reaction is expected to be a powerful tool for probing three-nucleon forces (3NFs) in nuclear medium since it can be essentially regarded as the $p$-$d$ elastic scattering inside nuclei. One of the important points in the theoretical description of the ($p,pd$) reaction is to calculate the $p$-$d$ scattering in a nucleus quantitatively using effective interactions. This work aims to develop a phenomenological approach to improve the quantitativity of the $p$-$d$ scattering cross section in free space calculated with effective interactions. The $p$-$d$ elastic amplitude is decomposed into a 2N part, described using 2N effective interactions, and a residual part, which the 2N part cannot describe. The latter is approximated by a superposition of Legendre polynomials, with coefficients treated as adjustable parameters. These parameters are determined to reproduce experimental $p$-$d$ differential cross-section data at various incident energies. The obtained parameters exhibit smooth energy dependence, which is approximated by quadratic functions. The numerical results with the analytic energy dependence also reproduce the experimental data. The developed approach works well for improving the $p$-$d$ scattering cross section in a wide range of incident energies. This work can be regarded as the first step toward the description of ($p,pd$) reactions taking 3NF effect in nuclear medium into account.

## Key claim

The paper develops a phenomenological correction to the $p$-$d$ elastic scattering amplitude calculated with two-nucleon (2N) effective interactions, where the residual amplitude is parameterized by Legendre polynomials with coefficients that exhibit smooth energy dependence approximated by quadratic functions; this approach is presented as a necessary first step toward studying three-nucleon forces (3NFs) in nuclei via the ($p,pd$) reaction, but the residual amplitude may include contributions beyond 3NFs and is only determined relative to a specific 2N interaction.

## Method

The $p$-$d$ elastic scattering amplitude was decomposed into a 2N part calculated using the Melbourne-group parameterization of the Bonn-B potential and a residual part approximated by a superposition of Legendre polynomials up to $l=1$. The complex coefficients $C_0$ and $C_1$ of the Legendre expansion were determined by fitting to experimental differential cross-section data at eight incident energies (108, 120, 135, 150, 155, 170, 190, and 250 MeV) using the Marquardt method with $\chi^2$ minimization, and their energy dependence was then approximated by quadratic functions.

## Key numbers

* At 108 MeV: Re[$C_0$] = -1.692$\times 10^1$ MeV fm$^3$, Im[$C_0$] = -6.379$\times 10^0$ MeV fm$^3$, Re[$C_1$] = +6.462$\times 10^1$ MeV fm$^3$, Im[$C_1$] = -7.481$\times 10^0$ MeV fm$^3$, with $C_\text{corr} = 0.792$
* At 250 MeV: Re[$C_0$] = -9.392$\times 10^0$ MeV fm$^3$, Im[$C_0$] = +5.787$\times 10^0$ MeV fm$^3$, Re[$C_1$] = +2.024$\times 10^1$ MeV fm$^3$, Im[$C_1$] = -1.452$\times 10^1$ MeV fm$^3$, with $C_\text{corr} = 0.711$
* The quadratic approximation coefficients for $C_0$: Re[$\alpha_0$] = +3.610$\times 10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-1}$ fm$^3$, Im[$\alpha_0$] = -1.862$\times 10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-1}$ fm$^3$
* The quadratic approximation coefficients for $C_1$: Re[$\alpha_1$] = +4.307$\times 10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-1}$ fm$^3$, Im[$\alpha_1$] = +1.012$\times 10^{-3}$ MeV$^{-1}$ fm$^3$
* The uncertainty from the parameter search is approximately 2%, comparable to the line width
* Experimental statistical errors are less than 1% and systematic errors are about 5%

## Context

This work extends the CDCCIA reaction model for ($p,pd$) reactions developed by Chazono et al. (2022), which used only 2N effective interactions (Franey-Love) and underestimated experimental cross sections around minima, a deficiency also reported in Faddeev calculations including only 2N forces by Witala et al. (1998, 2022). The approach enables future replacement of the free-space 2N effective interaction with a density-dependent interaction incorporating 3NF effects in finite nuclei, with the residual amplitude $\tilde{t}_\text{res}$ retained as the zero-density limit contribution.
