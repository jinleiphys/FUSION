# Heavy Ion Initial Conditions and Correlations Between Higher Moments in the Spatial Anisotropy

> Fluctuations in the initial conditions for relativistic heavy ion collisions are proving to be crucial to understanding final state flow and jet quenching observables. The initial geometry has been parametrized in terms of moments in the spatial anisotropy (i.e. $ε_2, ε_3, ε_4, ε_5...$), and it has been stated in multiple published articles that the vector directions of odd moments are uncorrelated with the even moments and the reaction place angle. In this article, we demonstrate that this is incorrect and that a substantial correlation exists between the even and odd moments in peripheral Au+Au collisions. These correlations persist for all centralities, though at a very small level for the 0-55% most central collisions.

# Heavy Ion Initial Conditions and Correlations Between Higher Moments in the Spatial Anisotropy

## Key claim

Contrary to previous claims that odd and even spatial anisotropy moments are uncorrelated in relativistic heavy ion collisions, the authors demonstrate that a substantial non-zero correlation exists between even and odd moments in peripheral Au+Au collisions within the Monte Carlo Glauber framework. These correlations persist for all centralities, though at a very small level for the 0-55% most central collisions.

## Method

The authors used the standard PHOBOS Monte Carlo Glauber code with Woods-Saxon parameters (R0 = 6.38 fm, a = 0.535 fm, d_min = 0 fm) to calculate the spatial anisotropy moments ε2, ε3, ε4, ε5 and their angular orientations for Au+Au, Cu+Cu, and Si+Si collisions. They quantified correlations by computing the root-mean-square (RMS) of the angular difference between moment directions as a function of number of participant nucleons (Npart), comparing against the flat distribution expected for uncorrelated quantities.

## Key numbers

- For uncorrelated moments, the RMS of the angular separation between ε2 and ε3 is 0.302 radians (the flat distribution expectation)
- For peripheral Au+Au collisions with Npart ≈ 10, the RMS angular difference between ε2 and ε3 shows a large downward deviation from 0.302 radians, indicating strong correlation
- For Npart ≈ 80-150, a very small anti-correlation persists, corresponding to approximately 1% lower probability of having the two moments within 0.0-π/12 and a correspondingly higher probability of separation by π/12-π/6
- A similar magnitude positive correlation exists for Npart > 200
- The correlation between ε3 and the reaction plane is much weaker than the ε2-ε3 correlation

## Context

This work contradicts the claims of Alver (2010), Agakishiev (2010), and Lacey (2010), who stated that odd and even harmonic angles are uncorrelated and that the orientation of triangular overlap shape is random relative to the event-plane direction. The authors also test and contradict the statement by Petersen (2010) that fluctuations are random with respect to the reaction plane. The findings have implications for experimental determination of momentum anisotropy moments vn and two-particle correlation measurements, and suggest that direct integration of Monte Carlo calculations of initial state geometries may be warranted to account for the full set of correlations.
