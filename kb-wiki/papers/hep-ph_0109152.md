# Determination of Parton Distribution Functions in Nuclei

> Nuclear parton distribution functions are obtained by a chi^2 analysis of lepton deep inelastic experimental data. It is possible to determine valence-quark distributions at medium x and antiquark distributions at small x; however, the distributions in other x regions and gluon distributions cannot be fixed. We need a variety of experimental data and also further analysis refinements.

## Key claim
Nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs) can be determined by a chi-squared analysis of lepton deep inelastic scattering data, but only valence-quark distributions at medium x and antiquark distributions at small x are well constrained; distributions in other x regions and gluon distributions cannot be fixed, and further analysis refinements and a wider variety of experimental data are needed.

## Method
The author performed a leading-order chi-squared analysis of electron and muon deep inelastic scattering data for 14 nuclei from helium to lead, parametrizing nuclear PDFs as weight functions multiplied by the MRST-LO nucleon PDFs at a starting scale Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2. The weight functions were expressed as a cubic polynomial in x with a Fermi-motion factor, using a simple A^(1/3) dependence, and the DGLAP evolution equations were used to evolve the distributions to experimental Q^2 values for comparison with the measured structure function ratios R_F2 = F2^A / F2^D.

## Key numbers
- The total chi-squared for 309 data points was 583.7 for the quadratic parametrization and 546.6 for the cubic parametrization.
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom was 1.93 (quadratic) and 1.82 (cubic).
- The starting scale for the nuclear PDFs was Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- Nuclear modifications to PDFs are typically of the order of 20%.
- The analysis covered nuclei from A=4 (helium) to A=208 (lead).

## Context
This work extends the first chi-squared analysis of nuclear PDFs reported in Reference 2, building on earlier investigations of nuclear parton distributions (Reference 1). The analysis is still at a preliminary stage compared to well-established nucleon PDF determinations, and the author notes that the results enable future improvements by including Drell-Yan data to constrain antiquark distributions at x ~ 0.1 and by incorporating data sensitive to gluon distributions, such as J/psi production. The obtained distributions and computer codes are made available for use in high-energy nuclear cross-section calculations, particularly for heavy-ion reactions searching for quark-gluon plasma signatures.
