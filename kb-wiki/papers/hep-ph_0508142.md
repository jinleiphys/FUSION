# Nature of the light scalar mesons

> Despite the apparent simplicity of meson spectroscopy, light scalar mesons cannot be accommodated in the usual $q\bar q$ structure. We study the description of the scalar mesons below 2 GeV in terms of the mixing of a chiral nonet of tetraquarks with conventional $q\bar q$ states. A strong diquark-antidiquark component is found for several states. The consideration of a glueball as dictated by quenched lattice QCD drives a coherent picture of the isoscalar mesons.

## Key claim

Light scalar mesons below 2 GeV can be described by mixing a chiral nonet of tetraquarks with conventional quark-antiquark states, and including a scalar glueball as predicted by quenched lattice QCD; however, the authors note that the final answer can only come from precise experimental data that discriminate between different theoretical models, and that the data set is so large and sometimes so poor that one can always find a positive or negative interpretation of some results.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with chiral symmetry breaking, one-gluon-exchange potential, and a screened confining potential, solving the two-body Schrodinger equation via the Numerov algorithm and the four-body problem via a variational method with Gaussian trial wave functions. They then introduced a perturbative mixing between two-quark and four-quark configurations, parametrized by constants C_n and C_s tuned to reproduce the f_0(980) mass, and finally included a scalar glueball with mass and mixing parameters taken from lattice QCD predictions.

## Key numbers

- The mixing parameters used are C_n = 165 MeV and C_s = 70 MeV, chosen to drive the f_0(980) state to its physical mass.
- The bare 1P q-qbar state mass (representing the a_0(980) as a two-quark state) is 1079 MeV.
- The bare n-nbar state mass is 1503 MeV, the bare s-sbar state mass is 1850 MeV, and the scalar glueball mass is taken as M_G = 1643 MeV.
- The glueball-quarkonium mixing matrix element is taken as <G|V|s-sbar> = 64 MeV with r = 1.2 (where r=1 is exact SU(3) flavor blindness).
- The predicted two-photon decay widths are: Gamma_gamma[a_0(980)] = 0.65 +/- 0.04 keV and Gamma_gamma[f_0(980)] = 0.23 +/- 0.02 keV, compared to experimental values of 0.3 +/- 0.1 keV and 0.39 (+0.10/-0.13) keV respectively.
- The four-quark state X(1600) with I^G(J^PC)=2^+(2^{++}) is calculated at 1500 MeV, consistent with the experimental mass of 1600 +/- 100 MeV.
- The glueball component is shared as 20% for f_0(1370), 2% for f_0(1500), and 76% for f_0(1710).
- The f_0(1500) four-quark component is 72% (ns-nbar-sbar), decomposed as 0.723 |MM> + 0.691 |QQ>, with 57% diquark-antidiquark structure.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model of Vijande, Fernandez, and Valcarce (2005), which successfully described all meson spectra except the scalar sector, by adding tetraquark and glueball components. It contradicts the interpretation of Close and Tornqvist (2002) that the a_0(1450) is the scalar member of the 1^3P_J multiplet, and differs from the conclusions of Amsler (2002) who found the f_0(1710) to be dominantly s-sbar rather than a glueball. The results enable future experimental tests through two-photon decay width measurements and radiative transitions, which could discriminate between the competing theoretical pictures of the scalar meson sector.
