# Electromagnetic form factors of the Delta baryon

> We develop a methodology that enables us to extract accurately the electromagnetic Delta form factors and their momentum dependence. We test our approach in the quenched approximation as a preparation for a study using dynamical fermions. Our calculation of the four form factors covers pion masses between about 410 MeV and 560 MeV on lattices with a size of 2.9 fm and a lattice spacing 0.09 fm. From the form factors we are able to obtain estimates of the magnetic moment and the charge radius of the Delta, which we compare to existing experimental and theoretical results.Our non-zero result for the electric quadrupole form factor signals a deformation of the Delta, pointing to an oblate charge distribution.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first accurate lattice QCD extraction of the electric quadrupole form factor of the Delta baryon, finding a non-zero negative value that signals an oblate deformation; however, the results are obtained in the quenched approximation as a preparation for a future study using dynamical fermions, and disconnected diagram contributions are neglected.

## Method
The authors used Wilson fermions and the Wilson plaquette gauge action on a 32^3 x 64 lattice with lattice spacing 0.092(3) fm and spatial extent 2.9 fm, in the quenched approximation. They computed two-point and three-point correlation functions with the final Delta state at rest, using sequential inversions through the sink and covariant Gaussian smearing on quark fields, then extracted the four multipole form factors (G_E0, G_E2, G_M1, G_M3) from an over-constrained linear system solved via least-squares with jackknife binning.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses covered: 411(4) MeV, 490(4) MeV, and 563(4) MeV, on a lattice with spacing 0.092(3) fm and spatial extent 2.9 fm.
- Charge radius of the Delta after chiral extrapolation to physical pion mass: 0.691(6) fm.
- Magnetic moment of the Delta^+ at physical pion mass: G_M1(0) = 3.04(21), corresponding to mu_Delta^+ = 2.32(16) mu_N (using the relation mu = G_M1(0) e/(2 m_Delta)).
- Magnetic moment of the Delta^{++} (obtained as 2 mu_Delta^+): 4.64(32) mu_N, compared to the experimental value 6.14(51) mu_N from LopezCastro (2000).
- Electric quadrupole form factor at zero momentum transfer: G_E2(0) = -0.6(3) at m_pi = 563 MeV, -0.7(4) at m_pi = 490 MeV, -0.7(4) at m_pi = 411 MeV.
- Delta masses: 1.470(15) GeV at kappa=0.1554, 1.425(16) GeV at kappa=0.1558, 1.382(15) GeV at kappa=0.1562.

## Context
This work extends the pioneering quenched lattice study of Leinweber (1992) by using finer lattice spacing, larger volume, smaller pion masses, and a fixed-sink approach that allows simultaneous calculation of form factors for all momentum transfers, yielding significantly improved statistical precision for the electric quadrupole form factor. The results confirm the dipole ansatz for G_E0 and G_M1 and provide the first accurate lattice determination of G_E2, enabling future comparisons with chiral perturbation theory (Arndt, 2003) and with ongoing dynamical fermion calculations.
