# Pi N -> Omega N in a Coupled-Channel Approach

> We describe the pi N -> omega N cross section from threshold to a center of mass energy of 2 GeV in a unitary coupled-channel model and analyze it in terms of rescattering and resonance excitations. The amplitude is mainly composed of D13, P13, and P11 contributions, where the D13 dominates over the complete considered energy range. We also outline the generalization of the standard partial-wave formalism necessary for the decomposition of the omega N final state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unitary coupled-channel model can describe the $\pi N \to \omega N$ cross section from threshold to 2 GeV center-of-mass energy, but the abstract glosses over the condition that this description requires the inclusion of rescattering effects and that the amplitude is mainly composed of $D_{13}$, $P_{13}$, and $P_{11}$ partial waves, with $D_{13}$ dominating over the entire energy range.

## Method

The authors extended a unitary coupled-channel $K$-matrix model, previously including $\gamma N$, $\pi N$, $2\pi N$, $\eta N$, and $K\Lambda$ final states, to also incorporate $\omega N$ and $K\Sigma$ channels. They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the $K$-matrix Born approximation, using effective Lagrangians for $s$-, $u$-, and $t$-channel diagrams with cutoff form factors, and performed a partial-wave decomposition generalized for the $\omega N$ final state due to the $\omega$ intrinsic spin. The model included 14 resonances and was fitted simultaneously to all available pion-induced data for $\pi N$, $2\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, $K\Sigma$, and $\omega N$ channels.

## Key numbers

- The overall $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom for the fit is 3.08, based on a total of 2360 data points.
- The $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom for the $\omega N$ channel specifically is 2.53.
- The $NN\omega$ couplings extracted from the fit are $g_1 = 4.50$ and $\kappa = g_2/g_1 = -0.70$.
- The nucleon cutoff value used for all $s$- and $u$-channel diagram vertices is $\Lambda_N = 1.15$ GeV.
- The $\rho$ exchange couplings used are $g_{\omega\rho\pi} = 2.056$, $g_{NN\rho} = 5.56$, and $\kappa_{NN\rho} = 1.58$.
- The $D_{13}(1950)$ resonance has a total width of 948 MeV and an $\omega N$ partial width of 226 MeV.
- The $P_{11}(1710)$ resonance has a total width of 686 MeV and an $\omega N$ partial width of 145 MeV.
- The $P_{13}(1720)$ resonance has a total width of 252 MeV and an $\omega N$ partial width of 0.67 MeV.
- The $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance has a total width of 585 MeV and an $\omega N$ partial width of 123 MeV.
- The $S_{11}(1650)$ resonance has a total width of 177 MeV and a coupling $g_1 = -0.224$ (no $\omega N$ width given).

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel model of Penner and Mosel (2001) to higher center-of-mass energies up to 2 GeV, incorporating the $\omega N$ final state which previous models such as Klingl (1997), Post (2000), Friman (1999), and Titov (1999) could not describe consistently. The analysis contradicts the claims of Hanhart (1999, 2001) regarding the correction of Karami (1979) data and the dominance of $J = 1/2$ contributions, instead finding that $D_{13}$, $P_{13}$, and $P_{11}$ partial waves dominate. The results enable future inclusion of photoproduction data to further constrain resonance widths and masses, and provide a formalism for adding additional final states such as $\rho N$ or $\pi\Delta$ for a more sophisticated description of the $2\pi N$ channel.
