# QCD in the cores of neutron stars

> I discuss why state-of-the art perturbative QCD calculations of the equation of state at large chemical potential that are reliable at asymptotically high densities constrain the same equation of state at neutron-star densities. I describe how these theoretical calculations affect the EOS at lower density. I argue that the ab-initio calculations in QCD offer significant information about the equation of state of the neutron-star matter, which is complementary to the current astrophysical observations.

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD calculations at asymptotically high densities (above ~40 nuclear saturation densities) can propagate non-trivial constraints on the equation of state all the way down to 2.2 nuclear saturation densities using only thermodynamic stability, consistency, and causality, without any interpolation assumptions.

## Method

The author connects the low-density chiral effective field theory (CET) endpoint with the high-density perturbative QCD (pQCD) starting point by constructing all possible curves in the baryon density-chemical potential plane that satisfy three conditions: thermodynamic stability (monotonically increasing density with chemical potential), causality (sound speed ≤ speed of light), and thermodynamic consistency (fixed area under the curve matching the pressure difference between endpoints). The resulting allowed region is then mapped from the density-chemical potential plane to the energy density-pressure plane to obtain constraints at neutron-star densities.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n_s = 0.16/fm^3
- pQCD calculations are reliable above ~40 n_s
- Central densities of maximally massive neutron stars are around 4-8 n_s
- pQCD input propagates constraints down to 2.2 n_s
- At 5 n_s, pQCD input excludes 75% of the otherwise allowed area in the energy density-pressure plane
- Softening of the EOS occurs around energy density ε ~750 MeV/fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the approach of Annala (2019) who first identified softening of the EOS at neutron-star densities as evidence for quark matter cores. The author builds on prior work by Komoltsev (2021) and Gorda (2022) to show that pQCD input is complementary to astrophysical observations from massive neutron star discoveries (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013, Fonseca 2021), mass-radius measurements (Miller 2021, Riley 2021), and gravitational-wave astronomy (LIGO/Virgo 2017). The results enable Bayesian inference studies that combine CET calculations, pQCD constraints, and astrophysical observations to determine the equation of state, with an easy-to-use Python script provided for consistency checks.
