# In-medium loop corrections and longitudinally polarized gauge bosons in high-energy showers

> The splitting processes of bremsstrahlung and pair production in a medium are coherent over large distances in the very high energy limit, which leads to a suppression known as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. We continue study of the case when the coherence lengths of two consecutive splitting processes overlap (which is important for understanding corrections to standard treatments of the LPM effect in QCD), avoiding soft-emission approximations. In this particular paper, we show (i) how the "instantaneous" interactions of Light-Cone Perturbation Theory must be included in the calculation to account for effects of longitudinally-polarized gauge bosons in intermediate states, and (ii) how to compute virtual corrections to LPM emission rates, which will be necessary in order to make infrared-safe calculations of the characteristics of in-medium QCD showering of high-energy partons. In order to develop these topics in as simple a context as possible, we will focus in the current paper not on QCD but on large-$N_f$ QED, where $N_f$ is the number of electron flavors.

## Key claim

The paper shows that to correctly compute overlapping-formation-time corrections to the LPM effect in high-energy showers, one must include instantaneous interactions from Light-Cone Perturbation Theory to account for longitudinally polarized gauge bosons in intermediate states, and one must compute virtual corrections to LPM emission rates to obtain infrared-safe results. The authors develop these methods in large-\(N_f\) QED as a warm-up theory, with the explicit hedge that the QCD generalization is left for future work.

## Method

The authors use Light-Cone Perturbation Theory (LCPT) in light-cone gauge \(A^+ = 0\) to integrate out longitudinal polarizations of intermediate gauge bosons, adding an effective 4-point instantaneous interaction to the amplitude. They then compute time-ordered interference diagrams for the real double-splitting process \(e \to e \bar e e\) and for virtual corrections to \(e \to \gamma e\) and \(\gamma \to e \bar e\), interpreting each interference diagram as simultaneous evolution of particles in the amplitude and conjugate amplitude. The medium is treated as large, static, and uniform in the multiple-scattering approximation characterized by the transport parameter \(\hat q\), and the problem is reduced to an effective \((N-2)\)-particle non-relativistic Schrodinger equation in the transverse plane.

## Key numbers

* The parametric hierarchy for large-\(N_f\) QED is \(\alpha \ll N_f \alpha \ll 1\), where \(N_f\) is the number of electron flavors.
* The mean free path between roughly-democratic splittings scales as \(l_{\rm rad} \sim l_{\rm form} / \alpha\).
* For the soft intermediate photon limit \(x_\gamma \ll 1\), the leading-log formula for the overlapping-formation-time correction to \(e \to e \bar e e\) is \(\Delta \, d\Gamma/dx_\gamma\,d\yfrake \approx (N_f \alpha^2 [\yfrake^2+(1-\yfrake)^2] / [2\pi^2 [\yfrake(1-\yfrake)]^{1/2} x_\gamma^{3/2}]) \ln(1/[x_\gamma [\yfrake(1-\yfrake)]^{1/2}]) \sqrt{\hat q / E}\).
* The NLO result for single splitting \(e \to \gamma e\) contains logarithmic terms of the form \([d\Gamma/dx]_{e\to\gamma e} = [d\Gamma/dx]_{e\to\gamma e}^{\rm LO} [1 + \beta_0 \alpha \ln(|E\Omega|^{1/2}/\mu) + \cdots]\), where \(\mu\) is the renormalization scale and \(\beta_0\) is the first coefficient of the QED beta-function.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of overlapping formation times for double gluon bremsstrahlung \(g \to ggg\) in QCD (Arnold, Chang, and Iqbal, 2009-2016), which had omitted longitudinal polarizations and virtual corrections. The methods are related to recent work by Beuf (2016-2017) and Hanninen, Lappi, and Paatelainen (2017-2018) on NLO deep inelastic scattering, but applied to a thick medium rather than an extremely thin one. The results enable future calculations of infrared-safe characteristics of in-medium QCD showering and energy loss, with the QCD calculation left for later work.
