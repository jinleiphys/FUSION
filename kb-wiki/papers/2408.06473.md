# Chemical freeze-out curve in heavy-ion collisions and the QCD critical point

> The chemical freeze-out curve in heavy-ion collisions is investigated in the context of a quantum chromodynamics (QCD) critical point (CP) search at finite baryon densities. Taking the hadron resonance gas picture at face value, chemical freeze-out points at a given baryochemical potential provide a lower bound on the possible temperature of the QCD CP. We first verify that the freeze-out data in heavy-ion collisions are well described by a constant energy per particle curve, $E/N = \rm const$, under strangeness neutrality conditions ($μ_S \neq 0$, $μ_Q \neq 0$). We then evaluate the hypothetical lower bound on the freeze-out curve based on this criterion in the absence of strangeness neutrality ($μ_S = 0$, $μ_Q = 0$) and confront it with recent predictions on the CP location. We find that recent estimates based on Yang-Lee edge singularities from lattice QCD data on coarse lattices ($N_τ = 6$) place the CP significantly below the freeze-out curve, hinting at the importance of performing continuum extrapolation within this method. Predictions based on functional methods and holography place the CP slightly above the freeze-out curve, indicating that the QCD CP may be located very close to the chemical freeze-out in $A$+$A$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$-$6$ GeV.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out curve in heavy-ion collisions, when evaluated under the same conditions of vanishing charge and strangeness chemical potentials ($\mu_Q = \mu_S = 0$) used in theoretical QCD critical point predictions, provides a lower temperature bound that is systematically lower than the freeze-out curve under actual heavy-ion collision conditions; this shift brings several recent critical point estimates (from functional methods and holography) slightly above the freeze-out curve, suggesting the QCD critical point may be located very close to chemical freeze-out in collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$-$5$ GeV.

## Method

The authors used the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model in its ideal, excluded volume, and quantum van der Waals versions to compute chemical freeze-out curves defined by a constant energy per particle criterion, $E/N = 0.951$ GeV. They first verified that this constant $E/N$ line, computed under strangeness neutrality ($n_S = 0$) and charge-to-baryon ratio $Q/B = 0.4$, describes existing freeze-out data from multiple experiments across $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.4$-$5020$ GeV, including a new fit to STAR data at 3 GeV. They then recomputed the same $E/N$ line under the condition $\mu_Q = \mu_S = 0$ and compared it with published QCD critical point predictions from lattice Yang-Lee edge analyses, Dyson-Schwinger equations, functional renormalization group, and holography.

## Key numbers

* The constant energy per particle value used to define the freeze-out curve is $E/N = 0.951$ GeV, chosen to give $T_{\rm ch} = 160$ MeV at $\mu_B = 0$ in the ideal HRG model.
* The freeze-out curve under $\mu_Q = \mu_S = 0$ lies systematically below the curve under heavy-ion conditions ($n_S = 0$, $Q/B = 0.4$) at finite $\mu_B$, with the shift being significant for comparisons with critical point estimates.
* The collision energy range identified as most promising for critical point search is $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$-$5$ GeV, based on the proximity of critical point estimates to the $\mu_Q = \mu_S = 0$ freeze-out curve.
* The parametrization $\mu_B \cong c/(1 + d\sqrt{s_{NN}})$ uses $c = 1.477$ GeV and $d = 0.343$ GeV$^{-1}$ to relate baryochemical potential to collision energy.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing chemical freeze-out curve analysis of Cleymans (2005) and others by explicitly addressing the mismatch between the conditions under which freeze-out is measured in heavy-ion collisions (strangeness neutrality, fixed charge-to-baryon ratio) and the conditions ($\mu_Q = \mu_S = 0$) used in theoretical QCD critical point predictions from Fu (2019), Gunkel (2021), Hippert (2023), Basar (2023), and Clarke (2024). The finding that the freeze-out curve disfavors the Yang-Lee edge singularity estimate from the Parma-Bielefeld group (Clarke 2024) based on $N_\tau = 6$ lattices, while being consistent with functional and holographic predictions, enables experimental searches at the RHIC fixed target program and the future CBM experiment at FAIR to focus on the $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$-$5$ GeV range.
