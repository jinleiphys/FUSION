# Doubly-heavy tetraquark bound states and resonances

> We review the predictions of the quark model for the doubly-heavy tetraquarks $QQ\bar q\bar q$. The possibility of resonances near the $BB^*$ threshold in addition to a deeply bound state is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews quark-model predictions for doubly-heavy tetraquarks \(QQ\bar q\bar q\) and discusses the possibility that, in addition to a deeply bound ground state, there may exist resonances near the \(BB^*\) threshold; however, the existence of a second bound state or resonance is model-dependent and disappears in the more realistic AL1' variant.

## Method

The authors solve the four-body Schrödinger equation for the \(QQ\bar u\bar d\) system using a Gaussian expansion method with a Hamiltonian that includes a central potential (linear or power-law confinement) plus a smeared chromomagnetic spin-spin interaction. They compare three parameter sets: the original AL1 model (linear confinement), the AP1 model (confinement exponent \(p=2/3\)), and a hybrid AL1' model that combines the central part of AL1 with the spin-spin part of AP1. Binding energies are computed relative to the appropriate meson-meson thresholds, and the real-scaling (stabilization) method is discussed for identifying resonances.

## Key numbers

* \(cc\bar u\bar d\) binding energy: 21 MeV (AP1), 13 MeV (AL1), 8 MeV (AL1') below the \(DD^*\) threshold.
* \(bc\bar u\bar d\) binding energy: 37 MeV (AP1), 23 MeV (AL1), 18 MeV (AL1') below threshold.
* \(bb\bar u\bar d\) binding energy: 171 MeV (AP1), 151 MeV (AL1), 133 MeV (AL1') below threshold.
* A second \(bb\bar u\bar d\) state (radial excitation) is bound by 2.9 MeV in AP1 and 0.6 MeV in AL1, but disappears in the AL1' variant.

## Context

This work extends the long line of quark-model studies of doubly-heavy tetraquarks that began with Ader et al. (1981) and includes later calculations by Semay et al. (1994), Janc et al. (2004), and Vijande et al. (2009). It directly compares with the recent AP1 results of Meng et al. (2020) and shows that the existence of a second bound state near threshold is sensitive to the choice of hyperfine interaction. The results enable future experimental searches for the \(T_{bb}\) tetraquark, which is predicted to be deeply bound and to have a significantly longer weak-decay lifetime than ordinary beauty hadrons, as argued by Hernandez et al. (2019).
