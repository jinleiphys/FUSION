# Excited baryons from Bayesian priors and overlap fermions

> Using the constrained-fitting method based on Bayesian priors, we extract the masses of the two lowest states of octet and decuplet baryons with both parities. The calculation is done on quenched 16^3x28 lattices of a=0.2 fm using an improved gauge action and overlap fermions, with the pion mass as low as 180 MeV. The Roper state N(1440)1/2+ is clearly observed for the first time as the 1st-excited state of the nucleon from the standard interpolating field. Together with other baryons, our preliminary results indicate that the level-ordering of the low-lying baryon states on the lattice is largely consistent with experiment. The realization is helped by cross-overs between the excited 1/2+ and 1/2- states in the region of pion mass from 300 to 400 MeV.

## Key claim
Using the constrained-fitting method with Bayesian priors on quenched lattices, the authors report the first lattice observation of the Roper state N(1440)1/2+ as the 1st-excited state of the nucleon from the standard interpolating field, with the caveat that these are preliminary results and further stability checks are underway.

## Method
The calculation was performed on quenched 16^3x28 lattices with lattice spacing a=0.202(1) fm set from f_pi, using an improved gauge action and overlap fermions. The authors analyzed 80 configurations across 26 quark masses, with the lowest pion mass at 180 MeV, and extracted masses of the two lowest states for octet and decuplet baryons with both parities using constrained curve-fitting with Bayesian priors.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: 16^3x28 with a=0.202(1) fm (scale set from f_pi)
- Lowest pion mass achieved: 180 MeV (corresponding to m_pi/m_rho=0.248)
- 80 configurations analyzed across 26 quark masses, with 18 masses below the strange quark mass
- Cross-overs between excited 1/2+ and 1/2- states occur in the region of m_pi ~ 300 to 400 MeV
- Lowest Lm_pi ≈ 3 on the primary lattice

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of excited baryon spectra using various actions (Derek 1995, Lee, Wally 2002, sas 2000, dgr 2000) and addresses the long-standing challenge of reproducing the level ordering of low-lying baryon states, particularly the elusive Roper state. The results support the flavor-color picture from valence QCD (Liu 1999) over the constituent quark model (Isgur 1978, Capstick 1986) and Goldstone-boson-exchange model (Glozman 1996). The authors note that further work is needed to check for finite-volume effects by repeating the calculation on a 12^3x28 lattice.
