# Quark matter nucleation in hot hadronic matter

> We study the quark deconfinement phase transition in hot $β$-stable hadronic matter. Assuming a first order phase transition, we calculate the enthalpy per baryon of the hadron-quark phase transition. We calculate and compare the nucleation rate and the nucleation time due to thermal and quantum nucleation mechanisms. We compute the crossover temperature above which thermal nucleation dominates the finite temperature quantum nucleation mechanism. We next discuss the consequences for the physics of proto-neutron stars. We introduce the concept of limiting conversion temperature and critical mass $M_{cr}$ for proto-hadronic stars, and we show that proto-hadronic stars with a mass $M < M_{cr}$ could survive the early stages of their evolution without decaying to a quark star.

## Key claim

The paper shows that proto-hadronic stars with a mass below a critical value \(M_{cr}\) could survive the early stages of their evolution without decaying to a quark star, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific models used for the hadronic and quark matter equations of state and on the assumed value of the surface tension (\(\sigma = 30\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\)).

## Method

The authors calculated the Gibbs energy per baryon for hadronic and quark phases at finite temperature using the GM1 relativistic mean-field model for hadronic matter and the MIT bag model (bag constant \(B = 85\) MeV/fm\(^3\), strange quark mass \(m_s = 150\) MeV) for quark matter. They computed the quantum nucleation rate via a semi-classical WKB tunneling approach and the thermal nucleation rate using Langer's theory of homogeneous nucleation, including dynamical and statistical prefactors with shear viscosity from Danielewicz (1984). The nucleation times were compared at fixed pressures and temperatures characteristic of proto-neutron star interiors, and the crossover temperature where thermal nucleation dominates was identified.

- Crossover temperature ranges from \(T_{co} = 5.0\) MeV at \(P = 53.98\) MeV/fm\(^3\) to \(T_{co} = 8.3\) MeV at \(P = 58.85\) MeV/fm\(^3\) (Table 2).

- Gravitational critical mass \(M_{cr}\) for proto-hadronic stars: 1.573 \(M_\odot\) at entropy \(\tilde{S}/k_B = 0\), 1.494 \(M_\odot\) at \(\tilde{S}/k_B = 1\), and 1.390 \(M_\odot\) at \(\tilde{S}/k_B = 2\) (Table 3).
- Baryonic critical mass \(M_{B,cr}\): 1.752 \(M_\odot\) at \(\tilde{S}/k_B = 0\), 1.643 \(M_\odot\) at \(\tilde{S}/k_B = 1\), and 1.492 \(M_\odot\) at \(\tilde{S}/k_B = 2\) (Table 3).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quark matter nucleation in cold neutron stars (Bombaci et al., 2004; Drago et al., 2004; Lugones et al., 2005) to the hot, neutrino-trapped conditions of proto-neutron stars. It improves upon earlier thermal nucleation estimates by Horvath (1992) and Olesen (1994), which used typical values rather than a consistent finite-temperature equation of state, and by including a proper dynamical prefactor from Venugopalan and Vischer (1994) instead of a dimensional \(T^4\) estimate. The results enable future studies of whether proto-hadronic stars can survive the first seconds after birth without converting to quark stars, and whether quark deconfinement could trigger a delayed supernova explosion with a peculiar neutrino signal (Sagert et al., 2009).
