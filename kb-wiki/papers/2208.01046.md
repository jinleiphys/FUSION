# Does quark-gluon plasma feature an extended hydrodynamic regime?

> We investigate the response of the near-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma (QGP) to perturbation at non-hydrodynamic gradients. We propose a conceivable scenario under which sound mode continues to dominate the medium response in this regime. Such a scenario has been observed experimentally for various liquids and liquid metals. We further show this extended hydrodynamic regime (EHR) indeed exists for both the weakly-coupled kinetic equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) and the strongly-coupled ${\cal N}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory. We construct a simple but nontrivial extension of M{ü}eller-Isareal-Stewart (MIS) theory, namely MIS*, and demonstrate that it describes EHR response for both RTA and SYM theory. This indicates that MIS* equations can potentially be employed to search for QGP EHR via heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that quark-gluon plasma (QGP) may exhibit an extended hydrodynamic regime (EHR) where sound modes continue to dominate the medium response at non-hydrodynamic gradients, even though conventional viscous hydrodynamics fails to describe their dispersion. This scenario is presented as a conceivable possibility rather than a proven fact, with the hedge that it has been observed experimentally in various liquids and liquid metals but remains to be tested for QGP.

## Method

The authors analyze sound mode dispersion in the linear response regime for two microscopic theories: weakly-coupled kinetic theory under relaxation time approximation (RTA) and strongly-coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory via AdS/CFT correspondence. They construct a modified hydrodynamic model called MIS* that divides the shear stress tensor into two components with different relaxation times, and compare its predictions for sound dispersion and energy-momentum response functions against the microscopic theories for both static and Bjorken-expanding backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- For RTA kinetic theory, the critical wavenumber where sound modes become gapped from non-hydrodynamic excitations is q_c = 1.2 (in units of inverse mean free path), with EHR identified in the range 0.2 < q < 1.2
- For SYM theory, the gap between sound modes and non-hydrodynamic excitations remains open for all q under study, with EHR identified for q > 0.3 (in units of eta/(3sT/4))
- For RTA kinetic theory, MIS* with parameters delta = 0.2-0.3 and gamma = 0.7-0.9 describes sound modes up to q'_c = 0.95
- For SYM theory, MIS* with parameters delta = 0.1-0.2 and gamma = 0.65-0.85 describes sound modes up to q'_c = 0.7
- The relaxation time in RTA scales as tau_R proportional to epsilon^{-1/4}, with nu = 4 tau_R/15 and C_pi = 15/4
- For SYM, nu = beta/(3 pi) and C_pi = 3(2 - log 2)/2

## Context

This work extends the concept of extended hydrodynamic regime from condensed matter systems, where high-wavevector sound modes have been observed in liquids and liquid metals such as liquid Zinc (PhysRevLett.114.187801, 2015) and liquid K-Cs alloys (PhysRevLett.85.5352, 2000), to the quark-gluon plasma context. The authors build on prior studies of hydrodynamic gradient expansion (Grozdanov 2019, Heller 2020) and the Hydro+ framework (Stephanov 2017), while addressing the need for phenomenological models that can describe QGP response at intermediate scales relevant to small colliding systems and jet-medium interactions. The MIS* construction enables future data-model comparisons to test whether QGP exhibits EHR, potentially filling the gap in understanding how QGP emerges from asymptotic parton gas.
