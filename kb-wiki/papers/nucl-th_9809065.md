# Chiral Symmetry and Three-Nucleon Forces

> After a brief review of the role three-nucleon forces play in the few-nucleon systems, the chiral-perturbation-theory approach to these forces is discussed. Construction of the (nominal) leading- and subleading-order Born terms and pion-rescattering graphs contributing to two-pion-exchange three-nucleon forces is reviewed, and comparisons are made of the types of such forces that are used today. It is demonstrated that the short-range $c$-term of the Tucson-Melbourne force is unnatural in terms of power counting and should be dropped. The class of two-pion-exchange three-nucleon forces then becomes rather uniform.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the short-range $c$-term of the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force is "unnatural" according to chiral perturbation theory power counting and should be dropped; once this term is removed, the class of two-pion-exchange three-nucleon forces becomes rather uniform across different models.

## Method

The authors review the construction of two-pion-exchange three-nucleon forces within chiral perturbation theory (CPT) at leading and subleading order, deriving the relevant Lagrangian terms and the resulting $\pi$-$N$ scattering amplitudes. They compare the coefficients $a$, $b$, $c$, $d$ that parameterize the $\pi$-$N$ $T$-matrix for six different three-nucleon force models (Fujita-Miyazawa, Tucson-Melbourne, Brazilian, Urbana-Argonne, Texas, Ruhr(Pot)), and analyze the off-shell behavior of the amplitudes using field redefinitions and dimensional power counting.

## Key numbers

- The pion-decay constant is $f_{\pi} \sim 93$ MeV, and the large-mass QCD scale is $\Lambda \sim 1$ GeV.
- The $c$-term coefficient in the Tucson-Melbourne force is $c = 1.03 / m_{\pi}^3$ (where $m_{\pi}$ is the pion mass).
- The dimensionless coefficient $x$ for the unnatural term is estimated as $x \sim g_A \sigma \Lambda^3 / (2 m_{\pi}^4) \sim 100$, which is vastly larger than the natural value of order 1.
- The $a'$ coefficient (the physically relevant combination $a - 2 m_{\pi}^2 c$) for the Tucson-Melbourne force is $a' = -1.03 / m_{\pi}$.
- The $c_1$ coefficient from $O(Q^3)$ fits ranges from $-0.87$ to $-1.53$ GeV$^{-1}$ across different determinations; $c_3$ ranges from $-5.25$ to $-6.19$ GeV$^{-1}$; $c_4$ ranges from $3.25$ to $4.12$ GeV$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to three-nucleon forces developed by the Texas group (van Kolck, 1994) and compares it with older models including the Tucson-Melbourne force (Coon et al., 1979), the Brazilian force (Robilotta et al., 1985), and the Fujita-Miyazawa force (Fujita and Miyazawa, 1957). The paper resolves an apparent conflict between CPT and current-algebra approaches by showing they differ only by a field redefinition, and it enables future calculations to drop the unnatural $c$-term while retaining the correct chiral constraints.
