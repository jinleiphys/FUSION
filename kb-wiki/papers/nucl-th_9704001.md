# Benchmark Calculations for the Triton Binding Energy for Modern NN Forces and the pi-pi Exchange Three-Nucleon Force

> We present high precision benchmark calculations for the triton binding energy using the most recent, phase equivalent realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials and the Tuscon-Melbourne pi-pi three-nucleon force (3NF). That 3NF is included with partial waves up to a total two-body angular momentum of j_max=6. It is shown that the inclusion of the 3NF slows down the convergence in the partial waves and j_max=5 is needed in order to achieve converged results within a few keV. We adjust the cut-off parameter Lambda in the form factors of the Tuscon-Melbourne 3NF separately for the different NN potentials to the triton binding energy. This provides a set of phenomenological three-nucleon Hamiltonians which can be tested in three-nucleon scattering and systems with A>3. A connection between the probability to find two nucleons at short distances in the triton and the effect of that 3NF on the triton binding energy is pointed out.

# Benchmark Calculations for the Triton Binding Energy for Modern NN Forces and the π-π Exchange Three-Nucleon Force

## Key claim

The Tucson-Melbourne π-π three-nucleon force (3NF) can be adjusted via its cut-off parameter Λ to reproduce the experimental triton binding energy for each modern phase-equivalent NN potential, but the required Λ values vary nonlinearly with the NN-only binding energy and correlate with the short-range behavior of the two-nucleon correlation function in the triton.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equation in momentum space including the Tucson-Melbourne π-π 3NF, using a new partial wave decomposition that avoids numerical instabilities for partial waves with total two-body angular momentum j > 2. They computed the triton binding energy for six modern phase-equivalent NN potentials (AV18, CD-Bonn, Nijm I, Nijm II, Nijm 93, Reid 93) with charge independence breaking included via an effective t-matrix, and then adjusted the cut-off parameter Λ in the 3NF form factors separately for each potential to match the experimental triton binding energy of 8.48 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Experimental triton binding energy: 8.48 MeV
- Triton binding energy without 3NF for CD-Bonn: 8.00 MeV (the highest among the potentials tested)
- Triton binding energy without 3NF for AV18: approximately 7.62 MeV (the lowest among the potentials tested)
- Charge symmetry breaking effect on binding energy: about 100 keV for AV18 and about 60 keV for CD-Bonn when replacing t_pp by t_nn in the effective t-matrix
- Convergence criterion: j_max = 5 needed for the 3NF to achieve results within a few keV; contributions from inner partial waves with j = 6 are less than 0.1%
- Numerical accuracy: binding energy accuracy of about 0.1%; energy expectation value differs from eigenvalue by less than 0.05%
- For the AV14 + TM 3NF (Λ = 5.13 m_π) test case: triton binding energy = 8.486 MeV, in agreement with the Pisa group's configuration space result of 8.486 MeV
- The 3NF expectation value ⟨V₄⟩ is about 2% of the NN potential energy ⟨V⟩, contributing approximately 1 MeV to the binding energy

## Context

This work extends prior momentum-space Faddeev calculations that were limited to j_max = 2 for the 3NF (Stadler 1991, Stadler 1995) by using a new partial wave decomposition (Huber 1997) that enables inclusion of higher partial waves up to j_max = 5. The results agree with independent configuration-space calculations by the Pisa group (Kievsky 1994) using the pair correlated hyperspherical harmonic basis. The phenomenological approach of adjusting the 3NF strength to reproduce the triton binding energy follows the precedent of the Urbana-Argonne collaboration (Carlson 1983, Schiavilla 1986). The fitted Hamiltonians enable future studies of three-nucleon scattering and systems with A > 3, similar to the work of Pudliner (1995), and provide benchmarks for testing consistent NN+3N forces being developed by the Bochum group (Ruhrpot potential, Eden 1996).
