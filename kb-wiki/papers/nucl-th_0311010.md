# Comparison of delta- and Gogny-type pairing interactions

> The matrix elements of the zero-range $δ$-force and the finite range Gogny-type pairing force are compared. The strengths of the $δ$-interaction for rare-earth nuclei are adjusted. Pairing gaps resulting from different pairing interactions are compared to experimental ones.

# Comparison of delta- and Gogny-type pairing interactions

## Key claim
The zero-range delta force and finite-range Gogny force produce essentially the same pairing matrix elements after proper renormalization, justifying the use of the simpler delta force in nuclear structure calculations. However, this equivalence holds only for the isovector pairing channel (T=1) and within the Nilsson mean-field model with BCS approximation, not in fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov frameworks.

## Method
The authors calculated pairing matrix elements for both the zero-range delta interaction and the finite-range Gogny force (using D1S parameters) in the J=0 pairing channel, within the Nilsson single-particle model and BCS approximation. They compared diagonal and non-diagonal matrix elements for protons and neutrons in several rare-earth nuclei, and adjusted the delta-force strengths by matching calculated lowest quasiparticle energies to experimental three-point pairing gap indicators Delta^(3) derived from nuclear binding energies.

## Key numbers
- Adjusted delta-force strengths for rare-earth nuclei: V0(protons) = 240 MeV·fm^3, V0(neutrons) = 230 MeV·fm^3
- Diagonal matrix elements of the delta force follow a g_aa(2n) = const * (hbar*omega_0l) / (2n)^(2/3) dependence, with fitted constants 0.310 hbar*omega_0N for neutrons and 0.300 hbar*omega_0Z for protons
- The monopole pairing constants from Boening (2000) are g_N = 0.284 hbar*omega_0N and g_Z = 0.290 hbar*omega_0Z, giving approximately 5% differences from the delta-force diagonal matrix elements
- State-dependent pairing gaps from the delta force differ from experimental Delta^(3) indicators by 10%, while monopole pairing gaps differ by 20%

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of Gogny-type pairing forces in conventional HFB (Dobaczewski, 1996) and relativistic RHB (Meng, 1998) by using a simpler Nilsson+BCS framework. The results support the argument of Bulgac (2002) that pairing in nuclei should be insensitive to interaction details due to the coherence length being comparable to nuclear size. The derived 2n^(-2/3) dependence of delta-force matrix elements provides an alternative method for determining pairing strengths in nuclei far from stability where experimental masses are unavailable, following the approach of Boening (2000) for monopole pairing.
