# $pΞ^- $ Correlation in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions with Nucleon-Hyperon Interaction from Lattice QCD

> On the basis of the $pΞ^-$ interaction extracted from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations at the physical point, the momentum correlation of $p$ and $Ξ^-$ produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions is evaluated. $C_{\rm SL}(Q)$ defined by a ratio of the momentum correlations between the systems with different source sizes is shown to be largely enhanced at low momentum due to the strong attraction between $p$ and $Ξ^-$ in the $I=J=0$ channel. Thus, measuring this ratio at RHIC and LHC and its comparison to the theoretical analysis will give a useful constraint on the $pΞ^-$ interaction.

## Key claim

The momentum correlation ratio \(C_{\rm SL}(Q)\) for the \(p\Xi^-\) system, defined between small and large source sizes, is predicted to show large enhancement at low relative momentum due to strong attraction in the \(I=J=0\) channel, but this result depends on the assumption that the \(I=1\) sector contributes negligibly and that absorptive coupling to the \(\Lambda\Lambda\) channel is weak.

## Method

The authors extracted the \(p\Xi^-\) interaction potential from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations at the physical point using the HAL QCD method, which derives energy-independent non-local potentials from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function. They parametrized the lattice results for the \(N\Xi\) potential in the \(I=0\) channels with a combination of Gauss and Yukawa functions, then solved the Schrodinger equation with both strong and Coulomb interactions to compute the two-particle momentum correlation function for a static spherical source. The SL ratio was defined as \(C_{\rm SL}(Q) = C_{R=2.5\ {\rm fm}}(Q) / C_{R=5\ {\rm fm}}(Q)\) to isolate strong interaction effects from Coulomb contamination.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass \(m_\pi \simeq 146\) MeV and kaon mass \(m_K \simeq 525\) MeV for the lattice setup at nearly physical quark masses.
* Lattice spacing \(a \simeq 0.085\) fm on a \(96^4\) lattice, giving physical size \(La = 8.1\) fm.
* Baryon masses from the lattice: neutron \(953 \pm 7\) MeV, \(\Lambda\) \(1123 \pm 3\) MeV, \(\Sigma\) \(1204 \pm 2\) MeV, \(\Xi\) \(1332 \pm 1\) MeV.
* The \(^1S_0\) system is close to the unitary region where the inverse scattering length is near zero, due to strong QCD attraction combined with Coulomb attraction.
* The SL ratio \(C_{\rm SL}(Q)\) shows large enhancement at small relative momentum \(Q\), with weak dependence on the Euclidean time \(t\) used for potential extraction.

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to the \(p\Omega^-\) system by Morita (2016), where the SL ratio was introduced to probe a possible spin-2 dibaryon state suggested by lattice QCD. The present analysis uses the \(p\Xi^-\) interaction from lattice QCD simulations at physical quark masses by Sasaki (2017), which showed strong attraction in the \(I=J=0\) channel. Measuring this correlation at RHIC and LHC and comparing to theory can constrain the \(p\Xi^-\) interaction, with implications for the possible \(H\)-dibaryon coupled to \(p\Xi^-\), properties of \(\Xi\)-hypernuclei, and \(\Xi^-\) in neutron star cores.
