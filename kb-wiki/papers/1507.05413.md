# Anti-shadowing Effect on Charmonium Production at a Fixed-target Experiment Using LHC Beams

> We investigate charmonium production in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC beam energy $E_{\text {lab}}$=2.76 A TeV at fixed-target experiment ($\sqrt {s_{\text{NN}}}$=72 GeV). In the frame of a transport approach including cold and hot nuclear matter effects on charmonium evolution, we focus on the anti-shadowing effect on the nuclear modification factors $R_{AA}$ and $r_{AA}$ for the $J/ψ$ yield and transverse momentum. The yield is more suppressed at less forward rapidity ($y_\text{lab}\simeq$2) than that at very forward rapidity ($y_\text{lab}\simeq$4) due to the shadowing and anti-shadowing in different rapidity bins.

## Key claim

The anti-shadowing effect on gluon distributions enhances the charm quark production cross section by 20% and the regenerated charmonium yield by a factor of 1.44 at very forward rapidity (y_lab=4.3), but this enhancement is conditional on the specific nuclear parton distribution parametrization (EKS98) and the assumption of thermalized charm quarks in the medium.

## Method

The authors solve a Boltzmann transport equation for charmonium phase-space distributions in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=72 GeV, including gluon dissociation and regeneration via detailed balance in a quark-gluon plasma described by 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamics. Cold nuclear matter effects (nuclear absorption with sigma_abs=2 mb, Cronin effect with a_gN=0.085 (GeV/c)^2/fm, and spatially-dependent nuclear shadowing using the EKS98 parametrization) are incorporated into the initial charmonium distribution at formation time tau_0=0.6 fm/c. The hot medium evolution uses an equation of state with a first-order phase transition at T_c=165 MeV, initialized with dN_ch/deta=515 charged particles at central rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN)=72 GeV from LHC beam energy E_lab=2.76 A TeV in fixed-target mode
- Maximum medium temperature T_0=310 MeV at central rapidity (y_lab=4.3) and T_0=245 MeV at backward rapidity (y_lab=2.3)
- Charged multiplicity dN_ch/deta=515 at central rapidity from empirical formula
- Charm quark production cross section dsigma_c\bar{c}^NN/dy=0.05 mb at sqrt(s_NN)=72 GeV, enhanced to 0.06 mb with anti-shadowing
- J/psi production cross section dsigma_J/psi/dy=0.35 micro-barn at sqrt(s_NN)=72 GeV
- Mean transverse momentum square in p+p collisions <p_t^2>_pp=2.7 (GeV/c)^2
- Dissociation temperatures: T_d=2.1 T_c for J/psi, 1.16 T_c for chi_c, 1.12 T_c for psi'
- Double ratio R_AA(y_lab=4.3)/R_AA(y_lab=2.3) reaches 1.3 in most central collisions with shadowing effects included
- Momentum fractions at y_lab=2.3: x_1=0.35 (EMC region), x_2=0.006 (shadowing region), leading to 15% reduction in charm cross section

## Context

This work extends transport model calculations of charmonium production (Zhu 2011, Tang 2013) to the fixed-target LHC energy regime proposed by Brodsky (2013), bridging the gap between SPS and RHIC energies. The rapidity-dependent shadowing analysis builds on nuclear parton distribution parametrizations EKS98 (Eskola 1998), EPS08, and EPS09 (Eskola 2009), and the predicted double ratio sensitivity to gluon distributions could enable discrimination between different shadowing models. The results enable experimental design for the proposed AFTER@LHC fixed-target program, where the 100-fold increase in J/psi yield per year compared to RHIC Au+Au at 62.4 GeV would allow precise measurement of the double ratio as a probe of gluon shadowing.
