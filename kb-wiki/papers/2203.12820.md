# A possible trial beyond the conventional random phase approximation for the su(2)-Lipkin model including the case of non-closed shell system

> Concerning the su(2)-Lipkin model, the calculation of the excitation energy to the 1st excited-state gives rise to the following fact: The two results based on the exact treatment and the conventional random phase approximation (RPA) are in unexpected disagreement. In order to remove this discrepancy, the conventional RPA is renewed. With the aim of this renewal, the terms, which cannot be dealt with in the conventional one, are estimated. The basic framework of this new form is not reorganized from that of the conventional one. In addition, the su(2)-Lipkin model itself is also modified so as to be applicable to the case with non-closed shell system. To this modification, one more su(2)-algebra, which has been already proposed by the present authors, is applied. Through the use of this algebra, the su(2)-Lipkin model is applicable to the case with any total fermion number permitted in the model.

## Key claim

The conventional random phase approximation (RPA) fails to reproduce the exact first-excited-state energy of the su(2)-Lipkin model, showing a conspicuous discrepancy with no phase transition visible; the authors propose a renewal that replaces certain expectation values with forms derived from the Casimir invariant, without reorganizing the basic RPA framework, and also extends the model to non-closed-shell systems using an auxiliary su(2)-algebra.

## Method

The authors decompose the su(2)-Lipkin model operators into two parts (active and spectator) using an auxiliary su(2)-algebra whose generators commute with the original ones, enabling treatment of total fermion numbers N not equal to 2Ω. They then linearize the equation of motion for the particle-hole operators, but instead of replacing bilinear terms with expectation values in the free vacuum (as in conventional RPA), they estimate these terms using the Casimir operator relation and a consistency condition derived from the commutator with the number operator, yielding closed equations for the forward and backward amplitudes and the occupation numbers n₀ and n₁.

## Key numbers

- For Ω=5 (s=Ω), the conventional RPA gives ω/(2ε) = √(1 − (G/ε · 2Ω)²), with a phase transition predicted at G·2Ω/ε = 1; the exact result shows no such transition and deviates strongly from the RPA curve.
- The renewed RPA yields the relation 2s − n₀ = −(2ε/ω) + √((2ε/ω)² + 4s(s+1)), with boundary values: when ω/(2ε)=1, n₀=0; when ω/(2ε)=0, n₀=2s.
- The difference n₁ − n₀ is determined as 2(ω/(2ε)), giving boundary values: when ω/(2ε)=1, n₁=2; when ω/(2ε)=0, n₁=2s.
- The total fermion number N in the modified model satisfies 2s ≤ N ≤ 4Ω − 2s, with the closed-shell case corresponding to N = 2Ω = 2s.

## Context

This work extends the su(2)-Lipkin model, originally introduced by Lipkin, Meshkov, and Glick (1965), which serves as a simple exactly solvable testbed for many-body approximations. The authors address the known failure of conventional RPA for this model, which has been noted in prior literature (the paper cites reference [6] for this discrepancy). The renewed RPA builds on an auxiliary su(2)-algebra previously introduced by the same authors (Tsue, Providencia, da Providencia, Yamamura, reference [5]) and relates to earlier attempts to include anharmonic effects, such as the second RPA (da Providencia, reference [JdP1]), Green function corrections (reference [JdP2]), and the generalized RPA via boson expansions (reference [JdP3]). The approach enables future application of the Lipkin model to non-closed-shell systems and provides a possible template for improving RPA in more realistic nuclear-structure calculations.
