# Efficiency corrections for mutually inclusive variables and particle identification effect for mixed-cumulants in heavy-ion collisions

> Mix-cumulants of conserved charge distributions are sensitive observables for probing properties of the QCD medium and phase transition in heavy-ion collisions. To perform precise measurements, efficiency correction is one of the most important step. In this study, using the binomial efficiency model, we derive efficiency correction formulas for mutually exclusive and inclusive variables. The UrQMD model is applied to verify the validity of these formulas for different types of correlations. Furthermore, we investigate the effect of the multiplicity loss and contamination emerging from the particle identifications. This study provides an important step toward future measurements of mixed-cumulants in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the observed deviations in charge-proton and charge-kaon correlators from model predictions in STAR data are caused by applying the wrong efficiency correction formula (designed for mutually exclusive variables) to mutually inclusive variables, rather than by new physics.

## Method

The authors derived efficiency correction formulas for second-order mixed-cumulants under the binomial efficiency model for two cases: mutually exclusive variables (Eq. 12) and mutually inclusive variables (Eq. 16). They validated these formulas using approximately one million UrQMD model events for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, applying binomial efficiency filters with momentum-dependent efficiencies and comparing corrected values to the true generated values. They also simulated particle identification effects using a TPC dE/dx resolution of 7.5% to study the impact of multiplicity loss and contamination from n-sigma cuts.

## Key numbers

- For a test case with Poisson-distributed independent variables with mean N1 = 4 and N2 = 6, and efficiencies epsilon1 = 0.5 and epsilon2 = 0.4, the wrong formula (Eq. 12) gave an efficiency-corrected mixed-cumulant value of 4.55, while the true value was 4.0.
- The UrQMD analysis used approximately one million events for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The kinematic acceptance applied was |eta| < 0.5 and 0.4 < p_T < 1.6 GeV/c, matching STAR data.
- The dE/dx resolution used for particle identification simulation was R = 7.5%.
- Results were presented for 9 different centrality bins represented by average number of participant nucleons.

## Context

This work extends the efficiency correction framework for cumulants developed by Bzdak (2012), Nonaka (2017), and Luo (2018) to the specific case of mixed-cumulants between mutually inclusive variables. It directly addresses the discrepancy between STAR measurements (Adam 2019) of charge-proton and charge-kaon correlators and model predictions, showing that the deviation arises from using the wrong efficiency correction formula rather than from physics. The study enables future measurements of mixed-cumulants in relativistic heavy-ion collisions by providing the correct efficiency correction procedure that requires particle identification for charged particles rather than using unidentified charged tracks.
