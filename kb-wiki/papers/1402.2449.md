# Nucleon-Nucleon scattering from dispersion relations: next-to-next-to-leading order study

> We study nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) scattering by applying the $N/D$ method in chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-leading (NNLO) order in the calculation of the imaginary part of the $NN$ partial-wave amplitudes along the left-hand-cut, which is the dynamical input for this approach. A quite good reproduction of the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis phase shifts and mixing angles is obtained, which implies a steady improvement in the accurateness achieved by increasing the chiral order in the calculation of the dynamical input. A power counting for the subtraction constants is established, which is appropriate for those subtractions attached to both the left- and right-hand cuts. We discuss that it is not necessary to modify the $NN$ chiral potential at NNLO to agree with data, but instead one should perform the iteration of two-nucleon intermediate states to finally achieve analytic and unitarity $NN$ partial-wave amplitudes in a well-defined way. We also confirm at NNLO the long-range correlations between the $NN$ $S$-wave effective ranges and scattering lengths, when employing only once-subtracted dispersion relations, that holds up to around 10% when compared with experimental values.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts can be reproduced quite well at next-to-next-to-leading order using the N/D method with chiral perturbation theory, without needing to modify the NN chiral potential at NNLO; instead, the key improvement comes from properly iterating two-nucleon intermediate states to enforce analyticity and unitarity.

## Method

The author applies the N/D method to nucleon-nucleon partial-wave amplitudes, using as dynamical input the imaginary part of the amplitudes along the left-hand cut (Δ(A)) calculated in chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). The discontinuity Δ(A) includes one-pion exchange, leading two-pion exchange, and subleading two-pion exchange with NLO πN vertices from heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. The resulting integral equations for the denominator function D(A) are solved numerically, and the subtraction constants are assigned a chiral power counting based on their variation under changes of the subtraction point.

## Key numbers

- The long-range correlations between NN S-wave effective ranges and scattering lengths, when using once-subtracted dispersion relations, hold up to around 10% when compared with experimental values.
- The onset of the left-hand cut due to one-pion exchange is at A = -M_π²/4, where M_π is the pion mass.
- The n-th iteration of two-nucleon intermediate states contributes to Δ(A) only for A < -(n+1)² M_π²/4.
- The phase space factor ρ(A) = m √A / (4π), where m is the nucleon mass.
- For the NLO πN low-energy constants, the author considers values from multiple analyses: c₁ ranges from -1.50 to -0.75 GeV⁻¹, c₃ from -6.74 to -4.48 GeV⁻¹, and c₄ from 3.00 to 3.96 GeV⁻¹ across different determinations.

## Context

This work extends the previous N/D studies at leading order (Paper1, Paper2) and next-to-leading order (Gor2013) by including NNLO contributions to the left-hand-cut discontinuity Δ(A). It confirms the long-range correlations between effective ranges and scattering lengths first noticed by Pavon (2006), and demonstrates that the chiral expansion in this dispersive approach is well-behaved, with steady improvement from LO to NLO to NNLO. The approach avoids the cutoff dependence that arises when solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a chiral NN potential, as discussed in works by Epelbaum (2008), Machleidt (2011), and others.
