# Spin-rotation coupling in non-exponential decay of hydrogenlike heavy ions

> We discuss a model in which a recently reported modulation in the decay of the hydrogenlike ions ${}^{140}$Pr$^{58 +}$ and ${}^{142}$Pm$^{60 +}$ arises from the coupling of rotation to the spin of electron and nuclei (Thomas precession). A similar model describes the electron modulation in muon $ g-2$ experiments correctly. Agreement with the GSI experimental results is obtained for the current QED-values of the bound electron g-factors, $g({}^{140}$Pr$^{58 +})=1.872$ and $g({}^{142}$Pm$^{60 +})=1.864$, if the Lorentz factor of the bound electron is $\sim 1.88$. The latter is fixed by either of the two sets of experimental data. The model predicts that the modulation is not observable if the motion of the ions is linear, or if the ions are stopped in a target.

## Key claim

The modulation observed in the decay of hydrogenlike ions ${}^{140}$Pr$^{58+}$ and ${}^{142}$Pm$^{60+}$ at GSI can be explained by spin-rotation coupling (Thomas precession) of the electron and nucleus, provided the bound electron's Lorentz factor is approximately 1.88, which is fixed by either set of experimental data.

## Method

The authors model the nucleus and bound electron as spin-1 and spin-1/2 particles, respectively, evolving under a Hamiltonian that includes magnetic field coupling and Thomas precession terms. They derive the spin-flip probabilities for electron and nucleus independently, then compute the joint probability that both spins are antiparallel (the condition enabling decay). The resulting modulation frequency $\Omega = \Omega_e - \Omega_n$ is expressed in terms of the bound electron g-factor, nuclear magnetic moment, and the electron Lorentz factor, then compared to the experimental modulation frequency of 0.14 Hz.

## Key numbers

- Modulation period in the laboratory frame: 7.069(8) s for ${}^{140}$Pr$^{58+}$ and 7.10(22) s for ${}^{142}$Pm$^{60+}$.
- Bound electron g-factors from bound-state QED: $g({}^{140}\text{Pr}^{58+}) = 1.872$ and $g({}^{142}\text{Pm}^{60+}) = 1.864$.
- Electron Lorentz factor required for agreement: $\gamma_e \sim 1.88$ (specifically 1.88135 for Pr$^{58+}$ and 1.87392 for Pm$^{60+}$).
- Corresponding average electron-nucleus distance: $R \simeq 123$ fm (0.145 $a_Z$) for Pr$^{58+}$ and $R \simeq 128$ fm (0.151 $a_Z$) for Pm$^{60+}$, compared to nuclear radius $\sim 6.2$ fm.
- Storage ring parameters: orbit length $L = 108.3$ m, magnetic rigidity $B\rho = 6.44$ Tm, ion Lorentz factor $\gamma = 1.43$, $\beta = 0.71$, revolution frequency $f \simeq 2$ MHz.

## Context

This work extends the spin-rotation coupling model of Bell and Leinaas (1987), which successfully described electron modulation in muon $g-2$ experiments, to the GSI decay modulation effect first reported by Litvinov et al. (2008). The model predicts that the modulation disappears for linear ion motion or stopped ions, and that the effect scales inversely with storage ring radius. It enables extraction of nuclear radii, bound-electron g-factors, and nuclear magnetic moments from precision decay measurements, with implications for bound-state QED tests and stellar nucleosynthesis.
