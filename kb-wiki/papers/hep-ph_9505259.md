# Light Front Quantization

> An introductory overview on Light-Front quantization, with some emphasis on recent achievements, is given. Light-Front quantization is the most promising and physical tool to study deep inelastic scattering on the basis of quark gluon degrees of freedom. The simplified vacuum structure (nontrivial vacuum effects can only appear in zero-mode degrees of freedom) and the physical basis allows for a description of hadrons that stays close to intuition. Recent progress has ben made in understanding the connection between effective LF Hamiltonians and nontrivial vacuum condesates. Discrete Light-Cone Quantization, the transverse lattice and Light-Front Tamm-Dancoff (in combination with renormalization group techniques) are the main tools for exploring LF-Hamiltonians nonperturbatively.

## Key claim

Light-Front quantization provides the most promising framework for studying deep inelastic scattering using quark and gluon degrees of freedom, but the trivial vacuum structure raises the question of whether nonperturbative LF field theory is equivalent to conventional field theory where nonperturbative effects produce a highly nontrivial vacuum.

## Method

The paper presents canonical quantization on the light-front by comparing it with equal-time quantization, constructing Hamiltonians for scalar fields, fermions with Yukawa interactions, and gauge fields. It introduces ε-coordinates on finite light-front intervals as a limiting procedure from a spacelike quantization surface to avoid causality issues with boundary conditions, and describes nonperturbative solution methods including Discrete Light-Cone Quantization, the transverse lattice, and Light-Front Tamm-Dancoff with renormalization group techniques.

## Key numbers

- The QCD scale is given as Λ_QCD ≈ 200 MeV
- In the Bjorken limit, Q² ≡ -q² → ∞ and P·q → ∞ with x_Bj = Q²/2P·q fixed
- For QCD₁₊₁(N_C → ∞), the LF calculation of the meson spectrum is more than one order of magnitude faster numerically than the equal-time approach
- The free dispersion relation in ε-coordinates gives p₊(n) = (L/2ε)(-p₋(n) ± √(p₋(n)² + 2εm²/L)) where p₋(n) = 2πn/L
- The LF dispersion relation for free fields is p₊ = (m² + p⃗_⊥²)/2p₋

## Context

This review extends Dirac's (1949) original formulation of field theory on the light-front and connects to the infinite momentum frame approach developed by Fubini and Furlan (1965), Weinberg (1966), and others. The paper enables practical nonperturbative calculations of parton distributions and hadron structure, with the key open question being whether LF Hamiltonians with trivial vacuum can describe spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.
