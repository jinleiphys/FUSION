# Neutron star in presence of torsion-dilaton field

> We develop the general theory of stars in Saa's model of gravity with propagating torsion and study the basic stationary state of neutron star. Our numerical results show that the torsion force decreases the role of the gravity in the star configuration leading to significant changes in the neutron star masses depending on the equation of state of star matter. The inconsistency of the Saa's model with Roll-Krotkov-Dicke and Braginsky-Panov experiments is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in Saa's model of gravity with propagating torsion, the torsion-dilaton field decreases the role of gravity in neutron star configurations, leading to significantly different masses compared to general relativity, but the model is inconsistent with the Roll-Krotkov-Dicke and Braginsky-Panov experiments.

## Method

The authors developed the general theory of stars in Saa's model of gravity with propagating torsion, deriving the field equations for a static, spherically symmetric neutron star with a perfect fluid matter source. They solved the resulting system of equations numerically using the Runge-Kutta-Merson method with automatic error control, employing two equations of state: a non-interacting neutron gas and an analytical approximation of the Tsuruta-Cameron equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Maximum tensor mass in the torsion-dilaton model for non-interacting neutron gas: approximately 0.35 solar masses, compared to the Oppenheimer-Volkoff mass of 0.7 solar masses in general relativity
- Radius corresponding to the maximum tensor mass: 4.2 km in the torsion-dilaton model, versus 9.6 km in general relativity
- Central density at which the maximum tensor mass occurs: approximately 4.5 x 10^16 g/cm^3 in the torsion-dilaton model, versus approximately 5 x 10^15 g/cm^3 in general relativity
- Average density in the torsion-dilaton model is about 4 times greater than in general relativity
- The parameter K (ratio of torsion force to gravitational force) lies in the range [1/3, 1/2] for realistic equations of state, giving tensor mass in the range [1/4, 1/2] of the Keplerian mass

## Context

This work extends Saa's model of gravity with propagating torsion (Saa, 1995-1997) to the study of neutron stars, building on the scalar-tensor gravity framework that arises from the low-energy limit of superstring theory. The results contrast with general relativity predictions for neutron star structure and with the spontaneous scalarization phenomenon discovered by Damour and Esposito-Farese (1993). The paper also discusses the inconsistency of Saa's model with the Roll-Krotkov-Dicke and Braginsky-Panov experiments, which constrains the viability of the model.
