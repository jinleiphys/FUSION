# Neutron Removal from the Deformed Halo 31Ne Nucleus

> Experimental data on Coulomb breakup and neutron removal indicate that 31Ne is one of the heaviest halo nuclei discovered so far. The possible ground state of 31Ne is either 3/2- coming from p-wave halo or 1/2+ from s-wave halo. In this work, we develop a treatable model to include deformed wave functions and a dynamical knockout formalism which includes the dependence on the nuclear orientation to study the neutron removal from 31Ne projectiles at energies around E=200 MeV/nucleon. A detailed account of the effects of deformation on cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions is made. Our numerical analysis indicates a preference for the 31Ne ground state with spin parity 3/2-.

## Key claim

The numerical analysis indicates a preference for the \(^{31}\)Ne ground state with spin parity \(3/2^-\), but this conclusion is conditional on the specific deformation parameters and binding energies considered; the \(1/2^+\) assignment cannot be ruled out for weak deformation (\(\beta_2 \lesssim 0.3\)) or strong binding.

## Method

The authors developed a treatable model combining deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions with a Glauber-type eikonal knockout formalism that includes orientation-dependent core-target S-matrices. They solved coupled differential equations for deformed states with quadrupole deformation (\(\lambda=2\) only), expanded the core density to linear orders of deformation, and calculated stripping and diffraction dissociation cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions using a modified version of the MOMDIS code. The calculations were performed for the \(^{12}\)C(\(^{31}\)Ne,\(^{30}\)Ne)X reaction at 230 MeV per nucleon, averaging over all projectile orientations.

## Key numbers

- For the deformed state \(\Psi_{3/2^-}\) with \(\beta_2=0.4\) and binding energy \(E=-0.15\) MeV, the normalized wave function composition is: \(\sqrt{0.24}\, p_{3/2} + \sqrt{0.01}\, f_{5/2} + \sqrt{0.75}\, f_{7/2}\).
- For the deformed state \(\Psi_{1/2^+}\) with \(\beta_2=0.6\) and \(E=-0.6\) MeV, the composition is: \(\sqrt{0.59}\, s_{1/2} + \sqrt{0.37}\, d_{3/2} + \sqrt{0.04}\, d_{5/2}\).
- The measured cross section feeding the ground state of the residual core is \(\sigma_{\rm exp}^{\rm gs} = 3.3(1.5)\) fm\(^2\).
- The experimental inclusive cross section is \(\sigma_{\rm exp}^{\rm inc} = 9.0(0.7)\) fm\(^2\).
- The measured momentum distribution has a full width at half maximum (FWHM) of \(77(18)\) MeV/c, extracted from a Lorentzian fit.
- Calculated momentum distributions for \(\Psi_{3/2^-}\) have FWHM approximately \(82-93\) MeV/c.
- The total cross section increases by \(2-11\%\) when using the deformed phase compared with the spherical phase.
- Diffraction dissociation amounts to \(15-19\%\) of the stripping cross section.
- For \(\Psi_{3/2^-}\) with \(0.3 \lesssim \beta_2 \lesssim 0.5\), agreement with experimental data requires \(0.3\) MeV \(\lesssim |E| \lesssim 0.45\) MeV.
- For \(\Psi_{3/2^-}\) with \(\beta_2 \approx 0.2\), agreement requires \(0.15\) MeV \(\lesssim |E| \lesssim 0.3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the deformed knockout formalism of Tostevin et al. (various references) and Zelevinsky et al. by using deformed Woods-Saxon wave functions rather than Nilsson model harmonic oscillator states, which decay too fast at long distances and are inadequate for halo nuclei like \(^{31}\)Ne. The approach builds on the Glauber model of Bertsch et al. and the MOMDIS code, incorporating orientation-dependent S-matrices from nuclear ground state densities. The results enable future determination of the \(^{31}\)Ne ground state spin-parity and accurate values of quadrupole deformation and neutron binding energy through comparison with experimental cross sections and momentum distributions from Nakamura et al. (experimental references).
