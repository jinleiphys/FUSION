# Nucleon-nucleon potentials in phase-space representation

> A phase-space representation of nuclear interactions, which depends on the distance $\vec{r}$ and relative momentum $\vec{p}$ of the nucleons, is presented. A method is developed that permits to extract the interaction $V(\vec{r},\vec{p})$ from antisymmetrized matrix elements given in a spherical basis with angular momentum quantum numbers, either in momentum or coordinate space representation. This representation visualizes in an intuitive way the non-local behavior introduced by cutoffs in momentum space or renormalization procedures that are used to adapt the interaction to low momentum many-body Hilbert spaces, as done in the unitary correlation operator method or with the similarity renormalization group. It allows to develop intuition about the various interactions and illustrates how the softened interactions reduce the short-range repulsion in favor of non-locality or momentum dependence while keeping the scattering phase shifts invariant. It also reveals that these effective interactions can have undesired complicated momentum dependencies at momenta around and above the Fermi momentum. Properties, similarities and differences of the phase-space representations of the Argonne and the N3LO chiral potential, and their UCOM and SRG derivatives are discussed.

# Nucleon-nucleon potentials in phase-space representation

## Key claim
The paper presents a phase-space representation of nuclear interactions that depends on both relative distance and relative momentum of nucleons, enabling visualization of non-local behavior introduced by renormalization procedures. However, the method assumes that matrix elements for different partial waves originate from operators with limited powers in angular momentum, and the mapping from antisymmetrized to direct matrix elements is not fully reversible due to information loss from antisymmetrization.

## Method
The authors develop a method to extract the direct interaction V(r,p) from antisymmetrized matrix elements given in a spherical basis with angular momentum quantum numbers, using the Kirkwood phase-space representation. They expand the phase-space representation in Legendre polynomials and solve for the direct components by pseudo-inversion of a matrix that accounts for the exchange term arising from antisymmetrization. The method is applied to the Argonne V18 potential, the N3LO chiral potential, and their UCOM and SRG derivatives for the S=0 channel.

## Key numbers
- The radial function used for illustrative examples is a sum of Gaussians: V(r) = 2 exp(-r^2/1 fm^2) - exp(-r^2/2 fm^2)
- For the Argonne potential (S=0), which has the form V^Arg_{S=0,T} = V^C_{0,T}(r) + V^{L2}_{0,T}(r) L^2, at most Lambda_V = 2 is needed to describe the antisymmetrized phase-space representation
- Typical maximum angular momentum considered is L_max = 8 due to the finite range of the interaction
- The SRG transformation parameter used in the deviation analysis is alpha = 0.2 fm^4

## Context
This work extends the investigation by Wendt, Furnstahl and Ramanan (2012) who studied local projections of effective nuclear interactions. The phase-space representation provides an intuitive visualization of how softened interactions (from UCOM or SRG transformations) reduce short-range repulsion in favor of non-locality or momentum dependence while preserving scattering phase shifts. The method is also motivated by the need for operator-based representations in many-body methods that work with wave packets, such as antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada-En'yo, 1995; 2001; 2012) or fermionic molecular dynamics (Feldmeier, 1990; 2004; 2011), because their basis states possess no angular momentum quantum numbers.
