# Quark distribution inside a pion in many-flavor (2 + 1)-dimensional QCD using lattice computations: UV listens to IR

> We study the changes in the short-distance quark structure of the Nambu-Goldstone boson when the long-distance symmetry-breaking scales are depleted controllably. We achieve this by studying the valence Parton Distribution Function (PDF) of pion in 2+1 dimensional two-color QCD, with the number $N$ of massless quarks as the tunable parameter that slides the theory from being strongly broken for $N=0$ to the conformal window for $N>4$, where the theory is gapped by the fixed finite volume. We perform our study non-perturbatively using lattice simulations with $N=0,2,4,8$ flavors of nearly massless two-component Wilson-Dirac sea quarks and employ the leading-twist formalism (LaMET/SDF) to compute the PDF of pion at a fixed valence mass. We find that the relative variations in the first few PDF moments are only mild compared to the changes in decay constant, but the shape of the reconstructed $x$-dependent PDF itself dramatically changes from being broad in the scale-broken sector to being sharply peaked in the near-conformal region, best reflected in PDF shape observables such as the cumulants.

## Key claim

The valence quark structure of the pion changes dramatically as the number of massless quark flavors is varied from the scale-broken regime (N=0,2,4) to the near-conformal regime (N=8), with the shape of the reconstructed x-dependent PDF becoming sharply peaked in the near-conformal region even though the first few PDF moments show only mild variation compared to changes in the decay constant.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of 2+1 dimensional two-color QCD with N=0,2,4,8 flavors of nearly massless two-component Wilson-Dirac sea quarks on 28×28×48 lattices at fixed lattice spacing (β=9.3333). They constructed boosted pion states with fixed valence pion mass Mπ^val=0.53 g^2 and computed equal-time bilocal matrix elements containing a spatial displacement and Wilson line, employing the leading-twist OPE formalism (LaMET/SDF) to extract the valence PDF and its moments.

## Key numbers

- Valence pion mass fixed at Mπ^val=0.53 g^2 (lattice units Mπ^val a=0.2265) across all N=0,2,4,8 flavor theories
- Tuned valence Wilson-Dirac quark masses: m_w^val=-0.02, -0.01875, -0.0235, and -0.051 for N=0,2,4,8 respectively
- Sea quark masses at approximate massless point: m_w=-0.06836, -0.06513, -0.06060 for N=2,4,8 respectively
- Generated 24.5k, 25.2k, 27.3k, and 30.2k gauge configurations for N=0,2,4,8 respectively
- Periodicity effect of 0.4% for operators separated by 12 lattice units (e^{-Mπ^val (aL3-2ts)}=0.0043)
- Infrared mass anomalous dimension γ_m≈0.4 for N=8 from previous lattice study (Karthik 2018)

## Context

This work extends the leading-twist lattice methodology (LaMET/SDF) previously applied to 3+1 dimensional QCD to a family of 2+1 dimensional QCD-like theories, building on prior lattice studies of the pion PDF in 3+1 dimensions (Gao 2020, Sufian 2020) that found the large-x behavior consistent with β≈1. The study uses the number of massless flavors as a tunable parameter to controllably deplete infrared symmetry-breaking scales, following the authors' previous determination (Karthik 2018) that the theory is spontaneously broken for N≲4 and conformal for N≥8. This approach enables studying correlations between the quark structure of the Nambu-Goldstone boson and the long-distance vacuum structure, and connects to theoretical developments in 2+1 dimensional field theories regarding symmetry-breaking operators and infrared fixed points (Chester 2016, Pufu 2013).
