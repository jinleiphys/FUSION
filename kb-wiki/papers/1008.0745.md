# Multi-galileons, solitons and Derrick's theorem

> The field theory Galilean symmetry, which was introduced in the context of modified gravity, gives a neat way to construct Lorentz-covariant theories of a scalar field, such that the equations of motion contain at most second-order derivatives. Here we extend the analysis to an arbitrary number of scalars, and examine the restrictions imposed by an internal symmetry, focussing in particular on SU(N) and SO(N). This therefore extends the possible gradient terms that may be used to stabilise topological objects such as sigma model lumps.

# Multi-galileons, solitons and Derrick's theorem

## Key claim

The authors construct the most general multi-galileon Lagrangians for an arbitrary number of scalar fields with internal SO(N) or SU(N) symmetry, and show that, contrary to a prior claim by Endlich (2010), the higher-order derivative terms in these theories can evade Derrick's theorem and stabilize static topological solitons, provided the scalar fields are constrained to a non-linear manifold (e.g., a sphere) rather than treated as a linear sigma model.

## Method

The authors extend the single-galileon construction of Nicolis (2009) to N-component real scalars (SO(N) symmetry) and complex scalars (SU(N) symmetry), in both fundamental and adjoint representations, in four spacetime dimensions. They classify all possible Lorentz-covariant terms up to fifth order in the fields that yield second-order equations of motion, using group-theoretic primitive invariants (Kronecker deltas and Levi-Civita symbols) to enforce the internal symmetry. As an explicit example, they construct a static hedgehog soliton in the SO(4) non-linear sigma model with a quartic galileon term, solving the Euler-Lagrange equations numerically for winding numbers n=1 and n=2.

## Key numbers

- For a general N-galileon theory without symmetry, the number of free parameters is sum_{m=1}^{5} (N+m-1)!/(m!(N-1)!); for N=3 this gives 55 free parameters.
- The static hedgehog soliton with winding number n=1 has energy E^(1) ≈ 101.8 (in units where the quartic coupling is absorbed into coordinate scaling).
- The static hedgehog soliton with winding number n=2 has energy E^(2) ≈ 462.3, which is greater than 2 × E^(1), indicating instability toward decay into two n=1 solitons.
- For SU(2), the most general adjoint galileon Lagrangian reduces to only two terms: the γ_2 and γ_4 terms.

## Context

This work extends the bi-galileon theory of Padilla, Saffin, and Zhou (2010) to arbitrary N-component fields with internal symmetry, and generalizes the single-galileon construction of Nicolis (2009) which was motivated by the DGP braneworld model. The authors explicitly contradict the claim of Endlich (2010) that galileon interactions cannot stabilize solitons, by noting that the non-linear sigma model constraint (fields confined to a sphere) prevents the full rescaling deformation used in Derrick's theorem. This opens the possibility of constructing new types of topological solitons, analogous to Skyrme model lumps (Skyrme 1961, 1962) and baby Skyrmions (Piette 1994), using galileon derivative interactions that avoid the ghost instabilities typical of higher-derivative theories.
