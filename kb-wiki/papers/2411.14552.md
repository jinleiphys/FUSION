# Constraining Jet Quenching in Heavy-Ion Collisions with Bayesian Inference

> Jet suppression and modification is a hallmark feature of heavy-ion collisions. This can be attributed to an accumulated set of effects, including radiative and elastic energy loss and reabsorption of thermalized energy within the jet cone, which are encoded in a quenching weight, determining the probability distribution for a shift of the $p_T$ (energy loss). We perform a data-driven analysis, based on Bayesian inference, to extract information about the energy-loss distribution experienced by propagating jets using generic and flexible parametrizations. We first establish the consistency between different data-sets and, thereby, provide evidence for the universality of the quark/gluon quenching weights for different observables. Furthermore, we extract that the color dependence of energy loss is slightly bigger than what expected from Casimir scaling, pointing to the importance of multi-parton quenching within high-$p_T$ jets at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a universal, event-averaged energy-loss distribution (quenching weight) can consistently describe multiple jet suppression observables in heavy-ion collisions, and finds that the color dependence of jet energy loss exceeds the Casimir scaling expectation of \(N_c/C_F = 2.25\), suggesting significant contributions from multi-parton quenching within high-\(p_T\) jets at the LHC.

## Method

The authors perform Bayesian inference using three flexible two-parameter families of probability distributions (normal, log-normal, and gamma) to parametrize the jet energy-loss distribution \(D(\varepsilon)\) separately for quark- and gluon-initiated jets. They apply this data-driven framework to inclusive jet spectra and photon-jet coincidence measurements from Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, fixing the centrality class to 0–10% and the jet cone parameter \(R\), while treating the energy-loss distribution as an event-averaged quantity independent of jet \(p_T\).

## Key numbers

- The Casimir scaling prediction for the color ratio \(C_R = \langle \varepsilon_g \rangle_\text{jet} / \langle \varepsilon_q \rangle_\text{jet}\) is \(N_c/C_F = 2.25\), which the analysis finds to be a lower bound, with evidence for super-Casimir scaling \(C_R > 2.25\).
- For the gamma distribution parametrization, the mean energy loss is \(\langle\varepsilon\rangle = \alpha\theta\), where \(\alpha\) is the shape parameter and \(\theta\) the scale parameter.
- The ratio of mean to maximum energy loss for the gamma distribution is \(\langle \varepsilon \rangle / \varepsilon_\text{max} = \alpha/(\alpha-1)\).
- For the log-normal distribution, the ratio of mean to maximum energy loss is \(\langle \varepsilon \rangle / \varepsilon_\text{max} = e^{\frac{3}{2}\sigma^2}\), showing strong sensitivity to the tail parameter \(\sigma\).
- The analysis considers jet energy scales of \(\mathcal{O}(p_T \sim 10^2\ \text{GeV})\) compared to medium scales of \(\mathcal{O}(T \sim 0.5\ \text{GeV})\).

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of jet quenching, including global analyses by Xie (2022) and the JETSCAPE collaboration (2024) that extracted the jet transport coefficient \(\hat q\) with uncertainties of a factor 2–3. It builds on the factorization approach of Baier (2001) and He (2018), and directly tests the universality hypothesis for quenching weights that is widely assumed in phenomenological models but had not been scrutinized in a data-driven fashion. The results enable future extraction of medium properties with reduced model dependence and motivate experimental measurements less affected by survival bias to better constrain the color dependence of jet quenching.
