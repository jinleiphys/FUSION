# Bayesian Learning of (n,p) Reaction Cross Sections with Quantified Uncertainties

> Accurate modeling of neutron-induced (n,p) reaction cross sections is essential for diverse applications in nuclear physics, including reactor design, nuclear astrophysics, and radionuclide production. However, experimental data are often sparse or incomplete, and theoretical results like TALYS Evaluated Nuclear Data Library (TENDL-2023) data may carry systematic uncertainties. In this work, we present a data-driven framework based on a Bayesian Neural Network (BNN), denoted as BNN-I6, to predict (n,p) reaction cross sections with quantified uncertainties. The model incorporates six physically motivated input features and is trained on Evaluated Nuclear Data from the ENDF/B-VIII.1 library. Leveraging stochastic variational inference, the BNN offers reliable uncertainty estimates in addition to accurate predictions. The performance of BNN-I6 is benchmarked against the TENDL-2023 library and experimental measurements across a wide range of nuclei. Additionally, SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP) based feature-importance analysis reveals the dominant role of theoretical cross-section inputs in driving predictions. These results highlight the potential of BNN-based approaches to enhance nuclear data evaluations and support future applications in data-scarce regimes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Bayesian Neural Network (BNN-I6) trained on ENDF/B-VIII.1 data can predict (n,p) reaction cross sections with quantified uncertainties that generally outperform TENDL-2023, but the abstract glosses over that the model's predictions are primarily driven by the TENDL-2023 theoretical cross-section input itself, as revealed by SHAP analysis, making the model heavily dependent on the very library it aims to supplement.

## Method

The authors constructed a dataset of 8,110 (n,p) reaction cross-section data points from the ENDF/B-VIII.1 library for target nuclei with Z=8-77 and neutron energies up to 20 MeV. They implemented a Bayesian Neural Network (BNN-I6) with six input features (Z, N, pairing term delta, energy offset Delta E, ln(sigma_TENDL), and (N-Z)/A), two hidden layers of 150 and 100 neurons with ELU activation, and trained it using stochastic variational inference with a two-stage learning rate schedule (8000 epochs at 10^-3, then 1000 epochs at 2x10^-4). The model outputs both a mean and standard deviation for each prediction, providing uncertainty quantification.

## Key numbers

- Dataset size: 8,110 data points from ENDF/B-VIII.1, split 70% training, 15% validation, 15% testing
- Input features: six (Z, N, delta, Delta E, ln(sigma_TENDL), (N-Z)/A)
- Hidden layer architecture: 150 neurons in first hidden layer, 100 neurons in second hidden layer
- Training epochs: 8000 initial at learning rate ~10^-3, followed by 1000 fine-tuning epochs at ~2x10^-4
- For heavy target nuclei 192Pt, 192Au, and 204Tl, BNN-I6 achieves r.m.s. deviations below 0.5 (in ln(sigma) units)
- The majority of nuclei exhibit r.m.s. deviations below 1 on the logarithmic scale across the nuclear chart
- Incident neutron energy range: up to approximately 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior applications of Bayesian Neural Networks to nuclear physics problems, including nuclear mass predictions (Utama 2016), nuclear charge radii (Utama 2016), and (n,p) reaction cross sections (Li 2024). It builds on the TENDL-2023 evaluated nuclear data library (Koning 2019) and the ENDF/B-VIII.1 library. The BNN-I6 framework enables future applications in data-scarce regimes where experimental measurements are unavailable, and could contribute to improved evaluated nuclear data libraries by providing statistically meaningful uncertainty estimates alongside predictions.
