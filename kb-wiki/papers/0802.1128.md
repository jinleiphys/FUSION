# Nuclear effects in neutrino induced reactions

> We discuss the relevance of nuclear medium effects in the analysis of some low and medium energy neutrino reactions of current interest. In particular, we study the Quasi-Elastic (QE) process, where RPA correlations and Final State Interactions (FSI) are shown to play a crucial role. We have also investigated the neutrino induced coherent pion production. We find a strong reduction of the cross section due to the distortion of the pion wave function and the modification of the production mechanisms in the nucleus. The sensitivity of the results to the axial $NΔ$ coupling $C_5^A(0)$ has been also investigated.

# Nuclear effects in neutrino induced reactions

## Key claim

Nuclear medium effects, specifically RPA correlations, final state interactions, and pion wave function distortion, are shown to be essential for accurately modeling quasi-elastic and coherent pion production cross sections in neutrino-nucleus reactions at low and medium energies; the abstract's claim of "strong reduction" is qualified by the finding that these effects reduce the coherent pion cross section by around 40% for 1 GeV neutrinos, with the exact magnitude depending on the axial NΔ coupling C5^A(0).

## Method

The authors developed a many-body framework for quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering that incorporates RPA correlations, realistic nucleon spectral functions, and final state interactions described by Monte Carlo simulation, building on models previously tested against photonuclear and electronuclear data. For coherent pion production, they used a model dominated by Δ excitation with background terms (cross Δ and nucleon-pole terms) and included nuclear medium modifications of the Δ propagator via an energy- and density-dependent selfenergy, plus pion final state interactions solved via the Klein-Gordon equation with an optical potential.

## Key numbers

- RPA correlations produce a "large reduction" of the inclusive (ν,l) cross section compared to impulse approximation descriptions at intermediate energies (no single numerical factor given for QE reduction).
- Coherent pion production cross section is reduced by around 40% for 1 GeV neutrinos due to nuclear medium modification of the Δ properties (selfenergy in the propagator).
- Pion distortion (final state interactions) further reduces the coherent cross section, with a "large reduction at the Δ resonance peak" due to pion absorption.
- The results are more sensitive to the axial NΔ coupling C5^A(0) in coherent than in incoherent pion production because at forward direction (q² = 0) only C5^A contributes to Δ excitation.

## Context

This work extends the many-body nuclear models previously applied to photonuclear processes like (γ, N) and (γ, Nπ) by Carrasco et al. (1989, 1992) and electronuclear reactions by Gil et al. (1997) to neutrino-induced reactions. It improves upon earlier coherent pion production calculations by Kelkar et al. (1996) and Singh et al. (2006) by using a more complete description of elementary pion production and better treatment of pion final state interactions. The results enable more reliable modeling of neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for neutrino oscillation experiments, and suggest that coherent pion production could provide complementary information on the NΔ transition axial form factor to that from parity-violating electroexcitation of the Δ.
