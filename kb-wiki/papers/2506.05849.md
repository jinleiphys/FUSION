# Femtoscopic signatures of unique nuclear structures in relativistic collisions

> One of the most vital topics of today's high-energy nuclear physics is the investigation of the nuclear structure of the collided nuclei. Recent studies at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) have shown that several observables, such as the collective flow and transverse-momentum correlations of the produced particles, can be sensitive to various nuclear structure and deformation parameters. Femtoscopy, another essential tool for investigating the space-time geometry of the matter created in nuclear collisions, has not yet been widely applied to such studies. Using a multiphase transport model (AMPT), in this Letter, it is demonstrated that the femtoscopic source parameters of pion pairs can also serve as a robust signal of unique nuclear structure. Through an analysis of $^{208}$Pb+$^{20}$Ne and $^{208}$Pb+$^{16}$O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 68.5 GeV, two collision systems especially relevant to the SMOG2 program of the LHCb experiment, it is shown that a deformed initial shape can significantly affect femtoscopic source parameters. This study highlights the importance of expanding the nuclear structure investigations to femtoscopic observables and serves as a baseline for numerous possible future studies in this new direction.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT transport model, the femtoscopic source parameters of pion pairs in Pb+Ne and Pb+O collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 68.5\) GeV can serve as a robust signal of unique nuclear structure, specifically that the freeze-out eccentricity determined from azimuthally sensitive pion femtoscopy is significantly increased for the NLEFT (alpha-cluster) configuration of the neon nucleus compared to the spherical Woods-Saxon configuration.

## Method

The study simulated 100,000 ultra-central (\(b=0\)) events each for \(^{208}\)Pb+\(^{20}\)Ne and \(^{208}\)Pb+\(^{16}\)O collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 68.5\) GeV using the AMPT parton transport model, with two initial nucleon configurations per system: the spherical Woods-Saxon model and the NLEFT model (which produces a tetrahedron shape for oxygen and a bowling-pin shape for neon). Same-charge pion pairs were selected in five ranges of average transverse momentum \(k_T\) and 15 ranges of pair azimuthal angle relative to the second-order event plane; the three-dimensional pair source distribution was reconstructed and fitted with an elliptically contoured Lévy-stable distribution to extract the six independent Lévy-scale parameters and the Lévy-exponent \(\alpha\). The azimuthal dependence of the \(R^2_{\text{side}}\) parameter was then used to extract the freeze-out eccentricity via the relation \(\varepsilon_F = 2 R^2_{\text{side},2} / R^2_{\text{side},0}\).

## Key numbers

*   Freeze-out eccentricity in Pb+Ne collisions with the NLEFT configuration is significantly increased compared to the Woods-Saxon configuration across the entire \(m_T\) range studied (approximately 0.35 to 0.65 GeV/\(c^2\)).
*   The ratio of freeze-out eccentricities between Pb+Ne and Pb+O collisions is below unity for the Woods-Saxon configuration and above unity for the NLEFT configuration over the full \(m_T\) range.
*   The analysis used 100,000 ultra-central events per collision system and per nucleon configuration, with pions selected in the kinematic range \(0.2 < p_T < 1.0\) GeV/\(c\) and \(-0.5 < \eta < 0.5\).
*   The source parameters were extracted in five \(k_T\) bins, with an example shown for the range \(0.35 < k_T < 0.40\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends recent studies at RHIC and LHC (Giacalone 2021, Bally 2021) that showed collective flow and transverse-momentum correlations are sensitive to nuclear deformation, but where femtoscopy had not yet been widely applied. It builds on prior AMPT and hydrodynamic calculations (Lu 2025, Giacalone 2024) that demonstrated azimuthal anisotropy sensitivity to the deformed neon shape, and on experimental femtoscopic measurements (PHENIX 2024, CMS 2023, NA61/SHINE 2023) that used Lévy-stable source parameterizations. The results enable future experimental studies at the LHCb SMOG2 program and suggest extending azimuthally sensitive femtoscopy to non-identical particle pairs, higher-order event planes, and hybrid hydrodynamic models.
