# Low energy reactions $K^-p\rightarrowΣ^0π^0$, $Λπ^0$, $\bar{K}^0n$ and the strangeness $S=-1$ hyperons

> A combined study of the reactions $K^-p\rightarrow Σ^0π^0$, $Λπ^0$ and $\bar{K}^0n$ at low energies is carried out with a chiral quark-model approach. Good descriptions of the experimental observations are obtained. The roles of the low-lying strangeness $S=-1$ hyperon resonances in these processes are carefully analyzed. We find that: (i) In the $K^-p\rightarrow Σ^0π^0$ process, both $Λ(1405)S_{01}$ and $Λ(1520)D_{03}$ play dominant roles. Significant contributions of $Λ(1670)S_{01}$ and $Λ(1690)D_{03}$ could be seen around their threshold; (ii) In the $K^-p\rightarrow Λπ^0$ process, some obvious evidence of $Σ(1775)D_{15}$ and $Σ(1750)S_{11}$ could be found. Some hints of $Σ(1620)S_{11}$ might exist in the reaction as well. $Σ(1750)S_{11}$ and $Σ(1620)S_{11}$ should correspond to the representations $[70,^48]S_{11}$ and $[70,^28]S_{11}$, respectively; (iii) In the $K^-p\rightarrow \bar{K}^0n$ process, the dominant resonances are $Λ(1405)$ and $Λ(1520)$. Some evidence of $Λ(1690)D_{03}$, $Σ(1670)D_{13}$ and $Σ(1775)D_{15}$ could be seen as well. A weak coupling of $Λ(1670)S_{01}$ to $\bar{K}N$ should be needed in the reactions $K^-p\rightarrow Σ^0π^0$ and $\bar{K}^0n$. Furthermore, by analyzing these reactions, we also find that the $u$-, t-channel backgrounds and s-channel Born term play crucial roles in the reactions.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model provides good descriptions of the experimental data for the three reactions, but the authors find that SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry breaking is required, as the coupling strength parameters C_R deviate from unity when fitted to the data.

## Method

The authors performed a combined analysis of the reactions K⁻p → Σ⁰π⁰, Λπ⁰, and K̄⁰n using a chiral quark model with an effective chiral Lagrangian for quark-meson couplings. They calculated s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel transition amplitudes, separated partial wave amplitudes for individual resonances up to the n=2 shell, and introduced empirical coupling strength parameters C_R to account for SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry breaking. The model parameters were determined by fitting to experimental differential cross section and polarization data at kaon beam momenta between 514 and 750 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- For K⁻p → Σ⁰π⁰: the coupling strength for Λ(1405)S₀₁ is C = 1.13⁺⁰·¹⁷₋₀·₀₅, for Λ(1520)D₀₃ is C = 2.49⁺⁰·⁰⁶₋₀·₀₅, and for Λ(1670)S₀₁ is C = 0.33⁺⁰·⁰⁵₋₀·₀₈
- For K⁻p → Λπ⁰: the coupling strength for Σ(1750)S₁₁ is C = 0.86⁺⁰·⁰⁸₋₀·₁₂, and for Σ(1775)D₁₅ is C = 0.78⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀·₁₃
- For K⁻p → K̄⁰n: the coupling strength for Λ(1405)S₀₁ is C = 0.72⁺⁰·⁰³₋₀·₀₆, for Λ(1520)D₀₃ is C = 2.87⁺⁰·¹⁵₋₀·₁₅, and for Λ(1670)S₀₁ is C = 0.08⁺⁰·⁰²₋₀·₀₃
- The u-channel background strength parameter C_u is 0.68⁺⁰·⁰⁵₋₀·₀₈ for Σ⁰π⁰ and 0.95⁺⁰·⁰³₋₀·₀₂ for Λπ⁰
- The flavor symmetry breaking parameter √(δₘᵢδₘ_f) is 0.99 ± 0.01 for Σ⁰π⁰, 1.13 ± 0.01 for Λπ⁰, and 1.08 ± 0.01 for K̄⁰n
- The harmonic oscillator parameter α = 0.4 GeV, with constituent quark masses m_u = m_d = 330 MeV and m_s = 450 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous chiral quark model studies of meson photoproduction and πN scattering by Zhong and collaborators (2007, 2009) to a combined analysis of three K⁻p reactions using new higher-precision data from Manweiler (2008) and Prakhov (2008). The results address open questions in the spectroscopy of S = -1 hyperons, particularly the classification of states such as Λ(1405), Λ(1520), and Λ(1670) as either excited three-quark states or dynamically generated resonances, as debated in the literature by Klempt (2009) and others. The analysis enables future extraction of information on unestablished hyperon resonances and tests of quark model predictions for states in the n=2 shell.
