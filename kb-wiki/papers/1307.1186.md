> A combined study of the reactions $K^-p\rightarrow Œ£^0œÄ^0$, $ŒõœÄ^0$ and $\bar{K}^0n$ at low energies is carried out with a chiral quark-model approach. Furthermore, by analyzing these reactions, we also find that the $u$-, t-channel backgrounds and s-channel Born term play crucial roles in the reactions.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model provides good descriptions of the experimental data for the three reactions, but the authors find that SU(6)‚äóO(3) symmetry breaking is required, as the coupling strength parameters C_R deviate from unity when fitted to the data.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral quark model studies of meson photoproduction and œÄN scattering by Zhong and collaborators (2007, 2009) to a combined analysis of three K‚Åªp reactions using new higher-precision data from Manweiler (2008) and Prakhov (2008). The results address open questions in the spectroscopy of S = -1 hyperons, particularly the classification of states such as Œõ(1405), Œõ(1520), and Œõ(1670) as either excited three-quark states or dynamically generated resonances, as debated in the literature by Klempt (2009) and others. The analysis enables future extraction of information on unestablished hyperon resonances and tests of quark model predictions for states in the n=2 shell.
