# Critical temperature for kaon condensation in color-flavor locked quark matter

> We study the behavior of Goldstone bosons in color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter at nonzero temperature. Chiral symmetry breaking in this phase of cold and dense matter gives rise to pseudo-Goldstone bosons, the lightest of these being the charged and neutral kaons K^+ and K^0. At zero temperature, Bose-Einstein condensation of the kaons occurs. Since all fermions are gapped, this kaon condensed CFL phase can, for energies below the fermionic energy gap, be described by an effective theory for the bosonic modes. We use this effective theory to investigate the melting of the condensate: we determine the temperature-dependent kaon masses self-consistently using the two-particle irreducible effective action, and we compute the transition temperature for Bose-Einstein condensation. Our results are important for studies of transport properties of the kaon condensed CFL phase, such as bulk viscosity.

## Key claim

The critical temperature for kaon condensation in color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter is computed using a self-consistent treatment of temperature-dependent kaon masses, going beyond the ideal gas approximation which fails when the kaon chemical potential exceeds the kaon mass.

## Method

The authors use the two-particle irreducible (CJT) effective action formalism to determine temperature-dependent kaon masses self-consistently, reducing the chiral effective theory for mesons in CFL to an effective phi^4 theory. They solve the self-consistent equations for the K^0 and K^+ masses and condensate numerically, and derive an approximate analytical expression for the critical temperature, considering both condensed and uncondensed kaon phases at nonzero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The quark chemical potential mu is of order 500 MeV for densities expected in neutron star interiors.
- The pion decay constant in the medium is f_pi^2 = (21 - 8 ln 2)/18 * mu^2/pi^2, and the pion velocity is v_pi = 1/sqrt(3).
- The kaon masses are given by m_{K^+}^2 = a m_d (m_s + m_u) and m_{K^0}^2 = a m_u (m_s + m_d), where a = 3 Delta^2/(pi^2 f_pi^2) and Delta is the zero-temperature fermionic energy gap.
- The kaon chemical potentials are mu_{K^+} = mu_Q + (m_s^2 - m_u^2)/(2 mu) and mu_{K^0} = (m_s^2 - m_d^2)/(2 mu).
- At zero temperature, condensation occurs when mu_i^2 > m_i^2, with the condensate given by cos(phi_i/f_pi) = m_i^2/mu_i^2 and free energy density U = -f_pi^2 (m_i^2 - mu_i^2)^2/(2 mu_i^2).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon condensation in CFL quark matter at zero temperature (Son 1999, Bedaque 2001, Kaplan 2001, Schafer 2002) to nonzero temperature, using self-consistent methods from general bosonic systems (Kapusta, Dolan 1973) and the CJT formalism (Cornwall, Jackiw, Tomboulis). The results are important for computing transport properties of the kaon condensed CFL phase, particularly bulk viscosity (Alford 2007), which is relevant for understanding r-mode damping and rotation frequencies of compact stars.
