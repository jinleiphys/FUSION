# Forward-backward multiplicity correlations at the LHC from independent sources

> It is argued that the superposition approach, where partons are independently emitted from longitudinally extended sources in the early stage, is fully compatible with the experimental results for the forward-backward multiplicity correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76 \textrm{ TeV}$. The pertinent correlation analysis is based on the PhD Thesis of Ref. [1], which includes an unpublished analysis of data taken by the ALICE Collaboration. Our calculations show that in the experimentally covered pseudorapidity range $Δη=1.2$, the initial sources in the backward and forward bins are maximally correlated, which complies to the string-like interpretation of the underlying early-stage production mechanism.

# Forward-backward multiplicity correlations at the LHC from independent sources

## Key claim
The superposition approach, where partons are independently emitted from longitudinally extended sources in the early stage, is fully compatible with experimental results for forward-backward multiplicity correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, provided the phenomenological constant δ remains approximately constant across centrality bins and pseudorapidity separations. The analysis shows that initial sources in the backward and forward bins are maximally correlated (ρ(s_F,s_B) ≈ 1) over the experimentally covered pseudorapidity range Δη = 1.2, consistent with a string-like interpretation of the early-stage production mechanism.

## Method
The authors applied a superposition framework with three stages: early production of initial particles from longitudinally extended sources, hydrodynamic or transport evolution in the intermediate phase, and hadron production with detection. Using data digitized from the PhD thesis of Sputowska (2016), which contains unpublished ALICE Collaboration measurements for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, they extracted the δ parameter from Eq. (omr1) using experimental values of the scaled variance ω(n_A) and the forward-backward correlation coefficient ρ(n_F,n_B). They then used the average δ value to compute the source correlation ρ(s_F,s_B) via Eq. (rhos) for various centralities and pseudorapidity separations.

## Key numbers
- The δ parameter values fall within the band 1.1 ± 0.1 for both centrality determination methods (VZERO and ZDCvsZEM) and for various centrality bin widths Δc and centralities c̄, at Δη = 1.2.
- The average value of δ used for subsequent calculations is δ = 1.11.
- The forward-backward correlation of sources ρ(s_F,s_B) is very close to 1 across the covered pseudorapidity range Δη < 1.2 and for all centrality values.
- The analysis uses Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV with forward-backward bin separation Δη = 1.2 (the largest accessible experimentally).

## Context
This work extends the superposition model developed by Olszewski and Broniowski (2013, 2015) to LHC data, confirming that the mechanism of early particle production may be understood as emission from independent sources extending over a wide longitudinal range. The results are consistent with the string model of Capella (1978) and contradict models where sources would be uncorrelated over the pseudorapidity range Δη = 1.2. The analysis enables model-independent tests of specific particle production models using forward-backward correlation data, and shows that the constant δ test works despite the effect of centrality fluctuations that affect standard correlation measures.
