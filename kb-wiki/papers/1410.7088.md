# 2alpha+t cluster feature of $3/2^-_3$ state in $^{11}$B

> We reanalyze $2α+t$ cluster features of $3/2^-$ states in $^{11}$B by investigating the $t$ cluster distribution around a $2α$ core in $^{11}$B, calculated with the method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). In the $3/2^-_3$ state, a $t$ cluster is distributed in a wide region around $2α$, indicating that the $t$ cluster moves rather freely in angular as well as radial motion. From the weak angular correlation and radial extent of the $t$ cluster distribution, we propose an interpretation of a $2α+t$ cluster gas for the $3/2^-_3$ state. In this study, we compare the $2α+t$ cluster feature in $^{11}$B($3/2^-_3$) with the $3α$ cluster feature in $^{12}$C($0^+_2$), and discuss their similarities and differences.

## Key claim

The $3/2^-_3$ state in $^{11}$B is interpreted as a $2\alpha+t$ cluster gas state, analogous to the $3\alpha$ cluster gas state in $^{12}$C($0^+_2$), but with the qualification that the $t$ cluster moves in a $P$-wave orbit (with $L^{(t)}_z=+1$) rather than an $S$-wave, resulting in a node at the $z$-axis that excludes the $\theta=0$ and $180^\circ$ regions from the free angular motion.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed AMD+VAP (antisymmetrized molecular dynamics with variation after spin-parity projection) wave functions for $^{11}$B and $^{12}$C from previous studies, using Brink-Bloch (BB) cluster model wave functions as test functions to measure the $t$-cluster and $\alpha$-cluster probability distributions around a $2\alpha$ core. They calculated the overlap $U(D,\bvec{R})$ between the AMD+VAP wave functions and $J^\pi$-projected BB wave functions for various $2\alpha$ distances $D$ and $t$ (or $\alpha$) positions $\bvec{R}$ on the $z\text{--}x$ plane, using the MV1 central force with G3RS spin-orbit term (m62 and m60 parameter sets).

## Key numbers

* $^{11}$B($3/2^-_3$) matter rms radius: 3.0 fm (m62) and 3.0 fm (m60); charge rms radius: 3.1 fm (m62) and 3.0 fm (m60)
* $^{12}$C($0^+_2$) matter rms radius: 3.3 fm (m62) and 3.0 fm (m60); charge rms radius: 3.4 fm (m62) and 3.2 fm (m60)
* $^{11}$B($3/2^-_1$) ground state matter rms radius: 2.5 fm (m62 and m60); charge rms radius: 2.6 fm (both), experimental charge radius: 2.406(291) fm
* $^{12}$C($0^+_1$) ground state matter rms radius: 2.5 fm (m62 and m60); charge rms radius: 2.7 fm (m62) and 2.6 fm (m60), experimental charge radius: 2.47(22) fm
* $^{12}$C($0^+_3$) matter rms radius: 4.0 fm (m62) and 3.9 fm (m60); charge rms radius: 4.1 fm (m62) and 4.0 fm (m60)
* $^7$Li energy from $\alpha+t$ threshold: $-$0.4 MeV (m62) and $-$0.8 MeV (m60); $^8$Be energy from $2\alpha$ threshold: 1.4 MeV (m62) and 1.2 MeV (m60)
* The $t$ cluster in $^{11}$B($3/2^-_3$) is distributed in the $R=3\text{--}5$ fm region; at $R\ge 5$ fm the angular distribution becomes almost flat for $30^\circ < \theta < 150^\circ$
* The $\alpha$ cluster in $^{12}$C($0^+_2$) shows significant distribution in the $R\ge 6$ fm region where angular distribution becomes isotropic

## Context

This work extends the cluster gas concept from the $3\alpha$ system in $^{12}$C($0^+_2$), originally proposed by Tohsaki et al. (2001), to the $2\alpha+t$ system in $^{11}$B($3/2^-_3$). It addresses two open problems: the negative parity of the $2\alpha+t$ state (contrasting with the original $S$-wave cluster gas idea) and the deeper binding of the $t$-$\alpha$ system compared to $\alpha$-$\alpha$, which reduces the size of $^{11}$B($3/2^-_3$) relative to $^{12}$C($0^+_2$). The analysis uses the body-fixed plane to connect the $P$-wave motion of the $t$ cluster to the $S$-wave motion of the $\alpha$ cluster, enabling an extended cluster gas interpretation. The results enable future searches for cluster gas states in other light nuclei such as $^8$He, $^{10}$Be, and $^{16}$O, as referenced in studies by Kawabata (2005), Kanada-En'yo (2006, 2007), Itagaki (2007), and others.
