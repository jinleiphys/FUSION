# Are these quasi-normal modes?

> We discuss how to extract numerically the expected lowest quasi-normal mode (QNM) associated with the pressure anisotropy for a Bjorken flux evolution to equilibrium. This QNM was easily decoded subtracting the hydrodynamical attractors and was compared with other authors calculations. After evolutions with transients close to the expected lowest QNM the system goes to a tail (pure imaginary frequency) for late times. We analyze the relevance of Navier-Stokes, second order and Borel attractors at each stage of the evolution, which begins far-from-equilibrium and ends close to equilibrium.

## Key claim

The authors claim that by subtracting hydrodynamic attractors (Navier-Stokes, second-order, or Borel) from the pressure anisotropy of a holographic Bjorken flow, they can numerically extract transient oscillatory signals that are close to, but not exactly, the expected lowest quasi-normal mode (QNM) for the system; the extracted QNM appears only transiently before the system transitions to a pure imaginary tail at late times.

## Method

The authors use a characteristic formulation of numerical relativity in 5D asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime to evolve the holographic dual of a strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma undergoing Bjorken flow. They subtract known analytic hydrodynamic attractor expressions (Navier-Stokes, second-order gradient expansion, and Borel resummation) from the numerically computed pressure anisotropy, then fit the residuals to extract decay rates and complex frequencies using the Harminv software. The analysis covers 25 different initial conditions evolved from far-from-equilibrium to near-equilibrium.

## Key numbers

* Decay parameter b = 0.786(4) for the Navier-Stokes attractor subtraction, fitted over the dimensionless time window ω_Λ ∈ [2.0, 3.5] for all 25 initial conditions
* Decay parameter b = 1.73(2) for the second-order attractor subtraction, fitted over the same time window for all 25 initial conditions
* For initial condition #23, a modeled QNM with complex frequency 14.45 − i12.75 was used to visually match the Borel attractor subtraction
* The ratio −Re(ω)/Im(ω) for the centered complex frequency (initial condition #20) extracted via Harminv is approximately 1.1369, compared to the Borel frequency ratio of approximately 1.1345 from Heller, Janik, and Witaszczyk (2013)
* The scale factor relating the extracted complex frequency for initial condition #20 (14.231 − 12.518i) to the expected lowest QNM (3.12 − 2.74i) is approximately 0.219

## Context

This work extends the holographic Bjorken flow studies of Rougemont, Noronha, Barreto, Denicol, and Dore (2021) and Rougemont, Barreto, and Noronha (2022) by analyzing late-time behavior and transient oscillations in the pressure anisotropy. The authors compare their extracted frequencies to the lowest nonhydrodynamic scalar QNM calculated by Janik and Peschanski (2006) and Starinets (2002) (ω_static/πT = 3.1194 − 2.74667i), and to the Borel-resummed frequency of Heller, Janik, and Witaszczyk (2013) (ω_Borel = 3.1193 − 2.7471i). The work also connects to homogeneous isotropization results from Rougemont and Barreto (2024), where the lowest QNM for the quintuplet channel was found to be approximately 9.8 − 8.6i. The results suggest that the Borel attractor subtraction reveals the most persistent QNM signal, and that the system transitions from transient QNM behavior to a pure imaginary tail at late times, consistent with the gravitational radiation picture of Chesler and Yaffe (2009).
