# Estimates for Parameters and Characteristics of the Confining SU(3)-gluonic Field in $ϕ$-meson from Leptonic Widths

> The paper is devoted to applying the confinement mechanism proposed earlier by one of the authors to estimate the possible parameters of the confining SU(3)-gluonic field in vector $ϕ$-meson. The estimates obtained are consistent with the leptonic widths of the given meson. The corresponding estimates of the gluon concentrations, electric and magnetic colour field strengths are also adduced for the mentioned field at the scales of the meson under consideration.

## Key claim

The confining SU(3)-gluonic field parameters for the φ-meson can be estimated from leptonic widths, but the estimates rely on an independent determination of the meson's root-mean-square radius from those widths rather than from the Van Royen–Weisskopf formula, because the latter requires an ambiguous interpretation of the wavefunction at the origin in the three-component framework.

## Method

The authors used the unique spherically symmetric nonperturbative solutions of SU(3) Yang-Mills equations (the "confining solutions") to describe the gluonic field between quarks. They solved the system of equations (8) and (10) compatibly for the φ-meson (mass 1019.455 MeV, strange quark mass 107.5 MeV, gauge coupling g≈3.771) to obtain the field parameters a₁, a₂, b₁, b₂, B₁, B₂, then computed the gluon concentrations, electric and magnetic colour field strengths from the energy-momentum tensor component T₀₀.

## Key numbers

- φ-meson mass: μ = 1019.455 MeV (experimental), theoretical value reproduced as 2m_s + ω_j(0,0,1) = 1019.455 MeV
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 107.5 MeV; reduced mass μ₀ = 53.750 MeV
- Gauge coupling constant: g ≈ 3.771 (from α_s with Λ=0.234 GeV, n_f=3)
- Independent radius estimate from leptonic widths: ⟨r_an⟩ ≈ 0.883 fm (from Γ₉ = 1.27 keV for φ→e⁺e⁻) and ≈ 0.889 fm (from Γ₁₀ = 1.22262 keV for φ→μ⁺μ⁻)
- Theoretical root-mean-square radius from the model: ⟨r⟩ = 0.861 fm
- Chiral-limit φ-meson mass (purely gluonic contribution): (μ)_chiral ≈ 767.740 MeV, corresponding to 75.310% of the total mass
- Gluon concentration at r = r₀ = 0.861 fm: n = 0.139×10⁵⁰ m⁻³ (n_coul = 0.942×10⁴⁹ m⁻³, n_lin = 0.448×10⁴⁹ m⁻³)
- Electric colour field strength at r₀: E = 0.132×10²³ V/m
- Magnetic colour field strength at r₀: H = 0.123×10²¹ A/m; magnetic induction B = 0.154×10¹⁵ T

## Context

This work extends the confinement mechanism previously proposed by Goncharov (2001, 2005) and applied to heavy quarkonia (Goncharov 2003, 2004, 2008) and light mesons including pions, kaons, η and η′ mesons (Goncharov 2006–2012). The approach uses the unique spherically symmetric nonperturbative solutions of SU(3) Yang-Mills equations that satisfy the Wilson confinement criterion (Wilson 1974, Bander 1981). The results enable estimation of gluonic contributions to meson masses and provide quantitative predictions for gluon concentrations and colour field strengths that could be compared with future lattice QCD calculations or phenomenological models of nuclear forces involving vector meson exchange.
