# Strangeness Production in Au--Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4$ GeV

> We obtain strangeness production as function of centrality in a statistical hadronization model analysis of all experimental hadron production data in Au--Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4\GeV$. Our analysis describes successfully the yield of strange and multi-strange hadrons recently published. We explore condition of hadronization as a function of centrality and find universality for the case of chemical non-equilibrium in the hadron phase space corresponding to quark--gluon plasma (QGP) in chemical equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a chemical non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model (SHM) with both light and strange quark phase space occupancies free to vary (γ_q ≠ 1, γ_s ≠ 1) successfully describes all strange and multi-strange hadron yields in Au–Au collisions at √s_NN = 62.4 GeV, contrary to the STAR collaboration's published results which forced γ_s < 1 and γ_q = 1 and therefore invalidated their conclusions.

## Method

The authors performed a statistical hadronization model (SHM) analysis using the SHARE code, fitting absolute hadron yields (not ratios) as a function of centrality, with the number of participants N_part determined via power-law interpolation between different centrality bins. They fitted both a semi-equilibrium model (γ_q = 1, γ_s free) and a full non-equilibrium model (γ_q and γ_s both free) to data including π^±, K^±, p^±, φ, Λ, Ξ, Ω and their antiparticles, while imposing charge and strangeness conservation constraints; due to an inconsistency in K_s^0 yields, they instead fitted the combined yield Λ + Λ̅ + 2·K_s^0 and the ratio Λ̅/Λ.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature in non-equilibrium model: T ≃ 142 MeV
- Strangeness phase space occupancy in non-equilibrium model: γ_s → 1.5 at central rapidity
- Light quark phase space occupancy in non-equilibrium model: γ_q ≃ 1.6
- Fixed hadronization pressure: P ≃ 82 MeV/fm³
- Entropy density at hadronization: σ ≃ 3.3 fm⁻³
- Energy density at hadronization: ε ≃ 0.48 GeV/fm³
- Strangeness-to-entropy ratio for most central collisions: s/S = 0.031
- Energy per baryon for most central collisions: E/B = 16.5 GeV, which equals 0.25 × √s_NN = 62.4 GeV
- Charge per baryon constraint: (Q − Q̅)/(B − B̅) = 0.39 ± 0.01
- Strangeness conservation constraint: (s − s̅)/(s + s̅) = 0 ± 0.05
- All SHM fits at all centralities have confidence level above 60%

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model analysis of Au–Au collisions at √s_NN = 62.4 GeV using newly published multi-strange hadron data from the STAR collaboration (Aggarwal 2010), and contradicts the STAR collaboration's own analysis which forced γ_s < 1 and γ_q = 1. The paper shows that the non-equilibrium SHM approach yields universal hadronization conditions (constant pressure, entropy density, and energy density) across centrality, supporting the picture of a quark–gluon plasma undergoing sudden breakup to hadrons, consistent with earlier findings at other RHIC energies (Rafelski 2009). The results enable further tests of QGP hadronization universality across different collision systems and energies.
