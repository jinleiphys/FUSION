# Momentum dependences of charmonium properties from lattice QCD

> Charmonia produced in initial hard parton scatterings during heavy ion collisions move with respect to the medium rather than flow with the medium. Lattice studies suggest that charmonium bound states at the rest are dissociated at $T\gtrsim 1.5 T_c$. We present results on momentum dependences of charmonium properties in a hot medium from lattice QCD Monte Carlo simulations. The dispersion relation of the screening mass and the change of correlation and spectral functions at various temperatures and momenta are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that for charmonia moving with respect to the thermal medium, the ratio of the screening mass at finite temperature to that at low temperature becomes closer to unity with increasing momentum, and that the longitudinal component of the vector correlation function shows almost no momentum dependence at each temperature, while the transverse and pseudoscalar components show momentum-dependent enhancement.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD Monte Carlo simulations on \(128^3 \times N_{\tau}\) lattices at temperatures of 0.73, 1.46, 2.20, and 2.93 \(T_c\). They computed spatial correlation functions to extract the dispersion relation of the screening mass, and temporal correlation functions to study the momentum dependence of the longitudinal and transverse components of the vector channel and the pseudoscalar channel, using the Maximum Entropy Method to extract spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- Screening mass dispersion relation was measured at four temperatures: 0.73, 1.46, 2.20, and 2.93 \(T_c\) on \(128^3 \times N_{\tau}\) lattices.
- At vanishing momentum, the deviation of the screening mass ratio from unity is around 10% at the lowest temperature (0.73 \(T_c\)) and larger at higher temperatures.
- The ratio of the screening mass at finite temperature to that at 0.73 \(T_c\) approaches unity with increasing momentum, and the free field theory prediction using a charm quark mass of 1.1 GeV reproduces this trend.
- At 1.46 \(T_c\), spectral functions in both the vector and pseudoscalar channels are attenuated in the small \(\omega\) region, and the threshold moves to the large \(\omega\) region.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of charmonium at rest (Ding 2012) to finite momentum, motivated by the experimental observation that \(J/\psi\) does not flow with the medium up to \(p_\perp \sim 8\) GeV/c (Tang 2011, Massacrier 2012). It contrasts with earlier modeling approaches (Haglin 2000) and weak-coupling effective theories (Escobedo 2011, Aarts 2012), as well as AdS/CFT studies (Liu 2006), all of which suggest bound state peaks attenuate at large momenta. The results enable future lattice studies at temperatures below 1.5 \(T_c\) to examine the sequential dissociation picture of charmonia and possible modifications of bound states at finite momentum.
