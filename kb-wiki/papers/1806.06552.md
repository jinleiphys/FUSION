# Isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron pairing in $N>Z$ nuclei

> We propose a particle number conserving formalism for the treatment of isovector-isoscalar pairing in nuclei with $N>Z$. The ground state of the pairing Hamiltonian is described by a quartet condensate to which is appended a pair condensate formed by the neutrons in excess. The quartets are built by two isovector pairs coupled to the total isospin $T=0$ and two collective isoscalar proton-neutron pairs. To probe this ansatz for the ground state we performed calculations for $N>Z$ nuclei with the valence nucleons moving above the cores $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca and $^{100}$Sn. The calculations are done with two pairing interactions, one state-independent and the other of zero range, which are supposed to scatter pairs in time-revered orbits. It is proven that the ground state correlation energies calculated within this approach are very close to the exact results provided by the diagonalization of the pairing Hamiltonian. Based on this formalism we have shown that moving away of N=Z line, both the isoscalar and the isovector proton-neutron pairing correlations remain significant and that they cannot be treated accurately by models based on a proton-neutron pair condensate.

# Isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron pairing in \(N>Z\) nuclei

## Key claim
The ground state of the isovector-isoscalar pairing Hamiltonian for \(N>Z\) nuclei can be accurately described by a quartet condensate (built from two isovector pairs and two collective isoscalar proton-neutron pairs) to which a condensate of neutron pairs in excess is appended, with correlation energy errors under 1% relative to exact diagonalization. Contrary to many BCS-like predictions, both isoscalar and isovector proton-neutron pairing correlations remain significant even when up to six extra neutrons are added to a \(N=Z\) nucleus, and they cannot be treated accurately by models based solely on a proton-neutron pair condensate.

## Method
The authors extend the quartet condensation model (QCM) from \(N=Z\) systems (Negrea et al., 2017) to \(N>Z\) nuclei by constructing a trial state that combines a quartet condensate (built from collective isovector and isoscalar pair operators) with a separate condensate of collective neutron-neutron pairs for the excess neutrons. The mixing amplitudes of the collective pair operators are determined variationally by minimizing the average of the pairing Hamiltonian, with the norm and Hamiltonian expectation values evaluated using recurrence relations derived via symbolic computing. Calculations are performed for valence nucleons moving above the cores \(^{16}\)O, \(^{40}\)Ca, and \(^{100}\)Sn, using two pairing interactions: a state-independent force and a zero-range delta interaction.

## Key numbers
- Correlation energy errors for the QCM ansatz (Eq. 2.6) are under **1%** relative to exact diagonalization for all tested systems.
- For the state-independent interaction, the isovector pairing strength is set to \(V_1 = -24/A\), where \(A\) is the atomic mass number.
- For the isoscalar interaction, two scaling factors are used: \(w = 1.2\) for \(sd\)-shell nuclei and \(w = 0.8\) for heavier nuclei, where \(V_0 = w V_1\).
- For the zero-range delta interaction, the isovector strength is \(V_0^{T=1} = 465\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), with isoscalar scaling \(w = 1.6\) for \(sd\)-shell nuclei and \(w = 1.0\) for heavier nuclei.
- Systems studied include up to **3** quartets (for \(N=Z\) nuclei) and up to **3** additional neutron pairs (for \(N>Z\) nuclei) above each core.

## Context
This work extends the quartet condensation model (QCM) previously developed for isovector pairing in \(N>Z\) nuclei (Negrea et al., 2017) and for combined isovector-isoscalar pairing in \(N=Z\) nuclei (Negrea et al., 2017). It directly tests the validity of the ansatz against exact diagonalization for the same pairing Hamiltonians used in BCS-like studies by Goodman (1999, 2001) and Gezerlis et al. (2011). The results contradict predictions from generalized Bogoliubov approaches that suggest a rapid suppression of proton-neutron pairing away from the \(N=Z\) line, and demonstrate that pair-condensate-only approximations (either isovector or isoscalar) yield much larger errors. The formalism enables particle-number-conserving and isospin-conserving calculations for medium-mass and heavy \(N>Z\) nuclei, providing a benchmark for future studies of proton-neutron pairing phases.
