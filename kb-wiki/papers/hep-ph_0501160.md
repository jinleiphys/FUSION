# Photon-meson transition form factors of light pseudoscalar mesons

> The photon-meson transition form factors of light pseudoscalar mesons $π^{0}$, $η$, and $η^{\prime}$ are systematically calculated in a light-cone framework, which is applicable as a light-cone quark model at low $Q^{2}$ and is also physically in accordance with the light-cone pQCD approach at large $Q^{2}$. The calculated results agree with the available experimental data at high energy scale. We also predict the low $Q^{2}$ behaviors of the photon-meson transition form factors of $π^{0}$, $η$ and $η^{\prime }$, which are measurable in $e+A({Nucleus})\to e+A+M$ process via Primakoff effect at JLab and DESY.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their light-cone framework, which uses minimal quark-antiquark Fock states for both the photon and meson, can describe photon-meson transition form factors for \(\pi^{0}\), \(\eta\), and \(\eta^{\prime}\) at both low and high \(Q^{2}\) within a single formalism, but this claim is qualified by the use of a Gaussian-type BHL wavefunction that is a non-relativistic solution extended to a relativistic form, and by the assumption that the \(\eta_{8}\)-\(\eta_{0}\) mixing scheme with uniform parameters for octet mesons is appropriate.

## Method

The authors calculate the transition form factors \(F_{\gamma^{*}\gamma \rightarrow M}(Q^{2})\) for \(M = \pi^{0}, \eta, \eta^{\prime}\) using light-cone perturbation theory, employing the Drell-Yan-West assignment and the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage (BHL) Gaussian wavefunction for the meson momentum-space wavefunction. For \(\eta\) and \(\eta^{\prime}\), they adopt the \(\eta_{8}\)-\(\eta_{0}\) mixing scheme, determining the mixing angle \(\theta\) and decay constants \(f_{8}, f_{0}\) via a limiting method using experimental two-photon decay widths and the high-\(Q^{2}\) asymptotic behavior of the form factors.

## Key numbers

*   Pion two-photon decay width: \(\Gamma(\pi^{0} \rightarrow \gamma\gamma) = 7.74 \pm 0.54~\text{eV}\) (from PDG).
*   Eta two-photon decay width (including Primakoff measurement): \(\Gamma(\eta \rightarrow \gamma\gamma) = 0.46 \pm 0.04~\text{keV}\) (from PDG).
*   Eta-prime two-photon decay width: \(\Gamma(\eta^{\prime} \rightarrow \gamma\gamma) = 4.29 \pm 0.15~\text{keV}\) (from PDG).
*   Mixing angle (using PDG data including Primakoff): \(\theta = -14.7^{\circ} \pm 2.0^{\circ}\).
*   Mixing angle (excluding Primakoff measurement): \(\theta = -16.1^{\circ} \pm 1.5^{\circ}\).
*   Decay constants (from PDG data including Primakoff): \(f_{0} = 1.13 \pm 0.08 f_{\pi}\) and \(f_{8} = 0.97 \pm 0.07 f_{\pi}\).
*   Mixing angle in quark-flavor basis: \(\phi = 38.6^{\circ}\), compatible with the value \(\phi = 39.3^{\circ} \pm 1.0^{\circ}\) from Feldmann et al. (1999).
*   CLEO pole fit results: \(\Lambda_{\pi} = 776 \pm 10 \pm 12 \pm 16~\text{MeV}\), \(\Lambda_{\eta} = 774 \pm 11 \pm 16 \pm 22~\text{MeV}\), and \(\Lambda_{\eta^{\prime}} = 859 \pm 9 \pm 18 \pm 20~\text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the light-cone framework previously applied to the pion-photon transition form factor by Xiao and Ma (2003) to the \(\eta\) and \(\eta^{\prime}\) mesons, using the \(\eta_{8}\)-\(\eta_{0}\) mixing scheme. It provides predictions for low \(Q^{2}\) (0.001-0.5 GeV\(^2\)) behaviors measurable via the Primakoff effect at JLab (PrimEx Collaboration) and DESY (HERMES Collaboration), and aims to clarify the disagreement between Primakoff and collider measurements of \(\Gamma(\eta \rightarrow \gamma\gamma)\). The approach is contrasted with chiral perturbation theory and other methods, and the mixing angle results are compared with those of Feldmann et al. (1999) and Cao and Signal (1999).
