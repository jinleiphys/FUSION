# Meson-baryon scattering to one-loop order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the T-matrices of pseudoscalar meson octet-baryon scattering to one-loop order in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The pertinent combinations of low-energy constants are determined by fitting to phase shifts of $πN$ and $K N$ scattering and the corresponding data. By using these low-energy constants, we obtain the strong phase shift difference of $πΛ$ scattering at the $Ξ$ mass, $δ_P-δ_S=(8.8 \pm 0.2)$ degrees, in agreement with experimental results. We find that the phase shifts in the $S_{01}$($πΣ$), $S_{01}$($K Ξ$), $S_{01}$($\overline{K} N$), and $S_{11}$($\overline{K} Σ$) waves are so strong that resonances may be generated dynamically in all these channels. We also predict the scattering lengths and make comparisons with the results obtained from the threshold T-matrices in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and the method of covariant infrared regularization. The issue of convergence is also discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The authors calculate T-matrices for all pseudoscalar meson octet-baryon scattering channels to one-loop order in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the phase shift predictions for πN P-waves above 100 MeV pion lab-momentum fail in SU(3) HBχPT due to large loop effects from internal kaon lines that cannot be absorbed in counterterms, whereas SU(2) HBχPT performs better in that regime.

## Method

The authors computed elastic meson-baryon scattering T-matrices in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory up to one-loop order (third chiral order), including tree-level diagrams at leading and next-to-leading order and one-loop diagrams at next-to-next-to-leading order. Sixteen low-energy constant combinations (B₁,₂,₃, C₁–₉, h₁–₄) were determined by fitting to S- and P-wave phase shifts of πN scattering (WI08 solution) and KN scattering (SP92 solution) at low momenta, using a common ±4% uncertainty for most phase shifts and ±20% for the πN P₁₁ wave. The remaining two parameters b₄ and b₈ were taken from coupled-channel analyses in the literature.

## Key numbers

* Strong phase shift difference of πΛ scattering at the Ξ mass: δ_P − δ_S = (8.8 ± 0.2) degrees, in agreement with experimental results.
* Fitted LEC combinations in SU(3) HBχPT: B₁⁽³⁾ = −2.80 ± 0.55 GeV⁻¹, C₁⁽³⁾ = −8.29 ± 0.12 GeV⁻¹, C₂⁽³⁾ = 6.55 ± 0.48 GeV⁻¹, C₃⁽³⁾ = 2.82 ± 0.05 GeV⁻¹, with χ²/dof ≃ 1.0.
* Fitted LEC combinations for KN channels: B₂ = −0.43 ± 1.91 GeV⁻¹, C₄ = −2.04 ± 0.05 GeV⁻¹, C₅ = 2.35 ± 3.79 GeV⁻¹, C₆ = 8.29 ± 0.08 GeV⁻¹, with χ²/dof ≃ 0.9; B₃ = −0.97 ± 0.07 GeV⁻¹, C₇ = −1.41 ± 0.04 GeV⁻¹, C₈ = 3.35 ± 0.13 GeV⁻¹, C₉ = 4.95 ± 0.05 GeV⁻¹, with χ²/dof ≃ 0.4.
* Scattering lengths used for consistency checks: a_πN⁽³/²⁾ ≃ −0.11 fm, a_πN⁽¹/²⁾ ≃ 0.24 fm (WI08 solution), a_KN⁽¹⁾ ≃ −0.33 fm, a_KN⁽⁰⁾ ≃ 0.00 fm (SP92 solution).
* Physical constants used: m_π = 139.57 MeV, m_K = 493.68 MeV, f_π = 92.07 MeV, f_K = 110.03 MeV, M_N = 938.9 MeV, M_Λ = 1115.7 MeV, M_Σ = 1193.2 MeV, M_Ξ = 1318.3 MeV, D = 0.8, F = 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SU(3) HBχPT study of KN and anti-KN scattering (Huang et al., 2015) to all pseudoscalar meson octet-baryon channels, fitting simultaneously to πN and KN phase shifts. It goes beyond earlier SU(3) HBχPT calculations of scattering lengths (Kaiser, 2001; Liu et al., 2007, 2011, 2012) and covariant infrared regularization results (Mai, 2009) by also computing partial-wave phase shifts, which contain more complete scattering information. The finding that phase shifts in S₀₁(πΣ), S₀₁(KΞ), S₀₁(anti-K N), and S₁₁(anti-K Σ) waves are strong enough to dynamically generate resonances enables future studies of resonance formation in these channels, while the convergence issues identified in SU(3) HBχPT for πN P-waves motivate further development of relativistic approaches.
