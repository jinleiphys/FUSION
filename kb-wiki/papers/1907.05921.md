# Matter And Gravitation In Collisions of heavy ions and neutron stars: equation of state

> The gravitational waves emitted from a binary neutron star merger, as predicted from general relativistic magneto-hydrodynamics calculations, are sensitive to the appearance of quark matter and the stiffness of the equation of state of QCD matter present in the inner cores of the stars. This is a new messenger observable from outer space, which does provide direct signals for the phase structure of strongly interacting QCD matter at high baryon density and high temperature. These astrophysically created extremes of thermodynamics do match, to within 20\%, the values of densities and temperatures which we find in relativistic hydrodynamics and transport theory of heavy ion collisions at the existing laboratories, if though at quite different rapidity windows, impact parameters and bombarding energies of the heavy nuclear systems. We demonstrate how one unified equation of state can be constructed and used for both neutron star physics and hot QCD matter excited at laboratory facilities. The similarity in underlying QCD physics allows the gravitational wave signals from future advanced LIGO and Virgo events to be combined with the analysis of high multiplicity fluctuations and flow measurements in heavy ion detectors in the lab to pin down the EoS and the phase structure of dense matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a single unified equation of state (the CMF model) can describe both neutron star physics and hot QCD matter created in heavy ion collisions, with the caveat that the densities and temperatures reached in these two scenarios match to within 20% but occur at quite different rapidity windows, impact parameters, and bombarding energies.

## Method

The authors construct the Chiral SU(3)-flavor parity-doublet Polyakov-loop quark-hadron mean-field (CMF) model, which includes both hadronic and quark degrees of freedom with chiral symmetry restoration implemented via parity doubling for baryons. The model parameters are tuned to reproduce lattice QCD data at vanishing chemical potential and empirical cold nuclear matter properties, then applied to both heavy ion collision trajectories (calculated using the relativistic Rankine-Hugoniot-Taub adiabat for isentropic expansion) and neutron star structure (solved via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation with beta-equilibrium and electric neutrality conditions).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear ground state density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3; binding energy per nucleon: E/B = -15.2 MeV; asymmetry energy: S0 = 31.9 MeV; compressibility: K0 = 267 MeV
- At T=0, chiral symmetry restoration occurs at nB ≈ 4 n0, with pure quark matter appearing only at nB ≳ 22 n0
- The chiral critical point lies at μB ≈ 1.5 GeV and T ≈ 20 MeV
- Heavy ion collisions at Elab ≈ 2 A GeV reach T ≈ 70 MeV, μB ≈ 1.2 GeV, and nB/n0 ≈ 3, with about 20% of dense matter transformed to quarks
- Maximum stable neutron star central density: nB = 6 n0
- Temperatures in neutron star mergers reach up to T ≈ 75 MeV in hot outer layers, while cold cores remain at T ≲ 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the CMF model approach previously developed by Steinheimer (2010) and Motornenko (2019) to unify the description of QCD matter across laboratory and astrophysical settings. It builds on lattice QCD calculations by Borsanyi (2013) and Bazavov (2014) for the equation of state at zero chemical potential, and connects to gravitational wave observations of the GW170817 event detected by LIGO and Virgo. The results enable combined analysis of gravitational wave signals from future advanced LIGO and Virgo events with high multiplicity fluctuations and flow measurements from heavy ion detectors at facilities such as SIS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC to determine the equation of state and phase structure of dense matter.
