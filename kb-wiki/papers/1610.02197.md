# New exact solutions of hydrodynamics for rehadronizing fireballs with lattice QCD equation of state

> We describe fireballs that rehadronize from a perfectly fluid quark matter to a chemically frozen, multi-component hadron gas. In the hydrodynamics of these fireballs, we utilize the lattice QCD equation of state, however, we also apply non-relativistic kinematics for simplicity and clarity. Two new classes of exact, analytic solutions of fireball hydrodynamics are presented: the first class describes triaxially expanding, non-rotating ellipsoidal fireballs, while the second class of exact solutions corresponds to spheroidally symmetric, rotating fireballs. In both classes of solutions, we find evidence for a secondary explosion, that happens just after hadrochemical freeze-out. A realistic, linear mass scaling of the slope parameters of the single particle spectra of various hadronic species is obtained analytically, as well as an also realistic, linear mass scaling of the inverse of the squared HBT radius parameters of the Bose-Einstein correlation functions.

# New exact solutions of hydrodynamics for rehadronizing fireballs with lattice QCD equation of state

## Key claim

The paper presents two new classes of exact, analytic solutions of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics that describe the transition from a perfectly fluid quark matter to a chemically frozen, multi-component hadron gas, using a lattice QCD equation of state. The authors find evidence for a secondary explosion occurring just after hadrochemical freeze-out, but this result depends on the assumption of a locally homogeneous temperature profile and non-relativistic kinematics.

## Method

The authors solve the equations of perfect fluid hydrodynamics for two distinct phases: a quark matter (QM) phase where entropy is conserved, and a chemically frozen, multi-component hadronic matter (HM) phase where each hadron species is locally conserved. They assume a lattice QCD equation of state with temperature-dependent speed of sound, non-relativistic kinematics, and parametric forms for the velocity and density fields with Gaussian spatial profiles. Two classes of solutions are constructed: triaxially expanding non-rotating ellipsoidal fireballs, and spheroidally symmetric rotating fireballs, with matching boundary conditions at the chemical freeze-out temperature.

## Key numbers

- Average mass of hadrons in the multi-component hadronic matter: ⟨m⟩ ≈ 280 MeV, based on 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Kaneta 2004)
- Chemical freeze-out temperature range: T_chem ≈ 150–170 MeV from experimental data (Kaneta 2004)
- Acceleration coefficient in QM phase at T_chem: 1/(1+κ(T_chem)) ≈ 0.11–0.15
- Acceleration coefficient in HM phase at T_chem: T_chem/⟨m⟩ ≈ 0.55–0.63
- Temperature of maximum κ(T) from lattice QCD: T_max ≈ 151 MeV
- Critical temperature used for EoS parametrization: T_c = 175 MeV
- χ²/NDF for lQCD parametrization: 0.12/5 with confidence level >99.9%
- χ²/NDF for fit with κ_Q = 3.833: 6.48/4 with confidence level 16.6%
- χ²/NDF for fit with T_f = 140 MeV: 86.56/6 with confidence level 1.6×10⁻¹⁴
- χ²/NDF for fit with T_f = 100 MeV: 7.71/6 with confidence level 26.0%

## Context

This work extends earlier exact solutions of fireball hydrodynamics, starting from the Zimányi-Bondorf-Garpman solution (1978) for spherical non-relativistic fireballs, through generalizations to ellipsoidal symmetries (Akkelin 2000, Csörgő 2001) and rotating solutions (Nagy 2007, Csörgő 2013, 2015). The key advance is the generalization from single-component to multi-component chemically frozen hadronic matter, using a lattice QCD equation of state from Borsanyi (2010). The results enable analytic calculation of experimentally observable linear mass scaling of inverse slope parameters and HBT radii for multiple hadron species simultaneously, and provide a new parametrization of the lattice QCD equation of state that allows for future exact solutions with inhomogeneous temperature profiles.
