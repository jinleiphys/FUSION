# Re-examining valence quark spin distributions

> The observed deep inelastic nucleon spin asymmetries $A_{1}^{p,n}$ and the spin dependent quark distributions break SU(6) spin - flavour symmetry. In quark models two mechanisms for breaking SU(6) symmetry are well known: the hyperfine interaction and the pion cloud of the nucleon. I re-evaluate these mechanisms and show how the breaking of SU(6) in each case affects spin dependent valence quark distributions of the proton. In particular I investigate the properties of these mechanisms in the kinematic region $0.3 < x < 0.7$ and attempt to test these against known data. Both mechanisms are able to explain the quantitative features of the spin asymmetries.

## Key claim

The paper re-evaluates two mechanisms for breaking SU(6) spin-flavour symmetry, the hyperfine interaction and the pion cloud of the nucleon, and shows that both can explain the quantitative features of the spin asymmetries, but that in the kinematic region \(0.3 < x < 0.7\) the pion cloud has the larger effect and is able to reproduce many known features of experimental data, whereas the hyperfine interaction alone does not necessarily generate large enough SU(6) breaking.

## Method

The author calculates the mixing between the nucleon and the \(N^{*}(70)\) excited state induced by the hyperfine interaction (one-gluon exchange) and by one-pion loops in the cloudy bag model (CBM), using MIT bag model wavefunctions with \(1s\) and \(2s\) quark states. The resulting mixed wavefunctions are used to compute the distortion term \(w(x)\) in the valence quark distributions via a Peierls-Yoccoz projection of the bag wavefunctions, and the spin-dependent valence distributions are obtained including relativistic spin-flip corrections.

## Key numbers

- The hyperfine mixing angle in the MIT bag model is \(\theta \approx +0.002\), compared to \(\theta \approx -0.23\) in the non-relativistic quark model.
- The one-pion loop contribution to the \(N-\Delta\) splitting is 53 MeV for a bag radius of 5 GeV\(^{-1}\) (0.99 fm).
- The mixing angle from the combined pion-loop and OGE calculation in the CBM is \(\theta = -0.033\) for \(R = 5\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(\theta = -0.036\) for \(R = 4\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The mass eigenvalues from the CBM calculation for \(R = 5\) GeV\(^{-1}\) are \(M_N = 0.821\) GeV, \(M_{N^*(56)} = 1.375\) GeV, and \(M_{N^*(70)} = 1.472\) GeV.
- The distortion term \(w(x)\) peaks around \(x = 0.5\) to \(0.6\), and its integral over positive \(x\) is \(0.1\) to \(0.2\), compared to the first moment of the valence distribution \(\langle v(x) \rangle = 0.87\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Isgur (1999) on SU(6) breaking in valence quark spin distributions, and contrasts the perturbative hyperfine approach of Close (1973) and Carlitz and Kaur (1977) with the exact hyperfine basis method. The author shows that the hyperfine mixing angle is highly model-dependent, being very small in the MIT bag model compared to the non-relativistic quark model of Isgur and Karl (1978). The pion cloud mechanism, previously used to explain the Gottfried sum rule violation (NMC, 1990) and the neutron charge radius (Thomas, 1984), is shown to produce a larger mixing and a distortion term \(w(x)\) that peaks at higher \(x\) than the valence distribution, enabling future tests against experimental data in the region \(x > 0.7\) and comparisons with pentaquark admixtures in excited nucleon states.
