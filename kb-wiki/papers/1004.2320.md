# Perturbative interpretation of relativistic symmetries in nuclei

> Perturbation theory is used systematically to investigate the symmetries of the Dirac Hamiltonian and their breaking in atomic nuclei. Using the perturbation corrections to the single-particle energies and wave functions, the link between the single-particle states in realistic nuclei and their counterparts in the symmetry limits is discussed. It is shown that the limit of S-V=const and relativistic harmonic oscillator (RHO) potentials can be connected to the actual Dirac Hamiltonian by the perturbation method, while the limit of S+V=const cannot, where S and V are the scalar and vector potentials, respectively. This indicates that the realistic system can be treated as a perturbation of spin-symmetric Hamiltonians, and the energy splitting of the pseudospin doublets can be regarded as a result of small perturbation around the Hamiltonian with RHO potentials, where the pseudospin doublets are quasidegenerate.

## Key claim

Perturbation theory can connect the realistic Dirac Hamiltonian in nuclei to the symmetry limits of S-V=const and relativistic harmonic oscillator (RHO) potentials, but not to the S+V=const limit; the energy splitting of pseudospin doublets can be regarded as a small perturbation around the Hamiltonian with RHO potentials, where the pseudospin doublets are quasidegenerate.

## Method

The authors split the Dirac Hamiltonian H into an unperturbed part H0 (which exhibits exact spin or pseudospin symmetry) and a symmetry-breaking perturbation W, using Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory. They then compute first-, second-, and third-order perturbation corrections to single-particle energies and wave functions for neutrons in the nucleus 132Sn, using self-consistent relativistic mean field (RMF) potentials from the PK1 effective interaction. The criterion |W_mk/(E_k-E_m)| << 1 is checked to assess whether the perturbation series converges.

## Key numbers

* The scalar-vector combination Delta(r) = S(r)-V(r) is approximately 700 MeV in realistic nuclei, while Sigma(r) = S(r)+V(r) is approximately 70 MeV.
* For the spin symmetry case, the largest value of |W_mk/(E_m-E_k)| is roughly 0.10 (from H0^SS to H) or 0.06 (from H to H0^SS).
* For the pseudospin symmetry case with S+V=const, the largest value of |W_mk/(E_m-E_k)| is about 0.6.
* For the RHO potential case, the largest perturbation correction is roughly 0.16 (from H0^RHO to H) or 0.10 (from H to H0^RHO).
* The constant potentials chosen for the calculations are -Delta_0-M = -350 MeV and Sigma_0+M = 900 MeV for the SU(2) symmetry limits, and c_0+M = 865 MeV with c_2 = 1.00 MeV/fm^2 for the RHO potential.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pseudospin symmetry in relativistic mean field theory, notably the work of Ginocchio (1997) who identified S+V=const as the exact pseudospin symmetry limit, and the work of Typel (2008) and Marcos (2008) who suggested that pseudospin doublet degeneracy in RHO potentials could explain the quasi-degeneracy in realistic nuclei. The paper provides a quantitative perturbative framework to test these ideas, showing that the S+V=const limit is non-perturbative while the RHO potential approach is perturbatively connected to realistic nuclei. This enables future studies of symmetry limits in systems with non-local potentials or tensor interactions, such as those in relativistic Hartree-Fock approaches.
