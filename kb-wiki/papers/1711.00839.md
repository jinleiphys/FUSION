# Valence Quark and Gluon Distributions of Kaon from J/Psi Production

> The only experimental information on the parton distribution of kaons was obtained from the scarce kaon-induced Drell-Yan cross section data. From these data, evidence for a valence $u$ quark distribution of kaon softer than that of pion was found. We study the feasibility to extract kaon's valence quark distribution via the existing kaon-induced J/psi production data. We compare the NA3 data on the K- / pi- and K+ / pi+ ratios for J/psi production on a platinum target with theoretical calculations based on the color-evaporation model. We show that the NA3 data on J/psi production provide an independent evidence for a valence $u$ quark distribution softer in kaon than in pion. The data also suggest different gluon distributions for kaon and pion.

## Key claim

The existing NA3 data on kaon-induced J/psi production provide an independent experimental evidence that the valence u quark distribution of the kaon is softer than that of the pion, and also suggest that the gluon distribution in the kaon differs from that in the pion, although the analysis relies on the color-evaporation model and the statistical precision of the data is limited.

## Method

The authors compared the NA3 Collaboration's measured ratios of K- to pi- and K+ to pi+ cross sections for J/psi production on a platinum target at 150 GeV/c and 200 GeV/c, respectively, with theoretical calculations based on the color-evaporation model. They used the SMRS (set 2) parton distribution function parametrizations for both kaon and pion, and the CTEQ5L parametrization for the nucleon PDFs in the platinum target. To test the effect of a softer valence u quark distribution in the kaon, they introduced a modified kaon valence-quark PDF where the u-bar valence distribution is multiplied by a factor (1-x)^a and the strange valence distribution is divided by the same factor, with the best-fit value a = 0.203 determined from a prior Drell-Yan analysis.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit value for the exponent a describing the softening of the u-bar valence quark distribution in K- relative to pi- is a = 0.203 ± 0.06, determined from the NA3 Drell-Yan data at 150 GeV/c on a platinum target.
* The NA3 Drell-Yan data comprised approximately 700 dimuon events from K- and 21,200 events from pi- in the dimuon mass range 4.1 ≤ M ≤ 8.5 GeV.
* For the K+/pi+ J/psi production ratio at 200 GeV/c, a calculation with the gluon distribution in kaon increased so that 45% of kaon's momentum is carried by gluons (rather than the nominal 40%) and the sea-quark distribution decreased to 10% (rather than 15%) gives a better description of the data.
* The factor F in the color-evaporation model for pi- + p J/psi production is F = 0.325, giving good agreement with the NA3 data.

## Context

This work extends the only previous experimental information on kaon parton distributions, which came from the scarce kaon-induced Drell-Yan data of the NA3 Collaboration (1980s) that first indicated a softer valence u quark distribution in kaon. The analysis uses the color-evaporation model, which has been shown by Barger, Falciano, Vogt, Peng (1995), and Gavai (1995) to describe many features of hadronic J/psi production. The results enable future global analyses of both Drell-Yan and J/psi production data to extract quark and gluon distributions in kaon, and the prospect for pursuing such measurements with an intense RF-separated kaon beam is under active consideration.
