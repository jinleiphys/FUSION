# Deuteron VVCS and nuclear structure effects in muonic deuterium at N3LO in pionless EFT

> We present our studies of the forward unpolarised doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) off the deuteron and the closely related two-photon-exchange ($2γ$-exchange) corrections to the Lamb shift of muonic deuterium. The deuteron VVCS amplitude is calculated in the framework of pionless effective field theory, up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) for the longitudinal and next-to-leading order (NLO) for the transverse amplitude. The charge elastic form factor of the deuteron, obtained from the residue of the longitudinal VVCS amplitude, is used to extract the value of the single unknown two-nucleon one-photon contact coupling that enters the longitudinal amplitude at N3LO. The obtained deuteron VVCS amplitude serves as a high-precision model-independent input to examine the $2γ$-exchange corrections. Substantial differences with the recent dispersive evaluations are identified, namely, the elastic contribution appears to be larger by several standard deviations, thus ameliorating the current discrepancy between theory and experiment on the size of $2γ$-exchange effects. A correlation between the values of the deuteron charge and Friar radii is found that can be used to judge on the quality of a parametrisation of the deuteron charge elastic form factor. The discrepancy between the theory and the empirical result for the $2γ$-exchange correction in muonic deuterium appears to be completely eliminated. To further confirm this, we revisit the hydrogen-deuterium isotope shift in the same framework. Our work provides an alternative self-consistent and high-precision evaluation of the $2γ$-exchange correction in (muonic) deuterium.

# Deuteron VVCS and nuclear structure effects in muonic deuterium at N3LO in pionless EFT

## Key claim

The two-photon-exchange correction to the Lamb shift of muonic deuterium, calculated in pionless effective field theory (piEFT) up to N3LO, completely eliminates the previously reported discrepancy between theory and the empirical result, provided the elastic contribution is evaluated using a deuteron charge form factor that satisfies a newly identified correlation between the charge radius and the Friar radius.

## Method

The forward unpolarised doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) amplitude off the deuteron was calculated in pionless effective field theory using the z-parametrisation scheme, with the longitudinal amplitude computed up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) and the transverse amplitude up to next-to-leading order (NLO). The single unknown two-nucleon one-photon contact coupling entering the longitudinal amplitude at N3LO was extracted from the elastic charge form factor of the deuteron, using the residue of the longitudinal VVCS amplitude. The resulting VVCS amplitude was then used to evaluate the two-photon-exchange corrections to the Lamb shift of muonic deuterium, with the truncation uncertainty estimated via Bayesian inference.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding momentum: gamma = 45 MeV
- N3LO contact coupling value: l1^{C0_S} = -1.80(38) x 10^{-3}
- Elastic contribution to the 2S state two-photon-exchange correction in muonic deuterium from piEFT: -0.4463(77) meV; from the Abbott et al. (2000) parametrisation: -0.417(2) meV; from the Sick (1998) parametrisation: -0.4456(18) meV; from chiral EFT: -0.451 meV
- Inelastic contribution to the 2S state two-photon-exchange correction in muonic deuterium from piEFT: -1.509(16) meV
- Total two-photon-exchange correction to the 2S state in muonic deuterium from piEFT: -1.752(20) meV
- Empirical two-photon-exchange correction from Pohl et al. (2016): -1.7638(68) meV
- Updated empirical two-photon-exchange correction from reanalysis of hydrogen-deuterium isotope shift: -1.7585(56) meV
- Deuteron charge radius extracted from muonic deuterium using piEFT result: 2.12763(78) fm
- Deuteron charge radius from muonic hydrogen plus isotope shift: 2.12788(16) fm

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless effective field theory to two-photon-exchange corrections in muonic deuterium, providing an alternative to dispersive evaluations such as those by Carlson et al. (2013) and Acharya et al. (2020), and to nuclear Hamiltonian approaches by Friar (1977), Pachucki (2011, 2015), and Hernandez et al. (2014, 2017). The identified correlation between the deuteron charge radius and Friar radius serves as a diagnostic for the quality of empirical form factor parametrisations, and the results enable improved extraction of the deuteron charge radius from muonic atom spectroscopy.
