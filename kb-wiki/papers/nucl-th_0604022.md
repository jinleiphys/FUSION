# Pionic Color Transparency

> We use a semi-classical approximation to investigate the effects of color transparency on pion electroproduction reactions. The resulting reduced nuclear interactions produce significant, but not dominating, differences with the results of conventional distorted-wave, Glauber-type treatments at kinematics accessible to Jefferson Laboratory. Nuclear effects that could mimic the influence of color transparency are also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors use a semi-classical approximation to show that color transparency effects in pion electroproduction produce "significant, but not dominating" differences from conventional Glauber-type calculations at kinematics accessible to Jefferson Laboratory, with the caveat that nuclear effects (such as the EMC effect and Fermi motion) could mimic the influence of color transparency.

## Method

The authors compute nuclear transparency T = A_eff/A using a semi-classical formula that integrates over the nuclear density (Woods-Saxon form with R = 1.1 fm A^(1/3) and diffuseness a = 0.54 fm) and an effective interaction sigma_eff that describes the expanding point-like configuration (PLC) for distances less than a coherence length l_c, then the full pion-nucleon cross section beyond. The PLC cross section is taken as sigma_PLC = sigma_piN * (n^2 <k_t^2> / Q^2) with n=2 and <k_t^2>^(1/2) = 0.35 GeV, and the coherence length is l_c = 2 p_pi / Delta M^2 with Delta M^2 = 0.7 GeV^2 as the primary value. The calculations are evaluated at the specific kinematics of the JLAB experiment (Garrow), using targets 12C, 64Cu, and 197Au.

## Key numbers

- The Glauber result for 12C gives T approximately 0.6, similar numerically to the value measured for protons in (e,e'p) with 12C.
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear density parameters used are R = 1.1 fm A^(1/3) and diffuseness a = 0.54 fm.
- The PLC cross section parameter uses n = 2 and <k_t^2>^(1/2) approximately 0.35 GeV.
- Two values of Delta M^2 are used: 0.7 GeV^2 (primary) and 1.4 GeV^2 (lower limit estimate, corresponding to expansion time c t_exp = 0.16 fm in the rest frame).
- The kinematics table lists 14 data points with Q^2 values ranging from 1 to 5 GeV^2, pion momenta p_pi from 1.877 to 4.571 GeV, and three-momentum transfer k from 0.23 to 0.88 GeV.
- Fermi motion effects shift the peak pion momentum by approximately 50 MeV/c with a spread of about 200 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier theory from a workshop (Ref. workshop) by evaluating at the specific kinematics of the JLAB experiment (Garrow), and includes expansion effects absent from the work of Kundu (1998). The paper discusses nuclear effects that could mimic color transparency, including the nuclear EMC effect (which suppresses quarks at large Bjorken x) and Fermi motion of the struck nucleon. The authors note that comparisons between heavy nuclei (A >= 12) may be more useful than comparisons with the deuteron, since the EMC effect is similar for all nuclei with mass as great as Carbon. The work enables future studies at the higher beam energies planned for JLab12, which would greatly extend the possibility of clean observation of color transparency.
