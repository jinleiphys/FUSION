# In-medium Eta-Nucleon interactions and Eta nuclear bound states

> The in-medium Eta-N interaction near and below threshold is constructed from a free-space chirally-inspired meson-baryon coupled-channel model that captures the physics of the N(1535) baryon resonance. Nucleon Pauli blocking and hadron self-energies are accounted for. The resulting energy dependent in-medium interaction is used in self-consistent dynamical calculations of Eta nuclear bound states. Narrow states of width about or less than 2 MeV are found across the periodic table, beginning with A=10, for this in-medium coupled-channel interaction model. The binding energy of the 1s-Eta state increases with A, reaching a value of B(1s-Eta) about 15 MeV. The implications of our self-consistency procedure are discussed with respect to procedures used in other works.

## Key claim

The paper claims that narrow eta-nuclear bound states (widths about or less than 2 MeV) exist across the periodic table starting from A=10, but this result depends on using a specific chirally-inspired in-medium coupled-channel interaction model (NLO30_eta of Cieply and Smejkal) that includes both Pauli blocking and hadron self-energies; the widths and binding energies are sensitive to the choice of in-medium interaction model and to the treatment of self-energies.

## Method

The authors constructed in-medium eta-N scattering amplitudes from the free-space chirally-inspired NLO30_eta coupled-channel model by incorporating nucleon Pauli blocking and hadron self-energies (for pions, kaons, nucleons, hyperons, and the eta meson itself) into the intermediate-state Green's function of the Lippmann-Schwinger equations. These in-medium amplitudes were then used in self-consistent dynamical calculations solving the Klein-Gordon equation for the eta meson coupled to relativistic mean-field equations for the nucleons, with the Mandelstam variable s evaluated at subthreshold energies including binding and kinetic energy contributions from both the eta and nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Widths of eta-nuclear bound states are about or less than 2 MeV across the periodic table beginning with A >= 10 for the in-medium coupled-channel interaction model
- The 1s_eta binding energy reaches approximately 15 MeV for heavy nuclei
- Including hadron self-energies reduces binding energies by 2-3 MeV compared to calculations with Pauli blocking only
- The downward subthreshold energy shift for the 1s_eta state in Ca ranges within 55 +/- 10 MeV at nuclear central densities
- The free-space eta-N scattering length in the CS model is a_etaN = 0.264 + i 0.245 fm (from the underlying IOV model)
- The eta-N threshold is at sqrt(s_etaN) = 1487 MeV

## Context

This work extends the recent analysis by Friedman, Gal, Mares (FGM) (2013) by using in-medium eta-N interactions constructed within the chirally-inspired coupled-channel model of Cieply and Smejkal (CS) (2013) that includes self-energy insertions disregarded by FGM. The results contrast with those of Garcia-Recio et al. (2002), who found that Pauli blocking increases the eta-N free-space attraction at threshold, whereas the present CS-based model finds it decreases this attraction. The paper enables future studies of eta-nuclear bound states using more refined in-medium interactions and self-consistent procedures.
