# Imprints of Nuclear Symmetry Energy on Properties of Neutron Stars

> Significant progress has been made in recent years in constraining the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy using terrestrial nuclear laboratory data. Around and below the nuclear matter saturation density, the experimental constraints start to merge in a relatively narrow region. At supra-saturation densities, there are, however, still large uncertainties. After summarizing the latest experimental constraints on the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, we highlight a few recent studies examining imprints of nuclear symmetry energy on the binding energy, energy release during hadron-quark phase transitions as well as the $w$-mode frequency and damping time of gravitational wave emission of neutron stars.

# Imprints of Nuclear Symmetry Energy on Properties of Neutron Stars

## Key claim

While terrestrial nuclear laboratory data have begun to converge on constraints for the nuclear symmetry energy around and below saturation density, at supra-saturation densities large uncertainties remain, and the trend of whether the symmetry energy increases or decreases with density is still controversial. The paper highlights that the symmetry energy's high-density behavior imprints on neutron star binding energy, energy release during hadron-quark phase transitions, and the w-mode frequency and damping time of gravitational wave emission, but these effects are often smaller than those from other uncertain parameters such as the bag constant in quark matter models.

## Method

The authors review experimental constraints on the density-dependent nuclear symmetry energy E_sym(ρ) from isospin diffusion, neutron/proton ratios in pre-equilibrium emissions, isoscaling, neutron-skin thicknesses, pygmy dipole resonances, and nucleon global optical potentials. They then use the MDI (momentum-dependent interaction) parametrization with different slope parameters L(ρ₀) and the MIT bag model for quark matter, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation to compute neutron star properties. The hadron-quark phase transition is treated via the Gibbs construction, and the w-mode gravitational wave frequencies and damping times are computed from the resulting equations of state.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at saturation density E_sym(ρ₀) is constrained to 30.5 ± 3 MeV, with slope L(ρ₀) = 52.5 ± 20 MeV, from combining constraints on E_sym at ρ_A = 0.1 fm⁻³ with neutron skin analyses of Sn isotopes (Chen 2011).
- The value of E_sym(ρ₀) from atomic mass analyses lies between 29.4 and 32.8 MeV, with L(ρ₀) between 52.9 and 78.7 MeV (Liu 2010).
- Extracted L(ρ₀) values from various nuclear structure and reaction studies scatter between about 20 and 115 MeV.
- For the double pulsar PSR J0737-3039B, consistency between nuclear constraints and progenitor modeling requires L(ρ₀) ≤ 70 MeV (Newton 2011).
- The total energy release during hadron-quark phase transition increases with baryonic mass and is higher with a smaller bag constant (B¹/⁴ = 170 MeV) and a stiffer symmetry energy (x = -1), with the bag constant having more significant effects than the symmetry energy parameter.
- For the w-mode of gravitational waves, the bag constant (B¹/⁴ = 170 vs 180 MeV) has a much stronger effect on frequency and damping time than the symmetry energy parameter, though the symmetry energy significantly affects the w-mode frequency for the stiffer EOS with B¹/⁴ = 180 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the line of work connecting nuclear symmetry energy constraints from terrestrial experiments (e.g., Chen 2005, Li 2005, Shetty 2007, Pawel 2009, Centelles 2009, Warda 2009, Chen 2010, Klimkiewicz 2007, Carbone 2010) to neutron star astrophysics, building on earlier studies by Lattimer (2000), Steiner (2005), and Horowitz (2006). It specifically examines how the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy affects neutron star binding energy (Newton 2011), energy release during hadron-quark phase transitions (Lin 2011), and gravitational wave w-mode signals (Krastev 2008, Worley 2009, Wen 2010). The paper enables future combined analyses of nuclear laboratory data and astrophysical observations (from LIGO, LISA, and x-ray satellites) to pin down the equation of state of neutron-rich matter at both sub- and supra-saturation densities.
