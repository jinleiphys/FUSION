# Relativistic Nuclear Energy Density Functionals: Mean-Field and Beyond

> Relativistic energy density functionals (EDF) have become a standard tool for nuclear structure calculations, providing a complete and accurate, global description of nuclear ground states and collective excitations. Guided by the medium dependence of the microscopic nucleon self-energies in nuclear matter, semi-empirical functionals have been adjusted to the nuclear matter equation of state and to bulk properties of finite nuclei, and applied to studies of arbitrarily heavy nuclei, exotic nuclei far from stability, and even systems at the nucleon drip-lines. REDF-based structure models have also been developed that go beyond the static mean-field approximation, and include collective correlations related to the restoration of broken symmetries and to fluctuations of collective variables. These models are employed in analyses of structure phenomena related to shell evolution, including detailed predictions of excitation spectra and electromagnetic transition rates.

## Key claim

Relativistic energy density functionals (REDFs) provide a complete and accurate global description of nuclear ground states and collective excitations, but the paper emphasizes that even if a fully microscopic EDF is eventually developed, its parameters will still have to be fine-tuned to structure data of finite nuclei because data on nucleon-nucleon scattering and few-nucleon systems cannot determine the density functional to the accuracy required for medium-heavy and heavy nuclei.

## Method

The paper reviews the framework of relativistic energy density functionals, starting from quantum hadrodynamics with meson exchange or point-coupling interactions, and extending to self-consistent mean-field models solved via the relativistic Kohn-Sham (Dirac) equations. It then describes how collective correlations are included beyond the static mean-field through symmetry restoration and configuration mixing, using methods such as the Generator Coordinate Method or the collective Hamiltonian in five dimensions, with the functional DD-PC1 used as a representative example for illustrative calculations.

## Context

This work reviews and extends the relativistic energy density functional framework, which has been applied at the mean-field level to describe ground-state properties and giant resonances using models such as NL3, PK1, FSUGold, DD-ME2, and DD-PC1. It builds on earlier non-relativistic approaches using Skyrme functionals and Gogny effective interactions (BHR.03, VALR.05, Meng.06, PVKC.07), and enables detailed spectroscopic predictions by incorporating symmetry restoration and configuration mixing, which had previously been routine only with non-relativistic density functionals (NVR.06a, NVR.06b, Ni.09, Yao.09, Yao.10).
