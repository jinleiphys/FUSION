# Photon Production from a Quark-Gluon-Plasma at Finite Baryon Chemical Potential

> We compute the photon production of a QCD plasma at leading order in the strong coupling with a finite baryon chemical potential. Our approach starts from the real time formalism of finite temperature field theory. We identify the class of diagrams contributing at leading order when a finite chemical potential is added and resum them to perform a full treatment of the LPM effect similar to the one performed by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe at zero chemical potential. Our results show that the contribution of $2\mapsto 3$ and $3\mapsto 2$ processes grows as the chemical potential grows.

## Key claim

The authors compute the leading-order photon production rate from a QCD plasma at finite baryon chemical potential, finding that the contribution of $2\mapsto 3$ and $3\mapsto 2$ processes grows as the chemical potential grows, but only as long as the chemical potential $\mu$ is much less than $T/g$; when $\mu = O(T/g)$, the resummation of ladder diagrams is no longer necessary because thermal masses become $O(T)$ instead of $O(gT)$.

## Method

The calculation starts from the real-time formalism of finite-temperature field theory, using the Keldysh or $r,a$ basis to identify the class of diagrams contributing at leading order. The authors perform a power-counting analysis to show that only ladder diagrams with a specific combination of $r,a$ indices ($G_{aarr}$) contribute at leading order, and they resum these diagrams to incorporate the full Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, following the approach of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) at zero chemical potential. The resummation leads to an integral equation for the photon vertex, which is solved numerically in impact-parameter space using a method developed by Aurenche et al. (2002).

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass at finite chemical potential is $m_D^2 = g^2\left(\frac{N_f T^2}{6} + \frac{N_c T^2}{3} + \frac{N_f \mu^2}{2\pi^2}\right)$.
- The asymptotic thermal quark mass is $m_\infty^2 = \frac{g^2 C_2(F)}{4}\left(T^2 + \frac{\mu^2}{\pi^2}\right)$.
- For $\mu$ of order the temperature $T$ (taken as $250\ \mathrm{MeV}$), the photon emission rate increases by about $10\%$ relative to the $\mu=0$ value.
- For $\mu = T$, the enhancement reaches up to $30\%$ relative to the $\mu=0$ case.
- At RHIC, the quark chemical potential is about $10\ \mathrm{MeV}$ and at SPS about $80\ \mathrm{MeV}$; with $T = O(200\ \mathrm{MeV})$, the enhancement at RHIC is negligibly small and at SPS no more than $5\%$.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order calculation of photon production from a quark-gluon plasma by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001), which was performed at zero chemical potential, to include a finite baryon chemical potential. Previous studies of $2\leftrightarrow 2$ processes with non-zero chemical potential were done by Dumitru (1993), Traxler (1994), Dutta (1999), and He (2005), but the complete leading-order treatment including the LPM effect for $2\to 3$ and $3\to 2$ processes at finite $\mu$ was not performed. The results enable more precise modeling of thermal photon yields in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and SPS, where the net baryon density is non-vanishing.
