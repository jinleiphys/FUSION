# Collective enhancement in the exciton model

> The pre-equilibrium reaction mechanism is considered in the context of the exciton model. A modification to the one-particle one-hole state density is studied which can be interpreted as a collective enhancement. The magnitude of the collective enhancement is set by simulating the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (LLNL) pulsed-spheres neutron-leakage spectra. The impact of the collective enhancement is explored in the context of the highly deformed actinide, 239-Pu. A consequence of this enhancement is the removal of fictitious levels in the Distorted-Wave Born Approximation often used in modern nuclear reaction codes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a collective enhancement factor applied to the 1-particle-1-hole state density in the exciton model, with magnitude set by simulating LLNL pulsed-sphere neutron-leakage spectra, improves the description of pre-equilibrium reactions in highly deformed actinides like 239-Pu, but the enhancement is phenomenological and the damping factor gamma is empirically estimated rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors modified the two-component exciton model in the CoH3 statistical model code by introducing a phenomenological collective enhancement factor K_coll = (kappa - 1)exp(-gamma E) + 1 applied only to 2-exciton configurations (1p-1h). They used single-particle state densities from the axially-symmetric folding Yukawa potential of the finite range droplet model (FRDM) and validated the enhancement by simulating LLNL pulsed-sphere neutron-leakage spectra with MCNP-6.2, comparing calculations at different angles to experimental data for various kappa values.

## Key numbers

- The collective enhancement factor kappa was determined to be approximately 10, based on comparison to LLNL pulsed-sphere data
- The damping factor gamma was empirically estimated to be approximately 1 MeV based on observed rotational-vibrational band heads in actinides
- For 239-Pu(n,2n) cross section at 12 MeV, varying kappa from 1 to 15 produced a spread of roughly 35 mb around the peak
- Single-particle state density constants were C_nu = 19.2 MeV for neutrons and C_pi = 16.0 MeV for protons from FRDM calculations
- The 1p-1h state density from Williams formula and combinatorial calculation showed good agreement between 0 to 10 MeV excitation energy

## Context

This work extends the combined approach of feeding quantum mechanical FKK calculations into the exciton model, as done by Kawano (2006) and Kerveno (2021), by adding a collective enhancement motivated by QRPA calculations from Dupuis (2015, 2017). The enhancement removes the need for fictitious DWBA levels used in evaluations like ENDF/B-VIII.0 to simulate collective effects, enabling a more consistent physical description of pre-equilibrium reactions in deformed actinides for use in Hauser-Feshbach statistical model codes.
