# The light scalar mesons as tetraquarks

> We present a numerical solution of the four-quark Bethe-Salpeter equation for ground-state scalar tetraquarks with J^PC = 0^++. We find that the four-body equation dynamically generates pseudoscalar-meson poles in the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The resulting tetraquarks are genuine four-quark states that are dominated by pseudoscalar meson-meson correlations. Diquark-antidiquark contributions are subleading because of their larger mass scale. In the light quark sector, the sensitivity of the tetraquark wave function to the pion poles leads to an isoscalar tetraquark mass M_sigma ~ 350 MeV which is comparable to that of the sigma/f0(500). The masses of its multiplet partners kappa and a0/f0 follow a similar pattern. This provides support for a tetraquark interpretation of the light scalar meson nonet in terms of 'meson molecules'.

## Key claim
The four-body Bethe-Salpeter equation for scalar tetraquarks dynamically generates pseudoscalar-meson poles that dominate the tetraquark wave function, producing an isoscalar tetraquark mass of approximately 350 MeV that is comparable to the sigma/f0(500). The authors qualify that this "molecular" interpretation arises from the dominance of meson-meson correlations over diquark-antidiquark contributions, but they note that their framework does not distinguish spatial separation of clusters, only the influence of analytic structures in the bound-state kinematics.

## Method
The authors solved the homogeneous four-quark Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for scalar tetraquarks with J^PC = 0^++, retaining only two-body irreducible interactions and neglecting three- and four-body forces. They employed a rainbow-ladder kernel based on nonperturbative gluon exchange that satisfies the axial Ward-Takahashi identity, with dressed quark propagators from the Dyson-Schwinger equation. The momentum dependence of the tetraquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitude was organized into multiplets of the permutation group S4, and the 16 dominant s-wave tensor structures were kept while p- and d-waves were omitted.

## Key numbers
- Isoscalar tetraquark mass M_sigma = 0.348(13) GeV (348 ± 13 MeV) for the sigma/f0(500) candidate
- Kappa tetraquark mass M_kappa = 0.750(12) GeV (750 ± 12 MeV)
- a0/f0 tetraquark mass M_{a0/f0} = 1.081(28) GeV (1081 ± 28 MeV)
- The sigma mass is just above the two-pion threshold (2m_pi ≈ 280 MeV at physical quark masses)
- Diquark mass scales in rainbow-ladder: scalar diquark ~800 MeV, axialvector diquark ~1 GeV
- The bound state mass when restricting to the symmetric singlet only was M ~ 1.5 GeV ("four times the constituent-quark mass")

## Context
This work extends the tetraquark interpretation of light scalar mesons proposed by Jaffe (1977) and improves upon the authors' earlier two-body framework (Heupel, 2012) by solving the full four-body Bethe-Salpeter equation without prejudice to flavor or spatial structure. It contrasts with diquark-antidiquark models and potential models for meson molecules (Weinstein, 1990) by showing that pseudoscalar meson poles dominate over diquark poles due to chiral symmetry breaking, naturally producing a meson-molecule picture. The results support a tetraquark interpretation of the light scalar nonet and enable future applications to tetraquarks in the charmonium region, where the approach could dynamically distinguish diquark-antidiquark, meson molecule, and hadrocharmonium configurations.
