# An unusual space-time evolution for heavy ion collisions at high energies due to the QCD phase transition

> The space-time evolution of high energy non-central heavy ion collisions is studied with relativistic hydrodynamics. The results are very sensitive to the Equation of State(EoS). For an EoS with the QCD phase transition, an unusual matter distribution develops. Before freeze-out, two shells are formed which then physically separate and leave a maximum in the center. We make specific predictions for the azimuthal dependence of the flow and for two-pion interferometry, contrasting our results with a resonance gas EoS.

## Key claim

For non-central heavy ion collisions at RHIC/LHC energies, an equation of state (EoS) with a QCD phase transition produces an unusual "nutcracker" flow pattern where two expanding shells physically separate and leave a maximum in the center, which is qualitatively different from the simple elliptic flow seen with a resonance gas EoS. The authors hedge that this pattern depends on the collision being sufficiently non-central and on the specific bag model EoS with a transition temperature of 160 MeV and a 1 GeV latent heat.

## Method

The authors solved 2+1 dimensional relativistic Euler equations in the transverse plane using the HLLE Gudunov method, assuming boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. They used a simple bag model equation of state with T_c=160 MeV and a 1 GeV latent heat for the QCD phase transition, and a resonance gas EoS with p=0.2ϵ for comparison. The initial entropy distribution was assumed proportional to the distribution of participating nucleons, and the initial energy density was parameterized by the total pion multiplicity dN_π/dy.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature: T_c = 160 MeV
- Latent heat: 1 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 140 MeV
- Impact parameter for calculations: b = 8 fm for PbPb collisions
- For central RHIC collisions: dN_π/dy = 850 (including all isospin states)
- For central SPS collisions: dN_π/dy = 525 (including all isospin states)
- Elliptic flow for nucleons: v_2 ≈ 7% and α_2 ≈ 13% from highest SPS energies to LHC energies
- At SPS: <p_t^2> = 0.67 GeV^2, α_2 = 12.0%, α_4 = 0.6%
- At RHIC: <p_t^2> = 0.84 GeV^2, α_2 = 13.8%, α_4 = 1.3%
- 75% of nucleons freeze-out during a short proper time interval of 2.5 fm/c
- Early freeze-out surface peak at x = 4-5 fm
- At RHIC, early pressure forces a 3-4% additional asymmetry in final net nucleon distributions beyond the elliptic component

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic studies of elliptic flow by Ollitrault (1992) and earlier work on the "softest point" of the QCD equation of state by Hung and Shuryak (1995). It contrasts with cascade event generator approaches by Sorge (1997) and others that had successfully reproduced AGS/SPS hadronic spectra. The paper predicts that the "nutcracker" flow pattern should be observable at RHIC through higher harmonics in azimuthal distributions and through Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry, and urges experimentalists to look for azimuthal dependence in all observables from the first day of RHIC operation.
