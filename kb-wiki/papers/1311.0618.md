# Formation of hybrid stars from metastable hadronic stars

> We study the consequences of quark matter nucleation in cold hadronic matter employing three relativistic-mean-field (RMF) models to describe the hadronic phase and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for the quark one. We explore the effect of a vector interaction in the NJL Lagrangian and of a phenomenological bag constant on neutron stars metastability. We delineate the region of parameters of the quark phase that allow for the formation of stable hybrid stars with mass compatible with the almost $2 \ M_\odot$ pulsars PSR J1614-2230 ($1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot$) and PSR J0348+0432 ($2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot$). It is shown, however, that not all hybrid star configurations with $\sim 2 \,M_\odot$ are populated after nucleation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, for certain combinations of the vector coupling strength and the effective bag constant in the NJL quark model, stable hybrid stars with masses compatible with the ~2 solar mass pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 can form after quark matter nucleation, but not all hybrid star configurations with ~2 solar masses are actually populated after the nucleation process.

## Method

The authors used three relativistic-mean-field (RMF) models (NL3, TM1, and TM1-2) to describe the hadronic phase and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for the quark phase, including a vector interaction term and a phenomenological bag constant. They calculated the quantum nucleation time of quark matter droplets in cold hadronic matter using the WKB approximation, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to obtain stellar mass sequences, and determined the critical mass for which the nucleation time is ~1 year.

## Key numbers

- The two observed pulsars used as references: PSR J1614-2230 with mass 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses and PSR J0348+0432 with mass 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses
- For the NL3 hadronic EOS with NJL model including vector interaction (G_V/G_S = 0.4) and B* = -49.23 MeV/fm^3, stable final stars with masses compatible with 2 solar masses were obtained for surface tensions between 5 and 15 MeV/fm^2; specifically, for sigma = 5 MeV/fm^2 the critical mass M_cr = 2.00 solar masses and final mass M_fin = 1.99 solar masses, for sigma = 10 MeV/fm^2 M_cr = 2.09 solar masses and M_fin = 2.08 solar masses
- For the TM1-2 model with NJL model including vector interaction (G_V/G_S = 0.2) and B* = -39.46 MeV/fm^3, a hybrid star with mass 2.03 solar masses and radius 12.41 km was obtained for surface tension around 8 MeV/fm^2
- The maximum hybrid star mass for TM1-2 with NJL model (G_V = 0, B* = 15.78 MeV/fm^3) was 2.07 solar masses with radius 12.65 km
- The surface tension range considered was 7-30 MeV/fm^2, based on calculations from the literature

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark matter nucleation in neutron stars by Iida and Sato (1998) and Bombaci et al. (2000-2007) that used the MIT bag model for the quark phase, and by Logoteta et al. (2009) and others that used NJL and Chromo Dielectric models. The authors improve on their earlier results (Logoteta et al. 2009, 2012) where only low-mass hybrid stars were found as final products of nucleation, by showing that with a hard hadronic EOS (NL3) and appropriate NJL parameters, stable ~2 solar mass stars with pure quark cores can form. The results enable future studies of the parameter space for hybrid star formation and show that the hadronic EOS must be sufficiently hard to allow massive hybrid stars, while the vector interaction in the NJL model is essential for producing stable high-mass final configurations.
