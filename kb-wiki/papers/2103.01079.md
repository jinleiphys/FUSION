# Transition distribution amplitudes and hard exclusive reactions with baryon number transfer

> Baryon-to-meson and baryon-to-photon transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) arise in the collinear factorized description of a class of hard exclusive reactions characterized by the exchange of a non-zero baryon number in the cross channel. These TDAs extend the concepts of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and baryon distribution amplitudes (DAs). In this review, we discuss the general properties and physical interpretation of baryon-to-meson and baryon-to-photon TDAs. We argue that these non-perturbative objects are a convenient complementary tool to explore the structure of baryons at the partonic level. We present an overview of hard exclusive reactions admitting a description in terms of TDAs. We discuss the first signals from hard exclusive backward meson electroproduction at JLab with the 6 GeV electron beam and explore further experimental opportunities to access TDAs at JLab@12 GeV, PANDA, J-PARC and EIC.

# Transition distribution amplitudes and hard exclusive reactions with baryon number transfer

## Key claim

Baryon-to-meson and baryon-to-photon transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) provide a collinear factorized description of hard exclusive reactions with non-zero baryon number exchange in the cross channel, extending the GPD and DA framework to near-backward kinematics; the authors review the theoretical status and argue that first experimental signals from JLab with 6 GeV beams support the approach, but the onset of the TDA description requires further data on color transparency effects.

## Method

The authors review the collinear factorization framework for hard exclusive reactions, extending the standard GPD-based near-forward description to the near-backward regime where nucleon-to-meson TDAs replace GPDs. They derive the real and imaginary parts of convolution integrals of nucleon-to-meson TDAs with leading-order hard scattering kernels using Sohotsky's formula and principal value prescriptions, and they present Fierz identities for the Dirac structures parametrizing nucleon DAs, Delta(1232) DAs, pi-N TDAs, and vector-meson-nucleon TDAs at leading twist-3.

## Key numbers

- The scaling law for the pion electromagnetic form factor is predicted as F_pi(Q) ~ 1/Q^2, and for the nucleon Dirac form factor as F_1N(Q) ~ 1/Q^4.
- The Bjorken variable is defined as x_B = Q^2/(2 p_N dot q), with Q^2 = -q^2 and W^2 = (p_N + q)^2.
- The Mandelstam variables satisfy s + t + u = -Q^2 + 2 m_N^2 + m_M^2, where m_N and m_M are the nucleon and meson masses.
- The largest possible value of t in the DVCS case is t_0 = -4 xi^2 m^2/(1 - xi^2).
- The support region for double distributions is the rhombus Omega: |alpha| + |beta| <= 1.

## Context

This review extends the collinear factorization approach originally developed for DIS (Mueller, 1981; Collins, 2011) and for electromagnetic form factors via DAs (Efremov and Radyushkin, 1979; Lepage and Brodsky, 1979-1980) to the near-backward regime using TDAs. It builds on the GPD framework for near-forward exclusive processes such as DVCS (Mueller et al., 1998; Radyushkin, 1996; Ji, 1996) and generalizes the concept to baryon-number-transfer reactions. The work enables experimental searches at JLab@12 GeV, PANDA, J-PARC, and EIC, and provides the theoretical foundation for interpreting data on hard exclusive backward meson electroproduction.
