# Equation of state extracted from the root-mean-square-radius-isospin

> Experimental correlation between the rms nucleus charge radius and the neutron-proton asymmetry is discussed. Simple attempt of explanation of this correlation using a semi-empirical liquid drop model which takes into account nuclear compressibility, nuclear deformation and neutron skin effects is presented. It seems that such procedure could be used as a tool for determination of the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed correlation between nuclear root-mean-square charge radius and neutron-proton asymmetry for nuclei with A > 100 can be explained by a modified liquid-drop model that incorporates the nuclear equation of state, but the author hedges that a precise determination of EOS parameters requires a more complex analysis including pairing, shell effects, and surface diffuseness in future work.

## Method

The author compared experimental charge radii from a set of 799 ground state nuclei (Angeli 2004) with predictions from a modified liquid-drop model that treats central density as a variable determined by minimizing binding energy per nucleon. The model incorporates the nuclear equation of state through volume, surface, and Coulomb terms with density-dependent corrections, and includes effects of quadrupole deformation (using beta parameters from Moller 1995) and neutron skin thickness. Calculations were performed for nuclei with A > 100, testing different combinations of the incompressibility K_delta=0 (120 and 500 MeV), symmetry energy slope L (-150 and 60 MeV), and symmetry energy curvature K_sym (500 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Experimental charge radii data set contains 799 ground state nuclei, with errors usually much lower than 1%
- Standard liquid-drop model coefficients used: a_v = -15.677 MeV, a_s = 18.560 MeV, a_c = 0.717 MeV, k_v = k_s = 1.79 MeV
- Test calculations used K_delta=0 = 240 MeV for binding energy curves, with L = +/- 100 MeV and K_sym = +/- 500 MeV
- For model comparisons, K_sym was fixed at 500 MeV while testing K_delta=0 = 120 MeV (soft) and 500 MeV (hard) with L = -150 MeV and L = 60 MeV
- Deformation parameter beta^2 is less than 0.1 for almost all masses, modifying rms radius by no more than 1%
- Literature values cited: K_sym varies from -566 +/- 1350 MeV to 34 +/- 159 MeV from giant monopole resonance data (Shlomo 1993); L varies from -50 to 200 MeV in theoretical models (Furnstahl 2002)

## Context

This work extends the liquid-drop model originally formulated by Weizsacker (1935), Bethe (1936), and Bohr (1936) by incorporating density dependence from the nuclear equation of state to explain charge radius systematics. The approach aims to constrain the poorly determined symmetry energy parameters L and K_sym, which are relevant for understanding neutron-rich nuclei stability (Todd-Rutel 2003), heavy-ion reactions (Li 2002, Daniel 2002), and core-collapse supernova dynamics (McLaughlin 2005, Lattimer 2001). The method enables future extraction of EOS parameters from systematic charge radius measurements, though the author notes that additional corrections for pairing, shell effects, and surface diffuseness are needed.
