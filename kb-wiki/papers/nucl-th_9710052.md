# Computer program for the relativistic mean field description of the ground state properties of even-even axially deformed nuclei

> A Fortran program for the calculation of the ground state properties of axially deformed even-even nuclei in the relativistic framework is presented. In this relativistic mean field (RMF) approach a set of coupled differential equations namely the Dirac equation with potential terms for the nucleons and the Glein-Gordon type equations with sources for the meson and the electromagnetic fields are to be solved self-consistently. The well tested basis expansion method is used for this purpose. Accordingly a set of harmonic oscillator basis generated by an axially deformed potential are used in the expansion. The solution gives the nucleon spinors, the fields and level occupancies, which are used in the calculation of the ground state properties.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Fortran 77 program (RMFD.f) that solves the relativistic mean field (RMF) equations for axially deformed even-even nuclei using a basis expansion method, claiming that 12 oscillator shells are sufficient for convergence across the periodic table, though superheavy nuclei may require more.

## Method

The coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations are solved self-consistently by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis. The Dirac equation reduces to a symmetric matrix diagonalization problem, while the Klein-Gordon equations become a set of inhomogeneous linear equations solved by basis expansion for massive mesons and by Green's function methods for the Coulomb field. Pairing is included via the BCS formalism with constant gap parameters taken from empirical odd-even mass differences.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy deviations from empirical values are ≤ 0.5% for Sr isotopes (A=70 to A=110) using the NL-SH parameter set
- The center-of-mass correction is given by E_CM = -(3/4) × 41 × A^(-1/3) MeV
- The pairing window extends to ε_i - λ ≤ 2 × (41 × A^(-1/3)) MeV, with the factor 2 determined to reproduce pairing correlation energy for neutrons in 118Sn using the Gogny force
- Charge radius is calculated as r_c = sqrt(r_p^2 + 0.64) fm, where 0.64 fm^2 accounts for the finite proton size
- The oscillator frequency for fermions is fixed at ℏω_0 = 41 × A^(-1/3) MeV
- Typical running time ranges from 30 minutes to several hours depending on the computer and number of shells included
- For Sr isotopes, the prolate-oblate energy difference for nuclei near A=98 is about 300 keV, increasing to 600-800 keV for heavier isotopes

## Context

This work extends the RMF approach from spherical nuclei (Ring, Gambhir, and Lalazissis, 1990) to axially deformed systems, providing a computational tool that reproduces binding energies, deformations, and isotope shifts across the periodic table. The program successfully reproduces the anomalous kink in isotope shifts near magic neutron numbers for Sr and rare-earth nuclei (Z=60-70), a feature that had eluded previous microscopic descriptions. The results are compared with the finite-range droplet model (Möller, Nix, and Myers, 1995) and the extended Thomas-Fermi with Strutinsky Integral model (Aboussir, Pearson, and Dutta, 1995), showing agreement within 1-2 MeV for binding energies.
