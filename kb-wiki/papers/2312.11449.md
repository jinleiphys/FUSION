# Holographic transport coefficients and jet energy loss for the hot and dense quark-gluon plasma

> We employ an Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton model, based on the gauge/gravity correspondence, to obtain the thermodynamics and transport properties for the hot and dense quark-gluon plasma. The model, which is constrained to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero density, predicts a critical point and a first order line at finite temperature and density, is used to quantify jet energy loss through simulations of high-energy collision events.

## Key claim

The holographic Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton model, constrained to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero baryon density, predicts a critical point at (T, μ_B) ≈ (89, 724) MeV and a first-order transition line, and is used to quantify jet energy loss through simulations where the jet quenching parameter is time-dependent rather than constant.

## Method

The authors employ a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton (EMD) holographic model with a 5-dimensional bulk metric coupled to a dilaton field and a Maxwell field, with free parameters and potentials fixed by matching holographic entropy density and second-order baryon susceptibility at zero baryon chemical potential to lattice QCD results for 2+1 flavors with physical quark masses. Transport coefficients including the jet quenching parameter are computed from the model across the phase diagram. The temperature-dependent jet quenching parameter is translated to a time dependence using the TRENTo initial-state model and ideal Bjorken hydrodynamics, then used in PYTHIA simulations of high-energy heavy-ion collisions to compute jet energy loss.

## Key numbers

- Critical point location: (T, μ_B)_CEP ≈ (89, 724) MeV
- t'Hooft coupling fixed to λ = 16 for jet simulations, based on comparison with JETSCAPE results
- Jet quenching ratio computed as number of jets with time-dependent q̂(τ) over number of jets with constant q̂ = 1 GeV²/fm
- Centrality range for event averaging: 0.25 – 0.30

## Context

This work extends the holographic EMD model previously developed by Critelli et al. (2017) and Grefa et al. (2021) to compute not only equilibrium thermodynamics but also transport coefficients and jet energy loss across the QCD phase diagram. The model is constrained by lattice QCD results from Borsanyi et al. (2013) and Bellwied et al. (2015) at zero density, and shows agreement with state-of-the-art lattice data from Borsanyi et al. (2021). The jet quenching parameter is compared to JETSCAPE results from Apolinario et al. (2022). This work enables more realistic modeling of parton energy loss with time-dependent quenching in heavy-ion collisions, motivating future studies with improved hydrodynamics and inclusion of additional transport coefficients.
