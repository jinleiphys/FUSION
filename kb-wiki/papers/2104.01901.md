# Low-momentum interactions for ultracold Fermi gases

> We consider a two-component Fermi gas with a contact interaction from the BCS regime to the unitary limit. Starting from the idea that many-body effects should not depend on short-distance or high-momentum physics which is encoded in the s-wave scattering length, but only on momentum scales of the order of the Fermi momentum, we build effective low-momentum interactions that reproduce the scattering phase shifts of the contact interaction below some momentum cutoff. Inspired from recent successes of this method in nuclear structure theory, we use these interactions to describe the equation of state of the Fermi gas in the framework of Hartree-Fock-Bogliubov theory with perturbative corrections. In the BCS regime, there is a range of cutoffs where we obtain fully converged results. Near unitarity, convergence is not yet reached, but we obtain promising results for the ground-state energies close to the experimental ones. Limitations and possible extensions of the approach are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors construct effective low-momentum interactions for ultracold Fermi gases, inspired by the nuclear-physics Vlowk approach, and show that in the BCS regime (where 1/(kF a) is sufficiently negative) there exists a range of momentum cutoffs yielding fully converged results for the equation of state, while near unitarity convergence is not yet reached but ground-state energies close to experimental values are obtained.

## Method

The authors build a separable interaction V0(q,q') = g0 F(q) F(q') whose s-wave phase shifts reproduce those of a contact interaction below a momentum cutoff Λ, using an exponential regulator and the Tabakin inverse-scattering construction. They then solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations with this interaction and compute second- and third-order perturbative corrections (Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory, BMBPT) to the ground-state energy, using Monte-Carlo integration with importance sampling for the multi-dimensional momentum integrals.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff Λ is chosen such that the regulator R(q/Λ) = exp(-(q/Λ)^(2n)) with n=10 drops to 10^(-10) at qmax ≈ 1.17 Λ.
- The effective range introduced by a finite cutoff is reff = 4/(πΛ).
- In the BCS regime (1/(kF a) << -1), the gap is exponentially suppressed and the dominant energy correction compared to the ideal gas comes from the normal (HF) part of the self-energy.
- For the third-order BMBPT correction, there are 27 distinct Goldstone-like diagrams contributing to the ground-state energy.

## Context

This work extends the Vlowk renormalization-group method, successful in nuclear structure theory for nuclei (Coraggio 2003, Tichai 2018) and neutron matter (Ramanan 2018), to ultracold Fermi gases in the BCS-BEC crossover. Unlike nuclear physics, the three-body force generated by the RG running is absent in two-component Fermi gases due to the Pauli principle, simplifying the approach. The method enables systematic perturbative calculations of the equation of state without the ladder-diagram resummations required in the standard contact-interaction regularization (Pieri 2004), and could be extended to the BEC side and to include three-body forces if more spin components are considered.
