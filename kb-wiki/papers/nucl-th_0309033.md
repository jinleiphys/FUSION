# $^1 S_0$ pairing correlation in symmetric nuclear matter with Debye screening effects

> The $^1 S_0$ pairing of symmetric nuclear matter is discussed in the frame work of relativistic nuclear theory with Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs). The in-medium nucleon and meson propagators are treated in a more self-consistent way through meson polarizations. The screening effects on mesons due to in-medium nucleon excitation are found to reduce the $^1S_0$ pairing gap and shift remarkably the gap peak to low density region.

## Key claim

The Debye screening effects from in-medium meson propagators, treated self-consistently through Dyson-Schwinger equations, significantly reduce the \(^1S_0\) pairing gap in symmetric nuclear matter and shift the gap peak to lower density, compared to earlier relativistic treatments that used frozen (bare) meson propagators.

## Method

The authors solved a coupled set of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the in-medium nucleon and meson (sigma and omega) propagators together with the BCS gap equation for \(^1S_0\) pairing in the ladder approximation, using the relativistic QHD-I (sigma-omega) model. The interaction kernel in the gap equation was computed using the in-medium (screened) meson propagators in the static (instantaneous) approximation, with the Debye screening masses determined self-consistently from the pole positions of the spacelike propagators. The parameters were fixed by the saturation condition of symmetric nuclear matter at normal density.

## Key numbers

* Maximum pairing gap \(\Delta_f^m = 1.9\) MeV at peak position \(k_f^m = 0.5\) fm\(^{-1}\) for symmetric nuclear matter in the self-consistent approach (SA).
* At zero density (\(k_f = 0\)), the gap \(\Delta(0) = 0\) MeV in the SA, compared to 1.94 MeV (MFT) and 0.36 MeV (RHA) from earlier approaches.
* Compression modulus \(\mathcal{K} = 338.00\) MeV in the SA, compared to 545.43 MeV (MFT) and 468.24 MeV (RHA).
* Effective nucleon mass ratio \(M_N^*/M_N = 0.794\) at saturation in the SA, with coupling constants \(C_s^2 = 123.17\) and \(C_v^2 = 66.078\).
* Momentum cutoff \(\Lambda_p = 20\) fm\(^{-1}\) used in the numerical integration of the gap equation.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of nuclear superfluidity by Kucharek and Ring (1991) and Matsuzaki (1998, 1999), who used frozen (bare) meson propagators and found gap values about three times larger than nonrelativistic results. The self-consistent inclusion of meson polarization and Debye screening reduces the gap and shifts its peak, bringing the relativistic results closer to nonrelativistic calculations (such as those using the Gogny force) and resolving the discrepancy at zero density where earlier relativistic approaches gave unphysical nonzero gaps. The approach enables more realistic studies of pairing in neutron stars and finite nuclei within relativistic field theory.
