# Relativistic Collective Coordinate System of Solitons and Spinning Skyrmion

> We consider constructing the relativistic system of collective coordinates of a field theory soliton on the basis of a simple principle: The collective coordinates must be introduced into the static solution in such a way that the equation of motion of the collective coordinates ensures that of the original field theory. As an illustration, we apply this principle to the quantization of spinning motion of the Skyrmion by incorporating the leading relativistic correction to the rigid body approximation. We calculate the decay constant and various static properties of nucleons, and find that the relativistic corrections are in the range of 5% -- 20%. We also examine how the baryons deform due to the spinning motion.

## Key claim

The authors propose a "EOM principle" for introducing collective coordinates into a static soliton solution: the collective coordinates must be introduced such that the equation of motion of the collective coordinates ensures that of the original field theory. When applied to the spinning Skyrmion, this principle yields leading relativistic corrections to the rigid body approximation that are in the range of 5% to 20%, though the comparison with experimental data is disappointing as the corrections move most theoretical values away from experiment.

## Method

The authors construct an improved spinning Skyrmion field by modifying the argument of the static hedgehog solution, introducing three radial functions A(r), B(r), and C(r) that multiply terms of order (angular velocity)^2 in the coordinate transformation. They determine these functions by requiring that the field theory equation of motion holds to O(p_t^2) when the collective coordinate equation of motion is satisfied, solving the resulting differential equations with appropriate boundary conditions. The Lagrangian with leading relativistic correction is then derived, and the static properties of nucleons are recalculated using the corrected Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Rigid body approximation gives M_cl = 866 MeV, moment of inertia I = 0.00512 MeV^{-1}, |Omega_N| = 169 MeV, |Omega_Delta| = 378 MeV
- Rotational velocity of the nucleon at radius 1 fm is 86% of light velocity; for Delta it is 190% of light velocity
- Relativistic corrections to static properties are in the range of 5% to 20%
- The pion mass m_pi is included and is indispensable for the relativistic corrections to be finite

## Context

This work extends the rigid body approximation of the Skyrmion developed by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) and Adkins and Nappi (1984), which treated baryons as spherical rigid rotors. The authors note that about 8% (30%) of the nucleon (Delta) mass comes from rotational energy, motivating relativistic corrections. The EOM principle was previously used for solitons in gauge theories, including magnetic monopoles and the baryon solution in the Sakai-Sugimoto model. The paper provides a systematic alternative to earlier attempts at improving the rigid body approximation by authors including Bander (1984), Hajduk (1984), Braaten (1984), and others.
