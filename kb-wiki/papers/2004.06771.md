# Possibility of bottom-catalyzed matter genesis near to primordial QGP hadronization

> We study bottom flavor abundance in the early Universe near to a temperature $T_\mathrm{H}\simeq150\,\mathrm{MeV}$, the condition for hadronization of deconfined quark-gluon plasma (QGP). We show bottom flavor abundance nonequilibrium lasting microseconds. In our study we use that in both QGP, and the hadronic gas phase (HG) $b$ and $\bar b$ quarks near $T_\mathrm{H}$ are bound in B-mesons and antimesons subject to $CP$ violating weak decays. A coincident non-equilibrium abundance of bottom flavor can lead to matter genesis at required strength: a) The specific thermal yield per entropy is $n_b^{th}/σ=10^{-10}\sim10^{-13}$. b) Considering time scales, millions of cycles of B-meson decays, and $b\bar b$-pair recreation processes occur.

## Key claim
Bottom flavor quarks can generate the observed matter-antimatter asymmetry during the QGP hadronization era near T_H ≈ 150 MeV, provided that a small CP-violating asymmetry of order ε ∼ 10⁻⁵ per B-meson decay cycle is present, and that a baryon-number-violating mechanism (not specified in this work) operates.

## Method
The authors compute the thermal equilibrium abundance of bottom quarks relative to entropy density near T_H using Boltzmann statistics for heavy quarks. They then solve a master equation for the fugacity of B_c mesons, balancing gluon-fusion production of b-bbar pairs against weak-interaction decay of B_c mesons, while accounting for the Hubble expansion of the early Universe. The adiabatic approximation is validated by showing that the temperature derivative of the fugacity is negligible compared to the production and decay rates.

## Key numbers
- The thermal bottom quark number density per entropy at T_H ≈ 150 MeV is n_b^th/σ = 10⁻¹⁰ to 10⁻¹³.
- The B_c meson lifespan is τ_Bc = 0.51 × 10⁻¹² s; other B meson lifespans range from 1.51 × 10⁻¹² s (B_s) to 1.64 × 10⁻¹² s (B_u).
- The number of B-meson formation-and-decay cycles during the QGP hadronization era (τ_H ≈ 10 μs) is Cycle = τ_H / τ_Bc ≈ 2 × 10⁷.
- The required CP-violating asymmetry per cycle to produce the observed baryon-to-entropy ratio B/S ≈ 7.69 × 10⁻¹¹ is ε ≈ 2.75 × 10⁻⁵.
- The charm quark abundance at T_H is about 10,000 times greater than bottom abundance.
- The relative yield of Upsilon (bbar) to open bottom is about 10⁻⁴ (citing Yao and Muller 2017).

## Context
This work extends the search for matter genesis beyond the conventional GUT-scale and electroweak-scale scenarios (Kuzmin 1985, Arnold 1987, Kolb 1996, Riotto 1999) to the much lower temperature of QGP hadronization (T ≈ 150 MeV). It identifies bottom flavor as uniquely positioned at a "sweet spot" where strong-interaction formation rates and weak-interaction decay rates become comparable, enabling millions of CP-violating decay cycles. The mechanism is distinct from the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007) and complements studies of heavy flavor in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Rafelski 2019). The results motivate future experimental searches for baryon-number violation involving bottom mesons and theoretical work on CP violation in B_c decays (Tully 2019, Amhis 2019).
