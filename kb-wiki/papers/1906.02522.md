# Astrophysical aspects of general relativistic mass twin stars

> In this chapter we will introduce an effective equation of state (EoS) model based on polytropes that serves to study the so called "mass twins" scenario, where two compact stars have approximately the same mass but (significant for observation) quite different radii. Stellar mass twin configurations are obtained if a strong first-order phase transition occurs in the interior of a compact star. In the mass-radius diagram of compact stars, this will lead to a third branch of gravitationally stable stars with features that are very distinctive from those of white dwarfs and neutron stars. We discuss rotating hybrid star sequences in the slow rotation approximation and in full general relativity and draw conclusions for an upper limit on the maximum mass of nonrotating compact stars that has recently be deduced from the observation of the merger event GW170817.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an effective polytropic equation of state model to study the "mass twins" scenario, where two compact stars have approximately the same mass but significantly different radii, which occurs only if a strong first-order phase transition in the stellar interior satisfies the Seidov condition that the energy density jump relative to central energy density is at least one half plus three halves of the central pressure over central energy density.

## Method

The authors construct an effective equation of state based on polytropes and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for spherically symmetric static stars to obtain mass-radius relations. They then extend the analysis to rotating hybrid star sequences using both the slow rotation approximation (Hartle-Thorne perturbation method) and full general relativistic solutions obtained with the RNS code, computing global parameters such as gravitational mass, circumferential radius, moment of inertia, and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

* The general relativistic correction factor for a pulsar of mass 2 solar masses with a typical radius of about 12 km amounts to 1/(1-2GM/R) = 2, representing a 100% correction relative to Newtonian gravity.
* The Seidov condition for mass twins is given by the relation Δε/ε_c ≥ 1/2 + (3/2)(P_c/ε_c), where ε_c is central energy density, P_c is central pressure, and Δε is the energy density jump at the phase transition.
* The Keplerian angular velocity for mass shedding is Ω_K = √(GM/R_e^3), and the expansion parameter for slow rotation is β = (Ω/Ω_0)^2 with Ω_0^2 = 4πGρ(0), where the condition Ω < Ω_K = Ω_0/√3 always holds.

## Context

This work extends the mass twin scenario originally studied by Glendenning (1998) and builds on the Seidov instability condition (1971) for first-order phase transitions in compact stars. The effective polytropic equation of state model follows the approach of Alvarez-Castillo (2017). The results enable constraints on the maximum mass of nonrotating compact stars from the GW170817 merger event and provide predictions for tidal deformability parameters that can be tested by future gravitational wave observations of neutron star mergers.
