# Tensor force induced isospin-dependence of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlation and high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy

> Quantitative information on the tensor force induced isospin-dependence of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlation (SRC) extracted from recent J-Lab experiments was used in constraining the high-momentum tails of single nucleon momentum distributions in both symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM). Its effects on the Equations of State of SNM and PNM as well as the nuclear symmetry energy are investigated. It is found that the tensor force induced isospin-dependence of SRC softens significantly the nuclear symmetry energy especially at supra-saturation densities.

## Key claim

The tensor force induced isospin-dependence of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlation (SRC), extracted from J-Lab experiments, significantly softens the nuclear symmetry energy especially at supra-saturation densities; this effect arises because the SRC stiffens the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) but has almost no effect on pure neutron matter (PNM).

## Method

The authors parameterized the single-nucleon momentum distributions in SNM and PNM using a two-region form (step function for low momenta, exponential tail for high momenta), constrained by J-Lab experimental findings that about 20% of nucleons in 12C are correlated and that np SRC strength is about 20 times that of pp/nn SRC. They then computed the kinetic and potential energy per nucleon using these momentum distributions within the MDI (Momentum-Dependent Interaction) phenomenological potential, fitting parameters to empirical saturation properties of SNM and low-density PNM constraints from the literature.

## Key numbers

- In SNM at saturation density, the kinetic energy per nucleon with SRC is about 40 MeV, compared to about 22 MeV for uncorrelated Fermi gas.
- For SNM, 80% of nucleons are taken as uncorrelated and 20% as correlated, based on J-Lab experiments.
- For PNM, 98% of nucleons are taken as uncorrelated and 2% as correlated, based on the experimental finding that only 2% of nucleons in 12C form nn/pp SRC pairs.
- At nuclear matter saturation density, the parameter b for the high-momentum tail is -1.899 for SNM and -2.435 for PNM.
- The MDI potential parameters for SNM are A = -114.079 MeV, B = 107.154 MeV, C = -0.0274 MeV, fitted to saturation density 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy -16 MeV, and incompressibility 220 MeV.
- For PNM, the MDI parameters are A = -120.570 MeV, B = 102.080 MeV, C = -7.61 × 10^-4 MeV, fitted to E = 1.7 MeV at k_F = 0.4 fm^-1 and E = 4.2 MeV at k_F = 0.8 fm^-1, plus symmetry energy 31 MeV at saturation density.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies by Pandharipande and Garde (1972) and others (Wir88a, bro1, xuli, Lee1) who predicted that the tensor force acting in isosinglet neutron-proton pairs affects the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy, but lacked quantitative experimental constraints. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star properties by incorporating experimentally constrained isospin-dependent SRC effects into the equation of state of dense neutron-rich matter. The approach contrasts with microscopic many-body theories by using a phenomenological method that directly incorporates the J-Lab experimental findings on SRC isospin-dependence.
