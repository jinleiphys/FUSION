# On high energy factorization: theoretical basics and phenomenological applications

> We overview some of theory and phenomenology aspects of high energy factorization. In the theory part we focus on basic equations of high energy factorization i.e. BFKL, CCFM, BK. In the phenomenology part we focus on forward-central jets correlations at Large Hadron Collider and on production of charged particles in HERA.

# On high energy factorization: theoretical basics and phenomenological applications

## Key claim

High energy factorization provides a unified framework for describing both hard processes and dense partonic systems at high energies, but the BFKL, CCFM, and BK evolution equations each apply only under specific conditions of energy scale, parton density, and observable type, the BFKL approximation applies when collision energies are of order 10^3 GeV for inclusive processes, while the BK equation or nonlinear extensions of CCFM are required when partons overlap and recombine, as in nuclei-nuclei collisions.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical foundations of high energy factorization, presenting the BFKL equation at leading logarithmic order in ln(1/x), the CCFM equation which imposes angular ordering of gluon emissions, and the BK equation as a nonlinear extension of BFKL that includes gluon recombination via a triple pomeron vertex. Phenomenological applications are demonstrated through Monte Carlo simulations using the CASCADE event generator (implementing CCFM evolution) and PYTHIA (implementing collinear factorization with DGLAP evolution), comparing predictions for forward-central jet correlations at the LHC and charged particle production at HERA.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies of order 10^3 GeV are cited as the regime where the BFKL approximation applies for inclusive processes in electron-proton Deep Inelastic Scattering at HERA.
- The saturation scale condition is defined as Q_s ≡ ∂_{ln k^2} φ(x, k^2) = 0, where the gluon density has a maximum at k = Q_s.
- The GBW saturation scale used as an absorptive boundary is Q_s = k_0 (x_0/x)^{λ/2}, with the condition that gluon densities with k < Q_s are set to zero.
- Transverse momentum spectra for forward-central jets are shown at total collision energy √s = 7 TeV with a requirement that p_⊥ > 10 GeV.
- Differential cross sections for charged hadron transverse momentum distributions are compared between CCFM, CCFM with saturation, and DGLAP approaches at HERA.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on high energy factorization initiated by Catani (1990) and covers the BFKL equation developed by Kuraev, Lipatov, Fadin, Balitsky, and Mueller (1977-1993), the BK equation by Balitsky and Kovchegov (1995-1999), and the CCFM equation by Ciafaloni, Catani, Fiorani, and Marchesini (1987-1989). The phenomenological results build on prior studies of forward-central jet correlations by Deak, Jung, Hautmann, and Kutak (2010) and charged particle production at HERA by Kutak and Jung (2008). The work enables future comparisons between high energy factorization predictions and LHC data, particularly for forward physics and saturation studies, and addresses open questions about the implementation of nonlinear saturation effects in Monte Carlo event generators.
