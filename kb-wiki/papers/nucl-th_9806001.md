# Baryon resonances in a chiral confining model (I)

> In this two part series a chiral confining model of baryons is used to describe low--lying negative parity resonances $N^*$, $Δ^*$, $Λ^*$ and $Σ^*$ in the mean field approximation. A physical baryon in this model consists of interacting valence quarks, mesons and a color and chiral singlet hybrid field coexisting inside a dynamically generated confining region. This first paper presents the quark contribution to the masses and wave functions of negative parity baryons calculated with an effective spin--isospin dependent instanton induced interaction. It does not include meson exchanges between quarks. The three--quark wave functions are used to calculate meson--excited baryon vertex functions to lowest order in meson--quark coupling. When the baryons are on mass--shell each of these vertex functions is a product of a coupling constant and a form factor. As examples, quark contributions to $N^*$ hadronic form factors as well as axial coupling constants are extracted from the vertex functions and problems with the analytical behaviour of the model form factors are discussed. The second paper will examine the mesonic corrections to excited baryon properties in the heavy baryon and one--loop approximations.

## Key claim

The paper presents quark contributions to masses, wave functions, and hadronic form factors of low-lying negative parity baryons (N*, Δ*, Λ*, Σ*) using a chiral confining model with an instanton-induced interaction, but explicitly excludes meson exchanges between quarks and treats the baryons as "bare" states without mesonic corrections, which will be addressed in a second paper.

## Method

The authors use the chiral confining model (CCM) in the mean field approximation, where baryons consist of valence quarks, mesons, and a color/chiral singlet hybrid field χ inside a dynamically generated confining region. They construct three-quark wave functions for negative parity baryons by exciting one quark from the ground S₁/₂ state to either P₁/₂ or P₃/₂ states, then diagonalize the bare baryon Hamiltonian H_Bare = H⁽⁰⁾_Toy + H_'t Hooft using a j-j coupled basis after projecting out spurious center-of-mass excitations using a prescription from DeGrand (1976). The 't Hooft interaction is treated as a two-body contact interaction with a regulator parameter r_c = 0.25 fm and strength C_s = 1.6 fm², and SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking is treated perturbatively by modifying single particle energies of strange quarks after diagonalization.

## Context

This work extends the chiral confining model previously applied to ground state nucleons and deltas by Kim and Banerjee (1993, 1994) to the strangeness sector and negative parity excited baryons. It builds on the approach of Blask et al. (1990) and Takeuchi (1994, 1995) who used the 't Hooft interaction in non-relativistic quark models, but differs by using the CCM framework with relativistic quarks and by examining spin-flavor compositions of wave functions rather than just masses. The paper enables future calculation of mesonic corrections to excited baryon properties in the heavy baryon and one-loop approximations (to appear in Paper II), and provides wave functions needed for computing radiative decay widths of excited hyperons such as Λ(1405) and Λ(1520) that will be measured at Jefferson Lab.
