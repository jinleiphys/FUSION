# The decay widths, the decay constants, and the branching fractions of a resonant state

> We introduce the differential and the total decay widths of a resonant (Gamow) state decaying into a continuum of stable states. When the resonance has several decay modes, we introduce the corresponding partial decay widths and branching fractions. In the approximation that the resonance is sharp, the expressions for the differential, partial and total decay widths of a resonant state bear a close resemblance with the Golden Rule. In such approximation, the branching fractions of a resonant state are the same as the standard branching fractions obtained by way of the Golden Rule. We also introduce dimensionless decay constants along with their associated differential decay constants, and we express experimentally measurable quantities such as the branching fractions and the energy distributions of decay events in terms of those dimensionless decay constants.

# The decay widths, the decay constants, and the branching fractions of a resonant state

## Key claim

The paper introduces exact expressions for differential, partial, and total decay widths of a resonant (Gamow) state decaying into a continuum of stable states, which reduce to the standard Golden Rule only in the approximation that the resonance is sharp and far from the energy threshold. The branching fractions derived from these expressions coincide with those from the Golden Rule only in this sharp-resonance limit, making the formalism falsifiable for non-sharp resonances.

## Method

The author derives the differential decay width of a resonant state from the time derivative of the probability density that the resonance has decayed into a stable particle of energy E, using the integral equation satisfied by Gamow states and Zeldovich normalization. For a resonance with multiple decay modes, partial decay widths are defined by integrating the differential width over energy for each mode, and branching fractions are formed as ratios of partial widths. Dimensionless decay constants are introduced by dividing the decay widths by the resonance width Gamma_R, connecting directly to experimental energy distributions of decay events.

## Key numbers

- The total decay width of a resonant state is given by the integral over energy of the Lorentzian lineshape times the squared matrix element: Gamma_bar = integral_0^infinity dE [Gamma_R / ((E - E_R)^2 + (Gamma_R/2)^2)] |<E|V|z_R>|^2
- In the sharp-resonance approximation (Gamma_R/(2E_R) small and resonance far from threshold E=0), the total decay width approximates to Gamma_bar ≈ 2 pi |<E_R|V|z_R>|^2
- The dimensionless decay constant Gamma is related to the decay width by Gamma = Gamma_bar / Gamma_R
- The survival probability of a resonant state follows the exponential law p_s(tau) = exp(-Gamma_R tau / hbar)
- The initial decay rate satisfies Gamma_R = -hbar dp_s(0)/dtau

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Gamow (resonant) states to provide rigorous definitions of decay widths and branching fractions, building on prior treatments by Goldberger, Merzbacher, and Bohm that involved similar Lorentzian lineshapes but derived from different time-dependent probabilities. The paper enables a falsifiable framework for calculating branching fractions of resonances that are not sharp, distinguishing it from the standard Golden Rule approach used in textbooks. The dimensionless decay constants introduced here provide a direct theoretical counterpart to experimental energy distributions of decay events, analogous to invariant mass distributions in particle physics as measured by experiments such as ATLAS, CMS, LHCb, and others.
