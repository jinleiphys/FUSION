# Simulating collectivity in dense baryon matter with multiple fluids

> A novel three-fluid dynamical model for simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan programme, called MUFFIN, has been developed. The novelty consists of modular inclusion of the Equation of State, use of hyperbolic coordinates that allow to simulate higher collision energies, and fluctuating initial conditions. The model reproduces rapidity and $p_t$ spectra of collisions from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=7.7 to 62.4 GeV. Ideal fluid is assumed, and the elliptic flow is over-predicted, particularly at lower collision energies.

# Simulating collectivity in dense baryon matter with multiple fluids

## Key claim
The MUFFIN three-fluid dynamical model reproduces rapidity and transverse momentum spectra of charged hadrons and net protons across the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range (7.7 to 62.4 GeV), but systematically over-predicts elliptic flow, especially at lower collision energies, because the current implementation assumes ideal fluid dynamics without viscosity.

## Method
The model treats the projectile nucleus, target nucleus, and produced fireball as three separate fluids evolved via the vHLLE hydrodynamics package in hyperbolic coordinates, with energy and momentum transferred between fluids through friction terms. Initial nucleon positions are sampled from a Woods-Saxon distribution at fixed center-of-mass time, projected onto a constant proper-time hyperbola, and smeared to form initial densities. When the summed energy density from all three fluids drops to 0.5 GeV/fm³, a common freeze-out hypersurface is constructed using the CORNELIUS routine, and hadrons are sampled and further propagated with the SMASH transport model.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy range simulated: √s_NN = 7.7 to 62.4 GeV (RHIC Beam Energy Scan)
- 3000 hydrodynamical events per energy, each oversampled with 500 transport events
- Freeze-out hypersurface constructed at energy density threshold of 0.5 GeV/fm³
- Pseudorapidity distributions shown for √s_NN = 19.6 GeV (charged hadrons) and net proton rapidity spectrum at 17.2 GeV (compared to NA49 data)

## Context
This work extends the three-fluid dynamical approach of Ivanov (2005) by adding event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions, modular equation-of-state implementation, and hyperbolic coordinates that enable simulations across the full RHIC Beam Energy Scan range. The model reproduces bulk observables but over-predicts elliptic flow, motivating the next step of introducing viscosity and revising friction terms to improve agreement with data at lower collision energies, particularly for antibaryons and flow observables.
