# Behavior of odd-even mass staggering around 132Sn

> We have performed shell-model calculations of binding energies of nuclei around $^{132}$Sn. The main aim of our study has been to find out if the behavior of odd-even staggering across N=82 is explainable in terms of the shell model. In our calculations, we have employed realistic low-momentum two-body effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential that have already proved quite successful in describing the spectroscopic properties of nuclei in the $^{132}$Sn region. Comparison shows that our results fully explains the trend of the experimental staggering.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the shell model with realistic effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential fully explains the trend of experimental odd-even staggering (OES) of binding energies around $^{132}$Sn, contradicting the suggestion by Hakala et al. (2012) that the OES behavior in $N=83$ isotones is anomalous and beyond current theoretical approaches.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations of binding energies for Sn, Sb, Te, and Xe isotopes beyond $N=82$, using a valence space comprising five proton orbits (50-82 shell) and six neutron orbits (82-126 shell). The two-body effective interaction was derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential renormalized via the $V_{low-k}$ procedure with a cutoff momentum of 2.2 fm$^{-1}$, and the $\hat Q$-box folded-diagram expansion was used to obtain the effective interaction. Single-particle and single-hole energies were taken from experimental spectra of $^{133}$Sb, $^{131}$Sn, and $^{133}$Sn, with exceptions for the proton $2s_{1/2}$ and neutron $0i_{13/2}$ energies taken from earlier work. The OES was calculated using the three-point formula $\Delta^{(3)}(N,Z) = \frac{1}{2} [B(N+1,Z) + B(N-1,Z) - 2B(N,Z)]$ for $N=81$ and $N=83$ isotones.

## Key numbers

- Calculated binding energy of $^{134}$Sn: 5.98 MeV, compared to experimental 6.03 MeV (relative to $^{132}$Sn)
- Calculated binding energy of $^{135}$Sn: 8.37 MeV, compared to experimental 8.30 MeV (relative to $^{132}$Sn)
- Calculated binding energy of $^{136}$Sb: 18.81 MeV, compared to experimental 19.47 MeV (relative to $^{132}$Sn)
- Calculated binding energy of $^{136}$Xe: 40.32 MeV, compared to experimental 39.04 MeV (relative to $^{132}$Sn)
- Largest discrepancy between calculated and experimental binding energies does not exceed 3% across all 12 nuclei considered
- The $J^{\pi}=0^+$ diagonal matrix element for the $(1f_{7/2})^{2}$ configuration in $^{134}$Sn is -0.65 MeV, about 0.5 MeV less attractive than that for the $(0h_{11/2})^{-2}$ configuration in $^{130}$Sn

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of neutron-rich nuclei around $^{132}$Sn (Coraggio et al. 2009, Covello et al. 2011, Danchev et al. 2011) from spectroscopic properties to binding energies and OES. It directly contradicts the conclusion of Hakala et al. (2012), who performed mass measurements using the JYFLTRAP Penning trap and found that self-consistent calculations with the SLy4 Skyrme energy density functional and contact pairing force could not reproduce the experimental OES in $N=83$ isotones. The results enable further confidence in using realistic shell-model effective interactions for predicting properties of neutron-rich nuclei in this region, relevant for upcoming radioactive ion beam facilities.
