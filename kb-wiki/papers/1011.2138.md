# Elastic electron-deuteron scattering and two-body current operators in the Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics

> The electromagnetic properties of the deuteron are investigated within a Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics framework, with a current operator that contains both one-body and two-body contributions. In this work, we are considering new two-body contributions, with a dynamical nature generated within a Yukawa model and a structure inspired by a recent analysis of the current operator, that acts on the three-dimensional valence component and fulfills the Ward-Takahashi identity. Preliminary results for the magnetic moment are shown.

## Key claim

The authors present preliminary results for the deuteron magnetic moment using a Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics framework that includes new dynamical two-body current contributions, but these results are obtained with only a subset of the two-body diagrams (the interaction terms, not the pair or instantaneous terms) and therefore represent an incomplete calculation.

## Method

The authors project a four-dimensional electromagnetic current from a Yukawa model in ladder approximation onto the three-dimensional Light-Front hyperplane, obtaining a current operator that fulfills the Ward-Takahashi identity. They then evaluate matrix elements of this current between deuteron states described by realistic NN wave functions (CD-Bonn, RSC93, AV18) in a Breit frame with longitudinal momentum transfer, using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction to maintain Poincaré covariance. The current includes one-body Dirac and Pauli nucleon currents plus first-order two-body contributions from one-pion exchange, though only the interaction-type diagrams (first line of Fig. 1) are included in the present preliminary results.

## Key numbers

- CD-Bonn interaction: deuteron magnetic moment with one-body plus two-body current = 0.863 ± 0.002 nuclear magnetons (experimental value 0.857406(1))
- RSC93 interaction: deuteron magnetic moment with one-body plus two-body current = 0.861 ± 0.002 nuclear magnetons
- AV18 interaction: deuteron magnetic moment with one-body plus two-body current = 0.860 ± 0.002 nuclear magnetons
- D-state probability for CD-Bonn: 4.83%; for RSC93: 5.70%; for AV18: 5.76%
- Probability of non-valence component: approximately 0.01
- Two-photon exchange effects estimated at approximately 1% (citing prior work)

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by the same group (LPS2, LPS3) that included only one-body currents plus a Hermiticity-preserving two-body term, by adding dynamical two-body currents derived from a Yukawa model analysis (WTI). The approach aims to provide a fully Poincaré-covariant description of deuteron electromagnetic observables that can be tested against upcoming Jefferson Lab 12 GeV data, and complements non-relativistic studies of isobar configurations (IC) and QCD-based descriptions of the deuteron (Brodsky, Gross).
