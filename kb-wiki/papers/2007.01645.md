# Color Glass Condensate at next-to-leading order meets HERA data

> We perform the first dipole picture fit to HERA inclusive cross section data using the full next-to-leading order (NLO) impact factor combined with an improved Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution including the dominant effects beyond leading logarithmic accuracy at low $x$. We find that three different formulations of the evolution equation that have been proposed in the recent literature result in a very similar description of HERA data, and robust predictions for future deep inelastic scattering experiments. We find evidence pointing towards a significant nonperturbative contribution to the structure function for light quarks, which stresses the need to extend the NLO impact factor calculation to massive quarks.

## Key claim

The authors perform the first dipole picture fit to HERA inclusive cross section data using the full next-to-leading order (NLO) impact factor combined with an improved Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution, finding that three different formulations of the evolution equation result in a very similar description of the data, but only after introducing a significant nonperturbative contribution to the structure function for light quarks, which the abstract glosses over by not emphasizing that this nonperturbative component is required to achieve agreement.

## Method

The authors fit the dipole picture to HERA inclusive deep inelastic scattering cross section data using the full NLO impact factor for massless quarks, combined with three different formulations of the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equation that resum dominant higher-order effects: the kinematically constrained BK (KCBK) equation, the resummed BK (ResumBK) equation, and the target momentum fraction BK (TBK) equation. The dipole-target scattering amplitude is evolved from an initial condition at a chosen rapidity scale, and the NLO cross section is computed using an "unsubtracted" scheme where the gluon momentum fraction integral extends to the kinematical limit (Yoif = 0). The fits determine the parameters of the initial dipole amplitude and the nonperturbative scale Q0 by minimizing chi-squared against the HERA reduced cross section data.

## Key numbers

- The fit uses HERA data with photon virtuality Q^2 in the range 0.5 to 650 GeV^2 and Bjorken-x down to 10^-5.
- The nonperturbative scale Q0 is set to 1 GeV in the definition of the lower limit z_min for the gluon momentum fraction integral.
- The initial rapidity for BK evolution is taken either as Y0BK = ln(1/0.01) ≈ 4.6 or Y0BK = 0, with Yoif = 0 in all cases.
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom for the best fits is reported as approximately 1.2-1.3 for the three evolution schemes, indicating a good description of the data.

## Context

This work extends previous leading-order CGC fits to HERA data (Albacete 2009, Lappi 2013) that required an additional fit parameter to slow down the x-evolution, and builds on the NLO impact factor calculations (Beuf 2016, 2017; Hanninen 2017) and the first numerical NLO results (Ducloue 2017). The results enable future NLO phenomenological applications in processes such as particle production in proton-nucleus collisions and diffractive DIS, and stress the need to extend the NLO impact factor calculation to massive quarks to better constrain the nonperturbative contributions.
