# Fermi-Dirac Integrals in Degenerate Regimes: A Novel Asymptotic Expansion

> We characterize in a novel manner the physical properties of the low temperature Fermi gas in the degenerate domain as a function of temperature and chemical potential. For the first time we obtain low temperature $T$ results in the domain where several fermions are found within a de Broglie spatial cell. In this regime, the usual high degeneracy Sommerfeld expansion fails. The other known semi-classical Boltzmann domain applies when fewer than one particle is found in the de Broglie cell. We also improve on the understanding of the Sommerfeld expansion in the regime where the chemical potential is close to the mass and also in the high temperature regime. In these calculcations we use a novel characterization of the Fermi distribution allowing the separation of the finite and zero temperature phenomena. The relative errors of the three approximate methods (Boltzmann limit, Sommerfeld expansion, and the new domain of several particles in the de Broglie cell) are quantified.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the traditional Sommerfeld expansion for low-temperature Fermi-Dirac integrals fails in the regime where the chemical potential is close to the particle mass (small \(\Delta\mu \equiv \mu - m\)) and where a few fermions (2 to 3) occupy a de Broglie volume, and that a novel asymptotic expansion derived here covers this previously uncharacterized "dilute quantum region."

## Method

The authors derive asymptotic expansions for thermal averages of the form \(\langle G \rangle_T = \int d^D p \, G(p) f_{FD}(p)\) by first decomposing the Fermi-Dirac distribution into a zero-temperature Heaviside step function plus two finite-temperature components that are exponentially decaying. They then apply Taylor expansion and error bounding techniques to obtain expansions valid in three distinct regimes: the traditional Sommerfeld regime (many particles per de Broglie volume), a new dilute quantum regime (a few particles per de Broglie volume), and the Boltzmann regime (fewer than one particle per de Broglie volume). The relative errors of each approximation are quantified by comparison with numerical quadrature.

## Key numbers

* For \(D=3\) systems, the traditional Sommerfeld expansion applies when the number of particles per de Broglie volume is greater than approximately 4.5 for \(T \ll m\).
* For \(D=3\) systems, the new dilute quantum regime applies when the number of particles per de Broglie volume is between approximately 2 and 3 at low temperature.
* The Boltzmann limit is valid for all temperatures when the number of particles per de Broglie volume is near to or below 1.
* For the energy density to pressure ratio \(\varepsilon/P\) in \(D=3\), the Sommerfeld regime applies when there are approximately 3.5 or more particles per de Broglie volume, while the new regime applies when \(\varepsilon/P\) is larger than around 10.0.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of Fermi-Dirac integrals beyond the traditional Sommerfeld expansion (Landau and Lifshitz, 2013) and the Boltzmann limit, addressing a gap noted in textbooks such as Kuebler (2021) where the Sommerfeld expansion is known to fail but no alternative expansion existed. The results are relevant to warm nuclear matter in neutron star mergers (Oertel 2016, Perego 2019, MUSES collaboration 2023), free-streaming cosmic neutrino backgrounds (Birrell 2013, Trautner 2016), and dense quark-gluon plasma (Elze 1980, Letessier 2002). The new expansion enables improved modeling of systems that pass through the transient regime of a few particles per de Broglie volume, such as during neutron star formation or cosmological expansion.
