# Systematic Analysis of Double-Beta Decay Half Lives

> Evaluated $2β^{-}(2ν)$ half-lives and their systematics were reexamined in the framework of a phenomenological approach. Decay rate dependence on nuclear deformation, decay energy, shape coexistence, and forbidden transitions was observed. The following analysis showed distinct impacts of decay energy on half-lives and deformation parameters on effective nuclear matrix elements. These findings were used to predict $T_{1/2}$ for 36 isotopes of interest. Present work results were compared with published data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that double-beta decay half-lives can be systematically described by a phenomenological formula inversely proportional to the eighth power of decay energy and the fifth power of the quadrupole deformation parameter, but this simple relation fails for nuclei exhibiting shape coexistence (e.g., \(^{96}\)Zr) or first-forbidden transitions (e.g., \(^{76}\)Ge, \(^{82}\)Se, \(^{150}\)Nd), requiring ad-hoc correction factors.

## Method

The author compiled evaluated \(2\beta^{-}(2\nu)\) half-lives for 11 even-even nuclei from the NNDC evaluation (Pritychenko 2014) and performed a least-squares fit to a phenomenological formula \(T_{1/2}^{2\nu} \approx x / (\xi^{2} Q_{2\beta}^{y} \beta_{2}^{z})\), where \(\xi\) is the Coulomb energy parameter, \(Q_{2\beta}\) is the decay energy, and \(\beta_{2}\) is the quadrupole deformation parameter. The fit yielded exponents \(y = 8.16(59)\) and \(z = 4.98(32)\). The formula was then used to predict half-lives for 36 isotopes, with corrections applied for shape coexistence (increasing \(\beta_2\) by a factor of 2.5 for \(^{96}\)Zr and \(^{98}\)Mo) and for negative-parity intermediate states (reducing \(\beta_2\) by a factor of 2/3 for transitions through odd-odd nuclei with negative ground-state parity).

## Key numbers

- Fitting parameters: \(x = 0.00301 \times 10^{24}\), \(y = 8.16(59)\), \(z = 4.98(32)\).
- Evaluated half-life of \(^{100}\)Mo: \((6.98 \pm 0.44) \times 10^{18}\) years.
- Evaluated half-life of \(^{130}\)Te: \((7.14 \pm 1.04) \times 10^{20}\) years.
- Evaluated half-life of \(^{136}\)Xe: \((2.34 \pm 0.13) \times 10^{21}\) years.
- Evaluated half-life of \(^{48}\)Ca: \((4.39 \pm 0.58) \times 10^{19}\) years.
- Correlation constant \(k \approx 29.3\) relating \(|M_{eff}^{2\nu}|^2\) to \(\beta_2^5\).

## Context

This work extends the Grodzins method (Grodzins 1962) and the Primakoff-Rosen phase-space scaling (Primakoff and Rosen 1959, 1969) to include nuclear deformation effects, building on earlier systematics for \(^{128,130}\)Te (Pritychenko 2014). It contradicts the approach of the Nanjing-Lanzhou group (Ren 2014), which used Geiger-Nuttall and Viola-Seaborg analogies but failed to capture nuclear structure effects like shape coexistence and forbidden transitions. The predictions enable sensitivity estimates for future double-beta decay experiments across the nuclear chart.
