# Clustering effects induced by light nuclei

> Since the pioneering discovery, half a century ago, of 12C+12C molecular resonances, a great deal of research work has been undertaken in theSince the pioneering discovery, half a century ago, of 12C+12C molecular resonances, a great deal of research work has been undertaken in the alpha-clustering study. Our knowledge in the physics of nuclear molecules has increased considerably and nuclear clustering remains one of the most fruitful domains of nuclear physics, facing some of the greatest challenges and opportunities in the years ahead. In this work, the occurence of "exotic" shapes in light N=Z alpha-like nuclei is investigated. Various approaches of superdeformed and hyperdeformed bands associated with quasimolecular resonant structures are presented. Clustering aspects are also discussed for light nuclei with neutron excess through very recent results on neutron-rich Oxygen isotopes.

## Key claim

Clustering phenomena in light nuclei, including superdeformed and hyperdeformed shapes, are linked to quasimolecular resonant structures, but the exact connection between scattering states in the ion-ion potential and true cluster states in compound systems remains unresolved.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical work on alpha-clustering, superdeformation, and hyperdeformation in light N=Z alpha-conjugate nuclei (A=20-60), and presents new results on neutron-rich oxygen isotopes. Data were collected using the Binary Reaction Spectrometer (BRS) in coincidence with the Euroball IV array at the Vivitron Tandem facility, including analysis of the 24Mg+12C reaction at Elab(24Mg)=130 MeV and alpha-transfer reactions to study 16O and the oxygen isotopes 18O and 20O.

## Key numbers

- The 3+ state of 16O at 11.085 MeV ± 3 keV has a total width Γ3+ < 0.23 eV, a value fifty times lower than known previously.
- The 3+ state of 16O decays to the 2+ state at 6.92 MeV with branching ratio 54.6 ± 2% and to the 3- state at 6.13 MeV with branching ratio 45.4%.
- A predicted 0+6 state in 16O with Hoyle-state-like structure is expected at about 15.1 MeV, approximately 700 keV above the 4α-particle breakup threshold.
- Superdeformed shapes in light nuclei have quadrupole deformation parameter β2 ≈ 0.6 (2:1 axis ratio), while hyperdeformed shapes have β2 ≈ 1.0 (3:1 axis ratio).
- The 14C–6He and 16C–4He molecular structures in 20O start well below thresholds of 16.8 MeV and 12.32 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the classic Ikeda diagram (Ikeda, 1968) for N=Z alpha-cluster nuclei and the extended Ikeda diagram proposed by von Oertzen (2001) for neutron-rich nuclei, by further including 14C cluster cores alongside 16O cores. The results support Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) calculations by Furutachi et al. (2008) and generator-coordinate-method calculations by Descouvemont, and enable future searches for alpha-condensate states in 16O (the 4α analog of the Hoyle state) and for hyperdeformed configurations in rapidly rotating medium-mass nuclei using combined gamma-ray and charged-particle detection techniques.
