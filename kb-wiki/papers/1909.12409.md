# Topology of Generalized Spinors and Chiral Anomaly

> Weyl fermions with nonlinear dispersion have appeared in real world systems, such as in the Weyl semi-metals and topological insulators. We consider the most general form of Dirac operators, and study its topological properties embedded in the chiral anomaly, in the index theorem, and in the odd-dimensional partition function, by employing the heat kernel. We find that all of these topological quantities are enhanced by a winding number defined by the Dirac operator in the momentum space, regardless of the spacetime dimensions. The chiral anomaly in $d=3+1$, in particular, is also confirmed via the conventional Feynman diagram. These interconnected results allow us to clarify the relationship between the chiral anomaly and the Chern number of the Berry connection, under dispute in some recent literatures, and also lead to a compact proof of the Nielsen-Ninomiya theorem.

## Key claim

The chiral anomaly and other topological quantities for generalized Dirac operators (where the Dirac operator is an arbitrary polynomial or smooth function of covariant derivatives) are multiplicatively enhanced by a winding number defined by the map from momentum space to the target space of the operator, regardless of spacetime dimension; this provides a rigorous infrared-ultraviolet connection in momentum space that clarifies the disputed relationship between the chiral anomaly and the Chern number of the Berry connection.

## Method

The authors employ the heat kernel method to compute the index density and chiral anomaly for a generalized Dirac operator of the form $\gamma^\mu \mathcal{P}_\mu(-iD)$, where $\mathcal{P}_\mu$ are smooth functions of the covariant momentum operator. They expand the heat kernel in an iterative series, using the free heat kernel in momentum space as the zeroth-order term, and extract the topological contribution from the commutator terms $[\mathcal{P}_\mu,\mathcal{P}_\nu]$ in the squared Dirac operator. The chiral anomaly in $d=3+1$ is also confirmed via the conventional triangular Feynman diagram approach.

## Key numbers

- The chiral anomaly in $d=3+1$ is enhanced by a factor $N_\mathcal{P}$, giving $N_\mathcal{P} \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{8\pi^2} \int F \wedge F\right)$ for the index density, where $N_\mathcal{P}$ is the winding number of the map $K \rightarrow \mathcal{P}(K)$.
- The winding number in $d$ dimensions is defined as $N_\mathcal{P} \equiv \frac{1}{(\sqrt{\pi})^d} \int_{\mathbb{R}^d} d^d K \; \det(\tilde{\partial}^\alpha \mathcal{P}_\mu) e^{-\mathcal{P}(K)^2}$, which produces an integer when $\mathcal{P}^2$ is asymptotically unbounded.
- For non-relativistic Weyl fermions in $3+1$ dimensions with $\mathcal{D} = \sigma^a P_a(-i\vec{D}) - D_4$, the anomaly coefficient is $N_{\vec{P}} \equiv \frac{s^{3/2}}{\sqrt{\pi}^3} \int d^3k \; \det\left(\frac{\partial P_a}{\partial k_h}\right) e^{-s\vec{P}^2}$, which equals the winding number of the map $\vec{k} \rightarrow \vec{P}(\vec{k})$.
- The Berry connection in momentum space for the Hamiltonian $\sigma^a P_a(\vec{k})$ carries precisely $2\pi N_{\vec{P}}$ total flux, where $N_{\vec{P}}$ is the same winding number.

## Context

This work extends the Fujikawa method (Fujikawa, 1979) for chiral anomaly to generalized Dirac operators with nonlinear dispersion, which have appeared in Weyl semi-metals and topological insulators (Wan, 2011; Burkov, 2011; Xu, 2011). It provides a rigorous answer to questions raised by Fujikawa (2005, 2017) and Mueller (2019) regarding the connection between chiral anomaly and Berry's curvature in momentum space. The results also lead to a compact proof of the Nielsen-Ninomiya theorem and clarify the relationship between the chiral anomaly and the Chern number of the Berry connection, which was under dispute in recent literature. The infrared-ultraviolet connection demonstrated here fills the logical gap between the kinetic theory description of chiral anomaly (Son, 2012; Stephanov, 2012) and the Berry phase approach.
