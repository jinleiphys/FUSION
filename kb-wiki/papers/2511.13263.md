# Vortex creep heating in neutron star cooling with direct Urca processes in heavy neutron stars

> Old, thermally bright neutron stars imply internal heating at late times. Among candidate mechanisms, vortex creep heating (VCH) provides a robust link between spin-down and frictional dissipation in the pinned inner-crust superfluid, yet its interplay with fast DUrca cooling in massive stars remains insufficiently explored. We (i) implement VCH in our cooling code and validate it; (ii) identify the physically consistent domain where the steady-state form $L_{\text{h}}=J|\dotΩ_\infty|$ applies; (iii) quantify how $(B,P_0)$ regulate observable VCH signatures under DUrca cooling; and (iv) introduce a 3D representation that resolves degeneracies hidden in standard 2D projections. Cooling is computed with BSk24 and APR EoS, standard pairing gaps, and iron/carbon envelopes. VCH is modeled with $J\simeq10^{42.9\text{--}43.8}$ erg s, and a quantum-creep coverage fraction $f_{\text{Q}}(t)$ diagnoses when steady-state heating is valid. We survey $B=10^{10\text{--}13}$ G and $P_0=10$--$570$ ms for $1.4$ and $2.0\,M_\odot$, and compare with a curated set of ordinary pulsars with measured $(P,\dot P)$. Results: (1) Our implementation reproduces published VCH bands. (2) The $(B,P_0)$ validity boundary follows magnetic-dipole spin-down, confirming consistency with $|\dotΩ|$. (3) DUrca+VCH maintains $T_{\text{s}}^\infty\gtrsim10^5$ K for $B\gtrsim10^{11-12}$ G up to $P_0\sim10^2$ ms. (4) The 3D representation shows that sources appearing coincident in $(t,T_{\text{s}}^\infty)$ occupy distinct $B$-layers, removing degeneracies. VCH can substantially reshape late-time thermal states when spin-down power remains high; its observability depends chiefly on $(B,P_0)$ rather than on mass alone. We provide a practical $(B,P_0)$ validity map for $L_{\text{h}}=J|\dotΩ_\infty|$ and advocate treating $B$ as a co-equal axis in cooling analyses. (Shortened due to the arXiv words limit.)

# Vortex Creep Heating in Neutron Star Cooling with Direct Urca Processes in Heavy Neutron Stars

## Key claim

Vortex creep heating (VCH) can substantially reshape the late-time thermal states of massive neutron stars undergoing rapid direct Urca cooling, but its observability depends primarily on the surface magnetic field strength and initial spin period rather than on mass alone; the steady-state heating formula L_h = J|Ω̇_∞| is only valid once the quantum-creep coverage fraction f_Q(t) approaches unity, which the authors verify through a diagnostic criterion rather than assuming a priori.

## Method

The authors implemented VCH as an inner-crust volumetric heating source in a one-dimensional general-relativistic cooling code, using the BSk24 and APR equations of state with standard baryon pairing gap sets (SFB neutron ¹S₀, CCDK proton ¹S₀, TToa neutron ³P₂) and iron/carbon envelopes. They introduced a quantum-creep coverage fraction f_Q(t) to diagnose when the system enters the temperature-independent quantum tunneling regime, requiring this condition for computational validity of the steady-state heating law. Cooling simulations were performed for 1.4 and 2.0 M⊙ stars, surveying magnetic fields B = 10¹⁰–10¹³ G and initial spin periods P₀ = 10–570 ms, with results compared against a curated set of ordinary pulsars with measured (P, Ṗ).

## Key numbers

- The proportional constant J for VCH is J ≃ 10⁴²·⁹–⁴³·⁸ erg s, adopted from Fujiwara (2024)
- The quantum-creep transition temperature T_Q ≃ 3.8 × 10⁸ K for typical inner-crust parameters with ω₀ ≃ 10²⁰ s⁻¹
- DUrca+VCH tracks in 2.0 M⊙ stars maintain T_s^∞ ≳ 10⁵ K for B ≳ 10¹¹–¹² G up to P₀ ∼ O(10²) ms
- The superfluid-fraction switch χ_sf changes T_s^∞ by ≲ 0.7% once heating matters
- Envelope composition explored over range 10⁻¹⁸ M⊙ ≤ ΔM ≤ 10⁻⁷ M⊙
- Magnetic field survey covers B = 10¹⁰–¹³ G; initial spin periods cover P₀ = 10–570 ms

## Context

This work extends the VCH framework of Fujiwara (2024) and Alpar (1984) to massive neutron stars where direct Urca cooling (Lattimer 1991) would otherwise overcool old objects, addressing an interplay previously unexplored. The authors validate their implementation against published VCH bands and provide a practical (B, P₀) validity map for using the steady-state heating law L_h = J|Ω̇_∞|. The 3D representation (log₁₀ t, log₁₀ T_s^∞, log₁₀ B) resolves degeneracies in standard 2D cooling projections and sets the stage for future upgrades including anisotropic conduction, magnetic field evolution, and microscopic calibration of J from pinning theory.
