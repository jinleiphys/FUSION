# Adiabatic hyperspherical analysis of realistic nuclear potentials

> Using the hyperspherical adiabatic method with the realistic nuclear potentials Argonne V14, Argonne V18, and Argonne V18 with the Urbana IX three-body potential, we calculate the adiabatic potentials and the triton bound state energies. We find that a discrete variable representation with the slow variable discretization method along the hyperradial degree of freedom results in energies consistent with the literature. However, using a Laguerre basis results in missing energy, even when extrapolated to an infinite number of basis functions and channels. We do not include the isospin $T=3/2$ contribution in our analysis.

## Key claim

Using the hyperspherical adiabatic method with realistic nuclear potentials, the authors find that a discrete variable representation (DVR) with the slow variable discretization (SVD) method yields triton bound state energies consistent with literature benchmarks, whereas a Laguerre polynomial basis with finite-difference coupling matrix elements systematically misses binding energy even after extrapolation to infinite basis size. The analysis excludes the isospin T=3/2 contribution.

## Method

The authors solve the three-nucleon Schrödinger equation in hyperspherical coordinates by first diagonalizing the adiabatic Hamiltonian at fixed hyperradius R using a basis of symmetrized hyperspherical harmonics (up to 1052 basis functions across 18 channels) for the Argonne V14, Argonne V18, and AV18 plus Urbana IX potentials. They then solve the coupled hyperradial equations using either a Laguerre polynomial basis with seven-point finite-difference coupling matrix elements or a DVR basis with the SVD method that avoids explicit calculation of the nonadiabatic P and Q coupling matrices.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy for AV14 potential using DVR/SVD method: -7.685(1) MeV
- Triton binding energy for AV18 potential using DVR/SVD method: -7.619(2) MeV
- Triton binding energy for AV18 with Urbana IX three-body potential using DVR/SVD method: -8.475(2) MeV
- Laguerre basis extrapolation for AV14 potential gave -7.671(2) MeV, missing approximately 10 keV of binding compared to literature values
- The hyperangular basis of 617 functions (227 with L=0, 185 with L=1, 205 with L=2) describes the three-nucleon bound state with about 1 keV accuracy
- DVR calculation used 40 SVD sectors: 20 from R=0.2 fm to 3 fm and 20 from R=3 fm to 50 fm, each with 5 DVR points

## Context

This work extends the hyperspherical adiabatic method, well-established in atomic physics by Macek (1968) and Lin (1974), to realistic nuclear potentials for the three-nucleon problem. The authors compare their DVR/SVD results against the benchmark calculations of Nogga (2003) and find complete agreement, while demonstrating that a simpler Laguerre basis with finite-difference couplings fails to converge properly. This technique enables future calculation of three-nucleon scattering observables, including breakup amplitudes, using methods described by Kievsky (2012) and Garrido (2014).
