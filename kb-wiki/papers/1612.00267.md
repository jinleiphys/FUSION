# Alpha Conjugate Neck Structures in the Collisions of 35 MeV/nucleon 40Ca with 40Ca

> The de-excitation of alpha-conjugate nuclei produced in reactions of 35 MeV/nucleon 40Ca with 40Ca has been investigated. Particular emphasis is placed on examining the dynamics of collisions leading to projectile-like fragment exit channels. A general exploration of the reaction systematics reveals the binary dissipative character of the collisions and a hierarchy effect similar to that seen for heavier systems. Investigation of the subset of events characterized by a total alpha-conjugate mass (alpha particles plus alpha-conjugate fragments) equal to 40 and atomic number equal to 20 reveals a dominance of alpha-conjugate exit channels. The hierarchy effect for these channels leads to the production of alpha-clustered neck structures with potentially exotic geometries and properties.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in mid-peripheral collisions of 40Ca with 40Ca at 35 MeV/nucleon, the de-excitation of projectile-like fragments with total alpha-conjugate mass 40 and atomic number 20 is dominated by alpha-conjugate exit channels, and that the observed hierarchy effect (mass-velocity correlation) indicates these fragments arise from alpha-clustered neck structures rather than from fully equilibrated compound nuclei. The authors hedge that the degree to which this large fraction of alpha-conjugate de-excitations reflects the initial alpha-conjugate nature of 40Ca versus the dynamic evolution of excitation and density warrants further investigation.

## Method

The experiment used a 40Ca beam at 35 MeV/nucleon from the K500 superconducting cyclotron at Texas A&M University, impinging on a 40Ca target. Reaction products were measured with the NIMROD-ISiS 4π array, which provided isotopic resolution up to Z=12 and elemental identification up to Z=20 in forward rings. Events were selected for projectile-like fragments with A=40 and Z=20, then further filtered for those with total detected alpha-conjugate mass equal to 40, excluding events with non-alpha-conjugate fragments (11% of events) and alpha particles with source frame energies above 40 MeV. Experimental results were compared to Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) calculations coupled with the GEMINI statistical de-excitation code, filtered through the experimental acceptance.

## Key numbers

* The linear fit of average excitation energy vs exit channel separation energy for A=40, Z=20 PLF exit channels yields a slope parameter of 2.39, well above the value of 2 expected for statistical emission.
* Events with alpha-conjugate mass = 40 account for 61% of the A=40, Z=20 PLF events detected.
* Detected events with alpha-conjugate mass = 40 account for 0.23% of the total experimental events collected; filtered AMD calculations predict about half that amount, 0.11%.
* The projectile velocity is 8.0 cm/ns and the center-of-mass velocity is 4.0 cm/ns.
* Average proton multiplicities are approximately 2, dominated by emission from an intermediate velocity source at approximately half the projectile velocity.

## Context

This work extends studies of alpha clustering in nuclear reactions at near-Fermi energies, building on prior observations of enhanced alpha emission during thermal expansion of projectile-like fragments in 25 MeV/nucleon 40Ca + 12C collisions by Borderie et al. (2016). The authors note that the observed hierarchy effect (mass-velocity correlation) is similar to that reported for heavier systems by Colin et al. (2003) and Baran et al. (2004), and that the sphericity-coplanarity analysis suggests exit channels with many alpha particles result from initial breakup into larger excited fragments followed by alpha particle de-excitation. The results enable further investigation of whether the large fraction of alpha-conjugate de-excitations reflects the initial alpha-conjugate nature of 40Ca or the dynamic evolution of excitation and density, and whether short-lived Bose condensates might be fleetingly produced in such collisions.
