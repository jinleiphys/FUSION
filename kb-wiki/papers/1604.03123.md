# On the symmetries of the 12C nucleus

> The consequences of some symmetries of the three-alpha system are discussed. In particular, the recent description of the low-energy spectrum of the 12C nucleus in terms of the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) is compared to that of the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM). The previous one applies interactions of a D3h geometric symmetry [1], while the latter one has a U(3) multichannel dynamical symmetry, that connects the shell and cluster pictures. The available data is in line with both descriptions.

# On the symmetries of the 12C nucleus

## Key claim

The paper argues that both the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) with its $D_{3h}$ geometric symmetry and the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) with its U(3) multichannel dynamical symmetry (MUSY) produce very similar descriptions of the low-energy spectrum of 12C, and that further experimental details on missing states and transitions are essential to decide which symmetry is realized to a better approximation.

## Method

The authors compare the energy spectrum of 12C calculated from the U(3) multichannel dynamical symmetry (MUSY) of the SACM, using a Hamiltonian with five fitted parameters, against the experimental spectrum and the spectrum from the $D_{3h}$ limit of the ACM. The U(3) MUSY Hamiltonian includes terms for oscillator quanta, SU(3) second-order and third-order invariants, a K-dependent term, and a rotational term with a classically calculated moment of inertia. The parameters were fitted to experimental data on an equal footing with the ACM treatment.

## Key numbers

- The U(3) MUSY Hamiltonian parameters fitted to experimental data: $e = 37.8$ MeV, $a = -2.4879$ MeV, $b = 0.045432$ MeV, $f = -0.98366$ MeV, $d = 1.1661$ MeV.
- The U(3) MUSY description uses 5 parameters, compared to 6 parameters in the $D_{3h}$ ACM model of the Birmingham group (Marin-Lambarri et al., 2014).
- The (0,4) SU(3) symmetry has the largest contribution to the ground state, and the (12,0) symmetry is the head of the symplectic band dominating the Hoyle-states.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between the $D_{3h}$ geometric symmetry description of the three-alpha system by the Birmingham group (Marin-Lambarri et al., 2014) and the U(3) multichannel dynamical symmetry approach developed by Cseh and collaborators (Cseh, 1992; Cseh and Lévai, 1994). The paper builds on the SACM framework (Cseh, 1992) and the SAQM (Cseh, 1997), which connect cluster and shell model pictures. The results enable future work where operators with $D_{3h}$ symmetry from the ACM could be applied on the SACM model space that incorporates the Pauli principle, combining all symmetries only partly used by different models so far.
