# Investigation of the Lorentz invariance violation in two-neutrino double-beta decay

> We make a comprehensive investigation of the Lorentz invariance violation (LIV) effects that may occur in two-neutrino double-beta ($2νββ$) decay for all the experimentally interesting nuclei. We deduce the formulas for the LIV deviations and provide single and summed energy electron spectra and angular correlation between electrons with and without LIV contributions, to be used for constraining the LIV coefficient $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$. First, we confirm the shifting of the electron spectra to higher electron energies due to LIV for all nuclei. Next, we analyze other LIV signatures that can be used in LIV investigations. Thus, from the comparison of the electron and angular correlation spectra calculated with the inclusion of the LIV contributions, with their standard forms, information can be obtained about the strength versus observability of the LIV effects in the current experimental statistics. Then, we present the alternative method of constraining $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$ from the measurement of the angular correlation coefficient and estimate the statistics that different double-beta decay experiments should reach to constrain the LIV coefficient at the level of the current beta decay experiments. We hope that our work will improve the theoretical support and further stimulate the search for LIV in double-beta decay.

# Investigation of the Lorentz invariance violation in two-neutrino double-beta decay

## Key claim

Lorentz invariance violation (LIV) effects in two-neutrino double-beta decay shift the single and summed energy electron spectra to higher electron energies for all experimentally interesting nuclei, but the magnitude and observability of these deviations depend strongly on the sign and magnitude of the LIV coefficient $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$, with deviations becoming pronounced only near the Q-value and being undetectable in current experimental statistics for the most stringent limits reported by NEMO-3.

## Method

The authors derived formulas for LIV deviations in the differential decay rate of $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay using the Standard-Model Extension (SME) framework, where the neutrino four-momentum is modified by the isotropic oscillation-free coefficient $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$. They computed phase-space factors and their LIV perturbations by numerically solving the Dirac equation with the RADIAL subroutine package to obtain exact electron radial wave functions in a realistic Coulomb potential that includes finite nuclear size and atomic screening effects, using either the single state dominance (SSD) hypothesis for $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, and $^{150}$Nd or the higher state dominance (HSD) hypothesis for the remaining nuclei.

## Key numbers

* For $^{48}$Ca (Q-value 4.2681 MeV): SM angular correlation coefficient $k_{\text{SM}} = -0.7673$, LIV parameter $\xi_{\text{LIV}} = -3.4931$ MeV$^{-1}$, and $8.4060 \times 10^8$ events needed to constrain $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$ at the tritium decay limit of $3 \times 10^{-5}$ MeV
* For $^{76}$Ge (Q-value 2.0391 MeV): $k_{\text{SM}} = -0.5608$, $\xi_{\text{LIV}} = -4.9831$ MeV$^{-1}$, requiring $4.4625 \times 10^8$ events for the same constraint
* For $^{100}$Mo$_{\text{SSD}}$ (Q-value 3.0344 MeV): $k_{\text{SM}} = -0.6690$, $\xi_{\text{LIV}} = -4.2939$ MeV$^{-1}$, requiring $5.7932 \times 10^8$ events
* For $^{136}$Xe (Q-value 2.4587 MeV): $k_{\text{SM}} = -0.6483$, $\xi_{\text{LIV}} = -4.6828$ MeV$^{-1}$, requiring $4.9082 \times 10^8$ events
* EXO collaboration limits on $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$: $-2.65 \times 10^{-2}$ MeV $\le \mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)} \le 7.6 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV
* NEMO-3 collaboration limits: $-4.2 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV $\le \mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)} \le 3.5 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV
* Tritium decay limit used for comparison: $|\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}| < 3 \times 10^{-5}$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of LIV effects in $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay from the single nucleus $^{100}$Mo (Nitescu et al., 2020; Nitescu et al., 2021) to all nine experimentally relevant isotopes: $^{48}$Ca, $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{110}$Pd, $^{116}$Cd, $^{130}$Te, $^{136}$Xe, and $^{150}$Nd. It improves upon earlier LIV investigations by EXO (2016), GERDA, AURORA (2018), NEMO-3 (2019), and CUORE (2019) that used approximate analytical Fermi functions from point-like nucleus models, by employing exact electron wave functions from numerical Dirac equation solutions. The work provides theoretical predictions for constraining the isotropic oscillation-free LIV coefficient $\mathring{a}_{\text{of}}^{(3)}$ and enables future experiments like SuperNEMO to search for LIV signatures with improved sensitivity.
