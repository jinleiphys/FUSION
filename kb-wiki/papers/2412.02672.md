# Soft QCD Physics at the LHC: highlights and opportunities

> The Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN in Switzerland became operational in 2009 and has since then produced a plethora of results for proton-proton (pp) collisions. This short review covers results that relates to soft QCD focusing on non-diffractive physics at mid-rapidity. Most of the presented results comes from transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) spectra and related/derived observables such as multiplicity, $\langle p_{\rm T} \rangle$ and ratios, but also the observed ``ridge'' and the questions of quark-gluon plasma in pp collisions will be discussed. The goal of the review is on one hand to introduce the topics and provide references for scientists joining the LHC program while at the same time highlighting what we consider the most interesting results and open questions to inspire novel measurements.

# Soft QCD Physics at the LHC: highlights and opportunities

## Key claim
Soft QCD phenomena in proton-proton collisions at the LHC cannot be fully described by perturbative QCD even for transverse momenta up to 8-10 GeV/c, and the question of whether color reconnection alone or quark-gluon plasma formation is responsible for observed collective effects remains unresolved, representing the biggest open question in the field.

## Method
The authors review experimental results from LHC collaborations (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, TOTEM) on non-diffractive soft QCD physics at mid-rapidity, focusing on transverse momentum spectra, charged-particle multiplicity distributions, and underlying event observables. They compare measurements to predictions from Monte Carlo event generators (Pythia 8, Herwig 7, EPOS LHC) that implement multiple parton interactions with different regularization schemes, color reconnection models, and hadronization mechanisms including the Lund string model, cluster model, and core-corona approach with hydrodynamic expansion.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale Q_s for protons at the LHC reaches approximately 1-2 GeV/c, increasing with center-of-mass energy
- The ratio of elastic to total cross section at LHC energies is approximately 0.3, approaching the theoretical black disk limit of 0.5
- The total cross section remains a factor of approximately 100 below the Froissart-Martin unitarity bound
- The p_T0 regularization scale in Pythia is of order 2 GeV/c at LHC energies, corresponding to length scales smaller than 0.1 fm
- The maximal rapidity for 13 TeV pp collisions is approximately ±9.6
- Baryon behavior remains distinctly different from hard QCD predictions out to p_T of 8-10 GeV/c
- Most particles with p_T ≤ 1-2 GeV/c are produced in soft processes, while p_T > 10 GeV/c is needed for the fully hard regime
- The turn-on region for UE observables shows discrepancies around p_T^lead of approximately 7 GeV/c

## Context
This review extends the pre-LHC overviews of soft QCD by Grosse-Oetringhaus (2009) and Kittel (2005), and the MPI review by Bartalini (2018), while updating the discussion of Monte Carlo tuning results from ATLAS (A14 tune, 2014) and CMS (CP5 tune, 2019). The paper enables future work on resolving whether color reconnection mechanisms in Pythia or hydrodynamic core expansion in EPOS better describe the observed mass-dependent rise of mean p_T with multiplicity, and whether quark-gluon plasma forms in small systems. The authors identify the need for incorporating first-principles frameworks like the Color Glass Condensate into event generators as a key opportunity for the coming years.
