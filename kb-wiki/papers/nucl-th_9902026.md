# Effect of q-deformation in the NJL gap equation

> We obtain a q-deformed algebra version of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model gap equation. In this framework we discuss some hadronic properties such as the dynamical mass generated for the quarks, the pion decay constant and the phase transition present in this model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that deforming the underlying $su(2)$ algebra in a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model enhances the quark condensate and dynamical mass, but does not change the sharpness of the chiral phase transition at the critical coupling, contrary to what might be expected from an explicit symmetry breaking term.

## Method

The authors construct a $q$-deformed version of the NJL model by replacing the usual fermionic creation and annihilation operators with $su_q(2)$-covariant ones, where only negative-helicity quark operators are modified. They derive a deformed gap equation using a BCS-like vacuum and a Bogoliubov-Valatin approach, then compute the deformed quark condensate and dynamical mass self-consistently with a non-covariant three-momentum cutoff regularization. The pion decay constant is calculated by substituting the deformed dynamical mass into the standard expression.

## Key numbers

* Dynamical mass for $q=1.0$: 365 MeV; for $q=1.1$: 375 MeV; for $q=1.2$: 384 MeV; for $q=1.3$: 392 MeV (cutoff $\Lambda = 600$ MeV, critical coupling $G_c = 4.57$ GeV$^{-2}$, coupling $G = 6.53$ GeV$^{-2}$, two flavors, three colors).
* Pion decay constant $f_\pi$ for $q=1.0$: 92.0 MeV; for $q=1.1$: 92.4 MeV; for $q=1.2$: 92.9 MeV; for $q=1.3$: 93.3 MeV (same parameters as above).
* Coupling constant $G$ required to produce a dynamical mass of 300 MeV: 6.04 GeV$^{-2}$ for $q=1.0$, decreasing to 5.90 GeV$^{-2}$ for $q=1.3$ (same cutoff and critical coupling).
* Coupling constant $G$ required to produce a dynamical mass of 350 MeV: 6.42 GeV$^{-2}$ for $q=1.0$, decreasing to 6.23 GeV$^{-2}$ for $q=1.3$ (same cutoff and critical coupling).

## Context

This work extends the application of $q$-deformed algebras to hadronic physics, following earlier studies of $q$-deformed phase transitions in the Lipkin model by Galetti and Pimentel (1995) and the $q$-covariant BCS formalism by Tripodi and Lima (1997). The authors use the $su_q(2)$-covariant fermionic representation developed by Ubriaco (1996) to embed chiral symmetry breaking effects directly into the operator algebra. The results enable future investigations of how quantum group deformations modify effective QCD models, particularly the relationship between deformation and the strength of the four-fermion interaction.
