# Ab initio charge form factors and radii of light isoscalar nuclei: Role of the two-body charge density

> We make \textit{ab initio} predictions of charge form factors (FFs) and radii for the isoscalar nuclei $^6$Li and $^8$Be using the Jacobi-coordinate No-Core Shell Model. The calculations employ chiral semilocal momentum-space regularized two- and three-nucleon interactions, together with consistently regularized one- and two-nucleon electromagnetic charge operators. With the short-range charge density fixed to the $^4$He charge radius, the predicted FFs and the $^6$Li radius show good agreement with available experimental data. We find that two-nucleon charge density contributions are essential for describing the FFs, particularly at intermediate and large momentum transfers. Although their influence on the charge radii is limited, these contributions remain crucial for attaining accurate predictions. The present results highlight the importance of two-nucleon charge operators in addressing the long-standing underestimation of nuclear charge radii in \textit{ab initio} calculations based on modern chiral interactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two-nucleon charge density contributions are essential for accurately predicting charge form factors and radii of light isoscalar nuclei, but the short-range part of the two-body charge density must be fixed by fitting one low-energy constant to the experimental charge radius of \(^4\)He, meaning the predictions are not fully parameter-free.

## Method

The authors use the Jacobi-coordinate No-Core Shell Model (J-NCSM) to compute wave functions for \(^6\)Li and \(^8\)Be, employing SRG-evolved chiral semilocal momentum-space regularized (SMS) nucleon-nucleon potentials at N\(^4\)LO\(^+\) together with three-nucleon forces at N\(^2\)LO. They combine these with consistently regularized one- and two-nucleon electromagnetic charge operators, where the two-body charge density includes one-pion-exchange and short-range contact terms; the low-energy constant in the isospin-one-to-isospin-one channel is fixed to the \(^4\)He charge radius, while the other two LECs were previously determined from deuteron data.

## Key numbers

- \(^4\)He charge radius: experimental value 1.67824(83) fm (Krauth 2021); the calculation with bare SMS interactions and FY method reproduces this value by construction.
- \(^6\)Li charge radius: calculated values \(\tilde{R}_c = 2.5851\) fm (SMS 450 MeV, \(\lambda=1.88\) fm\(^{-1}\)) and 2.5653 fm (SMS 500 MeV, \(\lambda=1.88\) fm\(^{-1}\)), compared to experimental values 2.517(30) fm (Tanihata 2013) and 2.589(39) fm (Nortershauser 2011).
- Two-body charge density contribution to the charge radius: approximately 0.04 fm for \(^4\)He, 0.03 fm for \(^6\)Li, and 0.035 fm for \(^8\)Be.
- Binding energies: \(^4\)He 28.32 MeV (SMS 450 MeV, bare FY) vs. experimental 28.29 MeV; \(^6\)Li 31.29 MeV (SMS 450 MeV, \(\lambda=1.88\) fm\(^{-1}\)) vs. experimental 31.99 MeV; \(^8\)Be 55.21 MeV (SMS 450 MeV, \(\lambda=1.88\) fm\(^{-1}\)) vs. experimental 56.50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral EFT studies of charge form factors and radii from the deuteron (Filin 2019, Filin 2020) and light nuclei with \(A\le 4\) (Filin 2025) to the \(p\)-shell nuclei \(^6\)Li and \(^8\)Be, using the same consistently regularized SMS interactions and charge operators. The results contradict the expectation that two-body charge contributions scale with the number of nucleon pairs or alpha clusters; instead, the contribution appears driven by short-distance currents involving mainly four nucleons in the \(s\)-shell. The framework enables future extension to isovector two-nucleon charge operators and application to heavier nuclei via other ab initio methods, and suggests that two-nucleon charge operators may help resolve the long-standing underestimation of nuclear charge radii in ab initio calculations.
