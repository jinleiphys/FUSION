# What lattice QCD spectral functions can tell us about heavy quarkonium in the QGP

> The bound states of a heavy quark and antiquark ($c\bar{c}, b\bar{b}$) are ideal probes to explore the quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the RHIC and LHC. Not only have they become experimentally accessible with high precision but also efficient tools, so called effective field theories (EFT) have been developed to treat them theoretically. Here we present recent progress in understanding the in-medium behavior of heavy-quarkonium with the help of EFT's combined with non-perturbative and first principles simulations in lattice QCD. In particular we discuss computations of heavy quarkonium spectral functions with the help of Bayesian unfolding methods and the physics we can extract from them. Limitations and the underlying assumptions of the used approaches are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that combining effective field theories (EFTs) with non-perturbative lattice QCD simulations and Bayesian unfolding methods can yield quantitative in-medium spectral functions for heavy quarkonium, but the results are limited by the assumption of full kinetic equilibration of heavy quarks and by systematic uncertainties in the reconstruction procedure.

## Method

The author uses two complementary approaches to compute charmonium spectral functions. The first is an indirect method using pNRQCD: the complex-valued in-medium heavy-quark potential is extracted from lattice QCD simulations of the Wilson loop via Bayesian reconstruction, then the Schrödinger equation is solved to obtain the spectral function. The second is a direct method using lattice NRQCD, where Euclidean current-current correlators from dynamical lattice QCD simulations (with almost physical pion mass) are unfolded into spectral functions using a Bayesian reconstruction approach.

## Key numbers

- Melting temperature for J/ψ: T_melt = 213 ± 13 MeV, defined as the temperature where the in-medium width equals the in-medium binding energy
- ψ'/J/ψ ratio at T = T_C: N_ψ' / N_J/ψ = 0.052 ± 0.009, and after including branching ratios to dimuons: 0.0069(11)
- Lattice QCD simulations used a pion mass m_π = 161 MeV for the direct NRQCD approach and m_π = 300 MeV for the potential-based approach
- At T = 1.61 T_C, the correlator ratio D(T>0)/D(T≈0) shows a 5% effect for J/ψ and 12% effect for χ_c

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of heavy quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma beyond earlier model potentials (such as color singlet free- or internal energies) by using the proper complex-valued potential derived from pNRQCD matching, as established by Laine (2006) and Rothkopf (2011). The Bayesian reconstruction method used here is a more recent alternative to the Maximum Entropy Method, developed by Burnier (2013). The results enable phenomenological predictions such as the ψ'/J/ψ ratio that can be compared to experimental measurements from ALICE and CMS at the LHC, and they set the stage for future quantitative determinations of in-medium masses and widths from direct lattice NRQCD simulations.
