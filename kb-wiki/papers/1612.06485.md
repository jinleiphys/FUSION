# Effects of volume corrections and resonance decays on cumulants of net-charge distributions in a Monte Carlo hadron resonance gas model

> The effects of volume corrections and resonance decays (the resulting correlations between positive charges and negative charges) on cumulants of net-proton distributions and net-charge distributions are investigated by using a Monte Carlo hadron resonance gas ({\tt MCHRG}) model. The required volume distributions are generated by a Monte Carlo Glauber ({\tt MC-Glb}) model. Except the variances of net-charge distributions, the {\tt MCHRG} model with more realistic simulations of volume corrections, resonance decays and acceptance cuts can reasonably explain the data of cumulants of net-proton distributions and net-charge distributions reported by the STAR collaboration. The {\tt MCHRG} calculations indicate that both the volume corrections and resonance decays make the cumulant products of net-charge distributions deviate from the Skellam expectations: the deviations of $Sσ$ and $κσ^{2}$ are dominated by the former effect while the deviations of $ω$ are dominated by the latter one.

# Effects of volume corrections and resonance decays on cumulants of net-charge distributions in a Monte Carlo hadron resonance gas model

## Key claim

The Monte Carlo hadron resonance gas (MCHRG) model, which includes volume corrections from a Monte Carlo Glauber model and resonance decays that generate correlations between positive and negative charges, can reasonably reproduce the STAR collaboration data for cumulants of net-proton and net-charge distributions except for the variances of net-charge distributions; the deviations of the cumulant products Sσ and κσ² from Skellam expectations are dominated by volume corrections, while deviations of ω are dominated by resonance decays.

## Method

The author constructed a Monte Carlo hadron resonance gas (MCHRG) model where primordial particle multiplicities in each event are randomly generated from Poisson distributions with parameters calculated from chemical freeze-out temperature and chemical potentials, using 319 primordial particle species and 26 stable particle species after Monte Carlo resonance decays. Volume distributions for each event were generated by a Monte Carlo Glauber (MC-Glb) model with parameters h = 15.4 fm³ and x = 0.1, and acceptance cuts matching the STAR experiment were applied to decay products using blast-wave model transverse momentum spectra and rapidity distributions. The first four cumulants of net-proton and net-charge distributions were calculated for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV and compared to STAR data and to Skellam and independent product baselines.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature: Tch = 151 MeV; baryon chemical potential: μB = 98.5 MeV, determined from p/π and p/π̄ multiplicity ratios at √sNN = 39 GeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: Tkin = 120 MeV; surface velocity parameter: βS = 0.6
- MC-Glb volume parameters: h = 15.4 fm³, x = 0.1, determined from centrality dependence of mean positive and negative charge multiplicities
- Centrality-dependent charge chemical potential parameterization: μQ0 = -4.7 MeV, aQ = 1.5 MeV
- Fixed volume for comparison case: V = 1540 fm³
- Acceptance cuts for net-charge fluctuation measures: |η| < 0.5, 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV (with protons and antiprotons removed for pT < 0.4 GeV); reference particles: 1.0 > |η| > 0.5, 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV
- Acceptance cuts for net-proton fluctuation measures: |y| < 0.5, 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV; reference particles: |η| < 1.0, 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous hadron resonance gas model studies of multiplicity distributions by Nahrgang (2014), which split resonance decay contributions into average and probabilistic parts but ignored correlations between positive and negative charges generated from resonance decays. The author's earlier work (Xu 2016) demonstrated that volume corrections play a significant role on cumulants of net-charge distributions. The MCHRG model provides more realistic baselines for cumulant measurements from the STAR Beam Energy Scan program than previous HRG models, and the results indicate that additional effects beyond the non-interacting MCHRG model (such as charge conservation correlations and hadronic phase dynamics) are needed to quantitatively reproduce the variances of net-charge distributions without significantly affecting skewness and kurtosis.
