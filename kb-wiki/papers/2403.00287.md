# Neural Simulation-Based Inference of the Neutron Star Equation of State directly from Telescope Spectra

> Neutron stars provide a unique opportunity to study strongly interacting matter under extreme density conditions. The intricacies of matter inside neutron stars and their equation of state are not directly visible, but determine bulk properties, such as mass and radius, which affect the star's thermal X-ray emissions. However, the telescope spectra of these emissions are also affected by the stellar distance, hydrogen column, and effective surface temperature, which are not always well-constrained. Uncertainties on these nuisance parameters must be accounted for when making a robust estimation of the equation of state. In this study, we develop a novel methodology that, for the first time, can infer the full posterior distribution of both the equation of state and nuisance parameters directly from telescope observations. This method relies on the use of neural likelihood estimation, in which normalizing flows use samples of simulated telescope data to learn the likelihood of the neutron star spectra as a function of these parameters, coupled with Hamiltonian Monte Carlo methods to efficiently sample from the corresponding posterior distribution. Our approach surpasses the accuracy of previous methods, improves the interpretability of the results by providing access to the full posterior distribution, and naturally scales to a growing number of neutron star observations expected in the coming years.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neural likelihood estimation (NLE) can, for the first time, infer the full posterior distribution of both the neutron star equation of state (EoS) parameters and nuisance parameters directly from telescope spectra, but this claim is conditioned on the method being applied to simulated spectra generated from a specific relativistic mean field model (GM1L) with a restricted two-parameter spectral expansion, and the performance is demonstrated only for a limited range of EoS parameter space.

## Method

The authors trained a Masked Autoregressive Flow (MAF) normalizing flow on simulated Chandra X-ray telescope spectra generated using the NSATMOS model in XSPEC, conditioned on EoS parameters (λ₁, λ₂ from a spectral expansion of the GM1L model) and nuisance parameters (distance d, hydrogen column N_H, effective temperature T_eff). The trained neural density estimator approximates the likelihood p(s|λ,ν) of a spectrum given the parameters, which is then combined with prior distributions and sampled using Hamiltonian Monte Carlo to obtain the full posterior distribution of both EoS and nuisance parameters simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- EoS parameters were sampled uniformly from λ₁ ∈ [4.75, 5.25] and λ₂ ∈ [-2.05, -1.85] for training
- Simulated spectra used Chandra ACIS-S response with 100 ks observation time, using only the first 250 energy bins
- Nuisance parameter ranges: d ∈ [2.3, 12.3] kpc, N_H ∈ [0.01, 3.16] × 10²¹ cm⁻², T_eff ∈ [1, 2] × 10⁶ K
- For one example EoS with 10 stars, increasing from 5 to 10 observations reduced the standard deviation of λ₁ by 23% (from 0.061 to 0.047), while increasing from 10 to 20 observations reduced it by only 6.4% (to 0.044)
- Performance was evaluated on a test set of 100 different equations of state with 10 simulated spectra each

## Context

This work extends the single-step inference approach of Farrell (2023a, 2023b) by replacing the two-step method (first inferring mass-radius posteriors, then EoS parameters) used by Riley (2018), Miller (2019a), and many subsequent Bayesian analyses. The NLE method avoids the computational expense of the surrogate likelihood approach in Farrell (2023b) and provides full posterior access including nuisance parameters, enabling straightforward scaling to the growing number of neutron star observations expected from future telescopes.
