# Chiral perturbation theory of muonic hydrogen Lamb shift: polarizability contribution

> The proton polarizability effect in the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift comes out as a prediction of baryon chiral perturbation theory at leading order and our calculation yields for it: $ΔE^{(\mathrm{pol})} (2P-2S) = 8^{+3}_{-1}\, μ$eV. This result is consistent with most of evaluations based on dispersive sum rules, but is about a factor of two smaller than the recent result obtained in {\em heavy-baryon} chiral perturbation theory. We also find that the effect of $Δ(1232)$-resonance excitation on the Lamb-shift is suppressed, as is the entire contribution of the magnetic polarizability; the electric polarizability dominates. Our results reaffirm the point of view that the proton structure effects, beyond the charge radius, are too small to resolve the `proton radius puzzle'.

## Key claim

The proton polarizability contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift, calculated at leading order in baryon chiral perturbation theory (B\(\chi\)PT), is \(8^{+3}_{-1}\) \(\mu\)eV for the \(2P-2S\) transition, which is about a factor of two smaller than the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) result but consistent with most dispersive sum-rule evaluations; the authors argue this effect is too small to resolve the proton radius puzzle.

## Method

The authors computed the forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) amplitude on the proton at leading order (\(O(p^3)\)) in a manifestly Lorentz-invariant variant of baryon chiral perturbation theory (B\(\chi\)PT), using the chiral Lagrangian with pion and nucleon fields and minimally coupled photons. They separated the VVCS amplitude into Born and non-Born parts, verified the dispersive sum rules, and substituted the non-Born amplitudes into the standard formula for the two-photon-exchange energy shift of the \(2S\) level in muonic hydrogen, working in the zero-energy forward kinematics and using the low-energy expansion (LEX) approximation. The \(\Delta(1232)\)-resonance excitation was omitted from the calculation, with the argument that its dominant magnetic-dipole contribution cancels out in the total polarizability effect.

## Key numbers

*   Proton polarizability contribution to the muonic-hydrogen \(2P-2S\) Lamb shift: \(\Delta E^{(\mathrm{pol})}(2P-2S) = 8^{+3}_{-1}\) \(\mu\)eV (from abstract; the text gives \(-8.2^{+1.2}_{-2.5}\) \(\mu\)eV for the \(2S\) level shift, which corresponds to \(8^{+3}_{-1}\) \(\mu\)eV for the \(2P-2S\) splitting).
*   LO-HB\(\chi\)PT result for the polarizability contribution to the \(2S\) level: \(-18.45\) \(\mu\)eV (Nevado and Pineda, 2007).
*   LO-B\(\chi\)PT result for the \(2S\) level shift using the exact formula: \(-8.16\) \(\mu\)eV.
*   LO-B\(\chi\)PT result for the \(2S\) level shift using the low-energy expansion (LEX): \(-8.20\) \(\mu\)eV.
*   LO-B\(\chi\)PT subtraction contribution to the \(2S\) level: \(-3.0\) \(\mu\)eV.
*   LO-B\(\chi\)PT inelastic contribution to the \(2S\) level: \(-5.2\) \(\mu\)eV.

## Context

This work extends the application of baryon chiral perturbation theory to the proton structure corrections in muonic hydrogen, following earlier HB\(\chi\)PT calculations by Nevado and Pineda (2007) and Birse and McGovern (2012). The B\(\chi\)PT result for the polarizability effect agrees with most dispersive sum-rule evaluations (e.g., Carlson and Vanderhaeghen, 2011; Birse and McGovern, 2012) but is significantly smaller than the LO-HB\(\chi\)PT result, highlighting the qualitative differences between the two chiral perturbation theory variants. The calculation enables a more reliable estimate of the proton polarizability contribution to the Lamb shift, reaffirming that proton structure effects beyond the charge radius are too small to resolve the proton radius puzzle, and it provides a foundation for next-order calculations including \(\pi\Delta\) loops.
