# Complete 1-loop study of exclusive $ J/ψ$ and $ Υ$ photoproduction with full GPD evolution

> We discuss the exclusive photoproduction of a heavy vector quarkonium, namely $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ at 1-loop in $α_s$. In collinear factorisation (CF), the amplitude for such a process is obtained by the convolution of a hard partonic sub-amplitude, with a universal generalised parton distribution (GPD). For the first time, we perform a complete calculation at 1-loop including full leading-log (LL) GPD evolution. We first demonstrate the huge instability of the cross section at high energies when the factorisation scale $μ_F$ is varied. This instability has been reported previously in the literature, and occurs due to the large logarithms generated by the huge difference between the hard scale of the process, which is the mass of the heavy quarkonium here, and the centre-of-mass energy of the process. This problem was also reported in inclusive heavy vector quarkonium photoproduction. We show that this issue can be resolved by resumming these large logarithms using high-energy factorisation (HEF), by performing a matching with the result in CF using the doubly logarithmic approximation (DLA) in order to be consistent with the fixed order evolution of GPDs. Finally, we show that the cross sections obtained from such a matching, besides being free from the previously mentioned factorisation scale variation instabilities, are consistent with the H1 data for $J/ψ$ production and with the ZEUS data for $Υ$ production.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the large factorisation-scale instability previously seen in NLO collinear-factorisation calculations of exclusive heavy quarkonium photoproduction can be resolved by matching the NLO collinear-factorisation result with a high-energy factorisation resummation truncated to the doubly logarithmic approximation, yielding cross sections consistent with HERA data.

## Method
The authors compute the exclusive photoproduction amplitude for J/ψ and Υ at 1-loop in αs within collinear factorisation, using the heavy quark mass as the hard scale and the static limit for the meson distribution amplitude. They include full leading-log GPD evolution starting from an initial scale μ0 = 2 GeV, using the Goloskokov-Kroll model for GPDs based on the double distribution model. To cure the large factorisation-scale instability at high energies, they perform a subtractive matching between the NLO collinear-factorisation coefficient function and the coefficient function computed in the doubly logarithmic approximation of high-energy factorisation, which resums logarithms of 1/ξ (where ξ is the skewness parameter) that arise from the large difference between the quarkonium mass and the centre-of-mass energy.

## Key numbers
- For J/ψ photoproduction, the matched NLO CF ⊕ DLA HEF result eliminates the huge factorisation-scale uncertainty that plagues the pure NLO CF result at high Wγp; the matched result remains stable and has smaller uncertainties than the LO CF result when μF is varied.
- For Υ photoproduction, the matched result has much smaller scale uncertainties compared to J/ψ and is completely compatible with the ZEUS data.
- The matched cross sections for J/ψ are consistent with the precise H1 data from HERA, although the central values overshoot the experimental values.
- The 9-point scale variation is performed for GPDs constructed from the CT18NLO PDF set, with central values also shown for 5 different PDF sets to assess PDF uncertainty.

## Context
This work extends the 1-loop computation of exclusive heavy vector quarkonium photoproduction by Ivanov et al. (2004), which showed that collinear factorisation holds at NLO in αs, and the extension to electroproduction by Flett et al. (2021). The large logarithms of ln(1/ξ) that cause the scale instability were previously reported in inclusive heavy vector quarkonium photoproduction, and a similar high-energy factorisation resummation had been adopted for inclusive quarkonium hadro- and photoproduction by Lansberg et al. (2021, 2023). The matching procedure enables stable predictions for exclusive photoproduction at high energies, allowing direct comparison with HERA data from H1 (2013) and ZEUS (2009), and opens the door to more reliable extraction of GPD information from these processes.
