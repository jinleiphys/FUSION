# Structure of Superheavy Nuclei Along Element 115 Decay Chains

> A recent high-resolution $α$, $X$-ray, and $γ$-ray coincidence-spectroscopy experiment offered first glimpse of excitation schemes of isotopes along $α$-decay chains of $Z=115$. To understand these observations and to make predictions about shell structure of superheavy nuclei below $^{288}115$, we employ two complementary mean-field models: self-consistent Skyrme Energy Density Functional approach and the macroscopic-microscopic Nilsson model. We discuss the spectroscopic information carried by the new data. In particular, candidates for the experimentally observed $E1$ transitions in $^{276}$Mt are proposed. We find that the presence and nature of low-energy $E1$ transitions in well-deformed nuclei around $Z=110, N=168$ strongly depends on the strength of the spin-orbit coupling; hence, it provides an excellent constraint on theoretical models of superheavy nuclei. To clarify competing theoretical scenarios, an experimental search for $E1$ transitions in odd-$A$ systems $^{275,277}$Mt, $^{275}$Hs, and $^{277}$Ds is strongly recommended.

## Key claim

The presence and nature of low-energy E1 transitions in well-deformed superheavy nuclei around Z=110, N=168 strongly depends on the strength of the spin-orbit coupling, providing an excellent constraint on theoretical models; however, the locally-optimized UNEDF1SO parametrization that performs well in the transfermium region does not offer a simple explanation of the observed E1 data, whereas the global UNEDF1 parametrization and the macroscopic-microscopic Nilsson-Strutinsky model each offer competing scenarios.

## Method

The authors employed two complementary mean-field models: the self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (SHFB) approach using the UNEDF1SO and UNEDF1 energy density functionals, and the macroscopic-microscopic Nilsson-Strutinsky (NS) model with a modified harmonic oscillator potential. One-quasiparticle excitations in odd-A nuclei were computed by blocking relevant orbits around the Fermi level, and two-quasiparticle-plus-rotor calculations for odd-odd nuclei were carried out using the NS model. The Skyrme calculations used 680 deformed harmonic-oscillator basis states and the symmetry-unrestricted solver HFODD (v2.52j).

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformations beta_2 for nuclei along the alpha-decay chain range from approximately 0.23 for 275Ds down to 0.05 for 293117, with a smooth increase of quadrupole deformation predicted along the chain by both SEDF models.
- The theoretical error on one-quasiparticle excitations due to the adopted size of the harmonic-oscillator basis is less than 60 keV when going from 680 stretched HO states to 969 states.
- In 276Mt, the splitting between the I=1 and I=2 members of the lowest K^pi=1^- (pi[505]9/2 x nu[606]11/2) band is 43 keV, which is particularly close to the experimentally suggested energy difference of approximately 47 keV.
- The agreement with measured Q_alpha values for the heaviest elements is reasonable, usually better than 1 MeV.
- In the UNEDF1SO model, the unique-parity high-Omega intruder level nu[716]13/2 in 275Ds appears at 0.151 MeV excitation energy, while the pi[615]11/2 intruder in 279Rg appears at 0.284 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous macroscopic-microscopic studies (Cwiok 1994, Parkhomenko 2004, 2005) and self-consistent studies (Cwiok 1999, Bender 2000) of superheavy nuclei, which showed that the number of opposite-parity single-particle orbitals around the Fermi level is fairly limited. The paper builds on a recent high-resolution alpha, X-ray, and gamma-ray coincidence-spectroscopy experiment (Rudolph 2013) that provided the first glimpse of excitation schemes in isotopes along the alpha-decay chains of Z=115. The results enable future experimental searches for E1 transitions in odd-A systems 275,277Mt, 275Hs, and 277Ds to clarify competing theoretical scenarios, which would require high-resolution alpha-photon coincidence spectroscopy of decay chains starting from 293117.
