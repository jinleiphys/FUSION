# Collective Coordinates and the Absence of Yukawa Coupling in the Classical Skyrme Model

> In systems with constraints, physical states must be annihilated by the constraints. We make use of this rule to construct physical asymptotic states in the Skyrme model. The standard derivation of the Born terms with asymptotic physical states shows that there is no Yukawa coupling for the Skyrmion. We propose a remedy tested in other solitonic models: A Wilsonian action obtained after integrating the energetic mesons and where the Skyrmion is a quantum state should have a Yukawa coupling.

## Key claim

The standard derivation of Born terms in the Skyrme model yields no Yukawa coupling for the Skyrmion when asymptotic states are constructed to satisfy the six constraints (three translational, three isospin) that physical states must obey; the author proposes that a Wilsonian action obtained after integrating out energetic mesons, where the Skyrmion is treated as a quantum state, should restore the Yukawa coupling.

## Method

The author constructs physical asymptotic states in the Skyrme model by requiring that they be annihilated by the six constraint operators arising from broken translational and isospin invariance of the classical Skyrme solution. The Born terms are then computed using the reduction formula with these physical states, employing Dirac brackets instead of Poisson brackets to obtain the correct equations of motion for fluctuations. The resulting expression for the Born amplitude is shown to vanish at leading order because the physical states satisfy orthogonality conditions with the constraint functions, unlike the plane-wave states used in prior work by U (1995), M (1995), and S (1995).

## Key numbers

- Six constraints exist in the Skyrme model: three from broken translational invariance and three from broken isospin invariance.
- The leading-order Born term expression (equation B4) contains factors \(\int d^3x \nabla \phi_S^a f_r(x)\) and \(\int d^3x \phi_S^a f_r(x)\), which vanish for physical asymptotic states satisfying the constraints.
- The Born term expression in the plane-wave limit (equation 3.25 of U (1995)) is recovered when asymptotic states are plane waves, but this limit is invalid because plane-wave states do not satisfy the constraints.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the claims of U (1995), M (1995), and S (1995), who reported finding a Yukawa coupling in the Skyrme model but failed to enforce all constraints or use physical asymptotic states. The analysis extends the Dirac bracket formalism of T (1995) and the constraint quantization approach of bk (1995) to the Skyrme model. The author suggests that following the approach of YO2 (1995) for other solitonic models, a Wilsonian action with a Yukawa coupling could be obtained for a quantum Skyrmion after integrating out high-energy mesons, which would resolve the absence found in the classical treatment.
