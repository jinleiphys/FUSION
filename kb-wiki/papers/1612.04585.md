# Forward $J/ψ$ and $D$ meson nuclear suppression at the LHC

> Using the color glass condensate formalism, we study the nuclear modification of forward $J/ψ$ and $D$ meson production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC. We show that relying on the optical Glauber model to obtain the dipole cross section of the nucleus from the one of the proton fitted to HERA DIS data leads to a smaller nuclear suppression than in the first study of these processes in this formalism and a better agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

Using the optical Glauber model to obtain the nuclear dipole cross section from the proton one (fitted to HERA DIS data) yields a smaller nuclear suppression for forward \(J/\psi\) and \(D\) meson production in proton-lead collisions than earlier CGC calculations, leading to better agreement with LHC data.

## Method

The authors compute \(c\bar{c}\) pair production in the dilute-dense limit of the color glass condensate (CGC) formalism, using the collinear approximation for the projectile proton (MSTW 2008 LO PDFs) and eikonal scattering off the target. The dipole correlator for the proton is obtained by solving the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with an initial condition fitted to HERA DIS data; for the lead nucleus, the initial condition is obtained via the optical Glauber model using the nuclear thickness function, with no new free parameters. The \(J/\psi\) cross section is obtained via the color evaporation model, and \(D^0\) mesons via fragmentation using a Kartvelishvili parametrization.

## Key numbers

- The proton dipole initial condition fit to HERA DIS data yields \(Q_{s0}^2 = 0.060\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(C^2 = 7.2\), \(e_c = 18.9\), and \(\sigma_0/2 = 16.36\ \text{mb}\).
- The charm quark mass is varied between \(1.2\) and \(1.5\ \text{GeV}\); the \(D\) meson mass is taken as \(m_D = 1.864\ \text{GeV}\).
- The nuclear thickness function uses parameters \(d = 0.54\ \text{fm}\) and \(R_A = (1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3})\ \text{fm}\).
- Results are shown for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\ \text{TeV}\) at the LHC; the nuclear modification factor \(R_\text{pA}\) is computed as a function of rapidity \(Y\) and transverse momentum \(P_\perp\).

## Context

This work extends earlier CGC studies of forward \(J/\psi\) and \(D\) meson production by Fujii et al. (2013), which used a simple \(A^{1/3}\) scaling of the initial saturation scale for the nucleus and predicted stronger suppression than observed by ALICE and LHCb. The authors follow the approach of Lappi and Mäntysaari (2013), who showed that using the optical Glauber model to relate nuclear and proton dipole cross sections resolves a similar discrepancy for single inclusive hadron production. The improved treatment enables more reliable predictions for heavy-flavor suppression at the LHC and can be applied to other processes in the CGC framework.
