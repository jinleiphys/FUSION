# Heavy flavor dynamics across system size at the LHC

> One of the fundamental signatures of the Quark Gluon Plasma has been the suppression of heavy flavor (specifically D mesons), which has been measured via the nuclear modification factor, $R_{AA}$ and azimuthal anisotropies, $v_n$, in large systems. However, multiple competing models can reproduce the same data for $R_{AA}$ to $v_n$. In this talk we break down the competing effects that conspire together to successfully reproduce $R_{AA}$ and $v_n$ in experimental data using Trento+v-USPhydro+DAB-MOD. Then using our best fit model we make predictions for $R_{AA}$ and $v_n$ across system size for $^{208}PbPb$, $^{129}XeXe$, $^{40}ArAr$, and $^{16}OO$ collisions. We find that 0--10\% centrality has a non-trivial interplay between the system size and eccentricities such that system size effects are masked in $v_2$ whereas in 30--50\% centrality the eccentricities are approximately constant across system size and, therefore, is a better centrality class to study D meson dynamics across system size.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in 0–10% centrality the system size and eccentricities have a non-trivial interplay that masks system size effects in D meson \(v_2\), whereas in 30–50% centrality the eccentricities are approximately constant across system size, making that centrality class a better place to isolate system size effects on heavy flavor dynamics.

## Method

The authors used the Trento initial conditions (with parameters \(p=0\), \(k=1.6\), \(\sigma=0.51\) fm) coupled to event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics (v-USPhydro) with \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm, \(\eta/s=0.047\), and freeze-out temperature \(T_\text{FO}=150\) MeV, matched to a lattice QCD equation of state. Heavy flavor quarks were sampled from FONLL pQCD calculations and evolved using either a Langevin description (with spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s(2\pi T)=2.23\)) or an energy loss model, then hadronized via a hybrid fragmentation/coalescence scheme in the DAB-MOD package. The model was tuned to PbPb 5.02 TeV data and then used to predict \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) for \({}^{208}\)PbPb, \({}^{129}\)XeXe, \({}^{40}\)ArAr, and \({}^{16}\)OO collisions.

## Key numbers

- The spatial diffusion coefficient used in the Langevin model is \(D_s(2\pi T)=2.23\) (dimensionless, from Moore (2004)).
- The initial condition parameters are \(p=0\), \(k=1.6\), and \(\sigma=0.51\) fm (from a Bayesian analysis by Bernhard (2016)).
- The hydrodynamical parameters are \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm, \(\eta/s=0.047\), and \(T_\text{FO}=150\) MeV.
- In 30–50% centrality, the minimum \(R_{AA}\) for OO collisions is approximately 0.8 at \(p_T\sim 10\) GeV.
- In 0–10% centrality, the minimum \(R_{AA}\) for OO collisions is approximately 0.5 at \(p_T\sim 10\) GeV.
- The Langevin model and energy loss model produce nearly identical \(R_{AA}\) at high \(p_T\), but between \(p_T\sim 5\)–10 GeV the energy loss model predicts significantly more suppression.
- For \(v_2\), the effect of coalescence shifts the peak to higher \(p_T\) up to roughly \(p_T\sim 10\) GeV in the energy loss results.

## Context

This work extends the heavy flavor modeling of Katz (2019) and Prado (2016) by applying the same best-fit framework (Trento+v-USPhydro+DAB-MOD) to predict D meson observables across system sizes from PbPb down to OO collisions, following the proposal for a system size scan at the LHC by Citron (2018). It contrasts with earlier findings that multiple models can reproduce PbPb \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) data; the authors note that Xu (2018) showed those similarities disappear when models share the same hydrodynamic background. The results enable future comparisons to upcoming small-system data (pPb and pp) and to RHIC measurements, and suggest that 30–50% centrality is the optimal class for isolating system size effects in heavy flavor flow.
