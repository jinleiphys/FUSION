# Rapidity dependence of particle production in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> We compute the rapidity dependence of particle and transverse energy production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at various beam energies and atomic numbers using the perturbative QCD + saturation model. The distribution is a broad gaussian near $y=0$ but the rapid increase of particle production with the beam energy will via energy conservation strongly constrain the rapidity distribution at large $y$.

## Key claim

The perturbative QCD + saturation model predicts a broad Gaussian rapidity distribution near y=0, but energy conservation strongly constrains the model's validity to a limited rapidity range (|y| less than about 1-2 at RHIC and 3-4 at LHC), beyond which independent subcollision models cannot be valid and the physical mechanism must change completely.

## Method

The authors compute the rapidity dependence of particle and transverse energy production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions using the leading-order perturbative QCD 2-to-2 partonic cross section with GRV94 parton distribution functions, nuclear shadowing from the EKS98 parametrization, and a K-factor of approximately 2 to simulate NLO contributions. The saturation scale p_sat is determined by a geometric saturation condition on the transverse plane of the two final-state particles in the 2-to-2 collision, and the rapidity distribution is obtained by integrating the perturbative cross section from this saturation scale. The results are compared with the 2-to-1 saturation model of Kharzeev and Levin (2001) and with RHIC pseudorapidity data from PHOBOS.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale at y=0 scales as p_sat(0) approximately 0.208 GeV times A^0.128 times (sqrt(s))^0.191, with numerical values computed including shadowing.
* The particle multiplicity at y=0 scales as dN/dy approximately 1.383 times A^0.922 times (sqrt(s))^0.383, with numerical values computed including shadowing.
* The Gaussian width sigma of the rapidity distribution is 5.8 at sqrt(s)=130 GeV, 5.9 at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, and 6.8 at sqrt(s)=5500 GeV for central collisions.
* At RHIC with sqrt(s)=130 GeV (beam rapidity y_B=4.93) and sqrt(s)=200 GeV (y_B=5.36), the model is valid up to |y| less than about 2 when allowing 40% of total energy.
* At LHC with sqrt(s)=5500 GeV (y_B=8.67), the model is valid up to |y| less than about 3.5 when allowing 40% of total energy.
* The saturation condition broadens the rapidity distribution relative to elementary subprocesses by a factor of sqrt(2+xi) approximately 1.6.

## Context

This work extends the saturation+pQCD model of Eskola, Kajantie, Ruuskanen, and Tuominen (2000) to include rapidity dependence, and contrasts with the 2-to-1 saturation model of Kharzeev and Levin (2001) and Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (2001), which predicts a sharply peaked rapidity distribution with a discontinuous derivative at y=0. The results enable predictions for LHC energies and establish the rapidity range where independent subcollision models are valid, beyond which correlations between subcollisions must be considered. The paper also connects to the analysis of saturation in deep inelastic scattering by Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998) and to hydrodynamic evolution studies by Eskola, Kajantie, and Ruuskanen (1998).
