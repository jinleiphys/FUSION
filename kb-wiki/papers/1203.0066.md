# Dynamical nucleus-nucleus potential and incompressibility of nuclear matter

> The dynamical nucleus-nucleus potentials for some fusion reactions are investigated by using the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model with different sets of parameters in which the corresponding incompressibility coefficient of nuclear matter is different. Two new sets of parameters SKP* and IQ3 for the ImQMD model are proposed with the incompressibility coefficient of 195 and 225 MeV, respectively. The measured fusion excitation function for 16O+208Pb and the charge distribution of fragments for Ca+Ca and Au+Au in multi-fragmentation process can be reasonably well reproduced. Simultaneously, the influence of the nuclear matter incompressibility and the range of nucleon-nucleon interaction on the nucleus-nucleus dynamic potential is investigated.

## Key claim
The dynamical nucleus-nucleus potential at short distances is influenced by both the nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient and the range of the nucleon-nucleon interaction (represented by the Gaussian wave-packet width), but the incompressibility mainly affects the potential at short distances only when the wave-packet width is held fixed.

## Method
The authors used the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model, a semi-classical microscopic dynamics model, to simulate head-on collisions of 16O+208Pb and 48Ca+208Pb at two incident energies each. They proposed two new parameter sets (SKP* and IQ3) with incompressibility coefficients of 195 MeV and 225 MeV, respectively, and compared results with the existing IQ2 set (also 195 MeV). The dynamical nucleus-nucleus potential was extracted from the time-dependent densities using the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation for kinetic energies.

## Key numbers
- Incompressibility coefficient K∞ = 195 MeV for parameter sets IQ2 and SKP*; K∞ = 225 MeV for parameter set IQ3.
- Saturation density ρ0 = 0.165 fm⁻³ for IQ2 and IQ3; ρ0 = 0.162 fm⁻³ for SKP*.
- Fusion reaction 16O+208Pb studied at incident energies Ec.m. = 80 MeV and Ec.m. = 120 MeV.
- Fusion reaction 48Ca+208Pb studied at incident energies Ec.m. = 179 MeV and Ec.m. = 205 MeV.
- Multi-fragmentation reactions 40Ca+40Ca and 197Au+197Au studied at incident energy 35 MeV/nucleon, with 500 events simulated for head-on collisions up to t = 6000 fm/c.
- For the 16O+208Pb reaction at Ec.m. = 80 MeV, the fusion cross section calculated with the barrier penetration approach using the IQ3 potential was very close to the result from the direct ImQMD simulation.

## Context
This work extends the ImQMD-based dynamical potential approach of Wang et al. (2010) by introducing two new parameter sets (SKP* and IQ3) to systematically vary the incompressibility coefficient and the nucleon-nucleon interaction range. It complements earlier double-folding studies by Esbensen and others that investigated the influence of incompressibility on fusion potentials using M3Y interactions. The results enable future studies to disentangle the effects of the nuclear equation of state from finite-range effects in heavy-ion fusion and multi-fragmentation reactions.
