# Universality for Three Bosons with Large, Negative Effective Range: Aspects and Addenda

> Resummed-Range Effective Field Theory is the consistent non-relativistic Effective Field Theory of point interactions in systems with large two-body scattering length $a$ and an effective range $r_0$ large in magnitude but negative. Its leading order is non-perturbative, and its observables depend only on the dimensionless ratio $ξ:=2r_0/a$ once $|r_0|$ is chosen as base unit. This presentation highlights aspects for three identical spinless bosons and adds details to a previous discussion [1]. At leading order, no three-body interaction is needed. A ground state exists only in the range $0.366\ldots\geξ\ge-8.72\ldots$, and excited states display self-similarity and Discrete Scale Invariance, with small corrections for nonzero $r_0$.

## Key claim

Resummed-Range Effective Field Theory predicts that for three identical spinless bosons with large, negative effective range, a ground state exists only within the range $0.366\ldots \ge \xi \ge -8.72\ldots$ (where $\xi = 2r_0/a$), and no three-body interaction is needed at leading order, contrary to the standard Short-Range EFT where such an interaction is required to stabilize the system.

## Method

The author solves the homogeneous Faddeev integral equation for three identical spinless bosons using Resummed-Range Effective Field Theory at leading order, where the two-body scattering length $a$ and effective range $r_0$ (negative and large in magnitude) are both treated non-perturbatively. The kernel is discretized on a logarithmic momentum mesh of 500 to 2000 points, and bound states are found by scanning for zeros of $\det[1-\mathcal{K}(-(\kappa_3^{(j)})^2;P,Q)]=0$ over a wide range of hard cutoffs $\Lambda \in [0.5; 3000] |r_0^{-1}|$. The trajectories of bound states are parametrized in polar coordinates using a seven-parameter fit inspired by Efimov's universal function.

## Key numbers

- Ground state exists only for $0.366\ldots \ge \xi \ge -8.72\ldots$, corresponding to $-0.204\ldots \le \kappa_2^- \le 2.118\ldots$ in units of $|r_0^{-1}|$
- Ground state binding momentum at threshold: $\kappa_{\mathrm{thr}}^{(0)} = 2.11862(2) |r_0^{-1}|$, about 30% smaller than the Efimov value of $3.31 |r_0^{-1}|$
- Ground state binding momentum at quasi-unitarity ($\xi=0$): $\kappa_{\mathrm{QU}}^{(0)} = 2.35412(3) \times 10^{-1} |r_0^{-1}|$
- Ground state zero-binding point: $\xi_{\mathrm{ZB}} = 3.6689(1) \times 10^{-1}$, $\kappa_2^-(\kappa_3=0) = -2.04318(6) \times 10^{-1} |r_0^{-1}|$
- First excited state at threshold: $\kappa_{\mathrm{thr}}^{(1)} = 1.25108(1) \times 10^{-1} |r_0^{-1}|$
- Second excited state at quasi-unitarity: $\kappa_{\mathrm{QU}}^{(2)} = 4.53987(1) \times 10^{-4} |r_0^{-1}|$
- Third excited state at zero binding: $\xi_{\mathrm{ZB}}^{(3)} = 2.6538(3) \times 10^{-5}$, $\kappa_2^- = -1.3269(2) \times 10^{-5} |r_0^{-1}|$
- Asymptotic Efimov scaling ratio for highly excited states: $e^{\pi/s_0} = 22.6944\ldots$ where $s_0 = 1.0062378\ldots$
- Predicted Efimov scale (in hard-cutoff regularization): $\Lambda_\ast^{(j\ge2)} = 0.610206(1) |r_0^{-1}|$

## Context

This work extends the Resummed-Range Effective Field Theory developed by Habashi, van Kolck, and collaborators (2020-2022) for two-body systems with large, negative effective range into the three-boson sector, adding detail to the author's comprehensive study (Griesshammer, 2023). It contrasts with standard Short-Range EFT (Efimov, 1970-1978; reviewed by Hammer, 2019) where a three-body interaction is required at leading order and the Efimov tower is infinite; here, the tower is truncated and its absolute position is predicted from two-body parameters alone. The results enable parameter-free predictions for systems with narrow Feshbach resonances, the $D_s^*(2317)$, and certain $\Xi N$ and $\Xi\Xi$ systems (van de Kraats, 2022; Matuschek, 2020; Haidenbauer, 2015, 2021), and provide a framework for future studies of three identical fermions, bosons with different masses, and higher-order effects.
