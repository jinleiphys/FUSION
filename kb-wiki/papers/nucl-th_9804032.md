# A Perturbative Calculation of the Electromagnetic Form Factors of the Deuteron

> Making use of the effective field theory expansion recently developed by the authors, we compute the electromagnetic form factors of the deuteron analytically to next-to-leading order (NLO). The computation is rather simple, and involves calculating several Feynman diagrams, using dimensional regularization. The results agree well with data and indicate that the expansion is converging. They do not suffer from any ambiguities arising from off-shell versus on-shell amplitudes.

## Key claim

The authors compute the deuteron electromagnetic form factors analytically to next-to-leading order (NLO) using their effective field theory expansion, finding that the results agree well with data and that the expansion is converging, but they note that at NLO the predictions are not as accurate as those of effective range theory, with NNLO expected to reach that precision.

## Method

The calculation uses the effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon interactions developed by the authors (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998), which employs a systematic low-momentum expansion with power counting in the PDS subtraction scheme. The authors compute the irreducible two-point function Σ and three-point function Γ^μ for the deuteron by evaluating Feynman diagrams with dimensional regularization, then extract the charge (F_C), magnetic (F_M), and quadrupole (F_Q) form factors from the matrix elements of the electromagnetic current. The calculation includes one-pion exchange, contact interactions with coefficients C_0, C_2, D_2, and L_2, and the nucleon magnetic moment operators, working to NLO (order Q^1 relative to leading order).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy enters via γ = √(MB), where M is the nucleon mass and B is the binding energy
- Leading-order charge form factor: F_C^(0)(q^2) = (4γ/q) tan^(-1)(q/(4γ))
- Leading-order magnetic moment: μ_M^(LO) = μ_p + μ_n = 2.79285 + (-1.91304) = 0.87981 nuclear magnetons (experimental value is 0.85741 e/2M)
- NLO charge radius: ⟨r²⟩^(NLO) = C_2(μ) M(μ-γ)²/(16πγ) + g_A² M m_π² (3m_π+10γ)/(96π f² γ (m_π+2γ)³)
- NLO quadrupole moment: μ_Q^(NLO) = g_A² M (6γ²+9γ m_π+4m_π²)/(30π f² (m_π+2γ)³)
- Experimental quadrupole moment: μ_Q = 0.2859 fm²
- Pion decay constant: f = 132 MeV
- Axial coupling: g_A = 1.25
- Scale Λ_NN = 8πf²/(g_A² M) ≈ 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach developed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for NN scattering phase shifts to dynamical electromagnetic processes involving the deuteron. It improves upon earlier effective field theory calculations of deuteron form factors by Bira (1997) and Park (1997) by providing fully analytic results rather than numerical ones, and by using a formalism that avoids ambiguities from off-shell versus on-shell amplitudes. The calculation enables systematic extension to NNLO and application to other processes such as np → dγ and γd → γd, and establishes a framework that can surpass effective range theory (Bethe, 1949; Bethe, 1950) in precision at higher orders.
