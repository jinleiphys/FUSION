# How magic is the magic 68Ni nucleus?

> We calculate the B(E2) strength in 68Ni and other nickel isotopes using several theoretical approaches. We find that in 68Ni the gamma transition to the first 2+ state exhausts only a fraction of the total B(E2) strength, which is mainly collected in excited states around 5 MeV. This effect is sensitive to the energy splitting between the fp shell and the g_{9/2}orbital. We argue that the small experimental B(E2) value is not strong evidence for the double-magic character of 68Ni.

## Key claim

The small experimental B(E2) value for the transition to the first 2+ state in 68Ni is not strong evidence for its double-magic character; instead, the smallness of this transition may indicate a rather small energy gap between the fp shell and the g9/2 orbital, because most of the total B(E2) strength resides in excited states around 5 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed three complementary theoretical calculations: Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) in the complete (fp)(gds) configuration space for even-even nickel isotopes from 56Ni to 78Ni, Quasi-particle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) using a Woods-Saxon potential with a quadrupole force and quadrupole pairing force, and large-scale diagonalization shell model (SM) calculations for 66,68Ni using the fpg valence space and effective interaction. The SMMC approach calculated the total summed B(E2) strength at low temperature, while QRPA and SM provided the detailed distribution of B(E2) strength across excited 2+ states.

## Key numbers

- The experimental B(E2, 0+_g.s. -> 2+_1) value for 68Ni is 280 +/- 60 e^2 fm^4 (Sorlin 2002), compared to 620 +/- 120 e^2 fm^4 for the well-established double-magic nucleus 56Ni (Kraus 1994).
- The SMMC total B(E2) strength for 68Ni is approximately 1250 e^2 fm^4, about five times greater than the measured transition to the first 2+ state.
- The centroid of the SMMC B(E2) strength in 68Ni lies at approximately 5 MeV, significantly higher than the energy of the first 2+ state.
- The SMMC calculation finds an average occupation number of 2.2 for neutrons in the g9/2 orbital in 68Ni, which reduces to 0.9 when all gds single-particle energies are shifted up by 1 MeV.
- The diagonalization shell model predicts a B(E2) value of about 280 e^2 fm^4 for the first 2+ state in 68Ni, with a total B(E2) strength of 900 e^2 fm^4, most of which resides at excitation energies around 5-6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the N=40 subshell closure in 68Ni, which was first suggested by Bernas (1982) and Lombard (1983) and tested experimentally by Grzywacz (1998), Mueller (2000), Ishii (2002), and Sorlin (2002). The paper contradicts the interpretation that the small B(E2) value alone indicates double-magicity, and instead argues that the total B(E2) strength distribution is the key diagnostic. The findings enable future experimental investigations of the B(E2) strength distribution in excited states around 5 MeV in 68Ni, as well as g-factor measurements, to resolve the question of magicity.
