# Soft dynamics of large transverse momentum processes

> We discuss the point that the processes with high transverse momenta could have a nonperturbative origin. This discussion is motivated by the fact that there are several experimental indications that such processes can originate from soft dynamics. The model combined with unitarity and impact parameter picture provides simple mechanism for the hadron production and reproduces power like dependence of the differential cross-sections in such processes at high transverse momenta and exponential behavior at small values of p_t. This model can be used for support of the argument that the soft dynamics can be responsible for large transverse momenta processes.

# Wiki Page: Soft Dynamics of Large Transverse Momentum Processes

## Key claim
High-transverse-momentum processes, typically attributed to perturbative QCD, could instead originate from nonperturbative soft dynamics. The paper argues that a model based on chiral quark dynamics, unitarity, and an impact-parameter picture can reproduce the power-like dependence of differential cross-sections at high transverse momenta *without* invoking hard scattering, but the argument is conditional on the model's specific assumptions about constituent quarks and Goldstone boson exchange.

## Method
The author constructs a nonperturbative model where hadrons consist of valence constituent quarks embedded in a quark condensate, with interactions mediated by Goldstone boson exchange (primarily pions). The inclusive cross-section for pion production is derived using a unitary scattering formalism in impact-parameter space, where the amplitude is expressed as a Fourier-Bessel integral. The analytic structure of the amplitude in the complex impact-parameter plane, specifically poles and a branch cut at zero impact parameter, yields exponential behavior at moderate transverse momenta and power-law behavior at high transverse momenta.

## Key numbers
- The power-law exponent for the inclusive cross-section at high $p_\perp$ is $p_\perp^{-6}$ from the model, compared to $p_\perp^{-N}$ with $N = 6.2 \pm 0.6$ observed for $\pi^0$ production at RHIC.
- The exponent $N$ does not depend on $x_F$ in the model, matching the experimental observation.
- The experimental $(1-x_F)^{5.1 \pm 0.6}$ dependence can be reproduced by choosing the relevant function $\bar{g}(x_F)$.
- The inverse slope parameter for the exponential region at moderate $p_\perp$ is $M x_F / (2\pi \zeta)$, with $\zeta = 2$ fixed in the model, giving a value about a pion mass.
- The exponent in inclusive cross-section dependence at high $p_\perp$ varies from 6 to 12 in different kinematic regions, almost twice the leading-twist perturbative QCD prediction of 4.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier nonperturbative model (Troshin, 2003) and contrasts with the standard perturbative QCD interpretation of high-$p_\perp$ processes, which assumes hard scattering and asymptotic freedom. The paper cites experimental indications from RHIC, including the strongly interacting liquid nature of quark matter and the constituent quark scaling of elliptic flow (Voloshin, 2003), as motivation for a common soft-dynamics origin of both heavy-ion and hadron-hadron high-$p_\perp$ phenomena. The model enables a unified description of exponential behavior at moderate $p_\perp$ and power-law tails at high $p_\perp$, and suggests that collective resonance phenomena in constituent quark matter may underlie the observed scaling.
