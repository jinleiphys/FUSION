# Parton physics from a heavy-quark operator product expansion: Lattice QCD calculation of the fourth moment of the pion distribution amplitude

> The pion light-cone distribution amplitude (LCDA) is an essential non-perturbative input for a range of high-energy exclusive processes in quantum chromodynamics. Building on our previous work, the continuum limit of the fourth Mellin moment of the pion LCDA is determined in quenched QCD using quark masses which correspond to a pion mass of $m_π= 550$ MeV. This calculation finds $\langleξ^2\rangle = 0.202(8)(9)$ and $\langle ξ^4 \rangle = 0.039(28)(11)$ where the first error indicates the combined statistical and systematic uncertainty from the analysis and the second indicates the uncertainty from working with Wilson coefficients computed to next-to-leading order. These results are presented in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme at a renormalization scale of $μ= 2$ GeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD determination of the fourth Mellin moment of the pion light-cone distribution amplitude (LCDA) in the continuum limit, using the heavy-quark operator product expansion (HOPE) method. The results are obtained in quenched QCD at a fixed pion mass of 550 MeV and at a renormalization scale of 2 GeV, with the caveat that the quoted uncertainties include both statistical/systematic errors and an additional uncertainty from using next-to-leading-order Wilson coefficients.

## Method

The HOPE method extracts Mellin moments of the pion LCDA from the Euclidean time dependence of a hadronic matrix element involving two axial-vector heavy-light currents, computed on four ensembles of quenched lattice QCD configurations with lattice spacings ranging from 0.0407 fm to 0.0813 fm. The three-point correlation functions are computed using the sequential source method with momentum-smearing and a variational analysis of two interpolating operators, and the resulting ratios are fit to the continuum twist-2 HOPE formula at one-loop order, with subsequent removal of lattice artifacts and higher-twist contributions through a model-dependent fit.

## Key numbers

* Second Mellin moment: ⟨ξ²⟩ = 0.202(8)(9) in the MSbar scheme at μ = 2 GeV, where the first error is combined statistical and systematic from the analysis and the second error is from the next-to-leading-order Wilson coefficients.
* Fourth Mellin moment: ⟨ξ⁴⟩ = 0.039(28)(11) in the MSbar scheme at μ = 2 GeV, with the same error structure.
* Pion mass used in the calculation: m_π = 550 MeV.
* Lattice spacings employed: a = 0.0813 fm (24³×48), 0.0600 fm (32³×64), 0.0502 fm (40³×80), and 0.0407 fm (48³×96).

## Context

This work extends the HOPE method previously developed by Detmold (2005) and applied to the second Mellin moment by Detmold (2021), demonstrating for the first time that the approach can access the fourth Mellin moment, which had been limited in the local operator approach due to power divergences (Martinelli, 1987). The results agree with other determinations in the literature after accounting for quenching, unphysical pion mass, and finite lattice spacing effects, and suggest that the HOPE method is a viable technique for studying higher moments of hadron structure observables like the LCDA.
