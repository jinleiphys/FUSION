# Four-Alpha Linear-Chain States in 16O

> We study 4$α$ linear-chain states in 16O in comparison with 3$α$ states in 12C by using the Generator Coordinate Method within a microscopic $Nα$-cluster model. It is shown to be very important to solve relative motion of $α$-clusters by taking into account the orthogonality between the linear-chain states and other low-lying states including the ground state. For 12C, the 3$α$ chain state disappears because of this orthogonality. The $4α$ chain state in 16O, on the other hand, is hardly affected by low-lying states and persists to remain above the 4$α$ threshold. The calculated moment of inertia of the $4α$ linear-chain rotational band (88 keV) reproduces the experimentally suggested value qualitatively.

## Key claim

The 4α linear-chain state in 16O persists above the 4α threshold and is hardly affected by coupling to low-lying states, whereas the 3α chain state in 12C disappears due to orthogonality with those states. The calculated moment of inertia of the 4α linear-chain rotational band (88 keV) qualitatively reproduces the experimentally suggested value, but the absolute energy of the band head is about 20 MeV higher than the 4α threshold.

## Method

The authors used the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) within a microscopic Nα-cluster model, employing Bloch-Brink wave functions for the α clusters. GCM basis states were generated using the Constraint Cooling Method, which minimizes the energy under constraints on total oscillator quantum numbers (nx, ny, nz). The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a wide space covering both linear-chain configurations and low-lying states, with angular momentum projection carried out numerically. The Volkov No.2 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction was used, with Majorana parameter M = 0.592 for 12C and M = 0.63 for 16O.

## Key numbers

- For 12C: the pure linear-chain intrinsic state (θ=π) projected to Jπ=0+ gave energy -71.72 MeV at the minimum point (l1=4 fm, l2=2.89 fm); after including longitudinal vibration the energy became -74.59 MeV; after including bending motion (θ = 11π/12, 10π/12, 9π/12) the lowest energy was -80.40 MeV; the full GCM calculation with 45 basis states gave ground state energy -91.23 MeV (experimental -92.2 MeV).
- For 16O: the optimal single-Slater-determinant linear-chain configuration (l1=3.29 fm, l2=1.50 fm, θ=0) had energy 34.79 MeV above the 4α threshold; the full GCM calculation gave ground state energy -129.1 MeV; the 0+ state at -91.68 MeV (0+6) had overlap (absolute square) with GCM basis states Nos.1-10 (θ=π and 11π/12) of 0.461 and with Nos.1-15 (θ=π, 11π/12, 10π/12) of 0.613.
- The calculated moment of inertia of the 4α linear-chain rotational band is 88 keV, compared to the experimentally suggested value of about 60 keV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of α-chain states by Ikeda (1972) and Horiuchi (1972), who analyzed stability against small vibrations around equilibrium but did not include coupling to other states. It also builds on the experimental suggestion by Chevallier et al. of a 4α linear-chain band in 16O from 12C+α → 8Be+8Be reaction data, and on theoretical work by Suzuki et al. (1972) analyzing decay widths. The paper enables future studies of even larger α-chain systems, such as the 6α linear-chain candidate observed by Wuosmma et al. in 12C(12C,12C(0+2))12C(0+2) reactions, by establishing that the 4α chain survives coupling effects while the 3α chain does not.
