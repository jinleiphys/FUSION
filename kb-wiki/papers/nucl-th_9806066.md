# Spinodal and dynamical instabilities at the phase transition from the quark-gluon plasma to hadrons

> The stability of an expanding parton plasma is analyzed within quasi-particle models. The effective mass of the parton is calculated self-consistently from a gap equation which is either obtained from the Nambu Jona-Lasinio Lagrangian or from a fit to observables calculated on the lattice. The latter shows an effective confinement. At thermal equilibrium the stability is studied within thermodynamics (mechanical stability) and via a linear response analysis of the Vlasov equation. The instabilities related to a first-order phase transition are found. For a plasma expanding in three and one dimensions far from equilibrium a new type of instability, called dynamical, appears. The relation to cluster formation is shown in a molecular dynamics calculation.

# Spinodal and dynamical instabilities at the phase transition from the quark-gluon plasma to hadrons

## Key claim

The authors show that instabilities in an expanding parton plasma can arise not only from thermodynamic (spinodal) mechanisms related to a first-order phase transition at finite baryon density, but also from a purely dynamical instability that appears in systems far from equilibrium even at zero baryon density, provided the effective parton mass increases sufficiently around the critical temperature. The dynamical instability is found for the phenomenological parton model (PPM) fitted to lattice data but not for the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model.

## Method

The authors model the parton plasma using quasi-particle descriptions with medium-dependent effective masses determined self-consistently from gap equations, comparing two variants: one derived from the NJL Lagrangian and one (PPM) fitted to lattice QCD data for energy density and chiral condensate. They analyze mechanical stability via the thermodynamic condition ∂P/∂ρ_B < 0, perform linear response analysis of the Vlasov equation around equilibrium to extract growth rates, extend this linear stability analysis to expanding systems in Hubble (3D) and Bjorken (1D) scenarios far from equilibrium, and illustrate cluster formation using molecular dynamics simulations with 100 partons.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 140 MeV for the two-flavor QCD used in the phenomenological parton model
- NJL model parameters: m_0 = 2 MeV, Λ = 653 MeV, GΛ² = 2.1
- For the PPM at equilibrium, mechanical instability occurs for temperatures below 120 MeV at finite baryon density
- Growth rates near the phase boundary in equilibrium: Γ/k = 0.1; for k = 1.0 fm⁻¹, the instability growth time 1/Γ is of order 10 fm/c
- For the 3D Hubble expansion at zero baryon density, the system becomes unstable at τ/τ_0 = 1.9, reaching instability time scales of order 1/Γ = 1.0 fm/c
- For the 1D Bjorken expansion at zero baryon density, the maximum longitudinal growth rate is Γ ≃ 0.5 c/fm at τ/τ_0 = 8 for k = 1.0 fm⁻¹
- Molecular dynamics simulation uses 100 partons with initial temperature T = 180 MeV in a fireball of radius r_0 = 2 fm, with a finite range σ = 0.5 fm

## Context

This work extends quasi-particle model studies of the quark-gluon plasma phase transition by analyzing both equilibrium and non-equilibrium instabilities, building on earlier lattice QCD calculations of equilibrium properties and on the Vlasov equation approach to collective modes in nuclear matter (Pethick, 1992). The authors confirm the result of Wojcik (1997) that no instabilities appear for the NJL model at finite temperature and zero baryon density. The dynamical instabilities found for expanding systems provide a mechanism for cluster formation that could be related to hadronization, though the model lacks color degrees of freedom and quantum mechanics. The molecular dynamics approach with medium-dependent masses offers a way to study the nonlinear development of these instabilities beyond the linear response regime.
