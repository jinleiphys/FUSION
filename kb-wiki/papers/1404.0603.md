# Effects of the tensor force on the ground and first $2^{+}$ states of the magic $^{54}$Ca nucleus

> The magic nature of the $^{54}$Ca nucleus is investigated in the light of the recent experimental results. We employ both HFB and HF+BCS methods using Skyrme-type SLy5, SLy5+T and T44 interactions. The evolution of the single-particle spectra is studied for the N=34 isotones: $^{60}$Fe, $^{58}$Cr, $^{56}$Ti and $^{54}$Ca. An increase is obtained in the neutron spin-orbit splittings of $p$ and $f$ states due to the effect of the tensor force which also makes $^{54}$Ca a magic nucleus candidate. QRPA calculations on top of HF+BCS are performed to investigate the first $J^π$=$2^{+}$ states of the calcium isotopic chain. A good agreement for excitation energies is obtained when we include the tensor force in the mean-field part of the calculations. The first $2^{+}$ states indicate a subshell closure for both $^{52}$Ca and $^{54}$Ca nuclei. We confirm that the tensor part of the interaction is quite essential in explaining the neutron subshell closure in $^{52}$Ca and $^{54}$Ca nuclei.

## Key claim

The tensor component of the Skyrme interaction is necessary to reproduce the experimentally observed subshell closure in \(^{54}\)Ca and \(^{52}\)Ca, but the claim of magicity is conditional on the inclusion of this tensor force in the mean-field part of the calculation; without it (using only the SLy5 interaction) the neutron gap is only 1.2 MeV and the nucleus appears non-magic.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and Hartree-Fock plus BCS (HF+BCS) calculations using three Skyrme-type interactions: SLy5 (no tensor), SLy5+T (tensor added to SLy5), and T44 (tensor included in the fit). They computed single-particle spectra for the N=34 isotones \(^{60}\)Fe, \(^{58}\)Cr, \(^{56}\)Ti, and \(^{54}\)Ca, and then performed Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations on top of the HF+BCS results to obtain the first \(2^{+}\) states in the calcium isotopic chain from \(^{42}\)Ca to \(^{54}\)Ca.

## Key numbers

- Neutron gap between the last occupied (\(2p_{1/2}\)) and first unoccupied (\(1f_{5/2}\)) states in \(^{54}\)Ca: 1.2 MeV with SLy5 (no tensor), increasing when tensor force is added.
- Increase in the first \(2^{+}\) state energy due to the tensor force: 1.43 MeV for \(^{48}\)Ca, 1.82 MeV for \(^{52}\)Ca, and 1.43 MeV for \(^{54}\)Ca.
- Maximum relative difference between HFB and HF+BCS single-particle energies: about 1.0%.
- Pairing strength used: \(V_0 = 350\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\) with surface-type pairing (\(\eta = 1\)) and nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Tensor interaction parameters: \(\alpha_T = -170\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\), \(\beta_T = 100\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\) for SLy5+T; \(\alpha_T = 8.96\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\), \(\beta_T = 113.02\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\) for T44.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies on the magicity of \(^{54}\)Ca (Hagen 2012, Rejmund 2007, Coraggio 2009, Holt 2013, Holt 2012) which had not reached a common conclusion, and directly addresses the experimental results of Steppenbeck (2013) who found the first \(2^{+}\) state at about 2 MeV. The authors build on the demonstration by Colo (2007) that adding a tensor term to SLy5 explains single-particle energy differences in Z=50 isotopes and N=82 isotones, and on the work of Marini (2014) showing that tensor-inclusive Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations produce magicity in \(^{52}\)Ca and \(^{54}\)Ca. The results enable future studies of shell evolution in the \(p\)-\(f\) shell region and confirm that the tensor force is essential for understanding neutron subshell closures near the drip line.
