# Studying chirality imbalance with quantum algorithms

> To describe the chiral magnetic effect, the chiral chemical potential $μ_5$ is introduced to imitate the impact of topological charge changing transitions in the quark-gluon plasma under the influence of an external magnetic field. We employ the (1+1) dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to study the chiral phase structure and chirality charge density of strongly interacting matter with finite chiral chemical potential $μ_5$ in a quantum simulator. By performing the Quantum imaginary time evolution (QITE) algorithm, we simulate the (1+1) dimensional NJL model on the lattice at various temperature $T$ and chemical potentials $μ$, $μ_5$ and find that the quantum simulations are in good agreement with analytical calculations as well as exact diagonalization of the lattice Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the Quantum Imaginary Time Evolution (QITE) algorithm can reliably simulate the (1+1) dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at finite temperature, baryon chemical potential, and chiral chemical potential, with results matching analytical calculations and exact diagonalization; however, the study is limited to a simplified (1+1) dimensional effective model rather than full QCD, and the quantum simulations are performed on classical simulators rather than actual quantum hardware.

## Method

The authors discretize the (1+1) dimensional NJL Hamiltonian on a lattice using staggered fermions and map it to spin operators via the Jordan-Wigner transformation. They then apply the QITE algorithm, which approximates the non-unitary Euclidean evolution operator e^{-βH} by a unitary operator e^{-iΔβA} parameterized in terms of Pauli strings, to generate thermal states and compute thermal averages of the chiral condensate ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ and chirality charge density n₅ = ⟨ψ̅γ₀γ₅ψ⟩. The results are compared with analytical mean-field calculations using the grand canonical potential with a hard momentum cutoff Λ = π/a, as well as with exact diagonalization of the lattice Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Bare quark mass m = 100 MeV, coupling constant g = 1 and g = 5, lattice spacing a = 1 MeV⁻¹, chemical potential μ = 100 MeV and μ = 50 MeV, chiral chemical potential μ₅ = 10 MeV used in ground-state energy comparisons between QITE and VQE algorithms.
- For the QITE algorithm, imaginary time step Δβ = 0.001 was chosen, with 500 operation steps shown in the comparison plots.
- At low (μ₅² + T²) with g = 5, μ = 50 MeV, m = 100 MeV, the dynamically generated mass Δm ≡ M - m is approximately 20 MeV.
- The momentum cutoff for analytical calculations is Λ = π/a, where a is the lattice spacing.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the chiral condensate at μ₅ = 0 (Czajka 2021) to include finite chiral chemical potential μ₅, which mimics the topological charge-changing transitions relevant to the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions. The QITE algorithm, originally demonstrated on the Transverse Field Ising Model (Ville 2021), is here applied to a fermionic field theory with four-fermion interactions. The approach offers a potential path around the sign problem that plagues Monte Carlo simulations at finite chemical potential in lattice QCD (Yamamoto 2011, Braguta 2015), and the authors compare QITE performance favorably against Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) methods using optimizers from QISKIT.
