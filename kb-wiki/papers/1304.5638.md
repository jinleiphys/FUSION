# Dense Baryonic Matter in Hidden Local Symmetry Approach: Half-Skyrmions and Nucleon Mass

> Hadron properties in dense medium are treated in a unified way in a skyrmion model constructed with an effective Lagrangian, in which the rho and omega vector mesons are introduced as hidden gauge bosons, valid up to O(p^4) terms in chiral expansion including the homogeneous Wess-Zumino terms. All the low energy constants - apart from f_pi and m_rho - are fixed by the master formula derived from the relation between 5-D hQCD and 4-D HLS. This allows one to pin down the density n_1/2 at which the skyrmions in medium fractionize into half-skyrmions, bringing in a drastic change in the EoS of dense matter. We find that the U(1) field that figures in the CS term in the hQCD action or equivalently the omega field in the hWZ term in the dimensionally reduced HLS action plays a crucial role in the half-skyrmion phase. The importance of the omega degree of freedom may be connected to what happens in the instanton structure of elementary baryon noticed in hQCD. The most striking and intriguing in what is found in the model is that f_pi that smoothly drops with increasing density in the skyrmion phase stops decreasing at n_1/2 and remains nearly constant in the half-skyrmion phase. In accordance with the large N_c consideration, the baryon mass also stays non-scaling in the half-skyrmion phase. This feature which is reminiscent of the parity-doublet baryon model with a chirally invariant mass m_0 is supported by the nuclear EFT with the parameters of the Lagrangian scaling modified at the skyrmion-half-skyrmion phase transition. It also matches with one-loop RG analysis based on HLS. A link between a non-vanishing m_0 and the origin of nucleon mass distinctive from dynamically generated mass is suggested. We briefly discuss the possible consequences of the topology change found in this paper on the forthcoming experiments at the RIB machines under construction.

## Key claim

The pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) stops decreasing at the half-skyrmion transition density \(n_{1/2}\) and remains nearly constant in the half-skyrmion phase, and the baryon mass also stays non-scaling in that phase, consistent with large \(N_c\) considerations; this behavior is reminiscent of the parity-doublet baryon model with a chirally invariant mass \(m_0\), but the authors note that a scalar (dilaton) degree of freedom is missing from their framework, which may be needed for more reliable calculations.

## Method

The authors construct a skyrmion model on a face-centered cubic (FCC) crystal lattice using an effective hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian that includes \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) vector mesons as hidden gauge bosons, valid up to \(O(p^4)\) in chiral expansion including homogeneous Wess-Zumino (hWZ) terms. All low-energy constants except \(f_\pi\) and \(m_\rho\) are fixed by a master formula derived from the relation between five-dimensional holographic QCD and four-dimensional HLS. The classical field configurations for pions, \(\rho\) mesons, and \(\omega\) mesons are obtained by Fourier expansion and energy minimization, with the \(\omega\) field determined by solving its equation of motion with sources from the hWZ terms.

## Key numbers

- The single skyrmion mass in HLS(\(\pi,\rho,\omega\)) is 1184 MeV, with root mean square winding number radius \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_W} = 0.433\) fm.
- The half-skyrmion transition density \(n_{1/2}\) in HLS(\(\pi,\rho,\omega\)) is larger than in the minimal model HLS\(_{\rm min}(\pi,\rho,\omega)\) by a factor of 1.7.
- In HLS(\(\pi,\rho\)) (without \(\omega\)), the half-skyrmion transition occurs at \(L \sim 0.8\) fm, corresponding to \(n_{1/2} \sim 6 n_0\) where \(n_0 = 0.17\)/fm\(^3\).
- The binding energy per baryon at the minimum energy configuration is \(\sim 100\) MeV for HLS(\(\pi,\rho,\omega\)), \(\sim 150\) MeV for HLS\(_{\rm min}(\pi,\rho,\omega)\), and \(\sim 50\) MeV for HLS(\(\pi,\rho\)).
- The minimum energy density occurs at \(n_{\rm min} / n_{1/2} \sim 2\) for HLS(\(\pi,\rho,\omega\)) and HLS\(_{\rm min}(\pi,\rho,\omega)\), and at \(n_{\rm min} / n_{1/2} \sim 1.5\) for HLS(\(\pi,\rho\)).
- The empirical values used to fix parameters are \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV and \(m_\rho = 775.5\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion crystal approach to dense matter, previously studied with pion-only Skyrme models (e.g., Kugler and Shtrikman, 1988, 1989) and with minimal vector meson implementations (Park and Vento, 2009; Park, Rho, and Vento, 2003), by including the full \(O(p^4)\) HLS Lagrangian with parameters fixed from holographic QCD. The finding that \(f_\pi\) and nucleon mass remain non-zero in the half-skyrmion phase matches results from a renormalization group analysis of HLS with baryons (Paeng et al., 2013) and from a phenomenological study of compact-star equation of state (Dong et al., 2012). The results suggest a link between the topology change and the origin of nucleon mass, with implications for experiments at rare isotope beam facilities.
