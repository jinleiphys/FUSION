# Enhanced J/Psi Production in Deconfined Quark Matter

> In high energy heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN, each central event will contain multiple pairs of heavy quarks. if a region of deconfined quarks and gluons is formed, a new mechanism for the formation of heavy quarkonium bound states will be activated. This is a result of the mobility of heavy quarks in the deconfined region, such that bound states can be formed from a quark and an antiquark which were originally produced in separate incoherent interactions. Model estimates of this effect for J/psi production at RHIC indicate that significant enhancements are to be expected. Experimental observation of such enhanced production would provide evidence for deconfinement unlikely to be compatible with competing scenarios.

# Enhanced J/Psi Production in Deconfined Quark Matter

## Key claim

If a deconfined quark-gluon plasma is formed in central heavy ion collisions at RHIC, J/psi production will be enhanced rather than suppressed, because bound states can form from charm and anticharm quarks that originated in separate incoherent nucleon-nucleon collisions. The enhancement factor is predicted to be between 1.2 and 5.5 relative to initial production, which is a qualitative reversal of the standard suppression signature.

## Method

The authors solve a kinetic rate equation for J/psi population in a longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma, including both gluon dissociation of J/psi and its inverse process: capture of a free charm quark and antiquark into a J/psi bound state with emission of a color octet gluon. The formation cross section is obtained from the dissociation cross section via detailed balance, using an operator product expansion form for the gluon dissociation of quarkonium. The calculation is performed for central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200A GeV with initial temperatures between 300 and 500 MeV, charm quark rapidity widths between 1 and 4 units, and isentropic expansion with thermalization time 0.5 fm.

## Key numbers

- Predicted enhancement factor for J/psi production at RHIC: 1.2 < S_J < 5.5, covering the full range of initial temperatures (300 to 500 MeV) and charm rapidity ranges (1 to 4)
- Average number of charm quark pairs per central collision at RHIC: N0 = 10, estimated from nuclear overlap function T_AA(b=0) = 29.3 mb^-1 and pQCD cross section sigma(pp -> c cbar) = 350 microbarns
- Initial fraction of charm pairs forming J/psi before deconfinement: x = 10^-2, reduced by a factor of 0.6 for nuclear suppression in central collisions
- For comparison, models extrapolating existing suppression mechanisms to RHIC predict suppression factors of 0.05 for central collisions
- Ratio of final J/psi to initial charm increases by about 50% between peripheral and central events
- Variations in model parameters produce changes of 15% to 35% in final J/psi yield (gluon density decrease by factor of 2 increases yield by 35%; transverse expansion velocity increase of 0.2 decreases beta parameter by 15%)

## Context

This work extends the original proposal by Matsui and Satz (1986) that J/psi suppression would signal deconfinement, and the subsequent observation of anomalous suppression by the NA50 collaboration at CERN SPS energies. It directly contradicts the standard assumption that quarkonium can only be formed in initial nucleon-nucleon collisions and then only suppressed by subsequent interactions. The authors build on their previous calculation of B_c meson formation in deconfined matter, and their results enable a new experimental signature: enhanced J/psi production with a characteristic quadratic dependence on initial charm number, which would be difficult to reproduce with conventional hadronic processes. The predicted strong increase of J/psi yield with collision energy provides a testable prediction for the initial RHIC runs at sqrt(s) = 56 and 130 GeV.
