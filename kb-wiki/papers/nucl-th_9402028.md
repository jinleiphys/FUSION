# Anomalous Crossing Frequency in Odd Proton Nuclei

> A generic explanation for the recently observed anomalous crossing frequencies in odd proton rare earth nuclei is given. As an example, the proton ${1\over 2} [541]$ band in $^{175}$Ta is discussed in detail by using the angular momentum projection theory. It is shown that the quadrupole pairing interaction is decisive in delaying the crossing point and the changes in crossing frequency along the isotope chain are due to the different neutron shell fillings.

## Key claim

The quadrupole pairing interaction is decisive in delaying the band crossing point in odd proton nuclei, and the changes in crossing frequency along the Ta isotope chain arise from different neutron shell fillings without requiring readjustment of the quadrupole pairing strength.

## Method

The authors used angular momentum projection theory to treat band crossings quantum mechanically at a given angular momentum, avoiding the uncertainties of the cranking approximation. They constructed basis states from one-quasiproton and three-quasiparticle (two quasineutrons plus one quasiproton) configurations built on a deformed Nilsson plus BCS vacuum, then diagonalized a Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing interactions. The quadrupole pairing strength was the only adjustable parameter, set proportional to the monopole pairing strength with constant C ≈ 0.20.

## Key numbers

- Crossing frequency for the proton 1/2[541] band in 175Ta: 0.375 MeV/ℏ
- Crossing frequencies for even-even neighbors: 0.292 MeV/ℏ for 174Hf and 0.291 MeV/ℏ for 176W
- Quadrupole deformation ε2 for 175Ta: 0.26
- The 1/2[541] band in 175Ta crosses the 3-qp band at spin 45/2 ℏ (compared to 37/2 ℏ for the 9/2[514] band)
- Without quadrupole pairing (C = 0), alignment could occur as early as spin 33/2 ℏ
- Increasing C from 0.16 to 0.24 produces a clear delay of the alignment process
- With C = 0.24 kept constant, the model reproduces observed crossing frequencies for isotopes 167, 169, 171, 173Ta

## Context

This work extends the earlier introduction of quadrupole pairing by Bes and Broglia (1971) and its known effects on attenuating Coriolis interaction in odd-mass systems (Hamamoto, 1974; Hamamoto and Karlsson, 1975) and shifting band crossings in even-even systems (Wahlborn and Freeman, 1978; Hara and Iwasaki, 1980). The study was motivated by new high-spin data from Beijing's Institute of Atomic Energy Tandem Laboratory (Wen et al., 1991) showing an anomalously large crossing frequency in 175Ta that could not be explained by the cranking shell model without an unreasonably large quadrupole deformation. The results enable systematic investigation of quadrupole pairing effects across odd-proton rare earth nuclei and suggest that high-spin data can serve as a sensitive probe for determining the quadrupole pairing interaction strength.
