# Rapid hydrodynamic expansion in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Hydrodynamic expansion of the hot fireball created in relativistic Au-Au collisions at 200GeV in 3+1-dimensions is studied. We obtain a simultaneous, satisfactory description of the transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow and pion correlation radii for different collision centralities and different rapidities. Early initial time of the evolution is required to reproduce the interferometry data, which provides a strong indication of the early onset of collectivity. We can also constraint the shape of the initial energy density in the beam direction, with a relatively high initial energy density at the center of the fireball.

## Key claim

A simultaneous and satisfactory description of transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and pion correlation radii for different collision centralities and rapidities in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV can be achieved using 3+1D perfect fluid hydrodynamics, but only with a realistic equation of state without a first-order phase transition and a relatively early initial time of τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c for the collective expansion.

## Method

The authors solved 3+1D relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamic equations starting from initial conditions at τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c with no transverse flow and Bjorken scaling flow in the longitudinal direction. The initial energy density distribution in the transverse plane was taken as a combination of Glauber model wounded nucleon and binary collision densities, with a plateau of width 2η₀ = 2.0 in space-time rapidity and Gaussian tails. The hydrodynamic evolution used an equation of state interpolating between lattice QCD data at high temperature and a hadron resonance gas at low temperatures, with freeze-out at fixed temperatures of 150 MeV or 165 MeV, followed by statistical emission and resonance decays using the THERMINATOR code.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at the center of the fireball for zero impact parameter: 107 GeV/fm³ at τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c, corresponding to a temperature of 510 MeV
- Initial proper time of hydrodynamic evolution: τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c
- Plateau width in space-time rapidity: 2η₀ = 2.0, with Gaussian tail half-width ση = 1.3
- Contribution of binary collisions to initial energy density: α = 0.145
- Freeze-out temperatures used: Tf = 150 MeV and Tf = 165 MeV
- For central collisions, the dense system exists for approximately 10 fm/c
- Maximum initial energy density in the 2+1D calculation: 86 GeV/fm³, compared to 107 GeV/fm³ in the 3+1D calculation
- The ratio Rout/Rside from the model comes within less than 10% of measured values
- Energy density at the center of the fireball at zero impact parameter: 107 GeV/fm³ at τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c

## Context

This work extends prior 2+1D boost-invariant hydrodynamic calculations (Chojnacki 2007, Broniowski 2008) to full 3+1D geometry, addressing the fact that at RHIC energies Bjorken boost-invariance is not realized outside central rapidities. The study builds on earlier 3+1D calculations by Hirano (2001, 2002), Hama (2005), Nonaka (2006), and Andrade (2008) that could describe spectra and elliptic flow but could not account for HBT correlations. The key advance is demonstrating that using a hard equation of state without a first-order phase transition and an early initial time simultaneously resolves the so-called RHIC HBT puzzle while also describing particle production at non-zero rapidities, enabling future studies of dissipative effects and viscosity.
