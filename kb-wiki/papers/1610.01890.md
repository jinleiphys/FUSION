# Oscillations in counting statistics

> The very large transverse momenta and large multiplicities available in present LHC experiments on pp collisions allow a much closer look at the corresponding distributions. Some time ago we discussed a possible physical meaning of apparent log-periodic oscillations showing up in p_T distributions (suggesting that the exponent of the observed power-like behavior is complex). In this talk we concentrate on another example of oscillations, this time connected with multiplicity distributions P(N). We argue that some combinations of the experimentally measured values of P(N) (satisfying the recurrence relations used in the description of cascade-stochastic processes in quantum optics) exhibit distinct oscillatory behavior, not observed in the usual Negative Binomial Distributions used to fit data. These oscillations provide yet another example of oscillations seen in counting statistics in many different, apparently very disparate branches of physics further demonstrating the universality of this phenomenon.

# Oscillations in counting statistics

## Key claim
The authors argue that coefficients \(C_j\) derived from experimentally measured multiplicity distributions \(P(N)\) via a recurrence relation used in cascade-stochastic processes exhibit distinct oscillatory behavior, which is not observed in the standard Negative Binomial Distribution fits to the same data. This oscillatory pattern is presented as evidence that the exponent of the power-like behavior in the underlying distributions may be complex, though the authors caution that the reliability of the \(C_j\) coefficients depends on the assumption that the measured \(P(N)\) are fully reliable and free from systematic uncertainties.

## Method
The authors analyze charged hadron multiplicity distributions \(P(N)\) from LHC pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV (CMS data) and compute the coefficients \(C_j\) using the recurrence relation \((N+1)P(N+1) = \langle N\rangle \sum_{j=0}^N C_j P(N-j)\), which connects each multiplicity \(N+1\) with all smaller multiplicities. They invert this relation to extract \(C_j\) directly from experimental data, then compare these coefficients with those obtained from standard NBD fits, two-component NBD fits, and their own Modified Negative Binomial Distribution (MNBD) where the parameter \(m\) depends on multiplicity \(N\) as \(m(N) = c\exp(a|N-b|)\).

## Key numbers
- CMS data at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV for pseudorapidity window \(|\eta| < 2\): best single NBD fit parameters are \(m = 25.5\) and \(k = 1.45\).
- The oscillatory \(C_j\) coefficients from CMS data are fitted by an exponentially damped triangular wave with parameters \(a_1 = 3.2\), \(a_2 = 0.6\), \(\omega = 16\), \(\delta = 1.67\), and \(\lambda = 25\).
- For the NBD case, the coefficients follow \(C_j = (k/\langle N\rangle) p^{j+1}\), where \(p = m/(m+k)\), giving a monotonic exponential decay with \(\lambda = -1/\ln p\).
- The amplitude of oscillations decreases with narrowing of the pseudorapidity window \(|\eta|\) and eventually vanishes for small \(|\eta|\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier observation of log-periodic oscillations in transverse momentum distributions (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2015) to multiplicity distributions, suggesting a common underlying phenomenon. The recurrence relation approach using coefficients \(C_j\) is connected to the concept of "combinants" \(C_j^{\star}\) previously studied by K. G. (1980s), V. V. P. (1990s), and others, with the relation \(C_j = (j+1)/\langle N\rangle C^{\star}_{j+1}\). The analysis enables a new test for models of multiparticle production: a successful model must describe both the multiplicity distributions \(P(N)\) and the coefficients \(C_j\) with the same set of parameters, revealing intrinsic correlations between multiplicities that were not accessible before the high-statistics LHC experiments.
