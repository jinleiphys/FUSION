# Most general neutron decay correlations: Standard Model recoil and radiative corrections

> To continue from our previous work Phys.Rev.D 109(2024),073007, we derive the full Standard Model prediction of the most general free neutron differential decay rate with all massive particles (neutron, proton and electron) polarized, including the $\mathcal{O}(1/m_N)$ recoil corrections and $\mathcal{O}(α/π)$ radiative corrections. For the latter we adopt the newly-developed pseudo-neutrino formalism which is compatible to realistic experimental setups, in which neutrinos and photons are not detected. We also provide readily-executable Mathematica notebooks to evaluate these corrections.

## Key claim

This paper provides the full Standard Model prediction for the most general free neutron differential decay rate with all massive particles (neutron, proton, and electron) polarized, including recoil corrections at order 1/m_N and radiative corrections at order alpha/pi, using the pseudo-neutrino formalism that is compatible with realistic experimental setups where neutrinos and photons are not detected.

## Method

The author derives the O(1/m_N) recoil corrections by expanding the squared 3-body decay amplitude and phase space in powers of 1/m_N, using the averaged nucleon mass m_N and a scaling parameter epsilon. The O(alpha/pi) radiative corrections are computed by combining previously studied one-loop virtual corrections with new bremsstrahlung contributions from the 4-body decay n -> p + e + antineutrino + gamma, using the pseudo-neutrino formalism where the pseudo-neutrino momentum is defined as p_nu' = -p_e - p_p. The bremsstrahlung integrals are split into an infrared-divergent part that combines with virtual corrections and a regular part that is evaluated numerically, with the full steps provided in supplementary Mathematica notebooks.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi coupling constant is G_F = 1.1663788(6) x 10^{-5} GeV^{-2} (from muon decay)
- The weak magnetic moment is mu_V = kappa_V + 1 = mu_p - mu_n = 4.7059
- The precision goal for the full SM prediction is 10^{-4}
- The pseudoscalar contribution scales as E_e^2/m_pi^2 < 10^{-4} and can be dropped
- The momentum-dependence of nucleon form factors scales as E_e^2/Lambda^2 < 10^{-4} and is neglected

## Context

This work is a direct sequel to Seng (2024) in Phys.Rev.D 109, which studied tree-level SM contributions with the Fermi function and virtual radiative corrections but without recoil corrections or the full bremsstrahlung treatment. The pseudo-neutrino formalism was developed by Seng (2023) to address the incompatibility pointed out by Gluck (2022) between conventional outer radiative corrections (Sirlin 1967, Shann 1971, Garcia 1978) and actual experiments, since the conventional formalism depends on the unmeasured neutrino momentum. This work enables precise tests of beyond-Standard-Model physics using the full correlation structure of neutron decay, including the newly measurable proton polarization.
