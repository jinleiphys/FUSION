# Jet quenching parameter in an expanding QCD plasma

> We present a new definition of the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal expansion. We propose a boost-invariant definition of $\hat{q}$, which is proportional to the broadening of the angular variables $η$ (the pseudo-rapidity) and $ϕ$ (the azimuthal angle). We furthermore consider radiative corrections to $\hat{q}$ and find potentially large corrections enhanced by a double logarithm like the case of a static medium. But unlike for the static medium, these corrections are now local in (proper) time.

# Jet quenching parameter in an expanding QCD plasma

## Key claim

The authors propose a boost-invariant definition of the jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}$ for a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma undergoing longitudinal expansion, defined via broadening in pseudo-rapidity $\eta$ and azimuthal angle $\phi$ rather than transverse momentum. Radiative corrections to $\hat{q}$ in this expanding medium contain a double-logarithmic enhancement similar to the static case, but these corrections are local in proper time, unlike the static medium where they depend on the total path length $L$.

## Method

The authors introduce a new coordinate system with basis vectors aligned with the initial jet direction, the azimuthal and pseudo-rapidity directions, and the proper-time direction, allowing them to map the expanding plasma problem onto the formalism used for static media. They compute the tree-level jet broadening via a Gaussian distribution in $(\Delta\phi, \Delta\eta)$ space, then calculate the one-gluon radiative correction to the dipole $S$-matrix using a propagator for a gluon and color dipole in the expanding plasma, extracting the double-logarithmic terms. The leading double-logarithmic corrections are resummed to all orders, yielding a renormalized $\hat{q}(\tau)$ that depends on proper time $\tau$ rather than total length $L$.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level transverse momentum broadening in $(\eta,\phi)$ space is given by $\mathcal{Q}_0^2(L) = \int_{\tau_0}^L d\tau \, \hat{q}_0(\tau) \simeq \hat{q}_0(L)L \, \frac{1-(\tau_0/L)^{1-\beta}}{1-\beta}$, where $\hat{q}_0(\tau) \simeq \hat{q}_0(\tau_0) (T(\tau)/T_0)^3 \simeq \hat{q}_0(\tau_0) (\tau_0/\tau)^\beta$.
- For $\beta=1$, the radiative correction to $p_\perp^2$ is $\delta\langle p_\perp^2(L)\rangle \simeq \mathcal{Q}_0^2(L) \, \frac{2\bar{\alpha}}{27} \ln^2(L/\tau_0)$.
- For $\beta<1$, the radiative correction is $\delta\langle p_\perp^2(L)\rangle \simeq \mathcal{Q}_0^2(L) \, \frac{\bar{\alpha}}{2} \left(1-\frac{\beta}{3}\right)^2 \ln^2(L/\tau_0)$.
- The resummed jet quenching parameter is $\hat{q}(\tau) = \hat{q}_0(\tau) \, \frac{I_1(2\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}} Y)}{\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}} Y}$, where $Y = \ln(\tau/\lambda(\tau))$ and $\lambda(\tau) = \lambda_0 (\tau/\tau_0)^{\beta/3}$ with $\lambda_0 \equiv 1/T_0$.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of radiative corrections to $\hat{q}$ from static media (Wu 2011, Liou 2013) to the physically relevant case of a boost-invariant expanding plasma, using the formalism of Baier (1998) for the expanding medium. The authors' definition of $\hat{q}$ via broadening in $(\eta,\phi)$ is motivated by experimental proposals to measure jet broadening in azimuthal angle (Mueller 2016, Chen 2016, Chen 2018) and by recent STAR measurements (Adamczyk 2017). The result that the renormalized $\hat{q}(\tau)$ is local in proper time enables its treatment as a quasi-local transport coefficient, which could be used in phenomenological models of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions and to test the fundamental relation between transverse momentum broadening and parton energy loss (Armesto 2005, Burke 2013).
