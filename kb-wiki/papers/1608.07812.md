# Multilayer network analysis of nuclear reactions

> The nuclear reaction network is usually studied via precise calculation of differential equation sets, and much research interest has been focused on the characteristics of nuclides, such as half-life and size limit. In this paper, however, we adopt the methods from both multilayer and reaction networks, and obtain a distinctive view by mapping all the nuclear reactions in JINA REACLIB database into a directed network with 4 layers: neutron, proton, $^4$He and the remainder. The layer names correspond to reaction types decided by the currency particles consumed. This combined approach reveals that, in the remainder layer, the $β$-stability has high correlation with node degree difference and overlapping coefficient. Moreover, when reaction rates are considered as node strength, we find that, at lower temperatures, nuclide half-life scales reciprocally with its out-strength. The connection between physical properties and topological characteristics may help to explore the boundary of the nuclide chart.

## Key claim

By mapping nuclear reactions from the JINA REACLIB database into a directed multilayer network with four layers (neutron, proton, 4He, and remainder), the authors find that beta-stability correlates with node degree difference and overlapping coefficient in the remainder layer, and that at lower temperatures (T9 ≤ 3) nuclide half-life scales reciprocally with out-strength in the remainder layer, but this relationship holds only for approximately 2500 out of 3000 nuclides with known half-lives.

## Method

The authors constructed a directed weighted nuclear reaction network using the substrate-product method on all 8048 nuclides and 82851 reactions from the JINA REACLIB database. They labeled each edge according to which of the three currency particles (neutron, proton, or 4He) was consumed as a reactant, creating four separate layers (n, p, h, r) with one-to-one dependent multilayer structure. Reaction rates were calculated using the standard seven-parameter REACLIB formula as a function of temperature T9, and these rates were assigned as edge weights to compute node in-strength and out-strength for each layer.

## Key numbers

- 8048 nuclides treated as nodes and 82851 reactions mapped into directed edges from the JINA REACLIB database
- Over 80% of stable nuclides satisfy the condition (k_in^[r] - k_out^[r] = 2) ∩ (k_in - k_out = 0) for the remainder layer and aggregated network
- At T9 = 0.01, approximately 2500 out of 3000 nuclides with half-life data fit the reciprocal relationship t^h ≈ 1/(e^0.4 · s_out^[r]) where t^h is in seconds
- The rate values range from approximately 10^-300 to 10^50 at T9 = 0.1
- The relationship holds for T9 ≤ 3 and the proportion of fitted nuclides drops as T9 increases

## Context

This work extends complex network analysis methods, previously applied to metabolic networks (Jeong 2000) and interstellar chemistry (Wagner 2001, Jolley 2010), to the domain of nuclear reactions. It contrasts with traditional approaches that study nuclear reaction networks via precise differential equation sets, and with prior substrate-product mapping methods evaluated by Holme (2009). The multilayer approach addresses the problem of currency nodes (neutron, proton, 4He) that would otherwise dominate degree distributions, following the framework of multilayer networks reviewed by Boccaletti (2014) and Kivela (2014). The results enable future work on network dynamics and nucleosynthesis processes such as the r-process, and may help predict half-lives of unknown nuclides when their reaction rates with neighbors are calculated.
