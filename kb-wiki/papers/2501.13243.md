# Distribution Functions of a Radially Excited Pion

> A nonperturbatively-improved, symmetry-preserving approximation to the quantum field equations relevant in calculations of meson masses and interactions is used to deliver predictions for all distribution functions (DFs) of the ground state pion, $π_0$, and its first radial excitation, $π_1$, viz. valence, glue, and sea. Regarding Mellin moments of the valence DFs, the $m=0,1$ moments in both states are identical; but for each $m\geq 2$, that in the $π_0$ is greater than its partner in the $π_1$. Working with such information, pointwise reconstructions of the hadron-scale $π_{0,1}$ valence DFs are developed. The predicted $π_0$ valence DF is consistent with extant results. The $π_1$ valence DF is novel: it possesses three-peaks, with the central maximum partnered by secondary peaks on either side, each separated from the centre by a zero: the zeroes lie at $x\approx 0.2,0.8$ and the secondary peaks at $x\approx 0.1,0.9$. Evolution to $ζ=3.2\,$GeV, a typical scale for nonperturbative calculations, is accomplished using an evolution scheme for parton DFs that is all-orders exact. At this higher scale, differences between the $π_{0,1}$ valence DFs remain significant, but analogous differences between glue and sea DFs are far smaller. This analysis shows that, owing to constraints imposed by chiral symmetry and the pattern by which it is broken in Nature, there are noticeable differences between the structural properties of the pion ground state and its radial excitations.

## Key claim

Using a nonperturbatively-improved, symmetry-preserving approximation to QCD's gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations, the authors predict that the valence quark distribution function of the first radial excitation of the pion, π₁, has a three-peaked structure with zeros at x ≈ 0.2 and 0.8, whereas the ground-state pion π₀ valence distribution is a broad concave function; the differences between π₀ and π₁ valence distributions remain significant after evolution to ζ = 3.2 GeV, but glue and sea distributions differ far less.

## Method

The authors solved the QCD gap equation and homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation using a process-independent effective charge and a gluon-quark vertex that includes a dressed-quark anomalous chromomagnetic moment (ACM) term with parameter η = 1.1, improving upon rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation. They computed Mellin moments ⟨x^m⟩ of the hadron-scale valence quark distribution functions for π₀ and π₁ up to m = 5 directly from the Euclidean-space integral formula, extended the moment set to m ≤ 10 using the Schlessinger point method, and then reconstructed the pointwise x-dependence of the valence distributions using an ansatz with logarithmic form for π₀ and a Gegenbauer-polynomial expansion for π₁. Evolution to ζ = 3.2 GeV was performed using an all-orders exact evolution scheme based on a process-independent effective charge.

## Key numbers

*   π₁ leptonic decay constant: f_{π₁} = 7.8(5) MeV.
*   π₁ valence DF zeros lie at x ≈ 0.2 and x ≈ 0.8; secondary peaks lie at x ≈ 0.1 and x ≈ 0.9.
*   Hadron-scale valence DF second moment ⟨x²⟩: 0.297 for π₀, 0.275 for π₁.
*   Hadron-scale valence DF third moment ⟨x³⟩: 0.195 for π₀, 0.162 for π₁ (column π₁⁽ᵃ⁾).
*   Hadron-scale valence DF fourth moment ⟨x⁴⟩: 0.137 for π₀, 0.104 for π₁.
*   Hadron-scale valence DF fifth moment ⟨x⁵⟩: 0.102 for π₀, 0.0725 for π₁.
*   Calculated π₀ mass: 0.14 GeV; empirical: 0.14 GeV.
*   Calculated π₁ mass: 1.24 GeV; empirical: 1.3(1) GeV.
*   Calculated π₀ decay constant: 0.098 GeV; empirical: 0.092 GeV.
*   Quark current masses (renormalisation-point invariant): \hat{m}_u = \hat{m}_d = 4.07 MeV, \hat{m}_s = 110 MeV.

## Context

This work extends continuum Schwinger function method (CSM) studies of pion structure, building on prior calculations of pion distribution amplitudes by Li et al. (2016) and distribution functions by Ding et al. (2019). It improves upon the rainbow-ladder truncation used in earlier moment calculations by including an anomalous chromomagnetic moment term, which was shown by Xu et al. (2022) to reduce the mean absolute relative difference between calculated and empirical meson masses by roughly a factor of 4.5. The results enable future comparisons with experimental extractions of excited-pion structure and provide benchmarks for lattice QCD calculations of radially excited meson parton distributions.
