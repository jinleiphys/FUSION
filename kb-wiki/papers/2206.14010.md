# Microscopic calculation of the $β^-$ decays of $^{151}$Sm, $^{171}$Tm, and $^{210}$Pb with implications to detection of the cosmic neutrino background

> The electron spectral shapes corresponding to the low-$Q$ $β^-$-decay transitions $^{151}$Sm$(5/2^-_{\rm g.s.})\to\,^{151}\textrm{Eu}(5/2^+_{\rm g.s.})$, $^{151}$Sm$(5/2^-_{\rm g.s.})\to\,^{151}\textrm{Eu}(7/2^+_{1})$, $^{171}$Tm$(1/2^+_{\rm g.s.})\to\,^{171}\textrm{Yb}(1/2^-_{\rm g.s.})$, $^{171}$Tm$(1/2^+_{\rm g.s.})\to\,^{171}\textrm{Yb}(3/2^-_{1})$, $^{210}\textrm{Pb}(0^+_{\rm g.s.})\to\,^{210}\textrm{Bi}(1^-_{\rm g.s.})$, and $^{210}\textrm{Pb}(0^+_{\rm g.s.})\to\,^{210}\textrm{Bi}(0^-_{1})$ have been computed using beta-decay theory with several refinements for these first-forbidden nonunique (ff-nu) $β^-$ transitions. These ff-nu $β^-$ transitions have non-trivial electron spectral shapes with transition nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) computed by using the microscopic Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM-2) for the decays of $^{151}$Sm and $^{171}$Tm, and the nuclear shell model (NSM) for the decay of $^{210}$Pb. Within the respective $Q$ windows, the computed ff-nu electron spectral shapes deviate maximally at sub-percent level from the universal allowed shape, except for the transition $^{210}\textrm{Pb}(0^+_{\rm g.s.})\to\,^{210}\textrm{Bi}(1^-_{\rm g.s.})$, where the maximal deviation is some 2.7$\%$. This confirms that the so-called $ξ$ approximation is fairly good for most of these low-$Q$ $β^-$ transitions and thus the allowed shape is a rather good first approximation. Our computed spectral shapes could be of interest for experiments aiming to measure the cosmic neutrino background (C$ν$B), like the PTOLEMY experiment. We have also derived C$ν$B cross sections for the ground-state transitions of the considered nuclei at the $β$ endpoint. Our findings indicate that more work on the atomic mismatch correction is needed in the future in order to extract reliable and precise C$ν$B cross sections for any nuclear target.

## Key claim

The computed first-forbidden nonunique (ff-nu) electron spectral shapes deviate from the universal allowed shape at the sub-percent level for most transitions, except for the decay of 210Pb to the 1- state in 210Bi, where the maximal deviation is 2.7%; this confirms that the xi approximation is fairly good for most of these low-Q beta transitions, but the 210Pb ground-state transition shows destructive interference where the xi approximation does not hold very well.

## Method

The nuclear matrix elements for the ff-nu beta transitions of 151Sm and 171Tm were computed using the microscopic Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM-2), while those for 210Pb were computed using the nuclear shell model (NSM) with the effective Hamiltonian khpe in the full model space spanning proton orbitals 0h9/2, 2p, 1f, 0i13/2 and neutron orbitals 0i11/2, 1g, 2d, 3s, 0j15/2. The shape factors were calculated within the Behrens-Buhring formalism, and the effective axial-vector coupling constant gAeff was varied between 0.80 and 1.20 and the mesonic enhancement factor epsilonMEC between 1.4 and 2.0 to quantify uncertainties. The cosmic neutrino background (CnuB) capture cross sections at the beta endpoint were derived from the computed shape factors, excluding the atomic mismatch correction due to its problematic behavior near the endpoint.

## Key numbers

* For 151Sm ground-state-to-ground-state decay (5/2- to 5/2+): endpoint correction of -0.39% with uncertainty -0.12/+0.10%
* For 151Sm decay to first excited state (5/2- to 7/2+): endpoint correction of -0.30% plus or minus 0.10%
* For 171Tm ground-state-to-ground-state decay (1/2+ to 1/2-): endpoint correction of 0.75% plus or minus 0.15%
* For 171Tm decay to first excited state (1/2+ to 3/2-): endpoint correction of -0.44% with uncertainty -0.19/+0.17%
* For 210Pb decay to 0- state in 210Bi: endpoint correction of -0.031% plus or minus 0.004%
* For 210Pb decay to 1- state in 210Bi: endpoint correction of 2.72% with uncertainty -0.42/+0.54%
* CnuB capture cross section for 151Sm ground-state transition: (4.79 plus or minus 0.44) times 10 to the -48 cm squared
* CnuB capture cross section for 171Tm ground-state transition: (1.14 plus or minus 0.07) times 10 to the -46 cm squared
* CnuB capture cross section for 210Pb ground-state transition: (3.27 plus or minus 0.05) times 10 to the -48 cm squared
* The shape-factor corrections relative to 1/xi were 0.71/xi for 151Sm, 1.16/xi for 171Tm, and 7.10/xi for 210Pb ground-state transitions

## Context

This work extends the indirect analysis of Brdar (2022) who found that the xi approximation should be valid for the decays of 151Sm and 171Tm, by performing explicit microscopic nuclear-structure calculations to verify the spectral shapes. The computed cross sections for 151Sm and 171Tm are somewhat larger than those reported by Brdar (2022), with the present work including all relevant sources of uncertainty. The results are relevant for the PTOLEMY experiment and other efforts to detect the cosmic neutrino background, and the authors identify the atomic mismatch correction as a key issue requiring more sophisticated treatment before reliable CnuB cross sections can be extracted for any nuclear target.
