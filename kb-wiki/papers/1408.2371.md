# Direct photon production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV at STAR

> We present the direct photon production for $1<p_{T}<10$ GeV/$c$ derived from continuum in the dielectron invariant mass region $0.1<M_{ee}<0.3$ GeV/$c^{2}$ from one billion $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV Au+Au events taken in year 2010 and 2011. A clear excess in the invariant yield compared to the number of binary collision scaled $p+p$ reference is observed in the \pt~range 1-4 GeV/$c$. Model calculations with contributions from thermal radiation and initial hard parton scattering are consistent within uncertainties with the direct photon invariant yield.

## Key claim

The paper reports a clear excess in direct photon invariant yield in Au+Au collisions compared to binary-collision-scaled p+p results for 1 < pT < 4 GeV/c, but states that model calculations including thermal radiation and initial hard parton scattering are consistent within uncertainties, not that the excess is definitively proven.

## Method

Direct photon yields were extracted from dielectron invariant mass spectra in the low-mass region 0.1 < Mee < 0.3 GeV/c2 using the virtual photon method, where the relation between real photons and associated electron-positron pairs is given by internal conversion. The dielectron spectra were fit with two components: a hadron cocktail contribution and a direct photon internal conversion component, with the ratio r = direct photon / inclusive photon as the free parameter. Data came from 258 million (Run10) and 488 million (Run11) minimum-bias Au+Au events at sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV, plus 39 million BEMC-triggered events from Run11 for high-pT coverage.

## Key numbers

* Direct photon invariant yield measured for 1 < pT < 10 GeV/c in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 200 GeV
* Dielectron invariant mass region used: 0.1 < Mee < 0.3 GeV/c2
* 258 million minimum-bias events from Run10 and 488 million from Run11 (0-80% centrality) passed quality cuts
* 39 million BEMC-triggered events from Run11, equivalent to 5389 million minimum-bias events
* Electron purity: about 95% in minimum-bias Au+Au; about 80% for BEMC-triggered electron sample
* Nuclear overlapping factor TAA = Nbin/42 mb = 6.95 mb-1 used for scaling p+p reference
* Clear excess observed in Au+Au over scaled p+p for 1 < pT < 4 GeV/c; no clear enhancement above 4 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the STAR direct photon measurement using the virtual photon method to high pT up to 10 GeV/c for the first time, building on prior dielectron measurements from STAR (PRL, 2014) and direct photon results from PHENIX in Au+Au, d+Au, and p+p collisions at the same energy. The results are compared to model calculations by Ralf Rapp and collaborators that include QGP thermal radiation, in-medium rho meson contributions, hadron gas interactions, and initial hard parton scattering, showing consistency within uncertainties and enabling extraction of QGP properties such as initial temperature.
