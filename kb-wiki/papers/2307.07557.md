# Photon and dilepton emission anisotropy for a magnetized quark-gluon plasma

> We study the higher-order anisotropy coefficients $v_4$ and $v_6$ in the photon and dilepton emission from a hot magnetized quark-gluon plasma. Together with the earlier predictions for $v_2$, these results show a distinctive pattern of the anisotropy coefficients in several kinematic regimes. In the case of photon emission, nonzero coefficients $v_n$ (with even $n$) have opposite signs at small and large values of the transverse momentum (i.e., $k_T\lesssim \sqrt{|eB|}$ and $k_T\gtrsim \sqrt{|eB|}$, respectively). Additionally, the $v_n$ signs alternate with increasing $n$, and their approximate values decrease as $1/n^2$ in magnitude. The anisotropy of dilepton emission is well pronounced only at large transverse momenta and small invariant masses (i.e., when $k_T\gtrsim \sqrt{|eB|}$ and $M\lesssim \sqrt{|eB|}$). The corresponding $v_4$ and $v_6$ coefficients are of the same magnitude and show a similar alternating sign pattern with increasing $n$ as in the photon emission.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that higher-order anisotropy coefficients \(v_4\) and \(v_6\) for photon and dilepton emission from a hot magnetized quark-gluon plasma show a distinctive alternating sign pattern with increasing \(n\) and a magnitude scaling approximately as \(1/n^2\), but these predictions assume a sufficiently strong magnetic field such that leading-order \(1\to 2\) processes dominate over the gluon-mediated \(2\to 2\) processes, which have not been analyzed in a magnetic field.

## Method

The authors calculated the one-loop photon polarization tensor in the Landau-level representation for a magnetized QGP at rest, using equal masses \(m_u = m_d = 5~\text{MeV}\) for up and down quarks. They then extracted the imaginary part of the Lorentz-contracted polarization tensor to obtain differential emission rates for photons (on-shell) and dileptons (virtual photons with invariant mass \(M\)). The anisotropy coefficients \(v_4\) and \(v_6\) were computed via Fourier decomposition of the azimuthal angle dependence of the rates, for magnetic field strengths \(|eB| = m_\pi^2\) and \(|eB| = 5m_\pi^2\) and temperatures \(T = 0.2~\text{GeV}\) and \(T = 0.35~\text{GeV}\).

## Key numbers

* Photon emission: at small transverse momenta (\(k_T \lesssim \sqrt{|eB|}\)), \(v_4 \simeq +0.05\) and \(v_6 \simeq -0.02\); at large transverse momenta (\(k_T \gtrsim \sqrt{|eB|}\)), \(v_4 \simeq -0.05\) and \(v_6 \simeq +0.02\).
* Dilepton emission: in the regime \(k_T \gtrsim \sqrt{|eB|}\) and \(M \lesssim \sqrt{|eB|}\), \(v_4 \simeq -0.05\) and \(v_6 \simeq +0.02\).
* The magnitude of the anisotropy coefficients scales approximately as \(1/n^2\) for the lowest nonzero even \(n\).
* The ellipticity coefficient \(v_2\) for both photon and dilepton emission is approximately \(\pm 0.2\) depending on kinematic regime, as reported in prior work Wang (2020) and Wang (2022).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the ellipticity coefficient \(v_2\) for magnetized QGP (Wang 2020; Wang 2022) to higher-order anisotropy coefficients \(v_4\) and \(v_6\). The results provide a theoretical fingerprint for the presence of a strong background magnetic field in noncentral heavy-ion collisions, which could help resolve the "direct photon puzzle" where observed photon elliptic flow is comparable to hadronic flow. The predictions need to be convolved with hydrodynamic simulations (e.g., Schenke 2010) to produce observable signatures, and may also apply to astrophysical plasmas in magnetars and gamma-ray bursts.
