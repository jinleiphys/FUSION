# Light nuclei under magnetic field and the lithium problem

> We analyze the effect of the magnetic field on the proton and neutron density distributions of the nuclei 2H, 3H, 3He, which are calculated ab initio, and the 6Li nucleus in the alpha-cluster model. It is found that the asymptotic exponential damp of the probability density at long distance is modified, and that the linear component of the exponent with respect to the magnetic field yields the leading contribution, while those of the second derivative and the confining magnetic force are subleading. Due to the linear dependence of the exponent, fluctuating magnetic fields always enhance the tunneling rate, i.e. the cross section of low energy nuclear reactions which occur at large separation across the Coulomb barrier. While this mechanism cannot suppress the production of 7Be in the early Universe, it has the potential to resolve the lithium problem either by increasing the reaction rate of 7Be + p -> 8B at the bigbang nucleosynthesis era if the magnetic field at this time was sufficiently strong, or by correcting the systematically enlarged 4He + 3He -> 7Be cross section by the unwanted magnetic field generated in the nuclear experimental setup, which was so far used as the input of the simulation of bigbang nucleosynthesis.

# Light nuclei under magnetic field and the lithium problem

## Key claim
The magnetic field modifies the asymptotic exponential damping of nucleon probability densities in light nuclei, with the linear component in the magnetic field providing the leading contribution. This mechanism cannot suppress 7Be production in the early Universe, but could resolve the lithium problem either by enhancing the 7Be + p -> 8B reaction rate during big bang nucleosynthesis if the magnetic field was sufficiently strong, or by correcting systematically enlarged 4He + 3He -> 7Be cross sections due to unwanted magnetic fields in nuclear experimental setups.

## Method
The author calculated the proton and neutron density distributions of 2H, 3H, and 3He using ab initio methods with the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon potential, and the 6Li nucleus using the alpha-cluster three-body model with the Kanada-Kaneko alpha-N potential and Argonne v8' N-N interaction. The wave functions were obtained via the variational Gaussian expansion method, and the asymptotic exponential damping of the probability density at large distance (7 fm and 12 fm) was fitted and Taylor expanded up to second order in the external magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- For 2H, the linear Taylor coefficient C_N^(1) for both proton and neutron is 1.5 x 10^-6 MeV^-2.fm^-1, and the second-order coefficient C_N^(2) is 2.8 x 10^-12 MeV^-4.fm^-1
- For 3H, the neutron linear coefficient C_N^(1) is -1.5 x 10^-5 MeV^-2.fm^-1, the largest magnitude among the nuclei studied
- For 3He, the proton linear coefficient C_N^(1) is 1.7 x 10^-5 MeV^-2.fm^-1, comparable in magnitude to the 3H neutron value
- For 6Li, the neutron linear coefficient C_N^(1) is 6.1 x 10^-7 MeV^-2.fm^-1 and the proton coefficient is 5.4 x 10^-7 MeV^-2.fm^-1
- To enhance the 7Be + p reaction cross section by a factor of two at a penetration of 60 fm, an external magnetic field of approximately 800 MeV^2 is required
- The unit used for magnetic field is eV^2, with 1 eV^2 approximately equal to 5.1 x 10^-3 T (51 G)

## Context
This work extends the study of magnetic field effects on quantum systems, previously applied to heavy quark systems by Suzuki (2016) and Yoshida (2016) using the Gaussian expansion method developed by Hiyama (2003). The paper addresses the lithium problem in big bang nucleosynthesis, where the predicted 7Li/H ratio of 5.1 x 10^-10 disagrees with the observed value of 1.6 x 10^-10 from metal-poor stars (Sbordone, 2010). Prior theoretical approaches to resolve this problem include adding long-lived non-standard-model particles (Ellis, 1984; Kawasaki, 1986; Jedamzik, 2007) and magnetic field effects on thermal velocity distributions (Luo, 2018) or Coulomb barrier screening (Park, 2023). The work enables future quantification of magnetic field effects on 7Be cluster structure and on experimental measurements of low-energy nuclear reactions.
