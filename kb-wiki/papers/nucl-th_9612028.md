# Mesonic and Binding Contributions to the EMC Effect in a Relativistic Many-body Approach

> Starting from a covariant relativistic formalism which uses nucleon and pion propagators in a nuclear medium, we revise the traditional effects of Fermi motion, binding and mesonic corrections to the EMC effect. The calculations are done in a very accurate way, using precise nucleon spectral functions and meson self-energies and a good reproduction of the EMC data is obtained outside the shadowing region which is not explored.

# Mesonic and Binding Contributions to the EMC Effect in a Relativistic Many-body Approach

## Key claim
The authors claim that a covariant relativistic formalism incorporating nucleon and pion propagators in nuclear matter, together with precise nucleon spectral functions and meson self-energies, reproduces the EMC effect data well outside the shadowing region, but only when the pion propagator satisfies an exact sum rule and static approximations for mesonic effects are avoided.

## Method
The authors compute the electron self-energy in infinite nuclear matter using relativistic nucleon and pion propagators, then convert this into the deep inelastic cross section via the local density approximation (LDA). The nucleonic contribution to the nuclear structure function is expressed using relativistic spectral functions, while the mesonic contribution is obtained from the pion and rho-meson self-energies in the medium, subtracting terms linear in density to avoid double counting with the nucleonic part.

## Key numbers
- The nucleonic contribution alone is about 10% lower around x = 0 than the nonrelativistic calculation of Ref. [12] that uses the flux factor of Ref. [7].
- Differences between the LDA calculation and a finite nucleus spectral function calculation are of the order of 2% in the EMC region.
- The pionic contribution is more moderate than that found in Refs. [1] or [11].
- The ratio R(x) for 40Ca and 56Fe agrees well with experiment outside the shadowing region (x < 0.1), which is not explored.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of the EMC effect by combining for the first time relativistic effects, binding, Fermi motion, and mesonic contributions (including rho-meson cloud renormalization) within a single covariant framework. It corrects inaccuracies in the static approximation for mesonic effects used in prior work (Refs. [1] and [11]) and shows that the flux factor prescription of Ref. [7] is numerically inaccurate. The approach enables more reliable calculations of nuclear structure functions and demonstrates that many-body effects remain relevant even at high lepton energies.
