# On the momentum-dependence of $K^{-}$-nuclear potentials

> The momentum dependent $K^{-}$-nucleus optical potentials are obtained based on the relativistic mean-field theory. By considering the quarks coordinates of $K^-$ meson, we introduced a momentum-dependent "form factor" to modify the coupling vertexes. The parameters in the form factors are determined by fitting the experimental $K^{-}$-nucleus scattering data. It is found that the real part of the optical potentials decrease with increasing $K^-$ momenta, however the imaginary potentials increase at first with increasing momenta up to $P_k=450\sim 550$ MeV and then decrease. By comparing the calculated $K^-$ mean free paths with those from $K^-n$/$K^-p$ scattering data, we suggested that the real potential depth is $V_0\sim 80$ MeV, and the imaginary potential parameter is $W_0\sim 65$ MeV.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the momentum dependence of the K⁻-nucleus optical potential can be reproduced within relativistic mean-field theory by introducing an exponential form factor that accounts for the internal quark structure of the kaon, and that the real potential depth is about 80 MeV and the imaginary parameter about 65 MeV, but only when constrained by comparing calculated mean free paths with those extracted from K⁻p and K⁻n scattering data.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian for kaon-nucleon interactions mediated by σ and ω mesons. They introduce a momentum-dependent exponential form factor F(P²_k) = exp(-P²_k / 4κ²) into the coupling vertices by treating the kaon as a two-quark system on a harmonic-oscillator basis. The parameters κ and β (for the imaginary part) are determined by fitting elastic differential cross section data for K⁻-¹²C and K⁻-⁴⁰Ca at P_k = 800 MeV/c. The imaginary part of the optical potential is constructed separately with an additional form factor and a phase-space factor f, and the mean free paths are computed and compared with those from experimental K⁻p and K⁻n total cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The form factor parameter for the real potential is κ = 0.275 GeV (determined by fitting K⁻-nucleus scattering data at P_k = 800 MeV/c).
- The form factor parameter for the imaginary potential is β = 0.49, 0.44, 0.42 GeV corresponding to W₀ = 50, 65, 80 MeV respectively.
- The real potential depth is V₀ ∼ 80 MeV, and the imaginary potential parameter is W₀ ∼ 65 MeV, as suggested by comparing calculated mean free paths with those from K⁻p scattering data.
- The imaginary potentials increase with momentum up to P_k = 450–550 MeV and then decrease.
- For the case V₀ = 83 MeV and W₀ = 65 MeV, the coupling constant g_σK = 1.044 and the real form factor parameter κ = 0.285 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMF study (Zhong et al.) which found a shallow real potential of (45 ± 5) MeV at P_k = 800 MeV/c, and contrasts with the density-dependent optical potential model (Friedman, 1999) predicting a very strong attractive real potential of 150–200 MeV, as well as the chiral model (Hirenzaki et al.) giving about 50 MeV. The results are compared with the momentum-dependent potential of Sibirtsev and Cassing (SC model) and the Jülich KN interaction (Ramos et al.), and the predicted potential depths are close to those indicated by a recent experiment on in-medium K⁻N potential depth. The authors conclude that no discrete K⁻ bound states in nuclei with A ≥ 12 can be found experimentally because the sum of half-widths of 1s and 1p states exceeds their separation.
