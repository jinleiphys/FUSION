# The Vector Probe in Heavy-Ion Reactions

> We review essential elements in using the $J^P=1^-$ channel as a probe for hot and dense matter as produced in (ultra-) relativistic collisions of heavy nuclei. The uniqueness of the vector channel resides in the fact that it directly couples to photons, both real and virtual (dileptons), enabling the study of thermal radiation and in-medium effects on both light ($ρ, ω, ϕ$) and heavy ($Ψ, Υ$) vector mesons. We emphasize the importance of interrelations between photons and dileptons, and characterize relevant energy/mass regimes through connections to Quark-Gluon-Plasma emission and chiral symmetry restoration. Based on critical analysis of our current understanding of data from fixed-target energies, we identify open key questions to be addressed.

## Key claim

The vector channel (J^P=1^-) provides a unique probe of hot and dense matter produced in heavy-ion collisions because it directly couples to both real and virtual photons, enabling the study of thermal radiation and in-medium modifications of vector mesons; however, the interpretation of data requires careful disentanglement of competing sources and the theoretical description must satisfy the consistency constraint that both photon and dilepton emission rates are governed by the same electromagnetic current correlation function.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for electromagnetic emission from hot and dense matter, where the emission rates for both photons and dileptons are expressed in terms of the imaginary part of the retarded photon self-energy (the electromagnetic current correlation function). For dileptons, the invariant-mass spectrum is analyzed across different mass regimes: low-mass (M ≤ 1 GeV) where light vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ) dominate and probe chiral symmetry restoration, and intermediate-mass (1.5-3 GeV) where continuum emission from quark-antiquark annihilation probes QGP radiation. The review compares theoretical calculations using hadronic many-body approaches (evaluating in-medium ρ propagators with selfenergies from baryon and meson interactions) and hydrodynamic/fireball models against experimental data from fixed-target experiments at SPS and BEVALAC/SIS energies.

## Key numbers

- At SPS energies (158 AGeV Pb+Au), theoretical analyses imply at least 10 generations of π⁺π⁻ → e⁺e⁻ annihilation are required to reproduce observed dilepton yields in semi-central collisions
- For dileptons at M=2 GeV, the temperature of maximal emission is T_max ≈ M/5.5 ≈ 360 MeV, well inside the QGP
- For RHIC conditions, initial temperatures T₀ ≈ 350 MeV and energy densities ε ≈ 30 GeV/fm³ are inferred from jet-quenching and hydrodynamic analyses
- The ρ→ee to ω→ee branching ratio gives an isovector dominance factor of g_ω²/g_ρ² ≈ 10 in the low-mass dilepton spectrum
- The pion decay constant f_π ≈ 92 MeV is related to the integrated difference of vector and axialvector spectral functions via the second Weinberg sum rule
- At SPS, thermal fireball calculations attribute 2/3 of the intermediate-mass thermal yield to the hadronic phase and 1/3 to QGP with uniform initial temperature T₀ ≈ 210 MeV
- Hydrodynamic calculations for the same SPS data use initial temperatures T₀ ≈ 300 MeV, assigning a larger fraction to QGP emission
- At RHIC, correlated charm decays increase by a factor ~100 over SPS yields, becoming the prevalent source at intermediate masses

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for using vector mesons as probes of hot and dense matter, building on earlier work by Shuryak (1980) on thermal electromagnetic radiation and by Rapp and Wambach (2000) on in-medium ρ meson modifications. The paper connects to specific experimental programs: CERES and NA50 at SPS, DLS at BEVALAC, and PHENIX at RHIC, as well as upcoming measurements from NA60 and HADES. The review identifies open questions including the need for in-medium a₁ spectral functions to strengthen connections to chiral symmetry restoration, and the importance of baryon effects in ρ and ω propagators that require more detailed analysis of baryon properties in hot hadronic matter.
