# Shear viscosity of neutron matter from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions

> The calculation of transport properties of Fermi liquids, based on the formalism developed by Abrikosov and Khalatnikov, requires the knowledge of the probability of collisions between quasiparticles in the vicinity of the Fermi surface. We have carried out a numerical study of the shear viscosity of pure neutron matter, whose value plays a pivotal role in determining the stability of rotating neutron stars, in which these processes are described using a state-of-the-art nucleon-nucleon potential model. Within our approach medium modifications of the scattering cross section are consistently taken into account, through an effective interaction obtained from the matrix elements of the bare interaction between correlated states. Inclusion of medium effects lead to a large increase of the viscosity at densities larger than $\sim 0.1$ fm^{-3}.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of pure neutron matter increases significantly when medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section are consistently taken into account, with the effect becoming large at densities above approximately 0.1 fm^{-3}, though the authors present this work as a first step toward a fully consistent calculation of neutron star matter properties.

## Method

The authors computed the shear viscosity of pure neutron matter using the Abrikosov-Khalatnikov formalism for Fermi liquids, with quasiparticle scattering probabilities derived from the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon potential. Medium modifications were incorporated through an effective interaction obtained from Correlated-Basis-Function perturbation theory, where the effective interaction was defined by equating the expectation value of the full Hamiltonian in correlated states to the expectation value of kinetic plus effective interaction in Fermi gas states. The effective interaction was then used to compute in-medium scattering cross sections via Fermi's golden rule, and the shear viscosity was obtained from the Brooker-Sykes exact solution to the transport equation.

## Key numbers

- At half nuclear matter density (rho = 0.08 fm^{-3}), inclusion of medium modifications increases the shear viscosity by approximately 75% relative to the free-space cross section calculation.
- At twice nuclear matter density (rho = 0.32 fm^{-3}), the medium-modified viscosity is larger by a factor of approximately 6 compared to the free-space result.
- The correction factor relating the Abrikosov-Khalatnikov approximate viscosity to the exact Brooker-Sykes solution lies in the range 0.750 < (eta/eta_AK) < 0.925.
- Using the reduced v8' potential instead of the full Argonne v18 changes eta T^2 by only a few percent over the density range 1/4 < (rho/rho_0) < 2, where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is the equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Flowers and Itoh (1970s) and Haensel (1990s) who used free-space scattering phase shifts or vacuum cross sections to estimate neutron matter viscosity, and by Cutler and Lindblom who studied viscosity effects on neutron star oscillations using various equations of state. The authors use the same Argonne v18 potential previously employed by Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998) for the equation of state, making this a step toward consistent calculations where transport properties and the equation of state derive from the same dynamical model. The results enable more quantitative analysis of the damping of neutron star oscillations and the gravitational radiation-driven instability of rotating neutron stars first discussed by Chandrasekhar.
