# Fluctuating parts of nuclear ground state correlation energies

> Background: Heavy atomic nuclei are often described using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method. In principle, this approach takes into account Pauli effects and pairing correlations while other correlation effects are mimicked through the use of effective density-dependent interactions. Purpose: Investigate the influence of higher order correlation effects on nuclear binding energies using Skyrme's effective interaction. Methods: A cut-off in relative momenta is introduced in order to remove ultraviolet divergences caused by the zero-range character of the interaction. Corrections to binding energies are then calculated using the quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (QRPA) and second order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT2). Result: Contributions to the correlation energies are evaluated for several isotopic chains and an attempt is made to disentangle which parts give rise to fluctuations that may be difficult to incorporate on the HFB level. The dependence of the results on the cut-off is also investigated. Conclusions: The improved interaction allows explicit summations of perturbation series which is useful for the description of some nuclear observables. However, refits of the interaction parameters are needed to obtain more quantitative results.

## Key claim

The paper shows that higher-order correlation effects beyond Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB), calculated using a regularized Skyrme interaction with a momentum cut-off, produce fluctuations in binding energies that are similar for different Skyrme parametrizations and are not very sensitive to the exact cut-off value, but a full refit of the interaction parameters is needed before quantitative predictions can be made.

## Method

The authors introduced a momentum cut-off (Lambda) in the Skyrme interaction to remove ultraviolet divergences arising from its zero-range character when used beyond mean-field level. They then calculated correlation energy corrections to nuclear binding energies for several isotopic chains using both the quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (QRPA) and second-order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT2) starting from HFB ground states, using the SLy5 and SKX Skyrme parametrizations. The fluctuating parts of the correlation energies were isolated by fitting and subtracting a liquid-drop model expression.

## Key numbers

- For O-16 with Lambda = 1.4 fm^-1, the MBPT2 correlation energy is 21% of the HFB energy; for Sn-132 it is 16%.
- The momentum cut-off values used in the main analysis are in the range 1.6-1.8 fm^-1.
- For the SLy5 interaction with Lambda = 1.8 fm^-1, the liquid-drop fit parameters for the average correlation energy from J=0+, 2+, 1- and 3- vibrations are {a_vol, a_surf, a_sym} = {0.99, -8.24, 0.77} MeV.
- The SKX interaction gives roughly twice as large average correlation energy as SLy5, attributed to its larger effective mass (m*/m = 0.99 for SKX versus 0.69 for SLy5).
- The pairing strength used with SKX is 560 MeV fm^-1 and with SLy5 is 640 MeV fm^-1, with a Gaussian range parameter a = 0.66 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that added corrections from shape vibrations (especially quadrupole) to HFB binding energies using methods like the generator-coordinate method (Bender 2006) or collective Bohr Hamiltonians (Delaroche 2010). The paper introduces a regularized Skyrme interaction to enable systematic many-body perturbation theory calculations, building on the authors' previous work on low-lying excitations (Carlsson 2012) and giant resonances (Vesely 2012). The results enable future refits of Skyrme interaction parameters at the MBPT2 level rather than the HFB level, which could improve the description of nuclear binding energies across the chart.
