# Nuclear Reaction Mechanisms and the Cronin Effect

> The influence of nuclear multiscattering and shadowing on pion spectra is investigated in $pA$ collisions from CERN SPS to RHIC energies. The calculations are performed in a next-to-leading order (NLO) pQCD-improved parton model, including intrinsic partonic transverse momentum distributions. The nuclear modification of the pion spectra (Cronin effect) is considered at different targets in a wide energy range. Theoretical predictions are displayed for planned $pA$ experiments at CERN SPS and recent $dAu$ experimental results are analysed at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Cronin effect (a 20-40% enhancement in particle production in proton-nucleus collisions compared to scaled proton-proton collisions) can be reproduced by a next-to-leading order (NLO) pQCD-improved parton model that includes intrinsic partonic transverse momentum, but only if the width of the transverse momentum distribution is set to approximately 2 GeV² and the number of semi-hard collisions contributing to broadening is limited to 3-4.

## Method

The authors calculated pion production cross sections in proton-proton (pp) and proton-nucleus (pA) collisions using a k_T-enhanced NLO pQCD parton model, incorporating a Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum distribution for partons. Nuclear effects were included via a Glauber geometry framework with impact-parameter-dependent broadening of the parton transverse momentum distribution, and shadowing was modeled using both the EKS and updated HIJING parameterizations. The nuclear modification factor R_AA' was computed as the ratio of cross sections with and without nuclear effects, and compared to experimental data from CERN SPS and RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- The Cronin effect shows a 20-40% enhancement in particle production in pA collisions compared to scaled pp collisions at FERMILAB energies (20 GeV ≤ √s ≤ 40 GeV)
- The intrinsic transverse momentum width required to reproduce the Cronin effect is ⟨k_T²⟩ ≈ 2 GeV²
- The average momentum squared imparted per semi-hard collision is C = 0.4 GeV²
- The maximum number of semi-hard collisions contributing to broadening is 3 ≤ ν_m ≤ 4
- The Cronin peak appears at p_T ≈ 4 GeV in the model
- At RHIC energy (√s = 200 AGeV), the intrinsic transverse momentum width used is ⟨k_T²⟩ = 2.5 GeV²
- The analysis covers the c.m. energy window 20 AGeV ≤ √s ≤ 200 AGeV
- At CERN SPS energies (√s ≤ 40 AGeV), shadowing effects are negligible at the ~5% level

## Context

This work extends earlier leading-order (LO) analyses by YZ02 to NLO accuracy, and builds on the k_T-enhanced pQCD approach developed by Wang01, Wong98, and YZ02. The calculations provide predictions for planned pA experiments at CERN SPS and successfully reproduce recent PHENIX π⁰ data from dAu collisions at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV). The results enable experimental discrimination between different shadowing models (EKS versus HIJING parameterizations) through centrality-dependent measurements of the Cronin effect in dAu collisions.
