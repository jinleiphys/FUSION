# Stability against $α$ decay of some recently observed superheavy elements

> The probability of $α$ particle emission for some recently observed superheavy nuclei (SHN) are investigated. The $α$-decay half lives of SHN are calculated in a quantum tunneling model with density dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nuclear interaction using theoretical and measured $Q_α$ values. We determine the density distribution of $α$ and daughter nuclei from the relativistic mean field theory (RMF) using FSUGold force, NL3 and TM1 parameter sets. The double folded nuclear potential is numerically calculated in a more microscopic manner using these density distributions. The estimated values of $α$-decay half-lives are in good agreement with the recent data. We compare our results with recently detected $α$-decay chains from new element with atomic number Z=117 reported by JINR, Dubna. Finally, we determine the half-lives of superheavy elements with Z=108-120 and neutron number N=152-190 to explore the long-standing predictions on the existence of an "island of stability" due to possible spherical proton (Z$\sim 114$) and neutron (N$\sim 184$) shell closures.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that alpha-decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei (SHN) can be reliably predicted using a quantum tunneling model with density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) interaction and relativistic mean field (RMF) density distributions, but the agreement with experiment depends strongly on whether measured or theoretical Q-values are used, and for some nuclei the calculated lifetimes differ from experiment by orders of magnitude unless theoretical Q-values from macroscopic-microscopic mass models are employed.

## Method
The authors calculated alpha-decay half-lives using the WKB approximation for quantum tunneling through a potential barrier composed of a double-folded nuclear potential (using DDM3Y effective interaction with RMF density distributions from FSUGold, NL3, and TM1 parameter sets), the Coulomb potential, and a Langer-modified centrifugal barrier. Density distributions for the alpha particle and daughter nuclei were obtained from RMF calculations assuming spherical shapes. The calculations were performed using both experimental Q-values (from measured alpha kinetic energies with recoil and electron shielding corrections) and theoretical Q-values from the NL-SV1 force and from macroscopic-microscopic mass calculations.

## Key numbers
- For $^{293}117$ with experimental Q=11.19 MeV: calculated lifetime 4.1 ms (FSUGold), experimental 21 ms
- For $^{274}107$ with experimental Q=8.94 MeV: calculated lifetime 11.3 s (FSUGold), experimental 1.3 min (78 s); using theoretical Q=8.56 MeV gives 3.02 min
- For $^{289}114$ with experimental Q=10.06 MeV: calculated half-life 0.35 s (FSUGold), experimental 0.97 s
- For $^{288}114$ with experimental Q=10.14 MeV: calculated half-life 0.09 s (FSUGold), experimental 0.47 s
- Half-lives calculated for Z=108-120 and N=152-190 using NL-SV1 Q-values show a sharp fall in log T_alpha around N=184 for Z=108, 110, 112, 114, indicating shell effects near N=184 or 186

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculations using DDM3Y interaction (Chowdhury 2006, 2008) to newly observed superheavy nuclei, particularly the Z=117 isotopes reported by Oganessian (2010) at JINR, Dubna, and the Z=114 isotopes reported by Dullmann (2010) at GSI, Darmstadt. The use of RMF density distributions from three different parameter sets (FSUGold, NL3, TM1) provides a more microscopic determination of the nuclear potential compared to earlier phenomenological approaches. The systematic survey of Z=108-120 with N=152-190 using NL-SV1 Q-values enables exploration of the predicted "island of stability" near Z=114 and N=184, showing that shell effects near N=184 or 186 may produce longer-lived superheavy nuclei, particularly for Z=110-114, though the effect becomes less prominent for Z=118 and 120.
