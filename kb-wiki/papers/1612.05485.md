# Event-by-event picture for the medium-induced jet evolution

> We discuss the evolution of an energetic jet which propagates through a dense quark-gluon plasma and radiates gluons due to its interactions with the medium. Within perturbative QCD, this evolution can be described as a stochastic branching process, that we have managed to solve exactly. We present exact, analytic, results for the gluon spectrum (the average gluon distribution) and for the higher n-point functions, which describe correlations and fluctuations. Using these results, we construct the event-by-event picture of the gluon distribution produced via medium-induced gluon branching. In contrast to what happens in a usual QCD cascade in vacuum, the medium-induced branchings are quasi-democratic, with offspring gluons carrying sizable fractions of the energy of their parent parton. We find large fluctuations in the energy loss and in the multiplicity of soft gluons. The multiplicity distribution is predicted to exhibit KNO (Koba-Nielsen-Olesen) scaling. These predictions can be tested in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, via event-by-event measurements of the di-jet asymmetry. Based on JHEP 1605 (2016) 008 and arXiv:1609.06104

## Key claim

The authors present an exact analytic solution for the stochastic branching process describing medium-induced gluon radiation from a jet in a quark-gluon plasma, showing that fluctuations in energy loss are of the same order as the average energy loss itself, contrary to the usual assumption that energy loss is deterministic for a fixed medium path length.

## Method

The authors solve the evolution equation for the gluon spectrum \(D(x,\tau)\) and higher n-point functions using the BDMPS-Z formalism with an approximate kernel \(\mathcal{K}_0(z)=1/[z(1-z)]^{3/2}\), where \(\tau = (\alpha_s N_c/\pi)\sqrt{\hat{q}/E}\,t\) is the dimensionless time and \(x\) is the energy fraction. They compute the 2-point function \(D^{(2)}(x,x',\tau)\) to obtain the variance of the energy loss, and derive the general n-point functions to study the multiplicity distribution of soft gluons.

## Key numbers

* The average energy fraction remaining in the jet is \(\langle X(\tau)\rangle = e^{-\pi\tau^2}\), leading to energy loss into the medium \(\mathcal{E}(\tau) = E(1-e^{-\pi\tau^2})\).
* In the LHC-relevant limit \(\tau\ll 1\), the variance of the energy loss is \(\sigma^2_{\mathcal{E}}(\tau) = E^2\left(\frac{1}{3}\pi^2\tau^4 - \frac{11}{15}\pi^3\tau^6\right) + \mathcal{O}(E^2\tau^8)\), showing the typical deviation scales as \(E\tau^2 \sim \omega_{br}\).
* The moments of the gluon multiplicity satisfy KNO scaling with \(C_p = \langle N^p\rangle/\langle N\rangle^p = (p+1)!/2^p\), corresponding to a negative binomial distribution with parameter \(k=2\).

## Context

This work extends the analytic framework developed by Blaizot et al. (2013) for medium-induced jet evolution, which identified the importance of democratic branching where daughter gluons carry similar energy fractions. The results contradict the usual interpretation of dijet asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, which attributes the asymmetry solely to different path lengths through the medium; the authors show that intrinsic fluctuations in the energy loss process are equally important. A similar conclusion was reached via Monte Carlo computation by Milhano and Zapp (2015). The KNO scaling prediction enables event-by-event tests using dijet asymmetry measurements at the LHC.
