# Statistical and Dynamical Fluctuations in Heavy Ion Collisions: Role of Conservation Laws in Event-by-Event Analysis

> The analysis of the statistical and dynamical fluctuations in nucleus-nucleus collisions on an event-by-event basis strongly relies on a comparison with specially constructed artificial events where statistical fluctuations and kinematical correlations are under control. In this paper, we present a novel, analytical method of constructing reference events based on independent emission, modified by the energy/momentum constraint, which can lead to a better understanding of the nature of the observed final-state fluctuations. This approach can be easily used in the analysis of other topics in the heavy ion field (e.g. flow, HBT etc.) allowing more precise measurements.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytical method for constructing reference ("mixed") events for event-by-event fluctuation analysis in heavy-ion collisions that, for the first time, imposes momentum/energy conservation constraints via a delta function, while relying on the central limit theorem to handle unobserved particles; the method is presented as a conceptual framework still being tested with Monte Carlo and experimental data, with quantitative results pending.

## Method

The method constructs mixed events by drawing one particle from N different data events of the same trigger and multiplicity, assuming independent particle emission, then imposing momentum/energy conservation via a delta function. The density of observed particles is obtained by integrating over unobserved particles using the central limit theorem to approximate their summed momentum distribution as a normal distribution, yielding a weighting factor that depends only on measured single-particle quantities. The weighting factor is converted to a binary accept/reject decision by comparing it to a random number between 0 and 1.

## Key numbers

- Multiplicity at CERN SPS energies ranges up to a few thousand particles in central Pb+Pb collisions, justifying use of the central limit theorem
- The weighting factor W is constrained to the interval (0,1)
- The method uses a four-vector (p_x, p_y, p_z, E) for momentum/energy conservation, with covariance matrix elements indexed i,k = 1,2,3,4

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event analysis approach proposed by Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski (1992) and applied by Roland et al. (NA49 Collaboration, 1998) to ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at CERN SPS energies. The method refines the traditional mixed-event technique by analytically incorporating conservation law constraints, building on earlier work by Danielewicz et al. (1988) in flow analysis of Bevalac streamer chamber data. The approach is intended to enable more precise measurements of dynamical fluctuations potentially related to Quark-Gluon Plasma formation, disoriented chiral condensates, jet quenching, and color fluctuations, and is claimed to be applicable to other topics such as flow and HBT analysis.
