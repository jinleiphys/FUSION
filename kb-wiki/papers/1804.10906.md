# Predictions for azimuthal anisotropy in Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.44 TeV using a multiphase transport model

> Xe+Xe collision at relativistic energies may provide us with a partonic system whose size is approximately in between those produced by p+p and Pb+Pb collisions. The experimental results on anisotropic flow in Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions should provide us with an opportunity to study system size dependence of $v_2$. In the present work, we have used AMPT transport model to calculate charged particles' $v_2$ for Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=5.44 TeV. We have also tried to demonstrate the no. of constituent quark, $N_q$, and $m_T$ scaling of the elliptic flow. We find that $n_q$ scaling of $v_2$ is not observed for the identified hadrons. The $v_2$ results from Xe+Xe collisions have also been compared to Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV. We find that flow of charged particles in (50-60)\% central collisions for Xenon nuclei is almost 30\% less than particle flow developed in lead ion collisions, implying the important role the system size play in development of particle collective motion in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT-SM model with default parameters, the authors predict that number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling of elliptic flow is not observed for identified hadrons in Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV, contrary to earlier expectations that smaller systems might show better scaling. The charged-particle elliptic flow in (50-60)% central Xe+Xe collisions is found to be almost 30% less than in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but the authors note that inclusion of nuclear deformation for Xenon may change these results.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model (version 2.26t7) with a parton scattering cross-section of 10 mb and default Lund string fragmentation parameters (a=2.2, b=0.5/GeV^2). They calculated the elliptic flow coefficient v2 for charged particles and identified hadrons (pions, kaons, protons) via the reaction-plane method for Xe+Xe collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.44 TeV in the midrapidity region (|eta|<0.8) and transverse momentum range 0 < pT < 2.7 GeV/c across multiple centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- Charged-particle v2 in (50-60)% central Xe+Xe collisions is almost 30% less than in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, with the difference increasing toward higher pT
- AMPT-SM results underestimate hydrodynamic predictions for v2 by roughly (13-30)% across centralities
- For (0-5)% central collisions, the calculated v2 is minimum; v2 increases from most central to mid-central collisions then decreases for peripheral collisions
- A clear mass ordering of v2 is observed for pT < 2 GeV/c, with lower-mass particles (pions, kaons) showing higher v2 than protons
- NCQ scaling of v2/nq vs pT/nq is not observed for pions, kaons, and protons in (50-60)% centrality
- The v2/nq as a function of (mT-m0)/nq shows a scaling-like behavior at low momenta, but the authors state NCQ scaling is not conclusively observed

## Context

This work extends AMPT model predictions to Xe+Xe collisions at LHC energies, complementing earlier hydrodynamic calculations by the same group (hydro_xexe, 2018) that found v2 larger by 25% in Xe+Xe than Pb+Pb for 0-5% centrality but smaller by 10% above 30% centrality. The study contradicts earlier AMPT predictions (AMPT_Scaling, 2015) that suggested NCQ scaling would hold better for smaller systems like Si+Si at 2.76 TeV. The results provide a baseline for comparison with ALICE experimental data (Acharya, 2018) and indicate that AMPT parameters need tuning to reproduce experimental v2 at higher pT.
