# Time fractals and discrete scale invariance with trapped ions

> We show that a one-dimensional chain of trapped ions can be engineered to produce a quantum mechanical system with discrete scale invariance and fractal-like time dependence. By discrete scale invariance we mean a system that replicates itself under a rescaling of distance for some scale factor, and a time fractal is a signal that is invariant under the rescaling of time. These features are reminiscent of the Efimov effect, which has been predicted and observed in bound states of three-body systems. We demonstrate that discrete scale invariance in the trapped ion system can be controlled with two independently tunable parameters. We also discuss the extension to n-body states where the discrete scaling symmetry has an exotic heterogeneous structure. The results we present can be realized using currently available technologies developed for trapped ion quantum systems.

## Key claim
The authors show that a one-dimensional chain of trapped ions can be engineered to produce discrete scale invariance and fractal-like time dependence, but only under the specific condition that the two-body potential exponent satisfies β = α − 1, and only for the low-energy dynamics of a second boson interacting with an immobilized boson at a chosen site.

## Method
The authors model a one-dimensional chain of trapped ions with qubits represented by hyperfine clock states, using off-resonant laser beams to drive stimulated Raman transitions that produce power-law interactions between all qubits. They map spin excitations to hardcore bosons and introduce a deep attractive potential at one site to immobilize a boson, then study the dynamics of a second boson interacting with the fixed boson. The Hamiltonian parameters are tuned so that the hopping coefficients scale as J₀/|r|^α and the two-body potential scales as V₀/|r|^β with β = α − 1, producing classical scale invariance that is broken to discrete scale invariance upon quantization.

## Key numbers
- For α = 2, β = 1, J₀ = −1, and V₀ = −30, the even-parity ground state energy is E₊⁽⁰⁾ = −27.05304149 (dimensionless lattice units).
- The theoretical discrete scaling factors for this case are λ₊ = λ₋ = 1.3895595319.
- For α = 2 and J₀ = −1, a time scaling factor λ₊ = 2 is produced by setting V₀ = −14.2388293.
- The best fit for the observed time fractal scale factor is approximately 1.9.
- The fractal dimension for the time fractal with equal-weight superposition is D = 2; for weighted superpositions with factor a < 1, D = 2 + log a / log λ₊.

## Context
This work extends the concept of the Efimov effect (Efimov 1971), originally observed in three-body bound states of ultracold atoms (Kraemer 2006) and helium trimers (Kunitski 2015), to trapped ion quantum systems where the power-law interactions are intrinsically tunable. The system enables experimental realization of quantum scale anomalies analogous to those in graphene with Dirac fermions and attractive Coulomb interactions (Ovdat 2017), and introduces the concept of time fractals, which the authors note appeared independently in a concurrent preprint (C. Gao 2019) under the name "dynamical fractals."
