# Chiral Symmetry in Nuclei -- Theoretical Expectations and Hard Facts

> It is widely believed that chiral symmetry is restored not only at high temperatures, but also at high nuclear densities. The drop of the order parameter of the chiral phase transition, the chiral condensate, with density has indeed been calculated in various models and is as such a rather robust result. In this talk I point out that the connection of this property with actual observables is far less clear. For this task a good hadronic description of the primary production of hadrons, their propagation inside the nuclear medium, their decay and the propagation of the decay products through the medium to the detector all have to be treated with equal accuracy and weight. In this talk I illustrate with the examples of $ω$ production and $π^0π^0$ production how important in particular final state interactions are.

## Key claim

While the drop of the chiral condensate with density is a robust theoretical result, the connection between this order parameter and actual experimental observables is far less clear, and a reliable interpretation requires that primary hadron production, propagation in the nuclear medium, decay, and final-state interactions of decay products all be treated with equal accuracy.

## Method

The author and his group performed state-of-the-art calculations of vector meson spectral functions in cold nuclear matter using a unitary coupled-channel analysis of all existing πN and γN data up to an invariant mass of 2 GeV, extracting the ω meson selfenergy from the ωN scattering amplitude. They then analyzed the CB/TAPS@ELSA experiment on ω photoproduction and the TAPS experiment on π⁰π⁰ production using the semiclassical transport code GiBUU, which treats final-state interactions in a coupled-channel framework.

## Key numbers

- The ω meson at rest in nuclear matter at density ρ₀ acquires a broadening of about 60 MeV, with a small upward shift of the peak mass, and the spectral function exhibits a small peak at a mass of around 550 MeV due to coupling to the S11(1535) resonance.
- Analysis of the nuclear transparency ratio for ω mesons at an average momentum of 1.1 GeV yields a collisional width of about 130-150 MeV, larger by about a factor of 3 than previously assumed.
- The ω spectral function published by the CB/TAPS@ELSA collaboration can be explained only if a lowering of the ω meson mass in medium by about 16% is assumed together with the appropriate collisional broadening.
- For densities less than 0.5 ρ₀, the linear scaling of selfenergies inherent in the low-density approximation is badly violated for the ρ and π mesons, while it is a reasonable approximation for the η meson.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions by Brown and Rho (1991) and Hatsuda and Lee (1992) that vector meson masses should decrease in medium due to partial chiral symmetry restoration, as well as calculations by Klingl et al. and Kunihiro et al. on scalar 2π strength. The author's coupled-channel calculation of the ω selfenergy qualitatively agrees with Lutz et al. in yielding a lower mass structure around 500-600 MeV and only a small shift of the main peak, in contrast to the results of Klingl et al. and Thomas et al. The analysis of the TAPS 2π⁰ data shows that the observed effects can be explained by mundane pion rescattering rather than chiral symmetry restoration, and that present-day data are all consistent with simple rescattering. The work enables future studies that disentangle in-medium mass shifts from collisional broadening and production/decay distortions, with the quest for chiral symmetry restoration in nuclei remaining a challenging topic requiring more theoretical and experimental work.
