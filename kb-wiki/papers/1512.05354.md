# Testing Hydrodynamic Descriptions of of p+p Collisions at \sqrt{s}=7 TeV

> In high energy collisions of heavy-ions, experimental findings of collective flow are customarily associated with the presence of a thermalized medium expanding according to the laws of hydrodynamics. Recently, the ATLAS, CMS and ALICE experiments found signals of the same type and magnitude in ultrarelativistic proton-proton collisions. In this study, the state-of-the-art hydrodynamic model SONIC is used to simulate the systems created in p+p collisions. By varying the size of the second-order transport coefficients, the range of applicability of hydrodynamics itself to the systems created in p+p collisions is quantified. It is found that hydrodynamics can give quantitatively reliable results for the particle spectra and the elliptic momentum anisotropy coefficient $v_2$. Using a simple geometric model of the proton based on the elastic form factor leads to results of similar type and magnitude to those found in experiment when allowing for a small bulk viscosity coefficient.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamics can give quantitatively reliable results for particle spectra and the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) in proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV, but only when the charged-particle pseudorapidity density satisfies \(\frac{dN}{d\eta}\gtrsim 2\) for shear viscosity over entropy ratio \(\frac{\eta}{s}\leq 0.08\); below this multiplicity threshold the hydrodynamic description breaks down.

## Method

The authors used the SONIC hydrodynamic model, which combines causal relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with shear and bulk viscosity followed by the B3D hadron cascade afterburner for temperatures below 0.17 GeV, assuming boost invariance. Initial conditions for the transverse energy density were generated using two geometric proton models: a round proton based on the Fourier transform of the elastic form factor (RND model) and a fluctuating proton shape incorporating spin fluctuations (FLC model). The applicability of hydrodynamics was quantified by varying the second-order transport coefficients (shear and bulk relaxation times \(\tau_\pi,\tau_\Pi\)) by 50 percent around a fiducial value and checking whether final results changed significantly.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic description of \(v_2\) breaks down for \(\frac{dN}{d\eta}\lesssim 2\) when \(\frac{\eta}{s}=0.08\).
- A bulk viscosity coefficient of \(\frac{\zeta}{s}=0.02\) causes a factor two decrease in pion mean transverse momentum in proton-proton collisions, compared to approximately 25 percent reduction for a bulk viscosity peaking at \(\frac{\zeta}{s}=0.3\) in heavy-ion collisions as found by Ryu (2015).
- The initialization time for hydrodynamics was \(\tau_0=0.25\) fm/c.
- The maximum impact parameter for the proton model was \(b_{\rm max}=1.6\) fm, corresponding to approximately twice the proton radius.
- The hadron cascade was switched on for temperatures \(T<0.17\) GeV.

## Context

This study extends hydrodynamic modeling, previously successful for heavy-ion collisions and for p+Pb, d+Au, and \(^3\)He+Au collisions, to the much smaller systems created in proton-proton collisions. It contradicts an earlier prediction by Luzum (2009) that hydrodynamics would not apply to such small systems, with the improvement here being the use of a hadronic cascade afterburner instead of continuing hydrodynamics below the QCD phase transition. The work enables future quantitative extraction of QCD transport coefficients (especially bulk viscosity) from proton-proton collision data and suggests that the quark-gluon plasma interpretation is consistent with experimental findings in these smallest colliding systems.
