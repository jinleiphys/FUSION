# The Omega Puzzle

> Recent data on particles associated with Omega trigger suggest that such events have jet structure. However, the exponential pT distribution of Omega suggests that no jets are involved. We resolve this puzzle by suggesting that the Omega is produced by the recombination of the enhanced thermal partons in the ridge that can have other hadrons in association with the trigger, all having exponential pT distributions.

# The Omega Puzzle

## Key claim

The apparent contradiction between jet-like associated particle distributions for Omega-triggered events and the exponential (non-jet-like) transverse momentum spectrum of Omega itself is resolved by proposing that Omega is produced via recombination of three thermal s-quarks from the ridge (the enhanced thermal parton component of a jet), rather than from the jet peak. The ridge can produce both the trigger Omega and associated hadrons, all with exponential pT distributions, while the jet peak (if present) is overwhelmed at the low associated pT values used in the measurement.

## Method

The author analyzes STAR data on Omega production in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, comparing the Omega pT spectrum (exponential up to ~6 GeV/c) with Omega-triggered associated particle distributions showing a peak in |Delta-phi|<0.5. The recombination model is applied, distinguishing between thermal-thermal recombination (producing exponential spectra) and shower-thermal recombination (producing power-law spectra). The ridge component from the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2004) is invoked to explain associated particles without requiring a jet peak, using the observation that for Lambda-triggered events the jet-to-ridge ratio J/R is already <0.1, and extrapolating to an even smaller ratio for Omega triggers.

## Key numbers

- Omega trigger pT range: 2.5 < pT_trig < 4.5 GeV/c
- Associated particle pT range: 1.5 < pT_assoc < pT_trig GeV/c
- Associated particle peak in |Delta-phi| < 0.5 radians
- Omega pT spectrum exponential up to pT ~ 6 GeV/c
- For unidentified hadrons at pT_assoc ~ 1.2 GeV/c, J/R ratio is 0.1-0.15
- For Lambda-triggered events, J/R ratio is <0.1
- s-quark temperature Ts = 0.392 GeV used to reproduce Omega distribution, ~20% higher than T = 0.317 GeV for pion and proton production
- Each s-quark needs pT ~ 0.8 GeV/c to form Omega at pT_trig >= 2.5 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2004) for strange particle production at intermediate pT, which had previously predicted no associated particles for Omega triggers based on the assumption that thermal partons were in the statistical background. The puzzle arose from STAR data presented by Bielcikova (2006) showing associated particles above background. The solution reinterprets the thermal partons as residing in the ridge rather than the background, consistent with the ridge phenomenology described by Putschke (2006) showing that ridge yield increases with centrality while jet yield does not. This enables predictions that the ridge should be visible in Delta-eta projections, associated hadrons should have exponential pT distributions, and the p/pi ratio among associated particles should be high.
