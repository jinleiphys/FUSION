# Scale-Invariant Hidden Local Symmetry, Topology Change and Dense Baryonic Matter II

> Exploiting certain robust topological inputs from the skyrmion description of compressed baryonic matter with a scale-chiral symmetric Lagrangian, we predict the equation of state that is consistent with the properties of nuclear matter at the equilibrium density, supports the maximum mass of massive compact star $\sim 2 M_\odot$ and surprisingly gives the sound velocity close to the "conformal velocity" $1/\sqrt{3}$ at densities $\gtrsim 3 n_0$. At the core of this result is the observation that parity-doubling occurs in the nucleon structure as density goes above $\sim 2n_0$ with a chiral-singlet mass $m_0 \sim (0.6-0.9) m_N$, hinting at a possible up-to-date unsuspected source of proton mass and an emergence at high density of scale symmetry and flavor local symmetry, both hidden in the QCD vacuum.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that at densities above approximately twice nuclear saturation density ($\sim 2n_0$), parity-doubling emerges in the nucleon structure with a chiral-singlet mass $m_0 \sim (0.6-0.9) m_N$, leading to an equation of state that supports a maximum compact star mass of $\sim 2 M_\odot$ and a sound velocity approaching the conformal limit $v_s^2/c^2 = 1/3$ at densities $\gtrsim 3 n_0$, contingent on a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions encoded in the intrinsic density dependence of the effective Lagrangian parameters.

## Method

The authors construct a scale-invariant hidden local symmetric Lagrangian ($bs$HLS) that includes nucleons, pions, rho and omega mesons, and a dilaton field representing the Nambu-Goldstone boson of scale symmetry. They map robust topological properties from skyrmion crystal simulations on a lattice (using the $s$HLS Lagrangian) onto the intrinsic density dependence of the $bs$HLS parameters, specifically implementing the skyrmion-to-half-skyrmion transition at a density $n_{1/2} \sim 2n_0$ where the in-medium nucleon mass and decay constants become approximately constant. The equation of state is then computed using a two-decimation renormalization group approach with $V_{lowk}$, first reaching the Fermi-liquid fixed point and then incorporating higher-order correlations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum compact star mass: $M_{max} \simeq 2.05 M_\odot$ with radius $R \simeq 12.19$ km and maximum central density $n_{\rm max} \simeq 5.1 n_0$, where $n_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is nuclear saturation density.
- Sound velocity approaches the conformal limit $v_s^2/c^2 \simeq 1/3$ at densities $\gtrsim 3 n_0$.
- The chiral-singlet mass in the parity-doubling regime is $m_0 \sim (0.6-0.9) m_N$, where $m_N$ is the free-space nucleon mass.
- The in-medium pion decay constant, dilaton decay constant, and nucleon mass all scale to a nearly density-independent constant $\kappa \sim (0.6-0.9)$ above the transition density $n_{1/2} \sim 2 n_0$.
- Saturation density from the $V_{lowk}$ RG calculation is $n_{\rm sat} = 0.154$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The parameter $c_I$ controlling the density dependence of $\Phi^\ast$ for $n \leq n_{1/2}$ is fine-tuned between 0.13 and 0.20, with $c_I \simeq 0.20$ at $n_0$ reproducing the empirical $f_\pi^\ast/f_\pi \simeq 0.8$ from deeply bound pionic nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Paeng et al., 2016; Paeng et al., 2015) by sharpening the implementation of intrinsic density dependence from skyrmion topology change in the $V_{lowk}$ renormalization group framework. The prediction that the sound velocity approaches the conformal limit $1/\sqrt{3}$ contrasts with conventional hadronic models based on energy density functionals, which typically give $v_c^2/c^2 > 1/3$ (Haensel, 2007). The emergent parity-doubling structure resembles the parity-doublet nucleon model (DeTar and Kunihiro, 1989) but with the chiral-invariant mass $m_0$ arising dynamically from the half-skyrmion phase rather than being inserted by hand, and the result suggests that scale symmetry and flavor local symmetry, both hidden in the QCD vacuum, may emerge at high density in compact stars.
