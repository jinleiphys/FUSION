# Equation of state and phase transitions in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The structure of the 3-dimension pressure-temperature-asymmetry surface of equilibrium of the asymmetric nuclear matter is studied within the thermal Thomas-Fermi approximation. Special attention is paid to the difference of the asymmetry parameter between the boiling sheet and that of the condensation sheet of the surface of equilibrium. We derive the condition of existence of the regime of retrograde condensation at the boiling of the asymmetric nuclear matter. We have performed calculations of the caloric curves in the case of isobaric heating. We have shown the presence of the plateau region in caloric curves at the isobaric heating of the asymmetric nuclear matter. The shape of the caloric curve depends on the pressure and is sensitive to the value of the asymmetry parameter. We point out that the experimental value of the plateau temperature T \approx 7 MeV corresponds to the pressure P = 0.01 MeV/fm^3 at the isobaric boiling.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the experimentally observed plateau temperature of approximately 7 MeV in nuclear caloric curves can be reproduced by isobaric boiling of asymmetric nuclear matter at a pressure of about 0.01 MeV/fm³, but only if one assumes the specific regime where vapor remains near the liquid (regime II). The authors note that surface and Coulomb effects, which are not included in their model, would be needed for a more accurate estimation.

## Method
The authors employ a temperature-dependent Thomas-Fermi approximation with the SkM Skyrme effective interaction to calculate the energy density, entropy density, and free energy density of asymmetric nuclear matter. They construct the three-dimensional pressure-temperature-asymmetry (P,T,X) surface of equilibrium by solving the Gibbs equilibrium conditions (equal pressures and chemical potentials) for coexisting liquid and vapor phases. Caloric curves for isobaric heating are then computed for two different boiling regimes: one where vapor is removed from the system (regime I) and one where vapor remains in contact with the liquid at fixed pressure (regime II).

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter: T_cr^(sym) = 14.61 MeV, obtained from the condition (∂P/∂ρ)_T,X=0 = (∂²P/∂ρ²)_T,X=0 = 0
- Critical pressure for symmetric nuclear matter: P_cr^(sym) = 0.2109 MeV/fm³
- Maximum possible asymmetry for bound liquid phase (SkM force): X = 0.68 at T = 10.4 MeV and P = 0.26 MeV/fm³, located at the crossing of the critical line with the drip line (μ_n = 0)
- Cold nuclear matter at T = 0 is bound only at X < 0.31
- Experimental plateau temperature T ≈ 7 MeV corresponds to pressure P = 0.01 MeV/fm³ in the isobaric boiling model (regime II)
- Caloric curves calculated for pressures: 10⁻³ MeV/fm³, 10⁻² MeV/fm³, and 10⁻¹ MeV/fm³ at fixed asymmetry X = 0.2
- Caloric curves calculated for asymmetries X = 0.1 and X = 0.3 at fixed pressure P = 0.01 MeV/fm³

## Context
This work extends previous studies of symmetric nuclear matter phase transitions (Bandyopadhyay 1990, Sauer 1976, Jaqaman 1983) and asymmetric nuclear matter thermal properties (Abd-Alla 1987, Lattimer and Ravenhall 1978, Lamb 1981, Lattimer 1985, Barranco and Buchler 1980, Mueller and Serot 1995, Lee and Mekjian 2001) by providing a systematic calculation of the full three-dimensional (P,T,X) equilibrium surface. The paper enables quantitative comparison with experimental caloric curve data (Cibor 2000) and predicts that the yield of symmetric clusters from vapor condensation should increase with excitation energy in the plateau region. The authors note that inclusion of surface and Coulomb effects (Levit and Bonche 1985) would be needed for more accurate comparisons with experiment.
