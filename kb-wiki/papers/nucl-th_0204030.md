# Instabilities of infinite matter with effective Skyrme-type interactions

> The stability of the equation of state predicted by Skyrme-type interactions is examined. We consider simultaneously symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. The stability is defined by the inequalities that the Landau parameters must satisfy simultaneously. A systematic study is carried out to define interaction parameter domains where the inequalities are fulfilled. It is found that there is always a critical density $ρ_{cr}$ beyond which the system becomes unstable. The results indicate in which parameter regions one can find effective forces to describe correctly finite nuclei and give at the same time a stable equation of state up to densities of 3-4 times the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter.

# Instabilities of infinite matter with effective Skyrme-type interactions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for any Skyrme-type interaction, there is always a critical density $\rho_{cr}$ beyond which the system becomes unstable, but that it is possible to find parameter domains where the equation of state remains stable up to densities of 3-4 times the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter, provided the empirical inputs (especially the effective mass $m^*_0/m$) are chosen appropriately.

## Method

The authors systematically analyze the stability of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter described by Skyrme-type interactions by imposing the Landau parameter inequalities ($F_l > -(2l+1)$, $G_l > -(2l+1)$, etc.) at densities up to $4\rho_0$. They fix seven of the ten Skyrme parameters using empirical nuclear matter properties (saturation density, energy per particle, effective mass, compression modulus, surface energy, and symmetry energy) and the spin-orbit strength, leaving three free parameter combinations ($x = t_1x_1$, $y = t_2x_2$, $z = t_3x_3$) to be constrained by the stability conditions. The allowed volume in $(x,y,z)$-space is mapped analytically as a function of density, and the critical density $\rho_{cr}$ is identified as the density where the allowed polygon shrinks to zero area.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, energy per particle $\epsilon_0 = -16.0$ MeV, compression modulus $K_0 = 230$ MeV, effective mass $m^*_0/m = 0.70$, surface energy $\epsilon_S = 18.0$ MeV, and symmetry energy $\epsilon_I = 32.0$ MeV are the empirical inputs used.
- The critical density $\rho_{cr}$ does not exceed $3.5-4\rho_0$ for a reasonable choice of empirical inputs.
- The sound velocity constraint gives $\rho_{sound} \approx 3.5\rho_0$ if $K_0 = 350$ MeV, and may become $6\rho_0$ if $K_0 = 250$ MeV.
- Without neutron matter constraints, $\rho_{cr}$ is above $4.5\rho_0$; adding neutron matter constraints lowers $\rho_{cr}$.
- For $z$ between 0 and $3 \times 10^4$ MeV fm$^{3(1+\sigma)}$, $\rho_{cr}$ is highest; for $z$ negative, the allowed area at $\rho = 2.5\rho_0$ is already fairly small.
- Increasing $m^*_0/m$ from 0.70 to 0.75, or $\epsilon_S$ from 18 MeV to 20 MeV, can lower $\rho_{cr}$ below $3\rho_0$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of instabilities in Skyrme interactions, particularly the ferromagnetic transition in neutron matter studied by Kutschera and Wojcik (1994) and the analysis of isospin asymmetry energy instabilities by Margueron et al. (2001). The authors build on the SLy parametrizations of Chabanat et al. (1997, 1998), which included constraints from microscopic variational calculations of Wiringa et al. (1988), but show that even these interactions can develop instabilities at high densities. The results enable the construction of new Skyrme parametrizations (the Skzn family) that are stable up to high densities, which would be useful for neutron star calculations such as neutrino mean free path or URCA process studies. The paper contrasts with Gogny-type interactions (D1, D1S, D1P), which do not predict instabilities below about $5\rho_0$ but are less amenable to algebraic analysis.
