# Moment of inertia and compactness of quark stars within the context of $f(R,T,L_m)$ gravity

> Within the metric formalism of $f(R,T,L_m)$ gravity theories, we investigate the hydrostatic equilibrium structure of compact stars taking into account both isotropic and anisotropic pressure. For this purpose, we focus on the $f(R,T,L_m)= R+ αTL_m$ model, where $α$ is a free parameter. We derive the modified TOV equations and the relativistic moment of inertia in the slowly rotating approximation. Using an equation of state (EoS) for color-superconducting quark stars, we examine the effects of the $αTL_m$ term on the different macroscopic properties of these stars. Our results reveal that the decrease of the parameter $α$ leads to a noticeable increase in the maximum-mass values. For negative $α$ with sufficiently small $\vertα\vert$, we obtain a qualitative behavior similar to the general relativistic (GR) context, namely, it is possible to obtain a critical stellar configuration such that the mass reaches its maximum. However, for sufficiently large values of $\vertα\vert$ keeping negative $α$, the critical point cannot be found on the mass-radius diagram. We also find that the inclusion of anisotropic pressure can provide masses and radii quite consistent with the current observational measurements, which opens an outstanding window onto the physics of anisotropic quark stars. By comparing the $I-C$ relations of isotropic quark stars in modified gravity, we show that such a correlation remains almost unchanged as the parameter $α$ varies from GR counterpart. On the other hand, given a fixed $α$, the $I-C$ relation is insensitive to variations of the anisotropy parameter $β$ to $\mathcal{O}(4\%)$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that for quark stars in the $f(R,T,L_m)= R+ \alpha TL_m$ gravity model, the universal $I-C$ relation (dimensionless moment of inertia vs. compactness) remains almost unchanged as the coupling parameter $\alpha$ varies from the General Relativity (GR) counterpart, but this holds only for isotropic stars; for anisotropic stars, the relation is insensitive to the anisotropy parameter $\beta$ to within $\mathcal{O}(4\%)$ for a fixed $\alpha$.

## Method
The authors derived modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations and the relativistic moment of inertia in the slowly rotating approximation for the $f(R,T,L_m)= R+ \alpha TL_m$ gravity model. They solved these equations numerically using an equation of state (EoS) for color-superconducting quark matter in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase, incorporating both isotropic and anisotropic pressure via the quasi-local anisotropy model of Horvat (2011). The macroscopic properties (mass, radius, moment of inertia) were computed for two specific EoS parameter sets (EoS I and EoS II) and analyzed as functions of the free parameters $\alpha$ (gravity coupling) and $\beta$ (anisotropy parameter).

## Key numbers
- For EoS I ($\Delta=80$ MeV, $B^{1/4}=130.6$ MeV), the minimum energy per baryon is $765$ MeV.
- For EoS II ($\Delta=180$ MeV, $B^{1/4}=161$ MeV), the minimum energy per baryon is $712$ MeV.
- The $I-C$ relation for anisotropic stars is insensitive to variations of the anisotropy parameter $\beta$ to within $\mathcal{O}(4\%)$ for a fixed $\alpha$.
- The strange quark mass $m_s$ used in the EoS is $95$ MeV, and the quartic coefficient $a_4$ is $0.65$.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of compact stars in $f(R,T,L_m)$ gravity by Mota (2024) and Tangphati (2024), which focused on mass-radius diagrams, by calculating the relativistic moment of inertia and its relation to compactness. It also generalizes the universal $I-C$ relations found in GR by Ravenhall and Pethick (1994) and refined by Lattimer (2001) and Bejger and Haensel (2002) to a modified gravity context. The results enable future observational tests of modified gravity using simultaneous mass and moment of inertia measurements of pulsars, potentially constraining the radius of a compact star.
