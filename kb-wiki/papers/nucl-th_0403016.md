# $ΛΛ$-$ΞN$-$ΣΣ$ coupling in $^{~6}_{ΛΛ}$He with the Nijmegen soft-core potentials

> The $ΛΛ$-$ΞN$-$ΣΣ$ coupling in $^{~6}_{ΛΛ}$He is studied with the [$α$ + $Λ$ + $Λ$] + [$α$ + $Ξ$ + $N$] + [$α$ + $Σ$ + $Σ$] model, where the $α$ particle is assumed as a frozen core. We use the Nijmegen soft-core potentials, NSC97e and NSC97f, for the valence baryon-baryon part, and the phenomenological potentials for the $α-B$ parts ($B$=$N$, $Λ$, $Ξ$ and $Σ$). We find that the calculated $ΔB_{ΛΛ}$ of $^{~6}_{ΛΛ}$He for NSC97e and NSC97f are, respectively, 0.6 and 0.4 MeV in the full coupled-channel calculation, the results of which are about half in comparison with the experimental data, $ΔB^{exp}_{ΛΛ}=1.01\pm0.20^{+0.18}_{-0.11}$ MeV. Characteristics of the $S=-2$ sector in the NSC97 potentials are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The calculated $\Lambda\Lambda$ bond energy $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ of $^{~6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He using the full $\Lambda\Lambda$-$\Xi N$-$\Sigma\Sigma$ coupled-channel calculation with the Nijmegen soft-core potentials NSC97e and NSC97f is about half the experimental value, but only after removing a deeply bound "pseudo bound state" from the wave function via an orthogonality condition.

## Method

The paper uses a three-body [$\alpha$+$\Lambda$+$\Lambda$] + [$\alpha$+$\Xi$+$N$] + [$\alpha$+$\Sigma$+$\Sigma$] cluster model with the $\alpha$ particle treated as a frozen $(0s)^4$ core. The valence baryon-baryon interactions are taken directly from the Nijmegen soft-core potentials NSC97e and NSC97f, while the $\alpha$-baryon potentials are obtained by folding the YNG-ND effective hyperon-nucleon interaction with the $\alpha$ density, adjusted to reproduce known binding energies of light hypernuclei. The Pauli principle between the $\alpha$ core and the valence nucleon in the $\Xi N$ channel is enforced via the orthogonality condition model, and a special exclusion operator is introduced to remove the flavor-SU(3)-$\{8s\}$-like pseudo bound state from the total wave function.

## Key numbers

- Calculated $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ of $^{~6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He: 0.61 MeV for NSC97e and 0.36 MeV for NSC97f in the full coupled-channel calculation with the pseudo-bound-state exclusion operator.
- Experimental $\Delta B^{exp}_{\Lambda\Lambda}=1.01\pm0.20^{+0.18}_{-0.11}$ MeV from the KEK-E373 (NAGARA) experiment.
- In the two-baryon system with $S=-2$ and $I=0$, a deeply bound state exists with $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}=1475$ MeV (NSC97e) and 1624 MeV (NSC97f), with channel components $P_{\Lambda\Lambda}=26.6\%$ (23.9%), $P_{\Xi N}=31.1\%$ (32.1%), $P_{\Sigma\Sigma}=42.4\%$ (44.0%).
- In the full $^{~6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He calculation with the exclusion operator, the channel components are $P_{\Lambda\Lambda}=99.77\%$ (99.81%), $P_{\Xi N}=0.21\%$ (0.18%), $P_{\Sigma\Sigma}=0.01\%$ (0.01%) for NSC97e (NSC97f).
- The $\Lambda\Lambda$ single-channel calculation (no coupling) gives $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}=0.33$ MeV (NSC97e) and 0.09 MeV (NSC97f).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei following the NAGARA event (Takahashi, 2001) by using realistic Nijmegen soft-core potentials directly rather than phenomenological $\Lambda\Lambda$ interactions. Previous calculations by Filikhin (2002-2003) using Faddeev-Yakubovsky methods and by Hiyama (2002) using phenomenological potentials reproduced the experimental $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}$ by construction, whereas the present work finds that the NSC97 potentials underpredict it by about a factor of two. The discovery of a deeply bound pseudo bound state in the NSC97 potentials, which must be removed by orthogonality, raises questions about the applicability of these potentials to $S=-2$ many-body systems and suggests that future experiments on $S=-2$ nuclei and the $H$-dibaryon state are needed to clarify the $YN$ and $YY$ interactions.
