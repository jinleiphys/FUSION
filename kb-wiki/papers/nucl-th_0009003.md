# Quantum corrections for pion correlations involving resonance decays

> A method is presented to include quantum corrections into the calculation of two-pion correlations for the case where particles originate from resonance decays. The technique uses classical information regarding the space-time points at which resonances are created. By evaluating a simple thermal model, the method is compared to semiclassical techniques that assume exponential decaying resonances moving along classical trajectories. Significant improvements are noted when the resonance widths are broad as compared to the temperature.

# Quantum corrections for pion correlations involving resonance decays

## Key claim
Quantum corrections to two-pion correlation functions from resonance decays become important when resonance widths are broad compared to the temperature, causing resonances to be produced far off-shell; semiclassical treatments assuming exponential decay along classical trajectories fail in this regime, but a modified weight prescription using the resonance creation point (rather than the decay point) can account for these quantum effects.

## Method
The authors derive quantum-mechanical expressions for two-pion correlation functions from resonance decays by integrating over all possible decay points using the resonance propagator, for scalar (A), vector (ρ), and three-pion-decay (ω) resonances. They compare these quantum results to three semiclassical prescriptions for the energy-dependent lifetime τ(m): (1) m/τ = Π(m²) motivated by the propagator form, (2) τ = 2m Im(m²−M_R²−iΠ)⁻¹ from the average emission time, and (3) a fixed lifetime 1/Γ. The comparison is performed for a thermal model at temperature 150 MeV with Monte Carlo generation of resonances.

## Key numbers
- Temperature of the thermal model: 150 MeV
- ρ meson mass: 770 MeV, width: 150 MeV
- ω meson mass: 783 MeV, width: 8.4 MeV
- For the ρ resonance, all three semiclassical prescriptions show significant deviations from the quantum result for relative momenta above 200 MeV/c
- For the ω resonance, semiclassical treatments are significantly more accurate, with prescription (1) best reproducing the quantum result for relative momenta below 200 MeV/c
- The mean propagation time for a narrow resonance is ⟨t⟩ = (Γ/2)/(ΔE² + (Γ/2)²), and the second moment ⟨t²⟩ = (1/8)(3Γ² − 4ΔE²)/(ΔE² + Γ²/4)² can become negative for far off-shell resonances

## Context
This work extends prior quantum treatments of resonance decays in correlation functions by Lednicky and Progulova (1992) and Bertsch, Danielewicz and Herrmann (1993), providing the first quantitative comparison of quantum versus semiclassical treatments. The method enables improved correlation function calculations from event-by-event simulations by using resonance creation points rather than decay points, which is particularly relevant for small sources such as pp collisions where source sizes are small enough that the reduced correlation function C′ matters; for heavy-ion collisions with large source sizes, the factor ⟨exp[i(p₁−p₂)·(x_A−x_B)]⟩ tends to zero for relative momenta above 50 MeV/c, making quantum corrections less important.
