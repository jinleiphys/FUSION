# Physics case for a polarised target for AFTER@LHC

> We review a number of ideas put forward in favour of the use of a polarised target along with the proposed idea of a fixed-target experiment using the LHC beams -- AFTER@LHC. A number of recent studies have shown that single transverse-spin asymmetries (STSAs) are large enough to be precisely measured in the region accessible with AFTER@LHC, in particular as regards the Drell-Yan process as well as single-pion, isolated-photon and jet production. AFTER@LHC with a polarised target would also be the ideal experimental set-up to measure the gluon Sivers effect via a number of original quarkonium STSA studies. We discuss first figures-of-merit based on simulations for AFTER@LHC with a polarised target.

## Key claim

Single transverse-spin asymmetries (STSAs) are expected to be large enough for precise measurement in the rapidity region accessible with AFTER@LHC, particularly for Drell-Yan, single-pion, isolated-photon, and jet production, and the setup would be ideal for measuring the gluon Sivers effect via quarkonium STSA studies, provided that a polarised target can be successfully integrated with the LHC beams.

## Method

The paper reviews two technical approaches for implementing a fixed-target experiment with a polarised target at the LHC: (1) a bent-crystal extraction technique that channels halo protons from the LHC beam onto a conventional polarised target, and (2) an internal polarised gas target using a storage cell, inspired by the HERMES experiment at DESY-HERA. The authors present luminosity projections based on simulations for both options and discuss the feasibility of measuring STSAs for various probes including Drell-Yan pairs, quarkonia (J/psi, Upsilon, eta_c, chi_c), J/psi pairs, isolated photons, single pions, and jets.

## Key numbers

- Bent-crystal option with 7 TeV proton beam on 100 cm liquid hydrogen target: instantaneous luminosity of 2000 microbarn^-1 s^-1, integrated luminosity of 20 fb^-1 per year (assuming 10^7 s running)
- Bent-crystal option with 7 TeV proton beam on 100 cm liquid deuterium target: instantaneous luminosity of 2400 microbarn^-1 s^-1, integrated luminosity of 24 fb^-1 per year
- Polarised internal gas target option with proton beam on hydrogen: areal density of 2.5 x 10^14 cm^-2, instantaneous luminosity of 900 microbarn^-1 s^-1, integrated luminosity of 9 fb^-1 per year
- Polarised internal gas target option with proton beam on deuterium: areal density of 3.2 x 10^14 cm^-2, instantaneous luminosity of 1200 microbarn^-1 s^-1, integrated luminosity of 12 fb^-1 per year
- Effective polarisation of H or D gas target can be as high as 0.8
- PHENIX collaboration measured J/psi STSA compatible with zero but allowing values as large as 20 percent
- LHCb pilot runs achieved centre-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN) = 86.6 GeV for p on Ne and 54.5 GeV for Pb on Ne

## Context

This work extends the physics case for the AFTER@LHC fixed-target proposal, building on prior studies of single transverse-spin asymmetries at RHIC and the Sivers effect first observed by Sivers (1989). The paper is complementary to the Drell-Yan COMPASS run and Fermilab experiments E1039 and E1027, which investigate the predicted sign change of the quark Sivers function between SIDIS and DY processes. The proposed measurements would enable direct constraints on the gluon Sivers function, addressing the nucleon-spin puzzle by assessing the importance of gluon orbital angular momentum, and would test factorisation-violating dynamics in QCD.
