# Scaling limit analysis of Borromean halos

> The analysis of the core recoil momentum distribution of neutron-rich isotopes of light exotic nuclei is performed within a model of the halo nuclei described by a core and two neutrons dominated by the $s-$wave channel. We adopt the renormalized three-body model with a zero-range force, that accounts for the universal Efimov physics. This model is applicable to nuclei with large two-neutron halos compared to the core size, and a neutron-core scattering length larger than the interaction range. The halo wave function in momentum space is obtained by using as inputs the two-neutron separation energy and the energies of the singlet neutron-neutron and neutron-core virtual states. Within our model, we obtain the momentum probability densities for the Borromean exotic nuclei Lithium-11 ($^{11}$Li), Berylium-14 ($^{14}$Be) and Carbon-22 ($^{22}$C). A fair reproduction of the experimental data was obtained in the case of the core recoil momentum distribution of $^{11}$Li and $^{14}$Be, without free parameters. By extending the model to $^{22}$C, the combined analysis of the core momentum distribution and matter radius suggest (i) a $^{21}$C virtual state well below 1 MeV; (ii) an overestimation of the extracted matter $^{22}$C radius; and (iii) a two-neutron separation energy between 100 and 400 keV.

# Scaling limit analysis of Borromean halos

## Key claim
The renormalized zero-range three-body model, which accounts for universal Efimov physics, can reproduce the core recoil momentum distributions of 11Li and 14Be without free parameters, but for 22C the combined analysis of momentum width and matter radius suggests the extracted matter radius may be overestimated and the two-neutron separation energy lies between 100 and 400 keV, with a preference for values below 400 keV when considering the clear presence of a narrow peak in the core recoil momentum distribution.

## Method
The authors adopt a renormalized three-body zero-range model for a neutron-neutron-core system dominated by s-wave interactions, solving the subtracted homogeneous Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosyan coupled equations for mass-imbalanced systems. The halo wave function in momentum space is obtained using as inputs the two-neutron separation energy and the energies of the singlet neutron-neutron and neutron-core virtual states. For 22C, the narrow peak in the core recoil momentum distribution is isolated by subtracting the 20C two-neutron removal cross-section from the 22C data, and the resulting difference is fitted with a Gaussian to extract the width parameter s.

## Key numbers
- For 11Li: theoretical width s_th = 19.5 MeV/c compared to experimental s_exp = 21 ± 3 MeV/c, using S_2n = 369.28 ± 0.64 keV
- For 14Be: theoretical width s_th = 37 ± 2 MeV/c compared to experimental s_exp = 39.3 ± 1.1 MeV/c, using S_2n = 1265.89 ± 132.26 keV
- For 22C: from the averaged fit of the difference between 22C and 20C removal cross-sections, the full width at half maximum is w = 79 ± 22 MeV/c, with s_av = 34 ± 9 MeV/c; after accounting for experimental resolution of s_exp = 27 MeV/c, s_av = 20 ± 15 MeV/c
- From the non-averaged fit: w = 104 ± 39 MeV/c, s = 44 ± 16 MeV/c, reduced to s = 34 ± 21 MeV/c after resolution correction
- The extracted matter radius of 22C from Tanaka et al. (2010) is sqrt(<r_m^2>) = 5.4 ± 0.9 fm
- The atomic mass evaluation (Ame2012) gives S_2n = 110 ± 60 keV for 22C; a mass measurement gives S_2n = -0.14 ± 46 keV
- The n-n virtual state energy is fixed to 143 keV

## Context
This work extends the renormalized zero-range three-body model previously applied by Yamashita et al. (2011) to constrain the 22C two-neutron separation energy from matter radius data, and by Souza et al. (2015) for universal correlations. It addresses the tension between the large matter radius of 22C extracted by Tanaka et al. (2010) and the narrow width of the core recoil momentum distribution measured by Kobayashi et al. (2012). The analysis enables constraints on the poorly known 21C virtual state energy and the 22C two-neutron separation energy, with implications for understanding halo structure at the neutron drip line. The approach follows the Efimov physics framework and the Serber model for interpreting fragmentation data, similar to analyses by Ershov et al. (2012) and Acharya et al. (2013).
