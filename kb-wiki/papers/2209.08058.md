# Beam Energy Dependence of Triton Production and Yield Ratio ($\mathrm{N}_t \times \mathrm{N}_p/\mathrm{N}_d^2$) in Au+Au Collisions at RHIC

> We report the triton ($t$) production in mid-rapidity ($|y| <$ 0.5) Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$= 7.7--200 GeV measured by the STAR experiment from the first phase of the beam energy scan at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The nuclear compound yield ratio ($\mathrm{N}_t \times \mathrm{N}_p/\mathrm{N}_d^2$), which is predicted to be sensitive to the fluctuation of local neutron density, is observed to decrease monotonically with increasing charged-particle multiplicity ($dN_{ch}/dη$) and follows a scaling behavior. The $dN_{ch}/dη$ dependence of the yield ratio is compared to calculations from coalescence and thermal models. Enhancements in the yield ratios relative to the coalescence baseline are observed in the 0\%-10\% most central collisions at 19.6 and 27 GeV, with a significance of 2.3$σ$ and 3.4$σ$, respectively, giving a combined significance of 4.1$σ$. The enhancements are not observed in peripheral collisions or model calculations without critical fluctuation, and decreases with a smaller $p_{T}$ acceptance. The physics implications of these results on the QCD phase structure and the production mechanism of light nuclei in heavy-ion collisions are discussed.

## Key claim

The compound yield ratio N_t x N_p / N_d^2 shows enhancements relative to a coalescence baseline in the 0%-10% most central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 and 27 GeV, with a combined significance of 4.1 sigma, which may be due to enhanced baryon density fluctuations induced by the QCD critical point or first-order phase transition, but the authors state that further dynamical modeling with a realistic equation of state is required to confirm this interpretation.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured triton production at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.5) in Au+Au collisions at nine beam energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV using the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) and Time-Of-Flight (TOF) detectors for particle identification. Raw triton yields were extracted from mass-squared distributions, corrected for tracking efficiency, energy loss, detector absorption, and TOF matching efficiency via embedding simulations, and then integrated over pT with Blast-Wave model extrapolations to obtain dN/dy. The yield ratio N_t x N_p / N_d^2 was constructed from the triton, proton, and deuteron yields, with protons corrected for weak decay feed-down via a data-driven approach.

## Key numbers

- The yield ratio N_t x N_p / N_d^2 shows enhancements relative to the coalescence baseline in 0%-10% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV with a significance of 2.3 sigma and at 27 GeV with a significance of 3.4 sigma, giving a combined significance of 4.1 sigma.
- The combined significance of enhancements for the full pT/A range is 4.1 sigma, decreasing to 2.5 sigma for 0.4 <= pT/A <= 1.2 GeV/c and to 1.6 sigma for 0.5 <= pT/A <= 1.0 GeV/c.
- The coalescence-inspired fit to the yield ratio versus dN_ch/deta yields best-fit parameters p0 = 0.37 +/- 0.008 and p1 = 0.75 +/- 0.04, using deuteron radius r_d = 1.96 fm and triton radius r_t = 1.59 fm.
- The thermal model overestimates the yield ratio by more than a factor of two at dN_ch/deta ~ 600 for central Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends the STAR Beam Energy Scan program's search for the QCD critical point, following prior non-monotonic observations in net-proton fluctuations (STAR 2021), baryon directed flow (STAR 2014, 2018), and pion HBT radii (STAR 2015). The yield ratio N_t x N_p / N_d^2 was predicted by Sun (2017, 2018) and Shuryak (2018, 2019) to be sensitive to neutron density fluctuations near a critical point. The results are compared to coalescence model calculations from AMPT and MUSIC+UrQMD hybrid models (Zhao 2021) which do not include critical fluctuations, and to thermal model calculations (Vovchenko 2015, 2020). The observed scaling of the yield ratio with charged-particle multiplicity provides strong evidence for nucleon coalescence as the formation mechanism for light nuclei, while the enhancements at 19.6 and 27 GeV motivate further dynamical modeling with a realistic equation of state to confirm whether they arise from critical phenomena.
