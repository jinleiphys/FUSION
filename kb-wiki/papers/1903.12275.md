# Theoretical study on η'--> 4 pions

> The $η'$ meson is associated with the U(1) anomaly. In this paper, a successful effective chiral theory of mesons has been applied to study the anomalous decay of $η'\rightarrow π^+π^-π^{+(0)}π^{-(0)}$. In this study, the contributions of triangle and box anomalies are calculated. It is shown that the contribution of box diagrams is important in this process. We predict branching ratios of $Br(η' \rightarrow π^+ π^- π^+ π^-)={1 \over 2}Br(η' \rightarrow π^+ π^- π^0 π^0) = (8.3 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-5}$, which is in good agreement with BESIII measurement.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the box anomaly (four-point quark loop) contributes significantly to the decay \(\eta' \to 4\pi\), contrary to the assumption in prior work by Guo, Kubis, and Wirzba (2012) that the triangle anomaly dominates. The authors hedge that their prediction of branching ratios agrees with BESIII measurement only when both triangle and box diagrams are included; the triangle-only contribution is too small.

## Method

The authors apply an effective chiral theory of pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons based on current algebra and large-\(N_C\) QCD, with quark loops generating meson kinetic terms and a cutoff \(\Lambda \sim 1.8\) GeV. They compute the triangle anomaly amplitude for \(\eta' \to \rho\rho \to 4\pi\) and the box anomaly amplitude for \(\eta' \to \rho\pi\pi \to 4\pi\), using the \(\rho\)-meson propagator with a momentum-dependent width. The total amplitude is the sum of both contributions, and the branching ratio for \(\eta' \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\pi^0\) is obtained via the isospin relation \(Br(\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\pi^0) = 2\,Br(\pi^+\pi^-\pi^+\pi^-)\).

## Key numbers

*   Predicted branching ratio \(Br(\eta' \to \pi^+ \pi^- \pi^+ \pi^-) = (8.3 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-5}\), in agreement with the BESIII measurement of \([8.53 \pm 0.69(\text{stat.}) \pm 0.64(\text{syst.})] \times 10^{-5}\).
*   Predicted branching ratio \(Br(\eta' \to \pi^+ \pi^- \pi^0 \pi^0) = (16.6 \pm 2.4) \times 10^{-5}\), compared to the BESIII value of \([1.82 \pm 0.35(\text{stat.}) \pm 0.18(\text{syst.})] \times 10^{-4}\).
*   Triangle-only contribution yields \(Br(\eta' \to \pi^+ \pi^- \pi^+ \pi^-) = (4.2 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-5}\), about half the total.
*   Input parameters: pion decay constant \(f_\pi/\sqrt{2} = 131.5(1.0)\) MeV, universal coupling constant \(g = 0.395(8)\), weighted average pion mass \(M_\pi = 138.4(1.2)\) MeV, and total \(\eta'\) width \(\Gamma_{\eta'} = 0.196\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the effective chiral theory of pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons developed by the authors in earlier papers (Jafari and Li, 2014–2019) to the anomalous \(\eta' \to 4\pi\) decays. It contradicts the theoretical study by Guo, Kubis, and Wirzba (2012), who used a combination of Chiral Perturbation Theory and vector meson dominance and found that the triangle anomaly dominates, predicting \(Br(\eta' \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^+\pi^-) = (1.0 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-4}\). The present result shows that the box anomaly is essential for agreement with the BESIII measurement, and it enables further tests of the effective chiral theory in other multi-pion anomalous decays.
