# The width of the omega meson in the nuclear medium

> We evaluate the width of the $ω$ meson in nuclear matter. We consider the free decay mode of the $ω$ into three pions, which is dominated by $ρπ$ decay, and replace the $ρ$ and $π$ propagators by their medium modified ones. We also take into account the quasielastic and inelastic processes induced by a vector-baryon interaction dominated by vector meson exchange, as well as the contributions coming from the $ω\to K \bar K$ mechanism with medium modified $K$, $\bar K$ propagators. We obtain a substantial increase of the $ω$ width in the medium, reaching a value of $129 \pm 10$ MeV at normal nuclear matter density for an $ω$ at rest, which comes mainly from $ωN \to ππN, ωN N \to πNN$ processes associated to the dominant $ω\to ρπ$ decay mode. The value of the width increases moderately with momentum, reaching values of around 200 MeV at 600 MeV/c.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the in-medium width of the ω meson at rest reaches 129 ± 10 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, a substantial increase from its free width of 8.49 ± 0.08 MeV, with the caveat that this result depends on the specific treatment of the ρ meson spectral function and the inclusion of interference effects from the ω → ρπ decay mechanism.

## Method

The authors evaluate the ω meson self-energy in nuclear matter by considering the free ω → 3π decay (dominated by the ω → ρπ channel) and replacing the ρ and π propagators with their medium-modified versions, including pion coupling to particle-hole and Δ-hole excitations with short-range correlations. They also compute contributions from ωN quasielastic and inelastic processes via a vector-baryon interaction described by t-channel vector meson exchange within a unitarized coupled-channel approach using local hidden gauge Lagrangians, and include the ω → K̄K mechanism with medium-modified kaon propagators. The ρ meson spectral function is treated using three different prescriptions: a phenomenological width parametrization, a model incorporating the N*(1520) resonance, and a complete coupled-channel unitary model.

## Key numbers

- Free ω meson width: Γ(0)ω = 8.49 ± 0.08 MeV, with 89.2% from the 3π decay channel
- In-medium ω width at rest at normal nuclear matter density (ρ0 = 0.17 fm⁻³): 129 ± 10 MeV
- In-medium ω width at momentum 600 MeV/c: approximately 200 MeV
- Free ρ meson width: Γρfree = 149.4 MeV
- Coupling constant G for ωρπ vertex: 11.9 ± 0.2 GeV⁻¹ from fit to vector meson radiative decays
- Free ω → ρπ width using G = 11.9 GeV⁻¹: 4.4 MeV (from literature) or 2.8 MeV (from authors' calculation without interference terms)
- Experimental ω → 3π width: 7.57 ± 0.07 MeV
- Contact term contribution needed to match free width: 3.17 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of vector meson properties in nuclear matter, particularly addressing the large in-medium ω width reported by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration (Kotulla 2008) of 130-150 MeV at normal density and the Monte Carlo analysis by Kaskulov (2006) finding a width around 100 MeV. The authors build on the local hidden gauge approach used for the K̄* meson in nuclei (Tolos 2010), which found a width increase of about 250 MeV for the K̄* at saturation density, and apply similar methods to the ω meson while also incorporating the specific ω → 3π decay channel. The results enable improved interpretation of photoproduction experiments at ELSA and KEK, and provide theoretical input for understanding whether the ω meson can form bound states in nuclei.
