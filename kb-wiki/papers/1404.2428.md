# Equation of state for neutron stars: Hyperon mixing in SU(3) flavor symmetry

> Using various kinds of relativistic mean-field models as well as the quark-meson coupling model, we study in detail the properties of neutron stars. We find that the equation of state in SU(3) flavor symmetry can support a neutron star with mass of $(1.8 \sim 2.1) M_{\odot}$ even if hyperons exist inside the core of a neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper claims that extending the coupling scheme for isoscalar vector mesons from SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry to SU(3) flavor symmetry, together with the inclusion of strange mesons (sigma* and phi), can produce neutron star equations of state that support masses in the range of 1.8 to 2.1 solar masses even when hyperons are present in the core, thereby offering a resolution to the hyperon puzzle.

## Method

The authors employed multiple relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (GM1, GM3, NL3, TM1, FSUGold, IU-FSU) as well as the quark-meson coupling (QMC) and chiral quark-meson coupling (CQMC) models, all extended to include the strange mesons sigma* and phi. They determined the meson-baryon coupling constants for the isoscalar vector mesons (omega and phi) using SU(3) flavor symmetry relations with parameters from the Nijmegen extended-soft-core model (mixing angle theta_v = 37.50 degrees and coupling ratio z = 0.1949), then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to compute neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1614-2230 has mass 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses (Demorest 2010)
- PSR J0348+0432 has mass 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses (Antoniadis 2013)
- In SU(3) symmetry, the CQMC model gives a maximum neutron star mass of 2.08 solar masses with radius 12.1 km and central density 0.90 fm^-3
- In SU(3) symmetry, the GM1 model gives a maximum neutron star mass of 2.14 solar masses with radius 12.2 km and central density 0.87 fm^-3
- In SU(3) symmetry, the TM1 model gives a maximum neutron star mass of 2.03 solar masses with radius 12.5 km and central density 0.86 fm^-3
- In SU(3) symmetry, the QMC model gives a maximum neutron star mass of 1.93 solar masses with radius 11.8 km and central density 0.96 fm^-3
- In SU(6) symmetry, the same models yield lower maximum masses: QMC 1.72, CQMC 1.84, GM1 1.86, TM1 1.72 solar masses
- The SU(3) mixing angle is 37.50 degrees and the coupling ratio z is 0.1949, from the Nijmegen extended-soft-core model (Rijken 2010)

## Context

This work addresses the hyperon puzzle, where standard mean-field equations of state become too soft to support two-solar-mass neutron stars once hyperons are included. The authors extend previous work using SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry (based on the quark model) to SU(3) flavor symmetry, following the Nijmegen extended-soft-core model of Rijken (2010). The results enable future studies of neutron star structure using more realistic hyperon-nucleon interactions and demonstrate that several popular RMF parameter sets (GM1, TM1, QMC, CQMC) can simultaneously accommodate hyperonic cores and the observed massive pulsars.
