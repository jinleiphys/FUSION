# Embedding a Critical Point in a Hadron to Quark-Gluon Crossover Equation of State

> It is shown how to embed a critical point in a smooth background equation of state so as to yield the critical exponents and critical amplitude ratios expected of a transition in the same universality class as the liquid-gas phase transition and the 3D Ising model. There are only two independent critical exponents; the relations $α+ 2β+ γ= 2$ and $β(δ- 1) = γ$ arise automatically, as does a new relation between the two critical amplitudes. The resulting equation of state has parameters which may be inferred by hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions in the Beam Energy Scan II at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

# Embedding a Critical Point in a Hadron to Quark-Gluon Crossover Equation of State

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate a method to embed a critical point into a smooth background equation of state that reproduces the critical exponents and amplitude ratios of the 3D Ising universality class, but the construction requires nine parameters total, of which only the two independent critical exponents and one amplitude ratio are universal; the numerical values of the critical temperature and chemical potential, as well as the spatial extent of the critical region, are not universal and must be determined by experiment.

## Method
The authors construct a multiplicative modification function R(T,μ) that acts on a smooth background pressure P_BG(T,μ) to produce the full equation of state P(T,μ) = P_BG(T,μ) R(T,μ). The function R is built from two functions: r(T,μ), which depends on the chemical potential μ and the coexistence curve μ_x(T), and Δ²(T), which scales as d±|T/T_c - 1|^p near the critical temperature T_c. The construction uses only two independent exponents k and p, from which the standard critical exponents α, β, γ, and δ are derived, and the coexistence curve μ_x(T) is determined by the condition that the baryon density at the coexistence curve equals the critical density n_c.

## Key numbers
- The critical point is illustrated at T_c = 100 MeV and μ_c = 750 MeV in the example coexistence curve.
- The critical exponents are derived as α = 2 - kp, β = (k-1)p, γ = (2-k)p, and δ = 1/(k-1).
- The construction automatically satisfies the scaling relations α + 2β + γ = 2 and β(δ - 1) = γ.
- A new relation between critical amplitudes is predicted: (c_+/c_-)^(2-k) = 4 (χ_-/χ_+)^k, where c_± are heat capacity amplitudes and χ_± are susceptibility amplitudes.
- The background equation of state is taken from a prior matching paper that interpolates from hadronic gas with excluded volume interactions at low energy density to an interacting quark-gluon gas at high energy density.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing problem of merging a critical equation of state with a smooth background, motivated by model calculations predicting a QCD critical point (Stephanov, CPOD proceedings) where lattice simulations (Bazavov 2020, Borsanyi 2020) are unreliable. The construction extends prior efforts to embed Ising universality class behavior into QCD equations of state, and the resulting parameterized equation of state is designed for use in hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions at the Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC, as well as for neutron star merger simulations. The predicted amplitude ratio relation agrees with published results from Guida and from Hasenbusch (multiple papers).
