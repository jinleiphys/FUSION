# The effect of the Landau--Zener transitions on nuclear fission dynamics

> In the paper, it is studied the influence of Landau-Zener transitions between nuclear many-body states on the dissipative properties of nuclear large--amplitude collective motion. Within the cranking-like approach, we describe the time evolution of a nuclear many-body system as the self-consistent motion in a space of intrinsic excitations and in a space of a single collective (deformation) parameter. By that we measure how the spectral statistics of the nuclear energy levels affects the fission rate at quite large initial temperatures of heavy nuclei.

# The effect of the Landau–Zener transitions on nuclear fission dynamics

## Key claim
The paper claims that memory effects in nuclear fission dynamics become stronger with increasing initial nuclear temperature, but this result is conditional on the weak-coupling regime of the macroscopic coordinate to the quantum system, where the perturbation parameter α ∼ (σ₀²Ω³(Δq)²)/(ΩΓ) must be much less than 1.

## Method
The authors derive a diffusion-like equation for the occupancies of adiabatic many-body states using the Zwanzig projection technique on the Liouville equation, then ensemble-average over random matrix statistics (GOE, GUE, GSE) for the coupling matrix elements and energy level spacings. Within a cranking-like approach, they obtain a generalized Langevin equation for a single collective deformation parameter q, which they solve numerically for a schematic parabolic fission barrier to compute the fission rate.

## Key numbers
- Memory times used: τ = 2 × 10⁻²³ s (small) and τ = 8 × 10⁻²³ s (large), for which the fission rate R_f is plotted as a function of initial temperature T.
- The mass parameter is taken as M = (1/5)AmR₀², where A is the mass number, m the nucleon mass, and R₀ the nuclear radius.
- The weak-coupling condition is α ∼ (σ₀²Ω³(Δq)²)/(ΩΓ) << 1.
- The correlation time is estimated as τ_cor ∼ 1/[(σ₀/√(ΩΓ))Ω·q̇].
- The ballistic regime gives var_E ≈ σ₀²q̇₀²·t² for 0 < t ≤ ħ/Γ.
- The diffusive regime gives var_E ≈ (ħσ₀²q̇₀²/Γ)·t for t ≥ ħ/Γ.

## Context
This work extends the random matrix approach to nuclear dissipation pioneered by Weidenmüller (1979) and Wilkinson (1995) by incorporating Landau–Zener transitions between many-body states into a self-consistent cranking description of fission. It enables future studies of non-Markovian effects in nuclear large-amplitude collective motion, particularly the transition from ballistic to diffusive energy transport as a function of the spreading width Γ of the coupling matrix elements. The paper does not compare directly to experimental fission data but provides a theoretical framework for interpreting temperature-dependent fission rates in heavy nuclei.
