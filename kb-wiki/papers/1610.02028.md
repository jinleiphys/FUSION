# Viscosity of a net-baryon fluid near the QCD critical point

> In the dynamics of the QCD critical point, the net-baryon fluid, linked to the slow component of the order parameter, relaxes to a 3d Ising system in equilibrium. An analytical study of shear and bulk viscosity, with constraints imposed by the dynamics of the critical net-baryon fluid, the universality property and the requirements of a class of strong coupling theories, is performed in the neighbourhood of the critical point. It is found that the shear viscosity of the net-baryon fluid is restricted in the domain $1.6\leq 4π\fracη{s}\leq 3.7$ for $T_c < T \leq 2T_c$ whereas the bulk viscosity is small, $4π\fracζ{s} < 0.05$ (for $T>1.27 T_c$) but rising towards the singularity at $T=T_c$.

## Key claim
The shear viscosity of the net-baryon fluid near the QCD critical point is restricted to the domain $1.6 \leq 4\pi \eta/s \leq 3.7$ for $T_c < T \leq 2T_c$, and the bulk viscosity is small ($4\pi \zeta/s < 0.05$ for $T > 1.27 T_c$) but rises toward the singularity at $T = T_c$, under the condition that the KSS bound is reached at the minimum of the shear viscosity ratio in the hadronic phase.

## Method
The authors performed an analytical study of shear and bulk viscosity for the net-baryon fluid near the QCD critical point, treating the baryon-number density as the slow component of the order parameter that relaxes to a 3d Ising system in equilibrium. They employed dimensional analysis to express viscosity in terms of singular thermodynamic quantities (correlation length, specific heats, sound velocity, compressibility), fixed the amplitudes of critical singularities using the ideal gas equation of state in the quark phase and universality constraints on Ising amplitude ratios, and determined the remaining dimensionless constants by imposing the KSS bound on the minimum of $\eta/s$ and a gauge/gravity-inspired parametrization for bulk viscosity.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity domain: $1.6 \leq 4\pi \eta/s \leq 3.7$ for $T_c < T \leq 2T_c$ (quark-matter phase, solution I)
- Bulk viscosity bound: $4\pi \zeta/s < 0.05$ for $T > 1.27 T_c$
- Minimum of shear viscosity reaches KSS bound: $(\eta/s)_{\rm min} = 1/(4\pi)$ at $t \simeq 0^-$ (hadronic phase)
- Average bulk viscosity in quark phase: $\langle \zeta/s \rangle_+ \simeq 0.030/(4\pi)$
- Critical exponents from dynamical consistency: $\gamma \simeq 1.34$, $\nu \simeq 0.61$
- Critical temperature and baryochemical potential used: $T_c \simeq 160$ MeV, $\mu_c \simeq 220$ MeV, $n_c \simeq 0.13$ fm$^{-3}$
- Correlation length scale: $\xi_+ \simeq 1$ fm
- Sound velocity squared average: $\langle v_s^2 \rangle \simeq 0.27$ for $0.5 \leq t \leq 1$
- Dimensionless constants: $f^{(s)} \simeq 8.2 \times 10^{-2}$, $f^{(b)} \simeq 2.0 \times 10^{-3}$
- Shear viscosity in hadronic phase: $1 \leq 4\pi \eta/s \leq 4.3$ for $T_c/2 \leq T < T_c$ (solution I)

## Context
This work extends the dynamical treatment of the QCD critical point by Minami (2010) and Fujii (2004), who identified the baryon-number density as the slow component of the order parameter, and builds on the universality class arguments of Csernai (2006) linking QCD to conventional liquid-gas systems. The results enable predictions for elliptic flow measurements of net protons at SPS (NA61 experiment) and RHIC Beam Energy Scan (STAR Collaboration), and provide a framework to test the KSS bound from AdS/CFT (Kovtun, 2005) in the context of the QCD phase diagram.
