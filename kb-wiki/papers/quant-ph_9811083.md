# Equivalence of Local and Separable Realizations of the Discontinuity-Inducing Contact Interaction and Its Perturbative Renormalizability

> We prove that the separable and local approximations of the discontinuity-inducing zero-range interaction in one-dimensional quantum mechanics are equivalent. We further show that the interaction allows the perturbative treatment through the coupling renormalization. Keywords: one-dimensional system, generalized contact interaction, renormalization, perturbative expansion. PACS Nos: 3.65.-w, 11.10.Gh, 31.15.Md

## Key claim
The paper proves that the local "epsilon" potential and the separable "prime-delta-prime" potential are equivalent representations of the discontinuity-inducing zero-range interaction in one-dimensional quantum mechanics, and demonstrates that this interaction can be treated perturbatively through coupling constant renormalization, despite the divergence of matrix elements in the zero-size limit.

## Method
The authors rederive Seba's separable "prime-delta-prime" interaction from a regularized square-well potential of width 2a and height v/(2a), then transform it into the local "epsilon" potential form using algebraic manipulations involving delta functions. They perform Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory up to second order for a particle on a ring with periodic boundary conditions, calculating energy eigenvalues for both the delta-function potential (v Δ_a) and the discontinuity-inducing potential (c_a E_a), and show that divergent terms in the latter case are cancelled by replacing the bare coupling c_a with the renormalized coupling c.

## Key numbers
- The first-order matrix element for the delta potential: ⟨n_+|Δ_a|n_+⟩ = 2/L
- The first-order matrix element for the E_a potential: ⟨n_-|E_a|n_-⟩ = -2κ_n²/L
- The second-order sum for the E_a potential diverges as L/a - 6 in the limit a → 0, where L is the system size and a is the regularization width
- The renormalized coupling expansion: c_a = 2ac/(2a + c) ≈ c - c²/(2a) + ...
- The perturbative expansion for the delta potential: k² ≈ κ² + (2/L)v - (1/(L²κ²))v² + ...
- The perturbative expansion for the discontinuity-inducing potential: k² ≈ κ² - (2κ²/L)c + (3κ²/L²)c² + ...

## Context
This work extends the mathematical theory of generalized contact interactions in one-dimensional quantum mechanics developed by Gesztesy and Kirsch (1985), Seba (1986), and Albeverio, Gesztesy, Hoegh-Krohn and Holden (1988). It establishes the equivalence between Seba's separable "prime-delta-prime" representation (1986) and the local "epsilon" potential introduced by Cheon and Shigehara (1998). The perturbative renormalization scheme enables application to many-body systems and the "one-dimensional Coulomb" problem (Loudon, 1959), and suggests that Skyrme-force-type interactions (Skyrme, 1956) in three dimensions require critical reexamination since they cannot be defined as renormalizable interactions outside one dimension.
