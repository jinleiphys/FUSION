# The entrainment matrix of a superfluid nucleon mixture at finite temperatures

> It is considered a closed system of non-linear equations for the entrainment matrix of a non-relativistic mixture of superfluid nucleons at arbitrary temperatures below the onset of neutron superfluidity, which takes into account the essential dependence of the superfluid energy gap in the nucleon spectra on the velocities of superfluid flows. It is assumed that the protons condense into the isotropic $^1$S$_0$ state, and the neutrons are paired into the spin-triplet $^3$P$_2$ state. It is derived an analytic solution to the non-linear equations for the entrainment matrix under temperatures just below the critical value for the neutron superfluidity onset. In general case of an arbitrary temperature of the superfluid mixture the non-linear equations are solved numerically and fitted by simple formulas convenient for a practical use with an arbitrary set of the Landau parameters.

## Key claim

The paper derives a closed system of non-linear equations for the entrainment matrix of a superfluid neutron-proton mixture at finite temperatures, accounting for the dependence of the superfluid energy gaps on the velocities of superfluid flows; the analytic solution is restricted to temperatures just below the critical temperature for neutron superfluidity onset, while for arbitrary temperatures the equations are solved numerically and fitted by simple formulas.

## Method

The author starts from Fermi-liquid theory and derives non-linear equations for the entrainment matrix components \(\mathsf{\tilde{\gamma}}_{ab}\) and the functions \(\tilde{\Phi}_a\), which depend on the effective velocities \(\mathbf{\tilde{V}}_a\) and the temperature-dependent energy gaps. The gap equations for spin-singlet \(^1\)S\(_0\) pairing of protons and spin-triplet \(^3\)P\(_2\) pairing of neutrons are coupled to the entrainment equations via the replacement of superfluid velocities by effective velocities. The system is solved analytically near the neutron critical temperature by expansion in small parameters, and numerically for arbitrary temperatures, with results fitted to simple formulas for practical use with any set of Landau parameters.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for \(^1\)S\(_0\) pairing of protons is about an order of magnitude higher than the critical temperature for \(^3\)P\(_2\) pairing of neutrons in the neutron star core (no explicit numerical values given for these temperatures).
* The zero-temperature energy gap for \(^1\)S\(_0\) pairing of protons is \(\Delta_0^{(p)} = \pi e^{-C} T_{cp} = 1.764\,T_{cp}\), where \(C = 0.577216\) is Euler's constant.
* The critical superfluid flow velocity for baryon species \(a\) is of order \(v_a^{\max} \sim T_{ca} / k_{Fa}\).
* The derivative of the Riemann zeta function \(\zeta'(-2) = -0.03045\) appears in the expansion for the \(^3\)P\(_2\) gap near \(T_{cn}\).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature non-linear theory of the entrainment matrix developed by Leinson (2017) and the finite-temperature linear theory of Gusakov and Haensel (2005) to finite temperatures with full inclusion of gap-velocity coupling. The results enable calculations of the entrainment matrix for warm neutron stars (temperatures near the neutron critical temperature) and for stars where pulsation energy dissipation heats the interior, which is crucial for computing bulk and shear viscosities in neutron star kinetics. The formalism is applicable to \(npe\mu\) matter and can be generalized to include hyperons.
