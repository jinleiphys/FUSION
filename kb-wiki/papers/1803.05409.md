# Thermodynamic limit in high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV

> An analysis is made of the particle composition in the final state of $pp$ collisions at 7 TeV as a function of the charged particle multiplicity ($dN_{ch}/dη$). The thermal model is used to determine the chemical freeze-out temperature as well as the radius and strangeness suppression factor $γ_s$. Three different ensembles are used in the analysis. The grand canonical ensemble, the canonical ensemble with exact strangeness conservation and the canonical ensemble with exact baryon number, strangeness and electric charge conservation. It is shown that for the highest multiplicity class the three ensembles lead to the same result. This allows us to conclude that this multiplicity class is close to the thermodynamic limit. It is estimated that the final state in $pp$ collisions could reach the thermodynamic limit when $dN_{ch}/dη$ is larger than twenty per unit of rapidity, corresponding to about 300 particles in the final state when integrated over the full rapidity interval.

## Key claim

The three statistical ensembles (grand canonical, canonical with exact strangeness, and full canonical with exact baryon/strangeness/charge) become equivalent for the highest charged-particle multiplicity class in pp collisions at 7 TeV, indicating that this multiplicity class is close to the thermodynamic limit. The authors estimate that the thermodynamic limit is reached when dNch/dη exceeds about 20 per unit of rapidity, corresponding to roughly 300 particles in the final state integrated over the full rapidity interval.

## Method

The authors used the thermal hadron resonance gas model implemented in the THERMUS program to fit measured yields of pions, kaons, protons, Lambdas, and Xi hyperons (and in some fits also Omega hyperons) from ALICE Collaboration data on pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV. They performed separate fits using three different statistical ensembles: the grand canonical ensemble (GCE), the canonical ensemble with exact strangeness conservation (SCE), and the full canonical ensemble with exact baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge conservation (FCE), extracting the chemical freeze-out temperature Tch, radius, and strangeness suppression factor γs as functions of charged-particle multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- The chemical freeze-out temperature converges to a common value around 160 MeV for the highest multiplicity class across all three ensembles.
- The thermodynamic limit is estimated to be reached when dNch/dη is larger than 20 per unit of rapidity.
- The total number of particles in the final state for the highest multiplicity class is of order 300 when integrated over the full rapidity interval.
- For the lowest multiplicity class (⟨dNch/dη⟩ = 2.89), the χ²/ndf values are 6.04/3 (SCE), 24.29/3 (FCE), and 29.05/3 (GCE) when Omega yields are included in the fits.
- For the highest multiplicity class (⟨dNch/dη⟩ = 21.29), the χ²/ndf values are 21.83/2 (SCE), 22.20/2 (FCE), and 21.55/2 (GCE) when Omega yields are excluded from the fits.

## Context

This work extends previous thermal model analyses of pp collisions at 200 GeV by Abelev (2006) and Becattini (2009, 2010), which did not study the dependence on charged multiplicity. The results show that high-multiplicity pp collisions at 7 TeV approach the thermodynamic limit where the three statistical ensembles become equivalent, enabling the use of the simpler grand canonical ensemble for such events. The analysis also demonstrates that the strangeness suppression factor γs converges to unity (full chemical equilibrium) at the highest multiplicities, and that the particle density at chemical freeze-out becomes constant, consistent with a system approaching the thermodynamic limit.
