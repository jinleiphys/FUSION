# Solving three-body scattering problem in the momentum lattice representation

> A brief description of the novel approach towards solving few-body scattering problems in a finite-dimensional functional space of the $L_2$-type is presented. The method is based on the complete few-body continuum discretization in the basis of stationary wave packets. This basis, being transformed to the momentum representation, leads to the cell-lattice-like discretization of the momentum space. So the initial scattering problem can be formulated on the multi-dimensional momentum lattice which makes it possible to reduce the solution of any scattering problem above the breakup threshold (where the integral kernels include, in general, some complicated moving singularities) to convenient simple matrix equations which can be solved on the real energy axis. The phase shifts and inelasticity parameters for the three-body $nd$ elastic scattering with MT I-III $NN$ potential both below and above the three-body breakup threshold calculated with the proposed wave-packet technique are in a very good agreement with the previous accurate benchmark calculation results.

## Key claim

The authors present a novel wave-packet discretization method that, for the first time, solves the three-body Faddeev scattering problem above the breakup threshold using only finite-dimensional \(L_2\)-type basis functions, achieving results in very good agreement with previous benchmark calculations.

## Method

The method discretizes the three-body continuum using stationary wave packets constructed as integrals of exact continuum wave functions over momentum bins (cells) for the two-body subsystem and the relative motion of the third particle. The three-body channel resolvent is projected onto this wave-packet basis, yielding a simple analytical diagonal matrix representation that avoids moving singularities. The resulting matrix Faddeev equation is solved directly on the real energy axis, and the permutation operator matrix elements are computed using the free wave-packet basis without multi-dimensional interpolations.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy \(E_b = -2.225\) MeV obtained with approximately 100 step-like functions in the free wave-packet basis (compared to 20 Gaussian functions for the same precision).
- Wave-packet basis functions coincide with exact scattering wave packets up to distances \(r \sim 1000\) fm.
- Phase shifts and inelasticity parameters for \(S\)-wave quartet and doublet \(nd\) elastic scattering with the MT I-III \(NN\) potential were calculated for basis dimensions up to \(200 \times 200\) (quartet) and \((100+100) \times 100\) (doublet).
- Results agree with benchmark calculations from refs. [benchm1] and [benchm2] both below and above the three-body breakup threshold.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier wave-packet formalism (Kukulin et al., 2004-2007), which had been tested for composite projectile scattering off heavy targets, to the full three-body Faddeev problem. It contrasts with conventional Faddeev-Yakubovsky methods (Faddeev, 1960; Yakubovsky, 1967) that require handling complicated moving singularities or time-consuming multi-dimensional interpolations, and with other \(L_2\)-type approaches (e.g., J-matrix, harmonic oscillator representation, CDCC) that were previously limited to special cases or energies below the breakup threshold. The method enables precise three-body scattering calculations above breakup using only simple matrix algebra and is promising for incorporating long-range Coulomb interactions in few-nucleon scattering.
