# Production of doubly strange hypernuclei via Ξ- doorways in the 16O(K-, K+) reaction at 1.8 GeV/c

> We examine theoretically production of doubly strange hypernuclei, 16 Ξ-C and 16 ΛΛC, in doublecharge exchange 16O(K-, K+) reactions using a distorted-wave impulse approximation. The inclusive K+ spectrum at the incident momentum pK- = 1.8 GeV/c and scattering angle θlab = 0^{\circ} is estimated in a one-step mechanism, K-p \to K+Ξ- via Ξ- doorways caused by a Ξ-p-ΛΛ coupling. The calculated spectrum in the Ξ- bound region indicates that the integrated cross sections are on the order of 7-12 nb/sr for significant 1- excited states with 14C(0+, 2+) \otimes sΛpΛ configurations in 16 ΛΛC via the doorway states of the spin-stretched 15N(1/2-, 3/2-) \otimes sΞ- in 16 Ξ-C due to a high momentum transfer qΞ- \approx 400 MeV/c. The Ξ- admixture probabilities of these states are on the order of 5-9%. However, populations of the 0+ ground state with 14C(0+) \otimes s2Λ and the 2+ excited state with 14C(2+) \otimes s2 Λ are very small. The sensitivity of the spectrum on the ΞN-ΛΛ coupling strength enables us to extract the nature of ΞN-ΛΛ dynamics in nuclei, and the nuclear (K-, K+) reaction can extend our knowledge of the S = -2 world.

## Key claim

The inclusive K+ spectrum in the 16O(K-, K+) reaction at 1.8 GeV/c and 0 degrees, calculated via a one-step mechanism through Ξ- doorways caused by a Ξ-p-ΛΛ coupling, predicts that the integrated cross sections for significant 1- excited states in 16ΛΛC are on the order of 7-12 nb/sr, but only if the Ξ- admixture probabilities of these states are on the order of 5-9%; populations of the 0+ ground state and the 2+ excited state with s2Λ configurations are very small.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations using nuclear ΛΛ-Ξ coupled-channel Green's functions to describe the one-step K-p → K+Ξ- reaction on a 16O target at an incident momentum of 1.8 GeV/c and scattering angle of 0 degrees. They employed a multichannel coupled wave function for the ΛΛ-Ξ nuclear state within a weak coupling basis, using Woods-Saxon optical potentials for the Ξ- and Λ hyperons and a zero-range interaction for the ΞN-ΛΛ coupling potential. The distorted waves for the incoming K- and outgoing K+ mesons were calculated using the eikonal approximation.

## Key numbers

- Incident momentum: pK- = 1.8 GeV/c; scattering angle: θlab = 0 degrees
- Momentum transfer: qΞ- ≈ 400 MeV/c
- Integrated cross sections for 1- excited states in 16ΛΛC: 7 nb/sr and 12 nb/sr for the 14C(0+) ⊗ sΛpΛ and 14C(2+) ⊗ sΛpΛ configurations, respectively
- Ξ- admixture probabilities: 5.2% and 8.8% for the two 1- excited states
- Energy difference between 15N+Ξ- and 14C+Λ+Λ channels: ΔM = 18.4 MeV
- For VΞ = -24 MeV: BΞ- = 13.4 MeV with Γ = 3.5 MeV for the [15N(1/2-) ⊗ sΞ-]1- state; integrated cross sections: 28 nb/sr for the 1- state and 77 nb/sr for the 2+ state in 16Ξ-C
- For VΞ = -14 MeV: BΞ- = 6.8 MeV with Γ = 3.8 MeV for the [15N(1/2-) ⊗ sΞ-]1- state; integrated cross sections: 6 nb/sr for the 1- state and 9 nb/sr for the 2+ state in 16Ξ-C
- For v0ΞN,ΛΛ = 500 MeV: ΔBΛΛ = 1.17 MeV and PΞ- = 5.24% in the [14C(0+) ⊗ sΛpΛ]1- excited state; ΔBΛΛ = 0.38 MeV and PΞ- = 0.58% in the [14C(0+) ⊗ s2Λ]0+ ground state
- For v0ΞN,ΛΛ = 250 MeV: ΔBΛΛ = 0.15 MeV and PΞ- = 0.87% in the [14C(0+) ⊗ sΛpΛ]1- excited state; ΔBΛΛ = 0.09 MeV and PΞ- = 0.14% in the [14C(0+) ⊗ s2Λ]0+ ground state
- Integrated cross section of the 0+ ground state: about 0.02 nb/sr
- Two-step mechanism estimate: 0.06-0.04 μb/sr for quasielastic ΛΛ production at 1.8 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of Ξ hypernuclei by Dover and Gal (1983), who found a Ξ-nucleus potential well depth of 24 ± 4 MeV from emulsion data, and subsequent analyses of (K-, K+) missing-mass spectra on 12C targets suggesting a Ξ well depth of 14-16 MeV (Fukuda 1998, Khaustov 2000). The paper provides the first theoretical evaluation of a production spectrum of ΛΛ-Ξ hypernuclei via the ΞN-ΛΛ coupling from the inclusive (K-, K+) reaction, and it enables future experiments at J-PARC facilities to extract quantitative information about ΞN-ΛΛ dynamics and the nature of the S = -2 world.
