# J/ψ$ Gluonic Dissociation Revisited : III. Effects of Transverse Hydrodynamic Flow

> In a recent paper [Eur. Phys. J {\bf C 44}, 567 (2005)] we developed a very general formulation to take into account explicitly the effects of hydrodynamic flow profile on the gluonic breakup of $J/ψ$'s produced in an equilibrating quark-gluon plasma. Here we apply that formulation to the case when the medium is undergoing cylindrically symmetric {\it transverse} expansion starting from RHIC or LHC initial conditions. Our algebraic and numerical estimates demonstrate that the transverse expansion causes enhancement of local gluon number density $ŋ$, affects the $\pt$-dependence of the average dissociation rate $\tilg$ through a partial-wave interference mechanism, and makes the survival probability $\spt$ to change with $\pt$ very slowly. Compared to the previous case of longitudinal expansion the new graph of $\spt$ is pushed up at LHC, but develops a rich structure at RHIC, due to a competition between the transverse catch-up time and plasma lifetime.

# J/ψ Gluonic Dissociation Revisited: III. Effects of Transverse Hydrodynamic Flow

## Key claim

The transverse hydrodynamic expansion of an equilibrating quark-gluon plasma enhances the local gluon number density and makes the J/ψ survival probability nearly pT-independent (or very slowly varying), in contrast to the previously studied longitudinal expansion case; however, at RHIC energies a rich structure emerges from the competition between the transverse catch-up time and the plasma lifetime, whereas at LHC the faster transverse cooling pushes the survival probability above the longitudinal-expansion result for all pT of interest.

## Method

The authors apply their previously developed general formulation (Patra and Menon, Eur. Phys. J C 44, 567 (2005)) for gluonic dissociation of J/ψ in a flowing medium to the specific case of cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion. They solve the ideal hydrodynamic conservation equations coupled with chemical master rate equations for gluon and quark fugacities, using a linear transverse velocity profile v = v_e r/R within a uniformly expanding cylinder. The J/ψ dissociation rate is computed from the gluon-breakup cross section in the ψ rest frame, incorporating Lorentz transformations between the fireball, medium, and ψ rest frames, and the survival probability is obtained by integrating the radioactive decay law over the production area and averaging over initial positions.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions for RHIC(1): Au+Au at √s = 200 GeV/nucleon, t_i = 0.7 fm/c, T_i = 0.55 GeV, λ_gi = 0.05, λ_qi = 0.008, R_i = 6.98 fm
- Initial conditions for LHC(1): Pb+Pb at √s = 5000 GeV/nucleon, t_i = 0.5 fm/c, T_i = 0.82 GeV, λ_gi = 0.124, λ_qi = 0.02, R_i = 7.01 fm
- Plasma freeze-out temperature: T(t_life) = 0.2 GeV
- J/ψ proper formation time: τ_F ≈ 0.89 fm/c
- At fixed fugacity λ_g, the enhancement factor γ for the gluon number density becomes 2.3 if |v| = 0.9 c
- At RHIC, the transition between regimes occurs at pT ≈ 5 GeV, where the catch-up time and plasma lifetime compete

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of J/ψ gluonic dissociation in an equilibrating quark-gluon plasma, moving from the no-flow case (Patra and Menon, 2005) and the longitudinal expansion case (Patra and Menon, Eur. Phys. J C 44, 567 (2005)) to the more realistic transverse expansion scenario. The transverse flow involves tougher kinematics because v·v_ψ ≠ 0 and a higher cooling rate compared to longitudinal expansion. The results enable future studies of asymmetric flow profiles arising from noncentral collisions at finite impact parameter, as planned by the authors. The hydrodynamic treatment follows the master rate equations approach of Biro, Munshi, and Jane (no specific year given in text) and uses the HIJING initial conditions.
