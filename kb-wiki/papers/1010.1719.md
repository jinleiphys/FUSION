# In-Medium Vector Mesons, Dileptons and Chiral Restoration

> Medium modifications of the electromagnetic spectral function in hadronic and quark-gluon matter are reviewed. A strong broadening of the $ρ$ meson, which dominates the spectral function in the low-mass regime, is quantitatively consistent with dilepton excess spectra measured in photoproduction off cold nuclei (CLAS/JLab) and in fixed-target ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (NA45,NA60/CERN-SPS). The large excess observed by PHENIX at RHIC remains unexplained to date, but is most likely not due to emission from the Quark-Gluon Plasma. Connections to thermal lattice QCD promise progress in the search for chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key claim

Medium modifications of the electromagnetic spectral function, specifically a strong broadening of the rho meson, are quantitatively consistent with dilepton excess spectra from photoproduction off cold nuclei (CLAS/JLab) and fixed-target ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (NA45, NA60/CERN-SPS), but the large excess observed by PHENIX at RHIC remains unexplained and is most likely not due to emission from the Quark-Gluon Plasma.

## Method

The paper reviews hadronic many-body calculations of the rho-meson propagator, where medium modifications are computed via self-energy insertions corresponding to dressing of its pion cloud and direct couplings to mesons and baryons in the heat bath. Coupling constants and formfactors of resonance excitations are fixed using empirical constraints from decay branchings and scattering data, including total photoabsorption cross sections on nucleons and nuclei. The resulting in-medium rho spectral functions are then applied to dilepton production experiments in photon-nucleus reactions and ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, with connections to thermal lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- The chiral quark condensate is approximately -2 fm^{-3} per light quark flavor (u, d, and maybe s), generating constituent light-quark masses of m_{u,d} ~ 350 MeV compared to bare masses of m_{u,d}^0 ~ 5-10 MeV.
- The rho meson approximately doubles (triples) its free width in cold nuclear matter at half (full) saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}).
- For Fe-Ti targets in CLAS photoproduction, an average rho width of Gamma_rho ~ (220 +/- 15) MeV was extracted without significant mass shift.
- The average density at the decay point for rho mesons in Fe-56 is about 0.4 rho_0 due to large rho 3-momenta (q ~ 1.5 GeV).
- The chi-squared per data point for CLAS spectra is 1.08 using the in-medium spectral function compared to 1.55 for the vacuum one.
- Nuclear transparency ratio predictions show nuclear absorption by 40% for A ~ 200 nuclei.
- The average in-medium rho width extracted from NA60 In-In collisions is Gamma_rho ~ 350-400 MeV, about 3 times the vacuum value.
- The fireball lifetime parameter in NA60 calculations is tau_FB ~ 6 +/- 1 fm/c.
- The ratio of the dilepton spectrum at M = 0.3 GeV to that at M = 1.5 GeV is about 100, close to the ratio of emission rates at T = 150 MeV and T = 180 MeV respectively.
- The PHENIX low-mass dilepton slope temperature is T_slope ~ 100 MeV.

## Context

This review extends earlier hadronic many-body calculations of vector-meson spectral functions (Urban 1998, Rapp 1999) and applies them to dilepton data from CLAS/JLab (Wood 2008), NA60 (Arnaldi 2006, 2008), and PHENIX (Adare 2009). The work connects to thermal lattice QCD computations (Karsch 2001, 2010) and hard-thermal-loop resummed perturbative QCD (Braaten 1990). The unexplained PHENIX excess at RHIC motivates further theoretical analysis and upcoming low-mass dilepton data from RHIC run-10. Progress in evaluating axialvector spectral functions in medium (Cabrera 2009, Roca 2010) supports a picture of chiral restoration through broadening.
