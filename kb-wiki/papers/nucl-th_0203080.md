# Radiative capture of polarized neutrons by polarized protons

> A model-independent irreducible tensor approach to p(n,gamma)d is presented and an explicit form for the spin-structure of the matrix for the reaction is obtained in terms of the Pauli spin-matrices for the neutron and the proton. Expressing the multipole amplitudes in terms of the triplet --> triplet and singlet --> triplet transitions, we point out how the initial singlet and triplet contributions to the differential cross section can be determined empirically.

# Radiative capture of polarized neutrons by polarized protons

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent irreducible tensor formalism for the reaction \(\vec{p}(\vec{n},\gamma)d\) that allows the empirical determination of the individual singlet and triplet differential cross sections at any energy through measurements of spin observables, without requiring the extreme precision (10\(^{-7}\)) needed to test effective field theory predictions.

## Method

The authors express the T-matrix for \(p(n,\gamma)d\) in terms of irreducible tensor operators built from Pauli spin matrices for the neutron and proton, and decompose the multipole amplitudes into triplet-to-triplet and singlet-to-triplet transitions. They then derive expressions for the differential cross section in terms of analyzing powers measurable with polarized beams and targets, and show how measurements with longitudinal and transverse polarizations can isolate the four channel-spin contributions (\(\text{d}\sigma_{1,1}\), \(\text{d}\sigma_{1,0}\), \(\text{d}\sigma_{1,-1}\), and \(\text{d}\sigma_{0,0}\)).

## Key numbers

- The measured \(\gamma\)-anisotropy from Muller et al. is \(\eta = (1.0 \pm 2.5) \times 10^{-4}\) at 50.5% polarization of both neutrons and protons in \(\vec{p}(\vec{n},\gamma)d\); this value cannot test effective field theory predictions at the \(10^{-7}\) level.
- The unpolarized differential cross section formula includes the factor \(\left(\frac{k}{2\pi E}\right)^2 \frac{E_n E_p E_d}{p}\) where \(E_n, E_p, E_d\) are c.m. energies of the neutron, proton, and deuteron respectively.
- The \(M1\) transition from S-wave capture and the \(E1\) transition from P-wave capture are found to be comparable at a neutron energy of 550 keV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier "polarized-target-beam test" proposed by Breit and Rustgi for thermal \(np\) capture, which focused on the isoscalar \(M1\) and \(E2\) amplitudes from the \(^3S_1\) state. The formalism enables experimental determination of the three triplet and one singlet differential cross sections individually, rather than just estimating the relative importance of triplet versus singlet amplitudes. The authors note that the recent measurement by Muller et al. (2002) provides the first experimental value for a spin-dependent observable in \(\vec{p}(\vec{n},\gamma)d\), and they propose extending that experiment to measure the observables in their Eqs. (d_z) through (d_y) to study the fusion reaction more incisively. The paper also connects to the problem of off-shell \(NN\) interaction uncertainties, noting that modern high-precision potentials (Nijmegen1, Nijmegen2, Argonne, Bonn) fit elastic scattering data with \(\chi^2/\text{datum} \approx 1\) but leave off-shell behavior unconstrained, and that \(np \to d\gamma\) necessarily involves off-shell \(NN\) interactions.
