# Unveiling Light-Quark Yukawa Flavor Structure via Dihadron Fragmentation at Lepton Colliders

> Directly probing light-quark Yukawa couplings and their flavor structure remains a major challenge due to their smallness and overwhelming QCD backgrounds. In this Letter, we propose a theoretical framework to access these couplings at lepton colliders through transverse spin dependent azimuthal modulations in dihadron fragmentation. These modulations arise from the interference between Higgs mediated and Standard Model amplitudes in $e^-e^+\to q\bar{q}Z$, producing angular structures that are linearly sensitive to the Yukawa couplings $y_q$, in contrast to conventional observables that scale as $y_q^2$. By combining channels with an identified accompanying single hadron, $h^\prime=π^\pm,K^\pm$, and $p/\bar{p}$, this approach cleanly disentangles the up- and down-quark Yukawa contributions, yielding typical limits at the $\mathcal{O}(10^{-4})$ level and establishing fragmentation dynamics as a novel and complementary probe of the Higgs flavor structure.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that transverse spin dependent azimuthal modulations in dihadron fragmentation at lepton colliders can probe light-quark Yukawa couplings with linear sensitivity (scaling as \(y_q\) rather than \(y_q^2\)), but the actual reach is conditional on combining multiple identified hadron channels (\(\pi^\pm, K^\pm, p/\bar{p}\)) and assumes \(CP\)-conserving interactions, with the quoted \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-4}\sim 10^{-3})\) limits representing 68% confidence level projections under those specific conditions.

## Method

The authors analyze the process \(e^-e^+\to q\bar{q}Z\) at \(\sqrt{s}=250\) GeV, where the Higgs-mediated amplitude interferes with the Standard Model continuum background, inducing a single helicity flip of the light quark that produces transverse spin polarization. This transverse spin is accessed through dihadron fragmentation of one quark into a \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pair, while the recoiling antiquark fragments into a single identified hadron \(h^\prime = \pi^\pm, K^\pm, \text{ or } p/\bar{p}\). The resulting azimuthal modulation in the dihadron relative momentum angle \(\phi_R\) is linearly proportional to the Yukawa couplings \(y_q\), and the flavor identity of the tagging hadron \(h^\prime\) allows separation of up- and down-quark contributions via the different combinations of fragmentation functions \(\langle D^{h^\prime}_{\bar{q}} \pm D^{h^\prime}_{q}\rangle\).

## Key numbers

* Projected 68% C.L. limits on light-quark Yukawa couplings reach the \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-4}\sim 10^{-3})\) level, combining \(\pi^\pm\), \(K^\pm\), and \(p/\bar{p}\) channels at a lepton collider with \(\sqrt{s}=250\) GeV and integrated luminosity \(\mathcal{L}=50\) ab\(^{-1}\).
* The analysis uses kinematic cuts: \(z \in (0.25, 0.9)\), \(\bar{z} \in (0.1, 0.9)\), and dihadron invariant mass \(M_h \in (0.3, 2.0)\) GeV, with the reconstructed \(Z\)-boson energy selected in the window \((109.2, 111.2)\) GeV.
* Current indirect limits from LHC data are \(|y_u|<0.030\) and \(|y_d|<0.026\) (CMS 2023, 2025), with projected HL-LHC sensitivity of \(|y_{u,d}|<0.007\) (de Blas 2019).

## Context

This work extends the approach of using transverse spin effects for new physics searches (Zhou 2011, Boughezal 2023, Wen 2024) and directly addresses the challenge of disentangling up- and down-quark Yukawa couplings, which previous indirect constraints from Higgs production rates and global effective field theory analyses (Bishara 2016, Soreq 2016, Falkowski 2020) could not resolve due to flavor degeneracy. The method contrasts with the quark Boer-Mulders fracture function approach proposed by Michel (2025) for \(pp\) collisions, avoiding pileup and detector acceptance issues at the LHC. The results enable future lepton colliders such as CEPC, ILC, and FCC-ee to probe the flavor structure of the Higgs Yukawa sector at the \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-4})\) level, complementing rate-based measurements that scale as \(y_q^2\).
