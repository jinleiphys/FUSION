# Tetra-quark mesons with exotic quantum numbers

> Tetra-quark mesons with exotic quantum numbers, their production rates and decay properties are studied, because they are useful to establish existence of tetra-quark mesons.

## Key claim

Tetra-quark mesons with exotic quantum numbers (such as charm C = -strangeness S = +1 for scalars, or C = 2 for axial-vectors) should be observable in B decays with branching fractions of order 10^{-4} to 10^{-3}, provided that the same quark-line diagram topologies that describe the production of the already-observed tetra-quark candidates D_{s0}^{+}(2317) and X(3872) also govern the production of these exotic states.

## Method

The author classifies tetra-quark mesons by the symmetry of their flavor wavefunctions under exchange of quarks and antiquarks, retaining only the color configuration (3-bar_c × 3_c) that gives the lowest-lying states. Masses are estimated via a simple quark counting using m_c - m_s ≈ 1.0 GeV and m_s - m_n ≈ 0.1 GeV. Production rates in B decays are estimated by comparing the quark-line diagrams for the exotic mesons with those for the known D_{s0}^{+}(2317) and X(3872), assuming that amplitudes of the same diagram type have the same order of magnitude and that decays are dominantly S-wave.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on the radiative decay ratio of D_{s0}^{+}(2317): Γ(D_{s0}^{+}(2317) → D_{s}^{*+}γ) / Γ(D_{s0}^{+}(2317) → D_{s}^{+}π^{0})|_{CLEO} < 0.059.
- Measured branching fraction for B → D̃ D_{s0}^{+}(2317)[D_{s}^{+}π^{0}]: (8.5^{+2.1}_{-1.9} ± 2.6) × 10^{-4}.
- Measured branching fraction for B → D̃ D_{s0}^{+}(2317)[D_{s}^{*+}γ]: (2.5^{+2.0}_{-1.8} (< 7.5)) × 10^{-4}.
- Ratio of X(3872) decays to π^{+}π^{-}π^{0}ψ vs. π^{+}π^{-}ψ: 1.0 ± 0.4 ± 0.3.
- Radiative decay ratio R^{γ} = Br(X(3872)→γψ)/Br(X(3872)→π^{+}π^{-}ψ): Belle value 0.14 ± 0.05, Babar value 0.33 ± 0.12.
- Estimated branching fractions for B decays producing exotic tetra-quark mesons K_{Acc}^{+} and K_{Acc}^{++}: Br(B_{c}^{+} → D^{*-}K_{Acc}^{++}) ∼ Br(B_{c}^{+} → D̄^{*0}K_{Acc}^{+}) ∼ 10^{-(4-3)}.
- Estimated branching fractions for B decays producing scalar and axial-vector mesons with C = -S = +1: ∼ 10^{-(4-3)}.
- Measured branching fraction for B̄_{d}^{0} → K^{-}D_{s0}^{+}(2317) times D_{s0}^{+}(2317) → D_{s}^{+}π^{0}: (5.3^{+1.5}_{-1.3} ± 0.7 ± 1.4) × 10^{-5}.
- Estimated mass of scalar exotic meson Ê^{0}: 2.32 GeV.
- Estimated mass of axial-vector exotic meson H_{Acc}^{+}: 3.87 GeV.
- Estimated mass of axial-vector exotic mesons K_{Acc}: 3.97 GeV.
- Estimated mass of axial-vector exotic mesons E_{A(cs)} and E_{A[cs]}: 2.97 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the tetra-quark interpretation previously applied to D_{s0}^{+}(2317) and X(3872) by Terasaki and collaborators (Terasaki, 2003-2009) to predict new mesons with genuinely exotic quantum numbers. It contrasts with charmonium interpretations of X(3872) (which would predict a much larger radiative decay ratio) and with loosely bound D^{0}D̄^{*0} molecule models (which face problems with prompt production cross sections). The paper enables experimental searches in B decays for these exotic states, noting that observation of the opposite C-parity partner of X(3872) in the ψπ^{0}π^{0} channel would further confirm the tetra-quark picture.
