# S-Wave Pion-Pion Correlations in Dense Nuclear Matter

> The s-wave correlated $π$-$π$ strength distribution in cold nuclear matter is investigated. The in-medium single pions are renormalized by means of the usual p-wave couplings to nucleons and $Δ$'s. Two groups of models for the $π$-$π$ interaction are studied. The first one uses phenomenological models which reproduce the phase shifts but do not respect the soft-pion theorems. With increasing density a strong accumulation of strength in the subhreshold region is observed. Slightly above saturation density there even occurs an instability with respect to s-wave $π$-$π$ pair condensation. These effects are strongly moderated or disappear altogether if constraints from chiral symmetry are built into the $π$-$π$ interaction.

## Key claim

The authors find that phenomenological π-π interaction models which reproduce vacuum phase shifts but do not respect chiral symmetry constraints predict a strong accumulation of subthreshold strength and even an s-wave π-π pair condensation instability at densities slightly above saturation density (≈1.3 ρ₀); however, when constraints from chiral symmetry are built into the π-π interaction, these effects are strongly moderated or disappear altogether, suggesting that such a pairing instability does not exist in dense nuclear matter.

## Method

The in-medium single-pion propagator was renormalized by including p-wave couplings to nucleons and Δ-isobars, with explicit treatment of the particle-hole continuum, Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, the in-medium Δ-decay width, and 2p-2h absorptive effects. The s-wave correlated π-π strength distribution was then computed using two phenomenological models (the separable Johnstone-Lee model and the meson-exchange Jülich model) that reproduce vacuum phase shifts but violate soft-pion theorems, and compared with results from three chirally symmetric models: the linear sigma model, the Weinberg Lagrangian (gauged non-linear sigma model), and a modified version of the Jülich model with chiral constraints imposed via a once-subtracted dispersion relation for the two-pion propagator.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for s-wave π-π pair condensation in the Johnstone-Lee model: slightly above 1.3 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ is nuclear saturation density)
- Threshold for single-pion condensation in the same model: 3.6 ρ₀
- Critical density for pairing in the Jülich model: ≈1.3 ρ₀ (slightly lower than in the Johnstone-Lee model)
- Bare σ-mass in the Johnstone-Lee model: M_σ = 2.2 GeV
- σ-mass in the linear sigma model: m_σ = 1.0 GeV
- Scattering length in the linear sigma model: a⁰⁰ = 0.23 m_π⁻¹
- Scattering length from the once-subtracted Weinberg Lagrangian: a⁰⁰ = 0.20 m_π⁻¹, a⁰² = -0.031 m_π⁻¹
- Cutoff at πNN and πNΔ vertices: 1.2 GeV/c
- Migdal parameters used: g'_NN = 0.8, g'_NΔ = g'_ΔΔ = 0.5

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Schuck et al. (1988) and Chanfray et al. (1991) that first showed strong medium effects on π-π correlations in the scalar-isoscalar channel, and by Aouissat et al. (1993) that found a possible π-π pair condensation instability. The present paper confirms the instability using a different phenomenological model (the Jülich model of Lohse et al.) but then demonstrates that imposing chiral symmetry constraints, via the linear sigma model, the Weinberg Lagrangian, or a chirally modified Jülich model, eliminates the instability. This resolves a tension with soft-pion theorems (Weinberg, 1966, 1967) and suggests that previous predictions of s-wave π-π pair condensation were artifacts of models that do not respect chiral symmetry. The results imply that the nuclear equation of state is not affected by a long-range scalar interaction from near-threshold π-π correlations, and they enable more reliable calculations of two-pion exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction in medium.
