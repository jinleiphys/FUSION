# Multi-Body Entanglement and Information Rearrangement in Nuclear Many-Body Systems

> We examine how effective-model-space (EMS) calculations of nuclear many-body systems rearrange and converge multi-particle entanglement. The generalized Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model is used to motivate and provide insight for future developments of entanglement-driven descriptions of nuclei. The effective approach is based on a truncation of the Hilbert space together with a variational rotation of the qubits (spins), which constitute the relevant elementary degrees of freedom. The non-commutivity of the rotation and truncation allows for an exponential improvement of the energy convergence throughout much of the model space. Our analysis examines measures of correlations and entanglement, and quantifies their convergence with increasing cut-off. We focus on one- and two-spin entanglement entropies, mutual information, and $n$-tangles for $n=2,4$ to estimate multi-body entanglement. The effective description strongly suppresses entropies and mutual information of the rotated spins, while being able to recover the exact results to a large extent with low cut-offs. Naive truncations of the bare Hamiltonian, on the other hand, artificially underestimate these measures. The $n$-tangles in the present model provide a basis-independent measures of $n$-particle entanglement. While these are more difficult to capture with the EMS description, the improvement in convergence, compared to truncations of the bare Hamiltonian, is significantly more dramatic. We conclude that the low-energy EMS techniques, that successfully provide predictive capabilities for low-lying observables in many-body systems, exhibit analogous efficacy for quantum correlations and multi-body entanglement in the LMG model, motivating future studies in nuclear many-body systems and effective field theories relevant to high-energy physics and nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that effective-model-space (EMS) techniques, which successfully accelerate energy convergence in nuclear many-body calculations, also exhibit analogous efficacy for quantum correlations and multi-body entanglement in the generalized Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model, but the improvement for multi-body entanglement measures (n-tangles) is significantly more dramatic than for energy convergence, while the effective description strongly suppresses entropies and mutual information of the rotated spins.

## Method

The authors apply an EMS approach to the generalized LMG model by truncating the Hilbert space with a cut-off on the number of particles in the upper level (spins up) and performing a variational rotation of the qubits (spins) around the y-axis by an angle β, which defines an effective Hamiltonian for the truncated space. They compute one- and two-spin entanglement entropies, mutual information, and n-tangles for n=2 and n=4 to quantify multi-body entanglement, comparing results from the effective Hamiltonian with those from naive truncations of the bare Hamiltonian (without variational rotation). The n-tangles are found to be basis-independent in the LMG model, providing a stringent test of the many-body method.

## Key numbers

- For a system of N=30 particles with interaction strength v̄_x=2.0 and χ=-1 (W=0), the effective Hamiltonian with variational rotation provides exponential improvement in energy convergence with increasing cut-off Λ compared to naive truncation, as previously reported in Robin (2023).
- At χ=1/4 with v̄_x=2.0, the Λ=1 solution reproduces the exact energy with an accuracy of approximately 10⁻⁵, corresponding to the Hartree-Fock mean-field solution at v̄_x=1/√χ.
- For the isotropic case χ=1 (V=0) with v̄_x=2.0, the exact ground state is a single |N₊⟩ state with N₊=8, and the effective Hamiltonian provides constant improvement of about one order of magnitude in energy for low Λ values.
- At χ=1/2 with v̄_x=√2, the ground-state wavefunction is exactly the Hartree-Fock solution, with optimal β approximately 3/4.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of entanglement rearrangement in nuclear systems via basis transformations (Robin 2020), where unitary transformations of the effective Hamiltonian were shown to minimize and move entanglement into the effective model space for light Helium nuclei. The present study uses the LMG model as a "sandbox" to gain insight for future applications to realistic nuclear many-body systems and effective field theories, building on prior quantum simulations using the HL-VQE algorithm (Robin 2023) that demonstrated exponential improvement in ground-state energy convergence. The analysis of n-tangles up to n=6 provides new analytical expressions not previously studied in the LMG model (except for n=2), and the results motivate future studies of entanglement-driven descriptions of nuclei and quantum simulations on NISQ devices.
