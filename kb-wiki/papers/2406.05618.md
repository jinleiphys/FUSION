# Photon induced proton and anti-proton pair production with ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC

> We investigate proton-antiproton ($p\bar{p}$) pair production via photon-photon fusion in the ultra-peripheral collisions at RHIC, employing a joint impact parameter and transverse momentum dependent formalism. We consider proton exchange, $s$-channel resonance and hand-bag mechanisms, predicting differential distributions of $p\bar p$ production. Our theoretical predictions can be tested against future measurements at RHIC, to enhance our understanding of photon-photon interactions in strong electromagnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that at RHIC energies (200 GeV), the dominant mechanism for proton-antiproton pair production via photon-photon fusion in ultraperipheral collisions is the $f_2(1950)$ resonance, with the hand-bag mechanism contributing negligibly, contrary to its importance at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors employ a joint impact parameter and transverse momentum dependent formalism for the equivalent photon approximation, incorporating the spatial configuration of the colliding nuclei and the transverse momentum distributions of the virtual photons. They calculate the cross section and differential distributions by including three production mechanisms: proton exchange via the proton electromagnetic vertex with off-shell form factors, $s$-channel resonance exchange through the $f_2(1950)$ meson, and the hand-bag mechanism factorized into a hard $\gamma\gamma\to q\bar q$ part and a non-perturbative $q\bar q\to p\bar p$ form factor.

## Key numbers

* The total transverse momentum distribution of $p\bar p$ pairs peaks at approximately 30 MeV, reflecting the coherent nature of the photons with nuclear radius $R$.
* The $f_2(1950)$ resonance parameters used are: mass $m_{f_2}=1944$ MeV, width $\Gamma_{f_2}=473$ MeV, with coupling product $a_{f_2\gamma\gamma}\,g_{f_2 p\bar p}=13.05\,\alpha_e$ GeV$^{-4}$ and $b_{f_2\gamma\gamma}\,g_{f_2 p\bar p}=0$.
* The proton exchange off-shell form factor uses $\Lambda_p=1.08$ GeV, obtained from fitting experimental data in $e^+e^-$ collisions.
* Kinematic cuts applied: $P_\perp > 0.2$ GeV, $|y_{1,2}|<0.5$, and invariant mass $2.1$ GeV $< M < 2.6$ GeV at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Klusek-Gawenda (2017) from electron-positron colliders to RHIC ultraperipheral collisions, improving upon their equivalent photon approximation by incorporating joint impact parameter and transverse momentum dependence that was missing in earlier approaches. The predictions can be tested against future RHIC measurements, enabling validation of the different production mechanisms (proton exchange, $s$-channel resonance, hand-bag) and enhancing understanding of photon-photon interactions in strong electromagnetic fields.
