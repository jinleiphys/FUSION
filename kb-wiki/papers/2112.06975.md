# Pursuing the Precision Study for Color Glass Condensate in Forward Hadron Productions

> With the tremendous accomplishments of RHIC and the LHC experiments and the advent of the future Electron-Ion Collider on the horizon, the quest for compelling evidence of the color glass condensate (CGC) has become one of the most aspiring goals in the high energy Quantum Chromodynamics research. Pursuing this question requires developing the precision test of the CGC formalism. By systematically implementing the threshold resummation, we significantly improve the stability of the next-to-leading-order calculation in CGC for forward rapidity hadron productions in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions, especially in the high $p_T$ region, and obtain reliable descriptions of all existing data measured at RHIC and the LHC across all $p_T$ regions. Consequently, this technique can pave the way for the precision studies of the CGC next-to-leading-order predictions by confronting them with a large amount of precise data.

## Key claim

By systematically implementing threshold resummation, the authors significantly improve the stability of the next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculation in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism for forward rapidity hadron productions in pp and pA collisions, especially in the high pT region, and obtain reliable descriptions of all existing data from RHIC and the LHC across all pT regions. The hedge is that the resummation technique is one of several feasible solutions to the issue of large negative NLO corrections, and the scheme is not unique.

## Method

The authors start from the full NLO cross-section for forward hadron production in the CGC framework, which includes contributions from one-loop diagrams and kinematic constraint corrections. They transform the NLO corrections from coordinate space to momentum space to improve numerical accuracy, then identify and extract large threshold logarithms (both single and double logs) that cause the perturbative expansion to break down in the high pT region. These logarithms are resummed by separating them into a soft part (resummed via a Sudakov factor) and a collinear part (resummed by resetting the factorization scale in the leading-order term via DGLAP evolution), yielding a resummed formula that depends on both the factorization scale μ and an auxiliary semi-hard scale Λ.

## Key numbers

- The auxiliary scale Λ² is set by the prescription Λ² ≈ max{ Λ_QCD² [(1-ξ)k_⊥²/Λ_QCD²]^{C_R/(C_R+N_c β_0)}, Q_s² }, where β_0 = 11/12 - n_f/(6 N_c) and C_R is the Casimir factor (C_F for quark channel, C_A for gluon channel).
- The factorization scale μ is varied as μ² = α²(μ_min² + p_T²) with α = 2 to 4, and a minimum value μ_min = 2 GeV is used in the low pT region.
- The LHCb data used for comparison are at √s_NN = 5 TeV in five rapidity ranges around y = 2.25, 2.75, 3.25, 3.75, and 4.2.
- For pp collisions, the transverse area is chosen as S_⊥^{pp} = 2π R_p² with R_p the proton radius.

## Context

This work extends the NLO CGC calculations for forward hadron production that were previously developed by Chirilli, Xiao, and Yuan (2011, 2012) and by Watanabe, Xiao, Yuan, and Zaslavsky (2015), which suffered from large negative NLO corrections in the high pT region. The threshold resummation technique follows the approach first proposed by Xiao, Yuan, and Zaslavsky (2018) and is similar in spirit to the Collins-Soper-Sterman formalism used in collinear factorization. This enables precision studies of the CGC formalism by confronting NLO predictions with a large amount of precise data from RHIC, the LHC, and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
