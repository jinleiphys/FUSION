# Factorization of Soft and Collinear Divergences in Non-Equilibrium Quantum Field Theory

> Proof of factorization of soft and collinear divergences in non-equilibrium QCD may be necessary to study hadronic signatures of quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC. In this paper we prove factorization of soft and collinear divergences in non-equilibrium QED by using Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path integral formalism in the background field method in pure gauge.

## Key claim

The paper proves factorization of soft and collinear divergences in non-equilibrium QED using the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path integral formalism in the background field method, but only for the case where the background field is a pure gauge. The author states that the proof of factorization in non-equilibrium QCD (which would be needed for RHIC and LHC phenomenology) is "addressed elsewhere."

## Method

The author extends the standard vacuum QED proof of factorization (which uses Wilson lines and background field methods) to non-equilibrium QED by employing the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time path (CTP) formalism. The generating functional is written with two time branches ("+" and "-"), each with its own fields and sources, and an initial density matrix ρ describing the non-equilibrium state. By performing a gauge transformation of the fermion fields and sources on each branch, the author shows that the correlation function in the presence of a pure-gauge background field Aμ(x) = ∂μ ω(x) factorizes into a product of exponential phase factors (Wilson lines) times the correlation function without the background field.

## Key numbers

* The non-equilibrium distribution function is written as f(k) with the equilibrium special case f(k) = 1/(e^(k0/T) ± 1) (no numerical value given for T).
* The initial time for the non-equilibrium medium is taken as t_in = 0 (no numerical value).
* The Wilson line phase factor is given by ω(x) = (1/(n·∂)) n·A(x) for a lightlike vector nμ = (1,0,0) or nμ = (0,1,0) (no numerical values).
* The paper contains no other explicit numerical results.

## Context

This work extends the vacuum QED factorization proof of Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1980s) to non-equilibrium systems, using the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism. It is a necessary step toward proving factorization in non-equilibrium QCD, which the author states will be needed to study hadron production from quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC using the non-equilibrium fragmentation function derived in Nayak (2009). The paper explicitly notes that the finite-temperature equilibrium case is a special case of the non-equilibrium formalism when f(k) = 1/(e^(k0/T) ± 1).
