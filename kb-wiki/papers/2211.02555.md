# Near-threshold resonances in 11C and the 10B(p,α)7Be aneutronic reaction cross section

> The nucleus 11C plays an important role in the boron-proton fusion reactor environment as a catalyzer of the 10B(p,α)7Be reaction which, by producing a long-lived isotope of 7Be, poisons the aneutronic fusion process 11B(p,2α)4He. The low-energy cross section of 10B(p,α)7Be depends on the near-threshold states 7/2+1 , 5/2+2 , 5/2+3 in 11C whose properties are primarily known from the indirect measurements. We investigate the continuum-coupling induced collectivization of these resonances in the shell model embedded in the continuum. We predict a significant enhancement of the 10B(p,α)7Be cross section at energies accessible to the laser-driven hot plasma facilities.

## Key claim

The low-energy cross section of the aneutronic fusion poison reaction 10B(p,α)7Be is predicted to be significantly enhanced by the presence of near-threshold resonances in 11C, particularly the 5/2+2 state, due to continuum-coupling-induced collectivization; this enhancement occurs at energies accessible to laser-driven hot plasma facilities, even though the resonance energies do not correspond to the maximum of the continuum coupling.

## Method

The authors used the shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC) to study the 7/2+1, 5/2+2, and 5/2+3 states in 11C near the proton emission threshold. The Hilbert space was divided into subspaces with 0 and 1 particle in the scattering continuum, and the energy-dependent effective Hamiltonian included couplings to one-proton decay channels [10B(3+) ⊗ p(s1/2)], [10B(3+) ⊗ p(d5/2)], and [10B(3+) ⊗ p(d3/2)]. Four calculation variants were performed, varying the monopole shifts in the WBP- Hamiltonian and the continuum-coupling strength V0, to compute the continuum-coupling correlation energy and spectroscopic factors for each state.

## Key numbers

* The 7/2+1 state in 11C is bound by 40 keV with respect to the proton-decay threshold.
* The 5/2+2 resonance is unbound by 10 keV, and the 5/2+3 resonance is unbound by 510 keV.
* The SMEC variant k=3 used a continuum coupling constant V0 = -430 MeV·fm3; variant k=4 used V0 = -150 MeV·fm3.
* The proton s1/2 spectroscopic factor for the 5/2+2 state increases from 0.29 (SM, k=1) to 0.38 (SMEC, k=3), and from 0.32 (SM, k=2) to 0.33 (SMEC, k=4).
* The proton d5/2 spectroscopic factor for the 7/2+1 state is 0.36 (SM, k=1) and 0.38 (SMEC, k=4).
* The continuum-coupling correlation energy for the 5/2+3 resonance is reduced by almost one order of magnitude compared to the 5/2+2 resonance.

## Context

This work extends the SMEC analysis previously applied to the mirror nucleus 11B (Okolowicz 2020, Okolowicz 2022) to 11C, where the near-threshold resonances influence the 10B(p,α)7Be reaction that poisons the aneutronic 11B(p,2α)4He fusion process. The study addresses the large model-dependent uncertainties in indirect Trojan Horse method measurements (Spitaleri 2014, Spitaleri 2017) and the inconsistent R-matrix extrapolations (Wiescher 2017) by providing a consistent theoretical framework. The predicted enhancement of the cross section at energies accessible to facilities such as the National Ignition Facility and OMEGA EP enables improved modeling of boron-proton fusion plasmas.
