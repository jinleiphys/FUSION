# Rho-meson resonance broadening in QCD at finite temperature

> Thermal Finite Energy QCD sum rules for the vector current correlator are used to study quark-gluon deconfinement. Assuming $ρ$-meson saturation of the correlator in the hadronic sector, and the Operator Product Expansion in QCD, we obtain the temperature behavior of the resonance parameters (coupling, mass, and width), and of the leading vacuum condensates, as well as the perturbative QCD threshold in the complex squared energy plane. The results are consistent with quark-gluon deconfinement at a critical temperature $T_c \simeq 197 MeV$. The temperature dependence of the $ρ$-meson width is of importance for current experiments on dimuon production in nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

Using thermal Finite Energy QCD sum rules (FESR) for the vector current correlator, the authors find that the ρ-meson width diverges at a critical temperature \(T_c \simeq 197\ \mathrm{MeV}\), signaling quark-gluon deconfinement. This result depends on the assumption of ρ-meson saturation of the hadronic spectral function and a scanning of the six-dimensional parameter space guided by expectations from other hadronic channels.

## Method

The authors employed the first three thermal Finite Energy QCD sum rules (FESR) for the vector current correlator, using the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) in the QCD sector at leading one-loop order and a Breit-Wigner parameterization for the ρ-meson in the hadronic sector. They solved the FESR simultaneously for the temperature-dependent perturbative QCD threshold \(s_0(T)\), the ρ-meson coupling, mass, and width, and the dimension \(d=4\) gluon condensate and \(d=6\) four-quark condensate, using lattice QCD results for the quark condensate as a first approximation and scanning the parameter space for physically sensible solutions.

## Key numbers

- Critical deconfinement temperature: \(T_c = 197\ \mathrm{MeV}\), at which \(s_0(T)\), \(f_\rho(T)\), and the gluon condensate vanish and the ρ-meson width diverges.
- Temperature for vanishing of the four-quark condensate: \(T_q^* = 187\ \mathrm{MeV}\), approximately 5% lower than \(T_c\).
- ρ-meson width follows \(\Gamma_\rho(T) = \Gamma_\rho(0) / [1 - (T/T_c)^a]\) with \(a = 3\) and \(\Gamma_\rho(0) = 0.145\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- ρ-meson mass follows \(M_\rho(T) = M_\rho(0)[1 - (T/T_M^*)^c]\) with \(c = 10\) and \(T_M^* = 222\ \mathrm{MeV}\), constrained to satisfy \(T_M^* > T_c\).
- At zero temperature, the FESR solution gives \(s_0 = 1.44\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), \(C_4\langle\hat{O}_4\rangle = 0.12\ \mathrm{GeV}^4\), and \(C_6\langle\hat{O}_6\rangle = -0.39\ \mathrm{GeV}^6\).
- The ρ-meson width increases by roughly a factor of 20 near \(T_c\).

## Context

This work extends earlier thermal QCD sum rule studies of the vector channel by Bochkarev and Shaposhnikov (1986) and others, which used Laplace transform sum rules and gave inconsistent results. It follows a recent analysis of the axial-vector channel by the same group (Ayala et al., 2012) and provides a determination of the ρ-meson width entirely from QCD, as opposed to earlier work based on current algebra. The results are important for current experiments on dimuon production in high energy nuclear collisions and show approximate universality of the deconfinement transition in light-quark systems, with the thermal gluon condensate agreeing with lattice QCD results.
