# Continuum particle-vibration coupling method in coordinate-space representation for finite nuclei

> In this paper we present a new formalism to implement the nuclear particle-vibration coupling (PVC) model. The key issue is the proper treatment of the continuum, that is allowed by the coordinate space representation. Our formalism, based on the use of zero-range interactions like the Skyrme forces, is microscopic and fully self-consistent. We apply it to the case of neutron single-particle states in $^{40}$Ca, $^{208}$Pb and $^{24}$O. The first two cases are meant to illustrate the comparison with the usual (i.e., discrete) PVC model. However, we stress that the present approach allows to calculate properly the effect of PVC on resonant states. We compare our results with those from experiments in which the particle transfer in the continuum region has been attempted. The latter case, namely $^{24}$O, is chosen as an example of a weakly-bound system. Such a nucleus, being double-magic and not displaying collective low-lying vibrational excitations, is characterized by quite pure neutron single-particle states around the Fermi surface.

## Key claim

The authors present a new formalism for implementing particle-vibration coupling (PVC) in nuclei that treats the continuum properly using coordinate-space representation, but they limit themselves to the first iteration of the Dyson equation by replacing the full Green's function with the unperturbed Hartree-Fock Green's function in the self-energy calculation, and they introduce a cutoff on phonon energies at 60 MeV to handle ultraviolet divergences from the zero-range Skyrme interaction.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson equation for the single-particle Green's function in coordinate-space representation, starting from a self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) mean field and Random Phase Approximation (RPA) phonons both calculated with the Skyrme interaction SLy5. The self-energy is constructed from the convolution of the HF Green's function with the causal RPA response function, using a contour integration in the complex energy plane to properly include continuum contributions. The resulting Dyson equation is solved numerically on a radial mesh to obtain the perturbed Green's function, from which the single-particle level density is extracted after subtracting the free-particle level density.

## Key numbers

- The angular momentum cutoff for unoccupied continuum states is set at \(l_{cut}=7\hbar\) for \(^{40}\)Ca and \(l_{cut}=12\hbar\) for \(^{24}\)O and \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The radial mesh size is \(\Delta r = 0.2\) fm.
- The smearing parameter \(\eta = 0.2\) MeV is used in the Green's functions and response functions.
- The cutoff on phonon energies is set at \(E_{\rm cut} = 60\) MeV.
- The HF neutron single-particle energies in \(^{40}\)Ca range from -48.3 MeV (1s\(_{1/2}\) hole state) to -1.3 MeV (1f\(_{5/2}\) particle state).
- The theoretical RPA energy of the low-lying \(3^-\) state in \(^{40}\)Ca is 4.13 MeV with transition strength \(1.13 \times 10^4\) e\(^2\)fm\(^6\), compared to the experimental values of 3.74 MeV and \(1.84 \times 10^4\) e\(^2\)fm\(^6\).
- The theoretical RPA energy of the \(5^-\) state in \(^{40}\)Ca is 5.25 MeV with transition strength \(2.22 \times 10^6\) e\(^2\)fm\(^{10}\), compared to the experimental energy of 4.49 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic PVC calculations based on Skyrme Hamiltonians (Colo 2010) and Relativistic Mean Field parameterizations (Litvinova, Anatolj) by introducing for the first time a proper treatment of the continuum in coordinate-space representation, whereas all prior PVC models discretized the single-particle continuum. The formalism builds on earlier experience with continuum RPA calculations (Matsuo, Mizuyama, Bertsch, Liu) and enables the calculation of PVC effects on resonant states and weakly-bound systems like \(^{24}\)O, as well as the proper calculation of spreading widths and spectroscopic factors for unbound single-particle states.
