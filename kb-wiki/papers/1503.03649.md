# Nucleon-pion-state contribution to nucleon two-point correlation functions

> We study the nucleon-pion-state contribution to the QCD two-point function of the standard nucleon interpolating fields. For sufficiently small quark masses these two-particle states are expected to have a smaller total energy than the single-particle excited states. We calculate the nucleon-pion-state contribution to leading order in chiral perturbation theory. Both parity channels are considered. We find the nucleon-pion-state contribution to be small, contributing at the few percent level to the effective mass in the positive parity channel.

## Key claim

The nucleon-pion-state contribution to the nucleon two-point correlation function is small, contributing at the few percent level to the effective mass in the positive parity channel, but only under the condition that the pion mass is sufficiently small such that these two-particle states have lower total energy than single-particle excited states.

## Method

The authors compute the nucleon-pion-state contribution to the QCD two-point function of standard nucleon interpolating fields using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to leading order. They consider both local and smeared interpolating fields, map them onto effective operators in ChPT based on their chiral and parity transformation properties, and evaluate the Feynman diagrams representing the leading contribution to the two-particle nucleon-pion part of the correlation function for both positive and negative parity channels.

## Key numbers

- For a pion mass satisfying M_pi * L approximately equal to 4 and M_pi / M_N approximately equal to 0.2, the ratio b_1/b_0 is approximately 0.1.
- The two-particle-state contribution contributes at the few-percent level for Euclidean times of about 0.5 fm.
- The naive estimate for the ratio of two-particle to one-particle contributions gives a suppression factor of approximately 1/30 for M_pi approximately 200 MeV and L approximately 4 fm, using the expression [2(f_pi L)^2 M_pi L]^(-1).

## Context

This work extends the approach proposed in Bar (2012) and Tiburzi (2009) of using chiral perturbation theory to estimate two-particle-state contributions to nucleon correlation functions. The calculation uses covariant baryon ChPT, while an independent computation by Tiburzi (2015) used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT); the authors show that expanding their results reproduces the HBChPT results. This work enables lattice QCD practitioners to estimate the systematic uncertainty from nucleon-pion-state contamination in nucleon mass extractions, particularly for simulations near physical quark masses where multi-particle states become significant.
