# SU(2N_F) symmetry of QCD at high temperature and its implications

> If above a critical temperature not only the SU(N_F)_L \times SU(N_F)_R chiral symmetry of QCD but also the U(1)_A symmetry is restored, then the actual symmetry of the QCD correlation functions and observables is SU(2N_F). Such a symmetry prohibits existence of deconfined quarks and gluons. Hence QCD at high temperature is also in the confining regime and elementary objects are SU(2N_F) symmetric "hadrons" with not yet known properties.

# SU(2N_F) symmetry of QCD at high temperature and its implications

## Key claim

If above a critical temperature both the SU(N_F)_L x SU(N_F)_R chiral symmetry and the U(1)_A symmetry are restored, then the actual symmetry of QCD correlation functions and observables is SU(2N_F), which prohibits deconfined quarks and gluons, implying QCD remains in a confining regime at high temperature with elementary objects being SU(2N_F)-symmetric "hadrons" of unknown properties.

## Method

The author analyzes the symmetry properties of the QCD partition function in Euclidean space by expanding fermion fields over eigenmodes of the Dirac operator. The exact zero modes of the Dirac operator, which break SU(2)_CS (chiralspin) invariance, are shown to be irrelevant in the thermodynamic limit as their contributions vanish as 1/V. The author then demonstrates that the partition function without these zero modes possesses hidden local SU(2)_CS and SU(2N_F) symmetries, which are broken by the axial anomaly and spontaneously by the quark condensate, but can become manifest above the chiral restoration temperature if a gap opens in the Dirac spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The exact zero mode contributions to Green functions and observables vanish in the thermodynamic limit as 1/V (where V is volume).
- The quark condensate in Minkowski space is expressed via the Banks-Casher relation as lim_{m -> 0} <0|bar Psi(x) Psi(x)|0> = -pi rho(0), where rho(0) is the density of near-zero Dirac modes.

## Context

This work extends lattice simulation results from Glozman and collaborators (2015-2016) where SU(4) symmetry was observed in N_F=2 Euclidean QCD after truncating low-lying Dirac modes, and from Denissenya et al. (2014-2015) showing hadrons survive such truncation with chiral partners becoming degenerate. The paper contradicts the conventional belief that QCD at high temperature is in a deconfinement regime with liberated quarks and gluons. It enables lattice tests of SU(2)_CS and SU(2N_F) restoration above T_c through predictions for correlators of vector meson operators, and opens the question of what properties SU(2N_F)-symmetric "hadrons" would have in Minkowski space.
