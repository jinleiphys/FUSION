# Semiclassical trace formula for truncated spherical well potentials: Toward the analyses of shell structures in nuclear fission processes

> Trace formulas for the contributions of degenerate periodic-orbit families to the semiclassical level density in truncated spherical hard-wall potentials are derived. In addition to the portion of the continuous periodic-orbit family contribution which persists after truncation, end-point corrections to the truncated family should be taken into account. I propose a formula to evaluate these end-point corrections as separate contributions of what I call marginal orbits. Applications to the two-dimensional billiard and three-dimensional cavity systems with the three-quadratic-surfaces shape parametrization, initiated to describe the nuclear fission processes, reveal unexpectedly large effects of the marginal orbits.

## Key claim

The paper derives a semiclassical trace formula for degenerate periodic-orbit families in truncated spherical hard-wall potentials, showing that in addition to the principal contribution from the continuous family (reduced by a geometric occupation factor), one must include "end-point corrections" from what the author calls marginal orbits at the truncation boundary, and these marginal orbits have unexpectedly large effects on the level density.

## Method

The author starts from the Balian-Bloch multiple-reflection expansion for the Green's function in hard-wall potentials and applies stationary-phase integration to the surface integrals that give the semiclassical level density. For the truncated spherical cavity, the integration range over the degenerate family parameters (rotation angles) is restricted; the interior portion yields a principal term proportional to the occupation rate (the fraction of the full parameter space that remains), while the endpoints are treated separately by extending the neighboring wall smoothly inward and evaluating the resulting isolated "marginal orbits" via the Gutzwiller trace formula. The method is applied to two-dimensional billiard and three-dimensional cavity systems using the three-quadratic-surfaces (TQS) shape parametrization designed for nuclear fission studies.

## Key numbers

* For the 2D circular billiard, the semiclassical trace formula using only the diameter (2,1) and triangle (3,1) orbit families reproduces the quantum level density with smoothing width γ = 0.3.
* For the 3D spherical cavity, the triangle (3,1) and square (4,1) orbit families reproduce the supershell beating pattern with smoothing width γ = 0.3.
* The occupation rate for the truncated diameter family in 3D is f₂ = cos ϑ_A, where ϑ_A is the polar angle of the truncation boundary.
* The occupation rate for polygon families (p,t) in 3D is f_p = ∫_{ϑ_A}^{ϑ_B} ψ_p(ϑ) sin ϑ dϑ, where ψ_p(ϑ) is given by an explicit trigonometric formula involving the truncation angle and the orbit geometry.

## Context

This work extends the Balian-Bloch trace formula (1972) and Gutzwiller's periodic-orbit theory (1971) to systems where continuous periodic-orbit families are truncated by a boundary, as occurs in the necked shapes of fissioning nuclei. It builds on earlier semiclassical analyses of shell effects in fission by Brack et al. (1997), who used a simple cavity model, and by Strutinsky and coworkers (1976, 1977), who applied trace formulas to deformed nuclear mean fields. The TQS parametrization was introduced by Nix (1969) for describing nuclear fission shapes. The results enable unambiguous identification of "prefragment" shell effects in fission by isolating the contributions of periodic orbits confined to each nascent fragment, which is difficult in purely quantum mechanical approaches where single-particle wave functions are delocalized.
