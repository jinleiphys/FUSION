# Algebraic Gorkov solution in finite systems for the separable pairing interaction

> An algebraic Quantum Field Theory formulation of separable pairing interaction for spherical finite systems is presented. The Lipkin formulation of the model Hamiltonian and model wave function is used. The Green function technique is applied to obtain the model energy through the spectral function. Closed equation for the many-body energy of the system is given and comparison with exact models are performed.

## Key claim

The paper presents an algebraic Gorkov formulation for the separable pairing interaction in spherical finite systems, showing that the resulting ground-state energy and gap equations reduce to only two coupled equations (particle number and gap equation) rather than a state-dependent set, but the approach yields results that differ from standard BCS for finite systems (e.g., the two-level model at half filling) unless the degeneracy \(\Omega\) is large.

## Method

The authors adopt the Lipkin model Hamiltonian \(\mathcal{H} = H - \lambda \hat{N}\) and a non-particle-number-conserving ground state \(|\Psi\rangle\) in Fock space. They define the normal one-particle Green function \(G_{\alpha\alpha'}(t,t')\) and the anomalous Green function \(\tilde{G}^\dagger_{\alpha\alpha'}(t,t')\), derive their equations of motion for the separable pairing interaction \(g_{ac} = -g f_a f_c\), and solve them algebraically in the energy domain via Fourier transform. The particle number and gap equations are then obtained from the Green functions, yielding closed expressions for the Fermi level \(\lambda\) and the gap parameter \(D\) (with \(\Delta_a = f_a D\)), and the ground-state energy is computed using the spectral function.

## Key numbers

* Degenerate model: ground-state energy \(E = N\varepsilon - g \frac{N}{4} \left( 2\Omega - N + \frac{N}{\Omega} \right)\); relative error with exact solution scales as \(\approx 1/\Omega\).
* Degenerate model: gap \(\Delta = \frac{g N}{2} \sqrt{\frac{2\Omega}{N} - 1}\).
* Two-level model at half filling (\(N=2\Omega\)): quasiparticle energies \(E_{u/d} = \frac{g \Omega}{2} f_{u/d} (f_u + f_d)\).
* Two-level model: critical coupling for positive gap \(g_c = \frac{2\varepsilon}{\Omega (f_u+f_d)^2 - 2 f_d}\).
* Constant pairing case (\(f_a=1\)): quasiparticle energies \(E_u = E_d = g \Omega\); modified single-particle energy \(\tilde{e}_u = \frac{\Omega \varepsilon}{2\Omega-1}\) (this work) vs. \(\tilde{e}_u^{BCS} = \frac{2\Omega \varepsilon}{4\Omega-1}\) (standard BCS).

## Context

This work extends the Gorkov quantum field theory approach (Gorkov, 1958) to spherical finite systems with a separable pairing interaction, building on the Lipkin formulation (Lipkin, 1960) and the Green function technique (Dickhoff, 2005). It provides an algebraic closed-form solution that avoids explicit quasiparticle definitions, enabling future extension to continuum spectra via complex energy representation (Berggren, 1968). The results are tested against the exactly solvable degenerate and symmetric two-level models (Lipkin, 1965; Suhonen, 2007), showing that the approach reproduces BCS in the large-\(\Omega\) limit but differs for small systems, highlighting the need for careful treatment of off-diagonal terms in finite nuclei.
