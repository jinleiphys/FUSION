# Hadrons as QCD Bound States

> Bound state perturbation theory is well established for QED atoms. Today the hyperfine splitting of Positronium is known to $O(α^7\logα)$. Whereas standard expansions of scattering amplitudes start from free states, bound states are expanded around eigenstates of the Hamiltonian including a binding potential. The eigenstate wave functions have all powers of $α$, requiring a choice in the ordering of the perturbative expansion. Temporal $(A^0=0)$ gauge permits an expansion starting from valence Fock states, bound by their instantaneous gauge field. This formulation is applicable in any frame and seems promising even for hadrons in QCD. The $O(α_s^0)$ confining potential is determined (up to a universal scale) by a homogeneous solution of Gauss' law.

## Key claim
Bound state perturbation theory in temporal gauge, where valence Fock states are bound by their instantaneous gauge field, may be applicable to hadrons in QCD even though the strong coupling is not large, because the confining potential at leading order in the strong coupling arises from a homogeneous solution of Gauss' law rather than from a large coupling constant.

## Method
The paper develops a "Bound Fock expansion" in which bound states are expanded around eigenstates of the Hamiltonian including an instantaneous binding potential, rather than around free states as in standard scattering expansions. For QCD in temporal gauge, the author solves Gauss' law constraint on physical states, adding a homogeneous solution to the particular solution for the color electric field, which introduces a universal scale and produces a linear confining potential. The bound state equation is then derived for meson and baryon valence Fock states using the instantaneous potential from this construction.

## Key numbers
- Positronium hyperfine splitting is known to O(alpha^7 log alpha), with the leading term (7/12)alpha^4 in units of the electron mass
- The Cornell potential parameters are V' = 0.18 GeV^2 for the linear slope and alpha_s = 0.39 for the Coulomb coefficient
- The QCD coupling freezes at hadronic scales, alpha_s(Q less than about 1 GeV) approximately 0.5
- The Bohr radius of Positronium is r = 2/(alpha m_e)
- The binding energy of Positronium at rest is E_b = -(1/4)alpha^2 m_e + O(alpha^4)

## Context
This work extends the bound state formalism used successfully in QED atoms, where the hyperfine splitting of Positronium is known to high precision (Penin 2014, Adkins 2015, 2018), to QCD hadrons. It builds on the Bethe-Salpeter equation approach (Salpeter 1951, Itzykson 1980) and the Cornell potential phenomenology (Eichten 1979, 2007) for charmonium and bottomonium. The approach enables a systematic perturbative expansion for hadrons starting from valence Fock states, potentially providing a field-theoretic foundation for the quark model and allowing calculations of hadron properties, Regge trajectories, and hadronization processes.
