# Low momentum nucleon-nucleon potential and shell model effective interactions

> A low momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential $V_{low-k}$ is derived from meson exhange potentials by integrating out the model dependent high momentum modes of $V_{NN}$. The smooth and approximately unique $V_{low-k}$ is used as input for shell model calculations instead of the usual Brueckner $G$ matrix. Such an approach eliminates the nuclear mass dependence of the input interaction one finds in the $G$ matrix approach, allowing the same input interaction to be used in different nuclear regions. Shell model calculations of $^{18}$O and $^{134}$Te using the {\bf same} input $V_{low-k}$ have been performed. For cut-off momentum $Λ$ in the vicinity of 2 $fm^{-1}$, our calculated low-lying spectra for these nuclei are in good agreement with experiments, and are weakly dependent on $Λ$.

## Key claim

The authors derive a low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential \(V_{low-k}\) from meson-exchange potentials by integrating out high-momentum modes, and show that this potential can serve as a common input for shell-model calculations across different nuclear mass regions, eliminating the nuclear-mass dependence inherent in the traditional Brueckner \(G\)-matrix approach. The claim is hedged by the condition that the cut-off momentum \(\Lambda\) must be in the vicinity of 2 fm\(^{-1}\) for the calculated spectra to be in good agreement with experiment and weakly dependent on \(\Lambda\).

## Method

The authors start from the half-on-shell \(T\)-matrix of a bare nucleon-nucleon potential (CD-Bonn) and define a low-momentum \(T\)-matrix with all loop integrals cut off at a momentum \(\Lambda\). They require the two \(T\)-matrices to be equal for momenta below \(\Lambda\), which leads to a folded-diagram effective interaction \(V_{low-k}\) derived using the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff (KLR) method and solved via the Andreozzi-Lee-Suzuki (ALS) iteration. This \(V_{low-k}\) is then used directly as the input interaction in shell-model calculations for \(^{18}\)O and \(^{134}\)Te, replacing the usual Brueckner \(G\)-matrix.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy \(BE_d\) given by \(V_{low-k}\) agrees with that from the bare \(V_{NN}\) to four decimal places for a range of cut-off momenta \(0.5 \text{ fm}^{-1} \leq \Lambda \leq 3 \text{ fm}^{-1}\).
* The \(^1S_0\) phase shifts calculated from \(V_{low-k}\) with \(\Lambda = 2.0 \text{ fm}^{-1}\) reproduce those from the CD-Bonn \(V_{NN}\).
* Shell-model calculations for \(^{18}\)O and \(^{134}\)Te were performed using the same \(V_{low-k}\) with cut-off momenta \(\Lambda = 2.0\) and \(2.2 \text{ fm}^{-1}\), and the resulting low-lying spectra are in satisfactory agreement with experiment and insensitive to the choice of \(\Lambda\).
* The maximum cut-off \(\Lambda_{max}\) beyond which \(V_{low-k}\) becomes dependent on the particular \(V_{NN}\) model is approximately 2.0–2.5 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization-group and effective-field-theory (RG-EFT) approach to nuclear structure, motivated by Lepage (1997) and others, by applying it to derive a low-momentum potential for shell-model calculations. It contrasts with the traditional Brueckner \(G\)-matrix approach used by Covello et al. (1997) and Jiang et al. (1992), which yields different input interactions for different nuclear mass regions due to Pauli blocking. The results enable the possibility of a common, nearly model-independent shell-model interaction suitable for a wide range of nuclei, with ongoing work extending the method to the \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{132}\)Sn regions.
