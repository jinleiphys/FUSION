# Cosmology and nuclear-physics implications of a subsolar gravitational-wave event

> Detecting a compact subsolar object would have profound implications in physics, the reach of which depends on the nature of the object. Here we explore such consequences for a putative subsolar-mass gravitational wave event detected by the LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA Collaboration. We forecast that the nature of a subsolar binary (made of light neutron stars, primordial black holes, or more exotic compact objects) can be inferred with a great statistical confidence level already during the ongoing fourth observing run, based on the large tidal deformability effects on the signal. The detection of a primordial black hole would have implications for cosmology and dark matter scenarios, while the measurement of the tidal deformability of a subsolar neutron star could rule out or confirm the existence of strange stars made of quarks.

## Key claim

The paper forecasts that during the ongoing LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA fourth observing run (O4), the nature of a subsolar-mass gravitational-wave binary (whether composed of light neutron stars, primordial black holes, or exotic compact objects like boson stars) can be inferred with high statistical confidence based on tidal deformability effects, but this conclusion is conditional on the signal-to-noise ratio being sufficiently high (e.g., SNR=25) and on the specific models considered.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference using the BILBY software on both real (the candidate event SSM200308 from O3b) and synthetic gravitational-wave data, employing six different waveform models: two black-hole models (BH1 with spin precession, BH2 without), an agnostic model with generic tidal deformability and quadrupole parameters, two neutron-star models (NS1 with Love-Q relations, NS2 without), and a boson-star model. For forecasts, they injected simulated signals into O4 and O5 noise curves and recovered them with both correct and incorrect models, computing Bayes factors to assess distinguishability. They also compared specific neutron-star equations of state (APR, WFF1, SLy4, BSk21, and strange quark matter SQM3) by implementing EoS-dependent tidal deformability relations.

## Key numbers

* For the candidate event SSM200308, the primary mass was inferred as $0.65^{+0.17}_{-0.15} M_\odot$ and secondary as $0.26^{+0.07}_{-0.04} M_\odot$ under the BH1 model (90% confidence interval).
* The Bayes factor comparing NS1 to BH1 for SSM200308 was $\log_{10}\mathcal{B} = -2.68$, indicating decisive evidence against the NS1 model according to Jeffreys's scale.
* For O4 forecasts at SNR=25, the $\log_{10}$ Bayes factor for distinguishing a BH2 injection from NS2 recovery was -5.1, and for NS2 injection from BH2 recovery was -11.0.
* For distinguishing between APR and SQM3 equations of state in O4 at SNR=25 with primary mass $0.63 M_\odot$, the $\log_{10}$ Bayes factor was -1.9 (APR injection recovered with SQM3) and -3.8 (SQM3 injection recovered with APR).
* For the same comparison in O5 at SNR=44 with primary mass $0.27 M_\odot$, the $\log_{10}$ Bayes factor was -37.5 (APR injection recovered with SQM3) and -88.8 (SQM3 injection recovered with APR).
* The inferred PBH abundance needed to explain a putative SSM event would be $f_{\rm PBH} \sim \mathcal{O}(10^{-2})$, based on the analysis of Franciolini (2022).

## Context

This work extends the forecasts of Crescimbeni (2024) on tidal deformability effects in subsolar binaries and applies them to the specific candidate SSM200308 previously analyzed by Prunier (2023) under the black-hole hypothesis. The paper enables future O4 and O5 detections to discriminate between primordial black holes, light neutron stars formed through nonstandard supernova mechanisms (Metzger 2024), and exotic compact objects like boson stars (Vaglio 2023). A confirmed primordial black hole detection would constrain dark matter scenarios and early-universe physics, while a confirmed subsolar neutron star detection with measured tidal deformability could rule out or confirm the existence of strange quark stars (Prakash 1995), providing constraints on the nuclear equation of state in a mass range currently lacking observational data.
