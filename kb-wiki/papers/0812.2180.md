# Exchange current contributions in null-plane quantum models of elastic electron deuteron scattering

> We investigate exchange current contributions to elastic electron-deuteron scattering using exactly Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics with a null-plane kinematic symmetry. Our model exchange current is motivated by one-pion-exchange physics. Exact current conservation and current covariance are satisfied by using this current to compute a linearly independent set of current matrix elements. The remaining current matrix elements are generated from the independent matrix elements using the covariance and current conservation constraints. The presence of the exchange current increases the sensitivity to the choice of independent current matrix elements. Two choices of independent matrix elements that have distinct motivations lead to a good description of all of the elastic scattering observables for momentum transfers below 8 (GeV)$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including a two-body exchange current, motivated by one-pion-exchange physics, in a Poincaré-invariant quantum mechanical model with null-plane kinematic symmetry yields good quantitative agreement with experiment for all three elastic electron-deuteron scattering observables, but the results are sensitive to the choice of which three independent current matrix elements are used to construct the fully covariant current.

## Method

The authors construct a dynamical unitary representation of the Poincaré group on the two-nucleon Hilbert space by adding a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction (Argonne V18 or CD Bonn) to the square of the non-interacting invariant mass operator, with the interaction chosen to be invariant under the null-plane kinematic subgroup. They compute deuteron current matrix elements using a two-body current operator motivated by pair-current contributions in covariant formulations, then use covariance and current conservation constraints to generate the remaining matrix elements from a linearly independent set. The resulting covariant current is evaluated for elastic electron-deuteron scattering observables and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range studied: below 8 (GeV)^2
- The deuteron binding energy correction from relativistic effects is about 1 part in 2000, which the authors ignore
- The structure functions A(Q^2), B(Q^2), and tensor polarization T_{20}(Q^2,θ) at θ_{lab} = 70° are computed and compared to experiment

## Context

This work extends null-plane impulse calculations (Leutwyler 1978, Grach 1984, Chung 1988, Keister 1991) that used only single-nucleon currents and gave good qualitative but not quantitative agreement with experiment, particularly for observables A and T_{20}. The approach differs from quasipotential treatments (Mandelstam 1955, VanOrden 2001) and from null-plane quantum field theory (Chang 1973, Chung 1997) by maintaining exact rotational covariance. The results enable systematic investigation of two-body current effects in relativistic few-body systems and clarify ambiguities in implementing the angular condition for current matrix elements.
