# Pseudocalar meson and vector meson interactions and dynamically generated axial-vector mesons

> The axial-vector mesons $a_1(1260)$, $b_1(1235)$, $f_1(1285)$, $h_1(1170)$, $h_1(1380)$, and $K_1(1270)$ are dynamically generated in the unitized chiral perturbation theory. Such a picture has been tested extensively in the past few years. In this work, we calculate the interaction kernel up to $\mathcal{O}(p^2)$ and study the impact on the dynamically generated axial-vector states. In anticipation of future lattice QCD simulations, we calculate the scattering lengths and the pole positions as functions of the pion mass, with the light-quark mass dependence of the kaon, the eta, and the vector mesons determined by the $n_f=2+1$ lattice QCD simulations of the PACS-CS Collaboration.

## Key claim

The axial-vector mesons \(a_1(1260)\), \(b_1(1235)\), \(f_1(1285)\), \(h_1(1170)\), \(h_1(1380)\), and \(K_1(1270)\) remain dynamically generated in unitized chiral perturbation theory even when the interaction kernel is extended to next-to-leading order (\(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\)), with the inclusion of higher-order chiral potentials not changing the leading-order results in any significant way.

## Method

The authors calculated the S-wave interaction kernel for pseudoscalar meson–vector meson scattering up to \(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\) in chiral perturbation theory, including the Weinberg–Tomozawa term at leading order and the \(\delta_1\), \(\delta_2\), and \(\kappa_V\) terms at next-to-leading order. They unitarized the kernel using the Bethe–Salpeter equation in the on-shell approximation, with the loop function regularized via dimensional regularization. The light-quark mass dependence of the pseudoscalar and vector meson masses was taken from the \(n_f=2+1\) lattice QCD simulations of the PACS-CS Collaboration, and the scattering lengths and pole positions were computed as functions of the pion mass.

## Key numbers

* The two poles of the \(K_1(1270)\) in the \((S,I)=(1,1/2)\) channel are found at \(\sqrt{s}=1119-i68\) MeV and \(\sqrt{s}=1216-i5\) MeV with the NLO kernel (compared to \(1111-i64\) MeV and \(1215-i4\) MeV at LO).
* In the \((S,I)=(0,0)\) channel, the \(h_1(1170)\) pole is at \(\sqrt{s}=925-i29\) MeV (NLO) versus \(918-i17\) MeV (LO); the \(h_1(1380)\) pole is at \(1257-i0\) MeV (NLO) versus \(1244-i7\) MeV (LO); the \(f_1(1285)\) pole is at \(1289-i0\) MeV (NLO) versus \(1286-i0\) MeV (LO).
* In the \((S,I)=(0,1)\) channel, the \(a_1(1260)\) pole is at \(\sqrt{s}=1013-i106\) MeV (NLO) versus \(1011-i84\) MeV (LO); the \(b_1(1235)\) pole is at \(\sqrt{s}=1293-i22\) MeV (NLO) versus \(1246-i27\) MeV (LO).
* The low-energy constants for the vector meson mass fit to PACS-CS data are \(M_0=0.711964\) GeV, \(\lambda_m=0.48901\), and \(\lambda_0=0.126032\) with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.}=0.723\).
* The scattering length \(a_{\rho\pi}\) in the \(a_1\) channel is of order 10 at physical pion mass, with threshold effects visible as the pion mass varies.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order unitized chiral perturbation theory studies of Lutz (2003) and Roca (2005), which first showed that axial-vector mesons can be dynamically generated from pseudoscalar–vector meson interactions. By including next-to-leading order chiral potentials, the authors test the stability of that picture and provide predictions for scattering lengths and pole positions as functions of the pion mass, enabling direct comparison with future lattice QCD simulations such as those by the PACS-CS Collaboration (Aoki, 2008) and the recent \(n_f=2\) lattice study of Lang (2014). The results also enable chiral extrapolations of lattice data and may help identify the two-pole structure of the \(K_1(1270)\) on the lattice, analogous to strategies proposed for the \(\Lambda(1405)\) by Martinez Torres (2012).
