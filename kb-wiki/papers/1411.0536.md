# K+'s Collective Flow in Heavy-ion Collisions predicted by Covariant Kaon Dynamics

> The directed and elliptic flows of positively charged Kaon produced in $^{58}_{28}$Ni + $^{58}_{28}$Ni reaction at incident kinetic energy 1.91 AGeV, experimental data are released newly by V. Zinyuk,et.al. in Ref.[arXiv: 1403.1504v2 [nucl-ex] 8 Apr 2014], are reproduced by using the covariant kaon dynamics. Our numerical results indicate qualitatively the Lorentz force is necessary to explained reasonably the data as soon as the space-like part of kaon's vector potential is involved. The sensitivity of $K^+$ directed as well as differential directed flow on the Lorentz force are also observed near target rapidity.

## Key claim

The covariant kaon dynamics model, which includes a Lorentz-like force from the space-like part of the kaon's vector potential, is necessary to reproduce the newly released experimental data for K⁺ directed and elliptic flows in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.91 AGeV; the model's success is conditional on the inclusion of this Lorentz force, and the Brown & Rho parametrization (BRP) yields a more reasonable pattern than the Ko and Li scheme (KLP).

## Method

The authors combined the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model with covariant kaon dynamics, where kaon equations of motion are derived from the chiral Lagrangian and include a Lorentz force term arising from the spatial components of the kaon vector potential. They simulated the reaction ⁵⁸₂₈Ni + ⁵⁸₂₈Ni at 1.91 AGeV using a soft nuclear equation of state (compression modulus K = 200 MeV) and compared results with and without the Lorentz force, using both the Brown & Rho parametrization (BRP: Σ_KN = 450 MeV, f_π*² = 0.6 f_π² for vector field) and the Ko and Li scheme (KLP: Σ_KN = 350 MeV, f_π = 0.93 MeV). The directed flow v₁ and elliptic flow v₂ were calculated as functions of rapidity and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Incident kinetic energy: 1.91 AGeV for the ⁵⁸₂₈Ni + ⁵⁸₂₈Ni reaction.
- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- Bare kaon mass: m_K = 0.496 GeV.
- Gaussian wave packet width: L = 1.08 fm², corresponding to a nucleon root mean square radius of 1.8 fm.
- Impact parameters used: b = 4.54 fm (peripheral) and b = 2.11 fm (central).
- K⁺N scattering cross section: approximately 10 millibarn.
- Kaon potential at saturation density: U_K⁺(ρ₀) ≈ 20 MeV for KLP and ≈ 30 MeV for BRP.

## Context

This work extends prior studies using covariant kaon dynamics combined with QMD (Zheng et al., various years) to reproduce collective flow of K⁺ mesons, and directly tests the model against newly released experimental data from the FOPI Collaboration at SIS (Zinyuk et al., 2014). The authors compare their approach to the IQMD and HSD transport models, which were used in the experimental paper to analyze the same data, and highlight that the Lorentz force is a distinguishing feature of their covariant treatment. The results enable further refinement of in-medium kaon potentials and the nuclear equation of state, particularly regarding the role of the vector field's spatial components.
