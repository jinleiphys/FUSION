# Power counting with one-pion exchange

> Techniques developed for handing inverse-power-law potentials in atomic physics are applied to the tensor one-pion exchange potential to determine the regions in which it can be treated perturbatively. In S-, P- and D-waves the critical values of the relative momentum are less than or of the order of 400 MeV. The RG is then used to determine the power counting for short-range interaction in the presence of this potential. In the P-and D-waves, where there are no low-energy bound or virtual states, these interactions have half-integer RG eigenvalues and are substantially promoted relative to naive expectations. These results are independent of whether the tensor force is attractive or repulsive. In the 3S1 channel the leading term is relevant, but it is demoted by half an order compared to the counting for the effective-range expansion with only a short-range potential. The tensor force can be treated perturbatively in those F-waves and above that do not couple to P- or D-waves. The corresponding power counting is the usual one given by naive dimensional analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the tensor one-pion exchange potential must be treated nonperturbatively in S-, P-, and D-waves for relative momenta up to about 400 MeV, but can be treated perturbatively in F-waves and above; the resulting power counting for short-range interactions is modified to half-integer RG eigenvalues in P- and D-waves, independent of whether the tensor force is attractive or repulsive.

## Method

The author applies techniques developed for inverse-power-law potentials in atomic physics to construct exact solutions of the Schrödinger equation for the tensor one-pion exchange potential in the chiral limit, using Bessel function expansions and continued fraction methods to determine critical momenta where perturbative expansions break down. A Wilsonian renormalisation group analysis in a distorted-wave basis is then used to determine the scaling behaviour and power counting of short-range contact interactions in the presence of the long-range tensor potential.

## Key numbers

- The scale \(\lambda_\pi = 16\pi F_\pi^2 / (g_A^2 M_N) \simeq 290\) MeV, constructed from the nucleon mass and pion decay constant
- Critical dimensionless coupling \(\kappa_c = 0.318058\) for \(l=0\) (uncoupled channels), corresponding to a critical relative momentum below which OPE can be treated perturbatively
- Critical dimensionless coupling \(\kappa_c = 2.51811\) for \(l=1\) (uncoupled channels)
- Critical dimensionless coupling \(\kappa_c = 8.33342\) for \(l=2\) (uncoupled channels)
- For coupled channels with \(j=1\) (\(l=0,2\)), critical values \(\kappa_{c1} = 0.683495\) and \(\kappa_{c2} = 2.48290\)
- For coupled channels with \(j=2\) (\(l=1,3\)), critical values \(\kappa_{c1} = 1.61857\) and \(\kappa_{c2} = 6.91983\)
- In S-, P-, and D-waves, critical relative momenta are less than or of the order of 400 MeV
- For large orbital angular momentum (\(l \gtrsim 20\)), \(\kappa_c\) grows roughly as \(0.3 l^3\)

## Context

This work addresses the debate between the Weinberg–van Kolck (WvK) scheme, which iterates one-pion exchange, and the Kaplan–Savage–Wise (KSW) scheme, which treats pion exchange perturbatively around a nontrivial fixed point. It extends the numerical observations of Nogga, Timmermans and van Kolck (2005) that iterated OPE cannot be consistently renormalised with naive dimensional analysis, and provides the RG-based power counting that explains those observations. The results enable a systematic EFT for nuclear forces where the tensor force is iterated in low partial waves but treated perturbatively in higher waves, with the modified power counting for short-range interactions given by half-integer RG eigenvalues.
