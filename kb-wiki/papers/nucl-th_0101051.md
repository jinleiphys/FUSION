# Meson exchange in the weak decay of Lambda hypernuclei and the Gamma_n/Gamma_p ratio

> We take an approach to the Lambda non-mesonic weak decay in nuclei based on the exchange of mesons. The one pion and one kaon exchange are considered, together with the exchange of two pions, either correlated, leading to an important scalar-isoscalar exchange (sigma-like exchange), or uncorrelated (box diagrams). Extra effects of omega exchange in the scalar-isoscalar channel are also considered. Constraints of chiral dynamics are used to generate these exchanges. A drastic reduction of the OPE results for the Gamma_n/Gamma_p ratio is obtained and the new results are compatible with all present experiments within errors. The absolute rates obtained for different nuclei are also in good agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The authors show that including two-pion exchange (both correlated, producing a sigma-like scalar-isoscalar exchange, and uncorrelated box diagrams) together with omega exchange and kaon exchange, within constraints from chiral dynamics, raises the predicted Gamma_n/Gamma_p ratio from the one-pion-exchange value of about 1/8 to values around 0.53, making the theoretical results compatible with all present experiments within errors.

## Method

The calculation is performed in infinite nuclear matter using a propagator approach with the local density approximation to extend results to finite nuclei. The authors include one-pion exchange (OPE) and one-kaon exchange, plus two-pion exchange in both correlated form (using chiral unitary theory to generate a sigma pole in the pi-pi scattering amplitude) and uncorrelated box diagrams, with additional omega exchange in the scalar-isoscalar channel. Short-range correlations are implemented via Landau-Migdal parameters, and the self-energy of the Lambda is computed with RPA resummation of ph and Delta h excitations.

## Key numbers

* Weak coupling constant: G * m_pi^2 = 2.211 x 10^-7
* Pion weak coupling constants: S = 1.06 (parity conserving), P = 0.527 (parity violating)
* Pion-nucleon strong coupling: f_piNN = 1.02
* Kaon strong coupling estimated from SU(3) flavor symmetry with D+F=1.26, D-F=0.33, pion decay constant f=93 MeV
* OPE alone gives Gamma_n/Gamma_p ~ 1/8 for 12_Lambda C
* Including all meson exchanges yields Gamma_n/Gamma_p ~ 0.53
* OPE total width: Gamma ~ 1.1 Gamma_Lambda^free
* With mesonic and two-nucleon induced width added: Gamma_tot = 1.6 Gamma_Lambda^free
* Experimental ratios for 5_Lambda He: 0.93 +/- 0.5; for 12_Lambda C: 1.33^{+1.12}_{-0.81}, 1.87^{+0.91}_{-1.59}, 0.70 +/- 0.30, 0.52 +/- 0.16

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between OPE predictions (Gamma_n/Gamma_p ~ 1/8 to 1/20) and experimental values near unity, following earlier attempts including heavier meson exchange by Parreno, Ramos, and Bennhold (1997) and by Dubach, Gibson, and collaborators (1996), as well as two-pion exchange studies by Itonaga, Motoba, and Shmatikov. The approach extends the chiral unitary treatment of pi-pi scattering from Oller and Oset (1997) to the hypernuclear decay problem, and the results suggest that the puzzle may be resolved without invoking quark degrees of freedom as in the works of Inoue, Oka, and collaborators.
