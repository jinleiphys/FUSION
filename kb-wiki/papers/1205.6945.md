# Timelike Compton Scattering - New Theoretical Results and Experimental Possibilities

> We review recent progress in the study of timelike Compton scattering (TCS), the crossed process of deeply virtual Compton scattering. We emphasize the need to include NLO corrections to any phenomenological program to extract Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) from near future experimental data. We point out that TCS at high energy should be available through a study of ultraperipheral collisions at RHIC and LHC, opening a window on quark and gluon GPDs at very small skewness.

## Key claim

Timelike Compton Scattering (TCS) offers a complementary window to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) for extracting Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs), but next-to-leading-order (NLO) corrections are essential for any phenomenological program; in particular, the NLO correction to the real part of the timelike Compton form factor can reach approximately -400% at small skewness, making it comparable in magnitude to the imaginary part.

## Method

The authors compute the factorized Compton scattering amplitude for TCS at NLO in QCD, using renormalized coefficient functions for quarks and gluons, and relate the timelike coefficient functions to the spacelike (DVCS) ones through a simple analytic continuation relation involving an imaginary term. They then evaluate the cross sections for ultraperipheral collisions at RHIC and the LHC by folding the photon flux from the hadron beams with the computed \(\gamma p \to p l^+ l^-\) cross section, using the Goloskokov-Kroll model for GPDs and NLO GRVGJR2008 PDFs.

## Key numbers

- NLO correction to \(\Re{\rm e}{^T{\cal H}}\) for TCS is of the order of -400% at small \(\xi\) ( \(10^{-4} \le \xi \le 10^{-2}\) ) for \(\mu^2 = Q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(t=0\).
- For ultraperipheral \(pp\) collisions at the LHC, the integrated Bethe-Heitler cross section is \(\sigma_{pp}^{BH} = 2.9\ \text{pb}\) and the TCS cross section is \(\sigma_{pp}^{TCS} = 1.9\ \text{pb}\), for cuts \(\theta = [\pi/4, 3\pi/4]\), \(\phi = [0, 2\pi]\), \(t = [-0.05\ \text{GeV}^2, -0.25\ \text{GeV}^2]\), \(Q'^2 = [4.5\ \text{GeV}^2, 5.5\ \text{GeV}^2]\), and photon energies \(k = [20, 900]\ \text{GeV}\).
- The predicted event rate at the LHC with nominal luminosity (\(10^{34}\ \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\)) is of order \(10^5\) events per year.
- For ultraperipheral collisions at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{pp}} = 500\ \text{GeV}\), \(t = -0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\)), the Bethe-Heitler contribution alone yields \(10^3\) events for \(10^7\ \text{s}\) of running.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for TCS, first proposed in the early 2000s, by providing the full NLO corrections and establishing a simple relation to the well-known DVCS results of Belitsky, Mueller, Niedermeier, and Schafer (2000). It enables experimental extraction of GPDs at very small skewness via ultraperipheral collisions at RHIC and the LHC, complementing the JLab 12 GeV program (which will probe the valence region around \(\xi \sim 0.2\)) and future Electron-Ion Collider studies. The authors also note that first attempts to measure TCS were performed at JLab at 6 GeV (Rafayel, 2010), serving as a feasibility test for the upgraded 12 GeV experiment.
