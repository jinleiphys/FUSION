# Collective effects in O-O and Ne-Ne collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$=5.36 TeV from a hybrid approach

> Many features of heavy-ion collisions are well described by hybrid approaches, where the droplet of strongly coupled quark gluon plasma (QGP) is modeled by hydrodynamics and the subsequent dilute stage is performed with a hadronic transport model. Conventionally, the formation of a QGP is well established in larger collision systems like lead and gold. However, hints of collectivity were found even in proton-proton collisions, raising the question where the onset of QGP formation lays. This study aims at making predictions for the light-ions run at the CERN Large Hadron Collider in July 2025, in order to explore the applicability of hybrid approaches in smaller collision systems. We employ three different models: the SMASH-vHLLE hybrid approach, the pure hadronic cascade of SMASH, and Angantyr to simulate O-O collisions at a center-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$=5.36 TeV. This setup allows us to compare evolutions with and without a hydrodynamic description on an equal basis, while Angantyr serves as a baseline for no collective effects.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that if a fluid-like quark-gluon plasma forms in O-O and Ne-Ne collisions at √sNN = 5.36 TeV, the hybrid approach will produce a characteristic decrease of anisotropic flow coefficients v₂ and v₃ with increasing centrality, opposite to the trend seen in non-collective models; however, the absolute magnitude of these flow results is highly parameter dependent, particularly on the shear viscosity over entropy ratio.

## Method

The authors simulate O-O and Ne-Ne collisions at √sNN = 5.36 TeV using three models: the SMASH-vHLLE hybrid approach (hadronic transport initial state, 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics, Cooper-Frye particlization, SMASH afterburner), the pure hadronic cascade SMASH, and Angantyr (a Pythia-based model treating nucleon-nucleon sub-collisions independently). Initial nuclear configurations use both Woods-Saxon distributions and α-clustered configurations from Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) for ¹⁶O (tetrahedron shape) and ²⁰Ne (bowling-pin shape). Anisotropic flow coefficients are computed using multiparticle cumulant methods with subevent rapidity gaps to reduce nonflow contributions.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: √sNN = 5.36 TeV for both O-O and Ne-Ne collisions
- The hybrid model with temperature-dependent η/s (decreasing with T, reaching zero in hot cells) is compared to a constant η/s = 0.08 simulation
- The nuclear modification factor RAA from the hybrid model shows enhancement at intermediate pT between 2 and 5 GeV, with larger enhancement for baryons than mesons
- v₂{2} from the hybrid model is largest in central to mid-central collisions and decreases for less central collisions, while SMASH transport and Angantyr show the opposite trend (increasing flow in more peripheral collisions)
- The α-clustered configuration of ²⁰Ne increases both ε₂ and ε₃ compared to Woods-Saxon, while the α-clustered ¹⁶O decreases eccentricity
- Four-particle cumulants from SMASH transport and Angantyr are close to zero, confirming their flow signals stem from nonflow contributions

## Context

This work extends hybrid modeling approaches previously applied to larger systems like Pb-Pb and Au-Au (Hirano 2012, Petersen 2014) to the smaller O-O and Ne-Ne systems, motivated by observations of collectivity in p-p collisions (CMS 2010) and p-Pb collisions (ALICE 2012). The study makes predictions for the light ions run at the CERN LHC in July 2025, enabling experimental tests of where the onset of QGP formation occurs. The α-clustered nuclear configurations from NLEFT (Giacalone 2024) allow investigation of how nuclear structure imprints on final-state QGP signatures, building on prior work showing this effect in hybrid approaches (Giacalone 2024, Prasad 2024).
