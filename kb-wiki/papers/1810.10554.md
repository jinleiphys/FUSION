# Prescaling and far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamics in the quark-gluon plasma

> Prescaling is a far-from-equilibrium phenomenon which describes the rapid establishment of a universal scaling form of distributions much before the universal values of their scaling exponents are realized. We consider the example of the spatio-temporal evolution of the quark-gluon plasma explored in heavy-ion collisions at sufficiently high energies. Solving QCD kinetic theory with elastic and inelastic processes, we demonstrate that the gluon and quark distributions very quickly adapt a self-similar scaling form, which is independent of initial condition details and system parameters. The dynamics in the prescaling regime is then fully encoded in a few time-dependent scaling exponents, whose slow evolution gives rise to far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamic behavior.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that far-from-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma evolution exhibits "prescaling," where distribution functions rapidly adopt a universal self-similar form much before the scaling exponents themselves reach their universal constant values; the dynamics in this regime is encoded in a few slowly evolving time-dependent exponents, giving rise to hydrodynamic behavior far from equilibrium.

## Method

The authors solved the leading-order QCD kinetic theory equations including both elastic (2-to-2) and inelastic (1-to-2) collision processes for gluons and quarks in a longitudinally expanding system, using the numerical setup of Kurkela and collaborators. They extracted time-dependent scaling exponents from multiple sets of integral moments of the distribution functions and verified prescaling by rescaling the distributions with these time-dependent exponents at early times.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon occupancy parameter sigma_0 = 0.1 and 0.6, with coupling g = 10^-3 (checked for g = 10^-3 to 10^-1)
- Initial time tau_0 Q_s = 70, reference time tau_ref Q_s = 7000
- Late-time fitted exponents: alpha ≈ -0.73, beta ≈ -0.01, gamma ≈ 0.29
- Number of colors N_c = 3, number of quark flavors N_f = 3
- Gluon degeneracy nu_g = 16, quark degeneracy nu_q = 6
- Initial anisotropy parameter xi = 2

## Context

This work extends previous classical-statistical lattice simulations of nonthermal fixed points in QCD (Berges 2013) and the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario (Baier 2000) to full leading-order QCD kinetic theory with both elastic and inelastic processes. It introduces the concept of prescaling, previously explored in Bose gases (Schmied 2018), to the quark-gluon plasma context. The results enable a new far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamic description based on expansion around a nonthermal fixed-point distribution, complementing conventional hydrodynamics around thermal equilibrium and anisotropic hydrodynamic formulations (Romatschke 2003, Martinez 2012).
