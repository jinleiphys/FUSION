# Pion Production via Proton Synchrotron Radiation in Strong Magnetic Fields in Relativistic Field Theory: Scaling Relations and Angular Distributions

> We study pion production by proton synchrotron radiation in the presence of a strong magnetic field when the Landau numbers of the initial and final protons are $n_{i,f} \sim 10^4 - 10^5$. We find in our relativistic field theory calculations that the pion decay width depends only on the field strength parameter which previously was only conjectured based upon semi-classical arguments. Moreover, we also find new results that the decay width satisfies a robust scaling relation, and that the polar angular distribution of emitted pion momenta is very narrow and can be easily obtained. This scaling implies that one can infer the decay width in more realistic magnetic fields of $10^{15}$G, where $n_{i,f} \sim 10^{12} - 10^{13}$, from the results for $n_{i,f} \sim 10^4 - 10^5$. The resultant pion intensity and angular distributions for realistic magnetic field strengths are presented and their physical implications discussed.

## Key claim

The paper confirms via relativistic quantum field theory that the pion decay width from proton synchrotron radiation depends only on the field strength parameter \(\chi = e B e_i / m_p^3\) and the ratio \(\Delta n_{if}/n_i\), a scaling relation previously only conjectured from semi-classical arguments; this scaling holds for Landau numbers \(n_i \sim 10^4 - 10^5\) and allows extrapolation to realistic magnetar fields of \(10^{15}\) G where \(n_i \sim 10^{12} - 10^{13}\).

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for a proton in a uniform magnetic field including the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM), using the Green's function method and pseudo-vector pion-nucleon coupling to compute the differential decay width for the transition \(p \rightarrow p + \pi\). They evaluate the harmonic oscillator overlap integrals \(\mathcal{M}(n_i, n_f)\) numerically for initial Landau numbers \(n_i = 5 \times 10^3\) to \(10^5\) at magnetic field strengths \(B \sim 10^{17} - 10^{18}\) G, then apply the discovered scaling relation to extrapolate to \(B = 10^{15}\) G and initial proton energies of 2 and 4 TeV.

## Key numbers

- The scaling relation holds for initial Landau numbers \(n_i \gtrsim 6 \times 10^4\), with results for \(n_i = 6 \times 10^4\) and \(n_i = 10^5\) being nearly indistinguishable.
- Peak positions in the decay width occur at \(\Delta n_{if} / n_i \approx 0.3 - 0.4\) for \(\chi = 0.02\) and \(\chi = 0.07\).
- The AMM enhances the decay width by about a factor of 50 compared to calculations without AMM, an effect that persists for all magnetic field strengths and proton energies.
- For \(B = 10^{15}\) G, the total decay width scales as \(\Gamma_{p\pi}(n_i) \propto n_i\), in contrast to semi-classical results which underestimate the width.
- Photon luminosity distributions are presented for initial proton energies \(e_i - m_p = 2\) TeV and 4 TeV at \(B = 10^{15}\) G.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quantum calculation (Maruyama et al., 2014) which was limited to \(n_i \lesssim 300\) and \(B \approx 5 \times 10^{18}\) G, by developing a new numerical method and scaling relation that enables treatment of realistic magnetar field strengths. It resolves ambiguities among semi-classical calculations (Ginzburg 1965, Zharkov 1965, Tokara 1999, Baring et al. 1995) which gave different results for the strong-field limit, and shows that the semi-classical assumption \(\Delta n_{if} \ll n_i\) underestimates the decay width because the emitted pion carries a substantial fraction of the proton energy. The results enable calculation of photon luminosity distributions from pion decay in magnetar environments with \(B = 10^{15}\) G, relevant for interpreting observations of soft gamma repeaters, anomalous X-ray pulsars, and gamma-ray bursts.
