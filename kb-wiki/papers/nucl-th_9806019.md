# Proton drip-line nuclei in relativistic mean-field theory

> The position of the two-proton drip line has been calculated for even-even nuclei with $10 \leq Z \leq 82$ in the framework of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory. The current model uses the NL3 effective interaction in the mean-field Lagrangian and describes pairing correlations in the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) formalism. The predictions of the RMF theory are compared with those of the Hartree-Fock+BCS approach (with effective force Skyrme SIII) and the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) and with the available experimental information.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the NL3 effective interaction and BCS pairing predicts the location of the two-proton drip line for even-even nuclei with $10 \leq Z \leq 82$, but the authors note that the BCS approach may not be a sufficiently good approximation for estimating proton radii near the drip line, and that proton-neutron pairing correlations (not included in the model) could have a strong influence on masses for $N \approx Z$ nuclei.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for nucleons and Klein-Gordon equations for mesons using an axially-deformed RMF code with oscillator expansion (12 shells for fermions, 20 for bosons), employing the NL3 effective interaction. Pairing correlations were treated in the BCS formalism with constant pairing gaps from the prescription of Moller and Nix (1992). The two-proton separation energies $S_{2p}$ were calculated for even-even isotopic chains from $Z=10$ to $Z=82$, and the most proton-rich nuclei with $S_{2p} > 0$ were identified as the drip-line nuclei.

## Key numbers

* The rms deviation between calculated and experimental binding energies is 3.1 MeV, based on comparison with the 1995 Atomic Mass Adjustment and other experimental data.
* The calculations predict approximately 60 unknown even-Z isotopes in the $10 \leq Z \leq 82$ region that are proton stable.
* In 14 of 37 cases (of even-Z elements), the proton drip line has apparently been reached experimentally.
* The RMF+BCS calculations were performed for over 1300 even-even nuclei on either side of the valley of stability (reported separately).
* For $^{100}$Sn, the calculated binding energy is 829.94 MeV compared to the experimental value of 825.2 $\pm$ 0.6 MeV.
* For $^{180}$Pb, the calculated binding energy is 1394.17 MeV compared to the experimental value of 1390.65 $\pm$ 0.03 MeV.

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of the proton drip line using the RMF theory, which had previously been applied mainly to stable and neutron-rich nuclei. The predictions are compared with those from the Hartree-Fock+BCS approach with Skyrme SIII force (Tajima, 1996) and the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller, Nix, Myers, and Swiatecki (1995) and Moller, Nix, and Kratz (1997). The results enable experimental planning for discovering the approximately 60 predicted unknown proton-stable even-Z isotopes and provide a baseline for future deformed Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations, which were not yet available at the time of this work.
