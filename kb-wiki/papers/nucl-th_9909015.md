# Thermodynamics of multi-boson phenomena

> Using the method of locally equilibrium statistical operator we consider the thermalized relativistic quantum fields in an oscillatory trap. We compare this thermal picture of the confined boson gas with non-relativistic model of independent factorized sources. We find that they are equivalent in the limit of very large effective sizes $R$, more exactly, when the Compton wave length $1/m$ and thermal wave length $1/\sqrt{% mT}$ are much smaller than {\}$R$ . Under this conditions we study the influence of Bose condensation in finite volumes on the structure of the Wigner function, momentum spectra and correlation function.

## Key claim

Using Richardson’s exact solution of the reduced BCS model, the authors show that for ultrasmall superconducting grains with randomly-spaced levels, pairing correlations never vanish no matter how large the mean level spacing becomes, and the crossover from the bulk superconducting regime to the fluctuation-dominated regime is completely smooth, contradicting the abrupt vanishing found in earlier grand-canonical mean-field treatments.

## Method

The authors numerically solved Richardson’s exact algebraic equations for the reduced BCS Hamiltonian with both uniformly-spaced and randomly-spaced (Gaussian orthogonal ensemble) non-degenerate levels, for half-filled bands of up to 260 electrons. They computed exact ground state energies, condensation energies, and spectroscopic gaps for even and odd grains, then performed ensemble averages over 150–1000 random matrix realizations to obtain mean values and variances.

## Key numbers

- For uniformly-spaced levels with coupling constant λ = 0.224, the bulk gap is Δ = ω_D / sinh(1/λ) (implicitly defined).
- The DMRG results of Dukelsky and Sierra reproduce the exact solution with a relative error of less than 10⁻⁴ for n ≤ 400.
- For randomly-spaced levels, the variance of the even condensation energy δE^C_0 ≈ 2 δE^C_1 ≈ Δ/2.
- The critical level spacings found by Smith and Ambegaokar using grand-canonical mean-field BCS were ⟨d^c_1⟩ = 1.8 Δ and ⟨d^c_0⟩ ≈ 14 Δ, but these are artifacts of the mean-field treatment.
- The ensemble size was 1000 for 24 ≤ N ≤ 40, and varied between 700 and 150 for 40 ≤ N ≤ 260.

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing correlations in ultrasmall superconducting grains, following experiments by Ralph, Black, and Tinkham (1996) and earlier theoretical work by von Delft et al. (1996), Braun et al. (1997), Smith and Ambegaokar (1996), Matveev and Larkin (1997), Mastellone, Falci, and Fazio (1998), Berger and Halperin (1998), and Dukelsky and Sierra (1999). The exact solution, originally developed by Richardson (1963–1977) in nuclear physics, provides a benchmark to validate approximate methods and enables exact study of how level statistics affect pairing correlations, which was previously inaccessible. The results enable future work on finite-temperature extensions and more realistic grain models.
