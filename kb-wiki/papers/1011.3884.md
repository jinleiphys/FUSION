# An Overview of Meson-Nuclear Physics

> This conference covers an extremely broad range of topics and in just a few pages it is impossible to even touch on all the areas which will be discussed. We have chosen to summarise just one area where there has recently been impressive progress, namely our quantitative understanding of strangeness in the nucleon. We also discuss a couple of examples where theoretical progress is of direct importance for future experiments as well as for the interpretation of hitherto anomalous results. Finally we make some remarks on meson and baryon spectroscopy and the exciting array of new facilities that are coming on-line in the near future.

## Key claim

The paper argues that recent theoretical and lattice QCD calculations have produced quantitatively small values for strangeness contributions to nucleon properties (below 1% for magnetic moment and charge radius), contradicting earlier speculation of larger effects, and that a new value for the strange sigma commutator around 30 MeV dramatically reduces predicted neutralino-nucleon cross sections for dark matter searches.

## Method

The author reviews recent lattice QCD calculations combined with chiral extrapolation techniques and finite-range regulated effective field theory to determine strange quark contributions to nucleon form factors and the strange sigma commutator. The paper also discusses the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for nuclear matter, which uses hadron structure at the quark level to calculate density-dependent forces without new parameters, and its application to the EMC effect and hypernuclear physics.

## Key numbers

- Strange magnetic moment and charge radius of the proton are below 1% of the corresponding proton values, confirmed by direct lattice calculation (Doi 2009)
- Strange sigma commutator value around 30 MeV (Young and Thomas 2009), an order of magnitude smaller than commonly used values
- Lattice QCD calculation of the combination ⟨x(s+¯s)⟩ yields 2.7 ± 0.3% (Kentucky group, Deka 2008)
- Total gluon spin contribution is less than about one half at a scale of a few GeV² (RHIC data)
- Ξ-hypernuclei are predicted to be bound by up to 20 MeV within the QMC model (Tsushima 2009)
- The Gluex experiment at JLab should access states up to 2.6–2.8 GeV

## Context

This conference summary extends the work of Leinweber (1999, 2004, 2006) on indirect determinations of strange nucleon properties and confirms the direct lattice calculation of Doi (2009). The new small value for the strange sigma commutator (Young and Thomas 2009) contradicts earlier larger estimates and has major implications for dark matter searches as discussed by Giedt (2009), reducing predicted neutralino-nucleon cross sections by at least an order of magnitude. The paper also discusses the QMC model of Guichon (1995, 2006) and its application to the NuTeV anomaly (Zeller 2001) and the EMC effect, with a recent reanalysis by Bentz (2009) applying isovector corrections to the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation.
