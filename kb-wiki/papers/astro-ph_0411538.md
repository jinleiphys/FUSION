# Strangelet propagation and cosmic ray flux

> The galactic propagation of cosmic ray strangelets is described and the resulting flux is calculated for a wide range of parameters as a prerequisite for strangelet searches in lunar soil and with an Earth orbiting magnetic spectrometer, AMS-02. While the inherent uncertainties are large, flux predictions at a measurable level are obtained for reasonable choices of parameters if strange quark matter is absolutely stable. This allows a direct test of the strange matter hypothesis.

# Strangelet propagation and cosmic ray flux

## Key claim
If strange quark matter is absolutely stable, the galactic flux of cosmic ray strangelets may be high enough to be detectable by lunar soil searches or the AMS-02 magnetic spectrometer, but the predictions carry order-of-magnitude uncertainties due to poorly constrained astrophysical parameters such as the binary strange star collision rate, the mass released per event, and the strangelet baryon number distribution.

## Method
The paper models strangelet propagation in the Galaxy using a steady-state leaky-box diffusion equation that includes a source term from supernova shock acceleration (powerlaw in rigidity with index α≈2.2), spatial diffusion and escape from the Galaxy, ionization and pion-production energy losses, spallation on interstellar hydrogen, and reacceleration by passing supernova shocks. The source normalization assumes a galactic production rate of 10⁻¹⁰ M⊙ yr⁻¹ from binary strange star coalescences, with all strangelets released at a single baryon number A. Solar modulation is applied via a 500 MeV electrostatic potential model, and geomagnetic cutoff is considered for Earth-orbiting detectors.

## Key numbers
- Galactic binary strange star coalescence rate: 83.0⁻⁶⁶·¹⁺²⁰⁹·¹ Myr⁻¹ (95% confidence interval), corresponding to one collision every 3,000–60,000 years.
- Canonical galactic strangelet mass production rate: Ṁ = 10⁻¹⁰ M⊙ yr⁻¹.
- Strangelet mass per baryon assumed: mc² = 0.93 GeV A.
- For color-flavor locked (CFL) strangelets with mₛ = 150 MeV: Z = 0.3 A²/³.
- For "ordinary" strangelets with mₛ = 150 MeV: Z = 0.1 A for A ≪ 700, and Z = 8 A¹/³ for A ≫ 700.
- Escape column density parameters: ξ₀ = 12.8 g/cm² at R₀ = 4.7 GV, with δ = 0.8 for R < R₀ and δ = −0.6 for R > R₀.
- Spallation time scale: τ_spallation = 2×10⁷ yr / (n β A²/³).
- Rule-of-thumb total flux for CFL strangelets (solar modulation dominated): F_total ≈ 4×10⁵ m⁻² yr⁻¹ sterad⁻¹ A⁻¹·⁴⁶⁷ Φ₅₀₀⁻⁰·⁶ Λ, where Λ encodes the product of uncertain astrophysical parameters.
- Rule-of-thumb total flux for CFL strangelets in terms of Z: F_total ≈ 2.8×10⁴ m⁻² yr⁻¹ sterad⁻¹ Z⁻²·² Φ₅₀₀⁻⁰·⁶ Λ.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for strangelet production from binary strange star collisions (Madsen 1989, Friedman 1990) by providing the first systematic propagation calculation across a wide parameter range, enabling quantitative predictions for the AMS-02 experiment (Aguilar 2002, Sandweiss 2004) and lunar soil searches. It contrasts with the numerical simulation of Medina-Tanco (1996), which assumed nucleon stripping rather than complete spallation destruction and studied only two specific injection cases; the present results are consistent with the possibility of a measurable flux. The paper enables direct experimental tests of the strange matter hypothesis (Bodmer 1971, Witten 1984, Farhi 1984) by providing flux estimates that can be compared with upcoming detector sensitivities.
