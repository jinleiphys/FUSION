# Quantum Monte Carlo study of inhomogeneous neutron matter

> We present an ab-initio study of neutron drops. We use Quantum Monte Carlo techniques to calculate the energy up to 54 neutrons in different external potentials, and we compare the results with Skyrme forces. We also calculate the rms radii and radial densities, and we find that a re-adjustment of the gradient term in Skyrme is needed in order to reproduce the properties of these systems given by the ab-initio calculation. By using the ab-initio results for neutron drops for close- and open-shell configurations, we suggest how to improve Skyrme forces when dealing with systems with large isospin-asymmetries like neutron-rich nuclei.

# Quantum Monte Carlo study of inhomogeneous neutron matter

## Key claim
The paper presents ab-initio Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron drops (neutrons confined by external potentials) and shows that existing Skyrme forces systematically overbind these systems, requiring a re-adjustment of the gradient, pairing, and spin-orbit terms to reproduce the microscopic results. The claim is conditional on the use of the AV8' two-body potential plus the UIX three-body force, and the comparison is limited to the SLY4 parametrization and a few other Skyrme forces.

## Method
The authors solve the many-body ground state of neutron drops using Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) for up to 12 neutrons and Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) for up to 54 neutrons, with neutrons confined by either a harmonic oscillator potential or a Woods-Saxon potential. The Hamiltonian includes the Argonne AV8' two-body potential and the UIX three-body force, with neutrons treated as non-relativistic point-like particles. The QMC results are compared with Skyrme force calculations, and the gradient, pairing, and spin-orbit terms of the SLY4 force are adjusted to match the ab-initio energies at closed-shell configurations (N=8, 20, 40) and open-shell configurations.

## Key numbers
- Energies of neutron drops in a harmonic oscillator well with hbar-omega = 10 MeV and 5 MeV are calculated for up to 54 neutrons; the two QMC methods (GFMC and AFDMC) agree within 1% for hbar-omega = 10 MeV and within 4% for hbar-omega = 5 MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters are V0 = -35.5 MeV, r0 = 3 fm, and a = 1.1 fm, with hbar-squared/m = 41.44 MeV-fm-squared.
- The AFDMC method has been used to simulate up to 114 neutrons in homogeneous systems and up to 70 neutrons in an external well.
- The adjusted SLY4 force reproduces QMC energies for neutron drops in a Woods-Saxon well as a prediction, after being adjusted only to harmonic oscillator results.

## Context
This work extends previous ab-initio studies of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes modeled as neutron drops (Chang 2004, Pieper 2005, Gandolfi 2006) and calcium isotopes (Gandolfi 2008), and provides benchmark data for calibrating energy density functionals used in nuclear structure and neutron star crust calculations. The results contradict the predictive power of standard Skyrme forces like SLY4 (Chabanat 1995) for highly neutron-rich systems, and enable improved density functionals such as the UNEDF0 and UNEDF1 forces (Kortelainen 2012), which show remarkable agreement with QMC results despite not including neutron drop energies as a constraint. The work also connects to the equation of state of pure neutron matter from AFDMC (Gandolfi 2009, 2010) and GFMC (Gezerlis and Carlson 2008, 2010), which is relevant for neutron star structure with masses above 2 solar masses (Demorest 2010, Steiner 2012).
