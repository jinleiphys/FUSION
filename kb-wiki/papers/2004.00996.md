# Spin-parity of the 13.35 MeV state and high-lying states around 20 MeV in excitation energy in $^{12}$C nucleus

> Study of the $^{11}$B($^{3}$He,d)$^{12}$C reaction at incident $^{3}$He energy E$_{lab}$ = 25 MeV has been performed at the K-130 cyclotron at the University of Jyväskylä, Finland. Differential cross sections have been measured for the 13.35 MeV state and for the states with excitation energy around 20 MeV. The data were analyzed with the DWBA method. A tentative assignment, 4$^{-}$, is given for the state at 13.35 MeV. For the state at 20.98 MeV, the spin-parity 3$^{-}$ and the isospin T = 0 are assigned for the first time. Our model description of the broad state at 21.6 MeV is consistent with the previous assignments of isospin T = 0 and spin-parity of 2$^{+}$ and 3$^{-}$. The excited state at 22.4 MeV may have possible spin-parities of either 6$^{+}$ or 5$^{-}$. The collected statistics was insufficient to solve this question.

## Key claim

The paper provides a tentative assignment of spin-parity 4⁻ for the 13.35 MeV state in ¹²C, but the DWBA analysis of the transfer reaction alone cannot distinguish between 4⁻ and 2⁻ with equal probability; the preference for 4⁻ relies on additional arguments from inelastic alpha scattering and radius estimates. For the state at 20.98 MeV, spin-parity 3⁻ and isospin T=0 are assigned for the first time.

## Method

The ¹¹B(³He,d)¹²C proton-transfer reaction was measured at an incident ³He energy of 25 MeV using the K-130 cyclotron at the University of Jyväskylä, with three ΔE-E detector telescopes covering a center-of-mass angular range of 4° to 60°. Differential cross sections were extracted for the ground state and eight excited states of ¹²C, and the data were analyzed with finite-range distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) using the FRESCO code, with optical model parameters taken from global potentials. For the 13.35 MeV state, additional analysis of inelastic α-particle scattering data at 110 MeV was performed using a cluster-model DWBA and the Modified Diffraction Model (MDM) to estimate the transferred angular momentum and nuclear radius.

## Key numbers

- Incident ³He beam energy: Elab = 25 MeV
- Center-of-mass angular range for measured differential cross sections: 4° to 60°
- Total energy resolution: 80–120 keV
- Target thickness: 0.275 mg/cm² of ¹¹B foil with 95% enrichment
- Beam intensity: about 20 particle nA
- For the 13.35 MeV state, the MDM analysis gave an rms radius Rrms ≈ 3 fm, close to the radius of the 3⁻ 9.64 MeV state (Rrms = 2.88 ± 0.11 fm)
- The state at 13.75 ± 0.12 MeV (Γ = 1.4 ± 0.15 MeV) was previously identified as 4⁺
- The state at 22.4(2) MeV was previously reported as a candidate for Jπ = 5⁻

## Context

This work extends studies of rotational bands in ¹²C, particularly the negative-parity band proposed by Ref. [15] (based on D₃h symmetry) containing the 3⁻ state at 9.64 MeV and the 4⁻ state at 13.35 MeV, and the Hoyle-state rotational band including the 0⁺₂ state at 7.65 MeV, the 2⁺₂ state near 9.9 MeV, and the 4⁺₂ state at 13.75 MeV. The results enable further identification of high-lying states around 20 MeV as members of these bands, with the 22.4 MeV state proposed as either the 6⁺ member of the Hoyle band or a 5⁻ member of the negative-parity band, though the possibility of two unresolved states at that energy cannot be excluded.
