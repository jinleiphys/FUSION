# Universal Centrality Dependence of Particle Multiplicities in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Starting from the light-cone plasma (LCP) distribution of gluons produced in proton-proton collisions, we derive the invariant charged-particle yield dN/dyd^2pT in heavy-ion collisions. Multiple scattering of partons in the other nucleus leads to pT broadening, which depends on the centrality of the collision. The resulting change of the LCP parameters determines the distributions of charged particles. The charged-particle multiplicity dN/deta at eta=0 normalized to the number of participating nucleons has a universal centrality dependence, common to RHIC and LHC. This universal increase is of geometrical origin. Such a universality can be broken by the energy dependent coherence effect, shadowing and mutual boosting of the saturation scales. However, we found these effects to essentially compensate each other, leaving the centrality dependence of multiplicity approximately universal.

## Key claim

The charged-particle multiplicity per participating nucleon in heavy-ion collisions exhibits a universal centrality dependence that is common to both RHIC and LHC energies, arising from geometrical effects. This universality is only approximate because energy-dependent coherence effects, shadowing, and mutual boosting of saturation scales tend to compensate each other, leaving the centrality dependence nearly universal.

## Method

The authors start from a light-cone plasma (LCP) distribution of gluons derived via a maximum-entropy principle for proton-proton collisions, which yields a Bose-Einstein distribution parameterized by an effective transverse temperature and a softness parameter. They extend this to nucleus-nucleus collisions by scaling with the number of participating nucleons and calculating the transverse-momentum broadening of gluons from multiple scattering using the dipole-nucleon cross section and the transport coefficient, which depends on the nuclear profile function and the parton-level center-of-mass energy. The resulting change in the LCP parameters determines the charged-particle distributions, and the centrality dependence is obtained by eliminating the impact parameter in favor of the number of participants.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at √s = 0.20 TeV: transverse size L⊥ = 1.34 fm, effective transverse temperature λ_pp = 0.183 GeV, softness w_pp = 3.44, mean transverse momentum squared ⟨p⊥²⟩ = 0.12 GeV², charged-particle multiplicity dN_ch/dη at η=0 = 2.20.
- For pp collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV: transverse size L⊥ = 1.28 fm, effective transverse temperature λ_pp = 0.252 GeV, softness w_pp = 6.81, mean transverse momentum squared ⟨p⊥²⟩ = 0.23 GeV², charged-particle multiplicity dN_ch/dη at η=0 = 4.56.
- Averaged transport coefficient for gluons: 11.26 for √s_NN = 0.2 TeV and 21.15 for √s_NN = 2.76 TeV.
- Power-law fit parameters for the mean profile function τ_B(N_part): n0 = 0.1155 fm⁻², a = 0.435 for A=197 (Au); n0 = 0.0775 fm⁻², a = 0.491 for A=208 (Pb).
- Infrared scales from self-consistency: Q₀²(0.2 TeV) = 0.35 GeV², Q₀²(2.76 TeV) = 0.41 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the maximum-entropy LCP approach developed by Pirner et al. (2011) for proton-proton collisions to heavy-ion collisions by incorporating transverse-momentum broadening from multiple scattering. The universal centrality dependence found here contrasts with expectations from models where energy-dependent coherence effects, shadowing, and mutual boosting of saturation scales would break such universality, but the authors find these effects approximately cancel. The results enable a geometrical interpretation of the centrality dependence of particle multiplicities at both RHIC and LHC energies, and the framework can be compared with more detailed hydrodynamical calculations such as those by Kolb and Heinz (2003).
