# Effective field theory for collective rotations and vibrations of triaxially deformed nuclei

> The effective field theory (EFT) for triaxially deformed even-even nuclei is generalized to include the vibrational degrees of freedom. The pertinent Hamiltonian is constructed up to next-to-leading order. The leading order part describes the vibrational motion, and the NLO part couples rotations to vibrations. The applicability of the EFT Hamiltonian is examined through the description of the energy spectra of the ground state bands, $γ$-bands, and $K=4$ bands in the $^{108, 110, 112}$Ru isotopes. It is found that by taking into account the vibrational degrees of freedom, the deviations for high-spin states in the $γ$-band observed in the EFT with only rotational degrees of freedom disappear. This supports the importance of including vibrational degrees of freedom in the EFT formulation for the collective motion of triaxially deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

By including vibrational degrees of freedom in the effective field theory (EFT) for triaxially deformed even-even nuclei, the deviations for high-spin states in the gamma-band that were observed in the earlier EFT with only rotational degrees of freedom disappear, supporting the importance of vibrations in the EFT formulation.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian and Hamiltonian up to next-to-leading order (NLO) using Nambu-Goldstone fields parametrizing the coset space SO(3)/D2, where the three Euler angles describe global rotations and three additional fields x, y, z describe small-amplitude vibrations of the nuclear surface. The leading order (LO) part describes the vibrational motion as uncoupled anisotropic harmonic oscillators, while the NLO part couples rotations to vibrations. The Hamiltonian was applied to describe the energy spectra of ground state bands, gamma-bands, and K=4 bands in the 108,110,112Ru isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The rotational energy scale xi is typically about 80 keV, while the vibrational energy scale Omega is typically about 1 MeV, giving a small expansion parameter sqrt(xi/Omega) much less than 1.
- The vibrational frequencies for each mode lambdamu are given by Omega_{lambdamu}^x = sqrt([lambda(lambda+1)D_a + mu^2 D_d]/B_a), with analogous expressions for the y and z modes.
- The energy eigenvalues of the LO vibrational Hamiltonian are E_Omega = sum_{lambdamu} [(n_{lambdamu}^x + 1/2)Omega_{lambdamu}^x + (n_{lambdamu}^y + 1/2)Omega_{lambdamu}^y + (n_{lambdamu}^z + 1/2)Omega_{lambdamu}^z].

## Context

This work extends the EFT for triaxially deformed nuclei developed by Chen et al. (2017) for pure rotations, which had shown deviations for high-spin states in gamma-bands when compared to data for 102-112Ru isotopes. The inclusion of vibrations follows the approach of Papenbrock and collaborators (2011-2016) who developed EFT for axially symmetric nuclei. The results are compared to five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) calculations based on covariant density functional theory (CDFT), and the extended EFT enables a systematic treatment of both rotational and vibrational collective motion in triaxial nuclei with quantifiable uncertainties.
