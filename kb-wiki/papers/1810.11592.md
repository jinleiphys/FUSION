# An event mixing method with invariant-mass/energy hierarchy correspondence cut for Bose-Einstein correlations in $ππX$ system

> A new event mixing constraint, namely invariant-mass/energy hierarchy correspondence (IMEHC) cut, is introduced for the low-multiplicity event mixing technique for the purpose of measuring Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) in exclusive reactions with $ππX$ final state particles.The mixing cut is relevant to the hierarchy of the invariant mass of $πX$ system and two bosons energy hierarchy. Numerical tests are performed to check the validity of the new mixing method. As long as the measurements of BEC parameters $r_0$ and $λ_2$ are considered, this new mixing method is effective to observe BEC effects and the systematic bias of $r_0$ and $λ_2$ are smaller than the previously proposed mixing cut.

## Key claim

The new invariant-mass/energy hierarchy correspondence (IMEHC) cut improves the systematic bias of the chaoticity parameter λ₂ compared to the previously proposed mixing method using only missing mass consistency (MMC) and energy sum order (ESO) cuts, but the improvement is still very limited and the method still introduces systematic bias that requires correction in practical application.

## Method

The author generated six γp → π⁰π⁰p event samples with and without Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) at incident photon energies from 1.0 to 1.15 GeV using the ROOT TGenPhaseSpace utility. The IMEHC cut randomly selects between two sub-cuts with equal probability: one requiring exchanged pions to have the same hierarchy in the invariant mass m(π,X) of the πX system, and the other requiring exchanged pions to have the same energy hierarchy. The mixing method also retains the previously proposed MMC and ESO cuts. Correlation functions were fitted with a modified parametrization C_BEC(Q) = N(1+αQ²)(1+λ₂e^{-r₀²Q²}) to account for a Q²-dependent pattern in the non-BEC reference sample.

## Key numbers

- Input BEC parameters for the six simulated samples were set to r₀ = 0.8 fm and λ₂ = 1.0.
- Summing over six energy bins (1.0, 1.03, 1.06, 1.09, 1.12, and 1.15 GeV), the error-weighted mean r₀ from the new mixing method was 0.84 ± 0.03, about 8% overestimated compared to the input mean of 0.78 ± 0.01.
- The error-weighted mean λ₂ from the new mixing method was 0.78 ± 0.03, about 18% underestimated compared to the input mean of 0.95 ± 0.02.
- The systematic bias of λ₂ was reduced from 22% (with the previously proposed MMC+ESO method from He (2018)) to 18% with the new IMEHC method.

## Context

This work extends the event mixing technique for BEC measurements in exclusive low-multiplicity reactions, specifically addressing the γp → π⁰π⁰p reaction at incident photon energies around 1 GeV. It improves upon the earlier mixing method proposed by He (2016) that used MMC and PE cuts (which discarded about 40% of events), and the subsequent ESO cut replacement by He (2018) which had large systematic errors for BEC parameters. The method enables measurement of the spatial extension of excited baryons decaying to two pions, but future work is needed to improve accuracy and find new mixing cuts to avoid the Q²-dependent fitting procedure.
