# Hadronic aspects of exotic baryons

> In this talk I look into three different topics, addressing first the possibility that the $Θ^+$ is a bound state of $K πN$, exploiting the results of this study to find out the contribution of two meson and one baryon components in the baryon antidecuplet and in the third place I present results on a new resonant exotic baryonic state which appears as dynamically generated by the Weinberg Tomozawa $ΔK$ interaction.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark can be understood as a bound state of $K\pi N$, finding that the attractive interaction is insufficient to bind the system; however, a new exotic baryonic resonance is predicted as a dynamically generated $\Delta K$ state with strangeness $S=+1$, isospin $I=1$, and spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^-$.

## Method

The authors construct the $K\pi N \to K\pi N$ scattering amplitude using a chiral unitary approach, including correlated $K\pi$ pairs (the $\kappa$ meson) interacting with the nucleon via the Weinberg-Tomozawa term from chiral perturbation theory. For the $\Delta K$ system, they use the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for the baryon decuplet and pseudoscalar meson octet, solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a cutoff regularization ($q_{max}=700$ MeV) to search for poles in the scattering amplitude. The regularization scale is fixed by reproducing known resonances in other strangeness channels, specifically the $\Xi(1820)$ and $\Sigma(1670)$.

## Key numbers

- The $\Theta^+$ mass is around 1540 MeV, and the $K\pi N$ threshold lies approximately 30 MeV above this mass.
- Artificially increasing the attractive potential by a factor of 5-6 is required to produce a pole at $\sqrt{s}=1540$ MeV with a width of $\Gamma=40$ MeV.
- The $K\pi N$ component contributes attractive self-energies of order 100-150 MeV to the antidecuplet baryons, with an energy splitting between members of about 20 MeV.
- A pole is found in the second Riemann sheet for the $\Delta K$ system at $\sqrt{s}=1635$ MeV for $I=1$, $S=+1$.
- The cutoff used in the loop function is $q_{max}=700$ MeV, reproducing the $\Xi(1820)$ and $\Sigma(1670)$ resonances.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to meson-baryon scattering (Kaiser 1995, Oset 1997, Oller 2000, Jido 2003, Nieves 2001) to the three-body $K\pi N$ system and to the decuplet-meson interaction. The $\Theta^+$ as a $K\pi N$ bound state was previously explored in chiral quark models by Bicudo (2003). The predicted $\Delta K$ resonance at 1635 MeV follows the same dynamical generation mechanism that successfully described the $\Xi(1820)$ and $\Sigma(1670)$ in Kolomeitsev (2003). The results enable experimental searches for the new exotic state via reactions such as $pp \to \Lambda \Delta^+ K^+$, $pp \to \Sigma^- \Delta^{++} K^+$, and $pp \to \Sigma^0 \Delta^{++} K^0$, and motivate further study of the $\Theta^+$ in nuclear matter where pionic medium polarization provides additional attraction of 50-100 MeV (Cabrera 2004).
