# Color superconductivity in quark matter

> A brief introduction into the properties of dense quark matter is given. Recently proposed gapless color superconducting phases of neutral and beta-equilibrated dense quark matter are discussed. The current status in the field is described, and the promising directions of the future research are outlined.

# Color superconductivity in quark matter

## Key claim
Dense quark matter at densities a few times nuclear saturation density (n₀ ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³) is expected to form color superconducting phases with gaps potentially as large as 100 MeV, but the specific ground state realized in compact stars depends sensitively on charge neutrality and β-equilibrium conditions, which force unequal Fermi momenta between pairing quarks and can lead to gapless phases that may themselves be chromomagnetically unstable.

## Method
The paper reviews the theoretical framework for color superconductivity using both first-principles QCD at asymptotic densities and effective models (primarily the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with local four-fermion interactions). It analyzes the gap equation in the mean-field approximation for two-flavor (2SC) and three-flavor (color-flavor-locked, CFL) quark matter, then imposes charge neutrality and β-equilibrium conditions via chemical potentials for baryon number, electric charge, and color charge to study the resulting phases.

## Key numbers
- The color superconducting gap Δ can be as large as 100 MeV at baryon densities a few times n₀ ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³ (the normal nuclear density).
- In the NJL model, the coupling constant G_S = 5.01 GeV⁻² and the momentum cutoff Λ = 0.65 GeV are adjusted to reproduce vacuum QCD values F_π = 93 MeV and ⟨ūu⟩ = ⟨dd̄⟩ = (−250 MeV)³.
- For quark chemical potential μ between 400 MeV and 500 MeV, and diquark coupling strength G_D = η G_S with η between 0.7 and 1, the gap is of order 100 MeV.
- The Coulomb energy estimate for a quark matter core of radius R = 1 km with charge density n_Q ∼ 10⁻² e/fm³ gives E_Coulomb ∼ 10²⁶ M_⊙ c², enforcing near-exact charge neutrality.
- In β-equilibrated neutral two-flavor matter, the electron chemical potential μ_e ≈ 0.26 μ_u, giving electron density n_e ≈ 6 × 10⁻³ n_u.

## Context
This review summarizes the state of the field following the observation by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) that the color superconducting gap could be as large as 100 MeV, which opened the study of color superconductivity in compact stars. It extends earlier work on the 2SC phase (weak-coupling QCD calculations by Son, 1999) and the CFL phase (Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek, 1999) to include the effects of charge neutrality and β-equilibrium, which were shown by Alford and Rajagopal (2002) to disfavor the simple 2SC phase. The paper discusses the gapless 2SC and gapless CFL phases proposed by Shovkovy and Huang (2003) and Alford, Kouvaris, and Rajagopal (2004), and the subsequent discovery of chromomagnetic instabilities in these phases (by Huang and Shovkovy, 2004), which remain an open problem. The review enables future work on the QCD phase diagram at densities inaccessible to lattice QCD, and on observable signatures of color superconductivity in compact star cooling, rotation, and mass-radius relations.
