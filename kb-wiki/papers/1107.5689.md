# Study of nuclear dynamics of neutron-rich colliding pair at energy of vanishing flow

> We study nuclear dynamics at the energy of vanishing flow of neutron-rich systems having N/Z ratio 1.0, 1.6 and 2.0 throughout the mass range at semi central colliding geometry. In particular we study the behavior of average and maximum density with N/Z dependence of the system.

# Study of nuclear dynamics of neutron-rich colliding pair at energy of vanishing flow

## Key claim

The average and maximum nuclear densities reached in heavy-ion collisions at the energy of vanishing flow (EVF) decrease slightly with increasing N/Z ratio of the system across a wide mass range, with a slight exception for the lightest Ca+Ca systems.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Zr+Zr, Sn+Sn, and Xe+Xe series with N/Z ratios of 1.0, 1.6, and 2.0 at semi-central colliding geometry (impact parameter b/b_max = 0.2–0.4). Each reaction was simulated at its respective balance energy (EVF), and the time evolution of average density and maximum density was extracted.

## Key numbers

- Balance energies for the N/Z = 1.0 systems: 105 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, 98 MeV for ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni, 86 MeV for ⁸¹Zr+⁸¹Zr, 82 MeV for ¹⁰⁰Sn+¹⁰⁰Sn, and 76 MeV for ¹¹⁰Xe+¹¹⁰Xe.
- Balance energies for the N/Z = 1.6 systems: 85 MeV for ⁵²Ca+⁵²Ca, 82 MeV for ⁷²Ni+⁷²Ni, 74 MeV for ¹⁰⁴Zr+¹⁰⁴Zr, 72 MeV for ¹²⁹Sn+¹²⁹Sn, and 68 MeV for ¹⁴⁰Xe+¹⁴⁰Xe.
- Balance energies for the N/Z = 2.0 systems: 73 MeV for ⁶⁰Ca+⁶⁰Ca, 72 MeV for ⁸⁴Ni+⁸⁴Ni, 67 MeV for ¹²⁰Zr+¹²⁰Zr, 64 MeV for ¹⁵⁰Sn+¹⁵⁰Sn, and 61 MeV for ¹⁶²Xe+¹⁶²Xe.
- Both average and maximum densities decrease slightly with increasing N/Z ratio for all system masses, with a slight exception for the Ca+Ca series.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of isospin effects on the energy of vanishing flow by Pak (1997) and Gautam (2011) to examine the density and temperature reached in neutron-rich systems at EVF. Previous work by Gautam and collaborators studied isospin effects in EVF at all colliding geometries, but few studies had examined the density evolution at EVF for neutron-rich matter. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from radioactive ion beam facilities and provide constraints on the density dependence of the nuclear equation of state for neutron-rich matter.
