# Resonance decay effect on conserved number fluctuations in a hadron resonance gas model

> We study the effect of charged secondaries coming from resonance decay on the net-baryon, net-charge and net-strangeness fluctuations in high energy heavy-ion collisions within the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model. We emphasize the importance of including weak decays along with other resonance decays in the HRG, while comparing with the experimental observables. The effect of kinematic cuts on resonances and primordial particles on the conserved number fluctuations are also studied. The HRG model calculations with the inclusion of resonance decays and kinematical cuts are compared with the recent experimental data from STAR and PHENIX experiments. We find a good agreement between our model calculations and the experimental measurements for both net-proton and net-charge distributions.

## Key claim

The hadron resonance gas (HRG) model can reproduce experimental net-proton and net-charge cumulant ratios from STAR and PHENIX only when weak decays are included alongside strong resonance decays, and the agreement is energy-dependent: the model under-predicts net-proton kurtosis times variance at collision energies above 30 GeV, possibly due to resonance regeneration not captured by the model.

## Method

The authors compute generalized susceptibilities (up to fourth order) for net-baryon, net-charge, and net-strangeness within the HRG model using a grand canonical ensemble with freeze-out parameters parametrized as functions of center-of-mass energy. They implement resonance decay contributions using two approaches: an "average decay" scheme where only resonance number fluctuations are considered, and a "full decay" scheme that also includes fluctuations in the number of decay daughters. Weak decays of particles such as Lambda, Sigma, Xi, Omega, K0, and eta0 are explicitly included, and kinematic acceptance cuts in transverse momentum and pseudorapidity are applied to both primordial particles and resonances.

- Freeze-out baryon chemical potential parametrization: μ_B(√s_NN) = 1.308 / (1 + 0.273 √s_NN) (units not specified in text, presumably GeV)
- Freeze-out temperature parametrization: T_f(μ_B) = 0.166 − 0.139 μ_B^2 − 0.053 μ_B^4 (units not specified in text, presumably GeV)
- Strangeness chemical potential ratio: μ_S/μ_B ≈ 0.164 + 0.018 √s_NN (units not specified in text)
- The HRG model includes mesons and baryons of mass up to 2.5 GeV as listed in the particle data book
- 26 weakly decaying particles are considered as stable in the case without weak decays
- Experimental comparisons use net-proton data within |y| < 0.5 and p_T range 0.4 to 0.8 GeV/c (STAR), and net-charge data within |η| < 0.5 and p_T range 0.2 to 2.0 GeV/c (STAR) or |η| < 0.35 and p_T range 0.3 to 2.0 GeV/c (PHENIX)

## Context

This work extends the HRG baseline calculations of conserved number fluctuations by Nahrgang (2014) and Garg (2013) by explicitly including weak decays of particles that those studies treated as stable. The results provide a more realistic baseline for comparison with experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, particularly for net-proton and net-charge cumulant measurements from STAR (Adamczyk 2013, Adamczyk 2014) and PHENIX (Adare 2015). The finding that resonance decay effects are larger for net-charge than net-baryon, and that weak decays significantly affect higher-order cumulant ratios, enables more precise extraction of freeze-out parameters and better identification of possible critical point signals in future analyses.
