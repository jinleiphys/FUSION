# Probing the delicate balance of the spontaneous fission instability in sub-μs superheavy nucleus 252Rf

> Stimulated by the recent experimental discovery of the sub-$μ$s fission nucleus $^{252}$Rf [Phys. Rev. Lett. 134 (2025) 022501], we perform an improved configuration-constrained potential-energy-surface calculation, revealing the mechanism of intricate balance for the enhanced stability due to the high-$K$ (e.g., $K^π= 6^+$) isomer, possibly building on a shape isomeric state. The different deformation and coupling effects, such as triaxial $γ$, reflection-asymmetric $β_{3}$ and high-order $β_{6}$ deformations, are discussed for both ground state and isomeric state based on the corresponding potential-energy curves along the fission valley. In particular, it is pointed out for the first time that possible multipath decay, e.g., from the high-$K$ isomeric state to those states formed between potential energy surfaces of this isomeric state and the ground state during the fission process, may reduce the nuclear lifetime and balance the fission stability. These results elucidate not only the enhanced stability of the high-$K$ isomeric state, including the inversion of stability between it and the ground state, but also the limitation of the stability increase of such an isomeric state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the enhanced fission stability of the high‑\(K\) isomeric state in \(^{252}\mathrm{Rf}\) is limited by a possible multipath decay process: the isomer may decay to intermediate two‑quasineutron excited states that appear between the isomeric and ground‑state potential‑energy surfaces along the fission path, thereby reducing the nuclear lifetime and balancing the stability increase.

## Method

The authors performed configuration‑constrained potential‑energy‑surface (PES) calculations in a multidimensional deformation space \((\beta_2, \gamma, \beta_3, \beta_4, \beta_6)\) using the macroscopic‑microscopic Strutinsky method with a deformed Woods‑Saxon potential and the Lipkin‑Nagami pairing treatment. Both adiabatic and diabatic blocking schemes were employed to track the single‑particle orbitals that define the \(K^\pi = 6^+\) two‑quasineutron configuration. The potential‑energy curves were minimized over subsets of the deformation parameters to isolate the effects of triaxial \(\gamma\), reflection‑asymmetric \(\beta_3\), and high‑order \(\beta_6\) deformations on the fission barriers.

## Key numbers

* The ground state of \(^{252}\mathrm{Rf}\) has a superdeformed minimum at \(\beta_2 \approx 0.7\), which is \(1.41\ \mathrm{MeV}\) lower than the normally deformed minimum.
* For the \(K^\pi = 6^+\) isomeric state, the inner fission barrier increases by \(1.65\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from \(4.83\) to \(6.48\ \mathrm{MeV}\)) and the outer barrier increases by \(4.01\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (from \(2.67\) to \(6.68\ \mathrm{MeV}\)) relative to the ground state.
* The energy difference between the normally deformed and superdeformed minima decreases from \(1.41\ \mathrm{MeV}\) in the ground state to \(0.20\ \mathrm{MeV}\) in the \(K^\pi = 6^+\) isomeric state.
* The measured half‑life of the high‑\(K\) isomeric state is \(13^{+4}_{-3}\ \mu\mathrm{s}\), only about two orders of magnitude longer than the ground‑state half‑life of \(60^{+90}_{-30}\ \mathrm{ns}\).

## Context

This work is stimulated by the experimental discovery of the sub‑\(\mu\)s fission nucleus \(^{252}\mathrm{Rf}\) reported in Phys. Rev. Lett. 134 (2025) 022501. It extends previous configuration‑constrained PES studies of high‑\(K\) isomers in superheavy nuclei (e.g., Xu (2004), Peterson (2006), David (2015)) and of shape isomers in the actinide region (e.g., Bjørnholm (1980)). The paper introduces for the first time the concept of multipath decay from the high‑\(K\) isomeric state to intermediate excited states as a mechanism that limits the stability enhancement, and it enables future refinements of fission‑half‑life calculations and models of superheavy‑nucleus synthesis.
