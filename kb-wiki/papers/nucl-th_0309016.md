# Proton rich nuclei at and beyond the proton drip line in the Relativistic Mean Field theory

> Ground state properties of proton-rich odd-$Z$ nuclei in the region $55\le Z \le 73$ are studied in the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. The RMF equations are solved by using the expansion method in the Harmonic-Oscillator basis. In the particle-particle channel, we use the state-dependent BCS method with a zero-range $δ$-force, which has been proved to be effective even for neutron-rich nuclei. All the ground state properties, including the one-proton separation energies, the ground state deformations, the last occupied proton orbits and the locations of proton drip line, are calculated. Good agreement with both the available experimental data and the predictions of the RHB method are obtained.

## Key claim

The deformed Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) plus BCS method with a zero-range delta-force pairing interaction can successfully describe ground state properties of proton-rich odd-Z nuclei near and beyond the proton drip line for 55 ≤ Z ≤ 73, but the agreement with experimental one-proton separation energies is systematically worse for odd-odd nuclei due to the absence of residual proton-neutron pairing in the model.

## Method

The authors solved the RMF equations using a Harmonic-Oscillator basis expansion (12-14 shells for fermions, 20 shells for bosons) with quadrupole constrained calculations to find ground state configurations. In the particle-particle channel, they employed a state-dependent BCS method with a zero-range delta-force pairing interaction, using a cutoff energy of 8.0 MeV and pairing strengths adjusted to reproduce experimental one-proton separation energies of selected nuclei (V0 = 375-420 MeV fm³ depending on the parameter set and mass region). For odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, a blocking method was used to exclude the unpaired particle from pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

* For Lu (Z=71) isotopes: drip-line nucleus predicted as ¹⁵⁶Lu by both TMA and NL3 parameter sets; calculated Sp = -1.239 MeV for ¹⁵¹Lu (experimental: -1.233(3) MeV)
* For Ta (Z=73) isotopes: drip-line nucleus predicted as ¹⁶⁰Ta by NL3 and ¹⁶¹Ta by TMA; calculated Sp = -0.974 MeV for ¹⁵⁷Ta (experimental: -0.927(7) MeV)
* For ¹⁵⁰Lu (odd-odd): calculated Sp = -1.613 MeV (TMA) versus experimental -1.261(4) MeV; an extra ~0.3 MeV needed to match experiment
* For ¹⁴⁰Ho: calculated Sp = -1.223 MeV (TMA) versus experimental -1.086(10) MeV
* For ¹⁴⁵Tm: oblate deformation β₂ = -0.220 (TMA) with binding energy E/A = -7.903 MeV at β₂m = -0.211; prolate minimum at E/A = -7.906 MeV at β₂m = 0.287
* For ¹³¹Eu: calculated Sp = -0.498 MeV (TMA) versus experimental -0.950(8) MeV
* For ¹¹²Cs (odd-odd): calculated Sp = -1.213 MeV (TMA) versus experimental -0.807(7) MeV
* Pairing strength values: V0 = 393.0 MeV fm³ for Z ≥ 71 with TMA; V0 = 375.0 MeV fm³ for Z < 71 with TMA

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field treatment of proton emitters previously performed by Lalazissis et al. (1999, 2001) using the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with NL3 parameter set and Gogny D1S pairing, by instead employing a simpler deformed RMF+BCS method with zero-range delta-force pairing. The authors compare their results systematically with both the RHB predictions and experimental data for proton emitters across six isotopic chains (Z=55 to Z=73). The study enables future investigations of pairing interactions in exotic nuclei and identifies specific nuclei (e.g., ¹³⁰Eu, ¹³⁵Tb, ¹³⁶Tb, ¹¹⁹Pr, ¹²⁰Pr, ¹²⁴Pm, ¹²⁵Pm, ¹¹⁵La, ¹¹⁶La) as candidate proton emitters for experimental verification.
