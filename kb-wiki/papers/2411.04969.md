# Systematic study of flow of protons and light clusters in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions with momentum-dependent potentials

> We study the influence of the nuclear equation-of-state (EoS) on collective observables -- the directed ($v_1$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) of nucleons and light clusters -- in heavy-ion collisions at GeV energies using the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) approach. A novel development in this work is the inclusion of a momentum-dependent nucleon potential in the PHQMD in addition to the static, density-dependent Skyrme interaction. This enables three distinct EoS scenarios: two static ("soft" and "hard", differing in compressibility) and a soft, momentum-dependent EoS calibrated to $pA$ elastic scattering data. We find a strong EoS sensitivity in proton and cluster rapidity and $p_T$ distributions: soft and soft momentum-dependent EoS yield similar results, markedly different from the hard EoS. Softening the EoS reduces proton yields at midrapidity while enhancing light-cluster production. The EoS also affects flow observables differently for nucleons and clusters. For protons, a soft momentum-dependent potential increases slightly the magnitude of $v_1$ and $v_2$ relative to the hard EoS, whereas cluster flows are nearly similar. The soft momentum-dependent EoS provides an overall good agreement with experimental data from HADES and FOPI Collaborations while the soft EOS is not in line with the data. A scaling of $v_2$ with cluster mass number $A$ is observed at midrapidity for low $p_T$, which breaks at higher $p_T$. Finally, we examine the sensitivity of flow observables to deuteron production mechanisms. Deuterons formed via MST clustering exhibit different flow patterns from those produced by coalescence at freeze-out, indicating that flow harmonics may help discriminate between cluster formation scenarios.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a momentum-dependent nucleon potential in the PHQMD transport model, calibrated to pA elastic scattering data, produces flow observables for protons and light clusters that agree well with HADES and FOPI experimental data, whereas the static soft equation-of-state (without momentum dependence) does not match the data, despite having the same cold-matter energy per nucleon as the momentum-dependent version.

## Method

The authors extended the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (PHQMD) approach by adding a momentum-dependent nucleon-nucleon potential derived from the Schrödinger equivalent optical potential extracted from pA elastic scattering data. They simulated Au+Au collisions at beam kinetic energies around 1.2 A GeV using three equation-of-state scenarios: a static soft EoS (compressibility K=200 MeV), a static hard EoS (K=380 MeV), and a soft momentum-dependent EoS (K=200 MeV) with the same cold-matter energy-density relation as the static soft case. Clusters were identified via the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) algorithm applied during the dynamical evolution, with additional deuteron production from hadronic kinetic reactions (πNN ↔ πd and NNN ↔ Nd), and separately via a coalescence prescription at freeze-out for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus K = 200 MeV for the soft (S) and soft momentum-dependent (SM) equations-of-state; K = 380 MeV for the hard (H) equation-of-state.
- The MST cluster recognition uses a maximum distance r_clus = 4 fm between nucleons in their center-of-mass frame.
- The coalescence condition for deuterons requires |r_1 - r_2| ≤ 3.575 fm and |p_1 - p_2| ≤ 285 MeV/c.
- The Gaussian width parameter in PHQMD is L = 2.16 fm².
- Normal nuclear matter density is taken as ρ₀ = 0.168 nucleons/fm³.
- The momentum-dependent two-body potential parameters are a = 236.326 MeV⁻¹, b = -20.73 MeV⁻², c = 0.901 MeV⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends prior QMD and BUU studies of the nuclear equation-of-state (Aichelin 1987, Gale 1987, Welke 1988, Danielewicz 1998) by incorporating a momentum-dependent potential into PHQMD and comparing flow of both protons and light clusters against recent HADES and FOPI data. The results confirm the earlier finding (Aichelin 1987, Welke 1988) that a static hard EoS and a soft momentum-dependent EoS produce similar directed flow for nucleons, while a static soft EoS gives lower flow. The study enables discrimination between cluster formation mechanisms (MST versus coalescence) via flow harmonics, and provides a benchmark for future comparisons with UrQMD (Hillmann 2019, Steinheimer 2024) and SMASH (Tarasovicova 2024, Mohs 2024) calculations at SIS energies.
