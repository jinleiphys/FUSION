# Probing Matter Radii of Neutron-Rich Nuclei by Antiproton Scattering

> We propose to use antiprotons to investigate the sizes of stable and neutron-rich exotic nuclei by measurements of the $\pbar A$ absorption cross section along isotopic chains in inverse kinematics. The expected effects are studied theoretically in a microscopic model. The $\pbar U$ optical potentials are obtained by folding free space $\pbar N$ scattering amplitudes with HFB ground state densities and solving the scattering equations by direct integration. The mass dependence of absorption cross sections is found to follow closely the nuclear root-mean-square radii. The total absorption cross section is shown to be a superposition of cross sections describing partial absorption on neutrons and protons, respectively. Thus measuring the differential cross sections for absorption on neutrons and protons will give information on their respective distributions. In neutron-rich nuclei the outer neutron layer shields the absorption on the protons giving access to investigations of antiproton-neutron interactions in matter.

## Key claim

Antiproton absorption cross sections on neutron-rich nuclei follow a nearly universal scaling law with the nuclear root-mean-square radii, and the total absorption cross section can be decomposed into separate contributions from annihilation on neutrons and protons, enabling measurements of neutron and proton density distributions in exotic nuclei.

## Method

The authors constructed microscopic optical potentials for antiproton-nucleus scattering by folding free-space antiproton-nucleon scattering amplitudes (taken from the Paris model) with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground state densities for the nickel isotopic chain. They solved the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation with these complex absorptive potentials by direct integration to obtain partial-wave S-matrix elements and absorption cross sections, and also employed an eikonal approximation to interpret the spatial distribution of absorption.

## Key numbers

- Absorption cross sections for Ni isotopes (A=48 to 88) were calculated at antiproton incident energies from T_lab = 50 MeV to T_lab = 400 MeV; the cross sections decrease with increasing energy and increase steadily with mass number.
- At T_lab = 200 MeV and T_lab = 400 MeV, the absorption cross sections follow the nuclear rms radii with a scaling law σ_abs ≈ α_0 ⟨r²⟩; the normalization constant α_0 decreases by about 5% from T_lab = 200 MeV to T_lab = 400 MeV.
- The eikonal approximation agrees with the exact optical model results to better than 10% for T_lab above about 100 MeV.
- The ratio of the real to imaginary part of the antiproton-nucleon T-matrix (ε) lies between 1/4 and 1/3 in the energy range considered.

## Context

This work extends earlier antiproton-nucleus experiments at BNL (Bugg 1973, Ashford 1984) and LEAR (Janouin 1986, Bruge 1986, Lubinski 1994, Wycech 1996) from fixed-target measurements on stable nuclei to a proposed collider geometry for short-lived exotic nuclei, enabled by the FAIR facility at GSI. The method enables systematic studies of neutron skins along isotopic chains and provides a new approach to investigate antiproton-neutron interactions, for which the data base is very scarce. The next step, as discussed in the AIC proposal (2005), is to perform differential measurements by gating on A-1 residual nuclei to isolate annihilation on single target neutrons or protons.
