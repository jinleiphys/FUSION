# Pion electroproduction, PCAC, chiral Ward identities, and the axial form factor revisited

> We re-investigate Adler's PCAC relation in the presence of an external electromagnetic field within the framework of QCD coupled to external fields. We discuss pion electroproduction within a tree-level approximation to chiral perturbation theory and explicitly verify a chiral Ward identity referred to as the Adler-Gilman relation. We critically examine soft-momentum techniques and point out how inadmissable approximations may lead to results incompatible with chiral symmetry. As a result we confirm that threshold pion electroproduction is indeed a tool to obtain information on the axial form factor of the nucleon.

# Pion electroproduction, PCAC, chiral Ward identities, and the axial form factor revisited

## Key claim

Threshold pion electroproduction is confirmed as a valid tool to extract the axial form factor of the nucleon, but only when chiral Ward identities (specifically the Adler-Gilman relation) are properly satisfied; inadmissible approximations in soft-momentum techniques can produce results incompatible with chiral symmetry.

## Method

The authors re-derive Adler's PCAC relation within QCD coupled to external fields, then analyze pion electroproduction using a tree-level approximation to chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with an effective Lagrangian up to and including O(p^3) in the baryonic sector. They explicitly verify the chiral Ward identity known as the Adler-Gilman relation and critically examine the soft-momentum techniques used in prior work, identifying a problematic step in the argument of Haberzettl (2000).

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: F_pi = 92.4 MeV (lowest-order value in chiral limit is F, with F_pi = F[1 + O(hat m)])
- Nucleon masses: m_N = 938.3 MeV (physical nucleon mass used in tree-level approximation)
- Axial-vector coupling constant: g_A = 1.267
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: g_piN = 13.21 (from Schroder, 1998)
- Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy: Delta_piN = 2.6%
- Isovector anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_v = 3.706
- Isoscalar anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_s = -0.120
- Lowest-order relation: m_pi^2 = 2B * hat m (where hat m is the average light quark mass)

## Context

This work extends the current-algebra and PCAC approaches of the 1960s (Adler, 1965; Fubini, Furlan, and Rossetti, 1965; Nambu and Shrauner, 1962) by placing them within the modern framework of QCD coupled to external fields and chiral perturbation theory. It clarifies a controversy triggered by Haberzettl (2000), who argued that PCAC provides no additional constraints beyond the Goldberger-Treiman relation for pion electroproduction. The paper enables a rigorous extraction of the axial form factor from threshold electroproduction experiments (Choi, 1993; Blomqvist, 1996; Liesenfeld, 1999; Bartsch, 1998; Mueller, 2002; Baumann, 2003) and explains how chiral pion-loop corrections (Bernard et al., 1992) resolve a discrepancy between axial radius determinations from anti-neutrino-proton scattering and charged threshold pion electroproduction.
