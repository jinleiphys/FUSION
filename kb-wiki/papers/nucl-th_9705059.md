# Importance of the direct knockout mechanism in relativistic calculations for (gamma, p) reactions

> Results of relativistic calculations of the direct knockout (DKO) mechanism for the photon induced removal of a proton from a target nucleus over a wide range of energies and nuclei are presented. Spectroscopic factors used in the calculations are fixed from consistent analyses of the quasifree electron scattering process (e,e'p). The results indicate that within the uncertainties of the model, the knockout contributions are generally close to the experimental data for missing momenta below approximately 500 MeV/c. This is in disagreement with nonrelativistic analyses which often find that the direct knockout contribution can be quite small compared to the data and that meson exchange corrections can be important. The present study suggests that meson exchange current contributions may not be as large when treated in a relativistic framework. We also point out some difficulties we encountered in analyzing the data for a 12C target at photon energies below 80 MeV.

## Key claim

Within the uncertainties of the relativistic model, the direct knockout (DKO) mechanism accounts for most of the observed (gamma, p) cross section data for missing momenta below approximately 500 MeV/c, in disagreement with nonrelativistic analyses which often find the DKO contribution to be quite small and require large meson exchange current (MEC) contributions. The authors suggest that MEC contributions may not be as large when treated in a relativistic framework, but note unresolved difficulties for a 12C target at photon energies below 80 MeV where calculations overestimate data by close to a factor of 2.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations of the direct knockout mechanism for (gamma, p) reactions, using bound state wave functions from Dirac-Hartree potentials and continuum proton wave functions from Dirac optical potentials fitted to elastic scattering data. Spectroscopic factors were fixed from parallel analyses of (e, e'p) data on the same target nuclei, with no free parameters adjusted in the (gamma, p) calculations. The model was applied to targets of 12C, 10B, 16O, and 208Pb over photon energies from 45 MeV to 361 MeV.

## Key numbers

* For 12C at photon energies below 80 MeV, relativistic DKO calculations lie consistently above the experimental cross section data by close to a factor of 2.
* For 16O, the reduced cross section calculations are close to data for missing momenta less than approximately 500 MeV/c, with deviations appearing above this value.
* For 208Pb at photon energies of 45 and 54 MeV, relativistic calculations come close to predicting the correct magnitudes of observed cross sections, in contrast to nonrelativistic DWIA analyses which found DKO contributions up to a factor of 10 below the data.
* For 12C(p, gamma) at proton energy 98 MeV, calculations lie slightly above data at all angles except theta_p = 140 degrees; at 176 MeV calculations are closer to data except at large angles.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic analysis of Johansson et al. (1996) for 60 MeV photons to a much wider range of energies and nuclei, and directly contradicts nonrelativistic analyses by Ireland and van der Steenhoven (1994), Bobeldijk et al. (1995), and Mori et al. (1995) which found DKO contributions to be very small and concluded MEC effects were dominant. The results suggest that relativistic treatments may suppress MEC contributions at lower missing momenta, and enable future tests using spin-dependent observables where normalization uncertainties cancel. The paper also identifies the need for improved description of the 12C ground state structure, possibly including deformation or configuration mixing.
