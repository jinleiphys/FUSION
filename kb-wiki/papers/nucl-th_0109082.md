# The $^{10}$Li spectrum and the $^{11}$Li properties

> The neutron--$^9$Li interaction and the corresponding low-energy $^{10}$Li spectrum are decisive for the properties of $^{11}$Li described as a three-body system (n+n+$^{9}$Li). We compute structure and breakup properties of $^{11}$Li as function of this interaction. The hyperfine structure due to the spin 3/2 of both $^{9}$Li and $^{11}$Li is needed and treated with special care. We use the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion of the Faddeev equations for the structure and the participant-spectator model for the breakup reactions of $^{11}$Li. We use established experimental constraints of both $^{11}$Li (binding energy, size and differential breakup cross sections) and of $^{10}$Li (a virtual s-state below 50 keV and a $p$-resonance around 0.54 MeV).Another $p$-resonance must then be present below 0.54 MeV and another $s$-level must be present between about 0.5 MeV and 1 MeV depending on unknown spin assignments. All established facts are in agreement with our predictions obtained within the same consistent model.

## Key claim

The properties of $^{11}$Li (binding energy, size, and breakup cross sections) can be consistently reproduced within a single model only when the neutron-${}^9$Li interaction includes both a low-lying virtual $s$-state (below 50 keV) and a $p$-resonance (around 0.54 MeV), plus an additional $p$-resonance below 0.54 MeV and another $s$-level between about 0.5 MeV and 1 MeV, with the precise energies depending on unknown spin assignments.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in coordinate space using a hyperspherical adiabatic expansion to obtain the three-body wave function of ${}^{11}$Li as a neutron-neutron-${}^9$Li system. The neutron-${}^9$Li interaction was modeled with central, spin-spin, and spin-orbit gaussian potentials, with strengths adjusted independently for each partial wave, and Pauli-forbidden states were removed using phase-equivalent potentials. Breakup cross sections were computed using the participant-spectator model, where one halo constituent interacts with the target via an optical potential while the other two are treated as spectators under the black disk model.

## Key numbers

- The experimental binding energy of ${}^{11}$Li is 295 $\pm$ 35 keV (You et al., 1993); the authors used 0.30 MeV in their calculations.
- The root mean square radius of ${}^9$Li is 2.32 fm; a gaussian potential range of 2.0 fm reproduces this value.
- The $p_{3/2}$-state in the neutron-${}^9$Li system has a binding energy of 4.1 MeV (from the one-neutron separation energy of ${}^9$Li).
- For the $s$-wave virtual state, strengths from $-99.0$ MeV to $-91.0$ MeV produce energies from 65 keV to 837 keV (from $S$-matrix poles).
- For the $p$-wave resonances, strengths from $-68.0$ MeV to $-64.0$ MeV produce resonance energies from 0.23 MeV to 0.76 MeV and widths from 0.09 MeV to 0.59 MeV.
- The two lowest eigenvalues in the hyperspherical expansion account for more than 98% of the total norm of the ${}^{11}$Li wave function.

## Context

This work extends earlier three-body calculations of ${}^{11}$Li by Garrido et al. (1996, 1997, 1999) and Fedorov et al. (1994), which had shown that momentum distributions after fragmentation require a low-lying $s$-state in ${}^{10}$Li and roughly 40% $p$-wave content in the neutron-core relative motion. The present study improves on those by including the full spin-3/2 structure of both ${}^9$Li and ${}^{11}$Li, which had been neglected in most prior theoretical work (e.g., the spin-zero approximations used in many earlier models). The results enable future extraction of the precise energies of the individual $1^-/2^-$ and $1^+/2^+$ doublets in ${}^{10}$Li from invariant mass spectra, which are more sensitive to these details than momentum distributions.
