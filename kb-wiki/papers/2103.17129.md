# Diffuse relaxation approximation in a heated Fermi system

> An expression for the two-particle relaxation time of collective excitations on a distorted Fermi surface in the diffusion approach to kinetic theory is obtained. The general case of momentum-dependent diffusion and drift coefficients is considered. The temperature dependence of the obtained expression is established.

# Diffuse relaxation approximation in a heated Fermi system

## Key claim

The two-particle relaxation time for collective multipole excitations on a distorted Fermi surface can be expressed exactly through the momentum-dependent diffusion coefficient alone, without requiring the drift coefficient, and in the low-temperature limit the result reduces to the earlier constant-coefficient approximation. The temperature dependence of the renormalized relaxation time follows an inverse quadratic law at low temperatures and becomes nearly linear at higher temperatures, with a finite value at zero temperature.

## Method

The author starts from the Landau-Vlasov kinetic equation with a collision integral expressed in the diffusion approximation, where the collision integral is expanded in small momentum transfer and written in terms of momentum-dependent diffusion and drift coefficients. A small deviation of the Wigner distribution function from equilibrium is expanded in spherical harmonics, and the l-th moment of the linearized collision integral is computed analytically for a spherically symmetric Fermi distribution. The resulting expression for the relaxation time τ_l is evaluated numerically using a previously derived diffusion coefficient based on a Gaussian-type internucleon potential with parameters r₀ = 0.8 fm and v₀ = -33 MeV, and the temperature dependence is compared with the low-temperature approximation.

## Key numbers

- The renormalized relaxation time l(l+1)τ_l at zero temperature is approximately 3.77 × 10⁻²¹ s.
- The low-temperature approximation (Eq. 24) coincides with the exact result (Eq. 23) up to approximately 3 MeV, where differences begin to appear.
- The Fermi energy is taken as ε_F = 37 MeV.
- The Gaussian potential parameters are r₀ = 0.8 fm and v₀ = -33 MeV, which yield a total in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section σ_tot ≈ 20 mb.
- At low temperatures, the renormalized relaxation time decreases as ∼ T⁻²; at higher temperatures the decrease becomes nearly linear.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Lukyanov and Kolomietz, 2014; 2015) in which the diffusion and drift coefficients were treated as constants and only rough estimates of the relaxation time were made. The present calculation uses the general momentum-dependent diffusion coefficient derived in those earlier papers and obtains an exact analytic expression for the relaxation time without the constant-coefficient simplification. The result enables more accurate modeling of dissipation in heated nuclear Fermi systems, such as collective vibrations in hot nuclei, and provides a basis for comparing with the relaxation time of particle-hole excitations studied by Wolschin (1982), which shows an opposite temperature dependence. The author notes that memory effects are not included in the diffusion coefficient expression and require further investigation.
