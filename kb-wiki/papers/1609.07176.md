# Initial energy density of $\sqrt{s}=$ 7 and 8 TeV p+p collisions at the LHC

> Results from the RHIC and LHC experiments show, that in relativistic heavy ion collisions, a new state of matter, a strongly interacting perfect fluid is created. Accelerating, exact and explicit solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics allow for a simple and natural description of this medium. A finite rapidity distribution arises from these solutions, leading to an advanced estimate of the initial energy density of high energy collisions. These solutions can be utilized to describe various aspects of proton-proton collisions, as originally suggested by Landau. We show that an advanced estimate based on hydrodynamics yields an initial energy density in $\sqrt{s}=7$ and 8 TeV p+p collisions at LHC on the same order as the critical energy density from lattice QCD, and a corresponding initial temperature around the critical temperature from QCD and the Hagedorn temperature. The multiplicity dependence of the estimated initial energy density suggests that in high multiplicity pp collisions at the LHC, there is large enough initial energy density to create a non-hadronic perfect fluid.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the average initial energy density in \(\sqrt{s}=7\) and 8 TeV p+p collisions at the LHC is below the critical value of 1 GeV/fm\(^3\) needed to form a non-hadronic medium, but that in high-multiplicity events (above a charged particle pseudorapidity density of about 9) the corrected estimate exceeds this critical threshold, suggesting the possible creation of a perfect fluid.

## Method

The authors fit an exact, accelerating 1+1 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic solution (with a constant speed of sound and a \(\kappa=1\) equation of state) to charged-particle pseudorapidity distributions measured by TOTEM and CMS at 7 and 8 TeV. From the fitted acceleration parameter \(\lambda\) and the Bjorken energy-density formula, they derive a corrected initial energy density that accounts for finite rapidity width and pressure work, using a conjectured extension to non-ideal equations of state.

## Key numbers

- Bjorken estimate for average 7 TeV p+p collisions: \(\epsilon_{\rm Bj}(7\;{\rm TeV}) = 0.507\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- Corrected estimate for average 7 TeV p+p collisions: \(\epsilon_{\rm corr}(7\;{\rm TeV}) = 0.640\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (from TOTEM-only fit) or \(0.645\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (from combined TOTEM+CMS fit).
- Bjorken estimate for average 8 TeV p+p collisions: \(\epsilon_{\rm Bj}(8\;{\rm TeV}) = 0.519\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- Corrected estimate for average 8 TeV p+p collisions: \(\epsilon_{\rm corr}(8\;{\rm TeV}) = 0.644\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- Fitted acceleration parameter for 7 TeV: \(\lambda = 1.073 \pm 0.001_{\rm stat} \pm 0.003_{\rm syst}\) (TOTEM only) and \(\lambda = 1.076\) (TOTEM+CMS).
- Fitted acceleration parameter for 8 TeV: \(\lambda = 1.067 \pm 0.001\) (TOTEM only) and \(\lambda = 1.066\) (TOTEM+CMS).
- Corrected estimate exceeds the critical energy density of 1 GeV/fm\(^3\) for charged particle pseudorapidity densities greater than about 9 (corrected estimate) or 12 (Bjorken estimate).

## Context

This work extends the classic Bjorken energy-density estimate (Bjorken, 1983) by incorporating acceleration effects from exact hydrodynamic solutions (Csorgo et al., 2006, 2007; Nagy et al., 2007) and applies them to p+p collisions at LHC energies, following earlier suggestions by Landau and by Hama and Padula (1987) for smaller systems. The results enable a quantitative comparison of initial energy densities in p+p and heavy-ion collisions, and support the possibility of collective, non-hadronic behavior in high-multiplicity p+p events, as also suggested by ridge observations (CMS, 2012) and by Shuryak and Zahed (2013).
