# Parton Equilibration in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Medium effects which include color screening and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression of induced radiation are discussed in connection with the equilibration of dense partonic system in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. Taking into account of these medium effects, the equilibration rate for a gluonic gas are derived and the consequences are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives equilibration rates for a gluonic gas in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, but the rates are significantly reduced when medium effects (color screening and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression of induced radiation) are included; without these effects, the equilibration would be much faster.

## Method

The author calculates the color screening mass from the initial gluon phase-space density, then derives the induced gluon radiation spectrum from single and multiple scatterings using perturbative QCD, incorporating the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect via an effective formation time. The gluon equilibration rate is obtained from the cross section for \(gg \rightarrow ggg\) processes, regularized by the color screening mass and suppressed by the LPM effect, and is used in a rate equation coupled with energy conservation under longitudinal expansion.

## Key numbers

- The effective color screening mass is \(\mu_D^2 = \lambda_g g^2 T^2\), where \(\lambda_g\) is the gluon fugacity and \(T\) is the temperature.
- The mean free path for gluons is \(\lambda^{-1} = \frac{9}{8} a_1 \alpha_s T\), independent of fugacity.
- The chemical equilibration rate is approximated by \(R_3 = 2.1 \alpha_s^2 T \left( 2\lambda_g - \lambda_g^2\right)^{1/2}\).
- The effective formation time in QCD is \(\tau_{\rm QCD}(k) = \frac{C_A}{2C_2} \frac{2\cosh y}{k_{\perp}}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative estimates of initial parton density by Eskola, Wang, Geiger, and others (1990s) by incorporating medium effects that were previously neglected in parton cascade simulations. The derived rate equations with LPM suppression and color screening provide a parameter-free framework for studying the time evolution of gluon fugacity and temperature toward equilibrium, enabling more realistic modeling of the early stage of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
