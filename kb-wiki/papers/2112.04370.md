# Band-like structures and quartets in deformed N=Z nuclei

> We provide a description of deformed $N=Z$ nuclei in a formalism of $α$-like quartets. Quartets are constructed variationally by resorting to the use of proper intrinsic states. Various types of intrinsic states are introduced which generate different sets of quartets for a given nucleus. Energy spectra are generated via configuration-iteraction calculations in the spaces built with these quartets. The approach has been applied to $^{24}$Mg and $^{28}$Si in the $sd$ shell and to $^{48}$Cr in the $pf$ shell. In all cases a good description of the low-lying spectra has been achieved. As a peculiarity of the approach, a close correspondence is observed between the various sets of quartets employed and the occurrence of well defined band-like structures in the spectra of the systems under study.

# Band-like structures and quartets in deformed N=Z nuclei

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that variational construction of alpha-like quartets from intrinsic states can reproduce low-lying spectra of deformed N=Z nuclei, with the specific hedge that the quartets must be defined dynamically (by minimizing the energy of intrinsic states) rather than statically (from the nearest one-quartet system), and that the quality of the description depends on which of two nearly degenerate minima is chosen for the ground intrinsic state in ²⁸Si.

## Method
The authors construct T=0 quartet creation operators in a spherical mean field and generate intrinsic states as condensates of these quartets. For each nucleus (²⁴Mg, ²⁸Si in the sd shell; ⁴⁸Cr in the pf shell), they minimize the energy of a ground intrinsic state to define dynamical quartets, then generate additional intrinsic states (beta, gamma, and higher-K) by promoting one quartet to an excited configuration. Energy spectra are obtained via configuration-interaction calculations in the m-scheme using the USDB interaction for sd-shell nuclei and the KB3G interaction for pf-shell nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Ground state correlation energy errors for all three nuclei are confined within 0.4% when using dynamical quartets (compared to shell model results)
- For ²⁸Si, two nearly degenerate minima in the ground intrinsic state minimization occur at E = -133.627 MeV (QCM condensate with only J=0 quartets) and 36 keV higher (with contributions from all J quartets)
- Using only J=0 and J=2 dynamical quartets, ground state correlation energy errors remain within 0.7% for ²⁴Mg and ²⁸Si, and 1.6% for ⁴⁸Cr; adding J=4 quartets reduces the ⁴⁸Cr error to 0.6%
- Static quartets (from ²⁰Ne for sd shell, ⁴⁴Ti for pf shell) produce markedly worse spectra than dynamical quartets

## Context
This work extends the Quartet Model (QM) approach previously developed by the same authors (Sambataro and Sandulescu, 2020) for describing N=Z nuclei, where quartets were defined statically from the nearest one-quartet system. The variational method for defining quartets is inspired by the approach of Maglione et al. (1994) and Catara et al. (1996) applied in the context of the Nucleon Pair Approximation (NPA). The paper also connects to the Quartet Condensation Model (QCM) of Sandulescu et al. (2012) and to exact analytical solutions of the isovector pairing Hamiltonian by Dukelsky et al. (2006). The results enable a description of deformed N=Z nuclei using only a few degrees of freedom, with band-like structures naturally emerging from different sets of quartets.
