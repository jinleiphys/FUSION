# X(1835), X(2120) and X(2370) in a flux tube model

> Nonstrange baryonium spectrum is systematically studied by using the Gaussian expansion method in a flux tube model with the six-body confinement potential. All the model parameters are fixed by baryon properties, so the baryonium calculation is parameter-free. We find that X(1835) and X(2370), which are observed in the radiative decay of $J/ψ$ by BES collaboration, can be described as $N_8\bar{N}_8$ and $Δ_8\barΔ_8$ bound states with quantum numbers $I^GJ^{PC}=0^+0^{-+}$, respectively, such bound states should be color confinement resonances with three-dimensional configurations similar to dumbbell, however, X(2120) can not be accommodated in our model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that X(1835) and X(2370) can be described as compact hexaquark states \(N_8\bar{N}_8\) and \(\Delta_8\bar{\Delta}_8\) with quantum numbers \(I^GJ^{PC}=0^+0^{-+}\), respectively, but only under the condition that these are bound states formed by hidden-color channels in a flux tube model with six-body confinement; X(2120) cannot be accommodated in this model.

## Method

The authors used the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) to solve the six-body Schrodinger equation for nonstrange \(q^3\bar{q}^3\) systems in a flux tube model with a six-body confinement potential. Two variants of the quark model were employed: Model I (Isgur-Karl model with one-gluon exchange) and Model II (chiral quark model with Goldstone boson exchange), with all parameters fixed by fitting the ground-state baryon spectrum, making the hexaquark calculation parameter-free. The confinement potential was taken as the minimum of two possible flux tube configurations: a hadronic molecule structure (color-singlet baryon plus color-singlet antibaryon) and a hidden-color structure (color-octet baryon plus color-octet antibaryon coupled to overall color singlet).

## Key numbers

* X(1835) mass: 1833.7 ± 6.5 ± 2.7 MeV (BES-II) and 1836.5 ± 3.0 +5.6/-2.1 MeV (BES-III); width: 67.7 ± 20.3 ± 7.7 MeV (BES-II) and 190 ± 9 +38/-36 MeV (BES-III)
* X(2120) mass: 2122.4 ± 6.7 +4.7/-2.7 MeV; width: 83 ± 16 +31/-11 MeV (BES-III)
* X(2370) mass: 2376.3 ± 8.7 +3.2/-4.3 MeV; width: 83 ± 17 +44/-6 MeV (BES-III)
* For the \(I^GJ^{PC}=0^+0^{-+}\) state, the \(N\bar{N}+N_8\bar{N}_8\) configuration has lowest energies of 1834 MeV (Model I) and 1844 MeV (Model II, with mixing)
* For the same quantum numbers, the \(\Delta\bar{\Delta}+\Delta_8\bar{\Delta}_8\) configuration has lowest energies of 2376 MeV (Model I) and 2388 MeV (Model II, with mixing)
* Binding energy for the \(0^+0^{-+}\) state: -44 MeV (Model I) and -34 MeV (Model II) relative to the \(N+\bar{N}\) threshold at 939+939 MeV
* Binding energy for the \(0^+2^{-+}\) state: -269 MeV (Model I) and -200 MeV (Model II) relative to the \(\Delta+\bar{\Delta}\) threshold at 1232+1232 MeV

## Context

This work extends the flux tube model, previously applied to tetraquark and meson systems by the same group (Deng, Ping, Wang), to the hexaquark \(q^3\bar{q}^3\) sector. It contrasts with earlier two-body confinement calculations by Entem and Fernandez (2006) and Vijande et al. (2010), which found no bound nonstrange hexaquark states, by showing that the multibody confinement potential from the flux tube picture provides additional attraction. The results enable future experimental searches for hidden-color baryonium states and suggest that X(1835) and X(2370) may be compact hexaquarks rather than hadronic molecules or glueballs, while X(2120) remains unexplained in this framework.
