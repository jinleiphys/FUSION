# Dynamic fast flavor oscillation waves in dense neutrino gases

> The flavor transformation in a dense neutrino gas can have a significant impact on the physical and chemical evolution of its surroundings. In this work we demonstrate that a dynamic, fast flavor oscillation wave can develop spontaneously in a one-dimensional (1D) neutrino gas when the angular distributions of the electron neutrino and antineutrino cross each other. Unlike the 2D stationary models which are plagued with small-scale flavor structures, the fast flavor oscillation waves remain coherent in the dynamic 1D model in both the position and momentum spaces of the neutrino. The electron lepton number is redistributed and transported in space as the flavor oscillation wave propagates, although the total lepton number remains constant. This result may have interesting implications in the neutrino emission in and the evolution of the compact objects such as core-collapse supernovae.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a dynamic, fast flavor oscillation wave can develop spontaneously in a one-dimensional (1D) neutrino gas when the angular distributions of electron neutrinos and antineutrinos cross each other, but only under the specific condition that the initial perturbation is seeded in an almost homogeneous flavor distribution. Unlike 2D stationary models, these waves remain coherent in both position and momentum space, though the distinction between convective and absolute instabilities is relative and depends on the choice of reference frame.

## Method
The authors solved the nonlinear equations of motion for flavor polarization vectors in a 1D neutrino gas with axial symmetry about the z-axis, using 200 equally spaced angle bins for velocity u in [-1,1] and a 1D lattice of 24,000 to 48,000 points in a periodic box. They initialized the system with small Gaussian perturbations (epsilon_0 = 10^-6 to 0.1) in an otherwise uniform flavor distribution and evolved the system numerically to study both linear and nonlinear regimes. The linearized equations were used to compute dispersion relations and classify instabilities as convective or absolute based on the analytic properties of the complex frequency and wavenumber branches.

## Key numbers
- Box size L = 1200 (for linear regime) and L = 2400 (for nonlinear regime), resolved by 24,000 and 48,000 lattice points respectively
- Initial perturbation amplitude epsilon_0 = 10^-6 for the Gca distribution and 10^-7 for the Gaa distribution in linear regime calculations; epsilon_0 = 0.1 for both in nonlinear regime
- Two ELN distributions studied: Gca with alpha = 0.9 and Gaa with alpha = 0.92, both of the form G(u) = g(u, 0.6) - alpha * g(u, 0.53) where g is a normalized Gaussian
- The leftward advection velocity of one perturbation component was approximately V ≈ -1 (in units where mu = 1)
- The width of the initial Gaussian perturbation was 50 (in units of mu^-1)

## Context
This work extends the linear stability analysis of fast neutrino oscillations (Dasgupta 2016, Izaguirre 2016, Yi 2019) into the nonlinear regime, where previous studies had been limited to homogeneous models (Abbar 2018) or few-beam toy models (Capozzi 2018). The results contrast with stationary 2D models (Mirizzi 2015, Martin 2019) which exhibit spurious instabilities and small-scale flavor structures, whereas the dynamic 1D model produces coherent oscillation waves. The findings call for self-consistent supernova simulations (Nagakura 2017) with detailed angular distributions of emitted neutrinos, as fast flavor conversions could affect neutrino emission properties and explosion dynamics in core-collapse supernovae.
