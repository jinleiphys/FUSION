# Rapidity Distributions of Dileptons from a Hadronizing Quark-Gluon Plasma

> It has been predicted that dilepton production may be used as a quark-gluon plasma probe. We calculate the rapidity distributions of thermal dileptons produced by an evolving quark-gluon plasma assuming a longitudinal scaling expansion with initial conditions locally determined from the hadronic rapidity density. These distributions are compared with Drell-Yan production and semileptonic charm decays at invariant mass $M = 2$, 4, and 6 GeV.

# Rapidity Distributions of Dileptons from a Hadronizing Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
Thermal dilepton rapidity distributions from an evolving quark-gluon plasma can be distinguished from Drell-Yan and charm-decay backgrounds at RHIC and LHC energies, but only if the charm contribution can be reliably subtracted using detector acceptance cuts; the thermal signal is competitive with backgrounds only for certain choices of initial conditions (e.g., the most optimistic uncertainty-bound scenario with C=1).

## Method
The authors model a longitudinally scaling quark-gluon plasma with initial conditions set by the hadronic rapidity density, using a Gaussian parametrization of the multiplicity distribution. They compute thermal dilepton yields from the plasma, mixed phase, and hadron gas under three initial-condition hypotheses: fixed formation time (tau_i = 1 fm), and two uncertainty-bound scenarios (C=3 and C=1). Drell-Yan production is calculated with Duke-Owens 1.1 structure functions, and charm production uses lowest-order QCD with K-factors, including semileptonic decays via a Monte Carlo decay simulation.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC, the maximum initial temperature T_i,max is ~255 MeV for fixed tau_i = 1 fm, ~300 MeV for C=3, and ~515 MeV for C=1.
- At LHC, T_i,max is ~350 MeV for fixed tau_i, ~465 MeV for C=3, and ~810 MeV for C=1.
- The charm hadroproduction cross section is ~190 microbarns at RHIC and 1.95 mb at LHC (using m_c = 1.5 GeV, K=2).
- At RHIC, the Drell-Yan rate increases with rapidity by approximately 30 percent.
- For a two-flavor plasma with C=1, T_i,max reaches 590 MeV at RHIC and 930 MeV at LHC.
- The hadron gas lifetime at central rapidities can reach ~80 fm when T_i ~ T_i,max.
- Detector acceptance for Drell-Yan and thermal dileptons is 4 to 20 times larger than for charm decay leptons at M=2, 4, and 6 GeV and y=2.

## Context
This work extends earlier predictions that dileptons can probe the quark-gluon plasma (Shuryak, Shor) by providing explicit rapidity distributions for comparison with backgrounds. It builds on calculations of Drell-Yan production (Duke-Owens structure functions) and charm hadroproduction (Vogt et al., Banerjee et al.) and addresses the need to separate thermal signals from initial nucleon-nucleon processes. The results enable experimental searches at RHIC and LHC, particularly with the PHENIX detector, by showing that acceptance cuts can make charm subtraction feasible, and suggest that thermal charm production may itself become a novel probe if thermalization times are short.
