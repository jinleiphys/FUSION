# Core - Corona Model analysis of the Low Energy Beam Scan at RHIC (Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider) in Brookhaven (USA)

> The centrality dependence of spectra of identified particles in collisions between ultrarelativistic heavy ions with a center of mass energy ($\sqrt{s}$) of 39 and 11.5 $AGeV$ is analyzed in the core - corona model. We show that at these energies the spectra can be well understood assuming that they are composed of two components whose relative fraction depends on the centrality of the interaction: The core component which describes an equilibrated quark gluon plasma and the corona component which is caused by nucleons close to the surface of the interaction zone which scatter only once and which is identical to that observed in proton-proton collisions. The success of this approach at 39 and 11.5 $AGeV$ shows that the physics does not change between this energy and $\sqrt{s}=200~ AGeV$ for which this model has been developed (Aichelin 2008). This presents circumstantial evidence that a quark gluon plasma is also created at center of mass energies as low as 11.5 $AGeV$.

## Key claim

The core-corona model, previously validated at \(\sqrt{s}=200~AGeV\), successfully describes the centrality dependence of identified particle spectra at \(\sqrt{s}=39~AGeV\) and \(11.5~AGeV\), providing circumstantial evidence that a quark-gluon plasma is created at center-of-mass energies as low as \(11.5~AGeV\).

## Method

The authors applied the core-corona model to STAR data from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=39~AGeV\) and \(11.5~AGeV\). They extracted the corona contribution from the most peripheral collisions (since pp data were unavailable at these energies) and the core contribution from the most central collisions, then used the Glauber Monte Carlo core fraction \(f_{core}(N_{part})\) from Aichelin (2008) to predict spectra for all centralities via a two-component superposition. The agreement was quantified by comparing the data to model predictions for both the absolute spectra and the ratio \(R_{cp}\) (spectrum at a given centrality divided by the most central spectrum).

## Key numbers

* The analysis covers Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=39~AGeV\) and \(11.5~AGeV\).
* The core-corona model reproduces the centrality dependence of \(K^-\), \(\pi^-\), and antiproton \(p_T\) spectra at both energies, with the antiproton slope showing the strongest centrality dependence and serving as the most sensitive probe.
* The critical temperature for the QCD crossover is cited as \(T_c \approx 152~MeV\) from lattice gauge calculations (Borsanyi et al. 2010).
* The core fraction \(f_{core}\) as a function of \(N_{part}\) was determined by a Glauber Monte Carlo approach (Aichelin 2008).

## Context

This work extends the core-corona model, previously applied to Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=64\) and \(200~AGeV\) (Aichelin 2008, 2010), down to \(\sqrt{s}=11.5~AGeV\) using data from the RHIC Low Energy Beam Scan (Kumar 2011, Zhu 2012). The success of the model suggests that the transition from hadronic to partonic degrees of freedom occurs at energies accessible at the NICA accelerator in Dubna and the FAIR facility in Darmstadt, making the \(\sqrt{s} \approx 10~AGeV\) region a target for future experimental investigation.
