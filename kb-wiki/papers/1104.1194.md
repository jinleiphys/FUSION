# Nuclear Density Functional Theory and the Equation of State

> A nuclear density functional can be used to find the binding energy and shell structure of nuclei and the energy gap in superconducting nuclear matter. In this paper, we study the possible application of a nuclear density functional theory to nuclear astrophysics. From energy density functional theory, we can deduce the interaction between nucleons to find a rough estimate of the charge radius of the specific nuclei. Compared to the Finite-Range Thomas Fermi model, we include three-body forces, which might be important at densities several times that of nuclear matter density. We also add the momentum dependent interaction to take into account the effective mass of the nucleons. We study matter in the neutron star crust using the Wigner-Seitz cell method. By constructing the mass-radius relation of neutron stars and investigating lepton-rich nuclear matter in proto-neutron stars, we find that the density functional can be used to construct an equation of state of hot dense matter.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a Gaussian nuclear density functional (GNDF), which includes three-body forces and momentum-dependent interactions, can be used to construct a unified equation of state for dense matter, applicable from isolated nuclei to neutron star cores and proto-neutron stars. The model’s validity is conditional on its ability to simultaneously reproduce five key saturation properties of nuclear matter and the surface tension of semi-infinite nuclear matter.

## Method
The author constructs a nuclear energy density functional using a Gaussian finite-range interaction, a zero-range three-body force, and a momentum-dependent term to account for the effective mass of nucleons. The model parameters are calibrated to reproduce five saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (binding energy, vanishing pressure, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and its slope parameter L) and the surface tension of semi-infinite nuclear matter. The functional is then applied to calculate the properties of finite nuclei, matter in the neutron star crust using the Wigner-Seitz cell method, and the mass-radius relation of cold neutron stars, as well as the properties of hot, lepton-rich matter in proto-neutron stars.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear incompressibility is set to \( K_0 = 265 \) MeV.
- The symmetry energy at saturation is \( S_v = 28 \) MeV, and its slope parameter is \( L = 54 \) MeV.
- The effective mass of a nucleon at saturation density is chosen to be \( m^{*} = 0.78 m_b \).
- The range of the Gaussian interaction is determined to be \( r_0 = 1.205 \) fm from the surface tension of semi-infinite nuclear matter (\( \omega = 1.250 \) MeV fm\(^{-2}\)).
- For \( ^{208}\text{Pb} \), the model predicts a charge radius of 5.44 fm and a binding energy per nucleon of 8.02 MeV.
- The phase transition from non-uniform to uniform nuclear matter in the neutron star crust is found to occur around a density of \( 0.5\rho_0 \).

## Context
This work extends the Finite-Range Thomas-Fermi (FRTF) model by Oyamatsu (1993) by explicitly including three-body forces and momentum-dependent interactions, which are important at supra-saturation densities. It provides a unified framework that connects the physics of finite nuclei to the equation of state of neutron stars, enabling future studies of neutron star structure, cooling, and proto-neutron star evolution. The results are compared to the APR equation of state (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998) and experimental data from Steiner et al. (2005).
