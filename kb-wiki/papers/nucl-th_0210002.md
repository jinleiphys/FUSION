# Few-body correlations in the QCD phase diagram

> From the viewpoint of statistical physics, nuclear matter is a strongly correlated many-particle system. Several regimes of the QCD phase diagram should exhibit strong correlations. Here I focus on three- and four-body correlations that might be important in the phase diagram.

## Key claim

Three- and four-body correlations may play a significant role in the QCD phase diagram, particularly for phenomena such as deuteron formation in heavy ion reactions and alpha-particle condensation, but the author hedges that these effects are "might be important" and that the significance of quartetting for mu > 0 "needs further investigation."

## Method

The author derives in-medium three- and four-body equations using Dyson equations and a cluster expansion of statistical Green functions at finite temperature and density, analogous to the Feynman-Galitskii and Bethe-Goldstone equations. The equations are solved numerically for nuclear matter using the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) transition operator formalism, with the two-body input given by the Feynman-Galitskii equation and the three-body input by the in-medium AGS equation. The medium-dependent reaction rates are then incorporated into a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) simulation of heavy ion collisions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-particle correlations in nuclear matter by Beyer (2000) and others to three- and four-body correlations, addressing questions raised by the Hubbard model results of Letz that three-particle contributions may lead to a lower critical temperature than the simple Thouless criterion. The approach enables future investigation of whether the chiral phase transition for nucleons (three-quark states) occurs at the same density and temperature as for mesons (quark-antiquark states), and whether alpha-particle condensation can occur in nuclear matter, building on the variational calculations of Tohsaki (2001) and Suzuki (2002).
