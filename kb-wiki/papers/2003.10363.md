# Experimental identification of the $T = 1$, $J^π = 6^+$ state of $^{54}$Co and isospin symmetry in $A = 54$ studied via one-nucleon knockout reactions

> New experimental data obtained from $γ$-ray tagged one-neutron and one-proton knockout from $^{55}$Co is presented. A candidate for the sought-after $T=1, T_z = 0, J^π = 6^+$ state in $^{54}$Co is proposed based on a comparison to the new data on $^{54}$Fe, the corresponding observables predicted by large-scale-shell-model (LSSM) calculations in the full $fp$-model space employing charge-dependent contributions, and isospin-symmetry arguments. Furthermore, possible isospin-symmetry breaking in the $A=54$, $T=1$ triplet is studied by calculating the experimental $c$ coefficients of the isobaric mass multiplet equation (IMME) up to the maximum possible spin $J=6$ expected for the $(1f_{7/2})^{-2}$ two-hole configuration relative to the doubly-magic nucleus $^{56}$Ni. The experimental quantities are compared to the theoretically predicted $c$ coefficients from LSSM calculations using two-body matrix elements obtained from a realistic chiral effective field theory potential at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO).

## Key claim

The paper proposes the 3097(6) keV excited state of 54Co as the strongest candidate for the sought-after T=1, Tz=0, Jπ=6+ state, based on agreement with shell-model predictions and the observed decay pattern, but does not confirm the previous candidate at 2979 keV reported by Rudolph et al. (2010).

## Method

One-neutron and one-proton knockout reactions from a 55Co secondary beam (produced from a 160 MeV/u 58Ni primary beam on a 9Be target) were performed at the NSCL Coupled Cyclotron Facility, with reaction residues identified event-by-event using the S800 spectrograph. Gamma rays emitted by the residues in flight were detected with the SeGA array of 16 segmented HPGe detectors, and Doppler reconstruction was performed event-by-event. Experimental partial cross sections were extracted from gamma-ray yields and compared to theoretical predictions combining large-scale-shell-model (LSSM) calculations in the full fp-model space using the GXPF1A-cd-pn Hamiltonian with eikonal reaction theory.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive cross section for one-neutron knockout from 55Co to bound states of 54Co: 39.0 ± 0.4 (stat.) ± 2.8 (sys.) mb
- Inclusive cross section for one-proton knockout from 55Co to bound states of 54Fe: 141 ± 3 (stat.) ± 16 (sys.) mb
- The candidate T=1, Jπ=6+ state in 54Co is established at 3097(6) keV, with gamma-ray transitions at 2903(4) keV (6+ → 7+1) and 1206(5) keV (6+ → 5+1)
- Experimental triplet energy difference (TED) for J=6: -174(6) keV
- Lifetime of the 6+1 level in 54Fe: τ = 1.76(3) ns
- Lifetime of the isomeric 7+1 state of 54Co: τ = 2.14(3) min

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin symmetry breaking in the A=54, T=1 triplet (54Ni, 54Co, 54Fe), building on prior work by Ormand et al. (2017) who calculated c coefficients of the isobaric mass multiplet equation for fp-shell nuclei, and by Gadea et al. (2006) who compared experimental data on A=54 nuclei with shell-model calculations. The new identification of the 6+ state enables testing the J-dependence of charge-symmetry breaking contributions predicted by realistic chiral effective field theory potentials, and the results show that reducing the predicted CSB part to 60% of the N3LO value provides almost perfect agreement for A=50 and A=54, though the necessary reduction remains a puzzle for nuclear theory.
