# Hidden local symmetry and the reaction e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-π^+π^- at energies \sqrt{s}\leq 1 GeV

> Based on the generalized hidden local symmetry as the chiral model of pseudoscalar, vector, and axial vector mesons, the excitation curve of the reaction e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-π^+π^- is calculated for energies in the interval 0.65\leq \sqrt{s}\leq1 GeV. The theoretical predictions are compared to available data of CMD-2 and BaBaR. It is shown that the inclusion of heavy isovector resonances ρ(1450) and ρ(1700) is necessary for reconciling calculations with the data. It is found that at \sqrt{s}\approx1 GeV the contributions of the above resonances are much larger, by the factor of 30, than the ρ(770) one, and are amount to a considerable fraction \sim0.3-0.6 of the latter at \sqrt{s}\sim m_ρ.

## Key claim

The generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) model, which includes ground-state vector and axial vector resonances with the minimal number of derivatives, fails to explain the cross section of the reaction \(e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^+\pi^-\) at energies \(0.8<\sqrt{s}\leq 1\) GeV; the inclusion of heavy isovector resonances \(\rho(1450)\) and \(\rho(1700)\) is necessary to reconcile calculations with the data, and their contributions are much larger than the \(\rho(770)\) contribution at \(\sqrt{s}\approx 1\) GeV.

## Method

The authors calculated the excitation curve of the reaction \(e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^+\pi^-\) using the generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) chiral model, which fixes all coupling constants in the non-anomalous sector from the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) and the gauge coupling \(g_{\rho\pi\pi}\). The amplitude included contributions from the \(\rho(770)\) meson, the \(a_1(1260)\) meson, and direct photon couplings, with the requirement that the amplitude obeys the Adler condition. The calculated cross section was compared to data from the CMD-2 and BaBar collaborations, and the contributions of the heavier resonances \(\rho(1450)\) and \(\rho(1700)\) were added via a Breit-Wigner parametrization with free parameters \(x_{\rho^\prime}\) and \(x_{\rho^{\prime\prime}}\) fitted to the data.

## Key numbers

- The contribution of the sum \(\rho^\prime+\rho^{\prime\prime}\) relative to the pure GHLS contribution is 0.3 at \(\sqrt{s}\approx m_\rho\) and 32 at \(\sqrt{s}=1\) GeV for the CMD-2 data fit with \(m_{a_1}=1.23\) GeV (variant 3).
- The contribution of the sum \(\rho^\prime+\rho^{\prime\prime}\) relative to the pure GHLS contribution is 0.6 at \(\sqrt{s}\approx m_\rho\) and 30 at \(\sqrt{s}=1\) GeV for the BaBar data fit with \(m_{a_1}=1.23\) GeV (variant 3).
- For the CMD-2 data fit with two heavy resonances (variant 3), the fitted parameters are \(x_{\rho^\prime}=96.8\pm1.5\) and \(x_{\rho^{\prime\prime}}=-208.7\pm2.5\) with \(\chi^2/N_{\rm d.o.f}=14.5/9\) for \(m_{a_1}=1.23\) GeV.
- For the BaBar data fit with two heavy resonances (variant 3), the fitted parameters are \(x_{\rho^\prime}=273.2\pm1.4\) and \(x_{\rho^{\prime\prime}}=-514.5\pm2.3\) with \(\chi^2/N_{\rm d.o.f}=11.2/15\) for \(m_{a_1}=1.23\) GeV.
- The masses and widths used for the heavy resonances are \(m_{\rho^\prime}=1.459\) GeV, \(\Gamma_{\rho^\prime}=0.147\) GeV, \(m_{\rho^{\prime\prime}}=1.72\) GeV, \(\Gamma_{\rho^{\prime\prime}}=0.25\) GeV, taken from the Particle Data Group.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral model calculations of the \(\rho(770)\to 4\pi\) decay width by Achasov and Kozhevnikov (2000, 2005) and tests the GHLS model against experimental data from CMD-2 and BaBar. The results contradict earlier analyses by the CMD-2 collaboration (1999) and by Ecker et al. (2002) and Lichard (1999), which found smaller contributions from heavy resonances; the authors attribute this disagreement to the fact that their parametrization satisfies chiral symmetry requirements (the Adler condition) while the isobar models used in those works do not. The findings enable future studies of the role of scalar resonances and the application of linear sigma-model approaches to evaluate the robustness of the heavy resonance contributions.
