# Comparative study of the direct $α+d$ $\rightarrow$ $^6$Li + $γ$ astrophysical capture reaction in few-body models

> A comparative analysis of the astrophysical S factor and the reaction rate for the direct $ α(d,γ)^{6}{\rm Li}$ capture reaction, and the primordial abundance of the $^6$Li element, resulting from two-body, three-body and combined cluster models is presented. It is shown that the two-body model, based on the exact-mass prescription, can not correctly describe the dependence of the isospin-forbidden E1 S factor on energy and does not reproduce the temperature dependence of the reaction rate from the direct LUNA data. It is demonstrated that the isospin-forbidden E1 astrophysical S factor is very sensitive to the orthogonalization procedure of Pauli-forbidden states within the three-body model. On the other hand, the E2 S factor does not depend on the orthogonalization method. This insures that the orthogonolizing pseudopotentials method yields a very good description of the LUNA collaboration's low-energy direct data. At the same time, the SUSY transformation significantly underestimates the data from the LUNA collaboration. On the other hand, the energy dependence of the E1 S factor are the same in both methods. The best description of the LUNA data for the astrophysical S factor and the reaction rates is obtained within the combined E1(three-body OPP)+E2(two-body) model. It yields a value of $(0.72 \pm 0.01) \times 10^{-14}$ for the $^6$Li/H primordial abundance ratio, consistent with the estimation $(0.80 \pm 0.18) \times 10^{-14}$ of the LUNA collaboration. For the $^6{\rm Li}/^7{\rm Li}$ abundance ratio an estimation $(1.40\pm 0.12)\times 10^{-5}$ is obtained in good agreement with the Standard Model prediction.

## Key claim

The best description of the LUNA collaboration's direct data for the astrophysical S factor and reaction rates is obtained within a combined model that uses the three-body orthogonalizing pseudopotentials (OPP) method for the E1 transition and the two-body model for the E2 transition, yielding a primordial ^6Li/H abundance ratio consistent with the LUNA estimate; however, the two-body model alone cannot correctly describe the energy dependence of the isospin-forbidden E1 S factor or the temperature dependence of the reaction rate.

## Method

The authors calculated the astrophysical S factor and reaction rate for the direct α(d,γ)^6Li capture reaction using two-body, three-body, and combined cluster models. The three-body α+p+n bound-state wave function of ^6Li was computed with the hyperspherical Lagrange-mesh method, using both the orthogonalizing pseudopotentials (OPP) method and the supersymmetric transformation (SUSY) method to handle Pauli-forbidden states in the α-N potential. The two-body model used a deep α-d potential with the exact-mass prescription, and the combined model took the E1 contribution from the three-body OPP calculation and the E2 contribution from the two-body calculation.

## Key numbers

- The combined E1(three-body OPP)+E2(two-body) model yields a ^6Li/H primordial abundance ratio of (0.72 ± 0.01) × 10^{-14}, consistent with the LUNA collaboration estimate of (0.80 ± 0.18) × 10^{-14}.
- The ^6Li/^7Li abundance ratio from the combined model is (1.40 ± 0.12) × 10^{-5}, in agreement with the Standard Model prediction.
- The isotriplet (T=1) component norm square of the ^6Li ground-state wave function is 5.27 × 10^{-3} with the OPP method and 1.10 × 10^{-4} with the SUSY method.
- The S-wave asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) for the α+d configuration is 2.31 fm^{-1/2} in the two-body model, 2.12 fm^{-1/2} in the three-body OPP model, and 2.05 fm^{-1/2} in the three-body SUSY model, compared to the empirical value of 2.30 ± 0.12 fm^{-1/2}.
- The two-body model yields a ^6Li/H abundance ratio range of (0.89-0.92) × 10^{-14}, the three-body OPP model yields (0.66-0.68) × 10^{-14}, and the combined model yields (0.71-0.73) × 10^{-14}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the α(d,γ)^6Li capture reaction using two-body potential models (Tursunov 2015) and three-body models (Tursunov 2016, 2018; Baye and Tursunov 2018) by performing a direct comparison of the OPP and SUSY orthogonalization methods and proposing a combined E1(three-body)+E2(two-body) approach. The results contradict the two-body exact-mass prescription (Nollett 2001; Typel 1997; Mukhamedzhanov 2011, 2016) for the E1 transition, showing it cannot reproduce the LUNA data's temperature dependence, while confirming that the OPP method (Baye and Tursunov 2018) describes the LUNA data well. The combined model enables improved predictions for primordial ^6Li abundance in Big Bang nucleosynthesis models, addressing the discrepancy between astronomical observations and standard BBN predictions noted by Serpico (2004).
