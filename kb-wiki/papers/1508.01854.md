# A Comparative study of hyperon equations of state in supernova simulations

> A comparative study of the $Λ$ hyperon equations of state of Banik, Hempel and Banyopadhyay (BHB) \citep{bhb} and \citet{shen11} (denoted as HShen $Λ$) for core collapse supernova (CCSN) simulations is carried out in this work. The dynamical evolution of a protoneutron star (PNS) into a black hole is investigated in core collapse supernova simulations in the general relativistic one dimensional code using the BHB$Λϕ$ and HShen $Λ$ equation of state (EoS) tables and different progenitor models from Woosley and Heger \citep{Woos}. Radial profiles of the mass fractions of baryons, the density as well as the temperature in the PNS at different moments in time, are compared for both EoS tables. The behaviour of the central density of the PNS with time is demonstrated for those two $Λ$ hyperon EoS tables and compared with their corresponding nuclear EoS tables. It is observed that the black hole formation time is higher in the BHB$Λϕ$ case than in the HShen $Λ$ EoS for the entire set of progenitor models adopted here, because the repulsive $Λ$-$Λ$ interaction makes the BHB$Λϕ$ EoS stiffer. Neutrino emission with the $Λ$ hyperon EoS ceases earlier than that of its nuclear counterpart. The long duration evolution of the shock radius and gravitational mass of the PNS after a successful supernova explosion with enhanced neutrino heating are studied with the BHB$Λϕ$ EoS and $s$20WH07 progenitor model. The PNS is found to remain stable for 4 s and might evolve into a cold neutron star.

# A Comparative study of hyperon equations of state in supernova simulations

## Key claim

The BHBΛφ equation of state, which includes repulsive Λ-Λ interactions mediated by φ mesons and is compatible with a 2 M⊙ neutron star, leads to longer black hole formation times in core collapse supernova simulations than the HShen Λ equation of state, because the repulsive interaction makes the BHBΛφ EoS stiffer. However, the paper notes that this conclusion is drawn from one-dimensional simulations with simplified neutrino treatment, and the quantitative differences between the two hyperon EoSs are small.

## Method

The authors performed spherically symmetric general relativistic core collapse supernova simulations using the GR1D hydrodynamics code with two different Λ hyperon equation of state tables: the BHBΛφ EoS (based on density dependent relativistic hadron field theory with the DD2 parameter set) and the HShen Λ EoS (based on a relativistic mean field model with the TM1 parameter set). They used multiple nonrotating progenitor models from Woosley and Heger (2007) with solar metallicity, ranging from s20WH07 to s40WH07, and compared radial profiles of baryon mass fractions, density, and temperature at various postbounce times. For studying successful supernova explosions and long-term protoneutron star evolution, they enhanced the neutrino heating scale factor to f_heat = 1.5.

## Key numbers

- Maximum cold neutron star mass for BHBΛφ EoS: 2.1 M⊙
- Maximum cold neutron star mass for HShen Λ EoS: 1.75 M⊙
- Black hole formation time for s23WH07 with BHBΛφ EoS: longer than for HShen Λ EoS (exact values not given for BHBΛφ; HShen Λ values are 1.511 s for HS(DD2) and 1.623 s for HShen nuclear EoS)
- Black hole formation time for s40WH07 with HS(DD2) nuclear EoS: 0.942 s after core bounce
- Black hole formation time for s40WH07 with HShen nuclear EoS: 1.084 s after core bounce
- Maximum gravitational PNS mass for s40WH07 with HS(DD2): 2.464 M⊙
- Maximum gravitational PNS mass for s40WH07 with HShen: 2.459 M⊙
- Maximum gravitational PNS mass for s23WH07 with HS(DD2): 2.428 M⊙
- Maximum gravitational PNS mass for s23WH07 with HShen: 2.383 M⊙
- Core bounce time for s40WH07 with HShen Λ: 0.273 s
- Core bounce time for s40WH07 with BHBΛφ: 0.321 s
- Core bounce time for s23WH07 with HShen Λ: 0.266 s
- Core bounce time for s23WH07 with BHBΛφ: 0.315 s
- PNS remained stable for 4 s in successful explosion simulation with BHBΛφ EoS and s20WH07 progenitor
- Final PNS mass after 4 s cooling: approximately 1.64 M⊙
- Maximum PNS mass in successful explosion case: 2.138 M⊙

## Context

This work extends previous core collapse supernova simulations using hyperon equations of state, specifically comparing the BHBΛφ EoS (Banik, Hempel, and Bandyopadhyay, 2014) with the HShen Λ EoS (Shen et al., 2011). It is the first study to perform supernova simulations with a Λ hyperon EoS that satisfies the 2 M⊙ neutron star constraint, addressing the "hyperon puzzle" identified after the discovery of massive neutron stars. The results contrast with earlier simulations using the Ishizuka hyperon EoS (Ishizuka et al., 2008) and the LS+Λ EoS (Oertel et al., 2012), which produced maximum cold neutron star masses below 2 M⊙. The work enables future studies of protoneutron star metastability and black hole formation in the context of events like SN1987A, and calls for more accurate neutrino transport treatments to confirm the neutrino emission signatures of hyperon appearance.
