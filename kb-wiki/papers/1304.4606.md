# Radiative corrections to chiral separation effect in QED

> We calculate the leading radiative corrections to the axial current in the chiral separation effect in dense QED in a magnetic field. Contrary to the conventional wisdom suggesting that the axial current should be exactly fixed by the chiral anomaly relation and is described by the topological contribution on the lowest Landau level in the free theory, we find in fact that the axial current receives nontrivial radiative corrections. The direct calculations performed to the linear order in the external magnetic field show that the nontrivial radiative corrections to the axial current are provided by the Fermi surface singularity in the fermion propagator at nonzero fermion density.

## Key claim

Contrary to the conventional wisdom that the axial current in the chiral separation effect is exactly fixed by the chiral anomaly and receives no higher-order corrections, the authors find that the axial current does receive nontrivial radiative corrections in dense QED in a magnetic field, specifically when calculations are performed to linear order in the external magnetic field and the Fermi surface singularity in the fermion propagator is properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors calculate the leading radiative corrections to the axial current in dense QED at nonzero fermion density in a weak external magnetic field, using an expansion in powers of the magnetic field up to linear order. They employ the Schwinger proper-time representation of the fermion propagator with a nonzero chemical potential, compute the one-loop fermion self-energy and the full fermion propagator to first order in the coupling constant α = e²/(4π), and extract the axial current density from the renormalization-group-invariant expression ⟨j₅³⟩ = -Z₂ tr[γ³γ⁵ G(x,x)].

## Key numbers

- The topological contribution to the axial current in the free theory is ⟨j₅³⟩₀ = -(eB sign(μ)/(2π²)) √(μ² - m²), which comes from the Fermi surface in the weak-field formalism.
- The wave function renormalization constant is δ₂ = -(α/(2π)) (½ ln(Λ²/m²) + ln(m_γ²/m²) + 9/4), where Λ is the ultraviolet regularization parameter and m_γ is an infrared regulator photon mass.
- The mass renormalization counterterm is δm = (3α/(4π)) m (ln(Λ²/m²) + 1/2).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of interaction effects in the chiral separation effect, which were performed in the nonrenormalizable Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by Gorbar, Miransky, and Shovkovy (2010-2011), where four-fermion interactions generated a chiral shift parameter Δ leading to additional dynamical contributions to the axial current. By performing the calculation in renormalizable QED, the authors provide a more rigorous foundation for the existence of radiative corrections to the axial current, resolving a long-standing question about whether the chiral anomaly relation alone fixes the current exactly. The result enables further investigation of how interactions modify transport phenomena in dense relativistic matter in magnetic fields, relevant for heavy-ion collisions and compact astrophysical objects.
