# Neutrino Nucleosynthesis of radioactive nuclei in supernovae

> We study the neutrino-induced production of nuclides in explosive supernova nucleosynthesis for progenitor stars with solar metallicity and initial main sequence masses between 15 M$_\odot$ and 40 M$_\odot$. We improve previous investigations i) by using a global set of partial differential cross sections for neutrino-induced charged- and neutral-current reactions on nuclei with charge numbers $Z < 76 $ and ii) by considering modern supernova neutrino spectra which have substantially lower average energies compared to those previously adopted in neutrino nucleosynthesis studies. We confirm the production of $^7$Li, $^{11}$B, $^{138}$La, and $^{180}$Ta by neutrino nucleosynthesis, albeit at slightly smaller abundances due to the changed neutrino spectra. We find that for stars with a mass smaller than 20 M$_\odot$, $^{19}$F is produced mainly by explosive nucleosynthesis while for higher mass stars it is produced by the $ν$ process. We also find that neutrino-induced reactions, either directly or indirectly by providing an enhanced abundance of light particles, noticeably contribute to the production of the radioactive nuclides $^{22}$Na and $^{26}$Al. Both nuclei are prime candidates for gamma-ray astronomy. Other prime targets, $^{44}$Ti and $^{60}$Fe, however, are insignificantly produced by neutrino-induced reactions. We also find a large increase in the production of the long-lived nuclei $^{92}$Nb and $^{98}$Tc due to charged-current neutrino capture.

## Key claim

The authors confirm that neutrino-induced reactions produce ⁷Li, ¹¹B, ¹³⁸La, and ¹⁸⁰Ta in supernova nucleosynthesis, but at slightly smaller abundances than previously found because they adopt modern supernova neutrino spectra with substantially lower average energies than those used in earlier studies.

## Method

The authors calculated partial differential cross sections for neutrino-induced charged- and neutral-current reactions on all nuclei with charge number Z < 76 using a Random Phase Approximation for nuclear excitation, followed by statistical model decay probabilities from the Modified Smoker and ABLA codes. They applied these cross sections in a reaction network that follows the composition of supernova ejecta for progenitor stars of 15-40 M☉, using a parametrized shock evolution and neutrino luminosity model with modern neutrino temperatures (T_νe = 2.8 MeV, T_ν̄e = T_νμ,τ = 4.0 MeV) based on recent supernova simulations.

## Key numbers

* For a 15 M☉ progenitor, the production factor (relative to solar, normalized to ¹⁶O) for ⁷Li is 0.28 with low-energy neutrinos, compared to 2.54 with high-energy neutrinos and 0.001 without neutrinos.
* For a 15 M☉ progenitor, the production factor for ¹¹B is 1.43 with low-energy neutrinos, compared to 6.13 with high-energy neutrinos and 0.007 without neutrinos.
* For a 25 M☉ progenitor, the production factor for ¹³⁸La is 0.63 with low-energy neutrinos, compared to 1.14 with high-energy neutrinos and 0.03 without neutrinos.
* For a 25 M☉ progenitor, the production factor for ¹⁸⁰Ta is 1.80 with low-energy neutrinos, compared to 2.81 with high-energy neutrinos and 0.14 without neutrinos.
* The yield of ²⁶Al is increased by factors between 1.25 and 2.51 across the progenitor mass range when neutrinos are included.
* The yield of ⁹²Nb is increased on average by a factor of 35 due to charged-current neutrino capture.
* The total yield for ⁹⁸Tc across all progenitors is between 1 × 10⁻¹¹ M☉ and 6 × 10⁻¹¹ M☉.
* The neutrino luminosity is modeled as L_ν = 5/3 × 10⁵² exp(-t/3) erg s⁻¹ with a total energy of 3 × 10⁵³ erg equally distributed in all flavors.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of the ν process in supernovae by Heger et al. (2005) and Woosley et al. (1990), which used higher neutrino energies and cross sections restricted to key nuclei. The authors improve upon those studies by using a global set of partial differential cross sections for all nuclei with Z < 76 and adopting modern neutrino spectra from simulations by Huedepohl et al. (2010) and Martinez-Pinedo et al. (2012, 2014) that have lower average energies. The results enable more reliable predictions for gamma-ray astronomy candidates ²²Na and ²⁶Al, and show that ⁴⁴Ti and ⁶⁰Fe are insignificantly produced by neutrino-induced reactions, while ⁹²Nb and ⁹⁸Tc show large enhancements from charged-current neutrino capture.
