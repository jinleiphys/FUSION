# Taming the Signal-to-Noise Problem in Lattice QCD by Phase Reweighting

> Path integrals describing quantum many-body systems can be calculated with Monte Carlo sampling techniques, but average quantities are often subject to signal-to-noise ratios that degrade exponentially with time. A phase-reweighting technique inspired by recent observations of random walk statistics in correlation functions is proposed that allows energy levels to be extracted from late-time correlation functions with time-independent signal-to-noise ratios. Phase reweighting effectively includes dynamical refinement of source magnitudes but introduces a bias associated with the phase. This bias can be removed by performing an extrapolation, but at the expense of re-introducing a signal-to-noise problem. Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics calculations of the $ρ$ and nucleon masses and of the $ΞΞ$ binding energy show consistency between standard results obtained using earlier-time correlation functions and phase-reweighted results using late-time correlation functions inaccessible to standard statistical analysis methods.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a phase-reweighting technique that allows energy levels to be extracted from late-time Lattice QCD correlation functions with signal-to-noise ratios constant in Euclidean time, but this introduces a bias that must be removed by extrapolation, which re-introduces a signal-to-noise problem that grows exponentially with the extrapolation parameter.

## Method
Phase-reweighted correlation functions are defined as G^θ(t,Δt) = ⟨e^{-iθ_i(t-Δt)} C_i(t)⟩, where θ_i(t-Δt) is the phase of the correlation function at an earlier time. This restricts the time interval over which the complex phase associated with baryon number is allowed to evolve, making the signal-to-noise ratio constant in t while introducing a bias that decreases exponentially with Δt. The bias is removed by extrapolating phase-reweighted effective masses to Δt → t using fits to a constant-plus-exponential form, using LQCD ensembles of approximately 130,000 correlation functions on 2889 gauge-field configurations at M_π ~ 450 MeV.

## Key numbers
- ρ⁺ meson mass extracted from phase-reweighted correlation functions at late times (t = 28→43) is consistent with the standard plateau result from Ref. Orginos (2015) obtained at earlier times (t = 18→28)
- Nucleon excited-state gap extracted from phase-reweighted effective mass extrapolation: δM_N = 786(44)(25) MeV, where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second is systematic from varying the fitting region
- Strange-baryon excited-state gaps: δM_Ξ = 822(44)(71) MeV and δM_{ΞΞ(¹S₀)} = 908(265)(82) MeV
- Ξ⁻Ξ⁻(¹S₀) binding energy from phase-reweighted correlation functions: B_{ΞΞ(¹S₀)} = 15.8(3.5)(2.6) MeV, consistent with the golden window result of 15.4(1.0)(1.4) MeV from Ref. Orginos (2015)
- Ξ⁻Ξ⁻(¹S₀) binding energy from magnitude-only correlation functions (Δt = 0): 7.1(0.6)(0.8) MeV
- Lattice spacing: a ~ 0.117(1) fm on 48³ × 96 lattices at M_π ~ 450 MeV

## Context
This work extends the observation by Wagman (2016) that the signal-to-noise problem in nucleon correlation functions is a sign problem arising from heavy-tailed Lévy flight statistics, and that separately considering magnitude and phase reveals the magnitude has no signal-to-noise problem. The phase-reweighting technique is analogous to phase-pinning methods used in Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations by Zhang (1995, 1996) and Wiringa (2000), and to Wigner-symmetric Hamiltonians used in Lattice Effective Field Theory by Lähde (2015). The method enables extraction of ground-state energies from the "noise region" at late times that is inaccessible to standard source and sink optimization techniques, and may permit calculations of systems without a golden window, such as larger nuclei and dense matter relevant to neutron star interiors.
