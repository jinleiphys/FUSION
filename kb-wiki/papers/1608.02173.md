# Elliptic flow as a probe for $ψ(2S)$ production mechanism in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> I discuss the elliptic flows of $ψ(2S)$ with different production mechanisms in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions. If the final $ψ(2S)$s are mainly from the recombination of uncorrelated charm and anticharm quarks at $T\approx T_c$, charm and anticharm quarks will carry large collective flows of the bulk medium, which will be inherited by the regenerated $ψ(2S)$s. This indicates a larger elliptic flow of $ψ(2S)$ than that of $J/ψ$ which can be regenerated at $T\ge T_c$, $v_2^{ψ(2S)}>v_2^{J/ψ}$. However, if the final $ψ(2S)$s are mainly from the transitions of $J/ψ\rightarrow ψ(2S)$ caused by the color screening of QGP, its elliptic flow should be close to the elliptic flow of $J/ψ$, $v_2^{ψ(2S)}\sim v_2^{J/ψ}$. Therefore, $ψ(2S)$ elliptic flow is a sensitive probe for its production mechanisms in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(\psi(2S)\) can distinguish between two production mechanisms in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV Pb-Pb collisions: if \(\psi(2S)\) comes from recombination of uncorrelated charm quarks at \(T\approx T_c\), its \(v_2\) should be larger than that of \(J/\psi\) (\(v_2^{\psi(2S)} > v_2^{J/\psi}\)); if it comes from transitions of \(J/\psi \rightarrow \psi(2S)\) due to color screening, its \(v_2\) should be similar to that of \(J/\psi\) (\(v_2^{\psi(2S)} \sim v_2^{J/\psi}\)).

## Method

The author simulates charm quark evolution in a (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic QGP background using a Langevin equation with a drag coefficient parameterized by a spatial diffusion coefficient \(D = C/(2\pi T)\), where \(C\) is fitted to D meson data from ALICE. Charmonium regeneration from uncorrelated charm and anticharm quarks is modeled via a coalescence process using a Gaussian Wigner function at a specified regeneration temperature \(T_\Psi\). For the correlated \(c\bar c\) dipole mechanism, the internal evolution of the dipole wavefunction is solved with a time-dependent Schrodinger equation using a finite-temperature heavy quark potential (free energy \(F\) from lattice results), and the fractions of \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\) eigenstates are obtained by projection.

## Key numbers

* The collision energy is \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV for Pb-Pb collisions.
* The maximum initial temperature of QGP at \(\tau_0^{\rm QGP} = 0.6\) fm/c is 484 MeV, with charged multiplicity \(dN_{ch}/dy = 1600\).
* The dissociation temperature of \(\psi(2S)\) is \(T_d(\psi(2S)) \approx T_c\); the dissociation temperature of \(J/\psi\) is \(T_d^{J/\psi} = (1.5-2.0)T_c\).
* The Gaussian width for the Wigner function uses \(\langle r_\Psi^2 \rangle = 0.5^2\) fm\(^2\) from potential model.
* The initial Gaussian width for the \(c\bar c\) dipole wavefunction is \(\sigma_0^{c\bar c} = 0.23\) fm, fitted to the ratio of direct \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2S)\) yields in proton-proton collisions.
* The parameter \(C\) in the spatial diffusion coefficient \(D = C/(2\pi T)\) is varied: \(C = 4.0, 2.0, 1.0\) are tested against D meson data; \(C = 2\) is used for \(\psi(2S)\) predictions.
* Regeneration temperatures considered for charmonium are \(T_\Psi = 1.5 T_c, 1.2 T_c, 1.0 T_c\).
* The hadronization process from charm quarks to D mesons can shift \(v_2(p_T)\) upward by about 20-25%.

## Context

This work extends the study of charmonium production in heavy ion collisions beyond the ground state \(J/\psi\) to the excited state \(\psi(2S)\), which has a smaller binding energy and a dissociation temperature near \(T_c\). It builds on prior models of charmonium regeneration (Thews 2000, Zhao 2011, Andronic 2003) and the time-dependent Schrodinger equation approach for \(c\bar c\) dipole evolution (Kopeliovich 2014, Song 2015). The paper proposes elliptic flow as a new observable to distinguish between the recombination mechanism and the transition mechanism for \(\psi(2S)\), which could be tested by future experimental measurements at the LHC.
