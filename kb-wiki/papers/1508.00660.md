# Identification of Highly Deformed Even-Even Nuclides in the Neutron- and Proton-Rich Regions of the Nuclear Chart from the B(E2) and E2 Predictions in the Generalized Differential Equation Model

> We identify here possible occurrence of large deformations in the neutron- and proton-rich regions of the nuclear chart from extensive predictions of the values of the reduced quadrupole transition probability B-E2 for the transition from the ground state to the first 2+ state and the corresponding excitation energy E2 of even-even nuclei in the recently developed Generalized Differential Equation model exclusively meant for these physical quantities. This is made possible from our analysis of the predicted values of these two physical quantities and the corresponding deformation parameters derived from them such as the quadrupole deformation beta-2, the ratio of beta-2 to the Weisskopf single-particle beta-2 and the intrinsic electric quadruplole moment , calculated for a large number of both known as well as hitherto unknown even-even isotopes of Oxygen to Fermium (Z=8 to 100). Our critical analysis of the resulting data convincingly support possible existence of large collectivity for the nuclides 30,32 Ne, 34 Mg, 60 Ti, 42,62,64 Cr, 50,68 Fe, 52,72 Ni, 72,70,96 Kr, 74,76 Sr,78,80,106,108 Zr , 82,84,110,112 Mo, 140 Te,144 Xe, 148 Ba, 122 Ce, 128,156 Nd, 130,132,158,160 Sm and 138,162,164,166 Gd, whose values of beta-2 are found to exceed 0.3 and even 0.4 in some cases. Our findings of large deformations in the exotic neutron-rich regions support the existence of another Island of Inversion in the heavy-mass region possibly caused by breaking of the N=70 sub-shell closure.

## Key claim

The paper identifies possible highly deformed even-even nuclides in neutron- and proton-rich regions by analyzing predictions from the Generalized Differential Equation (GDE) model, claiming that values of the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ exceeding 0.3 (and in some cases 0.4) indicate large collectivity. The authors hedge that these are predictions for "hitherto unknown" isotopes, supported by comparison with some existing experimental data, and that the findings suggest another "Island of Inversion" in the heavy-mass region possibly caused by breaking of the N=70 sub-shell closure.

## Method

The authors used the GDE model, which expresses B(E2)↑ and E2 for even-even nuclei in terms of their partial derivatives with respect to neutron and proton numbers, to generate recursion relations connecting neighboring nuclei. Using 330 known B(E2)↑ data points and 557 known E2 data points from Raman et al. (2001) and Pritychenko et al. compilations as input, they applied three to four generations of predictions to obtain B(E2)↑ values for 278 additional isotopes and E2 values for 175 additional isotopes. From these predicted values, they calculated the quadrupole deformation β₂, the ratio β₂/β₂(sp), and the intrinsic electric quadrupole moment Q₀ using standard model-dependent formulas assuming uniform charge distributions.

## Key numbers

- β₂ values exceeding 0.3 and in some cases 0.4 were found for nuclides including ³⁰,³²Ne, ³⁴Mg, ⁶⁰Ti, ⁴²,⁶²,⁶⁴Cr, ⁵⁰,⁶⁸Fe, ⁵²,⁷²Ni, ⁷⁰,⁷²,⁹⁶Kr, ⁷⁴,⁷⁶Sr, ⁷⁸,⁸⁰,¹⁰⁶,¹⁰⁸Zr, ⁸²,⁸⁴,¹¹⁰,¹¹²Mo, ¹⁴⁰Te, ¹⁴⁴Xe, ¹⁴⁸Ba, ¹²²Ce, ¹²⁸,¹⁵⁶Nd, ¹³⁰,¹³²,¹⁵⁸,¹⁶⁰Sm, and ¹³⁸,¹⁶²,¹⁶⁴,¹⁶⁶Gd
- For Ne (Z=10), β₂ = 0.59 at N=20 and 0.63 at N=22, with βᵣ values of 3.73 and 3.99 respectively
- For Ti (Z=22), β₂ increases from 0.075 to 0.513 with increasing neutron number from N=32 to 38, while βᵣ increases from 1.043 to 7.116
- For ⁶⁰Ti, β₂ = 0.51 and βᵣ = 7.11
- For isotopes of Kr, Sr, and Zr (Z=36, 38, 40), β₂ values lie in the range 0.40-0.49 and βᵣ values in the range 10.3-11.6
- For Mo isotopes (⁸²,⁸⁴,¹¹⁰,¹¹²Mo), β₂ values lie in the range 0.39-0.46 and βᵣ values in the range 10.3-12.2
- For Ce, Nd, Sm, and Gd isotopes (Z=58, 60, 62, 64), β₂ values lie in the range 0.36-0.46 and βᵣ values in the range 14.2-17.5
- Predicted E2 values for isotopes claimed to have large deformations in low- and medium-mass regions lie in the range 0.35-1.4 MeV
- Predicted and experimental E2 values for claimed isotopes in the heavy-mass region lie in the range 0.07-0.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the GDE model previously developed by the same authors for B(E2)↑ (Nayak and Pattnaik, earlier papers) and E2, applying it to predict deformations in exotic regions of the nuclear chart. The findings support the existence of "Islands of Inversion" at N=20 (already established experimentally by Yanagisawa et al. and others) and N=40 (supported by Sorlin et al. and Crawford et al.), and predict a new one at N=70. The predictions are compared with experimental data from Raman et al. (2001) and Pritychenko et al. compilations, and the authors note that 77 new B(E2)↑ data points and 65 new E2 data points from a recent Pritychenko et al. compilation are available for future testing of the model's reliability.
