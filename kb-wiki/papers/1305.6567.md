# Physical interpretation of the dressed Polyakov loop in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> We investigate the rapid rise of the dressed Polyakov loop in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as a function of temperature. In QCD such a behaviour is interpreted as a confinement- deconfinement phase transition. However, we demonstrate that in the NJL model this is simply a remnant of the chiral transition.

## Key claim

The rapid rise of the dressed Polyakov loop in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as a function of temperature is not a signal of confinement-deconfinement, but is simply a remnant of the chiral phase transition; the temperature at which the derivative of the dressed Polyakov loop diverges coincides exactly with the chiral restoration temperature in the chiral limit, independent of model parameters.

## Method

The author performs a semi-analytic Landau analysis of the NJL model thermodynamic potential in the chiral limit with two flavors and zero chemical potential, using twisted boundary conditions (equivalent to an imaginary chemical potential) to compute the dressed Polyakov loop as the first Fourier mode of the quark condensate. The analysis employs expansions in \(\sigma/\Lambda \ll 1\) and \(\sigma/T \ll 1\) to derive the quadratic coefficient \(a(T,\phi)\) and the critical temperature \(T_c(\phi)\), then computes the temperature derivative of the dressed Polyakov loop analytically, identifying its divergence at the chiral transition temperature \(T_\chi\).

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for general twisted angle \(\phi\) is given by \(T_c(\phi) = \frac{8\pi^2}{d_q a_0}\frac{1}{\frac{\pi^2}{3}-(\phi-\pi)^2}\), where \(d_q = 2\times 2\times N_f\times N_c\) and \(a_0 = 1/G_c - 1/G\).
- The boundaries between fermion-like and boson-like twisted angles occur at \(\phi_\pm = \pi \pm \pi/\sqrt{3}\).
- The numerical calculation uses parameters \(G\Lambda^2 = 4.636\) and \(\Lambda = 602.472\) MeV from Grigorian (2006).
- The derivative \(d\phi_c(T)/dT\) diverges as \(T \to T_\chi\) from above, scaling as \((1 - T_\chi^2/T^2)^{-1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the numerical observation of Mukherjee (2010) that the dressed Polyakov loop shows a rapid rise in the NJL model, providing an analytic proof that this behavior is entirely due to the chiral transition rather than confinement. The result contrasts with QCD, where the dressed Polyakov loop signals confinement-deconfinement (Polyakov 1978, Gattringer 2006, Bilgici 2008), and is consistent with lattice QCD calculations in the strong coupling limit by Fromm (2011), where a similar cusp in the Polyakov loop is interpreted as an imprint of the chiral transition. This enables a clearer interpretation of model calculations of the dressed Polyakov loop in non-confining effective theories.
