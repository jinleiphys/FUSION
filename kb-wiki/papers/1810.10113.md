# How different is the core of $^{25}$F from $^{24}$O$_{g.s.}$?

> The neutron-shell structure of $^{25}$F was studied using quasi-free (p,2p) knockout reaction at 270A MeV in inverse kinematics. The sum of spectroscopic factors of $π$0d$_{5/2}$ orbital is found to be $1.0 \pm 0.3$. However, the spectroscopic factor for the ground-state to ground-state transition ($^{25}$F, $^{24}$O$_{g.s.}$) is only $0.36\pm 0.13$, and $^{24}$O excited states are produced from the 0d$_{5/2}$ proton knockout. The result shows that the $^{24}$O core of $^{25}$F nucleus significantly differs from a free $^{24}$O nucleus, and the core consists of 35% $^{24}$O$_{g.s}$. and 65% excited $^{24}$O.

## Key claim

The core of 25F is not simply a free 24O ground-state nucleus; only about 35% of the 25F core overlaps with 24O(g.s.), while the remaining 65% consists of excited 24O configurations, indicating that the addition of a single 0d5/2 proton significantly alters the neutron-shell structure of the oxygen core.

## Method

The neutron-shell structure of 25F was studied using a quasi-free (p,2p) knockout reaction at 270A MeV in inverse kinematics at the RIKEN Radioactive Isotopes Beam Factory. The 25F beam impinged on a 1-mm thick C10H8 target, and the two scattered protons were detected in coincidence with the residual oxygen fragments. The excitation energy of the residual 24O was reconstructed from the missing four-momentum, and the spectroscopic factors for different knockout channels were extracted by comparing experimental cross sections to distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations using the codes PIKOE and THREEDEE.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for the ground-state to ground-state transition (25F, 24O(g.s.)): 0.36 ± 0.13
- Sum of spectroscopic factors for the π0d5/2 orbital from all channels: 1.0 ± 0.3
- Spectroscopic factor for the (25F, 23O) channel (0d5/2 knockout to excited states): 0.65 ± 0.25
- Spectroscopic factor for the (25F, 22O) channel (0p1/2 knockout): 3.1 ± 1.3
- Mean excitation energy of the (25F, 24O) channel: -0.5 ± 1.1 MeV (consistent with ground-state transition)
- Mean excitation energy of the (25F, 23O) channel: 6.5 ± 1.4 MeV
- Mean excitation energy of the (25F, 22O) channel: 12.7 ± 0.6 MeV
- Beam energy: 270A MeV; beam intensity: 1.69 × 10^4 pps; beam purity: 42%

## Context

This work extends earlier knockout measurements on 25F by Thoennessen (2003), which reported a spectroscopic factor of about 0.4 but lacked sufficient precision for detailed conclusions. The results contradict shell-model predictions using USDB, SFO, and SDPF-MU interactions, which all predict ground-state spectroscopic factors near 0.9-1.0. The finding that the 24O core of 25F differs substantially from free 24O(g.s.) provides insight into the oxygen dripline anomaly, where the neutron dripline extends from N=16 for oxygen to N=22 for fluorine. The authors suggest that a stronger proton-neutron tensor interaction, possibly combined with three-nucleon forces, may be responsible for the observed core modification, and that the ν0d3/2 single-particle energy may need to be lowered by 3-4 MeV to reproduce the experimental results.
