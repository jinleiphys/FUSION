# Impact of the Scalar Isovector $δ$-meson on the description of nuclear matter and neutron star properties

> The implications of including the scalar isovector $δ$-meson in a relativistic mean-field description of nuclear matter are discussed. A Bayesian inference approach is used to determine the parameters that define the isovector properties of the model. The properties of nuclear matter and neutron stars are discussed. The inclusion of the $δ$-meson has only a small effect on the maximum mass of the neutron star (NS) and on the speed of sound in its interior, but it has a strong effect on the radius and the tidal deformability of low and medium mass stars. This is mainly due to the effect of the $δ$-meson on the symmetry energy and its slope and curvature at saturation, increasing the range of possible values of these three properties, and in particular allowing positive values of the symmetry energy curvature. Due to the effect of the $δ$-meson on the symmetry energy, the proton content of the star is also strongly affected. The inclusion of the $δ$-meson in the relativistic mean-field description of nuclear matter extends the phase space spanned by the model, allowing for a more flexible density dependence of the symmetry energy compatible with experimental, observational, and ab initio constraints.

# Impact of the Scalar Isovector δ-meson on the description of nuclear matter and neutron star properties

## Key claim
Including the δ-meson in a relativistic mean-field description of nuclear matter has only a small effect on the maximum neutron star mass and interior speed of sound, but strongly affects the radius and tidal deformability of low and medium mass stars, primarily by broadening the allowed range of the symmetry energy, its slope, and curvature at saturation, including allowing positive values of the symmetry energy curvature.

## Method
Three representative relativistic mean-field equations of state (EOS8, EOS20, EOS21) without the δ-meson were selected from prior work (Malik 2024) based on their stiffness (maximum masses of 2.24, 2.38, and 2.57 solar masses respectively). A Bayesian inference approach using nested sampling with 2000 live points was performed to determine three free parameters: the ρ-meson coupling g_ρ, the δ-meson coupling g_δ, and the ωρ mixing coupling g_{ωρ}, constrained by empirical nuclear saturation properties (binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy) and χEFT pure neutron matter pressures at three densities.

## Key numbers
- Maximum masses of the three base EOS models: 2.24, 2.38, and 2.57 solar masses
- Symmetry energy E_sym at saturation: median ~32 MeV across all three models, with dispersion ~1.5 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope L: original models spanned 32 to 42 MeV; with δ-meson, two models reach values above 60 MeV at 3σ
- Symmetry energy curvature K_sym: three well-separated medians at approximately -10, -50, and -140 MeV, with one model allowing values above +50 MeV and another below -200 MeV
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass star: varies by 1 km (from 12.3 to 13.3 km) across the posterior distributions
- Radius of a 2.0 solar mass star: varies by 200 to 250 m around the original radii
- Percentage of models with direct Urca processes: 0.59% for EOS8, 99.98% for EOS20, 99.98% for EOS21
- 90% CI for radius of 1.0 solar mass star: 11.84 to 12.79 km (EOS8), 12.11 to 12.82 km (EOS20), 12.28 to 13.05 km (EOS21)

## Context
This work extends prior studies of the δ-meson in relativistic mean-field models (PhysRevC.65.045201; Gaitanos 2003; Menezes 2004) by performing a systematic Bayesian calibration of the isovector channel. It builds on the three unified EOS from Malik (2024) and compares with χEFT calculations from Hebeler (2013) and Huth (2021). The results show that including the δ-meson allows a more flexible density dependence of the symmetry energy, enabling simultaneous compatibility with experimental, observational, and ab initio constraints. The study enables future work to reconcile conflicting correlations between symmetry energy slope and curvature found in prior studies (Vidana 2009; Char 2023; Malik 2022; Salinas 2023), and to better describe neutron star observations from NICER and gravitational wave events like GW170817.
