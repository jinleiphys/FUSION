# Joint thrust and TMD resummation in electron-positron and electron-proton collisions

> We present the framework for obtaining precise predictions for the transverse momentum of hadrons with respect to the thrust axis in $e^+e^-$ collisions. This will enable a precise extraction of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions from a recent measurement by the Belle Collaboration. Our analysis takes into account, for the first time, the nontrivial interplay between the hadron transverse momentum and the cut on the thrust event shape. To this end, we identify three different kinematic regions, derive the corresponding factorization theorems within Soft Collinear Effective Theory, and present all ingredients needed for the joint resummation of the transverse momentum and thrust spectrum at NNLL accuracy. One kinematic region can give rise to non-global logarithms (NGLs), and we describe how to include the leading NGLs. We also discuss alternative measurements in $e^+e^-$ collisions that can be used to access the TMD fragmentation function. Finally, by using crossing symmetry, we obtain a new way to constrain TMD parton distributions, by measuring the displacement of the thrust axis in $ep$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first theoretical framework that accounts for the nontrivial interplay between the hadron transverse momentum and the cut on the thrust event shape, enabling a precise extraction of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions from Belle Collaboration data, but only under the condition that neither \(z_h\) nor \(1-z_h\) is much smaller than one, as additional logarithms in those limits are not resummed.

## Method

The authors identify three distinct kinematic regions based on the parametric relationship between the thrust \(\tau\) and the hadron transverse momentum \(q_T/Q\): Region 1 where \(q_T/Q \sim \tau\), Region 2 where \(q_T/Q\) is intermediate between \(\tau\) and \(\sqrt{\tau}\), and Region 3 where \(q_T/Q \sim \sqrt{\tau}\). For each region, they derive factorization theorems using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) and SCET\(_+\), presenting all ingredients needed for joint resummation of transverse momentum and thrust at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy. They also describe how to include leading non-global logarithms (NGLs) that arise in one kinematic region, and extend the formalism to electron-proton collisions via crossing symmetry.

## Key numbers

* The kinematic bound on phase space is \(\sqrt{\tau} \gtrsim q_T/Q \gtrsim \tau\), derived from the on-shell condition and thrust definition.
* The leading NGL contribution is \(S^{\rm NG}(q_T/(\tau Q)) = 1 - (\alpha_s^2 C_F C_A / 12) \ln^2(q_T/(\tau Q)) + \mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^3)\).
* The natural renormalization scales for Region 1 are: \(\mu_H \sim Q\), \(\mu_J \sim \sqrt{\tau} Q\), \(\mu_D \sim \mu_S \sim 1/b_T \sim \tau Q\), with rapidity scales \(\nu_D \sim Q\) and \(\nu_S \sim 1/b_T\).
* The natural renormalization scales for Region 2 are: \(\mu_H \sim \nu_D \sim Q\), \(\mu_J \sim \sqrt{\tau} Q\), \(\nu_\CS \sim 1/(b_T^2 \tau Q)\), \(\mu_D \sim \mu_\CS \sim 1/b_T\), \(\mu_{S_{\rm thr}} \sim \tau Q\).
* The natural renormalization scales for Region 3 are: \(\mu_H \sim Q\), \(\mu_J \sim \mu_{\mathcal{G}} \sim \sqrt{\tau} Q\), \(\mu_{S_{\rm thr}} \sim \tau Q\).

## Context

This work extends the factorization framework for joint measurements of thrust and transverse momentum, previously developed for Drell-Yan production by Procura et al. (2014) and Lustermans et al. (2019), to electron-positron annihilation and electron-proton collisions. It directly enables the extraction of TMD fragmentation functions from the recent Belle Collaboration measurement by Seidl et al. (2019), and provides a new method to constrain TMD parton distributions in deep-inelastic scattering by measuring the displacement of the thrust axis. The analysis also relates to earlier work by Jain et al. (2011) on factorization for these observables, and to studies by Boglione et al. (2017) and Soleymaninia et al. (2019) on phenomenological applications to Belle data.
