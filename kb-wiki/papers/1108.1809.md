# Electroproduction of $Λ$(1405)

> The electroproduction of $K^+ Λ$ (1405) was studied by analyzing the E1F data set collected in Hall B at Jefferson Lab. The analysis utilized the decay channel $Σ^+ π^-$ of the $Λ$ (1405) and $p π^0$ of the $Σ^+$. Simulations of background, $Λ$ (1405) and $Λ$ (1520) production according to PDG values were performed by using standard CLAS analysis tools adapted for the E1F run. Fits of the acceptance-corrected simulations were made to the acceptance-corrected data to determine contributions from signal and background processes. The line shape of $Λ$ (1405) varies with the four momentum transfer, $Q^2$, and does not match the line shape based on PDG resonance parameters. It corresponds approximately to predictions of a recent two-pole meson-baryon picture of this state.

## Key claim

The line shape of the Λ(1405) in electroproduction varies with the four-momentum transfer Q² and does not match the line shape based on PDG resonance parameters, corresponding approximately to predictions of a recent two-pole meson-baryon picture of this state.

## Method

The analysis used the E1F data set from Hall B at Jefferson Lab with a 5.5 GeV electron beam on a liquid hydrogen target, selecting events with exactly four detected charged particles (e⁻, K⁺, p, π⁻) to reconstruct the Λ(1405) via the decay chain Λ(1405) → Σ⁺π⁻, Σ⁺ → pπ⁰. Simulations of background (nonresonant K⁺Σ⁺π⁻ and resonant K*⁰Σ⁺), Λ(1405), and Λ(1520) production according to PDG values were performed using standard CLAS analysis tools adapted for the E1F run. Acceptance correction was performed using a three-body phase space simulation binned in Q², W, and kaon angle, and fits of acceptance-corrected simulations were made to acceptance-corrected data to determine contributions from signal and background processes.

## Key numbers

- Electron beam energy: 5.5 GeV
- Q² range after event selection: 1.0 GeV² to 3.0 GeV²
- For the higher Q² range (1.5 to 3.0 GeV²), fit parameters for the two relativistic Breit-Wigner functions gave m₀ = 1.422 GeV and Γ₀ = 0.016 GeV for the peak around 1.42 GeV, and m₀ = 1.393 GeV and Γ₀ = 0.100 GeV for the peak below 1.4 GeV
- The missing mass selection for Σ⁺ required the missing mass of e⁻K⁺π⁻ between 1.14 GeV and 1.24 GeV
- The missing mass selection for π⁰ required the missing mass of e⁻K⁺pπ⁻ between 0.05 GeV and 0.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of the Λ(1405) resonance into electroproduction, following earlier theoretical predictions by Jido (2003) using a chiral unitary formalism that described the Λ(1405) as formed by two I=0 poles at 1390+i0.066 GeV and 1420+i0.016 GeV. The observed Q² dependence of the line shape and the two-peak structure in the higher Q² range favor the two-pole theoretical prediction over a single Breit-Wigner description based on PDG resonance parameters, and the results enable further exploration of Λ(1405) phenomenology in electroproduction where no specific predictions had been made.
