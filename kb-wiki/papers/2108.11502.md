# Probing the Structure of Deuteron at Very Short Distances

> We study the electrodisintegration of deuteron at quasi-elastic kinematics and high transferred momentum as a probe for the short distance structure in nuclei. In this reaction, an electron hits a nucleus of deuterium, which breaks up into a proton-neutron pair. We focus our attention on events where fast nucleons emerge, corresponding to nuclear configurations where the bound nucleons have a high relative momentum (exceeding 700 MeV/c). The present research is relevant to physical systems where high-density nuclear matter is present. This condition covers a wide range of physics, from neutron stars to nuclei stability and the repulsive nuclear core. The present work differs from previous studies in two crucial features. One is the definition of the deuteron wave function, which include terms of a relativistic origin that can be ordered based on their relative contribution to the deuteron's internal momentum distribution. These terms, related to the off-shell properties of the nucleon-nucleon bound-state, do not occur in non-relativistic quantum mechanics. However, they become increasingly important in describing configurations with a high nucleon-nucleon relative momentum. The second difference is that we account for the off-shell nature of the bound nucleon that enters on the definition of the (half-off-shell) electromagnetic current. We avoid many of the difficulties inherent to the relativistic nature of the processes involved by adopting a theoretical framework known as Light Front dynamics. Simultaneous simplifications in the definition of the relativistic wave function for the proton-neutron bound state and the treatment of the (half-off-shell) electromagnetic current for the bound nucleon are among the essential advantages resulting from the use of the Light Front dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that relativistic corrections to the deuteron wave function and the off-shell nature of the bound nucleon's electromagnetic current, treated within Light Front dynamics, become increasingly important for describing electrodisintegration events where the proton-neutron relative momentum exceeds 700 MeV/c, and that this framework can explain recent Jefferson Laboratory experimental results probing internal momenta near 1 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors study deuteron electrodisintegration at quasi-elastic kinematics using Light Front dynamics, which simplifies the relativistic treatment of the bound-state wave function and the half-off-shell electromagnetic current. They incorporate a deuteron wave function that includes relativistic terms ordered by their contribution to the internal momentum distribution, and they account for the off-shell nature of the bound nucleon in the electromagnetic vertex using a form that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity. The nucleonic tensor is decomposed into propagating and instantaneous contributions, and explicit expressions for the structure functions \(w_L\), \(w_{TL}\), \(w_T\), and \(w_{TT}\) are derived in terms of the nucleon form factors \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) and kinematic variables expressed in light-cone coordinates.

## Key numbers

* The study focuses on nuclear configurations where bound nucleons have a high relative momentum exceeding **700 MeV/c**.
* The calculations are motivated by recent Jefferson Laboratory experiments that probed very high internal momentum configurations in the deuteron at approximately **1 GeV/c**.
* The off-shell factor \(\Delta p_i^-\) is given by \(\Delta p_i^- = \frac{Q^2}{q^+} - \frac{m_N^2 + \mathbf{p}_\text{T}^2}{p_f^+ p_i^+} q^+\), where \(m_N\) is the nucleon mass and \(Q^2\) is the squared four-momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deuteron electrodisintegration by incorporating relativistic corrections to the wave function and off-shell effects in the electromagnetic current, which are neglected in non-relativistic quantum mechanics. The authors note that their approach differs from prior work in two key features: the definition of the deuteron wave function with relativistic terms ordered by momentum contribution, and the treatment of the half-off-shell electromagnetic current for the bound nucleon. The framework is intended to provide explanations for recent Jefferson Laboratory experiments that have probed relativistic internal momentum configurations in the deuteron, and it is relevant to understanding high-density nuclear matter in systems such as neutron stars and the repulsive nuclear core.
