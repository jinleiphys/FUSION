# Time evolution of the chiral phase transition during a spherical expansion

> We examine the non-equilibrium time evolution of the hadronic plasma produced in a relativistic heavy ion collision, assuming a spherical expansion into the vacuum. We study the $O(4)$ linear sigma model to leading order in a large-$N$ expansion. Starting at a temperature above the phase transition, the system expands and cools, finally settling into the broken symmetry vacuum state. We consider the proper time evolution of the effective pion mass, the order parameter $\langle σ\rangle$, and the particle number distribution. We examine several different initial conditions and look for instabilities (exponentially growing long wavelength modes) which can lead to the formation of disoriented chiral condensates (DCCs). We find that instabilities exist for proper times which are less than 3 fm/c. We also show that an experimental signature of domain growth is an increase in the low momentum spectrum of outgoing pions when compared to an expansion in thermal equilibrium. In comparison to particle production during a longitudinal expansion, we find that in a spherical expansion the system reaches the ``out'' regime much faster and more particles get produced. However the size of the unstable region, which is related to the domain size of DCCs, is not enhanced.

## Key claim

The authors find that instabilities (exponentially growing long wavelength modes) which can lead to disoriented chiral condensates exist for proper times less than 3 fm/c, but the size of the unstable region is not enhanced compared to a longitudinal expansion, contrary to the expectation that the more rapid cooling of a spherical expansion would produce larger domains.

## Method

The authors studied the non-equilibrium time evolution of the hadronic plasma using the O(4) linear sigma model to leading order in a large-N expansion, assuming a spherical expansion into the vacuum with scale invariant kinematics (v = r/t). They solved the time-dependent equations of motion for the auxiliary field χ, the order parameter ⟨σ⟩, and the pion field, starting from thermal equilibrium at T = 200 MeV and proper time τ₀ = 1 fm, and examined the proper time evolution of the effective pion mass and particle number distribution.

## Key numbers

- Instabilities exist for proper times less than 3 fm/c
- The range of initial field derivatives that produce instabilities is 0.15 < |σ̇| < 4.95 (in units of fm⁻¹), compared to 0.25 < |σ̇| < 1.3 for longitudinal expansion
- Initial temperature was T = 200 MeV, giving σ_T = 0.3 fm⁻¹
- The pion decay constant f_π = 92.5 MeV and renormalized coupling λ_R = 7.3
- The cutoff Λ = 800 MeV was used (since Λ = 1 GeV is too close to the Landau pole)
- The system reaches the "out" regime at proper times greater than approximately 10 fm
- The size of the unstable region (χ < 0) is at most 2-3 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of disoriented chiral condensate formation by Cooper et al. (1995) from a longitudinal expansion to a spherical expansion, motivated by the fact that at late times the expansion becomes spherical and produces the most rapid cooling. The authors compare their results to a hydrodynamical model calculation assuming local thermal equilibrium, and find that the non-equilibrium evolution produces an enhancement of low momentum pions. The next step would be to incorporate next-order large-N effects (scattering in the background mean field) which could allow for re-equilibration.
