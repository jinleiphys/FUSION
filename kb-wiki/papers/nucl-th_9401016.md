# Seeing the QCD phase transition with phi mesons

> A double phi peak structure in the dilepton invariant mass spectrum from ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is proposed as a signal for the phase transition from the quark-gluon plasma to the hadronic matter. The low mass phi peak results from the decay of phi mesons with reduced in-medium mass during the transition. Furthermore, the measurement of the transverse momentum distribution of these low mass phi mesons offers a viable means for determining the temperature of the phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a double phi meson peak in the dilepton invariant mass spectrum from ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions can serve as a signal for a first-order phase transition from quark-gluon plasma to hadronic matter, but only if the phase transition is indeed first-order and strong enough to produce a mixed phase with sufficient duration.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules to calculate the temperature-dependent phi meson mass in hot hadronic matter, finding a reduction at high temperatures. They then performed boost-invariant hydrodynamical calculations with transverse flow for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, integrating the dilepton yield from phi meson decays in both the mixed phase (with reduced mass) and at freezeout (with free mass), using a Gaussian smearing function with sigma = 10 MeV to account for experimental mass resolution.

## Key numbers

- The phi meson vacuum lifetime is approximately 45 fm/c, while the duration time for the system to stay near the transition temperature is about 10 fm/c in boost-invariant hydrodynamical calculations with transverse flow
- The critical temperature T_c = 180 MeV and freezeout temperature T_f = 120 MeV were used in the calculation
- The standard initial temperature T_i = 250 MeV and initial proper time tau_0 = 1 fm were used for the main results
- The low mass phi peak appears around 880 MeV, compared to the normal phi meson mass of 1019 MeV
- The experimental mass resolution in future RHIC dilepton measurements is about 5 to 10 MeV around the phi meson mass
- The smearing function width sigma was taken as 10 MeV
- The phi meson width in hot hadronic matter from scattering with pions, kaons, rho mesons, and phi mesons is less than 10 MeV at all temperatures

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on M_T scaling in dilepton spectra as a quark-gluon plasma signature (Asakawa and Ko, 1993) and builds on QCD sum rule calculations of vector meson mass modifications at finite temperature by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and Furnstahl et al. (1990). The proposed double phi peak signal contrasts with an earlier suggestion for a double J/psi peak by Miao (1986), which was later shown to be unobservable due to the long J/psi lifetime. The measurement of the transition temperature from low mass phi meson transverse momentum distributions improves upon a similar proposal using rho mesons by Seibert (1992), since rho meson decays during hadronic expansion contaminate the signal. The paper enables experimental searches at RHIC and LHC for first-order phase transition signatures and temperature determination.
