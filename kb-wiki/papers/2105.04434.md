# Revisiting quark and gluon polarization in the proton at the EIC

> We present a comprehensive impact study of future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) data for parity-conserving and parity-violating polarization asymmetries on quark and gluon helicity distributions in the proton. The study, which is based on the JAM Monte Carlo global QCD analysis framework, explores the role of the extrapolation uncertainty and SU(3) flavor symmetry constraints in the simulated double-spin asymmetry, $A_{LL}$, at small parton momentum fractions $x$ and its effect on the extracted parton polarizations. We find that different assumptions about $A_{LL}$ extrapolations and SU(3) symmetry can have significant consequences for the integrated quark and gluon polarizations, for polarized proton, deuteron and $^3$He beams. For the parity-violating asymmetry, $A_{UL}$, we study the potential impact on the polarized strange quark distribution with different extrapolations of $A_{UL}$, finding the constraining power to be ultimately limited by the EIC machine luminosity.

## Key claim

The impact of future EIC data on quark and gluon helicity distributions depends critically on assumptions about the low-\(x\) extrapolation of the double-spin asymmetry \(A_{LL}\) and on whether SU(3) flavor symmetry constraints are imposed, with different choices leading to significantly different projected uncertainties for the integrated parton polarizations.

## Method

The authors performed a next-to-leading-order (NLO) global QCD analysis using the JAM Monte Carlo framework, first fitting spin-averaged PDFs to fixed-target DIS, HERA, Drell-Yan, and jet data, then fitting spin-dependent PDFs to polarized DIS and RHIC jet data. They simulated EIC pseudodata for the parity-conserving asymmetry \(A_{LL}\) (for proton, deuteron, and \(^3\)He beams) and the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_{UL}\) (for proton beams), generating low, mid, and high extrapolation scenarios below \(x \sim 0.01\) and considering both SU(2)-only and SU(2)+SU(3) axial-vector charge constraints.

## Key numbers

- The EIC will extend \(A_{LL}\) measurements down to \(x \approx 2 \times 10^{-4}\), nearly two orders of magnitude smaller than the range of existing data.
- For the spin-averaged fit, the total \(\chi^2\) was approximately 3,665 over 3,126 data points, with \(\chi^2_{\mathrm{dof}} = 1.17\).
- For the spin-dependent baseline fit, the total \(\chi^2\) was approximately 697 over 696 data points, with \(\chi^2_{\mathrm{dof}} = 1.00\).
- The assumed integrated luminosity for proton beams was 100 fb\(^{-1}\); for deuteron and \(^3\)He beams it was 10 fb\(^{-1}\).
- Deuteron pseudodata reduce uncertainties on the neutron \(g_1^n\) structure function by a factor of 2–4 depending on whether SU(3) is imposed.
- The EIC center-of-mass energies considered for the proton were \(\sqrt{s} = 29, 45, 63, 141\) GeV; for deuteron and \(^3\)He they were \(\sqrt{s} = 29, 66, 89\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous impact studies by Aschenauer et al. (2015, 2020) by performing a full global QCD analysis (rather than reweighting) that includes polarized deuteron and \(^3\)He beams, parity-violating \(A_{UL}\) asymmetries, and a systematic assessment of low-\(x\) extrapolation uncertainties. The results enable more realistic projections for the EIC's ability to constrain the strange quark helicity distribution and the total quark and gluon spin contributions to the proton, while also highlighting that the constraining power of \(A_{UL}\) is ultimately limited by the EIC machine luminosity.
