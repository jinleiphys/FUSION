# Quarkyonic Matter and the Revised Phase Diagram of QCD

> At high baryon number density, it has been proposed that a new phase of QCD matter controls the physics. This matter is confining but can have densities much larger than $Λ^3_{QCD}$ QCD. Its existence is argued from large Nc approximations, and model computations. It is approximately chirally symmetric.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that a new phase of QCD matter, called "Quarkyonic matter," exists at high baryon number density where matter remains confining even at densities much larger than the QCD scale, and is approximately chirally symmetric. The argument is based on large Nc approximations and model computations, with the caveat that the existence for Nc = 3 is not definitively proven and relies on extrapolation from the large Nc limit.

## Method
The author uses the large Nc approximation (Nc → ∞ with fixed coupling λ = g²Nc/4π) to analyze the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and baryon density. The suppression of fermion loops by a factor λ μ_Q²/Nc is computed to show that Debye screening of the confining potential does not occur until μ_Q ∼ √(Nc) Λ_QCD, implying deconfinement is independent of baryon density. Model computations using the PNJL model are cited to argue that the quarkyonic transition may be associated with chiral symmetry restoration for Nc = 3.

## Key numbers
- The number of degrees of freedom in the hadron phase is of order one in Nc, while in the Quark Gluon Plasma it is 2(Nc² − 1) + 4NcNf, requiring a jump in energy density at deconfinement.
- The threshold for baryon number generation occurs at μ_B = M_N (the nucleon mass) for T = 0.
- The deconfinement transition is independent of baryon density until μ_Q ∼ √(Nc) Λ_QCD, where μ_Q = μ_B/Nc.
- The width of the quarkyonic transition is estimated from the baryon chemical potential: for Fermi momenta k_F << M_N, μ_B = M_N + k_F²/2M_N = M_N(1 + O(1/Nc²)), with the transition completed when k_F ∼ Λ_QCD.
- For Nc = 3, the binding energy of nuclear matter is about 15 MeV, not of order the nucleon mass as predicted by large Nc.

## Context
This work extends the earlier proposal of Quarkyonic matter by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) and subsequent generalizations by Hidaka (2008). It contrasts with prior work on color superconductivity by Alford (1997) and Rapp (1997) at high baryon density. The paper enables future experimental studies at RHIC, FAIR, and NICA, and seeks to clarify the relationship between confinement and high energy density matter, which was first studied in QCD by Collins and Perry (1974).
