# Prediction of possible exotic states in the $η\bar{K}K^*$ system

> We investigate the $η\bar{K}K^*$ three body system in order to look for possible $I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(1^{-+})$ exotic states in the framework of the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equation. We assume the scattering of $η$ on a clusterized system $\bar{K}K^*$, which is known to generate the $f_1(1285)$, or a $\bar{K}$ on a clusterized system $ηK^*$, which is shown to generate the $K_1(1270)$. In the case of the $η$-$(\bar{K}K^*)_{f_1(1285)}$ scattering, we find evidence of a bound state $I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(1^{-+})$ below the $η{f_1(1285)}$ threshold with mass around 1700 MeV and width about 180 MeV. Considering the $\bar{K}$-$(ηK^*)_{K_1(1270)}$ scattering, we obtain a bound state $I(J^{P})=0(1^{-})$ just below the $\bar{K}{K_1(1270)}$ threshold with a mass around 1680 MeV and a width about 160 MeV.

# Prediction of possible exotic states in the ηK̅K* system

## Key claim

The authors predict the existence of two isoscalar exotic bound states in the ηK̅K* three-body system using the fixed center approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equation, but note that the results depend on the choice of the cluster configuration and that full Faddeev calculations would be needed for confirmation.

## Method

The authors apply the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equation, treating either the K̅K* system (which generates the f₁(1285) resonance) or the ηK* system (which generates the K₁(1270) resonance) as a cluster, with the third particle (η or K̅) scattering off the cluster. The two-body scattering amplitudes for ηK̅, ηK*, and K̅K* are obtained from the chiral unitary approach with dimensional regularization, using subtraction constants from previous work by Roca (2005), Geng (2006), and Guo (2005). The form factor of the cluster is computed with a cutoff Λ tuned to reproduce the known resonance masses, and the total three-body scattering amplitude T is solved from the FCA equations.

## Key numbers

- For the η-(K̅K*)_{f₁(1285)} scattering: a bound state with I^G(J^PC)=0⁺(1⁻⁺) is found below the ηf₁(1285) threshold with mass around 1700 MeV and width about 180 MeV.
- For the K̅-(ηK*)_{K₁(1270)} scattering: a bound state with I(J^P)=0(1⁻) is found just below the K̅K₁(1270) threshold with mass around 1680 MeV and width about 160 MeV.
- The cutoff Λ used for the f₁(1285) cluster form factor is 990 MeV, with variations of 890 and 1090 MeV tested; for the K₁(1270) cluster, Λ = 1000 MeV is used, with variations of 900 and 1100 MeV tested.
- The cluster mass for f₁(1285) is taken as 1281.3 MeV and for K₁(1270) as 1284 MeV, as obtained by Geng (2006).

## Context

This work extends the study of three-body systems with exotic quantum numbers, following the approach of Zhang (2016) who interpreted the π₁(1600) as a dynamically generated state from the π-(K̅K*)_{f₁(1285)} system. The predictions complement earlier theoretical studies of isoscalar 1⁻⁺ exotic states using the flux tube model by Page (1998), lattice QCD by Bernard (2003), and QCD sum rules by Chen (2008), which gave masses around 1.8–2.1 GeV. The results provide a specific prediction for experimental searches, with the dominant decay mode expected to be K̅Kππ from the K₁(1270) component.
