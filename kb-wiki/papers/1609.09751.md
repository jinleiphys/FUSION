# Reactions with a 10Be beam to study the one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be

> Halo nuclei are excellent examples of few-body systems consisting of a core and weakly-bound halo nucleons. Where there is only one nucleon in the halo, as in 11Be, the many-body problem can be reduced to a two-body problem. The contribution of the 1s1/2 orbital to the ground state configuration in 11Be, characterized by the spectroscopic factor, S, has been extracted from direct reaction data by many groups over the past five decades with discrepant results. An experiment was performed at the Holifield Radioactive Ion Beam Facility using a 10Be primary beam at four different energies with the goal of resolving the discrepancy through a consistent analysis of elastic, inelastic, and transfer channels. Faddeev-type calculations, released after the publication of the experimental results, show that dynamic core excitation in the transfer process can lead to reduced differential cross sections at higher beam energies. This reduction would lead to the extraction of decreasing values of S with increasing beam energy. A 10Be(d,p) measurement at Ed greater than 25 MeV is necessary to investigate the effects of core excitation in the reaction.

## Key claim

The spectroscopic factor S for the ground state of 11Be extracted from 10Be(d,p) data at four beam energies is consistent with a constant value of 0.69±0.06 within uncertainties, but the data are also compatible with a decreasing trend with increasing beam energy that could be explained by dynamic core excitation effects not included in standard DWBA or ADWA analyses.

## Method

A 10Be primary beam was used at the Holifield Radioactive Ion Beam Facility to measure elastic, inelastic, and transfer channels for the 10Be(d,p)11Be reaction in inverse kinematics at four beam energies (60, 75, 90, and 107 MeV, corresponding to equivalent deuteron energies of 12, 15, 18, and 21.4 MeV). The data were analyzed consistently using the finite-range adiabatic wave approximation (ADWA) with the Koning and Delaroche optical potential and fixed bound state parameters (radius 1.25 fm, diffuseness 0.65 fm). Faddeev-type calculations by Deltuva (2013) that included dynamic core excitation were subsequently compared to the experimental results.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of 11Be: Sn = 0.502 MeV, compared to a typical value of 7-8 MeV for stable isotopes
- Spectroscopic factor for the ground state of 11Be extracted from the consistent ADWA analysis: S = 0.69 ± 0.06
- Beam energies used: 60, 75, 90, and 107 MeV (equivalent deuteron energies Ed = 12, 15, 18, and 21.4 MeV)
- Previously reported S values for the ground state range from 0.73 ± 0.13 (Aumann, 2000) to 0.46 ± 0.15 (Lim, 2007)
- A 10Be(d,p) measurement at Ed greater than 25 MeV is proposed to investigate core excitation effects

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to determine the spectroscopic factor for the one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be, where results from direct reaction experiments over five decades have shown discrepancies, as summarized by Fortune and Sherr (2012). The experiment provides consistent data across four energies analyzed with the same optical potentials and reaction formalisms, enabling a test of whether S is energy-independent. The subsequent Faddeev-type calculations by Deltuva (2013) show that dynamic core excitation can reduce differential cross sections at higher beam energies, which would cause extracted S values to decrease with energy; this prediction motivates future measurements above Ed = 25 MeV to distinguish between constant S and core excitation effects.
