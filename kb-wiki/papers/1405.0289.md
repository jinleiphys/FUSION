# Parton distribution amplitudes of light vector mesons

> A rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations is used to calculate rho- and phi-meson valence-quark (twist-two parton) distribution amplitudes (PDAs) via a light-front projection of their Bethe-Salpeter wave functions, which possess S- and D-wave components of comparable size in the meson rest frame. All computed PDAs are broad concave functions, whose dilation with respect to the asymptotic distribution is an expression of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The PDAs can be used to define an ordering of valence-quark light-front spatial-extent within mesons: this size is smallest within the pion and increases through the perp-polarisation to the parallel-polarisation of the vector mesons; effects associated with the breaking of SU(3)-flavour symmetry are significantly smaller than those associated with altering the polarisation of vector mesons. Notably, the predicted pointwise behaviour of the rho-meson PDAs is in quantitative agreement with that inferred recently via an analysis of diffractive vector-meson photoproduction experiments.

## Key claim

The paper uses a rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations to compute rho- and phi-meson valence-quark distribution amplitudes, finding that the predicted pointwise behavior of the rho-meson PDAs is in quantitative agreement with that inferred from diffractive vector-meson photoproduction experiments, but only when the PDAs are reconstructed using a method that does not assume the conformal limit approximation is valid at accessible energy scales.

## Method

The authors compute the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for rho and phi mesons using rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations with the interaction from Qin (2011), then project these wave functions onto the light front to obtain twist-two parton distribution amplitudes. Rather than assuming the conformal expansion with fixed Gegenbauer order, they compute arbitrarily many moments of the PDAs from the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions and reconstruct the full PDAs by minimizing the difference between computed and reconstructed moments using Gegenbauer polynomials of order alpha, where alpha is determined by the moments themselves.

## Key numbers

- f_rho = 0.15 GeV, f_rho^perp = 0.11 GeV at zeta = 2 GeV, giving f_rho^perp/f_rho = 0.73
- f_phi = 0.19 GeV, f_phi^perp = 0.15 GeV at zeta = 2 GeV, giving f_phi^perp/f_phi = 0.79
- m_rho = 0.74 GeV, m_phi = 1.08 GeV (computed values)
- Canonical normalization ratio for rho: N^2_{S+0}/N^2_{S+D} = 0.59, corresponding to S-wave amplitude 0.77 and D-wave amplitude -0.64
- Canonical normalization ratio for phi: N^2_{S+0}/N^2_{S+D} = 0.71, corresponding to S-wave amplitude 0.85 and D-wave amplitude -0.53
- Current-quark masses used: hat_m_{u/d} = 6.4 MeV, hat_m_s = 143 MeV (renormalization point invariant)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation framework previously applied to the pion's electromagnetic form factor by Chang (2013) and the pion's PDA by Chang (2013), now computing vector meson PDAs for the first time within the same approach. The results contradict the interpretation of vector meson decay constant ratios as indicating nearly pure S-wave states at zeta = 2 GeV, as argued by Glozman (2011), showing instead that D-wave contributions are comparable to S-wave contributions when properly evaluated from the Poincare-covariant Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The computed PDAs enable improved predictions for hard exclusive processes involving vector mesons and provide a benchmark for lattice QCD calculations such as those by Braun (2003) and Jansen (2009).
