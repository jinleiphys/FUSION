# Properties of dense, asymmetric nuclear matter in Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach

> Within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, using the Bonn potentials, we investigate the properties of dense, asymmetric nuclear matter and apply it to neutron stars. In the actual calculations of the nucleon self-energies and the energy density of matter, we study in detail the validity of an angle-averaged approximation and an averaging of the total momentum squared of interacting two-nucleons in nuclear matter. For practical use, we provide convenient parametrizations for the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. We also parametrize the nucleon self-energies in terms of polynomials of nucleon momenta. Those parametrizations can accurately reproduce the numerical results up to high densities.

# Properties of dense, asymmetric nuclear matter in Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach

## Key claim
The Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach with Bonn potentials can accurately describe dense asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron stars, but only if the angle-averaged approximation is used for self-energies while the full three-dimensional integral (or at least a two-dimensional reduction) is retained for the energy density calculation; the one-dimensional approximation for energy density fails at high densities above approximately 0.5 fm^{-3}.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled Bethe-Salpeter equations in the ladder approximation using the Bonn A, B, C, and an averaged AB potential, employing the subtracted T-matrix representation and the Thompson propagator. They calculated nucleon self-energies (scalar, vector time-like, and vector space-like components) and the energy density of asymmetric nuclear matter, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to obtain neutron-star mass-radius relations. The validity of dimension-reduction approximations was tested by comparing one-dimensional, two-dimensional, and full three-dimensional calculations of the self-energies and potential energy.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density for Bonn A: Fermi momentum k_F^0 = 1.39 fm^{-1}, binding energy per particle = -16.62 MeV, incompressibility K = 233 MeV, symmetry energy S = 34.8 MeV, slope parameter L = 71.2 MeV, effective mass M_N* = 636 MeV
- Saturation density for Bonn B: k_F^0 = 1.34 fm^{-1}, binding energy = -15.04 MeV, K = 190 MeV, S = 31.2 MeV, L = 55.9 MeV, M_N* = 666 MeV
- Saturation density for Bonn C: k_F^0 = 1.30 fm^{-1}, binding energy = -14.14 MeV, K = 170 MeV, S = 28.9 MeV, L = 46.7 MeV, M_N* = 687 MeV
- Saturation density for Bonn AB: k_F^0 = 1.36 fm^{-1}, binding energy = -15.52 MeV, K = 204 MeV, S = 32.3 MeV, L = 61.1 MeV, M_N* = 653 MeV
- Maximum neutron-star mass with full calculation (Sigma1d-E3d): 2.33 solar masses, radius 11.5 km, central density 0.91 fm^{-3}
- Maximum neutron-star mass with two-dimensional energy density (Sigma1d-E2d): 2.30 solar masses, radius 11.5 km, central density 0.92 fm^{-3}
- Maximum neutron-star mass with one-dimensional energy density (Sigma1d-E1d): 2.22 solar masses, radius 11.4 km, central density 0.95 fm^{-3}
- Parametrizations valid for density range 0.03 fm^{-3} to 1.2 fm^{-3}, proton fraction 0 to 0.5, and nucleon momentum up to the Fermi momentum

## Context
This work extends the DBHF calculations of Horowitz and Serot (1987) and Gross-Boelting et al. (1999) to dense asymmetric matter and neutron stars, providing the first systematic test of dimension-reduction approximations at high densities. The results enable practical parametrizations for use in neutron-star calculations and establish that the angle-averaged approximation for self-energies is accurate up to 1.2 fm^{-3}, while the averaging of total momentum squared for the energy density fails above 0.5 fm^{-3}. The authors note that future work should include hyperons, as previous relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations by Katayama and Saito suggested hyperon creation may be suppressed.
