# The influence of strength of hyperon-hyperon interactions on neutron star properties

> An equation of state of neutron star matter with strange baryons has been obtained. The effects of the strength of hyperon-hyperon interactions on the equations of state constructed for the chosen parameter sets have been analyzed. Numerous neutron star models show that the appearance of hyperons is connected with the increasing density in neutron star interiors. The performed calculations have indicated that the change of the hyperon-hyperon coupling constants affects the chemical composition of a neutron star. The obtained numerical hyperon star models exclude large population of strange baryons in the star interior.

## Key claim

The strength of hyperon-hyperon (Y-Y) interactions significantly alters the chemical composition and equation of state of neutron star matter, but the resulting stable hyperon star models exclude large populations of strange baryons; specifically, for both weak and strong Y-Y interaction models, \(\Xi^{-}\) and \(\Xi^{0}\) hyperons do not appear in stable configurations, leaving only \(\Lambda\) hyperons as the strange baryons present.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model based on quantum hadrodynamics, including the full baryon octet (n, p, \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), \(\Xi\)) interacting via \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\rho\), \(\sigma^{\ast}\), and \(\phi\) mesons. They used two parameter sets (TM1 and TMA) and considered two cases for the hyperon-hyperon interaction strength: a "strong" case with a \(\Lambda\) well depth in \(\Lambda\) matter of \(U_{\Lambda}^{(\Lambda)} \simeq 20\) MeV and a "weak" case with \(U_{\Lambda}^{(\Lambda)} \simeq 5\) MeV, the latter motivated by recent data on the double hypernucleus \({}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{6}\)He. The equations of state were computed for \(\beta\)-equilibrated, charge-neutral neutron star matter at zero temperature, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- \(\Lambda\) well depth in nuclear matter: \(U_{\Lambda}^{(N)} \simeq 27-30\) MeV (from single hypernuclei data).
- \(\Xi\) well depth in nuclear matter: \(U_{\Xi}^{(N)} \simeq 20-25\) MeV.
- \(\Lambda-\Lambda\) interaction energy from recent data on \({}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{6}\)He: \(\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 1.01 \pm 0.20^{+0.18}_{-0.11}\) MeV, leading to \(U_{\Lambda}^{(\Lambda)} \simeq 5\) MeV (weak case).
- For the strong Y-Y interaction, \(U_{\Lambda}^{(\Lambda)} \simeq 20\) MeV and \(U_{\Xi}^{(\Xi)} \simeq 40\) MeV; for the weak case, \(U_{\Xi}^{(\Xi)} \simeq 10\) MeV.
- Maximum mass configurations for TM1 (weak): central density \(\rho_c = 6.16 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\), radius \(R = 12.88\) km, maximum mass \(M_{\text{max}} = 1.82 M_{\odot}\).
- Maximum mass configurations for TM1 (strong): \(\rho_c = 6.60 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\), \(R = 12.65\) km, \(M_{\text{max}} = 1.80 M_{\odot}\).
- Maximum mass configurations for TMA (weak): \(\rho_c = 6.04 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\), \(R = 12.64\) km, \(M_{\text{max}} = 1.64 M_{\odot}\).
- Maximum mass configurations for TMA (strong): \(\rho_c = 6.28 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\), \(R = 12.53\) km, \(M_{\text{max}} = 1.66 M_{\odot}\).
- Density for appearance of \(\Xi\) hyperons in TM1 (weak): \(\rho_x = 7.62 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\); in TM1 (strong): \(\rho_x = 9.40 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\).
- Density for appearance of \(\Xi\) hyperons in TMA (weak): \(\rho_x = 8.08 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\); in TMA (strong): \(\rho_x = 9.79 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hyperon star matter within the relativistic mean-field framework, particularly those using the TM1 and TMA parameter sets and the SU(6) symmetry for vector coupling constants. It directly addresses the tension between older estimates of the \(\Lambda-\Lambda\) interaction (Schaffner et al., 1994) giving \(U_{\Lambda}^{(\Lambda)} \simeq 20\) MeV and newer data from Takahashi et al. (2001) on \({}_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{6}\)He indicating a much weaker interaction (\(U_{\Lambda}^{(\Lambda)} \simeq 5\) MeV). The results contradict the possibility of a third family of stable compact stars with highly attractive hyperon-hyperon interactions proposed by Schaffner et al., as the weak interaction strength derived from recent data does not support such configurations. The findings enable future investigations of protoneutron star models with very high central densities that might allow \(\Xi\) hyperons to appear.
