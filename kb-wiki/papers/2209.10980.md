# Chiral restoration of strange baryons

> We review the results of a phenomenological model for cold and dense nuclear matter exhibiting a chiral phase transition. The idea is to model the quark-hadron phase transition under neutron star conditions within a single model, but without adding quark degrees of freedom by hand. To this end, strangeness is included in the form of hyperonic degrees of freedom, whose light counterparts provide the strangeness in the chirally restored phase. In the future, the model can be used for instance to compute the surface tension at the (first-order) chiral phase transition and to study the possible existence of inhomogeneous phases.

# Chiral restoration of strange baryons

## Key claim
The authors present a unified phenomenological model for cold, dense nuclear matter that describes both hadronic and chirally restored phases within a single Lagrangian, without introducing explicit quark degrees of freedom. The model can reproduce a first-order chiral phase transition and produce neutron stars compatible with the 2.1 solar mass limit, but only if hyperons appear exclusively in the chirally restored phase and the slope of the symmetry energy is constrained to a narrow range of approximately 88–92 MeV.

## Method
The authors construct a mean-field model including the full baryon octet coupled to scalar (sigma), vector (omega, rho, phi), and quartic vector meson interactions, with baryon masses generated entirely by the chiral condensate. They solve the stationarity equations for meson condensates under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality at zero temperature, compute the free energy density, and derive the equation of state to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star mass-radius relations. The parameter space is systematically explored by varying the effective nucleon mass at saturation, the quartic vector coupling, and hyperon potential depths, subject to constraints from nuclear saturation properties, astrophysical observations, and asymptotic QCD expectations.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: n0 = 0.153 fm^-3 with binding energy EB = -16.3 MeV for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter
- Symmetry energy fixed at S = 32 MeV and incompressibility at K = 250 MeV at saturation
- Hyperon potential depths: U_Lambda^(N) = -30 MeV, U_Sigma,Xi^(N) = -70 to +30 MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass constraint: M_max = 2.1 solar masses (from Fonseca 2021)
- Predicted slope of symmetry energy: L = 88–92 MeV from the allowed parameter region
- Asymptotic speed of sound squared: c_s^2 approaches 1/3 from above in the model
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation explored in range M0 = (0.7–0.92) m_N

## Context
This work extends previous unified chiral models by Drews (2013, 2014), Fraga (2018), and Schmitt (2020) that included only neutrons and protons, by adding the full baryon octet with hyperonic degrees of freedom to account for strangeness in the chirally restored phase. The approach contrasts with two-model constructions (Alford 2013, Pereira 2020, Blaschke 2020, Jokela 2020, Ferreira 2021, Lopes 2021) that stitch separate hadronic and quark models together. The model dynamically illustrates the hyperon puzzle (Tolos 2020) and its potential resolution: hyperons appear but only after chiral restoration, allowing the quark matter core to support massive neutron stars. Future applications include computing surface tension at the first-order chiral phase transition and studying inhomogeneous phases.
