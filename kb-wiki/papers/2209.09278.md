# $D^*$ and $D^*_s$ distribution amplitudes from Bethe-Salpeter wave functions

> We report on the first calculation of the longitudinal and transverse light front distribution amplitudes of the $D^*$ and $D^*_s$ mesons and their first four moments. As a byproduct, we also obtain these distribution amplitudes for the $ρ$, $ϕ$, $K^*$ and $J/Ψ$ mesons and confirm a prediction of lattice QCD for the vector kaon: while the longitudinal distribution amplitude is almost symmetric, the transverse one is oblique implying that the strange quark carries more momentum.

# D* and D*s Distribution Amplitudes from Bethe-Salpeter Wave Functions

## Key claim

The authors report the first calculation of the longitudinal and transverse light-front distribution amplitudes (LFDAs) for the D* and D*s mesons, but note that these distributions are reconstructed from Mellin moments up to order m=4 due to numerical integration limitations, and the heavy-meson parametrization uses a functional form different from the Gegenbauer expansion employed for lighter mesons.

## Method

The authors compute Mellin moments of the twist-2 LFDAs by projecting the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions of vector mesons onto the light front, using numerical solutions of the Dyson-Schwinger equation for quark propagators and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the vector meson amplitudes. For light vector mesons (rho, phi, K*), they reconstruct the LFDAs using a Gegenbauer polynomial expansion where the power alpha is treated as a free parameter, while for heavy mesons (J/Psi, D*, D*s) they employ an exponential parametrization more suitable for convex-concave-convex functional behavior. The moments are computed via direct numerical integration over complex momentum parabolas, avoiding the use of complex-conjugate pole parametrizations or Nakanishi representations.

## Key numbers

- For the D* meson: longitudinal first moment <x>_parallel = 0.694, transverse first moment <x>_perp = 0.742; longitudinal second moment <x^2>_parallel = 0.511, transverse second moment <x^2>_perp = 0.589 (at scale mu = 2 GeV)
- For the D*s meson: longitudinal first moment <x>_parallel = 0.627, transverse first moment <x>_perp = 0.655; longitudinal second moment <x^2>_parallel = 0.418, transverse second moment <x^2>_perp = 0.465 (at scale mu = 2 GeV)
- For the K* meson: longitudinal first moment <x>_parallel = 0.509, transverse first moment <x>_perp = 0.528; the transverse LFDA maximum is located at x = 0.78, indicating the strange quark carries more momentum
- For the rho meson: longitudinal <xi^2>_parallel = 0.263, transverse <xi^2>_perp = 0.250; the longitudinal moments are about 11% larger than those in Gao (2014)
- For the phi meson: longitudinal <xi^2>_parallel = 0.186, transverse <xi^2>_perp = 0.182; the authors use a strange quark mass m_s = 166 MeV at mu = 2 GeV
- For the J/Psi meson: longitudinal <x>_parallel = 0.500, transverse <x>_perp = 0.500; longitudinal alpha parameter = 4.549, transverse alpha = 12.703

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier DSE-BSE study of pseudoscalar D and Ds meson LFDAs (Serna, 2020) to their vector partners, and also provides LFDAs for rho, phi, K*, and J/Psi mesons that can be compared with prior DSE calculations (Gao, 2014), QCD sum rules (Ball, 2007), lattice QCD (Boyle, 2008; Hua, 2020), and HERA fits (Forshaw, 2010, 2012). The results confirm a lattice QCD prediction for the K*: the longitudinal distribution is nearly symmetric while the transverse distribution is oblique, implying the strange quark carries more momentum. The parametrized LFDAs for J/Psi and D*(s) mesons can be used in diffractive vector-meson production calculations relevant to the Electron-Ion Collider experimental program.
