# Evidence of the four-quark nature of $f_0(980)$ and $f_0(500)$

> There exists a great deal of concrete evidence in favor of the exotic four-quark nature of light scalars. At the same time, the further expansion of the area of the $q^2\bar q^2$ model validity for light scalars on ever new processes seems extremely interesting and important. We analyze the BESIII data on the decay $J/ψ\to γπ^0π^0$ and show that the results of this high-statistics experiment can be interpreted in favor of the four-quark nature of light scalar mesons $f_0(980)$ and $f_0(500)$.

## Key claim

The BESIII data on the decay \(J/\psi\to\gamma\pi^0\pi^0\) can be interpreted as evidence for the four-quark (\(q^2\bar q^2\)) nature of the light scalar mesons \(f_0(500)\) and \(f_0(980)\), provided that the production mechanism proceeds via seed four-quark fluctuations rather than through direct \(q\bar q\) transitions.

## Method

The authors performed a fit to the BESIII \(S\)-wave \(\pi^0\pi^0\) mass spectrum below 1 GeV using an amplitude that incorporates \(\pi\pi\) and \(K\bar K\) loop diagrams dressed by strong final-state interactions. The amplitude was constructed from known \(\pi\pi\to\pi\pi\) and \(\pi\pi\to K\bar K\) scattering amplitudes from Achasov and Kiselev (2006), with only two free parameters (subtraction constants in the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) and \(K^+K^-\) loops) plus an overall normalization. The fit assumed \(SU(3)\) symmetry for the seed coupling constants \(\lambda_{\pi\pi}=\lambda_{K\bar K}\).

## Key numbers

- Branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to\gamma f_0(500)\to\gamma\pi^0\pi^0) = 0.324\times10^{-4}\) (from \(S\)-wave events with \(\sqrt{s}\) between \(2m_\pi\) and 0.9 GeV).
- Branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to\gamma f_0(980)\to\gamma\pi^0\pi^0) = 0.425\times10^{-5}\) (from \(S\)-wave events with \(\sqrt{s}\) between 0.9 and 1 GeV).
- Branching fraction \(\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to\gamma f_2(1270)\to\gamma\pi^0\pi^0) = 0.482\times10^{-3}\) (from \(D\)-wave events with \(\sqrt{s}\) between \(2m_\pi\) and 1.5 GeV).
- Ratio \(\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to\gamma f_0(500))/\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to\gamma f_2(1270)) \approx 0.067\).
- Ratio \(\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to\gamma f_0(980))/\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to\gamma f_2(1270)) \approx 0.0088\).
- Fit quality: \(\chi^2/(n.d.f) \approx 57.4/(49-3) \approx 1.25\).
- Relative errors of fit parameters: \(\lambda\) \(\pm1.9\%\), \(\tilde{C}_{\pi^+\pi^-}\) \(\pm2.4\%\), \(\tilde{C}_{K^+K^-}\) \(\pm9.3\%\).
- Total \(J/\psi\to\gamma\pi^0\pi^0\) branching fraction from BESIII: \((1.15\pm0.05)\times10^{-3}\) (systematic uncertainty only).

## Context

This work extends the four-quark interpretation of light scalars previously applied to semileptonic \(D\) and \(B\) meson decays by Achasov, Kiselev, and Shestakov (2020) to the radiative decay \(J/\psi\to\gamma\pi^0\pi^0\). It contrasts with the \(q\bar q\) model predictions, which would expect comparable production rates for light scalars and tensor mesons like \(f_2(1270)\), and with chiral-symmetric Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model expectations that would relate \(f_0(500)\) and \(f_0(980)\) production to \(\eta\) and \(\eta'(958)\) production. The strong suppression of \(f_0(500)\) and \(f_0(980)\) relative to \(f_2(1270)\) and to \(\eta\) and \(\eta'(958)\) is presented as evidence for their four-quark structure. The results enable further tests of the four-quark model in other \(J/\psi\) decay channels and in future high-statistics experiments.
