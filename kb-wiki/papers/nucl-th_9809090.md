# Channel Coupling in $A(\vec{e},e' \vec{N})B$ Reactions

> The sensitivity of momentum distributions, recoil polarization observables, and response functions for nucleon knockout by polarized electrons to channel coupling in final-state interactions is investigated using a model in which both the distorting and the coupling potentials are constructed by folding density-dependent effective interactions with nuclear transition densities. Calculations for $^{16}$O are presented for 200 and 433 MeV ejectile energies, corresponding to proposed experiments at MAMI and TJNAF, and for $^{12}$C at 70 and 270 MeV, corresponding to experiments at NIKHEF and MIT-Bates. The relative importance of charge exchange decreases as the ejectile energy increases, but remains significant for 200 MeV. Both proton and neutron knockout cross sections for large recoil momenta, $p_m > 300$ MeV/c, are substantially affected by inelastic couplings even at 433 MeV. Significant effects on the cross section for neutron knockout are also predicted at smaller recoil momenta, especially for low energies. Polarization transfer for proton knockout is insensitive to channel coupling, even for fairly low ejectile energies, but polarization transfer for neutron knockout retains nonnegligible sensitivity to channel coupling for energies up to about 200 MeV. The present results suggest that possible medium modifications of neutron and proton electromagnetic form factors for $Q^2 \gtrsim 0.5 (GeV/c)^2$ can be studied using recoil polarization with relatively little sensitivity due to final state interactions.

## Key claim

Channel coupling in final-state interactions (FSI), including inelastic scattering and charge exchange, significantly affects momentum distributions for both proton and neutron knockout at large recoil momenta (p_m > 300 MeV/c) even at 433 MeV ejectile energy, but polarization transfer for proton knockout is insensitive to channel coupling, suggesting that medium modifications of nucleon electromagnetic form factors for Q^2 ≥ 0.5 (GeV/c)^2 can be studied using recoil polarization with relatively little uncertainty from FSI.

## Method

The author developed a coupled-channels model for A(e,e'N)B reactions that includes elastic reorientation, inelastic scattering, and charge exchange in final-state interactions, using density-dependent empirical effective interactions folded with nuclear transition densities within the local density approximation. The electron distortion was treated using the effective momentum approximation, and the relativized Schrodinger equation was employed for the nucleon-nucleus scattering with coupling potentials constructed from the same effective interaction. Calculations were performed for 16O at 200 and 433 MeV ejectile energies and for 12C at 70 and 270 MeV ejectile energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channels analyses of the 10B(e,e'p)9Be and 10B(gamma,p)9Be reactions by de Bever (1994), which used a simpler version of the model. The paper addresses the need to go beyond standard optical-model treatments of FSI, which previous work by Ryckebusch (1989) and van der Sluys (1996) had attempted using HF-RPA models limited to low ejectile energies, and by Jeschonnek (1994) using continuum RPA with less realistic effective interactions. The present model enables predictions for upcoming experiments at MAMI (experiment A1/2-93) and TJNAF (experiments 89-033 and 89-003) that will measure recoil polarization and large missing momenta in 16O(e,e'p) reactions at 200 and 433 MeV.
