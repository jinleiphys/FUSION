# Model for compound nucleus formation in various heavy-ion systems

> The statistical model for the calculation of the compound nucleus formation cross section and the probability of compound nucleus formation in heavy-ion collisions is discussed in detail. The light, heavy, and super-heavy nucleus-nucleus systems are considered in this model in the framework of one approach. It is shown that the compound nucleus is formed in competition between passing through the compound-nucleus formation barrier and the quasi-elastic barrier. The compound-nucleus formation barrier is the barrier separating the system of contacting incident nuclei and the spherical or near-spherical ground state of the compound nucleus. The quasi-elastic barrier is the barrier between the contacting and well-separated deformed ions. It is shown that the compound nucleus formation cross-section is suppressed when the quasi-elastic barrier is lower than the compound nucleus formation barrier. The critical value of angular momentum, which limits the compound nucleus formation cross-section values for light and medium ion-ion systems at over-barrier collision energies, is discussed in the model. The suppression of the compound nucleus formation cross-section even at small partial waves for very heavy ion-ion systems is obtained in the model. The values of the capture and compound nucleus formation cross-sections calculated for various light, heavy, and super-heavy nucleus-nucleus systems as well as the probability of the compound nucleus formation for super-heavy nuclei are well agreed with the available experimental data.

# Model for compound nucleus formation in various heavy-ion systems

## Key claim

The compound nucleus formation cross-section is suppressed when the quasi-elastic barrier is lower than the compound nucleus formation barrier, and this suppression can occur even at small partial waves for very heavy ion-ion systems. The model treats light, heavy, and super-heavy nucleus-nucleus systems within a single statistical framework, with the compound nucleus formation probability determined by the competition between passing through the compound-nucleus formation barrier and the quasi-elastic barrier.

## Method

The model describes compound nucleus formation in two consecutive steps: first, penetration through the capture barrier between two spherical incident nuclei to form a system of stuck-together nuclei in thermodynamic equilibrium; second, statistical competition between passing the one-body compound nucleus formation barrier (leading to a spherical or near-spherical compound nucleus) and passing the two-body quasi-elastic barrier (leading to separated deformed nuclei). The compound nucleus formation width is calculated using a modified Bohr-Wheeler approach with an energy-dependent barrier height that includes liquid-drop, shell-correction, and rotational contributions, where the shell-correction contribution damps exponentially with excitation energy. The quasi-elastic decay width is computed using the Bohr-Wheeler transition state approximation with the barrier obtained from the total potential energy of two deformed nuclei including Coulomb, nuclear, centrifugal, and deformation energy contributions.

## Key numbers

- The distance between the closest points of surfaces of colliding nuclei belongs to the range 1–2.5 fm at the capture barrier point
- The asymptotic level density parameter is given by $a^0_A = 0.0722396 A + 0.195267 A^{2/3}$ MeV$^{-1}$
- The damping parameter for the level density parameter is $\gamma = 0.410289 / A^{1/3}$ MeV$^{-1}$
- The back-shift energy is described by $\Delta = 12n / A^{1/2} + 0.173015$ MeV, where $n = -1, 0, 1$ for odd-odd, odd-A, and even-even nuclei respectively
- The damping coefficient for the shell-correction contribution to the barrier is approximately $\gamma_D \approx 1.15 / (0.0722396 A + 0.195267 A^{2/3})$ MeV$^{-1}$, derived from the condition that shell-correction energy disappears at compound-nucleus temperature $T_D \approx 2$ MeV
- The pairing force contribution to the compound nucleus formation barrier disappears at the critical temperature $T \approx 0.5$ MeV
- The temperature dependence of liquid-drop model constants is negligible at $T \lesssim 2$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the statistical treatment of compound nucleus formation by unifying the description of light, heavy, and super-heavy systems, building on earlier approaches such as the critical radius or critical angular momentum approximation (Galster and others, 1970s), classical friction models (Birkelund and others, 1979; Frobrich, 1984), and barrier-penetration models (Bjornholm and Swiatecki, 1982; Blocki and others, 1986). The model incorporates the energy-dependent fission barrier concept developed by Strutinsky (1966) and later refined by Ignatyuk and others (1975), and uses the Bohr-Wheeler transition state formalism (1939) adapted for excitation-energy-dependent barriers as derived in Denisov (2018). The approach enables systematic calculation of capture and compound nucleus formation cross-sections for various systems, including super-heavy nuclei production, where the suppression of compound nucleus formation even at small partial waves is a key challenge.
