# Tensor-optimized few-body model for s-shell nuclei

> As a new scheme of treating the tensor interaction of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, there is a proposal of a tensor-optimized shell-model (TOSM) for the study of medium and heavy nuclei. The TOSM includes the deuteron-like tensor correlation and provides quite a good reproduction of the binding energy and the size of $^4$He as compared with rigorous few body calculations. We propose a tensor-optimized few-body model (TOFM) using the spirit of the TOSM approximation in the few body framework with bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. We find that the TOFM can account for the strength of the tensor interaction very well and almost reproduces the full energy and various energy components as compared with rigorous few body calculations.

# Tensor-optimized few-body model for s-shell nuclei

## Key claim
The tensor-optimized few-body model (TOFM), which includes only a single D-wave (Y₂) component in the relative coordinate, almost reproduces the full energy and energy components of rigorous few-body calculations for A=2, 3, and 4 systems, though for ⁴He the tensor interaction energy is about 8 MeV smaller and the kinetic energy about 7 MeV smaller than the rigorous stochastic variational method (SVM) result.

## Method
The authors construct a variational wave function as a linear combination of S-wave and D-wave components, where the D-wave contains only one Y₂ spherical harmonic in the Jacobi coordinate x₁ = r₁ − r₂, with all necessary permutations handled by the antisymmetrization operator. Gaussian basis functions with variable range parameters B_S and B_D are used, and the amplitudes and ranges are optimized variationally by diagonalization, employing the stochastic variational method (SVM) code of Varga and Suzuki (2008). Calculations are performed with the AV8' and G3RS bare nucleon-nucleon interactions for the deuteron, ³H, and ⁴He.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron (AV8'): total energy −2.23 MeV, tensor interaction energy −16.64 MeV, kinetic energy 19.95 MeV.
- ³H (TOFM with AV8'): total energy −7.54 MeV, compared to SVM result −7.76 MeV; tensor interaction −30.47 MeV vs. SVM −30.84 MeV.
- ⁴He (TOFM with AV8'): total energy −24.08 MeV, compared to SVM result −25.92 MeV; tensor interaction −60.95 MeV vs. SVM −68.32 MeV; kinetic energy 95.53 MeV vs. SVM 102.35 MeV.
- ⁴He (TOFM with AV8'): total energy −24.08 MeV, compared to TOSM result −22.30 MeV (Myo et al., 2009).
- ⁴He (TOFM with G3RS): total energy −25.22 MeV, compared to SVM −26.05 MeV; tensor interaction −43.66 MeV vs. SVM −46.62 MeV.
- Second 0⁺ state in ⁴He: excitation energy 18.5 MeV (experimental 20.21 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM) approach of Myo et al. (2009), which treated tensor correlations in a shell-model basis with 2p-2h excitations, into a few-body framework using relative coordinates. The TOFM is compared against rigorous SVM calculations by Suzuki et al. (2008) and Kamada et al. (2001). For A=3, the single-Y₂ approximation is essentially exact; for A=4, the authors note that including a second Y₂ component brings the total energy very close to the rigorous result (Hiyama et al., 2003). The method enables extension to nuclei with A≥5 and provides guidance for improving the TOSM description of medium and heavy nuclei by better treating short-range correlations.
