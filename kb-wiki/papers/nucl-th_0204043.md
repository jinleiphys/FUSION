# Extension of the full-folding optical model for nucleon-nucleus scattering with applications up to 1.5 GeV

> The nonrelativistic full-folding optical model approach for nucleon-nucleus scattering is extended into the relativistic regime. In doing so, kinematical issues involving the off-shell Lorentz boost of the colliding particles between the two nucleons and the projectile-nucleus center-of-mass reference frames have been taken into account. The two-body effective interaction is obtained in the framework of the nuclear matter g-matrix using nucleon-nucleon optical model potentials that fully account for the inelasticities and isobar resonances in the continuum at nucleon energies up to 3 GeV. Diverse nucleon-nucleon potential models were constructed by supplementing the basic Paris, Nijmegen, Argonne or Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko inversion potentials with complex separable terms. In each case the additional separable terms ensured that the combination led to NN scattering phase shifts in excellent agreement with experimental values. With each phase shift fitting potential nuclear matter g-matrices have been formed and with each of those relativistic full-folding optical potentials for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering determined. Application to such scattering for projectile energies to 1.5 GeV have been made. Good and systematic agreement is obtained between the calculated and measured observables, both differential and integrated quantities, over the whole energy range of our study. A moderate sensitivity to off-shell effects in the differential scattering observables also is observed.

## Key claim

The authors extend the nonrelativistic full-folding optical model for nucleon-nucleus scattering into the relativistic regime by incorporating Lorentz boosts and Møller flux factors, achieving good agreement with experimental observables up to 1.5 GeV projectile energy, though they note only moderate sensitivity to off-shell effects in differential scattering observables.

## Method

The authors constructed nucleon-nucleon optical model potentials (NNOMP) by supplementing existing reference potentials (Paris, Nijmegen, Argonne, and Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko inversion) with complex separable terms to fit NN phase shifts up to 3 GeV. They then computed nuclear matter g-matrices using a Dirac-Brueckner approach with minimal relativity, and used these to generate relativistic full-folding optical potentials for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering via a momentum-space representation incorporating Lorentz transformations between the two-body and projectile-nucleus center-of-mass frames.

## Key numbers

- Projectile energies up to 1.5 GeV were considered for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering applications
- NN optical model potentials were constructed for the energy range 0.3 to 3 GeV (T_Lab)
- The Lorentz contraction becomes sizeable for projectile energies above 400 MeV, with relativistic kinematics considered necessary above 300 MeV
- The Δ(1232) resonance has a width of 120 MeV and is the only resonance clearly visible in the energy variation of elastic scattering phase shifts
- The approximation using E²_p/M ≈ 2(p²/2M + U(p)) + M yields a propagator equivalent to its nonrelativistic counterpart with accuracy better than 1% at normal densities where U/M ≲ 1/10

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic full-folding optical model approach of Arellano (1990) into the relativistic regime, addressing limitations of previous nonrelativistic applications that used inversion potentials up to 500 MeV (Arellano, 1996). It contrasts with relativistic Dirac approaches (Ray, 1992; Tjon and Wallace, 1991) which include relativistic kinematics but have used fitted NN interactions that do not describe phase shift data well. The approach enables reliable nucleon-nucleus scattering calculations up to 1.5 GeV, relevant for applications such as accelerator transmutation of waste and spallation source design.
