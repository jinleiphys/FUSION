# Isospin dependence of nucleon effective mass in Dirac Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach

> The isospin dependence of the nucleon effective mass is investigated in the framework of the Dirac Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach. The definition of nucleon scalar and vector effective masses in the relativistic approach is clarified. Only the vector effective mass is the quantity related to the empirical value extracted from the analysis in the nonrelatiistic shell and optical potentials. In the relativistic mean field theory, where the nucleon scalar and vector potentials are both energy independent, the neutron vector potential is stronger than that of proton in the neutron rich nuclear matter, which produces a smaller neutron vector effective mass than that of proton. It is pointed out that the energy dependence of nucleon potentials has to be considered in the analysis of the isospin dependence of the nucleon effective mass. In the DBHF the neutron vector effective mass is larger than that of proton once the energy dependence of nucleon potentials is considered. The results are consistent with the analysis of phenomenological isospin dependent optical potentials.

## Key claim
The paper clarifies that in the relativistic approach, only the vector effective mass (not the scalar effective mass) corresponds to the empirical nucleon effective mass extracted from nonrelativistic shell and optical model analyses. In the Dirac Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, once the energy dependence of nucleon potentials is considered, the neutron vector effective mass becomes larger than that of proton in neutron-rich nuclear matter, contrary to the result from relativistic mean field theory with energy-independent potentials.

## Method
The authors performed microscopic calculations of nucleon scalar and vector self-energies in asymmetric nuclear matter using the DBHF approach with the Bonn B bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. They applied a projection method to decompose the DBHF G-matrix into a bare interaction and a correlation term parameterized by pseudo-mesons, then computed the scalar and vector potentials for protons and neutrons as functions of energy and asymmetry parameter. The vector effective mass was derived from the Schroedinger equivalent central potential including the energy dependence of the self-energies.

## Key numbers
- In symmetric nuclear matter at Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.36\) fm\(^{-1}\), the scalar potential \(U_s\) is about -336 MeV and the vector potential \(U_0\) is 256 MeV at the Fermi surface.
- The scalar effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter at the Fermi surface is \(M^*_s/M = 0.64\).
- The vector effective mass in symmetric nuclear matter at the Fermi surface, neglecting energy dependence, is \(M^*_v/M = 0.73\).
- At nucleon energy \(\varepsilon = 50\) MeV in symmetric nuclear matter, the scalar effective mass is 0.66 and the vector effective mass is 0.68.
- The typical empirical nucleon effective mass from nonrelativistic analyses is \(M^*/M \approx 0.70 \pm 0.05\) at \(\varepsilon \approx 30\) MeV.
- The scalar potential in relativistic mean field theory is \(U_s = -375 \pm 40\) MeV, giving \(M_s^*/M = 0.60 \pm 0.04\).

## Context
This work extends the DBHF studies of asymmetric nuclear matter by Ma (2002) and Rong (2003) to the specific question of isospin-dependent effective masses. It clarifies a confusion noted by Jaminon and Mahaux (1989) between scalar and vector effective masses in relativistic approaches. The result that the neutron vector effective mass is larger than that of proton in neutron-rich matter is consistent with nonrelativistic microscopic calculations such as the Landau-Fermi liquid theory by Sjoberg (1976) and the BHF approach by Bombaci (1991), as well as with the analysis by Li (2004) of the Lane potential from nucleon-nucleus scattering data. This finding is important for understanding neutron star properties and nuclear collisions with radioactive beams.
