# Big Bang Nucleosynthesis in the presence of sterile neutrinos with altered dispersion relations

> Big Bang Nucleosynthesis imposes stringent bounds on light sterile neutrinos mixing with the active flavors. Here we discuss how altered dispersion relations can weaken such bounds and allow compatibility of new sterile neutrino degrees of freedom with a successful generation of the light elements in the early Universe.

# Big Bang Nucleosynthesis in the presence of sterile neutrinos with altered dispersion relations

## Key claim

Altered dispersion relations (ADRs) can suppress active-sterile neutrino mixing at high energies, thereby preventing sterile neutrinos from thermalizing before Big Bang Nucleosynthesis and allowing parameter space for sterile neutrinos that would otherwise be excluded by BBN bounds. The effect is analogous to suppression from a lepton asymmetry but does not require any lepton asymmetry.

## Method

The authors compute the effective two-flavor oscillation probability for active-sterile neutrino mixing including both the standard matter potential of the early Universe and an additional ADR term A^ADR = ±3.151 ε T, which models Lorentz-violating differences in propagation speeds between active and sterile neutrinos. They then compare the sterile neutrino production rate Γ_νs (given by the active neutrino interaction rate multiplied by the averaged oscillation probability) to the Hubble expansion rate H(T) for temperatures above T_BBN ≈ 0.8 MeV, imposing the condition that Γ_νs < H(T) to prevent thermalization. The resulting constraints on Δm² and sin²(2θ) are mapped for ν_e–ν_s and ν_μ,τ–ν_s oscillations with ADR parameters ε = 10⁻¹² and 10⁻¹¹, for both positive and negative ADR potentials.

## Key numbers

- T_BBN ≈ 0.8 MeV, defined as the temperature where the BBN reaction rate Γ_BBN falls below the Hubble rate.
- Active neutrino decoupling temperatures: T_νe = 2.6 MeV, T_νμ,τ = 4.4 MeV.
- Matter potential at BBN: A^matter(T_BBN) ∼ 10⁻¹⁴ eV; ADR potential at BBN: A^ADR = 3.151 ε T_BBN ∼ 10⁻⁶ eV for ε = 10⁻¹¹.
- For pure matter effects, the allowed region is Δm² sin²(2θ) ≲ 6.5 × 10⁻⁸ eV² for ν_e–ν_s and ≲ 1.7 × 10⁻⁸ eV² for ν_μ,τ–ν_s.
- With ADR (ε = 10⁻¹²), the allowed region expands to Δm² sin²(2θ) ≲ 0.71 eV² for ν_e–ν_s and ≲ 0.28 eV² for ν_μ,τ–ν_s.
- With ADR (ε = 10⁻¹¹), the allowed region expands to Δm² sin²(2θ) ≲ 0.95 eV² for ν_e–ν_s and ≲ 1.58 eV² for ν_μ,τ–ν_s.
- Resonance energies for the case with matter plus ADR (ε = 10⁻¹¹): E_res ≈ 0.2 MeV and 98.3 MeV; for ε = 10⁻¹²: E_res ≈ 0.07 MeV and 174.8 MeV.
- Adiabaticity parameter for the low-energy resonance: γ(ν_e–ν_s) ≈ 10⁻¹⁰, γ(ν_μ,τ–ν_s) ≈ 10⁻¹¹, giving MSW conversion probability P ≈ 0.97.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of sterile neutrino production in the early Universe by Foot (1995) and others, who considered suppression via lepton asymmetry, to the case of altered dispersion relations arising from Lorentz violation or extra-dimensional shortcuts as proposed by Pas (2005) and Coleman and Glashow (1998). The results show that ADR potentials can open parameter space for sterile neutrinos that is otherwise excluded by BBN, providing an alternative to the large-lepton-asymmetry scenario. The low-energy resonance predicted by the model leads to nearly complete MSW conversion of active to sterile neutrinos at late times, implying a sterile relic neutrino background that would make detection of the cosmic neutrino background even more challenging.
