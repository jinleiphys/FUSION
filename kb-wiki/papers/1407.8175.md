# Correlated Fermion Pairs in Nuclei and Ultracold Atomic Gases

> Background: The high momentum distribution of atoms in two spin-state ultra-cold atomic gases with strong short-range interactions between atoms with different spins, which can be described using Tan's contact, are dominated by short range pairs of different fermions and decreases as $k^{-4}$. In atomic nuclei the momentum distribution of nucleons above the Fermi momentum ($k>k_F \approx 250$ Mev/c) is also dominated by short rangecorrelated different-fermion (neutron-proton) pairs. Purpose: Compare high-momentum unlike-fermion momentum distributions in atomic and nuclear systems. Methods: We show that, for $k>k_F$ MeV/c, nuclear momentum distributions are proportional to that of the deuteron. We then examine the deuteron momentum distributions derived from a wide variety of modern nucleon-nucleon potentials that are consistent with $NN$-scattering data. Results: The high momentum tail of the deuteron momentum distribution, and hence of the nuclear momentum distributions appears to decrease as $k^{-4}$. This behavior is shown to arise from the effects of the tensor part of the nucleon-nucleon potential. In addition, when the dimensionless interaction strength for the atomic system is chosen to be similar to that of atomic nuclei, the probability for finding a short range different-fermion pair in both systems is the same. Conclusions: Although nuclei do not satisfy all of the conditions for Tan's contact, the observed similarity of the magnitude and $k^{-4}$ shape of nuclear and atomic momentum distributions is remarkable because these systems differ by about $20$ orders of magnitude in density. This similarity may lead to a greater understanding of nuclei and the density dependence of nuclear systems.

## Key claim

Although atomic nuclei do not satisfy all of the stringent conditions for Tan's contact theory (dilute systems where scattering length and inter-fermion distance are much larger than the interaction range), the momentum distributions of nucleons in nuclei and atoms in ultracold two-spin-state gases show a remarkable similarity in both their $k^{-4}$ scaling and the spectral weight of their high-momentum tails, despite differing by about 20 orders of magnitude in density.

## Method

The authors compared high-momentum momentum distributions of unlike fermions in nuclear and atomic systems by first showing that for $k>k_F$ (where $k_F \approx 250$ MeV/c for nuclei), nuclear momentum distributions are proportional to that of the deuteron. They then examined deuteron momentum distributions calculated from ten modern nucleon-nucleon potentials consistent with $NN$-scattering data (Nijmegen 1, 2, and 3, AV18, CD Bonn, wjc1 and 2, IIB, n3lo500 and n3lo600) and compared these to measured momentum distributions of $^{40}$K atoms in a symmetric two-spin-state ultracold gas with short-range interactions between different spin states.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron momentum distribution scaling coefficient $R_d = (k/k_F)^4 n_d(k/k_F) = 0.64 \pm 0.10$ for $1.3 \le k/k_F \le 2.5$, constant within about 15% across seven of ten realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials.
- The nuclear Fermi momentum is $k_F \approx 250$ MeV/c ($1.27$ fm$^{-1}$), with the $^3S_1$ neutron-proton scattering length $a = 5.42$ fm, placing medium and heavy nuclei in the unitary regime where $|k_F a|^{-1} \ll 1$.
- The scaled contact per particle $C/(k_F A)$ for nuclei: $3.04 \pm 0.49$ for $^{12}$C, $3.33 \pm 0.54$ for $^{56}$Fe, and $3.30 \pm 0.53$ for $^{197}$Au.
- Short-range $np$ pairs dominate over $pp$ pairs with a ratio $np/pp = 18 \pm 5$, with spin-triplet $np$ pairs accounting for $85 \pm 3$% of all pairs.
- Each fermion has approximately 20% probability of belonging to a high-momentum different-fermion pair in both nuclear and atomic systems.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Amado and Woloshyn (1976), who showed nuclear momentum distributions $n(k) \propto (k^{-2} v(k))^2$, and by Sartor and Mahaux (1980, 1982), who found approximately $k^{-4}$ behavior for dilute Fermi gases. It builds on comparisons between nuclear and cold atomic systems by Zinner and Jensen (2008, 2013), who suggested contact parameters should be universal for nuclear systems in the limit of large scattering length, and by Carlson et al. (2012), who compared quantum Monte Carlo approaches to neutron matter and atomic physics. The findings enable a potential universal description of short-range correlated pairs across vastly different Fermi systems and may lead to greater understanding of the density dependence of nuclear systems.
