# The specific charged hadron multiplicity in ep and eD semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering in the PYTHIA and PACIAE models

> We employed the PYTHIA 6.4 model and the extended parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE 2.2 to comparatively investigate the DIS normalized specific charged hadron multiplicity in the 27.6 GeV electron semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering off proton and deuteron. The PYTHIA and PACIAE results calculated with default model parameters not well and fairly well reproduce the corresponding HERMES data, respectively. In addition, we have discussed the effects of the differences between the PYTHIA and PACIAE models.

## Key claim

The PYTHIA 6.4 model with default parameters does not reproduce the HERMES data for the DIS normalized specific charged hadron multiplicity in 27.6 GeV electron SIDIS off proton and deuteron, while the PACIAE 2.2 model with default parameters reproduces the data fairly well, nearly as well as the tuned HLMC simulation.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA 6.4 event generator and the extended parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE 2.2 to simulate semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering of 27.6 GeV electrons off proton and deuteron targets. Both models were run with default parameters (no tuning) to compute the Born-level multiplicity of charged pions and kaons as a function of the hadron energy fraction z, normalized by the total DIS yield. The results were compared to the HERMES experimental data and to the HLMC (HERMES Lund Monte Carlo) simulation, which uses thirteen tuned parameters.

## Key numbers

- The beam energy is 27.6 GeV for electron SIDIS off proton and deuteron targets.
- The DIS total cross section for e-+p was calculated with cuts of Q^2 > 1 GeV and W^2 > 1.96 GeV.
- The e-+p DIS total cross section is a few orders of magnitude smaller than the pp collision cross section at sqrt(s) < 1000 GeV.
- The default PACIAE results reproduce HERMES data nearly as well as HLMC (which uses thirteen tuned model parameters), while default PYTHIA results disagree with the data.

## Context

This work extends the use of the PACIAE model, previously applied to nuclear-nuclear collisions, to lepton-nucleon and lepton-deuteron SIDIS by updating it to version 2.2. It directly compares with the HERMES collaboration measurements (HERMES, 2013) and the HLMC simulations based on the Lepto event generator (Ingelman, 1997) and JETSET/PYTHIA. The study enables further investigation of the role of the partonic initial state and the space-time evolution of hadronization in SIDIS, and it suggests that the initial partonic state introduced in PACIAE (but absent in PYTHIA) is the main reason for the improved agreement with data.
