# Proton-Deuteron Elastic Scattering from 2.5 to 22.5 MeV

> We present the results of a calculation of differential cross sections and polarization observables for proton-deuteron elastic scattering, for proton laboratory energies from 2.5 to 22.5 MeV. The Paris potential parametrisation of the nuclear force is used. As solution method for the charged-composite particle equations the 'screening and renormalisation approach' is adopted which allows to correctly take into account the Coulomb repulsion between the two protons. Comparison is made with the precise experimental data of Sagara et al. [Phys. Rev. C 50, 576 (1994)] and of Sperison et al. [Nucl. Phys. A422, 81 (1984)].

# Proton-Deuteron Elastic Scattering from 2.5 to 22.5 MeV

## Key claim

The screening and renormalisation approach, applied with the Paris potential in separable form, yields differential cross sections for proton-deuteron elastic scattering that agree well with experimental data across the energy range 2.5 to 22.5 MeV, except at lower energies where the calculations slightly overestimate the data; however, the vector analyzing power (A_y) is strongly underestimated at lower energies, confirming the persistence of the "A_y-puzzle" even with correct Coulomb treatment.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) three-body equations for effective-two-body transition amplitudes using the screening and renormalisation approach to handle the Coulomb repulsion between the two protons. The Paris potential was employed in the PEST1 separable form, including S and P waves in the pp and np spin singlet channels and coupled S-D waves in the np spin triplet channel, with total angular momenta up to J=17/2 for lower energies and J=19/2 for the two highest energies. The screening radius was increased to 625 fm until the renormalised amplitudes became independent of it, and the Born approximation was used for the pp Coulomb T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- Proton laboratory energies from 2.5 to 22.5 MeV were calculated, with the highest two energies requiring J=19/2 for convergence
- Screening radius of 625 fm was sufficient for all purposes
- The relative difference in the differential cross section minimum between theory and experiment (Sagara discrepancy) reached -25% at 18 MeV in earlier calculations, but with correct Coulomb treatment the overall magnitude of this discrepancy was greatly reduced
- The Born approximation for the pp Coulomb T-matrix introduced an error estimated to be well below 1 percent, since Coulomb modifies purely nuclear pd phase parameters by at most 10 percent

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations that used simple nuclear interaction models for pd elastic scattering (Alt et al., 1976; Alt et al., 1985) and pd breakup (Alessandro et al., 1993, 1994) by employing the realistic Paris potential. It directly addresses the "Sagara discrepancy" identified by Sagara et al. (1994), where earlier theoretical calculations using only approximate Coulomb corrections showed large energy-dependent deviations in the cross section minimum. The results are compared with experimental data from Sagara et al. (1994) and Sperison et al. (1984), and are found to be close to those of Kievsky et al. (2001). The persistence of the A_y-puzzle at lower energies indicates that three-body forces or other physics beyond the Paris potential may be needed, while the improved treatment of Coulomb enables more reliable tests of nuclear potential models in pd scattering.
