# Beating of monopole modes in nuclear dynamics

## Key claim

Time-dependent Hartree-Fock simulations of excited gold fragments show that the nuclear expansion dynamics is more complex than a single collective breathing mode; the minimum central density is often not reached during the first density oscillation because of the beating of several monopole compression modes, contradicting the common assumption that fragmentation occurs during the first expansion.

## Method

The authors performed TDHF simulations of spherical, spin-isospin saturated gold fragments (A=191) initialized with a slight dilation and a temperature of 0.55 MeV, using a mean-field parameterized to reproduce SkM* properties with an incompressibility modulus of 200 MeV. The single-particle wave functions were evolved on a 300 fm lattice with 0.2 fm steps for up to 1500 fm/c, and the density profiles were analyzed via Fourier transforms and fits to Fermi shapes to extract scaling parameters for radius, diffuseness, and central density.

## Key numbers

- The breathing mode frequency extracted from Fourier transforms of the radius and density scaling parameters is **ħω = 12.5 MeV**, close to the expected monopole resonance for the zero-temperature nucleus.
- Additional broad peaks in the Fourier spectrum of the diffuseness parameter appear at **17 MeV**, **26 MeV**, and a small peak near **40 MeV**.
- The minimum central density (averaged over a 2 fm sphere) reaches **ρ₂fm/ρ₀ = 0.4**, and this minimum is not reached during the first oscillation but at the **third** oscillation.
- The simulation time step was **0.75 fm/c** and the total evolution time was **1500 fm/c**, giving a frequency resolution of a few hundred keV.
- The nuclear matter saturation density is **ρ∞ = 0.16 fm⁻³**, with binding energy **E/A = -15.8 MeV** and incompressibility **K∞ = 200 MeV**.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of monopole vibrations (beating, 1997; monopole, 1997) and directly challenges the single-breathing-mode picture used in the expanding fireball scenario and fragmentation models (Ber1, Cug1, Fri1) that had been applied to interpret the ALADIN caloric-curve data (Papp1, 1997). The results are consistent with Vlasov-type simulations that observed hollow structures (buble, borderie) but show for the first time that these structures become more pronounced after several collective oscillations, not during the first expansion. This finding supports the interpretation of hard-photon data (Mar95, Sch1) that suggested at least one recompression before break-up, and it implies that fragmentation time scales could be significantly longer (100-200 fm/c) than the single-mode picture predicts.
