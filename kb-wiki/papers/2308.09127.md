# Deep Exclusive Meson Production as a probe to the puzzle of $Λ$ hyperon polarization

> In the 1970s, an unexpected transverse $Λ$ polarization in unpolarized proton-Beryllium collisions was discovered, which initiated extensive studies on spin phenomena in high-energy physics. Over the past five decades, similar transverse $Λ$ polarization has been observed across various collision systems, including lepton-hadron deep inelastic scattering, hadron-hadron collisions, and electron-positron collisions. Despite numerous promising theoretical models, the fundamental mechanism underlying this polarization phenomenon remains inconclusive to this day. However, in both longitudinally and transversely polarized lepton-hadron and hadron-hadron collisions, it is found that the $Λ$ hyperon is not polarized with respect to the initial parton spin direction. How the $Λ$ hyperon acquires its spin has become one of the most crucial questions to address in order to resolve this puzzle. In this paper, I propose to use an exclusive process that can be measured at the Electron-Ion Collider, the Deep Exclusive Meson Production, to explicitly test the mechanism of $Λ$ polarization. The outcomes of this experimental measurement are anticipated to unveil the dominant mechanism by which $Λ$ obtains its spin, eliminating many of the ambiguities that have been encountered in previous studies. Finally, experimental challenges and requirements will be discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring Lambda hyperon polarization in Deep Exclusive Meson Production (DEMP) at the Electron-Ion Collider can resolve the 50-year puzzle of Lambda polarization, but only if the measurement distinguishes between two orthogonal spin scenarios: transverse polarization with respect to the production plane or longitudinal spin transfer from the proton beam.

## Method

The author proposes measuring Lambda polarization in the exclusive process e + p → e' + K+ + Lambda at the EIC, using the self-analyzing weak decay Lambda → p + pi- to extract the polarization via the angular distribution of decay protons. Two orthogonal polarization scenarios are tested: (a) transverse polarization with respect to the production plane defined by p_beam × p_Lambda, and (b) longitudinal spin transfer along the Lambda momentum direction. A standalone GEANT simulation of the B0 spectrometer in the EIC far-forward region evaluates reconstruction feasibility for Lambda particles with energies between 30 and 41 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Extrapolated transverse Lambda polarization at x_F = 1 is predicted to be -0.58 (negative 58%) for scenario (a), based on a quadratic fit to ATLAS, HERA-B, and M2 data with chi^2/ndf = 1.42
- Predicted longitudinal spin transfer for scenario (b) is approximately 50% polarization, derived from proton spin structure: removing one up quark (60% contribution) and adding one strange quark yields roughly 30% reduction from 70% initial proton polarization
- The DEMP event rate for 5x41 GeV ep collisions at the EIC is approximately 18 Hz for the kaon-Lambda final state, requiring only about 10 pb^-1 integrated luminosity to generate 5x10^5 Lambda particles
- Statistical precision better than 2% is achievable with 10 pb^-1 integrated luminosity at 5x41 GeV
- Lambda reconstruction efficiency and acceptance in the B0 detector is approximately 20% for the 30-41 GeV energy range
- The mean displaced vertex for Lambda particles with energy 30-41 GeV is at 2.7 meters downstream from the interaction point

## Context

This work extends the long-standing puzzle of transverse Lambda polarization first discovered by Bunce et al. (1976) in proton-Beryllium collisions, which has since been observed in DIS (HERMES 2007, ZEUS 2006), hadron-hadron collisions (M2 experiment 1989), and electron-positron collisions (Belle 2018). The proposed DEMP measurement at the EIC offers advantages over previous inclusive measurements by eliminating fragmentation effects and feed-down from higher mass resonances, which have complicated interpretations in models such as the semi-classical quark recombination with Thomas Precession (DeGrand and Miettinen 1981) and polarizing fragmentation functions (Gamberg et al. 2021). The measurement could also enable access to Chiral-Odd Generalized Parton Distributions and complement GPD measurements using the same DEMP channel (Ahmed et al. 2024).
