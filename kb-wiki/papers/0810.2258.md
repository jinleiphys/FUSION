# Properties of the phi meson at high temperatures and densities

> We calculate the spectral density of the phi meson in a hot bath of nucleons and pions using a general formalism relating self-energy to the forward scattering amplitude (FSA). In order to describe the low energy FSA, we use experimental data along with a background term. For the high energy FSA, a Regge parameterization is employed. We verify the resulting FSA using dispersion techniques. We find that the position of the peak of the spectral density is slightly shifted from its vacuum position and that its width is considerably increased. The width of the spectral density at a temperature of 150 MeV and at normal nuclear density is more than 90 MeV.

## Key claim

The spectral density of the phi meson in a hot bath of nucleons and pions shows a small mass shift (at most 38 MeV) but a considerable broadening of its width, with the width exceeding 90 MeV at a temperature of 150 MeV and normal nuclear density.

## Method

The authors calculate the phi meson self-energy in a medium of nucleons and pions using a general formalism that relates the self-energy to the forward scattering amplitude (FSA). The low-energy FSA is constructed from experimental data using Breit-Wigner resonance terms plus a background term, while the high-energy FSA is described by a Regge parameterization. The resulting FSA is verified using dispersion relations, and the in-medium self-energy is then obtained by integrating the FSA over thermal distributions (Fermi-Dirac for nucleons, Bose-Einstein for pions).

## Key numbers

- The width of the phi meson spectral density at a temperature of 150 MeV and normal nuclear density (n0 = 0.16 nucleons/fm^3) is more than 90 MeV.
- The mass shift of the phi meson reaches at most 38 MeV.
- Results are presented for four nucleon densities: 0, 1/2 n0, n0, and 2 n0 (with n0 = 0.16 nucleons/fm^3) and two temperatures: T = 100 MeV and T = 150 MeV.
- The imaginary part of the phi meson propagator is shown for a 3-momentum of 0.3 GeV and temperature of 150 MeV, at three nucleon densities: 1/2 n0, n0, and 2 n0.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Eletsky, Belkacem, and Kapusta (2001) and Martell and Ellis (2004) for calculating vector meson self-energies in hot and dense matter, applying it specifically to the phi meson. The results are relevant for interpreting dilepton measurements in heavy-ion collisions, as electromagnetic decays of vector mesons provide a clean probe of the medium created in such collisions (Kapusta and Gale, 2006). The authors note that studies to estimate phenomenological implications are in progress.
