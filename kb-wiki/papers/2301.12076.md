# Difference between signal and background of the chiral magnetic effect relative to spectator and participant planes in isobar collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 200$ GeV

> The search for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions helps us understand the $\mathcal{CP}$ symmetry breaking in strong interactions and the topological nature of the QCD vacuum. Since the background and signal of the CME have different correlations with the spectator and participant planes, a two-plane method has been proposed to extract the fraction of the CME signal inside the CME observable of $Δγ$ from the experimental measurements relative to the two planes. Using a multiphase transport model with different strengths of the CME, we reexamine the two-plane method in isobar collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 200$ GeV. The ratios of the CME signals and the elliptic flow backgrounds relative to the two different planes are found to be different, which is inconsistent with the assumptions made in the current experimental measurements. This difference arises from the decorrelation effect of the chiral magnetic effect relative to the spectator and participant planes caused by final state interactions. Our finding suggests that the current experimental measurements may overestimate the fraction of the CME signal in the CME observable in the final state of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model with different strengths of the chiral magnetic effect (CME), the authors find that the ratio of CME signals relative to the spectator and participant planes is not equal to the ratio of elliptic flow backgrounds relative to those planes, contradicting the key assumption of the current experimental two-plane method; this discrepancy implies that current experimental measurements may overestimate the fraction of the CME signal in the final-state observable Δγ.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with a string-melting mechanism to simulate Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, incorporating a halo-type neutron skin for Zr and an initial CME-like charge separation controlled by a parameter p (the percentage of quarks undergoing charge separation along the magnetic field). They reconstructed the spectator plane (SP) from spectator neutrons and the participant plane (PP) from participating partons, then calculated elliptic flow v2 and the CME observable Δγ relative to each plane. The ratio a = v2{SP}/v2{PP} and the ratio b of the CME signal (obtained by subtracting the p=0 case from the p≠0 case) relative to the two planes were compared, and the CME fraction fCME was extracted using both the standard assumption (b = a) and the actual b value.

## Key numbers

- For centrality bins 20-50%, the ratio b/a is approximately 0.65 (±0.18) from a constant function fit, indicating b is smaller than a.
- The CME strength parameter p is defined such that p=2% in Ru+Ru corresponds to p=1.74% in Zr+Zr, maintaining p_Ru/p_Zr = 1.15.
- The AMPT results favor a small percentage of CME signal when compared to STAR data for Δγ{PP} and Δγ{SP} in isobar collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.
- For centrality bins 20-50%, the ratio fCME{b} (using actual b) is less than fCME (assuming b=a), and the ratio fCME{b}/fCME is less than unity, showing overestimation when b=a is assumed.
- The value of b decreases stage by stage through the AMPT evolution (initial, after parton cascade, after coalescence, after hadron rescatterings) for Ru+Ru with p=10%, while a remains unchanged during the last three stages.

## Context

This work extends the two-plane method proposed by Xu et al. (2017) and used by the STAR Collaboration (2021) to extract the CME fraction in Au+Au and isobar collisions, where it was assumed that the ratio of elliptic flows (a) equals the ratio of CME signals (b). The AMPT model results show that b is smaller than a due to decorrelation of the CME signal relative to the spectator and participant planes caused by final-state interactions, consistent with earlier findings by Ma et al. (2011) that final-state interactions reduce initial charge separation. This finding suggests that the STAR upper limit of approximately 10% for the CME fraction in isobar collisions at 95% confidence level (STAR, 2023) may be an overestimate of the final-state CME signal, though it remains a lower limit for the initial CME signal.
