# Investigation of the $\bar{K}$--$^{6}$Li Interaction and the Search for the $Λ(1405)$ Resonance

> We investigate the interaction of an antikaon ($K^-$) with the $^{6}$Li nucleus, described as an $α+ d$ cluster system. The study aims to explore the formation of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance through the $K^-d$ subsystem in the presence of a spectator $α$ particle. In the absence of dedicated experimental data for this reaction, particular attention is given to providing quantitative predictions for the manifestation of the $Λ(1405)$ structure in low-energy $\bar{K}N$ dynamics within a light nuclear environment. Employing different models of the $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ interaction, we calculate the $πΣn$ invariant-mass spectra and the $α$-particle missing-mass spectra, thereby identifying robust features of the $Λ(1405)$ signal and offering guidance for future experimental investigations.

## Key claim

The paper provides quantitative predictions for the manifestation of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance in the \(\pi\Sigma n\) invariant-mass spectra and \(\alpha\)-particle missing-mass spectra from the \(K^- + {}^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) reaction, but only within an \(s\)-wave framework that treats the \(\alpha\) particle as a spectator and neglects explicit four-body dynamics, with the four-body transition amplitude set to unity.

## Method

The authors model the \(^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) nucleus as an \(\alpha + d\) cluster system and reduce the four-body \(K^- + n + p + \alpha\) problem to an effective three-body \(\bar{K}NN\) subsystem by treating the \(\alpha\) particle as a spectator. They solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form of the Faddeev equations for the \(\bar{K}NN\) system with total spin \(s=1\) and isospin \(I=1/2\), using the energy-dependent pole expansion (EDPE) method to obtain a separable representation of the transition amplitudes. The \(\bar{K}N\) interaction is modeled using three different potentials: the phenomenological SIDD1 and SIDD2 potentials and a chiral SU(3) potential, all restricted to the \(s\)-wave channel, with the nucleon-nucleon interaction described by the PEST potential.

## Key numbers

- For the SIDD1 potential, the \(\bar{K}NN\) subsystem binding energy is \(B = 1.9~\mathrm{MeV}\) with a width \(\Gamma = 70.8~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- For the SIDD2 potential, the \(\bar{K}NN\) subsystem binding energy is \(B = 5.6~\mathrm{MeV}\) with a width \(\Gamma = 63.4~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- The dominant reaction channel \(K^{-}+ ^{6}\mathrm{Li} \rightarrow (\pi+\Sigma)^{0}+n+\alpha\) has a \(Q\)-value of \(94.3~\mathrm{MeV}\) and an estimated phase-space fraction of \(\sim 52\%\)
- The second dominant channel \(K^{-}+ ^{6}\mathrm{Li} \rightarrow (\pi+\Sigma)^{0}+d+t\) has a \(Q\)-value of \(76.7~\mathrm{MeV}\) and an estimated phase-space fraction of \(\sim 46\%\)
- Calculations are performed for incident kaon momenta of \(10\), \(100\), \(400\), \(600\), and \(900~\mathrm{MeV}/c\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon absorption on light nuclei such as \(d\), \(^{3}\mathrm{He}\), and \(^{4}\mathrm{He}\) (investigated by the FINUDA experiment and others) to the \(^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) target, motivated by the FINUDA experiment's measurements of pion momentum spectra from stopped-kaon absorption on various light nuclear targets. The study provides theoretical predictions in the absence of dedicated experimental data for the \(\pi\Sigma n\) invariant-mass and \(\alpha\)-particle missing-mass spectra in this reaction, and it enables future experimental investigations by identifying robust features of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) signal that are sensitive to the choice of \(\bar{K}N\) interaction model (SIDD1, SIDD2, or chiral). The authors note that a fully three-coupled-channel calculation including the \(\pi\Lambda\) channel has been reported by Shevchenko, showing improved agreement with J-PARC E15 data, and that such an extension constitutes an important subject for future work.
