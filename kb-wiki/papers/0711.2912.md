# Multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic gases. From simple models to experiment

> The aim of this paper is to give a short overview for the set of publications considering recently found effect of non-equivalence of multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic gases with globally conserved charge and energy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the non-equivalence of multiplicity fluctuations (scaled variances) across different statistical ensembles persists even in the thermodynamic limit, contrary to the textbook statement that all ensembles become equivalent for large systems. This effect, where global conservation laws suppress fluctuations, survives in the infinite volume limit and has been experimentally confirmed in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The author analytically calculates scaled variances for relativistic ideal gases of Boltzmann particles in the grand canonical (GCE), canonical (CE), and microcanonical (MCE) ensembles, first for simple one-component systems and then for multi-component hadron gases with conserved electric charge, baryon number, and strangeness. The microscopic correlator method is used to compute fluctuations in the MCE and CE in terms of GCE quantities, and resonance decays are incorporated via branching ratios. The model predictions are compared with preliminary NA49 experimental data on Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- For a one-component Boltzmann gas with zero net charge in the thermodynamic limit: \(\omega_{g.c.e.}^{\pm} = 1\) and \(\omega_{c.e.}^{\pm} = 1/2\), i.e., the CE scaled variance is two times smaller than in GCE.
- For massless neutral particles in the GMCE: \(\omega_{g.m.c.e.} \simeq 1/4\) in the thermodynamic limit.
- For massless charged particles with \(Q=0\) in the MCE: \(\omega_{m.c.e.}^{\pm}(Q=0) \simeq 1/8\) in the thermodynamic limit.
- The thermodynamic limit for the scaled variance in CE is reached at \(\langle N_{\pm}\rangle = z \sim 5\div 10\) particles.
- The thermodynamic limit in MCE is reached for \(3\div 4\) particles for the scaled variance.
- The chemical freeze-out condition used is \(\langle E\rangle /\langle N\rangle = 1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier findings of Begun et al. (2004) and Begun et al. (2005) on the non-equivalence of multiplicity fluctuations in canonical and microcanonical ensembles, which contradicted the standard assumption of ensemble equivalence in statistical physics. The paper provides a unified overview from simple analytic models to realistic hadron-resonance gas calculations using the THERMUS framework, and shows that the NA49 experimental data on Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (20A-158A GeV) are consistent with MCE predictions rather than GCE predictions. This enables future experimental searches for signals of Bose condensation in \(p+p\) collisions and motivates further studies of momentum conservation and excluded volume effects.
