# Robustness of third family solutions for hybrid stars against mixed phase effects

> We investigate the robustness of third family solutions for hybrid compact stars with a quark matter core that correspond to the occurrence of high-mass twin stars against a softening of the phase transition by means of a construction that mimics the effects of pasta structures in the mixed phase. We consider a class of hybrid equations of state that exploits a relativistic mean-field model for the hadronic as well as for the quark matter phase. We present parametrizations that correspond to branches of high-mass twin star pairs with maximum masses between $2.05~M_\odot$ and $1.48~M_\odot$ having radius differences between 3.2 km and 1.5 km, respectively. When compared to a Maxwell construction with a fixed value of critical pressure $P_c$ the effect of the mixed phase construction consists in the occurrence of a region of pressures around $P_c$ belonging to the coexistence of hadronic and quark matter phases between the onset pressure at $P_H$ and the end of the transition at $P_Q$. The maximum broadening which would still allow mass twin compact stars is found to be $(P_Q-P_H)_{\rm max} \approx P_c$ for all parametrizations within the present class of models. At least the heavier of the neutron stars of the binary merger GW170817 could have been a member of the third family of hybrid stars.

## Key claim

The existence of a third family of hybrid stars (high-mass twin stars) is robust against a softening of the phase transition caused by mixed-phase pasta structures, provided the dimensionless pressure shift parameter Δ_P does not exceed about 5-6% of the critical pressure P_c; this robustness is not universal and depends on the specific hybrid equation of state model.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid equations of state using a relativistic mean-field model (KVORcut02) for the hadronic phase and a relativistic density-functional approach (string-flip model) for the quark matter phase. They replaced the Maxwell construction of a first-order phase transition with a parabolic mixed-phase construction that mimics pasta-phase effects, introducing a free parameter Δ_P (the pressure shift at the critical chemical potential). They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius sequences and identified the maximum broadening of the phase transition region that still allows disconnected third-family branches.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses of high-mass twin star pairs range from 2.05 M_⊙ to 1.48 M_⊙, with radius differences between 3.2 km and 1.5 km, respectively.
- The maximum broadening of the phase transition region that still allows mass twin compact stars is (P_Q - P_H)_max ≈ P_c for all parametrizations in the present class of models.
- For the KVORcut02 hadronic EoS alone, the maximum neutron star mass is 2.26 M_⊙ and the maximum radius is about 13.9 km.
- The critical value of the mixed phase parameter that eliminates the third family branch is Δ_P ∼ 0.06 (about 6% of P_c) for the new class of EoSs.
- For the comparison EoS of Benic et al. (2014), the third family ceases to exist for Δ_P ≳ 0.02 (about 2% of P_c).
- For α = 0.18, the Maxwell construction phase transition occurs at P_c = 101 MeV/fm³; for α = 0.22, at P_c = 71 MeV/fm³.
- The energy density jump for the Maxwell construction is about 70%–75% of the critical energy density at the phase transition onset.
- The screening parameter α ranges from 0.18 to 0.30, lowering the onset mass from 2.05 M_⊙ to 1.48 M_⊙.

## Context

This work extends the classification of hybrid star phenomenology by Alford et al. (2013) and the high-mass twin star concept introduced by Benic et al. (2014) and Blaschke et al. (2013). It directly tests the robustness of third-family solutions against mixed-phase effects that mimic pasta structures, which had been studied by Voskresensky (2002), Maruyama et al. (2007), and Yasutake et al. (2014). The results enable future analyses of gravitational wave signals from neutron star mergers like GW170817, suggesting that the heavier component of that merger could have been a third-family hybrid star, a scenario later considered by Paschalidis et al. (2017).
