# Quantum fluctuation energies over a spatially inhomogeneous field background in a chiral soliton model

> Based on chiral soliton models, the quantum fluctuation energies of quarks over a spatially inhomogeneous meson field background have been thoroughly studied. We have used a systematic calculation scheme initiated by Schwinger, in which the loop quantum fluctuation energies are evaluated by a nontrivial level summation over the eigenvalue spectrum of the effective Hamiltonian of the system. The effective Hamiltonian can be constructed by one loop effective action of fluctuations of quarks over a static chiral soliton field background. The corresponding Dirac equation is obtained. In a static and spatially spherical case and by the hedgehog ansatz the radial part and the angular part of the grand spin of the wave function for the Dirac equation can be separated. Due to the soliton background the eigenvalue spectrum are distorted. The scattering phase shift can be determined by solve the radial equations at different momentum. The density of states in momentum space can be derived. The effective Hamiltonian has been diagonalized in a Hilbert space where the eigenfunctions are labeled by the parity, grand spin and energy. The renormalization scheme can be carried out by a Born subtraction of the phase shift and the compensating Feynman diagram renormalization. Finally the finite quantum fluctuation energies over chiral soliton background at different parities and grand spins have been numerically evaluated, compared and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper rigorously calculates the one-loop quantum fluctuation energy of quarks over a static, spherically symmetric chiral soliton background using a complete Born expansion and phase shift subtraction scheme up to fourth order, rather than relying on the fake boson substitution method used in prior work by Farhi et al. (2002) for higher-order contributions.

## Method

The authors start from the linear sigma model Lagrangian with explicit SU(2)_A symmetry breaking and obtain the classical chiral soliton solution by solving the coupled Dirac and meson field equations under the hedgehog approximation for the G=0, positive parity channel. They then compute the one-loop vacuum fluctuation energy of quarks by evaluating the scattering phase shifts from the radial Dirac equations at each grand spin G and parity, using a spectral method where the distorted density of states is related to the derivative of the phase shift. The divergences are removed by a Born subtraction of the phase shift to second order for G=0 and to fourth order for G≠0, with the subtracted contributions added back as renormalized Feynman diagrams (Γ₂ and Γ₄) computed via dimensional regularization and a fake boson matching procedure.

## Key numbers

- The valence quark energy eigenvalue is ε ≈ 0.216 fm⁻¹ ≈ 42.61 MeV.
- The classical chiral soliton energy is E_cl ≈ 5.83 fm⁻¹ ≈ 1149.8 MeV.
- Model parameters used: f_π = 93 MeV, m_π = 138 MeV, g ≈ 5.28, λ ≈ 82.1, m_σ = 1200 MeV, and quark mass m_q = 491 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the spectral method developed by Schwinger (1954) and later by Farhi, Graham, and Jaffe (1998, 2002) for calculating quantum corrections on soliton backgrounds, applying it specifically to the chiral soliton model (linear sigma model) used in baryon studies by Birse (1984) and Diakonov (1988). The authors improve upon Farhi et al. (2002) by implementing a complete Born expansion to fourth order instead of using the fake boson substitution for higher-order phase shifts. This enables future extension to the study of quantum fluctuation effects in inhomogeneous phases of quark matter, such as chiral density waves and chiral soliton lattices.
