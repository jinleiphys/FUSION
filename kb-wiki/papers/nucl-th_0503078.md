# Renormalized relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations with a zero-range pairing interaction

> A recently introduced scheme for the renormalization of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations in the case of zero-range pairing interaction is extended to the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. A density-dependent strength parameter of the zero-range pairing is adjusted in such a way that the renormalization procedure reproduces the empirical $^1S_0$ pairing gap in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. The model is applied to the calculation of ground-state pairing properties of finite spherical nuclei.

# Renormalized relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations with a zero-range pairing interaction

## Key claim

The renormalization scheme for zero-range pairing interactions, previously applied to non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations, is extended to the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model; however, the resulting effective coupling is too strong at low densities, leading to large pairing energies in neutron-rich nuclei with diffuse surfaces, so adjusting the strength parameter to symmetric nuclear matter alone does not provide sufficient information for nuclei far from stability.

## Method

The authors implement the Bulgac-Yu renormalization scheme for the RHB equations using a zero-range delta pairing interaction, where the effective position-dependent coupling constant is derived from a cut-off anomalous density with cut-off energy E_c. A density-dependent strength parameter g(rho) for the zero-range pairing is adjusted to reproduce the empirical ^1S_0 pairing gap in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter as calculated with the Gogny D1S interaction. The model is then applied to calculate ground-state pairing properties of spherical Sn isotopes using the DD-ME1 relativistic mean-field interaction in the particle-hole channel.

## Key numbers

- The constant pairing strength parameter g = -330 MeV fm^3 is used for the zero-range interaction, typical of values used by Bulgac and Yu.
- The density-dependent coupling is parameterized as g(rho) = 1/(a_0 + a_1 rho^{1/3} + a_2 rho^{2/3}) with a_0 = -0.064 fm^{-2}, a_1 = 0.447 fm^{-1}, and a_2 = -3.693.
- The Gogny D1S pairing gap in symmetric nuclear matter shows a maximum of approximately 2.5 MeV at low density rho = 0.03-0.04 fm^{-3} (Fermi momentum approximately 0.8 fm^{-1}).
- Convergence of the pairing gap in symmetric nuclear matter is achieved for cut-off energy E_c >= 10 MeV.
- For the Sn isotope chain (110 <= A <= 160), the pairing energy of ^150Sn calculated with renormalized density-dependent coupling is almost 25 MeV larger than that calculated with renormalized constant coupling.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization scheme for zero-range pairing interactions introduced by Bulgac and Yu (2002, 2003) from the non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework to the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. The approach contrasts with the use of finite-range Gogny forces (Berger, Girod, and Gogny, 1991) in the pairing channel, which automatically provide a proper momentum-space cut-off but are computationally expensive for deformed nuclei. The results enable application of zero-range pairing interactions in RHB calculations but highlight that the density dependence must be carefully constrained, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei far from stability where surface effects dominate.
