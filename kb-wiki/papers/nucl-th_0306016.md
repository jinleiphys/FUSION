# Relativistic heavy ion collider as a photon factory: from GDR excitations to vector meson production

> A variety of phenomena, which reveal itself in distant collisions of ultrarelativistic nuclei is discussed. One or both nuclei may be disintegrated in a single collision event by the long-range electromagnetic forces due to the impact of the Lorentz-boosted Coulomb fields of collision partners.The process is considered in the framework of the Weizsäcker-Williams method and simulated by the RELDIS code, which takes into account all possible channels of nuclear disintegration, including multiple neutron and proton emission and meson production. Mutual electromagnetic dissociation of nuclei in peripheral collisions can best be studied at RHIC and LHC. The contributions of next-to-leading-order processes with multiple photon absorption of equivalent photons by collision partners are considered in detail. As demonstrated, the rates of the correlated forward-backward 2n and 3n emission are very sensitive to the presence of double and triple excitations of Giant Resonances in colliding nuclei. A practical application consists in the possibility of beam luminosity monitoring in colliders via the registration of the correlated 1n and 2n emission in mutual electromagnetic dissociation.

## Key claim

Mutual electromagnetic dissociation of ultrarelativistic heavy ions at RHIC and LHC can be used to study double and triple excitations of Giant Resonances, with the rates of correlated forward-backward 2n and 3n emission being particularly sensitive to these higher-order excitations; the practical application is beam luminosity monitoring via registration of correlated 1n and 2n emission.

## Method

The paper uses the Weizsäcker-Williams method to represent the Lorentz-contracted Coulomb fields of colliding nuclei as equivalent virtual photon spectra, and simulates nuclear disintegration via the RELDIS code. Cross sections are calculated for leading-order (single photon exchange per nucleus) and next-to-leading-order (multiple photon exchange) processes of mutual electromagnetic dissociation, with probabilities derived from Poisson distributions for photon absorption at a given impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- At LHC, the Lorentz factor for Pb-Pb collisions is gamma ~ 1.7 x 10^7
- Total mutual electromagnetic dissociation cross section for Pb-Pb at 2.75+2.75 A TeV at LHC: sigma_m^ED(tot) = 6.21 barns
- Leading order contribution: sigma_m^ED(LO) = 3.92 barns (63% of total)
- Next-to-leading-order contributions: sigma_m^ED(NLO_12) + sigma_m^ED(NLO_21) = 1.50 barns
- Triple excitation contribution (NLO_TR): 0.56 barns (9% of total)
- Semi-inclusive cross section for 1nX | 1nY at LHC: 750 mb (LO only), 805 mb (LO+NLO)
- Semi-inclusive cross section for 3nX | D at LHC: 241 mb (LO only), 465 mb (LO+NLO)
- Double and triple excitations in mutual dissociation: ~28% and ~9% respectively

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of single electromagnetic dissociation in fixed-target experiments by Heckman and Olson, and by Scheidenberger and Hill, to the mutual dissociation of both colliding nuclei at RHIC and LHC. The calculations build on the Weizsäcker-Williams method as applied by Krauss and Baur (2001) and Baltz, and use the RELDIS code previously developed by Pshenichnov (2001). The results enable experimental studies of double and triple Giant Resonance excitations, for which only theoretical predictions exist from dePassos (2001), and provide a practical method for beam luminosity monitoring at heavy-ion colliders.
