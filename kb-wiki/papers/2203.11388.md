# The Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov correction to an imbalanced Fermi gas in the presence of impurities

> The effects of induced interactions are calculated in both clean and dirty situations, for balanced and imbalanced Fermi gases. We investigate the effects of nonmagnetic impurities on the induced interactions corrections to the transition temperature in the case of a balanced gas, and to the tricritical point in the case of an imbalanced Fermi gas at unitarity. We find that impurities act in detriment of the induced interactions, or particle-hole fluctuations, for the transition temperature and the tricritical point. For large impurity parameter, the particle-hole fluctuations are strongly suppressed. We have also found the Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit of an imbalanced Fermi gas at unitarity considering the effects of the induced interactions, both in the pure and impurity regimes.

## Key claim

The Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov (GMB) correction to the transition temperature and tricritical point of a Fermi gas is suppressed by nonmagnetic impurities, with particle-hole fluctuations being strongly reduced for large impurity parameter; this effect holds for both balanced and imbalanced gases at unitarity, but the authors note that weak nonmagnetic impurities satisfy Anderson's theorem in the BCS limit, meaning the suppression only becomes significant beyond that regime.

## Method

The authors calculate the induced interaction by including a finite quasiparticle lifetime (spectral broadening \(\gamma = 1/\tau\)) in the polarization function \(\chi_\text{ph}\), using the Born approximation for weak nonmagnetic impurities. They then incorporate this impurity-affected polarization into the effective pairing interaction via the T-matrix in the particle-hole channel, and solve the gap equation and Landau free-energy expansion coefficients to find the transition temperature and tricritical point for balanced and imbalanced 3D Fermi gases at unitarity.

## Key numbers

- The standard averaged Lindhard function in the clean balanced limit is \(\bar L(\tilde \gamma=0) = 0.7954\), corresponding to the GMB suppression factor \((4e)^{1/3} \approx 2.2\) for the transition temperature.
- For a clean imbalanced gas, \(\bar L(y)\) decreases from \(0.7954\) at \(y=0\) to \(0.69\) at \(y=1\), showing weakening of particle-hole fluctuations with increasing imbalance.
- In the BEC limit, the condensation temperature of an ideal Bose gas in 3D is \(T_c^{BEC} \simeq 0.218 E_F\).
- The Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit at zero temperature is \(h_{CC} = \Delta_0 / \sqrt{2}\), which is exponentially small.

## Context

This work extends the GMB correction calculations of Yu (2010) for imbalanced Fermi gases and of Qijin (previous work) for clean systems, by introducing the effects of nonmagnetic impurities on the particle-hole fluctuations. It also connects to the treatment of Pisani (previous work) for the GMB correction across the BCS-BEC crossover, and to experimental studies of impurity effects in superconductors by Mackenzie (experimental reference) and Norman (theoretical reference). The results enable future experimental tests with cold atoms where controlled impurities can probe the screening of interactions in Fermi gases.
