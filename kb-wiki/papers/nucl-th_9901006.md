# Potentials and Limits of Bose-Einstein Interferometry

> We complete the introduction of Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrization of the correlation function by deriving the corrections to the correlation radii which arise from releasing the popular smoothness approximation and approximation of setting the pair momentum on-shell. We investigate the definition range of this parametrization and find kinematic regions in which the YKP parametrization is inapplicable. These problems disappear if the newly proposed Modified Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii parametrization is used. We then focus on the physical interpretation of the correlation radii obtained in the different parametrizations. While the extraction of the longitudinal source expansion from the YK rapidity is found to be rather robust against variations of the source density profiles, the extracted emission duration is quite sensitive to such variations and cannot be reliably extracted except for the case of extremely long source lifetimes.

## Key claim

The Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrization of the correlation function can become ill-defined in certain kinematic regions, and while the extraction of longitudinal source expansion from the YK rapidity is robust, the extraction of emission duration from the YKP radius parameters is highly model-dependent and cannot be reliably performed except for extremely long source lifetimes.

## Method

The authors derive corrections to the YKP correlation radii by releasing the smoothness approximation and the on-shell approximation for the pair momentum, working to order O(q²). They investigate the definition range of the YKP parametrization and propose a Modified Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP') parametrization that avoids the kinematic regions where the original YKP parametrization fails. The physical interpretation of correlation radii is then studied using two model emission functions (Gaussian and box-shaped transverse profiles) with longitudinally and transversely expanding fireballs, comparing pion and kaon correlations.

## Key numbers

* For the box-shaped source with transverse radius R_B = 13 fm, the YKP parametrization does not exist for kaons at the studied kinematics (forward rapidity, Y = 0, η₀ = -1)
* Model parameters: temperature T = 0.1 GeV, average freeze-out proper time τ₀ = 7.5 fm/c, mean proper emission duration Δτ = 2 fm/c, width of space-time rapidity profile Δη = 1.3
* Transverse flow scaling parameter η_f = 0.57 for box-shaped source and η_f = 0.64 for Gaussian source, chosen to give the same average transverse expansion velocity
* Gaussian transverse radius R_G = 6.5 fm, box radius R_B = 13 fm, giving equal rms radii (r_rms = √2 R_G = R_B/√2)
* Pion mass = 0.139 GeV/c², kaon mass = 0.493 GeV/c²

## Context

This work extends the introduction of the YKP parametrization by Podgoretskii (1983) and the Cartesian parametrization studies by Chapman, Scotto, and Heinz (1995), completing the program by providing off-shell corrections analogous to those derived for the Cartesian parametrization by Chapman, Scotto, and Heinz (1995). The paper qualifies the optimistic claims about lifetime extraction from YKP radii made in earlier work by the same authors (1997, 1998), showing that the emission duration measurement is considerably more model-dependent than originally anticipated. The results enable experimentalists to cross-check YKP fits using the provided cross-relations between parametrizations and to avoid pitfalls from kinematic regions where the YKP parametrization is not defined.
