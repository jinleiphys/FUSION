# A moderately-sized nuclear network to assist multi-D hydrodynamic simulations of supernova explosions

> A key ingredient in any numerical study of supernova explosions is the nuclear network routine that is coupled with the hydrodynamic simulation code. When these studies are performed in more than one dimension, the size of the network is severely limited by computational issues. In this work, we propose a nuclear network, net87, which is close to one hundred nuclei and could be appropriate to simulate supernova explosions in multidimensional studies. One relevant feature is that electron and positron captures on free protons and neutrons have been incorporated to the network. Such addition allows for a better track of both, the neutronized species and the gas pressure. A second important feature is that the reactions are implicitly coupled with the temperature, which enhances the stability in the nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) regime. Here we analyze the performance of net87 in light of both: the computational overhead of the algorithm and the outcome in terms of the released nuclear energy and produced yields in typical Type Ia Supernova conditions.

## Key claim

The authors propose a nuclear network of 87 species (net87) that is suitable for multidimensional supernova simulations, claiming it can reproduce the released nuclear energy within a narrow deviation while including electron and positron captures on free protons and neutrons for the first time in a reduced network for Type Ia supernova simulations.

## Method

The authors constructed net87 by adding a cluster of proton and neutron reactions between each node of a standard alpha-network from 20Ne up to 60Zn, incorporating electron and positron captures on free protons and neutrons with well-known rates from Fuller (1980). The molar fractions and temperature are solved jointly using an implicit Newton-Raphson scheme, and the network was tested under typical Type Ia supernova conditions using a 12C+16O burning test with an artificial adiabatic expansion.

## Key numbers

- net87 contains 87 nuclear species, close to one hundred nuclei
- The network scales approximately as N log N with number of species; going from net14 (14 species) to net87 increases computing time by a factor of about 15
- Average wall-clock time for net87: 1.78 plus or minus 0.08 seconds, compared to 0.152 plus or minus 0.009 seconds for net14
- Average Newton-Raphson iterations per global iteration for net87: 4.87
- Electron and positron captures on free protons and neutrons may account for up to 60 percent of bulk captures for Chandrasekhar-mass supernova explosions
- The test used initial temperature T9=1 and density rho9=2 (2 times 10^9 g per cm^3)
- The helium shell detonation test uses densities of about 5 times 10^5 g per cm^3

## Context

This work extends the approach of small nuclear networks used in multidimensional supernova simulations, such as the 7 and 14 species networks analyzed by Timmes (2000) which achieved 20 percent precision, and the approximately 50-nuclei networks used in current multidimensional simulations that reproduce nuclear energy within less than 5 percent deviation as shown by Gronow (2021). The inclusion of electron and positron captures on free protons and neutrons is novel for reduced networks in multidimensional Type Ia supernova codes, and the implicit coupling of temperature with nuclear reactions follows the approach of Mueller (1986) and Cabezon (2004) for stability in nuclear statistical equilibrium.
