# Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay in Heavy Deformed Nuclei

> The zero neutrino mode of the double beta decay in heavy deformed nuclei is investigated in the framework of the pseudo SU(3) model, which has provided an accurate description of collective nuclear structure and predicted half-lives for the two neutrino mode in good agreement with experiments. In the case of $^{238}U$ the calculated zero neutrino half-life is at least three orders of magnitude greater than the two neutrino one, giving strong support of the identification of the radiochemically determined half-life as being the two neutrino double beta decay. For $^{150}Nd$ the zero neutrino matrix elements are of the order of magnitude of, but lesser than, those evaluated using the QRPA. This result confirms that different nuclear models produce similar zero neutrino matrix elements, contrary to the two neutrino case. Using these pseudo SU(3) results and the upper limit for the neutrino mass we estimate the $ββ_{0ν}$ half-lives for six nuclei. An upper limit for majoron coupling constant is extracted from the experimental data.

## Key claim

The pseudo SU(3) model predicts that for $^{238}U$ the neutrinoless double beta decay half-life is at least three orders of magnitude greater than the two-neutrino mode half-life, supporting the identification of the radiochemically measured decay as the two-neutrino mode; for $^{150}Nd$ the zero-neutrino matrix elements are of the same order as but smaller than those from QRPA calculations, confirming that different nuclear models produce similar zero-neutrino matrix elements unlike the two-neutrino case.

## Method

The authors used the pseudo SU(3) shell model to describe the ground states of six heavy deformed nuclei, restricting normal parity configurations to pseudo spin zero and abnormal parity configurations to seniority zero. They evaluated the nuclear matrix elements $M_{0\nu}$ for the neutrinoless double beta decay using the closure approximation with a neutrino potential, including finite nucleon size and short-range correlation effects, and combined these with phase space factors to compute half-lives.

## Key numbers

- For $^{238}U$, the predicted $0\nu$ half-life is $\tau_{0\nu}^{1/2} = 1.0 \times 10^{24}$ years, three orders of magnitude greater than the predicted $2\nu$ half-life of $1.4 \times 10^{21}$ years
- For $^{150}Nd$, the pseudo SU(3) $0\nu$ matrix element is about a factor four smaller than the QRPA estimation of $M_{0\nu}=6.075$ from Mut89
- The phase space integral for the majoron-emitting mode in $^{150}Nd$ is $G_{0\nu \chi} = 0.466 \times 10^{-14}$ years$^{-1}$
- The experimental lower limit for the majoron-emitting mode in $^{150}Nd$ is $\tau^{1/2}_{0\nu \chi} > 5.30 \times 10^{20}$ years
- The extracted upper limit for the majoron coupling constant is $<g_{\nu ,\chi}> \le 4.1 \times 10^{-4}$
- The average excitation energy parameterization used is $\overline E = 1.12 A^{1/2}$

## Context

This work extends the pseudo SU(3) model calculations previously applied to two-neutrino double beta decay in Cas94 to the neutrinoless mode, providing results for six heavy deformed nuclei including $^{238}U$ and $^{150}Nd$. The results are compared with QRPA calculations from Mut89 and Tom91, showing that zero-neutrino matrix elements are more stable across models than two-neutrino matrix elements, though the pseudo SU(3) values are systematically smaller. The findings enable improved extraction of the effective neutrino mass and majoron coupling constant from experimental limits, and identify $^{150}Nd$ as a candidate for future experiments aiming to reach sensitivity to neutrino masses down to 0.1 eV as discussed by Nem94.
