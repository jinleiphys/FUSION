# Analysis of fusion-fission dynamics by pre-scission neutron emission in $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb

> We analyzed the experimental data of the pre-scission neutron multiplicity in connection with fission fragments in the reaction $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb at the incident energy corresponding to the excitation energy of compound nucleus $E^{*}$=185.9 MeV, which was performed by DéMoN group. The relation between the pre-scission neutron multiplicity and each reaction process having different reaction time is investigated. In order to study the fusion-fission process accompanied by neutron emission, the fluctuation-dissipation model combined with a statistical model is employed. It is found that the fusion-fission process and the quasi-fission process are clearly distinguished in correlation with the pre-scission neutron multiplicity.

## Key claim
The pre-scission neutron multiplicity distribution in the reaction $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb at $E^{*}$=185.9 MeV shows two distinct components, which the authors attribute to the quasi-fission (QF) process (around $\nu_n=4$) and the fusion-fission (FF) process (around $\nu_n=8$), respectively, based on a fluctuation-dissipation model combined with a statistical model for neutron emission.

## Method
The authors employed a three-dimensional Langevin equation in a deformation space defined by two-center parameters ($z_0$, $\delta$, $\alpha$) with a fixed neck parameter, using a hydrodynamical inertia tensor and wall-and-window one-body dissipation. Neutron emission was coupled to the dynamical calculation via a Monte Carlo procedure using the statistical model decay width, with the level density parameter $a_n$ and neutron binding energy $B_n$ treated as deformation-dependent parameters. 1,000 trajectories were started from the contact point, and the fusion-fission (FF) process was distinguished from the quasi-fission (QF) process by whether the trajectory entered a defined "fusion box" inside the fission saddle point.

## Key numbers
- Excitation energy of the compound nucleus: $E^{*}=185.9$ MeV for the reaction $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb.
- Experimental pre-scission neutron multiplicity distribution shows two components: one around $\nu_n=4$ and another around $\nu_n=8$.
- Mean travelling time for the FF process: $7\times10^{-20}$ sec; for the QF process: approximately $2\times10^{-20}$ sec.
- Level density parameters used to reproduce experimental features: $a_n^{sph}=A/12.0$ (inside the fusion box) and $a_n^{def}=A/10.0$ (outside the fusion box).
- For the QF process, the mean value of $\nu_n$ increases from 3.53 to 3.60 when 20% of kinetic energy (32.4 MeV) is dissipated before contact; for the FF process, it decreases from 7.94 to 7.87.
- Neutron binding energy $B_n$ fluctuates by about $\pm0.3$ MeV across the deformation space compared to the spherical compound nucleus value.

## Context
This work extends the dynamical model of Aritomo (2004) by incorporating neutron emission into the Langevin calculation for the first time in the superheavy-mass region, building on the approach of Frobrich et al. (1993) for combining Langevin and statistical models. The analysis directly interprets experimental data from the DeMoN group (Dona, 1999) on the $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb reaction, demonstrating that pre-scission neutron multiplicity can distinguish fusion-fission from quasi-fission processes even when mass distributions are symmetric. The authors identify this method as a tool for future studies of reactions such as $^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, $^{48}$Ca+$^{244}$Pu, and $^{58}$Fe+$^{248}$Cm at lower excitation energies, where deep quasi-fission processes may be relevant.
