# Subspace-projected multireference covariant density functional theory

> Multireference density functional theory (MR-DFT) has been a pivotal method for studying nuclear low-lying states and neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay. However, quantifying their theoretical uncertainties has been a significant challenge due to the computational demands. This study introduces a subspace-projected covariant density functional theory (SP-CDFT), which efficiently emulates MR-CDFT calculations for nuclear low-lying states. This approach leverages the eigenvector continuation method combined with the quantum-number projected generator coordinate method, based on a relativistic energy density functional (EDF). We apply SP-CDFT to investigate the correlations among the physical quantities of nuclear matter, nuclear low-lying spectroscopy, and the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of $0νββ$ decay in the two heaviest candidate nuclei. Our findings reveal generally strong correlations between the NMEs of $0νββ$ decay and the excitation energy of the $2_1^+$ state, as well as the $E2$ transition strength, although these correlations vary significantly among nuclei. This work also paves the way for refining nuclear EDF parameters using spectroscopic data.

## Key claim

The paper introduces subspace-projected covariant density functional theory (SP-CDFT) as an efficient emulator for multireference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) calculations of nuclear low-lying states, enabling statistical uncertainty quantification that was previously computationally prohibitive. The authors find that nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of neutrinoless double-beta decay are strongly correlated with the excitation energy of the first 2+ state and E2 transition strength, but these correlations vary significantly between different nuclei.

## Method

The authors combine the eigenvector continuation method with the quantum-number projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) based on a relativistic energy density functional (EDF) with nine coupling constants. They construct an EC basis from wave functions of the first kmax states obtained from Nt sampling EDFs, then expand the target wave function as a linear combination of these basis states. The method is applied to compute nuclear low-lying states and 0νββ decay NMEs for the two heaviest candidate nuclei, 136Xe and 150Nd, using approximately 1.3×10^6 parameter sets sampled via quasi-Monte Carlo with uniform distribution around the PC-PK1 functional.

## Key numbers

- The speed-up factor of SP-CDFT increases almost linearly up to 10^4 when the number of sampling EDFs reaches 10^6
- For 64 testing sets, relative emulator errors are generally less than 6%
- Approximately 90% of the variance in quantities of interest is attributed to the parameters α_S and α_V
- Pearson coefficients for 150Nd: 0.93 (M^0ν vs E_x(2_1^+)) and -0.93 (M^0ν vs B(E2: 0_1^+ → 2_1^+))
- Pearson coefficients for 136Xe: -0.91 (M^0ν vs E_x(2_1^+)) and 0.67 (M^0ν vs B(E2: 0_1^+ → 2_1^+))
- Statistical uncertainties (92% C.L.) are within 21% for excitation energies and 12% for E2 transition strengths
- Bayesian analysis yields NMEs of 4.34^{+0.09}_{-0.10} for 136Xe and 5.51^{+0.24}_{-0.26} for 150Nd
- Bayesian model averaging gives NMEs of 4.34^{+0.09}_{-0.11} for 136Xe and 5.52^{+0.23}_{-0.26} for 150Nd

## Context

This work extends the eigenvector continuation method (Frame 2018) from ground-state calculations to nuclear excited states in heavy nuclei, where excitation energies are orders of magnitude smaller than ground-state binding energies. It enables the first uncertainty quantification of MR-DFT calculations for nuclear low-lying states and 0νββ decay NMEs, which was previously impossible due to prohibitive computational costs. The observed correlations between NMEs and spectroscopic observables provide a foundation for applying Bayesian model averaging to determine NMEs of 0νββ decay, and the method can be extended to PGCM calculations based on non-relativistic EDFs and chiral Hamiltonians.
