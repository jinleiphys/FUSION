# Superstatistics and the effective QCD phase diagram

> We study the effect of a partially thermalized scenario for chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature and quark chemical potential, and in particular for the position of the critical end point in an effective description of the QCD phase diagram. We show that these effects produce the critical end point to be displaced towards larger values of temperature and lower values of the quark chemical potential as compared to the case when the system can be regarded as completely thermalized. We conclude that these effects may be important for relativistic heavy ion collisions where the number of subsystems making the whole interaction volume can be linked to the finite number of participants in the reaction.

## Key claim

The critical end point (CEP) in the effective QCD phase diagram is displaced toward larger temperatures and lower quark chemical potentials when the system is only partially thermalized, compared to the fully thermalized case, with the displacement controlled by the finite number of subsystems N that make up the interaction volume.

## Method

The authors implement a superstatistics framework where the inverse temperature fluctuates according to a chi-squared distribution across N subsystems, each treated with ordinary Gibbs-Boltzmann statistics. They compute the effective potential for the linear sigma model with quarks (LSMq) at finite temperature and quark chemical potential, including ring-diagram plasma screening effects and working in the high-temperature approximation, then expand the partition function to first order in 1/N to obtain superstatistics corrections.

## Key numbers

- For a first-order phase transition in the phi-4 theory with lambda=5 and a=50 MeV, the ratio T_c/T_c^0 decreases below unity as N decreases, approaching unity only as N becomes large.
- For a second-order phase transition in the phi-4 theory with lambda=0.01 and a=50 MeV, the ratio T_c/T_c^0 also decreases with decreasing N, but requires considerably larger N to approach unity compared to the first-order case.
- The effective QCD phase diagram computed with a=133 MeV, g=0.51, and lambda=0.36 shows the CEP moving to larger T and lower mu_q as N decreases from infinity.
- The effective QCD phase diagram computed with a=133 MeV, g=0.63, and lambda=0.4 shows the same systematic displacement of the CEP with decreasing N.

## Context

This work extends the superstatistics approach previously applied to particle production in heavy-ion collisions (Wilk and collaborators, 2000s; Tsallis statistics) to the problem of locating the CEP in the QCD phase diagram. Prior theoretical efforts to locate the CEP using effective models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, Schwinger-Dyson equations, functional renormalization methods, and holography (Scoccola, 2013; Fischer, 2014; Ayala and collaborators, 2010-2017) all assumed full thermalization over the entire reaction volume. The results suggest that experimental programs such as STAR BES-I (200 GeV to 7.7 GeV) and future BES-II, FAIR, and NICA experiments should account for finite-number-of-subsystem effects when interpreting fluctuation measurements near the CEP.
