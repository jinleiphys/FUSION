# Distribution of Energy-Momentum Tensor around a Static Quark in the Deconfined Phase of SU(3) Yang-Mills Theory

> Energy momentum tensor (EMT) characterizes the response of the vacuum as well as the thermal medium under the color electromagnetic fields. We define the EMT by means of the gradient flow formalism and study its spatial distribution around a static quark in the deconfined phase of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory on the lattice. Although no significant difference can be seen between the EMT distributions in the radial and transverse directions except for the sign, the temporal component is substantially different from the spatial ones near the critical temperature $T_c$. This is in contrast to the prediction of the leading-order thermal perturbation theory. The lattice data of the EMT distribution also indicate the thermal screening at long distance and the perturbative behavior at short distance.

## Key claim

The spatial components of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) around a static quark in the deconfined phase of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory show no significant difference between radial and transverse directions except for sign, but the temporal component is substantially different from the spatial ones near the critical temperature T_c, contradicting the prediction of leading-order thermal perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors used lattice gauge theory with the Wilson gauge action and the gradient flow formalism to define a renormalized EMT operator. They computed the correlation between the EMT and the Polyakov loop at four temperatures (1.20 T_c, 1.44 T_c, 2.00 T_c, and 2.60 T_c) on lattices with aspect ratio N_s/N_tau = 4, then performed a double extrapolation (continuum limit a to 0 followed by flow time t to 0) to obtain the renormalized EMT distribution around a static quark.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures studied: 1.20 T_c, 1.44 T_c, 2.00 T_c, and 2.60 T_c
- Lattice spacings ranged from 0.0551 fm (at T/T_c = 1.20, beta = 6.336) to 0.0141 fm (at T/T_c = 2.60, beta = 7.387)
- Number of configurations per parameter set: 500 to 1,000
- Flow time fitting window: 0.00401 less than or equal to t T^2 less than or equal to 0.015
- At r = 0.1 fm and T/T_c = 2.60, extracted strong coupling alpha_s = 0.221(17) from the temporal component ratio, 0.286(24) from the radial component ratio, and 0.319(35) from the angular component ratio

## Context

This work extends the gradient-flow-based EMT method previously applied to the flux tube between quark and anti-quark in the confining phase (Yanagihara 2018, Yanagihara 2019) to the deconfined phase around a single static quark. It contrasts with leading-order thermal perturbation theory predictions and enables future comparison with higher-order thermal QCD calculations (Berwein, in progress), as well as extension to full QCD with dynamical fermions and to multi-quark systems such as QQ, Q\bar{Q}, and QQQ.
