# Continuum excitations of $^{26}$O in a three-body model: $0^+$ and $2^+$ states

> The structure and decay dynamics for $0^+$ and $2^+$ continuum excitations of $^{26}$O are investigated in a three-body $^{24}$O+$n$+$n$ model. The validity of a simple approximation for the cross section profile for long-lived $2n$ emission is demonstrated. A sequence of three $0^+$ monopole ("breathing mode" type) excited states is predicted. These states could probably be interpreted as analogues of Efimov states pushed in the continuum due to insufficient binding. The calculated energies of the $2^+$ states are related to the excitation spectrum of $^{25}$O. We discuss the correlation between the predicted $^{26}$O spectrum and experimental observations.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a sequence of three \(0^{+}\) monopole ("breathing mode" type) excited states in \(^{26}\)O that could be interpreted as analogues of Efimov states pushed into the continuum due to insufficient binding, but the authors hedge that these states are predicted within a specific three-body model and their population intensity depends on the reaction mechanism, with the simple sudden-removal approximation giving relative intensities of only \(2-3\%\) for the excited states.

## Method

The authors used a three-body \(^{24}\)O+\(n\)+\(n\) model in the hyperspherical harmonics formalism, solving the inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation with a source function to describe continuum excitations. The \(0^{+}\) continuum was populated via a sudden proton-removal model from \(^{27}\)F, while the \(2^{+}\) continuum was generated using a quadrupole operator acting on the valence neutrons. The nucleon-nucleon interaction employed the quasirealistic Gogny potential, and the \(^{24}\)O-\(n\) channel used a Woods-Saxon potential with depth parameters \(V_s=70\) MeV and \(V_p=70\) MeV, with \(d\)-waves modified by spin-orbit interaction.

## Key numbers

* Ground state of \(^{26}\)O obtained at three-body decay energy \(E_T = 10\) keV in the calculation shown in Fig. 2.
* Two excited \(0^{+}\) states predicted at about 1.76 MeV and 2.6 MeV above the three-body breakup threshold, with a hint of a broad state at about 4.2 MeV.
* Ground state \(0^{+}\) populated with relative intensity \(W_1 \sim 92\%\); excited \(0^{+}\) states populated at \(W_{i>1} \sim 2-3\%\) in the simple reaction model.
* For the \(2^{+}\) states, the lower state predicted at 1.6 MeV with a calculated three-body width \(\Gamma = 115\) keV, and the higher state at 4.5 MeV.
* The R-matrix approximation reproduced the three-body width \(\Gamma_K(E_{3r}) = 4.04 \times 10^{-3}\) keV for a resonance energy \(E_{3r} = 9.7\) keV.
* The spectroscopic factor \(\theta^2\) for the ground state varied from 0.007 to 0.004 for channel radii \(4 < \rho_{\text{ch}} < 25\) fm, consistent with the \([s^2]\) configuration weight \(W(s^2) = 0.0067\).

## Context

This work extends previous three-body studies of \(^{26}\)O by Grigorenko and Zhukov (2011, 2013) that focused on ground-state decay properties, now providing a more detailed treatment of \(0^{+}\) and \(2^{+}\) continuum excitations over a broad energy range. The predictions for \(2^{+}\) states are compared with continuum shell model calculations by Volya and Zelevinskiy (2006), who predicted the ground state at \(E_T = 21\) keV; the present work finds the \(2^{+}_1\) state about 100 keV lower in excitation energy than that reference. The excited \(0^{+}\) monopole states are absent in the shell model calculations of Volya (2006), possibly because those calculations lack the dynamical range of tens of fermis needed to describe these peripheral states. The results enable future experimental searches for these monopole excitations and provide a framework for understanding three-body continuum dynamics in \(s\)-\(d\) shell nuclei.
