# Thermodynamic Derivation of the Tsallis and Rényi Entropy Formulas and the Temperature of Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We derive Tsallis entropy, Sq, from universal thermostat independence and obtain the functional form of the corresponding generalized entropy-probability relation. Our result for finite thermostats interprets thermodynamically the subsystem temperature, T1, and the index q in terms of the temperature, T, entropy, S, and heat capacity, C of the reservoir as T1 = T exp(-S/C) and q = 1 - 1/C. In the infinite C limit, irrespective to the value of S, the Boltzmann-Gibbs approach is fully recovered. We apply this framework for the experimental determination of the original temperature of a finite thermostat, T, from the analysis of hadron spectra produced in high energy collisions, by analyzing frequently considered simple models of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper derives the Tsallis entropy formula from the requirement that the linear term in the Taylor expansion of the reservoir's entropy vanishes when maximizing the generalized entropy of a subsystem plus a finite reservoir, a condition the authors call Universal Thermostat Independence (UTI). The derivation interprets the non-extensivity parameter \(q\) as \(q = 1 - 1/C\), where \(C\) is the heat capacity of the finite reservoir, and the subsystem temperature as \(T_1 = T \exp(-S/C)\), with the Boltzmann-Gibbs limit recovered only when \(C \to \infty\).

## Method

The authors start from a general composition rule for entropy, \(L(S_{12}) = L(S_1) + L(S_2)\), where \(L\) is a monotonic function. They impose the microcanonical maximum condition for a subsystem with energy \(E_1\) and a reservoir with energy \(E - E_1\), then Taylor-expand the reservoir's entropy to first order in \(E_1\). By requiring that the coefficient of the linear term in \(E_1\) vanishes, they derive a differential equation for \(L(S)\) whose solution yields the Tsallis entropy formula. They then apply this framework to hadron spectra from heavy-ion collisions, using the derived relations to extract the reservoir temperature \(T\) from the measured inverse logarithmic slope of the transverse momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

* The non-additivity parameter \(a = 1/C\), where \(C\) is the heat capacity of the reservoir; equivalently \(q = 1 - 1/C\).
* The subsystem temperature is \(T_1 = T \exp(-S/C)\), with \(T\) and \(S\) the temperature and entropy of the finite reservoir.
* For the Stefan-Boltzmann bag model at constant volume, \(C_V = 3S\) and \(T_{1V} = T e^{-1/3} \approx 120\) MeV for a reservoir temperature \(T = 167\) MeV.
* For the adiabatic (isentropic) expansion scenario, \(C_S \le 3S/4\) and \(T_{1S} \le T e^{-4/3} \approx 45\) MeV for \(T = 167\) MeV.
* From RHIC \(AuAu\) data at 200 GeV, the valence quark heat capacity is \(C \approx 4.5\) and the common intercept \(T_0 \approx 48\) MeV.
* The extracted \(T_0\) values from experimental analysis under the coalescence assumption are in the range \(T_0 \approx 45 - 55\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of alternative entropy formulas and their composition rules by the same authors (Biro et al., 2010; Biro, 2011) and provides a thermodynamic foundation for the Tsallis and Renyi entropies that had been applied phenomenologically to high-energy particle spectra (Cleymans et al., 2012; Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2009). The derivation enables the experimental determination of the original temperature of a finite thermostat from hadron spectra, and the analysis of RHIC and LHC data suggests that an adiabatically expanding quark-gluon plasma thermostat best reproduces the observed slope parameters.
