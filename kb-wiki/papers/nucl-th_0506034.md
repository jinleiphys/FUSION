# The Vector Analyzing Power in Elastic Electron-Nucleus Scattering

> The vector analyzing power A_n is calculated for elastic electron scattering from a variety of spin zero nuclei at energies from 14 MeV to 3 GeV. Time reversal symmetry insures that A_n vanish in first Born approximation. Therefore A_n depends on Coulomb distortions and can be large for scattering from heavy nuclei. The vector analyzing power is a potential source of systematic error for parity violation experiments. We find that A_n=-0.361 ppm for the kinematics of the Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) involving 850 MeV electrons scattering at six degrees from 208Pb. This is comparable to the parity violating asymmetry. However for HAPPEX He involving 3 GeV electrons scattering on 4He we find that A_n is very small.

## Key claim

The vector analyzing power \(A_n\) in elastic electron-nucleus scattering, which vanishes in first Born approximation due to time reversal symmetry, can be large for heavy nuclei because it depends on Coulomb distortions; for the Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) kinematics (850 MeV electrons on \(^{208}\)Pb at six degrees), \(A_n = -0.361\) ppm is comparable to the parity violating asymmetry, but for HAPPEX He (3 GeV electrons on \(^{4}\)He) it is very small.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for elastic electron scattering from spin-zero nuclei, including recoil corrections via a reduced-mass-like factor, and assumed the nucleus remains in its ground state throughout the scattering. They used the code RUNT with a Numerov algorithm modified for high precision (including a constant-potential starting step for p-waves, a hyperbolic-cosine integration formula, and Richardsonian extrapolation) and summed the partial wave series using a Gaussian convergence factor \(\exp(-(l/a)^2)\) to handle forward-angle convergence. Results were cross-checked with an independent code ELASTIC and validated against known 14 MeV data and a scaling test where \(A_n\) was shown proportional to electron mass.

## Key numbers

* For 200 MeV electrons on \(^{208}\)Pb at 146 degrees: \(A_n = -890\) ppm.
* For PREX kinematics (850 MeV electrons on \(^{208}\)Pb at 6 degrees): \(A_n = -0.361\) ppm (i.e., \(-3.61 \times 10^{-7}\)).
* For HAPPEX He kinematics (3 GeV electrons on \(^{4}\)He at 5.88 degrees): \(A_n = -1.29 \times 10^{-10}\); at 7.71 degrees: \(A_n = 2.84 \times 10^{-12}\) (zero crossing).
* For 200 MeV electrons on \(^{208}\)Pb near 180 degrees: \(A_n \approx 0.2\) (very large, but cross section very small).
* The SAMPLE experiment result for electron-proton scattering at 200 MeV and 130–170 degrees: \(A_n = -15.4 \pm 5.4\) ppm (given for comparison).

## Context

This work extends calculations of the vector analyzing power to higher energies (14 MeV to 3 GeV) and heavier spin-zero nuclei, where prior work had focused on lower energies (e.g., 14 MeV results from an earlier reference) or on electron-proton scattering (SAMPLE experiment). The results are directly relevant to the PREX experiment (measuring the neutron radius of \(^{208}\)Pb) and the HAPPEX He experiment (measuring strange quark contributions to the nucleon electric form factor), where \(A_n\) is a potential systematic error for parity-violating asymmetry measurements. The paper enables experimentalists to correct for or measure this systematic effect, and provides a benchmark for future calculations of two-photon exchange contributions.
