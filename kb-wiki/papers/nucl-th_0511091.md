# Continuum RPA study of the incoherent mu-e conversion rate and its spurious 1- admixture

> The incoherent transition strength of the exotic mu-e conversion in the 208Pb nucleus is investigated by utilizing the Continuum RPA method, appropriate for the evaluation of the rate that goes to the continuum of the nuclear spectrum. We find that the contribution of resonances lying high in the continuum is not negligible. Special attention is paid to the detailed study of the pronounced 1- contribution which, according to previous calculations, dominates the overall incoherent rate in about all the nuclear targets. The spurious center of mass admixture to the partial rate originating from the 1- excitations is explored, and its elimination is performed by correcting properly the dipole operators. The results found this way show that the greatest portion of the total 1- contribution to the incoherent rate is spurious.

## Key claim

The greatest portion of the total 1- contribution to the incoherent mu-e conversion rate in 208Pb is spurious center-of-mass motion, with about 90% of the total 1- transition strength being spurious across all three examined mechanisms (photon, W-boson, and Z-boson exchange), though the exact percentage above 6 MeV varies by mechanism and is not free of numerical inaccuracies.

## Method

The authors employed a self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus Continuum-RPA (CRPA) model formulated in coordinate space to calculate the incoherent mu-e conversion strength distributions in 208Pb for multipolarities L=0 through 6 with natural parity. The spurious center-of-mass admixture in the dominant 1- channel was eliminated by constructing corrected dipole operators using a collective model prescription that generalizes the method of Van Giai and Sagawa (1981) to arbitrary isospin-dependent fields and arbitrary radial form factors.

## Key numbers

- Muon binding energy in 208Pb: epsilon_b = 10.475 MeV
- Maximum available excitation energy: E_max = m_mu - epsilon_b = 95.183 MeV
- Momentum transfer ranges from q = 0.482 fm^-1 (coherent process) to zero (maximum excitation)
- Particle threshold energy with SkM* force: E_thr = 8.09 MeV
- For the photon mechanism, 86.9% of total 1- strength is spurious; for W-boson, 96.3%; for Z-boson, 90.5%
- Spurious strength above 6 MeV: -1.3% (photon), 7.8% (W), 6.1% (Z) with SkM* force
- For all mechanisms, the fraction of total strength S_aL coming from states below 50 MeV is practically unity for L up to 6

## Context

This work extends previous state-by-state calculations of mu-e conversion using shell model and QRPA methods (Kosmas et al., 1997; Suhonen et al., 1997) by employing Continuum-RPA for the first time in this process, allowing explicit treatment of high-lying continuum excitations. The finding that the greatest portion of 1- strength is spurious agrees with the exact method of Bes and Civitarese (2002) for removing spurious contaminations, and suggests that the approximate treatment in Suhonen et al. (1997), which excluded only the lowest 1- state, may be inadequate since considerable spurious strength remains at higher excitation energies.
