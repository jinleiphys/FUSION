# Jet coherence in QCD media: the antenna radiation spectrum

> We study the radiation of a highly energetic partonic antenna in a colored state traversing a dense QCD medium. Resumming multiple scatterings of all involved constituents with the medium we derive the general gluon spectrum which encompasses both longitudinal color coherence between scattering centers in the medium, responsible for the well known Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, and transverse color coherence between partons inside a jet, leading, in vacuum, to angular ordering of the parton shower. We discuss shortly the onset of transverse decoherence which is reached in opaque media. In this regime, the spectrum consists of independent radiation off the antenna constituents.

## Key claim

The paper derives the general gluon radiation spectrum for a quark-antiquark antenna traversing a dense QCD medium, encompassing both longitudinal color coherence (the LPM effect) and transverse color coherence between partons inside a jet. The key hedge is that the interference spectrum is derived for a collimated antenna in the small-angle region, and the full physical picture including the onset of transverse decoherence in opaque media is presented with the condition that the decoherence time must be compared to the medium length.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in light-cone gauge for a highly energetic quark-antiquark pair created at time t=0, resumming multiple scatterings of all constituents with the medium via Wilson lines in the adjoint representation. The medium is modeled as uncorrelated static scattering centers with a Gaussian white noise background field, and the gluon propagation is described by a path integral satisfying a Schrodinger-like equation that accounts for both color rotation and Brownian motion in the transverse plane. The total spectrum is decomposed into independent radiation off the quark, off the antiquark, and an interference term, with the interference spectrum given in equation (4.5) as the main result.

## Key numbers

* The decoherence rate in the harmonic oscillator approximation is given by Δ_med(t,0) = 1 - exp(-(1/12) * q̂ * θ_qq̅² * t³), where q̂ is the medium transport coefficient and θ_qq̅ is the opening angle of the pair.
* The characteristic decoherence timescale is t_d ≡ (q̂ * θ_qq̅²)^(-1/3).
* For a singlet antenna (γ* → qq̅), the color charge scalar product is Q_q · Q_q̅ = -C_F, where C_F = (N_c² - 1)/(2N_c) for SU(3).
* For an octet antenna (g* → qq̅), the color charge scalar product is Q_q · Q_q̅ = -C_F + C_A/2, where C_A = N_c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous results for dilute media (Mehtar-Tani 2010) and for the strictly soft gluon limit (Mehtar-Tani 2011) to arbitrarily opaque media and large gluon energies by resumming multiple scatterings. It unifies the BDMPS-Z formalism for medium-induced radiation (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996) with vacuum color coherence phenomena (angular ordering, Dokshitzer 1991) in a single framework, enabling the development of an in-medium jet calculus that incorporates both longitudinal and transverse coherence effects. The decoherence phenomenon in opaque media is also discussed in complementary work by Casalderrey-Solana (2011).
