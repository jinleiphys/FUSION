# J=3/2 charmed hypertriton

> By solving exact three-body equations, we study the three-baryon system with charm $+1$. We look for possible bound states using baryon-baryon interactions obtained from a chiral constituent quark model. The smaller effect of the $Λ_c \leftrightarrow Σ_c$ conversion reverses the order of the $(I,J)=(0,1/2)$ and $(I,J)=(0,3/2)$ states, rather close on the strange sector. The diminishing of the kinetic energy due to the large reduced mass gives rise to a bound state in the $(I,J)=(0,3/2)$ channel. After correcting for Coulomb effects the binding energy would be between 140 and 715 keV.

## Key claim

By solving exact three-body Faddeev equations with baryon-baryon interactions from a chiral constituent quark model, the authors find a bound state in the \((I,J)=(0,3/2)\) channel of the \(\Lambda_c NN\) system (the charmed hypertriton), but its existence depends critically on the competition between strong and Coulomb contributions; after correcting for Coulomb effects the binding energy would be between 140 and 715 keV, meaning the state may be only marginally bound or unbound depending on the quark-model parameter.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the coupled \(\Sigma_c NN - \Lambda_c NN\) system in pure \(S\)-wave configurations, using a truncated \(t\)-matrix approximation where the two-body \(t\)-matrices were expanded in Legendre polynomials. The two-body interactions (nucleon-nucleon and charmed baryon-nucleon) were derived from a chiral constituent quark model, with the charmed baryon-nucleon potential simplified by the absence of strange and chiral boson exchanges. The integral equations were solved using the contour rotation method to handle three-body singularities below the \(\Sigma_c d\) threshold, and the \(\Lambda_c d\) and \(\Sigma_c d\) scattering lengths were extracted from the solutions.

## Key numbers

* \(\Lambda_c d\) scattering length in the \((I,J)=(0,3/2)\) channel: \(A_{0,3/2} = -10.27\) fm (negative, indicating a bound state).
* \(\Lambda_c d\) scattering length in the \((I,J)=(0,1/2)\) channel: \(A_{0,1/2} = 9.02\) fm (positive, repulsive).
* \(\Sigma_c d\) scattering lengths: \(A_{1,3/2}^\prime = 0.74 + i\,0.18\) fm and \(A_{1,1/2}^\prime = 2.08 + i\,0.47\) fm (both with positive real parts, repulsive).
* Binding energy of the \((I,J)=(0,3/2)\) charmed hypertriton from the strong interaction alone: 271 keV (for the preferred quark-model parameter \(b_c\)).
* Binding energy after Coulomb correction: between 140 and 715 keV, depending on the width parameter \(b_c\) for the charmed quark wave function (range from 1037 to 271 keV strong binding, Coulomb contribution 322 to 131 keV).
* Exact Coulomb contribution calculated: \(E_C = 131\) keV for the preferred parameter set.
* Mass difference between \(\Lambda_c\) and \(\Sigma_c\): 168 MeV (compared to 73 MeV in the strange sector).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous exact three-body formalism for heavy-flavor baryons (Garcilazo, 2007) from the strange sector to the charm sector, where the \(\Lambda_c NN\) system is studied for the first time. The reversal of the ordering of the \((I,J)=(0,1/2)\) and \((0,3/2)\) states compared to the strange hypertriton (where the \(J=1/2\) state is bound) is attributed to the smaller \(\Lambda_c \leftrightarrow \Sigma_c\) conversion effect, due to the larger mass difference (168 vs 73 MeV) and the absence of strange meson exchanges in the transition potential (Banerjee, 1983). The results enable experimental searches for charmed hypernuclei at facilities such as JPARC (Tamura, 2012), FAIR (Wienands, 2011), and the Super\(B\) collider (Feliciello, 2012), and provide parameter-free predictions for the binding energy that can be tested against future measurements.
