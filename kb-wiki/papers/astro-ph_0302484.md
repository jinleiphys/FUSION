# On the maximum rotational frequency of neutron and hybrid stars

> We construct self-consistent equilibrium sequences of general relativistic, rotating neutron star models. Special emphasis in put on the determination of the maximum rotation frequency of such objects. Recently proposed models for the equation of state of neutron star matter are employed, which are derived by describing the hadronic phase within the many-body Brueckner--Bethe--Goldstone formalism, and the quark matter phase within the MIT bag model using a density dependent bag constant. We find that the rotational frequencies of neutron stars with deconfined quark phases in their cores rival those of absolutely stable, self-bound strange quark matter stars. This finding is of central importance for the interpretation of extremely rapidly rotating pulsars, which are the targets of present pulsar surveys.

## Key claim

The authors find that neutron stars with deconfined quark matter cores, computed using a density-dependent bag constant in the MIT bag model, can rotate at Kepler periods down to half a millisecond, rivaling the rotational frequencies of absolutely stable strange quark matter stars. This result depends critically on using a density-dependent bag constant rather than a constant value, as the density-dependent bag constant provides additional compactness to hybrid stars.

## Method

The authors construct general relativistic, uniformly rotating neutron star models using Hartle's method, solving the stellar structure equations for sequences rotating at their Kepler frequencies. Three equations of state are employed, all based on the many-body Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone formalism for the hadronic phase: one with nucleons and leptons only, one including hyperons (Sigma- and Lambda), and one accounting for a hadron-quark phase transition described within the MIT bag model using a density-dependent bag constant constrained by CERN SPS experimental data on quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter saturation density: n0 = 0.176 fm^-3
- Nuclear incompressibility: K = 281 MeV
- Symmetry energy: a_sym = 33.6 MeV
- Maximum neutron star masses lie in the interval 1.4 solar masses ≤ M_max ≤ 1.7 solar masses
- For hybrid stars (EOSc) at the Kepler frequency: gravitational mass = 1.77 solar masses, central density = 11.54 epsilon_0, equatorial radius = 9.60 km, Kepler frequency = 1.436 × 10^4 s^-1
- For purely nucleonic stars (EOSa) at the Kepler frequency: gravitational mass = 2.35 solar masses, equatorial radius = 11.97 km, Kepler frequency = 1.195 × 10^4 s^-1
- For hyperonic stars (EOSb) at the Kepler frequency: gravitational mass = 1.45 solar masses, equatorial radius = 12.60 km, Kepler frequency = 0.88 × 10^4 s^-1
- Mass enhancement for rotating configurations relative to spherical stars: between approximately 14% and 17%
- Hybrid stars with density-dependent bag constant can rotate at Kepler periods down to half a millisecond

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars (Burgio et al. 2002a, 2002b) by computing rotating configurations and their Kepler frequencies. It contradicts the common belief, based on work by Glendenning (1990) and others, that only absolutely stable strange quark matter stars (Witten 1984) could rotate at half-millisecond periods. The finding enables the interpretation of extremely rapidly rotating pulsars, which are targets of current pulsar surveys, as either strange quark matter stars or conventional neutron stars with deconfined quark matter cores, rather than requiring the existence of exotic strange stars.
