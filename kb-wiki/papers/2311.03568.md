# Describing the Thermal Radiation in $Au+Au$ Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$200 GeV by an Analytic Solution of Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> In high-energy heavy-ion collisions a nearly perfect fluid, the so-called strongly coupled quark gluon plasma forms. After the short period of thermalisation, the evolution of this medium can be described by the laws of relativistic hydrodynamics. The time evolution of the quark gluon plasma can be understood through direct photon spectra measurements, which are sensitive to the entire period between the thermalisation and the freeze-out of the medium. I present a new analytic formula that describes the thermal photon radiation and it is derived from an exact and finite solution of relativistic hydrodynamics with accelerating velocity field. Then I compare my calculations to the most recent nonprompt spectrum of direct photons for $Au+Au$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$200 GeV collisions. I have found a convincing agreement between the model and the data, which allows to give an estimate of the initial temperature in the center of the fireball. My results predict hydrodynamic scaling behaviour for the thermal photon spectra of high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new analytic formula for thermal photon radiation derived from an exact 1+1 dimensional solution of relativistic hydrodynamics with an accelerating velocity field, and claims convincing agreement with PHENIX nonprompt direct photon data for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, but only in the low-acceleration (λ→1) limit, and only after applying a saddle-point approximation and Boltzmann approximation that neglects Bose-Einstein statistics below pT < 1 GeV.

## Method

The author derives the thermal photon spectrum by integrating a source function (Boltzmann-approximated Cooper-Frye formula) over space-time, using a 1+1 dimensional perfect-fluid hydrodynamic solution with accelerating velocity field (Csörgő et al. 2018). The integrals are performed analytically using saddle-point approximation around midrapidity (y=0) and the λ→1 limit, reducing the original seven parameters to four (α, N0, T0, Tf). The resulting formula is then fitted to the PHENIX nonprompt direct photon spectrum for Au+Au at √sNN=200 GeV, 0-20% centrality (PHENIX 2022).

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature in the fireball center: T0 = 0.7 +0.3 −0.2 (stat) +0.3 −0.2 (syst) GeV, from the fit to PHENIX nonprompt direct photon data for Au+Au at √sNN=200 GeV, 0-20% centrality.
* The fit excludes the last data point in the pT bin 7-10 GeV because it has a negative value; including it produces negligible change in parameter values.
* The parameter α is defined as α = 2κ/λ − 3, where κ = c_s^(−2) and λ is the acceleration parameter.
* The freeze-out temperature Tf and midrapidity density N0 are also extracted but their values are not explicitly quoted in the text.

## Context

This work extends the analytic hydrodynamic approach of Csörgő et al. (2018), which had previously been applied to pseudorapidity distributions of charged hadrons, to the thermal photon sector. It directly compares with the PHENIX collaboration's nonprompt direct photon measurement (PHENIX 2022) and with earlier analytic calculations by Csanád et al. (2009, 2011) who used a 1+3 dimensional Hubble-type (boost-invariant) solution to describe direct photon spectra, obtaining an initial temperature of at least 507±12 MeV. The present result (T0 ≈ 0.7 GeV) is above the Hagedorn temperature range (141-340 MeV from various authors) and above the lattice QCD transition temperature of about 160 MeV (Borsanyi et al. 2010), supporting QGP formation. The paper predicts a hydrodynamic scaling behavior for thermal photon spectra that would need to be tested in 1+3 dimensional accelerating solutions, which are not yet known analytically.
