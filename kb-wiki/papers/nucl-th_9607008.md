# Suppression of core polarization in halo nuclei

> We present a microscopic study of halo nuclei, starting from the Paris and Bonn potentials and employing a two-frequency shell model approach. It is found that the core-polarization effect is dramatically suppressed in such nuclei. Consequently the effective interaction for halo nucleons is almost entirely given by the bare G-matrix alone, which presently can be evaluated with a high degree of accuracy. The experimental pairing energies between the two halo neutrons in $^6$He and $^{11}$Li nuclei are satisfactorily reproduced by our calculation. It is suggested that the fundamental nucleon-nucleon interaction can be probed in a clearer and more direct way in halo nuclei than in ordinary nuclei.

## Key claim

The core-polarization effect is dramatically suppressed in halo nuclei when the halo nucleons are sufficiently far from the core (at outer length parameter \(b_{out} \cong 2.25\) fm), so that the effective interaction between halo nucleons is almost entirely given by the bare \(G\)-matrix alone, with negligible contribution from higher-order core-polarization diagrams.

## Method

Starting from the Paris and Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potentials, the authors employed a two-frequency shell model (TFSM) approach with a fixed inner oscillator length \(b_{in}=1.45\) fm for the core and a variable outer length \(b_{out}\) for the halo orbits. They calculated the Brueckner \(G\)-matrix with a Pauli exclusion operator, including intermediate states up to \(n_3=21\) for convergence, and then derived the energy-independent effective interaction \(V_{\rm eff}\) via the folded-diagram \(\hat{Q}\)-box method. The valence (pairing) interaction energies for \(^6\)He and \(^{11}\)Li were obtained by diagonalizing \(V_{\rm eff}\) in a \(p_{3/2}-p_{1/2}\) model space.

## Key numbers

- Experimental pairing energy for \(^6\)He: \(E_p^{\rm exp} = -2.77\) MeV; theoretical value at \(b_{out}=2.25\) fm with Paris potential: \(E_p^{\rm th} = -2.97\) MeV.
- Experimental pairing energy for \(^{11}\)Li: \(E_p^{\rm exp} = -1.14\) MeV; theoretical value at \(b_{out}=2.25\) fm with Paris potential: \(E_p^{\rm th} = -0.81\) MeV.
- Calculated r.m.s. radius for \(^6\)He: \(R^{\rm th}=2.51\) fm, compared to empirical value \(R^{\rm exp}=2.57\pm 0.1\) fm (Zhukov et al., 1993).
- Calculated r.m.s. radius for \(^{11}\)Li: \(R^{\rm th}=3.03\) fm, compared to empirical value \(R^{\rm exp}=3.1\pm 0.1\) fm (Zhukov et al., 1993).
- For \(^6\)Li (not a halo nucleus), valence interaction energy: \(E_v^{\rm exp}=-6.56\) MeV; theoretical value at \(b_{out}=1.75\) fm with Paris potential: \(E_v^{\rm th}=-6.64\) MeV.
- Two-frequency \(G\)-matrix element \(\langle (0p_{3/2})^2;TJ=01|G(\omega)|(0p_{3/2})^2;TJ=01\rangle\) at \(\omega=-5\) MeV with \(b_{in}=1.45\) fm, \(b_{out}=2.0\) fm, and \(n_3=21\): \(-2.217\) MeV (Paris potential); free \(G\)-matrix value: \(-6.896\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic effective interaction approach of Kuo and collaborators (Kuo, 1996) to halo nuclei, using a two-frequency shell model to handle the different spatial scales of core and halo. It contrasts with earlier calculations using empirical Skyrme-type forces (Nazarewicz et al., 1996; Hamamoto et al., 1996) by starting from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Paris and Bonn). The finding that core polarization is suppressed in halo nuclei suggests that these systems can serve as a cleaner testing ground for the fundamental NN interaction than ordinary nuclei, where higher-order core-polarization diagrams (studied by Hjorth-Jensen et al., 1995) complicate the interpretation. The discrepancy for \(^{11}\)Li indicates that a larger model space (e.g., including \(0d1s\) orbits) may be needed for a complete description.
