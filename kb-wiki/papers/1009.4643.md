# Non-Newtonian Properties of Relativistic Fluids

> We show that relativistic fluids behave as non-Newtonian fluids. First, we discuss the problem of acausal propagation in the diffusion equation and introduce the modified Maxwell-Cattaneo-Vernotte (MCV) equation. By using the modified MCV equation, we obtain the causal dissipative relativistic (CDR) fluid dynamics, where unphysical propagation with infinite velocity does not exist. We further show that the problems of the violation of causality and instability are intimately related, and the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation is inadequate as the theory of relativistic fluids. Finally, the new microscopic formula to calculate the transport coefficients of the CDR fluid dynamics is discussed. The result of the microscopic formula is consistent with that of the Boltzmann equation, i.e., Grad's moment method.

## Key claim

Relativistic fluids are necessarily non-Newtonian because the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation contains acausal propagation with infinite speed, which leads to instability in Lorentz-boosted frames; the causal dissipative relativistic (CDR) fluid dynamics, which incorporates memory effects via a modified Maxwell-Cattaneo-Vernotte equation, is causal and stable, and therefore all relativistic fluids must be described as non-Newtonian.

## Method

The paper derives the CDR fluid dynamics by introducing a memory effect into the constitutive equations for the bulk viscous pressure and shear stress tensor, using a modified Maxwell-Cattaneo-Vernotte (MCV) equation that includes a term from the deformation of fluid cells. The stability of the relativistic Navier-Stokes theory and the CDR fluid dynamics is analyzed by linearizing the equations around hydrostatic equilibrium and examining the dispersion relations in both the rest frame and a Lorentz-boosted frame. A new microscopic formula for the transport coefficients of the CDR fluid dynamics is derived using the projection operator method and compared with results from the Boltzmann equation using Grad's moment method.

## Key numbers

* The group velocity of the CDR fluid dynamics is \(v_G \approx \sqrt{c_s^2 + \frac{\zeta}{\tau_\Pi (\varepsilon_0 + P_0)}}\), which diverges in the vanishing \(\tau_\Pi\) limit, showing the relativistic Navier-Stokes theory contains infinite velocity propagation.
* For a massless ideal gas with \(\varepsilon_0 = 3 P_0\) and \(c_s^2 = 1/3\), using \(\zeta/s = 0.1\) and \((\zeta/s)/(\tau_\Pi/\beta) = 1/6\), the group velocity is \(v_G = 1/\sqrt{2}\), which is causal because it is slower than the speed of light.
* With an acausal parameter set where \((\zeta/s)/(\tau_\Pi/\beta) = 1\), the group velocity exceeds the speed of light, \(v_G = \sqrt{4}/3\), and the CDR fluid dynamics becomes unstable in a boosted frame.
* The new microscopic formula for the shear viscosity ratio is \(\frac{\eta}{\beta (\varepsilon + P)} = \frac{\eta_{GKN}}{\beta^2 \int d^3 {\bf x} (\hat{T}^{0x}({\bf x}),\hat{T}^{0x}({\bf 0}))}\) and for the shear relaxation time is \(\frac{\tau_{\pi}}{\beta} = \frac{\eta_{GKN}}{\beta^2 \int d^3 {\bf x} (\hat{T}^{yx}({\bf x}),\hat{T}^{yx}({\bf 0}))}\).
* The new microscopic formula for the bulk viscosity ratio is \(\frac{\zeta}{\beta (\varepsilon + P)} = \frac{\zeta_{GKN}}{\beta^2 \int d^3 {\bf x} (\hat{T}^{0x}({\bf x}),\hat{T}^{0x}({\bf 0}))}\) and for the bulk relaxation time is \(\frac{\tau_{\Pi}}{\beta} = \frac{\zeta_{GKN}}{\beta^2 \int d^3 {\bf x} (\delta \hat{\Pi}({\bf x}),\delta \hat{\Pi}({\bf 0}))}\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of acausal propagation from the diffusion equation to relativistic fluid dynamics, showing that the relativistic Navier-Stokes equation, as formulated in the Landau-Lifshitz theory, is inadequate because it violates causality and becomes unstable under Lorentz boosts. The CDR fluid dynamics resolves these issues and is consistent with the Boltzmann equation results from Israel and Stewart (1976) and the more recent work by Denicol, Koide, and Rischke (2010). The new microscopic formula for transport coefficients enables calculations for non-Newtonian relativistic fluids, where the standard Green-Kubo-Nakano formula is not applicable.
