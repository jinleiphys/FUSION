# Global analysis of the Sivers functions at NLO+NNLL in QCD

> We perform global fit to the quark Sivers function within the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization formalism in QCD. We simultaneously fit Sivers asymmetry data from Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering (SIDIS) at COMPASS, HERMES, and JLab, from Drell-Yan lepton pair production at COMPASS, and from $W/Z$ boson at RHIC. This extraction is performed at next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy. We find excellent agreement between our extracted asymmetry and the experimental data for SIDIS and Drell-Yan lepton pair production, while tension arises when trying to describe the spin asymmetries of $W/Z$ bosons at RHIC. We carefully assess the situation, and we study in details the impact of the RHIC data and their implications through different ways of performing the fit. In addition, we find that the quality of the description of $W/Z$ vector boson asymmetry data could be strongly sensitive to the DGLAP evolution of Qiu-Sterman function, besides the usual TMD evolution. We present discussion on this and the implications for measurements of the transverse-spin asymmetries at the future Electron Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The authors perform the first global extraction of the quark Sivers function at next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy, finding excellent agreement with SIDIS and Drell-Yan lepton pair data but tension when describing W/Z boson spin asymmetries from RHIC; the quality of the W/Z description is strongly sensitive to the DGLAP evolution scheme chosen for the Qiu-Sterman function.

## Method

The authors simultaneously fit Sivers asymmetry data from Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering (SIDIS) at COMPASS, HERMES, and JLab, from Drell-Yan lepton pair production at COMPASS, and from W/Z boson production at RHIC, using the TMD factorization formalism at NLO+NNLL accuracy. They employ the b*-prescription with b_max = 1.5 GeV^-1 to separate perturbative and non-perturbative contributions, and compare two approximate schemes for the DGLAP evolution of the Qiu-Sterman function: treating it as identical to the unpolarized PDF evolution versus using a large-x approximation for the splitting kernel.

## Key numbers

* The b_max value used in the b*-prescription is 1.5 GeV^-1, defining the transition between perturbative and non-perturbative regions.
* The canonical scale for TMDs is set to mu_b = c_0/b with c_0 = 2e^{-gamma_E}, where gamma_E is the Euler constant.
* The hard scale Q for W/Z boson production is set equal to the vector boson mass, Q = M_W or Q = M_Z.
* The Drell-Yan lepton pair data from E288 cover invariant mass bins from 4-5 GeV up to 13-14 GeV, plotted as a function of q_perp/Q.

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of the Sivers function at LO+NLL accuracy by Echevarria et al. (2014) and Bacchetta et al. (2020), and builds on unpolarized TMD extractions at NNLO+N^3LL by Scimemi et al. (2019) and Bacchetta et al. (2019). The paper enables future high-precision determinations of transverse-spin asymmetries at the Electron Ion Collider, and highlights the need for a full treatment of the DGLAP evolution of the Qiu-Sterman function, which has been studied by Kang et al. (2008), Zhou et al. (2008), Vogelsang (2009), and Braun et al. (2009) but lacks an established complete method.
