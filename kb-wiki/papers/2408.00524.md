# Hot and Dense QCD Shear Viscosity at (almost) NLO

> The next-to-leading order weak-coupling shear viscosity of QCD was computed 6 years ago. However, these results have never been applied at finite baryon chemical potential $μ$, even though intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions and merging neutron stars may explore the Quark-Gluon Plasma in a regime where baryon chemical potentials are large. Here, we extend the next-to-leading order shear viscosity calculations to finite $μ$, and we show that, while the convergence of the weak-coupling expansion is questionable for achievable plasmas, it is somewhat better at $μ> T$ than at $μ=0$.

## Key claim

The authors extend next-to-leading order (NLO) weak-coupling shear viscosity calculations to finite baryon chemical potential μ, finding that while the weak-coupling expansion convergence is questionable for achievable plasmas, it is somewhat better at μ > T than at μ = 0.

## Method

The calculation uses linearized effective kinetic theory with a variational method, expanding the departure from equilibrium in a finite basis set. The collision operator includes leading-order 2↔2 scattering processes with Hard-Thermal-Loop resummed propagators and 1↔2 collinear splitting processes, plus NLO corrections from momentum diffusion (including an O(g) shift in the transverse diffusion coefficient δq̂) and modified collinear splitting rates. The authors drop the gain term from soft scattering, making this an "almost NLO" calculation.

## Key numbers

* Debye mass squared: m_D^2 = g^2 (T^2 (N_c/3 + n_f/6) + n_f μ_q^2/(2π^2)) where μ_q = μ_B/3 is the quark chemical potential
* Thermal quark mass squared: m_∞,q^2 = (g^2/3)(T^2 + μ^2/π^2)
* NLO shift to transverse momentum diffusion: δq̂^a = g^4 C_{R_a} C_A m_D T^2 (3π^2 + 10 - 4 ln 2)/(32π^2)
* The shear viscosity rescaled as ηT/(ε+P) is a weak function of μ_q/T
* Perturbative series convergence improves when n_f μ_q^2 > N_c π^2 T^2, but the improvement is modest

## Context

This work extends the NLO shear viscosity calculation of Ghiglieri et al. (2018) from zero chemical potential to finite baryon chemical potential, building on the leading-log finite-μ results of Danhoni and Moore (2022). The results cannot be directly compared to lattice QCD at finite μ due to the sign problem, but can be used phenomenologically to model the (μ,T) behavior of η/s, as was recently done using leading-log results by Danhoni and Moore (2024). The perturbative results serve as a limiting behavior for interpolation between high-density perturbative, low-density hadron-resonance gas, and intermediate density data-driven estimates.
