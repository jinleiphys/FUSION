# The elusiveness of multifragmentation footprints in 1-GeV proton-nucleus reactions

> We use the tools of hybrid intranuclear-cascade/nuclear-de-excitation models to evaluate the sensitivity of several physical observables to the inclusion of a multifragmentation stage in the de-excitation chain and assess the need for a multifragmentation model in the quantitative description of p+56Fe and p+136Xe reactions at 1-GeV incident energy. We seek clear signatures of multifragmentation by comparing different state-of-the-art de-excitation models coupled with intranuclear-cascade models and by focusing on discriminating observables such as correlations and fragment longitudinal-velocity distributions. None of the considered observables can be unambiguously interpreted as a multifragmentation footprint. The experimental data are best described as originating from sequential binary decays. However, no de-excitation model can reproduce the experimental longitudinal-velocity distributions from 1-GeV p+136Xe.

## Key claim

None of the considered observables (correlations, fragment longitudinal-velocity distributions, inclusive residue-production cross sections) can be unambiguously interpreted as a multifragmentation footprint in 1-GeV proton-nucleus reactions; the experimental data are best described as originating from sequential binary decays, yet no de-excitation model can reproduce the experimental longitudinal-velocity distributions from 1-GeV p+136Xe.

## Method

The authors coupled two intranuclear-cascade models (INCL4.5 and Isabel) with three de-excitation models (ABLA07, GEMINI++, and SMM) to simulate 1-GeV p+56Fe and p+136Xe reactions. They compared the model predictions against experimental inclusive residue-production cross sections from the SPALADIN apparatus and the FRagment Separator (FRS), as well as SPALADIN correlation data and longitudinal-velocity distributions measured in inverse kinematics at GSI. The production mechanisms were partitioned by labelling each simulated event according to the de-excitation history (e.g., number of hot fragments in SMM, triggering of multifragmentation in ABLA07, number of asymmetric splits in GEMINI++).

## Key numbers

- For p+56Fe, INCL predicts average remnant excitation energy ⟨E*⟩ = 91.5 MeV and average remnant mass ⟨A_remnant⟩ = 52.7; Isabel predicts ⟨E*⟩ = 66.6 MeV and ⟨A_remnant⟩ = 53.4.
- For p+136Xe, INCL predicts ⟨E*⟩ = 139.2 MeV and ⟨A_remnant⟩ = 131.4; Isabel predicts ⟨E*⟩ = 116.3 MeV and ⟨A_remnant⟩ = 132.4.
- INCL/ABLA07 nominal multifragmentation cross sections: 13.3 mb (1.7% of reaction cross section) for p+56Fe, and 4.6 mb (0.3%) for p+136Xe.
- INCL/SMM nominal multifragmentation cross sections (nhot ≥ 2): 146.0 mb (18.7% of reaction cross section) for p+56Fe, and 137.7 mb (10.0%) for p+136Xe.
- Isabel consistently underestimates the p+136Xe cross sections for 10 ≤ Z ≤ 30 by a factor of about three compared to INCL-based combinations.
- ABLA07 freeze-out temperature for 56Fe: 7.97 MeV (corresponding to 6.22 A MeV excitation energy); for 136Xe: 6.36 MeV (3.70 A MeV).

## Context

This work extends the IAEA "Benchmark of Spallation Models" (David, Leray, and collaborators, 2008-2010) by focusing on more discriminating observables than the inclusive endpoints used in that benchmark. Previous studies by Legentil (2008) had indicated that inclusive observables are rather insensitive to multifragmentation. The paper contradicts earlier claims (Legentil, 2008) that INCL4.2 and Isabel produced remarkably similar excitation-energy distributions for p+56Fe, showing that later developments of INCL (version 4.5) produce significantly hotter remnants. The results enable future work to focus on sequential binary decay models for describing 1-GeV spallation reactions, while highlighting the need for improved de-excitation models that can reproduce longitudinal-velocity distributions.
