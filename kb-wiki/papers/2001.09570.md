# Nuclear medium effect on neutron capture reactions during neutron star mergers

> The discovery of gravitational waves has confirmed old theoretical predictions that binary systems formed with compact stars play a crucial role not only for cosmology and nuclear astrophysics. As a byproduct of these and subsequent observations, it is now clear that neutron-star mergers can be a competitive site for the production of half of the elements heavier than iron in the universe following a sequence of fast neutron capture reactions known as the r process. In this article we discuss an effect which has been so far neglected in calculations of r-process nucleosynthesis in neutron star mergers. We show that the corrections due to the neutron environment even at relatively small neutron densities, within the bounds of numerical hydrodynamical simulations of neutron star mergers and after the onset of the r process, are non-negligible and need to be taken into account to accurately describe the elemental abundance as determined by observations.

# Nuclear medium effect on neutron capture reactions during neutron star mergers

## Key claim
The authors show that the presence of a background neutron environment in neutron star merger ejecta produces a non-negligible energy shift in neutron-nucleus scattering energies due to Pauli blocking, which modifies (n,γ) and (γ,n) reaction rates even at relatively low neutron densities (ρ ~ 10⁻³–10⁻² ρ₀). However, they acknowledge that densities above 10⁻³ ρ₀ may not be directly relevant to the standard r-process, and their results only apply if non-statistical conditions prevail while high densities persist.

## Method
The authors calculate the environment mean potential (EMP) by integrating a neutron-neutron interaction over a homogeneous background neutron distribution, using a Fermi gas model with Pauli blocking to obtain the probability density for neutron positions. They then approximate the net effect as an energy shift ε_EMP added to the neutron-nucleus scattering energy, and compute the modified neutron capture reaction rate by folding the shifted cross section with a Maxwell-Boltzmann velocity distribution. The method is applied to neutron capture on ⁵⁸Ni and ⁷⁸Ni using the Koning and Delaroche global optical potential, with three different neutron-neutron interactions (AV8', Minnesota, and M3Y-Reid).

## Key numbers
- Neutron densities in merger ejecta reach up to ρ ~ 10⁻³–10⁻² ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density.
- The energy shift ε_EMP is about 20 keV at ρ/ρ₀ = 10⁻³ and about 200 keV at ρ/ρ₀ = 5×10⁻³.
- At ρ/ρ₀ = 10⁻², the energy shift ε_EMP is approximately 500 keV.
- The temperature chosen for rate calculations is T = 0.2 GK (2×10⁸ K).
- For ⁵⁸Ni, the integrand f decreases for 0 < ρ ≲ 5×10⁻³ ρ₀ and increases for ρ ≳ 10⁻² ρ₀.
- For ⁷⁸Ni, the integrand f changes very little for ρ ≲ 2×10⁻³ ρ₀.

## Context
This work extends the concept of Pauli blocking, well known in nuclear matter and β-decay in dense stellar environments, to neutron capture reactions in neutron star merger ejecta. The authors note that previous r-process nucleosynthesis calculations for neutron star mergers (e.g., Lattimer and Schramm 1974, Eichler et al. 1989, Thielemann et al. 2017) have neglected this neutron background effect. The paper enables future inclusion of this medium correction in full hydrodynamical simulations coupled to reaction network calculations, and suggests that the energy-shift method could also be applied to proton-induced reactions and to modify Saha-like equations for statistical equilibrium conditions.
