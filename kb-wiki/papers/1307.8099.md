# Spin Polarized Photons and Di-leptons from Axially Charged Plasma

> Axial charge in a QCD plasma is P- and CP-odd. We propose and study P- and CP-odd observables in photon and di-lepton emissions from an axially charged QCD plasma, which may provide possible experimental evidences of axial charge fluctuation and triangle anomaly in the plasma created in heavy-ion collisions. Our observables measure spin alignments of the emitted photons and di-leptons, and are shown to be related to the imaginary part of chiral magnetic conductivity at finite frequency-momentum, which ultimately arises from the underlying triangle anomaly of the QCD plasma with a finite axial charge density. We present an exemplar computation of these observables in strongly coupled regime using AdS/CFT correspondence.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that P- and CP-odd observables in photon and di-lepton emissions from an axially charged QCD plasma can serve as direct experimental tests of axial charge fluctuations and the triangle anomaly, but these observables are necessarily event-by-event because QCD is P- and CP-even and axial charge fluctuations can only be non-zero event-by-event.

## Method

The authors compute the circular polarization asymmetry for photons and a spin-polarization asymmetry for di-leptons from an isotropic, homogeneous QCD plasma with finite axial charge density, relating these observables to the imaginary part of the chiral magnetic conductivity at finite frequency-momentum. They perform an exemplar computation in the strongly coupled regime using the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic model, solving linearized equations of motion for vector gauge field fluctuations in the presence of a background axial gauge field configuration that encodes the axial chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The axial chemical potential μ_A is related to the model parameter α by μ_A = (2α)/(3 U_T^(3/2)) * _2F_1(3/10, 1/2, 13/10, -α^2/U_T^5), where U_T = R^3 (4πT/3)^2 with R^3 = 1.44 in units of GeV.
- The relaxation time scale of axial charges in the RHIC plasma is about 1-10 fm depending on α_s, and larger for LHC with a smaller α_s.
- The di-lepton asymmetry A_{±l\bar l} is proportional to the lepton mass squared m^2, vanishing for massless leptons.
- The photon circular polarization asymmetry A_{±γ} is given by A_{±γ} = Im σ_χ(k^0) / Re σ_11(k^0) evaluated at k^0 = |k|.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral magnetic wave (CMW) phenomena in heavy-ion collisions, which were previously proposed by Kharzeev (2007), Fukushima (2008), and Kharzeev (2010). Unlike CME and CMW observables that require external magnetic fields and are sensitive to background effects unrelated to the triangle anomaly (as discussed by Asakawa (2010), Bzdak (2009), and Pratt (2010)), the proposed spin-polarized observables do not rely on external electromagnetic fields and are direct consequences of the triangle anomaly. The work also relates to an earlier observation by Akamatsu (2013) on polarization-dependent photon instability in axially charged plasma, but argues that the more plausible phenomenon in heavy-ion collisions is a simple asymmetry in emission rates for different spins.
