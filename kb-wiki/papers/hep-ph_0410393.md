# Isospin quantum number of D_{s0}^+(2317)

> The D_s^+ pi^0 and D_s^{*+} gamma decays of the D_{s0}^+(2317) are studied by assigning it to various charmed strange scalar mesons. As a result, it is found that its assignment to an iso-triplet four-quark meson is favored by the severest experimental constraint on the ratio of the rates for these decays, while assigning it to an I=0 state (a four-quark or a conventional {c\bar{s}}) is inconsistent with this constraint.

## Key claim

The paper argues that assigning the \(D_{s0}^{+}(2317)\) resonance to an iso-triplet four-quark meson (\(\hat F_I^{+}\)) is favored by the experimental constraint on the ratio of its radiative to pionic decay rates, while assigning it to an \(I=0\) state (either a conventional \(\{c\bar s\}\) scalar or an iso-singlet four-quark meson) is inconsistent with this constraint. The authors use a phenomenological approach with modified asymptotic \(SU_f(4)\) symmetry and vector meson dominance, rather than a detailed model, to reach this conclusion.

## Method

The authors studied the \(D_{s0}^{+}(2317) \rightarrow D_s^{+}\pi^0\) and \(D_{s0}^{+}(2317) \rightarrow D_s^{*+}\gamma\) decays by assigning the resonance to three possible charmed strange scalar mesons: an iso-triplet four-quark state (\(\hat F_I^{+}\)), an iso-singlet four-quark state (\(\hat F_0^{+}\)), and a conventional \(\{c\bar s\}\) scalar (\(D_{s0}^{*+}\)). They used a hard-pion technique with PCAC and asymptotic \(SU_f(4)\) symmetry (modified by a suppression factor \(\beta\) from color-spin crossing matrices) to estimate the pionic decay rates, and vector meson dominance (VMD) with flavor \(SU_f(4)\) symmetry to estimate the radiative decay rates. For the \(I=0\) assignments, the isospin-violating pionic decay was assumed to proceed through \(\pi^0\)-\(\eta\) mixing, with the mixing parameter \(\epsilon\) extracted from the \(D_s^{*+}\rightarrow D_s^{+}\pi^0\) decay.

## Key numbers

*   Experimental constraint on the ratio of decay rates: \(R_{\rm exp} \equiv \Gamma(D_{s0}^{+}(2317)\rightarrow D_s^{*+}\gamma) / \Gamma(D_{s0}^{+}(2317)\rightarrow D_s^{+}\pi^0) < 0.059\).
*   Estimated pionic decay width for the iso-triplet four-quark assignment: \(\Gamma(\hat F_I^{+}\rightarrow D_s^{+}\pi^0) \sim 9\) MeV.
*   Estimated radiative decay width for the iso-triplet four-quark assignment: \(\Gamma(\hat F_I^{+}\rightarrow D_s^{*+}\gamma) \simeq 45\) keV.
*   Resulting ratio for the iso-triplet four-quark assignment: \(R_{\hat F_I} \sim 0.005\), which satisfies the experimental constraint.
*   Estimated pionic decay width for the iso-singlet four-quark assignment: \(\Gamma(\hat F_0^{+}\rightarrow D_s^{+}\pi^0) \sim 0.7\) keV.
*   Estimated radiative decay width for the iso-singlet four-quark assignment: \(\Gamma(\hat F_0^{+}\rightarrow D_s^{*+}\gamma) \simeq 4.7\) keV.
*   Resulting ratio for the iso-singlet four-quark assignment: \(R_{\hat F_0} \sim 7\), which violates the experimental constraint.
*   Estimated pionic decay width for the conventional \(\{c\bar s\}\) assignment: \(\Gamma(D_{s0}^{*+}\rightarrow D_s^{+}\pi^0) \sim 0.6\) keV.
*   Estimated radiative decay width for the conventional \(\{c\bar s\}\) assignment: \(\Gamma(D_{s0}^{*+}\rightarrow D_s^{*+}\gamma) \sim 35\) keV.
*   Resulting ratio for the conventional \(\{c\bar s\}\) assignment: \(R_{D_{s0}^*} \sim 60\), which violates the experimental constraint.

## Context

This work extends the discussion on the nature of the \(D_{s0}^{+}(2317)\) resonance, which was observed as a narrow \(D_s^{+}\pi^0\) peak at about 2317 MeV. It directly addresses the failure of heavy-hadron chiral perturbation theory (as used by Mehen and Springer, 2004) and other models (e.g., Colangelo et al., 2003) to satisfy the stringent experimental upper bound on the ratio of radiative to pionic decay rates. The paper builds on the four-quark meson interpretation for light scalar mesons (Jaffe, 1977; Achasov, 1982; Maiani, 2004) and the specific four-quark assignments for charmed mesons proposed by Terasaki (2003). The results enable a clear experimental test: the observation of neutral or doubly charged partners of the \(D_{s0}^{+}(2317)\) would confirm the iso-triplet four-quark assignment, while their absence would favor the \(I=0\) interpretations, which the authors find inconsistent with the decay ratio constraint.
