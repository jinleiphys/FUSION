# Isospin Equilibration in Neutron Star Mergers

> We analyze the isospin equilibration properties of neutrinoless nuclear ($npe$) matter in the temperature and density range that is relevant to neutron star mergers. Our analysis incorporates neutrino-transparency corrections to the isospin (``beta'') equilibrium condition which become noticeable at $T\gtrsim 1\,$MeV. We find that the isospin relaxation rate rises rapidly as temperature rises, and at $T\approx 5\,$MeV it is comparable to the timescale of the density oscillations that occur immediately after the merger. This produces a resonant peak in the bulk viscosity at $T\approx 5\,$MeV, which causes density oscillations to be damped on the timescale of the merger. Our calculations suggest that isospin relaxation dynamics may also be relevant when neutrinos are treated more accurately via neutrino transport schemes.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in neutrinoless nuclear matter at temperatures above about 1 MeV, the isospin (beta) equilibrium condition receives a non-negligible correction from neutrino-transparency effects, producing a nonzero isospin chemical potential that can reach 20 MeV; this correction makes the isospin relaxation rate and resulting bulk viscosity peak at temperatures around 5 MeV, where the relaxation timescale becomes comparable to the millisecond density oscillations in neutron star mergers.

## Method

The authors calculated the isospin relaxation rate and bulk viscosity of homogeneous neutrinoless npe matter by integrating the full phase-space direct Urca rates (neutron decay and electron capture) using relativistic dispersion relations and complete matrix elements, combined with modified Urca rates calculated in the Fermi surface approximation. They solved the rate equation for the isospin fraction perturbatively around the equilibrium state defined by zero net isospin production, using two relativistic mean-field theory models (IUF and QMC-RMF3) to describe nuclear matter properties. The calculations were performed in the isothermal regime for temperatures from 0 to 10 MeV and densities from one to several times nuclear saturation density.

## Key numbers

- The correction to the beta equilibrium condition (isospin chemical potential) becomes noticeable at T greater than about 1 MeV and can reach values as large as 20 MeV
- At T approximately 5 MeV, the isospin relaxation rate is comparable to the timescale of density oscillations that occur immediately after the merger, producing a resonant peak in the bulk viscosity
- The relaxation time is between 0.1 ms and 25 ms (the merger-relevant timescale) for temperatures in the range of approximately 2 to 4 MeV below the direct Urca threshold density for the IUF equation of state
- For the IUF equation of state, the direct Urca threshold occurs at a density of approximately 4 times nuclear saturation density (4 n0)
- The electron chemical potential at the densities studied is in the 100 MeV range, so T is much less than mu_e for the temperature range considered

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of isospin relaxation in neutrinoless matter (Alford 2019, Alford 2021, Most 2022, Alford 2023) by incorporating the proper equilibrium condition with nonzero isospin chemical potential, which was neglected in earlier treatments that assumed mu_n = mu_p + mu_e (Sawyer 1989, Haensel 1992, Haensel 2000, Jones 2001, Gusakov 2007, Alford 2010, Schmitt 2017). The results suggest that isospin relaxation dynamics should be included in merger simulations using more realistic neutrino transport schemes, as previous simulations have either assumed instantaneous equilibration or frozen composition (Celora 2022), or only partly implemented low-density approximations to Urca rates (Ruffert 1996, Most 2019, Foucart 2022, Zappa 2022, Musolino 2023).
