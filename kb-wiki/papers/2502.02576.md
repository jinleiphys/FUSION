# Neutrino Induced Charged Current Coherent Pion Production for Constraining the Muon Neutrino Flux at DUNE

> We study neutrino induced charge current coherent pion production ($ν_μ\text{CC-Coh}π$) as a tool for constraining the neutrino flux at the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE). The neutrino energy and flavor in the process can be directly reconstructed from the outgoing particles, making it especially useful to specifically constrain the muon neutrino component of the total flux. The cross section of this process can be obtained using the Adler relation with the $π$-Ar elastic scattering cross section, taken either from external data or, as we explore, from a simultaneous measurement in the DUNE near detector. We develop a procedure that leverages $ν_μ\text{CC-Coh}π$ events to fit for the neutrino flux while simultaneously accounting for relevant effects in the cross section. We project that this method has the statistical power to constrain the uncertainty on the normalization of the flux at its peak to a few percent. This study demonstrates the potential utility of a $ν_μ\text{CC-Coh}π$ flux constraint, though further work will be needed to determine the range of validity and precision of the Adler relation upon which it relies, as well as to measure the $π$-Ar elastic scattering cross section to the requisite precision. We discuss the experimental and phenomenological developments necessary to unlock the $ν_μ\text{CC-Coh}π$ process as a "standard candle'' for neutrino experiments.

# Neutrino Induced Charged Current Coherent Pion Production for Constraining the Muon Neutrino Flux at DUNE

## Key claim
The muon neutrino charged current coherent pion production process can constrain the DUNE muon neutrino flux normalization uncertainty to a few percent at its peak energy, but only if the range of validity and precision of the Adler relation is established and the pion-argon elastic scattering cross section is measured to the requisite precision.

## Method
The authors developed a template fit procedure using simulated neutrino charged current coherent pion production events in the DUNE near detector, fitting the two-dimensional distribution of reconstructed neutrino energy and reconstructed Q². The fit simultaneously accounts for systematic uncertainties from the axial-vector form factor (modeled with a z-expansion) and the pion-argon elastic scattering cross section, using Markov chain Monte Carlo to maximize the likelihood function. Two scenarios were considered: one where the pion-argon cross section is measured simultaneously in the DUNE near detector, and another where an external constraint on the pion-argon cross section is applied.

## Key numbers
- The method can reduce the fractional uncertainty on the muon neutrino flux from about 10% to 1.5% near the oscillation maxima energy when the pion-argon cross section is simultaneously measured in the DUNE near detector.
- With an external constraint on the pion-argon cross section of uncertainty 1-5%, the fractional flux uncertainty is reduced to approximately 2-5%.
- There will be about 270,000 charged current coherent pion production interactions per year in a 68-tonne fiducial volume in the DUNE near detector, compared to 7,500 neutrino-electron scattering events.
- The analysis limits phase space to Q² less than 0.2 GeV² and requires that the energy transfer to the pion is greater than three times the square root of Q².

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to constrain neutrino flux using neutrino-electron elastic scattering, demonstrated by MINERvA (MINERvAConstraint1, MINERvAConstraint2) and projected for DUNE (DUNEConstraint). It also builds on previous measurements of charged current coherent pion production by experiments including K2K (K2K:2005uiu), SciBooNE (SciBooNE:2008bzb), MiniBooNE (MiniBooNE:2008mmr), NOMAD (NOMAD:2009idt), ArgoNeuT (ArgoNeuT:2014uwh), T2K (T2K:2016soz), MINERvA (MINERvA:2014ani, MINERvA:2022esg), and NOvA (NOvA:2019bdw). The method enables a complementary flux constraint to neutrino-electron scattering by constraining different components of the flux, particularly the muon neutrino flavor which can be directly reconstructed from the outgoing muon and pion. Further work is needed to determine the range of validity of the Adler relation and to measure the elastic pion-argon cross section at experiments such as ProtoDUNE or LArIAT.
