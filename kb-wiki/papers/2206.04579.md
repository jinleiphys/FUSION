# Chiral condensate from a hadron resonance gas model

> In this work we address the question of how well the chiral crossover transition can be understood in terms of a noninteracting hadron resonance gas model. Using the latest results on the variation of hadron masses as a function of the pion mass from lattice quantum chromodynamics, we study the temperature dependence of the renormalized chiral condensate in 2+1 flavor QCD. Furthermore, we suggest a better criterion to estimate of the pseudocritical temperature, which gives $T_c = 161.2 \pm 1.7$ MeV, which is much improved compared to all the earlier results within the hadron resonance gas model or chiral perturbation theory. For the curvature of the pseudocritical line we find $κ_2 = 0.0203(7)$, which is in very good agreement with continuum extrapolated lattice results.

## Key claim

The chiral crossover transition in 2+1 flavor QCD can be described by a noninteracting hadron resonance gas model, but only if the quark-mass dependence of hadron masses (the sigma terms) is known precisely; the extracted pseudocritical temperature of \(T_c = 161.2 \pm 1.7\) MeV is much improved over earlier hadron resonance gas or chiral perturbation theory results, though the model fails above about 170 MeV due to a Hagedorn-like divergence.

## Method

The authors computed the renormalized chiral condensate in a noninteracting hadron resonance gas model using the QMHRG hadron list (Particle Data Group 2016 states plus additional resonances from quark models). They evaluated the temperature dependence of the condensate by taking the derivative of the HRG pressure with respect to the light quark mass, using lattice QCD results for the pion-mass dependence of hadron masses (sigma terms) for ground-state pseudoscalars, vectors, and baryon octet and decuplet states, and assigning sigma terms from the nearest ground state to excited resonances. The pseudocritical temperature was extracted from the inflection point of the renormalized chiral condensate as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

* Pseudocritical temperature: \(T_c = 161.2 \pm 1.7\) MeV, extracted from the renormalized chiral condensate in the HRG model.
* Curvature of the pseudocritical line: \(\kappa_2 = 0.0203(7)\), in agreement with continuum extrapolated lattice results.
* Low-energy constant: \(\Sigma^{1/3} = 272(5)\) MeV (from FLAG 2022 review for 2+1 flavor QCD), used to set the normalization constant \(d = 0.022791\).
* Pion mass derivative at the physical point: \(m_l \partial M_\pi / \partial m_l = M_\pi / 2\) to a very good approximation, with a relative error of about 6% from the uncertainty in the low-energy constant \(B\).
* Kaon mass derivative relative error: approximately 23%, dominated by the 18% uncertainty from \(\Sigma_0^{1/3}\) and \(F_0\) in SU(3) chiral perturbation theory.

## Context

This work extends earlier HRG model studies of the chiral condensate (Toublan 2004, Tawfik 2005, Borsanyi 2010, Jankowski 2012, Andersen 2022) and chiral perturbation theory calculations (Gerber 1989, GarciaMartin 2006) by systematically quantifying uncertainties from the poorly known quark-mass dependence of hadron masses. The extracted pseudocritical temperature is consistent with the continuum extrapolated lattice QCD result of \(T_c = 156.5 \pm 1.5\) MeV from HotQCD (2018) but is obtained with considerably less computational effort. The results enable extension of the HRG framework to finite baryon density regions of the QCD phase diagram where lattice QCD is limited by the sign problem, as reviewed by Schmidt (2017).
