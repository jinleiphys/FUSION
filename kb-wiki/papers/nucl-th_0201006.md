# Structure of Sn isotopes beyond N=82

> We have performed shell-model calculations for 134,135Sn using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. Comparison shows that the calculated results for 134Sn are in very good agreement with recent experimental data. This supports confidence in our predictions of the hitherto unknown spectrum of 135Sn.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential reproduce the experimental spectrum of 134Sn very well, supporting the authors' predictions for the previously unknown spectrum of 135Sn; however, the effective neutron charge needed to reproduce the measured B(E2;6+ -> 4+) transition rate is 0.70 +/- 0.06 e, which is significantly smaller than the value of 1.01e determined by Zhang (1997).

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for 134Sn and 135Sn using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn free nucleon-nucleon potential within a G-matrix folded-diagram formalism, including renormalizations from core polarization and folded diagrams through third order. They considered 132Sn as a closed core with valence neutrons occupying the six single-particle levels of the 82-126 shell (h9/2, f7/2, f5/2, p3/2, p1/2, and i13/2), with single-particle energies fixed from experimental data on 133Sn and neighboring nuclei. The effective neutron charge was determined by fitting the experimental B(E2;6+ -> 4+) value in 134Sn.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy of 134Sn relative to 132Sn: calculated 5.986 +/- 0.064 MeV, compared to experimental 6.365 +/- 0.104 MeV from Fogelberg (1999)
- Effective neutron charge needed to reproduce the experimental B(E2;6+ -> 4+) in 134Sn: 0.70 +/- 0.06 e, compared to 1.01e from Zhang (1997) and 0.62e from Sarkar (2001)
- Measured B(E2;6+ -> 4+) in 134Sn: 0.88 +/- 0.17 W.u. from Zhang (1997)
- Calculated B(E2) values in 134Sn: 2+ -> 0+ = 1.72 W.u., 4+ -> 2+ = 1.71 W.u., 6+ -> 4+ = 0.88 W.u., 8+ -> 6+ = 0.12 W.u.
- Predicted ground-state binding energy of 135Sn: 8.396 +/- 0.078 MeV, compared to 8.393 +/- 0.401 MeV from systematic trend by Audi (1995)
- Calculated excitation energies for the lowest states in 135Sn: 5/2- at 0.226 MeV, 3/2- at 0.356 MeV, 11/2- at 0.611 MeV, 9/2- at 0.706 MeV, 15/2- at 0.911 MeV
- Single-particle energies (relative to f7/2 at -2.455 MeV): p3/2 = 0.854 MeV, h9/2 = 1.561 MeV, p1/2 = 1.656 MeV, f5/2 = 2.055 MeV, i13/2 = 2.694 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of nuclei around 132Sn by Covello (2001) using realistic effective interactions, and provides the first theoretical predictions for the spectrum of 135Sn with three neutrons beyond the N=82 shell closure. The calculations contradict the interpretation of Zhang (1997) regarding the purity of the (f7/2)^2 configuration in 134Sn, and resolve discrepancies between the results of Korgul (2000) and Sarkar (2001) by using a different set of single-particle energies and a realistic interaction derived from the CD-Bonn potential. The predictions for 135Sn enable future experimental studies of neutron-rich Sn isotopes beyond N=82, which lie well away from the valley of stability.
