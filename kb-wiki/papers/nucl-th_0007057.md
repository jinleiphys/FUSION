# The three- and four-nucleon systems from chiral effective field theory

> Recently developed chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces at next-to-leading order (NLO) that describe NN phase shifts up to about 100 MeV fairly well have been applied to 3N and 4N systems. Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations have been solved rigorously. The chiral NLO forces depend on a momentum cut-off Λlying between 540-600 MeV/c. The resulting 3N and 4N binding energies are in the same range as found using standard NN potentials. In additon, low-energy 3N scattering observables are very well reproduced like for standard NN forces. Surprisingly, the long standing A_y-puzzle is resolved at NLO. The cut-off dependence of the scattering observables is rather mild.

## Key claim

The chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces at next-to-leading order (NLO), which are parameter-free for three- and four-nucleon systems, produce binding energies and low-energy scattering observables comparable to standard realistic NN potentials; surprisingly, they partially resolve the long-standing \(A_y\)-puzzle by predicting significantly higher maxima of the nucleon analyzing power than standard forces, though the authors caution that a final answer requires going to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and including three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors constructed chiral NN potentials at NLO using the method of unitary transformations to obtain energy-independent, hermitian nuclear forces, with a smooth momentum regulator \(\Lambda\) varied between 540 and 600 MeV/c. They solved the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations rigorously for the three-nucleon (3N) and four-nucleon (4N) bound states and scattering observables, using the chiral NN potentials fitted to NN phase shifts up to \(E_{\rm lab}=100\) MeV. The calculations were performed for the isospin-symmetric case, omitting charge-independence breaking effects.

## Key numbers

- Triton (³H) binding energies for the chiral NLO potential range from \(-8.284\) MeV (at \(\Lambda=540\) MeV/c) to \(-7.546\) MeV (at \(\Lambda=600\) MeV/c), compared to the experimental value of \(-8.48\) MeV and the CD-Bonn prediction of \(-8.012\) MeV.
- ⁴He binding energies range from \(-28.03\) MeV (at \(\Lambda=540\) MeV/c) to \(-23.96\) MeV (at \(\Lambda=600\) MeV/c), compared to the Coulomb-corrected experimental value of \(-29.00\) MeV and the CD-Bonn prediction of \(-27.05\) MeV.
- The \(D\)-state probability in ⁴He ranges from 6.00% (at \(\Lambda=540\) MeV/c) to 7.30% (at \(\Lambda=600\) MeV/c), compared to 10.72% for CD-Bonn.
- NN phase shifts at 10 MeV laboratory energy: \(^1S_0\) phase shift = 60.28° (chiral NLO), 59.97° (Nijmegen PSA), 59.96° (CD-Bonn); \(^3S_1\) phase shift = 102.87° (chiral NLO), 102.60° (Nijmegen PSA), 102.61° (CD-Bonn).

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg (1990) approach of applying chiral effective field theory to nuclear forces beyond the two-nucleon system, following the construction of NN potentials by Epelbaum et al. (1998, 2000) and earlier work by van Kolck (1994) and Ordonez et al. (1996). It provides the first rigorous application of chiral NN forces to 3N and 4N systems using Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, demonstrating that these forces can describe low-energy few-nucleon observables comparably to standard realistic potentials like CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 1996). The result challenges the suggestion by Huber et al. (1998) that NN forces alone cannot simultaneously describe 2N and 3N analyzing powers, and it enables future work at NNLO where three-nucleon forces appear consistently and where the coupling of external probes (e.g., photons) is well-defined.
