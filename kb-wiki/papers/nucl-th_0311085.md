# Chiral symmetry, strangeness and nuclear matter

> In this talk we review the important role played by chiral SU(3) symmetry in hadron physics. Exciting new results on the formation of baryon resonances as implied by chiral coupled-channel dynamics are presented and discussed. The results are the consequence of progress made in formulating a consistent effective field theory for the meson-baryon scattering processes in the resonance region. Strangeness channels are found to play a decisive role in the formation of resonances. As a further application of chiral coupled-channel dynamics the properties of antikaons and hyperon resonances in cold nuclear matter are reviewed.

## Key claim

Chiral SU(3) coupled-channel dynamics, implemented via the χ-BS(3) approach with natural subtraction scales fixed by baryon masses, predicts the existence of s- and d-wave baryon resonances without adjustable parameters at leading order, provided the SU(3) symmetry breaking pattern and the coupling to meson baryon-decuplet channels are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for meson-baryon scattering based on the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian, solving the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with a systematic on-shell reduction of the interaction kernel. The renormalization condition fixes subtraction points at subthreshold energies equal to the relevant baryon masses (e.g., m_N, m_Λ, m_Σ, m_Ξ), ensuring that subthreshold amplitudes match smoothly and crossing symmetry is approximated. The effective interaction kernel is decomposed into covariant projectors with definite total angular momentum J and parity P, and the leading-order calculation uses only the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction with f = 90 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Λ_χ ≃ 4π f ≃ 1.13 GeV, with f ≃ 90 MeV determined by the pion decay process.
- In the 'heavy' SU(3) limit (m_π = m_K = m_η = 500 MeV), the J^P = 3/2^- sector yields 72 bound states forming octet and decuplet representations; no 27-plet bound state is found unless the attraction is artificially increased by about 40%.
- The leading-order calculation predicts the four-star hyperon resonances Ξ(1820), Λ(1520), and Σ(1670) with masses close to empirical values; the nucleon resonance N(1520) and isobar Δ(1700) are generated with less accuracy.
- A bound state in the (I,S) = (0,-3) channel is predicted at mass 1950 MeV, below all thresholds, associated with a decuplet resonance not yet observed.
- The antikaon spectral function at nuclear saturation density ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3} shows a broad distribution at zero momentum, with support significantly below the free-space kaon mass; at momentum q = 400 MeV, the spectral function peaks near ω = sqrt(m_K^2 + q^2) with a pronounced low-energy tail.

## Context

This work extends the χ-BS(3) approach developed by Lutz and collaborators (2000-2002) to include both s- and d-wave resonances, building on earlier coupled-channel models by Wyld (1960s), Dalitz (1960s), and Ball and Rajasekaran (1960s) that used the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction. The results contrast with approaches that treat subtraction scales as free parameters (Oset and collaborators, 2001-2003), which the authors argue introduce an excessive number of parameters inconsistent with chiral power counting. The predictions for baryon resonances should be testable with unquenched QCD lattice simulations, and the in-medium results for antikaon and hyperon propagation enable more realistic calculations of kaon production in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star properties.
