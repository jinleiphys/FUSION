# Statistical Multifragmentation in Thermodynamical Limit: An Exact Solution for Phase Transitions

> An exact analytical solution of the statistical multifragmentation model is found in thermodynamic limit. Excluded volume effects are taken into account in the thermodynamically self-consistent way. The model exhibits a 1-st order phase transition of the liquid-gas type. An extension of the model including the Fisher's term is also studied. The possibility of the second order phase transition at or above the critical point is discussed. The mixed phase region of the phase diagram, where the gas of nuclear fragments coexists with the infinite liquid condensate, is unambiguously identified. The peculiar thermodynamic properties of the model near the boundary between the mixed phase and the pure gaseous phase are studied. The results for the caloric curve and specific heat are presented and a physical picture of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition is clarified.

## Key claim
The statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) is solved exactly in the thermodynamic limit, rigorously proving a first-order liquid-gas phase transition; however, the sharp peak and finite discontinuity in the specific heat at constant density arise from the rapid temperature dependence of the liquid volume fraction near the mixed-phase boundary, not from an infinite peak of zero width as previously claimed by Gu et al. (1998, 1999).

## Method
The authors derive an exact analytical solution of the SMM within the grand canonical ensemble by introducing the isobaric partition function (IPF), which reduces the problem to locating singularities of the IPF in the complex volume plane. The pressure is given by the farthest-right singularity of the IPF: a simple pole corresponds to the gaseous phase, while a singularity of the function F(s,T,mu) itself defines the liquid phase (infinite condensate). The model includes excluded-volume effects in a thermodynamically self-consistent Van der Waals approximation, with the fragment free energy taken from the liquid-drop model including a Fisher term with exponent tau.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density: rho_o = 0.16 fm^{-3} (used as the liquid density rho_l = 1/b).
- Bulk binding energy per nucleon: W_o = 16 MeV.
- Fermi-gas parameter: epsilon_o = 16 MeV.
- Surface tension parameterization: sigma_o = 18 MeV, critical temperature T_c = 18 MeV (sigma = 0 at T >= T_c).
- For tau = 0, the first-order phase transition occurs only for T < T_c = 18 MeV.
- Negative specific heat appears in the vicinity of T = 18 MeV at fixed density rho/rho_o = 1/3.
- Caloric curves show leveling of temperature at energies per nucleon between 10 and 20 MeV.
- For tau > 5/2, the liquid-gas phase transition extends to all temperatures; for 5/2 < tau < 7/2, it becomes second-order above T_c, with a tricritical point at T = T_c, rho = 1/b.

## Context
This work provides the first exact analytical solution of the statistical multifragmentation model in the thermodynamic limit, extending prior numerical studies by Ch. (1995) and Gu et al. (1998, 1999, 2000) that were limited to finite systems in the canonical and microcanonical ensembles. The authors correct earlier misinterpretations about the "boiling point" and the nature of the specific heat peak at constant density. The solution also generalizes Fisher's droplet model (Fisher, 1967) by incorporating excluded-volume effects, and it opens the way to studying the sensitivity of the nuclear liquid-gas phase diagram to the Fisher exponent tau, including the possibility of a tricritical point for 5/2 < tau < 7/2.
