# Efficient vacuum state preparation for quantum simulation of strongly interacting local quantum field theories

> We present an efficient approach for preparing ground states in the context of strongly interacting local quantum field theories on quantum computers. The approach produces the vacuum state in a time proportional to the square-root of the volume, which is a square-root improvement in speed compared to traditional approaches. The approach exploits a novel method for traversing the path in parameter space in which the resources scale linearly with a path length suitably defined in parameter space. Errors due to practical limitations are controlled and do not exhibit secular growth along the path. The final accuracy can be arbitrarily improved with an additive cost, which is independent of the volume and grows slower than logarithmically with the overlap between the state produced and the exact ground state. We expect that the method could potentially hold practical value not only within the realm of quantum field theories but also in addressing other challenges involving long path lengths.

## Key claim

The paper presents a quantum algorithm for preparing vacuum states of strongly interacting local quantum field theories that achieves a computational cost scaling as the square root of the volume, a square-root improvement over traditional adiabatic state preparation and quantum Zeno effect methods, provided that no phase transitions are encountered along the parameter path and the theory remains local for all parameter values.

## Method

The algorithm traverses a path in Hamiltonian parameter space using discrete points where adjacent ground states have an overlap squared of 1/2, corresponding to a path length of sqrt(log(2)) between points. At each step, the algorithm uses an oracle that projects onto the ground state of the next Hamiltonian and, if the system is found to be in an excited state, applies a reflection around the previous ground state to return the system to the target ground state. A modified version of the algorithm repeats the projection step after the reflection to suppress secular error growth, and an "afterburner" stage using exponentially more precise projection achieves arbitrarily high final accuracy.

## Key numbers

- The algorithm produces the vacuum state in a time proportional to the square-root of the volume, V^(1/2), compared to traditional approaches that scale linearly with volume.
- The path length L scales as (V/xi^d)^(1/2), where xi is the correlation length and d is the spatial dimension.
- Adjacent points on the path are chosen so that |<g(lambda_{i+1})|g(lambda_i)>|^2 = 1/2, giving L_{lambda_i,lambda_{i+1}} = sqrt(log(2)) for large volumes.
- In the idealized scenario, a path of length L is traversed by calling the oracle 3L/sqrt(4 log(2)) times when L >> 1.
- The total cost for long paths is C ~ V^(1/2) * <1/Delta>, where <1/Delta> is the average inverse spectral gap along the path.
- Numerical simulation with a toy model of 1024 levels showed ground state probabilities P_g greater than 0.947 for all steps, with no secular growth over 10000 steps.

## Context

This work extends the line of adiabatic state preparation (ASP) and quantum Zeno effect (QZE) methods for preparing ground states of lattice field theories, as previously studied by Boixo et al. (2010) and others. The key advance is that the algorithm avoids the L log L scaling of earlier error-mitigation schemes by demonstrating that errors do not accumulate secularly along the path, enabling the square-root volume scaling. The approach is designed for local quantum field theories with finite correlation lengths and gapped spectra, and could potentially be applied to other problems with long path lengths in parameter space, though practical implementation requires fault-tolerant quantum computers.
