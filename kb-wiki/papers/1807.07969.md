# Deep Inelastic Scattering on an Extremal RN-AdS Black Hole

> We consider deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on a large nucleus described as an extremal RN-AdS black hole using the holographic principle. Using the R-current correlators we determine the structure functions as a function Bjorken-x, and map it on a finite but large nucleus with fixed atomic number. The R-ratio of the nuclear structure functions exhibit strong shadowing at low-x.

## Key claim

Deep inelastic scattering on a large nucleus can be modeled holographically as DIS on an extremal RN-AdS black hole, with the nuclear R-ratio exhibiting strong shadowing at low Bjorken-x, but only in the leading-order coherent scattering regime where the structure functions are dominated by the bulk black hole rather than by subleading fermionic contributions.

## Method

The authors use the holographic principle to map a large nucleus at finite atomic number to an extremal RN-AdS black hole in the conformal limit, with the black hole's U(1) charge identified with the atomic number. They compute the retarded Green's function for R-current correlators by solving Maxwell equations in the extremal RN-AdS geometry with absorptive boundary conditions at the horizon, then extract the nuclear structure functions F_T and F_L from the imaginary part of the on-shell action in the low-x regime where k >> K.

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum for the nucleus is Q_AS = (sqrt(3)/2) * (mu / x_A) * A^(1/2), growing with atomic number as A^(1/2) rather than the weak-coupling A^(1/3)
- The nuclear structure functions at low-x scale as F^A_T ~ (A/x) * (3x^2 Q^2 / 4m_N^2)^(2/3) and F^A_L ~ (3A/4x) * (3x^2 Q^2 / 4m_N^2)
- The R-ratio for A=42 is parametrized as 0.52 * x^(1/3 + 0.08) + 2.85 / A^(1/3), with the Pomeron intercept Delta_P = 0.08
- The energy per particle in the conformal limit is E_A/A = (3/4) mu, compared to (3/5) mu for free massive fermions

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to DIS on a thermal black hole by Hatta (2008) to the extremal charged case, enabling the mapping to finite nuclei with fixed atomic number. The results contrast with the weak-coupling QCD expectation of Q_AS ~ A^(1/3) and with the intermediate-x structure functions on a spin-1/2 target that obey F_2 = 2F_1 as in the work of Polchinski and Strassler (2003). The holographic R-ratio shows strong shadowing at low-x, consistent with experimental data from the E665, NMC, SLAC, and BCDMS collaborations, and the approach enables future study of Fermi motion effects at large-x through subleading fermionic contributions.
