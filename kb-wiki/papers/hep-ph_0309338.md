# Lattice-QCD-based Schwinger-Dyson Approach for Chiral Symmetry Restoration at Finite Temperature

> We propose the Schwinger-Dyson (SD) formalism based on lattice QCD, i.e., LQCD-based SD formalism, for the study of dynamical chiral-symmetry breaking in QCD. We extract the kernel function K(p^2) in the SD equation from the lattice data of the quark propagator in the Landau gauge. As remarkable features, we find infrared vanishing and intermediate enhancement of the kernel function K(p^2) in the SD equation. We apply the LQCD-based SD equation to thermal QCD, and calculate the quark mass function at the finite temperature. We find chiral symmetry restoration at the critical temperature T_c \sim 100MeV.

# Lattice-QCD-based Schwinger-Dyson Approach for Chiral Symmetry Restoration at Finite Temperature

## Key claim

The authors propose a Schwinger-Dyson formalism where the kernel function is extracted from lattice QCD data of the quark propagator, rather than using perturbative approximations, and find chiral symmetry restoration at a critical temperature of approximately 100 MeV, a value lower than typical estimates from other approaches, and one that depends on the specific lattice-derived kernel.

## Method

The authors extract the kernel function K(p²) ≡ g² Γ(p²) d(p²) from lattice QCD data for the quark mass function M(p²) in the Landau gauge, using the quenched lattice result M(p²) = M₀ / {1 + (p/̄p)^γ} with M₀ = 260 MeV, ̄p = 870 MeV, and γ = 3.04. They then insert this kernel into the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark propagator at finite temperature, solving for the thermal quark mass Mₙ(p²) of the Matsubara frequencies ωₙ = (2n+1)πT to determine the temperature dependence of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass function parameters from lattice QCD: M₀ = 260 MeV, ̄p = 870 MeV, γ = 3.04
- Pion decay constant calculated from the lattice quark mass function using the Pagels-Stokar formula: fπ ≈ 87 MeV
- Quark condensate obtained from the lattice data: ⟨̄qq⟩_{Λ=1 GeV} ≈ −(220 MeV)³
- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: T_c ∼ 100 MeV
- The kernel function K(p²) exhibits infrared vanishing (K(p² ∼ 0) ≈ 0) and intermediate enhancement around p ∼ 0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger-Dyson approach beyond the standard Higashijima-Miransky approximation (Higashijima 1984, Miransky 1993), which uses perturbative gluon propagators and one-loop running couplings, by incorporating nonperturbative lattice QCD input. The extracted kernel function differs significantly from the perturbative running coupling both in the infrared and intermediate-energy regions. The approach enables quantitative study of chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature using a formalism that retains proper nonperturbative QCD effects, and the resulting critical temperature of ∼100 MeV can be compared with lattice QCD simulations and other model calculations of the chiral phase transition.
