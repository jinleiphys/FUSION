# Closed analytical solutions of the d-dimensional Schrödinger equation with deformed Woods-Saxon potential plus double ring-shaped potential

> By employing the Pekeris approximation, the D-dimensional Schrödinger equation is solved for the nuclear deformed Woods-Saxon potential plus double ring-shaped potential within the framework of the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM). The energy eingenvalues are given in a closed form and the corresponding normalized eigenfunctions are obtained in terms of hypergeometric functions. Our general results reproduce many predictions obtained in the literature, using the Nikiforov-Uvarov method (NU) and the Improved Quantization Rule approach, particulary those derived by considering Woods-Saxon potential without deformation and/or without ring shape interaction.

# Closed analytical solutions of the d-dimensional Schrödinger equation with deformed Woods-Saxon potential plus double ring-shaped potential

## Key claim

The paper claims to obtain closed-form energy eigenvalues and normalized eigenfunctions for the D-dimensional Schrödinger equation with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential plus a double ring-shaped potential, but only under the Pekeris approximation to handle the centrifugal term and the 1/r term, and with the condition that the nuclear surface diffuseness parameter a is much smaller than the nuclear radius R₀ (R₀ >> a).

## Method

The authors separate the D-dimensional Schrödinger equation into radial and angular parts using hyperspherical coordinates. For the radial equation, they apply the Pekeris approximation to expand the centrifugal term τ/r² and the K/r term in powers of x = (r-R₀)/R₀, then re-express these as functions of the deformed Woods-Saxon denominator (q₁ + q₂e^{αx}). The resulting differential equation is solved using the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM) after a change of variable z = 1/(q₁ + q₂e^{αx}). The angular equations for the D-dimensional hyperspherical harmonics are solved similarly with AIM, yielding eigenvalues expressed in terms of the quantum numbers ℓ_j and the ring-shaped potential parameters b and c.

## Key numbers

- The radial energy eigenvalues are given by equation (E45) and (E47), which depend on the spatial dimension D, radial quantum number n, angular momentum ℓ, potential depth V₀, deformation parameters q₁ and q₂, diffuseness a, nuclear radius R₀, and ring-shaped potential parameters b and c.
- The angular quantum number ℓ for the highest angular coordinate is given by ℓ_{n_{D-1}} = (4-D + √(1+4c) + √((D-3)² + 4(b+Λ_{D-1})))/2 + 2n_{D-1}, where n_{D-1} = 0,1,2,... and b ≠ 0, c > 0.
- For the special case c = 0, b ≥ 0, the angular quantum number becomes ℓ_{n_{D-1}} = (3-D)/2 + √((D-3)² + 4(b+Λ_{D-2}))/2 + n_{D-1}.
- The radial wave function normalization constant is A_{nℓ} = [q₁^{2μ} / (a · Q_n^{(μ,σ)})]^{1/2}, where Q_n^{(μ,σ)} involves double sums of Gamma functions and Pochhammer symbols.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Woods-Saxon potential in nuclear physics by incorporating both deformation (via parameters q₁, q₂) and a double ring-shaped angular potential, generalizing results to arbitrary spatial dimension D. The authors show that setting q₁ = q₂ = 1 and K = 0 reproduces the standard Woods-Saxon potential results of earlier work using the Nikiforov-Uvarov method (NU method) for D = 3. Setting q₁ = q₂ = 1 and K ≠ 0 recovers the modified Woods-Saxon potential results from prior literature. Setting K = 0, q₁ = q, q₂ = 1 reproduces the deformed Woods-Saxon spectrum obtained via the Improved Quantization Rule approach. The angular solutions for D = 3 match results reported by Chang-Yuan et al. (functional analysis method) and by Carpio-Bernido and Bernido (path integral method). The results may find applications in quantum chemistry for ring-shaped molecules and in nuclear physics for studying deformed nuclei interactions.
