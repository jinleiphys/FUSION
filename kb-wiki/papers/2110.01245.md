# Exposing the effect of $p$-wave in pion triplet under the strong magnetic field

> The static properties, masses and decay constants, of pseudoscalar meson triplet in a strongly magnetized medium are studied through the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach treatment of a contact interaction. Complementary to the usual vector-vector form, a symmetry-preserving formulation of couplings has been proposed in this work, without modifying the quark propagator, to control the strength of the $p$-wave component of Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. It is found that, with the help of flexible auxiliary interaction, our simple model is able to reproduce the observation in the lattice QCD simulation, where the spectra of the charged pseudo-scalar meson shows a non-monotonic behavior as the magnetic field grows. The discovery of this work implies the strong magnetic field affects the inner structure of mesons dramatically.

## Key claim

The authors propose a symmetry-preserving auxiliary interaction term in the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to control the strength of the \(p\)-wave component of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, enabling their simple contact-interaction model to reproduce the non-monotonic mass behavior of charged pions as a function of magnetic field observed in lattice QCD, which previous low-energy effective theories could not capture.

## Method

The authors employ a contact-interaction Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) and Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) framework in a uniform magnetic field, using Landau gauge and proper-time regularization. They introduce an auxiliary term in the quark-gluon vertex that does not modify the quark propagator but alters the meson-sector kernels, with a magnetic-field-dependent parameter \(\tilde{\eta}^2\) tuned to fit lattice QCD results for charged pion masses from Ding (2020). The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for pseudoscalar mesons includes both the pseudoscalar (\(E\)) and axial-vector (\(F\)) components, where the \(F\) term controls the \(p\)-wave contribution.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum dynamical quark mass: \(M_q = 0.37\ \mathrm{GeV}\) from the contact interaction model with parameters \(m_G = 0.132\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(\tau_{uv} = 1/0.905\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(\tau_{ir} = 1/0.24\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), and current quark mass \(m_0 = 0.0089\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* Vacuum pion mass and decay constant with both \(E\) and \(F\) terms: \(m_\pi(eB=0) = 0.157\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(f_\pi(eB=0) = 0.102\ \mathrm{GeV}\); with only the \(E\) term: \(m_\pi(eB=0) = 0.135\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(f_\pi(eB=0) = 0.119\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* In the conventional contact model (without auxiliary term), the charged pion bound state disappears for \(eB > 0.8\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) when both \(E\) and \(F\) terms are included.
* The auxiliary term parameters are \(a = 3.5\) and \(b = 0.153\) in \(\tilde{\eta}^2 = \tilde{\eta}_0^2 - a \bar{P}^2 \tau_{uv}^2 \tanh(b\, eB^2 \tau_{uv}^4)\), with \(\tilde{\eta}_0^2 = 1/4\) ensuring the \(F\) term vanishes at zero magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends DSE studies of magnetized mesons (e.g., Wang 2014) and addresses the discrepancy between two lattice QCD results: Ding (2020) found a non-monotonic charged pion mass as a function of \(eB\), while Bali (2018) observed monotonic saturation. The proposed auxiliary interaction enables the contact model to reproduce the non-monotonic behavior that low-energy effective theories such as chiral perturbation theory (Andersen 2012, Colucci 2013) and NJL models (Coppola 2019) could not capture. This enables future studies of the internal \(p\)-wave structure of mesons in strong magnetic fields relevant to magnetars and heavy-ion collisions.
