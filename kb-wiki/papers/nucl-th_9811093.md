# Quadrupole Deformation of Barium Isotopes

> The B(E2:0_1^+ -> 2_1^+) values of the Ba isotopes (Z=56) exhibit a sharp increase in deformation as the neutron numbers approach the mid-shell value of N=66. This behavior is anomalous because the 2_1^+ level energies are very similar to those of the neighboring isotopes. By means of the axially-symmetric deformed Woods-Saxon (WS) hamiltonian plus the BCS method, we investigated the systematics of B(E2) of the Ba isotopes. We showed that 15% of the B(E2) values at N=66 was due to the level crossing, occurring at the deformation with beta being nearly 0.3, between the proton orbits originating from the orbits Omega=1/2^-(h11/2) and 9/2^+(g9/2) at zero deformation. The latter of these two was an intruder orbit originating from below the energy gap at Z=50, rising higher in energy with the deformation and intruding the Z=50-82 shell. These two orbits have the largest magnitude of the quadrupole moment with a different sign among the orbits near and below the Fermi surface. Occupancy and non-occupancy of these orbits by protons thus affect B(E2:0_1^+ -> 2_1^+) significantly.

## Key claim

The sharp increase in B(E2:0_1^+ -> 2_1^+) for barium isotopes near the mid-shell neutron number N=66 is anomalous because the 2_1^+ level energies remain similar to those of neighboring isotopes; the authors show that 15% of the B(E2) value at N=66 arises from a level crossing between proton orbits originating from the Omega=1/2^-(h11/2) and 9/2^+(g9/2) spherical states, occurring at a deformation beta_WS of approximately 0.3.

## Method

The authors used an axially-symmetric deformed Woods-Saxon (WS) Hamiltonian plus the BCS method to calculate the intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0 for protons as a function of the deformation parameter beta_WS. They determined beta_WS for each barium isotope by matching the calculated Q0 to experimental values, rather than minimizing a total Hamiltonian. The pairing gap was fixed using the empirical relation Delta=12/sqrt(A) for 122Ba at beta_WS=0.3, yielding a coupling constant G=0.16 MeV, and all WS eigenstates below the barrier height were included in the BCS summations.

## Key numbers

- The jump in intrinsic quadrupole moment at the level crossing is Delta Q0 = 2(q(nu2) - q(nu1)) = 1.12 b, with q(nu1) = -0.16 b and q(nu2) = 0.40 b.
- This Delta Q0 amounts to 20% of the experimental Q0 = 5.2 +/- 0.2 b for 122Ba.
- Roughly 15% of B(E2:0_1^+ -> 2_1^+) for barium with N=66 is due to the pairwise proton transfer from Omega=9/2 (g9/2) to Omega=1/2 (h11/2).
- The pairing gap values for 122Ba are Delta = 1.34, 1.25, 0.99, 1.09, 1.06 MeV at beta_WS = 0.0, 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4 respectively.
- The Grodzins rule underestimates B(E2) by 20% at N=66 for barium.
- For xenon with N=66, the contribution of the level crossing to B(E2) is roughly 20% (Chepurnov set) or 10% (universal set); for cerium with N=66, the effect is smaller than 5%.

## Context

This work extends the systematics of B(E2) values for barium isotopes measured by Uchiyama and compiled by Raman (1995), and addresses the failure of single-shell models like the Nilsson model to reproduce the sharp B(E2) increase near N=66, as noted by Raman (1995). The authors compare their results with the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) calculations of Otsuka, Pan, and Arima (Pauli), showing that IBM-2 underestimates B(E2) at N=66 because the OAI mapping used in deriving the IBM-2 E2 operator does not account for the intruder orbit from below the Z=50 gap. The findings enable future investigation of the mechanism that cancels the Pauli factor effect in well-deformed nuclei.
