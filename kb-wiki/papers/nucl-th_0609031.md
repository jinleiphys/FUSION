# A realistic model of superfluidity in the neutron star inner crust

> A semi-microscopic self-consistent quantum approach developed recently to describe the inner crust structure of neutron stars within the Wigner-Seitz (WS) method with the explicit inclusion of neutron and proton pairing correlations is further developed. In this approach, the generalized energy functional is used which contains the anomalous term describing the pairing. It is constructed by matching the realistic phenomenological functional by Fayans et al. for describing the nuclear-type cluster in the center of the WS cell with the one calculated microscopically for neutron matter. Previously the anomalous part of the latter was calculated within the BCS approximation. In this work corrections to the BCS theory which are known from the many-body theory of pairing in neutron matter are included into the energy functional in an approximate way. These modifications have a sizable influence on the equilibrium configuration of the inner crust, i.e. on the proton charge Z and the radius R_c of the WS cell. The effects are quite significant in the region where the neutron pairing gap is larger.

# A realistic model of superfluidity in the neutron star inner crust

## Key claim
The authors introduce approximate many-body corrections to the BCS approximation for neutron pairing in the inner crust, using a constant suppression factor of 1/2 (P2 model) or 1/3 (P3 model) applied to the BCS gap, and find that these modifications significantly change the equilibrium proton charge Z and Wigner-Seitz cell radius R_c, especially in the density region where the neutron pairing gap is largest.

## Method
The authors use a semi-microscopic self-consistent approach within the Wigner-Seitz (WS) method, matching a phenomenological nuclear energy functional by Fayans et al. for the central nuclear cluster with a microscopic functional for neutron matter calculated with the Argonne v18 potential. The anomalous (pairing) part of the energy functional is modified by introducing a constant suppression factor f_m-b applied to the BCS gap value at the Fermi surface, which then enters the effective pairing interaction via an averaging procedure. Calculations are performed for two types of WS boundary conditions (BC1 and BC2) and compared with the earlier BCS (P1) results.

## Key numbers
- The suppression factors used are f_m-b = 1/2 (P2 model) and f_m-b = 1/3 (P3 model), compared to f_m-b = 1 (P1/BCS model).
- For k_F = 0.7 fm^{-1} in the P2 model (BC1), the equilibrium Z = 46 with R_c = 30.31 fm and binding energy E_B = 2.9320 MeV; in the P1 model at the same k_F, Z = 52 with R_c = 32.02 fm and E_B = 2.7908 MeV (BC1).
- The Fermi average gap Δ_F for k_F = 0.8 fm^{-1} in the P2 model (BC1, Z=40) is 1.256 MeV, compared to 2.555 MeV in the P1 model (BC1, Z=42).
- For k_F = 1.0 fm^{-1} in the P2 model (BC1, Z=20), Δ_F drops to 0.037 MeV, while in the BC2 case (Z=24) Δ_F = 1.426 MeV.
- The infinite neutron matter gap Δ^0_inf for the P2 model ranges from 1.46 MeV at k_F = 0.8 fm^{-1} to 0.83 MeV at k_F = 1.2 fm^{-1}.

## Context
This work extends the semi-microscopic approach developed by the same authors in earlier papers (crust3, crust4) for describing the neutron star inner crust with pairing correlations, which itself generalized the classical Wigner-Seitz method of Negele and Vautherin (1973) that did not include pairing. The paper addresses the known issue that BCS theory overestimates the neutron pairing gap in neutron matter, with many-body corrections typically suppressing the gap by a factor between 1/2 and 1/3, as discussed in the conventional view summarized by crust2. The results enable more realistic predictions for the inner crust composition and superfluid properties, though the authors note that at high densities (k_F ≥ 1.1 fm^{-1}) the spherical WS method may break down due to the expected transition to non-spherical "spaghetti" phases.
