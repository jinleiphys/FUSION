# Effects of medium modifications of nucleon form factors on neutrino scattering in dense matter

> Effects of the in-medium modifications of nucleon form factors on neutrino interaction in dense matter are presented by considering both the weak and electromagnetic interactions of neutrinos with the constituents of the matter. A relativistic mean field and the quark-meson coupling models are respectively adopted for the effective nucleon mass and in-medium nucleon form factors. We calculate the cross-section of neutrino scattering as well as the neutrino mean free path. We found the cross sections of neutrino scattering in cold nuclear medium decreases when the in-medium modifications of the nucleon weak and electromagnetic form factors are taken into account.This reduction results in the enhancement of the neutrino mean free path, in particular at the baryon density of around a few times of the normal nuclear matter density.

## Key claim

The in-medium modifications of nucleon weak and electromagnetic form factors cause a reduction in neutrino scattering cross sections in cold nuclear matter, which leads to an enhancement of the neutrino mean free path; this effect is particularly pronounced at baryon densities around a few times normal nuclear matter density, and the quenched axial-vector coupling constant gives the dominant contribution to the cross section reduction.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean field model to describe the effective nucleon mass in dense matter and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to estimate the in-medium modifications of nucleon weak and electromagnetic form factors. They calculated the differential cross section per volume for neutrino scattering with constituents of matter (neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons) using an effective Lagrangian, then integrated over energy and momentum transfer to obtain the neutrino mean free path at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(\rho_0 = 0.15~\text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Nucleon mass used: \(M_N = 939~\text{MeV}\)
- Meson masses used: \(m_\rho = 770~\text{MeV}\), \(m_\omega = 783~\text{MeV}\), \(m_\sigma = 520~\text{MeV}\)
- Coupling constants used: \((g_\sigma / m_\sigma)^2 = 9.148~\text{fm}^2\), \((g_\omega / m_\omega)^2 = 4.820~\text{fm}^2\), \((g_\rho / m_\rho)^2 = 4.791~\text{fm}^2\), \(b = 3.478 \times 10^{-3}\), \(c = 1.328 \times 10^{-2}\)
- Differential cross sections calculated at three-momentum transfer \(|\vec{q}| = 2.5~\text{MeV}\) and initial neutrino energy \(E_\nu = 5~\text{MeV}\)
- Baryon densities studied: \(0.5\rho_0\), \(1.0\rho_0\), \(1.5\rho_0\), \(2.0\rho_0\), \(2.5\rho_0\), and \(3.0\rho_0\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Hutauruk, Oh, and Tsushima, 2018) on the impact of in-medium nucleon form factor modifications on neutrino scattering in dense matter. The calculations use a relativistic mean field approach similar to that of Serot and Walecka (1997) and the quark-meson coupling model developed by Guichon (1987) and further refined by Saito, Tsushima, and Thomas (2007). The results are relevant for simulations of neutron star evolution and core-collapse supernova dynamics, where the neutrino mean free path is a critical input.
