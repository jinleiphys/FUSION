# Measurement of the rapidity-even dipolar flow in Pb-Pb collisions with the ATLAS detector

> The rapidity-even dipolar flow v1 associated with dipole asymmetry in the initial geometry is measured over a broad range in transverse momentum 0.5 GeV<pT<9 GeV, and centrality (0-50)% in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, recorded by the ATLAS experiment at the LHC. The v1 coefficient is determined via a two-component fit of the first order Fourier coefficient, v_{1,1}= cos Δϕ, of two-particle correlations in azimuthal angle Δϕ=ϕ_a-ϕ_b as a function of pT^a and pT^b. This fit is motivated by the finding that the pT dependence of v_{1,1}(pT^a,pT^b) data are consistent with the combined contributions from a rapidity-even v1 and global momentum conservation. The magnitude of the extracted momentum conservation component suggests that the system conserving momentum involves only a subset of the event (spanning about 3 units in ηin central collisions). The extracted v1 is observed to cross zero at pT~1.0 GeV, reaches a maximum at 4-5 GeV with a value comparable to that for v3, and decreases at higher pT. Interestingly, the magnitude of v1 at high pT exceeds the value of the v3 in all centrality interval and exceeds the value of v2 in central collisions. This behavior suggests that the path-length dependence of energy loss and initial dipole asymmetry from fluctuations corroborate to produce a large dipolar anisotropy for high pT hadrons, making the v1 a valuable probe for studying the jet quenching phenomena.

## Key claim

The rapidity-even dipolar flow \(v_1\) is extracted from two-particle correlations by assuming the data are consistent with a combination of a collective \(v_1\) component and a global momentum conservation term; the extracted \(v_1\) at high transverse momentum exceeds \(v_3\) in all centrality intervals and exceeds \(v_2\) in central collisions, suggesting that path-length-dependent energy loss and initial dipole asymmetry from fluctuations together produce a large dipolar anisotropy for high-\(p_T\) hadrons.

## Method

The \(v_1\) coefficient was determined via a two-component least-squares fit of the first-order Fourier coefficient \(v_{1,1} = \langle \cos \Delta\phi \rangle\) of two-particle correlations in azimuthal angle \(\Delta\phi = \phi_a - \phi_b\), measured as a function of \(p_T^a\) and \(p_T^b\) for \(2 < |\Delta\eta| < 5\). The fit model was \(v_{1,1}(p_T^a, p_T^b) = v_1(p_T^a) v_1(p_T^b) - c \, p_T^a p_T^b\), where the second term accounts for global momentum conservation; the \(v_1(p_T)\) function was represented by smooth interpolation through 15 discrete \(p_T\) points, yielding 16 fit parameters per centrality interval.

## Key numbers

* The \(v_1\) coefficient crosses zero at \(p_T \approx 1.0\) GeV (decreasing to about 0.9 GeV in the 40-50% centrality interval).
* The \(v_1\) reaches a maximum between 4 and 5 GeV, with a value comparable to that for \(v_3\).
* The magnitude of \(v_1\) at high \(p_T\) exceeds the value of \(v_3\) in all centrality intervals and exceeds the value of \(v_2\) in central collisions.
* The fit parameter \(c\) ranges from \(0.24 \pm 0.02 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) in 0-5% centrality to \(1.16 \pm 0.09 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) in 40-50% centrality.
* The product \(c \cdot dN/d\eta|_{|\eta|=0}\) is approximately constant across centrality, ranging from \(0.39 \pm 0.04\) GeV\(^{-2}\) in 0-5% to \(0.32 \pm 0.03\) GeV\(^{-2}\) in 40-50%.
* The effective system size conserving momentum in 0-5% central collisions is estimated to be \(M \sim 5000\), spanning about 3 units in \(\eta\).
* The typical \(\chi^2/\)DOF of the fit is between one and two depending on centrality.

## Context

This work extends the measurement of harmonic flow coefficients \(v_n\) (previously measured for \(n=2-6\) by ATLAS, ALICE, and CMS) to the dipolar flow \(v_1\), which had been difficult to isolate due to contamination from global momentum conservation. The method follows the approach of Retinskaya (2012) and uses a two-component decomposition similar to that applied in the AMPT model by Jia (2012). The results enable new constraints on the shear viscosity to entropy ratio of the quark-gluon plasma via the zero-crossing point and peak value of \(v_1\), and provide a probe of jet quenching phenomena through the large dipolar anisotropy observed at high \(p_T\).
