# Unraveling Trace Anomaly of Supradense Matter via Neutron Star Compactness Scaling

> The trace anomaly $Δ\equiv 1/3-P/\varepsilon=1/3-ϕ$ quantifies the possibly broken conformal symmetry in supradense matter under pressure $P$ at energy density $\varepsilon$. Perturbative QCD (pQCD) predicts a vanishing $Δ$ at extremely high energy or baryon densities when the conformal symmetry is realized but its behavior at intermediate densities reachable in neutron stars (NSs) are still very uncertain. The extraction of $Δ$ from NS observations strongly depends on the employed model for nuclear Equation of State (EOS). Using the IPAD-TOV method based on an Intrinsic and Perturbatively Analysis of the Dimensionless (IPAD) Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations that are further verified numerically by using $10^5$ EOSs generated randomly with a meta-model in a very broad EOS parameter space constrained by terrestrial nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations, here we first show that the compactness $ξ\equiv GM_{\rm{NS}}/Rc^2\equiv M_{\rm{NS}}/R$ of a NS with mass $M_{\rm{NS}}$ and radius $R$ scales very accurately with $\overlineΠ_{\rm{c}}\equivΠ_{\rm{c}}\cdot(1+18\x/25)\equiv\x/(1+3\x^2+4\x)\cdot(1+18\rm{X}/25)$ where $\x\equivϕ_{\rm{c}}= P_{\rm{c}}/\varepsilon_{\rm{c}}$ is the ratio of pressure over energy density at NS centers. The scaling of NS compactness thus enables one to readily read off the central trace anomaly $Δ_{\rm{c}}=1/3-\x$ directly from the observational data of either the mass-radius or red-shift measurements. We then demonstrate indeed that the available NS data themselves from recent X-ray and gravitational wave observations can determine model-insensitively the trace anomaly as a function of energy density in NS cores, providing a stringent test of existing NS models and a clear guidance in a new direction for further understanding the nature and EOS of supradense matter.

## Key claim

The authors show that neutron star compactness scales with a specific function of the central pressure-to-energy-density ratio, enabling model-insensitive extraction of the central trace anomaly directly from observational data, though the scaling includes a high-order correction term of "18X/25" that improves accuracy compared to earlier formulations.

## Method

The authors derived scaling relations for neutron star mass and compactness using the IPAD-TOV method, which performs an intrinsic and perturbative analysis of the dimensionless Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. They verified these scalings numerically using 10^5 randomly generated equations of state from a meta-model constrained by terrestrial nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations, plus 284 realistic EOSs from the literature. The compactness scaling ξ = M_NS/R was then inverted to read off the central trace anomaly Δ_c = 1/3 - X from observed mass-radius or redshift data for 17 neutron star instances.

## Key numbers

- The compactness scaling ξ ≈ A_ξ Π̄_c + B_ξ gives A_ξ = 1.56_{-0.016}^{+0.016} and B_ξ = 0.02_{-0.002}^{+0.002} with an r-value of 0.975 and standard error of 0.02.
- The mass scaling M_NS/M_⊙ ≈ A_M Γ̄_c + B_M gives A_M = 865_{-8}^{+8} and B_M = 0.265_{-0.014}^{+0.014} with an r-value of 0.978 and standard error of 0.001 M_⊙.
- The upper bound on compactness from general relativity is ξ_GR = 0.276_{-0.003}^{+0.003}, corresponding to X ≲ 0.381 and Δ_GR ≈ -0.048.
- For PSR J0740+6620, the central trace anomaly Δ_c = 0.040_{-0.057}^{+0.075} and reduced central energy density y = 3.90_{-1.19}^{+0.89} using the revised scaling scheme.
- For PSR J0030+0451, Δ_c = 0.211_{-0.030}^{+0.028} and y = 2.61_{-0.84}^{+0.86} using the revised scaling scheme.
- For PSR J0437-4715, Δ_c = 0.170_{-0.016}^{+0.024} and y = 3.93_{-1.07}^{+0.71} using the revised scaling scheme.
- The radius scaling R/km ≈ 572_{-25}^{+25} ν_c + 4.22_{-0.35}^{+0.35} has an r-value of about 0.712 and standard error of about 0.3 km.

## Context

This work extends the IPAD-TOV approach previously developed by the same authors (Cai and Li, 2023-2025) and independently verified by Lattimer (2024) using several hundred neutron star EOSs. The scaling relations improve upon earlier mass and radius scalings studied by Ofeng (2020, 2024) and Sotani and collaborators (2024). The extracted trace anomalies provide a stringent test of existing neutron star EOS models, including those incorporating pQCD constraints by Gorda (2023), Bayesian inference combining electromagnetic and gravitational-wave signals by Mamon (2021), and interpolations between chiral effective field theories by Essick (2021) and high-density pQCD by Fujimoto (2022). The results enable model-insensitive determination of the trace anomaly as a function of energy density in neutron star cores, pointing to tensions with several contemporary EOS modelings for massive neutron stars.
