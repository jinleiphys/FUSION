# Glauber modeling of high-energy nuclear collisions at sub-nucleon level

> Glauber models based on nucleon--nucleon interactions are commonly used to characterize the initial state in high-energy nuclear collisions, and the dependence of its properties on impact parameter or number of participating nucleons. In this paper, an extension to the Glauber model is presented, which accounts for an arbitrary number of effective sub-nucleon degrees of freedom, or active constituents, in the nucleons. Properties of the initial state, such as the number of constituent participants and collisions, as well as eccentricity and triangularity, are calculated and systematically compared for different assumptions of how to distribute the sub-nuclear degrees of freedom and for various collision systems. It is demonstrated that at high collision energy the number of produced particles scales with an average number of sub-nucleon degrees of freedom of between $3$ and $5$. The source codes for the constituent Monte Carlo Glauber extension, as well as for the calculation of the overlap area and participant density in a standard Glauber model, are made publicly available.

# Glauber modeling of high-energy nuclear collisions at sub-nucleon level

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at high collision energy, the number of produced particles scales with an average number of sub-nucleon degrees of freedom of between 3 and 5, but this scaling is conditional on the specific choice of constituent number and distribution method, and the effective number of relevant partonic degrees of freedom appears to increase with collision energy rather than being a fixed constant.

## Method

The author extends the standard Monte Carlo Glauber model by replacing each nucleon with an arbitrary but fixed number Nc of effective sub-nucleon constituents, which can be either bound to nucleons (radially distributed around each nucleon center) or freely distributed over the whole nucleus according to the nuclear density profile. Constituent-constituent interactions are modeled using a hard-sphere approximation with an effective parton-parton cross section sigma_cc, and the calculation is performed for pp, pPb, PbPb, and AuAu collisions at various energies, with parameters constrained by matching either the nucleon-nucleon cross section or the total nuclear cross section.

## Key numbers

- For PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV with Nc=5 and sigma_cc=10.3 mb, the average number of constituent participants mu=4.4 and average number of constituent collisions nu=3.7, matching sigma_NN=70.1 mb and sigma_PbPb=7.94 b
- For AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=19.6 GeV with Nc=3 and sigma_cc=6.3 mb, mu=2.8 and nu=1.7, matching sigma_NN=33.0 mb and sigma_AuAu=6.89 b
- The total PbPb cross section calculated by the MC Glauber model is 7.6 +/- 0.2 b at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, in agreement with the measured value of 7.7 +/- 0.6 b
- The total pPb cross section calculated is 2.1 +/- 0.1 b at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, in agreement with the measured value of 2.06 +/- 0.08 b
- For pp collisions at 13 TeV with Nc=3 and sigma_cc=21.6 mb (modified case), the eccentricity for central collisions (b<0.5 fm) ranges between 0.45 and 0.65, and triangularity between 0.46 and 0.54
- Scaling PbPb data at 5.02 TeV with Nc=5 yields a slope of 0.0000 +/- 0.0002 when fitting scaled dN/deta versus centrality, while Nc=3 gives a positive slope of 0.0016 +/- 0.0004 and Nc=7 gives a negative slope of -0.0009 +/- 0.0003
- The mid-rapidity dN/deta in central AA collisions scales with s_NN^0.155, while in inelastic pp collisions it scales with s_NN^0.103

## Context

This work extends the standard Glauber model approach described by Miller (2007) and Alver (2008) by incorporating sub-nucleon degrees of freedom, building on earlier work by Eremin (2003) and Nouicer (2006) who used three valence quarks per nucleon, and by d'Enterria (2010) who used up to 17 partonic degrees of freedom. The paper enables systematic comparison of initial-state properties across different assumptions about sub-nucleon structure, and provides publicly available source codes for the constituent Monte Carlo Glauber extension, allowing future studies of particle production scaling and initial-state anisotropy in small systems such as pA and pp collisions.
