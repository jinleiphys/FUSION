# Evaporation residue cross-sections as a probe for nuclear dissipation in the fission channel of a hot rotating nucleus

> Evaporation residue cross-sections are calculated in a dynamical description of nuclear fission in the framework of the Langevin equation coupled with statistical evporation of light particles. A theoretical model of one-body nuclear friction which was developed earlier, namely the chaos-weighted wall formula, is used in this calculation for the 224Th nucleus. The evaporation residue cross-section is found to be very sensitive to the choice of nuclear friction. The present results indicate that the chaotic nature of the single-particle dynamics within the nuclear volume may provide an explanation for the strong shape-dependence of nuclear friction which is usually required to fit experimental data.

# Evaporation residue cross-sections as a probe for nuclear dissipation in the fission channel of a hot rotating nucleus

## Key claim

The chaos-weighted wall formula (CWWF) for one-body nuclear friction, which has no free parameters, yields evaporation residue cross-sections for 224Th that are much closer to experimental data than those obtained with the standard wall formula, though the CWWF still considerably overestimates the measured cross-section. The authors suggest that inclusion of the neck degree of freedom in future work would further increase fission probability and reduce the calculated evaporation residue cross-section toward the experimental values.

## Method

The authors performed a dynamical description of nuclear fission using the Langevin equation in one dimension (elongation coordinate) coupled with statistical evaporation of light particles (neutrons, protons, alphas, and giant dipole gamma rays) via Monte Carlo sampling. The fission dynamics were evolved for a sufficiently long period to establish a steady flow across the fission barrier, after which a statistical model description was used; the stationary fission widths for the strongly shape-dependent CWWF and wall formula (WF) dissipations were obtained numerically and used in parametric form. The calculation was performed for the compound nucleus 224Th formed in the 16O + 208Pb fusion reaction at incident energies from 80 MeV to 140 MeV, using both the standard wall-and-window one-body dissipation and its chaos-weighted version.

## Key numbers

- The calculated evaporation residue cross-section with WF dissipation is a few times (typically 2-5) larger than that obtained with CWWF dissipation.
- A peak appears in the experimental evaporation residue excitation function at about 85 MeV beam energy, while the calculated peak is shifted by 10 MeV.
- The calculation used an ensemble of 20000 Langevin trajectories.
- The prescission neutron multiplicity calculated with both WF and CWWF dissipations was found to be quite close to the experimental data of Rossner (1992) in the energy range considered.
- The fusion cross-section spin distribution parameters l_c and delta l were obtained by fitting experimental fusion cross-sections.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Chaudhuri and Pal, 2002) which showed that prescission neutron multiplicity and fission probability calculated with CWWF agreed fairly well with experimental data for several heavy compound nuclei (A ~ 200) over a wide range of excitation energies. The present results are compared with experimental evaporation residue data from Brinkmann (1994) and prescission neutron data from Rossner (1992). The CWWF provides a theoretical basis for the shape-dependent nuclear dissipation that was found empirically necessary by Frobrich (1993) and Dioszegi (2000) to fit experimental data, where a strong increase in friction strength was needed for large deformations but very small friction was required for compact shapes. The authors note that a similar shift in the excitation function peak was also observed in the earlier work of Dioszegi (2000).
