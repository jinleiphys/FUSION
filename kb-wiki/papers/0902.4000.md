# Tomography of high-energy nuclear collisions with photon-hadron correlations

> Within the next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD (pQCD) parton model, suppression of away-side hadron spectra associated with a high $p_{T}$ photon due to parton energy loss is studied in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Dictated by the shape of the $γ$-associated jet spectrum in NLO pQCD, hadron spectra at large $z_T=p_T^{h}/p_T^γ \stackrel{>}{\sim} 1$ are more sensitive to parton energy loss and therefore are dominated by surface emission of $γ$-associated jets, whereas small $z_{T}$ hadrons mainly come from fragmentation of jets with reduced energy which is controlled by the volume emission. These lead to different centrality dependence of the $γ$-hadron suppression for different values of $z_{T}$. Therefore, a complete measurement of the suppression of $γ$-triggered hadron spectra, including its dependence on the orientation of the $γ$-hadron pair with respect to the reaction plane, allows the extraction of the spatial distribution of jet quenching parameters, achieving a true tomographic study of the quark-gluon plasma in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

# Tomography of high-energy nuclear collisions with photon-hadron correlations

## Key claim

Within next-to-leading order perturbative QCD, the suppression of away-side hadron spectra associated with a high transverse momentum photon depends on the ratio $z_T = p_T^{h}/p_T^{\gamma}$: hadrons at large $z_T \gtrsim 1$ are dominated by surface emission of $\gamma$-associated jets and are more sensitive to parton energy loss, while hadrons at small $z_T$ come from volume emission of jets with reduced energy. A complete measurement of the $\gamma$-hadron suppression, including its dependence on the orientation relative to the reaction plane, can therefore extract the spatial distribution of jet quenching parameters.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo implementation of next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD to compute the invariant cross sections for photon and photon-hadron production in $p+p$ and $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC energy. Parton energy loss in the dense medium was modeled via an effective form of medium-modified fragmentation functions, using an average energy loss parameterized for a one-dimensional expanding medium with initial gluon density $\rho_0$, with parameters $\epsilon_0 = 1.68$ GeV/fm, $\mu_0 = 1.5$ GeV, and $\tau_0 = 0.2$ fm/$c$ determined from a simultaneous fit to single and dihadron suppression. The KKP parameterization was used for vacuum fragmentation functions, CTEQ6M for parton distributions, and a hard-sphere nuclear overlap geometry.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss parameters determined from simultaneous fit to single and dihadron suppression: $\epsilon_0 = 1.68$ GeV/fm, $\mu_0 = 1.5$ GeV, $\tau_0 = 0.2$ fm/$c$.
- For $p_T^{\gamma} > 7$ GeV/$c$, the fraction of fragmentation photons after isolation cuts is reduced to less than 10%.
- The hard-sphere nuclear overlap geometry differs at most about 10% from a Wood-Saxon geometry.
- The $\gamma$-triggered fragmentation function in $p+p$ collisions at RHIC agrees well with PHENIX preliminary data for different values of $p_T^{\rm trig}$.
- The NLO pQCD results for $\gamma$-triggered fragmentation function in central $Au+Au$ collisions for $8 < p_T^{\rm trig} < 16$ GeV/$c$ agree with STAR preliminary data.
- The nuclear modification factor $I_{AA}(z_T)$ for $\gamma$-triggered hadrons at small $z_T \le 0.6$ is more sensitive to the energy loss parameter $\epsilon_0$ than the hadron-triggered case.
- For $z_T \approx 1$, contributions to $\gamma$-hadron pairs come mostly from the surface; for $z_T \approx 0.3$, contributions come from the whole volume except near the surface on the trigger photon side.
- The centrality dependence of $I_{AA}$ is weaker for $z_T < 0.6$ (volume emission) than for $z_T \geq 1$ (surface emission).

## Context

This work extends the study of jet quenching from single hadron and dihadron suppression (Wang and Gyulassy, 1990; PHENIX and STAR collaborations) to $\gamma$-hadron correlations, building on earlier NLO pQCD calculations by Owens (1987-1990) and the medium-modified fragmentation function approach of Wang (2003) and Zhang, Owens, and Wang (2007). The key advance is that the $z_T$ dependence of $\gamma$-hadron suppression allows one to distinguish surface from volume emission of jets, enabling tomographic extraction of the spatial distribution of jet quenching parameters in the quark-gluon plasma. The results are compared to STAR preliminary data on $\gamma$-hadron correlations, and the method can be extended to include orientation with respect to the reaction plane for full tomography.
