# Shell effect in $A=$116--124 Tin isotopes investigated using isotopic analysis of proton scattering at 295 MeV

> Proton elastic scattering off Sn isotopes at $E_p=295$ MeV in the mass number range of $A=$116--124 was investigated using calculation employing relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) with theoretical densities obtained for the Sn isotopes from relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) and nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (SHFB) calculations of spherical nuclei. In the RIA calculations, a modified version of the Murdock and Horowitz model that includes a density dependence in the effective nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction was used. A calculation using the theoretical density obtained from a relativistic calculation employing the DD-ME2 interaction successfully reproduced the experimental data for $^{122}$Sn$(p,p)$, but it overestimated the $^{116}$Sn$(p,p)$ and $^{118}$Sn$(p,p)$ cross sections at backward angles. Isotopic analyses of the $(p,p)$ reactions combined with nuclear structure properties were performed based on reaction calculations that used a model density modified from the DD-ME2 density to optimize the neutron density of the Sn isotopes by fitting the isotopic cross section ratios. The resulting optimized density reproduced the experimental $(p,p)$ data for the series of Sn isotopes from $^{116}$Sn to $^{124}$Sn. The neutron root-mean-square(rms) radii and the skin thickness of the Sn isotopes obtained in the present analysis exhibited smooth $A$ dependences in the range of $A=$116--124, which are consistent with the theoretical predictions obtained using the DD-ME2 interaction but seem to contradict the experimental results determined from the $(p,p)$ data. In a detailed analysis of the surface neutron density probed by proton elastic scattering, a signal of the shell effect at $N=66$ in Sn isotopes was found.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a signal of the shell effect at neutron number N=66 in Sn isotopes was found through a detailed analysis of the surface neutron density probed by proton elastic scattering, but this signal appears only after renormalizing the experimental cross sections for 120Sn and 124Sn by factors of 1.20 and 1.27, respectively, to correct for apparent normalization inconsistencies in the original data.

## Method

Proton elastic scattering off Sn isotopes at 295 MeV was calculated using the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) with a density-dependent modified Murdock and Horowitz (ddMH) model, employing theoretical densities from relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations with the DD-ME2 interaction and nonrelativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (SHFB) calculations. An isotopic analysis was performed by fitting the isotopic cross section ratios R(sigma) relative to 122Sn using a model density (me2-3s) that modifies the DD-ME2 neutron density by adjusting the 3s1/2 neutron component and a radial scaling parameter to optimize agreement with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Proton energy: Ep = 295 MeV for all Sn(p,p) measurements
- Mass number range investigated: A = 116 to 124 (Sn isotopes)
- Renormalization factors applied to experimental data: 1.20 for 120Sn(p,p) and 1.27 for 124Sn(p,p) cross sections
- Optimized me2-3s model parameters for 116Sn: v = 2 and delta_s = -0.7% (radial scaling)
- Optimized me2-3s model parameters for 118Sn: v = 1.5 and delta_s = -0.7%
- Optimized me2-3s model parameters for 124Sn: v = -1 and delta_s = 0
- Theoretical rms radii linear fits from DD-ME2: r_n = 3.396 + 0.0191N fm and r_p = 4.093 + 0.0069N fm
- Matter radius fit: r_m = 0.947 A^(1/3) fm
- Forward-angle region with minimal model dependence: theta_c.m. less than or approximately 16 degrees

## Context

This work extends the isotopic analysis method previously applied by Kanada-En'yo (2021) to Pb isotopes (204Pb, 206Pb, 208Pb) at 295 MeV, now applied to Sn isotopes in the range A=116-124 using experimental data from Terashima et al. (2008). The results contradict earlier experimental determinations of neutron rms radii from (p,p) data that showed non-smooth A dependence, while being consistent with theoretical predictions from the DD-ME2 interaction. The analysis enables extraction of neutron densities and skin thickness for a series of isotopes with reduced model dependence, and the finding of a shell effect signal at N=66 can guide future structure calculations and experiments probing neutron density distributions.
