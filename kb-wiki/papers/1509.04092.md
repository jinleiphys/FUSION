# Reduction of the $K^*$ meson abundance in heavy ion collisions

> We study the $K^*$ meson reduction in heavy ion collisions by focusing on the hadronic effects on the $K^*$ meson abundance. We evaluate the absorption cross sections of the $K^*$ and $K$ meson by light mesons in the hadronic matter, and further investigate the variation in the meson abundances for both particles during the hadronic stage of heavy ion collisions. We show how the interplay between the interaction of the $K^*$ meson and kaon with light mesons in the hadronic medium determines the final yield difference of the statistical hadronization model to the experimental measurements. For the central Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, we find that the $K^*/K$ yield ratio at chemical freeze-out decreases by $36\%$ during the expansion of the hadronic matter, resulting in the final ratio comparable to STAR measurements of 0.23 $\pm0.05$.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the observed discrepancy between the statistical hadronization model prediction and experimental measurements of the $K^*/K$ yield ratio in heavy ion collisions can be explained by hadronic interactions during the expansion of hadronic matter, specifically that approximately 36% of $K^*$ mesons produced at chemical freeze-out are absorbed before freeze-out.

## Method
The authors evaluate absorption cross sections of $K^*$ and $K$ mesons with light mesons (pions, $\rho$, $K$, and $K^*$) using effective Lagrangians with coupling constants obtained from empirical values and SU(3) flavor symmetry. They then solve coupled time evolution equations for $K^*$ and $K$ meson abundances during the hadronic stage of central Au+Au collisions, incorporating thermally averaged cross sections, a schematic model of accelerated transverse expansion based on the Bjorken picture, and the $K^*$ meson decay width.

## Key numbers
- $K^*/K$ yield ratio at chemical freeze-out decreases by 36% during the expansion of hadronic matter for central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV
- Final $K^*/K$ yield ratio is 0.25, comparable to STAR measurements of 0.23 $\pm$ 0.05
- Initial $K^*$ meson yield at chemical freeze-out is 55.7, decreasing to 35.6 at 9.8 fm/c after chemical freeze-out
- Kaon abundance increases from 88.1 to 106.5 during the hadronic expansion
- Sum of $K^*$ and kaon abundances changes slightly from 143.8 to 142.1
- Statistical hadronization model prediction for $K^*/K$ yield ratio is 0.33 $\pm$ 0.01
- $K^*$ meson lifetime is 4 fm/c, less than the presumed lifespan of the hadronic stage
- Isospin averaged $K^*$ meson decay width used is $\Gamma_{K^*}=49.1$ MeV
- Isospin averaged kaon mass used is $m_K=495.645$ MeV
- Coupling constants used: $g_{\pi K^*K}=3.25$, $g_{\rho KK}=3.05$, $g_{\rho K^*K^*}=3.25$
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda=1.8$ GeV
- Hadronization temperature $T_c=175$ MeV, kinetic freeze-out temperature $T_f=125$ MeV
- Chemical freeze-out time $\tau_c=5.0$ fm/c, freeze-out time $\tau_f=17.3$ fm/c

## Context
This work extends the study of hadronic effects on resonance abundances in heavy ion collisions, following earlier investigations of $J/\psi$ suppression in hadronic matter using effective Lagrangian methods by Matinyan (1998), Haglin (1999), Lin (1999), and Oh (2000). The authors apply similar approaches previously used for exotic mesons such as $D_{sJ}(2317)$ by Chen (2007) and $X(3872)$ by Cho (2013) and Torres (2014). The results explain the discrepancy between the statistical hadronization model by Braun-Munzinger (2001) and STAR measurements by Adams (2004) and Aggarwal (2010), and enable quantitative understanding of how hadronic interactions modify resonance yields during the hadronic stage of heavy ion collisions.
