# The neutron skin of 48Ca and 208Pb: a critical analysis

> We discuss the neutron skins of 48Ca and 208Pb. We review and critically examine modern predictions and empirical constraints, with special attention to the different interpretations of the findings from the PREX-II experiment and the recently reported value of the neutron skin in 48Ca extracted from the CREX experiment. We argue that, in the spirit of the ab initio philosophy, the path to understanding the behavior of dense neutron-rich matter must not circumvent fundamental nuclear forces. Based only on that argument, a thick neutron skin in 208Pb is highly unlikely.

# The neutron skin of 48Ca and 208Pb: a critical analysis

## Key claim
Based on the argument that understanding dense neutron-rich matter must proceed through fundamental nuclear forces derived from first principles (chiral effective field theory), a thick neutron skin in 208Pb is highly unlikely, even though the PREX-II experiment reports a large value of 0.283 ± 0.071 fm. The CREX result for 48Ca, in contrast, does not appear to be an outlier when compared to existing measurements and microscopic predictions.

## Method
The paper reviews and critically examines modern predictions and empirical constraints on the neutron skins of 48Ca and 208Pb, comparing results from parity-violating electron scattering experiments (PREX-II and CREX) with a wide range of hadronic scattering measurements, pionic probes, antiprotonic atom data, electric dipole polarizability studies, and ab initio theoretical predictions from chiral effective field theory. The analysis connects neutron skin thickness to the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy and the pressure in neutron matter, and examines the implications for direct Urca processes in neutron stars.

## Key numbers
- PREX-II combined result for 208Pb neutron skin: 0.283 ± 0.071 fm (range 0.212 fm to 0.354 fm)
- CREX result for 48Ca neutron skin: reported as the last point on the right in Figure 1 (exact value from CREX experiment cited in text)
- Ab initio predictions for 208Pb neutron skin: 0.139 - 0.200 fm (68% credibility region, Hu+2022); 0.120 - 0.221 fm (90% credibility region, Hu+2022); 0.184 - 0.236 fm (Nov+2023); 0.13 - 0.17 fm (Sam2022)
- Ab initio predictions for 48Ca neutron skin: 0.120 - 0.150 fm (Hag+2016); 0.141 - 0.187 fm (68% CR, Hu+2022); 0.123 - 0.199 fm (90% CR, Hu+2022); 0.114 - 0.186 fm (Nov+2023); 0.12 - 0.15 fm (Sam2022)
- PREX II value of symmetry energy at saturation: 38.1 ± 4.7 MeV
- Direct Urca threshold density decreases from 0.71 fm^-3 to 0.21 fm^-3 as the skin of 208Pb increases from 0.13 fm to 0.28 fm (Kumar+)
- Symmetry energy at 1.5ρ0 (from PREX II constraints): approximately 58 MeV

## Context
This work extends the ongoing debate following the PREX-II experiment (which reported a thick neutron skin in 208Pb) and the CREX experiment (which reported a smaller skin in 48Ca). The paper contrasts these results with a broad range of earlier measurements compiled from hadronic scattering experiments (Clark+2003, Zen+2010, Staro+1994, Shlo+1979, Ray+1979, Zen+2018), pionic probes (Fried+2012, Gib+1992), antiprotonic atom data (Bro+2007, Trz+2001), and electric dipole polarizability studies (Roca+2015, Tam+2011, Birk+2017). The analysis builds on the author's previous work (Sammarruca 2022) showing a nearly linear relation between neutron skin and isospin asymmetry, and argues that phenomenological density functionals designed to reconcile PREX and CREX (such as those in Hor+2023 incorporating the δ-meson) introduce unrealistic isovector contributions that circumvent the constraints of fundamental nuclear forces from chiral effective field theory. The paper enables future comparison with the upcoming MREX experiment at MESA.
