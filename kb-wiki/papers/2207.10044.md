# CME -- Experimental Results and Interpretation

> The experimental status is reviewed on the search for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Emphasis is put on background contributions to the CME-sensitive charge correlation measurements and their effects on data interpretation.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, if it exists, is likely small and dominated by background contributions from genuine particle correlations coupled with elliptic flow; the paper reviews experimental attempts to extract a possible CME signal, with results ranging from null to a tentative hint at about 2σ significance.

## Method

The paper reviews measurements of the charge-dependent azimuthal correlator Δγ ≡ γ_OS − γ_SS, where γ_αβ = ⟨cos(φ_α + φ_β − 2Ψ_RP)⟩, in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC. Backgrounds are assessed through event-shape engineering (ESE) that bins events by flow vector magnitude to extrapolate Δγ to zero v₂, through isobar collisions (⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru vs. ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr) designed to cancel backgrounds while preserving a CME signal difference, and through comparing Δγ measured with respect to the spectator plane (SP) versus the participant plane (PP) to extract the CME fraction f_CME.

## Key numbers

* In Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the extracted CME fraction f_CME from SP/PP measurements in mid-central 20–50% centrality indicates a finite CME signal with ~2σ significance (STAR, 2021).
* In isobar collisions (Ru+Ru vs. Zr+Zr) at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the ratio of Δγ observables lies above the 1/N baseline, but nonflow effects of magnitude 0.5–1% prevent a definitive CME conclusion (STAR, 2021).
* In p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, the Δγ magnitude is comparable to that in Pb+Pb collisions, indicating large backgrounds since no CME is expected in small systems (CMS, 2016).
* In Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 7.7–62.4 GeV, Δγ decreases toward lower energies (STAR, 2014).
* The background estimate from a simple order-of-magnitude calculation yields Δγ_bkg ∼ 0.2/100 × 0.5 × 0.1 ∼ 10⁻⁴, comparable to measured Δγ values.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing search for the chiral magnetic effect in QCD, following early measurements by STAR (Abelev et al., 2009) and ALICE (Abelev et al., 2012) that showed large Δγ signals qualitatively consistent with CME but warned of backgrounds. The paper critically assesses background subtraction methods proposed by Bzdak (2012), Schlichting and Pratt (2010), and others, and interprets recent isobar collision results from STAR (2021) and SP/PP measurements from STAR (2021) that together suggest backgrounds dominate but a small CME signal may remain. The work enables future high-statistics Au+Au runs at STAR (2023, 2025) to either identify the CME or set stringent upper limits.
