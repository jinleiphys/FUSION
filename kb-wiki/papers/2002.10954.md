# Pentaquark states from bound state approach with chiral partner structure

> The observation of the two states $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4445)$ [Phys.Rev.Lett. 115 (2015) 072001] solids the existence of the pentaquark state in Nature. In this work, we study the spectrum of the pentaquark states including one or two heavy quarks based on the bound state approach by binding the heavy-light mesons to the nucleon as a soliton in an effective Lagrangian approach. By regarding the $H$ doublet and $G$ doublet as chiral partners to each other and coupling them to light mesons with a minimal derivative, the chiral partner structure of the pentaquark states is calculated. We find that the bound state approach prefers to arrange $P_c^+(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ into a heavy quark doublet $(\frac{1}{2}^-,\frac{3}{2}^-)$ so both decay to $J/ψp$ through the $S$-wave. The predicted spectrum shown in this work serves as a guide for the experimental hunting of more pentaquark states.

# Pentaquark states from bound state approach with chiral partner structure

## Key claim
The bound state approach, treating the H and G heavy-light meson doublets as chiral partners and binding them to a nucleon soliton, can arrange the observed P_c^+(4440) and P_c^+(4457) into a heavy quark doublet (1/2^-, 3/2^-) that decays to J/ψ p through S-wave; however, the authors cannot identify the quantum numbers of P_c(4312) by simply comparing the mass, and the identification relies on the specific chiral partner structure and the assumption of infinite heavy quark and large N_c limits.

## Method
The authors use an effective Lagrangian with hidden local symmetry, coupling H doublet (J^P = 0^-, 1^-) and G doublet (J^P = 0^+, 1^+) heavy-light mesons as chiral partners to a nucleon soliton in the hedgehog ansatz. They compute binding potentials V_H and V_G from the Lagrangian, then obtain bound state masses by adding the soliton mass, spin-averaged meson masses, binding energies, and collective rotation energy from quantization. For hidden charm pentaquarks, two heavy-light mesons (one charm, one anticharm) are bound to the soliton, and the total mass includes both binding energies and a two-body rotation energy term.

## Key numbers
- Spin-averaged masses: M_H = 1.98 GeV (from D_0 and D*), M_G = 2.41 GeV (from D_0* and D_1)
- Soliton mass M_sol = 0.868 GeV, moment of inertia 1/I = 0.193 GeV, coupling g = 4.74, axial coupling |g_A| = 0.56, κ = -0.83
- Binding energies for H doublet: k=0 gives -0.556 GeV, k=1 gives +0.145 GeV; for G doublet: k=0 gives +0.496 GeV, k=1 gives -0.205 GeV
- Binding energies for anti-H doublet: k=0 gives +0.556 GeV, k=1 gives -0.145 GeV; for anti-G doublet: k=0 gives -0.496 GeV, k=1 gives +0.205 GeV
- Predicted open charm pentaquark masses: Θ_c(1/2^+, 3/2^+) at 2.745 GeV (H-bar S), Θ_c(1/2^-) at 2.778 GeV (G-bar S)
- Hidden charm HS G-bar bound state mass: 4.417 GeV, identified with P_c^+(4440) and P_c^+(4457) as a (1/2^-, 3/2^-) doublet
- Mass splitting between P_c^+(4440) and P_c^+(4457) is approximately 20 MeV, attributed to heavy quark symmetry breaking
- All predicted hidden charm states are above the J/ψ p threshold of approximately 4.04 GeV

## Context
This work extends the bound state approach previously applied to pentaquarks with one heavy quark by Harada et al. (2012) to hidden charm pentaquarks with two heavy quarks, incorporating the chiral partner structure of H and G doublets as formulated by Nowak et al. (1992) and Bardeen et al. (1993). The study is motivated by the LHCb observations of P_c(4380) and P_c(4450) reported by Aaij et al. (2015) and the updated analysis by Aaij et al. (2019) revealing P_c(4312), P_c(4440), and P_c(4457). The results enable predictions for additional pentaquark states that could decay into open charm mesons, and the framework can be straightforwardly extended to bottom quark pentaquarks by substituting the spin-averaged masses of bottom heavy-light meson doublets.
