# Realistic medium-averaging in radiative energy loss

> We present results from a jet energy loss calculation using the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) formalism and bulk medium evolution from the covariant transport model MPC. At both RHIC and LHC energies we find that realistic transverse expansion strongly reduces elliptic flow at high pT compared to calculations with transversely 'frozen' profiles. We argue that this is a generic feature of GLV energy loss. Transverse expansion also leads to stronger high-pT suppression, while fluctuations in energy loss with the location of scattering centers weaken the suppression. But, unlike the reduction of v2, these effects nearly disappear once alpha_s is adjusted to reproduce R_AA in central collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that realistic three-dimensional (3D) transverse expansion of the bulk medium strongly reduces high-transverse-momentum elliptic flow compared to calculations with transversely "frozen" (one-dimensional) profiles, and that this is a generic feature of the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) energy loss formalism, though the effect on the nuclear suppression factor largely disappears once the strong coupling constant is adjusted to reproduce central collision data.

## Method

The authors combined the GLV radiative energy loss formalism, using the leading single-scattering (opacity n=1) term, with bulk medium evolution from the covariant parton transport model MPC. They computed neutral pion suppression factors and elliptic flow at midrapidity for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, comparing scenarios with longitudinal Bjorken expansion only ("1D") versus full 3D expansion, and using either average energy loss or stochastic energy loss that depends on the location of scattering centers.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, with alpha_s = 0.29, realistic 3D expansion enhances jet quenching compared to 1D expansion (Fig. 1, left vs right panels).
- After tuning alpha_s to reproduce R_AA in central collisions above p_T >= 8 GeV, all four scenarios give practically the same R_AA result (Fig. 2).
- With realistic 3D medium evolution, elliptic flow v_2 at high p_T is reduced by 40-50% compared to the case of simplified 1D dynamics at RHIC energies (Fig. 3, right vs left panels).
- For Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, with alpha_s tuned to reproduce R_AA ≈ 0.18 at p_T = 6 GeV in central collisions, transverse expansion also reduces v_2 dramatically at LHC energies (Fig. 4).
- Initial conditions used dN(0)/dy = 1100 for Au+Au and 2400 for Pb+Pb, with formation time tau_0 = 0.6 fm.
- The opacity rescaling factor Z = 0.35 was used to account for dynamical scattering centers and elastic scattering.

## Context

This work extends the GLV energy loss approach by incorporating realistic 3D medium evolution from the MPC transport model, going beyond the transversely "frozen" profiles used in earlier calculations such as Buzzatti and Gyulassy (CUJET1.0). The finding that transverse expansion strongly reduces high-p_T elliptic flow raises the question of whether GLV produces too little elliptic flow at high p_T, and suggests that future full-fledged GLV calculations should include this effect. The authors note that unlike the v_2 reduction, the effects of transverse expansion and stochastic energy loss on R_AA nearly disappear once alpha_s is tuned to central collision data.
