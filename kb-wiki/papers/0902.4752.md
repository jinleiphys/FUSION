# Time-Dependent Variational Approach to the Non-Abelian Pure Gauge Theory

> The time-dependent variational approach to the pure Yang-Mills gauge theory, especially a color su(3) gauge theory, is formulated in the functional Schroedinger picture with a Gaussian wave functional approximation. The equations of motion for the quantum gauge fields are formulated in the Liouville-von Neumann form. This variational approach is applied in order to derive the transport coefficients, such as the shear viscosity, for the pure gluonic matter by using the linear response theory. As a result, the contribution to the shear viscosity from the quantum gluons is zero up to the lowest order of the coupling g in the quantum gluonic matter.

## Key claim

The contribution to the shear viscosity from quantum gluons (the quantum fluctuation part around the mean field) is zero up to the lowest order of the coupling constant \(g\) in pure gluonic matter, meaning that at leading order the quantum fluctuations do not contribute to the shear viscosity and the dominant contribution comes from the classical mean-field part.

## Method

The authors formulate a time-dependent variational approach to pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in the functional Schroedinger picture using a Gaussian wave functional as a trial state. The equations of motion for the quantum gauge fields are expressed in Liouville-von Neumann form for the reduced density matrix, and the shear viscosity is derived via linear response theory. The method treats mean fields and quantum fluctuations on an equal footing, with the Gaussian trial state parameterized by variational functions for the classical gauge fields and the two-point correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity \(\eta_C\) for gluonic matter at temperature \(T\) is expressed as \(\eta_C = d_f \frac{T^3}{g^4 \log(1/g^2)}\) up to the lowest order of the coupling \(g\), where \(d_f\) is a numerically determined constant.
- The two-point function \(G_k\) at zero temperature in the time-independent case is \(G_k = 1/(2|\mathbf{k}|)\).
- At finite temperature, the eigenvalue \(f_m\) for the reduced density matrix is \(f_m = n_m + 1/2\) with \(n_m = 1/(e^{E_m/T} - 1)\).

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent variational method developed by Dominique (Ref. [Dominique]) for pure Yang-Mills theory, which was shown to reproduce the one-loop running coupling constant. The paper applies this approach to evaluate transport coefficients, specifically the shear viscosity, for pure gluonic matter, building on earlier calculations by Kajantie (Ref. [Kajantie]) and Yaffe (Ref. [Yaffe]) that gave the leading-order shear viscosity. The result that quantum gluon contributions vanish at lowest order in \(g\) is consistent with the picture that the dominant shear viscosity arises from classical mean-field effects, and it enables future calculations of higher-order contributions from quantum fluctuations.
