# Simultaneous reweighting of Transverse Momentum Dependent distributions

> The Bayesian reweighting procedure is extended to the case of multiple independent extractions of transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs). By exploiting the data on transverse single spin asymmetries, $A_N$, for inclusive pion production in polarized proton-proton collisions measured at RHIC, we perform a simultaneous reweighting of the quark Sivers, transversity and Collins TMD functions extracted from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and $e^+ e^-$ annihilation into hadron pairs. The impact of the implementation of the Soffer bound, as well as the differences between older and newer $A_N$ data, are investigated. The agreement with $A_N$ data at large-$x_F$ values, a kinematical region complementary to those explored in SIDIS measurements, is enhanced, improving the knowledge of the polarized quark TMDs in the large-$x$ region.

## Key claim

The paper extends Bayesian reweighting to simultaneously update multiple independent TMD extractions using proton-proton $A_N$ data, showing that the agreement with large-$x_F$ data is enhanced, but the method's reliability depends on the effective number of Monte Carlo sets remaining large after reweighting.

## Method

The authors perform a simultaneous Bayesian reweighting of three independently extracted TMD functions (quark Sivers, transversity, and Collins) using data on transverse single-spin asymmetries $A_N$ for inclusive pion production in polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC. The prior distributions come from new fits to SIDIS and $e^+e^-$ data using updated COMPASS, HERMES, and JLab datasets, with the Sivers function parametrized as in Boglione (2018) and the transversity and Collins functions as in Anselmino (2015) and D'Alesio (2020). The reweighting is performed within both the Generalized Parton Model (GPM) and the Color Gauge Invariant GPM (CGI-GPM), using a compression algorithm to reduce the number of Monte Carlo sets from $O(10^5)$ to $2\times 10^3$ per extraction, yielding $4\times 10^6$ combinations for each $A_N$ bin.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian widths for unpolarized TMDs are fixed at $\langle k_\perp^2\rangle = 0.57$ GeV$^2$ and $\langle p_\perp^2\rangle = 0.12$ GeV$^2$, extracted from HERMES multiplicities in Anselmino (2013).
- The Sivers function parametrization uses five parameters: $N_u$, $N_d$, $\beta_u$, $\beta_d$, and $\langle k_\perp^2\rangle_S$.
- The transversity and Collins extraction uses eight free parameters total, with the Soffer bound applied a posteriori on generated MC sets.
- The tolerance for reweighting is $\Delta\chi^2 = 22.69$ for 13 free parameters (5 for Sivers, 4 for transversity, 4 for Collins), based on Wilks' theorem at a given confidence level.
- The compression algorithm selects $2\times 10^3$ MC sets from $O(10^5)$ per extraction, validated by Welch's $t$-statistic with $p$-values $\gtrsim 0.1$.
- Data selection requires $P_T > 1$ GeV for applicability of the (CGI-)GPM; data points with $P_T < 1.5$ GeV are highlighted as potentially questionable.
- The analysis uses $A_N$ data from BRAHMS at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV for $\pi^\pm$, STAR at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV for $\pi^0$, and STAR at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ and $500$ GeV for non-isolated $\pi^0$.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian reweighting approach of Boglione (2021) to the simultaneous case of multiple independent TMD extractions, building on prior global analyses of Sivers, transversity, and Collins functions from SIDIS and $e^+e^-$ data (Anselmino 2013, 2015; Boglione 2018; D'Alesio 2020). It enables improved constraints on polarized quark TMDs in the large-$x$ region by incorporating $A_N$ data from RHIC at large $x_F$, a kinematic region complementary to SIDIS measurements, and investigates the impact of the Soffer bound and differences between older and newer $A_N$ data. The compression algorithm for Monte Carlo sets follows the approach of Bauer (2022) but samples in parameter space rather than observable space.
