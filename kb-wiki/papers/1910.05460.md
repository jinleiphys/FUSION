# Fano effect on the neutron elastic scattering by open-shell nuclei

> By focusing on the asymmetric shape of cross section, we analyze the pairing effect on the partial wave components of cross section for neutron elastic scattering off stable and unstable nuclei within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework. Explicit expressions for Fano parameters $q_{lj}$ and $ε_{lj}$ have been derived and the pairing effects have been analyzed in term of these parameters, and the Fano effect was found on the neutron elastic scattering off the stable nucleus in terms of the pairing correlation. Fano effect was appeared as the asymmetric line-shape of the cross section caused by the small absolute value of $q_{lj}$ due to the small pairing effect on the deep-lying hole state of the stable nucleus. In the case of the unstable nuclei, the large $q_{lj}$ value is expected because of the small absolute value of the Fermi energy. The quasiparticle resonance with the large $q_{lj}$ forms the Breit-Wigner type shape in the elastic scattering cross section.

## Key claim

The Fano effect, characterized by an asymmetric line-shape in the neutron elastic scattering cross section, arises from pairing correlations in open-shell nuclei; the asymmetry is caused by a small absolute value of the Fano parameter \(q_{lj}\) due to weak pairing on deep-lying hole states in stable nuclei, whereas for unstable nuclei a large \(q_{lj}\) value (due to small Fermi energy) produces a Breit-Wigner type shape rather than an asymmetric one.

## Method

The authors derived explicit expressions for the Fano parameters \(q_{lj}\) and \(\epsilon_{lj}\) within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework by dividing the Jost function into scattering and pairing parts and applying the Gell-Mann-Goldberger relation (two-potential formula) to the T-matrix. They then numerically analyzed the partial wave components of the neutron elastic scattering cross section for \(p_{1/2}\) and \(d_{3/2}\) partial waves using a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential and pair potential, varying the pairing gap \(\langle\Delta\rangle\) from 2.0 to 3.0 MeV.

## Key numbers

* For \(p_{1/2}\) with \(\lambda = -8.0\) MeV and \(\langle\Delta\rangle = 2.0\) MeV: resonance energy \(E_r = 12.14\) MeV (incident energy \(E_i^r = 4.14\) MeV), width \(\Gamma_{lj} = 0.28\) MeV, Fano parameter \(q_{lj} = -0.55\), and dip energy \(E_c = 12.21\) MeV (\(E_i^c = 4.21\) MeV).
* For \(p_{1/2}\) with \(\lambda = -8.0\) MeV and \(\langle\Delta\rangle = 3.0\) MeV: \(E_r = 12.65\) MeV (\(E_i^r = 4.65\) MeV), \(\Gamma_{lj} = 0.56\) MeV, \(q_{lj} = -0.63\), \(E_c = 12.84\) MeV (\(E_i^c = 4.84\) MeV).
* For \(d_{3/2}\) with \(\lambda = -1.0\) MeV and \(\langle\Delta\rangle = 2.0\) MeV: \(E_r = 4.53\) MeV (\(E_i^r = 3.53\) MeV), \(\Gamma_{lj} = 0.20\) MeV, \(q_{lj} = 13.80\), \(E_c = 2.94\) MeV (\(E_i^c = 1.94\) MeV).
* For \(d_{3/2}\) with \(\lambda = -1.0\) MeV and \(\langle\Delta\rangle = 3.0\) MeV: \(E_r = 4.99\) MeV (\(E_i^r = 3.99\) MeV), \(\Gamma_{lj} = 0.52\) MeV, \(q_{lj} = 11.55\), \(E_c = 1.45\) MeV (\(E_i^c = 0.45\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of quasiparticle resonances in neutron elastic scattering from open-shell nuclei, previously studied within the HFB framework using the Jost function formalism (Mizuyama et al., 2015), by explicitly deriving and analyzing the Fano parameters for the first time in this context. It builds on earlier suggestions of Fano resonances in nuclear reactions, such as the \({}^{15}\)N(\({}^{7}\)Li,\({}^{7}\)Be)\({}^{15}\)C reaction analyzed by Orrigo et al. and the d(\({}^{9}\)Li,\({}^{10}\)Li)p reaction, but provides a quantitative framework for interpreting the asymmetric line shapes. The results enable future experimental analysis of pairing correlations and single-particle level structure in neutron-rich open-shell nuclei, though the authors note that channel-coupling effects (as in R-matrix analysis and cPVC calculations) must also be included for realistic data interpretation.
