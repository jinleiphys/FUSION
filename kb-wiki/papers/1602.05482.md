# Investigating the QCD phase diagram with hadron multiplicities at NICA

> We discuss the potential of the experimental programme at NICA to investigate the QCD phase diagram and particularly the position of the critical line at large baryon-chemical potential with accurate measurements of particle multiplicities. We briefly review the present status and we outline the tasks to be accomplished both theoretically and the experimentally to make hadronic abundances a sensitive probe.

## Key claim

The paper argues that accurate measurements of hadron multiplicities at NICA energies (√sNN between few and 10 GeV) can locate the QCD critical line at large baryon-chemical potential, provided that afterburning effects are accounted for to reconstruct the "latest chemical equilibrium point" (LCEP) rather than the raw chemical freeze-out temperature. The hedge is that this method works only if the system has actually crossed the critical line, which must be verified by checking that hadronic transport models alone cannot reproduce the data.

## Method

The authors propose to fit measured hadron multiplicities with a statistical hadronization model, then correct for the distortion caused by inelastic hadronic rescattering (afterburning) by using the UrQMD transport code to reconstruct the primordial LCEP. The LCEP is identified with the hadronization point on the QCD critical line, and its temperature and baryon-chemical potential are extracted via the Cooper-Frye prescription coupled to the transport simulation. The analysis is applied to central collisions from LHC down to SPS energy (NA49 data), and the authors outline the need for similar measurements at NICA energies (2–10 GeV) with full phase-space coverage and multiple hadronic species.

## Key numbers

- The NICA energy range √sNN between few and 10 GeV corresponds to a baryon chemical potential at chemical freeze-out between 300 and 800 MeV, according to most extrapolations.
- The critical line has been crossed down to √sNN = 7.6 GeV, based on analysis of NA49 data at SPS energies.
- The LCEP curve is "sizeably flatter" than the uncorrected chemical freeze-out curve, with curvature in better agreement with lattice QCD calculations.
- The AGS data below √sNN ≈ 5 GeV are considered unreliable for firm conclusions because measured multiplicities are few and full phase-space measurements are even fewer.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model approach of Becattini (2006), Cleymans, and Andronic (various) by incorporating afterburning corrections via UrQMD (Bass, 1998) to reconstruct the LCEP, as previously demonstrated in freez1, freez2, and freez3. It contrasts with the use of conserved-charge fluctuations proposed by the Karsch group and Ratti (various), which are more sensitive to conservation laws (Gorenstein) and finite acceptance (Koch). The paper enables the NICA experimental programme to test whether the critical line is crossed in the 2–10 GeV range, and suggests that a narrow sequence of LCEP points might exhibit non-monotonic behavior due to a possible QCD critical point, as proposed by Lacey (unelaborated).
