# Neutrino Mean Free Path in Neutron Stars

> The Landau parameters of nuclear matter and neutron matter are extracted from the Brueckner theory including three-body forces. The dynamical response function to weak neutrino current is calculated in terms of the Landau Parameters in the RPA limit. Then, the neutrino mean free path in neutron stars is calculated for different conditions of density and temperature.

## Key claim

The neutrino mean free path in neutron matter is predicted to be about one order of magnitude larger than in a free Fermi gas for densities in the neutron-star range, but this result depends on the low-frequency approximation (ω/(kv_F) ≪ 1) and neglects the temperature dependence of the Landau parameters.

## Method

The authors extracted the zero-order Landau parameters (F₀, F₀′, G₀, G₀′) from the equation of state of neutron matter calculated using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation with the Argonne V₁₈ two-body force plus a microscopic three-body force (3BF). They then computed the dynamical response function to weak neutrino current in the random-phase approximation (RPA) limit using these Landau parameters, and derived the neutrino mean free path from the transport equation in the low-frequency limit.

## Key numbers

- Landau parameter F₀ in neutron matter at density ρ = 0.20 fm⁻³: 0.615 (from Brueckner calculation)
- Landau parameter F₀ in neutron matter at density ρ = 0.32 fm⁻³: 3.395 (from Brueckner calculation)
- Landau parameter G₀ in neutron matter at density ρ = 0.20 fm⁻³: 1.170 (from Brueckner calculation)
- Landau parameter G₀ in neutron matter at density ρ = 0.32 fm⁻³: 1.461 (from Brueckner calculation)
- Compression modulus ratio K/K_F at ρ = 0.20 fm⁻³: 1.84 (Brueckner), compared to 2.13 (Monte Carlo) and 1.45 (Skyrme SLy230b)
- Compression modulus ratio K/K_F at ρ = 0.32 fm⁻³: 5.24 (Brueckner), compared to 4.76 (Monte Carlo) and 4.32 (Skyrme SLy230b)
- Magnetic susceptibility ratio χ/χ_F at ρ = 0.20 fm⁻³: 0.41 (Brueckner), compared to 0.37 (Monte Carlo) and 0.32 (Skyrme SLy230b)
- Magnetic susceptibility ratio χ/χ_F at ρ = 0.32 fm⁻³: 0.37 (Brueckner), compared to 0.33 (Monte Carlo) and 0.22 (Skyrme SLy230b)
- Neutrino mean free path in interacting neutron matter is larger by one order of magnitude than in a free Fermi gas for densities in the neutron-star range (at temperature T = 10 MeV and neutrino energy E_ν = 30 MeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations of neutrino propagation in neutron matter by Iwamoto and collaborators (Iwamoto, 1990s) and Margueron and collaborators (Margueron, 2000s), who used phenomenological Skyrme and Gogny forces in Hartree-Fock calculations. The present Brueckner-based approach with three-body forces yields a much faster increase of the neutrino mean free path with density compared to those Skyrme and Gogny results, due to the more repulsive character of the three-body force used here. The results imply a faster cooling of neutron stars via neutrino escape than predicted by the free Fermi gas model, but the authors note that future work should introduce temperature-dependent Landau parameters for application to proton-neutron stars.
