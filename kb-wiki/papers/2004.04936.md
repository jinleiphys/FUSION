# Neutron dominance in excited states of $^{26}$Mg and $^{10}$Be probed by proton and alpha inelastic scattering

> Isospin characters of nuclear excitations in $^{26}$Mg and $^{10}$Be are investigated via proton($p$) and alpha($α$) inelastic scattering. A structure model of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) is applied to calculate the ground and excited states of $^{26}$Mg and $^{10}$Be. The calculation describes the isoscalar feature of the ground-band $2^+_1$($K^π=0^+_1$) excitation and predicts the neutron dominance of the side-band $2^+_2$($K^π=2^+$) excitation in $^{26}$Mg and $^{10}$Be. The $p$ and $α$ inelastic scattering off $^{26}$Mg and $^{10}$Be is calculated by microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations with a $g$-matrix folding approach by using the matter and transition densities of the target nuclei calculated with AMD. The calculation reasonably reproduces the observed $0^+_1$, $2^+_1$, and $2^+_2$ cross sections of $^{26}$Mg+$p$ scattering at incident energies $E_p=$24 and 40 MeV and of $^{26}$Mg+$α$ scattering at $E_α=$104 and 120 MeV. For $^{10}$Be+$p$ and $^{10}$Be+$α$ scattering, inelastic cross sections to the excited states in the $K^π=0^+_1$ ground-, $K^π=2^+$ side-, $K^π=0^+_2$ cluster-, and $K^π=1^-$ cluster-bands are investigated. The isospin characters of excitations are investigated via inelastic scattering processes by comparison of the production rates in the $^{10}$Be+$p$, $^{10}$Be+$α$, and $^{10}$C+$p$ reactions. The result predicts that the $2^+_2$ state is selectively produced by the $^{10}$Be+$p$ reaction because of the neutron dominance in the $2^+_2$ excitation as in the case of the $^{26}$Mg+$p$ scattering to the $2^+_2$ state, whereas its production is significantly suppressed in the $^{10}$C+$p$ reaction.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $2^+_2$ state in $^{26}$Mg and $^{10}$Be exhibits neutron dominance in its excitation, meaning the neutron transition matrix element exceeds the proton one, based on microscopic coupled-channel calculations using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics densities, with the caveat that the experimental $M_n/M_p$ ratio for the $0^+_1\to 2^+_2$ transition in $^{26}$Mg carries significant uncertainty (estimated as 1.2–4 from prior work).

## Method

The authors performed microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations for proton and alpha inelastic scattering off $^{26}$Mg and $^{10}$Be, using nucleon-nucleus potentials derived by folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction with target densities from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) structure calculations. For $^{26}$Mg, AMD with variation after parity and total angular momentum projection (VAP) was used, while for $^{10}$Be, previously published AMD results were adopted. The transition densities were renormalized by scaling factors to fit experimental $B(E\lambda)$ values and mirror-transition matrix elements before being used in the reaction calculations.

## Key numbers

- For $^{26}$Mg, the calculated $B(E2;2^+_1\to 0^+_1)$ is 63 fm$^4$ (proton component) and $B_n(E2)=39$ fm$^4$ (neutron component), compared to the experimental $B(E2)=61.3\pm2.7$ fm$^4$.
- For $^{26}$Mg, the $2^+_2\to 0^+_1$ transition has theoretical $B(E2)=0.8$ fm$^4$ and $B_n(E2)=5.4$ fm$^4$, with experimental $B(E2)=1.8\pm0.2$ fm$^4$ and $M_n/M_p=1.90$ from mirror analysis.
- For $^{10}$Be, the $2^+_1\to 0^+_1$ transition has theoretical $B(E2)=11.6$ fm$^4$ and $B_n(E2)=8.9$ fm$^4$, with experimental $B(E2)=10.2\pm1.0$ fm$^4$ and $M_n/M_p=1.08$ from mirror symmetry.
- For $^{10}$Be, the $2^+_2\to 0^+_1$ transition has theoretical $B(E2)=0.2$ fm$^4$ and $B_n(E2)=3.2$ fm$^4$, with $M_n/M_p=-3.9$ (indicating opposite sign of proton and neutron matrix elements).
- The $^{26}$Mg+$p$ scattering calculations reproduce observed cross sections at $E_p=24$ and 40 MeV, and $^{26}$Mg+$\alpha$ scattering at $E_\alpha=104$ and 120 MeV.
- The calculated rms matter radius of $^{26}$Mg($0^+_1$) is 3.12 fm, and of $^{10}$Be($0^+_1$) is 2.54 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous MCC studies of $p$ and $\alpha$ inelastic scattering using AMD densities (Kanada-En'yo 2019, 2020) to the specific cases of $^{26}$Mg and $^{10}$Be, where prior experimental analyses by Alexander (1982) and Wiedner (1980) had found strong state-dependent isospin characters but with significant uncertainty in the neutron component of the $2^+_2$ excitation. The results confirm the neutron dominance prediction for the $2^+_2$ state in $^{10}$Be from an earlier AMD study by Kanada-En'yo (2011) and enable predictions for future inverse-kinematics experiments with $^{10}$Be+$p$, $^{10}$Be+$\alpha$, and $^{10}$C+$p$ reactions to probe isospin characters of cluster states.
