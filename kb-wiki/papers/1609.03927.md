# Energy loss as the origin of an universal scaling law of the elliptic flow

> It is shown that the excellent scaling of the elliptic flow found for all centralities, species and energies from RHIC to the LHC for $p_{T}$ less than the saturation momentum is a consequence of the energy lost by a parton interacting with the color field produced in a nucleus-nucleus collision. In particular, the deduced shape of the scaling curve describes correctly all the data. We discuss the possible extensions to higher $p_{T}$, proton-nucleus and proton-proton collisions as well as higher harmonics.

## Key claim

The universal scaling of elliptic flow observed across centralities, particle species, and collision energies from RHIC to the LHC for transverse momenta below the saturation momentum originates from energy loss of partons interacting with the color field of strings or string clusters, with the scaling curve following approximately a τ^{1/3} power law rather than the exact τ^{0.404} best fit.

## Method

The authors model nucleus-nucleus collisions as producing strings between partons of colliding nucleons; as energy or centrality increases, strings overlap into clusters with larger color fields. Partons produced via Schwinger-like pair creation in these fields lose energy through radiative processes as they traverse the nuclear overlap, with the energy loss formula adapted from the QED case of an ultra-relativistic charged particle in a strong external field. The resulting azimuthally anisotropic momentum distribution yields an expression for v₂ proportional to p^{2/3}T^{1/3}εL, which after identifying temperature T with the saturation momentum Q_s^A gives the scaling form v₂/(Q_s^A ε L) ∝ (p_T/Q_s^A)^{2/3} = τ^{1/3}.

## Key numbers

- Best fit to scaling data gives exponent b = 0.404 ± 0.025, compared to the predicted τ^{1/3} (0.333) from the energy loss model.
- Fit coefficient a = 0.1264 ± 0.0076 for the scaling function aτ^b.
- Saturation momentum parameters: Q₀ = 1 GeV, λ = 0.27, √s₀ = 245 GeV.
- String radius approximately 0.2 fm.
- The scaling holds for p_T less than Q_s^A; for central Pb-Pb at LHC, Q_s^A is close to 4 GeV/c.
- At high p_T, LHC data show v₂ ∝ p_T^b with b close to 1/2, suggesting a transition from τ^{1/3} to τ^{1/4} scaling.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous scaling law (Andrés, Braun, Pajares, 2015) by providing a physical mechanism (energy loss in color fields) as its origin, rather than relying on geometrical scaling of transverse momentum distributions. The approach contrasts with hydrodynamic descriptions of anisotropic flow and addresses the challenge of separating initial-state from final-state effects, particularly relevant for the ridge structure observed in A-A, p-Pb, and high-multiplicity pp collisions. The scaling enables predictions for smaller collision systems (p-A, pp) and higher harmonics, though v₃ scaling is found to be approximate and requires additional dynamics beyond energy loss.
