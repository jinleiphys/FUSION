# Possibility of spontaneous parity violation in hot QCD

> We suggest that for QCD in the limit of a large number of colors, N, the axial U(1) symmetry of massless quarks is effectively restored at the deconfining=chiral phase transition. If the deconfining transition is of second order, then the chiral transition is weakly first order. In this case, metastable states in which parity is spontaneously broken appear at temperatures below the phase transition. The production of these metastable states would have dramatic signatures, including enhanced production of eta and eta' mesons, which can decay through parity violating decay processes such as eta -> pi^0 pi^0, and global parity odd asymmetries for charged pions. Using a nonlinear sigma model, in QCD these metastable states only appear rather near the phase transition.

# Possibility of spontaneous parity violation in hot QCD

## Key claim
In the large N limit of QCD, if the deconfining phase transition is of second order, the axial U(1) symmetry is effectively restored at the transition, leading to metastable states with spontaneous parity violation in the hadronic phase. However, using a nonlinear sigma model, these metastable states only appear very near the phase transition and require the ratio a/μ₁² to be about 1% of its zero-temperature value, which may not be achievable in real QCD with N=3.

## Method
The authors use a nonlinear sigma model with a U(N_f) matrix field to incorporate axial U(1) symmetry breaking, analyzing the potential V(U) = (f_π²/2)[tr(M(U+U†)) - a(tr ln U - θ)²]. They study the temperature-dependent behavior of the topological susceptibility λ_YM(T), the η' mass, and the pion decay constant f_π as T approaches T_d from below, using mean field theory scaling relations and Veneziano's relation m²_η'(T) = N_f λ²_η'(T)/N. They numerically diagonalize the mass matrix for three flavors to find metastable solutions with nonzero φ_i fields that spontaneously break parity.

## Key numbers
- The critical ratio for metastable states in the one-flavor case is a_cr/μ² ∼ 0.217, with φ_c ∼ 4.493
- For three flavors, the metastable state becomes unstable in the π⁰ direction unless a < a_cr, with a_cr/μ₁² ∼ 0.2403
- At a_cr, the π⁰ is massless, while m_π± ∼ 106 MeV, m_η ∼ 150 MeV, and m_η' ∼ 687 MeV
- At a=0, m_π⁰ ∼ 114 MeV, m_π± ∼ 140 MeV, m_η ∼ 161 MeV, and m_η' ∼ 687 MeV
- Zero-temperature input masses: μ₁² = (114 MeV)², μ₂² = (161 MeV)², μ₃² = (687 MeV)², and a = (492 MeV)²
- The decay rate of the metastable state is Γ ∝ exp(-F_c/T) with F_c ∼ (32√2/3)(μ₁³ f_π² / a²)
- The critical exponent α ∼ -0.013 for the chiral transition

## Context
This work extends the large N approach to QCD at finite temperature, building on Witten's (1979) treatment of the η' mass and topological susceptibility, and Veneziano's (1980) relation between the η' form factor and the anomaly. It contrasts with instanton-based models where the topological susceptibility remains constant below the phase transition and only drops above it. The paper enables experimental searches for parity-violating signatures in heavy ion collisions, including enhanced η and η' production, parity-forbidden decays such as η → π⁰π⁰, and global parity-odd asymmetries for charged pions, as well as potential cosmological implications for generating magnetic fields at the QCD phase transition as suggested by Son (1998).
