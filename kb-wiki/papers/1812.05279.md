# Stochastic hydrodynamics and long time tails of an expanding conformal charged fluid

> We investigate the impact of hydrodynamic fluctuations on correlation functions in a scale invariant fluid with a conserved $U(1)$ charge. The kinetic equations for the two-point functions of pressure, momentum and heat energy densities are derived within the framework of stochastic hydrodynamics. The leading non-analytic contributions to the energy-momentum tensor as well as the $U(1)$ current are determined from the solutions to these kinetic equations. In the case of a static homogeneous background we show that the long time tails obtained from hydro-kinetic equations reproduce the one-loop results derived from statistical field theory. We use these results to establish bounds on transport coefficients. We generalize the stochastic equation to a background flow undergoing Bjorken expansion. We compute the leading fractional power $\mathcal{O}((τT)^{-3/2})$ correction to the $U(1)$ current and compare with the first order gradient term.

## Key claim

The paper shows that hydrodynamic fluctuations in a conformal charged fluid produce non-analytic "long time tail" corrections to correlation functions, and that these corrections can be computed within a stochastic hydrodynamics framework generalized to a Bjorken-expanding background, yielding a fractional power correction of order (τT)^(-3/2) to the U(1) current that competes with first-order gradient terms.

## Method

The authors linearize the stochastic Navier-Stokes equations around a static homogeneous background and around a Bjorken-expanding background, deriving kinetic equations for the two-point correlation functions of pressure, momentum density, and heat energy density. They solve these equations in the eigenmode basis of the acoustic matrix, compute the response functions of the energy-momentum tensor and U(1) current via variational derivatives with respect to metric and gauge field sources, and extract the leading non-analytic contributions from mode-coupling terms.

## Key numbers

- The leading fractional power correction to the U(1) current in a Bjorken-expanding background is O((τT)^(-3/2)).
- The shear viscosity diffusion constant is defined as γ_η = η_0 / (ε̄ + p̄), where η_0 is the bare shear viscosity and (ε̄ + p̄) is the enthalpy density.
- The diffusion coefficient D_0 = σ_0 α_2, where σ_0 is the bare charge conductivity and α_2 is a thermodynamic coefficient.
- The adiabatic speed of sound for the conformal equation of state is v_a^2 = 1/3.
- The eigenvalues of the acoustic matrix are λ_± = ± i v_a (sound modes) and λ = 0 (diffusive and shear modes).

## Context

This work extends the stochastic hydrodynamics formalism of Akamatsu et al. (2016) to include a conserved U(1) charge, which is important for studying baryon number fluctuations near a possible QCD critical point. The results reproduce the one-loop long-time tails from statistical field theory (Kovtun 2012) in the static homogeneous limit, and provide bounds on transport coefficients. The generalization to Bjorken flow enables future studies of critical fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, complementing numerical stochastic hydrodynamics approaches (Nahrgang 2018) and deterministic methods for n-point functions (Stephanov 2017, Mukherjee 2015).
