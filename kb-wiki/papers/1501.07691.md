# Radiative transition of negative to positive parity nucleon

> We investigate the $N^{\ast}(1535)\rightarrow Nγ$ transition in the framework of light cone QCD sum rules. In particular, using the most general form of the interpolating current for the nucleon as well as the distribution amplitudes of the photon, we calculate two transition form factors responsible for this channel and use them to evaluate the decay width and branching ratio of the transition under consideration. The result obtained for the branching fraction is in a good consistency with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors use light cone QCD sum rules (LCSR) to calculate the transition form factors for the radiative decay \(N^{\ast}(1535) \rightarrow N\gamma\), and their predicted branching ratio of \((0.21 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-2}\) is consistent with the experimental range of \(0.15\%\) to \(0.30\%\) from the Particle Data Group (PDG). The result depends on the choice of a general mixing parameter \(\beta\) in the interpolating current, and the authors identify working regions for this parameter where the form factors are stable.

## Method

The authors construct a two-point correlation function in the presence of a background photon field, using the most general interpolating current for the nucleon that couples to both positive and negative parity states. They compute the hadronic side by saturating with intermediate \(N\) and \(N^{\ast}\) states and parameterizing the transition matrix elements in terms of form factors \(f_1^T\) and \(f_2^T\). The OPE side is calculated using photon distribution amplitudes (DAs) of increasing twist, including perturbative, mixed, and non-perturbative contributions. After applying a double Borel transformation and continuum subtraction, the two sides are matched to obtain sum rules for the form factors at zero momentum transfer (\(q^2 = 0\)).

## Key numbers

* Transition form factors at \(q^2 = 0\): \(f_1^T = 1.26 \pm 0.37\) and \(f_2^T = 11.18 \pm 3.35\) (dimensionless, from Table 1).
* Decay width: \(\Gamma = (3.08 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{-4}\) GeV for the \(N^{\ast}(1535) \rightarrow N\gamma\) transition.
* Branching ratio: \(Br = (0.21 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-2}\) (i.e., \(0.21\% \pm 0.06\%\)), compared to the PDG experimental range of \(0.15\%\) to \(0.30\%\).
* Borel parameter working window: \(2 \text{ GeV}^2 \leq M^2 \leq 4 \text{ GeV}^2\) for the transition sum rules.
* Continuum threshold for \(N^{\ast}\): \(2.51 \text{ GeV}^2 \leq s_0 \leq 2.73 \text{ GeV}^2\).
* Residue parametrizations: \(\lambda_N^2(\beta) = e^{m_N^2/M^{\prime 2}} [2.28 - 3.20\beta + 64.10\beta^2] \times 10^{-6} \text{ GeV}^6\) and \(\lambda_{N^{\ast}}^2(\beta) = e^{m_{N^{\ast}}^2/M^{\prime 2}} [-7.38 - 4.20\beta + 2.82\beta^2] \times 10^{-5} \text{ GeV}^6\).

## Context

This work extends previous LCSR studies of electromagnetic transitions of negative parity baryons, such as the \(\gamma^\ast N \to N(1535)\) and \(\gamma^\ast N \to N^{\ast}(1520)\) transitions by Savci et al. (2010s) and the electromagnetic transitions of octet negative parity to octet positive parity baryons by the same group. It also builds on QCD sum rule calculations of the magnetic moments of negative parity baryons by Aliev (2000s) and on lattice QCD studies of nucleon distribution amplitudes by Braun (2000s). The results enable comparison with planned photo-production and electro-production experiments at Jefferson Laboratory and the Mainz Microton facility, and provide a theoretical prediction for the branching ratio that can be tested against more precise future data.
