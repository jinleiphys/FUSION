# Covariant kinetic freeze out description through a finite space-time layer

> We develop and analyze a covariant FO probability valid for a finite space-time layer.

## Key claim

The paper develops a covariant freeze-out probability valid for a finite space-time layer, rather than the usual assumption of an infinitely thin freeze-out hypersurface. The key hedge is that the approach is an approximation based on an "escape rate" that replaces the exact freeze-out probability, because the exact gain term in the Boltzmann equation cannot be calculated from first principles.

## Method

The authors split the distribution function into interacting and free components, then derive coupled equations from the relativistic Boltzmann Transport Equation. They approximate the freeze-out probability by an escape rate that depends on the remaining distance across the FO layer of thickness L, the particle momentum relative to the FO normal, and the flow velocity. The resulting covariant escape rate is \( \mathcal{P}_{esc} = \frac{1}{\lambda} \left( \frac{L}{L - x^\mu d\sigma_\mu} \right) \left( \frac{p^\mu d\sigma_\mu}{p^\mu u_\mu} \right) \Theta(p^\mu d\sigma_\mu) \), where \(\lambda\) is the initial characteristic length of the system.

## Key numbers

- FO layer thickness: \(L\) (no numerical value given; treated as a parameter)
- Initial characteristic length of the system: \(\lambda\) (no numerical value given; treated as a parameter)
- The escape rate factor \(W(p) = p^\mu d\sigma_\mu / p^\mu u_\mu\) equals 1 at point A on the hypersurface where \(d\sigma_\mu = u_\mu = (1,0,0,0)\)
- The contour plot shows \(W(p)=0\) when crossing the light cone from point C to point D in the Rest Frame of the Front

## Context

This work extends the covariant freeze-out description of Csernai and collaborators (2004-2005) by introducing a finite space-time layer instead of an infinitely thin hypersurface. It contradicts the common practice of assuming Jüttner or cut-Jüttner distributions as post-freeze-out distributions, showing these are generally not valid except when the FO normal is parallel to the local flow velocity. The approach enables more realistic calculations of post-FO momentum distributions that are non-equilibrated and anisotropic, applicable to both time-like and space-like freeze-out situations in high-energy heavy ion reactions.
