# Aharonov-Bohm effect and nucleon-nucleon phase shifts on the lattice

> We propose a method for the lattice QCD computation of nucleon-nucleon low-energy interactions. It consists in simulating QCD in the background of a ''electromagnetic" field whose potential is non-vanishing, but whose field strength is zero. By tuning the background field, phase-shifts at any (but small) momenta can be determined by measuring the shift of the ground state energy. Lattice sizes as small as 5 Fermi can be sufficient for the calculation of phase shifts up to momenta of order of $m_π/2$.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a method for lattice QCD computation of nucleon-nucleon low-energy interactions by simulating QCD in the background of a fictitious "electromagnetic" field with zero field strength. The method claims that lattice sizes as small as 5 fm can be sufficient for calculating phase shifts up to momenta of order mπ/2, but this is conditional on tuning the background field to values around φ ≈ π, and the approach is limited to momenta smaller than about half the pion mass (corresponding to an energy scale of about 6 MeV).

## Method
The method consists of simulating QCD on a finite torus in the presence of a U(1) background gauge field coupling to baryon number, with vector potential A = (φ/3L)ẑ, which produces an Aharonov-Bohm effect. By tuning the background field strength φ, the ground state energy of the two-nucleon system shifts, and the phase shifts at any small momentum can be extracted from the finite-volume energy levels using a modified Luscher formula: kL cot δ = (1/π) S(k²L²/4π², φ), where S is a known function of the momentum and the background field. The approach is equivalent to using twisted boundary conditions for the quark fields in one spatial direction.

## Key numbers
- Scattering lengths: 5.42 fm in the spin triplet channel and 23.7 fm in the spin singlet channel
- Effective range parameters used: singlet as = 23.7 fm, r0s = 2.73 fm; triplet at = 5.425 fm, r0t = 1.75 fm
- Lattice sizes as small as 5 fm can be used, compared to the 8-10 fm required without the background field
- The method is valid for momenta k < mπ/2, corresponding to an energy scale of about 6 MeV
- For L = 5 fm, finite pion mass effects are suppressed by e^{-mπL} ≈ 0.03

## Context
This work extends the finite-volume method of Luscher (Luscher 1986, 1991) for extracting phase shifts from lattice QCD, which previously required lattice sizes much larger than the scattering length (L ≫ a). The author's prior analysis (NPLQCD 2003) found that lattice sizes L ∼ 8 fm were necessary for the ground state to have small enough energy for the method to be valid. The new method enables smaller lattice sizes and provides information about phase shifts at any momenta smaller than mπ/2, rather than at a single kinematical point. The approach could also be applied to other hadronic interactions such as pion-pion scattering, though this would spoil positivity of the determinant and likely be feasible only in quenched calculations.
