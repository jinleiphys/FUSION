# Complex scaling : physics of unbound light nuclei and perspective

> The complex scaling method (CSM) is one of the most powerful methods of describing the resonances with complex energy eigenstates, based on non-Hermitian quantum mechanics. We present the basic application of CSM to the properties of the unbound phenomena of light nuclei. In particular, we focus on many-body resonant and non-resonant continuum states observed in unstable nuclei. We also investigate the continuum level density (CLD) in the scattering problem in terms of the Green's function with CSM. We discuss the explicit effects of resonant and non-resonant contributions in CLD and transition strength functions.

## Key claim

The complex scaling method (CSM) provides a powerful framework for describing many-body resonances and non-resonant continuum states in light unstable nuclei, but its practical implementation requires a sufficiently large number of basis functions to make the dependence on the scaling parameter theta negligible in the calculated solutions.

## Method

The authors apply the complex scaling transformation to the many-body Hamiltonian and wave functions, rotating all radial coordinates by a complex angle theta, which converts exponentially divergent resonance wave functions into square-integrable forms. They solve the complex-scaled Schrodinger equation using a finite set of Gaussian basis functions, obtaining eigenvalues classified as bound states, resonant states, and rotated continuum states on the 2-theta lines in the complex energy plane. The complex-scaled Green's function is constructed from these eigenstates using the extended completeness relation, enabling spectral decomposition of the continuum level density and strength functions into bound, resonant, and continuum contributions.

## Key numbers

- The schematic potential model uses V(r) = -8 exp(-0.16 r^2) + 4 exp(-0.04 r^2) to demonstrate CSM for two-body systems
- The 0+_3 resonant state in the schematic model has resonance energy E_r = 1.63 MeV and width Gamma = 0.246 MeV
- The complex-scaled wave function for the 0+_3 state shows damping behavior around 10 fm when using a scaling angle of 10 degrees

## Context

This work extends the foundational ABC theorem of Aguilar, Balslev, and Combes (1971) and the earlier Gamow (1928) and Siegert (1939) treatments of resonances to many-body systems relevant to unstable nuclear physics. The Hokkaido group's developments (Aoyama 2001, 2006; Ho 2012; Myo 2014) are followed to explain basic CSM properties. The method enables calculation of scattering phase shifts via continuum level density and the complex-scaled Lippmann-Schwinger equation for many-body reaction cross sections, applicable to Borromean three-body systems such as 6He and 11Li where no two-body subsystem has bound states.
