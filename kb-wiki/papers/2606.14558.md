# Tomography of Atomic Nuclei

> We carry out continuum quantum Monte Carlo calculations of the quantum-mechanical Wigner distribution functions of selected nuclei, up to $^{16}$O. These distributions provide a form of quantum tomography of the spatial and momentum structure of the system. They also help identify the location of high-momentum regions in atomic nuclei and provide insight into the onset of alpha clustering. Besides their intrinsic interest, these distributions will be useful for neutrino event generators, as they correlate the positions and momenta of nucleons in the initial target state. To facilitate their application, we address the need to store them compactly by developing an accurate Gaussian Process emulator that automatically preserves their normalization.

## Key claim
This work presents the first *ab initio* calculations of Wigner quasi-probability distributions for nuclei up to ¹⁶O using continuum quantum Monte Carlo methods, but the results are limited to variational Monte Carlo and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo approaches (not Green's function Monte Carlo), and the Wigner functions are computed with the caveat that AFDMC uses an asymmetric estimator for the bra and ket wave functions while the symmetric estimator is only available at the variational level.

## Method
The authors compute single-nucleon Wigner distributions from variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) wave functions using the Argonne v₁₈ or its reprojection Argonne v′₈ nucleon-nucleon interactions supplemented by Urbana IX or Urbana X three-nucleon forces. The Wigner function is evaluated by binning the off-diagonal one-body density matrix in coordinate space as a function of the distance |r| = |r_i + s/2|, with the symmetric displacement of bra and ket wave functions applied only at the VMC level. To enable compact storage for neutrino event generator applications, a Gaussian Process emulator is developed that enforces the integral normalization constraint ∫ dk/(2π)³ dr W_N(r,k) = A_N through a conditioned GP with a tapered squared-exponential kernel.

## Key numbers
- Calculations performed for selected nuclei up to ¹⁶O
- Number of generated configurations ranges from 10⁶ for ³He to 10⁴ for ¹²C
- Box size for trial moves: 1.2–1.4 fm, yielding approximately 50% acceptance rate
- Short-range regulator parameter c = 2.1 fm⁻² in the Urbana three-nucleon force
- Saturation density of isospin-symmetric nucleonic matter: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of nuclear Wigner distributions that were limited to the deuteron (Neff, 2016) or performed within mean-field approaches (Prakash, 1981; Bonasera, 1993; Nikolakopoulos, 2025) or lowest-order correlation operator approximations (Cosyn, 2021). The quantum Monte Carlo treatment treats long- and short-range correlations on the same footing, enabling the study of alpha clustering and high-momentum regions. The resulting Wigner distributions are intended for incorporation into the ACHILLES neutrino event generator (Isaacson, 2020; Isaacson, 2022), replacing the current approximation where initial momenta are sampled independently of position.
