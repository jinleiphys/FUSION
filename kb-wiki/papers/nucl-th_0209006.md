# A Systematic Investigation of Light Heavy-Ion Reactions

> We introduce a novel coupling potential for the scattering of deformed light heavy-ion reactions. This new approach is based on replacing the usual first-derivative coupling potential by a new, second derivative coupling potential in the coupled-channels formalism. The new approach has been successfully applied to the study of the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg, $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si and $^{16}$O+$^{24}$Mg systems and made major improvements over all the previous coupled-channels calculations for these systems. This paper also shows the limitations of the standard coupled-channels theory and presents a global solution to the problems faced in the previous theoretical accounts of these reactions.

# A Systematic Investigation of Light Heavy-Ion Reactions

## Key claim
The paper claims that replacing the standard first-derivative coupling potential with a new second-derivative coupling potential in the coupled-channels formalism provides a global solution to long-standing problems in light heavy-ion reactions, including anomalous large-angle scattering, oscillatory structure mismatches, and physically unjustifiable deformation parameters. However, the origin of this new coupling potential remains speculative and requires microscopic understanding.

## Method
The author applied the coupled-channels formalism with a deformed optical potential, replacing the usual first-derivative coupling potential with a second-derivative coupling potential parameterized as the second derivative of a Woods-Saxon shape. Calculations were performed for the ¹²C+¹²C, ¹²C+²⁴Mg, ¹⁶O+²⁸Si, and ¹⁶O+²⁴Mg systems using an extensively modified version of the code CHUCK, with empirical deformation parameters (β₂ values) rather than the artificially inflated values required by standard calculations.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁶O+²⁸Si: real potential depth V₀ = 706.5 MeV, radius parameter r₀ = 0.7490 fm, diffuseness a = 1.40 fm; imaginary volume potential W_V = 59.9 MeV with a_V = 0.127 fm, surface potential W_S = 25.0 MeV with a_S = 0.257 fm; deformation parameter β₂ = -0.64 for ²⁸Si
- For ¹⁶O+²⁸Si: new coupling potential parameters V = 155.0 MeV, R = 4.160 fm, a = 0.81 fm
- For ¹⁶O+²⁸Si: imaginary radii increase linearly with energy as R_V = 0.06084 E_CM - 0.442 and R_S = 0.2406 E_CM - 2.191 (units in fm, E_CM in MeV)
- ¹²C+¹²C system studied at laboratory energies from 20.0 to 126.7 MeV
- ¹⁶O+²⁸Si system studied at laboratory energies from 29.0 to 142.5 MeV
- For ¹²C+¹²C: standard coupled-channels model underestimates the mutual-2⁺ channel magnitude by a factor of at least two

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channels analyses of light heavy-ion reactions previously attempted by Kobos and Satchler (1984) for ¹⁶O+²⁸Si using double-folding potentials, and by various authors for ¹²C+¹²C (Wolf, Sakuragi, Fry). The paper addresses six persistent problems including anomalous large-angle scattering (ALAS), oscillatory structure mismatches near the Coulomb barrier, and physically unjustifiable deformation parameters. The new coupling potential enables simultaneous fits to elastic and inelastic scattering data, excitation functions, and mutual excitation channels across multiple systems, though the author notes the approach requires microscopic theoretical justification.
