# Analysis of nuclear structure in a converging expansion scheme

> In the framework of the KIDS generalized energy density functional (EDF), the nuclear equation of state (EoS) is expressed as an expansion in powers of the Fermi momentum or the cubic root of the density ($ρ^{1/3}$). Although an optimal number of converging terms was obtained in specific cases of fits to empirical data and pseudodata, the degree of convergence remains to be examined not only for homogeneous matter but also for finite nuclei. One goal of the present work is to validate the minimal and optimal number of EoS parameters required for the description of homogeneous nuclear matter over a wide range of densities relevant for astrophysical applications. The major goal is to examine the validity of the adopted expansion scheme for an accurate description of finite nuclei. To this end we vary the values of the high-order derivatives of the EoS, namely the skewness of the energy of symmetric nuclear matter and the kurtosis of the symmetry energy, at saturation and examine the relative importance of each term in $ρ^{1/3}$ expansion for homogeneous matter. For given sets of EoS parameters determined in this way, we define equivalent Skyrme-type functionals and examine the convergence in the description of finite nuclei focusing on the masses and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei. The EoS of symmetric nuclear matter is found to be efficiently parameterized with only 3 parameters and the symmetry energy (or the energy of pure neutron matter) with 4 parameters when the EoS is expanded in the power series of the Fermi momentum. Higher-order EoS parameters do not produce any improvement, in practice, in the description of nuclear ground-state energies and charge radii, which means that they cannot be constrained by bulk properties of nuclei.

## Key claim
The KIDS energy density functional expansion in powers of the Fermi momentum (or ρ^(1/3)) converges efficiently for symmetric nuclear matter with only 3 parameters and for the symmetry energy with 4 parameters; higher-order EoS parameters such as the skewness of symmetric matter and the kurtosis of the symmetry energy do not improve the description of nuclear ground-state energies and charge radii and therefore cannot be constrained by bulk properties of nuclei.

## Method
The authors varied the values of high-order density derivatives of the nuclear EoS at saturation, specifically the skewness Q0 of symmetric nuclear matter and the kurtosis Rsym of the symmetry energy, and examined the relative importance of each term in the ρ^(1/3) expansion for homogeneous matter. For given sets of EoS parameters, they defined equivalent Skyrme-type functionals and performed Hartree-Fock calculations to examine convergence in the description of finite nuclei, focusing on masses and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei from 16O to 208Pb.

## Key numbers
- Symmetric nuclear matter EoS is efficiently parameterized with only 3 parameters; the symmetry energy requires 4 parameters when expanded in powers of the Fermi momentum.
- For the baseline KIDS-ad2 set: saturation density ρ0 = 0.16 fm^(-3), energy per particle E0 = -16.0 MeV, compression modulus K0 = 240 MeV, skewness Q0 ≈ -373 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32.8 MeV, slope L = 49.3 MeV, Ksym = -156 MeV, Qsym = 583 MeV, Rsym = -2470 MeV.
- Varying Q0 between -360, -390, and -420 MeV produced binding energy deviations of at most 0.179% for 208Pb and charge radius deviations of at most 0.313% for 208Pb relative to experimental data.
- Neutron star maximum masses predicted with different Q0 values ranged from 2.04 to 2.13 solar masses, all allowing a 2 solar mass neutron star.
- For pure neutron matter fits to the APR EoS, the χ² per point improved from 5.3×10^(-4) with 3 parameters (P3) to 1.4×10^(-4) with 4 parameters (P4), but adding a 5th or 6th parameter gave no further improvement (χ² ≈ 6.3-6.5×10^(-5)).

## Context
This work extends the KIDS generalized energy density functional approach developed in prior publications by Gil, Papakonstantinou, Hyun, Oh and collaborators (2016-2018), which had established a baseline parameterization (KIDS-ad2) by fitting to the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) EoS and empirical saturation properties. The present study systematically tests whether adding higher-order terms beyond the minimal set (3 for symmetric matter, 4 for neutron matter) improves finite-nucleus predictions, finding they do not. This enables future efforts to refine the nuclear EDF by focusing on parameters not active in unpolarized homogeneous matter, such as effective tensor terms and time-odd terms, rather than adding more density-dependent terms.
