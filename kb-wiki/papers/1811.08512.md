# Basis light front quantization for the charged light mesons with color singlet Nambu--Jona-Lasinio interactions

> We apply the basis light front quantization (BLFQ) approach to describe the valence structures of the charged light meson ground states. Specifically, the light front wavefunctions of $π^\pm$, $ρ^\pm$, $K^\pm$, and $K^{*\pm}$ are obtained as the eigenvectors of the light front effective Hamiltonians with confinement potentials supplemented by the color singlet Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) interactions. We adjust our model such that the spectrum of these ground states and the charge radii of the pseudoscalar states agree with experimental results. We present the elastic form factors and parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) as illustrations of the internal structures of the pseudoscalar pions and kaons in terms of valence quarks.

## Key claim

The BLFQ-NJL model reproduces the mass spectrum of charged light meson ground states and the charge radii of pseudoscalar states by adjusting quark masses, confining strengths, and NJL coupling constants, but only within a truncated basis and under the assumption that vector meson masses are insensitive to the NJL interactions, being instead dominated by confinement potentials.

## Method

The authors solve the light front Schrödinger equation for the valence Fock sector of π±, ρ±, K±, and K*± using basis light front quantization (BLFQ), expanding the light front wavefunctions in a 2D harmonic oscillator basis for transverse momenta and Jacobi polynomials for longitudinal momentum fractions. The effective Hamiltonian includes kinematic terms, transverse and longitudinal confinement potentials, and color singlet Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) four-fermion interactions, with matrix elements evaluated analytically. The model parameters are constrained by fitting the ground state masses from the Particle Data Group and the charge radii of π+ and K+ from elastic form factor data.

## Key numbers

- π+ mass: 139.6 MeV (fitted to PDG value)
- ρ+ mass: 775.3 MeV (fitted to PDG value)
- K+ mass: 493.7 MeV (fitted to PDG value)
- K*+ mass: 891.7 MeV (fitted to PDG value)
- π+ charge radius: 0.672 fm (fitted to experimental data from Amendolia (1984, 1986))
- K+ charge radius: 0.560 fm (fitted to experimental data from Dally (1980) and Amendolia (1986))
- Basis truncation: Nmax = 8, Mmax = 2, Lmax = 8 (default for spectrum; Lmax increased for parton distribution amplitudes)
- Confining strength κ: set equal to the scale parameter b in the transverse basis functions

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach, previously applied to positronium (Maris 2013) and heavy quarkonium (Li 2015, 2016, 2017), to light mesons by incorporating color singlet NJL interactions as the effective quark dynamics, following earlier NJL studies using Bethe–Salpeter equations (Carrillo-Serrano 2015, Hutauruk 2016). The results enable further calculations of elastic form factors, decay constants, and parton distribution amplitudes for light mesons within a light front Hamiltonian framework, and can be compared with lattice QCD (Aoki 2016) and other nonperturbative approaches.
