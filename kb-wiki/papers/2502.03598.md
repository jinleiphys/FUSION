# Higgs Thermal Nonequilibrium in Primordial QGP

> In this work we investigate the chemical and kinetic nonequilibrium dynamics of the Higgs boson during the primordial Universe QGP (quark-gluon plasma) epoch $130\mathrm{\,GeV}>T>10\mathrm{\,GeV}$. We show that the Higgs bosons is always out of chemical abundance equilibrium with a fugacity $Υ_h = 0.69$ due to virtual decay channels. Additionally, Higgs momentum distribution is found to be ``cold'' for $T<25$\,GeV, since the scattering rate drops below the production rate.

## Key claim

The Higgs boson in the primordial quark-gluon plasma (QGP) epoch (130 GeV > T > 10 GeV) is always out of chemical abundance equilibrium with a fugacity Υ_h = 0.69 due to broken detailed balance, and its momentum distribution becomes "cold" for T < 25 GeV because the scattering rate drops below the production rate.

## Method

The authors solved the Higgs population equation in the expanding early Universe, considering production via bottom-quark fusion (inverse decay) and depletion via decay into virtual W* and Z* boson pairs. They computed thermal reaction rates for Higgs production, decay, and kinetic scattering (Compton-like scattering with bottom and top quarks) using tree-level Feynman diagrams and Boltzmann approximations, then compared these rates to the Hubble expansion parameter to determine equilibrium conditions.

## Key numbers

- Higgs fugacity in primordial QGP: Υ_h = 0.69, constant across the temperature range 125 GeV > T > 0.1 GeV
- Higgs mass: m_H = 124 GeV; W boson mass: m_W = 80.4 GeV; Z boson mass: m_Z = 91.2 GeV
- Higgs total decay width: Γ_decay = 3.7 MeV
- Branching ratios: bottom decay channel 53 ± 8%, W decay channel 25.7 ± 2.5%, Z decay channel 2.8 ± 0.3%
- Crossover temperature where scattering rate equals fusion rate: T = 25 GeV
- Crossover temperature where top quark scattering dominates over bottom quark scattering: T = 17 GeV
- Heavy particle to baryon density ratio at T = 10 GeV: approximately 10^5

## Context

This work extends the study of chemical nonequilibrium in QGP, originally developed for strangeness in heavy-ion collisions by Biro (1981) and Rafelski (1982), and formalized with fugacity parameters by Letessier (1993). The Higgs presents a unique case where detailed balance is broken because its mass lies below the WW and ZZ pair threshold, causing decay into virtual bosons without a corresponding back-reaction. This enables both chemical and kinetic nonequilibrium simultaneously, which the authors note is the only recognized system allowing kinetic nonequilibrium in the presence of chemical equilibrium within relativistic plasma physics. The findings suggest that electroweak baryogenesis could persist longer than previously assumed, referencing reviews by Canetti, Drewes, and Shaposhnikov (2012) and Morrissey and Ramsey-Musolf (2012).
