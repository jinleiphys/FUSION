# Dilepton production from resonance scattering in hot hadronic matter

> Dilepton production from resonance scattering in hot hadronic matter is studied. Including the widths of these resonances, which enhance the phase space for dilepton production, we find that the production rate is significantly increased if a resonance appears in the extended phase space. For the reaction $π+ρ\to l^++l^-$, the finite $ρ$ meson width extends the invariant mass below the $ω$-meson mass, so a peak at the $ω$ meson mass is seen in the dilepton spectrum. Similarly, a $ρ$-meson peak appears in the reaction $ρ+ρ\to l^++l^-$. On the other hand, the effect of particle widths in the reaction $π+a_1\to l^++l^-$ is small since the extended phase space does not include any resonance.

## Key claim

Including the finite widths of colliding hadrons (such as the rho meson) in the calculation of dilepton production from hot hadronic matter significantly increases the production rate when a resonance appears in the extended phase space; for the reaction π+ρ → l⁺+l⁻, the finite ρ meson width extends the invariant mass below the ω-meson mass, allowing an ω peak to appear in the dilepton spectrum that would otherwise be absent.

## Method

The authors calculated the dilepton production rate from hot hadronic matter using an effective chiral Lagrangian with vector meson dominance (VMD) and the gauged Wess-Zumino term. They replaced the on-mass-shell approximation for colliding particles with spectral functions of Breit-Wigner form for the ρ meson (width Γ_ρ = 150 MeV) and a delta function for the pion, then integrated the production rate formula (Eq. 7) numerically at T = 150 MeV. For the π+ρ reaction, they included interference between ω and φ meson intermediate states, and for the ω meson collisional broadening they used a Breit-Wigner cross section for π+ω → b₁(1236) scattering.

## Key numbers

- ρ meson width in free space: Γ_ρ = 150 MeV; a₁ meson width in free space: Γ_{a₁} ≈ 400 MeV
- ω meson width in free space: Γ_ω ≈ 8.5 MeV; calculated Γ(ω → π⁰γ) = 0.80 MeV, compared to experimental value 0.86 ± 0.05 MeV
- φ meson width from ρπ decay: Γ_{exp}(φ → ρπ) ≈ 0.62 MeV, giving mixing parameter |ε| = 0.076
- Collisional broadening of ω meson width at T = 150 MeV: Γ^{coll} ≈ 12 MeV; collisional broadening of ρ meson width at T = 150 MeV: Γ_{coll} ≈ 25 MeV; collisional broadening of φ meson width at T = 150 MeV: Γ_{coll} ≈ 10 MeV
- Temperature used for all numerical results: T = 150 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production from hadronic matter using effective Lagrangians, such as those by Kapusta (1989) and Lichard (1994), by including the finite widths of colliding particles which were previously neglected. The authors had earlier shown (Song, Ko, and Lee, 1995) that the pion electromagnetic form factor is reduced at finite temperature, suppressing dilepton production from pion-pion annihilation; the present paper adds the complementary effect of particle widths. The results enable more accurate modeling of the hadronic background in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, which is necessary for identifying signatures of the quark-gluon plasma and precursor phenomena related to chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement.
