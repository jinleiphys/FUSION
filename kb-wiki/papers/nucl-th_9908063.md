# An Update on NA50 and LUCIFER

> Recent analysis of both new (1998) and old (1995,1996) data obtained by the NA50 Collaboration on J/Psi suppression in Pb+Pb are examined in light of our already existing calculations with the relativistic heavy ion simulation LUCIFER. In particular we comment on the unexplained change of transverse energy scale by NA50 and the apparent disappearance from the data of discontinuities in the suppression with transverse energy.

# An Update on NA50 and LUCIFER

## Key claim

The apparent discontinuity in J/ψ suppression as a function of transverse energy (Et) seen in earlier NA50 Pb+Pb data disappears when the 1998 data with a thinner target are included and a minimum-bias smoothing procedure is applied; the authors argue that this absence of a singularity is consistent with their cascade-based LUCIFER simulation and that any claim for a phase-transition signature is not justified by the present data.

## Method

The authors compare their existing LUCIFER relativistic heavy-ion cascade simulation results for J/ψ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon against the combined NA50 data sets from 1995, 1996, and 1998, focusing on the ratio of J/ψ to Drell-Yan production as a function of neutral transverse energy. They also compare the LUCIFER transverse energy distribution to NA49 inclusive hadron spectra to validate the simulation's Et scale, and present an unscaled Et comparison between LUCIFER and the new NA50 QM'99 results.

## Key numbers

- The LUCIFER simulation used a transverse energy scaling factor of approximately 1.25 to reconcile the theoretical and experimental Et spectra in earlier comparisons (Kahana 1998).
- The overall scaling factor between LUCIFER and the NA50 1996 Et scale was close to 1.30, based on the discrepancy between the predicted cutoff near 120 GeV and the earlier published NA50 cutoff of 150-160 GeV.
- The normalization of the J/ψ to Drell-Yan ratio is subject to an estimated error of 7% or less from uncertainties in elementary cross-sections.
- A slightly revised value of approximately 5% for the Drell-Yan denominator was used in the unscaled comparison, consistent with uncertainties in elementary cross-sections.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LUCIFER cascade calculations (Kahana and Kahana, 1999; Kahana and Kahana, 1998) and directly addresses the NA50 Collaboration's claims of a discontinuity in J/ψ suppression that had been interpreted as evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation. The paper contradicts phase-transition-based explanations by showing that the cascade model, which uses only known hadronic cross-sections and a single formation-time parameter determined from S+S collisions, reproduces the NA49 inclusive meson spectra and the NA50 J/ψ suppression pattern without discontinuities. The analysis enables future comparisons at RHIC energies, where the authors argue that demonstrating a clear deviation from cascade background will be necessary for any plasma claim.
