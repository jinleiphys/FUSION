# Relativistic description of exotic collective excitation phenomena in atomic nuclei

> The low-lying dipole and quadrupole states in neutron rich nuclei, are studied within the fully self-consistent relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA), formulated in the canonical basis of the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model (RHB), which is extended to include the density dependent interactions. In heavier nuclei, the low-lying E1 excited state is identified as a pygmy dipole resonance (PDR), i.e. as a collective mode of excess neutrons oscillating against a proton-neutron core. Isotopic dependence of the PDR is characterized by a crossing between the PDR and one-neutron separation energies. Already at moderate proton-neutron asymmetry the PDR peak is calculated above the neutron emission threshold, indicating important implications for the observation of the PDR in (gamma,gamma') scattering, and on the theoretical predictions of the radiative neutron capture rates in neutron-rich nuclei. In addition, a novel method is suggested for determining the neutron skin of nuclei, based on measurement of excitation energies of the Gamow-Teller resonance relative to the isobaric analog state.

## Key claim

The low-lying E1 state in heavier neutron-rich nuclei is identified as a collective pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) of excess neutrons oscillating against a proton-neutron core, but only for medium-heavy and heavy nuclei; in light nuclei like oxygen isotopes the low-energy dipole strength instead arises from non-resonant single-particle excitations of loosely bound neutrons and does not correspond to a collective PDR mode.

## Method

The authors employed the fully self-consistent relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) formulated in the canonical basis of the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model, using the density-dependent effective interaction DD-ME1 for the particle-hole channel and the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S for the pairing channel. For charge-exchange excitations, the model was extended to the proton-neutron RQRPA (PN-RQRPA) including pion exchange with pseudo-vector coupling and a zero-range Landau-Migdal term with parameter g' = 0.55. The two-quasiparticle configuration space included bound states, states with one nucleon in the continuum, and states with both nucleons in the continuum.

## Key numbers

- In 132Sn, the pygmy dipole resonance peak is found at 7.8 MeV, exhausting 1.6% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR), where the enhancement factor kappa = 0.35.
- The isovector giant dipole resonance in 132Sn is calculated at 15.2 MeV.
- For Sn isotopes, the PDR excitation energy crosses the one-neutron separation energy at A = 124: for A < 124 the PDR lies below the neutron emission threshold, while for A >= 124 the PDR is above the threshold.
- In 22O, the low-lying 2+ state is calculated at 3.0 MeV, compared to the observed value of 3.2 MeV.
- Integrated energy-weighted dipole strength up to 15 MeV in oxygen isotopes (in % of TRK sum rule): 18O = 11.7%, 20O = 11.9%, 22O = 15.2%, 24O = 20.9%.

## Context

This work extends the earlier RHB+RQRPA studies of Paar et al. (2003) and Vretenar et al. (2001) by using the density-dependent interaction DD-ME1, which improves the description of neutron skin and isovector properties compared to interactions with nonlinear sigma-meson self-interaction. The results are compared with non-relativistic approaches including continuum QRPA based on HFB ground state (Matsuo, 2001), shell model calculations (Sagawa, 2001), and QRPA plus phonon coupling (Colo, 2001). The finding that PDR excitation energies cross the one-neutron separation energy at moderate asymmetry has implications for the observability of the PDR in (gamma,gamma') experiments and for radiative neutron capture rate calculations in neutron-rich nuclei. The proposed method to determine neutron skin thickness from the energy difference between Gamow-Teller resonance and isobaric analog state extends the work of Vretenar et al. (2003).
