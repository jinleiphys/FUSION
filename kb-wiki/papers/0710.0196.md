# Faddeev calculation of pentaquark $Θ^+$ in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model-based diquark picture

> A Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev (BSF) calculation is performed for the pentaquark $Θ^+$ in the diquark picture of Jaffe and Wilczek in which $Θ^+$ is a diquark-diquark-${\bar s}$ three-body system. Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is used to calculate the lowest order diagrams in the two-body scatterings of ${\bar s}D$ and $D D$. With the use of coupling constants determined from the meson sector, we find that ${\bar s}D$ interaction is attractive in s-wave while $DD$ interaction is repulsive in p-wave. With only the lowest three-body channel considered, we do not find a bound $ \frac 12^+$ pentaquark state. Instead, a bound pentaquark $Θ^+$ with $ \frac 12^-$ is obtained with a unphysically strong vector mesonic coupling constants.

## Key claim

Using a Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev calculation within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model-based diquark picture of Jaffe and Wilczek, the authors find that with coupling constants determined from the meson sector, no bound \( \frac{1}{2}^+ \) pentaquark state exists; a bound \( \Theta^+ \) with \( \frac{1}{2}^- \) is obtained only when unphysically strong vector mesonic coupling constants are used.

## Method

The authors performed a Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev (BSF) calculation for the pentaquark \( \Theta^+ \) treated as a three-body system of two scalar, isoscalar, color \( \mathbf{\bar 3} \) diquarks and a strange antiquark. The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model was used to compute the lowest-order two-body interactions for the \( \bar{s}D \) and \( DD \) channels, with the Blankenbecler-Sugar reduction scheme applied to convert the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equations into three-dimensional ones. Only the lowest three-body channel was considered, with the \( \bar{s}D \) subsystem in relative s-wave and the \( DD \) subsystem in relative p-wave to satisfy antisymmetry requirements.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses used: \( M_{u,d} = 400 \) MeV, \( M_s = 600 \) MeV; diquark mass \( M_D = 600 \) MeV.
- Dipole form factor parameters for the \( \bar{s}D \) and \( DD \) interactions: cutoff \( \Lambda = 0.84 \) GeV; for the scalar form factor, \( F_s(0) = c_s = 0.53 \) GeV.
- The \( \bar{s}D \) interaction was found to be attractive in s-wave, while the \( DD \) interaction was repulsive in p-wave.
- No bound \( \frac{1}{2}^+ \) pentaquark state was found; a bound \( \Theta^+ \) with \( \frac{1}{2}^- \) was obtained only with unphysically strong vector mesonic coupling constants.

## Context

This work extends the diquark picture of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) by providing a dynamical, relativistic Faddeev calculation using the NJL model, which had previously been applied successfully to nucleon properties (Huang 1994, Ishii 1995, Asami 1995, Mineo 1999). The study contradicts the original expectation of a \( \frac{1}{2}^+ \) pentaquark state and instead finds that a bound state requires unrealistic coupling strengths, consistent with the experimental uncertainty surrounding the \( \Theta^+ \) after null results from BELLE (2004) and HERA-B (2004). This enables future work to explore higher three-body channels or alternative interaction mechanisms within the diquark framework.
