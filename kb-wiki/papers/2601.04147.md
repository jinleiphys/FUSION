# Three-particle scattering amplitudes from lattice QCD

> I review recent progress in calculating scattering amplitudes and resonance properties involving three particles using results from lattice QCD. The necessary input is the finite-volume spectrum, and the outputs -- via solutions of integral equations -- are scattering amplitudes that can be continued into the complex plane to search for resonance poles. I describe the outlook for future extensions and applications of this work.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent progress in calculating three-particle scattering amplitudes from lattice QCD, but the actual hedge is that the formalism is valid only up to exponentially-suppressed corrections and up to the first relevant inelastic threshold (e.g., $E^*_{\rm max} = 5 M_\pi$ for the three-pion system), and that the three-particle K matrix $\cK_{\rm df,3}$ is an unphysical, cutoff-dependent quantity whose cutoff dependence cancels with other terms to yield a physical spectrum.

## Method

The workflow proceeds in two steps: first, finite-volume energies from lattice QCD simulations are fit to the predictions of the two-particle and three-particle quantization conditions (QC2 and QC3) to determine parametrized forms of the two-particle K matrix $\mathcal K_2$ and the three-particle divergence-free K matrix $\cK_{\rm df,3}$; second, integral equations with these K matrices as kernels are solved to obtain the infinite-volume three-particle scattering amplitude $\mathcal M_3$, which can be analytically continued to search for resonance poles. The QC3 takes the form $\det_{k \ell m x} ( \mathcal K_{\rm df,3}(E^*) + F_3(E,\boldsymbol P,L,\cK_2)^{-1} ) = 0$, where $F_3$ contains known kinematic functions $F$ and $G$ and depends on the two-particle K matrix, while $\cK_{\rm df,3}$ represents the short-distance three-particle interaction.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing $a \approx 0.063$ fm and lattice size $96^3 \times 192$ with $M_\pi L = 4.05$ used in the physical-mass calculation of three-particle amplitudes involving pions and kaons (Dawid 2025)
* About 130 $\pi^+ K^+ K^+$ levels determined in nine frames for all contributing irreducible representations (Dawid 2025)
* For the $\pi^+ K^+ K^+$ system, 115 total levels (40 $\pi K$, 25 $KK$, and 50 $\pi KK$) fit with 10 parameters yielding $\chi^2/{\rm dof}=1.95$ (Dawid 2025)
* For the $2\pi+3\pi$ system, fit quality $\chi^2/{\rm dof}=1.14$ (Dawid 2025)
* The QC3 is valid up to $E^*_{\rm max} = 5 M_\pi$ for the three-pion system, above which on-shell intermediate states with more than three particles appear

## Context

This work extends the foundational two-particle quantization condition of Luscher (1986, 1991) to three particles, following three independent formalisms: the RFT approach of Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015), the nonrelativistic EFT approach of Hammer, Pang, and Rusetsky (2017), and the finite-volume unitarity approach of Mai and Doring (2017). The review covers progress since the 2019 plenary by Rusetsky (2019), including extensions to systems with arbitrary isospin, nondegenerate particles, spin-1/2 particles, and multiple three-particle channels. The formalism enables first-principles calculations of three-body resonances such as the $\omega(780)$ and $\pi(1300)$, the Roper resonance $N(1440)$, and the doubly charmed tetraquark $T_{cc}(3875)^+$, as well as three-nucleon interactions relevant for neutron star equations of state.
