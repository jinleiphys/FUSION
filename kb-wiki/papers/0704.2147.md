# A simultaneous center-of-mass correction of nucleon density and momentum distributions in nuclei

> The approach exposed in the recent paper (A. Shebeko, P. Papakonstantinou, E. Mavrommatis, Eur. Phys. J. A 27, 143 (2006)) has been applied in studying center-of-mass motion effects on the nucleon density and momentum distributions in nuclei. We are focused upon effects due to the center-of-mass and short-range nucleon correlations embedded in translationally invariant ground-state wavefunctions. The latter are constructed in the so-called fixed center-of-mass approximation, starting with a Slater determinant wave function modified by some correlator (e.g., after Jastrow or Villars). It is shown how one can simplify evaluation of the corresponding expectation values that determine the distributions. The analytic expressions derived here involve the own "Tassie-Barker" factors for each distribution. As an illustration, numerical calculations have been carried out for the nucleus ^{4}He with the Slater determinant to describe the nucleon (1s)^4 configuration composed of single-particle orbitals which differ from harmonic oscillator ones at small distances. Such orbitals simulate somewhat short-range repulsion between nucleons. Special attention is paid to a simultaneous shrinking of the center--of--mass corrected density and momentum distributions compared to the purely (1s)^4 shell nontranslationally invariant ones.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that applying a fixed-center-of-mass (fixed-CM) correction to both the nucleon density distribution and momentum distribution in \(^{4}\text{He}\) simultaneously shrinks both distributions compared to the uncorrected shell-model results, but this conclusion is conditional on using a specific translationally invariant ground-state wavefunction constructed via the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) prescription, and the quantitative comparison with experiment is restricted because meson exchange currents and off-shell effects are omitted.

## Method

The authors adopt the fixed-CM approximation of Ernst, Shakin, and Thaler (EST) to restore translational invariance in a many-body wavefunction that initially lacks it, starting from a Slater determinant modified by a short-range correlation operator (e.g., Jastrow or Villars type). Using a Cartesian (boson) representation of coordinate and momentum operators, they derive analytic expressions for the intrinsic form factor, density distribution, and momentum distribution, which factorize into a Tassie-Barker factor, a harmonic-oscillator-model factor, and a correlation factor. Numerical calculations are performed for \(^{4}\text{He}\) using the Radhakant-Khadkikar-Banerjee (RKB) single-particle orbital, which mixes \(1s\) and \(2s\) harmonic oscillator states to simulate short-range repulsion, with parameters fitted to elastic electron scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit parameters for the RKB orbital: \(b_H = 0.8532\) fm and \(\beta = -0.4738\) (with \(\chi^2 \simeq 13.07\) against experimental form factor data from Frosch (1966)).
- The Tassie-Barker factor for the form factor is \(\exp\left(-\frac{\bar{r}_0^2 q^2}{4}\right)\) with \(\bar{r}_0 = \sqrt{\frac{A-1}{A}} r_0\); for the momentum distribution, the corresponding factor is \(\exp\left(-\frac{\bar{p}_0^2 x^2}{4}\right)\) with \(\bar{p}_0 = \sqrt{\frac{A-1}{A}} p_0\).
- The uncorrected RKB density at the origin is \(\rho^{RKB}_{sp}(0) = \frac{1}{(\sqrt{\pi} b_H)^3} \frac{1}{1+\beta^2} \left(1 + \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}} \beta\right)^2\), which for \(\beta = -0.4738\) is less than the harmonic oscillator value \(\rho^{HOM}(0) = \frac{1}{(\sqrt{\pi} b_H)^3}\).
- The first minimum of the charge form factor shifts to higher \(q^2\) after CM correction (qualitative shift, no specific numerical value given for the minimum position).

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Shebeko, Papakonstantinou, and Mavrommatis (2006) for center-of-mass corrections in light nuclei, applying it specifically to the simultaneous treatment of density and momentum distributions in \(^{4}\text{He}\). It contrasts with earlier studies that either omitted CM corrections or treated them only within the harmonic oscillator model (e.g., DOS76), and it provides a framework for incorporating short-range correlations (via Jastrow or Villars-type correlators) into translationally invariant calculations. The results enable future comparisons with microscopic ("exact") calculations for light nuclei and with experimental data from electron scattering, though the authors caution that meson exchange currents and off-shell effects must be included for quantitative agreement at high momentum transfers.
