# Description of collective magnetic states within a spherical single particle basis

> A many body Hamiltonian comprising pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-spin interaction is treated within a projected spherical basis with the aim of describing the detailed structure of the magnetic states of orbital and spin-flip nature in $^{144, 148-154}$Sm. The mean field for the single particle motion takes care of the volume conservation of a phenomenological core which results in having a complex dependence on deformation for single particle energies. Several experimental features like the total orbital and total spin strengths, the shape of the orbital strength distribution, the hump structure of the spin strength distribution, the quadratic dependence of the orbital strength on nuclear deformation, the saturation effect of the orbital strength with respect to the Casten's factor P are very well described within a quasiparticle random phase approximation formalism. Comparison of present results with those obtained in some previous treatments are also given.

# Description of collective magnetic states within a spherical single particle basis

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that using a projected spherical single particle basis with single particle energies that depend non-linearly on deformation (via volume conservation) yields a significantly improved description of both orbital and spin M1 strength distributions in even-even Sm isotopes compared to their earlier treatment where single particle energies depended linearly on deformation.

## Method

A many-body Hamiltonian including pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole, and spin-spin interactions is treated within a quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) formalism. The single particle basis is constructed by angular momentum projection from product states of spherical shell model wavefunctions and a coherent state describing a deformed phenomenological core, with the mean field defined by averaging a particle-core Hamiltonian under the constraint of volume conservation. QRPA equations are solved for the even-even isotopes $^{144,148-154}$Sm, and reduced M1 transition probabilities are calculated using the transition operator with effective gyromagnetic factors.

## Key numbers

- Total orbital strength below 4 MeV ($S_1$): ranges from 0.260 $\mu_N^2$ for $^{144}$Sm to 2.508 $\mu_N^2$ for $^{154}$Sm
- Total spin-flip strength in 4-10 MeV range ($S_2$): ranges from 5.473 $\mu_N^2$ for $^{148}$Sm to 7.961 $\mu_N^2$ for $^{154}$Sm
- For $^{154}$Sm, a state at 5.419 MeV has orbital-to-spin ratio of 2.11 with magnitude 0.33 $\mu_N^2$, showing orbital transitions exist beyond 4 MeV
- The saturation limit for orbital strength as a function of Casten's factor P is 2.9 $\mu_N^2$, with saturation beginning at $^{152}$Sm (P $\approx$ 5)
- Deviation of theoretical average energy from experimental data ranges from 100 keV for $^{154}$Sm to 210 keV for $^{144}$Sm
- Spin gyromagnetic factors are quenched by a factor of 0.85, and the core gyromagnetic factor is taken as $g_c = 0.166$
- Pairing strengths are given as $G_p = 20.70/A$ MeV for $^{144}$Sm and $G_n = 15.80/A$ MeV for $^{154}$Sm, with proton gap energies ranging from 0.777 MeV ($^{154}$Sm) to 1.592 MeV ($^{144}$Sm)

## Context

This work extends the QRPA treatment of magnetic dipole states in Sm isotopes previously developed by Raduta et al. (2001) by incorporating a more realistic mean field with volume conservation, yielding non-linear deformation dependence of single particle energies compared to the earlier linear dependence. The approach builds on the two rotor model of Lo Iudice and Palumbo (1978) and the Interacting Boson Approximation of Iachello (1981), while the strength parameters are consistent with those used by Hamamoto and Magnusson and by de Coster and Heyde. The results enable detailed comparison with experimental $(e,e')$ and $(\gamma,\gamma')$ data on M1 strength distributions, and confirm the quadratic dependence of orbital strength on deformation and the saturation effect with respect to Casten's factor P that were observed experimentally by Rangacharyulu et al.
