# Spin asymmetry and dipole moments in $τ$-pair production with ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions

> The anomalous magnetic (MDM) and electric (EDM) dipole moments of the $τ$ lepton serve as crucial indicators of new physics beyond the Standard Model. Leveraging azimuthal angular asymmetry as a novel tool in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs), we attain unparalleled precision in the study of these key properties. Driven by the highly linear polarization of coherent photons, this method uniquely enables both the MDM and EDM to contribute to the $\cos2ϕ$ angular distribution in similar magnitudes. Importantly, our approach substantially narrows the parameter space, excluding more than half of it compared to expected UPC-based measurements reliant solely on the total cross-section. This method not only provides improved constraints but also minimizes the need for additional theoretical assumptions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that using the azimuthal angular asymmetry (specifically the $\cos2\phi$ modulation) in $\tau$-pair production from ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions can simultaneously constrain both the anomalous magnetic moment and electric dipole moment of the $\tau$ lepton with fewer theoretical assumptions than methods relying solely on total cross-section measurements, and can exclude more than half of the parameter space compared to cross-section-only analyses.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross section for $\gamma\gamma\to\tau^+\tau^-$ in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{S}=5.02$ TeV, including contributions from the Standard Model and from anomalous magnetic (MDM) and electric (EDM) dipole moments via an effective $\tau^+\tau^-\gamma$ vertex. They define an azimuthal asymmetry $A_{c2\phi}$ based on the sign of $\cos2\phi$, where $\phi$ is the angle between the total transverse momentum $\bm q_\perp$ and the relative transverse momentum $\bm P_\perp$ of the $\tau$ pair. Using gamma-UPC with MadGraph5 and PYTHIA 8 for decay simulation, they apply the same kinematic cuts as the ATLAS and CMS experiments and perform a combined $\chi^2$ analysis to project constraints on $a_\tau$ and $d_\tau$ for an integrated luminosity of $30~\text{nb}^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

* The transverse momentum of coherent photons in UPCs is typically around $k_\perp\sim 30~\text{MeV}$, corresponding to the inverse of the nuclear radius.
* The most precise previous MDM measurement from DELPHI gave $a_\tau=-0.018\pm 0.017$.
* The most stringent previous EDM limits from Belle are $\text{Re}(d_\tau)=(1.5\pm 1.7)\times 10^{-17}~\text{e}\cdot\text{cm}$ and $\text{Im}(d_\tau)=(-0.83\pm 0.86)\times 10^{-17}~\text{e}\cdot\text{cm}$.
* The CMS collaboration measured a fiducial cross section of $\sigma = 4.8 \pm 0.6 (\text{stat}) \pm 0.5 (\text{syst})~\mu\text{b}$ with $404~\mu\text{b}^{-1}$ of Pb+Pb data.
* The ATLAS collaboration measured a signal strength of $\mu_{\tau\tau}=1.03\pm 0.05 (\text{stat})\pm 0.03 (\text{syst})$ with $1.44~\text{nb}^{-1}$ of Pb+Pb data.
* For an integrated luminosity of $30~\text{nb}^{-1}$ and assuming 1% systematic uncertainty, the projected 68% C.L. constraints are $-0.02 < a_{\tau} < 0.005$ and $|d_{\tau}| < 1.2 \times 10^{-16}~\text{e}\cdot\text{cm}$.

## Context

This work extends the use of linearly polarized coherent photons in UPCs, previously confirmed by the STAR Collaboration (2019) through a $\cos4\phi$ asymmetry in di-electron production, to probe the MDM and EDM of the $\tau$ lepton. It improves upon earlier UPC-based analyses by Knapen (2016), Beresford (2019), and Dyndal (2020) that relied solely on total cross-section measurements, and upon lepton collider measurements from DELPHI (2003) and Belle (2002). The method enables simultaneous constraints on both dipole moments while reducing dependence on theoretical assumptions about other new physics effects, and can be applied to future LHC data.
