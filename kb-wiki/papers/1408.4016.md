# J=0 T=1 Pairing Interaction Selection Rules

> Wave functions arising form a pairing Hamiltonian E(0) i,e, one in which the interaction is only between J=0^{+}T=1 pairs, lead to magnetic dipole and Gamow-Teller transition rates that are much larger than those from an interaction E(J_{max}) in which a proton and a neutron couple to J=2j. With realistic interactions the results are in between the 2 extremes. In the course of this study we found that certain M1 and GT matrix elements vanish with E(0) . These are connected to seniority and reduced isospin isospin selection rules. We find the surprising result that The M1 strength to the single j scissors is larger for a J=0 T=1 pairing interaction than it is for Q.Q.

## Key claim

For a J=0 T=1 pairing interaction, certain magnetic dipole (M1) and Gamow-Teller (GT) matrix elements vanish due to seniority and reduced isospin selection rules; specifically, transitions from a seniority v=1 state to v=3 states with J = j+1 or J = j-1 are zero, and the M1 strength to the single-j scissors mode is larger for the J=0 T=1 pairing interaction than for the quadrupole-quadrupole (Q.Q.) interaction.

## Method

The authors performed single-j shell calculations for nuclei in the f7/2 shell (specifically 43Sc, 45Sc, 44Ti, and 46Ti) using three schematic interactions: a J=0 T=1 pairing interaction E(0), a Jmax T=0 interaction E(7), and a quadrupole-quadrupole (Q.Q.) interaction, as well as the realistic MBZE interaction. They computed Gamow-Teller and isovector M1 transition matrix elements and classified states by seniority (v), isospin (T), and reduced isospin (t) using the Flowers and Edmonds formula to derive analytical selection rules.

## Key numbers

- For the 7/2 to 7/2 GT transition in 43Sc, the matrix element is 0.3849 for E(0), -0.2088 for MBZE, -0.10160 for E(7), and 0.1207 for Q.Q.
- For the 7/2 to 7/2 GT transition in 45Sc, the matrix element is 0.2666 for E(0), 0.0927 for MBZE, -0.0027 for E(7), and 0.0255 for Q.Q.
- The 7/2 to 5/2 and 7/2 to 9/2 GT matrix elements are exactly zero for the E(0) interaction in both 43Sc and 45Sc.
- In 44Ti with the pairing interaction, the B(M1) value from the J=0 v=0 T=0 state to the J=1 v=2 T=0 state is 2.69963, and from the J=0 v=0 T=2 state to the same J=1 state is 0.898554.
- The largest B(M1) value in 44Ti I=1 to I=0 with the pairing interaction is 8.0995 (from state 210 to state 020).
- The largest B(M1) value in 44Ti I=1 to I=2 with the pairing interaction is 17.5613 (from state 210 to a 400 state).
- The MBZE two-body matrix elements for the f7/2 shell (in MeV) are: (0.0000, 0.6111, 1.5863, 1.4904, 2.8153, 1.5101, 3.2420, 0.6163) for J=0 through J=7.

## Context

This work extends the single-j shell studies of schematic and realistic interactions by Harper and Zamick (2014) and provides analytical proofs for numerical results previously reported in an earlier publication. The authors build on the Flowers and Edmonds (1960) formulation of pairing interactions and the work of McCullen, Bayman, and Zamick (1964) on realistic interactions in the f7/2 shell, as well as Lawson's (1966) work on GT matrix elements and K selection rules. The results enable systematic classification of M1 and GT selection rules in terms of seniority and reduced isospin, and connect to studies of spin scissors excitations in Ti isotopes by Zamick (1993) and the analogy to scissors modes in deformed nuclei such as 156Gd.
