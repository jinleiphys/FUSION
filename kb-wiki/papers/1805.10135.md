# Calculation of francium hyperfine anomaly

> The Dirac-Hartree-Fock plus many-body perturbation theory (DHF+MBPT) method has been used to calculate hyperfine structure constants for Fr. Calculated hyperfine structure anomaly for hydrogen-like ion has been shown to be in good agreement with analytical expressions. It has been shown that the ratio of the anomalies for $s$ and $p_{1/2}$ states is weakly dependent on the principal quantum number. Finally, we estimate Bohr--Weisskopf corrections for several Fr isotopes. Our results may be used to improve experimental accuracy for the nuclear $g$ factors of short-lived isotopes.

## Key claim

The Dirac-Hartree-Fock plus many-body perturbation theory (DHF+MBPT) method can reliably calculate hyperfine structure anomalies for francium, but the ratios of the anomaly parameters for \(s\) and \(p_{1/2}\) states change by 10–15% when core spin-polarization (RPA corrections) is included, contrary to the simpler frozen-core approximation.

## Method

The authors used the Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF) method with many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) and random phase approximation (RPA) corrections, including Breit interactions, to calculate hyperfine structure constants for H-like Fr\(^{86+}\) and neutral Fr as functions of nuclear radius \(R\). The hyperfine anomaly was parametrized by coefficients \(b_N\) (charge distribution/Breit-Rosenthal correction) and \(b_M\) (magnetization distribution/Bohr-Weisskopf correction), extracted from fits of the calculated \(A\) constants to the form \(A = g_I \mathcal{A}_0 (1 - (b_N + b_M d_\mathrm{nuc}) R^{2\gamma-1})\). Experimental ratios \(A(7s)/A(7p_{1/2})\) for even-odd and odd-odd Fr isotopes were then fitted to extract nuclear factors \(d_\mathrm{nuc}\).

## Key numbers

- For H-like Fr\(^{86+}\), the fitted HFS constant for the point-like nucleus \(\mathcal{A}_0(1s) = 292.0\) THz, compared to the analytical value 291.5 THz from Shabaev (1994).
- For H-like Fr\(^{86+}\), the Breit-Rosenthal correction \(\delta(^{210}\mathrm{Fr}) = 0.1411\) for the \(1s\) state and the Bohr-Weisskopf correction \(\epsilon(^{210}\mathrm{Fr}, d_\mathrm{nuc}=1) = 0.0208\) for the \(1s\) state, at \(R = 7.1766\) fm.
- For neutral Fr, the final DHF+MBPT+Br+RPA calculation gives \(A(7s)/g_I = 9849.57\) MHz and \(A(7p_{1/2})/g_I = 1242.94\) MHz for \(^{210}\mathrm{Fr}\), compared to experimental values 9856(113) MHz and 1296(15) MHz respectively.
- The ratio of \(b_N\) parameters for \(7s\) and \(7p_{1/2}\) states in neutral Fr is \(b_N(7s)/b_N(7p_{1/2}) = 3.280\); the ratio of \(b_M\) parameters is \(b_M(7s)/b_M(7p_{1/2}) = 3.023\) (DHF+MBPT+Br+RPA).
- For even-odd Fr isotopes (A=207–213), the one-parameter fit gives \(\rho = 8.456\,(1 - 0.033\,R^{2\gamma-1})\) with \(\rho_0 = 8.43(3)\).
- The fitted nuclear factor for odd-odd Fr isotopes is \(d_\mathrm{nuc} = 0.49\), while for \(^{221}\mathrm{Fr}\) the fit gives \(d_\mathrm{nuc} = 0.05\).

## Context

This work extends the method developed in Konovalova et al. (2017) for calculating hyperfine anomalies to francium, building on prior experimental data for Fr isotopes (e.g., hyperfine anomaly measurements and laser spectroscopy) and theoretical calculations by Pendrill (1987), Sahoo (2015), and Dzuba et al. (1984). The results enable improved extraction of nuclear \(g\) factors for short-lived Fr isotopes from hyperfine structure measurements, and the extracted nuclear factor \(d_\mathrm{nuc}\) provides nuclear-structure information that can be compared with shell-model predictions, particularly for odd-odd isotopes and \(^{221}\mathrm{Fr}\).
