# Exploring the role of low-lying intrinsic degrees of freedom and their impact on fusion cross-sections

> The present work focuses on examining the low-lying intrinsic degrees of freedom and their impact on fusion dynamics. Fusion cross-sections were calculated using the coupled-channel code CCFULL for four specific reactions: $^{18}$O+$^{74}$Ge, $^{18}$O+$^{148}$Nd, $^{18}$O+$^{182}$W, and $^{18}$O+$^{186}$W, all conducted at energies below the Coulomb barrier across various energy levels. Vibrational and rotational features were studied concerning energy to distinguish their respective effects on fusion properties. The results indicate that the theoretical calculations for the nuclei $^{74}$Ge, $^{148}$Nd, $^{182}$W and $^{186}$W closely match the experimental data, particularly for the $2^+$ excited states. While slight discrepancies are observed for other excited states ($4^+$ and $6^+$), overall agreement remains significant. Additionally, the study reveals that hexadecapole deformation with different magnitudes have significant influences on the fusion cross-section. In cases where $β_4$ has a positive value, rotational levels beyond $6^+$ have minimal impact on the cross-section, resulting in a notable difference in the contribution of sequential channels. In contrast, for negative $β_4$ values, rotational energy levels up to the $2^+$ state substantially affect the fusion characteristics. Furthermore, the analysis extends to the estimation of the relative change ($Δσ_{fus}$) between the excited states and the ground state, both with and without considering coupling terms.

# Exploring the role of low-lying intrinsic degrees of freedom and their impact on fusion cross-sections

## Key claim

The coupled-channel calculations using CCFULL show that the sign and magnitude of hexadecapole deformation (β₄) critically determine which rotational levels contribute to sub-barrier fusion enhancement: for positive β₄, rotational levels beyond 6⁺ have minimal impact, while for negative β₄, only levels up to the 2⁺ state substantially affect fusion. The paper hedges that nucleon transfer channels, which may significantly affect below-barrier fusion, were not included in the calculations.

## Method

Fusion cross-sections were calculated using the coupled-channel code CCFULL for four reactions (¹⁸O+⁷⁴Ge, ¹⁸O+¹⁴⁸Nd, ¹⁸O+¹⁸²W, and ¹⁸O+¹⁸⁶W) at energies below the Coulomb barrier. The Woods-Saxon potential with Akyüz-Winther parameterization was employed, with parameters V₀, r₀, and a₀ fitted to reproduce above-barrier fusion data. Rotational states of the target nuclei (2⁺, 4⁺, 6⁺) and the 2⁺ vibrational state of the ¹⁸O projectile were included stepwise in the coupling scheme, and the relative change Δσ_fus between excited and ground states was computed.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁸O+¹⁸²W at 62 MeV center-of-mass energy, coupling the 2⁺ state of ¹⁸O with the 6⁺ state of the target yields a relative change Δσ_fus of 98.71% compared to the inert (1D-BPM) calculation.
- For ¹⁸O+¹⁸⁶W at 62 MeV, the same coupling yields Δσ_fus = 62.33%.
- The energy ratio R(4/2) = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) is 3.29 for ¹⁸²W, 3.25 for ¹⁸⁶W, 2.46 for ⁷⁴Ge, and 1.50 for ¹⁴⁸Nd.
- The 2⁺ excitation energy of ¹⁸O (projectile) is 1.982 MeV with quadrupole deformation β₂ = 0.355.
- For ¹⁸O+¹⁸²W at 63 MeV, the percentage change in σ_fus between the 3rd and 2nd excited states is 15.99% (without projectile excitation) and 13.98% (including the 2⁺ state of ¹⁸O).
- For ¹⁸O+¹⁸⁶W at 63 MeV, the percentage change between the 3rd and 2nd excited states is 30.32% (without projectile excitation) and 22.50% (including the 2⁺ state of ¹⁸O).
- Theoretical excitation energies for ⁷⁴Ge: 2⁺ at 0.60 MeV (experiment 0.595 MeV), 4⁺ at 1.98 MeV (experiment 1.463 MeV), 6⁺ at 4.17 MeV (experiment 2.569 MeV).
- Theoretical excitation energies for ¹⁸⁶W: 2⁺ at 0.12 MeV (experiment 0.12 MeV), 4⁺ at 0.41 MeV (experiment 0.40 MeV), 6⁺ at 0.81 MeV (experiment 0.85 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel treatment of sub-barrier fusion to examine how the rotational and vibrational character of target nuclei, combined with hexadecapole deformation, affects fusion enhancement. The study uses the CCFULL code developed by Hagino (1999) and compares with experimental data from Brod (1975) and Jish (2022). The results show that the 1D barrier penetration model (1D-BPM) systematically underpredicts sub-barrier fusion, consistent with earlier findings by Dasgupta (1998) and others. The paper identifies ¹⁴⁸Nd as a shape-transitional nucleus (following Ibbotson, 1997) and ⁷⁴Ge as the transition nucleus from soft to rigid in Ge isotopes (following Sun, 2014). The omission of nucleon transfer channels, noted by Jain (2024) and Tripathi (2001), leaves open the question of whether their inclusion would resolve remaining discrepancies between theory and experiment for the ¹⁸O+⁷⁴Ge and ¹⁸O+¹⁴⁸Nd reactions.
