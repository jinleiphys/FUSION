# Neutrino Spectra from Accretion Disks: Neutrino General Relativistic Effects and the Consequences for Nucleosynthesis

> Black hole accretion disks have been proposed as good candidates for a range of interesting nucleosynthesis, including the r-process. The presence of the black hole influences the neutrino fluxes and affects the nucleosynthesis resulting from the interaction of the emitted neutrinos and hot outflowing material ejected from the disk. We study the impact of general relativistic effects on the neutrinos emitted from black hole accretion disks. We present abundances obtained by considering null geodesics and energy shifts for two different disk models. We find that both the bending of the neutrino trajectories and the energy shifts have important consequences for the nucleosynthetic outcome

## Key claim

General relativistic effects on neutrinos emitted from black hole accretion disks, specifically the bending of neutrino trajectories and energy shifts, significantly alter the neutrino spectra and consequently the nucleosynthetic yields, but the magnitude and direction of these effects depend on the disk model and the location of the outflow, with rotation of the emitter and observer around the black hole having a larger impact than the choice between Kerr and Schwarzschild metrics for moderate black hole spin.

## Method

The authors computed null geodesics in the Schwarzschild metric to determine neutrino trajectories from emission points on the neutrino surface to observation points in the outflow, solving the geodesic equation for the impact parameter and the angles ξ and α on the observer's sky. Energy redshifts were calculated for both Schwarzschild and Kerr metrics, considering cases with and without rotation of the emitter and observer (Keplerian rotation). These general relativistic corrections were applied to the neutrino fluxes from two disk models: a dynamical 3D hydrodynamical model of a black hole–neutron star merger (Ruffert and Janka) and a one-dimensional steady state disk model (Beloborodov). The resulting neutrino fluxes were then used to compute nucleosynthetic abundances in hot outflows ejected from the disk.

## Key numbers

- Black hole mass of 2.5 M⊙ used for the dynamical merger model (Ruffert and Janka) and for the energy shift comparison plots.
- Black hole mass of 0.5 M⊙ used for the null geodesic trajectory examples (Figures 1–3).
- Schwarzschild radius r_s = 2.9 km for the 0.5 M⊙ black hole example.
- Critical impact parameter b_crit = √3 r_crit, with r_crit = 3r_s/2, such that null geodesics with b < b_crit are captured by the black hole.
- Energy shifts compared for a specific observer location at (r_ob = 64.03 km, φ_ob = 0, θ = 52.4°) and emitter at (r_em = 22.75 km, θ = 48.75°) for a 2.5 M⊙ black hole; the rotation of emitter and observer produced a significant effect on the energy shift, while varying black hole spin (a = 0 to a = 0.6) showed little difference.
- For the dynamical merger model, the inner disk boundary is located at ρ = 14 km; the black hole spin parameter is a = 0.6; the neutron star mass is 1.6 M⊙ and the black hole mass is 2.5 M⊙.
- Observed electron neutrino fluxes at (r_ob = 64.03 km, φ_ob = 0, θ = 52.4°) showed that including ray bending made fluxes larger compared to the non-relativistic case for energies around 10 MeV, while the high-energy tail was reduced; adding rotation decreased the flux at this location.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutrino effects on nucleosynthesis in accretion disk outflows by McLaughlin and Surman (2008) and Surman et al. (2008), which found that neutron-rich material in outflows could produce r-process nuclei under certain conditions. It also builds on general relativistic treatments of photon spectra from accretion disks by Luminet (1979) and Cunningham (1975), and on studies of neutrino pair annihilation rates in the context of gamma-ray bursts by Asano and Birk. The results enable more accurate predictions of nucleosynthetic yields from black hole accretion disks, particularly for the r-process and p-process elements, by incorporating the previously neglected effects of neutrino trajectory bending and energy shifts in the curved spacetime around the black hole.
