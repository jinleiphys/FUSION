# Surface properties of nuclear pairing with the Gogny force in a simplified model

> Surface properties of neutron-neutron (T=1) pairing in semi-infinite nuclear matter in a hard wall potential are investigated in BCS approximation using the Gogny force. Surface enhancement of the gap function, pairing tensor and correlation energy density is put into evidence.

## Key claim

Surface enhancement of the neutron-neutron (T=1) pairing gap, pairing tensor, and correlation energy density is demonstrated in semi-infinite nuclear matter using a hard-wall potential and the Gogny force in BCS approximation, but the authors note that the simplified model (semi-infinite matter with a hard wall) is used to reveal essential features that would otherwise be obscured by shell effects and fluctuations in finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors embed nuclear matter in a semi-infinite hard-wall potential and solve the pairing problem in BCS approximation using the finite-range Gogny D1S force. They compute the spatial dependence of the gap function, the pair density, and the correlation energy density as functions of the distance from the surface, comparing the results to the non-superfluid case.

## Key numbers

- Surface enhancement of the gap function is found to be of the same order as its mean value in the interior, as noted in prior work (reference [1] in the text).
- The study uses the Gogny D1S force, which gives realistic pairing properties in finite nuclei (reference [3] in the text).
- The T=1 (neutron-neutron) channel is density independent and yields surface enhancement, in contrast to the T=0 (neutron-proton) channel.

## Context

This work extends studies of nuclear pairing in finite nuclei using the Gogny force (Farine and Schuck, 1999) to a semi-infinite geometry that isolates surface effects from shell fluctuations. The authors note that fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations for semi-infinite matter are extremely difficult compared to the non-superfluid case (reference [2] in the text). The simplified model enables investigation of superfluid properties at the nuclear surface, which is relevant for understanding pairing in neutron-rich nuclei and exotic nuclear structure.
