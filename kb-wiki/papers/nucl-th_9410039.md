# Measurement of the Reaction $^{12}C(ν_μ,μ^-) X$ Near Threshold

> The reaction $^{12}C(ν_μ,μ^-) X$ has been measured near threshold using a $π^+$ decay-in-flight $ν_μ$ beam from the Los Alamos Meson Physics Facility and a massive liquid scintillator neutrino detector (LSND). In the energy region $123.7 < E_ν< 280$ MeV, the measured spectral shape is consistent with that expected from the Fermi Gas Model. However, the measured flux-averaged inclusive cross section ($(8.3 \pm 0.7 stat. \pm 1.6 syst.) \times 10^{-40} cm^2$) is more than a factor of 2 lower than that predicted by the Fermi Gas Model and by a recent random phase approximation calculation.

## Key claim

The measured flux-averaged inclusive cross section for $^{12}C(\nu_\mu,\mu^-)X$ near threshold is more than a factor of 2 lower than predictions from both the Fermi Gas Model and a recent random phase approximation calculation, indicating the presence of nuclear effects important at low energy that are not accounted for in these models.

## Method

The reaction was measured using a $\pi^+$ decay-in-flight $\nu_\mu$ beam from the Los Alamos Meson Physics Facility and the Liquid Scintillator Neutrino Detector (LSND), a 197 m$^3$ cylindrical mineral oil detector viewed by 1220 photomultiplier tubes. Events were selected by requiring a muon candidate followed within 17 $\mu$s by an electron from muon decay, with both events reconstructing within 200 cm of each other and within a central 108 m$^3$ fiducial volume, yielding 270 events after selection with an overall efficiency of $34\pm4\%$.

## Key numbers

- Flux-averaged inclusive cross section: $(8.3 \pm 0.7 \text{ stat.} \pm 1.6 \text{ syst.}) \times 10^{-40} \text{ cm}^2$ in the energy region $123.7 < E_\nu < 280$ MeV
- Flux-weighted average neutrino energy: $<E_\nu> = 180$ MeV
- Fermi Gas Model prediction for the same cross section: $24 \times 10^{-40} \text{ cm}^2$
- Continuum random phase approximation calculation prediction: $20 \times 10^{-40} \text{ cm}^2$
- Net number of inclusive events after background subtraction: $210 \pm 17$ events
- Measured muon lifetime from the data: $2.1 \pm 0.2 \mu$s
- Six events observed for the exclusive reaction to the $^{12}$N ground state, with seven expected from reliable calculations

## Context

This measurement extends the sparse data on low-energy neutrino-nucleus scattering, which is sensitive to axial-vector nuclear currents unlike electron-nucleus scattering. The result is substantially lower than predictions from the Fermi Gas Model (Gaisser and O'Connell, 1986) and a continuum RPA calculation (Kolbe, Langanke, and Krewald, 1994), but agrees with an earlier calculation using measured $\mu^-$ capture rates on $^{12}$C with a closure approximation (Kim and Mintz, 1985). The measurement enables improved modeling of nuclear effects at low momentum transfer, which is important for neutrino detection in several active detectors.
