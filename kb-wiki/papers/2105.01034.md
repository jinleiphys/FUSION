# Transient Relativistic Fluid Dynamics in a General Hydrodynamic Frame

> We propose a new theory of second-order viscous relativistic hydrodynamics which does not impose any frame conditions on the choice of the hydrodynamic variables. It differs from Mueller-Israel-Stewart theory by including additional transient degrees of freedom, and its first-order truncation reduces to Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun theory. Conditions for causality and stability are explicitly given in the conformal regime. As an illustrative example, we consider Bjorken flow solutions to our equations and identify variables which make a hydrodynamic attractor manifest.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new second-order viscous relativistic hydrodynamics that does not impose any frame conditions on the hydrodynamic variables, generalizing Mueller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory by including additional transient degrees of freedom; the authors clarify that this theory reduces to Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) theory when truncated to first order in gradients, and they provide explicit conditions for causality and stability only in the conformal regime.

## Method

The authors adopt an effective theory approach by writing down an entropy current containing all terms allowed by symmetries up to second order in deviations from equilibrium, then apply the Israel-Stewart argument to derive relaxation equations that guarantee local obedience of the second law of thermodynamics in a general hydrodynamic frame. They analyze the linearized equations of motion by splitting perturbations into sound (longitudinal) and shear (transverse) channels, computing dispersion relations expanded in powers of wavevector magnitude up to second order. For the conformal case at zero chemical potential, they study Bjorken flow solutions by reducing the evolution equations to a system of coupled first-order ordinary differential equations for the energy density, its out-of-equilibrium correction, and the shear-stress tensor.

## Context

This work extends the first-order BDNK theory developed by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha (2017-2020) and Kovtun (2019) into a second-order framework, while also generalizing the standard MIS theory of Mueller, Israel, and Stewart by removing frame conditions. The theory unifies these two previously separate approaches and introduces additional transient nonhydrodynamic degrees of freedom beyond those present in either MIS or BDNK theories. The results enable future simulations of relativistic viscous fluids, particularly for quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star mergers, where the dynamical deviation from the Landau frame can now be studied.
