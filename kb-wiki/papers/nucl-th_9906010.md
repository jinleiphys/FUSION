# Nucleon-Nucleon Effective Field Theory at NNLO: Radiation Pions and 1S0 Phase Shift

> Low energy phenomena involving two nucleons can be successfully described using effective field theory. Because of the relatively large expansion parameter, it is only at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) where one can expect to see agreement with experiment at the few percent level. The first part of this talk will focus on radiation pion effects, which first appear at NNLO. The power counting for radiation pions is simple for center of mass momentum p~\sqrt{M m_pi}=Q_r, the threshold for pion production. We explain how graphs calculated with the Q_r power counting scale for p~m_π. The Q_r^3 radiation pion contributions to nucleon-nucleon scattering are suppressed by inverse powers of the S-wave scattering lengths. However, we point out that order Q_r^4 radiation contributions might give a NNLO contribution for p~m_pi. In the second part of the talk, results for the potential pion and contact interaction part of the NNLO 1S0 phase shift are presented. We emphasize the importance of eliminating spurious poles in the expression for the amplitude at each order in the perturbative expansion. Doing this leaves a total of three free parameters at NNLO. We obtain a good fit to the 1S0 phase shift.

## Key claim
The authors argue that at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon scattering, radiation pion contributions first appear but are suppressed by inverse powers of the S-wave scattering lengths due to Wigner SU(4) symmetry; however, order Q_r^4 radiation contributions might give a NNLO contribution for momenta p ~ m_pi, and a complete NNLO calculation of the 1S0 phase shift requires including these effects.

## Method
The authors perform a power-counting analysis for radiation pions using the scale Q_r = sqrt(M m_pi) and determine how Q_r^n corrections scale with Q for p ~ m_pi. They compute the sum of leading Q_r^3 radiation pion graphs for nucleon-nucleon scattering and present a partial NNLO calculation of the 1S0 phase shift including potential pion exchange and contact interactions, while imposing constraints from renormalization group equations and requiring elimination of spurious poles in the amplitude at each order.

## Key numbers
- The expansion parameter Q/Lambda is estimated to be between 1/2 and 1/5 for p ~ m_pi, with Lambda between 300 MeV and 700 MeV
- The scale Q_r = sqrt(M m_pi) = 360 MeV
- The pion decay constant f = 131 MeV and the nucleon axial-vector coupling g_A = 1.25
- The leading radiation pion graphs scale as Q_r^3/(M^3 Lambda_chi^2) where Lambda_chi = 4 pi f
- At NNLO, a three-parameter fit to the 1S0 phase shift achieves less than 2% accuracy at p ~ m_pi

## Context
This work extends the KSW effective field theory approach (Kaplan, Savage, Wise 1998) for nucleon-nucleon scattering to NNLO, building on previous NLO calculations of phase shifts, deuteron properties, and other observables that showed 10% errors. The authors address the complication that radiation pions introduce a new scale Q_r, modifying the power counting, and they identify Wigner SU(4) symmetry (discussed by Wise in the same volume) as the reason for suppression of leading radiation pion contributions. The partial NNLO calculation presented here enables future complete NNLO calculations that include Q_r^4 radiation pion graphs, which the authors note are needed for full NNLO accuracy.
