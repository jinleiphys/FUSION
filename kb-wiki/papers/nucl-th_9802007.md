# Pauli coupling of vector meson and softening of the nuclear equation of state

> We investigate the equation of state (EOS) for nuclear matter, within the framework of the Relativistic Hartree Fock (RHF) theory, with special emphasis on the role of the Pauli coupling of the vector meson $ρ$ to the nucleon vis-a-vis the eventual softening of the EOS as revealed through a substantial reduction of the incompressibility parameter ($K_0$) for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The Pauli coupling of the rho meson to the nucleon, when included in a Relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) framework, substantially softens the nuclear equation of state by dramatically reducing the incompressibility parameter \(K_0\) for symmetric nuclear matter, but this effect only appears when exchange terms are included.

## Method

The authors constructed a Relativistic Hartree-Fock model of nuclear matter with nucleons interacting via scalar (\(\sigma\)), vector (\(\omega\) and \(\rho\)), and pseudoscalar (\(\pi\)) mesons. They solved the Dyson equation for the in-medium nucleon propagator to obtain the self-energy components, then computed the energy density and incompressibility parameter \(K_0\) for symmetric nuclear matter. The coupling constants \(g_\sigma\) and \(g_\omega\) were adjusted to reproduce the empirical saturation density (\(k_F = 1.4\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)) and binding energy per nucleon (15.75 MeV) for each scenario, while the rho-meson Pauli coupling strength \(\kappa_\rho\) was fixed at 3.7 based on vector meson dominance.

## Key numbers

- Saturation Fermi momentum: \(k_F = 1.4\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: 15.75 MeV
- Incompressibility in MFT (mean field theory, no exchange, no rho): \(K_0 = 550.00\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Incompressibility in HF (Hartree-Fock, sigma and omega only): \(K_0 = 598.01\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Incompressibility in HF with rho meson but no Pauli coupling (\(g_\rho = 2.63\), \(\kappa_\rho = 0.0\)): \(K_0 = 569.83\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Incompressibility in HF with rho meson and Pauli coupling (\(g_\rho = 2.63\), \(\kappa_\rho = 3.7\)): \(K_0 = 355.12\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Pauli coupling strength used: \(\kappa_\rho = 3.7\) (from vector meson dominance)

## Context

This work extends the Relativistic Hartree-Fock approach of Serot (1983) and Walecka (1986) by incorporating the Pauli coupling of the rho meson, which had been omitted in earlier mean-field treatments. The softening of the equation of state has implications for supernova core bounce (Swesty, 1994) and neutron star properties (Prakash, 1997), as well as for understanding giant monopole resonance data from Sn isotopes (Blaizot, 1980). The results enable future studies of asymmetric nuclear matter and the equation of state at different density regimes.
