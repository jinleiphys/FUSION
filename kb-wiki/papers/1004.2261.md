# The Dichotomous Nucleon: Some Radical Conjectures for the Large Nc Limit

> We discuss some problems with the large Nc approximation for nucleons which arise if the axial coupling of the nucleon to pions is large, g_A \sim Nc. While g_A \sim Nc in non-relativistic quark and Skyrme models, it has been suggested that Skyrmions may collapse to a small size, r \sim 1/f_pi \sim Lambda_QCD^{-1}/sqrt(Nc). (This is also the typical scale over which the string vertex moves in a string vertex model of the baryon.) We concentrate on the case of two flavors, where we suggest that to construct a nucleon with a small axial coupling, that most quarks are bound into colored diquark pairs, which have zero spin and isospin. For odd Nc, this leaves one unpaired quark, which carries the spin and isospin of the nucleon. If the unpaired quark is in a spatial wavefunction orthogonal to the wavefunctions of the scalar diquarks, then up to logarithms of Nc, the unpaired quark only costs an energy \sim Lambda_QCD. This naturally gives g_A \sim 1 and has other attractive features. In nature, the wavefunctions of the paired and unpaired quarks might only be approximately orthogonal; then g_A depends weakly upon Nc. This dichotomy in wave functions could arise if the unpaired quark orbits at a size which is parametrically large in comparison to that of the diquarks. We discuss possible tests of these ideas from numerical simulations on the lattice, for two flavors and three and five colors; the extension of our ideas to more than three or more flavors is not obvious, though.

## Key claim

The authors conjecture that in the large Nc limit, the nucleon can be constructed with a small axial coupling g_A ~ 1 (rather than g_A ~ Nc) if most quarks are bound into colored diquark pairs with zero spin and isospin, leaving one unpaired quark in a spatial wavefunction approximately orthogonal to those of the diquarks; this orthogonality could arise if the unpaired quark orbits at a size parametrically larger than that of the diquarks.

## Method

The authors analyze the large Nc scaling of the axial coupling g_A in the non-relativistic quark model, computing the contribution from quarks when the wavefunction of the unpaired quark has variable overlap with the wavefunctions of the paired diquarks. They also examine the stability of Skyrmions against collapse by considering the quark determinant in a background pion field, and relate the string vertex model of the baryon to a collapsed Skyrmion of size ~ 1/f_pi.

## Key numbers

- The canonical value of the axial coupling in the naive non-relativistic quark model is g_A = (Nc+2)/3.
- The Skyrmion size is r ~ 1/Lambda_QCD, while the collapsed Skyrmion size is r ~ 1/f_pi ~ Lambda_QCD^{-1}/sqrt(Nc).
- The binding energy of ordinary nuclear matter is delta E ~ 16 MeV, which is closer to Lambda_QCD/Nc than to Nc*Lambda_QCD.
- The nucleon-Delta mass splitting in QCD is ~ 300 MeV, which is ~ Lambda_QCD, while large Nc consistency conditions predict ~ Lambda_QCD/Nc.
- In the Skyrme model for Nc=3, the parameter 1/e^2 controlling the Skyrme term is taken to be 3.3 x 10^{-2} to provide a phenomenologically viable description.

## Context

This work extends the large Nc analysis of baryons initiated by 't Hooft (1973) and developed by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983), who described the nucleon as a topological Skyrmion with g_A ~ Nc. The authors contrast this with the sigma model treatment of Weinberg, where soft pion emission is suppressed by 1/sqrt(Nc) per pion, and with lattice results from Ishii (2009) showing no strong long-range nucleon-nucleon forces. The conjectured dichotomous nucleon wavefunction with orthogonal spatial states for paired and unpaired quarks offers a resolution to the tension between large Nc predictions and observed Nc=3 phenomenology, and could be tested through lattice simulations for two flavors with three and five colors.
