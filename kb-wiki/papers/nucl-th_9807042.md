# Parton Cascades in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> This is a review of the parton cascade model (PCM) which provides a QCD-based description of nucleus-nucleus reactions at very high energy. The PCM describes the collision dynamics within the early and dense phase of the reaction in terms of the relativistic, probabilistic transport of perturbative excitations (partons) of the QCD vacuum, combined with the renormalization group flow of the parton virtuality. The current state of numerical implementations of the model, as well as its predictions for nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC are discussed.

## Key claim
The parton cascade model (PCM) provides a QCD-based description of the early, dense phase of nucleus-nucleus collisions at very high energy, but its original formulation was critically dependent on several cut-off parameters that had to be determined from proton-proton collision data; recent advances in incorporating medium effects have reduced this dependence significantly.

## Method
The PCM combines three main ingredients: an initial state modeled as an incoherent ensemble of partons from nuclear parton distribution functions; a time evolution governed by a relativistic Boltzmann equation with leading-log improved lowest-order binary collision terms; and a hadronization stage when parton virtuality falls below a critical value. The evolution is described by two coupled transport equations: one for free propagation modified by binary collisions, and one for the evolution of parton distributions with respect to virtuality (off-shellness) via splitting processes. Two numerical implementations are discussed: the self-screened parton cascade model (SSPCM) which calculates the color-electric screening scale self-consistently, and the Monte-Carlo code VNI which uses heuristic rules linking space-time and virtuality evolution.

## Key numbers
- The self-screened parton cascade model finds that the Debye screening mass μ_D approaches about 1 GeV at low p_T in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy (100 GeV/u) and 1.5 GeV at LHC energy (2.75 TeV/u).
- The total deposited energy within one unit of rapidity after a formation time of 0.25 fm/c is ε_0 ≈ 60 GeV/fm³ (RHIC) and ε_0 ≈ 430 GeV/fm³ (LHC).
- The plasma cools to the critical temperature of QCD (T_c ≈ 150 MeV) after 5 fm/c (RHIC) and 10 fm/c (LHC).
- In the VNI code applied to CERN-SPS energies, the energy density in scattered partons at τ < 1 fm/c rises from about 2 GeV/fm³ in S+S to 5 GeV/fm³ in Pb+Pb.
- The kinetic equilibration time predicted by the PCM is τ_th ≪ 1 fm/c.

## Context
This review extends the inside-outside cascade model of hadronic reactions (Andersson et al., 1980) and Bjorken's hydrodynamical model (1983), incorporating minijet concepts from perturbative QCD developed in the mid-1980s (Hwa and Kajantie, 1986; Blaizot and Mueller, 1987; Kajantie et al., 1987; Eskola et al.). It contrasts with the HIJING code (Gyulassy and Wang) and the random light-cone source model of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994). The work enables further study of thermalization, chemical equilibration, and electromagnetic probe emission from the evolving QCD plasma, as well as connections to charmonium suppression observed by the NA50 experiment.
