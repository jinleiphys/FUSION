# Perturbative breaking of the pseudospin symmetry in the relativistic harmonic oscillator

> We show that relativistic mean fields theories with scalar, $S$, and vector, $V$, quadratic radial potentials can generate a harmonic oscillator with exact pseudospin symmetry {\it and positive energy bound states} when $S=-V$. The eigenenergies are quite different from those of the non-relativistic harmonic oscillator. We also discuss a mechanism for perturbatively breaking this symmetry by introducing a tensor potential. Our results shed light into the intrinsic relativistic nature of the pseudospin symmetry, which might be important in high density systems such as neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors show that a relativistic harmonic oscillator with scalar and vector potentials satisfying \(S=-V\) (so that the sum potential \(\Sigma=0\)) yields exact pseudospin symmetry _and_ positive-energy bound states, unlike the usual nuclear mean-field case where \(\Sigma=0\) produces no bound states. The symmetry can then be broken perturbatively by adding a tensor potential linear in \(r\), which introduces a controllable pseudospin-orbit splitting without the non-perturbative pole problems that arise when \(\Sigma\neq 0\).

## Method

The Dirac Hamiltonian is written with scalar \(S\) and vector \(V\) potentials, defining \(\Sigma=V+S\) and \(\Delta=V-S\). For \(\Sigma=0\) and \(\Delta = \frac{1}{2} m \omega_1^2 r^2\), the lower component of the Dirac spinor satisfies a second-order differential equation that depends only on the pseudo-orbital angular momentum \(\tilde{l}\), yielding eigenenergies given by \((\mathcal{E}+m)\sqrt{(\mathcal{E}-m)/(2m)} = \omega_1 (2\tilde{n}+\tilde{l}+3/2)\). To break the pseudospin symmetry, a tensor potential \(U(r)=m\omega_2 r\) is added via the term \(i\beta \boldsymbol{\alpha}\cdot\hat{r}U\), which preserves the overall harmonic oscillator central potential but introduces a pseudospin-orbit term \(-2\kappa m\omega_2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of pseudospin symmetry in relativistic mean-field theories, previously examined by Ginocchio (2000) and others, who noted that the limit \(\Sigma(r)\to 0\) cannot produce bound states for realistic nuclear potentials that vanish at infinity. The authors provide a solvable model (the relativistic harmonic oscillator with \(\Sigma=0\)) that does yield bound states, demonstrating the intrinsically relativistic nature of the symmetry. The perturbative breaking mechanism via a tensor potential offers a controlled way to study pseudospin-orbit splitting without the non-perturbative effects seen in nuclear systems (as discussed by Alberto et al. and Lisboa et al.). The results suggest that pseudospin symmetry may be relevant in ultra-relativistic systems such as neutron star matter.
