# Locating the QCD critical point with neutron-star observations

> We present a probabilistic model for the QCD critical endpoint (CEP) and the equation of state (EOS) at $β$-equilibrium, constrained by neutron-star observations. Using a hybrid framework that combines the holographic V-QCD model with an effective van der Waals description of nuclear matter, we generate a large ensemble of EOSs incorporating nuclear theory uncertainties. Constraining this ensemble with neutron-star mass-radius data and tidal deformability measurements from gravitational waves, we identify a strong first-order deconfinement transition at zero temperature, with a transition strength of $Δn_{\rm{PT}}/n_0 = 3.75^{+0.70}_{-0.53}$ and onset density $n_{\rm{PT}}/n_0 = 4.9^{+1.33}_{-1.17}$. The resulting posterior yields $95\%$ credible intervals for the CEP location: $μ_{\rm{crit}} = 626^{+90}_{-179}\,\rm{MeV}$, $T_{\rm{crit}} = 119^{+14}_{-6}\,\rm{MeV}$.

# Locating the QCD critical point with neutron-star observations

## Key claim
The authors present a probabilistic model that constrains the location of the QCD critical endpoint (CEP) using neutron-star observations, finding 95% credible intervals of μ_crit = 626^{+90}_{-179} MeV and T_crit = 119^{+14}_{-6} MeV, but these results depend on the specific hybrid holographic framework and the assumption that the V-QCD model's predictions for the quark matter phase are consistent with perturbative QCD only at densities below approximately 17 n_0.

## Method
The authors construct a hybrid equation of state (EOS) combining the holographic V-QCD model with an effective van der Waals description of nuclear matter, generating a large ensemble of EOSs that incorporate nuclear theory uncertainties. They constrain this ensemble using a Bayesian posterior that includes chiral effective theory calculations at nuclear densities, mass measurements of PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0740+6620, NICER mass-radius constraints, and the tidal deformability measurement from GW170817. The cold EOS is built from four components: a low-density Hempel-Schaffner-Bielich (SFHo) EOS, a piecewise-linear speed-of-sound interpolation, the V-QCD quark matter EOS, and a holographic nuclear matter extension, all matched thermodynamically at their boundaries. The finite-temperature extension uses an excluded-volume corrected van der Waals free energy, and the critical endpoint is located by evaluating where the latent heat across the deconfinement phase transition vanishes.

## Key numbers
- Transition strength: Δn_PT/n_0 = 3.75^{+0.70}_{-0.53} (first-order deconfinement at zero temperature)
- Onset density: n_PT/n_0 = 4.9^{+1.33}_{-1.17}, corresponding to e_PT = 935^{+335}_{-275} MeV/fm^3
- CEP location (95% credible intervals): μ_crit = 626^{+90}_{-179} MeV, T_crit = 119^{+14}_{-6} MeV
- Optimal model CEP: (μ_crit^opt, T_crit^opt) = (683, 116) MeV
- PT chemical potential at zero temperature: μ_PT = 1597^{+201}_{-218} MeV, optimal value 1772 MeV
- Neutron star radius at 1.4 M_⊙: R_1.4 = 12.71^{+0.67}_{-0.66} km
- Maximum TOV mass: M_TOV = 2.22^{+0.20}_{-0.22} M_⊙
- Binary tidal deformability at chirp mass 1.186 M_⊙: Λ̃_1.186 = 586^{+239}_{-175}
- Optimal model maximum speed of sound: max c_s,opt^2 = 0.55

## Context
This work extends the hybrid holographic approach of Demircik et al. (2021) by generalizing the parameter dependence of the V-QCD and van der Waals models and replacing the single nuclear theory EOS with a sampled family. It complements other studies of the QCD EOS and CEP using Dyson-Schwinger equations (Gunkel et al., 2021), functional renormalization group approaches (Fu et al., 2019; Gao et al., 2020), and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Buballa, 2005; Roessner et al., 2007; Costa et al., 2008). The framework enables testable predictions for heavy-ion collision experiments at Beam Energy Scan II, NA61/SHINE, FAIR, and NICA, and can serve as input for supernova and neutron-star merger simulations. The predicted CEP location may be influenced by a modulated instability identified by Cruz Rojas et al. (2024) and Demircik et al. (2024), which warrants further investigation.
