# Quantum Monte Carlo method applied to strongly correlated dilute fermi gases with finite effective range

> We discuss the Auxiliary Field Quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) method applied to dilute neutron matter at finite temperatures. We formulate the discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation for the interaction with finite effective range which is free from the sign problem. The AFQMC results are compared with those obtained from exact diagonalization for a toy model. Preliminary calculations of energy and chemical potential as a function of temperature are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents an Auxiliary Field Quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) method for dilute neutron matter at finite temperatures that is free from the fermion sign problem, but only for a specific discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation that requires the interaction to be attractive (g < 0) and uses at least seven auxiliary field values (k ≥ 7).

## Method

The authors place a dilute neutron system on a 3D spatial lattice with spacing b and size L = N_s b, using a Hamiltonian with a finite-range interaction that reproduces the neutron-neutron scattering length a_S and effective range r_eff. They decompose the imaginary-time propagator using a second-order Trotter expansion and apply a discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation with k ≥ 7 auxiliary field values to express the many-body interaction as a sum of one-body operators. The partition function is then evaluated via path integral Monte Carlo using the Metropolis algorithm, with the measure guaranteed positive because the one-body Hamiltonian preserves time-reversal symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_S ≈ -18.5 fm; effective range: r_eff ≈ 2.8 fm
- Lattice parameters: N_s = 8, lattice constant b = 3.21 fm, giving momentum cut-off p_cut = π/b
- System density: ρ ≈ 0.02 ρ_0 (where ρ_0 is nuclear saturation density), with total particle number 55
- Temperature range: from 0.06 ε_F (0.26 MeV) to 1.0 ε_F (3.8 MeV), where ε_F is the Fermi energy
- At lowest temperature (0.06 ε_F): E/E_FFG = 0.48 ± 0.02 (E/N = 1.28 MeV); chemical potential μ/ε_F = 0.40 (1.77 MeV)
- Imaginary time steps: 2360 for lowest temperature, 216 for highest temperature
- Comparison with exact diagonalization in a restricted space (7 single-particle states, 2^14 Fock states) at T/ε_F = 0.58, μ/ε_F = 0.41: AFQMC gives ⟨Ĥ⟩ = 0.23 vs exact 0.23; ⟨N̂⟩ = 5.58 vs exact 5.49

## Context

This work extends the discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation originally introduced by Hirsch (1983) to interactions with finite effective range, enabling AFQMC calculations for dilute neutron matter where the nonzero effective range cannot be neglected (unlike cold atomic gases). The results at the lowest temperature agree with fixed-node quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Carlson et al., 2003; Gezerlis and Carlson, 2008). The method enables fully non-perturbative finite-temperature calculations for neutron matter at densities relevant to neutron star crusts, providing constraints for nuclear energy density functionals.
