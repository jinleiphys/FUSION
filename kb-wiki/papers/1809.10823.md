# Form factors for pions couplings to constituent quarks under weak magnetic field

> By considering a one loop background field method for a nonperturbative one gluon exchange between quarks, leading electromagnetic form factors of pion couplings to constituent quarks are derived by means of from a large quark and gluon effective masses expansion. With this calculation the leading anisotropic corrections induced by a weak magnetic field for the pion-constituent quark form factors are obtained. Besides that, few effective couplings which emerge only due to the weak magnetic field that break chiral and isospin symmetry are also found. Numerical estimations for these magnetic field corrections are presented for two different nonperturbative gluon propagators. All of these corrections are ultraviolet finite and their relative values are found to be of order of $(eB_0/{M^*}^2)^n$ being $n=1$ for the vector and axial pion couplings and $n=2$ for the pseudoscalar and scalar ones. The corresponding anisotropic corrections to the Strong averaged quadratic radii in the plane perpendicular to the magnetic field are also calculated as functions of the quark effective mass.

## Key claim

The paper derives leading anisotropic corrections to pion-constituent quark form factors induced by a weak magnetic field, with the condition that the magnetic field is weak relative to the quark effective mass squared (eB₀ ≪ M*²), and these corrections are of order (eB₀/M*²)ⁿ where n=1 for vector and axial couplings and n=2 for pseudoscalar and scalar couplings.

## Method

The authors use a one-loop background field method starting from a nonperturbative one-gluon exchange interaction between quarks, with minimal coupling to a background electromagnetic field. They perform a large quark and gluon effective mass expansion of the sea quark determinant, keeping only the lowest energy modes (pions) in the pseudoscalar isotriplet channel. Two different nonperturbative gluon propagators are employed for numerical evaluation: the Tandy-Maris propagator and an effective confining propagator by Cornwall, both of which produce dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of magnetic field corrections to the axial form factor at zero momentum: G_(A,1)^B(0,0) / F_A^B(0,0) = 1/8
- The ratio of magnetic field corrections to the vector form factor at zero momentum: G_(V,1)^B(0,0) / F_V^B(0,0) = 1/16
- The ratio G_(V,1)^B(0,0) / G_(V,2)^B(0,0) = 1/8
- The gluon propagator normalization constants: h_I = 0.82 for Tandy-Maris propagator, h_II = 0.3 for Cornwall propagator
- The confining propagator parameters: K_F = (2π M_k / (3 k_e))² with k_e = 0.15 and M_k = 220 MeV
- The Tandy-Maris propagator parameters: ω = 0.5 GeV, D = 0.55³/ω (GeV²), Λ_QCD = 0.234 GeV, m_t = 0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of pion-constituent quark strong form factors in vacuum (Braghin, 2018) to include weak magnetic field corrections, and follows their earlier studies of electromagnetic and strong form factors of light vector and axial mesons coupled to constituent quarks (Braghin and de Sousa, 2018). The formalism builds on the Weinberg large N_c effective field theory derived from a dressed one-gluon exchange interaction (Braghin, 2016, 2018). The results enable systematic calculation of magnetic field effects on pion-mediated parts of the nucleon potential, which may be relevant for understanding nuclear structure changes under relatively weak external magnetic fields as envisaged in studies of nuclei in magnetic fields.
