# Electric field dependence of pairing temperature and tunneling

> Using the Bethe-Salpeter equation including high electric fields, the dependence of the critical temperature of onsetting superconductivity on the applied field is calculated analytically. The critical temperature of pairing is shown to increase with the applied field strength. This is a new field effect and could contribute to the explanation of recent experiments on field induced superconductivity. From the field dependence of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, the two--particle bound state solution is obtained as a resonance with a tunneling probability analogous to the WKB solution of a single particle confined in a potential and coupled to the electrical field.

## Key claim

Using the Bethe-Salpeter equation including high electric fields, the critical temperature of pairing is shown to increase with applied field strength, but this result is derived under the condition of equal charge-to-mass ratios for the two particles and for small field parameters where λ_E ≪ T_c ≪ ω_c ≪ ε_f; the author notes that for other parameter regimes (λ_E ∼ ω_c) one finds a decrease in T_c.

## Method

The author solves the field-dependent Bethe-Salpeter equation for the T-matrix using separable potentials in the contact potential limit, with the field-dependent kernel expressed in terms of Airy functions. The critical temperature is obtained by solving the resonance condition for the T-matrix denominator at twice the chemical potential, expanding the resulting integral equation to lowest order in the field parameter λ_E. The bound state problem is treated by finding complex poles of the T-matrix in the absence of medium effects, yielding energy shifts and lifetimes.

## Key numbers

- The field parameter is defined as λ_E = (E²ħ²/8 × (e_a²/m_a + e_b²/m_b))^(1/3)
- The critical temperature increase follows T_c ln(T_c/T_0[N]) = b λ_E, with b ≈ 0.190
- For small fields, T_c - T_0[N] ≈ λ_E b
- The energy shift of the bound state is Δ = (λ_E³)/(16 E_0²) × (3 + 4(-E_0/λ_E)^(3/2)) × exp(-(4/3)(-E_0/λ_E)^(3/2))
- The inverse lifetime is ħ/τ = (λ_E^(3/2))/(2√(-E_0)) × exp(-(2/3)(-E_0/λ_E)^(3/2))
- The experimental fit uses a threshold voltage of about 118 V with T_0 = 1 K and ε_ins/ε_cup ≈ 192, or alternatively T_0 = 2×10⁻⁸ K with ε_ins/ε_cup ≈ 10

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier treatment (Morawetz 1994) of two-particle properties in high electric fields, where bound states turn into resonances and Pauli blocking is suppressed. The result provides a new field effect on the pairing mechanism beyond the two standard mechanisms in high-T_c cuprates (change of hole concentration via Coulomb force and field-induced oxygen rearrangement, as reviewed by Kopp and Schrieffer 1998). The theory is compared to experiments on field-induced superconductivity in spin-ladder cuprates (S. 2001), though the author notes that without simultaneous measurement of doping concentration one cannot uniquely conclude on this new field effect. The bound state result generalizes the WKB tunneling formula for a single particle to the two-particle problem in electric fields.
