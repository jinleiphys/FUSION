# Superallowed nuclear beta decays and precision tests of the Standard Model

> For many decades, the main source of information on the top-left corner element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa quark mixing matrix $V_{ud}$ were superallowed nuclear beta decays with an impressive 0.01\% precision. This precision, apart from experimental data, relies on theoretical calculations in which nuclear structure-dependent effects and uncertainties play a prime role. This review is dedicated to a thorough reassessment of all ingredients that enter the extraction of the value of $V_{ud}$ from experimental data. We tried to keep balance between historical retrospect and new developments, many of which occurred in just five past years. They have not yet been reviewed in a complete manner, not least because new results are a-coming. This review aims at filling this gap and offers an in-depth yet accessible summary of all recent developments.

## Key claim

Superallowed nuclear beta decays provide the most precise extraction of the CKM matrix element \(V_{ud}\) at the 0.01% level, but this precision depends critically on theoretical calculations of nuclear structure-dependent effects whose uncertainties have recently increased rather than decreased, challenging the previously accepted central value of \(V_{ud}\).

## Method

The authors review the master formula \(\mathcal{F}t = K / [2 V_{ud}^2 G_F^2 (1+\Delta_R^V)]\) that separates nucleus-dependent quantities (modified \(ft\)-value) from nucleus-independent ones. They reassess all theoretical ingredients: the statistical rate function \(f\) including the Fermi function and shape factor, the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction \(\delta_C\), the outer radiative correction \(\delta_R'\), and the nuclear-structure-dependent correction \(\delta_{NS}\). For the shape factor, they use isospin symmetry to relate the weak current distribution \(\rho_{cw}(r)\) to linear combinations of nuclear charge distributions across isotriplets, enabling a data-driven evaluation from measured charge radii.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi constant \(G_F/(\hbar c)^3 = 1.1663788(6) \times 10^{-5}~\text{GeV}^{-2}\) and the factor \(K/(\hbar c)^6 = 8120.27648(26) \times 10^{-10}~\text{GeV}^{-4}\text{s}\) are both very precisely measured.
- The top-row unitarity constraint \(|V_{ud}|^2+|V_{us}|^2+|V_{ub}|^2=1\) is currently tested at 0.01% level, translating to a BSM scale \(\Lambda_{BSM} \gtrsim 20~\text{TeV}\).
- For the \(^{22}\text{Mg} \to ^{22}\text{Na}\) transition, the new statistical rate function is \(f = 418.27(2)_{\text{dist}}(7)_{\text{scr}}(13)_{Q_{\text{EC}}}\) compared to the Hardy-Towner value \(418.35(13)_{Q_{\text{EC}}}\), a relative shift of \(-0.02\%\).
- For the \(^{38}\text{Ca} \to ^{38m}\text{K}\) transition, the new \(f = 5327.49(39)_{\text{dist}}(98)_{\text{scr}}(31)_{Q_{\text{EC}}}\) versus \(5328.88(31)_{Q_{\text{EC}}}\), a shift of \(-0.03\%\).
- The weak charge radius \(\langle r_{cw}^2 \rangle^{1/2}\) for \(^{38}\text{Ca}\) is 3.999(35) fm, significantly larger than the charge radii of the isotriplet members (e.g., \(^{38}\text{Ar}\) at 3.4028(19) fm).

## Context

This review extends the decades-long program of superallowed beta decay studies initiated by Hardy and Towner (1975) and updated by Hardy (2020), which had established the standard extraction of \(V_{ud}\). It incorporates recent dispersive analyses by Seng (2018) and Gorchtein (2018) that revealed missing nuclear structure effects in radiative corrections, increasing theory uncertainties. The work enables future improvements through new experimental measurements of nuclear charge radii at facilities such as ISOLDE, FRIB, GSI/FAIR, RIKEN, and TRIUMF, combined with modern ab-initio nuclear many-body calculations to reduce the newly identified uncertainties.
