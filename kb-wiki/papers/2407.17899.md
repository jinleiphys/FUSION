# Heavy-flavor mesons in a strong electric field

> Very strong electromagnetic field can be generated in peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions. This work is devoted to exploring the interplay between the effects of a constant external electric field and confining potential on heavy-flavor mesons. As the corresponding vector potential linearly depends on one spatial coordinate for a constant electric field, it might be able to overcome the linear confining potential of QCD and induce deconfinement. To perform analytic calculations and for comparison, one and two dimensional systems are studied together with the realistic three dimensional systems. The one dimensional Schr$\ddot{\text o}$dinger equation can be solved analytically with the help of Airy functions, and deconfinement is indeed realized when the electric field is larger than the string tension. Focus on the confining case, the two and three dimensional Schr$\ddot{\text o}$dinger equations can be solved analytically in large $r$ limit with the help of elliptic cosine/sine functions, and the wave functions are dominated by the region antiparallel to the electric field. When a more realistic potential is applied, a non-monotonic feature is found for $Υ(2S)$ and $Υ(3S)$-like mesons with increasing electric field.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a constant external electric field can induce deconfinement of heavy-flavor mesons when the effective electric field strength exceeds the QCD string tension (σ = 0.2 GeV²), but this deconfinement is qualitatively different from thermal deconfinement because it is a direct effect of the field rather than an indirect modification of the string tension. The claim is hedged by the condition that the Coulomb part of the Cornell potential is neglected, and the analysis focuses on the linear confining potential only.

## Method
The authors solve the static two-body Schrödinger equation for heavy-flavor mesons (charmonium, bottomonium, and Bc mesons) with a linear confining potential σr and a constant electric field term qεx, neglecting the Coulomb interaction. They treat the problem in one, two, and three spatial dimensions: the 1D case is solved analytically using Airy functions, while the 2D and 3D cases are solved numerically and analytically in the large-r limit using Mathieu functions and elliptic cosine/sine functions. The relative motion is separated from the center-of-mass motion, and the string tension is taken as σ = 0.2 GeV² from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers
- String tension: σ = 0.2 GeV², taken from lattice QCD simulations (Eichten 1974).
- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.29 GeV; bottom quark mass: m_b = 4.7 GeV.
- Reduced masses: c-cbar = 0.645 GeV, c-bbar = 1.012 GeV, b-cbar = 1.012 GeV, b-bbar = 2.35 GeV.
- Reduced charges: c-cbar = 2/3 e, c-bbar = 0.45 e, b-cbar = -0.45 e, b-bbar = -1/3 e.
- Critical electric field for deconfinement: |q|ε_c = σ = 0.2 GeV² (the condition σ < |q|ε signals deconfinement).
- For the 1D case with subcritical field |q|ε = 0.1 GeV², the lowest 50 eigenenergies of bottomium are computed and shown to decrease with increasing field.
- For the 1D supercritical case |q|ε = 0.3 GeV² with boundary b = 10 GeV, the lowest 20 eigenenergies are computed; eigenfunctions oscillate strongly near the boundary, signaling deconfinement.
- Experimental relative energies (for comparison): ψ(2S) = 1.106 GeV, Bc(2S) = 0.881 GeV, Υ(2S) = 0.623 GeV, Υ(3S) = 0.955 GeV.
- Theoretical relative energies (with confining potential only): ψ(2S) = 0.735 GeV, Bc(2S) = 0.632 GeV, Υ(2S) = 0.477 GeV, Υ(3S) = 0.835 GeV.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of electromagnetic field effects on heavy mesons, particularly the magnetic field studies by Chen (2020) and Zhao (2020) which found that magnetic fields enhance J/ψ mass but reduce ηc mass. It also builds on the pure electric field study by Biaogang (2019) using spherical harmonic expansions, but here the authors solve the Schrödinger equation analytically in 1D and analytically in the large-r limit for 2D and 3D without such expansions. The paper enables future work on the non-monotonic behavior found for Υ(2S) and Υ(3S)-like mesons when a more realistic potential is applied, and it provides a quantum-mechanical framework for understanding electric-field-induced deconfinement in heavy-ion collisions.
