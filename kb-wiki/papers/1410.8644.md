# Continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation for astrophysical direct neutron capture reaction of neutron-rich nuclei

> We formulate a many-body theory to calculate the cross section of direct radiative neutron capture reaction by means of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field model and the continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA). A focus is put on very neutron-rich nuclei and low-energy neutron kinetic energy in the range of O(1 keV) - O(1 MeV), relevant for the rapid neutron-capture process of nucleosynthesis. We begin with the photo-absorption cross section and the E1 strength function, then, in order to apply the reciprocity theorem, we decompose the cross section into partial cross sections corresponding to different channels of one- and two-neutron emission decays of photo-excited states. Numerical example is shown for the photo-absorption of $^{142}$Sn and the neutron capture of $^{141}$Sn.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a many-body theory for direct radiative neutron capture cross sections in very neutron-rich nuclei using the continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (cQRPA), but the method is demonstrated only for a single numerical example (photo-absorption of $^{142}$Sn and neutron capture of $^{141}$Sn) and the authors note that special numerical procedures are required for the low-energy range relevant to the r-process (O(1 keV) - O(1 MeV)).

## Method

The authors begin with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field model and the continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (cQRPA) to calculate the total photo-absorption cross section and E1 strength function. They then decompose the strength function into partial cross sections corresponding to one-neutron and two-neutron emission decay channels using an extension of the Zangwill-Soven method, which allows application of the reciprocity theorem to obtain the direct neutron capture cross section. The partial cross sections are expressed in terms of quasiparticle wave functions and Green's functions in the coordinate representation, with the self-consistent field including both the external dipole operator and the induced field from density fluctuations.

## Key numbers

* The neutron kinetic energy range relevant for the r-process is stated as $E_n \sim$ O(1 keV) - O(1 MeV), corresponding to temperatures $T \sim$ O($10^7$) - O($10^9$) K.
* The one-neutron separation energy for nuclei near the neutron drip-line is given as $S_{1n} \sim 1$ MeV.
* The threshold energy for one-particle emission is $S_1 = |\lambda| + \min E_m$, and for two-particle emission is $S_2 = 2|\lambda|$, with $S_1 \le S_2$.
* Numerical examples are shown for photo-absorption of $^{142}$Sn and neutron capture of $^{141}$Sn (no numerical cross section values are provided in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the continuum RPA method of Zangwill and Soven (1980), originally developed for atomic and molecular photo-absorption, to superfluid neutron-rich nuclei by incorporating pair correlations through the Bogoliubov quasiparticle formalism. The approach addresses the need for microscopic many-body calculations of direct neutron capture in very neutron-rich nuclei, where compound-nucleus formation is suppressed due to small neutron separation energies and low level densities, as previously discussed by Mathews (1983), Goriely (1997), and Arnould (2007). The method enables future calculations of neutron capture cross sections for r-process nucleosynthesis that include the effects of new dipole excitation modes such as the pygmy resonance, which were previously studied only in the context of compound-process gamma-ray strength functions by Goriely and Khan (2002, 2004) and others.
