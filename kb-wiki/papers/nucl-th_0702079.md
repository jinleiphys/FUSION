# Hard and soft probe - medium interactions in a 3D hydro+micro approach at RHIC

> We utilize a 3D hybrid hydro+micro model for a comprehensive and consistent description of soft and hard particle production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC. In the soft sector we focus on the dynamics of (multi-)strange baryons, where a clear strangeness dependence of their collision rates and freeze-out is observed. In the hard sector we study the radiative energy loss of hard partons in a soft medium in the multiple soft scattering approximation. While the nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ does not reflect the high quality of the medium description (except in a reduced systematic uncertainty in extracting the quenching power of the medium), the hydrodynamical model also allows to study different centralities and in particular the angular variation of $R_{AA}$ with respect to the reaction plane, allowing for a controlled variation of the in-medium path-length.

# Hard and soft probe - medium interactions in a 3D hydro+micro approach at RHIC

## Key claim

The nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ for central collisions does not reflect the high quality of the medium description, except in a reduced systematic uncertainty for extracting the quenching power of the medium; the main advantage of the 3D hybrid model is the ability to study the angular variation of $R_{AA}$ with respect to the reaction plane, enabling controlled variation of the in-medium path-length.

## Method

The authors used a fully three-dimensional hybrid hydro+micro transport model, combining relativistic 3D hydrodynamics for the early deconfined stage with a microscopic non-equilibrium model for the later hadronic stage. For the hard sector, they calculated radiative energy loss of hard partons in the BDMPS formalism using quenching weights, with the local transport coefficient $\hat{q}(\xi)$ expressed as $K \cdot 2 \cdot \epsilon^{3/4}(\xi)$, where the scale parameter $K$ was adjusted to data from central collisions. For the soft sector, they analyzed the dynamics of (multi-)strange baryons including their $p_T$ spectra and the number of hadronic rescatterings as a function of pseudorapidity.

## Key numbers

- At midrapidity, the number of hadronic rescatterings for different baryon species shows a plateau region extending from $\eta = -3$ to 3, followed by a steep drop-off to forward and backward rapidities.
- The difference between in-plane and out-of-plane $R_{AA}$ grows with impact parameter: at $b = 2.4$ fm there is hardly any angular variation, whereas at 7.5 fm differences are of order 20%.
- The $\pm 50\%$ spread in the values of $K$ across different medium models (2D hydro, 3D hydro, parametrized fireball) is a measure of the systematic error inherent in tomographic analysis via $R_{AA}$.
- The number of collisions for $p$, $\Lambda$, $\Xi$, and $\Omega$ was analyzed as a function of $\eta$ at impact parameters $b = 2.4$, 4.5, and 6.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations by Hirano (2002, 2003) that estimated effects of 3D expansion on $R_{AA}$ but used a simplified heuristic treatment of energy loss, by instead applying the full BDMPS formalism with quenching weights from Salgado and Wiedemann (2003). The 3D hybrid model builds on the hydrodynamic code of Nonaka (2006) and enables a precision study of jet-medium interactions that reduces systematic uncertainties compared to earlier approaches using simple one- or two-dimensional Bjorken expansion. The results enable future comprehensive studies of probe-medium interactions treating the hard and soft sectors on equal footing.
