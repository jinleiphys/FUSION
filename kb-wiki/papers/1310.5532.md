# Implications of the ABC Resonance Structure on Elastic Neutron-Proton Scattering

> In recent WASA-at-COSY measurements of the basic double-pionic fusion reactions $pn \to dπ^0π^0$ and $pn \to dπ^+π^-$ a narrow resonance structure with $I(J^P) = 0(3^+)$ in the total cross section has been found. If this constitutes a s-channel resonance in the $pn$ system, then it should cause distinctive consequences in $pn$ scattering. The magnitude of the decay width into the $pn$ channel is estimated and the expected resonance effects in integral and differential $pn$ scattering observables are presented. The inclusion of the resonance improves the description of total cross section data. For the analyzing power a characteristic energy dependence is predicted, which should allow a crucial experimental check of the resonance hypothesis.

## Key claim

If the narrow resonance structure with $I(J^P) = 0(3^+)$ observed in double-pionic fusion reactions constitutes an s-channel resonance in the $pn$ system, it should produce distinctive but small effects in elastic neutron-proton scattering observables, with the magnitude of the decay width into the $pn$ channel estimated at 7(1) MeV.

## Method

The authors estimated the partial decay width of the proposed resonance into the elastic $pn$ channel using the measured peak cross sections of the resonance in double-pion production channels and applying the unitarity limit. They then added a Breit-Wigner resonance amplitude to the SAID SP07 partial wave amplitudes for the $^3D_3$ and $^3G_3$ partial waves, using a multiplicative S-matrix approach with a free resonance phase parameter $\Phi_R$ fixed by fitting total $np$ cross section data.

## Key numbers

- Resonance mass $m_R = 2.37$ GeV and total width $\Gamma = 70$ MeV
- Estimated elastic decay width $\Gamma_i = 7(1)$ MeV
- Estimated peak resonance cross section in elastic $pn$ channel: $\sigma_{pn \to pn} \approx 0.16$ mb
- Unitarity limit at resonance: $\sigma_0 = 16.4$ mb
- Measured peak cross section of $pn \to d\pi^0\pi^0$ at $\sqrt s = 2.37$ GeV: 0.27 mb (pure resonance contribution about 0.24 mb)
- Total resonance cross section in two-pion channels: $\sigma_{pn \to \Delta\Delta}(peak) \approx 1.4(1)$ mb
- Cutoff parameter for monopole form-factor: $\Lambda \approx 0.16$ GeV/c, corresponding to length scale $r \approx 2$ fm
- SAID $^3D_3$ total elastic cross section at $T_p = 1.2$ GeV: 1.46 mb (upper limit for elastic resonance cross section gives $\Gamma_i \leq 20$ MeV)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the ABC resonance structure discovered by the WASA-at-COSY collaboration in double-pionic fusion reactions (Adlarson et al., 2011; Bashkanov et al., 2013) to its implications for elastic neutron-proton scattering. The authors use the SAID SP07 partial wave analysis (Arndt et al.) as the non-resonant background and compare with total cross section data from Lisowski et al. (LAMPF), Devlin et al., and Sharov et al. The predicted characteristic energy dependence of the analyzing power $A_y$ near $\Theta = 90^\circ$ provides an experimental test of the resonance hypothesis, with opposite signs expected for $^3D_3$ versus $^3G_3$ resonance contributions.
