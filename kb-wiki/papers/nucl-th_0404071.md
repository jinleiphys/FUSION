# Faddeev calculation of 3 alpha and alpha alpha Lambda systems using alpha alpha resonating-group method kernel

> We carry out Faddeev calculations of three-alpha (3 alpha) and two-alpha plus Lambda (alpha alpha Lambda) systems, using two-cluster resonating-group method kernels. The input includes an effective two-nucleon force for the alpha alpha resonating-group method and a new effective Lambda N force for the Lambda alpha interaction. The latter force is a simple two-range Gaussian potential for each spin-singlet and triplet state, generated from the phase-shift behavior of the quark-model hyperon-nucleon interaction, fss2, by using an inversion method based on supersymmetric quantum mechanics. Owing to the exact treatment of the Pauli-forbidden states between the clusters, the present three-cluster Faddeev formalism can describe the mutually related, alpha alpha, 3 alpha and alpha alpha Lambda systems, in terms of a unique set of the baryon-baryon interactions. For the three-range Minnesota force which describes the alpha alpha phase shifts quite accurately, the ground-state and excitation energies of 9Be Lambda are reproduced within 100 - 200 keV accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a consistent description of the three-alpha (3α) and alpha-alpha-Lambda (ααΛ) systems can be achieved within a single Faddeev formalism using two-cluster resonating-group method (RGM) kernels, provided that the Pauli-forbidden states between clusters are treated exactly. The ground-state and excitation energies of ⁹_ΛBe are reproduced within 100–200 keV accuracy when using the three-range Minnesota force for the αα interaction, but this accuracy is conditional on the specific choice of the effective NN force and the newly derived ΛN potential.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the 3α and ααΛ systems using singularity-free T-matrices derived from two-cluster RGM kernels, which incorporate the exact treatment of Pauli-forbidden states between α clusters. For the αα interaction, they employed the three-range Minnesota effective two-nucleon force; for the Λα interaction, they derived a new effective ΛN potential (the "SB potential") of two-range Gaussian form for each spin-singlet and triplet state, generated from the phase-shift behavior of the quark-model hyperon-nucleon interaction fss2 using an inversion method based on supersymmetric quantum mechanics. The Λα T-matrices were obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with these potentials, and the energy dependence of the αα RGM kernel was treated self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- The Λ-separation energy of ⁹_ΛBe is B_Λ(⁹_ΛBe) = 6.71 ± 0.04 MeV (experimental value from the literature).
- The Λ-separation energy of ⁵_ΛHe is B_Λ(⁵_ΛHe) = 3.12 ± 0.02 MeV (experimental value from the literature).
- The triton binding energy calculated with the fss2 model is B_t = 8.519 MeV in a 50-channel calculation, compared to the empirical value B_t^exp = 8.482 MeV.
- The effective ΛN potential parameters are: v(¹S₀) = -128.0 exp(-0.8908 r²) + 1015 exp(-5.383 r²) and v(³S₁) = -56.31 f exp(-0.7517 r²) + 1072 exp(-13.74 r²), with f=1 and r in fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-cluster Faddeev formalism, which was applied to the three-dineutron, 3α, three-nucleon bound state, and hypertriton systems (Fujiwara et al., 2002–2004). It contrasts with earlier 3α calculations using the orthogonality condition model (OCM) by Hiyama et al. (1997) and others, which typically yielded large overbinding for the ground state, and with the 3α OCM of Tobita and Deguchi (2003) and Dote and Ebert (2003), which gave ground-state energies less than 1.5 MeV. The present formalism enables a consistent description of αα, 3α, and ααΛ systems using a unique set of baryon-baryon interactions, and provides a tractable effective ΛN force derived from quark-model interactions for future cluster calculations of p-shell hypernuclei, such as ΛΛα Faddeev calculations.
