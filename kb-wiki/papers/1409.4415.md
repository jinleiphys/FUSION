# On the Annihilation Rate of WIMPs

> We develop a formalism that allows one to systematically calculate the WIMP annihilation rate into gamma rays whose energy far exceeds the weak scale. A factorization theorem is presented which separates the radiative corrections stemming from initial state potential interactions from loops involving the final state. This separation allows us to go beyond the fixed order calculation, which is polluted by large infrared logarithms. For the case of Majorana WIMPs transforming in the adjoint representation of SU(2), we present the result for the resummed rate at leading double log accuracy in terms of two initial state partial wave matrix elements and one hard matching coefficient. For a given model, one may calculate the cross section by calculating the tree level matching coefficient and determining the value of a local four fermion operator. We find that the effects of resummation can be as large as 100% for a 20 TeV WIMP. The generalization of the formalism to other types of WIMPs is discussed.

## Key claim
For a 20 TeV WIMP, resummation effects can be as large as 100%, but for lighter masses relevant to the thermal relic scenario (around 3 TeV), leading-log resummation modifies the Sudakov factors only at the 10% level, and the total effect of radiative corrections on the semi-inclusive photon annihilation rate is found to be percent-level when comparably-sized Sommerfeld factors are included.

## Method
The authors develop a hybrid effective field theory combining Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) and Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to factorize the WIMP annihilation rate into photons. They separate initial-state potential interactions from final-state radiative corrections, allowing resummation of large infrared logarithms via renormalization group and rapidity renormalization group running. For Majorana WIMPs in the adjoint representation of SU(2), they compute the resummed rate at leading double-log accuracy in terms of two initial-state partial-wave matrix elements and one hard matching coefficient.

## Key numbers
- Resummation effects can be as large as 100% for a 20 TeV WIMP.
- For the thermal relic value of 3 TeV, the Sudakov factor modification is approximately 10%.
- The mass splitting between charged and neutral states for an SU(2) triplet is approximately 170 MeV, independent of the specific WIMP mass.
- The exponent in the Sudakov factors is given by a = 3α/π, where α = g₂²/(4π).
- The power counting parameter is λ ≡ M_W/(E_γ ~ M_χ).

## Context
This work extends the calculation of WIMP annihilation rates beyond fixed-order perturbation theory, which is polluted by large infrared logarithms of the form α log²(M_χ²/M_W²). It addresses claims by Cohen et al. (2013) and Fan et al. (2013) that higher-loop corrections could result in a significant factor-of-a-few decrease in the annihilation rate to γ + X. The formalism generalizes earlier hybrid effective theories used for radiative onium decays by Leibovich and builds on SCET in the electroweak context explored by Aneesh. The results enable model-independent calculation of the annihilation cross section for any WIMP model by evaluating tree-level matching coefficients and four-fermion operator matrix elements.
