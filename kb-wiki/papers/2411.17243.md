# $^{19}$F$(p,γ)$$^{20}$Ne reaction rate and the puzzling calcium abundance in metal poor stars

> The $^{19}$F$(p,γ)$$^{20}$Ne reaction is the only process to break out of the CNO cycle at temperature below 0.1 GK and may serve as the origin of calcium in first generation of stars after the Big Bang. In the recent measurement, the Jinping Underground Nuclear Experiment (JUNA) obtained the rate of $^{19}$F$(p,γ)$$^{20}$Ne reaction, significantly larger than the previously recommended values. In this work, we perform the theoretical studies of the $^{19}$F$(p,γ)$$^{20}$Ne reaction using the Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC). At temperature around 0.1 GK, the predicted rate by GSM-CC is close to the rate found by JUNA. Thus, based on GSM-CC, the break-out reaction $^{19}$F$(p,γ)$$^{20}$Ne from the CNO-cycle might win over its competing reaction $^{19}$F$(p,α)$$^{16}$O, and produce enough calcium in the metal poor stars.

## Key claim

The Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) predicts a reaction rate for $^{19}$F$(p,\gamma)$$^{20}$Ne that is close to the JUNA experimental rate at around 0.1 GK, but the GSM-CC rate is lower than JUNA below 0.1 GK and becomes significantly larger than JUNA at higher temperatures, suggesting the breakout reaction might win over the competing $^{19}$F$(p,\alpha)$$^{16}$O reaction and produce enough calcium in metal-poor stars.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic calculations of the $^{19}$F$(p,\gamma)$$^{20}$Ne reaction using the Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC), which provides a unified description of nuclear structure and reactions. The $^{12}$C nucleus was treated as an inert core with a Woods-Saxon potential, and the Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki finite-range two-body force was used for valence nucleon interactions. The astrophysical S-factors were computed for electromagnetic transitions (E1, M1, E2) connecting scattering states of $^{20}$Ne to the capturing states, with the experimental ground-state energy of $^{19}$F used to ensure the correct proton separation energy in $^{20}$Ne.

## Key numbers

- At $T_9$ = 0.1, the GSM-CC reaction rate is enhanced by a factor of 2.24 relative to the NACRE recommended rate.
- The JUNA collaboration reported a rate at $T_9$ = 0.1 that is 7.4 times larger than NACRE.
- The $3^-$ resonance at $E_{\rm c.m.}$ = 225.2 keV (JUNA value) gives an important contribution to the reaction rate around 0.1 GK.
- The $2^-$ resonance at $E_{\rm c.m.}$ = 212.7(10) keV has a strength less than 10% of that of the $3^-$ resonance.
- At $T_9$ = 0.01, the GSM-CC rate is over 100 times larger than the NACRE recommendation.
- At around 0.15 GK, the GSM-CC rate is about 1.7 times larger than the JUNA rate.
- The calculated binding energy of $^{19}$F with respect to the $^{12}$C core is -55.710 MeV, close to the experimental value -55.640 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the $^{19}$F$(p,\gamma)$$^{20}$Ne reaction that used either the R-matrix method (deBoer et al., JUNA collaboration) or the direct capture model (Wiescher et al., 1999). The GSM-CC approach provides the first microscopic study of this reaction. The results are relevant to resolving the calcium abundance puzzle in metal-poor stars, where NACRE rates predicted calcium abundances nearly two orders of magnitude lower than observations (Clarkson et al., 2021). The GSM-CC rates support the JUNA finding that the breakout reaction may explain the calcium abundance in first-generation stars, though with quantitative differences at temperatures below 0.1 GK and above 0.12 GK.
