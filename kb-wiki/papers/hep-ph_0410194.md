# Restoration of chiral symmetry in excited hadrons

> Physics of the low-lying and high-lying hadrons in the light flavor sector is reviewed. While the low-lying hadrons are strongly affected by the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry, in the high-lying hadrons the chiral symmetry is restored. A manifestation of the chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons is a persistence of the chiral multiplet structure in both baryon and meson spectra. Meson and baryon chiral multiplets are classified. A relation between the chiral symmetry restoration and the string picture of excited hadrons is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while low-lying hadrons are strongly affected by spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry, in high-lying hadrons (starting approximately from masses around 1.7 GeV) the chiral symmetry is effectively restored, meaning that the role of quark condensates that break chiral symmetry in the vacuum becomes progressively less important high in the spectrum rather than the vacuum itself becoming symmetric.

## Method

The author reviews empirical hadron spectra from the Particle Data Group and from the London-St. Petersburg group's partial wave analysis of proton-antiproton annihilation at LEAR (1.8-2.4 GeV), classifying observed meson and baryon states into chiral multiplets. The argument for chiral symmetry restoration is supported by operator product expansion analysis of two-point correlators at large space-like momenta, where contributions from chiral noninvariant condensates are suppressed by inverse powers of Q^2, and by the observed approximate equality of vector and axial vector spectral densities from tau decay data from ALEPH and OPAL collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The quark condensate at renormalization scale 1 GeV is approximately ⟨ūu⟩ ≃ ⟨dd⟩ ≃ -(240 ± 10 MeV)^3
- The nucleon excitation spectrum shows parity doubling starting from approximately M ∼ 1.7 GeV region
- The difference between vector and axial vector spectral densities becomes strongly reduced towards m = √s ∼ 1.7 GeV, as measured in tau decay by ALEPH and OPAL collaborations
- The pion mass relation is m_π^2 = -(1/f_π^2) × (m_u + m_d)/2 × (⟨ūu⟩ + ⟨dd⟩) + O(m_u,d^2)

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal (Glozman 2001) that parity doubling in excited hadrons reflects restoration of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, and formalizes the concept as "chiral symmetry restoration of the second kind" developed with Cohen and Glozman (2002-2003). The paper contrasts with constituent quark model approaches (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio 1961, Manohar and Georgi 1984) that are appropriate for low-lying states, and argues that a simple potential constituent quark model is incompatible with chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons. The work enables future experimental searches for chiral multiplets at facilities like PANDA at GSI, JPARC, JLAB, Bonn, SPRING8, and BES.
