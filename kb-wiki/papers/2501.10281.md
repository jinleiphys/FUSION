# $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$ tribaryons

> We study the possible existence of bound states of three $Ω_{bbb}$ baryons. We consider only $S$ wave interactions and we start from recent lattice QCD results which give a strongly attractive potential between two $Ω_{bbb}$ baryons in the $^1S_0$ channel. We analyze different scenarios. At baryonic level, the $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$ interaction could be understood to be basically spin-independent, so that the two contributing channels, $^1S_0$ and $^5S_2$, would have a very similar interaction. This baryonic analysis leads to the existence of bound states in the three-body system. At the quark level, repulsive effects would appear in the $^5S_2$ channel, making it more repulsive than the $^1S_0$ channel. We study the effect of such repulsion in terms of its range.

# Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb} tribaryons

## Key claim

The three-Ω_{bbb} system can form bound states, but only if the two-body interaction in the spin-2 channel (^5S_2) is sufficiently similar to the attractive spin-0 channel (^1S_0); if quark Pauli blocking introduces strong repulsion in the ^5S_2 channel, the binding energy is reduced but the system may still bind, with the binding energy depending on the range and strength of that repulsion.

## Method

The authors solved nonrelativistic Faddeev equations for three identical spin-3/2 fermions in S-wave, using the lattice QCD-derived Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb} potential from Mathieu et al. (2024) for the ^1S_0 channel. They first performed a single-channel calculation with only the ^1S_0 two-body interaction, then extended to a two-channel calculation including both ^1S_0 and ^5S_2 channels, modeling the ^5S_2 interaction either as identical to the ^1S_0 interaction (baryonic-level assumption) or as a repulsively modified version (quark-level Pauli blocking), parameterized by a strength factor A.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy of the three-Ω_{bbb} system with identical ^1S_0 and ^5S_2 interactions: −393.8 MeV (without Coulomb), −363.9 MeV (with Coulomb).
- First excited-state binding energy with identical interactions: −94.9 MeV (without Coulomb), −81.0 MeV (with Coulomb).
- The lattice QCD result for the Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb} ^1S_0 two-body binding energy from Mathieu et al. (2024) is 81 MeV.
- The repulsive modification radius r_0 = 0.104 fm, the position of the minimum of the ^1S_0 potential.
- With quark Pauli blocking repulsion (parameter A), the expected binding energy lies between 250 and 350 MeV for realistic values of A (A ≤ 20).

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of doubly-heavy dibaryons, specifically the Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb} system from Mathieu et al. (2024), to the three-body case. It contradicts the finding of Tian et al. (2024) that no three-Ω_{bbb} bound state exists using the same lattice interactions, by showing that inclusion of the ^5S_2 channel (neglected in that prior work) can produce binding. The analysis also contrasts with the one-boson-exchange model of Ming et al. (2024), which found bound three-Ω systems. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of the Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb} ^5S_2 interaction to determine definitively whether the three-body system binds.
