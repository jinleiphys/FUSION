# Recent Developments in the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) Method

> Recent results on electromagnetic reactions into the continuum of systems with A from 3 to 7 are presented. They have been obtained using the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method. The method is shortly reviewed, emphasizing how all the results, though obtained with the sole ingredient of the N-N potential, contain the full complicated dynamics of the A-body system, both in the initial and in the final states.

## Key claim

The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method enables *ab initio* calculation of electromagnetic reactions into the continuum for nuclear systems with A from 3 to 7 using only the nucleon-nucleon (N-N) potential as input, but the results are limited by non-relativistic kinematics as shown by frame-dependent shifts in the quasi-elastic peak at higher momentum transfers.

## Method

The LIT method solves a Schrodinger-like equation with a source term and complex energy (H - E0 - ω0 + iΓ)Ψ̃ = Θ|0⟩, where the solution has bound-state asymptotic conditions, avoiding explicit calculation of continuum wave functions. For inclusive reactions, the norm of this solution gives the Lorentz integral transform of the response function, which is then inverted to obtain R(ω). For exclusive reactions, a second equation with a potential source term is solved, and the overlap of the two solutions is connected via a Lorentz transform to the function F(E), from which the transition matrix element is obtained through an integral relation. The bound-state problems are solved using Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonics (CHH) and Effective Interaction in the Hyperspherical Harmonics expansions (EIHH) techniques.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal response functions for 3-body systems (³H and ³He) calculated with AV18+UIX potential show 3-body force effects visible in the quasi-elastic peak
- For ⁴He longitudinal 2-body electrodisintegration at kinematics with q=299 MeV/c and ω=57.78 MeV, the PWIA response is 110.1 (GeV/c)⁻³ sr⁻¹, with antisymmetrization effects of +10.9% and combined AS+FSI effects of -45%
- At higher momentum transfer q=680 MeV/c and ω=146.68 MeV for ⁴He, the PWIA response is 1.79 (GeV/c)⁻³ sr⁻¹, with AS effects of -0.4% and AS+FSI effects of +28.8%
- For ⁴He at q=544 MeV/c and ω=126.6 MeV, FSI effects become negligible at +0.6%
- Total photodisintegration of ⁶He shows two resonances (likely soft mode of halo neutrons against alpha-core and giant resonance of protons against neutrons), while ⁶Li shows a single resonance
- Total photoabsorption cross section of ⁷Li calculated fully *ab initio* with the LIT method

## Context

This work extends the LIT method benchmarked in 2- and 3-body systems (ELO94, Lapiana, Martinelli, Golak) to larger systems up to A=7, using both semirealistic potentials (MTI-III, AV4') and realistic potentials (AV18+UIX). The results enable comparison with experimental data from Saclay, Bates, Retzlaff, and Ahrens, and reveal limitations of non-relativistic calculations through frame dependence of the quasi-elastic peak. The method opens the possibility for *ab initio* studies of continuum reactions in light nuclei without calculating explicit continuum wave functions, with future work needed on 3-body force effects and more precise experimental data for systems like ⁶Li.
