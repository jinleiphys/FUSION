# Coupled-channels treatment of $^7\mathrm{Be}(p,γ)^8\mathrm{B}$ in effective field theory

> The E1 and M1 contributions to $^7\mathrm{Be}(p,γ)^8\mathrm{B}$ at low energies are calculated in halo effective field theory. The excited $^7\mathrm{Be}^\star$ core is included as an explicit degree of freedom in a coupled-channels calculation. The E1 transition is calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order. The leading contribution from M1 transition that gives significant contribution in a narrow energy region around the $1^+$ resonance state of $^8$B is included. We compare our results with previous halo effective field theory calculations that also included the $^7\mathrm{Be}^\star$ as an explicit degree of freedom. We disagree with these previous calculations in both the formal expressions and also in the analysis. Bayesian inference of the data gives $S_{17}(0)=21.0(7)$ eV b when combined with the expected theory error.

## Key claim
The paper calculates the E1 and M1 contributions to the $^7\mathrm{Be}(p,\gamma)^8\mathrm{B}$ reaction using halo effective field theory with a coupled-channels formalism that includes the excited $^7\mathrm{Be}^\star$ core as an explicit degree of freedom, obtaining $S_{17}(0)=21.0(7)$ eV b from Bayesian inference combined with expected theory error, while explicitly disagreeing with previous halo EFT calculations in both formal expressions and analysis.

## Method
The authors constructed two versions of halo EFT: one without the excited $^7\mathrm{Be}^\star$ core (EFT$_\text{gs}$) and one including it as an explicit degree of freedom (EFT$_\star$) in a coupled-channels formalism. The E1 transition was calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), and the leading M1 contribution around the $1^+$ resonance state of $^8$B was included. Bayesian inference was applied to fit EFT parameters to direct capture data, with the Coulomb interactions incorporated through dimensional regularization in the power divergence subtraction scheme.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of $^8$B: $B=0.1364$ MeV below the proton-$^7$Be threshold
- Binding momentum: $\gamma=14.961$ MeV
- Inverse Bohr radius: $k_C=23.956$ MeV
- Excitation energy of $^7\mathrm{Be}^\star$: $E_\star=0.4291$ MeV, with corresponding momentum scale $\gamma_\Delta=26.54$ MeV
- Resonance momentum: $p_R=32.15$ MeV, corresponding to resonance energy $E_R=0.630\pm0.003$ keV
- Reduced mass: $\mu\approx820.49$ MeV
- $S_{17}(0)=21.0(7)$ eV b from Bayesian inference combining data with expected theory error
- $s$-wave scattering length in $S=1$ channel: $a_0^{(1)}=17.34^{+1.11}_{-1.33}$ fm

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channels halo EFT formalism previously applied to the mirror reaction $^7\mathrm{Li}(n,\gamma)^8\mathrm{Li}$ (Higa 2020) to the $^7\mathrm{Be}(p,\gamma)^8\mathrm{B}$ reaction with Coulomb interactions. It disagrees with previous halo EFT calculations by Zhang, Nollett, and Phillips (2014, 2015, 2017) regarding formal expressions, power counting, and analysis, particularly in the initial state $s$-wave scattering and final $p$-wave bound state. The results enable improved low-energy extrapolations of capture data needed for precision solar neutrino studies, with the two-body current contribution to E1 transition estimated to enter at N$^3$LO rather than NLO as previously claimed.
