# Thermal modification of bottomonium spectra from QCD sum rules with the maximum entropy method

> The bottomonium spectral functions at finite temperature are analyzed by employing QCD sum rules with the maximum entropy method. This approach enables us to extract the spectral functions without any phenomenological parametrization, and thus to visualize deformation of the spectral functions due to temperature effects estimated from quenched lattice QCD data. As a result, it is found that Υand η_b survive in hot matter of temperature up to at least 2.3T_c and 2.1T_c, respectively, while χ_{b0} and χ_{b1} will disappear at T<2.5T_c. Furthermore, a detailed analysis of the vector channel shows that the spectral function in the region of the lowest peak at T=0 contains contributions from the excited states, Υ(2S) and Υ(3S), as well as the ground states Υ(1S). Our results at finite T are consistent with the picture that the excited states of bottomonia dissociate at lower temperature than that of the ground state. Assuming this picture, we find that Υ(2S) and Υ(3S) disappear at T=1.5-2.0T_c.

## Key claim

The bottomonium spectral functions extracted via QCD sum rules with the maximum entropy method (MEM) indicate that the S-wave states Υ and η_b survive in hot matter up to at least 2.3 T_c and 2.1 T_c respectively, while the P-wave states χ_{b0} and χ_{b1} disappear below 2.5 T_c; however, the authors caution that these results provide only qualitative guides for full QCD because the quenched lattice data used for the gluon condensates may deviate from full QCD behavior at the high temperatures relevant for bottomonium.

## Method

The authors employed QCD sum rules combined with the maximum entropy method (MEM) to extract bottomonium spectral functions without assuming a specific functional form. The operator product expansion (OPE) was used to compute the correlation functions for the vector (Υ), pseudoscalar (η_b), scalar (χ_{b0}), and axial-vector (χ_{b1}) channels, including contributions from the scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates up to mass dimension 4. The temperature dependence of the gluon condensates was obtained from quenched lattice QCD data for the energy density, pressure, and strong coupling constant, and the MEM analysis was applied to the Borel-transformed sum rules to reconstruct the spectral functions at various temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Υ (vector) lowest peak survives up to at least T/T_c > 2.3, with a bump still visible at T/T_c = 3.0.
- η_b (pseudoscalar) lowest peak survives up to at least T/T_c > 2.1.
- χ_{b0} (scalar) dissociation temperature range: T/T_c = 1.3 - 2.5.
- χ_{b1} (axial-vector) dissociation temperature: T/T_c < 2.5.
- Υ(2S) and Υ(3S) excited states disappear at T = 1.5 - 2.0 T_c, assuming the picture that excited states dissociate at lower temperature than the ground state.
- At zero temperature, the MEM-extracted peak masses are: m_Υ = 9.56 GeV, m_η_b = 9.51 GeV, m_χ_{b0} = 10.15 GeV, m_χ_{b1} = 10.42 GeV, which are higher than experimental masses (9.460, 9.389, 9.859, 9.893 GeV respectively) due to contributions from excited states.
- The residue of the vector channel peak at T=0 is 0.0476 GeV, consistent with the sum of residues from Υ(1S), Υ(2S), and Υ(3S) (experimental sum: 0.0483 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous MEM-based QCD sum rule analysis of charmonium spectral functions at finite temperature (Gubler 2011) to the bottomonium system, which has several excited states below the continuum threshold unlike charmonium. The results are consistent with the sequential melting picture observed in lattice QCD studies (Aarts 2011) and with experimental data from the LHC (Chatrchyan 2011) showing larger suppression of excited bottomonium states relative to the ground state. The study enables comparison between charmonium and bottomonium behavior at finite temperature and provides qualitative predictions for the dissociation temperatures of S-wave and P-wave bottomonia that can be tested by future full QCD lattice calculations.
