# Analytical solution of magneto-hydrodynamics with acceleration effects of Bjorken expansion in heavy-ion collisions

> In this work, we study the 1+1 longitudinal acceleration expansion of hot and dense quark matter as a conducting relativistic fluid with the electric conductivity $σ$. The plasma has embedded in the presence of electric and magnetic fields which are perpendicular together in the transverse plane. In order to be more realistic, we generalize the Bjorken solution, which includes the acceleration effects on the fluid expansion. We apply a perturbation fashion in initial condition to solve the relativistic magneto-hydrodynamics equations. This procedure leads us to achieve the exact algebraic expressions for both electric and magnetic fields. We also find the effects of the electromagnetic fields on the acceleration of the fluid, and the correction of energy density obtained from the magneto-hydrodynamics solutions.

## Key claim

This work provides an analytical solution for relativistic magneto-hydrodynamics (RMHD) that includes longitudinal acceleration effects beyond the standard boost-invariant Bjorken flow, under the condition that the electromagnetic fields are weak compared to the energy density of the fluid (perturbative regime).

## Method

The authors solve the 1+1 dimensional resistive relativistic magneto-hydrodynamic (RRMHD) equations in Milne coordinates for a conducting fluid with finite electrical conductivity σ, assuming the electric field points along the x-direction and the magnetic field along the y-direction. They apply a perturbative expansion in the electromagnetic field strength around the Bjorken solution, treating the acceleration parameter λ(τ,η) as a small deviation from unity, and obtain analytical expressions for the electric and magnetic fields, the acceleration parameter, and the energy density correction.

## Key numbers

- The initial magnetic field at mid-rapidity is set to b(τ₀,0) = 0.0018 GeV²/e.
- The magnetic field in vacuum (σ → 0) decays as b_y(τ,0) ≈ b₁ (τ₀³/τ³).
- For infinite conductivity (σ → ∞), the magnetic field becomes b_y(τ) = b₀ (τ₀/τ) and the electric field vanishes (e_x = 0).
- The homogeneous (field-free) acceleration parameter solution is v^h(τ,η) = A₀ (τ₀/τ)^(2/3) η, with κ = 1/3.
- The electrical conductivity values used in numerical evaluations are σ = 0.023 fm⁻¹, 0.2 fm⁻¹, 0.4 fm⁻¹, and 0.8 fm⁻¹.
- Proper time values in the figures range from τ = 0.5 fm to τ = 3.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the standard Bjorken boost-invariant solution (Bjorken 1983) by incorporating longitudinal acceleration effects from electromagnetic fields in a resistive fluid, generalizing earlier studies of RMHD with longitudinal boost invariance (such as those cited as b31a, b31b). The analytical solutions for the electromagnetic fields and acceleration parameter enable more realistic modeling of quark-gluon plasma evolution in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for phenomena like the Chiral Magnetic Effect and pressure anisotropy, and can be compared to numerical RMHD simulations (e.g., those cited as b26, b27, b28, b29, b30, b31).
