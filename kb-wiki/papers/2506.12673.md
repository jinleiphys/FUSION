# Imprint of $α$-Clustering on Ab Initio Correlations in Relativistic Light Ion Collisions

> This study investigates the influence of $α$-cluster structures in relativistic light nuclear collisions. Using a cluster framework, I extract the characteristics of the nucleonic configurations of $^{16}$O and $^{20}$Ne as predicted by various \textit{ab-initio} models, including Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT), Variational Monte Carlo (VMC), and the Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM). Additionally, I analyze configurations derived from a three-parameter Fermi (3pF) density function. The investigation focuses on the effects of cluster parameters on two-point correlators using a rotor model for symmetric collisions ($^{16}$O+$^{16}$O and $^{20}$Ne+$^{20}$Ne) and asymmetric collisions ($^{208}$Pb+$^{16}$O and $^{208}$Pb+$^{20}$Ne). The cluster parameters are determined by minimizing the \textit{chi-square} statistic to align the nucleon distributions with those predicted by the aforementioned theories. The results reveal that perturbative calculations effectively capture the structural features of these nuclei, while comparisons with Monte Carlo simulations validate these findings. Furthermore, the analysis reveals distinct cluster geometries: VMC suggests tetrahedral shapes, while NLEFT, PGCM, and 3pF indicate irregular triangular pyramids. Notably, NLEFT shows a bowling pin-like $α$ cluster structure for $^{20}$Ne. The study also identifies constraints on cluster parameters in the different oxygen structures, with a gradual increase in $\varepsilon_2\{2\}$ for the states of $α$+$^{12}$C. Accurate modeling of asymmetric collisions necessitates a range of nucleons from heavy spherical nuclei, leading to weighted correlators in perturbative calculations. I demonstrate consistency between perturbative calculations and Monte Carlo models, with analytical calculations providing more insights into asymmetric than symmetric collisions.

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that perturbative analytical calculations using a rigid rotor cluster model can effectively capture the structural features of alpha-clustered light nuclei (16O and 20Ne) as predicted by ab initio models, with the caveat that the sensitivity to cluster parameters enters entirely through fluctuations rather than the mean energy density, and that analytical calculations provide more insight for asymmetric collisions than for symmetric ones.

## Method

The author employs a cluster framework where nucleon distributions are modeled as Gaussian functions centered at alpha cluster positions, with cluster parameters (inter-cluster distance ℓc and alpha particle size rL) determined by minimizing chi-square statistics against nucleon configurations from NLEFT, VMC, PGCM, and a three-parameter Fermi (3pF) density function. The transverse one-body and two-body densities are computed with orientational averaging over Euler angles, and these are used to calculate initial-state correlators, specifically the variance of total energy var(E/⟨E⟩) and the mean-square elliptic anisotropy ε2{2}², for symmetric collisions (16O+16O, 20Ne+20Ne) and asymmetric collisions (208Pb+16O, 208Pb+20Ne) within a rigid rotor model. The perturbative calculations are validated against Monte Carlo simulations and the TRENTo initial-state generator.

## Key numbers

- For 16O: 3pF density parameters are R0 = 2.608 fm, a0 = 0.513 fm, w = -0.051
- For 20Ne: 3pF density parameters are R0 = 2.791 fm, a0 = 0.698 fm, w = -0.168
- The average energy density for spherical ion collisions is given by ⟨E⟩ev = 3As² / [4π(Rs² + 3w²)], where As is the total number of participating nucleons
- The variance for spherical collisions scales as var(E/⟨E⟩) ≈ As⁻²((1/6a²) - (11/12)) + As⁻¹(2/3) where a ≡ w/Rs, to first order in a
- The elliptic anisotropy for spherical collisions scales as ε2{2}² ≈ As⁻²((1/3a²) - (155/54)) + As⁻¹(128/54), to first order in a

## Context

This work extends the cluster model approach introduced by Rybczynski (2017) for studying alpha clustering in relativistic ion collisions, building on recent studies by Giacalone (2024), Li (2025), and Zhang (2024) that investigated how ab initio models predict tetrahedron structures for 16O and bowling-pin shapes for 20Ne. The study enables systematic comparison of different ab initio predictions (NLEFT, VMC, PGCM) through their imprint on flow observables, and provides analytical tools for interpreting upcoming SMOG2 experiment data at LHCb for asymmetric collisions like 208Pb+16O and 208Pb+20Ne.
