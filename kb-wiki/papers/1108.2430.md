# Pion Elastic Form Factor in a Rather Broad Range of Momentum Transfers from Local-Duality QCD Sum Rule

> 4 pages, 1 figure, talk presented at the "XIV International Conference on Hadron Spectroscopy", 13 - 17 June 2011, Munich, Germany, to appear in the proceedings

# Pion Elastic Form Factor in a Rather Broad Range of Momentum Transfers from Local-Duality QCD Sum Rule

## Key claim

The local-duality (LD) QCD sum rule model for the pion elastic form factor, which assumes the effective continuum threshold reaches its asymptotic limit already at moderate momentum transfers, provides an accurate description of experimental data for Q^2 above 3-4 GeV^2 and predicts significantly lower values than other theoretical approaches at Q^2 = 10-20 GeV^2. The authors argue that large deviations from the LD limit at Q^2 = 20-50 GeV^2, as claimed in recent analyses, are unlikely.

## Method

The authors construct a local-duality model for the pion elastic form factor F_pi(Q^2) by formulating an effective continuum threshold s_eff(Q) that interpolates between its behavior at Q=0, determined by a Ward identity, and its asymptotic limit at large Q, determined by factorization properties of the perturbative three-point spectral function. They test the accuracy of this LD model by comparing with exact solutions of the Schrodinger equation for quantum-mechanical systems with power-law confining potentials (r^n with n=2, 1, 1/2) that mimic the Coulomb-plus-confining structure of QCD.

## Key numbers

- The effective continuum threshold parametrization uses Q_0^2 = 2.02 GeV^2 as the scale parameter in the tanh interpolation.
- The LD model provides a perfect description of experimental data in the region Q^2 = 1-2.5 GeV^2.
- At Q^2 = 10 GeV^2, the LD model prediction differs from the experimental value by roughly two standard deviations.
- The LD limit s_eff(infinity) = 4 pi^2 f_pi^2 is reached already at Q^2 = 5-6 GeV^2.
- For Q^2 >= 20-30 GeV^2, the quantum-mechanical toy model shows the LD model yields rather accurate numerical results.
- The maximum deviations of LD-model predictions from exact form factors occur in the range Q^2 approximately 4-8 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the local-duality sum rule approach of Braguta (2007) and addresses recent analyses (referenced as "recent") that claim F_pi(Q^2) remains much larger than perturbative QCD results even at Q^2 around 50 GeV^2, as well as the nonlocal-condensate sum rule approach of Bakulev (2004) which observes large local-duality violation at Q^2 = 10-20 GeV^2. The authors use quantum-mechanical models with power-law confining potentials, following Lucha (1998), to estimate the accuracy of the LD method. The analysis enables future experimental measurements of F_pi in the Q^2 = 4-10 GeV^2 region to discriminate between competing theoretical predictions and determine the behavior of the pion form factor at asymptotically large momentum transfers.
