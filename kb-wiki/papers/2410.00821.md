# Recent $J/ψ$ measurements at the PHENIX experiment

> Quarkonium is an ideal probe to explore the properties of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Unlike Large Hadron Collider (LHC) measurements, quarkonium production at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) has different production mechanisms, can access different kinematic phase space and may experience different medium densities/temperatures. The PHENIX experiment has collected a large $J/ψ\to μ^{+}μ^{-}$ data set within its unique pseudorapidity region of $1.2<|η|<2.2$ in $p+p$, $p+A$ and $A+A$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV from 2014 to 2016. Latest results of normalized charged particle multiplicity dependent normalized forward $J/ψ$ yields and normalized forward $ψ(2S)$ to $J/ψ$ ratio in 200 GeV $p+p$ collisions as well as forward $J/ψ$ azimuthal anisotropy in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions will be shown. Comparison with other RHIC and LHC measurements and latest theoretical calculations will be discussed. These PHENIX results provide their unique contributions in improving the understanding of the multi-parton interaction contribution to charmonium production and the recombination/coalescence contribution to charmonium formation within Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The PHENIX experiment's forward $J/\psi$ measurements in 200 GeV $p+p$ and Au+Au collisions show that multi-parton interaction (MPI) effects are necessary to model charmonium production in $p+p$ collisions, while the forward $J/\psi$ azimuthal anisotropy in Au+Au collisions is consistent with zero within uncertainties, suggesting little rapidity dependence at RHIC energies.

## Method

The PHENIX experiment reconstructed $J/\psi \to \mu^{+}\mu^{-}$ decays in the forward muon arms covering $1.2<|\eta|<2.4$ using 47 pb$^{-1}$ of 200 GeV $p+p$ data from 2015 and 14.5 nb$^{-1}$ of 200 GeV Au+Au data from 2014 and 2016. Self-normalized event multiplicity dependent $J/\psi$ yields and $\psi(2S)$ to $J/\psi$ ratios were measured in $p+p$ collisions, while $J/\psi$ azimuthal anisotropy ($v_{2}$) was determined via the event plane method in Au+Au collisions for centralities $0\%-50\%$, $10\%-60\%$, and $10\%-40\%$.

## Key numbers

- 47 pb$^{-1}$ integrated luminosity of 200 GeV $p+p$ data collected in 2015
- 14.5 nb$^{-1}$ integrated luminosity of 200 GeV Au+Au data collected in 2014 and 2016
- Forward $J/\psi$ measured in pseudorapidity region $1.2<|y|<2.2$ in both collision systems
- $J/\psi$ $v_{2}$ measured in $10\%-60\%$ centrality Au+Au collisions with $0<p_{T}<5$ GeV/c, consistent with zero within uncertainties
- Self-normalized $\psi(2S)$ to $J/\psi$ ratio in $p+p$ collisions consistent with unity within $\sim 1\sigma$ uncertainties
- PYTHIA8 Detroit calculations with MPI effects agree with PHENIX measurements within $\sim 1\sigma$ uncertainties; calculations without MPI effects fail to reproduce data

## Context

These results extend the program of quarkonium measurements at RHIC, complementing STAR mid-rapidity $J/\psi$ measurements (STAR, 2017) and ALICE forward $J/\psi$ measurements at LHC energies. The multiplicity-dependent $J/\psi$ yields show similar trends to STAR results when measured in the same pseudorapidity region, while the forward $J/\psi$ $v_{2}$ at RHIC is systematically lower than ALICE forward $J/\psi$ $v_{2}$ in Pb+Pb collisions at 5 TeV (ALICE, 2021). These measurements enable improved constraints on the role of multi-parton interactions in charmonium production and the interplay between primordial production and coalescence mechanisms in quark-gluon plasma at RHIC energies.
