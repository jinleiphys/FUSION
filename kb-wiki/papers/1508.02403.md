# Strong diquark correlations inside the proton

> Quantum Chromodynamics is thought to be the relativistic quantum field theory that describes the strong interaction of the Standard Model. This interaction produces mesons but it is also able to generate quark-quark (diquark) correlations inside baryons. In this work, we employ a continuum approach to QCD based on Dyson-Schwinger equations to calculate the electromagnetic form factors of the proton and analyze in a deeper way the consequences of having strong diquark correlations. Comparison with the experimental data reveals that the presence of strong diquark correlations within the proton is sufficient to understand empirical extractions of the flavour-separated form factors. The explained reduction of the ratios $F_{1}^{d}/F_{1}^{u}$ and $F_{2}^{d}/F_{2}^{u}$ at high $Q^{2}$ in the quark-diquark picture are responsible of the precocious scaling of the $F_{2}^{p}/F_{1}^{p}$ observed experimentally.

## Key claim

The presence of strong scalar and pseudovector diquark correlations within the proton, computed using a Dyson-Schwinger equation approach, is sufficient to explain the empirical flavour-separated electromagnetic form factors, with the reduction of the ratios \(F_{1}^{d}/F_{1}^{u}\) and \(F_{2}^{d}/F_{2}^{u}\) at high \(Q^{2}\) being responsible for the precocious scaling of \(F_{2}^{p}/F_{1}^{p}\) observed experimentally.

## Method

The author employed a Poincare-covariant Faddeev equation framework based on Dyson-Schwinger equations, treating the nucleon as a quark-plus-diquark bound state with both scalar-isoscalar and pseudovector-isotriplet diquark correlations. The nucleon electromagnetic current was evaluated using the complete Faddeev wave function, including the dominant scalar diquark component (approximately 62% of the proton's normalisation) and the rest-frame \(L=1\) orbital angular momentum contributions.

## Key numbers

- Scalar diquark mass scale: \(m_{[ud]_{0^+}} \approx 0.7-0.8\) GeV
- Pseudovector diquark mass scale: \(m_{\{uu\}_{1^+}} \approx 0.9-1.1\) GeV, with \(m_{\{dd\}_{1^+}}=m_{\{ud\}_{1^+}} = m_{\{uu\}_{1^+}}\) in the isospin symmetric limit
- Scalar diquark strength in the nucleon: approximately 62% of the proton's normalisation
- The leading Faddeev amplitude \(S_1(|p|,\cos\theta)\) peaks at \(|p|\simeq M_N/6\) and \(\cos\theta=1\), corresponding to \(p_q \approx P/2 \approx p_d\)

## Context

This work extends the accumulated body of evidence from two decades of studying two- and three-body bound-state problems in hadron physics (Cahill 1987, Bender 1996, Cloet 2008, Segovia 2014, Segovia 2015) and is supported by lattice QCD simulations (Alexandrou 2006, Babich 2007). The results enable quantitative tests of the diquark picture through comparison with experimental data on flavour-separated form factors, with the prediction that at large \(x=Q^2/M_N^2\) the \(d\)-quark contribution should scale as \(F^d \sim F^u/x\), requiring measurements at \(x>4\) for confirmation.
