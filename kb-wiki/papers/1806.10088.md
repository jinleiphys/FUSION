# QCD determination of the magnetic field dependence of QCD and hadronic parameters

> A set of four Finite Energy QCD sum rules are used to determine the magnetic field dependence of the sum of the up- and down-quark masses of QCD, $(m_u + m_d)$, the pion decay constant $f_π$, the pion mass $m_π$, the gluon condensate, $\langle 0| α_s\, G^2|0\rangle$, and the squared energy threshold for the onset of perturbative QCD, $s_0$, related to the Polyakov loop of lattice QCD.

## Key claim
Using four finite energy QCD sum rules (FESR) with three different current correlators, the magnetic field dependence of QCD and hadronic parameters is determined to leading order in an expansion in powers of \(eB\), but the results for the gluon condensate are strongly dependent on which of three possible assumptions is made about the behavior of the pion and quark masses.

## Method
The authors consider three current correlators in the presence of a constant external magnetic field: the light-quark axial-vector current correlator \(\Pi_{\mu\nu}^{AA}\), the hybrid correlator \(\Pi_{5\nu}\) involving an axial-vector current and its divergence, and the correlator of the divergence of the axial-vector current \(\psi_5\). Four FESR are written (two for \(\Pi_{\mu\nu}^{AA}\) with kernels \(P(s)=1\) and \(P(s)=s\), one each for \(\Pi_{5\nu}\) and \(\psi_5\) with \(P(s)=1\)), and the magnetic field enters as a correction to the quark propagator expanded to order \((eB)^2\). The magnetic evolution of the chiral quark condensate is taken as input from lattice QCD or the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, and three different conditions for the quark and pion masses are explored.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum input values used: \(f_\pi = 92.28 \pm 0.07\ \text{MeV}\), charged pion mass \(m_\pi = 139.57018 \pm 0.00035\ \text{MeV}\), gluon condensate \(\langle \alpha_s G^2 \rangle = 0.037 \pm 0.015\ \text{GeV}^4\), quark mass sum \(m_{ud}(2\ \text{GeV}) = 8.2 \pm 0.4\ \text{MeV}\), and quark condensate \(\langle \bar{q} q \rangle(2\ \text{GeV}) = -(267 \pm 5\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- The flavor-symmetry-breaking parameter \(\Delta_{ud}\) is estimated as \(0.013\) at \(eB = 0.2\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(0.067\) at \(eB = 1\ \text{GeV}^2\), justifying its neglect.
- The threshold for perturbative QCD in vacuum is \(s_0 \gtrsim 1\ \text{GeV}^2\); \(s_0\) and \(f_\pi\) are found to increase with increasing magnetic field under all assumptions.

## Context
This work extends the QCD sum-rule approach to finite magnetic fields, building on a recent proposal by the same group (Dominguez et al., 2018) that treated the chiral limit. It uses the same FESR framework previously applied to finite temperature (QCDSRT review) and to heavy-heavy quark systems (Dominguez et al., 2012). The magnetic evolution of the quark condensate is taken from lattice QCD results (Bali et al., 2012) and NJL model calculations (Coppola et al., 2018). The results enable a systematic comparison of chiral and deconfinement parameters in a magnetic field, and the connection between \(s_0\) and the Polyakov loop (Carlo et al., 2018) is noted as an intriguing parallel to the finite-temperature case.
