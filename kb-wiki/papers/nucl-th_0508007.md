# Pentaquarks in the medium in the quark-meson coupling model

> We calculate the properties of the pentaquarks $Θ^+$ and $Ξ^{--,0}$ in symmetric nuclear matter using the quark meson coupling model (QMC). The stability of the $Θ^+$ in the medium with respect to the channel $Θ^+\to NK^+$ is discussed.

# Pentaquarks in the medium in the quark-meson coupling model

## Key claim

The pentaquark Θ⁺ is stable against decay to N+K⁺ in symmetric nuclear matter for densities above 1.5 ρ₀, but only if both the nucleon and kaon carry the Fermi momentum; if the kaon carries zero momentum, the pentaquark is not stable at any density. These stability limits are sensitive to the kaon optical potential and could shift to ρ₀ and 2.5 ρ₀ respectively when the K⁺ optical potential is set to the accepted value of 10 MeV at saturation density.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling model (QMC), treating nucleons and pentaquarks as static spherical MIT bags whose quarks interact with classical σ, ω, and ρ meson fields in the mean-field approximation. They solved the Dirac equation for quarks inside the bag, minimized the bag energy to obtain effective masses, and computed chemical potentials self-consistently for symmetric nuclear matter. Results were compared with the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) using the NL3 parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Θ⁺ pentaquark vacuum mass: 1540 MeV (from experimental references cited in the paper)
- Ξ⁻⁻ and Ξ⁰ pentaquark vacuum masses: 1862 MeV (from experimental references cited in the paper)
- Θ⁺ optical potential in QMC: approximately −75 MeV at saturation density
- Ξ pentaquark optical potential in QMC: approximately −50 MeV at saturation density
- Θ⁺ radius in vacuum: 16% larger than the nucleon radius (nucleon radius set to 0.6 fm)
- QMC nucleon effective mass at saturation density: approximately 0.8 M_N
- NLWM nucleon effective mass at saturation density: approximately 0.7 M_N
- Θ⁺ stable against N+K⁺ decay for densities > 1.5 ρ₀ (with both particles at Fermi momentum)
- With rescaled g_{ωK} coupling (1.625 g_{ωK}) giving K⁺ potential of 10 MeV: stable for densities > ρ₀ (p_K=0) or > 2.5 ρ₀ (p_K=p_F)
- Isospin asymmetry effect: at proton fraction y_p=0.1 and 3ρ₀, effective mass difference is only ~1% of symmetric matter value

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model, previously applied to nucleons by Guichon (1988) and Saito & Thomas, to the newly discovered pentaquark states Θ⁺ and Ξ⁻⁻,⁰. It contrasts with earlier calculations by Zhong et al. (2004), which did not treat the Θ⁺ self-consistently, and by Korean group (2004), which did not reproduce nucleon saturation data. The results enable future studies of pentaquark stability in dense nuclear matter, with implications for neutron star interiors and heavy-ion collision experiments, and are compatible with optical potential values of −70 to −120 MeV from self-energy calculations by Oset et al. (2004) and −90 to −40 MeV from QCD sum rule calculations by Marina et al. (2004).
