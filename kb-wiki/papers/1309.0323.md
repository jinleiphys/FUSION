# Probing the statistical decay and alpha-clustering effects in 12c+12c and 14n+10b reactions

> An experimental campaign has been undertaken at INFN Laboratori Nazionali di Legnaro, Italy, in order to progress in our understanding of the statistical properties of light nuclei at excitation energies above particle emission threshold, by measuring exclusive data from fusion-evaporation reactions. A first reaction 12C+12C at 7.9 AMeV beam energy has been measured, using the GARFIELD+Ring Counter experimental setup. Fusion-evaporation events have been exclusively selected. The comparison to a dedicated Hauser-Feshbach calculation allows us to give constraints on the nuclear level density at high excitation energy for light systems ranging from C up to Mg. Out-of-equilibrium emission has been evidenced and attributed both to entrance channel effects favoured by the cluster nature of reaction partners and, in more dissipative events, to the persistence of cluster correlations well above the 24Mg threshold for 6 alphas decay. The 24Mg compound nucleus has been studied with a new measurement 14N + 10B at 5.7 AMeV. The comparison between the two datasets would allow us to further constrain the level density of light nuclei. Deviations from a statistical behaviour can be analyzed to get information on nuclear clustering.

## Key claim

The paper claims that out-of-equilibrium alpha emission in the 12C+12C reaction at 95 MeV is partly due to entrance-channel effects favored by the cluster structure of the reaction partners, and that comparing with the 14N+10B reaction (which populates the same 24Mg compound nucleus at the same excitation energy) allows disentangling these entrance-channel effects from genuine cluster correlations persisting in the hot fused system.

## Method

Fusion-evaporation events from 12C+12C at 95 MeV (7.9 AMeV) and 14N+10B at 5.7 AMeV were measured using the GARFIELD + Ring Counter setup at INFN Laboratori Nazionali di Legnaro, providing nearly 4π coverage. Events with total detected charge Z=12 (complete fusion) were selected and compared to Monte Carlo Hauser-Feshbach calculations for the 24Mg compound nucleus at excitation energy ε* = 2.6 AMeV. Q-value distributions for the (2α, A O) channel were constructed to distinguish statistical decay from direct reaction contributions.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy for 12C+12C: 95 MeV (7.9 AMeV)
- Beam energy for 14N+10B: 5.7 AMeV
- Compound nucleus excitation energy: ε* = 2.6 AMeV for both reactions
- Q-value for opening of the 4-body channel 15O+n+α+α: Q_kin = -15.78 MeV for 12C reaction and Q_kin = -0.8 MeV for 14N reaction
- Ring Counter angular coverage: 6° ≤ θ ≤ 18° with angular resolution Δθ ≈ 0.7° and energy resolution 0.3% (silicon strips) and 2-3% (CsI(Tl))
- GARFIELD angular coverage: 30° to 150°

## Context

This work extends the experimental campaign of the NUCL-EX collaboration on fusion-evaporation reactions with light nuclei, aiming to constrain nuclear level densities in the A~20 region and study cluster structures. The 12C+12C data show deviations from Hauser-Feshbach calculations for alpha particles in coincidence with oxygen residues, attributed to direct (α-transfer/pick-up) reactions favored by the cluster nature of 12C, as previously discussed by Morelli et al. (2013). The new 14N+10B measurement, populating the same 24Mg compound nucleus at the same excitation energy but through a different entrance channel, shows reduced but residual discrepancies, enabling future channel-by-channel analysis to determine whether cluster correlations persist in the hot fused system above the multi-alpha decay threshold.
