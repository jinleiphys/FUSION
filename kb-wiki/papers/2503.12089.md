# Study of \b{eta}-Decay, log ft Values and Nuclear Structure Properties of Neutron-rich Ge Nuclei

> We use the relativistic mean field (RMF) model to conduct a thorough analysis of the ground-state properties of $^{67\text{--}80}$Ge nuclei. Binding energies and neutron skin thicknesses are computed for a total of 14 neutron-rich Ge isotopes. This study provides a comprehensive overview of the RMF model's explanation of nuclear ground-state properties. Furthermore, we examine the $\log ft$ values of the allowed $β^\pm$ decays and electron/positron capture rates on Ge isotopes in the mass region $A = 67\text{--}80$. The proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) method was employed to calculate the $β$-decay properties of selected Ge isotopes. The calculated $\log ft$ values show good consistency with the measured data. The predicted $β$-decay half-lives are within a factor of 2 of the measured values. For high temperature and density conditions in the core of massive stars, the calculated pn-QRPA stellar rates are up to an order of magnitude larger than the independent shell model rates. The findings reported in this study could be valuable for simulating the late-stage stellar evolution of massive stars.

# Study of β-Decay, log ft Values and Nuclear Structure Properties of Neutron-rich Ge Nuclei

## Key claim
The relativistic mean field (RMF) model with DD-ME2 interaction and the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) method together reproduce ground-state properties and β-decay half-lives of neutron-rich Ge isotopes (A = 67–80) within a factor of 2 of measured values, but the RMF binding energy calculations show systematic discrepancies from experimental data that the authors attribute to missing degrees of freedom such as triaxial deformations.

## Method
The authors used the axially symmetric RMF model with the density-dependent meson-exchange DD-ME2 functional to compute binding energies per nucleon and neutron skin thicknesses for 14 neutron-rich Ge isotopes (⁶⁷–⁸⁰Ge). They then employed the pn-QRPA model with separable particle-particle and particle-hole Gamow-Teller interactions in a multi-ħω Nilsson basis to calculate GT strength distributions, log ft values, β-decay half-lives, and stellar weak rates. The Q-values were taken from mass excess data (Kon21), and nuclear deformation parameters were adopted from the finite range droplet model (FRDM) (Mol16).

## Key numbers
- Binding energies per nucleon (BE/A) for ¹⁴Ge isotopes: calculated values range from 8.57 MeV (⁶⁷Ge) to 8.66 MeV (⁷²Ge, ⁷³Ge), compared to experimental values from 8.62 MeV (⁸⁰Ge) to 8.73 MeV (⁷²Ge); the largest discrepancy is 0.09 MeV for ⁶⁸Ge (calculated 8.59 vs. experimental 8.68)
- Predicted β-decay half-lives are within a factor of 2 of measured values (stated in abstract, no individual half-life numbers given in truncated text)
- For high temperature and density conditions in massive star cores, calculated pn-QRPA stellar rates are up to an order of magnitude larger than independent shell model rates
- The pn-QRPA calculation used interaction strengths κ = 0.58/A⁰·⁷ and χ = 5.2/A⁰·⁷, and the constant C for half-life calculation was taken as 6143 s (Har09), with gA/gV = −1.2694 (Nak10)
- The oscillator constant was calculated as ħω = (45/A¹/³ − 25/A²/³)

## Context
This work extends previous pn-QRPA studies of β-decay properties for neutron-rich nuclei, including recent work on iron isotopes (Nabi 2024) and earlier systematic calculations for approximately 6000 neutron-rich nuclei (Staudt 1990, Hirsch 1993). The RMF calculations with DD-ME2 interaction follow the covariant density functional theory framework (Lalazissis 2005). The results are intended for application to simulations of late-stage stellar evolution in massive stars, particularly for r-process nucleosynthesis scenarios where reliable β-decay rates for neutron-rich nuclei are needed. The authors note that inclusion of triaxial deformations may improve the RMF binding energy predictions, which they plan as future work.
