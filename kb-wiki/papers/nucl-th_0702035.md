# The NN phase shifts in the extended quark-delocalization, color-screening model

> An alternative method is applied to the study of nucleon-nucleon(NN) scattering phase shifts in the framework of extended quark delocalization, color-screening model(QDCSM), where the one-pion-exchange(OPE) with short-range cutoff is included.

## Key claim

The extended quark-delocalization, color-screening model (QDCSM), which adds one-pion-exchange (OPE) with a short-range cutoff, can describe nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering phase shifts quantitatively, but only when channel coupling (specifically S-D mixing) is included; the improvement is not seen in single-channel calculations.

## Method

The authors solve the six-quark Schrödinger equation using the resonating-group method (RGM) combined with generating coordinates, employing a Hamiltonian that includes confinement, one-gluon exchange (OGE) with its tensor part, and OPE with a step-function cutoff at short range. Instead of the usual asymptotic matching to spherical Bessel functions, they obtain phase shifts by solving the eigenvalue problem with a finite-range boundary condition and comparing the nodes of the resulting oscillating wavefunction to the roots of the free spherical Bessel function. The color screening parameter μ and the OPE cutoff r₀ are fixed by reproducing the deuteron binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit OPE cutoff parameter is r₀ = 0.8 fm; the corresponding color screening parameter is μ = 0.85 fm⁻² (with OPE) compared to μ = 1.0 fm⁻² (without OPE).
- The constituent quark mass is m = 313 MeV; the harmonic-oscillator quark wavefunction width is b = 0.6015 fm (with OPE) or b = 0.6034 fm (without OPE).
- The strong coupling constant is αₛ = 1.558 (with OPE) or 1.543 (without OPE); the confinement strength is a_c = 25.135 MeV fm⁻² (with OPE) or 25.132 MeV fm⁻² (without OPE).
- The pion mass used in the OPE potential is μ_π = 138.04 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the original QDCSM of Ping et al. (1998, 1999) by adding OPE with a short-range cutoff and the tensor part of OGE, which were missing in earlier versions and caused the long-range NN interaction to fall off too fast due to Gaussian quark wavefunctions. The new method for extracting phase shifts from a finite-volume eigenvalue problem is borrowed from lattice QCD techniques and is tested here against conventional RGM calculations. The results enable future quantitative fits to NN scattering data, including P-wave and higher partial waves once spin-orbit interactions are added, and allow predictions for NΞ scattering to discriminate between constituent quark models.
