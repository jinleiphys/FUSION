# Internal structure of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ from its light-quark mass dependence

> We employ a chiral effective field theory-based approach to connect $DD^*$ scattering observables at the physical and variable pion masses accessible in lattice QCD simulations. We incorporate all relevant scales associated with three-body $DDπ$ dynamics and the left-hand cut induced by the one-pion exchange for pion masses higher than the physical one, as required by analyticity and unitarity. By adjusting the contact interactions to match experimental data at the physical pion mass and lattice finite-volume energy levels at $m_π = 280$ MeV, we predict the trajectory of the $T_{cc}$ pole as a function of the pion mass, finding it consistent with the hadronic-molecule scenario. In particular, we find that the explicit treatment of the one-pion exchange has a pronounced effect on the pole trajectory for $m_π\gtrsim 230$ MeV by pushing it into the complex energy plane.

## Key claim
The trajectory of the \(T_{cc}(3875)^+\) pole as a function of pion mass is consistent with the hadronic-molecule scenario, but only when the one-pion exchange is explicitly included; for pion masses above approximately 230 MeV, the one-pion exchange pushes the pole into the complex energy plane, a feature that a pure contact theory cannot reproduce.

## Method
The authors construct a chiral effective field theory (EFT) potential for \(DD^*\) scattering up to \(\mathcal{O}(Q^2)\), including one-pion exchange (OPE) and contact interactions. They adjust the low-energy constants to reproduce experimental data at the physical pion mass and lattice finite-volume energy levels at \(m_\pi = 280\) MeV from Padmanath (2022), then solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to predict the \(T_{cc}\) pole trajectory for pion masses from the physical value up to \(3 m_\pi^{\rm ph}\).

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of the \(T_{cc}\) at the physical point: \(E_B \approx 300\) keV, corresponding to a spatial extension of \(1/\gamma \approx 8\) fm.
- The left-hand cut branch point from one-pion exchange at \(m_\pi = 280\) MeV is at center-of-mass momentum \(|p_{\rm lhc}^{1\pi}| = 126\) MeV.
- The three-body branch point at the physical pion mass is at \(|k_{\rm rhc_3}| \approx 88\) MeV.
- The transition from a three-body cut to a left-hand cut in the on-shell OPE potential occurs at \(\xi = \xi_0 \approx 1.03\) (i.e., \(m_\pi \approx 1.03 \times m_\pi^{\rm ph}\)).
- The \(T_{cc}\) becomes a bound state at \(\xi = \xi^\prime \approx 1.027\), and transitions from a bound to a virtual state at LO at \(\xi \approx 1.3\).
- At NLO, the \(T_{cc}\) becomes a resonance state after \(\xi \approx 1.68\).

## Context
This work extends the chiral EFT analysis of \(DD^*\) scattering from Du (2023) and Meng (2023), which previously addressed the left-hand cut problem in lattice QCD analyses of the \(T_{cc}\). It directly addresses the concern raised by Du (2023) that the effective range approximation used by Padmanath (2022) is invalid due to nearby left-hand cuts. The predicted pole trajectory as a function of pion mass provides a benchmark for future lattice QCD simulations and refines the compositeness criterion for hadronic molecules in the presence of long-range pion exchange.
