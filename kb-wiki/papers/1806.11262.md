# Novel approach to excitation spectrum from correlated ground state

> A novel approach to obtain the excitation spectrum of nuclei is presented as well as its proof-of-principle. The Monte Carlo Shell Model is extended so that the excitation spectrum can be calculated from its ground state with full of correlations. This new methodology is sketched with the example of E1 excitations from the nucleus 88Sr in comparison to experiment. From the B(E1; 0+1 -> 1- ) value, the photoabsorption cross section is calculated, with the Giant Dipole and Pygmy Dipole Resonances in agreement with experiment. Applications to 90Sr and 90,93Zr are shown with similar characteristics. The possible relevance to the transmutation of long-lived fission products is discussed

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel extension of the Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) that can calculate excitation spectra from a fully correlated ground state, with the condition that the method generates basis vectors specifically for the desired excitation mode rather than computing all possible excited states. The proof-of-principle demonstration for E1 excitations in Sr and Zr isotopes shows agreement with experimental Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) and Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) peaks, but the authors note that the Hamiltonian parameters were fitted to reproduce the 2+1 state of 88Sr and then applied to other nuclei without further adjustment.

## Method

The authors first obtain the correlated ground state wavefunction using the standard MCSM with 15 basis vectors in a model space spanning the full pf-shell, full sdg-shell, and lower pfh-shell above a 40Ca inert core. They then generate basis vectors for 1- states by applying the E1 operator (or its orbit-decomposed components) to the ground-state MCSM basis vectors, producing new Slater determinants that carry E1 transition character. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized in the subspace spanned by these E1-generated basis vectors with projection onto Jπ=1-, and the process is refined by adding orbit-decomposed E1 operators, variational shifts of the most important components, and additional standard MCSM basis vectors for the 1- space.

## Key numbers

- The model space dimension is approximately 10^37, making traditional shell-model diagonalization infeasible
- 15 MCSM basis vectors (NB=15) were used for the ground state calculation
- 18 combinations of single-particle orbits were used for the orbit-decomposed E1 operator
- 270 basis vectors (15 × 18) were generated for set (b) calculations
- 10 most important Xj,j' operators were selected for variational refinement
- 420 total basis vectors in set (c) after adding 150 variational basis vectors
- 240 additional standard MCSM basis vectors were added for set (d), totaling 660 basis vectors
- The summed B(E1) values from sets (b) or (c) reached 90% of the total sum calculated from the ground state expectation value
- The GDR appears as a large peak at Ex=16-18 MeV across all four nuclei
- The PDR appears as a small peak around Ex=9 MeV in each case

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo Shell Model approach from ground-state structure calculations to excitation spectra, providing an alternative to various Random Phase Approximation (RPA) methods such as those reviewed by Ring and Schuck (1980) and the Skyrme SIII RPA calculations of Beiner et al. (1975). The method is complementary to the Coupled-Cluster approach of Bacca et al. (2013, 2014) and can be applied to even-even, odd-A, and odd-odd nuclei with spherical, deformed, or transitional shapes. The results for PDR in 88Sr and 90Zr are compared to experimental data from Beard et al. (2012), and the authors note potential applications to the transmutation of long-lived fission products such as 90Sr and 93Zr in nuclear waste.
