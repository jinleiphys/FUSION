# Different scenarios of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the interacting instanton liquid model via flavor symmetry breaking

> We investigate a type of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (D$χ$SB) for various current quark masses using the interacting instanton liquid model. The type of D$χ$SB is classified based on the sign of the second derivative of the free energy density with respect to the quark condensate at the origin. We perform numerical simulations of the interacting instanton liquid model with the flavor SU(2) symmetric and (2+1)-flavor quarks. We find that the curvature is negative in the SU(2) case. This means the ordinary type of D$χ$SB. In contrast, in the (2+1)-flavor case, a positive curvature is observed when the strange quark mass is as small as those of the up and down quarks. This suggests that the anomaly-driven type of D$χ$SB can occur under the approximate flavor SU(3) symmetry. As the strange quark mass increases, the curvature gradually decreases and becomes negative when the strange quark mass is approximately three times larger than those of the light quarks. This difference can be understood in terms of the 't Hooft vertex which induces a six-quark interaction in the $N_f=3$ case and does a four-quark interaction in the $N_f=2$ case. Our results might indicate that the ratio between the strange and light quark masses plays a crucial role in understanding the microscopic relationship between D$χ$SB and the anomaly effect.

## Key claim

The type of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DχSB) in the interacting instanton liquid model depends on the number of active quark flavors: the ordinary type occurs for flavor SU(2) symmetric quarks (negative curvature), while the anomaly-driven type can occur for (2+1)-flavor quarks when the strange quark mass is comparable to the light quark masses (positive curvature), with a transition as the strange quark mass increases.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the interacting instanton liquid model (IILM) with 32 instantons and anti-instantons, using the Metropolis algorithm for color orientations and the HMC algorithm for instanton sizes and positions. They computed the free energy density via thermodynamic integration and the quark condensate from the zero-mode propagator at 72 different instanton densities for each set of quark masses. The curvature (second derivative of free energy density with respect to quark condensate at the origin) was extracted by polynomial regression of order k=3 on the (quark condensate, free energy) dataset.

## Key numbers

- For flavor SU(2) symmetric quarks with m_q = 26 MeV, the curvature C_2 = -7.73 × 10^{-5} MeV^{-2} (negative, ordinary type DχSB)
- For flavor SU(2) symmetric quarks across the range 17 MeV < m_q < 63 MeV, the curvature remains negative
- The light quark condensate at the vacuum density n = 1 fm^{-4} takes approximately ⟨q̄q⟩ = (-207 MeV)^3, independent of current quark mass
- For (2+1)-flavor quarks, the curvature is positive when the strange quark mass is smaller than 57-192 MeV (depending on light quark mass), and becomes negative as m_s exceeds these values
- For (2+1)-flavor quarks with m_q = 0.30 Λ (approximately 96 MeV), the curvature remains positive across the full range of m_s up to m_s = 1.2 Λ (approximately 355 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Suda 2024) which found anomaly-driven DχSB in flavor SU(3) symmetric IILM and ordinary type in quenched calculations, by now investigating the transition between these regimes via flavor symmetry breaking. The results connect to the NJL model analysis by Kono (2021) which classified DχSB types based on four-quark and anomaly-induced couplings, and suggest that the ratio m_s/m_q determines whether the 't Hooft vertex (six-quark interaction for N_f=3 versus four-quark interaction for N_f=2) drives the chiral symmetry breaking mechanism. This enables future studies of how the microscopic mechanism of DχSB affects hadron observables in the physical regime where flavor SU(3) is broken.
