# Momentum distribution of charm hadrons in a fluid-dynamic approach

> Exploiting a mapping between transport theory and fluid dynamics, we show how a fluid-dynamic description of the diffusion of charm quarks in the QCD plasma is feasible. We show results for spectra of charmed hadrons obtained with a fluid-dynamic description of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) coupled with the conservation of a heavy-quark - antiquark current. We compare our calculations with the most recent experimental data in order to provide further constraints on the transport coefficients of the QGP.

# Momentum distribution of charm hadrons in a fluid-dynamic approach

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a fluid-dynamic description of charm quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma is feasible, provided the relaxation time for charm quarks becomes much smaller than the typical expansion time of the fluid. The approach reproduces charmed hadron spectra up to transverse momenta of 4–5 GeV, but the model underestimates the integrated yield of Λc+ and shows a J/ψ momentum distribution peaked at higher pT than measured.

## Method
The authors extend second-order relativistic fluid dynamics to include a conserved current for heavy quark-antiquark pairs, with a relaxation-type equation for the diffusion current driven by gradients of the chemical potential. They solve the coupled equations numerically for Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV in the 0–10% centrality class, using T_RENTo for initial entropy density and a Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription at 156.5 MeV, with resonance decays computed via the FastReso algorithm. The charm quark back-reaction on the fluid background is neglected.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass: Mc ∼ 1.5 GeV; beauty quark mass: Mb ∼ 4.8 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature: 156.5 MeV
- The spatial diffusion coefficient Ds is varied from a non-diffusive case (Ds = 0) to an upper limit corresponding to 2π Ds T = 1.5 from Lattice-QCD calculations
- The fluid-dynamic description reproduces D meson spectra up to pT ∼ 4–5 GeV
- The relaxation time for charm quarks becomes much smaller than the fluid expansion time for a broad range of Ds values under Bjorken flow assumptions

## Context
This work extends the mapping between transport theory and fluid dynamics previously developed by the authors (Capellino 2022) to heavy quarks in the QGP. It builds on Lattice-QCD calculations of the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient (Altenkort 2023, Altenkort 2020) and experimental measurements of D meson and J/ψ elliptic flow from the ALICE Collaboration (ALICE 2020). The approach enables systematic computation of charmed hadron spectra and flow coefficients to further constrain QGP transport coefficients, with future work needed to include out-of-equilibrium freeze-out corrections and non-linear contributions to the dissipative currents.
