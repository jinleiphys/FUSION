# Calculation of Coulomb breakup cross sections using a new Coulomb dynamical polarization potential

> A new method for calculating the Coulomb breakup of unstable neutron-rich isotopes at high energies is presented. The calculations employ the eikonal approximation and use a new Coulomb dynamical polarization potential (CDPP), calculated by solving the Schrödinger equation for the entire motion of the exotic projectile as a two-body cluster structure using the adiabatic approximation and incorporating excitations to the continuum. Calculations for some exotic isotopes are compared with Coulomb dissociation cross section data and found to be in good agreement.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new method for calculating Coulomb breakup cross sections of neutron-rich exotic nuclei using an extended Coulomb dynamical polarization potential (CDPP) that includes both core excited states and excitations to the continuum, finding good agreement with experimental data when the calculated cross sections are scaled by spectroscopic factors that vary depending on the choice of bound-state wave function.

## Method
The authors solve the Schrodinger equation for a two-body projectile (core plus valence neutron) using the adiabatic approximation to derive a continuum-based CDPP that depends on the relative excitation energy of the continuum. They then use the eikonal approximation to calculate the differential Coulomb dissociation cross section from the imaginary part of this CDPP via the continuity equation, applying the method to 11Be, 15C, and 17C projectiles on lead targets at energies of several hundred MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers
- For 11Be at 520 MeV/nucleon on Pb: experimental cross section 605 +/- 30 mb; calculated cross section 1563 mb (single-particle) and 1649 mb (Whittaker); ratio R_sigma = 0.40 for both
- For 15C at 605 MeV/nucleon on Pb: experimental cross section 324 +/- 15 mb; calculated cross section 570 mb (single-particle) and 643 mb (Whittaker); ratio R_sigma = 0.52 and 0.48 respectively
- For 15C core excited state (1- at 6.09 MeV): experimental cross section 36 +/- 3 mb; calculated contributions from p1/2 and p3/2 orbitals total 8.2 mb (single-particle) and 17.1 mb (Whittaker) for the 1- state
- For 17C at 496 MeV/nucleon on Pb: experimental cross section 62 +/- 7 mb for the 2+ core state; calculated cross section 118.9 mb (single-particle) and 145.5 mb (Whittaker) for s1/2 configuration; ratio R_sigma = 0.28 and 0.23 respectively
- For 17C ground state core (0+): experimental cross section 9+15/-9 mb; calculated cross section 607 mb (single-particle) and 854 mb (Whittaker); ratio R_sigma = 0.015 and 0.011

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous CDPP formalism (Maridi 2021) by including both core excited states and continuum excitations, enabling calculation of differential Coulomb dissociation cross sections as a function of relative excitation energy. The method provides an alternative to the Equivalent Photon Method and continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) calculations used by Moro (2020) for analyzing nuclear contributions, and can be generalized to calculate neutron removal cross sections, gamma capture cross sections, and the dipole response function. The approach enables extraction of spectroscopic factors comparable to those from shell model calculations (Sauvan 2000, Terashima 2004) and previous Coulomb dissociation experiments (Palit 2003, Nakamura 2009).
