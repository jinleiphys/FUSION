# Polarization phenomena for meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions

> We analyze polarization phenomena for pseudoscalar and vector mesons production in nucleon-nucleon collisions. We identify three energy regions corresponding to different physics and different approaches in the analysis of polarization effects. In the threshold region, characterized by the S-wave production for all final particles, the general symmetry properties of strong interaction can be applied. The region of intermediate energies, T=2-4 GeV, is characterized by the essential role of central i.e. non-peripheral collisions, where only a small number of s-channel states with definite quantum numbers, ${\cal J}^P=1^-$ and $2^+$, contribute. At higher energies, T $\ge $10 GeV, the leading mechanism is the diffractive dissociation and it is especially interesting for baryon spectroscopy. The transition to this region is an open field for experimental research at the Nuclotron.

## Key claim

The paper argues that polarization phenomena in meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions can be classified into three distinct energy regions, each with a different dominant reaction mechanism and theoretical formalism. The authors claim that in the intermediate energy region (T = 2-4 GeV), central non-peripheral collisions dominate and only a small number of s-channel states with quantum numbers J^P = 1^- and 2^+ contribute, but this is presented as a model-dependent interpretation that successfully reproduces existing data rather than a proven fact.

## Method

The authors analyze the spin structure of matrix elements for pseudoscalar and vector meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions using two-component spinor formalism and symmetry principles (Pauli principle, P-invariance, conservation of total angular momentum). For the threshold region, they derive exact predictions for polarization observables based solely on symmetry properties. For the intermediate energy region, they compare predictions from a central collision model (with s-channel states J^P = 1^- and 2^+) against experimental data on differential cross sections and analyzing powers for reactions such as p+p → p+p+η, p+p → p+p+ω, and p+p → Δ^{++}+Δ^0.

## Key numbers

- The intermediate energy region is defined as T = 2-4 GeV (kinetic energy of proton beam in the laboratory system)
- The high energy region (diffractive dissociation) is defined as T ≥ 10 GeV
- For p+p → p+p+η at T = 1520 MeV, the differential cross section is fitted by dσ/dΩ_η ≈ A(1+cos²θ) + B(cos²θ - 1/3)²
- For p+p → p+p+ω at T = 2100 MeV, the differential cross section for events with M_pp between 2M_p and 2M_p + 5 MeV follows dσ/dΩ_ω ≈ |g_s|²(1+3cos²θ)
- For p+p → p+p+η at T = 1805 MeV, data are shown with and without cuts on M_pp ≥ 2M_p + 5 MeV
- The ratio of total cross sections R = σ(p+p → p+p+V^0) / σ(n+p → n+p+V^0) is bounded by 0 ≤ R ≤ 2 at threshold
- For p+p → p+p+η with small excitation energy, M_pp - 2m_p ≤ 5 MeV, the pp-system must be in ^1S_0 state
- For p+p → p+p+η without selection on M_pp, the analysis is limited to M_pp - 2m_p ≤ 40 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of polarization phenomena in meson production by classifying three energy regimes with distinct physics, building on earlier work by Rekalo and collaborators (1997) on threshold properties and by Faessler and collaborators (1999) on six-quark intermediate states. The authors contrast their central collision model (s-channel approach) with the standard Yukawa one-boson-exchange (OBE) description, noting that OBE models fail to describe T-odd polarization observables which are experimentally found to be large. The paper enables future experimental tests at the Nuclotron for the transition between intermediate and high energy regions, and suggests applications to gamma-d processes at Jefferson Laboratory.
