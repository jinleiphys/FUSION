# Chiral Effective Lagrangian and Quark Masses

> The status of lattice determinations of quark masses is reviewed (with the exception of m_b). Attempts to extract the low-energy constants in the effective chiral Lagrangian are discussed, with special emphasis on those couplings which are required to test the hypothesis of a massless up-quark. Furthermore, the issue of quenched chiral logarithms is addressed.

## Key claim

Lattice determinations of the low-energy constants (LECs) in the chiral effective Lagrangian, particularly the combination \(2\alpha_8-\alpha_5\), strongly disfavour the hypothesis of a massless up-quark, but the conclusion depends on whether the quark masses used in simulations are light enough to justify comparison with Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) at next-to-leading order.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD simulations that extract the LECs \(\alpha_5\) and \(\alpha_8\) by studying the quark-mass dependence of pseudoscalar meson masses and decay constants, using the ratio method introduced by the ALPHA collaboration to cancel renormalisation factors and enable continuum extrapolations. Results from quenched, two-flavour, and three-flavour dynamical simulations are compared with phenomenological estimates and with the values required for a massless up-quark. The review also covers determinations of the quenched chiral logarithm parameter \(\delta\) from fits to the pion mass dependence, and compiles lattice results for the strange quark mass \(m_s\) and the ratio \(m_s/\hat{m}\) using Wilson and staggered fermions with non-perturbative renormalisation.

## Key numbers

- The combination \(2\alpha_8-\alpha_5\) from the ALPHA collaboration for \(n_f=3\) is \(0.15(5)(15)\), compared to the value required for a massless up-quark of approximately \(-2.3\) (inferred from \(\alpha_8 = -0.9(4)\) and \(\alpha_5 = 0.5(6)\) from Cohen, Kaplan, and Nelson (1999)).
- The quenched chiral logarithm parameter \(\delta\) ranges from \(0.05(2)\) (RBC collaboration with domain-wall fermions) to \(0.26(3)\) (Kentucky group with overlap fermions), with the phenomenological estimate from the \(\eta'\) mass being \(\delta \approx 0.18\).
- The strange quark mass \(m_s\) in the \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) scheme at \(\mu = 2\) GeV is determined to be \(106(2)(8)\) MeV by the SPQ\(_{\mathrm{cd}}\)R collaboration (continuum extrapolated, Wilson fermions, \(K\)-input), and \(105(4)\) MeV by QCDSF (using the \(r_0\) scale and Schrödinger functional renormalisation).
- The ratio \(m_s/\hat{m}\) from lattice simulations is broadly consistent with the ChPT next-to-leading-order result \(24.4 \pm 1.4\) from Leutwyler (1996); for example, SPQ\(_{\mathrm{cd}}\)R reports \(24.3(2)(6)\) and CP-PACS (quenched) reports \(26.5(^{5.1}_{3.4})\).

## Context

This review extends the line of work by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) on Chiral Perturbation Theory and by Kaplan and Manohar (1986) on the ambiguity in the effective Lagrangian, which prevents a first-principles determination of the up-quark mass from chiral symmetry alone. The lattice results discussed here, particularly from ALPHA (Heitger et al., 2000), UKQCD (Irving et al., 2001), and the Ohio State University group (Nelson et al., 2002), provide first-principles constraints on the LECs that resolve the Kaplan-Manohar ambiguity and support the conclusion of Leutwyler (1996) that a massless up-quark is unlikely. The review also highlights the need for simulations with lighter sea quarks and larger volumes to improve the overlap with the chiral regime, and notes the analytic development by Rupak and Shoresh (2002) incorporating lattice-spacing effects into the chiral Lagrangian for Wilson fermions.
