# Aspects of Nucleon Compton Scattering

> We consider the spin-averaged nucleon forward Compton scattering amplitude in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory including all terms to order ${\cal O} (q^4)$. The chiral prediction for the spin-averaged forward Compton scattering amplitude is in good agreement with the data for photon energies $ω\le 110$ MeV. We also evaluate the nucleon electric and magnetic Compton polarizabilities to this order and discuss the uncertainties of the various counter terms entering the chiral expansion of these quantities.

## Key claim

The spin-averaged forward Compton scattering amplitude calculated in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to order ${\cal O}(q^4)$ agrees well with experimental data only for photon energies up to about 110 MeV, and the theoretical predictions for the nucleon electromagnetic polarizabilities carry significant uncertainties from counter terms, particularly from the poorly constrained $\Delta(1232)$ resonance contributions.

## Method

The authors used heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) in the one-loop approximation, including all terms up to chiral order $q^4$, to compute the spin-averaged forward Compton scattering amplitude and the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the proton and neutron. They evaluated 9 irreducible one-loop graphs with insertions from the leading-order Lagrangian ${\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(1)}$ and 67 one-loop diagrams with one insertion from ${\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(2)}$, plus tree-level contact terms from ${\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(4)}$, with unknown low-energy constants determined from resonance saturation (particularly the $\Delta(1232)$) and fits to pion-nucleon scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The chiral prediction for the spin-averaged forward Compton amplitude agrees with data for photon energies $\omega \le 110$ MeV.
- The electric polarizability of the proton: $\bar{\alpha}_p = (10.5 \pm 2.0) \cdot 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$.
- The magnetic polarizability of the proton: $\bar{\beta}_p = (3.5 \pm 3.6) \cdot 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$.
- The electric polarizability of the neutron: $\bar{\alpha}_n = (13.4 \pm 1.5) \cdot 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$.
- The magnetic polarizability of the neutron: $\bar{\beta}_n = (7.8 \pm 3.6) \cdot 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$.
- The Thomson limit for the proton: $e^2/m = 3.02$ $\mu$b GeV$^{-1}$.
- The constant $C = e^2/(96\pi^2 F_\pi^2) = 4.36 \cdot 10^{-4}$ fm$^2$.
- The $\Delta(1232)$ contribution to the magnetic polarizability counter term is estimated as $\delta \bar{\beta}_p^\Delta(m_\Delta) \simeq (7.0 \pm 7.0) \cdot 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$.
- The kaon loop contribution to the proton electric polarizability: $\delta \bar{\alpha}_p^K(m_\Delta) = 1.31 \cdot 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$.
- The kaon loop contribution to the neutron electric polarizability: $\delta \bar{\alpha}_n^K(m_\Delta) = 0.13 \cdot 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of nucleon polarizabilities at order $q^4$ (Bernard et al., 1993) by also analyzing the full energy dependence of the forward Compton amplitude and providing a detailed uncertainty analysis. It builds on the heavy baryon CHPT framework developed by Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and others, and uses the resonance saturation hypothesis (Ecker et al., 1989) to estimate counter terms from the $\Delta(1232)$ and Roper $N^*(1440)$ resonances. The results enable improved comparisons with experimental Compton scattering data and highlight the need for better constraints on the $\gamma N\Delta$ coupling parameters to reduce theoretical uncertainties in the polarizabilities.
