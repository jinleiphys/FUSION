# Higher-twist contributions to the transverse momentum broadening in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off large nuclei

> Multiple scattering leads to transverse momentum broadening of the struck quark in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic scatterings (SIDIS). Nuclear broadening of the transverse momentum squared at the leading twist is determined by the twist-four collinear quark-gluon correlation function of the target nucleus that is in turn related to the jet transport parameter inside the nuclear medium. The twist-six contributions to the transverse momentum broadening are calculated as power corrections $\sim 1/Q^2$. Such power corrections are found to have no extra nuclear enhancement beyond the twist-four matrix elements and are determined by the nuclear modification of collinear parton distribution and correlation functions. They become important for an accurate extraction of the jet transport parameter inside large nuclei and its scale evolution at intermediate values of the hard scale $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The twist-six contributions to transverse momentum broadening in SIDIS off large nuclei are calculated as power corrections of order \(1/Q^2\), and these corrections have no extra nuclear enhancement beyond the twist-four matrix elements; they are instead determined by the nuclear modification of collinear parton distribution and correlation functions, becoming important for accurate extraction of the jet transport parameter at intermediate \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors used the differential cross section of SIDIS expressed in terms of transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton distribution and correlation functions up to twist-four, derived via collinear expansion of the hard partonic parts. They computed the \(\vec k_\perp^2\)-weighted differential cross section, which involves higher-twist collinear matrix elements up to twist-six, and factorized these matrix elements under the assumption of a large, loosely bound nucleus. The nuclear-enhanced contributions were isolated by subtracting the single-nucleon cross section, and the transverse momentum broadening was defined as the difference between the \(\vec k_\perp^2\)-weighted cross section for a nuclear target and that for a nucleon target, normalized by the respective integrated cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The twist-six power corrections to transverse momentum broadening are of order \(1/Q^2\).
- The leading nuclear-enhanced contribution to \(\langle \vec k_\perp^2 f_q^A \rangle (x)\) is proportional to \(A^{4/3}\), arising from the nuclear size scaling \(R_A \approx 1.12 A^{1/3}\) fm.
- The leading term in \(\langle \vec k_\perp^4 \varphi_{\perp}^{(2)A} \rangle (x)\) with nuclear enhancement \(A^{5/3}\) cancels between left- and right-cut diagrams, leaving only contributions of order \(A^{4/3}\).
- The final expression for the nuclear-enhanced \(\vec k_\perp^2\)-weighted differential cross section contains the jet transport parameter \(\Delta_{2F} = \int d\xi_N^- \hat q_F(\xi_N^-)\), which is proportional to the nuclear size.

## Context

This work extends the leading-twist treatment of transverse momentum broadening in SIDIS, where the broadening is determined by the twist-four quark-gluon correlation function (related to the jet transport parameter \(\hat q\)), as previously studied by Qiu (1990), Luo (1993), Guo (1998), and Majumder (2007). The calculation of twist-six power corrections is the first attempt to go beyond leading twist, and it builds on the TMD factorization framework developed by Liang (2006) and Song (2010). The results enable more accurate extraction of the jet transport parameter from experimental data on transverse momentum broadening and its scale evolution at intermediate \(Q^2\), complementing phenomenological studies such as those by Burke (2013) for heavy-ion collisions and Deng (2009) and Chang (2014) for cold nuclear matter.
