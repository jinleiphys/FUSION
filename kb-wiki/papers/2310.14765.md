# One-neutron transfer reaction in the $^{18}$O + $^{48}$Ti collision at 275 MeV

> The present article reports new data on the $^{48}$Ti($^{18}$O,$^{17}$O)$^{49}$Ti reaction at 275 MeV incident energy as part of the systematic research pursued within the NUMEN project. Supplementary measurements of the same reaction on $^{16}$O and $^{27}$Al targets were also performed in order to estimate the background arising from the use of a composite target (TiO$_{2}$ + $^{27}$Al). These data were analyzed under the same theoretical framework as those obtained with the titanium target in order to reinforce the conclusions of our analysis. Differential cross-section angular distribution measurements for the $^{17}$O$^{8+}$ ejectiles were performed in a wide angular range by using the MAGNEX large acceptance magnetic spectrometer. The experimental results were analyzed within the distorted-wave and coupled-channels Born Approximation frameworks. The optical potentials at the entrance and exit channels were calculated in a double folding approach adopting the São Paulo potential, and the spectroscopic amplitudes for the projectile and target overlaps were obtained from large-scale shell model calculations. The differential cross-sections are well-described by the theoretical calculations, where a weak coupling to collective excitations of projectile and target is inferred. The sensitivity of transfer cross-sections on different model spaces adopted in nuclear structure calculations, is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the differential cross-sections for the one-neutron transfer reaction $^{48}$Ti($^{18}$O,$^{17}$O)$^{49}$Ti at 275 MeV are well-described by theoretical calculations within the DWBA and CCBA frameworks, with a weak coupling to collective excitations of projectile and target inferred. However, the analysis relies on background subtraction from composite target components (TiO$_2$ + $^{27}$Al) using supplementary measurements on $^{16}$O and $^{27}$Al targets, and the conclusions are reinforced by analyzing all data under the same theoretical framework rather than by independent validation.

## Method

Differential cross-section angular distributions for $^{17}$O$^{8+}$ ejectiles were measured over a wide angular range using the MAGNEX large acceptance magnetic spectrometer at the INFN-LNS laboratory. The experimental data were analyzed within the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) and coupled-channels Born approximation (CCBA) frameworks using the FRESCO code, with optical potentials calculated via the double-folding Sao Paulo potential and spectroscopic amplitudes obtained from large-scale shell model calculations. Supplementary measurements of the same reaction on $^{16}$O and $^{27}$Al targets were performed to estimate and subtract background contributions from the composite TiO$_2$ + $^{27}$Al target.

## Key numbers

- Incident beam energy: 275 MeV for $^{18}$O ions accelerated by the K800 Superconducting Cyclotron
- Target thickness: TiO$_2$ target of (510 $\pm$ 26) $\mu$g/cm$^2$ evaporated onto an aluminum foil of (216 $\pm$ 11) $\mu$g/cm$^2$
- Energy resolution: approximately 400 keV FWHM for the $^{27}$Al($^{18}$O,$^{17}$O)$^{28}$Al reaction and approximately 450 keV FWHM for the $^{48}$Ti($^{18}$O,$^{17}$O)$^{49}$Ti reaction
- Grazing angle: $\theta_{gr}^{c.m.}= \ang{5.6}$ for the $^{27}$Al($^{18}$O,$^{17}$O)$^{28}$Al reaction
- Normalization coefficient of the imaginary part of the optical potential: N$_I$ = 0.78 for DWBA calculations and N$_I$ = 0.60 for CCBA calculations
- Solid angle of the spectrometer: 14 msr

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of one- and two-nucleon transfer reactions initiated by the NUMEN project, complementing prior analysis of the one-proton transfer channel for the same system by Sgouros et al. and the sensitivity study of transfer reactions on nuclear structure models by Ciraldo et al. The results provide experimental constraints on nuclear structure models for the $^{48}$Ti nucleus, which is the daughter nucleus of $^{48}$Ca in the neutrinoless double beta decay process, and contribute to the broader goal of constraining nuclear matrix elements for 0$\nu\beta\beta$ decay through heavy-ion induced double charge exchange reactions.
