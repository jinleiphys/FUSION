# Transport coefficients in the Polyakov quark meson coupling model: A quasi particle approach

> We compute the transport coefficients, namely, the coefficients of shear and bulk viscosities as well as thermal conductivity for hot and dense matter. The calculations are performed within the Polyakov loop extended quark meson model. The estimation of the transport coefficients is made using the Boltzmann kinetic equation within the relaxation time approximation. The energy dependent relaxation time is estimated from meson meson scattering, quark meson scattering and quark quark scattering within the model. In our calculations, the shear viscosity to entropy ratio and the coefficient of thermal conductivity show a minimum at the critical temperature, while the ratio of bulk viscosity to entropy density exhibits a peak at this transition point. The effect of confinement modelled through a Polyakov loop potential plays an important role in the estimation of these dissipative coefficients both below and above the critical temperature.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy ratio and thermal conductivity exhibit a minimum at the critical temperature, while the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio shows a peak at this transition point, with the Polyakov loop potential modeling confinement playing an important role in these dissipative coefficients both below and above the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors computed transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and thermal conductivity) within the Polyakov loop extended quark meson (PQM) model using the Boltzmann kinetic equation in the relaxation time approximation. The energy-dependent relaxation time was estimated from meson-meson scattering, quark-meson scattering, and quark-quark scattering, with medium-dependent meson and quark masses. The thermodynamic potential included contributions from quarks, mesons, and a Polyakov loop potential with polynomial parametrization, with mean fields obtained by minimizing the effective potential.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity to entropy ratio ($\eta/s$) shows a minimum at the critical temperature (around 192 MeV for two flavors)
* Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio ($\zeta/s$) exhibits a peak at the critical temperature
* The constituent quark mass in vacuum is about 300 MeV, with $g_\sigma = 3.3$ and $f_\pi = 93$ MeV
* The pion mass is $m_\pi = 138$ MeV and the sigma meson mass is $m_\sigma = 600$ MeV, giving $\lambda = 19.7$
* The Polyakov loop potential parameter $T_0(2) = 192$ MeV for two flavors
* The velocity of sound shows a minimum near the crossover temperature, approaching $c_s^2 = 1/3$ at high temperature

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of transport coefficients in effective models of strong interaction, including chiral perturbation theory (Dobado and collaborators), quasi-particle models, the linear sigma model (Purnendu and collaborators), and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Sasaki and collaborators, Deb and collaborators). The PQM model incorporates both chiral symmetry breaking and confinement-deconfinement transition through the Polyakov loop potential, which was not present in earlier NJL model calculations. The results enable comparison with lattice QCD thermodynamics and hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic heavy ion collisions, particularly at finite baryon density where thermal conductivity becomes important.
