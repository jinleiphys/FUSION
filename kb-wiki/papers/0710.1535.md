# Charmonium dynamics in heavy ion collisions

> Applying the HSD transport approach to charmonium dynamics within the 'hadronic comover model' and the 'QGP melting scenario', we show that the suppression pattern seen at RHIC cannot be explained by the interaction with baryons, comoving mesons and/or by color screening mechanism. The interaction with hadrons in the late stages of the collision (when the energy density falls below the critical) gives a sizable contribution to the suppression. On the other hand, it does not account for the observed additional charmonium dissociation and its dependence on rapidity. Together with the failure of the hadron-string models to reproduce high v2 of open charm mesons, this suggests strong pre-hadronic interaction of c-cbar with the medium at high energy densities.

# Charmonium dynamics in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
Applying the HSD transport approach within both the 'hadronic comover model' and the 'QGP melting scenario' fails to reproduce the observed charmonium suppression pattern at RHIC; the failure of both models, together with the inability of hadron-string models to reproduce the high elliptic flow of open charm mesons, suggests that strong pre-hadronic interactions of charm-anticharm pairs with the medium at high energy densities dominate the dynamics.

## Method
The Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach was used to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, treating charm degrees of freedom perturbatively with initial charmonium production from primary nucleon-nucleon collisions scaled by the number of binary collisions. Two suppression scenarios were implemented: the 'hadronic comover model' with inelastic scattering off comoving mesons (including D-meson recombination channels via detailed balance) and the 'QGP threshold melting scenario' where charmonia are fully dissociated when local energy density exceeds threshold values (16 GeV/fm^3 for J/Psi, 2 GeV/fm^3 for chi_c and Psi'). An additional variant introduced a cut energy density epsilon_cut = 1 GeV/fm^3 below which comoving mesons exist.

## Key numbers
- Central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV reach local energy densities as high as 30 GeV/fm^3.
- Threshold energy densities adopted: 16 GeV/fm^3 for J/Psi, 2 GeV/fm^3 for chi_c, and 2 GeV/fm^3 for Psi'.
- Cut energy density for comover existence set to epsilon_cut = 1 GeV/fm^3, of order the critical energy density.
- At least 20% of J/Psi survive at RHIC according to PHENIX data, contradicting the threshold melting scenario where all initially created charmonia melt at the highest energy densities.

## Context
This work extends earlier HSD studies of charmonium suppression at SPS energies (Cass99, Cass00, brat03, Cass01) and directly tests two competing models: the geometrical Glauber/percolation approach of Blaizot and Satz, and the comover scattering model of Capella, Vogt, and others. The failure of both models at RHIC energies, combined with the inability of hadron-string models to reproduce open charm v2 (brat05), points toward the need for a strong QGP (sQGP) description of pre-hadronic charm-anticharm interactions. The results enable future work incorporating such non-hadronic, non-screening interactions into transport models, while noting that late-stage hadronic interactions still contribute sizably and should not be neglected when comparing QGP-induced suppression models to data.
