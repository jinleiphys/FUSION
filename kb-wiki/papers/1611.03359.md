# Formation of hypernuclei in evaporation and fission processes

> There are excellent opportunities to produce excited heavy hyper residues in relativistic hadron and peripheral heavy-ion collisions. We investigate the disintegration of such residues into hyper nuclei via evaporation of baryons and light clusters and their fission. Previously these processes were well known for normal nuclei as the decay channels at low excitation energies. We have generalized these models for the case of hyper-matter. In this way we make extension of nuclear reaction studies at low temperature into the strange sector. We demonstrate how the new decay channels can be integrated in the whole disintegration process. Their importance for mass and isotope distributions of produced hyper-fragments is emphasized. New and exotic isotopes obtained within these processes may provide a unique opportunity for investigating hyperon interaction in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The authors generalize standard nuclear evaporation and fission models to describe the decay of excited hypernuclei containing a small number of hyperons (primarily one \(\Lambda\)), under the condition that the hyperon fraction is negligible compared to the total nucleon number, so that the hyperon-nucleon interaction is of the same order as the nucleon-nucleon interaction and the hyperon potential is about 2/3 of the nucleon potential.

## Method

The authors extended the sequential evaporation model (Eq. \ref{eq:eva}) to include emission of \(\Lambda\)-hyperons and light hypernuclei (\(^{3}_{\Lambda}\)H, \(^{4}_{\Lambda}\)H, \(^{4}_{\Lambda}\)He, \(^{5}_{\Lambda}\)He, \(^{6}_{\Lambda}\)He) from excited compound hypernuclei, using a hyper binding energy parametrization (Eq. \ref{hyp}) proportional to the hyperon fraction \(H/A\). They also generalized the Bohr-Wheeler fission model (Eq. \ref{eq:fis}) for hypernuclei by adding a hyper fission barrier \(B_{f}^{hyp} = E_{0}^{hyp}/2\) (Eq. \ref{fishr}) and introduced a canonical probability (Eq. \ref{pah}) for the \(\Lambda\)-hyperon to be deposited in the larger fission fragment. The de-excitation cascade was simulated by Monte Carlo, with strict conservation of energy and momentum at each emission step.

## Key numbers

* The hyper binding energy parametrization: \(E_{b}^{\rm hyp}(A,H) = (H/A) \cdot (10.68 A - 21.27 A^{2/3})\) MeV (Eq. \ref{hyp}).
* The additional hyper fission barrier for heavy nuclei is estimated to increase the barrier by about 0.5 MeV, based on \(B_{f}^{hyp} = E_{0}^{hyp}/2\) at \(A' = A/2\).
* The temperature of the system is given by \(T = \sqrt{E_{AZ}^{*} / (aA)}\) with level density parameter \(a \approx 0.125\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
* For the \(^{209}_{\Lambda}\)Bi system, the initial nucleus can lose around ten nucleons during evolution to the saddle deformation (Fig. 6).
* The probability of \(\Lambda\)-hyperon evaporation from heavy compound hypernuclei remains essentially smaller than the dominating fission and evaporation-residue probabilities (Fig. 4).
* The yield of evaporated light hypernuclei (e.g., \(^{5}_{\Lambda}\)He) is more than one order of magnitude less than the yield of single \(\Lambda\)-hyperons (Fig. 5 compared with Fig. 4).

## Context

This work extends the well-established evaporation and fission models for normal nuclei (Weisskopf 1937, Botvina 1987, Bonasera 1995) into the strange sector, complementing previous studies of hypernuclei produced via multifragmentation at high excitation energies (Botvina 2007, Buyukcizmeci 2013). The authors note that the subthreshold production of hyperons becomes possible at ion energies down to about 1 \(A\) GeV (Botvina 2016), and that yields of hypernuclear residues in peripheral ion collisions saturate at energies above 3-5 \(A\) GeV. The work enables predictions for future experiments at FAIR (CBM, PANDA, Super-FRS) and NICA (BM@N, MPD) aimed at producing and studying exotic hypernuclei, including multistrange hyper-fragments.
