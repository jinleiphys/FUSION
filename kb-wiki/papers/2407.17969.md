# Effects of Final State Interactions on Landau Singularities

> In certain kinematic and particle mass configurations, triangle singularities may lead to line-shapes which mimic the effects of resonances. This well-known effect is scrutinized here in the presence of final-state rescattering. The goal is achieved first by utilizing general arguments provided by Landau equations, and second by applying a modern scattering formalism with explicit two- and three-body unitarity.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when an infinite set of final-state rescattering diagrams (ladder diagrams) is added to a triangle diagram, the triangle singularity remains at exactly the same kinematic position and with the same singularity structure, unlike resonance poles which shift under such dressing. This conclusion holds for the stable-particle case via Landau equations and is supported by numerical checks in a unitary three-body framework with unstable particles.

## Method

The authors first analyze the singularity structure of ladder diagrams with arbitrary numbers of loops using Landau equations, systematically examining all possible contractions of the "triangle + n boxes" diagrams to identify which subgraphs produce singularities. They then implement a toy model within the Infinite Volume Unitarity (IVU) three-body scattering formalism, which includes explicit two- and three-body unitarity, to compute line shapes for a spinless mock-up of the $a_1(1260)$ system decaying to $f_0\pi$ via $K^*K$ intermediate states, with all particles assigned physical masses.

## Key numbers

* The triangle singularity condition for the decay process is given by the determinant equation $y_{12}^2 + y_{23}^2 + r^2 - 2r y_{12} y_{23} - 1 = 0$, where $y_{12} = (m_1^2 + m_2^2 - s)/(2m_1m_2)$, $y_{23} = (m_2^2 + m_3^2 - \sigma)/(2m_2 m_3)$, and $r = (m_1^2 + m_3^2 - m_\pi^2)/(2m_1 m_3)$.
* The singularity exists on the physical boundary only when $s \geq (m_1+m_2)^2$ and $\sigma \geq (m_2+m_3)^2$, with the bounds on the invariant masses given by $(m_2+m_3)^2 < \sigma < m_2^2+m_3^2 + (m_2/m_1)(m_1^2+m_3^2-m_\pi^2)$ and $(m_1+m_2)^2 < s < m_1^2+m_2^2 + (m_2/m_3)(m_1^2+m_3^2-m_\pi^2)$.
* For the two-loop (triangle + 1 box) diagram, solving the Landau equations yields complex-valued solutions for $\sigma$ for any choice of particle masses in the loops, indicating no leading Landau singularity on the real axis.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of triangle singularities, previously studied for single-loop diagrams by Bayar (2016) and Guo (2019), to the case where infinite final-state rescattering is included. The paper uses the IVU formalism developed by Mai (2017) and Sadasivan (2021), which reformulates Faddeev three-body equations with separable two-body input and explicit unitarity. The results are directly relevant to the ongoing debate about whether the $a_1(1420)$ structure observed by COMPASS (2015) is an excited resonance or a kinematic singularity arising from $K^*K \to f_0\pi$ rescattering through kaon exchange, as discussed by Mikhasenko (2015) and Aceti (2016). The framework sets the stage for future quantitative analyses of experimental data that aim to distinguish resonance effects from kinematic singularities.
