# Operator Product Expansion in QCD Is Not Consistent With Quantum Field Theory For Gluon Distribution Function

> Since the operator product expansion (OPE) is applicable at short distance the OPE in QCD does not solve the long distance confinement problem involving hadron in QCD where the non-perturbative QCD is applicable. In this paper we show that the gauge invariant definition of the non-perturbative gluon distribution function inside the hadron consistent with the operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD at high energy colliders is not consistent with the gauge invariant definition of the non-perturbative gluon distribution function in the quantum field theory.

## Key claim

The gauge-invariant definition of the non-perturbative gluon distribution function inside a hadron that is consistent with the operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD is not consistent with the gauge-invariant definition of the non-perturbative gluon distribution function in quantum field theory, because the OPE-based definition contains infinite powers of the quantum gluon field rather than the quadratic powers required for a distribution function in quantum field theory.

## Method

The author derives the gauge-invariant gluon distribution function from quantum field theory (QFT) using the background field method of QCD, obtaining an expression (eq. o68) that contains quadratic powers of the quantum gluon field and a Wilson line built from a classical SU(3) pure gauge background field. This is contrasted with the standard OPE-based definition (eq. o69) used in the literature, which contains the gluon field tensor and a Wilson line built from the quantum gluon field, leading to infinite powers of the quantum gluon field. The author argues that the OPE-based definition reduces to a proper distribution function only in the light-cone gauge, whereas a gauge-invariant definition must work in any gauge.

## Key numbers

* The gauge-invariant gluon distribution function from QFT (eq. o68) contains quadratic powers of the quantum gluon field \(Q_\mu^a(x)\).
* The OPE-based gluon distribution function (eq. o69) contains the gluon field tensor \(F^{+\nu b}(0,y',0_T)\) and a Wilson line with the quantum gluon field \(Q^{+c}(0,y'',0)\), leading to infinite powers of the quantum gluon field.
* Even without the Wilson line, the gauge non-invariant definition from eq. o69 contains cubic and quartic powers of the quantum gluon field.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior factorization proofs using the background field method of QCD (Nayak 2015, Nayak 2016, Nayak 2017) to show a fundamental inconsistency between the OPE-based definition of the gluon PDF (Collins and Soper 1982) and the definition required by quantum field theory. The paper argues that the OPE, being a short-distance expansion, cannot properly incorporate the long-distance confinement problem involving hadrons, and that the standard OPE-based gluon PDF definition is only valid in the light-cone gauge. This challenges the theoretical foundation of how gluon distribution functions are defined in high-energy collider phenomenology and suggests that the QFT-based definition (eq. o68) should be used instead.
