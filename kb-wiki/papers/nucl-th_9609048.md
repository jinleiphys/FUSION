# Probing the Origin of the EMC Effect via Tagged Structure Functions of the Deuteron

> We demonstrate that measurement of tagged structure functions of the deuteron in (e,e'N) semi-inclusive reactions can discriminate between different hypotheses on the origin of the nuclear EMC effect. By choosing extreme backward kinematics for the spectator nucleon to minimize effects from the deuteron wave function and final state interactions, one can isolate the modifications in the structure of the bound nucleon within the impulse approximation. The same reaction can be used to extract the large-x neutron to proton structure function ratio.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that measuring tagged structure functions of the deuteron in semi-inclusive (e,e'N) reactions can discriminate between different hypotheses on the origin of the nuclear EMC effect, provided one selects extreme backward kinematics for the spectator nucleon to minimize effects from the deuteron wave function and final state interactions, thereby isolating modifications in the structure of the bound nucleon within the impulse approximation.

## Method

The authors formulate the semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering cross section for e + D → e + N + X in terms of invariant structure functions (F_L, F_T, F_TL, F_TT) that depend on x, Q^2, and the spectator nucleon's transverse momentum p_T and light-cone momentum fraction α_s. They compare predictions from two classes of models: binding models (where the EMC effect arises from conventional nucleon and pion degrees of freedom, with possible off-shell nucleon structure modifications) and the color screening model (where suppression of point-like configurations in bound nucleons is the main source). The analysis focuses on backward kinematics (α_s ≥ 1, p_T ≈ 0) where final state interactions are suppressed and the longitudinal structure function F_L is enhanced, allowing extraction of the effective bound nucleon structure function F_{2N}^{eff}.

## Key numbers

- The condition for spectator kinematics requires α_s ≥ 1 - x, and extreme backward kinematics additionally requires p_T ≈ 0.
- For nucleon momenta |p_s| > 300 MeV/c, the main contributions to the deuteron wave function come from distances < 1.2 fm.
- The parameter κ = |⟨U_A⟩/ΔE_A| ≈ 1/10 in the color screening model, with ⟨U_A⟩ ≈ -40 MeV for heavy nuclei and ΔE_A ≈ 0.5 GeV.
- For the deuteron, ΔE_D > 2(M_Δ - M) ≈ 0.6 GeV, since the N-Δ component is forbidden by isospin.
- Factorization-breaking corrections from negative energy components amount to < 1-2% for all x < 0.9.
- The spectator nucleon light-cone fraction satisfies α_s ≤ 2 - x in the Bjorken limit.
- The average transverse momentum transferred in hadronic soft interactions is ⟨p_T^2⟩^{1/2} ≈ 200-300 MeV/c.
- The spectator nucleon momentum threshold for detection is about 100-150 MeV/c in jet target experiments.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for understanding the nuclear EMC effect, first discovered by the European Muon Collaboration (EMC1) and studied in many subsequent measurements (EMC2, EMC3, EMC4, EMC5). The paper contrasts binding models (Frankfurt and Strikman 1988, Akulinichev et al. 1985, Dunne and Thomas 1985, Ericson and Thomas 1983) with the color screening model of suppression of point-like configurations (Frankfurt and Strikman 1985, 1988). The proposed tagged structure function measurements would become feasible at Jefferson Lab (K. Griffioen) and HERMES at HERA, and could enable extraction of the neutron to proton structure function ratio at large x while minimizing uncertainties from nuclear effects in the deuteron.
