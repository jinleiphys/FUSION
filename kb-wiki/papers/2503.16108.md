# Inclusive $\mathrm{D}^0$ photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions

> We compute the differential cross section for inclusive $\mathrm{D}^0$ production in ultraperipheral collisions within the Color Glass Condensate framework. Our predictions are found to be in relatively good agreement with the CMS data at small transverse momentum, which is the region of validity of our approach. Furthermore, we quantify saturation effects by a nuclear modification ratio $R_{pA}$ for $\mathrm{D}^0$ photoproduction and examine both analytically and numerically the collinear factorization limit of the $\mathrm{D}^0$ differential photoproduction cross section.

## Key claim

The authors compute the differential cross section for inclusive D⁰ production in ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions using the Color Glass Condensate framework, finding "relatively good agreement" with CMS data only at small transverse momentum, which they explicitly state is the region of validity of their approach.

## Method

The calculation uses Light-Cone Perturbation Theory to describe the photon splitting into a charm quark pair, with the subsequent eikonal scattering of the quark pair off the target nucleus described by Wilson lines in the Color Glass Condensate formalism. The partonic cross section for charm quark production is derived and then convoluted with a photon flux from the Weizsäcker-Williams method, a probability factor for zero-neutron emission, and a fragmentation function from Kniehl (2006) to obtain the D⁰ meson cross section. The dipole scattering amplitude is obtained from the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with initial conditions fit to HERA data.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to m = 1.5 GeV, approximating the D⁰ meson mass
- Saturation scale Qs ~ 1.5 GeV at LHC energies for charm quarks
- Photon virtuality maximum Q²_max ≈ 0.001 GeV² for a lead nucleus
- Lead nucleus parameters: charge Z=82, atomic number A=208, mass M_A=193.64 GeV
- Two values used for the zero-neutron emission parameter: S = (10.4 fm)² and S = (17.4 fm)², the latter from Baltz (1997, 1996)
- Fragmentation function parameters: N=0.694, ε=0.101 from Kniehl (2006)

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate approach to inclusive D⁰ photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions, following earlier CGC calculations for dijet production by Dominguez (2011) and for inclusive charm production by Goncalves (2017) and Marquet (2009). The calculation is compared to preliminary CMS data on inclusive D⁰ photoproduction, and the authors note that the small-x values probed at the LHC (down to 10⁻⁶) are beyond the reach of the future Electron-Ion Collider, but the study can lay the foundation for future inclusive measurements in electron-nucleus scattering at the EIC.
