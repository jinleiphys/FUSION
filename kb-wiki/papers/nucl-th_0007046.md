# Signatures of Quark-Gluon Plasma Phase Transition in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

> In high-energy nuclear collisions, the new phase of the quark-gluon plasma is indicated by an anomalous increase in pressure, an excess of direct photon production, an excess of strangeness production, and an anomalous J/psi suppression. We review these signatures and discuss how recent high-energy heavy-ion experiments at CERN are consistent with the production of the quark-gluon plasma in high-energy Pb+Pb collisions.

# Signatures of Quark-Gluon Plasma Phase Transition in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
The experimental data from CERN heavy-ion experiments appear to be consistent with the production of the quark-gluon plasma in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV, but the evidence for each signature carries caveats: the freeze-out volume anomaly requires further investigation, the strangeness enhancement evidence depends on poorly known strangeness-raising cross sections, and the direct photon and J/psi suppression signatures rely on model-dependent assumptions about initial conditions and critical energy density thresholds.

## Method
The paper reviews four proposed signatures of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation in high-energy nuclear collisions: anomalous freeze-out volume from increased pressure in the QGP phase, direct photon emission from the plasma, strangeness enhancement (particularly multi-strange hyperons), and anomalous J/psi suppression. For each signature, the author compares experimental data from CERN experiments (NA44, WA98, WA97, NA50) at 158A GeV Pb+Pb collisions with theoretical models, including Bjorken hydrodynamics for freeze-out volume, hard scattering calculations for direct photons, RQMD cascade calculations for strangeness, and both analytical and Monte Carlo cascade models for J/psi absorption.

## Key numbers
- The pressure in the quark-gluon plasma is given approximately by P_QGP ~ 37 pi^2 T^4/90, compared to the pressure of a pion gas P_pion = 6 pi^2 T^4/90
- For collisions at 158A GeV per nucleon, the energy density deposited per nucleon-nucleon collision is approximately N x 1 GeV/fm^3
- The critical nucleon-nucleon collision number N_c = 4 gives good agreement with pA, O+A, S+U, and Pb+Pb J/psi data, corresponding to a critical energy density of epsilon_c = 4.2 GeV/fm^3
- The production of Omega^- + anti-Omega^+ in Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV is enhanced by up to a factor of 15 relative to that of p+Be
- The direct photon model assumes an initial plasma formation time at tau = 1 fm/c, with a temperature of 300 MeV cooling to a critical temperature of 200 MeV
- The cross section for dissociation of J/psi by a pion has a peak of about 1 mb above the threshold of 640 MeV

## Context
This review paper extends the summary of Heinz and Jacobs (2000) on evidence for QGP creation at CERN, re-examining whether the excitement is justified. It discusses theoretical predictions from Feinberg (1976) on direct photons, Rafelski (1982) on strangeness enhancement, and Matsui and Satz (1986) on J/psi suppression. The paper enables future work by identifying open questions: the need for reliable strangeness-raising cross sections using the quark-interchange model of Barnes and Swanson (1992), and the need for refined Monte Carlo cascade calculations incorporating improved J/psi dissociation cross sections and rho meson dynamics.
