# BPS Skyrme neutron stars in generalized gravity

> We study the coupling of nuclear matter described by the BPS Skyrme model to generalized gravity. Concretely, we consider the Starobinsky model which provides the leading-order correction to the Einstein-Hilbert action. Static solutions describing neutron stars are found both for the full field theory and for the mean-field approximation. We always consider the full Starobinsky model in the nonperturbative approach, using appropriately generalized shooting methods for the numerical neutron star calculations. Many of our results are similar to previous investigations of neutron stars for the Starobinsky model using other models of nuclear matter, but there are some surprizing discrepancies. The "Newtonian mass" relevant for the surface redshift, e.g., results larger than the ADM mass in our model, in contrast to other investigations. This difference is related to the particularly high stiffness of nuclear matter described by the BPS Skyrme model and offers an interesting possibility to distinguish different models of nuclear matter within generalized gravity.

## Key claim

The BPS Skyrme model, when coupled to Starobinsky gravity ($f(R)=R-\alpha R^2$), produces neutron star solutions where the "Newtonian mass" relevant for surface redshift is larger than the ADM mass, a result that contradicts previous investigations using other models of nuclear matter and is attributed to the particularly high stiffness of the BPS Skyrme model.

## Method

The authors solved the modified Einstein equations for the Starobinsky $f(R)$ gravity model using a generalized double shooting method for the numerical integration, imposing spherical symmetry and static solutions. They coupled the BPS Skyrme model to gravity in two ways: using the exact field theory with a generalized hedgehog ansatz for the Skyrme field, and using a mean-field approximation that produces a barotropic equation of state. The numerical calculations were performed for four different Skyrme potentials (step function, Skyrme potential, quadratic Skyrme potential, and partially flat potential), with model parameters fixed by fitting to the nuclear saturation density $n_0 = 0.153\:\text{fm}^{-3}$ and binding energy per nucleon $E_B = 16.3$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density used for parameter fitting: $n_0 = 0.153\:\text{fm}^{-3}$
- Binding energy per nucleon used for parameter fitting: $E_B = 16.3$ MeV
- For the step function potential: $\mu^2 = 70.61\: \text{MeV/fm}^3$, $\lambda^2 = 30.99\: \text{MeV fm}^3$
- For the Skyrme potential: $\mu^2 = 88.26\: \text{MeV/fm}^3$, $\lambda^2 = 26.88\: \text{MeV fm}^3$
- For the quadratic Skyrme potential: $\mu^2 = 141.22\: \text{MeV/fm}^3$, $\lambda^2 = 15.493\: \text{MeV fm}^3$
- For the partially flat potential: $\mu^2 = 121.08 \: \text{MeV/fm}^3$, $\lambda^2 = 23.60\: \text{MeV fm}^3$
- The scalar field mass in the Starobinsky model is $m^2 = 1/(6\alpha)$, requiring $\alpha > 0$ for a real scalar field
- For a given ADM mass, the star radius always grows with increasing $\alpha$ (given in units of km$^2$)
- The maximum mass grows slightly with increasing $\alpha$ in the mean-field case
- The exact case always leads to a slightly more compact star than the mean-field case and does not reach as high maximal masses

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron stars in Starobinsky gravity (e.g., Astashenok (2017) and Kase (2019)) by using the BPS Skyrme model as the nuclear matter description, rather than phenomenological equations of state. The BPS Skyrme model was introduced by Adam (2010) and has the property that its stress-energy tensor takes the form of a perfect fluid, allowing direct coupling of microscopic degrees of freedom to gravity. The key finding that the "Newtonian mass" exceeds the ADM mass, contrary to other investigations, offers a potential observational test to distinguish between different models of nuclear matter within generalized gravity theories.
