# Symmetry constraints on generalizations of Bjorken flow

> I explain a generalization of Bjorken flow where the medium has finite transverse size and expands both radially and along the beam axis. If one assumes that the equations of viscous hydrodynamics can be used, with p=epsilon/3 and zero bulk viscosity, then the flow I describe can be developed into an exact solution of the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations. The local four-velocity in the flow is entirely determined by the assumption of symmetry under a subgroup of the conformal group.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalization of Bjorken flow that incorporates finite transverse size and radial expansion, where the local four-velocity is entirely determined by symmetry under a subgroup of the conformal group rather than by dynamical equations, provided one assumes conformally invariant viscous hydrodynamics with p=ε/3 and zero bulk viscosity.

## Method

The author replaces the translation invariance in the transverse plane of standard Bjorken flow with invariance under special conformal transformations that, combined with rotations around the beam axis, form an SO(3) subgroup of the conformal group parameterized by an inverse length q. The four-velocity profile is fixed by requiring that the covariant vector u_μ has ζ-weight -1 under the conformal Killing vector ζ, and the energy density is determined by solving the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations with conformal equation of state p=ε/3 and shear viscosity η = H_0 ε^(3/4).

## Key numbers

- The inverse length parameter q is set to 1/q = 4.3 fm, representative of gold-gold collisions at top RHIC energies.
- For the inviscid flow, the integration constant ε̂_0 ≈ 880, derived from comparing entropy per unit rapidity dS/dη ≈ 5000 with the phenomenological estimate dN_charged/dη = 660 at √s_NN = 200 GeV.
- The shear viscosity parameter H_0 = 0.33, based on the lattice result η/s = 0.134 for SU(3) gluodynamics.
- For the viscous flow, the integration constant T̂_0 = 5.55 is required to match the entropy per unit rapidity.
- The inviscid energy density scales as ε ∝ 1/τ^(4/3) in the q → 0 limit, recovering standard Bjorken flow.
- The pathology time for viscous Bjorken flow is τ_* = (H_0 / (2e_0))^(3/2), where the viscous correction dominates over pressure.

## Context

This work extends the standard Bjorken flow (Bjorken 1982) by relaxing the unrealistic assumption of translation invariance in the transverse plane, which forbids radial flow. The approach builds on earlier gauge-string duality calculations (Gubser 2008, Gubser 2009) that suggested an O(3) symmetry for heavy-ion collisions, but the present paper derives the flow purely from symmetry and hydrodynamics without relying on AdS/CFT dynamics. The solution enables quantitative estimates of deviations from Bjorken flow due to finite nuclear size and provides a framework for testing how initial-state deviations from SO(3)_q symmetry propagate through the hydrodynamic stage.
