# Quark Mass and Flavour Dependence of the QCD Phase Transition

> We analyze the quark mass and flavour dependence of the QCD phase transition temperature. When the lightest pseudo-scalar meson mass (m_PS) is larger than 2 GeV the critical temperature is controlled by the gluonic sector of QCD alone. For smaller values of the lightest meson mass the pseudo-critical temperature decreases slowly with m_PS. For a large regime of meson masses the pseudo-critical temperature of 2-flavour QCD is about 10% larger than in the 3-flavour case. On lattices with temporal extent N_t=4 an extrapolation to the chiral limit yields T_c = 173(8) MeV and 154(8) MeV for 2 and 3-flavour QCD, respectively. We also analyze dynamical quark mass effects on the screening of the heavy quark potential. A detailed analysis of the heavy quark free energy in 3-flavour QCD shows that close to T_c screening effects are approximately quark mass independent already for pseudo-scalar meson masses m_PS = 800 MeV and screening sets in at distances r = 0.3 fm.

## Key claim

The QCD phase transition temperature depends on the lightest pseudo-scalar meson mass (m_PS) and the number of quark flavours, with the critical temperature in the chiral limit on N_tau=4 lattices being 173(8) MeV for 2-flavour QCD and 154(8) MeV for 3-flavour QCD; however, these values are obtained on a single lattice spacing and the extrapolation to the chiral limit assumes specific critical scaling forms (O(4) for 2-flavour, linear for 3-flavour) that may still receive subleading corrections.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using an improved gauge action (tree-level Symanzik) and an improved staggered fermion action (p4-action with fat links) on lattices with temporal extent N_tau=4 and spatial sizes up to 16^3. They determined pseudo-critical couplings from maxima of Polyakov loop, chiral condensate, and gauge action susceptibilities via Ferrenberg-Swendsen reweighting for 2-flavour, 3-flavour, and (2+1)-flavour QCD at multiple bare quark masses, then set the scale using zero-temperature simulations on 16^4 lattices to extract the string tension and meson masses.

## Key numbers

- For 2-flavour QCD in the chiral limit on N_tau=4 lattices: T_c = 173(8) MeV
- For 3-flavour QCD in the chiral limit on N_tau=4 lattices: T_c = 154(8) MeV
- The pseudo-critical temperature of 2-flavour QCD is about 10% larger than in the 3-flavour case over a large regime of meson masses
- For pseudo-scalar meson masses m_PS larger than 2 GeV, the critical temperature is controlled by the gluonic sector alone
- Screening of the heavy quark free energy in 3-flavour QCD sets in at distances r = 0.3 fm and is approximately quark mass independent for m_PS less than or about 800 MeV
- For 3-flavour QCD with m_q=0.01 on 16^3x4 lattices, the pseudo-critical coupling from the chiral condensate susceptibility is beta_c = 3.2806(20)
- For 2-flavour QCD with m_q=0.10 on 16^3x4 lattices, the pseudo-critical coupling is beta_c = 3.646(4)
- The ratio T_c/sqrt(sigma) for 2-flavour QCD ranges from 0.456(11) at m_q=0.025 to 0.552(16) at m_q=0.20
- The ratio T_c/m_V for 3-flavour QCD ranges from 0.194(10) at m_q=0.025 to 0.084(1) at m_q=1.00

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of the QCD transition temperature using unimproved actions (Aoki 1998, Aoki 1999, Luetgemeier 1998, Fukugita 1990) by employing improved gauge and fermion actions to reduce cut-off effects, and provides a systematic mapping of the quark mass and flavour dependence. The results enable improved estimates for the physical QCD transition temperature with two light and one heavier strange quark, and establish that the transition temperature in 2-flavour and 3-flavour QCD provide upper and lower bounds for the (2+1)-flavour case. The analysis of screening effects in the heavy quark free energy extends the understanding of Debye screening in the presence of dynamical quarks, complementing work on string breaking at non-zero temperature (DeTar 1999).
