# Charm and hidden charm scalar mesons in the nuclear medium

> We study the renormalization of the properties of low lying charm and hidden charm scalar mesons in a nuclear medium, concretely of the D_{s0}(2317) and the theoretical hidden charm state X(3700). We find that for the D_{s0}(2317), with negligible width at zero density, the width becomes about 100 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, while in the case of the X(3700) the width becomes as large as 200 MeV. We discuss the origin of this new width and trace it to reactions occurring in the nucleus, while offering a guideline for future experiments testing these changes. We also show how those medium modifications will bring valuable information on the nature of the scalar resonances and the mechanisms of the interaction of D mesons with nucleons and nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors find that for the dynamically generated scalar mesons \(D_{s0}(2317)\) and \(X(3700)\), the width increases dramatically in nuclear matter, to about 100 MeV and 200 MeV respectively at normal nuclear matter density, but these results depend on the assumption that the dominant medium effect comes from dressing the \(D\) meson propagator while neglecting the \(\bar{D}\) selfenergy, and the quantitative values carry theoretical uncertainties from the SU(4) symmetry breaking scheme and the choice of form factor cutoff.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium modification of the \(D\) meson selfenergy by combining an \(s\)-wave contribution from a selfconsistent coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter calculation of \(DN\) scattering (including Pauli blocking, baryon mean-field bindings, and pion and \(D\) selfenergies) with a \(p\)-wave contribution from \(N^{-1}\Lambda_c\), \(N^{-1}\Sigma_c\), and \(N^{-1}\Sigma_c^*\) excitations using Lindhard functions. They then insert the dressed \(D\) propagator into the two-meson loop function \(G\) for the \(DK\) and \(D\bar{D}\) channels, which is used in the Bethe-Salpeter equation to find the pole positions of the \(D_{s0}(2317)\) and \(X(3700)\) in the medium.

## Key numbers

- For the \(D_{s0}(2317)\) at zero density, the width is negligible; at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), the width becomes about \(100\ \text{MeV}\).
- For the \(X(3700)\) at zero density, the pole is at \((3722 - i18)\ \text{MeV}\); at normal nuclear matter density, the width becomes as large as \(200\ \text{MeV}\).
- The \(X(3700)\) couples most strongly to \(D\bar{D}\) with \(|g_X| = 6198\ \text{MeV}\) for \(D^+D^-\) and \(D^0\bar{D}^0\).
- The \(D_{s0}(2317)\) couples most strongly to \(DK\) with \(|g_{D_{s0}}| = 5102\ \text{MeV}\) for both \(K^+D^0\) and \(K^0D^+\).
- The \(p\)-wave form factor uses \(\Lambda = 1.05\ \text{GeV}\) for light hadrons, but the authors note that a substantially larger \(\Lambda\) may be needed for heavy \(D\) mesons.

## Context

This work extends the study of medium modifications of dynamically generated scalar mesons from the light sector (e.g., \(\sigma(600)\), \(f_0(980)\), \(a_0(980)\) studied by Oset (2000) and others) into the charm sector, specifically the \(D_{s0}(2317)\) and the predicted hidden charm state \(X(3700)\). The \(D_{s0}(2317)\) is identified as a \(DK\) bound state following the theoretical framework of Kolomeitsev (2003) and Gamermann (2006), while the \(X(3700)\) is a pole below the \(D\bar{D}\) threshold predicted in Gamermann (2006) and possibly observed by the Belle collaboration (2007). The medium calculations rely on the \(D\) meson selfenergy from the coupled-channel approach of Tolos (2007) and Mizutani (2006), and the results provide a guideline for future experiments that could test the nature of these resonances by measuring their in-medium widths.
