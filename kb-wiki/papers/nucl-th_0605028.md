# Fusion reaction of halo nuclei: A real-time wave-packet method for three-body tunneling dynamics

> We investigate fusion cross section of a nucleus with a valence neutron, using the time-dependent wave-packet method. For a stable projectile, in which the valence neutron is tightly bound (e_n < -3 MeV), the neutron could enhance the fusion probability when the matching condition of orbital energies are satisfied. In contrast, for a halo nucleus, in which the binding energy of the neutron is very small (e_n>-1 MeV), the fusion probability is hindered by the presence of the weakly bound neutron.

## Key claim

For stable projectiles with a tightly bound valence neutron (binding energy below -3 MeV), the neutron can enhance sub-barrier fusion probability when orbital energies match between projectile and target; however, for halo nuclei with a very weakly bound neutron (binding energy above -1 MeV), the fusion probability is suppressed by the presence of the halo neutron, contrary to some earlier predictions of enhancement.

## Method

The authors used a time-dependent wave-packet (TDWP) method to solve the three-body Schrodinger equation for a projectile composed of a core nucleus and a valence neutron colliding with a target nucleus. The projectile was treated as a two-body bound system with the neutron in an s-wave orbital, and the core-target potential included an imaginary part inside the Coulomb barrier to absorb flux representing fusion. The wave function was expressed in a body-fixed frame with radial coordinates discretized on a mesh of 0.2 to 1 fm, and time evolution was performed using fourth-order Taylor expansion.

## Key numbers

- For the stable projectile case, the valence neutron binding energy was set to -3.5 MeV at the 2s orbital, using a fictitious "11Be" nucleus on 40Ca
- For the halo projectile case, the neutron energy was set to -0.6 MeV to describe halo 11Be on 209Bi
- The TDWP calculations included partial waves up to l = 70 hbar for the neutron-core relative angular momentum, while earlier CDCC calculations used at most l = 4 hbar
- When partial waves were truncated to l <= 4 hbar (matching CDCC calculations), strong sub-barrier fusion enhancement appeared, but this enhancement disappeared when higher partial waves were included

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of fusion probability for halo 11Be on 208Pb (Nakatsukasa 2003, 2004) and addresses a contradiction with coupled discretized continuum channels (CDCC) calculations by Hagino (2000) and Diaz-Torres (2002) that predicted strong sub-barrier fusion enhancement. The authors identify that the discrepancy arises from different truncation of relative angular momentum between the neutron and the 10Be core. The results are consistent with experimental measurements for 11Be on 209Bi (Signorini 2004) and 6He on 238U (Raabe 2004) that indicate no enhancement of fusion cross section for halo nuclei.
