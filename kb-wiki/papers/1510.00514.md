# The light-front energy-momentum tensor

> We present the first complete parametrization for the matrix elements of the generic light-front gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor, derive the expressions giving separately the spin and orbital angular momentum of quarks and gluons as probed in high-energy scattering experiments, and discuss the relations with two-parton generalized and transverse-momentum dependent distributions. As a by-product, we recovered the Burkardt sum rule, clarified its physical meaning and obtained similar new sum rules for higher-twist distributions.

# The light-front energy-momentum tensor

## Key claim
The paper presents the first complete parametrization of matrix elements for the generic light-front gauge-invariant energy-momentum tensor, but this parametrization is only valid when the non-local phase factor is identified with a Wilson line along the light-front direction, and the scalar functions are complex-valued because they depend on whether the Wilson lines are future-pointing or past-pointing.

## Method
The author constructs a parametrization of the generic light-front energy-momentum tensor for a spin-1/2 target using 32 independent Dirac structures constrained by Lorentz and discrete space-time symmetries, with the non-locality encoded via Wilson lines along the light-front direction. The scalar functions in the parametrization are expressed in terms of two scalar variables (ξ and t) and a parameter η indicating the direction of the Wilson lines, and are related to generalized parton distributions and transverse-momentum dependent distributions through moments of the GTMD correlator.

## Key numbers
- The generic light-front EMT parametrization contains 32 independent complex-valued amplitudes, corresponding to 32 independent Dirac structures.
- The momentum constraints give ∑_{a=q,G} A_a(0) = 1 and ∑_{a=q,G} \bar C_a(0) = 0 for the total four-momentum.
- The angular momentum constraint gives ∑_{a=q,G} B_a(0) = 0, known as the anomalous gravitomagnetic moment sum rule.
- The longitudinal quark spin contribution is given by -½[A^{e,1}_4(0,0) - A^{e,1}_5(0,0)] = -½ D_q(0).
- The longitudinal gluon spin contribution is given by -A^{e,5}_4(0,0).
- The total angular momentum for quarks is ½[A_q(0) + B_q(0)] and for gluons is ½[A_G(0) + B_G(0)].

## Context
This work extends the parametrization of the symmetric kinetic (Belinfante-Rosenfeld) energy-momentum tensor given by Ji (1996) and the asymmetric kinetic EMT discussed by Shore (1999) and Bakker (2004), providing the first complete parametrization of the general EMT with non-locality along the light-front direction. The paper recovers the Burkardt sum rule and derives three new sum rules for higher-twist transverse-momentum dependent distributions, all expressing conservation of transverse momentum, which can be tested using phenomenological models, lattice QCD, and perturbative QCD.
