# Uncertainty evaluation and correlation analysis of single-particle energies in phenomenological nuclear mean field: An investigation of propagating uncertainties for independent model parameters

> Based on Monte Carlo approach and conventional error analysis theory, taking the heaviest doubly magic nucleus $^{208}$Pb as an example, we firstly evaluate the propagated uncertainties of universal potential parameters for three typical types of single-particle energies in the phenomenological Woods-Saxon mean field. Accepting the Woods-Saxon modeling with uncorrelated model parameters, we find that the standard deviations of single-particle energies obtained by the Monte Carlo simulation and the error propagation rules are in good agreement with each other. It seems that the energy uncertaintis of the single-particle levels regularly evoluate with some quantum numbers to a large extent for the given parameter uncertainties. Further, the correlation properties of the single-particle levels within the domain of input parameter uncertainties are analyzed using the method of statistical analysis, e.g., with the aid of Pearson correlation coefficients. It is found that the positive, negative or unrelated relationship may appear between two selected single-particle levels, which will be very helpful for evaluating the theoretical uncertainty related to the single-particle levels (e.g., $K$ isomer) in nuclear structural calculations.

## Key claim

The authors evaluate how uncertainties in the six independent parameters of the Woods-Saxon potential propagate to single-particle energies in the doubly magic nucleus 208Pb, finding that the standard deviations from Monte Carlo simulation and from conventional error propagation formulas agree well, but only under the assumption that the model parameters are uncorrelated.

## Method

The authors used the Woods-Saxon Hamiltonian with the universal parameter set for 208Pb, treating the six parameters (V0, r0c, a0c, λ, r0so, a0so) as independent Gaussian random variables with standard deviations calibrated by percentage coefficients (0.1% to 10% of the mean values). They generated 10,000 random parameter sets via the Box-Muller algorithm, solved the Schrödinger equation for each set using an axially deformed harmonic-oscillator basis (principal quantum number N ≤ 12 for protons, N ≤ 14 for neutrons), and computed the single-particle energies. The uncertainties were then estimated both by Monte Carlo sampling and by the analytic error propagation formula (sum of squared sensitivity coefficients times parameter variances), and Pearson correlation coefficients between single-particle levels were calculated from the sampled distributions.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviations for the six Woods-Saxon parameters were calibrated using percentage coefficients: c1 = 0.1%, c2 = 0.1%, c3 = 1%, c4 = 3%, c5 = 1%, c6 = 10% of the universal parameter mean values.
- The Monte Carlo sampling used 10,000 samples for the parameter sets, which the authors state is large enough to suppress stochastic errors.
- The level-level coincidence matrix was constructed with 4096 × 4096 channels and an energy calibration of 10 keV/channel, covering an energy range from -40.96 to 0.00 MeV.
- The percentage differences PD(p1) for the potential depth parameter V0, extracted from experimental data across eight spherical nuclei, distribute between ±10%.

## Context

This work extends prior uncertainty quantification studies of the Woods-Saxon mean field, particularly the investigation by Irene (2018) on parameter adjustment and parametric correlations, and the authors' own previous macroscopic-microscopic model studies (Chai 2018, Yang, Li 2019). The paper enables future evaluations of theoretical uncertainties in nuclear structure calculations that depend on single-particle levels, such as predictions of K-isomer excitation energies, by providing both the uncertainties and the statistical correlation properties of the single-particle levels.
