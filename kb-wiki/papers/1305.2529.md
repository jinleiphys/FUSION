# Tensor force and shape evolution of Si isotopes in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model

> Much interest has been devoted to the shape evolution of neutron-rich Si isotopes recently both experimentally and theoretically. We provide our study of $^{28-42}$Si by Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with BCS approximation for the pairing channel. We use an empirical pairing parameter deduced from the experimental binding energy data for each nuclei and each Skyrme parametrization. The recent highlight of the deformation change from a prolate shape of $^{38}$Si to an oblate shape of $^{42}$Si is confirmed in the present model. We also emphasize the role of tensor force on deformations of neutron-rich $^{30,32}$Si, and discuss its underlying physics.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the deformation change from a prolate shape of \(^{38}\)Si to an oblate shape of \(^{42}\)Si using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with BCS pairing, but the key hedge is that the tensor force has a large effect on deformations only in \(^{30,32}\)Si, not in all neutron-rich Si isotopes, and that the resulting nuclear shapes are sensitive to the adopted pairing strengths.

## Method

The authors used the deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (DSHF) model with axially symmetric shape and BCS approximation for pairing correlations, incorporating a smooth energy-dependent cut-off weight to avoid the particle-gas problem. They employed five Skyrme parametrizations (T22, T24, T44, T64, T66) with varying tensor coupling strengths, and determined the pairing strength for each nucleus and each Skyrme force by reproducing empirical neutron pairing gaps extracted from experimental binding energies via the three-point mass difference formula.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strengths for all chosen effective forces average around 900 to 1000 MeV fm\(^3\), with variations up to 200 MeV fm\(^3\).
- For \(^{30}\)Si, T22 and T44 with larger pairing strengths (around 1000 MeV fm\(^3\)) fail to give deformed energy minima, while T24, T64, and T66 with smaller pairing strengths (around 800 MeV fm\(^3\)) give a large oblate deformation.
- All effective interactions overestimate binding energies by less than 0.7 MeV along the \(^{28-42}\)Si isotopic chain.
- Two-neutron separation energies S\(_{2n}\) remain above 3 MeV even in \(^{42}\)Si, justifying the BCS treatment.
- All parametrizations predict an oblate shape for \(^{28,40,42}\)Si, a spherical shape for \(^{34,36}\)Si, and a prolate shape for \(^{38}\)Si.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies by Werner et al. (1996) and Lalazissis et al. (1999) that predicted shape coexistence and the broken N=28 shell closure in Si isotopes, and by Tarpanov et al. (2008) who analyzed tensor effects on spin-orbit splitting in spherical symmetry. The paper directly confronts conflicting experimental evidence from MSU (2006) and GANIL (2007) on the structure of \(^{42}\)Si, and enables future measurements of quadrupole moments in \(^{39}\)Si and \(^{41}\)Si to further disentangle the prolate or oblate nature of \(^{40}\)Si.
