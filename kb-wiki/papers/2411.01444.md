# Radiative Capture of proton 14N(p,γ) 15O at Low Energy

> The CNO cycle is the main source of energy in stars more massive than our Sun. It defines the energy production and the duration contributes in determining the lifetime of massive stars. The cycle is an important tool for the determination of the age of globular clusters. Radiative capture p plus 14N 15O plus γ, at energies of astrophysical interest, is one of the important processes in the CNO cycle. In this project, we apply a potential model to describe both non resonant and resonant reactions in the channels where radiative capture occurs through electric E1 transitions. We employed the R matrix method to describe the reactions going via M1 resonant transitions, when it was not possible to correctly reproduce the experimental data by a potential model. The partial components of the astrophysical S factor are calculated for all possible electric and magnetic dipole transitions in 15O. The linear extrapolated S factor at zero energy (S(0)) is in good agreement with earlier reported values for all types of transitions considered in this work. Based on the value of the total astrophysical S factor, depending on the collision energy, we calculate the nuclear reaction rates for p plus 14N 15O plus γ. The computed rates are in good agreement with the results of the NACRE II Collaboration and the most recent existing measurements.

## Key claim
The total astrophysical S-factor at zero energy, S(0), is calculated to be 1.68641 keV·b, which is in good agreement with the most recent experimental and evaluated values, but this result depends on using a spectroscopic factor of unity for the potential model calculations, a choice that differs from some prior works which used smaller, renormalized values.

## Method
The authors employed a potential model to calculate the astrophysical S-factor for electric dipole (E1) transitions, both resonant and direct capture, using Woods-Saxon potentials fitted to reproduce the binding energies and resonance properties of 15O. For magnetic dipole (M1) resonant transitions, where the potential model could not reproduce experimental data, the R-matrix method was used with parameters from the literature. The total S-factor was computed as the sum of partial S-factors for all possible E1 and M1 transitions from the continuum to bound states in 15O below the proton separation threshold.

## Key numbers
- Total astrophysical S-factor at zero energy: S(0) = 1.68641 keV·b.
- Partial S(0) for the ground state transition (1/2⁻): 0.34849 keV·b.
- Partial S(0) for the transition to the third excited state (3/2⁻, Ex=6.1763 MeV): 0.35495 keV·b.
- Partial S(0) for the transition to the fourth excited state (3/2⁺, Ex=6.7931 MeV): 0.83419 keV·b.
- Partial S(0) for the transition to the first excited state (1/2⁺, Ex=5.183 MeV): 0.10405 keV·b.
- Partial S(0) for the transition to the second excited state (5/2⁺, Ex=5.2409 MeV): 0.00981 keV·b.
- The calculation considered two resonant states: Jπ=1/2⁺ at Ex=7.5565 MeV and Jπ=3/2⁺ at Ex=8.2840 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the long history of measurements and calculations of the 14N(p,γ)15O reaction, which is the slowest in the CNO cycle and critical for stellar evolution and neutrino production. The authors compare their results to prior R-matrix analyses by Imbriani (2005), Runkle (2005), and Frentz (2022), as well as potential model calculations by Xu (2013) and Huang (2010), noting that their use of a spectroscopic factor of unity differs from the renormalization approach in those works. The computed nuclear reaction rates, which agree with the NACRE II compilation, can be used in stellar models to improve predictions of energy generation and nucleosynthesis in massive stars.
