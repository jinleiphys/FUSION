# Feasibility of dark matter admixed neutron star based on recent observational constraints

> The equation of state (EOS) for neutron stars is modeled using the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach with a mesonic nonlinear (NL) interaction, a modified sigma cut potential (NL-$σ$ cut), and the influences of dark matter in the NL (NL DM). Using a Bayesian analysis framework, we evaluate the plausibility and impact of each scenario. Experimental constraints on the general properties of finite nuclei and heavy ion collisions, along with astrophysical observational data on neutron star radii and tidal deformation, have been taken into account. It was shown that all models, including the PREX-II data, were less favored, indicating that this experimental data seemed to be in tension with the other constraints included in the inference procedure, and were incompatible with chiral effective field theoretical calculations of pure neutron matter. Considering the models with no PREX-II constraints, we find the model NL-$σ$ cut with the largest Bayes evidence, indicating that the constraints considered favor the stiffening of the EOS at large densities. Conversely, the neutron star with a dark matter component is the least favorable case in light of recent observational constraints, among different scenarios considered here. The $f$ and $p$ modes were calculated within the Cowling approximation, and it can be seen that $f$ modes are sensitive to the EOS. An analysis of the slopes of the mass-radius curves and $f$-mode mass curves has indicated that these quantities may help distinguish the different scenarios.We also analyzed the impact of new PSR J0437-4715 measurements on neutron star mass-radius estimates, noting a $\sim$ 0.2 km reduction in the 90\% CI upper boundary across all models and a significant Bayes evidence decrease, indicating potential conflicts with previous data or the necessity for more adaptable models.

## Key claim

The paper finds that, among the three scenarios considered, the NL-sigma cut model (which stiffens the equation of state at high densities) has the largest Bayesian evidence when PREX-II data are excluded, while the neutron star with a dark matter component (NL-DM) is the least favorable case in light of recent observational constraints. The authors note that all models including PREX-II data were less favored, indicating tension between this experimental data and other constraints.

## Method

The authors modeled the neutron star equation of state using the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach with three variants: a nonlinear mesonic interaction (NL), a modified sigma cut potential (NL-sigma cut), and a dark matter admixed model (NL-DM) where fermionic dark matter is produced via a neutron decay channel. They employed Bayesian inference with nested sampling (PyMultiNest, 2000 live points, approximately 400,000 likelihood evaluations per case) incorporating constraints from finite nuclei properties, heavy ion collision data spanning densities from 0.03 to 0.32 fm^-3, mass-radius posteriors for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and tidal deformability from GW170817. Non-radial oscillation modes (f and p modes) were calculated within the Cowling approximation.

## Key numbers

- The bottle experiment neutron lifetime is tau_bottle = 879.6 +/- 0.6 s, and the beam experiment measurement gives tau_beam = 888.0 +/- 2.0 s, differing by 4 sigma
- The new PSR J0437-4715 measurements caused a ~0.2 km reduction in the 90% credible interval upper boundary for radius across all models
- The 90% credible interval for f-mode frequency in GW170817 ranges from 1.43 kHz to 2.90 kHz for the more massive neutron star and from 1.48 kHz to 3.18 kHz for the less massive one
- Nuclear stability requires 937.993 MeV < m_chi + m_phi < m_n = 939.565 MeV for the dark matter decay channel
- The heavy ion collision constraint at density 0.32 fm^-3 gives symmetric nuclear matter pressure P_SNM = 10.1 +/- 3.0 MeV/fm^3 (DLL) and 10.3 +/- 2.8 MeV/fm^3 (FOPI)
- The PREX-II constraint at density 0.11 fm^-3 gives symmetry energy pressure P_sym = 2.38 +/- 0.75 MeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of neutron star equations of state (Malik 2022, Dutra 2014) by systematically comparing three specific RMF scenarios against the same observational data set. The study builds on prior investigations of dark matter in neutron stars (Das 2020, 2021; Ellis 2018; Karkevandi 2021) and sigma-cut potential effects (Maslov 2015, Ma 2022). The results enable future work to prioritize the NL-sigma cut scenario for further study and suggest that dark matter admixed neutron stars are disfavored by current data, while also highlighting the tension between PREX-II results and other nuclear constraints that was noted in earlier work (Reed 2021).
