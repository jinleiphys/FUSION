# Probing new physics in the Neutrinoless double beta decay using electron angular correlation

> The angular correlation of the electrons emitted in the neutrinoless double beta decay ($0\nu2β$) is presented using a general Lorentz invariant effective Lagrangian for the leptonic and hadronic charged weak currents. We show that the coefficient $K$ in the angular correlation $dΓ/d\cos θ\propto (1-K\cos θ)$ is essentially independent of the nuclear matrix element models and present its numerical values for the five nuclei of interest ($^{76}{Ge}$, $^{82}{Se}$, $^{100}{Mo}$, $^{130}{Te}$, and $^{136}{Xe}$), assuming that the $0\nu2β$-decays in these nuclei are induced solely by a light Majorana neutrino, $ν_M$. This coefficient varies between $K=0.81$ (for the $^{76}{Ge}$ nucleus) and $K=0.88$ (for the $^{82}{Se}$ and $^{100}{Mo}$ nuclei), calculated taking into account the effects from the nucleon recoil, the $S$ and $P$-waves for the outgoing electrons and the electron mass. Deviation of $K$ from its values derived here would indicate the presence of New Physics (NP) in addition to a light Majorana neutrino, and we work out the angular coefficients in several $ν_M + {NP}$ scenarios for the $^{76}{Ge}$ nucleus. As an illustration of the correlations among the $0\nu2β$ observables (half-life $T_{1/2}$, the coefficient $K$, and the effective Majorana neutrino mass $|< m>|$) and the parameters of the underlying NP model, we analyze the left-right symmetric models, taking into account current phenomenological bounds on the right-handed $W_R$-boson mass and the left-right mixing parameter $ζ$.

## Key claim

The angular correlation coefficient \(K\) in the differential decay rate \(d\Gamma/d\cos\theta \propto (1-K\cos\theta)\) for neutrinoless double beta decay induced solely by a light Majorana neutrino is essentially independent of nuclear matrix element models, with values ranging from \(K=0.81\) for \({}^{76}\mathrm{Ge}\) to \(K=0.88\) for \({}^{82}\mathrm{Se}\) and \({}^{100}\mathrm{Mo}\); any deviation from these values would indicate the presence of new physics in addition to the light Majorana neutrino mechanism.

## Method

The authors derived the electron angular correlation for the long-range mechanism of \(0\nu2\beta\) decay using a general Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian for leptonic and hadronic charged weak currents, treating electrons relativistically and nucleons non-relativistically. They included the \(S_{1/2}\) and \(P_{1/2}\) partial waves for the outgoing electrons, nucleon recoil terms, and the electron mass, and computed the coefficient \(K = \mathcal{B}/\mathcal{A}\) from analytic expressions for \(\mathcal{A}\) and \(\mathcal{B}\) for various choices of nonstandard coefficients \(\epsilon_\alpha^\beta\). Numerical values were obtained for five nuclei using the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) nuclear matrix element models, and for the \({}^{76}\mathrm{Ge}\) nucleus the analysis was extended to scenarios combining a light Majorana neutrino with new physics contributions, including left-right symmetric models.

## Key numbers

* The angular correlation coefficient \(K\) for the light Majorana neutrino mechanism ranges from \(K=0.81\) (for \({}^{76}\mathrm{Ge}\)) to \(K=0.88\) (for \({}^{82}\mathrm{Se}\) and \({}^{100}\mathrm{Mo}\)).
* For \({}^{130}\mathrm{Te}\) and \({}^{136}\mathrm{Xe}\), the coefficient \(K\) takes values between these extremes (explicit numbers not given in the truncated text, but the range is stated as \(K=0.81\) to \(K=0.88\) across all five nuclei).
* The uncertainty in \(K\) due to nuclear matrix element models is modest, not exceeding 10% for the QRPA models discussed for \({}^{76}\mathrm{Ge}\).
* The conservative bound on the nonstandard coefficient \(\hat{\epsilon}_{V+A}^{V+A}\) from precision beta decay data is \(|\hat{\epsilon}_{V+A}^{V+A}| < 7 \times 10^{-2}\).
* The bound on \(|\epsilon_{V+A}^{V+A}|\) from the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment is \(|\epsilon_{V+A}^{V+A}| < 7.9 \times 10^{-7}\).
* From these bounds, the nonstandard mixing satisfies \(|U_{ei} V_{ei}| \lesssim 10^{-5}\).

## Context

This work extends the preliminary study by Ali, Borisov, and Zhuridov (2006) by including nucleon recoil and \(P\)-wave effects in the outgoing electron wave function, which were neglected in the earlier simplified treatment. The analytic expressions confirm the earlier detailed derivations by Doi et al. (1985) but go beyond them by providing numerical values for the angular correlation coefficient across five experimentally relevant nuclei and by analyzing the discriminating power of \(K\) among new physics scenarios such as left-right symmetric models and R-parity violating supersymmetry. The results enable future experiments to use electron angular correlation measurements to disentangle the underlying mechanism of neutrinoless double beta decay, complementing measurements of the half-life and effective Majorana neutrino mass.
