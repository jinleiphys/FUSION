# Resonance suppression of the r-mode instability in superfluid neutron stars: Accounting for muons and entrainment

> We calculate the finite-temperature r-mode spectrum of a superfluid neutron star accounting for both muons in the core and the entrainment between neutrons and protons. We show that the standard perturbation scheme, considering the rotation rate as an expansion parameter, breaks down in this case. We develop an original perturbation scheme which circumvents this problem by treating both the perturbations due to rotation and (weak) entrainment simultaneously. Applying this scheme, we propose a simple method for calculating the superfluid r-mode eigenfrequency in the limit of vanishing rotation rate. We also calculate the r-mode spectrum at finite rotation rate for realistic microphysics input (adopting, however, the Newtonian framework and Cowling approximation when considering perturbed oscillation equations) and show that the normal r-mode exhibits resonances with superfluid r-modes at certain values of temperatures and rotation frequencies in the parameter range relevant to neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs). This turns the recently suggested phenomenological model of resonance r-mode stabilization into a quantitative theory, capable of explaining observations. A strong dependence of resonance rotation rates and temperatures on the neutron superfluidity model allows us to constrain the latter by confronting our calculations with the observations of neutron stars in LMXBs.

## Key claim

The standard perturbation scheme treating rotation rate as an expansion parameter breaks down for calculating the r-mode spectrum of a superfluid neutron star when both muons in the core and entrainment between neutrons and protons are accounted for; the authors develop an alternative scheme that treats perturbations due to rotation and weak entrainment simultaneously, showing that the normal r-mode exhibits resonances with superfluid r-modes at temperatures and rotation frequencies relevant to neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs).

## Method

The authors adopt the Newtonian framework and Cowling approximation (neglecting metric perturbations) for the perturbed oscillation equations, and consider a neutron star core with npeμ composition (neutrons, protons, electrons, muons) allowing for superfluidity of baryons. They expand the oscillation equations in a power series in rotation frequency Ω and treat entrainment (the off-diagonal component Ynp of the entrainment matrix) as a small perturbing parameter, developing a perturbation scheme that accounts simultaneously for next-to-leading-order corrections in both rotation and entrainment. The resulting system of ordinary differential equations for the toroidal and radial displacement functions is solved with boundary conditions at the stellar center, core-crust interface, and stellar surface.

## Key numbers

- The normal r-mode eigenfrequency in the leading order in rotation is σ₀ = 2/(m+1) for a given azimuthal wavenumber m, independent of the entrainment.
- Superfluid r-modes in the absence of entrainment (Ynp = 0) have eigenfrequency σ₀ = 2m/[l(l+1)] when h₁(r)/h(r) is constant, reducing to σ₀ = 2/(m+1) for l = m.
- The resonance between normal and superfluid r-modes occurs at specific temperatures and rotation frequencies in the parameter range relevant to neutron stars in LMXBs, with the resonance rotation rates and temperatures showing strong dependence on the neutron superfluidity model.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological model of resonance r-mode stabilization proposed by Gusakov, Chugunov, and Kantor (2014a, 2014b) by providing a quantitative theory that accounts for both muons and entrainment in the neutron star core. It builds on previous calculations by Kantor and Gusakov (2017) that neglected entrainment, and by Dommes, Kantor, and Gusakov (2019) that accounted for entrainment but assumed only npe composition (no muons). The authors show that including both muons and entrainment together changes the spectrum qualitatively, and the strong sensitivity of resonance parameters to the neutron superfluidity model enables constraining the latter by comparing with observations of neutron stars in LMXBs.
