# Completeness of Coherent States Associated with Self-Similar Potentials and Ramanujan's Integral Extension of the Beta Function

> A decomposition of identity is given as a complex integral over the coherent states associated with a class of shape-invariant self-similar potentials. There is a remarkable connection between these coherent states and Ramanujan's integral extension of the beta function.

## Key claim

The paper provides a decomposition of the identity operator for q-analogue coherent states as an ordinary complex integral, rather than using q-integration, by exploiting shape-invariant algebraic techniques and Ramanujan's integral extension of the beta function; this completeness relation holds specifically for coherent states associated with self-similar potentials where the parameters scale as \(a_n = q^{n-1}a_1\) and the remainder is \(R(a_1)=ca_1\).

## Method

The authors start from the algebraic structure of shape-invariant potentials in supersymmetric quantum mechanics, where the operators \(\hat B_+\) and \(\hat B_-\) satisfy q-deformed oscillator commutation relations for self-similar potentials with scaling parameter \(q\). They define a generalized coherent state as an eigenstate of \(\hat B_-\) and specialize to the case where the functional \(f[R(a_n)] = R(a_n)\), yielding standard q-coherent states. The completeness relation is then proven by expressing the coherent states in the energy eigenbasis, performing the angular integration in the complex plane, and evaluating the remaining radial integral using Ramanujan's formula \(\int_0^{\infty} dt \, t^n / (-t;q)_{\infty} = (q;q)_n q^{-n(n+1)/2} (-\log q)\).

## Key numbers

- The scaling relation for self-similar potentials: \(a_n = q^{n-1}a_1\) (Eq. 3.1).
- The remainder for the simplest case: \(R(a_1) = c a_1\) where \(c\) is a constant (Eq. 3.2).
- Energy eigenvalues: \(E_n = R(a_1) (1-q^n)/(1-q)\) (Eq. 3.12).
- The completeness integral: \(I = \int (d\zeta d\zeta^* / 2\pi i) (1/(-\log q)) (1/(-\mid\zeta\mid^2;q)_{\infty}) \mid\zeta\rangle\langle\zeta\mid = \hat 1\) (Eq. 5.1).
- Ramanujan's integral: \(\int_0^{\infty} dt \, t^n / (-t;q)_{\infty} = (q;q)_n q^{-n(n+1)/2} (-\log q)\) (Eq. 5.3).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to shape-invariant potentials developed by Balantekin (1998) and the coherent state definitions for such systems by Fukui and Aizawa (1994) and Balantekin, Ribeiro, and Aleixo (1999). It contrasts with the earlier completeness relation for q-analogue coherent states by Gray and Nelson (1990), which used q-integration via Jackson's formula, by providing a resolution of identity as an ordinary integral over complex variables. This result enables the construction of coherent-state path integrals for q-analogue systems that can be evaluated using saddle-point approximations or Monte Carlo methods, and can be generalized to multi-dimensional q-oscillators and q-extensions of superalgebras.
