# Towards a Born term for hadrons

> We study bound states of abelian gauge theory in D=1+1 dimensions using an equal-time, Poincare-covariant framework. The normalization of the linear confining potential is determined by a boundary condition in the solution of Gauss' law for the instantaneous A^0 field. As in the case of the Dirac equation, the norm of the relativistic fermion-antifermion (f\bar{f}) wave functions gives inclusive particle densities. However, while the Dirac spectrum is known to be continuous, we find that regular f\bar{f} solutions exist only for discrete bound state masses. The f\bar{f} wave functions are consistent with the parton picture when the kinetic energy of the fermions is large compared to the binding potential. We verify that the electromagnetic form factors of the bound states are gauge invariant and calculate the parton distributions from the transition form factors in the Bjorken limit. For relativistic states we find a large sea contribution at low Bjorken x. Since the potential is independent of the gauge coupling the bound states may serve as "Born terms" in a perturbative expansion, in analogy to the usual plane wave in and out states.

## Key claim

The authors propose that bound states of an abelian gauge theory in 1+1 dimensions, defined using a linear confining potential derived from a homogeneous solution of Gauss' law, can serve as "Born terms" for a perturbative expansion of hadron properties, analogous to plane wave in and out states in standard perturbation theory. The key qualifier is that this framework works only for neutral (color singlet) states, since the linear potential is translation-invariant only for such states.

## Method

The authors solve the bound-state equation for a fermion-antifermion system in D=1+1 dimensions using an equal-time, Poincaré-covariant framework, where the linear confining potential V(x) = (1/2)|x| arises from a boundary condition on Gauss' law for the instantaneous A^0 field. They find analytic solutions for the 2×2 wave function Φ(x) in terms of confluent hypergeometric functions, and determine the wave function normalization by requiring duality between bound-state and fermion-loop contributions to current propagators. The electromagnetic form factors and parton distributions are then calculated from these wave functions in the Bjorken limit.

## Key numbers

- The linear potential is taken as V(x) = (1/2)|x|, with the coefficient set to unity as the energy scale (in units of Ze²)
- For the Dirac equation with linear potential, the ground state mass difference between solutions with different parameters (a/b ratio) decreases exponentially with m², with κ = π in the exponential factor exp(-κ m²)
- At large m (m ≳ 1), the Dirac eigenvalues become insensitive to the parameter ratio and merge with Schrödinger equation bound-state masses
- For relativistic states in D=1+1, the parton distribution at small Bjorken x behaves as x_Bj f(x_Bj) ∝ log²(x_Bj)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of Poincaré invariance in D=1+1 (Dietrich, Hoyer, Järvinen, 2012) and builds on earlier work by Hoyer (2009) on homogeneous solutions of Gauss' law in QCD. The approach contrasts with standard quark model treatments such as the Cornell potential (Eichten et al., 1978) by deriving the linear confining term from a boundary condition rather than postulating it. The bound states are analogous to solutions of the Dirac equation in a linear potential studied by Sauter (1931) and Plesset (1932), but unlike the continuous Dirac spectrum, the fermion-antifermion solutions exist only for discrete bound-state masses. The framework enables a perturbative expansion around zeroth-order bound states that resemble quark model states, with the S-matrix expanded around these Born terms rather than free fields.
