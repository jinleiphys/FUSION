# Sketching the Bethe-Salpeter kernel

> An exact form is presented for the axial-vector Bethe-Salpeter equation, which is valid when the quark-gluon vertex is fully dressed. A Ward-Takahashi identity for the Bethe-Salpeter kernel is derived therefrom and solved for a class of dressed quark-gluon vertex models. The solution provides a symmetry-preserving closed system of gap and vertex equations. The analysis can be extended to the vector equation. This enables a comparison between the responses of pseudoscalar- and scalar meson masses to nonperturbatively dressing the quark-gluon vertex. The result indicates that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking enhances spin-orbit splitting in the meson spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact Ward-Takahashi identity for the Bethe-Salpeter kernel that, when solved for a class of dressed quark-gluon vertex models, provides a symmetry-preserving closed system of gap and vertex equations; this enables the first exploration of how nonperturbative vertex dressing affects pseudoscalar- and scalar-meson masses, with the hedge that the analysis is illustrated using a Ball-Chiu vertex model rather than the true quark-gluon vertex.

## Method

The authors derive a Ward-Takahashi identity for the Bethe-Salpeter kernel from the axial-vector Bethe-Salpeter equation and solve it for a class of dressed quark-gluon vertex models satisfying a simplified identity. They then construct a symmetry-preserving Ansatz for the Bethe-Salpeter kernel consistent with the Ball-Chiu vertex model (Ball 1980) and solve the coupled gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations for ground-state pseudoscalar and scalar mesons composed of equal-mass u- and d-quarks, using a simplified effective interaction model (Maris 1997, Maris 1999, Maris 2002). Meson masses are obtained from inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations following the method of Bhagwat (2007).

## Key numbers

- For the Ball-Chiu vertex with D=1 GeV² and ω=0.5 GeV: A(0)=1.8 (dimensionless), M(0)=0.62 GeV, chiral-limit quark condensate -⟨q̄q⟩⁰^(1/3)=0.36 GeV, chiral-limit pion decay constant fπ⁰=0.16 GeV, pion mass mπ=0.13 GeV, scalar meson mass mσ=1.1 GeV.
- For rainbow-ladder truncation with D=1 GeV² and ω=0.5 GeV: A(0)=1.3, M(0)=0.40 GeV, -⟨q̄q⟩⁰^(1/3)=0.25 GeV, fπ⁰=0.091 GeV, mπ=0.14 GeV, mσ=0.64 GeV.
- The relative scalar meson mass deficit εσ^RL = [2M(0)-mσ]/[2M(0)] = 0.3±0.1 in rainbow-ladder truncation, while εσ^BC ≲ 0.1 for the Ball-Chiu-consistent kernel.
- The interaction strength satisfies ωD = (0.8 GeV)³ for ω∈[0.3,0.5] GeV.

## Context

This work extends the systematic Dyson-Schwinger equation truncation scheme of Munczek (1994) and Bender (1996) by providing a method to construct symmetry-preserving Bethe-Salpeter kernels for dressed quark-gluon vertices whose diagrammatic content cannot be specified. It addresses a longstanding shortcoming of the rainbow-ladder truncation noted by Maris (2006) and Cloët (2007), namely that the vector-axial-vector meson splitting is too small. The results indicate that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking enhances spin-orbit splitting in the meson spectrum, consistent with earlier separable Ansatz studies by Burden (1996) and Bloch (1999). This enables future calculations of mesons with masses above 1 GeV, particularly vector and axial-vector states.
