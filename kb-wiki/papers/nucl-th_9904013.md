# Excitation function of nucleon and pion elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Within a relativistic transport (ART) model for heavy-ion collisions, we show that the recently observed characteristic change from out-of-plane to in-plane elliptic flow of protons in mid-central Au+Au collisions as the incident energy increases is consistent with the calculated results using a stiff nuclear equation of state (K=380 MeV). We have also studied the elliptic flow of pions and the transverse momentum dependence of both the nucleon and pion elliptic flow in order to gain further insight about the collision dynamics.

# Excitation function of nucleon and pion elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The observed transition from out-of-plane to in-plane elliptic flow of protons in mid-central Au+Au collisions as beam energy increases is consistent with a stiff nuclear equation of state (K=380 MeV) in the ART model, without requiring a softening of the equation of state at high densities as suggested by earlier BEM model calculations.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic transport (ART) model to simulate mid-central Au+Au collisions at AGS energies, calculating the elliptic flow parameter v2 for both protons and pions as a function of beam energy and transverse momentum. They compared results using a stiff equation of state (K=380 MeV), a soft equation of state (K=210 MeV), and a cascade (no potential) calculation against experimental data from the EOS, E895, and E877 collaborations.

## Key numbers
- The transition energy E_tr for proton elliptic flow is more than 4 GeV/A using a stiff EOS, but decreases to below 3 GeV/A for a soft EOS
- The beam energy at which proton elliptic flow changes sign experimentally is about 4 GeV/A
- Calculations used an impact parameter of 5 fm for mid-central Au+Au collisions
- The stiff EOS has incompressibility K=380 MeV; the soft EOS has K=210 MeV
- At beam energies above about 6 GeV/A, the v2 value becomes insensitive to the nuclear EOS in the ART model
- Pion elliptic flow shows a transition energy and magnitude significantly smaller than those for nucleons
- Transverse momentum dependence shown at a beam momentum of 6 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends earlier predictions by Ollitrault (1992) that nucleons would change from out-of-plane to in-plane elliptic flow with increasing beam energy, and addresses experimental observations from the EOS, E895, and E877 collaborations. The results contradict the earlier BEM model study by Pin (1999) which suggested the equation of state softens at high densities, but are consistent with the UrQMD model. The authors provide predictions for pion elliptic flow excitation functions and transverse momentum dependence to help discriminate between different transport model predictions and better understand the collision dynamics.
