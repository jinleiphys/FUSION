# Heavy quark diffusion coefficient with gradient flow

> We calculate chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic correlators in quenched QCD at $1.5T_c$ and $10^4 T_c$ with the aim to estimate the heavy quark diffusion coefficient at leading order in the inverse heavy quark mass expansion, $κ_E$, as well as the coefficient of first mass suppressed correction, $κ_B$. We use gradient flow for noise reduction. At $1.5T_c$ we obtain: $1.70 \le κ_E/T^3 \le 3.12$ and $1.03< κ_B/T^3 < 2.61$. The latter implies that the mass suppressed effects in the heavy quark diffusion coefficient are 20% for bottom quarks and 34% for charm quark at this temperature.

## Key claim
The heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient at leading order in the inverse mass expansion, κ_E, and the first mass-suppressed correction, κ_B, are extracted from lattice chromoelectric and chromomagnetic correlators using gradient flow for noise reduction, with the results for κ_B implying mass-suppressed effects of 20% for bottom quarks and 34% for charm quarks at 1.5 T_c, though the quoted ranges for both coefficients are intervals rather than precise values.

## Method
The authors performed quenched SU(3) lattice QCD simulations at temperatures 1.5 T_c and 10^4 T_c using the standard Wilson gauge action, with temporal lattice sizes N_t = 20, 24, 28, and 34. They measured chromoelectric and chromomagnetic Euclidean correlators using gradient flow for noise reduction, performed continuum extrapolations linear in 1/N_t^2 at fixed flow-time ratios, and extracted κ_E and κ_B by modeling the spectral functions with a family of Ansätze that interpolate between the infrared form ρ(ω) = ωκ/(2T) and the leading-order perturbative ultraviolet form, using the same ω_IR = 0.01 T and ω_UV = 2.2 T as in Brambilla (2020).

## Key numbers
- At 1.5 T_c: 1.70 ≤ κ_E/T^3 ≤ 3.12
- At 1.5 T_c: 1.03 < κ_B/T^3 < 2.61
- Mass-suppressed effects in the heavy quark diffusion coefficient: 20% for bottom quarks and 34% for charm quarks at 1.5 T_c
- The ratio of chromomagnetic to chromoelectric correlators differs by less than 5% toward large separations τT

## Context
This work extends the approach of Brambilla (2020) and Francis (2015) for the chromoelectric correlator using the multilevel algorithm, now applying gradient flow for noise reduction as in Altenkort (2020). It provides the first lattice determination of the mass-suppressed correction κ_B from the chromomagnetic correlator, which was previously studied theoretically by Bouttefeux (2020) and Laine (2021) and measured concurrently by Banerjee (2022) using the multilevel algorithm. The results enable improved modeling of heavy quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma and enter evolution equations for heavy quarkonium out-of-equilibrium dynamics as in Brambilla (2017, 2019).
