# Dynamical constraints on some orbital and physical properties of the WD0137-349 A/B binary system

> In this paper I deal with the WD0137-349 binary system consisting of a white dwarf (WD) and a brown dwarf (BD) in a close circular orbit of about 116 min. I, first, constrain the admissible range of values for the inclination i by noting that, from looking for deviations from the third Kepler law, the quadrupole mass moment Q would assume unlikely large values, incompatible with zero at more than 1-sigma level for i < 35 deg and i > 43 deg. Then, by conservatively assuming that the most likely values for i are those that prevent such an anomalous behavior of Q, i.e. those for which the third Kepler law is an adequate modeling of the orbital period, I obtain i=39 +/- 2 deg. Such a result is incompatible with the value i=35 deg quoted in literature by more than 2 sigma. Conversely, it is shown that the white dwarf's mass range obtained from spectroscopic measurements is compatible with my experimental range, but not for i=35 deg. As a consequence, my estimate of $i$ yields an orbital separation of a=(0.59 +/- 0.05)R_Sun and an equilibrium temperature of BD of T_eq=(2087 +/- 154)K which differ by 10% and 4%, respectively, from the corresponding values for i=35 deg.

## Key claim

By using the quadrupole mass moment of the white dwarf as a diagnostic, the inclination of the WD0137-349 binary system is constrained to \(i = 39 \pm 2\) deg, under the conservative assumption that the third Kepler law must adequately model the orbital period; this result is incompatible at more than 2-sigma with the previously quoted value of \(i = 35\) deg.

## Method

The author compared the phenomenologically measured orbital period \(P_{\rm b} = 0.083 \pm 0.0002\) d to the Keplerian period \(P^{(0)}\) computed from the white dwarf mass \(M\) (obtained spectroscopically from effective temperature and surface gravity), the brown dwarf mass \(m\) (derived from the mass ratio \(\gamma = m/M = 0.134 \pm 0.006\) from radial-velocity semiamplitudes), and the orbital separation \(a\) expressed as a function of the unknown inclination \(i\). The quadrupole mass moment \(Q\) required to explain any discrepancy between \(P_{\rm b}\) and \(P^{(0)}\) was evaluated as a function of \(i\); values of \(i\) that forced \(Q\) to be large and physically implausible (\(|Q/MR^2| \approx 150\)–\(200\)) were rejected. The inclination was then solved directly by setting \(P_{\rm b} = P^{(0)}\) and using the measured radial-velocity data.

## Key numbers

* Inclination: \(i = 39 \pm 2\) deg, incompatible with the literature value of \(i = 35\) deg at more than 2-sigma.
* Orbital separation: \(a = (0.59 \pm 0.05) R_{\odot}\), differing by 10% from the value for \(i = 35\) deg.
* Equilibrium temperature of the brown dwarf: \(T_{\rm eq} = (2087 \pm 154)\) K, differing by 4% from the value for \(i = 35\) deg.
* White dwarf mass (spectroscopic): \(M = (0.39 \pm 0.035) M_{\odot}\).
* Brown dwarf mass: \(m = (0.052 \pm 0.007) M_{\odot}\).
* Orbital period: \(P_{\rm b} = 0.083 \pm 0.0002\) d (approximately 116 min).
* White dwarf radius: \(R = (0.0186 \pm 0.0012) R_{\odot}\).
* For \(i < 35\) deg or \(i > 43\) deg, the quadrupole moment \(Q\) becomes incompatible with zero at more than 1-sigma, with \(|Q/MR^2|\) reaching 150–200.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Maxted et al. (2006), who reported \(i = 35\) deg for the WD0137-349 system, and corrects that value using dynamical constraints from the quadrupole mass moment. The author also refines an earlier determination of \(Q\) by Iorio (2007), which had yielded an implausibly large value when using the incorrect inclination. The corrected inclination and orbital separation enable more reliable modeling of brown dwarf heating (Burleigh et al. 2006) and of the system’s evolutionary history, including the common-envelope phase that shrank the orbit.
