# Delineating the properties of neutron star matter in cold, dense QCD

> The properties of dense QCD matter are delineated through the construction of equations of state which should be consistent with the low and high density limits of QCD, nuclear laboratory experiments, and the neutron star observations. These constraints, together with the causality condition of the sound velocity, are used to develop the picture of hadron-quark continuity in which hadronic matter continuously transforms into quark matter (modulo small 1st order phase transitions). The resultant unified equation of state at zero temperature and $β$-equilibrium, which we call Quark-Hadron-Crossover (QHC19), is consistent with the measured properties of neutron stars as well as the microphysics known for the hadron phenomenology. In particular to $\sim 10n_0$ ($n_0$: saturation density) the gluons remain as non-perturbative as in vacuum and the strangeness can be as abundant as up- and down-quarks at the core of two-solar mass neutron stars. Within our modeling the maximum mass is found less than $\simeq 2.35$ times solar mass and the baryon density at the core ranges in $\sim 5$-8$n_0$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that neutron star matter at densities up to ~10 times nuclear saturation density is best described by a smooth "hadron-quark continuity" without sharp phase transitions, rather than by a first-order hadron-quark phase transition, based on the requirement that the resulting equation of state simultaneously satisfies observational constraints from two-solar-mass neutron stars, gravitational wave events, and the causality condition on sound velocity.

## Method

The author constructs a unified equation of state (EoS) called Quark-Hadron-Crossover (QHC19) using a "3-window" modeling approach: a nuclear EoS (Togashi EoS) is used up to 2 times nuclear saturation density (n0), a quark matter EoS based on a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type model with vector repulsion and diquark pairing is used above 5n0, and polynomial interpolation connects the two regimes between 2n0 and 5n0. The quark model parameters (vector coupling g_V and diquark coupling H) are varied to determine which combinations yield a causal sound speed and satisfy neutron star mass and radius constraints from observations including PSR J0740+6620 and GW170817.

## Key numbers

* Maximum neutron star mass is found less than ~2.35 solar masses within the modeling.
* Baryon density at the core of two-solar-mass neutron stars ranges in ~5-8 times nuclear saturation density (n0 = 0.16 fm^-3).
* The speed of sound squared (c_s^2) must exceed the conformal limit of 1/3 in the 2-5n0 density range to connect soft low-density EoS to stiff high-density EoS, while remaining below the causality limit of 1.
* The observed lower bound for maximum neutron star mass is 2.14 ± 0.10 solar masses (68.3% confidence) from PSR J0740+6620.
* The GW170817 event with total mass 2.73-2.78 solar masses implies maximum mass constraints of 1.82-2.32 solar masses under the hypermassive neutron star scenario.
* Radius constraints from GW170817 give R_1.4 = 11.9 ± 1.4 km (90% confidence) and lower bound R_1.4 ≳ 11.0-11.5 km from ejecta considerations.

## Context

This work extends the quark-hadron continuity picture originally discussed by Schafer and Wilczek (1999) in the context of color-flavor-locked superconductivity, and applies it to construct EoS consistent with modern neutron star observations. The 3-window modeling approach differs from hybrid constructions with first-order phase transitions (e.g., those using direct comparison of pure hadronic and pure quark EoS) by avoiding extrapolation of either description into density regimes where they are unreliable. The resulting QHC19 EoS enables future studies of neutron star structure, cooling, and merger dynamics, and provides a framework for lattice QCD calculations to constrain the nonperturbative gluon dynamics at densities up to ~10n0.
