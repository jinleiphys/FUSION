# Gamow-Teller strength in $^{48}$Ca and $^{78}$Ni with the charge-exchange subtracted second random-phase approximation

> We develop a fully self-consistent subtracted second random-phase approximation for charge-exchange processes with Skyrme energy-density functionals. As a first application, we study Gamow-Teller excitations in the doubly-magic nucleus $^{48}$Ca, the lightest double-$β$ emitter that could be used in an experiment, and in $^{78}$Ni, the single-beta-decay rate of which is known. The amount of Gamow-Teller strength below 20 or 30 MeV is considerably smaller than in other energy-density-functional calculations and agrees better with experiment in $^{48}$Ca, as does the beta-decay rate in $^{78}$Ni. These important results, obtained without \textit{ad hoc} quenching factors, are due to the presence of two-particle -- two-hole configurations. Their density progressively increases with excitation energy, leading to a long high-energy tail in the spectrum, a fact that may have implications for the computation of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-$β$ decay in the same framework.

## Key claim

The charge-exchange subtracted second random-phase approximation (SSRPA), developed here for the first time, reproduces Gamow-Teller strength distributions in $^{48}$Ca and the beta-decay half-life of $^{78}$Ni in better agreement with experiment than other energy-density-functional calculations, without requiring *ad hoc* quenching factors, because the explicit inclusion of two-particle–two-hole configurations generates a high-energy tail that draws strength away from lower excitation energies.

## Method

The authors constructed a fully self-consistent subtracted second random-phase approximation for charge-exchange processes using Skyrme energy-density functionals (parameterization SGII, and also SkM$^*$ and SkMP for comparison). The method includes two-particle–two-hole (2p2h) configurations explicitly, with a cutoff at 40 MeV for the 2p2h space, and applies a subtraction procedure that makes the response consistent with ground-state density-functional theory at zero frequency. They computed Gamow-Teller strength distributions for $^{48}$Ca and $^{78}$Ni, folding the theoretical responses with a Lorentzian of width 1 MeV for comparison with experimental data from Yako (2009) and Hosmer (2005).

## Key numbers

- In $^{48}$Ca, the ratio of experimental to theoretical integrated GT$^-$ strength below 20 MeV is 0.58 for RPA and 0.93 for SSRPA, showing that quenching factors are not needed in SSRPA.
- The integrated GT$^-$ strength in $^{48}$Ca up to 70 MeV is 23.84, and the integrated GT$^+$ strength at that energy is 0.10, reproducing the Ikeda sum rule value of 24 to within about 1%.
- The experimental total GT$^-$ strength below 30 MeV in $^{48}$Ca is 64 $\pm$ 9% of the Ikeda sum rule value.
- For $^{78}$Ni, the SSRPA beta-decay half-life is 0.19 seconds with the bare axial-vector coupling constant $g_A = 1.28$, compared to the experimental half-life; the RPA half-life with the same bare $g_A$ is 9.51 seconds.
- In the particle-vibration-coupling calculations of Niu (2015) with the same Skyrme interaction SGII, the integrated strength below 20 MeV in $^{48}$Ca is more than 20 units, and the ratio to experiment is $\le$ 0.68.

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field energy-density-functional approaches to charge-exchange excitations, improving upon earlier RPA calculations (Cao, 2014) that overestimated GT strength significantly and required quenching factors. It also improves upon particle-vibration-coupling models (Niu, 2015; Robin, 2016) that showed only minor improvement for $^{48}$Ca. The SSRPA framework enables more reliable EDF-based calculations of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay, and the authors plan to extend the approach to open-shell nuclei by including pairing correlations.
