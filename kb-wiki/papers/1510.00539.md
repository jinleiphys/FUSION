# Chemically non-equilibrated QGP and thermal photon elliptic flow

> It has been discovered in recent heavy-ion experiments that elliptic and triangular flow of direct photons are underpredicted by most hydrodynamic models. I discuss possible enhancement mechanisms based on late chemical equilibration of the QGP and in-medium modification of parton distributions. Numerical hydrodynamic analyses indicate that they suppress early photon emission and visibly enhance thermal photon elliptic flow.

## Key claim
Late chemical equilibration of the QGP and in-medium modification of parton distributions can visibly enhance thermal photon elliptic flow, offering a possible resolution to the "photon puzzle" where hydrodynamic models underpredict direct photon flow harmonics.

## Method
The author performs (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations with a Monte-Carlo Glauber initial condition for 200 GeV Au-Au collisions at impact parameter b = 6 fm. Chemical equilibration is modeled via rate equations for quark and gluon number densities with parametrized reaction rates, while in-medium parton distributions are implemented through a quasi-particle model that reproduces lattice QCD thermodynamics. Thermal photon emission rates are calculated using effective fugacities that incorporate both chemical non-equilibrium and in-medium corrections, with the QGP and hadronic rates smoothly interpolated at the crossover temperature.

## Key numbers
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c
- Crossover temperature between QGP and hadronic phases: T_c = 0.17 GeV
- Final temperature cutoff for thermal photon emission: T_f = 0.13 GeV
- Impact parameter for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV: b = 6 fm
- Chemical relaxation times for different reaction rate parameters: τ_chem ∼ 5, 2, and 0.5 fm/c for c_b = 0.2, 0.5, and 2.0 respectively, at average medium temperature 0.2 GeV
- Reaction rate parameters used: c_a = c_c = 1.5, with c_b = 0.2, 0.5, and 2.0

## Context
This work addresses the "photon puzzle" identified in experimental data from the PHENIX Collaboration (Adare 2011) and ALICE Collaboration (Lohner 2012, Mizuno 2014), where direct photon elliptic and triangular flow are as large as hadronic flow but underpredicted by standard hydrodynamic models such as those by Schenke (2010). The study extends prior work by Monnai (2014) on chemical equilibration and Monnai (2015) on in-medium parton distributions, and enables more quantitative comparisons with experimental direct photon data, with future prospects including prompt photon contributions and higher-order flow harmonics.
