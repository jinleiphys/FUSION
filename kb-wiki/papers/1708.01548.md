# Hydrodynamic tails and a fluctuation bound on the bulk viscosity

> We study the small frequency behavior of the bulk viscosity spectral function using stochastic fluid dynamics. We obtain a number of model independent results, including the long-time tail of the bulk stress correlation function, and the leading non-analyticity of the spectral function at small frequency. We also establish a lower bound on the bulk viscosity which is weakly dependent on assumptions regarding the range of applicability of fluid dynamics. The bound on the bulk viscosity $ζ$ scales as $ζ_{\it min} \sim (P-\frac{2}{3}{\cal E})^2 \sum_i D_i^{-2}$, where $D_i$ are the diffusion constants for energy and momentum, and $P-\frac{2}{3}{\cal E}$, where $P$ is the pressure and ${\cal E}$ is the energy density, is a measure of scale breaking. Applied to the cold Fermi gas near unitarity, $|λ/a_s|\geq 1$ where $λ$ is the thermal de Broglie wave length and $a_s$ is the $s$-wave scattering length, this bound implies that the ratio of bulk viscosity to entropy density satisfies $ζ/s \geq 0.1\hbar/k_B$. Here, $\hbar$ is Planck's constant and $k_B$ is Boltzmann's constant.

## Key claim

The paper derives a lower bound on the bulk viscosity that depends only on the shear viscosity, thermal conductivity, and a measure of conformal symmetry breaking in the equation of state; the bound is weakly dependent on assumptions about the range of applicability of fluid dynamics, and the authors note that the bound is similar to lower bounds on shear viscosity derived in prior work.

## Method

The authors used stochastic fluid dynamics to compute the retarded correlation function of the bulk stress operator \( \mathcal{O} = P - \frac{2}{3}\mathcal{E} \) (the trace anomaly) to second order in hydrodynamic variables. They evaluated one-loop diagrams involving diffusive heat modes and propagating sound modes, regularized the ultraviolet-divergent momentum integrals with a cutoff \(\Lambda\), and extracted both the non-analytic \(\omega^{3/2}\) tail and the cutoff-dependent contribution to the bulk viscosity. The bound follows from requiring that the bare viscosity at the cutoff scale be positive and that the physical viscosity exceed the fluctuation-induced contribution.

## Key numbers

* The minimum bulk viscosity scales as \(\zeta_{\rm min} \sim (P-\frac{2}{3}{\cal E})^2 \sum_i D_i^{-2}\), where \(D_i\) are the diffusion constants for energy and momentum.
* For the cold Fermi gas near unitarity (\(|\lambda/a_s| \geq 1\)), the bound implies \(\zeta/s \geq 0.1\,\hbar/k_B\).
* The long-time tail of the bulk stress correlation function decays as \(C_\zeta(t) \propto t^{-3/2}\).
* The frequency-dependent bulk viscosity near zero frequency behaves as \(\zeta(\omega) = \zeta(0) - \left( \frac{A_T}{(2D_T)^{3/2}} + \frac{A_\Gamma}{\Gamma^{3/2}}\right) \frac{\sqrt{\omega}}{36\sqrt{2}\pi}\).
* The high-frequency tail of the bulk viscosity spectral function is \(\zeta(\omega) = \frac{{\cal C}}{36\pi\sqrt{m\omega}} \frac{1}{1+a_s^2 m\omega}\), where \({\cal C}\) is the contact density.

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic tails in non-relativistic fluids, correcting an earlier calculation by Pomeau and Résibois (1975) that did not vanish for scale-invariant fluids. It also generalizes the analysis of critical fluctuations near the liquid-gas endpoint by Onuki (2002) and the relativistic calculation by Kovtun and Yaffe (2003) to non-relativistic fluids with non-zero density. The fluctuation bound on bulk viscosity is analogous to lower bounds on shear viscosity derived by Kovtun, Moore, and Romatschke (2011), Chafin and Schäfer (2012), and Romatschke (2012). The results enable model-independent constraints on the bulk viscosity spectral function of dilute quantum fluids such as the unitary Fermi gas, and can be combined with sum rules and operator product expansion results to construct a complete spectral function.
