# Sum rules and short-range correlations in nuclear matter at finite temperature

> The nucleon spectral function in nuclear matter fulfills an energy weighted sum rule. Comparing two different realistic potential, these sum rules are studied for Green's functions that are derived self-consistently within the $T$ matrix approximation at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The energy-weighted sum rules for the nucleon spectral function in nuclear matter are automatically fulfilled within the self-consistent T-matrix approximation at finite temperature, but their detailed saturation behavior provides a quantitative measure of the strength of short-range and tensor correlations produced by different realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Green's function calculations in the ladder (T-matrix) approximation for nuclear matter at finite temperature, using the complete spectral function to describe intermediate states in the Galitskii-Feynman equation. They computed the spectral function for two realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, the Argonne V18 and the CDBONN, at a density of 0.2 fm^-3 and a temperature of 10 MeV, and analyzed the first two energy-weighted sum rules (m_0 and m_1) by comparing the left-hand side (integral over the spectral function) with the right-hand side (kinetic energy plus the high-energy limit of the self-energy).

## Key numbers

- The m_0 sum rule is fulfilled better than 0.1% over the complete momentum range.
- The m_1 sum rule is satisfied better than 1% for both potentials at T=10 MeV and density 0.2 fm^-3.
- The m_1 result for the CDBONN interaction is about 65 MeV more attractive than the Argonne V18 result, with the A^> contribution being almost 50 MeV more repulsive for Argonne V18.
- For the m_0 sum rule at momentum k=500 MeV, the Argonne V18 requires integrating almost twice as far in energy as the CDBONN to reach the same percentage of exhaustion.
- At an integration limit of 700-800 MeV, the m_1 sum rule is exhausted by about 75% for the CDBONN potential, while about 40% of the contribution still lies above this energy for the Argonne V18.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature sum rule analysis of Pol94 to finite temperature within the self-consistent Green's function framework, building on the finite-temperature T-matrix calculations of Bozek (2002) and Frick (2003). The results enable a quantitative comparison of correlation strengths between different realistic potentials without requiring full many-body calculations, as the Hartree-Fock estimate of m_1 provides a good approximation. The findings are relevant for interpreting nucleon knock-out experiments and for describing nuclear matter in astrophysical environments and heavy-ion collisions.
