# $ωN$ final state interactions and $ω$-meson production from heavy-ion collisions

> We calculate the elastic and inelastic $ωN\to ωN, \to πN, \to ρN, \to ρπN, \to ππN, \to σN$ reactions within a boson exchange approximation where the $ωρπ$ coupling constant and form factor are fixed by the reaction $πN \to ωN$ in comparison to the experimental data. We find rather large $ωN$ cross sections at low relative momenta of the $ω$-meson which leads to a substantial broadening of the $ω$-meson width in nuclear matter. The implications of the $ωN$ final state interactions are studied for $ω$ production in $^{12}C{+}^{12}C, ^{40}Ca{+}^{40}Ca$ and $^{58}Ni{+}^{58}Ni$ reactions at about 2 A GeV within the HSD transport approach; the drastic changes of the transverse mass spectra relative to a general m_T-scaling (for $π^0$ and $η$ mesons) might be controlled experimentally by the TAPS Collaboration.

# ωN final state interactions and ω-meson production from heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The ωN total cross section is large at low relative momenta (dominated by inelastic channels), leading to substantial collisional broadening of the ω-meson width in nuclear matter (about 50 MeV at rest, rising to 100 MeV above 1 GeV/c) and a strong suppression of ω-meson production in heavy-ion collisions; however, the ωσω coupling constant remains poorly constrained, and the results for the total cross section are reduced by about 25% when the σ-exchange diagrams are omitted.

## Method

The authors calculated elastic and inelastic ωN reaction channels (ωN → ωN, πN, ρN, ρπN, ππN, σN) within a boson-exchange approximation, using the ωρπ coupling constant and form factor fixed by fitting experimental data on the πN → ωN reaction. The resulting ωN cross sections were implemented in the HSD transport approach to simulate ω-meson production in ¹²C+¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, and ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni collisions at about 2 A GeV, and the transverse mass spectra were compared to the m_T-scaling observed for π⁰ and η mesons.

## Key numbers

- ωρπ coupling constant: g_{ωρπ} = 11.79, evaluated from the ω → 3π partial width (close to 10.88 from Klingl et al.)
- Cut-off parameter for ωρπ vertex form factor: Λ = 2.7 GeV, fixed from fit to πN → ωN data
- Elastic ωN cross section parameterization: σ_el = 5.4 + 10 exp(-0.6 p_ω) mb, where p_ω is the ω laboratory momentum in GeV/c
- Inelastic ωN cross section parameterization: σ_inel = 20 + 4.0/p_ω mb, with p_ω in GeV/c
- Collisional width at nuclear saturation density ρ₀: about 50 MeV at low ω momenta, increasing to 100 MeV above p_ω ≈ 1 GeV/c
- Inclusive ω production cross section in C+C at 2 A GeV: 1.42 mb with final state interactions, versus 6.52 mb without (reduction factor ≈ 4.5)
- Inclusive ω production cross section in Ca+Ca at 2 A GeV: 10.56 mb with FSI, versus 91.4 mb without
- Inclusive ω production cross section in Ni+Ni at 1.9 A GeV: 19.5 mb with FSI, versus 206.8 mb without

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector meson properties in nuclear matter by Klingl, Kaiser and Weise (1997) and Friman (1997), who calculated some ωN reaction channels but noted strong sensitivity to model parameters. The authors provide a more complete set of ωN channels within a boson-exchange framework and apply the results to heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies, where the TAPS Collaboration has measured π⁰ and η meson spectra showing m_T-scaling (Bratkovskaya et al., 1997). The calculated suppression of ω-meson production and the deviation from m_T-scaling, especially at low transverse mass, can be tested experimentally by TAPS via the ω → π⁰γ Dalitz decay.
