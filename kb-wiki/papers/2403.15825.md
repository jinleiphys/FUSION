# Generalized nonlinear Langevin equation from quantum nonlinear projection operator

> We systematically derive the quantum generalized nonlinear Langevin equation using Morozov's projection operator method. This approach extends the linear Mori-Zwanzig projection operator technique, allowing for the inclusion of nonlinear interactions among macroscopic modes. Additionally, we obtain the quantum generalized Fokker-Planck equation within the Heisenberg picture, which is consistent with Morozov's original formulation. These equations are fundamentally significant in non-equilibrium statistical physics, particularly in scenarios characterized by enhanced fluctuations, such as anomalous transport phenomena near critical points. The quantum nature of the derived generalized Langevin and Fokker-Planck equations is anticipated to provide a more detailed description than their classical equivalents. Specifically, the noise kernel in the quantum generalized Langevin equation is multiplicative, which broadens the applicability beyond Gaussian approximations. Given specific interactions, these equations are expected to be instrumental in investigating critical transport phenomena.

## Key claim

The paper systematically derives a quantum generalized nonlinear Langevin equation using Morozov's projection operator method, extending the linear Mori-Zwanzig technique to include nonlinear interactions among macroscopic modes; the derivation assumes the initial state is in local equilibrium, which eliminates the initial noise contribution and yields a closed-form generalized Fokker-Planck equation.

## Method

The authors apply Morozov's quantum nonlinear projection operator, which uses a Dirac delta operator (constructed via the Weyl correspondence rule) to project arbitrary operators onto the subspace spanned by relevant slow variables (e.g., conserved densities and order parameters). They employ the Dyson-Duhamel identity to decompose the time evolution operator, then derive both the generalized Fokker-Planck equation (in operator form and averaged form) and the generalized Langevin equation in the Heisenberg picture, with the noise kernel related to the diffusion kernel through a generalized fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Key numbers

- The paper contains no explicit numerical results; it is a formal theoretical derivation.

## Context

This work extends the linear Mori-Zwanzig projection operator formalism (Mori, 1965; Zwanzig, 1960) to the quantum nonlinear regime, building on Morozov's classical nonlinear projection operator method (Morozov, 1981, 1992). The derived quantum generalized Langevin and Fokker-Planck equations are intended for studying non-equilibrium statistical physics problems with enhanced fluctuations, particularly anomalous transport near critical points (e.g., the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions). The framework enables future investigation of critical transport phenomena with multiplicative noise kernels beyond Gaussian approximations, with an immediate application to relativistic stochastic hydrodynamics.
