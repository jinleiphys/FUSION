# Universal Mass Dependence for Particle Production Rates in $e^+e^-$, $pp$ and $AA$ Collisions from the Quark-Gluon Plasma Perspective

> It is demonstrated that universal mass dependence for meson and baryon inclusive cross-sections advocated recently in $e^+e^-$ annihilation and in hadronic reactions is consistent also with heavy ion collisions data and can therefore be used as a reference for quark-gluon plasma studies.

# Universal Mass Dependence for Particle Production Rates in \(e^+e^-\), \(pp\) and \(AA\) Collisions from the Quark-Gluon Plasma Perspective

## Key claim
The universal quadratic mass dependence (\(\exp(-b M^2)\)) for meson and baryon inclusive cross-sections, previously observed in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation and hadronic reactions, is consistent with existing heavy ion collision data; therefore, any deviation from this universality toward a linear mass dependence (\(\exp(-\beta M)\)) could serve as a signal for quark-gluon plasma formation, but the current data do not yet require thermal or QGP-based explanations.

## Method
The authors compiled inclusive particle production rates (or cross-sections) for mesons and baryons from \(e^+e^-\) annihilation at PEP/PETRA and LEP energies, from \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 27.4\) GeV, 27.6 GeV, and 53 GeV, and from \(S+S\) and \(S+Ag\) collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon. They plotted the weighted yields (using spin and modified isospin factors) against the square of the particle mass \(M^2\) and compared them to a universal exponential fit with slope \(b = 3.9\ (\text{GeV}/c^2)^{-2}\) derived from \(e^+e^-\) data. Only antibaryons and neutral or negative mesons were used in hadronic and nuclear collisions to avoid initial-state quantum-number biases.

## Key numbers
- Slope parameter \(b = 3.9\ (\text{GeV}/c^2)^{-2}\) from \(e^+e^-\) annihilation data at LEP energies, describing the universal \(\exp(-b M^2)\) dependence.
- For \(pp\) collisions: data at \(p_{\text{LAB}} = 400\) GeV/c (\(\sqrt{s} = 27.4\) GeV) and \(p_{\text{LAB}} = 405\) GeV/c (\(\sqrt{s} = 27.6\) GeV), and ISR data at \(\sqrt{s} = 53\) GeV.
- For heavy ion collisions: \(S+S\) and \(S+Ag\) at 200 GeV/nucleon; the ratio \(\bar{\Lambda}/\bar{p} = 1.5 \pm 0.5\) for \(S+S\) and \(0.8 \pm 0.25\) for \(S+Ag\) from central rapidity fits.
- Pion production rates lie significantly above the universal curve in all collision systems, attributed to decays of high-mass resonances.

## Context
This work extends the universal mass-dependence observation first reported in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation by the authors of the CU paper (1995) and confirmed in hadronic reactions by the SZ paper (1995). It contrasts the quadratic mass dependence (\(\exp(-b M^2)\)) expected from string fragmentation (tunneling, with \(b = \pi/\kappa\) measuring colour field strength) with the linear mass dependence (\(\exp(-\beta M)\) or \(\exp(-\beta \langle M_T \rangle)\)) expected from thermal or chemically equilibrated models (hadron gas or QGP). The paper enables future high-energy heavy-ion experiments (e.g., at RHIC or LHC) to test whether deviations from universality appear, which would indicate collective effects or QGP formation, or whether universality persists, favoring string-based models such as those by the authors of the S, W, ABP, and C papers.
