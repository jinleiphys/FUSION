# Behavior of the collective rotor in wobbling motion

> The behavior of the collective rotor in wobbling motion is investigated within the particle-rotor model for the nucleus $^{135}$Pr by transforming the wave functions from the $K$-representation to the $R$-representation. After reproducing the experimental energy spectra and wobbling frequencies, the evolution of the wobbling mode in $^{135}$Pr, from transverse at low spins to longitudinal at high spins, is illustrated by the distributions of the total angular momentum in the intrinsic reference frame (azimuthal plot). Finally, the coupling schemes of the angular momenta of the rotor and the high-$j$ particle for transverse and longitudinal wobbling are obtained from the analysis of the probability distributions of the rotor angular momentum ($R$-plots) and their projections onto the three principal axes ($K_R$-plots).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the collective rotor angular momentum in the wobbling bands of \(^{135}\)Pr can be analyzed via probability distributions in the \(R\)-representation, showing that the wobbling mode evolves from transverse at low spins to longitudinal at high spins, with the rotor angular momentum playing a key role in this transition.

## Method

The particle-rotor model (PRM) was solved for \(^{135}\)Pr using a proton in the \(\pi(1h_{11/2})^1\) configuration with deformation parameters \(\beta=0.17\) and \(\gamma=-26.0^\circ\) and principal moments of inertia \(\mathcal{J}_1, \mathcal{J}_2, \mathcal{J}_3 = 13.0, 21.0, 4.0~\hbar^2/\text{MeV}\). The wave functions were transformed from the standard \(K\)-representation (strong-coupling basis) to the \(R\)-representation (weak-coupling basis) using an orthogonal transformation based on Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, enabling the calculation of probability distributions for the rotor angular momentum \(R\) and its projections \(K_R\) onto the three principal axes. Azimuthal plots of the total angular momentum orientation were also computed from the PRM eigenfunctions.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameters: \(\beta = 0.17\) and \(\gamma = -26.0^\circ\) for the \(\pi(1h_{11/2})^1\) configuration in \(^{135}\)Pr.
- Principal moments of inertia: \(\mathcal{J}_1 = 13.0\), \(\mathcal{J}_2 = 21.0\), \(\mathcal{J}_3 = 4.0~\hbar^2/\text{MeV}\) for the 1- (short), 2- (intermediate), and 3- (long) axes, respectively.
- The wobbling frequency decreases with spin for \(I \leq 14.5\hbar\) (transverse wobbling) and increases for \(I \geq 14.5\hbar\) (longitudinal wobbling), matching experimental data from Matta (2015).
- For the yrast band, the critical spin for the rotational mode change is \(I = 13.5\hbar\), where the azimuthal plot maxima occur at \(\varphi \simeq \pm 5^\circ\).
- In the transverse wobbling region (\(I \leq 13.5\hbar\)), the component \(R_i\) increases by about \(2\hbar\) from an yrast state \(I\) to a wobbling state \(I+1\); in the longitudinal region (\(I \geq 14.5\hbar\)), the increment is about \(1\hbar\).
- For the yrast band at \(I = 5.5\hbar\) (bandhead), the maximal weight in the \(R\)-plot occurs at \(R = |I-j| + 2 = 2\hbar\); for all other yrast states (\(I \geq 7.5\hbar\)), the peak is at \(R_{\text{min}} = |I-j|\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of wobbling motion in \(^{135}\)Pr using the particle-rotor model and tilted axis cranking (TAC) by Frauendorf and Donau (2014) and Matta (2015), as well as the collective Hamiltonian method of Chen (2016). It provides the first detailed analysis of the rotor angular momentum wave function structure in wobbling bands, complementing earlier work by Smith (1976) on axially symmetric nuclei. The results enable future studies of the microscopic coupling between the rotor and high-\(j\) particles in triaxial nuclei, and the method can be applied to other wobbling candidates such as \(^{133}\)La and \(^{161}\)Lu.
