# Mechanical properties of the nucleon in the chiral confining model. I -- formal developments

> We discuss the issue of the mechanical stability of the nucleon within a class of models in which massive constituent quarks are subject to a confining potential and are coupled to a surrounding pion cloud enveloping the quark core. The nucleon trial states (either localized factorized wave functions or momentum-projected states) are determined by imposing the von Laue stability condition. This article is primarily devoted to the formal aspects related to the detailed expressions for the total energy (mass), the average pressure, the energy density and the pressure distribution inside the nucleon. It will be accompanied by a complementary article addressing the evolution of nucleon properties with density, associated with the restoration of chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper develops the formal framework for computing the mechanical properties (energy density, pressure distribution, and stability) of the nucleon within a class of chiral confining models, but the actual numerical results and density evolution are deferred to a companion paper; the present work focuses on establishing the expressions for the total energy, average pressure, and the von Laue stability condition for trial nucleon states that are either localized factorized wave functions or momentum-projected states.

## Method

The authors start from a Lagrangian describing massive constituent quarks coupled to a confining potential (inspired by the field correlator method) and to a pion cloud via pseudo-vector coupling, with the finite size of the pion incorporated through a Gaussian spreading function. They construct two types of trial nucleon states: a localized product-state wave function with fixed center-of-mass position and a momentum-projected state with well-defined three-momentum, using a Gaussian single-quark Fock-space amplitude. The local energy-momentum tensor is derived in the presence of the non-local pion-quark coupling, and the von Laue stability condition is imposed to determine the nucleon trial states, with the pressure distribution obtained from the spatial components of the EMT.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass: \(M_q = 356.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\); pion mass: \(M_\pi = 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\); pion decay constant: \(F_\pi = 91.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (NJLSet1 parameter set).
- Depth of the confining potential pocket: \(V_0 \sim \sigma T_g \sim 250\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with the gluon correlation length \(T_g \sim 0.3\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Experimentally measured isoscalar radius of the nucleon: \(\sqrt{\langle r^2_S\rangle} \simeq 0.77\ \mathrm{fm}\); resulting quark core baryon number radius: \(\sqrt{\langle r^2_B\rangle} = 0.46\ \mathrm{fm}\) after subtracting the \(\omega\) meson contribution (\(m_\omega\)).
- Pion electromagnetic radius: \(\sqrt{\langle r^2_\pi\rangle} = 0.65\ \mathrm{fm}\); NJL model prediction: \(\langle r^2_\pi\rangle_{NJL} = 0.34\ \mathrm{fm}^2 \simeq (0.6\ \mathrm{fm})^2\).
- Gaussian spreading parameter for finite pion size: \(\rho^2_\pi = c_\pi / (4\pi^2 F_\pi^2) = c_\pi \times 0.34\ \mathrm{fm}^2\), with \(c_\pi\) an adjustable parameter expected near unity.

## Context

This work extends the chiral confining model (CCM) previously developed by Chanfray and collaborators (Universe 2024, Universe 2025) by providing the formal machinery to compute mechanical properties of the nucleon, including the energy-momentum tensor and pressure distributions. It addresses the instability problem of chiral bag models identified by Brown and Pirner, where the attractive pion-quark interaction can cause collapse of the quark core when the core size becomes too small. The paper enables future calculations of nucleon properties at finite density and the study of chiral symmetry restoration, to be presented in a companion article (Paper II). The approach connects to experimental observables through generalized parton distributions and gravitational form factors accessible in deeply virtual Compton scattering and meson production.
