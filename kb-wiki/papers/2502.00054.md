# $NJ/ψ$ and $Nη_c$ interactions from lattice QCD

> The interaction between nucleon and charmonia ($J/ψ$ and $η_c$) is expected to deepen our understanding of various aspects in nonperturbative QCD ranging from the origin of nucleon mass to $J/ψ$ mass modification in nuclear medium and properties of hidden-charm pentaquark states. Here, we present the low-energy $NJ/ψ$ and $Nη_c$ interactions based on ($2+1$) flavor lattice QCD simulations with nearly physical pion mass $m_π=146$ MeV. The interactions, extracted from the spacetime correlations of the nucleon and charmonium system by using the HAL QCD method, are found to be attractive in all distances and manifest a characteristic long-range tail consistent with the two-pion exchange interaction. The resulting scattering lengths are around $0.3$ fm, $0.4$ fm and $0.2$ fm for $NJ/ψ$ with spin $3/2$, with spin $1/2$, and $Nη_c$, respectively. Our results are orders of magnitude larger than those from the photoproduction experiments assuming the vector meson dominance.

# NJ/ψ and Nηc interactions from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The low-energy N-J/ψ and N-ηc interactions, extracted from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations at nearly physical pion mass (mπ = 146 MeV) using the HAL QCD method, are attractive at all distances and exhibit a long-range tail consistent with two-pion exchange, yielding scattering lengths around 0.3 fm, 0.4 fm, and 0.2 fm respectively, orders of magnitude larger than those inferred from photoproduction experiments assuming vector meson dominance.

## Method

The authors used (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD configurations on a 96⁴ lattice with spacing a ≈ 0.0846 fm and physical volume La ≈ 8.1 fm, employing the Iwasaki gauge action and nonperturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quark action with nearly physical quark masses. The N-c̅c potentials were extracted from spacetime R-correlators using the HAL QCD method, with local sink operators for nucleon, J/ψ, and ηc, and the charm quark treated via the relativistic heavy quark (RHQ) action. The leading-order local potential was obtained from the time-dependent Schrödinger-like equation, and scattering parameters were derived by fitting the potentials with three-range Gaussians and solving for phase shifts near threshold.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: mπ = 146.4(4) MeV (lattice) vs. 138.0 MeV (experimental, isospin-averaged)
- Nucleon mass: 954.0(2.9) MeV (lattice) vs. 938.9 MeV (experimental)
- J/ψ mass: 3121.1(1) MeV (set 1) and 3076.3(1) MeV (set 2) on lattice vs. 3096.9 MeV experimental
- ηc mass: 3022.8(1) MeV (set 1) and 2976.3(1) MeV (set 2) on lattice vs. 2984.1 MeV experimental
- Scattering length for N-J/ψ (spin 3/2): a0 = 0.30(2)(+0/-2) fm
- Scattering length for N-J/ψ (spin 1/2): a0 = 0.38(4)(+0/-3) fm
- Scattering length for N-ηc (spin 1/2): a0 = 0.21(2)(+0/-1) fm
- Effective range for N-J/ψ (spin 3/2): reff = 3.25(12)(+6/-9) fm
- Effective range for N-J/ψ (spin 1/2): reff = 2.66(21)(+0/-10) fm
- Effective range for N-ηc (spin 1/2): reff = 3.65(19)(+0/-6) fm
- Two-pion exchange fit parameter α = 22(2) MeV·fm² for N-J/ψ (spin 3/2), 23(3) MeV·fm² for N-J/ψ (spin 1/2), and 16(2) MeV·fm² for N-ηc
- Predicted J/ψ mass reduction in normal nuclear medium (ρnm = 0.17 fm⁻³): δmJ/ψ = 19(3) MeV
- Spin-averaged N-J/ψ scattering length from this work: 0.33(4) fm

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of N-charmonium interactions that were limited to quenched approximation, heavy pion masses, or large uncertainties, such as Yokokawa et al. (2006) in quenched QCD, Kawanai and Sasaki (2010) in quenched QCD, Sugiura et al. (2019) at mπ = 700 MeV, and Skerbis and Prelovsek (2019) at mπ = 266 MeV. The results contradict photoproduction-based extractions using vector meson dominance (Gryniuk and Vanderhaeghen, 2016; Pentchev and Strakovsky, 2021) which give scattering lengths of order 10⁻³ fm, while being more consistent with the JPAC (2023) unitarity-constrained photoproduction result of O(1) fm. The findings enable more reliable predictions for J/ψ mass modification in nuclear matter, inputs for coupled-channel analyses of hidden-charm pentaquark states (first observed by LHCb in 2015), and predictions for charmonium-nucleus bound states.
