# Central and tensor Lambda-nucleon potentials from lattice QCD

> We present our latest study of Lambda-Nucleon (LN) interaction by using lattice QCD, following up on our report at LATTICE 2008. We have calculated not only the scattering lengths but also the central and tensor potentials, which are obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) amplitude measured in lattice QCD. For these calculations, we employ two different types of gauge configurations: (i) 2+1 flavor full QCD configurations generated by the PACS-CS collaboration at $β=1.9$ ($a=0.0907(13)$ fm) on a $32^3\times 64$ lattice, whose spatial volume is (2.90 fm)$^3$, with the quark masses corresponding to $(m_π,m_K)\approx (301,592)$, $(414,637)$, $(570,724)$ and $(699,787)$ (in units of MeV). (ii) Quenched QCD configurations at $β=5.7$ ($a=0.1416(9)$ fm) on a $32^3\times48$ lattice, whose spatial volume is (4.5 fm)$^3$, with the quark masses corresponding to $(m_π,m_K)\approx (512,606)$, $(464, 586)$ and $(407,565)$. The following qualitative features are found: The LN potential has a relatively strong (weak) repulsive core in the $^1S_0$ ($^3S_1$) channel at short distance, while the potential has slight attractive region at medium distance. The tensor potential is found to be weaker than the $NN$ case. These results hold in both full and quenched QCD. The energy of the ground state on the finite lattice volume is calculated: In both spin channels, energy shift due to the finite volume, from which we extract the scattering length via the Lüscher's formula, is found to be negative at all values of quark masses, suggesting that the LN interaction is attractive. We have also discussed the quark mass dependences of the potentials and the scattering lengths.

# Central and tensor Lambda-Nucleon potentials from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The Lambda-Nucleon (ΛN) interaction is attractive in both spin channels, with a relatively strong repulsive core in the ¹S₀ channel and a weaker repulsive core in the ³S₁ channel at short distance, while the tensor potential is weaker than the nucleon-nucleon (NN) case; these qualitative features hold in both 2+1 flavor full QCD and quenched QCD, but the scattering lengths extracted are still much smaller than empirical estimates of 1.5–2.5 fm, and larger spatial volumes are needed for reliable extraction at pion masses near 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors computed the equal-time Bethe-Salpeter (BS) wave function for the ΛN system on the lattice and derived the central and tensor potentials from an effective Schrödinger equation using a velocity expansion, keeping only leading-order (LO) terms (V₀, V_σ, V_T). For the spin-triplet channel, the S-wave and D-wave components of the BS wave function were separated by projection operators, and the potentials were extracted from the resulting coupled-channel equations. Scattering lengths were obtained via Lüscher's formula from the energy shift of the ground state on the finite lattice volume.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing for full QCD: a = 0.0907(13) fm at β = 1.9 on a 32³ × 64 lattice, spatial volume (2.90 fm)³
- Lattice spacing for quenched QCD: a = 0.1416(9) fm at β = 5.7 on a 32³ × 48 lattice, spatial volume (4.5 fm)³
- Pion masses used in full QCD: (m_π, m_K) ≈ (301, 592), (414, 637), (570, 724), and (699, 787) MeV
- Pion masses used in quenched QCD: (m_π, m_K) ≈ (512, 606), (464, 586), and (407, 565) MeV
- Number of gauge configurations in full QCD: N_conf = 609, 481, 568, 422 for the four κ_ud values
- Number of gauge configurations in quenched QCD: N_conf ≈ 1000
- For m_π ≈ 700 MeV, the central potentials reach V_C → 0 at r ∼ 1.3 fm, which is smaller than half the lattice length (aL/2 ≈ 1.45 fm)
- Empirical ΛN scattering lengths: a₀ ∼ 1.5–2.5 fm (from total cross section and hypernuclei studies)
- Hadron masses at the lightest full QCD point (κ_ud = 0.13770): m_π = 301(3) MeV, m_N = 1079(12) MeV, m_Λ = 1248(15) MeV

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD approach to baryon-baryon potentials, previously applied to the NN system by Ishii (2006) and the HAL QCD Collaboration, to the ΛN system. It follows a preliminary report at LATTICE 2008 by Nemura (2009) and improves upon it by including tensor potentials, studying quark mass dependence, and increasing statistics. The results are compared to empirical scattering lengths from ΛN total cross section measurements and theoretical studies of Λ-hypernuclei. The calculations use 2+1 flavor full QCD configurations generated by the PACS-CS Collaboration (Aoki, 2008) and quenched QCD configurations. The authors note that larger spatial volumes and smaller lattice spacings at physical quark mass are needed for definitive conclusions, and that the present scattering lengths remain much smaller than empirical estimates.
