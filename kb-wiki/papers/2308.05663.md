# Nucleon axial-vector coupling constant in magnetar environments

> The nucleon axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$ is studied in the presence of an external magnetic field, and in dense nuclear environments, to emulate nuclear matter in magnetars. For this purpose we use QCD finite energy sum rules for two-current and three-current correlators, the former involving nucleon-nucleon correlators and the latter involving proton-axial-neutron currents. As a result, the axial-vector coupling constant decreases both with baryon density as well as with magnetic field. The axial-vector coupling evaluated with baryon density near the nuclear density $ρ_0$ leads to $g_A^*\approx 0.92$. In the presence of magnetic fields $g_A$ decreases in general, but $g_A^*$ does not show significant changes.

## Key claim

The nucleon axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) decreases with both baryon density and external magnetic field, but at nuclear density \(\rho_0\) the value \(g_A^* \approx 0.92\) is not significantly changed by the magnetic field for the field strengths considered, meaning the quenching in magnetar nuclear matter is dominated by density rather than magnetic effects.

## Method

The authors used QCD finite energy sum rules (FESR) for two-current correlators (nucleon-nucleon) and three-current correlators (proton-axial-vector-neutron) to determine the medium-dependent nucleon-current couplings, hadronic thresholds, and the axial-vector coupling constant. They incorporated baryon density effects through in-medium condensates (quark condensate, gluon condensate, energy-momentum tensor components) and magnetic field effects through the chiral condensate polarization \(\langle \bar q \sigma_{12} q \rangle\) and perturbative magnetic corrections, using a factorized approximation \(f(B,\rho_B) \approx f(B,0) f(0,\rho_B)/f(0,0)\) for the quark condensate and nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

* At nuclear density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3} = 1.22 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{3}\), the axial-vector coupling constant is \(g_A^* \approx 0.92\).
* The vacuum value of the axial-vector coupling constant is \(g_A \approx 1.275\) (used as experimental input to fix parameters).
* The quark condensate evolves with density as \(\langle \bar q q \rangle = \langle \bar q q \rangle_0 [1 - 0.329 (\rho_B/\rho_0)]\).
* The gluon condensate evolves with density as \(\langle G^2 \rangle = \langle G^2 \rangle_0 [1 - 0.066 (\rho_B/\rho_0)]\).
* The nucleon mass evolves with density as \(m_N^* = m_N [1 - 0.329 (\rho_B/\rho_0)]\).
* The energy-momentum tensor condensates at finite density are \(\langle \Theta_q \rangle = 2.6 \times 10^{-4} (\rho_B/\rho_0)\) and \(\langle \Theta_g \rangle = 6.1 \times 10^{-5} (\rho_B/\rho_0)\).
* Magnetar core magnetic fields can reach \(\sim 10^{19}\ \text{G}\), equivalent to \(eB \sim 3.4\ m_\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of the axial-vector coupling in vacuum (Villavicencio 2022) and in nuclear matter without magnetic fields (Rho 1974, Wilkinson 1974, Brown 1978, and others). It also builds on finite-density QCD sum rules for baryons developed by Cohen (1991), Furnstahl (1992), and Jin (1993-1994). The results enable more realistic modeling of neutrino emissivity and Urca processes in magnetar interiors, where both high density and strong magnetic fields are present, and suggest that magnetic field effects on \(g_A\) become noticeable only at densities above about \(1.25 \rho_0\).
