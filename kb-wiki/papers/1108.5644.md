# Three-body $D\bar{D}π$ dynamics for the X(3872)

> We investigate the role played by the three-body $D\bar{D}π$ dynamics on the near-threshold resonance X(3872) charmonium state, which is assumed to be formed by nonperturbative $D\bar D^*$ dynamics. It is demonstrated that, as compared to the naive static-pions approximation, the imaginary parts that originate from the inclusion of dynamical pions reduce substantially the width from the $D\bar{D}π$ intermediate state. In particular, for a resonance peaked at 0.5 MeV below the $D^0\bar D^{*0}$ threshold, this contribution to the width is reduced by about a factor of 2, and the effect of the pion dynamics on the width grows as long as the resonance is shifted towards the $D^0\bar{D^0}π^0$ threshold. Although the physical width of the $X$ is dominated by inelastic channels, our finding should still be of importance for the $X$ line shapes in the $D\bar{D}π$ channel below $D{\bar D}^*$ threshold. For example, in the scattering length approximation, the imaginary part of the scattering length includes effects of all the pion dynamics and does not only stem from the $D^*$ width. Meanwhile, we find that another important quantity for the $X$ phenomenology, the residue at the $X$ pole, is weakly sensitive to dynamical pions. In particular, we find that the binding energy dependence of this quantity from the full calculation is close to that found from a model with pointlike $D\bar D^*$ interactions only, consistent with earlier claims. Coupled-channel effects (inclusion of the charged $D\bar{D}^*$ channel) turn out to have a moderate impact on the results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including dynamical pions (rather than treating them as static) reduces the width of the X(3872) from the D Dbar pi intermediate state by about a factor of two for a resonance peaked at 0.5 MeV below the D0 Dbar*0 threshold, but the residue at the X pole is weakly sensitive to dynamical pions and remains close to that from a model with pointlike D Dbar* interactions only.

## Method

The authors solved a system of coupled Faddeev-type integral equations for D Dbar* scattering in the 1++ channel, including the three-body D Dbar pi intermediate state via one-pion exchange potentials. They performed partial-wave decomposition retaining S-wave and D-wave components, regularized the linearly divergent integrals with a sharp cutoff, and introduced a contact counter term to absorb short-range dynamics. The full dynamical calculation was compared to the static-pion approximation and to a model with only contact D Dbar* interactions.

## Key numbers

* For a resonance peaked at 0.5 MeV below the D0 Dbar*0 threshold, the width from D Dbar pi intermediate states is reduced from 102 keV to 44 keV when dynamical pions are included.
* The D*0 to D0 pi0 width used as input is 42 keV, and the D*0 to D0 gamma width is 21 keV.
* The D* D pi coupling constant extracted from the D*0 decay width is g = 1.29 x 10^-5 MeV^-3/2.
* The mass difference between the neutral and charged two-body thresholds is Delta M = 8.08 MeV.
* The neutral D meson mass is 1864.84 MeV, the D*0 mass is 2006.97 MeV, and the pi0 mass is 134.98 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the X(3872) as a D Dbar* molecule bound by one-pion exchange (Tornqvist 1994, Swanson 2004, Thomas and Close 2008) by properly including the three-body D Dbar pi unitarity cuts that were neglected in static-pion approximations. The results validate the central assumption of X-EFT (Bramon et al. 2004, Fleming et al. 2007) that pion effects can be treated perturbatively for most observables, and show that the effective coupling of X to D Dbar* is fixed by the binding energy as in a true two-body state, consistent with Voloshin (2004). The findings enable more accurate predictions for X(3872) line shapes in the D Dbar pi channel below the D Dbar* threshold.
