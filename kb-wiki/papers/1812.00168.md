# $η$ photoproduction off the deuteron and low-energy $η$-nucleon interaction

> We study $η$ photoproduction off the deuteron ($γd\toηpn$) at a special kinematics: $\sim 0.94$ GeV of the photon beam energy and $\sim 0^\circ$ of the scattering angle of the proton. This kinematics is ideal to extract the low-energy $η$-nucleon scattering parameters such as $a_{ηN}$ (scattering length) and $r_{ηN}$ (effective range) because the $η$-nucleon elastic scattering is significantly enhanced. We show that if a ratio $R$, the $γd\toηpn$ cross section divided by the $γp\toηp$ cross section convoluted with the proton momentum distribution in the deuteron, is measured with 5% error, ${\rm Re}[a_{ηN}]$ (${\rm Re}[r_{ηN}]$) can be determined at the precision of $\sim\pm$0.1 fm ($\sim\pm$0.5 fm), significantly narrowing down the currently estimated range of the parameters. The measurement is ongoing at the Research Center for Electron Photon Science (ELPH), Tohoku University.

## Key claim

The authors show that if the ratio \(R\) of the \(\gamma d\to\eta pn\) cross section to the \(\gamma p\to\eta p\) cross section convoluted with the proton momentum distribution in the deuteron is measured with 5% error at the ELPH kinematics (photon energy \(\sim 0.94\) GeV and proton scattering angle \(\sim 0^\circ\)), then the real part of the \(\eta\)-nucleon scattering length \(\mathrm{Re}[a_{\eta N}]\) can be determined to \(\sim\pm 0.1\) fm and the real part of the effective range \(\mathrm{Re}[r_{\eta N}]\) to \(\sim\pm 0.5\) fm, significantly narrowing the currently estimated ranges.

## Method

The authors model \(\gamma d\to\eta pn\) using a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) framework that includes impulse, \(\eta\)-exchange, \(\pi\)-exchange, and \(NN\)-rescattering mechanisms. The elementary \(\gamma N\to MN\) and \(MN\to M'N\) amplitudes are taken from a DCC model fitted to \(\sim 27,000\) data points for \(\pi N\) and \(\gamma N\) reactions up to \(\sqrt{s}\lesssim 2.1\) GeV, while the deuteron wave function and \(NN\) amplitudes are generated from the CD-Bonn potential. The full model is then used to compute the ratio \(R_{\mathrm{th}}\) of the full differential cross section to the impulse-only cross section at the ELPH kinematics, and the sensitivity of this ratio to variations in \(a_{\eta N}\) and \(r_{\eta N}\) is evaluated.

## Key numbers

- The photon beam energy for the ELPH kinematics is \(E_\gamma \sim 0.94\) GeV, with the recoil proton detected at \(\theta_p \sim 0^\circ\).
- The currently estimated range for \(\mathrm{Re}[a_{\eta N}]\) is 0.2–0.9 fm; the DCC model predicts \(a_{\eta N} = 0.75 + 0.26i\) fm.
- The currently estimated range for \(\mathrm{Re}[r_{\eta N}]\) is \(-6\) to \(+1\) fm; the DCC model predicts \(r_{\eta N} = -1.6 - 0.6i\) fm.
- With a 5% error per MeV bin in the measured ratio \(R_{\mathrm{expt}}\), \(\mathrm{Re}[a_{\eta N}]\) can be determined to \(\sim\pm 0.1\) fm and \(\mathrm{Re}[r_{\eta N}]\) to \(\sim\pm 0.5\) fm.
- At the quasi-free peak (\(M_{\eta n} \sim 1.488\) GeV), the ratio \(R_{\mathrm{th}}\) changes by \(\sim 25\%\) when \(\mathrm{Re}[a_{\eta N}]\) is varied over its full estimated range (0.2–1.0 fm), and by \(\sim 5\%\) when varied by \(\pm 0.1\) fm from 0.6 fm.
- At \(M_{\eta n} = 1.5\) GeV, the ratio \(R_{\mathrm{th}}\) changes by \(\sim 30\%\) when \(\mathrm{Re}[r_{\eta N}]\) is varied over \(-6\) to 0 fm, and by \(\sim 5\%\) when varied over \(-3.5\) to \(-2.5\) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to extract \(\eta N\) scattering parameters from \(\pi N\to \pi N, \eta N\) and \(\gamma N\to \pi N, \eta N\) reactions (summarized in a 2009 review) and from \(pn\to\eta d\) data (Potel et al., 2015). Those analyses gave consistent imaginary parts but left the real parts poorly constrained, with \(\mathrm{Re}[a_{\eta N}]\) ranging from 0.2 to 0.9 fm and \(\mathrm{Re}[r_{\eta N}]\) from \(-6\) to \(+1\) fm. The present study shows that the ongoing ELPH experiment at Tohoku University can determine these real parts with significantly improved precision, which is essential for predicting the existence of \(\eta\)-mesic nuclei.
