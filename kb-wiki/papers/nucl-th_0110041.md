# Descritpion of Exotic Nuclei Using Continuum Shell Model

> In weakly bound exotic nuclei, number of excited bound states or narrow resonances is small and, moreover, they couple strongly to the particle continuum. Hence, these systems should be described in the quantum open system formalism which does not artificially separate the subspaces of (quasi-) bound and scattering states. The Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum provides a novel approach which solves this problem. Examples of application in sd-shell nuclei will be presented.

# Description of Exotic Nuclei Using Continuum Shell Model

## Key claim

The Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) provides a unified description of both structure and reaction observables in weakly bound exotic nuclei, but the quantitative extraction of charge-dependent effects in mirror systems depends on the chosen valence space and effective interaction, as demonstrated by the overprediction of the $^{17}$N($\beta^-$)$^{17}$O decay rate by a factor of approximately 3 when using the ZBM-F interaction.

## Method

The SMEC formalism separates the Hilbert space into a $Q$ subspace of (quasi-)bound many-body states described by the multiconfigurational Shell Model with realistic effective interactions, and a $P$ subspace of scattering states containing asymptotic channels of an $(N-1)$-particle localized state plus one nucleon in the continuum. The coupling between bound and scattering states is described by a density-dependent interaction (DDSM1), and the initial Woods-Saxon potential is iteratively modified to yield a self-consistent potential $U^{(sc)}(r)$ that depends on channel angular momentum. For the oxygen isotope chain, the SMEC energy correction to ground-state masses is calculated in the full $sd$ shell using the Wildenthal effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- The surface diffuseness of the initial Woods-Saxon potential that reproduces the $^{16}$O$(p,\gamma)^{17}$F astrophysical $S$-factor data is $a = 0.55 \pm 0.05$ fm.
- The experimental $\beta^+$ decay rate $f^+$ for $^{17}$Ne($\beta^+$)$^{17}$F is reproduced by SMEC with ZBM-F interaction, but the measured rate $f^-$ for $^{17}$N($\beta^-$)$^{17}$O is overpredicted by a factor of approximately 3.
- The amplitude of the $(1s_{1/2}^2 0p_{1/2}^{-1})$ component in the ground state of $^{17}$N must be reduced by approximately 30% to reproduce experimental $f^-$ and $f^+/f^-$ values.
- For $^{26}$O ($N=18$), the $0d_{3/2}$ single-particle state becomes bound by approximately 200 keV when the standard isospin dependence of the central potential is used; keeping it in the continuum at approximately 50 keV changes the SMEC energy correction.

## Context

This work extends the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum formalism developed by Bennaceur, Nowak, Okolowicz, and Ploszajczak (1998, 2000) to treat weakly bound exotic nuclei as open quantum systems. The analysis of mirror $\beta$ decays in $A=17$ nuclei addresses the abnormal asymmetry previously discussed by Borge et al. (1993) and Millener (1997), but provides a consistent treatment of radial wave functions constrained by proton capture data from Morlock et al. (1997). The oxygen isotope calculations extend the Shell Model results of Caurier et al. (1998) by including continuum coupling effects that become large near the neutron drip line, and suggest that the position of the neutron drip line may shift significantly between oxygen and fluorine due to isoscalar couplings between proton and neutron fluids.
