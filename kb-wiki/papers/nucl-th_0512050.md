# U(3) and Pseudo-U(3) Symmetry of the Relativistic Harmonic Oscillator

> We show that a Dirac Hamiltonian with equal scalar and vector harmonic oscillator potentials has not only a spin symmetry but an U(3) symmetry and that a Dirac Hamiltonian with scalar and vector harmonic oscillator potentials equal in magnitude but opposite in sign has not only a pseudospin symmetry but a pseudo-U(3) symmetry. We derive the generators of the symmetry for each case.

## Key claim

A Dirac Hamiltonian with equal scalar and vector harmonic oscillator potentials possesses not only a spin symmetry but a full U(3) symmetry, and a Dirac Hamiltonian with scalar and vector potentials equal in magnitude but opposite in sign possesses a pseudo-U(3) symmetry; the generators of these symmetries are derived explicitly, though the quadrupole and monopole operators depend on the Hamiltonian itself, making the symmetry algebra energy-dependent.

## Method

The author constructs the Dirac Hamiltonian for a spherical harmonic oscillator with spin symmetry (scalar and vector potentials equal) and with pseudospin symmetry (potentials equal in magnitude but opposite in sign). By imposing commutation relations with the Hamiltonian, the relativistic quadrupole and monopole operators are derived in matrix form, and their closure under the U(3) algebra is verified. The non-relativistic limit is taken to confirm consistency with the standard U(3) generators of Elliott (1958). The pseudospin case is obtained via a gamma-5 transformation.

## Key numbers

- The non-relativistic U(3) generators are defined with the quadrupole operator \( q_m = \frac{1}{\hbar M\omega} \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}} (M^2 \omega^2 [rr]_m^{(2)} + [pp]_m^{(2)}) \) and the monopole operator \( \mathcal{N}_{NR} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{2} \hbar M\omega} (M^2 \omega^2 r^2 + p^2) - \frac{3}{2} \).
- The relativistic quadrupole operator normalization constant is \( \lambda_2 = \sqrt{3 / (M\omega^2 \hbar^2 (H+M))} \).
- The relativistic monopole operator is \( \mathcal{N} = \frac{1}{2\hbar \sqrt{(H+M)M\omega^2}} (\dots) - \frac{3}{2} \), with eigenvalue equal to the total harmonic oscillator quantum number \( N \).
- In the non-relativistic limit \( H \rightarrow M, M \rightarrow \infty \), the relativistic generators reduce to the standard non-relativistic forms.

## Context

This work extends the known U(3) symmetry of the non-relativistic harmonic oscillator, influential in connecting the shell model with collective motion (Elliott, 1958; Igal, 1990s), to the relativistic Dirac harmonic oscillator. It builds on prior analytic solutions for the Dirac Hamiltonian with equal scalar and vector harmonic oscillator potentials (Ginocchio, 2004, 2005) and on the pseudospin symmetry explanation for nuclear pseudospin doublets (Ginocchio, 1997). The results enable the study of relativistic symmetries in nuclear mean-field models and suggest that anti-nucleon spectra in nuclei may exhibit approximate U(3) symmetry if vector and scalar potentials are approximately equal and strong (Thomas, 2005).
