# Non-relativistic bound states in a moving thermal bath

> We study the propagation of non-relativistic bound states moving at constant velocity across a homogeneous thermal bath and we develop the effective field theory which is relevant in various dynamical regimes. We consider values of the velocity of the bound state ranging from moderate to highly relativistic and temperatures at all relevant scales smaller than the mass of the particles that form the bound state. In particular, we consider two distinct temperature regimes, corresponding to temperatures smaller or higher than the typical momentum transfer in the bound state. For temperatures smaller or of the order of the typical momentum transfer, we restrict our analysis to the simplest system, a hydrogen-like atom. We build the effective theory for this system first considering moderate values of the velocity and then the relativistic case. For large values of the velocity of the bound state, the separation of scales is such that the corresponding effective theory resembles the soft collinear effective theory (SCET). For temperatures larger than the typical momentum transfer we also consider muonic hydrogen propagating in a plasma which contains photons and massless electrons and positrons, so that the system resembles very much heavy quarkonium in a thermal medium of deconfined quarks and gluons. We study the behavior of the real and imaginary part of the static two-body potential, for various velocities of the bound state, in the hard thermal loop approximation. We find that Landau damping ceases to be the relevant mechanism for dissociation from a certain "critical" velocity on in favor of screening. Our results are relevant for understanding how the properties of heavy quarkonia states produced in the initial fusion of partons in the relativistic collision of heavy ions are affected by the presence of an equilibrated quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper develops effective field theories for non-relativistic bound states moving at constant velocity through a thermal bath, and finds that for temperatures larger than the typical momentum transfer, Landau damping ceases to be the relevant mechanism for dissociation above a certain "critical" velocity, with screening becoming dominant instead.

## Method

The authors use non-relativistic effective field theories (NRQED and pNRQED) in a frame where the bound state is at rest and the thermal bath moves with velocity v. They consider two distinct temperature regimes: temperatures smaller or of the order of the typical momentum transfer (studied for hydrogen-like atoms), and temperatures larger than the typical momentum transfer (studied for muonic hydrogen). For the high-temperature regime, they employ the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation to compute the real and imaginary parts of the static two-body potential as functions of the bound state velocity.

## Key numbers

- The effective temperature in the moving frame is given by T_eff(theta,v) = T sqrt(1-v^2) / (1 - v cos(theta)), which produces two light-cone temperature scales: T_+ = T sqrt((1+v)/(1-v)) and T_- = T sqrt((1-v)/(1+v))
- For the hydrogen atom at moderate velocities (v less than or about 0.5) with T much less than 1/r, the decay width for S-wave states is delta Gamma_n = (2 Z^2 alpha^3 T sqrt(1-v^2)) / (3 n^2 v) * log((1+v)/(1-v))
- For the hydrogen atom at moderate velocities with T much less than 1/r, the energy shift for S-wave states is delta E_n = (alpha pi T^2)/(3 m_e) - (4 Z alpha^2 |phi_n(0)|^2)/(3 m_e^2) * (-1/(2v) log((1+v)/(1-v)) + 1 - gamma) + (2 alpha)/(3 pi m_e^2) sum_r |<n|p|r>|^2 (E_n - E_r) log(2 pi T / |E_n - E_r|)
- For the hydrogen atom at moderate velocities with T much less than E, the dominant thermal corrections cancel between tad-pole and rainbow diagrams, leaving contributions of order O(alpha^3 T^4 / E^3)
- The tensor T_ij in the matching for T ~ 1/r is given by T_ij = (1/(6 pi^2)) [log(mu/T) - log 2 - log(2 pi) + gamma + (3/(8v))(1 + 1/(3v^2)) log((1+v)/(1-v)) - 1/(4v^2)] delta_ij + (rho(v)/(8 pi^2)) (v_i v_j / v^2), where rho(v) = (1/(2v))(1 - 1/v^2) log((1+v)/(1-v)) - 2/3 + 1/v^2

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of bound states in thermal media from the static case (Escobedo 2008, Brambilla 2008, Laine 2006) to the case where the bound state moves with respect to the medium. It is directly relevant for understanding heavy quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, where the quark-gluon plasma expands and heavy quarkonia produced in initial parton fusion are not comoving with the medium. The results for muonic hydrogen in a photon-electron-positron plasma serve as an analog for heavy quarkonium in a deconfined quark-gluon plasma, and the finding that screening overtakes Landau damping above a critical velocity provides a new mechanism for understanding the velocity dependence of quarkonium dissociation.
