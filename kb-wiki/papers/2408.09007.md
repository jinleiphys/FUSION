# Coulomb confinement in the Hamiltonian limit

> The Gribov--Zwanziger scenario attributes the phenomenon of confinement to the instantaneous interaction term in the QCD Hamiltonian in the Coulomb gauge. For a static quark-antiquark pair, it leads to a potential energy that increases linearly with the distance between them. Lattice studies of the SU(2) Yang--Mills theory determined the corresponding (Coulomb) string tension for sources in the fundamental representation, $σ_{C}$, to be about three times larger than the Wilson loop string tension, $σ_F$. It is far above the Zwanziger variational bound, $σ_C \geq σ_F$. We argue that the value established in the literature is artificially inflated. We examine the lattice definition of the instantaneous potential, find the source of the string tension's enhancement, and perform its improved determination in SU(2) lattice gauge theory. We report our conservative estimate for the value of the Coulomb string tension as $σ_C/σ_F = 2.0 \pm 0.4$ and discuss its phenomenological implications.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the previously established lattice value of the Coulomb string tension ratio sigma_C/sigma_F being about three times larger than the Wilson loop string tension is artificially inflated due to lattice artifacts, and they report a corrected conservative estimate of sigma_C/sigma_F = 2.0 plus or minus 0.4, which is closer to the Zwanziger variational bound sigma_C greater than or equal to sigma_F.

## Method

The authors performed SU(2) Yang-Mills lattice simulations on anisotropic lattices with dimensions N^3 times xi N for N = 16, 24, 32, couplings beta from 2.25 to 2.60, and anisotropies xi up to 8. They extracted the non-Abelian Coulomb potential by fitting the short-time dependence of the temporal Wilson line correlator G(r,t) with models that include explicit lattice artifact terms of the form (a_t/t)^n, then computed the logarithmic derivative at t=0 to obtain the Coulomb potential, and finally extrapolated the resulting Coulomb string tension to the Hamiltonian limit xi approaching infinity (a_t approaching 0).

## Key numbers

* The ratio of Coulomb string tension to Wilson loop string tension is reported as sigma_C/sigma_F = 2.0 plus or minus 0.4, based on SU(2) lattice gauge theory simulations.
* Lattice ensembles used spatial sizes N = 16, 24, and 32, with couplings beta ranging from 2.25 to 2.60 in steps of 0.05.
* Anisotropies xi ranged from 1 to 8, with the minimum number of measurements per ensemble being 494 for N=32, 1557 for N=24, and 1917 for N=16.
* The gauge fixing convergence criterion was Delta F less than 10^{-7}, where F is the functional to be minimized.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenario by addressing the long-standing discrepancy between the Coulomb string tension and the Wilson loop string tension found in previous lattice studies by Greensite (2003), Greensite (2004), Nakamura (2005), Nakagawa (2006), Iritani (2010), Greensite (2014), Burgio (2015), and Greensite (2015). The improved determination brings the ratio closer to the Zwanziger variational bound and offers hope of resolving puzzles in the Gribov-Zwanziger scenario while enabling connections with Casimir scaling observed in lattice simulations by Ambjorn (1984), Greensite (1979), Olesen (1981), Deldar (1999), Bali (2000), Piccioni (2005), and Cardoso (2011).
