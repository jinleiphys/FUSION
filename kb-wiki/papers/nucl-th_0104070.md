# Status of Nucleon Resonances with Masses $M<M_N+M_π$

> We discuss different interpretations of peaks observed a few years ago by Tatischeff et al. in missing mass spectra of the reaction $pp->pi^+pX$, which were declared as new exited nucleon states with small masses. A study of the possible production of such states in the process $γp->pi^+N^*->pi^+ +γγn$ by analyzing the invariant mass spectrum of $γγn$ is proposed. It is shown that the data, obtained recently at MAMI-B, can allow to analyze this process and to get information about an existence of exited nucleon states with small masses.

# Status of Nucleon Resonances with Masses \(M < M_N + M_\pi\)

## Key claim

The peaks at 1004 and 1044 MeV observed by Tatischeff et al. in missing mass spectra of \(pp \to \pi^+ p X\) may not be excited nucleon states at all, but rather resonancelike states \(X_1 = \gamma + n\) arising from the decay of supernarrow dibaryons (SNDs); the proposed analysis of the \(\gamma p \to \pi^+ \gamma\gamma n\) reaction at MAMI-B can discriminate between these interpretations only if the data are sufficient to reconstruct the \(\gamma\gamma n\) invariant mass spectrum.

## Method

The authors propose to study the reaction \(\gamma p \to \pi^+ N^* \to \pi^+ \gamma\gamma n\) by analyzing the invariant mass spectrum of the \(\gamma\gamma n\) system, using data from the radiative \(\pi^+\) meson photoproduction experiment already carried out at MAMI-B. To suppress background from double and triple pion photoproduction, they apply cuts on the two-photon invariant mass (\(M_{\gamma\gamma} < 110\) MeV) and on the azimuthal angle difference between the \(\pi^+\) and the \(N^*\) (\(160^\circ < |\phi_{\pi^+} - \phi_{N^*}| < 200^\circ\)). Simulations of the signal and background under these conditions are presented for an incident photon energy range of 537 to 817 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Three narrow bumps observed by Tatischeff et al. in missing mass spectra of \(pp \to p\pi^+ X\) at \(M_X = 1004\), 1044, and 1094 MeV.
- In the \(pd \to p + p X_1\) reaction, peaks in the invariant mass \(M_{pX_1}\) were found at \(1904 \pm 2\), \(1926 \pm 2\), and \(1942 \pm 2\) MeV.
- Missing mass \(M_{X_1}\) peaks in the same reaction were observed at \(966 \pm 2\), \(986 \pm 2\), and \(1003 \pm 2\) MeV.
- The peak at \(M_{X_1} = 1003 \pm 2\) MeV corresponds to the bump at 1004 MeV from Tatischeff et al.
- For the proposed \(\gamma p \to \pi^+ \gamma\gamma n\) analysis, the accessible invariant mass range of \(\gamma\gamma n\) is from 985 to 1075 MeV.
- With 1000 hours of exposition time, the expected event counts for hypothetical \(N^*\) states are approximately 600 events at 986 MeV, 3500 events at 1004 MeV, and 12000 events at 1044 MeV.

## Context

This work addresses the controversy surrounding the narrow peaks reported by Tatischeff et al. (1990s), which were initially interpreted as new low-mass nucleon resonances. An earlier analysis by Lvov of Compton scattering data had already excluded such states as intermediate resonances. The authors connect these peaks to their own prior work on supernarrow dibaryons (SNDs) in the \(pd \to p + p X_1\) reaction (Fil'kov et al., 2000), where the missing mass peaks at 966, 986, and 1003 MeV were interpreted as \(\gamma + n\) states from SND decay rather than as excited nucleons. The proposed experiment at MAMI-B aims to resolve this ambiguity by searching for \(N^* \to \gamma\gamma n\) decays in photoproduction, which would provide a direct test of the nucleon resonance hypothesis versus the SND interpretation.
