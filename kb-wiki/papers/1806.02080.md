# Spurious finite-size instabilities with Gogny-type interactions

> Recently, a new parameterization of the Gogny interaction suitable for astrophysical applications, named D1M*, has been presented. We investigate the possible existence of spurious finite-size instabilities of this new Gogny force by repeating a study that we have already performed for the most commonly used parameterizations (D1, D1S, D1N, D1M) of the Gogny force. This study is based on a fully-antisymmetrized random phase approximation (RPA) calculation of the nuclear matter response functions employing the continued fraction technique. It turns out that this new Gogny interaction is affected by spurious finite-size instabilities in the scalar isovector channel; hence, unphysical results are expected in the calculation of properties of nuclei, like neutron and proton densities, if this D1M* force is used. The conclusions from this study are then, for the first time, tested against mean-field calculations in a coordinate representation for several nuclei. Unphysical results for several nuclei are also obtained with the D1N parameterization of the Gogny force. These observations strongly advocate for the use of the linear response formalism to detect and avoid finite-size instabilities during the fit of the parameters of Gogny interactions as it is already done for some Skyrme forces.

# Spurious finite-size instabilities with Gogny-type interactions

## Key claim

The new D1M* Gogny interaction, which was designed for astrophysical applications and can reproduce neutron star masses of about 2 solar masses, is affected by spurious finite-size instabilities in the scalar isovector channel that make it unsuitable for calculating nuclear properties on a mesh; the D1N parameterization also shows instability problems for some nuclei, contrary to earlier assumptions that it was safe.

## Method

The authors performed fully-antisymmetrized random phase approximation (RPA) calculations of nuclear matter response functions using the continued fraction technique to extract critical densities at which poles appear in the zero-energy response. They then tested these predictions against self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations on a mesh with spacing 0.1 fm in a spherical box of radius 20 fm for several nuclei (208Pb, 120Sn, 4He), using the FINRES4 code.

## Key numbers

- The D1M* interaction has a symmetry energy slope L = 43.18 MeV, compared to L = 24.83 MeV for D1M
- For D1M*, critical densities in the scalar isovector channel reach values around ρc ≃ 1.2 ρ0, where ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ is the empirical saturation density
- For D1M*, the plateau of critical densities at approximately 1.2 × ρsat occurs for transferred momenta in the range 2.8 fm⁻¹ ≲ q ≲ 4 fm⁻¹
- For 48Ca calculated with D1M* using HFBTHO, the binding energy continues to change significantly with each increase of oscillator shells up to Nsh = 24, beyond which convergence could not be obtained

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (De Pace, 2016) of finite-size instabilities in Gogny forces (D1, D1S, D1N, D1M) to the new D1M* parameterization proposed by Gonzalez-Boquera (2017) for astrophysical applications. The study demonstrates a robust correspondence between RPA predictions in infinite nuclear matter and instabilities observed in finite nuclei, as previously established for Skyrme interactions by Hellemans (2013). The results advocate for incorporating linear response formalism checks into the fitting protocol for new Gogny interactions, similar to what is already done for Skyrme functionals by Kortelainen (2013) and others.
