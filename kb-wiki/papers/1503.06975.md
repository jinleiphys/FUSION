# Energy Resolution with the Lorentz integral transform

> A brief outline of the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method is given. The method is well established and allows to treat reactions into the many-body continuum with bound-state like techniques. The energy resolution that can be achieved is studied by means of a simple two-body reaction. From the discussion it will become clear that the LIT method is an approach with a controlled resolution and that there is no principle problem to even resolve narrow resonances in the many-body continuum. As an example the isoscalar monopole resonance of 4He is considered. The importance of the choice of a proper basis for the expansion of the LIT states is pointed out. Employing such a basis a width of 180(70) keV is found for the 4He isoscalar monopole resonance when using a simple central nucleon-nucleon potential model.

## Key claim

The Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method is an approach with a controlled resolution that can resolve narrow resonances in the many-body continuum, provided that the basis used to expand the LIT states explicitly includes the relevant dynamical variable (e.g., the relative vector between fragments) to achieve sufficient density of LIT states in the resonance region.

## Method

The LIT method solves a bound-state-like equation (H - σ) Ψ̃ = S for a localized wavefunction Ψ̃, from which the Lorentz integral transform L(σ) = ⟨Ψ̃|Ψ̃⟩ is obtained. For the two-body test case of deuteron photodisintegration in unretarded dipole approximation, the LIT equation was solved using expansions in Laguerre polynomials with varying basis size N and spatial extension parameter b. For the four-body case of ⁴He isoscalar monopole response, a hybrid basis was constructed from correlated hyperspherical harmonics (CHH) for three nucleons times single-particle basis states for the fourth nucleon, with the radial single-particle basis size N₄ varied to increase LIT state density in the resonance region.

## Key numbers

- For the two-body ³P₁ channel with AV18 potential and b=0.5 fm: lowest eigenenergies were 1.27 MeV (N=10), 0.19 MeV (N=30), and 0.076 MeV (N=50); highest eigenenergies were 2689 MeV (N=10), 11056 MeV (N=30), and 25457 MeV (N=50)
- For the two-body case with σ_I=10 MeV and b=0.5 fm, relative differences between N=20, 30, 40 and N=50 were less than 0.5% up to about 50, 60, and 85 MeV respectively
- ⁴He binding energy with TN potential (Coulomb included) converged to 31.41(5) MeV using the hybrid basis, agreeing with the CHH result of 31.40(5) MeV
- The ⁴He isoscalar monopole resonance width was found to be 120(10) keV (N₄=20) and 240 keV (N₄=22), with a mean value of 180(70) keV
- The experimental value for the ⁴He 0⁺ resonance width is 270(50) keV from Walcher (1970)

## Context

This work extends earlier LIT-EIHH calculations by Bacca et al. (2013) that could determine the ⁴He isoscalar monopole resonance strength but not its width due to insufficient density of LIT states in the resonance region. The paper demonstrates that the limitation was not inherent to the LIT method itself but to the choice of basis, and shows that including the relevant dynamical variable (the relative vector between the free nucleon and the bound three-nucleon system) enables width determination. This enables future calculations with realistic nuclear forces to resolve narrow resonances in the many-body continuum, and may also benefit calculations of astrophysical reactions at very low energies.
