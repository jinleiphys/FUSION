# Four-nucleon alpha-type correlations and proton-neutron pairing away of N=Z line

> We study the competition between alpha-type and conventional pair condensation in the ground state of nuclei with neutrons and protons interacting via a charge-independent pairing interaction. The ground state is described by a product of two condensates, one of alpha-like quartets and the other one of pairs in excess relative to the isotope with N=Z. It is shown that this ansatz for the ground state gives very accurate pairing correlation energies for nuclei with the valence nucleons above the closed cores 16O, 40Ca and 100Sn. These results indicate that alpha-type correlations are important not only for the self-conjugate nuclei but also for nuclei away of N=Z line. In the latter case alpha-like quartets coexist with the collective Cooper pairs formed by the nucleons in excess.

# Four-nucleon alpha-type correlations and proton-neutron pairing away of N=Z line

## Key claim

Alpha-type four-nucleon correlations coexist with conventional pair condensation in the ground state of nuclei away from the N=Z line, not only in self-conjugate nuclei. The authors show that a product of an alpha-like quartet condensate and a condensate of excess neutron pairs reproduces pairing correlation energies with errors below 1% relative to exact diagonalisation, indicating that alpha-type correlations persist regardless of the number of excess neutrons.

## Method

The authors describe the ground state of even-even nuclei with excess neutrons as a product of two condensates: an alpha-like quartet condensate built from collective proton-neutron, neutron-neutron, and proton-proton pairs coupled to total isospin T=0, and a separate condensate of collective neutron-neutron pairs for the excess neutrons. The mixing amplitudes for the collective pair operators are determined by minimising the expectation value of a charge-independent isovector pairing Hamiltonian, with the Pauli principle incorporated rigorously through recurrence relations. Calculations are performed for valence nucleons above the closed cores 16O, 40Ca, and 100Sn, using both seniority-type pairing forces with deformed mean fields from Skyrme-HF and more general isovector pairing forces from shell-model G-matrix interactions with spherical single-particle states.

## Key numbers

- For isotopes above 16O core: QCM correlation energies range from 6.539 MeV (20Ne, 0.17% error) to 7.994 MeV (30Ne, 0.04% error) for Ne isotopes, and from 8.388 MeV (24Mg, 0.41% error) to 8.609 MeV (32Mg, 0.06% error) for Mg isotopes; all errors relative to exact diagonalisation are below 1%
- For isotopes above 40Ca core: QCM correlation energies range from 3.142 MeV (44Ti, 0.16% error) to 3.956 MeV (50Ti, 0.43% error) for Ti isotopes, and from 4.227 MeV (48Cr, 0.49% error) to 4.855 MeV (54Cr, 0.29% error) for Cr isotopes
- For isotopes above 100Sn core with seniority force: QCM correlation energies range from 1.082 MeV (104Te, 0.18% error) to 1.880 MeV (110Te, 0.63% error) for Te isotopes, and from 1.863 MeV (108Xe, 0.37% error) to 2.954 MeV (114Xe, 0.34% error) for Xe isotopes
- For isotopes above 100Sn core with G-matrix interaction: QCM correlation energies range from 3.829 MeV (104Te, 0.05% error) to 6.764 MeV (112Te, 0.75% error) for Te isotopes, and from 6.696 MeV (108Xe, 0.83% error) to 7.947 MeV (116Xe, 1.05% error) for Xe isotopes
- In 30Mg, Schmidt number for protons in the quartet condensate is K=1.88 versus K=1.79 for protons in the pair condensate; neutron pairs in excess are about 64% more entangled than those in quartets

## Context

This work extends the quartet condensation model (QCM) previously developed for N=Z nuclei by Sandulescu et al. (Ref. I) to nuclei with neutron or proton excess. It builds on earlier proposals by Flowers et al. and Zelevinsky et al. for alpha-type superfluidity, and on particle-number-conserving studies by Eichler, Gambhir, Hasegawa, Dobes, and Talmi, which were limited to schematic interactions or small numbers of quartets. The results provide the first realistic microscopic evidence for coexistence of alpha-like quartets and conventional Cooper pairs in nuclei away from the N=Z line, and the QCM method can be applied to model spaces far beyond the capability of present shell model codes.
