# Electromagnetic anomaly in the presence of electric and chiral magnetic conductivities in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the spacetime evolution of electric $(\textbf{E})$ and magnetic $(\textbf{B})$ fields along with the electromagnetic anomaly $(\textbf{E}\cdot\textbf{B})$ in the presence of electric ($σ$) and chiral magnetic ($σ_χ$) conductivities in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200$~GeV. By comparing to the Lienard-Wiechert solutions with zero conductivities, we observe a symmetry breaking of the electromagnetic field in a conducting medium with respect to the reaction plane. The decay of the field is also significantly decelerated after the conductivities are introduced. Similar effects are also found for the dipole structure of $\textbf{E}\cdot\textbf{B}$ as well as the quadrupole structure of $(\textbf{E}\cdot\textbf{B})\textbf{B}$, which may finally affect the charge separation of the elliptic flow coefficient of hadrons observed in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including finite electric conductivity (σ) and chiral magnetic conductivity (σχ) in the QGP medium breaks the symmetry of electromagnetic fields with respect to the reaction plane and significantly slows their decay, but this conclusion depends on the specific choice of σ = 5.8 MeV and σχ = 1.5 MeV, with the analytical solution valid only in the limit σχ ≪ σ.

## Method

The authors used a Monte-Carlo Glauber model to generate the initial spatial distribution of source charges for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. For the vacuum case (σ = σχ = 0), they evaluated electromagnetic fields using the Lienard-Wiechert potential. For the conducting medium, they solved Maxwell's equations with electric and chiral magnetic conductivities using the Green's function method in cylindrical coordinates, obtaining algebraic solutions for the field components. Results were averaged over 50,000 MC Glauber events for each impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √sNN = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Electric conductivity: σ = 5.8 MeV, consistent with lattice QCD results around the top temperature of QGP at RHIC
- Chiral magnetic conductivity: σχ = 1.5 MeV (corresponding to μ5 ∼ 100 MeV)
- Impact parameters studied: b = 4 fm and b = 8 fm for spatial distributions
- For b = 10 fm, protons in spectators alone yield about 6% smaller By compared to protons in the whole nucleus
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: σinelNN = 42 mb
- Nuclear radius parameter: R = 6.38 fm, surface thickness: d = 0.535 fm
- Time snapshots shown: t = 0.1 fm/c and 0.3 fm/c for vacuum; t = 0.1 fm/c and 1.1 fm/c for finite conductivity cases

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Zhao (2019) and Siddique (2021) on the spatial distribution of E·B to include a more realistic nuclear medium with electric and chiral magnetic conductivities. It follows the approach of Li (2016) for solving electromagnetic fields in conducting media and builds on earlier work by Tuchin (2013, 2014) showing that conductivities decelerate electromagnetic field decay. The results enable more realistic calculations of the charge quadrupole moment and its effect on elliptic flow coefficient differences between positive and negative charged particles, which can be tested by the beam energy scan program at RHIC.
