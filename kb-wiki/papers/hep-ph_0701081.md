# Restoration of chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetries in excited hadrons

> The effective restoration of $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ and $U(1)_A$ chiral symmetries of QCD in excited hadrons is reviewed. While the low-lying hadron spectrum is mostly shaped by the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry, in the high-lying hadrons the role of the quark condensate of the vacuum becomes negligible and the chiral symmetry is effectively restored. This implies that the mass generation mechanisms in the low- and high-lying hadrons are essentially different. The fundamental origin of this phenomenon is a suppression of quark quantum loop effects in high-lying hadrons relative to the classical contributions that preserve both chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetries. Microscopically the chiral symmetry breaking is induced by the dynamical Lorentz-scalar mass of quarks due to their coupling with the quark condensate of the vacuum. This mass is strongly momentum-dependent, however, and vanishes in the high-lying hadrons where the typical momentum of valence quarks is large. This physics is illustrated within the solvable chirally-symmetric and confining model. Effective Lagrangians for the approximate chiral multiplets at the hadron level are constructed which can be used as phenomenological effective field theories in the effective chiral restoration regime. Different ramifications and implications of the effective chiral restoration for the string description of excited hadrons, the decoupling of excited hadrons from the Goldstone bosons, the glueball - quark-antiquark mixing and the OZI rule violations are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in highly excited hadrons, the spontaneously broken chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetries of QCD become effectively restored, even though the vacuum remains in the Nambu-Goldstone mode; this effective restoration occurs because the valence quarks in high-lying states have large typical momenta and decouple from the quark condensate, making the dynamical Lorentz-scalar quark mass negligible.

## Method

The author reviews experimental evidence from partial wave analyses of proton-antiproton annihilation into mesons at LEAR (1.9–2.4 GeV) and from the nucleon excitation spectrum, comparing observed parity doubling patterns with predictions of chiral symmetry restoration. The theoretical mechanism is illustrated using a solvable, chirally-symmetric confining model (a large-\(N_c\) model with a quadratic or linear confining potential) where the Bethe-Salpeter equation for mesons is solved, showing that the chiral-symmetry-breaking dynamical quark mass arises from quantum loop effects and vanishes at large momenta characteristic of high-lying states.

## Key numbers

* Quark condensate at renormalization scale 1 GeV: \(\langle \bar u u \rangle \simeq \langle \bar d d \rangle \simeq -(240 \pm 10\ \text{MeV})^3\)
* Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation: \(m_\pi^2 = -\frac{1}{f_\pi^2} \frac{m_u + m_d}{2} (\langle \bar u u \rangle + \langle \bar d d \rangle) + O(m_{u,d}^2)\)
* Goldberger-Treiman relation: \(g_{\pi N N} = \frac{g_A M_N}{f_\pi}\), with experimental values \(g_{\pi N N} \simeq 13.4\) and \(\frac{g_A M_N}{f_\pi} \simeq 12.7\)
* Onset of parity doubling in nucleon spectrum begins approximately from 1.7 GeV region
* High-lying \(\bar n n\) mesons in the interval 1.7–2.4 GeV show approximate degeneracy of states with same spin, different isospins, and opposite parities
* Meson spectrum clusters follow \(M^2(n) = a n + b\), typical for an open string

## Context

This review extends the suggestion by Glozman (2001) that parity doubling in excited baryons reflects chiral symmetry restoration, and incorporates later work by Glozman and collaborators (2003–2006) classifying chiral multiplets for both mesons and baryons. It contrasts with the traditional view that confinement is Lorentz-scalar and breaks chiral symmetry increasingly higher in the spectrum, and with the nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry appropriate for low-lying hadrons (which obey approximate \(SU(6)_{FS} \times O(3)\) symmetry as shown by Dashen and Manohar in the large-\(N_c\) limit). The paper enables construction of effective Lagrangians for approximate chiral multiplets in the restoration regime, and predicts decoupling of excited hadrons from Goldstone bosons (\(g_{\pi N} \simeq 0\), \(g_A \simeq 0\)), suppression of glueball–\(\bar n n\) mixing, and strong OZI rule violation in \(J/\Psi\) decays.
