# Quantum Interference Effects in N* Electroproduction and Propagation in Nuclei

> We discuss the unexpected enhancement of the $N^*$ electroproduction on nuclei recently observed in the $(γ^*,pπ^-)$ measurement. A mechanism which is able to explain this result is proposed. To clarify the situation, we suggest to perform a new kind of experiment within well specified kinematic conditions.

# Quantum interference effects in $N^*$ electroproduction and propagation in nuclei

## Key claim
The unexpected enhancement of $N^*$ electroproduction on nuclei observed in the $(\gamma^*,p\pi^-)$ measurement at high missing momentum can be explained by quantum interference between direct resonance production and two-step processes where a nucleon or a higher-mass resonance is produced first and then converted into the detected $N^*$ via final state interactions. The effect depends critically on the sign and magnitude of the missing momentum, and the authors suggest that the enhancement is most pronounced at large negative missing momentum in parallel kinematics.

## Method
The authors employ a time-independent coupled-channel distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) formalism with a 3x3 optical potential coupling three channels: the direct $N^*(1535)$, the nucleon, and a representative upper mass state $N^*(1680)$. The production amplitudes $B_i$ are calculated from the bound nucleon momentum distribution (Woods-Saxon) and a Breit-Wigner resonance shape, with all vertex form factors assumed equal. The final state wave function is a coherent sum of waves emitted from any nuclear point, and the nondiagonal optical potential terms cause transitions between channels, with the relative phases of diagonal and nondiagonal amplitudes tested for two opposite possibilities.

## Key numbers
- At $W \approx 1530$ MeV and high missing momentum $p_m = 222$ MeV, the data to DWBA ratio is $1.9 \pm 0.3$; at $p_m = 56$ MeV this ratio is approximately 1.
- The nondiagonal final state interaction amplitudes $f_{ij}$ are chosen in modulus as 1/2 the diagonal ones, estimated from diffraction resonance production data.
- Calculations are carried out for $(e,e'N^*)$ on $^{27}Al$ with shells up to $l=2$ using harmonic oscillator wave functions.

## Context
This work extends the experimental findings of a recent $(\gamma^*,p\pi^-)$ measurement in the second resonance region, where the authors of that measurement suggested final state interactions between $N^*$ and the residual nucleus as a possible cause of the observed enhancement. The proposed mechanism is analogous to the regeneration phenomenon in the $K^o\overline K^o$ system. The authors suggest a new experiment measuring the asymmetry between positive and negative missing momenta, which would be zero in plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) and sensitive to the predicted interference effects.
