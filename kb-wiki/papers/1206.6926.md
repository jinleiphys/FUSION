# Superfluidity and entrainment in neutron-star crusts

> Despite the absence of viscous drag, the neutron superfluid permeating the inner crust of a neutron star can still be strongly coupled to nuclei due to non-dissipative entrainment effects. Neutron superfluidity and entrainment have been systematically studied in all regions of the inner crust of a cold non-accreting neutron star in the framework of the band theory of solids. It is shown that in the intermediate layers of the inner crust a large fraction of "free" neutrons are actually entrained by the crust. The results suggest that a revision of the interpretation of many observable astrophysical phenomena might be necessary.

# Superfluidity and entrainment in neutron-star crusts

## Key claim
The neutron superfluid in the inner crust of a neutron star is strongly coupled to nuclei via non-dissipative entrainment effects, not viscous drag, and in the intermediate layers a large fraction of "free" neutrons are actually entrained by the crust, suggesting that a revision of the interpretation of many observable astrophysical phenomena might be necessary.

## Method
The authors used the nuclear energy density functional (EDF) theory with Brussels-Montreal EDFs based on Skyrme effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, complemented with a microscopic pairing EDF, to study neutron superfluidity and entrainment in all regions of the inner crust of a cold non-accreting neutron star. The calculations were carried out in the framework of the band theory of solids, which properly accounts for the effects of the periodic crystal lattice on the neutron superfluid. The momentum-averaged neutron effective mass was systematically calculated across all regions of the inner crust.

## Key numbers
- Maximum pairing gap in homogeneous neutron matter: Δ ∼ 2 MeV, corresponding to a critical temperature T_c = exp(γ)Δ/(k_B π) ∼ 10^10 K.
- The critical temperature for superfluidity in the crust is reduced by ∼ 10–20% compared to pure neutron matter.
- The largest effects of Bragg diffraction are found at baryon densities n̄ ∼ 0.02–0.03 fm^−3, where the momentum-averaged neutron effective mass m_n^⋆ ∼ 14 m_n.
- The neutron-star crust calculations were performed at average baryon density n̄ = 0.06 fm^−3 for T = 0.8 T_c.
- The Brussels-Montreal EDFs fit 2149 nuclei with rms deviations below 0.6 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of nuclear superfluidity in homogeneous neutron matter by Chang (2004) and Gezerlis (2010), and builds on the band theory approach to neutron-star crusts developed by Chamel (2012). The results challenge the standard interpretation of large pulsar glitches, as previously discussed by Chamel and Carter (2006), and have implications for the thermal relaxation of crusts observed in persistent soft X-ray transients, as studied by Shternin (2007) and Brown (2009). The entrainment effects also affect the spectrum of collective excitations and their damping, as considered by Pethick (2010) and Cirigliano (2011).
