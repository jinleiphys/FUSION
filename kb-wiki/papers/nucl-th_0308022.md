# Correlations and Fluctuations, A Summary of Quark Matter 2002

## Key claim
The HBT puzzle persists at RHIC: all hydrodynamic, Boltzmann, and hybrid treatments consistently overestimate both the longitudinal source size \(R_{\rm long}\) and the ratio \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\), despite significant theoretical progress. The abstract's summary of "a consistent picture" glosses over the fact that no existing dynamical model can simultaneously reproduce the small emission duration (\(\Delta\tau < 10\) fm/c) and the large transverse expansion (outer radius 13 fm, velocity \(\sim 0.7c\)) implied by the data.

## Method
The paper summarizes experimental results presented at Quark Matter 2002, covering Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) interferometry for pions, kaons, photons, and baryon pairs; event-by-event fluctuations in transverse momentum and multiplicity; and charge fluctuations and balance functions. For HBT, Gaussian source radii (\(R_{\rm out}\), \(R_{\rm side}\), \(R_{\rm long}\)) were extracted from correlation functions and compared across beam energies (AGS, SPS, RHIC) and across experiments (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS, CERES, NA49). Phase-space densities were extracted model-independently by combining spectra with HBT radii via sum rules. Balance functions were constructed from the distribution of relative rapidity of \(\pi^+-\pi^-\) pairs.

## Key numbers
- \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) ratio at RHIC: \(\approx 0.85\) for PHENIX to \(\approx 1.15\) for PHOBOS.
- Upper limit on emission duration from HBT: \(\Delta\tau < 10\) fm/c.
- Blast wave analysis yields outer source radius of 13 fm and outer velocity \(\sim 0.7c\).
- Breakup temperature from blast wave systematics: \(\sim 110\) MeV.
- Pionic phase-space density at low momentum at RHIC: overpopulated by nearly a factor of two compared to a thermally equilibrated Bose gas.
- Entropy per pion extracted from STAR results: approximately three units, significantly lower than expected for a Bose gas.
- Two-photon HBT from WA98: 10% of photons are direct photons (not from \(\pi_0\) or \(\eta\) decays), inferred from the correlation intercept \(1+\lambda\).
- \(R_{\rm long}\) values at RHIC suggest emission times of 10 fm/c, rather than the expected 15–20 fm/c.
- Balance function width narrows with centrality at RHIC, consistent with delayed hadronization.

## Context
This conference summary reviews the state of HBT and fluctuation measurements at the transition from SPS to RHIC energies. The HBT puzzle, the failure of hydrodynamic models (Teaney, 2002; Huovinen, 2002; Soff, 2002; Kolb, 2002) to reproduce the small \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) and \(R_{\rm long}\), extends earlier discrepancies seen at SPS energies with RQMD. The low entropy per pion extracted from STAR data (Cramer, 2002) challenges the assumption of early thermalization of a quark-gluon plasma, suggesting instead coherent initial fields or under-populated quark degrees of freedom. The narrowing of charge balance functions with centrality (STAR, 2002) provides evidence for delayed hadronization, building on theoretical work by Jeon and Koch (2000) and Bass, Danielewicz, and Pratt (2000). The two-photon HBT result from WA98 (Aggarwal, 2002) opens a new direct-photon probe. Open questions include whether three-dimensional hydrodynamics, reduced surface emissivity, or super-cooling can resolve the HBT puzzle, and whether jet quenching can be distinguished from other sources of \(p_t\) fluctuations.
