# Parity Violation in Low-Energy np->d gamma and the Deuteron Anapole Moment

> Parity violation in low-energy nuclear observables is included in the pionless effective field theory. The model-independent relation between the parity-violating asymmetry in polarized np -> d gamma and the non-nucleon part of the deuteron anapole moment is discussed. The asymmetry in np -> d gamma computed with KSW power-counting, and recently criticized by Desplanques, is discussed.

## Key claim

The parity-violating asymmetry in polarized neutron-proton capture to deuteron and gamma, \(A_\gamma\), is related in a model-independent way to the non-nucleon part of the deuteron anapole moment at leading order in the pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields, but this relation is expected to hold only up to corrections of order \(Q \sim \gamma/m_\pi \sim 0.3\).

## Method

The author formulates parity-violating (PV) interactions in the two-nucleon sector using pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields (\(\nopit\)), which resums effective range contributions to all orders. The PV asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) in \(\vec n p \rightarrow d\gamma\) is computed from the interference between the strong M1 capture amplitude from the \(^1S_0\) channel and the weak E1 capture amplitude from the \(^3S_1\) channel via a \(^3S_1\)-\(^3P_1\) coupling parameterized by the unknown constant \(h_{33}^{(1)}\). The deuteron anapole moment is computed from single-nucleon anapole contributions and from the same \(h_{33}^{(1)}\) coupling, and the relation between the two observables is extracted at leading order.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry \(A_\gamma\) is defined by \(d\Gamma/d\cos\theta = \Gamma (1 + A_\gamma \cos\theta)\), where \(\theta\) is the angle between the emitted photon momentum and the neutron polarization direction.
- The deuteron binding momentum is \(\gamma = \sqrt{M_N B}\), with \(B\) the deuteron binding energy; the expansion parameter is \(Q \sim \gamma/m_\pi \sim 0.3\).
- The product \(\gamma r^{(^3S_1)} \sim 0.4\), where \(r^{(^3S_1)}\) is the effective range in the triplet channel, motivates the resummation in \(\nopit\).
- The single-nucleon anapole moments, if dominated by weak one-pion exchange, are given by \(A_n = A_p = -e g_A h_{\pi NN}^{(1)} M_N^2 / (48\pi f_\pi m_\pi)\) at leading order in the chiral expansion.
- In the zero-momentum transfer limit, the non-nucleon contribution to the deuteron anapole moment reduces to \(A_h \rightarrow -e h_{33}^{(1)} M_N^{3/2} / (8\sqrt{2\pi}) \cdot 1/(1-\gamma r^{(^3S_1)}) \cdot (\kappa_1 - 1/6)\), where \(\kappa_1\) is the isovector nucleon magnetic moment.

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory approach to parity violation in the two-nucleon sector, building on the dibaryon formalism of Kaplan (1997) and Beane and Savage (2000). It provides a model-independent relation between the PV asymmetry in \(\vec n p \rightarrow d\gamma\) and the deuteron anapole moment, which contrasts with the single-meson-exchange framework of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) that has shown inconsistencies with data from light nuclei such as \(^{18}F\). The result enables future measurements of \(A_\gamma\) (planned by Snow et al., 2000) to determine the weak coupling \(h_{33}^{(1)}\) and to test the relation against measurements of the nucleon and deuteron anapole moments, though corrections of order \(Q\) are expected.
