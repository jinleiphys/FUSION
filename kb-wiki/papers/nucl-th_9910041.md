# Coulomb wave function corrections for n-particle Bose-Einstein correlations

> The effect of multi-particle Coulomb final state interactions on higher-order intensity correlations is determined in general, based on a scattering wave function which is solution of the n-body Coulomb Schrödinger equation in (a large part of) the asymptotic region of n-body configuration space. In particular, we study Coulomb effects on the n-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions of similarly charged particles and remove a systematic error as big as 100% from higher-order multi-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a systematic quantum-mechanical method to correct for Coulomb final-state interactions in n-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions, showing that the previously used generalized Gamow correction method introduces systematic errors as large as 100% for 5-particle correlations when source radii are in the 5–10 fm range characteristic of heavy-ion collisions, though the Gamow approximation is acceptable at the 10% level for source radii of 1 fm typical of elementary particle reactions.

## Method

The authors construct an approximate n-body Coulomb scattering wave function as a product of two-body Coulomb wave functions for all particle pairs, valid in the asymptotic region Ω₀⁽ⁿ⁾ where all interparticle distances become uniformly large. They then compute the Coulomb correction factor K_Coulomb(Q_n) by numerically integrating the squared, symmetrized n-body wave function over Gaussian source distributions of width R, and compare these results to the generalized Gamow approximation K_Coulomb^(G)(Q_n) = ∏_{i<j} G_{ij}, where G_{ij} is the two-body Gamow penetration factor.

## Key numbers

- For a source radius R = 1 fm (characteristic of elementary particle reactions), the difference between the n-particle Gamow and Coulomb wave function corrections was smaller than 10% for n = 4 and 5 particles.
- For R = 5 fm, the 5-particle Coulomb wave function integration method yields a factor of 2 deviation from the generalized Gamow method (i.e., 100% systematic error).
- The validity condition for the n-particle Coulomb wave function is E_total^(n) ≥ 0.066 × [n(n-1)/2] × (ħc / R_G(fm)) MeV, which for n=3 and R_G = 4 fm gives a threshold of 10 MeV.
- For R = 1 fm, the generalized Gamow approximation is not reliable at the 5% level for the five-body Coulomb correction factor.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 3-body Coulomb correction treatment (Alt, Csörgő, Lörstad, Schmidt-Sørensen, 1999) to the general n-body case, providing a systematic alternative to the ad-hoc multi-particle generalization of the Gamow formula previously used in the field. The method enables precise extraction of source sizes from higher-order Bose-Einstein correlation measurements at RHIC (PHENIX and STAR detectors), which are essential for distinguishing fully chaotic from partially coherent particle sources, a distinction that requires controlling Coulomb corrections to better than the factor-of-2 difference expected between these scenarios in 5th-order correlations (as shown in prior work by the pion-laser model of Pratt, Csörgő, and Zimányi).
