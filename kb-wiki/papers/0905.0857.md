# Hyperon Axial Charges in Two-Flavor Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We use two-flavor heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to investigate the isovector axial charges of the spin one-half hyperons. Expressions for these hyperon axial charges are derived at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. We utilize phenomenological and lattice QCD inputs to assess the convergence of the two-flavor theory, which appears to be best for cascades.

## Key claim

The two-flavor chiral perturbation theory expansion for hyperon axial charges converges best for cascades (strangeness S=2), with convergence worsening as strangeness decreases; the empirical quark-mass extrapolation used in Lin (2007) is consistent with the SU(2) formula for the cascade and possibly for the sigma, but the situation for the nucleon is less clear.

## Method

The authors used two-flavor heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to compute the isovector axial charges of spin-1/2 hyperons (Sigma, Lambda-Sigma transition, and Xi) at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion. They combined tree-level and one-loop computations, including contributions from spin-3/2 resonances, and used both experimental inputs and lattice QCD results from Lin (2007) to estimate the chiral limit couplings and low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon axial charge at physical pion mass: G_A(139 MeV) = 1.27 (experimental input)
- Lambda-Sigma transition axial charge at physical pion mass: G_LambdaSigma(139 MeV) = 1.47 (experimental input)
- Sigma axial charge at physical pion mass: G_SigmaSigma(139 MeV) = 0.78 (from empirical extrapolation in Lin (2007))
- Xi axial charge at physical pion mass: G_XiXi(139 MeV) = 0.24 (from empirical extrapolation in Lin (2007))
- Chiral limit axial couplings estimated from lattice data at m_pi = 354 MeV: g_A = 1.18, g_SigmaSigma = 0.73, g_LambdaSigma = 1.29, g_XiXi = 0.23
- Local term estimates at the chiral symmetry breaking scale: A_NN(Lambda_chi) = -12.0, A_SigmaSigma(Lambda_chi) = -2.9, A_XiXi(Lambda_chi) = -0.22
- Mass splittings from experiment: Delta_LambdaSigma = 77 MeV, Delta_XiXi* = 215 MeV, Delta_LambdaSigma* = 270 MeV, Delta = 290 MeV (nucleon-Delta splitting)
- Pion decay constant: f = 132 MeV

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor chiral perturbation theory framework for hyperons developed by Tiburzi (2008) and applies it to axial charges, complementing earlier SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Jenkins (1990, 1991). The results enable controlled chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data for hyperon axial charges at smaller pion masses, particularly for the cascade sector where convergence is best. The paper identifies the need for lattice data at lower pion masses and lattice calculations of the Lambda-Sigma axial transition to verify the convergence pattern and determine parameters more precisely.
