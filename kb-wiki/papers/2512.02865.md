# Evidence of Spin-Interference Effects in Exclusive $J/Žł\to e^+e^-$ Photoproduction in Ultraperipheral Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We report the first evidence of spin interference in exclusive $J/Žł\to e^+e^-$ photoproduction in ultraperipheral Au+Au and isobar (Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr) collisions ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$~GeV) at STAR. A negative $\cos(2ŽĢ)$ modulation is observed for $p_T < 100$ MeV/$c$, opposite in sign to that in $Žü^{0}\!\to\!ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ photoproduction. This establishes for the first time that the interference sign is controlled by the spin structure of the final-state daughters, resolving the ambiguity present in the all-boson $Žü^0$ channel. The compact $J/Žł$ probes gluon distributions at perturbative scales, resulting in a weaker modulation and providing stringent constraints on Color Glass Condensate calculations. These findings demonstrate that spin-dependent interference in heavy vector mesons provides a new, experimentally accessible handle on gluon structure beyond traditional cross-section measurements.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first evidence of spin interference in exclusive $J/\psi \to e^+e^-$ photoproduction, establishing that the sign of the $\cos(2\phi)$ modulation is controlled by the spin structure of the final-state decay daughters: fermionic $e^+e^-$ daughters yield a negative modulation, opposite to the positive modulation observed in the bosonic $\rho^0 \to \pi^+\pi^-$ channel, thereby resolving an ambiguity that could not be addressed with the $\rho^0$ alone.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration analyzed ultraperipheral Au+Au and isobar (Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, selecting exclusive $J/\psi \to e^+e^-$ events with exactly two tracks and requiring electron identification via ionization energy loss ($dE/dx$) in the time projection chamber. The azimuthal angle $\phi$ between the $J/\psi$ momentum and the relative momentum of its decay daughters was measured, and the $\cos(2\phi)$ modulation amplitude $a_2$ was extracted by fitting the distribution to $1 + a_2 \cos(2\phi)$, with corrections for combinatorial background, detector acceptance, and Bremsstrahlung effects using STARlight Monte Carlo events embedded in a GEANT3-based detector simulation.

## Key numbers

- The measured $a_2$ for $J/\psi$ with $p_T < 100$ MeV/$c$ is $-0.120 \pm 0.032\ (\text{stat.}) \pm 0.022\ (\text{syst.})$ for Au+Au UPCs and $-0.123 \pm 0.061\ (\text{stat.}) \pm 0.045\ (\text{syst.})$ for isobaric UPCs.
- The peak modulation reaches $-0.190 \pm 0.063\ (\text{stat.}) \pm 0.044\ (\text{syst.})$ for Au+Au and $-0.220 \pm 0.080\ (\text{stat.}) \pm 0.060\ (\text{syst.})$ for isobars, about half the $\rho^0$ peak amplitude.
- The analysis uses integrated luminosities of 13.5 nb$^{-1}$ for Au+Au, 4 nb$^{-1}$ for Ru+Ru, and 3.9 nb$^{-1}$ for Zr+Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the spin-interference measurements previously reported by STAR (2022) for $\rho^0 \to \pi^+\pi^-$ photoproduction in Au+Au UPCs and confirmed by ALICE (2024) in Pb+Pb collisions, where the bosonic nature of both parent and daughters left the role of daughter spin ambiguous. The $J/\psi$ result provides the first explicit demonstration that the fermionic daughters determine the sign of the modulation, and the compact $J/\psi$ probes gluon distributions at perturbative scales, enabling stringent tests of Color Glass Condensate calculations (Mantysaari 2023; Brandenburg 2022) that were debated for the $\rho^0$ channel. The data constrain CGC models: Model-I reproduces the sign and approximate magnitude, while Model-II overestimates the soft-photon contribution at higher $p_T$, pointing to needed refinements for larger nuclei.
