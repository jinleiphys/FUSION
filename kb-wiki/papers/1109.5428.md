# Hyperon effects in covariant density functional theory with recent astrophysical observations

> Motivated by recent observational data, the equations of state with the inclusion of strangeness-bearing $Λ$-hyperons and the corresponding properties of neutron stars are studied, based on the covariant density functional (CDF) theory. To this end, we specifically employ the density dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory and the relativistic mean field theory (RMF). The inclusion of $Λ$-hyperons in neutron stars shows substantial effects in softening the equation of state. Because of the extra suppression effect originated from the Fock channel, large reductions on both the star mass and radius are predicted by the DDRHF calculations. It is also found that the mass-radius relations of neutron stars with $Λ$-hyperons determined by DDRHF with the PKA1 parameter set are in fairly good agreement with the observational data where a relatively small neutron stars radius is required. Therefore, it is expected that the exotic degrees of freedom such as the strangeness-bearing structure may appear and play significant roles inside the neutron stars, which is supported further by the systematical investigations on the consistency between the maximum neutron star mass and $Λ$-coupling strength.

# Hyperon effects in covariant density functional theory with recent astrophysical observations

## Key claim
The inclusion of Lambda-hyperons in neutron star matter softens the equation of state and reduces both the maximum mass and radius, with the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory predicting larger reductions than relativistic mean field (RMF) theory due to Fock channel contributions. The mass-radius relations from DDRHF with the PKA1 parameter set agree with observational data requiring relatively small neutron star radii, but none of the calculations except NL-SH reach the 2.0 solar mass constraint from PSR J1614-2230 when Lambda-hyperons are included with standard coupling strengths.

## Method
The authors employed covariant density functional theory, specifically the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory with PKA1 and PKO3 parameter sets, and the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with NL-SH, PK1, TW99, and PKDD parameter sets. For beta-stable stellar matter containing nucleons, Lambda-hyperons, electrons, and muons, they solved the chemical equilibrium conditions and Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations, using the BPS and BBP models for the low-density equation of state.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass with Lambda-hyperons for PKA1: 1.713 solar masses, radius 10.425 km, central density 1.314 fm^-3
- Maximum mass with Lambda-hyperons for TW99: 1.647 solar masses, radius 10.333 km, central density 1.292 fm^-3
- Maximum mass with Lambda-hyperons for NL-SH: 2.213 solar masses, radius 13.633 km, central density 0.700 fm^-3
- Maximum mass without Lambda-hyperons for PKA1: 2.423 solar masses, radius 12.354 km
- Maximum mass without Lambda-hyperons for TW99: 2.078 solar masses, radius 10.632 km
- Mass reduction due to Lambda-hyperons in DDRHF calculations: approximately 0.7 solar masses (PKA1 and PKO3)
- Mass reduction due to Lambda-hyperons in RMF calculations: 0.4 to 0.5 solar masses (except NL-SH at 0.6 solar masses)
- Threshold density for Lambda emergence in PKA1: 0.272 fm^-3
- Threshold density for Lambda emergence in TW99: 0.368 fm^-3
- Central density increase with Lambda-hyperons in DDRHF: 25% to 38% (about 0.25 to 0.50 fm^-3)
- Radius reduction with Lambda-hyperons for PKA1: approximately 2 km (from 12.354 km to 10.425 km)
- Meson-Lambda coupling ratios fixed at g_sigma-Lambda/g_sigma = 0.600 and g_omega-Lambda/g_omega = 0.653
- Lambda-hyperon mass: 1115.0 MeV

## Context
This work extends prior neutron star studies using DDRHF theory by Sun et al. (2008) to include strangeness-bearing Lambda-hyperons, motivated by observational constraints from Ozel et al. (2010) and Steiner et al. (2010) requiring relatively small neutron star radii (8.7-12.5 km for a 1.4 solar mass star). The results contradict calculations without hyperons, which predict larger radii, and support the possibility that exotic degrees of freedom appear inside neutron stars. The study also addresses the 2.0 solar mass constraint from Demorest et al. (2011) for PSR J1614-2230, finding that standard Lambda-coupling strengths make it difficult to simultaneously satisfy both the mass and radius constraints, though varying the coupling strength can accommodate the 2 solar mass limit.
