# Open system of interacting fermions: Statistical properties of cross sections and fluctuations

> Statistical properties of cross sections are studied for an open system of interacting fermions. The description is based on the effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian that accounts for the existence of open decay channels preserving the unitarity of the scattering matrix. The intrinsic interaction is modelled by the two-body random ensemble of variable strength. In particular, the crossover region from isolated to overlapping resonances accompanied by the effect of the width redistribution creating super-radiant and trapped states is studied in detail. The important observables, such as average cross section, its fluctuations, autocorrelation functions of the cross section and scattering matrix, are very sensitive to the coupling of the intrinsic states to the continuum around the crossover. A detailed comparison is made of our results with standard predictions of statistical theory of cross sections, such as the Hauser-Feshbach formula for the average cross section and Ericson theory of fluctuations and correlations of cross sections. Strong deviations are found in the crossover region, along with the dependence on intrinsic interactions and degree of chaos inside the system.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the statistical properties of cross sections in an open system of interacting fermions depend strongly on the strength of intrinsic interactions and the degree of chaos inside the system, particularly in the crossover region from isolated to overlapping resonances, contradicting the standard Hauser-Feshbach and Ericson theories which assume such properties are independent of internal dynamics.

## Method

The authors model an open system of interacting fermions using an effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian that accounts for coupling to open decay channels while preserving unitarity of the scattering matrix. The intrinsic Hamiltonian is modeled by the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) with variable interaction strength, and the decay amplitudes are taken as random independent Gaussian variables. Numerical simulations are performed for N=924 internal states (n=6 fermions on m=12 orbitals) with up to M=25 equivalent decay channels, and results are compared with predictions from Hauser-Feshbach theory and Ericson fluctuation theory.

## Key numbers

- System dimension: N=924 internal many-body states (n=6 fermions, m=12 orbitals)
- Critical interaction strength for onset of chaos: λ_cr ≈ 1/20 (from Eq. estimate)
- Number of decay channels: up to M=25, giving M/N ≈ 2×10⁻²
- Energy interval for analysis: [-0.2, 0.2] at center of spectrum
- Number of realizations: N_r=30 for each value of κ
- Elastic enhancement factor F varies between 3 and 2 as κ increases from zero
- For GOE case and λ→∞, Hauser-Feshbach formula with F=2 describes elastic fluctuational cross section
- For λ=0, elastic enhancement factor F=2.5; for λ=1/30, F=2.25

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Celardo et al., 2006) on width statistics in open fermionic systems by now analyzing cross section fluctuations and correlations. It directly compares numerical results with the standard Hauser-Feshbach formula (Hauser and Feshbach, 1952) for average cross sections and Ericson theory (Ericson, 1963; Brink and Stephen, 1963) for fluctuations and correlations. The paper shows that the elastic enhancement factor depends on the degree of chaos inside the system, leading to deviations from Hauser-Feshbach predictions, and that the assumption of negligible width fluctuations for large numbers of channels is incorrect. The results enable improved statistical models of nuclear reactions that account for the interplay between intrinsic dynamics and continuum coupling.
