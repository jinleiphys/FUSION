# Fermion Pair Production From an Electric Field Varying in Two Dimensions

> The Hamiltonian describing fermion pair production from an arbitrarily time-varying electric field in two dimensions is studied using a group-theoretic approach. We show that this Hamiltonian can be encompassed by two, commuting SU(2) algebras, and that the two-dimensional problem can therefore be reduced to two one-dimensional problems. We compare the group structure for the two-dimensional problem with that previously derived for the one-dimensional problem, and verify that the Schwinger result is obtained under the appropriate conditions.

# Fermion Pair Production From an Electric Field Varying in Two Dimensions

## Key claim

The Hamiltonian for fermion pair production from an arbitrarily time-varying electric field in two spatial dimensions can be decomposed into two commuting SU(2) algebras, reducing the two-dimensional problem to two independent one-dimensional problems; however, this reduction holds only for the specific gauge choice A₀ = 0 and requires that the field be spatially homogeneous.

## Method

The authors start from the interaction Hamiltonian for fermions in an electric field in the gauge A₀ = 0, expressed in terms of creation and annihilation operators for free Dirac fields. They construct operators J₊⁽ⁱ⁾, J₋⁽ⁱ⁾, J₀, and Q that form an SO(4) algebra, then form linear combinations to obtain two commuting SU(2) algebras (denoted I and T) in the j = 1/2 representation. The Hamiltonian is rewritten as a linear combination of elements from these two algebras and diagonalized via a Bogoliubov transformation, which is expressed as a product of two operators R₁ and R₂, each acting on only one of the two SU(2) algebras. The time evolution operator Ũ factorizes into Ũ₁Ũ₂, yielding independent differential equations for the coefficients z₁ and z₂, which can be solved numerically to obtain the pair production rate.

## Key numbers

- The Schwinger formula for the rate of fermion pair production from a uniform, static electric field is ω = (αE²/π²) Σ_{n=1}^{∞} (1/n²) exp(-nπm²/|eE|), where m is the fermion mass and e is the fermion charge.
- For a uniform static electric field oriented at an angle θ to the x-axis, the parameter ˙α reduces to ˙α = (e k⊥ E₀)/ε², where k⊥ = √(k₀² − k∥²) and k∥ = (k·E)/|k|.
- When the vector potential A and electric field E are parallel, ˙γ = 0, and the two-dimensional differential equations reduce to the one-dimensional case.
- The probability amplitude for producing no pairs is |S₀|² = exp[−∫ d³k log((1+|z₁|²)(1+|z₂|²))].

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous group-theoretic approach for one-dimensional time-varying electric fields (Balantekin, 1995) to two spatial dimensions, where the Hamiltonian contains two Dirac γ-matrices instead of one. The SU(2) algebras derived here are distinct from those considered by Perelomov, as they follow from completeness and orthogonality of Dirac spinors rather than from the γ-matrices themselves. The formalism enables numerical calculation of pair production rates for arbitrary time-varying fields in two dimensions, and the authors verify consistency by recovering the Schwinger result for the special case of a uniform static field.
