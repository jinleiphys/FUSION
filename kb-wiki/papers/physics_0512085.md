# Large-N droplets in two dimensions

> Using lattice effective field theory, we study the ground state binding energy of N distinct particles in two dimensions with equal mass interacting weakly via an attractive SU(N)-symmetric short range potential. We find that in the limit of zero range and large N, the ratio of binding energies B_{N}/B_{N-1} approaches the value 8.3(6).

## Key claim

Using lattice effective field theory with a discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to regulate implicit N-body contact interactions, the ratio of binding energies B_N/B_{N-1} for N distinct particles in two dimensions with SU(N)-symmetric short-range attraction approaches 8.3(6) in the large-N limit, which is lower than the classical prediction of approximately 8.567 from Hammer (2004).

## Method

The author performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations using lowest-order lattice effective field theory with three different lattice actions (standard, O(a^2)-improved, and O(a^4)-improved kinetic energies) to compute ground state binding energies for N ≤ 10 particles. A discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation was employed to regulate the implicit N-body contact interaction that arises when multiple particles occupy the same lattice site. The simulations used a heat bath/Metropolis update procedure with roughly 2×10^5 successful updates per lattice site, and the ratio B_N/B_{N-1} was extrapolated to the large-N limit using fits with up to quadratic dependence on 1/N.

## Key numbers

- The large-N limit of B_N/B_{N-1} is 8.3(6), determined from extrapolation of lattice data for N ≤ 10
- For N=3, the known precise value of B_3/B_2 = 16.522688(1) from Hammer (2004)
- For N=4, the known value of B_4/B_2 = 197.3(1) from Platter (2004)
- The classical prediction for the large-N limit from Hammer (2004) is approximately 8.567
- The spatial lattice spacing was fixed at a = (40 MeV)^{-1}, corresponding to cutoff momentum Λ = πa^{-1} ≈ 126 MeV and cutoff energy Λ^2/m = 16.8 MeV
- The temporal lattice spacing was varied with values a_t = (16 MeV)^{-1}, (20 MeV)^{-1}, (30 MeV)^{-1}, and (40 MeV)^{-1}, corresponding to ε values of 3.0, 3.7, 5.6, and 7.5 respectively
- The two-particle binding energy B_2 was scaled as 2×10^{2-N} MeV for the main comparison of actions, with additional values of 3×10^{2-N} MeV and 1×10^{2-N} MeV tested

## Context

This work extends the study of few-body systems in two dimensions with zero-range interactions, following precise calculations of B_3/B_2 by Bruch (1979), Nielsen (1999), and Hammer (2004), and the first precise determination of B_4/B_2 by Platter (2004). The paper addresses the discrepancy between the classical large-N prediction of Hammer (2004) and the difficulty of reaching the zero-range limit for large N, as noted in diffusion Monte Carlo studies by Blume (2004) which found range corrections too large for N ≤ 7. The results enable future lattice studies of weakly attractive N-boson systems in two-dimensional optical lattices and raise questions about the convergence of effective field theory in the combined zero-range and large-N limits.
