# The Regge-plus-resonance model for kaon production on the proton and the neutron

> The Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) framework for kaon photoproduction on the proton and the neutron is an economical single-channel model with very few parameters. Not only does the RPR model allow one to extract resonance information from the data, it has predictive power. As an example we show that the RPR model makes fair predictions for the $p(e,e'K^{+})Λ$ and the $n(γ,K^{+})Σ^{-}$ observables starting from amplitudes optimized for the reaction $p(γ, K ^{+})Λ$ and $p(γ,K^{+})Σ^{0}$ respectively.

## Key claim

The Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) model can make fair predictions for kaon photoproduction on the neutron and for electroproduction on the proton, but only when starting from amplitudes already optimized for the corresponding proton photoproduction channels; the predictive power is heavily restrained by uncertainties in the helicity amplitudes for neutron resonances.

## Method

The RPR model describes the background for kaon photoproduction by reggeizing the Feynman diagrams for pseudoscalar K+(494) and vector K*+(892) meson exchange in the t-channel, using only three parameters. Resonance contributions from s-channel nucleon resonances are added at the amplitude level using standard Feynman diagrams, with parameters tuned against p(γ,K+)Λ data. For neutron targets, the coupling constants are converted using isospin symmetry, and the photocoupling helicity amplitudes from analyses of pion production data (SAID, RPP) are used to relate proton and neutron electromagnetic vertices.

## Key numbers

- The background for p(γ,K+)Λ is described by a mere three parameters, fully determining the background contribution.
- The background parameters are optimized against p(γ,K+)Λ data for Eγ ≥ 5 GeV (RPR-2007 version) or using high-energy Jefferson Lab data (RPR-2011 version).
- Systematic errors on the n(γ,K+)Σ− photon asymmetry data amount to |ΔΣ| ≈ 0.2.
- The RPR-2007 model considers contributions from five resonances: S11(1650), P11(1710), P13(1720), P13(1900), and D13(1900).
- The RPR-2011 model additionally studies the role of S11(1535), D15(1675), F15(1680), D13(1700), F15(2000), and P11(1900).

## Context

This work extends the Regge-plus-resonance framework developed by the Ghent group (Ryckebusch et al., earlier RPR papers) for kaon photoproduction on the proton to neutron targets and electroproduction channels. It contrasts with coupled-channel approaches, which are more demanding on human resources and involve a large number of parameters. The RPR model provides the elementary production operator for a covariant model of d(γ,KY)N reactions (Ryckebusch et al., deuteron model), enabling studies of YN final-state interactions and hypernuclear calculations.
