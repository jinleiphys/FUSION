# Electromagnetic Field Produced in High Energy Small Collision System within Charge Density Models of Nucleon

> Recent experiments show that $Δγ$, an observable designed for detecting the chiral magnetic effect (CME), in small collision system $p+A$ is similar with that in heavy ion collision $A+A$. This brings a challenge to the existence of CME because it is believed that there is no azimuthal correlation between the orientation of the magnetic field ($Φ_B$) and the participant plane ($Φ_2$) in small collision system. In this work, we introduce three charge density models to describe the inner charge distributions of proton and neutron, and calculate the electric and magnetic fields produced in small $p+A$ collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies. Our results show that the contribution of the single projectile proton to the magnetic field is the main source after average over all participants. The azimuthal correlation between $Φ_B$ and $Φ_2$ is small but not vanished. And due to the huge fluctuation of fields strength, the magnetic-field contribution to $Δγ$ could be large.

## Key claim

Using realistic charge density models for the nucleon (proton and neutron), the authors show that in small p+A collision systems, the azimuthal correlation between the magnetic field orientation and the participant plane is small but not zero, and that large fluctuations in field strength mean the magnetic-field contribution to the CME observable Δγ could be significant, contradicting the previous belief that this correlation vanishes.

## Method

The authors calculate the electromagnetic fields in p+A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies using the Lienard-Wiechert potentials, summing over all charged particles in the system. They implement three charge density models for the nucleon: a Point-Like model, a Hard-Sphere model with uniform charge density (radius 0.88 fm), and a Charge-Profile model derived from experimental electromagnetic form factors for both protons and neutrons. The collision geometry uses a Woods-Saxon distribution for the target nucleus and a Monte-Carlo Glauber model to determine participants, with fields evaluated at the center of mass of all participants.

## Key numbers

* For a single 100 GeV proton, the magnetic field By at the proton center (r=0, z=0) is exactly 0 in the Charge-Profile model, while the Point-Like model gives a divergent result at that point.
* The averaged fluctuation of magnetic field strength in p+Au at RHIC energy using the Point-Like model is approximately 80 times larger than the field strength in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy.
* In the Hard-Sphere model for p+Au at RHIC energy, the total magnetic field By reaches a maximum at impact parameter b ≈ 6.5 fm.
* The magnetic field strength By from the Charge-Profile model in p+Au is about half of that reported in the prior work by Belmont (2016).
* At LHC energy (p+Pb), the B²-weighted azimuthal correlation B²·cos2(Φ_B - Φ_2) is three orders of magnitude larger than at RHIC energy.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of electromagnetic fields in small collision systems by Zhao (2018), who treated nucleons as point-like charges with a distance cut-off, and by Belmont (2016), who used a Gaussian charge distribution with σ = 0.4 fm. Both prior studies found no correlation between magnetic field direction and participant plane. The present work introduces more realistic charge density models from experimental form factors (Miller 2010, Alberico 2008) and finds a non-vanishing azimuthal correlation, which could help clarify whether the observed Δγ in small systems (CMS 2017, 2016) is due to the chiral magnetic effect or to elliptic-flow backgrounds.
