# Goldberger-Treiman relation and Wu-type experiment in the decuplet sector

> The leading-order chiral Lagrangian for the baryon octet and decuplet states coupled to Goldstone bosons and external sources contains six low-energy constants. Five of them are fairly well known from phenomenology, but the sixth one is practically unknown. This coupling constant provides the strength of the (p-wave) coupling of Goldstone bosons to decuplet states. Its size and even sign are under debate. Quark model and QCD for a large number of colors provide predictions, but some recent phenomenological analyses suggest even an opposite sign for the Delta-pion coupling. The Goldberger-Treiman relation connects this coupling constant to the axial charge of the Delta baryon. This suggests a Wu-type experiment to determine the unknown low-energy constant. While this is not feasible in the Delta sector because of the large hadronic width of the Delta, there is a flavor symmetry related process that is accessible: the weak semileptonic decay of the Omega baryon to a spin 3/2 cascade baryon. A broad research program is suggested that can pin down at least the rough size and the sign of the last unknown low-energy constant of the leading-order Lagrangian. It encompasses experimental measurements, in particular the forward-backward asymmetry of the semileptonic decay, together with a determination of the quark-mass dependences using lattice QCD for the narrow decuplet states and chiral perturbation theory to extrapolate to the Delta sector. Besides discussing the strategy of the research program, the present work provides a feasibility check based on a simple leading-order calculation.

# Goldberger-Treiman relation and Wu-type experiment in the decuplet sector

## Key claim

The sixth low-energy constant \(H_A\) of the leading-order chiral Lagrangian, which governs the p-wave coupling of Goldstone bosons to decuplet baryons and whose sign is debated between quark-model/large-\(N_c\) predictions and recent phenomenological analyses, can be determined by measuring the weak semileptonic decay \(\Omega^- \to \Xi^{*0} \, \ell^- \bar\nu_\ell\) rather than the inaccessible \(\Delta\) weak decay, provided that flavor-symmetry breaking does not flip the sign between the \(\Omega\)-\(\Xi^*\)-\(K\) and \(\Delta\)-\(\Delta\)-\(\pi\) coupling constants.

## Method

The authors construct the leading-order chiral Lagrangian for octet and decuplet baryons coupled to Goldstone bosons and external sources, which contains six low-energy constants: \(m_{(8)}\), \(m_{(10)}\), \(D\), \(F\), \(h_A\), and \(H_A\). They derive the Feynman matrix element for the semileptonic decay \(\Omega^- \to \Xi^{*0} \, \ell^- \bar\nu_\ell\) in terms of \(H_A\) and the vector coupling \(c_V\), then compute the decay width and forward-backward asymmetry for electrons and muons using a leading-order calculation with \(\vert H_A \vert = 2\) as a sample value.

## Key numbers

- For \(\ell = e\), \(H_A = +2\): branching ratio \(\Gamma_{\Omega \to \Xi^* e \bar\nu_e} / \Gamma_{\Omega,\text{tot}} = 1.2 \times 10^{-4}\); forward-backward asymmetry \(\Gamma_{\text{fb}} / \Gamma_{\Omega \to \Xi^* e \nu} = +0.011\)
- For \(\ell = e\), \(H_A = -2\): branching ratio \(1.2 \times 10^{-4}\); forward-backward asymmetry \(-0.012\)
- For \(\ell = e\), \(H_A = 0\): branching ratio \(6.7 \times 10^{-5}\); forward-backward asymmetry \(-0.00043\)
- For \(\ell = \mu\), \(H_A = +2\): branching ratio \(4.3 \times 10^{-6}\); forward-backward asymmetry \(-0.23\)
- For \(\ell = \mu\), \(H_A = -2\): branching ratio \(4.3 \times 10^{-6}\); forward-backward asymmetry \(-0.25\)
- For \(\ell = \mu\), \(H_A = 0\): branching ratio \(2.5 \times 10^{-6}\); forward-backward asymmetry \(-0.33\)
- Large-\(N_c\) two-flavor QCD estimate: \(H_A = \frac95 g_A \approx 2.27\) (Pascalutsa 2006, Ledwig 2011)
- Large-\(N_c\) three-flavor QCD estimate: \(H_A = 9F - 3D \approx 1.74\) (Dashen 1994, Semke 2005)
- The \(\Xi^{*0}\) width is \(9\ \text{MeV}\), narrow enough for traditional lattice analysis treating it as stable

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework of Jenkins (1991) and the decuplet Lagrangian of Lutz (2001), Semke (2005), Pascalutsa (2006), and Ledwig (2014) by addressing the poorly known constant \(H_A\). It contrasts with indirect determinations from pion-nucleon scattering (Yao 2016, Unal 2021) that suggest a sign opposite to quark-model predictions. The proposed research program combines experimental measurement of \(\Omega^- \to \Xi^{*0} \, \ell^- \bar\nu_\ell\) at facilities like BESIII or LHCb with lattice QCD calculations of the form factors and chiral perturbation theory extrapolations to the \(\Delta\) sector, enabling a model-independent determination of the sign and size of \(H_A\) and testing the Goldberger-Treiman relation across strangeness sectors.
