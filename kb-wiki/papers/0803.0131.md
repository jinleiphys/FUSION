# Non-thermal fixed points: effective weak-coupling for strongly correlated systems far from equilibrium

> Strongly correlated systems far from equilibrium can exhibit scaling solutions with a dynamically generated weak coupling. We show this by investigating isolated systems described by relativistic quantum field theories for initial conditions leading to nonequilibrium instabilities, such as parametric resonance or spinodal decomposition. The non-thermal fixed points prevent fast thermalization if classical-statistical fluctuations dominate over quantum fluctuations. We comment on the possible significance of these results for the heating of the early universe after inflation and the question of fast thermalization in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

Strongly correlated systems far from equilibrium can approach scaling solutions with a dynamically generated weak coupling, but only when classical-statistical fluctuations dominate over quantum fluctuations; the non-thermal fixed points that arise prevent fast thermalization and are unstable with respect to quantum corrections, which eventually lead to thermalization.

## Method

The authors study a relativistic scalar N-component quantum field theory with quartic self-interaction following a parametric resonance instability, using the two-particle irreducible (2PI) large-N expansion to next-to-leading order. They analytically determine fixed point solutions of the evolution equations for correlation functions in the classical-statistical field theory limit, employing generalized Zakharov transformations to derive algebraic conditions for scaling exponents. They then compare these analytic predictions to classical-statistical field theory simulations on a lattice at late times.

## Key numbers

- The primary resonant amplification occurs for transverse modes in the momentum range \(0 \le {\bf p}^2 \le \sigma_0^2/2\).
- In the non-linear regime, \(F_{\perp} \sim {\cal O} (N^{1/3}\lambda^{-2/3})\); exponential growth stops when \(F_{\perp} \sim F_{\parallel} \sim {\cal O} (N^0\, \lambda^{-1})\).
- Two non-thermal fixed point solutions for the infrared scaling exponent are found: \(\alpha = 4\) and \(\alpha = 5\).
- For high momenta, the perturbative scaling exponent is \(\alpha = 3/2\).
- The occupation number at late times (t=8000) follows \(n(t,{\bf p})\sim |{\bf p}|^{-4}\) for low momenta and \(n(t,{\bf p})\sim |{\bf p}|^{-3/2}\) for higher momenta.
- The classical-statistical simulations use coupling \(\lambda = 10^{-5}\) and \(N=4\).
- In the context of heavy-ion collisions, classical simulations indicate possible fast isotropization due to instabilities in about 1-2 fm/c for low momenta of less than 1-2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of nonequilibrium instabilities in quantum field theory, previously investigated for parametric resonance by Berges (2002) and tachyonic preheating by Arrizabalaga (2004), by demonstrating that the non-perturbative regime after an instability exhibits scaling solutions with a dynamically generated weak coupling. The results contradict the assumption that perturbative analysis with apparent success in this regime (Micha 2002, Arnold 2005) captures the full physics, as the non-thermal fixed points found here arise from non-perturbative resummation effects. The findings enable a better understanding of thermalization delays in early universe reheating after inflation and in heavy-ion collision experiments, where QCD plasma instabilities may lead to similar scaling behavior.
