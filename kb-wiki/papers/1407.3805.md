# Double ratio of charmonia in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV

> Based on a kinetic description of J/psi dissociation and production in an expanding quark-gluon plasma that is described by a 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamics, we have studied the double ratio R_{p Pb}(psi')/R_{p Pb}(J/psi) of charmonia in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV by including not only the cold nuclear matter effects but also the hot nuclear matter effects. We find that the double ratio of prompt charmonia is significantly suppressed in the most central collisions as a result of the hot nuclear matter effects.

# Double ratio of charmonia in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV

## Key claim

The double ratio R_{pPb}(psi')/R_{pPb}(J/psi) of prompt charmonia is significantly suppressed in the most central 10% p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV due to hot nuclear matter effects, with the suppression being much stronger than what cold nuclear matter effects alone would produce. The authors note that cold nuclear matter effects are not expected to differ much between J/psi and psi' because their formation times are much longer than the time for the proton and Pb to pass through each other.

## Method

The authors used a 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model to describe the bulk dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in p+Pb collisions, with initial conditions from the AMPT model and an equation of state based on an ideal gas of massless partons and massive hadrons with a bag constant (critical temperature T_c=165 MeV). They solved a transport equation for J/psi and psi' including gluon dissociation and regeneration via detailed balance, with a velocity-dependent dissociation temperature to describe color screening, and incorporated canonical enhancement and relaxation reduction factors for charm quarks that are not in thermal equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial energy density: epsilon_0 ≈ 15 GeV/fm^3 at rapidity y=-2 in the most central 10% collisions, corresponding to initial temperature T_0 ≈ 290 MeV
- Initial time for hydrodynamics: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Critical temperature: T_c = 165 MeV at zero baryon chemical potential
- J/psi production cross section in pp collisions at 5.02 TeV: dσ_pp/dy = 5.68 μb, obtained from interpolation between sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and 7 TeV
- Average transverse momentum square: ⟨p_T^2⟩ = 9.5 GeV^2 at mid-rapidity
- Charm quark relaxation time: tau_r = 7 fm/c
- Proton radius for initial J/psi spatial distribution: r = 0.8 fm
- Prompt double ratio in most central 10% collisions: smaller than 0.1
- Prompt double ratio in 40-60% centrality collisions: between 0.3 and 0.4
- Inclusive double ratio (including B meson decay) in 40-60% centrality: above 0.4 in the whole rapidity range
- Feed-down proportions for J/psi production in pp collisions: 6:3:1 for direct J/psi, feed-down from chi_c, and feed-down from psi'

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Liu 2013) of charmonia in p+Pb collisions using a hydrodynamic model, now focusing specifically on the double ratio of psi' to J/psi as a cleaner probe of hot medium effects. The study builds on the original suggestion of charmonia suppression as a QGP signature (Matsui 1986) and prior work on cold nuclear matter effects including shadowing, the Cronin effect, and nuclear absorption studied in p+A and d+A collisions. The results suggest that measuring the double ratio of charmonia in p+Pb collisions at the LHC can distinguish hot medium effects from cold nuclear matter effects, enabling tests of whether a QGP is formed in these smaller collision systems.
