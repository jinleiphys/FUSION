# Relativistic Hamiltonians in many-body theories

> We discuss the description of a many-body nuclear system using Hamiltonians that contain the nucleon relativistic kinetic energy and potentials with relativistic corrections. Through the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation, the field theoretical problem of interacting nucleons and mesons is mapped to an equivalent one in terms of relativistic potentials, which are then expanded at some order in 1/m_N. The formalism is applied to the Hartree problem in nuclear matter, showing how the results of the relativistic mean field theory can be recovered over a wide range of densities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation can systematically map the relativistic field-theoretical problem of interacting nucleons and mesons onto an equivalent Hamiltonian formulation with relativistic potentials expanded in powers of 1/m_N, recovering the results of relativistic mean field theory in the Hartree problem over a wide range of densities, provided that self-consistency is properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors apply the Foldy-Wouthuysen unitary transformation to the nuclear many-body Green's function formalism, decoupling the large and small components of the Dirac wave function and projecting onto positive-energy states. They then expand the resulting relativistic potentials to second order in 1/m_N, comparing two reduction schemes: the Breit reduction and the minimal relativity prescription (used in the Bonn potential). The formalism is applied to the Hartree self-consistency problem in nuclear matter, solving for the effective nucleon mass m_N* as a function of Fermi momentum using scalar (sigma) and vector (omega) meson exchange.

## Key numbers

- The effective mass m_N* from the approximate self-consistency equation m_N* = m_N - rho * (g_s^2/m_s^2) * (1 - 3 k_F^2/(10 m_N*^2)) agrees remarkably well with the exact relativistic mean field result, with the two curves practically indistinguishable in the non-self-consistent case.
- For the Breit reduced potential, the non-relativistic effective mass formula yields m_N* = m_N / (1 + (rho/m_N) * (g_s^2/m_s^2)).
- For the minimal relativity potential, the non-relativistic effective mass formula yields m_N* = m_N / (1 + (rho/(2 m_N)) * (g_s^2/m_s^2 + g_v^2/m_v^2)).
- At k_F = 225 MeV/c (corresponding to 12C), the non-relativistic m_N* differs from the exact relativistic value by less than 3%.
- The expansion in 1/m_N breaks down above k_F approximately 300 MeV/c.
- The MFT coupling constants are g_s^2/4pi = 7.29 (m_s = 550 MeV) and g_v^2/4pi = 10.84 (m_v = 783 MeV); the Bonn potential values are g_s^2/4pi = 7.7823 and g_v^2/4pi = 20.0.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Barbaro et al. (1995), who evaluated the quasielastic charge response using the Bonn potential expanded to 1/m_N^2, and of Forest et al. (1995), who used relativistic Hamiltonians in variational Monte Carlo calculations. The paper provides a systematic framework for 1/m_N expansions in many-body systems, enabling future calculations of correlations beyond the mean field (such as random phase approximation) while maintaining consistency between the kinetic energy dispersion relation and the potential expansion scheme.
