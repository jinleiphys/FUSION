# Static linear response of hot and dense QCD matter to electromagnetic fields: Leading hard and soft QCD corrections

> We compute the static electromagnetic susceptibilities of a hot and dense quark-gluon plasma using perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). Our evaluation includes the leading $\mathcal{O}(α_s)$ correction as well as the leading soft, resummed contribution of $\mathcal{O}(α_s^{3/2})$ within electrostatic QCD. By matching to Lattice QCD at vanishing baryon chemical potential through Lattice perturbation theory, we establish a connection between perturbative results and Lattice simulations and assess the size of higher-order corrections. This extends the electromagnetic susceptibilities to finite baryon chemical potential, where Lattice methods are not applicable and establishes first-principle constraints on the quark-gluon plasma's electromagnetic response at temperatures and densities relevant for intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper computes the static electromagnetic susceptibilities of hot and dense quark-gluon plasma including the leading QCD correction at order α_s and the leading soft resummed contribution at order α_s^{3/2}, but the results are only applicable in the region where 2πT ≳ m_E (the Debye screening mass) in addition to the weak coupling regime, and the agreement with Lattice QCD data is limited to temperatures where perturbative QCD may not be fully reliable.

## Method

The authors evaluate two-loop Feynman diagrams for the photon polarization tensor in the static and homogeneous limit using integration-by-parts reduction at finite temperature and density, with fermionic Matsubara modes including a chemical potential shift. They compute the hard QCD correction at O(α_s) and the soft contribution from Electrostatic QCD (EQCD) at O(α_s^{3/2}), then match their perturbative results to Lattice QCD at vanishing baryon chemical potential through Lattice perturbation theory to determine the renormalization scale Λ_IR = 249(18) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The renormalization scale determined by matching to Lattice QCD is Λ_IR = 249(18) MeV
- The Lattice QCD subtraction scale used in the matching is μ_QED = 115(8) MeV from Bali (2020)
- The strong coupling reference value used is α_s(2 GeV) = 0.2994
- The Debye screening mass is m_E^2 = (4π α_s/3) { (N_c + N_f/2) T^2 + (3/(2π^2)) N_f μ_q^2 }
- The magnetic susceptibility at O(α_s^{3/2}) is χ|_O(α_s^{3/2}) = N_c C_F α_s m_E Q^2 Z(z) / (48 π^4 T)
- The electric susceptibility at O(α_s^{3/2}) is ξ|_O(α_s^{3/2}) = -χ|_O(α_s^{3/2})
- The leading log T coefficient is χ|_log T = -ξ|_log T = (N_c Q^2 / (12 π^2)) (1 + (α_s/π)(3C_F/4)) log(T^2)

## Context

This work extends the leading-order perturbative calculations of electromagnetic susceptibilities by Ferreira (2023) and Endrodi (2022) to include the first QCD corrections, and provides the first controlled first-principles determination at finite baryon chemical potential where Lattice QCD methods are inapplicable due to the Sign Problem. The results explicitly verify the conjecture by Bali (2014, 2020) and Endrodi (2023) that the coefficient of the leading log T behavior is proportional to the QED beta function with QCD corrections, and enable quantitative predictions for the electromagnetic response of quark-gluon plasma in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collision programs such as the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC and experiments at SPS, NICA, and FAIR.
