# Hadron gas with repulsive mean field

> We study the QCD equation of state, and fluctuations of baryon number and strangeness using the hadron resonance gas model with repulsive mean field. We find that including both the predicted but not observed resonances, a.k.a. missing states, and the repulsive mean field into the resonance gas model leads to better description of the lattice results. The repulsive mean field is particularly important for the higher order baryon number fluctuations.

## Key claim

Including both missing (predicted but not observed) resonances and a repulsive mean field in the hadron resonance gas model leads to a better description of lattice QCD results for the equation of state and fluctuations of baryon number and strangeness, but the quantitative agreement depends on the specific value of the mean-field parameter and whether baryon resonances are also affected by the mean field.

## Method

The authors used the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with a repulsive mean field, where the mean-field parameter K was fixed by comparing the virial expansion of a nucleon gas to the mean-field approach. They included both the Particle Data Group (PDG) resonance list and an extended list of missing states from quark model calculations (QM-HRG), and applied the repulsive mean field to both ground state baryons and baryon resonances. The model was then compared to lattice QCD results for the trace anomaly, second-order baryon number and strangeness fluctuations, and fourth- and sixth-order baryon number fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The mean-field parameter K = 250 MeV/fm^3 was used as the realistic value, with the largest value allowed by virial expansion being K = 450 MeV/fm^3.
- At temperatures above 150 MeV, the expanded mean-field expression is no longer accurate and exact self-consistent mean-field calculations are required.
- The QM-HRG model with missing states overshoots lattice results for baryon number fluctuations at T >= 150 MeV, while the repulsive mean field reduces the HRG prediction and restores agreement.
- When the mean field is included, the sixth order baryon number fluctuation becomes almost independent of the mass spectrum of resonances.

## Context

This work extends the HRG model studies by Huovinen (2017) and Vovchenko (2016, 2017) by including repulsive baryon-baryon interactions via a mean-field approach, and also incorporates missing resonance states from quark model calculations by Loring (2001) and Ebert (2009). The results show that repulsive interactions are essential for describing higher-order baryon number fluctuations from lattice QCD (Bazavov 2017, Borsanyi 2014, D'Elia 2016), and that these interactions must be included when extending the HRG model to non-zero baryon densities.
