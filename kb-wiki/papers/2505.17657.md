# Compositness and wave function of shallow bound states in relation to scattering observables

> We study the internal structure of exotic hadrons, especially focusing on the relation between the compositeness and physical observables. Defined as the probability of finding hadronic molecular components in the wave function, compositeness serves as a quantitative measure of the internal structure of exotic hadrons. We utilize the coupled-channel potential model incorporating both quark and hadron degrees of freedom, which naturally generate the ``bare state'' responsible for the elementary component as the bound state in the quark channel. The behavior of the compositeness under the variation of the model parameters is investigated by using the $X(3872)$ as an example. In particular, we analyze the associated scattering phase shifts and the bound-state wave functions to discuss the relation between the compositeness and the scattering observables for a shallow bound state. As a phenomenological application of the present framework, the compositeness of the $X(3872)$, $T_{cc}(3875)$, $D_{s0}(2317)$, and $D_{s1}(2460)$ is discussed.

## Key claim

The compositeness (probability of hadronic molecular component) of a shallow bound state like the X(3872) can be related to scattering observables such as phase shifts, scattering length, and effective range, but this relation depends on model parameters including binding energy, bare-state energy, momentum cutoff, and hadronic interaction strength; the compositeness remains near unity for weakly bound states unless the bare-state energy approaches the bound-state energy.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel potential model coupling quark degrees of freedom (a "bare state" from a confining potential) with hadronic degrees of freedom via a Yukawa-type transition form factor, and apply the Feshbach method to derive an effective nonlocal, energy-dependent hadron-hadron potential. They solve the Schrodinger equation analytically for bound and scattering states, compute the compositeness from both wave-function normalization and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and derive analytic expressions for the T-matrix, scattering amplitude, phase shift, scattering length, and effective range. Using the X(3872) as a test case with binding energy 0.04 MeV, they vary the binding energy, bare-state energy, cutoff, and direct hadronic interaction strength to study how the compositeness and scattering observables change.

## Key numbers

* X(3872) reference model: binding energy B = 0.04 MeV, bare energy E0 = 78 MeV, cutoff mu = 140 MeV, direct interaction omega_h = 0 MeV^2; yields compositeness X = 0.99, scattering length a0 = 24.5 fm, effective range re = 3.74 fm.
* As binding energy increases to B_b/4 = 2.53 MeV (where B_b = mu^2/(2m) is the artificial pole energy), compositeness decreases to X = 0.91, scattering length drops to approximately 5 fm, and effective range decreases by about 1.2 fm.
* When bare energy E0 is varied from 78 MeV down to -B = -0.04 MeV, compositeness remains near unity until E0 approaches -B, where it drops to X = 0 at E0 = -B.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel potential model of Terashima (2023) for the X(3872) by explicitly including direct hadron-hadron interactions and systematically analyzing the dependence of compositeness on model parameters. It builds on the weak-binding relations of Weinberg (1965) and Kamiya (2015, 2016) that connect compositeness to scattering length and effective range for s-wave near-threshold states, and on the HAL QCD method for local potential approximation (Aoki 2021). The results enable future extraction of compositeness from experimental scattering data for exotic hadrons such as X(3872), Tcc(3875), Ds0(2317), and Ds1(2460).
