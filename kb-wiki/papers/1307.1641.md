# α-Decay in Ultra-Intense Laser Fields

> We investigate the α-decay of a spherical nucleus under the influence of an ultra-intense laser field for the case when the radius vector joining the center-of-masses of the α-particle and the daughter is aligned with the direction of the external field. The time-independent part of the α-daughter interaction is taken from elastic scattering compilations whereas the time-varying part describes the interaction between the decaying system with the laser field. The time-dependent Schrödinger equation is solved numerically by appealing to a modified scheme of the Crank-Nicolson type where an additional first-order time derivative appears compared to the field-free case. The tunneling probability of the α-cluster, and derived quantities (decay rate, total flux) is determined for various laser intensities and frequencies for either continous waves or few-cycle pulses of envelope function F(t)=1. We show that in the latter case pulse sequences containing an odd number of half-cycles determine an enhancement of the tunneling probability compared to the field-free case and the continuous wave case. The present study is carried out taking as example the alpha decaying nucleus $^{106}$Te.

## Key claim

For the specific case of α-decay in \(^{106}\)Te, pulse sequences containing an odd number of half-cycles enhance the tunneling probability compared to both the field-free case and the continuous wave case, but only under the simplifying assumption that the radius vector joining the α-particle and daughter is aligned with the direction of the external field.

## Method

The authors solved the one-dimensional time-dependent Schrödinger equation (TDSE) for the relative α-cluster–daughter motion using a modified Crank-Nicolson scheme that includes an additional first-order time derivative. The static α-daughter interaction was taken from elastic scattering compilations (Woods-Saxon nuclear potential plus Coulomb), while the time-varying part described the dipole interaction with the laser field in the length gauge. The initial wavefunction was prepared as a bound eigenstate of a modified static potential, and transparent boundary conditions were employed to handle outgoing flux. The study used \(^{106}\)Te as the test nucleus and considered both continuous waves and few-cycle pulses with envelope function \(F(t)=1\).

## Key numbers

- The α-decay energy of \(^{106}\)Te used in the calculation is \(E_\alpha = 4.15\) MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters for \(^{106}\)Te are: depth \(V_0 = -137.7\) MeV, diffuseness \(a = 0.76\) fm, and radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.235\) fm.
- The spatial grid extended to \(\pm 192\) fm with a mesh size \(\Delta x = 1/8\) fm; the time step was \(\Delta t = 1/8\) in units of \(10^{-22}\) s.
- For a laser intensity of \(10^{29}\) W/cm\(^2\) and wavelength \(\lambda = 1.24\) Å, the ponderomotive energy is \(U_p \approx 2.9 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV and the relativistic parameter \(\eta \approx 1.8 \times 10^{-4}\).
- For a Ti:sapphire laser at the same intensity, the ponderomotive energy \(U_p \approx 1200\) MeV and \(\eta = 1.16\), indicating relativistic effects.
- The maximum time limit achieved in the TDSE solution was \(\sim 10^{-15}\) s.

## Context

This work extends earlier semi-classical studies of laser-induced recollisions in α-decay (Castañeda et al., 2011) by adopting a fully quantum non-perturbative framework. It also builds on the authors' previous time-dependent approach to α-decay (Misicu and Rizea, 2000), achieving a time limit roughly four orders of magnitude higher. The study is motivated by upcoming facilities such as the Extreme Light Infrastructure (ELI) near Bucharest, which aims to produce intensities up to \(10^{25}\) W/cm\(^2\), and X-ray free-electron lasers like LCLS and SwissFEL. The results suggest that ultra-intense laser pulses with an odd number of half-cycles could modify α-decay rates, opening the possibility of laser control over nuclear decay, though the parameters used here exceed currently available laser capabilities.
