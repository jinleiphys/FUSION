# Kinematic constrains on interacting nucleons in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV within the HIJING code

> The kinematic constrains on interacting nucleons in Large Hadron Collider (LHC) heavy-ion collisions are investigated in the framework of the Heavy Ion Jet Interaction Generator (HIJING) code incorporated with a collective cascade recipe. The latter is used to implement energy-momentum conservation constrains on both primary and secondary interacting nucleons. It is found that the energy-momentum conservation constrains on the interacting nucleons affect the whole charged particle pseudo-rapidity density distribution $(\frac{dN_{ch}}{dη})$, at different centralities (from central (0-5\%) to peripheral (70-80\%) collisions), in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =2.76$ TeV. In particular, the kinematic constrains on interacting nucleons are shown to reduce $(\frac{dN_{ch}}{dη})$ yield at mid-pseudorapidity $(\lvertη\rvert<2)$ in all centrality intervals, which is consistent with the LHC data. In addition, the model predicts an enhancement of the hadron production at $\lvertη\rvert>8$, which could be checked in future ALICE Zero Degree calorimeter. Such an enhancement is found to be mainly due to the interactions of protons at the spectator parts of the collision. This indicates that the kinematic constrains are important for a correct geometrical treatment of Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The authors show that imposing energy-momentum conservation constraints on both primary and secondary interacting nucleons within the HIJING framework reduces the charged particle pseudorapidity density at mid-pseudorapidity (|η| < 2) by about 5% across all centrality intervals in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, bringing the model into agreement with LHC data. The abstract does not specify the magnitude of the reduction or that the effect is independent of centrality.

## Method

The authors modified the HIJING 1.383 code by replacing the old Duke-Owen (1984) parton distribution functions with modern MSTW2009 PDFs and implemented a collective cascade recipe to enforce energy-momentum conservation on both primary interacting nucleons (determined by eikonal formalism) and secondary spectator nucleons (selected via a Gaussian probability based on transverse distance). The model was tuned with two parameter sets: one adjusting the Lund string fragmentation parameters (a=1.0, b=0.5 GeV⁻²) without jet quenching, and another keeping default fragmentation parameters (a=0.5, b=0.9 GeV⁻²) with jet quenching and impact-parameter-dependent parton shadowing. The code generated 7×10⁵ events for impact parameters from 0 to 2Rₐ, using ALICE centrality definitions.

## Key numbers

- The charged particle pseudorapidity density at mid-pseudorapidity (|η| < 2) is reduced by approximately 5% by kinematic constraints on primary interacting nucleons, independent of collision centrality.
- The mean interaction radius for secondary nucleon interactions is r_c = 1.2 fm (Equation 6).
- The parameters d and ⟨p_T²⟩ in the momentum distribution law are fixed at 0.5 and 0.5 (GeV/c)², respectively, from analysis of p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV.
- For the impact-parameter-dependent shadowing parameter s_q(g)(c), the maximum value for most central collisions is fixed at 0.1, decreasing to approximately 0.072 for 70-80% centrality (b ≤ 2Rₐ fm).
- Turning off jet quenching (dE/dx = 0) results in a reduction of total charged particles by as much as 25% at mid-pseudorapidity for 0-5% central events.
- The jet energy loss cutoff parameter is ε = 4.5 GeV/fm, taken equal to the jet production cutoff p₀.

## Context

This work extends HIJING-type models (originally developed by Wang and Gyulassy) by addressing the lack of kinematic constraints on interacting nucleons, which the authors argue affects the geometrical treatment of nucleus-nucleus collisions. The results are compared to ALICE (2009) and ATLAS (2010) data on charged particle pseudorapidity density in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV. The authors note that the UrQMD model, Color Glass Condensate-type models, and the AMPT model (which uses HIJING 2.0 as initial conditions) fail to reproduce the overall level and shape of the ALICE data, suggesting that the kinematic constraints implemented here are more consistent with LHC measurements. The work enables improved initial conditions for studies of collective flow and jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies.
