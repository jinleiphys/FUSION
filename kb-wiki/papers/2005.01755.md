# Nonadiabatic Nonlinear Optics and Quantum Geometry -- Application to the Twisted Schwinger Effect

> We study the tunneling mechanism of nonlinear optical processes in solids induced by strong coherent laser fields. The theory is based on an extension of the Landau-Zener model with nonadiabatic geometric effects. In addition to the rectification effect known previously, we find two effects, namely perfect tunneling and counterdiabaticity at fast sweep speed. We apply this theory to the twisted Schwinger effect, i.e., nonadiabatic pair production of particles by rotating electric fields, and find a nonperturbative generation mechanism of the opto-valley polarization and photo-current in Dirac and Weyl fermions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Landau-Zener model to include nonadiabatic geometric effects, finding that the effective tunneling gap is modified by the geometric amplitude factor (quantum geometric potential), which leads to three distinct phenomena: rectification, perfect tunneling at finite sweep speed, and counterdiabaticity at fast sweep speed.

## Method

The authors analyze a two-level Hamiltonian with a quadratic term in the parameter sweep that introduces geodesic curvature in the parameter space trajectory. They derive an analytic tunneling probability formula using the Dykhne-Davis-Pechasov (imaginary time) method after applying a unitary transformation to a "Landau-Zener frame" that eliminates the quadratic term. They then apply this formalism to the twisted Schwinger effect in 2D Dirac fermions by mapping the time-dependent Hamiltonian of a rotating electric field to the twisted Landau-Zener problem via quadratic expansion around the gap minimum.

## Key numbers

- Tunneling probability formula: \( P(F) = \exp\left[-\pi \frac{(m + \kappa_\parallel v F/4)^2}{v|F|}\right] \), where \(m\) is the gap, \(v\) the energy slope, \(\kappa_\parallel\) the geodesic curvature, and \(F\) the sweep speed
- Effective tunneling gap: \(\Delta_{\rm eff} = \Delta + \frac{F R_{+-}}{2}\), where \(R_{+-}\) is the geometric amplitude factor evaluated at \(q=0\)
- Rectification ratio: \(\gamma(F) \equiv P(|F|)/P(-|F|) = \exp\left(-\pi \frac{\Delta R_{+-}}{2v}\right)\)
- Perfect tunneling sweep speed: \(F_{\mathrm{PT}} = -2\Delta/R_{+-}\)
- Valley polarization ratio for 2D Dirac fermions: \(\gamma = \frac{\mathcal{P}_{+}(\boldsymbol{k})}{\mathcal{P}_{-}(\boldsymbol{k})} = \exp\left(\frac{\pi \Omega m}{v e E}\right)\), where \(\Omega\) is laser frequency, \(E\) electric field strength
- Schwinger limit for rotating fields: \(E_{\mathrm{S},\xi} = (m - \xi \Omega/4)^2/(v e)\)
- Crossover field: \(E_{\mathrm{co}} = \frac{|\Omega|}{e v} \sqrt{m(\xi \Omega/4 - m)}\)
- Low-frequency total production rate: \(\Gamma_{\xi} \simeq \frac{e E}{(2\pi)^2} \sqrt{\frac{e E}{v}} \exp\left(-\pi \frac{E_{\mathrm{S},\xi}}{E}\right)\)
- High-frequency production rate for optically allowed chirality: \(\Gamma_{\xi} \simeq \frac{|\Omega|}{(4\pi)^2 v} \sqrt{|\Omega| m} \sqrt{\frac{e E}{v}}\)
- Typical MoS\(_2\) parameters: Fermi velocity \(v = 3.5\;\AA \text{eV}\), mass parameter \(m = 0.83\;\text{eV}\)

## Context

This work extends the Landau-Zener model with geometric effects originally studied by Berry (1990) and the generalized adiabaticity condition developed by Wu (2008) and Xu (2018). It applies these concepts to the twisted Schwinger effect in rotating electric fields, which was previously studied for linearly polarized fields by Brezen (1970), Popov (1974), and Schutzhold (2008). The results provide a nonperturbative framework for opto-valley polarization and photocurrent generation in Dirac and Weyl materials, going beyond the perturbative second-order optical response theory of Sipe (2000) and the valley selection rules of Yao (2008) and Xiao (2012). The work enables understanding of strong-field nonlinear optics in materials such as monolayer transition metal dichalcogenides and graphene, where circularly polarized lasers can induce valley polarization.
