# Iterative Fluiddynamics

> An iterative scheme is presented to solve analytically the relativistic fluid dynamics equations. The scheme is applied to longitudinal expansion, transversal symmetric and transversal asymmetric (triaxial) expansion as well. Within this scheme it is possible to describe the dynamics of a strongly coupled (i.e. conformal) medium for parameters referring to heavy-ion collisions at LHC.

# Iterative Fluiddynamics

## Key claim
The paper presents an iterative scheme for analytically solving relativistic fluid dynamics equations that can describe the dynamics of a strongly coupled (conformal) medium for parameters referring to heavy-ion collisions at LHC, but only for smooth, analytically given initial conditions and up to a finite truncation order in the time coordinate expansion, with stability limited to a finite time interval before freeze-out.

## Method
The authors transform the local energy-momentum conservation equations into an infinite set of algebraic relations between Taylor coefficients of the unknown functions by formally expanding the energy-momentum tensor components in powers of the time coordinate. These algebraic recurrence formulas are solved iteratively up to a truncation order N, with the (k+1)th Taylor coefficient calculated algebraically from lower-order coefficients. The scheme is implemented in MAPLE and applied to longitudinal expansion, transverse symmetric expansion, and transverse asymmetric (triaxial) expansion using Gaussian initial energy distributions with synchronized Bjorken flow.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density at center: e(Theta_0, eta approx 0) ~ 65 GeV fm^{-3} corresponding to initial temperature T approx 430 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: T_fo ~ 165 MeV corresponding to energy density e_fo ~ 500 MeV fm^{-3}
- Longitudinal expansion: iterative scheme stable up to Theta = 3.2 (tau = 24.5 tau_eq) for truncation order N=48
- Longitudinal flow deviation from Bjorken: slope a(Theta) less than 2% for 0 <= Theta <= 3.2
- Transverse expansion: at Theta = 2.5, scaled energy density drops by approximately 5% for purely longitudinal expansion versus 58% for transverse expansion
- Transverse initial widths: sigma_2 = 7.5 fm and sigma_3 = 3.75 fm (ratio 2:1) corresponding to impact parameter b approx 9 fm for lead nuclei
- Triaxial expansion: truncation order N=24 for the full three-dimensional calculation

## Context
This work extends analytical solution methods for relativistic fluid dynamics beyond highly symmetric flows such as Bjorken's flow (Bjorken 1982), Gubser's flow (Gubser 2010), and the flow patterns studied by Csorgo and collaborators (Csorgo 2003, 2006). The iterative procedure is adapted from techniques established in general relativity (de Haro 2000) and used in the AdS/CFT correspondence (Janik 2010). The scheme enables analytical treatment of triaxial expansion including eccentricity evolution relevant for elliptic flow analysis at LHC energies, complementing numerical approaches that are typically required for realistic heavy-ion collision simulations.
