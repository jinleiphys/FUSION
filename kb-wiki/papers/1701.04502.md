# Constraining simultaneously nuclear symmetry energy and neutron-proton effective mass splitting with nucleus giant resonances from a dynamical approach

> With a newly improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction and an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, we have investigated the effects of the slope parameter $L$ of the nuclear symmetry energy and the isospin splitting of the nucleon effective mass $m_{n-p}^*=(m_n^*-m_p^*)/m$ on the centroid energy of the isovector giant dipole resonance and the electric dipole polarizability in $^{208}$Pb. With the isoscalar nucleon effective mass $m_s^*=0.7m$ constrained by the empirical optical potential, we obtain a constraint of $L=64.29\pm11.84 (\rm MeV)$ and $m_{n-p}^*= (-0.019 \pm 0.090)δ$, with $δ$ being the isospin asymmetry of nuclear medium. With the isoscalar nucleon effective mass $m_s^*=0.84m$ extracted from the excitation energy of the isoscalar giant quadruple resonance in $^{208}$Pb, we obtain a constraint of $L=53.85\pm10.29 (\rm MeV)$ and $m_{n-p}^*= (0.216 \pm 0.114)δ$.

## Key claim

The slope parameter \(L\) of the nuclear symmetry energy and the isospin splitting of the nucleon effective mass can be constrained simultaneously from the centroid energy and electric dipole polarizability of the isovector giant dipole resonance in \(^{208}\)Pb, but the extracted values depend on which isoscalar nucleon effective mass is adopted (either \(m_s^*=0.7m\) from the optical potential or \(m_s^*=0.84m\) from the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance), leading to two different allowed regions for \(L\) and \(m_{n-p}^*\).

## Method

The authors employed an improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction (ImMDI) together with the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate the isovector giant dipole resonance in \(^{208}\)Pb. The time evolution of the isovector dipole moment was fitted to a damped sinusoidal function, from which the centroid energy \(E_{-1}\) and electric dipole polarizability \(\alpha_D\) were extracted analytically. Linear correlations between \(E_{-1}\), \(\alpha_D\) and the parameters \(L\) and \(m_v^*\) were established from transport calculations, and experimental data were used to solve for \(L\) and the isovector effective mass, from which the neutron-proton effective mass splitting was deduced.

## Key numbers

* For \(m_s^* = 0.7m\) (constrained by the empirical optical potential): \(L = 64.29 \pm 11.84\) MeV and \((m_n^* - m_p^*)/m = (-0.019 \pm 0.090)\delta\), where \(\delta\) is the isospin asymmetry.
* For \(m_s^* = 0.84m\) (extracted from the excitation energy of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance in \(^{208}\)Pb): \(L = 53.85 \pm 10.29\) MeV and \((m_n^* - m_p^*)/m = (0.216 \pm 0.114)\delta\).
* The experimental centroid energy of the IVGDR in \(^{208}\)Pb used for the constraint is \(E_{-1} = 13.46\) MeV from photoabsorption measurements.
* The experimental electric dipole polarizability used is \(\alpha_D = 19.6 \pm 0.6\) fm\(^3\) from Tamii et al., with correction for quasideuteron excitations.
* The excitation energy of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance in \(^{208}\)Pb used to fix \(m_s^* = 0.84m\) is \(E_x = 10.9 \pm 0.1\) MeV from alpha-nucleus scattering experiments.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that separately constrained the symmetry energy slope parameter \(L\) (e.g., Chen (2012), Li (2013), Oertel (2016)) or the neutron-proton effective mass splitting (e.g., Li (2015) from optical model analyses) by showing that both can be constrained simultaneously from giant resonance observables. The results enable more precise predictions for the isospin dynamics in heavy-ion reactions and the properties of neutron-rich nuclear matter, though the uncertainty in the isoscalar nucleon effective mass remains a limiting factor. The approach complements random-phase approximation calculations of giant resonances (e.g., Zhang (2016), Roca-Maza (2013, 2015)) by using a dynamical transport model.
