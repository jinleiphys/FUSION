# Quantum Scaling in Energy Correlators Beyond the Confinement Transition

> We study the QCD scaling behavior of the small-angle Energy-Energy Correlator (EEC), focusing on the transition between its perturbative pre-confinement and non-perturbative post-confinement regimes. Applying the light-ray Operator Product Expansion (OPE), we develop a formalism that describes the scaling of the EEC with the input energy $Q$ in the transition and the post-confinement region, where the latter quantum scaling is determined by the $J=5$ DGLAP anomalous dimension. A key result of our work is a novel connection between the light-ray OPE and the dihadron fragmentation function (DFF), where we show that the non-perturbative OPE coefficients correspond to moments of the DFF. This finding establishes a new paradigm for studying hadronization. Our theoretical predictions are validated against Monte Carlo simulations for both $e^+e^-$ and $pp$ collisions, showing excellent agreement. The potential role of the quantum scaling in the precision determination of $α_s$ is also discussed.

# Quantum Scaling in Energy Correlators Beyond the Confinement Transition

## Key claim
The post-confinement plateau of the small-angle Energy-Energy Correlator (EEC) exhibits quantum scaling determined by the $J=5$ DGLAP anomalous dimension, but this scaling is only the leading contribution in a light-ray OPE expansion that also includes subleading terms from higher-twist operators; the formalism applies specifically in the regime $\zeta Q^2 \ll \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2$ where the expansion converges.

## Method
The authors apply the light-ray Operator Product Expansion (OPE) to the product of two energy operators ${\cal E}(\boldsymbol{n}_1){\cal E}(\boldsymbol{n}_2)$, decomposing it into hadronic operators with definite boost weight $J_L$ in the effective field theory of nearly free hadrons. These hadronic operators are then matched onto perturbative QCD light-ray operators (the twist-2 DGLAP detectors) via nonperturbative matching coefficients, and the renormalization group evolution of the DGLAP detectors governs the $Q^2$-scaling. The authors also derive an equivalent description using the dihadron fragmentation function (DFF) formalism, showing that the nonperturbative OPE coefficients correspond to moments of the DFF. Theoretical predictions at leading-logarithmic accuracy are compared to Pythia 8.2 Monte Carlo simulations for both $e^+e^-$ and $pp$ collisions.

## Key numbers
- The post-confinement quantum scaling is controlled by the DGLAP anomalous dimension at spin $J=5$, corresponding to the leading term ($n=0$) in the light-ray OPE expansion.
- The light-ray OPE fitting breaks down around $Q^2\zeta \sim {\cal O}(50)\>{\rm GeV^2}$, setting the boundary between post- and pre-confinement regimes.
- Theoretical predictions are validated against Pythia simulations at center-of-mass energies $Q = 200\>{\rm GeV}$, $500\>{\rm GeV}$, and $1000\>{\rm GeV}$ for both quark-initiated and gluon-initiated jets.
- The evolution kernel at leading-logarithmic accuracy is $U^{\text{LL}}_{J_L}(\mu_1^2,\mu_2^2) = \left[\frac{\alpha_s(\mu_2^2)}{\alpha_s(\mu_1^2)}\right]^{\widehat{\gamma}^{(0)}_{-J_L-1}/\beta_0}$, where $\beta_0=\frac{11}{3}C_A - \frac{4}{3}n_f T_F$ is the one-loop QCD beta function constant.
- The hard scale is chosen as $\mu_H \sim \mathcal{O}(Q)$ and the jet scale as $\mu_J\sim \mathcal{O}(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}})$.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of the post-confinement plateau in the EEC, which was observed in CMS Open Data analysis by Komiske et al. (2022) and in direct experimental measurements by ALICE (2024), CMS (2024), and STAR (2025). The pre-confinement scaling regime was previously understood in terms of perturbative quark/gluon splitting by Konishi et al. (1979) and Dixon et al. (2019), with systematic nonperturbative corrections developed by Lee et al. (2024) and Chen et al. (2024). The post-confinement plateau has inspired recent studies by Liu et al. (2024), Barata et al. (2024), Chicherin et al. (2023), and Csaki et al. (2024). The novel connection established between the light-ray OPE and the dihadron fragmentation function formalism (developed by de Florian et al. (2003), Majumder et al. (2004), Jaffe et al. (1997), Bacchetta et al. (2002, 2012), and others) creates a new paradigm for studying hadronization and enables precision determination of $\alpha_s$ from scaling measurements. The authors note that a concurrent study by Lee et al. (2025) also examined near-side scaling using transverse momentum dependent dihadron fragmentation.
