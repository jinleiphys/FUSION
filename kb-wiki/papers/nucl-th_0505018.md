# Shape Phase Transition of the Axially Symmetric States Between the U(5) and SU(3) Symmetries

> The shape phase structure and its transition of the nucleus in the transitional region between the U(5) and SU(3) symmetries is restudied within the framework of coherent-state theory with angular momentum projection in IBM-1. The certain angular momentum (or rotation-driven) effect on the nuclear shape is discussed. A coexistence of prolate and oblate shapes is found for the ground states of the transitional nuclei. A phase diagram in terms of the deformation parameter and angular momentum is given.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, when angular momentum projection is included in the coherent-state formalism for the IBM-1, the ground-state shape phase transition between U(5) and SU(3) symmetries is continuous rather than first-order, and that shape coexistence in the transitional region involves prolate and oblate deformed shapes rather than spherical and prolate shapes. This result depends on including negative values of the deformation parameter β, which correspond to oblate shapes.

## Method

The authors used the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1) with a Hamiltonian that interpolates between the U(5) and SU(3) dynamical symmetries by varying a control parameter η from 1 to 0 while fixing χ = −√7/2. They constructed potential energy surfaces using intrinsic coherent states with angular momentum projection, allowing the deformation parameter β to take both positive (prolate) and negative (oblate) values, and studied the minima of these surfaces for total boson number N=10 and various angular momenta L.

## Key numbers

- For the unprojected (L=0) potential, a double minimum structure (spherical and deformed) appears in the narrow control parameter range 0.771 ≤ η ≤ 0.795, with a first-order transition at η = 0.793.
- For the angular-momentum-projected potential with L=0, shape coexistence of prolate and oblate shapes occurs when the control parameter η > 0.850.
- In the SU(3) symmetry limit (η=0), the global minimum of the potential lies at β = √2 for low angular momentum states.
- For a transitional nucleus with η = 0.850, the ratio of U(5) to SU(3) interaction strengths is approximately 40, which the authors identify with the critical symmetry X(5).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the U(5)-SU(3) shape phase transition using coherent-state theory by Iachello (1987) and Iachello (1998), which found a first-order transition with spherical-deformed coexistence, and by Jolie (1999), who used angular momentum projection and found no shape coexistence and a continuous transition. The authors include negative β values (oblate shapes) that previous studies neglected, and they connect their results to the X(5) critical-point symmetry of Iachello (2001). The work enables further study of rotation-driven shape evolution and the variable moment of inertia phenomenon in transitional nuclei.
