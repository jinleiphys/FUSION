# Pion GPDs: A path toward phenomenology

> We introduce a new family of Generalised Parton Distribution models able to fulfil by construction all the theoretical properties imposed by QCD. These models are built on standard Parton Distribution Functions and extended to off-forward kinematics through a well-defined procedure. We apply this strategy on the pion, first handling a simple but insightful algebraic model, and then exploiting state-of-the-art computations obtained in continuum QCD. We compare these models with a more standard one relying on an xFitter extraction of the pion Parton Distribution Functions. The results for both quark and gluon Generalised Parton Distributions are presented and exploited for calculation of Electromagnetic, Gravitational and Compton Form Factors. Results on the latter highlight the relevance of next-to-leading order corrections, even in the so-called valence region.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new family of pion Generalised Parton Distribution (GPD) models that satisfy all QCD theoretical constraints by construction, using a covariant extension strategy based on the Radon transform; however, the models rely on a truncation of the Fock-space expansion to the leading two-body component and on factorised *Ansätze* for light-front wave functions, which are approximations that may be systematically improved.

## Method

The authors model the DGLAP (outer) kinematic region of pion GPDs using the overlap representation of light-front wave functions truncated to the valence two-body component, with a factorised *Ansatz* that separates longitudinal and transverse momentum dependence. They then apply the inverse Radon transform to obtain the associated double distribution, and use the Radon transform to extend the model to the ERBL (inner) region, guaranteeing polynomiality by construction. Two specific models are built: one using a simple algebraic PDF (30x²(1-x)²) and another using a state-of-the-art continuum QCD (CSM) PDF from Ding (2019), both with the same transverse-momentum dependence derived from the Nakanishi representation of the Bethe-Salpeter wave function.

## Key numbers

- The algebraic PDF parametrisation is given by q_π(x) = 30 x² (1-x)², used as input for one GPD model.
- The t-dependence of the GPD is governed by ζ = -t/(4M²) × (1-x)²/(1-ξ²), where M is the pion mass.
- At vanishing momentum transfer (t=0), the GPD saturates the positivity bound: H^q_π(x,ξ,0) = √[q_π(x_in) q_π(x_out)].
- The large-x behaviour of the pion GPD is constrained to H^q_π(x,ξ,t) ∼ (1-x)²/(1-ξ²) as x→1.
- The soft-pion theorem requires H^q_π(x,1,0) = ½ φ_π((1+x)/2), where φ_π is the leading-twist pion distribution amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the covariant extension strategy previously applied to algebraic models by Chouika (2017) to incorporate state-of-the-art continuum QCD computations of pion PDFs from Ding (2019) and Cui (2020). It provides the first realistic pion GPD model that fulfills all theoretical constraints (positivity, polynomiality, continuity, large-x behaviour, soft-pion theorem) by construction, enabling phenomenological studies of Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) on the pion at next-to-leading order. The results are relevant for future electron-ion colliders (EIC in the USA and EicC in China) and complement ongoing lattice-QCD efforts by Chen (2019) and Alexandrou (2020).
