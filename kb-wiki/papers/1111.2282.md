# Supernova neutrino flavor evolution at high densities

> These conference proceedings cover various aspects of neutrino propagation through the high matter and neutrino densities near the proto-neutron star in a core-collapse supernova. A significant feature of this regime is the impact of neutrino-neutrino interactions. Properties of this non-linear many-neutrino system are discussed with a particular emphasis on its symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the collective neutrino flavor evolution at high densities in core-collapse supernovae, driven by neutrino-neutrino interactions, possesses hidden symmetries that yield a large set of conserved quantities (invariants) in both the exact many-body description and the random phase approximation (RPA) effective one-body picture, but only under the simplifying single-angle approximation where all neutrino scattering angles are taken to be equal.

## Method

The author formulates the two-flavor neutrino system using SU(2) flavor isospin operators and a many-body Hamiltonian that includes vacuum mixing, matter effects, and the neutrino-neutrino interaction term. The evolution is analyzed both exactly via a path integral over SU(2) coherent states and in the RPA, which reduces the two-body interaction to a one-body mean field. The single-angle approximation is then applied to derive constants of motion (invariants) for the Hamiltonian, and a Bethe ansatz solution is presented for the exact eigenstates. The RPA equations of motion are also derived, and the spectral splitting phenomenon is interpreted using a Lagrange multiplier that enforces conservation of total lepton number.

## Key numbers

* The total number of neutrinos emitted by the proto-neutron star is approximately \(10^{58}\).
* The maximal value of the neutrino flavor isospin quantum number is \(J_{\text{max}} = N/2\), where \(N\) is the total number of neutrinos.
* The energy eigenvalues for the fully aligned and anti-aligned flavor states in the single-angle Hamiltonian are \(E_{(+J_{\text{max}})} = -\sum_p \frac{n_p}{2} \omega_p + J_{\text{max}}(J_{\text{max}}+1)\) and \(E_{(-J_{\text{max}})} = \sum_p \frac{n_p}{2} \omega_p + J_{\text{max}}(J_{\text{max}}+1)\), respectively.
* The Bethe ansatz equation for the eigenstates is \(\sum_p \frac{-j_p}{\omega_p - \xi_\alpha} = -\frac{1}{2} + \sum_{\beta \neq \alpha} \frac{1}{\xi_\alpha - \xi_\beta}\).
* The critical energy for spectral splitting is identified with the Lagrange multiplier \(\omega_c\), which separates neutrino modes into those with \(\omega_p < \omega_c\) (evolving into one mass eigenstate) and \(\omega_p > \omega_c\) (evolving into the other).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic many-body approach to collective neutrino oscillations developed in Balantekin (2006) and Pehlivan (2010, 2011), drawing a mathematical analogy to the BCS pairing Hamiltonian. It directly addresses the spectral splitting/swapping phenomenon studied by Raffelt (2007) and Duan (2007), providing an interpretation of the critical energy as a Lagrange multiplier. The paper notes that recent multi-angle calculations by Cherry (2010) show significant differences from the single-angle approximation, particularly in reducing adiabaticity for normal mass hierarchy, and that realistic supernova simulations by Ott (2008) suggest non-half-isotropic angular distributions, which may lead to multi-angle instabilities as discussed by Sawyer (2008) and Mirizzi (2011).
