# Unified description of the $Qs \bar q \bar q$ molecular bound states, molecular resonances and compact tetraquark states in the quark potential model

> We calculate the mass spectrum of the $Qs\bar q \bar q$ $(Q=c, b)$ tetraquark states with $J^P=(0,1,2)^+$ using the AL1 quark potential model, which successfully describes the conventional hadron spectrum. We employ the Gaussian expansion method to solve the four-body Schrödinger equation, and use the complex scaling method to identify the resonances. With the notation $T_{Q s, I(J)}^{\text {Theo. }}(M)$, we find several near-threshold bound states and resonances, including $T_{cs,0(0)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(2350)$, $T_{cs,0(0)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(2906)$, $T_{bs,0(0)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(5781)$, $T_{bs,0(1)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(5840)$, and $T_{bs,0(0)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(6240)$ which are close to the $D\bar{K}$, $D^*\bar{K}^*$, $\bar{B}\bar{K}$, $\bar{B}^*\bar{K}$ and $\bar{B}^*\bar{K}^*$ thresholds, respectively. Furthermore, their spatial structures clearly support their molecular natures. The resonance $T_{cs,0(0)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(2906)$ has a mass of $2906$ MeV, a width of $20$ MeV, and quantum numbers $I(J^P)=0(0^+)$, which may serve as a good candidate for the experimental $T_{cs0}(2900)$ state. We strongly urge the experimental search of the predicted states.

# Unified description of the $Qs \bar q \bar q$ molecular bound states, molecular resonances and compact tetraquark states in the quark potential model

## Key claim
The AL1 quark potential model, with no additional free parameters, predicts several near-threshold molecular bound states and resonances in the $Qs\bar q \bar q$ systems, including a candidate for the experimental $T_{cs0}(2900)$ state; however, the calculations may have errors of order tens of MeV from the quark model itself and do not account for the widths of constituent mesons, so the theoretical widths are expected to be smaller than experimental values.

## Method
The authors solved the four-body Schrodinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method with the AL1 quark potential model, whose parameters were fixed by a best fit to conventional meson spectra. They applied the complex scaling method to identify resonances from scattering states, and defined root-mean-square radii using a decomposition of the antisymmetrized wave function to avoid ambiguities from identical quarks.

## Key numbers
- $T_{cs,0(0)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(2350)$: bound state at 2350 MeV, binding energy 3 MeV relative to the $D\bar{K}$ threshold (2353 MeV), identified as a $D\bar{K}$ molecular state
- $T_{cs,0(0)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(2906)$: resonance at 2906 MeV with width 20 MeV, 14 MeV below the $D^*\bar{K}^*$ threshold (2920 MeV), candidate for the experimental $T_{cs0}(2900)$ state
- $T_{bs,0(0)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(5781)$: bound state at 5781 MeV, binding energy 3 MeV relative to the $\bar{B}\bar{K}$ threshold (5784 MeV), identified as a $\bar{B}\bar{K}$ molecular state
- $T_{bs,0(1)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(5840)$: bound state at 5840 MeV, binding energy 1 MeV relative to the $\bar{B}^*\bar{K}$ threshold (5841 MeV), identified as a $\bar{B}^*\bar{K}$ molecular state
- $T_{bs,0(0)}^{\mathrm{Theo.}}(6240)$: resonance at 6240 MeV with width 18 MeV, 14 MeV below the $\bar{B}^*\bar{K}^*$ threshold (6254 MeV), identified as a $\bar{B}^*\bar{K}^*$ molecular state

## Context
This work extends the benchmark calculations of Ma (2022, 2023) and Meng (2023) for tetraquark bound states using various quark models and few-body methods, now applied to the $Qs\bar q \bar q$ systems with both bound states and resonances. The results differ from the chiral quark model calculations of Yang (2021), which obtained a deeply bound $0(0^+)$ state and did not reproduce the experimental $T_{cs0}(2900)$. Lattice QCD simulations by Cheung (2020) with $m_\pi=239$ MeV and $m_\pi=391$ MeV found hints of a $0(0^+)$ virtual-state pole below $D\bar{K}$. The predicted molecular states provide targets for experimental searches in $B$ decays and strong decay processes, and the method enables future unified studies of compact tetraquarks and hadronic molecules within the same framework.
