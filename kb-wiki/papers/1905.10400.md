# Electron-capture Rates in $^{20}$Ne for a Forbidden Transition to the Ground State of $^{20}$F Relevant to Final Evolution of High-density O-Ne-Mg Cores

> Electron capture on $^{20}$Ne is critically important for the final stage of evolution of stars with the initial masses of 8 - 10 $M_{\odot}$. In the present paper, we evaluate electron capture rates for a forbidden transition $^{20}$Ne (0$_{g.s.}^{+}$) $\rightarrow$ $^{20}$F (2$_{g.s.}^{+}$) in stellar environments by the multipole expansion method with the use of shell-model Hamiltonians. These rates have not been accurately determined in theory as well as in experiments. Our newly evaluated rates are compared with those obtained by a prescription that treats the transition as an allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) transition with the strength determined from a recent $Î˛$-decay experiment for $^{20}$F (2$_{g.s.}^{+}$) $\rightarrow$ $^{20}$Ne (0$_{g.s.}^{+}$) \citep{Kirsebom}. We find that different electron energy dependence of the transition strengths between the two methods leads to sizable differences in the weak rates of the two methods. We also find that the Coulomb effects, that is, the effects of screening on ions and electrons are non-negligible. We apply our e-capture rates on $^{20}$Ne to the calculation of the evolution of high-density O-Ne-Mg cores of 8 - 10 $M_{\odot}$ stars. We find that our new rates affect the abundance distribution and the central density at the final stage of evolution.

## Key claim

Electron-capture rates for the forbidden transition \(^{20}\text{Ne}(0^{+}_{\text{g.s.}}) \rightarrow ^{20}\text{F}(2^{+}_{\text{g.s.}})\) evaluated via the multipole expansion method with shell-model Hamiltonians differ sizably from rates obtained by treating the transition as an allowed Gamow-Teller transition with a constant \(B(\text{GT})\) value derived from the measured \(\beta\)-decay log \(ft\) = 10.47 Â± 0.11 (Kirsebom 2019), because the two methods produce different electron-energy dependence of the transition strengths.

## Method

Electron-capture rates for the forbidden transition were calculated using the multipole expansion method (Walecka 1975; O'Connell 1972) with the USDB (Brown & Richter 2006) and YSOX (Yuan 2012) shell-model Hamiltonians, including contributions from Coulomb, longitudinal, transverse electric, and axial magnetic multipoles with \(J^{\pi}=2^{+}\). The rates were compared with a "GT prescription" that treats the transition as an allowed Gamow-Teller transition using a constant \(B(\text{GT}) = 1.04 \times 10^{-6}\) derived from the experimental log \(ft\) = 10.47. Coulomb screening effects on both electrons and ions were included using the dielectric function from relativistic random phase approximation (Itoh 1987) and the strong-coupling regime formalism (Ichimaru 1993). The resulting rates were applied to stellar evolution calculations of an 8.4 \(M_{\odot}\) O-Ne-Mg core using the MESA code (Paxton 2011, 2013, 2015).

## Key numbers

- The experimental \(\beta\)-decay log \(ft\) value for \(^{20}\text{F}(2^{+}_{\text{g.s.}}) \rightarrow ^{20}\text{Ne}(0^{+}_{\text{g.s.}})\) is 10.47 Â± 0.11 (Kirsebom 2019), corresponding to \(B(\text{GT}) = 1.04 \times 10^{-6}\).
- At \(T = 10^{8.6}\) K, the shell-model e-capture rates with Coulomb effects differ from the GT prescription by at most a factor of about 5 at \(\log_{10}(\rho Y_e) = 9.6\).
- For total e-capture rates including allowed GT transitions, the difference between USDB and GT prescription is at most about a factor of 3 at \(\log_{10}(\rho Y_e) = 9.5-9.7\) for \(T = 10^{8.6}\) K, and 3-4 times for \(T = 10^{8.4}\) K.
- The USDB shell-model calculation yields log \(ft = 11.18\) for the forbidden \(\beta\)-decay, with a branching ratio of \(2.15 \times 10^{-6}\) (19.5% of the observed value from Kirsebom 2019).
- The YSOX Hamiltonian gives log \(ft = 11.24\) and a branching ratio of \(1.87 \times 10^{-6}\).
- Off-center oxygen ignition occurs at \(6.9 \times 10^{-4} M_{\odot}\) (33 km) for USDB with Coulomb effects, compared to \(2.3 \times 10^{-3} M_{\odot}\) (50 km) for the GT prescription with Coulomb effects.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of forbidden transitions in \(^{20}\text{Ne}\) beyond the approximation used by Martinez-Pinedo (2014), who treated the transition as an allowed Gamow-Teller transition with a constant shape factor. The new rates are applied to stellar evolution calculations of O-Ne-Mg cores in 8-10 \(M_{\odot}\) stars, following the methodology of Jones (2013) and Schwab (2017, 2019). The results show that proper treatment of the forbidden transition via the multipole expansion method affects the central density and abundance distribution at the final stage of evolution, with implications for the fate of the core (neutron star formation versus electron-capture supernova) that will be explored in future work (Zha 2019).
