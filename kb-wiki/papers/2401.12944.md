# Modern nuclear and astrophysical constraints of dense matter in a renormalized chiral approach

> We explore the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) phase diagram's complexities, including quark deconfinement transitions, liquid-gas phase changes, and critical points, using the chiral mean-field (CMF) model that is able to capture all these features. We introduce a vector meson renormalization within the CMF framework, enabling precise adjustments of meson masses and coupling strengths related to vector meson interactions. Performing a new fit to the deconfinement potential, we are able to replicate recent lattice QCD results, low energy nuclear physics properties, neutron star observational data, and key phase diagram features as per modern constraints. This approach enhances our understanding of vector mesons' roles in mediating nuclear interactions and their impact on the equation of state, contributing to a more comprehensive understanding of the QCD phase diagram and its implications for nuclear and astrophysical phenomena.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that introducing a vector meson field redefinition within the chiral mean-field (CMF) model, combined with a new fit to the deconfinement potential, allows simultaneous reproduction of lattice QCD results, low-energy nuclear properties, neutron star observations, and phase diagram features, but only after reparametrizing the Polyakov loop-like potential couplings and vector meson-nucleon coupling strengths to match updated constraints.

## Method
The authors extended the CMF model by implementing a vector meson field redefinition that breaks the mass degeneracy among ω, ρ, φ, and K* mesons using chiral invariant terms, then refitted the vector meson coupling strengths to nucleons (gNω, gNρ, g4) to nuclear saturation properties. They additionally refitted the parameters of the Polyakov loop-inspired deconfinement potential (a0, a1, a2, a3, T0) to reproduce recent lattice QCD results for the pseudo-critical temperature and deconfinement transition, while simultaneously satisfying low-energy nuclear matter constraints and neutron star observational data.

## Key numbers
- Pseudo-critical temperature at μB=0: Tpc = 158 ± 0.6 MeV (from lattice QCD, used as constraint)
- Deconfinement critical temperature for pure glue: Tcd = 270 MeV (from lattice QCD, used as constraint)
- Liquid-gas critical temperature: TcLG ≃ 15–17 MeV with μB,cLG ≈ 910 MeV
- Hadron-quark critical temperature upper bound: TcHQ = 132+3−6 MeV for isospin symmetric matter at zero chemical potentials
- Nuclear saturation density from PREX: nsat = 0.1480 ± 0.0038 fm−3
- Binding energy per nucleon: B/A = −15.677 MeV at nsat = 0.16146 fm−3 and B/A = −16.24 MeV at nsat = 0.16114 fm−3
- Nuclear incompressibility: K = 240 ± 20 MeV from ISGMR data, with broader range 250 < K < 315 MeV from model-independent analysis
- Symmetry energy at saturation: Esym = 31.6 ± 2.7 MeV, slope parameter L = 58.9 ± 16 MeV (fiducial values from 28-model evaluation)
- Slope parameter from PREX-II: L = 106 ± 37 MeV (neutron skin) versus L = 54 ± 8 MeV (alternative analysis)
- Hyperon potentials: UΛ ≈ −28 MeV, UΣ = 30 ± 20 MeV, UΞ = −21.9 ± 0.7 MeV (KEK/JPARC), with ALICE/HAL-QCD giving UΞ = −4 MeV, UΛ = −28 MeV, UΣ = +15 MeV (±2 MeV statistical error)
- Neutron star radius from NICER: 12.39+1.30−1.98 km for 2.072+0.067−0.066 M⊙ and 13.7+2.6−1.5 km for 2.08 ± 0.07 M⊙
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817: Λ̃ < 800 for 1.4 M⊙ neutron stars
- Vector meson vacuum masses after field redefinition: ω = 770.87 MeV, ρ = 770.87 MeV, K* = 865.89 MeV, φ = 1007.76 MeV (fitted from mV = 687.33 MeV and μ = 0.41/σ0²)

## Context
This work extends the chiral mean-field model of Dexheimer (2009) by incorporating vector meson field redefinition previously applied in non-linear chiral models by Papazoglou (1998) and Zschiesche (2003), but now combined with a Polyakov loop-inspired deconfinement potential for the first time. The updated parametrization enables the model to simultaneously satisfy modern lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2020, 2021), low-energy nuclear constraints from PREX (2021) and NICER (2019), and gravitational wave constraints from LIGO/Virgo (2018), which earlier CMF parametrizations from 2008 could not achieve. This work enables future studies of in-medium vector meson properties and more reliable exploration of the QCD phase diagram across temperatures and densities relevant to heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars.
