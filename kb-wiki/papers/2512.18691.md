# Collapse of Coulomb Bound States of Vector Bosons

> Charged spin 1 (vector) particles behave very differently from electrons or scalars in a Coulomb field. For an infinitely heavy point-like nucleus their bound state wave functions fall to the centre, and embedding the system in a renormalisable electroweak-type theory does not remedy this short-distance pathology. We therefore solve the pure Coulomb problem for a finite nuclear radius $R$ and recover the point nucleus limit by letting $R\to 0$. This approach allows us to include the crucial Upsilon term in the wave equations, which for the point-like nucleus is proportional to delta(r) and was ignored in the previous calculations of the energy spectrum. Several unusual effects emerge: (i) The Upsilon term supports a tower of states located mainly inside the nucleus. As R -> 0 their number diverges, most lying in the negative energy continuum (energy epsilon < - m c^2). They trigger vacuum breakdown - particle-antiparticle pair creation that ultimately screens the nuclear charge. (ii) Ordinary Sommerfeld-like states (with binding energy smaller m c^2) persist, but a finite fraction of each wave function leaks into the nucleus, even as R -> 0. (iii) Charge density of a negatively charged vector particle changes sign in a vicinity of the nucleus and becomes positive charge density, whereas the Upsilon term ensures its density inside the nucleus remains negative. (iv) For weak coupling, Z alpha << 1, yet with mR <Z alpha, the non-relativistic solution differs qualitatively from Schrodinger theory despite binding energies are well below m c^2; agreement is recovered only when Z alpha << mR. These phenomena highlight the distinctive and subtle behaviour of spin-1 particles in the Coulomb field.

# Collapse of Coulomb Bound States of Vector Bosons

## Key claim

The collapse of Coulomb bound states for spin-1 (vector) particles in a point-like nucleus is not cured by embedding in a renormalisable electroweak-type theory; however, introducing a finite nuclear radius $R$ and taking the $R \to 0$ limit allows inclusion of the previously ignored $\Upsilon$ term (proportional to $\delta(r)$ for a point nucleus), which produces a tower of states inside the nucleus whose number diverges as $R \to 0$, most lying in the negative energy continuum and triggering vacuum breakdown.

## Method

The authors solve the Corben-Schwinger equations for a charged vector boson in the Coulomb field of a finite-radius nucleus, using a spherical nuclear charge distribution. They apply a Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) semiclassical method to find energy levels and wave functions for the longitudinal polarization ($j=0$) mode, which is the most interesting case. The point-nucleus limit is recovered by letting $R \to 0$, and the $\Upsilon$ term (defined as $\Upsilon \equiv e\rho/m^2 = -\Delta U/m^2$) is included in the wave equations, which for a point-like nucleus would be proportional to $\delta(r)$ and was omitted in prior calculations.

## Key numbers

- For the condition $mR \ll Z\alpha \ll 1$, the principal quantum number shift from finite nuclear size is $\Delta \approx 0.6 \, Z\alpha/(mR) \gg 1$, indicating many orbitals confined inside the nucleus.
- The energy of states inside the nucleus is approximately $\epsilon \approx -3Z\alpha/(2R) + \sqrt{8/3} \, m \, n$, valid for $n < 0.3 \, Z\alpha/(mR)$.
- The Sommerfeld-like energy spectrum for $j=0$ is $\epsilon_{n0} = m \left[1 + \zeta^2/(\gamma + n - 1/2)^2\right]^{-1/2}$ where $\zeta = Z\alpha$ and $\gamma = \sqrt{1/4 - \zeta^2}$.
- The ratio of charge inside the nucleus to charge outside for states without the $\Upsilon$ term is $Q^{no \Upsilon}_{\rm in}/Q_{\rm out} \approx (1 - 2\gamma)/3 \approx 2 Z^2 \alpha^2 / 3$, independent of $R$.
- With the $\Upsilon$ term included, the ratio becomes $Q_{\rm in}/Q_{\rm out} \sim -2 (Z\alpha)^4 / 3$, remaining finite as $R \to 0$.

## Context

This work extends the Corben-Schwinger formalism (Corben and Schwinger, 1940) for spin-1 particles in a Coulomb field, which gave a Sommerfeld-like spectrum but suffered from wave function collapse for $j=0$ and $j=1, l=0$ states. Prior work by Flambaum (2006) showed that renormalisability alone does not prevent collapse, and Kuchiev and Flambaum (2006) demonstrated that QED vacuum polarization can produce a repulsive barrier. The present paper contrasts with the scalar and spinor cases: for spin-1/2 particles the critical charge is $Z\alpha = 1$, for spin-0 it is $Z\alpha = 1/2$, while for spin-1 it is $Z\alpha = 0$ for a point nucleus. The finite-nucleus approach enables inclusion of the $\Upsilon$ term and reveals that the number of states inside the nucleus diverges as $R \to 0$, leading to vacuum breakdown and complete screening of nuclear charge, analogous to the supercritical charge phenomenon for electrons in nuclei with $Z > 170$ (Zeldovich and Popov).
