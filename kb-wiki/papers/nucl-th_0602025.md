# $ϕ$ and $Ω$ production in relativistic heavy ion collisions in a dynamical quark coalescence model

> Based on the phase-space information obtained from a multi-phase transport model within the string melting scenario for strange and antistrange quarks, we study the yields and transverse momentum spectra of $ϕ$ mesons and $Ω$ ($Ω^{-}+\barΩ^{+}$) baryons as well as their anisotropic flows in Au+Au collisions at RHIC using a dynamical quark coalescence model that includes the effect due to quark phase-space distributions inside hadrons. With current quark masses and fixing the $ϕ$ and $Ω$ radii from fitting measured yields, we first study the ratio of the yield of $Ω$ baryons to that of $ϕ$ mesons as well as their elliptic and fourth-order flows as functions of their transverse momentum. How the elliptic and fourth-order flows of $ϕ$ mesons and $Ω$ baryons are related to those of strange and antistrange quarks is then examined. The dependence of above results on $ϕ$ and $Ω$ radii as well as on the strange quark mass is also studied.

## Key claim

The dynamical quark coalescence model, which includes both the phase-space distributions of quarks at freeze-out and the internal quark structure of hadrons, can reproduce the measured yields of φ mesons and Ω baryons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but only when the hadron radii are treated as adjustable parameters fitted to those yields; the model's predicted transverse momentum spectra are significantly softer than the experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model in the string melting scenario to generate the phase-space distributions of strange and antistrange quarks at freeze-out in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They then applied a dynamical quark coalescence model that computes the overlap between the quark phase-space distributions and the Wigner phase-space functions of quarks inside the φ meson (taken as a spherical harmonic oscillator with two quarks) and the Ω baryon (three quarks). The hadron radii were treated as free parameters and fixed by fitting the measured yields: Rφ = 0.65 fm (STAR data) or 0.47 fm (PHENIX data), and RΩ = 1.2 fm.

## Key numbers

- Rapidity density dN/dy at mid-rapidity for strange and antistrange quarks at freeze-out: about 94; for light quarks and antiquarks: about 770, in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and b = 8 fm.
- Effective temperature of the partonic matter at freeze-out: about 161 MeV, from fitting the transverse momentum spectrum of quarks to a Boltzmann distribution.
- φ meson rapidity density dN/dy at mid-rapidity: about 7.7 (STAR data) or 4.5 (PHENIX data) for 0-5% centrality; the model reproduces these with Rφ = 0.65 fm or 0.47 fm, respectively.
- Ω baryon rapidity density dN/dy at mid-rapidity: about 0.64 for central collisions (preliminary STAR data); the model reproduces this with RΩ = 1.2 fm.
- For b = 8 fm (centrality about 31%), the model gives φ meson dN/dy ≈ 3.3 (with Rφ = 0.65 fm) or 2.0 (with Rφ = 0.47 fm), and Ω baryon dN/dy ≈ 0.26 (with RΩ = 1.2 fm).
- The fourth-order anisotropic flow v4 of strange and antistrange quarks scales approximately as 0.85 v2².

## Context

This work extends the dynamical quark coalescence model, previously applied to hadrons made of light and charm quarks (Greco et al., 2004; Hwa and Yang, 2003; Fries et al., 2003; Molnar and Voloshin, 2003), to the production of φ mesons and Ω baryons consisting of strange quarks. It differs from the approach of Yan et al. (2005), who reconstructed φ mesons from kaon-antikaon invariant mass distributions in the AMPT model. The results show that both the dynamical phase-space information of partons at freeze-out and the quark structure of hadrons are important for strange hadron production, and they enable future studies of how hadron radii and strange quark masses affect the scaling relations among anisotropic flows.
