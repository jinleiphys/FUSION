# Polarization degrees-of-freedom in electronuclear hadron production from finite nuclei

> The polarization degrees-of-freedom in electronuclear two-nucleon knockout reactions are discussed. Model calculations for the unpolarized $^{16}$O(e,e$'$pp) cross sections at ($\mid \vec{q} \mid$,$ω$)=(210 MeV,300 MeV/c) are compared to recently obtained data. Predictions for the polarization observables in electronuclear two-nucleon knockout are presented. It is stressed that detailed studies of two-nucleon emission processes permit to constrain the contribution from two-nucleon mechanisms (like meson-exchange and isobar effects) to the single-nucleon knockout channel (e,e$'$p).

## Key claim

The paper argues that polarization observables in two-nucleon knockout reactions (e,e'pp) can isolate the longitudinal channel and minimize uncertainties from final-state interactions, thereby providing a cleaner probe of short-range correlations (SRC) and meson-exchange/isobar effects than unpolarized cross sections alone. The hedge is that this selectivity is demonstrated for specific "super-parallel" kinematics, where only a limited set of structure functions contribute, and the calculations assume a direct knockout mechanism just above the two-nucleon emission threshold.

## Method

The authors use a distorted-wave model for (e,e'NN) and (γ,NN) reactions on finite nuclei, starting from a consistent shell-model description for initial and final states with a realistic Hartree-Fock single-particle basis. The model includes meson-exchange currents (MEC), Δ-isobar currents (IC), and central short-range correlations implemented via a correlation operator from many-body theory, with the two-body terms retained in a first-order cluster expansion. Calculations are performed for individual final states in the A-2 system, and the transition matrix elements are evaluated in the helicity basis.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized $^{16}$O(e,e'pp) cross sections are calculated at ($|\vec{q}|$, ω) = (210 MeV, 300 MeV/c), corresponding to electron kinematics e = 580 MeV, e' = 374 MeV, and θ_e = 26.2°.
- Two-nucleon spectroscopic factors for $^{16}$O → $^{14}$C transitions are approximately S_{lj,l'j'} / [(2j+1)(2j'+1)] ≈ 0.5, consistent with single-nucleon spectroscopic factors S_{lj}/(2j+1) ≈ 0.7 from quasi-elastic (e,e'p) measurements.
- For the $^{16}$O(e,e'p) reaction at quasi-elastic conditions (e = 850 MeV, e' = 610 MeV, q = 690 MeV/c, Q² = 0.42 (GeV/c)², x = 0.938), the two-nucleon effects reduce the 1p_{3/2} cross section but are almost negligible for the 1p_{1/2} cross section.
- In super-parallel kinematics, central short-range correlations dominate the ground-state transition up to pair missing momenta of about 150 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of two-nucleon knockout reactions beyond unpolarized cross sections, building on prior model calculations by the same group (Ryckebusch et al., 1997, 1998) and nuclear-structure calculations by Cohen and Kurath (1965) and Geurts et al. (1998). The results are compared to $^{16}$O(e,e'pp) data from NIKHEF (Gerco et al., 1998) and are intended to inform upcoming experiments at MAMI (A1/1-97) and TJNAF. The paper also connects to single-nucleon knockout studies, showing that two-nucleon effects can mimic or obscure medium modifications of nucleon form factors in (e,e'p) measurements, as discussed by Kelly (1998) and Lu et al. (1998).
