# Complete Study of Hadroproduction of a $Υ$ Meson Associated with a Prompt $J/ψ$

> We present the first complete study of $Υ$ and prompt $J/ψ$ production from single-parton scattering, including the complete $\mathcal{O}(α_S^6)$ color-singlet contribution, the $\mathcal{O}(α_S^2α^2)$ electroweak contribution, the complete nonrelativistic S-wave and P-wave color-octet contribution as well as the feeddown contribution. Our study was motivated by the recent evidence reported by D0 Collaboration of prompt $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ simultaneous production at the Tevatron. With our complete evaluation, we are able to refine the determination of the double parton scattering contribution made by D0 Collaboration. We find that the effective cross section characterizing the importance of double-parton scatterings is $σ_{\rm eff}\le 8.2$ mb at $68\%$ confidence level from the D0 measurement.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first complete calculation of all leading single-parton scattering (SPS) contributions to prompt \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) hadroproduction, and finds that even with these contributions included, the measured cross section at the Tevatron still requires a dominant double-parton scattering (DPS) component, yielding an upper limit on the effective DPS cross section of \(\sigma_{\rm eff}\le 8.2\) mb at 68% confidence level.

## Method
The authors computed the complete \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_S^6)\) color-singlet contribution (including both double-real emission and loop-induced diagrams), the \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_S^2\alpha^2)\) electroweak contribution, the interference between loop-induced and electroweak diagrams, and the complete nonrelativistic QCD color-octet contribution at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_S^4)\) including S-wave and P-wave Fock states up to \(\mathcal{O}(v_q^4)\), plus feeddown from higher-excited quarkonia. All calculations were implemented in the HELAC-Onia 2.0 framework, using the PDF4LHC15 parton distribution functions, with the D0 fiducial cuts requiring muons from \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) decay to have \(p_T(\mu^{\pm})>2\) GeV and \(|\eta(\mu^{\pm})|<2.0\) at \(\sqrt{s}=1.96\) TeV proton-antiproton collisions.

## Key numbers
- D0 measured cross section: \(27 \pm 42.2\%\) fb for \(\sigma(pp(\bar{p})\rightarrow J/\psi\Upsilon)\times{\rm Br}(J/\psi\rightarrow \mu^+\mu^-){\rm Br}(\Upsilon\rightarrow \mu^+\mu^-)\) in the D0 fiducial region.
- Total SPS cross section (CSM + COM, Set I) for prompt \(J/\psi+\Upsilon(1S,2S,3S)\): \(2.96^{+135\%}_{-56.2\%}\) fb (COM Set I) plus CSM contributions summing to approximately \(0.24\) fb, giving a total SPS yield far below the D0 measurement.
- Upper limit on effective DPS cross section: \(\sigma_{\rm eff}\le 8.2\) mb at 68% confidence level, extracted by subtracting the complete SPS yield from the D0 measurement.
- Color-octet contribution (COM Set I) for direct \(J/\psi+\Upsilon(1S)\): \(409^{+138\%}_{-56.7\%}\pm4.4\%\) fb, the largest single SPS channel.
- Loop-induced color-singlet contribution for direct \(J/\psi+\Upsilon(1S)\): \(56.2^{+264\%}_{-70.2\%}\pm4.7\%\) fb.
- Electroweak contribution for direct \(J/\psi+\Upsilon(1S)\): \(15.8^{+75.4\%}_{-46.4\%}\pm4.6\%\) fb.
- Interference term for direct \(J/\psi+\Upsilon(1S)\): \(-16.6^{+162\%}_{-62.0\%}\pm 4.8\%\) fb, nearly canceling the electroweak contribution in the D0 fiducial region.
- Double-real color-singlet contribution for direct \(J/\psi+\Upsilon(1S)\): \(3.58^{+233\%}_{-66.4\%}\pm4.4\%\) fb.

## Context
This work extends the earlier approximate treatment of Likhoded (2015) for the loop-induced color-singlet contribution and the color-octet calculations of Lansberg (2015) for AFTER@LHC kinematics, providing the first complete SPS calculation for this process at Tevatron energies. The D0 Collaboration (2015) had previously extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff}=2.2\pm 0.7 (stat) \pm 0.9 (syst)\) mb by assuming negligible SPS contributions; the present result refines this by demonstrating that SPS contributions, while small, are not negligible, and yields an upper limit \(\sigma_{\rm eff}\le 8.2\) mb. This value is close to the central value needed to explain CMS \(J/\psi+J/\psi\) data at large rapidity difference (Lansberg, 2014), supporting the importance of DPS in quarkonium-associated production. The large variation of color-octet yields across different LDME sets (Kramer, 2001; Sharma, 2012; Shao, 2014; Han, 2014; Gong, 2012; Feng, 2015) indicates this process can discriminate between competing nonrelativistic QCD fits.
