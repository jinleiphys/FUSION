# Magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of the 229Th nucleus

> We calculate the A and B hyperfine constants for the low-lying states of 229Th3+ using a high-precision relativistic all-order approach. By combining these calculations with measurements of the 6d and 5f hyperfine constants [C. J. Campbell et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 106, 223001 (2011)], we determine the magnetic dipole μ=0.360(7)μ_B and the electric-quadrupole Q=3.11(6) eb moments of the 229Th nucleus. Our value for μis five times more accurate and is 22% smaller than the best previous value μ=0.46(4) μ_B [S. Gerstenkorn et al., J. Phys. (Paris) 35, 483 (1974)], while our value for Q is the same, but 2.5 times more accurate than the 2011 result. A systematic study of hyperfine structure in eight other monovalent atoms supports our claim of 2% level uncertainty for $μ$ and Q in 229Th+3.

# Magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of the 229Th nucleus

## Key claim
The nuclear magnetic dipole moment of 229Th is determined to be μ = 0.360(7) μ_B, which is 22% smaller than the previous value of 0.46(4) μ_B from Gerstenkorn (1974), with the final value taken specifically from the 6d_{3/2} hyperfine constant because of a benchmark comparison with Ba+ that allowed additional improvement of the value.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic linearized coupled-cluster (all-order) method including single, double, and partial triple excitations (SDpT) to calculate theoretical hyperfine constants A/μ and B/Q for low-lying states of 229Th3+. They combined these calculations with experimental hyperfine constant measurements from Campbell et al. (2011) for the 6d and 5f states, extracting μ and Q as ratios of experimental to theoretical values. For the 6d_{3/2} state, the ab initio theory value was scaled by -0.75% using a comparison of theoretical 5d Ba+ values with experimental data, since Ba+ serves as an excellent benchmark due to its known magnetic moment with less than 0.01% uncertainty.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear magnetic dipole moment: μ = 0.360(7) μ_B (2% uncertainty), five times more accurate than the previous value μ = 0.46(4) μ_B from Gerstenkorn (1974)
- Nuclear electric quadrupole moment: Q = 3.11(6) e b (2% uncertainty), 2.5 times more accurate than the 2011 result of 3.11(16) e b
- The value of μ extracted from the 5f_{5/2} state (0.359 μ_B) differs by only 0.38% from the final value of 0.360 μ_B
- The value of Q determined from Coulomb excitation, Q = 3.15(3) e b from Bemis et al. (1988), agrees with the present result
- Experimental hyperfine constant A for 6d_{3/2}: 155.3(12) MHz with 0.8% uncertainty
- Experimental hyperfine constant A for 5f_{5/2}: 82.2(6) MHz with 0.7% uncertainty

## Context
This work extends the hyperfine structure measurements of Campbell et al. (2011) by providing improved theoretical calculations that yield significantly more accurate nuclear moments. The result for μ is 22% smaller than the previous determination by Gerstenkorn (1974), while the Q value agrees with the Coulomb excitation result of Bemis et al. (1988). The authors benchmarked their method against hyperfine constants in eight other monovalent atoms (Ca+, Sr+, Hg+, Rb, Cs, Ba+, Fr, and Ra+), finding that cases with correlation corrections below 30% agree with experiment to better than 2%. This work enables future extraction of the nuclear magnetic octupole moment of 229Th and supports the development of a nuclear clock based on the 229Th isomer transition.
