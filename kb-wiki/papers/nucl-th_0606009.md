# Resonances and fluctuations of strange particle in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions

> We perform an analysis of preliminary data on strange particles yields and fluctuations within the Statistical hadronization model. We begin by describing the theoretical disagreements between different statistical models currently on the market. We then show how the simultaneous analysis of yields and fluctuations can be used to differentiate between the different models, and determine if one of them can be connected to underlying physics. We perform a study on a RHIC 200 GeV data sample that includes stable particles, resonances, and the event-by-event fluctuation of the $K/π$ ratio. We show that the equilibrium statistical model can not describe the fluctuation, unless an unrealistically small volume is assumed. Such small volume then makes it impossible to describe the total particle multiplicity. The non-equilibrium model,on the other hand, describes both the $K/π$ fluctuation and yields acceptably due to the extra boost to the $π$ fluctuation provided by the high pion chemical potential. $Λ(1520)$ and $K^*$ abundance is described within error bars, but the $Σ^*$ is under-predicted to $\sim$ 1.5 standard deviations. We suggest further measurements that have the potential to test the non-equilibrium model, as well as gauge the effect of re-interactions between hadronization and freeze-out.

## Key claim

The equilibrium statistical hadronization model cannot simultaneously describe both the yields and the event-by-event $K/\pi$ fluctuation at 200 GeV Au-Au collisions unless an unrealistically small volume is assumed, whereas the non-equilibrium model (with $\gamma_q > 1$, $\gamma_s > 1$, and a lower freeze-out temperature) can describe both yields and fluctuations acceptably due to the extra boost to pion fluctuations from a high pion chemical potential.

## Method

The author performed a Grand Canonical ensemble statistical hadronization model fit to preliminary RHIC 200 GeV Au-Au data, including stable particle yields, resonance yields ($K^*$, $\Lambda(1520)$, $\Sigma^*$, $\phi$), and the dynamical event-by-event fluctuation $\sigma^{dyn}_{K/\pi}$. The fit parameters included temperature $T$, volume $d\langle V \rangle/dy$, chemical potentials $\lambda_{q,s,I_3}$, and phase-space occupancy factors $\gamma_q$ and $\gamma_s$, with constraints from strangeness, charge, and baryon number conservation. The statistical significance profile in $\gamma_q$ was obtained by minimizing all other parameters at fixed $\gamma_q$ values.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium fit ($\gamma_q=1$) under-predicts $\sigma^{dyn}_{K/\pi}$ by many standard deviations; forcing the fluctuation to fit requires an unrealistically small volume of $\sim 500$ fm$^3$, which then under-predicts particle yields by several standard deviations.
- The non-equilibrium fit yields a freeze-out temperature $T = 140$ MeV and a volume $d\langle V \rangle/dy \sim 1000$–$1500$ fm$^3$, compatible with HBT measurements.
- $\Lambda(1520)$ and $K^*$ abundances are described within error bars; $\Sigma^*$ is under-predicted to $\sim 1.5$ standard deviations.
- The non-equilibrium fit requires $\gamma_q$ well above unity and $\gamma_s$ significantly above 1, consistent with earlier studies by Janssen et al. and Torrieri et al.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model analysis of particle yields (previously applied at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies by Janssen et al., Cleymans et al., Becattini et al., and others) by incorporating event-by-event fluctuations as an additional constraint. It directly addresses the theoretical disagreement between equilibrium and non-equilibrium statistical models, particularly regarding the interpretation of the $K^+/\pi^+$ "horn" and strangeness enhancement. The analysis enables future measurements of cross-ratio fluctuations (e.g., $\sigma^{dyn}_{K^+/\pi^-}$) to falsify the non-equilibrium scenario and to gauge the amount of hadronic re-interaction between hadronization and freeze-out, as proposed by Jeon and Koch.
