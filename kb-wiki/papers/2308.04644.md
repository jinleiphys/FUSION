# Gravitational form factors of the proton from near-threshold vector meson photoproduction

> We embark on a systematical analysis of the quark and gluon gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the proton, by connecting energy-momentum tensor (EMT) and the near-threshold vector meson photoproduction (NTVMP). Concretely, the quark contributions of GFFs are determined by global fitting the cross section of the lightest vector meson $ρ^0$ photoproduction. Combined with the gluon GFFs achieved from heavy quarkonium $J/ψ$ photoproduction data, the complete GFFs are obtained and compared with the experimental results and Lattice QCD determinations. In addition, we use the Resonances Via Padé (RVP) method based on the Schlessinger Point Method (SPM) to obtain a model-independent quark $D$-term distribution by direct analytical continuation of Deep Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) experimental data. If errors are considered, the results obtained by RVP are basically consistent with those obtained by NTVMP. Moreover, the comprehensive information on GFFs helps us to uncover the mass distribution and mechanical properties inside the proton. This work is not only an important basis for delving the proton enigmatic properties, unraveling the secrets of the proton internal nature but also have significance theoretical guiding for future JLab and EICs experimental measurements.

# Gravitational form factors of the proton from near-threshold vector meson photoproduction

## Key claim

The quark and gluon gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the proton can be separately extracted from near-threshold vector meson photoproduction data, with the quark contributions determined from $\rho^0$ photoproduction and the gluon contributions from $J/\psi$ photoproduction, yielding complete GFFs that are broadly consistent with lattice QCD determinations, though the quark $A$-term is approximately half the lattice QCD value, which the authors attribute to the absence of $s$ quark contributions in their calculation.

## Method

The authors performed global fits of the differential cross sections for $\gamma p \to \rho^0 p$ (six center-of-mass energies from 1.92 to 2.36 GeV, with $|t| < 1$ GeV$^2$) to extract the quark GFF parameters $m_q$, $D_q(0)$, and $d_q$, using a dipole form for $A_q(t)$ and a tripole form for $D_q(t)$. The gluon GFF parameters $m_g$, $D_g(0)$, and $d_g$ were taken from a previous global fit to $J/\psi$ photoproduction data (Wang 2022). The total cross sections computed by integrating the fitted differential cross sections were compared with experimental data to validate the fits. Additionally, the Resonances Via Pade (RVP) method based on the Schlessinger Point Method was applied to Deep Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) experimental data to obtain a model-independent quark $D$-term distribution.

## Key numbers

- Quark GFF parameters from $\rho^0$ photoproduction: $m_q = 0.85 \pm 0.05$ GeV, $D_q(0) = -1.76 \pm 0.30$, $d_q = 0.88 \pm 0.05$ GeV
- Gluon GFF parameters from $J/\psi$ photoproduction: $m_g = 1.43 \pm 0.10$ GeV, $D_g(0) = -2.08 \pm 0.25$, $d_g = 0.90 \pm 0.07$ GeV
- Proton $A$-term mass radius: $0.69 \pm 0.04$ fm
- Proton mechanical radius: $0.77 \pm 0.05$ fm
- Proton energy radius: $0.85 \pm 0.06$ fm
- Pressure at the proton center: $p(0) = 1.49^{+0.87}_{-0.60}$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Energy density at the proton center: $T_{00}(0) = 5.40^{+2.54}_{-2.76}$ GeV/fm$^3$
- Ratio of mass radius to electromagnetic form factor radius: $0.82 \pm 0.05$
- The RVP method yields a pole in $D_q(t)$ at $|t| = 0.178$ GeV$^2$
- Zero crossing of the total pressure distribution occurs near $r = 0.63$ fm
- Shear forces distribution peaks near $r = 0.66$ fm

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kharzeev (2021) and Wang (2022) for extracting gluon GFFs from $J/\psi$ photoproduction to also extract quark GFFs from $\rho^0$ photoproduction, providing the first complete separation of quark and gluon contributions to proton GFFs from experimental data. The results are compared with lattice QCD determinations by Hackett (2023) and with DVCS experimental results by Burkert (2018). The authors note that their quark $A$-term is approximately half the lattice QCD value, which they attribute to the absence of $s$ quark contributions. The work establishes a framework for interpreting future high-precision measurements at JLab and the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), and introduces a new definition of the $A$-term mass radius that differs from previous direct extractions from vector meson photoproduction, which the authors argue actually reflect the energy distribution rather than the mass distribution.
