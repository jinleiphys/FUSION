# Collective Hamiltonian for chiral modes

> A collective model is proposed to describe the chiral rotation and vibration and applied to a system with one $h_{11/2}$ proton particle and one $h_{11/2}$ neutron hole coupled to a triaxial rigid rotor. The collective Hamiltonian is constructed from the potential energy and mass parameter obtained in the tilted axis cranking approach. By diagonalizing the collective Hamiltonian with a box boundary condition, it is found that for the chiral rotation, the partner states become more degenerate with the increase of the cranking frequency, and for the chiral vibrations, their important roles for the collective excitation are revealed at the beginning of the chiral rotation region.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a collective Hamiltonian that can describe both chiral rotation and chiral vibration within a unified framework, going beyond the mean-field tilted axis cranking (TAC) approach. The key hedge is that the model treats only the chiral degree of freedom \(\varphi\) as a dynamical variable, neglecting fluctuations in the \(\theta\) direction, and the comparison with exact particle rotor model (PRM) results is made with respect to rotational frequency rather than the observable spin \(I\).

## Method

The authors construct a collective Hamiltonian in the chiral degree of freedom \(\varphi\) using the potential energy \(V(\varphi)\) and mass parameter \(B(\varphi)\) extracted from tilted axis cranking (TAC) calculations for a system of one \(h_{11/2}\) proton particle and one \(h_{11/2}\) neutron hole coupled to a triaxial rigid rotor with \(\gamma = -30^\circ\). The mass parameter is derived from the cranking model using time-dependent perturbation theory, with the vibrational frequency \(\Omega\) set to zero for chiral rotation cases. The quantized collective Hamiltonian is diagonalized using a basis that satisfies box boundary conditions at \(\varphi = \pm \pi/2\), yielding energy levels and wave functions for each cranking frequency.

## Key numbers

* Potential barrier height \(\Delta V\) increases from 1 keV at \(\hbar\omega = 0.20\) MeV to about 2 MeV at \(\hbar\omega = 0.50\) MeV.
* Energy difference between the lowest two levels (levels 1 and 2) decreases from 1.19 MeV at \(\hbar\omega = 0.25\) MeV to 0.01 MeV at \(\hbar\omega = 0.50\) MeV.
* The coupling parameters are \(C_\pi = 0.25\) MeV for the proton particle and \(C_\nu = -0.25\) MeV for the neutron hole, with moment of inertia \(\mathcal{J}_0 = 40\ \hbar^2/\text{MeV}\).
* Potential energy surfaces are calculated at cranking frequencies \(\hbar\omega = 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4\) MeV, with the transition from planar to aplanar rotation occurring between \(\hbar\omega = 0.15\) and 0.20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the tilted axis cranking (TAC) approach, originally proposed by Frauendorf and Meng (1997), by constructing a collective Hamiltonian that goes beyond the mean-field approximation to describe quantum tunneling between left-handed and right-handed chiral configurations. It addresses limitations of the TAC plus random phase approximation (RPA) model used by Mukhopadhyay (2007) and Almehed (2011), which could only describe chiral vibration but not the tunneling in the chiral rotation region. The results are compared with exact particle rotor model (PRM) calculations from Frauendorf (1997), showing reasonable agreement for the yrast band and partner band, though the collective Hamiltonian does not capture the second chiral vibration character seen in PRM and TAC+RPA studies at higher frequencies.
