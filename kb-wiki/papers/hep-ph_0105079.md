# Self-consistent parametrization of the two-flavor isotropic color-superconducting ground state

> Lack of Lorentz invariance of QCD at finite quark chemical potential in general implies the need of Lorentz non-invariant condensates for the self-consistent description of the color-superconducting ground state. Moreover, the spontaneous breakdown of color SU(3) in this state naturally leads to the existence of SU(3) non-invariant non-superconducting expectation values. We illustrate these observations by analyzing the properties of an effective 2-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type Lagrangian and discuss the possibility of color-superconducting states with effectively gapless fermionic excitations. It turns out that the effect of condensates so far neglected can yield new interesting phenomena.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a fully self-consistent description of the two-flavor color-superconducting ground state requires including both Lorentz-non-invariant condensates (such as a time-component diquark condensate) and color-SU(3)-non-invariant expectation values (such as different quark condensates and densities for red and blue quarks), which are usually neglected in the literature but can qualitatively change the ground-state properties.

## Method

The authors analyze an effective Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type Lagrangian with four-fermion interactions, deriving the mean-field thermodynamic potential and a coupled set of six gap equations for the scalar diquark condensate, the time-component diquark condensate, the quark-antiquark condensate, the color-octet quark-antiquark condensate, the total density, and the color-octet density. They solve these equations numerically at zero temperature using a sharp three-momentum cutoff regularization, first for a Lorentz-invariant interaction (equal electric and magnetic coupling constants) and then for a Lorentz-non-invariant interaction with different electric and magnetic couplings.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum constituent quark mass: 407.7 MeV, obtained with cutoff Lambda = 600 MeV, coupling g*Lambda^2 = 2.75, and bare quark mass m = 5 MeV; corresponds to quark condensate phi = -2(245.7 MeV)^3.
* First-order phase transition occurs at critical chemical potential mu_crit = 403.3 MeV for the Lorentz-invariant interaction; at this point M_r = 95.7 MeV, M_b = 95.0 MeV, Delta_1 = 120.0 MeV, and Delta_2 = -8.4 MeV.
* At mu = mu_crit, red quark density rho_r = 0.42 fm^{-3} (about 2.5 times nuclear matter density) and blue quark density rho_b = 0.34 fm^{-3} (about twice nuclear matter density).
* For the Lorentz-non-invariant interaction (g_E = -1.75/Lambda^2, g_M = 4.25/Lambda^2), the phase transition becomes a smooth crossover rather than first order.

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of two-flavor color superconductivity, which typically considers only the scalar diquark condensate delta_1 and the quark-antiquark condensate phi, by systematically including the Lorentz-non-invariant diquark condensate delta_2 (previously discussed by Bailin and Love (1984) and by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999)) and color-octet expectation values phi_8 and rho_8. The authors show that the scalar diquark condensate inevitably induces a non-vanishing delta_2, contradicting the common practice of neglecting it. They also find that the unequal densities of red and blue quarks make a homogeneous system with equal color densities energetically disfavored, suggesting possible domain formation. The work enables future studies of gapless color-superconducting states, though the authors caution that such states may be unstable, similar to findings in the color-flavor locked phase by Alford, Bowers, and Rajagopal (2000).
