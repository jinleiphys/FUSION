# Final-state $NN$-rescattering in spin asymmetries of $d(γ,π^-)pp$ reaction

> The role of the final-state $NN$-rescattering ($NN$-FSI) in the polarization observables of the inclusive reaction $d(γ,π^-)pp$, involving polarization of the photon beam and/or the deuteron target, is investigated. Various single- and double-spin asymmetries are studied with respect to the influence of such interaction effect and numerical predictions are given for forthcoming experiments. It has been found that the effect of $NN$-FSI is quite important for the single-spin asymmetries $Σ$, $T_{20}$, $T_{21}$ and $T_{22}$ and the double-spin asymmetry $T^{\ell}_{20}$, whereas it is much less important for the vector target asymmetry $T_{11}$ and other beam-target double-polarization asymmetries. Furthermore, we found that the inclusion of the $NN$-FSI improves the description of the LEGS data for the linear photon asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including final-state nucleon-nucleon rescattering (NN-FSI) significantly improves the description of the LEGS data for the linear photon asymmetry compared to the pure impulse approximation, but the authors note that discrepancies remain in the region near the Delta-resonance which could be due to higher-order rescattering mechanisms not included in their model.

## Method

The authors calculated the transition matrix for the inclusive reaction d(γ,π⁻)pp using time-ordered perturbation theory, combining the impulse approximation (IA) with a final-state NN-rescattering term. The elementary pion photoproduction operator on a single nucleon was taken from the effective Lagrangian model of Schmidt et al. (1996), and the NN-scattering amplitudes were obtained from separable representations of the Paris potential using the EST method, including S, P, and D partial waves. The deuteron wave function was also taken from the Paris potential model.

## Key numbers

- Photon lab energies studied: 200, 270, 330, 370, 420, and 500 MeV (Delta-resonance region)
- The inclusion of NN-FSI leads to an overestimation of the plane wave results for the double-polarization asymmetry T²⁰_ℓ by about 10% in the energy region around the Delta-resonance
- The vector target asymmetry T₁₁ shows only a few percent change (slight increase in absolute size) when NN-FSI is added
- The linear photon asymmetry Σ vanishes at θ_π = 0°, while a tiny negative value is observed at 180°
- The tensor target asymmetries T₂₀, T₂₁, and T₂₂ vanish at θ_π = 0° and 180° (except T₂₀ which shows small negative values at extreme backward angles above the Delta-region)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Darwish et al. (2003, 2004) that studied unpolarized cross sections and polarization observables in the pure impulse approximation for incoherent pion photoproduction from the deuteron. It also builds on the work of Loginov (2000, 2004) who studied analyzing powers and tensor analyzing powers in the same reaction using a diagrammatic approach. The authors note that their previous results for the linear photon asymmetry showed major discrepancies with preliminary LEGS Spin collaboration data, and this work demonstrates that including NN-FSI leads to better agreement, though higher-order rescattering mechanisms may still be needed for a conclusive interpretation. The predictions are intended for forthcoming experiments at facilities such as MAMI, ELSA, LEGS, and JLab.
