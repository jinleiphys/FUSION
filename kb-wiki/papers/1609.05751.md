# Heavy quark dynamics in QCD matter

> Simultaneous description of heavy quark nuclear suppression factor $R_{AA}$ and the elliptic flow $v_2$ is a top challenge for all the existing models. We highlight how the temperature dependence of the energy loss/transport coefficients is responsible to address a large part of such a puzzle along with the the full solution of the Boltzmann collision integral for the momentum evolution of heavy quark. We consider four different models to evaluate the temperature dependence of drag coefficients of the heavy quark in the QGP. We have also highlighted the heavy quark dynamics in the presence of an external electromagnetic field which develops a sizable heavy quark directed flow, $v_1(y)$, can be measurable at LHC.

## Key claim

The temperature dependence of the drag coefficient is the key factor enabling a simultaneous description of heavy quark nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\), because \(R_{AA}\) develops at early high-temperature stages while \(v_2\) builds up later near the critical temperature; however, the full solution of the Boltzmann collision integral (rather than Langevin dynamics) and hadronic rescattering also contribute to the \(v_2\) buildup.

## Method

The authors solved Langevin dynamics for charm quark momentum evolution in a bulk QGP fireball with initial temperature \(T_i = 340\) MeV and initial time \(\tau_i = 0.6\) fm/c for Au+Au collisions at RHIC. They employed four different models to compute the temperature-dependent drag coefficients: pQCD with running coupling, AdS/CFT with \(\Gamma_{conf} = C T_{QCD}^2 / M_{HQ}\), a quasi-particle model (QPM) with T-dependent masses and bag constant, and a variant of QPM with massless partons but the same T-dependent coupling. For the electromagnetic field study, they included the Lorentz force as an external force in the Langevin equation for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) ATeV with impact parameter \(b = 9.5\) fm.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature in the center of the fireball for Au+Au at RHIC: \(T_i = 340\) MeV, with initial time \(\tau_i = 0.6\) fm/c.
- AdS/CFT drag coefficient parameter: \(C = 2.1 \pm 0.5\) in \(\Gamma_{conf} = C T_{QCD}^2 / M_{HQ}\).
- QPM coupling fit parameters: \(\lambda = 2.6\) and \(T/T_s = 0.57\) in \(g^2(T) = \frac{48\pi^2}{[(11N_c-2N_f)\ln[\lambda(\frac{T}{T_c}-\frac{T_s}{T_c})]^2}\).
- pQCD running coupling parameter: \(a = 1.3\) in \(g^{-2}(T) = 2\beta_0 \ln(2\pi T / (a T_c)) + (\beta_1/\beta_0) \ln[\ln(2\pi T / (a T_c))]\).
- For Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) ATeV and \(b = 9.5\) fm: electrical conductivity \(\sigma_{el} = 0.023\) fm\(^{-1}\), and the directed flow \(v_1\) peaks at rapidity \(y \simeq 1.75\).
- The \(R_{AA}\) develops mostly within the first 3-4 fm/c of QGP evolution.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to simultaneously describe heavy quark \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) that had shown difficulties across multiple models (Rapp, 2010; Alberico, 2011; Gossiaux, 2012; Cao, 2016). It builds on the finding from Das (2015) that \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) are sensitive to different stages of QGP evolution. The study also connects to the recognition of strong magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev, 2007; Fukushima, 2008; Tuchin, 2010) and suggests that heavy quark directed flow \(v_1\) can serve as a probe of the magnetic field, enabling future experimental measurements at LHC.
