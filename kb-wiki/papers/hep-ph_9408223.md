# Chiral Symmetry Restoration and the Georgi Vector Limit

> The chiral phase transition at high temperature and/or density is interpreted in terms of Georgi's vector limit. We discuss three cases as possible support for this scenario: Quark-number susceptibility, cool kaons in heavy-ion process and an instanton-molecule picture for chiral restoration. Both the notion of ``mended symmetry" and the Georgi vector limit are suggested to be relevant in nuclear physics of dense matter.

# Chiral Symmetry Restoration and the Georgi Vector Limit

## Key claim
The chiral phase transition at high temperature and/or density can be interpreted as the restoration of Georgi's vector symmetry, where the hidden gauge coupling constant of the vector mesons vanishes and the pion decay constant becomes equal to the scalar decay constant. The authors propose this as a unifying framework for understanding lattice results on quark-number susceptibility, cool kaon production in heavy-ion collisions, and instanton-molecule models, but note that the perturbative renormalization group results they cite "may not be realistic enough to be taken seriously" and that the gauge coupling likely falls off "much faster than logarithmically" to explain the observations.

## Method
The authors reinterpret the chiral phase transition using the hidden gauge symmetric Lagrangian of Bando et al. (1985), which describes pions and rho mesons in terms of a parameter kappa and a hidden gauge coupling g. They extrapolate one-loop beta functions from Harada and Yamawaki (1993) to argue that as temperature approaches the chiral transition temperature T_chiSR ~ 140 MeV, the parameter kappa approaches zero and the gauge coupling g approaches zero, restoring the Georgi vector limit where f_S = f_pi and m_pi = m_S = 0. They then apply this framework to three cases: quark-number susceptibility from lattice gauge calculations by Gottlieb et al. (1987), cool kaons from the E-814 experiment at Brookhaven, and an instanton-molecule model by Schafer (1994).

## Key numbers
- The chiral transition temperature is T_chiSR ~ 140 MeV.
- The pion decay constant at zero temperature is f_pi = 93 MeV, assumed to remain at this value up to near T_chiSR.
- For the quark-number susceptibility just below T_chiSR, the ratio chi/chi_0 is approximately 0.47, calculated as 1/(1 + 0.5*(T_chiSR/f_pi)^2) with kappa = 0.
- For the quark-number susceptibility just above T_chiSR, the ratio chi/chi_0 is approximately 0.68, calculated from a perturbative one-gluon-exchange model with alpha_s ~ 0.19.
- The cool kaon component from the E-814 experiment (28Si + Pb at 14.6 GeV) shows effective temperatures of 12 MeV for K+ and 10 MeV for K-, compared to conventional predictions of ~150 MeV.
- The freeze-out temperature for pions and nucleons in the AGS experiments is T_fo ~ 140 MeV.
- The expansion velocity of the fireball is v/c ~ 0.1, with an expansion time of ~25-30 fm/c and transverse size increasing from R_T = 2.5 fm to R_T = 6.7 fm.
- The repulsive vector-exchange potential for K+N is V ~ 90 MeV * (rho/rho_0), and the scalar sigma-term attraction is S ~ -45 MeV * (rho_s/rho_0), where rho_0 is nuclear matter density.

## Context
This paper extends the Georgi vector limit (Georgi, 1990) from the large N_c limit to the chiral phase transition in hot and/or dense matter, and connects it to Weinberg's "mended symmetry" (Weinberg, 1990). The quark-number susceptibility analysis builds on lattice calculations by Gottlieb et al. (1987) and the RPA approach of Kunihiro (1991), as well as the dimensional reduction model of Koch et al. (1992) and the perturbative argument of Prakash and Zahed (1994). The cool kaon discussion relies on the work of Koch (1994) and chiral perturbation theory results from Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and others. The instanton-molecule model follows Schafer (1994). The paper suggests that the "mended symmetry" with kappa=0 and g non-zero may also be relevant for nuclear physics below the critical values, connecting to the BR scaling proposal of Brown and Rho (1991) and the work of Beane and van Kolck (1994) on linearizing chiral Lagrangians.
