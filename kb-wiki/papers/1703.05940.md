# A simple model for a scalar two-point correlator in the presence of a resonance

> We present a simple toy model for a scalar-isoscalar two-point correlator, which can serve as a testing ground for the extraction of resonance parameters from Lattice QCD calculations. We discuss in detail how the model correlator behaves when it is restricted to a finite spatial volume, and how the finite-volume data can be used to reconstruct the spectral function of the correlator in the infinite volume, which allows to extract properties of the resonance from such data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple, exactly solvable toy model for a scalar-isoscalar two-point correlator that can serve as a testing ground for extracting resonance parameters from Lattice QCD data, but the authors explicitly note that the model neglects discretization effects and exponentially suppressed finite-volume effects, and should be viewed as an effective description valid only below inelastic thresholds.

## Method

The authors construct a unitary model for phi-phi scattering using a background amplitude with a real parameter alpha and a resonance pole part with an undressed mass m0 and coupling g, then build the two-point correlator from the same ingredients with additional parameters b and tilde{b} for the operator couplings. They derive the finite-volume generalization of the loop function by replacing momentum integrals with discrete sums over quantized momenta in a cubic box of side length L with periodic boundary conditions, and show how the finite-volume correlator becomes a sum over pole terms. The model amplitude fulfills elastic unitarity exactly but neglects all higher-lying multiparticle intermediate states and does not possess a left-hand cut.

## Key numbers

* The free two-particle loop contribution to the correlator asymptotically decays as b^2/(64 pi^(3/2)) * sqrt(2 epsilon_p^(phi phi))/(2M) * e^{-i epsilon_p^(phi phi) t}/(it)^(3/2) for large times t, where epsilon_p^(phi phi) = sqrt(|p|^2 + 4M^2)
* The finite-volume loop function is constructed with a subtraction term containing a sum over nonzero lattice vectors of K0(ML|k|), where K0 is the modified Bessel function, and the exponentially suppressed terms are of order e^{-ML}
* The pole positions in finite volume are shifted from the free values s_n^(0) = (E_+^(n) + E_-^(n))^2 - |q|^2 by the interaction, with s_j(n) = s_n^(0) + O(alpha, g^2)
* The density of poles in the free case (with N=0) gives rho(s)=1, and the spacing between neighboring poles is 4(2pi/L)^2/rho(s_j)

## Context

This work extends the well-developed theory of finite-volume effects in lattice QCD, referencing the classic articles by Luscher (1990, 1991) and Maiani (1990) as well as more recent developments by Hansen (2012), Briceno (2014, 2015), and others. The model provides an explicit testing ground for methods used to extract resonance parameters from realistic lattice data, and can serve pedagogic purposes for students struggling to understand the relation between resonance physics and finite-volume euclidean-time lattice data. The authors note that their model is much simpler than the general treatments cited, having only one more parameter than a Breit-Wigner parameterization.
