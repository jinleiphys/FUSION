# Isospin-breaking two-nucleon force with explicit Delta-excitations

> We study the leading isospin-breaking contributions to the two-nucleon two-pion exchange potential due to explicit Delta degrees of freedom in chiral effective field theory. In particular, we find important contributions due to the delta mass splittings to the charge symmetry breaking potential that act opposite to the effects induced by the nucleon mass splitting.

## Key claim

The leading isospin-breaking two-pion exchange potential due to explicit Delta degrees of freedom contains contributions from delta mass splittings that significantly reduce the charge-symmetry-breaking potential compared to the delta-less theory, with the effect acting opposite to the contributions induced by the nucleon mass splitting.

## Method

The authors evaluated triangle, box, and crossed-box Feynman diagrams with single and double Delta excitations in chiral effective field theory using the small scale expansion, eliminating the neutron-proton mass difference from the effective Lagrangian in favor of new isospin-violating vertices. They Fourier transformed the resulting momentum-space potential into coordinate space and compared the leading-order results in the delta-full theory with subleading-order results in the delta-less theory, using spectral function regularization with a cutoff of 700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Delta-nucleon mass splitting: Delta = 293 MeV
- Pion decay constant: F_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Squared pion mass difference: delta M_pi^2 = 1260 MeV^2
- Neutron-proton mass difference: delta m_N = -1.29 MeV
- Strong nucleon mass shift: delta m_N^str = -2.05 MeV
- Delta mass shift delta m_Delta^1 = -5.3 +/- 2.0 MeV (from average delta mass m_bar_Delta = 1233 MeV)
- Delta mass shift delta m_Delta^2 = -1.7 +/- 2.7 MeV (from average delta mass m_bar_Delta = 1233 MeV)
- Alternative values: delta m_Delta^1 = -3.9 MeV, delta m_Delta^2 = 0.3 +/- 0.3 MeV (from quark mass relation m_Delta+ - m_Delta0 = m_p - m_n)
- Axial coupling: g_A = 1.27
- Delta-nucleon axial coupling: h_A = 1.34 (from SU(4) relation h_A = 3g_A/(2*sqrt(2)))

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin-violating two-nucleon forces in chiral effective field theory, following the earlier delta-less calculations by Epelbaum (2005) which found unnaturally large subleading contributions to the charge-symmetry-breaking potential. The explicit inclusion of Delta degrees of freedom shifts a significant part of the subleading contributions to leading order, similar to the improved convergence demonstrated for the isospin-conserving two-pion exchange potential by Krebs (2007). The results enable future partial wave analyses of nucleon-nucleon scattering and provide input for three-nucleon force calculations, with the delta mass splittings determined in the authors' prior work (Epelbaum 2007).
