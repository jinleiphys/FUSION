# Ab initio studies of double Gamow-Teller transition and its correlation with neutrinoless double beta decay

> We use chiral interactions and several {\em ab initio} methods to compute the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for ground-state to ground-state double Gamow-Teller transitions in a range of isotopes, and explore the correlation of these NMEs with those for neutrinoless double beta decay produced by the exchange of a light Majorana neutrino. When all the NMEs of both isospin-conserving and isospin-changing transitions from the {\em ab initio} calculations are considered, the correlation is strong. For the experimentally relevant isospin-changing transitions by themselves, however, the correlation is weaker and may not be helpful for reducing the uncertainty in the NMEs for neutrinoless double-beta decay.

## Key claim

The paper finds that when all nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) from ab initio calculations are considered together, the correlation between double Gamow-Teller (DGT) and neutrinoless double beta decay NMEs is strong; however, for the experimentally relevant isospin-changing transitions alone, the correlation is much weaker and may not be useful for reducing uncertainty in neutrinoless double beta decay NMEs.

## Method

The authors used three ab initio many-body methods, importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM), valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG), and in-medium generator coordinate method (IM-GCM), starting from chiral nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon (NN+3N) interactions (EM1.8/2.0 and ΔN²LO_GO). They computed ground-state to ground-state DGT transition NMEs and 0νββ decay NMEs for isotopes ranging from A=6 to A=76, then analyzed the correlation between these quantities with mass-number scaling factors A^{-1/3} and A^{-1/6}.

## Key numbers

- Correlation coefficient r = 0.99 for M^DGT versus M^{0νββ}_Tot · A^{-1/3} when all transitions are included, with fit M^DGT = 1.21(3) M^{0νββ}_Tot · A^{-1/3} - 0.32(2)
- Correlation coefficient r = 0.99 for M^DGT versus M^{0νββ}_GT · A^{-1/3} when all transitions are included, with fit M^DGT = 1.57(4) M^{0νββ}_GT · A^{-1/3} - 0.37(3)
- For isospin-changing (ΔT=2) transitions alone, correlation coefficient r = 0.72 (σ_r = 0.08) for M^DGT versus M^{0νββ}_GT · A^{-1/3}
- For isospin-conserving (ΔT=0) transitions alone, correlation coefficient r = 0.95 (σ_r = 0.03) for M^DGT versus M^{0νββ}_GT · A^{-1/3}
- The average excitation energy E_d used in neutrino potentials ranges from 7.76 to 13.71 MeV for candidate nuclei with mass number 48 ≤ A ≤ 150

## Context

This work extends the correlation study between DGT and 0νββ decay NMEs first reported by Shimizu et al. (2018) using large-scale nuclear shell-model calculations, which found a linear correlation attributed to the short-range character of both transitions. The present ab initio results confirm that the correlation is strong when including both isospin-conserving and isospin-changing transitions, but reveal that the correlation is significantly weaker for the experimentally relevant isospin-changing transitions alone. This finding contradicts the QRPA results of Simkovic et al. (2018), which showed no such correlation, and suggests that HIDCE experiments may not provide model-independent constraints on 0νββ decay NMEs as previously hoped. The results enable future work to investigate in-medium renormalization effects and to explore whether other observables might better constrain the 0νββ decay NMEs.
