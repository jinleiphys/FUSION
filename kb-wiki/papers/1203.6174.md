# Linear Responses in Time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Method with Gogny Interaction

> A numerical method to integrate the time-dependent Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations with Gogny interaction is proposed. The feasibility of the TDHFB code is illustrated by the conservation of the energy, particle numbers, and center-of-mass in the small amplitude vibrations of oxygen 20. The TDHFB code is applied to the isoscalar quadrupole and/or isovector dipole vibrations in the linear (small amplitude) region in oxygen isotopes (masses A = 18,20,22 and 24), titanium isotopes (A = 44,50,52 and 54), neon isotope (A = 26), and magnesium isotopes (A = 24 and 34). The isoscalar quadrupole and isovector dipole strength functions are calculated from the expectation values of the isoscalar quadrupole and isovector dipole moments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully self-consistent time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) code using the Gogny interaction with the D1S parameter set can produce stable linear-response results for both spherical and deformed nuclei, but the method cannot separate out the effects of pairing correlations from the dynamical changes of the Bogoliubov transformation matrices.

## Method

The authors solve the TDHFB equations by numerically integrating the time evolution of the U and V Bogoliubov transformation matrices using a predictor-corrector method with the Gogny interaction (parameter set D1S) in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis. The initial condition is an impulse-type perturbation where the static HFB ground state is boosted by exponentiating a multipole operator with a small amplitude parameter epsilon. The strength functions for isoscalar quadrupole and isovector dipole modes are obtained by Fourier transforming the time-dependent expectation values of the corresponding multipole moments.

## Key numbers

- In oxygen-20, the excitation energy deviates from the initial value of 0.02554 MeV to 0.02561 MeV after 4500 fm/c (22500 time steps) of integration
- In oxygen-20, the neutron number is conserved within approximately 10^-5 and proton number within 10^-11 during the time integration
- The center-of-mass fluctuation in oxygen-20 is kept within 2 x 10^-5 fm for isovector dipole vibrations
- The lowest isoscalar quadrupole peak energy in oxygen-24 is 4.1 MeV from TDHFB, compared to experimental values around 4.7 MeV
- The isovector dipole peak in oxygen-20 is located at 25 MeV
- The pygmy state (1- peak) in neon-26 is at 11.9 MeV from TDHFB, compared to QRPA result of 10.64 MeV and experimental value around 9 MeV
- The lowest K=0 (K=2) peak energy in magnesium-24 is 8.06 (3.3) MeV from TDHFB, compared to experimental values of 6.43 (4.23) MeV
- The lowest K=0 (K=2) peak energy in magnesium-34 is 2.07 (3.0) MeV from TDHFB, compared to QRPA results of 2.65 (around 3.0) MeV
- The isovector dipole K=0 (K=1) peak energy in magnesium-24 is 20.2 (28.9) MeV from TDHFB, compared to QRPA results of 19.35 (27.44) MeV

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method to include pairing correlations self-consistently via the TDHFB formalism, building on earlier TDHF calculations by Negele, Maruhn, and Umar (various years) and the canonical-basis TDHFB approach by Ebata (2010). The Gogny interaction is advantageous over Skyrme interactions used in prior QRPA calculations by Khan (2009), Obertelli (2009), Yoshida (2008), and Losa (2010) because the particle-hole and particle-particle channels are treated on equal footing with a natural energy cutoff. The method enables systematic studies of giant resonances and low-lying collective states across the nuclear chart, though the author notes that separating pairing effects from the dynamics requires future improvements such as the canonical-basis TDHFB formulation.
