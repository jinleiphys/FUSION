# A New Class of Three Nucleon Forces and their Implications

> We identify a new class of three-nucleon forces that arises in the low-energy effective theory of nuclear interactions including pions. We estimate their contribution to the energy of neutron and nuclear matter and find that it can be as important as the leading-order three-nucleon forces previously considered in the literature. The magnitude of this force is set by the strength of the coupling of pions to two nucleons and is presently not well constrained by experiments. The implications for nuclei, nuclear matter, and the equation of state of neutron matter are briefly discussed.

## Key claim
The paper identifies a new class of three-nucleon forces (3NFs) arising from quark-mass-dependent and derivative pion-nucleon operators in chiral effective field theory, whose contributions to the energy of neutron and nuclear matter can be as large as the leading-order 3NFs previously considered, but whose magnitude is set by poorly constrained low-energy constants (LECs) that may be significantly enhanced by renormalization group scaling.

## Method
The authors derived the three-nucleon potentials from the D2, E2, and F2 operators in chiral effective field theory, using renormalization group equations to estimate the size of the associated LECs. They then computed the Hartree-Fock energy per particle in uniform neutron and symmetric nuclear matter by evaluating the matrix elements of these potentials in a Fermi gas ground state, performing the momentum integrals analytically.

## Key numbers
- The dimensionless ratio xi = |D2 / C0^2| is predicted to be 0.26 by renormalization group scaling, and the authors explore the range xi ~ 0.1 - 0.5.
- For typical cutoff values, |D2| is less than 1/(5 f_pi^4), corresponding to |D2| less than about 10 fm^4.
- The energy per particle in neutron matter from D2 and F2 contributions can reach about 10 MeV at nuclear saturation density (n_sat).
- The D2 and F2 contributions to the pressure of neutron matter at saturation density are about +/- 1 MeV/fm^3 for delta S0 = +/- 2 MeV, leading to an uncertainty in the parameter L = 3P(n_sat)/n_sat of about +/- 19 MeV.
- From pionic atom data, the authors find -15 fm^4 < D2 + E2 < -1 fm^4.

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory framework for nuclear forces (Weinberg 1990, 1991) by identifying new three-nucleon forces that arise from operators required for consistent renormalization of nucleon-nucleon scattering (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise 1996) and pion-nucleon scattering (Borasoy 2001, 2003). The new forces appear at N3LO in the power counting, one order below the leading N2LO 3NF (Fujita-Miyazawa force), but can be numerically comparable. The results imply that previous chiral EFT predictions for the equation of state of neutron matter and neutron star properties (Hebeler 2009, Drischler 2020, Tews 2024) may have underestimated truncation errors, and that the LECs D2 and F2 should be included in future ab initio calculations of light nuclei and fits to experimental data alongside the standard LECs cD and cE.
