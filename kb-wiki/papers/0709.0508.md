# Effective shell model Hamiltonians from density functional theory: quadrupolar and pairing correlations

> We describe a procedure for mapping a self-consistent mean-field theory (also known as density functional theory) into a shell model Hamiltonian that includes quadrupole-quadrupole and monopole pairing interactions in a truncated space. We test our method in the deformed N=Z sd-shell nuclei Ne-20, Mg-24 and Ar-36, starting from the Hartree-Fock plus BCS approximation of the USD shell model interaction. A similar procedure is then followed using the SLy4 Skyrme energy density functional in the particle-hole channel plus a zero-range density-dependent force in the pairing channel. Using the ground-state solution of this density functional theory at the Hartree-Fock plus BCS level, an effective shell model Hamiltonian is constructed. We use this mapped Hamiltonian to extract quadrupolar and pairing correlation energies beyond the mean field approximation. The rescaling of the mass quadrupole operator in the truncated shell model space is found to be almost independent of the coupling strength used in the pairing channel of the underlying mean-field theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a self-consistent mean-field theory (density functional theory) can be mapped onto an effective shell model Hamiltonian containing both quadrupole-quadrupole and monopole pairing interactions, but the mapping is tested only for deformed N=Z sd-shell nuclei and requires matching two separate energy scales (deformation energy and pairing energy excess) to determine the two coupling constants.

## Method

The authors start from the Hartree-Fock plus BCS (HF+BCS) solution of a mean-field theory, obtaining deformed single-particle wave functions, energies, BCS occupation amplitudes, and the density and anomalous density matrices. They transform these quantities to a spherical basis, project onto a truncated spherical valence space, and construct an effective shell model Hamiltonian containing a spherical single-particle term, a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and a monopole pairing interaction. The two coupling constants are determined by matching the deformation energy and the pairing energy excess between the mean-field theory and the shell model Hamiltonian treated in the HF+BCS approximation.

## Key numbers

- For Ne-20 using the USD interaction: deformation energy 5.64 MeV, pairing energy excess 7.04 MeV, correlation energy 4.1 MeV
- For Mg-24 using the USD interaction: deformation energy 7.05 MeV, pairing energy excess 7.00 MeV, correlation energy 5.7 MeV
- For Ar-36 using the USD interaction: deformation energy 1.27 MeV, pairing energy excess 3.55 MeV, correlation energy 4.0 MeV
- For Ne-20 using the USD interaction: average quadrupole moment 15.4 b^2 (with b the oscillator radius)
- For Mg-24 using the USD interaction: average quadrupole moment 18.0 b^2
- For Ar-36 using the USD interaction: average quadrupole moment -13.5 b^2
- For Ne-20 using the USD interaction: fitted quadrupole coupling constant g_Q = 0.0498 MeV/b^4, pairing coupling constant g_P = 0.2865 MeV
- For Mg-24 using the USD interaction: fitted g_Q = 0.0254 MeV/b^4, g_P = 0.4765 MeV

## Context

This work extends the mapping approach initiated by Alhassid et al. (2006), which only included quadrupolar correlations, by adding monopole pairing correlations to the effective shell model Hamiltonian. The method is tested against the empirical USD shell model interaction of Wildenthal (1984) and then applied to the SLy4 Skyrme energy density functional of Chabanat et al. (1998) plus a zero-range density-dependent pairing force. The approach aims to combine the global validity of density functional theory with the higher accuracy of configuration-interaction shell model calculations, and could enable global studies of nuclear level densities within the shell model Monte Carlo method.
