# In-medium changes of nucleon cross sections tested in neutrino-induced reactions

> Historically studied in the context of heavy-ion collisions, the extent to which free nucleon-nucleon cross sections are modified in-medium remains undetermined by these data sets. Therefore, we investigate the impact of NN in-medium modifications on neutrino-nucleus cross section predictions using the GiBUU transport model. We find that including an in-medium lowering of the NN cross section and density dependence on $Δ$ excitation improves agreement with MicroBooNE neutrino-argon scattering data. This is observed for both proton and neutral pion spectra in charged-current muon neutrino and neutral-current single pion production datasets. The impact of collision broadening of the $Δ$ resonance is also investigated. The absence of $Δ$ broadening is slightly favored, but the larger uncertainties on the pion production data prevent definitive conclusions.

# In-medium changes of nucleon cross sections tested in neutrino-induced reactions

## Key claim
Including an in-medium lowering of nucleon-nucleon cross sections (Li-Machleidt) and density dependence on Δ excitation (Song-Ko) improves agreement with MicroBooNE neutrino-argon scattering data for both proton and neutral pion spectra, but the absence of Δ collision broadening (Oset-Salcedo) is only slightly favored and larger uncertainties on pion production data prevent definitive conclusions.

## Method
The authors used the GiBUU transport model (2023 version) to simulate neutrino-argon interactions, implementing the Li-Machleidt in-medium reduction of elastic NN cross sections as a function of density, and the Song-Ko density-dependent suppression of Δ excitation cross sections. They compared predictions with and without these in-medium modifications, as well as with and without the Oset-Salcedo collisional broadening of the Δ resonance, against MicroBooNE measurements of charged-current muon neutrino (νμCC) and neutral-current single pion production (NCπ0) cross sections on argon.

## Key numbers
- χ²/bin for νμCC proton multiplicity distribution improved from 8.7/4 (free NN) to 2.7/4 (in-medium)
- χ²/bin for νμCC most energetic proton kinetic energy distribution improved from 21.9/15 (free NN) to 6.2/15 (in-medium)
- χ²/bin for the combined νμCC proton spectra (multiplicity, kinetic energy, angle) improved from 69.8/39 to 24.7/39
- χ²/bin across all νμCC measurements (704 bins) improved from 1064/704 (free NN) to 795/704 (in-medium)
- χ²/bin for NCπ0 double-differential measurement (π0 angle and momentum, 24 bins) improved from 20.3/24 (free NN) to 9.3/24 (in-medium without Oset broadening)
- χ²/bin for full NCπ0 blockwise results (78 bins, excluding forward 0p bins) improved from 43.0/78 to 31.9/78
- Coherent pion production cross section estimates from other generators: NEUT 5.4.0.1 gives 8.6×10⁻⁴⁰ cm²/Ar, GENIE v3.0.6 gives 3.7×10⁻⁴⁰ cm²/Ar
- Proton tracking threshold in MicroBooNE: 35 MeV kinetic energy

## Context
This work extends investigations of in-medium NN cross section modifications, previously studied primarily in heavy-ion collisions (TerHaar and Malfliet, 1986; Li and Machleidt, 1993) and giant resonance widths (DiToro, 1998; Wang, 2020), to lepton-induced reactions on at-rest nuclear targets. The authors note that electron-induced reactions like (e,e'p) have minimized final state interactions, reducing sensitivity to in-medium effects. The MicroBooNE data provide lower beam energies than MINERvA (Lu, 2019), limiting initial interactions to quasielastic scattering, meson exchange, and Δ excitation, with low detection thresholds enabling proton tracking down to tens of MeV. This work enables future studies of Δ resonance properties through πN invariant mass distributions, and suggests that measurements of neutron yields and coherent pion production on argon by MicroBooNE or the Short-Baseline Neutrino program could further constrain in-medium effects.
