# Modeling of Real and Imaginary Phase Shifts for $α-α$ Scattering using Malfliet-Tjon Potential

> The real and imaginary scattering phase shifts (SPS) and potentials for $\ell=0,2,4$ partial waves have been obtained by developing a novel algorithm$^{\ref{Fig1}}$ to derive inverse potentials using a phenomenological approach. The phase equation, which is a Riccati-type non-linear differential equation, is coupled with the Variational Monte Carlo method. Comparisons between the resulting SPS for various $\ell$ channels and experimental data are made using mean absolute percentage error (MAPE) as a cost function. Model parameters are fine-tuned through an appropriate optimization technique to minimize MAPE. The results for $\ell=0^+$, $2^+$, and $4^+$ partial waves are generated to align with experimental SPS with mean absolute error (MAE) calculated with respect to experimental data is 3.19, 8.74, 13.06 respectively corresponding to real part and 0.76, 0.76, 0.59 corresponding to imaginary parts of scattering phase shifts.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a complex Malfliet-Tjon potential, combined with a screened Hulthén Coulomb potential, can reproduce both real and imaginary scattering phase shifts for alpha-alpha scattering across a wide energy range (1-120 MeV laboratory energy), but the resulting potentials are strongly angular-momentum dependent, meaning no single potential works for all partial waves.

## Method

The phase function method (PFM), which transforms the time-independent Schrödinger equation into a first-order Riccati-type nonlinear differential equation for the phase shift directly, was coupled with a Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) optimization algorithm. Model parameters for the real and imaginary parts of the Malfliet-Tjon potential were iteratively adjusted to minimize the mean absolute percentage error (MAPE) between calculated and experimental scattering phase shifts for the ℓ=0, 2, and 4 partial waves. The long-range Coulomb interaction was treated using a screened Hulthén potential to avoid the integration difficulties associated with the long-range nature of the Coulomb potential.

## Key numbers

- Mean absolute error (MAE) for real scattering phase shifts: 3.19 (ℓ=0), 8.74 (ℓ=2), and 13.06 (ℓ=4), relative to experimental data
- Mean absolute error (MAE) for imaginary scattering phase shifts: 0.76 (ℓ=0), 0.76 (ℓ=2), and 0.59 (ℓ=4), relative to experimental data
- Coulomb barrier height for the ℓ=0 channel was approximately 0.09 MeV, corresponding to the quasi-bound S-state resonance energy of 0.09184 MeV
- The reduced mass constant ℏ²/(2μ) = 10.44217 MeV·fm² for the alpha-alpha system
- The screened Coulomb parameter aV₀ = 0.2758 fm⁻¹ (fixed for all interactions)
- Laboratory energy range covered: 1 to 120 MeV (center-of-mass energy is half the laboratory energy)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group that used Morse potential (achieving MAPE values of 1.17, 0.69, and 1.77 for ℓ=0,2,4) and double Gaussian potential (MAPE values of 2.9, 4.6, and 6.2) for alpha-alpha scattering phase shifts. It follows the complex potential approach of Darriulat (1965) and Jana, who used complex Woods-Saxon potentials, but replaces them with the Malfliet-Tjon potential. The use of the Hulthén screened Coulomb potential follows the approach of Laha and collaborators for alpha-nucleon and alpha-alpha scattering. The work enables future studies of inelastic scattering processes using the complex Malfliet-Tjon potential and demonstrates that the phase function method is effective for developing phenomenological potentials across a broad energy range for multiple partial waves.
