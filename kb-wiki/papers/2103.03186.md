# Nucleon mass radii and distribution: Holographic QCD, Lattice QCD and GlueX data

> We briefly review and expand our recent analysis for all three invariant A,B,D gravitational form factors of the nucleon in holographic QCD. They compare well to the gluonic gravitational form factors recently measured using lattice QCD simulations. The holographic A-term is fixed by the tensor $T=2^{++}$ (graviton) Regge trajectory, and the D-term by the difference between the tensor $T=2^{++}$ (graviton) and scalar $S=0^{++}$ (dilaton) Regge trajectories. The B-term is null in the absence of a tensor coupling to a Dirac fermion in bulk. A first measurement of the tensor form factor A-term is already accessible using the current GlueX data, and therefore the tensor gluonic mass radius, pressure and shear inside the proton, thanks to holography. The holographic A-term and D-term can be expressed exactly in terms of harmonic numbers. The tensor mass radius from the holographic threshold is found to be $\langle r^2_{GT}\rangle \approx (0.57-0.60\,{\rm fm})^2$, in agreement with $\langle r^2_{GT}\rangle \approx (0.62\,{\rm fm})^2$ as extracted from the overall numerical lattice data, and empirical GlueX data. The scalar mass radius is found to be slightly larger $\langle r^2_{GS}\rangle \approx (0.7\,{\rm fm})^2$.

## Key claim

The holographic QCD construction allows extraction of the tensor gravitational form factor (A-term) from current GlueX data, enabling a first determination of the tensor gluonic mass radius, pressure, and shear inside the proton, contingent on the assumption that near-threshold charmonium photoproduction proceeds primarily through tensor glueball exchange.

## Method

The authors compute the three invariant gravitational form factors A, B, D of the nucleon using holographic QCD in the soft wall construction, where the A-term is determined by the tensor T=2^{++} (graviton) Regge trajectory and the D-term by the difference between the tensor and scalar S=0^{++} (dilaton) Regge trajectories. They compare these holographic results to unquenched lattice QCD simulations of the gluonic gravitational form factors and to empirical GlueX data for J/Ψ photoproduction near threshold, using a dipole approximation to the exact harmonic number expressions for the form factors.

## Key numbers

- Tensor mass radius from holographic threshold: ⟨r²_GT⟩ ≈ (0.57–0.60 fm)², depending on whether κ_N is fixed by simultaneous fit to nucleon and rho trajectories (κ_N ≈ 350 MeV) or by the nucleon mass alone (κ_N ≈ 330 MeV)
- Tensor mass radius from lattice data and global GlueX fit: ⟨r²_GT⟩ ≈ (0.62 fm)², corresponding to dipole mass m̃_T = 1.13–1.124 GeV
- Scalar mass radius from holographic D-term fit to lattice data: ⟨r²_GS⟩ ≈ (0.7 fm)², corresponding to dipole mass m̃_S = 1.00 GeV
- D-term mass radius from holography: ⟨r²_GD⟩ ≈ −(0.87 fm)²; from lattice dipole fit: ⟨r²_GD⟩_lattice ≈ −(1.44 fm)²
- Holographic A(0) fixed to A(0) = 0.58 from lattice data; D(0) = −4 from fit to lattice D-term
- GlueX-extracted tensor mass radius from threshold analysis by Kharzeev (2021): ⟨r²_G⟩ = (0.55 ± 0.03 fm)²; from combined vector meson photoproduction analysis by Wang (2021): ⟨r²_G⟩ ≈ (0.64 ± 0.03 fm)²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous holographic analysis (Mamo and Zahed, 2019) of GlueX data and compares with unquenched lattice QCD calculations of gluonic gravitational form factors by Shanahan and collaborators (2018). The holographic construction ties the A- and D-gravitational form factors, enabling extraction of gluonic pressure and shear distributions inside the proton, comparable to experimentally extracted quark contributions by Burkert and collaborators (2018) and lattice QCD results by Shanahan and collaborators (2018). The approach enables empirical access to the gluonic mass radius, pressure, and shear from existing GlueX data, with further tests possible through future lattice calculations in the unquenched limit and improved photoproduction measurements.
