# Electromagnetic signals from Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV

> We analyse the recently available experimental data on direct photon productions from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV RHIC and from Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV LHC energies. The transverse momentum ($p_T$) distributions have been evaluated with the assumption of an initial quark gluon plasma phase at temperatures $T_i$=404 and 546 MeV with initial thermalisation times $τ_i$=0.2 and 0.1 fm/c respectively for RHIC and LHC energies. The theoretical evalutions agree reasonably well with the experimental observations. The thermal window for the LHC energy is justified through the ratio of the $p_T$ spectra of thermal photons to dileptons.

## Key claim

The measured direct photon spectra from Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC can be reproduced by ideal relativistic hydrodynamics assuming an initial quark-gluon plasma phase, with the theoretical evaluations agreeing "reasonably well" with experimental observations, but only for specific choices of initial temperature and thermalisation time parameters (T_i=404 MeV, τ_i=0.2 fm for RHIC; T_i=546 MeV, τ_i=0.1 fm for LHC).

## Method

The authors used (2+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry to model the space-time evolution of the collision system, employing an equation of state with a crossover transition (width parameter Γ=20 MeV) at T_c=163 MeV. Thermal photon emission rates from the QGP phase were computed using the complete O(αα_s) calculation from Arnold et al. (2001) in parametrised form, while hadronic phase photons included processes such as ππ→ργ, πρ→πγ, and radiative decays of ρ, ω, and other resonances. Prompt photon contributions were evaluated using NLO pQCD calculations scaled by the number of binary collisions, with K-factors (K_γ=1.4, K_brem=2.2 for RHIC; K_γ=3.2, K_brem=2.8 for LHC) to account for higher-order contributions.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature for Au+Au at √s_NN=200 GeV (0-5% centrality): T_i=404 MeV with initial thermalisation time τ_i=0.2 fm/c
- Initial temperature for Pb+Pb at √s_NN=2.76 TeV (0-40% centrality): T_i=546 MeV with initial thermalisation time τ_i=0.1 fm/c
- Transition temperature T_c=163 MeV (from HotQCD and Bielefeld lattice QCD groups), with sensitivity test showing results at p_T=2 GeV are only ~1.2% higher when using T_c=170 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature T_f=120 MeV for RHIC and T_f=113 MeV for LHC (constrained by pion data)
- Thermal photon window at LHC: 1.5 < p_T (GeV) < 4.0, with thermal contribution dominating the invariant yield up to p_T ~ 4 GeV
- Sensitivity to initial parameters: at p_T=2 GeV, results differ by 7.54% when using (T_i=433 MeV, τ_i=0.2 fm) versus (T_i=546 MeV, τ_i=0.1 fm)
- Prediction for √s_NN=5.5 TeV: T_i=843 MeV, τ_i=0.08 fm, charged particle multiplicity dN/dy=2500 for 0-5% centrality

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analyses of direct photon production at SPS energies (Nayak et al., 2010) and RHIC energies (Nayak and Alam, 2007) to the new LHC data from the ALICE collaboration. The paper tests the long-standing prediction by Sinha and collaborators (1989, 1991) that the ratio of thermal photon to dilepton p_T spectra forms a plateau in the window 1.5 ≤ p_T (GeV) ≤ 3.5, which is now validated at LHC energies with an extended window up to 4 GeV. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming data at √s_NN=5.5 TeV and further tests of the thermal photon window across multiple collision energies.
