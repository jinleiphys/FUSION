# In-medium Properties of $螛^{+}$ as a K$蟺$N structure in Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> The properties of nuclear matter are discussed with the relativistic mean-field theory (RMF).Then, we use two models in studying the in-medium properties of $螛^+$: one is the point-like $螛^*$ in the usual RMF and the other is a K$蟺$N structure for the pentaquark. It is found that the in-medium properties of $螛^+$ are dramatically modified by its internal structure. The effective mass of $螛^+$ in medium is, at normal nuclear density, about 1030 MeV in the point-like model, while it is about 1120 MeV in the model of K$蟺$N pentaquark. The nuclear potential depth of $螛^+$ in the K$蟺$N model is approximately -37.5 MeV, much shallower than -90 MeV in the usual point-like RMF model.

## Key claim

The in-medium properties of the pentaquark $\Theta^+$ are dramatically modified by its internal structure: treating $\Theta^+$ as a K$\pi$N molecule state rather than a point-like particle yields a much shallower nuclear potential and a significantly larger effective mass at normal nuclear density.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean-field theory (RMF) to describe symmetric nuclear matter, then calculated the effective mass and nuclear potential of $\Theta^+$ in two ways: (1) as a point-like baryon coupled to the scalar $\sigma$ and vector $\omega$ meson fields with coupling constants scaled from the nucleon values, and (2) as a K$\pi$N bound state where the kaon and nucleon components each couple separately to the meson fields, with the kaon-meson couplings determined from SU(3) relations and KN scattering data. The pion was assumed to be unaffected by the medium.

## Key numbers

- Effective mass of point-like $\Theta^+$ at normal nuclear density: about 1030 MeV (0.67 $M_{\Theta^+}$)
- Effective mass of $\Theta^+$ as K$\pi$N structure at normal nuclear density: about 1120 MeV (0.73 $M_{\Theta^+}$)
- Nuclear potential depth of $\Theta^+$ in the K$\pi$N model at normal nuclear density: approximately -37.5 MeV
- Nuclear potential depth of point-like $\Theta^+$ at normal nuclear density: -90 MeV
- Kaon energy at zero momentum at normal nuclear density: about 527 MeV, nearly independent of model and parameter set
- Kaon potential at normal nuclear density: 33 MeV for RMF model and 28-33 MeV for chiral perturbation theory model

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMF study of $\Theta^+$ (Zhong 2005) that treated it as a point-like particle, and it contrasts with earlier predictions of strong $\Theta^+$ binding in nuclei by Miller and by Cabrera et al. who found attractive potentials of 60-120 MeV. The results show that if $\Theta^+$ is a K$\pi$N molecule, its nuclear potential is much shallower than predicted by QCD sum-rules (40-90 MeV attraction) or the quark mean-field model (about 50 MeV attraction), which has implications for whether $\Theta^+$ can form bound states in nuclei.
