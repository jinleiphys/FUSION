# Faddeev Calculations of Proton-Deuteron Radiative Capture with Exchange Currents

> pd capture processes at various energies have been analyzed based on solutions of 3N-Faddeev equations and using modern NN forces. The application of the Siegert theorem is compared to the explicit use of $π$- and $ρ$-like exchange currents connected to the AV18 NN interaction. Overall good agreement with cross sections and spin observables has been obtained but leaving room for improvement in some cases. Feasibility studies for 3NF's consistently included in the 3N continuum and the 3N bound state have been performed as well.

## Key claim

The authors show that including meson-exchange currents (MECs) via the Siegert theorem or explicit π- and ρ-like currents yields overall good agreement with cross sections and spin observables in proton-deuteron radiative capture, but the agreement is not perfect and leaves room for improvement, especially at extreme angles and higher energies where the Siegert and explicit MEC predictions begin to differ.

## Method

The authors solved three-nucleon (3N) Faddeev equations for the initial pd scattering state and the final ³He bound state using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential. They compared two approaches for including two-body electromagnetic currents: applying the Siegert theorem (which replaces the current matrix element with a density matrix element via current conservation) and explicitly including π- and ρ-like exchange currents derived from the AV18 potential following the Riska prescription. For feasibility studies, they also included the Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon force (3NF) consistently in both the 3N continuum and the 3N bound state.

## Key numbers

- The ³H binding energy from the Faddeev calculation using AV18 without electromagnetic parts is 7.623 MeV.
- At Ed = 29.2 MeV, the Ayy observable at θ = 96° gives 0.0326 for the Siegert approach and 0.0315 for the explicit MEC approach, compared to 0.0339 from Torre's calculation using the Reid soft-core potential.
- The spread among different NN force predictions (using Siegert) is relatively low, at most about 5% in the maximum of the angular distribution.
- The photon lab energy for the inverse photodisintegration process ranges from 10 to 140 MeV across the energies studied.

## Context

This work extends earlier pd capture calculations by Torre (who treated initial and final 3N states correctly with MECs), Ishikawa and Sasakawa (who included 3NFs), and Fonseca and Lehman (who used finite-rank approximations of NN forces). It also builds on low-energy calculations by Friar et al. and Kievsky et al. The results enable future studies comparing different modern NN forces with consistently applied MECs to test the robustness of the dynamical picture, and they highlight the need for new precise measurements at extreme angles to constrain theoretical predictions.
