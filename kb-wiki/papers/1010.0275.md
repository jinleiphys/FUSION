# Dynamical coupled-channels study of meson production reactions from EBAC@JLab

> We present the current status of a combined and simultaneous analysis of meson production reactions based on a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model, which is conducted at Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) of Jefferson Lab.

## Key claim
The EBAC dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model, which previously analyzed reactions separately with only \(\pi N\) and \(\eta N\) channels in the fit, is now being extended to a full combined and simultaneous analysis of \(\pi N, \gamma N \rightarrow \pi N, \eta N, K\Lambda, K\Sigma\) reactions, with all parameters varied together; the results shown are preliminary and further improvements are necessary.

## Method
The reaction amplitudes are calculated from coupled-channels integral equations that include a meson-exchange interaction \(v_{\alpha,\beta}\) (derived from phenomenological Lagrangians) and a term describing s-channel exchange of bare \(N^*\) states with mass \(M^*\). The model unitarizes channels \(\gamma N, \pi N, \eta N, \pi\pi N, K\Lambda, K\Sigma, \omega N\) (with \(\pi\pi N\) containing quasi two-body \(\pi\Delta, \rho N, \sigma N\) channels) and determines parameters by fitting data up to \(W=2\) GeV for hadronic reactions and up to \(W=2\) GeV for \(\gamma N\to\pi N\).

## Key numbers
- \(\pi N\) scattering partial waves were analyzed up to \(W=2\) GeV; the current model shows significant improvement in the \(S_{31}\) and \(D_{35}\) partial waves over the early model (Juliá-Díaz et al., 2007).
- \(\gamma N\to\pi N\) analysis was extended from \(W\leq 1.6\) GeV to \(W\leq 2\) GeV, and now includes all measured polarization observables in addition to differential cross sections and photon asymmetry \(\Sigma\).
- \(\pi^- p\to \eta n\) differential cross sections are described reasonably well up to \(W=1.9\) GeV.
- \(\pi N\to KY\) reactions include data up to \(W=2.1\) GeV for three charge states (\(\pi^+ p\to K^+\Sigma^+\), \(\pi^- p\to K^0\Sigma^0\), \(\pi^- p\to K^0\Lambda^0\)).
- \(\gamma p\to K^+\Lambda\) results are shown at photon energies \(E_\gamma = 1250\) MeV and \(E_\gamma = 1650\) MeV, comparing differential cross sections and polarization observables \(P, C_{x'}, C_{z'}\) with CLAS data (CLAS-g11a, 2010; CLAS-g1c, 2007).

## Context
This work extends the EBAC-DCC analysis program that previously determined hadronic and electromagnetic parameters from \(\pi N\to\pi N\) (Juliá-Díaz et al., 2007), \(\pi N\to\eta N\) (Durand et al., 2008), \(\gamma N\to\pi N\) (Juliá-Díaz et al., 2008), and \(N(e,e'\pi)N\) (Juliá-Díaz et al., 2009), and was applied to predict \(\pi N\to\pi\pi N\) and \(\gamma N\to\pi\pi N\) cross sections (Kamano et al., 2009). The current combined analysis of \(\pi N,\gamma N\to \pi N,\eta N, K\Lambda, K\Sigma\) reactions aims to resolve inconsistencies among data sets (e.g., for \(\pi^- p\to\eta n\)) and to provide a reliable extraction of higher \(N^*\) states above 1.6 GeV, whose existence remains uncertain. Once completed, the analysis will be extended to include \(\pi\pi N\) and \(\omega N\) final states.
