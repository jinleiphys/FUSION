# Conservation and breaking of pseudospin symmetry

> Pseudospin symmetry (PSS) is a relativistic dynamical symmetry connected with the lower component of the Dirac spinor. Here, we investigate the conservation and breaking of PSS in the single-nucleon resonant states, as an example, using Green's function method that provides a novel way to precisely describe not only the resonant energies and widths but also the spacial density distributions for both narrow and wide resonances. The PSS restoration and breaking are perfectly displayed in the evolution of resonant parameters and density distributions with the potential depth: In the PSS limit, i.e., when the attractive scalar and repulsive vector potentials have the same magnitude but opposite sign, PSS is exactly conserved with strictly the same energy and width between the PS partners as well as identical density distributions of the lower components. As the potential depth increases, the PSS is broken gradually with energy and width splittings and a phase shift in the density distributions.

## Key claim

Pseudospin symmetry (PSS) in single-nucleon resonant states is exactly conserved only in the limit where the attractive scalar and repulsive vector potentials have the same magnitude but opposite sign (Σ(r)=0); as the potential depth increases, PSS is broken gradually, with energy and width splittings and a phase shift in the density distributions of the lower components.

## Method

The authors used the Green's function method within a relativistic Dirac framework to study single-particle resonant states in spherical Woods-Saxon potentials. They searched for poles of the Green's function in the fourth quadrant of the complex energy plane to determine resonant energies and widths, and computed spatial density distributions from the imaginary part of the Green's function for both the upper and lower components of the Dirac spinor. The potential depths C (for Σ(r)) were varied from 0 MeV (the PSS limit) to -66 MeV to study the evolution of PSS conservation and breaking.

## Key numbers

- For the resonant state 3d5/2 at potential depth C = -66 MeV, the energy is E = 2.2728 MeV and the half-width is Γ/2 = 1.9949 MeV, determined by searching for poles of the Green's function with an energy step of 0.1 keV in a coordinate space of Rmax = 20 fm and dr = 0.05 fm.
- For the PS partners (3d5/2, 2g7/2) at C = -66 MeV, the complex energies are ε(3d5/2) = 2.2728 − i1.9949 MeV and ε(2g7/2) = 2.5422 − i0.1019 MeV, showing energy and width splitting.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used are: Σ(r) depth C = -66 MeV, Δ(r) depth D = 650 MeV, radius R = 7 fm, and diffusivity a = 0.3 fm.
- In the PSS limit (C = 0), three pairs of resonant PS doublets have exactly the same energy and width, indicating exact conservation of PSS.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of PSS in resonant states by Lu et al. (2012), who gave a rigorous justification of PSS conservation in single-particle resonant states under the same conditions as bound states (Σ(r)=0 or dΣ(r)/dr=0) but mainly used a radial square-well potential and did not provide wave functions in the PSS limit. The present study uses Woods-Saxon potentials and the Green's function method, which treats bound and resonant states on the same footing and provides spatial density distributions for the first time in the PSS limit. This enables future experimental tests of the predicted phase shift in density distributions via low-energy neutron-nuclei scattering experiments.
