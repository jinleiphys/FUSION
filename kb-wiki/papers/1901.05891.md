# Experimental Evidence for Transverse Wobbling in $^{105}$Pd

> New rotational bands built on the $ν$$(h_{11/2})$ configuration have been identified in $^{105}$Pd. Two bands built on this configuration show the characteristics of transverse wobbling: the $Δ$$I$=1 transitions between them have a predominant E2 component and the wobbling energy decreases with increasing spin. The properties of the observed wobbling bands are in good agreement with theoretical results obtained using constrained triaxial covariant density functional theory and quantum particle rotor model calculations. This provides the first experimental evidence for transverse wobbling bands based on a one-neutron configuration, and also represents the first observation of wobbling motion in the $A$$\sim$100 mass region.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first experimental evidence for transverse wobbling bands based on a one-neutron configuration, and the first observation of wobbling motion in the A∼100 mass region, specifically in ¹⁰⁵Pd. The claim is hedged by the fact that the wobbling energy decreases with increasing spin only up to I=29/2, after which the configuration changes and the increasing energy difference between bands is not interpreted as longitudinal wobbling.

## Method

High-spin states in ¹⁰⁵Pd were populated using the ⁹⁶Zr(¹³C,4n) reaction with a beam from the Vivitron accelerator at IReS, Strasbourg. Gamma rays were detected by the EUROBALL IV spectrometer with 15 Cluster detectors and 24 Clover detectors, while charged-particle contaminants were eliminated using the DIAMANT detector array as an off-line veto. The level scheme was constructed from approximately 2×10⁹ triple- and higher-fold coincidence events using the Radware analysis package, and linear polarization and directional correlation from oriented states (DCO) ratios were derived for transitions of sufficient intensity.

## Key numbers

- The ¹⁰⁵Pd nucleus has a triaxial shape with β=0.27 and γ=25° for the ν(1h₁₁/₂)¹ configuration, from constrained triaxial covariant density functional theory calculations with the PC-PK1 effective interaction.
- Multipole mixing ratios for the ΔI=1 transitions from band B to band A: δ=1.8(5) for the 991 keV transition (17/2⁻ → 15/2⁻), δ=2.3(3) for the 1034 keV transition (21/2⁻ → 19/2⁻), and δ=2.7(6) for the 994 keV transition (25/2⁻ → 23/2⁻), corresponding to approximately 80% E2 content.
- The B(E2)out/B(E2)in ratios from band B to band A are 0.66(18) for the 991 keV transition, 0.60(9) for the 1034 keV transition, and 0.34(7) for the 994 keV transition.
- The B(M1)out/B(E2)in ratios from band B to band A are 0.162(97) μN²/e²b² for the 991 keV transition, 0.089(26) μN²/e²b² for the 1034 keV transition, and 0.029(16) μN²/e²b² for the 994 keV transition.
- The pairing gap used in the quantum particle rotor model calculations was Δ=12/√A=1.17 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear wobbling motion, previously observed in odd-proton nuclei such as ¹⁶³Lu (reported by the ¹⁶³Lu collaboration), ¹⁶¹Lu, ¹⁶⁵Lu, ¹⁶⁷Lu, ¹⁶⁷Ta, and ¹³⁵Pr (reported by Matta et al., 2015), to a neutron configuration for the first time. The transverse wobbling interpretation, where the wobbling energy decreases with increasing spin, follows the theoretical framework of Frauendorf and Dönau (2014). The results enable further studies of wobbling motion in the A∼100 mass region and provide a test case for theoretical models of triaxial deformation and wobbling in neutron-rich nuclei.
