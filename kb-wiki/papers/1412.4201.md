# Triaxially deformed relativistic point-coupling model for $Œõ$ hypernuclei: a quantitative analysis of hyperon impurity effect on nuclear collective properties

> The impurity effect of hyperon on atomic nuclei has received a renewed interest in nuclear physics since the first experimental observation of appreciable reduction of $E2$ transition strength in low-lying states of hypernucleus $^{7}_Œõ$Li. Many more data on low-lying states of $Œõ$ hypernuclei will be measured soon for $sd$-shell nuclei, providing good opportunities to study the $Œõ$ impurity effect on nuclear low-energy excitations. We carry out a quantitative analysis of $Œõ$ hyperon impurity effect on the low-lying states of $sd$-shell nuclei at the beyond-mean-field level based on a relativistic point-coupling energy density functional (EDF), considering that the $Œõ$ hyperon is injected into the lowest positive-parity ($Œõ_s$) and negative-parity ($Œõ_p$) states. We adopt a triaxially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach for hypernuclei and calculate the $Œõ$ binding energies of hypernuclei as well as the potential energy surfaces (PESs) in $(Œ≤, Œ≥)$ deformation plane. We also calculate the PESs for the $Œõ$ hypernuclei with good quantum numbers using a microscopic particle rotor model (PRM) with the same relativistic EDF. The triaxially deformed RMF approach is further applied in order to determine the parameters of a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) for the collective excitations of triaxially deformed core nuclei. Taking $^{25,27}_Œõ$Mg and $^{31}_Œõ$Si as examples, we analyse the impurity effects of $Œõ_s$ and $Œõ_p$ on the low-lying states of the core nuclei...

## Key claim

The paper provides a quantitative beyond-mean-field analysis of how a Œõ hyperon in either the lowest positive-parity (Œõ_s) or negative-parity (Œõ_p) state modifies the low-lying collective states of sd-shell nuclei, finding that the impurity effect depends critically on the hyperon orbital and that a mean-field treatment alone is insufficient for quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors extended a triaxially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with point-coupling interactions (PC-F1 for nucleons, PCY-S1 for NŒõ) to Œõ hypernuclei, solving the Dirac equations for nucleons and hyperon in a three-dimensional harmonic-oscillator basis. They computed potential energy surfaces (PESs) in the (Œ≤, Œ≥) deformation plane for the hypernuclei and their core nuclei, then used these PESs to determine the parameters of a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) for the core nucleus, incorporating dynamical correlations from symmetry restoration and shape fluctuation. The method was applied to ¬≤‚Åµ_ŒõMg, ¬≤‚Å∑_ŒõMg, and ¬≥¬π_ŒõSi with the hyperon placed in both Œõ_s and Œõ_p states.

## Context

This work extends previous beyond-mean-field studies of Œõ hyperon impurity effects that were limited to non-relativistic frameworks, such as the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS study of ¬≤‚Å¥Mg by Yao et al. (2011) and the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) studies by Isaka et al. (2012). It also generalizes the authors' earlier triaxial RMF code for ordinary nuclei (Yao et al., 2009) to include Œõ hyperons, enabling quantitative comparison with upcoming experimental data on sd-shell hypernuclei from Œ≥-ray spectroscopy. The results enable future studies of hyperon impurity effects on nuclear fission barriers and neutron star properties.
