# The improved saturation model in nuclei

> We consider the nuclear shadowing in deep-inelastic scattering corresponding to kinematic regions accessible by future experiments at electron-ion colliders. The gluon distribution at small $x$ is obtained using an improved dipole model depended on the impact parameter for atomic nucleus and compared with nCETQ15 parametrization group. The nuclear shadowing at small $x$ is defined within the color dipole formalism with respect to the mass number $A$. Its behavior is predicted for light nuclei in a wide range of the impact parameter $b$ and the transverse dipole size $r$. The nuclear saturation at large-$r$ (small $μ^2$) is observable. The behavior of the nuclear ratio $σ^{A}_{\mathrm{dip}}/σ_{0}$ is similar to the Golec-Biernat-W$\ddot{\mathrm{u}}$sthoff (GBW) model in a wide range of $r$ for light and heavy nuclei at small $x$.

# The improved saturation model in nuclei

## Key claim

The authors claim that an improved dipole model incorporating impact-parameter dependence and nuclear gluon distributions can describe nuclear shadowing in deep-inelastic scattering at small Bjorken x, and that the resulting nuclear dipole cross section ratio σ^A_dip/σ_0 behaves similarly to the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GBW) model across a wide range of dipole sizes for both light and heavy nuclei, provided the nuclear saturation scale Q^2_s,A is properly defined with respect to mass number A.

## Method

The authors employ the color dipole formalism for γ*-A scattering, using an eikonalized dipole cross section that depends on the impact parameter b and incorporates the nuclear gluon distribution g^A(x,Q^2). The nuclear gluon density is obtained from an improved saturation model where the saturation scale Q^2_s,A is scaled by A^(1/3) relative to the proton case, and the nuclear thickness function T_A(b) is modeled using a Woods-Saxon distribution for A>20 and a Gaussian profile for light nuclei (A<20). The resulting dipole cross sections are computed for various nuclei (C-12, Ca-40, Ag-108, Au-197, Pb-208) and compared with the nCTEQ15 parametrization group and the GBW model.

## Key numbers

- σ_0 = 29.12 mb, λ = 0.277, x_0 = 0.41×10^(-4) (parameters from Ref. [16])
- Light quark mass m_l = 0.14 GeV, charm quark mass m_c = 1.40 GeV
- Λ_QCD = 120 MeV (leading order running coupling, giving α_s(M_Z^2) = 0.118)
- B_G = 4.25 GeV^(-2) (impact parameter parameter for proton)
- Nuclear radius parametrization: R_A = 0.82A^(1/3) + 0.58 fm (except deuteron)
- Proton area πR_p^2 = 1.55 ± 0.02 fm^2
- δ = 0.79 ± 0.02 (exponent relating nuclear and proton saturation scales)
- Saturation visible for free proton at r ∼ 1 fm, decreasing as A increases
- Depletion in σ^A_dip/σ_0 occurs in interval 2×10^(-2) fm ≲ r ≲ 5×10^(-1) fm for x < 10^(-3)
- Nonlinear effects visible in range 1.75 GeV^2 < μ^2 < 3.3 GeV^2 at x = 10^(-6) for heavy nuclei
- dσ^A_dip/d^2b increases from approximately 0.1 to 0.5 as A increases from 12 to 40

## Context

This work extends the impact-parameter dependent saturation (IP-Sat) and b-CGC models, originally developed for proton targets by Kowalski and Teaney (2003) and Watt and Kowalski (2008), to nuclear targets using the color dipole formalism. The results are compared with the nCTEQ15 parametrization group (Kovarik et al., 2016) and the GBW model (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998). The paper enables predictions for nuclear shadowing and saturation effects at future electron-ion colliders (EIC, LHeC, FCC-he) in kinematic regions with center-of-mass energy 20 < √s < 140 GeV, where the gluon distribution at small x can be probed with higher luminosity than at HERA.
