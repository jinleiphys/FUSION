# Natural Units For Nuclear Energy Density Functional Theory

> Naive dimensional analysis based on chiral effective theory, when adapted to nuclear energy density functionals, prescribes natural units and a hierarchy of contributions that could be used to constrain fits of generalized functionals. By applying these units, a large sample of Skyrme parametrizations is examined for naturalness, which is signaled by dimensionless coupling constants of order one. The bulk of the parameters are found to be natural, with an underlying scale consistent with other determinations. Significant deviations from unity are associated with deficiencies in the corresponding terms of particular functionals or with an incomplete optimization procedure.

## Key claim

Naive dimensional analysis (NDA) adapted from chiral effective field theory can be applied to Skyrme energy density functionals to define natural units, and the bulk of existing Skyrme parameter sets yield dimensionless coupling constants of order unity when scaled with a cutoff scale of about 700 MeV; however, the limited range of density and gradient terms in standard Skyrme functionals means this is not a definitive test of NDA chiral naturalness.

## Method

The authors scaled the coupling constants of 48 Skyrme energy density functionals (EDFs) into natural units by multiplying each coupling constant by a factor \(S = f_{\pi}^{2(l-1)} \Lambda^{n+l-2}\), where \(f_\pi \approx 93\) MeV is the pion decay constant, \(\Lambda\) is the chiral breakdown scale, \(l\) is the power of densities in the term, and \(n\) is the number of derivatives. They then computed a logarithmic root-mean-square deviation (RMSD) of the scaled dimensionless constants from unity as a function of \(\Lambda\), and examined the distribution of scaled constants across all functionals to identify deviations from naturalness.

## Key numbers

- The optimal cutoff scale extracted from the RMSD minimization is \(\Lambda = 687\) MeV (using the scaled isovector prescription with an extra factor of four).
- The bulk of scaled coupling constants for the 48 Skyrme functionals lie within the interval \((1/3, 3)\), with most between \(1/2\) and \(2\).
- The isoscalar density-dependent coupling constant \(C^{\rho}_{0{\rm D}}\) has an average value of 0.7824, a minimum of 0.2723, and a maximum of 1.2616 across the 48 functionals at \(\Lambda = 687\) MeV.
- The isovector density-dependent coupling constant \(C^{\rho}_{1{\rm D}}\) has an average of \(-2.2010\), a minimum of \(-6.0116\), and a maximum of 1.9790 across the 48 functionals at \(\Lambda = 687\) MeV.
- The isovector surface coupling constant \(C^{\Delta\rho}_{1}\) for the RATP functional is anomalously small at approximately \(-0.0019\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion of naturalness for Skyrme functionals by Furnstahl (1997), which was limited to isoscalar parameters and a small set of functionals. The present study generalizes the scaling to include fractional density dependence and isovector couplings, and tests 48 modern Skyrme parameterizations. The results motivate using naturalness as a guiding principle for constructing new generalized Skyrme functionals, and the authors have created an online converter at massexplorer.org for practitioners to check naturalness of new parameter sets.
