# A relativistic Glauber approach to polarization transfer in 4He(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})

> Polarization-transfer components for 4He(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})3H are computed within the relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA). The RMSGA framework adopts relativistic single-particle wave functions and electron-nucleon couplings. The predictions with free and various parametrizations for the medium-modified electromagnetic form factors are compared to the world data.

## Key claim

The relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) predictions for the polarization-transfer double ratio \(R = (P'_x/P'_z)_{^4He}/(P'_x/P'_z)_{^1H}\) in \(^4\)He\((\vec{e},e'\vec{p})^3\)H show that using medium-modified electromagnetic form factors from either the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model or the chiral quark-soliton (CQS) model yields a better overall description of the world data than using free-nucleon form factors, though the improvement is modest at low \(Q^2\) and the two medium-modified models give similar effects at high \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors computed polarization-transfer components for the exclusive \(^4\)He\((\vec{e},e'\vec{p})^3\)H reaction within the relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA), which uses relativistic single-particle wave functions and electron-nucleon couplings. Final-state interactions (FSI) were treated via Glauber multiple-scattering theory rather than optical potentials, and the calculations were performed in an unfactorized, relativistic framework. Predictions using free-nucleon electromagnetic form factors were compared to those using medium-modified form factors from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model and the chiral quark-soliton (CQS) model.

## Key numbers

- The double ratio \(R = (P'_x/P'_z)_{^4He}/(P'_x/P'_z)_{^1H}\) was computed for \(^4\)He over the range \(0.4 \le Q^2 \le 2.6\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- At \(Q^2 \leq 1\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), the reduction in \(R\) from using CQS medium-modified form factors is smaller than that from using QMC medium-modified form factors.
- At higher \(Q^2\), both the CQS and QMC models predict very similar effects on \(R\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier RMSGA calculations (Ryckebusch 2003, Lava 2004) for polarization transfer in \(^4\)He\((\vec{e},e'\vec{p})\) by introducing alternative medium-modified form factors from the chiral quark-soliton (CQS) model (Smith 2004), which emphasizes the role of the sea and leaves the magnetic moment nearly unchanged, in contrast to the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model (Lu 1998). The results are compared to experimental data from Dieterich (2001) and Strauch (2003). The approach enables further tests of medium modifications of nucleon electromagnetic properties in a kinematic regime where optical-potential methods are less suitable, and it provides a baseline for future studies of final-state interaction effects in exclusive electron-scattering reactions.
