# Precise determination of resonance pole parameters through Padé approximants

> In this work, we present a precise and model--independent method to extract resonance pole parameters from phase-shift scattering data. These parameters are defined from the associated poles in the second Riemann sheet, unfolded by the analytic continuation to the complex pole using Padé approximants. Precise theoretical parameterizations of pion-pion scattering phase shifts based on once-- and twice-- subtracted dispersion relations are used as input, whose functional form allows us to show the benefit and accuracy of the method. In particular, we extract from these parametrization the pole positions of the $f_0(500)$ at $\sqrt{s}=(453\pm 15) - i(297 \pm 15)$ MeV, the $ρ(770)$ at $\sqrt{s}=(761.4\pm 1.2)-i(71.8 \pm 1.0)$ MeV, and the pole of the $f_2(1270)$, located at $\sqrt{s}=(1267.3\pm 1.7)-i(95.0\pm 2.3)$ MeV. The couplings of the resonances to two pions are also determined with high precision, obtaining respectively, $3.8\pm 0.4$ GeV, $5.92\pm 0.15$ and $4.41\pm 0.23$ GeV$^{-1}$. Special attention is dedicated to the systematic treatment of the theoretical and statistical uncertainties, together with their comparison with previous determinations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a precise and model-independent method for extracting resonance pole parameters from phase-shift scattering data using Padé approximants, with the caveat that the method's accuracy depends on the availability of precise input data and derivatives, and that the convergence of the Padé sequence is guaranteed by Montessus de Ballore's theorem only when the amplitude has a single pole inside the convergence disk.

## Method

The authors use the Constrained Fit to Data (CFD) parameterizations of pion-pion scattering phase shifts from Garcia-Martin et al. (2011), which satisfy forward dispersion relations, Roy equations, and GKPY equations. They compute the partial-wave amplitudes and their first four derivatives at a chosen expansion point \(s_0\), then construct sequences of one-pole Padé approximants \(P^N_1(s,s_0)\) to analytically continue the amplitude from the first Riemann sheet into the complex plane. The pole position and residue are extracted from the approximant coefficients, with the theoretical systematic error estimated from the difference between consecutive orders \(P^N_1\) and \(P^{N-1}_1\), and statistical uncertainties from the input data incorporated via Monte Carlo simulation.

## Key numbers

*   \(f_0(500)\) pole position: \(\sqrt{s} = (453 \pm 15) - i(297 \pm 15)\) MeV, with coupling \(|g_{\sigma\pi\pi}| = 3.8 \pm 0.4\) GeV.
*   \(\rho(770)\) pole position: \(\sqrt{s} = (761.4 \pm 1.2) - i(71.8 \pm 1.0)\) MeV, with coupling \(|g_{\rho\pi\pi}| = 5.92 \pm 0.15\).
*   \(f_2(1270)\) pole position: \(\sqrt{s} = (1267.3 \pm 1.7) - i(95.0 \pm 2.3)\) MeV, with coupling \(|g_{f_2\pi\pi}| = 4.41 \pm 0.23\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
*   Optimal expansion point for the \(f_0(500)\) was \(\sqrt{s_0} = 500\) MeV; for the \(\rho(770)\), \(\sqrt{s_0} = 740\) MeV; for the \(f_2(1270)\), \(\sqrt{s_0} = 1270\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the method proposed by Masjuan (2013) for using Padé approximants to extract resonance poles, applying it to the precise dispersive \(\pi\pi\) parameterizations of Garcia-Martin et al. (2011). The results agree well with previous dispersive determinations from Caprini (2005) and Garcia-Martin (2011) for the \(f_0(500)\) and \(\rho(770)\), and with Dai (2014) for the \(f_2(1270)\), while providing comparable or smaller uncertainties. The method enables a simpler, model-independent alternative to full dispersive continuations (such as GKPY equations) that can be applied to other scattering processes where dispersive techniques are difficult to implement.
