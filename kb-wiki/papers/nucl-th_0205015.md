# Relativistic field-theoretical approach to the inverse scattering problem

> The inverse scattering problem for the relativistic three-dimensional equation $\Bigl(2E_{\bf p'}-2E_{\bf p}\Bigr)<{\bf p'}|Ψ_{\bf p}>= -\int V(t)d^3{\bf p''}<{\bf p''}|Ψ_{\bf p}>$ with $E_{\bf p}=\sqrt{m^2+{\bf p}^2}$ and $t=\Bigl(E_{\bf p'}-E_{\bf p''}\Bigr)^2 -\Bigl({\bf p'}-{\bf p''}\Bigr)^2$ is considered. The field-theoretical potential $V(t)$ of this equation is constructed in the framework of the old perturbation theory. It contains all contributions of diagrams with intermediate off-mass shell particles. In particular, this potential reproduces the OBE Bonn model of the $NN$ potential exactly.For the $πN$ scattering it is generated by $σ,ρ, ω$-meson exchange diagrams. The inverse scattering problem is solved by reduction of these relativistic equations to the standard Schrödinger equations $\Bigl(Δ_{\bf r}+{\bf k}^2\Bigr)<{\bf r}|ϕ_{\bf p}>= - v({\bf r})<{\bf r}|ϕ_{\bf k}>$ with $E_{\bf p}={\bf k}^2/2m+m$. The relation between the relativistic potential $V(t)$ and its nonrelativistic representation $v({\bf r})$ is obtained.

# Relativistic field-theoretical approach to the inverse scattering problem

## Key claim

The inverse scattering problem for a relativistic three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger type equation can be reduced to the standard nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation, provided the relativistic potential depends only on the four-momentum transfer squared \(t\). This reduction holds exactly for the off-mass-shell meson exchange part of the NN potential, but the complete NN potential also includes a separately determined on-mass-shell pion exchange part that must be subtracted first.

## Method

The author starts from a time-ordered three-dimensional field-theoretical equation for the NN scattering amplitude, derived via S-matrix reduction techniques. The NN potential is separated into two parts: a nonlocal on-mass-shell meson exchange potential (constructed from πN vertex functions obtained via dispersion relations) and an off-mass-shell potential \({\cal Y}(t)\) generated by equal-time commutators. The inverse problem is solved for the \({\cal Y}(t)\) part by introducing auxiliary momentum variables that map the relativistic equation into a standard Schrödinger equation, allowing application of well-known nonrelativistic inverse scattering methods.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic energy relation \(E_{\bf p} = \sqrt{m^2 + {\bf p}^2}\) is used, with the NN total energy \(E({\bf p}) = 2E_{\bf p} \equiv 2\sqrt{{\bf p}^2 + m_N^2}\).
- The transformation to nonrelativistic variables uses \(k^2 = m_N(2E_{\bf p} - 2m_N)\) and \({\bf k}/k = {\bf p}/p\).
- The four-momentum transfer is defined as \(t = (E_{{\bf p}'} - E_{{\bf p}''})^2 - ({\bf p}' - {\bf p}'')^2\).
- The off-mass-shell potential \({\cal Y}(t)\) is expressed via meson propagators with denominators \(t - m_\sigma^2\), \(t - m_\pi^2\), and \(t - m_V^2\) for \(\sigma\), \(\pi\), and vector mesons respectively.

## Context

This work extends the inverse scattering formalism from one-particle relativistic equations (Dirac, Klein-Gordon) to a field-theoretical many-body context, specifically the NN system. The potential \({\cal Y}(t)\) reproduces exactly the Bonn one-boson-exchange (OBE) NN potential model, but without neglecting the off-mass-shell dependence of meson-nucleon vertices as was done in the original Bonn derivation. The method enables determination of the off-mass-shell meson exchange potential from NN phase shifts using standard nonrelativistic inversion techniques, and can be extended to πN and γN scattering reactions. The approach is contrasted with quasipotential reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, which suffer from "three-dimensional ambiguities" that this time-ordered formulation avoids.
