# Coulomb Excitation of Double Giant Dipole Resonances

> We implement the Brink-Axel hypothesis for the excitation of the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR): The background states which couple to the one-phonon giant dipole resonance are themselves capable of dipole absorption. These states (and the ones which couple to the two-phonon resonance) are described in terms of the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble of random matrices. We use second-order time-dependent perturbation theory and calculate analytically the ensemble-averaged cross section for excitation of the DGDR. Numerical calculations illuminate the mechanism and the dependence of the cross section on the various parameters of the theory, and are specifically performed for the reaction $^{208}$Pb + $^{208}$Pb at a projectile energy of 640 MeV/nucleon. We show that the contribution of the background states to the excitation of the DGDR is significant. We find that the width of the DGDR, the energy-integrated cross section and the ratio of this quantity over the energy-integrated cross section for the single giant dipole resonance, all agree with experiment within experimental errors. We compare our approach with that of Carlson {\it et al.} who have used a similar physical picture.

## Key claim

The Brink-Axel hypothesis, that background states coupled to the one-phonon giant dipole resonance are themselves capable of dipole absorption, produces a significant contribution to the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) excitation cross section, and the resulting width, energy-integrated cross section, and their ratio to the single GDR values all agree with experiment within experimental errors, but only under the assumption that the spreading widths for the one-phonon and two-phonon couplings are equal.

## Method

The authors model the projectile Hamiltonian using the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) of random matrices to describe the complex background states that couple to the collective one-phonon and two-phonon states. They implement second-order time-dependent perturbation theory for relativistic Coulomb excitation of the projectile in a collision with a point-like target, treating the relative motion classically. The ensemble-averaged cross section for DGDR excitation is derived analytically using supersymmetry techniques, with numerical calculations performed specifically for the reaction 208Pb + 208Pb at a projectile energy of 640 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The measured cross sections for DGDR excitation are enhanced by factors ranging from 1.3 to 2 compared to the predictions of the harmonic picture (cited from Bau86, Aum95, Eml94).
- The experimentally determined widths of the DGDR are close to √2 times the width of the GDR.
- For 208Pb, the fluctuating part of the three-point functions contributes less than one part in 10^4, justifying the factorization approximation.
- The spreading width Γ↓₁₀ of the GDR is expressed as 2πv²/d, where d is the mean level spacing of the background states and v² is the mean square coupling matrix element.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Carlson et al. (1999), who used a similar physical picture based on the Brink-Axel hypothesis but with approximations not based on a small-parameter expansion. The present paper provides a formulation with perfectly controlled approximations using random-matrix theory, enabling analytical calculation of the ensemble-averaged cross section. The results enable quantitative comparison with experimental data for 208Pb and provide a framework for understanding the enhancement of DGDR cross sections beyond the harmonic oscillator prediction, which had been observed by Schmidt et al. (1993), Aumann et al. (1993), and Ritman et al. (1993).
