# A family of three maximally symmetric boost-invariant flows in relativistic hydrodynamics

> I discuss the constructions of boost-invariant dissipative conformal hydrodynamic flows by elaborating on the geometric procedure by Gubser and Yarom, which starts from a static, maximally symmetric flow on dS$_3\times\mathbb{R}$. Three foliations of dS$_3$ preserve three-dimensional non-Abelian isometry groups, namely, the flat ISO(2)-invariant, the spherical (closed) SO(3)-invariant, and the hyperbolic (open) SO(2,1)-invariant slicings. I show that the fluids that preserve these symmetries, after they have been Weyl transformed to flat spacetime, give rise to three physically distinct and boost-invariant solutions of the relativistic dissipative Navier-Stokes equations: the well-known and widely studied Bjorken and Gubser flows, and a seemingly thus far unexplored solution that arises from the hyperbolic slicing of dS$_3$. The new solution combines the radial expansion characteristic of the Gubser flow with the late-proper-time applicability of Bjorken's solution, and features a finite, radially bounded droplet whose expanding edge resembles a free-streaming shockwave.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three distinct boost-invariant conformal hydrodynamic flows, the well-known Bjorken and Gubser flows, plus a previously unexplored third solution, arise as equivalent members of a single family, each corresponding to a different maximally symmetric foliation (flat, spherical, or hyperbolic) of an auxiliary three-dimensional de Sitter space. The new hyperbolic-slicing solution combines radial expansion with late-time applicability and features a finite, radially bounded droplet whose expanding edge resembles a free-streaming shockwave.

## Method

The author starts from a static, maximally symmetric ideal conformal fluid on dS₃ × ℝ, then foliates dS₃ in three ways that preserve three-dimensional non-Abelian isometry groups: flat ISO(2)-invariant, spherical SO(3)-invariant, and hyperbolic SO(2,1)-invariant slicings. Solving the conservation equations for each foliation yields three ρ-dependent energy densities, which are then Weyl-transformed to flat Minkowski spacetime in Milne coordinates to obtain the physical flows. Viscous corrections are added by including shear viscosity η = H₀ ε^(3/4) and solving the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations, giving temperature fields T_κ(τ,r) for each case.

## Key numbers

- Bjorken flow (κ = 0) temperature: T₀(τ) = CT₀/τ^(1/3) − H₀/(2τ); becomes unphysical (negative) at τ = (H₀/(2 CT₀))^(3/2)
- Gubser flow (κ = +1) temperature: T_{+1}(τ,r) = (1/(τ(g²+1)^(1/3))) [CT_{+1} − (H₀ g³/9) ₂F₁(3/2,7/6;5/2;−g²)] with g = (1−q²(τ²−r²))/(2qτ)
- New hyperbolic flow (κ = −1) temperature: T_{-1}(τ,r) = (1/(τ(h²−1)^(1/3))) [CT_{-1} − (H₀ h³/(h²−1)^(1/6)) ₂F₁(4/3,1;5/6;−(h²−1))] with h = (1+q²(τ²−r²))/(2qτ)
- New solution exists only within the future lightcone: τ − r > 1/q
- For τ → ∞, Bjorken flow scales as T₀ ∼ CT₀/τ^(1/3); both κ = ±1 flows scale as T_{\pm 1} ∼ ∓ H₀/(2τ)
- The Gubser flow expansion rate ∇_μ u^μ lies in the range −2 < ∇_μ u^μ < 2; the hyperbolic flow has ∇_μ u^μ > 2

## Context

This work extends the geometric construction of boost-invariant flows by Gubser and Yarom (2010), who showed that the Gubser flow arises from spherical foliation of dS₃. The paper shows that the Bjorken flow (Bjorken, 1982) and the Gubser flow are not hierarchical but rather two members of a three-member family, with the third hyperbolic-slicing solution being previously unexplored. The new solution enables analytic modeling of finite plasma droplets with radial expansion and a causal boundary, potentially relevant for describing the transition from hydrodynamic to ballistic expansion in heavy-ion collisions, and for benchmarking numerical simulations with finite spatial support.
