# Low-lying dipole response: isospin character and collectivity in ${}^{68}$Ni, ${}^{132}$Sn and ${}^{208}$Pb

> The isospin character, the collective or single-particle nature, and the sensitivity to the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy of the low-energy isovector dipole response (known as pygmy dipole resonance) are nowadays under debate. In the present work we study, within the fully self-consistent non-relativistic mean field (MF) approach based on Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA), the measured even-even nuclei ${}^{68}$Ni, ${}^{132}$Sn and ${}^{208}$Pb. To analyze the model dependence in the predictions of the pygmy dipole strength, we employ three different Skyrme parameter sets. We find that both the isoscalar and the isovector dipole responses of all three nuclei show a low-energy peak that increases in magnitude, and is shifted to larger excitation energies, with increasing values of the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation. We highlight the fact that the collectivity associated with the RPA state(s) contributing to this peak is different in the isoscalar and isovector case, or in other words it depends on the external probe. While the response of these RPA states to an isovector operator does not show a clear collective nature, the response to an isoscalar operator is recognizably collective, for {\it all} analyzed nuclei and {\it all} studied interactions.

# Low-lying dipole response: isospin character and collectivity in ⁶⁸Ni, ¹³²Sn and ²⁰⁸Pb

## Key claim

The low-energy peak in the dipole response (pygmy dipole strength) of neutron-rich nuclei shows a qualitatively different degree of collectivity depending on the external probe: while the response to an isovector operator does not display a clear collective nature, the response to an isoscalar operator is recognizably collective for all three studied nuclei and all three Skyrme interactions. This conclusion is conditional on the fully self-consistent non-relativistic mean-field plus RPA framework and the specific Skyrme parameter sets employed.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations for the spherical, double-magic nuclei ⁶⁸Ni, ¹³²Sn and ²⁰⁸Pb, using three different Skyrme parameter sets (SGII, SLy5 and SkI3) that span a broad range of values for the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation (L parameter). They analyzed the isoscalar and isovector dipole strength functions, transition densities, and the coherence of particle-hole amplitudes contributing to the RPA-pygmy state to assess its collective or single-particle character.

## Key numbers

- The L parameter values for the three Skyrme interactions are: SGII = 37.63 MeV, SLy5 = 48.27 MeV, SkI3 = 100.52 MeV.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the RPA-pygmy state excitation energies are: SGII = 7.61 MeV, SLy5 = 7.74 MeV, SkI3 = 8.01 MeV; these agree with the experimental centroid of 7.37 MeV within a 6-8 MeV window (Ryez 2002).
- For ¹³²Sn, the RPA-pygmy state excitation energies are: SGII = 8.52 MeV, SLy5 = 8.64 MeV, SkI3 = 9.23 MeV; the experimental peak is at 9.8 ± 0.7 MeV (Adri 2005).
- For ⁶⁸Ni, the RPA-pygmy state excitation energies are: SGII = 9.77 MeV, SLy5 = 9.30 MeV, SkI3 = 10.45 MeV; the experimental peak is at 11 MeV with width less than 1 MeV (Weil 2009).
- In single-particle units (Weisskopf units), the isovector B(E1) of the RPA-pygmy state ranges from 1.1 to 6.4 s.p.u. across all nuclei and interactions, while the isoscalar B(E1) ranges from 9 to 20 s.p.u.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) peak energy is predicted at 12-13 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 13.43 MeV with total width 2.42 MeV (Berm 1975); the isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) is predicted at 20-21 MeV, compared to experimental 20.1-20.5 MeV (Garg 1999).

## Context

This work extends the debate on the nature of the pygmy dipole resonance, which has been interpreted either as a collective oscillation of the neutron skin against the core (Klim 2007, Carb 2010, Piek 2011) or as a single-particle shell effect (Rein 2010). The authors find that the collectivity depends on the probe operator, reconciling aspects of both views. The results enable future studies of how different experimental probes (e.g., photonuclear reactions versus alpha scattering) will see the same RPA-pygmy state with different apparent collectivity, and support the linear correlation between the energy-weighted sum rule of the PDS and the slope of the symmetry energy L reported by Carb 2010. A similar analysis was performed concurrently by Yuks 2011.
