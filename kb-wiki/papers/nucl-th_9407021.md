# The Role of the $Δ(1920)$ Resonance for Kaon Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The long mean free path of $K^+$ mesons in nuclear matter makes this particle a suitable messenger for the dynamics of nucleus-nucleus reactions at intermediate energies (100 MeV to 3 GeV per nucleon). A prerequisite for this is the knowledge of the elementary production cross sections $πN \rightarrow ΣK$. Here these cross sections are studied for the first time with the explicite inclusion of the relevant baryon resonances up to 2 GeV as intermediate states. The baryon resonances -- $N(1710)\, I(J^P) = \frac{1}{2} (\frac{1}{2}^+),\, N(1720)\, \frac{1}{2} (\frac{3}{2}^+)$ and $Δ(1920)\, \frac{3}{2} (\frac{3}{2}^+)\,$ -- are taken into account coherently in the calculations of the $πN \rightarrow ΣK$ process. (We refer to this model as the `resonance model'.) Also $K^*(892)\frac{1}{2} (1^-)$ vector meson exchange is included. It is shown that the total cross sections for different channels of the $πN \rightarrow Σk$ reactions, i.e. $π^+ p \rightarrow Σ^+ K^+$, $π^- p \rightarrow Σ^- K^+$, $π^+ n \rightarrow Σ^0 K^+$ ($π^- p \rightarrow Σ^- K^+$) and $π^0 p \rightarrow Σ^0 K^+$ differ not only by absolute values but also by their energy dependence. This shape differences are due to the mixture of the isospin $I = 3/2$ $Δ(1920)$ with isospin $I = 1/2$ nucleon resonances. However, this $I = 3/2$ resonance does not give a contribution to the $πN \rightarrow ΛK$ reactions. So the shapes of the total cross sections $πN \rightarrow ΛK$ for different isospin projections are the same. ## Key claim

The isospin I=3/2 Δ(1920) resonance differentiates the energy dependence of total cross sections for different channels of πN → ΣK reactions, unlike the πN → ΛK reactions where only I=1/2 nucleon resonances contribute and all channels share the same energy dependence. The model's success in reproducing the second peak of π⁻p → Σ⁻K⁺ around √s = 1.95 GeV depends on rescaling the Δ(1920) coupling constants from their branching-ratio values, which otherwise underestimate the π⁺p → Σ⁺K⁺ cross section by about a factor of 12.

## Method

The authors calculated total cross sections for πN → ΣK reactions using a coherent resonance model that includes s-channel exchanges of the N(1710), N(1720), and Δ(1920) baryon resonances, plus t-channel K*(892) vector meson exchange, with effective interaction Lagrangians and Breit-Wigner propagators including full decay widths. Coupling constants were determined from experimental branching ratios for the resonances, except for the Δ(1920) couplings and the K*(892)ΣN coupling, which were fitted to reproduce the π⁺p → Σ⁺K⁺ data; relative signs among interference terms were fixed by best fit to π⁻p → Σ⁻K⁺ data. Parametrizations of the total cross sections for each separate channel were provided for use in heavy-ion collision simulations.

## Key numbers

- The π⁺p → Σ⁺K⁺ total cross section has a peak value of about 0.7 mb located around √s = 1.92 GeV (experimental data from Baldini et al.).
- The incoherent Δ(1920) contribution to each channel at the peak is estimated as: 0.08 mb for channels in eq. (iso1), 0.16 mb for channels in eqs. (iso2) and (iso3), and 0.32 mb for channels in eq. (iso4).
- The cut-off parameters used are: Λ_C = 0.8 GeV for N(1710) and N(1720), Λ_C = 0.5 GeV for Δ(1920), and Λ_C = 1.2 GeV for the K*(892)ΣN vertex.
- The parametrization for π⁺p → Σ⁺K⁺ is: σ = 0.03591 (√s-1.688)^0.9541 / [(√s-1.890)^2+0.01548] + 0.1594 (√s-1.688)^0.01056 / [(√s-3.000)^2+0.9412] mb, valid for √s ≥ 1.688 GeV.
- The parametrization for π⁻p → Σ⁻K⁺ is: σ = 0.009803 (√s-1.688)^0.6021 / [(√s-1.742)^2+0.006583] + 0.006521 (√s-1.688)^1.4728 / [(√s-1.940)^2+0.006248] mb.
- The parametrization for π⁺n → Σ⁰K⁺ and π⁰n → Σ⁻K⁺ is: σ = 0.05014 (√s-1.688)^1.2878 / [(√s-1.730)^2+0.006455] mb.
- The parametrization for π⁰p → Σ⁰K⁺ is: σ = 0.003978 (√s-1.688)^0.5848 / [(√s-1.740)^2+0.006670] + 0.04709 (√s-1.688)^2.1650 / [(√s-1.905)^2+0.006358] mb.

## Context

This work extends earlier kaon production studies that used simple Breit-Wigner parametrizations without including interferences among resonances (e.g., Brown et al., Ko et al.) and that did not investigate πN → ΣK reactions. The parametrizations provided are intended for use in Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) and quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations of heavy-ion collisions, replacing the widely used parametrizations by Randrup and Ko (1980) for B₁B₂ → B₃YK and by Cugnon and Lombard (1984) for πN → YK. The failure of the branching-ratio-determined coupling constants (set 2) to simultaneously reproduce π⁺p → Σ⁺K⁺ and π⁻p → Σ⁻K⁺ data suggests the possible existence of other I=3/2 resonances with masses around 1.9 GeV.
