# The Convergence Problem Of Gradient Expansion In The Relaxation Time Approximation

> We obtain a formal integral solution to the 3+1 D Boltzmann Equation in relaxation time approximation. The gradient series obtained from this integral solution contains exponentially decaying non-hydrodynamic terms. It is shown that this gradient expansion can have a finite radius of convergence under certain assumptions of analyticity. We then argue that, in the relaxation time model, proximity to local thermal equilibrium is not necessary for the system to be described by hydrodynamic equations.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the gradient expansion of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation can have a finite radius of convergence under assumptions of analyticity, and that proximity to local thermal equilibrium is not necessary for a hydrodynamic description as long as the exponentially decaying non-hydrodynamic transient terms are sufficiently small.

## Method

The authors obtain a formal integral solution to the 3+1D relativistic Boltzmann equation with the Anderson-Witting relaxation time approximation kernel using characteristic curves (free particle trajectories). They then reparametrize the integration variable in terms of the damping exponent and derive the exact gradient expansion as a series containing both the Chapman-Enskog gradient series and exponentially decaying non-hydrodynamic terms. The convergence properties are analyzed using the Lagrange-Burmann inversion theorem and comparison tests between the full series and the Lie series representation.

## Key numbers

- The nth order coefficient in the gradient expansion contains the factor \(1 - e^{-\xi_0} \sum_{k=0}^{n} \frac{\xi_0^k}{k!}\), where \(\xi_0 = \xi(t,t_0)\) is the damping exponent integrated from initial time \(t_0\) to time \(t\)
- The lower incomplete gamma function \(\gamma(n+1,\xi_0) = n!\left[1 - e^{-\xi_0} \sum_{k=0}^{n} \frac{\xi_0^k}{k!}\right]\) grows asymptotically as \(\gamma(n,\xi) \sim \frac{e^{-\xi}\xi^n}{n} \left[1 + O(\xi/n)\right]\)
- If gradient terms grow as \(n! K^n\) with \(|K| < \infty\), the nth order term in the exact expansion grows as \(\sim \xi^n K^n\), reducing divergence from factorial to geometric; convergence is guaranteed when \(\xi K < 1\)
- For large n, the decay time of non-hydrodynamic terms scales as \(\xi_0 \sim n\), giving a length scale \(\sigma_n \sim \sigma_0 n\) where \(\sigma_0\) is the zeroth-order relaxation scale

## Context

This work extends the analysis of McNelis (2020) who obtained the transient structure for the relaxation time Boltzmann equation up to finite orders and conjectured that the exact solution is the Borel re-summation of the gradient expansion. The paper rigorously proves this conjecture and generalizes it to 3+1 dimensions. The results contradict earlier studies (PhysRevLett.110.211602, Buchel 2016, Denicol 2016, Heller 2015) that found the gradient expansion to be divergent with zero radius of convergence due to factorial growth. The work enables a rigorous understanding of hydrodynamization in the relaxation time model and provides a foundation for studying whether similar convergence properties hold for the full nonlinear Boltzmann collision kernel.
