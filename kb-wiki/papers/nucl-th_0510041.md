# The Nuclear matter stability in a non-local chiral quark model

> We study the stability of the nuclear matter in a non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We work out the equation of state in a relativistic Faddeev approach where we take into account the internal structure of nucleon. We show that the binding energy saturates when the nucleon as a composite particle made of quarks is incorporated. After truncation of the two-body channels to the scalar and axial-vector diquarks, a relativistic Faddeev equation for nucleon bound states in medium is solved in the covariant diquark-quark picture. We investigate the nucleon properties in the nuclear medium such as the role of diquarks within the nucleon and the in-medium modification of the nucleon mass and size.

## Key claim

The authors show that nuclear matter saturation can be achieved in a non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model when the nucleon is treated as a composite object made of quarks and diquarks, but only after solving the relativistic Faddeev equation without invoking the static approximation and including both scalar and axial-vector diquark channels.

## Method

The authors construct a non-local NJL model with a Gaussian form factor and fix parameters by fitting the pion mass and decay constant. They introduce scalar and axial-vector diquark channels and solve the relativistic Faddeev equation for the nucleon as a bound state of a diquark and a quark in the medium, using a covariant diquark-quark picture. The nuclear matter equation of state is then obtained by treating these composite nucleons in a mean field approximation, with the vector meson coupling adjusted to reproduce the empirical binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass is fixed to 139.6 MeV and the pion decay constant to 92.4 MeV for parameter fitting.
- Three parameter sets are analyzed: set A (cutoff Lambda = 1046.8 MeV, constituent quark mass M(0) = 297.9 MeV), set B (Lambda = 1008.1 MeV, M(0) = 313 MeV), and set C (Lambda = 847.8 MeV, M(0) = 351.6 MeV).
- The current quark mass is 7.9 MeV for set A, 8.5 MeV for set B, and 11.13 MeV for set C.
- The quark condensate values are 215 MeV (set A), 212 MeV (set B), and 191 MeV (set C) to the one-third power.
- The Fermi momentum at nuclear matter saturation is approximately 262 MeV, comparable to 2 times the pion mass (about 280 MeV).
- The delta-nucleon mass splitting is 293 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear matter in chiral quark models, which had failed to produce saturation (e.g., Buballa 1996, Bentz and Thomas 2001). It contrasts with the Walecka model, which achieves saturation phenomenologically but lacks chiral symmetry. The authors improve upon the approach of Bentz and Thomas (2001) by including both scalar and axial-vector diquark channels and solving the Faddeev equation without the static approximation, which had introduced unphysical in-medium singularities. This enables a unified description of the nucleon internal structure and nuclear matter properties within a single framework.
