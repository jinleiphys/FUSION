# Nuclear matter with off-shell propagation

> Symmetric nuclear matter is studied within the conserving, self-consistent T-matrix approximation. This approach involves off-shell propagation of nucleons in the ladder diagrams. The binding energy receives contributions from the background part of the spectral function, away form the quasiparticle peak. The Fermi energy at the saturation point fulfills the Hugenholz-Van Hove relation. In comparison to the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, the binding energy is reduced and the equation of state is harder

## Key claim

The self-consistent T-matrix approximation, which includes off-shell propagation of nucleons, yields a binding energy per nucleon that is reduced by about 4 MeV at normal nuclear density and a harder equation of state compared to the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach, while satisfying the Hugenholz-Van Hove relation at saturation, a condition that BHF calculations badly violate.

## Method

The author solves the self-consistent T-matrix equations for symmetric nuclear matter using a separable parameterization of the Paris potential for S, P, D, and F partial waves, with a rank‑3 and rank‑4 parameterization for the ¹S₀ and ³S₁‑³D₁ channels and a Mongan I interaction for the ³P₀ wave. The full spectral function is discretized; for momenta near the Fermi momentum it is separated into a background part and a quasiparticle peak approximated by a delta function. The ladder diagrams include both particle‑particle and hole‑hole propagation, and the chemical potential is adjusted iteratively to satisfy the density constraint.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density (ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³), the binding energy per nucleon from the T‑matrix approach is reduced by **4 MeV** relative to the BHF result.
- The saturation point in the T‑matrix approach occurs at **ρ = 1.4 ρ₀** (instead of 2.4 ρ₀ in BHF).
- The quasiparticle strength at the Fermi momentum is **Z_{p_F} ≈ 0.74**.
- The effective mass at normal nuclear density is **m* = 0.9 m**.
- The single‑particle width −2 Im Σ(p, ω_p) is proportional to **(p − p_F)²** near the Fermi momentum.

## Context

This work extends self‑consistent T‑matrix calculations, previously performed with simple interactions (e.g., by the author in Ref. jc), to a realistic Paris potential for symmetric nuclear matter. It contrasts with standard BHF calculations (using the continuous choice for single‑particle energies, as in Baldo) which violate the Hugenholz‑Van Hove relation; the T‑matrix approach restores that relation by including off‑shell background contributions to the binding energy. The results enable future inclusion of ring diagrams, higher partial waves, and three‑body forces to recover modern BHF saturation properties, while providing thermodynamically consistent single‑particle properties (spectral functions, effective mass, quasiparticle strength) for applications such as electron scattering and finite‑temperature extensions.
