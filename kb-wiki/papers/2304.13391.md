# Two-proton emission and related phenomena

> One of characteristic phenomena for nuclei beyond the proton dripline is the simultaneous emission of two protons (2\emph{p}). The current status of our knowledge of this most recently observed and the least known decay mode is presented. First, different approaches to theoretical description of this process, ranging from effective approximations to advanced three-body models are overviewed. Then, after a brief survey of main experimental methods to produce 2\emph{p}-emitting nuclei and techniques to study their decays, experimental findings in this research field are presented and discussed. This review covers decays of short-lived resonances and excited states of unbound nuclei as well as longer-lived, ground-state radioactive decays. In addition, more exotic decays like three- and four-proton emission are addressed. Finally, related few-body topics, like two-neutron and four-neutron radioactivity, and the problem of the tetraneutron are shortly discussed.

## Key claim

This review consolidates the current understanding of two-proton (2p) emission, the most recently discovered and least known decay mode for nuclei beyond the proton dripline, covering both theoretical approaches from effective approximations to advanced three-body models and experimental findings from short-lived resonances to ground-state radioactive decays, while also addressing related phenomena such as three- and four-proton emission and the tetraneutron problem.

## Method

The authors present a comprehensive review of theoretical frameworks for 2p emission, including direct decay approximations, diproton decay approximations, and three-body models using Jacobi coordinates and cluster-orbital shell model (COSM) coordinates, with decay widths extracted via Gamow theory or flux current methods. They survey experimental techniques based on projectile fragmentation and in-flight identification of single ions that decay at rest, as well as tracking methods for decays with very short half-lives, and discuss results from democratic decays, beta-delayed two-proton emission, and ground-state 2p radioactivity.

## Key numbers

* Four ground-state 2p emitters have been discovered: 45Fe (2002, GSI and GANIL), 54Zn (2005, GANIL), 48Ni (2011, NSCL MSU), and 67Kr (2016, RIKEN)
* The Gamow factor for diproton penetration changes by a few orders of magnitude when the decay energy Q2p changes by a few hundred keV, based on WKB calculations with Woods-Saxon potential and Coulomb term assuming diproton tunneling with orbital angular momentum ℓ=0
* Beta decay partial decay constant is proportional to Qβ^5, and beta half-lives for nuclei far from stability can be as short as a few milliseconds
* Experimental studies of 2p emission have reached up to Z=36 (krypton)
* The first case of beta-delayed two-proton emission was discovered in 22Al in 1983
* The 2p radioactivity of 19Mg was identified in 2007 at GSI Darmstadt using in-flight tracking techniques

## Context

This review extends and updates earlier reviews by Blank and Borge (2008), Blank and Ploszajczak (2008), and Grigorenko (2009), as well as a recent summary by Zhou et al. (2022). It covers the theoretical and experimental progress since the first observation of ground-state 2p radioactivity in 45Fe in 2002, which was predicted more than 40 years earlier by Goldansky (1960, 1961). The work enables future studies of more exotic decays like three- and four-proton emission, and connects to related few-body topics such as two-neutron and four-neutron radioactivity and the tetraneutron problem.
