# Correlations of flow harmonics in 2.76A TeV Pb--Pb collisions

> Using the event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics VISH2+1 with MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, and AMPT initial conditions, we investigate the correlations of flow harmonics, including the symmetric cumulants $SC^{v}(m, n)$, the normalized symmetric cumulants $NSC(m, n)$, and the Pearson correlation coefficients $C(v_{m}^{2}, v_{n}^{2})$ in 2.76A TeV Pb--Pb collisions. We find $SC^{v}(m, n)$ is sensitive to both initial conditions and the specific shear viscosity $η/s$. A comparison with the recent ALICE data show that our hydrodynamic calculations can qualitatively describe the data of $SC^{v}(3, 2)$ and $SC^{v}(4, 2)$ for various initial conditions, which demonstrate that $v_2$, $v_4$ are correlated and $v_2$, $v_3$ are anti-correlated. Meanwhile, the predicted symmetric cumulants $SC^{v}(5, 2)$, $SC^{v}(5, 3)$, and $SC^{v}(4, 3)$ reveal that $v_2$ and $v_5$, $v_3$ and $v_5$ are correlated, $v_3$ and $v_4$ are anti-correlated in most centrality classes. We also find $NSC^{v}(3, 2)$ and $C(v_{3}^{2}, v_{2}^{2})$, which are insensitive to $η/s$, are mainly determined by corresponding $NSC^{\varepsilon}(3, 2)$ and $C(\varepsilon_{3}^{2}, \varepsilon_{2}^{2})$ correlators from the initial state. In contrast, other $NSC^{v}(m, n)$ and $C(v_{m}^{2}, v_{n}^{2})$ correlators are influenced by both initial conditions and $η/s$, which illustrates the non-linear mode couplings in higher flow harmonics with $n \geq 4$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic calculations can qualitatively describe the ALICE data for symmetric cumulants SCv(3,2) and SCv(4,2) across various initial conditions, but the abstract glosses over the fact that none of the combinations of initial conditions and specific shear viscosity can quantitatively reproduce the data, only capturing the sign and rough centrality dependence.

## Method

The authors used the event-by-event (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 with three different initial condition models (MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, and AMPT) to simulate 2.76A TeV Pb-Pb collisions. They calculated symmetric cumulants SCv(m,n), normalized symmetric cumulants NSC(m,n), and Pearson correlation coefficients C(vm2, vn2) from the final-state flow harmonics, and compared these with corresponding initial-state eccentricity correlators SCε(m,n), NSCε(m,n), and C(εm2, εn2). The specific shear viscosity η/s was varied (0.08 and 0.20 for MC-Glauber and MC-KLN; 0.08 and 0.16 for AMPT) to study its influence on the correlations.

## Key numbers

- SCv(3,2) shows negative values and SCv(4,2) shows positive values for all three initial conditions at 2.76A TeV, consistent with ALICE data
- For MC-Glauber initial conditions, VISH2+1 with η/s=0.08 fits integrated v2 from central to semi-peripheral collisions but overestimates v3 and v4
- For MC-KLN initial conditions, VISH2+1 with η/s=0.20 reproduces integrated v2 but underestimates v3 and v4
- The decoupling temperature Tdec was set to 120 MeV for all calculations
- For AMPT initial conditions, η/s=0.16 was used instead of 0.20 for peripheral collisions due to validity regime of hydrodynamics

## Context

This work extends the study of flow harmonic correlations beyond the individual vn coefficients, following the introduction of symmetric cumulants by Bilandzic (2013) and their first experimental measurement by ALICE (2016). The paper builds on prior hydrodynamic studies by Qiu (2011) and Shen (2014) using VISH2+1, and compares with the EKRT+viscous hydrodynamic calculations by Niemi (2015) which also could not quantitatively reproduce the centrality-dependent SCv(3,2) and SCv(4,2) measurements. The results enable future work to use these correlation observables as more sensitive probes of initial conditions and QGP viscosity than individual flow harmonics alone.
