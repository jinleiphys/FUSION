# A large Hilbert space QRPA and RQRPA calculation of neutrinoless double beta decay

> A large Hilbert space is used for the calculation of the nuclear matrix elements governing the light neutrino mass mediated mode of neutrinoless double beta decay of Ge76, Mo100, Cd116, Te128 and Xe136 within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) and the renormalized QRPA with proton-neutron pairing (full-RQRPA) methods. We have found that the nuclear matrix elements obtained with the standard pn-QRPA for several nuclear transitions are extremely sensitive to the renormalization of the particle-particle component of the residual interaction of the nuclear hamiltonian. Therefore the standard pn-QRPA does not guarantee the necessary accuracy to allow us to extract a reliable limit on the effective neutrino mass. This behaviour, already known from the calculation of the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements, manifests itself in the neutrinoless double-beta decay but only if a large model space is used. The full-RQRPA, which takes into account proton-neutron pairing and considers the Pauli principle in an approximate way, offers a stable solution in the physically acceptable region of the particle-particle strength. In this way more accurate values on the effective neutrino mass have been deduced from the experimental lower limits of the half-lifes of neutrinoless double beta decay.

## Key claim

The standard pn-QRPA method yields neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements that are extremely sensitive to the particle-particle interaction strength when a large Hilbert space is used, making it unreliable for extracting limits on the effective neutrino mass; the full-RQRPA, which includes proton-neutron pairing and an approximate treatment of the Pauli principle, provides stable matrix elements in the physically acceptable region of the particle-particle strength.

## Method

The authors performed calculations of the nuclear matrix elements for the light neutrino mass mediated mode of neutrinoless double beta decay for five nuclei (Ge-76, Mo-100, Cd-116, Te-128, Xe-136) using both the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) and the renormalized QRPA with proton-neutron pairing (full-RQRPA). They employed large single-particle model spaces of 21 levels for the lighter systems and 20 levels for the heavier systems, with single-particle energies from a Coulomb-corrected Woods-Saxon potential and a Brueckner G-matrix derived from the Bonn one-boson-exchange potential as the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The particle-particle strength parameter g_pp was varied in the interval 0.70 to 1.30, with the particle-hole strength fixed at g_ph = 0.8.

## Key numbers

- For Ge-76: full-RQRPA matrix element M^0nu_mass = 1.86 at g_pp = 1.0, yielding an upper limit on effective neutrino mass <m_nu> ≤ 1.1 eV from the experimental half-life limit T^0nu-exp_1/2 ≥ 7.4 × 10^24 years (90% C.L., Heidelberg-Moscow collaboration)
- For Mo-100: full-RQRPA matrix element M^0nu_mass = 4.22 at g_pp = 1.0, with G_01 = 5.731 × 10^-14 years^-1 and T^0nu-1eV_1/2 = 2.6 × 10^23 years
- For Te-128: full-RQRPA matrix element M^0nu_mass = 3.28 at g_pp = 1.0, yielding <m_nu> ≤ 1.2 eV from T^0nu-exp_1/2 ≥ 7.3 × 10^24 years (68% C.L.)
- For Cd-116: full-RQRPA matrix element M^0nu_mass = 2.47 at g_pp = 1.0, with <m_nu> ≤ 4.9 eV
- For Xe-136: full-RQRPA matrix element M^0nu_mass = 0.96 at g_pp = 1.0, with <m_nu> ≤ 3.7 eV
- The short-range correlation function used parameters a = 1.1 fm^-2 and b = 0.68 fm^-2, with dipole nucleon form factor cutoff Lambda = 0.85 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Simkovic et al. (1997) for Ge-76 that increasing the model space causes pn-QRPA results for neutrinoless double beta decay to become extremely sensitive to the particle-particle interaction, similar to the well-known quenching behavior in two-neutrino double beta decay calculations by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986) and Civitarese, Faessler, and Tomoda (1987). The full-RQRPA method, developed by Schwieger, Simkovic, and Faessler (1996) and building on the pn-RQRPA of Toivanen and Suhonen (1995), provides stable matrix elements that enable more reliable extraction of limits on the effective neutrino mass from experimental half-life limits. The results suggest that Mo-100 is the most promising candidate for future experimental searches due to its large matrix element and kinematical factor.
