# Structure of ground and excited states of $^{12}$C

> We studied the ground and excited states of $^{12}$C based on variational calculations after spin-parity projection in a framework of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics(AMD). The calculations systematically reproduce various experimental data. It was found that the sub-shell closure and SU(3)-limit $3α$ cluster components are contained in the ground state, while various $3α$ cluster structures develop in the excited states. We discussed effects of $α$ breaking and show the importance of coexistence of the cluster and shell-model-like aspects.

## Key claim

The ground state of \(^{12}\)C contains both sub-shell closure and SU(3)-limit \(3\alpha\) cluster components, while excited states develop various \(3\alpha\) cluster structures; the coexistence of cluster and shell-model-like aspects is essential, and \(\alpha\) breaking effects are important for describing properties such as \(\beta\) decay strengths and \(E2\) transitions that pure \(3\alpha\) cluster models fail to reproduce.

## Method

The author performed variational calculations after spin-parity projection (VAP) within the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework, where single-nucleon wave functions are independent Gaussian wave packets and no cluster structure is assumed a priori. The VAP method was applied to obtain the lowest \(J^\pi\) states, and higher states were obtained by VAP in the orthogonal space to lower states. Finally, 23 AMD wave functions were superposed by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian and norm matrices to produce the final wave functions for the \(J^\pi_n\) states.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(^{12}\)C: 88.0 MeV (experimental value 92.16 MeV)
- Ground state mass radius \(R_m = 2.53\) fm (observed value deduced from charge radius: \(R_m = 2.32-2.33\) fm)
- \(0^+_2\) state mass radius \(R_m = 3.27\) fm (compared to \(3\alpha\)GCM: 3.40 fm, \(3\alpha\)RGM: 3.47 fm, Funaki et al.: 3.83 fm)
- \(B(E2;2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1) = 8.5\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (experimental: \(7.6 \pm 0.4\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\))
- \(B(E2;2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_2) = 5.1\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (experimental: \(2.6 \pm 0.4\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\))
- \(B(E2;2^+_2\rightarrow 0^+_3) = 310\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\)
- \(B(E2;4^+_2\rightarrow 2^+_2) = 600\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\)
- \(M(E0;0^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_2) = 6.7\) fm\(^2\) (experimental: \(5.4 \pm 0.2\) fm\(^2\))
- \(M(E0;0^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_3) = 2.0\) fm\(^2\)
- log \(ft\) for \(\beta^+\) decay from \(^{12}\)N to \(0^+_1\): 3.8 (experimental: \(4.118 \pm 0.003\))
- log \(ft\) for \(\beta^+\) decay from \(^{12}\)N to \(0^+_2\): 4.3 (experimental: \(4.34 \pm 0.07\))

## Context

This work extends previous AMD VAP calculations by Kanada-En'yo (2005) and provides a systematic study of both ground and excited states of \(^{12}\)C, addressing the coexistence of cluster and shell-model-like aspects that pure \(3\alpha\) cluster models (such as RGM by Fujiwara (1980) and GCM by Descouvemont (1987)) cannot describe. The results show that \(\alpha\) breaking effects, absent in \(3\alpha\) models, are crucial for reproducing \(\beta\) decay strengths and the \(B(E2;2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_2)\) value, which was underestimated by factor 2-4 in earlier \(3\alpha\) calculations. The work enables future studies of the \(0^+_3\) state and the broad resonances around \(E_x \sim 10\) MeV, and provides a framework for investigating the dilute \(\alpha\) condensate interpretation proposed by Tohsaki (2001) while accounting for \(\alpha\) breaking and shell-model components.
