# Effects of chiral imbalance and magnetic field on pion superfluidity and color superconductivity

> The effects of chiral imbalance and external magnetic field on pion superfluidity and color superconductivity are investigated in extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio models. We take Schwinger approach to treat the interaction between charged pion condensate and magnetic field at finite isospin density and include simultaneously the chiral imbalance and magnetic field at finite baryon density. For the superfluidity, the chiral imbalance and magnetic field lead to catalysis and inverse catalysis effects, respectively. For the superconductivity, the chiral imbalance enhances the critical baryon density, and the magnetic field results in a de Haas--van Alphan oscillation on the phase transition line.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how chiral imbalance (nonzero chiral chemical potential μ₅) and an external magnetic field affect pion superfluidity (at finite isospin density) and color superconductivity (at finite baryon density) in extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio models, finding that chiral imbalance catalyzes pion condensation while the magnetic field inversely catalyzes it, and that chiral imbalance enhances the critical baryon density for color superconductivity while the magnetic field introduces de Haas–van Alphen oscillations on the phase transition line.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model extended with a chiral chemical potential μ₅ and, for the magnetic field case, employ the Schwinger proper-time approach to treat the interaction between the charged pion condensate and the magnetic field. For pion superfluidity, they derive the phase transition line via a Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the thermodynamic potential around the critical point, solving the coupled gap equations for the dynamical quark mass and the pion condensate. For color superconductivity, they work in the Nambu-Gorkov formalism, include both μ₅ and magnetic field simultaneously, and use Pauli-Villars regularization to handle divergences.

## Key numbers

- Pion superfluidity critical isospin chemical potential in real case (m₀ = 5 MeV) at zero field: μ_I^c = m_π = 134 MeV (μ_I^c/Λ = 0.21 with cutoff Λ = 0.653 GeV).
- Model parameters for real case: G = 4.93 GeV⁻², Λ = 0.653 GeV, m₀ = 5 MeV, fitted to m_π = 134 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV, and quark condensate ⟨σ⟩ = −2 × (0.25 GeV)³.
- Model parameters for chiral limit: G = 5.01 GeV⁻², Λ = 0.65 GeV.
- Critical coupling constant for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in vacuum: G_c = π²/(2N_c Λ²).
- For color superconductivity, the diquark coupling constant is related to the scalar coupling by G_D = 3G_S/4 from Fierz transformation.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of pion superfluidity in magnetic fields using the NJL model (Kang 2014), linear sigma model (Loewe 2012), and lattice QCD (Endrődi 2014), where the lattice result showed inverse magnetic catalysis while earlier model calculations at hadron level showed magnetic catalysis. The authors resolve this discrepancy by using the Schwinger approach at quark level, obtaining inverse magnetic catalysis consistent with lattice QCD. For color superconductivity, the work builds on studies of magnetic field effects (Fayazbakhsh 2012) and chiral imbalance effects (Fukushima 2010, Chao 2013, Cao 2014) on chiral symmetry restoration, extending them to the diquark condensation channel. The results enable further investigation of the combined effects of chiral imbalance and magnetic fields in dense QCD matter relevant to neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions.
