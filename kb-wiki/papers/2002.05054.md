# Multiplicity Dependence of Shear Viscosity, Isothermal Compressibility and Speed of Sound in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV

> In order to understand the detailed dynamics of systems produced in $pp$ collisions, it is essential to know about the Equation of State (EoS) and various thermodynamic properties. In this work, we study the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, isothermal compressibility and speed of sound of the system by considering a differential freeze-out scenario. We have used a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis non-extensive statistics to have a better explanation for the dynamics of $pp$ collision systems. While the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio provides information about the measure of fluidity of a system formed in high energy collisions, the isothermal compressibility gives a clear idea about the deviation of the system from a perfect fluid. The speed of sound in the system as a function of $\langle dN_{\rm ch}/dη\rangle$ gives us a vivid picture of the dynamics of the system. The results show quite an intuitive perspective on high multiplicity $pp$ collisions and give us a limit of $\langle dN_{\rm ch}/dη\rangle$ $\gtrsim$ (10 - 20), after which a change in the dynamics of the system may be observed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, isothermal compressibility, and speed of sound all show a change in behavior at a charged-particle multiplicity threshold of ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ≳ 10–20, which may indicate a transition in system dynamics and possible formation of QGP droplets; however, the authors caution that the physical interpretation of low-multiplicity systems must be done with care.

## Method

The authors used a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis non-extensive distribution function to fit the transverse momentum spectra of identified particles (pions, kaons, protons, etc.) from ALICE pp collision data at √s = 7 TeV. From the extracted temperature T and non-extensivity parameter q for each multiplicity class, they calculated the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) via the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation-time approximation, the isothermal compressibility (κT) from multiplicity fluctuations, and the speed of sound squared (c_s²) from the derivatives of pressure and energy density with respect to temperature. A constant hadronic cross-section of 11.3 mb was used for all calculations.

## Key numbers

* η/s decreases with increasing ⟨dNch/dη⟩ and approaches the KSS bound at high multiplicity; at ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ≳ 10–20, η/s becomes almost constant and independent of particle species.
* κT of hadron gases at high charged-particle multiplicity is around 10 fm³/GeV, compared to ∼0.3 fm³/GeV for a QGP-like medium found in prior work (Sahu 2020).
* c_s² ranges from 0.15 to 0.32 for different particles, with pions having the highest value; at high multiplicities, c_s² tends toward 1/3, the value for an ideal gas.
* The threshold for a change in system dynamics is identified at ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ≳ 10–20.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of thermodynamic properties in small systems (Sahu 2020, Khuntia 2018) by applying a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis formalism to pp collisions at LHC energies. The observed multiplicity threshold of ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ≳ 10–20, where η/s and κT become independent of particle species, suggests a transition from differential to single kinetic freeze-out similar to that seen in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable future comparisons with p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions to search for critical behavior and a smooth transition from hadronic to nuclear collision dynamics.
