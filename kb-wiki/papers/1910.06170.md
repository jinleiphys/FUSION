# Probing the neutron skin with ultrarelativistic isobaric collisions

> Particle production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions depends on the details of the nucleon density distributions in the colliding nuclei. We demonstrate that the charged hadron multiplicity distributions in isobaric collisions at ultrarelativistic energies provide a novel approach to determine the poorly known neutron density distributions and thus the neutron skin thickness in finite nuclei, which can in turn put stringent constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy.

# Probing the neutron skin with ultrarelativistic isobaric collisions

## Key claim
The charged hadron multiplicity distributions in isobaric collisions of Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 200 GeV can determine the neutron skin thickness (Δr_np) of finite nuclei, provided that the proton density is already well known from electron scattering experiments. The precision achievable from this method may be comparable to or exceed traditional low-energy nuclear experiments, but the result depends on the specific heavy-ion collision model used, with an overall spread in the observable R (relative mean multiplicity difference) that widens at larger neutron skin values.

## Method
The authors used four dynamical heavy-ion collision models (AMPT default and string melting versions, HIJING, and UrQMD) and two static models (Glauber and Trento) to simulate Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. Nuclear density distributions for ⁹⁶Ru and ⁹⁶Zr were calculated using the extended Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (eSHF) model with three different symmetry energy slope parameters (L(ρ_c) = 20, 47.3, and 70 MeV) and the standard Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with the SLy4 interaction. The relative difference in mean charged hadron multiplicity between the two isobar systems in the top 5% most central collisions was computed and compared against the neutron skin thickness of the Zr nucleus.

## Key numbers
- The neutron skin thickness Δr_np of ⁹⁶Zr ranges from 0.115 fm (Lc20) to 0.232 fm (Lc70) across the eSHF parameter sets, with SLy4 giving 0.161 fm.
- The neutron skin thickness Δr_np of ⁹⁶Ru ranges from 0.011 fm (Lc20) to 0.066 fm (Lc70), with SLy4 giving 0.030 fm.
- The symmetry energy slope parameter L(ρ_c) at subsaturation cross density ρ_c ≈ 0.11 fm⁻³ is fixed at 47.3 ± 7.8 MeV from electric dipole polarizability data of ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The symmetry energy at subsaturation density is E_sym(ρ_c) = 26.65 ± 0.20 MeV from nuclear binding energies.
- The relative mean multiplicity difference R between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr in the top 5% centrality ranges from approximately 0.002 to 0.012 depending on the density set and model, with a monotonic increase as a function of Δr_np of Zr.
- The precision in determining Δr_np of Zr from a measurement of R is estimated to be about 0.05 fm, as covered by the spread among the four dynamical models and two static models.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Xu (2017) and Li (2018) that showed nuclear density distributions affect total charged hadron multiplicity in isobaric collisions. It provides a novel alternative to traditional neutron skin measurements such as those from the PREX experiment on ²⁰⁸Pb (Abrahamyan, 2012), which reported Δr_np = 0.33⁺⁰·¹⁶₋₀·₁₈ fm, and to parity-violating electron scattering approaches (Horowitz, 1999). The method can be readily applied to isobar collision data collected at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider in 2018, potentially yielding improved constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy and the equation of state of neutron star matter.
