# Stochastic Variational Search for $^{4}_{ΛΛ}$H

> A four-body calculation of the $pnΛΛ$ bound state, $^{\ 4}_{ΛΛ}$H, is performed using the stochastic variational method and phenomenological potentials. The $NN$, $ΛN$, and $ΛΛ$ potentials are taken from a recent Letter by Filikhin and Gal, PRL{\bf 89}, 172502 (2002). Although their Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation found no bound-state solution over a wide range of $ΛΛ$ interaction strengths, the present variational calculation gives a bound-state energy, which is clearly lower than the $_Λ^3{H}+Λ$ threshold, even for a weak $ΛΛ$ interaction strength deduced from a recent experimental $B_{ΛΛ}(^{6}_{ΛΛ}{He})$ value. The binding energies obtained are close to, and slightly larger than, the values obtained from the three-body $dΛΛ$ model in the Letter.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the four-body $pn\Lambda\Lambda$ system $^{\ 4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$H does have a particle-stable bound state, contradicting the Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation of Filikhin and Gal (2002) which found no bound state; however, they caution that this conclusion depends sensitively on the choice of the $\Lambda N$ interaction, particularly the $^3S_1$ channel strength, and that the $\Lambda N$ potential used by Filikhin and Gal (2002) may be too attractive in that channel to be consistent with experimental data on $A=4$ single-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors performed a four-body variational calculation of the $pn\Lambda\Lambda$ system using the stochastic variational method (SVM) with correlated Gaussian basis functions. They employed the same phenomenological $NN$, $\Lambda N$, and $\Lambda\Lambda$ three-range Gaussian potentials as used by Filikhin and Gal (2002), and also tested an alternative $\Lambda N$ potential (Set A) from Nemura et al. (2000) which has a different $^3S_1$ strength. The variational parameters were optimized stochastically, and the accuracy was checked by examining the virial theorem.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $^{\ 4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$H with a weak $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction ($a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -0.77$ fm) is $B_{\Lambda\Lambda} \sim 0.4$ MeV, slightly larger than the $d\Lambda\Lambda$ three-body model value of $\sim 0.3$ MeV from Filikhin and Gal (2002).
- With a strong $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction ($a_{\Lambda\Lambda} \sim -3$ fm), $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}(^{\ 4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}{\rm H}) \gsim 1.2$ MeV using the NSC97f(FG) $\Lambda N$ potential.
- For the $pn$ subsystem in $^{\ 4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}{\rm H}$ with $a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -0.77$ fm: kinetic energy $\langle T_c \rangle = 22.28$ MeV, potential energy $\langle V_{NN} \rangle = -23.17$ MeV, rms $pn$ distance $\sqrt{\langle r_{NN}^2 \rangle} = 3.34$ fm, rms $\Lambda N$ distance $\sqrt{\langle r_{\Lambda N}^2 \rangle} = 7.92$ fm.
- For the $pn$ subsystem in $^{\ 4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}{\rm H}$ with $a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -2.8$ fm: kinetic energy $\langle T_c \rangle = 24.73$ MeV, potential energy $\langle V_{NN} \rangle = -24.55$ MeV, rms $pn$ distance $\sqrt{\langle r_{NN}^2 \rangle} = 3.08$ fm, rms $\Lambda N$ distance $\sqrt{\langle r_{\Lambda N}^2 \rangle} = 4.82$ fm.
- Virial theorem ratio $2\langle T \rangle / \langle W \rangle = 1.000016$ for weak $\Lambda\Lambda$ ($a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -0.77$ fm) and $0.999962$ for strong $\Lambda\Lambda$ ($a_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -2.8$ fm).
- Calculated $\Lambda$ separation energies using NSC97f(FG) $\Lambda N$ potential: $B_\Lambda(^3_\Lambda{\rm H}) = 0.24$ MeV, $B_\Lambda(^4_\Lambda{\rm H}) = 2.69$ MeV, $B_\Lambda(^4_\Lambda{\rm H}^\ast) = 1.99$ MeV; experimental values are $0.13 \pm 0.05$ MeV, $2.04 \pm 0.04$ MeV, and $1.00 \pm 0.04$ MeV respectively.

## Context

This work directly addresses the contradictory results between the four-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculation of Filikhin and Gal (2002), which found no bound $^{\ 4}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$H, and their own three-body $d\Lambda\Lambda$ model which did find a bound state. The authors extend their previous variational studies (Nakaichi-Maeda and Akaishi, 1990; Nemura et al., 2000) to test the specific potentials used by Filikhin and Gal (2002), and find that the variational method yields a bound state even for weak $\Lambda\Lambda$ interactions. The work highlights that the $^3S_1$ $\Lambda N$ interaction strength must be carefully constrained by data on $A=4$ single-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei, not just $^3_\Lambda$H, and that future studies should incorporate $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ coupling and five- and six-body calculations to properly determine the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction from the Nagara event data on $^{\ 6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He.
