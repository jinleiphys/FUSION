# Constrained correlated-Gaussians for hyperspherical calculations

> We formulate a hyperspherical approach within standard configuration interaction calculations aiming at a description of large-scale dynamics of $N$-particle system. The channel wave function and the adiabatic channel energy are determined by solving a hyperradius-constrained eigenvalue problem of the adiabatic Hamiltonian. The needed matrix elements are analytically evaluated using correlated Gaussians with good orbital angular momentum and parity. The feasibility of the approach is tested in three-$α$ system. A spectrum of the adiabatic channel energies is determined depending on the degree of localization of the basis functions.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a hyperspherical approach using correlated Gaussian basis functions to solve the N-particle Schrödinger equation, but the emphasis is on testing feasibility and discussing potential problems rather than solving a specific physical problem. The approach replaces the standard coupled differential equation with a constrained eigenvalue problem of the adiabatic Hamiltonian, where the hyperradius is treated as a c-number parameter.

## Method

The authors set up basis functions (correlated Gaussians with good orbital angular momentum and parity) that satisfy a hyperradius-squared constraint, then solve a constrained eigenvalue problem of the adiabatic Hamiltonian to obtain channel wave functions and adiabatic channel energies at specific hyperradial values. All matrix elements of the adiabatic Hamiltonian are evaluated analytically using generating functions for the correlated Gaussians, with the hyperangular kinetic energy obtained indirectly via the identity T_Omega = T - T_rho. The method is tested on the three-alpha system using a modified Ali-Bodmer potential plus Coulomb and three-body hyperscalar potentials.

## Key numbers

- The alpha particle mass parameter is hbar^2/m = 10.5254408 MeV fm^2
- The charge constant is e^2 = 1.4399644 MeV fm
- The two-body Ali-Bodmer potential uses ranges 1.53 fm and 2.85 fm with strengths 125 MeV and -30.18 MeV respectively
- The Coulomb potential uses the error function form with parameter 0.60141 fm^-1
- The three-body potential range parameter R_3 = 2.58 fm
- The three-body potential strength v_3 is -151.737 MeV for L=0 and -179.463 MeV for L=2

## Context

This work extends the correlated Gaussian approach to hyperspherical calculations for N-particle systems, building on prior developments in hyperspherical methods reviewed by Zhukov (1993), Lin (1995), Krivec (1998), Nielsen (2001), and Greene (2017). The approach addresses the slow convergence problem of hyperspherical harmonics expansions noted by Thompson (2000) and Macek (1968), and the difficulties in extending hyperspherical methods beyond three-body systems discussed by Barnea (2000, 2003, 2010), Bacca (2012), Timofeyuk (2002, 2008), and Gattobigio (2011). The method enables standard configuration interaction calculations for large-scale dynamics of N-particle systems without solving coupled hyperradial differential equations.
