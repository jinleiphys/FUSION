# Thermal fluctuations on the freeze-out surface of heavy-ion collisions and their impact on particle correlations

> Particle momentum distributions originating from a quark-gluon plasma as produced in high-energy nuclear collisions can be influenced by thermal fluctuations in fluid dynamic fields. We study this effect by generalizing the commonly used kinetic freeze-out prescription by allowing for small fluctuations around an average in fluid velocity, chemical potentials and temperature. This leads to the appearance of specific two-body momentum correlations. Combining a blast-wave parametrization of the kinetic freeze-out surface with the thermal correlation functions of an ideal resonance gas, we perform an exploratory study of angular net-charge correlations induced by thermal fluctuations around vanishing chemical potential. We note a diffusion of the near-side peak around $Δy=Δϕ=0$ induced by variances of different chemical potentials, which could be investigated experimentally.

## Key claim

Thermal fluctuations in fluid velocity, temperature, and chemical potentials on the freeze-out surface of heavy-ion collisions generate specific two-body momentum correlations that go beyond the standard independent-particle-production picture; the authors note that these correlations are short-range and may be detectable experimentally through angular net-charge correlations even when the average chemical potentials vanish.

## Method

The authors generalize the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription by allowing small fluctuations around the average fluid velocity, temperature, and chemical potentials on a fixed freeze-out surface, then expand the equilibrium distribution function to linear order in these fluctuations. They relate the resulting two-particle momentum correlation function to two-point correlation functions of the fluctuating fluid fields via a mode-by-mode decomposition in azimuthal and rapidity Fourier space. For a numerical exploratory study, they combine a blast-wave parametrization of the average freeze-out surface with local thermal correlation functions of an ideal resonance gas, using classical statistical field theory to compute the prefactors of the delta-correlated fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature: \(T_{\rm fo} = 156\) MeV, characterizing the transition from QGP to hadron gas.
- Freeze-out proper time: \(\tau = \tau_\text{fo}\), with freeze-out radius \(R_\text{fo}\) and fluid velocity profile \(v^r_0(r) = \beta_{S} (r/R_{\mathrm{fo}})^{n}\).
- The system expands as a relativistic fluid for about 10 fm/\(c\) before decoupling to hadrons.
- Local thermal equilibrium is reached over a time scale of order 1 fm/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the study of thermal (hydrodynamic) fluctuations in QGP expansion, previously investigated by Kapusta (2011), Young (2013), Ling (2013), Murase (2015), Yan (2015), and others, but takes a simpler approach by not evolving fluctuations dynamically over the fluid evolution. Instead, it focuses on generic phenomenological signatures of thermal fluctuations at the freeze-out surface that have not been discussed in the literature, particularly differential net-charge fluctuations depending on transverse momentum and rapidity cuts. The formalism enables experimental searches for dynamical net-charge fluctuations at very high-energy colliders, complementing studies of critical phenomena at the QGP-hadron transition by Stephanov (1998) and more recent work on statistical fluctuations and conservation laws by Schwarz (2017), Oliinychenko (2019, 2020), and Vovchenko (2021).
