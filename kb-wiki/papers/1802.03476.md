# The surface properties of neutron-rich exotic nuclei within relativistic mean field formalisms

> In this theoretical study, we establish a correlation between the neutron skin thickness and the nuclear symmetry energy for the even$-$even isotopes of Fe, Ni, Zn, Ge, Se and Kr within the framework of the axially deformed self-consistent relativistic mean field for the non-linear NL3$^*$ and density-dependent DD-ME1 interactions. The coherent density functional method is used to formulate the symmetry energy, the neutron pressure and the curvature of finite nuclei as a function of the nuclear radius. We have performed broad studies for the mass dependence on the symmetry energy in terms of the neutron-proton asymmetry for mass 70 $\leq$ A $\leq$ 96. From this analysis, we found a notable signature of a shell closure at $N$ = 50 in the isotopic chains of Fe, Ni, Zn, Ge, Se and Kr nuclei. The present study reveals an interrelationship between the characteristics of infinite nuclear matter and the neutron skin thickness of finite nuclei

## Key claim

The paper establishes a correlation between the neutron skin thickness of finite nuclei and the nuclear symmetry energy of infinite nuclear matter, but this correlation is demonstrated specifically within the axially deformed self-consistent relativistic mean field framework using only the NL3* and DD-ME1 interactions, and is limited to even-even isotopes of Fe, Ni, Zn, Ge, Se and Kr in the mass range 70 ≤ A ≤ 96.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed self-consistent relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the non-linear NL3* and density-dependent DD-ME1 interactions to obtain ground state properties of neutron-rich even-even isotopes. They then applied the coherent density functional method (CDFM) to compute the symmetry energy, neutron pressure, and curvature for finite nuclei as weighted averages of the corresponding infinite nuclear matter quantities, using the monopole term of the nuclear density distribution to construct the weight function. The neutron skin thickness was calculated from the difference between neutron and proton root-mean-square radii.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.150 fm⁻³ for NL3* and 0.152 fm⁻³ for DD-ME1
- Symmetry energy at saturation S^NM = 38.68 MeV for NL3* and 33.06 MeV for DD-ME1
- Incompressibility K₀^NM = 258.27 MeV for NL3* and 244.72 MeV for DD-ME1
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation E/A = -16.31 MeV for NL3* and -16.04 MeV for DD-ME1
- Dirac effective mass ratio M/M* = 0.594 for NL3* and 0.586 for DD-ME1
- Ground state binding energy of ⁷⁸Ni: 635.85 MeV (NL3*) and 635.96 MeV (DD-ME1), compared to experimental extrapolated value 641.94 MeV
- Ground state binding energy of ⁸⁶Kr: 747.73 MeV (NL3*) and 747.56 MeV (DD-ME1), compared to experimental value 749.23 MeV
- Charge radius of ⁸⁶Kr: 4.174 fm (NL3*) and 4.170 fm (DD-ME1), compared to experimental value 4.1835 fm

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies by Warda et al. (2009, 2010, 2014) who demonstrated the dependence of symmetry energy stiffness on bulk and surface components of neutron skin thickness, and by Antonov et al. (1979, 1980, 1994) who developed the coherent density functional method. The paper enables future constraints on nuclear matter symmetry energy parameters using measured neutron skin thicknesses of neutron-rich medium-mass nuclei, particularly those near the N=50 shell closure, and connects finite-nucleus observables to the equation of state of neutron-rich matter relevant for astrophysical applications.
