# Hydrodynamics of anisotropic quark and gluon fluids

> The recently developed framework of anisotropic hydrodynamics is generalized to describe the dynamics of coupled quark and gluon fluids. The quark and gluon components of the fluids are characterized by different dynamical anisotropy parameters. The dynamical equations describing such mixtures are derived from kinetic theory with the collisional kernel treated in the relaxation- time approximation allowing for different relaxation times for quarks and gluons. Baryon number conservation is enforced in the quark and anti-quark components of the fluid, but overall parton number non-conservation is allowed in the system. The resulting equations are solved numerically in the (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant case at zero and finite baryon density.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes anisotropic hydrodynamics to describe coupled quark and gluon fluids with different dynamical anisotropy parameters, but the system of equations is underdetermined and requires the assumption that quark and antiquark anisotropy parameters are equal to close the system.

## Method

The authors derive dynamical equations from kinetic theory using the relaxation-time approximation with different constant relaxation times for quarks and gluons. They take zeroth and first moments of the Boltzmann equation for quark, antiquark, and gluon distribution functions, enforce baryon number conservation in the quark sector, and apply Landau matching conditions for energy density. The resulting coupled equations are solved numerically in (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant geometry for both zero and finite baryon density cases.

## Key numbers

- Relaxation times: gluon relaxation time τ_{g,eq} = 0.25 fm/c, quark relaxation time τ_{q,eq} = (9/4) × τ_{g,eq} based on Casimir scaling with C_A/C_F = 9/4
- Initial proper time τ_i = 0.1 fm/c for numerical evolution
- Internal degrees of freedom: g_q = 12 (quarks), g_g = 16 (gluons), giving ratio r = g_g/g_q = 4/3
- Late-time asymptotic behavior in Case I: ξ_q = -(1/2) B r e^{-γτ} and ξ_g = B e^{-γτ} with γ = α/τ_{g,eq} and α = (2+r)/(2+{\bar r})
- Late-time asymptotic behavior in Case II: ξ_q = ξ_g = 4α τ_q τ^{-1} at leading order, with difference ξ_q - ξ_g = (16/15) α τ_q (τ_q - τ_g) τ^{-2}

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework developed by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010), and Ryblewski (2010-2012) from single-component fluids to coupled quark-gluon mixtures. It addresses the problem that standard second-order viscous hydrodynamics breaks down when large momentum-space anisotropies develop in the early stages of heavy-ion collisions, as noted by Martinez (2009). The results enable more realistic modeling of the quark-gluon plasma produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly during the early pre-equilibrium phase where quarks and gluons may thermalize at different rates.
