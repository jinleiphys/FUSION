# Neutron Stars and the EOS

> Implications of recently well-measured neutron star masses, particularly near and above 2 solar masses, for the equation of state (EOS) of neutron star matter are highlighted. Model-independent upper limits to thermodynamic properties in neutron stars, which only depend on the neutron star maximum mass, established from causality considerations are presented. The need for non-perturbative treatments of quark matter in neutron stars is stressed through studies of self-bound quark matter stars, and of nucleon-quark hybrid stars. The extent to which several well-measured masses and radii of individual neutron stars can establish a model-independent EOS through an inversion of the stellar structure equations is briefly discussed.

# Neutron Stars and the EOS

## Key claim

The paper argues that the recently measured neutron star masses near and above 2 solar masses, if taken as representing the true maximum mass, establish rigorous upper bounds on the central energy density, pressure, baryon number density, and chemical potential of neutron star matter, but only under the assumption that the high-density equation of state is as stiff as causality permits (squared speed of sound equal to 1).

## Method

The author applies the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations of general relativistic stellar structure to a maximally compact equation of state, which combines a soft low-density region (zero pressure) with a stiff high-density region at the causal limit (dp/dε = 1). For hybrid stars, a single first-order phase transition between nuclear and quark matter is assumed with a sharp interface, using a parameterized quark matter equation of state with constant squared speed of sound varied between 1/3 and 1. The deconstruction method for model-independent EOS determination reformulates the TOV equations using the variable h = ∫ dp/(p+ρ) and iteratively solves from the center to the surface.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1614-2230: mass = 1.97 ± 0.04 M⊙
- PSR J0348+0432: mass = 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙
- For a 1.97 M⊙ maximum mass with causal-limit EOS: central energy density ε_max < 1.97 GeV fm⁻³, central pressure p_max < 1.32 GeV fm⁻³, central baryon number density n_max < 1.56 fm⁻³, central chemical potential μ_max < 2.1 GeV
- Maximally compact EOS (s=1) yields M_max = 4.09 (ε_s/ε₀)^(1/2) M⊙ and R_max = 17.07 (ε_s/ε₀)^(1/2) km, where ε_s ≈ 150 MeV fm⁻³
- For the s=1/3 case (MIT bag model): M_max = 2.48 (ε_s/ε₀)^(1/2) M⊙, R_max = 13.56 (ε_s/ε₀)^(1/2) km, μ_B,max ≈ 1.46 GeV
- Binding energy of a neutron star: B.E. ≈ (0.6 ± 0.05) GM²/(Rc²) (1 - GM/(Rc²))⁻¹
- Minimum spin period for maximum mass: P_min(M_max) = 0.83 (M_max/M⊙)^(-1/2) (R_max/10 km)^(3/2) ms
- Maximum moment of inertia: I_max = 0.6 × 10⁴⁵ (M_max/M⊙)(R_max/10 km)² / (1 - 0.295(M_max/M⊙)/(R_max/10 km)) g cm²

## Context

This work extends the analysis of maximally compact EOS configurations from Haensel (1989) and Koranda (1997) to incorporate the recently measured 2 M⊙ neutron stars from Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013). The model-independent upper limits on thermodynamic properties build on the causality-based approach of Lattimer and Prakash (2011). The hybrid star analysis follows Alford (2013) and demonstrates that heavy hybrid stars above 2 M⊙ require non-perturbative treatments of quark matter, as perturbative QCD with c_s² = 1/3 cannot support such masses. The deconstruction method for model-independent EOS determination, first outlined by Lindblom (1992), is shown to yield results to hundredths of percent accuracy when multiple precise mass-radius measurements become available.
