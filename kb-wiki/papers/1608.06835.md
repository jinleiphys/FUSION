# Study of W and Z Boson Production in Proton-Lead Collisions at the LHC with KP Nuclear Parton Distributions

> We present a detailed study of the (pseudo)rapidity distributions of massive vector bosons produced in p+p and p+Pb collisions at the LHC within the next-to-leading order approximation in perturbative quantum chromodynamics. In particular, we discuss the impact of different cold nuclear matter effects on this process using the nuclear parton distributions calculated from the microscopic model developed by Kulagin and Petti (KP). This model was successfully applied to study nuclear effects in the deep-inelastic scattering and the Drell-Yan reactions off various (fixed) target nuclei. Results are compared with the recent CMS and ATLAS p+Pb data with \sqrt{s}=5.02 TeV per two colliding nucleons. We found an excellent agreement between the predictions of the KP model and the recent LHC data on W+- and Z0 production in p+Pb collisions, including the differential cross sections, the forward-backward asymmetries, and W charge asymmetry. We also discuss the sensitivity of the current and future LHC data to the underlying mechanisms responsible for the nuclear modifications of parton density functions.

## Key claim

The KP nuclear parton distribution model, which incorporates multiple microscopic mechanisms (Fermi motion and binding, off-shell corrections, meson exchange currents, and coherent nuclear interactions), provides an excellent description of recent LHC data on W and Z boson production in proton-lead collisions at next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, though the agreement depends on the specific choice of input proton PDF set and the kinematic region considered.

## Method

The authors computed (pseudo)rapidity distributions for W and Z boson production in p+p and p+Pb collisions using the DYNNLO program at next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, with nuclear parton distributions from the microscopic KP model. They compared these predictions against CMS and ATLAS data at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV per nucleon-nucleon collision, using two different proton PDF sets (ABMP15 and CT10) and analyzing differential cross sections, forward-backward asymmetries, and W charge asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV per two colliding nucleons for p+Pb collisions at the LHC
- Typical momentum fraction carried by nuclear partons at central rapidity: x0=m_V/sqrt(s_NN) approximately 0.017
- Q^2 scale for nuclear PDF ratios shown: Q^2=m_Z^2 (approximately (91 GeV)^2)
- Invariant mass range for Z boson selection: 60<m_ll<120 GeV (from CMS measurement at sqrt(s)=7 TeV)
- The NNLO corrections to the partonic cross sections are described as "rather small" and "even more marginal" in the forward-backward asymmetry and W charge asymmetry

## Context

This work extends the microscopic KP nuclear PDF model, previously validated against deep-inelastic scattering data from SLAC E139, FNAL E665, and CERN NMC experiments (Kulagin and Petti, 2004) and Drell-Yan data from E772 and E866 experiments at Fermilab (Kulagin and Petti, 2014), to the LHC energy regime for vector boson production. The results enable interpretation of CMS and ATLAS p+Pb data in terms of specific nuclear physics mechanisms (Fermi motion, off-shell effects, meson exchange currents, coherent interactions) rather than purely phenomenological parametrizations like EPS09 (Eskola et al., 2009), and provide sensitivity to flavor-dependent nuclear modifications of parton distributions that could be further constrained by future LHC measurements.
