# Understanding LHC jets in the light of RHIC data

> Hard probes are a cornerstone in the ongoing program to determine the properties of hot and dense QCD matter as created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. LHC measurements have so far resulted in a wealth of high P_T data, opening new kinematic windows with high statistics. Yet on first glance, several observations are counter-intuitive and seem to contradict results from the RHIC high P_T program. This calls for a combined analysis of high P_T hadrons and reconstructed jets at RHIC and LHC in a unified framework testing a large number of theoretical models for both medium evolution and shower medium interactions against the systematics of the data. A consistent picture of shower-medium interaction emerges from this analysis which explains where and why results appear counter-intuitive.

## Key claim

LHC jet data, which initially appeared to contradict RHIC results, can be consistently explained within a unified framework where jet quenching is dominated by medium-induced pQCD radiation with a small component of energy deposited directly into medium degrees of freedom, and no exotic behavior such as that suggested by AdS/CFT calculations is required.

## Method

The author performed a combined analysis of high-\(p_T\) hadrons and reconstructed jets at both RHIC and LHC energies using the in-medium shower evolution code YaJEM and its variants (YaJEM-D, YaJEM-DE). A large number of theoretical models for medium evolution and shower-medium interactions were tested against the systematic data, including models based on elastic energy loss, the ASW (Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann) formalism, and AdS/CFT calculations.

## Key numbers

* At RHIC, the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) shows a trend that is opposite to the decrease expected from fractional energy loss (\(\Delta E \sim zE\)).
* An incoherent component of energy loss is constrained from above to about 10% based on the measured dependence of suppression on the angle of outgoing hadrons with the reaction plane.
* Energy deposition into medium excitation (e.g., via elastic collisions) is constrained from below to about 10%, in agreement with the pathlength constraint.
* The medium-modified fragmentation function (MMFF) shows a flat (but suppressed) behavior above a scale of \(\sim 3\) GeV and strongly increases below that scale.
* At LHC, the dijet imbalance as a function of cone radius and jet energy, jet \(R_{CP}\), single hadron \(R_{AA}\), and h-jet correlations (as measured by ALICE) are all in fair agreement with the YaJEM-DE model.

## Context

This work extends the systematic multi-observable analysis approach previously applied to RHIC data (e.g., SysJet, gamma-h, ElPh, EMC, DihadronEl) to the new kinematic regime of the LHC. The analysis resolves apparent contradictions between RHIC and LHC results by showing that the same physics picture of medium-induced pQCD radiation with a small elastic component explains both datasets. The findings enable a shift from testing new ideas about jet quenching toward quantitative understanding of medium properties, and they rule out models such as pure elastic energy loss or AdS/CFT-based calculations that fail to describe the full set of observables.
