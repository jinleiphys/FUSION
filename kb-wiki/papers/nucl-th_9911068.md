# Selected nucleon form factors and a composite scalar diquark

> A covariant, composite scalar diquark, Fadde'ev amplitude model for the nucleon is used to calculate pseudoscalar, isoscalar- and isovector-vector, axial-vector and scalar nucleon form factors. The last yields the nucleon sigma-term and on-shell sigma-nucleon coupling. The calculated form factors are soft, and the couplings are generally in good agreement with experiment and other determinations. Elements in the dressed-quark-axial-vector vertex that are not constrained by the Ward-Takahashi identity contribute ~20% to the magnitude of g_A. The calculation of the nucleon sigma-term elucidates the only unambiguous means of extrapolating meson-nucleon couplings off the meson mass-shell.

## Key claim

A covariant nucleon model with a composite scalar diquark correlation yields meson-nucleon form factors and couplings in semi-quantitative agreement with experiment and phenomenological models, but the calculated axial-vector coupling g_A is only 0.80-0.99 compared to the experimental value of 1.259, with the discrepancy attributed to the omission of axial-vector diquark correlations and the transverse part of the dressed-quark-axial-vector vertex contributing approximately 20% to g_A.

## Method

The nucleon is represented as a three-quark bound state with a nonpointlike scalar diquark correlation using a Fadde'ev amplitude model. The dressed-quark propagator is given by an algebraic parametrization fitted to light-meson observables, and meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are specified for the pion, vector mesons (rho and omega), and axial-vector meson. Five impulse-approximation diagrams are evaluated using Monte-Carlo integration to compute the meson-nucleon vertices, with the diquark breakup diagrams (3-5) implementing the antisymmetrization of the nucleon's Fadde'ev amplitude.

## Key numbers

- g_piNN = 14.9 (calculated) versus phenomenological estimates of 13.4
- Pion-nucleon radius: 0.71 fm (Ansatz a) and 0.80 fm (Ansatz b) versus 0.93-1.06 fm from dipole fits
- g_rhoNN = 5.92 (Ansatz a), 6.26 (Ansatz b), 4.82 (Ansatz c) versus estimate of 6.4
- g_omegaNN = 9.74 (Ansatz a), 10.2 (Ansatz b), 11.5 (Ansatz c) versus estimates of 7-10.5
- g_A = 0.80 (Ansatz a) and 0.99 (Ansatz b) versus experimental value 1.259 +/- 0.017
- Axial radius: 0.75 fm (both Ansatze) versus experimental 0.68 +/- 0.12 fm
- sigma/M_N = 0.015 (calculated) versus lattice-QCD result 0.019 +/- 0.05
- g_sigma = 9.3 (calculated) versus estimate of 10
- Scalar radius: 0.89 fm (calculated) versus 1.2 fm from estimates
- Dipole pole masses for piNN: 0.96 GeV (Ansatz a) and 0.85 GeV (Ansatz b)
- Dipole pole masses for vector mesons: Lambda_1^omega = 1.12 GeV, Lambda_2^omega = 1.11 GeV, Lambda_1^rho = 1.06 GeV, Lambda_2^rho = 1.18 GeV
- Monopole pole masses for axial form factor: 0.65 GeV (Ansatz a) and 0.64 GeV (Ansatz b)

## Context

This work extends the covariant quark-diquark nucleon model previously applied to electromagnetic form factors by Bloch, Roberts, and Schmidt (1999) to calculate meson-nucleon form factors for the pion, rho, omega, axial-vector, and scalar channels. The calculated soft form factors support the phenomenological approach of Ref. (Harry) and the arguments of Ref. (Tony) for soft cutoff parameters, while the sensitivity of f_omegaNN to diagram 2 repeats the pattern observed in the isoscalar electromagnetic form factor calculation. The results enable improved constraints on meson exchange currents in light nuclear systems and provide guidance for extrapolating meson-nucleon couplings off the meson mass-shell, with the scalar sector calculation demonstrating the only unambiguous means of such extrapolation.
