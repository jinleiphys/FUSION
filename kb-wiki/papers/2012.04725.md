# Single-nucleon properties and pair correlations in nuclear matter from chiral two- and three-nucleon forces

> We investigate single-particle properties in infinite nuclear matter using a variety of interactions. One of the focal points is to study the impact of chiral three-nucleon forces on the nucleon self-energy and related quantities, such as spectral function and momentum distribution. We also present results for pairing correlations in nuclear matter. We find characteristic and systematic differences between the predictions obtained with the (softer) chiral interactions and those based on one-boson-exchange or phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral two- and three-nucleon forces produce systematically different single-particle properties and pairing correlations in nuclear matter compared to traditional one-boson-exchange or phenomenological potentials, with the softer chiral interactions yielding larger particle-particle correlation effects and smaller pairing gaps, but the authors note that the momentum distribution shows only moderate sensitivity to three-nucleon forces despite large effects on the self-energy.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation for infinite nuclear matter, then added the hole-hole (2h1p) contribution perturbatively to obtain the total nucleon self-energy. They computed spectral functions and momentum distributions from the self-energy, and evaluated pairing gaps in the \(^1S_0\) partial wave by solving the pphh Random Phase Approximation (RPA) eigenvalue problem, which yields complex eigenvalues whose imaginary part gives the pairing gap at the Fermi surface. The calculations used chiral two-nucleon forces from second to fifth order (NLO to N\(^4\)LO) with a regulator cutoff \(\Lambda\) between 450 and 500 MeV, and included the leading chiral three-nucleon force at N\(^2\)LO expressed as density-dependent effective two-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.35\) fm\(^{-1}\) used for most calculations, corresponding to a density of 0.166 fm\(^{-3}\), close to empirical saturation density.
- The N\(^4\)LO two-nucleon potential reproduces \(NN\) data below pion production threshold with \(\chi^2/\text{datum} = 1.15\).
- The chiral three-nucleon force reduces the Hartree-Fock contribution to the self-energy by almost a factor of 1/2 at saturation density.
- The gap function in the \(^1S_0\) partial wave at half saturation density vanishes for momenta around 4 fm\(^{-1}\) and larger for chiral interactions, while the CD-Bonn gap function extends to higher momenta.
- The imaginary part of the BHF self-energy from the chiral N\(^3\)LO(450) interaction shows larger absolute values at energies above the Fermi energy compared to CD-Bonn or Reid93, with Reid93's imaginary part increasing even above 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of single-particle properties in nuclear matter from traditional meson-theoretic potentials (CD-Bonn, Reid93) to modern chiral effective field theory interactions from the Entem-Machleidt-Nosyk (2017) family, and is the first to systematically compare order-by-order chiral predictions for self-energies, spectral functions, momentum distributions, and pairing gaps. The results enable future inclusion of subleading three-nucleon forces at N\(^3\)LO (derived by Kaiser and collaborators in 2018-2019) and motivate the use of self-consistent Green's function methods (Dickhoff and collaborators) over the hole-line expansion when using soft chiral interactions, since the particle-particle and hole-hole contributions are more comparable in magnitude than for traditional potentials.
