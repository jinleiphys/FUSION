# Three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model at finite isospin chemical potential

> QCD at finite isospin chemical potential $μ_{\text I}$ possesses a positively definite fermion determinant and the lattice simulation can be successfully performed. While the two-flavor effective models may be sufficient to describe the phenomenon of pion condensation, it is interesting to study the roles of the strangeness degree of freedom and the U$_{\rm A}(1)$ anomaly. In this paper, we present a systematic study of the three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) term that mimics the U$_{\rm A}(1)$ anomaly at finite isospin chemical potential. In the mean-field approximation, the model predicts a phase transition from the vacuum to the pion superfluid phase, which takes place at $μ_{\rm I}$ equal to the pion mass $m_π$. Due to the U$_{\rm A}(1)$ anomaly, the strangeness degree of freedom couples to the light quark degrees of freedom and the strange quark effective mass depends on the pion condensate. However, the strange quark condensate and the strange quark effective mass change slightly in the pion superfluid phase, which verifies the validity of the two-flavor models. The effective four-fermion interaction of the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft term in the presence of the pion condensation is constructed. Due to the U$_{\rm A}(1)$ anomaly, the pion condensation generally induces scalar-pseudoscalar interaction. The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the mesonic excitations is established and the meson mass spectra are obtained at finite isospin chemical potential and temperature. Finally, the general expression for the topological susceptibility $χ$ at finite isospin chemical potential $μ_{\rm I}$ is derived. In contrast to the finite temperature effect which suppresses $χ$, the isospin density effect leads to an enhancement of $χ$.

## Key claim

The three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft term predicts that the strange quark condensate and effective mass change only slightly (about 1% and 12 MeV respectively at 5 times the pion mass) in the pion superfluid phase, thereby verifying the validity of two-flavor models for describing pion condensation despite the coupling introduced by the U_A(1) anomaly.

## Method

The authors studied the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft term at finite isospin chemical potential using the mean-field approximation to obtain the thermodynamic potential and gap equations for chiral and pion condensates. They constructed an effective four-fermion interaction from the six-fermion KMT term in the presence of pion condensation, then used the Bethe-Salpeter equation within the random-phase approximation to compute meson mass spectra. The topological susceptibility was derived to leading order in the 1/N_c expansion.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition from vacuum to pion superfluid occurs at mu_I equal to the pion mass m_pi = 135.0 MeV (the vacuum pion mass used in parameter fitting)
- Model parameters: m_l = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV, cutoff Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G*Lambda^2 = 1.835, K*Lambda^5 = 12.36, yielding vacuum constituent quark masses M_l = 367.7 MeV and M_s = 549.5 MeV
- Vacuum chiral condensates: <bar{l}l>_0 = -(241.9 MeV)^3 and <bar{s}s>_0 = -(257.7 MeV)^3
- The strange quark effective mass M_s increases by about 12 MeV at mu_I = 5*m_pi
- The strange quark chiral condensate increases by about 1% at mu_I = 5*m_pi
- The scaled energy density epsilon/epsilon_SB shows a peak near mu_I approximately 1.3*m_pi, consistent with lattice simulation results for a larger pion mass m_pi ~ 390 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-flavor effective models for pion condensation at finite isospin density (ISO, ISOother01, ISOother02) by incorporating strangeness and the U_A(1) anomaly via the KMT term. It builds on the three-flavor NJL model studies without the anomaly by Barducci et al. (2004) and Warringa et al. (2006), and on the topological susceptibility derivation at finite temperature by Fukushima et al. (2001). The results enable future lattice QCD simulations to test the predicted enhancement of topological susceptibility at finite isospin density, in contrast to the suppression observed at finite temperature.
