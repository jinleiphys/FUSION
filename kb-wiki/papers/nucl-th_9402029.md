# Quantum Size Effects in Classical Hadrodynamics

> We discuss future directions in the development of classical hadrodynamics for extended nucleons, corresponding to nucleons of finite size interacting with massive meson fields. This new theory provides a natural covariant microscopic approach to relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions that includes automatically spacetime nonlocality and retardation, nonequilibrium phenomena, interactions among all nucleons, and particle production. The present version of our theory includes only the neutral scalar ($σ$) and neutral vector ($ω$) meson fields. In the future, additional isovector pseudoscalar ($π^+$,~$π^-$,~$π^0$), isovector vector ($ρ^+$,~$ρ^-$,~$ρ^0$), and neutral pseudoscalar ($η$) meson fields should be incorporated. Quantum size effects should be included in the equations of motion by use of the spreading function of Moniz and Sharp, which generates an effective nucleon mass density smeared out over a Compton wavelength. However, unlike the situation in electrodynamics, the Compton wavelength of the nucleon is small compared to its radius, so that effects due to the intrinsic size of the nucleon dominate.

## Key claim
Classical hadrodynamics for extended nucleons can provide a covariant microscopic description of relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, but quantum size effects from the nucleon's Compton wavelength are secondary to effects from the nucleon's intrinsic finite size, because the Compton wavelength (0.20783 fm) is much smaller than the nucleon's root-mean-square radius (0.862 fm).

## Method
The author derives classical relativistic many-body equations of motion for N extended nucleons interacting via neutral scalar (sigma) and neutral vector (omega) meson fields, using an exponential nucleon mass density with root-mean-square radius 0.862 fm. Quantum spreading is introduced via the Moniz-Sharp spreading function, which smears the intrinsic mass density over a Compton wavelength, and the resulting effective mass density is computed analytically for the exponential density profile. The equations are solved numerically without mean-field approximation, perturbative expansion, or superposition of two-body collisions for soft nucleon-nucleon collisions at several laboratory momenta.

## Key numbers
- Proton charge root-mean-square radius: 0.862 ± 0.012 fm (from elastic electron scattering)
- Bare nucleon mass M0 = 949.47 MeV/c^2, giving Compton wavelength λ = 0.20783 fm
- Nucleon mass density is exponential: ρ(r) = (μ^3/8π) exp(-μ r) with μ = √12 / R_rms and R_rms = 0.862 fm
- Electron Compton wavelength: λ_e = 386.15933 fm (for comparison)
- Soft nucleon-nucleon collisions solved at p_lab = 14.6, 30, 60, 100, and 200 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends the author's previous development of classical hadrodynamics for extended nucleons (Nix 1990, Nix and Bush 1992, Nix and Bush 1993a,b) by addressing future directions including additional meson fields (pion, rho, eta) and quantum size effects. The quantum spreading function follows the nonrelativistic quantum electrodynamics treatment of Moniz and Sharp (1977). The approach contrasts with hard-scattering quark-gluon descriptions, which are relevant for only about one in a billion events at high energies, whereas this theory targets the dominant soft collisions.
