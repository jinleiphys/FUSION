# Generalized relativistic second order magnetohydrodynamics: A correlation function approach using Zubarev's nonequilibrium statistical operator

> We use total energy-momentum conservation and the Bianchi identity (magnetic-flux conservation) to construct second-order relativistic magnetohydrodynamics in a Zubarev's non-equilibrium statistical operator (NESO) framework. We obtain all dissipative tensors in the medium by focusing on a relativistic magnetized plasma that preserves parity and is symmetric to charge-conjugation. We also provide Kubo formulas for all transport coefficients that arise at second order. Moreover, we extend the NESO formalism to systematically take into account for nonlocal contributions.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a second-order relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD) framework using Zubarev's nonequilibrium statistical operator (NESO), treating the magnetic field as a hydrodynamic variable via the Bianchi identity rather than assuming infinite electrical conductivity, which allows consistent inclusion of dissipative corrections up to second order in gradients.

## Method

The authors start from total energy-momentum conservation and the Bianchi identity (magnetic-flux conservation) as fundamental dynamical equations, then perform a tensorial decomposition of the energy-momentum tensor and dual electromagnetic field-strength tensor along the fluid velocity and magnetic field direction. They use Zubarev's NESO formalism to expand the nonequilibrium density operator around local equilibrium, expressing dissipative tensors in terms of two-point and three-point correlation functions, and systematically separate local and nonlocal contributions via Taylor expansion of hydrodynamic fields. The entropy current analysis at first order establishes linear constitutive relations and parity assignments for dissipative tensors, which then guide the construction of second-order constitutive relations and evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field is treated as an $\mathcal{O}(\partial^0)$ quantity, while the electric field appears only at higher order in the gradient expansion
- The entropy production rate is given by $\partial_\mu S^\mu = T_1^{\mu\nu}(\partial_\mu\beta_\nu) + \tilde F_1^{\mu\nu}(\partial_\mu\mathcal{H}_\nu)$, where $\beta_\nu = \beta u_\nu$ and $\mathcal{H}_\nu = \beta H_\nu$
- First-order transport coefficients include $\bar{\zeta}_\parallel$, $\bar{\zeta}_\perp$, $\bar{\eta}_\parallel$, $\bar{\eta}_\perp$, $\bar{\rho}_\parallel$, and $\bar{\rho}_\perp$, with the cross-coupling constraint $\bar{\zeta}_\parallel \bar{\zeta}_\perp - \bar{\zeta}^2_\times \geq 0$ for charge-conjugate symmetric plasma
- The thermodynamic relation $p_\parallel = p_\perp - B H$ connects longitudinal and transverse pressures

## Context

This work extends the first-order RMHD formulation based on total energy-momentum conservation and the Bianchi identity, previously developed by Hongo (2020) and Hattori (2022), to second order in gradients using Zubarev's NESO formalism. It provides Kubo formulas for all second-order transport coefficients and systematically incorporates nonlocal contributions, enabling causal and stable constitutive relations for magnetized relativistic plasmas relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, neutron star mergers, and highly magnetized astrophysical environments.
