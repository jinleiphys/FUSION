# Anisotropic transverse flow and the quark-hadron phase transition

> We use (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics with exact longitudinal boost-invariance to study the influence of collision centrality and initial energy density on the transverse flow pattern and the angular distributions of particles emitted near midrapidity in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We concentrate on radial flow and the elliptic flow coefficient v2 as functions of the impact parameter and of the collision energy. We demonstrate that the finally observed elliptic flow is established earlier in the collision than the observed radial flow and thus probes the equation of state at higher energy densities. We point out that a phase transition from hadronic matter to a color-deconfined quark-gluon plasma leads to non-monotonic behaviour in both beam energy and impact parameter dependences which, if observed, can be used to identify such a phase transition. Our calculations span collision energies from the Brookhaven AGS (Alternating Gradient Synchrotron) to beyond the LHC (Large Hadron Collider); the QGP phase transition signature is predicted between the lowest available SPS (CERN Super Proton Synchrotron) and the highest RHIC (Brookhaven Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider) energies. To optimize the chances for applicability of hydrodynamics we suggest to study the excitation function of flow anisotropies in central uranium-uranium collisions in the side-on-side collision geometry.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that elliptic flow is established earlier in the collision than radial flow and therefore probes the equation of state at higher energy densities; a quark-hadron phase transition leads to non-monotonic behavior in both beam energy and impact parameter dependences of these flow observables, but the authors hedge that this signature is predicted to appear between the lowest available SPS and the highest RHIC energies, not at currently accessible energies.

## Method

The authors used (3+1)-dimensional relativistic ideal hydrodynamics with exact longitudinal boost-invariance, reduced to (2+1) dimensions for the transverse dynamics, to simulate the expansion of the fireball created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. They employed three different equations of state: a hadron resonance gas (EOS H), a quark-gluon plasma ideal gas (EOS I), and a combined equation of state with a Maxwell-constructed phase transition (EOS Q). The hydrodynamic evolution was initialized using a Glauber model for energy deposition, tuned to reproduce midrapidity particle spectra from NA49 data for central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV/c, and terminated at a fixed decoupling energy density where particle spectra were computed via the Cooper-Frye prescription including resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV/c: e0 = 9.0 GeV/fm^3 (corresponding to initial central temperature T0 = 258 MeV)
- Initial baryon density in fireball center: n0 = 1.1 fm^{-3}
- Starting time for hydrodynamic expansion: tau0 = 0.8 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperature: T_dec approximately 120 MeV (corresponding to e_dec = 0.06 GeV/fm^3)
- Average radial flow velocity at freeze-out: <v_perp> = 0.45 c
- Bag constant used for QGP phase: B^{1/4} = 230 MeV, giving critical temperature T_c(n=0) = 164 MeV at vanishing net baryon density
- Critical energy density for quark-hadron transition: e_c less than or approximately 1 GeV/fm^3
- Nuclear radius for Pb: R0 = 6.5 fm with surface thickness xi = 0.54 fm
- Nuclear radius for U: R0 = 6.8 fm with deformation delta = 0.27, giving long axis R_l = 8.0 fm and short axis R_s = 6.2 fm
- Ground-state nuclear density: rho0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- For central side-on-side U+U collisions at 155 A GeV/c: central energy density 8% lower than for b=0 Pb+Pb, but 14% higher than in Pb+Pb at b=7 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Sorge (1997, 1999) on elliptic flow as a phase transition signature and by Rischke (1995) on directed flow softening, while also building on the authors' own previous short reports (Kolb, Sollfrank, Heinz 1999). The paper provides a systematic hydrodynamic treatment contrasting with microscopic kinetic approaches (Sorge 1997, 1999) and enables future experimental searches for the quark-hadron phase transition by predicting the excitation function of elliptic flow and radial flow across beam energies from AGS to beyond LHC, with the specific recommendation to study central uranium-uranium collisions in side-on-side geometry to optimize the applicability of hydrodynamics.
