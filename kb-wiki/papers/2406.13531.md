# LQCD constrained magnetic field dependent coupling constant in an effective model

> A magnetic field dependent coupling constant $G(eB)$ is investigated in the two-flavor magnetized NJL model. Based on LQCD results of the neutral (charged) pion mass spectra at vanishing temperature and finite magnetic field, we determine the $G(eB)=G^0(eB)$ ($G(eB)=G^+(eB)$) in the NJL model. $G^0(eB)$ and $G^+(eB)$ are both non-monotonic functions of magnetic fields, but they are different from each other. Furthermore, we calculate the pseudo-critical temperatures $T_{pc}(eB)$ of chiral restoration phase transition with $G^0(eB)$ and $G^+(eB)$ in the magnetized NJL model, respectively. The resulting $T_{pc}(eB)$ are non-monotonic functions of magnetic fields. In previous work, $G(eB)$ in the NJL model fitted from the chiral condensate or pseudo-critical temperature of LQCD simulations is a decreasing function of magnetic field. It can not explain the saturation behavior of mass spectra of neutral pion and decreasing behavior of mass spectra of charged pion with strong magnetic field. We conclude that a magnetic field dependent coupling constant $G(eB)$ in the NJL model can not simultaneously explain the reduction of pseudo-critical temperature of chiral restoration phase transition and the light meson mass spectra under external magnetic field.

## Key claim

A magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant \(G(eB)\) in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, when separately fitted to Lattice QCD results for neutral or charged pion masses at zero temperature, cannot simultaneously reproduce both the reduction of the pseudo-critical temperature of chiral restoration and the light meson mass spectra under an external magnetic field.

## Method

The author determines two distinct magnetic-field-dependent coupling constants, \(G^0(eB)\) and \(G^+(eB)\), by fitting the NJL model's neutral pion mass \(m_{\pi^0}\) and charged pion mass \(m_{\pi^+}\) respectively to Lattice QCD results at vanishing temperature and finite magnetic field (Ding 2008). Using each fitted \(G(eB)\), the pseudo-critical temperature \(T_{pc}(eB)\) of the chiral restoration phase transition is then calculated from the gap equation for the dynamical quark mass. The NJL model is regularized with the gauge-invariant Pauli-Villars scheme, with parameters fixed to reproduce the chiral condensate, pion mass, and pion decay constant at zero field.

## Key numbers

*   At zero magnetic field, the NJL model parameters are: current quark mass \(m_0 = 14.6\) MeV, coupling constant \(G_0 = 3.62\) GeV\(^{-2}\), Pauli-Villars mass parameter \(\Lambda = 1094\) MeV, yielding a pseudo-critical temperature \(T_{pc} = 169\) MeV (compared to the LQCD result of approximately 170 MeV from Ding 2022).
*   For \(G^0(eB)\) fitted from the neutral pion mass: at \(eB = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\), the coupling is fixed at \(G = 0.95 G_0\); at \(eB = 0.63\) GeV\(^2\), \(G = 0.83 G_0\); at \(eB = 1.04\) GeV\(^2\), \(G = 0.67 G_0\).
*   For \(G^+(eB)\) fitted from the charged pion mass: at \(eB = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\), the coupling is fixed at \(G = 1.02 G_0\); at \(eB = 0.63\) GeV\(^2\), \(G = 0.98 G_0\); at \(eB = 1.04\) GeV\(^2\), \(G = 0.7 G_0\).

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies that introduced a magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant \(G(eB)\) fitted to the chiral condensate or pseudo-critical temperature from Lattice QCD (e.g., Gebhardt 2014, Mao 2022). Those earlier fits produced a monotonically decreasing \(G(eB)\) that could not explain the saturation of the neutral pion mass or the decrease of the charged pion mass at strong magnetic fields. The present paper shows that fitting \(G(eB)\) to the pion masses instead yields a non-monotonic \(G(eB)\) but still fails to simultaneously describe the decreasing pseudo-critical temperature observed in Lattice QCD (Ding 2022). The conclusion points to the need for additional physical mechanisms beyond a running coupling constant in the NJL model.
