# Reliable extraction of the $dB({\rm E1})/dE$ for $^{11}$Be from its breakup at 520 MeV/nucleon

> We analyze the breakup of the one-neutron halo nucleus $^{11}$Be measured at 520 MeV/nucleon at GSI on Pb and C targets within an eikonal description of the reaction including a proper treatment of special relativity. The Coulomb term of the projectile-target interaction is corrected at first order, while its nuclear part is described at the optical limit approximation. Excellent agreement with the data is obtained using a description of $^{11}$Be, which fits the breakup data of RIKEN. This solves the apparent discrepancy between the $dB({\rm E1})/dE$ estimations from GSI and RIKEN for this nucleus.

## Key claim

The apparent discrepancy between the E1 strength extracted from GSI and RIKEN breakup measurements of 11Be is resolved by using a consistent Halo-EFT description of the projectile and a reaction model that includes both Coulomb and nuclear interactions and their interferences, rather than by any fundamental difference in the underlying nuclear structure.

## Method

The authors analyze the breakup of 11Be on Pb and C targets at 520 MeV/nucleon using an eikonal description of the reaction with a first-order correction to the Coulomb interaction (Margueron et al. approach) and the optical limit approximation for the nuclear part. Special relativity is treated by using relativistic kinematics and boosting the Coulomb phase according to Winther and Alder's relativistic Coulomb excitation result. The structure of 11Be is described using a Halo-EFT potential fitted to the output of an ab initio calculation by Calci et al.

## Key numbers

* The beam energy for the GSI experiment is 520 MeV/nucleon.
* For the Pb target, the relativistic parameters are: gamma = 1.4874, gamma_P = 1.4872, v/c = 0.798, v_P/c = 0.740, K_0 = 57.7 fm^-1, eta = 3.00.
* For the C target, the relativistic parameters are: gamma = 1.14090, gamma_P = 1.14089, v/c = 0.931, v_P/c = 0.481, K_0 = 28.8 fm^-1, eta = 0.188.
* The nuclear contribution to the breakup on Pb is at most 5%.
* The Halo-EFT potentials used have Gaussian widths of sigma = 1.2 fm, 1.5 fm, and 2 fm, all producing identical results for the breakup cross section.

## Context

This work resolves a longstanding discrepancy between the E1 strength estimates from the GSI experiment at 520 MeV/nucleon and the RIKEN experiment at 69 MeV/nucleon (Fukuda et al., 2004). The analysis shows that the GSI data analysis, which subtracted an estimated nuclear contribution from the total breakup cross section, neglected quantal interferences between Coulomb and nuclear contributions, leading to an underestimated E1 strength. The model developed here provides a reliable tool for analyzing breakup measurements of one-neutron halo nuclei at GSI and the RIBF facility, and can be extended to two-neutron halo nuclei like 6He and 11Li.
