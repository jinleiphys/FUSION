# Representations for Three-Body T-Matrix on Unphysical Sheets

> Explicit representations are formulated for the Faddeev components of three-body T-matrix continued analytically on unphysical sheets of the energy Riemann surface. According to the representations, the T-matrix on unphysical sheets is obviously expressed in terms of its components taken on the physical sheet only. The representations for T-matrix are used then to construct similar representations for analytical continuation of three-body scattering matrices and resolvent. Domains on unphysical sheets are described where the representations obtained can be applied.

## Key claim

The paper provides explicit representations for the Faddeev components of the three-body T-matrix continued analytically onto unphysical sheets of the energy Riemann surface, expressing them solely in terms of the components taken on the physical sheet and certain truncations of the scattering matrix. The representations are justified only on a specific part of the three-body Riemann surface, and the interaction potentials are assumed to decrease in coordinate space not slower than exponentially.

## Method

The author constructs analytical continuation of the Faddeev equations for the three-body T-matrix components onto unphysical sheets of the energy Riemann surface, using momentum-space integral equations. The continuation is performed by expressing the T-matrix on unphysical sheets in terms of the physical-sheet T-matrix and truncations of the scattering matrix, with the type of truncation determined by the index of the unphysical sheet. The representations are then used to construct similar expressions for the analytical continuation of three-body scattering matrices and the resolvent.

## Key numbers

- The pair potentials satisfy the estimate |v_α(k)| ≤ c/(1+|k|)^θ₀ exp(a₀|Im k|) for all k∈C³, with θ₀∈(3/2, 2) and a₀>0 (first variant), or the same estimate with a₀=0 in a strip |Im k|<2b (second variant)
- The discrete spectrum eigenvalues λ_αj of the pair subsystem Hamiltonians are negative and finite, with λ_max = max_{α,j} λ_αj < 0
- The continuous spectrum of the three-body Hamiltonian H is σ_c(H) = (λ_min, +∞) where λ_min = min_{α,j} λ_αj
- The two-body T-matrix continuation on the unphysical sheet is expressed as t(z)|_Π₁ = t(z) - a₀(z) τ(z) where a₀(z) = -πi√z

## Context

This work extends the author's previous results on analytical continuation of T-matrix in multichannel scattering problems with binary channels (Motovilov, 1994) to the three-body case. It builds upon the Faddeev equations (Faddeev, 1963) and their modifications for the physical sheet, and generalizes the s-wave continuation for separable pair potentials developed by Orlov and collaborators (Orlov, 1991). The results enable rigorous identification of three-body resonances as zeros of certain truncations of the scattering matrix on the physical sheet, and provide a foundation for studying the structure of the resolvent and scattering matrices on unphysical sheets beyond what is accessible via complex scaling methods (Balslev and Combes, 1971).
