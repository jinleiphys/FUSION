# Nuclear structure corrections to the Lamb shift in $μ^3$He$^+$ and $μ^3$H

> Measuring the 2S-2P Lamb shift in a hydrogen-like muonic atom allows one to extract its nuclear charge radius with a high precision that is limited by the uncertainty in the nuclear structure corrections. The charge radius of the proton thus extracted was found to be 7-sigma away from the CODATA value, in what has become the yet unsolved "proton radius puzzle". Further experiments currently aim at the isotopes of hydrogen and helium: the precise extraction of their radii may provide a hint at the solution of the puzzle. We present the first ab initio calculation of nuclear structure corrections, including the nuclear polarization correction, to the 2S-2P transition in $μ^3$He$^+$ and $μ^3$H, and assess solid theoretical error bars. Our predictions reduce the uncertainty in the nuclear structure corrections to the level of a few percents and will be instrumental to the on-going $μ^3$He$^+$ experiment. We also support the mirror $μ\,^3$H system as a candidate for further probing of the nucleon polarizabilities and shedding more light on the puzzle.

## Key claim

The authors present the first *ab initio* calculation of nuclear structure corrections, including nuclear polarization, to the 2S-2P Lamb shift in muonic helium-3 and muonic tritium, reducing the uncertainty in these corrections to the level of a few percent. However, the precision of the total two-photon exchange correction is ultimately limited by the hadronic (nucleon-level) contributions, which are estimated rather than calculated from first principles and carry larger relative uncertainties.

## Method

The nuclear polarization correction was evaluated using sum rules of nuclear response functions computed with the Lanczos sum rule method, while ground-state observables and Lanczos coefficients were obtained via the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics method. The nuclear Hamiltonian employed either the phenomenological AV18/UIX potential (two-nucleon plus three-nucleon forces) or a chiral effective field theory potential with two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces. The nucleon-size corrections were incorporated via a low-momentum expansion of nucleon form factors parameterized by their mean square charge radii, and the nucleonic two-photon exchange contributions were estimated by scaling results from muonic hydrogen.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear polarization correction for muonic helium-3: delta^A_pol = -4.16(06)(16) meV (first uncertainty: nuclear-model dependence; second: all other sources)
- Nuclear polarization correction for muonic tritium: delta^A_pol = -0.476(10)(13) meV
- Total nuclear-structure two-photon exchange correction for muonic helium-3: delta^A_TPE = -14.64(25)(27) meV
- Total nuclear-structure two-photon exchange correction for muonic tritium: delta^A_TPE = -0.703(16)(11) meV
- Zemach term for muonic helium-3 using r_p(mu^-): delta^A_Zem = -10.49(19)(16) meV
- Zemach term for muonic tritium using r_p(mu^-): delta^A_Zem = -0.227(5)(3) meV
- Nucleonic two-photon exchange correction for muonic helium-3: delta^N_TPE = -0.762(125) meV
- Nucleonic two-photon exchange correction for muonic tritium: delta^N_TPE = -0.065(16) meV

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* approach previously applied to muonic helium-4 by Ji (2013) to the three-body nuclei helium-3 and tritium, replacing outdated estimates from Joachain (1961) and Rinker (1976) that were based on simplistic nuclear models. The results are instrumental for the ongoing CREMA collaboration experiment at PSI measuring the Lamb shift in muonic helium-3, and the authors propose the mirror muonic tritium system as a candidate for further probing nucleon polarizabilities to help resolve the proton radius puzzle. The calculations also provide the first predictions for the unmeasured electric dipole polarizability of tritium.
