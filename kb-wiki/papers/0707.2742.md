# Description of double beta decay within continuum-QRPA

> A method to calculate the nuclear double beta decay ($2νββ$- and $0νββ$-) amplitudes within the continuum random phase approximation (cQRPA) is formulated. Calculations of the $ββ$ transition amplitudes within the cQRPA are performed for ^{76}Ge, ^{100}Mo and ^{130}Te. A rather simple nuclear Hamiltonian consisting of phenomenological mean field and zero-range residual particle-hole and particle-particle interaction is used. The calculated M^{2ν} are almost not affected when the single-particle continuum is taken into account. At the same time, a regular suppression of the $0νββ$-amplitude is found that can be associated with additional ground state correlations due to collective states in the continuum. It is expected that future inclusion of the nucleon pairing in the single-particle continuum will somewhat compensate the suppression.

# Description of double beta decay within continuum-QRPA

## Key claim

The continuum QRPA (cQRPA) method for calculating double beta decay amplitudes is formulated for the first time, and the authors find that including the single-particle continuum produces a regular suppression of the neutrinoless double beta decay ($0\nu\beta\beta$) amplitude due to additional ground-state correlations from collective states in the continuum, while the two-neutrino double beta decay ($2\nu\beta\beta$) amplitudes remain almost unaffected. The authors note that the calculated $0\nu\beta\beta$ matrix elements should be considered lower limits because nucleon pairing in the single-particle continuum is not yet included, and they expect future inclusion of such pairing will somewhat compensate the suppression.

## Method

The authors reformulate the proton-neutron QRPA (pn-QRPA) in coordinate representation using four-component radial transition densities and free two-quasiparticle propagators, then extend this formalism to include the single-particle continuum by replacing sums over unbound states with radial single-particle Green's functions. The residual interactions are taken as zero-range Landau-Migdal forces for both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels, with pairing treated only within a discrete BCS basis. The $2\nu\beta\beta$ amplitude $M^{2\nu}$ is obtained from the static nuclear polarizability at $\omega=0$, while the $0\nu\beta\beta$ amplitude $M^{0\nu}$ requires integration over energy of the imaginary part of the total two-quasiparticle propagator.

## Key numbers

- For $^{76}$Ge, $^{100}$Mo, and $^{130}$Te, the calculated $M^{2\nu}$ amplitudes are "almost not affected" when the single-particle continuum is taken into account (no specific numerical change given for $M^{2\nu}$)
- A "regular suppression" of the $0\nu\beta\beta$ amplitude $M^{0\nu}$ is found when including the continuum, which the authors associate with additional ground-state correlations from collective states in the continuum
- The calculations use a nuclear Hamiltonian with the isovector particle-hole strength $f^0_{ph}=1.0$ (in units of $C_0=300$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$)
- The s.p. basis for discrete QRPA calculations includes $N=1-5$ major Saxon-Woods shells for $^{76}$Ge and $^{100}$Mo (16 successive s.p. levels), and $N=1-6$ major shells for $^{130}$Te (22 successive s.p. levels including bound and 6 quasistationary states)
- The approximation of setting $v_\alpha=0$, $u_\alpha=1$ for continuum states is stated to have accuracy $\frac{\Delta}{|\varepsilon-\lambda|}$, which is "good enough already for $\varepsilon_\alpha-\lambda_i \ge E_{max}\simeq$ 10 MeV"
- The omission of high-order terms in the nucleonic weak current and finite nucleon size corrections is noted to "lead to a slight overestimation of the calculated $M^{0\nu}$ by 20--30%"

## Context

This work extends the standard QRPA calculations of double beta decay matrix elements performed by Rodin et al. (2003, 2005), which used discrete harmonic oscillator bases comprising $N=2$, 3, and 5 major shells and found that adjusting the particle-particle strength $g_{pp}$ to reproduce experimental $2\nu\beta\beta$ rates made $M^{0\nu}$ essentially independent of basis size. The present cQRPA approach allows inclusion of the entire single-particle continuum without truncation, addressing the question of whether the basis-size independence found in those earlier works holds when $N\to\infty$. The continuum QRPA method builds on the continuum RPA (cRPA) formulated by Shlomo and Bertsch (1975) and the charge-exchange pn-cQRPA developed by Borzov et al. (1990) and Rodin et al. (2003), which had previously been applied to describe giant resonances, muon capture, and neutrino-nucleus reactions. The present work enables future inclusion of nucleon pairing in the continuum, which the authors expect will partially compensate the suppression of $M^{0\nu}$ found here.
