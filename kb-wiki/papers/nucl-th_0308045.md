# Trinucleon photonuclear reactions with $Δ$-isobar excitation: Processes below pion-production threshold

> Radiative nucleon-deuteron capture and two- and three-body photo disintegration of the three-nucleon bound state are described. The description uses the purely nucleonic charge-dependent CD-Bonn potential and its coupled-channel extension CD Bonn + $Δ$. The $Δ$-isobar excitation yields an effective three-nucleon force and effective two- and three-nucleon currents besides other $Δ$-isobar effects; they are mutually consistent. Exact solutions of three-particle equations are employed for the initial and final states of the reactions. The current has one-baryon and two-baryon contributions and couples nucleonic with $Δ$-isobar channels. $Δ$-isobar effects on the observables are isolated. Shortcomings of the theoretical description are discussed and their consequence for the calculation of observables is estimated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including explicit $\Delta$-isobar degrees of freedom in a coupled-channel potential (CD Bonn + $\Delta$) yields mutually consistent effective three-nucleon forces and exchange currents for trinucleon photonuclear reactions below pion-production threshold, but the theoretical description still has technical shortcomings that affect the accuracy of calculated observables.

## Method

The authors solve three-particle scattering equations exactly using Chebyshev expansion of the two-baryon transition matrix as an interpolation technique, employing both the purely nucleonic charge-dependent CD-Bonn potential and its coupled-channel extension CD Bonn + $\Delta$ that allows for single $\Delta$-isobar excitation in isospin-triplet partial waves. The electromagnetic current includes one-baryon and two-baryon contributions with $\pi$, $\rho$, $\rho\pi\gamma$, and $\omega\pi\gamma$ exchanges, coupling nucleonic with $\Delta$-isobar channels, and is expanded in electric and magnetic multipoles up to total two-baryon angular momentum $I=6$. The hadronic interaction is considered up to $I=4$, and the calculations omit the Coulomb potential between charged baryons.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ${}^3\mathrm{He}$: -7.941 MeV for CD Bonn, -8.225 MeV for CD Bonn + $\Delta$ (purely hadronic values without Coulomb)
- With Coulomb interaction included, binding energies shift to -7.261 MeV (CD Bonn) and -7.544 MeV (CD Bonn + $\Delta$), compared to the experimental value of -7.718 MeV
- The $\Delta$-isobar rest mass used is $m_{\Delta}c^2 = 1232$ MeV
- The average nucleon rest mass used is $m_N c^2 = 938.919$ MeV
- The coupled-channel potential CD Bonn + $\Delta$ is fitted to experimental two-nucleon data up to 350 MeV nucleon lab energy

## Context

This work updates previous calculations by Yuan (2002) of trinucleon photo reactions, extending them to higher energies and including three-nucleon breakup for the first time, while remaining below pion-production threshold. The coupled-channel extension CD Bonn + $\Delta$ improves upon earlier coupled-channel potentials constructed by the subtraction technique used by Hajduk (1983) by being properly fitted to data with quality matching the original CD-Bonn potential. The results are expected to be qualitatively similar to alternative descriptions by Golak (2000) and Skibinski (2003) that use different two-nucleon potentials, explicit irreducible three-nucleon forces, and different electromagnetic currents.
