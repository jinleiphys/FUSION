# Renormalization of the three-boson system with short-range interactions revisited

> We consider renormalization of the three-body scattering problem in low-energy effective field theory of self-interacting scalar particles by applying time-ordered perturbation theory to the manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation. The obtained leading-order equation is perturbatively renormalizable and non-perturbatively finite and does not require a three-body counter term in contrast to its non-relativistic approximation.

## Key claim

The leading-order integral equation for three-boson scattering, derived from a manifestly Lorentz-invariant effective field theory using time-ordered perturbation theory, is perturbatively renormalizable and yields a cutoff-independent non-perturbative solution without requiring a three-body counter term, in contrast to the non-relativistic Skornyakov-Ter-Martirosyan equation which requires such a term for a unique cutoff-independent solution.

## Method

The authors start from a Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian for self-interacting scalar particles with a phi^4 contact interaction, introduce a dummy "dimeron" field to resum two-body scattering, and apply time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) retaining only particle intermediate states. They derive a half-off-shell integral equation for the particle-dimeron scattering amplitude, project it onto the S-wave, and solve the resulting equation numerically with a sharp momentum cutoff. The ultraviolet behavior of the relativistic equation is compared to its non-relativistic limit, which reproduces the Skornyakov-Ter-Martirosyan equation.

## Key numbers

- The two-body scattering length is set to a_2 = 1 MeV^{-1} and the scalar particle mass to m = 939 MeV for the numerical demonstration.
- The amplitude t(k,p)/(m g^2) is evaluated at k = 0 MeV and p = 10 MeV as a function of the cutoff Lambda.
- The non-relativistic equation (Eq. 7 of Bedaque, Hammer, van Kolck (1999)) does not converge to a fixed limit as the cutoff is removed, while the relativistic equation converges to a well-defined limit.
- The effective range induced by relativistic corrections is r_eff ~ 1/m, which is approximately 1/(939 MeV) in natural units.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of three-body renormalization in effective field theory originally formulated by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999), who found that a three-body force is necessary in the non-relativistic approach. The authors show that the Lorentz-invariant formulation, using TOPT, avoids this problem because the relativistic propagators provide a logarithmic improvement in the ultraviolet behavior of the integral equation. This approach enables a renormalization scheme for three-body systems with large two-body scattering length that does not require a three-body counter term at leading order, though the authors note that for systems where a_2 >> r_2 ~ 1/m, the non-perturbative inclusion of the two-body range is necessary, and the actual impact of three-body forces on observables remains an open question.
