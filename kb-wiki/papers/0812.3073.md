# Excited mesons in a Bethe-Salpeter approach

> In theoretical hadron physics mesons are a center of attention. Constructed in a simpler way than baryons in the quark model, they still present a considerable challenge if one aims at an understanding of all their aspects in terms of quarks and gluons in the context of Quantum Chromodynamics, the quantum field theory of the strong interaction. Complementary to (constituent-) quark models, reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, lattice QCD, and effective field theories, the Dyson-Schwinger-equation approach has emerged as a well-suited formalism for the covariant study of hadron properties. In particular, radially excited mesons exhibit a sensitivity to long-range strong-interaction physics. This sensitivity has recently been studied with the help of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. Here these studies are reviewed and continued together with an account of possible future developments.

## Key claim

Radially excited meson masses computed in the rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach considerably underestimate experimental values, even when the model parameter \(\omega\) (the inverse effective range of the strong interaction) is varied; quantitatively reliable results will require corrections beyond RL truncation and explicit coupling to hadronic decay channels.

## Method

The author solves the homogeneous, ladder-truncated quark-antiquark Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) together with the rainbow-truncated QCD gap equation, using the effective interaction model of Maris and Tandy (1999). To handle the complex momentum arguments required for the quark propagator in the BSE, a Cauchy-integral method is employed with a modified parabolic contour and a numerical correction scheme from Ioakimidis (1991). Pseudoscalar and vector meson masses are computed for ground states and first radial excitations as functions of the current-quark mass and of the model parameter \(\omega\).

## Key numbers

* Pseudoscalar and vector meson masses for ground and first radially excited states are computed as functions of the current-quark mass; the light-, strange-, and charm-quark masses are fixed by requiring the vector ground-state mass to agree with the experimental value.
* For light and charm quarks, the radially excited vector meson masses show a strong dependence on the model parameter \(\omega\) (the inverse effective range of the interaction), yet they still considerably underestimate the experimental values, which lie well above the plotted region.
* The model interaction contains a term \(\mathcal{G}(k^2)/k^2 \sim \frac{D}{\omega^4}\frac{k^2}{\omega^2} e^{-k^2/\omega^2}\), where \(D\) and \(\omega\) are free parameters fitted to pion observables and the chiral condensate in Maris and Tandy (1999).

## Context

This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter studies of pseudoscalar and scalar meson excitations (Holl 2004, 2005; Krassnigg 2006; Bhagwat 2007) to include vector-meson radial excitations. The rainbow-ladder truncation, which is the lowest-order systematic truncation scheme (Munczek 1994; Bender 1996), has been used extensively for ground-state meson properties (Maris 1999; Bhagwat 2006) and for heavy quarks (Maris 2006). The results show that while qualitative studies of radial excitations are feasible, future work must incorporate corrections to the rainbow-ladder kernel and an explicit treatment of hadronic decay channels to achieve quantitative agreement with experiment.
