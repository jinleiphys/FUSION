# Neutron star universal relations with microscopic equations of state

> We calculate neutron star's moment of inertia and deformabilities using various microscopic equations of state for nuclear and hybrid star configurations. Correlations between the various observables are examined and we confirm several universal relations. We focus in particular on the constraints for the neutron star radii imposed by a determination of the average tidal deformability of the binary neutron star system GW170817. We find compatible radii between 12 and 13 kilometers and identify the suitable equations of state.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that several universal (EOS-independent) relations between neutron star moment of inertia, tidal deformability, and quadrupole moment hold for a set of microscopic equations of state, including hybrid stars with a quark phase, but notes that hyperonic EOSs show larger deviations from the universal fits, particularly near the maximum mass configuration.

## Method

The authors computed neutron star moment of inertia, tidal Love number, and quadrupole moment by solving the general relativistic structure equations (TOV equations) together with the slow-rotation and small-tidal-deformation formalisms, using 22 different equations of state. These included microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) nucleonic EOSs with various two-body and three-body forces, hyperonic extensions, Dirac-BHF and variational (APR) results, two phenomenological relativistic mean-field models (LS220, SFHo), and hybrid stars constructed by a Gibbs phase transition to a Dyson-Schwinger model of quark matter.

## Key numbers

- Radii for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star span the range 11.6 km to 13.2 km across all considered EOSs.
- The BOB nucleonic EOS gives a maximum mass of 2.51 solar masses with a corresponding radius of 11.32 km.
- The V18 nucleonic EOS gives a maximum mass of 2.34 solar masses with a corresponding radius of 10.63 km.
- The APR EOS gives a maximum mass of 2.20 solar masses with a radius of 9.92 km at that mass.
- The tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses ranges from 6 (UIX+DS4 hybrid) to 681 (DBHF nucleonic).
- The universal fit for I/MR^2 versus compactness beta is I/MR^2 = 0.207 + 0.857 beta, with a scatter of plus or minus 0.011.
- The universal relation between I/M^3 and tidal deformability lambda has fractional errors of less than 1 percent for most EOSs, slightly larger for hyperonic EOSs.
- The GW170817 constraints (400 less than tidal deformability less than 800 at 1.365 solar masses) imply radii between 12 km and 13 km.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star universal relations (I-Love-Q relations) by Yagi and Yunes (2013, 2017) to a set of microscopic equations of state based on many-body theory, complementing the phenomenological EOSs used in those earlier works. The paper also applies the constraints from the GW170817 gravitational wave event, as analyzed by the LIGO/Virgo collaboration (2017) and Radice et al. (2018), to determine which microscopic EOSs are compatible with the observed tidal deformability limits. The results enable future simultaneous measurements of mass, radius, and moment of inertia to potentially distinguish between purely nucleonic stars and stars containing hyperons or quark matter.
