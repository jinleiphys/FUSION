# Mean-Field Effects on Collective Flows in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions at 2-158A GeV energies

> Collective flows in heavy-ion collisions from AGS ($(2-11)A$ GeV) to SPS ($(40,158)A$ GeV) energies are investigated in a nonequilibrium transport model with nuclear mean-field (MF). Sideward $<p_x>$, directed $v_1$, and elliptic flows $v_2$ are systematically studied with different assumptions on the nuclear equation of state (EOS). We find that momentum dependence in the nuclear MF is important for the understanding of the proton collective flows at AGS and SPS energies. Calculated results with momentum dependent MF qualitatively reproduce the experimental data of proton sideward, directed, and elliptic flows in a incident energy range of $(2-158)A$ GeV.

## Key claim

Momentum dependence in the nuclear mean-field (MF) is essential for qualitatively reproducing proton collective flow data (sideward, directed, and elliptic) across the full incident energy range of 2-158A GeV, while momentum-independent MF fails to describe the data consistently. The authors find that the effect of MF on directed flow is strong at SPS energies, but elliptic flow is relatively insensitive to MF at those energies because string excitations dominate early-stage particle production and do not feel the nuclear mean field.

## Method

The authors combined the hadronic cascade model JAM (Jet AA Microscopic Transportation Model) with a covariant prescription of mean-field (RQMD/S framework) using a Skyrme-type density-dependent potential plus a Lorentzian-type momentum-dependent potential. They simulated Au+Au collisions at AGS energies (2-11A GeV) and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (40,158A GeV) with four different equation of state parameter sets: momentum-dependent hard (MH, K=448 MeV), momentum-dependent soft (MS, K=314 MeV), momentum-independent hard (H, K=380 MeV), and momentum-independent soft (S, K=200 MeV), plus cascade without mean-field (CS). Potential interactions were included only between baryons, with a simulation time step of 0.1 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility values for the four EOS parameter sets: MH = 448 MeV, MS = 314 MeV, H = 380 MeV, S = 200 MeV
- Saturation condition: total energy per nucleon at normal nuclear density E/A = -16 MeV, pressure P = 0 MeV/fm^3
- High-energy limit of optical potential fixed to U = 77 MeV at infinite incident energy, giving constraint alpha+beta = 77 MeV
- Energy conservation violation in simulations: about 0.4% in average of time and events
- Impact parameter range for mid-central collisions: 4 < b < 8 fm
- Simulation time step: dt = 0.1 fm/c at all incident energies

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective flows using transport models such as RQMD, BEM, RBUU, ART, and UrQMD, which had been successful up to AGS energies but had not seriously investigated MF effects at SPS energies. The authors build on the finding by Sahu et al. (2002) that saturating momentum dependence of the mean-field, a large number of hadronic resonances, and string degrees of freedom are essential at AGS energies, and on the work of Danielewicz et al. (2002) showing that stiffness values K=167-380 MeV cannot be uniquely determined from flow data up to AGS energies. The results enable future investigation of whether the incident energy dependence of elliptic flow has a dip at around 40A GeV (as suggested by NA49 data) or is monotonic, and motivate inclusion of mean-field effects for strings and pions for better understanding of collective flows at SPS energies.
