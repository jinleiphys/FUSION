The spectral functions exhibit strong medium effects coming from the hard thermal loop approximation for the quark propagator. # Hadron Correlators in the Deconfined Phase

## Key claim
Temporal meson correlators computed using hard thermal loop (HTL) resummed quark propagators do not differ significantly from free correlators with bare quark propagators, despite strong medium effects in the spectral functions. This is in contrast to lattice calculations that show clear deviations from free correlation functions, indicating that HTL medium effects alone are insufficient to explain the lattice results.

## Method
The authors computed temporal meson correlators and their spectral functions in the deconfined phase using the hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation technique for the quark propagator in the imaginary time formalism. The spectral function of the temporal correlator was decomposed into pole-pole, pole-cut, and cut-cut contributions, corresponding to the pole and cut structures of the HTL quark propagator, which includes effective temperature-dependent quark masses and Landau damping. The correlators were then obtained by integrating the spectral functions over energy using the relation between the temporal correlator and the spectral function.

## Key numbers
- The effective quark mass in the HTL approximation is given by \( m_q = g(T) T / \sqrt{6} \).
- The transition process from the upper to the lower branch of the quark dispersion relation starts at zero energy and continues until the maximum difference \( \omega = 0.47 \, m_q \) between the two branches at \( k_2 = 1.18 \, m_q \).
- The plasmino annihilation starts at \( \omega = 1.86 \, m_q \) with a Van Hove singularity corresponding to the minimum of the plasmino branch at \( k_3 = 0.41 \, m_q \).
- The free correlator screening mass is \( \mu = 2\pi T \), describing the long-range behavior of free correlators in time and space.

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice calculations by Boyd et al. (1994) that showed mesonic correlation functions in the pseudo-scalar channel differ clearly from free correlators above the critical temperature \( T_c \). The authors use the HTL resummation technique developed by Braaten and Pisarski (1990) to investigate whether in-medium modifications of collective quark modes in the quark-gluon plasma can explain these lattice results. The finding that HTL correlators remain close to free correlators supports speculation about bound states or other non-perturbative effects in the QGP close to the critical temperature, and motivates further investigation of the origin of non-trivial correlations observed on the lattice.
