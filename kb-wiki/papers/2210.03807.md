# A convolution formalism for defining spatial densities of hadrons

> We clarify the meaning of spatial densities of hadrons. A physical density is given by the expectation value of a local operator for a physical state, and depends on both internal structure and the hadron's wave packet. In some particular cases, the physical density can be written as a convolution between a density function that depends on internal structure but not wave packet, and a smearing function that depends on wave packet but not internal structure. We show that the light front densities often encountered in the literature have this property but that instant form densities do not. For hadrons prepared in broad wave packets, physical instant form densities approximately obey such a convolution relation, with Breit frame densities as the apparent internal densities. However, there is an infinite series of relativistic corrections to this convolution formula.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a physical density of a hadron can be separated into a convolution of an internal density (independent of the hadron's wave packet) and a smearing function (dependent only on the wave packet) only for certain densities; light front densities have this property, but instant form densities do not, and for broad wave packets instant form densities only approximately obey such a convolution with an infinite series of relativistic corrections.

## Method

The authors define a physical density as the expectation value of a local operator for a single-hadron state and analyze whether it can be written as a convolution of a wave-packet-independent internal density and a wave-packet-dependent smearing function. They derive general expressions for physical densities in light front coordinates for spin-zero targets without assuming a specific wave packet form, using momentum-space completeness relations and Fourier transforms to separate barycentric and internal contributions. They then examine specific operators: the electromagnetic current, the energy-momentum tensor components (momentum densities, energy density, and stress tensor), classifying each as either a "simple density" (obeying the convolution form) or a "compound density" (requiring multiple terms).

## Key numbers

- For the light front charge density of a spin-zero hadron, the internal density is given by the two-dimensional Fourier transform of the electromagnetic form factor \(F(-\boldsymbol{\Delta}_\perp^2)\): \(j^+_{\mathrm{int.}}(\mathbf{b}_\perp) = \int \frac{\mathrm{d}^2\boldsymbol{\Delta}_\perp}{(2\pi)^2} F(-\boldsymbol{\Delta}_\perp^2) e^{-i\boldsymbol{\Delta}_\perp \cdot \mathbf{b}_\perp}\).
- For the light front \(P^+\) density, the internal density is the Fourier transform of the gravitational form factor \(A(-\boldsymbol{\Delta}_\perp^2)\): \(T^{++}_{\mathrm{int.}}(\mathbf{b}_\perp) = \int \frac{\mathrm{d}^2\boldsymbol{\Delta}_\perp}{(2\pi)^2} A(-\boldsymbol{\Delta}_\perp^2) e^{-i\boldsymbol{\Delta}_\perp\cdot\mathbf{b}_\perp}\).
- The light front energy density is a compound density with two terms: one involving the smearing function \(\mathcal{W}^-(\mathbf{R}_\perp) = \int \mathrm{d}R^- \Psi^*(R^-,\mathbf{R}_\perp) \frac{-\olra{\boldsymbol{\nabla}}_\perp^2}{4} \Psi(R^-,\mathbf{R}_\perp)\) and the other involving the probability density \(\mathcal{P}(\mathbf{R}_\perp) = \int \mathrm{d}R^- |\Psi(R^-,\mathbf{R}_\perp)|^2\).
- The light front stress tensor is a compound density with two terms: one involving the smearing function \(\mathcal{W}^{ij}(\mathbf{R}_\perp) = \int \mathrm{d}R^- \Psi^*(R^-,\mathbf{R}_\perp) \left(\frac{-i\olra{\boldsymbol{\nabla}}_\perp^i}{2}\right) \left(\frac{-i\olra{\boldsymbol{\nabla}}_\perp^j}{2}\right) \Psi(R^-,\mathbf{R}_\perp)\) and the other involving \(\mathcal{P}(\mathbf{R}_\perp)\).

## Context

This work extends the debate on defining relativistic spatial densities of hadrons, which has three established camps: the Breit frame density approach (Sachs, 1962), the light front coordinate formalism (Burkardt, 2000; Miller, 2010; Freese, 2021), and the instant form localization approach (Fleming, 1974; Epelbaum, 2022; Panteleeva, 2022; Carlson, 2022). The paper clarifies that light front densities for spin-zero targets are "simple densities" independent of wave packet choice, resolving the need for localization procedures used in prior work, while showing that instant form densities are always "compound densities" with infinitely many contributions, as previously found by Li et al. (2022). This enables a rigorous foundation for interpreting spatial densities from upcoming Electron Ion Collider data.
