# The $^3S_1-^3D_1$ effective-range parameters of some realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials

> We consider the $P$- and $Q$-shape parameters in the eigenphase and `bar' phase shift representations for the two Reid potentials and the more recent Moscow potential. The values of these parameters were obtained from integrals of the zero-energy wave function in a similar manner to that for the effective range. Such expressions for the shape parameters yield more reliable numerical values of the shape parameter than those obtained by least-mean-square or polynomial fits. We show that the eigenphase representation is more meaningful than the `bar' phase shift representation and that the variation in the values of $P_α$ in the models may be of significance.

# The $^3S_1-^3D_1$ effective-range parameters of some realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials

## Key claim

The eigenphase representation yields more meaningful shape parameters than the `bar` phase shift representation for the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ coupled channel, and the variation in the eigenphase shape parameter $P_\alpha$ across different realistic potentials (Reid hard-core, Reid soft-core, and Moscow) may be physically significant, though no physical reason for this variation is yet identified.

## Method

The authors computed the effective-range parameters (scattering length $a_\alpha$, effective range $r_\alpha$, shape parameters $P_\alpha$ and $Q_\alpha$, and the mixing-parameter coefficients $q$ and $q_1$) for the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ channel using integral expressions derived from zero-energy wave functions, rather than by polynomial fitting of $k\cot\delta$. For the eigenphase parameters, $P_\alpha$ was obtained from integrals involving the zero-energy wave function and its energy derivative; $Q_\alpha$ was extracted by evaluating the function $g(k^2)$ at low energies and taking its derivative at $k^2=0$ using five- or six-point formulas. The `bar` phase shift parameters $\bar P_1$ and $\bar Q_1$ were then obtained from the eigenphase parameters using analytic relations derived from the Blatt-Biedenharn and Stapp-Ypsilantis-Metropolis formalisms.

## Key numbers

- Reid hard-core potential (Rhc1): $a_\alpha = 5.39713$ fm, $r_\alpha = 1.72493$ fm, $P_\alpha = -0.007303$, $\bar P_1 = -0.003779$, $Q_\alpha = 0.0502$, $\bar Q_1 = 0.0719$
- Reid soft-core potential (Rsc): $a_\alpha = 5.38989$ fm, $r_\alpha = 1.72225$ fm, $P_\alpha = -0.019085$, $\bar P_1 = -0.015437$, $Q_\alpha = 0.0483$, $\bar Q_1 = 0.0708$
- Moscow potential: $a_\alpha = 5.39615$ fm, $r_\alpha = 1.72266$ fm, $P_\alpha = -0.005972$, $\bar P_1 = -0.002119$, $Q_\alpha = 0.0516$, $\bar Q_1 = 0.0752$
- Mixing parameter coefficient $q$ ranges from 0.310356 fm$^2$ (Rhc3) to 0.326004 fm$^2$ (Moscow); $q_1$ ranges from -2.20509 fm$^4$ (Moscow) to -2.07289 fm$^4$ (Rhc3)
- The optimum energy interval for the derivative fit of $Q$ is approximately 1.1 MeV
- For the eigenphase case, $|\alpha^2 Q_\alpha / P_\alpha| < 0.5$, so the $Q_\alpha$ term may be neglected; for the `bar` phase shift case, $|\alpha^2 \bar Q_1 / \bar P_1|$ can be as large as 1.9 (Moscow potential)

## Context

This work extends the integral method for computing effective-range parameters from zero-energy wave functions, previously applied by van Dijk and Kermode (1992), to obtain both $P$ and $Q$ shape parameters for the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ coupled channel. It resolves ambiguities in the literature where different authors (Reid, 1968; Kermode et al., 1981; Wong, 1992) reported varying shape parameter values for the same potential, often without distinguishing between eigenphase and `bar` phase shift representations. The results enable more accurate determination of the deuteron radius from scattering parameters via expansions developed by Kermode et al. (1991), where the shape parameter $P$ enters the coefficient $a_3$ of the expansion. The finding that the `bar` effective-range function has infinite derivative at the deuteron bound state (Kermode et al., 1983) explains why $\bar Q_1$ can be significant even when $Q_\alpha$ is negligible.
