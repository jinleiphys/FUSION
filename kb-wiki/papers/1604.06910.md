> In a recent paper [Nuc. Phys. A {\bf 948}, (2016) 19] we have demonstrated the possible existence of Tsallis' q-Gamow states. Now, accelerators' experimental evidence for Tsallis' distributions has been ascertained only at very high energies. Here, instead, we develop a different set of q-Gamow states for which the associated q-Breit-Wigner distribution could easily be found at intermediate energies, for which accelerators are available at many locations. # q-Gamow States for intermediate energies

## Key claim

The paper develops a new set of q-Gamow states for which the associated q-Breit-Wigner distribution could be found at intermediate energies (not only at very high energies as previously demonstrated), but only for q-values in the immediate neighborhood of q=1, using perturbation theory around that value.

## Method

The authors start from previously established q-Gamow states and apply perturbation theory around q=1, keeping only first-order terms in (q-1). They derive the explicit form of the new q-Gamow states, compute their norm and mean energy value, and verify that these states satisfy the nonlinear q-Schroedinger equation of Nobre, Rego-Monteiro, and Tsallis. They then compute the q-Breit-Wigner distribution for these states.

## Key numbers

- The q-value observed in LHC experiments for transverse momentum distributions is of the order of 1.15, clearly distinct from the unity value of Gibbs-Boltzmann statistics.
- The measurement of the p_T distribution over a logarithmic range of fourteen decades demonstrates that q=1.15 fits the data over this large range.
- The norm of the new q-Gamow state is given by A^2(q,p) = (hbar / (2|Im(p)|)) * {1 + ((q-1)/4) * [1 + (Im(p)^2 - Re(p)^2) / Im(p)^2]}.
- The mean energy value in the limit q approaches 1 reduces to <H> = Re(p^2) / (2m).
- The q-Breit-Wigner distribution contains a correction factor X = (q-1) * (1/4 + (Im(p)^2 - Re(p)^2)/(4 Im(p)^2) + (p+p*)/(4(k-p)) - (p^2 + p*^2)/(2(k-p)^2)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous demonstration of the possible existence of Tsallis' q-Gamow states (Plastino, Rocca, Zamora, Nuclear Physics A 948, 2016) from very high energies to intermediate energies accessible at many accelerator facilities. The paper builds on the Nobre-Rego-Monteiro-Tsallis nonlinear q-generalization of Schroedinger's equation (Nobre, Rego-Monteiro, Tsallis, 2011) and on the empirical observation by Vignat and Plastino (Physica A 388, 2009) that detectors never exactly detect pure Gaussians but rather q-Gaussians. The new q-Gamow states and their associated q-Breit-Wigner distribution could be tested against existing accelerator data to confirm their existence.
