# Deformation-induced splitting of isoscalar E0 giant resonance: Skyrme random-phase-approximation analysis

> The deformation-induced splitting of isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) is systematically analyzed in a wide range of masses covering medium, rare-earth, actinide, and superheavy axial deformed nuclei. The study is performed within the fully self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase-approximation (QRPA) method based on the Skyrme functional. Two Skyrme forces, one with a large (SV-bas) and one with a small (SkP) nuclear incompressibility, are considered. The calculations confirm earlier results that, due to the deformation-induced E0-E2 coupling, the isoscalar E0 resonance attains a double-peak structure and significant energy upshift. Our results are compared with available analytic estimations. Unlike earlier studies, we get a smaller energy difference between the lower and upper peaks and thus a stronger E0-E2 coupling. This in turn results in more pumping of E0 strength into the lower peak and more pronounced splitting of ISGMR. We also discuss widths of the peaks and their negligible correlation with deformation.

## Key claim

The deformation-induced splitting of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) into a double-peak structure is confirmed across a wide mass range, but the energy difference between the lower and upper peaks is smaller than earlier analytic estimates predicted, indicating a stronger E0-E2 coupling than previously assumed.

## Method

The study employs a fully self-consistent quasiparticle random-phase-approximation (QRPA) method based on the Skyrme energy functional, implemented on a two-dimensional cylindrical mesh for axially deformed nuclei. Two Skyrme forces with different nuclear incompressibilities (SV-bas with K∞=234 MeV and SkP with K∞=202 MeV) are used, and pairing is treated at the BCS level with a contact δ-force interaction. The separable random-phase approximation (SRPA) model is also used to deliberately switch the E0-E2 coupling on and off for comparison.

## Key numbers

- SV-bas incompressibility: K∞ = 234 MeV; SkP incompressibility: K∞ = 202 MeV
- For 154Sm, the computed splitting ΔEM = 4.34 MeV agrees with the experimental value 4.12 MeV from TAMU data (Yo04)
- The deformation shift dEM varies between -0.4 and 1.0 MeV depending on the nucleus; for Cd isotopes it is negative, contradicting SS and VM estimates
- The splitting ΔEM lies in the range 2.5 – 5 MeV across all studied nuclei
- For 284Fl (β=0.170), the lower-peak strength fraction S1 = 28%; for 238U (β=0.275), S1 = 38%; for 242No (β=0.294), S1 = 43%
- The monopole strength summed in the energy interval 9-45 MeV exhausts the energy-weighted sum rule by 100-105% for SV-bas
- For δ=0.3, SS gives dEM = 7.2 A−1/3 MeV and ΔEM = 34.7 A−1/3 MeV; VM gives dEM = 6.2 A−1/3 MeV and ΔEM = 34.0 A−1/3 MeV
- The SS and VM estimates overshoot the QRPA splitting by 20-35%

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of ISGMR splitting in deformed nuclei from the 1980s, including the cranking model (Ab80), variational method (Ja83), and fluid-dynamical model in the simple scaling approximation (Ni85), as well as more recent self-consistent QRPA calculations for specific isotopic chains (Yosh13, Peru08, Peru11). The systematic survey across medium (Cd), rare-earth (Nd, Sm, Dy, Er, Yb), actinide (U, No), and superheavy (Fl) nuclei allows comparison with analytic estimates and reveals that earlier models overestimate the splitting. The results also address discrepancies between RCNP and TAMU experimental data for 154Sm and Cd isotopes, calling for additional measurements to resolve the systematic deviations.
