# Phase conversion dissipation in multicomponent compact stars

> We propose a mechanism for the damping of density oscillations in multicomponent compact stars. The mechanism is the periodic conversion between different phases, i.e., the movement of the interface between them, induced by pressure oscillations in the star. The damping grows nonlinearly with the amplitude of the oscillation. We study in detail the case of r-modes in a hybrid star with a sharp interface, and we find that this mechanism is powerful enough to saturate the r-mode at very low saturation amplitude, of order $10^{-10}$, and is therefore likely to be the dominant r-mode saturation mechanism in hybrid stars with a sharp interface.

## Key claim

The authors propose that periodic conversion between hadronic and quark phases at a sharp interface in hybrid stars provides a powerful damping mechanism for r-mode oscillations, saturating them at amplitudes as low as $\alpha_{\rm sat} \lesssim 10^{-10}$, but this mechanism is only dominant in hybrid stars with a sharp interface and the damping grows nonlinearly with oscillation amplitude, being vanishingly small at infinitesimal amplitude.

## Method

The authors construct a schematic piston-cylinder model with two incompressible phases in a gravitational field, where periodic pressure oscillations cause the phase boundary to move as one phase converts into the other at a finite rate, producing a phase lag that leads to dissipation via net $p\,dV$ work. They then apply this model to r-modes in a hybrid star with a sharp interface between a quark matter core and hadronic mantle, using the Olinto (1986) steady-state approximation for the conversion front speed, where flavor-changing weak interactions and strangeness diffusion determine the boundary velocity. The dissipation is calculated by integrating over the stellar surface the energy dissipated per cycle in radially oriented cylinders straddling the phase boundary.

## Key numbers

- The r-mode saturation amplitude from phase conversion dissipation can be as low as $\alpha_{\rm sat} \lesssim 10^{-10}$ for conditions present in observed pulsars
- The r-mode frequency is $\omega = \frac{2}{3}\Omega$, where $\Omega$ is the rotational frequency of the star
- The energy density fluctuation of an m=2 r-mode to leading order in $\Omega$ is $\frac{\delta \ep}{\bar \ep} = \sqrt{\frac{8}{189}} \alpha A R^2 \Omega^2 \left(\frac{r}{R}\right)^3 \text{Re}[Y_3^2(\theta,\phi)e^{i\omega t}]$
- The maximum velocity of the ideal boundary is $v_{\rm ib}^{\rm max} = \frac{C_{\rm b}\alpha\omega}{g_{\rm b}}|\sin^2\theta\cos\theta|$, where $C_{\rm b} = \sqrt{\frac{105}{756\pi}}\Omega^2 \frac{\bar R_{\rm b}^3}{R}$
- The difference in dissipation between assuming harmonic pressure oscillation in the low-density versus high-density phase is $\Delta W/W \simeq \Delta \ep/\ep_{\rm L}$, where $\Delta \ep$ is the energy density discontinuity at the interface

## Context

This work extends the study of bulk viscosity damping in compact stars (Sawyer 1989, Haensel 1992, Madsen 1992, Alford 2006) by proposing a new dissipation mechanism from phase boundary motion, which is distinct from Ekman layer damping at fluid-solid boundaries (Lindblom 2000). The mechanism is likely to be the dominant r-mode saturation mechanism in hybrid stars with a sharp interface, pre-empting other proposed mechanisms such as suprathermal bulk viscosity (Alford 2011) which becomes relevant only at much higher amplitudes. The results enable predictions for gravitational wave signatures from r-modes in hybrid stars and provide a potential observational test for the existence of sharp phase transitions in neutron star interiors.
