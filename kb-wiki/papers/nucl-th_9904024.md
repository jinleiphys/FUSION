# Dilepton Spectra from Decays of Light Unflavored Mesons

> The invariant mass spectrum of the $e^{+}e^{-}$ and $μ^{+}μ^{-}$ pairs from decays of light unflavored mesons with masses below the $ϕ(1020)$-meson mass to final states containing along with a dilepton pair one photon, one meson, and two mesons are calculated within the framework of the effective meson theory. The results can be used for simulations of the dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions and for experimental searches of dilepton meson decays.

## Key claim

The invariant mass spectra of electron-positron and muon-antimuon pairs from decays of light unflavored mesons below the phi(1020) mass are calculated within effective meson theory, with the caveat that the branching ratios for dilepton decay modes are only known in a few cases, so the calculations rely on Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model transition form factors constrained by quark counting rules and, where experimental data are unavailable, SU(3) symmetry.

## Method

The authors computed dilepton decay rates for vector mesons (rho, omega, phi), pseudoscalar mesons (pi, eta, eta-prime), and scalar mesons (f0(980), a0(980)) by relating each decay M -> M' l+l- to the corresponding radiative decay M -> M' gamma* using the relation dGamma(M -> M' l+l-) = dGamma(M -> M' gamma*) * M * Gamma(gamma* -> l+l-) * dM^2/(pi M^4). Vertex couplings were fixed from measured strong and radiative decay widths, and transition form factors were constructed within the VMD model with asymptotic behavior constrained by quark counting rules, using monopole or multi-pole parametrizations depending on the meson structure.

## Key numbers

- The SU(3) predictions for vector meson coupling constants are g_rho = 5.03, g_omega = 17.1, and g_phi = -12.9, extracted from e+e- decay widths of rho, omega, and phi mesons
- The monopole pole masses for pseudoscalar transition form factors are Lambda_P = 0.75 +/- 0.03 GeV (pi0), 0.77 +/- 0.04 GeV (eta), and 0.81 +/- 0.04 GeV (eta-prime)
- The experimental branching ratio B(eta-prime -> gamma mu+ mu-) = (1.04 +/- 0.26) x 10^-4 is reproduced with c_rho = 0.9 and c_omega = 0.1, giving B(eta-prime -> gamma mu+ mu-) = 0.90 x 10^-4
- The coupling constant f_omega_gamma_pi = 2.3 GeV^-1 is obtained from the experimental omega -> pi0 gamma width
- The eta-prime mixing angle theta = -19.5 degrees from SU(3) symmetry is close to the experimental value theta_exp = -15.5 +/- 1.3 degrees

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions by providing a systematic calculation of background contributions from light unflavored meson decays, addressing the "DLS puzzle" where transport calculations underestimate dilepton yields by a factor of 2 to 3 compared to DLS Collaboration data at BEVALAC energies around 1 A*GeV. The results enable more precise background subtraction for experiments such as HADES at GSI and CERES/HELIOS-3 at CERN SPS, and complement earlier work by Rapp (1997), Peters (1998), and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model studies by Faessler, which investigated in-medium modifications of meson properties.
