# Gluon Distribution Functions for Very Large Nuclei at Small Transverse Momentum

> We show that the gluon distribution function for very large nuclei may be computed for small transverse momentum as correlation functions of an ultraviolet finite two dimensional Euclidean field theory. This computation is valid to all orders in the density of partons per unit area, but to lowest order in $α_s$. The gluon distribution function is proportional to $1/x$, and the effect of the finite density of partons is to modify the dependence on transverse momentum for small transverse momentum.

# Gluon Distribution Functions for Very Large Nuclei at Small Transverse Momentum

## Key claim

The gluon distribution function for very large nuclei at small transverse momentum can be computed as correlation functions of an ultraviolet finite two dimensional Euclidean field theory, valid to all orders in the density of partons per unit area but only to lowest order in $\alpha_s$. The authors explicitly note that they do not address the small $x$ enhancement (Lipatov enhancement) that may modify the $1/x$ dependence, and that if such enhancement occurs, the weak coupling expansion will only be formal since $\alpha_s(\mu^2) \ln(1/x)$ will not be small.

## Method

The authors treat valence quarks in a large nucleus as static, classical sources of color charge localized along the light cone and uniformly distributed in transverse space, with Gaussian fluctuations around zero color charge. They solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in light cone gauge for a source that is a delta function in $x^-$, obtaining a field configuration that is a gauge transform of the vacuum for a two dimensional gauge theory. The gluon distribution function is then obtained by integrating over all charge configurations, which is equivalent to integrating over unitary matrices with a weight given by a fourth-order derivative action in two Euclidean dimensions.

## Key numbers

- The density of charge squared fluctuations per unit area is $\mu^2 = 1.1 A^{1/3} \text{ fm}^{-2}$; the strong coupling is small when $\mu^2 \gg \Lambda^2_{QCD}$.
- The condition for treating valence quarks as delta functions of charge along the light cone is $x \ll A^{-1/3}$.
- The perturbative result for the gluon distribution function per unit area is ${1 \over {\pi R^2}} {{dN} \over {dxd^2q_t}} = {{\alpha_s \mu^2(N_c^2-1)} \over \pi^2} {1 \over {xq_t^2}}$, valid for $\alpha_s \mu^2 \ll q_t^2 \ll \mu^2$.
- The expansion parameter for the two dimensional theory is $\alpha_s^2 \mu^2/k_t^2$; the weak coupling region is $k_t^2 \gg \alpha_s^2 \mu^2$.
- The gluon distribution function has the general form ${1 \over {\pi R^2}} {{dN} \over {dx d^2k_t}} = {{(N_c^2-1)} \over \pi^2} {1 \over x}~ {1 \over \alpha_s}H(k_t^2/\alpha_s^2 \mu^2)$, where $H(y) = 1/y$ for $1 \ll y \ll 1/\alpha_s^2$.

## Context

This paper extends the authors' previous work (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1993) where they computed the gluon distribution function perturbatively for $\alpha_s^2 \mu^2 \ll q_t^2$. Here they relax that condition and set up the computation for the soft region $q_t^2 \ll \mu^2$, summing to all orders in $\alpha_s^2 \mu^2$. The work draws on light cone quantization techniques from Soper (1977) and Mueller (1986), and the authors note the parallel to finite temperature field theory where a non-perturbative magnetic screening length scale $\lambda \sim 1/\alpha_s T$ exists. The results enable lattice Monte Carlo simulations of the two dimensional Euclidean theory to compute the infrared behavior of gluon distribution functions for large nuclei.
