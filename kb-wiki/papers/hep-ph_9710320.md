# Some Phenomenological Properties of the Chiral Transition in QCD

> On the basis of the NJL model as an effective theory of QCD and analogies with condensed matter physics, we extract simple physical pictures of the chiral phase transition at finite temperature $T$ and/or chemical potential $μ$ and predict interesting phenomena associated with the phase transition which may be seen in relativistic heavy-ion collisions to be performed in RHIC or LHC. We indicate that the macroscopic equation describing non-equilibrium phenomena of the chiral transition like DCC might be given by a diffusion-like equation rather than a wave equation especially when the baryon density is present.

## Key claim

Based on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and analogies with condensed matter physics, the macroscopic equation describing non-equilibrium phenomena of the chiral transition like disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) might be given by a diffusion-like equation rather than a wave equation, especially when baryon density is present.

## Method

The author uses the generalized SU(3) NJL model with an anomaly (determinantal) term to compute the thermodynamic potential as a function of the quark condensates for u,d and s quarks at finite temperature T and chemical potential μ. The temperature dependence of hadron masses, the quark-number susceptibility, and the nature of soft modes in the scalar and pseudoscalar channels are examined within this effective theory, with comparisons drawn to lattice QCD results and condensed matter analogies.

## Key numbers

- For m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV and m_s = 135 MeV, the thermodynamic potential in the u-d sector has an asymmetric double well, while in the strangeness sector it has only a single minimum for all temperatures.
- The ratio of the quark-number susceptibility in the high-T and low-T phases reads χ_q(T_c+ε,0)/χ_q(T_c-ε,0) = 4 to 5 with light two flavors, where ε is a small number (e.g., 0.03 T_c).
- The enhancement of χ_q for a free quark gas as M(T) decreases to zero is merely about 1.6, insufficient to explain the lattice result.
- At low temperatures below about 50 MeV, the phase transition is of strong first order in the density direction.
- The sigma meson mass decreases as T is raised toward T_c, while m_π remains constant as long as T < T_c; the sigma meson may appear as a sharp resonance with mass m_σ >~ 2m_π in the mass region 300 to 400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the review of the NJL model by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994) and builds on lattice QCD results from Kanaya (1997) and others. The paper predicts that DCC phenomena may be relevant only to the u,d sector, not the strangeness sector, and suggests that vector correlations and collective vector modes disappear in the chirally restored phase. The analogy with superconductors and the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau equation, following Abraham and Tsuneto (1979), opens the question of what macroscopic equation is suitable for describing DCC in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
