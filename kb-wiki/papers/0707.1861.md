# Global analysis of proton elastic form factor data with two-photon exchange corrections

> We use the world's data on elastic electron--proton scattering and calculations of two-photon exchange effects to extract corrected values of the proton's electric and magnetic form factors over the full Q^2 range of the existing data. Our analysis combines the corrected Rosenbluth cross section and polarization transfer data, and is the first extraction of G_Ep and G_Mp including explicit two-photon exchange corrections and their associated uncertainties. In addition, we examine the angular dependence of the corrected cross sections, and discuss the possible nonlinearities of the cross section as a function of epsilon.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first global extraction of the proton's electric and magnetic form factors that explicitly includes two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections and their associated uncertainties, applied directly to the world's elastic electron-proton scattering data. The authors find that TPE corrections can largely resolve the long-standing discrepancy between form factor ratios extracted via Rosenbluth separation and polarization transfer techniques, but note that residual uncertainties from higher-mass intermediate states require a phenomenological additional correction with 100% uncertainty.

## Method

The authors applied TPE corrections calculated by Blunden et al. (2005) to the world's elastic electron-proton cross section data, supplemented by an additional linear-in-epsilon correction for Q^2 > 1 GeV^2 to account for higher-mass intermediate states (nucleon resonances and generalized parton distribution contributions). They performed a global fit to 569 cross section data points and 54 polarization transfer measurements using a rational function parameterization with 8 fit parameters per form factor, extracting normalization factors for each independent cross section data set and yielding a reduced chi-squared of 0.770.

## Key numbers

- The global fit included 569 cross section data points and 54 polarization transfer data points, yielding a reduced chi-squared of 0.770
- The additional TPE correction (Eq. 13) decreases the epsilon=0 cross section by 1% at Q^2 = 2.2 GeV^2, by 2% at Q^2 = 4.8 GeV^2, and by 3% at Q^2 = 10.6 GeV^2
- The Delta(1232) resonance contribution to TPE was found to be approximately 1/4 of the nucleon elastic contribution at low and intermediate Q^2, modifying the cross section by between -1% and 2%
- The parameterization is valid up to at least Q^2 = 6 GeV^2 for G_E and Q^2 = 30 GeV^2 for G_M
- TPE corrections to polarization transfer measurements were less than 2% for all existing data except the largest Q^2 point, where corrections were of order 5%
- The ratio of e+ p to e- p cross sections shows predicted enhancements up to approximately 10% at backward angles and moderate Q^2

## Context

This work extends the global form factor analysis of Arrington (2004) by incorporating explicit TPE corrections from Blunden et al. (2005) and including additional low-Q^2 cross section data, more recent cross section results from Christy (2004) and Qattan (2005), and the full set of polarization transfer measurements from Punjabi (2005), Gayou (2001, 2002), and others. The analysis resolves the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer extractions of the form factor ratio that had been a puzzle since the Jefferson Lab polarization measurements, and enables more accurate determinations of proton form factors for use in other nuclear physics measurements. The results also motivate future experiments at VEPP-3 and Jefferson Lab to measure e+ p versus e- p scattering ratios for direct tests of TPE contributions.
