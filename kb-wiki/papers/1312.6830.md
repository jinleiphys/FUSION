# Multi-dimensional constraint relativistic mean field model and applications in actinide and transfermium nuclei

> In this contribution we present some results of potential energy surfaces of actinide and transfermium nuclei from multi-dimensional constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) models. Recently we developed multi-dimensional constrained covariant density functional theories (MDC-CDFT) in which all shape degrees of freedom $β_{λμ}$ with even $μ$ are allowed and the functional can be one of the following four forms: the meson exchange or point-coupling nucleon interactions combined with the non-linear or density-dependent couplings. In MDC-RMF models, the pairing correlations are treated with the BCS method. With MDC-RMF models, the potential energy surfaces of even-even actinide nuclei were investigated and the effect of triaxiality on the fission barriers in these nuclei was discussed. The non-axial reflection-asymmetric $β_{32}$ shape in some transfermium nuclei with $N=150$, namely $^{246}$Cm, $^{248}$Cf, $^{250}$Fm, and $^{252}$No were also studied.

## Key claim

The multi-dimensional constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) model, which allows all shape degrees of freedom βλμ with even μ, reveals that triaxial deformation lowers the outer fission barrier of actinide nuclei by about 1 MeV, a feature that can only be found when axial and reflection symmetries are simultaneously broken; the paper presents this as a new finding for the specific case of 240Pu.

## Method

The authors performed one-, two-, and three-dimensional constrained self-consistent calculations using the MDC-RMF model with the PC-PK1 parameter set for the actinide nucleus 240Pu, and with the DD-PC1 parameter set for the N=150 isotones 246Cm, 248Cf, 250Fm, and 252No. The Dirac equation was solved in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator (ADHO) basis, and pairing correlations were treated with the BCS method. Potential energy surfaces (PES) were obtained by constrained calculations where the nucleus was assumed symmetric under the V4 group, allowing expansion of potentials and densities in cos(2nφ) terms.

## Key numbers

* For 240Pu, the inner barrier is located at β20 ≈ 0.65 and β22 ≈ 0.06; the outer barrier is located at β20 ≈ 1.21, β22 ≈ 0.02, and β30 ≈ 0.37.
* Triaxial deformation reduces the inner barrier height of 240Pu by more than 2 MeV compared to the axially symmetric calculation.
* The outer barrier of 240Pu is lowered by about 1 MeV when triaxial deformation is allowed.
* For even-even actinide nuclei systematically studied, triaxial deformation lowers the outer barriers by about 0.5 to 1 MeV (about 10 to 20% of the barrier height).
* For 248Cf, the potential energy curve as a function of β32 shows a pocket with depth more than 0.3 MeV.
* For 248Cf and 250Fm, the axial-quadrupole deformation is β20 ≈ 0.3.

## Context

This work extends the multi-dimensional constrained covariant density functional theories (MDC-CDFT) developed by Lu et al. (2012, 2013) to study fission barriers in actinides and non-axial octupole shapes in transfermium nuclei. The finding that triaxiality lowers the outer fission barrier goes beyond earlier studies such as Pashkevich (1969), Moeller (1970), Girod (1983), Rutz (1995), Abusara (2010), and Prassa (2012, 2013), which had emphasized the role of reflection asymmetry for the outer barrier. The results on β32 correlations in N=150 isotones are consistent with the experimental observation of low-energy 2- bands in 248Cf reported by Robinson (2008) and the RASM calculations by Chen (2008), and enable further microscopic studies of fission dynamics and exotic shapes in heavy and superheavy nuclei.
