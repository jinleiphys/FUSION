# Solution of the Bethe-Goldstone Equation without Partial Wave Decomposition

> We present a method for solving the nucleon-nucleon scattering equation without the use of a partial wave expansion of the scattering amplitude. After verifying the accuracy of the numerical solutions, we proceed to apply the method to the in-medium scattering equation (the Bethe-Goldstone equation) in three dimensions. A focal point is a study of Pauli blocking effects calculated in the (angle-dependent) three-dimensional formalism as compared to the usual spherical approximation. We discuss our results and their implications.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for solving the in-medium Bethe-Goldstone equation without partial wave decomposition, enabling exact treatment of the Pauli operator rather than the usual spherical (angle-averaged) approximation. The authors claim that while the three-dimensional solution requires initial effort, the inclusion of the exact Pauli operator is then "absolutely straightforward," and they aim to settle the question of whether non-spherical components in the Pauli operator are important.

## Method

The authors solve the Thompson equation (a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) in a helicity basis without partial wave expansion, using a one-boson-exchange potential (Bonn B) as input. They remove the azimuthal degree of freedom through integration, obtaining a set of six coupled Fredholm integral equations of the second kind for each isospin, which they solve using Nystrom's method or matrix inversion. For the in-medium case, they incorporate the Pauli operator into the three-dimensional formalism and compare results using the exact (angle-dependent) Pauli operator versus the spherical (angle-averaged) approximation at a Fermi momentum of 1.4 fm⁻¹, corresponding to a density of 0.185 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum used: \(k_F = 1.4 \text{ fm}^{-1}\), corresponding to density \(\rho = 0.185 \text{ fm}^{-3}\) (close to normal nuclear matter density)
- Free-space on-shell matrix elements verified at laboratory energies of 50, 100, 200, and 300 MeV, with most errors less than approximately 0.1% compared to partial wave solutions
- The worst observed discrepancy was in the \(^1P_1\) partial wave at 300 MeV: \(-1.33 \times 10^{-7} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) versus \(-1.43 \times 10^{-7} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) (approximately 7% error)
- The \(^3S_1\) real part at 300 MeV showed approximately 3% error
- The \(\phi\)-integrated Bonn B potentials require momentum of at least 4000 MeV to approach zero

## Context

This work extends prior three-dimensional free-space scattering calculations (Refs. RK, FEG, CPW, Veerasamy (2012)) to the in-medium case, which the authors state had not been reported previously. It directly addresses the question of non-spherical components in the Pauli operator, which had been studied earlier within partial wave formalisms by Sam (2000) and others, where inclusion of such components required handling couplings between states of different total angular momentum \(J\) and dependence on the magnetic quantum number \(M\). The method enables future applications to isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter by straightforward modification of the Pauli operator to accommodate two different Fermi momenta.
