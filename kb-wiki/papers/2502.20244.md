# Generative adversarial neural networks for simulating neutrino interactions

> We propose a new approach to simulate neutrino scattering events as an alternative to the standard Monte Carlo generator approach. Generative adversarial neural network (GAN) models are developed to simulate charged current neutrino-carbon collisions in the few-GeV energy range. We consider a simplified framework to generate muon kinematic variables, specifically its energy and scattering angle. GAN models are trained on simulation data from \nuwro{} Monte Carlo event generator. Two GAN models have been obtained: one simulating quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scatterings and another simulating all interactions at given neutrino energy. The models work for neutrino energy ranging from 300 MeV to 10 GeV. The performance of both models has been assessed using two statistical metrics. It is shown that both GAN models successfully reproduce the distribution of muon kinematics.

## Key claim

The authors propose generative adversarial neural networks (GANs) as an alternative to standard Monte Carlo event generators for simulating neutrino scattering events, but only in a simplified framework that generates muon kinematic variables (energy and scattering angle) rather than full final-state particle information, and only for charged-current neutrino-carbon collisions in the few-GeV energy range.

## Method

The authors trained conditional GAN models on simulation data from the NuWro Monte Carlo event generator for muon neutrino charged-current interactions on carbon targets. Two separate GAN models were developed: one for quasielastic scattering only and another for inclusive scattering (all interaction modes at a given neutrino energy). The models take neutrino energy as input and generate the muon energy and scattering angle as output, using a latent vector drawn from a standard normal distribution. The GAN architectures used fully connected dense layers with normalization, skip connections, and dropout, optimized with the AdamW algorithm using cross-entropy loss functions. Model performance was assessed using the mean absolute value of pulls (MAP) and the Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) metrics.

## Key numbers

* Neutrino energy range: 300 MeV to 10 GeV
* Training dataset size: 3.88 million events for each model (QE and INC), with approximately 40,000 events per 100 MeV energy interval
* For the QE model, EMD values ranged from 0.11 (for the full uniform energy distribution) to 0.15 (for 500 MeV and 7 GeV fixed energies)
* For the QE model, MAP values ranged from 0.84 (for the full uniform energy distribution, without tails) to 1.17 (for 500 MeV, without tails)
* The expected MAP value for two identical NuWro samples is approximately 0.80 (sqrt(2/pi))
* The EMD computed for two NuWro samples is approximately 0.03 to 0.04
* Minibatch sizes: B = 10,000 samples for QE analysis and B = 1,000 samples for INC analysis
* Learning rates: 10^-4 for discriminators and 10^-5 for generators

## Context

This work extends the application of generative adversarial networks to neutrino-nucleus scattering simulations, following prior efforts to adapt GANs for parton showers (deOliveira 2017, Monk 2018), hadronization (Ghosh 2022, Ilten 2022, Chan 2023), and electron-proton collisions (Alanazi 2020). The approach is presented as a potential alternative to standard Monte Carlo generators such as NEUT (Hayato 2021), GENIE (Andreopoulos 2009), GiBUU (Mosel 2018), NuWro (Golan 2012), and Achilles (Isaacson 2022). The simplified framework generating only muon kinematics represents a first step toward developing GAN-based models that could eventually learn directly from experimental cross-section data and adapt predictions accordingly, potentially addressing limitations in theoretical models of nuclear effects.
