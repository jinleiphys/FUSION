# Coulomb Effects on Electromagnetic Pair Production in Ultrarelativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We discuss the implications of the eikonal amplitude on the pair production probability in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion transits. In this context the Weizsäcker-Williams method is shown to be exact in the ultrarelativistic limit, irrespective of the produced particles' mass. A new equivalent single-photon distribution is derived which correctly accounts for the Coulomb distortions. As an immediate application, consequences for unitarity violation in photo-dissociation processes in peripheral heavy-ion encounters are discussed.

## Key claim

The Weizsacker-Williams method is shown to be exact in the ultrarelativistic limit for pair production, irrespective of the produced particles' mass, but only when the correct Coulomb boundary conditions are accounted for via a gauge-transformed potential; the naive Taylor expansion of the Fourier-transformed T matrix does not commute with Fourier transformation, so first-order perturbation theory fails to capture the correct small-coupling limit.

## Method

The authors derive the exact eikonal S-operator for electron scattering at an ultrarelativistic pointlike charge using a gauge transformation that converts Coulomb boundary conditions into a modified short-range potential, yielding a delta-function interaction on the light front. They then apply crossing symmetry to obtain the pair production amplitude in the field of two colliding ultrarelativistic ions, and recast the result into the Weizsacker-Williams equivalent-photon formalism using the modified potential. The impact-parameter-dependent single-photon distribution is computed analytically for pointlike charges and numerically for extended nuclei with a Gaussian form factor, comparing the retarded Coulomb potential to the modified potential that accounts for Coulomb distortions.

## Key numbers

- The condition for validity of the ultrarelativistic limit is \(|\vec{k}_\perp| \gg \omega/\gamma\) (Equation applcond).
- The equivalent single-photon distribution for pointlike charges is \(n(\omega) = \frac{2Z^2 \alpha}{\pi\omega} \ln\left(\frac{m\gamma}{\omega}\right)\), with integration limits \(\omega/\gamma\) to \(m\).
- For pointlike charges, the photon distribution function for the modified potential is \(n(\omega,b) = \frac{Z^2\alpha}{\pi^2}\frac{\omega}{\gamma^2}\left[\frac{K_{1+iZ\alpha}(\omega b/\gamma)}{\Gamma(1-iZ\alpha)}\right]^2\), while for the retarded Coulomb potential it is \(n(\omega,b) = \frac{Z^2\alpha}{\pi^2}\frac{\omega}{\gamma^2}\left[K_1\left(\frac{\omega b}{\gamma}\right)\right]^2\).
- The Gaussian form factor for lead uses \(Q_0 = 60\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- At small transverse distances, the modified potential gives a deviation of up to \(70\%\) from the Coulomb potential for small photon energies; at large distances, the asymptotic deviation is approximately \(40\%\), independent of photon energy.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies (\(\gamma \approx 3000\)), the dissociation probability violates unitarity at impact parameters up to 25 fm.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of pair production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions by Eichmann (1998) and Segev (1997) to include exact Coulomb boundary conditions via an eikonal amplitude, showing that the Weizsacker-Williams method becomes exact in the ultrarelativistic limit. It contradicts the naive expectation that first-order perturbation theory (the two-photon result of Bottcher (1997)) is valid for all momentum transfers, demonstrating instead that the cross section reduces to the second-order perturbative result only after integration over the impact parameter plane. The results enable improved calculations of electromagnetic dissociation and pair production in peripheral collisions at LHC energies, and suggest that combining the modified photon distribution with multi-phonon excitations (Bertulani (1997), Norbury (1997)) may resolve unitarity violations in photodissociation processes.
