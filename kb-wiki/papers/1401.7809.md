# The Bound-State Beta Decay of the Neutron Revisited

> This paper is addressed to the analysis of the set of observables of the bound-state beta-decay, which can be used for the experimental investigation of contributions of i) interactions beyond the Standard Model (SM) and of ii) the left-handed polarisation state of antineutrinos. For this aim we calculate the branching ratio, probabilities and angular distributions of probabilities of hydrogen in the hyperfine states and of the proton-electron pair in different spinorial states, induced by left-handed and right-handed hadronic and leptonic currents. The branching ratio of the bound-state beta-decay is calculated by taking into account radiative corrections. We show that the probabilities of the bound-state beta-decay can be good observables for experimental investigations of contributions of interactions beyond the SM, whereas the angular distributions of probabilities are good observables for experimental searches of contributions of the left-handed polarisation state of antineutrinos.

## Key claim

The branching ratio and angular distributions of the bound-state beta-decay of the neutron into hydrogen and an antineutrino can serve as observables for detecting interactions beyond the Standard Model and for searching for left-handed polarisation states of antineutrinos, but only if the antineutrino is massive enough to allow such a polarisation state; the probabilities are sensitive to scalar and tensor couplings beyond the Standard Model, while the angular distributions are sensitive to right-handed leptonic currents.

## Method

The authors calculated the amplitude of the bound-state beta-decay \(n \to \text{H} + \bar{\nu}_e\) starting from the continuum-state amplitude including radiative corrections, then took the non-relativistic limit for the electron in the hydrogen bound state. They used Dirac wave functions for antineutrinos in both right-handed and left-handed polarisation states, expanded the amplitude to leading order in the fine-structure constant, and derived branching ratios and angular distributions for hydrogen in hyperfine states and for proton-electron spinorial states, keeping terms up to second order in scalar and tensor coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Standard Model branching ratio of bound-state beta-decay: \(R_{\rm SM} = 3.905 \times 10^{-6}\)
- Antineutrino energy in the bound-state decay: \(E = 0.782\ \text{MeV}\)
- Phase-space factor of neutron beta-decay: \(f_n = 1.755\)
- Riemann zeta function value used: \(\zeta(3) = 1.202\)
- Fine-structure constant: \(\alpha = 1/137.036\)
- Averaged electron mass over energy ratio: \(\langle m_e/E_e\rangle_{\rm SM} = 0.6556\)
- Constant from electroweak boson exchanges and QCD corrections: \(C_{WZ} = 10.249\)

## Context

This work extends the earlier revision of bound-state beta-decay by Faber et al. (2009) by incorporating the updated axial coupling constant \(\lambda = -1.2750(9)\) and by expanding angular distributions to second order in scalar and tensor couplings. The formalism builds on the effective weak interaction framework of Herczeg (2001) and the treatment of beyond-Standard-Model couplings by Ivanov et al. (2012). The results enable experimental searches for right-handed currents and left-handed antineutrino polarisation states, which would be signatures of neutrino mass or interactions beyond the Standard Model.
