# Exploring Hyperons and Hypernuclei with Lattice QCD

> In this work we outline a program for lattice QCD that would provide a first step toward understanding the strong and weak interactions of strange baryons. The study of hypernuclear physics has provided a significant amount of information regarding the structure and weak decays of light nuclei containing one or two Lambda's, and Sigma's. From a theoretical standpoint, little is known about the hyperon-nucleon interaction, which is required input for systematic calculations of hypernuclear structure. Furthermore, the long-standing discrepancies in the P-wave amplitudes for nonleptonic hyperon decays remain to be understood, and their resolution is central to a better understanding of the weak decays of hypernuclei. We present a framework that utilizes Luscher's finite-volume techniques in lattice QCD to extract the scattering length and effective range for Lambda-N scattering in both QCD and partially-quenched QCD. The effective theory describing the nonleptonic decays of hyperons using isospin symmetry alone, appropriate for lattice calculations, is constructed.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD, combined with Luscher's finite-volume techniques and effective field theory, can provide first-principles determinations of the Lambda-N scattering length and effective range that are currently poorly constrained by experiment, but the power-counting scheme may require next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) calculations or resummation via the Schrodinger equation to achieve convergence, and partial-quenching may seriously complicate the extraction of effective ranges.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory (EFT) for Lambda-N scattering in Weinberg power-counting, computing the scattering length and effective range to next-to-leading order (NLO) in both QCD and partially-quenched QCD (PQQCD). They use Luscher's finite-volume formalism to relate the energy levels of the two-particle system in a periodic box to the continuum scattering parameters, and also construct the effective theory for nonleptonic hyperon decays using isospin symmetry alone, appropriate for lattice calculations.

## Context

This work extends the application of Luscher's finite-volume techniques (Luscher, 1986, 1990) from the nucleon-nucleon sector to the hyperon-nucleon sector, motivated by the large experimental uncertainties in Lambda-N scattering data compiled at the Nijmegen NN-Online website and the poor theoretical understanding of P-wave amplitudes in nonleptonic hyperon decays. It builds on prior EFT work for hyperon-nucleon scattering (Rob, 2003) and partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory for single-nucleon observables (Chen, 2001; Beane, 2002) and NN interactions (Beane, 2002). The framework enables future lattice QCD calculations to provide rigorous first-principles determinations of Lambda-N scattering parameters, which are required input for systematic hypernuclear structure calculations, and to address the long-standing discrepancies in P-wave amplitudes for nonleptonic hyperon decays that have eluded theoretical understanding for decades.
