# Isotopic trends of quasifission and fusion-fission in the reactions $^{48}$Ca+$^{239,244}$Pu

> Background: Quasifission and fusion-fission are primary mechanisms to prevent the production of superheavy elements. The recent experimental measurements reveal that the fusion-evaporation cross section in the $3n$ reaction channel of $^{48}$Ca+$^{239}$Pu is 50 times lower than using $^{244}$Pu as target nucleus. However, the precise mechanisms of this remarkable isotopic dependence are not well understood. Purpose: To understand the experimental observation of the rapid decrease of stability of superheavy nuclei as the neutron number decreases, the theoretical studies of quasifission and fusion-fission in connection with experimental production for $Z$=114 flerovium isotopes are required to investigate the possible differences in reaction mechanisms induced by these two targets. Methods: We propose an approach called TDHF+HIVAP to take into account both the evolution of dinuclear system and the deexcitation of compound nucleus, which combines the microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method for the fusion and quasifission dynamics with the statistical evaporation model HIVAP for fusion-fission dynamics. Results: ......The quantum shell effect displays a crucial role in both the quasifission and the fusion-fission processes. The quasifission is considerably reduced and the survival probability is enhanced around one order of magnitude in the reaction using $^{244}$Pu target as compared to the $^{239}$Pu case. Conclusions: The studies by using TDHF+HIVAP method well account for the experimental observations and the present method clearly shows its applicability in the reaction mechanisms of quasifission and fusion-fission dynamics. The experimental and theoretical results encourage the use of neutron-rich targets for the production of new superheavy elements.

## Key claim

The TDHF+HIVAP approach accounts for the experimental observation that the fusion-evaporation cross section in the 3n channel of ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁹Pu is about 50 times lower than using ²⁴⁴Pu as target, with the quantum shell effect playing a crucial role in both quasifission and fusion-fission processes and the survival probability enhanced by roughly one order of magnitude for the neutron-rich target.

## Method

The authors combine the microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method with the statistical evaporation model HIVAP to study both the evolution of the dinuclear system and the deexcitation of the compound nucleus. TDHF calculations using the Skyrme SLy5 force with all time-odd terms are performed on a three-dimensional coordinate grid without symmetry restrictions to simulate fusion and quasifission dynamics for ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁹,²⁴⁴Pu at various energies, impact parameters, and target orientations. The HIVAP code then calculates the survival probability of the compound nucleus against fusion-fission by considering neutron evaporation and fission widths.

## Key numbers

- The fusion-evaporation cross section in the 3n reaction channel of ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁹Pu is 50 times lower than using ²⁴⁴Pu as target nucleus (experimental result from Utyonkov 2015).
- The TDHF capture barrier for tip collision is 177 MeV with ²³⁹Pu target and 181 MeV for ²⁴⁴Pu; side collision gives a barrier of 199 MeV for both targets.
- The critical maximum impact parameter for fusion in side collisions is 1.7 fm for ²³⁹Pu and 2.3 fm for ²⁴⁴Pu, giving a fusion window about 0.6 fm larger for the neutron-rich target.
- The ratio of fusion cross section between ²⁴⁴Pu and ²³⁹Pu side collisions is roughly 1.8 using the sharp cut-off formula.
- The survival probability W_sur(4n) for ²⁹²Fl at excitation energy 41.7 MeV is 8.21×10⁻⁸, while W_sur(3n) for ²⁸⁷Fl at 37.7 MeV is 5.5×10⁻⁹, giving a ratio of 14.9.
- The approximate ratio of evaporation residue cross sections between the two reactions is roughly 27, close to the experimental ratio.
- For tip collisions of ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁹Pu at 204.02 MeV and b=2.5 fm, the contact time is 8.17 zs with roughly 30 nucleons transferred, producing heavy fragments around ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The difference in fission barrier ΔB_f between ²⁹²Fl and ²⁸⁷Fl is predicted by several models to be 0.82–1.98 MeV, averaging at 1.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends microscopic TDHF studies of fusion and quasifission dynamics previously applied to reactions such as ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U (Oberacker 2014) and ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁹Bk (Umar 2016), and addresses the experimental observation from Utyonkov (2015) that neutron-rich targets enhance superheavy element production. The combined TDHF+HIVAP approach enables direct comparison with experimental mass-angle distributions and total kinetic energy distributions, and the results encourage the use of neutron-rich targets for producing new superheavy elements, particularly for investigating the predicted stability island around N=184 and Z=114–126.
