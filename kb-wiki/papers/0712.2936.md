# A new effective interaction for $0\hbarω$ shell model calculations in the sdpf valence space

> The neutron rich isotopes with Z<20, in particular those with neutron numbers around N=28,have been at the focus of a lot experimental and theoretical scrutiny during the last few years.Shell model calculations using the effective interaction SDPF-NR, were able to predictor to explain most of the properties featured by these nuclei. Prominent among them is the disappearance of the N=28 shell closure for Z<=16. We have incorporated to SDPF-NR some modifications, either on purely theoretical grounds or guided by new experimental information. The proposed interaction, SDPF-U, contains USD as its sd-shell part and KB3G as its pf-shell-part. Its range of applicability is enlarged and its reliability enhanced with respect to the earlier version.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new effective interaction, SDPF-U, for shell model calculations in the sdpf valence space, which improves upon the earlier SDPF-NR interaction by incorporating modifications based on theoretical grounds and new experimental data, particularly for neutron-rich isotopes with Z less than or equal to 14 where the N=28 shell closure vanishes.

## Method

The authors modified the monopole part of the SDPF-NR interaction using experimental data from nuclei with one particle or one hole on top of neutron and proton closures, including the single-particle spectrum of 35Si, the spin-orbit splitting in 47Ar, and the spectrum of 37S. They also removed a schematic pairing Hamiltonian in the pf-shell with G=-0.09 MeV to correct for excessive pairing renormalization in silicon isotopes, resulting in two versions of the interaction: one for Z greater than 14 and another for Z less than or equal to 14.

## Key numbers

- Neutron gap in 48Ca: 4.74 MeV (SDPF-U) compared to experimental value 4.78 MeV
- Excitation energy of the 3/2- state in 35Si: 0.93 MeV (SDPF-U) compared to experimental 0.91 MeV
- Excitation energy of the 2+ state in 42Si: 0.82 MeV (SDPF-U) compared to 1.49 MeV (SDPF-NR)
- B(E2) for 2+ to 0+ transition in 42Si: 86 e2 fm4 (SDPF-U) compared to 53 e2 fm4 (SDPF-NR)
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment Qs for 2+ state in 42Si: +20 e fm2 (SDPF-U) compared to +15 e fm2 (SDPF-NR)
- Excitation energy of the 2+ state in 36Mg: 0.48 MeV (SDPF-U theory) compared to 0.66 MeV (experiment)
- B(E2) for 2+ to 0+ transition in 34Mg: 65 e2 fm4 (SDPF-U theory) compared to 110(20) e2 fm4 (experiment)
- Two-neutron separation energy S2n for 40Mg: 2.67 MeV (SDPF-U theory)
- Z=14 proton gap in 42Si: 5.36 MeV (SDPF-U) compared to 6.08 MeV (SDPF-NR)
- N=28 neutron gap in 42Si: 2.75 MeV (SDPF-U) compared to 3.50 MeV (SDPF-NR)

## Context

This work extends the SDPF-NR interaction developed by Retamosa et al. (1997) and Caurier et al. (1998, 2005), which successfully predicted the disappearance of the N=28 shell closure for Z less than or equal to 16. The new SDPF-U interaction addresses discrepancies in the silicon isotope chain, particularly the conflicting experimental evidence on the nature of 42Si from MSU and GANIL (Friedman et al. 2005, Gade et al. 2005, Grevy et al. 2005). The interaction enables improved predictions for neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, including the potential identification of 40Mg as the first well-deformed nucleus with a neutron halo, and allows exploration of shape coexistence in 42Si and 40Mg.
