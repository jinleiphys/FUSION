# Spin-Excitation Mechanisms in Skyrme-Force Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock

> We investigate the role of odd-odd (with respect to time inversion) couplings in the Skyrme force on collisions of light nuclei, employing a fully three-dimensional numerical treatment without any symmetry restrictions and with modern Skyrme functionals. We demonstrate the necessity of these couplings to suppress spurious spin excitations owing to the spin-orbit force in free translational motion of a nucleus but show that in a collision situation there is a strong spin excitation even in spin-saturated systems which persists in the departing fragments. The energy loss is considerably increased by the odd-odd terms.

## Key claim

The odd-odd (time-odd) spin-orbit couplings in the Skyrme functional are necessary to suppress spurious spin excitations during free translational motion of a nucleus, but in a collision situation these same couplings produce a genuine spin-twist excitation that persists in the departing fragments and substantially increases the energy loss.

## Method

The authors performed fully three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on a Cartesian coordinate-space grid with 1 fm spacing, using the Skyrme energy-density functional with no symmetry restrictions. They compared results with and without the odd-odd spin-orbit terms for head-on and noncentral collisions of light nuclei, primarily oxygen-16 on oxygen-16 at center-of-mass energies of 75-150 MeV, using the SkM* and SLy6 parametrizations. Fragment kinetic energies were extracted from the time-dependent density via a two-body analysis of the mass quadrupole tensor.

## Key numbers

- Free translational motion of a single nucleus shows energy oscillations of only |ΔT| < 0.2 MeV with the full Skyrme force, but immediate deceleration and large oscillations when odd-odd spin-orbit couplings are omitted.
- In head-on oxygen-16 on oxygen-16 collisions at 125 MeV, the full interaction produces more true dissipation in the exit channel compared to the case without odd-odd spin-orbit terms.
- For carbon-12 on carbon-12, the additional energy loss from including odd-odd terms reached 30 MeV; for calcium-48 on calcium-48 it was almost negligible at about 1 MeV.
- The fusion cross section for oxygen-16 on oxygen-16 at 100 MeV is estimated to be somewhat larger than 500 mb, compared to 550 mb from systematics.
- The additional dissipation from odd-odd terms remains similar up to an impact parameter of about 5 fm for oxygen-16 on oxygen-16 at 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF studies from the 1980s (e.g., Umar (1986), Reinhard (1988)) that were restricted by axial symmetry and incomplete treatment of the spin-orbit force, and which showed that including the even-even spin-orbit term improved dissipation. The present fully three-dimensional calculations with all odd-odd terms included resolve the long-standing puzzle of too much transparency in older TDHF calculations and produce fusion cross sections that agree well with experimental systematics from Birkelund (1979). The results enable future large-scale investigations of dissipation under varying scattering conditions and experimental assessment of the predicted spin-twist mode.
