# Supersymmetric structure in the Dirac equation with cylindrically-deformed potentials

> Classes of relativistic symmetries accommodating supersymmetric patterns are considered for the Dirac Hamiltonian with axially-deformed scalar and vector potentials.

## Key claim

Classes of relativistic symmetries (pseudospin, spin, and factorized potentials) in the Dirac Hamiltonian with axially-deformed scalar and vector potentials can accommodate supersymmetric patterns, but the specific type of supersymmetry (good or broken) depends on the class and the particular form of the potentials, with the pseudospin limit yielding good SUSY and the spin limit yielding broken SUSY.

## Method

The author considers the Dirac Hamiltonian for a fermion of mass M moving in axially-symmetric scalar and vector potentials, and identifies four classes of relativistic symmetries based on conditions on the potentials (constant sum, constant difference, or factorized dependence on different coordinates). For each class, the author constructs conserved operators (R and L) that anticommute, and uses these to derive supersymmetric partner Hamiltonians and charges that satisfy the supersymmetric algebra, analyzing the resulting spectral degeneracies and the presence or absence of non-degenerate ground states.

## Key numbers

- For class III with \(V_V(\rho) = \alpha_V/\rho\) and arbitrary \(V_S(z)\), the energy eigenvalues are \(E^{(\epsilon)}_{n_{\rho},\Omega} = |\epsilon|/\sqrt{1 + \alpha_V^2/(n_{\rho}+\gamma)^2}\) with \(\gamma = \sqrt{\Omega^2 - \alpha_V^2}\) and \(n_{\rho}=0,1,2,\ldots\)
- For class IV with \(V_S(\rho) = \alpha_S/\rho\) and arbitrary \(V_V(z)\), the allowed values are \(\tilde{\epsilon} = \pm M\sqrt{1 - \alpha_S^2/(n_{\rho}+\tilde{\gamma})^2}\) with \(\tilde{\gamma} = \sqrt{\Omega^2 + \alpha_S^2}\) and \(n_{\rho}=0,1,2,\ldots\)
- For class III, states with \(n_{\rho}\geq 1\) and \(\pm\epsilon\) are degenerate; for \(n_{\rho}=0\) only one state with \(\epsilon>0\) is acceptable (assuming \(\alpha_V < 0\))
- For class IV, states with \(n_{\rho}\geq 1\) and \(\pm\tilde{\epsilon}\) are degenerate; for \(n_{\rho}=0\) only one state with \(\tilde{\epsilon}>0\) is acceptable

## Context

This work extends previous studies of supersymmetric patterns in Dirac Hamiltonians with spherically-symmetric potentials (Leviatan 2004) to the axially-deformed case. The pseudospin symmetry (class I) has been tested in numerous realistic mean field calculations of nuclei (Ginocchio and Leviatan 2004, Ginocchio 2005) and explains degeneracies in deformed nuclei including superdeformation and identical bands (Ginocchio 2005), while the spin symmetry (class II) is relevant to heavy-light quark mesons (Page 2001). The results enable the identification of supersymmetric structures in relativistic mean-field descriptions of deformed nuclei and hadrons, and the factorized potential classes (III and IV) connect to electron channeling in crystals (Greiner 1994) and the Schwinger mechanism for particle production (Wang 1988).
