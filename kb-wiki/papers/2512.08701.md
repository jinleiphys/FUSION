# $J/ψ$-Meson Nucleon Scattering Length from Threshold Photoproduction on Light Nuclei

> The quality of recent SRC/CT Collaboration $J/ψ$ photoproduction data off a $^4$He target from Hall~D at Jefferson Laboratory, combined with the feasibility of measuring the reaction close to the free-nucleon energy threshold, opens the door to using incoherent $J/ψ$ photoproduction to access a variety of interesting physics aspects. An example is an estimate of the $J/ψ~p$ scattering length $|α_{J/ψ~p}|$ on the bound proton obtained using the Vector Meson Dominance model. This value can be compared with that of the free proton from the GlueX Collaboration. One may then project what would be expected from the SRC/CT Collaboration Experiment E12--25--002, which was recently approved by the JLab PAC. Using a plane-wave theoretical model to generate quasi-data, we find the experiment could achieve a result of $|α_{J/ψ~p}| = 3.08\pm 0.45~\mathrm{mfm}$, an uncertainty competitive with that of the free-proton measurement. A comparison between the two would allow an evaluation of the effects of medium modification in the case of light nuclei.

## Key claim

Using a plane-wave theoretical model to generate quasi-data for the upcoming SRC/CT Collaboration Experiment E12-25-002, the authors project that the experiment could achieve a determination of the absolute value of the J/psi-proton scattering length on a bound proton in helium-4 with an uncertainty of 0.45 mfm, competitive with the free-proton measurement from GlueX, but the extraction relies on the Vector Meson Dominance model and neglects Fermi motion effects in the initial analysis.

## Method

The authors generated quasi-data for incoherent J/psi photoproduction on a helium-4 target using a Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) model, where the J/psi production cross section from a single proton is factorized from a nuclear spectral function. The spectral function was divided into a mean-field component calculated using variational Monte Carlo techniques and a short-range correlation component estimated using the Generalized Contact Formalism. The J/psi-proton scattering length was then extracted from the simulated total cross sections using the Vector Meson Dominance approach, fitting the cross section as a series in odd powers of the J/psi center-of-mass momentum.

## Key numbers

* Projected J/psi-proton scattering length from the helium-4 quasi-data: |alpha_{J/psi p}| = 3.08 +/- 0.45 mfm
* Free-proton scattering length from GlueX high-statistics data: |alpha_{J/psi p}| = 2.98 +/- 0.25 mfm
* Free-proton scattering length from GlueX initial data: |alpha_{J/psi p}| = 3.08 +/- 0.55 mfm (Strakovsky 2019)
* Linear coefficient a from fit to helium-4 quasi-data: a = 0.46 +/- 0.13 x 10^{-6} microbarn/(MeV/c)
* Estimated total tagged photon flux: 5.2 x 10^{7} photons/s, with coherent flux of 5.6 x 10^{6} photons/s and incoherent flux of 4.6 x 10^{7} photons/s
* Assumed experimental duration: 80 days on a 30 cm liquid helium-4 target with thickness 5.68 x 10^{23} nuclei/cm^2
* Assumed nuclear transparency factor: 60%; reconstruction efficiency: 15%
* J/psi branching fraction to e+e-: 6%; J/psi mass: 3096.900 +/- 0.006 MeV; J/psi partial decay width to e+e-: 5.53 +/- 0.10 keV

## Context

This work extends the GlueX Collaboration's determination of the J/psi-proton scattering length on a free proton (Strakovsky 2019) to the bound proton case using helium-4 targets. It builds on the first SRC/CT Collaboration measurements of J/psi photoproduction on light nuclei (Pybus 2024) and provides projections for the approved Jefferson Lab Experiment E12-25-002 (Pybus 2025). The results enable a comparison between free and bound proton scattering lengths to evaluate medium modification effects in light nuclei, and complement theoretical studies from QCD sum rules (Hayashigaki 1998, Klingl 1998, Kim 2000, Yeo 2025), effective hadronic models (Krein 2010), and lattice QCD (Skerbis 2018, Lyu 2024) that show a wide spread of predicted values.
