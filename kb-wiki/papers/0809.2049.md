# Particle-Number Restoration within the Energy Density Functional formalism: Nonviability of terms depending on noninteger powers of the density matrices

> We discuss the origin of pathological behaviors that have been recently identified in particle-number-restoration calculations performed within the nuclear energy density functional framework. A regularization method that removes the problematic terms from the multi-reference energy density functional and which applies (i) to any symmetry restoration- and/or generator-coordinate-method-based configuration mixing calculation and (ii) to energy density functionals depending only on integer powers of the density matrices, was proposed in [D. Lacroix, T. Duguet, M. Bender, arXiv:0809.2041] and implemented for particle-number restoration calculations in [M. Bender, T. Duguet, D. Lacroix, arXiv:0809.2045]. In the present paper, we address the viability of non-integer powers of the density matrices in the nuclear energy density functional. Our discussion builds upon the analysis already carried out in [J. Dobaczewski \emph{et al.}, Phys. Rev. C \textbf{76}, 054315 (2007)]. First, we propose to reduce the pathological nature of terms depending on a non-integer power of the density matrices by regularizing the fraction that relates to the integer part of the exponent using the method proposed in [D. Lacroix, T. Duguet, M. Bender, arXiv:0809.2041]. Then, we discuss the spurious features brought about by the remaining fractional power. Finally, we conclude that non-integer powers of the density matrices are not viable and should be avoided in the first place when constructing nuclear energy density functionals that are eventually meant to be used in multi-reference calculations.

## Key claim

Non-integer powers of the density matrices in nuclear energy density functionals are not viable for multi-reference calculations, because even after regularizing the integer part of the exponent, the remaining fractional power introduces spurious branch cuts that cannot be removed without breaking fundamental properties such as shift invariance.

## Method

The authors analyzed the analytical structure of particle-number-restored energy kernels in the complex plane, extending the variable \(z = e^{i\varphi}\) to the entire complex plane. They examined a toy Skyrme energy density functional with density-dependent terms of the form \(\rho^{\alpha}\) and \(\rho^{\gamma}\), and performed numerical applications using the realistic SLy4 Skyrme EDF combined with a local pairing part from a density-independent delta interaction. The regularization method from Lacroix et al. (2008) was applied to the integer-power part of the exponent, while the remaining fractional-power part was studied separately.

## Key numbers

- The toy functional uses a density-dependent term \(\rho^{\alpha}(\vec{r})\) with exponent \(\alpha\) and a pairing term \(\rho^{\gamma}(\vec{r})\) with exponent \(\gamma\), where \(\rho_{sat} = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is the saturation density used in the pairing form factor.
- The transition density matrices have simple poles at \(z = \pm z_{\mu} \equiv \pm i |u_{\mu}|/|v_{\mu}|\) in the complex plane.
- For bilinear functionals, a term of order \(n\) in the density matrices can generate a pole at \(\pm z_{\mu}\) of order at most \((n-1)\); the pole disappears only if \((n-1)\) additional factors from the norm kernel cancel the denominator \((u_{\mu}^{2} + v_{\mu}^{2} z^{2})^{-(n-1)}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Dobaczewski et al. (2007), who first identified two distinct pathologies in particle-number-restoration calculations: spurious poles from integer powers and spurious branch cuts from non-integer powers of density matrices. The regularization method for integer-power functionals was proposed by Lacroix et al. (2008) and implemented by Bender et al. (2008). The present paper concludes that any energy density functional providing multi-valued kernels over the complex plane, including the family of functionals proposed by Fayans and collaborators, is critically problematic, and that only low-order polynomial functionals can be handled in practical multi-reference calculations.
