# Cross section of the $pp\to K^+Σ^+n$ reaction close to threshold

> We have measured inclusive data on $K^+$-meson production in $pp$ collisions at COSY Jülich close to the hyperon production threshold and determined the hyperon-nucleon invariant mass spectra. The spectra were decomposed into three parts: $Λp$, $Σ^0p$ and $Σ^+n$. The cross section for the $Σ^+n$ channel was found to be much smaller than a previous measurement in that excess energy region. The data together with previous results at higher energies are compatible with a phase space dependence.

## Key claim

The total cross section for the $pp\to K^+\Sigma^+n$ reaction near threshold is much smaller than a previous measurement by COSY-11 (Rozek 2006), and the data together with previous results at higher energies are compatible with a simple phase space dependence without requiring a strong final-state interaction enhancement.

## Method

Inclusive $K^+$ momentum spectra were measured at forward angles using the Big Karl magnetic spectrograph at COSY Jülich at two beam momenta (2.735 and 2.870 GeV/c). The hyperon-nucleon invariant mass spectra were decomposed into contributions from $pp\to K^+\Lambda p$, $pp\to K^+\Sigma^0 p$, and $pp\to K^+\Sigma^+ n$ by first fitting the $\Lambda p$ final-state interaction parameters from the sub-threshold region, then subtracting the well-known $K^+\Sigma^0 p$ cross section (obtained from a fit to world data) to isolate the $K^+\Sigma^+ n$ channel.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for $pp\to K^+\Sigma^+ n$ at excess energy $\epsilon = 102.6$ MeV: $\sigma = 7.5 \pm 1.2$ $\mu$b (beam momentum 2.870 GeV/c).
- At the lower beam momentum (2.735 GeV/c, $\epsilon = 58.2$ MeV for $\Sigma^+ n$), the cross section was too small to extract (listed as "-" in Table 1).
- Total cross section for $pp\to K^+\Lambda p$ at $\epsilon = 133.2$ MeV: $\sigma = 14.5 \pm 1.3$ $\mu$b; at $\epsilon = 177.6$ MeV: $\sigma = 21.4 \pm 2.0$ $\mu$b.
- Total cross section for $pp\to K^+\Sigma^0 p$ at $\epsilon = 56.3$ MeV: $\sigma = 0.46 \pm 0.03$ $\mu$b; at $\epsilon = 100.7$ MeV: $\sigma = 1.48 \pm 0.09$ $\mu$b.
- Missing mass resolution: $\sigma = 0.84$ MeV.

## Context

This work resolves a large discrepancy between two previous COSY measurements of the $pp\to K^+\Sigma^+ n$ cross section: the COSY-11 result (Rozek 2006) showed a strong near-threshold enhancement, while the ANKE result (Valdau 2007) gave a much smaller value. The present HIRES measurement supports the smaller ANKE value and shows that the excitation function follows phase space without requiring strong $\Sigma^+ n$ final-state interactions. The method of extracting the $\Sigma^+ n$ channel from inclusive kaon spectra, validated by comparison with exclusive $pp\to K^+\Lambda p$ data from TOF (Bilger 1998, Metzger, Fritsch), enables future studies of hyperon-nucleon interactions near threshold.
