# Remarks on monopole charge properties within the Generalized Coherent State Model

> The Generalized Coherent State Model, proposed previously for a unified description of magnetic and electric collective properties of nuclear systems, is used to study the ground state band charge density as well as the E0 transitions from $0^+_β$ to $0^+_g$. The influence of the nuclear deformation and of angular momentum projection on the charge density is investigated. The monopole transition amplitude has been calculated for ten nuclei. The results are compared with some previous theoretical studies and with the available experimental data. Our results concerning angular momentum projection are consistent with those of previous microscopic calculations for the ground state density. The calculations for the E0 transitions agree quite well with the experimental data. Issues like how the shape transitions or shape coexistence are reflected in the $ρ(E0)$ behavior are also addressed.

## Key claim

The Generalized Coherent State Model (GCSM) can describe ground-state charge densities and E0 transition amplitudes for ten nuclei, with the angular momentum projection having negligible effect on the charge density for well-deformed nuclei (d > 3), but the monopole transition amplitude is independent of the angular momentum of the states involved.

## Method

The authors used the GCSM, which describes protons and neutrons via separate quadrupole boson operators, to construct intrinsic states for ground, beta, gamma, and isovector bands. They expanded the charge density operator in terms of quadrupole collective coordinates within the liquid drop model, quantized the coordinates using boson operators, and calculated expectation values of the scalar monopole charge density operator on both unprojected and angular-momentum-projected states. The monopole transition amplitude for the beta-to-ground band transition was derived analytically and computed for ten nuclei.

## Key numbers

* For the ratio k_p/d, the value from the liquid drop model for 156Gd is 1.176, while the value from Eq. (3.30) at q = 0.54 fm^{-1} is 1.381.
* For 156Gd at qR_0 = 0.2, the total charge for d > 1 is close to the nuclear charge Ze = 64e; at qR_0 = 1.2, the charge for J = 20 deviates from J = 0 for d < 3, converging to approximately 55e at large deformation.
* For 154Gd at qR_0 = 1.2, the charge for J = 20 deviates from J = 0, 2, 4 for d < 3, with a deviation of about ten units from the total charge at the deformation d = 3.0545.
* The monopole transition amplitude rho(E0) for the beta-to-ground band transition is given by (3 sqrt(5) Ze R_0^2) / (8 sqrt(2) pi k_p^2), independent of the state angular momentum.

## Context

This work extends the GCSM, previously applied to M1 and E2 properties (Raduta 2003, 2004), to scalar monopole operators. The results for angular momentum projection are consistent with microscopic calculations by Zaringhalam (1973) for ground state densities. The E0 transition calculations agree with experimental data and relate to the mixing coefficient approach of Sarriguren and Moya de Guerra (2001) for shape coexistence. The model enables future studies of shape transitions and shape coexistence via rho(E0) behavior, and can provide mixing coefficients for states with different deformations.
