# Effects of the tensor force on low-energy heavy-ion fusion reactions: A mini review

> In recent several years, the tensor force, one of the most important components of the nucleon-nucleon force, has been implemented in time-dependent density functional theories and it has been found to influence many aspects of low-energy heavy-ion reactions, such as dissipation dynamics, sub-barrier fusions, low-lying vibration states of colliding partners. Especially, the effects of tensor force on fusion reactions have been investigated from the internuclear potential to fusion cross sections systematically. In this work we present a mini review on the recent progresses on this topic. Considering the recent progress of low-energy reaction theories, we will also mention more possible effects of the tensor force on reaction dynamics.

## Key claim

The tensor force, implemented in time-dependent density functional theories, systematically influences low-energy heavy-ion fusion reactions by modifying internuclear potentials and fusion cross sections, but its effects are significant only for systems with spin-unsaturated proton or neutron shells, while having negligible impact on systems with spin-saturated shells.

## Method

The authors review calculations using the Skyrme energy density functional with and without tensor terms (e.g., SLy5 vs. SLy5t, and TIJ parameter sets) within three approaches: frozen Hartree-Fock (FHF), density-constrained frozen Hartree-Fock (DCFHF), and density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF). Internuclear potentials are extracted from these methods, and fusion cross sections are computed by solving the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation with incoming wave boundary conditions or using the coupled-channels code CCFULL.

## Key numbers

- For $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$, the tensor force increases the capture barrier height and the minimum of the potential pocket, with changes from a fraction of an MeV to almost 2 MeV for medium and heavy spin-unsaturated systems.
- For $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$, barriers from DC-TDHF calculations are well consistent with the experimental capture barrier from Stefanini (2009), while FHF and DCFHF barriers are higher than experiment.
- For $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{40}\mathrm{Ca}$, the tensor force has almost no influence on the internuclear potential because the shell closure at N(Z)=20 is spin-saturated.
- For $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ and $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+{}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$, the tensor force increases the capture barriers and minimum of potential pockets.

## Context

This mini review extends the work of Dai (2014), Stevenson (2016), and Guo (2018) on tensor force effects in heavy-ion reactions, and builds on the development of time-dependent density functional theories with tensor components. It enables future studies of tensor force effects on sub-barrier fusion, dissipation dynamics, and couplings to low-lying vibrations and nucleon transfer, particularly for exotic nuclei with spin-unsaturated shells. The review also contrasts with time-dependent covariant density functional theory (Ren, 2019; Ren, 2020), which does not include the tensor force.
