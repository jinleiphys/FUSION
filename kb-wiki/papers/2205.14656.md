# Momentum-dependence of $ρ-ω$ mixing in the pion vector form factor and its effect on $(g-2)_μ$

> The inclusion of the $ρ-ω$ mixing effect is essential for a precise description of the pion electromagnetic form factor in the $e^+e^- \rightarrow π^+π^-$ process, which quantifies the two-pion contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $a_μ$. In this paper, we analyse the momentum dependence of the $ρ-ω$ mixing by considering loop contributions at the next-to-leading order in the $1/N_C$ expansion within the framework of resonance chiral theory. We revisit the work [Y. H. Chen, D. L. Yao, and H. Q. Zheng, Commun. Theor. Phys. 69 (2018) 1], considering the contribution arising from the kaon mass splitting in the kaon loops and the latest experimental data. We perform two kinds of fits (with momentum-independent or momentum-dependent $ρ-ω$ mixing amplitude) to describe the $e^+e^-\rightarrow π^+π^-$ and $τ\rightarrow ν_τ2π$ data within the energy region of 600$\sim$900 MeV and the decay width of $ω\rightarrow π^+π^-$, and compare their results. Our findings indicate that both the momentum-independent and momentum-dependent $ρ-ω$ mixing schemes provide appropriate descriptions of the data. However, the momentum-dependent scheme exhibits greater self-consistency, considering the reasonable imaginary part of the mixing matrix element $Π_{ρω}$ obtained. Regarding the contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, $a_μ^{ππ}|_{[0.6,0.9]\text{GeV}}$, the results obtained from the fits considering the momentum-dependent $ρ-ω$ mixing amplitude agree well with those obtained without incorporating the momentum dependence of the $ρ-ω$ mixing, within the margin of errors. Furthermore, based on the fitted values of the relevant parameters, we observe that the decay width of $ω\rightarrow π^+π^-$ is predominantly influenced by the $ρ-ω$ mixing effect.

## Key claim
Both momentum-independent and momentum-dependent ρ-ω mixing schemes provide appropriate descriptions of the e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ and τ → ν_τ2π data in the 600–900 MeV region, but the momentum-dependent scheme exhibits greater self-consistency because it yields a physically reasonable imaginary part of the mixing matrix element Π_{ρω}, whereas the momentum-independent fits produce either an unreasonably large imaginary part or one with large error bars.

## Method
The authors compute the ρ-ω mixing amplitude up to next-to-leading order in the 1/N_C expansion within resonance chiral theory (RχT), including one-loop contributions from kaon loops with kaon mass splitting. They perform two types of fits (momentum-independent Π_{ρω} and momentum-dependent Π_{ρω}) to the e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ form factor data from OLYA, CMD, BaBar, BESIII, KLOE, CLEO, and SND, the τ → ν_τ2π form factor data from ALEPH and CLEO, and the ω → π⁺π⁻ decay width, restricting the energy region to 600–900 MeV.

## Key numbers
- ρ meson mass from Fit IIb (momentum-dependent, excluding KLOE data): M_ρ = 775.55 ± 0.11 MeV
- ω meson mass from Fit IIb: M_ω = 782.45 ± 0.11 MeV
- ω meson width from Fit IIb: Γ_ω = 8.16 ± 0.17 MeV
- Mixing amplitude at s = M_ω² in Fit IIb: Π_{ρω}(M_ω²) = (−3316.3 + 113.7i) MeV²
- Ratio of two-pion couplings G = g_{ω_Iππ}/g_{ρ_Iππ} = (2.6 ± 1.2) × 10⁻³ in Fit IIb
- Branching fraction B(ω → π⁺π⁻) = (1.48 ± 0.10) × 10⁻² from Fit IIb
- a_μ^{ππ}|_{[0.6,0.9] GeV} = (375.29 ± 2.21) × 10⁻¹⁰ from Fit IIb
- a_μ^{ππ}|_{[0.6,0.9] GeV} = (367.80 ± 2.92) × 10⁻¹⁰ from Fit Ib (momentum-dependent, excluding BaBar data)

## Context
This work revisits the earlier RχT analysis of Chen, Yao, and Zheng (2018) by adding kaon mass splitting in kaon loops and using the latest experimental data. It extends the study of momentum-dependent ρ-ω mixing originally investigated via quark loop mechanisms by Goldman (1980s) and later in models such as the global color model, extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, chiral constituent quark model, and hidden local symmetry model. The results enable more self-consistent extraction of the two-pion contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment and show that the ω → π⁺π⁻ decay width is predominantly influenced by ρ-ω mixing rather than direct ω_I → π⁺π⁻ coupling.
