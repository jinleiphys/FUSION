# On the origin of the anomalous behaviour of 2+ excitation energies in the neutron-rich Cd isotopes

> Recent experimental results obtained using $β$ decay and isomer spectroscopy indicate an unusual behaviour of the energies of the first excited 2$^{+}$ states in neutron-rich Cd isotopes approaching the N=82 shell closure. To explain the unexpected trend, changes of the nuclear structure far-off stability have been suggested, namely a quenching of the N=82 shell gap already in $^{130}$Cd, only two proton holes away from doubly magic $^{132}$Sn. We study the behaviour of the 2$^+$ energies in the Cd isotopes from N=50 to N=82, i.e. across the entire span of a major neutron shell using modern beyond mean field techniques and the Gogny force. We demonstrate that the observed low 2$^+$ excitation energy in $^{128}$Cd close to the N=82 shell closure is a consequence of the doubly magic character of this nucleus for oblate deformation favoring thereby prolate configurations rather than spherical ones.

## Key claim

The observed low 2+ excitation energy in 128Cd near the N=82 shell closure is caused by the doubly magic character of this nucleus for oblate deformation, which favors prolate configurations rather than spherical ones, without requiring any quenching of the N=82 shell gap.

## Method

The authors used a symmetry-conserving configuration mixing approach with particle number and angular momentum projection, employing the finite-range density-dependent Gogny interaction (D1S parametrization) with all exchange terms included. They generated collective subspaces of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave functions by quadrupole-constrained particle number projection before variation, then performed configuration mixing within the generator coordinate method framework with additional angular momentum projection.

## Key numbers

- The 2+ state in the N=80 isotope 128Cd lies at an excitation energy of 645 keV, which is 7 keV lower than the 2+ level in 126Cd
- For 128Cd at oblate deformation β=-0.1, the energy difference E(β=-0.1)-E(β=0) equals 2.5 MeV, compared to 1.0 MeV for 132Te
- The ratio between calculated and experimental 2+ energies is nearly constant at about 1.6 over a large range of isotopes
- The prolate deformed minimum for the 2+ state in 128Cd lies about 3.5 MeV lower than the oblate minimum

## Context

This work extends earlier exploratory studies of neutron-rich Cd isotopes by Jungclaus et al. (2006) that used a simplified approach without particle number projection and without exchange terms of the Gogny force. The calculations contradict interpretations by Kautzsch et al. (2000) and Dilling et al. (2003) that suggested a quenching of the N=82 shell closure in 130Cd, and instead show that standard nuclear structure effects explain the anomalous 2+ energies. The results enable understanding of shape coexistence in the Cd isotopes and demonstrate that beyond mean field techniques with effective interactions can describe nuclear properties across entire major shells without invoking exotic shell quenching.
