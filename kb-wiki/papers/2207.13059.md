# Reaching percolation and conformal limits in neutron stars

> Generating an ensemble of equations of state that fulfill multimessenger constraints, we statistically determine the properties of dense matter found inside neutron stars (NSs). We calculate the speed of sound and trace anomaly and demonstrate that they are driven towards their conformal values at the center of maximally massive NSs. The local peak of the speed of sound is shown to be located at values of the energy and particle densities which are consistent with deconfinement and percolation conditions in QCD matter. We also analyze fluctuations of the net-baryon number density in the context of possible remnants of critical behavior. We find that the global maxima of the variance of these fluctuations emerge at densities beyond those found in the interiors of NSs.

## Key claim

The paper claims that matter at the centers of maximally massive neutron stars is driven towards conformal values (speed of sound approaching 1/3 and trace anomaly approaching zero), but this conclusion is hedged by the statistical nature of the analysis: the reported values are medians with 1-sigma credibility intervals, and the average equation of state gives a speed of sound of 0.42 at the center, which differs from the median value of 0.28.

## Method

The authors constructed an ensemble of 4.62 × 10^5 equations of state using a piecewise-linear parametrization of the speed of sound as a function of baryon chemical potential, with 7 segments between 1.1 times nuclear saturation density and the perturbative QCD matching point at μ = 2.6 GeV. They imposed multimessenger constraints: the maximum mass must exceed 2.08 solar masses (from PSR J0740+6620), and the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star must fall within the range from the GW170817 event. They then computed the speed of sound, trace anomaly, and net-baryon number susceptibility from the ensemble.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound peak: c_s,peak^2 = 0.82^{+0.07}_{-0.08} at energy density ε_peak = 0.559^{+0.110}_{-0.088} GeV/fm^3 (median at 1σ confidence)
- Baryon density at the speed of sound peak: n_B,peak = 0.54^{+0.09}_{-0.07} fm^{-3}, corresponding to μ_B,peak = 1.283^{+0.090}_{-0.070} GeV
- Central speed of sound in maximally massive neutron stars: c_s,TOV^2 = 0.28 ± 0.06 at 1σ confidence
- Central trace anomaly in maximally massive neutron stars: Δ_TOV = -0.01 ± 0.03 at 1σ confidence
- Central energy density in maximally massive neutron stars: ε_TOV = 1.163^{+0.107}_{-0.097} GeV/fm^3 at 1σ confidence
- Percolation density from proton mass radius: n_c^per = 0.57^{+0.12}_{-0.09} fm^{-3}, consistent with n_B,peak
- Median energy density of global maximum in net-baryon number susceptibility: ε_χ = 4.084^{+1.834}_{-1.275} GeV/fm^3 at 1σ confidence
- Average equation of state yields M_TOV = 2.20 solar masses and Λ_1.4 = 408

## Context

This work extends the statistical approach to neutron star equations of state developed by Annala et al. (2019) and Altiparmak et al. (2022), using the same piecewise-linear speed-of-sound parametrization. The authors connect their results to the percolation theory of hadrons (Braun-Munzinger et al., 2014) and to lattice QCD results for the chiral crossover energy density (HotQCD Collaboration, 2018). The finding that the global maximum of net-baryon number fluctuations lies beyond neutron star densities suggests that remnants of critical behavior are unlikely to be observable in neutron star cores, which constrains the search for QCD critical phenomena. The work enables future studies using parity doublet models to connect the emergence of conformal matter with chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement.
