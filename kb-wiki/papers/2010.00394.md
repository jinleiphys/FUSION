# Apparent convergence of Padé approximants for the crossover line in finite density QCD

> We propose a novel Bayesian method to analytically continue observables to real baryochemical potential $μ_B$ in finite density QCD. Taylor coefficients at $μ_B=0$ and data at imaginary chemical potential $μ_B^I$ are treated on equal footing. We consider two different constructions for the Padé approximants, the classical multipoint Padé approximation and a mixed approximation that is a slight generalization of a recent idea in Padé approximation theory. Approximants with spurious poles are excluded from the analysis. As an application, we perform a joint analysis of the available continuum extrapolated lattice data for both pseudocritical temperature $T_c$ at $μ_B^I$ from the Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration and Taylor coefficients $κ_2$ and $κ_4$ from the HotQCD Collaboration. An apparent convergence of $[p/p]$ and $[p/p+1]$ sequences of rational functions is observed with increasing $p.$ We present our extrapolation up to $μ_B\approx 600$ MeV.

## Key claim

The authors propose a Bayesian method for analytic continuation of QCD observables to real baryochemical potential that treats Taylor coefficients at zero chemical potential and data at imaginary chemical potential on equal footing, and they observe an apparent convergence of diagonal and subdiagonal Padé sequences with increasing order, but only after explicitly excluding approximants with spurious poles from the analysis.

## Method

The authors construct two types of Padé approximants: classical multipoint Padé approximants determined solely from function values at imaginary chemical potential, and a mixed Padé-type approximant that jointly uses function values at imaginary chemical potential and Taylor coefficients at zero chemical potential. A Bayesian analysis is performed with a likelihood that includes chi-squared contributions from both the Taylor coefficients and the imaginary chemical potential data, while the prior excludes approximants with spurious poles in a wide range of squared chemical potential and prevents unwarranted oscillations. The posterior distribution of the crossover temperature is studied as a function of real baryochemical potential, with the median as the central value and asymmetric error bars representing the central 68% of the distribution.

## Key numbers

* The crossover temperature at zero chemical potential is given by the lattice data from the Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration, with seven imaginary chemical potential values at \(\hat\mu_B(j)=i j\pi/8\) for \(j=0,2,3,4,5,6,6.5,7\).
* The Taylor coefficients from the HotQCD Collaboration are \(\kappa_2\) and \(\kappa_4\) from the parametrization \(T_c(\mu_B)=T_c(0)[1-\kappa_2(\mu_B/T_c(0))^2 - \kappa_4(\mu_B/T_c(0))^4]\), which yield \(c_1=-1.878\) and \(c_2=0.0451\) with errors \(\sigma_{c_1}=0.626\) and \(\sigma_{c_2}=0.627\).
* The extrapolation is presented up to \(\mu_B\approx 600\) MeV.
* In the chiral effective model test, the sampling range for imaginary chemical potential is \(\hat\mu_B^2\in(-7.35,0]\) and the radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion is \(\hat\mu_B^2\approx 44.2\).

## Context

This work extends prior lattice QCD studies of the crossover line at finite baryochemical potential, specifically combining continuum extrapolated data from the Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration (Borsanyi 2020) for the pseudocritical temperature at imaginary chemical potential with Taylor coefficients from the HotQCD Collaboration (Bazavov 2018). The Bayesian approach is novel for the analytic continuation problem in finite density QCD, as previous applications of Padé approximants in this context (Lombardo 2005, Cea 2009, Cea 2012, Bellwied 2015) used low-order approximants without studying convergence of the Padé sequence. The method enables going to considerably higher orders than previously attempted, at the cost of physically reasonable extra assumptions, and provides a nontrivial consistency check by comparing two different types of Padé approximants.
