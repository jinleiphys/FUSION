# Does the quark cluster model predict any isospin two dibaryon resonance?

> We analyze the possible existence of a resonance in the $J^P=0^-$ channel with isospin two by means of nucleon-$Δ$ interactions based on the constituent quark model. We solve the bound state and the scattering problem using two different potentials, a local and a non-local one. The non-local potential results to be the more attractive, although not enough to generate the experimentally predicted resonance.

# Does the quark cluster model predict any isospin two dibaryon resonance?

## Key claim
The non-local quark-model-based nucleon-Δ potential generates additional attraction compared to the local potential, but neither potential is attractive enough to produce the experimentally predicted resonance at 2065 MeV without artificially adjusting the sigma meson mass. However, when the sigma mass is tuned to force the resonance mass to 2065 MeV, the resulting width becomes very narrow (0.6–1.6 MeV), consistent with the narrow peak observed in pionic double charge exchange reactions.

## Method
The authors derived a nucleon-Δ interaction from a constituent quark model using a two-center quark-cluster approach, with quark-quark interactions including confinement, one-gluon exchange, one-pion exchange, and one-sigma exchange. They solved the bound state and scattering problem for the J^P = 0^- channel with isospin two using both a local potential (assuming R = R') and a non-local potential, calculating the Fredholm determinant to assess attraction and then computing phase shifts and Argand diagrams with relativistic kinematics including the finite width of the Δ resonance.

## Key numbers
- The non-local potential produces a resonance at 2145.6 MeV with a width of 148.12 MeV for the sigma meson mass predicted by chiral symmetry requirements (m_σ ≈ 675 MeV).
- When the sigma meson mass is artificially reduced to 422.0 MeV in the non-local potential, the resonance mass becomes 2064.5 MeV with a width of 1.6 MeV.
- When the sigma meson mass is artificially reduced to 234.0 MeV in the local potential, the resonance mass becomes 2064.4 MeV with a width of 0.6 MeV.
- The experimentally predicted resonance from Bilger and Clement (1993) has a mass of 2065 MeV and a width of 0.51 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the πNN three-body system by Gale and Duck (1968) and Garcilazo and Mathelitsch (1986), which predicted large attraction in the 0^- channel, and addresses the controversy surrounding the narrow peak observed in pionic double charge exchange reactions on nuclei from 14C to 48Ca. The results support the interpretation of Bilger and Clement (1993) that the peak could be a nucleon-Δ resonance with isospin two, rather than requiring more exotic processes, and contradict earlier assumptions that the resonance must have isospin zero based on QCD string models. The strong correlation found between resonance mass and width near the πNN threshold enables future experimental searches to distinguish between candidate resonances based on their width.
