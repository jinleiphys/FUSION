# A chiral crystal in cold QCD matter at intermediate densities?

> The analogue of Overhauser (particle-hole) pairing in electronic systems (spin-density waves with non-zero total momentum $Q$) is analyzed in finite-density QCD for 3 colors and 2 flavors, and compared to the color-superconducting BCS ground state (particle-particle pairing, $Q$=0). The calculations are based on effective nonperturbative four-fermion interactions acting in both the scalar diquark as well as the scalar-isoscalar quark-hole ('$σ$') channel. Within the Nambu-Gorkov formalism we set up the coupled channel problem including multiple chiral density wave formation, and evaluate the resulting gaps and free energies. Employing medium-modified instanton-induced 't Hooft interactions, as applicable around $μ_q\simeq 0.4$ GeV (or 4 times nuclear saturation density), we find the 'chiral crystal phase' to be competitive with the color superconductor.

## Key claim
At intermediate quark chemical potentials around 0.4 GeV (approximately 4 times nuclear saturation density), a chiral crystal phase formed by particle-hole pairing (Overhauser-type) can be energetically competitive with the color-superconducting BCS ground state, provided the nonperturbative instanton-induced interactions are used rather than simpler NJL-type forces.

## Method
The authors set up coupled gap equations within the Nambu-Gorkov formalism for both scalar diquark (particle-particle, BCS) and scalar-isoscalar quark-hole (particle-hole, Overhauser) channels, including multiple chiral density waves forming a cubic crystal structure. They solved these equations using two types of effective four-fermion interactions: a schematic NJL-type interaction with dipole formfactors and a more microscopic instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction with medium-modified formfactors, for 3 colors and 2 flavors of massless quarks.

## Key numbers
- The NJL treatment at μq = 0.4 GeV yields Overhauser gaps around 5 MeV for wave vectors Qx ≤ 0.150 GeV, with a free energy gain of only Ω = -1.2973 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻⁴ compared to the free Fermi gas value of Ω = -1.2969 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻⁴
- With a larger vacuum constituent quark mass of Mq = 0.5 GeV (coupling λ = 73 fm²), the NJL solution gives Qx ≈ 0.2 GeV and σ ≈ 20 MeV
- The BCS ground state at μq = 0.4 GeV in the NJL treatment has a pairing gap Δ ≈ 0.13 GeV and free energy Ω_BCS ≈ -1.375 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻⁴
- The instanton approach is applicable for chemical potentials in the range 0.4 GeV ≲ μq ≲ 0.6 GeV, corresponding to baryon densities of 3.5-12 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear matter density)

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Overhauser-type pairing in QCD from the asymptotic high-density regime, where Deryagin et al. (1992) showed it dominates over BCS in the Nc → ∞ limit but Shuster and Son (1999) found it requires Nc ≈ 10³ to be relevant, down to intermediate densities where nonperturbative interactions become important. The authors compare their results to previous two-flavor color-superconducting calculations using instanton forces by Rapp et al. (2000) and Carter and Diakonov (1999), and find that the chiral crystal phase can be competitive with the color superconductor when using the more realistic instanton-induced interactions rather than schematic NJL forces.
