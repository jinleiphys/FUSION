# Jet p_T Resummation in Higgs Production at NNLL'+NNLO

> We present predictions for Higgs production via gluon fusion with a p_T veto on jets and with the resummation of jet-veto logarithms at NNLL'+$NNLO order. These results incorporate explicit O(alphas^2) calculations of soft and beam functions, which include the dominant dependence on the jet radius R. In particular the NNLL' order accounts for the correct boundary conditions for the N3LL resummation, for which the only unknown ingredients are higher-order anomalous dimensions. We use scale variations in a factorization theorem in both rapidity and virtuality space to estimate the perturbative uncertainties, accounting for both higher fixed-order corrections as well as higher-order towers of jet-p_T logarithms. This formalism also predicts the correlations in the theory uncertainty between the exclusive 0-jet and inclusive 1-jet bins. At the values of R used experimentally, there are important corrections due to jet algorithm clustering that include logarithms of R. Although we do not sum logarithms of R, we do include an explicit contribution in our uncertainty estimate to account for higher-order jet clustering logarithms. Precision predictions for this H+0-jet cross section and its theoretical uncertainty are an integral part of Higgs analyses that employ jet binning.

## Key claim

The paper presents predictions for Higgs production via gluon fusion with a jet pT veto at NNLL'+NNLO order, incorporating explicit O(alpha_s^2) calculations of soft and beam functions that include the dominant dependence on the jet radius R, but does not resum logarithms of R and instead includes an explicit contribution in the uncertainty estimate to account for higher-order jet clustering logarithms.

## Method

The cross section is factorized using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) into hard, beam, and soft functions, with resummation performed by renormalization group evolution in both virtuality and rapidity space. The NNLL' order includes the full O(alpha_s^2) dependence of all functions appearing in the factorization theorem, providing the correct boundary conditions for N3LL resummation, while the missing ingredients for complete N3LL are the three-loop non-cusp and four-loop cusp anomalous dimensions. The nonsingular O(alpha_s^2) corrections that vanish as pTcut goes to zero are also included, incorporating the complete NNLO cross section for all values of pTcut.

## Key numbers

- The jet veto scale is typically pTcut ~ 25-30 GeV with eta_cut ~ 4.5-5, using jet radius R = 0.4 for ATLAS and R = 0.5 for CMS
- The leading double logarithm has the form sigma_0(pTcut) = sigma_LO (1 - (alpha_s C_A/pi) * 2 ln^2(pTcut/m_H) + ...)
- For the hard function with m_H = 125 GeV, using mu_H = m_H gives H_gg/H_gg^(0) = 1 + 0.815 + 0.356 + ... while using mu_H = -i m_H gives 1 + 0.274 + 0.042 + ...
- The two-loop non-cusp soft anomalous dimension gamma_{S,1}^g = 16 C_A^2 * (-3.83)
- The two-loop rapidity anomalous dimension gamma_{nu,1}^g(R) = 16 C_A^2 * (4.16) + C_2(R), where C_2(R) = 16 C_A^2 * (-2.49 ln R^2 - 0.49) + O(R^2)
- The two-loop soft function constant s_2(R) = 16 C_A^2 * (0.43 ln R^2 - 1.69) + O(R^2)

## Context

This work extends previous analytic resummation for Higgs jet vetoes by Berger et al. (2010), Stewart et al. (2011), Banfi et al. (2012), Becher et al. (2012), Tackmann et al. (2012), and Liu et al. (2012, 2013), providing the first two-loop calculation of the soft function using rapidity renormalization. The formalism enables precision predictions for the H+0-jet cross section and its theoretical uncertainty that are integral to Higgs analyses employing jet binning, and automatically determines correlations in perturbative uncertainties between total inclusive, exclusive 0-jet, and inclusive 1-jet cross sections.
