# Many-channel microscopic cluster model of $^{8}$Be. I. Formation of high-energy resonance states

> The nature and structure of high-energy resonance states in $^{8}$Be, located just below and above the $p+^{7}$Li threshold, are investigated in detail. A microscopic many-cluster and many-channel model is employed to study the formation of these resonances. This model includes three distinct three-cluster configurations: $^{4}$He+$^{3}$H+$p$, $^{4}$He+$^{3}$He+$n$, and $^{4}$He+$d$+$d$, enabling a comprehensive treatment of all major binary decay channels of $^{8}$Be, namely $^{4}$He+$^{4}$He, $p+^{7}$Li, $n+^{7}$Be, and $d+^{6}$Li. The primary focus of our analysis is the structure and dominant decay channels of the twin $1^{+}$, $2^{+}$, $3^{+}$, and $4^{+}$ resonance states. Additionally, we propose and implement a model to clarify how the $2^{+}$ resonance states lying below the $p+^{7}$Li threshold are formed. We demonstrate that these resonances are Feshbach-type states arising due to coupling of the open $^{4}$He+$^{4}$He channel with the closed channels $p+^{7}$Li, $n+^{7}$Be, and $d+^{6}$Li at these energies. Overall, the present approach provides a realistic description of the experimentally observed resonance spectrum near the $^7$Li+$p$ decay threshold, including negative-parity states $1^-$ and $2^-$. Our results are consistent with other microscopic calculations but offer more detailed insight into the internal structure and decay pathways of these resonances.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the twin $2^{+}$ resonance states in $^{8}\text{Be}$ lying below the $p+^{7}\text{Li}$ threshold are Feshbach-type states, formed specifically by coupling the open $^{4}\text{He}+^{4}\text{He}$ channel with the closed $p+^{7}\text{Li}$, $n+^{7}\text{Be}$, and $d+^{6}\text{Li}$ channels, a mechanism the abstract states but does not qualify as conditional on the inclusion of cluster polarization and an eight-channel model space.

## Method

The authors employed a microscopic many-cluster and many-channel model based on the resonating group method, using three-cluster configurations $^{4}\text{He}+^{3}\text{H}+p$, $^{4}\text{He}+^{3}\text{He}+n$, and $^{4}\text{He}+d+d$ (and optionally $^{4}\text{He}+2p+2n$) to generate up to eight binary decay channels. The wave function was expanded in oscillator and Gaussian basis functions, with the Hasegawa-Nagata nucleon-nucleon potential (Majorana parameter $m=-0.008$, spin-orbit strength $f_{LS}=0.3$, oscillator length $b=1.362$ fm) adjusted to reproduce the ground and first excited states of $^{7}\text{Li}$ and $^{7}\text{Be}$. Calculations were performed with and without cluster polarization, and resonance parameters were extracted from the energy dependence of phase shifts and inelasticity coefficients.

## Key numbers

*   $2^{+}$ resonance energies (8-channel model with polarization): first state at $E=-0.816$ MeV (width $\Gamma=16.82$ keV), second state at $E=-0.238$ MeV ($\Gamma=243.08$ keV), compared to experimental values of $-0.628$ MeV ($\Gamma=108$ keV) and $-0.262$ MeV ($\Gamma=74$ keV).
*   $1^{+}$ resonance energies (5-channel model with polarization): first state at $E=0.385$ MeV ($\Gamma=9.63$ keV), second state at $E=1.237$ MeV ($\Gamma=350.90$ keV), compared to experimental values of $0.386$ MeV ($\Gamma=11$ keV) and $0.869$ MeV ($\Gamma=138$ keV).
*   $3^{+}$ resonance energies (5-channel model with polarization): first state at $E=1.671$ MeV ($\Gamma=280.91$ keV), second state at $E=2.339$ MeV ($\Gamma=260.56$ keV), compared to experimental values of $1.816$ MeV ($\Gamma=270$ keV) and $1.986$ MeV ($\Gamma=230$ keV).
*   $4^{+}$ resonance energies (6-channel model with polarization): first state at $E=5.191$ MeV ($\Gamma=34.2$ keV), second state at $E=5.572$ MeV ($\Gamma=7.8$ keV), compared to experimental values of $5.376$ MeV ($\Gamma=100.0$ keV) and $5.726$ MeV ($\Gamma=230.0$ keV).
*   Theoretical threshold energies for $^{4}\text{He}+^{4}\text{He}$ and $d+^{6}\text{Li}$ relative to the $p+^{7}\text{Li}$ threshold differ from experimental values by 2.6 MeV and 3.0 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic three-cluster model of Descouvemont and Baye (1994), which used the Generator Coordinate Method with $\alpha+\alpha$, $\alpha+t+p$, and $\alpha+^{3}\text{He}+n$ configurations, by including additional three-cluster configurations ($\alpha+d+d$ and $\alpha+2p+2n$) and accounting for cluster polarization effects. It also complements the Gamow Shell Model calculations of Fernandez et al. (2024), which emphasized the role of the deuteron channel for $1^{+}$, $2^{+}$, and $3^{+}$ doublets, by providing a more comprehensive phase-shift analysis and detailed partial widths for both positive- and negative-parity states. The results enable future studies (Part II) of $^{7}\text{Li}(p,\alpha)\alpha$, $^{7}\text{Be}(n,p)^{7}\text{Li}$, and $^{6}\text{Li}(d,\alpha)\alpha$ reactions, potentially addressing the cosmological lithium problem.
