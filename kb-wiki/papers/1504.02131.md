# $W$-boson production in association with a jet at next-to-next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD

> We present the complete calculation of $W$-boson production in association with a jet in hadronic collisions through next-to-next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD. To cancel infrared divergences we introduce a new subtraction method that exploits the fact that the $N$-jettiness event-shape variable fully captures the singularity structure of QCD amplitudes with final-state partons. This method holds for processes with an arbitrary number of jets, and is easily implemented into existing frameworks for higher-order calculations. We present initial phenomenological results for $W$+jet production at the LHC. The NNLO corrections are small and lead to a significantly reduced theoretical error, opening the door to precision measurements in the $W$+jet channel at the LHC.

## Key claim

The NNLO corrections to W+jet production at the LHC are small (approximately 1% decrease relative to NLO) and significantly reduce the theoretical error estimated by scale variation, but the authors note that these initial results should be viewed only as an estimate of the theoretical error and that a more detailed phenomenological study will follow.

## Method

The authors introduce a new subtraction scheme based on the N-jettiness event-shape variable tau_N, which fully captures the singularity structure of QCD amplitudes with final-state partons. The phase space is partitioned according to a resolution parameter tau_cut: above the cutoff the cross section is given by the NLO calculation for N+1 jets, while below the cutoff the cross section is obtained by expanding the factorization theorem for tau_N to NNLO using known universal functions and process-dependent two-loop virtual corrections. The method is validated by checking the cancellation of logarithmic tau_cut dependence between the two phase-space regions and by reproducing known results for Higgs+jet production at NNLO.

## Key numbers

- The NNLO correction decreases the NLO cross section by approximately 1% for the fiducial cross section at sqrt(s)=8 TeV with CT14 PDFs
- Fiducial cross section at LO: 561 (+47/-35) pb for pT_jet > 30 GeV, |eta_jet| < 2.4
- Fiducial cross section at NLO: 796 (+62/-49) pb for the same cuts
- Fiducial cross section at NNLO: 807 (+0/-10) pb for the same cuts
- Scale variation is approximately ±7% at LO and NLO, reduced to the percent level at NNLO
- The sum of low and high tau regions is stable to better than 1 pb (0.1% of total cross section) over the range 0.05 GeV < tau_cut < 0.08 GeV
- NLO corrections increase the cross section by 40% at pT_jet = 40 GeV and by nearly a factor of two at pT_jet = 180 GeV
- Scale variation at NLO reaches approximately ±20% for pT_jet = 180 GeV

## Context

This work extends the q_T-subtraction method (Catani and Grazzini, 2007), which could only handle color-neutral final states, to processes with final-state jets by using the N-jettiness event-shape variable. It provides the first complete NNLO calculation for W+jet production, a process needed for LHC phenomenological applications including the determination of the gluon distribution function. The method enables precision measurements in the W+jet channel at the LHC and is expected to be applicable to other processes with final-state jets, building on earlier partial results for inclusive jet production (Gehrmann-De Ridder et al., 2013) and Higgs+jet production (Boughezal et al., 2013).
