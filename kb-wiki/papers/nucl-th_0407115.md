# The associated photoproduction of positive kaons and $π^0 Λ$ or $πΣ$ pairs in the region of the $Σ$(1385) and $Λ$(1405) resonances

> The $γp \to K^+ π^0 Λ$ and $γp \to K^+ πΣ$ reactions are studied in the kinematic region where the $π^0 Λ$(1116) and $πΣ$(1192) pairs originate dominantly from the decay of the $Σ$(1385) and $Λ$(1405) resonances. We consider laboratory photon energies around 2 GeV, significantly above the threshold for producing the $K^+ Σ$(1385) and $K^+ Λ$(1405) final states. We compute for both reactions the process in which the ingoing photon dissociates into a real K$^+$ and a virtual K$^-$, the off-shell K$^-$ scattering subsequently off the proton target to produce the $π^0 Λ$ or $πΣ$ pair. The $K^- p \to π^0 Λ$ and $K^- p \to πΣ$ amplitudes are calculated in the framework of a chiral coupled-channel effective field theory of meson-baryon scattering. The structure of the amplitudes reflects the dominance of the $Λ(1405)$ in the $πΣ$ channel and of the $Σ(1385)$ in the $πΛ$ channel. The full pion-hyperon final state interaction is included in these amplitudes. We extract from the calculated cross section the gauge-invariant double kaon pole term. We found this term to be large and leading to sizeable cross sections for both the $γp \to K^+ π^0 Λ$ and $γp \to K^+ πΣ$ reactions, in qualitative agreement with the scarce data presently available. Accurate measurements of these cross sections should make it possible to extract the contribution of the double kaon pole and hence to assess the possibility of studying kaon-nucleon dynamics ust below threshold through these reactions.

## Key claim

The double kaon pole term in the t-channel K⁻-exchange mechanism is large and leads to sizeable cross sections for both the γp → K⁺ π⁰ Λ and γp → K⁺ πΣ reactions, in qualitative agreement with the scarce data available; however, the authors caution that only the double pole term can be determined gauge-invariantly, while the single pole term receives contributions from many processes and cannot be isolated without accurate t-distribution measurements.

## Method

The authors computed the t-channel K⁻-exchange contribution to the γp → K⁺ π⁰ Λ and γp → K⁺ πΣ reactions at laboratory photon energies around 2 GeV, using K⁻p → πY amplitudes from a chiral coupled-channel effective field theory of meson-baryon scattering developed in Lutz (2002). They extracted the gauge-invariant double kaon pole term by applying the pole scheme method, calculating the residue at the pole, and integrated over the momentum transfer t to obtain cross sections as functions of the πY invariant mass.

## Key numbers

- The K⁻p → π⁰ Λ cross section below threshold shows a symmetric shape close to a Breit-Wigner form, with the s-wave contribution being small as expected for a p-wave resonance (the Σ(1385)).
- The K⁻p → πΣ cross sections for the three charge channels show an asymmetric spectral shape largely given by s-wave dynamics, reflecting Λ(1405) dominance.
- At Eγ = 1.7 GeV, the calculated double kaon pole cross sections for the πΣ channels are roughly twice larger than predictions of the model of Nacher (1999).
- The cross section for the π⁰ Λ channel at Eγ = 1.7 GeV is about an order of magnitude larger than the result displayed in Nacher (1999), attributed partly to the neglect of the Σ(1385) resonance in that work.
- The threshold photon energies are Eγᵗʰʳᵉˢʰ = 1.41 GeV for K⁺Σ(1385) and Eγᵗʰʳᵉˢʰ = 1.45 GeV for K⁺Λ(1405) final states.
- The total center of mass energy at Eγ ≈ 2 GeV is √s = 2.15 GeV.
- The K⁻p → π⁰ Λ and K⁻p → πΣ amplitudes were tested against data up to laboratory K⁻ momenta of about 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channel approach of Lutz (2002) for kaon-nucleon scattering to photoproduction reactions, providing a method to study K⁻p dynamics below the K̄N threshold (≈ 1.435 GeV) where the Σ(1385) and Λ(1405) resonances dominate. The approach contrasts with the model of Nacher (1999) which neglected the Σ(1385) resonance, leading to significantly smaller cross sections for the π⁰ Λ channel. The results enable future experiments at LEPS (SPring-8), SAPHIR (ELSA), and the Crystal Barrel (ELSA) to extract the double kaon pole term from accurate t-distributions, thereby constraining subthreshold kaon-nucleon dynamics.
