# From controversy to precision on the sigma meson: a review on the status of the non-ordinary $f_0(500)$ resonance

> The existence and properties of the sigma meson have been controversial for almost six decades, despite playing a central role in the spontaneous chiral symmetry of QCD or in the nucleon-nucleon attraction. This controversy has also been fed by the strong indications that it is not an ordinary quark-antiquark meson. Here we review both the recent and old experimental data and the model independent dispersive formalisms which have provided precise determinations of its mass and width, finally settling the controversy and leading to its new name: $f_0(500)$. We then provide a rather conservative average of the most recent and advanced dispersive determinations of its pole position $\sqrt{s_σ}=449^{+22}_{-16}-i(275\pm12)$. In addition, after comprehensive introductions, we will review within the modern perspective of effective theories and dispersion theory, its relation to chiral symmetry, unitarization techniques, its quark mass dependence, popular models, as well as the recent strong evidence, obtained from the QCD $1/N_c$ expansion or Regge theory, for its non ordinary nature in terms of quarks and gluons.

## Key claim
This review argues that the long-standing controversy over the existence and properties of the sigma meson has been resolved through model-independent dispersive formalisms, yielding precise pole parameters, but the sigma is not an ordinary quark-antiquark meson; the abstract's claim of "finally settling the controversy" is hedged by the fact that the quoted pole position is described as a "rather conservative average" of the most recent dispersive determinations.

## Method
The author reviews experimental data on pion-pion scattering phase shifts from experiments such as Protopopescu et al. (1973) and the CERN-Munich Collaboration (1973-1975), together with model-independent dispersive techniques including Roy equations (Roy, 1971) that incorporate crossing and analyticity constraints. These methods are used to extract the sigma meson pole position from the scalar-isoscalar partial wave, avoiding the misleading Breit-Wigner parameterization that had caused confusion for decades.

## Key numbers
- Pole position of the sigma meson: sqrt(s_sigma) = 449^(+22)_(-16) - i(275 +/- 12) MeV, from a conservative average of the most recent dispersive determinations.
- In the 2012 Review of Particle Properties, the sigma mass uncertainties were reduced by a factor of 5 compared to earlier editions, and its name was changed to f0(500).
- The sigma was listed in the 1964 RPP with a mass around 390 MeV and width between 50 and 150 MeV; by 1996 it reappeared as f0(400-1200) with mass range 400-1200 MeV and width 500-1000 MeV.

## Context
This review extends the dispersive approach pioneered by Roy (1971) and later revived in the late 1990s and early 2000s, which provided the precise pole determination that led to the 2012 RPP revision. It contradicts earlier confusion from Breit-Wigner fits and the 1976-1996 period when the sigma was absent from the RPP tables. The work enables further studies of the sigma's non-ordinary nature via QCD 1/N_c expansion and Regge theory, and clarifies its role in chiral symmetry breaking and nucleon-nucleon attraction, building on the tetraquark proposal of Jaffe (1976).
