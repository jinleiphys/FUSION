# Hadron attenuation at HERMES by (pre)hadronic FSI

> We investigate hadron formation in high energy electroproduction off complex nuclei in the framework of a BUU transport model. Our approach combines a quantum mechanical treatment of the photon's initial state interactions with a semi-classical coupled channel simulation of the (pre)hadronic final-state interactions (FSI). This allows us to study the hadron attenuation observed at HERMES and to get information on the space-time picture of hadron formation separately for $π^{\pm}$, $π^0$, $K^\pm$, $p$ and $\bar{p}$.

# Hadron attenuation at HERMES by (pre)hadronic FSI

## Key claim

The observed hadron attenuation in deep inelastic scattering off nuclei at HERMES can be reproduced by a BUU transport model that treats (pre)hadronic final-state interactions, but only if the prehadron production time from the Lund model is reduced by a factor of 0.2 relative to the JETSET value, or alternatively if the first constituent production time is used instead.

## Method

The authors combine a quantum mechanical treatment of the virtual photon's initial-state interactions (including vector meson fluctuations and quark-antiquark pair branching) with a semi-classical BUU coupled-channel transport simulation of the (pre)hadronic final-state interactions. Elementary photon-nucleon reactions are modeled using the PYTHIA event generator with the Lund string fragmentation model, from which the proper times for prehadron production and hadron formation are extracted. The reaction products are then propagated through the nuclear medium with elastic and inelastic final-state interactions treated probabilistically.

## Key numbers

- Multiplicity ratios $R_M^h(z_h,\nu)$ for $\pi^{\pm}$, $\pi^0$, $K^\pm$, $p$ and $\bar{p}$ were calculated for a $^{84}$Kr nucleus as a function of the hadron energy fraction $z_h = E_h/\nu$ and the photon energy $\nu$.
- Using the Lund time $\tau_{p2}$ as the prehadron production proper time gave too weak hadron attenuation; reducing $\tau_{p2}$ by a factor of 0.2 (dotted line) yielded reasonable agreement with experimental HERMES data.
- Using the Lund time $\tau_{p1}$ (the first constituent production time) as the prehadron production time also achieved satisfying agreement with experimental data.
- The required reduction of $\tau_{p2}$ by a factor of 0.2 corresponds to an increase of the string tension $\kappa$ by a factor of about five in the nuclear medium.

## Context

This work extends the BUU transport approach developed in earlier work by the same group (Falter et al., 2002; Falter et al., 2003) to describe high-energy photon and electron induced reactions on complex nuclei. It addresses the hadron attenuation observed at HERMES (HERMES Collaboration, 2003) and provides a space-time picture of hadron formation separately for pions, kaons, protons and antiprotons. The results enable discrimination between models where hadron attenuation arises from quark energy loss via induced gluon radiation (Wang, 2000; Arleo, 2003) versus (pre)hadronic final-state interactions, and suggest that interactions of the hadronic string with the nuclear medium begin effectively at the moment of the $\gamma^*N$ interaction.
