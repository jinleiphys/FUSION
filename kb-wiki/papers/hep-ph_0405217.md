# $ΔS=0$ Effective Weak Chiral Lagrangian from the Instanton Vacuum

> We investigate the $ΔS=0$ effective chiral Lagrangian from the instanton vacuum. Based on the $ΔS=0$ effective weak Hamiltonian from the operator product expansion and renormalization group equations, we derive the strangeness-conserving effective weak chiral Lagrangian from the instanton vacuum to order ${\cal O}(p^2)$ and the next-to-leading order in the $1/N_c$ expansion at the quark level. We find that the quark condensate and a dynamical term which arise from the QCD and electroweak penguin operators appear in the next-to-leading order in the $1/N_c$ expansion for the $ΔS =0$ effective weak chiral Lagrangian, while they are in the leading order terms in the $ΔS = 1$ case. Three different types of the form factors are employed and we find that the dependence on the different choices of the form factor is rather insensitive. The low-energy constants of the Gasser-Leutwyler type are determined and discussed in the chiral limit.

# ΔS=0 Effective Weak Chiral Lagrangian from the Instanton Vacuum

## Key claim

The authors derive the strangeness-conserving (ΔS=0) effective weak chiral Lagrangian to order O(p²) and next-to-leading order in the 1/Nc expansion from the instanton vacuum, finding that the quark condensate and a dynamical term from QCD and electroweak penguin operators appear at next-to-leading order in 1/Nc for ΔS=0, whereas they appear at leading order in the ΔS=1 case. The results are presented with the caveat that the next-to-leading order corrections considered are only those at the quark level, not including mesonic loop contributions, and that a scale matching problem between the effective weak Hamiltonian (≈1 GeV) and the instanton vacuum (≈600 MeV) remains unresolved.

## Method

The authors start from the ΔS=0 effective parity-violating weak Hamiltonian derived by Desplanques (1979) from operator product expansion and renormalization group equations, then incorporate it into the nonlocal chiral quark model from the instanton vacuum. They compute the vacuum expectation value of four-quark operators by expanding the nonlocal Dirac operator to order O(∂²), taking traces over color, spin, and flavor, and summing over all four-fermion operators to obtain the effective chiral Lagrangian with low-energy constants (LECs) expressed in terms of dynamical integrals I₁, I₂, I₃, I₄, the Wilson coefficients, and the Fermi constant. Three different form factors (zero-mode, dipole-type, and Gaussian-type) for the momentum-dependent quark mass are employed to study the sensitivity of the results.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy constant N₁ is 7.38 × 10⁻⁵ MeV² for K=1, 8.31 × 10⁻⁵ MeV² for K=4, and 9.34 × 10⁻⁵ MeV² for K=7, using the zero-mode form factor with M₀ = 350 MeV.
- The low-energy constant N₉ ranges from -7.49 × 10⁻⁵ MeV² (K=1) to 0.53 × 10⁻⁵ MeV² (K=7) for the zero-mode form factor; for the dipole form factor it ranges from -6.45 × 10⁻⁵ MeV² (K=1) to 0.61 × 10⁻⁵ MeV² (K=7); for the Gaussian form factor it ranges from -2.81 × 10⁻⁵ MeV² (K=1) to 0.90 × 10⁻⁵ MeV² (K=7), all at M₀ = 350 MeV.
- The dynamic factor I₂ is identical to the Pagels-Stokar expression for fπ², which is approximately 20% smaller than the correct fπ².
- The scale of the nonlocal chiral quark model from the instanton vacuum is 1/ρ ≈ 600 MeV, while the effective weak Hamiltonian scale is around 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Franz et al. (1999, 2000) who derived the ΔS=1 effective weak chiral Lagrangian from the instanton vacuum, showing that the structure of the ΔS=0 Lagrangian differs significantly in the 1/Nc counting. The results can be used to derive weak meson-nucleon coupling constants such as hπ¹, which have been studied within the Skyrme model by Kaiser (1988, 1989) and Meissner (1998), and to study parity-violating nonleptonic weak interactions of mesons such as η → π⁺π⁻ or η → 2π⁰. The work enables systematic calculation of strangeness-conserving weak hadronic processes based on the effective weak Hamiltonian rather than factorization schemes.
