# Suppressed Electric Quadrupole Collectivity in $^{49}$Ti

> Single-step Coulomb excitation of $^{46,48,49,50}$Ti is presented. A complete set of $E2$ matrix elements for the quintuplet of states in $^{49}$Ti, centered on the $2^+$ core excitation, was measured for the first time. A total of nine $E2$ matrix elements are reported, four of which were previously unknown. $^{49}_{22}$Ti$_{27}$ shows a $20\%$ quenching in electric quadrupole transition strength as compared to its semi-magic $^{50}_{22}$Ti$_{28}$ neighbour. This $20\%$ quenching, while empirically unprecedented, can be explained with a remarkably simple two-state mixing model, which is also consistent with other ground-state properties such as the magnetic dipole moment and electric quadrupole moment. A connection to nucleon transfer data and the quenching of single-particle strength is also demonstrated. The simplicity of the $^{49}$Ti-$^{50}$Ti pair (i.e., approximate single-$j$ $0f_{7/2}$ valence space and isolation of yrast states from non-yrast states) provides a unique opportunity to disentangle otherwise competing effects in the ground-state properties of atomic nuclei, the emergence of collectivity, and the role of proton-neutron interactions.

## Key claim

The sum of $B(E2{\uparrow})$ strengths in $^{49}$Ti is suppressed by roughly 20% relative to the semi-magic core $^{50}$Ti, contrary to the empirical expectation from the weak-coupling limit that the total quadrupole excitation strength should be conserved. This suppression can be explained by a simple two-state mixing model with a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction between the odd neutron and the core, but the effect is conditional on the approximate single-$j$ valence space and the isolation of yrast states in the core.

## Method

Single-step Coulomb excitation of $^{46,48,49,50}$Ti was performed in inverse kinematics at the Florida State University John D. Fox Laboratory using beams of 74.0–85.0 MeV incident on natural C and Al targets, with the safe Coulomb-excitation criterion of 5 fm separation maintained. Gamma rays and charged particles were detected with the CLARION2-TRINITY array, and matrix elements were extracted from efficiency-corrected particle-gamma peak areas normalized to Rutherford particle singles counts using the semi-classical Coulomb excitation program GOSIA. A total of nine $E2$ matrix elements for the quintuplet of states in $^{49}$Ti were measured, four of which were previously unknown.

## Key numbers

- $B(E2; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ in $^{50}$Ti: 352(17)(23) e$^2$fm$^4$ (first uncertainty statistical, second systematic)
- Sum of $B(E2{\uparrow})$ strengths for five transitions in $^{49}$Ti: 276(^{+29}_{-8})(19) e$^2$fm$^4$
- Ratio of $\Sigma B(E2{\uparrow})$ in $^{49}$Ti to $B(E2; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ in $^{50}$Ti: 0.78(^{+9}_{-4}) (systematic effects largely cancel)
- $B(E2; 7/2^- \rightarrow 11/2^-)$ in $^{49}$Ti: 81(4)(6) e$^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2; 7/2^- \rightarrow 9/2^-)$ in $^{49}$Ti: 135(5)(9) e$^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2; 7/2^- \rightarrow 3/2^-)$ at 1381 keV: 12.0(12)(8) e$^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2; 7/2^- \rightarrow 3/2^-)$ at 1586 keV: 17.0(21)(12) e$^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2; 7/2^- \rightarrow 5/2^-)$ at 1762 keV: 32(5)(2) e$^2$fm$^4$
- $B(E2; 7/2_1^- \rightarrow 7/2_2^-)$ at 2262 keV: 0(^{+28}_{-0}) e$^2$fm$^4$ (upper limit only)
- Best-fit mixing amplitude $\alpha^2 = 0.76(3)$ from the two-state mixing model, meaning the $7/2_1^-$ ground state is 76% $0^+ \otimes f_{7/2}$ and 24% $2^+ \otimes f_{7/2}$

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of particle-core coupling near shell closures, following the recent observation of a 40% enhancement in $^{129}$Sb relative to semi-magic $^{128}$Sn by Gray (2020). The $^{49}$Ti–$^{50}$Ti pair is unique among stable cases with $Z>8$ because $^{50}$Ti has the lowest $B(E2; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ of any semi-magic core and resides in an approximate single-$j$ ($0f_{7/2}$) valence space. The suppression of quadrupole strength is reproduced by shell-model calculations in the $fp$ space with KB3 interactions and by ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations, but all calculations fail to reproduce the large increase in $B(E2{\uparrow})$ from $^{49}$Ti to $^{48}$Ti, likely due to missing excitations across $N$ and $Z=20$. The results enable disentangling the role of proton-neutron interactions from other effects in the emergence of collectivity, and the simple two-state mixing model connects electromagnetic moments, $B(E2)$ strengths, and nucleon transfer spectroscopic factors.
