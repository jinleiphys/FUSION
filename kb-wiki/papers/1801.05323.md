# Characteristic αand ^{6}He decays of the linear-chains in ^{16}C

> The linear-chain states of $^{16}$C and their decay modes are theoretically investigated by using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. It is found that the positive-parity linear-chain states have the $(3/2^-_π)^2(1/2^-_σ)^2$ configuration and primary decay to the $^{12}$Be($2^+_1$) as well as to the $^{12}$Be(g.s.) by the $α$ particle emission. Moreover, we show that they also decay to the $^6{\rm He}+{}^{10}{\rm Be}$ channel. In the negative-parity states, it is found that two types of the linear-chains exist. One has the valence neutrons occupying the molecular-orbits $(3/2^-_π)^2(1/2^-_σ)(3/2^+_π)$, while the other's configuration cannot be explained in terms of the molecular orbits because of the strong parity mixing. Both configurations constitute the rotational bands with large moment of inertia and intra-bands $E2$ transitions. Their $α$ and ${}^{6}{\rm He}$ reduced widths are sufficiently large to be distinguished from other non-cluster states although they are smaller than those of the positive-parity linear-chain.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the positive-parity linear-chain states in \(^{16}\)C, which have the \((3/2^-_\pi)^2(1/2^-_\sigma)^2\) configuration, will primarily decay by \(\alpha\) emission to both the \(^{12}\)Be(\(2^+_1\)) and \(^{12}\)Be(g.s.) states, and also decay via the \(^{6}\)He+\(^{10}\)Be channel; the negative-parity linear-chain states have smaller \(\alpha\) and \(^{6}\)He reduced widths than the positive-parity ones, but these widths are still large enough to distinguish them from other non-cluster states.

## Method

The authors used the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework with the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, applying a \(\beta\gamma\)-constrained variational calculation followed by angular-momentum projection and the generator coordinate method (GCM) with quadrupole deformation parameters \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) as generator coordinates. They calculated reduced width amplitudes (RWA) for the \(\alpha+^{12}\)Be and \(^{6}\)He+\(^{10}\)Be decay channels using the Laplace expansion method, and derived partial decay widths from the reduced widths and Coulomb penetration factors.

## Key numbers

- The positive-parity linear-chain band head is the \(0^+_6\) state at an excitation energy of 16.81 MeV.
- The moment of inertia for the positive-parity linear-chain band is \(\hbar/2\Im = 112\) keV.
- The intra-band \(B(E2)\) strength for the positive-parity linear-chain band is 380.3 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) for the \(2^+_9 \rightarrow 0^+_6\) transition, 544.3 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) for \(4^+_{10} \rightarrow 2^+_9\), and 891.4 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) for \(6^+_5 \rightarrow 4^+_{10}\).
- The \(\alpha\) reduced width \(\gamma_\alpha\) at channel radius \(a = 6.0\) fm for the positive-parity linear-chain \(0^+_6\) state is 0.28 MeV\(^{1/2}\).
- The negative-parity linear-chain band 1 has a moment of inertia \(\hbar/2\Im = 118\) keV, and its band head is the \(1^-_7\) and \(1^-_8\) states around \(E_x = 18.5\) MeV.
- The negative-parity linear-chain band 2 is built on the \(1^-_{11}\) state at \(E_x = 22.1\) MeV, with a moment of inertia \(\hbar/2\Im = 98\) keV.
- The ground state binding energy is calculated as 111.2 MeV, close to the observed 110.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD study of linear-chain states in \(^{14}\)C (Baba 2016, Baba 2017) and their earlier prediction of a positive-parity linear-chain band in \(^{16}\)C (Baba 2014). It provides new predictions for negative-parity linear-chain bands and detailed decay patterns to \(\alpha+^{12}\)Be and \(^{6}\)He+\(^{10}\)Be channels, which are intended to guide future experiments searching for these exotic configurations in neutron-rich carbon isotopes. The results contrast with \(^{14}\)C, where no negative-parity linear-chain band was found in AMD calculations (Suha 2010, Baba 2016, Baba 2017).
