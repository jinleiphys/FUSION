# Parity Violation in Photonuclear Reactions at HIGS -- Submission to Snowmass 2013: Intensity Frontier

> We discuss the scientific motivation, possible experiments, and beam requirements for measurements of parity violation in photonuclear reactions at an intensity upgraded HIGS facility, HIGS2.

# Parity Violation in Photonuclear Reactions at HIGS

## Key claim
Measurements of parity violation in the photodisintegration of the deuteron ($\vec \gamma d \rightarrow n p$) at an intensity-upgraded HIGS facility (HIGS2) can provide a third independent constraint on the five low-energy parity-violating constants (LECs) that parameterize the weak nucleon-nucleon interaction, complementing existing measurements from polarized proton-proton scattering and the NPDGamma experiment. The abstract's claim of "possible experiments" glosses over the condition that achieving the required statistical uncertainty of $10^{-8}$ demands a total of $10^{16}$ photodisintegration reactions in a liquid deuterium target over a calendar year, using photon fluxes of $10^{11}$ to $10^{12}$ photons/second with rapid helicity reversal.

## Method
The proposed experiment measures the parity-violating asymmetry in the circularly polarized photon breakup of the deuteron, $\vec \gamma d \rightarrow n p$, using a high-intensity gamma-ray beam from HIGS2 with energies of 2–12 MeV, high circular polarization (90%–95%), and rapid helicity reversal at 1–100 Hz. The signal is formed as the helicity dependence of the ratio of neutron to gamma signals, where moderated neutrons are detected using $^{3}$He ion chambers (insensitive to gamma rays) and gamma detectors operate in current mode, similar to those used in the NPDGamma experiment. The photon beam is produced via a high-power Fabry-Perot cavity driven by a mode-locked infrared laser, based on demonstrated technology from JILA and other laboratories.

## Key numbers
- The predicted parity-violating asymmetry in $\vec \gamma d \rightarrow n p$ is on the order of $5 \times 10^{-8}$, based on naive dimensional analysis and estimates of weak couplings.
- The desired statistical uncertainty of $10^{-8}$ requires $10^{16}$ photodisintegration reactions in a liquid deuterium target over a calendar year of operation.
- HIGS2 would provide total gamma-ray flux of $10^{11}$ to $10^{12}$ photons/second with minimum beam energy resolution less than 0.5% and photon energies of 2–12 MeV.
- The beam polarization is specified as 90% to 95% linear or circular polarization.
- The photon helicity reversal rate is specified as 1 to 100 Hz.
- The existing constraint from polarized proton-proton scattering is at a low energy of 13.6 MeV (Potter 1974, Eversheim 1991).

## Context
This work extends the program of parity-violation measurements in few-nucleon systems, following the existing measurement of the longitudinal asymmetry in polarized proton-proton scattering at 13.6 MeV (Potter 1974, Eversheim 1991) and the ongoing NPDGamma experiment at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (Gericke 2011), which measures the photon asymmetry in polarized neutron capture on protons. The proposed measurement of $\vec \gamma d \rightarrow n p$ provides a completely orthogonal observable to the time-reversed NPDGamma reaction, and would enable determination of all five leading-order low-energy parity-violating constants in the pionless effective field theory (EFT($\not \! \pi$)), resolving whether current measurements are consistent with the Standard Model. Previous limits exist from Chalk River (Earle 1988) and the reverse reaction (Knyaz'kov 1984), and similar experiments have been proposed at JLAB (Sinclair 2000, Wojtsekhowski 2000), SPRING-8, and the Shanghai Synchrotron.
