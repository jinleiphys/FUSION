# Exact Excited-State Functionals of the Asymmetric Hubbard Dimer

> The exact functionals associated with the (singlet) ground and the two singlet excited states of the asymmetric Hubbard dimer at half-filling are calculated using both Levy's constrained search and Lieb's convex formulation. While the ground-state functional is, as commonly known, a convex function with respect to the density (or, more precisely, the site occupation), the functional associated with the (highest) doubly-excited state is found to be concave. Also, because the density of the first-excited state is non-invertible, its ``functional'' is a partial, multi-valued function composed of one concave and one convex branch that correspond to two separate sets of values of the external potential. Remarkably, it is found that, although the one-to-one mapping between density and external potential may not apply (as in the case of the first excited state), each state-specific energy and corresponding universal functional are ``functions'' whose derivatives are each other's inverse, just as in the ground state formalism. These findings offer insight into the challenges of developing state-specific excited-state density functionals for general applications in electronic structure theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for the asymmetric Hubbard dimer, the universal functional associated with the doubly-excited state is concave (not convex like the ground-state functional), and the functional for the first-excited state is a partial, multi-valued function with both a concave and a convex branch, yet each excited-state energy and its corresponding functional remain conjugate functions whose derivatives are each other's inverse, just as in ground-state DFT.

## Method

The authors computed exact state-specific functionals for the singlet ground state and the two singlet excited states of the asymmetric Hubbard dimer at half-filling using both Levy's constrained search and Lieb's convex formulation. For each state, they identified all stationary points of the energy functional with respect to the wave function parameter y (for a given site-occupation difference rho) and, equivalently, all stationary points of the Lieb functional with respect to the external potential difference Dv. The model Hamiltonian includes a hopping parameter t, an on-site interaction U, and a site-potential difference Dv.

## Key numbers

- For t = 1/2 and U = 1, the ground-state energy E0 is concave with respect to Dv, the doubly-excited-state energy E2 is convex with respect to Dv, and the first-excited-state energy E1 is concave for Dv less than a critical value Dv_c and convex for Dv greater than Dv_c.
- The density rho_1 of the first excited state reaches a critical value rho_c at Dv_c, then decays to 0 as Dv approaches infinity.
- The critical density rho_c decreases with U, reaching zero at U = 0, and rho_c approaches 1 as U approaches infinity.
- For rho = 0, the derivative of the doubly-excited-state functional F2 with respect to rho gives Dv_2 = 0; as rho approaches 1, Dv_2 approaches infinity (while for the ground state, Dv_0 approaches negative infinity as rho approaches 1).

## Context

This work extends the ground-state DFT formalism of Hohenberg (1964), Levy (1979), and Lieb (1983) to excited states in a simple model, building on Gorling's (1999) stationarity principle and the work of Ayers and coworkers (2009, 2012, 2015, 2018) on one-to-one mappings for excited states. The asymmetric Hubbard dimer has been used previously by Schonhammer and Gunnarsson (1987), Carrascal and coworkers (2015, 2018), Cohen and coworkers (2016), and others to test density-functional concepts. The results provide insight into the mathematical structure of excited-state functionals and the challenges of developing state-specific density functionals for general electronic structure applications, particularly for systems where the density-potential mapping is non-invertible.
