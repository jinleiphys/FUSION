# Excitation functions in central Au+Au collisions from SIS/GSI to AGS/Brookhaven

> Using the relativistic transport model (ART), we predict the energy dependence of the stopping power, maximum baryon and energy densities, the population of resonance matter as well as the strength of the transverse and radial flow for central Au+Au reactions at beam momentum from 2 to 12 GeV/c available at Brookhaven's AGS. The maximum baryon and energy densities are further compared to the predictions of relativistic hydrodynamics assuming the formation of shock waves. We also discuss the Fermi-Landau scaling of the pion multiplicity in these reactions.

# Excitation functions in central Au+Au collisions from SIS/GSI to AGS/Brookhaven

## Key claim
Using the relativistic transport model (ART 1.0), the authors predict that the maximum energy density in central Au+Au collisions exceeds the estimated QGP transition density of about 2 GeV/fm³ at beam momenta above approximately 8 GeV/c, but note that the model is purely hadronic and its validity breaks down if QGP is actually formed.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic transport model ART 1.0, which includes baryons (N, Δ(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535), Λ, Σ) and mesons (π, ρ, ω, η, K) with explicit isospin degrees of freedom, to simulate central Au+Au collisions at beam momenta from 2 to 12 GeV/c at an impact parameter of 2 fm. They calculated the time evolution of baryon and energy densities on a 40×40×48 lattice with 1 fm³ cells, and compared results from pure cascade mode and a soft equation of state (compressibility 200 MeV) with predictions from relativistic hydrodynamics solving the Rankine-Hugoniot equation. They also analyzed transverse flow using the Danielewicz-Odyniec method and radial flow via average radial flow velocity.

## Key numbers
- Maximum baryon density reaches about 8ρ₀ at the highest beam momentum (12 GeV/c), with the increase slowing but not saturating above 8 GeV/c.
- Maximum energy density exceeds the estimated QGP transition density of 2 GeV/fm³ at beam momenta above about 8 GeV/c.
- The resonance/baryon ratio (NΔ + NN*)/(At + Ap) increases from about 20% to 45% as beam momentum increases from 2 to 12 GeV/c, corresponding to resonance density from about 0.6ρ₀ to 3ρ₀.
- The transverse flow parameter F decreases quickly from 2 to about 7 GeV/c and then saturates; the cascade result is about a factor of two smaller than the soft equation of state result across the whole energy range.
- The radial flow velocity saturates at about 0.78 c at t=20 fm/c.
- The reaction time to reach stable rapidity distribution decreases from about 17 fm/c at 2 GeV/c to about 10 fm/c at 12 GeV/c.

## Context
This work extends previous studies using the ART model (Li and Ko, 1995) and compares with relativistic hydrodynamics predictions from Danielewicz (1995) for maximum compression, finding good agreement especially below 9 GeV/c. The results provide a hadronic baseline for the E895 and E866 experiments at AGS, which will measure collective flow and pion multiplicities across beam momenta from 2 to 12 GeV/c. The predicted excitation functions can be used to identify signals of chiral symmetry restoration or QGP formation, as suggested by Rischke et al. (1995) who predicted a minimum in collective flow at about 6 GeV/nucleon if QGP transition occurs.
