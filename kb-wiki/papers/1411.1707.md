# Non-Perturbative Calculation of the Scalar Yukawa Theory in Four-Body Truncation

> The quenched scalar Yukawa theory is solved in the light-front Tamm-Dancoff approach including up to four constituents (one scalar nucleon, three scalar pions). The Fock sector dependent renormalization is implemented. By studying the Fock sector norms, we find that the lowest two Fock sectors dominate the state even in the large-coupling region. The one-body sector shows convergence with respect to the Fock sector truncation. However, the four-body norm exceeds the three-body norm at the coupling $α\approx 1.7$.

## Key claim

The quenched scalar Yukawa theory solved in the light-front Tamm-Dancoff approach with up to four constituents (one scalar nucleon, three scalar pions) shows that the lowest two Fock sectors dominate the state even in the large-coupling region, but the four-body norm exceeds the three-body norm at coupling alpha approximately 1.7, indicating a breakdown of the Fock sector hierarchy.

## Method

The authors quantized the scalar Yukawa theory on the light front and applied Fock sector dependent renormalization (FSDR) to a system truncated to include up to four constituents (one nucleon and three pions). They derived a set of coupled integral equations for the vertex functions and solved them numerically using an iterative method on a 5-dimensional grid with Gauss-Legendre quadrature, using the three-body truncation solution as the initial guess. The calculations were performed on a Cray XE6 supercomputer with a hybrid MPI/OpenMP code at physical parameters mu = 0.14 GeV, m = 0.94 GeV, and a Pauli-Villars regulator mass mu_1 = 15 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The four-body norm exceeds the three-body norm at coupling alpha approximately 1.7.
- The lowest two Fock sectors (one-body and two-body) constitute about 85% of the full norm at alpha = 1.8.
- The breakdown of the two-body truncation (I_2 greater than I_1) occurs at alpha approximately 1.3; the three-body truncation (I_3 greater than I_2) at alpha approximately 1.6; the four-body truncation (I_4 greater than I_3) at alpha approximately 1.7.
- The system was solved at mu = 0.14 GeV, m = 0.94 GeV, mu_1 = 15 GeV for various couplings.
- The numerical grid used N_rad = 47, N_ang = N_lfx = 20 for the vertex function calculations.
- Typically 50 to 100 iterations were needed to achieve absolute convergence of 10^{-4}.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the Yukawa theory with FSDR that were solved up through the three-body truncation by Karmanov et al. (2008, 2010, 2012). The four-body truncation demonstrates the scalability of the FSDR scheme and allows investigation of convergence of the Fock sector expansion by comparing with lower truncations. The results provide the first step toward studying the bound-state problem in the two-nucleon sector, which has been extensively studied in various approaches by Hiller (1993), Savkli (1999), Hwang (2004), and Ji (2012), though not all from first principles with systematic non-perturbative renormalization.
