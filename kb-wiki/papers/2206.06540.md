# Impact of fragment formation on shear viscosity in the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition region

> Within the improved quantum molecular dynamic (ImQMD) model we follow the evolution of nuclear matter for planar Couette flow in a periodic box. We focus on the region of liquid-gas phase transition and extract the shear viscosity coefficient from the local stress tensor, directly following viscosity definition. By switching on and off the mean field and thus inducing the phase transition, we are able to observe the impact of clumping in the phase-transition region onto the viscosity.

# Impact of fragment formation on shear viscosity in the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition region

## Key claim
The shear viscosity coefficient drops significantly in the liquid-gas phase transition region when the mean field is included, but only at low average densities (below 0.4 ρ₀) and low temperatures (below 10 MeV); outside this region the mean field has little impact. The drop is attributed to fragment formation rather than to the phase transition itself, as the fragments move slowly and stall momentum transport.

## Method
The authors used the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model to simulate symmetric nuclear matter (A = 600 nucleons) in a periodic cubic box with planar Couette flow. They extracted the shear viscosity coefficient from the local stress tensor using the SLLOD algorithm, applying a shear rate of 0.002 c/fm. The mean field was switched on and off to isolate the effect of the liquid-gas phase transition, and the Pauli principle was enforced using local equilibrium Fermi-Dirac distributions rather than the standard QMD wave-packet blocking.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity ratio (with mean field / without mean field) drops to values significantly below unity at densities below 0.4 ρ₀ and temperatures below 10 MeV, while remaining close to unity at temperatures above ~12 MeV and densities near ρ₀.
- Critical density for the liquid-gas phase transition: ρ_c ~ 0.4 ρ₀ (0.064 fm⁻³); critical temperature: T_c ~ 18 MeV.
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section fixed at 40 mb; Gaussian wave-packet width σ = 2 fm; system size A = 600 nucleons.
- Shear viscosity over entropy density (η/s) for infinite nuclear matter in this work: 2–55 times ℏ/(4π) over temperature range 4–25 MeV and density range 0.1–0.8 ρ₀.
- Relaxation time decreases with increasing temperature; at low T and low density in the phase transition domain, relaxation time is reduced.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of shear viscosity in nuclear matter by explicitly accounting for fragment formation (long-range correlations) in the liquid-gas phase transition region, which previous approaches could not capture. Earlier work by Danielewicz (1984) using the BUU equation imposed uniform density and could not treat correlations; the Maxwell construction approach by Xu et al. (2013) treated the coexistence region as a linear combination of uniform phases, yielding higher viscosity than without the transition. The present results show a reduction in viscosity opposite to that prediction, similar to the suppression of η/s observed in quark-gluon plasma near the QCD phase transition (Csernai et al., 2006). The findings enable testing of shear viscosity in nuclear collisions at tens of MeV/nucleon, where the liquid-gas phase transition is crossed.
