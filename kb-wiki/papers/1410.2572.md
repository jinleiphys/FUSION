# On the Influence of Stochastic Moments in the Solution of the Neutron Point Kinetics Equation

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that stochastic fluctuations in the neutron point kinetics equations produce standard deviations for neutron density that can be of the same order of magnitude as the mean values, indicating that deterministic approaches may be insufficient for accurate computation. This conclusion is qualified by the specific parameter regimes tested, which include constant and linear reactivity insertions for one and six delayed neutron precursor groups.

## Method

The authors reproduce the stochastic formulation of Hayes and Allen (2005) to derive an Ito stochastic differential equation system for neutron density and delayed neutron precursor concentrations. They implement Monte Carlo simulations alongside two numerical approaches: the Stochastic Piecewise Constant Approximation (Stochastic PCA) method and the Euler-Maruyama approximation, comparing results for constant reactivity cases (one and six precursor groups) and a linear reactivity case (one precursor group) against the deterministic model.

## Key numbers

- For one precursor group with reactivity rho = -1/3 at t=2 s: Monte Carlo mean neutron density E(n(2)) = 400.032 with standard deviation sigma(n(2)) = 27.311 (approximately 7% of the mean)
- For six precursor groups with reactivity rho = 0.003 at t=0.1 s: Monte Carlo mean neutron density E(n(0.1)) = 183.04 with standard deviation sigma(n(0.1)) = 168.79 (same order of magnitude as the mean)
- For six precursor groups with reactivity rho = 0.007 at t=0.001 s: Monte Carlo mean neutron density E(n(0.001)) = 135.66 with standard deviation sigma(n(0.001)) = 93.376
- For six precursor groups with reactivity rho = 0.003: Monte Carlo mean total precursor concentration E(sum c_i(0.1)) = 4.478 x 10^5 with standard deviation 1495.72
- For six precursor groups with reactivity rho = 0.007: Monte Carlo mean total precursor concentration E(sum c_i(0.001)) = 4.464 x 10^5 with standard deviation 16.226
- Monte Carlo statistical error of mean values less than 0.05% with 95% confidence

## Context

This work extends the stochastic point kinetics formulation of Hayes and Allen (2005) by providing Monte Carlo validation and comparison with the Euler-Maruyama approach used by Ray (2012) and the deterministic analytical solution of Petersen et al. (2009) and Wollmann da Silva et al. (2014). The paper is the first in a planned sequence studying time-dependent reactivity scenarios and stochastic moments, aiming to develop a hierarchic solution procedure that separates deterministic and stochastic components, contrasting with existing methods that rely on inhour equation roots. The stiffness of the stochastic equations remains a key difficulty for future optimization.
