# Neutrino Interactions in Octet Baryon Matter

> Neutrino processes caused by the neutral current are studied in octet baryon matter. Previous confusion about the baryonic matrix elements of the neutral current interaction is excluded, and a correct table for them improved by consideration of the proton spin problem is presented instead.

## Key claim

The paper corrects previous confusion about the baryonic matrix elements of the neutral current interaction in octet baryon matter, presenting a corrected table that incorporates the proton spin problem; the authors show that the sea-quark contribution, parameterized by the singlet axial-vector matrix element Σ, can suppress neutron processes by up to 30% and enhance proton processes by up to 40% relative to the pure SU(3) values.

## Method

The authors evaluate the matrix elements of the hadronic neutral current between octet baryon states using the SU(6) quark model and SU(3) symmetry, then incorporate the flavor-singlet current contribution from the GIM mechanism. They correct the axial-vector coefficients by including the sea-quark contribution derived from the proton spin problem, using experimental data from EMC and SMC and lattice QCD results for the singlet axial-vector matrix element Σ.

## Key numbers

- sin²θ_W ≃ 0.23 (Weinberg angle used in vector coefficient calculations)
- g_A ≃ 1.27 (axial-vector coupling constant for the nucleon)
- D ≃ 0.80, F ≃ 0.47 (SU(3) coupling constants for axial-vector matrix elements)
- C_A^0 = 0 – 0.32 (singlet axial-vector matrix element from EMC and SMC data at Q² = 10.7 GeV² and 5 GeV² respectively)
- C_A^0 = 0.08 – 0.37 (singlet axial-vector matrix element from lattice simulations)
- Sea-quark contribution extracted from EMC data: Δ_sea u ≃ Δ_sea d ≃ Δ_sea s ≃ -0.16
- Neutron processes suppressed at most by 30% due to sea-quark contribution
- Proton processes enhanced at most by 40% due to sea-quark contribution

## Context

This work extends the standard SU(3) treatment of neutral current matrix elements used in neutron star cooling and supernova studies, correcting errors in earlier works (e.g., Refs. 4b and 6) that incorrectly claimed vanishing Λνν̅ coupling. The authors incorporate the proton spin problem discovered by the European Muon Collaboration (1988) and subsequent Spin Muon Collaboration measurements, showing that the flavor-singlet current contribution, previously neglected in baryonic neutral current calculations, significantly affects neutrino reaction rates in dense matter. This enables more accurate calculations of neutrino mean-free paths, bremsstrahlung emissivities, and Cooper-pair cooling rates in neutron stars with hyperon admixtures.
