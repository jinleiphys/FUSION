# To bind or not to bind: The H-dibaryon in light of chiral effective field theory

> We analyse the quark mass dependence of the binding energy of the H-dibaryon in the framework of chiral effective field theory. We show that the SU(3) breaking effects induced by the differences of the pertinent two-baryon thresholds (Lambda-Lambda, Xi-N, Sigma-Sigma) have a very pronounced impact that need to be incorporated properly in future lattice QCD simulations. We also point out that if the H-dibaryon is a two-baryon bound state, its dominant component is Xi-N rather than Lambda-Lambda, which is a consequence of the approximate SU(3) flavor symmetry of the two-baryon interactions.

## Key claim

The H-dibaryon, if it exists as a two-baryon bound state, is predominantly a \(\Xi N\) bound state rather than a \(\Lambda\Lambda\) bound state, and the SU(3) breaking effects from the different two-baryon thresholds (\(\Lambda\Lambda\), \(\Xi N\), \(\Sigma\Sigma\)) reduce its binding energy by as much as 60 MeV compared to a calculation with degenerate thresholds, a factor that must be incorporated in lattice QCD extrapolations.

## Method

The authors used leading-order (LO) chiral effective field theory (EFT) with Weinberg power counting to construct baryon-baryon (\(BB\)) potentials for the strangeness \(S=-2\), isospin \(I=0\), \(J=0\) sector, consisting of four-baryon contact terms and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges. They solved a coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the three channels \(\Lambda\Lambda\), \(\Xi N\), and \(\Sigma\Sigma\), using five low-energy constants (LECs) fixed from hyperon-nucleon scattering data and a sixth LEC (\(C^1\)) in the flavor-singlet channel that was fine-tuned to produce a bound H-dibaryon with a given binding energy. They then varied the pion mass and the baryon masses to study the quark mass dependence and the effect of SU(3) breaking from the physical threshold splittings.

## Key numbers

*   For a H-dibaryon binding energy (BE) adjusted to the NPLQCD value at \(M_\pi = 389\) MeV, the BE is reduced by 7 MeV at the physical pion mass.
*   For physical values of the thresholds, the BE of the H is reduced by as much as 60 MeV compared to a calculation based on degenerate (SU(3) symmetric) \(BB\) thresholds.
*   The physical \(\Lambda\Lambda\), \(\Xi N\), and \(\Sigma\Sigma\) thresholds are at 2231.2, 2257.7, and 2385.0 MeV, respectively, using isospin-averaged masses (\(m_\Lambda=1115.6\) MeV, \(m_\Sigma=1192.5\) MeV, \(m_\Xi=1318.1\) MeV, \(m_N=939.6\) MeV).
*   For the NPLQCD case, after extrapolating their baryon and pion masses to physical values, the H-dibaryon survives as a quasi-bound state in the \(\Xi N\) system around 5 MeV below its threshold, corresponding to a resonance at a kinetic energy of 21 MeV in the \(\Lambda\Lambda\) system.
*   For the HAL QCD case, after extrapolating their masses (pseudoscalar mass \(m_{ps} = 673\) MeV, baryon mass \(m_B = 1485\) MeV, BE = -35 MeV) to physical values, the bound state disappears.

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT framework previously applied to hyperon-nucleon scattering (Polinder 2006) and \(S=-2\) interactions (Polinder 2007) to analyze the H-dibaryon. It directly addresses the lattice QCD results from the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane 2011) and the HAL QCD collaboration (Inoue 2011), which claimed evidence for a bound H-dibaryon at unphysical pion masses. The authors show that the SU(3) breaking from threshold differences is a much larger effect than the pion mass dependence, and that the H-dibaryon, if bound, is primarily a \(\Xi N\) state, contradicting the naive \(\Lambda\Lambda\) picture. This work enables future lattice QCD simulations to properly incorporate the physical threshold splittings when extrapolating to the physical point, and it suggests that the H-dibaryon may be a virtual state or resonance rather than a true bound state.
