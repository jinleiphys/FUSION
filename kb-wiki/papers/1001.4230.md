# Higher order forward spin polarizability

> As a guideline for future experiments to extract the four (leading) spin polarizabilities of the nucleon, we have constructed the forward amplitude for polarized Compton scattering by dispersion integrals. These integrals have been saturated by recently measured helicity-dependent photoabsorption cross sections as well as predictions for pion photoproduction multipoles from several phenomenological descriptions and chiral perturbation theory. The comparison of these results corroborates the strategy to extract the spin polarizabilities by fitting them to polarized Compton data and fixing all higher order spin effects by dispersion relations based on pion photoproduction multipoles.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the spin-dependent forward Compton amplitude using dispersion integrals saturated by measured helicity-dependent photoabsorption cross sections and pion photoproduction multipoles, concluding that the leading spin polarizabilities can be extracted from future polarized Compton experiments by treating them as free parameters while fixing higher-order spin effects via dispersion relations based on pion photoproduction multipoles.

## Method

The authors evaluated dispersion integrals for the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule and the forward spin polarizabilities (FSPs) using helicity-dependent total photoabsorption cross section data from the GDH Collaboration at MAMI and ELSA, covering photon energies from 0.175 GeV to 2.82 GeV. Contributions from kinematical regions not covered by data were determined using multipole analyses for pion photoproduction from HDT (Hanstein et al.), SAID09, MAID07, and DMT models, with systematic errors estimated by comparing different model predictions. The unmeasured threshold region for the nπ⁺ channel was evaluated using the HDT multipoles, exploiting the low-energy theorem for charged pion photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- GDH sum rule total: 210 ± 6 (stat.) ± 14 (syst.) μb, compared to the GDH sum rule prediction of 204 μb
- Leading forward spin polarizability γ₀: (−0.90 ± 0.08 ± 0.11) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Subleading forward spin polarizability γ̄₀: (0.60 ± 0.07 ± 0.07) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁶
- Fifth-order FSP in c.m. system γ̃₀: (0.42 ± 0.09 ± 0.09) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁶
- Recoil term: (0.18 ± 0.02 ± 0.02) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁶
- Multi-pion contribution to GDH sum rule from resonance model: (20 ± 2) μb; from data on γp → Nππ channels (325 MeV < Eγ < 800 MeV): (39 ± 1 ± 3) μb
- Up to Eγ = 50 MeV, the forward scattering amplitude is described by γ₀ within 10% accuracy; at 80 MeV, γ̄₀ must also be included for the same precision

## Context

This work extends dispersion relation approaches to Compton scattering (Babusci 1998, Drechsel 2002) by using recent experimental helicity-dependent photoabsorption data to construct the forward spin amplitude, providing an independent check on theoretical frameworks needed to extract the four spin polarizabilities. The results corroborate the strategy proposed by Pasquini (2007) to extract spin polarizabilities from polarized Compton data between pion threshold and the Δ(1232) region, and enable future experiments at MAMI to determine these fundamental nucleon structure constants by fixing higher-order spin effects through dispersion relations rather than treating all parameters as free.
