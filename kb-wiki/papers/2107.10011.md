# Second order cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations revisited I. Vanishing chemical potentials

> We update lattice QCD results for second order cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations and correlations at non-zero temperature and vanishing values of the conserved charge chemical potentials. We compare these results to hadron resonance gas calculations with and without excluded volume terms as well as S-matrix results in the hadronic phase of QCD, and comment on their current limitations. We, furthermore, use these results to characterize thermal conditions in the vicinity of the pseudo-critical line of the chiral transition in QCD. We argue that the ratio of strange to baryon chemical potentials is a robust observable that, on the one hand, deviates only little from hadron resonance gas results, but, on the other hand, is very sensitive to the spectrum of strange baryon resonances.

## Key claim

The paper updates lattice QCD results for second order cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations at vanishing chemical potentials, finding that the ratio of strange to baryon chemical potentials is a robust observable that deviates little from hadron resonance gas results but is very sensitive to the spectrum of strange baryon resonances, though the authors note that hadron resonance gas models have current limitations in describing QCD thermodynamics.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations by the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2017, Bazavov 2018) with significantly increased statistics, and provides updated continuum extrapolated results for second order cumulants that serve as benchmarks for hadron resonance gas models. The results enable improved comparisons with HRG models using excluded volume terms (Hagedorn 1980, Vovchenko 2016) and S-matrix approaches (Dashen 1969, Venugopalan 1992), and will be extended to non-vanishing chemical potentials in a follow-up paper. The authors also compare with results from Bellwied (2019) and find good agreement between the two analyses.
