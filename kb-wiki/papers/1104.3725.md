# Energy dependence of the saturation scale and the charged multiplicity in pp and AA collisions

> A natural framework to understand the energy dependence of bulk observables from lower energy experiments to the LHC is provided by the Color Glass Condensate, which leads to a "geometrical scaling" in terms of an energy dependent saturation scale Q_s. The measured charged multiplicity, however, seems to grow faster (~\sqrt{s}^0.3) in nucleus-nucleus collisions than it does for protons (~\sqrt{s}^0.2), violating the expectation from geometric scaling. We argue that this difference between pp and AA collisions can be understood from the effect of DGLAP evolution on the value of the saturation scale, and is consistent with gluon saturation observations at HERA.

# Energy dependence of the saturation scale and the charged multiplicity in pp and AA collisions

## Key claim

The observed faster growth of charged multiplicity with energy in nucleus-nucleus collisions (~√s^0.3) compared to proton-proton collisions (~√s^0.2) can be explained by DGLAP evolution effects on the saturation scale, rather than contradicting the Color Glass Condensate framework; the difference arises because the gluon distribution's x-dependence becomes steeper at higher Q² scales, and the nuclear saturation scale Q_s is larger than the proton one.

## Method

The author analyzes two dipole cross section parametrizations (IPsat and bCGC) that were fit to HERA data, extending them to nuclei by assuming independent scattering off individual nucleons via a Glauber-like averaging procedure. The saturation scale Q_s is defined as the solution of the dipole cross section reaching a specific fraction of the black disk limit, and its energy dependence is extracted separately for protons and lead nuclei at the median impact parameter. The charged multiplicity is then estimated from Q_s using the relation dN_ch/dη ∝ Q_s² S_⊥, with a gluon liberation coefficient c ≈ 1.1 from classical Yang-Mills simulations, assuming fast thermalization and ideal hydrodynamical expansion.

## Key numbers

- Charged multiplicity in AA collisions grows as ~√s^0.3, while in pp collisions it grows as ~√s^0.2
- In the IPsat model, the saturation scale for nuclei follows Q_s² ~ x^{-0.31}, while for protons it follows Q_s² ~ x^{-0.20}
- In the bCGC model, the saturation scale for nuclei follows Q_s² ~ x^{-0.22}, while for protons it follows Q_s² ~ x^{-0.18}
- The bCGC fit parameters are λ = 0.159, x₀ = 5.95 × 10⁻⁴, N₀ = 0.417, and B_CGC = 5.5 GeV⁻²
- The gluon liberation coefficient c ≈ 1.1 from classical Yang-Mills simulations
- For RHIC, the initial gluon mean transverse momentum is ⟨p_T⟩ ≈ 1.5 GeV, corresponding to ⟨p_T⟩ ≈ 1.3 Q_s
- For LHC at 2.75 A TeV, the initial gluon ⟨p_T⟩ ≈ 2.0 GeV
- The transverse area per participant pair in central Au-Au collisions is 2S_⊥/N_part ≈ 154 fm²/(0.5 × 350)

## Context

This work addresses a puzzle where the energy dependence of charged multiplicity in pp and AA collisions appears to violate the geometric scaling expectation from the Color Glass Condensate framework. The author extends the analysis of the IPsat model (Kowalski, 2003; Kowalski, 2006) and the bCGC model (Kowalski, 2006; Watt, 2007), which include impact parameter dependence in the saturation scale, to show they can simultaneously describe both pp and AA data. This contrasts with fixed-coupling BK/JIMWLK evolution (Mueller, 2003) which would predict slower growth for nuclei, and with b-independent fits like GBW (Golec-Biernat, 1998) that favor a faster evolution speed. The work enables a unified description of bulk observables from lower energies to the LHC within the CGC framework, though it notes that the k_T-factorized approach (Albacete, 2010) fails to simultaneously predict both multiplicity and transverse energy without arbitrary normalization adjustments.
