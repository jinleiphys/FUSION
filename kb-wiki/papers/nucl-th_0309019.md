# Chiral effective field theory for few-nucleon systems

> 9 pages, 6 figures, plenary talk at 17th International IUPAP Conference on Few-body Problems in Physics, June 5-10, 2003, Durham, North Carolina, USA

# Chiral effective field theory for few-nucleon systems

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory (EFT) can describe few-nucleon systems up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), but the numerically large low-energy constants (LECs) \(c_{3,4}\) extracted from \(\pi N\) scattering produce an unphysically strong attraction in the subleading two-pion exchange potential that degrades predictions for peripheral partial waves; this problem can be resolved by a spectral-function regularization that removes high-mass components from the pion-loop integrals without introducing model dependence.

## Method
The author reviews the Weinberg approach to nuclear forces, in which a chiral effective potential is constructed order-by-order in the expansion in soft scale \(Q \sim M_\pi\) over the chiral-symmetry-breaking scale \(\Lambda\). At NNLO, the potential includes one-pion exchange, two-pion exchange (both leading and subleading), and contact interactions; the subleading two-pion exchange involves the LECs \(c_{1,3,4}\) fixed from \(\pi N\) scattering. For the two-nucleon system, the Lippmann–Schwinger equation is solved with an exponential regulator \(f^\Lambda(p) = \exp[-p^6/\Lambda^6]\), and the spectral-function regularization is applied to the pion-loop integrals by introducing a cutoff \(\lambda\) on the spectral mass \(\mu\). For three- and four-nucleon systems, Faddeev–Yakubovsky equations are solved exactly, with the two unknown parameters of the NNLO three-nucleon force fixed from the triton binding energy and the \(nd\) scattering length.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of \(^3\)H at NLO: \(-7.53\) to \(-8.54\) MeV for cutoff \(\Lambda = 500\)–\(560\) MeV (empirical: \(-8.68\) MeV).
- Binding energy of \(^4\)He at NLO: \(-23.87\) to \(-29.57\) MeV for \(\Lambda = 500\)–\(560\) MeV (empirical: \(-29.8\) MeV).
- Binding energy of \(^4\)He at NNLO with chiral three-nucleon force: \(-29.51\) to \(-29.98\) MeV for \(\Lambda = 500\)–\(600\) MeV.
- LECs from \(\pi N\) analysis (Paul00): \(c_1 = -0.81\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -4.70\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 3.40\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- In the spectral-function regularization, the cutoff \(\lambda\) is varied in the range 500–800 MeV; the momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) in the Lippmann–Schwinger equation is varied 450–600 MeV at NLO and 450–650 MeV at NNLO.
- The \(\Delta\)-nucleon mass splitting \(m_\Delta - m_N \sim 293\) MeV partially saturates the large values of \(c_{3,4}\).

## Context
This work reviews the status of chiral EFT for few-nucleon systems as of 2003, extending the original Weinberg (1990) proposal and the two-nucleon calculations by Ordóñez et al. (1996) to three- and four-nucleon systems at NNLO. It addresses the convergence problem identified by Kaiser et al. (1997), where the subleading two-pion exchange with dimensionally regularized loops destroyed agreement in D- and F-waves. The spectral-function regularization introduced here (detailed further in Epelbaum, Glöckle, Meißner 2004) improves convergence and removes spurious deeply bound states that plagued earlier NNLO potentials (Epelbaum et al. 2000, 2002). The results enable parameter-free predictions for \(Nd\) scattering and the \(\alpha\)-particle binding energy, and set the stage for the next (NNNLO) order, work on which was in preparation at the time of this talk.
