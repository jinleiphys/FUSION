# Nucleon-Nucleon Potential: Drift Effects

> In the rest frame of a many-body system, used in the calculation of its static and scattering properties, the center of mass of a two-body subsystem is allowed to drift. We show, in a model independent way, that drift corrections to the nucleon-nucleon potential are relatively large and arise from both one- and two-pion exchange processes. As far as chiral symmetry is concerned, corrections to these processes begin respectively at $\cO(q^2)$ and $\cO(q^4)$. The two-pion exchange interaction also yields a new spin structure, that promotes the presence of $P$ waves in trinuclei and is associated with profile functions which do not coincide with neither central nor spin-orbit ones. In principle, the new spin terms should be smaller than the $\cO(q^3)$ spin-orbit components. However, in the isospin even channel, a large contribution reverts this expectation and gives rise to the prediction of important drift effects.

## Key claim

Drift corrections to the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential, arising because the two-body center of mass is allowed to move in the rest frame of a many-body system, are shown to be relatively large in a model-independent way; although chiral symmetry suggests drift terms should be smaller than spin-orbit components (beginning at $\mathcal{O}(q^4)$ versus $\mathcal{O}(q^3)$), in the isospin even channel a large contribution from the $\mathcal{O}(q^3)$ central component subverts this expected hierarchy and predicts important drift effects.

## Method

The author derives drift corrections to the NN potential by evaluating the covariant $t$-matrix for on-shell nucleon-nucleon scattering, then expanding the result in terms of Pauli spin operators and expressing it in the Jacobi coordinates of a three-nucleon system. The analysis uses a relativistic chiral expansion of the two-pion exchange (TPEP) potential to $\mathcal{O}(q^4)$, previously developed by the author's group (Robilotta, Higa, and Robilotta, 2005), and restricts to the linear gradient approximation, keeping only terms proportional to single gradients acting on the wave function. The drift operator $\mathcal{O}_D$ is identified from the spin structure of the amplitude, and its profile functions are expressed in terms of the known TPEP components.

## Key numbers

- Drift corrections to one-pion exchange begin at $\mathcal{O}(q^2)$ in the chiral expansion; drift corrections to two-pion exchange begin at $\mathcal{O}(q^4)$.
- The two-pion exchange drift operator $\mathcal{O}_D$ is given by $\mathcal{O}_D = i (\boldsymbol{\sigma}^{(1)} - \boldsymbol{\sigma}^{(2)}) \cdot \mathbf{q}_r \times \mathbf{Q}_\rho / 2\sqrt{3}$, where $\mathbf{q}_r$ and $\mathbf{Q}_\rho$ are Jacobi momenta.
- In the isospin even channel, the drift profile function satisfies $t_D^+ = (3\mathbf{q}_r^2/4m^2) t_{SS}^+ - (1/2) t_C^+$, where $t_{SS}^+$ and $t_C^+$ are the spin-spin and central profile functions respectively.
- In the isospin odd channel, $t_D^- = -(1/2) t_C^-$.
- The spin-orbit potential $V_{LS}^\pm$ is $\mathcal{O}(q)$ in the chiral expansion, while the drift potential $V_D^\pm$ is $\mathcal{O}(q^2)$ in principle; however, keeping the $\mathcal{O}(q^3)$ term in $V_D^+$ produces a large effect that reverses this hierarchy.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of the NN potential by Robilotta, Higa, and collaborators (2005), which provided a relativistic formulation of two-pion exchange free from particular reference frames. The drift corrections address a question posed by Kievsky (2000), who introduced a phenomenological three-body force to improve the description of the $N$-$d$ vector analyzing power $A_y$. The results enable the inclusion of drift effects in three-nucleon (and larger) calculations, where the new spin structure $\mathcal{O}_D$ promotes $P$-wave components in trinuclei, going beyond the standard central, spin-orbit, tensor, and spin-spin operators.
