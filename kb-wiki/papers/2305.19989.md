# Jet quenching in mini-quark-gluon plasma: Medium modification factor $I_{pA}$ for photon-tagged jets

> We calculate the medium modification factor $I_{pA}$ for the photon-tagged jet fragmentation functions for scenario with the quark-gluon plasma formation in $pA$ and $pp$ collisions. We perform calculations of radiative and collisional parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma with running $α_s$ which has a plateau around $Q\sim κT$ with $κ$ fitted to the LHC data on the heavy ion $R_{AA}$. We find that the theoretical prediction for $I_{pA}$ in $5.02$ TeV $p$+Pb collisions are within errors consistent with the data from ALICE [1]. However, a definite conclusion about the presence or absence of jet quenching in $pA$ collisions cannot be drawn due to large experimental errors of the ALICE data [1]. Our calculations show that this requires a significantly more accurate measurement of $I_{pA}$.

## Key claim

The theoretical prediction for the medium modification factor \(I_{pA}\) in 5.02 TeV \(p\)+Pb collisions is within errors consistent with ALICE data, but a definite conclusion about the presence or absence of jet quenching in \(pA\) collisions cannot be drawn due to large experimental errors.

## Method

The author calculates the medium modification factor \(I_{pA}\) for photon-tagged jet fragmentation functions using the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to induced gluon emission, including both radiative and collisional parton energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma with a running \(\alpha_s\) that has a plateau around \(Q\sim \kappa T\). The medium-modified fragmentation functions are computed via a triple \(z\)-convolution formula, and the mQGP fireball is modeled using Bjorken's 1+1D isentropic longitudinal expansion with parameters fitted to LHC heavy ion \(R_{AA}\) data. Calculations are performed for several values of the charged hadron multiplicity density associated with the mQGP fireball in \(pA\) collisions, using a Monte Carlo wounded nucleon Glauber model to estimate the corona contribution.

## Key numbers

- The ALICE measurement gives \(\langle I_{pA}\rangle\approx 0.84\pm 0.11\)(stat)\(\pm 0.19\)(sys) for 5.02 TeV \(p\)+Pb collisions with trigger photon momentum \(12<p_T^{\gamma}<40\) GeV.
- For the scenario with mQGP formation in both \(pA\) and \(pp\) collisions, \((1-I_{pA})\sim 0.1\) at \(z_T\sim 0.5\) and \((1-I_{pA})\sim 0.2\) at \(z_T\sim 0.9\) for \(\xi\sim 0.5\).
- For the scenario without mQGP formation in \(pp\) collisions, \((1-I_{pA})\sim 0.2\) at \(z_T\sim 0.5\) and \((1-I_{pA})\sim 0.45\) at \(z_T\sim 0.9\) for \(\xi\sim 0.5\).
- The fireball radii for \(pA\) collisions are \(R_f(pA)\approx[1.62,1.85,2.03,2.16]\) fm for \(\xi=0,1/3,2/3,1\) respectively.
- The initial mQGP temperatures for \(pA\) collisions range from \(T_0\approx 214\) MeV (ideal gas) / 244 MeV (lattice) for \(\xi=0\) to \(T_0\approx 246\) MeV (ideal gas) / 275 MeV (lattice) for \(\xi=1\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous LCPI-based analyses of jet quenching in \(pp\) collisions (Zakharov, 2013, 2021) and heavy ion \(R_{AA}\) (Zakharov, 2020) to the photon-tagged jet channel in \(pA\) collisions. The calculations use the same running \(\alpha_s\) parametrization with \(\kappa=2.55\) fitted to LHC \(R_{AA}\) data for Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions. The results are compared to ALICE data on \(I_{pA}\) (ALICE, 2023) and show that significantly more accurate measurements (with errors \(\lesssim 0.1-0.2\)) are needed to distinguish the mQGP formation scenario from the absence of jet quenching in \(pA\) collisions.
