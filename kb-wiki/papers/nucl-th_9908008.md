# Transport equations of a consistent description of the kinetics and hydrodynamics of dense quantum systems. I: General approach within the frame of nonequilibrium thermo field dynamics

> We present basic equations of nonequilibrium thermo field dynamics of dense quantum systems. A formulation of nonequilibrium thermo field dynamics has been performed using the nonequilibrium statistical operator method by D.N.Zubarev. Generalized transfer and hydrodynamic equations of a consistent description of kinetics and hydrodynamics have been obtained in thermo field representation. To demonstrate how obtained results do work at the description of kinetics and hydrodynamics of a dense nuclear matter we consider quantum system with strongly coupled states.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general theoretical framework for deriving transport equations that consistently describe both kinetic and hydrodynamic stages of dense quantum systems with strongly coupled states, using nonequilibrium thermo field dynamics based on Zubarev's nonequilibrium statistical operator method; the approach is intended to overcome limitations of Boltzmann-like kinetic equations and nonequilibrium Green function techniques for systems where no small parameter (such as density) exists.

## Method

The authors formulate nonequilibrium thermo field dynamics by mapping the quantum Liouville equation for the statistical operator onto a Schrodinger-type equation for a nonequilibrium thermo vacuum state vector in a doubled (thermal Liouville) space of creation and annihilation superoperators. They introduce a quasiequilibrium thermo vacuum state vector defined via the maximum entropy principle, with parameters determined by self-consistency conditions, and use the Kawasaki-Gunton projection operator technique in thermo field representation to obtain generalized transport equations for the chosen set of observables. For dense quantum systems with coupled states, they select as the shortened description the Wigner distribution functions of A-particle coupled states and the average total energy density, and derive coupled integro-differential transport equations (8.12)-(8.13) with generalized transport kernels (8.14)-(8.17) that describe dissipative processes.

## Key numbers

* The distance between nucleons in the center of a dense quark-gluon matter reaches approximately 10^{-13} cm (stated as context for strong interactions).
* The density of such systems increases by a factor of ten in the fourth degree (10^4) relative to lower-density regimes (stated as context for the breakdown of Boltzmann-like equations).

## Context

This work extends the nonequilibrium thermo field dynamics formalism previously developed by Arimitsu (1988-1994) and Zubarev, Tokarchuk (1991) to the specific problem of dense nuclear matter with strongly coupled states, building on the quantum kinetic equation for dense systems obtained by Morozov and Ropke (1995) which included a one-particle Wigner distribution function and average energy density. The approach enables a consistent description of kinetics and hydrodynamics for systems such as quark-gluon plasma created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (Negele 1982, McLerran 1986, Blaettel et al. 1993) and hot compressed nuclear matter, where Boltzmann-like kinetic equations and the Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation are insufficient due to strong correlations and the absence of a small density parameter.
