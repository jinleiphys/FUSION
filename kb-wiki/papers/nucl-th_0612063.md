# Baryon-Baryon Interactions from the Lattice

> I discuss the latest progress in calculating baryon-baryon interactions with lattice QCD. The latest results from the NPLQCD collaboration for nucleon-nucleon scattering along with preliminary calculations of hyperon-nucleon scattering are presented.

## Key claim
Lattice QCD calculations of baryon-baryon interactions are now feasible at currently available pion masses (above physical values), but the nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths are only of natural size at these masses and cannot yet be extrapolated to the physical point using lattice data alone without combining with experimental values and effective field theory constraints.

## Method
The NPLQCD collaboration performed lattice QCD calculations using domain-wall valence propagators on MILC configurations with staggered sea quarks at a single lattice spacing of b ~ 0.125 fm. For two-particle scattering, they computed energy eigenvalues in a finite volume and extracted scattering phase shifts using the Luscher formalism, which relates the finite-volume energy shift to the infinite-volume scattering amplitude. For hyperon-nucleon scattering, strange quark propagators were generated in addition to light-quark propagators, and correlation functions were constructed for channels including ΛN, Σ⁻n, Σ⁻Σ⁻, ΛΛ, and Ξ⁻Ξ⁻.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-proton mass difference from strong isospin breaking only: M_n - M_p |^(m_d-m_u) = +2.26 ± 0.57 ± 0.42 ± 0.10 MeV, compared to Cottingham sum-rule estimate of +2.05 ± 0.3 MeV
- For Σ⁻n scattering at m_π ~ 500 MeV and b ~ 0.125 fm: phase shift δ = -61.8 ± 7.2° at kinetic energy T = 26.9 ± 7.2 MeV, giving scattering length a = +0.61 ± 0.12 fm
- Tensor potential between two infinitely massive B-mesons: V_T ≤ 40 MeV (consistent with zero within uncertainty)
- Lattice spacing used throughout: b ~ 0.125 fm
- Pion masses for hyperon-nucleon calculations: m_π ~ 290, 350, 500, and 600 MeV

## Context
This work extends the Luscher finite-volume method (Luscher 1986) from meson-meson scattering (where NPLQCD had previously calculated ππ and Kπ scattering) to the baryon-baryon sector, including both nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon systems. The calculations are performed at pion masses where the scattering lengths are of natural size (set by the inverse pion mass), unlike the physical unnaturally large scattering lengths. The results enable future matching to chiral effective field theory (Weinberg 1990, 1991; Kaplan 1998) and many-body methods such as Green Function Monte Carlo (Pieper 2004) and the No-Core Shell Model (Forssen 2004), ultimately aiming to connect QCD to nuclear and hypernuclear properties.
