# The Jlab Upgrade - Studies of the Nucleon with CLAS12

> An overview is presented on the program to study the nucleon structure at the 12 GeV JLab upgrade using the CLAS12 detector. The focus is on deeply virtual exclusive processes to access the generalized parton distributions, semni-inclusive processes to study transverse momentum dependent distribution functions, and inclusive spin structure functions and resonance transition form factors at high Q^2 and with high precision.

## Key claim

The 12 GeV JLab upgrade with the CLAS12 detector will enable a comprehensive program to map nucleon structure via generalized parton distributions (GPDs), transverse momentum dependent distribution functions (TMDs), and spin structure functions, but the actual extraction of these quantities depends on the dominance of the handbag mechanism for deeply virtual meson production, which must first be established at the upgrade energy.

## Method

The paper reviews the planned experimental program using the CLAS12 large acceptance spectrometer at Jefferson Lab's 12 GeV continuous-wave electron beam. CLAS12 will achieve a luminosity of 10^35 cm^-2 sec^-1, an order of magnitude higher than the previous CLAS detector, with improved particle identification via threshold gas Cerenkov counters, upgraded time-of-flight systems, and a new central detector with a high-field solenoid magnet for polarized targets. The program encompasses deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and deeply virtual meson production to access GPDs, semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) for TMDs, inclusive spin structure function measurements, and elastic and transition form factor measurements at high Q^2.

## Key numbers

- Luminosity of CLAS12: 10^35 cm^-2 sec^-1, an order of magnitude increase over the previous CLAS detector
- Projected beam spin asymmetry for DVCS at 11 GeV beam energy, x=0.2, Q^2=3.3 GeV^2, -t=0.45 GeV^2
- Projected measurements of the ratio F^n_2/F^p_2 and d/u ratio up to x values approaching 0.9 using spectator proton detection with momenta as low as 70 MeV/c
- Expected precision on the polarized gluon distribution xΔG from CLAS12 data included in LSS QCD analysis, showing dramatic improvement over existing CLAS EG1 data
- Moments of spin structure functions measurable up to Q^2=6 GeV^2 with statistical accuracy improved several fold over existing world data
- Total point-to-point uncorrelated uncertainty on the twist-4 term for the Bjorken sum f_2^(p-n) decreases by a factor of 5.6 compared to results from Deur (2004)
- Projected extension of the neutron magnetic form factor G_M^n to higher Q^2 with CLAS12
- Projected transverse photocoupling amplitude A_1/2 for the N(1520)D_13 resonance up to Q^2 values beyond 6 GeV^2
- Transition form factors for the Δ(1232) measured up to Q^2=6 GeV^2, for N(1535)S_11 up to 4 GeV^2, and for the Roper resonance N(1440)P_11 up to 4.5 GeV^2 from existing CLAS data

## Context

This paper extends the nucleon structure program initiated with the 6 GeV CLAS detector, which produced measurements of DVCS beam asymmetries (CLAS-DVCS-1, CLAS-DVCS-3), target asymmetries (CLAS-DVCS-2), and resonance transition form factors for the Δ(1232) (Frolov 1999, Joo 2002, Ungaro 2006), N(1535)S_11 (Armstrong 1999, Thompson 2001, Denizli 2007), and Roper resonance (Park 2007, Aznauryan 2007). The upgrade enables the first comprehensive measurements of GPDs H, E, and H̃ through combined beam and target asymmetries, and extends the BONUS technique for spectator proton detection (BONUS experiment) to determine d/u ratios at much larger x. The program will enable spatial imaging of the nucleon via Fourier transformation of GPD t-dependence, extraction of quark angular momentum from GPD moments, and determination of higher-twist parameters from spin structure function moments at moderate Q^2.
