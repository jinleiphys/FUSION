# Gamow-Teller transitions of neutron-rich $N=82,81$ nuclei by shell-model calculations

> $β$-decay half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei around $N=82$ are key data to understand the $r$-process nucleosynthesis. We performed large-scale shell-model calculations in this region using a newly constructed shell-model Hamiltonian, and successfully described the low-lying spectra and half-lives of neutron-rich $N=82$ and $N=81$ isotones with $Z=42-49$ in a unified way. We found that their Gamow-Teller strength distributions have a peak in the low-excitation energies, which significantly contributes to the half-lives. This peak, dominated by $ν0g_{7/2} \to π0g_{9/2}$ transitions, is enhanced on the proton deficient side because the Pauli-blocking effect caused by occupying the valence proton $0g_{9/2}$ orbit is weakened.

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations using a newly constructed Hamiltonian successfully describe low-lying spectra and beta-decay half-lives of neutron-rich N=82 and N=81 isotones with Z=42-49 in a unified way, but the half-life of 131In is underestimated because the calculated Gamow-Teller transition to the lowest 7/2+ state of the daughter 131Sn is too large, implying further improvement of the theoretical model is needed.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations for N=81 and N=82 isotones using a model space spanning proton orbits from 0f5/2 through 0h11/2 and neutron orbits from 0g7/2 through 0h11/2 with a 78Ni inert core. The effective interaction was constructed by combining the JUN45 and SNBG3 realistic interactions with the monopole-based universal (VMU) interaction, and single-particle energies were determined from experimental levels of one-nucleon neighbors of 132Sn. The Lanczos strength function method with 250 iterations was used to calculate Gamow-Teller strength distributions, and the quenching factor was taken as qGT=0.7.

## Key numbers

- The M-scheme dimension of the shell-model Hamiltonian matrix reaches 3.1 x 10^9 for the largest calculations
- The calculated half-life of 131In is 156 ms (using theoretical Q-value) compared to the experimental value of 261(3) ms
- The calculated half-life of 130Cd is 158 ms (theoretical Q) compared to 127(2) ms experimental
- The calculated half-life of 128Pd is 28 ms compared to 35(3) ms experimental
- For 124Mo, the predicted half-life is 4.0 ms
- The low-energy Gamow-Teller peak for even-Z N=82 parents is located at approximately 2 MeV excitation energy
- For odd-Z N=82 parents, the low-energy GT peak is located at approximately 3.5 MeV
- The B(GT) value for the low-energy peak in 122Zr is 2.7 with the quenching factor 0.7
- The first-forbidden transition contribution to half-lives is approximately 13% for Z=42-48, N=82 isotones
- The fitted quenching factor to reproduce experimental half-lives of 129Cd, 130Cd and the 3/2+ isomer is qGT=0.67

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies that were restricted to singly-magic N=82 isotones (Zhi 2013, Garcia 2007, Pinedo 1999) to also cover N=81 isotones within a unified description, motivated by the need for beta-decay properties of both N=82 and N=81 nuclei to determine the r-process path around the second abundance peak. The calculations predict enhanced low-energy Gamow-Teller strengths toward proton-deficient nuclei due to weakening of Pauli blocking in the pi0g9/2 orbit, and identify 124Mo and 122Zr as new candidates for superallowed Gamow-Teller transitions analogous to the case of 100Sn (Hinke 2012, Lubos 2019), enabling future experimental investigations at RI beam facilities.
