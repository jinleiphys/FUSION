# Analysis of the thermal cross section of the capture reaction 13C(n,gamma)14C

> We investigate the thermal cross section of the reaction 13C(n,gamma)14}C which takes place in the helium burning zones of red giant star as well as in the nucleosynthesis of Inhomogeneous Big Bang models. We find that we can reproduce the experimentally known thermal capture cross section only if we take into account a strong hindrance of the E1 transition in the nuclear interior. This effect can be explained by the strong coupling to the giant dipole resonance.

## Key claim

The thermal neutron capture cross section of 13C(n,γ)14C can be reproduced in a potential model only if a strong hindrance of the E1 transition in the nuclear interior is included, an effect attributed to coupling to the giant dipole resonance.

## Method

The authors calculated the direct s-wave capture cross section using a potential model with real folding potentials in the DDM3Y parametrization. They adjusted the potential strength parameter λ for the scattering state to match the known coherent and incoherent scattering lengths for neutrons on 13C, and used spectroscopic factors from the 13C(d,p)14C reaction and a shell-model calculation. To account for the E1 hindrance, they applied a simple cutoff model where the effective charge vanishes inside a cutoff radius Rc.

## Key numbers

- The calculated thermal capture cross section without the cutoff model is 5.28 mb, which is higher than the measured value of 1.37 mb.
- The experimental thermal cross section of 1.37 mb is reproduced when the cutoff radius Rc is approximately 4 fm.
- The coherent scattering length for neutrons on 13C is a_c = 5.76 fm, and the incoherent scattering length is a_i = -0.48 fm.
- The potential strength parameters for the two channel spins are λ0 = 1.1396 (for Ja=0) and λ1 = 1.2465 (for Ja=1).
- The spectroscopic factor for the s-wave transition to the ground state (0+ at 0.000 MeV, Q-value 8.176 MeV) is C²S = 1.734; for the 0+ state at 6.589 MeV (Q-value 1.587 MeV) it is 0.14; for the 2+ state at 7.012 MeV (Q-value 1.164 MeV) it is 0.065.

## Context

This work extends earlier direct capture calculations by Kim et al. (1987), Oberhummer et al. (1991), and Mohr et al. (1993) by applying a cutoff model from Uchida et al. (1985) to account for giant dipole resonance coupling. The reaction is important for the s-process in low-mass AGB stars, where 13C(α,n)16O is the main neutron source and the capture reaction acts as a neutron poison, and for nucleosynthesis in Inhomogeneous Big Bang models as discussed by Iben (1976) and Kajino (1990). The results enable improved predictions for neutron capture rates in astrophysical environments where s-wave capture dominates.
