# Coupled-channel influence on the $a_0(1700/1800)$ line shape

> The current situation with the recently discovered $a_0(1700/1800)$ resonance is very paradoxical: it is believed that $a_0(1700/1800)$ must be strongly coupled with two vector mesons, but there is no direct experimental confirmation of this yet. Based on the assumption that the $a_0(1700/1800)$ is a state similar to the four-quark state from the MIT bag, belonging to either the $\underline{9}^*$ or the $\underline{36}^*$ $q^2\bar q^2$ multiplet, we analyze the influence of the strong $a_0(1700/1800)$ coupling to the vector channels $K^* \bar K^*$, $ρϕ$, and $ρω$ on its line shape in the decay channels into pseudoscalar mesons $K\bar K$, $πη$, and $πη'$. This effect depends on the location of the resonance mass $m_{a_0}$ relative to the nominal thresholds of vector channels. For example, if $m_{a_0}\approx 1700$ MeV, then the influence turns out to be hidden in a fairly wide range of coupling constants. On the whole, our analysis shows that, to confirm the presence of the strong $a_0(1700/1800)$ coupling to the vector channels, utterly required is the direct detection of the decays $a_0(1700/1800)\to K^*\bar K^*$, $ρϕ$, $ρω$. The appearance of even certain hints at the existence of these decays would make it possible to fundamentally advance in understanding the nature of the new $a_0$ state.

## Key claim

Based on the assumption that the $a_0(1700/1800)$ is a four-quark state from the MIT bag model belonging to either the $\underline{9}^*$ or $\underline{36}^*$ $q^2\bar q^2$ multiplet, the strong coupling of this resonance to vector meson channels ($K^*\bar K^*$, $\rho\phi$, $\rho\omega$) can be hidden in its pseudoscalar decay line shapes when the resonance mass is near 1700 MeV, making direct detection of decays into vector meson pairs necessary for confirmation.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel model for the $a_0(1710)$ resonance using a propagator formalism with polarization operators for both pseudoscalar ($K\bar K$, $\pi\eta$, $\pi\eta'$) and vector ($K^*\bar K^*$, $\rho\phi$, $\rho\omega$) intermediate states, including finite-width effects for the $K^*$ and $\rho$ mesons via Breit-Wigner distributions. They considered two four-quark scenarios from the MIT bag model: the $C^s_\pi(\underline{9}^*)$ state with OZI-superallowed coupling to $K^*\bar K^*$, $\rho\phi$, $K\bar K$, and $\pi\eta_s$, and the $C_\pi(\underline{36}^*)$ state with coupling to $\rho\omega$ and $\pi\eta_0$. The mass spectra for decays into pseudoscalar and vector meson pairs were computed as functions of the resonance mass and coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- For $m_{a_0}\approx 1710$ MeV with $g^2_2/g^2_1=12.8$ (variant 3), $\Gamma_{a_0\to PP}(m^2_{a_0})\approx 218$ MeV and $\Gamma^{\text{tot}}_{a_0}(m^2_{a_0})\approx 294$ MeV, yet the visible width of the $K^+\bar K^0$ peak remains about 100 MeV.
- For $m_{a_0}\approx 1840$ MeV with $g^2_2/g^2_1=22.4$ (variant 5), $\Gamma_{a_0\to PP}(m^2_{a_0})\approx 31$ MeV and $\Gamma^{\text{tot}}_{a_0}(m^2_{a_0})\approx 158$ MeV.
- The ratio $g^2_{a^+_0 K^{*+}\bar K^{*0}}/g^2_{a^+_0K^+\bar K^0}$ for the $a_0(1710)=C^s_\pi(\underline{9}^*)$ is approximately 13.2.
- For the $C_\pi(\underline{36}^*)$ scenario with $m_{a_0}=1710$ MeV and $g^2_2/(16\pi)=0.75$ GeV$^2$, the total width at $\sqrt{s}=m_{a_0}$ is about 600 MeV with a visible width of approximately 300 MeV.
- The nominal thresholds for vector channels are: $2m_{K^*}\approx 1.791$ GeV, $m_\rho+m_\phi\approx 1.795$ GeV, and $m_\rho+m_\omega\approx 1.558$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Jaffe (1977) on four-quark scalar states from the MIT bag model, specifically the $C^s_\pi(\underline{9}^*)$ and $C_\pi(\underline{36}^*)$ states, to the recently discovered $a_0(1700/1800)$ resonance observed by BESIII, BABAR, and LHCb in $D^+_s$ and $B^+$ decays. It contrasts with earlier coupled-channel approaches by Osmanovic (2009) and others that treat the $a_0(1710)$ as dynamically generated from vector meson interactions, showing that the strong coupling to vector channels can be hidden in pseudoscalar decay spectra. The analysis enables future experimental searches for the decays $a_0(1700/1800)\to K^*\bar K^*$, $\rho\phi$, and $\rho\omega$ as decisive tests of the four-quark nature of this state, and suggests that the isoscalar partner $f_0(1710)$ recently observed in $\omega\phi$ decays by Dong (2023) warrants complementary study.
