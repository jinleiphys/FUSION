# Perturbative Color Transparency in Electroproduction Experiments

> We calculate quasi-exclusive scattering of a virtual photon and a proton or pion in nuclear targets. This is the first complete calculation of ``color transparency" and "nuclear filtering " in perturbative QCD. The calculation includes full integrations over hard interaction kernels and distribution amplitudes in Feynman -x fractions and transverse spatial separation space $b$. Sudakov effects depending on $b$ and the momentum transfer $Q^2$ are included. Attenuation of the hadronic states propagating through the medium is calculated using an eikonal Glauber formalism. Nuclear correlations are included explicitly. We find that the color transparency ratio is comparatively insensitive to theoretical uncertainties inherent in perturbative formalism, such as choice of infrared cutoff scales. However, the $Q^2$ dependence of the transparency ratio is found to depend sensitively on the model of the distribution amplitude, with endpoint-dominated models failing to be dominated by short-distance. Color transparency experiments should provide an excellent test of the underlying theoretical assumptions used in the pQCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete perturbative QCD calculation of color transparency and nuclear filtering for quasi-exclusive electroproduction of pions and protons on nuclear targets, finding that the transparency ratio is comparatively insensitive to theoretical uncertainties like infrared cutoff scales but depends sensitively on the model of the distribution amplitude, with endpoint-dominated models failing to be dominated by short-distance contributions.

## Method

The authors performed full integrations over hard interaction kernels and distribution amplitudes in Feynman-x fractions and transverse spatial separation space b, including Sudakov effects depending on b and momentum transfer Q^2. Attenuation of hadronic states propagating through the nuclear medium was calculated using an eikonal Glauber formalism with explicit inclusion of nuclear correlations, and the transparency ratio was computed as the ratio of nuclear cross section to A times the free-space cross section.

## Key numbers

- At Q^2 = 5 GeV^2, approximately 90% of the contribution to the free-space pion form factor comes from a region where alpha_s/pi is less than 0.7, indicating perturbative reliability
- For finite momentum transfer K_T and K_z ranging from -25 MeV to 25 MeV, results for Au-197 changed by less than 10%: decreasing by a maximum of 7.7% for the pion and 8.3% for the proton
- The infrared cutoff parameter c = 1.14 was chosen for the proton calculation, following the work of Belitsky and Li
- The parameter k in the cross section parametrization sigma(b) = k b^2 was set to 10, determined self-consistently from agreement with SLAC proton data
- Nuclear correlation parameters used: alpha = 1.1, beta = 0.68 fm^{-2}, Fermi momentum k_F = 1.36 fm^{-1}
- Lambda_QCD = 200 MeV was used throughout the calculations

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD approach to color transparency pioneered by Mueller and Brodsky, and nuclear filtering proposed by Ralston and collaborators, by performing the first complete calculation including Sudakov effects following the formalism of Li and Sterman (1992). The calculations contradict earlier claims that pQCD is unreliable at accessible momentum transfers (Isgur, Radyushkin) by showing that nuclear filtering reduces long-distance contributions, making pQCD more reliable in nuclear targets than in free space. The results enable experimental tests of distribution amplitude models and suggest that uncertainties in nuclear spectral functions and correlations now dominate theoretical uncertainties, representing surprising progress compared to earlier work where infrared cutoff choices were the main concern.
