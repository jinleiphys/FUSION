# Trojan Horse as an indirect technique in nuclear astrophysics. Resonance reactions

> The Trojan Horse method is a powerful indirect technique that provides information to determine astrophysical factors for binary rearrangement processes $x + A \to b + B$ at astrophysically relevant energies by measuring the cross section for the Trojan Horse reaction $a + A \to y+ b + B$ in quasi-free kinematics. We present the theory of the Trojan Horse method for resonant binary subreactions based on the half-off-energy-shell R matrix approach which takes into account the off-energy-shell effects and initial and final state interactions.

## Key claim

The Trojan Horse method can reliably extract the astrophysical factor for resonant binary reactions by using a half-off-energy-shell R-matrix approach that accounts for off-shell effects and initial/final state interactions, provided the TH triple differential cross section is normalized to the on-energy-shell astrophysical factor at a resonance energy.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical formalism for the Trojan Horse method applied to resonant binary subreactions \(x + A \to b + B\). They start from the exact three-body reaction amplitude and introduce a spectral decomposition of the final-state wave function using a shell-model-based resonant R-matrix representation. The TH reaction amplitude is expressed as a sum over resonances, where the direct transfer amplitude \(a + A \to y + F_\tau\) populating the compound state \(F_\tau\) is approximated by the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA). The triple differential cross section for the TH process is then derived for both single isolated resonances and two interfering resonances, with the off-energy-shell character of the transferred particle \(x\) entering through the DWBA form factor.

## Key numbers

* For the \({}^{15}\mathrm{N}(p,\alpha)^{12}\mathrm{C}\) reaction, two interfering \(1^{-}\) resonances are analyzed at \(E_{R_1}=312\) keV and \(E_{R_2}=962\) keV.
* Best-fit resonance parameters for the first resonance: \(\Gamma_{xA(1)} \equiv \Gamma_{p(1)} = 1.1\) keV and \(\Gamma_{bB(1)} \equiv \Gamma_{\alpha(1)} = 93.4\) keV.
* Best-fit resonance parameters for the second resonance: \(\Gamma_{xA(2)} \equiv \Gamma_{p(2)} = 95.31\) keV and \(\Gamma_{bB(2)} \equiv \Gamma_{\alpha(2)} = 45\) keV.
* The ratio of DWBA amplitudes in the plane wave approximation is \(M_{21}^{0} \approx 1.13\), while the ratio of reduced widths \(\gamma_{(xA)21} = 1.1 \pm 0.1\).

## Context

This work extends the Trojan Horse method formalism of Baur (1986) and the surface approximation of Typel (2003) to resonant binary reactions, addressing concerns about off-shell effects and final-state interactions that had raised reservations about the method's reliability. The theoretical framework justifies the phenomenological procedures used in previous TH analyses (Spitaleri, 2004) and demonstrates that the plane wave approximation works well for the ratio of DWBA amplitudes. The formalism enables extraction of astrophysical factors for resonant reactions down to zero relative kinetic energy without distortion from electron screening, as demonstrated by the excellent agreement between the calculated TH astrophysical factor and direct measurements for the \({}^{15}\mathrm{N}(p,\alpha)^{12}\mathrm{C}\) reaction (Schardt, 1952; Zyskind, 1979; Redder, 1982).
