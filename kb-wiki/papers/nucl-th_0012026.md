# Structure and properties of neutron stars in the Relativistic Mean - Field Theory

> Properties of rotating neutron stars with the use of relativistic mean-field theory are considered. The performed analysis of neutron star matter is based on the nonlinear Lgrangian density. The presence of nonlinear interaction of vector mesons modifies the density dependence of the rho field and influences bulk parameters of neutron stars. The observed quasi-periodic X-ray oscillations of low mass X-ray binaries can be used in order to constrain the equation of state of neutron star matter. Having assumed that the maximum frequency of the quasi periodic oscillations originates at the circular orbit it is possible to estimate masses and radii of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the inclusion of nonlinear vector-isoscalar and vector-isovector meson interactions (specifically an omega-rho coupling term) in the relativistic mean-field theory modifies the density dependence of the rho field and the symmetry energy, which in turn influences the bulk parameters of neutron stars such as mass and radius; the authors then use the observed kilohertz quasi-periodic oscillations from low-mass X-ray binaries to constrain the equation of state, assuming the maximum QPO frequency originates at the innermost stable circular orbit.

## Method

The authors construct a composite equation of state for neutron star matter using the relativistic mean-field approximation with a Lagrangian density that includes nucleons, electrons, and scalar, vector-isoscalar, and vector-isovector meson fields, supplemented by a nonlinear omega-rho coupling term. They combine the RMF-based EOS for the high-density interior (using NL1, NL3, NL3s, and TM1 parameter sets) with the Negele-Vautherin EOS, the Machleidt-Holinde-Elster Bonn potential, and the Haensel-Pichon EOS for lower density regions. The structure of both nonrotating and rotating neutron stars is computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and using the Butterworth-Ipser numerical method for rapid rotation, respectively, and the Kepler frequency is estimated using the empirical relation of Haensel and Zdunik.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: rho_0 approximately 2.5 x 10^14 g/cm^3
- Density ranges for neutron star layers: 2 x 10^3 < rho < 1 x 10^11 g/cm^3 (light metals, electron gas); 1 x 10^11 < rho < 2 x 10^13 g/cm^3 (heavy metals, relativistic electron gas); 2 x 10^13 < rho < 5 x 10^15 g/cm^3 (nucleons, relativistic leptons)
- Composite EOS constructed by joining the RMF EOS with the Negele-Vautherin and Bonn EOS for the density range between 10^14 and 5 x 10^10 g/cm^3, and the Haensel-Pichon EOS for the density region 9.6 x 10^10 g/cm^3 to 3.3 x 10^7 g/cm^3
- Proton fraction values considered: Y_p = 0.07 and Y_p = 0.17
- Empirical relation for Kepler frequency: Omega_K approximately C_Hz sqrt((M_s / M_sun) / (R_s / 10 km)^3) = 0.6 (the numerical value 0.6 is given but its units are not specified in the truncated text)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach to neutron star structure by incorporating nonlinear vector meson interactions, following the proposal by Bodmer to achieve agreement with Dirac-Brueckner calculations at high densities. The inclusion of the omega-rho coupling was inspired by work indicating a relationship between the neutron-rich skin of heavy nuclei and neutron star crust properties. The paper enables the use of observed kilohertz quasi-periodic oscillations from low-mass X-ray binaries (discovered by the Rossi X-Ray Timing Explorer) to constrain the equation of state and estimate neutron star masses and radii, building on the framework of Haensel and Zdunik for Kepler frequency estimation.
