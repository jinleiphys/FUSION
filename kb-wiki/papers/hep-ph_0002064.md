# The Dark Side of the Solar Neutrino Parameter Space

> Results of neutrino oscillation experiments have always been presented on the (sin^2 2theta, Delta m^2) parameter space for the case of two-flavor oscillations. We point out, however, that this parameterization misses the half of the parameter space pi/4 < theta <= pi/2 (``the dark side''), which is physically inequivalent to the region 0 <= theta <= pi/4 (``the light side'') in the presence of matter effects. The MSW solutions to the solar neutrino problem can extend to the dark side, especially if we take the conservative attitude to allow higher confidence levels, ignore some of the experimental results in the fits, or relax theoretical predictions. Furthermore even the so-called ``vacuum oscillation'' solution distinguishes the dark and the light sides. We urge experimental collaborations to present their results on the entire parameter space.

# The Dark Side of the Solar Neutrino Parameter Space

## Key claim
The standard parameterization of two-flavor neutrino oscillations using ($\sin^2 2\theta$, $\Delta m^2$) with $0 \leq \theta \leq \pi/4$ (the "light side") is incomplete for solar neutrinos because matter effects make the region $\pi/4 < \theta \leq \pi/2$ (the "dark side") physically inequivalent; the MSW solutions to the solar neutrino problem can extend into the dark side, especially when one allows higher confidence levels, ignores some experimental results, or relaxes theoretical predictions.

## Method
The authors performed global fits to solar neutrino event rates from water Cherenkov detectors (Kamiokande and Super-Kamiokande), the chlorine target (Homestake), and gallium targets (GALLEX and SAGE) across the full parameter space $0 \leq \theta \leq \pi/2$ using $\tan^2 \theta$ as the mixing parameter. They computed the $\nu_e$ survival probability using the MSW matter effect formula with an exponential approximation for the solar electron density profile, accounted for Earth matter effects during the night, integrated over neutrino production regions in the Sun's core, and treated the transition between the MSW and vacuum oscillation regions continuously from $\Delta m^2 = 10^{-11}$ to $10^{-3}$ eV$^2$.

## Key numbers
- The global fit at 3$\sigma$ (99.7% CL) shows that both the LMA and LOW solutions extend into the dark side ($\tan^2 \theta > 1$); at 99% CL the LMA solution is confined to the light side.
- When the $^8$B flux is treated as a free parameter, the preferred region extends further into the dark side, with the range $3 \times 10^{-7} \lesssim \Delta m^2 \lesssim 10^{-5}$ eV$^2$ for $0.2 \lesssim \tan^2 \theta < 1$ potentially excluded by Super-Kamiokande day-night asymmetry data.
- When the Homestake experiment is excluded from the fit, the preferred region extends into the dark side even at 95% CL, with asymmetry between dark and light sides persisting for $\Delta m^2 < 10^{-9}$ eV$^2$.
- The vacuum oscillation region ($\Delta m^2 \lesssim 10^{-9}$ eV$^2$) shows asymmetry between light and dark sides for $\Delta m^2 \gtrsim 10^{-10}$ eV$^2$ due to matter effects on low-energy pp neutrinos.

## Context
This work extends the two-flavor oscillation analysis of solar neutrinos beyond the traditional $(\sin^2 2\theta, \Delta m^2)$ parameter space used by all previous experimental collaborations, correcting the misconception that physics is discontinuous at maximal mixing ($\theta = \pi/4$). The authors build on their earlier work (de Gouvea, Friedland, Murayama, 1999) showing continuity across $\theta = \pi/4$, and on three-flavor analyses by Fogli, Lisi, Montanino, and Palazzo (1999) and four-flavor analyses that had explored the dark side. The paper enables future experiments (Super-Kamiokande, SNO, Borexino, GNO, KamLAND) to report results on the full parameter space without theoretical bias toward the light side, and demonstrates that the artificial separation between "MSW region" and "vacuum oscillation region" in global fits is misleading.
