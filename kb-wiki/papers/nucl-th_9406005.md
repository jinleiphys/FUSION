# Color transparency after the NE18 and E665 experiments: Outllok and perspectives at CEBAF

> CEBAF is a high-luminocity factory of virtual photons with variable virtuality $Q^{2}$ and transverse size. This makes CEBAF, in particular after the energy upgrade to (8-12)GeV, an ideal facility for uncovering new phenomena, and opening new windows, at the interface of the perturbative and nonperturbative QCD. We discuss color transparency as the case for a broad program on electroproduction of vector mesons $ρ^{0},\,ω^{0},\,ϕ^{0}$ and their radial excitations $ρ',\,ω',\,ϕ'$ at CEBAF. We also comment on the second generation of experiments on color transparency in $^{4}He(e,e'p)$ scattering, which are also feasible at CEBAF. In 1994, we can make more reliable projections into future because our understanding of the onset of color transparency has greatly been augmented by two experiments completed in 1993:\\ i) no effect of CT was seen in the SLAC NE18 experiment on $A(e,e'p)$ scattering at virtualities of the exchanged photon $Q^{2} \lsim 7$ GeV$^{2}$, \\ ii) strong signal of CT was observed in the FNAL E665 experiment on exclusive $ρ^{0}$- meson production in deep inelastic scattering in the same range of $Q^{2}$. \\ We discuss the impact of these observations on the CEBAF experimental program. We argue they both are good news, both were anticipated theoretically, and both rule in the correct QCD mechanism of the onset of CT.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the apparently contradictory results from the NE18 experiment (no color transparency seen in A(e,e'p) scattering at Q^2 ≤ 7 GeV^2) and the E665 experiment (strong color transparency signal in exclusive ρ^0 production in the same Q^2 range) are both consistent with theoretical expectations, and that the correct QCD mechanism of color transparency onset is confirmed rather than ruled out by these results.

## Method

The authors use the lightcone dipole-cross section representation, where mesons are treated as color dipoles with transverse size r, and the dipole cross section σ(ν,r) describes interaction with target nucleons. They compute nuclear transparency for exclusive vector meson production by evaluating matrix elements of σ(r) between the virtual photon wave function Ψ_γ*(r,z) and the vector meson wave function Ψ_V(r,z), incorporating coherence length l_c and formation length l_f effects. For nuclear targets, they calculate the attenuation of the qqbar pair as it propagates through nuclear matter using the optical thickness T(b) and the observable Σ_V = ⟨V|σ(r)^2|γ*⟩/⟨V|σ(r)|γ*⟩.

## Key numbers

- NE18 experiment: no color transparency effects seen at Q^2 ≤ 7 GeV^2 in A(e,e'p) scattering on D, C, Fe, and Au targets
- E665 experiment: strong color transparency signal observed in exclusive ρ^0 production in the same Q^2 range (Q^2 ≤ 7 GeV^2)
- Scanning radius for vector meson production: r_S = 6/√(m_V^2 + Q^2) (in femtometers)
- Radius of J/Ψ: R_J/Ψ ≈ 0.45 fm, with σ_tot(J/Ψ N) ≈ 5-6 mb
- For ρ^0 production at ⟨Q^2⟩ = 7 GeV^2, E665 gives T_Pb/T_C = 0.6 ± 0.25
- For real photoproduction of J/Ψ, NMC gives T_Sn/T_C = 0.7 ± 0.1
- Coherent production ratios for J/Ψ: R_coh(Sn/C) = 2.15 ± 0.10 (NMC), R_coh(Fe/Be) = 2.28 ± 0.32 and R_coh(Pb/Be) = 3.47 ± 0.50 (E691)
- Predicted no-FSI ratios: R_coh(Sn/C) = 2.76, R_coh(Fe/Be) = 2.82, R_coh(Pb/Be) = 4.79
- Formation length for ρ^0: l_f ≈ 0.4 fm · (ν/1 GeV)
- For fully developed CT: ν ≥ (3-4)·A^(1/3) GeV
- Ratio σ(γN → Ψ' N)/σ(γN → J/Ψ N) = 0.17 (theory) vs 0.20 ± 0.05 ± 0.07 (NMC experiment)

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical framework for color transparency developed by the authors (Nemchik, Nikolaev, Zakharov) in prior work (1992-1994), which predicted a slow onset of CT in A(e,e'p) scattering and precocious CT in vector meson production. The NE18 experiment (1993) contradicted earlier predictions of precocious CT in quasielastic scattering by Farrar, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1988), while the E665 experiment (1993) confirmed the authors' predictions for vector meson production. The paper enables a concrete experimental program at CEBAF (particularly after the 8-12 GeV energy upgrade) for exclusive electroproduction of ρ^0, ω^0, φ^0 and their radial excitations, as well as second-generation experiments on color transparency in ^4He(e,e'p) scattering.
