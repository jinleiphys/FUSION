# Multi-hadron molecules: status and prospect

> Starting from 2003, the discovery of a large amount of the so-called exotic hadronic states, i.e., the $XYZ$ states, the pentaquark states as well as the tetraquark states, have not only revived studies of hadron spectroscopy, but also hinted at the existence of new multi-hadron states made of hadrons other than nucleons and hyperons. We briefly comment on some of the latest studies on multi-hadron molecules in the light and heavy flavor sectors and highlight what should be done in the future.

## Key claim

The paper argues that studying multi-hadron molecules (three-body or more) built from known two-body molecular candidates can provide a highly nontrivial check on the molecular picture of exotic hadrons, but notes that the existence of a two-body bound state is a necessary but not sufficient condition for the corresponding three-body system to bind, and that factors such as reduced mass and quantum number configurations also play a role.

## Method

The authors review theoretical studies that use known two-body hadron-hadron interactions (inferred from molecular candidates such as D_{s0}*(2317), X(3872), P_c(4312), and T_{cc}+(3875)) to solve three-body Schrödinger equations via methods such as the Gaussian expansion method, Faddeev equations, variational methods, and fixed center approximation. They then compute binding energies and partial decay widths of the predicted three-body bound states using triangle diagrams and other decay mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- The K^-pp quasibound state (lightest K-nuclear state) has isospin I=1/2 and spin-parity J^P=0^-, with binding energies ranging from 9-23 MeV in unitary chiral models and 40-80 MeV in phenomenological models, and a large width of about 30-90 MeV
- The DDK three-body bound state has binding energy ~70 MeV and decays to DD_s* (~0.3 MeV width) and D*D_s (~2.4 MeV width)
- The D\bar{D}K three-body bound state has binding energy ~49 MeV and decays to D_s\bar{D}* (~0.5 MeV width) and J/psi K (~0.2 MeV width)
- The D\bar{D}*K three-body bound state has binding energy ~77 MeV and decays to D_s^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)} (~1.9 MeV width) and J/psi K*(892) (~6.7 MeV width)
- The Sigma_c\bar{D}\bar{K} three-body bound state has binding energy ~78 MeV and decays to D\Xi' (~90 MeV width) and D_s\Sigma_c (~20 MeV width)
- The DDD* three-body bound state has binding energy ~1 MeV
- The D_{s0}*(2317) is located 45 MeV below the DK threshold with a decay width less than 3.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of two-body exotic hadrons (such as X(3872), D_{s0}*(2317), the P_c pentaquark states observed by LHCb in 2015 and 2019, and T_{cc}+(3875) observed by LHCb in 2021) into the three-body and multi-body domain, following the same logic used to understand atomic nuclei and hypernuclei as bound states of nucleons and hyperons. The authors note that the multi-rho study by Roca (2010) showed that resonances f_2(1270) through f_6(2510) can be understood as molecules of increasing numbers of rho(770) mesons. The predicted multi-hadron states, such as DDK, have already been searched for by the Belle Collaboration in e+e- collisions, and the paper calls for more experimental searches at LHCb, BEPC, KEKb, and future facilities to confirm or refute the molecular picture of exotic hadrons.
