# Periodic orbit theory including spin degrees of freedom

> We summarize recent developments of the semiclassical description of shell effects in finite fermion systems with explicit inclusion of spin degrees of freedom, in particluar in the presence of spin-orbit interactions. We present a new approach that makes use of spin coherent states and a correspondingly enlarged classical phase space. Taking suitable limits, we can recover some of the earlier approaches. Applications to some model systems are presented.

# Periodic orbit theory including spin degrees of freedom

## Key claim

The paper presents a new semiclassical approach using spin coherent states and an extended classical phase space that includes spin degrees of freedom, which is free of the mode conversion problem that plagues earlier strong-coupling-limit treatments; however, the approach suffers from a large number of bifurcations of periodic orbits under variations of coupling strength, energy, and deformation parameters, limiting its practical applicability without further approximations.

## Method

The authors construct a semiclassical trace formula by introducing spin coherent states to define classical spin components and enlarging the classical phase space by one pair of canonical variables (u,v), independently of the spin value s. The dynamics in this extended phase space is governed by a Hamiltonian that explicitly couples orbital degrees of freedom (r,p) with spin degrees of freedom (u,v), and periodic orbits in this enlarged space are used as input into the Gutzwiller trace formula. The approach is applied to a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator with Thomas-type spin-orbit interaction (Nilsson-type Hamiltonian) and to a two-dimensional semiconductor quantum dot with Rashba spin-orbit interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator with deformation parameters α=0.1212, β=2 and spin-orbit strength κ=0.1 ω₀⁻¹, the semiclassical shell correction to the level density using only six primitive planar orbits shows excellent agreement with quantum mechanics, with coarse-graining over γ=0.5 ħω₀.
- For the two-dimensional quantum dot with Rashba interaction (ωx=1.56 ω₀, ωy=1.23 ω₀), a set of 12 shortest periodic orbits in the extended phase space were found for 0 < κ ≲ 0.7, including two pairs of librating orbits A±x and A±y with fully polarized spin and four pairs of oscillating orbits D±x1, D±x2, D±y1, D±y2.
- For the quantum dot at κ=0.67 and energy E=60 (in units where ħ=m*=ω₀=1), the semiclassical extended-phase-space result reproduces the phases of quantum oscillations well, though amplitudes are too large, while the weak-coupling-limit result is identical to ignoring spin-orbit interaction and multiplying by a spin factor of two.

## Context

This work extends the periodic orbit theory (POT) originally developed by Gutzwiller (1970) and previously applied to nuclear shell effects by Strutinsky and collaborators (1975) and to fission barriers in actinide nuclei by Brack and collaborators (1998). The paper addresses the long-standing problem of including spin-orbit interactions semiclassically, which was previously treated via the strong coupling limit (SCL) of Littlejohn and Flynn (1991) suffering from mode conversion singularities, and the weak coupling limit (WCL) of Bolte and Keppeler (1999) valid only for weak spin-orbit couplings. The new extended-phase-space approach enables future applications to realistic nuclear shell-model potentials with spin-orbit interactions and to mesoscopic transport phenomena such as weak anti-localization.
