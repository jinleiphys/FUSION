# Origin of the second peak in the cross section of the $K^+Λ$ photoproduction

> By using a covariant isobar model and the latest experimental data we have analyzed the role of the $P_{13}(1900)$ and $D_{13}(2080)$ resonances in the kaon photoproduction process $γp\to K^+Λ$. Special attention has been paid to the region where the second peak in the cross section is located, i.e. at total c.m. energies around 1.9 GeV. It is found that this peak originates mostly from the $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance contribution. Although the contribution of the $D_{13}(2080)$ resonance is not negligible, it is much smaller than that of the $P_{13}(1900)$ state. Our finding confirms that the $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance is also important in explaining the beam-recoil double polarization data $C_x$ and $C_z$, provided that the mass and the width of this resonance are 1871 and 131 MeV, respectively.

## Key claim
The second peak in the $\gamma p\to K^+\Lambda$ cross section at total c.m. energy around 1.9 GeV originates mostly from the $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance, not from the $D_{13}(2080)$ resonance as previously claimed by the Kaon-Maid model. However, the evidence for a $D_{13}$ resonance with mass around 1900 MeV is not rejected, only shown to be weak.

## Method
A covariant isobar model was constructed from Feynman diagrams including background terms (Born terms, $K^{*+}(892)$ and $K_1(1270)$ $t$-channel vector mesons, hyperon resonances $S_{01}(1800)$ and $P_{01}(1810)$) and nine nucleon resonances up to spin 3/2. The model was fitted to a database of 3566 data points (differential cross sections, recoil polarization, beam-recoil double polarizations $C_x$, $C_z$, $O_{x'}$, $O_{z'}$, photon asymmetry $\Sigma$, and target asymmetry $T$) from CLAS, LEPS, and GRAAL collaborations. Two fits were performed: Model A fixed the $P_{13}(1900)$ mass and width to PDG values (1900 MeV, 180 MeV) while fitting the $D_{13}(2080)$ parameters; Model B fixed the $D_{13}(2080)$ to PDG values (2080 MeV, 450 MeV) while fitting the $P_{13}(1900)$ parameters.

## Key numbers
- The fitted mass of the $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance in Model B is 1871 MeV with a width of 131 MeV.
- The fitted mass of the $D_{13}(2080)$ resonance in Model A is 1886 MeV with a width of 244 MeV.
- The $\chi^2/N_{\rm dof}$ for Model A is 2.57; for Model B it is 2.68.
- Without the $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance, the best $\chi^2/N_{\rm dof}$ obtained is 3.52.
- The $C_x$ and $C_z$ data (202 data points up to 2.2 GeV) contribute $\chi^2\approx 1832$ without the $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance, $\chi^2\approx 1414$ in Model A, and $\chi^2\approx 756$ in Model B.
- The Kaon-Maid model was fitted to only 319 data points, compared to 3566 in the present work.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the second peak in $\gamma p\to K^+\Lambda$ first observed by the SAPHIR Collaboration (Tran, 1998) and previously interpreted as evidence for a "missing" $D_{13}(1895)$ resonance in the Kaon-Maid model. It confirms the finding of the Bonn-Gatchina group (Nikonov, 2007) that the $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance is responsible for the structure, using a covariant isobar model rather than coupled-channels partial wave analysis. The result is also consistent with a single-channel covariant isobar model study (Maxwell) and a coupled-channels study (Julia-Diaz, 2006). The paper enables future dynamical coupled-channels studies that fully account for final state interaction effects, which are needed to settle the question definitively.
