# Microscopic description of cluster radioactivity in actinide nuclei

> Cluster radioactivity is the emission of a fragment heavier than $α$ particle and lighter than mass 50. The range of clusters observed in experiments goes from $^{14}$C to $^{32}$Si while the heavy mass residue is always a nucleus in the neighborhood of the doubly-magic $^{208}$Pb nucleus. Cluster radioactivity is described in this paper as a very asymmetric nuclear fission. A new fission valley leading to a decay with large fragment mass asymmetry matching the cluster radioactivity products is found. The mass octupole moment is found to be more convenient than the standard quadrupole moment as the parameter driving the system to fission. The mean-field HFB theory with the phenomenological Gogny interaction has been used to compute the cluster emission properties of a wide range of even-even actinide nuclei from $^{222}$Ra to $^{242}$Cm, where emission of the clusters has been experimentally observed. Computed half-lives for cluster emission are compared with experimental results. The noticeable agreement obtained between the predicted properties of cluster emission (namely, clusters masses and emission half-lives) and the measured data confirms the validity of the proposed methodology in the analysis of the phenomenon of cluster radioactivity. A continuous fission path through the scission point has been described using the neck parameter constraint.

## Key claim
Cluster radioactivity in actinide nuclei can be fully described microscopically as a very asymmetric fission process, using the octupole moment rather than the standard quadrupole moment as the driving coordinate. The authors find that the agreement between predicted and experimental half-lives validates this methodology, but the results depend on a phenomenological zero-point energy parameter set to 0.5 MeV for all isotopes.

## Method
The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field equations with the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction, constraining the quadrupole, octupole, and hexadecapole moments to map the potential energy surface. They identified a new "hyper-asymmetric" fission valley leading to cluster radioactivity products, tracked the fission path using the octupole moment as the leading coordinate, and computed half-lives via the WKB approximation with collective inertia from the ATDHFB cranking approximation.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state quadrupole moment of 224Ra: Q2 = 8.3 b (beta2 = 0.18)
- Ground-state quadrupole moment of 238Pu: Q2 = 14.1 b (beta2 = 0.27)
- Ground-state octupole moment of 224Ra: Q3 = 4.2 b^(3/2) (beta3 = 0.14)
- Hyper-asymmetric fission barrier height: approximately 25 MeV above ground state for 224Ra and 238Pu
- Classical fission barrier height: approximately 10 MeV for both nuclei
- Half-lives for cluster emission computed using the formula t_(1/2) = 2.86 x 10^(-21) (1 + exp(2S)) seconds
- Zero-point energy parameter set to 0.5 MeV for all isotopes considered

## Context
This work extends the fission-asymmetry description of cluster radioactivity first proposed by Sandulescu et al. (1980) and applies it to all even-even actinide nuclei where cluster emission has been experimentally observed, from 222Ra to 242Cm. The microscopic HFB approach with Gogny forces had previously been used to describe standard fission in heavy nuclei by Warda et al. (2002, 2005) and others; this paper identifies a new hyper-asymmetric fission valley that produces the cluster masses observed in experiments. The results enable future microscopic calculations of cluster emission half-lives without relying on the poorly understood cluster preformation factor used in Gamow-type models.
