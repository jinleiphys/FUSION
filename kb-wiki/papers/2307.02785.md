# Effects of Hoyle state de-excitation on $νp$-process nucleosynthesis and Galactic chemical evolution

> The partcle-induced hadronic de-excitation of the Hoyle state in $^{12}$C induced by inelastic scattering in a hot and dense plasma can enhance the triple-alpha reaction rate. This prevents the production of heavy nuclei within the neutrino-driven winds of core-collapse supernovae and raises a question as to the contribution of proton-rich neutrino-driven winds as the origin of $p$-nuclei in the solar system abundances. Here we study $νp$-process nucleosynthesis in proton-rich neutrino-driven winds relevant to the production of $^{92,94}\mathrm{Mo}$ and $^{96,98}\mathrm{Ru}$ by considering such particle-induced de-excitation. We show that the enhancement of the triple-alpha reaction rate induced by neutron inelastic scattering hardly affects the $νp$-process, while the proton scattering contributes to the nucleosynthesis in proton-rich neutrino-driven winds at low temperature. The associated enhanced triple-alpha reaction rate decreases the production of $^{92,94}\mathrm{Mo}$ and $^{96,98}\mathrm{Ru}$ in a wind model of ordinary core-collapse supernovae. On the other hand, the abundances of these $p$-nuclei increase in an energetic hypernova wind model. Hence, we calculate the galactic chemical evolution of $^{92,94}\mathrm{Mo}$ and $^{96,98}\mathrm{Ru}$ by taking account of both contributions from core-collapse supernovae and hypernovae. We show that the hypernova $νp$-process can enhance the calculated solar isotopic fractions of $^{92,94}\mathrm{Mo}$ and $^{96,98}\mathrm{Ru}$ and make a significant impact on the GCE of $p$-nuclei regardless of the particle-induced Hoyle state de-excitation.

## Key claim

The particle-induced hadronic de-excitation of the Hoyle state enhances the triple-alpha reaction rate, but its effect on the $\nu p$-process depends critically on the astrophysical site: in ordinary core-collapse supernova winds it suppresses heavy-element production, while in energetic hypernova winds it actually increases the yields of $^{92,94}\mathrm{Mo}$ and $^{96,98}\mathrm{Ru}$ by 20-30%, and the hypernova contribution dominates Galactic chemical evolution regardless of this enhancement.

## Method

The authors calculated $\nu p$-process nucleosynthesis in proton-rich neutrino-driven winds using general-relativistic steady-state, spherically symmetric wind trajectories for both a standard core-collapse supernova (SN II) model and an energetic hypernova (HN) model, with the enhanced triple-alpha reaction rate from the Hauser-Feshbach model of Beard (2017) implemented via the LIBNUCNET reaction network engine with JINA Reaclib rates. They then performed Galactic chemical evolution calculations using the model of Timmes (1995), incorporating contributions from the $\gamma$-process in both SNe Ia and SNe II and the $\nu p$-process in both SNe II and HNe, to compute the evolution of $^{92,94}\mathrm{Mo}$ and $^{96,98}\mathrm{Ru}$ abundances.

## Key numbers

- The SN II wind model has entropy per baryon $S = 55.2\ k_B\ \mathrm{nuc}^{-1}$, expansion timescale $\tau_{\mathrm{dyn}} = 16.9$ ms, and mass ejection rate $\dot{M} = 5.62 \times 10^{-4}\ M_\odot/\mathrm{s}$.
- The HN wind model has $S = 125\ k_B\ \mathrm{nuc}^{-1}$, $\tau_{\mathrm{dyn}} = 5.18$ ms, and $\dot{M} = 4.46 \times 10^{-3}\ M_\odot/\mathrm{s}$, with a proto-neutron star mass of $3\ M_\odot$ and neutrino luminosity $L_\nu = 10^{53}$ erg/s.
- In the SN II wind model, the enhanced triple-alpha reaction suppresses the mass fractions of $^{92,94}\mathrm{Mo}$ and $^{96,98}\mathrm{Ru}$ by about a factor of 100.
- In the HN wind model, the mass fractions of $^{92,94}\mathrm{Mo}$ and $^{96,98}\mathrm{Ru}$ increase by 20-30% when the enhanced triple-alpha reaction is included.
- The neutron-induced enhancement of the triple-alpha rate is negligible for the $\nu p$-process; the proton-induced term dominates at temperatures $2 < T_9 < 6$.

## Context

This work extends the study of Jin (2020), who reported that enhanced triple-alpha from Hoyle state de-excitation suppresses the $\nu p$-process in standard supernova winds, by showing that the effect is reversed in hypernova winds due to their higher entropy and shorter expansion timescale. It builds on the Galactic chemical evolution model of Sasaki (2022) that demonstrated hypernova $\nu p$-process can dominate the production of $^{92,94}\mathrm{Mo}$ and $^{96,98}\mathrm{Ru}$, and now shows this conclusion holds even when including the Hoyle state de-excitation. The results enable more quantitative comparisons between $\nu p$-process predictions and meteoritic molybdenum isotopic anomalies, such as those reported by Dauphas (2002) and Budde (2016).
