# Cluster-Daughter Overlap as a New Probe of Alpha-Cluster Formation in Medium-Mass and Heavy Even-Even Nuclei

> We study the possibility to use the cluster-daughter overlap as a new probe of alpha-cluster formation in medium-mass and heavy even-even nuclei. We introduce a dimensionless parameter $\mathfrak{O}$, which is the ratio between the root-mean-square (rms) intercluster separation and the sum of rms point radii of the daughter nucleus and the alpha particle, to measure the degree of the cluster-daughter overlap quantitatively. By using this parameter, a large (small) cluster-daughter overlap between the alpha cluster and daughter nucleus corresponds to a small (large) $\mathfrak{O}$ value. The alpha-cluster formation is shown explicitly, in the framework of the quartetting wave function approach, to be suppressed when the $\mathfrak{O}$ parameter is small, and be favored when the $\mathfrak{O}$ parameter is large. We then use this $\mathfrak{O}$ parameter to explore systematically the landscape of alpha-cluster formation probabilities in medium-mass and heavy even-even nuclei, with $\mathfrak{O}$ being calculated from experimentally measured charge radii. The trends of alpha-cluster formation probabilities are found to be generally consistent with previous studies. The effects of various shell closures on the alpha-cluster formation are identified, along with some hints on a possible subshell structure at $N=106$ along the Hg and Pb isotopic chains. The study here could be a useful complement to the traditional route to probe alpha-cluster formation in medium-mass and heavy even-even nuclei using alpha-decay data. Especially, it would be helpful in the cases where the target nucleus is stable against alpha decay or alpha-decay data are currently not available.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the dimensionless parameter $\mathfrak{O}$ (the ratio between the rms intercluster separation and the sum of the rms point radii of the daughter nucleus and the alpha particle) can serve as a new probe of alpha-cluster formation probabilities in medium-mass and heavy even-even nuclei, with the caveat that the relation between $\mathfrak{O}$ and the alpha-cluster formation probability $P_\alpha$ is approximately linear and positively correlated, as demonstrated within the quartetting wave function approach for the specific case of $^{212}$Po.

## Method

The authors introduce a dimensionless parameter $\mathfrak{O} = R_i / (R_\alpha + R_d)$, where $R_i$ is the rms intercluster separation, $R_\alpha$ is the rms point radius of the alpha particle, and $R_d$ is the rms point radius of the daughter nucleus. They extract $R_i$ from experimentally measured ground-state charge radii of parent, daughter, and alpha particle using a master formula derived from the definition of the rms charge radius of the parent nucleus. The $\mathfrak{O}$ parameter is then calculated for isotopic and isotonic chains of medium-mass and heavy even-even nuclei, and the resulting landscapes are interpreted as proxies for alpha-cluster formation probabilities based on a linear relation established within the quartetting wave function approach for $^{212}$Po.

## Key numbers

- The Mott density used in the quartetting wave function approach is $\rho_B^\text{Mott} = 0.02917 \text{ fm}^{-3}$, above which the alpha cluster dissolves.
- For the $^{212}$Po case study, the critical radius marking alpha-cluster dissolution is $r_\text{cluster} = 7.43825$ fm.
- The intrinsic energy of the alpha particle in vacuum is $E_\alpha^{(0)} = -28.3$ MeV.
- The rms point radii are obtained from experimental charge radii using $R_\text{point} = \sqrt{R^2_\text{ch} - R_o^2}$ with $R_o = 0.88$ fm.
- The $\mathfrak{O}$ parameter is calculated for isotopic chains of Te, Xe, Ba, Hg, Pb, Po, Rn, and Ra, and for isotonic chains near $N=64$, $66$, $114$, $116$, $118$, $120$ and $Z=50$, $82$.

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of alpha-cluster formation that traditionally rely on alpha-decay data, such as those by Batchelder (1997) and Xu (2017), by instead using experimentally measured charge radii. It provides a complementary probe particularly useful when target nuclei are stable against alpha decay or when alpha-decay data are unavailable. The results are consistent with previous studies on shell effects at $N=82$, $N=126$, $Z=50$, and $Z=82$, and additionally identify a possible subshell structure at $N=106$ along the Hg and Pb isotopic chains, which could motivate future experiments to measure charge radii in these regions.
