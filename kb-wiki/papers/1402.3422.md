# Observation of anti-magnetic rotations in 104Pd

> The electric quadrupole transition rates for the high spin yrast states of 104 Pd have been measured by using the DSAM technique. These values decrease with the increase of angular momentum which is a signature of anti-magnetic rotation. A numerical calcula- tion based on semi-classical particle plus rotor model for anti-magnetic rotation gives a good description of the experimental routhian and the transition rates. This is the first observation of AMR in a nucleus other than Cadmium.

## Key claim

This paper reports the first observation of anti-magnetic rotation (AMR) in a nucleus other than Cadmium, specifically in \(^{104}\)Pd, based on measured electric quadrupole transition rates that decrease with increasing angular momentum. The claim is hedged by the fact that the identification of AMR relies on lifetime measurements of high-spin yrast states and agreement with a semi-classical particle-plus-rotor model whose interaction strengths were assumed to be the same as those found in Cd isotopes rather than independently determined for \(^{104}\)Pd.

## Method

The authors populated high-spin states in \(^{104}\)Pd via a \(^{13}\)C beam at 63 MeV on a \(^{96}\)Zr target with a \(^{206}\)Pb backing, and detected de-exciting gamma rays using the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) of 18 Compton-suppressed clover detectors. Level lifetimes for states above \(I = 14\hbar\) were measured using the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method (DSAM), with lineshapes extracted from 803-keV gated spectra at forward (\(40^\circ\)) and backward (\(157^\circ\)) angles and fitted using the code LINESHAPE with Monte Carlo simulations of the slowing-down process. The B(E2) transition rates were then derived from the measured lifetimes, and the experimental routhian and transition rates were compared to a semi-classical particle-plus-rotor model calculation for AMR.

## Key numbers

- The measured lifetime of the \(16^+\) state is 0.45(3) ps, corresponding to B(E2) = 0.26(2) \((eb)^2\).
- The measured lifetime of the \(26^+\) state is 0.66(3) ps, corresponding to B(E2) = 0.02(1) \((eb)^2\).
- The B(E2) value decreases monotonically from 0.26(2) \((eb)^2\) at \(16^+\) to 0.02(1) \((eb)^2\) at \(26^+\) over the spin range \(16\hbar \leq I \leq 26\hbar\).
- The \( \mathcal{J}^{(2)}/\)B(E2) ratio for the \(24^+\) state is 554.3(79.2) \(\hbar^2\) MeV\(^{-1}\) \((eb)^{-2}\), an order of magnitude larger than for a well-deformed collective rotor.
- The maximum angular momentum from the shears mechanism \(I_{sh}^{max}\) is \(24\hbar\), while the highest observed state is \(26\hbar\), giving \(\Im \omega_{sh}|_{(\theta=0^\circ)} = 2\hbar\) and \(\Im = 5\) MeV\(^{-1}\hbar^2\).
- The effective quadrupole moment \(eQ_{eff}\) used in the B(E2) calculation is 1.3 \(eb\), derived from Nilsson states with Woods-Saxon potential at \(\beta_2 = 0.19\).

## Context

This work extends the observation of anti-magnetic rotation from the even-even Cd isotopes \(^{106}\)Cd (Simons et al.) and \(^{108}\)Cd (Datta et al.) to the neighboring even-even nucleus \(^{104}\)Pd, which is the isotone of \(^{106}\)Cd. The study builds on the semi-classical particle-rotor model previously applied to Cd isotopes by Roy et al. (2011) and uses the same interaction strengths (\(V_{\pi\nu} = 1.2\) MeV, \(V_{\pi\pi} = 0.15\)–0.2 MeV) determined from Cd systematics. The results establish AMR as a general mechanism for generating high angular momentum in nuclei beyond the Cd isotopic chain, and the measured B(E2) values and \( \mathcal{J}^{(2)}/\)B(E2) ratios provide definitive evidence that distinguishes pure AMR from the interplay between collective rotation and AMR seen in \(^{110}\)Cd.
