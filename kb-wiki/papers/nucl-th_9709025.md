# Transverse Energy Evolution as a Test of Parton Cascade Models

> We propose a test of Monte Carlo Parton Cascade models based on analytic solutions of covariant kinetic theory for longitudinally boost and transverse translation invariant boundary conditions. We compute the evolution of the transverse energy per unit rapidity for typical mini-jet initial conditions expected in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions. The kinetic theory solutions under these conditions test the models severely because they deviate strongly from free-streaming and also from ideal Euler and dissipative Navier-Stokes hydrodynamical approximations. We show that the newly formulated ZPC model passes this test. In addition, we show that the initial mini-jet density would need to be approximately four times higher than estimated with the HIJING generator in central Au+Au collisions at c.m. energies 200 AGeV in order that parton cascade dynamics can be approximated by Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the evolution of transverse energy per unit rapidity under longitudinally boost-invariant and transversely translation-invariant boundary conditions can serve as a test of Monte Carlo Parton Cascade models, and demonstrates that the ZPC model passes this test; however, the abstract glosses over the condition that this test is valid only for the specific case of thermal initial conditions and within the relaxation time approximation, and that the Navier-Stokes approximation only becomes applicable when the initial mini-jet density is approximately four times higher than HIJING estimates for central Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV.

## Method

The authors derived analytic solutions of covariant kinetic theory using the relaxation time approximation for the collision term, assuming longitudinal boost invariance and transverse translation invariance. They formulated integral equations for the proper energy density and transverse energy per unit rapidity evolution, solved them numerically, and compared the results to both Euler and Navier-Stokes hydrodynamic approximations as well as to the ZPC parton cascade model. The initial conditions were based on HIJING event generator estimates for central Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV center-of-mass energy.

## Key numbers

- Initial transverse energy per unit rapidity at proper time tau_0 = 0.2 fm: 484 GeV
- Initial parton density at tau_0 = 0.2 fm: 20/fm^3
- For transport cross sections sigma_t < 2 mb (corresponding to coupling parameter alpha < 0.8), the Navier-Stokes approximation fails badly
- The initial mini-jet density must be approximately four times higher than HIJING estimates for central Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV to approach Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics
- The shear viscosity coefficient in the Navier-Stokes limit is eta = (4/15) * tau_c * epsilon for constant relaxation time, and eta = (4/15) * (tau * epsilon / alpha) for scaling relaxation time

## Context

This work extends the analytic solutions of relativistic Boltzmann kinetic theory found by Baym (1984), Kajantie and Matsui (1985), and Gavin (1991) for the evolution of energy density under boost-invariant boundary conditions, applying them to initial conditions relevant to RHIC energy domain. The paper provides a standardized test for Monte Carlo parton cascade models being developed in the Open Standard Codes and Routines (OSCAR) format, and demonstrates that the ZPC model (Zhang, Pang, and Gyulassy, 1997) reproduces the analytic kinetic theory solutions. This enables rigorous testing of parton cascade algorithms and helps determine whether observables such as transverse energy can be interpreted in terms of thermodynamic concepts, particularly given that pQCD estimates suggest very high viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma.
