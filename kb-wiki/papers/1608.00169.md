# A short summary on the search of trineutron and tetraneutron

> In light of a new experiment which claims an identification of tetraneutron [3], we discuss the results of experimental search of trineutron and tetraneutron in different nuclear reactions. A summary of theoretical studies for trineutron and tetraneutron within variety of approaches such as variational methods, the method of Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, and the method of hyperspherical harmonics are presented.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the experimental and theoretical status of trineutron and tetraneutron searches, concluding that while no bound trineutron has been found and most theoretical work excludes a bound tetraneutron with realistic nuclear forces, a candidate resonant tetraneutron state at 0.83 MeV above threshold was reported in 2016, and a recent calculation with the JISP16 potential predicts a low-energy resonance near 0.8 MeV that is compatible with that observation.

## Method

The author surveys experimental searches for trineutrons and tetraneutrons using reactions such as pion double charge exchange on 3He and 4He, heavy-ion transfer reactions, breakup of 14Be, and alpha-particle induced fission of 238U. Theoretical approaches reviewed include variational methods, Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, the hyperspherical harmonics method, and complex scaling, applied with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials such as Reid soft core, Argonne AV8', and JISP16.

## Key numbers

- A candidate resonant tetraneutron state was found at 0.83 ± 0.65(stat) ± 1.25(syst) MeV above the four-neutron decay threshold in the 4He(8He,8Be) reaction at 186 MeV/u (Kisamori 2016).
- A calculation with the JISP16 potential predicts a 4n resonance near 0.8 MeV above threshold with a width of about 1.4 MeV (Shirokov 2016).
- For the trineutron, a calculation predicts a resonance state with a width of 13 MeV (Csoto 1996).
- Upper limits on 6n and/or 8n emission from 238U cluster decay relative to alpha decay are λ(6n)/λ_α ≤ 9.3×10^{-9} and λ(8n)/λ_α ≤ 3.6×10^{-10} at 90% confidence level (Bystritskiy).
- A calculation using the Faddeev method with the Reid potential found that the interaction must be enhanced by about a factor of 4 to bind 3n near zero energy (Glockle 1979).

## Context

This review summarizes over fifty years of experimental and theoretical work on multineutron systems, extending earlier compilations by Fiarman (1975) and Tilley (1987) and responding to the recent tetraneutron claim by Kisamori (2016). The existence of bound or resonant trineutrons and tetraneutrons would challenge modern realistic models of the nucleon-nucleon force and three-nucleon interaction, and would have implications for neutron star structure and superfluidity. The paper enables future searches, such as the proposed high-energy pion double charge exchange experiment on 4He at J-PARC (Fujioka 2016), and motivates further theoretical work on whether multineutron systems can exist without unrealistic modifications to nuclear forces.
