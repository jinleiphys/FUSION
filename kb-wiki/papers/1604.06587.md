# From dilute matter to the equilibrium point in the energy--density--functional theory

> Due to the large value of the scattering length in nuclear systems, standard density--functional theories based on effective interactions usually fail to reproduce the nuclear Fermi liquid behavior both at very low densities and close to equilibrium. Guided on one side by the success of the Skyrme density functional and, on the other side, by resummation techniques used in Effective Field Theories for systems with large scattering lengths, a new energy--density functional is proposed. This functional, adjusted on microscopic calculations, reproduces the nuclear equations of state of neutron and symmetric matter at various densities. Furthermore, it provides reasonable saturation properties as well as an appropriate density dependence for the symmetry energy.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new local energy-density functional (YGLO) that, unlike standard Skyrme or Gogny functionals, simultaneously reproduces the correct low-density behavior of neutron matter (via the Lee-Yang expansion) and the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter, but only by using a resummation technique that treats the scattering length as a fixed physical parameter rather than a free fit parameter.

## Method
The authors construct a density functional with a resummed denominator term (inspired by Effective Field Theory resummation techniques) that reproduces the first two terms of the Lee-Yang expansion at low densities, combined with explicit \(\rho^{5/3}\) and \(\rho^{\alpha+1}\) terms mimicking velocity-dependent and density-dependent Skyrme interactions. The functional parameters are adjusted to reproduce benchmark microscopic equations of state: Quantum Monte Carlo AV4 results for neutron matter at low densities, and Friedman-Pandharipande or Akmal et al. results at higher densities, with symmetric matter fitted to Friedman-Pandharipande points.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-neutron \(^1S_0\) scattering length fixed at \(a = -18.9\) fm; symmetric matter uses an average \(a \simeq -20\) fm
- YGLO saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.1683\) fm\(^{-3}\) with energy \(E/A = -15.9\) MeV
- Incompressibility modulus: \(K = 261.71\) MeV
- Incompressibility modulus from the mean-field fit (SLy5-MF benchmark): 240.52 MeV
- Parameter \(\alpha = 0.7\) used in all YGLO fits
- For neutron matter YGLO (FP): \(C_\beta = 100.87\) fm\(^2\), \(D_\beta = -9264.18\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\), \(F_\beta = 9571.90\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{3+3\alpha}\)
- For neutron matter YGLO (Akmal): \(C_\beta = 70.19\) fm\(^2\), \(D_\beta = -8377.83\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\), \(F_\beta = 8743.85\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{3+3\alpha}\)
- For symmetric matter YGLO (FP): \(C_\beta = 8.188\) fm\(^2\), \(D_\beta = -6624.87\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^5\), \(F_\beta = 6995.46\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{3+3\alpha}\)

## Context
This work extends efforts to connect phenomenological energy-density functionals (EDFs) with Effective Field Theory (EFT) approaches, following earlier work by Furnstahl (2012) and the Dyson expansion framework of Fetter and Walecka. It addresses the known failure of standard Skyrme functionals (e.g., SLy5) and Gogny functionals to reproduce the low-density Lee-Yang expansion, while also improving upon earlier attempts that required unphysical scattering lengths. The YGLO functional enables consistent description of both neutron and symmetric matter from dilute regimes to saturation, and its symmetry energy slope \(L\) falls within the phenomenological band constrained by electric dipole polarizability measurements in \(^{208}\)Pb, \(^{68}\)Ni, and \(^{120}\)Sn as compiled by Roca-Maza et al.
