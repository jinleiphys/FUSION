# Regularization Methods for Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory

> We investigate Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory for the two-body system for several lattice spacings at lowest order in the pionless as well as in the pionful theory. We discuss issues of regularizations and predictions for the effective range expansion. In the pionless case, a simple Gaussian smearing allows to demonstrate lattice spacing independence over a wide range of lattice spacings. We show that regularization methods known from the continuum formulation are necessary as well as feasible for the pionful approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice spacing independence of observables in Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory can be achieved at leading order for both the pionless and pionful theories, but only when appropriate regularization methods are applied: Gaussian smearing for contact interactions in the pionless case, and an additional position-space regularization of the one-pion-exchange potential in the pionful case, with the caveat that the results are demonstrated for a physically relevant range of lattice spacings rather than the full continuum limit.

## Method

The authors used the Hamiltonian formalism with a stretched O(a^4)-improved free Hamiltonian and solved the two-nucleon system using the Lanczos method on cubic lattices. For the pionless theory, they introduced Gaussian smearing of the contact interactions with a single smearing parameter b for both the 1S0 and 3S1 channels. For the pionful theory, they additionally regularized the one-pion-exchange potential in position space using a function [1-exp(-r^2/(2b))]^n with n=4. They extracted infinite-volume scattering parameters using Luscher's finite-volume formulas for bound states and scattering states across multiple lattice volumes.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings studied: a = 0.49 fm to 1.97 fm (a^{-1} = 100 to 400 MeV) for pionless theory; a = 0.28 fm to 1.97 fm for pionful theory
- Experimental targets: a_{1S0} = -23.76(1) fm, r_{1S0} = 2.75(5) fm, deuteron binding energy E_b = -2.224575(9) MeV
- Pionless theory predictions for 3S1 channel: a_{3S1} = 5.601(1) to 5.630(1) fm, r_{3S1} = 2.005(1) to 2.047(1) fm across all lattice spacings
- Pionful theory predictions for 3S1 channel: a_{3S1} = 5.470(1) to 5.649(1) fm, r_{3S1} = 1.818(1) to 1.899(1) fm across all lattice spacings
- Smearing parameter b in pionless theory: 3.83 x 10^{-5} to 4.27 x 10^{-5} MeV^{-2}
- Smearing parameter b in pionful theory: 2.40 x 10^{-5} to 2.92 x 10^{-5} MeV^{-2}
- Binding energy from consistency relation in pionful theory: E_B = 2.026(1) to 2.033(1) MeV for a = 0.5 to 2.0 fm

## Context

This work extends the Nuclear Lattice EFT method of Lee (2008) by systematically analyzing the lattice spacing dependence, which had not been done previously as most calculations used a fixed lattice spacing of about 2 fm. It addresses problems noted in earlier work by Borasoy (2007) regarding the poor description of the 3S1-3D1 mixing angle and by Lahde (2013) regarding overbinding in medium-mass nuclei. The position-space regularization of the one-pion-exchange potential follows the approach of Epelbaum (2014). The results enable future calculations with smaller lattice spacings to reduce discretization errors, particularly for higher momenta in partial wave decomposition, and suggest that the modified leading-order approach could improve auxiliary field Monte Carlo simulations in Nuclear Lattice EFT.
