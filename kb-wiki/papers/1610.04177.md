# Low-energy electronic recoil in xenon detectors by solar neutrinos

> Low-energy electronic recoil caused by solar neutrinos in multi-ton xenon detectors is an important subject not only because it is a source of the irreducible background for direct searches of weakly-interacting massive particles (WIMPs), but also because it provides a viable way to measure the solar $pp$ and $^{7}\textrm{Be}$ neutrinos at the precision level of current standard solar model predictions. In this work we perform $\textit{ab initio}$ many-body calculations for the structure, photoionization, and neutrino-ionization of xenon. It is found that the atomic binding effect yields a sizable suppression to the neutrino-electron scattering cross section at low recoil energies. Compared with the previous calculation based on the free electron picture, our calculated event rate of electronic recoil in the same detector configuration is reduced by about $25\%$. We present in this paper the electronic recoil rate spectrum in the energy window of 100 eV - 30 keV with the standard per ton per year normalization for xenon detectors, and discuss its implication for low energy solar neutrino detection (as the signal) and WIMP search (as a source of background).

## Key claim

Atomic binding effects in xenon suppress the neutrino-electron scattering cross section at low recoil energies, reducing the predicted event rate by about 25% compared to the free electron approximation used in prior work (Baudis 2013, Aalbers 2016). The authors qualify that their calculation is benchmarked to a 2-3% averaged theoretical error in the 2-30 keV window, which is slightly larger than the 1% precision goal for solar neutrino flux measurements.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio many-body calculations using the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) to compute the structure, photoionization, and neutrino-ionization of xenon atoms. They benchmarked their calculations against experimental photoabsorption data and single-particle energies from Dirac-Hartree-Fock theory, then computed the differential cross section for neutrino-electron scattering by folding with solar neutrino fluxes from the standard solar model and accounting for neutrino oscillations via the MSW-LMA solution.

## Key numbers

- The pp neutrino flux used is 5.98 x 10^10 cm^-2 s^-1 and the 7Be neutrino flux is 5.00 x 10^9 cm^-2 s^-1, taken from the Serenelli (2011) standard solar model.
- The survival probability of electron neutrinos is P_ee = 0.553, using sin^2(2theta_12) = 0.846 and sin^2(2theta_13) = 0.085 from the Particle Data Group (2014).
- For a 1-ton-year exposure in the 2-30 keV energy window, the RRPA calculation gives 57.8 events from pp neutrinos and 5.4 events from 7Be neutrinos, compared to 80.7 and 7.1 respectively in the free electron approximation (28% and 24% reductions).
- For the dark matter search channel (2-10 keV, 99.98% electronic recoil rejection, 30% nuclear recoil acceptance), the total event rate is 3.9 x 10^-3 per ton-year in RRPA versus 5.2 x 10^-3 in the free electron approximation (24% reduction).
- The theoretical error is estimated as less than 5% in the 100 eV to 30 keV range, and 2-3% averaged over the 2-30 keV window.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous many-body calculations on germanium detectors (Chen 2013, Chen 2014) to xenon, and directly addresses the atomic binding corrections missing from the free electron approximation used by Baudis (2013) and Aalbers (2016) for solar neutrino backgrounds in multi-ton xenon detectors. The results enable more accurate predictions for both the irreducible background to WIMP searches (the "neutrino floor") and the feasibility of measuring pp and 7Be solar neutrino fluxes at the 1% precision level targeted by next-generation experiments like DARWIN, XENON1T, and LZ.
