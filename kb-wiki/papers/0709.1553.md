# Probing Transversity GPDs in Photo and Electroproduction of Two Vector Mesons

> Electroproduction of two mesons well separated in rapidity allows the first feasible selective access to chiral-odd transversity GPDs provided one of these mesons is a transversely polarized vector meson rho_T.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that electroproduction of two mesons well separated in rapidity, where one is a transversely polarized vector meson, provides the first feasible selective access to chiral-odd transversity GPDs, but only if the hard scale is provided by the transverse momentum of the first meson and the process is calculable within collinear factorization at the Born level.

## Method

The authors compute the Born-term scattering amplitude for the process γ(*) p → ρ_L^0 ρ_T^+ n within the collinear factorization method, where the amplitude is a convolution of a hard perturbative part, two non-perturbative meson distribution amplitudes (DAs), and the target's GPDs. The hard scale is taken as the "Pomeron" virtuality p² = p_T² = -p̅². Two models for the transversity GPD are used: a meson pole model generalized from Gamberg et al. and a bag model from Scopetta, and the resulting cross sections are compared for photoproduction kinematics relevant to COMPASS.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal ρ meson decay constants: f_{ρ^0_L} = 216 ± 5 MeV, f_{ρ^+_L} = 198 ± 7 MeV
- Transverse ρ^+ meson decay constant: f_{ρ^+_T} = 160 ± 10 MeV
- Magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum: χ ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ 40 - 70 MeV
- The skewedness parameter is defined as ξ ≈ (s₁ + Q²)/(2s), with s₁ >> p̅² and s₂ ≈ p̅²(1-ξ)/(2ξ)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for accessing chiral-odd transversity GPDs, which had only recently been shown possible (Pire and Szymanowski, 2005), by providing a concrete, factorizable process using transverse ρ meson production. The proposal enables experiments like COMPASS (at large energy) or JLab (at lower energies) to measure transversity GPDs for the first time, and also suggests a connection to probing the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum through a related process with a real photon.
