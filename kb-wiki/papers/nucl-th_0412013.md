# Rapidity Spectra of the Secondaries Produced in Heavy Ion Collisions and the Constituent Picture of the Particles

> The present study is both a reanalysis and an extension of the approach initiated by Eremin and Voloshin(referenced in the text). We attempt to interpret here the rapidity-spectra of the various particles produced in both $Pb+Pb$ and $Au+Au$ collisions at CERN-SPS and RHIC-BNL. The study made here is wider in scope and is more species-specific than the earlier ones for which the results obtained here have been compared with those suggested by some previous works based on HIJING, VENUS etc, at various centralities. The study reconfirms that the constituent parton picture of the particles provides a better and more unified description of the rapidity-density yields for the various secondaries, including some light cluster particles like deuteron even in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The constituent parton picture provides a better and more unified description of rapidity-density yields for secondaries in heavy ion collisions than the nucleon-participant picture, but this scaling holds for kaons and protons while pions do not agree, and deuterons show the opposite behavior.

## Method
The authors calculated the number of participant nucleons and participant partons using a Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile and the Glauber model formalism of Eremin and Voloshin, with parton density set to three times nucleon density and parton-parton cross sections set to one-ninth of the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section. They then fitted the integrated yields of pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, and deuterons produced in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS (158A GeV) and Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=130 and 200 GeV) as a function of both participant nucleon number and participant parton number, comparing the scaling exponents alpha and beta.

## Key numbers
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section sigma_NN^{inel} = 30 mb used for c.m. energies sqrt(s_NN)=4-17.3 GeV
- Nuclear density parameter n_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}; parton density n_0^q = 3n_0 = 0.51 fm^{-3}
- For Pb+Pb collisions, the scaling exponent |beta| was less than |alpha| for pions, kaons, and protons/antiprotons, indicating better scaling with participant parton number
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the exponent alpha for pions was quite small relative to beta, favoring nucleon-participant scaling over parton-participant scaling
- The combined fit for A+A and P+anti-P collisions normalized by participant parton pairs gave: (1/(0.5<N_q-part>)) dN_ch/deta = -(0.010 +/- 0.003) + (0.27 +/- 0.01) ln(sqrt(s_NN)/GeV) with chi^2/ndf = 3.632/12

## Context
This work extends the approach of Eremin and Voloshin (referenced in the text) by applying the constituent parton picture to species-specific rapidity spectra of pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, and deuterons, going beyond earlier studies that focused on total charged particle yields. The results are compared with predictions from HIJING and VENUS event generators, and with data from the NA49, WA98, and PHENIX collaborations. The study also connects to the work of Sarkisyan and Shakarov (referenced in the text) on pseudorapidity density scaling across hadron-hadron, nucleus-nucleus, and e+e- collisions, and reinforces the idea that partonic participant scaling unifies the energy dependence of particle production across different collision systems.
