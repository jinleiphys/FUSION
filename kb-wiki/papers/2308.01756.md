# Separation energies of light $Λ$ hypernuclei and their theoretical uncertainties

> Separation energies of light $Λ$ hypernuclei ($A\leq 5$) and their theoretical uncertainties are investigated. Few-body calculations are performed within the Faddeev-Yakubovsky scheme and the no-core shell model. Thereby, modern and up-to-date $N\!N$ and $Y\!N$ potentials derived within chiral effective field theory are employed. % It is found that the numerical uncertainties of the few-body methods are well under control and an accuracy of around $1$ keV for the hypertriton and of less than $20$ keV for the separation energies of the $^4_Λ\mathrm{He}$ and $^5_Λ\mathrm{He}$ hypernuclei can be achieved. Variations caused by differences in the $N\!N$ interaction are in the order of $10$ keV for $^3_Λ\mathrm{H}$ and no more than $110$ keV for $A=4,\,5$ $Λ$ hypernuclei, when recent high-precision potentials up to fifth order in the chiral expansion are employed. The variations are smaller than expected contributions from chiral $Y\!N\!N$ three-body forces (3BFs) which arise at the chiral order of state-of-the-art $Y\!N$ potentials. Estimates for those 3BFs are deduced from a study of the truncation uncertainties in the chiral expansion.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that numerical uncertainties in few-body calculations of light Lambda hypernuclei (A <= 5) are well controlled, but the theoretical uncertainties from truncation of the chiral expansion are larger than previously assumed, particularly for A=4 and A=5 systems, and are comparable to or larger than expected contributions from three-body forces.

## Method
Few-body calculations were performed using the Faddeev-Yakubovsky scheme and the no-core shell model with chiral effective field theory potentials for nucleon-nucleon (NN) and hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions up to fifth order in the chiral expansion. A Bayesian statistical analysis was applied to quantify truncation uncertainties from the chiral expansion, using different priors for the expansion parameter Q and reference scales, with separate and combined analyses for NN and YN convergence patterns.

## Key numbers
- Numerical uncertainties of around 1 keV for the hypertriton (³_ΛH) separation energy
- Numerical uncertainties of less than 20 keV for ⁴_ΛHe and ⁵_ΛHe separation energies
- Variations from different NN interactions: about 10 keV for ³_ΛH, no more than 110 keV for A=4,5 hypernuclei
- For the preferred case (di) at NLO: Δ_68(³_ΛH) = 0.015 MeV (YN) and 0.011 MeV (NN); Δ_68(⁴_ΛHe(0+)) = 0.239 MeV (YN) and 0.157 MeV (NN); Δ_68(⁴_ΛHe(1+)) = 0.214 MeV (YN) and 0.114 MeV (NN); Δ_68(⁵_ΛHe) = 0.881 MeV (YN) and 0.529 MeV (NN)
- For the preferred case (di) at N²LO: Δ_68(³_ΛH) = 0.006 MeV (YN) and 0.004 MeV (NN); Δ_68(⁴_ΛHe(0+)) = 0.096 MeV (YN) and 0.063 MeV (NN); Δ_68(⁴_ΛHe(1+)) = 0.086 MeV (YN) and 0.046 MeV (NN); Δ_68(⁵_ΛHe) = 0.352 MeV (YN) and 0.212 MeV (NN)
- Expansion parameter Q posterior values range from 0.24 to 0.46 depending on prior choices, with preferred values around 0.40

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian uncertainty quantification framework developed by Melendez (2017, 2019) for nuclear interactions to hypernuclear systems. It uses the same SMS-regulated chiral potentials at cutoff Λ_NN=450 MeV and Λ_YN=550 MeV employed in previous studies of hypernuclear structure. The results enable quantitative comparisons with experimental separation energies and provide estimates for the size of missing chiral YNN three-body forces, which are expected to be of similar magnitude to the truncation uncertainties reported here.
