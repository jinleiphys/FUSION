# The 2p-2h electromagnetic response in the quasielastic peak and beyond

> The contribution to the nuclear transverse response function R_T arising from two particle-two hole (2p-2h) states excited through the action of electromagnetic meson exchange currents (MEC) is computed in a fully relativistic framework. The MEC considered are those carried by the pion and by Delta degrees of freedom, the latter being viewed as a virtual nucleonic resonance. The calculation is performed in the relativistic Fermi gas model in which Lorentz covariance can be maintained. All 2p-2h many-body diagrams containing two pionic lines that contribute to R_T are taken into account and the relative impact of the various components of the MEC on R_T is addressed. The non-relativistic limit of the MEC contributions is also discussed and compared with the relativistic results to explore the role played by relativity in obtaining the 2p-2h nuclear response.

## Key claim

The fully relativistic calculation of the 2p-2h meson exchange current contribution to the transverse response function \(R_T\) yields results that differ significantly from the only previous fully relativistic computation (DBT), particularly in the high-energy region beyond the quasielastic peak, where the present response is larger by about a factor two to three; however, the authors note that the issue of gauge invariance is left for future work and that the calculation uses the same form factors and \(\Delta\)-width as DBT for direct comparison.

## Method

The transverse response function \(R_T\) for a relativistic Fermi gas was computed by evaluating all 2p-2h many-body diagrams containing two pionic lines that contribute through meson exchange currents (MEC) carried by the pion and the \(\Delta\) resonance. The relativistic expressions for the pion-in-flight, seagull, and \(\Delta\) currents were inserted into the hadronic tensor, and the resulting seven-dimensional integrals were evaluated using Monte Carlo techniques with a sample size of \(10^6\) to \(10^7\) configurations to achieve a standard deviation better than 1%. The non-relativistic limit of the same currents was also derived and compared to the relativistic results to assess the role of relativity.

## Key numbers

- At \(q = 550\) MeV/c, the present non-relativistic \(R_T\) is larger than the DBT non-relativistic result by about a factor of two in the high-energy region near the light cone.
- At \(q = 1140\) MeV/c, the present non-relativistic \(R_T\) is larger than the DBT non-relativistic result by over a factor of three in the high-energy region.
- The relativistic calculation at \(q = 550\) MeV/c and \(q = 1140\) MeV/c shows essential agreement with DBT up to the \(\Delta\) peak, but in the dip and above the \(\Delta\) peak the present relativistic \(R_T\) is larger by about a factor of two to three.
- The hadronic monopole form factor cutoffs used are \(\Lambda_\pi = 1300\) MeV and \(\Lambda_{\pi N\Delta} = 1150\) MeV; the electromagnetic form factor cutoff is \(\Lambda_D^2 = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\).
- The Fermi momentum used for comparison with DBT is \(k_F = 1.3\) fm\(^{-1}\), and a binding energy shift of \(\bar{\epsilon}_2 = 70\) MeV is applied to the energy-conserving delta function.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of 2p-2h MEC contributions to the transverse response beyond the only previous fully relativistic computation, that of Dekker et al. (1994) (referred to as DBT), and finds significant discrepancies in the high-energy region. The non-relativistic results agree with earlier non-relativistic calculations by Van Orden and Donnelly (1980) and Alberico et al. (1984), which were limited to moderate momenta and energies not exceeding 300 MeV. The present results indicate that MEC effects beyond the quasielastic peak may be larger than previously foreseen, motivating future work that incorporates gauge invariance and updated form factors and \(\Delta\)-width.
