# Equations of State for Dense Matter and Astrophysical Constraints

> This conference proceeding presents an overview of the modern approaches in the study of baryonic matter at high densities, focusing on the use of online repositories such as CompOSE and MUSES for the calculation of neutron star properties. In this context, relevant astrophysical constraints for the equations of state (mass-radius relation, speed of sound, tidal deformability) are discussed.

# Equations of State for Dense Matter and Astrophysical Constraints

## Key claim
This conference proceeding argues that online repositories such as CompOSE and MUSES, combined with astrophysical constraints from neutron star observations (mass-radius relation, speed of sound, tidal deformability), are progressively narrowing the allowed properties of dense baryonic matter, but the authors note that many open questions remain and that new LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA data from the observing run beginning March 2023 will be needed to further discriminate between models.

## Method
The authors review the structure and capabilities of two online equation-of-state repositories (CompOSE, with nearly 300 EoS tables, and the MUSES modular cyberinfrastructure project) and discuss how astrophysical constraints from neutron star observations, specifically the mass-radius relation, speed of sound behavior, and tidal deformabilities from gravitational-wave measurements, can be used to test and differentiate between EoS models. They illustrate these constraints using published parametric studies of the speed of sound and binary Love relations from Tan et al. (2022).

## Key numbers
- Neutron stars are typically about 12 km across and may contain up to 2 solar masses (2 M_sun), implying core densities as high as 10 times nuclear saturation density (~10^15 g/cm^3).
- CompOSE offers almost 300 equations of state, divided into families including cold neutron star EoS, cold matter EoS, neutron matter EoS, general purpose EoS, and neutron star crust EoS.
- The conformal limit for massless free quarks requires c_s^2 approaches 1/3 from below at asymptotically large densities.
- Ultra-heavy neutron stars with masses larger than 2.5 M_sun can pass all observational and theoretical constraints, including those from LIGO/Virgo gravitational-wave observations and NICER X-ray observations.

## Context
This work extends the ongoing effort to constrain dense matter EoS using multi-messenger astrophysics, building on the CompOSE repository described by Dexheimer et al. (2022) and the MUSES collaboration framework. The authors specifically reference the parametric speed-of-sound study by Tan et al. (2022) that correlates bumps in c_s with features in the mass-radius diagram, and the binary Love relations analysis by Tan et al. (2022) that shows how phase transitions create characteristic structures (slopes, hills, drops, swooshes) in tidal deformability curves. The paper enables future work by highlighting that changes in slope in binary Love relations may be observable during the fifth LIGO observing run, while detection of drops and swooshes would require exceptionally loud signals.
