# Structure of the nucleon and its first radial excitation

> A Poincaré-covariant continuum approach to the three valence-quark bound-state problem in quantum field theory is used to perform a detailed analysis of the nucleon's ground and first excited states: the so-called $N(940)\frac{1}{2}^+$ and $N(1440)\frac{1}{2}^+$. Such analysis predicts the presence of nonpointlike, fully-interacting quark-quark (diquark) correlations within them, being the isoscalar-scalar and isovector-pseudovector diquarks overwhelmingly dominant with similar relative strengths in both states. Moreover, the rest-frame wave functions of both states are largely $S$-wave in nature and the first excited state in this $1/2^+$ channel has the appearance of a radial excitation of the ground state. All these features have numerous observable consequences, we show herein those related with the nucleon's elastic, Roper's elastic and nucleon-to-Roper transition electromagnetic form factors, for both charged and neutral channels.

## Key claim

The nucleon's ground state and its first excited state (the Roper resonance) are both dominated by isoscalar-scalar and isovector-pseudovector diquark correlations with identical relative strengths (62% and 38% respectively), and the first excited state has the character of a radial excitation of the ground state; however, these results describe only the dressed-quark core of the baryons, not the fully-dressed observable states, because meson-baryon final-state interactions are omitted from the Faddeev kernel.

## Method

A Poincare-covariant continuum Faddeev equation was solved for the three valence-quark bound-state problem, using dressed-quark propagators parametrized to exhibit dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and confinement, together with scalar and pseudovector diquark correlations whose propagators are pole-free on the timelike axis to ensure diquark confinement within the baryon. The resulting Faddeev amplitudes were used to compute the electromagnetic current via a six-term vertex that separately probes quarks and diquarks, yielding elastic and transition form factors for both charged and neutral channels.

## Key numbers

- Dressed-quark core masses: nucleon = 1.19 GeV, Roper = 1.73 GeV (Eq. 4.1)
- Diquark fractions in both nucleon and Roper: scalar diquark 62%, pseudovector diquark 38% (Eq. 4.4)
- Diquark masses: scalar = 0.8 GeV, pseudovector = 0.9 GeV
- Diquark couplings: scalar = 14.8, pseudovector = 12.7
- Charged-Roper core static properties: electric radius r_E * M_N = 6.23, magnetic radius r_M * M_N = 4.49, magnetic moment mu = 2.67 (Table 1)
- Proton core static properties: electric radius r_E * M_N = 3.65, magnetic radius r_M * M_N = 3.17, magnetic moment mu = 2.50 (Table 1)
- Neutral-Roper core static properties: magnetic radius r_M * M_N = 4.15, magnetic moment mu = -1.24 (Table 1)
- Neutron core static properties: magnetic radius r_M * M_N = 4.19, magnetic moment mu = -1.83 (Table 1)
- All S-wave components of the Roper Faddeev wave function exhibit a single zero at z_R approximately 0.4 GeV (approximately 1/[0.5 fm])

## Context

This work extends the continuum Faddeev equation approach previously applied to baryons by Oettel (1998), Roberts (2011), Eichmann (2016), Chen (2017), and Lu (2017), using the realistic quark-quark interaction developed by Binosi (2014, 2016). The bare Roper mass of 1.73 GeV agrees with the value of 1.76 GeV extracted from dynamical coupled-channels analyses by Suzuki (2009), confirming that the dressed-quark core plus meson-cloud picture is consistent. The results enable future calculations of leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes for the proton and Roper, as already reported by Mezrag (2017), and provide predictions testable with CLAS12 at Jefferson Lab at photon virtualities up to Q^2 approximately 12 m_N^2.
