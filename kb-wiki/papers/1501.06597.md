# Gluon effects on the equation of state of color superconducting strange stars

> Compact astrophysical objects are a window for the study of strongly interacting nuclear matter given the conditions in their interiors, which are not reproduced in a laboratory environment. Much has been debated about their composition with possibilities ranging from a simple mixture of mostly protons and neutrons to deconfined quark matter. Recent observations on the mass of two pulsars, PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432, have posed a great restriction on their composition, since the equation of state must be hard enough to support masses of about at least two solar masses. The onset of quarks tends to soften the equation of state, but it can get substantially stiffer since in the high-dense medium a repulsive vector interaction channel is opened. Nevertheless, we show that once gluon effects are considered, the equation of state of quark matter in the color-flavor-locked phase of color superconductivity becomes softer decreasing the maximum stellar mass that can be reached when not considering their influence. This may indicate that stars made entirely of color superconducting matter can only be favored to describe compact stars if the repulsive vector constant is high enough.

# Gluon effects on the equation of state of color superconducting strange stars

## Key claim

The inclusion of gluon rest-energy contributions (via Debye and Meissner masses acquired in the color-flavor-locked background) softens the equation of state of strange quark matter, reducing the maximum stellar mass compared to calculations that neglect gluon effects; this may exclude pure color superconducting strange stars as candidates for the observed 2-solar-mass pulsars unless the repulsive vector coupling constant is sufficiently high (G_V/G_S greater than 0.2).

## Method

The authors employed a three-flavor gauged Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector and diquark channels in the mean-field approximation, working in the chiral limit and at zero temperature. They computed the thermodynamic potential including both the quark contribution in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase and the gluon contribution from Debye and Meissner masses obtained via the hard-loop approximation for the one-loop gluon self-energy. The equation of state (pressure and energy density) was derived from the thermodynamic potential, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were integrated to obtain mass-radius relationships for strange stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum stellar masses for CFL matter with gluons are decreased by up to 20% compared to sequences without gluons, with the effect more prominent for lower vector coupling values.
- For vector coupling G_V/G_S less than 0.2, sequences including gluons cannot reach 2 solar masses, falling below the observed masses of PSR J1614-2230 (M = 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses) and PSR J0348+0432 (M = 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses).
- The bag constant B_0 was calculated as 57.3 MeV/fm^3 for the parameter set with cutoff Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G_S Lambda^2 = 1.835, and K Lambda^5 = 12.36.
- The diquark coupling G_D = 1.2 G_S produces a gap Delta approximately 10 MeV at chemical potential mu = 500 MeV.
- The speed of sound squared v_S^2/c^2 exceeds 1/3 for all vector couplings compatible with absolute stability, even with gluon softening.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of strange stars using NJL models (Buballa, 2005; Kitazawa, 2002) by incorporating gluon degrees of freedom typically neglected at zero temperature. It contradicts earlier perturbative QCD calculations (Fraga, 2008; Kurkela, 2010) that predicted maximum masses above 2 solar masses for strange stars, and addresses the tension between nonperturbative NJL calculations (Bordbar, 2011) that produced too-soft equations of state and the observational constraints from PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest, 2010) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis, 2013). The results enable future work to determine whether hybrid stars with hadronic outer layers and CFL cores, rather than pure strange stars, can satisfy the 2-solar-mass constraint when gluon effects are included.
