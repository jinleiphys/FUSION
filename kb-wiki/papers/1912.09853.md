# Data-driven study of timelike Compton scattering

> In the framework of collinear QCD factorization, the leading twist scattering amplitudes for deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS) are intimately related thanks to analytic properties of leading and next-to-leading order amplitudes. We exploit this welcome feature to make data-driven predictions for TCS observables to be measured in near future experiments. Using a recent extraction of DVCS Compton form factors from most of the existing experimental data for that process, we derive TCS amplitudes and calculate TCS observables only assuming leading-twist dominance. Artificial neural network techniques are used for an essential reduction of model dependency, while a careful propagation of experimental uncertainties is achieved with replica methods. Our analysis allows for stringent tests of the leading twist dominance of DVCS and TCS amplitudes. Moreover, this study helps to understand quantitatively the complementarity of DVCS and TCS measurements to test the universality of generalized parton distributions, which is crucial e.g. to perform the nucleon tomography.

## Key claim

Using a recent neural-network-based extraction of DVCS Compton form factors from global experimental data, the authors make data-driven predictions for timelike Compton scattering observables under the assumption of leading-twist dominance, with the key hedge that the NLO predictions carry large uncertainties because the available DVCS data do not constrain the Q² dependence of the spacelike CFFs well.

## Method

The authors exploit analytic spacelike-to-timelike relations at LO and NLO (derived in Muller (2012)) to convert DVCS Compton form factors (CFFs) extracted from a global fit of DVCS data into TCS CFFs. The DVCS CFFs were previously extracted using artificial neural networks with replica-method uncertainty propagation, as described in Moutarde (2019). From the TCS CFFs, the authors compute several TCS observables: the unpolarized cross section, the R ratio, the circular beam asymmetry A_CU, the linear-polarization observable C, and the transverse target spin asymmetry A_UT.

## Key numbers

- The differential TCS cross section is predicted for Q'² = 4 GeV², t = -0.1 GeV², and photon beam energy E_γ = 10 GeV, integrated over θ ∈ (π/4, 3π/4).
- The R ratio is evaluated for Q'² = 4 GeV² and t = -0.35 GeV² as a function of ξ.
- The circular asymmetry A_CU is shown for Q'² = 4 GeV², t = -0.1 GeV², and E_γ = 10 GeV, with its magnitude at φ = π/2 presented as a function of ξ.
- The linear-polarization ratio C is evaluated for Q'² = 4 GeV² and ξ = 0.1 as a function of t.
- The transverse target spin asymmetry A_UT is shown for Q'² = 4 GeV², t = t₀, and E_γ = 10 GeV as a function of φ_S, with model predictions estimating its size at about 15%.

## Context

This work extends the global fit of DVCS data performed by Moutarde (2019) to the crossed channel TCS, providing the first multi-channel data-driven analysis of exclusive processes going beyond LO approximation. It enables quantitative tests of leading-twist dominance and GPD universality, and demonstrates that TCS measurements will be crucial to constrain the Q² dependence of GPDs, which remains poorly determined from DVCS data alone. The analysis uses the PARTONS framework (Berthou, 2015) and compares predictions with the Goloskokov-Kroll GPD model (Goloskokov, 2005; Goloskokov, 2007; Goloskokov, 2009).
