# The extraction of the axial coupling from finite volume systems: The long and short of it

> Due to a pion-pole contribution, the nucleon axial current matrix elements can be visualized in position space as becoming delocalized as the chiral limit is approached, with one-third of the current at distance of order the inverse pion mass. However, this delocalization effect will not cause calculations of the axial coupling in a finite system with standard boundary conditions (periodic for boson fields, antiperiodic for fermions) to have large finite volume effects. This is seen by calculating axial coupling using a nonstandard current with axial quantum numbers. The matrix elements of this new current lacks a pion pole and is not delocalized; however, its zero-momentum matrix element is identical to that of the standard axial current both for infinite and finite volumes.

# The extraction of the axial coupling from finite volume systems: The long and short of it

## Key claim

The delocalization of the axial current in the chiral limit, where one-third of the current is pushed to distances of order the inverse pion mass, does **not** cause large finite volume effects in lattice calculations of the axial coupling constant \(g_A\), provided the standard boundary conditions (periodic for bosons, antiperiodic for fermions) are imposed. The limits \(V \to \infty\) and \(m_\pi \to 0\) are uniform, meaning the order of limits does not affect the result.

## Method

The author constructs an alternative axial current operator \(A'^\mu_a = (1 + \partial^\alpha\partial_\alpha / m_\pi^2) A^\mu_a\) whose nucleon matrix elements lack the pion-pole contribution responsible for delocalization. Using the Goldberger-Treiman relation and a chiral expansion, the angular-averaged matrix element \(\overline{G'}(Q^2)\) is shown to have no delocalized component. The finite-volume matrix element of this new current is proven equal to that of the standard axial current via a surface integral that vanishes under periodic boundary conditions, demonstrating that \(g_A^{\rm Vol}\) is identical for both currents.

## Key numbers

- One-third of the axial current is delocalized to distances of order \(1/m_\pi\) as the chiral limit is approached.
- The pion decay constant is taken as \(f_\pi \approx 93\ {\rm MeV}\).
- The hadronic scale is \(\Lambda \sim 1\ {\rm GeV}\), much larger than \(m_\pi\).
- The Goldberger-Treiman relation gives \(g_A M = f_\pi g_{\pi NN} + \mathcal{O}(m_\pi^2/\Lambda^2)\).
- The moments of the axial current density diverge as \(\langle r^{2n} \rangle_A \sim g_A \, n! \, \prod_{j=1,n} (2+4j) / (3 m_\pi^{2n})\) for \(n \ge 1\) in the chiral limit.
- For the new current \(A'\), the second moment \(\langle r^2 \rangle_{A'} = -4/m_\pi^2\) (plus corrections), but all higher moments are \(\mathcal{O}(m_\pi^{-2n})\) with no divergent behavior characteristic of delocalization.

## Context

This paper directly addresses the argument by Jaffe (2001) that the axial charge becomes delocalized in the chiral limit, which had been suggested as a source of large finite volume errors in lattice QCD calculations of \(g_A\). The work extends earlier mean-field chiral soliton and bag model results (Adkins, Nappi, and Witten, 1983; Jaffe, 1981; Cohen and Broniowski, 1986) by providing a model-independent demonstration that delocalization does not imply large finite volume effects under standard boundary conditions. The result leaves open the question of why contemporary lattice calculations (Sasaki, Blum, Ohta, and Orginos, 2001) substantially underpredict \(g_A\), noting that other effects, not the delocalization mechanism, may be responsible.
