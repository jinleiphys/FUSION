# Spin Physics in the Resonance Region

> Recent results from Jefferson Lab on measurement of inclusive double polarization asymmetries in the nucleon resonance region are discussed. Preliminary results on the first moment of the structure function g_1(x,Q^2) for protons, and on the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral for neutrons are presented as well. The first moment for protons shows a strong Q^2 dependence below Q^2 = 1 GeV^2, and changes sign near Q^2 = 0.3 GeV^2. Strong double polarization asymmetries are observed in exclusive electroproduction of pions from polarized protons.

## Key claim

The first moment of the spin structure function g1 for protons shows a strong Q2 dependence below Q2 = 1 GeV2 and changes sign near Q2 = 0.3 GeV2, but the author notes these results are preliminary and the final results may change within systematic uncertainties, though the qualitative features will not change.

## Method

Inclusive double polarization asymmetries were measured at Jefferson Lab using polarized ammonia (NH3) targets for protons and polarized 3He targets for neutrons, with data taken at fixed scattering angles and multiple beam energies. The spin structure function g1(x,Q2) was extracted from the measured asymmetries using a parametrization of world data on F1 and A2, and the first moment Γ1(Q2) was evaluated by integrating g1 over x with a model extrapolation to x→0. For the neutron, the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn integral was evaluated from pion threshold to W=2 GeV, with nuclear corrections applied using a prescription by Ciofi degli Atti.

## Key numbers

- The first moment Γ1p(Q2) for protons changes sign near Q2 = 0.3 GeV2
- At Q2 = 0.8 GeV2 and W = 1.5 GeV, the asymmetry A1+ηA2 for protons reaches peak values of about 0.6
- The asymmetry in the Δ(1232) region remains negative at higher Q2
- The D13(1520) resonance shows a rapid change from dominant A3/2 amplitude at the photon point to A1/2 dominance for Q2 > 0.5 GeV2
- New data cover a Q2 range from 0.05 to 2.5 GeV2 with beam energies from 1.6 GeV to 5.75 GeV

## Context

This work extends measurements of polarized structure functions from high-energy laboratories like SLAC and HERMES into the low-Q2 resonance region at Jefferson Lab, where the transition from parton physics to hadronic degrees of freedom occurs. The results test the generalized Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule introduced by Ji and Osborne (2000) and the Bjorken sum rule, and provide constraints for Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations at low Q2 and pQCD evolution at high Q2. The measurements enable tests of nucleon structure models for resonance excitations and exploration of spin duality between the resonance and deep inelastic regimes.
