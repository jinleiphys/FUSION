# Hypermassive quark cores

> Using a quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) and MIT based models we construct hybrid stars within the Maxwell criteria of hadron-quark phase transition. We are able to produce a hybrid star with maximum mass of 2.15$M_\odot$. Furthermore, a 2.03$M_\odot$ star with a quark core corresponding to more than $80\%$ of both, its total mass and radius, is also possible.

# Hypermassive quark cores

## Key claim

Using a quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) hadronic model combined with MIT-based quark models via the Maxwell construction, the authors claim that hybrid stars with quark cores constituting more than 80% of both total mass and radius can reach 2.03 solar masses, but only when the vector MIT bag model is used without the Dirac sea contribution and with a specific vector coupling strength (G_V = 0.35 fm²); the original MIT bag model and the version including Dirac sea contributions fail to produce hybrid stars reaching the 2.0 solar mass threshold.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star equations of state by combining a non-linear σωρ QHD model (Walecka model with hyperons and ω-ρ coupling) with three variants of the MIT bag model: the original version, a version with a vector channel (vector MIT), and a version with both vector channel and a quartic term mimicking Dirac sea contributions. The hadron-quark phase transition is determined using the Maxwell construction (equal chemical potential and pressure at the interface), and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations for the resulting hybrid stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hybrid star mass of 2.15 M⊙ for the pure hadronic QHD model (no quark core)
- Hybrid star with 2.03 M⊙ and a quark core of 1.65 M⊙ (81% of total mass) and 9.06 km radius (81% of total radius), achieved with vector MIT model at G_V = 0.35 fm² and B^{1/4} = 158 MeV
- For the original MIT bag model, the maximum hybrid star mass is only 1.40 M⊙ (at B^{1/4} = 175 MeV), well below the PSR J0740+6620 constraint
- For the vector MIT model with Dirac sea contribution (G_V = 0.8 fm²), maximum hybrid star masses range from 1.82 M⊙ (b_4 = 22) to 1.98 M⊙ (b_4 = 10), all below 2.0 M⊙
- Critical neutron chemical potential for phase transition ranges from 1063 MeV (G_V = 0.35 fm²) to 1394 MeV (G_V = 0.40 fm²) in the vector MIT model
- Radius of canonical 1.4 M⊙ hybrid star is 12.54 km for G_V = 0.35 fm², compared to 12.96 km for the pure hadronic case

## Context

This work extends studies of hybrid stars using the vector MIT bag model by Lopes et al. (2021) and Gomes et al. (2019), but with a thermodynamically consistent treatment that includes the vector meson mass term. The results contradict the findings of Annala et al. (2020), who suggested that two-solar-mass stars have quark cores of about 40% of total mass; here the authors find quark cores can reach 81% of total mass. The paper enables future comparisons between Maxwell construction and Gibbs construction for hybrid stars, and provides specific parameter sets that simultaneously satisfy the PSR J0740+6620 mass constraint and produce large quark cores.
