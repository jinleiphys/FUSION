# There Is No Proof of Thermalized Quark-Gluon Plasma at RHIC and LHC

> Although Tevatron has discovered top quark and LHC (pp collisions) has discovered Higgs boson but the RHIC and LHC heavy-ion colliders have not discovered thermalized quark-gluon plasma (QGP). This is because the experimental data of top quark at Tevatron and the experimental data of Higgs boson at LHC are compared with the exact first principle calculation but the experimental data at RHIC and LHC are compared with simplistic models and assumptions which are not exact first principle calculation. In this paper we show that the exact first principle method to study quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC is the nonequilibrium-nonperturbative QCD by using closed-time path integral formalism. Hence in the absence of such exact first principle calculation we conclude that there is no proof of thermalized quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC.

# There Is No Proof of Thermalized Quark-Gluon Plasma at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

The paper argues that there is no proof of a thermalized quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at RHIC and LHC heavy-ion colliders because experimental data are compared with simplistic models and assumptions rather than with exact first-principle calculations. The hedge is that the claim rests on the absence of a nonequilibrium-nonperturbative QCD calculation using the closed-time path integral formalism; if such a calculation were performed and still described the data, the conclusion would change.

## Method

The author reviews the limitations of lattice QCD at finite temperature, hydrodynamics, color glass condensate, AdS/CFT, and super Yang-Mills plasma for describing non-equilibrium QGP at RHIC and LHC. The paper then presents the generating functional for non-equilibrium QCD using the closed-time path integral formalism, including light quarks (u, d, s) and heavy quarks (H), with ghost determinants and an initial density of states kernel. The author derives gauge-invariant quark-to-hadron and gluon-to-hadron fragmentation functions in non-equilibrium QCD and argues that these are the correct first-principle tools for studying QGP.

## Key numbers

- The energy density required to produce QGP is greater than 2 GeV/fm^3; the energy density of normal hadronic matter is approximately 0.15 GeV/fm^3.
- The center-of-mass energy per nucleon in Au-Au collisions at RHIC is sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The center-of-mass energy per nucleon in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC is sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
- The hadronization time scale in QCD is approximately 1 fm/c, or about 10^{-24} seconds.
- The temperature of QGP is greater than or approximately 200 MeV (greater than 10^{12} kelvin).

## Context

This paper extends the author's prior work on non-equilibrium QCD fragmentation functions (Nayak, 2017, 2018) and factorization proofs for heavy quarkonium production from non-equilibrium QGP. It directly contradicts the standard heavy-ion community interpretation that hydrodynamics fits to data (Heinz and Kolb, 2002; Teaney, 2003; Romatschke and Romatschke, 2007) prove thermalization. The paper also criticizes lattice QCD at finite temperature (Karsch, 2002; Bazavov et al., 2014) as inapplicable to non-equilibrium systems, and dismisses AdS/CFT approaches (Maldacena, 1998; Witten, 1998) and super Yang-Mills plasma studies as irrelevant because string theory and supersymmetry are not experimentally verified. The work calls for developing nonequilibrium-nonperturbative QCD calculations as the only path to a definitive proof or disproof of thermalized QGP.
