# Dissociation of large transverse-momentum prompt J/psi produced in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

> A collision of a light meson and a charmonium produces quarks and antiquarks first, then the charm quark fragments into a charmed hadron, and finally three or more mesons are produced. This is the mechanism that we consider and propose to understand the prompt-J/psi nuclear modification factor in the range of J/psi transverse momentum from 10 GeV/c to 18 GeV/c, as measured by the CMS Collaboration and the ATLAS Collaboration. Unpolarized cross sections are derived and calculated for the reactions pion+charmonium ->H_c+X with H_c being a charmed meson. Numerical cross sections are parametrized and used to calculate the dissociation rate of charmonium in the interaction with pions in hadronic matter. The momentum dependence of the rate obtained is so as to lead to a decreasing charmonium nuclear modification factor with increasing transverse momentum.

# Dissociation of Large Transverse-Momentum Prompt J/ψ Produced in Pb-Pb Collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The authors propose that prompt J/ψ suppression at transverse momenta between 10 and 18 GeV/c in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC can be partially explained by a new dissociation mechanism where a pion and a charmonium produce four quarks, the charm quark fragments into a charmed meson, and three or more mesons are formed, in contrast to the quark-interchange mechanism which dominates at lower momenta. The mechanism yields a dissociation rate that increases with charmonium momentum, leading to a decreasing nuclear modification factor with increasing transverse momentum, but the effect is modest: at 20 GeV/c and T/Tc = 0.95, the rate is only 0.0108 c/fm for π + J/ψ.

## Method
The authors derive unpolarized cross sections for the reactions π + charmonium → H_c + X (where H_c = D⁺, D⁰, Dₛ⁺, D*⁺, or D*⁰ and X represents two or more mesons) using a quark potential model with a temperature-dependent spin-independent potential and spin-spin interaction. The transition amplitude is computed from the quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with the potential, and the charm quark fragmentation into charmed mesons is described by DGLAP-evolved fragmentation functions fitted to e⁺e⁻ data. The cross sections are then used to calculate the dissociation rate of charmonium in thermal pions via thermal averaging over a Bose-Einstein distribution.

## Key numbers
- The dissociation rate for π + J/ψ at a charmonium momentum of 20 GeV/c and T/Tc = 0.95 is 0.0108 c/fm.
- The dissociation rate for π + ψ′ at 20 GeV/c and T/Tc = 0.95 is 0.0142 c/fm.
- The dissociation rate for π + χ_c at 20 GeV/c and T/Tc = 0.95 is 0.0149 c/fm.
- The total cross section for π + J/ψ → D⁺X + D⁰X + Dₛ⁺X + D*⁺X + D*⁰X is 1.46 mb at √s = 10 GeV using the potential at T = 0, and 1.49 mb using the vacuum Buchmüller-Tye potential.
- At √s = 7 GeV and T/Tc = 0.95, the cross section for π + J/ψ → D⁺+X is 1.97 times the cross section from the vacuum potential; for D⁰+X it is 1.94 times; for Dₛ⁺+X it is 1.54 times; for D*⁺+X it is 3.17 times.
- If the lifetime of hadronic matter with freeze-out temperature 0.7Tc is 5–10 fm/c, inclusion of the π + J/ψ reaction yields a nuclear modification factor that is 0.973–0.946 times the factor R′_AA from other suppression mechanisms.

## Context
This work extends the study of charmonium dissociation beyond the quark-interchange mechanism (used by Martins, Blaschke, and others in the 1990s) which produces two charmed mesons and gives negligible cross sections at large √s. It also contrasts with the short-distance approach (Peskin, 1979; Bhanot and Peskin, 1979) and the meson-exchange approach (Haglin and Gale, 2001; Oh, Song, and Lee, 2001) which yield either very small or unrealistically large cross sections. The proposed mechanism provides a new source for the decreasing nuclear modification factor of prompt J/ψ with increasing transverse momentum observed by CMS at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV and ATLAS at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, complementing other factors such as decreasing dissociation temperature with velocity (Liu, Rapp, and others) and gluon radiative energy loss. Future work will include collisions with K, η, ρ, K*, and φ mesons.
