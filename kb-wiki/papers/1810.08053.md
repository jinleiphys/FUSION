# A New Effective Potential for Deuteron

> We calculate for the first time the static properties of the deuteron, within the framework of supersymmetric quantum mechanics, analytically. A new effective potential and its partner are derived from a superpotential so that all parameters are fitted by the experimental data. An analytical expression is obtained for the deuteron wave function and contributions of the orthogonal $^{13}S_{1}$ and $^{13}D_{1}$ states are determined, explicitly. Compared to one pion exchange, the superpotential produces an electrostatic as well as two pion exchange terms for the potential. The saddle point radius of the potential and the maximum of the wave function are linearly proportional.

## Key claim

The paper claims to calculate the static properties of the deuteron analytically for the first time using supersymmetric quantum mechanics, but the model is a phenomenological fit where all parameters are determined by experimental data rather than derived from first principles. The superpotential produces an electrostatic (Coulomb) term and a two-pion exchange term automatically, but the approach relies on the assumption that the S-state and D-state radial wave functions are linearly proportional.

## Method

The authors introduce a superpotential of OPEP-like form with four adjustable parameters (A, L, C, D) and derive supersymmetric partner potentials via the Riccati equation. The ground state wave function is obtained analytically from the superpotential, and the S-state and D-state contributions are assumed proportional with a constant ratio xi = a_D / a_S. The six parameters (A, a_S, a_D, L, C, D) are determined by fitting to experimental deuteron properties: binding energy, quadrupole moment, structure radius, and the condition that the wave function maximum occurs at a specific radius.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron ground state energy: E_0 = -2.22456627(46) MeV (experimental input)
- S-state probability: P_S = 98.10%; D-state probability: P_D = 1.89% (for R_M = R)
- S-state coefficient: a_S = ±0.99049; D-state coefficient: a_D = ±0.13755
- Superpotential parameter A = 0.2316 fm^{-1} (from binding energy)
- Effective angular momentum: L = 0.1029
- Superpotential parameter C = 28.09; D = -37.16 fm (for R_M = R)
- Normalization constant: N = 0.082
- Deuteron structure radius: r_str = 1.97507(78) fm (experimental input)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: Q = 0.2859(3) e fm^2 (experimental input)
- Saddle point radius R_S is related to wave function maximum radius R_M by R_S = R_M - 0.09 (linear fit)

## Context

This work extends the application of supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSY QM) to nuclear physics for the first time for deuteron static properties, following the theoretical framework of Cooper (1995) and Gangopadhyaya (2017). The new effective potential V_-(r) is compared to the OPEP-based unifying potential V_U(r) and to phenomenological NN potentials such as CD-Bonn, Reid93, and AV18 (PhysRevC.63.024001, PhysRevC.49.2950, PhysRevC.51.38). The approach enables analytical treatment of the deuteron ground state and can be developed further to include magnetic moment, scattering length, phase shifts, and other NN interaction properties.
