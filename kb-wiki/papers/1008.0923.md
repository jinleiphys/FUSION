# Polarization properties of the quark-gluon medium

> Collective properties of the quark-gluon medium induced by its polarization are described by macroscopic QCD equations. The parton currents traversing it lead to emission of Cherenkov gluons, the wake effect and the transition radiation. Comparison with experimental data of RHIC reveals large value of the chromopermittivity of the medium while cosmic ray data at higher energies (close to LHC) favor much smaller values. The dispersion equations show that the proper modes of the medium are unstable.

# Polarization properties of the quark-gluon medium

## Key claim
The quark-gluon medium exhibits a large chromopermittivity (real part about 6) at RHIC energies, but cosmic ray data at energies close to LHC favor much smaller values of chromopermittivity close to 1, with the dispersion equations showing that the proper modes of the medium are unstable.

## Method
The author applies macroscopic in-medium QCD equations, analogous to electrodynamics with a dielectric permittivity, to describe the collective response of the quark-gluon medium to traversing parton currents. Classical solutions of the in-medium gluodynamics equations are derived for a point-like current, yielding expressions for the scalar and vector potentials that describe Cherenkov gluon emission, wake effects, and transition radiation. The dispersion equation for the medium is solved to determine the proper modes and their stability.

## Key numbers
- Real part of chromopermittivity from RHIC two-particle correlations (STAR and PHENIX): about 6, with imaginary part about 0.8.
- Density of scattering centers in the quark-gluon plasma estimated as exceeding 20 within the single proton volume.
- Instability exponent at LHC is about 16 times smaller than at RHIC, scaling asymptotically as gamma^{-1/3}.
- Cherenkov gluons emitted with constant transverse momentum k_t = omega_0, where omega_0 is a free parameter in the model.

## Context
This work extends the analogy between QED and QCD to describe collective effects in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior observations of ringlike events in cosmic ray data (Apanasenko et al.; Dremin, 1979, 1981) and the asymmetry of rho-meson mass shapes at SPS (Danielewicz et al., 1995). The results enable predictions for new effects at LHC, including Cherenkov gluon emission from extremely high energy forward moving partons, and connect the universal increase of hadronic total cross sections to the instability of the quark-gluon medium.
