# Effective field theories for baryons with two- and three-heavy quarks

> Baryons made of two or three heavy quarks can be described in the modern language of non-relativistic effective field theories. These, besides allowing a rigorous treatment of the systems, provide new insight in the nature of the three-body interaction in QCD.

# Effective field theories for baryons with two- and three-heavy quarks

## Key claim
Non-relativistic effective field theories (EFTs) can describe baryons containing two or three heavy quarks in a rigorous framework analogous to that used for quarkonium, provided the typical distance between heavy quarks is smaller than the inverse of the hadronic scale Lambda_QCD. The three-body part of the static potential for three-heavy-quark baryons emerges at two-loop order in perturbation theory and shows a genuine dependence on geometry that is smoother than the Coulomb potential.

## Method
The author constructs EFTs for QQq and QQQ baryons by integrating out gluons of energy and momentum of order the heavy-quark mass m and the typical momentum transfer p, organizing the Lagrangian as an expansion in powers of 1/m and in the distance between heavy quarks. The static potentials for QQ antitriplet and QQQ singlet configurations are computed up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in perturbation theory in the MS scheme, and the three-body part of the QQQ singlet potential is isolated as the part that vanishes when one quark is taken to infinite distance. Matching coefficients for spin-dependent terms at order 1/m are derived, and the hyperfine splitting of doubly heavy baryons is related to that of heavy-light mesons.

## Key numbers
- The QQ antitriplet static potential at NNLO is given by an expression starting with -2/3 * alpha_s(1/|r|)/|r|, with coefficients involving gamma_E = 0.577..., zeta(3) = 1.202..., pi^4, and n_f (number of light flavors).
- The QQ sextet static potential at leading order is repulsive: V_Sigma^(0) = alpha_s/(3|r|).
- The hyperfine splitting relation: M_Xi*_QQ - M_Xi_QQ = (3 m_Q')/(4 m_Q) * (c_F^(Q)/c_F^(Q')) * (M_P*_Q' - M_P_Q') * [1 + O(alpha_s^2, Lambda_QCD/m_Q, Lambda_QCD/m_Q')].
- The three-body potential V_S^3body is proportional to alpha_s * (alpha_s/(4pi))^2 and is finite for all configurations, vanishing when one quark is pulled to infinite distance and remaining finite when two quarks are placed at the same position.
- The octet potential at leading order is a 2x2 matrix with entries involving 1/|r_1|, 1/|r_2|, 1/|r_3| and coefficients -2/3, 1/3, 1/12, -5/12, and +/- sqrt(3)/4.
- The decuplet potential at leading order: V_Delta^(0) = (alpha_s/3) * (1/|r_1| + 1/|r_2| + 1/|r_3|).

## Context
This work extends the EFT framework developed for quarkonium by Brambilla et al. (2004) to baryons with two or three heavy quarks, building on earlier formulations by Brambilla et al. (2005) and Fleming et al. (2005). The perturbative three-body potential at two loops was first computed by Brambilla et al. (2009). The lattice evaluation of the QQQ static potential has a long tradition (Takahashi et al., 2000), while the QQq static potential was evaluated on the lattice only recently by Yamamoto et al. (2007). The hyperfine splitting relation connects doubly heavy baryons to heavy-light mesons following the work of Savage. The results enable systematic inclusion of 1/m and finite-size corrections to QQq and QQQ spectra, and motivate lattice studies using non-equilateral geometries to precisely identify the transition region from Coulombic to confining behavior.
