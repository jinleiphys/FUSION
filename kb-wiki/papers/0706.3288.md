# Alternative experimental evidence for chiral restoration in excited baryons

> Given existing empirical spectral patterns of excited hadrons it has been suggested that chiral symmetry is approximately restored in excited hadrons at zero temperature/density (effective symmetry restoration). If correct, this implies that mass generation mechanisms and physics in excited hadrons is very different as compared to the lowest states. One needs an alternative and independent experimental information to confirm this conjecture. Using very general chiral symmetry arguments it is shown that strict chiral restoration in a given excited nucleon forbids its decay into the N πchannel. Hence those excited nucleons which are assumed from the spectroscopic patterns to be in approximate chiral multiplets must only "weakly" decay into the N πchannel, (f_{N^*Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 << 1. However, those baryons which have no chiral partner must decay strongly with a decay constant comparable with f_{NNπ}. Decay constants can be extracted from the existing decay widths and branching ratios. It turnes out that for all those well established excited nucleons which can be classified into chiral doublets N_+(1440) - N_-(1535), N_+(1710) - N_-(1650), N_+(1720) - N_-(1700), N_+(1680) - N_-(1675), N_+(2220) - N_-(2250), N_+(?) - N_-(2190), N_+(?) - N_-(2600), the ratio is (f_{N^*Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 ~ 0.1 or much smaller for the high-spin states. In contrast, the only well established excited nucleon for which the chiral partner cannot be identified from the spectroscopic data, N(1520), has a decay constant into the Nπchannel that is comparable with f_{NNπ}. This gives an independent experimental verification of the chiral symmetry restoration scenario.

# Alternative experimental evidence for chiral restoration in excited baryons

## Key claim
Strict chiral restoration in a given excited nucleon forbids its decay into the Nπ channel; therefore, excited nucleons that appear from spectroscopic patterns to belong to approximate chiral multiplets must decay only weakly into Nπ, with (f_{N*Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 << 1, while those without a chiral partner must decay strongly with a decay constant comparable to f_{NNπ}. The paper provides an independent experimental verification of the chiral symmetry restoration scenario using existing decay data, but notes that in reality chiral symmetry is never completely restored in excited hadrons, only approximately.

## Method
The author uses general chiral symmetry arguments to derive a selection rule: for a baryon in the (0,1/2)+(1/2,0) representation of the parity-chiral group (a strict chiral doublet), the diagonal axial charge is zero and the πB±B± coupling constant must vanish, forbidding decay into Nπ. Decay constants f_{BNπ} are then extracted from existing experimental decay widths and branching ratios using the Rarita-Schwinger formalism for higher-spin fields, and the ratios (f_{BNπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 are compared for excited nucleons that can be classified into chiral doublets versus those that cannot.

## Key numbers
- For the chiral doublet N+(1440) - N-(1535), the ratio (f_{B+Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.15 and (f_{B-Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.026, with chiral asymmetry χ = 0.032.
- For the chiral doublet N+(1710) - N-(1650), the ratio (f_{B+Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.0030 and (f_{B-Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.026, with χ = 0.02.
- For the chiral doublet N+(1720) - N-(1700), the ratio (f_{B+Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.023 and (f_{B-Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.13, with χ = 0.01.
- For the chiral doublet N+(1680) - N-(1675), the ratio (f_{B+Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.18 and (f_{B-Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.012, with χ = 0.002.
- For the high-spin state N-(2190) (7/2), the ratio (f_{B-Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.00053.
- For the chiral doublet N+(2220) - N-(2250) (9/2), the ratio (f_{B+Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.000022 and (f_{B-Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.0000020, with χ = 0.01.
- For the high-spin state N-(2600) (11/2), the ratio (f_{B-Nπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 0.000000064.
- For N-(1520) (3/2), which has no identified chiral partner, the ratio (f_{BNπ}/f_{NNπ})^2 = 2.5, comparable to the nucleon value.
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant is f_{NNπ} = 1.0.

## Context
This work extends the conjecture of effective chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons, previously suggested based on spectral patterns (Glozman 2000, Cohen and Glozman 2002, Glozman 2003), by providing an independent experimental test using strong decay data. The selection rule derived here is model-independent and based on general chiral symmetry properties, contrasting with alternative assignments such as those of Jaffe, Kiritsis, and Hatzis (1990) who placed excited baryons into (1,1/2)+(1/2,1) representations requiring large pion-baryon coupling constants. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted missing chiral partners of N(2190) and N(2600), and support the view that mass generation mechanisms in excited hadrons are fundamentally different from those in the lowest states.
