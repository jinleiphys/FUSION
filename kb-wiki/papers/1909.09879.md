# Temperature Dependence of the Axion Mass in a Scenario Where the Restoration of Chiral Symmetry Drives the Restoration of the $U_A(1)$ Symmetry

> The temperature ($T$) dependence of the axion mass is predicted for $T$'s up to $\sim 2.3 \times$ the chiral restoration temperature of QCD. The axion is related to the $U_A(1)$ anomaly. The squared axion mass $m_a(T)^2$ is, modulo the presently undetermined scale of spontaneous breaking of Peccei-Quinn symmetry $f_a$ (squared), equal to QCD topological susceptibility $χ(T)$ for all $T$. We obtain $χ(T)$ by using quark condensates calculated in two effective Dyson-Schwinger models of nonperturbative QCD. They exhibit the correct chiral behavior, including the dynamical breaking of chiral symmetry and its restoration at high $T$. This is reflected in the $U_A(1)$ symmetry breaking and restoration through $χ(T)$. In our previous studies, such $χ(T)$ yields the $T$-dependence of the $U_A(1)$-anomaly-influenced masses of $η'$ and $η$ mesons consistent with experiment. This in turn supports our prediction for the $T$-dependence of the axion mass. Another support is a rather good agreement with the pertinent lattice results. This agreement is not spoiled by our varying $u$ and $d$ quark mass parameters out of the isospin limit.

## Key claim

The axion mass squared is proportional to the QCD topological susceptibility at all temperatures, and the authors predict its temperature dependence up to approximately 2.3 times the chiral restoration temperature of QCD, using effective Dyson-Schwinger models that exhibit a smooth crossover rather than a sharp phase transition for the chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method

The authors compute the QCD topological susceptibility χ(T) from quark condensates calculated in two effective Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) models of nonperturbative QCD at finite temperature. They then relate χ(T) to the squared axion mass m_a(T)^2 modulo the unknown Peccei-Quinn symmetry breaking scale f_a, and compare their results with lattice QCD data on topological susceptibility.

## Key numbers

- The temperature range studied extends up to approximately 2.3 × T_c, where T_c = (154 ± 9) MeV is the pseudocritical temperature of the chiral transition in QCD
- The pure-gauge Yang-Mills critical temperature T_YM ≈ 260 MeV, with some lattice groups finding T_YM = (300 ± 3) MeV
- The ratio of strange to light quark masses is 2 m_s/(m_u + m_d) = 27.3 ± 0.7
- The experimental bound on the strong CP-violating parameter is |θ̄| < 10^{-10}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Horvatić et al., 2018) on the temperature dependence of η' and η meson masses, which used Shore's generalization (Shore, 2006) of the Witten-Veneziano relation to describe the crossover behavior of chiral symmetry restoration. The approach contrasts with earlier work (Benić et al., 2011) that predicted a sharp second-order phase transition leading to a massless axion at T_c, which is not supported by lattice QCD results (Aoki et al., 2012; Buchoff et al., 2013; Dick et al., 2015) showing a smooth crossover. The results enable predictions for axion mass temperature dependence that can be tested against future lattice calculations and inform axion dark matter models.
