# Solution of the center-of-mass problem in nuclear structure calculations

> The coupled-cluster wave function factorizes to a very good approximation into a product of an intrinsic wave function and a Gaussian for the center-of-mass coordinate. The width of the Gaussian is in general not identical to the oscillator length of the underlying single-particle basis. The quality of the separation can be verified by a simple procedure.

## Key claim
The coupled-cluster wave function factorizes into an intrinsic part and a Gaussian center-of-mass part even when the model space is not a complete \(N\hbar\omega\) oscillator space, provided the model space is sufficiently large. The width of the Gaussian is generally not identical to the oscillator length of the underlying single-particle basis, and the quality of the separation can be verified by computing expectation values of a generalized center-of-mass Hamiltonian.

## Method
The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock calculations followed by coupled-cluster calculations in the singles and doubles approximation (CCSD) for \(^{16}\)O, using a low-momentum interaction \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) with a smooth momentum cutoff \(\lambda = 1.8\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) and a chiral N\(^3\)LO interaction. They computed expectation values of a generalized center-of-mass Hamiltonian \(H_{\text{cm}}(\tilde{\omega})\) via the Hellmann-Feynman theorem, adding a small perturbation \(\beta H_{\text{cm}}(\omega)\) with \(\beta = 0.001\) to the intrinsic Hamiltonian. For the higher-momentum interaction, they employed the \(\Lambda\)-CCSD(T) approximation with model spaces of up to \(N=19\) oscillator shells and maximal single-particle orbital angular momentum \(l \le 13\).

## Key numbers
- For \(^{16}\)O with the low-momentum interaction and \(\hbar\omega = 20\ \text{MeV}\), the ground-state energy varies by about 1 MeV as \(\hbar\omega\) varies by more than a factor of two (model space of \(N=9\) oscillator shells).
- For \(^{16}\)O with the low-momentum interaction at \(\hbar\omega = 20\ \text{MeV}\), adding \(\beta H_{\text{cm}}(\omega)\) with \(\beta\) from zero to one changes the ground-state energy by merely 15 keV.
- For \(^{16}\)O with the low-momentum interaction, the expectation value \(E_{\text{cm}}(\tilde{\omega})\) of the generalized center-of-mass Hamiltonian is very small (some values near -0.01 MeV) compared to \(\hbar\tilde{\omega} \approx 20\ \text{MeV}\).
- For \(^{16}\)O with the chiral N\(^3\)LO interaction, the expectation values \(E_{\text{cm}}(\tilde{\omega})\) are below 1 MeV in size, and a simple two-level model yields about 6% squared overlap with spurious states, compared to \(\hbar\tilde{\omega} \approx 16\ \text{MeV}\).
- For \(^4\)He with the chiral potential, the ground-state energy in \(\Lambda\)-CCSD(T) is \(E \approx -25.56\ \text{MeV}\), compared to virtually exact Faddeev-Yakubowski calculations giving \(E \approx -25.41\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context
This work addresses the center-of-mass problem in nuclear structure calculations, which has been handled rigorously only in two ways: using translationally invariant Jacobi coordinates (restricted to \(A<7\) or so, as in Bishop, Nogga, and Bacca) or using a full \(N\hbar\omega\) oscillator space (as in no-core shell-model calculations by Navratil (2000, 2009) and \(0\hbar\omega\) shell-model calculations by Caurier and Otsuka). Both rigorous approaches scale exponentially with nucleon number. The present paper extends the coupled-cluster method (previously applied by Coester (1958, 1960), Cizek (1966, 1969), Kummel (1978), Mihaila (2000), Dean (2004), and Bartlett (2007)) by demonstrating that its wave functions approximately factorize into intrinsic and center-of-mass parts, enabling verifiable translationally invariant calculations for heavier nuclei and weakly bound systems where the oscillator basis is inadequate.
