# High-precision $Q$-value measurement and nuclear matrix elements for the double-$β$ decay of $^{98}$Mo

> Neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay and the standard two-neutrino double-beta ($2νββ$) decay of $^{98}$Mo have been studied. The double-beta decay $Q$-value has been determined as $Q_{ββ}=113.668(68)$ keV using the JYFLTRAP Penning trap mass spectrometer. It is in agreement with the literature value, $Q_{ββ}=109(6)$ keV, but almost 90 times more precise. Based on the measured $Q$-value, precise phase-space factors for $2νββ$ decay and $0νββ$ decay, needed in the half-life predictions, have been calculated. Furthermore, the involved nuclear matrix elements have been computed in the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) and the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2) frameworks. Finally, predictions for the $2νββ$ decay are given, suggesting a much longer half-life than for the currently observed cases.

## Key claim
The double-beta decay Q-value of ⁹⁸Mo is measured directly for the first time as 113.668(68) keV, which agrees with the literature value of 109(6) keV but is almost 90 times more precise; based on this, the predicted half-life for the two-neutrino mode is much longer than for currently observed cases, on the order of 10²⁹ years.

## Method
The Q-value was determined by measuring cyclotron frequency ratios of singly-charged ⁹⁸Mo⁺ and ⁹⁸Ru⁺ ions using the phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance (PI-ICR) technique at the JYFLTRAP Penning trap mass spectrometer. Nuclear matrix elements for both two-neutrino and neutrinoless double-beta decay were computed in the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with a large no-core single-particle basis and in the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2) with closure approximation. Phase-space factors were calculated using exact Dirac electron wave functions including screening by the electron cloud.

## Key numbers
- Q-value for ⁹⁸Mo → ⁹⁸Ru: Q_ββ = 113.668(68) keV, measured directly for the first time
- Literature Q-value: 109(6) keV (Wang 2021), improved by a factor of about 90 in precision
- Phase-space factor for neutrinoless mode: G_0ν = 6.18 × 10⁻¹⁸ y⁻¹
- Phase-space factor for two-neutrino mode: G_2ν = 3.71 × 10⁻²⁹ y⁻¹
- Predicted two-neutrino half-life (pnQRPA, g_A^eff = 1.27): t_1/2^(2ν) = 1.031 × 10²⁹ y
- Predicted two-neutrino half-life (IBM-2, g_A^eff = 1.27): t_1/2^(2ν) = 0.718 × 10²⁹ y
- Neutrinoless half-life range (pnQRPA, g_A^eff = 1.27, m_ββ = 0.01–0.1 eV): 0.55 × 10²⁹ y to 66.3 × 10²⁹ y
- Neutrinoless half-life range (IBM-2, g_A^eff = 1.27, m_ββ = 0.01–0.1 eV): 0.72 × 10²⁹ y to 77.4 × 10²⁹ y
- Q-value for ⁹⁶Ru double-electron capture: Q_εε = 2714.583(50) keV, 2.6 times more precise than the SHIPTRAP value of 2714.51(13) keV (Eliseev 2011)

## Context
This work provides the first direct Penning-trap measurement of the ⁹⁸Mo double-beta decay Q-value, replacing the earlier indirect value from Damerow (1963) that relied on a mass difference measured with a double-focusing mass spectrometer. The calculated nuclear matrix elements are the first for ⁹⁸Mo in the no-core pnQRPA and IBM-2 frameworks, and the predicted half-lives are consistently smaller than those from earlier PHFB (Chandra 2005) and SRQRPA (Bobyk 2000) calculations. The results enable more reliable half-life predictions for future experimental searches, though the half-lives are well beyond current experimental sensitivities of about 10²⁵–10²⁶ years.
