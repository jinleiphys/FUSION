# Insights into neutron star equation of state by machine learning

> Due to its powerful capability and high efficiency in big data analysis, machine learning has been applied in various fields. We construct a neural network platform to constrain the behaviors of the equation of state of nuclear matter with respect to the properties of nuclear matter at saturation density and the properties of neutron stars. It is found that the neural network is able to give reasonable predictions of parameter space and provide new hints into the constraints of hadron interactions. As a specific example, we take the relativistic mean field approximation in a widely accepted Walecka-type model to illustrate the feasibility and efficiency of the platform. The results show that the neural network can indeed estimate the parameters of the model at a certain precision such that both the properties of nuclear matter around saturation density and global properties of neutron stars can be saturated. The optimization of the present modularly designed neural network and extension to other effective models are straightforward.

## Key claim

The neural network platform can estimate the parameters of a relativistic mean field model such that both nuclear matter properties around saturation density and global neutron star properties are simultaneously satisfied, but the optimal parameter set does not coincide with the center values of the six input constraints, likely because the dimension of the data space exceeds the number of free parameters (seven).

## Method

The authors constructed a neural network with 40 fully connected layers (with batch normalization between each pair) to solve the coupled equations of motion of the meson fields in a Walecka-type relativistic mean field model. The loss function was defined as the squared residuals of the equations of motion themselves. The pretraining set was generated randomly over nucleon densities from zero to 10 times saturation density with a distribution function designed to improve low-density description. After solving the equations of motion, the equation of state was obtained from the Lagrangian, and neutron star properties were calculated by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.155 \pm 0.05\) fm\(^{-3}\) (constraint), with optimal value 0.161 fm\(^{-3}\).
* Binding energy per nucleon: \(e_0 = -16 \pm 1.0\) MeV (constraint), with optimal value \(-15.7\) MeV.
* Symmetry energy: \(E_{\mathrm{sym}} = 32.0 \pm 2.0\) MeV (constraint), with optimal value 28.6 MeV.
* Incompressibility coefficient: \(K_0 = 250 \pm 50\) MeV (constraint), with optimal value 266 MeV.
* Neutron star mass (primary in GW170817): \(M = 1.48 \pm 0.12\) \(M_\odot\) (constraint).
* Neutron star radius (primary in GW170817): \(R = 11.9 \pm 1.4\) km (constraint).
* Optimal coupling constants: \(g_\sigma = 9.82\), \(g_\omega = 11.8\), \(g_\rho = 3.42\), \(g_3 = 1.26\), \(c_3 = 72.6\), \(g_2 = -1550\) MeV, \(m_\sigma = 531\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of machine learning to nuclear matter equation of state studies, following prior efforts using neural networks with parameterizations such as SLy4, APR, and BSK20 (Drischler 2020, Fujimoto 2019, Morawski 2020, Traversi 2020, Ferreira 2021, Annala 2021, Krastev 2021, Soma 2022, Chatterjee 2023, Zhou 2023). The platform is designed to be modular and extendable to other effective models, including chiral effective field theories (Li 2016), and future work will incorporate additional constraints such as tidal deformability, symmetry energy density slope, and skewness coefficient.
