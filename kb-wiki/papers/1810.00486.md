# Hard-core Radius of Nucleons within the Induced Surface Tension Approach

> In this work we discuss a novel approach to model the hadronic and nuclear matter equations of state using the induced surface tension concept. Since the obtained equations of state, classical and quantum, are among the most successful ones in describing the properties of low density phases of strongly interacting matter, they set strong restrictions on the possible value of the hard-core radius of nucleons. Therefore, we perform a detailed analysis of its value which follows from hadronic and nuclear matter properties and find the most trustworthy range of its values: the hard-core radius of nucleons is 0.30--0.36 fm. A comparison with the phenomenology of neutron stars implies that the hard-core radius of nucleons has to be temperature and density dependent.

## Key claim

The hard-core radius of nucleons is constrained to 0.30–0.36 fm when derived from hadronic and nuclear matter properties using the induced surface tension approach, but this range must be temperature and density dependent to remain consistent with neutron star phenomenology.

## Method

The authors employed the induced surface tension (IST) equation of state, a multicomponent formulation that extends beyond the Van der Waals approximation by including both eigenvolume and eigensurface terms for each hadronic species. They fitted 111 independent hadron yield ratios measured at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, and separately fitted 11 ratios from ALICE, using the IST EoS with five distinct hard-core radii for baryons, mesons, pions, kaons, and Lambda hyperons. For nuclear matter, they developed a quantum IST (QIST) EoS with four adjustable parameters (hard-core radius, mean-field strength, mean-field exponent, and constant surface potential) and required it to simultaneously reproduce normal nuclear matter properties and obey the proton flow constraint from heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Hard-core radius of nucleons from hadronic multiplicity fits: \(R_N = 0.365 \pm 0.03\) fm (from IST EoS fit to AGS, SPS, RHIC data)
- Combined fit quality for AGS, SPS, RHIC, and ALICE data: \(\chi^2_{tot}/dof \simeq 66.02/60 \simeq 1.1\)
- Range of nucleon hard-core radius from nuclear matter QIST EoS obeying proton flow constraint: \(0.28\ \text{fm} \le R_N \le 0.4\) fm (after applying maximal packing fraction \(\eta_{max} = 0.2\))
- Range from S-matrix second virial coefficient constraint at \(100\ \text{MeV} \le T \le 170\ \text{MeV}\): \(0.31\ \text{fm} \le R_N \le 0.42\) fm
- Final constrained range from combined virial coefficient analysis: \(R_N \in [0.275; 0.36]\) fm
- Normal nuclear matter density: \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\); binding energy per nucleon: \(W_0 = -16\) MeV
- Incompressibility factor for \(\kappa=0.1\): \(K_0 \simeq 306\) MeV (minimal value)
- Hard-core radii of other hadrons from IST EoS: pions \(R_\pi = 0.15 \pm 0.02\) fm, kaons \(R_K = 0.395 \pm 0.03\) fm, Lambda hyperons \(R_\Lambda = 0.085 \pm 0.015\) fm

## Context

This work extends the induced surface tension approach developed by Bugaev et al. (2017) and the IST-based hadron resonance gas model (IST1, IST2, IST3) to constrain the nucleon hard-core radius more precisely than previous Van der Waals-based models. The results contradict the single-radius HRGM of Andronic et al. (2005) which used \(R_{all} = 0.3\) fm and achieved worse fit quality (\(\chi^2_{tot}/dof \simeq 1.37\) vs. 1.1). The analysis enables future application of the IST EoS to neutron star phenomenology, though the authors note that neutron star constraints from Antoniadis et al. (2013) and Klahn et al. (2006) require larger HCR values (0.42–0.47 fm) and temperature/density dependence, while the recent gravitational wave event GW170817 (Annala et al., 2017) sets upper limits on neutron star compactness that may conflict with the largest HCR values used in models such as Typel's DD2_p40 EoS (2016).
