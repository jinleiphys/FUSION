# Electroweak Form Factors of the $Δ$(1232) Resonance

> Nucleon $\to Δ(1232)$ transition electroweak form factors are discussed in a single pion production model with nonresonant background terms originating from a chiral perturbation theory. Fits to electron-proton scattering $F_2$ as well as neutrino scattering bubble chamber experimental data are performed. Both $ν$-proton and $ν$-neutron channel data are discussed in a unified statistical model. A new parametrization of the $N\toΔ(1232)$ vector form factors is proposed. Fit to neutrino scattering data gives axial mass $M_{AΔ}=0.85{+0.09}{-0.08}\;$(GeV) and $C_5^A(0) = 1.10{+0.15}{-0.14}$ in accordance with Goldberger-Treiman relation as long as deuteron nuclear effects are considered.

# Electroweak Form Factors of the Δ(1232) Resonance

## Key claim

The authors propose a new parametrization of the N→Δ(1232) vector form factors and perform a combined fit to electron-proton scattering and neutrino bubble chamber data, finding that the axial mass M_{AΔ}=0.85+0.09-0.08 GeV and C_5^A(0)=1.10+0.15-0.14 are consistent with the Goldberger-Treiman relation only when deuteron nuclear effects are included in the analysis.

## Method

The authors use the HNV model (Hernandez et al., 2007) for single pion production, which includes nonresonant background terms from chiral perturbation theory, and fit vector form factors to inclusive electron-proton F_2 structure function data from Osipenko et al. (2003) supplemented by MAID2007 predictions. Axial form factors are extracted from ANL bubble chamber data for both neutrino-proton and neutrino-neutron channels using a unified statistical model that incorporates flux normalization uncertainties and deuteron nuclear effects via a Paris potential nucleon momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass from joint fit with deuteron effects: M_{AΔ}=0.85+0.09-0.08 GeV
- Axial form factor at Q^2=0 from joint fit with deuteron effects: C_5^A(0)=1.10+0.15-0.14
- Best fit vector form factor at Q^2=0: C_3^V(0)=2.10
- Best fit vector form factor at Q^2=0: C_5^V(0)=0.63
- Vector mass used in dipole parametrization: M_V=0.84 GeV
- χ^2/NDF for joint fit with deuteron effects: 2.06 with 30 degrees of freedom
- Normalization factor for ANL data in joint deuteron fit: p_{ANL}=0.90
- Q^2 range of electron data included: 0.225 GeV^2 to 2.025 GeV^2
- Invariant mass cut for neutrino analysis: W<1.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the statistical framework of Graczyk et al. (2009) to include neutron channels and nonresonant background, addressing tensions between previous analyses by Hernandez et al. (2007, 2010) that found C_5^A(0) values far from the Goldberger-Treiman relation estimate of approximately 1.2. The new vector form factor parametrization improves upon the Lalakulich-Paschos form factors (Lalakulich et al., 2006) when used with the same HNV background model, giving better agreement with electron scattering data. The results enable improved modeling of single pion production for neutrino oscillation experiments such as T2K, MINOS, NOvA, MiniBooNE, and LBNE, where this interaction channel contributes about one-third of the total cross section at 1 GeV.
