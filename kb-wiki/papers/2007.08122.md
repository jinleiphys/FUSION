# Second-forbidden nonunique $β^-$ decays of $^{24}$Na and $^{36}$Cl assessed by the nuclear shell model

> We have performed a systematic study of the log$ft$ values, shape factors and electron spectra for the second-forbidden nonunique $β^-$ decays of $^{24}$Na$(4^+) \rightarrow ^{24}$Mg$(2^+)$ and $^{36}$Cl$(2^+) \rightarrow ^{36}$Ar$(0^+)$ transitions under the framework of the nuclear shell model. We have performed the shell model calculations in the $sd$ model space, using more recent microscopic effective interactions such as Daejeon16, chiral N3LO, and JISP16. These interactions are derived from the no-core shell model wave functions using Okubo-Lee-Suzuki transformation.For comparison, we have also shown the results obtain from the phenomenological USDB interaction. To test the predictive power of these interactions first we have computed low-lying energy spectra of parent and daughter nuclei involved in these transitions. The computed results for energy spectra, nuclear matrix elements, log$ft$ values, shape factors, electron spectra and decomposition of the integrated shape factor are reported and compare with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper assesses whether modern microscopic effective interactions (Daejeon16/DJ16A, chiral N3LO, JISP16) derived from no-core shell model wave functions can reproduce second-forbidden nonunique beta-decay observables for 24Na and 36Cl, but finds that pure shell-model predictions for logft values deviate significantly from experiment; only after constraining the relativistic vector matrix element using conserved vector current (CVC) theory and experimental partial half-lives does agreement become satisfactory.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the sd model space using the NuShellX code, with one-body transition densities computed from three microscopic effective interactions (DJ16A, JISP16, N3LO) and the phenomenological USDB interaction. The single-particle matrix elements were evaluated using harmonic-oscillator wave functions in the Condon-Shortley phase convention, and the shape factors, electron spectra, and logft values were computed within the Behrens-Buhring formalism for forbidden nonunique beta decay. The relativistic vector matrix element, which vanishes in the 0-hbar-omega sd-shell space, was instead determined by fitting to the experimental partial half-life under CVC theory.

## Key numbers

- For 24Na(4+) to 24Mg(2+) second-forbidden nonunique decay: experimental logft = 11.340(4); pure shell-model predictions range from 12.237 (USDB) to 14.227 (N3LO); after CVC constraint all interactions give logft near 11.34 (11.331 to 11.367)
- For 36Cl(2+) to 36Ar(0+) second-forbidden nonunique decay: experimental logft = 13.321(3); pure shell-model predictions range from 12.635 (USDB) to 13.978 (DJ16A); after CVC constraint values range from 13.108 (DJ16A) to 13.555 (JISP16)
- Q-value for 24Na decay: 4.147 MeV; for 36Cl decay: 0.710 MeV (709.547 keV)
- For 24Na allowed transition 4+ to 3_1+: experimental logft = 6.60(2); USDB gives 5.997 (g_A=1.27) and 6.205 (g_A=1.00)
- For 24Na allowed transition 4+ to 4_1+: experimental logft = 6.11(1); USDB gives 5.685 (g_A=1.27) and 5.892 (g_A=1.00)
- For 36Cl, the branching ratio of the second-forbidden nonunique decay is 98.1%; for 24Na it is 0.064%

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of forbidden beta decay by applying modern ab initio derived interactions (DJ16A, N3LO, JISP16) to second-forbidden nonunique transitions, where earlier theoretical work on 36Cl by Sadler (1993) used pure 1d3/2 to 1d3/2 transitions and sd-shell calculations, and no theoretical estimate existed for 24Na. The study is motivated by recent observations of second-forbidden nonunique decay in 20F by Kirsebom (2019) that changed understanding of intermediate-mass star evolution, and by the need to test whether microscopic interactions can reproduce forbidden decay observables. The results enable future experimental comparisons for 24Na, where no experimental shape factors or electron spectra currently exist, and demonstrate the necessity of CVC-constrained matrix elements for accurate predictions.
