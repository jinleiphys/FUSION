# Mass spectra and transition magnetic moments of low lying charmed baryons in a quark model

> Excited state mass spectra of the low lying single charmed baryons with non strangeness have been calculated in a hypercentral approach. The six-dimensional hyperradial Schrödinger equation is solved by applying a simple variational method. We extend our scheme to predict the magnetic moments and the $ \frac{3}{2}^+ \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}^+ $ transition magnetic moments of $ Σ_{c} $ and $ Λ_{c} $ state baryons. A comparison of our results with the experimental data and predictions obtained in recent models is also presented.

# Mass spectra and transition magnetic moments of low lying charmed baryons in a quark model

## Key claim
The hypercentral constituent quark model with a Coulomb-plus-linear confining potential, solved variationally and treated with first-order perturbation theory for hyperfine interactions, reproduces the known masses and predicts transition magnetic moments for non-strange single-charmed baryons, but the method is explicitly stated to be valid only "to a good approximation, for the low lying state baryons."

## Method
The authors solve the six-dimensional hyperradial Schrödinger equation for a three-quark system using a variational method with hyperCoulomb wavefunctions as the unperturbed basis. The confining potential is the Cornell-type form \(-\tau/x + \beta x\); the linear term is treated as a perturbation on the hyperCoulomb part. Hyperfine interactions (spin-spin, isospin-isospin, and spin-orbit) are added perturbatively, with parameters fitted to light baryon resonances from prior work and to the \(\Sigma_c(2455)\)–\(\Lambda_c(2286)\) mass difference. Effective quark masses are computed from the binding energy, and magnetic moments (including \(\frac{3}{2}^+ \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}^+\) transition moments) are obtained from spin-flavour wavefunctions.

## Key numbers
- \(\Lambda_c(2286)^+\) ground state mass: reproduced as the known experimental value (2286 MeV).
- \(\Lambda_c(2595)^+\) (first orbital excited, \(J=1/2^-\)) and \(\Lambda_c(2625)^+\) (\(J=3/2^-\)): masses are predicted and compared to experiment.
- \(\Lambda_c(2765)^+\): predicted as the first radial excited state of \(\Lambda_c(2286)^+\) with \(J^P = 1/2^+\).
- \(\Sigma_c(2800)\): predicted as a \(1P\) state with a mass difference of 62 MeV to the experimental mass of 2806 MeV.
- Transition magnetic moments for \(\frac{3}{2}^+ \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}^+\) of \(\Sigma_c\) and \(\Lambda_c\) states are tabulated and compared with other models (e.g., Sharma (2010), Dhir (2013), Bernotas (2013), Aliev (2012)).

## Context
This work extends the hypercentral constituent quark model previously applied to light baryons by Ghalenovi (2013) to the single-charmed sector. It builds on the non-relativistic quark model approach of Isgur and Karl (1978) and the Cornell potential from lattice QCD. The results are compared with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (2018) and CLEO (2001), as well as with theoretical predictions from Shah (2016), Ebert (2011), Yoshida (2015), Chen (2017), and Yamaguchi (2017). The calculated transition magnetic moments are closest to those of Dhir (2013). The work enables future studies of radiative decays of charmed baryons and identification of excited states such as \(\Lambda_c(2765)^+\) and \(\Sigma_c(2800)\).
