# Effects of a phase transition on HBT correlations in an integrated Boltzmann+Hydrodynamics approach

> A systematic study of HBT radii of pions, produced in heavy ion collisions in the intermediate energy regime (SPS), from an integrated (3+1)d Boltzmann+hydrodynamics approach is presented. The calculations in this hybrid approach, incorporating an hydrodynamic stage into the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics transport model, allow for a comparison of different equations of state retaining the same initial conditions and final freeze-out. The results are also compared to the pure cascade transport model calculations in the context of the available data. Furthermore, the effect of different treatments of the hydrodynamic freeze-out procedure on the HBT radii are investigated. It is found that the HBT radii are essentially insensitive to the details of the freeze-out prescription as long as the final hadronic interactions in the cascade are taken into account. The HBT radii $R_L$ and $R_O$ and the $R_O/R_S$ ratio are sensitive to the EoS that is employed during the hydrodynamic evolution. We conclude that the increased lifetime in case of a phase transition to a QGP (via a Bag Model equation of state) is not supported by the available data.

## Key claim

The HBT radii \(R_L\) and \(R_O\) and the \(R_O/R_S\) ratio are sensitive to the equation of state employed during the hydrodynamic evolution, but the increased lifetime predicted by a first-order phase transition to a QGP (via a Bag Model equation of state) is not supported by the available SPS data.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid (3+1)d Boltzmann+hydrodynamics approach that embeds a full ideal relativistic one-fluid evolution into the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model. The hydrodynamic stage was evolved with three different equations of state (hadron gas, Bag Model, and chiral hadron gas) while keeping the same initial conditions and final freeze-out procedure. The Cooper-Frye formula was used to convert hydrodynamic fields to particles on either an isochronous or a gradual freeze-out hypersurface, after which final-state hadronic rescatterings and resonance decays were handled by the UrQMD cascade.

## Key numbers

- Central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies \(E_{\rm lab}=20A\), \(30A\), \(40A\), \(80A\), and \(158A\) GeV were simulated.
- The hydrodynamic evolution started at \(t_{\rm start} = {2R}/{\sqrt{\gamma^2 -1}}\) and stopped when the energy density of all cells dropped below \(5\cdot\varepsilon_0\) (approximately \(730\ {\rm MeV/fm}^3\)).
- For the gradual freeze-out procedure, transverse slices of thickness \(\Delta z = 0.2\ {\rm fm}\) were frozen out individually.
- About 2500 events were calculated for each equation of state, with one hundred million negatively charged pion pairs analyzed per event sample.
- The \(R_O/R_S\) ratio was evaluated at a transverse momentum bin of \(k_T = 200-300\ {\rm MeV}/c\).

## Context

This work extends previous HBT studies using the UrQMD model (Li et al., 2006-2008) by incorporating a full (3+1)d hydrodynamic stage to test the sensitivity of HBT radii to the equation of state. It contrasts with earlier hydrodynamic studies (Rischke, 1996; Zschiesche, 2001) by using the same initial conditions and freeze-out for all equations of state. The results enable future investigations of bulk and shear viscosity effects in the hydrodynamic phase and more precise treatment of non-equilibrated particle emission from early stages.
