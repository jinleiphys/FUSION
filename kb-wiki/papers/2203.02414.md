# Consequences of the Dresden-II reactor data for the weak mixing angle and new physics

> The Dresden-II reactor experiment has recently reported a suggestive evidence for the observation of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, using a germanium detector. Given the low recoil energy threshold, these data are particularly interesting for a low-energy determination of the weak mixing angle and for the study of new physics leading to spectral distortions at low momentum transfer. Using two hypotheses for the quenching factor, we study the impact of the data on: (i) The weak mixing angle at a renormalization scale of $\sim 10\,\text{MeV}$, (ii) neutrino generalized interactions with light mediators, (iii) the sterile neutrino dipole portal. The results for the weak mixing angle show a strong dependence on the quenching factor choice. Although still with large uncertainties, the Dresden-II data provide for the first time a determination of $\sin^2θ_W$ at such scale using coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data. Tight upper limits are placed on the light vector, scalar and tensor mediator scenarios. Kinematic constraints implied by the reactor anti-neutrino flux and the ionization energy threshold allow the sterile neutrino dipole portal to produce up-scattering events with sterile neutrino masses up to $\sim 8\,$MeV. In this context, we find that limits are also sensitive to the quenching factor choice, but in both cases competitive with those derived from XENON1T data and more stringent that those derived with COHERENT data, in the same sterile neutrino mass range.

## Key claim

The Dresden-II reactor data provide the first determination of the weak mixing angle at a renormalization scale of approximately 10 MeV using coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, but the results show a strong dependence on the choice of quenching factor model, with best-fit values differing by about 6.5% between the two hypotheses considered.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared statistical analysis of the Dresden-II reactor experiment's residual event spectrum (20 energy bins from 0.2 to 0.4 keV_ee, from 96.4 days exposure) using two different quenching factor models: a modified Lindhard model with free parameters k and q, and an iron-filter quenching factor from the experiment's ancillary files. They convolved the reactor antineutrino flux with coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections including Standard Model and new physics contributions, applied detector energy resolution smearing, and marginalized over nuisance parameters including a 5% flux normalization uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Weak mixing angle from modified Lindhard quenching factor: sin^2(theta_W) = 0.178 (+0.280, -0.090) at 1 sigma
- Weak mixing angle from iron-filter quenching factor: sin^2(theta_W) = 0.190 (+0.039, -0.046) at 1 sigma
- Vector mediator coupling limit at 90% C.L. for masses below 100 keV: g_V less than 8 x 10^{-6} (Lindhard QF) and g_V less than 4.5 x 10^{-6} (iron-filter QF)
- Sterile neutrino mass kinematic upper bound from reactor antineutrino energies: m_4 less than approximately 8 MeV
- Detector mass: 2.924 kg germanium; energy threshold: 0.2 keV_ee; reactor power: 2.96 GW; distance: approximately 10 m; exposure: 96.4 days
- Antineutrino flux normalization: 4.8 x 10^{13} antineutrinos per cm^2 per second

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of the Dresden-II data by Liao (2022) and Coloma (2022) to include the weak mixing angle determination and neutrino generalized interactions with light mediators, as well as the sterile neutrino dipole portal. The results improve upon limits from COHERENT CsI and LAr data (which found g_V less than 6 x 10^{-5} at 90% C.L.) by roughly an order of magnitude for light vector mediators, and produce sterile neutrino dipole portal limits competitive with XENON1T and more stringent than COHERENT in the same sterile neutrino mass range. Future improvements require better understanding of the germanium quenching factor at sub-keV recoil energies.
