# Charge and isospin fluctuations in a non-ideal pion gas with dynamically fixed particle number

> We study the behavior of the non-ideal pion gas with the dynamically fixed number of particles, formed on an intermediate stage in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The pion spectrum is calculated within the self-consistent Hartree approximation. General expressions are derived for cross-covariances of the number of various particle species in the pion gas of an arbitrary isospin composition. The behavior of the cross-variances is analyzed for the temperature approaching from above the maximal critical temperature of the Bose-Einstein condensation for the pion species $a=\pm,0$, i.e. for $T>\max T_{\rm cr}^a$. It is shown that in case of the system with equal averaged numbers of isospin species, the variance of the charge, $Q=N_{+}-N_{-}$, diverges at $T\to T_{\rm cr}=T_{\rm cr}^a$, whereas variances of the total particle number, $N=N_{+} + N_{-} + N_{0}$, and of a relative abundance of charged and neutral pions, $G=(N_{+}+N_{-})/2 - N_{0}$, remain finite in the critical point. Then fluctuations are studied in the pion gas with small isospin imbalance $0<|G|\ll N$ and $0<|Q|\ll N$\, and shifts of the effective masses, chemical potentials and values of critical temperatures are calculated for various pion species, and the highest critical temperature, $\mbox{max}T_{\rm cr}^{a}$ is found, above which the pion system exists in the non-condensed phase. Various pion cross variances are calculated for $T>\mbox{max}T_{\rm cr}^{a}$, which prove to be strongly dependent on the isospin composition of the system, whereas the variances of $N$ and $G$ are found to be independent on the isospin imbalance up to the term linear in $G/N$ and $Q/N$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a non-ideal pion gas with dynamically fixed particle number, the variance of the charge diverges at the critical temperature of Bose-Einstein condensation only for the isospin-symmetric case, whereas the variances of the total particle number and of the relative abundance of charged and neutral pions remain finite at that critical point; for systems with small isospin imbalance, the cross-variances depend strongly on the isospin composition, but the variances of total number and of the charged-neutral imbalance are independent of the imbalance up to linear order in the small parameters.

## Method

The authors model the non-ideal pion gas using a λφ⁴ interaction Lagrangian, retaining only terms with equal numbers of creation and annihilation operators to enforce a dynamically fixed particle number between chemical and kinetic freeze-out. They compute the pion spectrum within the self-consistent Hartree approximation, where the vacuum pion mass is replaced by an effective mass that depends on the chemical potentials and temperature through integral functions. General expressions for cross-variances of pion numbers are derived from the grand-canonical partition function, and these are evaluated analytically near the critical temperature of Bose-Einstein condensation using expansions of the relevant integrals.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant is estimated as λ = mπ²/(2fπ²) ≈ 1.13, where mπ = 139 MeV and fπ = 93 MeV.
- The nuclear saturation density is n0 = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- For a gas with one pion species at λ ≳ 1, the reduced critical temperature tcr,a = Tcr,a/μcr,a is less than 1 for densities 0 < n ≲ 1.5 n0.
- For an isospin-symmetrical gas with three species, tcr,a < 1 for densities 0 < n ≲ 5 n0.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature is Tchem ≈ 155 MeV, and the kinetic freeze-out temperature is Tkin ∼ 100–120 MeV.
- Pion spectra at LHC with √sNN = 2.76 TeV can be fitted with chemical potential μ ≈ 134.9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of fluctuations in a non-ideal pion gas within the Hartree approximation (KV18, BKV18), which showed that the variance of the pion number remains finite at the critical temperature for an isospin-symmetric system. It generalizes those results to systems with arbitrary isospin composition and small isospin imbalance, relevant for heavy-ion collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies where the pion number is approximately fixed between chemical and kinetic freeze-out (Goity 1989, Gerber 1990). The analysis enables future event-by-event comparisons with experimental fluctuation measurements, potentially signaling proximity to pion Bose-Einstein condensation in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
