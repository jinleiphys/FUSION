# Event patterns extracted from anisotropic spectra of charged particles produced in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV

> Event patterns extracted from anisotropic spectra of charged particles produced in lead-lead collisions at 2.76 TeV are investigated. We use an inverse power-law resulted from the QCD calculus to describe the transverse momentum spectrum in the hard scattering process, and a revised Erlang distribution resulted from a multisource thermal model to describe the transverse momentum spectrum and anisotropic flow in the soft excitation process. The pseudorapidity distribution is described by a three-Gaussian function which is a revision of the Landau hydrodynamic model. Thus, the event patterns at the kinetic freeze-out are displayed by the scatter plots of the considered particles in the three-dimensional velocity, momentum, and rapidity spaces.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hybrid model combining a QCD-inspired inverse power-law for hard scattering, a revised Erlang distribution (with deformation and movement parameters) for soft excitation, and a three-Gaussian pseudorapidity distribution can approximately describe the anisotropic spectra of charged particles in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the authors note that the solid curves for the intermediate centrality classes are "not the best fitted results" due to the constraint of simultaneously describing the elliptic flow data.

## Method

The authors fit the transverse momentum spectra using a superposition of an inverse power-law (from QCD calculus) for hard scattering and a revised Erlang distribution (from a multisource thermal model) for soft excitation, where the revision introduces deformation parameters \(a_x, a_y\) and movement parameters \(b_x, b_y\) to account for anisotropic flow. The pseudorapidity distributions are fitted with a three-Gaussian function as a revision of the Landau hydrodynamic model. Using the fitted parameters, Monte Carlo methods generate discrete kinematic variables to produce scatter plots in three-dimensional velocity, momentum, and rapidity spaces for event patterns at kinetic freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- For charged particles in the 10-20% centrality class: \(p_0 = 0.66 \pm 0.03\) GeV/\(c\), \(n = 6.15 \pm 0.31\), \(k_1 = 0.52 \pm 0.03\), \(\langle p_{Ti} \rangle = 0.39 \pm 0.01\) GeV/\(c\), with \(\chi^2/\text{dof}|_1 = 94.424/58\) for the solid curve
- For the elliptic flow of charged particles in the 10-20% centrality class: \(a_x = 1.09 \pm 0.01\), \(b_x = 0.23 \pm 0.01\) (with 50% probability for positive and negative values), \(a_y = 1.00 \pm 0.01\), \(b_y = 0.00 \pm 0.01\), with \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 5.304/8\)
- For charged particles in the 0-5% centrality class pseudorapidity distribution: \(\delta\eta = 0.90 \pm 0.05\), \(\sigma_{\eta1} = 1.40 \pm 0.07\), \(\sigma_{\eta2} = 2.00 \pm 0.10\), \(k_2 = 0.20 \pm 0.01\), with \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 0.720/1\) for the solid curve
- The average rest mass used for charged particles is \(m_0 = 0.174\) GeV/\(c^2\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous isotropic emission studies (Chen and Liu, 2015-2017) by incorporating non-zero elliptic flow through a revised Erlang distribution with deformation and movement parameters. It enables the construction of three-dimensional event patterns at kinetic freeze-out that include anisotropic flow effects, using ALICE experimental data (Abelev et al., 2013; Adam et al., 2016) for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. The approach contrasts with purely hydrodynamic models (Heinz and Snellings, 2013; Gale et al., 2013) by using a hybrid parameterization that separates hard and soft processes.
