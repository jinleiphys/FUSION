# Nuclear matter saturation in a relativistic chiral theory and QCD susceptibilities

> We study a chiral relativistic theory of nuclear matter aimed at the desciption of both the binding and saturation properties and the QCD properties, quark condensate and QCD susceptibilities. For this purpose the nucleon scalar response of the quark-meson coupling model is introduced in the linear sigma model. The consequences for the nuclear and the QCD scalar susceptibilities are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors show that incorporating the nucleonic scalar response (scalar polarizability) from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model into the linear sigma model can counterbalance the chiral softening of the sigma mass and restore nuclear matter saturation, which otherwise fails in standard chiral effective theories.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic chiral Lagrangian based on the linear sigma model with an additional omega meson field, and introduce a term proportional to the nucleon scalar susceptibility κ_NS that couples the scalar field squared to the nucleon density. They solve the mean-field equations of state, compute the in-medium sigma mass, the QCD scalar susceptibility, and the RPA polarization propagators for scalar and vector channels, then adjust parameters (m_σ, g_ω, and κ_NS) to reproduce empirical saturation properties.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ_0 = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A = −15.3 MeV (for the set with m_σ = 715 MeV, g_ω = 7.47, and no chiral potential)
- Incompressibility K = 260 MeV (for the set with m_σ = 715 MeV, g_ω = 7.47, and no chiral potential)
- Effective nucleon mass at ρ_0: M*_N = 760 MeV (for the set with field-dependent susceptibility, m_σ = 750 MeV, g_ω = 6.8, C = 1)
- Dimensionless nucleon susceptibility parameter C = (f_π² / 2 M_N) κ_NS = 0.45 (from QMC), increased to 0.8–1.0 in the chiral model
- Incompressibility K = 270 MeV (for the set with field-dependent susceptibility, m_σ = 750 MeV, g_ω = 6.8, C = 1)
- Residual interaction Landau-Migdal parameter F_0 ≈ 0.03 at ρ_0 (for the field-dependent susceptibility set)
- QCD scalar susceptibility enhancement factor at ρ_0: about 5 times the vacuum value
- Difference between pseudoscalar and scalar susceptibilities (normalized to vacuum scalar): 28 in vacuum, 23 at ρ_0

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral effective model (Chanfray, Ericson, Guichon 2002) by incorporating the nucleonic scalar response from the quark-meson coupling model (Guichon 1988, Guichon et al. 1996) to solve the saturation problem identified in chiral effective theories (Birse, Tarrus 2001). It contrasts with pure Quantum Hadrodynamics (Serot, Walecka 1986, 1997) which lacks chiral constraints and obtains saturation through large Dirac-sea polarization. The results enable a consistent description of both nuclear saturation and QCD susceptibilities, and predict a density-dependent residual particle-hole interaction that turns from attraction to repulsion near saturation density, with implications for nuclear response functions and the interpretation of (e,e') scattering data (Alberico et al. 1987).
