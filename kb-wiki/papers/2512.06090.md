# Photon emission from weakly magnetized neutral pions

> Using a hadronic framework, we derive an explicit expression for photon production from neutral pions in a weak background magnetic field. Our calculation is built on the proton triangle diagram with an effective Yukawa $π^0$-proton coupling, offering an alternative to quark-level descriptions that is advantageous when the magnetic length greatly exceeds the proton size. Corrections to the pion decay constant are computed up to second order in the magnetic field strength, revealing that the field generally suppresses the decay rate. Quantitatively, however, the effect remains modest even for fields as strong as $|eB|\simeq m_π^2$. The differential photon emission rate exhibits anisotropy, with the strongest suppression occurring when the pion momentum is perpendicular to the magnetic field. Overall, the modification of the $π^0 \to γγ$ rate is parametrically small, scaling as $|eB|^2/m_P^4$, where $m_P$ is the proton mass. Nevertheless, under favorable conditions, the resulting angular anisotropy could lead to observable signatures in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using a hadronic framework based on the proton triangle diagram, the authors derive that a weak background magnetic field suppresses the neutral pion decay rate into two photons, with the correction scaling as \(|eB|^2/m_P^4\); however, the effect remains modest even for fields as strong as \(|eB|\simeq m_\pi^2\), producing only about a 0.02% suppression, and the quantitative results differ slightly from those obtained using quark-level or chiral perturbation theory approaches.

## Method

The calculation uses an effective Yukawa \(\pi^0\)-proton coupling with coupling constant \(\lambda = m_P/f_\pi\) and expands the proton propagator and the product of Schwinger phases in powers of the magnetic field strength \(eB\) up to second order. The decay amplitude is derived from the triangle Feynman diagrams, and the decay rate is computed in the center-of-mass frame with the small-pion-mass approximation (CS) and in the general frame with the small-pion-mass approximation (SP), yielding analytical expressions for the field-induced corrections.

## Key numbers

* For magnetic fields of order \(|eB| \simeq m_\pi^2 \approx 0.018\ \text{GeV}^2\), the suppression of the decay rate is about \(0.02\%\).
* For substantially stronger fields \(|eB| \simeq 10 m_\pi^2 \approx 0.2\ \text{GeV}^2\), the suppression reaches approximately \(2\%\).
* The zero-field decay rate is \(\Gamma(0) = \alpha_e^2 m_\pi^3 / (64 \pi^3 f_\pi^2)\), where \(f_\pi \approx 92\ \text{MeV}\) and \(m_P\) is the proton mass.
* In the CS approximation, the decay rate is \(\Gamma^{\text{CS}}(B) \approx \Gamma(0) \left(1 - 2|eB|^2/(5m_P^4) - 2|eB|^2 m_\pi^2/(189 m_P^6)\right)\).
* The field-induced correction scales as \(|eB|^2/m_P^4\), where \(m_P\) is the proton mass.

## Context

This work extends studies of magnetic field effects on neutral pion decay, which have been previously addressed using the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation (Shushpanov 1997, Agasian 2001, Simonov 2015, Coppola 2025) and chiral perturbation theory (Andersen 2012, Brauner 2017). The hadronic framework employed here offers an alternative to quark-level descriptions and is advantageous when the magnetic length greatly exceeds the proton size. The results enable future exploration of the strong-field regime beyond perturbative expansion, incorporation of medium effects, and generalization to other anomalous meson decay channels, with potential observable signatures in heavy-ion collisions and magnetized astrophysical environments.
