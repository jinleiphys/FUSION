# Backward timelike Compton scattering to decipher the photon content of the nucleon

> The exclusive photoproduction off nucleon of a large invariant mass lepton pair in the backward region specified by the small Mandelstam variable $-u$ is discussed in the framework of collinear QCD factorization. The amplitude is factorized in terms of photon-to-nucleon Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) which encode the photon content of the nucleon. Model estimates of these new non-perturbative objects are described and the corresponding cross sections calculated. The background due to the electromagnetic Bethe-Heitler process is shown to be negligible in the kinematical regime of interest.

## Key claim

The exclusive photoproduction of a large-invariant-mass lepton pair off a nucleon in the backward region (small Mandelstam variable \(-u\)) can be described within collinear QCD factorization using photon-to-nucleon Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs), and the Bethe-Heitler background is negligible in this regime, but the cross-section estimates rely on an oversimplified vector-meson-dominance (VMD) model that should be taken only as a strawman to motivate future experiments.

## Method

The authors derive the collinear factorization formalism for backward timelike Compton scattering (bTCS), \(\gamma N \to \gamma^* N' \to \ell^+ \ell^- N'\), in the regime where \(Q'^2\) and \(W^2\) are large, \(\tau = Q'^2/(2 p_N \cdot q)\) is fixed, and \(|u| \ll W^2, Q'^2\). The scattering amplitude is expressed in terms of photon-to-nucleon TDAs (parametrized at leading twist-3 by 16 independent functions) and nucleon distribution amplitudes, with the hard coefficient functions computed from 21 Feynman diagrams at leading order in \(\alpha_s\). A VMD-based model for the \(\gamma N\) TDAs is constructed by relating them to nucleon-to-vector-meson TDAs (\(\rho^0, \omega, \phi\)) via vector-meson-to-photon couplings extracted from \(V \to e^+ e^-\) decay widths, and the Bethe-Heitler contribution is evaluated using the dipole representation for nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon light-cone wave function normalization constant used is \(f_N = 5.0 \times 10^{-3} \; \text{GeV}^2\) (Chernyak, 1987).
- The electromagnetic coupling is taken as \(\alpha_{\mathrm{em}} \simeq 1/137\) and the strong coupling as \(\alpha_s \simeq 0.3\).
- The Bethe-Heitler differential cross section is evaluated at \(Q'^2 = 3 \; \text{GeV}^2\) and \(s = 23 \; \text{GeV}^2\) for various lepton angles, showing peaks in the near-backward region but otherwise tiny magnitudes.
- The vector-meson-to-photon couplings \(f_{\rho,\omega,\phi}\) are estimated from \(\Gamma_{V \to e^+ e^-}\) decay widths using the relation \(\Gamma_{V \to e^+ e^-} = \frac{1}{3} \alpha_{\rm em}^2 m_V \frac{4 \pi}{f_V}\) (Dumbrajs, 1983).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of nucleon-to-photon TDAs previously developed for backward deeply virtual Compton scattering (Pire, 2004; Lansberg, 2006) to the timelike Compton scattering case, which is argued to be experimentally cleaner due to reduced \(\pi^0 N\) contamination. The crossing relation between photon-to-nucleon and nucleon-to-photon TDAs is analogous to that established for pion-to-nucleon TDAs in the context of near-backward \(J/\psi\) production (Pire, 2016). The forward TCS case, factorized in terms of GPDs, has been studied by Berger (2001) and recently measured by the CLAS collaboration (2021). The VMD-based model for TDAs follows the approach used for nucleon-to-vector-meson TDAs (Pire, 2015). The results enable feasibility studies for measuring bTCS at existing and future electron-beam facilities, with the goal of extracting realistic TDAs from experimental data.
