# Shedding Light on the EOS-Gravity Degeneracy and Constraining the Nuclear Symmetry Energy from the Gravitational Binding Energy of Neutron Stars

> A thorough understanding of properties of neutron stars requires both a reliable knowledge of the equation of state (EOS) of super-dense nuclear matter and the strong-field gravity theories simultaneously. To provide information that may help break this EOS-gravity degeneracy, we investigate effects of nuclear symmetry energy on the gravitational binding energy of neutron stars within GR and the scalar-tensor subset of alternative gravity models. We focus on effects of the slope $L$ of nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density and the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy. We find that the variation of either the density slope $L$ or the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy leads to large changes in the binding energy of neutron stars. The difference in predictions using the GR and the scalar-tensor theory appears only for massive neutron stars, and even then is significantly smaller than the difference resulting from variations in the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The gravitational binding energy of neutron stars is significantly more sensitive to variations in the nuclear symmetry energy (both its slope L at saturation density and its high-density behavior) than to the difference between General Relativity and scalar-tensor theories of gravity, meaning that within this subset of gravity models, neutron star measurements primarily constrain the equation of state rather than the theory of gravity.

## Method

The authors constructed four equations of state for neutron-rich nucleonic matter using the IU-FSU Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) and SkIU-FSU Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) models, with symmetry energy slopes L ranging from 47.2 MeV to 100 MeV. They solved the stellar structure equations for hydrostatic equilibrium in both General Relativity and scalar-tensor gravity with coupling parameters set to the observational upper bounds (alpha_0^2 < 2.0 x 10^-5, beta_0 > -5.0), then computed the gravitational binding energy and mass-radius relations for neutron stars across the mass range.

## Key numbers

- The slope of the symmetry energy at saturation for the base IU-FSU and SkIU-FSU models is L = 47.2 MeV; two additional RMF models were introduced with L = 60 MeV and L = 100 MeV.
- Relative changes in fractional gravitational binding energy between the SkIU-FSU and IU-FSU models (different high-density symmetry energy) are 5.32%, 6.52%, and 9.89% for 1.25 M_sun, 1.4 M_sun, and 1.9 M_sun neutron stars respectively.
- Relative changes in fractional gravitational binding energy between IU-FSU (L=47.2 MeV) and IU-FSU^2 (L=100 MeV) are -10.26%, -8.85%, and -7.44% for 1.25 M_sun, 1.4 M_sun, and 1.9 M_sun neutron stars respectively.
- The gravitational mass of PSR J0737-3039B is M_G = 1.2489 +/- 0.0007 M_sun.
- The baryonic mass of the precollapse O-Ne-Mg core for PSR J0737-3039B is estimated to lie between 1.366 M_sun and 1.375 M_sun (Podsiadlowski 2005) and between 1.358 M_sun and 1.362 M_sun (Kitaura 2005).

## Context

This work extends the longstanding effort to break the degeneracy between the equation of state of super-dense nuclear matter and strong-field gravity theories, following studies by Will (2005), Harada (1998), Sotani (2004), Lasky (2008), Wen (2009), Cooney (2009), Horbatsch (2010), Arapoglu (2010), Deliduman (2011), Pani (2011), Sotani (2012), Lin (2013), Yagi (2013), Sotani (2014), and Eksi (2014). The results enable future observational measurements of neutron star masses and radii to primarily constrain the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, and specifically show that only equations of state with a soft symmetry energy at high density are consistent with the gravitational binding energy constraints from PSR J0737-3039B.
