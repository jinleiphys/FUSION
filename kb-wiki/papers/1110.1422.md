# Lattice study on $πK $ scattering with moving wall source

> The s-wave pion-kaon ($πK$) scattering lengths at zero momentum are calculated in lattice QCD with sufficiently light $u/d$ quarks and strange quark at its physical value by the finite size formula. The light quark masses correspond to $m_π= 0.330 - 0.466$ GeV. In the "Asqtad" improved staggered fermion formulation, we measure the $πK$ four-point correlators for both isospin $I=1/2$ and 3/2 channels, and analyze the lattice simulation data at the next-to-leading order in the continuum three-flavor chiral perturbation theory, which enables us a simultaneous extrapolation of $πK$ scattering lengths at physical point. We adopt a technique with the moving wall sources without gauge fixing to obtain the substantiable accuracy, moreover, for $I = 1/2$ channel, we employ the variational method to isolate the contamination from the excited states. Extrapolating to the physical point yields the scattering lengths as $m_πa_{3/2} = -0.0505(19)$ and $m_πa_{1/2} = 0.1827(37)$ for $I=3/2$ and 1/2 channels, respectively. Our simulation results for $πK$ scattering lengths are in agreement with the experimental reports and theoretical predictions, and can be comparable with other lattice simulations. These simulations are carried out with MILC $N_f = 2+1$ flavor gauge configurations at lattice spacing $a \approx 0.15$ fm.

## Key claim
The paper reports lattice QCD calculations of s-wave πK scattering lengths for both isospin channels, extrapolated to the physical point using next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory, but the results are obtained at a single lattice spacing (a ≈ 0.15 fm) and with pion masses in the range 0.330–0.466 GeV, meaning the extrapolation carries systematic uncertainties from discretization and the chiral fit.

## Method
The authors used MILC N_f = 2+1 flavor Asqtad-improved staggered fermion gauge configurations at a ≈ 0.15 fm. They measured πK four-point correlators for both I=1/2 and I=3/2 channels using moving wall sources without gauge fixing to compute the direct, crossed, and rectangular quark-line diagrams. For the I=1/2 channel, a 2×2 correlation matrix including a κ meson interpolating operator was diagonalized via the variational method to isolate the ground state from excited-state contamination. Energy shifts were extracted from ratios of four-point to two-point correlators, and scattering lengths were obtained via Lüscher's finite-size formula, with a simultaneous chiral extrapolation to the physical point using continuum three-flavor χPT at next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass range: m_π = 0.330 – 0.466 GeV (light u/d quark masses)
- Lattice spacing: a ≈ 0.15 fm, inverse lattice spacing a⁻¹ = 1.358⁺³⁵₋₁₃ GeV
- Physical-point scattering length for I=3/2 channel: m_π a_{3/2} = -0.0505(19)
- Physical-point scattering length for I=1/2 channel: m_π a_{1/2} = 0.1827(37)
- Lattice volume: 16³ × 48, physical volume ≈ 2.5 fm, 450 gauge configurations

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of πK scattering, notably the fully-dynamical calculation by Beane et al. (2006) which obtained m_π a_{3/2} = -0.0574 and indirectly evaluated the I=1/2 channel, and the quenched calculation by Nagata et al. (2008) that first directly computed the I=1/2 channel. It also improves upon the technique of Sasaki et al. (2010) by using moving wall sources without gauge fixing, eliminating the need for an exponential factor. The results enable more precise comparisons with chiral perturbation theory predictions and experimental constraints from the DIRAC collaboration, and provide a lattice-QCD benchmark for three-flavor hadronic interactions.
