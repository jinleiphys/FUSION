# Graphene flakes with defective edge terminations: Universal and topological aspects, and one-dimensional quantum behavior

> Systematic tight-binding investigations of the electronic spectra (as a function of the magnetic field) are presented for trigonal graphene nanoflakes with reconstructed zigzag edges, where a succession of pentagons and heptagons, that is 5-7 defects, replaces the hexagons at the zigzag edge. For nanoflakes with such reczag defective edges, emphasis is placed on topological aspects and connections underlying the patterns dominating these spectra. The electronic spectra of trigonal graphene nanoflakes with reczag edge terminations exhibit certain unique features, in addition to those that are well known to appear for graphene dots with zigzag edge termination. These unique features include breaking of the particle-hole symmetry, and they are associated with nonlinear dispersion of the energy as a function of momentum, which may be interpreted as nonrelativistic behavior. The general topological features shared with the zigzag flakes include the appearance of energy gaps at zero and low magnetic fields due to finite size, the formation of relativistic Landau levels at high magnetic fields, and the presence between the Landau levels of edge states (the socalled Halperin states) associated with the integer quantum Hall effect. Topological regimes, unique to the reczag nanoflakes, appear within a stripe of negative energies E_b < E < 0, and along a separate feature forming a constant-energy line outside this stripe. The lower bound (E_b) specifying the energy stripe is independent of size. A main finding concerns the limited applicability of the continuous Dirac-Weyl equation, since the latter does not reproduce the special reczag features. (See also the extended abstract in the paper.)

## Key claim

The paper claims that trigonal graphene nanoflakes with reconstructed zigzag (reczag) edges exhibit unique electronic features beyond those shared with zigzag-terminated flakes, including broken particle-hole symmetry and nonlinear energy-momentum dispersion; however, these unique features are not reproduced by the continuous Dirac-Weyl equation, which fails to capture essential nonlinear physics from the multiple topological defect.

## Method

The authors performed systematic tight-binding (TB) calculations of the single-particle electronic spectra as a function of magnetic field for equilateral trigonal graphene nanoflakes with reczag edge terminations (pentagon-heptagon 5-7 defects replacing hexagons at the zigzag edge). The TB Hamiltonian used nearest-neighbor hopping with four distinct hopping matrix elements for the defective edge, extracted from density functional theory (DFT) by Koskinen et al. (2008). For comparison, they also solved the continuous Dirac-Weyl equation for a circular reczag graphene quantum dot and contrasted the results with the TB spectra.

## Key numbers

* The lower bound of the unique negative-energy stripe is \(\varepsilon_b = -0.205 t\) (with \(t = 2.7\) eV the hopping parameter), independent of flake size and shape.
* A constant-energy line appears at \(\varepsilon_c \approx -0.297 t\) for type-I corners; for type-II corners this line shifts to \(\varepsilon_c \approx 0.120 t\).
* The hopping matrix elements for the reczag edge (relative to \(t\)) are: \(t_1/t = 0.91\), \(t_2/t = 0.99\), \(t_3/t = 0.97\), and \(t_4/t = 1.5\).
* The trigonal flake with reczag edges (type-I corner) has 60 hexagons in the outer unreconstructed row along each side and a total of 4731 carbon atoms; the comparable zigzag flake has 61 hexagons per side and 3966 carbon atoms.
* The magnetic flux is given in units of \(\Phi_0 = hc/e\); the graphene lattice constant is \(a = 0.246\) nm.

## Context

This work extends prior tight-binding studies of graphene quantum dots with zigzag edges (e.g., Pala et al. 2007, Potasz et al. 2010, Ezawa 2010) and graphene nanorings (e.g., Bahamon et al. 2009, Romanovsky et al. 2012) to the case of reconstructed reczag edges, which were theoretically predicted by Koskinen et al. (2008) and observed experimentally. The paper demonstrates that reczag nanoflakes exhibit coexisting singly-connected quantum-dot behavior and doubly-connected nanoring-like behavior (Aharonov-Bohm oscillations), a combination with no analog in semiconductor quantum dots. The finding that the Dirac-Weyl continuum equation fails to capture the unique reczag features challenges the universal applicability of the relativistic description for graphene nanostructures with topological edge defects, and it motivates future work using tight-binding or other lattice-level approaches for such systems.
