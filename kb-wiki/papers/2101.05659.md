# Transfer reactions between odd-odd and even-even nuclei by using IBFFM

> Spectroscopic Amplitudes (SA) in the Interacting Boson Fermion Fermion Model (IBFFM) are necessary for the computation of $0νββ$ decays but also for cross sections of heavy-ion reactions, in particular, Double Charge Exchange reactions for the NUMEN collaboration, if one does not want to use the closure limit. We present for the first time: i) the formalism and operators to compute in a general case the spectroscopic amplitudes in the scheme IBFFM from an even-even to odd-odd nuclei, in a way suited to be used in reaction code, i.e., extracting the contribution of each orbital; 2) the odd-odd nuclei as described by the old IBFFM are obtained for the first time with the new implementation of Machine Learning (ML) techniques for fitting the parameters, getting a more realistic description. The one body transition densities for $^{116}$Cd $\rightarrow$ $^{116}$In and $^{116}$In $\rightarrow$ $^{116}$Sn are part of the experimental program of the NUMEN experiment, which aims to find constraints on Neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements.

# Transfer reactions between odd-odd and even-even nuclei by using IBFFM

## Key claim

The authors present for the first time the explicit formalism and operators for computing spectroscopic amplitudes (SA) in the Interacting Boson Fermion Fermion Model (IBFFM) for transitions between even-even and odd-odd nuclei, in a form suitable for use in reaction codes without requiring the closure approximation. The odd-odd nuclei are described using a new implementation of Machine Learning techniques for fitting parameters, but the results are limited to the specific case of $^{116}$Cd, $^{116}$Sn, and $^{116}$In, and the spectroscopic amplitudes are found to converge to zero for intermediate state energies above 6 MeV.

## Method

The authors derive the one-body transition density operator in the IBFFM framework by mapping fermionic creation and annihilation operators into the boson-fermion-fermion space, considering both the case where the number of bosons is conserved and where it changes by one unit. The odd-odd nucleus $^{116}$In is described by coupling a proton and a neutron to the even-even core $^{116}$Cd, with the Hamiltonian including IBM-2 boson terms, single-particle energies from a Woods-Saxon potential, boson-fermion coupling, and a residual neutron-proton interaction. The parameters for the residual interaction (Surface Delta Interaction and tensor terms) are fitted using a Machine Learning approach with the Nelder-Mead simplex algorithm to minimize the Mean Square Error between calculated and experimental low-lying energy levels.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle energies for neutrons in the 50-82 shell range from -0.2890 MeV (2d3/2) to 2.7090 MeV (2d5/2), with quasi-particle energies from 1.2286 MeV (3s1/2) to 2.1439 MeV (2d5/2)
- The single-particle energies for protons in the 28-50 shell range from 0.0000 MeV (1g9/2) to 2.3530 MeV (1f5/2), with quasi-particle energies from 1.9485 MeV (1f5/2) to 4.1075 MeV (1g9/2)
- The best-fit parameters for the residual interaction in $^{116}$In are $V_\delta = -0.1500$ MeV and $V_T = 0.200$ MeV, with a minimum Mean Square Error of $6.53 \times 10^{-5}$
- The calculated excitation energies for $^{116}$In include: $1^+$ at 0.0 keV, $5^+$ at 120.4 keV, $4^+$ at 219.8 keV, $2^+$ at 183.4 keV, compared to experimental values of 0.0, 127.267, 223.330, and 272.966 keV respectively
- The spectroscopic amplitudes for $^{116}$Sn($0^+_1$) $\rightarrow$ $^{116}$In($5^+_1$) via the 3s1/2 neutron and 1g9/2 proton orbitals is 0.21360, with OBTD of 0.0644
- The spectroscopic amplitudes for $^{116}$Cd($0^+_1$) $\rightarrow$ $^{116}$In($5^+_1$) via the 3s1/2 neutron and 1g9/2 proton orbitals is -0.25093, with OBTD of -0.0757
- The squared spectroscopic amplitudes for both transitions tend to zero within higher energies above 6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the IBFFM formalism developed by Lopac and Bianco (1986), Blasi (1987), and Brant and Paar (1988) to compute spectroscopic amplitudes for transfer reactions between even-even and odd-odd nuclei, going beyond the earlier supersymmetry approach of Isacker (1985), Warner (1986), and Balantekin (1986) which was restricted to a few nuclei. The formalism is specifically designed for the NUMEN collaboration's experimental program studying Double Charge Exchange reactions on $^{116}$Cd and $^{116}$Sn, and enables computation of $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay matrix elements without the closure approximation. The results provide the necessary input for reaction codes to calculate cross sections for heavy-ion charge exchange reactions, with the finding that intermediate states above 6 MeV contribute negligibly to the spectroscopic amplitudes.
