# Short-Range Correlations and Meson-Exchage Currents in Electron and Neutrino Scattering

> We investigate meson-exchange currents (MEC) in the one-particle emission transverse response of nuclear matter, incorporating short-range correlations via the Bethe-Goldstone equation with a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The interference between one-body and two-body currents, strengthened by the high-momentum components of correlated pairs, produces a marked enhancement of the transverse response. We also indicate how the formalism extends to neutrino scattering, where similar effects are expected to impact oscillation experiments.

# Short-Range Correlations and Meson-Exchange Currents in Electron and Neutrino Scattering

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) combined with meson-exchange currents (MEC) produce an enhancement of the transverse response in the one-particle–one-hole (1p1h) channel of nuclear matter, compensating the reduction predicted by the Fermi gas model alone; this enhancement arises directly from the realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction without additional fitting, and the formalism extends to neutrino scattering where similar effects are expected.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation for correlated nucleon pairs in nuclear matter using the Granada 2013 realistic nucleon-nucleon potential, expressed as a delta-shell potential with five shells. They compute the transverse response function in the 1p1h channel including one-body and two-body meson-exchange currents (seagull, pion-in-flight, and Delta diagrams), with the two-body matrix elements evaluated using correlated two-particle states that contain high-momentum components from short-range correlations. The calculation adopts a superscaling approach (SuSA) where the longitudinal scaling function is extracted from inclusive electron scattering data on carbon-12.

## Key numbers

- The Granada 2013 potential uses N=5 delta shells at positions r_i with a coarse-grained resolution of Δr ~ 0.6 fm.
- The relative momentum of the correlated pair studied is p = 225 MeV/c.
- The longitudinal scaling function is extracted from data on 12C at momentum transfers up to q ≈ 570 MeV/c.
- The transverse response enhancement is mainly produced by the interference of the coupled 3S1-3D1 partial waves, driven by the tensor component of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Context

This work extends the correlated basis function (CBF) calculations of Fabrocini (1997), who found an enhancement in nuclear matter from the combined effect of SRC and MEC, in contrast to single-particle models that consistently predict a reduction of the 1p1h transverse response due to MEC (Kohno 1981, Alberico 1990, Amaro 1994, Amaro 2003, Casale 2023). The results provide an independent validation of the role of correlations and MEC in enhancing the 1p1h transverse response in electron scattering, and the formalism enables future calculations for neutrino-nucleus scattering where similar effects may impact oscillation experiments.
