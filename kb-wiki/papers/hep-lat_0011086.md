# An improved random matrix model for the chiral phase transition in QCD at finite chemical potential

> We consider a lattice-inspired random matrix model for the QCD chiral phase transition at finite chemical potential. Useful features of the usual RMM for QCD at finite chemical potential are reobtained, some being brought closer to their lattice equivalent. The simple physical requirement of a vanishing quark number density in the broken phase is fulfilled in the limit of a large number of timeslices. It is argued that the suppression of the partition function at nonzero chemical potential in the broken phase, seen in the usual RMM, is possibly present in lattice simulations and is simply a result of the discretization in time.

# An improved random matrix model for the chiral phase transition in QCD at finite chemical potential

## Key claim

The paper claims that the unphysical suppression of the partition function at nonzero chemical potential in the broken phase, seen in the usual random matrix model (RMM), is possibly present in lattice simulations and is simply a result of discretization in time; the physical requirement of a vanishing quark number density in the broken phase is fulfilled only in the limit of a large number of timeslices.

## Method

The author constructs a lattice-inspired random matrix model where the chemical potential enters through factors of e^μ and e^{-μ} multiplying forward and backward step matrices, mimicking the time-structure of the lattice Dirac operator. Using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and algebraic reduction, the partition function is reduced from an integral over an N × N complex matrix to one over an N_f × N_f matrix. The model is analyzed in the saddle point approximation with respect to the number of spatial points N_s, and the phase structure is determined by comparing the value of the saddle point function at u=0 with its maximum.

## Key numbers

- For N_t' = 2 (N_t = 4) and g = 1, the critical chemical potential is μ_c = 0.1966, where Φ(0, g=1, N_t'=2, μ_c) = Φ(ū_1, g=1, N_t'=2, μ_c).
- In the large N_t limit at g = 1, the maximum of the saddle point function occurs at ū = 0.236... with corresponding λ̄ = 1.618..., and the approximate condition for equality of Φ values gives μ_c = 0.1226...
- For N_t = 8 (N_t' = 4) and N_s = 10, the eigenvalue distributions show qualitative similarity to lattice results, with eigenvalues originally on the imaginary axis at μ = 0 spreading out in the complex plane as μ increases.
- For N_t = 8 and N_s = 10 at g = 0.3, the fugacity eigenvalues for small g are concentrated around the N_t order roots of unity and merge into a circular band as g increases.

## Context

This work extends the random matrix model for chiral symmetry breaking in QCD at finite chemical potential introduced by Stephanov (1996), which explained the failure of the quenched approximation in lattice QCD at finite μ. The model builds on the earlier RMM that included both temperature and chemical potential to describe the tricritical point in QCD. The paper directly addresses the suppression of the partition function at nonzero μ seen in the usual RMM, which was previously studied in the context of the Glasgow method applied to the same model. The results suggest that lattice Glasgow simulations using small numbers of timeslices (N_t = 8, as in the Glasgow group's work) may suffer from the same spurious suppression of the partition function, which is an artifact of time discretization that could be cured by using a large number of timeslices and a small spatial lattice.
