# The quark condensate in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

> We compute the modification of the quark condensate $<\bar{q} q>$ in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions and estimate the 4-volume, where the quark condensate is small ($<\bar{q}q>/<\bar{q} q>_0\leq$ 0.1--0.3) using hadron phase-space distributions obtained with the quark-gluon string model. As a function of the beam energy the 4-volume rises sharply at a beam energy $E_{lab}/A \simeq$ (2--5) GeV, remains roughly constant up to beam energies $\simeq 20$ GeV and rises at higher energies. At low energies the reduction of the condensate is mainly due to baryons, while at higher energies the rise of the 4-volume is due to the abundant mesons produced. Based on our results we expect that moderate beam energies on the order of 10 GeV per nucleon are favourable for studying the restoration of chiral symmetry in a baryon-rich environment in nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The quark condensate is a better measure for chiral restoration in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions than baryon density because it is a Lorentz scalar and accounts for both baryons and mesons; the authors estimate that moderate beam energies around 10 GeV per nucleon are favourable for studying chiral symmetry restoration in a baryon-rich environment, but note that the optimal energy for a given probe may vary due to unknown relative importance of time and volume.

## Method

The authors computed the quark condensate modification in Au+Au collisions using hadron phase-space distributions from the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM), which includes baryons and mesons from the two lowest SU(3) multiplets with elastic collisions, hadron production, and decay processes. They implemented the low-density relation for the condensate with a cutoff limiting the baryon contribution to a maximum reduction of 60%, and evaluated the 4-volume where the condensate ratio falls below thresholds of 0.1-0.3 by summing over cylindrical cells of radial extension 1 fm and longitudinal extension 2 fm.

## Key numbers

- The quark condensate in nuclear matter at normal density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is reduced by about one third from its vacuum value, using σ_N = 45 MeV.
- The 4-volume where the condensate is small rises sharply at a threshold beam energy E_lab/A ≃ (2-5) GeV, remains roughly constant up to ~20 GeV, and rises at higher energies.
- The maximum baryon density reached at E_lab/A = 10 GeV occurs at t ≈ 4 fm/c, with a value in good agreement with prior work.
- The time period Δt_qc where ⟨q̄q⟩/⟨q̄q⟩₀ < 0.1-0.3 in the cylindrical test volume is between 5 and 7 fm/c for all bombarding energies beyond 5 GeV.
- The formation time for produced hadrons is taken as τ_f = 1 fm/c.
- The pion decay constant used is f_π = 94 MeV.

## Context

This work extends static equilibrium studies of the quark condensate by Gasser and Leutwyler (1987) and Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1992) to the dynamic environment of nucleus-nucleus collisions, using the Quark-Gluon String Model previously applied to SIS, AGS, and SPS energies. The results indicate that experimental signatures of chiral symmetry restoration should exhibit a characteristic threshold behavior at moderate beam energies (2-10 GeV per nucleon), and that for probes relying on high baryon densities the optimal conditions are probably reached around 10 GeV per nucleon, which could inform future experiments at facilities like the future FAIR facility.
