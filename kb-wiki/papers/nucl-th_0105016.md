# An Analysis of the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C Reaction Using a New Type of Coupling Potential

> A new approach has been used to explain the experimental data for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system over a wide energy range in the laboratory system from 32.0 MeV to 126.7 MeV. This new coupled-channels based approach involves replacing the usual first derivative coupling potential by a new, second-derivative coupling potential. This paper first shows and discusses the limitation of the standard coupled-channels theory in the case where one of the nuclei in the reaction is strongly deformed. Then, this new approach is shown to improve consistently the agreement with the experimental data: the elastic scattering, single-2$^{+}$ and mutual-2$^{+}$ excitation inelastic scattering data as well as their 90$^{\circ}$ elastic and inelastic excitation functions with little energy-dependent potentials. This new approach makes major improvement on all the previous coupled-channels calculations for this system.

## Key claim
The paper claims that replacing the standard first-derivative coupling potential with a new second-derivative coupling potential within the coupled-channels formalism provides a consistent global description of elastic and inelastic scattering data for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system over a wide energy range (32.0 MeV to 126.7 MeV), which previous standard coupled-channels calculations had failed to achieve simultaneously.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-channels calculations for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system using a deformed optical potential with a real potential of Woods-Saxon squared shape and an imaginary potential of standard Woods-Saxon volume shape whose depth increases quadratically with energy. Instead of the usual first-derivative coupling potential derived from a Taylor expansion of the real potential, they introduced a new second-derivative coupling potential designed to be oblate (attractive) at large distances and prolate (repulsive) at short distances, reflecting the deformed structures of $^{12}$C and the compound nucleus $^{24}$Mg. They modified the code CHUCK to include simultaneous mutual excitation of both nuclei and compared their results to experimental elastic scattering, single-2$^{+}$ excitation, and mutual-2$^{+}$ excitation inelastic scattering angular distributions and 90$^{\circ}$ excitation functions from 32.0 MeV to 126.7 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Energy range studied: 32.0 MeV to 126.7 MeV in the laboratory system.
- Deformation parameter $\beta_{2}$ = -0.6 for $^{12}$C, derived from B(E2) = 42 e$^{2}$fm$^{4}$ (with a more recent average of 39$\pm$4 e$^{2}$fm$^{4}$).
- Imaginary potential depth parameterization: $W = -2.69 + 0.145E_{Lab} + 0.00091E_{Lab}^{2}$.
- Standard coupled-channels calculations under-predicted mutual-2$^{+}$ excitation inelastic scattering data by factors of 3 to 10.
- Potential parameters showed only 1 to 3 percent changes away from resonance regions, with fluctuations in the energy range $E_{Lab} \sim$ 90 to 110.0 MeV attributed to possible resonances.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system by Stokstad et al. (1979), Wolf, Satchler and others (1982), Fry et al. (1997), and Sakuragi (1999), which all failed to simultaneously describe elastic and inelastic scattering data over a wide energy range using standard coupled-channels methods. The new approach addresses the long-standing magnitude problem for mutual-2$^{+}$ excitation and the out-of-phase problem for single-2$^{+}$ excitation. It also connects to the double-peaked real potential found by Ordoñez et al. (1986) in their phase-shift analysis, which the authors interpret as arising from coupling effects due to the strongly deformed $^{12}$C nucleus. The method has also been applied successfully to the $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si and $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg systems, and predicts previously unobserved resonances in the 90 to 110.0 MeV energy range that may be associated with single and mutual-4$^{+}$ excited states of $^{12}$C.
