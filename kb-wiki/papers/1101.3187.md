# Kaon and Lambda productions in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> A microscopic approach has been employed to study the kaon and $Λ$ productions in heavy ion collisions. The productions of $K^+$ and $Λ$ have been studied within the framework of Boltzmann transport equation for various beam energies. We find a non-monotonic horn like structure for $K^+/π^+$ and $Λ/π$ when plotted against centre of mass energies ($\sqrt s_{\mathrm NN}$) with the assumption of initial partonic phase for $\sqrt s_{\mathrm NN}$ beyond a certain threshold. However, the ratio $K^+/π^+$ shows a monotonic nature when a hadronic initial state is considered for all $\sqrt s_{\mathrm NN}$. Experimental values of $K^-/π^-$ for different $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}$ are also reproduced within the ambit of the same formalism.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a non-monotonic "horn-like" structure in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) and \(\Lambda/\pi\) ratios as a function of center-of-mass energy can be reproduced only when an initial partonic phase (quark-gluon plasma) is assumed for \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} > 7.6\) GeV; a purely hadronic initial state for all energies yields a monotonic rise, failing to match the experimental data.

## Method

The authors solve the momentum-integrated Boltzmann transport equation for the time evolution of strange quark and kaon densities, assuming non-strange quarks and hadrons are in complete thermal equilibrium. They consider five scenarios: (I) hadronic initial state for all \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}\); (II) partonic initial state for \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} > 7.6\) GeV; (III) strangeness in full thermal equilibrium without secondary production; (IV) full thermal equilibrium with secondary production; and (V) zero initial strangeness. The baryonic chemical potential is taken from a parametrization of freeze-out data, and the temperature and net baryon density evolution are obtained from boost-invariant relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for assuming an initial partonic phase is set at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} > 7.6\) GeV.
- The transition temperature from partonic to hadronic phase is taken as \(T_c = 190\) MeV.
- The center-of-mass energies studied range from \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 3.32\) to 200 GeV.
- The ratio \(K^+/\pi^+\) shows a monotonic rise for all \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}\) in scenario I (hadronic initial state), but a non-monotonic horn-like structure in scenario II (partonic initial state above 7.6 GeV).
- The ratio \(\Lambda/\pi\) also exhibits a horn-like structure in scenario II, resembling experimental data.
- The ratio \(K^-/\pi^-\) is reproduced within the same formalism and increases monotonically with \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}\), saturating at higher energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strangeness production as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, particularly addressing the "horn" structure observed by the NA49 collaboration. The authors compare their results to experimental data from the NA49 collaboration and to prior theoretical models (e.g., Randrup, Brown, Kapusta). The analysis enables discrimination between purely hadronic and partonic initial-state scenarios, supporting the interpretation that a partonic phase is formed above 7.6 GeV. The paper also examines the role of secondary production and thermal equilibrium assumptions, showing that full equilibrium with secondary production overestimates the data at high energies.
