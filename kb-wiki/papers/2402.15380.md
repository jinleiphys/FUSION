# Extended Fayans energy density functional: optimization and analysis

> The Fayans energy density functional (EDF) has been very successful in describing global nuclear properties (binding energies, charge radii, and especially differences of radii) within nuclear density functional theory. In a recent study, supervised machine learning methods were used to calibrate the Fayans EDF. Building on this experience, in this work we explore the effect of adding isovector pairing terms, which are responsible for different proton and neutron pairing fields, by comparing a 13D model without the isovector pairing term against the extended 14D model. At the heart of the calibration is a carefully selected heterogeneous dataset of experimental observables representing ground-state properties of spherical even-even nuclei. To quantify the impact of the calibration dataset on model parameters and the importance of the new terms, we carry out advanced sensitivity and correlation analysis on both models. The extension to 14D improves the overall quality of the model by about 30%. The enhanced degrees of freedom of the 14D model reduce correlations between model parameters and enhance sensitivity.

## Key claim

Adding an isovector pairing term (increasing the model from 13 to 14 free parameters) improves the overall quality of the Fayans energy density functional fit by about 30%, but this improvement is conditional on the specific dataset of 194 observables from spherical even-even nuclei and the adopted error weights inherited from prior calibrations.

## Method

The authors optimized two versions of the Fayans EDF, a 13-parameter baseline without the isovector pairing term \(f_{\mathrm{ex},-}^\xi\) and a 14-parameter extended version including it, against a heterogeneous dataset of 194 experimental observables (binding energies, charge radii, diffraction radii, surface thicknesses, single-particle energies, differential radii, and three-point energy staggering) from 69 spherical even-even nuclei. They used the derivative-free optimization software POUNDERS to find least-squares minima and performed sensitivity and correlation analysis via the Jacobian matrix and approximate covariance matrix at the optimal parameter points.

## Key numbers

* Total objective function (chi-squared) reduced by about 30% when moving from the 13D to the 14D model.
* Dataset comprises 194 observables from 69 spherical even-even nuclei, including 60 binding energies, 54 charge radii, 28 diffraction radii, 26 surface thicknesses, 3 proton single-level energies, 4 neutron single-level energies, 3 differential radii, 5 neutron radius staggerings, and 11 proton radius staggerings.
* For the 14D solution, the nuclear matter properties are: equilibrium density \(\rho_\mathrm{eq} = 0.164331\) fm\(^{-3}\), energy per nucleon \(E_{\rm B}/A = -15.881322\) MeV, incompressibility \(K = 214.169984\) MeV, symmetry energy \(J = 30.248343\) MeV, and slope of symmetry energy \(L = 62.427904\) MeV.
* For the 14D solution, the isovector pairing parameter \(f_{\mathrm{ex},-}^\xi = 2.17\) (scaling length at starting point) with optimal value \(\hat{x}_\alpha = -4.315720\) and standard deviation \(\hat{\sigma}_\alpha = 0.169836\).
* The reduced chi-squared per datum is considerably below one for both models, with \(\hat{\sigma}^2 \approx 1/4\) for 13D and \(\approx 1/5\) for 14D.

## Context

This work extends the Fayans EDF calibration studies of Reinhard (2017) and Bollapragada (2021) by introducing separate proton and neutron pairing strengths, motivated by the observation from Bertsch (2009) that effective pairing requires larger strength in the proton channel. The 14D parameterization Fy(\(\Delta r\), 14D) improves upon the earlier Fy(\(\Delta r\)) and Fy(\(\Delta r\), HFB) sets from Miller (2019), which had difficulty reproducing differential charge radii in heavier nuclei such as Sn and Pb. The results enable more accurate predictions of isotopic radius trends across the nuclear chart and provide a framework for future Bayesian uncertainty quantification of Fayans EDF predictions.
