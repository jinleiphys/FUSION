# Nuclear beta decay with Lorentz violation

> We consider the possibility of Lorentz-invariance violation in weak-decay processes. We present a general approach that entails modifying the W-boson propagator by adding a Lorentz-violating tensor to it. We describe the effects of Lorentz violation on nuclear beta decay in this scenario. In particular we show the expression for a first-forbidden transition with a spin change of two. Using data from an old experiment on the rotational invariance of yttrium-90, we derive several bounds on the Lorentz-violating parameters of the order of 10^(-6)-10^(-8).

# Nuclear beta decay with Lorentz violation

## Key claim
Lorentz-violating effects in nuclear beta decay can be enhanced by a factor of αZ/pR in transitions with a spin change of two or more, allowing existing experimental data on yttrium-90 to set bounds on Lorentz-violating parameters of order 10^(-6) to 10^(-8) at 95% confidence level, but only under the specific assumption that Lorentz violation is introduced via a modified W-boson propagator tensor.

## Method
The authors modify the W-boson propagator by adding a general complex tensor χ^(μν) to the Minkowski metric, parametrizing Lorentz violation. They calculate the beta-decay rate for a first-forbidden transition with spin change ΔI = 2 and parity change π_i π_f = -1, obtaining an expression that depends on the emission direction of the beta particle. They then reanalyze data from a 1976 experiment (Newman and collaborators) that measured rotational asymmetries in the decay of yttrium-90 to zirconium-90 using a rotating turntable at 0.75 Hz over 232 good 2-hour runs, fitting the asymmetries to sidereal-time distributions and extracting bounds on the Lorentz-violating parameters.

## Key numbers
- Bounds on Lorentz-violating parameters at 95% confidence level: |2X_r^(30) - tilde-X_i^3| < 2 × 10^(-8)
- Bound on combination of parameters: |3 X_r^(33) - X_r^(00)| < 3 × 10^(-6)
- Bound on combination of parameters: [(X_r^(12)+X_r^(21))^2 + (X_r^(22)-X_r^(11))^2]^(1/2) < 1 × 10^(-6)
- Bound on combination of parameters: [(X_r^(13)+X_r^(31))^2 + (X_r^(23)+X_r^(32))^2]^(1/2) < 1 × 10^(-6)
- Bound on combination of parameters: [(2X_r^(20)-tilde-X_i^2)^2 + (2X_r^(10)-tilde-X_i^1)^2]^(1/2) < 4 × 10^(-8)
- The experiment used a 10 Ci strontium-90 source and a turntable rotation frequency of 0.75 Hz

## Context
This work extends the Standard-Model Extension (SME) framework for Lorentz violation into the weak-decay sector, which had previously been largely unexplored. Prior dedicated experiments on rotational invariance violation in beta decay were limited to Newman (1976) and Ullman (1978), and the authors note that theoretical calculations of cross sections and decay rates in this context were far from settled. The results enable future experiments at KVI (Groningen) to search for Lorentz violation in beta decay with improved sensitivity, and provide the best direct bounds on the χ^(μν) parameters of which the authors are aware.
