# Subbarrier fusion reactions of an aligned deformed nucleus

> We discuss heavy-ion fusion reactions of a well-deformed odd-mass nucleus at energies around the Coulomb barrier. To this end, we consider the $^{16}$O+$^{165}$Ho reaction and take into account the effect of deformation of $^{165}$Ho using the orientation average formula. We show that fusion cross sections are sensitive to magnetic substates of the target nucleus, and cross sections for the side collision, which are relevant to a synthesis of superheavy elements, may be enhanced by a factor of around two by aligning the deformed target nuclei. We also discuss the effect of alignment on the fusion barrier distribution. We find that, for a particular choice of alignment, the shape of barrier distribution becomes similar to a typical shape of barrier distribution for a deformed nucleus with a negative hexadecapole deformation parameter, $β_4$, even if the intrinsic $β_4$ is positive in the target nucleus.

## Key claim

Fusion cross sections for the side collision, which are relevant to superheavy element synthesis, may be enhanced by a factor of around two by aligning the deformed target nucleus to a specific magnetic substate (M = ±1/2), but this enhancement depends on the quantization axis being parallel to the beam direction and the target being prepared in that particular substate.

## Method

The authors employed the iso-centrifugal approximation and the sudden tunneling approximation to describe fusion of an inert projectile (¹⁶O) with a well-deformed odd-mass target (¹⁶⁵Ho). Fusion cross sections for each magnetic substate M were computed as a weighted average of orientation-dependent cross sections, with weight factors given by the square of the ground state rotational wave function of the target nucleus using Wigner D-functions. The nuclear potential was taken as a Woods-Saxon form and the Coulomb potential was expanded up to second order in β₂ and first order in β₄.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹⁶O+¹⁶⁵Ho system, the deformation parameters used were β₂ = 0.284 and β₄ = 0.020, with a target radius parameter of R_T = 1.2A_T^(1/3) fm.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters were depth V₀ = 104 MeV, radius r₀ = 1.15 fm, and diffuseness a = 0.63 fm.
- Fusion cross sections for side collision (θ ∼ π/2) may be enhanced by a factor of around two compared to the unpolarized case when the target is prepared with M = ±1/2 and the quantization axis is parallel to the beam direction.
- For the case where the quantization axis is perpendicular to the beam direction, the weight factor for M_x = 7/2 at θ = π/2 is larger than the unpolarized case by a factor of about 1.5.
- The barrier distribution for M_x = 1/2 (quantization axis perpendicular to beam) resembles the unpolarized barrier distribution with a negative β₄, shifted in energy by −0.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies from the 1980s and 1990s by Satchler (1981), Jacobs and Smilansky (1985), and Christley (1994) on fusion of aligned/polarized nuclei, which had only discussed cross sections and tensor analyzing powers without examining barrier distributions. The paper also connects to recent experimental work by Tanaka (2018) on barrier distributions for systems relevant to superheavy element synthesis. The results suggest that spin-aligned targets could be used to control the effective hexadecapole deformation in fusion reactions and to enhance evaporation residue cross sections for superheavy element production, though a spin-aligned target applicable to fusion measurements would need to be developed.
