# Charged and superconducting vortices in dense quark matter

> Quark matter at astrophysical densities may contain stable vortices due to the spontaneous breaking of hypercharge symmetry by kaon condensation. We argue that these vortices could be both charged and electrically superconducting. Current carrying loops (vortons) could be long lived and play a role in the magnetic and transport properties of this matter. We provide a scenario for vorton formation in protoneutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that global $U(1)_Y$ vortices arising from kaon condensation in dense quark matter may be electrically charged and superconducting due to a $K^+$ condensate forming in their cores, but this conclusion depends on the core size and the superconducting gap not being too large.

## Method

The authors use chiral perturbation theory to analyze the phase diagram of color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter with a nonzero strange quark mass, deriving the free energy and equations of motion for meson fields. They numerically compute the profile of a $K^0$ vortex and then linearize the equation of motion for $K^+$ excitations about this vortex background to test for instability toward $K^+$ condensation in the core.

## Key numbers

- The core size of $K^0$ vortices is $r_0 \sim c \frac{v}{\mu_{K^0}}$ where $c = O(1)$.
- For $\mu_Q = 0$, the kaon chemical potentials satisfy $\mu_{K^+} \simeq \mu_{K^0} \simeq m_s^2/(2\mu)$.
- The scale for transition from logarithmic to linear behavior in the vortex-antivortex potential is estimated to be $\sim 10^3$ fm (Son 2001).
- The analysis of the unstable $K^+$ mode is performed for the case $\phi_0 = \pi/2$, corresponding to $M_{K^0} \ll \mu_{K^0}$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of vortices in dense quark matter by Son (2001) and Forbes (2001), who first noted the existence of $U(1)_Y$ vortices from kaon condensation, but did not analyze their core properties. The paper builds on the phase diagram for charge-neutral CFL matter derived by Kaplan (2001) and provides the first analysis of vortex core structure, showing that $K^+$ condensation can make these vortices charged and superconducting. This enables future work on vorton formation in protoneutron stars and their potential impact on neutrino opacity and magnetic properties of compact stars.
