# Complex Absorbing Potential Green's Function Methods for Resonances

> The complex absorbing potential (CAP) formalism has been successfully employed in various wavefunction-based methods to study electronic resonance states. In contrast, Green's function-based methods are widely used to compute ionization potentials and electron affinities but have seen limited application to resonances. We integrate the CAP formalism within the $GW$ approximation, enabling the description of electronic resonances in a Green's function framework. This approach entails a fully complex treatment of orbitals and quasiparticle energies in a non-Hermitian setting. We validate our CAP-$GW$ implementation by applying it to the prototypical shape resonances of \ce{N2^-}, \ce{CO^-}, \ce{CO_2^-}, \ce{C2H2-}, \ce{C2H4-}, and \ce{CH2O-}. It offers a fast and practical route to approximate both the lifetimes and positions of resonance states, achieving an accuracy comparable to that of state-of-the-art wavefunction-based methods.

# Complex Absorbing Potential Green's Function Methods for Resonances

## Key claim

The authors integrate the complex absorbing potential (CAP) formalism within the $GW$ approximation to describe electronic resonances, achieving accuracy comparable to state-of-the-art wavefunction-based methods such as CAP-selected configuration interaction (CAP-SCI) and CAP-equation-of-motion electron-attached coupled-cluster with singles and doubles (CAP-EOM-EA-CCSD), but with the caveat that the method is validated only for shape resonances in small molecules and requires careful selection of the optimal CAP strength parameter $\eta_{\text{opt}}$ via minimization of the energy velocity.

## Method

The authors perform complex restricted Hartree-Fock (cRHF) calculations incorporating a quadratic CAP to obtain a mean-field reference for the neutral closed-shell system, then carry out complex-valued $GW$ calculations using the CAP-modified Hamiltonian. Three levels of $GW$ self-consistency are implemented: one-shot $G_0W_0$, eigenvalue self-consistent $GW$ (ev$GW$), and quasiparticle self-consistent $GW$ (qs$GW$), with a similarity renormalization group (SRG) regularization applied to improve convergence in large basis sets. Resonance positions and widths are extracted from the complex quasiparticle energies of the anionic states, identified as the states with the smallest imaginary part among those with real part above the Fermi level.

- CAP strength parameter $\eta$ given in atomic units (\si{\hartree\per\bohr\squared})

## Context

This work extends the CAP formalism, previously combined with wavefunction-based methods such as CAP-EOM-EA-CCSD (Ghosh, 2012; Zuev, 2014) and CAP-selected configuration interaction (Damour, 2024), to the $GW$ approximation for the first time. The only prior application of $GW$ to unbound anions used the stabilization method with analytical continuation (Falcetta, 2014), rather than the non-Hermitian CAP framework. The CAP-$GW$ approach enables treatment of resonances in larger systems than wavefunction methods like EOM-CCSD can handle, thanks to the cubic-scaling implementations of $GW$ (Forster and Visscher, 2021), and opens the door to studying shape resonances in systems where wavefunction-based CAP methods become computationally prohibitive.
