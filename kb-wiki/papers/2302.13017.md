# Radiative decays of the spin-$2$ partner of $X(3872)$

> It has been generally expected that the $X(3872)$ has a spin-2 partner, $X_2$, with quantum numbers $J^{PC}=2^{++}$. In the hadronic molecular model, its mass was predicted to be below the $D^*\bar D^*$ threshold, and the new structure reported in the $γψ(2S)$ invariant mass distribution by the Belle Collaboration with mass $M= (4014.3 \pm 4.0 \pm 1.5)$ MeV and decay width $Γ= (4 \pm 11 \pm 6)$ MeV, with a global significance of 2.8 $σ$, is a nice candidate for it. We consider the radiative decay widths for the $X_{2}\to γψ$ with $ψ=J/ψ, ψ(2S)$ treating the $X_2$ as a $D^*\bar{D}^*$ shallow bound state, and estimate the events of $X_2$ in two-photon collisions that can be collected in the $γJ/ψ\toγ\ell^+\ell^-$ ($\ell=e,μ$) final states at Belle. Based on the upper limit for the ratio of decay widths of $X(3872)\to γψ(2S)$ and $X(3872)\to γJ/ψ$ measured by BESIII, we predict the similar ratio $Γ(X_2\to γψ(2S))/Γ(X_2\to γJ/ψ)$ to be smaller than $1.0$. We suggest searching for the $X_2$ signal in the $γJ/ψ$ invariant mass distribution via two-photon fusions. The results will lead to insights into both the $X(3872)$ and the new structure observed by Belle.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that the ratio of radiative decay widths for the spin-2 partner of X(3872), denoted X₂, into γψ(2S) versus γJ/ψ is smaller than 1.0, based on the upper limit for the analogous ratio for X(3872) measured by BESIII, assuming X₂ is a D*D̅* shallow bound state.

## Method
The authors calculated the radiative decay widths for X₂ → γψ (ψ = J/ψ, ψ(2S)) using a nonrelativistic effective field theory, treating X₂ as a D*D̅* molecular state with couplings derived from heavy-quark spin symmetry. Loop diagrams involving charmed mesons and photon couplings (both electric and magnetic) were evaluated using dimensional regularization with the MS̅ subtraction scheme, and counterterms were set to zero while varying the renormalization scale from 1.5 to 7.0 GeV to estimate uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Mass of the candidate X₂ structure: M = (4014.3 ± 4.0 ± 1.5) MeV, with decay width Γ = (4 ± 11 ± 6) MeV and global significance 2.8σ, reported by Belle in the γψ(2S) invariant mass distribution.
- Predicted ratio R_X₂ ≡ Γ(X₂ → γψ(2S))/Γ(X₂ → γJ/ψ) is smaller than 1.0, constrained by the BESIII upper limit R_X(3872) < 0.59 at 90% confidence level.
- The X₂ coupling to D*D̅* is χ_nr = 1.3^{+1.0}_{-1.3} GeV^{-1/2}, derived from the binding energy relative to the D*⁰D̅*⁰ threshold.

## Context
This work extends the hadronic molecular model predictions for X(3872) and its spin-2 partner X₂, following earlier predictions by Nieves (2012) and Guo (2013) that a D*D̅* bound state with J^PC = 2^{++} should exist near 4012 MeV. The radiative decay ratio R_X₂ is proposed as a discriminant between the molecular interpretation and the conventional charmonium assignment (χ_c2(2P)), where quark model calculations by Barnes (2003, 2005) give R_X₂ ≈ 3.8–3.9, much larger than the molecular prediction. The results enable experimental searches for X₂ in the γJ/ψ invariant mass distribution via two-photon fusion at Belle and Belle II.
