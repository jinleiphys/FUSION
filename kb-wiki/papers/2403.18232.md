# Trineutron resonances in the SS-HORSE-NCSM approach

> The SS-HORSE-NCSM method is generalized to the case of democratic decay into an odd number of fragments. This method is applied to the search for resonances in three-neutron system (trineutron) using ab initio No-Core Shell Model calculations with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. The $3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$ strongly overlapping resonances are predicted when softened $NN$ interactions are used and are preferred over the case where bare $NN$ interactions of the chiral effective field theory are used with no resonance obtained.

# Trineutron resonances in the SS-HORSE-NCSM approach

## Key claim

The paper predicts two strongly overlapping trineutron resonances with spin-parities 3/2^- and 1/2^- when softened nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions are used, but no resonance is obtained when bare chiral effective field theory NN interactions are employed. The authors note that three-nucleon (NNN) forces are not included in their calculations, which have yet to be designed for three-nucleon systems with isospin T=3/2.

## Method

The authors generalize the SS-HORSE-NCSM (Single-State Hyperspherical Orthogonalization of the Reaction Space - No-Core Shell Model) method to treat democratic decay into an odd number of fragments, extending previous work that handled even-number fragment decays. They perform ab initio NCSM calculations using the MFDn code with N_max ranging from 4 to 20 and oscillator parameters hbar-omega spanning 2 to 50 MeV, employing various realistic NN interactions (Daejeon16, JISP16, and SRG-evolved Idaho N^3LO). The 3-to-3 scattering S-matrix is parametrized using an expansion in even powers of momentum, and resonance energies and widths are obtained by numerically locating S-matrix poles in the complex momentum plane.

## Key numbers

- Daejeon16 interaction: 3/2^- resonance energy E_r = 0.48(6) MeV, width Gamma = 0.96(21) MeV; 1/2^- resonance E_r = 0.48(8) MeV, Gamma = 0.96(17) MeV
- JISP16 interaction: 3/2^- resonance E_r = 0.35(8) MeV, Gamma = 0.70(9) MeV; 1/2^- resonance E_r = 0.35(11) MeV, Gamma = 0.67(22) MeV
- SRG-evolved Idaho N^3LO (Lambda=2 fm^-1): 3/2^- resonance E_r = 0.34(8) MeV, Gamma = 0.70(19) MeV; 1/2^- resonance E_r = 0.35(9) MeV, Gamma = 0.68(16) MeV
- Convergence study with Daejeon16 at N_max=20 and W=5: E_r = 0.481 MeV, Gamma = 0.963 MeV for 3/2^- state
- R.m.s. deviation xi for Daejeon16 fits ranges from 1.8 to 3.9 keV depending on N_max and number of parameters

## Context

This work extends the SS-HORSE-NCSM approach previously applied to tetraneutron resonances (Shirokov et al., 2016) and to nuclei such as ^5He, ^5Li, ^7He, and ^9Li, now generalized to odd-number democratic decays. The results are compared with prior trineutron studies: Gandolfi et al. obtained E_r = 1.11(21) MeV by extrapolating trapped three-neutron calculations, and Li et al. (2019) predicted E_r = 1.29 MeV with Gamma = 0.91 MeV using the no-core Gamow shell model. The present work finds lower resonance energies (0.34-0.48 MeV) but widths consistent with Li et al. (2019) for the Daejeon16 interaction. The paper notes that bare chiral NN interactions (Idaho N^3LO and LENPIC N^4LO) do not support trineutron resonances, and that a recent experimental search by Miki et al. (2024) found no trineutron resonance in the ^3H(t,^3He)3n reaction. The results enable future studies of multi-neutron systems and probe details of neutron-neutron interactions not accessible from scattering experiments.
