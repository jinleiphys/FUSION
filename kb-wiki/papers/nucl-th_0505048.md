# Polarized quark distributions in nuclear matter

> We compute the polarized quark distribution function of a bound nucleon. The Chiral Quark-Soliton model provides the quark and antiquark substructure of the nucleon embedded in nuclear matter. Nuclear effects cause significant modifications to the polarized distributions including an enhancement of the axial coupling constant.

# Polarized quark distributions in nuclear matter

## Key claim
Nuclear medium effects cause significant modifications to polarized quark distributions, including a 9.8% enhancement of the axial coupling constant, but the full calculation shows only a moderate depletion in the region 0.3 < x < 0.7 comparable to the unpolarized EMC effect, because a large enhancement in the sea quark contribution mitigates the larger depletion seen in the valence-only calculation.

## Method
The authors compute polarized quark distribution functions for a nucleon embedded in symmetric nuclear matter using the Chiral Quark-Soliton (CQS) model, working to leading order in the number of colors (N_C=3) with two flavors in the chiral limit. Nuclear medium effects are incorporated by adding a Fermi-momentum-dependent scalar interaction term to the soliton field equation, modeling scalar exchange via multiple pion pairs between nucleons, with a phenomenological vector meson exchange providing short-distance repulsion. The quark distributions are calculated using the Kahana-Ripka basis at Fermi momenta k_F=0 and k_F=1.38 fm^{-1}, then evolved from the model scale Q^2 = 0.34 GeV^2 to Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 using one-loop perturbative QCD evolution.

## Key numbers
- The isovector axial coupling g_A^{(3)} is enhanced by 9.8% in nuclear matter at k_F = 1.38 fm^{-1}.
- The valence-only calculation produces a 17.8% depletion in the isovector axial coupling g_A^{(3)}.
- The model scale is Q^2 = M_PV^2 = 0.34 GeV^2, with the Pauli-Villars mass M_PV = 0.58 GeV determined by reproducing f_pi = 0.093 GeV.
- The constituent quark mass is M = 0.42 GeV.
- The vector meson mass is fixed at m_v = 0.77 GeV.
- The nucleon momentum distribution uses Delta_F = k_F / Mbar_N with Mbar_N = M_N(0) - 15.75 MeV.
- Results are evolved to Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 for comparison with experimental data.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculations of unpolarized nuclear quark distributions (Smith and Miller, 2003, 2004) to the polarized case, providing a "polarized EMC effect" analogous to the well-known unpolarized EMC effect discovered by the European Muon Collaboration (Aubert et al., 1983). The calculation differs from the valence-quark-only approach of Cloet, Bentz, and Thomas (2005) by including sea quarks at the model scale, which significantly alters the predicted medium modifications. The results enable predictions for future polarized Drell-Yan experiments and tests of the Bjorken sum rule in nuclear matter, and are consistent with an earlier finding of approximately 25% enhancement of g_A in a different soliton model by Birse (1993).
