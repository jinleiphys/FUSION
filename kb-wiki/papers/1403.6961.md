# Is the first excited state of the $α$-particle a breathing mode?

> The isoscalar monopole excitation of 4He is studied within a few-body ab initio approach. We consider the transition density to the low-lying and narrow 0+ resonance, as well as various sum rules and the strength energy distribution itself at different momentum transfers q. Realistic nuclear forces of chiral and phenomenological nature are employed. Various indications for a collective breathing mode are found: i) the specific shape of the transition density, ii) the high degree of exhaustion of the non-energy-weighted sum rule at low q and iii) the complete dominance of the resonance peak in the excitation spectrum. For the incompressibility K of the alpha-particle values between 20 and 30 MeV are found.

## Key claim

The first excited 0⁺ state of ⁴He exhibits properties consistent with a collective breathing mode, but the conclusion depends on the specific sum rule used: the non-energy-weighted sum rule (r₀) supports collectivity, while the energy-weighted sum rule (r₁) does not, and the authors argue that r₁ is an inappropriate criterion for this system.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio few-body calculations of the isoscalar monopole excitation of ⁴He using the hyperspherical harmonics method with effective interaction. They employed two realistic nuclear force models: the chiral N³LO nucleon-nucleon potential plus N²LO three-nucleon force, and the phenomenological AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus UIX three-nucleon force. The transition density, sum rules (m₀, m₁, m₋₁), and strength distributions at various momentum transfers q were computed using the Lorentz integral transform method to separate resonant from background contributions.

## Key numbers

- For the chiral (phenomenological) potential, the transition strength to the 0⁺_R state is 3.53 (2.25) fm⁴, with m₀ = 6.80 (5.99) fm⁴, yielding r₀^LW = 52% (38%).
- At q = 50 MeV/c, r₀ = 53% for the chiral force and 38% for the phenomenological force; r₁ = 34% and 28%, respectively.
- The incompressibility K₄^I = 36 MeV for both potentials; K₄^II = 23 MeV (chiral) and 22 MeV (phenomenological).
- The root mean square radius is about 1.46 fm for both potential models.
- The resonance energy E_R = 21.25 MeV (chiral) and 21.06 MeV (phenomenological).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on whether the 0⁺ resonance in ⁴He is a collective breathing mode, which began with the electron scattering experiment of Frosch et al. (1965) and the interpretation by Werntz and Uberall (1966). It contradicts the conclusion of Hiyama et al. (2004), who used a sum rule argument based on the energy-weighted sum rule to argue against collectivity, and instead follows the criterion of Rowe (1970) that the non-energy-weighted sum rule is the proper measure. The results enable future experimental determination of the low-q strength distribution to resolve the collectivity question, and provide ab initio predictions for the incompressibility of the alpha particle.
