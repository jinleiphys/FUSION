# Suppression of the multiplicity fluctuations in particle correlations

> Multiplicity fluctuations play a crucial role in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In this work, we explore how the multiplicity fluctuations can be effectively suppressed in the measurement of particle correlations. In particular, through proper normalization, particle correlations can be evaluated in a manner irrelevant to multiplicity. When the multiplicity fluctuations are adequately extracted, Monte Carlo simulations show that the remaining correlations possess distinct features buried in the otherwise overwhelming fluctuations. Moreover, we argue that such a normalization scheme naturally agrees with the multi-particle correlator, which can be evaluated using the Q-vectors. The implications of the present study in the data analysis are also addressed.

## Key claim

Multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be effectively suppressed in particle correlation measurements through a proper normalization scheme, making the remaining correlations sensitive to the initial geometric fluctuations rather than the overall event-by-event multiplicity variations. The authors argue that this normalization naturally agrees with multi-particle correlators evaluated using Q-vectors, though the effectiveness depends on adequately extracting the multiplicity fluctuations.

## Method

The authors performed hydrodynamic simulations using the SPheRIO code for 200 A GeV Au-Au collisions, generating 1000 events with initial conditions consisting of a background energy distribution plus a high-energy peripheral tube. They implemented event-by-event fluctuations by introducing a normal distribution in the background energy density with mean 1.0 and variance 0.01 at initial time tau = 1.0 fm/c. The proposed suppression scheme normalizes the multiplicity of different events to their average value when evaluating particle pairs, randomly discarding or duplicating hadrons to achieve equal pair counts across events.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions parameterized with background energy density epsilon_0 following a normal distribution N(mean = 1.0, variance sigma^2 = 0.01) at tau = 1.0 fm/c, in units of GeV per fm^3
- Peripheral tube placed at r_tube = 4.3 fm from center, with energy density profile epsilon_tube = 12 * exp(-(x - x_tube)^2 - (y - y_tube)^2) in GeV per fm^3
- 1000 events generated for 200 A GeV Au-Au collisions, with Monte-Carlo hadron emission performed 50 times per event
- Background energy distribution parameterized as epsilon_bgd = epsilon_0 * (9.33 + 7r^2 + 2r^4) * exp(-r^1.8) where r = sqrt(0.41x^2 + 0.186y^2)

## Context

This work extends the peripheral tube model developed by the SPheRIO collaboration (Ye et al., 2015-2022) which explains ridge and shoulder structures in two-particle correlations through local flow deflection by high-energy tubes near the system boundary. The proposed normalization scheme directly addresses the issue identified in earlier analytic work that the background multiplicity variance term (proportional to <N_bgd^2> - <N_bgd>^2) dominates the subtracted correlations and obscures the physically interesting contributions from initial geometric fluctuations. This enables cleaner extraction of the correlation features associated with initial-state eccentricities, which can be compared with experimental data from STAR (2005-2015) and PHENIX collaborations.
