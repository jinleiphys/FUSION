# Observation of charge-dependent azimuthal correlations and possible local strong parity violation in heavy ion collisions

> Parity-odd domains, corresponding to non-trivial topological solutions of the QCD vacuum, might be created during relativistic heavy-ion collisions. These domains are predicted to lead to charge separation of quarks along the orbital momentum of the system created in non-central collisions. To study this effect, we investigate a three particle mixed harmonics azimuthal correlator which is a ¶-even observable, but directly sensitive to the charge separation effect. We report measurements of this observable using the STAR detector in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 and 62~GeV. The results are presented as a function of collision centrality, particle separation in rapidity, and particle transverse momentum. A signal consistent with several of the theoretical expectations is detected in all four data sets. We compare our results to the predictions of existing event generators, and discuss in detail possible contributions from other effects that are not related to parity violation.

## Key claim
The STAR Collaboration reports a charge-dependent azimuthal correlator signal in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions that is consistent with several theoretical expectations for local strong parity violation, but they explicitly discuss possible contributions from other effects not related to parity violation, meaning the signal is not a definitive observation of parity violation itself.

## Method
The analysis uses a three-particle mixed-harmonics azimuthal correlator, which is a parity-even observable but directly sensitive to charge separation effects predicted from parity-odd domains. The correlator is measured as a function of collision centrality, particle separation in rapidity, and particle transverse momentum using the STAR detector. Results are compared to predictions from existing event generators to evaluate possible non-parity-violation contributions.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and 62 GeV, and Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and 62 GeV were analyzed across all four data sets.
- The correlator is measured as a function of collision centrality (e.g., 0-5%, 5-10%, etc., up to 60-80% centralities) for both Au+Au and Cu+Cu systems.
- Results are presented for particle transverse momentum ranges and rapidity separations, with the signal observed to be consistent with theoretical expectations across all four data sets.

## Context
This work extends theoretical predictions by Kharzeev, McLerran, and Warringa (2008) regarding local parity violation in QCD through topological solutions (sphalerons and instantons) in heavy-ion collisions. The measurement enables future studies to disentangle the parity-violation signal from background effects such as those from resonance decays and elliptic flow, and provides experimental constraints for event generators like HIJING and AMPT.
