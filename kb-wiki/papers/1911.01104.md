# Influence of surface effects on neutron skin in atomic nuclei

> The influence of the diffuse surface layer of a finite nucleus on the mean square radii and their isotopic shift is investigated. We present the calculations within the Gibbs-Tolman approach using the experimental values of the nucleon separation energies. Results are compared with that obtained by means of direct variational method based on Fermi-like trial functions.

## Key claim

The diffuse surface layer of a nucleus increases the neutron and proton root-mean-square (rms) radii by about 20% compared to a sharp step-like distribution, but this effect largely cancels in the neutron-skin difference Δr_np, so that the simpler step distribution remains adequate for describing the neutron skin.

## Method

The authors compare two models for nucleon spatial distributions in finite nuclei. First, the Gibbs-Tolman (GT) approach treats the nucleus as having a sharp boundary at an equimolar radius, with bulk densities determined by equating the nuclear-matter chemical potentials to experimental single-nucleon separation energies (corrected for Coulomb). Second, a direct variational method uses Fermi-like trial functions (a power of the Fermi function) for the diffuse neutron and proton densities, with the central densities normalized to the GT bulk values and the remaining parameters (diffuseness and exponent) varied to minimize the total energy under the Skyrme SkM* interaction. The rms radii are then computed for both distributions and compared for isotopes of sodium, tin, and lead.

## Key numbers

* For sodium isotopes, the step-like (GT) distribution underestimates the experimental proton rms radii by about 0.5 fm; the diffuse distribution reproduces them almost identically.
* The inclusion of the diffuse surface layer increases the proton rms radii by about 20% compared to the step distribution.
* For sodium isotopes, the step-like distribution underestimates the experimental neutron rms radii by about 0.5 fm.
* For tin and lead isotopes, the difference between the diffuse and step calculations for Δr_np is about 25–30%.
* The experimental linear fit for Δr_np as a function of asymmetry X is Δr_np = (−0.04 ± 0.03) + (1.01 ± 0.15) X (from Trzaska et al. (2001)).

## Context

This work extends the Gibbs-Tolman approach (Gibbs (1928), Tolman (1949), Rowlinson (1982), Kolomietz and Sanzhur (1986, 2012, 2017)) and the direct variational method (Lukyanov and Sanzhur (2016), Kolomietz and Sanzhur (2012, 2017)) to systematically compare the effects of a diffuse versus sharp surface on rms radii and neutron skin. The key finding, that the diffuse layer strongly affects individual radii but largely cancels in the isovector shift, justifies the continued use of simpler step distributions for neutron-skin studies and enables more reliable extraction of bulk nuclear-matter properties from experimental radii. The pairing effect, visible as a sawtooth pattern when using single-nucleon separation energies, is removed by using half the two-nucleon separation energy, as noted in earlier work by Kolomietz and Sanzhur (2017).
