# Neutron-skin thickness of finite nuclei in relativistic mean-field models with chiral limits

> We study several structure properties of finite nuclei using relativistic mean-field Lagrangians constructed according to the Brown-Rho scaling due to the chiral symmetry restoration at high densities. The models are consistent with current experimental constraints for the equations of state of symmetric matter at both normal and supra-normal densities and of asymmetric matter at sub-saturation densities. It is shown that these models can successfully describe the binding energies and charge radii of finite nuclei. Compared to calculations with usual relativistic mean-field models, these models give a reduced thickness of neutron skin in ^{208}Pb between 0.17 fm and 0.21 fm. The reduction of the predicted neutron skin thickness is found to be due to not only the softening of the symmetry energy but also the scaling property of $ρ$ meson required by the partial restoration of chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The authors show that relativistic mean-field models incorporating Brown-Rho scaling (chiral limits) predict a reduced neutron-skin thickness in \({}^{208}\)Pb between 0.17 fm and 0.21 fm, which is smaller than predictions from standard RMF models like NL3 or TM1. This reduction arises not only from a softened symmetry energy but also from the scaling property of the \(\rho\) meson required by partial chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method

The authors constructed relativistic mean-field Lagrangians with density-dependent meson masses and coupling constants following the Brown-Rho scaling, without nonlinear meson self-interactions. They solved the coupled Dirac and meson field equations iteratively for finite nuclei, including a rearrangement term for thermodynamic consistency, and adjusted the scalar meson mass \(m_\sigma\) to improve binding energies and charge radii. Three parameter sets (SL1, SLC, SLCd) were used, with SLCd differing only in the \(\rho\) meson coupling and its density dependence to test the role of the symmetry energy slope.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-skin thickness in \({}^{208}\)Pb: 0.21 fm (SL1 and SLC), 0.17 fm (SLCd); range 0.17–0.21 fm.
- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) for all models.
- Incompressibility \(\kappa = 230\) MeV and symmetry energy 31.6 MeV at saturation density for all models.
- Binding energy per nucleon for \({}^{208}\)Pb: 7.68 MeV (SL1), 7.87 MeV (SLC), 7.79 MeV (SLCd); experimental value 7.87 MeV.
- Charge radius of \({}^{208}\)Pb: 5.46 fm (SL1), 5.47 fm (SLC), 5.48 fm (SLCd); experimental value 5.50 fm.
- Effective nucleon Dirac mass \(M^*/M = 0.679\) (SL1), 0.685 (SLC and SLCd).
- For \({}^{48}\)Ca: neutron-skin thickness 0.20 fm (SL1, SLC), 0.18 fm (SLCd).
- For \({}^{132}\)Sn: neutron-skin thickness 0.27 fm (SL1, SLC), 0.23 fm (SLCd).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous construction of RMF models with chiral limits (Jiang et al., 2007) that were constrained by nuclear matter EOS data, giant monopole resonances (Youngblood et al., 1999), heavy-ion collective flow (Danielewicz et al., 2002), and isospin diffusion data (Tsang et al., 2004; Chen et al., 2005; Li et al., 2005). It contrasts with standard RMF models such as NL3 (Lalazissis et al., 1997) and TM1 (Sugahara and Toki, 1994) that predict thicker neutron skins (0.28 fm and 0.26 fm for \({}^{208}\)Pb, respectively), and with FSUGold (Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz, 2005) which predicts 0.21 fm. The results enable further tests of chiral symmetry restoration effects in finite nuclei and refine the connection between the symmetry energy slope and neutron-skin thickness.
