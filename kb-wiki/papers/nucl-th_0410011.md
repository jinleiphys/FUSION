# Interacting Vector Boson Model Analysis of the ^{160}Dy Excited States Spectrum

> Analysis of the recently obtained experimental data for collective states of ^{160}Dy is presented. The Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) was applied for the classification of low lying states with positive parity 0^{+},2^{+},4^{+},6^{+}and for description of rotational ground, S, &#947; and two octupole bands. The energies of the bands are reproduced with high accuracy using only one set of the model parameters for all bands.

# Interacting Vector Boson Model Analysis of the ^{160}Dy Excited States Spectrum

## Key claim
The Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) can reproduce the energies of five rotational bands (ground, S, gamma, and two octupole bands) in ^{160}Dy with high accuracy using a single set of model parameters, provided that certain high-spin states are reassigned between the ground and S bands relative to the experimental classification of prior work.

## Method
The authors applied the algebraic IVBM based on the sp(12,R) algebra and its reduction through SU(3) to classify collective states in ^{160}Dy. They first classified low-lying 0+ states using a simplified pairing vibrational model with a Holstein-Primakoff transformation to ideal monopole bosons, then extended the parabolic energy distribution to states with nonzero spin. Rotational band energies were calculated using expressions derived from the IVBM Hamiltonian with a single set of five parameters, where the moment of inertia depends on the number of monopole bosons.

## Key numbers
- Average energy deviation for 0+ states: 10.7 keV per point
- Average energy deviations for excited states: 39.4 keV (2+), 21.9 keV (4+), and 50.4 keV (6+)
- Average energy deviation for all five rotational bands combined: less than 9 keV per point
- Model parameters: alpha = 0.00568396, alpha_1 = -0.0274276, alpha_3 = 0.05485516, x = 0.0020997, beta_0 = 0.23
- Ground band states observed up to excitation energy 7231 keV with spin 28+
- S band observed to 4875 keV with spin 20+
- Gamma band observed to 6642 keV with spin 25+
- Octupole band K^pi = 2- observed to 6967 keV with spin 26-
- Octupole band K^pi = 1- observed to 4937 keV with spin 19-
- Proposed reassignment: states at 4.181 MeV (18+) and 4.875 MeV (20+) moved from S band to ground band; states at 3.67 MeV (18+), 4.279 MeV (20+), 4.936 MeV (22+), 5.648 MeV (24+), 6.413 MeV (26+), and 7.231 MeV (28+) moved from ground band to S band

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical analyses of ^{160}Dy that applied the Bohr-Mottelson model, Q-phonon model, variable moment of inertia model with dynamical asymmetry, Bohr-Mottelson model with Coriolis interaction, and IBM-1 (all cited as previous work from 2003). Those approaches gave good descriptions for low spins but showed increasing disagreement with experiment at higher spins. The IVBM analysis enables a unified description of both positive and negative parity bands with a single parameter set, and the authors propose a specific reassignment of high-spin states between the ground and S bands that improves agreement with staggering functions, though they note that detailed B(E2) transition analysis is still needed to confirm this reassignment.
