# Single pion production in neutrino nucleus scattering

> We study one pion production in both charged and neutral current neutrino nucleus scattering for neutrino energies below 2 GeV. We use a theoretical model for one pion production at the nucleon level that we correct for medium effects. The results are incorporated into a cascade program that apart from production also includes the pion final state interaction inside the nucleus. Besides, in some specific channels coherent pion production is also possible and we evaluate its contribution as well. Our results for total and differential cross sections are compared with recent data from the MiniBooNE Collaboration. The model provides an overall acceptable description of data, better for NC than for CC channels, although theory is systematically below data. Differential cross sections, folded with the full neutrino flux, show that most of the missing pions lie on the forward direction and at high energies.

## Key claim

The model provides an overall acceptable description of MiniBooNE data, better for neutral-current (NC) than for charged-current (CC) channels, although theory is systematically below data; most of the missing pions lie in the forward direction and at high energies.

## Method

The authors start from a one-pion production model on nucleons that includes the Œî(1232) resonance and background terms from chiral symmetry, then add a D13(1520) resonance contribution to extend the model to neutrino energies up to 2 GeV. They incorporate nuclear medium corrections (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, modified Œî propagator with in-medium self-energy) and use a cascade simulation code for pion final-state interactions (quasielastic scattering, charge exchange, absorption). Coherent pion production is evaluated using a microscopic model with updated nucleon-to-Œî form factors from a combined fit to ANL and BNL data including deuteron effects.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-level pion production model of Hern√°ndez (2007, 2010) by adding the D13(1520) resonance and comprehensive nuclear medium effects, then compares to MiniBooNE data on mineral oil (CH‚ÇÇ). It contrasts with the GiBUU approach of Lalakulich (2012), which was limited to incoherent CC reactions and did not compare to NC data, and with the NuWro Monte Carlo study of Golan (2012) that used a simpler production model and the Rein-Sehgal coherent model. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions for oscillation experiments, but indicate that additional mechanisms producing forward high-energy pions may be needed to fully describe the data.
