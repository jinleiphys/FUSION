# Treatment of isomers in nucleosynthesis codes

> The decay properties of long-lived excited states (isomers) can have a significant impact on the destruction channels of isotopes under stellar conditions. In sufficiently hot environments, the population of isomers can be altered via thermal excitation or de-excitation. If the corresponding lifetimes are of the same order of magnitude as the typical time scales of the environment, the isomers have to be the treated explicitly. We present a general approach to the treatment of isomers in stellar nucleosynthesis codes and discuss a few illustrative examples. The corresponding code is available online at http://exp-astro.de/isomers/

## Key claim

The paper presents a general algorithm for calculating effective stellar coupling and decay rates between long-lived nuclear states (isomers and ground states) that accounts for thermal excitation and de-excitation via intermediate short-lived states, but the approach relies on the assumption that bound-state beta decays can be ignored for now and that missing transition probabilities can be estimated using the Weisskopf approximation.

## Method

The authors derive effective coupling constants Lambda_i->j(T) and effective beta-decay constants Lambda_beta_i(T) by summing over all possible paths through short-lived intermediate states, using terrestrial nuclear properties (excitation energies, degeneracies, decay constants, branching ratios) as input. Thermal excitation rates are computed from the detailed balance condition using Maxwell-Boltzmann factors. The algorithm is implemented in a publicly available code that accepts input files listing the properties of each nuclear state and a user-defined half-life threshold separating long-lived (explicitly treated) from short-lived (implicitly treated) states.

## Key numbers

- For 34Cl: ground state half-life = 1.5 s; isomer at 146 keV has half-life = 32 min; intermediate 1+ state at 461 keV; experimental upper limit for transition from intermediate state to isomer = 0.5%; Weisskopf estimate for that transition = 0.022%
- For 26Al: ground state half-life ≈ 720,000 years; isomer at 228 keV has half-life = 6.3 s; M5 transition from isomer to ground state (Weisskopf) = 5×10⁻¹³%; M3 transition from 417 keV short-lived isomer to 228 keV isomer (Weisskopf) = 1.4×10⁻⁹%
- For 85Kr: ground state half-life ≈ 10 years; isomer at 305 keV has half-life ≈ 4.5 hours
- For 34Cl, perfect agreement with Coc et al. (2000) is reached when the transition probability from the intermediate state to the isomer is set to 0.015%

## Context

This work extends the treatment of isomers in stellar nucleosynthesis beyond the common simplified approach of using a temperature threshold below which terrestrial conditions apply and above which complete instantaneous coupling is assumed. The authors compare their results for 34Cl and 26Al with the sophisticated estimates of Coc et al. (2000), and for 85Kr with implementations in codes by Takahashi and Yokoi (1987), Jaag (1991), Wiescher et al. (2015), and Kappeler et al. (2016). The code enables systematic inclusion of isomer effects in nucleosynthesis calculations and highlights where improved nuclear data (especially transition probabilities between short-lived states) are urgently needed, as demonstrated for 85Kr where knowledge of higher-lying states is poor.
