# From homogeneous matter to finite nuclei: Role of the effective mass

> Recent astronomical observations, nuclear-reaction experiments, and microscopic calculations have placed new constraints on the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and revealed that most nuclear structure models fail to satisfy those constraints upon extrapolation to infinite matter. A reverse procedure for imposing EoS constraints on nuclear structure has been elusive. Here we present for the first time a method to generate a microscopic energy density functional (EDF) for nuclei from a given immutable EoS. The method takes advantage of a natural Ansatz for homogeneous nuclear matter, the Kohn-Sham framework, and the Skyrme formalism. We apply it to the realistic nuclear EoS of Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall and describe successfully closed-(sub)shell nuclei. In the process, we provide predictions for the neutron skin thickness of nuclei based directly on the given EoS. Crucially, bulk and static nuclear properties are found practically independent of the assumed effective mass value - a unique result in bridging EDF of finite and homogeneous systems in general.

# From homogeneous matter to finite nuclei: Role of the effective mass

## Key claim

The authors present a method to reverse-engineer a microscopic energy density functional (EDF) for finite nuclei directly from a given, immutable nuclear equation of state (EoS) without refitting the EoS parameters, and find that bulk and static nuclear properties are practically independent of the assumed effective mass value, a result that holds provided the momentum-dependent terms are treated separately from the density-dependent terms in the EDF.

## Method

The authors parameterize homogeneous nuclear matter using the KIDS (Korea: IBS-Daegu-SKKU) Ansatz, which expresses the energy per particle as a sum of kinetic energy and up to four density-dependent terms with fractional powers of density. They fit the symmetric nuclear matter parameters to saturation properties (density 0.16 fm⁻³, energy -16 MeV, compression modulus 240 MeV) and the pure neutron matter parameters to the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) EoS over 0.02–0.96 fm⁻³. They then map these EoS parameters onto a generalized Skyrme-type force with multiple density-dependent couplings, where the momentum-dependent terms (which determine the effective mass) are fitted separately to the energy and charge radius of ⁴⁰Ca, and the spin-orbit coupling W₀ is fitted to ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Context

This work extends the KIDS framework previously developed by the same group (Papakonstantinou et al., 2016; Gil et al., 2016, 2017) for homogeneous nuclear matter and neutron stars, now applying it to finite nuclei. It addresses the long-standing problem that most nuclear structure models fail to satisfy EoS constraints from astronomical observations and microscopic calculations (Dutra et al., 2012; Steiner et al., 2012). The method enables direct testing of realistic EoS candidates (such as the APR EoS of Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998) on nuclear properties without refitting, and provides predictions for neutron skin thickness and exotic nuclei that can be tested at rare-isotope facilities.
