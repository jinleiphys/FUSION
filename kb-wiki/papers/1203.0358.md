# Scattering of Two Spinless Particles in 3D Formulation with Coulomb Admixtures

> Scattering of two spinless charge particles for simple forces including coulomb admixtures is calculated without partial wave decomposition. The coulomb interaction being taken is of the type of screened coulomb potential. For the forces range are not infinite, the standard scattering theory is applied. The differential and total cross section is calculated and coulomb effects are shown.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a three-dimensional (3D) approach without partial wave decomposition can be applied to calculate scattering of two spinless charged particles with a screened Coulomb potential, provided the screening range is finite so that standard scattering theory applies. The claim is qualified by the use of a screened rather than pure Coulomb potential, with the screening parameter mu_C = 10 MeV considered sufficient to represent an effective Coulomb range for the system studied.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the T-matrix in a 3D basis of relative momentum states, without expanding into partial waves. They used a Malfliet-Tjon-type short-range potential combined with an exponentially screened Coulomb potential, and performed the angular integration over the azimuthal angle analytically. Differential and total cross sections were computed for two-boson scattering at various laboratory energies and Coulomb screening parameters.

## Key numbers

- Malfliet-Tjon potential parameters: V_R = 7.291, mu_R = 613.69 MeV; V_A = -3.1769, mu_A = 305.86 MeV
- Coulomb strength parameter: V_C = 0.0073
- Differential cross sections computed at E_lab = 500 MeV for Coulomb screening parameters mu_C = 10, 25, 50, and 100 MeV, plus the no-Coulomb case
- Total cross sections computed for energies up to E_lab = 1000 MeV with mu_C = 10 MeV
- The screening parameter mu_C = 10 MeV was found sufficient to represent an effective Coulomb range for the system considered

## Context

This work extends the 3D scattering technique developed by Elster, Thomas, and Gloeckle (1998) for two-boson systems to include Coulomb admixtures, addressing the long-standing challenge of Coulomb scattering in nuclear physics. The approach avoids the partial-wave summation difficulties that arise in standard methods (Taylor, 1974; Semon and Taylor, 1975) when dealing with the large effective range of screened Coulomb interactions. The results enable future application of 3D techniques to more complex systems with Coulomb interactions, such as charged-particle scattering at higher energies.
