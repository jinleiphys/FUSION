# Energy loss in unstable QGP - problem of the upper cut-off

> The energy loss of a highly energetic parton in a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma is studied as an initial value problem. An extremely prolate plasma, where the momentum distribution is infinitely elongated along one direction, is considered. The energy loss is strongly time and direction dependent and its magnitude can much exceed the equilibrium value. It is logarithmically ultraviolet divergent. We argue that a good approximation to the energy loss can be obtained if this divergence is cut off with the parton energy.

## Key claim

The energy loss of a highly energetic parton in an extremely prolate, unstable quark-gluon plasma is logarithmically ultraviolet divergent, and the authors argue that a good approximation to the energy loss can be obtained if this divergence is cut off with the parton energy, analogous to the equilibrium case.

## Method

The authors treat the parton as a classical colored particle and compute its energy loss as an initial value problem using the Wong equations and linearized Yang-Mills equations in the Hard Loop approximation. They consider an extremely prolate plasma with momentum distribution infinitely elongated along one direction (the beam axis), where the spectrum of collective excitations is known analytically. The energy loss is computed by performing the frequency integral analytically over the poles of the inverse gluon propagator, and the remaining momentum integral is evaluated numerically with a finite upper cut-off \(k_{\rm max}\).

## Key numbers

* The equilibrium energy loss value is \(0.18 \, g^2\mu^2\) for \(k_{\rm max} = 5 \mu\).
* The energy loss per unit time is shown as a function of time for three angles \(\Theta = 15^\circ, 45^\circ, 60^\circ\) between the parton velocity and the \(z\) axis, with \(k_{\rm max} = 5 \mu\), \(g=1\), and \(C_R = N_c = 3\).
* The energy loss per unit time as a function of upper cut-off \(k_{\rm max}\) is shown at \(t = 3 \, \mu^{-1}\) for \(\Theta = 15^\circ, 45^\circ, 60^\circ\), and at \(t = 3 \, \mu^{-1}\) and \(t = 7 \, \mu^{-1}\) for \(\Theta = 45^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formalism (Carrington 2011, Carrington 2013) for computing energy loss as an initial value problem in unstable plasmas, which recovers the known equilibrium result (Le Bellac) when applied to an equilibrium system. The paper specifically addresses the ultraviolet divergence problem in the anisotropic case, showing that the logarithmic dependence on the cut-off persists, and argues that the same cut-off procedure used in equilibrium (replacing the cut-off with the parton energy) provides a good approximation. This enables future calculations of parton energy loss in more realistic, time-evolving anisotropic plasmas relevant to heavy-ion collisions.
