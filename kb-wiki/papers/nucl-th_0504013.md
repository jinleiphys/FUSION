# Probing Correlated Ground States with Microscopic Optical Model for Nucleon Scattering off Doubly-Closed-Shell Nuclei

> The RPA long range correlations are known to play a significant role in understanding the depletion of single particle-hole states observed in (e, e') and (e, e'p) measurements. Here the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) theory, implemented using the D1S force is considered for the specific purpose of building correlated ground states and related one-body density matrix elements. These may be implemented and tested in a fully microscopic optical model for NA scattering off doubly-closed-shell nuclei. A method is presented to correct for the correlations overcounting inherent to the RPA formalism. One-body density matrix elements in the uncorrelated (i.e. Hartree-Fock) and correlated (i.e. RPA) ground states are then challenged in proton scattering studies based on the Melbourne microscopic optical model to highlight the role played by the RPA correlations. Effects of such correlations which deplete the nuclear matter at small radial distance (r $<$ 2 fm) and enhance its surface region, are getting more and more sizeable as the incident energy increases. Illustrations are given for proton scattering observables measured up to 201 MeV for the $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb target nuclei. Handling the RPA correlations systematically improves the agreement between scattering predictions and data for energies higher than 150 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incorporating Random Phase Approximation (RPA) long-range correlations into the ground state one-body density matrix systematically improves the agreement between microscopic optical model predictions and proton scattering data for doubly-closed-shell nuclei, but only for incident energies higher than 150 MeV and center-of-mass scattering angles larger than approximately 30 degrees.

## Method

The authors built correlated ground states for 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, and 208Pb using self-consistent RPA theory with the finite-range D1S effective interaction, including a correction for the double-counting inherent to the RPA formalism. They then folded the resulting one-body density matrix elements with the Melbourne g-matrix optical potential (derived from the Bonn-B NN interaction) to calculate elastic scattering observables, which were compared to experimental data for proton scattering in the 40-201 MeV energy range.

## Key numbers

- The 3s1/2 proton orbital in 208Pb was found to be depleted by (18 ± 9)% from joint (e,e') and (e,e'p) experiments, with absolute occupation probability evaluated at 0.76 ± 0.07
- For 208Pb, the HF+RPA calculation gives charge rms radius 5.504 fm, compared to experimental value 5.503(7) fm
- For 208Pb, the HF+RPA calculation gives neutron rms radius 5.592 fm and neutron skin Δrnp = 0.125 fm
- For 40Ca, the HF+RPA calculation gives charge rms radius 3.483 fm, compared to experimental value 3.482(25) fm
- For 48Ca, the HF+RPA calculation gives charge rms radius 3.510 fm, compared to experimental value 3.470(9) fm
- For 16O, the HF+RPA calculation gives charge rms radius 2.728 fm, compared to experimental value 2.730(25) fm
- RPA correlations deplete nuclear matter at radial distances r < 2 fm and enhance the surface region
- Differences between HF- and RPA-based calculations become significant for momentum transfer q larger than 1.7 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends previous studies that used Hartree-Fock predictions based on Skyrme forces for proton and neutron elastic scattering at medium energy, and no-core shell model wave functions for light nuclei. The paper builds on the Melbourne microscopic optical model established by Amos et al. (2000) and the self-consistent RPA theory with the D1S force developed by Berger and Gogny. The results enable future applications to inelastic scattering and provide a framework for probing ground state correlations through nucleon scattering, complementing electron scattering measurements that revealed the quenching of shell model occupation probabilities.
