# A Transport Model for Nuclear Reactions Induced by Radioactive Beams

> Major ingredients of an isospin and momentum dependent transport model for nuclear reactions induced by radioactive beams are outlined. Within the IBUU04 version of this model we study several experimental probes of the equation of state of neutron-rich matter, especially the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy. Comparing with the recent experimental data from NSCL/MSU on isospin diffusion, we found a nuclear symmetry energy of $% E_{sym}(ρ)\approx 31.6(ρ/ρ_{0})^{1.05}$ at subnormal densities. Predictions on several observables sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy at supranormal densities accessible at GSI and the planned Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) are also made.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy at subnormal densities can be extracted by comparing transport model calculations with experimental isospin diffusion data, yielding a specific parameterization of \(E_{sym}(\rho)\). The hedge is that this result is obtained using only free-space nucleon-nucleon cross sections, and the authors note that effects of in-medium cross sections are still under investigation.

## Method

The authors developed the IBUU04 version of an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model based on a single nucleon potential derived from the Hartree-Fock approximation using a modified Gogny effective interaction (MDI). They simulated the reaction \(^{124}\text{Sn} + ^{112}\text{Sn}\) and compared the calculated degree of isospin diffusion, quantified by the ratio \(R_i\), with experimental data from NSCL/MSU. The parameter \(x\) in the MDI interaction was varied to mimic different predictions for the symmetry energy, and the value that best reproduced the data was identified.

## Key numbers

- The extracted symmetry energy at subnormal densities is \(E_{sym}(\rho) \approx 31.6 (\rho / \rho_0)^{1.05}\) MeV, where \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is normal nuclear matter density.
- The symmetry energy at normal density is set to 30 MeV.
- The incompressibility \(K_0\) of symmetric nuclear matter at \(\rho_0\) is 211 MeV.
- The isospin diffusion process occurs from about 30 fm/c to 80 fm/c, corresponding to an average central density from about \(1.2\rho_0\) to \(0.3\rho_0\).
- For the reaction \(^{132}\text{Sn} + ^{124}\text{Sn}\) at 400 MeV/nucleon and an impact parameter of 1 fm, the maximum baryon density is about \(2\rho_0\).
- The \(\pi^{-}\) multiplicity increases by about 20% when the \(x\) parameter is changed from -2 to 1, while the \(\pi^{+}\) multiplicity remains about the same.
- The \(\pi^{-}\) multiplicity is about 2 to 3 times that of \(\pi^{+}\).
- The isospin asymmetry of free nucleons at midrapidity decreases by about a factor of 2 as the parameter \(x\) increases from -2 to 1.

## Context

This work extends prior transport model studies of the nuclear equation of state, particularly the work of Tsang et al. (2004) on isospin diffusion measurements at NSCL/MSU. The IBUU04 model builds on earlier versions of the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) approach and incorporates a momentum-dependent symmetry potential derived from the MDI interaction of Das et al. (2003). The results enable future experiments at GSI and the Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) to probe the symmetry energy at supranormal densities using observables such as the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio, isospin fractionation, and neutron-proton differential flow.
