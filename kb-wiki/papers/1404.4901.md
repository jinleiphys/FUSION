# Global performance of covariant energy density functionals: ground state observables of even-even nuclei and the estimate of theoretical uncertainties

> Covariant density functional theory (CDFT) is a modern theoretical tool for the description of nuclear structure phenomena. The current investigation aims at the global assessment of the accuracy of the description of the ground state properties of even-even nuclei. We also estimate {\it theoretical uncertainties} defined here as the spreads of predictions within four covariant energy density functionals (CEDF) in known regions of the nuclear chart and their propagation towards the neutron drip line. Large-scale axial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations are performed for all $Z\leq 104$ even-even nuclei between the two-proton and two-neutron drip lines with four modern covariant energy density functionals such as NL3*, DD-ME2, DD-ME$δ$ and DD-PC1. The physical observables of interest include the binding energies, two-particle separation energies, charge quadrupole deformations, isovector deformations, charge radii, neutron skin thicknesses and the positions of the two-proton and two-neutron drip lines. The predictions for the two-neutron drip line are also compared in a systematic way with the ones obtained in non-relativistic models. As an example, the data set of the calculated properties of even-even nuclei obtained with DD-PC1 CEDF is provided as Supplemental Material with this article (file ddpc1.pdf).

## Key claim

The paper provides a global assessment of how well four modern covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs) describe ground-state properties of even-even nuclei, defining "theoretical uncertainties" as the spread of predictions among these functionals rather than as statistical errors from parameter variations, which the authors note is only a crude approximation to true systematic theoretical errors.

## Method

Large-scale axial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations were performed for all even-even nuclei with Z ≤ 104 between the two-proton and two-neutron drip lines using four covariant energy density functionals: NL3*, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ, and DD-PC1. The calculations employed an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis and included pairing correlations via the RHB framework with finite-range pairing. The physical observables computed include binding energies, two-particle separation energies, charge quadrupole deformations, isovector deformations, charge radii, neutron skin thicknesses, and the positions of the two-proton and two-neutron drip lines.

## Key numbers

- The four CEDFs used are NL3* (6 independent parameters, fitted to 12 spherical nuclei), DD-ME2 (8 independent parameters, fitted to 12 spherical nuclei), DD-MEδ (14 parameters, of which 4 fitted to 161 spherical nuclei and 10 to ab-initio nuclear matter calculations), and DD-PC1 (10 parameters, fitted to 64 axially deformed nuclei)
- The NL3* functional has meson masses m_σ = 502.5742 MeV, m_ω = 782.600 MeV, and m_ρ = 763.000 MeV
- The DD-ME2 functional has meson masses m_σ = 550.1238 MeV, m_ω = 783.000 MeV, and m_ρ = 763.000 MeV
- The DD-MEδ functional includes the δ-meson with mass m_δ = 983.0 MeV, and has meson masses m_σ = 566.1577 MeV, m_ω = 783.00 MeV, and m_ρ = 763.0 MeV
- The DD-PC1 functional uses coupling constants with parameters including a_S = -10.04616, a_V = 5.91946, and b_TV = 1.83595
- The theoretical systematic uncertainty for a given observable is defined as ΔO(Z,N) = |O_max(Z,N) - O_min(Z,N)|, where O_max and O_min are the largest and smallest values among the four CEDFs for nucleus (Z,N)

## Context

This work extends the global performance assessment of covariant density functionals, which had previously been limited to small sets of nuclei near those used in fitting protocols, to the entire nuclear chart up to Z=104. The authors note that earlier statistical uncertainty analyses (such as those by Reinhard and Nazarewicz (2010), Erler et al. (2012), and Dobaczewski et al. (2014)) focused on spherical nuclei or selected isotopic chains, whereas this study provides systematic uncertainties from functional-to-functional spread across all even-even nuclei. The results enable evaluation of extrapolation reliability toward neutron-rich nuclei and define strategies for future CEDF fits, with the DD-PC1 data set provided as supplemental material.
