# Exploring jet transport coefficients by elastic and radiative scatterings in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma

> We investigate the interaction of leading jet partons within a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) medium, using the effective dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM). The DQPM offers a description of the sQGP's non-perturbative nature at finite temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ through a propagator representation of massive off-shell partons (quarks and gluons). These partons are characterized by spectral functions with $T,μ_B$ dependent masses and widths, adjusted to reproduce the lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (lQCD) equation-of-state (EoS) for the QGP in thermodynamic equilibrium. Our focus lies on examining the jet transport coefficients by elastic scattering in sQGP, specifically the transverse momentum transfer squared per unit length denoted as $\hat{q}$, within the QGP. Furthermore, we investigate the dependence of these coefficients on both the medium temperature $T$ and the jet parton energy. By studying the jet transport coefficients and their relationship to temperature and parton energy, we aim to gain insights into the dynamics of jet propagation in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma medium.

# Exploring jet transport coefficients by elastic and radiative scatterings in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
The jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ (transverse momentum transfer squared per unit length) in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma, computed using the off-shell dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM), shows a strong temperature dependence near the critical temperature $T_C$ due to the rise of the strong coupling $\alpha_S(T,\mu_B)$, but when the coupling at the jet parton vertex is fixed to 0.3, this temperature dependence weakens significantly and the results approach those of perturbative QCD models.

## Method
The authors compute the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ from kinetic theory using the off-shell DQPM, where medium partons (quarks, antiquarks, and gluons) are described by dressed propagators with temperature- and baryon-chemical-potential-dependent masses and widths fitted to lattice QCD thermodynamics. The transport coefficient is evaluated by integrating the squared matrix element for elastic scattering of a leading jet parton with off-shell medium partons, weighted by the transverse momentum transfer squared, over the phase space of initial and final state partons including spectral functions and quantum statistics factors.

## Key numbers
- For a quark jet with momentum 10 GeV/c at $\mu_B=0$, the scaled coefficient $\hat{q}/T^3$ rises substantially as temperature approaches $T_C = 0.158$ GeV, with the DQPM result exceeding perturbative QCD model results (LBT, BAMPS) at low temperatures.
- At high $E/T \gg 1$ with $\alpha_s = 0.3$, $\hat{q}/T^3$ exhibits logarithmic scaling: $\hat{q}/T^3 = \text{const} \cdot \ln(q_{\text{max}}^2 / (4\mu_D^2)) = \text{const} \cdot \ln(E/T)$, where $q_{\text{max}} = 2.6 E_{\text{jet}} T$.
- The DQPM result with $\alpha_{jet}=0.3$ at the jet parton vertices for a quark jet at $T=0.3$ GeV and $T=0.4$ GeV shows lower $\hat{q}/T^3$ values than the full DQPM calculation, and becomes smaller than the BAMPS and LBT model results at low temperatures.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Grishmanovskii 2022) on jet transport coefficients using the DQPM, focusing on elastic scattering contributions to $\hat{q}$. The results are compared with several approaches: the LBT model (He 2015), BAMPS model (Senzel 2020, Uphoff 2014), lattice QCD estimates for pure SU(3) gauge theory and (2+1)-flavor QCD (Kumar 2020), phenomenological extractions by the JET Collaboration (Burke 2013) and JETSCAPE Collaboration (2021), and the color string percolation model (Mishra 2021). The study demonstrates that non-perturbative QGP effects lead to stronger energy loss than massless pQCD partons, but notes that quantitative comparison with experimental jet observables requires including radiative energy loss, which is addressed in a companion study (Grishmanovskii 2023).
