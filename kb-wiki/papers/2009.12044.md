# Emergent Structure in QCD

> The structure of the SU(3) gauge-field vacuum is explored through visualisations of centre vortices and topological charge density. Stereoscopic visualisations highlight interesting features of the vortex vacuum, especially the frequency with which singular points appear and the important connection between branching points and topological charge. This work demonstrates how visualisations of the QCD ground-state fields can reveal new perspectives of centre-vortex structure.

## Key claim

Visualisations of centre vortices and topological charge density in SU(3) lattice gauge theory reveal that branching points (where three vortex lines meet) are surprisingly prevalent and show a significant correlation with topological charge density, suggesting a mechanism for generating instanton-like field configurations that dynamically break chiral symmetry.

## Method

Centre vortices are identified on lattice gauge-field configurations through a gauge-fixing procedure that brings link variables close to centre elements of SU(3), then projecting links to the centre phase. The vortex-only field is examined by computing the centre charge around elementary plaquettes, where a nontrivial charge indicates a vortex piercing that plaquette. Visualisations are presented as stereoscopic images of 3D slices of the 4D space-time lattice at fixed time, with jets indicating directed flow of centre charge, arrows showing vortex sheet movement in time, and spheres marking singular points where vortex sheets span all four dimensions.

## Key numbers

- Centre vortices are characterised by the three values of the cube roots of unity, with centre charge values \(z = e^{2\pi i m/3}\) where \(m = -1, 0, 1\)
- Plaquettes with \(m = +1\) are illustrated by blue jets in the forward direction, and plaquettes with \(m = -1\) by red jets in the backward direction
- Singular points (spheres) indicate lattice sites where the vortex sheet spans all four space-time dimensions, with sphere colour indicating the number of times the adjacent sheet can generate a topological charge contribution

## Context

This work extends the centre vortex perspective of 't Hooft (1977, 1979) and prior lattice studies showing that centre vortices reproduce confinement indicators (linear static quark potential) and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking indicators (infrared quark mass enhancement, hadron mass splitting). The visualisation approach reveals patterns hidden in large datasets, building on the authors' earlier work (Biddle 2019) that provided full 3D models. The findings enable deeper understanding of how vortex branching generates topological charge density and governs the size of instanton-like configurations that dynamically break chiral symmetry.
