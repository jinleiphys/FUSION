# Fixed-t subtracted dispersion relations for Compton scattering off the nucleon

> We present fixed-$t$ subtracted dispersion relations for Compton scattering off the nucleon at energies $E_γ\leq$ 500 MeV, as a formalism to extract the nucleon polarizabilities with a minimum of model dependence. The subtracted dispersion integrals are mainly saturated by $πN$ intermediate states in the $s$-channel $γN \to πN \to γN$ and $ππ$ intermediate states in the $t$-channel $γγ\to ππ\to N \bar N$. For the subprocess $γγ\to ππ$, we construct a unitarized amplitude and find a good description of the available data. We show results for Compton scattering using the subtracted dispersion relations and display the sensitivity on the scalar polarizability difference $α- β$ and the backward spin polarizability $γ_π$, which enter directly as fit parameters in the present formalism.

## Key claim

Fixed-\(t\) subtracted dispersion relations for Compton scattering off the nucleon, once-subtracted at \(\nu=0\), converge for all six invariant amplitudes and are essentially saturated by \(\pi N\) intermediate states, reducing the model dependence in extracting nucleon polarizabilities compared to unsubtracted dispersion relations; the subtraction constants at \(\nu=0, t=0\) are linear combinations of the polarizabilities, and only two parameters, the scalar polarizability difference \(\alpha - \beta\) and the backward spin polarizability \(\gamma_\pi\), need to be fitted to low-energy Compton data.

## Method

The authors construct once-subtracted fixed-\(t\) dispersion relations for the six invariant Compton amplitudes \(A_i(\nu, t)\), subtracting at \(\nu=0\) to ensure convergence; the subtraction functions \(A_i(0, t)\) are then determined via a second once-subtracted dispersion relation in \(t\), saturated by \(\pi\pi\) intermediate states in the \(t\)-channel. The \(s\)-channel dispersion integrals are evaluated using pion photoproduction multipoles from the HDT analysis (up to 500 MeV) and SAID SP98K (higher energies), with multipion contributions approximated by scaling resonant \(\pi N\) amplitudes via inelastic branching ratios. The \(t\)-channel contribution is computed from a unitarized \(\gamma\gamma \to \pi\pi\) amplitude combined with \(\pi\pi \to N\bar N\) amplitudes, and the subtraction constants \(a_4, a_5, a_6\) are fixed by unsubtracted sum rules at \(t=0\) while \(a_3\) (related to \(\alpha+\beta\)) is taken from Baldin’s sum rule.

## Key numbers

- Baldin sum rule value used: \(\alpha + \beta = 13.69 \pm 0.14\) (in units of \(10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\)), from Ref. BGM.
- \(\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma\) decay width: \(\Gamma = 7.74\) eV, giving \(F_{\pi^0\gamma\gamma} = -0.0252\) GeV\(^{-1}\); product \(F_{\pi^0\gamma\gamma}\, g_{\pi NN} \approx -0.331\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- Forward spin polarizability \(\gamma_0^p\) from HDT multipoles: \(-0.75\) (in units of \(10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\)); from SAID-SP98K: \(-1.26\).
- Forward spin polarizability \(\gamma_0^n\) from HDT: \(-0.06\); from SAID-SP98K: \(-0.03\).
- Unsubtracted DR analysis of MacGibbon et al. (1997) gave \(\alpha = (12.1 \pm 0.8 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\), \(\beta = (2.1 \mp 0.8 \mp 0.5) \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\).
- Backward spin polarizability \(\gamma_\pi\) from Tonnison et al.: \(-(27.1 \pm 2.2\,{+2.8 \atop -2.4}) \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\).

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-relation approach to nucleon Compton scattering by L’vov et al. (1997, 1998), who used unsubtracted fixed-\(t\) dispersion relations requiring an asymptotic contribution parametrized by a \(\sigma\) meson pole; the subtracted formalism avoids that model dependence. It also contrasts with chiral perturbation theory calculations (Bernard et al., 1991; Hemmert, Holstein, Kambor, 1997) that predicted \(\alpha+\beta \approx 25.5\) (in units of \(10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\)), nearly twice the precise dispersion-relation value. The method enables extraction of \(\alpha-\beta\) and \(\gamma_\pi\) directly from Compton data with minimal reliance on higher-energy models, and the \(t\)-channel analysis connects nucleon polarizabilities to the \(\gamma\gamma\to\pi\pi\) subprocess and pion polarizabilities, as noted by Holstein and Nathan (1994).
