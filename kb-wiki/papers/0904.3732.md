# Electric Dipole Polarizabilities of Hydrogen and Helium Isotopes

> The electric dipole polarizabilities of $^3$H, $^3$He, and $^4$He are calculated directly using the Schrödinger equation with the latest generation of two- and three-nucleon interactions. These polarizabilities are necessary in order to obtain accurate nuclear-polarization corrections for transitions involving S-waves in one- and two-electron atoms. Our results are compared to previous results, and it is shown that direct calculations of the electric polarizability of $^4$He using modern nuclear potentials are smaller than published values calculated using experimental photoabsorption data. The status of this topic is assessed in the context of precise measurements of transitions in one- and two-electron atoms.

## Key claim

Direct calculations of the electric dipole polarizability of ⁴He using modern chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions yield values smaller than those obtained from experimental photoabsorption data, but the authors note that the uncertainty in the underlying nuclear dynamics (rather than convergence uncertainties) dominates the error estimate, and that relativistic corrections from meson-exchange currents could affect the result by as much as ±2% for the well-bound ⁴He.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation using the no-core shell model (NCSM) in relative coordinates with harmonic oscillator basis states, employing the nucleon-nucleon interaction at N³LO and the three-nucleon interaction at N²LO from chiral perturbation theory. They applied Podolsky's technique combined with the Lanczos-moment method to compute the electric dipole polarizability from the ground-state wave function without explicitly calculating continuum states, and tested convergence by varying the model-space truncation parameter N_max and the harmonic oscillator frequency Ω.

## Key numbers

- ³H electric polarizability: 0.139(2) fm³ (this work, with three-nucleon forces)
- ³He electric polarizability: 0.149(5) fm³ (this work, with three-nucleon forces)
- ⁴He electric polarizability: 0.0683(8)(14) fm³ (this work, with three-nucleon forces; first uncertainty from model spread, second from systematic nuclear dynamics)
- ⁴He polarizability without three-nucleon force: 0.0822(5) fm³ (22% higher than the value with three-nucleon forces)
- ⁴He binding energy with three-nucleon forces: 28.50(3) MeV (within approximately 1% of experiment)
- ³H and ³He binding energies reproduced within 8 keV (about one part per thousand)

## Context

This work extends prior NCSM calculations of light nuclei by Navrátil (2007) and Quaglioni (2007) to compute electric dipole polarizabilities directly from the Schrödinger equation, rather than from experimental photoabsorption data as done by Pachucki (2000) and the He4-pol reference. The results provide the nuclear-polarization corrections needed for interpreting high-precision isotope-shift measurements in helium atoms, particularly the ³He-⁴He shift studied by Shiner (1995) and Drake (2006), where the polarizability correction is currently about 2/3 of the experimental uncertainty. Future improvements in measurements of ⁶He and ⁸He isotope shifts will require reliable polarizability values, which this work aims to supply.
