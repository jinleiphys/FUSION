# Ridge and Transverse Correlation at Separated Rapidities

> A simple phenomenological relationship between the ridge distribution in $Δη$ and the single-particle distribution in $η$ can be established from the PHOBOS data on both distributions. The implication points to the possibility that there is no long-range longitudinal correlation. An interpretation of the relationship is then developed, based on the recognition that longitudinal uncertainty of the initial configuration allows for non-Hubble-like expansion at early time. It is shown that the main features of the ridge structure can be explained in a model where transverse correlation stimulated by semihard partons is the principal mechanism.

# Ridge and Transverse Correlation at Separated Rapidities

## Key claim

The ridge distribution in Δη can be reproduced simply by shifting and integrating the single-particle η distribution, suggesting that there is no intrinsic long-range longitudinal correlation beyond what is already present in the single-particle distribution itself.

## Method

The authors compared PHOBOS data on the per-trigger ridge yield (1/N^trig) dN_R^ch/dΔη for 0-30% centrality Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with the single-particle charged hadron distribution dN^ch/dη, fitting the latter with a three-Gaussian function. They then proposed and tested a phenomenological formula relating the ridge distribution to an integral of the single-particle distribution over the trigger acceptance, with a single scaling parameter r adjusted to match the ridge height.

## Key numbers

- The ridge distribution in Δη is reproduced using the single-particle distribution fit with parameters A = 468, σ_0 = 2.69, a_1 = 0.31, η̂ = 2.43, σ_1 = 1.15 for the three-Gaussian fit to dN^ch/dη
- The scaling parameter r relating ridge to single-particle distribution is 4.4 × 10^{-4}
- Trigger particle transverse momentum threshold is p_T^trig > 2.5 GeV/c
- Ridge particles have p_T^a >~ 7 MeV/c at η^a = 3 and p_T^a >~ 35 MeV/c at η^a = 0
- The width parameter for the azimuthal correlation function in the Correlated Emission Model is λ = 0.11, corresponding to σ_c = 0.34 rad
- Inverse slope parameters from the model: T_0 = 0.245 GeV (background), T = 0.283 GeV (ridge), giving T' = 1.825 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of ridge formation in the recombination model (Chiu and Hwa, various years), where ridges arise from energy loss by semihard partons traversing the medium. It contrasts with models invoking long-range longitudinal correlation (Adcox et al., 2005; Gavin et al., 2009) and with hydrodynamic approaches that attribute azimuthal anisotropy to initial-state transverse fluctuations (Teaney and Yan, 2011; Schenke et al., 2011). The phenomenological relationship established here enables a simpler interpretation of the ridge as arising from transverse correlation without requiring long-range longitudinal dynamics, and has been extended to explain ridge formation in pp collisions at LHC energies.
