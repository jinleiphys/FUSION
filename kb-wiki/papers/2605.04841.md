# Nuclear Structure and Shape Evolution of Nd Isotopes

> In this work, we have analyzed the structural properties of even-even $^{126-188}Nd_{60}$ isotopes. For this we have used axially deformed Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with PK1 and NL-SH parametrization. In structural properties, We have estimated and analyzed binding energy per nucleon (B.E./A), two neutron separation energy ($S_{2n}$), differential variation of two neutron separation energy ($dS_{2n}$), quadrupole deformation parameter ($β_{2}$), root mean square nuclear charge radius ($r_{ch}$), neutron skin thickness ($r_{np}$) and single particle energy (SPE) levels of Nd isotopes. Some bulk properties are also compared with experimentally accessible results and with results of Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM). To understand the shape evolution around N = 92, the variation of the potential energy curves (PECs) with quadrupole deformation parameter are also investigated. From all the investigations, We observe some sign of stability at N = 92 and shape transitions around it.

## Key claim

The paper claims to find evidence for a shell or sub-shell closure at neutron number N = 92 in even-even neodymium isotopes, based on signatures in separation energies, single-particle energy gaps, and potential energy curves; however, the authors qualify that this stability arises from deformed shell effects rather than spherical magicity, as the N = 92 gap is smaller than the spherical N = 82 gap and does not yield a spherical minimum in the potential energy curves.

## Method

The authors used the axially deformed Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with two nonlinear parameter sets, PK1 and NL-SH, to calculate ground-state properties of even-even Nd isotopes in the mass range A = 126 to 188. They computed binding energy per nucleon, two-neutron separation energy, differential variation of two-neutron separation energy, quadrupole deformation parameter, charge radius, neutron skin thickness, and single-particle energy levels, and compared bulk properties with experimental data from the National Nuclear Data Centre and with the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM). Potential energy curves as a function of quadrupole deformation were also generated for isotopes in the range A = 140 to 162 to study shape evolution around N = 92.

## Key numbers

- Maximum error in binding energy per nucleon compared to experiment: 0.042 MeV for PK1 and 0.069 MeV for NL-SH.
- The splitting between the 1g9/2 and 1g7/2 orbitals in 142Nd is approximately 5 MeV for PK1 and 6 MeV for NL-SH.
- The 1h11/2 orbital drops by over 2.0 MeV in energy when transitioning from 142Nd (N = 82) to 152Nd (N = 92) according to both parameter sets.
- For 146Nd, the energy gap between prolate and oblate minima in the potential energy curves is around 0.01 MeV for NL-SH and 0.1 MeV for PK1.
- At N = 92, the gap between prolate minimum and oblate minimum is around 2.5 MeV for PK1 and 3.5 MeV for NL-SH.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape transitions and shell closures in the rare-earth region, particularly around N = 90 where quantum phase transitions of the X(5) type have been identified by Krucken (2002), Fossion (2005), and others. The authors note that a shell or sub-shell closure at N = 92 was previously predicted for samarium isotopes by Swain (2025). The results enable further investigation of deformed shell effects and shape coexistence in neutron-rich Nd isotopes, and the authors suggest that the shallow competing minima in axial potential energy curves may indicate gamma softness and triaxial correlations, pointing to the need for beyond-mean-field calculations including configuration mixing.
