# Structure of $Λ$(1405) resonance and $γ{p}\rightarrow K^{+}+(πΣ)^{0}$ reaction

> Three-body calculations of $K\bar{K}N$ system with quantum numbers $I=1/2$, $J^π=(\frac{1}{2})^{+}$ were performed. Using separable potentials for two-body interactions, we calculated the $πΣ$ mass spectra for the $(\bar{K}N)_{I=0}+K^{+}\rightarrow(πΣ)^{0}K^{+}$ reaction on the basis of three-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations in the momentum representation. In this regard, different types of $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ potentials based on phenomenological and chiral SU(3) approach are used. The possibility to observe the trace of $Λ(1405)$ resonance in $(πΣ)^{0}$ mass spectra was studied. Using the $χ^{2}$ fitting, it was shown that the mass of $Λ$(1405) resonance is about 1417 $\mathrm{MeV}/c^{2}$.

## Key claim

Using three-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) calculations of the \(K\bar{K}N\) system with separable two-body potentials, the authors show that the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance mass extracted from \(\chi^2\) fitting of the \(\pi\Sigma\) mass spectra in the \(\gamma p \rightarrow K^+(\pi\Sigma)^0\) reaction is about 1417 MeV/\(c^2\), but this value depends on which \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) potential model (phenomenological or chiral) is used and on whether one-channel or full coupled-channel AGS equations are solved.

## Method

The authors performed three-body Faddeev calculations in the AGS form for the \(K\bar{K}N\) system with quantum numbers \(I=1/2\), \(J^\pi=(1/2)^+\), using separable potentials for the two-body interactions. They computed the \(\pi\Sigma\) mass spectra for the \((\bar{K}N)_{I=0}+K^+ \rightarrow (\pi\Sigma)^0 K^+\) reaction, first with a one-channel approximation using exact optical potentials, then with full coupled-channel AGS equations including the \(K\pi\Sigma\), \(\pi\pi N\), and \(\pi\eta N\) channels. Five different \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) potentials were employed: four phenomenological (one- and two-pole versions of KEK and SIDD potentials) and one chiral SU(3) based potential.

## Key numbers

* The \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance mass extracted from \(\chi^2\) fitting is about 1417 MeV/\(c^2\).
* The pole positions of the \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) potentials used: SIDD\(^1\) first pole at \(1428.1 - i46.6\) MeV; SIDD\(^2\) first pole at \(1418.1 - i56.9\) MeV and second pole at \(1382.0 - i104.2\) MeV; KEK\(^1\) first pole at \(1411.3 - i35.8\) MeV; KEK\(^2\) first pole at \(1410.8 - i35.9\) MeV and second pole at \(1380.8 - i104.8\) MeV; Chiral first pole at \(1420.6 - i20.3\) MeV and second pole at \(1343.0 - i72.5\) MeV.
* The \(\chi^2\) values for the \(\pi^0\Sigma^0\) channel at \(W=2.0\) GeV using the full coupled-channel calculation (labeled B) range from 11.2 (SIDD\(^1\)) to 28.9 (KEK\(^2\)); at \(W=2.5\) GeV they range from 12.2 (Chiral) to 49.7 (KEK\(^2\)).
* The \(KN\) scattering lengths used: \(a^{I=0}_{KN} = -0.035\) fm and \(a^{I=1}_{KN} = -0.310 \pm 0.003\) fm.
* The \(K\bar{K}\) scattering lengths used: \(a_{K\bar{K}}^{I=0} = -0.14 - i1.99\) fm and \(a_{K\bar{K}}^{I=1} = 0.18 - i0.61\) fm.
* The \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) resonances were assigned mass 980 MeV and width 80 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance using chiral SU(3) dynamics (Kaiser et al., Oset et al., Jido et al.) and phenomenological models (Akaishi et al., Yamazaki et al., Shevchenko et al.) by applying three-body AGS equations to the \(\gamma p \rightarrow K^+(\pi\Sigma)^0\) reaction. It directly compares with CLAS collaboration data (Morini et al.) and prior analyses of those data using chiral dynamics (Roca et al., Nakayama et al., Mai et al.) and phenomenological approaches (Hassan). The results enable further discrimination between one-pole and two-pole descriptions of the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance and demonstrate the importance of including full coupled-channel effects in three-body calculations of this reaction.
