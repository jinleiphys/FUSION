# The topological structures in strongly coupled QGP with chiral fermions on the lattice

> The nature of chiral phase transition for two flavor QCD is an interesting but unresolved problem. One of the most intriguing issues is whether or not the anomalous U(1) symmetry in the flavor sector is effectively restored along with the chiral symmetry. This may determine the universality class of the chiral phase transition. Since the physics near the chiral phase transition is essentially non-perturbative, we employ first principles lattice techniques to address this issue. We use overlap fermions, which have exact chiral symmetry on the lattice, to probe the anomalous U(1) symmetry violation of 2+1 flavor dynamical QCD configurations with domain wall fermions. The latter also optimally preserves chiral and flavor symmetries on the lattice, since it is known that the remnant chiral symmetry of the light quarks influences the scaling of the chiral condensate in the crossover transition region. We observe that the anomalous U(1) is not effectively restored in the chiral crossover region. We perform a systematic study of the finite size and cut-off effects since the signals of U(1) violation are sensitive to it. We also provide a glimpse of the microscopic topological structures of the QCD medium that are responsible for the strongly interacting nature of the quark gluon plasma phase. We study the effect of these microscopic constituents through our first calculations for the topological susceptibility of QCD at finite temperature, which could be a crucial input for the equation of state for anomalous hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors find that the anomalous U(1) symmetry is not effectively restored in the chiral crossover region of QCD up to 1.2 times the chiral crossover temperature (T_c), based on lattice calculations using overlap fermions on domain wall fermion gauge configurations. This conclusion is hedged by the observation that the near-zero part of the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum still requires detailed study, though the contribution from the analytic (bulk) part of the spectrum to U(1) breaking is robust against lattice cut-off effects.

## Method

The authors used 2+1 flavor dynamical QCD gauge configurations generated with Möbius domain wall fermions and the Iwasaki gauge action at physical light and strange quark masses. They employed overlap fermions as valence quarks to probe the eigenvalue spectrum of the QCD Dirac operator, measuring 50 eigenvalues per configuration on 100-150 configurations at each temperature using the Kalkreuter-Simma Ritz algorithm. The topological susceptibility was computed from the topological charge measured via the overlap fermion index theorem, and the U(1) breaking was quantified through the difference between integrated pion and delta meson correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The residual chiral symmetry breaking for the domain wall fermion configurations is O(10^{-4})
- The overlap operator sign function was measured to a precision of about 10^{-10}
- Lattice volumes satisfy V^{1/3} >= 4/m_pi
- The quantity (chi_pi - chi_delta)/T^2 is non-zero at 1.2 T_c
- The topological susceptibility chi_t, when fitted to A * T^{-B}, gives exponent B = 11.4(5) for physical quark masses and B = 10.9(4) for m_s/m_l ~ 12
- The dilute instanton gas prediction gives chi_t ~ T^{-7}, which is not observed at 1.2 T_c
- For light quark masses about twice the physical values, chi_t is enhanced by a factor of two near T_c

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Sharma et al., 2015) which found hints that U(1) may not be effectively restored at 1.5 T_c, by now investigating temperatures between 1 and 1.2 T_c. The results contradict the possibility of effective U(1) restoration in this temperature range, which would have led to either a first-order transition or a second-order transition with U(2) x U(2) universality class rather than O(4) critical exponents. The findings enable future studies of anomalous hydrodynamics by providing the topological susceptibility as an input parameter, and suggest that dyonic structures may be present in the QCD medium just above T_c, potentially contributing to jet-quenching phenomenology in the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma.
