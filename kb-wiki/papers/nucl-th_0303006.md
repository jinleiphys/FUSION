# Self-consistent Approach to Off-Shell Transport

> The properties of two forms of the gradient expanded Kadanoff--Baym equations, i.e. the Kadanoff--Baym and Botermans-Malfliet forms, suitable to describe the transport dynamics of particles and resonances with broad spectral widths, are discussed in context of conservation laws, the definition of a kinetic entropy and the possibility of numerical realization. Recent results on exact conservations of charge and energy-momentum within Kadanoff-Baym form of quantum kinetics based on local coupling schemes are extended to two cases relevant in many applications. These concern the interaction via a finite range potential, and, relevant in nuclear and hadron physics, e.g. for the pion--nucleon interaction, the case of derivative coupling.

# Self-consistent Approach to Off-Shell Transport

## Key claim
The paper extends previous results on exact conservation laws within the Kadanoff-Baym form of quantum kinetics to two additional cases relevant in nuclear and hadron physics: interactions via a finite range potential, and derivative coupling (as occurs in pion-nucleon interactions). The authors discuss the properties of two gradient-expanded forms of the Kadanoff-Baym equations (the Kadanoff-Baym and Botermans-Malfliet forms) in terms of conservation laws, kinetic entropy definition, and numerical realizability.

## Method
The authors analyze the gradient expansion of the Kadanoff-Baym equations for quantum transport, comparing the Kadanoff-Baym and Botermans-Malfliet forms. They extend previous proofs of exact charge and energy-momentum conservation from local coupling schemes to the cases of finite-range potentials and derivative couplings, which are relevant for nuclear and hadron physics applications such as pion-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers
- No original numerical results are presented; the paper is a formal theoretical analysis of transport equation forms and conservation properties.

## Context
This work extends the formal foundation of quantum transport theory for particles with broad spectral widths, building on the Kadanoff-Baym equations and the Botermans-Malfliet formulation. It addresses the need for consistent conservation laws in transport descriptions of resonances and unstable particles in nuclear and hadron physics. The results enable future numerical implementations of off-shell transport for systems involving finite-range interactions and derivative couplings, such as those encountered in pion-nucleon dynamics.
