# Evolution of order and chaos across a first-order quantum phase transition

> We study the evolution of the dynamics across a generic first order quantum phase transition in an interacting boson model of nuclei. The dynamics inside the phase coexistence region exhibits a very simple pattern. A classical analysis reveals a robustly regular dynamics confined to the deformed region and well separated from a chaotic dynamics ascribed to the spherical region. A quantum analysis discloses regular bands of states in the deformed region, which persist to energies well above the phase-separating barrier, in the face of a complicated environment. The impact of kinetic collective rotational terms on this intricate interplay of order and chaos is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper shows that across a generic first-order quantum phase transition in the interacting boson model, the dynamics in the phase coexistence region exhibits a simple pattern: robustly regular dynamics is confined to the deformed region and well separated from chaotic dynamics in the spherical region, and this pattern persists for energies well above the phase-separating barrier.

## Method

The authors study the classical and quantum dynamics of an interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian that describes first-order quantum shape-phase transitions between spherical and axially-deformed nuclei. They resolve the Hamiltonian into an intrinsic part, which determines the Landau potential, and a collective part with kinetic rotational terms. Classical dynamics is analyzed via Poincaré sections for L=0 vibrations, while quantum dynamics is examined through Peres lattices of eigenstates for boson numbers N=50 and N=80.

## Key numbers

- Barrier height at the critical point: V_b/h_2 = 0.231 for β_0 = 1.35 (compared to V_b/h_2 = 0.0018 in previous works)
- Spinodal point: ρ* = 0.546 for β_0 = 1.35
- Anti-spinodal point: ξ** = 0.354 for β_0 = 1.35
- Critical point: ρ_c = β_0^{-1}, ξ_c = 0
- Normal mode frequencies for large N: ε = 2h̄_2 N β_0^2 (spherical side), ε_β = 2h̄_2 N β_0^2 (2ξ + 1), ε_γ = 18 h̄_2 N β_0^2 (1 + β_0^2)^{-1} (deformed side)
- Energy limit for classical dynamics: E_lim = 2h_2 for ρ ≤ ρ_c; E_lim = h_2(2 + ξ) for ξ > ξ_c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous observation of a simple mixed-dynamics pattern at the critical point (MacLev11) to the entire coexistence region of a first-order quantum phase transition. It contrasts with earlier IBM studies (Whel93) that used simplified Hamiltonians with extremely low barriers and narrow coexistence regions. The results demonstrate a clear connection between order, chaos, and structural changes of coexisting phases, relevant not only for nuclear physics but also for other interacting systems undergoing first-order quantum phase transitions, such as those studied in condensed matter physics (Belitz05).
