# Muon capture in nuclei: an ab initio approach based on quantum Monte Carlo methods

> An ab initio quantum Monte Carlo method is introduced for calculating total rates of muon weak capture in light nuclei with mass number $A \leq 12$. As a first application of the method, we perform a calculation of the rate in $^4$He in a dynamical framework based on realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions and realistic nuclear charge-changing weak currents. The currents include one- and two-body terms induced by $π$- and $ρ$-meson exchange, and $N$-to-$Δ$ excitation, and are constrained to reproduce the empirical value of the Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium. We investigate the sensitivity of theoretical predictions to current parametrizations of the nucleon axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors as well as to two-body contributions in the weak currents. The large uncertainties in the measured values obtained from bubble-chamber experiments (carried out over 50 years ago) prevent us from drawing any definite conclusions.

## Key claim
The authors introduce an ab initio quantum Monte Carlo method for calculating total muon weak capture rates in light nuclei (A ≤ 12), but the large uncertainties in experimental measurements from 1960s bubble-chamber experiments prevent any definite conclusions about the validity of their current model.

## Method
The method uses Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) techniques to evaluate Euclidean response functions via Laplace transforms of the nuclear response, then inverts these using maximum-entropy methods to obtain the energy-dependent response functions. The calculation employs the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Illinois-7 three-nucleon interactions, with charge-changing weak currents including one- and two-body terms from π- and ρ-meson exchange and N-to-Δ excitation. The two-body axial current coupling is constrained to reproduce the empirical Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium β-decay.

## Key numbers
- Total muon capture rate in ⁴He with full charge-changing current including two-body terms: 306 ± 9 s⁻¹
- Total muon capture rate in ⁴He with full charge-changing current including two-body terms but without induced pseudoscalar term: 355 ± 12 s⁻¹
- Experimental values from bubble-chamber experiments: 336 ± 75 s⁻¹ (Bizzarri 1964), 375⁺³⁰₋₃₀₀ s⁻¹ (Auerbach 1967), 364 ± 46 s⁻¹ (Block 1968)
- Maximum neutrino energy in ⁴He capture: approximately 83.6 MeV
- The induced pseudoscalar form factor reduces the capture rate by approximately 15% (one-body) and 14% (two-body) calculations
- The axial form factor cutoff Λ_A ≈ 1 GeV; varying it by ±10% changes the total rate by less than 1%

## Context
This work extends ab initio quantum Monte Carlo methods previously applied to electromagnetic response functions (Lovato 2013, 2015, 2016, 2018) to the weak capture process, bridging the momentum transfer regime between β-decays and neutrino scattering. The only prior theoretical estimates for ⁴He muon capture were from Caine and Jones (1963) using closure approximations and Walecka (1975) using Foldy-Walecka sum rules. The method enables future calculations for other light nuclei where more accurate data exist, and can be extended to chiral effective field theory based interactions and currents.
