# Variation of fundamental constants

> We present a review of recent works devoted to the variation of the fine structure constant alpha, strong interaction and fundamental masses. There are some hints for the variation in quasar absorption spectra, Big Bang nucleosynthesis, and Oklo natural nuclear reactor data. A very promising method to search for the variation of the fundamental constants consists in comparison of different atomic clocks. Huge enhancement of the variation effects happens in transition between accidentally degenerate atomic and molecular energy levels. A new idea is to build a ``nuclear'' clock based on the ultraviolet transition between very low excited state and ground state in Thorium nucleus. This may allow to improve sensitivity to the variation up to 10 orders of magnitude! Huge enhancement of the variation effects is also possible in cold atomic and molecular collisions near Feschbach resonance.

# Variation of fundamental constants

## Key claim
There are hints for variation of the fine structure constant, strong interaction strength, and fundamental masses from quasar absorption spectra, Big Bang nucleosynthesis, and Oklo natural nuclear reactor data, but the majority of publications report only limits on possible variations; the results may be explained by spatial variation of constants rather than temporal variation, as data from the Keck telescope (Northern hemisphere) and VLT (Southern hemisphere) give different results.

## Method
The paper reviews multiple approaches: comparison of quasar absorption spectra with laboratory spectra using relativistic many-body calculations of the sensitivity coefficients q for atomic transitions; comparison of different atomic clocks (optical and microwave); analysis of Big Bang nucleosynthesis and Oklo reactor data; and theoretical calculations of enhanced sensitivity in accidentally degenerate atomic levels (dysprosium), molecular rotational-hyperfine levels, the 229Th nuclear transition, and cold collisions near Feshbach resonances.

## Key numbers
- Quasar absorption data (143 absorption systems, redshift range 0.2 < z < 4.2): delta alpha / alpha = (-0.543 +/- 0.116) x 10^{-5}; assuming linear time dependence gives d ln alpha / dt = (6.40 +/- 1.35) x 10^{-16} per year over about 12 billion years
- For the dysprosium atom near-degenerate transition: enhancement coefficient K exceeds 10^8, with q = 30,000 cm^{-1} and omega ~ 10^{-4} cm^{-1}
- For diatomic molecules (LaS, LaO, LuS, LuO, YbF): enhancement coefficients K between 10 and 1000
- Big Bang nucleosynthesis hint for variation: ~10^{-3} (4 standard deviations from zero)
- Oklo natural reactor hint for variation: ~10^{-9} (11 standard deviations from zero)
- Variation of X_e = m_e / Lambda_QCD from hydrogen molecule spectra: delta X_e / X_e = delta mu / mu = (-2.4 +/- 0.6) x 10^{-5} (4 standard deviations)
- 229Th nucleus: excited state at (3.5 +/- 1) eV above ground state, width estimated at about 10^{-4} Hz; relative variation enhancement factor ~10^5
- For Cs-Cs collisions near Feshbach resonance: enhancement factor K ~ 400

## Context
This review extends the theoretical framework of grand unification models (Marciano, year not specified) which predict that variation in alpha should be accompanied by larger variations in the QCD scale and fundamental masses, with typical relations delta Lambda_QCD / Lambda_QCD ~ 34 delta alpha / alpha and delta m / m ~ 70 delta alpha / alpha. The work builds on prior calculations of atomic sensitivity coefficients (Dzuba et al., 1999) and nuclear calculations of mass ratio dependence (Tedesco, year not specified). The proposed nuclear clock based on 229Th could improve sensitivity to variation by up to 10 orders of magnitude compared to current atomic clock methods, and the spatial variation interpretation of the Keck versus VLT results opens the possibility of testing anthropic explanations for the fine-tuning of fundamental constants.
