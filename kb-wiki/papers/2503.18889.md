# Role of the $δ$ Meson in the Equation of State an Direct Urca Cooling of Neutron Stars

> The direct Urca (dUrca) process is a key mechanism driving rapid neutrino cooling in neutron stars, with its baryon density activation threshold determined by the microscopic model for nuclear matter. Understanding how nuclear interactions shape the dUrca threshold is essential for interpreting neutron star thermal evolution, particularly in light of recent studies on exceptionally cold objects. We investigate the impact of incorporating the scalar isovector $δ$ meson into the neutron star equation of state, which alters the internal proton fraction and consequently affects the dUrca cooling threshold. Since proton superfluidity is known to suppress dUrca rates, we also examine the interplay between the nuclear interaction mediated by the $δ$ meson and the $^1S_0$ proton pairing gap. We perform a Bayesian analysis using models built within a relativistic mean-field approximation, incorporating constraints from astrophysical observations, nuclear experiments, and known results of \textit{ab initio} calculations of pure neutron matter. We then impose a constraint on the dUrca threshold based on studies of fast-cooling neutron stars. The inclusion of $δ$ meson expands the range of possible internal compositions, directly influencing the stellar mass required for the central density to reach the dUrca threshold. Furthermore, we observe that the observation of relatively young and cold neutron stars provides insights into $^1S_0$ proton superfluidity in the core of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the scalar isovector $\delta$ meson in relativistic mean-field models expands the range of possible internal compositions of neutron stars, directly influencing the stellar mass required for the central density to reach the direct Urca cooling threshold, but the actual effect depends on the interplay with proton superfluidity which can suppress the cooling process.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with density-dependent nucleon-meson couplings, comparing two sets: Set A (with $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\rho$ mesons) and Set B (adding the $\delta$ meson). They sampled 9 parameters for Set A and 12 for Set B using uniform priors, filtered models for physical consistency, and applied constraints from astrophysical observations (NICER, GW170817), nuclear experiments (nuclear matter parameters from Margueron (2017)), and chiral effective field theory calculations of pure neutron matter from Huth (2021). They then imposed hypothetical constraints on the direct Urca threshold mass and introduced a simplified model for the $^1S_0$ proton pairing gap with two scaling parameters $s_x$ and $s_y$.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density prior range: 0.140 to 0.170 fm$^{-3}$ for both Set A and Set B
- Symmetry energy $J_{sym}$ prior range: 28 to 36 MeV for both sets
- Slope of symmetry energy $L_{sym}$ prior range: 0 to 120 MeV for both sets
- Bare nucleon mass: $M = 938.9$ MeV used in the effective mass calculation
- The $^1S_0$ proton pairing gap scaling parameters: $0.4 \leq s_x \leq 1.4$ and $0.2 \leq s_y \leq 1.0$, taken from Das (2024)
- Three temperature scenarios considered for pairing gap suppression: $T = 10^8$ K, $T = 10^9$ K, and $T = 10^{10}$ K
- The critical gap threshold for dUrca suppression: $\Delta_T = 1.56 k_B T$ from BCS (1957)
- Three dUrca threshold scenarios: "Always" ($M_{dUrca} < 1.3 M_\odot$), "Sometimes" ($1.3 M_\odot \leq M_{dUrca} \leq 2.1 M_\odot$), and "Never" ($M_{dUrca} > 2.1 M_\odot$)

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian studies of neutron star equations of state within the RMF framework, particularly Scurto (2024) and Malik (2022), by systematically incorporating the $\delta$ meson which introduces isospin dependence in the effective nucleon mass. The study is motivated by Marino (2024) who suggested that direct Urca processes may be necessary to explain exceptionally cold young neutron stars observed by XMM-Newton and Chandra. The results enable future comparisons with neutron star cooling observations to constrain both the equation of state and proton superfluidity in neutron star cores, building on the approach of Wei (2020) and Das (2024) for pairing gaps.
