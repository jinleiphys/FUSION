# Comment on "Problems in the derivations of the renormalization group equation for the low momentum nucleon interactions"

> The criticisms in manuscript arXiv:0803.4371 do not change the renormalization group equation for the "$\vlowk$" interaction nor do they affect any results in the literature. Several other potentially misleading statements about low-momentum interactions are also addressed.

## Key claim

The criticisms in arXiv:0803.4371 do not alter the renormalization group equation for the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) and have no impact on any results in the literature; the extra term \(\delta\beta^\Lambda(k',k)\) that the critic claimed must be added in channels with bound states is identically zero.

## Method

The authors demonstrate analytically that the proposed bound-state correction term \(\delta\beta^\Lambda(k',k)\) vanishes by using the cutoff independence of the half-on-shell T-matrix and the bound-state Schrödinger equation. They show that taking the derivative of the bound-state equation with respect to the cutoff \(\Lambda\) and using the fact that the bound-state momentum \(k_{B_i}\) is cutoff-independent leads to exact cancellation of the two terms in \(\delta\beta^\Lambda\). The argument is supplemented by a practical numerical test in the \(^3\)S\(_1\)–\(^3\)D\(_1\) channel, which shows no special problems or enhanced errors compared to other channels.

## Key numbers

* The low-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) is used in units of fm\(^{-1}\); the RG equation is derived for a sharp cutoff \(\Lambda\) with Cauchy principal value integrals.
* The bound-state momentum \(k_{B_i}\) satisfies \(k_{B_i}^2 < \Lambda^2\) for the bound state to be preserved; the demonstration shows \(\frac{d}{d\Lambda} k_{B_i}^2 = 0\).
* The numerical test in the \(^3\)S\(_1\)–\(^3\)D\(_1\) channel (the only channel with a bound state) shows no enhanced errors as the cutoff is lowered, consistent with the analytic proof.

## Context

This comment responds to Harada (2008), who claimed that the RG equation for \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) derived by Bogner, Furnstahl, and Schwenk (2007) required a correction in the presence of bound states. The authors reaffirm that the original RG equation is correct and that model-space methods (Lee-Suzuki, Okubo) and the RG treatment are equivalent, as acknowledged in later versions of Harada’s manuscript. The work enables continued use of \(V_{\text{low}\,k}\) in many-body calculations and clarifies that the RG evolution preserves the systematic nature of chiral effective field theory interactions when many-body forces are also evolved.
