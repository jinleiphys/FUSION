# Three-Nucleon Force in the 4He Scattering System

> We report on a consistent, microscopic calculation of the bound and scattering states in the 4He system employing modern realistic two-nucleon and three-nucleon potentials in the framework of the resonating group model (RGM). We present for comparison with these microscopic RGM calculations the results from a charge-independent, Coulomb-corrected R-matrix analysis of all types of data for reactions in the A=4 system. Comparisons are made for selected examples of phase shifts and measurements from reactions sensitive to three-nucleon force effects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that modern realistic two-nucleon forces alone cannot reproduce the splitting between the \(^3P_2\) and \(^3P_0\) phase shifts in the \(A=4\) system, and that neither the Urbana IX nor the TLA three-nucleon force significantly improves this splitting; instead, including negative parity states of the three-nucleon subsystem is more important than adding these three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic Resonating Group Model (RGM) calculations for bound and scattering states in the \(^4\)He system using the Argonne AV18 two-nucleon potential combined with the Urbana IX and \(V_3^*\) three-nucleon forces. They employed a model space with six two-fragment channels and included \(S\), \(P\), and \(D\) wave contributions for \(J^\pi = 0^+, 1^+, 2^+, 0^-, 1^-, 2^-\) channels, using the Kohn-Hulthén variational principle. The results were compared to a charge-independent, Coulomb-corrected \(R\)-matrix analysis of all types of data for reactions in the \(A=4\) system.

## Key numbers

- The small 29-dimensional model space for \(^3\)He using AV18 yields a binding energy of -6.37 MeV, an rms radius of 1.78 fm, and a \(D\) state probability of 7.7%.
- The large 35-dimensional model space for \(^3\)He using AV18 yields a binding energy of -6.69 MeV, compared to -6.92 MeV from Faddeev calculations.
- In the large model space, AV18 plus Urbana IX yields a \(^4\)He binding energy of -27.81 MeV, close to the experimental -28.296 MeV.
- The \(V_3^*\) three-nucleon force adds 650 keV additional binding to \(^4\)He.
- Experimental neutron-\(^3\)He spin-dependent scattering lengths are \(a_0 = 7.370(58)\) fm and \(a_1 = 3.278(53)\) fm; corresponding \(R\)-matrix results are \(a_0 = 7.398\) fm and \(a_1 = 3.257\) fm.
- Microscopic calculations for the large model space yield \(a_0 = 7.402\) to 7.590 fm and \(a_1 = 3.289\) to 3.424 fm, depending on the force combination.

## Context

This work extends the RGM calculations of Hofmann and Hale (2001) for the \(^4\)He system by including modern three-nucleon forces and negative parity distortion channels. It contradicts the expectation that standard three-nucleon forces like Urbana IX can resolve discrepancies in \(P\)-wave phase shifts, and instead supports the need for new \(LS\)-type three-nucleon force contributions as proposed by Kievsky (1997) for \(N-d\) analyzing powers. The comprehensive \(R\)-matrix analysis of Hale (2001) provides a benchmark for future microscopic calculations aiming to determine the operator structure of the three-nucleon force.
