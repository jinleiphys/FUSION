# Thermalization at lowest energies? A view from a transport model

> Using the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model we analyzed the production of pions and kaons in the energy range of 1-2 AGeV in order to study the question why thermal models could achieve a successful description. For this purpose we study the variation of pion and kaon yields using different elementary cross sections. We show that several ratios appear to be rather robust versus their variations.

## Key claim

The success of thermal models in describing particle yields at 1–2 AGeV is not due to full dynamical equilibration, but rather because certain particle ratios (especially \(K^-/K^+\) and the double ratio \((K^-/K^+)/(\pi^-/\pi^+)\)) are robust against variations of the underlying cross sections in the IQMD transport model, while other observables (like the \(K^+/\pi\) ratio) are not.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at 1.5 AGeV and other systems, systematically scaling the elementary cross sections for \(NN \leftrightarrow N\Delta\), \(\pi N \rightarrow \Delta\), and the \(\Delta\) decay width \(\Gamma_\Delta\) by global multiplicative factors. They then computed the yields of pions, \(K^+\), and \(K^-\) and compared the resulting excitation functions and ratios to experimental data from the FOPI and KaoS collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The total pion yield in central Au+Au collisions at 1.5 AGeV shows a maximum when the scaling factor for all cross sections is 1 (the standard parametrization); scaling factors above or below 1 reduce the yield.
- The pion yield divided by participant number scales linearly with system size only for the standard parametrization (scaling factor 1); other scaling factors produce increasing or decreasing curves.
- IQMD calculations reproduce the experimental pion and kaon excitation functions within about 10–20% precision.
- The \(\Delta\) decay width at the pole is about 120 MeV; varying it by a multiplicative factor changes the kaon yield only moderately, with the dominant production channel \(N_1N_2 \to N\Delta\), \(N_3\Delta \to NYK\) being partially balanced by the chain \(N_1N_2 \to N\Delta\), \(\Delta \to N\pi\), \(N_3\pi \to YK\).
- The ratio \(K^-/K^+\) shows only a small effect from scaling of cross sections and its excitation function follows the same shape as the experimental pion yield scaled by participant size.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport-model studies (e.g., Hartnack et al. 1994, bigpr) that supported the idea of a law of mass action for antikaon production, and directly compares to thermal-model calculations from THERMUS (Spencer 2005). It shows that the apparent success of thermal models at SIS energies (Cleymans 2005) arises from the robustness of certain ratios (especially \(K^-/K^+\) and the double ratio) against cross-section uncertainties, rather than from true thermal equilibrium. The results enable future studies to focus on which observables can reliably test thermalization and which are contaminated by dynamical uncertainties.
