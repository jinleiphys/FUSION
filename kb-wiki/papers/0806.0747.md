# Relativistic mean-field theory applied to the study of neutron star properties

> Nuclear physics can be applied in various ways to the study of neutron stars. This thesis reports on one such application, where the relativistic mean-field approximation has been employed to calculate the equations of state of matter in the neutron star interior. In particular the equations of state of nuclear and neutron star matter of the NL3, PK1 and FSUGold parameter sets were derived. A survey of available literature on neutron stars is presented and we use the derived equations of state to reproduce the properties of saturated nuclear matter as well as the mass-radius relationship of a static, spherical symmetric neutron star. Results are compared to published values of the properties of saturated nuclear matter and to available observational data of the mass-radius relationship of neutron stars.

## Key claim

This thesis applies the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation to the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) model to derive equations of state for neutron star matter, but the results are limited by the fact that the description of dense matter should not be based on a single set of observables and that further measurements of both nuclear matter properties and neutron star observations are necessary for a complete description.

## Method

The author wrote FORTRAN90 programs to solve the self-consistent RMF equations for the meson fields (scalar sigma, vector omega, and isovector rho) given proton and neutron Fermi momenta and a chosen parameter set. The equations of state for nuclear and neutron star matter were calculated for the NL3, PK1, and FSUGold parameter sets, including crustal equations of state (BPS for outer crust, BBP or polytropic for inner crust). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation was then solved using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method to obtain the mass-radius relationship of static, spherically symmetric neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- The self-consistent meson field equations were solved to a tolerance of about 8.0 × 10⁻⁷ fm⁻⁴ for the difference between left and right sides of the field equations.
- The minimum neutron Fermi momentum for beta-equilibrium was hard-coded as 1.49 × 10⁻¹ fm⁻¹.
- The TOV integration was stopped at a pressure of 10⁻⁸ MeV/fm³ when crustal effects were included, or at 2.0 × 10⁻⁵ MeV/fm³ when crustal effects were not considered.
- The QHD-I coupling constants were fitted to saturation at a Fermi wavenumber of 1.30 fm⁻¹ and binding energy of -15.75 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of RMF theory from finite nuclei to neutron star structure, following the approach of Todd and Piekarewicz (2003) and Piekarewicz (2007) as well as Ban et al. (2004). The author compares results for the NL3, PK1, and FSUGold parameter sets against published saturated nuclear matter properties and observational mass-radius data. The work enables future comparisons between theoretical predictions and upcoming experimental data from particle accelerator facilities and new radio telescopes, highlighting the need for cooperation between nuclear and astrophysics communities.
