# Distribution of Partial Neutron Widths for Nuclei close to a Maximum of the Neutron Strength Function

> For nuclei near a maximum of the neutron strength function, the secular dependence on energy E of s-wave partial neutron widths differs from the canonical form given by the square root of E. We derive the universal form of that dependence and show that it is expected to significantly influence the analysis of neutron resonance data.

## Key claim

For nuclei near a maximum of the neutron strength function, the secular energy dependence of s-wave partial neutron widths includes an additional universal enhancement factor of the form (E + |E0|)^{-1} on top of the canonical sqrt(E) dependence, and this factor may significantly influence the analysis of neutron resonance data; the validity of the Porter-Thomas distribution can only be reliably assessed when this dependence is accounted for.

## Method

The author derives the universal form of the energy-dependent factor f(E) for s-wave neutron partial widths by calculating the projection of the neutron single-particle scattering wave function onto the nuclear volume for an attractive square-well potential, analyzing the poles of the single-particle scattering amplitude in the complex k-plane. The enhancement factor is obtained by expanding around the location of a weakly bound state or virtual state pole near threshold, and the result is argued to be universal for any single-particle potential lacking a barrier.

## Key numbers

- For a square-well potential with radius a = 6 fm, the point of coalescence of poles on the negative imaginary k-axis has a distance in energy of about 0.5 MeV from threshold.
- For a weakly bound 4s-state, the binding energy is given by E0 = -(7π/2)^2 (ħ^2 ε^2)/(2 m a^2) where ε << 1.
- The next-order correction term in the expansion is (k a)^2/(7π); for 0 ≤ E ≤ 100 keV and a = 6 fm, this term is not larger than 0.01.
- For |E0| = 50 keV, the resonance enhancement factor changes by a factor of 1.4 over an interval of 20 keV starting from threshold.
- For |E0| = 10 keV, the resonance enhancement factor changes by a factor of 3 over an interval of 20 keV starting from threshold.
- For A ≈ 200, a weakly bound single-particle level changes by about 150 keV when A changes by one unit.

## Context

This work addresses the recent experimental result by Koehler et al. (2010), who measured neutron widths for Pt isotopes and concluded that the Porter-Thomas distribution must be rejected with 99.997% statistical significance, calling into question earlier successful tests of Random-Matrix Theory in nuclei reviewed by Weidenmueller (2009). The paper derives a universal enhancement factor for nuclei near a maximum of the neutron strength function, which has never been taken into account in any previous analysis of neutron width data, and suggests that the conclusions about RMT failure may not be upheld when this factor is accounted for. The result enables a new approach to determining the shell-model potential for neutrons in heavy nuclei with high accuracy.
