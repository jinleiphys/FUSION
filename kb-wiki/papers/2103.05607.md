# Unusual Features of QCD Low-Energy Modes in IR Phase

> It was recently proposed that there is a phase in thermal QCD (IR phase) at temperatures well above the chiral crossover, featuring elements of scale invariance in the infrared (IR). Here we study the effective spatial dimensions, $d_{IR}$, of Dirac low-energy modes in this phase, in the context of pure-glue QCD. Our $d_{IR}$ is based on the scaling of mode support toward thermodynamic limit, and hence is an IR probe. Ordinary extended modes, such as those at high energy, have $d_{IR}=3$. We find $d_{IR}<3$ in the spectral range whose lower edge coincides with $λ_{IR}=0$, the singularity of spectral density defining the IR phase, and the upper edge with $λ_A$, the previously identified Anderson-like non-analyticity. Details near $λ_{IR}$ are unexpected in that only exact zero modes are $d_{IR}=3$, while a thin spectral layer near zero is $d_{IR}=2$, followed by an extended layer of $d_{IR}=1$ modes. With only integer values appearing, $d_{IR}$ may have topological origin. We find similar structure at $λ_A$, and associate its adjacent thin layer ($d_{IR} >\approx 2$) with Anderson-like criticality. Our analysis reveals the manner in which non-analyticities at $λ_{IR}$ and $λ_A$, originally identified in other quantities, appear in $d_{IR}(λ)$. This dimension structure may be important for understanding the near-perfect fluidity of the quark-gluon medium seen in accelerator experiments. The role of $λ_A$ in previously conjectured decoupling of IR component is explained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the IR phase of pure-glue QCD at temperatures well above the chiral crossover, the effective spatial dimension d_IR of Dirac low-energy modes is less than 3 in a spectral range bounded by lambda_IR = 0 and lambda_A (the Anderson-like non-analyticity), but the abstract's suggestion of a simple low-dimensional regime is qualified by the finding that only exact zero modes have d_IR = 3, while a thin spectral layer near zero has d_IR = 2 followed by an extended layer of d_IR = 1 modes, with only integer values appearing.

## Method

The authors computed the effective infrared spatial dimension d_IR of Dirac eigenmodes in pure-glue QCD at T = 1.12 T_IR using lattice QCD with the overlap operator on hypercubic lattices. They extracted d_IR from the scaling of the effective mode support (based on the minimal effective number scheme) toward the thermodynamic limit, analyzing systems with L/a = 16, 20, 24, 32, 40, 48, 64 at fixed lattice spacing a = 0.085 fm and temperature 1/(Ta) = 7. The dimension was obtained from fits to the asymptotic form <efNm> proportional to L^{d_IR} using the four largest lattice sizes.

## Key numbers

- The IR phase temperature studied is T = 1.12 T_IR, with T_IR in the range 200-250 MeV and chiral crossover T_c approximately 155 MeV.
- The lattice spacing is a = 0.085 fm, determined via r_0 = 0.5 fm.
- The upper edge of the low-d_IR regime is at approximately 750 MeV, identified with the Anderson-like transition point lambda_A.
- Zero modes have d_IR = 3, occupying approximately 4% of the volume in the thermodynamic limit.
- Near-zero modes in a thin spectral layer have d_IR = 2.
- The central plateau of the low-d_IR regime has d_IR approximately 1.
- The spectral range analyzed for the left rise was 5-15 MeV, and for the right rise was 500-700 MeV.
- The Anderson-like critical layer near lambda_A has d_IR greater than or approximately 2.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Alexandru and Horvath (2019) that a new IR phase exists in thermal QCD at temperatures above the chiral crossover, characterized by scale invariance in the infrared and a power-law spectral density rho(lambda) approximately 1/lambda. The study connects the previously identified Anderson-like localization point lambda_A (studied by Giordano et al., 2013) to the IR phase structure, showing that lambda_A serves as the upper edge of the low-dimensional spectral region. The findings enable a more detailed understanding of how the conjectured decoupling of IR glue fields from the bulk produces non-analyticities in Dirac spectra, and may be important for modeling the near-perfect fluidity of the quark-gluon plasma observed at RHIC and LHC.
