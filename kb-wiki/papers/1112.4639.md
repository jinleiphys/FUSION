# Resonances in 19Ne with relevance to the astrophysically important 18F(p,α)15O reaction

> The most intense gamma-ray line observable from novae is likely to be from positron annihilation associated with the decay of 18F. The uncertainty in the destruction rate of this nucleus through the 18F(p,α)15O reaction presents a limit to interpretation of any future observed gamma-ray flux. Direct measurements of the cross section of both this reaction and the 18F(p,p)18F reaction have been performed between center of mass energies of 0.5 and 1.9 MeV. Simultaneous fits to both data sets with the R-Matrix formalism reveal several resonances, with the inferred parameters of populated states in 19Ne in general agreement with previous measurements. Of particular interest, extra strength has been observed above ECM \sim1.3 MeV in the 18F(p,p)18F reaction and between 1.3-1.7 MeV in the 18F(p,α)15O reaction. This is well described by a broad 1/2+ state, consistent with both a recent theoretical prediction and an inelastic scattering measurement. The astrophysical implications of a broad sub-threshold partner to this state are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports direct measurements of the 18F(p,alpha)15O and 18F(p,p)18F cross sections between center-of-mass energies of 0.5 and 1.9 MeV, and through simultaneous R-Matrix fits identifies a broad 1/2+ state in 19Ne at ECM = 1.455 MeV that accounts for extra strength above ~1.3 MeV; this state supports the Generator Coordinate Method prediction of a broad sub-threshold 1/2+ partner that would significantly enhance the astrophysical S-factor and reduce the uncertainty in the 18F destruction rate in novae.

## Method

A radioactive 18F beam of 3.924 MeV/A from the GANIL-SPIRAL facility was degraded to 1.7 MeV/A and stopped in a thick CH2 target. Protons and alpha particles from the 18F(p,p)18F and 18F(p,alpha)15O reactions were detected in a double-sided silicon strip detector at 248 mm downstream, with particle identification via energy versus time-of-flight. Center-of-mass excitation functions were reconstructed using an angle-dependent algorithm with SRIM2008.04 energy losses, and simultaneous R-Matrix fits using the DREAM code were performed to extract resonance parameters for states in 19Ne.

## Key numbers

- The broad 1/2+ state (resonance G) was found at ECM = 1.455 +/- 0.038 MeV with proton partial width Gamma_p = 55 +/- 12 keV and alpha partial width Gamma_alpha = 347 +/- 92 keV
- The well-known 3/2+ state (resonance A) at ECM = 0.665 MeV had constrained widths Gamma_p = 15.2 keV and Gamma_alpha = 23.8 keV from Bardayan (2001)
- The reduced chi-squared for the simultaneous fit including resonance G was 1.633; excluding it worsened the fit to 2.483 for the full data set and from 1.306 to 2.486 for the 1.0-1.8 MeV region
- Energy resolution for detected alpha particles ranged from 40-70 keV (rms) at ECM = 0.6 MeV to 60-160 keV at ECM = 1.9 MeV; for protons from 30-40 keV at 0.6 MeV to 25-50 keV at 1.9 MeV
- The 18F beam intensity was approximately 2 x 10^4 particles per second, with 18Ne contamination at about 3% intensity

## Context

This work extends direct measurements of the 18F(p,alpha)15O reaction, which is the dominant source of uncertainty in 18F destruction in novae and thus in predicted 511 keV gamma-ray flux from positron annihilation. The results confirm the broad 1/2+ state predicted by Dufour and Descouvemont (2007) using the Generator Coordinate Method and observed in inelastic scattering by Dalouzy (2009), but which was not seen in the earlier thick-target measurement by Murphy (2009) that only reached ECM ~1.6 MeV. The existence of this state supports the prediction of a sub-threshold 1/2+ partner that, when included, increases and tightens the astrophysical S-factor within the Gamow window and removes the possibility of strongly destructive interference between 3/2+ states highlighted by de Sereville (2005), thereby constraining the reaction rate to relatively high values and reducing the predicted detectability distance for novae.
