# Enhanced spin-dependent parity non-conservation effect in the $7s {}^2S_{1/2} \to 6d {}^2D_{5/2}$ transition in Fr: A possibility for unambiguous detection of nuclear anapole moment

> Employing the relativistic coupled-cluster method, comparative studies of the parity non-conserving electric dipole amplitudes for the $7s \ ^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow 6d \ ^2D_{5/2}$ transitions in $^{210}$Fr and $^{211}$Fr isotopes have been carried out. It is found that these transition amplitudes, sensitive only to the nuclear spin dependent effects, are enhanced by more than 3 orders compared to the low-lying $S-D_{5/2}$ transitions in Ba$^+$ and Ra$^+$ owing to the very large contributions from the electron core-polarization effects in Fr. This translates to a relatively large and, in principle, measurable induced light shift, which would be a signature of nuclear spin dependent parity nonconservation that is dominated by the nuclear anapole moment in a heavy atom like Fr. A plausible scheme to measure this quantity using the Cyclotron and Radioisotope Center (CYRIC) facility at Tohoku University has been outlined.

## Key claim

The parity non-conserving electric dipole amplitude for the $7s \ ^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow 6d \ ^2D_{5/2}$ transition in Fr is enhanced by more than 3 orders of magnitude compared to similar transitions in Ba$^+$ and Ra$^+$, but this enhancement depends critically on the inclusion of large core-polarization effects in the relativistic coupled-cluster calculation; the resulting light shift is predicted to be measurable only if the experimental uncertainty can be reduced to the shot-noise limit with at least 20 seconds of measurement time.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic coupled-cluster method in the singles, doubles, and important partial triples excitations approximation (CCSD$_{\text{t3}}$ method) to calculate the nuclear spin dependent parity non-conserving reduced matrix elements $\mathcal{Y}$ for transitions between hyperfine levels of $^{210}$Fr and $^{211}$Fr. They computed these matrix elements using a sum-over-states approach, including explicit contributions from low-lying $7P$ through $11P$ states from the CCSD$_{\text{t3}}$ method and estimating smaller contributions from core-valence and higher-level excitations using second-order many-body perturbation theory. The parity non-conserving induced light shift was then estimated from the interference of the NSD $E1^{PNC}$ and E2 amplitudes for the $7s \ ^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow 6d \ ^2D_{5/2}$ transition in $^{210}$Fr.

## Key numbers

- The reduced matrix element $\mathcal{Y}$ for the $7s \ ^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow 6d \ ^2D_{5/2}$ transition in $^{211}$Fr ranges from 3.77 to 8.78 (in units of $iea_0 \mathcal{K}_W \times 10^{-11}$) depending on the hyperfine levels, compared to values from 0.197 to 0.458 from Roberts et al., a difference of at least one order of magnitude.
- The $\langle 6d \ ^2D_{5/2} | D | 7p \ ^2P_{3/2} \rangle$ matrix element is approximately 10.51(7) $ea_0$.
- The estimated parity non-conserving induced light shift $\Delta \omega^{PNC}_M/2\pi$ for the $F_i=11/2 \rightarrow F_f=11/2$ transition in $^{210}$Fr with $M=1/2$ is $1406.7 \times 10^{-4}$ Hz, and for the $F_i=13/2 \rightarrow F_f=13/2$ transition it is $-1414.1 \times 10^{-4}$ Hz, using an applied electric field of $2 \times 10^6$ V/m and $\mathcal{K}_W \approx 0.57$.
- The E2 light shift $\Delta \omega^{E2}_{|M|}/2\pi$ for the $F_i=11/2 \rightarrow F_f=11/2$ transition with $M=1/2$ is 2.32 MHz, and for $M=3/2$ it is approximately 5.00 MHz.
- The lifetime of the $6d \ ^2D_{5/2}$ state is approximately 1704 ns.
- To achieve the shot-noise limit for the 0.28 Hz PNC induced light shift, the total measurement time $T$ must be more than 20 s for $N = 10^4$ trapped atoms.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Fortson (1993) to measure parity non-conservation using a single trapped ion, specifically applying it to Fr instead of Ba$^+$ or Ra$^+$. The calculations show that the $7s \ ^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow 6d \ ^2D_{5/2}$ transition in Fr has significantly larger NSD PNC amplitudes than the corresponding transitions in Ba$^+$ and Ra$^+$ studied by Geetha et al. and Bijaya et al., due to strong core-polarization effects and the favorable energy level structure of Fr. The results differ substantially from earlier calculations by Roberts et al. (using the correlation potential method) and Johnson et al. (using random phase approximation), highlighting the importance of higher-order correlation effects. The proposed measurement at the CYRIC facility at Tohoku University could provide an unambiguous detection of the nuclear anapole moment, complementing previous NAM observations in Cs by Wood et al. (1997) and Yb by Tsigutkin et al.
