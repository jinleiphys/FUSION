# Identity Method Revisited

> We discuss the impact of finite particle losses associated with instrumental effects in measurements of moments of produced multiplicities with the Identity Method towards the evaluation of fluctuation measures such as $ν_{dyn}$. We show that the identity method remains applicable provided it is modified to determine factorial moments $\langle N(N-1)\rangle$, rather than moments $\langle N^2\rangle$. We further show that $ν_{dyn}$ remains robust if detection efficiencies are uniform across the measurement's acceptance. The robustness is lost, however, if detection efficiencies are momentum dependent, although the identity methods remains applicable provided detection efficiencies can be determined with sufficient accuracy.

## Key claim

The identity method remains applicable in the presence of finite particle losses from instrumental effects, provided it is modified to determine factorial moments ⟨N(N−1)⟩ rather than moments ⟨N²⟩; the νdyn fluctuation measure remains robust if detection efficiencies are uniform across the measurement's acceptance, but this robustness is lost if efficiencies are momentum dependent, although the method still applies if efficiencies can be determined with sufficient accuracy.

## Method

The author derives expressions for the first and second moments of the identity variables Wp in the presence of binomial particle losses with species-dependent efficiencies εp, showing that the measured moments ⟨Wp⟩, ⟨Wp²⟩, and ⟨WpWq⟩ can be related to the true factorial moments ⟨Np(Np−1)⟩ and covariances ⟨NpNq⟩ through a system of linear equations involving efficiency-corrected quantities. The method is further extended to account for momentum-dependent efficiencies by binning particles in transverse momentum and defining identity variables Wpα for each species p and momentum bin α, with the moments then summed across bins with appropriate efficiency corrections.

## Key numbers

- For an example with average multiplicity ⟨Np⟩ = 100 and variance ⟨ΔNp²⟩ = 90, assuming efficiency εp = 0.8, the measured ratio ⟨Δnp²⟩/⟨np⟩² = 0.0115 amounts to a 28% error compared to the true ratio ⟨ΔNp²⟩/⟨Np⟩² = 0.009.
- The ratio ⟨n(n−1)⟩/⟨np⟩² is shown to equal ⟨N(N−1)⟩/⟨Np⟩² exactly when efficiencies are uniform, confirming robustness of factorial moment ratios.

## Context

This work extends the identity method originally developed by Gazdzicki (1997) and Gorenstein (2011) for measuring multiplicity moments in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, which neglected particle losses. The paper corrects this oversight by incorporating binomial efficiency losses into the formalism, showing that the original method's neglect of detector response can bias extracted moments. The results enable reliable measurements of fluctuation observables such as νdyn in experiments with non-uniform or momentum-dependent detection efficiencies, provided the efficiencies can be determined accurately.
