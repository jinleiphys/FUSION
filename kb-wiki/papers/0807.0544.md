# Comparing the first and second order theories of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics using the 1+1 dimensional relativistic flux corrected transport algorithm

> Focusing on the numerical aspects and accuracy we study a class of bulk viscosity driven expansion scenarios using the relativistic Navier-Stokes and truncated Israel-Stewart form of the equations of relativistic dissipative fluids in 1+1 dimensions. The numerical calculations of conservation and transport equations are performed using the numerical framework of flux corrected transport. We show that the results of the Israel-Stewart causal fluid dynamics are numerically much more stable and smoother than the results of the standard relativistic Navier-Stokes equations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the truncated Israel-Stewart (second-order) formulation of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics produces numerically more stable and smoother solutions than the relativistic Navier-Stokes (first-order) equations for bulk viscosity driven expansion scenarios in 1+1 dimensions, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific numerical implementation using the flux corrected transport algorithm and on the chosen test cases (expansion into vacuum with small and large dissipation).

## Method

The author solved the 1+1 dimensional conservation equations for charge, energy, and momentum together with either the relativistic Navier-Stokes (first-order) or truncated Israel-Stewart (second-order) transport equations for bulk viscosity, using the SHASTA version of the flux corrected transport (FCT) algorithm. The relaxation equations for the dissipative corrections in the second-order theory were rewritten in conservation form with source terms to be solved by the same numerical scheme. The expansion scalar was calculated using second-order accurate central differences with an additional five-point stencil smoothing, and an upper bound was imposed on the bulk pressure such that it could not exceed the equilibrium pressure.

## Key numbers

- Courant number set to λ = 0.4, cell size dz = 0.2 fm, giving dt = 0.08 fm/c
- Grid contains 240 cells, total of n = 200 time steps, corresponding to 16 fm/c expansion time
- Anti-diffusion mask coefficient A_ad = 0.8 (reduced by 20% from standard value of 1)
- For perfect fluid, entropy production is between 0.5% and 5% depending on setup; with A_ad = 0.8 the lower value was found, while error is less than 0.2% using A_ad = 1 after 200 time steps
- Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratios tested: ζ/s = 0.2 (small) and ζ/s = 1 (large)
- Speed of sound squared c_s² = 1/3 for the massless ideal gas equation of state
- Degeneracy of massless particles g = 16 for Stefan-Boltzmann relations

## Context

This work extends the study of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics to compare first-order (Eckart 1940, Landau and Lifshitz 1959) and second-order (Israel and Stewart 1979) theories numerically, motivated by the discovery of near-perfect fluidity at RHIC and lattice QCD results showing a large increase of bulk viscosity near the critical temperature (Kharzeev 2007, Karsch 2008, Meyer 2008). The paper establishes a numerical framework using the FCT algorithm that can be extended to multi-dimensional models, and addresses the numerical challenges of solving the relaxation equations for dissipative corrections, particularly the need for smoothing the expansion rate and imposing bounds on dissipative pressures.
