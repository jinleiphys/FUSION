# Electroproduction, photoproduction, and inverse electroproduction of pions in the first resonance region

> Methods are set forth for determining the hadron electromagnetic structure in the sub-$N\bar{N}$-threshold timelike region of the virtual-photon ``mass'' and for investigating the nucleon weak structure in the spacelike region from experimental data on the process $πN\to e^+e^- N$ at low energies. These methods are formulated using the unified description of photoproduction, electroproduction, and inverse electroproduction of pions in the first resonance region in the framework of the dispersion-relation model and on the basis of the model-independent properties of inverse electroproduction. Applications of these methods are also shown.

## Key claim

The paper presents methods for extracting nucleon electromagnetic and weak form factors from inverse pion electroproduction (IPE, πN → e⁺e⁻N) data in the first resonance region, relying on the quasithreshold theorem which states that the IPE amplitude is dominated by the Born terms (with accuracy better than 5%) up to total πN energy w ≈ 1.5 GeV, rather than requiring a full model-independent extraction.

## Method

The authors develop a unified dispersion-relation model for photoproduction, electroproduction, and inverse electroproduction of pions, using fixed-t dispersion relations without subtractions for isovector amplitudes and Born approximations for isoscalar amplitudes. The spectral functions describe the magnetic excitation of the P₃₃(1232) resonance via the πN scattering phase shift δ₃₃(w) and the nucleon magnetic form factor G_M^v(λ²), while quadrupole excitations (E₁₊/M₁₊ and S₁₊/M₁₊) are neglected as a first approximation. The model is validated against experimental photoproduction and electroproduction data, then applied to IPE to extract form factors using the quasithreshold theorem and compensation curves.

## Key numbers

- The E₁₊/M₁₊ ratio from photoproduction data is (−2.5 ± 0.1 ± 0.2)% (Beck reference)
- The E₁₊/M₁₊ and S₁₊/M₁₊ ratios do not exceed 7% and 15% respectively up to λ² = −4 (GeV/c)²
- The model is valid for total πN energy w up to approximately 1.5 GeV (E_γ^lab ≈ 0.73 GeV)
- The πN coupling constant used is g²/4π = 14.6
- Form factor normalizations: F₁^v(0) = F_π(0) = 1, 2m F₂^v(0) = 3.7, 2m F₂^s(0) = −0.12
- Table 1 gives extracted values of F₁^v and F^π at ten λ² values from 2.77 m_π² to 6.11 m_π², with errors ranging from 0.08 to 0.16; for example, at λ² = 2.77 m_π²: F₁^v = 0.96, F^π = 0.91, error = 0.10; at λ² = 6.11 m_π²: F₁^v = 1.36, F^π = 1.16, error = 0.08
- The quasithreshold theorem states the IPE amplitude is given by Born terms with accuracy better than 5% below w ≈ 1.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier methods from Surovtsev, Blokhintseva, and collaborators (1972, 1975) for extracting pion and nucleon electromagnetic form factors from IPE at low energies, which had been successfully applied to data on nucleon, ¹²C, and ⁷Li nuclei in the timelike region λ² from 0.05 to 0.22 (GeV/c)². The paper also builds on the quasithreshold theorem from Surovtsev and Tkebuchava (1972) and uses the dispersion-relation approach of Adler (1968) and Ball amplitudes. The methods enable future extraction of the pseudoscalar nucleon form factor G_P(λ²) from IPE data, which is important for testing the Goldberger–Treiman relation and studying chiral symmetry breaking, and also allow investigation of the γ*NN*₃/₂ vertex form factor G*_M(λ²) at positive λ² via asymmetry measurements in the Δ(1232) region.
