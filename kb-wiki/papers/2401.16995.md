# Compton Scattering on 4He with Nuclear One- and Two-Body Densities

> We present the first \emph{ab initio} calculation of elastic Compton scattering from 4He. It is carried out to $\mathcal{O}(e^2 δ^3)$ [N3LO] in the $δ$ expansion of $χ$EFT. At this order and for this target, the only free parameters are the scalar-isoscalar electric and magnetic dipole polarisabilities of the nucleon. Adopting current values for these yields a parameter-free prediction. This compares favourably with the world data from HI$γ$S, Illinois and Lund for photon energies $50\;\mathrm{MeV}\lesssimω\lesssim120\;\mathrm{MeV}$ within our theoretical uncertainties of $\pm10\%$. We predict a cross section up to 7 times that for deuterium. As in 3He, this emphasises and tests the key role of meson-exchange currents between np pairs in Compton scattering on light nuclei. We assess the sensitivity of the cross section and beam asymmetry to the nucleon polarisabilities, providing clear guidance to future experiments seeking to further constrain them. The calculation becomes tractable by use of the Transition Density Method. The one- and two-body densities generated from 5 chiral potentials and the AV18$+$UIX potential are available using the python package provided at \url{https://pypi.org/project/nucdens/}.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first *ab initio* calculation of elastic Compton scattering from 4He, carried out to N3LO in the delta expansion of chiral effective field theory. The calculation is parameter-free when adopting current values for the scalar-isoscalar electric and magnetic dipole polarisabilities of the nucleon, but the authors caution that this order may not suffice to reliably *extract* polarisabilities from 4He data, only to investigate the *sensitivity* of observables to them.

## Method

The calculation uses the Transition Density Method, which separates the Compton process into an interaction kernel of nucleons that react with photons and a background density of nucleons that do not, with the quantum numbers of the latter traced over before folding with Compton-scattering operators. One- and two-body densities were generated from five chiral potentials and the AV18+UIX potential, and the photon-nucleon Compton kernels previously used for deuteron and 3He calculations were employed. The calculation was performed to O(e^2 delta^3) [N3LO] in the delta expansion of chiral effective field theory, where the only free parameters are the scalar-isoscalar electric and magnetic dipole polarisabilities of the nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Cross section predicted to be up to 7 times that for deuterium
- Calculation compared with world data from HIgammaS, Illinois and Lund for photon energies 50 MeV less than or similar to omega less than or similar to 120 MeV
- Theoretical uncertainties estimated at +/- 10 percent
- Adopted scalar-isoscalar electric polarisability alpha_s = 11.1 +/- 0.6_stat +/- 0.2_BSR +/- 0.8_th (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)
- Adopted scalar-isoscalar magnetic polarisability beta_s = 3.4 +/- 0.6_stat +/- 0.2_BSR +/- 0.8_th (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3)

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory Compton scattering analysis from the proton, deuteron, and 3He to 4He, and is the first *ab initio* computation of this reaction (contrasting with the phenomenological approach of Sikora (2017)). The same scalar-isoscalar polarisability combinations probed in the isoscalar deuteron are probed in 4He, but with the advantage that 4He is a scalar, so the analysis is free from contamination by spin polarisabilities. The calculation enables future experiments to better constrain nucleon polarisabilities, particularly the proton-neutron polarisability difference, which currently stands at alpha_E1^(p) - alpha_E1^(n) = [-0.9 +/- 1.6_tot] and needs improvement to about one third of its present uncertainty.
