# Non-resonant direct p- and d-wave neutron capture by 12C

> Discrete gamma-rays from the neutron capture state of 13C to its low-lying bound states have been measured using pulsed neutrons at En = 550 keV. The partial capture cross sections have been determined to be 1.7+/-0.5, 24.2+/-1.0, 2.0+/-0.4 and 1.0+/-0.4 microb for the ground (1/2-), first (1/2+), second (3/2-) and third (5/2+) excited states, respectively. From a comparison with theoretical predictions based on the non-resonant direct radiative capture mechanism, we could determine the spectroscopic factor for the 1/2+ state to be 0.80 +/- 0.04, free from neutron-nucleus interaction ambiguities in the continuum. In addition we have detected the contribution of the non-resonant d-wave capture component in the partial cross sections for transitions leading to the 1/2- and 3/2- states. While the s-wave capture dominates at En < 100 keV, the d-wave component turns out to be very important at higher energies. From the present investigation the 12C(n,gamma)13C reaction rate is obtained for temperatures in the range 10E+7 - 10E+10 K.

# Non-resonant direct p- and d-wave neutron capture by 12C

## Key claim

The partial capture cross sections for the 12C(n,gamma)13C reaction at En = 550 keV agree with calculations based on the non-resonant direct radiative capture (DRC) mechanism, and the d-wave capture component becomes dominant at energies above approximately 100 keV, whereas the abstract notes that s-wave capture dominates at En < 100 keV.

## Method

The experiment used a pulsed neutron beam from the 7Li(p,n)7Be reaction at a 3.2 MV Pelletron accelerator, with an average neutron energy of 550 keV at the sample position. Prompt discrete gamma-rays from the capture state of 13C to its low-lying bound states were measured using an anti-Compton NaI(Tl) spectrometer at 125.3 degrees, where the second Legendre polynomial is zero, allowing angle-integrated intensity extraction. Partial capture cross sections were determined relative to the well-known 197Au(n,gamma)198Au cross section, with corrections for neutron multiple scattering, neutron shielding, gamma-ray absorption, and finite sample size calculated via Monte-Carlo methods.

## Key numbers

- Partial capture cross sections at En = 550 keV: 1.7 ± 0.5 microb (ground state, 1/2-), 24.2 ± 1.0 microb (first excited state, 1/2+), 2.0 ± 0.4 microb (second excited state, 3/2-), and 1.0 ± 0.4 microb (third excited state, 5/2+)
- Spectroscopic factor for the 1/2+ state at 3.09 MeV in 13C: 0.80 ± 0.04, determined from the measured capture cross section at 550 keV with 5% experimental uncertainty
- Calculated thermal capture cross section (En = 0.0253 eV) from the model: 3.15 mb, compared to the experimental value of 3.53 ± 0.07 mb
- Calculated thermal scattering radius R' = 6.45 fm, compared to the experimental R' = 6.3 ± 0.1 fm
- Maxwellian-averaged reaction rate: N_A <sigma v> = 476.0 + 4765.2 T_9 + 122.5 T_9^2 cm^3 mole^-1 sec^-1, where T_9 is temperature in units of 10^9 K

## Context

This work extends the previous measurements of the 12C(n,gamma)13C reaction at neutron energies up to 200 keV reported by Ohsaki et al. (1994) to the higher energy of 550 keV, confirming the non-resonant direct radiative capture mechanism described by Mengoni et al. (1995). The spectroscopic factor derived for the 1/2+ state (0.80 ± 0.04) differs from the value obtained from (d,p) transfer reaction analysis by Ohnuma et al. (1985) (Sb = 0.65), with the present method being free from uncertainties in the neutron-nucleus interaction in the continuum. The reaction rate obtained differs substantially from previously adopted rates (e.g., Caughlan and Fowler 1988), with the p-wave component making neutron capture competitive with other 12C processing mechanisms across the full temperature range from 10^7 to 10^10 K, with consequences for inhomogeneous big-bang models, s-process, and r-process nucleosynthesis studies.
