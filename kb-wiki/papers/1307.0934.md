# Nuclear equation of state in a relativistic independent quark model with chiral symmetry and variation with quark masses

> We have calculated the properties of nuclear matter in a self-consistent manner with quark-meson coupling mechanism incorporating structure of nucleons in vacuum through a relativistic potential model; where the dominant confining interaction for the free independent quarks inside a nucleon, is represented by a phenomenologically average potential in equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic form. Corrections due to spurious centre of mass motion as well as those due to other residual interactions such as the one gluon exchange at short distances and quark-pion coupling arising out of chiral symmetry restoration; have been considered in a perturbation manner to obtain the nucleon mass in vacuum. The nucleon-nucleon interaction in nuclear matter is then realized by introducing additional quark couplings to sigma and omega mesons through mean field approximations. The relevant parameters of the interaction are obtained self consistently while realizing the saturation properties such as the binding energy, pressure and compressibility of the nuclear matter. We also discuss some implications of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter along with the nucleon and nuclear sigma term and the sensitivity of nuclear matter binding energy with variations in the light quark mass.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a relativistic independent quark model with a confining potential in equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic form, combined with perturbative corrections for center-of-mass motion, one-gluon exchange, and chiral-symmetry-restoring pion couplings, can reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties self-consistently; however, the results depend sensitively on the choice of the light quark mass parameter \(m_q\), which is treated as a free parameter ranging from current-mass values (40–50 MeV) to a constituent-mass value (300 MeV).

## Method

The authors model each nucleon as three independent quarks confined by a phenomenologically average potential \(U(r) = \frac{1}{2}(1+\gamma^0)(ar^2+V_0)\) in equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic form. They compute the free nucleon mass by adding perturbative corrections for spurious center-of-mass motion, one-gluon exchange (color magnetic contribution with \(\alpha_c = 0.58\)), and pion coupling from chiral symmetry restoration via the PCAC relation. For nuclear matter, they introduce additional quark couplings to sigma and omega mesons in a mean-field approximation, fitting the coupling constants \(g_\sigma^q\) and \(g_\omega\) to reproduce the saturation binding energy and density of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation binding energy per nucleon: \(-15.7\) MeV at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) (fitted input).
- Compressibility modulus \(K\): 222.48 MeV for \(m_q = 40\) MeV, 223.81 MeV for \(m_q = 50\) MeV, and 258.913 MeV for \(m_q = 300\) MeV at saturation density.
- Effective nucleon mass ratio \(M_N^*/M_N\) at saturation: 0.90 for \(m_q = 40\) and 50 MeV, and 0.77 for \(m_q = 300\) MeV.
- Free nucleon charge radius \(\langle r_N \rangle = 0.87\) fm (fitted input); at saturation density, \(\langle r_N \rangle\) becomes 0.94784 fm for \(m_q = 40\) MeV, 0.94794 fm for \(m_q = 50\) MeV, and 0.94902 fm for \(m_q = 300\) MeV.
- Free nucleon axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\): 1.1179 for \(m_q = 40\) MeV, 1.1334 for \(m_q = 50\) MeV, and 1.3844 for \(m_q = 300\) MeV.
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{NN\pi}\) in free space: 10.19 for \(m_q = 40\) MeV, 10.34 for \(m_q = 50\) MeV, and 12.68 for \(m_q = 300\) MeV.
- Quark-meson coupling constants: \(g_\sigma^q = 5.46761\) and \(g_\omega = 3.96975\) for \(m_q = 40\) MeV; \(g_\sigma^q = 5.28816\) and \(g_\omega = 4.30828\) for \(m_q = 50\) MeV; \(g_\sigma^q = 4.07565\) and \(g_\omega = 9.09078\) for \(m_q = 300\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model previously developed by Barik and collaborators (Barik, 1985; Frederico, 1989; Batista, 2002), which uses a relativistic independent quark potential model rather than a bag model to describe nucleon structure. It contrasts with earlier quark-meson coupling studies by Guichon (1988) and Saito and Thomas (1995) by incorporating chiral symmetry restoration through explicit pion coupling and by examining the sensitivity of nuclear matter binding energy to variations in the light quark mass, following investigations by Flambaum (2007) and Carillo (2012) on quark-mass dependence of nuclear binding. The results enable future studies of how fundamental QCD parameter variations affect the nuclear equation of state, with implications for the stability of heavy nuclei and neutron stars.
