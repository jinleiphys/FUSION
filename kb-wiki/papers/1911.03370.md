# Photon production and elliptic flow from momentum-anisotropic quark-gluon plasma

> The emission of real photons from a momentum-anisotropic quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is affected by both the collective flow of the radiating medium and the modification of local rest frame emission rate due to the anisotropic momentum distribution of partonic degrees of freedom. In this paper, we first calculate the photon production rate from an ellipsoidally momentum-anisotropic QGP including hard contributions from Compton scattering and quark pair annihilation and soft contribution calculated using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation. We introduce a parametrization of the nonequilibrium rate in order to facilitate its further application in yield and flow calculations. We convolve the anisotropic photon rate with the space-time evolution of QGP provided by 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) to obtain the yield and the elliptic flow coefficient $v_2$ of photons from QGP generated at Pb-Pb collisions at LHC at 2.76 TeV and Au-Au collisions at RHIC at 200 GeV. We investigate the effects of various parameters on the results. In particular we analyze the sensitivity of results to initial momentum anisotropy.

## Key claim

The paper calculates for the first time the photon production rate from a quark-gluon plasma with ellipsoidal (transverse plus longitudinal) momentum anisotropy, and convolves it with 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics to obtain yield and elliptic flow. The authors hedge that the strong sensitivity of photon \(v_2\) to initial transverse anisotropy likely represents an overestimate because a full event-by-event fluctuating treatment was not performed.

## Method

The authors compute the real photon production rate from an ellipsoidally momentum-anisotropic QGP by combining hard contributions (Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation at tree level) with a soft contribution calculated using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, separated by a momentum scale \(p^*\) chosen at the minimum of the total rate. They parametrize the numerically obtained nonequilibrium rate with an exponential ansatz whose coefficients are tabulated on a grid of anisotropy parameters \(\xi_1, \xi_2\). This rate is then convolved with the space-time evolution from 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) with a quasiparticle equation of state, using smooth Glauber initial conditions, for Pb-Pb collisions at LHC 2.76 TeV and Au-Au collisions at RHIC 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

* For Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV with initially isotropic momentum distributions, the QGP photon yield in central collisions is several times higher than in peripheral ones, and the elliptic flow \(v_2\) becomes negative for \(p_T > 2\) GeV (0-20% centrality).
* For 0-80% Pb-Pb collisions, varying the initial longitudinal anisotropy parameter \(\alpha_z(\tau_0)\) from 1 (isotropic) to 0.3 increases the yield and reduces \(v_2\) for \(p_T \gtrsim 2\) GeV.
* Setting a small initial transverse anisotropy \(\alpha_x(\tau_0) = 1.01\) (with \(\alpha_z(\tau_0)=1\)) causes the QGP photon \(v_2\) at \(p_T \gtrsim 2\) GeV to grow to more than two times higher values compared to the isotropic case, while the yield is essentially unchanged.
* For Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV with isotropic initial conditions, both yield and \(v_2\) are smaller than for Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, due to differences in initial central temperature \(T_0 = 455\) MeV and shear viscosity to entropy ratio \(\eta/s = 2.25/4\pi\) (compared to \(T_0 = 600\) MeV and \(\eta/s = 2/4\pi\) for LHC).
* The uncertainty due to the conformal approximation (neglecting the quasiparticle mass \(\hat{m}\) when converting aHydro parameters to photon rate parameters) is shown to be small for the yield and \(v_2\) in the case with initial anisotropy \(\{\alpha_z(\tau_0)=0.5, \alpha_x(\tau_0)=1.01\}\) for 0-80% Pb-Pb.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of photon production from a spheroidally anisotropic QGP by Schenke et al. (2006) and earlier anisotropic hydrodynamics studies by Bhattacharya et al. (2015), by including transverse momentum anisotropy via an ellipsoidal parametrization of parton distribution functions. It also builds on the authors' own prior work on quark self-energies in anisotropic QGP (Kasmaei and Strickland, 2018) and on dilepton emission from anisotropic hydrodynamics (Kasmaei and Strickland, 2018). The results enable future phenomenological studies that incorporate a more complete set of photon sources (prompt, hadronic gas, decay photons) and event-by-event fluctuations to better connect with experimental data from RHIC and LHC.
