# New Observables in Inclusive Production of Quarkonia

> After an introduction motivating the study of quarkonium production, we review the recent developments in the phenomenology of quarkonium production in inclusive scatterings of hadrons and leptons. We naturally address data and predictions relevant for the LHC, the Tevatron, RHIC, HERA, LEP, B factories and EIC. An up-to-date discussion of the contributions from feed downs within the charmonium and bottomonium families as well as from b hadrons to charmonia is also provided. This contextualises an exhaustive overview of new observables such as the associated production along with a Standard Model boson (photon, W and Z), with another quarkonium, with another heavy quark as well as with light hadrons or jets. We address the relevance of these reactions in order to improve our understanding of the mechanisms underlying quarkonium production as well as the physics of multi-parton interactions, in particular the double parton scatterings. An outlook towards future studies and facilities concludes this review.

## Key claim

The Color Evaporation Model (CEM) for quarkonium production, while successfully describing the energy dependence of total cross sections, systematically overshoots high-$P_T$ data because its leading-$P_T$ fragmentation contributions produce a harder spectrum than observed; this shortcoming persists at both LO and NLO in QCD, and the first NLO fit of the CEM parameter $\mathcal{P}^{\rm prompt}_{J/\psi}$ using $P_T$-differential ATLAS data yields a value about a factor of 2–3 smaller than that obtained from $P_T$-integrated yields, challenging the universality of the CEM parameter.

## Method

The author reviews the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) and the Color-Octet Mechanism (COM) within Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) for inclusive quarkonium hadroproduction, focusing on $P_T$-differential cross sections at NLO (one-loop) accuracy. For the CEM, the first NLO fit of $\mathcal{P}^{\rm prompt}_{J/\psi}$ was performed using MadGraph5_aMC@NLO to compute $J/\psi$ + a recoiling parton at $\sqrt{s}=8$ TeV, with the charm quark mass $m_c=1.27$ GeV and NNPDF 2.3 PDFs, fitting to ATLAS single-$J/\psi$ data. For NRQCD, the review summarizes NLO studies by three groups (Hamburg, IHEP, PKU) that include the three leading color-octet transitions ($^1S_0^{[8]}$, $^3S_1^{[8]}$, $^3P_J^{[8]}$) and discusses the role of feed-down contributions and polarization.

## Key numbers

- $\mathcal{P}^{\rm LO, prompt}_{J/\psi} = 0.014 \pm 0.001$ and $\mathcal{P}^{\rm NLO, prompt}_{J/\psi} = 0.009 \pm 0.0004$, obtained from fitting ATLAS $P_T$-differential data at $\sqrt{s}=8$ TeV with $m_c=1.27$ GeV (Lansberg 2016).
- The $K$ factor affecting $d\sigma/dP_T$ from LO to NLO in the CEM is close to 1.6.
- The CEM parameter from $P_T$-differential yields is a factor of 2–3 smaller than that from $P_T$-integrated yields.
- For $J/\psi$ photoproduction, the CEM at NLO describes total cross section data with $\mathcal{P}_{J/\psi} = 0.048 \div 0.056$ and $m_c \simeq 1.45$ GeV (Amundson 1996).
- In $e^+e^-$ annihilation at $B$ factories, the CEM predicts $\sigma(J/\psi+X_{\text{non } c \bar c}) = 0.095$ pb with $\mathcal{P}_{J/\psi}=0.025$ and $m_c=1.4$ GeV (Kang 2004), about 4.5 times below the Belle measurement.
- The NRQCD-CEM total cross section overshoots data by a factor as large as 100 at both low and high energies (Feng 2015).
- Vogt's NLO fits of $\mathcal{P}^{\rm direct/prompt}_{J/\psi}$ using $P_T$-integrated data up to $\sqrt{s}=62$ GeV yield values between 1.5% and 2.5% (Bedjidian 2004).
- For $\Upsilon$, Vogt's fit yields a similar magnitude around 2%.

## Context

This review extends the phenomenology of the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) and the Color-Octet Mechanism (COM) within NRQCD, addressing the long-standing tension between the CEM's too-hard $P_T$ spectrum and experimental data, first identified in the late 1990s (Edin 1997, Damet 2001, BrennerMariotto 2001). The first NLO CEM fit of $\mathcal{P}^{\rm prompt}_{J/\psi}$ using $P_T$-differential data (Lansberg 2016) provides a benchmark for future studies, while the improved CEM (ICEM) by Ma and Vogt (2016) introduces kinematic shifts to address yield ratios of excited states. The review also compares CEM predictions with NRQCD results, noting that the NRQCD-CEM implementation (Bodwin 2005) dramatically overshoots data, and highlights the need for further theoretical work on polarization and multi-parton interactions. Future studies at the LHC, EIC, and other facilities are expected to test these models more precisely.
