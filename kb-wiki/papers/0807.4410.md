# Unified description of 0+ states in a large class of nuclear collective models

> A remarkably simple regularity in the energies of 0+ states in a broad class of collective models is discussed. A single formula for all 0+ states in flat-bottomed infinite potentials that depends only on the number of dimensions and a simpler expression applicable to all three IBA symmetries in the large boson number limit are presented. Finally, a connection between the energy expression for 0+ states given by the X(5) model and the predictions of the IBA near the critical point is explored.

## Key claim

The energies of all excited 0+ states in any flat-bottomed infinite square well potential in D dimensions follow a single universal formula, E = A n (n + (D+1)/2), where n is the ordinal number of the 0+ state and A is model-dependent; this regularity is exact for D=3 and an excellent approximation for low ν in other dimensions.

## Method

The authors derived the energy eigenvalues for 0+ states in the E(5), Z(5), X(5), Z(4), and X(3) geometric models by solving the Bohr Hamiltonian with an infinite square well in β, obtaining Bessel function roots of order ν = (D-2)/2. They then normalized these energies to the first excited 0+ state and extracted a universal quadratic formula in n. Separately, they computed the 0+ energies for the three IBA dynamical symmetries (U(5), SU(3), O(6)) in the large boson number limit and for an IBA Hamiltonian near the critical point with NB = 250 and ζ = 0.473.

## Key numbers

- Normalized 0+ energies (E(0₂⁺) = 1.0) for E(5), Z(5), and X(5) are identical: 0, 1.0, 2.50, 4.50, 7.00, 10.00 for 0₁⁺ through 0₆⁺.
- IBA calculation near the critical point (NB = 250, ζ = 0.473) gives normalized 0+ energies: 0, 1.0, 2.48, 4.62, 7.13, 9.85 for 0₁⁺ through 0₆⁺.
- For X(5) candidates: ¹⁵⁰Nd has an experimental 0₃⁺ at 1738 keV versus X(5) prediction of 1688 keV; ¹⁵²Sm has 0₃⁺ at 1659 keV versus 1712 keV; ¹⁵⁴Gd has 0₃⁺ at 1650 keV versus 1702 keV.
- For SU(3) candidates: ¹⁵⁶Gd has experimental 0⁺ states at 1851 and 1989 keV versus SU(3) predictions of 1916 and 2054 keV; ¹⁵⁸Gd has states at 2276 and 2338 keV versus 2201 and 2344 keV.

## Context

This work extends the geometric critical point symmetries E(5) (Iachello, 2000) and X(5) (Iachello, 2001) by showing that their 0+ energies follow a universal pattern dependent only on dimensionality, and connects these to the IBA dynamical symmetries (Arima and Iachello, 1970s) in the large NB limit. The results enable systematic comparison of 0+ states across geometric models and the IBA, and provide a simple test for experimental candidates near phase transitional regions, as demonstrated for ¹⁵⁰Nd, ¹⁵²Sm, and ¹⁵⁴Gd. The formula also applies to models of pairing phase transitions (Clark, 2007) and hadronic spectra (Brodsky, 2007).
