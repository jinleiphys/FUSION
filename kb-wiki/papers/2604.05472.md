# Multi-Quark Clustering in Neutron-Star Matter from Color-Spin Molecular Dynamics

> We study the equation of state of neutron-star matter with color-spin molecular dynamics. The calculation includes the internal color and spin degrees of freedom and their time evolution. The matter composition, including strangeness under beta equilibrium, is determined by energy minimization. We find two main trends. First, within the present CSMD framework and under the adopted clustering criterion along the stable neutron-star branch, isolated quark-like configurations do not appear; instead, color-magnetic interactions favor the self-consistent formation of multi-quark clusters. Within the same criterion, the cluster-size distribution is concentrated at quark numbers that are multiples of three, corresponding to integer baryon numbers. Second, the interaction between strange and light quarks has a strong impact on neutron-star radii. This suggests that future radius measurements may help constrain flavor-sector interactions, including those involving strangeness.

## Key claim

Within the color-spin molecular dynamics (CSMD) framework and under the adopted clustering criterion along the stable neutron-star branch, isolated quark-like configurations do not appear; instead, color-magnetic interactions favor the self-consistent formation of multi-quark clusters whose size distribution is concentrated at quark numbers that are multiples of three, corresponding to integer baryon numbers.

## Method

The authors model neutron-star matter using a time-dependent variational ansatz where each quark is represented by a Gaussian wave packet, with internal color and spin degrees of freedom evolved via Euler-Lagrange equations. The Hamiltonian includes relativistic kinetic energy, color-dependent confinement and one-gluon-exchange potentials, color-spin (color-magnetic) interactions, and a color-independent quark-meson-coupling term extended to a σ-ω-ρ-φ-K* framework that explicitly includes strange-light (sq) quark interactions. The matter composition under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality is determined by energy minimization with frictional cooling, and the system is studied in a box with periodic boundary conditions.

- Color-magnetic coupling g_CS = 0.34, calibrated to reproduce experimental baryon masses
- Nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.172 fm⁻³, symmetry energy J = 30.3 MeV, symmetry energy slope L = 79.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous molecular-dynamics studies (Akimura 2005, Yasutake 2024) by including strangeness, solving evolution equations for internal spin degrees of freedom, and adding a K* meson channel to incorporate strange-light quark interactions that were absent in earlier σ-ω-ρ-φ models. The results address the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron-star matter, where inclusion of strangeness typically softens the equation of state too much to support 2 M⊙ stars. The finding that the strange-light coupling strength must satisfy g_qK*/g_qω ≳ 0.4 to achieve realistic onset densities suggests that future neutron-star radius measurements from NICER (Vinciguerra 2024, Choudhury 2024) and gravitational-wave detectors (Abbott 2017) may help constrain flavor-sector interactions involving strangeness.
