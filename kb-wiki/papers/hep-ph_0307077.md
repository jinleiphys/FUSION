# Light cone nucleon wave function in the quark-soliton model

> The light-cone wave function of the nucleon is calculated in the limit N_c -> infinity in the quark-soliton model inspired by the theory of the instanton vacuum of QCD. The technique of the finite time evolution operator is used in order to derive expressions for all components of the Fock vector describing the nucleon in the infinite momentum frame. It is shown that nucleon wave function for large N_c can be expressed in terms of the wave function of the discrete level in the self-consistent meson field and light cone wave functions of 1,2, etc mesons. The 3-quark components of the nucleon and Delta-resonance are estimated. Wave function of the nucleon appears to be positive in the whole region of x and it has rather small asymmetry. It differs strongly both from Chernyak-Zhitnitsky wave function and the asymptotic one. Large momentum transfer asymptotic of the electromagnetic and axial form factors is discussed.

## Key claim

The light-cone wave function of the nucleon is calculated in the limit \(N_c \to \infty\) using the quark-soliton model derived from the instanton vacuum of QCD; the authors show that the nucleon wave function is positive in the whole region of \(x\) and has rather small asymmetry, differing strongly from both the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky wave function and the asymptotic one, but they note that the large momentum transfer asymptotic of form factors cannot be calculated explicitly because the relevant integrals depend strongly on the small-\(x\) region where the model is not valid.

## Method

The authors use the finite-time evolution operator technique to derive expressions for all components of the Fock vector describing the nucleon in the infinite momentum frame. In the large-\(N_c\) limit, the functional integral over the pion field is evaluated via the saddle-point method, where the stationary solution corresponds to the nucleon-soliton at rest; the nucleon wave function is then expressed as a product of \(N_c\) one-quark wave functions (from the discrete level in the self-consistent meson field) and a coherent exponential of quark-antiquark pairs (the sea quarks), with the pair wave function obtained from the finite-time Feynman Green function expanded in the pion field.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass \({\cal M}_N\) is of order \(O(N_c)\); the minimum of the energy functional \({\cal E}[\pi] = E_{\text{field}}[\pi] + N_c E_{\text{level}}[\pi]\) gives the nucleon mass.
- The discrete level wave function in the stationary hedgehog field has components \(h(r)\) (orbital angular momentum \(L=0\)) and \(j(r)\) (\(L=1\)), obtained by numerical integration of the Dirac equation.
- The quark-antiquark pair wave function in the interpolation formula (first non-trivial term) is given by an expression with denominator \((z_1+z_2)z_1z_2{\cal M}_N^2 + (M^2+p_{\perp 1}^2)z_2 + (M^2+p_{\perp 2}^2)z_1\), where \(z_1, z_2\) are momentum fractions and \(M\) is the effective quark mass.
- The accuracy of the interpolation formula for the Green function is typically around 10%, as checked in the calculation of the nucleon structure function.

## Context

This work extends the program of computing high-energy properties of baryons in the quark-soliton model, following earlier calculations of parton distributions (Part1, Part2) and generalized distributions (Offw) at a low normalization point. It differs from the only previous calculation of light-cone nucleon wave functions, which used QCD sum rules (Chernyak, 1984), and from the asymptotic wave function predicted by perturbative QCD. The results enable future calculation of ratios of electromagnetic and axial form factors at large momentum transfer, and provide a framework for systematic \(1/N_c\) corrections.
