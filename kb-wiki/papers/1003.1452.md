# Many-body interactions and nuclear structure

> This article presents several challenges to nuclear many-body theory and our understanding of the stability of nuclear matte r. In order to achieve this, we present five different cases, starting with an idealized toy model. These cases expose problems that need to be understood in order to match recent advances in nuclear theory with current experimental programs in low-energy nuclear physics. In particular, we focus on our current understanding, or lack thereof, of many-body forces, and how they evolve as functions of the number of particles . We provide examples of discrepancies between theory and experiment and outline some selected perspectives for future research directions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that understanding how many-body forces evolve as a function of particle number is crucial for predictive nuclear many-body theory, and demonstrates through a toy model that effective interactions derived from sub-cluster approximations become unreliable when particle-hole interactions are present, even for small interaction strengths.

## Method

The authors constructed a toy model with N doubly degenerate single-particle levels, a pairing interaction with strength g, and a particle-hole interaction with strength f. They computed effective Hamiltonians using sub-cluster approximations for a=2, 3, and 4 particles, then compared the eigenvalues obtained from these effective interactions to exact eigenvalues for an A=5 body system. The effective interactions were constructed using a similarity transformation with overlap-based eigenvector selection.

## Key numbers

- For pure pairing interaction (f=0) with A=5, N=8 levels, and model space N_P=5, the error in the three lowest eigenvalues showed an almost perfect g^3 behavior for g>0
- For pairing plus particle-hole interaction with f=0.05g, the fraction of "good" selections of eigenpairs was 15/15 for a=2, 4/40 for a=3, and 9/50 for a=4 at g=0.05
- For f=0.5g, the fraction of "good" selections was 11/15 for a=2, 4/40 for a=3, and 8/50 for a=4 at g=1.0
- The typical relation between single-particle spacing and nuclear pairing interaction strength is roughly |xi/g| ~ 1-5, with the shell-gap in 16O being 11.25 MeV and effective interactions of order 1-5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier requirements for many-body theory from 2004 (Hjorth-Jensen and Dean, 2004) and addresses challenges raised by developments in effective field theories (Epelbaum, 2009), lattice QCD calculations (Ishii, 2007; Ishii, 2009), and many-body methods applied to nuclei (Bogner, 2010; Navratil, 2009; Hagen, 2009; Barbieri, 2009). The toy model demonstrates problems that arise in realistic no-core shell-model calculations (Navratil, 2009) and standard shell-model calculations with effective interactions (Hjorth-Jensen, 1995), particularly regarding the reliability of sub-cluster approximations when particle-hole correlations are present. The paper motivates experimental measurements of binding energies for neutron-rich isotopes including 28O, 54Ca, 60Ca, and 140Sn.
