# Taylor expansions and Padé approximants for cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations at non-vanishing chemical potentials

> Using high statistics datasets generated in (2+1)-flavor QCD calculations at finite temperature we present results for low order cumulants of net baryon-number fluctuations at non-zero values of the baryon chemical potential. We calculate Taylor expansions for the pressure (zeroth order cumulant), net baryon-number density (first order cumulant) and the variance of the distribution on net-baryon number fluctuations (second order cumulant). We obtain series expansions from an eighth order expansion of the pressure and compare these to diagonal Padé approximants. This allows us to estimate the range of values for the baryon chemical potential in which these expansions are reliable. We find $μ_B/T\le 2.5$, $2.0$ and $1.5$ for the zeroth, first and second order cumulants, respectively. We furthermore, construct estimators for the radius of convergence of the Taylor series of the pressure. In the vicinity of the pseudo-critical temperature, $T_{pc}\simeq 156.5$ MeV, we find $μ_B/T \gtrsim\ 2.9$ at vanishing strangeness chemical potential and somewhat larger values for strangeness neutral matter. These estimates are temperature dependent and range from $μ_B/T \gtrsim\ 2.2$ at $T=135$ MeV to $μ_B/T\ \gtrsim\ 3.2$ at $T=165$ MeV. The estimated radius of convergences is the same for any higher order cumulant.

## Key claim

The authors find that Taylor expansions for cumulants of net baryon-number fluctuations in (2+1)-flavor QCD are reliable up to specific values of the baryon chemical potential that depend on the order of the cumulant, with the radius of convergence of the underlying pressure series estimated to be larger than these reliability ranges.

## Method

The authors used high-statistics lattice QCD calculations with the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action for (2+1)-flavor QCD at physical quark masses, generating datasets on lattices with temporal extent Nτ=8, 12, and 16. They computed Taylor expansion coefficients for the pressure up to eighth order in the baryon chemical potential, then constructed diagonal Padé approximants from these series to study the analytic structure and estimate the radius of convergence.

## Key numbers

- The Taylor series for the pressure (zeroth order cumulant) is reliable for μB/T ≤ 2.5
- The Taylor series for the net baryon-number density (first order cumulant) is reliable for μB/T ≤ 2.0
- The Taylor series for the variance of net baryon-number fluctuations (second order cumulant) is reliable for μB/T ≤ 1.5
- At the pseudo-critical temperature Tpc ≃ 156.5 MeV, the radius of convergence estimator gives μB/T ≳ 2.9 at vanishing strangeness chemical potential
- The radius of convergence estimate ranges from μB/T ≳ 2.2 at T = 135 MeV to μB/T ≳ 3.2 at T = 165 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by the HotQCD Collaboration (Bazavov et al., 2020; Bollweg et al., 2021) using significantly higher statistics datasets, with more than a factor 10 higher statistics on Nτ=8 lattices and a factor 20 higher statistics on Nτ=12 lattices than used in earlier studies. The results enable improved determinations of the QCD equation of state at non-vanishing chemical potentials and provide constraints on the location of the QCD critical point through the estimated radius of convergence of the Taylor series.
