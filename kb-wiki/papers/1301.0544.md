# Finite temperature effects on anisotropic pressure and equation of state of dense neutron matter in an ultrastrong magnetic field

> Spin polarized states in dense neutron matter with recently developed Skyrme effective interaction (BSk20 parametrization) are considered in the magnetic fields $H$ up to $10^{20}$ G at finite temperature. In a strong magnetic field, the total pressure in neutron matter is anisotropic, and the difference between the pressures parallel and perpendicular to the field direction becomes significant at $H>H_{th}\sim10^{18}$ G. The longitudinal pressure decreases with the magnetic field and vanishes in the critical field $10^{18}<H_c\lesssim10^{19}$ G, resulting in the longitudinal instability of neutron matter. With increasing the temperature, the threshold $H_{th}$ and critical $H_c$ magnetic fields also increase. The appearance of the longitudinal instability prevents the formation of a fully spin polarized state in neutron matter and only the states with moderate spin polarization are accessible. The anisotropic equation of state is determined at densities and temperatures relevant for the interiors of magnetars. The entropy of strongly magnetized neutron matter turns out to be larger than the entropy of the nonpolarized matter. This is caused by some specific details in the dependence of the entropy on the effective masses of neutrons with spin up and spin down in a polarized state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in ultrastrong magnetic fields (above ~10^18 G), the longitudinal pressure in dense neutron matter vanishes at a critical field H_c between 10^18 and 10^19 G, causing a longitudinal instability that prevents the formation of a fully spin polarized state; only states with moderate spin polarization remain accessible, and this behavior persists at finite temperatures up to about 30 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent calculations within a Fermi liquid formalism using the generalized Skyrme effective interaction BSk20 parametrization, solving for the normal distribution function of neutrons and the single-particle energies under an external magnetic field. They computed the longitudinal and transverse pressures from the stress tensor, accounting for the Maxwell term that breaks rotational symmetry, and determined the spin polarization parameter, entropy, and free energy as functions of density, temperature, and magnetic field strength up to 10^20 G.

## Key numbers

- The threshold magnetic field for significant pressure anisotropy is H_th ~ 10^18 G
- The critical magnetic field for longitudinal instability lies in the range 10^18 < H_c ≲ 10^19 G
- The nuclear saturation density for BSk20 is ρ_0 = 0.1596 fm^-3
- At H=0, spontaneous spin polarization does not appear below a critical temperature T_c > 35 MeV; at T=37 MeV and T=40 MeV spontaneous polarization exists in a finite density interval
- At T=30 MeV and density ρ=3ρ_0, the spin polarization parameter as a function of magnetic field is shown up to H ~ 10^19 G, with the maximum attainable polarization indicated by vertical arrows
- The neutron magnetic moment used is μ_n = -1.9130427(5) μ_N

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron matter in strong magnetic fields by Isayev and collaborators (Isayev 2006, Isayev and Yang 2010) that considered spin polarization without pressure anisotropy, and by Ferrer et al. (2010) and Khalilov (2002) who noted the anisotropy effect but disregarded interparticle interactions. The paper contrasts with the approach of Broderick et al. (2000), who used a variational method with the Argonne V_18 potential and found fully spin polarized states could form at H ≳ 10^19 G without considering pressure anisotropy. The results enable more realistic modeling of magnetar interiors, particularly for understanding the maximum achievable spin polarization and the stability conditions that affect neutrino transport and thermal evolution of neutron stars.
