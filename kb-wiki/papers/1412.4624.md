# Low-energy excitations and quasielastic contribution to electron-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus scattering in the continuum random phase approximation

> We present a detailed study of a continuum random phase approximation approach to quasielastic electron-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus scattering. The formalism is validated by confronting ($e,e'$) cross-section predictions with electron scattering data for the nuclear targets $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca, in the kinematic region where quasielastic scattering is expected to dominate. We examine the longitudinal and transverse contributions to $^{12}$C($e,e'$) and compare them with the available data. Further, we study the $^{12}$C($ν_μ,μ^{-}$) cross sections relevant for accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation experiments. We pay special attention to low-energy excitations which can account for non-negligible contributions in measurements, and require a beyond-Fermi-gas formalism.

## Key claim

The continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) approach provides an overall satisfactory description of quasielastic electron-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus scattering data across a broad kinematic range, but the model is restricted to one-particle one-hole excitations and requires a phenomenological folding procedure with a width of 3 MeV to account for the spreading width of particle states that is otherwise neglected.

## Method

The authors solve Hartree-Fock equations with a Skyrme (SkE2) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to obtain mean-field single-particle wave functions, then implement long-range correlations through a continuum random phase approximation solved via a Green's function formalism in coordinate space. The nuclear response functions are computed for inclusive quasielastic electron scattering on 12C, 16O, and 40Ca, and for charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering on 12C, with relativistic corrections implemented through a kinematic substitution and lepton wave function distortion treated via an effective momentum approximation.

## Key numbers

- The folding procedure uses an effective width of Gamma = 3 MeV to spread strength in the giant-resonance region
- A dipole hadronic form factor with cutoff parameter Lambda = 455 MeV is introduced at nucleon-nucleon interaction vertices, optimized via chi-squared test against electron scattering data
- For neutrino energies as low as 200 MeV, multipoles up to J = 4 contribute to the cross section; for energies as high as 2 GeV, multipoles up to J = 16 need to be considered
- The axial form factor uses M_A = 1.03 +/- 0.02 GeV in standard dipole parametrization
- Electron scattering comparisons cover momentum transfers 95 less than q less than 1050 MeV/c and Q^2 from 0.009 to 0.900 (GeV/c)^2
- The HF and CRPA cross sections become identical for Q^2 greater than approximately 0.25 (GeV/c)^2
- The cross section drops by two orders of magnitude when shifting scattering angle from 36 degrees to 145 degrees at fixed incoming energy of 160 MeV

## Context

This work extends the CRPA formalism previously applied to exclusive photo-induced and electron-induced quasielastic processes (Jan 1988, Jan 1989) and inclusive neutrino scattering at supernova energies (Jachowicz 1999, 2002, 2004, 2006) to the kinematic regime of accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE and T2K. The approach improves upon the relativistic Fermi-gas model used in Monte Carlo event generators, which describes quasielastic scattering adequately at momentum transfers around 500 MeV/c but performs poorly at smaller momentum transfers where nuclear effects are more prominent. The results enable more accurate modeling of low-energy nuclear excitations in neutrino-nucleus scattering, which are non-negligible in oscillation experiments and require a beyond-Fermi-gas formalism.
