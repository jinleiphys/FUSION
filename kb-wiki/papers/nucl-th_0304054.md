# Electromagnetic dissociation of 8B and the astrophysical S factor for 7Be(p,gamma)8B

> We present S factor data obtained from the Coulomb dissociation of 83 MeV/nucleon 8B, and analyze 7Be longitudinal momentum distributions measured at 44 and 81 MeV/nucleon using a potential model, first-order perturbation theory, and dynamical solution of the time-dependent Schroedinger equation. Comparing our results with independent continuum-discretized coupled channels calculations, we study the reaction model and beam energy dependence of the E2 contribution to the dissociation cross section. By fitting radiative capture and Coulomb breakup data taken below relative energies of 400 keV with potential models constrained by 7Li + n and 7Be + p elastic scattering data, we examine the mutual consistency of recent S17 measurements and obtain a recommended value for S17(0) of 18.6 +/- 0.4 (experimental) +/- 1.1 (extrapolation) eV b (1 sigma). This result is in good agreement with recent experimental determinations of the asymptotic normalization coefficient of the valence proton wave function in 8B.

## Key claim

The paper recommends a value for S17(0) of 18.6 ± 0.4 (experimental) ± 1.1 (extrapolation) eV b (1 sigma) by fitting radiative capture and Coulomb breakup data below relative energies of 400 keV with potential models constrained by elastic scattering data, but this recommendation excludes the measurement of Junghans et al. which was found to be discrepant with the other six mutually consistent measurements.

## Method

The authors analyzed 7Be longitudinal momentum distributions from Coulomb dissociation of 8B at 44 and 81 MeV/nucleon using first-order perturbation theory and dynamical solution of the time-dependent Schroedinger equation, scaling E2 matrix elements to fit the measured asymmetries. They then extracted astrophysical S factors from an exclusive measurement of the Coulomb dissociation of 83 MeV/nucleon 8B on a Pb target, correcting for E2 contributions using the Esbensen and Bertsch structure model calibrated against the longitudinal momentum distributions. Finally, they fitted radiative capture and Coulomb breakup data below 400 keV relative energy using potential models with parameters fixed by 7Li + n and 7Be + p elastic scattering data to extrapolate to zero energy.

## Key numbers

- Recommended S17(0) = 18.6 ± 0.4 (experimental) ± 1.1 (extrapolation) eV b (1 sigma) from six mutually consistent measurements
- Best-fit E2 scaling factor for 44 MeV/nucleon data: 1.01 ± 0.21 (perturbative) and 1.6 ± 0.2 (dynamical)
- Best-fit E2 scaling factor for 81 MeV/nucleon data: 0.63 ± 0.12 (perturbative) and 1.2 ± 0.2 (dynamical)
- S17(0) from 83 MeV/nucleon Coulomb breakup data alone: 16.6 ± 1.1 (experimental) ± 0.9 (extrapolation) eV b
- S17(0) from Filippone et al. data: 19.1(8) eV b (7Li+n parameters) and 19.8(8) eV b (7Be+p parameters)
- S17(0) from Junghans et al. data: 21.5(3) eV b (7Li+n parameters) and 22.2(3) eV b (7Be+p parameters)
- Ratio SE2/SE1(0.6 MeV) = 5.9×10^-4 from the present potential model, compared with 9.5×10^-4 from the Esbensen and Bertsch model
- Measured cross section at Erel = 0.192 MeV: 50.4 ± 4.9 mb/MeV, yielding S17 = 15.2 ± 1.5 eV b

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of 8B Coulomb dissociation by Davids et al. (2001) and addresses the controversy over E2 contributions that began with the first measurement by Motobayashi et al. (1994). The authors compare their dynamical calculations with continuum-discretized coupled channels calculations by Mortimer et al. (2002), finding consistent predictions for higher-order effects. The recommended S17(0) value is consistent with asymptotic normalization coefficient determinations by Azhari et al. (2001), Trache et al. (2001), and Trache et al. (2003), and the paper identifies the need for more precise measurements of 7Be + p scattering lengths to reduce extrapolation uncertainties.
