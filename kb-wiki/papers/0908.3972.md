# New Approach for Evaluating Incomplete and Complete Fusion Cross Sections with Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels Method

> We propose a new method for evaluating incomplete and complete fusion cross sections separately using the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels method. This method is applied to analysis of the deuteron induced reaction on a 7Li target up to 50 MeV of the deuteron incident energy. Effects of deuteron breakup on this reaction are explicitly taken into account. Results of the method are compared with those of the Glauber model, and the difference between the two is discussed. It is found that the energy dependence of the incomplete fusion cross sections obtained by the present calculation is almost the same as that obtained by the Glauber model, while for the complete fusion cross section, the two models give markedly different energy dependence. We show also that a prescription for evaluating incomplete fusion cross sections proposed in a previous study gives much smaller result than an experimental value.

## Key claim
The authors propose a new method using the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) method to separately calculate incomplete and complete fusion cross sections, based on a physical condition on the absorption of each projectile constituent by the target. The method contains a free absorption radius parameter, which is determined by tuning to the Glauber model result at 40 MeV, and the authors find that the Glauber model works well for incomplete fusion down to 10 MeV but fails for complete fusion even at 50 MeV.

## Method
The three-body (p, n, 7Li) Schrödinger equation is solved with CDCC, expanding the wave function in eigenfunctions of the p-n system including the deuteron ground state and discretized continuum states. The total fusion cross section is obtained from the expectation value of the imaginary parts of the nucleon-7Li optical potentials. To separate complete and incomplete fusion, the integration over the coordinates of the proton and neutron relative to the target is divided into regions where each nucleon is inside or outside an absorption radius \(r_c^{\rm ab}\), which is a free parameter set to 4.0 fm by matching the calculated \((d,n)\) incomplete fusion cross section to the Glauber model result at 40 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The absorption radius \(r_c^{\rm ab}\) is determined to be 4.0 fm, used for both proton and neutron at all energies from 10 to 50 MeV.
- At \(E_d^{\rm L}=10\) MeV, the \((d,p)\) incomplete fusion cross section \(\sigma_{\rm IF}^{(p)}\) is three times as large as the \((d,n)\) incomplete fusion cross section \(\sigma_{\rm IF}^{(n)}\).
- The incomplete fusion cross sections calculated with \(r_c^{\rm ab}=4\) fm and 5 fm differ from each other by about 30%.
- The deuteron ground state energy used in the calculation is \(\varepsilon_0 = -2.23\) MeV.
- The calculation covers deuteron incident energies from 10 MeV to 50 MeV in the laboratory system.

## Context
This work extends the CDCC approach to fusion reactions, which was previously applied to study breakup effects on fusion by Diaz-Torres et al. (2007) and Iijima et al., but those earlier attempts used an unphysical separation of complete and incomplete fusion that neglected contributions from the elastic channel to incomplete fusion and from breakup channels to complete fusion. The new method enables reliable evaluation of incomplete fusion cross sections for the \(^7\)Li\((d,n)\) reaction, which is important for the International Fusion Materials Irradiation Facility (IFMIF) and for accelerator-based applications such as Boron Neutron Capture Therapy (BNCT). The results show that the Glauber model, which was shown by Ye et al. (2009) to reproduce experimental double differential cross sections at 40 MeV, remains valid for incomplete fusion down to 10 MeV, while a fully quantum mechanical CDCC treatment is necessary for complete fusion.
