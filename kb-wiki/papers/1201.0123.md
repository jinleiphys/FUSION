# Cross section and single transverse target spin asymmetry for backward pion electroproduction

> Nucleon to meson transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), non-diagonal matrix elements of nonlocal three quark operators between a nucleon and a meson states, arise within the collinear factorized description of hard exclusive electroproduction of mesons off nucleons in the backward direction. Below we address the problem of modelling pion to nucleon TDAs. We suggest a factorized Ansatz for quadruple distributions with input from the soft pion theorem for pi N TDAs. In order to satisfy the polynomiality property in its full form the spectral representation is complemented with a D-term like contribution from the nucleon exchange in the u-channel of the reaction. We present our estimates for the backward pion electroproduction unpolarized cross section and its transverse target single spin asymmetry within our composite model for pi N TDAs.

## Key claim

The authors present a model for pion-to-nucleon transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs) that satisfies polynomiality and support properties, enabling estimates of the unpolarized cross section and single transverse target spin asymmetry for backward pion electroproduction; however, the results depend on a factorized Ansatz for quadruple distributions with input from the soft pion theorem and a D-term from nucleon exchange, meaning the predictions are model-dependent and await experimental verification.

## Method

The authors construct a factorized Ansatz for quadruple distributions describing πN TDAs, using the soft pion theorem at ξ=1 to relate TDAs to nucleon distribution amplitudes (DAs), and complement the spectral representation with a D-term from u-channel nucleon exchange to satisfy polynomiality. They then compute the leading-order (in α_s and 1/Q) hard scattering amplitude for backward pion electroproduction by convolving these πN TDAs with nucleon DAs, evaluating 21 diagrams for the γ* p → π^0 p and γ* p → π^+ n channels. The unpolarized transverse cross section and single transverse target spin asymmetry (STSA) are derived from the resulting helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV
- Cross section estimates are presented for Q² = 10 GeV² and u = -0.5 GeV² in units of nb/sr
- The unpolarized cross section scales as 1/Q⁸
- The normalization constant C includes (4π α_s)² √(4π α_em) f_N² / (54 f_π)
- STSA is computed for Q² = 10 GeV² and u = -0.5 GeV² and found to be sizable in the valence region

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization framework for hard exclusive meson electroproduction from the forward (GPD) regime to the backward kinematics regime, following the conjecture of Frankfurt (1999) and the detailed framework of Lansberg (2007). It builds on prior modeling of πN TDAs within the light cone quark model by Pasquini (2009) and the spectral representation approach of Pire (2010, 2011). The results enable experimental tests at high-luminosity facilities such as JLab at 12 GeV and the EIC, where data on backward π⁺, π⁰, η, and ω production from JLab at 6 GeV are currently being analyzed.
