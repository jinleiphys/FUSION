# Kaon photo-production on the nucleon and deuteron

> Isobaric models for the photo-production of K$^+$ are discussed and their predictions are shown in the K$^0$ photo-production. The models are further used in spectator model calculations of the K$^0$ photo-production on deuteron. Considerable dependence of the inclusive cross section on the elementary amplitude was found.

# Kaon photo-production on the nucleon and deuteron

## Key claim
Isobaric models that fit the p(γ,K⁺)Λ data equally well still produce significantly different predictions for K⁰ photo-production on the neutron and deuteron, particularly at forward kaon angles, meaning that upcoming Tohoku deuteron data can discriminate between elementary amplitudes that are otherwise indistinguishable in the charged-kaon channel.

## Method
The authors used several isobaric models (SLA, K-MAID, M2, H2) that include Born terms and resonance exchanges with hadronic form factors, fitted to CLAS and SAPHIR data for p(γ,K⁺)Λ. They then applied these models to the n(γ,K⁰)Λ channel using SU(2) isospin relations, with the K₁ meson electromagnetic coupling ratio r_KK1 as a free parameter. For the deuteron target, they performed spectator-model calculations in the impulse approximation using non-relativistic Bonn wave functions, integrating over the spectator proton momentum to obtain inclusive d(γ,K⁰)Λp cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of neutral to charged K* decay widths is fixed at r_KK* = −1.53 from the quark model prediction.
- In K-MAID, the K₁ ratio was fitted to K⁰Σ⁺ data as r_KK1 = −0.45.
- For the SLA model, two values of r_KK1 were found that give similar forward-angle results: −1.6 (SLA1) and −3.4 (SLA2).
- Models with hadronic form factors (K-MAID, M2, H2) give much smaller cross sections at E_γ^lab > 1.5 GeV for forward kaon angles compared to models without them.
- The bump structure in n(γ,K⁰)Λ at 1.1 GeV photon energy is produced by N*(1720) exchange.
- The K₁ exchange contribution is sensitive in K-MAID and SLA up to 1.5 GeV and kaon lab angles up to 50 degrees, but less so in M2 and H2.
- Calculations for d(γ,K⁰)Λp were performed at photon lab energy of 1 GeV and kaon lab angle of 10 degrees.

## Context
This work extends the isobaric model approach previously applied to p(γ,K⁺)Λ (Adelseck-Saghai 1990, William-Ji-Cotanch 1992, Saclay-Lyon, Kaon-MAID 1999, Janssen et al. 2001) into the poorly measured K⁰ channel, using the deuteron as a neutron target. The authors note that while coupled-channel approaches (Penner, Chiang et al. 2001) include πN rescattering, the simpler isobaric models neglect final-state interactions, though ΛN FSI was shown by Li and Wright (1991) to be less significant for inclusive than exclusive deuteron reactions. The results enable the Tohoku experiment (Tsu 2004) to distinguish between elementary amplitudes, which will reduce theoretical uncertainty in hypernuclear photo-production calculations (Motoba 2003).
