# From Kadanoff--Baym to Boltzmann equations for massive spin-1/2 fermions

> We derive Boltzmann equations for massive spin-1/2 fermions with local and nonlocal collision terms from the Kadanoff--Baym equation in the Schwinger--Keldysh formalism, properly accounting for the spin degrees of freedom. The Boltzmann equations are expressed in terms of matrix-valued spin distribution functions, which are the building blocks for the quasi-classical parts of the Wigner functions. Nonlocal collision terms appear at next-to-leading order in $\hbar$ and are sources for the polarization part of the matrix-valued spin distribution functions. The Boltzmann equations for the matrix-valued spin distribution functions pave the way for simulating spin-transport processes involving spin-vorticity couplings from first principles.

## Key claim

The authors derive Boltzmann equations for massive spin-1/2 fermions from the Kadanoff-Baym equation in the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, including both local and nonlocal collision terms up to next-to-leading order in $\hbar$, under the assumption that the interaction range is much smaller than all other scales (effectively reducing to a four-fermion vertex) and working to lowest diagrammatic order in the $T$-matrix approximation.

## Method

Starting from the Dyson-Schwinger equation on the closed-time-path contour, the authors derive the Kadanoff-Baym equation for the Wigner function in the quasiparticle approximation, neglecting principal-value parts of retarded and advanced propagators. They perform a Clifford decomposition of the Wigner function into scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, and tensor components, then expand the Kadanoff-Baym equation in powers of $\hbar$ to obtain coupled equations of motion. The Boltzmann equations for matrix-valued spin distribution functions are obtained by taking traces with appropriate Clifford generators and separating on-shell and off-shell contributions, showing that off-shell terms cancel to lowest order in the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- The expansion in $\hbar$ is carried out to next-to-leading order, with the collision term containing contributions at order $\mathcal{O}(\hbar)$ (leading) and $\mathcal{O}(\hbar^2)$ (next-to-leading).
- The two-loop self-energy diagram in the $T$-matrix approximation is of order $\mathcal{O}(G_c^2)$ in the coupling constant of the NJL Lagrangian and of order $\mathcal{O}(\hbar)$.
- The four-fermion coupling constant $G_c$ has dimension [energy $\times$ length$^3$].
- The fermion fields $\psi, \overline{\psi}$ have dimension [length]${}^{-3/2}$, while the boson field $\phi$ has dimension [energy $\times$ length${}^3$]${}^{-1/2}$.
- The Yukawa coupling $g$ has dimension [energy $\times$ length]${}^{3/2}$.

## Context

This work extends previous kinetic theory approaches for massive spin-1/2 fermions based on the Wigner-function formalism (Gao 2012, Weickgenannt 2019) by systematically including collision terms derived from quantum field theory. It builds upon the authors' prior work (Weickgenannt 2020, 2021) where the collision term to order $\mathcal{O}(\hbar)$ was derived using a different method, and addresses the missing piece in the wave-packet model of Zhang (2019) where back reaction converting spin into vorticity was not considered. The resulting Boltzmann equations for matrix-valued spin distribution functions enable first-principles simulations of spin transport processes with spin-vorticity couplings, going beyond statistical models (Becattini 2007, 2013) and spin-hydrodynamical models (Florkowski 2017, Montenegro 2017) that do not include particle collisions.
