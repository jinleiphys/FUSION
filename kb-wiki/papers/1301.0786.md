# System Size and Energy Dependence of Dilepton Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions at SIS Energies

> We study the dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at energies of 1-2 AGeV as well as in proton induced pp, pn, pd and p+A reactions from 1 GeV up to 3.5 GeV. For the analysis we employ three different transport models - the microscopic off-shell Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport approach, the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) approach as well as the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) approach. We confirm the experimentally observed enhancement of the dilepton yield (normalized to the multiplicity of neutral pions $N_{π^0}$) in heavy-ion collisions with respect to that measured in $NN = (pp+pn)/2$ collisions. We identify two contributions to this enhancement: a) the $pN$ bremsstrahlung which scales with the number of collisions and not with the number of participants, i.e. pions; b) the dilepton emission from intermediate $Δ$'s which are part of the reaction cycles $Δ\to πN ; πN \to Δ$ and $NN\to NΔ; NΔ\to NN$. With increasing system size more generations of intermediate $Δ$'s are created. If such $Δ$ decays into a pion, the pion can be reabsorbed, however, if it decays into a dilepton, the dilepton escapes from the system. Thus, experimentally one observes only one pion (from the last produced $Δ$) whereas the dilepton yield accumulates the contributions from all $Δ$'s of the cycle. We show as well that the Fermi motion enhances the production of pions and dileptons in the same way. Furthermore, employing the off-shell HSD approach, we explore the influence of in-medium effects like the modification of self-energies and spectral functions of the vector mesons due to their interactions with the hadronic environment.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed enhancement of dilepton yield in heavy-ion collisions relative to elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions is explained by two specific mechanisms: (a) pN bremsstrahlung scaling with the number of collisions rather than participants, and (b) dilepton emission from intermediate Delta resonances that undergo multiple generations in larger systems, where each Delta can emit a dilepton that escapes while only the final pion is observed.

## Method

The authors employed three transport models, the off-shell Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) approach, the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) approach, and the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) approach, to simulate dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at 1-2 AGeV and in proton-induced reactions from 1 GeV to 3.5 GeV. The HSD model was updated with improved parametrizations for eta and vector meson production cross sections, explicit isospin separation for vector meson production, and a corrected isospin decomposition for Delta production in the FRITIOF string model. Dilepton sources included Dalitz decays of pi0, eta, omega, and Delta resonances, direct decays of rho, omega, and phi mesons, and pN bremsstrahlung based on One-Boson-Exchange calculations by Kaptari and Kampfer (2005).

## Key numbers

- The HADES collaboration found that in Ar+KCl collisions at 1.75 AGeV, the dilepton invariant mass yield between 0.2 and 0.6 GeV is about 2-3 times larger than expected from a superposition of pp and pn collisions (Agakishiev 2011).
- The collisional broadening coefficients used were approximately 150 MeV for the rho meson and approximately 70 MeV for the omega meson, as obtained in Bratkovskaya (2007).
- The HSD model uses a threshold of sqrt(s_th) approximately 2.6 GeV for inelastic baryon-baryon collisions described by the FRITIOF string model, and sqrt(s_th) approximately 2.3 GeV for meson-baryon collisions.
- For the exclusive channel pp to NDelta, the HSD model assumes 3/4 of produced Deltas are in the Delta++ state and 1/4 in the dilepton-producing Delta+ state.

## Context

This work extends previous transport studies that addressed the "DLS puzzle", a factor-of-five discrepancy between DLS data for C+C and Ca+Ca collisions and earlier model predictions (Wolf 1992, Bratkovskaya 1996, Xiong 1990). The authors build on the suggestion in Bratkovskaya (2007) that stronger pn and pp bremsstrahlung contributions, in line with updated OBE model calculations from Kaptari and Kampfer (2005), could resolve the puzzle. The study enables quantitative predictions for Au+Au collisions at 1.25 AGeV currently being analyzed, and provides a framework for distinguishing in-medium effects from trivial system-size scaling in dilepton production at SIS energies.
