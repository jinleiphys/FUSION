# Hybrid neutron stars within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and confinement

> Recently, it has been shown that the standard Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is not able to reproduce the correct QCD behavior of the gap equation at large density, and therefore a different cutoff procedure at large momenta has ben proposed. We found that, even with this density dependent cutoff procedure, the pure quark phase in neutron stars (NS) interiors is unstable, and we argue that this could be related to the lack of confinement in the original NJL model.

# Hybrid neutron stars within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and confinement

## Key claim

Even after implementing a density-dependent cutoff procedure designed to fix the NJL model's incorrect QCD behavior at large density, the pure quark phase in neutron star interiors remains unstable; this instability may be caused by the absence of confinement in the original NJL model.

## Method

The authors modified the standard NJL model by introducing a chemical-potential-dependent momentum cutoff Λ(μ) and a corresponding μ-dependent coupling constant g(μ), using smooth monotonically increasing functions of μ with different slopes. They assumed a first-order phase transition and combined a nucleonic equation of state from the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone approach for the hadronic phase with the modified NJL model for the quark phase, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The effective bag constant from the NJL model at chiral symmetry restoration is B_NJL ≈ 140 MeV/fm³.
- Neutron star gravitational masses are plotted in units of solar mass M⊙ = 2×10³³ g as functions of radius and central density for different choices of the density-dependent cutoff.
- All mass-central density curves show a cusp at the maximum mass, indicating the instability of the pure quark phase.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Casalbuoni (2005) showing that the standard NJL model fails to reproduce the QCD gap equation at large density, and of Baldo (2007) who proposed the density-dependent cutoff procedure. The results contradict the possibility of stable pure quark matter in neutron star interiors under the NJL framework, and suggest that adding a confining potential switched off at the chiral phase transition, as proposed by Thomas (1984), could remove the instability by reducing the effective bag constant.
