# $ρ$ - nucleus bound states in Walecka model

> Possible formation of $ρ$ nucleus bound state is studied in the framework of Walecka model. The bound states are found in different nuclei ranging from $^3He$ to $^{208}Pb$. These bound states may have a direct bearing on the recent experiments on the photoproduction of $ρ$ meson in the nuclear medium.

# ρ-nucleus bound states in Walecka model

## Key claim
The formation of ρ⁰-nucleus bound states is possible across a wide range of nuclei from ³He to ²⁰⁸Pb within the Walecka mean-field model, but the binding energy depends strongly on the choice of the tensor coupling constant f_ρ; the strongest binding occurs when both vector and tensor couplings are included, while omitting the tensor coupling yields significantly less binding.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled mean-field equations of the Walecka model self-consistently to obtain nuclear density distributions, then evaluated the ρ⁰ meson mass as the pole of its propagator as a function of density and radius. The resulting position-dependent effective mass m*_ρ⁰(r) was used in a Klein-Gordon-like equation with a complex mass term (including a phenomenological in-medium width parameter γ_ρ) to find bound-state energies and widths via a relaxation method in coordinate space. Two parameter sets were used: PI with vector coupling g_ρ = 8.912 and tensor coupling f_ρ = 2.866, and PII with the same g_ρ but f_ρ = 0.

## Key numbers
- In ³He with parameter set PI (tensor coupling included), the l=0 bound state energy E = -199.14 MeV with width Γ = 133.87 MeV (γ_ρ = 0).
- In ³He with parameter set PII (no tensor coupling), the l=0 bound state energy E = -132.95 MeV with width Γ = 137.50 MeV (γ_ρ = 0).
- In ²⁰⁸Pb with parameter set PI, the l=0 bound state energy E = -266.14 MeV with width Γ = 147.95 MeV (γ_ρ = 0).
- In ²⁰⁸Pb with parameter set PII, the l=0 bound state energy E = -191.59 MeV with width Γ = 148.44 MeV (γ_ρ = 0).
- The average ρ⁰ mass in ³He for parameter set PI is 657 MeV without tensor interaction and 600 MeV with tensor interaction (free ρ mass is 770 MeV/c²).
- Experimental values cited for m*_ρ⁰ from photoproduction: 642±40 MeV/c² (photon energies 800-880 MeV) and 669±32 MeV/c² (880-960 MeV), and 490±40 MeV/c² (380-700 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Papandreou et al. (1999), who showed that ρ⁰-nucleus bound states could explain the observed reduction in ρ⁰ meson mass in nuclear medium, using a potential depth consistent with Dirac phenomenology and Brown-Rho scaling. The present calculation uses the Walecka model, a standard mean-field approach for nuclear matter, and finds binding energies comparable to those of Papandreou et al. (1999) when tensor coupling is omitted, but significantly stronger binding when tensor coupling is included. The results suggest that the tensor coupling must be carefully constrained, possibly by introducing form factors, to avoid overbinding; this enables future work to refine the in-medium ρ meson potential and connect to experiments on ρ photoproduction below the free production threshold.
