# Fragmentation of the Giant Pairing Vibration in 14C induced by many-body processes

> We present a theoretical framework for treating the full excitation spectrum of Jπ = 0+ pair addition modes, including the well-known low-lying and bound Pairing Vibration on par with the predicted Giant Pairing Vibration lying in the continuum. Our formalism includes the coupling to low-energy collective quadrupole modes of the core, in such a way that both single-particle self-energy effects and the pairing interaction induced by phonon exchange are accounted for. The theory is applied to the case of the excitation spectrum of 14C, recently populated by two-neutron transfer reactions.

# Fragmentation of the Giant Pairing Vibration in 14C induced by many-body processes

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Giant Pairing Vibration (GPV) in 14C, predicted by standard pp-RPA calculations as a concentrated bump of sd-shell strength near threshold, is substantially fragmented and shifted by particle-vibration coupling (PVC) effects, producing two bound excited 0+ states and a continuum bump with over 50% admixture of quadrupole phonon components. The authors hedge that the continuum structure may be populated by coupled-channel processes rather than direct two-neutron transfer, and that challenging reaction studies are required to assess PVC effects on measured cross sections.

## Method

The authors extended the particle-particle Random Phase Approximation (pp-RPA) by including coupling to the low-energy 2+ quadrupole vibrational mode of the 12C core, constructing a Hamiltonian matrix with four types of basis states: fermion pp, fermion pp plus one phonon, fermion ph plus one phonon, and fermion hh configurations. Single-particle states were computed in a Woods-Saxon mean field with parameters fitted to 13C, including PVC self-energy effects, and the continuum was treated by discretizing in spherical boxes of varying radius (20 to 28 fm) and averaging the resulting strength functions. The monopole pairing interaction used a Gogny force scaled by 0.9, with an additional quadrupole pairing interaction of strength G2 = 0.075 fm^2/MeV included for type-ii configurations.

## Key numbers

- The 2+ phonon energy of the 12C core: hbar omega_2+ = 4.44 MeV, with deformation parameter beta_2 = 0.46
- The calculated ground state energy of 14C: E(0+_1) = -13.5 MeV, compared to the experimental two-neutron separation energy S_2n = 13.1 MeV
- Two bound excited 0+ states found at E ≈ -6.1 MeV (E* ≈ 7 MeV) and E ≈ -3.5 MeV (E* ≈ 9.6 MeV), with pp' ⊗ 2+ admixtures of 37% and 33% respectively
- The ground state wavefunction contains 13% pp' ⊗ 2+ admixture
- The continuum GPV bump is located in the excitation region E* ≈ 16-20 MeV
- The continuum bump has about 60% admixture with pp' ⊗ 2+ configurations
- The bare mean field potential for 13C has depth V0 = 72 MeV, diffusivity a = 0.65 fm, and radius R = 2.27 fm
- Single-particle energies after PVC renormalization: tilde_e_1/2- = -5.0 MeV, tilde_e_1/2+ = -2.0 MeV, tilde_e_5/2+ = -1.4 MeV (experimental: -4.9, -1.9, -1.1 MeV respectively)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of pair addition modes beyond the standard pp-RPA framework of Bes and Broglia (1966) and Brink (2005), which previously predicted Giant Pairing Vibrations at energies around 2 hbar Omega from schematic calculations. The authors build on their previous studies of N=7 isotones (11Be, 10Li) where PVC successfully reproduced parity inversion and spectroscopic factors. The calculation addresses the long-standing difficulty in identifying GPV experimentally, as noted in attempts by Assie and Laskin across different mass regions, and provides a microscopic framework for interpreting recent transfer experiments on 14C and 15C by Cappuzzello (2015) and Bonaccorso. The results enable future coupled-channel reaction calculations for two-neutron transfer cross sections, and suggest that coincidence measurements of ~4 MeV gamma transitions from the 2+ phonon could serve as a signature of the predicted admixtures.
