# Heavy-baryon quark model picture from lattice QCD

> The ground state and excited spectra of baryons containing three identical heavy quarks, $b$ or $c$, have been recently calculated in nonperturbative lattice QCD. The energy of positive and negative parity excitations has been determined with high precision. Lattice results constitute a unique opportunity to learn about the quark-confinement mechanism as well as elucidating our knowledge about the nature of the strong force. We analyze the nonperturbative lattice QCD results by means of heavy-quark static potentials derived using SU(3) lattice QCD. We make use of different numerical techniques for the three-body problem.

## Key claim

The static three-quark potential derived from SU(3) lattice QCD, with parameters from quenched QCD, does **not** reproduce the nonperturbative lattice QCD spectra of triply-bottom and triply-charm baryons, despite the expectation that potential models should work best in the heavy-quark regime. The authors find a systematic lack of strength in either the confining or Coulomb parts of the potential.

## Method

The authors solved the nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation for three identical heavy quarks using three independent numerical techniques: generalized Gaussians variational approach, hyperspherical harmonics, and Faddeev equations. They employed the static three-quark potential in both the Y-shape (string junction) and Delta-shape (sum of two-body) forms, with parameters taken from quenched SU(3) lattice QCD calculations of the static potential. The heavy quark masses were fixed to reproduce the ground state masses of the bbb and ccc systems from nonperturbative lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- The Y-shape and Delta-shape confining potentials give results differing by only 0.15% for triply-heavy baryon masses, confirming their near-equivalence at short distances.
- For the bbb system, a single Delta-shape confining potential fitted to the E2(3/2+)-E1(3/2+) mass difference requires sigma_{3Q}^{Delta} = 0.2076 GeV^2, more than double the lattice QCD value of 0.0925 GeV^2.
- For the bbb system, a single Coulomb potential fitted to the same mass difference requires A_{3Q} = 0.4231, three times the lattice QCD value of 0.1410.
- The bbb ground state mass is 14.372 GeV (from lattice QCD), requiring a bottom quark mass m_b = 4.655 GeV in the model.
- The ccc ground state mass is 4.758 GeV (from lattice QCD), requiring a charm quark mass m_c = 1.269 GeV in the model.
- The root-mean-square radii of triply-heavy baryons are 0.211-0.236 fm for the ground state and 0.299-0.334 fm for the first excited state, much smaller than the 0.7 fm distance where Y-shape and Delta-shape potentials begin to differ.

## Context

This work tests the static three-quark potentials from SU(3) lattice QCD (Takahashi et al., 2002) against the nonperturbative lattice QCD spectra of bbb baryons (Meinel, 2010, 2012) and ccc baryons (Padmanath et al., 2013). The failure of the static potential to reproduce the excited spectra contradicts the expectation that potential models should work well for heavy quarks, as they do for charmonium and bottomonium. The authors suggest that a constituent quark-model description analogous to the Cornell potential for heavy mesons (Quigg, 1979; Eichten, 1980, 2008) may be needed, and report that such work is in progress.
