# Heavy-flavor transport and hadronization in pp collisions

> Recent experimental results on the Lambda_c/D^0 ratio in proton-proton collisions have revealed a significant enhancement compared to expectations based on universal fragmentation fractions/functions across different colliding systems, from e+e- to pp. This unexpected enhancement has sparked speculation about the potential effects of a deconfined medium impacting hadronization, previously considered exclusive to heavy-ion collisions. In this study, we propose a novel approach that assumes the formation of a small, deconfined, and expanding fireball even in pp collisions, where charm quarks can undergo rescattering and hadronization. We make use of the same in-medium hadronization mechanism developed for heavy-ion collisions, which involves local color-neutralization through recombination of charm quarks with nearby opposite color charges from the background fireball. Our model incorporates the presence of diquark excitations in the hot medium, which promotes the formation of charmed baryons. Moreover, the recombination process, involving closely aligned partons from the same fluid cell, effectively transfers the collective flow of the system to the final charmed hadrons. We show that this framework can qualitatively reproduce the observed experimental findings in heavy-flavor particle-yield ratios, $p_T$-spectra and elliptic-flow coefficients. Our results provide new, complementary supporting evidence that the collective phenomena observed in small systems naturally have the same origin as those observed in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the same in-medium hadronization mechanism (local color-neutralization via recombination with light antiquarks or diquarks from the same fluid cell) that operates in heavy-ion collisions also occurs in proton-proton collisions, assuming a small deconfined fireball is formed. The authors show this framework can qualitatively reproduce experimental heavy-flavor data, but note that the shorter fireball lifetime in pp collisions affects only kinematic distributions of final hadrons, not their integrated yields.

## Method

The authors model pp collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV using T_RENTo initial conditions with sub-nucleonic fluctuations, followed by 2+1 relativistic hydrodynamic evolution with MUSIC code. Charm quark pairs are generated with POWHEG-BOX interfaced with PYTHIA, then their stochastic propagation through the fireball is simulated via a relativistic Langevin equation using weak-coupling (HTL) and lattice-QCD transport coefficients. Hadronization occurs at T_H=155 MeV via local recombination of charm quarks with light antiquarks or diquarks from the same fluid cell, forming color-singlet clusters that decay into final charmed hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Average initial entropy density at midrapidity for minimum-bias pp: <dS/deta_s> = 37.59, translating to dN_ch/deta ≈ 5.22 (compared to experimental values 4.63 and 5.74 at 2.76 and 7 TeV respectively)
- For high-multiplicity (0-1% percentile) events: <dS/deta_s> = 187.53
- Average longitudinal proper time for charm hadronization in minimum-bias pp: <tau_H> ≈ 1.95 fm/c (maximum 4.25 fm/c); in high-multiplicity: <tau_H> ≈ 2.92 fm/c (maximum 4.79 fm/c)
- Average radial velocity of fluid cells where hadronization occurs: <u_perp>_pp^mb ≈ 0.33-0.34, <u_perp>_pp^hm ≈ 0.53-0.54, <u_perp>_PbPb^0-10% ≈ 0.65-0.67
- Average eccentricity of fireball: <epsilon_2> ≈ 0.31, independent of event activity
- Charm quark mass set to m_c = 1.3 GeV
- Hadronization temperature T_H = 155 MeV; freezeout temperature for soft hadrons T_FO = 145 MeV
- Shear viscosity eta/s = 0.13; initial proper time tau_0 = 0.4 fm/c
- Proportionality coefficient between entropy and charged multiplicity: K ≈ 7.2

## Context

This work extends the in-medium hadronization approach previously developed for heavy-ion collisions by Beraudo et al. (2022) to pp collisions, addressing the unexpected enhancement of Lambda_c/D0 ratios observed by ALICE (2020, 2021). It contrasts with vacuum fragmentation expectations from e+e- data and with PYTHIA 8 Color-Reconnection models by Christiansen (2015), and differs from the Statistical Hadronization Model by He (2019). The results provide independent evidence that collective phenomena in small systems have the same origin as in heavy-ion collisions, complementing previous inferences from soft hadron observables. The framework enables future extension to p-Pb collisions and bottom-quark hadronization.
