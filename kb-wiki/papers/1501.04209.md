# Parity violation and dynamical relativistic effects in $(\vec{e},e'N)$ reactions

> It is well known that coincidence quasielastic $(\vec{e},e'N)$ reactions are not appropriate to analyze effects linked to parity violation due the presence of the fifth electromagnetic (EM) response $R^{TL'}$. Nevertheless, in this work we develop a fully relativistic approach to be applied to parity-violating (PV) quasielastic $(\vec{e},e'N)$ processes. This is of importance as a preliminary step in the subsequent study of inclusive quasielastic PV $(\vec{e},e')$ reactions. Moreover, our present analysis allows us to disentangle effects associated with the off-shell character of nucleons in nuclei, gauge ambiguities and the role played by the lower components in the nucleon wave functions, i.e., dynamical relativistic effects. This study can help in getting clear information on PV effects. Particular attention is paid to the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation where the explicit expressions for the PV single-nucleon responses are shown for the first time.

## Key claim

Although coincidence quasielastic $(\vec{e},e'N)$ reactions are generally considered unsuitable for studying parity violation because the purely electromagnetic fifth response $R^{TL'}$ also depends on electron helicity, the authors develop a fully relativistic formalism for parity-violating (PV) quasielastic $(\vec{e},e'N)$ processes as a necessary preliminary step toward analyzing inclusive PV $(\vec{e},e')$ reactions, where the fifth EM response does not enter.

## Method

The authors employ a fully relativistic framework within the impulse approximation, using Dirac-Hartree bound nucleon wave functions from a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model and either plane-wave spinors (RPWIA) or distorted waves from relativistic optical potentials (RDWIA) for the ejected nucleon. They compute the six parity-violating response functions and the helicity asymmetry for exclusive $(\vec{e},e'N)$ reactions, comparing two current operator prescriptions (CC1 and CC2) and three gauge choices (Landau, Coulomb, Weyl) to isolate off-shell and gauge ambiguities, and separating contributions from positive-energy ($uu$), negative-energy ($vv$), and interference ($uv$) components of the bound nucleon wave function.

## Key numbers

* Transfer momentum fixed to $q = 500$ MeV/c with energy transfer $\omega = 132$ MeV (near the quasielastic peak) for all results.
* The parameter ${\cal A}_0$ that scales PV effects is defined as $-\dfrac{{\cal A}_0}{2} \equiv \dfrac{2 Q^2}{e^2M_Z^2}\left(\dfrac{g}{4\cos\theta_W}\right)^2$.
* Results are presented versus missing momentum $p_m$ (denoted $p$ in the text) for protons in the $1p_{1/2}$-shell of $^{16}$O.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic analyses of parity-conserving electromagnetic responses in exclusive $(e,e'N)$ reactions (Caballero 1998a, Caballero 1998b, Martinez 2002a, Martinez 2002b) to the parity-violating sector, providing the first explicit expressions for PV single-nucleon responses within the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation. The study is a companion to an inclusive $(\vec{e},e')$ analysis (Gonzalez-Jimenez 2013a) and aims to disentangle off-shell, gauge, and dynamical relativistic uncertainties that also affect inclusive PV observables, thereby enabling more reliable extraction of nucleon weak form factors from future PV electron scattering experiments.
