# The Nucleosynthesis and Reaction Rates of Fluorine 19 ($^{19}F$) in the Sun

> We investigate the abundance of $^{19}F$ in the Sun through the nucleosynthesis scenario. In addition, we calculate the rate equations and reaction rates of the nucleosynthesis of $^{19}F$ at different temperature scale. Other important functions of this nucleosynthesis (nuclear partition function and statistical equilibrium conditions) are also obtained. The resulting stability of $^{19}F$ occurs at nucleus with A = 19 and Mass Excess= -1.4874 MeV. As a result, this will tend to a series of neutron captures and beta-decay until $^{19}F$ is produced. The reaction rate of $^{15}N$ ($α$, $γ$) $^{19}F$ was dominated by the contribution of three low-energy resonances, which enhanced the final $^{19}F$ abundance in the envelope.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the reaction rate of $^{15}N(\alpha,\gamma)^{19}F$ is dominated by three low-energy resonances, which enhance the final $^{19}F$ abundance in the solar envelope; however, the authors note that several recent experimental studies point toward a significantly higher reaction rate than the NACRE rate they adopted, and that the final abundance increase from these resonances is only a few percent because the temperature in thermal pulses is high enough that all $^{15}N$ is transformed into $^{19}F$ regardless.

## Method

The authors used the nucnet tools code to compute nuclear masses, nuclear partition functions, and nuclear statistical equilibria for $^{19}F$ in the Sun, drawing on nuclear data from the Joint Institute for Nuclear Astrophysics (JINA) databases. They calculated reaction rates for the nucleosynthesis chains producing and destroying $^{19}F$ during H-burning and He-burning sequences, adopting specific rates from the literature for key reactions including $^{13}C(\alpha,n)^{16}O$, $^{14}C(\alpha,\gamma)^{18}O$, $^{14}N(\alpha,\gamma)^{18}F$, $^{15}N(\alpha,\gamma)^{19}F$, $^{18}O(\alpha,\gamma)^{22}Ne$, $^{18}O(p,\alpha)^{15}N$, and $^{19}F(\alpha,p)^{22}Ne$, with all other reactions taken from the REACLIB Data Tables.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of $^{19}F$: -1.4874 MeV for the stable nucleus with A = 19 and Z = 9.
- The $^{13}C(\alpha,n)^{16}O$ rate adopted is about 50% lower than the NACRE rate in the temperature range of interest.
- The $^{14}C(\alpha,\gamma)^{18}O$ reaction rate is dominated at higher temperatures by a single strong $4^{+}$ resonance at center-of-mass energy $E_{cm}=0.89$ MeV, with possible contributions from a $3^{-}$ resonance at $E_{cm}=0.176$ MeV ($E_{x}=6.404$ MeV) and a $1^{-}$ subthreshold state at $E_{x}=6.198$ MeV.
- The $^{14}N(\alpha,\gamma)^{18}F$ reaction rate is reduced by about a factor of 3 compared to NACRE based on measurements by Gorres et al. (2000).
- The $^{15}N(p,\alpha)^{14}C$ reaction was investigated at $E_{p}(lab) = 78-810$ keV.
- The $^{18}O(\alpha,\gamma)^{22}Ne$ reaction has low-energy resonances at 470 and 566 keV from direct measurement by Dufour et al. (2003).
- The $^{18}O(p,\alpha)^{15}N$ reaction cross section was measured down to energies of approximately 70 keV.
- The $^{19}F(\alpha,p)^{22}Ne$ reaction has experimental data limited to energies above $E_{\alpha}= 1.3$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of $^{19}F$ nucleosynthesis in the Sun by computing reaction rates and nuclear statistical equilibrium conditions, building on the foundational abundance survey of K giants by Jorissen et al. (1992) and the chemical evolution model of Renda et al. (2004) which treated AGB stars as major $^{19}F$ contributors. The results enable more detailed chemical evolution models incorporating yields from very hot stellar interiors, and the authors note that further theoretical studies are required to explain the stability behavior of $^{19}F$ and to control dynamical calculations of nuclear statistical equilibrium and expansion time.
