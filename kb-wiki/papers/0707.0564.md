# Predictions for the LHC heavy ion programme

> Apparently universal trends have been observed in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions up to RHIC energies. Here, we review these trends and we discuss their agnostic extrapolation to heavy ion collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparently universal trends observed in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions up to RHIC energies may either persist at the LHC, forcing a revision of the dynamical ideas used to explain them, or be violated, providing highly discriminatory constraints on the underlying dynamics; the authors emphasize that none of the model predictions for LHC multiplicities can be reconciled with the assumption that extended longitudinal scaling persists.

## Method

The authors review generic trends in data from AGS, SPS, and RHIC for multiplicity distributions, hadrochemistry, transverse-momentum spectra, and azimuthal anisotropy, and then perform agnostic extrapolations of these trends to LHC energies (Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV). They compare these extrapolations with specific model predictions, including saturation models (pocket formula, EKRT model) and hydrodynamic models, to identify where the trends and models diverge.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, the agnostic extrapolation assuming limiting fragmentation and trapezoidal shape gives dN_PbPb^ch/deta ~ 1100.
- The saturation pocket formula (Armesto 2004) predicts dN_PbPb^ch/deta ~ 1650 for central Pb-Pb at the LHC.
- An alternative saturation model (Kharzeev 2004) predicts dN_PbPb^ch/deta ~ 2200.
- The EKRT model predicts a sqrt(s_NN)^0.38 dependence of charged multiplicity at mid-rapidity.
- The baryon chemical potential at mid-rapidity decreases from mu_B ~ 250 MeV at SPS to mu_B ~ 20-40 MeV at RHIC, and is expected to be mu_B << 10 MeV at LHC.
- The chemical decoupling temperature approaches a limiting value T ~ 160-170 MeV, almost reached at RHIC and expected to persist at the LHC.
- The kinetic freeze-out temperature saturates at T_f.o. ~ 120 MeV at SPS energy, expected to persist to the LHC.
- The average transverse velocity reaches <beta_T> ~ 0.55 at RHIC.
- If longitudinal scaling of v_2 persists, one expects v_2(eta=0) ~ 0.075 for mid-central Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.
- Ideal hydrodynamics extrapolations from RHIC to LHC give v_2(eta=0) ~ 0.06 for the multiplicities shown in figure 1.

## Context

This topical review extends the work of Busza (2004) on universal trends in multiparticle production and the saturation model framework of Armesto (2004) and Kharzeev (2004). It contrasts these with the thermal model fits of Andronic (2005) and the hydrodynamic approaches of Teaney (2001) and Kolb (2003). The paper enables future LHC data to discriminate between the persistence of universal trends (which would challenge current dynamical explanations) and their violation (which would provide new constraints on the dynamics of multiparticle production, collective flow, and hadrochemistry).
