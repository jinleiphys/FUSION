# J/Psi Production at RHIC in a QGP

> In central collisions at RHIC, the initial production of heavy quarks will for the first time yield multiple pairs of c-cbar in each central event. If a region of deconfined quarks and gluons is subsequently formed, a new mechanism for the formation of heavy quarkonium bound states will be activated. This will result from the mobility of heavy quarks in the deconfined region, such that bound states can be formed from a quark and an antiquark which were originally produced in separate incoherent interactions. Our model estimates of this effect predict a dramatic increase in the number of observed J/Psi at RHIC, over that predicted from extrapolation of color-screening or gluon dissociation mechanisms from the lower CERN-SPS energies. The centrality and energy dependence of this effect should be readily observable by the Star and Phenix detectors. Thus the J/Psi abundance at RHIC will continue to provide a signature of QGP formation. However, it is in this environment a more useful probe, since contrary to prior expectations this large predicted J/Psi abundance should be relatively easy to measure.

## Key claim

The authors predict a dramatic increase in observed J/Psi abundance at RHIC over extrapolations from CERN-SPS energies, but this enhancement depends on the condition that a deconfined quark-gluon plasma region is formed and that recombination of charm quarks from separate initial interactions occurs within that region.

## Method

The authors constructed a kinetic model with competing formation and dissociation rates for J/Psi in deconfined matter, using a rate equation where formation proceeds via radiative (gluon) capture from a c-cbar pair in a color octet state and dissociation occurs via collisions with deconfined gluons. The gluon density was taken from an ideal gas in thermal and chemical equilibrium, initial charm quark production was based on pQCD calculations, and a one-dimensional isentropic expansion provided the time-temperature profile. The model varied charm quark momentum distributions from thermal to the initial pQCD production distribution.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 10 charm quark pairs will be present in each central Au-Au collision at 200A GeV energy at RHIC.
- The final J/Psi population from the model is about 0.2 J/Psi per collision for a typical parameter choice.
- The canonical expectation from superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions is about 1% of initial charm (approximately 0.1 J/Psi from 10 charm pairs).
- The screening suppression factor typically used for RHIC is 0.05.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of "anomalous suppression" of J/Psi observed by the NA50 experiment in Pb-Pb collisions at CERN, which was interpreted in terms of quark-gluon plasma formation by Satz and others. Prior extrapolations to RHIC by Vogt and Dinesh predicted virtually all initially-produced J/Psi would be suppressed. The authors distinguish their mechanism from statistical recombination models proposed by Braun-Munzinger and Stachel and by Frankfurt and Strikman, noting that their mechanism requires a space-time region of color deconfinement as a necessary condition. The work enables experimental tests by the Star and Phenix detectors at RHIC, with predicted quadratic dependence on initial charm and increase with centrality providing model-independent signatures.
