# The Momentum Dependence of the $ρ-ω$ Mixing Amplitude in a Hadronic Model

> We calculate the momentum dependence of the $ρ-ω$ mixing amplitude in a purely hadronic model. The basic assumption of the model is that the mixing amplitude is generated by $N{\bar{N}}$ loops and thus driven entirely by the neutron-proton mass difference. The value of the amplitude at the $ω$-meson point is expressed in terms of only the $NNω$ and the $NNρ$ coupling constants. Using values for these couplings constrained by empirical two-nucleon data we obtain a value for the mixing amplitude in agreement with experiment. Extending these results to the spacelike region, we find a $ρ-ω$ contribution to the NN interaction that is strongly suppressed and opposite in sign relative to the conventional contribution obtained from using the constant on-shell value for the mixing amplitude.

# The Momentum Dependence of the ρ-ω Mixing Amplitude in a Hadronic Model

## Key claim
The ρ-ω mixing amplitude, when calculated from N N̅ loops driven by the neutron-proton mass difference, shows a strong momentum dependence such that in the spacelike region relevant to nucleon-nucleon scattering the mixing amplitude has the opposite sign and is strongly suppressed relative to the conventional constant on-shell value. This suggests that the ρ-ω contribution to charge-symmetry-violating (CSV) nucleon-nucleon potentials may be effectively nonexistent, though the authors caution that the impact on specific CSV observables is hard to predict without further study.

## Method
The authors calculate the ρ-ω mixing amplitude from N N̅ loop diagrams using standard Feynman rules, with the mixing driven entirely by the neutron-proton mass difference. The nucleon propagators are those of free protons and neutrons, and the NNρ and NNω vertices use coupling constants constrained by empirical two-nucleon data from the Bonn one-boson exchange model. Dimensional regularization is used to isolate divergences, which cancel in the proton-neutron difference, yielding a finite analytic expression for the mixing amplitude as a function of momentum transfer q². The result is then extended to the spacelike region (q² < 0) to compute the ρ-ω contribution to the NN potential, both with and without the inclusion of on-shell form factors at the external nucleon legs.

## Key numbers
- The calculated on-shell ρ-ω mixing amplitude at q² = m_ω² is −4314 MeV², compared to the experimental value of (−4520 ± 600) MeV².
- The ρ meson mass is 770 MeV, the ω meson mass is 783 MeV; the ρ coupling constant g²_ρ/4π = 0.41 with tensor-to-vector ratio C_ρ = 6.1, and the ω coupling constant g²_ω/4π = 10.6 with C_ω = 0.0.
- The cutoff parameters for on-shell form factors are Λ_ρ = 1400 MeV and Λ_ω = 1500 MeV.
- A node in the ρ-ω contribution to the NN potential occurs at a nucleon-nucleon separation of r ∼ 0.9 fm.

## Context
This work extends the study of ρ-ω mixing in charge symmetry violation beyond the conventional assumption of a constant on-shell mixing amplitude, which had been used in earlier treatments by Henley and Miller (1979) and Coon and Scadron (1975, 1977). The authors compare their results to a quark-based calculation by Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (1991), who used q q̅ loops driven by the u-d quark mass difference and also found significant momentum dependence. The present hadronic model agrees with that work's main conclusion that the momentum dependence severely suppresses the ρ-ω contribution to the CSV potential. The findings raise questions about the explanation of the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and the neutron-proton analyzing power difference ΔA at T_lab = 183 MeV, where ρ-ω mixing had been thought to dominate; the authors note that additional CSV mechanisms may need to be found, and that the momentum dependence of π-η mixing must also be studied.
