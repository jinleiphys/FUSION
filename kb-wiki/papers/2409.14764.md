# Effect of isoscalar and isovector scalar fields on baryon semileptonic decays in nuclear matter

> The precise determination of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix elements is very important, because it could be a clue to new physics beyond Standard Theory. This is particular true of $V_{ud}$, because it is the main contribution to the unitary condition of the CKM matrix elements. The level of accuracy for the test of the unitarity involving the element $V_{ud}$ is now of the order of $10^{-4}$. Because the precise data for $V_{ud}$ is usually extracted from super-allowed nuclear $β$ decay, it is quite significant to investigate the breaking of SU(3) flavor symmetry on the weak vector coupling constant in nuclear matter. The purpose of this paper is to investigate how the isoscalar scalar ($σ$) and the isovector scalar ($δ$ or $a_0$) mean-fields affect the weak vector and axial-vector coupling constants for semileptonic baryon (neutron, $Λ$ or $Ξ^-$) decay in asymmetric nuclear matter. To do so, we use the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, where nuclear matter consists of nucleons including quark degrees of freedom bound by the self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons. We pay careful attention to the center of mass correction to the quark currents in matter. We then find that, for neutron $β$ decay in asymmetric nuclear matter, the defect of the vector coupling constant due to the $δ$ field can be of the order of $10^{-4}$ at the nuclear saturation density, which is the same amount as the level of the current uncertainty in the measurements. It is also interesting that, in neutron-rich matter, there exists a certain low density at which isospin symmetry is restored, that is, the $u$-$d$ quark mass difference vanishes. We conclude that the effect of the isoscalar scalar and the isovector scalar fields should be considered in baryon semileptonic decays in nuclei.

## Key claim
The isovector scalar (δ) mean-field in asymmetric nuclear matter can produce a defect in the weak vector coupling constant for neutron β decay of order 10⁻⁴ at nuclear saturation density, which matches the current experimental uncertainty level in CKM unitarity tests, but this result depends on the specific quark-model treatment and the center-of-mass correction applied.

## Method
The authors use the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model with a relativistic scalar-vector harmonic oscillator confinement potential to describe baryons (proton, neutron, Λ, Ξ⁻) in asymmetric nuclear matter. They calculate the weak vector and axial-vector coupling constants for semileptonic decays by including the mean-field effects of σ (isoscalar scalar), ω (isoscalar vector), ρ (isovector vector), and δ (isovector scalar) mesons, with careful treatment of the center-of-mass correction to the quark currents.

## Key numbers
- For neutron β decay in vacuum (case 2), the SU(3) breaking correction to the vector coupling constant δf₁ = 2.08 × 10⁻⁶, and the axial-vector correction δg₁ = 0.082
- For Λ → p decay in vacuum (case 2), δf₁ = 0.009 and δg₁ = 0.062
- For Ξ⁻ → Λ decay in vacuum (case 2), δf₁ = 0.007 and δg₁ = 0.077
- The defect of the vector coupling constant due to the δ field in asymmetric nuclear matter can be of order 10⁻⁴ at nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- The proton charge radius in vacuum is fixed to ⟨r²⟩_p^(1/2) = 0.841 fm, and increases by about 5% at saturation density
- The nuclear matter saturation properties are fitted to binding energy E_b(ρ₀, 0) = -16 MeV, incompressibility K₀ = 240 MeV, and bulk symmetry energy E_sym(ρ₀) = 32.0 MeV

## Context
This work extends the QMC model studies of Guichon et al. (2011) on Fermi decay constant renormalization in nuclear matter, and the Crawford and Miller (2022) study of charge-symmetry-breaking effects on neutron β decay using the nonrelativistic quark model. It also builds on the earlier investigation by Saito (1995) of isospin breaking effects on the Fermi decay constant. The results are relevant for interpreting super-allowed nuclear β decay data used to extract V_ud, and for the proposed J-PARC experiment to measure vector and axial charges in Λ hypernuclei β decay (Kamada et al., 2022). The paper enables future full calculations including pionic cloud and gluon-exchange contributions to SU(3) breaking.
