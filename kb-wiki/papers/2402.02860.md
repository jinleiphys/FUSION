# Constraining kaon PDFs from Drell-Yan and $J/ψ$ production

> The kaon parton distribution functions (PDFs) are poorly known due to paucity of kaon-induced Drell-Yan data. Nevertheless, these Drell-Yan data suggest a softer valence $u$ quark distribution of kaon than that of pion. We discuss the opportunity to constrain kaon PDFs utilizing existing kaon-induced $J/ψ$ production data. We compare the $K^- / π^-$ and $K^+ / π^+$ cross-section ratio data with calculations based on two global-fit parametrizations and two recent theoretical predictions for the kaon and pion PDFs, and test the results with two quarkonium production models. The $K^- / π^-$ cross-section ratio for $J/ψ$ production provides independent evidence of different valence quark distributions in pion and kaon. The $K^+ / π^+$ $J/ψ$ data are found to be sensitive to the gluon distribution in kaon. We show that these $J/ψ$ production data provide valuable constraints for evaluating the adequacy of currently available sets of kaon PDFs.

## Key claim

Existing kaon-induced $J/\psi$ production data can provide independent constraints on kaon parton distribution functions (PDFs), particularly the valence $\bar u$ quark distribution via the $K^-/\pi^-$ ratio and the gluon distribution via the $K^+/\pi^+$ ratio, but the conclusions depend on which quarkonium production model (color evaporation model or nonrelativistic QCD) is used and which set of meson PDFs is adopted.

## Method

The authors compared $K^-/\pi^-$ and $K^+/\pi^+$ cross-section ratios for $J/\psi$ production from the NA3 (150 and 200 GeV on platinum) and WA39 (39.5 GeV on tungsten) experiments with next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculations using two quarkonium production models (color evaporation model and nonrelativistic QCD). They tested four sets of meson PDFs: GRV, JAM, DSE, and MEM, where the GRV and JAM kaon PDFs were constructed using the GRS ansatz relating kaon valence quark distributions to pion valence distributions via a suppression factor $(1-x)^\kappa$, with $\kappa = 0.19 \pm 0.04$ determined from a fit to the NA3 $K^-/\pi^-$ Drell-Yan data.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit value of $\kappa$ (the exponent modifying the valence $\bar u$ distribution in kaon relative to pion) from NLO fit to NA3 $K^-/\pi^-$ Drell-Yan data at 150 GeV is $0.19 \pm 0.04$ at the scale of the Drell-Yan data.
- The NA3 Drell-Yan data cover dimuon masses $4.1 \le M \le 8.5$ GeV on a platinum target at 150 GeV beam momentum.
- The $K^+/\pi^+$ $J/\psi$ ratios at 200 GeV over $0.0 < x_F < 0.7$ are approximately 20% lower than the $K^-/\pi^-$ ratios at 150 GeV.
- The WA39 $J/\psi$ data were taken at 39.5 GeV beam momentum on a tungsten target.
- The normalization factor $F$ for the color evaporation model is set to $0.05$ for both pion and kaon beams.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using pion-induced $J/\psi$ data to constrain pion PDFs (Peng 2017, Chang 2020, Hsieh 2021, Chang 2023) to the kaon sector, where only limited Drell-Yan data from the NA3 collaboration exist. The GRS ansatz (Gluck, Reya, Stratmann) relating kaon and pion valence distributions is tested against $J/\psi$ production data for the first time. The results show that the GRV kaon PDFs, which assume nearly equal gluon distributions in pion and kaon, describe the $J/\psi$ ratio data better than the JAM, DSE, and MEM PDFs, which have smaller gluon distributions at large $x$. This analysis enables future global fits of kaon PDFs incorporating both Drell-Yan and $J/\psi$ data, particularly with anticipated data from the AMBER experiment and from Sullivan-process deep inelastic scattering at the Electron-Ion Collider.
