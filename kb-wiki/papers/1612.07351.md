# Extracting Neutron Polarizabilities from Compton Scattering on The Quasi-Free Neutrons in $γ\text{d}\rightarrowγ\text{n p}$

> Compton scattering processes are ideal to study electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities of nucleons. These fundamental quantities parametrize the responses of the internal degrees of freedoms to a monochromatic photon probe. In this work, the inelastic channel $γd\rightarrow γnp$ is treated in $χ$EFT, with a focus on the kinematic region of the neutron quasi-free peak, where the momentum of the outgoing proton is small enough that it is considered to remain at rest. This provides access to the Compton scattering process $γn \rightarrow γn$, from which the scalar polarizabilites of the neutron, $α_{E1}$ and $β_{M1}$, are extracted. Using $χ$EFT, triple differential cross-sections $d^3σ/dE_ndΩ_{γ'} Ω_n$ in the photon energy range of 200-to-400 MeV are computed. The biggest contribution comes from the impulse approximation, with small corrections stemming from final state interactions, meson exchange currents and rescattering. In this presentation, we report progress and preliminary results.

## Key claim

Using chiral Effective Field Theory (chiEFT) to re-analyze the inelastic Compton scattering reaction gamma d -> gamma n p in the neutron quasi-free peak region provides access to the neutron scalar polarizabilities alpha_E1 and beta_M1, with preliminary results showing that the impulse approximation dominates and that final state interactions and meson exchange current corrections are small, but the analysis remains preliminary and no final extracted values are reported.

## Method

The authors treat the inelastic Compton scattering reaction gamma d -> gamma n p using heavy baryon chiral Effective Field Theory (chiEFT), focusing on the kinematic region of the neutron quasi-free peak where the outgoing proton momentum approaches zero. They compute triple differential cross-sections d^3 sigma / dE_n dOmega_gamma' dOmega_n for incoming photon energies from 200 to 400 MeV at a scattering angle theta_gamma = 136 degrees, including the impulse approximation, final state interactions from leading-order nucleon-nucleon interactions, and meson exchange current corrections, while omitting rescattering diagrams as negligible. The Delta(1232) resonance is included as an explicit degree of freedom with non-zero width, and the neutron polarizabilities are fixed to previously extracted values from elastic Compton scattering on the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- Triple differential cross-sections computed for incoming photon energy range 200-to-400 MeV at scattering angle theta_gamma = 136 degrees
- With all amplitudes included (IA, FSI, MEC), the fit to neutron quasi-free peak data yields chi^2 per degree of freedom of about 1.2
- For the proton quasi-free peak region, the chi^2 per degree of freedom is about 3.4, indicating discrepancy between theory and data
- The model from Levchuk et al. (1994) required a scaling factor of 0.93 to better describe the proton quasi-free peak data

## Context

This work extends the chiEFT treatment of Compton scattering from the proton (McGovern 2013) and from elastic deuteron Compton scattering (Myers 2014) to the inelastic channel gamma d -> gamma n p, aiming to extract neutron polarizabilities more directly by minimizing proton and nuclear binding contributions. The analysis re-examines the quasi-free neutron experiment by Kossert et al. (2003) at MAMI, which used dispersion-relation calculations with model uncertainties now believed to be underestimated (Griesshammer 2012), and aims to provide model-independent results with well-defined theoretical uncertainties from chiEFT. The preliminary results show good agreement with neutron quasi-free data but reveal a discrepancy with proton quasi-free data that was also observed in the earlier model of Levchuk et al. (1994), which the authors plan to investigate further through two-parameter and one-parameter fits with the Baldin sum rule constraint.
