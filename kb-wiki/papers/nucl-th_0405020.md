# Fluctuations from Thermalization at RHIC

> The centrality dependence of dynamic fluctuations of the transverse momentum and the net charge can signal the approach to local thermal equilibrium in nuclear collisions. I explore this signal by comparing transport-theory calculations to STAR and PHENIX data at a range of energies. In particular, I find that this model can describe PHENIX data on the dependence of fluctuations on the transverse momentum range in which they are measured.

# Fluctuations from Thermalization at RHIC

## Key claim
The centrality dependence of dynamic transverse momentum and net-charge fluctuations can signal the approach to local thermal equilibrium in nuclear collisions, but the model is presented as work in progress and the strongest observed changes occur in peripheral collisions where equilibrium is not expected, meaning the explanation is a nonequilibrium one.

## Method
The author uses a Boltzmann transport equation with Langevin noise terms to describe fluctuations of the phase-space distribution, parameterizing the survival probability S as a function of the number of participants N_part. The model incorporates a wounded nucleon model for initial correlations and Gaussian spatial correlations for the near-equilibrium limit, with HIJING used to estimate the robust variance R_AA and pp correlation inputs.

## Key numbers
- The survival probability S is parameterized with alpha = 4 and gamma = 0.15 in central collisions, with alpha proportional to N_part^(1/2) and tau_F - tau_0 proportional to N_part.
- The dimensionless factor F in the near-equilibrium fluctuation formula is 0.046 for xi_t/R_t = 1/6.
- ISR measurements imply <delta p_t1 delta p_t2>_pp / <p_t>_pp^2 is approximately 0.015.
- The acceptance dependence calculation uses a thermal distribution at the freeze-out temperature obtained from measured <p_t>, with a dashed curve shown for T = 600 MeV.
- The model is compared to PHENIX data for Au+Au collisions in the acceptance region 0.2 GeV < p_t < p_t,max as a function of p_t,max.
- STAR data are shown for beam energies of 20, 130, and 200 GeV for both p_t and net charge fluctuations.

## Context
This work extends the transport-theory formulation of Gavin (2004) to confront new PHENIX measurements of the p_t-range dependence of fluctuations, and compares to STAR data from Pruneau and collaborators. The approach seeks a nonequilibrium explanation because similar behavior could accompany quark-gluon plasma hadronization only if the matter is thermalized, as discussed by Wilk. The paper notes that collective flow (Voloshin) and jets (Mitchell) may contribute to deviations in central collisions at the highest energies, and that HIJING alone does not describe the data. The work enables further study of whether precise measurements can disentangle thermalization, flow, and jet contributions to fluctuations.
