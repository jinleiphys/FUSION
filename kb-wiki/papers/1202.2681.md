# Pairing dynamics in particle transport

> We analyze the effect of pairing on particle transport in time-dependent theories based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) or BCS approximations. The equations of motion for the HFB density matrices are unique and the theory respects the usual conservation laws defined by commutators of the conserved quantity with the Hamiltonian. In contrast, the theories based on the BCS approximation are more problematic. In the usual formulation of TDHF+BCS, the equation of continuity is violated and one sees unphysical oscillations in particle densities. This can be ameliorated by freezing the occupation numbers during the evolution in TDHF+BCS, but there are other problems with the BCS that make it doubtful for reaction dynamics. We also compare different numerical implementations of the time-dependent HFB equations. The equations of motion for the $U$ and $V$ Bogoliubov transformations are not unique, but it appears that the usual formulation is also the most efficient. Finally, we compare the time-dependent HFB solutions with numerically exact solutions of the two-particle Schrodinger equation. Depending on the treatment of the initial state, the HFB dynamics produces a particle emission rate at short times similar to that of the Schrodinger equation. At long times, the total particle emission can be quite different, due to inherent mean-field approximation of the HFB theory.

## Key claim

The TDHF+BCS approximation violates the equation of continuity and produces unphysical density oscillations in particle transport, while the TDHFB theory respects conservation laws; however, even TDHFB overestimates particle emission at long times due to its inherent mean-field approximation, and the short-time emission rate can match the exact Schrodinger solution only with proper treatment of the initial state.

## Method

The authors compared TDHFB and TDHF+BCS dynamics in a one-dimensional model system with a Woods-Saxon potential and finite-range Gaussian two-body interaction, using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm for time integration. They implemented two formulations of the TDHFB equations (Ehrenfest and Thouless forms) and compared their numerical performance, and also compared the TDHFB results with numerically exact solutions of the two-particle Schrodinger equation for particle evaporation from an initially compressed system.

## Key numbers

- Three interaction strengths were considered: v0 = -1.096e-2, -3.344e-2, and -6.280e-2 (in units of hbar^2/(m Delta x^2)), referred to as cases (a), (b), and (c) respectively
- The Woods-Saxon potential depth was adjusted to give binding energy E = -2.2e-2 for all cases, yielding U0 = -2.7e-2 (case a), -1.929e-2 (case b), and -7.716e-3 (case c)
- The box dimension was Xmax = 500, giving HFB matrices of dimension 2000, with time step Delta t = 0.263 (in units of m Delta x^2/hbar)
- The imaginary absorbing potential used Vim = -7.716e-3 with xim = 37.5
- The Ehrenfest formulation was found to be three to four times faster than the Thouless formulation

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pairing dynamics in time-dependent theories, motivated by unphysical oscillations observed in earlier TDHF+BCS calculations of neutron evaporation from O-22 by Washiyama and Lacroix (2011). The authors show that the continuity equation violation in TDHF+BCS is systematic, and that the continuum gas problem in BCS, previously noted by Dobaczewski (1984) in nuclear structure, also plagues dynamical calculations. The study enables benchmarking of TDHFB implementations and clarifies which approximations are suitable for large-amplitude collective motion and particle transport in nuclear reactions.
