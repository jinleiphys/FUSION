# Theoretical study on $Λα$ and $Ξα$ correlation functions

> We examine the $Λ$-${}^4\mathrm{He}$ ($α$) and $Ξα$ momentum correlation in high-energy collisions to further elucidate the properties of the hyperon-nucleon interactions. For the $Λα$ system, we compare $Λα$ potential models with different strengths at short range. We find that the difference among the models is visible in the momentum correlation from a small-size source. This indicates that the $Λα$ momentum correlation can constrain the property of the $ΛN$ interaction at short range, which plays an essential role in dense nuclear matter. For the $Ξα$ system, we employ the folding $Ξα$ potential based on the lattice QCD $ΞN$ interactions. The $Ξα$ potential supports a Coulomb assisted bound state of ${}^5_Ξ\mathrm{H}$ in the $Ξ^-α$ channel, while the $Ξ^0α$ channel is unbound. To examine the sensitivity of the correlation function to the nature of the $Ξα$ potential, we vary the potential strength simulating stronger and weaker interactions. The result of the correlation function clearly reflects the bound state nature in the $Ξ^-α$ correlation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that momentum correlations between hyperons (Λ or Ξ) and alpha particles (⁴He) can reveal the short-range behavior of hyperon-nucleon interactions, but this sensitivity is conditional on the source size being small (around 1 fm) for the Λα system, while the Ξα correlation shows sensitivity even at larger source sizes (3 fm).

## Method

The authors calculate Yα momentum correlation functions using the Koonin-Pratt formula with a spherical Gaussian source function, including strong interaction effects only in the s-wave state. For the Λα system, they compare four potential models (Chi3, LY-IV, Isle, SG) with different short-range strengths but similar long-range behavior. For the Ξα system, they employ a folding potential based on HAL QCD ΞN interactions and vary its strength by factors of 2 and 1/2 to simulate stronger and weaker interactions.

## Key numbers

- The Λα binding energy of ⁵ΛHe is 3.12 MeV, used to constrain the overall attraction of the Λα potential
- The α charge radius is 1.68 fm, reproduced using a Gaussian width parameter ν = 0.20 fm⁻²
- For the Ξ⁻α system with the HAL QCD folding potential plus Coulomb interaction, a bound state of ⁵ΞH is found with binding energy B = 0.47 MeV
- With the strong potential (V_strong = 2×V_HAL) plus Coulomb, the Ξ⁻α binding energy becomes 2.08 MeV
- With the weak potential (V_weak = V_HAL/2) plus Coulomb, the Ξ⁻α binding energy is 0.18 MeV
- The Ξ⁰α system is bound only with the strong potential, with binding energy 1.15 MeV
- The Ξ⁻α correlation shows a bump structure around relative momentum q ≈ 50–200 MeV/c for source size R = 1 fm

## Context

This work extends femtoscopy studies of hyperon-nucleon interactions, which have previously been applied to pΞ⁻ correlations by ALICE (2019, 2020) using HAL QCD potentials and chiral EFT. The Λα study builds on earlier work by Motoba (1985) showing that Λ hypernuclei binding energies are insensitive to short-range potential variations, and by Kumagai-Fuse (1994) on weak-decay widths. The Ξα study uses folding potentials from Hiyama (2022) based on HAL QCD ΞN interactions, and compares with chiral EFT results by Le (2021) that predict a ⁵ΞH bound state with B = 2.16 MeV. The work enables future measurements at facilities such as FAIR, NICA, HIAF, and J-PARC HI to constrain hyperon-nucleon interactions relevant to neutron star physics.
