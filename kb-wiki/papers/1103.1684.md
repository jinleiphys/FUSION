# Precision Holographic Baryons

> We overview a holographic QCD based on the D4-D8 string theory model, with emphasis on baryons and nucleon-meson interactions thereof. Baryons are realized as holographic images of Skyrmions, but with much qualitative changes. This allows us to derive, without adjustable parameters, couplings of baryons to the entire tower of spin one mesons and also to pseudoscalar mesons. We find some surprisingly good match against empirical values for nucleons, in particular. Tensor couplings to all axial-vectors and iso-singlet vectors all vanish, while, for $ρ$ mesons, tensor couplings are found to be dominant. We close with various cautionary comments and speculations.

## Key claim

The D4-D8 holographic QCD model can derive baryon-meson couplings without adjustable parameters, but the author cautions that the model's truncation to gravity and low-energy partners may only be trustworthy below about 1 GeV, and that comparisons with experiment must involve direct computation of scattering amplitudes since empirical coupling values are often model-dependent.

## Method

The authors start from a five-dimensional U(N_f) flavor gauge theory derived from the D4-D8 string theory model, where baryons appear as solitonic objects with unit Pontryagin number. They quantize the soliton to obtain a five-dimensional Dirac field representing the nucleon, then perform a Kaluza-Klein reduction along the holographic w-direction to extract four-dimensional meson and baryon fields. All coupling constants are computed from overlap integrals of the baryon wavefunctions with the meson mode eigenfunctions, using the large λN_c limit where the baryon wavefunctions become sharply peaked at w=0.

## Key numbers

- The model requires M_KK ≈ 0.94 GeV to match the lowest-lying ρ meson mass, and λ ≈ 17 to fit f_π to the pion decay constant
- The baryon soliton size is ρ_baryon ≈ (2·3^7·π^2/5)^{1/4} / (M_KK √λ) when λ ≫ 1
- For ρ mesons, the tensor coupling dominates over vector coupling: 2M_KK × g^{(1)}_{dρ} ≈ 6.0 × g^{(1)}_ρ after extrapolating to N_c=3 and λ≈17
- The ratio of iso-singlet to iso-triplet vector couplings is g_ω^{(1)}/g_ρ^{(1)} ≈ 3.6 (from 3 + 0.6)
- The predicted g_A = (24/5π^2)^{1/2} × N_c/3 + ... gives an extrapolated value of 0.84, about 30% better than the Adkins-Nappi-Witten (1983) Skyrmion estimate

## Context

This work extends the D4-D8 holographic QCD model of Sakai and Sugimoto (2005) from the meson sector into the baryon sector, building on earlier holographic baryon formulations by Hong et al. (2007) and Hata et al. (2007). The model reproduces the empirical fact that tensor couplings to iso-singlet ω mesons vanish, which has been used for decades in nucleon-nucleon potential models without theoretical understanding, and also predicts that tensor couplings to all axial-vector mesons vanish. The results enable future experimental verification of these predictions for axial mesons and provide a theoretical foundation for nucleon-nucleon potential models.
