# Constrained Orthogonal Polynomials

> We define sets of orthogonal polynomials satisfying the additional constraint of a vanishing average. These are of interest, for example, for the study of the Hohenberg-Kohn functional for electronic or nucleonic densities and for the study of density fluctuations in centrifuges. We give explicit properties of such polynomial sets, generalizing Laguerre and Legendre polynomials. The nature of the dimension 1 subspace completing such sets is described. A numerical example illustrates the use of such polynomials.

## Key claim

This paper defines sets of orthogonal polynomials that satisfy an additional constraint of vanishing average, which is motivated by the need to expand density variations in a basis that automatically enforces particle number conservation without requiring a Lagrange multiplier. The constraint is incompatible with a polynomial of order n=0, so the order hierarchy runs from n=1 to infinity rather than from n=0 to infinity.

## Method

The author constructs constrained polynomial sets by first creating "trivial seeds" of the form s_n^d(r) = r^n - ⟨r^n⟩_d, where the subtraction of the average ensures each seed fulfills the constraint, then orthogonalizing such seeds by a Gram-Schmidt algorithm. For the Laguerre case with weight e^{-r/2} on [0,∞), the author derives a recursion relation G_n^d(r) = (r-d) G_{n-1}^d(r) - 2r G_{n-1}^{d'}(r) + (n+d-1)(n-2) G_{n-2}^d(r). For the Legendre case on [0,1] with constant weight, the author provides recursion relations for dimensions d=1,2,3 and a differential equation r(r-1) G_n^{d''} + [(d+1)r - d] G_n^{d'} - n(n+d) G_n^d = 0.

## Key numbers

- For the constrained Laguerre polynomials G_n^d, the normalization is g_n^d = (n-1)! (n+d)!
- For the constrained Legendre polynomials G_n^d, the normalization is γ_n^d = 1/(2n+d)
- The first constrained Laguerre polynomial for d=1 is G_1^1 = r - 2
- The first constrained Laguerre polynomial for d=2 is G_1^2 = r - 4
- The first constrained Laguerre polynomial for d=3 is G_1^3 = r - 6
- The first constrained Legendre polynomial for d=2 is G_1^2 = 3r - 2
- The first constrained Legendre polynomial for d=3 is G_1^3 = 4r - 3
- For the toy model with Z=4 fermions, a perturbation Δu = λ_4 w_4 with λ_4=2 gives density coordinates {0.016, -0.267, -0.055, 0.023, 0.018} with strong dominance of Δρ_4
- For the toy model with Z=4 fermions, a perturbation Δu = λ_6 w_6 with λ_6=2 gives density coordinates {-0.013, -0.041, -0.376, 0.008, 0.040} with strong dominance of Δρ_6

## Context

This work extends the author's prior construction of constrained Hermite polynomials (Giraud, Mehta and Weiguny, 2004) to Laguerre-type and Legendre-type polynomials, motivated by applications to the Hohenberg-Kohn functional in nuclear, atomic, and molecular physics (Hohenberg and Kohn, 1964; Mermin, 1965; Kohn and Sham, 1965). The new polynomials enable expansions of density variations that automatically conserve particle number in cylindrical and spherical geometries, and the author demonstrates their use in a toy model of non-interacting fermions in a harmonic oscillator potential, finding that low-order modes produce density perturbations with nearly the same shape as the driving potential perturbation.
