# Polarized photons in radiative muon capture

> We discuss the measurement of polarized photons arising from radiative muon capture. The spectrum of left circularly polarized photons or equivalently the circular polarization of the photons emitted in radiative muon capture on hydrogen is quite sensitive to the strength of the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant $g_P$. A measurement of either of these quantities, although very difficult, might be sufficient to resolve the present puzzle resulting from the disagreement between the theoretical prediction for $g_P$ and the results of a recent experiment. This sensitivity results from the absence of left-handed radiation from the muon line and from the fact that the leading parts of the radiation from the hadronic lines, as determined from the chiral power counting rules of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, all contain pion poles.

## Key claim

Measuring the circular polarization of photons emitted in radiative muon capture on hydrogen, or equivalently the spectrum of left-circularly polarized photons, is highly sensitive to the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant \(g_P\) and could resolve the current puzzle between theory and experiment, although such a measurement is technically extremely challenging and may require future very intense muon beams.

## Method

The authors calculated the spectrum of left- and right-handed photons and the circular polarization for radiative muon capture on a proton using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO, one-loop order). They also compared these results with a relativistic phenomenological model parameterized by weak form factors, using two values of the pseudoscalar coupling constant \(g_P/g_P^{PCAC}=1\) and \(1.5\). The calculations were performed for the "experimental state" (6.1% atomic hyperfine singlet, 85.4% ortho \(p\)-\(\mu\)-\(p\) molecular state, 8.5% para \(p\)-\(\mu\)-\(p\) molecular state) and separately for each spin state (singlet, triplet, statistical, ortho).

## Key numbers

- The spectrum of left-handed photons for \(g_P/g_P^{PCAC}=1.5\) is larger by about a factor of three than for \(g_P/g_P^{PCAC}=1\) over the photon energy range \(E_\gamma = 60\) to 100 MeV.
- The circular polarization differs for the two values of \(g_P\) by a more or less constant amount of 0.2 over the whole relevant photon energy region.
- The rate for right-handed photons is about 2.5 times larger than for left-handed photons for the singlet state and 17.3 times larger for the triplet state, when integrated over \(E_\gamma = 60\) to 99 MeV.
- The ratio of singlet to triplet state spectra, integrated over photon energy, is 0.34 for left-handed photons and 0.05 for right-handed photons.
- In the unpolarized photon spectrum, the difference between the two \(g_P\) values is only of the order of 30-40% in the measurable region.

## Context

This work extends earlier HBChPT calculations of radiative muon capture on a proton (Ando et al., 2001; Meissner, 1998) and the relativistic phenomenological model (Fearing, 1980), which all found a discrepancy between the theoretical prediction for \(g_P\) and the experimental result from the TRIUMF group (1998). The authors propose measuring photon polarization as a way to filter out the dominant muon-radiation diagram and enhance sensitivity to the hadronic current, which contains pion poles. This approach could help resolve the "\(g_P\) puzzle" and, if successful, would enable a more precise determination of the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant, though the measurement is likely impossible with current muon beams and may require future very intense muon sources.
