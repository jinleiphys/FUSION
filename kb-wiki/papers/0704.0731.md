# The tensor part of the Skyrme energy density functional. I. Spherical nuclei

> We perform a systematic study of the impact of the J^2 tensor term in the Skyrme energy functional on properties of spherical nuclei. In the Skyrme energy functional, the tensor terms originate both from zero-range central and tensor forces. We build a set of 36 parameterizations, which covers a wide range of the parameter space of the isoscalar and isovector tensor term coupling constants, with a fit protocol very similar to that of the successful SLy parameterizations. We analyze the impact of the tensor terms on a large variety of observables in spherical mean-field calculations, such as the spin-orbit splittings and single-particle spectra of doubly-magic nuclei, the evolution of spin-orbit splittings along chains of semi-magic nuclei, mass residuals of spherical nuclei, and known anomalies of charge radii. Our main conclusion is that the currently used central and spin-orbit parts of the Skyrme energy density functional are not flexible enough to allow for the presence of large tensor terms.

## Key claim

The tensor terms of the Skyrme energy density functional, when varied freely, do not lower the chi-squared fit quality within a standard Skyrme functional; the currently used central and spin-orbit parts are not flexible enough to accommodate large tensor terms, and the standard spin-orbit interaction does not scale properly with the principal quantum number, a problem that tensor terms with realistic coupling constants cannot cure.

## Method

The authors built a set of 36 Skyrme parameterizations covering a wide range of the isoscalar and isovector tensor term coupling constants, using a fit protocol very similar to that of the successful SLy parameterizations. They performed spherical mean-field calculations for doubly-magic and semi-magic nuclei, analyzing spin-orbit splittings, single-particle spectra, mass residuals, and charge radii anomalies. The tensor terms were not added perturbatively; instead, the entire parameter set was refitted for each parameterization.

## Key numbers

- 36 parameterizations were constructed, covering a wide range of the parameter space of the isoscalar and isovector tensor term coupling constants.
- The study examined spin-orbit splittings and single-particle spectra in doubly-magic nuclei including 16O, 48Ca, and 208Pb.
- The evolution of spin-orbit splittings was analyzed along chains of Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes.
- Positive values of the coupling constants of proton-neutron and like-particle tensor terms allow a qualitative description of the evolution of spin-orbit splittings in these isotope chains.

## Context

This work extends earlier exploratory studies by Stancu, Brink and Flocard (1977), who added the tensor force perturbatively to the SIII parameterization, and by Tondeur (1983), who attempted a complete fit including tensor terms. It also relates to recent work by Otsuka et al. (2005, 2006) on the proton-neutron tensor force from pion exchange, and by Brown et al. (2006) who fitted a Skyrme interaction with added zero-range tensor force. The study enables future work on deformed nuclei and time-odd contributions from the tensor force, which may allow distinguishing between non-local central and tensor parts of the Skyrme force.
