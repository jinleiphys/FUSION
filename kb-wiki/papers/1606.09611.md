# Quantized chiral magnetic current from reconnections of magnetic flux

> We introduce a new mechanism for the chiral magnetic effect that does not require an initial chirality imbalance. The chiral magnetic current is generated by reconnections of magnetic flux that change the magnetic helicity of the system. The resulting current is entirely determined by the change of magnetic helicity, and it is quantized.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a mechanism for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) that does not require an initial chirality imbalance; instead, the chiral magnetic current is generated by reconnections of magnetic flux that change the magnetic helicity, and the resulting current is quantized and entirely determined by the change in magnetic helicity.

## Method
The authors consider closed tubes of magnetic flux in the presence of massless fermions, assuming a strong magnetic field such that fermions are localized on lowest Landau levels. They derive the generated current from the change in magnetic helicity due to either magnetic reconnection (which changes the linking number of flux tubes) or twisting of a flux tube, using Faraday's law to relate the change in magnetic flux to the induced electric field and then computing the resulting change in Fermi momenta and particle production for left- and right-handed fermions.

## Key numbers
- The main result is the formula: $\sum_{i} \oint_{C_i} \Delta \bm J \cdot d \bm x = - \frac{e^3}{2 \pi^2} \Delta \mathcal H$, where $e$ is the electric charge and $\Delta \mathcal H$ is the change in magnetic helicity.
- For the unlinking of two flux tubes with fluxes $\varphi_1$ and $\varphi_2$, the change in helicity is $\Delta \mathcal H = 2 \varphi_1 \varphi_2$, and the integrated current is $-\frac{e^3}{2 \pi^2} \times 2 \varphi_1 \varphi_2$.
- For twisting a flux tube with flux $\varphi$, the helicity increment is $\Delta \mathcal H = \varphi^2 \Delta n_0$, and the induced current is $-\frac{e^3}{2\pi^2} \varphi^2 \Delta n_0$.
- The reconnection time scale is $t_{\rm rec} \sim \sigma L^2$, where $\sigma$ is Ohmic conductivity and $L$ is the typical length scale of magnetic field inhomogeneity.
- The condition for the lowest Landau level approximation to hold is $\sigma L^2 \sqrt{eB} \gg 1$.
- The condition for the homogeneity assumption is $L \sqrt{eB} \gg 1$.

## Context
This work extends the standard understanding of the chiral magnetic effect, which previously required an initial chirality imbalance generated by external topological backgrounds such as parallel electric and magnetic fields (as used in experiments on Dirac semimetals by Li (2014), Xiong (2015), and Huang (2015)) or non-Abelian sphaleron transitions. The paper builds on prior studies of magnetic helicity generation in CME contexts by Joyce (1997), Boyarsky (2011), Tashiro (2012), Tuchin (2014), and Manuel (2015), and on the self-similar inverse cascade of magnetic helicity pointed out by Hirono (2015) and Yamamoto (2016). The result enables new applications in chiral magnetohydrodynamics, the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, Dirac and Weyl semimetals, and primordial electroweak plasma in the early Universe.
