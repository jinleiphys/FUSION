# On the Extraction of Low-energy Constants of Single- and Double-$β$ Decays from Lattice QCD: A Sensitivity Analysis

> Lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) has the promise of constraining low-energy constants (LECs) of nuclear effective field theories (EFTs) from first-principles calculations that incorporate the dynamics of quarks and gluons. Given the Euclidean and finite-volume nature of LQCD outputs, complex mappings are developed in recent years to obtain the Minkowski and infinite-volume counterparts of LQCD observables. In particular, as LQCD is moving toward computing a set of important few-nucleon matrix elements at the physical values of the quark masses, it is important to investigate whether the anticipated precision of LQCD spectra and matrix elements will be sufficient to guarantee tighter constraints on the relevant LECs than those already obtained from phenomenology, considering the non-trivial mappings involved. With a focus on the leading-order LECs of the pionless EFT, $L_{1,A}$ and $g_ν^{NN}$, which parametrize, respectively, the strength of the isovector axial two-body current in a single-$β$ decay (and other related processes such $pp$ fusion), and of the isotensor contact two-body operator in the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay within the light neutrino exchange scenario, the expected uncertainty on future extractions of $L_{1,A}$ and $g_ν^{NN}$ are examined using synthetic data at the physical values of the quark masses. It is observed that achieving small uncertainties in $L_{1,A}$ will be challenging, and (sub)percent-level precision in the two-nucleon spectra and matrix elements is essential in reducing the uncertainty on this LEC compared to the existing constraints. On the other hand, the short-distance coupling of the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay, $g_ν^{NN}$, is shown to be less sensitive to uncertainties on both LQCD energies and the matrix element, and can likely be constrained with percent-level precision in the upcoming LQCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the anticipated precision of future lattice QCD calculations at physical quark masses will be sufficient to constrain the low-energy constants \(L_{1,A}\) and \(g_{\nu}^{NN}\) more tightly than existing phenomenological constraints, given the nontrivial finite-volume and Euclidean-to-Minkowski mappings involved. The authors find that achieving competitive uncertainties for \(L_{1,A}\) will require (sub)percent-level precision in two-nucleon spectra and matrix elements, while \(g_{\nu}^{NN}\) can likely be constrained with percent-level precision even with few-percent uncertainties on lattice QCD inputs.

## Method

The authors performed a sensitivity analysis using synthetic data that mimics future lattice QCD results at physical quark masses. First, they used experimental NN scattering phase shifts from the Nijmegen potential to compute finite-volume energy eigenvalues via Luscher's quantization condition for the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) channels at volumes of 8, 12, and 16 fm. They then introduced Gaussian fluctuations on these energies and on the corresponding three- and four-point correlation functions to generate synthetic lattice QCD datasets with varying precision levels (1%, 5%, and 10% uncertainties). Finally, they applied the matching relations from Davoudi (2020) to extract the LECs \(L_{1,A}\) and \(g_{\nu}^{NN}\) from the synthetic data and propagated the uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The experimental constraint on \(L_{1,A}\) is \(4.9^{+1.9}_{-1.5}\) fm\(^3\) at \(\mu = m_\pi\) from Acharya (2019), while the previous lattice QCD determination at \(m_\pi \approx 806\) MeV gave \(L_{1,A} = 3.9(0.2)(1.4)\) fm\(^3\) from Savage (2016).
- The indirect estimate of the dimensionless parameter \(\widetilde{g}_{\nu}^{NN}\) is \(1.3 \pm 0.6\) from Cirigliano (2020, 2021).
- The effective-range expansion parameters used are: \(a = -23.5\) fm and \(r = 2.75\) fm for the \(^1S_0\) channel, and \(\widetilde{a} = 5.42\) fm and \(\widetilde{r} = 1.75\) fm for the \(^3S_1\) channel, obtained from the Nijmegen potential.
- The analysis considered volumes of \(L = 8\) fm, 12 fm, and 16 fm, with energy uncertainties of \(\Delta_E = 10\%\), 5%, and 1% for the sensitivity study.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Davoudi (2020) for matching lattice QCD outputs to pionless EFT LECs for single- and double-beta decay processes. It follows the spirit of Briceno (2013) which demonstrated the feasibility of extracting the S-D mixing parameter in the deuteron channel from lattice QCD. The analysis enables future lattice QCD calculations at physical quark masses to be designed with appropriate statistical precision and volume choices to meaningfully constrain the axial two-body current in processes like \(pp\) fusion and the short-range operator in neutrinoless double-beta decay, which currently suffer from large uncertainties in phenomenological determinations.
