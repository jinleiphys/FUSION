# Effect of a fermion on quantum phase transitions in bosonic systems

> The effect of a fermion with angular momentum j on quantum phase transitions of a (s,d) bosonic system is investigated. It is shown that the presence of a fermion strongly modifies the critical value at which the transition occurs, and its nature, even for small and moderate values of the coupling constant. The analogy with a bosonic system in an external field is mentioned. Experimental evidence for precursors of quantum phase transitions in bosonic systems plus a fermion (odd-even nuclei) is presented.

## Key claim

The presence of a single fermion coupled to a bosonic system strongly modifies both the critical value and the nature of the quantum phase transition, even for small and moderate values of the Bose-Fermi coupling constant; the fermion acts as either a catalyst or a retarder depending on the projection quantum number \(K\) of the fermion state.

## Method

The authors use the Interacting Boson Fermion Model (IBFM) to describe a system of \(N\) (\(s,d\)) bosons coupled via a quadrupole interaction to a single fermion with angular momentum \(j=11/2\). They perform a classical analysis by constructing a combined Bose-Fermi potential energy surface (Landau potential) from a boson condensate and diagonalizing the fermion single-particle matrix in the deformed boson field, then minimize the total energy functional with respect to the deformation parameter \(\beta\) for each fermion projection \(K\).

## Key numbers

- The critical point for the purely bosonic U(5)-SU(3) transition occurs at \(\xi_{c} \cong 1/2\).
- Physical values of the Bose-Fermi quadrupole coupling strength \(\Gamma\) in nuclei where the phase transition occurs (Pm, Eu, Tb) are \(\Gamma \cong -0.125\) (in units of \(\varepsilon_0\)).
- The deformation contribution to two-neutron separation energies, \(S(2n)_{\mathrm{def}}\), is enhanced in odd-even nuclei by approximately \(300\) keV at neutron number 92 compared to the even-even case.
- The effect of the odd fermion on \(S(2n)_{\mathrm{def}}\) is about 20%.
- For the even-even nuclei Nd-Sm-Gd, the linear subtraction parameters are \(A_{2n} = -14.61,\,-15.82,\,-16.997\) MeV and \(B_{2n} = 0.657\) MeV; for the odd-even nuclei Pm-Eu-Tb, \(A_{2n} = -15.185,\,-16.37,\,-17.672\) MeV and \(B_{2n} = 0.670\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quantum phase transitions in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) for even-even nuclei (Iachello, 2001; Casten, 2011) to odd-even nuclei via the Interacting Boson Fermion Model (IBFM). It builds on earlier investigations of QPTs in IBFM by Alonso et al. and Boyukata, and draws an analogy with the Landau-Lifshitz treatment of a bosonic system in an external field (Landau, 1958). The results enable systematic experimental and theoretical studies of shape phase transitions in odd-even nuclei, including applications to other spin-boson systems such as a spin-1/2 particle in a bath of harmonic oscillator bosons.
