# Paring correlations within the micro-macroscopic approach for the level density

> Level density $ρ(E,N,Z)$ is calculated for the two-component close- and open-shell nuclei with a given energy $E$, and neutron $N$ and proton $Z$ numbers, taking into account pairing effects within the microscopic-macroscopic approach (MMA). These analytical calculations have been carried out by using the semiclassical statistical mean-field approximations beyond the saddle-point method of the Fermi gas model in a low excitation-energies range. The level density $ρ$, obtained as function of the system entropy $S$, depends essentially on the condensation energy $E_{\rm cond}$ through the excitation energy $U$ in super-fluid nuclei. The simplest super-fluid approach, based on the BCS theory, accounts for a smooth temperature dependence of the pairing gap $Δ$ due to particle number fluctuations. Taking into account the pairing effects in magic or semi-magic nuclei, excited below neutron resonances, one finds a notable pairing phase transition.Pairing correlations sometimes improve significantly the comparison with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pairing correlations, treated through the condensation energy shift within the microscopic-macroscopic approach (MMA), significantly improve the agreement between theoretical level densities and experimental data for magic nuclei such as $^{40}$Ca, but the observability of the superfluid-normal phase transition depends critically on the distance between the first excited state and the estimated total excitation energy for pairing collapse, which varies strongly among nuclei.

## Method

The authors calculate the nuclear level density $\rho(E,N,Z)$ using the microscopic-macroscopic approach (MMA), which combines a micro-canonical ensemble for the energy integration with a grand-canonical ensemble for the particle number integration, going beyond the standard saddle-point method (SPM) of the Fermi gas model. They incorporate pairing correlations through the BCS theory, introducing a condensation energy $E_{\rm cond}$ that shifts the effective excitation energy $U_{\rm eff} = U - E_{\rm cond}$, and use the modified Bessel function expression $\rho \approx \overline{\rho}_\nu S^{-\nu} I_\nu(S)$ with $\nu=2$ or $\nu=3$ depending on the shell structure contribution parameter $\xi$. The inverse level density parameter $K$ is determined by least mean-square fits to experimental data from the ENSDF database using a sample method with plateau condition.

## Key numbers

- Condensation energy $E_{\rm cond} \approx 216/(\pi^2 K)$ MeV, yielding values of 2.3 MeV for $^{40}$Ca, 0.80 MeV for $^{56}$Ni, 1.23 MeV for $^{54}$Fe and $^{52}$Fe, 1.0 MeV for $^{48}$Ca, 1.4 MeV for $^{115}$Sn, 0.6 MeV for $^{144}$Sm, and 0.9 MeV for $^{208}$Pb
- Total excitation energy for superfluid-normal phase transition $U^{\rm tot}_{\rm c}$: 7.1 MeV for $^{40}$Ca, 2.50 MeV for $^{56}$Ni, 3.82-3.84 MeV for $^{54}$Fe and $^{52}$Fe, 3.2 MeV for $^{48}$Ca, 4.4 MeV for $^{115}$Sn, 2.0 MeV for $^{144}$Sm, and 2.9 MeV for $^{208}$Pb
- Inverse level density parameter $K$ from MMA with pairing: 9.6 MeV for $^{40}$Ca, 27.3 MeV for $^{56}$Ni, 17.9 MeV for $^{54}$Fe, 17.7 MeV for $^{52}$Fe, 21.6 MeV for $^{48}$Ca, 15.6 MeV for $^{115}$Sn, 34.2 MeV for $^{144}$Sm, and 23.9 MeV for $^{208}$Pb
- Critical temperature for pairing collapse: $T_{\rm c} = e^C \Delta_0/\pi$ where $C \approx 0.577$ is the Euler constant
- Empirical pairing gap: $\Delta_0 \approx 12 A^{-1/2}$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the microscopic-macroscopic approach (MMA) previously developed by Magner and collaborators (2021-2023) for level density calculations, incorporating pairing correlations through the BCS theory. It addresses the question raised by recent experimental studies (BC22) regarding superfluidity in magic nuclei like $^{56}$Ni and $^{52}$Fe. The approach contrasts with the standard Fermi gas model (Er60) which diverges at low excitation energies, and with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory (RS80) which predicts a sharp phase transition, whereas the authors find that particle number fluctuations lead to a smooth temperature dependence of the pairing gap. The results enable improved level density predictions for low-energy nuclear spectra below neutron resonances, relevant for nuclear astrophysics and statistical model calculations.
