# Interplay between chiral and deconfinement phase transitions

> By using the dressed Polyakov loop or dual chiral condensate as an equivalent order parameter of the deconfinement phase transition, we investigate the relation between the chiral and deconfinement phase transitions at finite temperature and density in the framework of three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. It is found that in the chiral limit, the critical temperature for chiral phase transition coincides with that of the dressed Polyakov loop in the whole $(T,μ)$ plane. In the case of explicit chiral symmetry breaking, it is found that the phase transitions are flavor dependent. For each flavor, the transition temperature for chiral restoration $T_c^χ$ is smaller than that of the dressed Polyakov loop $T_c^{\cal D}$ in the low baryon density region where the transition is a crossover, and, the two critical temperatures coincide in the high baryon density region where the phase transition is of first order. Therefore, there are two critical end points, i.e, $T_{CEP}^{u,d}$ and $T_{CEP}^{s}$ at finite density. We also explain the feature of $T_c^χ=T_c^{\cal D}$ in the case of 1st and 2nd order phase transitions, and $T_c^χ<T_c^{\cal D}$ in the case of crossover, and expect this feature is general and can be extended to full QCD theory.

## Key claim

In the three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the critical temperature for chiral restoration coincides with that of the dressed Polyakov loop for first- and second-order phase transitions, but is smaller than the dressed-Polyakov-loop critical temperature in the crossover region; this pattern is expected to be general and extendable to full QCD.

## Method

The authors computed the dressed Polyakov loop (dual chiral condensate with winding number n=1) from the Fourier transform of the U(1)-boundary-condition-dependent quark condensate in the mean-field approximation of the two- and three-flavor NJL model. They determined transition temperatures from the slope (or second derivative) of the conventional chiral condensate and of the dressed Polyakov loop as functions of temperature at fixed chemical potential, for both the chiral limit and explicit chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

* For two-flavor NJL with m=5.5 MeV at zero chemical potential, the chiral transition temperature T_c^χ is about 7 MeV lower than the dressed-Polyakov-loop transition temperature T_c^D.
* For two-flavor NJL with m=200 MeV at zero chemical potential, the difference between T_c^χ and T_c^D increases to about 26 MeV.
* In the three-flavor finite-mass case (m_u=m_d=5.5 MeV, m_s=140.7 MeV), the critical end point for u,d quarks is located at (T_CEP^(u,d), μ_CEP^(u,d)) = (91 MeV, 315 MeV).
* In the same three-flavor case, the critical end point for the s quark is located at (T_CEP^s, μ_CEP^s) = (75 MeV, 445 MeV).
* The three-flavor NJL parameters used: cutoff Λ = 602.3 MeV, G_s Λ^2 = 1.918 (finite-mass) or 1.926 (chiral limit), K Λ^5 = 12.36.

## Context

This work extends studies of the relation between chiral and deconfinement phase transitions using the dressed Polyakov loop as an order parameter, previously investigated in lattice QCD (e.g., Wuppertal-Budapest group, Fodor 2009) and in Dyson-Schwinger approaches (Fischer 2009). The results qualitatively agree with the lattice finding that in the crossover region chiral restoration occurs at a lower temperature than deconfinement, and they predict two separate critical end points in the three-flavor phase diagram. The analysis enables future checks in other effective models such as Dyson-Schwinger equations and full QCD.
