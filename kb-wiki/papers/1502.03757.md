# Toward a solution to the $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ puzzle for heavy quarks

> The heavy quarks constitutes a unique probe of the quark-gluon plasma properties. Both at RHIC and LHC energies a puzzling relation between the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}(p_T)$ and the elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ has been observed which challenged all the existing models, especially for D mesons. We discuss how the temperature dependence of the heavy quark drag coefficient is responsible for a large part of such a puzzle. In particular, we have considered four different models to evaluate the temperature dependence of drag and diffusion coefficients propagating through a quark gluon plasma (QGP). All the four different models are set to reproduce the same $R_{AA}(p_T)$ observed in experiments at RHIC and LHC energy. We point out that for the same $R_{AA}(p_T)$ one can generate 2-3 times more $v_2$ depending on the temperature dependence of the heavy quark drag coefficient. A non-decreasing drag coefficient as $ T \rightarrow\ T_c \,$ is a major ingredient for a simultaneous description of $R_{AA}(p_T)$ and $v_2(p_T)$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the puzzling simultaneous description of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) and elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) for heavy quarks can be largely resolved by the correct temperature dependence of the heavy quark drag coefficient, specifically that a non-decreasing drag coefficient as temperature approaches the critical temperature \(T_c\) is a major ingredient, even when models are tuned to reproduce the same \(R_{AA}\).

## Method

The authors employed a 3+1D Langevin dynamics to evolve charm and bottom quarks through a quark-gluon plasma background, using a relativistic transport code with Glauber initial conditions and fixed \(\eta/s=0.16\). They considered four different models for the heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients: pQCD with running coupling, AdS/CFT with a \(T^2\) dependence, a quasi-particle model (QPM) tuned to lattice QCD thermodynamics, and a variant with massless partons but QPM coupling. Each model was rescaled to reproduce the same \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) at both RHIC and LHC energies, and the resulting \(v_2(p_T)\) was compared.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) AGeV, Au+Au), the maximum initial temperature of the fireball center is \(T_i=340\) MeV and the initial time is \(\tau_i=0.6\) fm/c.
- At LHC energy (\(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) ATeV, Pb+Pb), the initial maximum temperature is \(T_0=510\) MeV and the initial time is \(\tau_0=0.3\) fm/c.
- For the same \(R_{AA}(p_T)\), the elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) can differ by up to a factor of about 2.5 between models, depending on the temperature dependence of the drag coefficient (peak region comparison).
- The AdS/CFT drag coefficient is proportional to \(T^2\) with a constant \(C=2.1\pm0.5\) in the expression \(\Gamma_{conf}= C \frac{T_{QCD}^2}{M_c}\).
- The Peterson fragmentation parameter used is \(\epsilon_c=0.04\) for charm quarks and \(\epsilon_c=0.005\) for bottom quarks.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing puzzle that heavy quark \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) measured at RHIC and LHC could not be simultaneously described by existing models, including pQCD-based approaches (e.g., Djordjevic 2005, Armesto 2005) and AdS/CFT calculations (e.g., Gubser 2006). The study extends earlier findings by showing that the temperature dependence of the drag coefficient, rather than its absolute magnitude, is the critical factor, and it contrasts with models like the T-matrix approach (Rapp and collaborators) which yield a nearly constant or weakly decreasing drag near \(T_c\). The results enable future work to focus on non-perturbative interactions and liquid-like behavior of the QGP near \(T_c\), and suggest that coalescence hadronization and Boltzmann dynamics (rather than Langevin) may further improve agreement with data.
