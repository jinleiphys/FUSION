# Vector-Interaction-Enhanced Bag Model

> A commonly applied quark matter model in astrophysics is the thermodynamic bag model (tdBAG). The original MIT bag model approximates the effect of quark confinement, but does not explicitly account for the breaking of chiral symmetry, an important property of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). It further ignores vector repulsion. The vector-interaction-enhanced bag model (vBag) improves the tdBAG approach by accounting for both dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and repulsive vector interactions. The latter is of particular importance to studies of dense matter in beta-equilibriumto explain the two solar mass maximum mass constraint for neutron stars. The model is motivated by analyses of QCD based Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE), assuming a simple quark-quark contact interaction. Here, we focus on the study of hybrid neutron star properties resulting from the application of vBag and will discuss possible extensions.

## Key claim

The vector-interaction-enhanced bag model (vBag) improves the thermodynamic bag model by accounting for both dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and repulsive vector interactions, which is necessary for hybrid quark-hadron neutron stars to reach the two solar mass maximum mass constraint; however, the model relies on a specific set of approximations to the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation that reproduce the standard Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and the vector coupling strength \(K_v\) is treated as an independent model parameter.

## Method

The authors derive vBag from the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation using a rainbow truncation and a constant gluon propagator with a hard three-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\), equivalent to a quark-quark contact interaction. This yields gap equations for the scalar and vector densities of an ideal spin-degenerate Fermi gas, from which the single-flavor pressure is expressed as an ideal Fermi gas term plus a vector condensate term minus a chiral bag constant \(B_{\chi,f}\). The deconfinement transition is modeled by adding a bag constant \(B_{dc}\) defined as the hadron pressure at the chiral transition chemical potential, ensuring simultaneous chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement via a Maxwell construction.

## Key numbers

* The chiral transition temperature from lattice QCD is \(154 \pm 9\) MeV (cited from Borsanyi et al. and Bazavov et al.).
* The pulsar PSR J1614-2230 has a mass of \(1.928 \pm 0.017\) \(M_{\odot}\) (Demorest 2010, Fonseca 2016).
* The pulsar PSR J0348+0432 has a mass of \(2.01 \pm 0.04\) \(M_{\odot}\) (Antoniadis 2013).
* Chiral bag constants used in the neutron star calculations are \(B^{1/4}_{\chi,u,d} = 155\) MeV and \(B^{1/4}_{\chi,s} = 170\) MeV.
* The Munczek-Nemirovsky model parameter \(\eta = 1\) GeV is used for momentum-dependent mass-gap solutions.
* Bare quark masses used in the MN model are: up quark \(m = 3\) MeV, down quark \(m = 5\) MeV, strange quark \(m = 100\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic bag model (tdBag) of Farhi (1984) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Nambu 1961, Klevansky 1992, Buballa 2003) by incorporating repulsive vector interactions and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, which are absent in both prior approaches. The vBag model was introduced earlier by Klähn (2015) and has been extended to finite temperatures and arbitrary isospin asymmetry (Klähn 2016, Fischer 2016, Klähn 2017). The model enables studies of hybrid neutron star properties and can be extended to include momentum-dependent quark properties via the Dyson-Schwinger equation formalism, as illustrated with the Munczek-Nemirovsky model (Munczek 1983, Klähn 2009, Cierniak 2017).
