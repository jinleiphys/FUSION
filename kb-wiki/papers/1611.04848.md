# Direct-Photon Spectra and Anisotropic Flow in Heavy Ion Collisions from Holography

> The thermal-photon emission from strongly coupled gauge theories at finite temperature is calculated by using holographic models for QCD in the Veneziano limit (V-QCD). These emission rates are then embedded in hydrodynamic simulations combined with prompt photons from hard scattering and the thermal photons from hadron gas to analyze the spectra and anisotropic flow of direct photons at RHIC and LHC. The results from different sources responsible for the thermal photons in the quark gluon plasma (QGP) including the weakly coupled QGP (wQGP) from perturbative calculations, strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}$=4 super Yang-Mills (SYM) plasma (as a benchmark for reference), and Gubser's phenomenological model mimicking the strongly coupled QGP (sQGP) are then compared. It is found that the direct-photon spectra are enhanced in the strongly coupled scenario compared with the ones in the wQGP, especially at intermediate and high momenta, which improve the agreements with data. Moreover, by using IP-glassma initial states, both the elliptic flow and triangular flow of direct photons are amplified at high momenta ($p_T$>2.5 GeV) for V-QCD, while they are suppressed at low momenta compared to wQGP. The distinct results in holography stem from the blue-shift of emission rates in strong coupling. In addition, the spectra and flow in small collision systems were evaluated for future comparisons. It is found that thermal photons from the deconfined phase are substantial to reconcile the spectra and flow at high momenta.

## Key claim

The direct-photon spectra from strongly coupled holographic QCD models (V-QCD and Gubser-Nellore) improve agreement with experimental data compared to perturbative QCD rates, especially at intermediate and high momenta; however, the anisotropic flow (v2, v3) shows a more nuanced pattern where holographic rates yield larger flow at high pT (>2.5 GeV) but smaller flow at low pT compared to weakly coupled QGP rates, with the specific behavior depending on the interplay between temperature-dependent emission rates and hydrodynamic flow development.

## Method

The authors computed thermal-photon emission rates from two bottom-up holographic models (Gubser-Nellore and V-QCD) that break conformal invariance and match lattice QCD properties at finite temperature, then embedded these rates into (3+1)D hydrodynamic simulations with IP-glasma initial conditions. They combined these thermal QGP photons with prompt photons from hard scattering and thermal photons from hadron gas to compute direct-photon spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients (v2, v3) for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV (RHIC) and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (LHC), as well as for p+Pb and d+Au small collision systems.

## Key numbers

- Direct-photon spectra from V-QCD and SYM models are enhanced compared to pQCD rates, with the largest enhancement from SYM; the enhancement is most pronounced at intermediate and high momenta (pT > 2.5 GeV)
- For Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV (0-20% centrality), the ratio of experimental data to theoretical results shows improved agreement for holographic models compared to pQCD rates
- For Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (0-40% centrality), direct photon v2 from holographic models exceeds pQCD results at pT > 3.0 GeV
- In small collision systems (0-1% p+Pb at 5.02 TeV and 0-5% d+Au at 200 GeV), thermal radiation can reach up to a factor of 2 of the prompt contribution
- The electric conductivity from V-QCD models exceeds that from SYM for T > 1.5 Tc, yet direct photon yields from V-QCD are smaller than SYM because most thermal photons come from the phase transition region (150 < T < 250 MeV)

## Context

This work extends prior holographic studies of thermal-photon production (Caron-Huot 2006, Mamo 2013, Yee 2013, Wu 2013) by embedding the emission rates into full hydrodynamic simulations with medium evolution and including contributions from prompt and hadronic photons, enabling direct comparison with experimental data from RHIC (PHENIX) and LHC (ALICE). The results contradict the standard perturbative approach of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (AMY) by showing that strongly coupled emission rates produce a "blue-shift" that enhances spectra and modifies flow patterns, and the findings suggest that future measurements of direct photons in small collision systems (p+Pb, d+Au) will be crucial to distinguish between weakly and strongly coupled QGP scenarios.
