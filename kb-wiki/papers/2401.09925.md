# Dense Hadronic Matter in Neutron Stars

> In this lecture we discuss the properties of dense hadronic matter inside neutron stars. In particular, we pay attention to the role of strangeness in the core of neutron stars, by analysing the presence of baryons and mesons with strangeness. We consider two interesting possible scenarios in their interior, that is, the existence of hyperons leading to the so-called hyperon puzzle and the presence of a kaon condensed phase inside neutron stars.

## Key claim
This lecture review argues that strangeness-bearing hadrons (hyperons and antikaons) are likely present in neutron-star cores, but their existence creates tensions with observed 2-solar-mass stars (the "hyperon puzzle") and with kaon-condensation scenarios, depending on whether microscopic or phenomenological theoretical models are used.

## Method
The paper surveys the theoretical and experimental status of dense hadronic matter in neutron stars, focusing on two strangeness scenarios: hyperonic matter and kaon condensation. It reviews constraints from nuclear experiments (e.g., heavy-ion collisions, hypernuclei) and neutron-star observations (masses, radii, gravitational-wave tidal deformability), and compares predictions from microscopic ab-initio approaches (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, chiral effective field theory) and phenomenological models (relativistic mean-field, Skyrme functionals) for the equation of state.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-star masses: typically 1–2 solar masses (M☉), radii about 10–12 km, average density ~10¹⁴ g/cm³.
- Nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15–0.16 fm⁻³; binding energy per nucleon at saturation (E/A)(ρ₀) = –16 ± 1 MeV.
- Incompressibility at saturation K₀ ~ 200–300 MeV; symmetry energy at saturation S₀ ~ 30 MeV.
- Observed neutron-star masses exceeding 2 M☉: PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432, and PSR J0740+6620 (2.14⁺⁰·¹⁰₋₀·₀₉ M☉).
- Hyperons appear at densities ≈ 2–3 ρ₀; Λ and Σ single-particle potentials become repulsive at 2–3 times saturation density.
- Ξ single-particle potential in nuclear matter: –3 to –5 MeV.
- Antikaon potential at saturation density: attractive, below 100 MeV (from microscopic unitarized schemes), with a significant imaginary part (width).

## Context
This lecture extends the long-standing line of work on strangeness in neutron stars initiated by Ambartsumyan and Saakyan (1960) and Kaplan and Nelson (1986). It reviews the hyperon puzzle, the tension between hyperon-softened equations of state and 2-solar-mass stars observed by Demorest (2010), Antoniadis (2013), and Cromartie (2019), and contrasts microscopic approaches (e.g., Haidenbauer 2014, 2016; Tolos 2006, 2008) with phenomenological models (e.g., Schaffner 1996; Gupta 2013). The paper enables future work by identifying open questions: whether hyperonic three-body forces or early quark-matter transitions resolve the hyperon puzzle, and whether kaon condensation is ruled out by microscopic calculations that predict smaller in-medium mass shifts.
