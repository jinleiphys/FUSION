# Nearly model-independent constraints on dense matter equation of state in a Bayesian approach

> We apply Bayesian approach to construct a large number of minimally constrained equations of state (EOSs) and study their correlations with a few selected properties of a neutron star (NS). Our set of minimal constraints includes a few basic properties of saturated nuclear matter and low-density pure neutron matter EOS which is obtained from a precise next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order (N$^{3}$LO) calculation in chiral effective field theory. The tidal deformability and radius of NS with mass $1-2 M_\odot$ are found to be strongly correlated with the pressure of $β$-equilibrated matter at densities higher than the saturation density ($ρ_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$) in a nearly model-independent manner. These correlations are employed to parametrize the pressure for $β$-equilibrated matter, around 2$ρ_0$, as a function of neutron star mass and the corresponding tidal deformability. The maximum mass of neutron star is also found to be strongly correlated with the pressure of $β$-equilibrated matter at densities $\sim 4.5ρ_0$.

## Key claim
The authors find that tidal deformability and radius of neutron stars with masses 1-2 solar masses are strongly correlated with the pressure of beta-equilibrated matter at densities above saturation density in a "nearly model-independent manner," but this correlation is demonstrated only for two specific expansion schemes (Taylor and n/3) and may break down for the maximum mass, which correlates with pressure at much higher densities (~4.5 rho_0).

## Method
The authors used a Bayesian approach to generate large ensembles of equations of state (EOSs) based on two different expansion schemes (Taylor expansion and n/3 expansion) for the energy per nucleon of asymmetric nuclear matter. They imposed minimal constraints including Gaussian priors on low-order nuclear matter parameters at saturation density and a likelihood function based on the pure neutron matter EOS from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order (N3LO) over the density range 0.08-0.16 fm^-3. The resulting EOSs were filtered for thermodynamic stability, causality, and the requirement that maximum neutron star mass exceeds 2 solar masses, then used to compute neutron star properties by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Prior for binding energy per nucleon: epsilon_0 = -16.0 +/- 0.3 MeV (Gaussian)
- Prior for incompressibility coefficient: K_0 = 240 +/- 50 MeV (Gaussian)
- Prior for symmetry energy coefficient: J_0 = 32.0 +/- 5 MeV (Gaussian)
- Prior for slope parameter L_0 = 50 +/- 50 MeV (Gaussian)
- Low-density pure neutron matter EOS used over density range rho = 0.08-0.16 fm^-3
- Correlation coefficient r ~ 0.9 between tidal deformability Lambda_1.4 and radius R_1.4
- Correlation coefficient r ~ 0.8 between Lambda_1.4 and R_2.07 for Taylor expansion; r ~ 0.7 for n/3 expansion
- Maximum mass from Taylor EOS: M_max = 2.48_{-0.07}^{+0.06} solar masses
- 90% confidence interval for Lambda_1.4: [70, 580] (from GW170817, cited for comparison)
- 90% confidence interval for R_1.4: [11.41, 13.61] km (from Miller 2021, cited for comparison)
- 90% confidence interval for R_2.07: [11.8, 13.1] km (from Riley 2021, cited for comparison)

## Context
This work extends the approach of Lattimer (2000), Maselli (2013), and Tsang (2019, 2020) who found correlations between tidal deformability and EOS at twice saturation density, but uses a Bayesian framework with minimal priors rather than specific nuclear models. The authors compare their results to constraints from the GW170817 gravitational-wave event (Abbott 2018, 2019), NICER X-ray observations of PSR J0030+0451 (Riley 2019, Miller 2019) and PSR J0740+6620 (Riley 2021), and the PREX-II neutron-skin measurement (Reed 2021). The parametrization of pressure around 2 rho_0 as a function of neutron star mass and tidal deformability enables future gravitational-wave observations to directly constrain the dense matter EOS without assuming a specific nuclear model.
