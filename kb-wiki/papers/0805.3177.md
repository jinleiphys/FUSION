# Low mass dilepton production at ultrarelativistic energies

> Dilepton production in $pp$ and $Au+Au$ nucleus-nucleus collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV as well as in $In+In$ and $Pb+Au$ at 158 A$\cdot$GeV is studied within the microscopic HSD transport approach. A comparison to the data from the PHENIX Collaboration at RHIC shows that standard in-medium effects of the $ρ, ω$ vector mesons - compatible with the NA60 data for $In+In$ at 158 A$\cdot$GeV and the CERES data for $Pb+Au$ at 158 A$\cdot$GeV - do not explain the large enhancement observed in the invariant mass regime from 0.2 to 0.5 GeV in $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV relative to $pp$ collisions.

## Key claim
Standard in-medium modifications of vector mesons (collisional broadening or dropping mass plus collisional broadening) that successfully describe the NA60 and CERES dilepton data at SPS energies do not explain the large enhancement observed in the invariant mass range 0.2 to 0.6 GeV in Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV relative to pp collisions; the authors attribute this additional low-mass enhancement to non-hadronic sources, possibly virtual gluon-Compton scattering.

## Method
The authors employed the off-shell version of the relativistic HSD (Hadron-String-Dynamics) transport approach, which incorporates nucleons, Δ's, N* resonances, hyperons, and mesons, with inelastic collisions described by the FRITIOF string model at high energies. They implemented three scenarios for the ρ and ω vector meson spectral functions: vacuum (free) spectral functions, a "collisional broadening" scenario where the width increases with baryon density, and a "dropping mass + collisional broadening" model where the pole mass shifts with density. The calculated dilepton yields were passed through the experimental acceptance filters and mass resolution routines of the PHENIX, NA60, and CERES collaborations for direct comparison.

## Key numbers
- In Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the HSD calculations with vacuum spectral functions underestimate the PHENIX data in the invariant mass range 0.2 to 0.6 GeV by an average factor of 3.
- For In+In collisions at 158 A·GeV, the NA60 data are well described by the "collisional broadening" scenario for ρ mesons in the invariant mass range up to 0.9 GeV.
- For Pb+Au collisions at 158 A·GeV (7% central), the CERES data agree with the "collisional broadening" scenario; the "dropping mass + collisional broadening" model also compares reasonably well up to invariant masses of 0.8 GeV.
- For Pb+Au collisions at 40 A·GeV (30% central), the experimental dilepton yields are normalized to <N_ch> = 216 in the pseudorapidity interval 2.1 ≤ η ≤ 2.65.
- For pp collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the HSD calculations well reproduce the PHENIX experimental spectrum, which is entirely described by meson Dalitz and direct decays plus open charm decays.

## Context
This work extends earlier HSD transport studies by Bratkovskaya, Cassing, and collaborators (1997, 1998, 2008) that described dilepton production at SIS and SPS energies, and it directly compares with the NA60 data for In+In at 158 A·GeV (2006) and the CERES data for Pb+Au at 40 and 158 A·GeV (with enhanced mass resolution). The results are very close to the calculated spectra from van Hees and Rapp (2008) and from Dusling and Zahed (2008). The failure of standard in-medium scenarios at RHIC energies enables the search for new dilepton sources from partonic degrees of freedom, such as quark-antiquark annihilation or virtual gluon-Compton scattering, which the authors plan to investigate further within the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) model.
