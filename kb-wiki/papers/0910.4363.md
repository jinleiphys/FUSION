# Finite-size effects and the search for the critical endpoint in heavy ion collisions

> We discuss how the finiteness of the system created in a heavy-ion collision affects possible signatures of the QCD critical endpoint. We show sizable results for the modifications of the chiral phase diagram at volume scales typically encountered in current heavy-ion collisions and address the applicability of finite-size scaling as a tool in the experimental search for the critical endpoint.

## Key claim

Finite-size effects in heavy-ion collisions produce sizable shifts in the chiral phase diagram and in the location of the QCD critical endpoint at volume scales typically encountered in current experiments, meaning that the actual phase diagram probed differs quantitatively from the thermodynamic-limit picture that most signatures assume.

## Method

The authors compute the effective potential of the linear sigma model with constituent quarks in the mean-field approximation, including one-loop quark thermal fluctuations. For a finite system of linear size \(L\), the momentum integral in the quark contribution is replaced by a discrete sum over spatial Fourier modes, with results compared for periodic boundary conditions (PBC) and antiperiodic boundary conditions (APC). The pseudocritical transition lines and the pseudocritical endpoint are then extracted as functions of \(L\).

## Key numbers

* For a system with \(L = 2\) fm and APC, the isentropic trajectories in the \(T\)-\(\mu\) plane show large discrepancies from the thermodynamic limit around and below the transition region.
* For PBC, the chemical potential of the pseudocritical endpoint is about \(30\%\) larger than for APC at the same small volume.
* Both boundary conditions reproduce the infinite-volume limit for \(L \gtrsim 10\) fm.
* Typical linear sizes in heavy-ion collisions are \(L \lesssim d_{\rm ion} \approx 10\)–\(15\) fm, corresponding to energies \(L^{-1} \gtrsim 13.1\)–\(19.7\) MeV, which are not negligible compared to the critical temperature scale \(T_c \lesssim 200\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of finite-volume effects in QCD (e.g., Gopie (1998), Spieles (1997), Fraga (2003), Braun (2004), Yamamoto (2009), Elze (1986), Bazavov (2007)) by focusing specifically on the chiral critical endpoint in heavy-ion collisions. The results suggest that finite-size scaling analysis, as used in condensed matter physics (e.g., for spin glass transitions in disordered Ising systems), could be applied to centrality-dependent heavy-ion data as a complementary tool to locate the genuine critical endpoint and its critical exponents. The authors note that dynamical effects (finite lifetime, causality) may further constrain the growth of correlated domains and could require replacing the system size with a "horizon" size in future analyses.
