# Toward a unified description of hadro- and photoproduction amplitudes

> The near-term objectives of the research program at the Data Analysis Center are established within the context of the existing partial wave analyses available through the online suite of analysis and database codes accessible through SAID, the Scattering Analysis Interactive Database. This presentation reviews the efforts to determine a model independent method to obtain sets of partial wave amplitudes for strong and electromagnetic reactions, the interpretation of the amplitudes in terms of the excited states of the nucleon, the role of new precision unpolarized and polarized data, and new developments aimed at determining the photoproduction mulitpoles in a unitary, coupled-channel approach. The Chew-Mandelstam technique is discussed and applied to the problem of the S-wave pion- and eta-photoproduction amplitudes. The resulting eta production amplitudes exhibit the expected resonant behavior near the eta production threshold. Application of this method to a unified description of the hadro- and photoproduction amplitudes is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Chew-Mandelstam approach to unify hadro- and photoproduction amplitudes, but the actual demonstration is limited to an exploratory study of only the S-wave pion- and eta-photoproduction multipoles, with the claim that the resulting eta production amplitudes "exhibit the expected resonant behavior near the eta production threshold" rather than a fully validated unified description.

## Method

The authors applied the Chew-Mandelstam parametrization, derived from the unitarity constraint on the S-matrix in the physical region (W > m_i + m_t), to the electromagnetic sector by generalizing the N/D representation. They constructed a form for the amplitude that incorporates multichannel hadronic rescattering effects consistent with unitarity, and applied this specifically to the S-wave pion- and eta-photoproduction multipoles. The analysis used the Heitler integral equation as the starting point, with the K-matrix taken to be analytic throughout the physical region except for possible isolated poles.

## Key numbers

- The study focuses on the center-of-mass energy region near W = 1535 MeV, where the eta production threshold lies
- The S-wave pion-photoproduction multipole E_{0+}^pi and the eta-photoproduction multipole E_{0+}^eta are the specific amplitudes analyzed
- The unitarity constraint involves channel thresholds at W = m_{sigma+}, with the density of states given by rho_sigma(kbar_sigma) = (pi kbar_sigma E_{sigma1} E_{sigma2})/W

## Context

This work extends the partial wave analyses available through the SAID (Scattering Analysis Interactive Database) online suite, building on previous two-channel treatments of eta photoproduction amplitudes by Green (1997) and Arndt (1998). The paper aims to develop a framework that can simultaneously analyze hadro- and electroproduction reactions, enabling the new precision data from electromagnetic facilities to "back-constrain" hadronic amplitudes that were conventionally determined only from hadroproduction data. The approach is contrasted with the MAID model and other phenomenological parametrizations.
