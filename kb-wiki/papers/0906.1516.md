# Coherent Neutrino Scattering

> We present a microscopic model for coherent pion production off nuclei induced by neutrinos. This model is built upon a model for single nucleon processes that goes beyond the usual Delta dominance by including non resonant background contributions. We include nuclear medium effects: medium corrections to Delta$ properties and outgoing pion absortion via an optical potential. This results in major modifications to cross sections for low energy experiments when compared with phenomenological models like Rein-Sehgal's.

# Coherent Neutrino Scattering

## Key claim

The authors present a microscopic model for coherent pion production off nuclei induced by neutrinos that goes beyond the usual Delta dominance by including non-resonant background contributions and nuclear medium effects, and they find that the Rein-Sehgal model becomes less reliable at the low neutrino energies relevant for MiniBooNE and T2K experiments, predicting cross sections around a factor of (1.2/0.9)^2 ~ 2 smaller than approaches assuming the Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Method

The model builds upon a microscopic description of the single nucleon process (nu N to l- N pi+) that includes the Delta(1232) pole mechanism plus background terms required by chiral symmetry: nucleon pole terms, contact term, pion pole contribution, and pion-in-flight term. The authors sum coherently the contributions of all nucleons in the initial nucleus modeled after a Fermi gas in Local Density Approximation, and include nuclear medium corrections to the Delta self-energy and outgoing pion absorption via an optical potential derived from a Klein-Gordon equation.

## Key numbers

- The axial form factor C5^A(0) was fit to ANL data giving a value of 0.867, significantly smaller than the value of about 1.2 deduced from the Goldberger-Treiman relation used in PCAC-based approaches like Rein-Sehgal
- The axial mass M_A Delta was fit to 0.985 GeV
- Including Delta in-medium self-energy and pion distortion reduces the total cross section by around 60% compared to plane wave impulse approximation
- The approximation of putting nucleons at rest results in a ~15% decrease of the total cross section
- For CC nu_mu + 12C at MiniBooNE, the flux-averaged cross section is 2.99 x 10^-40 cm^2 with a maximum neutrino energy of 1.45 GeV
- For NC nu_mu + 12C at MiniBooNE, the flux-averaged cross section is 1.97 x 10^-40 cm^2 with a maximum neutrino energy of 1.34 GeV
- Switching off the quasi-elastic contribution to the pion-nucleus optical potential gives a 20% enhancement in the MiniBooNE flux-averaged NC cross section (NC* entry: 2.38 x 10^-40 cm^2)
- For CC nu_mu + 12C at K2K, the flux-averaged cross section is 4.68 x 10^-40 cm^2 with a maximum neutrino energy of 1.80 GeV, lying well below the experimental upper bound of 7.7 x 10^-40 cm^2

## Context

This work extends the microscopic model for single nucleon pion production developed by Hernandez et al. (2007) to coherent nuclear processes, and improves upon earlier calculations by Alvarez-Ruso et al. (2007) by including nucleon motion beyond the Delta-hole approximation and correcting numerical inaccuracies. The model provides an alternative to the Rein-Sehgal model used in MiniBooNE analyses, showing that the RS model's assumption of parallel kinematics (t=0 approximation) becomes unreliable at neutrino energies below 2 GeV, and that the predicted E_pi (1-cos theta_pi) distribution is significantly narrower than the RS prediction, which may affect background subtraction in oscillation analyses. The results enable more reliable predictions for T2K and MiniBooNE experiments and highlight the need for proper treatment of pion distortion in coherent processes rather than Monte Carlo cascade algorithms.
