# Topological phases for bound states moving in a finite volume

> We show that bound states moving in a finite periodic volume have an energy correction which is topological in origin and universal in character. The topological volume corrections contain information about the number and mass of the constituents of the bound states. These results have broad applications to lattice calculations involving nucleons, nuclei, hadronic molecules, and cold atoms. We illustrate and verify the analytical results with several numerical lattice calculations.

## Key claim

Bound states moving in a finite periodic volume acquire an energy correction that is topological in origin and universal in character, with the correction depending on the number and mass of the constituents; however, the authors note that in asymptotically large volumes these corrections are exponentially small and can be neglected, but in practice lattice simulations often use volumes that are not asymptotically large.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for finite-volume energy corrections of moving bound states by combining Luscher's finite-volume formalism with phase factors arising from center-of-mass motion in a periodic cube. They verify the analytic results using lattice calculations of the triton in pionless effective field theory at leading order, and of fermion-dimer scattering for two-component fermions, using the Lanczos algorithm for sparse-matrix eigenvector iteration to compute energies on periodic lattices.

## Key numbers

- For the triton, the ratio Delta E_k(L)/Delta E_0(L) for k=(1,0,0) approaches tau(k,1/3)=0.500 in the large-L limit, with lattice values of 0.395 at L=6, 0.432 at L=7, and 0.458 at L=8
- For the triton with k=(1,1,0), the finite-volume energy correction Delta E_k(L) is 0.029 MeV at L=5, 0.006 MeV at L=6, 0.001 MeV at L=7, and 0.0002 MeV at L=8
- For the triton at rest with k=(0,0,0), the finite-volume energy correction Delta E_0(L) is -0.603 MeV at L=5, -0.169 MeV at L=6, -0.049 MeV at L=7, and -0.015 MeV at L=8
- The fermion-dimer scattering length from the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation is a_FD kappa_D = 1.17907(1) and the effective range parameter is r_FD kappa_D = -0.0383(3)
- The experimental quartet neutron-deuteron scattering length is 4a_nd = 6.35(2) fm, with ratio 4a_nd/3a_np = 1.17(1)

## Context

This work extends Luscher's finite-volume method (Luscher, 1986; Luscher, 1991) for two-body scattering to include topological phase corrections for moving bound states, and was adapted by Davoudi and Savage (2011) into a general method for reducing finite-volume errors using boosted frames. The results enable more accurate lattice calculations of scattering for composite bound states including nucleons on nuclei, hadronic molecules, and cold atomic systems, with the topological volume factor being essential for obtaining the correct continuum limit in fermion-dimer scattering calculations.
