# Functional Ideal Hydrodynamics incorporating Quantum-Field Theoretical Fluctuation

> We propose new ideal hydrodynamics in the function space which describes a fluid composed of the 1+1 dimensional real scalar field in the framework of the stochastic variational method (SVM). In the derivation, the thermal equilibrium is assumed to the internal state of fluid elements in the function space of the scalar-field configuration. The deterministic trajectory of the functional fluid element is related to the functional generalization of the Bohmian trajectory in relativistic quantum field theory. To find the correspondence relation to standard hydrodynamics, a further coarse-graining should be introduced. Thus functional hydrodynamics is regarded as a mesoscopic theory such as the Boltzmann equation in the dynamical hierarchy of many-body systems. Functional hydrodynamics reproduces the exact behaviors of relativistic quantum field theory in a certain limit. We thus expect that our theory is applicable to study the influence of quantum-field theoretical fluctuation in collective flows of produced particles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new "functional ideal hydrodynamics" for a 1+1 dimensional real scalar field, derived via the stochastic variational method (SVM), which reproduces exact relativistic quantum field theory behavior in a certain limit; however, the correspondence to standard hydrodynamics requires further coarse-graining, making this a mesoscopic theory analogous to the Boltzmann equation in the many-body hierarchy.

## Method

The authors discretize the 1+1 dimensional real scalar field into 2N spatial points, defining a 2N-dimensional function space of field configurations. They introduce functional fluid elements in this space, assume a local thermal equilibrium ansatz for their internal state using thermodynamics in function space, and apply the stochastic variational method (SVM) to the action of the functional fluid. The variation yields a momentum equation for the mean velocity functional, which includes a fluctuation-induced correction term proportional to the square of the noise intensity.

## Key numbers

* The spatial discretization uses 2N points with interval Δx = l/(2N), where l is the total system length with periodic boundary conditions.
* The noise intensity in the stochastic differential equations is parameterized by ν, with the diffusion coefficient given by ν/Δx in the functional Fokker-Planck equation.
* The fluctuation correction term in the momentum equation is proportional to 2ν²/(Δx)² times a functional derivative of the quantum potential-like term involving √ρ_F.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic variational method previously applied to particle systems and continuum media (Yasue 1979, Zambrini 1985, Koide 2012) to a field-theoretical system. It builds on thermodynamics in function space developed in Koide and Kodama (2022) for the scalar field. The functional Bohmian trajectory generalizes the Bohmian trajectory from quantum mechanics (Holland 1993) to relativistic quantum field theory, and the theory is expected to enable studies of quantum-field theoretical fluctuations in collective flows from relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
