# Symmetry properties of pair correlations in heavy deformed nuclei

> Nucleons are known to form pairing correlations with various types of spin-symmetries. Spin-singlet neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairing is abundant in the nuclear chart but spin-triplet and mixed-spin proton-neutron pairing correlations have also been predicted to form at least in the ground states of certain nuclei. A realistic candidate region is that of the lightest Lanthanides where it was recently demonstrated that the nuclear deformation expected to emerge enhances spin-triplet pairing correlations. In this paper we provide the details of the deformed multimodal Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov theory that lead to this conclusion, as well as the details of the effects identified. We present in detail the response of different pairing correlations to various deformation modes and calculate their signatures in the odd-even staggering of masses. This paper provides a detailed discussion, and some resolutions, on the long-standing question ``what is the effect of nuclear deformation on the various pairing correlations?''

# Symmetry properties of pair correlations in heavy deformed nuclei

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that spin-triplet and mixed-spin proton-neutron pairing correlations in the light Lanthanides region are not artifacts of neglecting nuclear deformation, but the analysis is restricted to quadrupole-type deformation only (ε=0 in the Cassini oval parametrization), leaving the effect of more extreme elongation modes for future work.

## Method
The authors develop a deformed multimodal Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) theory using an axially symmetric Woods-Saxon potential parametrized via Cassini ovals, with a zero-range pairing interaction acting in six distinct spin-isospin channels (three spin-singlet isovector and three spin-triplet isoscalar channels). The HFB ground state is found via gradient descent in the space of Bogolyubov quasiparticle states, with constraints applied to target specific pairing symmetries. Pairing amplitudes are defined for each channel using antisymmetrized channel amplitudes, and the spin-symmetry character of each HFB state is classified by a normalized spin-singlet amplitude x_S.

## Key numbers
- The pairing interaction is defined with 6 distinct spin-isospin channels labeled α=0 through 5, corresponding to spin-singlet (T=1, T_z=1,0,-1) and spin-triplet (S=1, S_z=1,0,-1) channels.
- The classification thresholds for pairing symmetry are: x_S^2 ≥ 4/5 for singlet, 1/5 < x_S^2 < 4/5 for mixed, and x_S^2 ≤ 1/5 for triplet.
- The study focuses on the light Lanthanides region with mass number A ~ 130, near the N=Z line.
- The pairing matrix blocks have dimensions 4N_n × 4N_n for |j_z| < j_z_max, and 2N_n × 2N_n for |j_z| = j_z_max.

## Context
This work extends the mean-field descriptions of pairing correlations in heavy deformed nuclei beyond previous approaches that either ignored deformation entirely, limited deformation to quadrupole modes only (Gambacurta 2015), or allowed only spin-singlet pairing (Marevic 2022, Doba 2021). The paper builds on the authors' earlier work (Palkanoglou 2025) that first demonstrated spin-triplet and mixed-spin pairing survive in deformed light Lanthanides. The formalism enables future studies of pairing signatures in odd-even staggering of masses, which could be compared to precision mass measurements, and can be extended to treat extreme deformations appearing in fissioning nuclei.
