# Cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory: Superdeformation in the $A\sim 190$ mass region

> A systematic investigation of the yrast superdeformed (SD) rotational bands in even-even nuclei of the $A\sim 190$ mass region has been performed within the framework of the cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory. The particle-hole channel of this theory is treated fully relativistically, while a finite range two-body force of Gogny type is used in the particle-particle (pairing) channel. Using the well established parameter sets NL1 for the Lagrangian and D1S for the Gogny force, very good description of experimental data is obtained with no adjustable parameters.

## Key claim

The cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory, using the NL1 parameter set for the Lagrangian and D1S for the Gogny force with no adjustable parameters, provides a very good description of experimental data for yrast superdeformed rotational bands in even-even nuclei of the A∼190 mass region, with the exception of the yrast SD band in 198Pb where further investigation of odd nuclei is needed.

## Method

The particle-hole channel was treated fully relativistically at the Hartree level, while the particle-particle (pairing) channel used a finite-range two-body Gogny force (D1S parameter set). Approximate particle number projection was performed via the Lipkin-Nogami method. The calculations were cranked (rotating frame) and applied to yrast superdeformed rotational bands in even-even nuclei of the A∼190 mass region.

## Key numbers

- Transition quadrupole moment Q_t = 19.6 e b calculated for the yrast SD band in 192Hg at rotational frequency Ω_x = 0.1 MeV.
- Maximal scalar density reached along the symmetry axis at a distance of 6–8 fm from the center of the nucleus for 192Hg.
- The yrast SD bands in 190,192Hg, 196,198Pb, and 198Po are not linked to the low-spin level scheme, so their experimental spin values were established by comparison with calculated kinematic moments of inertia.

## Context

This work extends the cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) theory, which neglected pairing correlations, by incorporating pairing via the Gogny force within a Hartree-Bogoliubov framework. The CRHB results agree with experimental transition quadrupole moments within error bars, as detailed in Afanasjev and Ring (2000). The study enables future investigations of odd and odd-odd nuclei to better understand the discrepancies seen in 198Pb and the role of pairing in superdeformed bands.
