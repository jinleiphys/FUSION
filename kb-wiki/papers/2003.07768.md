# Predictions for charmed nuclei based on $Y_c N$ forces inferred from lattice QCD simulations

> Charmed nuclei are investigated utilizing $Λ_c N$ and $Σ_c N$ interactions that have been extrapolated from lattice QCD simulations at unphysical masses of $m_π= 410$--$570$ MeV to the physical point using chiral effective field theory as guideline. Calculations of the energies of $Λ_c$ single-particle bound states for various charmed nuclei from $^{\ 5}_{Λ_c}$Li to $^{209}_{Λ_c}$Bi are performed using a perturbative many-body approach. This approach allows one to determine the finite nuclei $Λ_c$ self-energy from which the energies of the different bound states can be obtained. Though the $Λ_c N$ interaction inferred from the lattice results is only moderately attractive, it supports the existence of charmed nuclei. Already the lightest nucleus considered is found to be bound. The spin-orbit splitting of the p- and d-wave states turns out to be small, as in the case of single $Λ$ hypernuclei. Additional calculations based on the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations suggest that also $A=4$ systems involving a $Λ_c$ baryon are likely to be bound, but exclude a bound $^{\, 3}_{Λ_c}$He state.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that charmed nuclei (bound states of a Λ_c baryon with ordinary nuclei) are likely to exist, but only for systems with mass number A ≥ 4, and the binding is weak (separation energies of order 100–400 keV) because the Λ_c N interaction inferred from lattice QCD is only moderately attractive.

## Method
The authors extrapolated Λ_c N and Σ_c N interactions from lattice QCD simulations at unphysical pion masses (m_π = 410–570 MeV) to the physical point using chiral effective field theory as a guide. For light systems (A=3 and A=4), they solved Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations with full Coulomb interactions. For heavier nuclei from ⁵_Λ_c Li to ²⁰⁹_Λ_c Bi, they used a perturbative many-body approach to compute the Λ_c self-energy and single-particle bound states.

## Key numbers
- Λ_c N scattering lengths: a(¹S₀) = -0.85 to -1.00 fm and a(³S₁) = -0.79 to -0.98 fm, depending on cutoff (Λ = 500–600 MeV)
- Λ_c single-particle potential depth in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density (k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹): U_Λ_c(k_Λ_c=0) ≈ -20 to -18 MeV
- Λ_c separation energy for ⁴_Λ_c He (J=1⁺ state): 0.11 to 0.37 MeV, depending on cutoff
- Λ_c separation energy for ⁴_Λ_c He (J=0⁺ state): 0.10 to 0.18 MeV for cutoff 600 MeV; no bound state found for cutoff 500 MeV
- No bound state found for ³_Λ_c He with any of the interactions considered

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous extrapolation of HAL QCD lattice results for Λ_c N interactions (Haidenbauer, 2018) to predictions for finite charmed nuclei. It contradicts earlier phenomenological predictions based on meson-exchange models and SU(4) flavor symmetry (Bando, 1985; Liu, 2012; Vidana, 2019) that found stronger attraction, and also differs from quark model results (Garcilazo, 2015; Garcilazo, 2019) that predicted bound A=3 systems. The results enable experimental searches for charmed nuclei at facilities like LHC, RHIC, J-PARC, and FAIR, and suggest that only systems with A ≥ 4 are likely to be observed.
