# Nuclear Energy Density Optimization

> We carry out state-of-the-art optimization of a nuclear energy density of Skyrme type in the framework of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory. The particle-hole and particle-particle channels are optimized simultaneously, and the experimental data set includes both spherical and deformed nuclei. The new model-based, derivative-free optimization algorithm used in this work has been found to be significantly better than standard optimization methods in terms of reliability, speed, accuracy, and precision. The resulting parameter set UNEDFpre results in good agreement with experimental masses, radii, and deformations and seems to be free of finite-size instabilities. An estimate of the reliability of the obtained parameterization is given, based on standard statistical methods. We discuss new physics insights offered by the advanced covariance analysis.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a new model-based, derivative-free optimization algorithm (POUNDERS) significantly outperforms standard optimization methods for fitting Skyrme energy density functional parameters, but the resulting UNEDFpre parameter set is presented as a proof-of-principle rather than a final universal parameterization, with the caveat that the fit focuses on heavy nuclei and global properties like masses and radii rather than spectroscopic detail.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous optimization of the particle-hole and particle-particle channels of a Skyrme-type nuclear energy density functional within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework, using a derivative-free optimization algorithm (POUNDERS) to minimize a chi-squared objective function. The experimental data set comprised binding energies of 44 deformed and 28 spherical even-even nuclei, proton rms radii for the 28 spherical nuclei, and neutron and proton odd-even mass staggering values, with calculations performed using the HFBTHO solver for axially deformed nuclei and the HFODD solver for spherical nuclei. Seven of the 13 Skyrme coupling constants were expressed in terms of nuclear matter properties (saturation density, energy per nucleon, incompressibility, isoscalar effective mass, symmetry energy, symmetry energy slope, and isovector effective mass) to provide physically motivated search ranges, while the remaining six parameters (isoscalar and isovector gradient, spin-orbit, and tensor terms) were varied freely.

## Context

This work extends the line of Skyrme energy density functional optimization that includes parameterizations such as SLy4 (Cha95), SkM* (Bar82), and the Brussels-Montreal MSk and HFB series (Ton00, Gor07), which were historically limited to spherical nuclei and nuclear matter properties due to computational cost. The authors build on recent efforts to apply statistical methods for error analysis and parameter correlations (Ber05, Kor08, Toi08, Klu09, Rei10) and enable the inclusion of deformed nuclei in the fit through high-performance computing resources. The resulting methodology and UNEDFpre parameterization pave the way toward a universal nuclear energy density functional of spectroscopic quality, with the next steps being the inclusion of odd-mass systems, excited states, and symmetry-restored calculations.
