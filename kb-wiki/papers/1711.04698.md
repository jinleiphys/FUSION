# Recent Applications of Self-Consistent Green's Function Theory to Nuclei

> We discuss recent \emph{ab initio} calculations based on self-consistent Green's function theory. It is found that a simple extension of the formalism to account for two-nucleon scattering outside the model space allows to calculate non-soft interactions. With this, it is possible to make predictions for Lattice QCD potentials, obtained so far at pion masses of $m_π= 0.47$GeV/c$^2$. More traditional calculations that use saturating chiral EFT forces yield a good description of nuclear responses and nucleon knockout spectroscopy.

# Recent Applications of Self-Consistent Green's Function Theory to Nuclei

## Key claim
Self-consistent Green's function theory, extended with a Bethe-Goldstone resummation of two-nucleon scattering outside the model space, can handle non-soft interactions such as Lattice QCD potentials at pion mass m_π = 0.47 GeV/c², but the resulting binding energies for ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca show systematic deviations from experiment that may exceed conservative 10% error estimates from many-body truncations.

## Method
The authors solve the Dyson equation for the one-body propagator using the third-order algebraic diagrammatic construction [ADC(3)] approximation, which resums 2p1h and 2h1p configurations non-perturbatively. For interactions with strong short-range repulsion (HAL QCD potentials), they additionally solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation in the excluded space to generate a T-matrix, extract a static effective interaction via Hartree-Fock, and then compute ADC(3) correlations within the included model space. For chiral EFT forces (NNLO_sat), they compute the isovector dipole response by solving the random phase approximation (RPA) in the particle-hole channel starting from the same mean-field propagator used as reference state.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy of ⁴He with HAL469 potential: 4.80(3) MeV (converged binding energy from IR extrapolation), benchmarked against exact Stochastic Variational result of -5.09 MeV
- Ground state energy of ¹⁶O with HAL469 potential: -17.9(0.3)(1.8) MeV (first error from IR extrapolation, second from estimated 10% many-body truncation error); ¹⁶O is unstable against 4-α breakup by ~2.5 MeV
- Ground state energy of ⁴⁰Ca with HAL469 potential: -75.4(6.7)(7.5) MeV; stable against α breakup by ~24 MeV
- Dipole polarizability α_D for ¹⁶O (NNLO_sat): 0.50 fm² (SCGF), compared to 0.57(1) fm² (CC-LIT) and 0.585(9) fm² (experiment)
- Dipole polarizability α_D for ⁴⁸Ca (NNLO_sat): 2.08 fm² (SCGF), compared to 2.45 fm² (CC-LIT) and 2.07(22) fm² (experiment)
- Dipole polarizability α_D for ⁴⁰Ca (NNLO_sat): 1.79 fm² (SCGF), compared to 1.87 fm² (CC-LIT) and 1.87(3) fm² (experiment)
- Dipole polarizability α_D for ²²O (NNLO_sat): 0.72 fm² (SCGF), compared to 0.86(4) fm² (CC-LIT) and 0.43(4) fm² (experiment)

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent Green's function approach to handle hard-core interactions from Lattice QCD (HAL QCD collaboration, Ishii 2006, Aoki 2012, Doi 2011) by incorporating ladder resummation outside the model space, following techniques from Hjorth-Jensen (1995) and Engeland (2008). The dipole response calculations using NNLO_sat (Ekstrom 2015) are compared to Coupled Cluster with Lorentz Integral Transform results (PhysRevC.94.034317, PhysRevLett.118.252501) and experimental photoabsorption data (Ahrens 1975, Ishkhanov). The spectroscopic factor analysis for oxygen isotopes shows that NNLO_sat yields results close to phenomenologically constrained FRPA calculations (Barbieri 2002) and agrees with (p,2p) measurements from R³B at GSI (Aumann, this proceedings). The method enables ab initio predictions for Lattice QCD-derived nuclear forces and provides a framework for extending calculations beyond RPA to improve agreement with experimental dipole polarizabilities.
