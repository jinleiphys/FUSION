# Strong LHCb evidence supporting the existence of the hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks

> On March 26th, 2019, at the Rencontres de Moriond QCD conference, the LHCb Collaboration reported the observation of three new pentaquarks, namely $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$, which are consistent with the loosely bound molecular hidden-charm pentaquark states composed of an S-wave charmed baryon $Σ_c$ and an S-wave anti-charmed meson ($\bar{D}, \bar{D}^*$). In this work, we present a direct calculation by the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model and demonstrate explicitly that the $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ do correspond to the loosely bound $Σ_c\bar{D}$ with $(I=1/2,J^P=1/2^-)$, $Σ_c\bar{D}^*$ with $(I=1/2,J^P=1/2^-)$ and $Σ_c\bar{D}^*$ with $(I=1/2,J^P=3/2^-)$, respectively.

## Key claim
The three pentaquarks observed by LHCb in 2019 (Pc(4312), Pc(4440), Pc(4457)) correspond exactly to the loosely bound molecular states predicted by the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model for the ΣcD̅ and ΣcD̅* systems with isospin I=1/2, but only when the cutoff parameter Λ is tuned to specific values (1.04 GeV for the two lower states and 1.32 GeV for the highest state).

## Method
The authors employed the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model, including coupled-channel effects and S-D wave mixing, to calculate effective potentials for the ΣcD̅ and ΣcD̅* systems. They constructed effective Lagrangians based on chiral symmetry, heavy quark symmetry, and hidden local symmetry, then solved the Schrödinger equation to obtain binding energies and wave functions for the molecular states with isospin I=1/2.

## Key numbers
- Pc(4312)+: mass = 4311.9 ± 0.7 +6.8/-0.6 MeV, width = 9.8 ± 2.7 +3.7/-4.5 MeV (LHCb data)
- Pc(4440)+: mass = 4440.3 ± 1.3 +4.1/-4.7 MeV, width = 20.6 ± 4.9 +8.7/-10.1 MeV (LHCb data)
- Pc(4457)+: mass = 4457.3 ± 1.3 +0.6/-4.1 MeV, width = 6.4 ± 2.0 +5.7/-1.9 MeV (LHCb data)
- For Pc(4312) as ΣcD̅ with I=1/2, J^P=1/2^-: binding energy = -8.00 MeV, RMS radius = 1.22 fm, with cutoff Λ = 1.04 GeV
- For Pc(4440) as ΣcD̅* with I=1/2, J^P=1/2^-: binding energy = -19.27 MeV, RMS radius = 0.88 fm, with cutoff Λ = 1.04 GeV
- For Pc(4457) as ΣcD̅* with I=1/2, J^P=3/2^-: binding energy = -4.38 MeV, RMS radius = 1.61 fm, with cutoff Λ = 1.32 GeV
- The mass difference between the two higher peaks and the lowest Pc(4312) peak is approximately 140 MeV, matching the D and D* meson mass difference

## Context
This work extends the OBE model predictions for ΣcD̅ and ΣcD̅* molecular pentaquarks originally made by Yang et al. (2011), which was one of the pioneering papers exploring hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks. The 2015 LHCb observation of Pc(4380) and Pc(4450) (Aaij et al., 2015) had stimulated extensive debate about whether these states were loosely bound molecules or tightly bound pentaquarks. The 2019 LHCb data resolves this ambiguity by showing that the three new states match the molecular predictions, and the authors also identify a predicted Σc*D̅ state with mass 4379.11 MeV that may correspond to the previously observed Pc(4380). The work enables future searches for isospin I=3/2 partners decaying to J/ψ Δ(1232) rather than J/ψ p.
