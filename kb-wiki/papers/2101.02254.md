# Net-baryon number fluctuations

> The appearance of large, none-Gaussian cumulants of the baryon number distribution is commonly discussed as a signal for the QCD critical point. We review the status of the Taylor expansion of cumulant ratios of baryon number fluctuations along the freeze-out line and also compare QCD results with the corresponding proton number fluctuations as measured by the STAR Collaboration at RHIC. To further constrain the location of a possible QCD critical point we discuss poles of the baryon number fluctuations in the complex plane. Here we use not only the Taylor coefficients obtained at zero chemical potential but perform also calculations of Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure at purely imaginary chemical potentials.

# Net-baryon number fluctuations

## Key claim

The paper reviews the status of Taylor expansion of cumulant ratios of baryon number fluctuations along the freeze-out line and compares lattice QCD results with net-proton number fluctuations measured by STAR, finding no indication of a diverging baryon number fluctuation in the range where the Taylor expansion is trusted; however, the authors note that higher-order cumulants and poles of baryon number fluctuations in the complex plane may further constrain the location of a possible QCD critical point, with preliminary results from imaginary chemical potential calculations showing agreement with model predictions for the Lee-Yang edge singularity.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD calculations using (2+1)-flavor of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) at physical light and strange quark masses on lattices of size 32^3 × 8 and 48^3 × 12, computing Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure up to 8th order in the baryon chemical potential. They also perform simulations at purely imaginary chemical potential on 24^3 × 4 lattices to construct Padé approximants of the baryon number density, and compare their results with the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model and with experimental data from the STAR Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical transition temperature at physical masses: T_pc = 156(±1.5) MeV (Bazavov 2018)
- Critical temperature in the chiral limit: T_c = 132^{+3}_{-6} MeV (Ding 2019)
- Curvature coefficient of the pseudo-critical line: κ_2^B = 0.012(4) (Bazavov 2018)
- Roberge-Weiss temperature for N_τ = 4: T_RW = 201 MeV (Goswami 2018)
- The HRG model deviates from QCD for μ_B ≳ 150 MeV along the pseudo-critical line
- A high freeze-out temperature of T^f > 155 MeV appears excluded by the STAR data
- For T > 140 MeV, many expansion coefficients turn negative, providing an upper bound for the critical endpoint temperature T_cep

## Context

This work extends the program of comparing lattice QCD cumulant ratios to experimental heavy-ion collision data, following the procedure proposed by Bazavov (2012) for direct comparison without freeze-out model dependence. The results are compared with net-proton number fluctuations measured by the STAR Collaboration (Adam 2020, Pandav 2020) and with the HRG model. The paper also builds on previous determinations of the pseudo-critical line (Bazavov 2018, Borsanyi 2020) and the chiral transition temperature (Ding 2019). The use of imaginary chemical potential calculations to construct Padé approximants and locate singularities in the complex plane enables future constraints on the QCD critical point location, with model predictions based on mapping to the universal 3d O(2)-model (Connelly 2020).
