# Nonlocal optical potential with core excitation in ${}^{10}\mathrm{Be}(d,p){}^{11}\mathrm{Be}$ and ${}^{11}\mathrm{Be}(p,d){}^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ reactions

> We propose a new nonlocal form of the nucleon-nucleus optical potential and demonstrate its reliability. We extend the nonlocal potential to include the excitation of the nuclear core and develop energy-independent roton-${}^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ potential reasonably reproducing the experimental data at low energies. We apply the new potential to the study of deuteron stripping and pickup reactions ${}^{10}\mathrm{Be}(d,p){}^{11}\mathrm{Be}$ and ${}^{11}\mathrm{Be}(p,d){}^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ using rigorous three-body Faddeev-type equations for transition operators that are solved in the momentum-space partial-wave framework. The achieved description of the experimental data is considerably more successful as compared to previous studies with local potentials. The values of spectroscopic factors consistent with the data are determined, exhibiting only weak energy dependence. The results possibly indicate an increased predicting power of the proposed calculational scheme.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new energy-independent nonlocal nucleon-nucleus optical potential that includes core excitation, and demonstrates that it provides a considerably more successful description of experimental data for deuteron stripping and pickup reactions on beryllium isotopes compared to previous studies using local potentials, though the spectroscopic factors require a slight readjustment (approximately 5%) at the highest energy studied.

## Method

The authors solve rigorous three-body Faddeev-type (Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas) equations for transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave framework, extended to include dynamical quadrupole excitation of the 10Be core via a rotational model. They develop a new nonlocal potential form (Eq. Vdj) that allows separate partial-wave and multipole expansion of the nonlocality function and the deformed local part, fit its parameters to elastic and inelastic p+10Be scattering data at multiple energies, and apply it to 10Be(d,p)11Be and 11Be(p,d)10Be reactions.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for 11Be ground state (1/2+): S(0+,1/2+) = 0.78 ± 0.04, with weak energy dependence (5% uncertainty at 15 MeV, 3% at 21 MeV)
- Spectroscopic factor for 11Be excited state (1/2-): S(0+,1/2-) = 0.65 ± 0.01
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β2 for 10Be: ranges between 0.71 and 0.78 from fit to p+10Be scattering data
- Binding energies reproduced: 0.504 MeV for 11Be ground state, 0.184 MeV for excited state
- Example potential parameters: V_V = 91.17 MeV, W_V = 1.35 MeV, W_S = 7.61 MeV, r_V = 1.14 fm, r_W = 1.26 fm, a_V = 0.50 fm, a_W = 0.65 fm, β2 = 0.72
- Asymptotic normalization coefficient for ground state: 0.79 ± 0.02 fm^{-1/2}; for excited state: 0.149 ± 0.005 fm^{-1/2}
- Energies studied: deuteron beam energies 15, 21.4, and 40.9 MeV (the latter via time-reversed reaction at Ep = 35.3 MeV/nucleon)

## Context

This work extends earlier Faddeev-type calculations with core excitation by Deltuva (2013, 2017) that were limited to local potentials, and addresses the shortcoming that standard local optical potentials like Chapel Hill 89 (CH89) or Koning and Delaroche (KD) do not accurately describe p+10Be scattering data. The new nonlocal potential enables energy-independent parametrization that reproduces data over a broader energy range, and the results suggest increased predictive power compared to earlier local-potential calculations and ADWA approaches that showed stronger energy dependence in extracted spectroscopic factors.
