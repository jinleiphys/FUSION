# Threshold effects in relativistic gases

> Particle multiplicities and ratios in the microcanonical ensemble of relativistic gases near production thresholds are studied. It is shown that the ratio of heavy to light particle multiplicity may be enhanced in comparison to its thermodynamic limit.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, contrary to naive expectations, the exact conservation of energy and momentum in the microcanonical ensemble (MCE) can lead to an *enhancement* of heavy particle multiplicity relative to the grand canonical ensemble (GCE) near production thresholds, rather than a monotonic suppression. This enhancement is conditional on the system energy being just above the threshold for heavy particle production and is most pronounced for lighter heavy particles.

## Method

The authors analytically studied a simplified system of massless (neutral and charged) and non-relativistic heavy Boltzmann particles, calculating MCE partition functions and mean multiplicities via sums over fixed-multiplicity phase-space volumes. They then performed Monte Carlo importance-sampling calculations for a full ideal hadron-resonance gas (including all species up to ~1.8 GeV, with Boltzmann statistics and fixed resonance masses), comparing MCE and canonical ensemble (CE) results to GCE results at the same energy density (0.3895 GeV/fm³, corresponding to T=160 MeV in the GCE).

## Key numbers

- For the analytical model with heavy particle mass M=0.7 GeV and T=160 MeV, the MCE/GCE ratio for heavy particles shows "strong oscillations" and an enhancement (ratio > 1) near threshold, with the enhancement increasing strongly as M decreases.
- For the full hadron-resonance gas, the MCE/GCE ratio for the η meson (second lightest neutral meson) shows an enhancement of about a factor of ~10 near its production threshold.
- For the J/ψ meson in the full hadron gas, the MCE/GCE ratio shows an enhancement of about a factor of ~3.5 near its production threshold.
- The energy density used for comparisons across ensembles was fixed to 0.3895 GeV/fm³.

## Context

This work extends the study of "canonical suppression" (the reduction of heavy charged hadron yields in the CE due to exact charge conservation, studied for strange particles by Becattini (2004) and for open charm by Gorenstein (2001)) by introducing exact energy-momentum conservation via the MCE. The results contradict the naive expectation that energy conservation would simply add further suppression; instead, it produces rich threshold structures and enhancements. The paper enables future statistical-model analyses of hadron production in small systems (e.g., elementary collisions) where MCE effects are significant, and suggests that the non-monotonic energy dependence of MCE yields could be related to collision-energy-dependent experimental ratios.
