# Application of efficient generator-coordinate subspace-selection algorithm to neutrinoless double-$β$ decay

> The generator coordinate method begins with the variational construction of a set of non-orthogonal mean-field states that span a subspace of the full many-body Hilbert space. These states are then often projected onto states with good quantum numbers to restore symmetries, leading to a set with members that can be similar to one another, and it is sometimes possible to reduce this set without greatly affecting results. Here we propose a greedy algorithm that we call the energy-transition-orthogonality procedure (ENTROP) to select subsets of important states. As applied here, the approach selects on the basis of diagonal energy, orthogonality, and contribution to the matrix element that governs neutrinoless double-$β$ decay. We present both shell-model and preliminary ab initio calculations of this matrix element for the decay of $^{76}$Ge, with quadrupole deformation parameters and the isoscalar pairing strength as generator coordinates. ENTROP converges quickly, reducing significantly the number of basis states needed for an accurate calculation.

## Key claim

The energy-transition-orthogonality procedure (ENTROP) can select a small subset of generator-coordinate basis states that reproduces the full neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element (NME) for $^{76}$Ge, but the algorithm requires tuning a cutoff parameter $L_c$ on small subspace pairs and assumes the same cutoff will work in the full space, an assumption that "cannot be rigorously justified but is reasonable."

## Method

The authors implement a greedy algorithm that orders projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) quasiparticle vacua by their diagonal energies, then iteratively selects states from initial and final nuclei based on low diagonal energy, inclusion of "partner" states with the same generator coordinates in the other nucleus, and a linear-independence criterion measured by the squared projection length $L$ of a candidate state onto the already-selected subspace. The cutoff parameter $L_c$ is determined by testing the procedure on small subspace pairs (e.g., the first 20, 40, and 60 states) and choosing the smallest value that reproduces the NME in all pairs. The method is applied to $^{76}$Ge decay using both a shell-model valence-space calculation with the GCN2850 interaction and an ab initio calculation with an IMSRG-evolved chiral NN+3N interaction in seven major shells ($e_{\text{max}}=6$), using quadrupole deformation parameters ($\hat{Q}_{20}$, $\hat{Q}_{22}$) and isoscalar pairing strength ($\hat{P}_0$) as generator coordinates.

## Key numbers

- In the shell-model test with the GCN2850 interaction, a cutoff $L_c = 0.995$ was the smallest value that reproduced the full NME in subspace pairs of 20, 40, and 60 states; with this cutoff, the NME converged after approximately 20 combined states from both nuclei.
- In the ab initio calculation with $e_{\text{max}}=6$, a cutoff $L_c = 0.902$ was sufficient to obtain the correct NME within subspace pairs of 20, 30, and 40 total states; the NME converged by about 20 states from the two nuclei combined.
- The ab initio NME components at $e_{\text{max}}=6$ were: $M_{0\nu}^{GT} = 2.68$, $-\frac{g_V^2}{g_A^2} M_{0\nu}^{F} = 0.65$, and $M_{0\nu}^{T} = -0.16$, using $g_A = 1.27$.
- A recent valence-space IMSRG calculation (Belley 2021) with the same chiral interaction and $e_{\text{max}}=6$ obtained $M_{0\nu}^{GT} = 2.76$, $g_V^2/g_A^2 M_{0\nu}^{F} = 0.54$, and $M_{0\nu}^{T} = -0.49$, with the main difference being in the tensor component.
- In the shell-model test, ENTROP reduced computation time by more than a factor of 100 compared to the full GCM calculation with 184 quasiparticle vacua.

## Context

This work extends the generator coordinate method (GCM) approach to neutrinoless double-beta decay NMEs previously applied to $^{48}$Ca by Yao (2020) and to $^{76}$Ge by Belley (2021), and builds on the GCM treatment of triaxial deformation and isoscalar pairing in $^{76}$Ge by Jiao (2017). The ENTROP algorithm is contrasted with an alternative subspace-selection procedure based on imaginary-time evolution from random starting points, originally developed by Nakatsukasa (2007) for energy-density-functional calculations of collective spectra, which the authors find cannot reproduce the NME as accurately because it does not ensure similarity between states in the initial and final nuclei. The method enables the addition of new generator coordinates to GCM calculations that would otherwise be computationally prohibitive, particularly for ab initio frameworks where full calculations with three generator coordinates in a large single-particle space are too time-consuming to carry out.
