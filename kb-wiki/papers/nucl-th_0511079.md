# Elliptic flow of thermal photons in relativistic nuclear collisions

> We predict the transverse momentum (pT) dependence of elliptic flow of thermal photons for Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. We model the system hydrodynamically, assuming formation of a thermalized quark-gluon plasma at some early time, followed by cooling through expansion, hadronization and decoupling. Photons are emitted throughout the expansion history. Contrary to hadron elliptic flow, which hydrodynamics predicts to increase monotonically with pT, the elliptic flow of thermal photons is predicted to first rise and then fall again as pT increases. Photon elliptic flow at high pT is shown to reflect the quark momentum anisotropy at early times when it is small, whereas at low pT it is controlled by the much larger pion momentum anisotropy during the late hadronic emission stage. An interesting structure is predicted at intermediate pT ~ 0.4 GeV/c where photon elliptic flow reflects the momenta and the (compared to pions) reduced v2 of heavy vector mesons in the late hadronic phase.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the elliptic flow of thermal photons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC does not increase monotonically with transverse momentum like hadron elliptic flow, but instead first rises and then falls. The authors qualify that this hydrodynamic prediction should be considered an upper limit for photon elliptic flow once pT exceeds 1 GeV/c, where viscous corrections become significant.

## Method

The authors used the boost-invariant hydrodynamic code AZHYDRO to model the space-time evolution of the fireball, starting from an initial time of 0.2 fm/c with a peak initial temperature of 520 MeV. They integrated thermal photon emission rates from the quark-gluon plasma (using leading-order QCD rates) and from the hadronic gas (using rates for processes like ππ → ργ) over the entire expansion history, and then extracted the elliptic flow coefficient v2 as a function of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Peak initial temperature of T0 = 520 MeV for b=0 collisions, corresponding to an initial maximum entropy density of s0 = 351 fm^-3
- Critical temperature for the QGP to hadron gas transition: Tc = 164 MeV, with energy densities εq(Tc) = 1.6 GeV/fm^3 and εh(Tc) = 0.45 GeV/fm^3
- Hadron freeze-out energy density: εf = 0.075 GeV/fm^3
- An interesting structure in photon elliptic flow is predicted at intermediate pT ~ 0.4 GeV/c, where hadronic photon flow transitions from tracking pion v2 to tracking the reduced v2 of heavier vector mesons
- The QGP photon elliptic flow peaks around pT ~ 1.5 GeV/c
- The hadronic contribution to the photon spectrum is suppressed by more than an order of magnitude below the QGP contribution once pT exceeds 1.5-2 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic calculations of elliptic flow, previously applied to hadrons by Heinz and collaborators (2004), to the case of thermal photons. The prediction that photon elliptic flow at high pT reflects the small quark momentum anisotropy at early times offers a complementary probe to hadron elliptic flow, which is sensitive to later stages. The authors note that jet-photon contributions, as discussed by Fries, Muller, and Srivastava (2005), may also affect high-pT photon elliptic flow and could even lead to negative values, which will need to be addressed in future work.
