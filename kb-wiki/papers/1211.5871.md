# Collective excitations of $Λ$ hypernuclei

> We discuss low-lying collective excitations of $Λ$ hypernuclei using the self-consistent mean-field approaches. We first discuss the deformation properties of $Λ$ hypernuclei in the $sd$-shell region. Based on the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach, we show that the oblate deformation for $^{28}$Si nucleus may disappear when a $Λ$ particle is added to this nucleus. We then discuss the rotational excitations of $^{25}_Λ$Mg nucleus using the three-dimensional potential energy surface in the deformation plane obtained with the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method. The deformation of $^{25}_Λ$Mg nucleus is predicted to be slightly reduced due to an addition of $Λ$ particle. We demonstrate that this leads to a reduction of electromagnetic transition probability, $B(E2)$, in the ground state rotational band. We also present an application of random phase approximation (RPA) to hypernuclei, and show that a new dipole mode, which we call a soft dipole $Λ$ mode, appears in hypernuclei, which can be interpreted as an oscillation of $Λ$ particle against the core nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper claims that adding a Lambda hyperon to certain nuclei can cause nuclear deformation to disappear (e.g., for oblate-deformed Si-28) or slightly reduce deformation, and that a new low-energy dipole mode, the "soft dipole Lambda mode", appears in hypernuclei, but these effects depend on the specific nucleus and the mean-field approach used (RMF vs. Skyrme-Hartree-Fock give different results for deformation disappearance).

## Method

The authors used self-consistent mean-field approaches: the relativistic mean-field (RMF) method with the NL3 parameter set for deformation studies of sd-shell hypernuclei, and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) method with the SGII parameter set for three-dimensional potential energy surfaces. For rotational excitations of Mg-25 with a Lambda particle, they employed the five-dimensional collective Bohr Hamiltonian approach based on the Gaussian overlap approximation. For vibrational excitations of the double-Lambda hypernucleus O-18 with two Lambda particles, they applied the random phase approximation (RPA) using the SkM* parameter set for the nucleon-nucleon interaction and the No.5 parameter set for the Lambda-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- The energy difference between the oblate and spherical configurations in Si-28 is 0.754 MeV, which can be inverted when a Lambda particle is added.
- For Mg-24 without a Lambda, the average deformation parameters are beta = 0.54 and gamma = 20.0 degrees; with a Lambda particle added, these change to beta = 0.52 and gamma = 20.8 degrees.
- The Lambda particle increases the excitation energy of the 2+_1 state in the ground state rotational band of Mg-25 by approximately 7 percent.
- The B(E2: 2+_1 -> 0+_1) transition strength is reduced by approximately 9 percent when a Lambda particle is added to Mg-24.
- For O-18 with two Lambda particles, the first 2+ state has excitation energy 13.8 MeV and B(E2) = 0.529 e^2 fm^4, compared to 13.1 MeV and 0.726 e^2 fm^4 for O-16.
- For O-18 with two Lambda particles, the first 3- state has excitation energy 6.32 MeV and B(E3) = 67.7 e^2 fm^6, compared to 6.06 MeV and 91.1 e^2 fm^6 for O-16.
- The electromagnetic transition probabilities for the 2+ and 3- states in O-18 with two Lambda particles are decreased by 26-28 percent relative to O-16.
- A new dipole peak appears at 11.4 MeV in O-18 with two Lambda particles, with the strongest RPA amplitude (xi = 0.873) coming from Lambda particle excitation from the 1s to the 1p state.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hypernuclear deformation by Zofka (1980) using Gaussian interactions and by Zhou et al. (2007) using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations for axially deformed hypernuclei. The paper shows that the RMF approach predicts a stronger polarization effect of the Lambda hyperon than the SHF approach, and that deformation disappearance occurs in both approaches only when the energy difference between the optimum deformation and spherical configuration is less than about 1 MeV, as shown in earlier work by the authors (Win et al., 2010). The soft dipole Lambda mode is analogous to the soft dipole motion in halo nuclei studied by Ikeda (2010), but differs because Lambda particles are located in the nuclear interior rather than the surface. The results are relevant for planned experiments at J-PARC using the Hyperball-J Ge detector array to study low-lying energy levels of Lambda hypernuclei in the sd-shell region.
