# Hadronic correlations above the chiral/deconfinement transition

> The statistical bootstrap model is critically revised in order to include a medium-dependent resonance width in it. We show that a thermodynamic model with a vanishing width below the Hagedorn temperature T_H and a Hagedorn spectrum-like width above T_H may not only eliminate the divergence of the thermodynamic functions above T_H, but also gives a satisfactory description the lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) data on the energy density above the chiral/deconfinement transition as the main result of this contribution. This model allows to explain the absence of heavy resonance contributions in the fit of the experimentally measured particle ratios at SPS and RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The authors propose that introducing a medium-dependent resonance width into the statistical bootstrap model, with a vanishing width below the Hagedorn temperature \(T_H\) and a Hagedorn spectrum-like width above \(T_H\), can eliminate the divergence of thermodynamic functions above \(T_H\) and describe lattice QCD energy density data, but this description is conditional on the width growing exponentially with mass like the Hagedorn spectrum itself.

## Method

The authors modify the statistical bootstrap model by incorporating a Breit-Wigner spectral function for hadronic resonances, where the resonance width \(\Gamma\) is set to zero for temperatures \(T \le T_H\) and grows as \(\Gamma \sim C_{\Gamma} (m/T_H)^{N_m} (T/T_H)^{N_T} \exp(m/T)\) for \(T > T_H\). They then compute the energy density as a sum over light hadrons plus an integral over the Hagedorn mass spectrum weighted by this spectral function, and fit the resulting expression to lattice QCD data on energy density above the chiral/deconfinement transition.

## Key numbers

* The Hagedorn temperature is set to \(T_H = 165\) MeV for the fit to lattice QCD data.
* The spectral function parameters used in the fit are \(N_T = 2.325\) and \(N_m = 2.5\), with mass thresholds \(m_a = m_b = 1\) GeV.
* The equilibration time for Au + Au collisions at RHIC energies is estimated as \(\tau_{eq} \approx 0.5\) fm, and the formation time is \(\tau_f \approx 1 - 2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the statistical bootstrap model of Hagedorn by addressing its divergence problem above the Hagedorn temperature, which was unresolved in earlier formulations such as those reviewed by Tawfik (2003). The model provides an alternative to the conventional interpretation of the deconfinement transition as the emergence of quasifree quarks and gluons, instead describing hot QCD matter in terms of strongly correlated hadronic degrees of freedom with medium-dependent widths. It enables future studies of heavy-ion collisions by explaining the absence of heavy resonance contributions in particle ratios measured at SPS and RHIC energies, and suggests that only weakly interacting hadrons like \(\Omega\), \(\phi\), \(J/\psi\), and \(\psi^\prime\) may test the model.
