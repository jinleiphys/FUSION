# Resummation of Jet Shapes and Extracting Properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Understanding the properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) that is produced in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions has been one of the top priorities of the heavy ion program at the LHC. Energetic jets are produced and subsequently quenched in the collisions. Such jet quenching phenomena provide promising tools to probe the medium properties by studying the modification of jets due to the medium interactions. Significant modifications of jet shapes have been measured. In this talk we focus on the calculation of jet shapes in both proton-proton and lead-lead collisions using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), with Glauber gluon interactions in the medium. Large logarithms in jet shapes are resummed at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy by the renormalization-group evolution between hierarchical jet scales. The medium interactions contribute as power corrections, and we calculate the modification of jet shapes at leading order in opacity with the static QGP model. Preliminary results are presented with good agreement with the recent CMS jet shape measurements.

## Key claim

The paper presents a calculation of jet shapes in both proton-proton and lead-lead collisions using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) with Glauber gluon interactions in the medium, achieving next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy through resummation; the medium interactions are treated as power corrections at leading order in opacity using a static QGP model, and the results are described as preliminary with good agreement with CMS data.

## Method

The author uses soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive a factorization theorem for the jet shape, separating hard, collinear, and soft sectors. Large logarithms of the form ln(r/R) are resummed at NLL accuracy via renormalization-group evolution between hierarchical jet scales. For heavy ion collisions, SCET is extended to include Glauber gluon interactions as background fields from the QGP, and medium-induced splitting functions are used to calculate modifications to jet energy functions at leading order in opacity with a static QGP model.

## Key numbers

- Jet shape calculations are performed for anti-kT jets with cone size R=0.3 and R=0.7 in proton-proton collisions at center-of-mass energies of 2.76 TeV and 7 TeV, with jet pT > 100 GeV and rapidity range 0.3 < |y| < 2.
- For lead-lead collisions at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, centrality 0-10%, with the same jet pT and rapidity cuts, the static QGP model uses parameters: bulk size L=5 fm, gluon mean free path lambda=1 fm, and inverse range of Glauber gluon interaction m=0.75 GeV.
- The medium-induced splitting function for the q→qg channel includes the LPM effect via a cosine term with argument (q_perp - k_perp)^2 Δz / (x ω).

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based resummation framework for jet shapes in proton-proton collisions (Chien 2014) to heavy ion collisions, building on earlier medium-induced splitting function calculations (Ovanesyan 2011, Fickinger 2013). It contrasts with the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev formalism (Vitev 2008) and Monte Carlo simulations (Ma 2013, Ramos 2014) for medium modification of jet shapes. The approach enables more precise extraction of QGP properties by comparing calculations with different QGP models to upcoming LHC data at 5.02 TeV.
