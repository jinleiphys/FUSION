# Systematic study of large-momentum distribution in nuclei with the operator product expansion

> The operator product expansion (OPE) is applied in conjunction with Pionless effective field theory to study the short-rang structure of nuclei. By matching the OPE with the selected nuclear potentials for nucleon-nucleon scattering states, we obtain the Wilson coefficients. The nucleon momentum distribution in the deuteron is then used to test the OPE against the predictions of these nuclear potentials. In order to achieve a systematic separation of short-range and long-range interactions, we discuss how the OPE approximation can be improved by including higher-order EFT potentials and higher-dimension local operators.

## Key claim

The operator product expansion (OPE) combined with Pionless effective field theory can systematically separate short-range and long-range contributions to the single-nucleon momentum distribution in nuclei, but the approach is tested only for the deuteron and relies on matching Wilson coefficients using nucleon-nucleon scattering states rather than bound states.

## Method

The authors apply the operator product expansion to expand the nonlocal operator defining the single-nucleon momentum distribution into a series of local Pionless EFT operators with state-independent Wilson coefficients. They determine these Wilson coefficients by matching the OPE/EFT predictions against exact calculations from underlying nuclear potentials for nucleon-nucleon scattering states in the coupled \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channel, using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to compute the T-matrix. The deuteron momentum distribution is then computed using the OPE expansion and compared with direct potential model predictions.

## Key numbers

- The breakdown scale of Pionless EFT is \(M_{\text{hi}} \sim m_\pi\), the pion mass
- The \(S\)-wave scattering lengths are approximately 5 fm or -20 fm
- The pion Compton wavelength is \(m_\pi^{-1} \simeq 1.4\) fm
- The Fermi momentum for medium and heavy nuclei is \(k_F \approx 250\) MeV
- The nucleon-nucleon scattering data used for potential calibration extends to laboratory energy \(T_{lab} = 350\) MeV
- The deuteron binding energy is denoted \(B_d\) in the formalism

## Context

This work extends the conceptual framework of the Generalized Contact Formalism (Weiss 2014, 2015, 2016, 2023) by providing a systematic EFT-based approach to short-range correlations that can incorporate range corrections and pion-exchange forces. It contrasts with the unitary transformation approach of Tropiano (2021, 2024) and the direct variational Monte Carlo calculations using the Argonne V18 potential (AV18UXVMC14) and chiral EFT potentials (ChiralVMC24). The method enables future incorporation of lattice QCD inputs for nucleon-nucleon systems and can be extended to heavier nuclei by combining with chiral EFT.
