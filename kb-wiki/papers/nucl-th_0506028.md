# Excitation of nuclear giant resonances in neutrino scattering off nuclei

> The cross sections for neutrino scattering off the 12C and 16O nuclei are calculated within the framework of the continuum Random Phase Approximation. A model to consider also the final state interactions is developed. Total charge-conserving and charge-exchange cross sections for both electron neutrinos and antineutrinos have been calculated up to projectile energies of 100 MeV. The sensitivity of the cross sections to the residual interaction and to the final state interactions is investigated. A direct comparison between neutrino and electron scattering cross sections calculated under the same kinematic conditions is presented. We found remarkable differences between electromagnetic and weak nuclear responses. The model is applied to describe cross sections of neutrinos produced by muon decay at rest and in supernovae explosions.

# Excitation of nuclear giant resonances in neutrino scattering off nuclei

## Key claim

The cross sections for neutrino scattering off 12C and 16O are calculated using Continuum Random Phase Approximation (CRPA) with a phenomenological model for Final State Interactions (FSI), but the authors find that the axial vector part of the weak current dominates all neutrino cross sections, leading to remarkable differences between electromagnetic and weak nuclear responses that make predictions of neutrino cross sections from electron scattering data unreliable.

## Method

The authors solve CRPA equations using the Fourier-Bessel expansion technique with Woods-Saxon mean fields tuned to reproduce single-particle energies around the Fermi surface and empirical charge density distributions for 12C and 16O. They test three different residual particle-hole interactions (two zero-range forces labeled LM1 and LM2, and a finite-range polarization potential PP), each renormalized to reproduce either the 3- state at 6.13 MeV in 16O or the main photo-absorption peak at 22.5 MeV in 12C. The FSI model folds the CRPA response with a Lorentzian function whose width is extracted from empirical single-particle self-energy data and optical potential parameters, with an energy-dependent parameterization connecting the giant resonance region (below 40 MeV) to the quasi-elastic region (above 40 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Axial mass used for nucleon form factor: M_A = 1014 MeV
- CRPA calculations include multipole excitations up to total angular momentum J = 16
- For 12C, the normalization constant for LM1 and PP forces was fixed to reproduce the main photo-absorption peak at 22.5 MeV
- For 16O, normalization constants were fixed to reproduce the collective low-lying 3- state at 6.13 MeV
- The 1- multipole contributes more than 97% of the photo-absorption cross section in both nuclei
- In 16O, the 2- state in (nu, e-) cross section appears at 23.40 MeV, compared to a 2- state at 24.09 MeV found in (p,n) reactions
- The quasi-elastic peak appears at excitation energy omega = 150 MeV for 1 GeV incoming lepton energy at theta = 30 degrees
- Total cross sections increase by about two orders of magnitude when projectile energy increases from 50 MeV to 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the CRPA formalism previously applied to electron scattering (Co' 1984, 1985, 1988) to neutrino-nucleus scattering, using the Fourier-Bessel technique of Dehesa (1982). The FSI model adapts approaches used for hadronic processes (Bakker 1997, Smith 1988) and electron scattering in the quasi-elastic region (Co' 1988, Amaro 1994, 2001). The authors find that the procedure proposed by Lanni (2004) and Amaro (2005) to predict neutrino cross sections from electron scattering universal response functions should be used with caution, because the axial current dominance in neutrino scattering produces no dominance of natural parity multipoles, unlike electromagnetic interactions. The results enable improved predictions for neutrino detectors using water (16O) and liquid scintillators (12C), particularly for neutrinos from muon decay at rest and supernova explosions.
