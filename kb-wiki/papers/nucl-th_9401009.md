# Relativistic Coulomb Sum Rules for $(e,e^\prime)$

> A Coulomb sum rule is derived for the response of nuclei to $(e,e^\prime)$ scattering with large three-momentum transfers. Unlike the nonrelativistic formulation, the relativistic Coulomb sum is restricted to spacelike four-momenta for the most direct connection with experiments; an immediate consequence is that excitations involving antinucleons, e.g., $N{\bar N}$ pair production, are approximately eliminated from the sum rule. Relativistic recoil and Fermi motion of target nucleons are correctly incorporated. The sum rule decomposes into one- and two-body parts, with correlation information in the second. The one-body part requires information on the nucleon momentum distribution function, which is incorporated by a moment expansion method. The sum rule given through the second moment (RCSR-II) is tested in the Fermi gas model, and is shown to be sufficiently accurate for applications to data.

## Key claim

The relativistic Coulomb sum rule (RCSR) must be restricted to spacelike four-momenta ($\omega < |\mathbf{q}|$) to match electron scattering experiments, which approximately eliminates antinucleon pair production from the sum; unlike the nonrelativistic sum rule, the relativistic sum does not directly count the number of charged scatterers but requires kinematic recoil corrections that depend on nucleon momentum.

## Method

The authors derived a relativistic Coulomb sum rule for $(e,e^\prime)$ scattering by starting from the first-order Born approximation and the nuclear response function in Coulomb gauge, using a one-body nucleon current operator with off-shell form factors. They restricted the energy integration to spacelike four-momenta ($\omega < |\mathbf{q}|$) to exclude antinucleon excitations, then decomposed the sum into one-body and two-body parts, with the one-body part expanded in moments of the nucleon momentum distribution. The sum rule through the second moment (RCSR-II) was tested in the Fermi gas model.

## Key numbers

- For a free nucleon at rest, the spacelike Coulomb sum gives $(E_{\mathbf{q}} + M)/(2E_{\mathbf{q}})$, which tends to $1/2$ in the ultrarelativistic limit $|\mathbf{q}| \gg M$ and to unity in the nonrelativistic limit $|\mathbf{q}| \ll M$.
- For a Fermi gas of Dirac nucleons, the spacelike Coulomb sum approaches $Z/2$ (for $Z$ protons) as $|\mathbf{q}| \rightarrow \infty$.
- Experimental studies at momentum transfers up to $|\mathbf{q}| = 550$ MeV/c showed that for heavier targets, the measured nonrelativistic Coulomb sum $S_{NR}(\mathbf{q})$ is 20-30% below theoretical predictions based on independent particle models.
- The nucleon electric form factor parameterization used is $G_{E,p}(Q^2) = (1 + Q^2/0.71\ \mathrm{GeV}^2)^{-2}$.
- The proton and neutron anomalous magnetic moments are $\kappa_p = +1.79$ and $\kappa_n = -1.91$, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic Coulomb sum rule (NRCSR) formalism to the relativistic domain, addressing problems identified by Matsui (1990s) who showed that the relativistic sum rule must be restricted to spacelike momenta to avoid antinucleon contributions. It builds on earlier work by Walecka (1990s) on relativistic extensions and by DeForest (1990s) who proposed a modified electric form factor correction, as well as studies by Donnelly et al. (1990s) on moment expansions. The derived sum rule enables analysis of upcoming experiments at CEBAF with beam energies around 4 GeV, where nonrelativistic assumptions fail, and provides a framework for extracting nucleon-nucleon correlation information from relativistic electron scattering data.
