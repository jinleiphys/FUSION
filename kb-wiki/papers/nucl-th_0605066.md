# Excitation of soft dipole modes in electron scattering

> The excitation of soft dipole modes in light nuclei via inelastic electron scattering is investigated. I show that, under the proposed conditions of the forthcoming electron-ion colliders, the scattering cross sections have a direct relation to the scattering by real photons. The advantages of electron scattering over other electromagnetic probes is explored. The response functions for direct breakup are studied with few-body models. The dependence upon final state interactions is discussed. A comparison between direct breakup and collective models is performed. The results of this investigation are important for the planned electron-ion colliders at the GSI and RIKEN facilities.

## Key claim

Under the specific conditions of proposed electron-ion colliders (such as those planned at GSI and RIKEN), the cross sections for inelastic electron scattering exciting soft dipole modes in light nuclei have a direct relation to photo-nuclear cross sections, but this equivalence relies on the plane wave Born approximation and very forward scattering angles.

## Method

The author derives the inelastic electron scattering cross section in the plane wave Born approximation and shows that for forward scattering angles it can be expressed as a product of a virtual photon spectrum and the photo-nuclear cross section. The nuclear response is then calculated using two-body models for one-neutron halo nuclei and three-body hyperspherical harmonics models for two-neutron halo nuclei, incorporating final state interactions through effective range expansions and phase shifts. A hydrodynamical model mixing Goldhaber-Teller and Steinwedel-Jensen modes is also developed to describe collective pygmy dipole resonances.

## Key numbers

- For 1 GeV electrons, the virtual photon spectrum at giant resonance energies (E0 = 15 MeV) yields dN(Egamma = E0)/dEgamma approximately 6 x 10^{-3} per MeV.
- With a luminosity of L_{eA} = 10^{25} cm^{-2}s^{-1}, the estimated event rate for giant resonances is N_tau approximately 10^{-3} A s^{-1}, yielding thousands of events per day for medium mass nuclei.
- Only 5% to 10% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule goes into soft dipole mode excitation, but these modes occur at much lower energy (Er approximately 1 MeV) where the equivalent photon number is at least one order of magnitude larger than at giant resonance energies.
- For E1 excitations in a two-body model, the peak in the response function occurs at E0 approximately 3S_n/5, where S_n is the neutron separation energy.
- For the three-body model of 11Li, the peak is predicted at E0^{(E1)} approximately 1.8S_{2n}, fitting the experimentally determined peak position.
- The effective range parameters used for n+4He p-wave scattering include a1 = -13.82 fm^{-3} and r1 = -0.419 fm^{-1} for p_{1/2} and a1 = -62.95 fm^{-3} and r1 = -0.882 fm^{-1} for p_{3/2}.
- The neutron-neutron scattering length is a_{nn} = -23.7 fm and effective range r_{nn} = 2.7 fm.
- For the n-9Li system, a resonance is assumed with E_R = 0.53 MeV and Gamma = 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for studying soft dipole modes in neutron-rich nuclei, which has been debated as either direct breakup of loosely-bound systems or collective pygmy dipole resonances (Iek93, Sac93, Sag95, Hus96). The author compares electron scattering with Coulomb excitation using heavy ions (BB88), noting that while heavy ion reactions have larger cross sections, electron scattering provides a cleaner electromagnetic probe without nuclear interaction contamination. The results are directly relevant to planned electron-ion collider experiments at the FAIR facility at GSI (Haik05) and at RIKEN (Sud01), and the connection to astrophysical r-process nucleosynthesis is noted through the work of Go98 on pygmy resonances affecting radiative neutron capture rates.
