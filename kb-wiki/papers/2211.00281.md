# Ab initio no-core shell model description of $^{10-14}$C isotopes

> We present a systematic study of the $^{10-14}\text{C}$ isotopes within the \textit{ab initio} no-core shell model theory. We apply four different realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions: (i) the charge-dependent Bonn 2000 (CDB2K) potential (ii) the inside non-local outside Yukawa (INOY) potential (iii) the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) potential, and (iv) the optimized next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO$_{opt}$) potential. We report the low-lying energy spectra of both positive and negative parity states for the $^{10-14}\text{C}$ isotopes and investigate the level structures. We also calculate electromagnetic properties such as transition strengths, quadrupole and magnetic moments. The dependence of point-proton radii on the harmonic oscillator frequency and basis space is shown. We present calculations of the translation invariant one-body density matrix in the no-core shell model and discuss isotopic trends in the density distribution. The maximum basis space reached is $10 \hbar Ω$ for $^{10}\text{C}$ and $8 \hbar Ω$ for $^{11-14}\text{C}$, with a maximum M-scheme dimension of $1.3 \times 10^{9}$ for $^{10}\text{C}$. We found that while the INOY interaction gives the best description of the ground state energies, the N$^3$LO interaction best reproduces the point-proton radii.

# Ab initio no-core shell model description of $^{10-14}$C isotopes

## Key claim

The INOY interaction provides the best description of ground state energies across the $^{10-14}$C isotopic chain, while the N$^3$LO interaction best reproduces point-proton radii; however, these conclusions are conditional on the maximum basis spaces reached ($10\hbar\Omega$ for $^{10}$C and $8\hbar\Omega$ for $^{11-14}$C), and the NCSM calculations with CDB2K, INOY, and N$^3$LO are not variational due to the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki renormalization applied.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for $^{10-14}$C isotopes using four realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions: CDB2K, INOY, N$^3$LO, and N$^2$LO$_{opt}$. The Hamiltonian was solved in a harmonic oscillator basis truncated by $N_\mathrm{max}$, with Okubo-Lee-Suzuki renormalization applied for the CDB2K, INOY, and N$^3$LO interactions (no renormalization for N$^2$LO$_{opt}$), and the optimal harmonic oscillator frequency was determined for each nucleus and interaction by minimizing the ground state energy in the largest accessible basis space.

## Key numbers

- Maximum basis space reached: $10\hbar\Omega$ for $^{10}$C and $8\hbar\Omega$ for $^{11-14}$C, with a maximum M-scheme dimension of $1.3 \times 10^{9}$ for $^{10}$C
- Optimal frequencies for $^{10}$C: $\hbar\Omega = 14$ MeV (CDB2K), 18 MeV (INOY), 12 MeV (N$^3$LO), and 22 MeV (N$^2$LO$_{opt}$)
- Optimal frequencies for $^{12}$C: $\hbar\Omega = 14$ MeV (CDB2K), 20 MeV (INOY), 12 MeV (N$^3$LO), and 24 MeV (N$^2$LO$_{opt}$)
- Excitation energy of the $2^+_1$ state in $^{10}$C at $N_\mathrm{max}=10$: 3.590 MeV (CDB2K), 3.798 MeV (INOY), 3.388 MeV (N$^3$LO), and 3.440 MeV (N$^2$LO$_{opt}$), compared to the experimental value of 3.354 MeV
- The NCSM calculates the $0^+_2$ (Hoyle) state in $^{12}$C at approximately 15 MeV across interactions, while the experimental value is 7.65 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous NCSM studies of carbon isotopes by Forssen et al. (CDB2K potential for even carbon isotopes up to A=20) and Navratil et al. (studies of $^{12}$C and $^{13}$C with AV8' and chiral potentials at smaller basis spaces), reaching larger basis spaces ($10\hbar\Omega$ for $^{10}$C) and systematically comparing four NN interactions. The results enable benchmarking of NN-only interactions against experimental data for carbon isotopes and establish the need for larger basis spaces and inclusion of three-nucleon forces to reproduce the Hoyle state in $^{12}$C and achieve full convergence of ground state energies and radii.
