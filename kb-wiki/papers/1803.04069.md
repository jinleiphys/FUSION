# Electromagnetic form factors of singly heavy baryons in the self-consistent SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model

> The self-consistent chiral quark-soliton model is a relativistic pion mean-field approach in the large $N_c$ limit, which describes both light and heavy baryons on an equal footing. In the limit of the infinitely heavy mass of the heavy quark, a heavy baryon can be regarded as $N_c-1$ valence quarks bound by the pion mean fields, leaving the heavy quark as a color static source. The structure of the heavy baryon in this scheme is mainly governed by the light-quark degrees of freedom. Based on this framework, we evaluate the electromagnetic form factors of the lowest-lying heavy baryons. The rotational $1/N_c$ and strange current quark mass corrections in linear order are considered. We discuss the electric charge and magnetic densities of heavy baryons in comparison with those of the nucleons. The results of the electric charge radii of the positive-charged heavy baryons show explicitly that the heavy baryon is a compact object. The electric form factors are presented. The form factor of $Σ_c^{++}$ is compared with that from a lattice QCD. We also discuss the results of the magnetic form factors. The magnetic moments of the baryon sextet with spin 1/2 and the magnetic radii are compared with other works and the lattice data.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic form factors of singly heavy baryons are governed primarily by the light-quark degrees of freedom, with the heavy quark treated as a static color source; the electric charge radii of positive-charged heavy baryons show that the heavy baryon is a compact object, but this conclusion depends on the approximation that the momentum transfer remains much smaller than the heavy-quark mass (i.e., \(q^2 \ll m_Q^2\)).

## Method

The authors compute the electromagnetic form factors of the lowest-lying singly heavy baryons (antitriplet and sextet with spin 1/2) within the self-consistent SU(3) chiral quark-soliton model (\(\chi\)QSM), treating a heavy baryon as \(N_c-1\) valence quarks bound by pion mean fields with the heavy quark as a static color source. They evaluate the matrix elements of the electromagnetic current in the large \(N_c\) limit, including rotational \(1/N_c\) corrections and strange current quark mass corrections at linear order, and obtain the Sachs electric and magnetic form factors \(G_E(Q^2)\) and \(G_M(Q^2)\) from the time and space components of the current in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

* The dynamical quark mass is fixed to \(M = 420\) MeV, the value used for light baryons and for heavy baryon mass splittings in Kim (2018).
* The average current up/down quark mass is \(\overline{m} = 6.131\) MeV, fixed by reproducing the pion mass.
* The strange current quark mass is taken as \(m_s = 174\) MeV for charmed baryons and \(m_s = 166\) MeV for bottom baryons, based on the mass splittings from Kim (2018).
* The neutron electric form factor increases up to around \(0.4\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and then starts to decrease slowly, consistent with experimental data from Sulkosky (2017).

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark-soliton model, previously applied successfully to light baryons (Kim 1995, Silva 2005, Ledwig 2010), to singly heavy baryons following the approach of Yang (2016) and Diakonov (2010) where heavy baryons are treated as \(N_c-1\) valence quarks bound by pion mean fields. The results for the electric form factor of \(\Sigma_c^{++}\) are compared with lattice QCD data from Can (2013), and the magnetic moments of the baryon sextet with spin 1/2 are compared with other works and lattice data. This framework enables future studies of heavy baryon structure at low momentum transfer and can be extended to examine the hyperfine splitting between spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 sextet states.
