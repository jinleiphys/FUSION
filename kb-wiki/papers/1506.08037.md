# Probing the neutron-skin thickness by photon production from reactions induced by intermediate-energy protons

> Photon from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung in p+Pb reactions is examined as a potential probe of the neutron-skin thickness in different centralities and at different proton incident energies. It is shown that the best choice of reaction environment is about 140MeV for the incident proton and the 95\%-100\% centrality for the reaction system since the incident proton mainly interacts with neutrons inside the skin of the target and thus leads to different photon production to maximal extent. Moreover, considering two main uncertainties from both photon production probability and nucleon-nucleon cross section in the reaction, I propose to use the ratio of photon production from two reactions to measure the neutron-skin thickness because of its cancellation effects on these uncertainties simultaneously, but the preserved about 13\%-15\% sensitivities on the varied neutron-skin thickness from 0.1 to 0.3fm within the current experimental uncertainty range of the neutron-skin size in $^{208}$Pb.

## Key claim
Photon production from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung in p+Pb reactions can probe the neutron-skin thickness, but only if the ratio of photon yields from two reactions (p+Pb and p+Ca) is used to cancel uncertainties from the nucleon-nucleon cross section and photon production probability; the raw photon multiplicity is too contaminated by these uncertainties to extract useful information.

## Method
The author performed transport-model simulations using the IBUU code for p+Pb reactions at intermediate energies, with the 208Pb target initialized with neutron-skin thicknesses of 0.10 and 0.30 fm from Hartree-Fock calculations based on the MSL model. Photon production from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung was treated perturbatively, and the sensitivity of the total photon multiplicity (10–50 MeV) to the neutron-skin thickness was evaluated across different centralities and incident proton energies. The ratio of photon multiplicity from p+Pb to p+40Ca reactions was then tested for its ability to cancel uncertainties from the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section and two different photon production probability formulas.

## Key numbers
- The best reaction environment is an incident proton energy of about 140 MeV and a centrality of 95%–100% for the p+Pb system.
- The total photon multiplicity shows about 15% relative sensitivity to the neutron-skin thickness when varied from 0.10 to 0.30 fm.
- The ratio of photon multiplicity from p+Pb to p+40Ca reactions preserves about 13%–15% sensitivity on the neutron-skin thickness (0.10 to 0.30 fm) while canceling uncertainties from both the nucleon-nucleon cross section and photon production probability.
- With a centrality of 90%–100%, the ratio shows about 10%–15% sensitivity on the neutron-skin thickness.
- Photon production with energy beyond about 50 MeV can be ignored in the intermediate-energy p+Pb reaction.

## Context
This work extends the use of photon production from heavy-ion reactions, previously employed to probe the nuclear caloric curve (Ortega, 2004), nucleon-nucleon dynamics (Schubert, 1997; Martinez, 1999; Entem, 2002), and the symmetry potential (Giuliani, 2006), to the specific problem of neutron-skin thickness. It addresses the large uncertainty in the 208Pb neutron-skin thickness, where the PREX-I experiment (2012) measured 0.33+0.16−0.18 fm, differing from the earlier value of 0.11±0.06 fm from π+-Pb scattering (Friedman, 2012). The proposed ratio method enables a non-hadronic probe that avoids the strong-force model assumptions of hadronic probes like nucleon elastic scattering (Hoffmann, 1980; Karataglidis, 2002; Clark, 2003; Zenihiro, 2010) and inelastic excitation of giant resonances (Krasznahorkay, 1999, 2004).
