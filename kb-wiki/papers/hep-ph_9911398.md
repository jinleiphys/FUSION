# Gluon Condensate and Non-Perturbative Quark-Photon Vertex

> We evaluate the quark-photon vertex non-perturbatively taking into account the gluon condensate at finite temperature. This vertex is related to the previously derived effective quark propagator by a QED like Ward-Takahashi identity. The importance of the effective vertex for the dilepton production rate from a quark-gluon plasma is stressed.

# Gluon Condensate and Non-Perturbative Quark-Photon Vertex

## Key claim

The authors derive a non-perturbative quark-photon vertex that includes the gluon condensate at finite temperature, and show that it satisfies the QED-like Ward-Takahashi identity when combined with the previously derived effective quark propagator containing the same gluon condensate. The result is gauge independent but applies only above the critical temperature, where the quark condensate vanishes and the deconfined phase is realized.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop correction to the quark-photon vertex by inserting the non-perturbative gluon propagator (subtracted of its perturbative part to ensure gauge independence) into the standard Feynman diagram. They expand the product of quark propagators for small loop momentum, keeping terms bilinear in the loop momentum, and relate the resulting integrals to the gluon condensate via its definition as a moment of the gluon propagator. At finite temperature, the gluon propagator is decomposed into longitudinal and transverse components, whose moments are expressed in terms of the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensates; the calculation is performed in the imaginary time formalism retaining only the lowest Matsubara mode.

## Key numbers

* The zero-temperature gluon condensate value used is $\langle G^2\rangle_{T=0} = (2.5 \pm 1.0) T_c^4$, taken from Boyd et al. (1996).
* The chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensates at finite temperature are expressed in terms of plaquette expectation values as $\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \langle {\cal E}^2\rangle_T = \frac{4}{11} T^4 \Delta_\tau - \frac{2}{11} \langle G^2\rangle_{T=0}$ and $\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \langle {\cal B}^2\rangle_T = -\frac{4}{11} T^4 \Delta_\sigma + \frac{2}{11} \langle G^2\rangle_{T=0}$.
* The zero-temperature quark self energy is $\Sigma_G(P) = g^2 \frac{P\!\!\!\!\slash}{P^4} \frac{\langle :G^2: \rangle}{36}$.
* The zero-temperature vertex correction contains a prefactor $\frac{ieg^2}{36 P_1^2 P_2^2} \langle :G^2: \rangle$.

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative approach of Schafer and Thoma (1999), who introduced the temperature-dependent gluon condensate into parton propagators, by constructing the corresponding effective quark-photon vertex that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity. The vertex is intended for use in dilepton production rate calculations from a quark-gluon plasma, which were previously performed using only the effective propagator by Mustafa, Schafer, and Thoma (1999). The authors note that in the analogous case of pion annihilation, Korpa and Pratt (1991) found that enforcing the Ward identity leads to a strong suppression of the dilepton rate, whereas the HTL vertex used by Braaten, Pisarski, and Yuan (1990) results in an enhancement; the present work enables a similar consistency check for the gluon condensate approach.
