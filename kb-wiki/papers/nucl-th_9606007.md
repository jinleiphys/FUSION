# Neutron--Antineutron Oscillations at the Surface of Nuclei

> We discuss some aspects of possible neutron--antineutron oscillations in nuclei. The phenomenon occurs mostly at the surface of nuclei, and hence {\sl i)} is not very sensitive to medium corrections and {\sl ii)} makes use of the antinucleon-nucleus interaction in a region probed by experiments at CERN.

# Neutron–Antineutron Oscillations at the Surface of Nuclei

## Key claim

Neutron–antineutron oscillations in nuclei occur predominantly at the nuclear surface, making the process relatively insensitive to medium corrections and allowing the use of antinucleon-nucleus interactions constrained by CERN experiments, but the authors stress that this peripheral character is a property of the potential-model approach which they argue is reliable.

## Method

The authors solve a radial Schrodinger equation for a neutron in a nucleus, then treat the neutron–antineutron transition via an inhomogeneous equation for the antineutron wave function driven by a transition potential epsilon. The decay width of the nucleus is computed from the integral of the squared antineutron wave function times the imaginary part of the antineutron potential, and this formalism is generalized to larger nuclei using shell-model wave functions for each neutron.

## Key numbers

- For typical medium-size nuclei used in proton-decay experiments, a nuclear lifetime T greater than or equal to 10^32 years implies a free-neutron oscillation period tau greater than or equal to 10^8 seconds.
- The scaling relation between nuclear lifetime T and free-neutron oscillation period tau is T = T_R * tau^2, where T_R is a nuclear factor computed from the model.
- The authors note that checking tau greater than or equal to 10^11 seconds in a foreseen Oak Ridge experiment corresponds to limits on T that are not reachable in underground experiments.

## Context

This work extends the potential-model approach to neutron–antineutron oscillations previously developed by the same authors in Dover, Gal, and Richard (1995) and confirmed in further investigations. The peripheral character of the oscillation is contrasted with earlier concerns about medium renormalization of the transition operator, as discussed by Kabir (1980). The results are compared to the free-neutron oscillation limits from the Grenoble experiment and to the nuclear lifetime constraints from underground experiments, with the nuclear factor T_R computed using methods analogous to those of Ericson and Rosa-Clot (1983) in a different context. The antineutron potential near the surface is constrained by antiprotonic atom and antiproton scattering experiments at the CERN LEAR facility, as reviewed by Dover (1990).
