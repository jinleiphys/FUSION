# New Excitation Mode of the Nucleon?

> We consider properties of narrow baryon states observed recently in reaction $pp\to pπ^+X$. Two lowest of them (with mass 1004 and 1044 MeV, respectively) are stable against strog decay. Moreover we conclude that they cannot decay to $γN$ and thus this states are a kind of metastable levels in quark system. The simplest decay channel is assumed to be $2γN$ and possible quark configuration with such properties is discussed. New experiment for verification of status of this states in $γN$ collision is proposed.

# New Excitation Mode of the Nucleon?

## Key claim
The two lowest narrow baryon states observed in the reaction $pp\to p\pi^+X$ with masses 1004 and 1044 MeV are metastable levels in a nonstrange three-quark system that cannot decay via single photon emission ($\gamma N$), contrary to naive expectation; their simplest decay channel is instead $2\gamma N$, and the analysis of L'vov and Workman that excluded them as intermediate states in Compton scattering does not rule out their existence, only that the $\gamma N \leftrightarrow N^{\prime}$ transition is forbidden for these two states.

## Method
The author analyzed the symmetry properties of the observed narrow baryon states $N^{\prime}(1004)$, $N^{\prime}(1044)$, and $N^{\prime}(1094)$ within the constituent quark model, assigning them to the totally antisymmetric $\underline{20}$-plet representation of the spin-flavor $SU(6)_{FS}$ group. Using the translationally invariant shell model with harmonic oscillator wave functions, the author identified the orbital configuration as $|2(01)[1^3]1(123)\rangle$ (second excitation, $N=2$, total orbital momentum $L=1$) and proposed a two-step production mechanism via intermediate resonances with mixed spin-flavor symmetry, analogous to laser pumping. The author also considered a possible $LL$-coupling potential to explain the mass reduction from typical resonance energies (1600-1700 MeV) to the observed masses near 1000-1100 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Masses of the three narrow bumps in missing mass spectra of $pp\to p\pi^+X$: 1004, 1044, and 1094 MeV (observed by Tatischeff, Yonnet et al.)
- Bump widths are in the range 4-15 MeV, determined by experimental resolution
- The 1004 and 1044 MeV states are below the $m_N + m_\pi$ threshold (approximately 1077 MeV, using $m_N \approx 939$ MeV and $m_\pi \approx 138$ MeV)
- The 1094 MeV bump is 19 MeV above the $m_N + m_\pi$ threshold
- Photon energies for expected $\gamma N$ decay of the three states: 68, 112, and 169 MeV (excluded by L'vov and Workman)
- Photon energy for inelastic Compton scattering $\gamma N \to \gamma N^{\prime}$ via the $D_{13}$ or $S_{11}$ resonance at 1520 MeV: $E_\gamma = 774$ MeV
- Parameters of the confining oscillator potential: $V_0 = 296.3$ MeV, $\hbar\omega = 157.4$ MeV; pion exchange potential expectation value $P_{11} = 45.2$ MeV (positive)
- Splitting between the two $N^{\prime}$ states: 40 MeV

## Context
This work extends the experimental observations of narrow baryon states in $pp\to p\pi^+X$ by Tatischeff, Yonnet et al. (1990s) and addresses the contradiction with the Compton scattering analysis of L'vov and Workman, which excluded these states as intermediate resonances. The author proposes that these states belong to the $\underline{20}$-plet of $SU(6)_{FS}$, a representation previously noted by Feynman as having special selection rules. The model contrasts with the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman and Riska (1990s), which explains the low mass of the Roper resonance but cannot account for these states without additional $LL$-coupling. The work enables experimental verification via inelastic Compton scattering $\gamma N \to \gamma N^{\prime}$ at $E_\gamma = 774$ MeV or higher, or through reactions where photons are replaced by pions at appropriate kinematics.
