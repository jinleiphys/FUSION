# Approximate particle number projection with density dependent forces: Superdeformed bands in the A=150 and A=190 regions

> We derive the equations for approximate particle number projection based on mean field wave functions with finite range density dependent forces. As an application ground bands of even-A superdeformed nuclei in the A=150 and A=190 regions are calculated with the Gogny force. We discuss nuclear properties such as quadrupole moments, moments of inertia and quasiparticle spectra, among others, as a function of the angular momentum. We obtain a good overall description.

## Key claim

The paper derives the Lipkin-Nogami (LN) approximation for approximate particle number projection with finite-range density-dependent forces (the Gogny force), and shows that this method eliminates the unphysical sharp superfluid-to-normal phase transition previously seen in cranked HFB calculations, yielding a good overall description of superdeformed ground bands in the A=150 and A=190 regions.

## Method

The authors formulate the Lipkin-Nogami method from the Kamlah expansion to second order, first for non-density-dependent forces and then extended to density-dependent forces like the Gogny interaction, obtaining additional rearrangement terms. They apply the resulting cranked HFB-Lipkin-Nogami (CHFBLN) approximation to calculate superdeformed ground bands of even-even nuclei in the A=190 region (190-192-194Hg and 194Pb) and the A=150 region (152Dy and 150Gd), using the D1S parameterization of the Gogny force and solving the equations with the Conjugate Gradient Method.

## Key numbers

- For the A=190 region, calculations are performed for 190Hg, 192Hg, 194Hg, and 194Pb; for the A=150 region, for 152Dy and 150Gd.
- The Gogny force D1S parameterization (Berger et al., 1984, 1991) is used throughout.
- The Kamlah expansion is carried out to second order (M=2), yielding the coefficients h_0, h_1, and h_2 from equations involving moments of the particle number fluctuation up to fourth order: ⟨(ΔN)^2⟩, ⟨(ΔN)^3⟩, and ⟨(ΔN)^4⟩.
- The coefficient h_2 is given by an expression involving ⟨(H-⟨H⟩)(ΔN)^2⟩, ⟨HΔN⟩, ⟨(ΔN)^3⟩, ⟨(ΔN)^2⟩, and ⟨(ΔN)^4⟩ (equation labeled h_2).
- For density-dependent forces, the effective h_2 parameter (h_2^eff) includes an additional term from the rearrangement of the density dependence, involving the operator ℋ = H + Σ_ij ⟨∂H/∂ρ f_ij(r)⟩ c^†_i c_j.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formulation of the Lipkin-Nogami method for density-dependent forces (Valor et al., 1996, 1997) to superdeformed bands at high spin, building on earlier cranked HFB calculations with the Gogny force (Egido and Robledo, 1993; Egido et al., 1994) that exhibited an unphysical sharp phase transition. The approach contrasts with calculations using the Skyrme force (SkM* parameterization of Bonche and Flocard) where pairing must be added by hand, and with Woods-Saxon plus Strutinsky calculations using monopole pairing (McDermott et al., 1993). The results enable systematic studies of superdeformed bands with a consistent treatment of pairing from the same finite-range force that describes low-spin properties, and open the possibility of applying the method to other density-dependent forces and to odd-mass nuclei.
