# Beta-decay properties of Zr and Mo neutron-rich isotopes

> Gamow-Teller strength distributions, beta-decay half-lives, and beta-delayed neutron emission are investigated in neutron-rich Zr and Mo isotopes within a deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation. The approach is based on a self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field with pairing correlations and residual separable particle-hole and particle-particle forces. Comparison with recent measurements of half-lives stresses the important role that nuclear deformation plays in the description of beta-decay properties in this mass region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear deformation is essential for reproducing measured beta-decay half-lives of neutron-rich Zr and Mo isotopes, with spherical approaches failing to match experimental data; the authors find that self-consistent prolate and oblate deformations from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations bracket the experimental values, suggesting that the true ground-state shapes lie between these configurations.

## Method

The authors performed deformed quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations built on a self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field with BCS pairing, using the SLy4 Skyrme force. They computed potential energy curves via constrained Hartree-Fock calculations as a function of quadrupole deformation, then calculated Gamow-Teller strength distributions, beta-decay half-lives, and beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities for the equilibrium shapes (prolate, oblate, and spherical minima) of even-even Zr and Mo isotopes.

## Key numbers

- For Zr isotopes, ground-state deformations are found at prolate values of β ≈ 0.4, with oblate minima at β ≈ −0.2 separated by potential energy barriers of 3–5 MeV
- For Mo isotopes, the lightest isotope 104Mo has a prolate ground state with an oblate minimum very close in energy; 106Mo shows practically degenerate oblate-prolate minima; heavier isotopes have oblate ground states at β ≈ −0.2
- The particle-hole residual interaction coupling strength used is χ^(ph)_GT = 0.15 MeV; the particle-particle strength is κ^(pp)_GT = 0.03 MeV
- Pairing gaps for protons and neutrons are roughly around 1 MeV, with pairing strengths G_p ∼ 0.25 MeV and G_n ∼ 0.30 MeV for cutoffs of about 5 MeV around the Fermi level
- For 104Zr and 106Zr, oblate calculations give half-lives lower than experiment while self-consistent prolate deformations produce somewhat larger ones, with the experimental values falling between the two predictions
- Experimental deformations include β = 0.47(7) for 104Zr and β = 0.36(7) for 106Mo extracted from half-lives of their 2+ states

## Context

This work extends previous deformed QRPA calculations that used phenomenological mean fields (Nilsson or Woods-Saxon potentials) by employing a self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock basis with SLy4 force, which was fitted to properties of unstable nuclei. The results are compared to recent measurements by Pereira et al. (2009) and to prior calculations using the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) by Moller et al., the finite-range liquid-drop model (FRLDM), Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approaches, and relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations. The study enables improved predictions for r-process nucleosynthesis modeling in the A ∼ 100–110 mass region, particularly for isotopes where experimental masses are unavailable and theoretical mass formulas (such as Duflo-Zuker or SLy4) must be used for Q_β and S_n values.
