# Medium effects on the electromagnetic form factors of the $ρ$ meson

> The dynamics of partons inside the light $ρ$ meson is found to be essential for its properties and internal structure, both in free space and in the nuclear medium. In this paper, we systematically investigate the in-medium structure changes of $ρ^+$ mesons within the covariant Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, utilizing the Schwinger proper-time regularization scheme. We solve the Bethe-Salpeter equations to guarantee the bound meson-state condition. At the quark level, the nuclear medium effects are also derived within the same NJL model to maintain a consistent approach with the in-medium $ρ^+$ meson electromagnetic form factors. To this end, we analyze the spacelike electromagnetic form factors of the $ρ^+$ meson in free space and in a nuclear medium. We find that the charge radius and quadrupole moment of the $ρ^+$ meson increase with increasing nuclear matter density, while the magnetic moment decreases, in agreement with the existing previous theoretical predictions. The enhancement of the $ρ^+$ meson charge radius at normal density relative to that in free space is about 11\% (0.08 fm), while the reduction of $ρ^+$ meson magnetic moment is about 8\% (0.20 $μ_N$). Our predictions for the charge radius, magnetic moment, and quadrupole moment of the $ρ^+$ meson in both free space and nuclear medium, remain challenging to be verified experimentally.

The charge radius and quadrupole moment of the ρ⁺ meson increase with nuclear matter density while the magnetic moment decreases, with the authors noting that these predictions remain challenging to verify experimentally.

## Method
The authors used the covariant Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with the Schwinger proper-time regularization scheme to compute the in-medium electromagnetic form factors of the ρ⁺ meson. They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equations to enforce the bound meson-state condition and derived nuclear medium effects at the quark level within the same NJL model for consistency. The spacelike electromagnetic form factors were analyzed in both free space and symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers
- Enhancement of the ρ⁺ meson charge radius at normal nuclear density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³) relative to free space: about 11% (0.08 fm)
- Reduction of the ρ⁺ meson magnetic moment at normal density: about 8% (0.20 μ_N)
- ρ meson effective mass at ρ_B/ρ₀ = 1.0: 694 MeV, a reduction of about 10% relative to the free-space mass of 770 MeV
- ρ⁺ meson charge radius in free space: 0.67 fm; at ρ_B/ρ₀ = 1.0: 0.75 fm
- ρ⁺ meson magnetic moment in free space: 2.53 μ_N; at ρ_B/ρ₀ = 1.0: 2.33 μ_N
- ρ⁺ meson quadrupole moment in free space: -0.057 fm²; at ρ_B/ρ₀ = 1.0: -0.071 fm²

## Context
This work extends previous studies of ρ meson electromagnetic form factors in the nuclear medium by deMelo (2018), who used a hybrid model combining the light front constituent quark model and quark-meson coupling model. The authors also compare their ρ meson mass reduction with results from QCD sum rule calculations by Hatsuda (1991) and inverse QCD sum rule analysis by Mutuk (2025), which reported reductions of 10-20%. The consistent treatment within the NJL model for both the meson structure and the nuclear medium enables systematic investigation of density-dependent modifications, though experimental verification of the predicted charge radius, magnetic moment, and quadrupole moment remains an open challenge.
