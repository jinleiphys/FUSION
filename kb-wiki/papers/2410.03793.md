# Accessing Generalized Parton Distributions through $2 \to 3$ exclusive processes

> We review our results on a new class of $2 \to 3$ exclusive processes, as a probe of both chiral-even and chiral-odd quark GPDs. We consider the exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair, in the kinematics where the pair has a large invariant mass, described in the collinear factorization framework. We cover the whole kinematical range from medium energies in fixed target experiments to very large energies of colliders, by considering the experimental conditions of JLab 12-GeV, COMPASS, future EIC and LHC (in ultra-peripheral collisions) cases. Our analysis covers neutral and charged rho-mesons, as well as charged pions. The case of the rho-meson, depending on its polarization, provides access to either chiral-even or chiral-odd GPDs, at leading twist. We find that the order of magnitude of the obtained cross sections are sufficiently large for a dedicated experimental analysis to be performed, especially at JLab. Furthermore, we compute the linear photon beam polarization asymmetry, which we find to be sizeable, in the case of a longitudinally polarized $ρ$-meson or of a charged pion. These predictions are obtained for both asymptotic distribution amplitude (DA) and holographic DA.

# Accessing Generalized Parton Distributions through $2 \to 3$ exclusive processes

## Key claim

The exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair with large invariant mass provides a viable channel for probing both chiral-even and chiral-odd quark GPDs at leading twist, with cross sections sufficiently large for dedicated experimental analysis, particularly at JLab 12-GeV, provided that the kinematic cuts of $-u', -t' > 1$ GeV$^2$ and $-t < 0.5$ GeV$^2$ are applied to avoid resonances and ensure collinear factorization.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order amplitude for the process $\gamma + N \to \gamma + M + N'$ (where M is a $\rho$-meson of any charge and polarization, or a charged pion) using collinear factorization, convolving a short-distance coefficient function with a GPD and a meson distribution amplitude. The GPDs are parametrized via double distributions using two scenarios (standard and valence) for the polarized and transversity PDFs, while the meson DAs are taken in both asymptotic and holographic forms. The unpolarized differential cross sections and linear photon beam polarization asymmetries are evaluated for the experimental conditions of JLab 12-GeV, COMPASS, future EIC, and LHC in ultra-peripheral collisions.

## Key numbers

- Expected number of events at JLab (integrated luminosity 864 fb$^{-1}$): $1.3\text{--}2.4 \times 10^5$ for $\rho^0_L$, $2.1\text{--}4.2 \times 10^4$ for $\rho^0_T$, and $0.3\text{--}1.8 \times 10^5$ for $\pi^+$ on a proton target.
- Expected number of events at future EIC (integrated luminosity 10 fb$^{-1}$): $1.3\text{--}2.4 \times 10^4$ for $\rho^0_L$, $1.2\text{--}2.4 \times 10^3$ for $\rho^0_T$, and $0.2\text{--}1.3 \times 10^4$ for $\pi^+$.
- Expected number of events at LHC in UPCs (integrated luminosity 1200 nb$^{-1}$): $0.9\text{--}1.6 \times 10^4$ for $\rho^0_L$, $0.8\text{--}1.7 \times 10^3$ for $\rho^0_T$, and $1.6\text{--}9.3 \times 10^3$ for $\pi^+$.
- At small skewness ($S_{\gamma N} > 300$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to $5 \cdot 10^{-5} < \xi < 5 \cdot 10^{-3}$), expected events at EIC: $0.6\text{--}1.2 \times 10^3$ for $\rho^0_L$ and $1.4\text{--}5.0 \times 10^2$ for $\pi^+$; at LHC in UPCs: $4.1\text{--}8.1 \times 10^2$ for $\rho^0_L$ and $1.0\text{--}3.4 \times 10^2$ for $\pi^+$.
- Linear photon beam polarization asymmetry (LPA) reaches up to 60% for the $\rho^+_L$ and $\pi^+$ cases, constructed from differential cross sections in $M_{\gamma M}^2$.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Ivanov (2002), Enberg (2006), and El Beiyad (2010) to use two-meson photoproduction for accessing chiral-odd GPDs, and the specific $\gamma \rho_T$ channel proposed by Boussarie (2016). It builds on the factorization proof for $2 \to 3$ exclusive processes by Qiu (2022) and applies it to photon-meson pair production, covering both chiral-even GPDs (accessible via DVCS and DVMP) and chiral-odd GPDs (which vanish in standard $2 \to 2$ processes like transversely polarized $\rho$-meson electroproduction, as shown by Diehl (1998) and Collins (1999)). The results enable experimental extraction of GPDs across multiple facilities, with the linear polarization asymmetry providing a tool for disentangling GPD models, while the small-$\xi$ reach at EIC and LHC allows scanning the $x$-dependence of GPDs beyond the $x = \pm \xi$ constraint of LO DVCS.
