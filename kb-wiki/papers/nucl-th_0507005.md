# Density Dependent Parametrization Models: Formalism and Applications

> In this work we derive a formalism to incorporate asymmetry and temperature effects in the Brown-Rho (BR) scaled lagrangian model in a mean field theory. The lagrangian density discussed in this work requires less parameters than the usual models with density dependent couplings. We also present the formalism with the inclusion of the eight lightest baryons, two lightest leptons, beta equilibrium and charge neutrality in order to apply the BR scaled model to the study of neutron stars. The results are again compared with the ones obtained from another density dependent parametrization model. The role played by the rearrangement term at T=0 for nuclear or neutron star matter and at finite temperature is investigated. The BR scaled model is shown to be a good tool in studies involving density dependent effective masses and in astrophysics applications.

## Key claim

The Brown-Rho (BR) scaled Lagrangian model, when extended to include asymmetry and temperature effects in a mean-field theory, provides a good tool for studying density-dependent effective masses and astrophysical applications, but the specific parametrization (S3 vs. MA) significantly affects the predicted behavior of meson masses and coupling constants at high densities.

## Method

The authors derived a formalism incorporating asymmetry and finite-temperature effects into the Brown-Rho scaled Lagrangian model within a mean-field approximation, using density-dependent meson masses and coupling constants that scale with baryon density. They then extended this formalism to include the eight lightest baryons (nucleons, Lambda, Sigma+, Sigma0, Sigma-, Xi-, Xi0) and two lightest leptons (electron and muon) under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions to study neutron star matter. The results were compared with those from the TW model (Typel-Wolter density-dependent parametrization) and with constant-coupling models (NL3 and TM1).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density for S3 parametrization: 0.155 fm^-3 (marked with asterisk as not given in original Song reference)
- Nuclear saturation density for MA parametrization: 0.148 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: 16.3 MeV for MA, 16.1 MeV for S3
- Incompressibility: 269 MeV for S3, 258 MeV for MA
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 32.0 MeV for both S3 and MA
- Ratio M*/M (nucleon effective mass at saturation): 0.78 for S3, 0.9 for MA
- Landau mass ratio m*_L/M: 0.68 for S3, 0.748 for MA
- Scaling parameter y=0.28 for S3 (giving Phi(rho_0)=0.78, i.e., 22% mass reduction at saturation)
- Scaling parameter y=0.1 for MA (giving 10% mass reduction at saturation, as found experimentally by Naruki (2004))
- Nucleon mass M=939 MeV, omega meson mass m_v=783 MeV, rho meson mass m_rho=763 MeV
- Scalar meson mass m_s: 700 MeV for S3, 500 MeV for MA

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling approach originally proposed by Brown and Rho (1991) and further developed by Song (1998) to include asymmetry effects via the rho meson (the first such inclusion in a BR scaled model, as stated by the authors) and finite-temperature effects using the formalism previously developed for density-dependent hadron field theory by Typel (2001, 2002). The results are compared with the TW model of Typel and Wolter (1999) and with constant-coupling models such as NL3 (Lalazissis, 1997) and TM1 (Sugahara and Toki, 1994). The work enables future applications to neutron star structure calculations and finite nuclei studies, with the authors noting that work on calculating the spin-orbit splitting strength in finite nuclei is in progress.
