# Revisiting the hypertriton lifetime puzzle

> Conflicting values of the hypertriton ($_Λ^{3}$H) lifetime were extracted in recent relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments. The ALICE Collaboration's reported $_Λ^{3}$H lifetime $τ(_Λ^{3}$H) is compatible within measurement uncertainties with the free $Λ$ lifetime $τ_Λ$, as naively expected for a loosely bound $Λ$ hyperon in $_Λ^{3}$H, whereas STAR's reported range of $τ(_Λ^{3}$H) values is considerably shorter: $τ_{\rm STAR}(_Λ^{3}$H)$\sim$(0.4-0.7)$τ_Λ$. This $_Λ^{3}$H lifetime puzzle is revisited theoretically using $_Λ^{3}$H three-body wavefunctions generated in a chiral effective field theory approach to calculate the decay rate $Γ(_Λ^{3}$H$\,\to ^3$He$\,+π^-$). Significant but opposing contributions arise from $ΣNN$ admixtures in $_Λ^{3}$H and from $π^-$-$^3$He final-state interaction. Evaluating the inclusive $π^-$ decay rate $Γ_{π^-}(_Λ^{3}$H) via a branching ratio $Γ(_Λ^{3}$H$\,\to ^{3}$He+$π^-)/Γ_{π^-}(_Λ^{3}$H) determined in helium bubble-chamber experiments, and adding $Γ_{π^0}(_Λ^{3}$H) through the $ΔI=\frac{1}{2}$ rule, we derive $τ(_Λ^{3}$H) assuming several different values of the $Λ$ separation energy $B_Λ(_Λ^{3}$H). It is concluded that each of ALICE and STAR reported $τ(_Λ^{3}$H) intervals implies its own constraint on $B_Λ(_Λ^{3}$H): $B_Λ\lesssim 0.1$ MeV for ALICE, $B_Λ\gtrsim 0.2$ MeV for STAR.

## Key claim

The hypertriton lifetime puzzle can be resolved if each experimental collaboration's reported lifetime interval implies its own constraint on the poorly known Λ separation energy BΛ(Λ³H): BΛ ≲ 0.1 MeV for ALICE, BΛ ≳ 0.2 MeV for STAR, rather than requiring a single universal value.

## Method

The authors calculated the partial decay rate Γ(Λ³H → ³He + π⁻) using Λ³H three-body wavefunctions from a chiral effective field theory (χEFT) leading-order YN interaction within the no-core shell model (NCSM) method, extrapolated to infinite model space. They incorporated ΣNN admixtures (≈0.5% probability) and π⁻-³He final-state interaction via distorted waves from a pion optical potential, then derived the inclusive π⁻ decay rate using the experimental branching ratio R₃ = Γ(Λ³H → ³He+π⁻)/Γπ⁻(Λ³H) = 0.35±0.04 from helium bubble-chamber experiments, and added Γπ⁰(Λ³H) via the ΔI = ½ rule.

## Key numbers

- The calculated Λ³H lifetime at BΛ = 159 keV is τ(Λ³H) = 180±21 ps, shorter than τΛ = 263±2 ps by (30±8)%.
- The ΣNN admixture probability PΣ ≲ 0.5% reduces the two-body decay rate by ≈9% from the purely ΛNN value.
- The use of distorted waves for the π⁻-³He final-state interaction increases the two-body decay rate by ≈15% compared to plane waves.
- At BΛ = 69 keV, the calculated lifetime is τ(Λ³H) = 234±27 ps, compatible with ALICE's reported value.
- At BΛ = 410 keV (STAR's mean claimed value), the extrapolated lifetime is τ(Λ³H) = 163±18 ps, overlapping with STAR's reported range.
- The branching ratio R₃ = 0.35±0.04 from Keyes (1973) bubble-chamber experiments was used.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Kamada et al. (1998) using a complete Faddeev approach with the Nijmegen SC89 interaction, and by Gal and Garcilazo (2019) who derived a τ(Λ³H) value shorter than τΛ by ≈20%. It contradicts the naive expectation that a loosely bound Λ hyperon in Λ³H should have a lifetime close to τΛ, showing instead that the lifetime varies strongly with the poorly known BΛ. The results enable future experiments at MAMI, JLab, J-PARC, and ELPH to resolve the puzzle by precisely determining BΛ(Λ³H) to better than 50 keV, which would also constrain two-body YN and three-body YNN forces relevant to the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars.
