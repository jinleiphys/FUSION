# Heavy Quarkonium Physics

> This report is the result of the collaboration and research effort of the Quarkonium Working Group over the last three years. It provides a comprehensive overview of the state of the art in heavy-quarkonium theory and experiment, covering quarkonium spectroscopy, decay, and production, the determination of QCD parameters from quarkonium observables, quarkonia in media, and the effects on quarkonia of physics beyond the Standard Model. An introduction to common theoretical and experimental tools is included. Future opportunities for research in quarkonium physics are also discussed.

## Key claim

This report provides a comprehensive overview of the state of the art in heavy-quarkonium theory and experiment, covering spectroscopy, decay, production, QCD parameter determination, quarkonia in media, and beyond-Standard-Model effects, but it is a review document produced by the Quarkonium Working Group rather than a presentation of a single new experimental or theoretical result.

## Method

The Quarkonium Working Group collaborated over three years to compile and review the existing literature on heavy-quarkonium physics. The report synthesizes theoretical frameworks including Nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD), potential Nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD), and velocity Nonrelativistic QCD (vNRQCD), alongside experimental results and lattice QCD simulations, to present a unified overview of the field.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass: \(m_c = 1.15 \div 1.35\) GeV (MS-bar mass at scale 2 GeV)
- Bottom quark mass: \(m_b = 4.1 \div 4.4\) GeV (MS-bar mass at scale 2 GeV)
- Top quark mass: \(m_t = 174.3 \pm 5.1\) GeV
- Light quark masses: \(m_u = 1.5 \div 4.0\) MeV, \(m_d = 4 \div 8\) MeV, \(m_s = 80 \div 130\) MeV (MS-bar masses at scale 2 GeV)
- For a \(b\bar{b}\) state where \(m/mv^2 \sim 10\), lattice simulations would require lattices as large as \(100^4\), which are beyond present computing capabilities

## Context

This report extends and summarizes the work of the Quarkonium Working Group, covering the same scope as earlier reviews of heavy-quarkonium physics but with updated results through 2004. It builds on the effective field theory frameworks of NRQCD (Caswell 1985, Thacker 1990, Bodwin 1994), pNRQCD (Pineda 1997, Brambilla 1999), and vNRQCD (Luke 1999, Manohar 1999, Hoang 2002), and it enables future research by identifying open questions in quarkonium spectroscopy, production, and in-medium behavior.
