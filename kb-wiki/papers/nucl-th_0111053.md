# Multipole Expansion for Relativistic Coulomb Excitation

> We derive a general expression for the multipole expansion of the electro-magnetic interaction in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which can be employed in higher-order dynamical calculations of Coulomb excitation. The interaction has diagonal as well as off-diagonal multipole components, associated with the intrinsic and relative coordinates of projectile and target. A simple truncation in the off-diagonal components gives excellent results in first-order perturbation theory for distant collisions and for beam energies up to 200 MeV/nucleon.

# Multipole Expansion for Relativistic Coulomb Excitation

## Key claim
The multipole expansion of the electromagnetic interaction in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, including both diagonal and off-diagonal components caused by Lorentz contraction, can be truncated to just the first off-diagonal terms (Λ=0,2) and still give excellent results in first-order perturbation theory for distant collisions, but only for beam velocities up to v/c ≈ 0.6 (about 200 MeV/nucleon); at higher velocities, additional off-diagonal terms become necessary.

## Method
The authors derived a general multipole expansion of the Liénard-Wiechert potential for a charged particle moving on a straight-line trajectory with constant velocity, including the effect of the convection current but ignoring magnetization current. They expressed the potential in terms of radial integrals involving spherical Bessel functions and angular matrix elements containing Legendre functions of the second kind, then tested a truncation to diagonal (Λ=0) and first off-diagonal (Λ=2) terms by comparing the resulting orbital integrals in first-order perturbation theory against the exact relativistic results of Winther and Alder (1979).

## Key numbers
- The truncation to Λ=0,2 gives excellent results for beam velocities up to v/c = 0.6, corresponding to about 200 MeV/nucleon.
- At v/c = 0.8, some discrepancy starts to occur, indicating the need to include higher values of Λ.
- The functions g_λ(β) decrease rapidly as a function of λ when (v/c)^2 < 0.5.
- For the dipole field, the convection current contribution S'_10 ≈ -β² (iω/c) S_00, while S'_11 = 0.
- For the quadrupole field, the convection current contribution S'_2m ≈ -(iβ²/2)(ω/v) S_1,m √(4-m²)/3.
- The exact relativistic orbital integrals include factors G_lm such as G_10 = G_20 = G_2±2 = 1/γ, G_1±1 = 1, and G_2±1 = 1 - β²/2.

## Context
This work extends the non-relativistic Coulomb excitation formalism of Winther and Alder (1979) to include relativistic effects through a full multipole decomposition, and is similar in approach to the method developed by Baltz et al. (1991) for e⁺e⁻ pair production. The derived form factors are intended for use in higher-order dynamical calculations of two-body breakup reactions, such as continuum discretized coupled-channels calculations by Nunes and Thompson (1999) or numerical methods evolving the two-body wave function in coordinate space by Esbensen, Bertsch, and Bertulani (1995), Kido, Yabana, and Suzuki (1996), and Melezhik and Baye (1999). The work enables more realistic treatment of Coulomb dissociation of halo nuclei at intermediate energies where both relativistic and higher-order effects are important.
