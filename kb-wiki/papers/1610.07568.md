# Functional renormalization group studies of nuclear and neutron matter

> Functional renormalization group (FRG) methods applied to calculations of isospin-symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter as well as neutron matter are reviewed. The approach is based on a chiral Lagrangian expressed in terms of nucleon and meson degrees of freedom as appropriate for the hadronic phase of QCD with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry. Fluctuations beyond mean-field approximation are treated solving Wetterich's FRG flow equations. Nuclear thermodynamics and the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition are investigated in detail, both in symmetric matter and as a function of the proton fraction in asymmetric matter. The equations of state at zero temperature of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter are found to be in good agreement with advanced ab-initio many-body computations. Contacts with perturbative many-body approaches (in-medium chiral perturbation theory) are discussed. As an interesting test case, the density dependence of the pion mass in the medium is investigated. The question of chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear and neutron matter is addressed. A stabilization of the phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry is found to persist up to high baryon densities once fluctuations beyond mean-field are included. Neutron star matter including beta equilibrium is discussed under the aspect of the constraints imposed by the existence of two-solar-mass neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how functional renormalization group (FRG) methods, applied to a chiral nucleon-meson model, can treat fluctuations beyond mean-field approximation in nuclear and neutron matter, finding that the phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry remains stabilized up to high baryon densities once these fluctuations are included, contrary to mean-field predictions of a chiral phase transition at moderate densities.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral Lagrangian with nucleon and meson degrees of freedom (the chiral nucleon-meson model), including scalar-isoscalar sigma and pseudoscalar-isovector pion fields coupled to nucleons, plus vector bosons to parametrize short-range repulsion. They solve Wetterich's FRG flow equations to incorporate fluctuations beyond mean-field, treating pions and nucleon-hole excitations as the prominent low-energy modes while suppressing fluctuations of heavy degrees of freedom. The approach is applied to isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, asymmetric nuclear matter as a function of proton fraction, and pure neutron matter, including beta equilibrium for neutron star matter.

## Context

This review extends earlier mean-field studies of nuclear matter using NJL and PNJL models (Buballa 2005, Fukushima 2004, 2008) that predicted a first-order chiral phase transition at baryon chemical potentials around 0.9-1 GeV, a region where only the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition is empirically observed (Chomaz 2001, Agostinelli 2005). The FRG approach enables non-perturbative treatment of fluctuations that stabilize chiral symmetry to higher densities, and the resulting zero-temperature equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter agree with advanced ab-initio many-body computations. The work enables more reliable modeling of neutron star matter under the constraint of two-solar-mass neutron stars, and establishes contact with in-medium chiral perturbation theory (Holt, Kaiser, Weise 2016).
