# Shell-model calculation of neutrinoless double-$β$ decay of $^{76}$Ge

> In this article we present a more detailed version of our recent Rapid Communication [Phys. Rev. C 90, 051301(R) (2014)] where we calculate the nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-$β$ decay of $^{76}$Ge. For the calculations we use a novel method that has perfect convergence properties and allows one to obtain the nonclosure nuclear matrix elements for $^{76}$Ge with a 1% accuracy. We present a new way of calculation of the optimal closure energy, using this energy with the closure approximation provides the most accurate closure nuclear matrix elements. In addition, we present a new analysis of the heavy-neutrino-exchange nuclear matrix elements, and we compare occupation probabilities and Gamow-Teller strength with experimental data.

# Shell-model calculation of neutrinoless double-β decay of ⁷⁶Ge

## Key claim

The mixed method for calculating neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements achieves convergence within 1% accuracy for ⁷⁶Ge using only about 100 intermediate states per spin-parity, but this result depends on the choice of the JUN45 effective Hamiltonian and the restricted jj44 model space, which lacks the f₇/₂ and g₇/₂ orbitals and therefore does not satisfy the Ikeda sum rule.

## Method

The authors used the NuShellX@MSU shell-model code with the jj44 model space (core ⁵⁶Ni, valence orbitals f₅/₂, p₃/₂, p₁/₂, g₉/₂) and the JUN45 effective Hamiltonian. They applied the mixed method, which combines exact treatment of low-lying intermediate states of ⁷⁶As with a closure approximation for higher-energy states, using a cutoff parameter N to separate the two regimes. The nuclear matrix elements were calculated for both light-neutrino-exchange and heavy-neutrino-exchange mechanisms, with short-range correlations treated using CD-Bonn, AV18, and Miller-Spencer parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- Mixed method total NME for light-neutrino exchange with CD-Bonn SRC: M⁰ν_total = 3.57 (compared to closure value of 3.35)
- Mixed method achieves 1% accuracy using only the first 100 intermediate states for each J^π of ⁷⁶As
- With closure energy varying from 2 MeV to 10 MeV, mixed NMEs change by about 2%, while closure NMEs change by 12%
- Optimal closure energy for ⁷⁶Ge: approximately 3.5 MeV (significantly lower than the standard value of 9.41 MeV from Tomoda)
- Quenching factor of 0.64 used for Gamow-Teller strength to describe 2νββ decay data
- For CD-Bonn SRC with quenching factors of 0.64² for J^π=1⁺ and 0.40² for J^π=2⁻ contributions, the NME reduces to 2.369 rather than 3.572

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Rapid Communication (Sen'kov and Horoi, Phys. Rev. C 90, 051301(R) 2014) and applies the mixed method previously developed for ⁴⁸Ca and ⁸²Se to the important case of ⁷⁶Ge, which is the isotope used in the GERDA and MAJORANA experiments. The results are compared with other approaches including QRPA (Vesely 2012, Faessler 2011, Simkovic 2013), the interacting boson model (IBM-2), the generator coordinate method, and the projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model. The I-pair decomposition analysis connects to a new method proposed by Brown (2014) for calculating matrix elements and to constraints from pair transfer reactions (Freeman 2007). The paper enables more reliable interpretation of experimental limits from germanium-based experiments and provides a method to estimate optimal closure energies for other isotopes.
