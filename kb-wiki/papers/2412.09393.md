# Extended Skyrme effective interactions with higher-order momentum-dependence for transport models and neutron stars

> The recently developed extended Skyrme effective interaction based on the so-called N3LO Skyrme pseudopotential is generalized to the general N$n$LO case by incorporating the derivative terms up to 2$n$th-order into the central term of the pseudopotential. The corresponding expressions of Hamiltonian density and single-nucleon potential are derived within the Hartree-Fock approximation under general nonequilibrium conditions. The inclusion of the higher-order derivative terms provides additional higher-order momentum dependence for the single-nucleon potential, and in particular, we find that the N5LO single-nucleon potential with momentum dependent terms up to $p^{10}$ can give a nice description for the empirical nucleon optical potential up to energy of $2$ GeV. At the same time, the density-dependent terms in the extended Skyrme effective interaction are extended correspondingly in the spirit of the Fermi momentum expansion, which allows highly flexible variation of density behavior for both the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state and the symmetry energy. Based on the Skyrme pseudopotential up to N3LO, N4LO and N5LO, we construct a series of interactions with the nucleon optical potential having different high-momentum behaviors and with the symmetry potentials featuring different linear isospin-splitting coefficients for nucleon effective mass, by which we study the properties of nuclear matter and neutron stars. Furthermore, within the lattice BUU transport model, some benchmark simulations with selected interactions are performed for the Au+Au collisions at a beam energy of $1.23$ GeV/nucleon, and the predicted collective flows for protons are found to nicely agree with the data measured by HADES collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the extended Skyrme effective interaction to arbitrary N$n$LO order by including derivative terms up to 2$n$th-order in the central term, and they find that the N5LO single-nucleon potential with momentum-dependent terms up to $p^{10}$ can describe the empirical nucleon optical potential up to 2 GeV, whereas the N3LO version fails above 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors derive the Hamiltonian density and single-nucleon potential within the Hartree-Fock approximation under general nonequilibrium conditions for the N$n$LO Skyrme pseudopotential, extending the central term to include up to tenth-order derivative terms (N5LO) and the density-dependent terms via a Fermi momentum expansion. They construct 24 parameter sets based on N3LO, N4LO, and N5LO pseudopotentials, fitting to empirical nucleon optical potential data, symmetric nuclear matter properties, and neutron star constraints, then implement selected interactions in the lattice BUU transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 1.23 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The N5LO single-nucleon potential with momentum-dependent terms up to $p^{10}$ describes the empirical nucleon optical potential up to an energy of 2 GeV.
- The N3LO single-nucleon potential fails to describe the saturated behavior of the nuclear optical potential when kinetic energy exceeds 1 GeV.
- The isoscalar nucleon effective mass at saturation density for the three models is $m^{\ast}_{s,0}/m \approx 0.77$, with an alternative N3LO model having $m^{\ast}_{s,0} = 0.83m$.
- Benchmark simulations are performed for Au+Au collisions at a beam energy of 1.23 GeV/nucleon (corresponding to $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.4$ GeV).
- The density of nuclear matter produced in HICs at incident energies of about 2 GeV/nucleon is expected to exceed $3\rho_{0}$.
- Eight symmetry potentials are constructed with $\Delta m_{1}^{\ast}(\rho_{0})$ values of $\pm 0.7$, $\pm 0.5$, $\pm 0.3$, and $\pm 0.1$.

## Context

This work extends the N3LO Skyrme pseudopotential approach of Wang (2018) and Wang (2023), which could describe the nucleon optical potential only up to 1 GeV, by adding eighth- and tenth-order derivative terms to reach N5LO. The new interactions enable transport model simulations for heavy-ion collisions at incident energies up to 2 GeV/nucleon, such as those conducted by HADES at GSI, and simultaneously allow studies of neutron star properties through the flexible density-dependent terms based on the Fermi momentum expansion of Patra (2022).
