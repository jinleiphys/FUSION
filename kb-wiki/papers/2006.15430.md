# Constraints from GW170817 on the bulk viscosity of neutron star matter and the r-mode instability

> We perform a systematic study of the dependence of the r-mode phenomenology in normal fluid pulsar neutron stars on the symmetry energy slope parameter $L$. An essential ingredient in this study is the bulk viscosity, which is evaluated consistently for several equations of state corresponding to different values of the slope parameter $L$. Direct Urca processes, which are allowed from a critical $L$-value onwards, enhance the bulk viscosity and have large influence on the $r$-mode features, such as the instability boundary and spin-down properties of newborn neutron stars. The magnitude of the changes in the $r$-mode properties induced by the direct Urca processes are driven by the $L$-value of the equation of state and the mass of the pulsar. The study has been done by using a family of equations of state of $β$-equilibrated neutron star matter obtained with the finite range simple effective interaction, which provides realistic results for nuclear matter and finite nuclei properties. These equations of state predict the same properties in symmetric nuclear matter and have the same value of the symmetry energy parameter, $E_s(ρ_0)$, but differ in the slope parameter $L$. The range chosen for the variation of $L$ is decided from the tidal deformability data extracted from the GW170817 event and the maximum mass constraint.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the slope parameter \(L\) of the nuclear symmetry energy, constrained by the GW170817 tidal deformability data, determines whether direct Urca processes are allowed in neutron star matter and thereby controls the bulk viscosity and the r-mode instability boundary; the authors find that the direct Urca process becomes possible only above a critical \(L\)-value, and that the magnitude of its effect on r-mode properties depends on both the \(L\)-value and the pulsar mass.

## Method

The authors used a family of equations of state generated with the finite-range simple effective interaction (SEI), all having the same symmetry energy at saturation \(E_s(\rho_0)=35\) MeV and the same incompressibility \(K(\rho_0)=246\) MeV (corresponding to \(\gamma=1/2\)), but differing in the slope parameter \(L\) over the range \(70 \leq L \leq 90\) MeV. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, and computed the bulk viscosity from direct and modified Urca processes in the high-frequency limit appropriate for r-mode oscillations. The r-mode instability boundary and spin-down properties were then evaluated for neutron stars of 1.4 \(M_\odot\) and 1.8 \(M_\odot\).

## Key numbers

* The slope parameter \(L\) was varied in the range 70 MeV \(\leq L \leq\) 90 MeV, with the characteristic (stiffest) value being \(L = 76.26\) MeV.
* The symmetry energy at saturation was fixed at \(E_s(\rho_0) = 35\) MeV.
* The nuclear matter incompressibility was \(K(\rho_0) = 245.6\) MeV (rounded to 246 MeV in the text).
* The saturation density was \(\rho_0 = 0.1571\) fm\(^{-3}\) and the binding energy per particle at saturation was \(-16.0\) MeV.
* The maximum neutron star mass for the \(L=70\) MeV set was 1.85 \(M_\odot\), which does not reach the \(\sim 2 M_\odot\) constraint; sets with \(L \geq 76.26\) MeV are compatible with the \(\sim 2 M_\odot\) maximum mass constraint.
* The mass-weighted tidal deformability from GW170817 was reported as \(\tilde{\Lambda} = 300^{+420}_{-230}\) with chirp mass \(\mathcal{M} = 1.186^{+0.001}_{-0.001} M_\odot\) and component masses \(M_1 \in (1.36, 1.60) M_\odot\) and \(M_2 \in (1.16, 1.36) M_\odot\).
* The bulk viscosity under direct Urca scales as \(T^4\) (Eq. 12), while under modified Urca it scales as \(T^6\) (Eqs. 13-16).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Alford et al. (2012, 2019) on the role of bulk viscosity in r-mode damping, and the study by Vidana (2012) which showed that larger \(L\)-values shift the high-temperature instability boundary to lower temperatures. The authors use the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint from Abbott et al. (2017, 2019) to narrow the allowed range of \(L\), complementing earlier constraints from heavy-ion collision studies by Krastev and Li (2019) and from \(\pi^+/\pi^-\) production and elliptic flow by Xiao et al. (2009), Kohley et al. (2012), and Hong et al. (2014). The results enable predictions of the r-mode instability boundary and spin-down evolution for newborn neutron stars with a specific equation-of-state family, and can be used to assess the detectability of gravitational waves from r-mode emission.
