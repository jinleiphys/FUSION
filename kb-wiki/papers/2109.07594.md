# Mean-field update in the JAM microscopic model: Mean-field effects on collective flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2-20$ GeV energies

> The beam energy dependence of the directed flow is a sensitive probe for the properties of strongly interacting matter. We consider different implementations of momentum-dependent hadronic mean fields in the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) framework. First, Lorentz scalar implementation of a Skyrme type potential is examined. Then, full implementation of the Skyrme type potential as a Lorentz vector in the RQMD approach is proposed. We find that scalar implementation of the Skyrme force is too weak to generate repulsion explaining observed data of sideward flows at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}<10$ GeV, while vector implementation gives collective flows compatible with the data for a wide range of beam energies $2.7 <\sqrt{s_{NN}}<20$ GeV. We show that our approach reproduces the negative proton directed flow at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}>10$ GeV discovered by experiments. We discuss the dynamical generation mechanisms of the directed flow within a conventional hadronic mean field. A positive slope of proton directed flow is generated predominantly during compression stages of heavy-ion collisions by the strong repulsive interaction due to high baryon densities. In contrast, in the expansion stages of the collision, the negative directed flow is generated more strongly than the positive one by the tilted expansion and shadowing by the spectator matter. At lower collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}<10$ GeV, the positive flow wins against the negative flow because of a long compression time. On the other hand, at higher energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}>10$ GeV, negative flow wins because of shorter compression time and longer expansion time. A transition beam energy from positive to negative flow is highly sensitive to the strength of the interaction.

## Key claim

The transition from positive to negative proton directed flow slope at around $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=10$ GeV can be explained by a conventional hadronic mean-field model (without invoking a phase transition) if the Skyrme-type potential is implemented as a Lorentz vector rather than a Lorentz scalar, with the balance between positive flow from compression-stage repulsion and negative flow from expansion-stage tilted matter and spectator shadowing determining the sign.

## Method

The authors implemented three different treatments of momentum-dependent hadronic mean fields within the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) framework in the JAM2 microscopic transport model: a nonrelativistic Skyrme potential (RQMD/S, correcting a previous factor-of-2 error), a Lorentz scalar implementation of the Skyrme potential (RQMDs), and a novel Lorentz vector implementation (RQMDv). They simulated Au+Au collisions across beam energies from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.7$ to 20 GeV, comparing the resulting directed flow $v_1$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ with experimental data from AGS, SPS, and RHIC experiments.

## Key numbers

* The scalar implementation (RQMDs) produces collective flows too weak to explain sideward flow data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}<10$ GeV, while the vector implementation (RQMDv) gives flows compatible with data across $2.7 < \sqrt{s_{NN}} < 20$ GeV.
* The transition beam energy from positive to negative proton directed flow slope occurs at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 10$ GeV, consistent with experimental observations from NA49 and STAR.
* Nuclear incompressibility values used: $K=380$ MeV (hard EoS) and $K=210$ MeV (soft EoS), with normal nuclear density $\rho_0=0.168$ fm$^{-3}$ and binding energy $B=-16$ MeV at saturation.
* The optical potential parameters are fixed to $U_\mathrm{opt}(\rho_0,p=1.7\ \mathrm{GeV})=60$ MeV and $U_\mathrm{opt}(\rho_0,p=0.65\ \mathrm{GeV})=0$ MeV for the nonrelativistic and vector implementations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RQMD/S model (Isse et al., 2005) by correcting an overestimation of the density-dependent force and proposing a more consistent Lorentz vector implementation. It addresses the failure of existing hybrid models (Steinheimer, 2014; Shen, 2020) to reproduce the beam energy dependence of directed flow, and contrasts with three-fluid dynamics (3FD) models (Ivanov, 2014) that require a phase transition to explain the flow sign change. The results enable improved treatment of the nonequilibrium "corona" part in hybrid models and provide a baseline for identifying the onset of partonic degrees of freedom at higher beam energies.
