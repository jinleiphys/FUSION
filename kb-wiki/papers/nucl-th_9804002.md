# Increase of Effective String Tension and Production of Strange Particles

> The increase of effective string tension as a result of the hard gluon kinks on a string is investigated using a parametrization form. In this form the effective string tension increasing with energies in hadron-hadron collisions is due to the mini-jet (gluon) production in the collisions. The data of the energy dependence of the strange quark suppression factor in hh collisions are very well reproduced with this mechanism. Meanwhile, the experimental phenomena of approximate energy independence of the strange quark suppression factor in e$^+$e$^-$-annihilations are discussed.

# Increase of Effective String Tension and Production of Strange Particles

## Key claim
The strange quark suppression factor in hadron-hadron collisions increases with energy because hard gluon kinks on a string increase the effective string tension, but this effect is parametrized rather than derived from first principles, and the approximate energy independence of the strange quark suppression factor in e+e- annihilations above 30 GeV is attributed to different string formation mechanisms rather than the same mechanism.

## Method
The authors parametrize the effective string tension of a multigluon string state using the hardest gluon transverse momentum relative to the total string multiplicity, then relate changes in effective string tension to changes in the strange quark suppression factor and transverse momentum width via the Lund string fragmentation tunneling probability. They retune the JETSET parameters PARJ(1), PARJ(2), PARJ(3), and PARJ(21) to reproduce pp data at 200 GeV/c, then use the parametrization to compute energy-dependent parameter values for other hadron-hadron collision energies and for e+e- annihilations, comparing with experimental data from the Lund group compilation and OPAL.

## Key numbers
- Strange quark suppression factor lambda increases from 0.2 at ISR energies to about 0.3 at the top of SPS energies in hadron-hadron collisions
- UA1 extracted lambda = 0.29 for p-pbar collisions at sqrt(s) = 630 GeV
- Retuned JETSET parameters for pp at 200 GeV/c: PARJ(1)=0.046, PARJ(2)=0.2, PARJ(3)=0.3, PARJ(21)=0.32
- JETSET parameters for p-pbar at sqrt(s)=540 GeV: PARJ(1)=0.115, PARJ(2)=0.304, PARJ(3)=0.406, PARJ(21)=0.376
- Parameter alpha in the effective string tension formula determined to be about 3.5
- Parameter sqrt(s0) in the scale formula determined to be 0.8 GeV
- In e+e- annihilations, lambda is approximately constant at 0.3 for sqrt(s) above 30 GeV
- In hadron-hadron collisions, saturation of lambda occurs at sqrt(s) approximately 200 GeV

## Context
This work extends the Lund string fragmentation model (specifically the FRITIOF model and its extension LUCIAE) to explain the energy dependence of strange quark suppression in hadron-hadron collisions, which had been observed experimentally but lacked a quantitative description. The authors note that HIJING (Topor 1994) with default JETSET parameters overestimates strange particle production in pp at 200 GeV/c but reproduces multiplicities at 540 GeV. The scenario enables dynamic calculation of energy-dependent fragmentation parameters rather than treating the strange quark suppression factor as a constant, and provides a framework for understanding why e+e- annihilations show different energy dependence than hadron-hadron collisions.
