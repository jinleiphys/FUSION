# Pion production in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions with a relativistic quantum molecular dynamics model

> The relativistic mean field approach by distinguishing \com{the slope of symmetry energy} is implemented into the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics transport model (LQMD.RMF). The collective flows in the isotopic nuclear reactions are systematically investigated by the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics model \com{with various slopes of symmetry energy}. The structure of the directed and elliptic flows is consistent with the results of the nonrelativistic transportation of nucleon system. \com{The directed flow difference between free neutrons and protons appears in the midrapidity region. The transverse momentum spectra of $π^+$ production is close to each other in the nearly symmetric $^{108}\mathrm{Sn} + ^{112}\mathrm{Sn}$ system and the neutron-rich $^{132}\mathrm{Sn} + ^{124}\mathrm{Sn}$ system. However, since there are more neutron-neutron scatterings in neutron-rich system, the transverse momentum spectra of $π^-$ production in the neutron-rich system are higher than one in the nearly symmetric system. For a given reaction system, the transverse momentum spectra of $π^+$ and $π^-$ production are independent on the stiffness of symmetry energy. This leads to the fact that the single ratio and the double ratio are independent on the stiffness of symmetry energy. Moreover, the double ratio without the $π$-nucleon potential decreases with increasing the transverse momentum. However, the double ratio with the inclusion of $π$ potential increases with increasing the transverse momentum.

# Pion production in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions with a relativistic quantum molecular dynamics model

## Key claim

The transverse momentum spectra of π⁺ and π⁻ production, as well as the single ratio π⁻/π⁺ and the double ratio, are independent of the stiffness of symmetry energy for a given reaction system at incident energies of 270 A MeV, contrary to expectations that these observables would be sensitive probes of the symmetry energy slope. This insensitivity arises because the three symmetry energy parametrizations (set1, set2, set3) do not differ greatly at densities below 2ρ₀.

## Method

The relativistic mean field (RMF) approach with isovector-vector ρ and isovector-scalar δ mesons was implemented into the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics transport model (LQMD.RMF). Three parameter sets (set1, set2, set3) were constructed with the same symmetry energy of 31.6 MeV at saturation density but different slope parameters L = 85.3, 109.3, and 145.0 MeV, respectively. The model was used to simulate ¹⁰⁸Sn + ¹¹²Sn and ¹³²Sn + ¹²⁴Sn collisions at 270 A MeV and impact parameter b = 3 fm, computing collective flows and charged pion production with and without a π-nucleon potential in the medium.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 31.6 MeV for all three parameter sets (set1, set2, set3)
- Slope parameter L of symmetry energy: 85.3 MeV (set1), 109.3 MeV (set2), and 145.0 MeV (set3)
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³; binding energy per particle E/A − M_N = −16 MeV
- Incident energy: 270 A MeV; impact parameter b = 3 fm
- Nuclear matter compression in central collisions: approaching 2ρ₀
- Directed flow V₁ is an order of magnitude larger than elliptic flow V₂
- The double ratio with π potential increases with increasing transverse momentum, while the double ratio without π potential decreases with increasing transverse momentum
- The single ratio of ¹⁰⁸Sn + ¹¹²Sn is consistent with experimental data at p_T > 200 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the LQMD transport model by incorporating RMF with both ρ and δ mesons to study symmetry energy effects, following earlier RQMD.RMF implementations by the same group (Wei and Feng, prior work). The results contradict the expectation from previous studies (FOPI collaboration data analyses) that π⁻/π⁺ ratios are sensitive to the slope of symmetry energy; instead, the authors find that the double ratio with π potential shows an increasing trend with transverse momentum consistent with SπRIT experimental data, while the version without π potential shows the opposite trend. This work enables future extraction of symmetry energy properties once the π potential in the medium is better understood.
