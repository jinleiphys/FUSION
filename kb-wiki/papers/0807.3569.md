# Conservation Laws and the Multiplicity Evolution of Spectra at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Transverse momentum distributions in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions carry considerable information about the dynamics of the hot system produced. Direct comparison with the same spectra from $p+p$ collisions has proven invaluable to identify novel features associated with the larger system, in particular, the "jet quenching" at high momentum and apparently much stronger collective flow dominating the spectral shape at low momentum. We point out possible hazards of ignoring conservation laws in the comparison of high- and low-multiplicity final states. We argue that the effects of energy and momentum conservation actually dominate many of the observed systematics, and that $p+p$ collisions may be much more similar to heavy ion collisions than generally thought.

## Key claim

Energy and momentum conservation constraints (EMCICs) may dominate the observed multiplicity evolution of single-particle spectra in relativistic heavy ion collisions, and p+p collisions may be much more similar to heavy ion collisions than generally thought, provided one accounts for these phase-space effects rather than attributing spectral differences solely to new physics like collective flow or jet quenching.

## Method

The authors derive a formalism based on Hagedorn's generalization of Fermi's Golden Rule, in which dynamics and kinematics factorize, to calculate how finite-multiplicity phase-space restrictions distort single-particle spectra relative to an underlying "parent" distribution. They then test the extreme postulate that all experimentally-measured multiplicity dependence of spectra arises solely from EMCICs, by comparing ratios of measured p_T distributions from p+p and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV to the ratio predicted by their phase-space factor, treating multiplicity and kinematic parameters as free but physically reasonable.

## Key numbers

- For the soft sector (p_T less than about 1 GeV/c), the ratio of minimum-bias p+p spectra to 0-5% most central Au+Au spectra is described using a parent distribution with ⟨p_T²⟩ = 0.12 (GeV/c)², ⟨E²⟩ = 0.43 GeV², and ⟨E⟩ = 0.61 GeV, with multiplicities N = 10.3 for p+p and N greater than 300 for central Au+Au.
- Blast-wave fits to the STAR data show kinetic freezeout temperature about 100 MeV and average collective flow velocity about 0.6c for most central Au+Au collisions, while for lower multiplicity collisions the freezeout temperature grows to about 130 MeV and flow velocity decreases to about 0.25c.
- For p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV simulated with PYTHIA, the average number of primary particles within pseudorapidity |η| less than 1.0 is 7.5, with ⟨p_T²⟩_c = 0.58 (GeV/c)², ⟨E²⟩_c = 1.45 GeV², and ⟨E⟩_c = 0.98 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of energy and momentum conservation-induced correlations in multiparticle distributions by Borghini (2000) and Chajecki (2008), applying the same formalism to single-particle spectra. The authors challenge the standard interpretation that the multiplicity evolution of spectra, strong collective flow in high-multiplicity Au+Au versus weak flow in p+p, and jet quenching in Au+Au versus enhancement in high-multiplicity p+p, necessarily reflects different physics, suggesting instead that much of the observed systematics may be kinematic in origin. This enables a re-evaluation of whether p+p collisions might develop bulk collective behavior similar to heavy ion collisions, and warns that ignoring EMCICs, especially for low-multiplicity systems, may be dangerous even at high p_T.
