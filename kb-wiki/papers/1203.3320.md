# Baryon-baryon interaction of strangeness S=-1 sector

> We present our recent studies on hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions in the strangeness S=-1 that $pΛ, Σ^0 p$ and $Σ^+ n$, by extracting corresponding potentials through Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions. We calculate $ΛN$ and $ΣN$ potentials in the isospin I=3/2 channel, using the $N_f=2+1$ gauge configurations generated by PACS-CS collaboration and employing an improved method to obtain potentials in lattice QCD simulations. For the $^1S_0$ channel, the central $ΣN (I=3/2, ^1S_0)$ potential and the central $ΛN (^1S_0)$ potential are found to be very similar. In the spin triplet ($^3S_1-^3D_1$) channels, the central $ΛN(^3S_1-^3D_1)$ potential is attractive while the central $ΣN(I=3/2, ^3S_1-^3D_1)$ potentials is repulsive. Tensor potentials, on the other hand, are rather weak in the diagonal part of both $ΛN$ and $ΣN(I=3/2)$ systems.

## Key claim

The authors extract single-channel $\Lambda N$ and $\Sigma N (I=3/2)$ potentials from lattice QCD, but explicitly postpone the coupled-channel $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ analysis that would be needed for a complete description of the strangeness $S=-1$ baryon-baryon interaction, noting that such work is "now in progress."

## Method

The authors compute Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions for two-baryon systems on $2+1$ flavor full QCD gauge configurations generated by the PACS-CS collaboration, using an improved method that extracts the leading-order (LO) central and tensor potentials from the time-dependent Schrödinger-like equation. The normalized four-point correlation function is calculated on a $32^3 \times 64$ lattice with wall sources, and the potentials are obtained by decomposing the spin-singlet and spin-triplet wave functions into $S$-wave and $D$-wave components.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: $a = 0.0907(13)$ fm, giving a spatial volume of $(2.90\ \text{fm})^3$
- Quark masses correspond to $(m_\pi, m_K) \cong (699, 787)$ MeV
- Baryon masses from the simulation: $(m_N, m_\Lambda, m_\Sigma) = (1.574(3), 1.635(3), 1.650(3))$ GeV
- 399 gauge configurations were used, with 9 source positions per configuration
- The $\Lambda N$ central potential in the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ channel shows an attractive well at $r \approx 0.6$ fm, deeper than the $\Lambda N$ $^1S_0$ central potential

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2009) and the H-dibaryon system (Inoue 2010), to hyperon-nucleon interactions in the strangeness $S=-1$ sector. The results update earlier reports from LATTICE 2008 (Nemura 2009) and LATTICE 2009 (Nemura 2010) by including $\Sigma N (I=3/2)$ potentials and using an improved extraction method (Ishii 2011). The repulsive nature of the $\Sigma N (I=3/2, ^3S_1-^3D_1)$ central potential is consistent with predictions from the naive quark model (Oka 2000). The single-channel potentials presented here are a step toward the coupled-channel $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ analysis needed for hypernuclear physics and neutron star equations of state.
