# Pairing correlations and odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections of weakly bound nuclei

> We investigate the odd-even staggering (OES) in reaction cross sections of weakly bound nuclei with a Glauber theory, taking into account the pairing correlation with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method. We first discuss the pairing gap in extremely weakly bound nuclei and show that the pairing gap persists in the limit of zero separation energy limit even for single-particle orbits with the orbital angular momenta $l=0$ and $l=1$. We then introduce the OES parameter defined as the second derivative of reaction cross sections with respect to the mass number, and clarify the relation between the magnitude of OES and the neutron separation energy. We find that the OES parameter increases considerably in the zero separation energy limit for $l=0$ and $l=1$ single-particle states, while no increase is found for higher angular momentum orbits with {\it e.g.,} $l=3$. We point out that the increase of OES parameter is also seen in the experimental reaction cross sections for Ne isotopes, which is well accounted for by our calculations.

# Pairing correlations and odd-even staggering in reaction cross sections of weakly bound nuclei

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering (OES) parameter in reaction cross sections increases considerably in the zero neutron separation energy limit for low-angular-momentum single-particle states (l=0 and l=1), but only if the pairing gap persists in that limit; the claim that the pairing gap disappears at the drip line is shown to be an artifact of fixing the Fermi energy equal to the single-particle energy without self-consistently conserving particle number.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for neutrons in a spherical Woods-Saxon potential with a density-dependent contact pairing interaction of surface type, using a box boundary condition at 60 fm and including angular momenta up to l=12. Reaction cross sections for projectile nuclei on a 12C target at 240 MeV/nucleon were computed using the Glauber theory in the optical limit approximation, with a modified phase-shift function to account for multiple scattering effects. The OES parameter gamma_3 was defined as the second derivative of reaction cross sections with respect to mass number, using a three-point difference formula.

## Key numbers

- The OES parameter gamma_3 for 74,75,76Cr nuclei reaches up to approximately 80 mb in the limit of small separation energy for the 3s1/2 orbit.
- For higher angular momentum orbits with l=3 (1f7/2), the staggering parameter stays almost constant at gamma_3 ~ 2 mb even in the zero separation energy limit.
- The HFB calculations used a box size of R_box = 60 fm, a pairing strength V_pair = -420 MeV·fm^3, and an energy cutoff of 50 MeV above the Fermi energy.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters were V_0 = -51 + 30(N-Z)/A MeV, R_0 = 1.27 A^(1/3) fm, V_ls = -0.71 V_0 MeV·fm^2, and diffuseness a = 0.67 fm.
- The experimental staggering parameter for Ne isotopes (30,31,32Ne) agrees with the calculated value for the 2p3/2 orbit when the valence neutron in 31Ne occupies that orbit.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (Hagino and Sagawa, 2011) of pairing correlations in reaction cross sections and addresses the contradictory arguments of Hamamoto (who claimed the pairing gap disappears at the drip line) versus Yamagami (2005) and others who argued it persists. The paper demonstrates that the pairing anti-halo effect first discussed by Bennaceur (2000) is the main reason for observed OES in reaction cross sections of drip-line nuclei. The proposed staggering parameter gamma_3 provides a quantitative measure that enables systematic experimental studies of pairing correlations in weakly-bound nuclei and in the limit of zero neutron separation energy.
