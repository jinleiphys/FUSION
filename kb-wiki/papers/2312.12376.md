# Tracing baryon and electric charge transport in isobar collisions

> It is of fundamental interest to understand the carrier of conserved quantum charges within protons and nuclei at high energy. Preliminary data from isobar collisions at RHIC reveal a scaled net-baryon to net-electric charge ratio ($B/ΔQ \times ΔZ/A$) at mid-rapidity between 1.2 and 2, consistent with string junction model predictions. Here, we compute the initial stage scaled net-baryon to net-electric charge ratio for isobar collisions. Our model incorporates a realization of the string junction model and models the nuclear structure. Our predictions identify the baseline expectations for such measurement and quantify the impact of the nuclear structure.

# Tracing baryon and electric charge transport in isobar collisions

## Key claim
Even when baryon and electric charge have equal probability of stopping at mid-rapidity (λ_B = λ_Q = 0.2), the scaled net-baryon to net-electric charge ratio in isobar collisions deviates from unity due to differences in nuclear structure between ruthenium and zirconium nuclei, not solely due to flavor-dependent stopping dynamics.

## Method
The authors used the 3D-Glauber initial-state model to simulate Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, incorporating a string junction model where baryon number can be placed at string ends (valence quark picture) or toward mid-rapidity (string junction picture) with probability λ_B. Electric charge stopping was modeled with an independent parameter λ_Q, and nuclear structure was implemented via deformed Woods-Saxon distributions with separate parameters for protons and neutrons, including neutron skins, based on Density Functional Theory calculations.

## Key numbers
- The scaled net-baryon to net-electric charge ratio (B/ΔQ × ΔZ/A) at mid-rapidity (−0.5 < y < 0.5) is consistently below unity in the full nuclear structure calculation with λ_B = λ_Q = 0.2
- When Ru and Zr nuclei are set to identical shapes, the ratio becomes close to unity and exhibits a flatter centrality trend
- Different neutron skin sizes between Ru and Zr give a 20% smaller ratio in peripheral collisions compared to the case without neutron skins
- Ruthenium nuclear parameters: R_0,p = 5.09 fm, a_p = 0.46 fm, β_2^WS = 0.16, dR = 0.015 fm, da = 0.01 fm
- Zirconium nuclear parameters: R_0,p = 5.02 fm, a_p = 0.52 fm, β_2^WS = 0.06, β_3^WS = 0.2, dR = 0.1 fm, da = 0.05 fm
- The string junction probability parameter λ_B = 0.2 is favored by STAR measurements on mid-rapidity net-proton yields in Au+Au collisions from the Beam Energy Scan program

## Context
This work extends the string junction model proposed by Kharzeev (1996) and the 3D-Glauber framework developed by Shen (2017, 2022) to the specific case of isobar collisions at RHIC. The study provides baseline predictions for the STAR isobar collision measurement (STAR Collaboration, 2021), which showed preliminary scaled net-baryon to net-electric charge ratios between 1.2 and 2. The results enable disentangling nuclear structure effects from flavor-dependent stopping dynamics in future experimental comparisons, and establish that the ratio's centrality dependence must account for the different shapes and neutron skins of Ru and Zr nuclei before interpreting deviations as evidence for the string junction mechanism.
