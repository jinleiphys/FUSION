# Nature of Chiral Transition in QCD and Sigma Meson

> After showing the characteristic features of the chiral transition in QCD at finite temperature $T$, we note the significance of the sigma-mesonic mode in the chiral transition : Sigma meson may be regarded as the "Higgs particle" in QCD, though the existence of such a mode in the real world is still unclear. We show the properties of the sigma meson in hot and/or hadronic matter, from which we propose experiments to reveal the existence the sigma meson clearly; the experiments include relativistic heavy-ion collisions to create hot hadronic matter, and electro-production in heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The sigma meson, understood as the quantum fluctuation of the chiral order parameter \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle\), may serve as the "Higgs particle" of QCD, but its existence in the real world at zero temperature remains unclear because its large width (\(\Gamma_\sigma \sim 450\)–\(500\) MeV) prevents a clear resonance signal in \(\pi\pi\) scattering below 900 MeV; however, at high temperature or density the sigma meson is predicted to become a sharp resonance, making it observable in relativistic heavy-ion collisions or electro-production in nuclei.

## Method

The analysis uses a generalized Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with three flavors (\(N_f=3\)) and three colors (\(N_c=3\)), incorporating explicit \(SU_f(3)\) breaking via flavor-dependent current quark masses and the \(U_A(1)\) anomaly via a determinantal six-fermion interaction. The temperature and baryon-density dependence of the quark condensates, constituent quark masses, and meson masses (pion, kaon, sigma) are computed in the mean-field approximation, and the behavior of the sigma meson mass and decay width is extracted as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

* The sigma meson mass at zero temperature is estimated to be \(600 \sim 700\) MeV, with a width \(\Gamma_\sigma \sim 450\) MeV (and later \(\sim 500\) MeV) due to the \(\sigma \rightarrow 2\pi\) decay.
* The non-strange condensate shows a large variation with temperature, while the strange condensate changes only moderately; the constituent quark masses reflect this contrast, with \(SU_f(3)\) symmetry becoming badly broken above \(T \sim 150\) MeV.
* The sigma meson mass \(m_\sigma(T)\) decreases with temperature and becomes smaller than twice the pion mass (\(2 m_\pi\)) at a temperature \(T_\sigma \sim 190\) MeV, at which point the \(\sigma \rightarrow \pi\pi\) decay width vanishes.
* The pion mass remains nearly constant up to \(T \sim 200\) MeV, only starting to increase near that temperature.
* The kaon mass \(m_K(T)\) stays almost constant as long as the system remains in the Nambu-Goldstone phase.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor NJL results of Hatsuda and Kunihiro (character) to the three-flavor case including the axial anomaly, and it contrasts with lattice QCD simulations (DeTar, 1992) that at the time could only provide screening masses rather than real masses. The predictions for a sharp sigma resonance at high temperature motivate experimental searches in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (to create hot hadronic matter) and in electro-production on heavy nuclei at facilities such as CEBAF, building on earlier suggestions by Weldon regarding charged systems and lepton-pair production.
