# Three-body forces and shell structure in calcium isotopes

> Understanding and predicting the formation of shell structure from nuclear forces is a central challenge for nuclear physics. While the magic numbers N=2,8,20 are generally well understood, N=28 is the first standard magic number that is not reproduced in microscopic theories with two-nucleon forces. In this Letter, we show that three-nucleon forces give rise to repulsive interactions between two valence neutrons that are key to explain 48Ca as a magic nucleus, with a high 2+ excitation energy and a concentrated magnetic dipole transition strength. The repulsive three-nucleon mechanism improves the agreement with experimental binding energies.

# Three-body forces and shell structure in calcium isotopes

## Key claim
Three-nucleon forces provide repulsive interactions between valence neutrons that are essential to explain the doubly-magic nature of ⁴⁸Ca, but the agreement with experiment depends on the inclusion of an extended valence space (including the g₉/₂ orbital) and on the choice of initial single-particle energies in ⁴¹Ca.

## Method
The authors derived two-body interactions in the pf and pfg₉/₂ shells using low-momentum interactions (V_low_k) from a chiral N³LO NN potential evolved to a cutoff Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹, and also a standard G-matrix based on the Bonn C NN potential. Three-nucleon forces were included via the Fujita-Miyazawa Δ-excitation mechanism and chiral EFT 3N interactions at N²LO, with normal-ordered two-body parts from 3N forces among two valence neutrons and one core nucleon. Single-particle energies were calculated consistently in many-body perturbation theory to third order, and shell-model diagonalizations were performed in the valence spaces.

## Key numbers
- ⁴⁸Ca ground-state energy relative to ⁴⁰Ca: -159.3 MeV (MBPT) vs. -155.0 MeV (coupled-cluster) for V_low_k interactions
- ⁵²Ca ground-state energy relative to ⁴⁰Ca: -230.7 MeV (MBPT) vs. -235.9 MeV (CC)
- ⁵⁴Ca ground-state energy relative to ⁴⁰Ca: -259.1 MeV (MBPT) vs. -268.6 MeV (CC)
- Harmonic oscillator basis: ħω = 11.48 MeV for calcium isotopes
- Intermediate-state excitations converged to 18 ħω
- First excited (1/2⁻) state in ⁴⁹Ca: ≈ 1.0 MeV for V_low_k+3N(N²LO) in pf and pfg₉/₂ shells, compared to experimental value 2.02 MeV; MBPT results yield 1.8 MeV
- g₉/₂ orbital placed at -1 MeV in ⁴¹Ca for empirical SPE calculations

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous finding that three-nucleon forces explain the oxygen anomaly (why ²⁴O is the heaviest oxygen isotope) to medium-mass nuclei, specifically the calcium isotopes. It addresses the long-standing failure of microscopic two-nucleon-only theories to reproduce N=28 as a magic number, which had previously required phenomenological adjustments in interactions such as GXPF1 (a quasi-global fit) and KB3G (which modifies the monopole part of a G-matrix). The results enable parameter-free microscopic predictions for neutron-rich calcium isotopes up to the drip line, suggesting a drip line around ⁶⁰Ca, and establish that the repulsive 3N mechanism discovered for oxygen is general for neutron-rich nuclei.
