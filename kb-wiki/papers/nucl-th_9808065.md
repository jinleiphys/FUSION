# Fragment Multiplicity Distributions, a Signal of True Nuclear Multifragmentation

> Multiplicity fluctuations of intermediate-mass fragments are studied with the percolation model. It is shown that super-Poissonian fluctuations occur near the percolation transition and that this behavior is associated with the fragmentative nature of the percolation model. The consequences of various choices in defining and binning fragments are also evaluated. Several suggestions for experiments in nuclear fragmentation are presented.

# Fragment Multiplicity Distributions, a Signal of True Nuclear Multifragmentation

## Key claim

Super-Poissonian fluctuations in intermediate-mass fragment (IMF) multiplicity distributions occur near the percolation transition and signal positive correlations between fragments, but the observation of super-Poissonian or sub-Poissonian behavior alone is not decisive because binning choices (by multiplicity, by number of broken bonds, or over a range of excitation energies) can push distributions in either direction.

## Method

Bond percolation was performed on spherical sections of a cubic lattice with two sizes: 123 sites and 4169 sites. Bonds between sites were randomly broken with probability p, and fragments were defined as groups of connected sites. For each event, the number of intermediate-mass fragments (defined as clusters of size 3 to 20 sites) was recorded, and multiplicity distributions were generated for fixed values of p. The variance-to-mean ratio was used to classify distributions as sub-Poissonian (binomial), Poissonian, or super-Poissonian (negative binomial). Additional calculations examined binning by fixed number of broken bonds, by overall fragment multiplicity, and by varying the IMF mass range.

## Key numbers

- The percolation threshold for bond percolation on the cubic lattice is p_c = 0.7512.
- Two lattice sizes were used: N_sites = 123 and N_sites = 4169.
- The default IMF definition was cluster size Z satisfying 3 ≤ Z ≤ 20.
- For the restricted IMF mass range, the definition was 15 ≤ Z ≤ 20.
- Calculations were performed for bond-breaking probabilities p = 0.7 and p = 0.8, among other values.

## Context

This work extends percolation model studies of nuclear fragmentation (Bauer 1985, Campi 1986, Stauffer 1985) which had successfully reproduced fragment yields and their moments. It directly addresses the interpretation of experimental IMF multiplicity distributions reported by Moretto (1995, 1997), which were described as binomial. The paper shows that percolation models predict qualitatively different behavior from sequential evaporative models: percolation produces positive correlations (super-Poissonian variance) near the transition, while evaporative pictures introduce negative correlations due to energy conservation. The results enable experimental tests using full-acceptance detector systems to distinguish between fragmentative and sequential fragmentation mechanisms.
