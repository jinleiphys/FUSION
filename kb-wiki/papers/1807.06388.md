# Signature of gluon saturation in forward di-hadron correlations at the Large Hadron Collider

> We argue that gluon saturation effects may be visible in Large Hadron Collider (LHC) data. We look at the centrality dependence of the away-side peak of two-particle correlations measured in p-Pb collisions, using both ALICE data at midrapidity, and LHCb data at forward rapidity. Once the collective flow-like signal measured at the near-side peak is removed from the correlation function, the centrality dependence of the away-side peak turns out to be much stronger in the forward region than at midrapidity. We argue that this is a very specific prediction of the saturation framework, and we compare LHCb data to a state-of-the-art calculation within the Color Glass Condensate theory.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the suppression of the away-side peak in forward di-hadron correlations in p-Pb collisions at the LHC provides strong evidence for gluon saturation effects, but only after the collective flow-like signal measured at the near-side peak is removed from the correlation function. The centrality dependence of this suppression is much stronger at forward rapidity than at midrapidity, which is presented as a specific prediction of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework.

## Method

The authors analyzed two-particle correlation functions Y(Δφ) measured by ALICE at midrapidity and LHCb at forward rapidity in p-Pb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV. They subtracted the long-range "double-ridge" component (fitted with a cos(2Δφ) modulation) from the correlation functions to isolate the away-side peak. They then compared the centrality dependence of the resulting flow-subtracted away-side yields to a state-of-the-art calculation within the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) limit of the CGC effective theory, using the dilute-dense factorization formula for back-to-back di-hadron production.

## Key numbers

- The ALICE data at midrapidity and LHCb data at forward rapidity were both taken at √s = 5.02 TeV in p-Pb collisions
- The forward rapidity region corresponds to y ∼ 4, where x₁ ∼ 1 and x₂ ∼ 10⁻⁴ for the correlated partons
- The LHCb data implement a rapidity gap of 2 units of y between correlated hadrons
- The CGC calculation includes a factor of 3 to account for all charged particles measured by LHCb (the calculation itself only includes fragmentation to neutral pions)
- Absolute errors on the data points in both ALICE and LHCb measurements are approximately 0.01

## Context

This work extends the long-standing search for gluon saturation signatures in forward di-hadron correlations, previously studied in d-Au collisions at RHIC by Albacete (2010), Stasto (2011), and Lappi (2012). The authors use LHCb forward rapidity data (Aaij 2015) and ALICE midrapidity data (Abelev 2012) to demonstrate a rapidity-dependent suppression pattern that matches CGC predictions. The calculation builds on the TMD factorization framework of Dominguez (2011) and uses small-x QCD evolution equations (JIMWLK or rcBK) as implemented by Albacete (2018). The work enables future measurements in p-p collisions at the same center-of-mass energy to confirm the saturation interpretation, and motivates inclusion of soft gluon radiation effects to broaden the theoretical correlation function.
