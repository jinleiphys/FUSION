# QCD and dimensional deconstruction

> Motivated by phenomenological models of hidden local symmetries and the ideas of dimensional deconstruction and gauge/gravity duality, we consider the model of an "open moose". Such a model has a large number K of hidden gauge groups as well as a global chiral symmetry. In the continuum limit K->infinity the model becomes a 4+1 dimensional theory of a gauge field propagating in a dilaton background and an external space-time metric with two boundaries. We show that the model reproduces several well known phenomenological and theoretical aspects of low-energy hadron dynamics. We derive the general formulas for the mass spectrum, the decay constants of the pion and vector mesons, and the couplings between mesons. We then consider two simple realizations, one with a flat metric and another with a "cosh" metric interpolating between two AdS boundaries. For the pion form-factor, the single pole rho-meson dominance is exact in the latter case and approximate in the former case. We discover that an AdS/CFT-like prescription emerges in the computation of current-current correlators. We speculate on the role of the model in the theory dual to QCD.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an “open moose” model with a large number \(K\) of hidden local gauge groups, in the continuum limit \(K\to\infty\), becomes a 5-dimensional gauge theory in a curved metric and dilaton background, and that this limit naturally reproduces vector meson dominance (VMD) and other low-energy hadron phenomenology without the parameter tuning required in finite-\(K\) hidden local symmetry models.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian with \(K+1\) nonlinear sigma-model fields \(\Sigma^k\) (representing pions) and \(K\) hidden gauge bosons \(A_\mu^k\), arranged in a linear “moose” chain with global chiral symmetry at the ends. They derive general discrete formulas for the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\), vector meson masses \(m_n\), decay constants \(g_{nV}, g_{nA}\), and couplings \(g_{n\pi\pi}\), then take the continuum limit \(K\to\infty\) to obtain a 5-dimensional Yang–Mills theory in a warped metric and dilaton background. Two exactly solvable examples are studied: a flat background (constant \(f_k, g_k\)) and a “cosh” metric interpolating between two AdS boundaries.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant relation: \(\displaystyle \frac{4}{f_\pi^2} = \sum_{k=1}^{K+1} \frac{1}{f_k^2}\) (Eq. (fpi)), which in the continuum becomes \(\displaystyle \frac{4}{f_\pi^2} = \int_{-u_0}^{+u_0} \frac{du}{f^2(u)}\).
* First Weinberg sum rule: \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^K \left( \frac{g_{nV}^2}{m_n^2} - \frac{g_{nA}^2}{m_n^2} \right) = f_\pi^2\) (Eq. (weinberg1)).
* Second Weinberg sum rule: \(\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^K (g_{nV}^2 - g_{nA}^2) = 0\) for \(K>1\) (Eq. (weinberg2)).
* For the flat background, the functions \(f(u)=f\) and \(g(u)=g\) are constant; the specific numerical values of \(f\) and \(g\) are not given in the text.

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry (HLS) approach of Bando et al. (1985) and its extension to two hidden gauge groups (Bando et al., 1986) by considering an arbitrarily large number \(K\) of hidden gauge groups. It also connects to the ideas of dimensional deconstruction (Arkani-Hamed, Cohen, Georgi, 2001) and gauge/gravity duality (Maldacena, 1998; Gubser, Klebanov, Polyakov, 1998; Witten, 1998). The continuum limit yields a 5-dimensional gauge theory that reproduces an AdS/CFT-like prescription for current-current correlators, suggesting that the model may serve as a toy dual to QCD. The results enable future work on the \(U(1)_A\) problem, chiral anomaly, Wess–Zumino–Witten term, and baryons.
