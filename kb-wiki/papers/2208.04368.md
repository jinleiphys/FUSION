# Hadronic $J/ψ$ Regeneration in Pb+Pb Collisions

> We make use of published yields for $D$-mesons and $J/ψ$ in Pb+Pb collisions at ALICE and a schematic description of the expansion of the hadron gas to study $D$-meson collisions during the hadronic break-up phase as a production mechanism for charmonium in relativistic heavy ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. Our calculation is based on chemical reaction rates with thermal cross sections for an effective meson interaction among pseudoscalar and vector mesons. We find that due to regeneration, the newly measured $J/ψ$ yields are consistent with anywhere from roughly $25\%$ to $110\%$ of the total yield present at hadronization time. This allows us to bound the fractional abundance of $J/ψ$ immediately after hadronization: $0.28 \leq \frac{dN_0^{J/ψ}/dy}{dN_{\rm eq}^{J/ψ}/dy} \leq 1.13$. Our results are robust under the relaxation of the particulars of our schematic description and imply that it will be difficult to distinguish regeneration during hadronization from regeneration by final-state hadronic interactions. Therefore, regeneration must be taken into account when modelling.

## Key claim

The measured \(J/\psi\) yields in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC are consistent with hadronic regeneration from \(D\)-meson collisions accounting for anywhere from roughly 25% to 110% of the total yield present at hadronization time, making it impossible to distinguish regeneration during hadronization from regeneration by final-state hadronic interactions.

## Method

The authors used published ALICE yields for \(D\)-mesons and \(J/\psi\) in 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV, combined with a schematic description of the expanding hadron gas. They solved chemical rate equations using thermally averaged cross sections from an effective SU(4) chiral meson interaction model (Abreu et al., 2017) for reactions such as \(D+\bar{D} \to J/\psi + h\), where \(h\) is a light hadron. The fireball volume evolution was modeled using two different cylindrical expansion schemes (Abreu et al., 2017; Andronic et al., 2021) and integrated to infinite time rather than stopping at thermal freeze-out.

## Key numbers

*   The constant prefactor \(C_{J/\psi}\) in the rate equation, which encodes the \(D\)-meson abundance and production cross sections, is \(53^{+28}_{-33}~{\rm fm}^2\).
*   Starting from zero \(J/\psi\) at hadronization, the final yield from \(D\)-meson reactions alone is \(0.047 < N_{J/\psi} < 0.073\), compared to the measured equilibrium yield of \(N_{\rm exp}(J/\psi) = 0.12 \pm 0.017\).
*   The fractional abundance of \(J/\psi\) immediately after hadronization is bounded by \(0.28 \leq \frac{dN_0^{J/\psi}/dy}{dN_{\rm eq}^{J/\psi}/dy} \leq 1.13\).
*   The hadronization volume is \(V(\tau_H) = 4,997 \pm 455~{\rm fm}^3\), with a hadronization temperature \(T_H \approx 155\) MeV and freeze-out temperature \(T_F \approx 90\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hadronic \(J/\psi\) regeneration (Ko et al., 1997; Zhang et al., 2002; Bratkovskaya et al., 2003; Linnyk et al., 2008) by using actual LHC data for both \(D\)-meson and \(J/\psi\) yields, which were not available for those prior calculations. The results challenge the interpretation that the observed \(J/\psi\) yield in the statistical hadronization model for charm (SHMc) (Andronic et al., 2021) provides unambiguous evidence for formation entirely at the QGP phase boundary. The findings imply that microscopic transport models of the hadronic stage must include \(D\)-meson collision channels, and that further tightening of the bounds requires improved expansion models or reduced experimental uncertainties on \(D\)-meson and \(J/\psi\) yields.
