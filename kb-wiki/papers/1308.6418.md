# Non-collective excitations in low-energy heavy-ion reactions: applicability of the random-matrix model

> We investigate the applicability of a random-matrix model to the description of non-collective excitations in heavy-ion reactions around the Coulomb barrier. To this end, we study fusion in the reaction $^{16}$O + $^{208}$Pb, taking account of the known non-collective excitations in the $^{208}$Pb nucleus. We show that the random-matrix model for the corresponding couplings reproduces reasonably well the exact calculations, obtained using empirical deformation parameters. This implies that the model may provide a powerful method for systems in which the non-collective couplings are not so well known.

## Key claim

The random-matrix model reproduces reasonably well the exact coupled-channels calculations for fusion in the $^{16}$O + $^{208}$Pb reaction that use empirical deformation parameters for non-collective excitations, provided the model parameters are chosen appropriately; this suggests the model may be a powerful method for systems where non-collective couplings are not well known.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations for the $^{16}$O + $^{208}$Pb reaction, including the known non-collective excitations in $^{208}$Pb up to 7.382 MeV. They compared these exact calculations, which used empirical deformation parameters, with calculations employing a random-matrix model for the non-collective coupling matrix elements, where the second moment of the coupling distribution was parameterized using a Gaussian energy dependence and a level density derived from a polynomial fit to the experimental level counting function.

## Key numbers

- The experimental level counting function $N(\epsilon)$ for $^{208}$Pb was fitted with a sixth-order polynomial $f(\epsilon) = \sum_{n=0}^6 a_n\epsilon^n$ over the interval 4 MeV to 7.5 MeV, with coefficients $a_0=-7479$, $a_1=6969$ (MeV$^{-1}$), $a_2=-2612$ (MeV$^{-2}$), $a_3=497.5$ (MeV$^{-3}$), $a_4=-49.59$ (MeV$^{-4}$), $a_5=2.347$ (MeV$^{-5}$), and $a_6=-0.03632$ (MeV$^{-6}$).
- The parameter $\Delta$ in the Gaussian energy dependence of the random-matrix coupling form factor was chosen to be 7 MeV.
- The parameter $w$ (the overall coupling strength) was chosen to be $w = 38000$ MeV$^{3/2}$ to reproduce the main peak height in the fusion barrier distribution.
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear potential used had surface diffuseness $a=0.671$ fm, radius $R = 8.39$ fm, and depth $V_0 = 550$ MeV.
- The collective excitations included were the $3^-$ state at 2.615 MeV ($\beta_3 = 0.122$), the $5^-$ state at 3.198 MeV ($\beta_5 = 0.058$), and the $2^+$ state at 4.085 MeV ($\beta_2 = 0.058$) in $^{208}$Pb.
- The random-matrix model reproduced the exact fusion barrier distribution, with both calculations showing a similar smearing around 80 MeV and a similar second peak around 87.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous exact coupled-channels study (Yusa, Hagino, Rowley, 2012) of the $^{16}$O + $^{208}$Pb reaction, where all 70 known non-collective states in $^{208}$Pb were included explicitly. It also builds on the random-matrix model originally introduced by Weidenmuller et al. in the 1970s for deep inelastic collisions (KPW76, AKW78, BSW78) and applied by the authors to a schematic one-dimensional barrier-penetration problem (Yusa, Hagino, Rowley, 2010). The validation of the random-matrix model for $^{208}$Pb enables its application to systems like $^{20}$Ne + $^{90,92}$Zr, where non-collective state properties are poorly known but are conjectured (Piasecki et al.) to explain differences in measured quasi-elastic barrier distributions.
