# An efficient method for evaluating energy-dependent sum rules

> Energy-dependent sum rules are useful tools in many fields of physics. In nuclear physics, they typically involve an integration of the response function over the nuclear spectrum with a weight function composed of integer powers of the energy. More complicated weight functions are also encountered, e.g., in nuclear polarization corrections of atomic spectra. Using the Lorentz integral transform method and the Lanczos algorithm, we derive a computationally efficient technique for evaluating such sum rules that avoids the explicit calculation of both the continuum states and the response function itself. Our numerical results for electric dipole sum rules of the Helium-4 nucleus with various energy-dependent weights show rapid convergence with respect to the number of Lanczos steps. This demonstrates the usefulness of the method in a variety of electroweak reactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a computationally efficient technique for evaluating energy-dependent sum rules that avoids explicit calculation of both continuum states and the response function itself, with the caveat that the method's applicability depends on the existence of an inverse Lorentz integral transform of the weight function, which may become prohibitively difficult for narrowly peaked or discontinuous weight functions.

## Method

The authors combine the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method with the Lanczos algorithm to evaluate sum rules of the form I = ∫ dω S(ω) g(ω), where S(ω) is the nuclear response function and g(ω) is an arbitrary energy-dependent weight function. They express the sum rule in terms of the LIT function and a transformed weight function h(σ,Γ), then use the Lanczos algorithm to construct a tridiagonal Hamiltonian matrix in a Krylov subspace, starting from the normalized transition vector |φ₀⟩ = Ô|i⟩/√⟨i|Ô†Ô|i⟩. The sum rule is then evaluated as I_N = ⟨i|Ô†Ô|i⟩ Σ_{ν=0}^{N-1} |Q_{ν0}|² g(ω_ν), where Q is the unitary matrix diagonalizing the Lanczos tridiagonal matrix and ω_ν are the resulting eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

* For the unretarded dipole response of ⁴He, approximately N ≈ 200 Lanczos steps are sufficient for convergence of sum rules with weight functions ωⁿ for n = -3/2, -1, -1/2, 0, 1/2, 1, 3/2, compared to a model space size of M ≈ 10⁵.
* The electric dipole polarizability α_E of ⁴He is calculated as 0.0694 fm³, in agreement with a previous calculation by Stetcu (2009).
* For the sum rule with weight ω⁻³/², the LSR result is 8.414 fm⁷/² compared to the explicit integration (ESR) result of 8.434 fm⁷/², a relative difference of ∼10⁻³.
* For the sum rule with weight ω⁻¹, the LSR result is 3.405 fm³ compared to the ESR result of 3.409 fm³, a relative difference of ∼10⁻³.
* For the sum rule with weight ω³/², the LSR result is 0.133 fm¹/² compared to the ESR result of 0.117 fm¹/², a relative difference of ∼10⁻¹, attributed to the ESR integration being limited to ω_max = 518 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the sum rule method previously applied by the authors in a 2013 study of ⁴He sum rules (Ji 2013a) to evaluate the electric polarizability, generalizing it to weight functions of arbitrary analytic form beyond integer powers of energy. The method is directly applicable to calculating nuclear polarization corrections in muonic atoms, which have recently drawn renewed interest from Pachucki (2011), Antognini (2011), Ji (2013), and Friar (2013). The technique enables efficient evaluation of sum rules for electroweak reactions in nuclei without requiring explicit calculation of the response function, which typically requires an order of magnitude more Lanczos steps than the proposed method.
