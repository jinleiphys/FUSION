# Energy density at kinetic freeze-out in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC using the Tsallis distribution

> The thermodynamic parameters like energy density, pressure, entropy density, temperature and particle density are determined from the transverse momentum distributions of charged particles in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. The results show a clear increase with the centrality and the beam energy in all parameters. It is determined that in the final freeze-out stage the energy density reaches a value of about 0.039 GeV/fm$^3$ for the most central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV. This is less than that at chemical freeze-out where the energy density is about 0.36 GeV/fm$^3$. This decrease approximately follows a $T^4$ law. The results for the pressure and entropy density are also presented for each centrality class at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV.

# Energy density at kinetic freeze-out in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC using the Tsallis distribution

## Key claim

The energy density at kinetic freeze-out in the most central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV reaches about 0.039 GeV/fm^3, which is an order of magnitude smaller than the chemical freeze-out value of about 0.36 GeV/fm^3; this decrease approximately follows a T^4 law, but the Tsallis fit systematically overshoots data above p_T of about 3 GeV for central events and then undershoots at very high p_T, indicating the two-parameter fit is insufficient for the most central collisions.

## Method

The transverse momentum distributions of charged hadrons (pi+, K+, protons) measured by ALICE in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV were fitted using a thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution with three parameters: temperature T, Tsallis parameter q, and volume V. From the fitted T and q values, the energy density, pressure, entropy density, and particle density at kinetic freeze-out were calculated via integrals over the Tsallis distribution function for each centrality class.

## Key numbers

- Energy density at kinetic freeze-out for most central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: 0.03933 +/- 0.00049 GeV/fm^3
- Energy density at kinetic freeze-out for most central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: 0.03272 +/- 0.00041 GeV/fm^3
- Energy density at chemical freeze-out (from THERMUS): 0.36 +/- 0.07 GeV/fm^3
- Temperature for most central (0-5%) collisions at 5.02 TeV: T = 98.2 +/- 1.3 MeV
- Temperature for most central (0-5%) collisions at 2.76 TeV: T = 95.9 +/- 1.4 MeV
- Tsallis parameter q for most central (0-5%) collisions at 5.02 TeV: q = 1.1405 +/- 0.0009
- Tsallis parameter q for most central (0-5%) collisions at 2.76 TeV: q = 1.1355 +/- 0.0009
- Ratio of kinetic to chemical freeze-out proper time for top 5% central collisions at 5.02 TeV: tau(kinetic)/tau(chemical) approximately 3.9 (using Bjorken model with isentropic expansion)
- Energy density inside a proton (for comparison): 0.334 GeV/fm^3
- Lattice QCD energy density in phase transition region: 0.34 +/- 0.16 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution approach previously developed by Cleymans et al. (2011, 2012) and applied to pp collisions by Grigoryan (2017), now applied to Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies. The results provide the first systematic determination of thermodynamic quantities at kinetic freeze-out across centrality classes at two beam energies, enabling comparison with chemical freeze-out values from THERMUS (Wheaton, 2004) and lattice QCD results (Ding, 2015). The extracted energy densities and the estimated hadronic phase lifetime (approximately factor 3.9 in proper time between chemical and kinetic freeze-out) constrain hydrodynamic models of the expansion stage, particularly the Bjorken model (1982). The Tsallis fit quality worsens for central collisions compared to peripheral ones, suggesting that an additional parameter beyond T and q may be needed to describe the S-shaped transverse momentum distributions in central events.
