# Hard thermal loops, to quadratic order, in the background of a spatial 't Hooft loop

> We compute the simplest hard thermal loops for a spatial 't Hooft loop in the deconfined phase of a SU(N) gauge theory. We expand to quadratic order about a constant background field A_0 = Q/g, where Q is a diagonal, color matrix and g is the gauge coupling constant. We analyze the problem in sufficient generality that the techniques developed can be applied to compute transport properties in a "semi"-Quark Gluon Plasma. Notably, computations are done using the double line notation at finite N. The quark self-energy is a Q-dependent thermal mass squared, of order g^2T^2, where T is the temperature, times the same hard thermal loop as at Q=0. The gluon self-energy involves two pieces: a Q-dependent Debye mass squared, of order g^2T^2, times the same hard thermal loop as for Q=0, plus a new hard thermal loop, of order g^2T^3, due to the color electric field generated by a spatial 't Hooft loop.

## Key claim

The authors compute the simplest hard thermal loops for a spatial 't Hooft loop in the deconfined phase of an SU(N) gauge theory, expanding to quadratic order about a constant background field A₀ = Q/g. The key hedge is that while the quark self-energy and part of the gluon self-energy retain the same functional form as at Q=0 (only with Q-dependent thermal masses), the gluon self-energy contains a new hard thermal loop term of order g²T³/p that arises uniquely from non-Abelian gauge fields in the presence of the background field.

## Method

The authors use the double line notation at finite N to compute one-loop self-energies for quarks and gluons in a constant background A₀ field, working in the imaginary time formalism and then analytically continuing to real time. They expand about a background field in background field gauge, where the background field is a constant diagonal matrix Q/g for the timelike component of the vector potential, and compute hard thermal loops by integrating over hard momenta of order T while keeping external momenta soft (of order gT). The propagators and vertices are modified by shifting Euclidean energies by Q-dependent color factors, and the authors treat the background field as present only on the imaginary time contour.

## Key numbers

- The quark self-energy acquires a Q-dependent thermal mass squared of order g²T², times the same hard thermal loop function as at Q=0
- The gluon self-energy contains a Q-dependent Debye mass squared of order g²T², times the same hard thermal loop as for Q=0
- A new hard thermal loop appears in the gluon self-energy of order g²T³/p, where p is the spatial momentum
- The tadpole integral for scalar fields with Q≠0 gives T²/12 times the function 𝒜(Q), where 𝒜(Q) is the same function appearing in the quark self-energy
- The integral ∫ dK (k₀+Q)/[(k₀+Q)²+k²] equals πT³/3 times 𝒩𝒜(Q), where 𝒩𝒜(Q) = q(1-q)(1-2q) for Q = 2πT q

## Context

This work extends the computation of hard thermal loops to the case of a nonzero background A₀ field, relevant for understanding spatial 't Hooft loops (ZN interfaces) in the deconfined phase of SU(N) gauge theories. The authors note that Smilga has argued that ZN domain walls are only valid in imaginary time, but they contend that such structures must be relevant for small fluctuations about thermal equilibrium. The techniques developed are intended to be applied to compute transport properties in a "semi"-Quark Gluon Plasma, building on earlier work by Furuuchi and by Korthals-Altes on free energies with nonzero A₀. The double line notation follows Cvitanović and generalizes the large-Nc approach of 't Hooft to finite N.
