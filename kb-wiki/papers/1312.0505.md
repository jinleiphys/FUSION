# Critical temperature and superfluid gap of the Unitary Fermi Gas from Functional Renormalization

> We investigate the superfluid transition of the Unitary Fermi Gas by means of the Functional Renormalization Group, aiming at quantitative precision. We extract $T_{\rm c}/μ=0.38(2)$ and $Δ/μ=1.04(15)$ for the critical temperature and the superfluid gap at zero temperature, respectively, within a systematic improvement of the truncation for the effective average action. The key new ingredient in comparison to previous approaches consists in the use of regulators which cut off both frequencies and momenta. We incorporate renormalization effects on both the bosonic and the fermionic propagator, include higher order bosonic scattering processes, and investigate the regulator and specification parameter dependence for an error estimate. The ratio $Δ/T_{\rm c}=2.7(3)$ becomes less sensitive to the relative cutoff scale of bosons and fermions when improving the truncation. The techniques developed in this work are easily carried over to the cases of finite scattering length, lower dimensionality, and spin-imbalance.

## Key claim

The authors use the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) with regulators that cut off both frequencies and momenta to extract the critical temperature and superfluid gap of the Unitary Fermi Gas, reporting best estimates of \(T_{\rm c}/\mu = 0.38(2)\) and \(\Delta/\mu = 1.04(15)\), with the caveat that these values depend on the truncation scheme and the relative cutoff scale between bosons and fermions, with systematic errors estimated from residual regulator dependence.

## Method

The authors employ a partially bosonized model of the Unitary Fermi Gas and solve the FRG flow equation for the effective average action, using regulators that cut off both Matsubara frequencies and spatial momenta (the "Q-exp" regulator) to implement a shell-by-shell integration. They systematically extend the truncation from fermion-only diagrams (F) to include bosonic diagrams (FB) and mixed fermion-boson diagrams (FBM), incorporating running couplings for the boson propagator (wave function renormalization, linear and quadratic frequency terms), the effective potential (up to \(\phi^8\) order), the fermion mass, the fermion wave function renormalization, and the Yukawa coupling.

## Key numbers

*   Critical temperature: \(T_{\rm c}/\mu = 0.38(2)\) (best estimate, systematic error in parentheses).
*   Superfluid gap at zero temperature: \(\Delta/\mu = 1.04(15)\) (best estimate, systematic error in parentheses).
*   Ratio of gap to critical temperature: \(\Delta/T_{\rm c} = 2.7(3)\) (best estimate, systematic error in parentheses).
*   For the highest truncation (FBM), the critical temperature is \(T_{\rm c}/\mu = 0.38(2)\) and the gap is \(\Delta/\mu = 0.89(5)\), with the error from uncertainty in the chemical potential.
*   The anomalous dimension at the critical temperature for a \(\phi^4\)-truncation settles at \(\eta = 0.05(1)\), consistent with the O(2)-universality class.

## Context

This work extends previous FRG studies of the BCS-BEC crossover (e.g., Birse (2004), PhysRevA.76.021602, PhysRevA.76.053627) by introducing frequency-and-momentum regulators to improve the separation of scales, and by systematically incorporating bosonic and mixed diagrams to achieve quantitative precision. The results enable benchmarking against quantum Monte Carlo simulations (e.g., PhysRevLett.91.050401, PhysRevLett.93.200404) and T-matrix approaches (e.g., Haussmann (1991), PhysRevA.75.023610), and the techniques are directly applicable to finite scattering length, lower dimensionality, and spin-imbalanced systems.
