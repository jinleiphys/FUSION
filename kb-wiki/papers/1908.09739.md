# Coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering on 40Ar from first principles

> Coherent elastic neutrino scattering on the 40Ar nucleus is computed with coupled-cluster theory based on nuclear Hamiltonians inspired by effective field theories of quantum chromodynamics. Our approach is validated by calculating the charge form factor and comparing it to data from electron scattering. We make predictions for the weak form factor, the neutron radius, and the neutron skin, and estimate systematic uncertainties. The neutron-skin thickness of 40Ar40 is consistent with results from density functional theory. Precision measurements from coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering could potentially be used to extract these observables and help to constrain nuclear models.

## Key claim
The authors compute the weak form factor and neutron-skin thickness of 40Ar using coupled-cluster theory from first principles, but note that the Hamiltonian dependence in the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering cross section is likely too small to be disentangled by the current COHERENT experiment, requiring much improved precision to probe differences in nuclear Hamiltonians.

## Method
The ground state of the open-shell nucleus 40Ar (Z=18, N=22) is calculated using a double-charge-exchange equation-of-motion technique starting from the closed-shell nucleus 40Ca within coupled-cluster theory. The calculations employ several chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians (NNLOsat, NNLOgod, and soft EM-family potentials) at the coupled-cluster singles and doubles level with linearized triples (T-1), using a model space of 15 major oscillator shells with harmonic oscillator parameter hbar-Omega = 16 MeV. The charge form factor is validated against electron scattering data from Ottermann et al., and the weak form factor is then computed from the neutron and proton form factors.

## Key numbers
- The neutron-skin thickness of 40Ar is predicted in the range 0.035 to 0.09 fm across the different Hamiltonians.
- At q = 50 MeV, the weak form factor FW shows a 2% spread among Hamiltonians; at q = 100 MeV, the spread is 6%.
- For the NNLOsat interaction at q = 0.25 fm^-1, FW is 0.5% smaller than the charge form factor Fch; at q = 1 fm^-1, FW is 20% smaller.
- The first dip of FW falls about 0.035 fm^-1 earlier than that of Fch for the NNLOsat interaction.
- Intersecting the correlation band of Rp and Rn with the experimental Rp value yields a constraint 3.36 <= Rn <= 3.45 fm.

## Context
This work extends ab initio calculations of nuclear electroweak properties to the medium-mass nucleus 40Ar, following similar coupled-cluster studies on 48Ca by Hagen et al. (2015) and ab initio computations by Barbieri et al. (2019). The results are consistent with density functional theory calculations by Patton et al. (2012) and Schunk et al., though DFT predicts a slightly larger neutron-skin thickness. The predictions provide a first-principles benchmark for the COHERENT experiment's planned liquid argon target and for future measurements at DUNE, and could help constrain chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians if experimental precision improves sufficiently.
