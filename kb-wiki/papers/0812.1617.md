# Combined study of gamma p to eta p and pi-p to eta n in a chiral constituent quark approach

> Within a chiral constituent quark model approach, $η$-meson production on the proton {\it via} electromagnetic and hadron probes are studied. With few parameters, differential cross-section and polarized beam asymmetry for γp \to ηp$ and differential cross section for $π^- p\to ηn$ processes are calculated and successfully compared with the data in the center-of-mass energy range from threshold up to 2 GeV. The five known resonances $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, and $F_{15}(1680)$ are found to be dominant in the reaction mechanisms in both channels. Possible roles plaied by new resonances are also investigated and in the photoproduction channel, significant contribution from $S_{11}$- and $D_{15}$-resonances, with masses around 1715 MeV and 2090 MeV, respectively, are deduced. For the so-called missing resonances, no evidence is found within the investigated reactions. The helicity amplitudes and decay widths of $N^* \to πN, ηN$ are also presented, and found consistent with the PDG values.

## Key claim

Within a chiral constituent quark model, the five known resonances $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, and $F_{15}(1680)$ dominate both the electromagnetic and hadronic eta-production channels, but the photoproduction data require significant contributions from two additional new resonances: an $S_{11}$ with mass around 1715 MeV and a $D_{15}$ with mass around 2090 MeV; no evidence is found for the so-called missing resonances.

## Method

The authors extended a chiral constituent quark model formalism, previously applied to photoproduction, to the hadronic channel $\pi^- p \to \eta n$ and performed a combined analysis of both reactions. They used an effective chiral Lagrangian with quark and meson fields, introduced configuration mixings via a one-gluon-exchange model to break the $SU(6) \otimes O(3)$ symmetry, and fitted 21 free parameters simultaneously to 1783 experimental data points (differential cross sections and polarized beam asymmetries) plus the PDG mass spectrum of 12 known nucleon resonances with masses up to 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The harmonic oscillator strength $\alpha$ was extracted as 348 MeV, lowered by about 20% compared to the photoproduction-only fit due to inclusion of the strong channel.
* The $\eta$-nucleon coupling constant $g_{\eta NN}$ was determined to be 0.376, compatible with previous photoproduction-only fits.
* The new $S_{11}$ resonance has a mass of 1715 MeV and a width of 207 MeV; the new $D_{15}$ resonance has a mass of 2090 MeV and a width of 345 MeV.
* The total $\chi^2$ per data point for the combined fit is 2.05 (3627/1772), with 2.27 for $\gamma p \to \eta p$ (3187/1404) and 1.15 for $\pi^- p \to \eta n$ (408/354).
* Turning off the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance increases the total $\chi^2$ to 136 without further minimization, demonstrating its dominant role.

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model approach of He and Saghai (2008) from photoproduction alone to a combined analysis of both $\gamma p \to \eta p$ and $\pi^- p \to \eta n$ reactions, building on earlier constituent quark model studies by Li (1997, 1998) and Saghai (2001). The results are consistent with coupled-channels effective Lagrangian approaches such as those by the EBAC collaboration (Durand 2008) but use fewer adjustable parameters due to the quark-level degrees of freedom. The findings enable future searches for new resonances in other meson production channels and provide a unified framework for interpreting data from facilities like CLAS, ELSA, GRAAL, and MAMI.
