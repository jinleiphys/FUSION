# Charmonium mass in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

> We calculate the in-medium masses of J/$ψ$ and of the excited states of charmonium ($ψ$(3686) and $ψ$(3770)) in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures. These mass modifications arise due to the interaction of the charmonium states with the gluon condensates of QCD, simulated by a scalar dilaton field introduced to incorporate the broken scale invariance of QCD within an effective chiral model. The change in the mass of J/$ψ$ in the nuclear matter with density is seen to be rather small, as has been shown in the literature by using various approaches, whereas, the masses of the excited states of charmonium ($ψ$(3686) and $ψ$(3770)) are seen to have considerable drop at high densities. The dependence of the masses of the charmonium states on the isospin asymmetry has also been investigated in the hot nuclear matter and is seen to be appreciable at moderate temperatures and high densities. These medium modifications of the charmonium states should modify the experimental observables arising from the compressed baryonic matter produced in asymmetric heavy ion collision experiments in the future facility of FAIR, GSI.

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of J/psi, psi(3686), and psi(3770) in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures are calculated using the leading order QCD mass shift formula, with the gluon condensate modification obtained from the medium dependence of a scalar dilaton field within a chiral SU(3) model; the mass shift of J/psi is small, while the excited states show considerable drop at high densities, and the isospin asymmetry dependence is appreciable only at moderate temperatures and high densities.

## Method

The gluon condensate in the nuclear medium is related to the fourth power of the dilaton field chi via the trace anomaly relation, and the medium modification of chi is obtained by solving the coupled mean-field equations for the scalar fields (sigma, zeta, delta, and chi) within a chiral SU(3) model at finite temperature and baryon density. The mass shift of each charmonium state is then computed using the leading order QCD formula involving the momentum-space wave function of the state, taken as Gaussian wave functions with oscillator constants determined from the mean squared radii (0.46^2 fm^2 for J/psi, 0.96^2 fm^2 for psi(3686), and 1 fm^2 for psi(3770)), and the charm quark mass is taken as 1.95 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature and isospin symmetric nuclear matter (eta = 0), the dilaton field chi drops from its vacuum value of 409.8 MeV to about 406.4 MeV at nuclear matter saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}, and to about 396.7 MeV at 4 rho_0.
- At zero temperature and rho_0, the dilaton field chi changes from 406.4 MeV in symmetric matter (eta = 0) to 406.5 MeV in asymmetric matter (eta = 0.5); at 4 rho_0, chi changes from 396.7 MeV (eta = 0) to 398 MeV (eta = 0.5).
- At rho_0 and eta = 0, the dilaton field chi increases with temperature up to about 145 MeV before decreasing; at rho_0 and eta = 0.5, chi rises up to about 120 MeV before decreasing.
- At rho_0 and T = 150 MeV, chi is 407.3 MeV for eta = 0 and 407 MeV for eta = 0.5; at 4 rho_0 and T = 150 MeV, chi is 399.1 MeV for eta = 0 and 398.7 MeV for eta = 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lee (2007) for charmonium mass shifts in nuclear matter by incorporating finite temperature and isospin asymmetry effects through the chiral SU(3) model of Papazoglou et al. (1999), which has previously been applied to kaons, antikaons, and D mesons in asymmetric nuclear matter. The results are relevant for interpreting experimental observables from compressed baryonic matter produced in asymmetric heavy ion collisions at the future FAIR facility at GSI.
