# Equilibration dynamics and isospin effects in nuclear reactions

> We discuss equilibration times and isospin effect for various quantities in low-energy heavy-ion reactions. These include equilibration of mass, isospin, and total kinetic energy (TKE) in quasifission and deep-inelastic reactions. The calculations are performed using the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory. The influence of shell effects on the equilibration times are also discussed in the context of theoretical and experimental results.

## Key claim
The paper argues that mass equilibration times in low-energy heavy-ion reactions are much longer than isospin and total kinetic energy (TKE) equilibration times, with typical mass equilibration around 10 zs, but the authors hedge that these values depend on shell effects, orientation of deformed nuclei, and the specific projectile-target combination.

## Method
The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations for several heavy-ion reaction systems, including \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+^{249}\mathrm{Bk}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=234\) MeV, \(^{54}\mathrm{Cr}+^{186}\mathrm{W}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=218.6\) MeV, and \(^{78}\mathrm{Kr}+^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) at 8.5 MeV/nucleon. They extracted equilibration times by fitting exponential functions to the time evolution of mass asymmetry, isospin asymmetry \((N-Z)/A\), and exit-channel TKE as functions of contact time for various impact parameters and orientations of deformed nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Mass equilibration time for \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}+^{249}\mathrm{Bk}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=234\) MeV: between 5 and 10 zs, depending on orientation of the deformed \(^{249}\mathrm{Bk}\) nucleus.
- Mass equilibration time for \(^{54}\mathrm{Cr}+^{186}\mathrm{W}\) at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=218.6\) MeV: between 7 and 12 zs, depending on orientation of the deformed \(^{186}\mathrm{W}\) nucleus.
- Isospin equilibration time for \(^{78}\mathrm{Kr}+^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) at 8.5 MeV/nucleon: approximately 0.5 zs, extracted from the final \((N-Z)/A\) value of the fragments.
- TKE equilibration time for \(^{78}\mathrm{Kr}+^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) and \(^{92}\mathrm{Kr}+^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) at 8.5 MeV/nucleon: in the range of 1.5 to 2 zs, based on the stabilization of exit-channel TKE as a function of contact time.
- Experimental isospin equilibration time in the Fermi energy range (cited from Jedele 2017): about 0.3 zs.

## Context
This work extends TDHF studies of quasifission and deep-inelastic reactions, building on prior calculations by Golabek (2009), Kedziora (2010), and Wakhle (2014) that showed TDHF predicts mass-angle distributions well. It also connects to experimental charge equilibration studies by Jedele (2017) at Fermi energies and by Planeta (1988, 1990) at lower energies. The results enable future comparisons with experiments at radioactive ion-beam facilities and motivate further TDHF calculations to refine equilibration time systematics across a wider range of projectile-target combinations.
