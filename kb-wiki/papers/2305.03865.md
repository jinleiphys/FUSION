# Strong-Field Physics in QED and QCD: From Fundamentals to Applications

> We provide a pedagogical review article on fundamentals and applications of the quantum dynamics in strong electromagnetic fields in QED and QCD. The fundamentals include the basic picture of the Landau quantization and the resummation techniques applied to the class of higher-order diagrams that are enhanced by large magnitudes of the external fields. We then discuss observable effects of the vacuum fluctuations in the presence of the strong fields, which consist of the interdisciplinary research field of nonlinear QED. We also discuss extensions of the Heisenberg-Euler effective theory to finite temperature/density and to non-Abelian theories with some applications. Next, we proceed to the paradigm of the dimensional reduction emerging in the low-energy dynamics in the strong magnetic fields. The mechanisms of superconductivity, the magnetic catalysis of the chiral symmetry breaking, and the Kondo effect are addressed from a unified point of view in terms of the renormalization-group method. We provide an up-to-date summary of the lattice QCD simulations in magnetic fields for the chiral symmetry breaking and the related topics as of the end of 2022. Finally, we discuss novel transport phenomena induced by chiral anomaly and the axial-charge dynamics. Those discussions are supported by a number of appendices.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the non-Abelian extension of the Heisenberg-Euler effective action provides a perturbative resummation framework for understanding the QCD vacuum structure, but notes that a nonzero chromo-magnetic field having specific color and spatial directions breaks gauge and Lorentz symmetries and therefore cannot be regarded as the true vacuum by itself, requiring instead the "Spaghetti vacuum" proposal of Ambjorn and Olesen (1979, 1980) to recover symmetries on average at large scales.

## Method

The authors derive the one-loop effective action for fermions in strong electromagnetic fields at finite temperature using the imaginary-time formalism, extending the Heisenberg-Euler effective action to include thermal effects through the Poisson summation formula. They perform a gauge transformation to the Fock-Schwinger gauge, which modifies the boundary conditions and shifts the Matsubara frequencies by a quantity analogous to a chemical potential, then evaluate the proper-time integral to obtain the effective Lagrangian in both parallel and general field configurations. The formalism is applied to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the strong-field limit to study magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- The strong-field limit condition: $|eB| \gg m^2$, where the lowest Landau level contribution dominates
- The density of states in a magnetic field: $\rho_B = |q_f B|/(2\pi)$
- The effective potential in the strong-field limit at zero temperature: $V^\one_\vac \sim \rho_B \frac{m^2}{4\pi} \left[ \frac{\Lambda^2}{m^2} - \ln \frac{\Lambda^2}{m^2} + \gamma_{\rm E} -1 \right]$, where $\gamma_{\rm E}=0.577\cdots$ is the Euler-Mascheroni constant
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for massless fermions at finite temperature: $\lim_{q_f \vphi,m_f\to 0}{\cal L}^\one_T = (2 \cdot 2\cdot N_f) \times \frac{7}{8} \cdot \frac{\pi^2}{90} T^4$

## Context

This pedagogical review extends the Heisenberg-Euler effective action framework from QED to QCD, building on early work by Batalin (1976), Savvidy (1977), and Matinyan (1976) on the effective potential in homogeneous chromo-magnetic fields, which led to the prediction of the Savvidy vacuum. The paper connects this line of research to modern applications in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the "glasma" state produces strong color electromagnetic fields with strength $g{\cal F}\gg m_q^2$, and discusses how the generalized Heisenberg-Euler effective action at finite temperature, including the Polyakov loop, enables studies of electromagnetic field effects on the deconfinement phase transition.
