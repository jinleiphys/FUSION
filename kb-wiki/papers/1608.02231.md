# Directed Flow of Charm Quarks as a Witness of the Initial Strong Magnetic Field in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Ultra-relativistic Heavy-Ion Collision (HIC) generates very strong initial magnetic field ($\vec B$) inducing a vorticity in the reaction plane. The high $\vec{B}$ influences the evolution dynamics that is opposed by the large Faraday current due to electric field generated by the time varying $\vec{B}$. We show that the resultant effects entail a significantly large directed flow ($v_1$) of charm quarks (CQs) compared to light quarks due to a combination of several favorable conditions for CQs, mainly: (i) unlike light quarks formation time scale of CQs, $τ_f \simeq \, 0.1 \rm fm/c$ is comparable to the time scale when $\vec B$ attains its maximum value and (ii) the kinetic relaxation time of CQs is similar to the QGP lifetime, this helps the CQ to retain the initial kick picked up from the electromagnetic field in the transverse direction. The effect is also odd under charge exchange allowing to distinguish it from the vorticity of the bulk matter due to the initial angular momentum conservation; conjointly thanks to its mass, $M_c >>Λ_{QCD}$, there should be no mixing with the chiral magnetic dynamics. Hence CQs provide very crucial and independent information on the strength of the magnetic field produced in HIC.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the directed flow ($v_1$) of charm quarks can serve as a clean, independent probe of the initial strong magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions, because charm quarks are produced very early (formation time ~0.1 fm/c) when the magnetic field is maximal, and their long kinetic relaxation time (~5-8 fm/c) allows them to retain the electromagnetic kick, unlike light quarks which thermalize too quickly to preserve the signal.

## Method

The authors solve a relativistic Langevin equation for charm quarks in an expanding quark-gluon plasma background, including the external Lorentz force from electromagnetic fields. The space-time evolution of the magnetic field $B_y$ and the induced electric field $E_x$ (from Faraday's law) is computed analytically assuming a constant electrical conductivity $\sigma_{el}=0.023$ fm$^{-1}$ from lattice QCD, for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV with impact parameter $b=9.5$ fm. The background QGP evolution and the charm quark drag coefficient $\Gamma$ (in the range 0.15-0.2 fm$^{-1}$) are constrained by experimental data on $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ of D mesons.

## Key numbers

* Initial magnetic field strength at LHC: $eB_y \sim 50\,m^2_{\pi}$ (estimated from prior work, cited in text)
* Charm quark formation time: $\tau_f \simeq 0.1$ fm/c
* Charm quark relaxation time: $\tau_{c}^{eq} \simeq 1/\Gamma \approx 5-8$ fm/c
* Light quark equilibration time: $\tau_{QGP}^{eq} \approx 0.5-1$ fm/c (for comparison)
* Slope of directed flow at mid-rapidity for D mesons: $dv_1/dy|_{y=0} \simeq -1.75 \times 10^{-2}$ for $p_T > 1$ GeV at $b=9.5$ fm
* Peak of $v_1$ occurs at rapidity $y \simeq 1.75$
* Electrical conductivity used: $\sigma_{el}=0.023$ fm$^{-1}$
* Maximum temperature at initial time $\tau_0=0.2$ fm/c: $T_0=580$ MeV
* Impact parameter for calculations: $b=9.5$ fm

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the electromagnetic field in heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev 2007, Tuchin 2012, Deng 2012, Gursoy 2014) by applying the field effects specifically to heavy quark dynamics, rather than to chiral magnetic effects, jet energy loss, or thermal photons. The authors build on their own previous work (Das 2015) that constrained the charm quark drag coefficient from $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ data. The paper demonstrates that charm quarks provide a cleaner signal than light quarks for measuring the magnetic field, because light quark $v_1$ is suppressed by early thermalization and by the fact that many light hadrons come from gluons which are not electromagnetically coupled. The charge-odd nature of the directed flow also allows separation from vorticity-driven flow (Becattini 2015) and from chiral magnetic/vortex effects (Jiang 2015, Liao 2016).
