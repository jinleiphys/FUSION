# Anisotropic Transverse Flow and the HBT Correlation Function

> Utilizing the Lorentz invariance of the correlation function we study the effects of anisotropic transverse flow on the HBT correlation function. In particular we show that directed flow would evidence itself by non zero ``side--long'' and ``side--out'' terms in the correlation function. We also show that the study of ratios of the correlation functions evaluated on different event subsamples and/or different Lorentz systems provides information on the source dimensions and velocity which could be less biased by corrections of different kinds. We present a fitting technique appropriate for HBT analysis which does not require binning and is especially useful for multidimensional fitting.

# Anisotropic Transverse Flow and the HBT Correlation Function

## Key claim

Directed flow in heavy-ion collisions would manifest as nonzero "side-long" and "side-out" cross terms in the HBT correlation function, even when the correlation function is determined from an azimuthally symmetric event sample where the reaction plane is not determined. The authors also claim that studying ratios of correlation functions evaluated in different Lorentz systems or event subsamples can extract source dimensions and flow velocities with less bias from corrections.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the HBT correlation function parameters under Lorentz transformations, assuming a Gaussian source in its rest frame with no cross terms except those required by the mass-shell constraint. They then apply Lorentz boosts representing transverse directed flow (with transverse rapidity y_perp) and longitudinal boosts to the analysis frame, obtaining expressions for the fitted parameters A_x, A_y, A_z, A_xy, A_xz, and A_yz as functions of the source parameters and flow velocity. They also present a maximum-likelihood fitting technique that avoids binning by using the mixed-pair distribution to evaluate the normalization integral.

## Key numbers

- The "side-long" cross term parameter B_yz = (a_perp + a_0) * sinh(2*y_perp) * sinh(tilde_y - y*) / sinh(tilde_y - y), where a_perp and a_0 are source parameters, y_perp is the transverse rapidity of the flow, tilde_y is the pair rapidity, and y* is the source rapidity.
- The log-likelihood function for a Gaussian correlation function C(x) = 1 + lambda * exp(-a*x^2) is given by: ln L(a,lambda) = sum_{true pairs} ln(1 + lambda * exp(-a*x_k^2)) - N_true * ln{ (1/N_mixed) * sum_{mixed pairs} (1 + lambda * exp(-a*x_i^2)) }.
- Fluctuations in mixed-pair distributions scale as delta N_mixed proportional to N_mixed^(3/4), in contrast to true-pair fluctuations delta N_true proportional to N_true^(1/2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study using RQMD-generated events (Voloshin and Cleland, 1996) that showed how directed flow affects extracted HBT radii. It builds on the discovery of transverse directed flow in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions (E877 Collaboration, 1995) and addresses the need to account for flow effects in HBT analysis. The paper enables experimental tests of Gaussian source assumptions through the rapidity dependence of fitted parameters, particularly the prediction that the "side radius squared" (A_y) should be independent of analysis frame rapidity if a single source rest frame exists. The likelihood fitting method presented avoids problems with low bin statistics and mixed-pair fluctuations that affect conventional binning approaches.
