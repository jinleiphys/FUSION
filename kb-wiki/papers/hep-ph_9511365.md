# Chiral perturbation theory analysis of baryon temperature mass shifts

> We compute the finite temperature pole mass shifts of the octet and decuplet baryons using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and the 1/N_c expansion, where N_c is the number of QCD colors. We consider temperatures of the order of the pion mass m_π, and truncate the chiral and 1/N_c expansions assuming that m_π\sim 1/N_c. There are three scales in the problem: the temperature T, the pion mass m_π, and the octet--decuplet mass difference. Therefore, the result is not simply a power series in T. We find that the nucleon and Δtemperature mass shifts are opposite in sign, and that their mass difference changes by 20% in the temperature range 90 MeV < T < 130 MeV, that is the range where the freeze out in relativistic heavy ion collisions is expected to occur. We argue that our results are insensitive to the neglect of 1/N_c- supressed effects; the main purpose of the 1/N_c expansion in this work is to justify our treatment of the decuplet states.

# Chiral Perturbation Theory Analysis of Baryon Temperature Mass Shifts

## Key claim

The nucleon and Delta baryon temperature mass shifts are opposite in sign, and their mass difference changes by 20% in the temperature range 90 MeV < T < 130 MeV, which is the range where freeze-out in relativistic heavy ion collisions is expected to occur. The result is not simply a power series in T because there are three scales in the problem: the temperature T, the pion mass m_pi, and the octet-decuplet mass difference.

## Method

The calculation uses heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBchiPT) combined with the 1/N_c expansion, where N_c is the number of QCD colors, to compute the leading-order temperature-dependent pole mass shifts of octet and decuplet baryons. The real-time formalism for finite-temperature field theory is employed, with the baryon propagator resummed to include the zero-temperature octet-decuplet mass splitting, and only pion thermal loops are retained since kaon and eta loops are suppressed by Boltzmann factors. The leading contribution comes from a one-loop graph with the baryon propagator resummed to include the octet-decuplet mass splitting, evaluated at the tree-level mass shell.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon and Delta mass difference changes by 20% in the temperature range 90 MeV < T < 130 MeV
- The coupling constant g = 3(D+F)/5 is approximately 0.8 in the large-N_c and SU_F(3) limits
- The pion decay constant f is approximately 93 MeV
- At T = 200 MeV, the nucleon residue Z^{-1} is reduced by 15% relative to the zero-temperature value
- The mass shifts are of order 20% at T approximately 150 MeV
- The width changes are about the same size as the mass shifts

## Context

This work extends the application of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Georgi 1990, Jenkins 1991) and the 1/N_c expansion (Dashen and Manohar 1993) to finite-temperature baryon properties, building on earlier studies of the temperature dependence of the chiral condensate in a pion gas (Gerber and Leutwyler 1989). The results are consistent with a general theorem (Leutwyler and Smilga 1990) that there is no contribution at order T^2 to the baryon mass in the chiral limit, and the calculation enables predictions for relative yields of different baryon species in relativistic heavy ion collisions based on temperature-dependent masses in the Fermi distribution at freeze-out.
