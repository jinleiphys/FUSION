# The QCD Equation of State with Thermal Properties of $ϕ$ mesons

> In this work a first attempt is made to extract the Equation of State (EoS) using experimental results of the $ϕ$ meson produced in nuclear collisions at AGS, SPS and RHIC energies. The data are confronted to simple thermodynamic expectations and lattice results. The experimental data indicate a first order phase transition, with a mixed phase stretching energy density between $\sim$ 1 and 3.2 GeV/$fm^3$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that experimental data on the inverse slope parameter of the phi meson, when plotted as a function of initial energy density, indicate a first-order phase transition with a mixed phase stretching between approximately 1 and 3.2 GeV/fm^3, but the authors note that these results do not exclude a change in the order of the transition at smaller baryochemical potentials.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental data on the inverse slope (effective temperature) of the phi meson transverse mass spectra at mid-rapidity from AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies. They extracted the thermal component of the inverse slope by subtracting the collective transverse flow contribution using published values of the average transverse flow velocity. The effective temperature and its thermal component were then plotted as functions of both the center-of-mass energy and the Bjorken initial energy density, and compared to simple thermodynamic expectations and lattice QCD predictions.

## Key numbers

- The mixed phase is found to stretch between initial energy density of approximately 1 and 3.2 GeV/fm^3.
- The critical energy density from lattice QCD is approximately 1 GeV/fm^3.
- The phi meson lifetime is approximately 45 fm/c.
- The phi-nucleon hadronic rescattering cross section is 10 mb.
- The bag constant derived from the latent heat density gives B^{1/4} approximately 250 MeV.
- The ratio of energy densities epsilon_Q/epsilon_H is approximately 3.2, comparable to the ratio of statistical degeneracies g_Q/g_H.
- The critical temperature T_c is approximately 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Van Hove signature approach, previously applied to pions, kaons, and protons by B. Mohanty and Marek Gazdzicki, to the phi meson for the first time. The phi meson is of special interest due to its small hadronic rescattering cross section and longer lifetime, making it a cleaner probe of the early stages of the collision. The results support a first-order phase transition at non-zero baryochemical potential as predicted by lattice QCD, and the authors suggest future work should map out the order of the QCD phase transition as a function of energy and baryochemical potential, including hydrodynamic calculations and studies of elliptic flow and transverse flow velocity.
