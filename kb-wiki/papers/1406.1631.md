# Constraining the nuclear energy density functional with quantum Monte Carlo calculations

> We study the problem of an impurity in fully polarized (spin-up) low density neutron matter with the help of an accurate quantum Monte Carlo method in conjunction with a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading-order. Our calculations show that the behavior of the proton spin-down impurity is very similar to that of a polaron in a fully polarized unitary Fermi gas. We show that our results can be used to put tight constraints on the time-odd parts of the energy density functional, independent of the time-even parts, in the density regime relevant to neutron-rich nuclei and compact astrophysical objects such as neutron stars and supernovae.

## Key claim

The behavior of a proton spin-down impurity in fully polarized low-density neutron matter is qualitatively very similar to that of a polaron in a fully polarized unitary Fermi gas, and the difference between proton spin-up and spin-down impurity energies depends only on the time-odd part of the nuclear energy density functional, independent of the time-even part.

## Method

The authors used the configuration interaction Monte Carlo (CIMC) method, a quantum Monte Carlo technique that performs stochastic projection in Fock space using Slater determinants constructed from a finite set of single-particle basis states (momentum eigenstates with periodic boundary conditions). They employed the chiral NNLO$_{\rm opt}$ nucleon-nucleon interaction and calculated ground state energies for fully polarized neutron matter with and without an additional impurity particle (spin-down neutron or spin-up/spin-down proton), using coupled cluster double-type wave functions as guiding wave functions to control the sign problem.

## Key numbers

- At $k_F=0.4$ fm$^{-1}$, the neutron spin-down impurity energy ratio $\varepsilon_{n\downarrow}/E_F = -0.582 \pm 0.002$ (CIMC with NNLO$_{\rm opt}$), compared to $-0.589 \pm 0.005$ from GFMC and $-0.567 \pm 0.006$ from AFDMC with Argonne $v_8'$
- For $\rho=0.04$ fm$^{-3}$, $\varepsilon_{n\downarrow}/E_F = -0.6698 \pm 0.0005$ with $N=7$ neutrons and $-0.664 \pm 0.006$ with $N=33$ neutrons
- For $\rho=0.06$ fm$^{-3}$, $\varepsilon_{n\downarrow}/E_F = -0.6617 \pm 0.0003$ with $N=7$ and $-0.647 \pm 0.004$ with $N=33$
- The proton spin-down impurity energy $\varepsilon_{p\downarrow}/E_F$ varies by less than 2% (between $-0.681$ and $-0.666$) over the density range $10^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$ to $5\times10^{-2}$ fm$^{-3}$
- The difference $(\varepsilon_{p\uparrow} - \varepsilon_{p\downarrow})/E_F$ is fit by $A - B/(k_F|a_s|) - C k_F r_e$ with $A=0.17\pm0.01$, $B=1.4\pm0.1$, $C=0.101\pm0.001$, using $a_s=-23.75$ fm and $r_e=2.75$ fm

## Context

This work extends the polaron problem from ultracold atomic gases (Chevy 2010, Massignan 2014) to nuclear systems, using quantum Monte Carlo methods to provide microscopic constraints for nuclear energy density functionals. The results show that current Skyrme-type EDFs (including SLy4, SkM*, BSk21, SkP, SkO', SAMi, TOV-min, and UNEDF-pol) fail to reproduce the QMC calculations, particularly the flat density dependence of the proton spin-down impurity energy, indicating that qualitative changes in the form of the EDFs are needed for systems with large spin and isospin polarization. The findings enable new constraints on the time-odd parts of nuclear energy density functionals relevant to neutron-rich nuclei, neutron star crusts, and supernova neutrinospheres.
