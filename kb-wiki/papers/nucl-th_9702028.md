# Treatment of baryonic resonances in the RQMD approach including scalar-vector mean fields

> In the relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) approach baryons are described within the framework of covariant hamilton constraint dynamics. The inclusion of a relativistic mean field results in a quasiparticle picture for the baryons. This requires to distinguish between canonical and kinetic variables of the particles. As resonances we include the $Δ$(1232) and the $N^*$(1440) resonance. The resonance masses are distributed according Breit-Wigner functions. However, the scalar self energy leads to a shift in the masses and introduces an additional medium dependence. Consequences of this description on resonance and pion dynamics are discussed.

## Key claim

The inclusion of relativistic scalar-vector mean fields in the RQMD approach shifts the mass distributions of baryonic resonances toward higher values and reduces the thresholds for excitation of high-lying resonances, but this effect depends on the specific mean-field model applied (e.g., the non-linear Walecka model NL2 produces smaller fields than the original Walecka model).

## Method

The authors extended the Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) approach by incorporating scalar and vector self-energies from the Walecka model (non-linear version NL2) into the covariant Hamiltonian constraint dynamics formalism. Baryons and resonances (Delta(1232) and N*(1440)) are treated as quasiparticles with effective Dirac masses, where the resonance masses are distributed according to Breit-Wigner functions and the scalar self-energy introduces a medium-dependent mass shift. The equations of motion are generated from the Hamiltonian formed by the on-shell constraints and time-fixation constraints, with Lagrange multipliers determined from the constraint algebra.

## Key numbers

- The bare decay widths are taken as Gamma_0^Delta = 120 MeV and Gamma_0^{N*} = 200 MeV for the Delta(1232) and N*(1440) resonances, respectively.
- In a central Ca+Ca collision at 1.85 A.GeV, the high-density phase occurs between approximately 10 fm/c and 20 fm/c.
- Pions originating from N* resonances contribute about 10% to the total pion yield in a 1.85 A.GeV Ni+Ni collision, with contributions exceeding 20% for high-energy pions.
- The transverse flow per particle for the reaction Ar+KCl at 1.8 A.GeV shows that static Skyrme forces produce far too little repulsion compared to the NL2 model and experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the RQMD approach of Sorge (1989) and Lehmann (1995), which previously used only static Skyrme forces treated as scalar potentials, by incorporating the full Lorentz structure of the nucleon-nucleon interaction with scalar and vector components as in the Walecka model (Serot, 1988). The study builds on earlier covariant generalizations of BUU-type models by Ko (1989), Cassing (1990), and Fuchs (1995), and on the formulation of scalar-vector RQMD given by Fuchs (1996). The results enable more realistic simulations of heavy-ion reactions in the SIS energy range, particularly for studying medium effects on baryonic resonances through high-energy pion production.
