# A Fluctuation Probe of Disoriented Chiral Condensates

> We show that an event-by-event fluctuation of the ratio of neutral pions or resulting photons to charged pions can be used as an effective probe for the formation of disoriented chiral condensates. The fact that the neutral pion fraction produced in case of disoriented chiral condensate formation has a characteristic extended non gaussian shape, is shown to be the key factor which forms the basis of the present analysis.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that event-by-event fluctuations of the ratio of neutral pions (or their decay photons) to charged pions can serve as an effective probe for the formation of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC), provided that the characteristic non-Gaussian probability distribution of the neutral pion fraction in DCC events is the key distinguishing feature.

## Method

The authors define the fluctuation observable \( D = \langle \delta R^2 \rangle / \langle R \rangle^2 \) where \( R = N_{\pi^0}/N_{ch} \). They derive an analytic expression showing that \( D \) depends only on the fluctuation of the neutral pion fraction \( f = N_{\pi^0}/N_{\pi} \). They then compute \( D \) for non-DCC events (binomial distribution, \( \langle f \rangle = 1/3 \)) and for DCC events (using the characteristic probability distribution \( P(f) = 1/(2\sqrt{f}) \)), and extend the analysis to mixtures of DCC and non-DCC events, multiple DCC domains, and the presence of background pions from non-DCC sources.

## Key numbers

- For DCC events, the fluctuation observable \( D_{DCC} = 1.8 \), independent of multiplicity.
- For non-DCC events with total pion multiplicity \( N_{\pi} = 300 \), \( D_{non-DCC} \approx 0.015 \).
- For non-DCC events, \( D_{non-DCC} \sim 4.5/N_{\pi} \).
- With typical detector efficiency and purity fluctuations of \( \sim 3\% \) (giving \( \langle \delta \epsilon^2 \rangle / \langle \epsilon \rangle^2 \approx 0.0009 \)), the non-DCC fluctuation becomes \( 0.015 \pm 0.0027 \).
- The method is sensitive to DCC event fractions \( \alpha \geq 0.2\% \) when all pions in DCC events are of DCC origin.
- The method is sensitive to DCC pion fractions \( \beta \geq 2.5\% \) in a sample of DCC events.

## Context

This work extends the use of event-by-event fluctuation observables, previously applied to charge and baryon number fluctuations as probes of quark-gluon plasma (Koch, Asakawa, Pratt, all around 2000), to the search for disoriented chiral condensates. The analysis builds on theoretical predictions for DCC formation (Rajagopal, Gavin, both early 1990s) and on experimental upper limits from WA98 at CERN SPS and NA49. The proposed method is intended for application at RHIC (STAR) and LHC (ALICE) experiments, where higher pion multiplicities improve sensitivity.
