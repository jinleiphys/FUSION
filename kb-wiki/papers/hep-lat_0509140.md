# A study of the N to Delta transition form factors in full QCD

> The N to Delta transition form factors GM1, GE2 and GC2 are evaluated using dynamical MILC configurations and valence domain wall fermions at three values of quark mass corresponding to pion mass 606 MeV, 502 MeV and 364 MeV on lattices of spatial size $20^3$ and $28^3$. The unquenched results are compared to those obtained at similar pion mass in the quenched theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary evidence that unquenching (including dynamical sea quarks) reduces the magnetic dipole transition form factor \(G_{M1}\) toward experimental values compared to quenched lattice results, but the statistical errors are too large to draw a firm conclusion, and the quadrupole ratios \(R_{EM}\) and \(R_{SM}\) remain too noisy for meaningful comparison.

## Method

The authors compute the \(N \to \Delta\) transition form factors \(G_{M1}\), \(G_{E2}\), and \(G_{C2}\) using dynamical MILC configurations (with \(N_f = 2+1\) flavors of improved staggered sea quarks) and valence domain wall fermions. Three-point correlation functions are evaluated with the \(\Delta\) at rest, and the form factors are extracted via a simultaneous overconstrained analysis of ratios of three-point to two-point functions, using optimized linear combinations (\(S_1\) and \(S_2\)) of interpolating fields to maximize the number of contributing lattice momenta for each \(Q^2\). The calculation is performed at three quark masses on lattices of spatial size \(20^3\) and \(28^3\).

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: 606(2) MeV, 502(4) MeV, and 364(1) MeV, corresponding to valence domain wall quark masses \((am_q)^{DW} = 0.0478, 0.0313, 0.0138\) respectively.
- Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.125\) fm; spatial volumes: \(20^3 \times 64\) and \(28^3 \times 64\).
- Number of configurations analyzed: 125 at \(am_{u,d}=0.03\), 75 at \(am_{u,d}=0.02\), and 38 at \(am_{u,d}=0.01\).
- Nucleon and \(\Delta\) masses at the lightest pion mass (364 MeV): \(m_N = 1.196(25)\) GeV, \(m_\Delta = 1.643(63)\) GeV.
- At a similar pion mass of about 0.5 GeV, no unquenching effects are observed in \(G_{M1}\) within statistical errors.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior quenched lattice studies (Alexandrou et al., 2004; 2005) of the \(N \to \Delta\) transition form factors by introducing dynamical sea quarks via the MILC configurations, using a hybrid scheme of staggered sea quarks and domain wall valence fermions previously employed by the LHP collaboration (2005). The results aim to test whether unquenching resolves the discrepancy between quenched lattice values of \(G_{M1}\) and experimental measurements, and to assess the role of pion cloud contributions highlighted by chiral effective-field theory calculations (2005). The preliminary nature of the data calls for more configurations to enable chiral extrapolation and comparison with experiment.
