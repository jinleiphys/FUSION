# Frame Dependence of Parton Cascade Results

> Frame dependence of parton cascade results is studied for different schemes of doing cascade simulations. We show that different schemes do not always agree and results may have strong frame dependence. When the interaction range is on the order of mean free path and the collisions are done in the two particle center of mass frame, the results are not sensitive to the global frame that the collisions are ordered. Effects of different differential cross sections are also discussed.

## Key claim

When the interaction range is on the order of the mean free path and collisions are evaluated in the two-particle center-of-mass frame, parton cascade results for distributions such as dN/dy, dE_T/dy, and dN/dp_T are not sensitive to the global frame (collider lab frame vs. target frame) used to order the collisions; however, this frame independence fails when the mean free path becomes much smaller than the interaction range.

## Method

The authors used the Generic Parton Cascade (GPC) code to simulate a system of 4000 gluons initialized at temperature 500 MeV, uniformly distributed in space-time rapidity from -5 to 5, occupying a transverse disk of radius 5 fm at t=0 with formation proper time tau_0=0.1 fm. They compared four collision schemes: (a) collisions determined by closest approach in the two-particle center-of-mass frame with global frame ordering; (b) same as (a) but with each particle having its own scattering time and ordering by average time; (c) ordering by the earlier of the two scattering times; (d) closest distance evaluated in the global frame with global ordering. The Rutherford cross section regulated by a screening mass was used, with total cross section sigma = 9 pi alpha_S^2 / (2 mu^2) and alpha_S = sqrt(2/9) ≈ 0.47.

## Key numbers

- Initial system: 4000 gluons at temperature 500 MeV, transverse radius 5 fm, formation proper time tau_0 = 0.1 fm, dN/dy = 400, number density n ≈ 25/fm^3
- For scheme (a) with sigma = pi/9 fm^2 (mu = 3 fm^-1, alpha_S = 0.47): dE_T/dy drops from initial ~385 GeV to ~335 GeV; average collisions per event ~6900 in collider frame, ~6800 for scheme (b), ~6200 for scheme (c); target frame gives several hundred fewer
- For scheme (d) with sigma = pi/25 fm^2 ≈ 1 mb (mu = 5 fm^-1): ~22,000 collisions per event in collider frame, ~97,000 in target frame
- For scheme (a) with sigma = pi fm^2 (mu = 1 fm^-1, alpha_S = 0.47): mean free path initially ~0.01 fm; dN/dy shows a dip in the middle for collider frame ordering but not for target frame
- For scheme (b) in collider frame with mu = 3 fm^-1: 6,800 total collisions per event with 900 non-causal; with mu = 1 fm^-1: 21,000 total collisions with 10,400 non-causal
- For scheme (b) in target frame with mu = 3 fm^-1: 6,500 total collisions with 1,000 non-causal
- Particle masses of 0.01 GeV and 0.1 GeV showed no significant changes in distributions or collision numbers compared to massless case

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of causality violation in cascade simulations for energies below RHIC (Peter, year not specified) and studies of superluminous signals in pA collisions (Kortmeyer, year not specified). The authors developed the GPC code (Zhang, year not specified) to systematically compare collision ordering schemes, showing that schemes using the two-particle center-of-mass frame (a, b, c) can produce frame-independent results even when the interaction range is comparable to the mean free path, unlike the global-frame scheme (d). The results suggest that for systems with strong inside-outside correlations, the computationally expensive full ensemble method (Welke, year not specified) may not be necessary if a sensible scattering prescription is used, though further study including radiation processes and quantum statistics is needed for more realistic applications.
