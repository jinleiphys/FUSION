# On the possibility of q-scaling in high energy production processes

> It has been noticed recently that transverse momenta (p_T) distributions observed in high energy production processes exhibit remarkably universal scaling behaviour. This is the case when a suitable variable replaces the usual p_T. On the other hand, it is also widely known that transverse momentum distributions in general follow a power-like Tsallis distribution, rather than an exponential Boltzmann-Gibbs, with a (generally energy dependent) nonextensivity parameter q. Here we show that it is possible to choose a suitable variable such that all the data can be fitted by the same Tsallis distribution (with the same, energy independent value of the q-parameter). Thus they exhibit q-scaling.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that transverse momentum ($p_T$) distributions from high-energy $p+p$ and $p+\bar{p}$ collisions at different center-of-mass energies can be collapsed onto a single universal Tsallis distribution with an energy-independent nonextensivity parameter $q$, provided one uses a suitably redefined scaling variable $u = p_T/(\langle p_T \rangle - b \cdot p_T)$ rather than $p_T$ itself. The scaling is not unique: it can be achieved with $q = 1.172$ using one choice of the energy-dependent coefficient $b(s)$, or with $q \approx 1$ using a different $b(s)$.

## Method

The authors fit Tsallis distributions $h_q(p_T) = C_q [1 - (1-q)p_T/T]^{1/(1-q)}$ to published $p_T$ spectra from NA49 (17.3 GeV), UA1 (200 GeV), and CMS (0.9 and 7 TeV) for $p+p$ and $p+\bar{p}$ collisions, obtaining energy-dependent $q(s)$ and $T(s)$. They then introduce a scaled variable $u = p_T/(\langle p_T \rangle - b \cdot p_T)$ and fit all energies simultaneously with a single Tsallis distribution $h_q(u)$, using an energy-dependent coefficient $b(s)$ to absorb the $s$-dependence. The same procedure is also applied to central $A+A$ collision data from NA49, PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS.

## Key numbers

- For $p+p$ and $p+\bar{p}$ collisions, a universal Tsallis fit in the variable $u$ yields $q = 1.172$ with $C = 79.4$ and $u_0 = 0.17$ (using $b(s) = -0.0397 + 0.08(\sqrt{s})^{-0.075}$).
- An alternative universal fit with $q = 0.955$ is obtained using $b(s) = -0.109 + 0.115(\sqrt{s})^{-0.3}$, with $C = 5$ and $u_0 = 0.6$.
- For central $A+A$ collisions (0-5% centrality), a universal fit gives $q = 0.9999$ with $C = 2800$ and $u_0 = 0.32$, using $b(s) = -0.052 - 0.0002(\sqrt{s})^{0.7}$.
- The energy dependence of the nonextensivity parameter from direct $p_T$ fits is parameterized as $q(s) = 4/3 - 0.4(\sqrt{s})^{-0.075}$.
- The mean transverse momentum follows $\langle p_T(s) \rangle = 0.235 (\sqrt{s})^{0.096}$ (in GeV), and the temperature from Tsallis fits follows $T(s) = 0.141 (\sqrt{s})^{0.021}$ (in GeV).

## Context

This work extends the search for scaling in $p_T$ distributions, previously pursued via geometrical scaling by McLerran and collaborators (2010-2011) using a variable $p'_T$ and a $p_T$-dependent saturation scale, and via KNO-like scaling in the variable $z = p_T/\langle p_T \rangle$. The authors show that Tsallis statistics provides an alternative framework for achieving energy-independent scaling, and they interpret the scaling variable $u$ in terms of an effective temperature $T_{eff} = T_0 + T_v(p_T)$ that can be related to preferential attachment in complex network models of hadronization or to multiplicative noise in a Langevin/Fokker-Planck description. The approach enables a unified description of $p_T$ spectra across a wide range of collision energies and systems ($p+p$, $p+\bar{p}$, and $A+A$) with a single $q$ parameter.
