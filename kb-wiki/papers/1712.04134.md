# Parameter-independent predictions for nuclear shapes and B(E2) transition rates in the proxy-SU(3) model

> Using a new approximate analytic parameter-free proxy-SU(3) scheme, we make predictions of shape observables for actinides and superheavy elements, namely beta and gamma deformation variables, and compare these with predictions by relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field theories. Furthermore, we make predictions for B(E2) transition rates of deformed nuclei and compare these with existing data and predictions of other theoretical approaches.

# Parameter-independent predictions for nuclear shapes and B(E2) transition rates in the proxy-SU(3) model

## Key claim
The proxy-SU(3) model provides parameter-free predictions for nuclear deformation parameters (β and γ) and B(E2) transition rate ratios that agree well with experimental data and with relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field calculations, but only for nuclei that fall within specific proxy-SU(3) shells and for which the relevant SU(3)→SO(3) coupling coefficients can be computed.

## Method
The authors applied the proxy-SU(3) algebraic approach to obtain deformation parameters β and γ for even-even actinides and superheavy elements with Z between 84 and 98 and N between 128 and 220, using the pfh proxy-SU(3) shell for protons (approximating the 82-126 shell) and the sdgi or pfhj proxy-SU(3) shells for neutrons (approximating the 126-184 and 184-258 shells). For B(E2) transition rates, they computed ratios normalized to the B(E2;2_g→0_g) transition using SU(3)→SO(3) coupling coefficients from the SU3CGVCS code, applied to the irreps (54,12) for ¹⁶⁸Er and (52,14) for ¹⁶²Dy and ¹⁶⁶Er.

## Key numbers
- Deformation predictions were obtained for actinides and superheavy elements with Z in the range 84 ≤ Z ≤ 98 and N in the range 128 ≤ N ≤ 220.
- The proxy-SU(3) irreps used for B(E2) calculations were (54,12) for ¹⁶⁸Er and (52,14) for ¹⁶²Dy and ¹⁶⁶Er.
- B(E2) ratios within the ground state band showed excellent agreement with data for ¹⁶²Dy and ¹⁶⁶Er, while for ¹⁶⁸Er three data points were missed.
- B(E2) ratios within the γ₁ band showed clear distinction between ΔL = -2 transitions (increasing with L) and ΔL = -1 transitions (decreasing with L), consistent with data.

## Context
This work extends the proxy-SU(3) model, previously applied to rare earth nuclei by Bonatsos et al. (2017), to actinides and superheavy elements, and provides the first parameter-free B(E2) ratio predictions within this framework. The deformation predictions are compared against five alternative theoretical approaches: the D1S Gogny interaction calculations, relativistic mean-field theory with NL3 parametrization (Lalazissis), the FRDM(2012) mass table (Moller), covariant density functional theory with PC-PK1 and DD-PC1 functionals (Ring), and a microscopic-macroscopic method (Skalski). The B(E2) results are compared to pseudo-SU(3) predictions and experimental data from Warner (for ¹⁶⁸Er), Aprahamian (for ¹⁶²Dy), and ENSDF (for ¹⁶⁶Er). Future work will extend the model to nuclear spectra using three- and four-body terms and to B(M1) transition rates.
