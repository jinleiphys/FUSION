# Superfluid neutron matter in the s-channel exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction models

> 10 pages, REVTeX. Presented as a part of invited talks at Conference on Isospin, Structure, Reactions and Energy of Symmetry (ISTROS), Casta - Papiernicka, Casta, Slovakia, May 14 - 19, 2017 and Workshop on Spectroscopy and Reactions of Exotic Nuclei (Huzhou-CUSTIPEN), Huzhou, China, July 3 - 9, 2017

## Key claim

The Quark Compound Bag (QCB) model, which generates nucleon-nucleon interactions through s-channel exchange of Jaffe-Low primitives (6-quark states), can reproduce the superfluid pairing gap of neutron matter below saturation density consistently with one-boson exchange (OBE) models, but also predicts a second superfluid phase at supranuclear densities where the pairing gap reappears above a Fermi momentum of about 2.0 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Method

The author formulates and solves the self-consistency equations for the neutron pairing gap in neutron matter using the QCB model with parameters fitted to nucleon-nucleon scattering phases in the \(^1S_0\) channel up to 350 MeV and the \(^3S_1\) channel up to 500 MeV in the laboratory frame. The pairing gap is computed from Eliashberg-type equations adapted for separable potentials, where the T-matrix is determined by the compound-state propagator \(1/D(s)\) and the form factor \(\mathcal{F}(s)\). The chemical potential is varied iteratively to solve for the gap, the quasiparticle occupation probabilities, and the neutron self-energy \(\Sigma\) until convergence is achieved.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap vanishes at Fermi momentum \(p_F > 1.6\) fm\(^{-1}\) due to a zero of the form factor \(\mathcal{F}(s)\) at \(p^* = 353\) MeV.
- A new branch of superfluid solutions appears for \(p_F > 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\), with the primitive mass of 2007 MeV corresponding to \(p_F = 2.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The model predicts a dibaryon Bose condensation at a density of 0.55 fm\(^{-3}\), approximately three times the saturation density.
- The region of applicability of the model is limited to Fermi momenta below \(p_F = 2.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the QCB model of nucleon-nucleon scattering, previously applied to light nuclei and scattering phases by Simonov (various years) and collaborators, to the study of neutron matter superfluidity for the first time. The results below saturation density are compared with predictions from advanced OBE models including Paris, Argonne V\(_{14}\), Argonne V\(_{18}\), CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, and Nijmegen II as reported in a 2005 compilation. The calculation enables future studies of the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter, the role of multiple primitives in each channel, and the dynamical treatment of primitives that may convert to resonances and induce dibaryon condensation.
