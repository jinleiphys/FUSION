# Photodisintegration of the deuteron in the few GeV region using asymptotic amplitudes

> Exclusive photodisintegration of the deuteron in the 1-4GeV range is described in terms of a simple covariant and gauge invariant approach using an effective counting rule as the hard part of the d-np vertex. At a scattering angle of theta_cm=90 degrees a prescaling behavior of the differential cross section proportional to 1/s^(n-2) with n ~ 12 is obtained; going away from 90 degrees the value of n decreases slowly, in qualitative agreement with the recent data.

## Key claim

The observed prescaling behavior of the deuteron photodisintegration cross section at 90 degrees (proportional to 1/s^(n-2) with n ~ 12) can be reproduced by a hadronic model with a hard component in the deuteron-nucleon vertex, without invoking perturbative QCD; the effective exponent n decreases away from 90 degrees, in qualitative agreement with data that deviate from the simple counting rule prediction of n=13.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant and gauge-invariant Born amplitude for gamma + d -> n + p using pole diagrams in the s, t, and u channels, with a contact term added to enforce current conservation. The deuteron-nucleon vertex is parametrized with a hard component using an effective counting rule form factor G(p^2) = C/(Lambda^2/2 + m^2 - p^2)^g, with the normalization parameter C fixed by fitting data at E_gamma = 1 GeV. The cross section is computed in both instant-form (covariant) and light-front kinematics, with the light-front approach using a factorized form factor that depends on both the squared momentum and the longitudinal momentum fraction alpha.

## Key numbers

- At theta_cm = 90 degrees, the differential cross section exhibits prescaling behavior proportional to 1/s^(n-2) with n ~ 12 (n-2 = 10) for g=3 in the 1-4 GeV region
- Previous SLAC data up to E_gamma = 3 GeV at theta = 90 degrees indicated scaling consistent with n = 13.1 +/- 0.3
- Jefferson Lab data up to 4 GeV confirmed scaling with n ~ 13 at theta = 90 degrees and 69 degrees, but at theta = 36 degrees and 52 degrees the best fit yielded n ~ 11.5 and 11.6 respectively
- In the present model, n-2 decreases from approximately 10 at 90 degrees to approximately 8 at 10 degrees (or 170 degrees) in the 1-4 GeV energy region
- The cross section is normalized by fitting the data at E_gamma = 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous hadronic approaches to deuteron photodisintegration, which fail above E_gamma = 0.5 GeV (Lee, Arenhovel, Laget), and contrasts with the reduced nuclear amplitude (RNA) approach of Brodsky and Hiller (1992) and the quark-gluon string model of Kondratyuk (1993). The RNA approach, normalized at 1 GeV, falls below data at 90 degrees for E_gamma > 3 GeV, while the QGS model describes data only at small angles. The present model uses a light-front formalism to achieve a flatter angular distribution than the instant-form approach, and the authors suggest that semi-exclusive reactions like d(e,e'p)X at large Q^2 could discriminate between models for the deuteron vertex at high virtualities.
