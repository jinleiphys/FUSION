# Sub-barrier fusion of intermediate and heavy nuclear systems

> A potential model to describe the total cross section of nuclear fusion reactions at low energies is proposed. It is shown that within an approach with a simple, single barrier potential a satisfactory description of data is hindered, while with models with more complex interactions, e.g., with two-barrier potentials, a good description of data can be achieved in a large interval of colliding nuclei and center of mass energies. In particular, the two-barrier model allows to describe data at low, sub-barrier energies where data exhibits a steep falloff of the cross section. It is also shown that the position of the two barriers is almost independent up on masses of colliding nuclei. Comparison with available experimental data is presented as well.

## Key claim

A two-barrier potential model can describe the steep falloff ("hindrance") of fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies, but only when interference effects between the two barriers are accurately included; without such interference, the model cannot reproduce the observed slope change at extremely low energies.

## Method

The author solves a decoupled Schrödinger equation in a semiclassical (WKB) approximation for a two-barrier ion-ion potential, where the outer barrier is a Woods-Saxon Coulomb barrier and the inner barrier arises from repulsion at the critical distance where nuclei lose their individual structure. The total penetrability is computed both with and without averaging over the phase between the two barriers (equations a11 and a17 respectively), and the fusion cross section is obtained by multiplying the penetrability by a deexcitation factor that separates evaporation residue from fission. The free parameters of the inner potential are fitted to experimental data for five reaction systems: 64Ni+64Ni, 60Ni+89Y, 90Zr+92Zr, 28Si+64Ni, and 16O+208Pb.

## Key numbers

- For all five reactions studied, the distance between the two barriers (R_B minus R_C) is approximately constant at 4.5 fm, roughly twice the diffuse edge of nuclei.
- The inner barrier becomes larger than the Coulomb barrier when the parameter eta_cr exceeds 71.3.
- For 64Ni+64Ni: Coulomb barrier V_B = 93.55 MeV at R_B = 11.28 fm (r_0^B = 1.41 fm) with curvature hbar omega_B = 3.57 MeV; inner barrier V_C = 85.11 MeV at R_C = 6.68 fm (r_0^C = 0.84 fm) with curvature hbar omega_C = 12.17 MeV.
- For 16O+208Pb: Coulomb barrier V_B = 74.47 MeV at R_B = 11.98 fm (r_0^B = 1.42 fm) with curvature hbar omega_B = 4.76 MeV; inner barrier V_C = 61.86 MeV at R_C = 7.44 fm (r_0^C = 0.88 fm) with curvature hbar omega_C = 14.83 MeV.
- At the threshold energy E = Q_R, the phase nu in equation (a14) becomes approximately pi/2, indicating the decaying system was in its first quasi-stationary state.

## Context

This work extends the "critical distance model" of Galin, Guerreau, Lefort, and Tarrago (1974) and Glas and Mosel (1974) by introducing a two-barrier potential to explain the hindrance effect observed in sub-barrier fusion measurements by Jiang, Rehm, Janssens, et al. (2004) and others. The results agree with earlier analyses by Tarakanov, Shilov, and Schmidt (1991) for systems with eta > eta_cr (such as 96Zr+124Sn, 86Kr+123Sb, and 50Ti+208Pb). The scaling-like behavior of the inner potential relative to the Coulomb barrier position enables predictions for other colliding nuclei and for heavy cluster decay processes.
