# Few-Body Effects in Cold Atoms and Limit Cycles

> Physical systems with a large scattering length have universal properties independent of the details of the interaction at short distances. Such systems can be realized in experiments with cold atoms close to a Feshbach resonance. They also occur in many other areas of physics such as nuclear and particle physics. The universal properties include a geometric spectrum of three-body bound states (so-called Efimov states) and log-periodic dependence of low-energy observables on the physical parameters of the system. This behavior is characteristic of a renormalization group limit cycle. We discuss universality in the three- and four-body sectors and give an overview of applications in cold atoms.

## Key claim

The universal properties of few-body systems with large scattering length, including the Efimov effect and log-periodic dependence of observables, are manifestations of a renormalization group limit cycle in the three-body system, but the experimental evidence from cold cesium atoms is indirect and the universal expressions are rigorously valid only when the scattering length is much larger than the van der Waals scale.

## Method

The author uses an Effective Field Theory (EFT) for nonrelativistic bosons with large S-wave scattering length, where the Lagrangian includes two-body and three-body contact interactions. The atom-dimer scattering amplitude is obtained by solving an integral equation that sums one-atom exchange and three-body interaction diagrams to all orders, with renormalization requiring a cutoff-dependent three-body coupling that runs on a limit cycle. The EFT is then applied to calculate three-body recombination coefficients for cold cesium atoms near a Feshbach resonance, fitting the universal expressions to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The preferred scaling factor for the Efimov geometric spectrum is lambda^2 approximately 515, corresponding to lambda = exp(pi/s_0) approximately 22.7 where s_0 = 1.00624.
- The three-body parameter Lambda_* from the EFT is related to the Efimov binding wavenumber kappa_* by Lambda_* = 2.62 kappa_*.
- For negative scattering length in Cs-133, the fit to experimental data gives kappa_* = 0.945/a_0 and eta_* = 0.06, with the Efimov resonance observed at a = -850(20) a_0.
- For positive scattering length in Cs-133, the fit gives kappa_* = 0.707/a_0 and eta_* = 0.06, with a local minimum in loss rate at a = 210(10) a_0.
- The van der Waals scale for Cs atoms is l_vdW approximately 200 a_0.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Efimov (1971) on three-body bound states with large scattering length and the EFT approach of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999). It directly interprets the experimental results of the Innsbruck group (Grimm, 2006) on Cs-133 three-body recombination, which provided the first indirect evidence for Efimov states in cold atoms. The framework enables future extensions to four-body systems (Platter, 2004; Yamashita, 2006) and finite temperature calculations (Jonsell, 2006; Yamashita, 2006; and others).
