# On the impact of large amplitude pairing fluctuations on nuclear spectra

> The influence of large amplitude pairing fluctuations is investigated in the framework of beyond mean field symmetry conserving configuration mixing calculations. In the numerical application the finite range density dependent Gogny force is used. We investigate the nucleus $^{54}$Cr with particle number and angular momentum projected wave functions considering the axial quadrupole deformation and the pairing gap degree of freedom as generator coordinates. We find that the effects of the pairing fluctuations increase with the excitation energy and the angular momentum. The self-consistency in the determination of the basis states plays an important role.

# On the impact of large amplitude pairing fluctuations on nuclear spectra

## Key claim

The authors show that including the pairing gap as a generator coordinate alongside quadrupole deformation in symmetry-conserving configuration mixing (SCCM) calculations compresses the overly stretched spectra obtained from one-dimensional (deformation-only) calculations, with the effect increasing for higher excitation energies and angular momenta; however, the magnitude of this compression depends critically on whether particle number projection is performed and on the self-consistency of the basis state determination.

## Method

The authors perform Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) calculations for the nucleus ⁵⁴Cr using the finite-range density-dependent Gogny force (D1S parametrization). They generate intrinsic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) or variation-after-particle-number-projection (PN-VAP) wave functions constrained on both the axial quadrupole moment q and the square root of the mean square deviation of particle number δ = ⟨(ΔN̂)²⟩¹/², then apply angular momentum projection (AMP) and/or particle number projection (PNP) before solving the Hill-Wheeler equation. Three approaches are compared: HFB+AMP (no PNP), HFB+PNAMP, and PN-VAP+PNAMP, each in one dimension (q only) and two dimensions (q and δ).

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy in plain HFB: -470.096 MeV; after PN-VAP: -473.066 MeV (2.97 MeV lowering); after PN-VAP+PNAMP with AMP to I=0⁺: -475.860 MeV; after SCCM with q only (1D): -476.636 MeV; after SCCM with q and δ (2D): -476.865 MeV (0.23 MeV additional lowering from pairing fluctuations)
- For the 0⁺₁ state in PN-VAP+PNAMP, the 2D SCCM energy lowering relative to HFB+AMP is 1.0 MeV; for HFB+PNAMP it is 1.27 MeV; for HFB+AMP it is 1.65 MeV
- The q grid spans -220 fm² to +400 fm² with Δq = 20 fm²; the δ grid spans 0 to 4.5 with step 0.5
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment of 2⁺₁ state in PN-VAP+PNAMP-2D: -29.556 e²fm² (prolate); 2⁺₂: +15.299 e²fm² (oblate); 2⁺₃: -42.722 e²fm² (prolate)
- Proton pairing energy of 0⁺₁ state in PN-VAP+PNAMP-2D: -4.9 MeV; neutron pairing energy: -5.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field calculations that previously included only the quadrupole deformation parameter β as a generator coordinate (Bender 2006, Rodriguez 2002, Niksic 2006, Rodriguez 2007) by adding the pairing degree of freedom explicitly with a finite-range force for the first time. Prior treatments of pairing vibrations used schematic interactions in collective Hamiltonian approaches (Bes 1965, Pomorski 1985), microscopic models (Matsuyanagi 2003, 2005), reduced configuration spaces (Faessler 1973), or earlier beyond-mean-field methods (Meyer, Heenen). The results enable future studies disentangling genuine pairing vibrations from quadrupole-pairing coupling and highlight the necessity of particle number projection and self-consistent basis generation for reliable spectra.
