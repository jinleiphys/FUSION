# Pion spectra and HBT radii at RHIC and LHC

> We describe RHIC pion data in central A+A collisions and make predictions for LHC based on hydro-kinetic model, describing continuous 4D particle emission, and initial conditions taken from Color Glass Condensate (CGC) model.

# Pion spectra and HBT radii at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim
The hydro-kinetic model (HKM), which accounts for continuous 4D particle emission and its back reaction on fluid dynamics, can describe RHIC pion data and predict LHC pion observables, but only when using a crossover equation of state (EoS) with moderate initial transverse flow; a first-order phase transition EoS would require unrealistically high initial transverse flows to fit the same RHIC data.

## Method
The authors solved ideal hydrodynamics with a realistic crossover EoS adjusted to QCD lattice data, then computed non-local equilibrium distribution functions and emission functions in a first approximation of the hydro-kinetic approach. Initial conditions at proper time τ₀ = 1 fm/c were taken from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) model, assuming boost-invariance, cylindrical symmetry, a Gaussian transverse energy density profile, and a transverse velocity profile parameterized by α = 0.2. The calculation was iterated once to account for conservation laws during particle emission, and resonance contributions to pions were not included.

## Key numbers
- Maximal initial energy density at RHIC: ε₀ = 30 GeV/fm³ at τ₀ = 1 fm/c.
- Maximal initial energy density at LHC: ε₀ = 70 GeV/fm³ at τ₀ = 1 fm/c.
- Transverse Gaussian radius for RHIC top energy: R_T = 4.3 fm (assumed same for LHC).
- Transverse velocity profile parameter: α = 0.2 for both RHIC and LHC.
- For a first-order phase transition EoS, fitting RHIC HBT data required α = 0.3, described as "non-realistic high initial transverse flows."
- Calculated one-particle spectra were enhanced by a factor of 1.4 for comparison with PHENIX data.

## Context
This work extends the hydro-kinetic approach developed by Sinyukov, Akkelin, and collaborators (AkkSin1, AkkSin2) to describe continuous particle emission from a hydrodynamically expanding fireball, going beyond sharp freeze-out prescriptions. It uses the ideal fluid model of Hirano and the crossover EoS of Laine adjusted to QCD lattice data. The results enable predictions for LHC Pb+Pb central collisions and show that the HBT radii increase only modestly from RHIC to LHC, in contrast to what would be expected from a first-order phase transition scenario.
