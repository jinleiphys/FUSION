# Reduced cross sections of electron and neutrino charged current quasielastic scattering on nuclei

> The semi-exclusive averaged reduced cross sections for (anti)neutrino charged current quasi-elastic scattering on carbon, oxygen, and argon are analyzed within the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation. We found that these cross sections as functions of missing nucleon energy are similar to those of electron scattering and are in agreement with electron scattering data for three nuclei. The difference between the electron and neutrino cross sections can be attributed to Coulomb distortion on the electron wave function. The averaged reduced cross sections depend slowly upon incoming lepton energy. The approach presented in this paper provide novel constraints on nuclear models of quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering and can be easily applied to test spectral functions and final state interactions, employed in neutrino event generators.

## Key claim

The semi-exclusive averaged reduced cross sections for (anti)neutrino charged-current quasielastic scattering on carbon, oxygen, and argon, calculated within the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA), are similar to those from electron scattering and agree with electron scattering data for all three nuclei, with the difference between electron and neutrino cross sections attributed to Coulomb distortion of the electron wave function; however, the averaged reduced cross sections depend slowly upon incoming lepton energy, and the agreement is qualified by the specific kinematics and shell-model occupancy factors used.

## Method

The author calculated semi-exclusive averaged reduced cross sections for (anti)neutrino charged-current quasielastic scattering on carbon-12, oxygen-16, and argon-40 within the relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA), using the independent particle shell model (IPSM) with relativistic bound-state functions from a mean-field approach and distorted outgoing nucleon wave functions from a Dirac equation with a phenomenological relativistic optical potential (EDAD1 parameterization). The reduced cross sections were averaged over phase space in missing momentum, missing energy, and lepton kinematics, and compared directly to measured (e,e'p) reduced cross sections from Saclay, SLAC, NIKHEF, and JLab experiments. The nuclear current employed the de Forest prescription for off-shell nucleons, with vector form factors from electron scattering data and axial form factors in the dipole approximation with axial mass M_A.

## Key numbers

- For carbon-12, the normalization factor for the 1p_{3/2} shell is S(1p_{3/2}) = 84%, and for the 1s_{1/2} shell S(1s_{1/2}) = 100%, with an average factor of about 89%, from RDWIA analysis of JLab data (Dutta, Kelly1).
- For oxygen-16, the normalization factors are S(1p_{3/2}) = 66%, S(1p_{1/2}) = 70%, and S(1s_{1/2}) = 100%, from RDWIA analysis of JLab data (Fissum).
- For argon-40, the occupancy of the orbitals is approximately 87% on average, from RDWIA analysis of NIKHEF data (Kramer1, Kramer2, Kramer3).
- The difference between electron and (anti)neutrino reduced cross sections calculated for Saclay kinematics is less than 10%.
- The JLab data for carbon-12 were taken at beam energy E_beam = 2.455 GeV, proton kinetic energy T_p = 350 MeV, and Q^2 = 0.64 (GeV/c)^2.
- The Saclay data for carbon-12 used beam energy E_beam = 500 MeV; SLAC data used E_beam = 2015 MeV; JLab data used E_beam = 2455 MeV.
- For oxygen-16, the calculated cross sections at maximum are systematically higher by about 15-20% than measured ones for NIKHEF data.
- The reduced cross sections measured for argon-40 covered missing momentum range 15 ≤ p_m ≤ 300 MeV/c and missing energy range 12 ≤ E_m ≤ 80 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the RDWIA approach previously applied to electron scattering (Pick, Udias, JKelly) and to neutrino scattering on carbon, oxygen, calcium, and argon (Butkevich 2015, 2017, 2019) by directly comparing averaged reduced cross sections for (anti)neutrino CCQE scattering with measured (e,e'p) reduced cross sections from multiple experiments (Tokyo, Saclay, NIKHEF, SLAC, JLab). The results enable testing of spectral functions and final-state interaction models employed in neutrino event generators (such as those used by T2K, NOvA, DUNE, and SBN collaborations) by providing a direct comparison between the distorted spectral function from electron scattering and the corresponding neutrino scattering predictions, leveraging the similarity of nuclear effects between electron and neutrino interactions.
