# Dissipative hydrodynamic evolution of hot quark matter at finite baryon density

> High-energy heavy ion collider experiments at RHIC and LHC have revealed that relativistic hydrodynamic models describe the hot and dense quark matter quantitatively. In this study, I develop a novel dissipative hydrodynamic model at finite baryon density to investigate the net baryon rapidity distribution. The results show that the distribution is widened in hydrodynamic evolution, which implies that the transparency of the collisions is effectively enhanced. This suggests that the kinetic energy loss for medium production at the initial stage could be larger. Furthermore, the net baryon distribution is found sensitive to baryon diffusion, implying that dissipative hydrodynamic modeling would be important for understanding the hot medium.

# Dissipative hydrodynamic evolution of hot quark matter at finite baryon density

## Key claim
The net baryon rapidity distribution is widened by hydrodynamic evolution, which effectively enhances the transparency of collisions and implies that more kinetic energy could be available for hot medium production than previously deduced from experimental data alone. The effect is sensitive to baryon diffusion, but the magnitude of this sensitivity depends on transport coefficients that are not well known.

## Method
A relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic model based on the Israel-Stewart formulation extended for systems with particle number changing processes was developed at finite baryon density. The transverse dynamics was integrated out, and the equations of state were constructed from lattice QCD calculations using Taylor expansion. Initial conditions for energy and net baryon density were estimated from the color glass condensate framework, and the hydrodynamic equations were solved for Au-Au collisions at RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC.

## Key numbers
- Mean rapidity loss for the initial conditions: 2.67 at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV) and 3.36 at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV)
- After ideal, viscous, and dissipative hydrodynamic evolution, mean rapidity loss reduced to 2.09, 2.16, and 2.26 at RHIC, respectively
- After ideal, viscous, and dissipative hydrodynamic evolution, mean rapidity loss reduced to 2.82, 2.86, and 2.92 at LHC, respectively
- Freeze-out temperature set to T_f = 0.16 GeV
- Initial time tau_0 chosen as 1 fm/c
- Shear viscosity set to eta = s/(4 pi) following Kovtun (2004)
- Bulk viscosity set to zeta = 5 * [(1/3) - c_s^2] * eta following Hosoya (1983) and Monnai (2012)

## Context
This work extends prior hydrodynamic analyses that neglected net baryon number (Schenke 2010) by incorporating finite baryon density into a causal dissipative framework. It builds on the Israel-Stewart formulation (Israel 1979) and earlier work on baryon stopping at AGS (E917 Collaboration 2000), SPS (NA49 Collaboration 1998), and RHIC (BRAHMS Collaboration 2003). The results enable more accurate modeling of the QCD phase diagram at finite chemical potential, relevant for beam energy scans at RHIC, FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC.
