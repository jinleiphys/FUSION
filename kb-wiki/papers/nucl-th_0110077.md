# New Class of Compact Stars at High Density

> We discuss the equation of state for cold, dense quark matter in perturbation theory, and how it might match onto that of hadronic matter. Certain choices of the renormalization scale correspond to a strongly first order chiral transition, and may generate a new class of small and very dense quark stars. The results for the mass-radius relation are compatible with the recent determination of the mass and the radius of an isolated neutron star by Pons et al.

## Key claim

Certain choices of the renormalization scale (specifically \(\bar\Lambda/\mu\) in the range of 2 to 3) in a perturbative QCD equation of state for cold, dense quark matter can produce a strongly first order chiral transition, which may generate a new class of small and very dense quark stars with masses around \(1 M_\odot\) and radii around 6 km.

## Method

The authors computed the thermodynamic potential for cold, dense quark matter with three massless flavors using perturbative QCD to order \(\sim \alpha_s^2\) in the \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) scheme, with the running coupling taken to three-loop order. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations using this equation of state, matching it onto hadronic matter either smoothly (weakly first order transition) or abruptly (strongly first order transition), and compared the resulting mass-radius relations to astrophysical data.

## Key numbers

- For \(\bar\Lambda = 2\mu\), the perturbative corrections to the ideal gas pressure are \(\sim 30\%\) at \(\mu = 1\) GeV (where \(\alpha_s = 0.31\)) and \(7\%\) at \(\mu = 100\) GeV (where \(\alpha_s = 0.095\)).
- For a strongly first order chiral transition (e.g., \(\bar\Lambda = 2\mu\)), the new branch of quark stars has maximum masses \(M \sim 1 M_\odot\) and radii \(R \sim 6\) km.
- For a weakly first order transition (e.g., \(\bar\Lambda = 3\mu\)), hybrid stars have maximum masses \(M \sim 2 M_\odot\) and radii \(R \sim 12\) km.
- The hadronic pressure at nuclear matter density is \(0.04\) times the pressure of an ideal gas of three-flavor massless quarks at \(\mu = 313\) MeV.
- The energy per baryon (minus rest mass) in pure neutron matter is approximately \(15\) MeV times the baryon density relative to nuclear saturation density \(n_0 \sim 0.16\) baryons/fm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends perturbative QCD calculations by Freedman and McLerran (1977) and Baluni (1978) to the astrophysical context of quark stars, and contrasts with the traditional bag model approach used for hybrid or strange stars. The results are compatible with the mass-radius determination for an isolated neutron star by Pons et al. (2001), and the new branch of small, dense stars was previously suggested by several groups (e.g., Glendenning and others in the 1990s). The paper enables future work on the electromagnetic signatures of color-flavor locked quark stars and raises the open question of why the hadronic pressure remains small up to the chiral transition.
