# ArgoLOOM: agentic AI for fundamental physics from quarks to cosmos

> Progress in modern physics has been supported by a steadily expanding corpus of numerical analyses and computational frameworks, which in turn form the basis for precision calculations and baseline predictions in experimental programs. These tools play a central role in navigating a complex landscape of theoretical models and current and potential observables to identify and understand fundamental interactions in physics. In addition, efforts to search for new fundamental interactions increasingly have a cross-disciplinary nature, such that understanding and leveraging interoperabilities among computational tools may be a significant enhancement. This work presents a new agentic AI framework, which we call ArgoLOOM, designed to bridge methodologies and computational analyses across cosmology, collider physics, and nuclear science. We describe the system contours, key internal aspects, and outline its potential for unifying scientific discovery pipelines. In the process, we demonstrate the use of ArgoLOOM on two small-scale problems to illustrate its conceptual foundations and potential for extensibility into a steadily growing agentic framework for fundamental physics.

## Key claim
ArgoLOOM is a proof-of-concept agentic AI framework that can orchestrate cross-domain computational physics workflows across cosmology, collider physics, and nuclear science, but its demonstrated capabilities are currently limited to two small-scale illustrative problems using a minimal set of computational modules and a curated knowledge base of only about 15 papers.

## Method
The authors built a single-agent system using the OpenAI API (defaulting to GPT-4o) as a backbone orchestrator. This agent interfaces with three domain-specific computational modules: the CLASS Boltzmann solver for cosmology, the MadGraph5 event generator for collider physics, and a set of kinematic matching and detector-smearing routines for nuclear physics. The agent also consults a curated knowledge base of approximately 5 arXiv papers per domain, accessed via retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) using FAISS indexing, to inform its reasoning and guide downstream calculations.

## Key numbers
- The curated knowledge base consists of approximately 5 arXiv papers in each of the three domains (collider phenomenology, cosmology, and nuclear physics/QCD), for a total of roughly 15 papers.
- The default backbone model used is GPT-4o, with GPT-4o-mini and GPT-5 also tested as alternatives.
- The collider case study uses a center-of-mass energy of 13 TeV for LHC proton-proton collisions.
- The kinematic matching for $pp \to Z$ production at Born-level uses the relation $x_{1,2} = (M_Z / \sqrt{s}) \exp(\pm y)$ with $Q = M_Z$.

## Context
This work extends the recent emergence of agentic AI systems for scientific tasks, such as the DR MACS system for cosmology (DRMACS, 2025) and other agents for biology, chemistry, and gamma-ray astronomy. It specifically targets the challenge of multi-domain modeling in fundamental physics, where computational tools for cosmology, collider physics, and nuclear science are typically used in isolation. The framework is designed to be extensible, with the potential to incorporate additional modeling tools (e.g., ExoCLASS, CLASSgal, CAMB) and reasoning capabilities in future work.
