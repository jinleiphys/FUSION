# Structure of 8B from elastic and inelastic 7Be+p scattering

> Motivation: Detailed experimental knowledge of the level structure of light weakly bound nuclei is necessary to guide the development of new theoretical approaches that combine nuclear structure with reaction dynamics. Purpose: The resonant structure of 8B is studied in this work. Method: Excitation functions for elastic and inelastic 7Be+p scattering were measured using a 7Be rare isotope beam. Excitation energies ranging between 1.6 and 3.4 MeV were investigated. An R-matrix analysis of the excitation functions was performed. Results: New low-lying resonances at 1.9, 2.5, and 3.3 MeV in 8B are reported with spin-parity assignment 0+, 2+, and 1+, respectively. Comparison to the Time Dependent Continuum Shell (TDCSM) model and ab initio no-core shell model/resonating-group method (NCSM/RGM) calculations is performed. This work is a more detailed analysis of the data first published as a Rapid Communication. [J.P. Mitchell, et al, Phys. Rev. C 82, 011601(R) (2010)] Conclusions: Identification of the 0+, 2+, 1+ states that were predicted by some models at relatively low energy but never observed experimentally is an important step toward understanding the structure of 8B. Their identification was aided by having both elastic and inelastic scattering data. Direct comparison of the cross sections and phase shifts predicted by the TDCSM and ab initio No Core Shell Model coupled with the resonating group method is of particular interest and provides a good test for these theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of three low-lying resonances in 8B at 1.9, 2.5, and 3.3 MeV with spin-parity assignments 0+, 2+, and 1+, respectively, which were predicted by some models but never experimentally observed; the identification was only possible because both elastic and inelastic scattering data were measured simultaneously, allowing separation of the two channels.

## Method

Excitation functions for elastic 7Be(p,p)7Be and inelastic 7Be(p,p')7Be(1/2−; 0.43 MeV) scattering were measured using a radioactive 7Be beam at three beam energies (27.2, 22.0, and 18.5 MeV) incident on solid polyethylene (C2H4) targets of optimized thickness. Kinematic coincidence between protons detected in an array of three annular silicon strip detectors and 7Be recoils detected in a downstream S2 detector was used to distinguish elastic from inelastic events. A multi-channel multi-level R-matrix analysis of the excitation functions was performed to extract resonance parameters.

## Key numbers

* New 0+ state at excitation energy 1.9 ± 0.1 MeV with total width 0.53 (+0.6, −0.1) MeV, inelastic partial width 0.47 (+0.4, −0.1) MeV
* New 2+ state at excitation energy 2.50 ± 0.04 MeV with total width 0.27 ± 0.04 MeV, inelastic partial width 0.22 MeV
* New 1+ state at excitation energy 3.3 ± 0.2 MeV with total width 3.2 ± 0.9 MeV, inelastic partial width 0.4 MeV
* Previously known 1+ state at 0.768 ± 0.004 MeV with total width 0.027 ± 0.006 MeV
* Previously known 3+ state at 2.31 ± 0.02 MeV with total width 0.33 ± 0.03 MeV
* Best fit normalized χ² = 0.89 for the combined R-matrix analysis including low-energy data from Angulo (2003)

## Context

This work extends the earlier rapid communication by Mitchell et al. (2010) with a more detailed analysis and extends the measured excitation energy region to higher energies. The results provide direct experimental tests for the Time Dependent Continuum Shell Model (TDCSM) and the ab initio no-core shell model/resonating-group method (NCSM/RGM) calculations, which had predicted these low-lying positive-parity states in 8B. The new states are compared to the mirror nucleus 8Li, where previous work by Knox (1987) had suggested a 0+ state at 3.02 MeV, and the present analysis shows that the new states in 8B must be shifted up by approximately 800 keV in 8Li to be consistent with existing neutron scattering data.
