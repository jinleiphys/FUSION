# Anomalous Internal Pair Creation

> In recent electron-positron angular correlation measurements the observed significant enhancements relative to the internal pair creation at large angles was interpreted as indication of the creation of $J^{π}=1^{+}$ boson called X17 particle. In this paper it is brought up that such enhancements can be generated by higher order processes. It is found that nuclear transitions, the transition energy of which is significantly lower than the whole transition energy, can cause peaked angle dependence in electron-positron angular correlation.

## Key claim

The observed enhancements in electron-positron angular correlations, previously interpreted as evidence for a hypothetical X17 boson, can instead be explained by higher-order nuclear processes in which strong and electromagnetic interactions are coupled, specifically when nuclear transitions have an energy significantly lower than the total transition energy.

## Method

The authors performed standard perturbation calculations to third and fourth order, deriving T-matrix elements for processes where proton capture (strong interaction) and internal pair creation (electromagnetic interaction) occur as a single joined process rather than two successive steps. They analyzed the angular dependence of the electron-positron pair creation yield by examining the denominator structure of the electromagnetic matrix element, showing that when the nuclear transition wavenumber K approaches zero or is smaller than the total transition wavenumber, the angular dependence can produce peaks at large angles. The formalism was applied to the specific cases of the 7Li(p,e-e+)8Be reaction populating 17.640 MeV and 18.15 MeV states of 8Be, and the 3H(p,e-e+)4He reaction populating the 21.01 MeV state of 4He.

## Key numbers

- For 8Be: the 18.15 MeV (1+) state has width Gamma_r2 = 168 keV; the 17.640 MeV (1+) state has width Gamma_r1 = 12.2 keV; resonant proton beam energies are 441 keV (epsilon_01 = 450 keV, D1 = 9 keV) and 1030 keV (epsilon_02 = 1100 keV, D2 = 70 keV)
- For 8Be: transition energies hbar*c*K_21 = 5.276 MeV and hbar*c*K_31 = 5.572 MeV yield peak angles Theta_2,m = 146.2 degrees and Theta_3,m = 144.2 degrees respectively (with k- = k+); the half-width of state 1 (Gamma_1 approx 3.5 MeV) gives peak spread of about +/- 12 degrees
- For 8Be: the 17.640 MeV state may have upward M1 coupling to the 27.4941 MeV (0+) state with transition energy hbar*c*K_40 = 9.854 MeV giving Theta_4,m = 114.1 degrees
- For 4He: the 21.01 MeV (0-) state has center-of-mass width Gamma = 0.84 MeV; the 20.21 MeV (0+) state has width Gamma_1 = 0.5 MeV
- For 4He: transition energies hbar*c*K_21 = 7.21 MeV, hbar*c*K_31 = 8.46 MeV, hbar*c*K_41 = 9.68 MeV yield peak angles Theta_2,m = 138.7 +/- 26 degrees, Theta_3,m = 131.2 +/- 12 degrees, Theta_4,m = 123.5 +/- 32 degrees respectively

## Context

This paper directly challenges the interpretation of anomalous electron-positron angular correlation measurements by Krasznahorkay et al. (2016, 2019) that were attributed to a 16.7 +/- 0.35 MeV X17 boson. The authors argue that previous analyses neglected higher-order processes coupling strong and electromagnetic interactions, and demonstrate that such processes can produce peaked angular distributions without invoking new particles. The work extends the standard theory of internal pair creation from Rose (1949) and Greiner (1985) by considering off-resonant nuclear transitions and interference effects between resonant and non-resonant terms. If correct, this explanation would eliminate the need for the X17 particle hypothesis and redirect attention to conventional nuclear reaction mechanisms in the 8Be and 4He systems.
