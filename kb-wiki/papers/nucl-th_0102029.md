# Propagation of mesons in asymmetric nuclear matter in a density dependent coupling model

> We study the propagation of the light mesons sigma, omega, rho, and a0(980) in dense hadronic matter in an extended derivative scalar coupling model. Within the scheme proposed it is possible to unambiguously define effective density-dependent couplings at the Lagrangian level. We first apply the model to study asymmetric nuclear matter with fixed isospin asymmetry, and then we pay particular attention to hypermatter in beta-equilibrium. The equation of state and the potential contribution to the symmetry coefficient arising from the mean field approximation are investigated.

## Key claim
The paper proposes an extension of the derivative scalar coupling model (DSCM) that unambiguously defines effective density-dependent couplings at the Lagrangian level, but the model is non-renormalizable and the linearized expansion used for quantum corrections is valid only in the regime where quantum fluctuations are negligible compared to mean field values.

## Method
The authors extended the DSCM of Zimanyi and Moszkowski to include isovector mesons (rho and a0(980)) and applied it to asymmetric nuclear matter and hypermatter in beta-equilibrium. They solved the mean field equations self-consistently to obtain the equation of state and symmetry energy, and evaluated meson propagators in the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) including particle-antiparticle contributions to extract effective meson masses.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: n0 = 0.15 fm^-3
- Binding energy at saturation: epsilon_B = -15 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_s = 32 MeV
- Coupling constants for set A: g_s = 12.379, g_w = 14.624, g_r = 11.583, g_d = 0
- Coupling constants for set B: g_s = 12.379, g_w = 14.624, g_r = 15, g_d = 6.538
- Isothermal compressibility at saturation: kappa_T = 165 MeV (compared to 220 MeV for standard DSCM)
- Meson masses used: m_s = 550 MeV, m_d = 984 MeV, m_w = 783 MeV, m_r = 770 MeV
- At n/n0 = 2, the sigma coupling drops by 60% and the delta, omega, rho couplings drop by 40% in the model, compared to 20% for isoscalar mesons and 42% for rho in DDHFT
- Lambda binding energy at n0: epsilon_Lambda = -28 MeV, giving g_s_Lambda = 2.335, g_w_Lambda = 2.099
- Sigma and Xi binding energies at n0: epsilon_Sigma = -28.11 MeV, epsilon_Xi = -28.27 MeV
- At n = 5 n0 with set B, the mass splitting Delta M* reduces by about 5 MeV when temperature increases from 0 to 100 MeV, and increases by approximately 50 MeV when asymmetry varies from chi=0 to chi=1
- Sigma- particles appear at n approximately 3.3 n0 with set A

## Context
This work extends the derivative scalar coupling model of Zimanyi and Moszkowski to include isovector mesons and hyperons, building on prior QHD approaches by Walecka and density-dependent hadron field theory by Lenske and collaborators. The results are compared with QHD-I model predictions and variational calculations by Wiringa, and the model enables studies of neutron star composition, cooling rates, and heavy-ion collision dynamics with radioactive beams. The paper notes that the behavior of the symmetry energy depends on the evaluation method and interaction model, and that the inclusion of the delta coupling (set B) slightly increases the proton fraction in star matter compared to set A.
