# Effect of symmetry energy on properties of rapidly rotating neutron stars and universal relations

> We investigated universal relations for compact stars rotating at the Keplerian (mass-shedding) limit, which is highly relevant for understanding the rapidly rotating objects formed in the aftermath of a neutron star-neutron star merger. Our analysis is based on a set of nucleonic equations of state (EoSs) featuring systematic variations in the symmetry energy slope parameter $L_{\rm sym}$ and the isoscalar skewness parameter $Q_{\rm sat}$, varied within ranges that are broadly consistent with current laboratory and astrophysical constraints. The global observable properties of isolated maximally rotating stars are examined, focusing on the mass-radius relation, moment of inertia, quadrupole moment, and the Keplerian (maximum) rotation frequency, as well as their variations in the $L_{\rm sym}$-$Q_{\rm sat}$ parameter space. Next, we demonstrate that, in the limit of Keplerian rotation, universal relations remain valid across the same set of EoSs characterized by varying $L_{\rm sym}$ and $\Qsat$. In particular, we present explicit results for the moment of inertia ($I$) and quadrupole moment ($Q$) as functions of compactness, as well as for the moment of inertia-quadrupole moment relation. All of these relations exhibit excellent universality, with deviations typically within a range from a few percent to 10\% across a wide range of parameters. Additionally, we verify for our set of EoSs that the universality of $I$-$Q$ holds to higher accuracy (at the level of 1\%) in the slow-rotation approximation compared with the Kepler limit, where the relative error increases up to $\lesssim 10\%$. Our findings support the applicability of $I$-Love-$Q$-type universal relations in observational modeling of maximally rotating compact stars and the gravitational wave emitted by them.

# Effect of symmetry energy on properties of rapidly rotating neutron stars and universal relations

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that I-Love-Q-type universal relations remain valid for neutron stars rotating at the Keplerian (mass-shedding) limit across a set of nucleonic equations of state with systematic variations in the symmetry energy slope parameter L_sym and the isoscalar skewness parameter Q_sat, with deviations typically within a few percent to 10%. However, the universality of the I-Q relation holds to higher accuracy (at the level of 1%) in the slow-rotation approximation compared with the Kepler limit, where the relative error increases up to approximately 10%.

## Method

The authors constructed 81 nucleonic equations of state based on covariant density functionals (DDME2 parametrization) by varying the symmetry energy slope L_sym from 30 to 110 MeV in steps of 10 MeV and the isoscalar skewness Q_sat from -600 to 1000 MeV in steps of 200 MeV, with fixed saturation properties (E_sat = -16.14 MeV, K_sat = 251.15 MeV, E_sym = 32.31 MeV, rho_sat = 0.152 fm^-3). They solved for equilibrium configurations of rigidly rotating neutron stars at the Keplerian (mass-shedding) limit using the public domain RNS code, computing mass, equatorial radius, moment of inertia, quadrupole moment, and Keplerian frequency for each equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy slope L_sym was varied across the range 30 to 110 MeV, and the isoscalar skewness Q_sat across -600 to 1000 MeV, with step sizes of Delta L_sym = 10 MeV and Delta Q_sat = 200 MeV, producing 81 distinct equations of state.
- For Q_sat = -600 MeV, the maximum TOV mass reaches approximately 2.0 solar masses, consistent with PSR J0740+6620; for Q_sat >= 600 MeV, the maximum mass is >= 2.5 solar masses, matching the lighter companion of GW190814 at 2.59 (+0.08, -0.09) solar masses.
- The Keplerian frequency for a canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star is typically 1200-1500 Hz, compared with the fastest known pulsar J1748-2446ad at 716 Hz.
- The fit for the normalized moment of inertia I^<(C) = I/(M R^2) used a polynomial plus exponential form (Eq. 11) with 7 parameters, yielding sigma = 6.55706 x 10^-3 and reduced chi-squared = 4.2995 x 10^-5.

## Context

This work extends the covariant density functional equations of state developed by Li and Sedrakian (2023), who introduced systematic variations in L_sym and Q_sat for nonrotating neutron stars, to the regime of maximally rotating configurations at the Keplerian limit. The universal relations examined here build on the I-Love-Q framework established by Yagi and Yagi (2013a, 2013b) for slowly rotating stars, and on prior studies of rapidly rotating configurations by Doneva (2013), Breu (2016), and others. The results support the applicability of universal relations for interpreting gravitational wave data from binary neutron star mergers, particularly for supramassive remnants that may form after events such as GW170817, where Rezzolla (2018) used such relations to place upper limits on the maximum static neutron star mass.
