# Gluon saturation effects at forward rapidities at LHC in pp collisions

> We investigate hadrons and direct photon production in pp collisions at the LHC energy within the color-dipole approach. We show that greatest sensitivity to gluon saturation effects is reached at very forward rapidities in pp collisions at LHC (\sqrt{s}=14 TeV). The discrepancies among various saturation models (fitted to HERA data) results can be about a factor of 2-3 at forward rapidities. We found that the ratio of direct-photon to pion production can be about 20-10 at forward rapidities η=7-8. Therefore, direct photon production at forward rapidities should provide a rather clean probe as the background from radiative hadronic decays is significantly suppressed.

# Gluon saturation effects at forward rapidities at LHC in pp collisions

## Key claim
The greatest sensitivity to gluon saturation effects in pp collisions at LHC energy (sqrt(s)=14 TeV) is reached at very forward rapidities, but the discrepancies among various saturation models fitted to HERA data can be about a factor of 2-3 at forward rapidities, indicating that model-dependent uncertainties remain significant.

## Method
The author used the light-cone color-dipole factorization scheme to calculate invariant cross-sections for hadron (pion) and direct photon production in pp collisions at LHC energy. Several color-dipole models (CGC, GBW, KLR-AdS/CFT, AAMS-BK1, and a Semi-Sat model) were employed, all fitted to HERA data, to compute differential cross-sections as functions of rapidity and transverse momentum. The ratio of direct-photon to pion production was also evaluated to assess the cleanliness of the direct photon probe.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of direct-photon to pion production can be about 20-10 at forward rapidities eta=7-8 at LHC energy sqrt(s)=14 TeV.
- Discrepancies among various saturation model results can be about a factor of 2-3 at forward rapidities.
- The Semi-Sat model fit to HERA data achieved chi^2/d.o.f.=0.92.
- The preferred value of anomalous dimension 1-gamma_eff=0.57 at very forward rapidities is close to the value predicted from the BK equation.
- Saturation effects become essential when the anomalous dimension reaches gamma_cr=1-gamma_eff=0.37, which occurs at forward rapidities.

## Context
This work extends the color-dipole approach previously applied at RHIC energies (Iancu 2003) to LHC energies, testing multiple saturation models including those from Gribov (1984), the GBW model, and the KLR-AdS/CFT model. The finding that direct photon production at forward rapidities provides a clean probe with suppressed background from radiative hadronic decays enables future LHC measurements to test saturation physics more definitively, addressing the ongoing debate about whether saturation effects are essential or whether alternative approaches can describe the same data.
