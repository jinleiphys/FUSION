# Massive neutron star with strangeness in a relativistic mean-field model with a high-density cut-off

> The properties of strangeness neutron star are studied within relativistic mean-field (RMF) model via including a logarithmic interaction as a function of scalar meson field. This logarithmic interaction, named as the $σ$-cut potential, can largely reduce the attractive contributions of scalar meson field at high density without any influence on nuclear structure around normal saturation density. In this work, the TM1 parameter set is chosen as the RMF interaction, while the strengths of logarithmic interaction are constrained by the properties of finite nuclei so that we can obtain a reasonable effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The hyperons, $Λ,~Σ$, and $Ξ$ are also considered in neutron stars within this framework, whose coupling constants with mesons are determined by the latest hyperon-nucleon and $Λ$-$Λ$ potentials extracted from the experimental data of hypernuclei. The maximum mass of neutron star can be larger than two solar mass with these hyperons. Furthermore, the nucleon mass at high density will be saturated due to this additional $σ$-cut potential, which is consistent with the conclusions from the microscopic calculations such as, Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and quark mean-field model.

## Key claim
The authors show that adding a logarithmic "sigma-cut" potential to the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the TM1 parameter set can produce neutron stars with hyperons that have maximum masses above 2 solar masses, but only if the free parameter f_s is chosen between 0.55 and 0.60; outside this range the model either fails to reproduce finite-nuclei properties or cannot reach the observed 2-solar-mass threshold.

## Method
The authors extended the RMF Lagrangian with a logarithmic sigma-cut potential U_cut(sigma) = alpha ln{1+exp[beta(g_sigma sigma/M_N - f_s)]}, with fixed alpha = m_pi^4 and beta = 120. They used the TM1 parameter set for the nucleon-nucleon interaction and determined the hyperon-meson coupling constants from hypernuclei data and SU(6) symmetry. They solved the mean-field equations for finite nuclei (16O, 90Zr, 208Pb) and for neutron-star matter under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, then integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of neutron star with hyperons and sigma-cut (f_s=0.55): 2.14 solar masses, with corresponding radius 14.09 km and central density 0.62 fm^-3.
- Maximum mass of neutron star with hyperons and sigma-cut (f_s=0.60): 2.02 solar masses, with corresponding radius 13.70 km and central density 0.63 fm^-3.
- Maximum mass of neutron star with TM1 and hyperons but no sigma-cut: 1.68 solar masses.
- Maximum mass of neutron star with TM1 without hyperons: 2.18 solar masses.
- Radius of 1.4-solar-mass neutron star with sigma-cut (f_s=0.55): 14.50 km; with f_s=0.60: 14.30 km.
- Binding energy of 208Pb with TM1: -1637.8920 MeV; with sigma-cut and f_s=0.55 or 0.60: identical value (-1637.8920 MeV).
- Onset density for Lambda hyperons in TM1: 0.32 fm^-3.

## Context
This work extends the sigma-cut approach of Maslov et al. (2015) and Dutra et al. (2016) from nuclear matter to finite nuclei and neutron stars with strangeness. It addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where standard RMF models with hyperons produce maximum neutron-star masses below 2 solar masses, contradicting observations of PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al., 2010; Fonseca et al., 2016) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013). The resulting saturated nucleon effective mass at high density is consistent with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and the quark mean-field model. The work enables future studies of hyperonic neutron stars with a mechanism that stiffens the equation of state at high density without affecting low-density nuclear properties.
