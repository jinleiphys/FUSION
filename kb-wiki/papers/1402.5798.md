# On the sensitivity of jet quenching to near T_C enhancement of the medium opacity

> One of the main goals of the study of high transverse momentum (P_T) observables in the context of ultrarelativisic heavy-ion collisions is the determination of properties of the produced QCD matter. In particular, the transport coefficients qhat and ehat, characterizing the interaction of the medium with a high p_T parton, are accessible via high P_T probes. However, a precision extraction of their temperature dependence from current data faces the problem that neither the spacetime geometry of the evolving matter droplet nor the link between thermodynamics and transport coefficients is unambiguously known, and various conjectured scenarios how thermodynamics and transport coefficients behave close to the phase transition exist. While often a behaviour with the energy density qhat ~ epsilon^3/4 is assumed which leads to a parametric decrease of the scaled qhat(T)/T^3 close to the critical temperature T_C, other scenarios expect instead a near T_C enhancement of jet quenching. In this work, the systematic response of both the extraction of qhat and v_2 at high P_T to modification of jet quenching close to T_C is systematically investigated within YaJEM, a well-tested in-medium shower evolution Monte-Carlo (MC) code.

## Key claim

The extraction of the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}(T)/T^3\) from central-collision \(R_{AA}\) data is not substantially influenced by assumptions about near-\(T_C\) enhancement of jet quenching, but the high-\(P_T\) elliptic flow \(v_2\) is sensitive to such enhancement, with a maximum relative increase of 35% over the default scenario.

## Method

The author uses the YaJEM-DE in-medium shower evolution Monte Carlo code, coupled to a 2+1D fluid-dynamical simulation of the bulk matter for 2.76 ATeV PbPb collisions, to compute the single inclusive hadron suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) and its angular modulation. The near-\(T_C\) enhancement is parametrized as a Gaussian bump on top of the default \(\hat{q} \sim T^3\) scaling, with width \(\sigma = 10\) or 30 MeV and strength parameter \(c\) from 0 to 3. The overall normalization \(K\) is fitted to the angular-averaged \(R_{AA}\) at \(P_T = 10\) GeV in 0–10% central collisions, and the same \(\hat{q}(T)\) is then used to compute \(v_2\) in 30–40% centrality.

## Key numbers

- \(\hat{q}(T)/T^3 \sim 2.4\) to 2.5 GeV for all near-\(T_C\) scenarios when the decoupling temperature is \(T_F = 0.13\) GeV; this rises to \(\sim 3.8\) to 4.1 GeV if \(T_F = 0.16\) GeV.
- The maximum relative enhancement of \(v_2\) at \(P_T = 10\) GeV in 30–40% central collisions is 35% over the default \(\hat{q} \sim \epsilon^{3/4}\) scenario.
- The Gaussian width parameters tested are \(\sigma = 10\) MeV and \(\sigma = 30\) MeV; the strength parameter \(c\) is varied from 0 to 3.

## Context

This work extends the systematic investigation of jet quenching model uncertainties by Renk (2013) and addresses the scenario of near-\(T_C\) enhancement proposed by Liao and collaborators (2010, 2011). It shows that while the high-temperature value of \(\hat{q}\) is robust against near-\(T_C\) assumptions, the high-\(P_T\) \(v_2\) is sensitive to such enhancement at the 35% level, comparable to other systematic effects such as viscous entropy production. The results enable future multi-observable fits to determine whether near-\(T_C\) enhancement is required by the data.
