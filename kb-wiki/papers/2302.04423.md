# $0νββ$-decay nuclear matrix elements in self-consistent Skyrme quasiparticle random phase approximation: uncertainty from pairing interaction

> The uncertainty in the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of $0νββ$ decay for $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{128}$Te, $^{130}$Te, and $^{136}$Xe in the self-consistent quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) method is investigated by using eighteen Skyrme interactions supplemented with either a volume- or surface-type of pairing interactions. The NMEs for the isotopes concerned (except $^{136}$Xe) are less sensitive to the particle-hole ($ph$) interactions, while strongly dependent on the employed isovector particle-particle ($pp$) pairing interactions even though the pairing strengths are optimized to the same pairing gap. The results indicate that a precise determination of the isovector $pp$ pairing interaction in the Skyrme energy density functional is of importance to reduce the uncertainty in the NMEs within the QRPA framework.

## Key claim

The nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double-beta decay in the self-consistent Skyrme quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) are largely insensitive to the choice of particle-hole (ph) Skyrme interaction, but they depend strongly on the form of the isovector particle-particle (pp) pairing interaction (volume-type versus surface-type), even when the pairing strengths are adjusted to reproduce the same experimental pairing gaps.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and QRPA calculations using 18 different Skyrme interactions for the ph channel, combined with either a volume-type or surface-type delta pairing interaction for the pp channel. The isoscalar proton-neutron pairing strength was fixed by reproducing the experimental 2νββ Gamow-Teller matrix elements, while the isovector pairing strength was determined by requiring the Fermi matrix element for 2νββ decay to vanish due to isospin symmetry. The NMEs for 0νββ decay were computed for five candidate nuclei (76Ge, 82Se, 128Te, 130Te, and 136Xe) including intermediate states up to J^P = 10^±.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge with 18 Skyrme interactions: mean NME = 5.65 with standard deviation 0.45 for volume pairing; mean NME = 8.40 with standard deviation 0.66 for surface pairing.
- For 136Xe with 18 Skyrme interactions: mean NME = 1.72 with standard deviation 0.11 for volume pairing; mean NME = 1.35 with standard deviation 0.15 for surface pairing.
- The standard deviations of NMEs across different Skyrme ph interactions are only about 10% of the mean values for both nuclei.
- The overlap factor between HFB ground states of mother and daughter nuclei is around 0.82 for all considered nuclei except 136Xe, where it is about 0.45 for volume pairing and 0.25 for surface pairing.
- The discrepancy between NMEs obtained with different ph interactions is less than 15% for all five nuclei studied when using the same type of pairing.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent Skyrme QRPA studies of 0νββ decay (Terasaki 2012, 2015; Mustonen 2013) by systematically quantifying the uncertainty from the pairing interaction across a large set of Skyrme functionals. It contrasts with earlier G-matrix-based QRPA studies (Rodin 2003) which found NMEs independent of the residual interaction but used a non-self-consistent Woods-Saxon potential for the ground state. The results indicate that reducing the uncertainty from isovector pairing interactions is critical for improving NME predictions in the QRPA framework, and that the correlation between double Gamow-Teller matrix elements and 0νββ NMEs found in some other models (Simkovic 2018; Shimizu 2018) is not present in the self-consistent QRPA approach.
