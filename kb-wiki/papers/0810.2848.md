# Microscopic calculation of the wobbling excitations by using the Woods-Saxon potential as a nuclear mean-field

> The wobbling excitations of the triaxial superdeformed (TSD) bands in the Lu and Hf region are studied by the microscopic framework of the cranked mean-field and the random-phase approximation (RPA). In contrast to the previous works, where the Nilsson potential was used, the more realistic Woods-Saxon potential is employed as a nuclear mean-field. The wobbling-like RPA solutions have been found systematically in the nuclei studied and their characteristic properties are investigated in details. This confirms the wobbling phonon excitations in TSD nuclei from the microscopic calculations. The result of $B(E2)$ values indicates that the triaxial deformation is increasing as a function of spin in the observed TSD bands in $^{163}$Lu.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that wobbling-like RPA solutions exist systematically in triaxial superdeformed (TSD) nuclei of the Lu and Hf region when using the more realistic Woods-Saxon potential as the nuclear mean-field, whereas previous calculations using the Nilsson potential overestimated the moment of inertia by about 30% and required a correction that, when applied, eliminated the wobbling RPA solutions entirely.

## Method

The authors performed cranked mean-field calculations using a triaxially deformed Woods-Saxon potential (with central, spin-orbit, and Coulomb terms) plus a monopole pairing residual interaction, then solved the random-phase approximation (RPA) equation in the uniformly rotating frame to extract wobbling phonon eigenmodes. The residual interaction was constructed from the commutators of the mean-field Hamiltonian with the angular momentum operators \(J_y\) and \(J_z\), with strengths determined by the decoupling condition of the Nambu-Goldstone modes. The RPA eigenfrequencies were obtained via a dispersion matrix technique, and the out-of-band \(B(E2)\) transition probabilities were calculated from the RPA matrix elements of the quadrupole operators.

## Key numbers

- The moment of inertia was overestimated by about 30% in previous calculations using the Nilsson potential (Sec. I, paragraph 8).
- The out-of-band to in-band \(B(E2)\) ratio from previous Nilsson-potential RPA calculations was systematically smaller by about a factor of two than experimental data (Sec. I, paragraph 7).
- The rotational \(E2\) transition probabilities inside the TSD bands are typically about 500 Weisskopf units, and those between the wobbling phonon bands are about 100 Weisskopf units (Sec. I, paragraph 1).
- The triaxiality parameter \(\gamma\) in the Woods-Saxon potential (\(\gamma(\text{pot:WS})\)) differs considerably from the one defined by quadrupole moments (\(\gamma(\text{den})\)); for example, \(\gamma(\text{den}) = 20^\circ\) corresponds to \(\gamma(\text{pot:WS}) \approx 30^\circ\) for large deformation such as the TSD bands (Sec. II.A, final paragraph).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic RPA wobbling calculations previously performed with the Nilsson potential by Matsuzaki et al. (1990s) and Shimizu et al. (2000s) to the more realistic Woods-Saxon potential, which avoids the velocity-dependent \(\boldsymbol{l}^2\) term that caused a 30% overestimate of the moment of inertia. The authors note that the correction method of Kinouchi (1980s) and Nazarewicz et al. (1996) to restore local Galilean invariance in the Nilsson potential, when applied, eliminated the wobbling RPA solutions entirely because only the cranking-axis component was corrected. The present results confirm the wobbling phonon interpretation of the TSD bands observed in Lu isotopes (e.g., \(^{163}\)Lu) and enable future microscopic studies of the triaxial deformation evolution with spin, as the \(B(E2)\) values indicate that triaxial deformation increases as a function of spin in \(^{163}\)Lu.
