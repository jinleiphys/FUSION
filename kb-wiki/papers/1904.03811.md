# First microscopic coupled-channel calculation of $α$ inelastic cross sections on $^{16}$O

> The $α$ inelastic scattering on $^{16}$O is investigated with the coupled-channel calculation using the $α$-nucleus coupled-channel potentials, which are microscopically derived by folding the the Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction with the $^{16}$O and $α$ densities. The matter and transition densities of $^{16}$O are calculated by a microscopic structure model of the variation after the spin-parity projections combined with the generator coordinate method of $^{12}$C+$α$ in the framework of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The calculation reproduces the observed elastic and inelastic cross sections at incident energies of $E_α$=104 MeV, 130 MeV, 146 MeV, and 386 MeV. The coupled-channel effect on the cross sections is also discussed.

## Key claim

The first microscopic coupled-channel (CC) calculation of α inelastic scattering on ¹⁶O, using α-nucleus CC potentials derived from the Melbourne g-matrix NN interaction and transition densities from an antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) structure model, reproduces observed elastic and inelastic cross sections at four incident energies without an overshooting problem for the 0⁺ cross sections, but the scaling law between α-scattering cross sections and B(E0) is not necessarily valid for cluster states due to significant CC effects.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channel calculations for ¹⁶O(α,α') using α-nucleus CC potentials constructed by folding the Melbourne g-matrix NN interaction with the α density (one-range Gaussian) and the matter and transition densities of ¹⁶O. The ¹⁶O densities were obtained from a microscopic structure calculation using variation after spin-parity projections combined with the generator coordinate method of ¹²C+α in the AMD framework (VAP+GCM). The calculated transition densities were scaled by factors f_tr = sqrt(B_exp(Eλ)/B_cal(Eλ)) to match experimental transition strengths where available, and the CC calculation included states up to λ ≤ 3 transitions (0⁺₁,₂,₃,₄,₅, 2⁺₁,₂,₃,₄, 1⁻₁,₂, and 3⁻₁,₂) with experimental excitation energies.

## Key numbers

- Incident energies studied: E_α = 104 MeV, 130 MeV, 146 MeV, and 386 MeV (with 400 MeV data also used for comparison)
- The VAP+GCM calculation gives the ground state rms matter radius R = 2.73 fm (experimental charge radius gives R = 2.55 fm)
- For the 0⁺₂ state, the calculated excitation energy is 9.7 MeV (experimental: 6.0494 MeV) and R = 3.29 fm
- For the 0⁺₄ state, the calculated excitation energy is 13.6 MeV (experimental: 13.6 MeV) and R = 3.64 fm
- The B(E0:0⁺₂→0⁺₁) from VAP+GCM is 12.0 e²fm⁴ (experimental: 12.6 e²fm⁴), with scaling factor f_tr = 1.03
- The B(E0:0⁺₃→0⁺₁) from VAP+GCM is 16.7 e²fm⁴ (experimental: 16.2 e²fm⁴), with scaling factor f_tr = 0.99
- The B(E3:3⁻₁→0⁺₁) from VAP+GCM is 207 e²fm⁶ (experimental: 205 e²fm⁶), with scaling factor f_tr = 0.99
- At E_α = 104-146 MeV, the CC effect reduces the peak amplitude of the 0⁺₂ cross sections by about a factor of three from the DWBA result; at E_α = 386 MeV, the reduction is about a factor of two

## Context

This work extends the g-matrix folding model approach previously applied to ¹²C(α,α') by Minomo and Ogata (2016) and Kanada-En'yo (2018) to the ¹⁶O(α,α') reaction for the first time. It addresses the overshooting problem of 0⁺ cross sections reported in earlier reaction model analyses of ¹⁶O(α,α') data by Adachi (2018) and others, and shows that microscopic CC calculation resolves this issue. The approach enables quantitative analysis of cluster structures in ¹⁶O, particularly the ¹²C+α cluster bands and the 4α-gas state, and provides a framework for extracting cluster information from (α,α') reactions on other nuclei.
