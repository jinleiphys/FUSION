# Hyperon and charmed baryon masses and nucleon excited states from lattice QCD

> We discuss the status of current dynamical lattice QCD simulations in connection to the emerging results on the strange and charmed baryon spectrum, excited states of the nucleon and the investigation of the structure of scalar mesons.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD simulations with physical light quark masses now reproduce the experimentally measured masses of low-lying octet and decuplet baryons and charmed baryons without requiring chiral extrapolation, but the first excited positive-parity nucleon state (the Roper) remains higher than the experimental value even at near-physical pion mass, possibly due to volume effects.

## Method

The authors compute baryon masses from Euclidean two-point correlation functions in the large Euclidean time limit, using twisted mass fermion (TMF) gauge configurations with dynamical quarks. For the nucleon excited states, they construct a correlation matrix from multiple interpolating fields with different Gaussian smearings and solve a generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) to extract the lowest eigenstates in both positive and negative parity channels.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings for the \(N_f=2+1+1\) TMF ensembles: \(a=0.094\) fm, \(0.082\) fm, and \(0.065\) fm
- Pion mass range for the \(N_f=2\) TMF ensembles used in the nucleon excited state study: approximately 270 MeV to 450 MeV, with lattice spacing \(a=0.089\) fm
- Preliminary prediction for the yet unmeasured \(\Xi_{cc}^*\) mass: 3.678(8) GeV
- Preliminary prediction for the yet unmeasured \(\Omega^+_{cc}\) mass: 3.708(10) GeV
- Preliminary prediction for the yet unmeasured \(\Omega^{*+}_{cc}\) mass: 3.767(11) GeV
- Preliminary prediction for the yet unmeasured \(\Omega^{++}_{ccc}\) mass: 4.746(3) GeV
- Lattice spacing for the \(N_f=2\) Clover ensemble with \(m_\pi\sim 160\) MeV: \(a\simeq 0.073\) fm
- Lattice size for the PACS-CS clover configurations used in the scalar meson study: \(32^3\times64\) with \(a \approx 0.09\) fm, analyzed at \(m_\pi \approx 300\) MeV and \(m_\pi \approx 150\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD baryon spectrum calculations by the BMW collaboration (Durr 2008), PACS-CS collaboration (Aoki 2008), and QCDSF-UKQCD collaborations (Bietenholz 2011) to include results at physical quark masses using twisted mass fermions, eliminating the need for chiral extrapolation. The nucleon excited state results are compared with those from the CSSM collaboration (Mahbub 2012) and the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Edwards 2011), and the ongoing scalar meson study aims to resolve the structure of the \(a_0(980)\) by testing the four-quark interpretation against conventional quark model predictions.
