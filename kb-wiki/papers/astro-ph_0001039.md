# Antikaon condensation in neutron stars

> We investigate the condensation of charged $K^-$ meson and neutral $\bar K^0$ meson in dense neutron star matter. Calculations are performed in relativistic mean field models in which both the baryon-baryon and (anti)kaon-baryon interactions are mediated by meson exchange. It is found that $\bar K^0$ condensation is quite sensitive to the antikaon optical potential and depends more strongly on the nucleonic equation of state. For moderate values of antikaon potential and a rather stiff equation of state, a significant region of maximum mass star will contain $\bar K^0$ meson. The critical density of $\bar K^0$ condensation is always higher than that of $K^-$ condensation. With the appearance of $K^-$ and $\bar K^0$ condensates, pairs of $p-K^-$ and $n-\bar K^0$ are produced with equal proportion leading to a perfectly symmetric matter of nucleons and antikaons in neutron stars. Along with $K^-$ condensate, $\bar K^0$ condensate makes the equation of state much softer resulting in smaller maximum mass stars compared to the case without any condensate.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutral $\bar K^0$ meson condensation can occur in neutron star matter alongside charged $K^-$ condensation, but only for moderate values of the antikaon optical potential and a rather stiff nucleonic equation of state; the critical density for $\bar K^0$ condensation is always higher than that for $K^-$ condensation.

## Method

The authors use relativistic mean field (RMF) models where baryon-baryon and (anti)kaon-baryon interactions are mediated by $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ meson exchange. They solve the meson field equations in the mean field approximation for neutron star matter containing nucleons, leptons, and $s$-wave antikaon condensates, imposing chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions. The calculations are performed for three parameter sets (GM1, TM1, and GMT) with different nuclear equation of state stiffness and for antikaon optical potential depths ranging from $-100$ MeV to $-180$ MeV at normal nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for $K^-$ condensation: $3.05 n_0$ for $U_{\bar K}(n_0) = -120$ MeV with the GM1 parameter set.
- Critical density for $\bar K^0$ condensation: $4.83 n_0$ for $U_{\bar K}(n_0) = -120$ MeV with the GM1 parameter set.
- At densities above $7.32 n_0$, antikaon densities become equal, producing perfectly symmetric matter of nucleons and antikaons with strangeness fraction $f_S = |S|/B = (n_{K^-} + n_{\bar K^0})/n_B$ as large as unity.
- For $U_{\bar K}(n_0) \geq -140$ MeV, the threshold densities for $\bar K^0$ condensate occur before the central densities for maximum mass stars in the GM1 set.
- The $\rho$-meson field shifts the energy of $\bar K^0$ meson below that of the $K^-$ meson by approximately $120$ MeV.
- For the TM1 set, only for $\bar K$ depth as large as $-160$ MeV can a $\bar K^0$ condensate form in the limiting mass star.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $K^-$ condensation in neutron stars by Kaplan and Nelson (1986), Brown (1992), Thorsson et al. (1994), and Ellis et al. (1995) to include neutral $\bar K^0$ condensation, which had not been calculated before. The authors use the RMF approach of Glendenning and Moszkowski (1991) for the GM1 parameter set and the TM1 set of Sugahara and Toki (1994), and they compare with the variational chain summation results of Akmal et al. (1998) who predicted neutral pion condensation. The results enable future studies of the impact of both charged and neutral antikaon condensates on neutron star structure and maximum masses, and highlight the sensitivity of $\bar K^0$ condensation to the high-density equation of state.
