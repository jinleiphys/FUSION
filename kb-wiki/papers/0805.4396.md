# Eikonal analysis of Coulomb distortion in quasi-elastic electron scattering

> An eikonal expansion is used to provide systematic corrections to the eikonal approximation through order $1/k^2$, where $k$ is the wave number. Electron wave functions are obtained for the Dirac equation with a Coulomb potential. They are used to investigate distorted-wave matrix elements for quasi-elastic electron scattering from a nucleus. A form of effective-momentum approximation is obtained using trajectory-dependent eikonal phases and focusing factors. Fixing the Coulomb distortion effects at the center of the nucleus, the often-used ema approximation is recovered. Comparisons of these approximations are made with full calculations using the electron eikonal wave functions. The ema results are found to agree well with the full calculations.

## Key claim

The eikonal expansion provides systematic corrections to the eikonal approximation through order \(1/k^2\), and the often-used effective-momentum approximation (ema) is recovered when Coulomb distortion effects are fixed at the center of the nucleus; however, the ema results agree well with full calculations only when a reduced momentum shift \(\delta k = f_{ema} V_c(0)\) with \(f_{ema} \approx 0.7\) is used, rather than the full central potential value.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for electron wave functions in a Coulomb potential using an eikonal expansion that includes trajectory-dependent phases and focusing factors up to second order in \(1/k\). They computed the longitudinal response function for quasi-elastic electron scattering from a nucleus using a simple Gaussian model for the nuclear current, and compared the full distorted-wave calculations with two effective-momentum approximations: the standard ema (with phases and focusing factors fixed at the nuclear center) and an improved EMAr approximation (retaining the full spatial dependence of the eikonal phase and focusing factors).

## Key numbers

- For a 500 MeV electron scattering at \(\theta_e = 60^\circ\) from a Gaussian nuclear model (with \(V_0 = 0.0273\) GeV, \(R = 2.0\) fm), the ema with \(f_{ema} = 0.7\) reproduces the full calculation of the longitudinal response function well.
- For positron scattering at 540 MeV and \(\theta_e = 60^\circ\) using the same Gaussian model, the ema with \(f_{ema} = 0.7\) also agrees well with the full calculation.
- The eikonal expansion converges rapidly: results using \(\chi_0 + \chi_1\) and \(\omega_1\) differ from the full second-order calculation by about 0.3% at the quasi-elastic peak for the energies and parameters used.
- The ratio \(V_c(0)/E \approx 0.05\) for a 500 MeV electron, leading to an expected ratio \(\chi_2/\chi_0 \approx 0.0025\) for the second-order correction.
- The focusing factor at the nuclear center is approximately \(1 - V_c(0)/E_i\) for the initial state and \(1 - V_c(0)/E_f\) for the final state, yielding an overall focusing factor of \((1 - V_c(0)/E_f)(1 - V_c(0)/E_i)\).

## Context

This work extends the systematic eikonal expansion for Dirac wave functions developed by Tjon and Wallace (2006) to the analysis of Coulomb distortion in quasi-elastic electron scattering. It provides a theoretical basis for the effective-momentum approximation (ema) previously used by Traini (1988, 2001) and others, and clarifies the role of focusing factors derived from WKB analyses by Yennie, Boos, and Ravenhall (1965). The results support the use of ema with a reduced momentum shift (\(f_{ema} \approx 0.7\)) as a practical tool for analyzing experimental data from Bates, Saclay, and SLAC, and address discrepancies in nuclear response function extraction noted by Morgenstern (2001) and Benhar (2006).
