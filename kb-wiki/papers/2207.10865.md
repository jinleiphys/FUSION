# Study of elastic and inelastic scattering of $^7$Be + $^{12}$C at 35 MeV

> The elastic and inelastic scattering of $^7$Be from $^{12}$C have been measured at an incident energy of 35 MeV. The inelastic scattering leading to the 4.439 MeV excited state of $^{12}$C has been measured for the first time. The experimental data cover an angular range of $θ_{cm}$ = 15$^{\circ}$-120$^{\circ}$. Optical model analyses were carried out with Woods-Saxon and double-folding potential using the density dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction. The microscopic analysis of the elastic data indicates breakup channel coupling effect. A coupled-channel analysis of the inelastic scattering, based on collective form factors, show that mutual excitation of both $^7$Be and $^{12}$C is significantly smaller than the single excitation of $^{12}$C. The larger deformation length obtained from the DWBA analysis could be explained by including the excitation of $^7$Be in a coupled-channel analysis. The breakup cross section of $^7$Be is estimated to be less than 10$\%$ of the reaction cross section. The intrinsic deformation length obtained for the $^{12}$C$^*$ (4.439 MeV) state is $δ_2$ = 1.37 fm. The total reaction cross section deduced from the analysis agrees very well with Wong's calculations for similar weakly bound light nuclei on $^{12}$C target.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement of inelastic scattering of ⁷Be from ¹²C to the 4.439 MeV excited state of ¹²C, but the data are quasi-elastic because the bound excited state of ⁷Be at 0.429 MeV could not be separated from the ground state.

## Method

Elastic and inelastic scattering angular distributions of ⁷Be on ¹²C were measured at 35 MeV incident energy over θ_cm = 15°–120° using silicon strip detectors at the CERN HIE-ISOLDE facility. Optical model analyses were performed with Woods-Saxon potentials and double-folding potentials using the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction, with coupled-channel calculations including the ⁷Be(1/2⁻) bound excited state, the ⁷Be(7/2⁻) resonance at 4.57 MeV, and the ¹²C(2⁺) state at 4.439 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 35 MeV for ⁷Be beam on ¹²C target
- Angular range covered: θ_cm = 15°–120°
- Intrinsic deformation length for ¹²C*(4.439 MeV) state: δ₂ = 1.37 fm (from coupled-channel analysis)
- Deformation parameter β₂ = 0.49 for the ¹²C 2⁺ state (with variation of 0.05)
- Renormalization factors from microscopic fit: N_R = 0.68, N_I = 0.30
- Renormalization factors from coupled-channel fit: N_R = 1.00, N_I = 0.20
- Total reaction cross section from CC calculations: 1503 mb
- Reduced total reaction cross section: 85 mb
- Breakup cross section of ⁷Be estimated as less than 10% of the reaction cross section
- Breakup and inelastic cross section to ⁷Be bound excited state: 9.12% and 0.67% of reaction cross section respectively (from preliminary CDCC calculations)
- Deformation length from DWBA analysis: δ₂ = 1.83 fm

## Context

This work extends the earlier ⁷Be + ¹²C measurement at 34 MeV by Amro et al. (2007), which had limited angular range and large beam energy spread, by providing higher-resolution elastic data over a wider angular range and the first inelastic scattering data to the 4.439 MeV state of ¹²C. The extracted optical model parameters and deformation lengths enable future analyses of α-transfer reactions relevant to nuclear astrophysics, particularly for studying high-excitation α-clustering states in residual nuclei in the context of helium-burning processes.
