# Lattice studies of magnetic phenomena in heavy-ion collisions

> We review some experimental consequences of the presence of superstrong magnetic fields of order of the nuclear scale in noncentral heavy-ion collisions. We present lattice estimates for the strength of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) for different quark flavours and argue that the dependence of the anisotropy of the distribution of emitted hadrons on their flavor content might be used as another experimental evidence of the CME. Another possible effect of superstrong magnetic field might be the observed abnormal enhancement of dilepton yield. We show that the presence of the magnetic field leads to a specific anisotropy of the dilepton emission rate.

## Key claim

Lattice estimates of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) show that the observable asymmetry in charged hadron emission should decrease strongly with increasing quark mass, providing a way to discriminate the CME from other effects that also produce charge-dependent azimuthal correlations; the magnetic-field-induced anisotropic conductivity in the confinement phase may contribute up to 20-30% of the abnormal dilepton yield at large impact parameters, but this contribution is reduced because the emitted dileptons are preferentially hidden inside the scattering plane.

## Method

The authors performed quenched SU(2) lattice gauge theory simulations with a background magnetic field, calculating the vacuum expectation values of squared current densities \(\langle j_\mu^{f\,2}\rangle\) for different quark flavors (u/d, s, c) at masses \(m_q = 50\) MeV, 110 MeV, and 1 GeV. They related these current dispersions to the experimental observable \(a_{++}+a_{--}-2a_{+-}\) using a spherical fireball model with parameters \(\tau \sim 0.3\) fm, \(\rho \sim 0.2\) fm, and compared the results to STAR collaboration data. For the dilepton analysis, they extracted the anisotropic conductivity from lattice spectral functions via the Maximal Entropy Method and used Green-Kubo relations to estimate the dilepton emission rate anisotropy.

## Key numbers

* The combination \(a_{++}+a_{--}-2a_{+-}\) decreases by approximately a factor of 5 when the quark mass increases from 50 MeV to 110 MeV, and by a factor of almost 20 when the quark mass further increases to 1 GeV.
* The magnetic field strength in heavy-ion collisions is estimated as \(eB \sim (0.1\, b/R)\) GeV\(^2\), where \(b\) is the impact parameter and \(R\) is the nuclear radius.
* The ratio of the magnetic-field-induced conductivity in the confinement phase to the conductivity of quark-gluon plasma at \(T=1.12\,T_c\) is \(\sigma(B,T<T_c)/\sigma(B=0,T=1.12\,T_c) \approx eB/(0.5\,\text{GeV})^2\).
* The contribution of the magnetic field to the dilepton emission rate is \(R(B,T=0)/R(B=0,T=1.12\,T_c) \approx (0.4/3)\,b/R\), yielding up to 20-30% increase at large impact parameters \(b \sim 2R\).

## Context

This work extends lattice-based studies of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (Kharzeev 2008) and anisotropic electric conductivity (Buividovich 2010) by providing quantitative flavor-dependent predictions that can distinguish the CME from alternative explanations such as nuclear medium effects on jet formation (Petersen 2011) and other in-medium effects (Ma 2011). The results enable experimental tests using mesons of different quark content (pions, kaons, D-mesons) at RHIC and future facilities like FAIR and NICA, and suggest that the magnetic-field dependence of dilepton yields might serve as a signature of the confinement-deconfinement phase transition.
