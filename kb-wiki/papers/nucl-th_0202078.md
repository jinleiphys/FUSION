# Lectures on Effective Field Theories for Nuclei, Nuclear Matter and Dense Matter

> This note is based on four lectures that I gave at the 10th Taiwan Nuclear Spring School held at Hualien, Taiwan in January 2002. It aims to correlate the old notion of Cheshire Cat Principle developed for elementary baryons to the modern notion of quark-baryon and gluon-meson "continuities" or "dualities" in dilute and dense many-body systems and predict what would happen to mesons when squeezed by nuclear matter to high density as possibly realized in compact stars. Using color-flavor locking in QCD, the vector mesons observed at low density can be described as the Higgsed gluons dressed by cloud of collective modes, i.e., pions just as they are in superdense matter, thus showing the equivalence between hidden $flavor$ gauge symmetry and explicit $color$ gauge symmetry. Instead of going into details of well-established facts, I focus on a variety of novel ideas -- some solid and some less -- that could be confirmed or ruled out in the near future.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Cheshire Cat Principle (CCP), the idea that low-energy hadronic properties are independent of the size of the confinement region, can be extended from elementary baryons to dense many-body systems, suggesting that vector mesons at low density are equivalent to Higgsed gluons dressed by pions, with the caveat that this equivalence is established exactly only in (1+1) dimensions and holds approximately in (3+1) dimensions for topological quantities.

## Method

The author constructs a chiral bag model in (1+1) dimensions with a massless fermion confined inside a region coupled to a free boson ("pion") outside, using boundary conditions that enforce chiral symmetry and vector current conservation. Quantum effects (anomalies) are shown to cause fermion number leakage from the bag, which is compensated by the pion field carrying the leaked charge as a soliton. The same construction is extended to (3+1) dimensions using a Lagrangian with quarks and gluons inside the bag and chiral meson fields (pions, eta-prime) outside, including the Wess-Zumino-Witten term and axial anomaly contributions.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant: f = f_pi ~ 93 MeV (used in the (3+1)-dimensional Cheshire Cat Lagrangian).
* Scalar mass in the Schwinger model derived via CCP: m_phi^2 = e^2/pi (from the (1+1)-dimensional calculation with a U(1) gauge field).
* Eta-prime mass: m_eta (appears in the axial current divergence relation partial_mu A^mu_eta = f m_eta^2 eta, with f_0 = sqrt(N_f/2) f).
* Bag radius R appears in the parameter y_eta = m_eta R, used in the eta contribution to the flavor singlet axial current.

## Context

This work extends the Cheshire Cat Principle developed by Rho, Zahed, and collaborators (NRZ, NRWZ) from single-baryon systems to nuclear matter and dense matter, connecting it to the concept of color-flavor locking in QCD. It builds on earlier Skyrme-type models for baryons and the MIT bag model, and aims to enable predictions for meson properties at high density relevant to compact stars, as well as to resolve the "proton spin" problem within the chiral bag framework. The approach contrasts with standard nuclear physics approaches (SNPA) by seeking a unified EFT description from QCD.
