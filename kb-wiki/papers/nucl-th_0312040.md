# Non-Mesonic Weak Decay of Lambda-hypernuclei: a new determination of the Gn/Gp ratio

> Theoretical descriptions of the non-mesonic weak decay of Lambda-hypernuclei are unable to reproduce the experimental values of the ratio Gn/Gp=G(Lambda n -> nn)/G(Lambda p -> np). In this contribution we discuss a new approach to this problem. We have incorporated a one-meson-exchange model for the Lambda N -> nN transition in finite nuclei in an intranuclear cascade code for the calculation of double-coincidence nucleon distributions corresponding to the non-mesonic decay of 5_Lambda-He and 12_Lambda-C. The two-nucleon induced decay mechanism, Lambda np -> nnp, has been taken into account within a local density approximation scheme using a one-pion-exchange model supplemented by short range correlations. A weak decay model independent analysis of preliminary KEK coincidence data for 5_Lambda-He allows us to extract Gn/Gp=0.39 \pm 0.11 when the two-nucleon induced channel is neglected (i.e., G2=0) and Gn/Gp=0.26 \pm 0.11 when G2/G1=0.2.

# Non-Mesonic Weak Decay of Lambda-hypernuclei: a new determination of the Gn/Gp ratio

## Key claim

The ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_p extracted from a weak-decay-model-independent analysis of preliminary KEK coincidence data for 5_Lambda-He is 0.39 +/- 0.11 when the two-nucleon induced channel is neglected, and 0.26 +/- 0.11 when Gamma_2/Gamma_1 = 0.2; these values are substantially smaller than previous determinations from single-nucleon spectra analyses and agree with recent theoretical predictions for the first time.

## Method

A one-meson-exchange model for the Lambda N -> nN transition in finite nuclei was incorporated into an intranuclear cascade code to calculate double-coincidence nucleon distributions for the non-mesonic decay of 5_Lambda-He and 12_Lambda-C. The two-nucleon induced decay mechanism (Lambda np -> nnp) was treated within a local density approximation using a one-pion-exchange model supplemented by short range correlations. The nucleon propagation through the residual nucleus was simulated with a Monte Carlo code using free-space nucleon-nucleon cross sections corrected for Pauli blocking.

## Key numbers

- For 5_Lambda-He, Gamma_n/Gamma_p = 0.39 +/- 0.11 when the two-nucleon induced channel is neglected (Gamma_2 = 0)
- For 5_Lambda-He, Gamma_n/Gamma_p = 0.26 +/- 0.11 when Gamma_2/Gamma_1 = 0.2
- The preliminary KEK-E462 data for 5_Lambda-He give N_nn/N_np = 0.44 +/- 0.11 for back-to-back angles (cos theta_NN <= -0.8) with a 30 MeV nucleon energy threshold
- The OMEa model predicts N_nn/N_np = 0.51 for 5_Lambda-He and 0.39 for 12_Lambda-C (cos theta_NN <= -0.8, T_n, T_p >= 30 MeV)
- The OMEf model predicts N_nn/N_np = 0.61 for 5_Lambda-He and 0.43 for 12_Lambda-C (cos theta_NN <= -0.8, T_n, T_p >= 30 MeV)
- The OPE model predicts N_nn/N_np = 0.25 for 5_Lambda-He and 0.24 for 12_Lambda-C (cos theta_NN <= -0.8, T_n, T_p >= 30 MeV)
- Total non-mesonic decay rates predicted for 5_Lambda-He: 0.43 (OPE and OMEa) and 0.32 (OMEf) in units of the free Lambda decay width
- Total non-mesonic decay rates predicted for 12_Lambda-C: 0.75 (OPE), 0.73 (OMEa), and 0.55 (OMEf) in units of the free Lambda decay width
- Gamma_n/Gamma_p predictions for 5_Lambda-He: 0.09 (OPE), 0.34 (OMEa), 0.46 (OMEf)
- Gamma_n/Gamma_p predictions for 12_Lambda-C: 0.08 (OPE), 0.29 (OMEa), 0.34 (OMEf)

## Context

This work addresses the longstanding puzzle that theoretical descriptions of non-mesonic weak decay of Lambda-hypernuclei could not reproduce experimental values of Gamma_n/Gamma_p, which previous single-nucleon analyses (Szymanski 1991, Noumi 1995) found to be around 1 or larger, while one-pion-exchange models predicted values of 0.05-0.20. The authors argue that coincidence observables eliminate interference terms between neutron- and proton-induced channels that contaminated earlier single-nucleon analyses. The extracted Gamma_n/Gamma_p values agree with recent theoretical calculations including heavy-meson exchange and direct quark contributions (Parreno 2001, Itonaga 1998, Oka 1999), and the approach enables future coincidence data from KEK and FINUDA to provide new determinations of Gamma_n/Gamma_p and first constraints on Gamma_2/Gamma_1.
