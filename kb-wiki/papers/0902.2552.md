# Plasmon excitations in homogeneous neutron star matter

> We study the possible collective plasma modes which can affect neutron-star thermodynamics and different elementary processes in the baryonic density range between nuclear saturation ($ρ_0$) and $3ρ_0$. In this region, the expected constituents of neutron-star matter are mainly neutrons, protons, electrons and muons ($npeμ$ matter), under the constraint of beta equilibrium. The elementary plasma excitations of the $peμ$ three-fluid medium are studied in the RPA framework. We emphasize the relevance of the Coulomb interaction among the three species, in particular the interplay of the electron and muon screening in suppressing the possible proton plasma mode, which is converted into a sound-like mode. The Coulomb interaction alone is able to produce a variety of excitation branches and the full spectral function shows a rich structure at different energy. The genuine plasmon mode is pushed at high energy and it contains mainly an electron component with a substantial muon component, which increases with density. The plasmon is undamped for not too large momentum and is expected to be hardly affected by the nuclear interaction. All the other branches, which fall below the plasmon, are damped or over-damped.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in homogeneous neutron star matter (npeμ composition), the proton plasmon mode does not actually exist as a collective excitation because electron and muon screening converts it into a damped sound-like mode; the only genuine undamped plasmon is a high-energy mode dominated by electrons with a substantial muon component at higher densities.

## Method

The authors study the three-fluid (proton, electron, muon) plasma in the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) framework, using the Vlasov approximation for the polarization propagators. They solve the coupled RPA equations for the three-component system with Coulomb interactions only, computing the spectral functions for each species to identify excitation branches and their damping. The proton fraction is determined by beta equilibrium using the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone (BBG) equation of state, and calculations are performed at zero temperature for baryon densities between ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and 3ρ₀.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is the lower bound of the density range studied; the upper bound is 3ρ₀.
- The screening wavevector is given by q_c² = 3[(ω_p^e/v_F^e)² + (ω_p^μ/v_F^μ)²], where ω_p^e and ω_p^μ are the electron and muon gas plasmon frequencies and v_F^e, v_F^μ are the corresponding Fermi velocities; in neutron star matter conditions q_c << k_F^p.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of collective excitations in neutron star matter, particularly addressing the role of proton plasmons in neutrino emissivity calculations by Leinson (2007) and others. The authors show that the proton plasmon mode invoked in previous work is actually suppressed by electron and muon screening, becoming a sound-like mode, which has implications for neutrino cooling processes. The results provide the spectral functions needed to compute neutrino mean free paths and emissivities, and the authors note that the genuine plasmon mode is expected to be largely unaffected by nuclear interactions or superfluidity, while the lower-energy modes will require a full treatment including nuclear forces and neutrons in future work.
