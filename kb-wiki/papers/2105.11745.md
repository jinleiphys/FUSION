# Efimov effect evaporation after confinement

> The continuous confinement of quantum systems can be described by means of the $d$-method, where the dimension $d$ is taken as a continuous parameter. In this work we describe in detail how this method can be used to obtain the root mean square radii for a squeezed three-body system. These observables are used to investigate the disappearance of the Efimov states around the two-body threshold during a progressive confinement of the system from three to two dimensions. We illustrate how the disappearance takes place through the loss of one of the particles, whereas the other two remain bound.

# Efimov effect evaporation after confinement

## Key claim

The disappearance of Efimov states during continuous confinement from three to two dimensions occurs through the progressive loss of one heavy particle from the bound heavy-light subsystem, rather than through simultaneous breakup of all three particles. This evaporation process is demonstrated for the third excited Efimov state at dimensions below the critical value d_E = 2.75, where the Efimov conditions are fulfilled.

## Method

The authors use the d-method, treating the spatial dimension d as a continuous parameter decreasing from d=3 to d=2, to describe confinement of a three-body system. They compute root mean square radii from the d-dimensional wave function by interpreting it as a 3D wave function deformed along the squeezing direction via a scale parameter s, then evaluate the ratio ⟨r_y²⟩^{1/2}/⟨r_x²⟩^{1/2} in different Jacobi coordinate sets. The system studied consists of two identical heavy particles (mass m_H) and one light particle (mass m_L) with mass ratio m_H/m_L = 133/6, using a Gaussian heavy-light potential with zero heavy-heavy interaction, including only relative s-waves in the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion.

## Key numbers

- Mass ratio m_H/m_L = 133/6, corresponding to an energy scale factor between Efimov states of 46.5
- Critical dimension d_E = 2.75, where the Efimov conditions are fulfilled and the two-body binding energy equals zero
- At d = d_E = 2.75, the radius ratio ⟨r_y²⟩^{1/2}/⟨r_x²⟩^{1/2} takes values of approximately 1.64 in the first Jacobi set and approximately 0.57 in the second Jacobi set
- The third excited Efimov state disappears at approximately d ≈ 2.51, where the first Jacobi set ratio approaches 0.5 and the second Jacobi set ratio diverges to infinity

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Garrido 2021) showing that Efimov states appear and disappear within a very small dimension interval around d_E during confinement from 3D to 2D. It builds on the established equivalence between the d-method and explicit squeezing potentials (Garrido 2019, 2020), and on the known result that the Efimov effect does not exist in 2D (Nielsen 1997, Volosniev 2013, Naidon 2017, Bruch 1979, Lim 1980). The results enable visualization of how individual Efimov states evaporate by particle loss rather than simultaneous breakup, and the formalism can be applied to other squeezing scenarios such as 3D to 1D or 2D to 1D confinement.
