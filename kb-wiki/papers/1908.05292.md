# Normalized symmetric cumulants as a measure of QCD phase transition: a viscous hydrodynamic study

> Finding the existence and the location of the QCD critical point is one of the main goals of the RHIC beam energy scan program. To make theoretical predictions and corroborate with the experimental data requires modeling the space-time evolution of the matter created in heavy-ion collisions by dynamical models such as the relativistic hydrodynamics with an appropriate Equation of State (EoS). In the present exploratory study, we use a viscous 2+1 dimensional event-by-event (e-by-e) hydrodynamic code at finite baryon densities with two different EoSs (i) Lattice QCD + HRG with a crossover transition and (ii) EoS with a first-order phase transition to studying the normalized symmetric cumulants of charged pions $v_n$ $(n=2-4)$. We show that the normalized symmetric cumulants can differentiate the two EoSs while all other conditions remain the same. The conclusion does not change for various initial conditions and shear viscosity. This indicates that these observables can be used to gain information about the QCD EoS from experimental data and can be used as an EoS meter.

## Key claim

The normalized symmetric cumulants of charged pion flow harmonics \(v_n\) (n=2-4) can differentiate between a crossover equation of state (EoS) and a first-order phase transition EoS in viscous hydrodynamic simulations, and this conclusion holds for various initial conditions and shear viscosity values, suggesting these observables can serve as an "EoS meter" for the QCD phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1-dimensional event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations at finite baryon density using two different equations of state: (i) a lattice QCD + hadron resonance gas EoS with a crossover transition, and (ii) an EoS with a first-order phase transition constructed from a non-interacting massless QGP gas and a hadron resonance gas with a bag constant \(B^{1/4}=230\) MeV yielding \(T_c=164\) MeV. The conservation equations for energy-momentum and net baryon current were solved using the SHASTA (SHarp And Smooth Transport Algorithm) flux-corrected transport method, with the shear-stress tensor evolution following the Israel-Stewart formalism. The normalized symmetric cumulants of charged pions were computed from the final-state flow harmonics for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- The bag constant for the first-order phase transition EoS was set to \(B^{1/4}=230\) MeV, yielding a critical temperature of \(T_c=164\) MeV.
- The freeze-out energy density was set to \(\epsilon_F=0.3\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The maximum initial energy density used was \(\epsilon_0=16.2\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- Simulations were performed for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4\) GeV with impact parameter \(b=8\) fm.
- The constant entropy per baryon number (\(s/n_B\)) values for different regions of the fireball were 156, 175, and 206.

## Context

This exploratory study extends the approach of Niemi (2012) who showed that correlations between initial eccentricities and flow coefficients are sensitive to the EoS, but the present work focuses on experimentally accessible normalized symmetric cumulants of flow harmonics rather than eccentricities. The authors note that while event-averaged elliptic flow is suppressed by shear viscosity and thus not a good EoS indicator, the normalized symmetric cumulants \(c(v_2,v_3)\) and \(c(v_3,v_4)\) (as defined by the ALICE Collaboration, 2016) can differentiate between crossover and first-order phase transitions regardless of initial conditions and shear viscosity. This work is complementary to the deep-learning approach of Pang (2016) for identifying the QCD EoS from heavy-ion collision data, and it enables future experimental searches for the QCD critical point using beam energy scan data from RHIC.
