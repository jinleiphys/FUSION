# Saturation Physics in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We discuss expectations of saturation physics for various observables in heavy ion collisions. We show how simple saturation-inspired assumptions about particle production in heavy ion collisions lead to Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi model. Comparing this model to RHIC data on particle multiplicities we conclude that saturation effects may play an important role in particle production and dynamics at the early stages of Au-Au collisions already at RHIC energies. We then estimate the contribution of the initial state two-particle azimuthal correlations to elliptic flow observable v_2 in Au-Au collisions by constructing a lower bound on these non-flow effects based on v_2 obtained from the analysis of proton-proton (pp) collisions.

## Key claim

Saturation physics may play an important role in particle production and dynamics at the early stages of Au-Au collisions already at RHIC energies, but the phenomenological success of saturation models does not contradict the possibility of strong final state interactions leading to formation of quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The author derives the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi model from saturation-inspired assumptions about gluon production, using classical field theory with a single hard scale Q_s and running coupling. The model is compared to PHOBOS and BRAHMS data on charged particle multiplicities as functions of centrality, energy, and rapidity at sqrt(s) = 130 and 200 GeV. A model-independent lower bound on non-flow contributions to elliptic flow v_2 in Au-Au collisions is constructed by scaling v_2 measured in pp collisions by the square root of the ratio of multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Total charged particle multiplicity at mid-rapidity for most central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 GeV: dN/dη = 555 ± 12 (stat) ± 35 (syst)
- Predicted total charged multiplicity at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: dN/dη = 616 to 634, using λ = 0.25 to 0.3 from HERA data analysis by Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998)
- Measured PHOBOS result at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: dN/dη = 650 ± 35 (stat)
- Lower bound on non-flow contribution to v_2 in Au-Au collisions at high p_T: v_2^{AA}|_{non-flow} ≥ 7%, derived using N^{AA}_{part} = 394, N^{pp}_{part} = 2, and v_2^{pp}(p_T) ≈ 1 at high p_T

## Context

This work extends the saturation/Color Glass Condensate framework developed by Gribov, Levin, Ryskin (1983), Mueller and Qiu (1986), and McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) to heavy ion collisions at RHIC. The Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi model predictions for multiplicity as a function of centrality and energy are shown to agree with PHOBOS and BRAHMS data. The lower bound on non-flow v_2 contributions provides a baseline for isolating genuine collective flow effects in heavy ion collisions, and the author notes that thermalization in the saturation framework as argued by Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2001) would not introduce new scales.
