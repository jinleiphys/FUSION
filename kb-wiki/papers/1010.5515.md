# Deconfinement in Yang-Mills theory through toroidal compactification with deformation

> We introduce field theory techniques through which the deconfinement transition of four-dimensional Yang-Mills theory can be moved to a semi-classical domain where it becomes calculable using two-dimensional field theory. We achieve this through a double- trace deformation of toroidally compactified Yang-Mills theory on R2 \times S1_L \times S1_β. At large N, fixed-L, and arbitrary β, the thermodynamics of the deformed theory is equivalent to that of ordinary Yang-Mills theory at leading order in the large N expansion. At fixed-N, small L and a range of β, the deformed theory maps to a two-dimensional theory with electric and magnetic (order and disorder) perturbations, analogs of which appear in planar spin-systems and statistical physics. We show that in this regime the deconfinement transition is driven by the competition between electric and magnetic perturbations in this two-dimensional theory. This appears to support the scenario proposed by Liao and Shuryak regarding the magnetic component of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a double-trace deformation of Yang-Mills theory on R^2 x S^1_L x S^1_beta that moves the deconfinement transition into a semi-classical regime calculable via two-dimensional field theory, but only in the regime where L is smaller than (Lambda N)^{-1} and at finite N; at large N and fixed L, the deformed theory remains equivalent to ordinary Yang-Mills theory and equally incalculable.

## Method

The authors deform the Yang-Mills action by adding double-trace operators of the form (1/L^4) sum_n a_n |tr(Omega_L^n)|^2 with sufficiently positive coefficients a_n to stabilize the (Z_N)_L center symmetry. They then compactify the theory on R^2 x S^1_L x S^1_beta and, in the regime LN Lambda less than or similar to 1 where the Wilson line acquires a center-symmetric vacuum expectation value, they perform Abelian duality to obtain a two-dimensional dual theory with cosine (sine-Gordon type) interactions. The deconfinement transition is analyzed by studying the relevance of electric versus magnetic perturbations in this two-dimensional theory as the temperature (beta) is varied.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement temperature of QCD is T_c = beta_c^{-1} approximately 170 MeV (from lattice simulations, cited for context).
- The W-boson mass in the semi-classical regime is m_W = 2 pi / (L N).
- The mass gap for the photon species is m_p = m_sigma sin(pi p / N) with m_sigma = A Lambda (LN Lambda / (2 pi))^{5/6} |ln(LN Lambda / (2 pi))|^{8/11}, where A is an O(1) coefficient.
- The conformal dimension of the operator e^{i alpha_i dot sigma} in the two-dimensional dual theory is Delta = 4 pi L / (beta g^2).
- The semi-classical domain is defined by LN Lambda / (2 pi) much less than 1.
- The regime of validity for the two-dimensional reduction is m_W^{-1} much less than beta much less than m_sigma^{-1} approximately m_W^{-1} e^{S_0/2}.

## Context

This work extends the large-N volume independence framework of Unsal (2008) to finite temperature, providing a calculable semi-classical description of deconfinement that is smoothly connected to the strongly coupled regime. The two-dimensional electric-magnetic competition mechanism supports the scenario proposed by Liao and Shuryak (2006, 2008) regarding the magnetic component of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC. The approach differs from the gauge/gravity correspondence of Witten (1997, 1998) and the S^3 x S^1 matrix model approach of Aharony (2003, 2005) and Unsal (2007) by achieving calculability at finite N in infinite spatial volume rather than requiring the large-N limit for the thermodynamic limit.
