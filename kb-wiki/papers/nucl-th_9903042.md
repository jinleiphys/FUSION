# Shell model calculations of stellar weak interaction rates: I. Gamow-Teller distributions and spectra of nuclei in the mass range A=45-65

> Electron capture and beta-decay rates on nuclei in the mass range A=45-65 play an important role in many astrophysical environments. The determination of these rates by large-scale shell model calculations is desirable, but it requires to reproduce the Gamow-Teller strength distributions and spectra of the pf shell nuclei. We show in this paper that large-scale shell model calculations, employing a slightly monopole-corrected version of the wellknown KB3 interaction, fulfill these necessary requirements. In particular, our calculations reproduce the experimentally available GT+ and GT- strength distributions and the nuclear halflives, and describe the nuclear spectra appropriately.

## Key claim

Large-scale shell model calculations using a slightly monopole-corrected version of the KB3 interaction can reproduce the Gamow-Teller strength distributions and nuclear spectra needed for reliable stellar weak interaction rate estimates in the mass range A=45-65, but the authors note that the monopole corrections are "the minimal ones which allow a fair reproduction" and that "slight energy misplacements in the level scheme do not matter" because experimental energies will replace shell model energies in the final rate calculations.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model diagonalization calculations in the complete pf shell using a modified KB3 interaction with adjusted monopole centroids: V^T_{7/2,r} lowered by 0.1 MeV, V^T_{3/2,3/2}, V^T_{3/2,1/2}, and V^T_{1/2,1/2} raised by 0.1 MeV, and V^T_{5/2,5/2}, V^T_{5/2,3/2}, and V^T_{5/2,1/2} lowered by 0.2 MeV for both isospins T=0,1. Calculations were performed at truncation levels involving typically 10 million configurations or more, with GT strength distributions scaled by a universal factor of (0.74)^2 to account for the overestimate inherent in 0ℏω shell model calculations.

## Context

This work extends the earlier FFN parametrization by Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1980-1985) which used an independent particle model with empirical log ft=5 for unmeasured transitions, and improves upon early shell model attempts by Aufderheide and collaborators (1991-1996) that used strongly restricted model spaces. The paper corrects deficiencies of the KB3 interaction identified in SMMC studies by Langanke (1995) and diagonalization studies by Nowacki (1998), particularly the overenhancement of the N=28 shell closure in ⁵⁶Ni. The results enable a forthcoming compilation of stellar electron capture and beta-decay rates for over 100 nuclei in the mass range A=45-65, which will be used in simulations of core collapse in massive stars.
