# Phase conversion in a weakly first-order quark-hadron transition

> We investigate the process of phase conversion in a thermally-driven {\it weakly} first-order quark-hadron transition. This scenario is physically appealing even if the nature of this transition in equilibrium proves to be a smooth crossover for vanishing baryonic chemical potential. We construct an effective potential by combining the equation of state obtained within Lattice QCD for the partonic sector with that of a gas of resonances in the hadronic phase, and present numerical results on bubble profiles, nucleation rates and time evolution, including the effects from reheating on the dynamics for different expansion scenarios. Our findings confirm the standard picture of a cosmological first-order transition, in which the process of phase conversion is entirely dominated by nucleation, also in the case of a weakly first-order transition. On the other hand, we show that, even for expansion rates much lower than those expected in high-energy heavy ion collisions, nucleation is very unlikely, indicating that the main mechanism of phase conversion is spinodal decomposition. Our results are compared to those obtained for a strongly first-order transition, as the one provided by the MIT bag model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a weakly first-order quark-hadron transition, the standard cosmological picture of phase conversion dominated by bubble nucleation holds, but for expansion rates much lower than those in heavy ion collisions, nucleation is very unlikely and spinodal decomposition becomes the main mechanism.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective potential by combining the equation of state from Lattice QCD for the partonic sector (with Nf=2+1 quark flavors) with that of a gas of over 250 free resonances for the hadronic phase. They computed bubble profiles, critical radii, surface tension, and free energy as functions of temperature, then evaluated nucleation rates and time evolution including reheating effects for different expansion scenarios using standard techniques for first-order transition dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The spinodal temperature relative to critical temperature is T_sp/T_c = 0.984, leading to H*t_sp approximately 0.01
- The overestimated hadronic fraction from nucleation before reaching spinodal temperature is f(t_sp) less than approximately 10^-3, which is much less than 1
- The Hubble parameter inverse H^-1 was taken as 10 fm/c for the estimate ruling out nucleation in heavy ion collisions
- The expansion rate of QGP in heavy ion collisions is faster than in the early universe by a factor of approximately 10^18

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark-hadron transition dynamics that were mostly based on the MIT bag model or effective models like the linear sigma model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, which assume a strongly first-order transition. The authors compare their results for a weakly first-order transition to those obtained with the MIT bag model, and note that earlier studies by Csernai (1992), Kapusta (1994), and Scavenius (2001) used strongly first-order equations of state. The findings enable future work on whether spinodal decomposition can describe the dynamics even for a smooth but very fast crossover in heavy ion collisions.
