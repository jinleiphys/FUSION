# Quark-gluon densities in the nuclear fragmentation region in heavy ion collisions at LHC

> At the LHC, the leading partons in the nuclei are expected to interact with the maximal possible strength - black disk limit - up to transverse momenta of the order of few GeV. We demonstrate that in this limit the densities of the quark - gluon systems produced in the central AA collisions in the nucleus fragmentation regions should exceed 300 GeV/fm^3 which is at least as high as the densities discussed for the central region. Experimental signatures of such a regime are also discussed.

## Key claim

At LHC energies, the quark-gluon densities produced in the nuclear fragmentation region of central AA collisions should exceed 300 GeV/fm^3, at least as high as densities discussed for the central region, provided the leading partons interact at the black disk limit up to transverse momenta of a few GeV.

## Method

The analysis uses the rest frame of one nucleus and considers partons with light-cone fraction x ≥ 10^{-2} propagating through the center of a heavy nucleus. The emission angles, Lorentz factors, and volume compression factors are calculated from the conservation of light-cone fraction and the transverse momenta generated in the black disk limit. The energy density is then estimated from the enhancement factor R_E, which depends on the number of partons, their x values, and their average transverse momenta.

## Key numbers

- For gluons with x ≥ 0.05 at LHC, average transverse momentum squared ⟨p_{g t}^2⟩ ≥ 16 GeV^2, growing with increasing x; for quarks ⟨p_{q t}^2⟩ is a factor of two smaller.
- Taking N_q=3, N_g=6, x_q=1/6, x_g=1/12, and ⟨p_{g t}^2⟩ = 20 GeV^2 gives enhancement factor R_E = 2300, corresponding to energy density ~ 370 GeV/fm^3.
- At RHIC, assuming proximity of black disk limit for p_t ~ 1 GeV/c, quark-gluon energy densities are ~ 10 GeV/fm^3.
- The produced system has a small principal axis of ~ 0.5 fm and density ρ ≥ 50 partons per fm^3.
- The coherence length for parton conversion to hadrons is l_coh ≈ 0.3-0.4 fm · p_q [GeV], corresponding to p_q = 4 GeV.
- Average invariant energy for parton interactions is s ≈ 2p_t^2 ~ 32 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the initial estimates of hadron matter densities in the nuclear fragmentation region by Larry (1995) using soft hadronic dynamics, and the first quark-gluon density estimates in the black disk limit by Strikman (2003). The paper updates and revises those earlier analyses, finding densities at least a factor of ten larger than the soft hadronic estimates. The results enable predictions for experimental signatures including production of c\bar c and b\bar b pairs via gluon fusion, leading photons from q g → γ q subprocesses, and leading nucleon production via quark recombination.
