# One-loop Matching for Spin-Dependent Quasi-TMDs

> Transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) provide a unique probe of the three-dimensional spin structure of hadrons. We construct spin-dependent quasi-TMDPDFs that are amenable to lattice QCD calculations and that can be used to determine spin-dependent TMDPDFs. We calculate the short-distance coefficients connecting spin-dependent TMDPDFs and quasi-TMDPDFs at one-loop order. We find that the helicity and transversity distributions have the same coefficient as the unpolarized TMDPDF. We also argue that the same is true for pretzelosity and that this spin universality of the matching will hold to all orders in $α_s$. Thus, it is possible to calculate ratios of these distributions as a function of longitudinal momentum and transverse position utilizing simpler Wilson line paths than have previously been considered.

## Key claim

The matching kernel connecting spin-dependent quasi-TMDPDFs to spin-dependent TMDPDFs is identical to the unpolarized matching kernel at one-loop order for helicity and transversity distributions, and the authors argue this "spin universality" extends to pretzelosity and holds to all orders in \(\alpha_s\), enabling simpler lattice calculations of ratios of these distributions.

## Method

The authors construct spin-dependent quasi-TMDPDFs as equal-time analogs of TMDPDFs, amenable to lattice QCD, using staple-shaped Wilson lines of length \(L\) along the \(z\)-direction. They calculate the short-distance matching coefficients at one-loop order in \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\) scheme by comparing on-shell quark matrix elements of the spin-dependent TMDPDFs and quasi-TMDPDFs, regulating both UV and IR divergences with dimensional regularization in \(d=4-2\epsilon\) dimensions.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized matching kernel at one-loop order: \(C_{\ns}(\mu, x P^z) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{4\pi} \left[ -\ln^2\frac{(2 x P^z)^2}{\mu^2} + 2 \ln\frac{(2 x P^z)^2}{\mu^2} - 4 + \frac{\pi^2}{6} \right] + \mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\)
- The helicity and transversity matching kernels are found to be identical to the unpolarized kernel at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)\)
- The canonical logarithm is defined as \(L_b = \ln\frac{b_T^2 \mu^2}{b_0^2}\) with \(b_0 = 2 e^{-\gamma_E}\)
- The tree-level traces yield: for \(\Gamma = \gamma^\lambda\): \(\Tr[u_s(p)\bar u_s(p)\gamma^\lambda/2] = p^\lambda\); for \(\Gamma = \gamma^\lambda\gamma_5\): \(\Tr[u_s(p)\bar u_s(p)\gamma^\lambda\gamma_5/2] = \Lambda p^\lambda\); for \(\Gamma = i\sigma^{\alpha\lambda}\gamma_5\): \(\Tr[u_s(p)\bar u_s(p)i\sigma^{\alpha\lambda}\gamma_5/2] = p^\lambda s_\perp^\alpha\)

## Context

This work extends the one-loop matching calculation for unpolarized quasi-TMDPDFs from Ebert et al. (2019) and Ji et al. (2018) to the full set of leading-power spin-dependent TMDPDFs, including helicity, transversity, pretzelosity, Sivers, Boer-Mulders, and worm-gear distributions. The spin universality result enables lattice QCD calculations of ratios of spin-dependent to unpolarized TMDPDFs using simpler Wilson line paths than previously considered, building on the ratio method proposed by Ebert et al. (2019) and the LaMET framework of Ji (2013, 2014). The results also apply to two different quasi-TMDPDF definitions from Ji et al. (2018, 2019) and Ji et al. (2019, 2020), which agree in the infinite staple length limit.
