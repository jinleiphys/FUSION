# Approximate solution of the pairing Hamiltonian in the Berggren basis

> We derive the approximate solution for the pairing Hamiltonian in the Berggren ensemble of single particle states including bound, resonance and non-resonant scattering states. We show that this solution is reliable in the limit of a weak pairing interaction.

## Key claim

The paper derives an approximate solution for the pairing Hamiltonian in the Berggren basis (including bound, resonance, and non-resonant scattering states) that is reliable only in the limit of weak pairing correlations, not as an exact solution for arbitrary pairing strength.

## Method

The authors start from the rational Richardson-Gaudin (RG) pairing Hamiltonian and express it in the complete Berggren single-particle basis, discretizing the continuum using Gauss-Legendre quadrature weights. They modify the commutation relations for continuum states by replacing a Kronecker delta with a Dirac delta, then derive a generalized Richardson equation for pair energies by applying the Hamiltonian to a product of pair-creation operators acting on a vacuum of unpaired particles.

## Key numbers

- The pairing Hamiltonian is defined with single-particle energies epsilon_a and pairing strength G, with D = 2N + nu where N is the number of pairs and nu is the number of unpaired particles.
- The degeneracy Omega_i for each single-particle state is defined as Omega_i = 2j_i + 1.
- The generalized Richardson equation in the continuum limit includes a sum over bound and resonance states with factor 2G d_i / (2 epsilon_i - E_J_gamma) and an integral over scattering states with factor 2G d_k_c / (hbar^2 k_c^2 / m - E_J_gamma).
- In the special case of equal weights w for all states, the pairing strength is renormalized as G' = G w, and the Richardson equation reduces to G integral (d_k / (2 epsilon_k - E_i)) dk = 1.

## Context

This work extends the exact Richardson solution for the rational RG model (Richardson 1963, 1964) to include continuum states via the Berggren basis (Berggren 1968). The approximate solution enables studies of pairing correlations in weakly bound or unbound exotic nuclei where continuum coupling is important, complementing exact Gamow Shell Model calculations (GSM references) that will be compared in future work.
