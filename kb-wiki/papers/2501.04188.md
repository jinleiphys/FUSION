# Two-loop form factors for $P$-wave quarkonium production and decay

> We present the analytical results for the two-loop form factors needed for $χ_{Q,J}$ production and decay. We consider the two-loop corrections to the process $γγ\leftrightarrow {^3 P_J^{[1]}}$, that has been known only numerically before, and the processes $gg \leftrightarrow {^3 P_J^{[1]}}$, $γg \leftrightarrow {^3 P_J^{[8]}}$ and $gg \leftrightarrow {^3 P_J^{[8]}}$, which have not been computed before. We observe that the NRQCD pole structure of the two-loop amplitude in the $gg$ channel is more involved for the spin-triplet $P$-wave case than for the pseudo-scalar $S$-wave case. It involves in addition to the standard Coulomb singularity also a new singularity whose cancellation requires the inclusion of the $gg \leftrightarrow {^3S_1^{[8]}}$ form factor. We give the high precision numerical results for the hard functions that can be used to compute $χ_{Q,J}$ production and decay up to NNLO accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first analytical two-loop form factors for several processes involving spin-triplet P-wave quarkonium (χ_Q,J) production and decay, where previous results for the γγ ↔ ³P_J^[1] process were only known numerically, and the gg ↔ ³P_J^[1], γg ↔ ³P_J^[8], and gg ↔ ³P_J^[8] processes had not been computed at all. The NRQCD pole structure for the gg channel in the spin-triplet P-wave case is more involved than for the pseudo-scalar S-wave case, requiring cancellation of a new singularity via inclusion of the gg ↔ ³S_1^[8] form factor.

## Method

The author computed the two-loop corrections to the form factors for the processes γγ ↔ ³P_J^[1], gg ↔ ³P_J^[1], γg ↔ ³P_J^[8], and gg ↔ ³P_J^[8] using NRQCD factorization. The analytical expressions involve functions in the class of multiple polylogarithms and their elliptic generalization (elliptic multiple polylogarithms), including iterated integrals of modular form as defined in the companion paper Abreu (2022). The results are presented as expansions of master integrals F_I in the dimensional regularization parameter ε, with the full expressions for elliptic integrals provided in ancillary material.

## Key numbers

- Catalan constant C = 0.915966... appears in the analytical expressions for the form factors
- The two-loop form factor coefficients a_i^(2) for i = 1 through 20 are given explicitly, containing numerical constants such as ζ₃, π², π⁴, and logarithms of 2 and 3
- The expressions involve special constants including the polygamma function ψ^m(z) and the elliptic polylogarithm combination hpli(0,-,+,-) defined as a sum of eight Goncharov polylogarithms G evaluated at i

## Context

This work extends the numerical results previously known only for the γγ ↔ ³P_J^[1] process to analytical form, and provides first-time computations for the gg ↔ ³P_J^[1], γg ↔ ³P_J^[8], and gg ↔ ³P_J^[8] processes. The analytical expressions build upon the master integrals and elliptic polylogarithm framework developed in Abreu (2022). The results enable computation of χ_Q,J production and decay up to NNLO accuracy, and the high-precision numerical results for the hard functions are provided for direct phenomenological use.
