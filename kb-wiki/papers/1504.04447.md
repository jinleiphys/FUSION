# Direct Evidence of Washing out of Nuclear Shell Effects

> Constraining excitation energy at which nuclear shell effect washes out has important implications on the production of super heavy elements and many other fields of nuclear physics research. We report the fission fragment mass distribution in alpha induced reaction on an actinide target for wide excitation range in close energy interval and show direct evidence that nuclear shell effect washes out at excitation energy ~40 MeV. Calculation shows that second peak of the fission barrier also vanishes around similar excitation energy.

# Direct Evidence of Washing out of Nuclear Shell Effects

## Key claim
The paper reports direct, model-independent evidence that nuclear shell effects wash out at an excitation energy of approximately 40 MeV in the compound nucleus 236U, based on fission fragment mass distributions measured in alpha-induced fusion-fission on 232Th. The claim is conditional on the observation that asymmetric fission components vanish at excitation energies above 40.5 MeV and that the second peak of the fission barrier also vanishes around the same excitation energy in macroscopic-microscopic calculations.

## Method
Fission fragment mass distributions were measured for the 4He + 232Th reaction over an excitation energy range of 21–64 MeV using two large-area position-sensitive multiwire proportional counters placed at folding angles. Fragment masses were determined event-by-event from precise measurements of flight paths and flight time differences of complementary fission fragments. The mass distributions were fitted with Gaussian functions: three Gaussians (one symmetric and two asymmetric) at lower excitation energies and a single symmetric Gaussian at higher excitation energies.

## Key numbers
- Excitation energy range studied: 21–64 MeV for the compound nucleus 236U.
- Shell effects wash out at excitation energy ~40 MeV, with asymmetric fission components vanishing at 43.6 MeV.
- At excitation energies between 43.6 and 64.2 MeV, mass distributions are symmetric and fitted by a single Gaussian peaking at approximately half the compound nucleus mass (A ~ 118).
- At lower excitation energies (≤ 40.5 MeV), asymmetric peaks appear at A ~ 132 and A ~ 100.
- The second fission barrier height becomes less than 500 keV at excitation energy ~40 MeV.
- Target thickness: 1.1 mg/cm² self-supporting 232Th.
- Angular resolution: polar angle accuracy better than 0.2°, azimuthal angle accuracy about 0.8°.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of fission fragment mass distributions in actinide nuclei by Back et al. (1981) and Colby et al., who observed asymmetric distributions in 242Pu up to 40–50 MeV but could not determine the exact energy for shell effect disappearance. The results are consistent with earlier indirect evidence from fission fragment angular distributions by Ramamurthy et al. (1995) and proton evaporation spectra by others, which suggested shell effects on level density parameters damp around 40–50 MeV. The present measurement provides the first direct, model-independent evidence for the washing out of shell effects at a specific excitation energy, which has implications for optimizing production cross sections of superheavy elements.
