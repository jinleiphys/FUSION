# Kinetic theory with spin: From massive to massless fermions

> We find that the recently developed kinetic theories with spin for massive and massless fermions are smoothly connected. By introducing a reference-frame vector, we decompose the dipole-moment tensor into electric and magnetic dipole moments. We show that the axial-vector component of the Wigner function contains a contribution from the transverse magnetic dipole moment which accounts for the transverse spin degree of freedom (DOF) and vanishes smoothly in the massless limit. As a result, the kinetic equations, describing four DOF for massive fermions, becomes smoothly the chiral kinetic equations describing two DOF in the massless limit. We also confirm the small-mass behavior of the Wigner function by explicit calculation using a Gaussian wave packet.

## Key claim

The authors show that the kinetic theory for massive fermions with spin, formulated via the Wigner function, can be smoothly connected to the chiral kinetic theory for massless fermions by introducing a reference-frame vector and decomposing the dipole-moment tensor, with the transverse spin degree of freedom vanishing in the massless limit.

## Method

The authors decompose the antisymmetric dipole-moment tensor Σ^{μν} into electric and magnetic dipole moments relative to an arbitrary time-like reference-frame vector u^{μ}, analogous to the decomposition of the electromagnetic field tensor. Using the constraint equation for the dipole-moment tensor and the Schouten identity, they express Σ^{μν} in terms of the magnetic dipole moment, the fermion number density V, and the axial-charge density A, then derive the corresponding form of the spin polarization vector n^{μ}. The transverse magnetic dipole moment M_⟂^{μ} is identified as the component that accounts for the transverse spin degree of freedom and vanishes as m→0.

## Key numbers

* The Wigner function components are expanded up to O(ℏ), with the scalar component F = m[V δ(p²−m²) − (ℏ/2)F^{αβ}Σ_{αβ} δ′(p²−m²)] + O(ℏ²).
* The axial-vector component A^{μ} = n^{μ} δ(p²−m²) + ℏ F̃^{μν} p_ν V δ′(p²−m²) + O(ℏ²), where n^{μ} is the spin polarization vector.
* The dipole-moment tensor is decomposed as Σ^{μν} = E^{μ}u^{ν} − E^{ν}u^{μ} − ε^{μναβ} u_α M_β, with E^{μ} = Σ^{μν}u_ν and M^{μ} = −(1/2)ε^{μναβ}u_ν Σ_{αβ}.
* The transverse magnetic dipole moment is defined as M_⟂^{μ} = Ξ^{μν}M_ν, where Ξ^{μν} projects perpendicular to both u^{μ} and p^{μ}.
* The spin polarization vector is expressed as n^{μ} = p^{μ}A + (p²/(u·p))M^{μ} + (ℏ/(2(u·p)))ε^{μναβ}p_ν u_α ∇_β V, with A = −(M·p)/(u·p) identified as the axial-charge density.

## Context

This work extends the spin kinetic theories for massive Dirac fermions developed by Gao (2019), Weickgenannt (2019), Hattori (2019), and others using covariant Wigner functions, and the chiral kinetic theory for massless fermions by Son (2012), Stephanov (2012), Hidaka (2016), and Gao (2018). The paper resolves the long-standing question of how the O(3) little-group symmetry of massive fermions transitions to the E(2) little-group symmetry of massless fermions in the kinetic theory framework, as originally discussed by Wigner (1939) and Kim (1986). This enables a unified treatment of spin transport for quarks of any mass in heavy-ion collisions, relevant for describing Λ hyperon polarization and the chiral magnetic effect.
