# Universal fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions in the Fermi energy domain

> We discuss the scaling laws of both the charged fragments multiplicity fluctuations and the charge of the largest fragment fluctuations for Xe+Sn collisions in the range of bombarding energies between 25 MeV/A and 50 MeV/A. We show close to E_{lab}=32 MeV/A the transition in the fluctuation regime of the charge of the largest fragment which is compatible with the transition from the ordered to disordered phase of excited nuclear matter. The size (charge) of the largest fragment is closely related to the order parameter characterizing this process.

# Universal fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions in the Fermi energy domain

## Key claim
The transition in the fluctuation regime of the charge of the largest fragment (Zmax) from second-scaling (Δ = 1/2) at low bombarding energies to first-scaling (Δ = 1) at higher energies is compatible with a transition from ordered to disordered phase of excited nuclear matter, but the existing data do not show critical behavior (anomalous tail exponent ν̃ > 2) in the transition region.

## Method
The authors applied the theory of universal fluctuations of observables in finite systems to experimental data from the INDRA multidetector for Xe+Sn collisions at bombarding energies from 25 to 50 MeV per nucleon. They analyzed the Δ-scaling properties of the probability distributions P[Zmax] (charge of the largest fragment) and P[n] (charged fragment multiplicity) for central and semi-central collisions, using centrality conditions based on completeness of detected charge and momentum and the flow angle Θflow.

## Key numbers
- Bombarding energy range: 25 MeV/A to 50 MeV/A for Xe+Sn collisions
- Transition in Zmax fluctuation regime occurs at Elab/A ≈ 32 MeV
- For central collisions at Elab/A = 39, 45, 50 MeV, P[Zmax] follows first-scaling (Δ = 1)
- For central collisions at Elab/A = 25, 32 MeV, P[Zmax] follows second-scaling (Δ = 1/2)
- Fitted tail exponent for first-scaling branch: ν̃ = 1.6 ± 0.4
- Fitted tail exponent for second-scaling branch: ν̃ = 1.8 ± 0.4
- About 20000 events per collision energy used in the analysis
- Centrality condition: more than 80% of total charge and momentum detected, Θflow ≥ π/3

## Context
This work extends the theory of universal fluctuations in finite systems (order parameter scaling theory) to heavy-ion collisions in the Fermi energy domain, providing a model-independent method that does not assume equilibrium in fragment production. The analysis distinguishes between aggregation scenarios (Fisher droplet model, Ising model, percolation model, Smoluchowski gelation model) where <Zmax> is the order parameter, and fragmentation scenarios (fragmentation-inactivation-binary model) where <n> is the order parameter. The results favor the aggregation scenario and indicate that the critical region, if it exists, should be searched for in a narrow window near 32 MeV/A, requiring future studies with smaller bombarding energy steps to distinguish between a cross-over phenomenon and a true phase transition.
