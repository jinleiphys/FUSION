# Meson coupling constants at high mass and large N_c

> Quark-hadron duality implies that a process described in terms of quark loops should be the hadronic amplitude when averaged over a sufficient number of states. Ambiguities associated with the notion of quark hadron duality can be made arbitrarily small for highly excited mesons at large $N_c$. QCD is expected to form a string like description at large $N_c$ yielding an exponentially increasing Hagedorn spectrum for high mass. It is shown that in order to reconcile quantum-hadron duality with a Hagedorn spectrum, the magnitude of individual coupling constants between high-lying mesons in a typical decay process must be characteristically larger than the average of the coupling constants to mesons with nearby masses. The ratio of the square of the average coupling to the average of the coupling squared (where the average is over mesons with nearby masses) drops {\it exponentially} with the mass of the meson. Scenarios are discussed by which such a high precision cancellation can occur.

## Key claim

To reconcile quark-hadron duality with a Hagedorn spectrum at large \(N_c\), the magnitude of individual coupling constants between high-lying mesons in a typical decay process must be characteristically larger than the average of the coupling constants to mesons with nearby masses; the ratio of the square of the average coupling to the average of the coupling squared drops exponentially with the mass of the meson.

## Method

The authors consider a three-point correlation function for currents with mesonic quantum numbers, evaluated in two ways: as a quark loop (valid at sufficiently high masses and large averaging regions) and as a sum over narrow meson states (valid at sufficiently large \(N_c\)). They parameterize the meson coupling constants by an average value over many states times a state-specific factor \(R\), then match the averaged meson description to the quark-loop description via duality. The decay width for a typical meson is computed assuming the average coupling equals the individual coupling (\(R=1\)), and this result is compared to the string-theory prediction that the decay width grows linearly with mass.

## Key numbers

- The Hagedorn spectrum for the density of meson states at high excitation is given by \(\rho(m) = A (M/T_H)^{-2B} e^{M/T_H}\), where \(A\) and \(B\) are constants.
- The string-theory decay width for a meson is \(\Gamma_s(m) \sim \frac{1}{N_c} \frac{\Lambda}{\sigma} m\), where \(\Lambda\) is the QCD scale and \(\sigma\) is the string tension (independent of \(N_c\)).
- If \(R=1\) (individual coupling equals average), the decay width \(\Gamma(m_0,\lambda_0) = \frac{1}{N_c} \frac{1}{\rho_{a \lambda_0}(m_0)} F(m_0)\) would decrease exponentially with mass under a Hagedorn spectrum, contradicting the linear increase from string theory.
- To resolve the contradiction, \(|R(m_0)|^2 \sim m_0 \rho_{a \lambda_0}(m_0)\); with the Hagedorn spectrum, \(\rho_{a \lambda_0}(m_0)\) is exponentially increasing, so \(|R(m_0)|^2\) must also be exponentially increasing.

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N_c\) QCD framework of Witten (1979) and the concept of quark-hadron duality from Bloom and Gilman (1970) to the regime of highly excited mesons. It reconciles the Hagedorn spectrum (Hagedorn, 1965; Polchinski, 1991; Dienes, 1994) with the string-theory expectation that decay widths grow linearly with mass (Nussinov, 1981). The result enables future studies of how the required cancellations occur, either through rapidly oscillating phases of the coupling constants or through couplings that are nonzero only to an exponentially small fraction of final states, possibly related to the degenerate band structure of the string spectrum (Polchinski, 1991; Afonin, 2006).
