# Baryon masses, chiral extrapolations, and all that

> We calculate the baryon octet masses to fourth order in chiral perturbation theory employing dimensional and cut-off regularization. We analyze the pion and kaon mass dependences of the baryon masses based on the MILC data. We show that chiral perturbation theory gives stable chiral extrapolation functions for pion (kaon) masses below 550 (600) MeV. The pion-nucleon sigma term in SU(3) is also investigated, we find σ_{πN} (0) = 50.7 - 53.7 MeV.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory provides stable chiral extrapolation functions for baryon masses when pion (kaon) masses are below 550 (600) MeV, but only if the low-energy constants are adjusted from earlier determinations; the two lowest pion mass points of the more recent MILC data cannot be well described under the constraint that the nucleon mass passes through its physical value at the physical pion mass.

## Method

The authors calculated the baryon octet masses to third and fourth order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory using both dimensional regularization and cut-off regularization, with an improvement term at third order to cancel the leading cut-off dependence. They fixed the fourth-order low-energy constants by requiring the nucleon mass to pass through its physical value at the physical pion and kaon masses, then compared the resulting chiral extrapolation functions to lattice data from the MILC collaboration for the nucleon, Lambda, Sigma, and Xi masses as functions of pion and kaon masses.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon sigma term: sigma_{pi N}(0) = 50.7 to 53.7 MeV
- Chiral perturbation theory gives stable extrapolation functions for pion masses below 550 MeV and kaon masses below 600 MeV
- Baryon octet mass in the chiral limit lies in the interval 710 MeV less than or equal to m_0 less than or equal to 1070 MeV
- Physical nucleon mass: m_N = 940 MeV at physical pion mass M_pi = 140 MeV and physical kaon mass M_K = 494 MeV
- Pseudoscalar decay constant in the chiral limit: F_0 = 100 MeV
- Leading axial-vector couplings: D = 0.75 and F = 0.5

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor sector analysis of BHMcut (2004) to SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, building on earlier SU(3) calculations by BM (1997) and FM (2004). The authors analyze lattice data from the MILC collaboration (2004) and compare with prior determinations of low-energy constants from BM (1997). The results enable chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD baryon mass calculations to physical quark masses, though the authors note that finite volume, finite lattice spacing, and staggered approximation effects were ignored in this study and should be included in future work.
