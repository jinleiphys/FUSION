# Bjorken Flow of the quark-gluon plasma and Gauge/Gravity Correspondence

> The contribution presents a brief summary of the Gauge/Gravity approach to the study of hydrodynamic flow of the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions, in a boost-invariant setting (Bjorken flow). Considering the ideal case of a supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory for which the AdS/CFT correspondence gives a precise form of the Gauge/Gravity duality, the properties of the strongly coupled expanding plasma are put in one-to-one correspondence with the metric of a 5-dimensional black hole with the horizon moving away in the 5th dimension and its deformations consistent with the relevant Einstein equations. Several recently studied aspects of this framework are recalled and put in perspective. New results in collaboration with G.Beuf and M.Heller on the early time expansion towards the hydrodynamical regime are provided giving a new insight on the far-from-equilibrium behavior of the fluid at strong coupling and the thermalization and isotropization problems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Gauge/Gravity duality, specifically the AdS/CFT correspondence applied to a supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, can describe the hydrodynamic flow of a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma in a boost-invariant (Bjorken) setting, but the early-time far-from-equilibrium behavior depends on initial conditions and lacks the universal scaling found at late times.

## Method

The authors use holographic renormalization to relate the 4-dimensional stress-energy tensor of the plasma to the metric of a 5-dimensional Anti de Sitter (AdS) space. For late times, they solve the Einstein equations in Fefferman-Graham coordinates under boost-invariant symmetry, finding a black brane solution with a moving horizon. For early times, they solve the full nonlinear Einstein equations as a power series with an ansatz for initial conditions, analyzing the energy density and pressure anisotropy.

## Key numbers

* The perfect fluid (Bjorken flow) corresponds to an energy density scaling as eps(tau) proportional to tau^{-4/3}, with s = 4/3.
* At late times, the black hole horizon scales as z_h proportional to tau^{1/3}, the temperature scales as tau^{-1/3}, and the entropy (proportional to area) scales as tau * 1/z_h^3 = constant.
* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is eta/s = 1/(4 pi), derived from the dissipative corrections to the perfect fluid flow.

## Context

This work extends the Gauge/Gravity approach to hydrodynamic flow of the quark-gluon plasma, building on the AdS/CFT correspondence established by Maldacena (1998) and the holographic renormalization framework of Skenderis (2002). The late-time results reproduce the ideal Bjorken flow and known viscosity results from static calculations. The new early-time results by Beuf, Heller, and the authors provide insight into the far-from-equilibrium regime and the thermalization and isotropization problems, though a complete numerical solution of Einstein's equations is still needed for firm conclusions.
