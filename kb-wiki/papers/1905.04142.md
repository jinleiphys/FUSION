# Is the structure of 42Si understood?

> A more detailed test of the implementation of nuclear forces that drive shell evolution in the pivotal nucleus \nuc{42}{Si} -- going beyond earlier comparisons of excited-state energies -- is important. The two leading shell-model effective interactions, SDPF-MU and SDPF-U-Si, both of which reproduce the low-lying \nuc{42}{Si}($2^+_1$) energy, but whose predictions for other observables differ significantly, are interrogated by the population of states in neutron-rich \nuc{42}{Si} with a one-proton removal reaction from \nuc{43}{P} projectiles at 81~MeV/nucleon. The measured cross sections to the individual \nuc{42}{Si} final states are compared to calculations that combine eikonal reaction dynamics with these shell-model nuclear structure overlaps. The differences in the two shell-model descriptions are examined and linked to predicted low-lying excited $0^+$ states and shape coexistence. Based on the present data, which are in better agreement with the SDPF-MU calculations, the state observed at 2150(13)~keV in \nuc{42}{Si} is proposed to be the ($0^+_2$) level.

## Key claim

The one-proton removal cross sections from 43P to 42Si are better described by the SDPF-MU shell-model interaction than by SDPF-U-Si, but the paper's conclusion is hedged: the authors state that 42Si is "not yet sufficiently understood" because the two leading shell-model effective interactions predict starkly different observables beyond the 2+1 energy, and the data only favor SDPF-MU over SDPF-U-Si without fully resolving the underlying physics.

## Method

A 140 MeV/u 48Ca primary beam was fragmented on a 1363 mg/cm2 9Be production target, and the A1900 separator selected a secondary beam of 43P at 81 MeV/nucleon. This beam impinged on a 476 mg/cm2 9Be reaction target at the S800 spectrograph, and prompt gamma rays from the one-proton knockout reaction 9Be(43P,42Si+gamma) were detected with the GRETINA array of 40 segmented HPGe crystals. Partial cross sections to individual 42Si final states were extracted from efficiency-corrected gamma-ray peak areas with discrete feeding subtracted, then compared to eikonal reaction calculations using spectroscopic factors from the SDPF-MU and SDPF-U-Si shell-model effective interactions.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive one-proton removal cross section from 43P to 42Si: sigma_inc = 3.4(2) mb
- Energy of the 2+1 to 0+1 transition in 42Si: 737(8) keV
- Energy of the proposed 0+2 state in 42Si: 2150(13) keV
- Higher-energy gamma-ray transitions observed in 42Si: 1413(10), 2037(10), 2351(10), and 2743(10) keV
- Reduction factor R_s = 0.33(2) deduced from measured and calculated cross sections using SDPF-MU, with Delta S = 15.6 MeV
- Neutron separation energy of 42Si: S_n = 3721(747) keV
- SDPF-U-Si predicts 4(3) 0+ states below 4(2.5) MeV excitation energy, while SDPF-MU predicts 3(1) 0+ states in the same energy windows

## Context

This work extends the earlier spectroscopy of 42Si by Takeuchi et al. (2012), who used a two-proton removal reaction and suggested a 4+1 state at 2173(14) keV, and by Tostevin et al. (2013), who could not reconcile the gamma-ray spectra with shell-model calculations and proposed one-proton removal to clarify the situation. The present results favor the SDPF-MU interaction (Utsuno et al., 2012) over SDPF-U-Si (Nowacki et al., 2009) and enable future tests of whether the 2150(13) keV state is indeed the 0+2 level, which would require high-statistics parallel momentum distribution measurements. The findings also highlight the difficulty in extrapolating configuration-interaction calculations toward the neutron dripline, particularly for 40Mg (Crawford et al., 2019), where continuum effects may be important.
