# Unveiling potential neutron halos in intermediate-mass nuclei: an \textit{ab initio} study

> Halos epitomize the fascinating interplay between weak binding, shell evolution, and deformation effects, especially in nuclei near the drip line. In this Letter, we apply the state-of-the-art \textit{ab initio} valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group approach to predict potential candidates for one- and two-neutron halo in the intermediate-mass region. Notably, we use spectroscopic factors (SF) and two-nucleon amplitudes (TNA) as criteria for suggesting one- and two-neutron halo candidates, respectively. This approach is not only theoretically sound but also amenable to experimental validation. Our research focuses on Mg, Al, Si, P, and S neutron-drip-line nuclei, offering systematic predictions of neutron halo candidates in terms of separation energies, SF (TNA), and average occupation. The calculation suggests the ground states of $^{40,42,44,46}$Al, $^{41,43,45,47}$Si, $^{46,48}$P, and $^{47,49}$S are promising candidates for one-neutron halos, while $^{40,42,44,46}$Mg, $^{45,47}$Al, $^{46,48}$Si, $^{49}$P, and $^{50}$S may harbor two-neutron halos. In addition, the relative mean-square neutron radius between halo nuclei and \textit{inner core} is calculated for suggested potential neutron halos. Finally, the relations of halo formations and shell evolution are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors use spectroscopic factors (SF) and two-nucleon amplitudes (TNA) as criteria for suggesting one- and two-neutron halo candidates, rather than relying solely on root-mean-square radii or density distributions; they qualify that nuclei with separation energies larger than -1 MeV are still considered weakly bound and meet the condition for neutron halos despite being formally unbound, accounting for theoretical uncertainties and the absence of continuum coupling.

## Method

The authors apply the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) approach with chiral effective field theory NN+3N interactions (EM1.8/2.0 and NN+3N lnl) to generate valence-space effective Hamiltonians for a valence space of protons in the sd shell and neutrons in the pf shell above the 28O inner core. They then exactly diagonalize these Hamiltonians using the large-scale shell model code KSHELL to calculate separation energies, spectroscopic factors, two-nucleon amplitudes, and average occupations for Mg, Al, Si, P, and S neutron-drip-line nuclei. The calculations use a harmonic-oscillator basis at ħω = 16 MeV with emax = 14 and E3max = 14.

## Key numbers

- The one-neutron separation energy threshold for potential halos is set at Sn < 1 MeV, slightly above the approximately 900 keV Sn for the p-wave halo in 29Ne
- The two-neutron separation energy window considered is -1 ≤ S2n ≤ 1.4 MeV, referencing established halos 17B (S2n = 1.384(0.205) MeV) and 29F (S2n = 1.126(0.539) MeV)
- For 37Mg, the calculated spectroscopic factors are 0.272 for the 1/2− ground state and 0.267 for the 3/2−1 state, with closely aligned states
- The relative mean-square neutron radius ΔRn for the 1n halo candidate 37Mg is 0.644 fm (1/2−g.s.) and 0.655 fm (3/2−1)
- The relative mean-square neutron radius ΔR2n for the 2n halo candidate 42Mg is 0.909 fm (0+g.s.)
- The relative mean-square neutron radius ΔR2n for the 2n halo candidate 40Mg is 0.825 fm (0+g.s.)

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron halos from light nuclei (such as 6He, 11Li, 11Be, 29F, and 37Mg identified in prior experimental work) into the intermediate-mass region of Mg, Al, Si, P, and S isotopes. The approach using spectroscopic factors and two-nucleon amplitudes as halo indicators differs from traditional methodologies relying on root-mean-square radii (as in Li (2022) and others) and density distributions. The predictions enable future experimental validation through direct mass measurements, spectroscopic factor measurements, and two-nucleon amplitude measurements, which could provide crucial insights into halo phenomena in medium-mass nuclei and the interplay between shell evolution and halo formation.
