# Nuclear Many-Body Theory of Electroweak Interactions with Nuclei at Intermediate Energies

> The Quasi-Elastic (QE) contribution of the nuclear inclusive electron model developed in reference ¥cite{GNO97} is extended to the study of electroweak Charged Current (CC) induced nuclear reactions at intermediate energies of interest for future neutrino oscillation experiments. Long range nuclear (RPA) correlations, Final State Interaction (FSI) and Coulomb corrections are included within the model. RPA correlations are shown to play a crucial role in the whole range of neutrino energies, up to 500 MeV, studied in this work. Predictions for inclusive muon capture for different nuclei through the Periodic Table and for the reactions $^{12}$C $(ﾎｽ_ﾎｼ,ﾎｼ^-)X$ and $^{12}$C $(ﾎｽ_e,e^-)X$ near threshold are also given.

## Key claim

The quasi-elastic contribution of a previously developed nuclear inclusive electron model is extended to charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions at intermediate energies, with the claim that long-range RPA correlations play a crucial role across the entire neutrino energy range studied (up to 500 MeV), though the model's predictions for inclusive muon capture show discrepancies of up to about 15% for some nuclei.

## Method

The authors extend the inclusive electron scattering model of GNO97 to charged-current neutrino reactions by computing the imaginary part of the neutrino self-energy in nuclear matter via Cutkosky's rules, using a local Fermi gas description of the nucleus. They incorporate long-range RPA correlations through a series of particle-hole and Delta-hole excitations with an effective Landau-Migdal interaction, include final state interactions by dressing nucleon propagators with realistic energy- and momentum-dependent self-energies, and account for Coulomb corrections via a lepton self-energy term.

## Context

This work extends the inclusive electron scattering model of GNO97 and the photo-nuclear model of CO92 to charged-current neutrino reactions, building on a nuclear microscopic framework developed in OTW82 and earlier pion-nucleus studies. The model successfully describes LSND measurements of $^{12}$C($¥nu_¥mu,¥mu^-$)X near threshold and provides one of the best combined descriptions of inclusive muon capture across the Periodic Table, though the authors note that experimental muon capture data have precisions smaller than 1%, far exceeding the theoretical uncertainties of any existing model.
