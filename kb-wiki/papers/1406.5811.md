# Do we see change of phase in proton-proton collisions at the Large Hadron Collider?

> High multiplicity events in proton-proton collisions at the Large Hadron Collider exhibit features resembling those found in relativistic nuclear collisions, indicating formation of medium of similar nature. Analyzing the proton-proton collision data, we find a clear signal of occurrence of change in phase between thermalized partonic matter and hadronic one as has been predicted by the lattice quantum chromodynamics.

## Key claim
The authors find a signal consistent with a crossover phase change between thermalized partonic matter and hadronic matter in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC, but they explicitly state this cannot be considered a "smoking gun" and that the result reveals a new feature rather than definitive proof.

## Method
The authors analyzed identified particle transverse momentum spectra (pions, kaons, protons) from CMS data at √s = 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV, fitting the transverse mass spectra to extract inverse slope parameters. They computed the effective number of degrees of freedom as σ/⟨pT⟩³ (where σ is entropy density estimated via Bjorken's formalism and ⟨pT⟩ is mean transverse momentum) and studied its dependence on ⟨pT⟩ separately for pions and kaons, using HBT radii to parameterize the interaction area. Results were compared with PYTHIA8.1 Tune 4C simulations (with and without Color Reconnection) and with pPb collision data at √s = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers
- CMS measured pT ranges: π± from 0.1 to 1.2 GeV/c, K± from 0.2 to 1.050 GeV/c, p and p̄ from 0.35 to 1.7 GeV/c
- High multiplicity pp events at √s = 7 TeV have d⟨Nch⟩/dη up to ≈30
- The radius parameterization used: R(⟨Nch⟩) = a·⟨Nch⟩^{1/3} with a = 0.504 ± 0.0097 fm
- χ² values for mT fits: pions 0.3-2.48, kaons 0.26-1.08, protons 0.06-4.92
- pions with pT < 0.45 GeV/c were excluded from fitting to avoid resonance decay effects

## Context
This work extends prior searches for QGP in pp collisions, particularly studies by Van Hove (1982) proposing ⟨pT⟩ as a temperature probe and LQCD calculations predicting a crossover (Borsanyi et al., 2010). It builds on observations of collectivity in high-multiplicity pp events at the LHC (CMS, ALICE collaborations) and earlier attempts to find phase change signals in pp̄ collisions. The result enables further characterization of the medium formed in high-multiplicity pp events at higher LHC energies (Run-II starting 2015) and suggests species-dependent analysis as a tool for heavy-ion collisions.
