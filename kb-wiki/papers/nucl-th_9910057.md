# Matter-induced hadronic processes

> Two examples of ``exotic'' phenomena which become possible and important in the presence of nuclear matter are discussed: omega -> pi pi decay, and rho-omega mixing. Significance of these processes for the low-mass dilepton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is indicated.

# Matter-induced hadronic processes

## Key claim

The paper argues that two "exotic" hadronic processes forbidden or negligible in vacuum, the decay ω → ππ and ρ-ω mixing, become large and phenomenologically important in nuclear matter, but only under specific conditions: the ω meson must have a momentum above approximately 200 MeV relative to the medium for the decay to occur, and the ρ-ω mixing requires an asymmetry between neutron and proton densities in the nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors calculate the in-medium ω → ππ decay width using an effective hadronic theory with mesons (ω, σ, π) interacting with nucleons and Δ(1232) baryons, working to leading order in nuclear density. They evaluate four classes of Feynman diagrams (bubble and triangle diagrams) in the rest frame of nuclear matter, using in-medium nucleon propagators with a Fermi momentum cutoff. For ρ-ω mixing in asymmetric nuclear matter, they compute the off-diagonal polarization tensor from proton and neutron loop diagrams, parameterize the diagonal parts of the vector-meson correlator with in-medium masses, widths, and wave-function renormalization factors, and solve for the mixed eigenstates and mixing angles.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum partial width for ω → ππ is only ~0.2 MeV (tiny isospin-violation effect)
- For ω moving with momentum above ~200 MeV at nuclear saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³), the matter-induced width reaches ~100 MeV
- The σ meson parameters used in the calculation are m_σ = 789 MeV and Γ_σ = 237 MeV, fitted to reproduce ππ scattering at q² = m_ω² = (780 MeV)²
- The ωNN coupling constant used is g_ω = 9; the πNN coupling constant is g_πNN = 12.7; the πNΔ coupling constant is f_πNΔ = 2.1
- When the in-medium ω mass is reduced to m*_ω = 0.70 m_ω, the calculated width decreases by a factor of 2.5 but remains substantial
- For ρ-ω mixing at ρ_B = 2ρ₀ with M*/M = 0.5 and asymmetry x = x_Pb (as in ²⁰⁸Pb), mixing angles are of order 10° or larger across 8 representative parameter cases
- The mixing angle Re(θ₁) ranges from 3° to 45° depending on input parameters (masses, widths, and wave-function renormalization)
- The HADES spectrometer at GSI is anticipated to achieve mass resolution of 1% in the relevant region

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Wolf, Friman, and Soyeur (1998), who first pointed out the significance of ω-σ mixing for in-medium ω → ππ decay, by including additional triangle diagrams involving Δ(1232) resonances. The ρ-ω mixing study extends the work of Dutt-Mazumder, Dutta-Roy, and Kundu (1997), who studied isospin asymmetry effects in the Walecka model and found mixing angles of about 2% at nuclear saturation density; the present authors find much larger mixing (angles of order 10°). The results are relevant for interpreting low-mass dilepton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly the enhanced dilepton yield observed by the CERES and HELIOS-3 collaborations, and may help improve agreement between transport calculations and data by accounting for the increased hadronic width of the ω meson.
