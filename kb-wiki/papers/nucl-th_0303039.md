# Generalized contour deformation method in momentum space: two-body spectral structures and scattering amplitudes

> A generalized contour deformation method (GCDM) which combines complex rotation and translation in momentum space, is discussed. GCDM gives accurate results for bound, virtual (antibound), resonant and scattering states starting with a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. It provides a basis for full off-shell $t$-matrix calculations both for real and complex input energies. Results for both spectral structures and scattering amplitudes compare perfectly well with exact values for the separable Yamaguchi potential. Accurate calculation of virtual states in the Malfliet-Tjon and the realistic CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interactions are presented. GCDM is also a promising method for the computation of in-medium properties such as the resummation of particle-particle and particle-hole diagrams in infinite nuclear matter. Implications for in-medium scattering are discussed.

## Key claim

The Generalized Contour Deformation Method (GCDM), which combines complex rotation and translation in momentum space, can accurately compute bound, virtual (antibound), resonant, and scattering states for realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, including virtual states that are inaccessible to standard contour rotation methods because the maximum rotation angle does not allow rotation into the third quadrant of the complex momentum plane.

## Method

The authors transform the momentum-space Schrodinger equation onto a distorted integration contour in the complex momentum plane, using either a simple rotation (contour C1+) or a rotation followed by translation (contour C2+). The rotated-plus-translated contour enables exposure of virtual states on the negative imaginary k-axis while avoiding singularities of the potential. The full off-shell t-matrix is then computed either via the spectral representation expanded on the deformed-contour basis or via the Fredholm (Lippmann-Schwinger) equation with principal-value prescription, with the GCDM providing non-singular kernels for complex input energies.

## Key numbers

- For the Yamaguchi s-wave potential, the t-matrix pole condition gives bound and virtual states at κ = -β ± √(λ/(2β)), with a bound state (κ > 0) and virtual state (κ ≤ 0) for λ > 2β³, and two virtual states for λ ≤ 2β³.
- For the Yamaguchi p-wave potential, the bound state condition is λ > 2β, with the interaction supporting bound, virtual, and resonant states; the pole equation yields κ = λ/2 - β ± (1/4)√(4λ² - 8λβ).
- The method is tested against exact analytical results for the separable Yamaguchi potential and applied to the Malfliet-Tjon and CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interactions, with accurate calculation of virtual states for the CD-Bonn interaction reported as not previously performed.

## Context

This work extends the contour deformation method (CDM) previously applied in nuclear physics by Brayshaw (1960s), Nuttall, and Stelbovics, who used only contour rotation. The GCDM overcomes the limitation that pure rotation cannot expose virtual states, which was used as an argument for alternative approaches such as the analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) method of Aoyama. The method enables full off-shell t-matrix calculations for complex energies relevant to in-medium scattering in infinite nuclear matter, such as resummation of particle-particle and particle-hole diagrams.
