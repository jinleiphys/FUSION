# Elliptic Flow of Protons and Antiprotons in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7--62.4 GeV within Alternative Scenarios of Three-Fluid Dynamics

> Analysis of elliptic flow of protons and antiprotons in Au+Au collisions is performed in a wide range of incident energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7--62.4 GeV. Simulations has been done within the three-fluid model employing a purely hadronic equation of state (EoS) and two versions of the EoS involving deconfinement transition: an EoS with the first-order phase transition and that with a smooth crossover transition. It is found that the proton data are reproduced approximately to the same extent within all of the scenarios, including the hadronic one, while the deconfinement scenarios look certainly preferable for the antiproton elliptic flow. The fact that difference between elliptic flows of protons and antiprotons decreases with the incident energy rise is a consequence of reducing baryon stopping rather than an onset of deconfinement.

# Elliptic Flow of Protons and Antiprotons in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7–62.4 GeV within Alternative Scenarios of Three-Fluid Dynamics

## Key claim

The proton elliptic flow data are reproduced approximately equally well by all three equation-of-state scenarios (purely hadronic, first-order phase transition, and smooth crossover), while the deconfinement scenarios are clearly preferable for antiproton elliptic flow. The decreasing difference between proton and antiproton elliptic flow with increasing incident energy is attributed to reduced baryon stopping rather than to the onset of deconfinement.

## Method

Simulations were performed using the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats projectile, target, and fireball fluids separately to account for finite stopping power. Three equations of state were employed: a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with a first-order phase transition (2-phase EoS), and an EoS with a smooth crossover transition. The calculated elliptic flow values were rescaled using the ratio of eccentricities from realistic nuclear surface diffuseness (d=0.6 fm) to the sharp-edged sphere approximation (d=0) used in the 3FD model, because the model overestimates spatial eccentricity.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range: $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7–62.4 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Centrality bins analyzed: 0%–10% (b=3 fm), 10%–40% (b=6 fm), and 40%–80% (b=8 fm)
- Nuclear radius parameter: $R_A = 1.12 A^{1/3}$ with diffuseness d=0.6 fm for realistic eccentricity calculations
- At 62.4 GeV, proton and antiproton elliptic flows nearly coincide, indicating near-complete separation of baryon-rich fluids from the fireball fluid in rapidity
- At 7.7 GeV, the model predicts irregular behavior within deconfinement scenarios that does not agree with the data

## Context

This work extends the three-fluid dynamics analysis of heavy-ion collisions previously reported by Ivanov (2013) and Ivanov (2012) on baryon stopping and particle production, and by the same author on transverse flow at AGS and SPS energies using only the hadronic EoS. The results contradict the interpretation of the particle-antiparticle $v_2$ difference as evidence for deconfinement, instead supporting an explanation based on nuclear stopping and the three-fluid structure. The study enables future incorporation of viscosity and kinetic afterburner corrections, which are absent in the current model and would likely reduce the systematic overestimation of the data.
