# Quantum statistics effects and fluctuations of particle numbers near the critical point of nuclear matter

> Equation of state with quantum statistics corrections is derived for a multi-component gas of particles interacting through the repulsive and attractive van der Waals (vdW) forces up to first few orders over a small parameter $δ\approx \hbar^3 n(mT)^{-3/2}[g(1- bn)]^{-1}$, where $n$ and $T$ are the particle number density and temperature, $m$ and $g$ the particle mass and degeneracy factor. The parameter $b$ corresponds to the vdW excluded volume. For interacting system of Fermi nucleon and Bose $α$ particles, a small impurity of $α$ particles to the nucleon system at leading first order in both $α$ particle and nucleon small parameters $δ$ does not change much the basic results for the symmetric nuclear matter. The particle number fluctuations $ω$ determined by the isothermal in-compressibility $\mathcal{K}(n,T)$ can be obtained analytically at the same first order quantum-statistics approximation for symmetric nucleon matter. Our approximate analytical results appear to be in good agreement with the accurate numerical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper derives an equation of state with quantum statistics corrections for a multi-component van der Waals gas, but the key hedge is that the analytical first-order expansion in a small parameter δ works only for small temperatures (T ≲ 30 MeV) and not-too-large particle densities, and that the small impurity of α particles (Xα ≈ 0.013) does not change the basic results for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors start from the grand canonical ensemble expressions for ideal Fermi and Bose gases, then expand the pressure and particle number density in power series over a small quantum statistics parameter δ ≈ ħ³ n (mT)⁻³/² [g(1 − bn)]⁻¹. They incorporate van der Waals interactions through the repulsive excluded volume parameters bij and attractive parameters aij, and apply the expansion up to first order in δ for a mixed system of nucleons (Fermi) and α particles (Bose). The critical point parameters are obtained by solving the simultaneous equations (∂P/∂n)T = 0 and (∂²P/∂n²)T = 0 at first order, and particle number fluctuations ω are computed from the isothermal susceptibility χ = (∂n/∂μ)T.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter in the classical van der Waals model: Tc = 29.2 MeV
- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter with first-order quantum statistics correction: Tc = 19.0 MeV
- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter from exact numerical QvdWM: Tc = 19.7 MeV
- Critical density for symmetric nuclear matter in the classical van der Waals model: nc = 0.100 fm⁻³
- Critical density for symmetric nuclear matter with first-order quantum statistics correction: nc = 0.065 fm⁻³
- Critical density for symmetric nuclear matter from exact numerical QvdWM: nc = 0.072 fm⁻³
- Critical pressure for symmetric nuclear matter in the classical van der Waals model: Pc = 1.09 MeV·fm⁻³
- Critical pressure for symmetric nuclear matter with first-order quantum statistics correction: Pc = 0.48 MeV·fm⁻³
- Critical pressure for symmetric nuclear matter from exact numerical QvdWM: Pc = 0.52 MeV·fm⁻³
- For the mixed N+α system with Xα = 0.013, first-order QS correction gives Tc = 19.4 MeV, nc = 0.072 fm⁻³, Pc = 0.51 MeV·fm⁻³
- For the mixed N+α system with Xα = 0.013, exact QvdWM gives Tc = 19.9 MeV, nc = 0.073 fm⁻³, Pc = 0.56 MeV·fm⁻³
- van der Waals parameters: aNN = 329.8 MeV·fm³, bNN = 3.35 fm³, bαα = 16.76 fm³, bαN = 13.95 fm³, bNα = 2.85 fm³
- Impurity ratio: Xα = 0.013 (approximately 1.3% α-particle impurity)
- Nucleon mass: mN = 938 MeV; α-particle mass: mα = 3727 MeV
- Nucleon degeneracy factor: gN = 4; α-particle degeneracy factor: gα = 1

## Context

This work extends the van der Waals model with quantum statistics corrections previously developed for pure nucleon matter (FMG19) to a mixed system of nucleons and α particles, and applies the analytical first-order results to particle number fluctuations near the critical point. The approach is contrasted with the classical van der Waals model (LLv5) and with numerical QvdWM results from Marik and Vovchenko (vova). The paper enables analytical calculations of fluctuations ω from the isothermal in-compressibility K(n,T) without full numerical integration, and suggests that higher-order quantum corrections beyond first order can be neglected for practical evaluations of critical point parameters.
