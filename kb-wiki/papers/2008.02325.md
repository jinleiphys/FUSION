# Effects of QCD critical point on light nuclei production

> Using the nucleon coalescence model, which can naturally take into account the correlations in the nucleon density distribution, we study the effects of QCD critical point on light nuclei production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We find that the yield ratio $N_tN_p/N_d^2$ of proton ($p$), deuteron ($d$) and triton ($t$) increases monotonically with the nucleon density correlation length, which is expected to increase significantly near the critical point in the QCD phase diagram. Our study thus demonstrates that the yield ratio $N_tN_p/N_d^2$ can be used as a sensitive probe of the QCD critical phenomenon. We further discuss the relation between the QCD phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions and the possible non-monotonic behavior of \pdt~in its collision energy dependence.

## Key claim

The yield ratio \(N_t N_p / N_d^2\) of proton, deuteron, and triton increases monotonically with the nucleon density correlation length \(\xi\), which is expected to become large near the QCD critical point; however, the enhancement saturates for \(\xi \gg \sigma_d\) (the deuteron size scale), so the ratio does not diverge even if the correlation length diverges.

## Method

The authors used the nucleon coalescence model, in which the yields of deuterons and tritons are computed from the overlap of nucleon phase-space distributions with Gaussian Wigner functions of the nuclei. They introduced a two-nucleon density correlation function \(C_2(\mathbf{x}_1,\mathbf{x}_2)\) that takes the Ornsteinâ€“Zernike form \(\lambda \langle\rho_n\rangle\langle\rho_p\rangle e^{-|\mathbf{x}_1-\mathbf{x}_2|/\xi} / |\mathbf{x}_1-\mathbf{x}_2|^{1+\eta}\) near the critical point, with the anomalous exponent \(\eta\) neglected. By expanding the joint density distributions to include these correlations, they derived analytic expressions for the deuteron and triton numbers and formed the yield ratio, keeping only leading-order terms in the correlation function.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron Wigner function width parameter \(\sigma_d \approx 2.26\) fm, related to the deuteron root-mean-square radius \(r_d\) by \(\sigma_d = \sqrt{4/3}\, r_d\).
- The triton Wigner function width parameter \(\sigma_t = r_t = 1.59\) fm.
- The intrinsic resolution scale for light-nucleus production is \(\Delta x \sim 2\) fm and \(\Delta p \sim 100\) MeV.
- The correlation length \(\xi\) that could develop in heavy-ion collisions is limited to about 2â€“3 fm due to critical slowing down (Berdnikov 1999).
- The function \(G(\xi/\sigma)\) saturates to \(\sqrt{2/\pi}\) for \(\xi \gg \sigma_d\); for small \(\xi\) it increases as \(\xi^2\), and near \(\xi \sim \sigma_d\) it increases linearly with \(\xi\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Sun, Ko, and Li (2017, 2018) that showed the yield ratio \(N_t N_p / N_d^2\) is enhanced by neutron density fluctuations \(\Delta\rho_n\) from a first-order phase transition. The present paper adds the effect of long-range spatial correlations characteristic of the QCD critical endpoint, providing an explicit relation between the ratio and the correlation length \(\xi\). It also generalizes the result to heavier nuclei such as \(^4\)He. The authors note that transport models without a critical point (e.g., Liu 2019, Sun 2020) give an energy-independent constant for this ratio and fail to describe the preliminary STAR data, whereas a multi-phase transport model with a first-order transition (Sun 2020) can produce an enhancement. The work enables future extraction of the correlation length and density fluctuations from precise measurements of light-nucleus yields in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, FAIR, NICA, HIAF, and J-PARC.
