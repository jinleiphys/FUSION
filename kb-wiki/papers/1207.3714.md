# Chiral Transition Within Effective Quark Models Under Magnetic Fields

> We consider the simplest versions of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and the Linear Sigma Model (LSM), in the Mean Field Approximation (MFA), in order to analyze hot and dense two flavor quark matter subject to strong magnetic fields. We pay especial attention to the case of a finite chemical potential, which has not yet been fully explored. Our results, for the NJL model, are in qualitative agreement with other recent applications showing that, for stronger fields, the first order segment of the transition line increases with the magnetic strength while the coexistence chemical potential value, at low temperatures, decreases. In the present work, one of the most important results is related to the analysis of how these features affect the phase coexistence region in the $T-ρ_B$ plane. We find that the coexistence boundary oscillates around the B=0 value for magnetic fields of the order $eB \lesssim 9.5\, m_π^2$ which can be understood by investigating the filling of Landau levels at vanishing temperature. So far, most investigations have been concerned with the effects of the magnetic field over the $T-μ$ plane only while other thermodynamical quantities such as the adiabats, the quark number susceptibility, the interaction measure and the latent heat have been neglected. Here, we take a step towards filling this gap by investigating the influence of a magnetic field over these quantities. Finally, we argue that a naive application of the MFA does not seem to be appropriate to treat the LSM in the presence of magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The authors find that within the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the Mean Field Approximation (MFA), the coexistence boundary in the temperature–baryon density plane oscillates around its zero-field value for magnetic fields up to approximately \(eB \lesssim 9.5\, m_\pi^2\), an effect not previously reported; they also argue that a naive MFA treatment of the Linear Sigma Model (LSM) is inadequate for describing the chiral transition under magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors compute the free energy of the two-flavor NJL model and the LSM in the MFA, including vacuum, magnetic, and medium contributions, with a sharp cutoff \(\Lambda = 590\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the NJL and two parameter sets for the LSM (yielding \(m_\sigma = 600\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(m_\sigma = 450\ \mathrm{MeV}\)). They solve the gap equation for the effective quark mass self-consistently at finite temperature \(T\), chemical potential \(\mu\), and magnetic field \(B\), then derive the pressure, baryon density, entropy, quark number susceptibility, interaction measure, and latent heat.

## Key numbers

* Critical endpoint for the NJL at \(B=0\): \(T_c \simeq 78.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\mu_c \simeq 328\ \mathrm{MeV}\); at \(eB = 6\, m_\pi^2\): \(T_c \simeq 81.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\mu_c \simeq 318\ \mathrm{MeV}\); at \(eB = 15\, m_\pi^2\): \(T_c \simeq 115.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\mu_c \simeq 279.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Vacuum effective quark mass in the NJL at \(T=0\), \(\mu=0\): \(M^H \simeq 403\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(B=0\), \(M^H \simeq 416\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(eB = 6\, m_\pi^2\), \(M^H \simeq 467\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(eB = 15\, m_\pi^2\).
* Coexistence chemical potential at \(T=0\) in the NJL: \(\mu \simeq 383\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(B=0\), \(\mu \simeq 370\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(eB = 6\, m_\pi^2\), \(\mu \simeq 339\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(eB = 15\, m_\pi^2\).
* Oscillations in the coexistence boundary and latent heat at \(T=0\) persist for \(eB \lesssim 9.5\, m_\pi^2\); the latent heat \(\Delta \mathcal{E}\) oscillates around its \(B=0\) value in this range.
* Nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) used as unit for baryon density.
* LSM parameters: \(f_\pi = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_\pi = 138\ \mathrm{MeV}\); first parameter set gives \(m_\sigma = 600\ \mathrm{MeV}\), second gives \(m_\sigma = 450\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram under magnetic fields, which had mostly focused on the \(T-\mu\) plane, by analyzing the coexistence and spinodal boundaries, the interaction measure, the quark number susceptibility, and the latent heat in the NJL model. It builds on the NJL and LSM calculations of Inagaki et al. (2004) and the three-flavor NJL results of Nois et al. (2012), and contrasts with the LSM-MFA results of Andersen and Khan (2012) by showing that the MFA fails to reproduce the expected inverse magnetic catalysis in the LSM. The findings enable more detailed comparisons with lattice QCD and with future heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, FAIR, and NICA.
