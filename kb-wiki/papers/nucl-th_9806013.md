# Novel approach to pion and eta production in proton-proton collisions near threshold

> We evaluate the threshold matrix-element for the reaction $pp \to ppπ^0$ in a fully relativistic Feynman diagrammatic approach. We employ a simple effective range approximation to take care of the S-wave $pp$ final-state interaction. The experimental value for the threshold amplitude ${\cal A} = (2.7 - i 0.3)$ fm$^4$ can be reproduced by contributions from tree level chiral (long-range) pion exchange and short-range effects related to vector meson exchanges, with $ω$ exchange giving the largest individual contribution. Pion loop effects appear to be small. We stress that the commonly used heavy baryon formalism is not applicable in the NN-system above the pion production threshold due to the large external momentum, $|\vec p | \simeq \sqrt {Mm_π}$, with $M$ and $m_π$ the nucleon and the pion mass, respectively. We furthermore investigate the reaction $pp\to p n π^+$ near threshold within the same approach. We extract from the data the triplet threshold amplitude as ${\cal B}= (2.8 -i 1.5)$ fm$^4$. Its real part can be well understood from (relativistic) tree level meson-exchange diagrams. In addition, we investigate the process $pp \to pp η$ near threshold. We use a simple factorization ansatz for the $ppη$ final-state interaction and extract from the data the modulus of the threshold amplitude, $|{\cal C}| = 1.32 $fm$^4$. With $g_{ηN}=5.3$, this value can be reproduced by (relativistic) tree level meson-exchange diagrams and $η$-rescattering, whose strength is fixed by the $ηN$ scattering length. We also comment on the recent near threshold data for $η'$-production.

## Key claim

The threshold amplitude for \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) can be reproduced by a fully relativistic Feynman diagram calculation combining tree-level chiral pion exchange and short-range vector meson exchanges, contrary to the widespread belief that this channel is chirally suppressed. The commonly used heavy baryon formalism (HBChPT) is not applicable for \(NN\) systems above the pion production threshold because the external momentum \(|\vec p| \simeq \sqrt{M m_\pi}\) diverges in the heavy nucleon limit, making the \(1/M\) expansion invalid.

## Method

The authors performed a fully relativistic Feynman diagram calculation of tree-level and one-loop diagrams for \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) and \(pp \to pn\pi^+\) at threshold, using pseudovector \(\pi NN\) and \(\eta NN\) vertices and a second-order chiral \(\pi\pi NN\) contact interaction. The S-wave \(pp\) final-state interaction was treated via an effective range approximation (Watson's approach) with scattering length \(a_p = 7.8098\) fm and effective range \(r_p = 2.767\) fm, allowing extraction of the constant threshold amplitude from total cross section data. For \(pp \to pp\eta\), a factorization ansatz for the \(pp\eta\) final-state interaction was employed, with \(\eta\)-rescattering strength fixed by the \(\eta N\) scattering length.

## Key numbers

- Threshold amplitude for \(pp \to pp\pi^0\): \(\mathcal{A} = (2.7 - i\,0.3)\) fm\(^4\), extracted from data up to \(T_{\rm lab} = 326\) MeV.
- Proton center-of-mass momentum at threshold: \(|\vec p| = 362.2\) MeV, from \(|\vec p| = \sqrt{m_\pi (M + m_\pi/4)}\) with \(M = 938.27\) MeV and \(m_\pi = 134.97\) MeV.
- Tree-level direct \(\pi^0\) exchange contribution: \(\mathcal{A}^{(\pi, {\rm dir})} = 0.48\) fm\(^4\).
- Tree-level \(\pi^0\) rescattering contribution: \(\mathcal{A}^{(\pi, {\rm res})} = 0.46\) fm\(^4\).
- Sum of vector meson exchanges (\(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\phi\)): \(\mathcal{A}^{(\rm V)} = 1.80\) fm\(^4\), with \(\omega\) exchange giving the largest individual contribution at \(1.35\) fm\(^4\) (using \(g_{\omega N}=10\)).
- Total pion loop contribution: \(\mathcal{A}^{(\rm loop)} = -0.14\) fm\(^4\), a small correction.
- Triplet threshold amplitude for \(pp \to pn\pi^+\): \(\mathcal{B} = (2.8 - i\,1.5)\) fm\(^4\).
- Modulus of threshold amplitude for \(pp \to pp\eta\): \(|\mathcal{C}| = 1.32\) fm\(^4\), reproduced with \(g_{\eta N} = 5.3\).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to understand the unexpectedly large cross section for \(pp \to pp\pi^0\) near threshold, which was first observed experimentally and found to be a factor of five larger than predictions based on direct pion production and neutral pion rescattering fixed from on-shell \(\pi N\) data. It contradicts the widespread belief that this channel is chirally suppressed, showing instead that the threshold amplitude does not vanish in the chiral limit \(m_\pi \to 0\). The authors explicitly demonstrate the failure of the heavy baryon formalism (HBChPT) used in prior calculations by Lee and Riska (1993), Horowitz et al. (1994), and others, and instead perform fully relativistic calculations. The approach enables a simple description of pion and eta production in \(pp\) collisions near threshold using final-state interaction and well-known relativistic meson-exchange diagrams, and the authors note that further improvements require systematic inclusion of initial-state and final-state \(NN\) interactions and more exclusive observables.
