# Doubly heavy hadrons and the domain of validity of doubly heavy diquark--anti-quark symmetry

> In the limit of heavy quark masses going to infinity, a symmetry is known to emerge in QCD relating properties of hadrons with two heavy quarks to analogous states with one heavy anti-quark. A key question is whether the charm mass is heavy enough so that this symmetry is manifest in at least an approximate manner. The issue is crucial in attempting to understand the recent reports by the SELEX Collaboration of doubly charmed baryons. We argue on very general grounds that the charm quark mass is substantially too light for the symmetry to emerge automatically via colour coulombic interactions. However, the symmetry could emerge approximately depending on the dynamical details.

## Key claim

The charm quark mass is substantially too light for the doubly heavy diquark–antiquark (DHDA) symmetry to emerge automatically via color Coulombic interactions, but the symmetry could emerge approximately depending on the dynamical details of non-perturbative physics.

## Method

The authors analyze the scale hierarchy required for DHDA symmetry using the effective field theories NRQCD (for interactions between two heavy quarks) and HQET (for interactions between heavy particles and light degrees of freedom). They compute the characteristic size of a color-Coulombic diquark, its excitation energies, and the leading corrections to DHDA symmetry from the diquark form factor, then compare these quantities to the SELEX Collaboration's reported doubly charmed baryon spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy of the excited doubly charmed baryon states reported by SELEX is 78 MeV.
- For a charm quark mass of 1.15 GeV and a relative velocity of 0.53, the color-Coulombic diquark excitation energy is 26.9 MeV.
- The RMS radius of the ground-state color-Coulombic diquark is 1.64 fm.
- The correction to DHDA symmetry from the diquark form factor is 3.02 × (Λ_H²/GeV²), which for Λ_H ∼ 1 GeV is not much smaller than 1.
- For bottom quarks, the type (c) diquark excitation energy is 32.8 MeV, the type (a) spin excitation from B-meson splitting is 34.3 MeV, the characteristic diquark size is 0.79 fm, and the DHDA symmetry correction is 0.69 × (Λ_H²/GeV²).

## Context

This paper extends the work of Savage and Wise (1990) on DHDA symmetry and critically examines whether the charm quark is heavy enough for the symmetry to be useful, motivated by the SELEX Collaboration's reports of doubly charmed baryons. The authors argue that the scale separation required for the symmetry to emerge automatically from color-Coulombic interactions does not hold for charm, contradicting the semi-quantitative success claimed by Bicudo, Rivera, and Rocha (2005) and Fleming and Mehen (2005) for the 78 MeV splitting. The work motivates the need for a new combined expansion incorporating both NRQCD and HQET to systematically treat doubly heavy systems with finite-mass quarks.
