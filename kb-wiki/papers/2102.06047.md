# Pion form factor and charge radius from Lattice QCD at physical point

> We present our results on the electromagnetic form factor of pion over a wide range of $Q^2$ using lattice QCD simulations with Wilson-clover valence quarks and HISQ sea quarks. We study the form factor at the physical point with a lattice spacing $a=0.076$ fm. To study the lattice spacing and quark mass effects, we also present results for 300 MeV pion at two different lattice spacings $a=0.04$ and 0.06 fm. The lattice calculations at the physical quark mass appear to agree with the experimental results. Through fits to the form factor, we estimate the charge radius of pion for physical pion mass to be $\langle r_π^2 \rangle=0.42(2)~{\rm fm}^2$.

## Key claim

The lattice QCD calculations of the pion electromagnetic form factor at the physical quark mass appear to agree with experimental results, but the authors note that the 3-state fit is required to describe the ratio data with acceptable chi-squared, while the 2-state fit results in chi-squared per degree of freedom much greater than 1, indicating that excited state contamination must be carefully controlled.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using Wilson-clover valence quarks on HISQ sea quark ensembles generated by the HotQCD collaboration, with lattice spacings of 0.076 fm (physical pion mass of 140 MeV) and 0.04 and 0.06 fm (valence pion mass of 300 MeV). They calculated pion two-point and three-point correlation functions using boosted Gaussian sources in Coulomb gauge, extracted bare matrix elements through multi-state fits (2-state and 3-state) to the standard ratio of three-point to two-point functions, and obtained renormalized form factors by dividing by the forward matrix element to remove discretization effects.

## Key numbers

- Pion charge radius at physical pion mass: ⟨r_π^2⟩ = 0.42(2) fm^2
- Lattice spacing for physical point calculation: a = 0.076 fm
- Lattice spacings for 300 MeV pion calculations: a = 0.04 fm and 0.06 fm
- For the a = 0.076 fm ensemble, the wrap-around effect from thermal states gives e^{-aL_t m_π} ∼ 0.03
- For a = 0.04 fm and 0.06 fm ensembles, the wrap-around effect gives e^{-aL_t m_π} ∼ 0.003 and 0.02 respectively
- The vector current renormalization factor Z_V = 0.946(12) from RI-MOM scheme for a = 0.076 fm
- Discretization errors in forward matrix element are smaller than 1% for P_z^i < 1 GeV and less than 2% for P_z^i < 1.6 GeV
- The first excited state energy follows the dispersion relation with m_{π'} = 1.3 GeV, consistent with the radial excitation π(1300)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of the pion form factor (Brommel 2006, Frezzotti 2008, Aoki 2009, Brandt 2013, Alexandrou 2017, Boyle 2008, Nguyen 2011, Fukaya 2014, Feng 2019, Wang 2020, Koponen 2015) by performing calculations at smaller lattice spacings (down to 0.04 fm) and at the physical pion mass, enabling direct comparison with experimental data from CERN (Amendolia 1986) and the F_π collaboration (Huber 2008). The results enable future lattice studies of pion generalized parton distributions using LaMET, and the high-precision determination of the pion charge radius has potential to shed light on the hadronic vacuum polarization discrepancy between e+e- cross-section measurements and lattice calculations (Borsanyi 2020).
