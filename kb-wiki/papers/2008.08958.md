# Measurement of the Generalized Polarizabilities of the Proton at Intermediate $Q^2$

> Background: Generalized polarizabilities (GPs) are important observables to describe the nucleon structure, and measurements of these observables are still scarce. Purpose: This paper presents details of a virtual Compton scattering (VCS) experiment, performed at the A1 setup at the Mainz Microtron by studying the $e p \to e p γ$ reaction. The article focuses on selected aspects of the analysis. Method: The experiment extracted the $P_{LL} -P_{TT} / ε$ and $P_{LT}$ structure functions, as well as the electric and magnetic GPs of the proton, at three new values of the four-momentum transfer squared $Q^2$: 0.10, 0.20 and 0.45 GeV$^2$. Results: We emphasize the importance of the calibration of experimental parameters. The behavior of the measured $e p \to e p γ$ cross section is presented and compared to the theory. A detailed investigation of the polarizability fits reveals part of their complexity, in connection with the higher-order terms of the low-energy expansion. Conclusions: The presented aspects are elements which contribute to minimize the systematic uncertainties and improve the precision of the physics results.

## Key claim

The paper presents the experimental details and calibration methodology behind previously published measurements of the proton's electric and magnetic generalized polarizabilities at three momentum transfers (0.10, 0.20, and 0.45 GeV²), emphasizing that achieving competitive systematic uncertainties requires controlling the cross-section systematic error to approximately 1% through careful calibration of spectrometer optics, offsets, and the cryogenic deposit on target walls.

## Method

The experiment used the unpolarized MAMI electron beam and the A1 setup with two high-resolution magnetic spectrometers in coincidence to detect the scattered electron and outgoing proton from the ep → epγ reaction at three values of Q² (0.10, 0.20, and 0.45 GeV²). At each Q², three kinematic settings were employed: a high-q' out-of-plane setting with large sensitivity to the electric GP, a high-q' in-plane setting with mixed sensitivity to electric and magnetic GPs, and a low-q' setting with no GP sensitivity used for normalization. The generalized polarizabilities were extracted from absolute cross sections using both low-energy expansion (LEX) fits and dispersion relation (DR) fits, with the analysis focusing on calibrating optical transfer matrices, offsets in momenta and angles, and the cryogenic deposit parameter.

## Key numbers

- Three new Q² values were measured: 0.10, 0.20, and 0.45 GeV²
- About 900,000, 1,100,000, and 300,000 VCS events were recorded at Q² = 0.10, 0.20, and 0.45 GeV² respectively
- The instantaneous luminosity reached (0.6-1.8)×10³⁷ cm⁻²/s
- The beam intensity was 5-15 μA
- The cryotarget center was found shifted upstream by 1.4 mm to 3.3 mm depending on the data taking period
- The first-order optical element (y|y) for spectrometer B varied by approximately 1% in the saturation region (P_B from 634 to 770 MeV/c)
- The out-of-plane angle (OOP_B) was 8-9° in the laboratory frame to reach φ_cm = 90° in the center-of-mass
- The low-q' region corresponds to q' < 50 MeV/c, while the pion production threshold is at q' = 126 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous VCS measurements of proton GPs at Q² values from 0.06 to 1.76 GeV² by experiments such as Roche (2000), d'Hose (2006), Laveissiere (2004), Bourgeois (2006, 2011), and Doria (2015), and specifically surrounds the intriguing point at Q² = 0.33 GeV² where prior measurements exist. The results were previously published in Beričič (2019), and this paper provides the detailed calibration methodology that enabled those results. The improved calibration techniques, particularly for spectrometer optics in the saturation region and for the cryogenic deposit parameter, enable more precise GP extractions at intermediate Q² and can inform future VCS experiments at facilities like MAMI.
