# Vorticity and hyperon polarization at NICA energies

> We study the formation of fluid vorticity and the hyperon polarization in heavy-ion collisions at NICA energies in the framework of the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamic Model, taking into account both hadronic and quark-gluonic (partonic) degrees of freedom. The vorticity properties in peripheral Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$7.7\,GeV are demonstrated and confronted with other models. The obtained result for the $Λ$ polarization is in agreement with the experimental data by the STAR collaboration, whereas the model is not able to explain the observed high values of the anti-hyperon $\barΛ$ polarization.

## Key claim

The PHSD model reproduces the measured global polarization of Λ hyperons in peripheral Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7 GeV, but it cannot explain the experimentally observed high polarization of anti-hyperons (Λ̅), which is about four times larger than that of Λ at this energy.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport model, which includes both hadronic and partonic degrees of freedom, to simulate peripheral Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7 GeV with impact parameter b = 7.5 fm. They computed the kinetic and thermal vorticity fields by fluidizing the particle distributions on a space-time grid using a smearing function, then calculated the Λ and Λ̅ polarization from the thermal vorticity using the leading-order formula from Becattini (2013), including feed-down contributions from resonance decays (Σ*, Σ, Ξ, Ξ*).

## Key numbers

- Λ polarization at t ≈ 10 fm/c: P*Λ,tot ≈ 2%, which agrees with the STAR experimental value of 2.0 ± 0.6% for Au+Au at √sNN = 7.7 GeV with centrality 20–50%
- Λ̅ polarization measured by STAR: P*Λ̅,tot = 8.7 ± 3.5%, about four times larger than the Λ value at the same energy
- Feed-down contribution to Λ polarization: about 25%
- The relativistic vorticity reaches a maximum soon after local equilibrium and decreases rapidly at ≈ 5 fm/c after the beginning of fluid dynamical expansion
- Multiplicity of Λ and Λ̅ differs by roughly two orders of magnitude at this energy

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic and transport studies of vorticity and hyperon polarization at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, particularly the three-fluid hydrodynamical model of Ivanov and Soldatov (2017) and the QGSM approach of Baznat et al. (2013) and Teryaev and Usubov (2015). The PHSD model results for Λ polarization agree with STAR experimental data from the Beam Energy Scan program, but the model's failure to reproduce the large Λ̅ polarization highlights an open puzzle in the field. The authors plan to extend these calculations to higher energies and different centralities to determine optimal conditions for vorticity formation.
