# Transverse momentum distribution of net baryon number at RHIC

> We calculate the transverse momentum distribution of net quarks (quarks minus antiquarks) in Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider in the framework of the parton cascade model at two different rapidities. Parton re-scattering and fragmentation is seen to lead to a substantial difference in the slopes of these distributions between mid- and forward-rapidities, in qualitative agreement with the corresponding data for the net baryon distribution.

# Transverse momentum distribution of net baryon number at RHIC

## Key claim
The transverse momentum slopes of net quarks (and thus net baryons) in Au+Au collisions at RHIC differ by about 20% between mid-rapidity and forward rapidity, and this difference arises from the greater number of multiple parton scatterings at central rapidities rather than from initial-state rapidity–pT correlations.

## Method
The authors used the parton cascade model (PCM) implemented in the VNI/BMS code, solving a relativistic Boltzmann equation for one-body parton distribution functions with all lowest-order QCD scattering processes and time-like branchings (q → qg, q → qγ, g → gg, g → qq̅). They calculated net quark distributions (quarks minus antiquarks) in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 130 GeV and 200 GeV, comparing results at yCM = 0, 1, and 3, and performed a control calculation with only primary-primary collisions to isolate the effect of multiple rescattering.

## Key numbers
- The inverse slope (“temperature”) of the net quark transverse momentum distribution at mid-rapidity is about 20% larger than at forward rapidity (yCM = 3) for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 130 GeV.
- The same 20% difference in slopes between yCM = 0 and yCM = 3 is confirmed for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.
- The initialization scale and low momentum cut-off for pQCD cross sections were set to Q₀² = (pT^min)² = 0.50 GeV².
- Time-like branchings were terminated when parton virtuality dropped below μ₀ = 1 GeV.
- Partons ending at central rapidities suffer many more collisions on average than those at larger rapidities, as shown by the ncoll vs. y distribution.

## Context
This work extends the authors’ previous study (Bass, Müller, Srivastava, 2002) showing that valence quark distributions combined with multiple scattering explain the net baryon excess at central rapidity in RHIC Au+Au collisions. The results are in qualitative agreement with preliminary data from the BRAHMS collaboration (2003). The finding that net quark distributions at forward rapidity freeze earlier than at mid-rapidity suggests that forward-rapidity measurements probe the very early stage of the collision (where saturation phenomena may be important), while mid-rapidity distributions reflect the multiply interacting partonic system. The authors note that the recombination model (Rainer, 2003) predicts that hadron momentum slopes remain proportional to parton slopes, supporting the connection to measured net baryon distributions.
