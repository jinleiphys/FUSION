# Ab initio calculation of neutral-current $ν$-$^{12}$C inclusive quasielastic scattering

> Quasielastic neutrino scattering is an important aspect of the experimental program to study fundamental neutrino properties including neutrino masses, mixing angles, the mass hierarchy and CP-violating phase. Proper interpretation of the experiments requires reliable theoretical calculations of neutrino-nucleus scattering. In this paper we present calculations of response functions and cross sections by neutral-current scattering of neutrinos off $^{12}$C. These calculations are based on realistic treatments of nuclear interactions and currents, the latter including the axial-, vector-, and vector-axial interference terms crucial for determining the difference between neutrino and anti-neutrino scattering and the CP-violating phase. We find that the strength and energy-dependence of two-nucleon processes induced by correlation effects and interaction currents are crucial in providing the most accurate description of neutrino-nucleus scattering in the quasielastic regime.

## Key claim

The authors find that two-nucleon contributions from correlation effects and interaction currents are crucial for an accurate description of neutral-current neutrino-12C scattering in the quasielastic regime, with the caveat that the calculations are limited in kinematical scope to the quasielastic region and rely on freezing the energy dependence of the currents at the quasielastic energy transfer.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of neutral-current response functions and cross sections for neutrino-12C scattering, using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potential with one- and two-body weak neutral currents. They computed Laplace transforms of the response functions via imaginary-time propagation of current-current correlators, then used maximum entropy techniques to analytically continue these Euclidean response functions to obtain the energy-dependent response functions at a fixed momentum transfer of 570 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- At momentum transfer q = 570 MeV/c, two-body terms in the axial neutral current increase the one-body Rxx response by about 20% in the quasielastic region
- The axial contribution to Rxx is approximately 3 times larger than the vector contribution (Rxx^ANC ≈ 3 × Rxx^VNC)
- The vector charge response R00 and transverse response Rxx in 12C are roughly proportional to the electromagnetic longitudinal and transverse responses: R00/xx^VNC ≈ R_L/T/4
- Parallelization efficiency close to 95% was achieved using 8192 nodes of the Mira supercomputer, with approximately one million node hours used for the calculation
- The Fermi constant used is GF = 1.1803 × 10^-5 GeV^-2 from super-allowed 0+ → 0+ beta decays

## Context

This work extends previous GFMC calculations of electromagnetic response functions in 12C (Lovato 2016) to the weak neutral-current sector, and validates the dynamical framework that was shown to agree with electron scattering data. The results provide a benchmark for testing event generators used in neutrino oscillation experiments such as T2K, MINERvA, and DUNE, and enable comparison with more approximate treatments of neutrino-nucleus scattering. The two-body current contributions found here are much larger (20%) than the 2-4% increases seen in Gamow-Teller rates between low-lying states near threshold (Pastore 2017), highlighting the evolution of correlation effects with momentum transfer.
