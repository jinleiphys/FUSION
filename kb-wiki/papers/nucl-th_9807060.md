# Transport Simulations with Pi and Delta In-Medium Properties

> Transport simulations including in-medium properties derived in a microscopic pi + nucleon-hole + delta-hole model in infinite nuclear matter are presented. In-medium pion dispersion relations, partial delta decay widths, pion absorption cross sections and delta cross sections are incorporated into the transport description by means of a local-density approximation. Strong modifications of pi and delta production and absorption rates are found, but only small effects on pion observables.

## Key claim

Transport simulations that incorporate in-medium pion and Delta properties derived from a microscopic pi + nucleon-hole + Delta-hole model show strong modifications of pion and Delta production and absorption rates, but only small effects on the final spectra of emitted pions, because most emitted pions are created at the surface where in-medium effects are small.

## Method

The authors performed BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport simulations for central 197Au + 197Au collisions at 1.0 A GeV, incorporating in-medium pion dispersion relations, partial Delta decay widths, pion absorption cross sections, and NN ↔ ΔN cross sections calculated from a microscopic π + NN⁻¹ + ΔN⁻¹ model in infinite nuclear matter. These in-medium quantities were implemented into the transport description via a local-density approximation, using two different form factor sets (FF1 and FF2) and comparing three levels of medium modification (M1, M2, M3) against a standard simulation using vacuum cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Central 197Au + 197Au collisions at 1.0 A GeV were simulated.
- For simulation M3 with FF2, the net reduction in the number of emitted pions was only about 3-10% compared to the standard simulation.
- The enhancement of low-energy pions was observed for transverse momenta below 250 MeV/c, with a reduction at momenta above 350 MeV/c.
- Normal nuclear matter density used was ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³.
- The Delta isobar mass used was m_Δc² = 1.23 GeV, the pion mass m_πc² = 0.14 GeV, and the nucleon mass m_Nc² = 0.940 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier exploratory transport simulations by Helgesson and Randrup (1997) that used a less complete set of in-medium quantities, and differs from earlier studies by Weise (1988), Bertsch (1988), and the Giessen and Tübingen groups which employed simpler two-level ΔN⁻¹ models. The results suggest that while in-medium modifications of pions and Deltas have small effects on pion observables directly, they may be important for understanding production of secondary particles like kaons and dileptons, which are produced through multistep processes involving pions and Deltas as intermediate particles.
