# Coulomb distortion of relativistic electrons in the nuclear electrostatic field

> Continuum states of the Dirac equation are calculated numerically for the electrostatic field generated by the charge distribution of an atomic nucleus. The behavior of the wave functions of an incoming electron with a given asymptotic momentum in the nuclear region is discussed in detail and the results are compared to different approximations used in the data analysis for quasielastic electron scattering off medium and highly charged nuclei. It is found that most of the approximations provide an accurate description of the electron wave functions in the range of electron energies above 100 MeV typically used in experiments for quasielastic electron scattering off nuclei only near the center of the nucleus. It is therefore necessary that the properties of exact wave functions are investigated in detail in order to obtain reliable results in the data analysis of quasielastic (e,e'p) knockout reactions or inclusive quasielastic (e,e') scattering. Detailed arguments are given that the effective momentum approximation with a fitted potential parameter is a viable method for a simplified treatment of Coulomb corrections for certain kinematical regions used in experiments. Numerical calculations performed within the framework of the single particle shell model for nucleons lead to the conclusion that our results are incompatible with calculations performed about a decade ago, where exact electron wave functions were used in order to calculate Coulomb corrections in distorted wave Born approximation. A discussion of the exact solutions of the Dirac equation for free electrons in a Coulomb field generated by a point-like charge and some details relevant for the numerical calculations are given in the appendix.

## Key claim

The effective momentum approximation (EMA) with a fitted potential parameter (approximately 0.75 to 0.8 times the central potential value) is a viable simplified method for treating Coulomb corrections in quasielastic electron scattering, but only for certain kinematical regions where electron energies and momentum transfer are sufficiently high. The authors find that their exact numerical calculations are incompatible with earlier DWBA calculations performed about a decade ago by Kim et al. and the Ohio group.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation numerically using a partial wave expansion for continuum states of electrons in the electrostatic field generated by the charge distribution of atomic nuclei, modeled with Woods-Saxon (for 208Pb) and three-parameter Fermi (for 40Ca) charge distributions. They compared the exact wave functions to various approximations including the effective momentum approximation (EMA), first-order expansion in alpha Z, and the eikonal approximation, and tested these against a harmonic oscillator single-particle shell model for nucleons in a Pb model nucleus with 82 scalar protons.

## Key numbers

- Central Coulomb potential for 208Pb: V0 = -25.7 MeV, with rms charge radius 5.5 fm
- Central Coulomb potential for 40Ca: V0 = -10.4 MeV, with rms charge radius 3.48 fm
- For 208Pb at 400 MeV electron energy, the averaged focusing factor is 1.050, corresponding to an effective potential of -20.07 MeV (compared to V0 = -25.7 MeV)
- For 40Ca at 400 MeV, the effective potential is -7.76 MeV (compared to V0 = -10.4 MeV)
- The effective potential extracted by Gueye et al. from positron-electron scattering data for 208Pb was (18.7 +/- 1.5) MeV
- For 208Pb at 100 MeV, the EMA focusing factor is (100+25.7)/100 = 1.257, deviating less than 0.5% from the exact value
- For 208Pb at 400 MeV, the central focusing factor is approximately 425.7/400 = 1.0642, decreasing to 1.0187 at 8 fm transverse distance
- For 208Pb at 300 MeV initial and 200 MeV final energy with 60 degree scattering angle, the ratio of focused to free charge density is 0.985, showing overcompensation of the 17% focusing effect

## Context

This work extends the treatment of Coulomb distortions in quasielastic electron scattering beyond the plane wave Born approximation, which is inadequate for highly charged nuclei. The authors compare their exact Dirac equation solutions to the effective momentum approximation (EMA) developed by Yennie (1964) and to first-order expansion methods by Lenz, Knoll, and Rosenfelder. They find that earlier DWBA calculations by Kim et al. and the Ohio group are incompatible with their results, and that the first-order expansion fails to reproduce the transverse decrease and downstream increase of focusing inside the nucleus. The work enables improved data analysis for upcoming experiments at TJNAF (Jefferson Lab) on quasielastic electron scattering from 4He, 12C, 56Fe, and 208Pb, and suggests that a modified EMA with fitted effective potential provides a practical method for Coulomb corrections in inclusive quasielastic (e,e') scattering.
