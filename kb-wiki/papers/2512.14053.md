# Substructure grooming of inclusive and photon-tagged jets in heavy-ion collisions

> Jet substructure provides a powerful probe of partonic interactions within the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy-ion collisions. In this paper, we present a systematic theoretical study of the groomed substructures for both inclusive jets and photon-tagged jets ($γ+$jets) utilizing the Dynamical and Soft-Drop Grooming algorithms in PbPb collisions by employing the SHELL transport model. Our theoretical calculations exhibit a suppression at high $k_{\rm T,g}$, the relative transverse momentum between the two subjets in the groomed substructure, consistent with the recent ALICE measurements. We show that the suppression of high $k_{\rm T,g}$ arises from the combined effects of the reduction of the subleading subjet transverse momentum due to partonic energy loss and the narrowing of the groomed jet radius $R_g$ induced by selection bias. Our findings demonstrate that no enhancement is observed at high $k_{\rm T,g}$, even in the complete absence of selection bias. Furthermore, we propose that the broadening of $R_g$ in photon-tagged jets, which are less susceptible to selection bias compared to inclusive jets, provides relatively direct evidence of the jet substructure broadening. Our analysis reveals that the $R_g$ broadening becomes more pronounced as the jet radius increases, where the medium-induced gluon radiation plays a dominant role in driving such broadening. In particular, we find that as the jet radius increases, the Soft Drop grooming algorithm exhibits a better resolving power for the contribution of the medium response to the jet substructure broadening.

## Key claim

The suppression of high $k_{\rm T,g}$ in inclusive jets arises from the combined effects of reduced subleading subjet transverse momentum due to partonic energy loss and narrowing of the groomed jet radius $R_g$ induced by selection bias; even in the complete absence of selection bias, no enhancement is observed at high $k_{\rm T,g}$.

## Method

The authors employed the SHELL transport model to simulate jet evolution in the quark-gluon plasma, using the higher-twist formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation and a hard-thermal-loop approximation for elastic energy loss. They applied both Dynamical Grooming (with parameter $a=1.0$) and Soft-Drop Grooming (with $z_{\rm cut}=0.2$, $\beta=0$) algorithms to inclusive jets and photon-tagged jets in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, using Pythia8 with Monash Tune for the pp baseline and the CLVisc hydrodynamic model for medium evolution. A jet-by-jet matching analysis was performed to isolate the effects of selection bias from genuine medium modifications.

## Key numbers

- Jet transverse momentum range: $60 < p_T < 80$ GeV for inclusive jets, with jet cone size $R=0.2$ and pseudorapidity $|\eta| < 0.7$
- Jet transport parameter: $\hat{q}_0 = 1.2 \pm 0.2$ GeV$^2$/fm, extracted via global $\chi^2$ fit to identified hadron yields
- Critical temperature for hadronization: $T_c = 165$ MeV
- Simulation time step: $\Delta t = 0.1$ fm
- Running coupling constant: $\alpha_s = 0.3$
- Soft-Drop grooming parameter: $z_{\rm cut} = 0.2$, $\beta = 0$
- Dynamical Grooming parameter: $a = 1.0$ (corresponding to $k_t$-Drop mode)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of jet substructure modifications in heavy-ion collisions, building on the ALICE measurements of $k_{\rm T,g}$ nuclear modification (ALICE, 2024). The authors demonstrate that the suppression of high $k_{\rm T,g}$ cannot be simply attributed to selection bias alone, unlike other observables such as girth (studied by Wang, 2024) and $\Delta R_{\rm axis}$ (measured by ALICE, 2023). The study enables future experimental searches for genuine jet substructure broadening using photon-tagged jets, which are less susceptible to selection bias compared to inclusive jets, and provides a framework for comparing Dynamical Grooming and Soft-Drop Grooming algorithms in heavy-ion collisions.
