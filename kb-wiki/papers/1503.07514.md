# Hydrodynamics Beyond the Gradient Expansion: Resurgence and Resummation

> Consistent formulations of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics involve short lived modes, leading to asymptotic rather than convergent gradient expansions. In this Letter we consider the Mueller-Israel-Stewart theory applied to a longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma system and identify hydrodynamics as a universal attractor without invoking the gradient expansion. We give strong evidence for the existence of this attractor and then show that it can be recovered from the divergent gradient expansion by Borel summation. This requires careful accounting for the short-lived modes which leads to an intricate mathematical structure known from the theory of resurgence.

# Hydrodynamics Beyond the Gradient Expansion: Resurgence and Resummation

## Key claim

The authors propose that relativistic viscous hydrodynamics should be defined not by a truncated gradient expansion (which is divergent), but by a universal attractor that governs the late-time behavior of systems approaching equilibrium. They show that the divergent hydrodynamic gradient series can be recovered and resummed via Borel summation and transseries techniques, but only by carefully accounting for short-lived nonhydrodynamic modes, with the result matching the attractor only after including an integration constant that implies the attractor is not simply the gradient expansion plus exponential corrections.

## Method

The authors study the Mueller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory applied to a longitudinally expanding (Bjorken flow) conformal fluid, reducing the equations to ordinary differential equations in proper time. They introduce dimensionless variables w = tau T and f = tau (dw/dtau)/w, and analyze the resulting first-order equation for f(w). They generate the hydrodynamic gradient expansion to 200 orders, perform Borel transforms with diagonal Pade approximants for analytic continuation, and construct a transseries solution including exponential corrections from the nonhydrodynamic mode. They compare the resummed series to a numerically determined attractor solution.

## Key numbers

* The Borel transform singularity nearest the origin is located at xi_0 = 7.21187 (from Pade poles) or xi_0 = 7.21181 (from ratio method), with exponent gamma = 1.1449.
* For N=4 SYM parameter values, the first and second order hydrodynamic approximations match the attractor with error smaller than 10% for w > 0.5.
* The slow-roll expansion at second order matches the numerically computed attractor even for w as small as 0.1.
* The resummation result has an imaginary part below 0.01% relative to the real part for w > 0.25.
* The integration constant r = 0.049 was required to match the resummation to the numerically determined attractor.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Heller (2013) that the hydrodynamic gradient expansion diverges for strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma due to nonhydrodynamic quasinormal modes. It provides a concrete realization of resurgence ideas (Dunne, Aniceto, Argyres, Cherman) in the context of relativistic hydrodynamics, showing that the divergent series can be resummed into a transseries whose ambiguities cancel against exponential corrections from the nonhydrodynamic mode. This enables a rigorous definition of hydrodynamics beyond the gradient expansion and provides a roadmap for understanding higher-order gradients in the AdS/CFT fluid-gravity duality (Bhattacharyya 2008). The findings also suggest why MIS theory (Mueller 1967, Israel 1979, Baier 2007) describes QGP evolution well despite only matching QCD transport coefficients to second order, as the attractor is not very sensitive to higher-order terms.
