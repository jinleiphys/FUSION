# Speed of Sound in Magnetized Nuclear Matter

> Employing the non-linear Walecka model we investigate the characteristics of nuclear matter under the influence of a background magnetic field at a finite temperature and baryon chemical potential. In the presence of the magnetic field the spinodal lines and the critical end point (CEP) undergo changes in the $T-μ_B$ plane. The squared speed of sound exhibits anisotropic behavior, dividing into parallel and perpendicular components. Additionally, the presence of a magnetic field induces anisotropy in the isothermal compressibility. It is found that the parallel component is smaller than the perpendicular one for all values of temperature, chemical potential and magnetic field indicating that the equation of state is stiffer along the magnetic field direction.

## Key claim

Using the non-linear Walecka model, the squared speed of sound in nuclear matter under a background magnetic field splits into parallel and perpendicular components, with the parallel component always smaller than the perpendicular one for all temperatures, chemical potentials, and magnetic field strengths studied, indicating a stiffer equation of state along the field direction.

## Method

The authors employed the non-linear Walecka model in the mean-field approximation to compute the free energy of nuclear matter in a constant background magnetic field oriented along the z-direction, including contributions from the Dirac sea and thermo-magnetic terms with up to 1000 Landau levels for convergence. The effective nucleon mass and effective chemical potential were obtained self-consistently by minimizing the free energy, and the squared speed of sound was derived for both parallel and perpendicular directions using thermodynamic derivatives of pressure, energy density, entropy, baryon density, and magnetization with respect to temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density used: \(n_0 = 0.153\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\); compression modulus \(K = 240\ \mathrm{MeV}\); binding energy \(E_{\mathrm{bind}} = -16.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\); effective nucleon mass \(M = 0.8 m_N\) at saturation; chemical potential at saturation \(\mu_0 = 922.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Two representative magnetic field strengths studied: \(eB = 0.02\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(eB = 0.05\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), compared with \(eB = 0\).
- Temperatures considered for nucleon mass variation: \(T = 0,\ 10,\ 20,\ 30\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Isentropic curves shown for \(s/n_B\) values ranging from 0.05 to 15.0.
- The critical end point (CEP) shifts towards higher temperature and lower baryon chemical potential as the magnetic field increases from \(eB = 0\) to \(eB = 0.05\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the liquid-gas phase transition and speed of sound in nuclear matter using the non-linear Walecka model, such as He (2022), by incorporating a background magnetic field. It also builds on earlier investigations of magnetic field effects on nucleon mass in the Walecka model by Mukherjee (2018) and on transport properties of magnetized hadronic matter by Kadam (2014), Das (2019), and Ghosh (2022). The results enable future studies of neutron star structure and heavy-ion collision dynamics under strong magnetic fields, particularly for magnetars and non-central collisions at RHIC.
