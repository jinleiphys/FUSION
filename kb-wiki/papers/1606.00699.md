# Fusion and quasifission dynamics in the reactions $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk and $^{50}$Ti+$^{249}$Bk using TDHF

> Background: Synthesis of superheavy elements (SHE) with fusion-evaporation reactions is strongly hindered by the quasifission (QF) mechanism which prevents the formation of an equilibrated compound nucleus and which depends on the structure of the reactants. New SHE have been recently produced with doubly-magic $^{48}$Ca beams. However, SHE synthesis experiments with single-magic $^{50}$Ti beams have so far been unsuccessful. Purpose: In connection with experimental searches for $Z=117,119$ superheavy elements, we perform a theoretical study of fusion and quasifission mechanisms in $^{48}$Ca,$^{50}$Ti+$^{249}$Bk reactions in order to investigate possible differences in reaction mechanisms induced by these two projectiles. Methods: The collision dynamics and the outcome of the reactions are studied using unrestricted time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations as well as the density-constrained TDHF method to extract the nucleus-nucleus potentials and the excitation energy in each fragment. Results: Nucleus-nucleus potentials, nuclear contact times, masses and charges of the fragments, as well as their kinetic and excitation energies strongly depend on the orientation of the prolate $^{249}$Bk nucleus. Long contact times associated with fusion are observed in collisions of both projectiles with the side of the $^{249}$Bk nucleus, but not on collisions with its tip. The energy and impact parameter dependences of the fragment properties, as well as their mass-angle and mass-total kinetic energy correlations are investigated. Conclusions: Entrance channel reaction dynamics are similar with both $^{48}$Ca and $^{50}$Ti projectiles. Both are expected to lead to the formation of a compound nucleus by fusion if they have enough energy to get in contact with the side of the $^{249}$Bk target.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk and $^{50}$Ti+$^{249}$Bk reactions exhibit similar entrance channel dynamics and are both expected to lead to compound nucleus formation by fusion, but only if the projectile has enough energy to collide with the side (not the tip) of the prolate $^{249}$Bk target nucleus.

## Method

The authors performed unrestricted time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on a 3D Cartesian grid without symmetry restrictions, using the Skyrme SLy4 and SLy4d energy density functionals, to simulate collisions of $^{48}$Ca and $^{50}$Ti projectiles with $^{249}$Bk targets at various orientations, center-of-mass energies, and impact parameters. They also employed the density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) method to extract nucleus-nucleus potentials, coordinate-dependent mass parameters, and excitation energies of individual fragments from the TDHF time evolution.

## Key numbers

- For $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk, the DC-TDHF barrier height for tip orientation is $E_B$(tip)$=191.22$ MeV at $R_B$(tip)$=15.04$ fm; for side orientation it is $E_B$(side)$=204.36$ MeV at $R_B$(side)$=12.47$ fm.
- For $^{50}$Ti+$^{249}$Bk, the DC-TDHF barrier height for tip orientation is $E_B$(tip)$=211.2$ MeV at $R_B$(tip)$=14.48$ fm; for side orientation it is $E_B$(side)$=224.6$ MeV at $R_B$(side)$=12.96$ fm.
- In central collisions of $^{48}$Ca with the side of $^{249}$Bk, contact times reach up to $22$ zs at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=210$ MeV, with fusion found at energies $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=211,212,218,220$ MeV.
- For $^{50}$Ti+$^{249}$Bk central collisions with the side orientation, quasifission is confined to an extremely narrow energy window around $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=227.4-227.7$ MeV, with fusion setting in at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}>228$ MeV.
- At $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=218$ MeV for $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk, fusion with the side of $^{249}$Bk is only observed for impact parameters $b<0.3$ fm; quasifission contact times range from $5.6$ to $13.6$ zs for $b=0.3-3.0$ fm.
- For $^{50}$Ti+$^{249}$Bk at $E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=233.2$ MeV, fusion with the side of $^{249}$Bk is observed at $b=0$ fm and $b=0.5$ fm; quasifission contact times are $8-16$ zs at $b=1$ and $2$ fm.
- The total excitation energy of both systems at small impact parameters (where fusion occurs for side orientation) is almost identical at $b=0$ fm and $b=1$ fm.

## Context

This work extends microscopic TDHF studies of fusion and quasifission dynamics to reactions relevant for superheavy element synthesis, specifically comparing the successful $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk reaction (which produced element $Z=117$ with cross-sections of $2-3$ picobarns at Dubna and GSI-TASCA) with the unsuccessful $^{50}$Ti+$^{249}$Bk reaction (upper cross-section limit of $50$ fb for element $119$ at GSI-TASCA, reported by Dullmann (2013)). The results contradict the conjecture that different excitation energies between the two systems explain the experimental failure, finding instead that excitation energies are nearly identical at fusion-relevant impact parameters. The study enables future experimental guidance by identifying that both projectiles require sufficient energy to collide with the side of the deformed $^{249}$Bk target to achieve fusion, and that the narrow fusion windows and strong orientation dependence may explain the difficulty in synthesizing element $119$.
