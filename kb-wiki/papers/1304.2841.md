# Dijet asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV within a multiphase transport model

> Within a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model, dijet asymmetry is studied in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV. It is found that a large dijet asymmetry ($A_{J}$) is produced by strong interactions between jets and partonic matter. It is demonstrated that hadronization and final-state hadronic rescatterings have little effects on $A_{J}$. The final $A_{J}$ is found to be driven by both initial $A_{J}$ and partonic jet energy loss, which is consistent with an increasing jet energy loss in a hot and strongly interacting partonic medium in more central Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

A large dijet asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV is produced by strong interactions between jets and partonic matter, while hadronization and final-state hadronic rescatterings have little effect; the final asymmetry is driven by both the initial asymmetry and partonic jet energy loss, consistent with increasing energy loss in more central collisions.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model with string melting to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}\) = 2.76 TeV, performing two sets of simulations with partonic interaction cross sections set to 1.5 mb (partonic+hadronic interactions) and 0 mb (hadronic interactions only). Jets are reconstructed using the anti-\(k_t\) algorithm with cone size R=0.5, requiring leading jet \(p_T > 120\) GeV/\(c\), subleading jet \(p_T > 50\) GeV/\(c\), and azimuthal separation \(\Delta\phi_{1,2} > 2\pi/3\). The dijet asymmetry is defined as \(A_J = (p_{T,1} - p_{T,2})/(p_{T,1} + p_{T,2})\), and evolution functions are extracted to trace how initial asymmetry and jet energy loss contribute to the final asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Leading jet \(p_T\) threshold: \(p_{T,1} > 120\) GeV/\(c\); subleading jet threshold: \(p_{T,2} > 50\) GeV/\(c\).
- Jet cone size: R = 0.5; pseudorapidity range: \(|\eta_{1,2}| < 2\); azimuthal cut: \(\Delta\phi_{1,2} > 2\pi/3\).
- Partonic interaction cross sections: 1.5 mb (partonic+hadronic) and 0 mb (hadronic only).
- For the 0-10% most central collisions, AMPT with 1.5 mb gives a much better description of the \(A_J\) distribution than AMPT with 0 mb, which underestimates the mean \(\langle A_J \rangle\).
- The mean \(\langle A_J \rangle\) and variance \(\sigma(A_J)\) are shown as functions of number of participant nucleons \(N_{part}\) for centrality bins 0-10%, 10-20%, 20-30%, 30-50%, and 50-100%.
- Event-averaged energy loss fractions \(\langle \Delta p_T / p_T \rangle\) increase with \(N_{part}\) for both leading and subleading jets; leading jets lose less energy than subleading jets due to dijet surface bias.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dijet asymmetry at the LHC observed by ATLAS (Aad, 2010) and CMS (Chatrchyan, 2011), and complements theoretical efforts by Casalderrey-Solana (2010), Qin and Muller (2010), Young (2011), He (2011), and Renk (2012). The AMPT model with string melting, previously used to describe many experimental observables at RHIC and LHC energies, is here applied to dijet asymmetry for the first time. The results enable quantitative extraction of dijet asymmetry evolution functions, showing that the large asymmetry in central collisions arises from partonic energy loss, and that hadronic-only interactions cannot reproduce the data.
