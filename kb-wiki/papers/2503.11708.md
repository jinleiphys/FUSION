# Radiative corrections for the two-nucleon interaction in effective field theory

> Radiative corrections are playing an increasingly significant role in low-energy nuclear physics. We investigate the influence of photons as explicit degrees of freedom within nuclear EFT on the deuteron binding energy, charge form factor, and the radiative capture process $ np \to dγ$ using the velocity renormalization group. In each case, evolving the subtraction velocity to a characteristic nucleon velocity in the bound state induces percent-level modifications in the relevant observables. These findings indicate that electromagnetic corrections represent a non-negligible source of uncertainty in current few-body calculations.

## Key claim
Radiative corrections from explicit photon degrees of freedom induce percent-level modifications in deuteron binding energy, charge form factor, and the radiative capture process \( np \to d\gamma \), but the analysis is performed at next-to-leading-order plus leading-logarithm-in-alpha (NLO+LLalpha) or next-to-next-to-leading-order plus leading-logarithm-in-alpha (N2LO+LLalpha), and the quoted shifts depend on the choice of subtraction velocity \(\nu\).

## Method
The authors combine pionless effective field theory (EFT\(_{\not{\pi}}\)) with nonrelativistic QED (NRQED) and the velocity renormalization group (vRG). They compute the leading anomalous dimensions of the strong potential coefficients \(C_0\), \(C_2\), and \(C_4\) in the \(^3S_1\) and \(^1S_0\) channels from ultrasoft photon exchange, then solve the vRG equations to evolve these low-energy coefficients from the hard scale \(\nu = m_\pi/M_N\) down to a characteristic nucleon velocity in the bound state. The running QED coupling is used with the electron mass kept as a light degree of freedom.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: at NLO+LLalpha, shifting \(\nu\) from the hard scale to \(\nu \approx 0.04\) (corresponding to momenta around 38 MeV) produces a shift of about 2.5%; at N2LO+LLalpha, the shift is about 7%, and the predicted binding energy intersects the experimental value \(B = 2.224575\) MeV near \(\nu \approx 0.04\).
- Deuteron charge radius: at \(\nu = m_\pi/M_N\) (no QED), the theoretical charge radius is \(r_{\text{th}} = 2.15(6)\) fm; after vRG improvement at \(\nu = 0.1\), it becomes \(r_{\text{th}} = 2.11(6)\) fm, a shift of about 2%. The result agrees within pionless EFT uncertainty for \(\nu \in [0.06, 0.1]\) with the CREMA experimental result \(r_{\text{CREMA}} = 2.12562(13)_{\text{exp}} (77)_{\text{th}}\).
- Radiative capture \(np \to d\gamma\): running from \(\nu = m_\pi/M_N\) to \(\nu = 0.05\) induces a shift in the cross section of 6-8% at low energies; at higher energies the vRG variation is negligible.

## Context
This work extends the pionless EFT framework of Kaplan (1996, 1998) and van Kolck (1999) by incorporating explicit photon degrees of freedom via the vRG method developed by Luke and Manohar (2000). It builds on the radiative corrections analysis of Richardson (2024) for the two-nucleon system. The results indicate that QED corrections constitute an unquantified source of uncertainty in existing few-body calculations, including the \(np \to d\gamma\) cross section used in big bang nucleosynthesis simulations by Rupak (2000) and Ando (2006), and suggest that similar percent-level corrections may affect other reactions such as proton-proton fusion.
