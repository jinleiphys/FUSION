# The Glauber model and heavy ion reaction and elastic scattering cross sections

> We revisit the Glauber model to study the heavy ion reaction cross sections and elastic scattering angular distributions at low and intermediate energies. The Glauber model takes nucleon-nucleon cross sections and nuclear densities as inputs and has no free parameter and thus can predict the cross sections for unknown systems. The Glauber model works at low energies down to Coulomb barrier with very simple modifications. We present new parametrization of measured total cross sections as well as ratio of real to imaginary parts of the scattering amplitudes for pp and np collisions as a function of nucleon kinetic energy. The nuclear (charge) densities obtained by electron scattering form factors measured in large momentum transfer range are used in the calculations. The heavy ion reaction cross sections are calculated for light and heavy systems and are compared with available data measured over large energy range. The model gives excellent description of the data. The elastic scattering angular distributions are calculated for various systems at different energies. The model gives good description of the data at small momentum transfer but the calculations deviate from the data at large momentum transfer.

# The Glauber model and heavy ion reaction and elastic scattering cross sections

## Key claim

The Coulomb Modified Glauber Model (CMGM), with no free parameters, gives an excellent description of heavy ion reaction cross sections across a wide energy range and a good description of elastic scattering angular distributions at small momentum transfer, but the calculations systematically deviate from data at large momentum transfer (high angles), where the model produces diffractive oscillations of larger magnitude than observed.

## Method

The authors calculate reaction cross sections and elastic scattering angular distributions using the Coulomb Modified Glauber Model (CMGM), which incorporates a non-eikonal trajectory correction via the Coulomb distance of closest approach. They use nuclear (charge) densities obtained from electron scattering form factors measured over large momentum transfer ranges, including Modified Harmonic Oscillator (MHO) densities for light nuclei and two-parameter or three-parameter Fermi densities for heavier nuclei. New parametrizations of nucleon-nucleon total cross sections and the ratio of real to imaginary parts of the scattering amplitude for pp and np collisions are derived from Particle Data Group data and phase shift analysis, respectively, and used as inputs.

## Context

This work extends the systematic reaction cross section study of Shukla (2003) to many more systems and collision energies, and performs elastic scattering angular distribution calculations similar to Gibbs (2012) but for a broader set of systems. The new parametrizations of NN cross sections and $\alpha$ values improve upon earlier parametrizations by Charagi and Gupta (which were good only below 50-60 MeV) and by Bertulani and Conti (which differed in the 120-300 MeV range for $\sigma_{pp}$ and could not be extrapolated below 8 MeV for $\sigma_{np}$). The results enable reliable prediction of reaction cross sections for unknown systems and extraction of nuclear radii from measured reaction cross sections, while the limitations at large momentum transfer point to the need for additional free parameters in the nuclear potential as used in studies by Khoa (2000) or Ahmad (2004).
