# Effective double-beta-decay operator for 76Ge and 82Se

> We use diagrammatic many-body perturbation theory in combination with low-momentum interactions derived from chiral effective field theory to construct effective shell-model transition operators for the neutrinoless double-beta decay of 76Ge and 82Se. We include all unfolded diagrams to first- and second-order in the interaction and all singly folded diagrams that can be constructed from them. The resulting effective operator, which accounts for physics outside the shell-model space, increases the nuclear matrix element by about 20% in 76Ge and 30% in 82Se.

## Key claim

Using diagrammatic many-body perturbation theory with low-momentum interactions from chiral effective field theory, the effective shell-model neutrinoless double-beta decay operator increases the nuclear matrix element by about 20% in 76Ge and 30% in 82Se, but these results omit third- and higher-order terms in the X-box, additional folding contributions, chiral three-nucleon forces, and induced three-body operators, so the final numbers represent current best estimates rather than definitive values.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective two-body transition operator for neutrinoless double-beta decay by computing all unfolded diagrams to first and second order in the low-momentum interaction Vlowk (cutoff 2.0 fm^-1) and all singly folded diagrams constructed from them, using a model space of the 0f5/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2, and 0g9/2 orbitals above a 56Ni core in a harmonic-oscillator basis of 13 major shells with hbar-omega = 10.0 MeV. They evaluated the Q-box (effective Hamiltonian) to third order and the X-box (effective transition operator) to second order in the interaction, then combined these with two-body shell-model transition densities from existing calculations by Horoi (2012) for 76Ge and Menendez et al. (2008) for 82Se. Intermediate-state particle lines in the X-box were restricted to be essentially unoccupied to account for Pauli exclusion, with specific occupancy factors applied to partially filled orbits.

## Key numbers

- Bare matrix element M0nu for 76Ge: 3.12 (dimensionless)
- Bare matrix element M0nu for 82Se: 2.73 (dimensionless)
- Final corrected matrix element for 76Ge: 3.77 (dimensionless), approximately 20% larger than bare
- Final corrected matrix element for 82Se: 3.62 (dimensionless), approximately 30% larger than bare
- Full first-order X-box matrix element for 76Ge: 2.20 (dimensionless)
- Full second-order X-box matrix element for 76Ge: 4.14 (dimensionless)
- Full first-order X-box matrix element for 82Se: 2.40 (dimensionless)
- Full second-order X-box matrix element for 82Se: 3.92 (dimensionless)
- Convergence test for 82Se at 18 hbar-omega excitations: final matrix element 3.618 (dimensionless), converged to 3-4 digits
- Using CD-Bonn G-matrix instead of Vlowk gave final matrix element 3.62 for 76Ge and 3.45 for 82Se
- Using N3LO G-matrix gave final matrix element 3.48 for 76Ge and 3.33 for 82Se
- Two-neutrino double-beta decay closure matrix element for 76Ge with restricted intermediate states: bare 0.57, final 0.96 (dimensionless)

## Context

This work extends the only prior application of many-body perturbation theory to the neutrinoless double-beta decay operator, which was by Engel (2009) and considered only first-order diagrams plus a few selected higher-order contributions. The results provide a more comprehensive treatment of core-polarization effects on the transition operator, complementing nonperturbative methods like coupled-cluster theory (Hagen 2010, Jansen 2011) and the in-medium similarity renormalization group (Tsukiyama 2010, 2012; Hergert 2013) that have not yet been applied to double-beta decay. The approach enables future work incorporating chiral three-nucleon forces, two-body currents in the bare operator (Menendez 2011), and consistent non-empirical valence-space Hamiltonians from NN+3N forces, as well as investigation of the apparent suppression of the axial-vector coupling constant gA in the nuclear medium.
