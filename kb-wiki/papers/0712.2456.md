# Nonlinear Fluid Dynamics from Gravity

> Black branes in AdS5 appear in a four parameter family labeled by their velocity and temperature. Promoting these parameters to Goldstone modes or collective coordinate fields -- arbitrary functions of the coordinates on the boundary of AdS5 -- we use Einstein's equations together with regularity requirements and boundary conditions to determine their dynamics. The resultant equations turn out to be those of boundary fluid dynamics, with specific values for fluid parameters. Our analysis is perturbative in the boundary derivative expansion but is valid for arbitrary amplitudes. Our work may be regarded as a derivation of the nonlinear equations of boundary fluid dynamics from gravity. As a concrete application we find an explicit expression for the expansion of this fluid stress tensor including terms up to second order in the derivative expansion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Einstein's equations with a negative cosmological constant, when supplemented with regularity conditions and boundary conditions, reduce to the nonlinear equations of boundary fluid dynamics in a long-wavelength limit, with the derivation being perturbative in the boundary derivative expansion but valid for arbitrary amplitudes of the fluid fields.

## Method

The authors promote the constant temperature and velocity parameters of boosted black brane solutions in AdS5 to slowly varying functions of the boundary coordinates, then solve Einstein's equations order by order in a derivative expansion (with the small parameter being the ratio of the thermal length scale to the length scale of boundary variation). They work in Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, impose a background field gauge, and iteratively solve constraint equations (which become fluid dynamical conservation equations) and dynamical equations (which determine metric corrections) to construct the dual gravitational metric and extract the boundary stress tensor.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is eta/s = 1/(4 pi), confirming the result of Policastro, Son and Starinets (2001).
* The derivative-free terms in the stress tensor describe a perfect fluid with pressure pi^4 T^4 and entropy density s = 4 pi^4 T^3.
* The viscosity eta is given by pi^3 T^3, read off from the coefficient of the shear tensor sigma^{mu nu} in the first-order stress tensor.
* The second-order stress tensor coefficients include specific numerical factors: (ln 2) for the T_{2a}^{mu nu} term, 2 for the T_{2b}^{mu nu} term, and (2 - ln 2) multiplied by 1/3 for T_{2c}^{mu nu}, 1 for T_{2d}^{mu nu}, and 1 for T_{2e}^{mu nu}.

## Context

This work extends earlier derivations of linearized fluid dynamics from linearized gravity by Policastro, Son and Starinets (2001) and builds on nonlinear fluid-gravity duality studies by Janik (2005, 2006), Nakamura (2006), and Bhattacharyya (2007). The paper provides a systematic framework analogous to a "Chiral Lagrangian" for black brane horizons, enabling the explicit construction of gravitational metrics dual to any solution of the distinguished conformal fluid dynamics, and determines all coefficients of the nonlinear two-derivative terms in the fluid stress tensor for gravity on AdS5.
