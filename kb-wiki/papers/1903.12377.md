# Geometry of ultraperipheral nuclear collisions

> It is advocated that geometry of the interaction region of two heavy nuclei colliding at large impact parameters is important for the relative role of light-by-light scattering and QCD-initiated processes. Exclusive production of resonances is possible by dense electromagnetic fields in the interior space between the nuclei. The cross section of these processes is evaluated and some examples are considered. It is speculated that the exclusive production of $ρ^0$-mesons by two-photon processes forbidden by the Landau-Yang rule may become allowed within strong magnetic fields.

# Geometry of ultraperipheral nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The geometry of the interaction region in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions determines whether exclusive resonance production proceeds via light-by-light scattering or QCD-initiated processes; the exclusive production of ρ⁰-mesons by two-photon processes, normally forbidden by the Landau–Yang rule, may become allowed within strong magnetic fields due to the involvement of an odd number of photons.

## Method

The author evaluates the exclusive cross section for resonance production in ultraperipheral Pb-Pb collisions using the equivalent photon approximation, integrating the coherent photon flux over the two colliding nuclei and folding it with the two-photon resonance production cross section in the narrow-width approximation. The analytical formula for the exclusive cross section is derived and applied to π⁰ and η' mesons, with numerical estimates at LHC energies of √sₙₙ = 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV. The possibility of ρ⁰ production via two-photon collisions in the strong magnetic field between the ions is discussed qualitatively, drawing an analogy to photon splitting in astrophysical magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- The effective perturbation parameter for photon exchange in Pb-Pb collisions is Zα ≈ 0.6 (Z=82 for Pb), compared to α = 1/137 for elementary charges.
- The impact parameter for ultraperipheral Pb-Pb collisions must exceed 2Rₐ ≈ 14 fm; ions must be at least 5 fm apart to form dense electromagnetic fields without additional soft QCD interactions.
- The photon flux density dn/dx ≈ 300 for x ∼ 3×10⁻⁴ at √sₙₙ = 2.76 TeV.
- The exclusive cross section for π⁰ production is estimated as σₐₐ = 15.2 mb at √sₙₙ = 2.76 TeV and 22 mb at 5.02 TeV.
- The exclusive cross section for η' production is estimated as σₐₐ = 1.6 mb at √sₙₙ = 2.76 TeV and 2.8 mb at 5.02 TeV, using mₙ' = 957.8 MeV, Γₜₒₜ = 0.196 MeV, Br(γγ) = 0.0222, and Br(ρ⁰γ) = 0.29.
- The magnetic field between colliding ions can be as large as eB ≈ (10–15)mπ² ≈ 10¹⁸ G.
- The energy threshold condition for resonance production is √sₙₙ ≫ 4Rₐ m mₙ ≈ 138 mₙ, which is satisfied at LHC energies but RHIC energies are close to the threshold and NICA energies are below it.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of ultraperipheral collisions in heavy-ion physics, building on the equivalent photon approximation of Baur et al. (1990s) and the photon flux formulation of Bertulani et al. (2000s). The author contrasts the predicted diffractive structure in ρ⁰ pₜ-distributions from the black-disk model of Klein et al. (2010s) with the smooth distributions observed by the STAR Collaboration (2010s) and the absence of distinct diffractive structure in elastic hadron scattering data from TOTEM (2010s). The paper enables future experimental searches at the LHC for exclusive resonance production via two-photon processes, particularly for π⁰ and η' mesons, and motivates theoretical work on collective effects of strong magnetic fields on photon-photon interactions in nuclear collisions.
