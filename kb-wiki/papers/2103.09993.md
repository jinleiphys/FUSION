# Light-front holography with chiral symmetry breaking

> We present an analytically solvable 3D light-front Hamiltonian model for hadrons that extends light-front holography by including finite mass quarks and a longitudinal confinement term. We propose that the model is suitable as an improved analytic approximation to QCD at a low resolution scale. We demonstrate that it preserves desired Lorentz symmetries and it produces improved agreement with the experimental mass spectroscopy and other properties of the light mesons. Importantly, the model also respects chiral symmetry and the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation.

## Key claim
The paper presents an analytically solvable 3D light-front Hamiltonian model that extends light-front holography by adding a longitudinal confinement term and finite quark masses, claiming it respects chiral symmetry and the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation. The hedge is that the model is proposed as an improved analytic approximation to QCD at a low resolution scale, not as an exact solution.

## Method
The authors constructed a light-front Schrodinger equation with a separable effective potential consisting of the standard holographic transverse confinement (V_perp) plus a novel longitudinal confining term V_parallel = -sigma^2 partial_x (x(1-x) partial_x). They solved this equation analytically using separation of variables, obtaining Jacobi polynomial solutions for the longitudinal wave functions and 2D harmonic oscillator functions for the transverse part. The model parameters were fixed by fitting to the rho-pi mass splitting, the GMOR relation for pions and kaons, and the mass splitting between vector and axial-vector mesons.

## Key numbers
- Quark masses: m_{u,d} = 15 MeV, m_s = 261 MeV (compared to 46 MeV and 357 MeV in the invariant mass ansatz approach)
- Holographic confinement strength kappa = 0.59 GeV (for spin-0 mesons) and 0.54 GeV (for spin-1 mesons)
- Longitudinal confinement strength sigma = 0.62 GeV
- Predicted quark condensate: <q qbar> = (0.26 GeV)^3, compared to the chiral perturbation theory value of (0.34 GeV)^3 at mu=2 GeV
- Pion mass from GMOR relation: M_pi^2 = 2 sigma m_{u,d} + 4 m_{u,d}^2, yielding M_pi = 140 MeV
- Excited pion masses: M_{pi'} = 1.52 GeV (experiment: 1.30 GeV), M_{pi''} = 2.12 GeV (experiment: 1.81 GeV)
- Excited pion decay constants: f_{pi'} = 12 MeV, f_{pi''} = 8.5 MeV (ground state f_pi = 182 MeV vs experimental 130 MeV)

## Context
This work extends light-front holography (LFH) as developed by Brodsky, de Teramond, and collaborators (Brodsky 2014, Brodsky 2020) by adding longitudinal dynamics that were previously unexplored in that framework. It addresses the failure of the invariant mass ansatz (IMA) approach to reproduce the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation, which requires linear quark mass dependence of M_pi^2 rather than the quadratic dependence predicted by IMA. The model enables future quantum many-body calculations by providing analytically solvable wave functions that can serve as a starting point for incorporating more realistic effective interactions, such as the one-gluon exchange interaction already applied to heavy quarkonium in Li (2017).
