# The Effective Chiral Lagrangian From the Theta Term

> We construct the effective chiral Lagrangian involving hadronic and electromagnetic interactions originating from the QCD theta term. We impose vacuum alignment at both quark and hadronic levels, including field redefinitions to eliminate pion tadpoles. We show that leading time-reversal-violating (TV) hadronic interactions are related to isospin-violating interactions that can in principle be determined from charge-symmetry-breaking experiments. We discuss the complications that arise from TV electromagnetic interactions. Some implications of the expected sizes of various pion-nucleon TV interactions are presented, and the pion-nucleon form factor is used as an example.

## Key claim

The QCD theta term's time-reversal-violating (TV) effects at low energies are intimately connected to isospin-violating interactions from the quark mass difference, such that leading TV hadronic operators in the chiral Lagrangian are related to charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) operators that can in principle be determined from CSB experiments, though this connection becomes ineffective at higher orders due to unpaired operators.

## Method

The authors constructed the effective chiral Lagrangian for pions and nucleons (using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with SU(2)×SU(2) chiral symmetry) that includes all TV interactions originating from the QCD theta term, imposing vacuum alignment at both the quark and hadronic levels through field redefinitions to eliminate pion tadpoles. They classified TV operators by chiral index (power counting in Q/M_QCD) and included electromagnetic interactions from both soft photons and integrated-out hard photons, following the same chiral-symmetry-breaking pattern as the theta term.

## Key numbers

- The neutron EDM from the Standard Model with theta=0 is expected to be d_n ~ 10^{-32} e cm, while the experimental bound is |d_n| < 2.9 × 10^{-26} e cm
- The proton EDM bound extracted from 199Hg is |d_p| < 7.9 × 10^{-25} e cm
- Planned deuteron EDM sensitivity is |d_d| ~ 10^{-29} e cm
- The Jarlskog invariant for CKM CP violation is J_CP ~ 3 × 10^{-5}
- The pion decay constant is F_pi ~ 186 MeV
- The pion-nucleon axial-vector coupling is g_A ~ 1.267
- The quark mass difference parameter is epsilon = (m_d - m_u)/(m_u + m_d)
- The chiral-symmetry-breaking scale is M_QCD ~ 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the pioneering treatment of the theta term by Baluni (1979) and Crewther, Di Vecchia, Veneziano, and Witten (1979) to higher orders in the chiral expansion using modern effective field theory methods, building on the systematic construction of isospin-violating operators by van Kolck (1994) and the earlier work of Hockings and van Kolck. The results enable a systematic classification of TV pion-nucleon interactions by their chiral order (rather than treating all non-derivative couplings on the same footing as in much of the nuclear physics literature), and provide a framework for relating TV observables (such as the pion-nucleon form factor) to CSB experiments, potentially allowing identification of the dominant mechanism if hadronic EDMs are measured.
