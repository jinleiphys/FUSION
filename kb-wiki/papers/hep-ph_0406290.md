# Dynamics of confined gluons

> Propagation of gluons in the confining vacuum is studied in the framework of the background perturbation theory, where nonperturbative background contains confining correlators. Two settings of the problem are considered. In the first the confined gluon is evolving in time together with static quark and antiquark forming the one-gluon static hybrid. The hybrid spectrum is calculated in terms of string tension and is in agreement with earlier analytic and lattice calculations. In the second setting the confined gluon is exchanged between quarks and the gluon Green's function is calculated, giving rise to the Coulomb potential modified at large distances. The resulting screening radius of 0.5 fm presents a serious problem when confronting with lattice and experimental data. A possible solution of this discrepancy is discussed.

## Key claim

The confined gluon exchange between static quarks produces a screened Coulomb potential with a screening radius of approximately 0.5 fm, but this screening is in serious conflict with lattice and experimental data; the author suggests that the bosonic string term may compensate for the screening to restore agreement.

## Method

The author uses background perturbation theory (BPT) within the field correlator method (FCM), where the nonperturbative vacuum field contains confining correlators. The gluon Green's function is computed for two settings: first, for a gluon evolving with static quark and antiquark to form a one-gluon static hybrid; second, for a gluon exchanged between quarks to obtain the modified Coulomb potential. The path integrals are evaluated using einbein variables and stationary point analysis.

## Key numbers

* Hybrid mass at large R: M_hybrid(R) = sigma R + (3/2)(sigma/R)^(1/3) + sqrt(12)/R, where sigma is the string tension and R is the quark separation.
* Gluelump mass in the R to 0 limit: M_gluelump = 2 sqrt(3 sigma), agreeing with dedicated calculations at the 1.5% level.
* Screening factor xi(R) at R = 0.46 fm: xi = 0.469, indicating the Coulomb potential is reduced by approximately half at this distance.
* Screening factor xi(R) at R = 0.98 fm: xi = 0.241.
* Screening factor xi(R) at R = 1.645 fm: xi = 0.086.
* The screening radius where xi(R) is approximately 0.5 is about 0.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analytic calculations of hybrid and gluelump spectra (Simonov, 2002-2003) and the field correlator method developed by the author and collaborators. The calculated screening factor xi(R) contradicts lattice results from Bali (2000) and experimental heavy quarkonia spectra (Badalian, 2003-2004) which show that the unscreened Coulomb potential works well up to distances of about 1 fm. The author proposes that the bosonic string Casimir energy term, as studied in lattice analyses around 2004, may compensate for the screening and restore agreement with data.
