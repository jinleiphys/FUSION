# Nuclear structure in strong magnetic fields: nuclei in the crust of a magnetar

> Covariant density functional theory is used to study the effect of strong magnetic fields, up to the limit predicted for neutron stars (for magnetars $B \approx10^{18}$G), on nuclear structure. All new terms in the equation of motion resulting from time reversal symmetry breaking by the magnetic field and the induced currents, as well as axial deformation, are taken into account in a self-consistent fashion. For nuclei in the iron region of the nuclear chart it is found that fields in the order of magnitude of $10^{17}$G significantly affect bulk properties like masses and radii.

## Key claim

Using covariant density functional theory with full self-consistency, the authors find that magnetic fields of at least approximately 10^17 G are required to appreciably modify nuclear ground-state properties in the iron region, but the exact threshold depends strongly on the specific nucleus and its single-particle level structure.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean-field (RMF) equations with the NL3 parameter set, including the coupling of the external magnetic field to both the proton orbital motion (Landau coupling) and the intrinsic magnetic dipole moments of protons and neutrons (Pauli coupling). The equations were solved self-consistently in axial symmetry using an oscillator expansion in N=20 major shells, with time-reversal symmetry broken by the magnetic field, leading to induced currents and time-odd mean fields (nuclear magnetism). Pairing correlations were neglected because static magnetic fields are expected to suppress superconductivity.

## Key numbers

- For 16O, the first level crossing at the Fermi energy occurs at B ≈ 5 × 10^17 G, with a jump in binding energy per particle of around 400 keV.
- For 16O, the change in binding energy is less than 100 keV per particle for field strengths less than 5 × 10^17 G.
- For nuclei in isotopic chains close to 56Fe, the minimum field for the first level crossing ranges between approximately 0.5 × 10^17 G and 3 × 10^17 G.
- At B ≈ 0.5 × 10^17 G, changes of a few tenths of keV in binding energy per nucleon occur for some N=Z nuclei near 56Fe.
- At B ≈ 2 × 10^17 G, the hierarchy of binding energies among the most bound nuclei near 56Fe is altered.
- The maximum field considered is B ≈ 10^18 G, near the virial theorem limit of B ≈ 2 × 10^18 G.

## Context

This work extends earlier qualitative studies by Kondratyev and collaborators (2000, 2001), who used a simple non-self-consistent method to suggest that fields as low as 10^16 G could modify nuclear shell structure. The present fully self-consistent covariant DFT approach shows that the frozen-field approximation breaks down after the first level crossing, because spin polarization and induced currents (nuclear magnetism) become important. The results enable future quantitative studies of magnetar crust composition, elastic properties, glitches, and cooling, provided pairing and one-pion exchange are included in the model.
