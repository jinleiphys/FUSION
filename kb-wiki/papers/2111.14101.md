# Momentum dependence of near-threshold photoproduction of $Ξ^-$ hyperons off nuclei and their properties in the nuclear medium

> We study the near-threshold inclusive photoproduction of $Ξ^-$ hyperons off $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W target nuclei within a first-collision model relying on the nuclear spectral function and including incoherent $Ξ^-$ production in direct elementary $γp \to K^+{K^+}Ξ^-$ and $γn \to K^+{K^0}Ξ^-$ processes. The model takes into account the impact of the nuclear effective scalar $K^+$, $K^0$, $Ξ^-$ and their Coulomb potentials on these processes as well as the absorption of final $Ξ^-$ hyperons in nuclear matter, the binding of intranuclear nucleons and their Fermi motion. We calculate the absolute differential cross sections and their ratios for the production of $Ξ^-$ hyperons off these nuclei at laboratory polar angles $\le$ 45$^{\circ}$ by photons with energies of 2.5 and 3.0 GeV, with and without imposing the phase space constraints on the $Ξ^-$ emission angle in the respective $γp$ center-of-mass system. We also calculate the momentum dependence of the transparency ratio for $Ξ^-$ hyperons for the $^{184}$W/$^{12}$C combination at these photon energies. We show that the $Ξ^-$ momentum distributions (absolute and relative) at the adopted initial photon energies possess, contrary to the transparency ratio for $Ξ^-$ hyperons, a definite sensitivity to the considered changes in the scalar $Ξ^-$ nuclear mean-field potential at saturation density $ρ_0$ in the low-momentum range of 0.1--0.8 GeV/c. Therefore, the precise measurements of these distributions in a dedicated experiment at the CEBAF facility will provide valuable information on the $Ξ^-$ in-medium properties, which will be supplementary to that inferred from studying of the ($K^-$,$K^+$) reactions at initial momenta of 1.6--1.8 GeV/c.

## Key claim

The momentum distributions of $\Xi^-$ hyperons photoproduced off $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W nuclei near threshold are sensitive to the $\Xi^-$ nuclear mean-field potential in the low-momentum range of 0.1–0.8 GeV/c, but the transparency ratio for $\Xi^-$ hyperons is not sensitive enough to reliably determine this potential.

## Method

The author employs a first-collision model based on the nuclear spectral function, including incoherent $\Xi^-$ production in elementary $\gamma p \to K^+K^+\Xi^-$ and $\gamma n \to K^+K^0\Xi^-$ processes. The model accounts for nuclear effective scalar potentials for $K^+$, $K^0$, and $\Xi^-$, their Coulomb potentials, final-state absorption of $\Xi^-$ hyperons in nuclear matter, nucleon binding, and Fermi motion. Calculations are performed for three scenarios of the $\Xi^-$ scalar potential at saturation density $\rho_0$: a momentum-dependent potential with $U_{\Xi^-}(0) = -24.3$ MeV, one with $U_{\Xi^-}(0) = -11.96$ MeV, and a zero potential at all momenta.

## Key numbers

- Incident photon energies: 2.5 and 3.0 GeV.
- Target nuclei: $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W.
- Laboratory polar angle range for $\Xi^-$ detection: $\le 45^\circ$.
- Free threshold energy for $\gamma p \to K^+K^+\Xi^-$: $E_\gamma^{\rm th} = 2.372$ GeV.
- $\Xi^-$ momentum range studied: 0.2–2.5 GeV/c.
- Low-momentum sensitivity range: 0.1–0.8 GeV/c.
- $\Xi^-$–nucleus potential depths at zero momentum: $U_{\Xi^-}(0) = -11.96$ MeV and $U_{\Xi^-}(0) = -24.3$ MeV.
- $\Xi^-$–nucleus potential depth from BNL–E906 analysis: $(-17 \pm 6)$ MeV in momentum transfer range 390–600 MeV/c.
- Free total cross section parametrization for $\gamma p \to K^+K^+\Xi^-$: at $E_\gamma = 2.5$ GeV, off-shell cross section $\approx 5$ nb; at $E_\gamma = 3.0$ GeV, $\approx 10$ nb (for a bound proton in $^{12}$C with 16 MeV binding and 250 MeV/c momentum opposite the beam).
- $\Xi^-$–nucleon elastic cross section: $\sim 10$ mb in the momentum range 0.2–2.5 GeV/c.
- Mean free path for elastic $\Xi^-N$ scattering: $\approx 11$ fm for $^{12}$C, $\approx 8$ fm for $^{184}$W.
- Nuclear radii: $^{12}$C $\approx 3$ fm, $^{184}$W $\approx 7.4$ fm.
- Average nucleon densities: $<\rho_N> = 0.55\rho_0$ for $^{12}$C, $0.76\rho_0$ for $^{184}$W, with $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Coulomb potentials for charged hadrons: $\approx +3.6$ MeV and $+17.3$ MeV for positively charged hadrons in $^{12}$C and $^{184}$W, respectively; $-3.6$ MeV and $-17.3$ MeV for negatively charged hadrons.

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach previously developed by Paryev (2021) for near-threshold $\Xi^-$ production in antikaon-induced reactions to photon-induced reactions, motivated by the weaker initial-state interactions of photons compared to hadrons. The results are intended to complement information on the $\Xi^-$ nuclear potential obtained from ($K^-$,$K^+$) reactions at beam momenta of 1.6–1.8 GeV/c studied by the KEK–PS E224 and BNL–AGS E885 Collaborations, and from emulsion data analyzed by the J-PARC E05 and E07 Collaborations. The predictions are designed for future dedicated experiments at the CEBAF facility, where exclusive $\Xi^-$ photoproduction has already been studied using CLAS and GlueX detectors.
