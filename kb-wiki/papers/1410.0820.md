# $B_s$ mesons: semileptonic and nonleptonic decays

> In this contribution we compute some nonleptonic and semileptonic decay widths of $B_s$ mesons, working in the context of constituent quark models \cite{Albertus:2014gba, Albertus:2014bfa}. For the case of semileptonic decays we consider reactions leading to kaons or different $J^π$ $D_s$ mesons. The study of nonleptonic decays has been done in the factorisation approximation and includes the final states enclosed in Table 2.

## Key claim
The authors compute semileptonic and nonleptonic decay widths of \(B_s\) mesons using constituent quark models, with the nonleptonic results relying on the factorisation approximation; the semileptonic \(B_s \to K\) form factor combines valence quark contributions with a \(B^*\)-pole term and a multiply-subtracted Omnes dispersion relation fitted to both quark model results at high \(q^2\) and light cone sum rule results at low \(q^2\).

## Method
Semileptonic decays \(B_s \to K\) and \(B_s \to D_s^{(*)}\) (with \(J^\pi = 0^-, 0^+, 1^-, 1^+, 2^-, 2^+\)) were evaluated using constituent quark model form factors, supplemented by a \(B^*\)-pole term and a multiply-subtracted Omnes dispersion relation for the \(B_s \to K\) case. Nonleptonic decays \(\bar B_s \to c\bar s M_F\) (with \(M_F\) a light pseudoscalar or vector meson) were computed in the factorisation approximation, where hadron matrix elements are products of quark-current matrix elements. Helicity formalism from Ivanov (2005) was used for the leptonic and hadronic tensor contractions.

## Key numbers
- Total decay width for \(B_s \to K l^+ \nu_l\): \(\Gamma = (5.47^{+0.54}_{-0.46}) |V_{ub}|^2 \times 10^{-9}\) MeV.
- Branching fraction for \(B_s \to D_s^+\) (semileptonic, \(l = e,\mu\)): 2.32%.
- Branching fraction for \(B_s \to D_s^{*+}\) (semileptonic, \(l = e,\mu\)): 6.26%.
- Branching fraction for \(\bar B_s \to D_s^+ \pi^-\) (nonleptonic): 0.53%.
- Branching fraction for \(\bar B_s \to D_s^+ \rho^-\) (nonleptonic): 1.26%.
- Branching fraction for \(\bar B_s \to D_s^{*+} \pi^-\) (nonleptonic): 0.45%.
- Branching fraction for \(\bar B_s \to D_s^{*+} \rho^-\) (nonleptonic): 1.35%.

## Context
This work extends constituent quark model calculations of \(B_s\) decays, with semileptonic results compared to light cone sum rules from Duplancic (2008) and lattice QCD from Bouchard (2014), and nonleptonic results compared to relativistic quark model calculations by Faustov (2012) and earlier quark model work by Zhao (2006). The results enable improved constraints on CKM matrix elements, particularly \(|V_{ub}|\), and provide predictions for branching fractions of orbitally excited \(D_s\) mesons that can be tested by future experiments.
