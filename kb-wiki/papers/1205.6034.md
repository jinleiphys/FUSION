# Determination of 8B(p,gamma)9C reaction rate from 9C breakup

> The astrophysical factor of the 8B(p,gamma)9C at zero energy, S18(0), is determined from three-body model analysis of 9C breakup processes. The elastic breakup 208Pb(9C,p8B)208Pb at 65 MeV/nucleon and the one-proton removal reaction of 9C at 285 MeV/nucleon on C and Al targets are calculated with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) and the eikonal reaction theory (ERT), respectively. The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of 9C in the p-8B configuration extracted from the two reactions show good consistency, in contrast to in the previous studies. As a result of the present analysis, S18(0) = 66 \pm 10 eVb is obtained.

## Key claim

The astrophysical factor S18(0) for the 8B(p,gamma)9C reaction is determined to be 66 ± 10 eVb from a three-body model analysis of 9C breakup processes, resolving a previous discrepancy of about 50% between values obtained from Coulomb dissociation and ANC methods.

## Method

The elastic breakup 208Pb(9C,p8B)208Pb at 65 MeV/nucleon was calculated using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), and the one-proton removal reaction of 9C at 285 MeV/nucleon on C and Al targets was analyzed using the eikonal reaction theory (ERT). The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of 9C in the p-8B configuration was extracted from both reactions and used to determine S18(0).

## Key numbers

- S18(0) = 66 ± 10 eVb, the principal result from combining elastic breakup and proton removal analyses
- (C_{p 8B}^{9C})^2 = 1.7 ± 0.3 fm^{-1}, the extracted ANC squared value
- From elastic breakup: S18(0) = 67.3 eVb, with (C_{p 8B}^{9C})^2 = 1.78 fm^{-1}
- From one-proton removal (average of C and Al targets): S18(0) = 63.7 eVb, with (C_{p 8B}^{9C})^2 = 1.69 fm^{-1}
- One-proton removal cross sections: 44.9 mb (calc.) vs 48 ± 8 mb (expt.) for 12C target; 53.9 mb (calc.) vs 55 ± 11 mb (expt.) for 27Al target
- Uncertainty from optical potential variation: 8% for elastic breakup, 20% for one-proton removal; additional 5% uncertainty from neglecting Coulomb breakup in proton removal

## Context

This work resolves a significant discrepancy between previous S18(0) values: around 80 eVb from Coulomb dissociation (Motobayashi, 2002) and cluster model (Descouvemont, 1993), versus around 45 eVb from ANC methods using transfer reactions (Beaumel, 1994) and proton removal (Trache, 2003). The present result of 66 ± 10 eVb lies between these groups, slightly favoring the higher values. The analysis enables more reliable determination of the 8B(p,gamma)9C reaction rate for the hot pp chain in low-metallicity supermassive stars, and the authors note that further investigation of the d(8B,9C)n transfer reaction and inclusion of p+p+7Be configurations would be important next steps.
