# Quarkonium Production in Heavy Ion Collisions: From Open Quantum System to Transport Equation

> Using the open quantum system formalism and effective field theory of QCD, we derive the Boltzmann transport equation of quarkonium inside the quark-gluon plasma. Our derivation illuminates that the success of transport equations in quarkonium phenomenology is closely related to the separation of scales in the problem.

## Key claim

The Boltzmann transport equation for quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma can be systematically derived from QCD using the open quantum system formalism and potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD), but this derivation is valid only under the specific separation of scales \(M \gg Mv \gg Mv^2 \gtrsim T\), which is approximately satisfied in current heavy ion collision experiments.

## Method

The authors treat the total system (heavy quark-antiquark pairs plus quark-gluon plasma) as a closed unitary system, then trace out the plasma environment to obtain a non-unitary evolution for the quarkonium subsystem. Working in the quantum optical limit (justified by the hierarchy \(\tau_R \gg \tau_E\) and \(\tau_R \gg \tau_S\)), they expand the time evolution operator to second order in perturbation theory, obtaining a Lindblad equation. Taking the Wigner transform of the subsystem density matrix then reproduces the Boltzmann transport equation, which is coupled with transport equations for open heavy quarks.

## Key numbers

* The melting temperature of \(\Upsilon\)(2S) is set to 210 MeV.
* The coupling constant is fixed to \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) at the scale \(Mv\).
* The singlet attractive potential is \(V_S = -0.56 / r\).
* The branching ratio of \(\Upsilon\)(2S) to \(\Upsilon\)(1S) in the hadronic phase is 0.26.
* Results are shown for 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends the open quantum system approach to quarkonium dissociation and transport previously developed by Brambilla et al. (2011, 2016) by deriving the full Boltzmann transport equation from QCD and demonstrating its connection to the Lindblad equation. The derivation explains why transport equations have been phenomenologically successful in describing quarkonium production, as the separation of scales \(M \gg Mv \gg Mv^2 \gtrsim T\) justifies the Markovian approximation. The results enable future inclusion of additional excited bottomonium states (1P, 2P, 3S) and generalization to doubly charmed baryons.
