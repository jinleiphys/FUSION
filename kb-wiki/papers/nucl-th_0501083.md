# A Novel Dynamical Approach To Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> A transport model for ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions based on the mean free path approach is proposed. The method is manifestly Lorentz invariant. We discuss some calculations for pp and AA collisions and compare to a previously proposed transport model and to data. We demonstrate that our approach gives a different impact parameter distribution already in pp collisions as compared to the previous one. The role of hadronization times is discussed. Comparison to data is reasonable and the model can be easily modified to take into account genuine many body effects and quantum statistics similarly to low energy heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel, manifestly Lorentz-invariant transport model for ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions based on a mean free path approach, and demonstrates that this method gives a different impact parameter distribution in pp collisions compared to the standard "black sphere" method, even though average quantities may agree. The authors hedge that the model's comparison to data is "reasonable" but that quantum statistics and genuine many-body effects are not yet included, and that the hadronization time is treated as a free parameter whose value critically affects the results.

## Method

The method (called Relativistic Boltzmann equation, ReB) selects collision partners by searching for particles that are close in coordinate space and far apart in velocity space, then calculates a collision probability from the local density, relative velocity, and elementary cross section using the mean free path formalism. For each event, at each time step, the closest pair of particles in phase space is identified via a Lorentz-invariant quantity, and a random number determines whether a collision occurs; if it does, the elementary process is modeled by PYTHIA for center-of-mass energies above 4 GeV, and by a parametrization of data for lower energies. The partons produced are then hadronized by JETSET after a user-specified hadronization time, during which partons are assumed not to collide.

## Key numbers

- In pp collisions at 200 GeV and 2000 GeV center-of-mass energy, the multiplicity of charged particles decreases with impact parameter b, and the BEL (Blacker, Edger, Larger) effect is reproduced: at higher energies the elementary cross section increases, so the number of involved impact parameters increases.
- For zero hadronization time in pp collisions at 200 GeV, the model fails to reproduce UA5 data unless the low-energy cutoff for PYTHIA is increased to 30 GeV.
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV with zero hadronization time and a 30 GeV cutoff, the model overestimates data for pseudorapidities less than about 3 units, especially for kaons.
- With finite hadronization times (0.1 fm/c or larger) and a 4 GeV cutoff, the model fits pp data reasonably, but Au+Au data at low pseudorapidity are still poorly reproduced.
- After implementing a parametrization of elementary collisions for energies below 4 GeV down to the pion production threshold, the agreement with Au+Au data improves, though the model still slightly overestimates data at small pseudorapidities.
- In Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, the energy density in a central cell of radius 2 fm reaches values much larger than in pp or d+Au collisions, and kinetic equilibrium is reached at about 4 fm/c, similar to UrQMD calculations.
- At 4 fm/c in Au+Au collisions, the ratio of partons to hadrons in the central cell is about 10, suggesting a QGP state.
- The transverse energy density in the central cell for Au+Au collisions at equilibrium is less than 0.5 GeV/fm^3.

## Context

This work extends the mean free path method (known as Boltzmann Nordheim Vlasov, BNV) previously developed for low-energy heavy ion collisions by Aldo (1994) to the relativistic regime, and compares it to the standard "black sphere" transport method used in codes such as JPCIAE (based on PYTHIA) and the parton cascade model of Geiger (1997). The approach enables future inclusion of quantum statistics (Bose and Fermi) and many-body effects, similar to what has been done at low energies, and the authors plan to incorporate a phase transition to the quark-gluon plasma using a Constrained Molecular Dynamics model for quarks developed by Terranova (2004). The paper also notes that low-pseudorapidity particles are produced at late times (greater than 5 fm/c) from low-energy hadron-hadron collisions, which may wash out signals of the QGP such as fluctuations in D-meson production.
