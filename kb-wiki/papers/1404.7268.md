# The role of excited states in rp-process for sd shell nuclei

> We investigate the importance of proton capture on low-lying excited states that are thermally excited in hot stellar environments. In addition to the previously known case of $^{32}$Cl$(p,γ)^{33}$Ar we find several other sd-shell nuclei with large stellar enhancement factors. We discuss the uncertainty this introduces into rp-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The paper identifies several sd-shell nuclei beyond the previously known case of \(^{32}\)Cl where proton capture on thermally excited low-lying states significantly enhances reaction rates, but emphasizes that the largest uncertainty in these calculations is the precise location of resonances near proton decay thresholds, not the spectroscopic factors or gamma widths.

## Method

The authors calculated direct proton capture rates using the RADCAP code with Woods-Saxon potentials and resonant capture rates using shell-model spectroscopic factors from the USD interaction, with proton single-particle widths obtained from a Coulomb penetration code. For each target nucleus, they computed the total reaction rate as the sum of contributions from the ground state and all thermally populated excited states, weighted by their Boltzmann population factors, and defined the stellar enhancement factor (SEF) as the ratio of the total rate to the ground-state-only rate.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{32}\)Cl(p,\(\gamma\))\(^{33}\)Ar, the stellar enhancement factor (SEF) is approximately 5 for temperatures in the range 0.1 to 0.8 GK, driven by the \(l=0\) spectroscopic factor of the first excited state.
- The first excited state of \(^{32}\)Cl lies at 89.9 keV with spin \(2^+\), while the ground state has spin \(1^+\).
- For \(^{31}\)Cl, the first excited state at 747 keV (spin \(1/2^+\)) does not produce a large SEF; direct capture on the ground state dominates.
- For \(^{23}\)Al, the first excited state at 460 keV (assumed spin \(1/2^+\)) does not significantly enhance the rate; the total rate is dominated by resonant capture on the ground state via the second \(2^+\) state at 3.742 MeV (USD shell-model prediction).
- For \(^{24}\)Al, the first excited state at 425.8 keV (spin \(1^+\)) contributes to the rate, with several resonances on the ground state (spin \(4^+\)) dominating the total reaction rate.
- For \(^{34}\)Cl, the first excited state at 146 keV (spin \(3^+\)) was investigated; the ground state has spin \(0^+\).
- The uncertainty in spectroscopic factors and gamma decay widths is on the order of 20%, but the largest uncertainty is the precise location of resonances near proton decay thresholds.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Schatz et al. (2005) that capture on the first excited state of \(^{32}\)Cl is crucial for the \(^{32}\)Cl(p,\(\gamma\))\(^{33}\)Ar reaction rate, by systematically surveying other sd-shell nuclei for similar stellar enhancement effects. Previous predictions of rp-process rates (Wiescher and Woosley 1981, Iliadis 2001, Woehr and Görres 1994, Herndl et al. 1995, Vouzoukas 1994) generally assumed all interacting nuclei are in their ground states. The results enable improved rp-process nucleosynthesis calculations for X-ray burst models by identifying which reaction rates carry additional uncertainty from thermally excited target states.
