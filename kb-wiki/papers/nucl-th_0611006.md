# Dynamics of $^{1}S_0$ diproton formation in the $pd\to \{pp\}_sn$ and $pN\to \{pp\}_sÏ€$ reactions in the GeV region

> Mechanisms for the production of $^1S_0$ diproton pairs, ${pp}_s$, in the $pd\to {pp}_s n$ reaction are studied at proton beam energies 0.5 -- 2 GeV in kinematics similar to those of backward elastic $pd$ scattering. This reaction provides valuable information on the short-range $NN$ and $pd$ interactions that is complementary to that investigated in the well known $pd\to dp$ and $dp\to p(0^\circ)X$ processes. The $pd\to {pp}_sn$ reaction is related to the subprocesses $Ï€^0 d\to pn$ and $pN\to {pp}_s Ï€$ using two different one--pion--exchange (OPE) diagrams. Within both these models a reasonable agreement could be obtained with the data below 1GeV. The similar energy dependence of the $pd\to\{pp\}_s n$ and $pd\to dp$ cross sections and the small ratio of about 1.5% in the production of $\{pp\}_s$ to deuteron final states follow naturally within the OPE models.

# Dynamics of $^{1}S_0$ diproton formation in the $pd\to \{pp\}_sn$ and $pN\to \{pp\}_s\pi$ reactions in the GeV region

## Key claim
The one-pion-exchange (OPE) mechanisms can reproduce the $pd\to \{pp\}_s n$ cross section data below 1 GeV, but only when the ONE (one-nucleon-exchange) contribution is suppressed near 0.8 GeV; above 1 GeV the OPE models fall faster than the data, indicating that additional mechanisms are needed.

## Method
The authors analyzed two distinct one-pion-exchange (OPE) diagrams: OPE-I, which connects the $pd\to \{pp\}_s n$ reaction to the $pN\to \{pp\}_s \pi$ subprocess, and OPE-II, which connects it to the $\pi^0 d\to pn$ subprocess. They also considered baryon/Reggeon exchange (BRE) in the $t$-channel. The calculations used the CD Bonn potential for the deuteron wave function and the SAID SP96 solution for the $pn\to d\pi^0$ cross section, with a $\pi NN$ form factor cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ varied between 0.65 and 1.0 GeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of $\{pp\}_s$ to deuteron production in $pd$ collisions is $R_{II}\approx 0.016 - 0.013$ from OPE-II for beam energies 0.6-1.9 GeV, compared to the experimental value $R^{exp}=0.010 - 0.011$.
- The $pd\to \{pp\}_s n$ data were measured for proton beam kinetic energies in the range 0.6-1.9 GeV, with diproton excitation energy cut $E_{pp}<3$ MeV.
- The $\pi NN$ coupling constant used is $f_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi=0.0796$.
- The OPE-II model gives best agreement with $pd\to \{pp\}_s n$ data at $T_p\approx 0.8$ GeV, where the ONE mechanism vanishes due to the repulsive core in the $NN$ interaction.
- The OPE-I model predicts the ratio $R_I$ depends on the pion production mechanism: $R_I = (4/9)r$ for $\Delta$ excitation, $(1/9)r$ for $N$ or $N^*$ rescattering, or $r$ for $T=1/2$ baryon exchange, where $r$ is the ratio of $pp\to \{pp\}_s\pi^0$ to $pn\to d\pi^0$ cross sections.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the $T_{20}$ puzzle in backward $pd$ elastic scattering, where the impulse approximation (ONE) fails to describe the tensor analyzing power for nucleon momenta above 0.3 GeV/c (Arvieux et al., Punjabi et al., Azhgirey et al.). The authors build on earlier OPE models for $pd\to dp$ (Wilkin, 1969) and on the ONE+$\Delta$ analysis of $pd\to \{pp\}_s n$ (Haidenbauer et al., 2003). The recent measurement of $pp\to \{pp\}_s\pi^0$ at 0.8 GeV (Dymov et al., 2006) enabled the OPE-I analysis. The results enable future measurements of the tensor analyzing power $T_{20}$ in $pd\to \{pp\}_s n$ (SPINCOSY experiment) to distinguish between ONE, OPE, and $\Delta$-isobar contributions.
