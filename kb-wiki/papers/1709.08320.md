# Effective description of hot QCD medium in strong magnetic field and longitudinal conductivity

> Hot QCD medium effects have been studied in the effective quasi-particle description of quark-gluon plasma. This model encodes the collective excitation of gluons and quarks/anti-quarks in the thermal medium in terms of effective quarks and gluons having non-trivial energy dispersion relation. The present investigation involves the extension of the effective quasi-particle model in strong magnetic field limit. Realizing, hot QCD medium in the strong magnetic field as an effective grand canonical system in terms of the modified quark, anti-quark and gluonic degrees of freedom, the thermodynamics has been studied. Further, the Debye mass in hot QCD medium has to be sensitive to the magnetic field, and subsequently the same has been observed for the effective hot QCD coupling. As an implication, electrical conductivity (longitudinal) has been studied within an effective kinetic theory description of hot QCD in the presence of the strong magnetic field. The hot QCD equation of state (EoS), dependence entering through the effective coupling and quasi-parton distribution function, found to have a significant impact on the longitudinal electrical conductivity in strong magnetic field background.

## Key claim

The paper extends the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM) to describe hot QCD medium in a strong magnetic field, finding that the hot QCD equation of state (EoS) dependence, entering through the effective coupling and quasi-parton distribution functions, has a significant impact on the longitudinal electrical conductivity in the strong magnetic field background, but only within the regime where the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation is valid (\(T^2 \ll |eB|\)).

## Method

The authors extended the EQPM by incorporating relativistic Landau levels into the quark/anti-quark dispersion relations and distribution functions, applying the LLL approximation for the strong field limit (\(|eB| \gg T^2\)). They computed QCD thermodynamic quantities (energy density, pressure, entropy density, sound velocity) and the Debye mass from the modified distribution functions, then defined an effective running coupling \(\alpha_{eff}(T)\). Using this effective coupling and the quasi-parton distributions, they solved the Boltzmann equation with a collision integral dominated by \(1 \rightarrow 2\) processes (gluon to quark/anti-quark pair) to calculate the longitudinal electrical conductivity.

## Key numbers

*   The magnetic field in off-central heavy-ion collisions can reach \(eB \sim (1 - 15) m_{\pi}^{2}\) (where \(m_{\pi}\) is the pion mass), and in early cosmological stages up to \(eB \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\).
*   The QCD transition temperature is taken as \(T_c = 170\) MeV throughout the calculations.
*   The Debye mass in the strong magnetic field is given by \((m_{D}^{2}/\alpha_s) = 4\left( \frac{6T^{2}}{\pi} \text{PolyLog}[2,z_{g}] + \frac{3eB}{\pi} \frac{z_{q}}{1+z_{q}} \right)\), where \(z_g\) and \(z_q\) are effective fugacities encoding the EoS.
*   The effective coupling is defined as \(\alpha_{eff} = \alpha_s(T) \frac{ \left( \frac{6T^{2}}{\pi^{2}} \text{PolyLog}[2,z_{g}] + \frac{3eB}{\pi^{2}} \frac{z_{q}}{(1+z_{q})} \right) }{ \left( T^{2} + \frac{3eB}{2\pi^{2}} \right) }\).

## Context

This work extends the EQPM, originally developed by Chandra and Ravisankar (2011, 2007) for hot QCD without magnetic fields, to include strong magnetic fields. It builds on prior studies of QCD thermodynamics in magnetic fields using lattice QCD (Levkova 2013) and hadron resonance gas models (Bali 2014), with which the authors compare their thermodynamic results. The calculation of longitudinal conductivity in a strong magnetic field, using a kinetic theory approach with LLL approximation and \(1 \rightarrow 2\) processes, follows the work of Hattori (2016). This enables future studies of transport coefficients in a magnetized QGP that incorporate realistic QCD equations of state.
