# Experimental constraint on quark electric dipole moments

> The electric dipole moments (EDMs) of nucleons are sensitive probes of additional $\cal CP$ violation sources beyond the standard model to account for the baryon number asymmetry of the universe. As a fundamental quantity of the nucleon structure, tensor charge is also a bridge that relates nucleon EDMs to quark EDMs. With a combination of nucleon EDM measurements and tensor charge extractions, we investigate the experimental constraint on quark EDMs, and its sensitivity to $\cal CP$ violation sources from new physics beyond the electroweak scale. We obtain the current limits on quark EDMs as $1.27\times10^{-24}\,e\cdot{\rm cm}$ for the up quark and $1.17\times10^{-24}\,e\cdot{\rm cm}$ for the down quark at the scale of $4\,\rm GeV^2$. We also study the impact of future nucleon EDM and tensor charge measurements, and show that upcoming new experiments will improve the constraint on quark EDMs by about three orders of magnitude leading to a much more sensitive probe of new physics models.

# Experimental constraint on quark electric dipole moments

## Key claim

The authors combine current nucleon electric dipole moment (EDM) measurements with tensor charge extractions to place upper limits on up and down quark EDMs at the scale of 4 GeV^2, under the assumptions that the strong CP-violating theta-term is zero and that contributions from chromo-EDM and Weinberg terms are negligible.

## Method

The authors relate nucleon EDMs to quark EDMs through the tensor charge using isospin symmetry, with the proton and neutron EDM expressed as linear combinations of up, down, and strange quark EDMs weighted by the corresponding tensor charges. They take the current upper limit on the proton EDM (2.6 x 10^-25 e.cm, derived from the mercury-199 atomic EDM measurement via the Schiff moment method) and the neutron EDM limit (3.0 x 10^-26 e.cm from direct ultra-cold neutron measurements), combine these with tensor charge values from a global analysis of transversity distributions at 4 GeV^2, and include a 10% uncertainty for isospin symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- Current upper limit on up quark EDM: 1.27 x 10^-24 e.cm at 4 GeV^2 (90% C.L.)
- Current upper limit on down quark EDM: 1.17 x 10^-24 e.cm at 4 GeV^2 (90% C.L.)
- Current neutron EDM limit used: 3.0 x 10^-26 e.cm (90% C.L.) from Afach (2015)
- Current proton EDM limit used: 2.6 x 10^-25 e.cm (derived from Graner (2016) mercury-199 EDM measurement)
- Tensor charge values at 4 GeV^2: g_T^u = 0.405 +/- 0.130, g_T^d = -0.225 +/- 0.092 (90% C.L.) from Ye (2016)
- Strange quark tensor charge: g_T^s = 0.008 +/- 0.009 (1 sigma) from Bhattacharya (2015), scaled to 90% C.L.
- Future combined constraint (with improved tensor charge and nucleon EDM measurements): up quark EDM limit 1.20 x 10^-27 e.cm, down quark EDM limit 7.18 x 10^-28 e.cm at 4 GeV^2
- Future neutron EDM limit assumed: 3.0 x 10^-28 e.cm (90% C.L.)
- Future proton EDM limit assumed: 2.6 x 10^-29 e.cm (90% C.L.) from proposed storage ring experiment

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bhattacharya (2015) by combining experimental nucleon EDM limits with phenomenological tensor charge extractions from global analyses of semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering data, rather than relying solely on lattice QCD calculations. The results enable quantitative constraints on new physics models such as split-supersymmetry, where the quark EDM dominates over chromo-EDM and Weinberg operator contributions at leading order. The analysis shows that upcoming experiments at Jefferson Lab (SoLID), next-generation neutron EDM experiments, and the proposed storage ring proton EDM experiment will improve quark EDM limits by about three orders of magnitude, probing new physics scales up to 30-40 TeV.
