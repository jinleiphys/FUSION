# Symmetries and Systematics of Doubly Heavy Hadrons

> We discuss the extension of the superflavor symmetry of doubly heavy baryons to states which contain an excited heavy diquark, and we examine some of the consequences of this symmetry for the spectra of doubly heavy baryons and heavy mesons. We explore the ramifications of a proposed symmetry that relates heavy diquarks to doubly heavy mesons. We present a method for determining how the excitation energy of a system containing two heavy quarks will scale as one changes the strength of the interactions and the reduced mass of the system. We use this to derive consequences of the heavy diquark-doubly heavy meson symmetry. We compare these consequences to the results of a quark model as well as the experimental data for doubly and singly heavy mesons. We also discuss the possibility of treating the strange quark as a heavy quark and apply the ideas developed here to strange hadrons.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the superflavor symmetry relating doubly heavy baryons to singly heavy mesons can be extended to include states where the heavy diquark is in an excited state, provided that the excitation energy of the heavy diquark is not negligible compared to the excitation energy of the light degrees of freedom, contrary to the original assumption of Savage and Wise (1990) that diquark excitations are much larger.

## Method

The authors derive an energy scaling rule for two-heavy-quark systems using the Hellmann-Feynman theorem applied to a nonrelativistic Schrodinger Hamiltonian with a general potential, showing that excitation energies scale as E proportional to mu^(d-1) b^d where mu is the reduced mass and b is the interaction strength. They then use this scaling rule together with a factorization approximation for doubly heavy baryons to relate excitation energies of doubly heavy mesons to those of heavy diquarks, and compare these symmetry predictions to results from the nonrelativistic constituent quark model of Roberts and Pervin (2008) as well as experimental data for singly and doubly heavy mesons.

## Key numbers

- For a Coulomb potential, the scaling index d = 2, so energy scales as mu^1 (proportional to reduced mass)
- For a linear potential, the scaling index d = 2/3, so energy scales as mu^(-1/3)
- For a harmonic oscillator potential, the scaling index d = 1/2, so energy scales as mu^(-1/2)
- The ratio of doubly heavy meson excitation energies for different quark flavors is predicted to equal (mu_Q1Q2 / mu_Q'1Q'2)^(d-1), independent of the specific radial and orbital quantum numbers n, n', l, l'
- The ratio of doubly heavy meson excitation energies to doubly heavy baryon excitation energies is predicted to equal 2^d, where d is the scaling index

## Context

This work extends the superflavor symmetry originally proposed by Savage and Wise (1990) for ground-state doubly heavy baryons, which related hyperfine splittings of doubly heavy baryons to those of heavy mesons. The authors challenge the assumption from Fleck and Richard (1989) that heavy diquark excitations are much larger than light degree of freedom excitations, citing the Roberts-Pervin (2008) quark model which predicts that heavy diquark excitation energy decreases as heavy quark mass increases. The paper enables future tests of whether the proposed extended symmetry emerges in quark models that do not assume a quark-diquark structure, and suggests that experimental data on doubly heavy mesons could be used to extract heavy diquark spectra.
