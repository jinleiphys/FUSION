# Initial State Summary of Hard Probes 2020

> The description of the initial state of heavy ion collisions, which covers the description of the incoming nuclei, the initial hard and soft interactions, the resulting spatial geometry of the produced matter, as well as the dynamic approach to a medium well described by hydrodynamics, has important consequences for the study of hard and electromagnetic probes. I will review new developments presented at Hard Probes 2020 that have an impact on these aspects of our understanding of the initial state of heavy ion and smaller system collisions.

# Initial State Summary of Hard Probes 2020

## Key claim
The initial state of heavy ion collisions, including nuclear wave functions, initial geometry, and early-time dynamics, is being understood with increasing precision, but the rapidity dependence of flow harmonics and the transition to hydrodynamics remain incompletely described, particularly for small systems where initial momentum anisotropies can dominate the final correlator for multiplicities below dN_ch/dη < 10.

## Method
The paper reviews new developments presented at the Hard Probes 2020 conference, covering measurements of flow harmonic correlations, nuclear modification of J/ψ, electroweak bosons, and light mesons, as well as theoretical progress in the color glass condensate effective field theory at next-to-leading order, and studies of chemical and kinetic equilibration using the Boltzmann equation with 2↔2 and 1↔2 processes.

## Key numbers
- The correlator between elliptic anisotropy v₂ and mean transverse momentum is dominated by initial geometry for all systems with dN_ch/dη > 10; below this threshold, initial momentum anisotropies cause a characteristic sign change in the correlator as a function of multiplicity.
- For dijet forward angular correlations at √s = 8.8 TeV, the ideal range to observe saturation effects is dijet transverse momenta of 5 to 8 GeV at forward rapidities.
- Using a reduced nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section of 41.5^{+16.2}_{-12.0} mb compared to the usual 70 ± 5 mb at √s = 5 TeV leads to much better agreement with the R_PbPb for W and Z bosons measured at LHC.
- Light meson nuclear modification has been measured out to p_T of up to 200 GeV in 8.16 TeV p+Pb collisions by ALICE.
- In the weak coupling limit described by the Boltzmann equation with relaxation time approximation, hydrodynamization occurs within 2τ₀, where τ₀ is the initial time.
- Chemical equilibration occurs for all systems with dN_ch/dη ≳ 100, independent of system size.

## Context
This review extends the ongoing program of understanding initial state effects in heavy ion collisions, building on the Trento model (Moreland 2014) for initial geometry and the color glass condensate effective field theory (McLerran 1993) for small-x physics. The work enables better constraints on 3+1 dimensional models of the initial state, particularly through longitudinal flow decorrelation measurements by ATLAS (Aad 2020) in Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb systems, and through photoproduction measurements of J/ψ in d+Au collisions by STAR (Tu 2020) that can constrain deuteron shape and fluctuations.
