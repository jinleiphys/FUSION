# Distorted quarkonia and spin alignment

> It is well-known that atoms can change their shape when subject to external electromagnetic fields. Analogous phenomenon is expected for particles, which are much smaller in size with the corresponding shape change much harder to observe. We point out that shape change of particles can be accessed by measurements of their spin alignment. Motivated by recent measurements of spin alignment in relativistic heavy ion collisions, we consider quarkonia in electromagnetic fields as an example. We show that the quarkonia spin alignment receives both spin contribution from spin states mixing and orbital contribution from shape change. By a proper choice of quantization axis, it is possible to switch off the spin contribution, leaving only the orbital contribution. This makes spin alignment measurement a valuable probe of quarkonium structure.

## Key claim

The authors show that spin alignment of quarkonia in external electromagnetic fields receives two distinct contributions: a spin contribution from mixing of spin states and an orbital contribution from distortion of the spatial wave function (shape change). By choosing the quantization axis appropriately, the spin contribution can be switched off, isolating the orbital contribution and making spin alignment a probe of quarkonium structure.

## Method

The authors calculate the photon self-energy for a J/psi-like S-wave spin triplet quarkonium in an external magnetic field, using a non-relativistic quantum mechanical description of the quarkonium state with the Cornell potential. They treat the diamagnetic interaction, the motional Stark effect from boosted electric fields, and the Zeeman interaction as perturbations, computing the resulting modifications to the spatial wave function and spin density matrix up to second order in the field strength. The spin alignment parameter lambda_theta is then extracted from the angular distribution of the decay leptons.

- Transition coefficient for diamagnetic interaction from n=3 D-wave state: c_3 = 0.109 in units of (e|B|)^2/GeV^4
- Transition coefficient for diamagnetic interaction from n=4 D-wave state: c_4 = 0.0206 in units of (e|B|)^2/GeV^4
- Transition coefficient for diamagnetic interaction from n=5 D-wave state: c_5 = 0.00673 in units of (e|B|)^2/GeV^4
- Transition coefficient for diamagnetic interaction from n=6 D-wave state: c_6 = 0.00341 in units of (e|B|)^2/GeV^4
- Cornell potential parameters used for J/psi: kappa = 0.61, a = 2.38 GeV^{-1}, quark mass m = 1.84 GeV
- Spin alignment from diamagnetic interaction: lambda_theta approximately equals -2/sqrt(5) times the ratio of integrals involving the D-wave correction, multiplied by (3 cos^2 theta_{Bl} - 1)/4
- Spin alignment from Stark effect: lambda_theta approximately equals -2/sqrt(5) times the ratio of integrals involving the D-wave correction, multiplied by (3 cos^2 theta_{El} - 1)/4

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for spin alignment in relativistic heavy ion collisions, following measurements by STAR (2022) and ALICE (2019, 2022) of spin alignment for phi and J/psi mesons. Prior work by Liang and Wang (2004) predicted spin polarization based on spin-orbit coupling, and subsequent studies by Sheng (2022, 2023), Zhao (2023), and others have examined various sources of spin alignment. The authors note that previous treatments have ignored the orbital contribution from shape change, which this paper shows can be separated from the spin contribution. The calculation uses the Cornell potential as parameterized by Eichten (1978, 1979, 1995, 1994) and follows the non-relativistic QCD framework of Bodwin (1994). This work enables using spin alignment measurements as a probe of quarkonium internal structure, potentially more sensitive than conventional scattering methods.
