# Pygmy dipole resonances in relativistic random phase approximation

> The isovector dipole response in $^{208}$Pb is described in the framework of a fully self-consistent relativistic random phase approximation. The NL3 parameter set for the effective mean-field Lagrangian with nonlinear meson self-interaction terms, used in the present calculations, reproduces ground state properties as well as the excitation energies of giant resonances in nuclei. In addition to the isovector dipole resonance in $^{208}$Pb, the present analysis predicts the occurrence of low-lying E1 peaks in the energy region between 7 and 11 MeV. In particular, a collective state has been identified whose dynamics correspond to that of a dipole pygmy resonance: the vibration of the excess neutrons against the inert core composed of equal number of protons and neutrons.

# Pygmy dipole resonances in relativistic random phase approximation

## Key claim

The paper predicts the occurrence of low-lying E1 peaks in 208Pb between 7 and 11 MeV within a fully self-consistent relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) using the NL3 parameter set, and identifies a collective state whose dynamics correspond to a dipole pygmy resonance, the vibration of excess neutrons against an inert core of equal proton and neutron numbers.

## Method

The isovector dipole response of 208Pb was calculated using a fully self-consistent relativistic random phase approximation based on the NL3 effective mean-field Lagrangian with nonlinear meson self-interaction terms. The same parameter set was used to describe both ground state properties and excitation energies of giant resonances, ensuring consistency between the mean-field and RPA calculations.

## Key numbers

- The low-lying E1 peaks appear in the energy region between 7 and 11 MeV in 208Pb.
- The NL3 parameter set reproduces ground state properties and giant resonance excitation energies in nuclei.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent relativistic mean-field plus RPA calculations to the study of pygmy dipole resonances, which had previously been described in non-relativistic frameworks. The identification of a collective pygmy resonance in 208Pb provides a prediction that can be tested against experimental data and enables further studies of neutron-rich nuclei where such low-lying dipole strength may be enhanced.
