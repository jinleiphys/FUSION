# Collective inertial masses in nuclear reactions

> Towards the microscopic theoretical description for large amplitude collective dynamics, we calculate the coefficients of inertial masses for low-energy nuclear reactions. Under the scheme of energy density functional, we apply the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method, as well as the Inglis' cranking formula to calculate the inertias for the translational and the relative motions, in addition to those for the rotational motion. Taking the scattering between two $α$ particles as an example, we investigate the impact of the time-odd components of the mean-field potential on the collective inertial masses. The ASCC method asymptotically reproduces the exact masses for both the relative and translational motions. On the other hand, the cranking formula fails to do so when the time-odd components exist.

# Collective inertial masses in nuclear reactions

## Key claim

The adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method asymptotically reproduces the exact inertial masses for both relative and translational motions in low-energy nuclear reactions, whereas the Inglis cranking formula fails to do so when time-odd components of the mean-field potential are present, specifically, the cranking formula underestimates the total mass when the energy functional has an effective mass m*/m < 1.

## Method

The authors applied the ASCC method and the Inglis cranking formula within the energy density functional framework to calculate collective inertial masses for translational, relative, and rotational motions. Using the α+α ↔ ⁸Be reaction as a test case, they solved the moving random-phase approximation (RPA) equations with the finite amplitude method to construct the collective path and extract the inertial mass parameter M(R) with respect to the relative distance coordinate R. They introduced a B₃(ρτ − j²) term to the BKN energy density functional to study the impact of time-odd mean-field components on the inertial masses.

## Key numbers

- The translational inertial mass of a single alpha particle approaches 4m (in units of nucleon mass) as the mesh size decreases, which is the exact total mass of the alpha particle.
- For the α+α system, the asymptotic reduced mass μ_red is 2m (nucleon masses), which both ASCC and cranking methods reproduce at large distances.
- The ground state of ⁸Be is located at a potential minimum at R = 3.55 fm, with the Coulomb barrier top at R = 6.50 fm.
- The lowest physical excitation mode of the alpha particle (monopole vibration) is calculated to be about 20 MeV.
- The ASCC and cranking masses were calculated with B₃ values of 0, 25, and 75 MeV fm⁵ to examine time-odd effects.

## Context

This work extends the ASCC method, previously applied to nuclear structure problems with separable interactions (Hinohara et al., 2007, 2009), to nuclear reaction dynamics by combining it with imaginary-time evolution and the finite amplitude method (Wen and Nakatsukasa, 2016). The study addresses the known failure of the Inglis cranking formula to reproduce the total mass for translational motion (Ring and Schuck, 1980) and demonstrates that the ASCC method preserves Galilean symmetry even when the energy functional contains time-odd components that produce an effective mass m*/m < 1. This enables microscopic determination of collective inertial masses for large-amplitude nuclear dynamics such as fusion and fission without a priori assumptions about the collective coordinate.
