# Comment on QED Corrections to the Parity Violating Asymmetry in High-Energy Electron-Nucleus Scattering

> In a recent letter Roca-Maza and Jakubassa-Amundsen claim a large $\approx 5\%$ radiative correction to the parity-violating asymmetry $A_{pv}$ in electron-nucleus scattering. If true, this would be important for the interpretation of the PREX, CREX, and MREX experiments. However, Roca-Maza and Jakubassa-Amundsen do not explicitly include the axial-vector vertex correction to $Z^0$ exchange (at the momentum transfer of the experiment). We show that including this cancels much of the change in $A_{pv}$.

## Key claim

Roca-Maza and Jakubassa-Amundsen (2025) claimed a large ≈ +5% radiative correction to the parity-violating asymmetry A_pv in electron-nucleus scattering, but they omitted the axial-vector vertex correction to Z^0 exchange at the finite momentum transfer of the experiment; including this momentum-dependent part cancels most of the claimed change in A_pv.

## Method

The authors computed the axial-vector vertex correction Γ_a^μ(Q^2) for electron-nucleus scattering in the large momentum transfer limit (Q^2 ≫ m^2), regulating infrared divergences with a small photon mass m_γ and ultraviolet divergences in D = 4 − 2ε dimensions. They compared this correction to the vector vertex correction v_ver and showed that the two are nearly identical, dominated by the same ln^2 and ln terms, so they largely cancel in the ratio that defines A_pv. They also evaluated the vacuum polarization contributions from photon and Z^0 exchange.

## Key numbers

- The claimed correction by Roca-Maza and Jakubassa-Amundsen is ≈ +5% to A_pv.
- The axial-vector vertex radiative correction is v_ver^a = (α/2π)[−1/2 ln^2(Q^2/m^2) + 3/2 ln(Q^2/m^2) + π^2/6 − 1].
- The vector vertex correction v_ver differs only in the constant term: π^2/6 − 2 instead of π^2/6 − 1.
- Vacuum polarization reduces A_pv for PREX and CREX kinematics by about 0.7%.
- The change from including vacuum polarization is less than 1/2 of the quoted systematic error and less than 1/4 of the statistical error for the extracted neutron skin.

## Context

This comment directly addresses the claim by Roca-Maza and Jakubassa-Amundsen (2025) of a large QED radiative correction to A_pv, which would affect interpretation of the PREX (2021), CREX (2022), and MREX experiments. The authors show that including the axial-vector vertex correction at finite Q^2 (neglected in the original work) cancels the large vertex contribution, leaving only a small vacuum polarization effect previously considered by Milstein and Sushkov (2005). This enables future work to incorporate the ≈0.7% vacuum polarization reduction into neutron skin extractions from parity-violating electron scattering.
