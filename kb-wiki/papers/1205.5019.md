# Effects of initial flow velocity fluctuation in event-by-event (3+1)D hydrodynamics

> Hadron spectra and elliptic flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions are studied within a (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic model with fluctuating initial conditions given by the AMPT Monte Carlo model. Results from event-by-event simulations are compared with experimental data at both RHIC and LHC energies. Fluctuations in the initial energy density come from not only the number of coherent soft interactions of overlapping nucleons but also incoherent semi-hard parton scatterings in each binary nucleon collision. Mini-jets from semi-hard parton scatterings are assumed to be locally thermalized through a Gaussian smearing and give rise to non-vanishing initial local flow velocities. Fluctuations in the initial flow velocities lead to harder transverse momentum spectra of final hadrons due to non-vanishing initial radial flow velocities. Initial fluctuations in rapidity distributions lead to expanding hot spots in the longitudinal direction and are shown to cause a sizable reduction of final hadron elliptic flow at large transverse momenta.

## Key claim

Fluctuations in initial local flow velocities, arising from mini-jets produced in semi-hard parton scatterings and modeled via Gaussian smearing in the AMPT initial conditions, lead to harder transverse momentum spectra and a sizable reduction of elliptic flow at large transverse momenta, effects that are not captured by event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations that assume zero initial local flow velocities.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic simulations using initial conditions from the AMPT Monte Carlo model, which includes both soft interactions (excited strings from participant nucleons) and semi-hard parton scatterings (mini-jets). Mini-jets were assumed to be locally thermalized through Gaussian smearing, producing non-vanishing initial local flow velocities in both transverse and longitudinal directions. The hydrodynamic evolution was solved using the FCT-SHASTA algorithm with a projection method for freeze-out surface calculation, and final hadron spectra were computed via the Cooper-Frye formula including resonance decays up to 1.68 GeV, using the s95p-v1 equation of state by Huovinen and Petreczky (2009).

## Context

This work extends event-by-event hydrodynamic studies that previously assumed zero initial local flow velocities, such as those using MC Glauber or MC-KLN initial conditions by Holopainen (2010), Schenke (2010, 2011, 2012), and others. Some prior simulations by Steinheimer (2007), Werner (2010), and Andrade (2010) included local velocity and longitudinal fluctuations but without systematic study of their effects. The results demonstrate that initial flow velocity fluctuations must be accounted for when extracting specific shear viscosity from comparisons between hydrodynamic calculations and experimental data at RHIC and LHC energies.
