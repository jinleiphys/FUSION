# Heavy and heavy-light mesons and the Lorentz structure of the quark-antiquark interaction

> We solve a Minkowski-space integral equation, derived in the Covariant Spectator Theory, for quark-antiquark bound states describing heavy and heavy-light mesons. The equation's kernel contains a one-gluon exchange interaction and a covariant generalization of a linear confining potential with a mixed scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector Lorentz structure, characterized by a continuous mixing parameter. We investigate to what extent the Lorentz structure of the confining kernel can be determined by fitting the mixing parameter to the meson spectrum.

## Key claim
By fitting a continuous mixing parameter that controls the scalar+pseudoscalar versus vector Lorentz structure of the confining kernel to the meson spectrum, the authors find that the minima in the rms difference are so shallow that values of the mixing parameter between 0 and roughly 0.25 are equally compatible with the data, preventing a definite conclusion about the Lorentz structure from masses alone.

## Method
The authors solve the one-channel spectator equation (1CSE) from the Covariant Spectator Theory in Minkowski space for quark-antiquark bound states. The kernel contains a one-gluon exchange interaction and a covariant linear confining potential with a mixed scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector Lorentz structure controlled by a continuous mixing parameter y. They perform least-square fits of the model parameters (string tension sigma, strong coupling alpha_s, constant C, quark masses, and mixing parameter y) to selected data sets of heavy and heavy-light meson masses with J^P = 0^± and 1^±.

## Key numbers
- The rms difference between calculated and experimental meson masses for the largest data set S3 (39 states) ranges from 30 to 40 MeV across all fitted models.
- For the fit to pseudoscalar states only (set S1, 9 states), the mixing parameter y prefers y = 0.0000 (pure scalar+pseudoscalar coupling).
- For larger data sets, the minimum in rms difference lies between y = 0.2002 and y = 0.2677, depending on the data set (S2, S2', S3).
- The string tension sigma ranges from 0.1893 to 0.2493 GeV^2 across all fitted models.
- The strong coupling alpha_s ranges from 0.3614 to 0.4172 across all fitted models.
- The constant C ranges from 0.0591 to 0.3491 GeV across all fitted models.
- The bottom quark mass ranges from 4.768 to 4.917 GeV, the charm quark mass from 1.398 to 1.624 GeV, the strange quark mass from 0.2349 to 0.4616 GeV, and the light quark mass from 0.1000 to 0.3514 GeV across all fitted models.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Leitao 2017) where the mixing parameter was fixed at y=0, by allowing y and quark masses to vary in global fits to heavy and heavy-light meson spectra. The approach uses the Covariant Spectator Theory, which is related to the Bethe-Salpeter equation but goes beyond ladder approximation by effectively including crossed-ladder diagrams (Gross 1969, Stadler 2011). The results show that meson masses alone cannot distinguish between pure scalar+pseudoscalar confinement and a mixture with up to about 25% vector coupling, but the authors plan to study decay constants as more sensitive observables to determine the Lorentz structure.
