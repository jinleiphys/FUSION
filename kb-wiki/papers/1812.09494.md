# Asymmetric Nuclear Light Clusters In Supernova Matter

> We explore the appearance of light clusters at high densities of collapsing stellar cores. Special attention is paid to the unstable isotope H4, which was not included in previous studies. The importance of light clusters in the calculation of rates for neutrino matter interaction is discussed. The main conclusion is that thermodynamic quantities are only weakly sensitive to the chemical composition. The change in pressure and hence the direct change in collapse dynamics will be minor. But the change in neutrino heating and neutronization processes can be significant.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the unstable hydrogen isotope ⁴H, which was omitted from previous supernova equation-of-state studies, can reach mass fractions of nearly 10% in collapsing stellar cores, but its inclusion changes thermodynamic quantities like pressure and entropy only weakly (below 1% and 4% respectively), while the significant impact is on neutrino-matter interaction rates.

## Method

The authors compare four equation-of-state models: a pure Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium (NSE) model extended to 398 nuclides, the HS(DD2) EoS with and without modifications to include ⁴H and correct spin information, and the BPRS EoS. They compute chemical compositions for a 15 solar mass progenitor star at 1 ms before bounce and at 200 ms after bounce, using thermodynamic profiles from Hempel (2012), and systematically vary the treatment of ⁴H's binding energy, excited states, and nuclear partition functions.

## Key numbers

- Mass fraction of ⁴H reaches 0.083 (8.3%) at the center of the collapsing core in the base NSE calculation; this drops to 0.044 when only the ground state is used, and to 0.033 when the older binding energy of 1.394 MeV per nucleon is used instead of 1.72 MeV per nucleon.
- The binding energy per nucleon of ⁴H is 1.72 MeV (Audi 2014), updated from the earlier value of 1.394 MeV (Audi and Wapstra 1995).
- The ground state of ⁴H has spin J=2, giving a statistical factor of 2J+1=5, and its lifetime is 1.43 × 10⁻²² seconds; three excited states exist at excitation energies 0.31, 2.08, and 2.83 MeV with lifetimes 0.98, 0.74, and 0.51 × 10⁻²² seconds respectively.
- The relative change in total pressure from including ⁴H is below 1%; the relative change in entropy reaches about 4% in the central region.

## Context

This work extends the line of NSE-based supernova equations of state that include multiple light nuclei beyond just alpha particles, following studies by Sumiyoshi and Roepke (2008), Typel (2010), Hempel and Schaffner-Bielich (2010), and Pais (2015). The key novel element is the inclusion of ⁴H, which was absent in prior work. The results enable improved calculations of neutrino opacities in core-collapse supernova simulations, since the coherent scattering rate on nuclei scales as A², and the authors identify even more neutron-rich light isotopes (e.g., ⁷H, ¹⁰He) as requiring further investigation, consistent with findings by Gulminelli and Raduta (2015).
