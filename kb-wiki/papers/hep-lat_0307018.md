# N to Delta electromagnetic transition form factors from Lattice QCD

> The magnetic dipole, the electric quadrupole and the Coulomb quadrupole amplitudes for the transition $γN\to Δ$ are evaluated both in quenched lattice QCD at $β=6.0$ and using two dynamical Wilson fermions simulated at $β=5.6$. The dipole transition form factor is accurately determined at several values of momentum transfer. On the lattices studied in this work, the electric quadrupole amplitude is found to be non-zero yielding a negative value for the ratio, $ R_{EM}$, of electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole amplitudes at three values of momentum transfer.

## Key claim
The electric quadrupole amplitude for the γN→Δ transition is non-zero on the lattices studied, yielding a negative value for the ratio REM of electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole amplitudes at three values of momentum transfer, though the authors note that the Coulomb quadrupole extraction is noisy and only usable when the Δ is produced at rest.

## Method
The authors computed three-point correlation functions for the γN→Δ transition using lattice QCD, both in the quenched approximation at β=6.0 and with two dynamical Wilson fermions at β=5.6. They extracted the Sachs form factors GM1, GE2, and GC2 from plateau values of ratios of three-point to two-point correlators, using Wuppertal smearing to enhance ground state dominance and averaging over photon momenta q and -q to improve statistics. The form factors were evaluated at several momentum transfer values, with the nucleon at rest and the Δ at rest providing two different kinematic setups.

## Key numbers
* Magnetic dipole form factor GM1 was accurately determined at several momentum transfer values on both quenched and dynamical lattices; for the unquenched SESAM lattice at κ=0.1565 with momentum transfer q=(2π/16a,0,0), GM1 showed a plateau starting as early as two time slices from the current insertion.
* Electric quadrupole form factor GE2 was found to be non-zero, yielding a negative REM ratio at three values of momentum transfer; for the same SESAM lattice at κ=0.1565, GE2 showed a plateau extending over four time slices.
* Coulomb quadrupole form factor GC2 was extracted only when the Δ was produced at rest, using Eq. (C2 11); for the SESAM lattice at κ=0.1565, GC2 results from Eq. (C2 11) had smaller statistical errors than those from Eq. (C2 22+33).
* The quenched calculation used β=6.0 on lattices of size 16^3×32 and 32^3×64; the unquenched calculation used β=5.6 on a 16^3×32 lattice with two dynamical Wilson fermions.

## Context
This work extends the pioneering lattice QCD study by Leinweber (1993), which had limited quenched statistics and could not statistically exclude a zero value for REM. The authors improve upon that study by using smearing techniques, larger volumes, smaller quark masses (giving a pion-to-rho mass ratio of 50 percent), and both quenched and dynamical fermion configurations from the SESAM collaboration. The results provide direct lattice evidence for nucleon deformation and enable comparison with experimental measurements from Bates and Jefferson Lab, as well as with model predictions from constituent quark models, cloudy bag models, and soliton models.
