# Surface-peaked medium effects in the interaction of nucleons with finite nuclei

> We investigate the asymptotic separation of the optical model potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering in momentum space, where the potential is split into a medium-independent term and another depending exclusively on the gradient of the density-dependent g matrix. This decomposition confines the medium sensitivity of the nucleon-nucleus coupling to the surface of the nucleus. We examine this feature in the context of proton-nucleus scattering at beam energies between 30 and 100 MeV and find that the pn coupling accounts for most of this sensitivity. Additionally, based on this general structure of the optical potential we are able to treat both, the medium dependence of the effective interaction and the full mixed density as described by single-particle shell models. The calculated scattering observables agree within 10% with those obtained by Arellano, Brieva and Love in their momentum-space g-folding approach.

# Surface-peaked medium effects in the interaction of nucleons with finite nuclei

## Key claim

The optical model potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering can be separated into a medium-independent term (free t-matrix) and a term proportional exclusively to the gradient of the density-dependent g-matrix, which confines intrinsic medium effects to the nuclear surface. The pn coupling accounts for most of this surface sensitivity, and the calculated scattering observables agree within 10% with those from the earlier momentum-space g-folding approach of Arellano, Brieva and Love (1995).

## Method

The authors derived a momentum-space decomposition of the optical potential by splitting the two-body effective interaction into a medium-independent free t-matrix term and a term proportional to the radial derivative of the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone g-matrix with respect to the mean coordinate Z. They applied the momentum-conserving approximation (MCA) to reduce the sevenfold integral to a tractable form, then evaluated the resulting "δg-folding" potential using single-particle shell-model wavefunctions for the full mixed density and off-shell g-matrices from the Paris NN potential. Scattering observables were obtained by solving the nonlocal Schrödinger equation with Coulomb term for proton elastic scattering from 16O and 90Zr at beam energies between 30 and 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 30 to 100 MeV for proton-nucleus scattering
- For 16O(p,p) at 30.4 MeV, the real part of the neutron-density contribution to U1 was approximately -23 MeV fm³, compared to U0(n) ≈ -10 MeV fm³ from the free t-matrix
- The imaginary part of U0(n) was approximately -24 MeV fm³ at 30.4 MeV
- The radial derivative -k'(Z) peaks near 3 fm for 16O and 6 fm for 90Zr, with both peaks having a width of approximately 3 fm and corresponding to local Fermi momentum k ≈ 0.6 fm⁻¹
- The δg-folding and ABL approach agree within 10% in the diffractive maxima of the differential cross section
- The δg-folding calculation is approximately 150 times more computationally demanding than the ABL approach
- The g-matrix was calculated off-shell (J ≤ 7) at 30 values of Fermi momentum from 0 to 1.6 fm⁻¹; subsequent tests showed 16 Fermi momenta yield equally reliable results
- The Z integration used a uniform mesh with steps of 0.1 fm and 25 radial mesh points for momentum integrals

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space g-folding approach of Arellano, Brieva and Love (1995), which used the Slater approximation for the mixed density, by introducing the δg-folding that treats the full mixed density from single-particle shell models. The surface-peaked medium effects are consistent with recent findings on pickup effects in p+10Be elastic scattering near 40A MeV reported by Keeley (2008). The approach enables more selective tracking of surface contributions to the optical potential, which could prove useful for interpreting scattering data from rare isotope beam facilities such as EURISOL, ISOLDE, SPIRAL, RIKEN, and MSU, where beams of unstable nuclei are produced.
