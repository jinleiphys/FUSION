# Polarization as a signature of local parity violation in hot QCD matter

> We show that local parity violation due to chirality imbalance in relativistic nuclear collisions can be revealed by measuring the projection of the polarization vector onto the momentum, i.e. the helicity, of final state baryons. The proposed method does not require a coupling to the electromagnetic field, like in the Chiral Magnetic Effect. By using linear response theory, we show that, in the presence of a chiral imbalance, the spin 1/2 baryons and anti-baryons receive an additional contribution to the polarization along their momentum and proportional to the axial chemical potential. The additional, parity-breaking, contribution to helicity can be detected by studying helicity-helicity azimuthal angular correlation.

## Key claim

Local parity violation in hot QCD matter can be detected by measuring the helicity (projection of the polarization vector onto momentum) of final-state baryons, without requiring a coupling to the electromagnetic field as in the Chiral Magnetic Effect. The additional parity-breaking contribution to helicity is proportional to the axial chemical potential and fluctuates event-by-event with zero mean, so it must be detected through helicity-helicity azimuthal angular correlations rather than through event-averaged polarization.

## Method

The authors used linear response theory to expand the local equilibrium density operator, including a term proportional to the axial chemical potential that violates parity. They derived the mean spin vector of spin-1/2 baryons (particularly Lambda hyperons) induced by this axial chemical potential at leading order, using the Wigner function formalism integrated over the freeze-out hypersurface. The resulting additional contribution to the spin vector is purely longitudinal (along the particle momentum), and the authors propose measuring helicity-helicity azimuthal angular correlations of Lambda pairs in the same event to detect the parity-breaking component.

## Key numbers

- The axial chemical potential at hadronization is estimated as zeta_A = O(10^{-2}) based on calculations in Shi (2017) and private communication with Liao
- If zeta = O(10^{-2}), the magnitude of the spin vector induced by the axial chemical potential is comparable to the one from hydrodynamics (vorticity and shear tensor contributions)
- The helicity function for constant zeta_A over the freeze-out hypersurface takes the form h_chi(p) = (g_h/2) * (|p|/epsilon) * zeta_A, under the approximation 1 - n_F ~ 1

## Context

This work extends the study of local parity violation in heavy-ion collisions beyond the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) proposed by Fukushima (2008) and Kharzeev (2007), which requires electromagnetic fields and suffers from large uncertainties on the magnetic field magnitude in the plasma phase. The authors build on the recent measurement of global Lambda polarization by the STAR experiment (2017) and the subsequent hydrodynamic predictions by Becattini (2020), as well as a similar idea by Finch (2018) who proposed correlating net helicity of Lambdas with charge separation due to CME. The proposed method enables detection of local parity violation without the uncertainties associated with electromagnetic field mediation, and the helicity-helicity correlation does not require identification of the reaction plane.
