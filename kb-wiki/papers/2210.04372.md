# Physical Implications of the Extrapolation and Statistical Bootstrap of the Nucleon Structure Function Ratio $\frac{F_2^n}{F_2^p}$ for Mirror Nuclei $^3$He and $^3$H

> A nuclear physics example of statistical bootstrap is used on the MARATHON data nucleon structure function ratio, $\frac{F_2^n}{F_2^p}$, in the quark momentum fraction $x_B\rightarrow0$ and $x_B\rightarrow1$ regions. The extrapolated $F_2$ ratio value as quark momentum fraction $x_B\rightarrow 1$ approaches 0.4 and this value is compared to theoretical predictions. The extrapolated ratio when $x_B\rightarrow 0$ favors the simple model of isospin symmetry with the complete dominance of seaquarks at low momentum fraction. At high-$x_B$, the proton quark distribution function ratio $d/u$ is derived from the $x\rightarrow 1$ ratio $\frac{F_2^n}{F_2^p}\rightarrow 0.4$ and found to be $d/u \rightarrow 1/6$. Our extrapolated values for both the $\frac{F_2^n}{F_2^p}$ ratio and the $d/u$ parton distribution function ratio most closely match perturbative QCD values from quark counting and helicity conservation arguments but still differ by roughly $7\%$. The mismatch to theoretical predictions may be ameliorated if two compatible models act simultaneously in the nucleon wavefunction. One such example is nucleon wavefunctions composed of a linear combination of a quark-diquark state and a 3-valence quark correlated state with coefficients that combine to give the extrapolated $F_2$ ratio of $0.4$.

## Key claim

The extrapolated value of the nucleon structure function ratio \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) as \(x_B \rightarrow 1\) is \(0.4 \pm 0.05\), which most closely matches perturbative QCD predictions from quark counting and helicity conservation (which give \(3/7 \approx 0.43\)) but still differs by roughly 7%; the authors suggest this mismatch may be resolved if two compatible models, such as a quark-diquark state and a 3-valence quark correlated state, act simultaneously in the nucleon wavefunction.

## Method

The authors applied a statistical bootstrap resampling technique to the 22 MARATHON data points covering \(0.19 < x_B < 0.83\), fitting each resampled set with a rational function \(R_f = n_0 \frac{1+x n_1}{1+x m_1}\) and extrapolating to \(x_B \rightarrow 0\) and \(x_B \rightarrow 1\). One thousand such fits were performed by randomly selecting 22 points with replacement (allowing one, two, or three substitutions) to generate an uncertainty band. The extrapolated ratio at \(x_B=1\) was then used to derive the \(d/u\) parton distribution function ratio under isospin symmetry assumptions.

## Key numbers

* Extrapolated \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) as \(x_B \rightarrow 1\): \(0.4 \pm 0.05\) (from statistical bootstrap of MARATHON data).
* Derived \(d/u\) parton distribution function ratio as \(x_B \rightarrow 1\): \(d/u \rightarrow 1/6\) (from the \(F_2\) ratio using isospin symmetry).
* Perturbative QCD prediction for \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) as \(x_B \rightarrow 1\): \(3/7 \approx 0.43\) (from quark counting rules and helicity conservation).
* Scalar diquark model prediction for \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) as \(x_B \rightarrow 1\): \(1/4 = 0.25\).
* DSE-II contact interaction model prediction for \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) as \(x_B \rightarrow 1\): \(0.41\).
* Extrapolated \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) as \(x_B \rightarrow 0\): approaches 1 (consistent with isospin symmetry and sea quark dominance).

## Context

This work extends the MARATHON experiment results (Jefferson Lab Hall A Tritium Collaboration, 2021) by extrapolating the measured \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) ratio into the unmeasured \(x_B \rightarrow 0\) and \(x_B \rightarrow 1\) regions using a statistical bootstrap method. The extrapolated high-\(x_B\) value of \(0.4 \pm 0.05\) disfavors the scalar diquark model prediction of 0.25 (Close, 1973; Selem, 2006) and the SU(6) flavor model prediction of 2/3 (Kuti, 1971), while being consistent within uncertainties with perturbative QCD helicity conservation (Farrar, 1975) and quark counting rules (Brodsky, 1994). The authors note that their result is also within range of the DSE-II contact interaction model (Roberts, 2013) and the JAM global fit (Cocuzza, 2021), and they propose that a linear combination of a quark-diquark state and a 3-valence quark correlated state could reproduce the extrapolated value, consistent with recent work on nucleon wavefunctions in A=3 nuclei (West, 2020; Li, 2022).
