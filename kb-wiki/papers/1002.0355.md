# Transverse Charge Densities

> Electromagnetic form factors have long been used to probe the underlying charge and magnetization densities of hadrons and nuclei. Traditional three-dimensional Fourier transform methods are not rigorously applicable for systems with constituents that move relativistically. The use of the transverse charge density is a new, rigorously defined way to analyze electromagnetic form factors of hadrons.This review is concerned with the following issues: what is a transverse charge density; how is one extracted one from elastic scattering data; the existing results; what is the relationship with other observable quantities; and, future prospects.

# Transverse Charge Densities

## Key claim

The paper argues that the traditional three-dimensional Fourier transform interpretation of electromagnetic form factors as charge densities is not rigorously valid for relativistic systems like hadrons, and that the transverse charge density, defined in the infinite momentum frame, provides the only model-independent way to extract charge density information from elastic scattering data. The hedge is that this approach yields only transverse (two-dimensional) information; no model-independent longitudinal charge density can be obtained because the probe momentum transfer has no longitudinal component.

## Method

The author derives the transverse charge density ρ(b) as the two-dimensional Fourier transform of the electromagnetic form factor F(Q²) in the Drell-Yan frame (q⁺ = 0, Q² = q²), using transversely localized states with the transverse center of momentum set to zero. The relationship is established through light-front quantization and the operator J⁺ in the infinite momentum frame, showing that ρ(b) = ∫ d²q/(2π)² F(Q² = q²) e^{-iq·b}. The method is applied to existing pion form factor data, and the resulting transverse density is compared with predictions from a relativistic constituent quark model.

## Key numbers

- The monopole fit to pion form factor data gives R² = 0.431 fm², with F_π(Q²) = 1/(1 + R²Q²/6)
- The resulting transverse charge density is ρ(b) = 3 K_0(√6 b / R) / (π R²), which diverges as log(b) at small b
- The perturbative QCD asymptotic prediction gives F_π(Q²) → 16π α_s(Q²) f_π² / Q², with f_π = 93 MeV, leading to ρ(b) ~ log log(1/b) at short distances
- The non-relativistic three-dimensional density from the same monopole form is ρ_NR(r) = 3/(2π r R²) e^{-√6 r / R}, also diverging at r = 0
- The model calculation uses binding fractions ε = 0.01 and 0.1 for a scalar two-body bound state, with the smaller binding giving greater spatial extent

## Context

This review extends the work of Soper (1977) on transverse densities in the infinite momentum frame and Burkardt (2002) on impact parameter dependent parton distributions, showing how the transverse charge density relates to generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse momentum distributions (TMDs). The analysis of the pion transverse density from recent Jefferson Lab data (Blok 2008, Huber 2008) reveals a logarithmic singularity at the origin that appears in any model yielding a monopole form factor, including holographic QCD (Brodsky 2007, Kwee 2007, Grigoryan 2007). The work enables future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations and provides a framework for interpreting upcoming higher-Q² pion form factor measurements.
