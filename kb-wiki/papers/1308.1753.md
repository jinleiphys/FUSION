# Elliptic flow splitting as a probe of the QCD phase structure at finite baryon chemical potential

> Using a partonic transport model based on the 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and a relativistic hadronic transport model to describe, respectively, the evolution of the initial partonic and the final hadronic phase of heavy-ion collisions at energies carried out in the Beam-Energy Scan program of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, we have studied the effects of both the partonic and hadronic mean-field potentials on the elliptic flow of particles relative to that of their antiparticles. We find that to reproduce the measured relative elliptic flow differences between nucleons and antinucleons as well as between kaons and antikaons requires a vector coupling constant as large as 0.5 to 1.1 times the scalar coupling constant in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Implications of our results in understanding the QCD phase structure at finite baryon chemical potential are discussed.

## Key claim

The observed elliptic flow splitting between particles and antiparticles in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV can be reproduced only when both partonic and hadronic mean-field potentials are included, and this constrains the ratio of vector to scalar coupling in the NJL model to between 0.5 and 1.1, which in turn suggests that the hadron-quark phase transition may always be a smooth crossover rather than featuring a critical point.

## Method

The authors extended the string-melting version of the AMPT model by replacing the usual parton cascade with a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) transport model that includes both scalar and vector mean-field potentials for quarks and antiquarks. After hadronization via spatial coalescence, the hadronic phase was evolved using a relativistic transport (ART) model that includes empirically determined hadronic potentials for nucleons, antinucleons, kaons, and antikaons. The parton scattering cross section was fixed to 1 mb by fitting the measured charged-particle elliptic flow in mid-central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- Parton scattering cross section fixed to \(\sigma = 1\) mb to reproduce the measured charged-particle elliptic flow at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV.
- The ratio \(R_V = G_V/G\) of vector to scalar coupling in the NJL model is constrained to values between 0.5 and 1.1 to simultaneously reproduce the relative \(v_2\) differences for nucleons/antinucleons and kaons/antikaons.
- The total nucleus-nucleus inelastic cross section used is \(\sigma_{in} \approx 686\) fm\(^2\) from a Glauber model with nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section of about 30.8 mb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV.
- The NJL model parameters include \(m_u = m_d = 3.6\) MeV, \(m_s = 87\) MeV, \(G\Lambda^2 = 3.6\), \(K\Lambda^5 = 8.9\), and cutoff \(\Lambda = 750\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Xu (2012) and Song (2012) that separately considered only hadronic or only partonic potentials, which underestimated the nucleon-antinucleon \(v_2\) splitting and either over- or underestimated the kaon-antikaon splitting. The results suggest that the extracted \(R_V\) range would make the QCD critical point disappear, implying a smooth crossover transition at all baryon chemical potentials, and also has implications for the composition of two-solar-mass neutron stars as studied by Demorest (2010). The approach enables mapping the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon chemical potential using elliptic flow measurements from the RHIC Beam-Energy Scan program.
