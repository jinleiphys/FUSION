# Ab initio optical potentials and nucleon scattering on medium mass nuclei

> We show the first results for the elastic scattering of neutrons off oxygen and calcium isotopes obtained from ab initio optical potentials. The potential is derived using self consistent Green's function theory (SCGF) with the saturating chiral interaction NNLO$_{\textrm{sat}}$. Our calculations are compared to available scattering data and show that it is possible to reproduce low energy scattering observables in medium mass nuclei from first principles.

# Ab initio optical potentials and nucleon scattering on medium mass nuclei

## Key claim
The authors show that ab initio optical potentials derived from self-consistent Green’s function theory with the NNLO\(_{\text{sat}}\) chiral interaction can reproduce low-energy neutron elastic scattering observables for oxygen and calcium isotopes, but with the caveat that the calculations show a general lack of absorption compared to experimental data, likely due to missing doorway configurations or model space limitations.

## Method
The irreducible self-energy (optical potential) was computed using the third-order algebraic diagrammatic construction [ADC(3)] method within self-consistent Green’s function theory, employing the NNLO\(_{\text{sat}}\) chiral interaction. The optical potential was expressed in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with up to \(N_{\text{max}} = 13\) oscillator shells, and the scattering problem was solved in the full one-body space to obtain phase shifts and differential cross sections for neutron scattering on \(^{16}\)O, \(^{40}\)Ca, \(^{48}\)Ca, and \(^{54}\)Ca.

## Key numbers
- For neutron-\(^{16}\)O scattering, the dominant \(d_{3/2}\) resonance was computed at approximately 1.15 MeV center-of-mass energy, compared to the experimental value of 0.94 MeV.
- The \(f_{7/2}\) resonance in neutron-\(^{16}\)O scattering was found within 1 MeV of the experimental value from Lister (1966).
- NNLO\(_{\text{sat}}\) predicts the location of dominant quasiparticle and hole states in \(^{16}\)O with a conservative accuracy of less than 2 MeV.
- The differential cross section for neutron elastic scattering on \(^{40}\)Ca was compared to experiment at 13.56 MeV center-of-mass energy.
- Volume integrals of the imaginary part of the optical potential were shown for \(^{40}\)Ca, \(^{48}\)Ca, and \(^{54}\)Ca at \(N_{\text{max}} = 11\), with the gap at the Fermi surface shifting to higher energies with increasing neutron number.

## Context
This work extends earlier SCGF calculations of phase shifts by Barbieri (2005) and investigations of optical potential properties by Waldecker (2011), which were limited to two-body forces and could not be directly compared to experiment due to the lack of realistic interactions reproducing nuclear radii. The introduction of the NNLO\(_{\text{sat}}\) interaction by Ekstrom (2015) and its implementation for SCGF by Carbone (2013), Cipollone (2013), and Cipollone (2015) enabled this comparison. The results enable future ab initio calculations of transfer reactions such as \((d,p)\), where a consistent optical potential from the same Hamiltonian used in the proton-neutron channel is needed, as emphasized by Bailey (2016). The approach complements the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) method used by Navratil (2010), Baroni (2013), and Raimondi (2016) for light targets, extending ab initio scattering calculations to medium-mass nuclei.
