# Equation of state of the hot dense matter in a multi-phase transport model

> Within the framework of a multi-phase transport model, we study the equation of state and pressure anisotropy of the hot dense matter produced in central relativistic heavy ion collisions. Both are found to depend on the hadronization scheme and scattering cross sections used in the model. Furthermore, only partial thermalization is achieved in the produced matter as a result of its fast expansion.

## Key claim

The equation of state (EOS) and pressure anisotropy of hot dense matter produced in central relativistic heavy ion collisions depend on the hadronization scheme and scattering cross sections used in the AMPT model, and only partial thermalization is achieved due to fast expansion, contrary to the full thermalization assumed in ideal hydrodynamic models.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, a hybrid framework combining HIJING for initial conditions, ZPC for parton cascade with elastic scatterings, and ART for hadronic evolution, to simulate central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They extracted the energy density and pressure components from the energy-momentum tensor by averaging over particles and events in a central cell, and computed the EOS as P/epsilon versus epsilon and the pressure anisotropy as P_L/P_T as a function of proper time. Two hadronization schemes were compared: the default AMPT (string fragmentation via Lund/PYTHIA) and the string melting AMPT (coalescence of closest partons into hadrons), with parton scattering cross sections of 3 mb, 6 mb, and 10 mb.

## Key numbers

- In the default AMPT model, hadronization starts when energy density epsilon is around a few GeV/fm^3, while in the string melting model, hadronization starts when epsilon is an order of magnitude smaller.
- In the string melting model with a 10 mb parton cross section, the system stays in the partonic stage until about 7 fm/c, when hadron energy density is about 10^-3 GeV/fm^3; hadrons dominate the energy density at about 13 fm/c.
- In the default model, the pressure anisotropy parameter P_L/P_T crosses 1 at about 10 fm/c; in the string melting model, it crosses 1 after about 6 fm/c.
- The anisotropy parameter in the central cell reaches a saturation value of about 0.8 during the initial stage, indicating only partial thermalization.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of elliptic flow and thermalization in transport models (e.g., Molnar (2004), Xu (2004)) by directly extracting the EOS and pressure anisotropy from the AMPT model, showing that the resulting EOS differs from that used in ideal hydrodynamic models and from lattice QCD results (Ejiri (2005)). The findings enable future investigations into the effects of inelastic parton scatterings (as in Xu (2004)), mean fields (Tan (2006)), fixed-time hadronization (Meiling (2006)), and plasma instabilities (Dumitru (2005), Arnold (2005), Rebhan (2005), Romatschke (2006)) on the EOS and observables in heavy ion collisions.
