# Equation of State of Hypermatter in beta Equilibrium in a Quark Model with Excluded Volume Correction

> We study the effects of the finite size of the baryons on the equation of state of homogeneous hadronic matter with hyperons. The finite extension of hadrons is introduced in order to improve the perfomance of field theoretical models at very high densities, simulating the short-range hard-core repulsive part of the baryon-baryon potential. We choose the Quark Meson Coupling model to describe the baryon dynamics because this model provides a density dependent radius of the bag. In addition we calculate finite size corrections for the Zimanyi-Moszkowski model in order to make a definite comparison. In both cases finite size effects are implemented through a Van der Waals like correction in the normalization of the baryon fields. In this approach we investigate $β$-stable matter in conditions that could be found in the interior of massive stars. We find that the excluded volume corrections contribute significantly for densities above twice the symmetric nuclear matter saturation density, although for the quark model the results strongly depends upon the equilibrium conditions for the confinement volume immersed in a dense medium.

## Key claim

The excluded volume correction, implemented via a Van der Waals-like normalization of baryon fields, contributes significantly to the equation of state of hypermatter only for densities above twice the symmetric nuclear matter saturation density; however, for the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model the results depend strongly on the equilibrium condition chosen for the confinement volume immersed in a dense medium.

## Method

The authors introduce finite-size corrections for baryons by replacing the total volume with an available volume in the normalization of baryon fields, using a Van der Waals-like prescription with an effective volume per baryon \(v_B = 4\sqrt{2} R_B^3\). They apply this scheme to two models: the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) hadronic model with a constant baryon radius, and the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model where the bag radius is density-dependent. For the QMC model, two equilibrium conditions for the bag are considered: one minimizing the baryon effective mass with respect to radius at fixed sigma field (QMCa), and another balancing the internal quark pressure against the external baryonic pressure (QMCb). The equations are solved in the mean field approximation for homogeneous beta-stable matter containing neutrons, protons, and lambda hyperons at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(n_0 = 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: \({E_b}_0 = -16\ \text{MeV}\)
- Symmetry energy coefficient: \(a_s = 35\ \text{MeV}\)
- Nuclear matter compressibility at saturation for ZM model without volume correction (NC): \(\kappa = 221.4\ \text{MeV}\); with volume correction (VC): \(\kappa = 254.1\ \text{MeV}\)
- Nuclear matter compressibility at saturation for QMCa model NC: \(\kappa = 300.6\ \text{MeV}\); QMCa VC: \(\kappa = 323.4\ \text{MeV}\)
- Nuclear matter compressibility at saturation for QMCb model NC: \(\kappa = 296.1\ \text{MeV}\); QMCb VC: \(\kappa = 318.6\ \text{MeV}\)
- Proton effective mass ratio at saturation for ZM model NC: \(M_p^\ast/M_p = 0.850\); ZM VC: \(0.862\)
- Proton effective mass ratio at saturation for QMCa NC: \(0.773\); QMCa VC: \(0.792\)
- Proton effective mass ratio at saturation for QMCb NC: \(0.772\); QMCb VC: \(0.791\)
- Baryon radius at zero density for protons and neutrons: \(0.6\ \text{fm}\); for lambda: \(0.606\ \text{fm}\)
- Bag constant: \(B_0^{1/4} = 210.85\ \text{MeV}\)
- Meson masses used: \(m_\sigma = 550\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_\omega = 783\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_\rho = 770\ \text{MeV}\)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hypermatter equations of state that treated baryons as point-like particles, such as the non-relativistic work of Schulze and the relativistic work of Zhang. It also builds on the Van der Waals-like excluded volume approach previously applied to nuclear matter and quark-gluon plasma phase transitions by Kapusta and by Cleymans and collaborators. The results enable more realistic modeling of the interior of massive neutron stars, where densities can exceed twice saturation density and hyperons are expected to appear, by incorporating both finite baryon size and the density-dependent bag radius from the QMC model.
