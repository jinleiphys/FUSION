# Single-particle potential of the $Λ$ hyperon in nuclear matter with chiral effective field theory NLO interactions including effects of YNN three-baryon interactions

> Adopting hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interactions parametrized in chiral effective field theory, single-particle potentials of the $Λ$ and $Σ$ hyperons are evaluated in symmetric nuclear matter and in pure neutron matter within the framework of lowest order Bruckner theory. The chiral NLO interaction bears strong $Λ$N-$Σ$N coupling. Although the $Λ$ potential is repulsive if the coupling is switched off, the $Λ$N-$Σ$N correlation brings about the attraction consistent with empirical data. The $Σ$ potential is repulsive, which is also consistent with empirical information. The interesting result is that the $Λ$ potential becomes shallower beyond normal density. This provides the possibility to solve the hyperon puzzle without introducing ad hoc assumptions. The effects of the $Λ$NN-$Λ$NN and $Λ$NN-$Σ$NN three-baryon forces are considered. These three-baryon forces are first reduced to normal-ordered effective two-baryon interactions in nuclear matter and then incorporated in the $G$-matrix equation. The repulsion from the $Λ$NN-$Λ$NN interaction is of the order of 5 MeV at the normal density, and becomes larger with increasing the density. The effects of the $Λ$NN-$Σ$NN coupling compensate the repulsion at normal density. The net effect of the three-baryon interactions to the $Λ$ single-particle potential is repulsive at higher densities.

## Key claim

The chiral NLO hyperon-nucleon interaction, with its strong ΛN-ΣN coupling, produces a Λ single-particle potential in nuclear matter that becomes shallower beyond normal density, potentially resolving the hyperon puzzle without ad hoc assumptions; however, this behavior depends on the ΛN-ΣN coupling being active, as the Λ potential is repulsive when the coupling is switched off.

## Method

The author evaluated Λ and Σ single-particle potentials in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using lowest-order Brueckner theory with chiral effective field theory NLO hyperon-nucleon interactions. The ΛNN-ΛNN and ΛNN-ΣNN three-baryon forces were reduced to normal-ordered effective two-baryon interactions in nuclear matter by integrating over one nucleon in occupied states, then incorporated into the G-matrix equation. Partial waves up to total angular momentum J=6 were included, with continuous choice for intermediate spectra and angle-average approximation for the Pauli operator.

## Key numbers

- Λ single-particle potential depth at normal density (ρ₀ = 0.166 fm⁻³) is about 30 MeV, consistent with empirical data from hypernuclei
- Repulsion from the ΛNN-ΛNN three-baryon interaction is about 5 MeV at normal density in symmetric nuclear matter
- Repulsion from ΛNN-ΛNN interaction grows to about 20 MeV at 2ρ₀ in symmetric nuclear matter
- The ΛNN-ΣNN coupling compensates the repulsion at normal density, but net three-baryon effect becomes repulsive at higher densities
- Imaginary part of Λ potential is very small (Γ = -2 Im U_Λ), consistent with narrow Λ hypernuclear states
- Imaginary Σ potential Im U_Σ(0) is approximately -15 to -20 MeV
- Coupling constants for three-baryon forces: 2b₂+3b₄ ≈ -3.0 GeV⁻¹, N₃ᵃ ≈ -6.7 GeV⁻¹, N₂ᵇ ≈ -10.7 GeV⁻¹, N₃ᵇ ≈ -2.7 GeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends calculations by Haidenbauer and Meissner (2015) and Petschauer et al. (2016) using the same chiral EFT hyperon-nucleon interactions from Haidenbauer et al. (2013), but improves the treatment of three-baryon forces by using general expressions for off-shell components and including ΛNN-ΣNN coupling effects not considered before. The results show that the Λ potential becomes shallower beyond normal density, which provides a mechanism to address the hyperon puzzle where standard neutron-star equations of state with hyperons cannot explain observed 2 M⊙ neutron stars. The three-baryon force treatment follows the derivation by Petschauer et al. (2016) and uses nuclear matter properties from Kohno (2013) obtained with N³LO nucleon-nucleon potentials from Epelbaum et al. (2005).
