# Single-freeze-out model for ultra relativistic heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV and the LHC proton puzzle

> The single-freeze-out model with parametrized hypersurface and flow geometry is employed to analyze the transverse-momentum spectra of hadrons produced in the Pb+Pb collisions at the collision energy of {$\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV} at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). With the notable exception for protons and antiprotons, we find a very good agreement between the model results and the data for the measured hadron species. The additional analysis of the HBT radii of pions helps us to select, from several different types of freeze-out studied in this work, the most realistic form of the freeze-out hypersurface. We find that discrepancy ratio between the model and experiment for the proton/antiproton spectra depends on $p_T$, dropping from 2 in the soft region to 1 around $p_T=1.5$ GeV.

## Key claim

The single-freeze-out model with parametrized hypersurface and flow geometry describes the transverse-momentum spectra of all measured hadron species in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV except for protons and antiprotons, where the discrepancy ratio between model and experiment is pT-dependent, dropping from 2 in the soft region to 1 around pT = 1.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors employed the single-freeze-out model implemented in the THERMINATOR 2 Monte-Carlo generator, using either the Cracow model (constant proper-time hypersurface with Hubble-like flow) or a generalized Blast-Wave model (with a slope parameter A controlling the freeze-out curve in Minkowski space). Thermodynamic parameters were fixed at T = 165.6 MeV and μB = 0, while geometric parameters (proper time τ, transverse size rmax, and transverse flow parameter vT) were fitted by least squares to the transverse-momentum spectra of charged pions and kaons only; spectra of other hadrons were then predictions. HBT radii of pions were computed to discriminate between different freeze-out scenarios.

## Key numbers

- Temperature fixed at T = 165.6 MeV (chemical freeze-out temperature from RHIC analyses, close to lattice QCD crossover temperature at μB = 0)
- For the Cracow model in central collisions (c = 0–5%): τ3f = 9.0 fm, rmax = 11.4 fm
- For the Cracow model in semi-central collisions (c = 10–20%): τ3f = 7.4 fm, rmax = 9.6 fm
- For the Cracow model in less central collisions (c = 30–40%): τ3f = 5.9 fm, rmax = 7.25 fm
- For the generalized Blast-Wave model with A = -0.5 in central collisions (c = 0–5%): rmax = 10.4 fm, vT = 0.46
- Discrepancy ratio for proton/antiproton spectra: drops from 2 in the soft region to 1 around pT = 1.5 GeV
- Model overpredicts proton spectra only below pT ~ 1.5 GeV; harder part agrees with data

## Context

This work extends the single-freeze-out approach previously applied at RHIC energies (Broniowski 2001, Florkowski 2001, Retiere 2003) to the first LHC Pb+Pb data at √sNN = 2.76 TeV. It confirms the "proton puzzle" first noted in thermal-model abundance analyses (Kalweit 2011) and shows it persists in pT-dependent spectra, with the discrepancy concentrated in the soft region. The HBT analysis selects the freeze-out scenario with A = -0.5 (earlier freeze-out at the edges), consistent with hydrodynamic expansion patterns (Kisiel 2006), and rules out the Cracow model and Blast-Wave models with A ≥ 0. The results enable future work to focus on soft-physics mechanisms (e.g., resonance decays, baryon transport, or non-equilibrium effects) that could resolve the proton discrepancy.
