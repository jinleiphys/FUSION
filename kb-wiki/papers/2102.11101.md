# $γγ$ decay as a probe of neutrinoless $ββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements

> We study double gamma ($γγ$) decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for a wide range of nuclei from titanium to xenon, and explore their relation to neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) NMEs. To favor the comparison, we focus on double-magnetic dipole transitions in the final $ββ$ nuclei, in particular the $γγ$ decay of the double isobaric analog of the initial $ββ$ state into the ground state. For the decay with equal-energy photons, our large-scale nuclear shell model results show a good linear correlation between the $γγ$ and $0νββ$ NMEs. Our analysis reveals that the correlation holds for $γγ$ transitions driven by the spin or orbital angular momentum due to the dominance of zero-coupled nucleon pairs, a feature common to $0νββ$ decay. Our shell-model findings point out the potential of future $γγ$ decay measurements to constrain $0νββ$ NMEs, which are key to answer fundamental physics questions based on $0νββ$ experiments.

# γγ decay as a probe of neutrinoless ββ decay nuclear matrix elements

## Key claim
The authors find a good linear correlation between neutrinoless double-beta (0νββ) and double-gamma (γγ) nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) in large-scale nuclear shell model calculations, but only for the specific case of double-magnetic dipole (M1M1) transitions from the double isobaric analog state (DIAS) to the ground state with equal-energy photons. The correlation holds across a wide range of nuclei from titanium to xenon and is independent of the nuclear interaction used, but the authors caution that it must be tested with other many-body methods before drawing firm conclusions about its universality.

## Method
The authors performed nuclear shell model calculations for 44 nuclei spanning mass numbers A=46–136, using three different configuration spaces (pf shell, pfg space, and sdgh space) with multiple effective interactions (KB3G, GXPF1B, GCN2850, JUN45, JJ4BB, GCN5082, and QX). They calculated double-magnetic dipole γγ NMEs for DIAS-to-ground-state transitions using the Lanczos strength function method with experimental energies when available, and compared these to previously calculated 0νββ NMEs from Shimizu (2018) obtained with the same configuration spaces and interactions.

## Key numbers
- Correlation coefficients: ρ = 0.83 for nuclei with 46 ≤ A ≤ 60 (pf shell), ρ = 0.84 for nuclei with 72 ≤ A ≤ 136 (pfg and sdgh spaces)
- Best-fit linear parameters: a = 0.872, b = 0.459 for A = 46–60; a = 1.29, b = 1.11 for A = 72–136
- A hypothetical γγ branching ratio measurement with ±15% uncertainty would lead to a ±(30%–40%) uncertainty on the extracted 0νββ NME
- The Lanczos strength function method gives converged results to ~1% after 50–100 iterations
- Using experimental energies modifies Mγγ(M1M1) results by less than 5%
- For 48Ti, using experimental input changes the result by only 0.2% compared to using calculated energies

## Context
This work extends the connection between first-order weak and electromagnetic transitions involving isobaric analogue states previously exploited by Ejiri (1968), Fujita (2011), and Ejiri (2013). It builds on the correlation found between 0νββ and double Gamow-Teller transitions by Shimizu (2018), which could in principle be exploited in double charge-exchange reactions but faces challenges from tiny cross sections and involved reaction mechanisms. The authors propose γγ decay measurements as a new experimental avenue to constrain 0νββ NMEs, which currently disagree by up to a factor of 3 between different calculations (Engel 2017). A flagship experiment to measure the γγ decay of the 48Ca DIAS in 48Ti has been proposed by Valiente-Dobón et al.
