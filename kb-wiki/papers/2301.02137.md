# Reduced nuclear helicity amplitudes for deuteron electrodisintegration and other processes

> We extend the original idea of reduced nuclear amplitudes to capture individual helicity amplitudes and discuss various applications to exclusive processes involving the deuteron. Specifically, we consider deuteron form factors, structure functions, tensor polarization observables, photodisintegration, and electrodisintegration. The basic premise is that nuclear processes at high momentum transfer can be approximated by tree graphs for point-like nucleons supplemented by empirical form factors for each nucleon. The latter represent the internal structure of the nucleon, and incorporate nonperturbative physics, which can allow for early onset of scaling behavior. The nucleon form factors are evaluated at the net momentum transfer experienced by the given nucleon, with use of $G_E$ for a no-flip contribution and $G_M$ for a helicity-flip contribution. Results are compared with data where available. The deuteron photodisintegration asymmetry $Σ$ is obtained with a value of $Σ(90^\circ)\simeq -0.06$, which is much closer to experiment than the value of -1 originally expected. The method also provides an estimate of the momentum transfer values required for scaling onset. We find that the deuteron structure function $B$ is a good place to look, above momentum transfers of 10 GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The reduced nuclear helicity amplitude (RNHA) method extends the original reduced nuclear amplitude (RNA) idea of Brodsky and Chertok to capture individual helicity amplitudes for deuteron processes, but the overall normalization of the RNHA amplitude is unknown and must be eliminated by considering ratios rather than absolute cross sections.

## Method

The authors model nuclear processes at high momentum transfer as tree-level Feynman diagrams for point-like nucleons, then multiply each nucleon line by an empirical form factor evaluated at the net momentum transfer to that nucleon. For helicity-conserving (no-flip) nucleon transitions they use the electric form factor \(G_E\), and for helicity-flip transitions they use the magnetic form factor \(G_M\). The four tree diagrams differ in which nucleon absorbs the photon and the order of photon absorption and momentum exchange between nucleons; the full amplitude is constructed by summing these diagrams weighted by Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for the deuteron helicity state.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron photodisintegration asymmetry \(\Sigma\) at \(90^\circ\) is \(\Sigma(90^\circ)\simeq -0.06\), much closer to experiment than the originally expected value of -1.
- The deuteron structure function \(B\) is predicted to show leading scaling behavior above momentum transfers of 10 GeV\(^2\).
- For elastic deuteron form factors, the leading power-law behavior is \(G_C \sim (m/Q)^{10}\), \(G_M \sim (m/Q)^{12}\), and \(G_Q \sim (m/Q)^{12}\) in the large-\(\eta\) regime, where \(\eta = Q^2/(16m^2)\).
- The ratio \(G_M/G_Q\) approaches \(-11.2 + \mathcal{O}(m^2/Q^2)\) at large \(Q^2\), deviating from the point-like spin-one particle ratio of -2.
- The tensor polarization observable \(t_{20}\) approaches \(-\sqrt{2}\) at very large \(Q^2\) for \(\theta_e = 0^\circ\), consistent with perturbative QCD predictions from Dymarz and CarlsonGross.
- The momentum transfer threshold for each nucleon to be above 1 GeV\(^2\) in deuteron photodisintegration is given by \(m_N E_\gamma[1 - \sqrt{E_\gamma/(m_N+E_\gamma)}|\cos\theta|] \geq 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- The recent JLab experiment by Yero (2022) reached momentum transfers up to 5 GeV\(^2\) for electrodisintegration, with a nominal transfer of 4.2 GeV\(^2\).
- The structure function expansions are \(A(Q^2) \sim 0.1041\mathcal{N}^2 (m/Q)^{20}\) and \(B(Q^2) \sim 13.06\mathcal{N}^2 (m/Q)^{20}\) at leading order.

## Context

This work extends the reduced nuclear amplitude approach pioneered by Brodsky and Chertok (1975), which was previously applied to deuteron form factors, deuteron disintegration, pion photoproduction, and photodisintegration of \(^3\)He. The RNHA method enables modeling of polarization observables such as recoil-proton polarization, transferred polarizations, and the photon asymmetry \(\Sigma\), which were not accessible in the original RNA framework. The results suggest that the deuteron structure function \(B\) is the most promising observable for testing scaling onset at momentum transfers above 10 GeV\(^2\), and that experiments at Jefferson Lab with the upgraded 10.6 GeV beam are beginning to reach the kinematic regime where the model can be applied.
