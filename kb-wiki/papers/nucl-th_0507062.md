# LPM Interference and Cherenkov-like Gluon Bremsstrahlung in Dense Matter

> Gluon bremsstrahlung induced by multiple parton scattering in a finite dense medium has a unique angular distribution with respect to the initial parton direction. A dead-cone structure with an opening angle $θ^2_0 \approx 2(1-z)/(zLE)$ for gluons with fractional energy $z$ arises from the Landau-Pomeranchuck-Migdal (LPM) interference. In a medium where the gluon's dielectric constant is $ε>1$, the LPM interference pattern is shown to become Cherenkov-like with an increased opening angle determined by the dielectric constant $\cos^2θ_c=z+(1-z)/ε$. For a large dielectric constant $ε\gg 1+2/z^2LE$, the corresponding total radiative parton energy loss is about twice that from normal gluon bremsstrahlung. Implications of this Cherenkov-like gluon bremsstrahlung to the jet correlation pattern in high-energy heavy-ion collisions is discussed.

# LPM Interference and Cherenkov-like Gluon Bremsstrahlung in Dense Matter

## Key claim

In a medium where gluons acquire a space-like dispersion relation with dielectric constant ε > 1, the Landau-Pomeranchuck-Migdal (LPM) interference pattern in induced gluon bremsstrahlung becomes Cherenkov-like, with the angular distribution peaking at an angle determined by the dielectric constant rather than by the medium size; however, this result depends on the assumption that the gluon self-energy Π_T(ℓ) is negative and that the imaginary part is small enough that soft gluons are not damped during propagation.

## Method

The authors start from the twist-expansion formalism for gluon bremsstrahlung induced by double scattering of a fast quark in a static dense medium with a Gaussian density distribution, obtaining the gluon spectrum as a function of fractional energy z and transverse momentum ℓ_T. They then replace the light-like gluon propagator with an effective propagator containing a transverse self-energy Π_T(ℓ) that gives a space-like dispersion relation, and compute the modified gluon spectrum by substituting ℓ_T^2 → ℓ_T^2 + (1−z)Π_T(ℓ) in the original formula. The resulting angular distribution is analyzed for the case where Π_T(ℓ) is negative and approximately independent of ℓ_T for soft gluons.

## Key numbers

- The dead-cone opening angle for normal LPM-interfered gluon bremsstrahlung is θ_0 ≈ √[2(1−z)/(zEL)], where z is the gluon fractional energy, E is the initial parton energy, and L is the medium size.
- For Cherenkov-like gluon bremsstrahlung, the cone angle satisfies cos²θ_c = z + (1−z)/ε, where ε is the gluon dielectric constant.
- When the gluon dielectric constant satisfies ε ≫ 1 + 2/(z²EL), the total radiative parton energy loss is about twice that from normal gluon bremsstrahlung.
- In the limit |Π_T(ℓ)|L/(2E) ≪ 1, the energy loss is ΔE ≈ ΔE_0 × (1 + (1/3)|Π_T(ℓ)|L/(2E)), where ΔE_0 ≈ \widetilde{C}α_s²m_N L² 3 ln(E/μ) is the normal radiative energy loss.
- The condition for the LPM interference pattern to be observable in soft hadron spectra is zE/L > Λ_QCD²/2.

## Context

This work extends the theory of LPM-interfered gluon bremsstrahlung in dense QCD matter, previously developed by the authors and others (Wang and Guo, 2001; Baier et al.; Zakharov; Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev; Wiedemann), to include the effect of a gluon dielectric constant ε > 1. The Cherenkov-like pattern is contrasted with the Mach-cone conical flow proposed by Shuryak, Chaudhuri, and Stocker as an explanation for the broadened angular distribution of soft hadrons observed at RHIC by STAR and PHENIX. The authors note that true Cherenkov gluon radiation without multiple scattering (Ruppert; Dremin) gives a much smaller energy loss than scattering-induced radiation, as pointed out by Koch, Majumder, and Wang (2005). The results imply that previous extractions of initial gluon density from RHIC high-p_T hadron suppression data (Wang and Wang, 2002; Gyulassy and Vitev, 2002; Wang, 2003) may need revision if the medium has a large gluon dielectric constant.
