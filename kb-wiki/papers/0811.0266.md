# Perturbative QCD predictions for two-photon exchange

> We study two-photon exchange (TPE) in the elastic electron-nucleon scattering at high Q^2 in the framework of pQCD. The obtained TPE amplitude is of order alpha/alpha_s with respect to Born approximation. Its shape and value are sensitive to the choice of nucleon wavefunction, thus study of TPE effects can provide important information about nucleon structure. With the wavefunctions based on QCD sum rules, TPE correction to the electron-proton cross section has negative sign, is almost linear in epsilon and grows logarithmically with Q^2 up to 7% at Q^2 = 30 GeV^2. The results of existing "hadronic" calculations, taking into account just the nucleon intermediate state, can be smoothly connected with pQCD result near Q^2 = 3 GeV^2. Above this point two methods disagree, which implies that "hadronic" approach becomes inadequate at high Q^2. Other relevant observables, such as electron/positron cross section ratio, are also discussed.

# Perturbative QCD predictions for two-photon exchange

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange (TPE) amplitude in elastic electron-nucleon scattering at high $Q^2$ is of order $\alpha/\alpha_s$ relative to the Born amplitude (not simply $\alpha$ as naively expected), making TPE increasingly important at higher $Q^2$; however, the shape and magnitude of the correction are highly sensitive to the choice of nucleon quark distribution amplitude, and the leading-order pQCD result disagrees with the "hadronic" approach above $Q^2 \sim 3\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$, implying the hadronic method becomes inadequate at high $Q^2$.

## Method

The authors calculate the TPE amplitude in elastic electron-nucleon scattering using leading-order perturbative QCD, representing the nucleon as three collinearly moving quarks with momenta $x_i p$ and employing the convolution formalism of Brodsky-Lepage and Chernyak-Zhitnitsky for baryon form factors. The hard scattering amplitude is computed from the four leading-order Feynman diagrams where the two photons couple to different quarks (diagrams where both photons couple to the same quark are subleading in $\alpha_s$), and the result is sandwiched between model nucleon quark distribution amplitudes. The ratio $\delta\mathcal{G}_M/G_M$ is evaluated for several proposed wavefunctions (CZ, KS, COZ, GS, Het) and compared with existing "hadronic" calculations that include only the nucleon intermediate state.

## Key numbers

- TPE amplitude $\delta\mathcal{G}_M/G_M$ reaches 3.5% of the Born amplitude at $Q^2 = 30\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$, corresponding to a cross section correction of about 7%.
- The smooth connection between pQCD and "hadronic" calculations occurs near $Q^2 \sim 3\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$; above this point the two methods disagree.
- For the neutron target with GS and Het wavefunctions, TPE corrections are anomalously large, up to 25%.
- The amplitude $\delta\mathcal{G}_3$, which determines the correction to longitudinal recoil polarization, is small (less than 1%) for both proton and neutron.
- The strong coupling constant is parameterized with $\Lambda = 0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the pQCD formalism previously applied to nucleon form factors by Brodsky-Lepage (1979) and Chernyak-Zhitnitsky (1984) to the two-photon exchange process, which had not been directly calculated in pQCD before. It contradicts the approach of the "Parton" paper (2007) which used generalized parton distributions and assumed the dominant diagrams are those where both photons interact with the same quark; the pQCD result shows the opposite situation. The results enable TPE observables (cross section ratio, recoil polarization) to serve as a new tool for discriminating between proposed nucleon quark distribution amplitudes, with existing data already ruling out the GS wavefunction (1990).
