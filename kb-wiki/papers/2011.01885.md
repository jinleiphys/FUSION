# Electric dipole moments of three-nucleon systems in the pionless effective field theory

> We calculate the electric dipole moments (EDMs) of three-nucleon systems at leading order in pionless effective field theory. The one-body contributions that arise from permanent proton and neutron EDMs and the two-body contributions that arise from CP-odd nucleon-nucleon interactions are taken into account. Neglecting the Coulomb interaction, we consider the triton and ${}^3$He, and also investigate them in the Wigner-SU(4) symmetric limit. We also calculate the electric dipole form factor and find numerically that the momentum dependence of the electric dipole form factor in the Wigner limit is, up to an overall constant (and numerical accuracy), the same as the momentum dependence of the charge form factor.

## Key claim

The electric dipole moments (EDMs) of triton and ${}^3$He are calculated at leading order in pionless effective field theory, neglecting the Coulomb interaction, and are expressed in terms of one-body nucleon EDMs and five two-nucleon CP-odd couplings; the momentum dependence of the electric dipole form factor in the Wigner-SU(4) symmetric limit is found to be the same as that of the charge form factor up to an overall constant and numerical accuracy.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory (EFT($\slashed{\pi}$)) with auxiliary dimer fields for the $^3S_1$ and $^1S_0$ channels and a trimer field for the three-nucleon bound state. They derive integral equations for the CP-even vertex function and for T-odd vertex functions that incorporate insertions of CP-odd two-nucleon operators mediating $S$-wave to $P$-wave transitions. The electric dipole form factor is computed from three-particle irreducible diagrams containing either one-body nucleon EDM insertions or two-body CP-odd operator insertions, with the Coulomb interaction neglected and the binding energies of $^3$H and $^3$He taken equal.

## Key numbers

- Binding momentum of the deuteron: $\gamma_t \simeq 45.7$ MeV
- $^1S_0$ virtual-state momentum: $\gamma_s \simeq -7.9$ MeV
- Binding energy used to fix three-body parameters: $B(^3{\rm H}) = -8.48$ MeV
- Estimate for neutron EDM from QCD $\bar\theta$-term: $d_n(\bar\theta) \simeq 2 \cdot 10^{-3} \, \bar\theta \, e$ fm
- Expansion parameter of pionless EFT: $\gamma_t \rho_t \approx 0.4$, where $\rho_t$ is the effective range in the triplet channel
- Cutoffs larger than approximately 1.5 GeV are needed for numerically converged results
- Numerical uncertainty of the results is estimated to be 1% or lower

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless effective field theory to CP-violating observables in three-nucleon systems, following the formalism developed for charge form factors by Vanasse (2015, 2017) and the construction of CP-odd nucleon-nucleon operators by Maekawa (2011) and deVries (2020). It complements chiral effective field theory calculations of light-nuclei EDMs by Bsaisou (2014) and Gnech (2019), and addresses the need for systematic uncertainty estimates in connecting nuclear EDMs to quark-level CP violation. The results enable direct matching to lattice QCD calculations of few-nucleon matrix elements and provide a framework for interpreting upcoming storage-ring EDM experiments on light nuclei.
