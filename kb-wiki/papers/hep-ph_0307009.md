# K^-/K^+ ratio at GSI in hot and dense matter

> The $K^-/K^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions at GSI energies is studied including the properties of the participating hadrons in hot and dense matter. The determination of the temperature and chemical potential at freeze-out conditions compatible with the ratio $K^-/K^+$ is very delicate, and depends on the approach adopted for the antikaon self-energy. Three approaches for the $K^-$ self-energy are considered: non-interacting $K^-$, on-shell self-energy and single-particle spectral density. With respect to the on-shell approach, the use of an energy dependent $\bar{K}$ spectral density, including both s- and p-wave components of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction, lowers considerably the freeze-out temperature and gives rise to the "broad-band equilibration" advocated by Brown, Rho and Song.

# K^-/K^+ ratio at GSI in hot and dense matter

## Key claim

The temperature and chemical potential at freeze-out compatible with the measured K^-/K^+ ratio of 0.031 ± 0.005 in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV depend very strongly on the approach used for the antikaon self-energy; the "broad-band equilibration" advocated by Brown, Rho and Song is only observed when the K^- is described by the full spectral density including both s- and p-wave components, but this approach yields a K^-/K^+ ratio in excess by a factor of 2 relative to the experimental value.

## Method

The authors compute the K^-/K^+ ratio within a statistical hadronic gas model assuming thermal and chemical equilibrium, using the one-particle partition functions for K^+, K^-, Λ, Σ, and Σ^*(1385) hyperons. Three approaches for the K^- self-energy are compared: non-interacting K^- mesons, an on-shell (mean-field) self-energy from a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach based on the Jülich group meson-exchange potential, and a full single-particle spectral density including both s-wave and p-wave (YN^-1 hyperon-hole) components of the K^-N interaction.

## Key numbers

- Experimental K^-/K^+ ratio: 0.031 ± 0.005 for Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV (Menzel)
- For K^+/K^- = 30 with the full K^- spectral density: freeze-out temperature T ≈ 35 MeV and baryonic chemical potential μ_B up to 850 MeV
- For the full spectral density, a ratio of K^+/K^- ≈ 15 gives a more plausible temperature of T ≈ 70 MeV
- Λ mean-field potential: U_Λ(ρ) = -340ρ + 1087.5ρ^2 MeV, with ρ in fm^-3
- Σ mean-field potential: U_Σ(ρ) = 30ρ/ρ_0 MeV, with ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^-3
- Σ^*(1385) mass: 1385 MeV, width: 37 MeV

## Context

This work extends statistical model calculations of the K^-/K^+ ratio by Cleymans and collaborators, incorporating in-medium modifications of hadron properties at finite temperature and density. The study directly addresses the interpretation by Brown, Rho and Song of the centrality independence of the K^-/K^+ ratio as "broad-band equilibration" due to medium effects, in contrast to the interpretation by Cleymans that such independence signals the absence of in-medium effects. The results show that while the full spectral density approach produces a flat region in the ratio versus density, the resulting freeze-out temperatures are too low to be compatible with measured pion spectra, suggesting that dynamical non-equilibrium effects may be needed to explain the observed number of on-shell K^- at freeze-out.
