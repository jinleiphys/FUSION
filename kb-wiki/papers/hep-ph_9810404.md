# Glueball Spectrum from an Effective Hamiltonian

> Using simple and general arguments we propose an effective Hamiltonian for the description of low-energy pure QCD. The Hamiltonian is a function of spatially constant collective modes. Its eigenstates can be organized into bands classified by the irreducible representations of an O(8) group. The latter also determine parity and charge conjugation of the states. The energy spectrum agrees well with the glueball spectrum as measured on the lattice, and in particular the level ordering with respect to spin is naturally explained.

## Key claim

The authors propose an effective Hamiltonian for low-energy pure QCD that depends only on spatially constant collective modes, and they argue that its spectrum agrees well with lattice glueball results, but the agreement is conditional on fitting several free parameters to lattice data rather than being a parameter-free prediction from first principles.

## Method

The authors construct a group-theoretical classification of many-gluon states using a U(24) ⊃ U(8) × U(3) chain that implements Bose-Einstein statistics, with O(8) irreps determining parity and charge conjugation. They then introduce an effective Hamiltonian in terms of spatially constant gauge fields, perform a change of variables to collective coordinates (monopole, quadrupole, and rotational angles in space and colour space), and expand the kinetic term in powers of 1/σ² and β/σ. The potential term is modelled with a simple ansatz, and the resulting Hamiltonian depends on several parameters that are fixed by fitting to lattice glueball masses.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter 1/σ² starts from 1/6 and decreases with larger ω_k values, where σ² = ω₁ + ω₂ + ω₃ + 6.
- The O(8) quantum numbers (ω₁ω₂ω₃0) determine parity via P = (-1)^N = (-1)^(ω₁+ω₂+ω₃).
- The number of constituent gluons N is given by N = h₁ + h₂ + h₃ = 2(n₁ + n₂ + n₃) + ω₁ + ω₂ + ω₃.
- The volume element after transformation includes the factor (β/σ)⁴ sin(3c) dϱ dβ dc dΩ_θ dΩ_φ for small β/σ.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lüscher, van Baal and collaborators (1990s), who used a Born-Oppenheimer approximation to integrate out fast modes in a small box with periodic boundary conditions, but whose perturbation theory breaks down for boxes larger than about 0.7 fm. The authors instead motivate an ansatz for the effective Hamiltonian based on group-theoretical structure, enabling predictions for glueball states beyond the range of the Lüscher–van Baal method. The classification scheme using O(8) irreps explains the level ordering observed in lattice simulations by Peardon (1990s) and others, and the results can be compared with the MIT bag model and future lattice calculations.
