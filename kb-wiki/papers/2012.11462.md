# Reweighted nuclear PDFs using Heavy-Flavor Production Data at the LHC: nCTEQ15_rwHF & EPPS16_rwHF

> We present the reweighting of two sets of nuclear PDFs, nCTEQ15 and EPPS16, using a selection of experimental data on heavy-flavor meson [D0, J/psi, J/psi from B and Upsilon(1S)] production in proton-lead collisions at the LHC which were not used in the original determination of these nuclear PDFs. The reweighted PDFs exhibit significantly smaller uncertainties thanks to these new heavy-flavor constraints. We present a comparison with another selection of data from the LHC and RHIC which were not included in our reweighting procedure. The comparison is overall very good and serves as a validation of these reweighted nuclear PDF sets, which we dub nCTEQ15_rwHF & EPPS16_rwHF. This indicates that the LHC and forward RHIC heavy-flavor data can be described within the standard collinear factorization framework with the same (universal) small-x gluon distribution. We discuss how we believe such reweighted PDFs should be used as well as the limitations of our procedure.

## Key claim
The reweighted nuclear PDF sets nCTEQ15_rwHF and EPPS16_rwHF, obtained by applying Bayesian reweighting to LHC heavy-flavor production data not used in the original fits, show that the LHC and forward RHIC heavy-flavor data can be described within standard collinear factorization with a universal small-x gluon distribution, but the reweighting carries a significant dependence on the choice of factorization scale that can be as large as the resulting uncertainty of the reweighted PDFs themselves.

## Method
The authors performed Bayesian reweighting of the nCTEQ15 and EPPS16 nuclear PDF sets using experimental data on D0, J/psi, B to J/psi, and Upsilon(1S) production in proton-lead collisions at the LHC. The Hessian PDF sets were first converted into 10,000 replicas, then each replica was assigned a weight based on its chi-squared description of the new heavy-flavor data using a tolerance criterion T. The reweighted PDFs were computed as weighted averages over the replicas, and the results were converted into LHAPDF grids with Hessian uncertainties for practical use.

## Key numbers
- The reweighting constrains the gluon density in lead down to x approximately 7 times 10 to the minus 6, a region where no other data exist.
- The significance of gluon depletion (shadowing) at x smaller than 0.01 was larger than 7 sigma according to the reweighting analysis.
- The reweighting used 10 to the 4 replicas for the conversion from Hessian to replica representation.
- The Upsilon(1S) data had very limited impact on the original nPDFs due to large uncertainties of the data.

## Context
This work extends the earlier reweighting study by Kusina et al. (2017) by releasing the reweighted nPDFs as LHAPDF grids with Hessian uncertainties and providing new comparisons with LHC and RHIC data that serve as post-validation. The study builds on the nCTEQ15 fit by Kovarik et al. (2015) and the EPPS16 fit by Eskola et al. (2016), which had left the nuclear gluon content undetermined at x below 10 to the minus 3. The reweighted PDFs enable future studies of initial-state nuclear effects on hard probes of the quark-gluon plasma, but the authors caution that a full NLO fit using these data would be needed before definitive conclusions can be drawn.
