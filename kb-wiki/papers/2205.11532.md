# Improved indirect limits on charm and bottom quark EDMs

> We derive indirect limits on the charm and bottom quark electric dipole moments (EDMs) from paramagnetic AMO and neutron EDM experiments. The charm and bottom quark EDMs generate $CP$-odd photon-gluon operators and light quark EDMs at the $c$- and $b$-quark mass thresholds. These $CP$-odd operators induce the $CP$-odd semi-leptonic operator $C_S$ and the neutron EDM below the QCD scale that are probed by the paramagnetic and neutron EDM experiments, respectively. The bound from $C_S$ is $\vert d_c \vert < 1.3\times 10^{-20}\,e\,\mathrm{cm}$ for the charm quark and $\vert d_b \vert < 7.6\times 10^{-19}\,e\,\mathrm{cm}$ for the bottom quark, with its uncertainty estimated as 10%. The neutron EDM provides a stronger bound, $\vert d_c \vert < 6\times 10^{-22}\,e\,\mathrm{cm}$ and $\vert d_b \vert < 2\times 10^{-20}\,e\,\mathrm{cm}$, though with a larger hadronic uncertainty.

## Key claim

The paper derives indirect limits on charm and bottom quark electric dipole moments (EDMs) from paramagnetic and neutron EDM experiments, with the paramagnetic bound being theoretically clean (10% uncertainty) while the neutron EDM bound is stronger but suffers from larger hadronic uncertainties; these limits apply directly to the heavy quark EDMs at the quark mass threshold without assuming any cancellation between EDM and chromo-EDM contributions.

## Method

The authors integrate out charm and bottom quarks with EDMs to generate CP-odd photon-gluon operators and light quark EDMs at the heavy quark mass thresholds. They compute the CP-odd semi-leptonic operator C_S from the two-photon two-gluon operator using the nucleon matrix element of the gluon operator from the trace anomaly and a one-loop photon diagram, and estimate the neutron EDM from both the light quark EDM contribution (using the formula d_n = (4d_d - d_u)/3) and the three-gluon one-photon operator contribution (using QCD sum rule techniques with the quark-gluon condensate).

## Key numbers

* Charm quark EDM bound from C_S (paramagnetic): |d_c| < 1.3 × 10^{-20} e cm, with 10% uncertainty
* Bottom quark EDM bound from C_S (paramagnetic): |d_b| < 7.6 × 10^{-19} e cm, with 10% uncertainty
* Charm quark EDM bound from neutron EDM: |d_c| < 6 × 10^{-22} e cm
* Bottom quark EDM bound from neutron EDM: |d_b| < 2 × 10^{-20} e cm
* Current experimental bound on equivalent electron EDM used: |d_e^{(equiv)}| < 1.1 × 10^{-29} e cm (ACME experiment, 2018)
* Current experimental bound on neutron EDM used: |d_n| < 1.8 × 10^{-26} e cm (Abel et al., 2020)
* Direct bound from e+e- → qqbar at LEP: ~10^{-17} e cm (Blinov et al., 2008)

## Context

This work extends previous indirect limits on charm and bottom quark EDMs from Gisbert et al. (2019), which reinterpreted bounds on heavy quark chromo-EDMs under the assumption of no cancellation between EDM and chromo-EDM contributions. The present analysis provides independent constraints that apply directly to the heavy quark EDMs at the mass threshold without such assumptions, and uses the paramagnetic EDM constraint (which avoids large hadronic uncertainties) as a clean cross-check. The results enable future improvements as paramagnetic EDM experiments continue to advance, and complement proposed LHC-based direct measurements of charm baryon dipole moments (Bagli et al., 2017; Aiola et al., 2020).
