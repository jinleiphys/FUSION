# Critical Temperature for $α$-Particle Condensation within a Momentum Projected Mean Field Approach

> Alpha-particle (quartet) condensation in homogeneous spin-isospin symmetric nuclear matter is investigated. The usual Thouless criterion for the critical temperature is extended to the quartet case. The in-medium four-body problem is strongly simplified by the use of a momentum projected mean field ansatz for the quartet. The self-consistent single particle wave functions are shown and discussed for various values of the density at the critical temperature.

# Critical Temperature for α-Particle Condensation within a Momentum Projected Mean Field Approach

## Key claim

The critical temperature for α-particle (quartet) condensation in homogeneous symmetric nuclear matter can be computed using a strongly simplified momentum-projected mean field ansatz for the four-body wave function, yielding results that agree with more complex Faddeev-Yakubovsky solutions; however, the approach is limited to densities where well-defined quartets exist and cannot reach the BEC limit or the weak-coupling regime.

## Method

The authors extend the Thouless criterion for pair condensation to the four-body case, where the critical temperature is determined when the in-medium four-body equation eigenvalue reaches 4μ. They employ a momentum-projected mean field ansatz for the quartet wave function, assuming all four fermions occupy the same 0S single-particle wave function, and replace the two-body interaction with a spin-isospin averaged separable potential. The self-consistent nonlinear Hartree-Fock type equation for the single-particle wave function is solved iteratively, with the chemical potential determined from the free Fermi gas relation.

## Key numbers

- Free α-particle binding energy: −28.3 MeV (used to fix interaction parameters)
- Free α-particle radius: 1.71 fm (used to fix interaction parameters)
- Adjusted separable potential parameters: λ = −992 MeV fm³, b = 1.43 fm⁻¹
- Maximum critical temperature for α-condensation occurs at μ = 5.5 MeV
- α-condensation exists up to μ_max = 11 MeV
- Deuteron binding energy: −2.2 MeV (used to fix deuteron interaction parameters)
- Deuteron radius: 1.95 fm (used to fix deuteron interaction parameters)
- Deuteron separable potential parameters: λ = −1305 MeV fm³, k₀ = 1.46 fm⁻¹
- At μ = −2.22 MeV: T_c = 6.61 MeV, n = 9.41 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³
- At μ = 6.17 MeV: T_c = 8.45 MeV, n = 3.07 × 10⁻² fm⁻³
- At μ = 10.6 MeV: T_c = 5.54 MeV, n = 3.34 × 10⁻² fm⁻³
- T_c^α results are about 25 percent higher than those of Röpke et al. (1998)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of α-particle condensation in nuclear matter by Röpke et al. (1998) and Beyer (using Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations), confirming their results with a much simpler variational approach. The momentum-projected mean field ansatz is analogous to the THSR wave function used by Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, and Röpke (2001) for α-cluster states in nuclei. The authors aim toward a Nozières and Schmitt-Rink (1985) type treatment for quartets, which would incorporate bound state contributions to the total nucleon density and enable reaching the BEC regime; this is left for future work. The approach may also be applicable to trion systems and three-color quark models.
