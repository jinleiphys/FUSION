# Classical shadows for sample-efficient measurements of gauge-invariant observables

> Classical shadows provide a versatile framework for estimating many properties of quantum states from repeated, randomly chosen measurements without requiring full quantum state tomography. When prior information is available, such as knowledge of symmetries of states and operators, this knowledge can be exploited to significantly improve sample efficiency. In this work, we develop three classical shadow protocols for $\mathbb{Z}_2$ lattice gauge theory, where a dual formulation enables a rigorous analysis of resource requirements, including both circuit depth and sample complexity. Our approaches can offer exponential improvements in sample complexity over symmetry-agnostic methods, albeit at the cost of increased circuit complexity. While our analysis is restricted to $\mathbb{Z}_2$ lattice gauge theory, our approach offers a blueprint for similar protocols for more general lattice gauge theory models which are currently at the forefront of quantum simulation efforts.

## Key claim
The paper develops three classical shadow protocols for Z2 lattice gauge theory that can offer exponential improvements in sample complexity over symmetry-agnostic methods, but only at the cost of increased circuit complexity; the analysis is restricted to Z2 lattice gauge theory but is presented as a blueprint for more general lattice gauge theory models.

## Method
The authors exploit the exact duality between Z2 lattice gauge theory in (2+1)D and an Ising model, using the dual Ising formulation to design and analyze three symmetry-aware random measurement protocols. The Global Dual Pairs protocol randomly pairs dual lattice sites and applies two-qubit parity-respecting unitaries mapped back to the gauge theory, while the Local Dual Pairs protocol restricts this pairing to local patches for geometrically local observables; the Dual Product protocol applies the standard Product Protocol to the dual Ising model. All protocols involve classical pre-processing to construct the random unitaries, quantum measurement on the lattice gauge theory state, and classical post-processing where measurement outcomes are mapped to the dual Ising picture for efficient shadow channel inversion.

## Key numbers
- The physical Hilbert space within a given Vx,Vy sector has dimension 2^(NxNy-1), whereas the full Hilbert space has dimension 2^(2NxNy), representing an exponential reduction.
- The Global Dual Pairs protocol requires circuit depth O(V) without parallelization and O(V^2) as an upper bound with parallelization, where V = Nx * Ny is the system volume.
- The Dual Product protocol requires circuit depth O(V^2) and an additional ancilla qubit for periodic boundary conditions.
- For constant-norm observables with wXY+wZ = O(1) and wI = Theta(V), the factor f*alpha scales as Theta(V^((wZ-k_dual)/2)), yielding polynomial sample complexity in system size.
- The number of samples nu needed scales as O((log M / epsilon^2) * max_j Var(o_j)), with variance bounded by (3^(wXY/2))/(f*alpha) * ||O_j||_inf^2.

## Context
This work extends the classical shadows framework (Huang 2020) to lattice gauge theories by incorporating prior knowledge of local symmetries, specifically building on the "All-Pairs" approach of Hearth (2024) for particle-number-preserving randomization. It complements recent numerical studies of deep-randomization circuits for lattice gauge theories (Bringewatt 2024) by providing shallow protocols with rigorous asymptotic performance guarantees tailored for efficient estimation of gauge-invariant observables. The protocols enable sample-efficient measurements on near-term quantum devices simulating Z2 lattice gauge theory and provide a blueprint for extending similar symmetry-aware shadow protocols to more general lattice gauge theories, including non-Abelian cases.
