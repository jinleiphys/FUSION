# Phenomenological study on the $\bar p N\to \bar NNππ$ reactions

> We extend our recent phenomenological study of $pN \to NNππ$ reactions to the $\bar{p}N \to \bar{N}Nππ$ reactions for anti-proton beam momenta up to 3.0 GeV within an effective Lagrangian approach. The contribution of $N^*(1440)$ with its $Nσ$ decay mode is found to be dominant at the energies close to threshold for $\bar NNπ^+π^-$ and $\bar NNπ^0π^0$ channels. At higher energies or for $p\bar nπ^-π^-$ and $\bar NNπ^\pmπ^0$ channels where $N^*(1440)\to Nσ$ mode cannot contribute, large contributions from double-$Δ$, $Δ(1600) \to N^*(1440)π$, $Δ(1600) \to Δπ$ and $Δ(1620) \to Δπ$ are found. In the near-threshold region, sizeable contributions from $Δ\to Δπ$, $Δ\to Nπ$, $N \to Δπ$ and nucleon pole are also indicated. Although these results are similar to those for $pN\to NNππ$ reactions, the antinucleon-nucleon collisions are shown to be complementary to the nucleon-nucleon collisions and may even have advantages in some aspects. The PANDA/FAIR experiment is suggested to be an excellent place for studying the properties of relevant $N^*$ and $Δ^*$ resonances.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previous phenomenological study of nucleon-nucleon double-pion production to antiproton-nucleon reactions, finding that the N*(1440) resonance with its Nσ decay mode dominates near threshold for the \(\bar NN\pi^+\pi^-\) and \(\bar NN\pi^0\pi^0\) channels, while at higher energies or in channels where that mode cannot contribute, double-Δ, Δ(1600), and Δ(1620) contributions become large. The authors note that their model overestimates the \(\bar pp\) channel data, likely due to neglected initial and final state interactions, but argue that the relative resonance contributions are robust.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with Feynman diagrams for \(\bar N N \to \bar N N \pi\pi\) reactions, using the same coupling constants, form factors, and resonance parameters (N*(1440), Δ(1232), Δ*(1600), Δ*(1620)) previously determined from fits to \(NN \to NN\pi\pi\) data. They computed total and differential cross sections for four isospin channels at antiproton beam momenta up to 3.0 GeV, without introducing any new free parameters, and performed Monte Carlo phase-space integration using the CERN FOWL code.

## Key numbers

- Beam momenta studied: up to 3.0 GeV.
- N*(1440) pole position: (1365, 190) MeV; Breit-Wigner width: 300 MeV.
- Δ*(1600) pole position: (1600, 300) MeV; Breit-Wigner width: 350 MeV.
- Δ*(1620) pole position: (1600, 118) MeV; Breit-Wigner width: 145 MeV.
- Coupling constants used: \(f_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi = 0.078\), \(g_{\sigma NN}^2/4\pi = 5.69\), \(g_{\rho NN}^2/4\pi = 0.9\).
- Cutoff parameters: \(\Lambda_\pi = \Lambda_\eta = 1.0\) GeV, \(\Lambda_\sigma = 1.3\) GeV, \(\Lambda_\rho = 1.6\) GeV.
- σ-meson mass: 550 MeV; width: 500 MeV.
- For the \(\bar{p}n \to p\bar{n}\pi^-\pi^-\) channel, the Δ → Nπ → Nππ term dominates below 2000 MeV; above 2300 MeV the Δ*(1600) → N*(1440)π term becomes largest.
- For \(\bar{p}p \to \bar{p}p\pi^+\pi^-\), the N*(1440) → Nσ term gives the largest contribution below 1800 MeV; above 1800 MeV the double-Δ term takes over.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of \(NN \to NN\pi\pi\) reactions (Cao et al., 2010) to the antiproton-nucleon sector, motivated by the need to better constrain the properties of N*(1440), Δ(1600), and Δ(1620) resonances. It contrasts with earlier theoretical studies of \(\bar p N \to \bar N N\pi\pi\) that used only one-pion exchange (OPE) or Regge pole models (1970s) and included only double-Δ excitations. The paper suggests that the PANDA experiment at FAIR can provide complementary data to nucleon-nucleon collisions, particularly for the \(\bar{p}n \to p\bar{n}\pi^-\pi^-\) channel, which is cleaner than the analogous \(pp \to nn\pi^+\pi^+\) channel for studying Δ* resonances.
