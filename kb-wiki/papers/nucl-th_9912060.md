# Lippmann-Schwinger Resonating-Group Formalism for NN and YN Interactions in an SU6 Quark Model

> We formulate a Lippmann-Schwinger-type resonating-group equation to calculate invariant amplitudes of the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction. When applied to our recent SU6 quark model for the nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions, this technique yields very accurate phase-shift parameters for all partial waves up to the energies of several GeV. The technique also has a merit of a straightforward extension to the G-matrix equation. A new analytic method is proposed to calculate the quark-exchange Born kernel for the momentum-dependent two-body interaction. The partial-wave decomposition in the momentum representation is carried out numerically. The invariant amplitudes are then used to calculate single-nucleon potentials in normal nuclear matter for high incident momenta q_1 > 3 (1/fm), in which the so-called t^eff-rho prescription is found to be a good approximation to the single-particle potentials directly calculated in the lowest-order Brueckner theory.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a Lippmann-Schwinger-type resonating-group equation (LS-RGM) in momentum representation to calculate invariant amplitudes of the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction, claiming that this technique yields very accurate phase-shift parameters for all partial waves up to several GeV, though the method requires more CPU time than the variational method used previously.

## Method

The authors derive an LS-RGM equation from the standard RGM equation for the (3q)-(3q) system by converting to momentum representation and using the Noyes-Kowalski technique to handle the pole singularity in the Green function. A new analytic transformation formula is developed to calculate the quark-exchange Born kernel for momentum-dependent two-body interactions, with partial-wave decomposition carried out numerically using Gauss-Legendre quadrature. The formalism is applied to the authors' SU6 quark model (versions FSS and RGM-H) which incorporates effective meson-exchange potentials at the quark level for nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- Phase-shift parameters are calculated for all partial waves up to energies of several GeV
- Single-nucleon potentials in normal nuclear matter are calculated for high incident momenta q1 >= 3 fm^-1 (T_lab ~ 200 MeV)
- The t^eff-rho prescription is found to be a good approximation to single-particle potentials directly calculated in lowest-order Brueckner theory for q1 >= 3 fm^-1
- The model FSS shows too attractive single-particle potentials in the momentum region q1 = 5-20 fm^-1
- NN scattering observables are discussed for intermediate energies between T_lab = 400-800 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RGM formulations (RGM-F, FSS, and RGM-H) developed in Fujiwara et al. (1995, 1996) and Fujita et al. (1998) for simultaneous description of NN and YN interactions in the spin-flavor SU6 quark model. The new LS-RGM formalism overcomes limitations of the improved variational method by Kamimura (1977), which becomes inaccessible at higher energies due to rapid oscillation of relative wave functions. The technique enables straightforward extension to G-matrix calculations as in Kohno et al. (1999), and allows analysis of single-particle potentials in nuclear matter using the t^eff-rho prescription.
