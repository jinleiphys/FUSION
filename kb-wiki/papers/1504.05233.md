# Ab Initio Approach to the Non-Perturbative Scalar Yukawa Model

> We report on the first non-perturbative calculation of the scalar Yukawa model in the single-nucleon sector up to four-body Fock sector truncation (one "scalar nucleon" and three "scalar pions"). The light-front Hamiltonian approach with a systematic non-perturbative renormalization is applied. We study the $n$-body norms and the electromagnetic form factor. We find that the one- and two-body contributions dominate up to coupling $α\approx 1.7$. As we approach the coupling $α\approx 2.2$, we discover that the four-body contribution rises rapidly and overtakes the two- and three-body contributions. By comparing with lower sector truncations, we show that the form factor converges with respect to the Fock sector expansion.

## Key claim

The first non-perturbative calculation of the scalar Yukawa model in the single-nucleon sector with up to four-body Fock sector truncation (one scalar nucleon and three scalar pions) demonstrates that the Fock space expansion of the electromagnetic form factor converges as the number of scalar pions increases, but only up to a critical coupling of approximately α ≈ 2.2, beyond which the iterative procedure diverges due to singularities propagating from lower truncations.

## Method

The light-front Hamiltonian approach with Fock sector dependent renormalization (FSDR) was applied to the scalar Yukawa model in the single-nucleon sector, truncating the Fock space to include up to one scalar nucleon and three scalar pions (four-body truncation). A system of coupled linear integral equations for the two-, three-, and four-body vertex functions was derived and solved numerically using an iterative procedure with Gauss-Legendre quadrature on discretized momentum grids, with Pauli-Villars regularization for ultraviolet divergences. The calculations were performed at nucleon mass m = 0.94 GeV and pion mass μ₀ = 0.14 GeV, using a Pauli-Villars mass μ₁ = 15 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The one- and two-body Fock sector contributions dominate the Fock space up to coupling α ≈ 1.7, where a natural hierarchy I₁ > I₂ > I₃ > I₄ is observed.
- At coupling α ≈ 2.1, the four-body contribution I₄ exceeds both I₂ and I₃, with I₄ rising rapidly and I₂ beginning to decrease.
- The lowest two Fock sectors (|N⟩ + |Nπ⟩) constitute 80% of the full norm up to α ≈ 2.0.
- Two critical couplings exist: α_c ≈ 2.6 (where the two-body bare coupling diverges, analogous to a Landau pole) and α'_c ≈ 2.2 (where the three-body Hamiltonian determinant crosses zero).
- The one-body norm I₁ changes little between three-body and four-body truncations, even around α ≈ 1.7.
- The electromagnetic form factor F(Q²) satisfies F(0) = 1 (charge conservation) and F(∞) = I₁ (point-like charge limit).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the scalar Yukawa model using the same approach up to three-body truncation (one scalar nucleon, two scalar pions) by Karmanov (2008), which showed agreement at small couplings but deviation in the large coupling region. The four-body truncation results demonstrate convergence of the Fock sector expansion for the form factor, enabling systematic non-perturbative studies of the two-nucleon sector as a bound-state problem. The approach has also been applied to more realistic theories including the Yukawa model (Karmanov, 2012) and QED (Hiller, 1998), and provides an alternative to lattice gauge theory for hadron structure calculations.
