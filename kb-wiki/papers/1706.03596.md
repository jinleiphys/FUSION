# Stellar equilibrium configurations of white dwarfs in the $f(R,T)$ gravity

> In this work we investigate the equilibrium configurations of white dwarfs in a modified gravity theory, na\-mely, $f(R,T)$ gravity, for which $R$ and $T$ stand for the Ricci scalar and trace of the energy-momentum tensor, respectively. Considering the functional form $f(R,T)=R+2λT$, with $λ$ being a constant, we obtain the hydrostatic equilibrium equation for the theory. Some physical properties of white dwarfs, such as: mass, radius, pressure and energy density, as well as their dependence on the parameter $λ$ are derived. More massive and larger white dwarfs are found for negative values of $λ$ when it decreases. The equilibrium configurations predict a maximum mass limit for white dwarfs slightly above the Chandrasekhar limit, with larger radii and lower central densities when compared to standard gravity outcomes. The most important effect of $f(R,T)$ theory for massive white dwarfs is the increase of the radius in comparison with GR and also $f(R)$ results. By comparing our results with some observational data of massive white dwarfs we also find a lower limit for $λ$, namely, $λ>- 3\times 10^{-4}$.

## Key claim

White dwarfs in $f(R,T)$ gravity with $f(R,T)=R+2\lambda T$ can have maximum masses slightly above the Chandrasekhar limit (up to $1.467 M_\odot$) with substantially larger radii and lower central densities than in general relativity, but only for negative values of the coupling constant $\lambda$, and only for $\lambda > -4\times 10^{-4}$ to maintain stability.

## Method

The authors solved the hydrostatic equilibrium equations (a modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation) for the $f(R,T)=R+2\lambda T$ gravity theory, using a perfect fluid energy-momentum tensor and the Chandrasekhar equation of state for a relativistic degenerate electron gas with $\mu_e=2$ (valid for helium, carbon, and oxygen white dwarfs). The coupled differential equations for mass and pressure were integrated numerically using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method for various central densities and values of $\lambda$, with boundary conditions $m(0)=0$ and $p(R)=0$.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass in GR ($\lambda=0$): $1.416 M_\odot$ at radius $1021$ km and central density $2.307\times 10^{10}$ g/cm$^3$
- Maximum mass for $\lambda=-4.0\times 10^{-4}$: $1.467 M_\odot$ at radius $2970$ km and central density $3.366\times 10^{9}$ g/cm$^3$
- Maximum mass for $\lambda=-3.0\times 10^{-4}$: $1.450 M_\odot$ at radius $2168$ km and central density $5.345\times 10^{9}$ g/cm$^3$
- Maximum mass for $\lambda=-2.0\times 10^{-4}$: $1.437 M_\odot$ at radius $1590$ km and central density $9.567\times 10^{9}$ g/cm$^3$
- Maximum mass for $\lambda=-1.0\times 10^{-4}$: $1.425 M_\odot$ at radius $1247$ km and central density $1.558\times 10^{10}$ g/cm$^3$
- Maximum mass for $\lambda=-0.5\times 10^{-4}$: $1.420 M_\odot$ at radius $1146$ km and central density $1.803\times 10^{10}$ g/cm$^3$
- Lower limit on $\lambda$ from observational data of massive white dwarfs: $\lambda > -3\times 10^{-4}$
- Minimum value of $\lambda$ for stable equilibrium configurations: $\lambda \sim -4\times 10^{-4}$
- Central density range explored: $1.3\times 10^{8}$ to $4.2\times 10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$ (neutron drip limit)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of white dwarfs in modified gravity, particularly the $f(R)$ gravity analysis by Das and Mukhopadhyay (2015) which used $f(R)=R+\alpha R^2$ and found super- and sub-Chandrasekhar masses. The present $f(R,T)$ results show that for massive white dwarfs, the most important effect is the increase of radius compared to both GR and $f(R)$ predictions, with central densities ($\sim 10^9-10^{10}$ g/cm$^3$) that respect constraints from Hamada and Salpeter (1961) and more recent work, unlike the higher central densities ($\sim 10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$) found in $f(R)$ gravity. The analysis enables future work on quadratic $T$ terms ($f(R,T)=R+2\lambda T+\xi T^2$) to potentially explain super-Chandrasekhar white dwarfs, and extensions to nonminimal torsion-matter coupling models.
