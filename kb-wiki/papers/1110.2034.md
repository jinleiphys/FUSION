# Ensemble in-equivalence in supernova matter within a simple model

> A simple, exactly solvable statistical model is presented for the description of baryonic matter in the thermodynamic conditions associated to the evolution of core-collapsing supernova. It is shown that the model presents a first order phase transition in the grandcanonical ensemble which is not observed in the canonical ensemble. Similar to other model systems studied in condensed matter physics, this ensemble in-equivalence is accompanied by negative susceptibility and discontinuities in the intensive observables conjugated to the order parameter. This peculiar behavior originates from the fact that baryonic matter is subject to attractive short range strong forces as well as repulsive long range electromagnetic interactions, partially screened by a background of electrons. As such, it is expected in any theoretical treatment of nuclear matter in the stellar environment. Consequences for the phenomenology of supernova dynamics are drawn.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that supernova matter exhibits ensemble in-equivalence, a first-order phase transition appears in the grandcanonical ensemble but is absent in the canonical ensemble, due to the combination of attractive short-range nuclear forces and repulsive long-range Coulomb interactions partially screened by electrons, a feature expected in any theoretical treatment of nuclear matter in stellar environments.

## Method

The authors construct an exactly solvable statistical model combining a nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) description for clustered nucleons with a self-consistent Hartree-Fock treatment for unbound nucleons using the SKM* Skyrme interaction, incorporating excluded-volume effects and Wigner-Seitz Coulomb corrections. They compute thermodynamic quantities in both the grandcanonical ensemble (with chemical potential fixed) and a hybrid canonical ensemble (with baryon density fixed), using a recursion relation for the canonical partition sum of composite clusters and comparing the resulting equations of state.

## Key numbers

- Temperature considered: T ≈ 10^10 K (≈ 0.86 MeV) and T = 1.6 MeV for numerical applications
- Density range explored: 10^10 < ρ < 10^14 g cm^-3 (sub-saturation densities)
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm^-3 ≈ 1.6 × 10^14 g cm^-3
- Typical nuclear binding energy: E_b ≈ 8 MeV/nucleon ≈ 10^11 K
- Isovector chemical potential used in representative calculations: μ_I = 1.68 MeV
- Canonical calculations require total system size approximately ten times larger than the most probable cluster size for convergence; characteristic cluster radii at the in-equivalence region are only 5–10 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid model for supernova matter (Gulminelli and Raduta, 2011) which was solved numerically via Monte-Carlo simulations, now providing an analytic version. It contradicts the standard assumption of ensemble equivalence in stellar matter and shows that the quenching of the first-order phase transition due to Coulomb frustration (as argued by Ising-star model studies) leads to qualitatively different thermodynamics depending on the chosen ensemble. The results enable improved equations of state for supernova simulations, demonstrating that the common use of Maxwell constructions in the (T, ρ, y_p) ensemble is incorrect, and that clusterized matter persists continuously across subsaturation densities rather than exhibiting phase coexistence.
