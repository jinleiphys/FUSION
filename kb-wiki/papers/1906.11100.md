# Equation of State of Hot Dense Hyperonic Matter in the Quark-Meson-Coupling (QMC-A) model

> We report a new equation of state (EoS) of cold and hot hyperonic matter constructed in the framework of the quark-meson-coupling (QMC-A) model. The QMC-A EoS yields results compatible with available nuclear physics constraints and astrophysical observations. It covers the range of temperatures from T=0 to 100 MeV, entropies per particle S/A between 0 and 6, lepton fractions from Y$_L$=0.0 to 0.6, and baryon number densities n$_B$=0.05-1.2 fm$^{-3}$. Applications of the QMC-A EoS are made to cold neutron stars (NS) and to hot proto-neutron stars (PNS) in two scenarios, (i) lepton rich matter with trapped neutrinos and (ii) deleptonized chemically equilibrated matter. We find that the QMC-A model predicts hyperons in amounts growing with increasing temperature and density, thus suggesting not only their presence in PNS but also, most likely, in NS merger remnants. The nucleon-hyperon phase transition is studied through the adiabatic index and the speed of sound c$_s$. It is shown that the lowering of (c$_s$/c)$^2$ to and below the conformal limit of 1/3 is a general consequence of instabilities due to any phase transition and is not a unique fingerprint of the hadron-quark matter transition. Rigid rotation of cold and hot stars, their moments of inertia and Kepler frequencies are also explored. The QMC-A model results are compared with two relativistic models, the chiral mean field model (CMF), and the generalized relativistic density functional with hyperons (GRDF-Y). Similarities and differences are discussed.

## Key claim

The QMC-A model predicts hyperons in amounts that grow with increasing temperature and density, suggesting their presence not only in proto-neutron stars but also most likely in neutron star merger remnants; the lowering of the squared speed of sound to and below the conformal limit of 1/3 is shown to be a general consequence of instabilities due to any phase transition and not a unique fingerprint of the hadron-quark matter transition.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state (EoS) for hot dense hyperonic matter using the quark-meson-coupling (QMC-A) model, which treats interactions self-consistently between valence quarks confined in non-overlapping baryons rather than between point-like particles. The model was extended to finite temperature and applied to two proto-neutron star scenarios: (i) lepton-rich matter with trapped neutrinos at entropy per particle S/A=1 and lepton fraction Y_L=0.4, and (ii) deleptonized chemically equilibrated matter at S/A=2. The QMC-A results were compared with the chiral mean field (CMF) model and the generalized relativistic density functional with DD2Y-T interaction.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range covered: T=0 to 100 MeV
- Baryon number density range: n_B=0.05-1.2 fm^{-3}
- Entropy per particle range: S/A between 0 and 6
- Lepton fraction range: Y_L=0.0 to 0.6
- Saturation density n_0=0.156 fm^{-3} for QMC-A
- Saturation energy E_sat/A=-16.2 MeV for QMC-A
- Symmetry energy J=28.5 MeV for QMC-A
- Slope of symmetry energy L=54 MeV for QMC-A
- Incompressibility K=292 MeV for QMC-A
- Lambda hyperon threshold densities: 0.5-0.6 fm^{-3} (QMC-A), 0.4-0.5 fm^{-3} (CMF), 0.3-0.4 fm^{-3} (DD2Y-T)
- Pressure at twice nuclear saturation density for nucleonic matter: 32.99 MeV/fm^3 (QMC-A), 32.93 MeV/fm^3 (CMF), 32.14 MeV/fm^3 (DD2Y-T), all within the GW170817 constraint of 11.23-38.7 MeV/fm^3
- Lambda single-particle potential at saturation: U_NLambda=-28 MeV (all three models)

## Context

This work extends the QMC model previously applied to cold neutron stars by Rikovska-Stone (2006) and Guichon (2018) to finite temperature, covering conditions relevant to core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers. It provides a microscopic EoS that contrasts with phenomenological approaches such as the CMF model of Dexheimer (2008) and the DD2Y-T model of Typel (2020), where hyperon couplings are fitted or determined by symmetries rather than emerging from quark structure. The QMC-A EoS enables simulations of proto-neutron star evolution and binary neutron star merger dynamics with a self-consistent treatment of hyperons across a wide range of temperatures, entropies, and lepton fractions.
