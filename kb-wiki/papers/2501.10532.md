# Bayesian Inferring Nucleon's Gravitation Form Factors via Near-threshold $J/ψ$ Photoproduction

> With Bayesian inference, we investigate the impact of recent near-threshold $J/ψ$ production measurements by the $J/ψ$ 007 experiment and GlueX collaboration on the extraction of proton's gravitational form factors. We apply the generalized parton distribution framework at the next-to-leading order and demonstrate a stable expansion for the near-threshold kinematics. We find that the experimental constraints are in good agreement with the state-of-the-art lattice simulations, where negative $C_q(t)$ and $C_g(t)$ are strongly preferred. This highlights a great potential to extract them from future high-precision experiments.

# Bayesian Inferring Nucleon's Gravitation Form Factors via Near-threshold $J/\psi$ Photoproduction

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections in the generalized parton distribution (GPD) framework, combined with Bayesian inference, allow simultaneous constraints on both quark and gluon gravitational form factors (GFFs) from near-threshold $J/\psi$ photoproduction data, but only after fixing the momentum fractions $A_{q,g}(0)$ from global PDFs due to limited large-$\xi$ experimental statistics.

## Method

The authors apply the GPD framework at NLO in QCD, using conformal expansion of Compton-like form factors (CFFs) with leading-moment approximation for large skewness $\xi>0.5$. They perform Bayesian inference via Monte-Carlo Markov Chain (MCMC) to fit the GFFs parameterized as dipole (for $A_{q,g}$) and tripole (for $C_{q,g}$) form factors, using experimental data from the $J/\psi$ 007 experiment and GlueX collaboration combined with lattice QCD results from Hackett (2023) and CTEQ global PDF constraints.

## Key numbers

- The NLO Wilson coefficient for gluons is $\bar{c}_g^1 \approx -0.369$ and for quarks $\bar{c}_q^1 \approx -0.891$, with the LO gluon coefficient $\bar{C}_g^{(1)} = 5/4$
- The theoretical uncertainty from scale variation ($\mu=1$ GeV to $\mu=2m_c$) is reduced from 50% at LO to 10% at NLO for the gluon channel
- The truncation error from large-$\xi$ expansion is estimated at 30% for $\xi\gtrsim 0.5$
- The extracted proton scalar radius from combined experimental and lattice data is $0.95(09)$ fm
- The gluon GFF $C_g(0)$ from combined lattice+experiment fit is $-0.51$, compared to $-0.12$ from experiment-only fit with fixed $A_g(0)=0.40$

## Context

This work extends the leading-order GPD framework for near-threshold heavy quarkonium photoproduction developed by Guo (2021, 2023) and Hatta (2018, 2019) to NLO, enabling access to quark GFFs that are absent at LO. The results are consistent with lattice QCD simulations by Hackett (2023) and Shanahan (2018), and complement DVCS analyses by Burkert (2018, 2023) that constrain quark $C_q(t)$. The approach enables future combined analyses of $J/\psi$ photoproduction and DVCS data to determine the proton's mechanical properties, particularly the tensor monopole moments identified by Ji (2021, 2022).
