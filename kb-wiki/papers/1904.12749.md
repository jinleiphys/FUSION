# Conformal perturbation theory confronts lattice results in the vicinity of a critical point

> We study the accuracy and predictive power of conformal perturbation theory by a comparison with lattice results in the neighborhood of the finite-temperature deconfinement transition of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory, assuming that the infrared properties of this non-Abelian gauge theory near criticality can be described by the Ising model. The results of this comparison show that conformal perturbation theory yields quantitatively accurate predictions in a broad temperature range. We discuss the implications of these findings for the description of the critical point (belonging to the same universality class) of another strongly coupled, non-supersymmetric non-Abelian gauge theory: the critical end-point in the phase diagram of QCD at finite temperature and finite quark chemical potential.

## Key claim

Conformal perturbation theory yields quantitatively accurate predictions for the two-point correlation function of Polyakov loops in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory across a broad temperature range near the deconfinement transition, under the assumption that the infrared properties of this gauge theory near criticality can be described by the three-dimensional Ising model.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo lattice simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on hypercubic lattices with varying temporal extents (Nt = 8, 10, 12) and spatial volumes up to 96^3, measuring the non-connected two-point correlation function of Polyakov loops at temperatures between 0.90 Tc and 1.10 Tc. They compared these lattice results to conformal perturbation theory predictions for the Ising model universality class, fitting the correlator to the operator-product expansion form G(R) = (C_P^2 / R^{2Δ_σ}) (1 + c_1 R^{Δ_ε} + c_2 R^{Δ_t}) with fixed critical exponents Δ_σ = 0.5181489(10) and Δ_ε = 1.412625(10) from conformal bootstrap calculations.

## Key numbers

* The critical exponents used are Δ_σ = 0.5181489(10) and Δ_ε = 1.412625(10) for the three-dimensional Ising model, taken from Komargodski (2016) and Kos (2016).
* The ratio of the deconfinement temperature to the square root of the zero-temperature string tension is Tc/√σ = 0.7091(36), from Lucini (2003).
* The universal ratio of correlation length amplitudes for the Ising universality class is ξ_0+/ξ_0- = 1.95(2), from Caselle (1997).
* The coefficient k_σσ𝟙 appearing in the conformal perturbation theory expansion is approximately -62.5336.
* The normalization constant C_P^2 for the critical correlator on the Nt=10 lattice is 0.00547(2), obtained from fits with Rmax between 12a and 20a.
* Fitted parameters c_1 and c_2 range from -0.169(1) and 0.099(1) at T/Tc = 0.90 to 0.221(3) and -0.081(3) at T/Tc = 1.10 on the Nt=10 lattice.

## Context

This work extends the conformal perturbation theory framework developed by Zamolodchikov (1987) and Guida (1995, 1996) from two-dimensional models to three-dimensional systems, building on recent conformal bootstrap results for the Ising model from Kos (2016) and Komargodski (2016). The comparison with SU(2) Yang-Mills lattice data serves as a test case for applying the same methodology to the QCD critical end-point at finite temperature and chemical potential, which belongs to the same Ising universality class but cannot be studied directly via lattice methods due to the sign problem. The results enable future theoretical predictions for the behavior of strongly coupled QCD matter along trajectories passing near the critical end-point in heavy-ion collision experiments.
