# Global analysis of nuclear PDFs - latest developments

> In this review talk I discuss the latest developments in the DGLAP-based global analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), focusing on the recent EPS09, nCTEQ and DSSZ global fits. I also briefly review the recent analysis for assigning a spatial dependence to the globally analysed nPDFs, resulting in the new sets EPS09s and EKS98s ("s" for spatial). With these, one can now compute nuclear hard-process cross sections and estimate their nPDF-originating uncertainties in different centrality classes for the first time consistently with the global nPDF fits.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the latest global DGLAP analyses of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), specifically EPS09, nCTEQ, and DSSZ, suggest that factorization works and nPDFs appear universal in the region \(x \gtrsim 0.001\) and \(Q^2 \gtrsim 1\) GeV\(^2\), but this conclusion is hedged by unresolved tensions between charged-lepton and neutrino deep inelastic scattering data, and by the fact that the role of nuclear fragmentation functions in interpreting pion production data remains to be clarified.

## Method

The author reviews three recent NLO global DGLAP analyses of nPDFs: EPS09, which parametrizes nuclear modifications \(R_i^A(x,Q^2)\) relative to CTEQ6.1M free-proton PDFs and fits to 929 datapoints from charged-lepton DIS, Drell-Yan, and inclusive \(\pi^0\) production in d+Au; nCTEQ, which parametrizes bound-proton PDFs directly and includes neutrino-DIS data (3134 datapoints) alongside charged-lepton DIS and Drell-Yan; and DSSZ, which parametrizes nuclear modifications relative to MSTW free-proton PDFs and fits to 1579 datapoints including charged-lepton DIS, Drell-Yan, neutrino-DIS, and pion production in d+Au computed with nuclear fragmentation functions. The review also covers the EPS09s and EKS98s sets, which assign a spatial (impact-parameter) dependence to globally analysed nPDFs by fitting powers of the nuclear thickness function \(T_A(\mathbf{s})\) to reproduce the \(A\)-dependence of the average modifications.

## Key numbers

* EPS09 best fit: \(\chi^2/\text{datapoints} = 0.79\) for 929 datapoints from charged-lepton DIS, Drell-Yan, and \(\pi^0\) production in d+Au.
* EPS09 error sets: 15+15 sets determined with a “90% confidence criterion” equivalent to \(\Delta\chi^2 = 50\) (ca. 5%).
* nCTEQ global fit: total \(\chi^2/\text{datapoint}\) worsens from 0.90 (no neutrino-DIS data) to 1.30 (full weight to neutrino-DIS data), indicating tension between charged-lepton DIS/DY and neutrino-DIS data.
* DSSZ best fit: \(\chi^2/\text{datapoint} = 0.98\) for 1579 datapoints, with 25+25 error sets corresponding to \(\Delta\chi^2 = 30\) (2% increase).
* EPS09 initial scale: \(Q_0 = 1.3\) GeV; DSSZ initial scale: \(Q_0 = 1.0\) GeV.
* EPS09s/EKS98s spatial fits: the \(A\)-independent coefficients \(c^i_j(x,Q^2)\) in the expansion \(r_i^A(x,Q^2,\mathbf{s}) = 1 + \sum_{j=1}^{n} c^i_j(x,Q^2)[T_A(\mathbf{s})]^j\) reproduce the \(A\)-systematics with \(n=4\).

## Context

This review extends the line of global DGLAP analyses of nPDFs that began with EKS98 (1998) and continued through HKM, HKN04, nDS, EKPS, HKN07, and EPS08. It directly compares the three most recent NLO analyses, EPS09 (Eskola 2009), nCTEQ (Kovarik 2010), and DSSZ (deFlorian 2011), highlighting that EPS09 and DSSZ find universal nPDFs while nCTEQ reports tension between charged-lepton and neutrino DIS data, a conclusion that depends on the treatment of systematic errors (Paukkunen 2010). The review also presents the first spatially dependent nPDF sets (EPS09s and EKS98s) derived from globally analysed nPDFs, enabling centrality-dependent calculations for the first time. Future work should include combined global fits of nPDFs and nuclear fragmentation functions, and data from RHIC d+Au, LHC p+Pb, and future electron-ion colliders to constrain the gluon sector.
