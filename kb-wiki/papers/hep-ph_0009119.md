# Cosmic Ray Air Shower Characteristics in the Framework of the Parton-Based Gribov-Regge Model NEXUS

> The purpose of this paper is twofold: first we want to introduce a new type of hadronic interaction model (NEXUS), which has a much more solid theoretical basis as, for example, presently used models like QGSJET and VENUS, and ensures therefore a much more reliable extrapolation towards high energies. Secondly, we want to promote an extensive air shower (EAS) calculation scheme, based on cascade equations rather than explicit Monte Carlo simulations, which is very accurate in calculations of main EAS characteristics and extremely fast concerning computing time. We employ the NEXUS model to provide the necessary data on particle production in hadron-air collisions and present the average EAS characteristics for energies 10^14 - 10^17 eV. The experimental data of the casa-blanka group are analyzed in the framework of the new model.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the NEXUS hadronic interaction model, which claims a "much more solid theoretical basis" than existing models like QGSJET and VENUS, but the actual hedge is that this superiority concerns theoretical consistency and the model allows "much safer extrapolation to superhigh energies" rather than proven accuracy at those energies; the model's predictions for cosmic ray composition are validated only indirectly through comparison with air shower data, not through direct collider measurements at the highest energies.

## Method

The authors developed the NEXUS model based on a "Parton-based Gribov-Regge Theory" that treats cross sections and particle production consistently with energy conservation in both cases, incorporating hard processes without dependence on artificial cutoff parameters. They then used NEXUS to generate inclusive particle production spectra for hadron-nitrogen collisions at reference energies from 10^11 to 10^17 eV, fitted these Monte Carlo results with analytic functions using the Levenberg-Marquardt method, and solved a system of hadronic cascade equations (rather than performing full Monte Carlo simulations) to compute average extensive air shower characteristics. The cascade equations were solved for nucleons, charged pions, charged kaons, and neutral kaons, with electron and muon numbers computed as functionals of the hadron spectra using Greisen's formula and decay kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Primary energy range covered: 10^14 to 10^17 eV
- Reference energies for NEXUS simulations: 10^11 eV up to 10^17 eV, with two points per order of magnitude (e.g., 10^11, 10^11.5, 10^12 eV, etc.)
- Decay constants used: B_pi = 114 GeV for charged pions, B_K = 852 GeV for charged kaons, B_K0_L = 205 GeV for neutral kaons, B_K0_S = 1.19 x 10^5 GeV (all at height 11 km)
- Energy discretization: E_min ~ 1 GeV, with c = 10^0.1 and i_max = 81 (10 points per order of magnitude)
- Step size for cascade integration: Delta X ~ 5 g/cm^2, achieving ~1% accuracy
- Critical energy for electrons in air: beta = 81.1 x 10^6 eV
- Radiation unit: T_0 = 37.1 g/cm^2
- Statistics used for each reference energy in NEXUS: 10^5 events, yielding ~0.1-0.2% accuracy for inclusive spectra
- Shower maximum for incident nucleons and iron shown as function of energy; mean logarithmic mass shows minimum around 10^15.5 eV

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Regge theory approach previously used in models like VENUS (Werner, 1993) and QGSJET (Kalmykov et al., 1997) by providing a consistent treatment of energy conservation in both cross section and particle production calculations, which those earlier models lacked. The cascade equation method contrasts with explicit Monte Carlo simulations like CORSIKA (Heck et al., 1998), which the authors note produce varying results (e.g., average electron number at 10^15 eV ranging from 1.11 x 10^5 to 1.62 x 10^5 depending on the hadronic model). The analysis of CASA-BLANCA data confirms earlier findings by the Moscow State University group (Khristiansen et al., 1994; Fomin et al., 1993) of a change to heavier primary composition above the knee, and the novel "lightening" of composition just before the knee discovered by the CASA-BLANCA collaboration. The approach enables faster calculation of average EAS characteristics and is intended for future application to ultra-high energy cosmic rays (above 10^20 eV) for experiments like the Pierre Auger Observatory.
