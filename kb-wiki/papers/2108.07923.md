# Strangeness in astrophysics: Theoretical developments

> In this conference proceeding, we review important theoretical developments related to the production of strangeness in astrophysics. This includes its effects in supernova explosions, neutron stars, and compact-star mergers. We also discuss in detail how the presence of net strangeness affects the deconfinement to quark matter, expected to take place at large densities and/or temperatures. We conclude that a complete description of dense matter containing hyperons and strange quarks is fundamental for the understanding of modern high-energy astrophysics.

# Strangeness in astrophysics: Theoretical developments

## Key claim
Strangeness is not ruled out in dense matter and is expected to appear in significant quantities across multiple astrophysical environments, but the softening of the equation of state caused by strange degrees of freedom can be compensated by tuning interactions and allowing crossover transitions to quark matter, enabling consistency with 2 solar mass neutron stars and other observational constraints.

## Method
The authors review theoretical models of dense matter containing hyperons and strange quarks, including the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, parametric equations of state with non-monotonic speed of sound, and the QMC-A and QHC19 models. They examine how net strangeness affects deconfinement to quark matter by calculating phase diagrams as functions of temperature, baryon chemical potential, charge fraction, and isospin fraction, and compare model predictions to observational data from NICER, LIGO/VIRGO, and the GW190814 merger event.

## Key numbers
- Glendenning's early model reached neutron star masses up to 1.8 solar masses with hyperon populations occupying most of the star's radius.
- Witten's strange quark star model could reproduce 2 solar mass stars under an adequate choice of bag constant.
- Kaon condensation models in the 1990s found it hard to support neutron stars larger than 1.44 solar masses in the presence of large strangeness content.
- Including hyperons and their negative parity states with a crossover transition to quark matter can still be consistent with 2 solar mass neutron stars.
- Parametric equations of state with early bumps in speed of sound (before 2 times saturation density) can generate massive stars beyond 2.5 solar masses, relevant for the GW190814 secondary object.
- Neutron stars with masses larger than 2.1 solar masses can be reproduced when the isovector coupling is rescaled using hyperon potential measurements from ALICE at CERN.
- Net strangeness affects the position of deconfinement by more than 40 MeV in free energy per baryon, depending on temperature and charge fraction.

## Context
This conference proceeding reviews and extends work beginning with Glendenning (1982) on hyperons in neutron stars, Witten (1984) on strange quark stars, and Thorsson (1994) on kaon condensation, which together defined the "hyperon puzzle" where strange degrees of freedom soften the equation of state and reduce maximum neutron star masses. The authors discuss how later work by Dexheimer (2018, 2020) and Mukherjee (2017) showed that tuning interactions and allowing crossover transitions can resolve this tension. The review enables future connections between multi-messenger observations (neutrinos, gravitational waves, electromagnetic signals) and the role of strangeness in supernovae, neutron stars, and compact-star mergers, while noting that deconfinement signals from neutron-star mergers could provide additional information about strangeness content inside compact stars.
