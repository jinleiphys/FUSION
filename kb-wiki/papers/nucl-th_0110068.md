# Vlasov calculations of nuclear ground states

> We describe some properties of nuclear ground states using a microscopic Vlasov approach. We propose a new method to find the energy minimum of the system enforcing the fermionic nature of the system. Calculations are performed for two different parametrization of the mean field: a simple momentum independent Skyrme potential and a momentum dependent one as proposed by Gale, Bertsch and Das Gupta. We show that the binding energies and radii of nuclei from $^{16}$O to $^{208}$Pb are reasonably reproduced by opportunely tuning the surface term. We also made some calculations for neutron rich/poor nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that nuclear ground states can be reasonably reproduced using a semiclassical Vlasov approach with a frictional minimization method, provided the surface term in the mean field is opportunely tuned to match the binding energy of a reference nucleus (here \(^{16}\)O). The method does not claim to match Hartree-Fock precision but offers a simpler numerical path that can be extended to collision problems.

## Method

The Vlasov equation is solved numerically using the test particle method, with over 400,000 test particles per nucleus. A frictional equation of motion is introduced with a constraint on the phase-space occupation factor (enforcing the Pauli principle) to drive the system to its energy minimum. Calculations are performed for two mean-field parametrizations: a local Skyrme potential and a momentum-dependent interaction from Gale, Bertsch, and Das Gupta. The surface term coefficient \(c_s\) is adjusted to reproduce the binding energy of \(^{16}\)O, compensating for a spuriously introduced surface term from the numerical grid.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies and radii are calculated for nuclei from \(^{16}\)O to \(^{208}\)Pb using both a local Skyrme potential and a momentum-dependent interaction.
- The surface term coefficient is set to \(c_s = 6.9\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm}^5\) for the local Skyrme potential, fitted to the binding energy of \(^{16}\)O.
- For the momentum-dependent interaction, the surface term coefficient is half of the canonical value (canonical \(c_s = -138\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm}^5\)).
- The density grid uses a spacing of 1 fm side.
- The average occupation factor in phase space stabilizes around 1 after about 200 fm/\(c\) for \(^{16}\)O.
- The compressibility of the Skyrme parametrization is set to 225 MeV.
- The symmetry energy coefficient is \(c_a = 32\) MeV, with \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the Vlasov approach, previously applied to heavy-ion collisions and metallic cluster collisions (Bonasera 1999), to the calculation of nuclear ground states, which had been a known shortcoming of the method. It provides a semiclassical alternative to Hartree-Fock (Schuck) that is numerically simpler and can be extended to include a collision term for low-energy phenomena such as fusion, deep inelastic reactions, and collective flow. The method also enables calculations for exotic neutron-rich or neutron-poor nuclei, such as \(^{19}\)C, where a neutron halo is observed.
