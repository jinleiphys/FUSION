# Second-order Charge Currents and Stress Tensor in Chiral System

> We solve the Wigner equation for massless spin-1/2 charged fermions near global equilibrium. The Wigner function can be obtained order by order in the power expansion of the vorticity and electromagnetic field. The Wigner function has been derived up to the second order from which the non-dissipative charge currents and the stress tensor can be obtained. The charge and energy densities and the pressure have contributions from the vorticity and electromagnetic field at the second order. The vector and axial Hall currents can be induced along the direction orthogonal to the vorticity and electromagnetic field at the second order. We also find that the trace anomaly emerges natually in renormalizing the stress tensor by including the quantum correction from the electromagnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper derives second-order non-dissipative charge currents and stress tensor for massless spin-1/2 charged fermions in global equilibrium, but only under the restrictive condition of static and uniform vorticity and electromagnetic fields, with the additional caveat that the fermion mass is neglected (chiral limit) which is valid only when temperature is much greater than the particle's static mass.

## Method

The authors solve the Wigner equation for massless spin-1/2 charged fermions using a covariant perturbation method that expands the Wigner function order by order in powers of the vorticity and electromagnetic field strength, treating both as small expansion parameters of the same order. The zeroth-order solution is taken as the Fermi-Dirac distribution function, and the first and second-order solutions are obtained iteratively from the constraint equations derived from the Wigner equation under global equilibrium conditions. The vector and axial currents and the energy-momentum tensor are then computed by integrating the corresponding components of the Wigner function over four-momentum.

## Key numbers

- The first-order chiral vortical effect (CVE) transport coefficient for the vector current is ξ = μμ₅/π²
- The first-order chiral magnetic effect (CME) transport coefficient for the vector current is ξ_B = μ₅/(2π²)
- The first-order local polarization effect (LPE) transport coefficient for the axial current is ξ₅ = (1/(6π²))[π²T² + 3(μ² + μ₅²)]
- The first-order chiral separate effect (CSE) transport coefficient for the axial current is ξ_{B5} = μ/(2π²)
- The second-order vector Hall current coefficient along the direction orthogonal to both E and ω is 1/(4π²)
- The second-order vector Hall current coefficient along the direction orthogonal to both E and B is C/(6π²), where C ≈ 0.4263 μ/T² when |μₛ| ≪ T
- The second-order axial Hall current coefficient along the direction orthogonal to both E and B is C₅/(6π²), where C₅ ≈ 0.4263 μ₅/T² when |μₛ| ≪ T

## Context

This work extends previous first-order results for chiral transport phenomena including the chiral magnetic effect (CME) from Kharzeev (2007) and Fukushima (2008), the chiral vortical effect (CVE) from Vilenkin (1978) and Erdmenger (2008), and the chiral separate effect (CSE) from Son (2004) and Metlitski (2005). The paper builds on the Wigner function formalism developed by Gao (2012, 2015) that successfully reproduced first-order currents, and addresses the need for second-order terms in relativistic hydrodynamics to resolve causality and stability issues identified by Hiscock (1983, 1985, 1987). The results enable calculation of second-order transport coefficients for chiral systems under strong vorticity and electromagnetic fields, as encountered in non-central heavy ion collisions.
