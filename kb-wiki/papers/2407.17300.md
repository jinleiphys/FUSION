# Fine-structure constant sensitivity of the Th-229 nuclear clock transition

> State-resolved laser spectroscopy at the 10$^{-12}$ precision level recently reported in $arXiv$:2406.18719 determined the fractional change in nuclear quadrupole moment between the ground and isomeric state of $^{229}\rm{Th}$, $ΔQ_0/Q_0$=1.791(2) %. Assuming a prolate spheroid nucleus, this allows to quantify the sensitivity of the nuclear transition frequency to variations of the fine-structure constant $α$ to $K=5900(2300)$, with the uncertainty dominated by the experimentally measured charge radius difference $Δ\langle r^2 \rangle$ between the ground and isomeric state. This result indicates a three orders of magnitude enhancement over atomic clock schemes based on electron shell transitions. We find that $ΔQ_0$ is highly sensitive to tiny changes in the nuclear volume, thus the constant volume approximation cannot be used to accurately relate changes in $\langle r^2 \rangle$ and $Q_0$. The difference between the experimental and estimated values in $ΔQ_0/Q_0$ raises a further question on the octupole contribution to the alpha-sensitivity.

## Key claim
The sensitivity of the Th-229 nuclear clock transition frequency to variations of the fine-structure constant α is quantified as K = 5900(2300), assuming a prolate spheroid nucleus; this result is dominated by the uncertainty in the experimentally measured charge radius difference Δ⟨r²⟩ between the ground and isomeric state, and the constant volume approximation is shown to be unreliable for relating changes in ⟨r²⟩ and Q₀.

## Method
The authors used state-resolved laser spectroscopy at 10⁻¹² precision (from Zhang et al., 2024) to determine the fractional change in nuclear quadrupole moment between the ground and isomeric state of ²²⁹Th, ΔQ₀/Q₀ = 1.791(2)%. They then applied a geometric prolate spheroid nuclear model to relate the measured quadrupole moment ratio and the previously measured mean-square charge radius difference to the Coulomb energy difference ΔE_C between the two states, from which the α-sensitivity factor K = ΔE_C / E was computed.

## Key numbers
- ΔQ₀/Q₀ = 1.791(2)% (fractional change in intrinsic quadrupole moment between ground and isomeric state of ²²⁹Th)
- K = 5900(2300) (sensitivity factor of the nuclear clock transition frequency to fine-structure constant variation)
- Δ⟨r²⟩ = 0.0105(13) fm² (difference in mean-square charge radius between isomer and ground state)
- Q₀ᵐ/Q₀ = 1.01791(2) (ratio of intrinsic quadrupole moments of isomer to ground state)
- ν_Th = 2,020,407,384,335(2) kHz (EFG-free isomer transition frequency)
- ΔE_C = 0.049(19) MeV (Coulomb energy difference between isomer and ground state)
- ζ = 0.035(4) (ratio of isomer shift to isotope shift between ²²⁹Th and ²³²Th)

## Context
This work extends the prediction by Flambaum (2006) of enhanced sensitivity of the ²²⁹Th nuclear clock to fundamental constant variation, and directly uses the precision laser spectroscopy results from Zhang et al. (2024) to extract the α-sensitivity factor for the first time from experimental data. The result contradicts the earlier constant-volume approximation used by Faddeev et al. (2020), which predicted a negative K = -8200(2500); the present work shows that even a -0.055% volume change between states leads to a factor-of-two difference in ΔQ₀/Q₀. The three-orders-of-magnitude enhancement over atomic clock schemes (e.g., Yb E3 transitions with K = -6) enables probing dark matter with three orders of magnitude smaller couplings. Future work requires improved measurement of the isomer-to-isotope shift ratio ζ and determination of the octupole deformation contribution, which can change K by more than 1σ with even 1% variation.
