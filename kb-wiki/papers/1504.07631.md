# Neutrino-driven explosion of a 20 solar-mass star in three dimensions enabled by strange-quark contributions to neutrino-nucleon scattering

> Interactions with neutrons and protons play a crucial role for the neutrino opacity of matter in the supernova core. Their current implementation in many simulation codes, however, is rather schematic and ignores not only modifications for the correlated nuclear medium of the nascent neutron star, but also free-space corrections from nucleon recoil, weak magnetism or strange quarks, which can easily add up to changes of several 10% for neutrino energies in the spectral peak. In the Garching supernova simulations with the Prometheus-Vertex code, such sophistications have been included for a long time except for the strange-quark contributions to the nucleon spin, which affect neutral-current neutrino scattering. We demonstrate on the basis of a 20 M_sun progenitor star that a moderate strangeness-dependent contribution of g_a^s = -0.2 to the axial-vector coupling constant g_a = 1.26 can turn an unsuccessful three-dimensional (3D) model into a successful explosion. Such a modification is in the direction of current experimental results and reduces the neutral-current scattering opacity of neutrons, which dominate in the medium around and above the neutrinosphere. This leads to increased luminosities and mean energies of all neutrino species and strengthens the neutrino-energy deposition in the heating layer. Higher nonradial kinetic energy in the gain layer signals enhanced buoyancy activity that enables the onset of the explosion at ~300 ms after bounce, in contrast to the model with vanishing strangeness contributions to neutrino-nucleon scattering. Our results demonstrate the close proximity to explosion of the previously published, unsuccessful 3D models of the Garching group.

# Neutrino-driven explosion of a 20 solar-mass star in three dimensions enabled by strange-quark contributions to neutrino-nucleon scattering

## Key claim

A moderate strangeness-dependent contribution of g_a^s = -0.2 to the axial-vector coupling constant g_a = 1.26 can turn an unsuccessful three-dimensional (3D) model of a 20 solar-mass progenitor into a successful explosion, demonstrating that previous non-exploding 3D models of the Garching group were close to the explosion threshold. The hedge is that the chosen value of g_a^s = -0.2 is somewhat larger in absolute value than theoretical and experimental determinations of g_a^s ~ -0.1, representing an "extreme" effect.

## Method

The authors performed 2D and full 4pi 3D simulations of a nonrotating, solar-metallicity 20 solar-mass pre-supernova progenitor using the Prometheus-Vertex hydrodynamics code with three-flavor, energy-dependent, ray-by-ray-plus (RbR+) neutrino transport including the full set of neutrino reactions and microphysics. They compared models with strangeness corrections (g_a^s = -0.2, models 2Ds and 3Ds) to corresponding simulations without strange quark effects (g_a^s = 0, models 2Dn and 3Dn), using the high-density equation of state of Lattimer and Swesty (1991) with nuclear incompressibility K = 220 MeV and a radial grid of 400 nonequidistant zones refined up to over 500 zones.

## Key numbers

- Strangeness contribution of g_a^s = -0.2 to the axial-vector coupling constant g_a = 1.26 was used, compared to experimental determinations of g_a^s ~ -0.1
- For a neutron-proton mixture with electron fractions Y_e = 0.2, 0.1, 0.05, the neutral-current opacity is reduced by 15%, 21%, and 23% respectively
- Onset of explosion occurs at approximately 300 ms after bounce in model 3Ds
- At the end of the simulation at 530 ms post-bounce, the proto-neutron star has a baryonic mass of 1.91 solar masses and the shock has expanded to an average radius of approximately 1000 km
- The diagnostic energy E_exp reached 0.2 x 10^51 erg and rises steeply with a rate of dE_exp/dt approximately 1.2 x 10^51 erg/s
- Mean energies of radiated neutrinos in model 3Ds are up to approximately 1 MeV higher than in model 3Dn
- Luminosities of nu_e and anti-nu_e are up to approximately 10-15% higher in model 3Ds
- nu_x luminosities rise by up to approximately 30% in model 3Ds
- The increase of total neutrino luminosity is more than 6 x 10^52 erg/s at maximum
- For Y_e = 0.1-0.05, strangeness effects reduce the effective opacity for nu_e by only 2-3% and for anti-nu_e by 8-10%
- Mass outflow rate in rising plumes after explosion is approximately 0.08-0.1 solar masses per second
- Mass inflow rate in accretion downdrafts reaches up to approximately 0.3 solar masses per second

## Context

This work extends the Garching group's previous 3D simulations of 11.2, 20, and 27 solar-mass progenitors (Hanke 2013, Tamborra 2013, 2014a, 2014b) which did not produce explosions despite corresponding 2D models exploding, consistent with studies by Hanke (2012), Couch (2013, 2014), and Couch and Ott (2015) showing explosion onset is less favored in 3D than 2D. The results demonstrate that remaining uncertainties in neutrino opacities, particularly neutrino-nucleon interactions at subnuclear densities, can change the outcome of 3D core-collapse simulations, and that strangeness corrections are similarly beneficial for shock revival as "softer" nuclear equations of state (Marek 2009, Janka 2012, Suwa 2013). The work enables future investigations of nucleon-strangeness effects in 3D simulations of other progenitors and highlights the need for higher angular resolution to ensure explosion robustness against turbulent energy cascading to small structures.
