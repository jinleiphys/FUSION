# Quark Spectrum above but near Critical Temperature of Chiral Transition

> We explore the quark properties at finite temperature near but above the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition. We investigate the effects of the precursory soft mode of the phase transition on the quark dispersion relation and the spectral function. It is found that there appear novel excitation spectra of quasi-quarks and quasi-antiquarks with a three-peak structure, which are not attributed to the hard-thermal-loop approximation. We show that the new spectra originate from the mixing between a quark (anti-quark) and an anti-quark hole (quark hole) caused by a ``resonant scattering'' of the quasi-fermions with the thermally-excited soft mode which has a small but finite excitation energy.

## Key claim

The quark spectral function near but above the chiral transition critical temperature exhibits a novel three-peak structure at low frequency and low momentum, which arises from the mixing between quarks and anti-quark holes (and vice versa) induced by resonant scattering with propagating soft modes (para-sigma and para-pion) that have small but finite excitation energies; this structure is not captured by the hard-thermal-loop approximation and appears only for reduced temperatures epsilon less than or approximately equal to 0.2.

## Method

The authors employ the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the chiral limit with vanishing chemical potential, using a coupling constant G_S = 5.5 GeV^{-2} and a three-dimensional cutoff Lambda = 631 MeV. They compute the quark self-energy in the random phase approximation (RPA) by dressing free quarks with the collective soft modes (para-sigma and para-pion) constructed from undressed quark-antiquark polarization functions, then obtain the retarded quark Green function and spectral functions rho_+ and rho_- for quasi-quarks and quasi-antiquarks.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of the chiral transition in the NJL model: T_c = 193.5 MeV.
- Reduced temperatures studied: epsilon = (T - T_c)/T_c = 0.1, 0.2, and 0.5.
- The three-peak structure in the quark spectral function is clearly present at epsilon = 0.1, barely exists at epsilon = 0.2, and disappears completely at epsilon = 0.5.
- Coupling constant: G_S = 5.5 GeV^{-2}; cutoff: Lambda = 631 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1985) that hadronic elementary excitations (para-sigma and para-pion modes) exist above T_c in the chiral transition, and applies similar reasoning to the quark sector. It parallels recent studies by Kitazawa et al. (2003, 2004, 2005) on pseudogap formation in the diquark channel at high density, but here the soft modes are propagating rather than diffusive, leading to a qualitatively different three-peak structure rather than a single gap. The results enable future investigations of quark properties at finite chemical potential near the critical end point, and suggest that any bosonic mode in the quark-gluon plasma with mass comparable to T can produce similar spectral features.
