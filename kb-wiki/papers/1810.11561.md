# Mass Measurements of Neutron-Rich Gallium Isotopes Refine Production of Nuclei of the First r-Process Abundance Peak in Neutron Star Merger Calculations

> We report mass measurements of neutron-rich Ga isotopes $^{80-85}$Ga with TRIUMF's Ion Trap for Atomic and Nuclear science (TITAN). The measurements determine the masses of $^{80-83}$Ga in good agreement with previous measurements. The masses of $^{84}$Ga and $^{85}$Ga were measured for the first time. Uncertainties between $25-48$ keV were reached. The new mass values reduce the nuclear uncertainties associated with the production of A $\approx$ 84 isotopes by the \emph{r}-process for astrophysical conditions that might be consistent with a binary neutron star (BNS) merger producing a blue kilonova. Our nucleosynthesis simulations confirm that BNS merger may contribute to the first abundance peak under moderate neutron-rich conditions with electron fractions $Y_e=0.35-0.38$.

## Key claim

The new mass measurements of neutron-rich gallium isotopes 84Ga and 85Ga reduce nuclear uncertainties in r-process nucleosynthesis calculations for binary neutron star mergers, but only under the specific condition that the ejecta have moderate neutron richness with electron fractions Ye = 0.35-0.38, which may be consistent with the blue kilonova component of GW170817.

## Method

The masses of 80-85Ga were measured using TRIUMF's Multiple-Reflection Time-of-Flight Mass Spectrometer (MR-TOF-MS) at the TITAN facility, with a 20 ms cycle time and mass resolving powers of m/Δm ≈ 200,000. Neutron-rich gallium isotopes were produced by a 480 MeV, 10 μA proton beam on a UCx target at the ISAC facility, and the ions were stored for 330-372 turns in the mass analyzer resulting in flight times between 6.00 ms and 6.82 ms. Two independent analyses using Gaussian and Lorentzian line shapes were performed, and a systematic uncertainty of δm/msyst = 3 × 10^-7 was applied.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of 84Ga: -44,094(30) keV/c^2 (first measurement, compared to extrapolated AME2016 value of -44,090(200) keV/c^2)
- Mass excess of 85Ga: -39,744(37) keV/c^2 (first measurement, compared to extrapolated AME2016 value of -39,850(300) keV/c^2)
- Mass excess of 80Ga: -59,212(48) keV/c^2
- Mass excess of 81Ga: -57,616(31) keV/c^2
- Mass excess of 82Ga: -52,974(31) keV/c^2
- Mass excess of 83Ga: -49,258(25) keV/c^2
- Uncertainties on all measurements ranged from 25-48 keV
- Electron fraction range for the nucleosynthesis simulations: Ye = 0.35-0.38

## Context

This work extends previous mass measurements of neutron-rich gallium isotopes performed by JYFLTRAP (Hakala 2008) to the more neutron-rich isotopes 84Ga and 85Ga, which were measured for the first time. The new masses reduce nuclear uncertainties in r-process calculations for binary neutron star mergers, particularly for the production of A ≈ 84 isotopes that contribute to the first r-process abundance peak. The results enable more precise nucleosynthesis simulations for the blue kilonova component observed in GW170817, and the comparison with mass models (FRDM, Duflo-Zucker, ETFSI-Q, HFB-21) shows that FRDM and HFB-21 agree well with the experimental data in this region.
