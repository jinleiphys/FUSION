# Quantum Kinetic Equation in the Rotating Frame and Chiral Kinetic Theory

> A modified quantum kinetic equation which takes account of the noninertial features of rotating frame is proposed. The vector and axial-vector field components of the Wigner function for chiral fluids are worked out in a semiclassical scheme. It is demonstrated that the chiral currents and energy-momentum tensor computed by means of them are consistent with the hydrodynamical results. A new semiclassical covariant chiral transport equation is established by inspecting the equations satisfied by the chiral vector fields. It uniquely provides a new three-dimensional semiclassical chiral kinetic theory possessing a Coriolis force term. The particle number and current densities deduced from this transport equation satisfy the anomalous continuity equation and generate the magnetic and vortical effects correctly.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a modified quantum kinetic equation (QKE) that incorporates noninertial features of a rotating frame by adding a term proportional to the circulation tensor minus the kinematic vorticity tensor, but only for massless chiral fermions; the modification is designed so that the Coriolis force vanishes in the rest frame of massive particles, and the resulting three-dimensional chiral kinetic theory uniquely produces a Coriolis force term.

## Method

The authors start from the standard QKE derived from the Dirac equation with constant electromagnetic fields and modify the covariant derivative by adding a term built from the enthalpy current and fluid four-velocity to account for rotation. They solve the resulting equations for the chiral vector and axial-vector components of the Wigner function in a semiclassical expansion to first order in ℏ, first using a Fermi-Dirac distribution in the fluid comoving frame to verify consistency with hydrodynamical results, then deriving a general covariant chiral transport equation. They integrate this four-dimensional transport equation over p₀ in the fluid rest frame to obtain a unique three-dimensional semiclassical chiral kinetic theory with explicit Coriolis force.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical chiral kinetic theory developed by Gao, Liang, Pu, Wang, and Wang (2012) and the covariant approaches of Chen, Pu, Wang, and Wang (2013) and Hidaka, Pu, and Yang (2017), which lacked a unique three-dimensional limit with Coriolis force. It also builds on the observation by Son and Yamamoto (2012) that vorticity in hydrodynamics matches angular velocity in the comoving frame. The new three-dimensional chiral kinetic theory enables studies of nonlinear transport in chiral plasma and can incorporate collisions using methods from Chen, Sadofyev, and Su (2015) and Hidaka, Pu, and Yang (2017).
