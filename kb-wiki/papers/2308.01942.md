# One-proton emission from 148,149,150,151Lu in the DRHBc plus WKB approach

> One-proton radioactivity in 149Lu, the latest identified proton emitter, is studied in the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approach with the proton-nucleus potential extracted from the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) for the first time. The predicted half-life turns out to be consistent with the experimental measurement within uncertainties and (almost) independent of the density functionals in the DRHBc theory. Such a microscopic self-consistent calculation reveals that 149Lu is oblately deformed with a quadrupole deformation -0.18, and rules out the possibility of a prolate quadrupole deformation suggested in the nonadiabatic quasiparticle model. We also check the validity of this approach in the description of 150,151Lu and their isomeric states. The deviations of the predicted half-lives from their experimental counterparts are mostly smaller than those of the theoretical studies without considering deformation effects. Furthermore, we predict 148Lu to be a more oblately deformed proton-emitter with a longer half-life than that of 149Lu, which can be checked in the future. Our studies show that the DRHBc plus WKB approach provides a new alternative method to evaluate the half-lives of well-deformed proton emitters.

## Key claim

The DRHBc plus WKB approach predicts a half-life for the newly identified proton emitter 149Lu that is consistent with the experimental measurement within 1σ uncertainty, and reveals that 149Lu is oblately deformed with a quadrupole deformation β₂ ≈ -0.18, ruling out the prolate deformation (β₂ ≈ 0.15) suggested by the nonadiabatic quasiparticle model.

## Method

The authors combined the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) to self-consistently generate the proton-nucleus potential, spectroscopic factors, and deformation parameters, with the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximation to compute the proton emission half-life. Three relativistic density functionals (PC-PK1, NL3*, TMA) were used to check the robustness of the results. The half-life was calculated by averaging the decay width over all orientation angles of the emitted proton with respect to the symmetry axis of the daughter nucleus, using the experimental decay energy Q as input.

## Key numbers

- 149Lu half-life predicted with PC-PK1: 538^{+159}_{-131} ns, compared to experimental value 450^{+170}_{-100} ns
- 149Lu half-life predicted with NL3*: 469^{+146}_{-110} ns
- 149Lu half-life predicted with TMA: 544^{+169}_{-128} ns
- 149Lu quadrupole deformation from DRHBc: β₂ = -0.178 (PC-PK1), -0.185 (NL3*), -0.197 (TMA)
- 149Lu spectroscopic factor S_F: 0.77 (PC-PK1), 0.76 (NL3*), 0.68 (TMA)
- 148Lu predicted half-life: 13.92 μs, with β₂ = -0.184
- 150Lu half-life predicted with PC-PK1: 51.11^{+3.89}_{-7.58} ms (experimental: 64.0 ± 5.6 ms)
- 151Lu half-life predicted with PC-PK1: 109.37^{+8.67}_{-7.95} ms (experimental: 127.1 ± 1.8 ms)
- 150mLu half-life predicted with PC-PK1: 27.34^{+3.40}_{-3.00} μs (experimental: 43^{+7}_{-5} μs)
- 151mLu half-life predicted with PC-PK1: 14.99^{+3.84}_{-3.03} μs (experimental: 16 ± 1 μs)

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc plus WKB approach, previously successful for neutron decay widths and halo nuclei such as 31Ne, to the study of proton emission in lutetium isotopes. It directly addresses the ambiguity in the deformation of 149Lu left unresolved by the nonadiabatic quasiparticle model (Agramunt et al., 2022), which could not distinguish between oblate (β₂ ≲ -0.17) and prolate (β₂ ≈ 0.15) shapes. The results improve upon the spherical RCHB plus WKB calculations of Lim et al. (2016), with deviations from experimental half-lives typically half as large, demonstrating the importance of including deformation effects. The prediction of 148Lu as a more oblately deformed proton emitter with a longer half-life than 149Lu provides a target for future experimental verification.
