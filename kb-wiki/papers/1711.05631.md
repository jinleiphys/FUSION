# Light cluster production at NICA

> In a recent publication the importance of measuaring light clusters at future NICA and FAIR experiments as "rare probes" for in-medium characteristics was presented. Within theoretical conciderations is shown how clusters would be affected by medium affects as given collider energies.

## Key claim

Light nuclear clusters (deuterons, tritons, alpha particles) can serve as "rare probes" for in-medium characteristics in heavy-ion collisions at NICA and FAIR, but their abundance and dissociation depend on medium effects such as Mott dissociation, which modifies the simple freeze-out picture from the law of mass action.

## Method

The authors construct a phase diagram of dense nuclear matter by combining a DD2 equation of state for the hadronic phase (with a liquid-gas phase transition) and a PNJL model for the quark-gluon matter phase, with deconfinement lines determined by equal baryon chemical potential and pressure between the two phases. They overlay Mott dissociation lines for deuterons, tritons, and alpha particles from prior work (Schuck 1999, Röpke 1983) onto this diagram, together with the chemical freeze-out line from statistical model fits (Begun 2012). The quark matter pressure is shifted by a bag constant of either 250 or 260 MeV/fm³ to generate the deconfinement boundaries.

## Key numbers

- Mott dissociation lines are shown for deuterons (d), tritons (t), and alpha particles (α) in the temperature–baryochemical potential plane, taken from Schuck (1999) and Röpke (1983).
- The deconfinement lines are obtained using a bag constant shift of either 250 or 260 MeV/fm³ for the quark matter pressure.
- Laboratory energies from the NICA accelerator complex are shown as labeled dots on the chemical freeze-out line from Begun (2012), which is parametrized in terms of laboratory energy per nucleon \(E_{\rm lab}\) for fixed target.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of light cluster production in heavy-ion collisions from earlier theoretical studies of Mott dissociation (Schuck 1999, Röpke 1983) and statistical model fits of hadron abundances (Begun 2012, Andronic 2010, Cleymans 2011). It specifically targets the NICA energy range, including both fixed-target (BM@N) and collider (MPD) setups, and builds on the recent proposal by Bastian (2016) that light clusters can act as rare probes for in-medium effects. The results enable future experimental searches at NICA and FAIR to use cluster yields as diagnostics for the phase structure of dense nuclear matter, particularly the location of the critical endpoint and the deconfinement transition.
