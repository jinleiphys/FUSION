# Derivation of Brown-Rho scaling from scale-chiral perturbation theory

> The medium modified hadron properties are studied by using the scale-chiral perturbation theory $χ$PT$_σ$ in which the lightest scalar meson $f_0(500)$ is included as an explicit degree of freedom by regarding it as the Nambu-Goldstone boson of scale symmetry both spontaneously broken and explicitly broken by the QCD trace anomaly. We derive Brown-Rho scaling at the leading order of scale symmetry from $χ$PT$_σ$ and formulate how to make higher-order scale-chiral corrections going beyond the leading order of scale symmetry. By taking into account the intrinsic density dependence given by the dilaton condensate in the "bare" parameters of the Lagrangian, the medium modified hadron properties are investigated. Relying on available experimental information and certain reasonable assumptions, we arrive at the leading order scale symmetric effective theory that can be confronted with nature .

## Key claim

The authors derive Brown-Rho scaling at leading order of scale symmetry from scale-chiral perturbation theory, but only under the condition that the hidden scale symmetry parameter \(\kappa \approx 1\) (the "leading order of scale symmetry" or LOSS limit), and only in the regime where mean-field approximation is valid at relatively low density near nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors construct a scale-chiral effective theory (HLS\(_\sigma\) and \(bs\)HLS) by coupling the dilaton field \(\chi\) (the Nambu-Goldstone boson of spontaneously broken scale symmetry) to the hidden local symmetry Lagrangian for vector mesons and baryons. They incorporate intrinsic density dependence (IDD) into the bare parameters of the Lagrangian via the medium-modified vacuum expectation value of the dilaton \(\langle\chi\rangle^\ast = f_\sigma^\ast\), then derive general expressions for medium-modified hadron masses, decay constants, and coupling strengths. The analysis is performed in the even-parity sector of the theory, and the reduction to leading order scale symmetry is achieved by taking the limit where the parameter \(\kappa \approx 1\) in the general CT-approach Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

* The dilaton decay constant \(f_\sigma\) is taken to be at the same order as \(f_\pi\) (the pion decay constant in vacuum).
* The density regime of applicability is \(\lesssim n_{1/2} \simeq 2 n_0\), where \(n_0\) is normal nuclear density and \(n_{1/2}\) is the density at which the skyrmion topological object factorizes into two half-skyrmions.
* The sigma meson mass in matter-free space is given by \(m_\sigma^2 = 4c\beta^\prime(4+\beta^\prime)/f_\sigma^2\) with \(c > 0\).
* The vector meson mass in vacuum is \(m_\rho^2 = a g^2 f_\pi^2\), where \(a\) is the HLS parameter and \(g\) is the HLS gauge coupling.

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling framework (Brown 1991) by deriving it from a fundamental scale-chiral effective theory, rather than treating it as a phenomenological ansatz. It builds on the Crewther-Tunstall (CT) approach (Crewther 2013) for scale-chiral perturbation theory and the hidden local symmetry approach (Bando 1984, Bando 1987, Harada 2003) for vector mesons. The results enable systematic higher-order scale-chiral corrections beyond leading order, and provide a framework for connecting medium-modified hadron properties to QCD condensates via the dilaton condensate, with potential applications to nuclear matter and compact star studies.
