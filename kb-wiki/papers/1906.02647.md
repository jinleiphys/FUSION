# Finite size effects on cumulants of the critical mode

> In this work we study the temperature dependence of the equilibrium variance of critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point. In particular, we take the finite size of the fireball created in heavy-ion collisions into account and systematically obtain corrections to the leading-order result. We find that not only is the variance globally reduced in a finite size system, but for certain combinations of parameters a two-peak structure can develop for temperatures near the critical point.

## Key claim

The equilibrium variance of critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point is globally reduced in a finite-size system compared to the infinite-volume limit, and for certain combinations of parameters a two-peak structure in the temperature dependence can develop near the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors model the fireball as a quasi-one-dimensional system of finite size V = LA, decoupling longitudinal fluctuations from the transverse extension A. They expand the two-point correlation function of the order parameter σ around the free-propagator result using a diagrammatic approach, keeping terms up to next-to-leading order in ξ²/A. The variance is obtained by integrating the two-point function over the region of size L, with the thermodynamic potential including σ³, σ⁴, and σ⁶ terms whose couplings are scaled according to the 3d-Ising universality class.

## Key numbers

- The maximal equilibrium correlation length ξ is about 3 fm near Tc = 0.15 GeV, falling off quickly to < 1 fm below and above Tc.
- For a fixed longitudinal size L = 40 fm, the leading-order variance decreases as the transverse area A increases from 49 fm² to 100 fm² to 169 fm².
- The next-to-leading order correction term is −(3/2)(λ̃₄ − λ̃₃²) T L ξ⁴ / V², where λ̃₃ = 1 and λ̃₄ = 4 are the chosen dimensionless couplings.
- The expansion parameter is effectively ξ²/A, not the previously reported ξ³/V.

## Context

This work extends the infinite-volume scaling results of Stephanov (2008) by computing finite-size corrections to the variance of critical fluctuations, motivated by the fact that heavy-ion collision fireballs are neither infinite nor long-lived. The approach is closely related to the dynamical study of net-baryon density fluctuations by Nahrgang (2018), which also found finite-size effects on the growth of the correlation length and cumulant scaling. The results enable more realistic comparisons with experimental fluctuation measurements from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, NA61/SHINE, and HADES, and will be extended to higher-order non-Gaussian cumulants in future work.
