# $־±$ knockout reaction as a new probe for $־±$ formation in $־±$-decay nuclei

> The $־±$-decay phenomennon has been studied for more than a century but the mechanism of the $־±$ particle formation and its tunneling process have not yet been fully understood. As an alternative to the $־±$-decay lifetime measurement, we propose the proton-induced $־±$ knockout reaction, ($p$,$p־±$), as a new probe for the surface $־±$ formation probability of $־±$-decay nuclei. The $^{210,212}$Po($p$,$p־±$)$^{206,208}$Pb reaction is described by the distorted-wave impulse approximation framework. It shows that the $^{212}$Po/$^{210}$Po ratio of the $־±$ knockout cross sections agrees with that of the surface $־±$ formation probabilities determined by lifetime measurements. The result implies that the ($p$,$p־±$) cross section is a direct probe for the surface $־±$ formation probability, which is an essential quantity to lead us to a complete understanding of the $־±$-decay phenomenon.

## Key claim

The ratio of the $^{212}$Po/$^{210}$Po alpha knockout cross sections agrees with the ratio of their surface alpha formation probabilities determined from lifetime measurements, implying that the ($p$,$p\alpha$) cross section is a direct probe for the surface alpha formation probability. This agreement relies on the surface sensitivity of the knockout reaction, which probes the surface amplitude rather than the total spectroscopic factor.

## Method

The $^{210,212}$Po($p$,$p\alpha$)$^{206,208}$Pb reaction was described using the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework, considering 200 MeV/nucleon Po beams in inverse kinematics on a proton target. The Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction was used for the $p$-$\alpha$ effective interaction, with global optical potentials for proton-nucleus scattering (Koning and Delaroche) and $\alpha$-nucleus scattering (Avrigeanu, Hodgson, and Avrigeanu). The alpha cluster wave functions were taken from Qi (2010), and the triple differential cross sections, longitudinal momentum distributions, and total cross sections were calculated at the recoil-less condition.

## Key numbers

- The $^{212}$Po/$^{210}$Po ratio of the surface alpha formation probability $|RF(R)|^2$ at $R = 9.0$ fm is 10.2, from the structure theory of Qi (2010).
- The $^{212}$Po/$^{210}$Po ratio of the triple differential cross section peak heights is 8.79.
- The $^{212}$Po/$^{210}$Po ratio of the longitudinal momentum distribution peak heights is 12.2.
- The $^{212}$Po/$^{210}$Po ratio of the total cross sections is 11.9.
- The total cross sections are 0.0306 mb for $^{210}$Po and 0.3644 mb for $^{212}$Po.
- The LMD peak heights are 0.104 pb/(MeV/c) for $^{210}$Po and 1.264 pb/(MeV/c) for $^{212}$Po.
- The alpha spectroscopic factors $S_\alpha$ are $7.63\times 10^{-4}$ for $^{210}$Po and $1.46\times 10^{-3}$ for $^{212}$Po, giving a ratio of 1.92.

## Context

This work extends the use of alpha knockout reactions, previously applied to medium-mass nuclei by Tanaka (2021) and Yoshida (2018), to heavy alpha-decay nuclei as an alternative to traditional alpha-decay lifetime measurements. It contrasts with transfer reactions such as alpha-particle pickup (Arima treatise) and stripping (DeVries, 1975), which are complicated by Coulomb barrier effects at low energies. The results enable future experimental studies of ($p$,$p\alpha$) reactions on alpha-decay nuclei to directly extract surface alpha formation probabilities, potentially resolving the long-standing puzzle of alpha clustering in heavy nuclei.
