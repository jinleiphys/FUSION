# Disentangling two- and four-quark state pictures of the charmed scalar mesons

> We suggest that the recently observed charmed scalar mesons $D_0^{0}(2308)$ (BELLE) and $D_0^{0,+}(2405)$ (FOCUS) are considered as different resonances. Using the QCD sum rule approach we investigate the possible four-quark structure of these mesons and also of the very narrow $D_{sJ}^{+}(2317)$, firstly observed by BABAR. We use diquak-antidiquark currents and work to the order of $m_s$ in full QCD, without relying on $1/m_c$ expansion. Our results indicate that a four-quark structure is acceptable for the resonances observed by BELLE and BABAR: $D_0^{0}(2308)$ and $D_{sJ}^{+}(2317)$ respectively, but not for the resonances observed by FOCUS: $D_0^{0,+}(2405)$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the scalar charmed mesons observed by BELLE ($D_0^0(2308)$) and BABAR ($D_{sJ}^+(2317)$) can be described as four-quark (diquark-antidiquark) states using QCD sum rules, but that the FOCUS resonances ($D_0^{0,+}(2405)$) cannot be accommodated in this picture and should instead be interpreted as conventional two-quark $c\bar{q}$ states. The authors explicitly treat the BELLE and FOCUS resonances as distinct particles, contrary to interpretations that might identify them as the same state.

## Method

The authors use QCD sum rules (QCDSR) to compute the masses of scalar charmed mesons modeled as diquark-antidiquark bound states, using interpolating currents with zero, one, and two strange quarks. They work at leading order in the operator product expansion (OPE), include condensates up to dimension six, treat the strange quark as light to order $m_s$, and keep the charm quark mass finite without relying on a $1/m_c$ expansion. The resonance masses are extracted from the ratio of Borel-transformed sum rules, with the continuum contribution parametrized by a threshold parameter $s_0$, and the analysis is checked by introducing a Breit-Wigner width for the broader resonances.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the four-quark state with no strange quarks ($D_0^{(0s)}$, corresponding to $(cq)(\bar{u}\bar{d})$): $2.22 \pm 0.21$ GeV
- Mass of the four-quark state with one strange quark ($D_0^{(1s)}$, corresponding to $(cq)(\bar{q}\bar{s})$): $2.32 \pm 0.18$ GeV
- Mass of the four-quark state with two strange quarks ($D_0^{(2s)}$, corresponding to $(cs)(\bar{u}\bar{s})$): $2.30 \pm 0.20$ GeV
- The $D_{sJ}^+(2317)$ observed by BABAR has a mass of 2317 MeV, approximately 160 MeV below the prediction of the Godfrey-Isgur quark model (1985)
- The $D_0^0(2308)$ observed by BELLE has a mass approximately 100 MeV below the Godfrey-Isgur prediction
- The FOCUS resonances $D_0^0(2407)$ and $D_0^+(2403)$ have masses in complete agreement with the Godfrey-Isgur quark model
- Input parameters used: $m_s = 0.13$ GeV, $m_c = 1.2$ GeV, $\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -(0.23)^3$ GeV$^3$, $\langle \bar{s}s \rangle = 0.8 \langle \bar{q}q \rangle$, $m_0^2 = 0.8$ GeV$^2$, $\langle g^2 G^2 \rangle = 0.5$ GeV$^4$, $\langle g^3 G^3 \rangle = 0.045$ GeV$^6$

## Context

This work extends the four-quark interpretation of light scalar mesons, originally proposed by Jaffe (1977), into the charm sector, and applies the diquark-antidiquark approach previously used by Brito, Navarra, Nielsen, and Bracco (2005) for light scalars. It contrasts with earlier QCD sum rule studies of charmed scalar mesons by Dai, Huang, Liu, and Zhu (2003), Hayashigaki and Terasaki (2004), and Narison (2005), which treated these states as conventional two-quark $c\bar{q}$ or $c\bar{s}$ configurations. The results enable a classification scheme where the BABAR and BELLE resonances are identified as four-quark states while the FOCUS resonances are identified as ordinary quark-model states, and predict a yet-unobserved four-quark scalar state $(cq)(\bar{u}\bar{d})$ with mass around 2.2 GeV that should be broad.
