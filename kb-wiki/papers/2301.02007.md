# $0^+$ to $2^+$ neutrinoless double-$β$ decay of $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{130}$Te and $^{136}$Xe in the microscopic interacting boson model}

> Here, we study the neutrinoless double-$β$ ($0νββ$) decay between the ground state and the first $2^+$ state of $^{76}\mbox{Ge} \rightarrow {}^{76}\mbox{Se}$, $^{82}\mbox{Se} \rightarrow{}^{82}\mbox{Kr}$, $^{130}\mbox{Te} \rightarrow {}^{130}\mbox{Xe}$ and $^{136}\mbox{Xe} \rightarrow {}^{136}\mbox{Ba}$ systems. The relevant nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) involved in the process are calculated within the formalism of the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2). The IBM-2 has been widely used to obtain predictions for nuclear observables, such as the spectrum, but also to explore the possible emergence of beyond-the-Standard Model effects in the weak interactions of nuclei. Our calculations are carried out by considering the exchange of a Majorana neutrino between two nucleons ($2N$-mechanism). In addition to NMEs, we calculate the associated leptonic phase-space factors (PSFs) using electron radial wave functions, which are obtained by solving numerically the Dirac equation of a screened Coulomb potential that takes into account finite nuclear size. By combining our IBM-2 results for the NMEs with those for the PSFs along with experimental half-life limits, we can set limits on the $\langle λ\rangle$ and $\langle η\rangle$ couplings of left-right (L-R) models.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for the $0^+$ to $2^+$ neutrinoless double-beta decay of four nuclei using the microscopic interacting boson model (IBM-2), finding that the $M_6$ and $M_7$ operator contributions vanish for these systems because protons and neutrons occupy the same major shell, leading to $M'_{\eta}=0$ for all studied cases.

## Method

The authors computed NMEs for $0^+ \rightarrow 2^+$ $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay via the $2N$-mechanism (exchange of a Majorana neutrino between two nucleons) within the IBM-2 formalism, using Hamiltonian parameters fitted to experimental energy levels and electromagnetic properties from prior literature. They calculated leptonic phase-space factors (PSFs) by solving the Dirac equation numerically for a screened Coulomb potential that includes finite nuclear size, and combined the NMEs and PSFs with experimental half-life limits to set constraints on the $\langle \lambda \rangle$ and $\langle \eta \rangle$ couplings of left-right symmetric models.

## Key numbers

- $^{76}$Ge: $M_{\lambda}=0.035$, $M_{\eta}=0.108$, $G_1=1.669\times10^{-15}$ yr$^{-1}$, $G_2=1.157\times10^{-15}$ yr$^{-1}$, $Q$-value = 1479.9 keV
- $^{82}$Se: $M_{\lambda}=-0.051$, $M_{\eta}=-0.053$, $G_1=12.357\times10^{-15}$ yr$^{-1}$, $G_2=9.159\times10^{-15}$ yr$^{-1}$, $Q$-value = 2221.4 keV
- $^{130}$Te: $M_{\lambda}=0.056$, $M_{\eta}=0.112$, $G_1=18.464\times10^{-15}$ yr$^{-1}$, $G_2=14.462\times10^{-15}$ yr$^{-1}$, $Q$-value = 1991.4 keV
- $^{136}$Xe: $M_{\lambda}=0.077$, $M_{\eta}=0.092$, $G_1=8.611\times10^{-15}$ yr$^{-1}$, $G_2=6.269\times10^{-15}$ yr$^{-1}$, $Q$-value = 1639.3 keV
- Most stringent limits on L-R couplings from $^{136}$Xe: $|\langle \lambda \rangle| < 1.7\times10^{-5}$ and $|\langle \eta \rangle| < 1.4\times10^{-5}$ (90% C.L., using KamLAND-Zen half-life limit $>2.6\times10^{25}$ y)

## Context

This work extends previous IBM-2 calculations for $0^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay to the $0^+ \rightarrow 2^+$ transition, which is sensitive primarily to right-handed currents and thus provides complementary information to the ground-state-to-ground-state mode. The results are compared with prior calculations using the projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method by Tomoda (1988) and the quasiparticle random-phase approximation by Fang (2021), with the IBM-2 NMEs falling between these two approaches for $^{76}$Ge. The improved PSFs, computed with exact numerical solutions of the Dirac equation rather than approximate power-series expansions used by Tomoda (1990), yield slightly smaller values than those earlier estimates.
