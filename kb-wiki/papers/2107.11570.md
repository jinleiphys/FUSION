# Quantum field-theoretical description of neutrino oscillations in magnetic field

> It is shown that the processes of neutrino oscillations in a magnetic field can be consistently described in the framework of a new quantum field-theoretical approach without use of the neutrino flavor states. It is based on the Feynman diagram technique with a modified distance-dependent propagator, which takes into account the geometry of neutrino oscillation experiments. Processes of neutrino oscillations in a magnetic field, where the neutrinos are detected through the weak charged- and neutral-current interaction, have been studied and numerical calculations have been carried out for some specific examples. Implications for the solar neutrinos are briefly discussed, and formulas for the asymptotic values of the normalized probability of solar neutrino oscillation processes are derived, which coincide with the observable ratio of the measurable neutrino flux to that predicted by the standard solar model.

## Key claim

Neutrino oscillations in a magnetic field can be consistently described within quantum field theory using a modified distance-dependent propagator, without invoking neutrino flavor states; the approach relies on the adiabaticity condition that the magnetic field varies slowly along the neutrino path, with the transverse component replaced by its mean value over the propagation distance.

## Method

The authors develop a quantum field-theoretical formalism based on Feynman diagrams in the coordinate representation, where a delta function fixes the macroscopic distance L between the neutrino production and detection points along the direction from source to detector. This leads to a modified distance-dependent propagator for neutrino mass eigenstates in momentum space, which is then applied to processes where neutrinos are produced and detected via weak charged- and neutral-current interactions with nuclei and electrons, while propagating through a region of homogeneous or adiabatically varying transverse magnetic field. The neutrino magnetic moment is taken into account through the Standard Model loop-induced value, and the squared amplitude is factorized into production, detection, and oscillation probability terms, with integration over the neutrino momentum magnitude.

## Key numbers

- The neutrino magnetic moment parameter is given by μ₀ = 3eG_F / (8√2 π²), which yields neutrino magnetic moments at least 10 orders of magnitude smaller than the Bohr magneton for experimentally allowed neutrino masses.
- The adiabaticity condition for the magnetic field is |μ₀ m_max (n·∇)|H|| ≪ |p|/d, where d is the characteristic size of the field region and m_max is the largest neutrino mass.
- The oscillation probability P_ee contains terms with arguments (Δm_ik²/(4|p|) ± μ₀ Δm_ik H̅/2)L and (Δm_ik²/(4|p|) ± μ₀ Σm_ik H̅/2)L, where H̅ is the mean transverse magnetic field, Δm_ik = m_i - m_k, and Σm_ik = m_i + m_k.
- The integration limits for neutrino momentum magnitude are |p|_min = ((M_2' + m_e)² - M₂²)/(2M₂) and |p|_max = (M₁² - (M_1' + m_e)²)/(2M₁), where M_l are nuclear masses and m_e is the electron mass.

## Context

This work extends the quantum field-theoretical approach to neutrino oscillations developed by the same authors (Egorov and Volobuev, 2017-2021) and earlier by Okun (1982), who first proposed using distance-dependent propagators with delta-function approximations for nuclear positions. The formalism provides an alternative to the standard quantum-mechanical wave packet approach (Giunti, 2007; Grimus, 1996) and to the flavor-state description of neutrino oscillations in magnetic fields (Cisneros, 1971; Fujikawa, 1980; Okun, 1986; Akhmedov; Popov, 2019; Chukhnova, 2019). The results enable consistent quantum field-theoretical calculations of solar neutrino oscillation probabilities in magnetic fields, with the derived asymptotic formulas for normalized probabilities coinciding with the observable ratio of measurable neutrino flux to that predicted by the standard solar model.
