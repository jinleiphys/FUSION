# Evidence for a narrow N(1685) resonance in eta photoproduction off the nucleon

> Revised analysis of Sigma beam asymmetry for eta photoproduction off the free proton from GRAAL is presented. New analysis reveals a narrow structure near W ~1.685 GeV. We describe this structure by the contribution of a narrow resonance with quantum numbers P_{11}, or P_{13}, or D_{13}. Being considered together with the recent observations of a bump-like structure at W~1.68 GeV in the quasi-free eta photoproduction off the neutron, this result provides an evidence for a narrow (Gamma < 25 MeV) N*(1685) resonance. Properties of this possible new nucleon state, namely the mass, the narrow width, and the much stronger photocoupling to the neutron, are similar to those predicted for the non-strange member of anti-decouplet of exotic baryons.

## Key claim

A narrow structure near W ~ 1.685 GeV in the Sigma beam asymmetry for eta photoproduction off the free proton, combined with previous observations of a bump in quasi-free eta photoproduction off the neutron, provides evidence for a narrow (Gamma ≤ 25 MeV) N*(1685) resonance; however, the quantum numbers remain ambiguous and could be P11, P13, or D13.

## Method

The authors performed a revised analysis of GRAAL data on the Sigma beam asymmetry for eta photoproduction off the free proton, using narrow energy bins of ΔEγ ~ 16 MeV and wide angular bins of 20-40 degrees. They compared the data to the SAID partial-wave analysis solution E429 and added a narrow resonance in Breit-Wigner form (either S11, P11, P13, or D13) to model deviations, varying mass, width, photocouplings multiplied by the square of the eta N branching ratio, and phase to achieve best fit.

## Key numbers

- The narrow structure appears at W ~ 1.685 GeV, with mass constrained to the range MR = 1.685 - 1.690 GeV and best fit at MR = 1.688 GeV, with an uncertainty of ±5 MeV from tagging system calibration and ±4 MeV from energy binning.
- The width is Γ ≤ 25 MeV, with best fit values of Γ ~ 8 MeV for P13 and D13, and Γ ~ 19 MeV for P11.
- At forward angles θcm = 43° and Eγ = 1.04 GeV, the beam asymmetry reaches values as large as 0.94.
- The χ²/dof improves from 74/24 for the SAID multipoles alone to 56/22 for SAID plus P11, 25/20 for SAID plus P13, and 39/20 for SAID plus D13.
- The extracted photocoupling for the P11 resonance is √BrηN A^p_{1/2} ~ 1 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}.
- For the P13 resonance: √BrηN A^p_{1/2} ~ -0.3 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} and √BrηN A^p_{3/2} ~ 1.7 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}.
- For the D13 resonance: √BrηN A^p_{1/2} ~ -0.1 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} and √BrηN A^p_{3/2} ~ 0.9 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}.
- The ratio A^n_{1/2}/A^p_{1/2} ~ 10-20, comparing the proton photocoupling to the neutron value extracted from GRAAL and CBELSA/TAPS data.

## Context

This work extends the search for a narrow nucleon resonance following observations of a bump-like structure at W ~ 1.68 GeV in quasi-free eta photoproduction off the neutron by GRAAL (Kuznetsov et al., 2004), CBELSA/TAPS (Krusche et al., 2007), and LNS-Sendai (Kasagi et al., 2007). The results contradict alternative explanations by Shklyar et al. (2005) and Anisovich et al. (2005) that attribute the neutron anomaly to interference of known resonances without producing structure in the proton channel. The properties of the candidate N*(1685) resonance match predictions for the non-strange member of the anti-decouplet of exotic baryons in the Chiral Quark Soliton Model (Diakonov et al., 1997; Polyakov and Rathke, 2003), and the findings encourage further dedicated high-resolution experiments to determine the quantum numbers and confirm the existence of the Theta+ pentaquark.
