# Giant Dipole Resonance photofission and photoneutron reactions in $^{238}$U and $^{232}$Th

> New measurements of photofission and photoneutron reactions on $^{238}$U and $^{232}$Th in the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) energy region have been performed at the laser Compton-scattering $γ$-ray source of the NewSUBARU synchrotron radiation facility using a high-and-flat efficiency moderated $^3$He detection array. The neutron-multiplicity sorting of high-multiplicity fission neutron coincidence events has been performed using a dedicated energy dependent, multiple firing statistical treatment. The photoneutron $(γ,\,in)$ with $i$~=~1~--~3 and photofission $(γ,\,F)$ reactions have been discriminated by considering a Gaussian distribution of prompt-fission-neutron (PFN) multiplicities predicted by the theory of evaporation in sequential neutron emission from excited fission fragments. We report experimental $(γ,\,n)$, $(γ,\,2n)$, $(γ,\,3n)$ and $(γ,\,F)$ cross sections, average energies of PFNs and of $(γ,\,in)$ photoneutrons, as well as the mean number of PFNs per fission act and the width of the PFNs multiplicity distribution. Based on these primary experimental results and combined with reasonable assumptions, we extract also the first- and second-chance fission contributions. The new experimental results are compared with statistical-model calculations performed with the EMPIRE-3.2 Malta and TALYS-1.964 codes on the present data and with prompt fission emission calculations obtained with the Los Alamos model in the frame of the most probable fragmentation approach with and without sequential emission.

## Key claim

The paper reports new measurements of photofission and photoneutron cross sections on 238U and 232Th that resolve long-standing discrepancies between earlier Saclay and Livermore data, but the discrimination between photoneutron and photofission reactions relies on assuming a Gaussian distribution of prompt-fission-neutron multiplicities predicted by evaporation theory, and the extraction of first- and second-chance fission contributions requires additional reasonable assumptions beyond the primary experimental results.

## Method

Measurements were performed at the NewSUBARU laser Compton-scattering gamma-ray facility using quasi-monochromatic beams at 40 energies between 5.87 and 20.14 MeV. A flat-efficiency moderated 3He detection array (31 counters in three concentric rings) recorded neutron coincidence events, and neutron-multiplicity sorting with an energy-dependent multiple-firing statistical treatment separated photoneutron (gamma,in) with i=1-3 from photofission (gamma,F) reactions. The ring-ratio method using inner versus outer ring detection efficiencies determined average neutron energies, and the experimental results were compared with statistical-model calculations using EMPIRE-3.2 Malta and TALYS-1.964 codes and with Los Alamos model prompt fission calculations.

## Key numbers

- Incident gamma-ray beam energies from 5.87 to 20.14 MeV, with energy spread (FWHM) varying between 120 keV and 750 keV
- Mean number of photons per pulse varied between 5 and 15, corresponding to incident photon fluxes of (4-12) x 10^3 photons per second
- Total neutron detection efficiency varies within 5% from 38.1% at 10 keV to 33.1% at 5 MeV for Maxwell neutron spectra
- 238U separation energies: Sn = 6.15 MeV, S2n = 11.28 MeV, S3n = 17.82 MeV; first-chance fission threshold BF(gamma,f) = 5.8 MeV, second-chance BF(gamma,nf) = 12.3 MeV
- 232Th separation energies: Sn = 6.44 MeV, S2n = 11.56 MeV, S3n = 18.35 MeV; first-chance fission threshold BF(gamma,f) = 6.0 MeV, second-chance BF(gamma,nf) = 12.6 MeV
- Target areal densities: 8.08 g/cm2 for U3O8, 17.15 g/cm2 for ThO2
- Maximum recorded neutron multiplicities varied between 3 and 7 depending on target and energy region

## Context

This work extends the IAEA Coordinated Research Project on Photonuclear Data and Photon Strength Functions (2016-2019) that previously resolved discrepancies between Saclay and Livermore data for 11 nuclei from 9Be to 209Bi (Gheorghe 2017, Kawano 2020). The new measurements on actinides address the systematic discrepancies between the Saclay group (Veyssiere 1973) and Livermore group (Caldwell 1980) data for 237Np, 238U and 232Th, where photofission and photoabsorption cross sections were systematically higher at Livermore. The results enable improved statistical-model calculations of photonuclear reactions on actinides and better determination of fission barrier properties, complementing neutron-induced fission studies.
