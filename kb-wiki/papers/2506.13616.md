# Global QCD analysis of spin PDFs in the proton with high-$x$ and lattice constraints

> We perform a comprehensive global QCD analysis of spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs), combining all available data on inclusive and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (DIS), as well as inclusive weak boson and jet production in polarized $pp$ collisions, simultaneously extracting spin-averaged PDFs and fragmentation functions. Including recent Jefferson Lab DIS data at high $x$, together with subleading power corrections to the leading twist framework, allows us to verify the stability of the PDFs for $W^2 \geq 4$ GeV$^2$ and quantify the uncertainties on the spin structure functions more reliably. We explore the use of new lattice QCD data on gluonic pseudo Ioffe-time distributions, which, together with jet production and high-$x$ DIS data, improve the constraints on the polarized gluon PDF. The expanded kinematic reach afforded by the data into the high-$x$ region allows us to refine the bounds on higher twist contributions to the spin structure functions, and test the validity of the Bjorken sum rule.

## Key claim

The paper performs a comprehensive global QCD analysis of spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) that simultaneously extracts spin-averaged PDFs and fragmentation functions, and demonstrates that including recent Jefferson Lab high-\(x\) DIS data together with subleading power corrections allows stable PDF extraction for \(W^2 \geq 4\) GeV\(^2\), while combining lattice QCD data on gluonic pseudo-Ioffe-time distributions with jet production and high-\(x\) DIS data eliminates the possibility of negative gluon polarization in a model-independent manner.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian Monte Carlo global QCD analysis at next-to-leading order (NLO) in \(\alpha_s\), simultaneously fitting spin-dependent and spin-averaged PDFs along with fragmentation functions to all available data on inclusive and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) from protons, deuterons, and \(^3\)He, as well as inclusive \(W\) boson and jet production in polarized \(pp\) collisions. They incorporated target mass corrections and phenomenological higher twist contributions to extend the kinematic reach to high \(x\) and low \(Q^2\), and included lattice QCD data on gluonic pseudo-Ioffe-time distributions as additional constraints on the polarized gluon PDF. The analysis used the collinear factorization framework with hard-scattering coefficients at NLO, and applied cuts on the invariant mass of the unobserved hadronic system (\(W^2 \geq 4\) GeV\(^2\) for inclusive DIS, \(W^2_{\text{SIDIS}} > 20\) GeV\(^2\) for SIDIS) to ensure the validity of the leading-twist description.

## Key numbers

- The analysis constrains PDFs over the range \(0.005 \lesssim x \lesssim 0.85\) for scales down to \(\mu^2 = m_c^2\).
- The stability of the fitted results was verified by comparing \(\chi^2\) values and \(Z\)-scores as a function of the cut on the invariant mass of final state hadrons, from \(W^2 = 3.5\) GeV\(^2\) to 10 GeV\(^2\).
- The lattice QCD data used were generated on 1901 gauge configurations with pion mass \(m_\pi = 358(3)\) MeV and lattice spacing \(a = 0.096(1)\) fm.
- For SIDIS data, cuts of \(W^2_{\text{SIDIS}} > 20\) GeV\(^2\) and \(z < 0.8\) were applied, with a low-\(z\) cut of \(z > 0.2\) to avoid final state hadron mass corrections.

## Context

This work extends the JAM collaboration's systematic program of spin-dependent PDF analyses (Jimenez-Delgado 2013, Sato 2016, Ethier 2017, Cocuzza 2022, Zhou 2022, Karpie 2023, Hunt-Smith 2024) by combining all elements from earlier studies into a comprehensive global fit covering both small-\(x\) and large-\(x\) regions. The analysis demonstrates that only by combining world data with lattice QCD constraints on pseudo-Ioffe-time distributions sensitive to \(\Delta g\), together with high-precision large-\(x\) data from Jefferson Lab, was it possible to eliminate the possibility of negative gluon polarization in a model-independent manner, resolving a long-standing ambiguity in earlier extractions (Zhou 2022, Karpie 2023). The resulting PDF set, called JAMpol25, enables more reliable tests of the Bjorken sum rule and refined bounds on higher twist contributions to spin structure functions, with future improvements expected from next-generation facilities.
