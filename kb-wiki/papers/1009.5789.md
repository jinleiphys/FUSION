# Photoproduction of K Sigma(1385) from the nucleon

> The reactions of $K Σ(1385)$ photoproduction, i.e., $γp \to K^+ Σ^0(1385)$ and $γn \to K^+ Σ^-(1385)$, are investigated in the resonance energy region for studying the role of the nucleon and $Δ$ resonances of masses around 2 GeV. The Lagrangians for describing the decays of these resonances into the $K Σ(1385)$ channel are constructed and the decay amplitudes are obtained, which allows us to determine the coupling constants using the predictions of quark models or the data listed by the Particle Data Group. The resulting cross sections are compared to the data from the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility and the SPring-8, which indicates nontrivial contributions from the two-star-rated resonances in the Particle Data Group as well as from some missing resonances predicted by a quark model.

## Key claim

The photoproduction reactions γp → K⁺ Σ⁰(1385) and γn → K⁺ Σ⁻(1385) receive nontrivial contributions from two-star-rated and missing non-strange baryon resonances around 2 GeV, but the model's difficulty in describing the measured beam asymmetry for the neutron-target reaction indicates that more detailed analyses are required to fully understand the production mechanism.

## Method

The author constructs effective Lagrangians for the decays of nucleon and Δ resonances (up to spin-5/2) into the K Σ(1385) channel, and determines coupling constants using quark-model predictions or Particle Data Group listings. The model includes t-channel K and K* exchanges, s-channel non-strange baryon exchanges, u-channel hyperon exchanges, and a generalized contact term to restore gauge invariance. Cross sections and beam asymmetries are computed and compared to data from the CLAS Collaboration at Jefferson Lab and the LEPS Collaboration at SPring-8.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants for five PDG-listed resonances and three missing resonances are tabulated; for example, the missing resonance N(3/2⁻)(2095) has h₁ = 0.99, h₂ = 0.27, f₁ = 0.49, f₂ = -0.83
- The resonance Δ(3/2⁻)(2080) (PDG D₃₃*(1940)) has h₁ = -0.68, h₂ = 1.00, f₁ = 0.39, f₂ = -0.57
- The resonance N(3/2⁻)(1960) (PDG D₁₃**(2080)) has h₁ = 0.24, h₂ = -0.54, f₁ = -1.25, f₂ = 1.21
- The missing resonance Δ(3/2⁻)(2145) has h₁ = 0.25, h₂ = 0.46, f₁ = 0.11, f₂ = -0.059

## Context

This work extends the rare theoretical investigations of K Σ(1385) photoproduction, following earlier studies by Lee and collaborators (2005) and by Doring and collaborators (2006). The analysis uses quark-model predictions from Capstick and Roberts (1998) to identify missing resonances that may have large partial decay widths into the K Σ(1385) channel. The results enable future coupled-channel analyses that could help identify new baryon resonances and extract their properties, particularly for the neutron-target reaction where the LEPS Collaboration (2008) data on beam asymmetry remain unexplained.
