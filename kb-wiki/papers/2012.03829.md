# Transverse expansion of (1 + 2) dimensional magneto-hydrodynamics flow with longitudinal boost invariance

> In the present work, we investigate the effects of magnetic field on expanding hot and dense nuclear matter as an ideal fluid. We consider QGP, on the particular case of a (1 + 2) dimensional longitudinally boost-invariant fluid expansion, in the background of an inhomogeneous magnetic field that is generated by external sources. We assume the magnetic field points in the direction perpendicular to the reaction plane, follows the power-law decay in proper time, and has two components on the transverse plane. To simplify the calculation, we suppose the investigated fluid has azimuthal symmetry, and magneto-hydrodynamic equations are described in a polar coordinate system on the transverse plane of reaction. Our results depict the space-time evolution of the transverse expansion of the fluid in the presence of an inhomogeneous external magnetic field. Ultimately, we utilize transverse velocity and correction of energy density to estimate the transverse momentum spectrum of final particles that emerge from heavy-ion collisions based on experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper presents perturbative analytical solutions for the transverse expansion of a (1+2)-dimensional longitudinally boost-invariant ideal fluid in the presence of an external inhomogeneous magnetic field, but only under the condition that the magnetic field energy density is much smaller than the fluid energy density (\(b^2/\epsilon \ll 1\)) and that magnetization and dissipative effects are ignored.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic ideal magneto-hydrodynamic conservation equations perturbatively to order \(O(\lambda^2)\) in a cylindrical coordinate system with azimuthal symmetry, assuming the magnetic field points in the transverse plane with components \(b_r\) and \(b_\phi\). The magnetic field is taken to decay as \(\tau^{n}\) in proper time, and the fluid is assumed to have a linear equation of state \(P = \kappa\epsilon\) with \(\kappa = 1/3\) for massless particles. The transverse velocity \(u_r(\tau, r)\) and the correction to the energy density \(\epsilon_1(\tau, r)\) are obtained analytically by expanding the magnetic field squared in a series of zero-order Bessel functions and solving the resulting ordinary differential equations.

## Key numbers

* The initial energy density of the medium is taken as \(\epsilon_c = T_0^4 \approx (300\ \text{MeV})^4\) at initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\ \text{fm}\).
* The ratio of magnetic field energy density to fluid energy density is set to \(B^2/\epsilon_c = 0.6\).
* The typical magnetic field in Au-Au peripheral collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\ \text{GeV}\) is estimated as \(|eB| \approx 5\text{--}10\ m_\pi^2\) at \(\tau = 0\), with \(m_\pi \approx 150\ \text{MeV}\).
* The first twenty Bessel expansion coefficients \(B_m^2\) for the magnetic field profile with \(r_0 = 1\) are listed, starting from \(0.118491\,B_c^2\) to \(0.000208585\,B_c^2\).
* The analytical solutions are invalid for \(n = -4/3\) due to divergence in the inhomogeneous solution.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Emamian et al., earlier paper) which considered a magnetic field pointing only in the \(\phi\) direction and obtained analytical solutions only for \(n = -1\) and \(n = -4/3\). It also builds on prior analytical approaches with longitudinal boost invariance and external transverse magnetic fields (Refs. a25, a26, a27, a28), and on the (1+2)-dimensional expansion studied in Ref. a29. The results enable estimation of the transverse momentum spectrum of final particles from heavy-ion collisions and show that for magnetic field decay exponents \(n < -2\) the fluid expands outward in the transverse plane, while for \(n > -2\) the fluid contracts inward, with \(n = -2\) (ideal-MHD limit) giving no transverse flow.
