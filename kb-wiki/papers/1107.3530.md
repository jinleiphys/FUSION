# Multipole strength function of deformed superfluid nuclei made easy

> We present an efficient method for calculating strength functions using the finite amplitude method (FAM) for deformed superfluid heavy nuclei within the framework of the nuclear density functional theory. We demonstrate that FAM reproduces strength functions obtained with the fully self-consistent quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA) at a fraction of computational cost. As a demonstration, we compute the isoscalar and isovector monopole strength for strongly deformed configurations in $^{240}$Pu by considering huge quasi-particle QRPA spaces. Our approach to FAM, based on Broyden's iterative procedure, opens the possibility for large-scale calculations of strength distributions in well-bound and weakly bound nuclei across the nuclear landscape.

## Key claim

The finite amplitude method (FAM) applied to the quasi-particle random-phase approximation (QRPA) reproduces strength functions obtained with the fully self-consistent QRPA at a fraction of the computational cost, but only when using Broyden's iterative procedure to achieve convergence within no more than 40 iterations, whereas earlier coordinate-space FAM implementations required 500-1,000 iterations.

## Method

The authors solve the QRPA equations in a harmonic oscillator (HO) basis using the finite amplitude method, where the QRPA amplitudes are iterated at desired complex frequencies ω + iγ. The method replaces the explicit calculation and diagonalization of large QRPA matrices A and B with a self-consistent iterative procedure that computes the induced fields δh(ω) and δΔ(ω) via numerical derivatives of the HFB densities with respect to a small parameter η. The Broyden mixing scheme is adopted to stabilize convergence, and the strength function is computed from the converged QRPA amplitudes at each frequency point.

## Key numbers

- FAM-QRPA requires no more than 40 Broyden iterations to converge, compared to 500-1,000 iterations needed in earlier coordinate-space FAM implementations.
- For 20 HO shells with v_crit = 10^{-4}, the matrix QRPA (MQRPA) requires 16.4 GB memory for a matrix dimension of 32,039; with v_crit = 10^{-20}, the matrix rank reaches 211,159 requiring 713.41 GB memory.
- FAM-QRPA using the full space of 20 HO shells without any QRPA-level truncations requires only 0.572 GB memory.
- The FAM results are practically independent of the parameter η over the range 10^{-6} to 10^{-8}.
- For the monopole strength in 240Pu, about 99% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) is exhausted when integrating up to ω = 50 MeV.
- The imaginary part γ is set to 0.5 MeV, corresponding to a Lorentzian smearing width Γ = 2γ = 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite amplitude method (Nakatsukasa et al., 2007) from the RPA to the QRPA for deformed superfluid nuclei, building on earlier FAM applications to spherical nuclei (Avogadro and Nakatsukasa, 2011) and to giant dipole resonances (Inakura et al., 2009, 2011). The method enables large-scale calculations of strength distributions in well-bound and weakly bound nuclei across the nuclear landscape, and is especially suited for multiprocessor tasks since the strength function converges rapidly regardless of frequency. The approach is benchmarked against the matrix QRPA method of Losa et al. (2010) and demonstrates the ability to treat heavy deformed superfluid nuclei such as 240Pu in its ground state and fission isomer.
