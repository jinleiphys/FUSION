# Saturation effects in SIDIS at very forward rapidities

> Using the dipole picture for electron-nucleus deep inelastic scattering at small Bjorken $x$, we study the effects of gluon saturation in the nuclear target on the cross-section for SIDIS (single inclusive hadron, or jet, production). We argue that the sensitivity of this process to gluon saturation can be enhanced by tagging on a hadron (or jet) which carries a large fraction $z \simeq 1$ of the longitudinal momentum of the virtual photon. This opens the possibility to study gluon saturation in relatively hard processes, where the virtuality $Q^2$ is (much) larger than the target saturation momentum $Q_s^2$, but such that $z(1-z)Q^2\lesssim Q_s^2$. Working in the limit $z(1-z)Q^2\ll Q_s^2$, we predict new phenomena which would signal saturation in the SIDIS cross-section. For sufficiently low transverse momenta $k_\perp\ll Q_s$ of the produced particle, the dominant contribution comes from elastic scattering in the black disk limit, which exposes the unintegrated quark distribution in the virtual photon. For larger momenta $k_\perp\gtrsim Q_s$, inelastic collisions take the leading role. They explore gluon saturation via multiple scattering, leading to a Gaussian distribution in $k_\perp$ centred around $Q_s$. When $z(1-z)Q^2\ll Q^2$, this results in a Cronin peak in the nuclear modification factor (the $R_{pA}$ ratio) at moderate values of $x$. With decreasing $x$, this peak is washed out by the high-energy evolution and replaced by nuclear suppression ($R_{pA}<1$) up to large momenta $k_\perp\gg Q_s$. Still for $z(1-z)Q^2\ll Q_s^2$, we also compute SIDIS cross-sections integrated over $k_\perp$. We find that both elastic and inelastic scattering are controlled by the black disk limit, so they yield similar contributions, of zeroth order in the QCD coupling.

## Key claim

Gluon saturation in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) can be observed even at high virtualities $Q^2 \gg Q_s^2$ by tagging a very forward hadron or jet carrying a large longitudinal momentum fraction $z \simeq 1$, provided the condition $z(1-z)Q^2 \lesssim Q_s^2$ is satisfied, a regime where the dipole scattering becomes strong despite the hard scale.

## Method

The authors use the colour-dipole picture and the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory at leading order, describing the virtual photon as a quark-antiquark dipole that scatters off the nuclear target via multiple eikonal scattering encoded in Wilson lines. They compute the SIDIS cross-section for single inclusive hadron or jet production by integrating out the kinematics of the unmeasured quark from the known dijet cross-sections, specializing to the saturation regime $\bar{Q}^2 \equiv z(1-z)Q^2 \ll Q_s^2$ using both analytic approximations (in the strongly ordered limit) and numerical solutions of the collinearly improved Balitsky–Kovchegov (BK) equation with running coupling.

## Key numbers

* The saturation momentum for a large nucleus ($A \simeq 200$) at EIC energies is in the ballpark of 1 to 2 GeV$^2$.
* The saturation momentum grows with energy as $Q_s^2 \propto x^{-\lambda_s}$ with $\lambda_s \simeq 0.2$.
* The saturation momentum scales with nucleon number as $Q_s^2 \propto A^{1/3}$ for $A \gg 1$.
* For $\bar{Q}^2 \ll Q_s^2$ and low transverse momenta $k_\perp \ll Q_s$, the $k_\perp$-distribution follows a $1/k_\perp^2$ power law from elastic scattering in the black disk limit.
* For $\bar{Q}^2 \ll Q_s^2$ and high transverse momenta $k_\perp \gg Q_s$, the distribution shows a power tail $\propto 1/k_\perp^4$ (up to logarithms) from single hard scattering.
* The Gaussian distribution from inelastic multiple scattering is centred around $Q_s$ with dispersion $\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle = Q_s^2$.
* The Cronin peak in the nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ disappears after approximately one unit in rapidity under high-energy evolution.

## Context

This work extends the study of gluon saturation in DIS from inclusive measurements (where low $Q^2 \lesssim Q_s^2$ suffers from non-perturbative contamination from vector meson fluctuations) to the semi-inclusive channel of single hadron/jet production at forward rapidities. It builds on prior work on dijet production in dilute-dense collisions by Marquet (2007), Dominguez et al. (2011), and others, where saturation effects appear via broadening of back-to-back peaks but are ambiguous due to Sudakov effects. The authors propose that SIDIS with very forward kinematics avoids this ambiguity and enables cleaner saturation signals. The predicted behaviour of the $R_{pA}$ ratio, Cronin peak at moderate $x$ washed out by evolution into nuclear suppression, mirrors findings in d+Au collisions at RHIC studied by Kharzeev et al. (2002), Baier et al. (2003), and Iancu et al. (2004). The results enable experimental searches at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) by mapping out the phase space where $z(1-z)Q^2 \lesssim Q_s^2$ can be satisfied even at high $Q^2$.
