# Nucleon Structure Functions from the NJL-Model Chiral Soliton

> We present numerical simulations for unpolarized and polarized structure functions in a chiral soliton model. The soliton is constructed self-consistently from quark fields from which the structure functions are extracted. Central to the project is the implementation of regularizing the Dirac sea (or vacuum) contribution to structure functions from first principles. We discuss in detail how sum rules are realized at the level of the quark wave-functions in momentum space. The comparison with experimental data is convincing for the polarized structure functions but exhibits some discrepancies in the unpolarized case. The vacuum contribution to the polarized structure functions is particularly small.

## Key claim

The paper presents numerical simulations of nucleon structure functions from the NJL-model chiral soliton, claiming that the comparison with experimental data is convincing for polarized structure functions but exhibits some discrepancies in the unpolarized case, with the vacuum contribution to polarized structure functions being particularly small.

## Method

The authors construct a self-consistent chiral soliton from quark fields in the Nambu-Jona-Lasino (NJL) model, then extract unpolarized and polarized structure functions by computing the hadronic tensor via the Compton amplitude in the Bjorken limit. The key innovation is implementing Pauli-Villars regularization of the Dirac sea (vacuum) contribution from first principles at the level of the defining action functional, rather than imposing regularization a posteriori on the distributions. The structure functions are computed from momentum-space quark wave-functions obtained by Fourier transforming the self-consistent soliton eigenspinors, with the valence quark contribution obtained from the cranked valence level including rotational corrections.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass is fixed at \(m_\pi = 138\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the pion decay constant at \(f_\pi = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}\), using the constituent quark mass \(m\) as the single tunable parameter
- The regularization requires two Pauli-Villars subtractions with conditions \(\sum_{i=0}^2 c_i = 0\) and \(\sum_{i=0}^2 c_i \Lambda_i^2 = 0\), implemented with a single cut-off \(\Lambda\) via the prescription \(\sum_i c_i f(\Lambda_i^2) = f(0) - f(\Lambda^2) + \Lambda^2 f'(\Lambda^2)\)
- The moment of inertia \(\alpha^2\) is computed as a sum over basis states, with the valence contribution \(\frac{N_C}{4}[1+\mathrm{sign}(\epsilon_{\mathrm{v}})] \sum_{\beta \neq \mathrm{v}} \frac{|\langle \mathrm{v} | \tau_3 | \beta \rangle|^2}{\epsilon_\beta - \epsilon_{\mathrm{v}}}\) and a regularized vacuum contribution involving sums over \(i=1,2\) with coefficients \(c_i\)

## Context

This work extends earlier approaches to nucleon structure functions from the chiral quark soliton model, specifically the valence quark only approximation (Weigel, 1996) which neglected vacuum contributions, and the studies by Diakonov (1996, 1997), Pobylitsa (1998), and Wakamatsu (1997, 1998, 2002, 2003) that included vacuum contributions but required a posteriori regularization by a single Pauli-Villars subtraction. The present paper implements regularization from first principles at the action level, following the formalism developed by Weigel (1999) whose numerical simulation had been long outstanding. This enables proper treatment of sum rules and provides a more stringent theoretical framework for comparing model predictions with experimental DIS data.
