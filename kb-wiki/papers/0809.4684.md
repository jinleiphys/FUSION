# Cold nuclear matter effects on J/psi production: intrinsic and extrinsic transverse momentum effects

> Cold nuclear matter effects on J/psi production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions are evaluated taking into account the specific J/psi production kinematics at the partonic level, the shadowing of the initial parton distributions and the absorption in the nuclear matter. We consider two different parton processes for the c-cbar pair production: one with collinear gluons and a recoiling gluon in the final state and the other with initial gluons carrying intrinsic transverse momentum. Our results are compared to RHIC observables. The smaller values of the nuclear modification factor R_AA in the forward rapidity region (with respect to the mid rapidity region) are partially explained, therefore potentially reducing the need for recombination effects.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the rapidity dependence of the J/psi nuclear modification factor R_AA in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC can be partially explained by cold nuclear matter effects alone, potentially reducing the need for recombination (quarkonium regeneration) mechanisms. This conclusion depends on using a 2-to-2 partonic process (extrinsic scheme) with a final-state gluon, rather than the simpler 2-to-1 intrinsic transverse momentum scheme used in prior work.

## Method

The authors implemented a probabilistic Glauber Monte-Carlo framework to simulate J/psi production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. They compared two partonic production schemes: an intrinsic scheme (2-to-1 process with gluons carrying intrinsic k_T) where the J/psi transverse momentum comes entirely from initial gluon intrinsic k_T, and an extrinsic scheme (2-to-2 process g+g to J/psi+g) where the J/psi transverse momentum is balanced by a final-state gluon. The framework incorporated gluon shadowing via the EKS98 nuclear parton distribution functions, nuclear absorption via an effective cross section sigma_abs, and the spatial dependence of shadowing proportional to the nuclear path length.

## Key numbers

- In the intrinsic scheme, the nuclear absorption cross section needed to reproduce PHENIX d+Au data was sigma_abs = 2.8 mb, taken from Adare (2007)
- In the extrinsic scheme, a larger absorption cross section of sigma_abs = 4.2 mb was required to fit the same d+Au data
- The nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV was taken as sigma_NN = 42 mb
- The maximal nucleon density was rho_0 = 0.17 nucleons/fm^3
- The factorization/renormalization scale was set to mu_R = m_T = sqrt(M^2 + P_T^2), with the J/psi mass M
- The EKS98 nuclear PDF parametrization was used at an initial scale mu^2_F,0 = 2.25 GeV^2, valid for x >= 10^-6

## Context

This work extends prior studies of J/psi production in nuclear collisions by Vogt (2004) and the authors' own earlier work (OurIntrinsicPaper), which only considered the intrinsic (2-to-1) scheme with gluon intrinsic k_T. The paper demonstrates that the choice of partonic production mechanism significantly affects the predicted cold nuclear matter effects, particularly the rapidity dependence of R_AA. The extrinsic scheme produces a stronger rapidity dependence in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions that partially explains the PHENIX data from Adare (2006, 2008), whereas the intrinsic scheme yields nearly rapidity-independent R_AA. This enables future studies at LHC energies for Upsilon production and comparisons with NLO calculations from Campbell (2007) and Artoisenet (2007, 2008).
