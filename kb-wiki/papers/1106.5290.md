# Predicted charged charmonium-like structures in the hidden-charm dipion decay of higher charmonia

> In this work, we predict two charged charmonium-like structures close to the $D^\ast\bar{D}$ and $D^\ast\bar{D}^\ast$ thresholds, where the Initial Single Pion Emission mechanism is introduced in the hidden-charm dipion decays of higher charmonia $ψ(4040)$, $ψ(4160)$, $ψ(4415)$ and charmonium-like state Y(4260). We suggest BESIII to search for these structures in the $J/ψπ^+$, $ψ(2S)π^+$ and $h_b(1P)π^+$ invariant mass spectra of the $ψ(4040)$ decays into $J/ψπ^+π^-$, $ψ(2S)π^+π^-$ and $h_b(1P)π^+π^-$. In addition, the experimental search for these structures in the $J/ψπ^+$, $ψ(2S)π^+$ and $h_c(1P)π^+$ invariant mass spectra of the $ψ(4260)$ hidden-charm dipion decays will be accessible at Belle and BaBar.

## Key claim
The authors predict two charged charmonium-like enhancement structures near the $D^\ast\bar{D}$ and $D^\ast\bar{D}^\ast$ thresholds in the hidden-charm dipion decays of $\psi(4040)$, $\psi(4160)$, $\psi(4415)$ and $Y(4260)$, based on the Initial Single Pion Emission (ISPE) mechanism. The claim is conditional: the structures appear in the calculated line shapes of differential decay widths, and the authors note that the peak near $D^\ast\bar{D}$ in $\psi(4040)\to J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$ is less obvious than in the $\psi(2S)\pi^+$ or $h_c(1P)\pi^+$ channels.

## Method
The authors apply the Initial Single Pion Emission (ISPE) mechanism, previously proposed for bottomonium decays, to the hidden-charm dipion decays of higher vector charmonia $\psi(4040)$, $\psi(4160)$, $\psi(4415)$ and the charmonium-like state $Y(4260)$. They compute hadron-level loop diagrams where the initial charmonium emits a single pion and dissociates into $D^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ meson pairs, which then rescatter via $D^{(*)}$ exchange into $J/\psi\pi$, $\psi(2S)\pi$, or $h_c(1P)\pi$ final states. The differential decay widths as functions of the $J/\psi\pi^+$, $\psi(2S)\pi^+$, and $h_c(1P)\pi^+$ invariant masses are evaluated using effective Lagrangians based on heavy quark and chiral symmetries, with monopole form factors to account for off-shell effects.

## Key numbers
- The predicted charged structures appear close to the $D^\ast\bar{D}$ threshold (approximately 3870 MeV) and the $D^\ast\bar{D}^\ast$ threshold (approximately 4010 MeV), as indicated by vertical dashed and dotted lines in the figures.
- For $\psi(4160)\to h_c(1P)\pi^+\pi^-$, the predicted $h_c(1P)\pi^\pm$ mass distribution is consistent with CLEO-c data taken at $E_{CM}=4170$ MeV.
- The phenomenological parameter $\beta$ in the form factor $\Lambda = m_E + \beta \Lambda_{QCD}$ (with $\Lambda_{QCD}=220$ MeV) is set to $\beta=1$ for the main results; the line shapes are shown to be weakly dependent on $\beta$ for values 1, 2, and 3.
- The coupling constants used include $g=0.59$ from the $D^\ast\to D\pi$ branching ratio measured by CLEO-c, $f_{\psi}=0.416$ GeV, and $f_{\chi_{c0}}=0.297$ GeV (with an alternative QCD sum rule value of approximately 0.51 GeV).

## Context
This work extends the Initial Single Pion Emission (ISPE) mechanism, previously proposed by the same authors (Chen and Liu, 2011) to explain the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ structures observed by Belle in $\Upsilon(5S)$ decays, to the charmonium sector. The predicted charged charmonium-like structures are the charmonium analogues of those $Z_b$ states. The study is motivated by the similarity between the charmonium and bottomonium families, and it builds on the non-resonant explanation for $Y(4260)$ proposed by Chen (2010). The results enable experimental searches at BESIII (for $\psi(4040)$ decays) and at Belle and BaBar (for $Y(4260)$ decays), and the authors note that the predicted $h_c(1P)\pi^\pm$ mass distribution for $\psi(4160)$ is consistent with a subsequent CLEO-c measurement at $E_{CM}=4170$ MeV.
