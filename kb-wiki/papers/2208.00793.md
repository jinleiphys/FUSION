# Electroweak production of $χ_{Q1}$ states in $e^+e^-$ collisions: A brief review

> A brief review of the available experimental and theoretical results on the production of the $χ_{Q1}$ states in $e^+e^-$ annihilation and photon-photon $γγ^*$ interactions is presented. Future data on the production of the $χ_{c1}(1P)$, $χ_{c1}(3872)$, $χ_{c1} (4140)$, $χ_{c1}(4274)$, $χ_{c1}(4685)$, $χ_{b1} (1P)$, $χ_{b1}(2P )$, and $χ_{b1}(3P)$ resonances in $e^+e^-$ annihilation and $γγ^*$ interactions will help the development and unification of theoretical predictions related to the electroweak decays of heavy quarkonia, the reduction of their spread and model uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical results on the electroweak production of axial-vector heavy quarkonium states (χ_Q1) in e+e- collisions, arguing that while the BESIII measurement of Γ(χ_c1(1P)→e+e-) = 0.12^{+0.13}_{-0.08} eV is consistent with the two-photon mechanism dominating for charmonium, the weak neutral current (Z0 exchange) likely dominates for bottomonium states, and the nature of the X(3872) remains an open challenge with multiple competing scenarios (c-cbar resonance, D-D* molecule, or hybrid).

## Method

The authors compile experimental results from BESIII and Belle on χ_c1(1P) and χ_c1(3872) production in e+e- annihilation and γγ* collisions, then compare these with theoretical calculations of the electronic decay width Γ(χ_Q1→e+e-) using the logarithmic approximation formula that includes Z0 exchange and two-virtual-photon mechanisms. They also compute the reduced two-photon width for χ_c1 states using nonrelativistic potential model relations between P-wave charmonium states, scaling from the known Γ(χ_c2→γγ) values.

## Key numbers

- Γ(χ_c1(1P)→e+e-) = 0.12^{+0.13}_{-0.08} eV, measured by BESIII (2022) at 5.1σ significance via interference with coherent background
- Upper limit Γ(χ_c1(3872)→e+e-) < 0.32 eV, improved by BESIII by a factor of about 13 over the previous limit of <4.3 eV
- Reduced γγ width product: Γ̃(χ_c1(3872)→γγ) × B(χ_c1(3872)→π+π-J/ψ) = 5.5^{+4.1}_{-3.8}(stat) ± 0.7(syst) eV, from Belle (3.2σ, 3 candidates)
- Allowed range for reduced γγ width: 24 eV < Γ̃(χ_c1(3872)→γγ) < 615 eV, using updated branching ratio B(χ_c1(3872)→π+π-J/ψ) = (3.8 ± 1.2)%
- Theoretical estimate for χ_c1(1P): Γ(χ_c1(1P)→e+e-) ≈ 0.06192 eV from the logarithmic approximation formula
- Theoretical estimate for χ_b1(1P): Γ(χ_b1(1P)→e+e-) ≈ 0.0316 eV, dominated by Z0 exchange
- Estimated reduced γγ width for χ_c1(1P): Γ̃(χ_c1(1P)→γγ) ≈ 0.93 keV, scaled from Γ(χ_c2(1P)→γγ) ≈ 0.56 keV using the ratio 5/3
- Estimated reduced γγ width for χ_c1(3872): Γ̃(χ_c1(3872)→γγ) ≈ 350 eV, scaled from Γ(χ_c2(3930)→γγ) × B(χ_c2(3930)→D-Dbar) = 0.21 ± 0.04 keV

## Context

This review extends the line of theoretical work on P-wave heavy quarkonium electroweak decays dating to the late 1970s (Kuhn 1981, Krammer and Krasemann 1979) and compares with recent BESIII measurements (Ab22, Ab22a) and Belle results (Ter21). The paper highlights that the X(3872) nature remains contested between the c-cbar resonance interpretation, the D-D* molecular scenario (Brambilla 2020), and hybrid models (Cincioglu 2022), and that future measurements at BESIII, Belle II, and the Super Charm-Tau factory will help discriminate between these models and reduce the spread in theoretical predictions.
