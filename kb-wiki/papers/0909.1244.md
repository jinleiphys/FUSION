# Neutrino Interactions Importance for Nuclear Physics

> We review the general interplay between Nuclear Physics and neutrino-nucleus cross sections at intermediate and high energies. The effects of different reaction mechanisms over the neutrino observables are illustrated with examples in calculations using several nuclear models and ingredients.

## Key claim

The interplay between nuclear physics and neutrino-nucleus cross sections at intermediate and high energies is reviewed, with the caveat that the effects of different reaction mechanisms on neutrino observables are illustrated only through selected examples using several nuclear models, rather than providing a comprehensive or definitive theoretical treatment.

## Method

The authors present the general formalism for charge-changing quasielastic neutrino scattering from nuclei, defining the relevant nuclear response functions (R_CC, R_CL, R_LL, R_T, R_T') and the hadronic current. They then compute these observables using four distinct nuclear models: the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG), the Local Fermi Gas (LFG) with corrections for RPA correlations, Coulomb effects, and final state interactions (FSI), the Semi-relativistic Shell Model (SR-SM), and the Super-Scaling Analysis (SuSA) model. Results are shown for response functions, inclusive cross sections, integrated cross sections, angular distributions, and polarization observables across a range of kinematics.

## Key numbers

* Flux-averaged neutrino cross section from 12C for (nu_mu, mu-): LFG with Pauli+Q gives 20.7 x 10^-40 cm^2; LFG with RPA and Coulomb corrections gives 11.9 x 10^-40 cm^2; experimental LSND value is 10.6 +/- 0.3 +/- 1.8 x 10^-40 cm^2.
* Flux-averaged neutrino cross section from 12C for (e, nu_e): LFG with Pauli+Q gives 0.19 x 10^-40 cm^2; LFG with RPA and Coulomb corrections gives 0.14 x 10^-40 cm^2; experimental LSND value is 0.15 +/- 0.01 +/- 0.01 x 10^-40 cm^2.
* Theoretical uncertainties on integrated cross sections in the LFG model are of the order of 10-15%, based on a Monte Carlo simulation with 10% uncertainties in the real part of the nucleon self-energy and densities.
* The weak coupling constant used is G = 1.1664 x 10^-5 GeV^-2, and the Cabibbo angle is cos(theta_c) = 0.974.

## Context

This review extends the work of Nieves (2004) on the Local Fermi Gas model and Amaro (2005) on the Super-Scaling Analysis, which connects electron and neutrino cross sections through an experimental scaling function extracted from (e,e') data by Maieron (2002). The paper enables future development of more precise nuclear models by showing that neutrino cross sections incorporate richer information on nuclear structure and interactions than electron cross sections, and that theoretical uncertainties partially cancel in ratios like sigma(mu)/sigma(e) relevant for atmospheric neutrino experiments.
