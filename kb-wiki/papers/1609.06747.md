# Neutrino Astrophysics

> We summarize the progress in neutrino astrophysics and emphasize open issues in our understanding of neutrino flavor conversion in media. We discuss solar neutrinos, core-collapse supernova neutrinos and conclude with ultra-high energy neutrinos.

## Key claim

This lecture summary reviews the current understanding of neutrino flavor conversion in astrophysical media, emphasizing that while the solar neutrino deficit problem is solved by the MSW effect and oscillations, the role of neutrino self-interactions in core-collapse supernovae remains incompletely understood, with many open questions about the conditions for collective flavor modifications and their impact on neutrino spectra.

## Method

The paper presents a pedagogical review of neutrino astrophysics, covering solar neutrino measurements from Borexino, SNO, and KamLAND; the SN1987A neutrino observations from Kamiokande II, IMB, and Baksan; and the theoretical framework for neutrino flavor evolution using density matrices and mean-field equations derived from the BBGKY hierarchy. The author derives the Liouville-Von Neumann equations for neutrino and antineutrino density matrices, including extensions for wrong-helicity correlators and pair correlators, and discusses the "bulb" model and linearized stability analysis used to study collective neutrino oscillations in supernovae.

- Core-collapse supernova progenitor mass range: 9 to 300 solar masses

## Context

This review extends the work of Pontecorvo (1957) on neutrino oscillations, Wolfenstein (1977) and Mikheev & Smirnov (1986) on the MSW effect, and Pantaleone (1992) on neutrino self-interactions. It summarizes the resolution of the solar neutrino deficit problem by SNO (Ahmad, 2002) and KamLAND (Eguchi, 2002), and discusses open questions about the supernova explosion mechanism, the r-process nucleosynthesis site, and the role of collective neutrino oscillations in supernovae. The paper enables future work on the neutrino mass ordering, CP violation, and the detection of the relic supernova neutrino background by Super-Kamiokande with Gadolinium.
