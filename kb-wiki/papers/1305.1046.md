# High-pT suppression and elliptic flow from radiative energy loss with realistic bulk medium expansion

> We investigate nuclear suppression and elliptic flow in A+A reactions using Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) radiative energy loss with the covariant transport MPC for bulk medium evolution. At both RHIC and LHC energies, we find that inclusion of realistic transverse expansion for the medium strongly suppresses elliptic flow at high p_T compared to calculations with longitudinal Bjorken expansion only. We argue that this is a generic feature of GLV energy loss. Transverse expansion also enhances the high-p_T suppression, while fluctuations in energy loss with the rescattering location of the jet parton in the medium lead to weaker suppression and smaller elliptic flow. However, unlike the strong reduction of elliptic flow with transverse expansion, these latter effects get nearly washed out once calculations are adjusted to reproduce R_AA in central collisions.

## Key claim

Including realistic transverse expansion of the bulk medium in GLV radiative energy loss calculations strongly suppresses elliptic flow at high pT compared to calculations with only longitudinal Bjorken expansion, and this is argued to be a generic feature of GLV energy loss coming from interference bias against early rescattering in the medium.

## Method

The authors coupled Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) radiative energy loss for light partons to the covariant transport model MPC for bulk medium evolution, using the leading n=1 single-scattering term of the GLV gluon radiation spectrum with kinematic cutoffs. They compared four scenarios: transversely frozen (1D Bjorken) versus realistic 3D transverse expansion, and path-averaged (deterministic) energy loss versus stochastic energy loss that fluctuates with the rescattering location of the jet parton. Initial jet momentum distributions were taken from leading-order perturbative QCD with CTEQ5L parton distribution functions, and jets were fragmented independently using LO BKK95 fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with b = 8 fm, using fixed alpha_s = 0.29, transverse expansion reduces v2 by approximately 40% at high pT ~ 10-15 GeV compared to transversely frozen calculations, after alpha_s is tuned to reproduce R_AA in central collisions.
- For Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV with b = 8 fm, after alpha_s is adjusted to reproduce R_AA at pT ~ 8 GeV, v2 is reduced by more than half by pT = 5 GeV and by more than two-thirds above pT = 15 GeV due to transverse expansion.
- The ratio of LHC to RHIC effective couplings is about alpha_s^{LHC}/alpha_s^{RHIC} ≈ 0.8 for transversely static (1D) scenarios and about 0.75 with transversely expanding medium.
- In central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, tuning alpha_s to reproduce R_AA required approximately 10-20% higher alpha_s with fluctuating energy loss than with path-averaged energy loss, and approximately 10-15% lower alpha_s with transverse expansion than without it.

## Context

This work extends GLV energy loss calculations by Buzzatti and Gyulassy (CUJET1.0) and Horowitz (Horowitzv2) by including realistic 3D transverse expansion of the bulk medium, which those prior works omitted. The results suggest GLV energy loss will have difficulty simultaneously describing nuclear suppression and elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC, and the authors call for testing their findings with more full-fledged GLV implementations and other bulk evolution models such as hydrodynamics. The work provides a cross-check of the GLV framework in response to a recent PHENIX compilation highlighting the difficulty perturbative QCD energy loss frameworks have with reproducing R_AA(phi) at RHIC.
