# Heavy Quark Pair Energy Correlators: From Profiling Partonic Splittings to Probing Heavy-Flavor Fragmentation

> We introduce heavy-flavor energy correlators, $\langle Ψ|\mathcal{E}_{\mathcal{H}}(\vec{n}_1) \mathcal{E}_{\mathcal{H}}(\vec{n}_2)|Ψ\rangle$, as a powerful observable for profiling partonic splittings and characterizing heavy-flavor fragmentation. We present its collinear factorization, perform resummation, and demonstrate the angular distribution's sensitivity to both the heavy-quark pair splitting and their subsequent fragmentation. We then apply the heavy-flavor EEC to probe medium-induced effects, revealing its sensitivity to medium modifications to heavy-quark pair splitting functions and to the medium's spatial structure.

## Key claim

Heavy-flavor energy-energy correlators (EEC) can isolate the heavy-quark pair splitting function $P_{g\to Q\bar{Q}}^{(1)}$ within a jet without requiring flavor-sensitive jet algorithms, but the observable's sensitivity to the splitting function is conditional on the angular region where $2p_T\sqrt{z}\sim m_Q$, and the factorization relies on the collinear limit with resummation of logarithms.

## Method

The authors define a two-point heavy-flavor energy correlator $\langle \Psi | \mathcal{E}_{\mathcal{H}}(\vec{n}_1) \mathcal{E}_{\mathcal{H}}(\vec{n}_2) | \Psi \rangle$ that measures the energy flux carrying heavy-quark quantum number along two directions within a jet state. They derive a collinear factorization formula for the cumulant $\Sigma_{\mathcal{H}}(z, Q^2/\mu^2, \mu)$ in terms of hard functions $\vec{H}$ and jet functions $\vec{J}_{\mathcal{H}}$ in flavor space, perform renormalization group evolution to resum collinear logarithms, and apply the formalism to both vacuum and medium-modified splitting functions using a static brick approximation for the quark-gluon plasma with length $L=4$ fm and quenching parameter $\hat{q}=1.25$ GeV$^2$/fm.

## Key numbers

- Jet transverse momentum threshold: $p_T \ge 200$ GeV for vacuum calculations shown in Figure 1
- Medium length: $L = 4$ fm for static brick approximation
- Quenching parameter: $\hat{q} = 1.25$ GeV$^2$/fm for medium calculations
- Normalization reduction for $B^0$ subset relative to all $B$ hadrons: approximately 0.3, implying $\chi_b^{B^0} \approx \sqrt{0.2} \approx 0.45$
- Normalization reduction for $D^0$ subset relative to all $D$ hadrons: approximately 0.2, implying $\chi_c^{D^0} \approx \sqrt{0.3} \approx 0.55$
- $B^0$ production fraction from $e^+e^-$ colliders: $0.407 \pm 0.007$ (HFLAV 2016, ALEPH 2001)
- $D^0$ production fraction from HERA and $e^+e^-$ data: $0.544 \pm 0.022$ (ZEUS 2013, Gladilin 1999)
- Spectral index for energy loss prescription: $n=6$
- Cluster size for energy loss: $\theta_c = 2 / \sqrt{\hat{q} L^3}$

## Context

This work extends the energy correlator formalism developed for inclusive jets (Lee 2022, Komiske 2022, Chen 2020) and charged-hadron measurements (Jaarsma 2023, Lee 2023) to heavy-flavor hadrons, building on factorization derivations for EEC in lepton colliders and jet substructure (Dixon 2019, Lee 2022, Lee 2024, Lee 2025). The heavy-flavor EEC addresses the open problem of how parton splitting functions are modified by the quark-gluon plasma, complementing efforts to study heavy-flavor dynamics in jet substructure (Andres 2023, Chen 2024, Cunqueiro 2022, Craft 2022) and medium-induced effects (Armesto 2003, Sievert 2019, Zhang 2003). The observable enables tomographic studies of the QGP's spatial structure through anisotropic transport coefficients, extending prior work on jet evolution in anisotropic media (Barata 2024, Ipp 2020, Kuzmin 2023, Sadofyev 2021).
