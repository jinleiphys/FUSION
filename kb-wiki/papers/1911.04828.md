# Systematics of chemical freeze-out parameters in heavy-ion collision experiments

> We discuss systematic uncertainties in the chemical freeze-out parameters from the $χ^2$ analysis of hadron multiplicity ratios in the heavy-ion collision experiments. The systematics due to the choice of specific hadron ratios are found to lie within the experimental uncertainties. The variations obtained by removing the usual constraints on the conserved charges show similar behavior. The net charge to net baryon ratios in such unconstrained systems are commensurate with the expected value obtained from the protons and neutrons of the colliding nuclei up to the center of mass energies $\sim 40$ GeV. Beyond that the uncertainties in this ratio gradually increases, possibly indicating the reduction in baryon stopping.

## Key claim

The systematic uncertainties in chemical freeze-out parameters (temperature and chemical potentials) arising from the choice of different statistically independent sets of hadron yield ratios lie within the experimental uncertainties, and the variations obtained by removing the usual constraints on conserved charges (net baryon, charge, and strangeness) show similar behavior, indicating the system is close enough to chemical equilibrium that inclusion or exclusion of external constraints becomes irrelevant.

## Method

The authors performed chi-squared fits of hadron multiplicity ratios using the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model in the grand canonical ensemble, considering hadrons with mass up to 2 GeV. They generated multiple sets of statistically independent hadron yield ratios from the available experimental data (pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, Lambdas, anti-Lambdas, and Xis) at AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, and extracted freeze-out parameters both with and without imposing the usual constraints of fixed net charge to net baryon ratio and vanishing net strangeness.

## Key numbers

- The net charge to net baryon ratio in unconstrained systems is commensurate with the expected value from protons and neutrons of colliding nuclei up to center-of-mass energies of approximately 40 GeV; beyond that the uncertainties in this ratio gradually increase.
- For the ten hadron yields in SPS, RHIC, and LHC data, nine independent ratios were constructed from the 45 possible ratios (10 choose 2).
- For AGS data at sqrt(s) = 4.85 GeV, seven yield ratios were used (excluding those involving Xi).
- For lower AGS energies, five yield ratios were used from six hadron yields.
- The convergence criteria for the Broyden's method numerical solution was set to 10^{-6} or better.

## Context

This work extends the systematic analysis of chemical freeze-out parameters from earlier studies by Andronic et al. (2005), Becattini et al. (2005), and the STAR BES collaboration (2017), which typically used a single chosen set of hadron ratios or included a strangeness suppression factor gamma_s. The authors quantify systematic uncertainties by considering multiple independent sets of hadron yield ratios, and by removing the usual constraints on conserved charges entirely. This enables future studies to better understand the robustness of extracted freeze-out parameters and to explore whether the system at high energies (LHC) naturally satisfies charge conservation constraints without imposing them externally.
