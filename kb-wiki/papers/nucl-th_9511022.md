# Pion flow and antiflow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Within the framework of a relativistic transport model (ART) for heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies, we study the transverse flow of pions with respect to that of nucleons using two complementary approaches. It is found that in central collisions pions develop a weak flow as a result of the flow of baryon resonances from which they are produced. On the other hand, they have a weak antiflow in peripheral collisions due to the shadowing of spectators. Furthermore, it is shown that both pion flow and antiflow are dominated by those with large transverse momenta.

# Pion flow and antiflow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
In central collisions pions develop a weak flow in the same direction as nucleons due to the flow of baryon resonances from which they are produced, while in peripheral collisions they exhibit a weak antiflow due to spectator shadowing; both flow and antiflow are dominated by pions with large transverse momenta, and the transition from flow to antiflow occurs at an impact parameter of about 7 fm for Au+Au at 10.8 GeV/c per nucleon.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic transport model (ART 1.0) to simulate Au+Au collisions at AGS energies (beam momentum 10.8 GeV/c per nucleon). They analyzed pion transverse flow using two complementary approaches: the average transverse momentum in the reaction plane as a function of rapidity, and the ratio R(p_t) of pions emitted in the direction of nucleon flow versus opposite direction as a function of transverse momentum at fixed rapidity near the projectile rapidity.

## Key numbers
- Transition from pion flow to antiflow occurs at impact parameter of about 7 fm for Au+Au at p_beam/A = 10.8 GeV/c
- At t=20 fm/c, the multiplicity ratio of bound/free pions is about 40/420 at impact parameter 2 fm and 20/140 at impact parameter 8 fm
- For central collisions (b ≤ 4 fm), pions with transverse momenta larger than 0.2 GeV/c show flow in the same direction as nucleons
- For peripheral collisions (8 fm ≤ b ≤ 10 fm), pions with p_t ≤ 0.1 GeV/c are preferentially emitted in the direction of nucleon flow, while those with larger p_t show antiflow (R(p_t) ≤ 1)
- Analysis included particles with azimuthal angles smaller than 20 degrees with respect to the reaction plane and within rapidity range |y - 2.95| ≤ 0.35 (projectile rapidity is 3.1)

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies using RQMD and ARC that predicted pion antiflow for minimum biased Au+Au events but did not provide impact parameter dependence. It also builds on predictions at BEVALAC and SIS/GSI energies by Li (1991), Bass (1993), Li (1994), and Danielewicz (1995) where the transition from pion flow to antiflow was found at about 3 fm impact parameter. The larger transition impact parameter (7 fm) at AGS energies compared to SIS/GSI energies is attributed to spectators flying away with very large longitudinal momenta, reducing shadowing effects. The results are intended to guide experimental studies by the E877 collaboration and to probe in-medium cross sections of elementary processes involving pions and baryon resonances.
