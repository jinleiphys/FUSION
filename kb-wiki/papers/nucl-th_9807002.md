# Coherent Photoproduction of Eta-Mesons on Spin-Zero Nuclei in a Relativistic, Non-local Model

> The coherent photoproduction of $η$-mesons on spin-zero nuclei is studied in a relativistic, non-local model, which we have previously applied to the coherent photoproduction of pions. We find that different off-shell extrapolations of the elementary production operator lead to large effects in the cross section. We also show that the almost complete suppression of the N(1535) seen in earlier studies on this reaction is a result of the local or factorization approximation used in these works. Non-local effects can lead to a considerable contribution from this resonance. The relative size of the N(1535) contribution depends on the structure of the nucleus under consideration. We give an estimate for the contribution of the N(1520) resonance and discuss the effect of an $η$-nucleus optical potential

## Key claim

The almost complete suppression of the N(1535) resonance contribution to coherent eta photoproduction, seen in earlier studies, is an artifact of the local or factorization approximation; non-local effects can produce a considerable contribution from this resonance, with the relative size depending on the nuclear structure of the target.

## Method

The authors calculated coherent photoproduction of eta mesons on spin-zero nuclei (⁴He, ¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca) using a relativistic, non-local model in the impulse approximation, where the production operator is taken directly from Feynman diagrams without rewriting it via on-shell relations. Bound nucleon wave functions were obtained from a relativistic mean-field calculation with Woods-Saxon scalar and vector potentials, and the six-dimensional integrals arising from the non-local resonance propagator were evaluated in position space. Results were compared with the local (factorization) approximation used in prior works by Fix and by Piekarewicz, and the effects of three different eta-nucleus optical potentials were examined.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C at a photon laboratory energy of 650 MeV, the omega-graph cross section in the present model is about 30% below the result given by Fix.
- For ⁴⁰Ca at a photon laboratory energy of 700 MeV, the omega-graph cross section is about a factor of two lower than the result given by Piekarewicz.
- For ¹²C at 700 MeV, the cross section in Piekarewicz is larger than the present result by an order of magnitude.
- The N(1535) resonance width is taken as Γ₀ = 160 MeV, with branching ratios b_η = 0.5, b_π = 0.4, and b_ππ = 0.1.
- The ωNN form factor uses Λ² = 1.2 GeV².
- For one optical potential, the inelastic ηN cross section is taken as 30 mb, leading to the relation -2ω_η V_η = i 3 fm² p_η ρ.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic non-local model for coherent pion photoproduction (Peters, Lenske, Mosel, 1998) to eta mesons. It contradicts the findings of Fix (1996) and Piekarewicz (1997), who used local or factorization approximations and found strong suppression of the N(1535) contribution. The study enables future experimental extraction of the eta-nucleus optical potential, for which no data exist, by showing that coherent photoproduction can reveal the N(1535) contribution when non-local effects are properly included.
