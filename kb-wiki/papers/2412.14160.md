# $2νββ$ Spectrum in Chiral Effective Field Theory

> We investigate two-neutrino double beta decay ($2νββ$) in chiral effective field theory. We find contributions from weak magnetism and double-weak pion-exchange at next-to-leading-order in the chiral power counting. We discuss the impact of the chiral corrections on the electron spectra and find that they should be included in analyses of $2νββ$ decay that aim to uncover new physics signatures in the electron spectrum. We illustrate this point by revisiting the effect of sterile neutrinos and non-standard charged interactions. We also find that the pion-exchange contributions involve nuclear matrix elements that are related to those appearing in neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$). We investigate whether the $0νββ$ nuclear matrix elements can be obtained from detailed measurements of the energy spectrum of the outgoing electrons in $2νββ$ transitions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that chiral effective field theory corrections to the two-neutrino double beta decay spectrum, specifically from weak magnetism and double-weak pion-exchange, must be included in analyses that aim to uncover new physics signatures such as sterile neutrinos or non-standard interactions, but the pion-exchange contributions depend on unknown low-energy constants that currently prevent a complete numerical prediction.

## Method

The authors used chiral effective field theory to systematically organize corrections to the $2\nu\beta\beta$ amplitude in a double expansion in the small parameters $\epsilon_\chi = \mathcal O(k_F/\Lambda_\chi)$ and $\epsilon_\pi = \mathcal O(\mathcal Q/k_F)$, where $k_F \sim m_\pi \sim 100$ MeV and $\mathcal Q$ is the reaction Q-value. They computed the leading-order amplitude from double Gamow-Teller transitions, then added next-to-leading-order contributions from weak magnetism (diagram b) and from two-nucleon weak currents induced by pion exchange (diagrams c-e) along with the necessary short-range contact terms (diagram f). The nuclear matrix elements for the pion-exchange contributions were evaluated using both quasiparticle random phase approximation and nuclear shell model calculations for $^{76}$Ge and $^{136}$Xe.

## Key numbers

* Weak magnetism correction enters at $\mathcal O(\mathcal Q/\Lambda_\chi) \sim 10^{-3}$ relative to leading order
* The pion-exchange corrections scale as $\epsilon_F \sim \epsilon_{GT} = \mathcal O(m_e/(4\pi F_\pi)) = \mathcal O(m_e/\Lambda_\chi) \simeq 0.1\%$
* For $^{76}$Ge, the double Gamow-Teller matrix element $M_{GT}^{(-1)} = 0.069$ (QRPA) and 0.058 (shell model)
* For $^{136}$Xe, $M_{GT}^{(-1)} = 0.010$ (QRPA) and 0.013 (shell model)
* The ratio $M_{GT}(m_\pi)/M_{GT}(0)$ is 0.71 (QRPA) and 0.66 (shell model) for $^{76}$Ge, and 0.68 (both methods) for $^{136}$Xe
* The ratio $M_F(m_\pi)/M_F(0)$ is 0.67 (QRPA) and 0.71 (shell model) for $^{76}$Ge, and 0.63 (QRPA) and 0.70 (shell model) for $^{136}$Xe
* The weak magnetism coupling $g_M = 1+\kappa_1 = 4.7$

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory framework previously applied to neutrinoless double beta decay ($0\nu\beta\beta$) by Cirigliano et al. (2018) and others to the two-neutrino channel ($2\nu\beta\beta$). The pion-exchange contributions involve nuclear matrix elements closely related to those appearing in $0\nu\beta\beta$ induced by massive Majorana neutrino exchange, providing a potential pathway to extract $0\nu\beta\beta$ nuclear matrix elements from precise measurements of the $2\nu\beta\beta$ electron energy spectrum. This connection is more theoretically rigorous than phenomenological correlations studied by Shimizu et al. (2017), Yao et al. (2022), and Jokiniemi et al. (2023) between $0\nu\beta\beta$ matrix elements and other observables like double Gamow-Teller transitions or double-magnetic dipole transitions.
