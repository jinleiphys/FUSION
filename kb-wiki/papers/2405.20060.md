# Detailed nuclear structure calculations for coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering

> Any discovery of `new physics' in the neutrino sector first requires a precise prediction of the expected Standard Model cross section. Currently, Coherent Elastic neutrino-Nucleus Scattering (CEvNS) experiments are statistics limited. However, as new and future experiments scale up, it will be necessary to improve the theoretical predictions. Here we review the calculation of the CEvNS cross section in a consistent theory of hadronic currents and compute the relevant nuclear form factors using the nuclear shell model. The uncertainty on the form factors is explored by repeating the calculation for various shell model interactions and with Skyme-Hartree-Fock evaluations of the Weak-charge radii. We then refine the Standard Model predictions for the recent experimental results of the COHERENT experiment. We find that our cross sections are in good agreement with previous predictions, but with significantly smaller uncertainties - by up to a factor of 10. Near-future CEvNS experiments will meaningfully benefit from improved predictions through an increased sensitivity to new-physics signals.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear shell model calculations can reduce the theoretical uncertainty on CEvNS cross sections by up to a factor of 10 compared to previous predictions, but this improvement is conditional on future CEvNS experiments becoming systematics-limited rather than statistics-limited as they are currently.

## Method

The authors computed nuclear form factors for CEvNS using the nuclear shell model with NuShellX, evaluating the spin-independent weak form factors and spin-dependent axial form factors for argon, germanium, cesium, and iodine targets. They compared these results against form factors derived from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations using four different parametrizations (SkM*, SLy6, SV-min, UNEDF1) and against empirically derived form factors based on electric-charge radii with a conservative range for neutron radii. The full cross section was calculated using the formalism of PhysRevD.102.074018, which includes both vector and axial-vector hadronic currents.

## Key numbers

- The weak charge of the neutron is Q_w^n = -1.02352(25), and the flavor-dependent proton weak charges are Q_w^{p,nu_e} = 0.0747(34) and Q_w^{p,nu_mu} = 0.0582(34)
- The proton rms charge radii range from 3.4274(26) fm for Ar-40 to 4.8041(46) fm for Cs-133
- Skyrme-Hartree-Fock predictions give weak radii (for nu_e) ranging from 3.514(17) fm for Ar-40 to 4.957(9) fm for Cs-133
- The axial form factor contribution is a factor of at least 10^3 smaller than the spin-independent term
- The empirical neutron-proton radius difference is estimated as Delta r_np = 0.050 fm for Ge-70 and 0.15 fm for Xe-136

## Context

This work extends the formalism of PhysRevD.102.074018, which provided a full bottom-up calculation of CEvNS cross sections using effective field theory, by applying it to specific targets relevant to the COHERENT experiment. It also builds on Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations by VanDessel (2020) for carbon, oxygen, argon, iron, and lead, and on coupled cluster theory calculations by Payne (2019) for argon. The improved predictions will enable future CEvNS experiments to have increased sensitivity to new-physics signals, particularly as the COHERENT collaboration aims to reduce flux uncertainties from the current 10% level to 2-3%.
