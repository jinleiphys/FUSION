# Damping of giant resonances in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The giant collective modes in asymmetric nuclear matter are investigated within a dynamic relaxation time approximation. We derive a coupled dispersion relation and show that two sources of coupling appear: (i) a coupling of isoscalar and isovector modes due to different mean-fields acting and (ii) an explicit new coupling in asymmetric matter due to collisional interaction. We show that the latter one is responsible for a new mode arising besides isovector and isoscalar modes.

# Damping of giant resonances in asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in asymmetric nuclear matter, collisional interactions between neutrons and protons produce a new coupling term that gives rise to a third collective mode beyond the standard isoscalar and isovector giant resonances, but this mode only appears at extreme asymmetries and/or strong collisional coupling.

## Method
The authors derive a coupled dispersion relation for collective modes in asymmetric nuclear matter using a dynamic (non-Markovian) relaxation time approximation for the collision integral in coupled quantum kinetic equations. They linearize the kinetic equations for a two-component system of neutrons and protons, obtaining a generalized Mermin polarization function for multicomponent systems, and expand the dispersion relation in the long-wavelength limit appropriate for giant resonances in finite nuclei using the Steinwedel-Jensen model.

## Key numbers
- For the isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) in $^{208}$Pb: calculated energy = 21.0 MeV, calculated width = 3.4 MeV, compared to experimental values of 22.4 ± 0.5 MeV and 3.0 ± 0.5 MeV
- The wave vector for dipole modes: first order k = 2.08/R for the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR), second order k = 5.94/R for the ISGDR, with nuclear radius R = 1.13 A^{1/3}
- The model parameters reproduce the Weizsäcker formula with volume energy a1 = 15.68 MeV, Coulomb energy a3 = 0.717 MeV, and symmetry energy a4 = 28.1 MeV
- The dynamic relaxation time includes a memory correction: 1/τ_{ab}(ω) = 1/τ_{ab}(0) [1 + (3/4)(ω/πT)^2]
- The classical damping rate in the long-wavelength limit is γ = 1/(2τ), with full width at half maximum Γ = 2γ

## Context
This work extends the Mermin (1970) relaxation-time treatment of collective modes to asymmetric nuclear matter, building on prior studies of giant resonances by Grotz and collaborators (1996) and the authors' own earlier work (Morawetz, Fuhrmann, Walke 1998). The dispersion relation is similar to that of Colonna, Toro, and Lanza (1997) when collisional coupling is neglected. The results enable predictions for a new soft collective mode in asymmetric systems and suggest a transition from zero-sound to first-sound damping behavior for isovector modes that may be observable experimentally.
