# Superscaling in electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering

> The superscaling properties of electron scattering data are used to extract model-independent predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections.

## Key claim

Superscaling properties of electron scattering data can be used to extract model-independent predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections, but the method is limited to kinematic regions where superscaling holds (primarily at the left side of the quasielastic peak and the Delta peak), and the error from scaling violations is bound by data to be within at most 10%.

## Method

The inclusive electron-nucleus cross section is divided by a single-nucleon factor to obtain a scaling function that depends only on a scaling variable at high momentum transfer (above about 400 MeV/c). Two empirical scaling functions are extracted from electron data: one for the quasielastic peak region (f_QE) and one for the Delta resonance region (f_Delta). These scaling functions are then inserted into the relativistic Fermi gas formalism for charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering, replacing the RFG scaling function with the empirical ones.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer threshold for superscaling: q greater than or about 400 MeV/c
- Energy shift parameter E_shift: typically about 20 MeV, used to account for nucleon separation energy
- Typical deviations between reconstructed electron cross sections and data: 10% or less
- For 1 GeV neutrinos on carbon-12 at 45 degrees scattering angle, the RFG cross section differs significantly from the superscaling prediction, lying higher and extending over a narrower range in outgoing muon momentum
- At backward angles, the transverse and axial-transverse responses R_T and R_T' contribute less than 5% from R_CC, R_CL and R_LL combined, but interfere constructively for neutrinos and tend to cancel for antineutrinos
- For neutral-current reactions, inclusion of magnetic strangeness (mu_s = G_M^(s)(0)) and axial-vector strangeness (g_A^s = -0.2) produces visible effects on the cross section

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis of electron scattering data (previously established by Sick and collaborators) to neutrino-nucleus reactions, providing an alternative to microscopic models such as RPA, final state interactions, and Green's function methods that are often non-relativistic. The approach enables model-independent predictions for neutrino experiments in the few-GeV regime, where relativistic effects become important and consistent relativistic descriptions are difficult to achieve. The method has been applied to both charged-current and neutral-current reactions, and the neutral-current results show sensitivity to nucleon strangeness form factors, connecting to parity-violating electron scattering studies by Musolf (1993) and Donnelly (1991).
