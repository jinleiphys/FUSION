# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory with finite range pairing forces in coordinate space: Neutron halo in light nuclei

> The Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) model is applied in the self-consistent mean-field approximation to the description of the neutron halo in the mass region above the s-d shell. Pairing correlations and the coupling to particle continuum states are described by finite range two-body forces. Finite element methods are used in the coordinate space discretization of the coupled system of Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov integro-differential eigenvalue equations, and Klein-Gordon equations for the meson fields. Calculations are performed for the isotopic chains of Ne and C nuclei. We find evidence for the occurrence of neutron halo in heavier Ne isotopes. The properties of the 1f-2p orbitals near the Fermi level and the neutron pairing interaction play a crucial role in the formation of the halo. Our calculations display no evidence for the neutron halo phenomenon in C isotopes.

# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory with finite range pairing forces in coordinate space: Neutron halo in light nuclei

## Key claim
The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with finite range Gogny-type pairing forces predicts the formation of a multi-neutron halo in heavier Ne isotopes (N >= 20) but not in C isotopes, with the caveat that calculations assume spherical symmetry while several Ne isotopes are expected to be deformed.

## Method
The RHB equations were solved self-consistently in coordinate space using finite element methods for discretization, with the NL3 parameter set for the mean-field Lagrangian and the D1S parameter set for the finite range Gogny-type pairing interaction in the particle-particle channel. Calculations were performed for even-even Ne and C isotopic chains, solving the coupled system of Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov integro-differential eigenvalue equations and Klein-Gordon equations for the meson fields.

## Key numbers
- The last bound Ne isotope is $^{40}$Ne; for N >= 32 the neutron Fermi level becomes positive
- The last bound C isotope is $^{24}$C; C isotopes are not bound for N > 20
- Neutron radii of Ne isotopes follow the N$^{1/3}$ curve up to N approximately 22, then show a sharp increase
- For C isotopes, the 1f$_{7/2}$ level is still at approximately 4 MeV in the continuum at N = 20
- $^{28}$O is the last bound O isotope for the NL3 parameter set

## Context
This work extends the RHB model from previous applications to Sn and Pb isotopes using oscillator basis expansions (Gonzalez-Llarena et al., 1996) and from a coordinate-space RHB model with zero-range pairing forces applied to $^{11}$Li (Meng, 1996). The use of finite range Gogny-type pairing forces provides a more realistic description of pairing correlations without requiring a cut-off. The results enable future studies of the influence of proton number on neutron halo formation in the mass region above the s-d shell, with forthcoming calculations planned for N, O, F, Na, and Mg isotopes.
