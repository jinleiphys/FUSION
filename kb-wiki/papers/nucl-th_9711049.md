# $π^+$ Emission from Hypernuclei and the Weak $ΔI=3/2$ Transitions

> Low energy $π^+$ emission in hypernuclear weak decays is studied in the soft pion limit. It is found that the $π^+$ decay amplitude is dominated by the $ΔI=3/2$ part of nonmesonic weak decays according to the soft pion theorem. The ratios, $R(π^+/soft π^-) $, for light hypernuclei are estimated in the direct quark mechanism, which predicts violation of the $ΔI=1/2$ rule, and found to be about 1/3 for $^4_ΛHe$.

# π⁺ Emission from Hypernuclei and the Weak ΔI=3/2 Transitions

## Key claim

The π⁺ emission from hypernuclear weak decay is induced only by the ΔI=3/2 component of the weak Hamiltonian in the soft-pion limit, meaning that low-energy π⁺ emission provides a direct probe of ΔI=3/2 amplitudes in strangeness-changing weak interactions. The ratio R(π⁺/soft π⁻) for ⁴_ΛHe is estimated at about 1/3 using the direct quark mechanism, but this value cannot be directly compared with the observed ratio of π⁺ to all π⁻ because most observed π⁻ come from the one-body quasi-free Λ→pπ⁻ decay.

## Method

The soft-pion theorem was applied to the process Λp → nnπ⁺ in the limit of zero pion four-momentum, showing that the commutator [Q₅⁻, H_W] reduces to -[I⁻, H_W], which vanishes for the ΔI=1/2 part but yields a nonzero ΔI=3/2 contribution. The two-body matrix elements for π⁺ and π⁻ emission were expressed in terms of reduced matrix elements M₀, M₁, and M₂, with the ΔI=3/2 amplitudes (a₂, b₂, f₂) taken purely from the direct quark mechanism, while π and K exchanges contributed only to ΔI=1/2 amplitudes (a₁, b₁, f₁, c₀, d₀, e₀).

## Key numbers

- R(π⁺/soft π⁻) ≈ 0.34 for ⁴_ΛHe, calculated from the direct quark mechanism combined with one-pion and one-kaon exchange contributions
- The direct quark plus one-pion exchange (omitting kaon exchange) gives R(π⁺/soft π⁻) ≈ 0.31 for ⁴_ΛHe
- The observed ratio of π⁺ to all π⁻ from ⁴_ΛHe is about 5% from experimental data
- The calculated π⁺/π⁻ ratio is 0.35 for ⁵_ΛHe and 0.77 for ⁴_ΛH, though these are expected to be overestimates due to Pauli blocking and phase space effects
- The charge exchange mechanism (Λ→nπ⁰ followed by π⁰p→π⁺n) explains only 1.3% of the π⁺/π⁻ ratio according to Table II of reference CG
- The Σ⁺ decay mechanism gives at most 0.2% for the π⁺/π⁻ ratio

## Context

This work extends the direct quark mechanism approach previously developed by Oka and collaborators (ITO, IOMI) for non-mesonic weak decays of hypernuclei, which had shown that ΔI=3/2 components give sizable contributions to J=0 transition amplitudes. The paper resolves a long-standing puzzle about π⁺ emission from ⁴_ΛHe by showing that previous mechanisms, the charge exchange process studied by Dalitz and collaborators (DH, CG) and the Σ⁺ mixing mechanism (GT), fail because they are ΔI=1/2 processes that vanish in the soft-pion limit. The result enables future experimental measurements of low-energy π⁺ spectra to directly determine ΔI=3/2 weak amplitudes, and motivates consistent chiral perturbation theory calculations (currently underway by Seno) that include all diagrams of the same order.
