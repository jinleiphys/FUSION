# The electromagnetic Nucleon to Delta transition in Chiral Effective Field Theory

> We present a calculation of the three complex form factors parametrizing the nucleon to Delta transition matrix element in the framework of chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta degrees of freedom. The interplay between short and long range physics is discussed and estimates for systematic uncertainties due to higher order effect are given.

## Key claim

The leading-one-loop chiral effective field theory calculation of the three complex form factors parametrizing the nucleon to Delta electromagnetic transition is trustworthy for momentum transfer \(Q^2\) smaller than 0.2 GeV\(^2\), but beyond that value higher order effects can play a decisive role; the quadrupole moment predictions are not in contradiction with most models due to uncertainties from the extraction of low energy constants.

## Method

The authors performed a calculation in the "small scale expansion" (SSE) framework of chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta degrees of freedom, working to order \(\epsilon^3\) (leading one loop). They extracted three free parameters from experimental data for the magnetic dipole form factor \(\mathcal{G}_M^*(Q^2)\) at \(Q^2 < 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) and the value of the electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole ratio (EMR) at the real photon point \(Q^2 = 0\). The calculation included short range contributions to the static limit of \(G_1\) and \(G_2\) and the radius of \(G_2\), while all other coupling constants were taken from other observables.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic dipole form factor at zero momentum transfer: \(\text{Re}[\mathcal{G}_M^*(0)] = 2.98\), with squared radius \(r_{M,\text{Re}}^2 = 0.627\) fm\(^2\).
- Electric quadrupole form factor at zero momentum transfer: \(\text{Re}[\mathcal{G}_E^*(0)] = 0.0441\), with squared radius \(r_{E,\text{Re}}^2 = -0.836\) fm\(^2\).
- Coulomb quadrupole form factor at zero momentum transfer: \(\text{Re}[\mathcal{G}_C^*(0)] = 1.10\), with squared radius \(r_{C,\text{Re}}^2 = -0.729\) fm\(^2\).
- Form factor \(G_1\) at zero momentum transfer: \(\text{Re}[G_1(0)] = 4.95\), with squared radius \(r_{1,\text{Re}}^2 = 0.679\) fm\(^2\).
- Form factor \(G_2\) at zero momentum transfer: \(\text{Re}[G_2(0)] = 5.85\), with squared radius \(r_{2,\text{Re}}^2 = 3.15\) fm\(^2\).
- Form factor \(G_3\) at zero momentum transfer: \(\text{Re}[G_3(0)] = -2.28\), with squared radius \(r_{3,\text{Re}}^2 = 3.39\) fm\(^2\).
- The real part of the magnetic dipole form factor at zero momentum transfer separates into pion cloud contribution \(-1.06\) and short distance contribution \(+4.04\) at renormalization scale \(\lambda = 1\) GeV.
- The real part of the electric quadrupole form factor at zero momentum transfer separates into pion cloud contribution \(+0.155\) and short distance contribution \(-0.110\) at \(\lambda = 1\) GeV.
- The real part of the Coulomb quadrupole form factor at zero momentum transfer separates into pion cloud contribution \(+1.47\) and short distance contribution \(-0.365\) at \(\lambda = 1\) GeV.
- The calculation is valid for momentum transfer \(Q^2 < 0.2\) GeV\(^2\); beyond this value higher order effects become decisive.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory treatment of the nucleon to Delta transition by using the "small scale expansion" (SSE) scheme, which includes explicit Delta degrees of freedom and counts the pion mass, momentum transfer, and Delta-nucleon mass splitting as small parameters of the same order. It contrasts with the calculation by Pascalutsa and Vanderhaeghen (2005), who used the \(\delta\)-expansion scheme and a covariant (relativistic) approach, leading to different estimates of diagram relevance and different treatment of short range physics. The results enable predictions for the electric and Coulomb quadrupole ratios (EMR and CMR) at low momentum transfer that can be compared with experimental data from MAMI, OOPS, and other facilities, and with model calculations such as Sato-Lee (2001) and the DMT/MAID models.
