# $Λ_b\to p$ transition form factors in perturbative QCD

> We reanalyze the $Λ_b\to p$ transition form factors in the perturbative QCD (PQCD) approach by including higher-twist light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) of a $Λ_b$ baryon and a proton. The previous PQCD evaluation performed decades ago with only the leading-twist $Λ_b$ baryon and proton LCDAs gave the form factors, which are two orders of magnitude smaller than indicated by experimental data. We find that the twist-4 $Λ_b$ baryon LCDAs and the twist-4 and -5 proton LCDAs contribute dominantly, and the enhanced form factors become consistent with those from lattice QCD and other nonperturbative methods. The estimated branching ratios of the semileptonic decays $Λ_b\to p\ell\barν_\ell$ and the hadronic decay $Λ_b\to pπ$ are also close to the data. It implies that the $b$ quark mass is not really heavy enough, and higher-power contributions play a crucial role, similar to the observation made in analyses of $B$ meson transition form factors. With the formalism established in this work, we are ready to study various exclusive heavy baryon decays systematically in the PQCD approach.

## Key claim

The authors show that the previously calculated $\Lambda_b\to p$ transition form factors in the perturbative QCD (PQCD) approach, which were two orders of magnitude smaller than experimental data, become consistent with lattice QCD and other nonperturbative methods once higher-twist light-cone distribution amplitudes (LCDAs) are included, specifically the twist-4 $\Lambda_b$ baryon LCDAs and the twist-4 and -5 proton LCDAs.

## Method

The authors computed the $\Lambda_b\to p$ transition form factors in the PQCD approach based on $k_T$ factorization, including $\Lambda_b$ baryon LCDAs up to twist 4 and proton LCDAs up to twist 6. They derived the hard kernels from the leading-order Feynman diagrams involving two hard gluon exchanges, applied Sudakov factors from $k_T$ resummation to suppress long-distance contributions, and performed convolutions of the hard kernels with the baryon wave functions. The form factors at large recoil were then extrapolated to the full kinematic range to estimate branching ratios of semileptonic decays $\Lambda_b\to p\ell\bar{\nu}_\ell$ and the hadronic decay $\Lambda_b\to p\pi$.

## Key numbers

* The previous PQCD leading-twist result gave the $\Lambda_b\to p$ form factor at maximal recoil $f_1 = (2.2^{+0.8}_{-0.5})\times10^{-3}$ (Lu, 2009) and $2.3\times10^{-3}$ (Shih, 1998), while lattice QCD yielded $f_1 = 0.22\pm0.08$ (Detmold, 2015), a discrepancy of about two orders of magnitude.
* The $\Lambda_b$ baryon LCDA normalization constant used is $f_{\Lambda_b} = 0.021\pm0.004$ GeV$^3$, consistent with $f_{\Lambda_b} = 0.022\pm0.001$ GeV$^3$ from leading-order sum rule calculations (Groote, 1997).
* The SCET sum rule gave the $\Lambda_b\to\Lambda$ transition form factor $\xi_\Lambda = 0.38$ (Feldmann, 2011), compared to the leading-power SCET result $\xi_\Lambda = -0.012^{+0.009}_{-0.023}$ (Wang, 2011).

## Context

This work extends the earlier PQCD evaluation of $\Lambda_b\to p$ form factors by Lu (2009) and Shih (1998), which only considered leading-twist LCDAs and produced results far below experimental data. The authors resolve this long-standing discrepancy by including higher-twist contributions, analogous to the observation in $B$ meson transition form factors that the $b$ quark mass is not heavy enough for leading-power dominance. The formalism established here enables systematic PQCD studies of exclusive heavy baryon decays, including two-body hadronic decays where $W$-exchange and penguin-annihilation diagrams are important.
