# Chiral NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$ descriptions of nuclear multipole resonances within the random phase approximation

> We study nuclear multipole resonances in the framework of the random phase approximation using the chiral potential NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$. This potential includes two- and three-body terms that has been simultaneously optimized to low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering data and selected nuclear structure data. Our main foci have been the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole resonances of the closed-shell nuclei, $^4$He, $^{16,22,24}$O, and $^{40,48}$Ca. These resonance modes have been widely observed in experiment. In addition, we use a renormalized chiral potential $V_{\text{low-}k}$, based on the N$^3$LO two-body potential by Entem and Machleidt [Phys. Rev. \textbf{C68}, 041001 (2011)]. This introduces a dependency on the cutoff parameter used in the normalization procedure as reported in previous works by other groups. While NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$ can reasonably reproduce observed multipole resonances, it is not possible to find a single cutoff parameter for the $V_{\text{low-}k}$ potential that simultaneously describe the different types of resonance modes. The sensitivity to the cutoff parameter can be explained by missing induced three-body forces in the calculations. Our results for neutron-rich $^{22,24}$O show a mixing nature of isoscalar and isovector resonances in the dipole channel at low energies. We predict that $^{22}$O and $^{24}$O have low-energy isoscalar quadrupole resonances at energies lower than 5 MeV.

## Key claim

While the chiral NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$ potential can reasonably reproduce observed multipole resonances in closed-shell nuclei, it is not possible to find a single cutoff parameter for the renormalized $V_{\text{low-}k}$ potential (based on the N$^3$LO two-body potential) that simultaneously describes the different types of resonance modes, with the sensitivity to the cutoff parameter explained by missing induced three-body forces.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations followed by random phase approximation (RPA) calculations using the chiral NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$ potential, which includes two- and three-body terms optimized to low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering data and selected nuclear structure data. The three-nucleon force was included fully in the HF calculations but only through normal-ordered zero-, one-, and two-body terms in the RPA calculations. For comparison, they also performed HF-RPA calculations with a chiral N$^3$LO two-body potential renormalized by the $V_{\text{low-}k}$ technique at different low-momentum cutoff parameters.

## Key numbers

- For $^{22}$O, the strength below 15 MeV exhausts about 4.3% of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule, while for $^{24}$O it exhausts 10.7% (experimental data for $^{22}$O give about 8% of the classical TRK sum rule up to 15 MeV).
- The maximum energy of the spurious $1^-$ state in $^{48}$Ca is 0.8 MeV, well separated from the lowest resonance peak at approximately 11 MeV.
- For $^{22}$O at 10.8 MeV, the main wave function components are neutron excitations: 62% $0d_{5/2}\rightarrow 1p_{3/2}$, 15% $0p_{1/2}\rightarrow 1s_{1/2}$, and 8% $0d_{5/2}\rightarrow 0f_{7/2}$.
- For $^{24}$O at 8.7 MeV, the main components are neutron excitations: 87% $1s_{1/2}\rightarrow 1p_{3/2}$ and 5% $0d_{5/2}\rightarrow 0f_{7/2}$.
- The RPA calculations with NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$ show small discrepancies of approximately 1-4 MeV between calculated and experimental centroid energies for isoscalar giant monopole resonances.

## Context

This work extends previous RPA studies of nuclear multipole resonances using realistic forces, such as those using the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) with the AV18 potential by Paar (2006) and Papakonstantinou (2007), and coupled-cluster calculations with chiral forces by the Oak Ridge group. The study enables systematic comparisons between chiral potentials that include explicit three-body forces (NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$) and renormalized two-body potentials ($V_{\text{low-}k}$), demonstrating that induced three-body forces are necessary when using softened potentials. The predictions for low-energy isoscalar quadrupole resonances below 5 MeV in $^{22}$O and $^{24}$O provide targets for future experimental searches.
