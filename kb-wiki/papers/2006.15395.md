# A Dyson-Schwinger model beyond isospin limit prepared for investigating U_A(1)-breaking temperature dependence

> Motivated by our earlier findings of sensitive quark-flavor dependence of QCD topological susceptibility on products of current quark masses and corresponding condensates, we allow the breaking of isospin symmetry. For the purpose of future investigations of U_A(1) symmetry breaking and restoration at T > 0, we perform (at T = 0) refitting of the quark-mass parameters of a phenomenologically successful effective model of low-energy QCD. It belongs to the class of separable-interaction models within the Dyson-Schwinger approach to the quark-antiquark substructure of mesons.

## Key claim

The authors refit the quark-mass parameters of a Dyson-Schwinger separable-interaction model to allow isospin breaking ($m_u \neq m_d$), not to improve zero-temperature observables but because the harmonic-average dependence of the topological susceptibility on $m_q \langle \bar{q}q \rangle$ makes the $U_A(1)$ anomaly contribution at $T>0$ potentially sensitive to the lightest flavor, so that reducing $m_u$ below $m_d$ may significantly affect the predicted temperature dependence of the $\eta'$-$\eta$ complex.

## Method

The authors take a rank-2 separable-interaction Dyson-Schwinger model (Blaschke et al., 2000) with fixed interaction parameters and vary the three current quark masses $m_u$, $m_d$, $m_s$ away from the isosymmetric values $m_u=m_d=5.49$ MeV, $m_s=115$ MeV. They solve the coupled gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations in rainbow-ladder approximation at $T=0$ to obtain the non-anomalous pseudoscalar bound-state masses and decay constants, then incorporate the $U_A(1)$ anomaly via a perturbative mass matrix (Witten-Veneziano-Shore formalism) to predict the physical $\pi^0$, $\eta$, and $\eta'$ masses. Two fits are performed: one to the experimental charged-pion and kaon masses (fit ${}^{(2)}$) and one to the FLAG (Aoki et al., 2014) QCD-only masses with electromagnetic contributions removed (fit ${}^{(1)}$).

## Key numbers

* Fit ${}^{(1)}$ (to QCD-only masses): $m_u = 3.40$ MeV, $m_d = 6.80$ MeV ($m_u/m_d = 0.50$), $m_s = 115.61$ MeV; yields $M_{\pi^\pm}^{\rm fit}=134.9$ MeV, $M_{K^\pm}^{\rm fit}=492.6$ MeV, $M_{K^0}^{\rm fit}=498.7$ MeV.
* Fit ${}^{(2)}$ (to experimental masses): $m_u = 4.37$ MeV, $m_d = 6.55$ MeV ($m_u/m_d = 0.67$), $m_s = 115.34$ MeV; yields $M_{\pi^\pm}^{\rm fit}=139.6$ MeV, $M_{K^\pm}^{\rm fit}=493.7$ MeV, $M_{K^0}^{\rm fit}=497.6$ MeV.
* Predicted $\eta'$ mass: 994.2 MeV (fit ${}^{(1)}$), 995.5 MeV (fit ${}^{(2)}$), compared to experimental 957.78 MeV.
* Predicted $\eta$ mass: 554.6 MeV (fit ${}^{(1)}$), 554.5 MeV (fit ${}^{(2)}$), compared to experimental 547.86 MeV.
* Predicted $\pi^0$ mass: 134.9 MeV (fit ${}^{(1)}$), 139.6 MeV (fit ${}^{(2)}$), compared to experimental 134.98 MeV.
* Topological susceptibility $\chi$ from fit ${}^{(1)}$: $(69.61\ {\rm MeV})^4$; from fit ${}^{(2)}$: $(72.22\ {\rm MeV})^4$.
* Chiral-limit condensate $\langle \bar{q}q\rangle_0 = (-217\ {\rm MeV})^3$ used for $\chi_0$ evaluation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Dyson-Schwinger studies of $U_A(1)$ breaking in the $\eta'$-$\eta$ complex at $T=0$ (Klabucar et al., 1997; Kekez et al., 2000, 2003, 2005; Horvatic et al., 2007; Benic et al., 2014) and at $T>0$ (Horvatic et al., 2007, 2010, 2018, 2019) by relaxing the isospin-symmetric limit $m_u=m_d$ that was used in all those prior works. The refitting is motivated by the finding (Horvatic et al., 2018) that the harmonic-average structure of the topological susceptibility makes the $U_A(1)$ anomaly contribution at finite temperature dominated by the lightest flavor, so that even small isospin breaking may matter. The resulting parametrization, especially fit ${}^{(1)}$ with $m_u/m_d=0.5$, is intended for future investigations of $U_A(1)$ restoration at $T>0$ and of the temperature dependence of the $\eta'$-$\eta$ complex and axion physics.
