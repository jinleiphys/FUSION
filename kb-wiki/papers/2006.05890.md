# Chiral theory of nucleons and pions in the presence of an external gravitational field

> We extend the standard second order effective chiral Lagrangian of pions and nucleons by considering the coupling to an external gravitational field. As an application we calculate one-loop corrections to the one-nucleon matrix element of the energy-momentum tensor to fourth order in chiral counting, and next-to-leading order tree-level amplitude of the pion-production in an external gravitational field. We discuss the relation of the obtained results to experimentally measurable observables. Our expressions for the chiral corrections to the nucleon gravitational form factors differ from those in the literature. That might require to revisit the chiral extrapolation of the lattice data on the nucleon gravitational form factors obtained in the past.

## Key claim

The authors extend the standard second-order effective chiral Lagrangian for pions and nucleons to include coupling to an external gravitational field, and their one-loop calculations of nucleon gravitational form factors yield expressions that differ from those in the heavy-baryon calculation of Diehl et al. (2006), which may require revisiting the chiral extrapolation of lattice data on nucleon gravitational form factors obtained in the past.

## Method

The authors couple the standard second-order chiral effective Lagrangian of pions and nucleons to an external gravitational field by introducing two new low-energy constants (c8 and c9) that depend explicitly on curvature characteristics of spacetime. They perform manifestly Lorentz-invariant one-loop calculations of the nucleon matrix element of the energy-momentum tensor up to fourth order in chiral counting, applying the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme to remove divergences and power-counting-violating contributions. They also calculate the tree-level amplitude of pion graviproduction in an external gravitational field to next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

* The D-term at zero momentum transfer expanded in pion mass: D(0)/m_N = c8 + (g_A^2)/(16π F^2) M_π + [(-3g_A^2/m_N + 2(-4c1 + c2 + 2c3))/(8π^2 F^2)] M_π^2 ln(M_π/m_N) + O(M_π^2)
* Slope of the D-form factor s_D contains a leading term -g_A^2 m_N/(40π F^2) * 1/M_π
* Large-distance asymptotic of the energy density: ρ_E(r) = (9 g_A^2)/(64π^2 F^2) * 1/r^6 + O(1/r^7) in the region 1/Λ_strong ≪ r ≪ 1/M_π
* Large-distance asymptotic of the shear force distribution: s(r) = (9 g_A^2)/(64π^2 F^2) * 1/r^6 + O(1/r^7)
* Large-distance asymptotic of the pressure distribution: p(r) = -(3 g_A^2)/(64π^2 F^2) * 1/r^6 + O(1/r^7)
* Large impact-parameter asymptotic of Belinfante-improved total angular momentum: ⟨J_Bel⟩(b_⊥) = g_A^2/(16π^3 F^2) * 1/b_⊥^4 + O(1/b_⊥^5)

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective Lagrangian approach for pions in curved spacetime developed by Donoghue (1991) to include nucleons, building on the standard chiral perturbation theory framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) and Fettes et al. (2000). The results for nucleon gravitational form factors differ from the heavy-baryon calculation of Diehl et al. (2006), with the authors reporting that the origin of this difference was clarified and the authors of Diehl et al. (2006) agreed with the present results. The derived effective Lagrangian can be applied to hadronic processes induced by gravity, including pion graviproduction accessible in hard exclusive processes, and the large-distance asymptotics can be used for analyzing lattice data on nucleon gravitational form factors and for deriving constraints on bound states in the hadro-quarkonium picture of exotic pentaquarks.
