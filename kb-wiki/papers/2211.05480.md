# Hydrodynamization in hybrid Bjorken flow attractors

> Hybrid fluid models, consisting of two sectors with more weakly and more strongly self-interacting degrees of freedom coupled consistently as in the semi-holographic framework, have been shown to exhibit an attractor surface for Bjorken flow. Retaining only the simple viscid fluid descriptions of both sectors, we find that, on the attractor surface, the hydrodynamization times of both subsectors decrease with increasing total energy density at the respective point of hydrodynamization following a conformal scaling, reach their minimum values, and subsequently rise rapidly. The minimum values are obtained when the respective energy densities are of the order of the inverse of the dimensionful inter-system coupling. Restricting to attractor curves which can be matched to glasma models at a time set by the saturation scale for both $p$-$p$ and Pb-Pb collisions, we find that the more weakly coupled sector hydrodynamizes much later, and the strongly coupled sector hydrodynamizes earlier in $p$-$p$ collisions, since the total energy densities at the respective hydrodynamization times of these sectors fall inside and outside of the conformal window. This holds true also for phenomenologically relevant solutions that are significantly away from the attractor surface at the time we match to glasma models.

## Key claim

In a hybrid fluid model with two conformal sectors coupled via effective metrics, the hydrodynamization times of both sectors on the attractor surface follow a conformal scaling when energy densities are much smaller than the inverse intersystem coupling, but rise rapidly outside this window; for phenomenologically relevant glasma-matched initial conditions, the more weakly coupled sector hydrodynamizes later in p-p collisions than in Pb-Pb collisions, while the strongly coupled sector hydrodynamizes earlier in p-p collisions, and these results hold even for solutions significantly away from the attractor surface at the matching time.

## Method

The authors consider two conformal fluids under Bjorken flow, each described by Muller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) viscous hydrodynamics, coupled via democratic effective metric couplings with a single dimensionful coupling constant gamma. The system reduces to four dynamical variables (energy densities and non-equilibrium pressures for each sector) plus six algebraic coupling equations for the effective metric components, which are solved numerically for given initial conditions. The attractor surface in the four-dimensional phase space is identified by evolving many initial conditions and finding the two-dimensional surface to which solutions converge, with curves on this surface parameterized by the ratio of energy densities at an early reference time tau_0 = 0.001 gamma^{1/4}.

## Key numbers

- The specific viscosities are set as C_eta = 10/(4pi) for the weakly coupled sector and tilde{C}_eta = 1/(4pi) for the strongly coupled sector, with relaxation times C_tau = 5 C_eta and tilde{C}_tau = (2-log 2)/(2pi)
- In the conformal window (energy densities much less than gamma^{-1}), the hydrodynamization time of the perturbative sector follows tau_hd approx 0.63 x (4pi C_eta) x E(tau_hd)^{-1/4}
- For the strongly coupled sector in the conformal window, the constant tilde{p} increases from 0.64 to 2.1 as the early-time energy density ratio (perturbative to holographic) increases from 1:1 to 1000:1
- The minimum hydrodynamization times for both sectors occur when their respective energy densities are of order gamma^{-1}, i.e., E(tau_hd), tilde{E}(tilde{tau}_hd) ~ gamma^{-1}
- The intersystem coupling parameter r = -gamma'/gamma is set to 2

## Context

This work extends the hybrid fluid attractor framework established in Mitra (2020) by systematically studying hydrodynamization times across the attractor surface, connecting to the phenomenology of small versus large collision systems. The bottom-up-like early-time behavior resembles the thermalization scenario of Baier (2000), and the glasma matching at times set by the saturation scale follows the approach of Kowalski (2007) using IPsat and bCGC models. The results provide a novel explanation for rapid hydrodynamization of softer degrees of freedom in small system collisions (high-multiplicity p-p or A-p collisions) compared to Pb-Pb collisions, suggesting that collective flow can emerge from softer components even in small systems.
