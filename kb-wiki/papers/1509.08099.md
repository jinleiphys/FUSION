# Role of the $f_1(1285)$ state in the $J/ψ\to ϕ\bar{K} K^*$ and $J/ψ\to ϕf_1(1285)$ decays

> We study the role of the $f_1(1285)$ resonance in the decays of $J/ψ\to ϕ\bar{K} K^*$ and $J/ψ\to ϕf_1(1285)$. The theoretical approach is based on the results of chiral unitary theory where the $f_1(1285)$ resonance is dynamically generated from the $K^* \bar{K} - c.c.$ interaction. In order to further test the dynamical nature of the $f_1(1285)$ state, we investigate the $J/ψ\to ϕ\bar{K} K^*$ decay close to the $\bar{K} K^*$ threshold and make predictions for the ratio of the invariant mass distributions of the $J/ψ\to ϕ\bar{K} K^*$ decay and the $J/ψ\to ϕf_1(1285)$ partial decay width with all the parameters of the mechanism fixed in previous studies. The results can be tested in future experiments and therefore offer new clues on the nature of the $f_1(1285)$ state.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the ratio \(R_{\Gamma} = d\Gamma_{J/\psi \to \phi \bar{K}K^*}/dM_{\rm inv}\) divided by \(\Gamma_{J/\psi \to \phi f_1(1285)}\) shows a clear threshold enhancement in the \(\bar{K}K^*\) invariant mass distribution, which would confirm the dynamically generated nature of the \(f_1(1285)\) resonance from the \(\bar{K}K^*\) interaction, but only if future experiments can measure this distribution, since the chiral unitary approach is only reliable within about 200–300 MeV above the \(\bar{K}K^*\) threshold.

## Method

The authors use the chiral unitary approach of Roca (2005), where the \(f_1(1285)\) resonance is dynamically generated by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the \(\bar{K}K^* \to \bar{K}K^*\) scattering amplitude. They compute the \(\bar{K}K^*\) invariant mass distribution for \(J/\psi \to \phi \bar{K}K^*\) and the partial decay width for \(J/\psi \to \phi f_1(1285)\), then form the ratio \(R_{\Gamma}\) to cancel the unknown bare production vertex \(V_P\). All parameters (cutoff \(q_{\rm max}\), coupling \(g_{f_1}\)) are fixed from previous studies, making the ratio a parameter-free prediction.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the \(f_1(1285)\) resonance: \(M_{f_1} = 1281.9 \pm 0.5\) MeV (from PDG).
- Total decay width of the \(f_1(1285)\): \(\Gamma_{f_1} = 24.2 \pm 1.1\) MeV (from PDG).
- Coupling of the \(f_1(1285)\) to \(\bar{K}K^*\): \(g_{f_1} = 7555\) MeV, obtained using a cutoff \(q_{\rm max} = 990\) MeV.
- Experimental branching ratio: \(Br(J/\psi \to \phi \bar{K} K^*) = (2.18 \pm 0.23) \times 10^{-3}\).
- Experimental branching ratio (re-derived): \(Br(J/\psi \to \phi f_1(1285)) = (6.00 \pm 3.16) \times 10^{-4}\).
- Ratio of experimental branching fractions: \(R = Br(J/\psi \to \phi \bar{K} K^*) / Br(J/\psi \to \phi f_1(1285)) = 3.6 \pm 2.0\).
- The \(\bar{K}K^*\) invariant mass range considered in the prediction extends from threshold up to about 300 MeV above threshold (i.e., up to approximately 1.7 GeV), though the kinematic limit is \(M_{J/\psi} - m_\phi = 2077\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary description of the \(f_1(1285)\) as a dynamically generated state from the \(\bar{K}K^*\) interaction, previously developed by Roca (2005) and applied to decays such as \(f_1(1285) \to \eta \pi^0 \pi^0\) and \(f_1(1285) \to \pi K \bar{K}\) by Aceti (2015). It also builds on the experimental measurements of \(J/\psi \to \phi f_1(1285)\) by the DM2 Collaboration (Falvard 1988, Jousset 1988) and \(J/\psi \to \phi \bar{K} K^*\) by the BES Collaboration (Ablikim 2007). The predicted ratio \(R_{\Gamma}\) can be tested at BESIII or BelleII, offering a direct experimental check of the molecular nature of the \(f_1(1285)\) versus a conventional \(q\bar{q}\) interpretation.
