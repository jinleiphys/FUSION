# Multiple scattering effects on inclusive particle production in the large-x regime

> We study the multiple scattering effects on inclusive particle production in p+A and $γ$+A collisions. Specifically, we concentrate on the region where the parton momentum fraction in the nucleus $x\sim {\cal O}(1)$ and incoherent multiple interactions are relevant. By taking into account both initial-state and final-state double scattering, we derive the nuclear size-enhanced power corrections to the differential cross section for single inclusive hadron production in p+A and $γ$+A reactions, and for prompt photon production in p+A reactions. We find that the final result can be written in a simple compact form in terms of four-parton correlation functions, in which the second-derivative, first-derivative and non-derivative terms of the correlation distributions share the same hard-scattering functions. We expect our result to be especially relevant for understanding the nuclear modification of particle production in the backward rapidity regions in p+A and e+A collisions.

## Key claim

The authors derive nuclear size-enhanced power corrections to inclusive particle production in p+A and γ+A collisions specifically in the regime where the parton momentum fraction in the nucleus x ∼ O(1), outside the small-x regime, where incoherent multiple interactions dominate and coherent effects such as dynamical shadowing are suppressed.

## Method

The authors employ the high-twist expansion approach within generalized QCD factorization to compute double scattering contributions as twist-4 power-suppressed corrections to the differential cross sections. They take the partonic subprocess qq' → qq' as an example to demonstrate the collinear expansion technique, integrating out momentum fractions using on-shell conditions and pole propagators, then performing a k⊥-expansion to second order. The method is extended to all relevant partonic channels for single inclusive hadron production in p+A collisions, prompt photon production in p+A collisions, and single hadron production in γ+A collisions.

## Context

This work extends the high-twist expansion approach previously applied to forward rapidity (small-x) p+A collisions by Qiu, Vitev, and collaborators (2003-2004), where coherent multiple scattering leads to dynamical shadowing. The authors instead focus on the x ∼ O(1) regime where incoherent double scattering dominates, relevant for backward rapidity regions in p+A and e+A collisions. The result enables phenomenological studies of the Cronin effect and nuclear modification of particle production in kinematic regions where the classical double scattering picture is preserved, with the compact form in terms of four-parton correlation functions providing a framework for future numerical calculations.
