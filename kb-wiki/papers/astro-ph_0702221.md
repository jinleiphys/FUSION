# Rotating Neutron Stars

> Because of the tremendous densities that exist in the cores of neutron stars, a significant fraction of the matter in the cores of such stars is likely to exist in the form of hyperons. Depending on spin frequency, the hyperon content changes dramatically in rotating neutron stars, as discussed in this paper.

## Key claim

The hyperon content of neutron star matter changes dramatically with spin frequency, but this conclusion depends on the specific equation of state and the relativistic many-body framework used (e.g., relativistic mean-field versus relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock), which can give different predictions for whether baryon resonances like Δ particles appear in stable stars.

## Method

The authors solve Einstein's field equations for rotating neutron stars using a metric that includes frame dragging, with the stellar structure computed from the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation for non-rotating stars and a more complex set of equations for rotating stars that accounts for rotational deformation and the Lense-Thirring effect. The equation of state is derived from relativistic nuclear field-theoretical models, specifically density-dependent relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DD-RBHF) calculations using Bonn and Groningen potentials, which determine the composition including nucleons, hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ), and in some cases quarks or Δ resonances.

## Key numbers

- The most rapidly rotating known neutron star, PSR J1748-2446ad, rotates at 1.39 ms (719 Hz); PSR B1937+21 rotates at 1.58 ms (633 Hz); PSR B1957+20 rotates at 1.61 ms (621 Hz).
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the Kepler period (mass-shedding limit) scatters around 1 ms for typical equations of state; strange quark matter stars can reach periods around 0.5 ms.
- For a CFL quark-hybrid star, the binding energy is 0.12 solar masses, leading to a mass-shedding period of 0.7 ms.
- For a very compact neutron star (CFL case), the local inertial frames at the star's center rotate at about half the star's rotational frequency (ω(r=0) ≈ Ω/2), dropping to about 15% at the star's equator.
- For ultrafast rotation at the Kepler frequency, mass increases up to about 20% depending on the equation of state; the ratio of polar to equatorial radius is around 2/3 except near the Kepler frequency.
- Ignoring interactions, Λ hyperons appear at densities of about 2 times nuclear matter density (n₀), corresponding to a neutron Fermi momentum of about 3 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron star structure and composition, particularly the work of Glen (1985) on hyperon populations and Weber (1999) on rotating neutron star models. The paper shows that rotation significantly alters hyperon thresholds and populations, which has implications for understanding pulsar glitch models (since the crustal fraction of the moment of inertia can be up to 50% smaller if the star contains CFL quark matter) and for interpreting observations of rapidly rotating pulsars like PSR J1748-2446ad discovered by Hessels (2006).
