# Relativistic mean field theory for deformed nuclei with pairing correlations

> We develop a relativistic mean field (RMF) description of deformed nuclei with the pairing correlations in the BCS approximation. The treatment of the pairing correlations for nuclei with the Fermi surface being close to the threshold of unbound states needs a special attention. To this end, we take the delta function interaction for the pairing interaction with the hope to pick up those states with the wave function being concentrated in the nuclear region and perform the standard BCS approximation for the single particle states generated by the RMF theory with deformation. We apply the RMF + BCS method to the Zr isotopes and obtain a good description of the binding energies and the nuclear radii of nuclei from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line.

# Relativistic Mean Field Theory for Deformed Nuclei with Pairing Correlations

## Key claim

The RMF+BCS method with a delta-function pairing interaction can describe deformed nuclei from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line, provided the harmonic oscillator basis expansion picks up continuum states whose wave functions are concentrated in the nuclear region; the method is justified for spherical nuclei and may also be effective for deformed nuclei, though the authors note that the harmonic oscillator basis gives relatively small neutron radii for giant halo nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic mean field (RMF) equations for axially deformed nuclei by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in a deformed harmonic oscillator basis, using the TMA parameter set with A-dependent coupling strengths. Pairing correlations are treated with the BCS approximation using a delta-function pairing interaction (strength V0 = 343.7 MeV fm^3) instead of the conventional constant pairing interaction, with the pairing matrix element computed from the single-particle wave functions to select continuum states localized in the nuclear region. The coupled Dirac equations, Klein-Gordon equations, gap equations, and particle number condition are solved self-consistently by iteration for Zr isotopes from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for Zr isotopes: the largest difference between RMF+BCS results and experimental values is less than 0.04 MeV
- Pairing strength: V0 = 343.7 MeV fm^3, obtained by reproducing the experimental proton pairing gap in 90Zr of 1.714 MeV with energy cutoff Emax - lambda <= 8.0 MeV
- TMA parameter set masses: M = 938.900 MeV, m_sigma = 519.151 MeV, m_omega = 781.950 MeV, m_rho = 768.100 MeV
- The drip-line nucleus for Zr isotopes is 140Zr, consistent with spherical RCHB predictions
- For 124Zr, specific resonant states include: 13/2+ [606] at E = 5.276 MeV with v^2 = 0.005; 9/2- [505] at E = 1.857 MeV with v^2 = 0.041; 7/2- [503] at E = 0.464 MeV with v^2 = 0.196; 5/2- [503] at E = 1.683 MeV with v^2 = 0.028
- Basis expansion used 14 oscillator shells for both fermion and boson fields, with hbar*omega_0 = 41*A^(-1/3) for fermions

## Context

This work extends the RMF+BCS approach of Yadav (2002) from spherical to deformed nuclei, building on the deformed RMF framework of Gambhir (1990) and the earlier systematic study of even-even nuclei by Hirata (1997) that included deformation but not pairing. It contrasts with the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) method of Meng (1996, 1998), which treats pairing more rigorously in coordinate space but is limited to spherical nuclei due to computational demands. The method enables systematic calculations of deformed nuclei across the entire nuclear chart, though the authors note that the harmonic oscillator basis underestimates neutron radii for giant halo nuclei compared to the spherical RCHB results of Meng (1998), and they identify improving the numerical method as a future goal.
