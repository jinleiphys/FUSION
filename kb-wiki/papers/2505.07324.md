# Angular momentum of glasma

> The earliest phase of an ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision can be described as a highly populated system of gluons called glasma. We study some glasma characteristics related to the system's angular momentum. The first one is the global angular momentum perpendicular to the reaction plane, which is spanned by the beam axis and impact parameter vector. We show that only a small fraction of the enormous initial angular momentum of the colliding ultrarelativistic nuclei is transferred to the glasma. Our main focus is the glasma angular momentum directed along the beam axis. This quantity has a local character and results from the inhomogeneous velocity field generated in the glasma. We calculate the vorticity and local angular momentum which show noticeably different behaviour. The results are analyzed in detail and discussed in the context of existing experimental data. We argue that neither vorticity nor thermal vorticity but instead the local angular momentum controls the polarization of final-state hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the local angular momentum of the glasma, rather than vorticity or thermal vorticity, controls the polarization of final-state hadrons; the authors show that only a small fraction of the enormous initial angular momentum of the colliding ultrarelativistic nuclei is transferred to the glasma, casting doubt on the idea that heavy-ion collisions produce a rapidly rotating quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors use a proper time expansion of the chromodynamic potentials in powers of the proper time tau, with the dimensionless parameter tau times the saturation scale Q_s as the small expansion parameter, to solve the Yang-Mills equations iteratively for the glasma fields. They compute the energy-momentum tensor from the chromodynamic fields averaged over colour configurations of the colliding nuclei using the Glasma Graph approximation, with the colour charge surface densities modeled as two-dimensional projections of Woods-Saxon distributions. The calculations are performed for SU(3) gauge group with g=1, saturation scale Q_s = 2 GeV, infrared cutoff m = 0.2 GeV, at mid-rapidity and tau = 0.06 fm, using a gradient expansion of the charge densities truncated at second order and restricting the transverse integration region to where the gradient expansion is valid.

## Key numbers

* The global angular momentum per unit rapidity -dL^y/deta is computed for Pb-Pb collisions at impact parameters up to about 6 fm, with results shown at sixth and eighth order in the proper time expansion; the angular momentum is found to be very small compared to the initial angular momentum of the colliding nuclei, which is of order 10^5 at maximum RHIC energies and larger at LHC energies.
* The saturation scale is set to Q_s = 2 GeV, the infrared cutoff to m = 0.2 GeV, and the proper time to tau = 0.06 fm for the main calculations.
* The nuclear radius for A=207 is R_A = 7.4 fm using r_0 = 1.25 fm and skin thickness a = 0.5 fm.
* The gradient expansion is trusted for distances from the center of the charge distribution less than about R_max = 6.5 fm, where the magnitude of the derivatives of the charge density are less than about 50 percent of the charge density itself.
* The local angular momentum L^z is expressed as a Fourier series with leading term proportional to tau * Delta^4 / 12, where Delta is the box size used for the local integration, and subleading term proportional to tau * Delta^6 / 120.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of papers (Carrington et al., 2020-2024) that showed various phenomena observed in final states of heavy-ion collisions originate in the glasma phase, including collective flow quantified by Fourier coefficients v_1, v_2, v_3. The paper addresses the "spin sign puzzle" where the sign of observed Lambda polarization along the beam direction contradicts the simple argument that polarization is generated by vorticity from elliptic flow gradients. The authors argue that neither vorticity nor thermal vorticity, but instead local angular momentum, controls hadron polarization, which contrasts with hydrodynamic and statistical models that require additional shear effects (Fu, 2021; Becattini, 2021; Alzhrani, 2022; Florkowski, 2019) to obtain the correct sign and magnitude. The results enable further study of spin polarization mechanisms in the pre-hydrodynamic glasma phase, particularly for interpreting recent CMS data on Lambda polarization in high-multiplicity p-Pb collisions at the LHC (CMS, 2025).
