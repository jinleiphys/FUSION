# Cold QCD at finite isospin density: confronting effective models with recent lattice data

> We compute the QCD equation of state for zero temperature and finite isospin density within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the mean field approximation, motivated by the recently obtained Lattice QCD results for a new class of compact stars: pion stars. We have considered both the commonly used Traditional cutoff Regularization Scheme and the Medium Separation Scheme, where in the latter purely vacuum contributions are separated in such a way that one is left with ultraviolet divergent momentum integrals depending only on vacuum quantities. We have also compared our results with the recent results from Lattice QCD and Chiral Perturbation Theory.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the mean field approximation, using either the Traditional cutoff Regularization Scheme or the Medium Separation Scheme, produces thermodynamic results for cold QCD at finite isospin density that are largely in agreement with recent lattice QCD data for pion stars, but the Medium Separation Scheme is more reliable at high isospin chemical potential (near the cutoff scale) because it properly separates vacuum divergent effects from medium contributions.

## Method

The authors computed the QCD equation of state at zero temperature and finite isospin chemical potential using the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the mean field approximation, solving the gap equations for the chiral condensate and pion condensate. They implemented two regularization schemes: the Traditional cutoff Regularization Scheme, which imposes a sharp three-momentum cutoff on all integrals, and the Medium Separation Scheme, which isolates all ultraviolet divergent integrals into pure vacuum contributions (at zero isospin chemical potential) and performs the remaining finite medium integrals over the full momentum range. The resulting thermodynamic quantities (pressure, isospin density, energy density, and equation of state) were compared with recent lattice QCD results from Brandt et al. (2018) and with chiral perturbation theory results from Adhikari et al. (2019) at both leading and next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass used for NJL parameter fitting: m_pi = 131.7 MeV (matching the lattice QCD value from Endrodi)
- NJL model parameters: current quark mass m = 4.76 MeV, cutoff Lambda = 659 MeV, coupling G = 4.78 GeV^{-2}, yielding vacuum mass M_0 = 303.5 MeV
- At mu_I ~ Lambda (approximately 5 m_pi), the difference in the pion condensate Delta between TRS and MSS reaches 30-35 MeV
- For the region m_pi < mu_I < 2 m_pi (where lattice data are available), the difference between TRS and MSS results is relatively small, with TRS showing infinitesimally better agreement with lattice QCD
- Chiral perturbation theory results used a pion mass of 135 MeV (Particle Data Group value) and sqrt(2) f_pi = 130.2 MeV, with uncertainties from low-energy constants

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies of finite isospin density (Frank 2003, Toublan 2003, He 2005, among others) by confronting them with the new improved lattice QCD results from Brandt et al. (2018) that use physical quark masses, motivated by the recently proposed pion stars scenario from Carignano et al. (2016) and Brandt et al. (2018). The Medium Separation Scheme, previously applied to color superconductivity by Farias et al. (2005) and to chiral imbalance by Farias et al. (2016) and Duarte et al. (2018), is shown to be more reliable than the traditional cutoff scheme for high isospin chemical potentials near the model cutoff. The results enable further investigation of the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon and isospin chemical potential (inaccessible to lattice QCD due to the sign problem) and exploration of pion condensation in the early universe at finite temperature.
