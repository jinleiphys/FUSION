# Photoproduction $γp \to K^{*+} Λ$ in a Reggeized model

> The high-precision differential cross-section data for the reaction $γp \to K^{*+}Λ$ are reanalyzed within a Regge-inspired effective Lagrangian approach. The model adopts Regge phenomenology to constrain the $t$-channel contributions from the $κ$, $K$, and $K^*$ exchanges. A minimal number of resonances in the $s$ channel are introduced in constructing the reaction amplitudes in order to describe the data. It is shown that the differential cross-section data for $γp \to K^{*+}Λ$ can be satisfactorily described by introducing the only $N(2060){5/2}^-$ resonance in the $s$ channel, which is quite different from our earlier work performed in an effective Lagrangian approach [A. C. Wang {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. C 96, 035206 (2017)], where the amplitudes are computed by evaluating Feynman diagrams and it is found that introducing at least one additional resonance apart from the $N(2060){5/2}^-$ is indispensable for reproducing the data. The roles of individual contributions from meson and baryon exchanges on the angular distributions are found to be highly model dependent. The extracted mass of $N(2060){5/2}^-$ turns out to be well determined, independent of how the $t$-channel amplitudes are constructed, whereas the width does not.

## Key claim

The high-precision differential cross-section data for $\gamma p \to K^{*+}\Lambda$ can be satisfactorily described by introducing only the $N(2060){5/2}^-$ resonance in the $s$ channel when using Reggeized or interpolated Regge amplitudes for the $t$-channel exchanges, which contrasts with the authors' earlier Feynman-diagram approach (Wang et al., Phys. Rev. C 96, 035206 (2017)) where at least one additional resonance was indispensable for reproducing the data.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the CLAS differential cross-section data for $\gamma p \to K^{*+}\Lambda$ using a Regge-inspired effective Lagrangian approach, replacing the $t$-channel Feynman propagators for $\kappa$, $K$, and $K^*$ exchanges with either standard Regge amplitudes or interpolated Regge amplitudes that smoothly transition from Feynman-type behavior at low energies to Regge-type behavior at high energies. The $s$-channel contributions included nucleon and nucleon-resonance exchanges, while $u$-channel hyperon exchanges ($\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Sigma^*(1385)$) and a generalized interaction current were also incorporated to maintain gauge invariance. A minimal number of $s$-channel resonances were introduced by testing candidate resonances near the $K^{*+}\Lambda$ threshold ($N(2000)5/2^+$, $N(2040)3/2^+$, $N(2060)5/2^-$, $N(2100)1/2^+$, $N(2150)3/2^-$, $N(2190)7/2^-$) and selecting those that produced acceptable fits.

## Key numbers

- The fitted mass of the $N(2060){5/2}^-$ resonance is $2033 \pm 4$ MeV in the Regge model and $2028 \pm 2$ MeV in the interpolated Regge model, consistent with the range $2009$–$2043$ MeV from the earlier Feynman model (Wang et al., 2017).
- The fitted width of the $N(2060){5/2}^-$ resonance is $124 \pm 7$ MeV in the Regge model and $57 \pm 2$ MeV in the interpolated Regge model, compared to $65 \pm 4$ MeV in the Feynman model.
- The reduced helicity amplitude $\sqrt{\beta_{\Lambda K^*}}A_{1/2}$ for $N(2060){5/2}^-$ is $(0.66 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ in the Regge model and $(0.84 \pm 0.08) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ in the interpolated Regge model.
- The reduced helicity amplitude $\sqrt{\beta_{\Lambda K^*}}A_{3/2}$ for $N(2060){5/2}^-$ is $(-0.93 \pm 0.14) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ in the Regge model and $(-1.19 \pm 0.11) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ in the interpolated Regge model.
- The cutoff parameter $\Lambda_K$ for the $K$ exchange is $1940 \pm 25$ MeV in the Regge model and $762 \pm 12$ MeV in the interpolated Regge model, compared to $1000 \pm 6$ MeV in the Feynman model.
- The interpolated Regge model parameters include $s_R = 4.95 \pm 0.07$ GeV$^2$, $s_0 = 0.66 \pm 0.06$ GeV$^2$, $t_R = 0.46 \pm 0.02$ GeV$^2$, and $t_0 = 0.25 \pm 0.01$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous effective Lagrangian analysis of $\gamma p \to K^{*+}\Lambda$ (Wang et al., Phys. Rev. C 96, 035206 (2017)), which used Feynman amplitudes for $t$-channel exchanges and required at least two $s$-channel resonances, by testing whether Reggeized or interpolated Regge $t$-channel amplitudes change the required resonance content. The study contrasts with earlier Regge models by Ozaki (2010) and Yu (2017) that included no nucleon resonances and could not describe near-threshold angular distributions, and with the isobar model analyses by Oh (2006) and Kim (2011, 2014). The finding that only the $N(2060){5/2}^-$ resonance is needed in the Reggeized approach, with a well-determined mass but model-dependent width, enables future coupled-channel analyses (e.g., Anisovich (2012), Ronchen (2013), Kamano (2015)) to better constrain this resonance's properties and clarifies how strongly the extracted resonance content depends on the treatment of $t$-channel background amplitudes.
