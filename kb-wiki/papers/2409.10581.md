# Nuclear-level effective theory of $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion: Inelastic process

> Mu2e and COMET will search for electrons produced via the neutrinoless conversion of stopped muons bound in 1s atomic orbits of $^{27}$Al, improving existing limits on charged lepton flavor violation (CLFV) by roughly four orders of magnitude. Conventionally, $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion experiments are optimized to detect electrons originating from transitions where the nucleus remains in the ground state, thereby maximizing the energy of the outgoing electron. Clearly, detection of a positive signal in forthcoming experiments would stimulate additional work $-$ including subsequent conversion experiments using complementary nuclear targets $-$ to further constrain the new physics responsible for CLFV. Here we argue that additional information can be extracted without the need for additional experiments, by considering inelastic conversion in $^{27}$Al. Transitions to low-lying nuclear excited states can modify the near-endpoint spectrum of conversion electrons, with the ratio of the elastic and inelastic responses being sensitive to the underlying CLFV operator. We extend the nuclear effective theory of $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion to the inelastic case, which adds five new response functions to the six that arise for the elastic process. We evaluate these nuclear response functions in $^{27}$Al and calculate the resulting conversion-electron signal, taking into account the resolution anticipated in Mu2e/COMET. We find that $^{27}$Al is an excellent target choice from the perspective of the new information that can be obtained from inelastic $μ\rightarrow e$ conversion.

## Key claim

The authors argue that additional information about the source of charged lepton flavor violation (CLFV) can be extracted from muon-to-electron conversion experiments without performing additional experiments on different nuclear targets, by instead studying inelastic transitions to low-lying excited states of the same target nucleus (Al-27), provided the ratio of elastic and inelastic responses is sensitive to the underlying CLFV operator.

## Method

The authors extend the non-relativistic effective theory (NRET) of elastic muon-to-electron conversion to the inelastic case, deriving the most general expression for the conversion rate to any final nuclear state. They evaluate the resulting 11 nuclear response functions (five new ones added to the six from the elastic process) for Al-27 using shell-model calculations, and compute the expected conversion-electron signal including the energy resolution anticipated at Mu2e and COMET. The leptonic fields are treated with an effective momentum approximation that accounts for Coulomb distortion, and the muon wave function is factorized from the nuclear transition density using an effective charge Zeff.

## Key numbers

* The current best limit on muon-to-electron conversion is R_mu e less than 7.0e-13 at 90% confidence level, obtained by SINDRUM II using a gold target.
* Mu2e and COMET will reach a single-event sensitivity of R_mu e less than or approximately 1e-17, improving existing limits by roughly four orders of magnitude.
* For Al-27, the elastic conversion electron momentum is 104.98 MeV and the effective momentum (including Coulomb distortion) is 110.81 MeV.
* The three low-lying excited states of Al-27 considered have excitation energies of 0.844 MeV (1/2+), 1.015 MeV (3/2+), and 2.212 MeV (7/2+), with corresponding electron momenta of 104.13, 103.96, and 102.77 MeV.
* The reduction factor R (ratio of squared effective muon density to point-nucleus density) for inelastic transitions in Al-27 is 0.604 plus or minus 0.014, compared to 0.657 for the elastic isoscalar monopole response.
* For Ti-48, the elastic conversion electron momentum is 104.28 MeV and the effective momentum is 112.43 MeV; the inelastic R value is 0.360 plus or minus 0.015 compared to 0.433 for the elastic charge response.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NRET formulation of elastic muon-to-electron conversion (Rule 2021, Haxton 2022) to the inelastic case, and complements the matching to quark-level weak effective theory (Haxton 2024). Prior studies of inelastic conversion (Weinberg and Feinberg 1959, Kosmas 1990-2001, Chiang 1993) focused on specific operators (charge or spin) and on the total inclusive response from all excited states, rather than on using resolved transitions to individual excited states as a diagnostic of the CLFV operator. The present work shows that Al-27, already selected as the target for Mu2e and COMET due to its suitability for pulsed-beam operation (muon capture lifetime greater than approximately 250 ns, restricting Z less than or approximately 25), is also an excellent choice for inelastic studies because its low-lying states are sufficiently separated to be distinguishable given expected experimental resolutions.
