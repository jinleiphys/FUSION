# The role of $N^*(2120)$ nucleon resonance in $KΛ(1520)$ photon and hadronic productions

> The associate $KΛ(1520)$ photon and hadronic production in the $γp \to K^+Λ(1520)$, $p p \to p K^+ Λ(1520)$ and $π^- p \to K^0 Λ(1520)$ reactions are investigated within the effective Lagrangian approach and the isobar model. We are interested in the contribution from the $N^*(2120)$ (previously called $N^*(2080)$) resonance, which has a significant coupling to the $KΛ(1520)$ channel. The theoretical results show that the current experimental data for the $γp \to K^+Λ(1520)$ reaction favor the existence of the $N^*(2120)$ resonance, and that these measurements can be used to further constrain its properties. We present results, including the $N^*(2120)$ contribution, for total cross sections of the $γp \to K^+Λ(1520)$, $π^- p \to K^0 Λ(1520)$, and $p p \to p K^+ Λ(1520)$ reactions. For this latter one, we also calculate invariant mass and Dalitz plot distributions.

## Key claim

The current experimental data for the γp → K⁺Λ(1520) reaction favor the existence of the N*(2120) resonance, but the paper hedges that this conclusion depends on the resonance having a significant coupling to the KΛ(1520) channel and that the measurements can be used to further constrain its properties rather than definitively prove its existence.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach and the isobar model to calculate total and differential cross sections for γp → K⁺Λ(1520), π⁻p → K⁰Λ(1520), and pp → pK⁺Λ(1520) reactions. They included contributions from t-channel K and K* exchange, s-channel nucleon pole, u-channel Λ(1115) and Σ⁺ hyperon poles, and the s-channel N*(2120) resonance, using parameters for the N*(2120)KΛ(1520) strong couplings obtained from the photoproduction reaction. For the pp reaction, they used a Monte Carlo multi-particle phase space integration program to compute total cross sections and invariant mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- For the pp → pK⁺Λ(1520) reaction at plab = 3.65 GeV, the predicted total cross section is 0.01 μb, which is 20 times smaller than the experimental upper limit of 0.2 μb measured by the COSY-ANKE Collaboration.
- For the pp → pK⁺Λ(1520) reaction at plab = 4.34 GeV (kinetic energy Tp = 3.5 GeV), the theoretical result is 11.5 μb, compared to the HADES measurement of 5.6 ± 1.1 ± 0.4 +1.1/-1.6 μb.
- The production threshold for the pp → pK⁺Λ(1520) reaction is plab = 3.59 GeV.
- The N*(2120) resonance is characterized as having spin-parity Jᴾ = 3/2⁻, a mass around 2.1 GeV, and a width of at least 200 MeV with a large partial decay width into Λ(1520)K.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of Λ(1520) photoproduction by Nam et al. (2010), Xie et al. (2013), and He et al. (2013), and provides a unified description of both LEPS and CLAS experimental data. The results support the identification of the N*(2120) resonance with the two-star PDG state and are consistent with constituent quark model predictions by Capstick and Roberts (1998). The calculations for hadronic production reactions provide predictions that can be tested by future experiments at DISTO and other facilities, and the authors note that including Λ(1520)p final-state-interaction effects could increase the pp cross section by up to a factor of 10 near threshold.
