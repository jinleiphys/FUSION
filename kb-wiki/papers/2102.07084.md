# Configuration-interaction approach to nuclear fission

> We propose a configuration-interaction (CI) representation to calculate induced nuclear fission with explicit inclusion of nucleon-nucleon interactions in the Hamiltonian. The framework is designed for easy modeling of schematic interactions but still permits a straightforward extension to realistic ones. As a first application, the model is applied to branching ratios between fission and capture in the decay modes of excited fissile nuclei. The ratios are compared with the Bohr-Wheeler transition-state theory to explore its domain of validity. The Bohr-Wheeler theory assumes that the rates are insensitive to the final-state scission dynamics; the insensitivity is rather easily achieved in the CI parameterizations. The CI modeling is also capable of reproducing the branching ratios of the transition-state hypothesis which is one of the key ingredients in the present-day theory of induced fission.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a configuration-interaction (CI) framework for induced fission that can reproduce the branching ratios predicted by Bohr-Wheeler transition-state theory, but only under specific conditions, namely, when the barrier region has either a long chain of Q-blocks or a severely truncated spectrum, and the authors note that the transition-state hypothesis still lacks microscopic justification under realistic Hamiltonians.

## Method

The authors construct a CI Hamiltonian built on reference states (Q-blocks) ordered by deformation Q₂, each generating quasiparticle excitations with a uniform orbital energy spacing d. They model the neutron-proton residual interaction as a Gaussian-distributed random matrix with strength vₙₚ = 0.05d, and couple adjacent Q-blocks via an off-diagonal interaction attenuated by a factor fₒₔ. The branching ratio between fission and capture is computed using the S-matrix formalism, with decay widths Γ_cap and Γ_fis added as imaginary terms to the Hamiltonian, and the ratio is averaged over an energy window.

## Key numbers

- The orbital energy spacing d in actinide nuclei is in the range 0.4–0.5 MeV.
- The strength of the neutron-proton interaction is taken as vₙₚ = 0.05d in most examples.
- For a three-block model with identical Q-blocks (k ≤ 4) and fₒₔ ≥ 1, the branching ratio matches the compound-nucleus limit B_cap,fis = Γ_fis/Γ_cap = 3.0 (with Γ_cap = 0.001d, Γ_fis = 0.003d).
- With fₒₔ = 0.5, the branching ratio is only 15% less than the compound-nucleus limit.
- For a three-block model with a barrier (E_gs = (0, 6d, 0)) and k ≤ 6, the branching ratio is sensitive to Γ_fis, varying between approximately 2 and 6 as Γ_fis changes from 0.1d to 0.3d.
- For a five-block model with a parabolic barrier (E_gs = (0.0, 4.5, 6.0, 4.5, 0.0)), the branching ratio becomes nearly independent of Γ_fis, staying near 1.0 for Γ_fis = 0.1d and 0.3d.
- Experimental reference values: Γ_cap ≈ 0.04 eV and B_cap,fis ≈ 3.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic treatment of fission dynamics beyond earlier diffusion-equation approaches (Barranco et al., 1990; Bernard et al., 1992; Cârjan and Bertsch, 1992) and provides the first CI-based test of the Bohr-Wheeler transition-state formula (Bohr and Wheeler, 1939; Bjørnholm and Lynn, 1980), which assumes insensitivity to final-state scission dynamics. The model enables future studies of barrier transmission factors using the Hill-Wheeler formula (Hill and Wheeler, 1953) and can be extended to include pairing interactions to explore their competition with neutron-proton interactions in barrier-crossing dynamics.
