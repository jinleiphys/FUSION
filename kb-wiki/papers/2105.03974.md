# Scale symmetry breaking, quantum anomalous energy and proton mass decomposition

> We study the anomalous scale symmetry breaking effects on the proton mass in QCD due to quantum fluctuations at ultraviolet scales. We confirm that a novel contribution naturally arises as a part of the proton mass, which we call the quantum anomalous energy (QAE). We discuss the QAE origins in both lattice and dimensional regularizations and demonstrate its role as a scheme-and-scale independent component in the mass decomposition. We further argue that QAE role in the proton mass resembles a dynamical Higgs mechanism, in which the anomalous scale symmetry breaking field generates mass scales through its vacuum condensate, as well as its static and dynamical responses to the valence quarks. We demonstrate some of our points in two simpler but closely related quantum field theories, namely the 1+1 dimensional non-linear sigma model in which QAE is non-perturbative and scheme-independent, and QED where the anomalous energy effect is perturbative calculable.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that a novel contribution to the proton mass, called the quantum anomalous energy (QAE), naturally arises from anomalous scale symmetry breaking due to quantum fluctuations at ultraviolet scales, and that this QAE is a scheme-and-scale independent component in the mass decomposition, analogous to a dynamical Higgs mechanism.

## Method

The authors derive the proton mass sum rule by studying Ward identities for the two-point correlation function under a temporal rescaling transformation, first in lattice regularization using the Wilson action for pure Yang-Mills theory, then generalizing to dimensional regularization. They analyze the renormalization properties of the QCD energy-momentum tensor (EMT) by decomposing it into traceless (tensor) and trace (scalar) parts according to the Lorentz group, and demonstrate the scheme independence of this decomposition. The results are illustrated in two simpler quantum field theories: the 1+1 dimensional O(N) non-linear sigma model in the large N limit, and QED where the anomalous energy effect is perturbatively calculable.

## Key numbers

- In pure Yang-Mills theory, the scalar part of the Hamiltonian contributes exactly 1/4 of the hadron mass, consistent with the virial theorem from Ji (1994)
- The relation for the lambda derivative of the bare coupling in lattice regularization is dg_0/dλ|_{λ=1} = (1/4)β(g_0), where β(g_0) is the bare beta function
- In the 1+1 dimensional O(N) non-linear sigma model, the quantum anomalous energy contributes exactly half of the mass of the pion-like bosons
- The Lamb shift in hydrogen receives a trace anomaly contribution at O(α^5)

## Context

This work extends the original proton mass decomposition by Ji (1994, 1995) which found that 1/4 of the proton mass can be attributed to the trace of the EMT, and addresses ongoing controversy about the anomalous term discussed by Metz (2020). The paper provides detailed derivations supporting the earlier short communication by Ji, Liu, and Schäfer (2021), and relates the QAE contribution to a dynamical Higgs mechanism where the glueball-proton coupling is proportional to the proton mass, consistent with the effective theory for the lowest glueball by Ellis (1984). The results enable unambiguous extraction of the QAE contribution from heavy-quarkonium electroproduction off a nucleon at threshold, as being pursued at Jefferson Lab and planned for the future Electron-Ion Collider.
