# Violation of Chandrasekhar Mass Limit: The Exciting Potential of Strongly Magnetized White Dwarfs

> We consider a relativistic, degenerate, electron gas under the influence of a strong magnetic field, which describes magnetized white dwarfs. Landau quantization changes the density of states available to the electrons, thus modifying the underlying equation of state. In the presence of very strong magnetic fields a maximum of either one, two or three Landau level(s) is/are occupied. We obtain the mass-radius relations for such white dwarfs and their detailed investigation leads us to propose the existence of white dwarfs having a mass ~2.3M_Sun, which overwhelmingly exceeds the Chandrasekhar mass limit.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that white dwarfs with interior magnetic fields greater than about 10¹⁵ G can have masses up to ~2.3 M☉, exceeding the Chandrasekhar limit of ~1.44 M☉, but only when the maximum Fermi energy of the system is sufficiently high (e.g., εFmax = 20 mₑc² or 200 mₑc²); for low Fermi energies (εFmax = 2 mₑc²) the Chandrasekhar limit is not exceeded regardless of central density.

## Method

The authors consider a relativistic, degenerate electron gas in a uniform, static magnetic field, where Landau quantization modifies the density of states. They restrict the system to one, two, or three occupied Landau levels (νₘ = 1, 2, or 3) by choosing appropriate combinations of magnetic field strength B and maximum Fermi energy εFmax. They numerically eliminate the Fermi momentum from the expressions for matter density and electron degeneracy pressure to obtain the equation of state, then solve the hydrostatic equilibrium equation with central density ρc as a boundary condition to compute mass-radius relations for magnetized white dwarfs.

## Key numbers

- Maximum white dwarf mass found: ~2.3 M☉, exceeding the Chandrasekhar limit of ~1.44 M☉.
- For a three-level system (νₘ = 3) with εFmax = 20 and B = 2.94 × 10¹⁵ G, the central density at maximum mass is ρc = 2.2 × 10⁹ gm/cc.
- The minimum magnetic field required to produce a 2.3 M☉ white dwarf is B = 2.94 × 10¹⁵ G.
- For a one-level system with εFmax = 20, the required magnetic field is B = 8.81 × 10¹⁵ G.
- The critical magnetic field Bc = mₑ²c³/ℏe = 4.414 × 10¹³ G.
- The original star of radius R☉ collapsing to a white dwarf of radius ~10⁸ cm with central field 2.94 × 10¹⁵ G would have had a surface field of ~6 × 10⁹ G based on flux freezing.

## Context

This work extends the classical result of Chandrasekhar (1931) on the maximum mass of non-magnetic white dwarfs by incorporating strong magnetic fields that cause Landau quantization. It builds on earlier models of magnetized white dwarfs by Ostriker and Hartwick (1968) and recent theoretical work by Kundu (2012), and is motivated by observations of peculiar Type Ia supernovae (SN2006gz, SN2007if, SN2009dc) that suggest super-Chandrasekhar mass progenitors. The results enable future studies of magnetized white dwarfs with realistic magnetic field profiles and anisotropic pressure effects, which could produce super-Chandrasekhar masses at even lower field strengths.
