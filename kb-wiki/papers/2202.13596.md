# Emergent geometry and duality in the carbon nucleus

> The carbon atom provides the backbone for the complex organic chemistry composing the building blocks of life. The physics of the carbon nucleus in its predominant isotope, $^{12}$C, is similarly full of multifaceted complexity. Some nuclear states of $^{12}$C can be preferentially treated as a collection of independent particles held by the mean field of the nucleus, while other states behave more as a collection of three alpha-particle clusters. But these two pictures are not mutually exclusive, and some states can be described in either fashion$^{1,2}$. In this work, we provide the first model-independent tomographic scan of the three-dimensional geometry of the nuclear states of $^{12}$C using the {\it ab initio} framework of nuclear lattice effective field theory. We find that the well-known but enigmatic Hoyle state is composed of a "bent-arm" or obtuse triangular arrangement of alpha clusters. We identify all of the low-lying nuclear states of $^{12}$C as having an intrinsic shape composed of three alpha clusters forming either an equilateral triangle or an obtuse triangle. From these basic structural formations, the various nuclear states correspond to different rotational and vibrational excitations as well as either distortions or large-amplitude displacements of the alpha clusters. The states with the equilateral triangle formation also have a dual description in terms of particle-hole excitations in the mean-field picture. We compare our theoretical calculations with experimental data for binding energies, quadrupole moments, electromagnetic transitions, charge densities, and form factors. The overall agreement is good, and further studies using higher-fidelity interactions are planned.

# Emergent geometry and duality in the carbon nucleus

## Key claim
Using ab initio nuclear lattice effective field theory, the authors provide the first model-independent tomographic scan of the three-dimensional geometry of nuclear states in ¹²C, finding that all low-lying states have an intrinsic shape composed of three alpha clusters forming either an equilateral or an obtuse triangle. The Hoyle state is identified as an obtuse isosceles triangular arrangement of alpha clusters, and states with equilateral triangle formation have a dual description in terms of particle-hole excitations in the mean-field picture, though this duality does not hold for the obtuse triangle states.

## Method
The authors performed unconstrained auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations using nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) on a spatial lattice with spacing 1.64 fm and temporal spacing 0.55 fm/c, with the center of mass constrained to a finer grid of spacing 0.137 fm. They used a spin-independent and isospin-independent interaction with two-body and three-body contact terms, with parameters fitted to ground-state energies of ⁴He and ¹²C, the ground-state charge radius of ¹²C, and several electromagnetic transition rates. The pinhole algorithm was used to determine probability distributions for nucleon positions, spins, and isospins, and alpha clusters were identified by locating the closest spin-down proton, spin-up neutron, and spin-down neutron for each spin-up proton.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy of ¹²C: -91.6(1) MeV (NLEFT) compared to experimental -92.2 MeV
- Hoyle state (0₂⁺) energy: -85.7(1) MeV (NLEFT) compared to experimental -84.5 MeV
- Charge radius of ground state: 2.54(1) fm (NLEFT) compared to experimental 2.47(2) fm
- Point radius of Hoyle state: 3.45(2) fm (NLEFT)
- Point radius of 0₃⁺ state: 3.47(1) fm (NLEFT)
- Electric quadrupole moment Q(2₁⁺): 6.8(3)(1.2) e·fm² (NLEFT) compared to experimental 8.1(2.3) e·fm²
- Electric quadrupole moment Q(2₂⁺): -35(1)(1) e·fm² (NLEFT)
- B(E2, 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺): 11.4(1)(4.3) e²·fm⁴ (NLEFT) compared to experimental 7.9(4) e²·fm⁴
- RMS matter radii of identified alpha clusters: 1.57 fm to 1.62 fm for low-lying states, close to the isolated alpha matter radius of 1.63 fm
- Spatial lattice spacing: 1.64 fm; temporal lattice spacing: 0.55 fm/c; tomographic resolution: 0.137 fm

## Context
This work extends previous NLEFT studies of ¹²C (Shen 2021) and provides the first model-independent determination of intrinsic cluster geometries, resolving long-standing debates about the Hoyle state structure. The results contradict recent suggestions by Li (2022) that the Hoyle state and 0₃⁺ state have equilateral triangular shapes, and instead support older NLEFT findings (Epelbaum 2011, 2012) of an obtuse triangular Hoyle state. The identification of rotational bands built on equilateral and obtuse triangular configurations is consistent with group theoretical considerations (Marin-Lambarri 2014). The duality between shell model and cluster descriptions for equilateral triangle states connects to earlier work by Kanada-Enyo (2006, 2018). Future studies using higher-fidelity chiral effective field theory interactions are planned.
