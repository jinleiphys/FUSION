# P-matrix Description of Interaction of Two Charged Hadrons And Low-energy Nuclear-Coulomb Scattering Parameters

> The scattering of two charged strongly interacting particles is described on the basis of the P-matrix approach. In the P matrix, it is proposed to isolate explicitly the background term corresponding to purely Coulomb interaction, whereby it becomes possible to improve convergence of the expansions used and to obtain a correct asymptotic behavior of observables at high energies. The expressions for the purely Coulomb background P matrix, its poles and residues, and purely Coulomb eigenfunctions of the P-matrix approach are obtained. The nuclear-Coulomb low-energy scattering parameters of two charged hadrons are investigated on the basis of this approach combined with the method of isolating the background P matrix. Simple explicit expressions for the nuclear-Coulomb scattering length and effective range in terms of the residual P matrix are derived. These expressions give a general form of the nuclear-Coulomb low-energy scattering parameters for models of finite-range strong interaction. Specific applications of the general expressions derived in this study are exemplified by considering some exactly solvable models of strong interaction containing hard core repulsion, and, for these models, the nuclear-Coulomb low-energy scattering parameters for arbitrary values of the orbital angular momentum are found explicitly. In particular, the nuclear-Coulomb scattering length and effective range are obtained explicitly for the boundary-condition model, the model of a hard-core delta-shell potential, the Margenau model, and the model of hard-core square-well potential.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that explicitly isolating the purely Coulomb background term in the P-matrix approach improves convergence of the pole expansions and yields correct asymptotic behavior of observables at high energies, whereas without this isolation the phase shift incorrectly tends to infinity rather than vanishing at high energies.

## Method

The authors define the P-matrix as the logarithmic derivative of the radial wave function at the surface of the strong-interaction region (radius R). They isolate the purely Coulomb background P-matrix, defined as the logarithmic derivative of the regular Coulomb wave function, from the full nuclear-Coulomb P-matrix. Using this decomposition, they derive expressions for the nuclear-Coulomb S-matrix and phase shift in terms of the residual P-matrix, and then expand the Coulomb-modified effective-range function to obtain general formulas for the nuclear-Coulomb scattering length and effective range in terms of the residual P-matrix parameters.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb parameter is defined as ξ = μ e₁ e₂ / (ℏ² k) = 1/(a_B k), where a_B is the Bohr radius given by a_B ≡ ℏ²/(μ e₁ e₂)
- The dimensionless parameter z ≡ 2√(2R/a_B) appears in the zero-energy Coulomb P-matrix expressions
- For S-wave scattering (l=0), the residual inverse scattering length is γ̂ = 1/(2 I₁²(z) P̂), where P̂ is the residual P-matrix at zero energy
- The expansion of the residual P-matrix converges as 1/n⁴, while the original P-matrix expansion converges as 1/n²
- For hard-core scattering, the dimensionless inverse scattering length is γ_l^h = K_ν(z)/I_ν(z) with ν = 2l+1

## Context

This work extends the P-matrix approach originally proposed by Jaffe and Low (1979) and the method of isolating a free background P-matrix developed by the authors' group (1990s) to the case of charged hadrons with Coulomb interaction. The authors improve upon earlier work by Babenko and Petrov (2002) that gave more cumbersome expressions for nuclear-Coulomb scattering parameters without the isolation method. The results enable direct calculation of nuclear-Coulomb scattering length and effective range for any finite-range strong-interaction model, demonstrated for the boundary-condition model, hard-core delta-shell potential, Margenau model, and hard-core square-well potential.
