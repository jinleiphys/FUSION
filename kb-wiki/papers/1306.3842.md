# Bulk viscosity of hot dense Quark matter in PNJL model

> Starting from the Kubo formula and the the QCD low energy theorem, we study the the bulk viscosity of hot dense quark matter in the PNJL model from the equation of state . We show that the bulk viscosity has a sharp peak near the chiral phase transition, and the ratio of bulk viscosity over entropy rises dramatically in the vicinity of the phase transition. These results agrees with that from lattice and other model calculations. In addition, we show that the increase of chemical potential raises the bulk viscosity.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of hot dense quark matter has a sharp peak near the chiral phase transition, and the ratio of bulk viscosity over entropy density rises dramatically in that vicinity; the increase of chemical potential raises the bulk viscosity but decreases the ratio of bulk viscosity to entropy density because the entropy density increases more rapidly than the bulk viscosity.

## Method

The authors start from the Kubo formula and QCD low-energy theorems at finite temperature and chemical potential to relate the bulk viscosity to the retarded Green's function of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor. They then compute the equation of state and the necessary thermodynamic derivatives within the two-flavor Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, solving the mean-field equations for the chiral condensate and Polyakov loops numerically. The bulk viscosity is extracted using a small-frequency ansatz for the spectral density, with the scale parameter \(\omega_0\) set to 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Chiral phase transition temperature is about 0.24 GeV at a quark chemical potential \(\mu = 0.2\) GeV (crossover transition).
* Model parameters: cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.651\) GeV, current quark mass \(m = 0.0055\) GeV, coupling constant \(g = 5.04\) GeV, \(T_0 = 0.27\) GeV, zero-temperature quark condensate \(|\sigma_0| = 0.251^3\) GeV, vacuum energy density \(|\epsilon_v|^{1/4} = 0.25\) GeV, and \(\omega_0 = 1\) GeV.
* The bulk viscosity shows a sharp peak near the chiral phase transition temperature, and finite chemical potential increases the bulk viscosity at the same temperature.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of bulk viscosity at zero baryon density (lattice QCD results and model calculations) to both finite temperature and finite baryon density using the PNJL model, which incorporates both confinement and chiral symmetry. It agrees with lattice results and other model calculations such as those by Masashi Mizutani, and complements studies at finite density using the NJL model and Dyson-Schwinger equations (Jiang et al.). The results are relevant for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions and for understanding the properties of compact stars.
