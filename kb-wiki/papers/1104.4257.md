# Possible Deuteron-like Molecular States Composed of Heavy Baryons

> We perform a systematic study of the possible loosely bound states composed of two charmed baryons or a charmed baryon and an anti-charmed baryon within the framework of the one boson exchange (OBE) model. We consider not only the $π$ exchange but also the $η$, $ρ$, $ω$, $ϕ$ and $σ$ exchanges. The $S-D$ mixing effects for the spin-triplets are also taken into account. With the derived effective potentials, we calculate the binding energies and root-mean-square (RMS) radii for the systems $Λ_cΛ_c(\barΛ_c)$, $Ξ_cΞ_c(\barΞ_c)$, $Σ_cΣ_c(\barΣ_c)$, $Ξ_c^\primeΞ_c^\prime(\barΞ_c^\prime)$ and $Ω_cΩ_c(\barΩ_c)$. Our numerical results indicate that: (1) the H-dibaryon-like state $Λ_cΛ_c$ does not exist; (2) there may exist four loosely bound deuteron-like states $Ξ_cΞ_c$ and $Ξ_c^\primeΞ_c^\prime$ with small binding energies and large RMS radii.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within the one boson exchange (OBE) model, there may exist four loosely bound deuteron-like molecular states composed of two charmed baryons (specifically \(\Xi_c\Xi_c\) and \(\Xi_c^\prime\Xi_c^\prime\)), but only under the condition that the cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) is taken in a specific range (0.80–2.00 GeV) and that the binding energies are small and RMS radii large; the \(\Lambda_c\Lambda_c\) system (the heavy analogue of the H-dibaryon) does not form a bound state.

## Method

The authors derived effective potentials for systems of two charmed baryons (or a charmed baryon and an anti-charmed baryon) using the OBE model, including exchanges of \(\pi\), \(\eta\), \(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\phi\), and \(\sigma\) mesons, and accounting for \(S\)-\(D\) mixing for spin-triplet states. Coupling constants for heavy baryon–light meson interactions were estimated from known nucleon–meson couplings using a quark-model matching procedure. The binding energies and root-mean-square (RMS) radii were then calculated by solving the Schrödinger equation (single-channel for spin-singlets, coupled-channel for spin-triplets) with a monopole form factor and a cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) varied from 0.80 to 2.00 GeV.

## Key numbers

*   \(\Lambda_c\Lambda_c\) system: no bound state found for any cutoff in the range 0.80–2.00 GeV.
*   \(\Xi_c\Xi_c\) system, state \(\Psi_{\Xi_c\Xi_c}^{[1,1]}\): binding energy 0.14 MeV (at \(\Lambda = 1.01\) GeV) to 0.35 MeV (at \(\Lambda = 1.10\) GeV); RMS radius 5.58 fm to 4.62 fm.
*   \(\Xi_c\Xi_c\) system, state \(\Psi_{\Xi_c\Xi_c}^{[0,3]}\): binding energy 2.53 MeV (at \(\Lambda = 0.95\) GeV) to 36.59 MeV (at \(\Lambda = 1.20\) GeV); RMS radius 2.17 fm to 0.78 fm.
*   \(\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c\) system, state \(\Psi_{\Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c}^{[0,1(3)]}\): binding energy 4.61 MeV (at \(\Lambda = 0.90\) GeV) to 142.19 MeV (at \(\Lambda = 1.10\) GeV); RMS radius 1.76 fm to 0.52 fm.
*   \(\Xi_c\bar{\Xi}_c\) system, state \(\Psi_{\Xi_c\bar{\Xi}_c}^{[0,1(3)]}\): binding energy 1.48 MeV (at \(\Lambda = 0.87\) GeV) to 82.86 MeV (at \(\Lambda = 1.10\) GeV); RMS radius 2.72 fm to 0.60 fm.

## Context

This work extends the OBE model, previously applied to heavy meson–anti-meson molecular states (e.g., by the authors' group in studies of \(X(3872)\) and related systems, and by Tornqvist (1994) using one-pion-exchange), to the case of heavy baryon–baryon and baryon–anti-baryon systems. It also parallels earlier studies of the H-dibaryon (e.g., by Aerts (1983) and Iwasaki (1987)), finding that the \(\Lambda_c\Lambda_c\) system does not bind, unlike the \(\Xi_c\Xi_c\) and \(\Xi_c^\prime\Xi_c^\prime\) systems which may form loosely bound states. The results suggest that future experimental searches for these predicted deuteron-like dibaryons could test the OBE model in the heavy baryon sector.
