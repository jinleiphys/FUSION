# Near-the-origin divergence of Dirac wave functions of hydrogen and operator product expansion

> There is a long-standing puzzle concerning the Coulomb solutions of the Dirac equation, i.e., what is the physics governing the weakly divergent near-the-origin behavior of the Dirac wave functions of the $nS_{1/2}$ hydrogen? As a sequel of our preceding work that aim to demystifying the universal near-the-origin behavior of the atomic Schrödinger and Klein-Gordon wave functions, the goal of this work is to demonstrate that, within the nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) tailored for Coulombic atoms, the universal logarithmic divergence of the Dirac wave functions can be accounted by the perturbatively calculable Wilson coefficient emerging from the operator product expansion (OPE) of the electron and the nucleus fields. The cause is due to the relativistic kinetic correction and Darwin (zitterbewegung) term in the NREFT. With the aid of renormalization group equation, one can resum the leading logarithms to all orders in $Zα$ and recover the $r^{-Z^2α^2/2}$ anomalous scaling behavior exhibited by the Dirac wave function for the $nS_{1/2}$ hydrogen. It appears somewhat counterintuitive that these universal logarithmic divergences can not be accounted by the OPE set up in the relativistic QED. We are thereby enforced to conclude that the Dirac wave function must cease to be meaningful when $r$ is shorter than the electron's Compton wavelength.

## Key claim
The universal logarithmic divergence of Dirac wave functions for $nS_{1/2}$ hydrogen near the origin can be accounted for by perturbatively calculable Wilson coefficients from the operator product expansion within nonrelativistic effective field theory, specifically arising from the relativistic kinetic correction and Darwin (zitterbewegung) term, but the Dirac wave function ceases to be meaningful when the distance is shorter than the electron's Compton wavelength.

## Method
The authors formulated the Dirac wave function as a vacuum-to-atom matrix element of the electron field and the heavy nucleus effective theory field, then applied operator product expansion in the nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) framework for Coulombic atoms. They included relativistic corrections up to order $v^2/c^2$ (kinetic ${\bf p}^4$ term and Darwin term) in the NREFT Lagrangian, computed the Wilson coefficient through perturbative matching, and used the renormalization group equation to resum leading logarithms to all orders in $Z\alpha$.

## Key numbers
- The Dirac wave function for $nS_{1/2}$ hydrogen scales as $r^{-Z^2\alpha^2/2}$ near the origin, where $\alpha$ is the fine structure constant
- The singularity has noticeable effect only when $r \lessapprox \frac{n a_0}{2} \exp(-2/Z^2\alpha^2) \sim \frac{n a_0}{2} 10^{-16300/Z^2}$, where $a_0$ is the Bohr radius
- The Wilson coefficient in NREFT at order $Z^2\alpha^2$ is ${\mathcal C}(r) = 1 - mZ\alpha r - \frac{Z^2\alpha^2}{2} (\ln \mu r + \text{const}) + {\cal O}(Z^3\alpha^3)$
- In momentum space, the Wilson coefficient scales as $\widetilde{\mathcal C}(q) = \frac{8\pi mZ\alpha + {\cal O}(Z^3\alpha^3)}{{\bf q}^4} - \frac{\pi^2 Z^2\alpha^2 + {\cal O}(Z^4\alpha^4)}{|{\bf q}|^3}$

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Huang, Jia, Yu 2018) that applied NREFT and operator product expansion to explain the universal near-the-origin behavior of atomic Schrodinger and Klein-Gordon wave functions, forming a trilogy. The paper contradicts earlier attempts (Durand 1983, Castorina 1984, Malenfant 1987, Chen 2008) that treated the divergence as a nuisance without recognizing its perturbative nature. The result enables a field-theoretic understanding of relativistic wave function singularities and clarifies that the Dirac wave function loses physical meaning below the electron's Compton wavelength.
