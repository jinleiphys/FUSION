# Delta isobars and nuclear saturation

> We construct a nuclear interaction in chiral effective field theory with explicit inclusion of the $Δ$-isobar $Δ(1232)$ degree of freedom at all orders up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). We use pion-nucleon ($πN$) low-energy constants (LECs) from a Roy-Steiner analysis of $πN$ scattering data, optimize the LECs in the contact potentials up to NNLO to reproduce low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, and constrain the three-nucleon interaction at NNLO to reproduce the binding energy and point-proton radius of $^{4}$He. For heavier nuclei we use the coupled-cluster method to compute binding energies, radii, and neutron skins. We find that radii and binding energies are much improved for interactions with explicit inclusion of $Δ(1232)$, while $Δ$-less interactions produce nuclei that are not bound with respect to breakup into $α$ particles. The saturation of nuclear matter is significantly improved, and its symmetry energy is consistent with empirical estimates.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the Delta isobar (Delta(1232)) as an explicit degree of freedom in chiral effective field theory interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) yields much improved binding energies and radii for medium-mass nuclei compared to Delta-less interactions, and produces nuclei that are bound with respect to breakup into alpha particles, whereas Delta-less interactions at the same order do not.

## Method

The authors constructed non-local chiral effective field theory potentials at LO, NLO, and NNLO both with and without explicit Delta isobar degrees of freedom, using pion-nucleon low-energy constants from a Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering data. They optimized the contact-potential low-energy constants up to NNLO to reproduce low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts from the Granada analysis up to 200 MeV laboratory energy, and constrained the three-nucleon interaction at NNLO by simultaneously reproducing the binding energy and point-proton radius of He-4. For heavier nuclei (O-16, O-22, O-24, Ca-40, Ca-48, He-8) they employed the coupled-cluster method with singles, doubles, and perturbative triples (Lambda-CCSD(T)) in a model space of 15 oscillator shells.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of O-16 at DeltaNNLO: -117.0(1.8) MeV (experiment: -127.62 MeV)
* Binding energy of Ca-40 at DeltaNNLO: -309(14) MeV (experiment: -342.05 MeV)
* Charge radius of O-16 at DeltaNNLO: 2.73(0.10) fm (experiment: 2.699(5) fm)
* Charge radius of Ca-40 at DeltaNNLO: 3.55(0.17) fm (experiment: 3.478(2) fm)
* Neutron skin of Ca-48 at DeltaNLO and DeltaNNLO: 0.15 fm (consistent with ranges 0.14-0.20 fm and 0.12-0.15 fm from prior work)
* Binding energy of He-4 at DeltaNNLO with cutoff 450 MeV: 28.29(78) MeV (experiment: 28.30 MeV)
* Charge radius of He-4 at DeltaNNLO with cutoff 450 MeV: 1.67(2) fm (experiment: 1.6755(28) fm)
* Delta-N mass splitting used: delta = 293 MeV

## Context

This work extends the program of chiral effective field theory nuclear interactions by explicitly including the Delta isobar, which had been largely neglected in quantitative potentials since early work by van Kolck (1994) and Bernard (1997). It contrasts with the Delta-less NNLOsat approach of Ekstrom (2015) and with the local Delta-full potentials of Piarulli (2015, 2016) and Logoteta (2016), which used different fitting strategies. The results enable systematic studies of medium-mass nuclei and nuclear matter with improved convergence and saturation properties, and provide a foundation for future Bayesian model selection and uncertainty quantification in chiral effective field theory.
