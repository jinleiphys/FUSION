# New forms of non-relativistic and relativistic hydrodynamic equations as derived by the renormalization-group method - possible functional ansatz in the moment method consistent with Chapman-Enskog theory -

> After a brief account of the derivation of the first-order relativistic hydrodynamic equation as a construction of the invariant manifold of relativistic Boltzmann equation, we give a sketch of derivation of the second-order hydrodynamic equation (extended thermodynamics) both in the nonrelativistic and relativistic cases. We show that the resultant equation suggests a novel ansatz for the functional form to be used in Grad moment method, which turns out to give the same expressions for the transport coefficients as those given in the Chapman-Enskog theory as well as the novel expressions for the relaxation times and lenghts allowing natural physical interpretaion.

## Key claim

The renormalization-group (RG) method applied to the relativistic Boltzmann equation yields first-order and second-order (extended thermodynamics) hydrodynamic equations without needing the ad hoc matching conditions required by the Chapman-Enskog or Grad moment methods; the resulting second-order equations suggest a novel ansatz for the distribution function in the moment method that reproduces the Chapman-Enskog transport coefficients while giving new microscopic expressions for relaxation times and lengths.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation and rewrite it using a timelike macroscopic-frame vector to separate temporal evolution from spatial inhomogeneity, treating the spatial gradient (Knudsen number) as a small parameter. They apply the renormalization-group method to construct the invariant manifold of the linearized collision operator, first using only its five zero modes to obtain first-order hydrodynamics (Landau-Lifshitz frame), then incorporating the first excited modes to derive second-order (extended thermodynamics) equations. The resulting distribution function is then read off to propose a new functional ansatz for the Grad moment method.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity from the RG method: \(\eta^{\mathrm{TK}} = -\frac{1}{10T} \langle \hat{\pi}^{ij}, L^{-1} \hat{\pi}^{ij} \rangle_{\mathrm{eq}}\), which agrees with the Chapman-Enskog expression \(\eta^{\mathrm{CE}}\).
- Relaxation time for shear stress: \(\tau^{\mathrm{TK}}_{\pi} = -\frac{\langle \hat{\pi}^{ij}, L^{-2} \hat{\pi}^{ij} \rangle_{\mathrm{eq}}}{\langle \hat{\pi}^{kl}, L^{-1} \hat{\pi}^{kl} \rangle_{\mathrm{eq}}}\), expressed as \(\tau_\pi = \int_0^\infty ds \, s \, R_\eta(s) / \int_0^\infty ds \, R_\eta(s)\) where \(R_\eta(s)\) is the relaxation function.
- Relaxation time for bulk viscous pressure: \(\tau_\Pi = \int_0^\infty ds \, s \, R_\zeta(s) / \int_0^\infty ds \, R_\zeta(s)\).
- Relaxation time for heat current: \(\tau_J = \int_0^\infty ds \, s \, R_\lambda(s) / \int_0^\infty ds \, R_\lambda(s)\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RG-based derivation of first-order relativistic hydrodynamics (Tsumura and Kunihiro, 2011) to second order, providing an explicit construction of the thirteen-moment invariant manifold sought in the mesoscopic dynamics literature (Gorban, 2006; Mesoscopic Dynamics). The resulting transport coefficients match those of Chapman-Enskog theory (Chapman and Cowling, 1970), while the relaxation times are new and differ from those in the standard Grad moment method (Grad, 1949) and from the Israel-Stewart approach (Israel and Stewart, 1979). The work enables a physically transparent interpretation of relaxation times as correlation times of the relaxation functions, and provides a systematic framework for deriving causal, stable relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics from kinetic theory.
