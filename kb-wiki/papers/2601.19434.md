# Extracting Nucleon Resonance Transition GPDs from $e^- N\to e^-γNπ$ Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering

> We investigate the process in which Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) excites a baryon resonance. In particular, we assess, in DVCS leading to the Roper resonance, the relative importance of a "background'' process in which a pion is first emitted by the nucleon, which then undergoes a DVCS event. Our numerical results, using realistic DVCS kinematics, indicate that there can be measurable interference effects. They suggest that this process could substantially modify the experimentally observed cross sections at CLAS12-like kinematics, motivating their inclusion in precision analyses of DVCS experiments. We further find that in spite of this background, the transition to a Roper-like state through DVCS does contribute significantly to the $e^- N\to e^-γNπ$ cross section in some kinematic regions. This suggests that the creation of nucleon resonances via DVCS is a useful method for extracting information about the nucleon transition GPDs and the internal structure of the excited states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a "background" process, where the nucleon first emits a pion and then undergoes Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS), can produce measurable interference effects with the resonance-mediated DVCS process leading to the Roper resonance, and that this background must be included in precision analyses, but that the transition to a Roper-like state through DVCS still contributes significantly enough in some kinematic regions to be a useful method for extracting transition generalized parton distributions (GPDs).

## Method

The authors constructed the DVCS amplitude for both a "diagonal" subprocess (pion emission followed by DVCS on a nucleon) and a "transition" subprocess (DVCS exciting the Roper resonance, which decays to Nπ), using a leading-twist handbag factorization framework. They modeled the transition GPDs and diagonal GPDs using a light-cone double-distribution representation with Regge-inspired t-dependence, fixing normalizations to MAID2008 transition form factors and to nucleon charges and anomalous moments, respectively. The 7-fold differential cross section for e⁻N → e⁻γNπ was then evaluated at CLAS12-like kinematics, with the diagonal and transition amplitudes combined coherently including a final-state interaction phase factor for the diagonal case.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: E_e = 10.6 GeV; photon virtuality: Q² = 2.3 GeV²; Bjorken-x: x_B = 0.2; scattering plane angle: Φ = π/2.
- The transition GPDs for the proton peak at around -t = 1.74 GeV², and for the neutron at around -t = 2.26 GeV².
- The dipole form factor dressing the pion emission vertex uses a cutoff Λ = 0.7 GeV.
- The Roper resonance width is taken as Γ = 0.350 GeV (PDG value).
- The diagonal GPD H̃₂ contributes less than 2% at -t = 0.35 GeV² and is negligible by -t = 1.5 GeV².
- The cut M_{πγ} > 1 GeV is applied to minimize the ρ⁺ → γπ background channel.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Semenov-Tian-Shansky et al. (2023) for nucleon-to-Roper transition GPDs by adding a previously neglected "diagonal" background process where the nucleon emits a pion before undergoing DVCS. The authors note that such pion emission channels have typically been neglected in previous DVCS analyses by HERMES (2001), CLAS (2001), H1 Collaboration, and Jefferson Lab Hall A (2022). The study targets CLAS12 kinematics at JLab and aims to provide a controlled signal model for upcoming experimental analyses, with the broader goal of distinguishing whether the Roper resonance is a genuine three-quark excited state (Burkert, 2017) or dynamically generated by meson-baryon scattering (Wu, 2017; Owa, 2025).
