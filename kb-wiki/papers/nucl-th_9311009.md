# A Systematic Treatment of Triaxial Systems at High Spins. II

> The BRST treatment of triaxial nuclei rotating at high spins, which had been previously developed, is extended to relate states with neighboring values of the angular momentum $I$. As a consequence, the irreducible multipole transition operators can be unambiguously separated into their physical and spurious components. As a by product, we give explicit expressions for the operators associated with the Goldstone-Nambu modes in terms of the final orthonormal set of physical and spurious modes.

## Key claim

The BRST treatment of triaxial nuclei rotating at high spins is extended to relate states with neighboring angular momentum values \(I\) and \(I+\iota\), enabling the unambiguous separation of irreducible multipole transition operators into physical and spurious components, provided the condition \(|\iota| \ll I\) holds and the formalism is applied perturbatively.

## Method

The authors extend the previously developed BRST (Becchi-Rouet-Stora-Tyutin) formalism for triaxial cranking-model nuclei by constructing states with angular momentum \(I+\iota\) in terms of the basis associated with \(I\), using Marshalek's generalization of the Holstein-Primakoff algebra to represent the rotational matrices \(D^{\lambda}_{\mu\nu}\). They then expand the laboratory-frame transition operators into zero-phonon and one-phonon terms, separate the physical from the spurious components using the BRST charge, and explicitly cancel unphysical contributions by combining terms from different orders of the expansion.

## Key numbers

* Wobbling mode frequency: \(\omega_\mathrm{w} = I \Delta_y \Delta_z\), where \(\Delta_{y/z} = \sqrt{1/\Im_{y/z} - 1/\Im}\) (Eq. z80).
* The linear term in the residual hamiltonian for a change \(\iota\) in angular momentum is \(H^{(1)}_{\iota} = -\iota \sqrt{\omega_x/(2\Im_x)} (\Gamma^{\dag}_{1x}+\Gamma_{1x}) + O_\mathrm{null}\) (Eq. z10).
* For the quadrupole operator, the wobbling-excitation coefficients are \(t^{(\mathrm{w})}_{2,1(\pm)} = \sqrt{6/I}\, Q_0 \pm (2/\sqrt{I})\, Q_2 - \mathrm{i}\sqrt{2I}([\theta_{-1},T^{(1)}_{2,1}]^{(0)} \pm [\theta_1,T^{(1)}_{2,1}]^{(0)})\) (Eq. y81), which are a factor \(I^{-1/2}\) smaller than the yrast transitions.
* For the magnetic dipole operator, the wobbling-excitation coefficient is \(t^{(w)}_{1,1(\pm)} = \sqrt{2/I}\, T^{(0)}_{1,0} - \mathrm{i}\sqrt{2I}([\theta_{-1},T^{(1)}_{1,1}]^{(0)} \pm [\theta_1,T^{(1)}_{1,1}]^{(0)})\) (Eq. y82).

## Context

This work extends the BRST-based cranking model formalism of Garrahan and Bes (1993, Paper I, nucl-th/9311008) and of Kerman, Bohr, and Casten (1990) by adding the treatment of transitions between states of different angular momentum. It contrasts with earlier RPA treatments of the cranking model by Marshalek (1977), Marshalek and Johnson (1978), and Shimizu and Matsuyanagi (1983), which assumed the quadrupole-plus-pairing model and did not explicitly separate spurious modes from the physical wobbling mode. The formalism enables perturbative calculations of transition probabilities and excitation of the wobbling mode in triaxial nuclei at high spins, including velocity-dependent residual interactions as stressed by Hamamoto and Nazarewicz (1993).
