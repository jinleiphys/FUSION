# Damping of Quantum Vibrations Revealed in Deep Sub-barrier Fusion

> We demonstrate that when two colliding nuclei approach each other, their quantum vibrations are damped near the touching point. We show that this damping is responsible for the fusion hindrance phenomena measured in the deep sub-barrier fusion reactions. To show those, we for the first time apply the random-phase-approximation (RPA) method to the two-body $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca systems. We calculate the octupole transition strengths for the two nuclei adiabatically approaching each other. The calculated transition strength drastically decreases near the touching point, strongly suggesting the vanishing of the quantum couplings between the relative motion and the vibrational intrinsic degrees of freedom of each nucleus. Based on this picture, we also calculate the fusion cross section for the $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca system using the coupled-channel method with the damping factor simulating the vanishing of the couplings. The calculated results reproduce well the experimental data, indicating that the smooth transition from the sudden to adiabatic processes indeed occurs in the deep sub-barrier fusion reactions.

# Damping of Quantum Vibrations Revealed in Deep Sub-barrier Fusion

## Key claim
The damping of quantum octupole vibrations near the touching point of two colliding nuclei, demonstrated through random-phase-approximation (RPA) calculations for the first time in two-body systems, is the physical origin of the fusion hindrance observed in deep sub-barrier fusion reactions. The damping factor previously introduced phenomenologically by Ichikawa et al. (2009) to reproduce experimental fusion cross sections is shown to correlate strongly with the calculated octupole transition strengths.

## Method
The authors applied the random-phase-approximation (RPA) method to the two-body systems 16O + 16O and 40Ca + 40Ca, calculating octupole transition strengths B(E3) as a function of the center-of-mass distance between the nuclei. They constructed mean-field potentials using a folding procedure with a single Yukawa function, described nuclear shapes with lemniscatoids parametrization after the touching point, and solved the axially-symmetric Schrodinger equation with spin-orbit force. The RPA equation was solved at each distance using a density-dependent contact residual interaction, with a new scheme based on Tomonaga theory to separate center-of-mass, relative motion, and intrinsic degrees of freedom. Fusion cross sections for 40Ca + 40Ca were then calculated using the coupled-channel method with a damping factor simulating the vanishing of the couplings.

## Key numbers
- For 16O + 16O at R = 15 fm: excitation energy of the first excited 3- state is 5.29 MeV and B(E3, 3_1- → 0_1+) = 102.07 e² fm⁶
- For 40Ca + 40Ca at R = 15.0 fm: excitation energy is 3.24 MeV and B(E3, 3_1- → 0_1+) = 1253.17 e² fm⁶
- The touching point for 16O + 16O is at R = 6.4 fm
- The B(E3) value for right-sided 16O falls off at around R = 8 fm
- Best-fit damping factor parameters for 40Ca + 40Ca: R_d = 9.6 fm and a_d = 0.9 fm
- Radius parameter used: R_0 = 1.26 A^{1/3} fm for 16O + 16O and R_0 = 1.27 A^{1/3} fm for 40Ca + 40Ca
- Yukawa-plus-exponential model parameters: r_0 = 1.191 fm and a = 0.68 fm

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel model with a damping factor proposed by Ichikawa et al. (2009) by providing its physical foundation: the damping of quantum vibrations due to changes in single-particle wave functions as nuclei approach. It contrasts with the sudden-picture model of Misicu and Esbensen (2006), which introduced a repulsive core from nuclear incompressibility, and with the quantum decoherence concept of Dasgupta et al. (2007). The results enable a unified description of the smooth transition from sudden to adiabatic processes in deep sub-barrier fusion, and the authors note the approach can be extended to asymmetric di-nuclear systems and compared with time-dependent Hartree-Fock dynamics as in Keser et al. (2012).
