# Few-nucleon matrix elements in pionless effective field theory in a finite volume

> Pionless effective field theory in a finite volume (FVEFT$_{π\!/}$) is investigated as a framework for the analysis of multi-nucleon spectra and matrix elements calculated in lattice QCD (LQCD). By combining FVEFT$_{π\!/}$ with the stochastic variational method, the spectra of nuclei with atomic number $A\in\{2,3\}$ are matched to existing finite-volume LQCD calculations at heavier-than-physical quark masses corresponding to a pion mass $m_π=806$ MeV, thereby enabling infinite-volume binding energies to be determined using infinite-volume variational calculations. Based on the variational wavefunctions that are constructed in this approach, the finite-volume matrix elements of various local operators are computed in FVEFT$_{π\!/}$ and matched to LQCD calculations of the corresponding QCD operators in the same volume, thereby determining the relevant one and two-body EFT counterterms and enabling an extrapolation of the LQCD matrix elements to infinite volume. As examples, the scalar, tensor, and axial matrix elements are considered, as well as the magnetic moments and the isovector longitudinal momentum fraction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that finite-volume pionless effective field theory (FVEFT$_{\pi\!/}$) combined with the stochastic variational method can be used to match lattice QCD calculations of few-nucleon spectra and matrix elements, enabling infinite-volume extrapolations of both binding energies and matrix elements of local operators, but only at the specific unphysical quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of 806 MeV where the LQCD data exist.

## Method

The authors implement the stochastic variational method (SVM) in a periodic cubic volume using shifted correlated-Gaussian wavefunctions to solve the few-body Schrödinger equation with pionless EFT interactions. They tune the two-body low-energy constants $C_0$, $C_1$ (or equivalently $C_S$, $C_T$) and the three-body constant $D_0$ by matching the finite-volume ground-state energies of $A\in\{2,3\}$ nuclei to existing LQCD results from Beane et al. (2012) at three different lattice volumes ($L=3.4$, $4.5$, $6.7$ fm). Using the variational wavefunctions obtained from this matching, they then compute finite-volume matrix elements of scalar, tensor, axial, magnetic, and twist-two operators and match these to LQCD calculations to determine the relevant one-body and two-body counterterms for each current.

## Key numbers

- LQCD calculations at $m_\pi=806$ MeV provide finite-volume binding energies for $pp$: $17.8(3.3)$ MeV ($L=3.4$ fm), $15.1(2.8)$ MeV ($L=4.5$ fm), $15.9(3.8)$ MeV ($L=6.7$ fm)
- LQCD deuteron binding energies: $25.4(5.4)$ MeV ($L=3.4$ fm), $22.5(3.5)$ MeV ($L=4.5$ fm), $19.5(4.8)$ MeV ($L=6.7$ fm)
- LQCD ${}^3$H binding energies: $65.6(6.8)$ MeV ($L=3.4$ fm), $63.2(8.0)$ MeV ($L=4.5$ fm), $53.9(10.7)$ MeV ($L=6.7$ fm)
- Two-body LEC $C_0 = -131(2)$ (for regulator $r_0=0.2$ fm), $-220(5)$ ($r_0=0.3$ fm), $-330(9)$ ($r_0=0.4$ fm)
- Two-body LEC $C_1 = -2(1)$ (for $r_0=0.2$ fm), $-4(2)$ ($r_0=0.3$ fm), $-8(4)$ ($r_0=0.4$ fm)
- Three-body LEC $D_0 = 17(2)$ (for $r_0=0.2$ fm)
- SVM wavefunctions with 100 terms converge to within 1% (typically 0.1%) of the final ground-state energy

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume pionless EFT approach of Eliyahu et al. (2019), which matched binding energies of $A\in\{2,3\}$ nuclei to LQCD, to include matrix elements of external currents for the first time. It provides an alternative to Lüscher's method (1986) for extracting infinite-volume physics from LQCD, with the advantage that it can be applied to any system the EFT can describe, whereas Lüscher's method is limited to two and three-particle systems. The approach enables future LQCD calculations at physical quark masses to be analyzed within the same framework, and can be extended to larger nuclei and additional operator types.
