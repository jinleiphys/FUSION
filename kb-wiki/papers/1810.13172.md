# High $p_{t}$ squeezed-out n/p ratio as a probe of $K_{\rm{sym}}$ of the symmetry energy

> By involving the constraints of the slope of nuclear symmetry energy $L$ into the question of determination of the high-density symmetry energy, one needs to probe the curvature of nuclear symmetry energy $K_{\rm{sym}}$. Based on the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, effects of the curvature of nuclear symmetry energy on the squeezed-out nucleons are demonstrated in the semi-central Au+Au reaction at 400 and 600 MeV/nucleon. It is shown that the squeezed-out isospin-dependent nucleon emissions at high transverse momenta are sensitive to the curvature of nuclear symmetry energy. The curvature of nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density thus can be determined by the high momentum squeezed-out isospin-dependent nucleon emissions experiments from the semi-central Au+Au reaction at 400 or 600 MeV/nucleon.

# High $p_{t}$ squeezed-out n/p ratio as a probe of $K_{\rm{sym}}$ of the symmetry energy

## Key claim

The curvature of nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density ($K_{\rm{sym}}$) can be determined by measuring the high-transverse-momentum squeezed-out neutron-to-proton ratio in semi-central Au+Au reactions at 400 or 600 MeV/nucleon, provided the slope parameter $L$ is already constrained to a narrow range (here fixed at $L = 60$ MeV).

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, updated with short-range correlations and high-momentum tails in nucleon momentum distributions, to simulate semi-central Au+Au collisions at 400 and 600 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter $b = 7$ fm. They constructed two density-dependent symmetry energy curves with identical saturation values ($E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0) = 30$ MeV) and slopes ($L = 60$ MeV) but different curvatures ($K_{\rm{sym}} = 0$ and $K_{\rm{sym}} = -400$ MeV), implemented via density-dependent $x$ parameters in the single-nucleon mean-field potential. They then computed the elliptic flow difference $v_2^n - v_2^p$ and the squeezed-out neutron-to-proton ratio (nucleons emitted perpendicular to the reaction plane, azimuthal angle cuts $75^\circ \leq \phi \leq 105^\circ$ and $255^\circ \leq \phi \leq 285^\circ$) as functions of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The curvature $K_{\rm{sym}}$ is known to lie in the range $-400 \leq K_{\rm{sym}} \leq 100$ MeV from prior surveys (Tews 2017, Zhang 2017).
- The slope $L$ is constrained to $L = 58.7 \pm 28.1$ MeV and the symmetry energy at saturation to $E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0) = 31.7 \pm 3.2$ MeV from a survey of 53 analyses (Oertel 2017, Li 2013).
- At transverse momenta around $p_t = 0.8$ or 1.3 GeV/c, the effects of the curvature on the squeezed-out n/p ratio reach about 40-60%.
- The effects of the slope $L$ on the squeezed-out n/p ratio are about 7-10% when changing $L$ from 60 to 40 MeV.
- The simulations used impact parameter $b = 7$ fm and mid-rapidity cut $|(y/y_{\rm{beam}})_{\rm{c.m.}}| \leq 0.5$.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to constrain the high-density symmetry energy by incorporating the now-narrow constraints on the slope $L$ (Oertel 2017, Li 2013) and shifting focus to the curvature $K_{\rm{sym}}$, which is more directly related to high-density behavior and neutron-star tidal deformability (Tsang 2018). The study builds on earlier transport model work on elliptic flow (Li 2001, Di Toro 2010) and squeezed-out nucleon emissions (Greiner 1988, Reisdorf 1997) and proposes a specific observable, the high-$p_t$ squeezed-out n/p ratio, that can be measured at existing facilities such as RIBF/RIKEN, FOPI/GSI, CSR/Lanzhou, FRIB, and RISP. The authors suggest using double ratios in isotopically different reaction systems (e.g., $^{132}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn over $^{108}$Sn+$^{112}$Sn) to reduce systematic uncertainties.
