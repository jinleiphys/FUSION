# Neutral and charged mesons in magnetic fields: A resonance gas in a non-relativistic quark model

> We analyze mesons in constant magnetic fields ($B$) within a non-relativistic constituent quark model. Our quark model contains a harmonic oscillator type confining potential, and we perturbatively treat short range correlations to account for the spin-flavor energy splittings. We study both neutral and charged mesons taking into account the internal quark dynamics. The neutral states are labelled by two-dimensional momenta for magnetic translations, while the charged states by two discrete indices related to angular momenta. For $B \ll Λ_{\rm QCD}^2$ ($Λ_{\rm QCD} \sim 200$ MeV: the QCD scale), the analyses proceed as in usual quark models, while special precautions are needed for strong fields, $B \sim Λ_{QCD}^2$, especially when we treat short range correlations such as the Fermi-Breit-Pauli interactions. We compute the energy spectra of mesons up to energies of $\sim 2.5$ GeV and use them to construct the meson resonance gas. Within the assumption that the constituent quark masses are insensitive to magnetic fields, the phase space enhancement for mesons significantly increases the entropy, assisting a transition from a hadron gas to a quark gluon plasma. We confront our results with the lattice data, finding reasonable agreement for the low-lying spectra and the entropy density at low temperature less than $\sim 100$ MeV, but our results at higher energy scale suffer from artifacts of our confining potential and non-relativistic treatments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, under the assumption that constituent quark masses remain insensitive to magnetic fields, the phase space enhancement for mesons significantly increases entropy and assists the transition from a hadron gas to a quark-gluon plasma, but the author notes that results at higher energy scales suffer from artifacts of the confining potential and non-relativistic treatments, and that agreement with lattice data is limited to low-lying spectra and entropy density at low temperatures below about 100 MeV.

## Method

The author uses a non-relativistic constituent quark model with a harmonic oscillator confining potential and perturbatively treats short-range correlations (Coulomb and color-magnetic Fermi-Breit-Pauli interactions) to account for spin-flavor energy splittings. Neutral meson states are labeled by two-dimensional momenta for magnetic translations, while charged meson states are labeled by two discrete indices related to angular momenta. The energy spectra of mesons are computed up to about 2.5 GeV and used to construct a meson resonance gas for comparison with lattice data.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark masses: \(m_{ud} = 0.30\) GeV and \(m_s = 0.50\) GeV
* Confining potential strength: \(\alpha = (0.160\ {\rm GeV})^3\)
* Running coupling nonperturbative scale: \(\Lambda_{\rm NP} \simeq 0.25\) GeV, giving \(\alpha_s(1\ {\rm GeV}) \simeq 0.5\)
* Ground state meson masses at \(B=0\): \(\pi\) (theory 140 MeV, experiment 137 MeV), \(K\) (theory 500 MeV, experiment 497 MeV), \(\eta\) (theory 564 MeV, experiment 547 MeV), \(\rho,\omega\) (theory 780 MeV, experiment 776 MeV), \(K^*\) (theory 887 MeV, experiment 894 MeV), \(\phi\) (theory 1014 MeV, experiment 1020 MeV)
* Color-magnetic interaction cutoff: \(\Lambda_M = 1.0\) GeV with \(C_M = 2.02\)
* Energy spectra computed up to \(\sim 2.5\) GeV
* QCD scale: \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \sim 200\) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutral mesons in magnetic fields by Simonov (2012) and Orlovsky (2013) for fictitious charged mesons, and adds new treatment of charged mesons and finite-momentum states. The author compares results with lattice data from Bali (2017) and others, finding reasonable agreement for low-lying spectra and entropy density below about 100 MeV. The work enables global analyses of meson spectra in magnetic fields for hadron resonance gas models, addressing the structural changes in hadrons that are neglected in models based on the Particle Data Group spectra with Zeeman couplings, such as those by Endrodi (2013) and Fukushima (2016).
