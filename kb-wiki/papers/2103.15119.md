# Constraints on the maximum mass of neutron stars with a quark core from GW170817 and NICER PSR J0030+0451 data

> We perform a Bayesian analysis of the maximum mass $M_{\rm TOV}$ of neutron stars with a quark core, incorporating the observational data from tidal deformability of the GW170817 binary neutron star merger as detected by LIGO/Virgo and the mass and radius of PSR J0030+0451 as detected by \nicer. The analysis is performed under the assumption that the hadron-quark phase transition is of first order, where the low-density hadronic matter described in a unified manner by the soft QMF or the stiff DD2 equation of state (EOS) transforms into a high-density phase of quark matter modeled by the generic "Constant-sound-speed" (CSS) parameterization. The mass distribution measured for the $2.14 \,{\rm M}_{\odot}$ pulsar, MSP J0740+6620, is used as the lower limit on $M_{\rm TOV}$. We find the most probable values of the hybrid star maximum mass are $M_{\rm TOV}=2.36^{+0.49}_{-0.26}\,{\rm M}_{\odot}$ ($2.39^{+0.47}_{-0.28}\,{\rm M}_{\odot}$) for QMF (DD2), with an absolute upper bound around $2.85\,{\rm M}_{\odot}$, to the $90\%$ posterior credible level. Such results appear robust with respect to the uncertainties in the hadronic EOS. We also discuss astrophysical implications of this result, especially on the post-merger product of GW170817, short gamma-ray bursts, and other likely binary neutron star mergers.

# Constraints on the maximum mass of neutron stars with a quark core from GW170817 and NICER PSR J0030+0451 data

## Key claim

Under the assumption of a first-order hadron-quark phase transition, the maximum mass of hybrid neutron stars with a quark core is constrained to a most probable value of approximately 2.36-2.39 solar masses, with an absolute upper bound around 2.85 solar masses at the 90% posterior credible level, and these results appear robust with respect to uncertainties in the hadronic equation of state.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using the Python Bilby package with a nested sampling algorithm, incorporating three observational constraints: the tidal deformability from the GW170817 binary neutron star merger detected by LIGO/Virgo, the mass and radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 from NICER, and the mass measurement of MSP J0740+6620 (2.14 solar masses) as a lower limit on the maximum mass. The low-density hadronic matter was described by two unified equations of state (the soft QMF and the stiff DD2 models), while the high-density quark matter phase was modeled using the generic Constant-sound-speed (CSS) parameterization with three free parameters: transition density, transition strength, and sound speed squared in quark matter.

## Key numbers

- Most probable hybrid star maximum mass: M_TOV = 2.36^{+0.49}_{-0.26} solar masses for QMF hadronic EOS (90% posterior credible level)
- Most probable hybrid star maximum mass: M_TOV = 2.39^{+0.47}_{-0.28} solar masses for DD2 hadronic EOS (90% posterior credible level)
- Absolute upper bound on M_TOV: approximately 2.85 solar masses (90% posterior credible level)
- Transition density favored by joint analysis: n_trans/n_0 = 2.04^{+1.22}_{-0.83} for QMF and 1.61^{+0.92}_{-0.46} for DD2
- Sound speed squared in quark matter: c_QM^2 = 0.81^{+0.17}_{-0.28} for QMF and 0.80^{+0.18}_{-0.29} for DD2 (joint analysis)
- Pure hadronic QMF EOS maximum mass: 2.07 solar masses with radius R_1.4 = 11.77 km
- Pure hadronic DD2 EOS maximum mass: 2.42 solar masses with radius R_1.4 = 13.17 km
- MSP J0740+6620 mass used as lower bound: 2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} solar masses (68% confidence level)
- PSR J0030+0451 mass and radius from NICER: M = 1.34^{+0.15}_{-0.16} solar masses, R = 12.71^{+1.14}_{-1.19} km (68.3% credibility interval, ST+PST model)

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of neutron star maximum mass by explicitly including a first-order hadron-quark phase transition, which earlier studies using piecewise polytrope models (e.g., LIGO/Virgo and NICER collaborations) did not incorporate. The results are consistent with model calculations by Xia et al. (2021) and Alford et al. (2021) that found low transition densities and stiff quark matter are needed for hybrid stars above two solar masses. The inferred maximum mass around 2.4 solar masses suggests that the remnant of GW170817 could be a massive rotating neutron star rather than a black hole, with implications for short gamma-ray burst engines and kilonova modeling, and that the secondary component of GW190814 (approximately 2.6 solar masses) could potentially be a supramassive neutron star, though a black hole interpretation remains more likely.
