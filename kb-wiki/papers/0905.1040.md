# To what systems does the Bohigas conjecture apply?

> We test the applicability of the Bohigas conjecture to systems whose Hamiltonian is not written as a closed form analytic expression. A class of such Hamiltonians is created and appear to violate the conjecture. Numerical methods are employed to find the spectra of a two-dimensional, classically chaotic "billiard" system whose Hamiltonian is in this class. We find that the spectral fluctuations are not in agreement with the conjecture.

## Key claim

The Bohigas conjecture does not hold for all quantum systems whose classical limits are chaotic and lack discrete symmetries; it fails for Hamiltonians that cannot be written as closed-form analytic expressions in terms of position and momentum operators, even when the classical dynamics are strongly chaotic.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian \(\hat{H}(\epsilon,\tau) = \hat{H}_0 + \frac{\epsilon}{2\tau} \int_{-\tau}^{\tau} dt \, \hat{\mathcal{O}}^H(t)\), where \(\hat{H}_0\) is a standard chaotic billiard Hamiltonian and \(\hat{\mathcal{O}}\) is a bounded operator. In the large-\(\tau\) limit, the off-diagonal matrix elements vanish, yielding a Hamiltonian \(\hat{H}(\epsilon)\) whose eigenvalues are \(E_n = E_n^{(0)} + \epsilon \langle n|\hat{\mathcal{O}}|n\rangle\). The classical analog is obtained by the same time-averaging procedure. Numerical diagonalization of the matrix representation of \(\hat{H}(\epsilon)\) for a two-dimensional billiard with two parabolic walls yields the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution.

## Key numbers

- The nearest-neighbor spacing distribution for \(\hat{H}_0\) (the standard chaotic billiard) follows the Wigner GOE distribution \(\rho(s) = (\pi s/2) \exp(-\pi s^2/4)\), confirming the Bohigas conjecture for that system.
- The nearest-neighbor spacing distribution for \(\hat{H}(\epsilon)\) is approximately Poissonian, \(\rho(s) = e^{-s}\), violating the Bohigas conjecture.
- The operator \(\hat{\mathcal{O}} = \hat{p}_x^2/(\hat{p}_x^2 + \hat{p}_y^2)\) is bounded between 0 and 1, and its expectation value fluctuations are of order unity in \(\hbar\) counting.
- The parameter \(\epsilon\) is chosen as \(\sqrt{\bar{E} \Delta}\), where \(\bar{E}\) is the average energy and \(\Delta\) is the average level spacing, ensuring \(\epsilon \sim \hbar\) so the classical limit reduces to \(H_0^{\text{class}}\).

## Context

This work extends the known exception of chaotic billiards on pseudo-spheres (Bunimovich and Veech, earlier work) to flat-geometry systems, showing that the Bohigas conjecture fails when the Hamiltonian is not a closed-form analytic expression. The construction exploits the non-uniqueness of the quantum-to-classical mapping: two distinct quantum Hamiltonians can share the same chaotic classical limit. The paper enables future work to develop criteria for identifying which quantum systems with chaotic classical limits do obey the Bohigas conjecture, without requiring explicit spectral calculation.
