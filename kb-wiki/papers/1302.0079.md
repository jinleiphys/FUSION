# $Ξ$ baryon strong decays in a chiral quark model

> The strong decays of $Ξ$ baryons up to N=2 shell were studied in a chiral quark model. The strong decay properties of these well-established ground decuplet baryons were reasonably described. We found that (i) $Ξ(1690)$ and $Ξ(1820)$ could be assigned to the spin-parity $J^P=1/2^-$ state $|70,^{2}{8},1,1,1/2^->$ and the spin-parity $J^P=3/2^-$ state $|70,^{2}{8},1,1,3/2^->$, respectively. Slight configuration mixing might exist in these two negative parity states. (ii) $Ξ(1950)$ might correspond to several different $Ξ$ resonances. The broad states ($Γ\sim 100$ MeV) observed in the $Ξπ$ channel could be classified as the pure $J^P=5/2^-$ octet state $Ξ^0|70,^{4}8,1,1,5/2^->$ or the mixed state $|Ξ1/2^->_3 $ with $J^P=1/2^-$. The $Ξ$ resonances with moderate width ($Γ\sim 60$ MeV) observed in the $Ξπ$ channel might correspond to the $J^P=1/2^+$ excitation $|56,^{4}10,2,2,1/2^+>$. The second orbital excitation $|56,^{4}10,2,2,3/2^+>$ and the mixed state $|Ξ1/2^->_1$ might be candidates for the narrow width state observed in the $Λ\bar{K}$ channel, however, their spin-parity numbers are incompatible with the moment analysis of the data. (iii) $Ξ(2030)$ could not be assigned to either any spin-parity $J^P=7/2^+$ states or any pure $J^P=5/2^+$ configurations. It seems to favor the $|70,^{2}8,2,2,3/2^+>$ assignment, however, its spin conflicts with the moment analysis of the data. To find more $Ξ$ resonances, the observations in the $Ξ(1530)π$ and $Σ(1385) \bar{K}$ channels are necessary.

## Key claim

The paper assigns specific SU(6)⊗O(3) quark-model configurations to the poorly known Ξ resonances Ξ(1690), Ξ(1820), Ξ(1950), and Ξ(2030) based on their strong decay properties, but notes that for Ξ(1950) and Ξ(2030) the assignments are ambiguous and often conflict with the spin-parity determinations from moment analysis of experimental data.

## Method

The authors calculated strong decay widths of Ξ baryons up to the N=2 harmonic-oscillator shell using a nonrelativistic chiral quark model with a tree-level quark-meson pseudoscalar coupling. The model parameters (constituent quark masses mu=md=350 MeV, ms=450 MeV, oscillator parameter αh=0.40 GeV, meson decay constants fπ=132 MeV, fK=fη=160 MeV) were fixed, and the global coupling strength δ=0.576 was determined by fitting the measured width of Ξ(1530)0 → Ξπ (9.1 MeV). Partial and total widths were then computed for each candidate assignment and compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Ξ(1530)0 total width: 9.1 MeV (input, experimental value 9.1 ± 0.5 MeV)
- Ξ(1530)− total width: predicted 10.3 MeV (experimental 9.9 +1.7 −1.9 MeV)
- Global coupling parameter δ = 0.576
- For Ξ(1690) assigned to |70,²8,1,1,1/2−⟩: predicted total width ≈ 48 MeV, with partial width ratios Γ(Ξπ)/Γ(ΣK̅) ≈ 0.2, Γ(ΣK̅)/Γ(ΛK̅) ≈ 1.0, Γ(Ξ(1530)π)/Γ(ΣK̅) ≈ 0.0002
- For Ξ(1690) assigned to |70,⁴8,1,1,1/2−⟩: predicted total width 86.78 MeV
- For Ξ(1690) assigned to |70,²10,1,1,1/2−⟩: predicted total width 10.63 MeV
- Broad Ξ(1950) states observed in Ξπ channel have Γ ∼ 100 MeV; moderate width states have Γ ∼ 60 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach previously applied to charmed baryons and heavy-light mesons (Zhong 2007, Liu 2012) to the poorly known Ξ baryon spectrum. It addresses the long-standing controversy over the spin-parity of Ξ(1690), which had been assigned as J^P=1/2+ by Chao (1980) and Melde (2008) but as J^P=1/2− by Pervin and Roberts (2007) and the Skyrme model (Oh 2007). The results support the J^P=1/2− assignment for Ξ(1690) and J^P=3/2− for Ξ(1820), consistent with the BABAR evidence (Aubert 2008) and the PDG determination, respectively. The analysis enables future experimental searches for missing Ξ resonances, particularly in the Ξ(1530)π and Σ(1385)K̅ channels at J-PARC, PANDA, JLab, and BESIII.
