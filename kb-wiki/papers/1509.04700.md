# One-nucleon transfer reactions and the optical potential

> We provide a summary of new developments in the area of direct reaction theory with a particular focus on one-nucleon transfer reactions. We provide a status of the methods available for describing (d,p) reactions. We discuss the effects of nonlocality in the optical potential in transfer reactions. The results of a purely phenomenological potential and the optical potential obtained from the dispersive optical model are compared; both point toward the importance of including nonlocality in transfer reactions explicitly. Given the large ambiguities associated with optical potentials, we discuss some new developments toward the quantification of this uncertainty. We conclude with some general comments and a brief account of new advances that are in the pipeline.

## Key claim

The paper argues that including nonlocality explicitly in optical potentials is necessary for a reliable description of one-nucleon transfer reactions, and that modern uncertainty quantification techniques can address the large ambiguities associated with optical potentials, though this work is still in its infancy.

## Method

The authors compare transfer reaction calculations using local and nonlocal optical potentials within the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) and the Adiabatic Distorted Wave Approximation (ADWA), using both the Perey-Buck nonlocal potential and potentials from the dispersive optical model. They also perform a chi-squared minimization fit to elastic scattering data for neutrons on Ca-48 at 23.2 MeV, using a Woods-Saxon parameterization, and then propagate the uncertainties from the optical potential parameters to the predicted transfer cross section for Ca-48(d,p)Ca-49 by sampling 200 parameter sets from the Gaussian distribution around the minimum.

## Key numbers

- For Sn-126(d,p)Sn-127 at E_d = 20 MeV, including nonlocality in the exit channel within DWBA changes the cross section by up to 20% in magnitude.
- For Ca-40 hole states studied with DWBA and nonlocality only in the exit channel, the effect of nonlocality on transfer is up to 50%.
- For Sn-126(d,n)Sb-127 at E_d = 20 MeV, the effect of nonlocality doubles the cross section at the peak.
- The best-fit parameters for elastic scattering of Ca-48(d,d)Ca-48 include a volume term real depth of 162.4 MeV (initialized at 103.2 MeV), a radius of 0.98 fm (initialized at 1.05 fm), and a diffuseness of 0.69 fm (initialized at 0.86 fm).
- The 95% confidence band for the transfer cross section Ca-48(d,p)Ca-49 at 23.2 MeV is obtained by pulling 200 parameter sets from the Gaussian distribution and discarding the largest 5 and lowest 5 predicted cross sections per angle.

## Context

This work extends benchmarks of CDCC and Faddeev methods for (d,p) reactions (Upadhyay 2012, Nunes 2011) and studies of nonlocality in transfer reactions (Titus 2014, Ross 2015). It also builds on the dispersive optical model (DOM) and the Perey-Buck nonlocal potential. The paper enables future work on solving Faddeev equations in a Coulomb basis for heavy nuclei, extracting optical potentials from coupled-cluster theory, and developing systematic uncertainty quantification for reaction theory.
