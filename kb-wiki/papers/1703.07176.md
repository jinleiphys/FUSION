# Two-body Wave Functions, Compositeness, And The Internal Structure Of Dynamically Generated Resonances

> Recently, the compositeness, defined as the norm of a two-body wave function for bound and resonance states, has been investigated to discuss the internal structure of hadrons in terms of hadronic molecular components. From the studies of the compositeness, it has been clarified that the two-body wave function of a bound state can be extracted from the residue of the scattering amplitude at the bound state pole. Of special interest is that the two-body wave function from the scattering amplitude is automatically normalized. In particular, while the compositeness is unity for energy-independent interactions, it deviates from unity for energy-dependent interactions, which can be interpreted as a missing-channel contribution. In this manuscript, we show the formulation of the two-body wave function from the scattering amplitude, evaluate the compositeness for several dynamically generated resonances such as $f_{0} (980)$, $Λ(1405)$, and $Ξ(1690)$, and investigate their internal structure in terms of the hadronic molecular components.

## Key claim

The compositeness (norm of the two-body wave function) extracted from the scattering amplitude is automatically normalized and equals unity for energy-independent interactions, but deviates from unity for energy-dependent interactions; this deviation can be interpreted as a missing-channel contribution, and the method is applied to several dynamically generated resonances to assess their hadronic molecular character.

## Method

The authors formulate the two-body wave function and compositeness from the residue of the scattering amplitude at the bound-state or resonance pole in the complex energy plane, using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a separable interaction. They then evaluate the compositeness for several dynamically generated hadronic resonances by employing chiral perturbation theory for the interaction, computing the compositeness as \(X_j = -\gamma_j^2 [dG_j/dE]_{E=E_{\text{pole}}}\) and the missing-channel contribution \(Z\) from the sum rule.

## Key numbers

- For \(f_0(980)\): pole at \(988 - 4i\) MeV; \(X_{K\bar{K}} = 0.87 - 0.04i\); \(\tilde{X}_{K\bar{K}} = 0.87\); \(Z = 0.07 + 0.02i\); \(U = 0.00\)
- For \(\Lambda(1405)\) higher pole: pole at \(1424 - 26i\) MeV; \(X_{\bar{K}N} = 1.14 + 0.01i\); \(\tilde{X}_{\bar{K}N} = 0.64\); \(Z = -0.08 + 0.19i\); \(U = 0.77\)
- For \(\Xi(1690)\): pole at \(1684 - 1i\) MeV; \(X_{\bar{K}\Sigma} = 0.95 - 0.14i\); \(\tilde{X}_{\bar{K}\Sigma} = 0.85\); \(Z = 0.06 + 0.11i\); \(U = 0.13\)
- For \(\Delta(1232)\): pole at \(1207 - 50i\) MeV; \(X_{\pi N} = 0.87 + 0.35i\); \(\tilde{X}_{\pi N} = 0.78\); \(Z = 0.13 - 0.35i\); \(U = 0.31\)
- For \(f_0(500)\): pole at \(443 - 217i\) MeV; \(Z = 1.09 - 0.37i\); \(U = 0.54\) (interpretation not clear)
- For \(K_0^*(800)\): pole at \(750 - 227i\) MeV; \(\tilde{X}_{\pi K} = 0.38\); \(\tilde{Z} = 0.62\); \(U = 0.28\)

## Context

This work extends the compositeness framework developed by Weinberg (1965) and later by Hyodo (2013) and Sekihara (2014, 2015) for analyzing hadronic molecular components of resonances. The method enables model-dependent but systematic classification of dynamically generated resonances into hadronic molecular states versus states with missing-channel contributions, with specific results showing that \(f_0(980)\), the higher pole of \(\Lambda(1405)\), and \(\Xi(1690)\) are dominated by two-body molecular components in the chiral perturbation theory model used.
