# Dilaton emission and absorption from far-from-equilibrium non-abelian plasma

> Using gauge/gravity duality, we study emission and absorption rates of scalar quanta from far-from-equilibrium N = 4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma. By comparing the emission and absorption rates to expectations from the Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem, we study how the spectrum thermalizes and how the thermalization time depends on the four momentum of the emitted quanta.

## Key claim
Using gauge/gravity duality, the authors compute emission and absorption rates of scalar (dilaton) quanta from far-from-equilibrium N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma and show that the thermalization time of these rates depends on the four-momentum of the quanta, with timelike modes equilibrating faster than lightlike modes at high frequency.

## Method
The authors create a homogeneous but anisotropic non-equilibrium SYM plasma by applying a time-dependent Gaussian gravitational field to an initially equilibrium plasma at temperature T_i, then solve the 5d Einstein equations numerically to obtain the dual black brane geometry. They compute the 5d Wightman functions for a dilaton field using a prescription where ultraviolet vacuum fluctuations near the event horizon propagate along horizon-skimming geodesics and emerge from a stretched horizon, with the resulting bulk correlators encoding the SYM Wightman functions g_± via boundary asymptotics. The emission and absorption rates are analyzed through Gabor transforms (minimally averaged Wigner transforms) with temporal resolution parameter σ = 1/πT_f, and the degree of thermalization is quantified by comparing the ratio of emission to absorption rates against the Fluctuation-Dissipation Theorem.

## Key numbers
- The energy density changes by a factor of fifty, with final temperature T_f = 50^{1/4} T_i.
- The Gaussian boundary metric parameter has amplitude c = 2.11 and width T = 1/πT_f.
- The temporal resolution parameter for Gabor transforms is σ = 1/πT_f.
- Emission rates for timelike and lightlike dilatons are shown at ω = 8πT_f.
- The horizon-to-boundary transit time for equilibrated Hawking flux is Δt ∼ 2/πT_f.
- Lightlike modes at high frequency have a parametrically long equilibration time scaling as t ∼ (ωσ)^{1/4}/πT_f.

## Context
This work extends the holographic study of thermalization in strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma initiated by Chesler (2008) and builds on the numerical recipe for computing Wightman functions developed by Caron-Huot, Chesler, and Teaney (2011). The results contrast with weakly coupled plasmas where quantum fluctuations seed instabilities that grow exponentially (Mrowczynski 1993, Arnold 2003), whereas in the gravitational theory fluctuations equilibrate even in the collisionless approximation. The findings enable future semi-analytic studies of the equilibration of high-momentum lightlike modes and provide a benchmark for understanding thermalization in heavy-ion collisions.
