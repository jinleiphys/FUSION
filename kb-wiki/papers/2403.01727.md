# Identification of the $G(3900)$ as the P-wave $D\bar{D}^*/\bar{D}D^*$ resonance

> The BESIII Collaboration recently performed a precise measurement of the $e^+e^-\rightarrow D\bar{D}$ Born cross sections, and confirmed the $G(3900)$ structure reported by BaBar and Belle with high significance. We identify the $G(3900)$ as the first P-wave $\DDbar$ molecular resonance. The experimental and theoretical identification of the P-wave dimeson state holds paramount importance in enhancing our comprehension of the non-perturbative QCD and few-body physics. Its existence is firmly established in a unified meson-exchange model which simultaneously depicts the features of the $χ_{c1}(3872)$, $Z_c(3900)$ and $T_{cc}(3875)$. This scenario can be directly examined in the $e^+e^-\rightarrow D\bar{D}^*/\bar{D}D^*$ cross section by seeing whether a resonance exists at the threshold. The credibility of the investigations is also ensured by the fact that the P-wave interaction dominantly arises from the well-known long-range pion exchange. Additionally, thanks to the centrifugal barrier, it is easier to form resonances in P-wave than in S-wave. We extensively calculate all systems up to P-wave with various quantum numbers and predict a dense population of the $\DDbar$ and $\DD$ states, where the S-wave $\DDbar$ state with $I^G (J^{PC})=0^- (1^{+-})$, P-wave $\DDbar$ state with $I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(0^{-+})$, and P-wave $\DD$ state with $I(J^P)=0(0^-)$ are more likely to be observed in experiments.

## Key claim

The paper identifies the $G(3900)$ structure, recently confirmed by BESIII in $e^+e^-\rightarrow D\bar{D}$ cross sections, as the first P-wave $D\bar{D}^*/\bar{D}D^*$ molecular resonance, but this identification is conditional on a specific unified meson-exchange model that simultaneously describes the $\chi_{c1}(3872)$, $Z_c(3900)$, and $T_{cc}(3875)$ states, and the authors note that a recent $K$-matrix analysis by Husken et al. (2024) found no additional bare pole near 3900 MeV, which they argue is due to defects in that analysis that they correct.

## Method

The authors construct one-boson-exchange (OBE) potentials for the $D\bar{D}^*$ and $DD^*$ systems, including exchanges of $\pi$, $\eta$, $\sigma$, $\rho$, and $\omega$ mesons, with coupling constants fixed to reproduce the $\chi_{c1}(3872)$, $Z_c(3900)$, and $T_{cc}(3875)$ states. They solve the complex-scaled Schrodinger equation in momentum space to obtain resonance and bound state poles, using a monopole regulator with cutoff $\Lambda$ as the only free parameter, adjusted to generate a pole at threshold in the $1^{++}$ isoscalar $D\bar{D}^*$ channel (the $\chi_{c1}(3872)$). They then search for poles in all channels up to orbital angular momentum $L=1$ for both $D\bar{D}^*$ and $DD^*$ systems.

## Key numbers

- The $G(3900)$ pole position is found at $-4.0-27.3i$ MeV (relative to the $D\bar{D}^*$ threshold) for $\Lambda=0.5$ GeV, and at $2.0-27.3i$ MeV for $\Lambda=0.6$ GeV, corresponding to a resonance with width approximately 55 MeV.
- The $\chi_{c1}(3872)$ appears as a loosely bound state at $-3.1$ MeV (for $\Lambda=0.5$ GeV) and $-6.5$ MeV (for $\Lambda=0.6$ GeV) below the $D\bar{D}^*$ threshold.
- The $T_{cc}(3875)$ appears as a bound state at $-0.41$ MeV (for $\Lambda=0.5$ GeV) and $-4.3$ MeV (for $\Lambda=0.6$ GeV) below the $DD^*$ threshold.
- The $Z_c(3900)$ appears as a virtual state at $-34.8$ MeV (for $\Lambda=0.5$ GeV) and $-39.5$ MeV (for $\Lambda=0.6$ GeV) below the $D\bar{D}^*$ threshold.
- The BESIII Breit-Wigner fit gives the $G(3900)$ mass as $3872.5\pm 14.2\pm 3.0$ MeV and width as $179.7\pm 14.1\pm 7.0$ MeV.
- The cutoff $\Lambda$ is varied from 0.4 GeV to 1.3 GeV in the pole trajectory analysis.

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange model approach previously applied to S-wave dimeson states by Tornqvist (1991, 1993) and Li et al. (2012), who predicted the $T_{cc}$ as a $DD$ bound state using parameters from the $\chi_{c1}(3872)$. The authors directly contradict the recent $K$-matrix analysis by Husken et al. (2024), which concluded no additional pole near 3900 MeV was needed, arguing that analysis did not fulfill analyticity requirements. The identification of $G(3900)$ as a P-wave resonance enables future experimental searches in $e^+e^-\rightarrow D\bar{D}^*$ cross sections near threshold and in hidden-charm final states such as $J/\psi\eta$ and $\eta_c\omega$, and predicts additional P-wave states in the $D\bar{D}^*$ and $DD^*$ systems that could be observed.
