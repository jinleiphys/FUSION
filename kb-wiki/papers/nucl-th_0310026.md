# Proton-nucleus elastic scattering and the equation of state of nuclear matter

> We calculate differential cross sections for proton-nucleus elastic scattering by using a Glauber theory in the optical limit approximation and nucleon distributions that can be obtained in the framework of macroscopic nuclear models in a way dependent on the equation of state of uniform nuclear matter near the saturation density. We find that the peak angle calculated for unstable neutron-rich nuclei in the small momentum transfer regime increases as the parameter L characterizing the density dependence of the symmetry energy decreases. This is a feature associated with the L dependence of the predicted matter radii.

## Key claim

The peak angle in the small-momentum-transfer regime of proton-nucleus elastic scattering, calculated for unstable neutron-rich nuclei using a Glauber model, increases as the density symmetry coefficient \(L\) decreases; this correlation is larger for larger neutron excess and suggests a possible method to determine \(L\) from future measurements, though the comparison of absolute peak angles is accompanied by systematic errors from the neglect of pairing, shell effects, and nucleon distribution tails.

## Method

The authors used a macroscopic nuclear model based on a simplified extended Thomas-Fermi theory to generate neutron and proton density distributions for spherical nuclei, with the energy per nucleon of uniform nuclear matter parametrized by the equation-of-state parameters \(n_0, w_0, S_0, K_0,\) and \(L\). These distributions were then input into a Glauber multiple scattering model in the optical limit approximation to calculate differential cross sections for proton-nucleus elastic scattering at incident energies of 400, 800, and 1047 MeV, using a Monte Carlo integration for the phase shift function and free proton-nucleon scattering parameters from Ray (1979).

## Key numbers

- For \(p\)-\(^{124}\)Sn elastic scattering at \(T_p=800\) MeV, the first scattering peak angle decreases with \(L\) for \(L\) values ranging from 5 to 80 MeV (with \(K_0=230\) MeV), with the correlation larger for larger neutron excess.
- For \(p\)-\(^{80}\)Ni elastic scattering at \(T_p=800\) MeV, the \(L\)-induced change in the peak angle difference between \(^{58}\)Ni and \(^{80}\)Ni amounts to order 0.1 deg.
- The \(L\)-induced change in the matter radius difference, \(\Delta R_{\rm m} \sim 0.1\) fm, is related to the angle change by \(k \Delta \theta_{\rm c.m.} \sim \pi \Delta R_{\rm m}/R_{\rm m}^2\).
- To estimate \(L\) within \(\pm 20\) MeV, systematic measurements of peak angles for neutron excesses \(\alpha\lesssim0.3\) with accuracy of \(\pm 0.01\) deg are desired.
- For stable nuclei, \(K_0\)-induced uncertainties in the peak angle are typically \(\pm 0.03\) deg, and \(L\)-induced uncertainties in the peak height are typically \(\pm 3\%\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation (Iida, Oyamatsu, and Abu-Ibrahim, 2001) which showed that matter radii of unstable neutron-rich nuclei depend appreciably on \(L\) while being almost independent of \(K_0\). The present study directly calculates the elastic scattering cross sections that would be measured in experiments, rather than requiring extraction of radii from cross sections via approximate scattering theory as in Batty (1981) and Ray (1979). The results suggest that future systematic measurements of proton elastic scattering off unstable nuclei, such as Ni isotopes, could help determine the density dependence of the symmetry energy, complementing constraints from stable nuclei masses and radii which leave \(L\) uncertain.
