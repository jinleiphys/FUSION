# Bound state structure and electromagnetic form factor beyond the ladder approximation

> We investigate the response of the bound state structure of a two-boson system, within a Yukawa model with a scalar boson exchange, to the inclusion of the cross-ladder contribution to the ladder kernel of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The equation is solved by means of the Nakanishi integral representation and light-front projection. The valence light-front wave function and the elastic electromagnetic form factor beyond the impulse approximation, with the inclusion of the two-body current, generated by the cross-ladder kernel, are computed. The valence wave function and electromagnetic form factor, considering both ladder and ladder plus cross-ladder kernels, are studied in detail. Their asymptotic forms are found to be quite independent of the inclusion of the cross-ladder kernel, for a given binding energy. The asymptotic decrease of form factor agrees with the counting rules. This analysis can be generalized to fermionic systems, with a wide application in the study of the meson structure.

## Key claim

The asymptotic large-momentum behavior of both the valence light-front wave function and the elastic electromagnetic form factor of a two-boson bound state in the Yukawa model is essentially independent of whether the cross-ladder contribution is included in the Bethe-Salpeter kernel, provided the binding energy is held fixed by adjusting the coupling constant.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for two spinless bosons exchanging a scalar boson was solved in Minkowski space using the Nakanishi integral representation for the BS amplitude, with the kernel taken either as the ladder exchange alone or as the ladder plus the lowest-order cross-ladder diagram. The valence light-front wave function was obtained from the Nakanishi weight function via a light-front projection, and the elastic electromagnetic form factor was computed from the BS amplitude using both the impulse (triangle) diagram and the two-body current diagram generated by the cross-ladder kernel, with the loop integrals evaluated analytically via Feynman parametrization.

## Key numbers

- For binding energy \(B = 1.5\,m\) and exchanged boson mass \(\mu = 0.15\,m\), the coupling constant \(\alpha = g^2/(16\pi m^2)\) is \(\alpha^{(L)} = 6.2812\) for the ladder kernel and \(\alpha^{(L+CL)} = 4.1399\) for the ladder plus cross-ladder kernel; the ratio \(\alpha^{(L)}/\alpha^{(L+CL)} = 1.5172\) matches the ratio of the asymptotic LF wave functions \(\psi^{(L)}_{LF}/\psi^{(L+CL)}_{LF} = 1.5774\) at \(\gamma = 500\,m^2\) and \(\xi = 1/2\).
- For \(B = 1.5\,m\) and \(\mu = 0.5\,m\), \(\alpha^{(L)} = 7.7294\), \(\alpha^{(L+CL)} = 5.1568\), ratio \(1.4988\); wave function ratio \(1.5395\).
- For \(B = 0.1\,m\) and \(\mu = 0.15\,m\), \(\alpha^{(L)} = 1.4365\), \(\alpha^{(L+CL)} = 1.1052\), ratio \(1.2997\); wave function ratio \(1.2763\).
- The impulse contribution to the form factor decays as \(F_I(Q^2) \sim Q^{-4}\) for large \(Q^2\); the two-body current contribution decays as \(F_X(Q^2) \sim Q^{-6}\), i.e., a factor \(Q^{-2}\) faster.
- The maximal two-body current contribution at \(Q^2 = 0\) is about 15% of the total form factor, achieved for \(\mu = 0.15\,m\) and \(B = 1.5\,m\).

## Context

This work extends the Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter approach beyond the ladder approximation, which was previously applied to two-boson bound states in the ladder kernel by Gutierrez et al. (2016) and to the cross-ladder kernel by Carbonell et al. (2006). The asymptotic behavior of the form factor agrees with the quark counting rules of Lepage and Brodsky (1980) and with the Wick-Cutkosky model results of Hwa (2004). The analysis enables future studies of fermionic systems, with applications to meson structure, and provides a framework to separate leading-twist (impulse) from higher-twist (two-body current) contributions to electromagnetic form factors.
