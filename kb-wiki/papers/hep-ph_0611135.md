# Longitudinal Broadening of Quenched Jets in Turbulent Color Fields

> The near-side distribution of particles at intermediate transverse momentum, associated with a high momentum trigger hadron produced in a high energy heavy-ion collision, is broadened in rapidity compared with the jet cone. This broadened distribution is thought to contain the energy lost by the progenitor parton of the trigger hadron. We show that the broadening can be explained as the final-state deflection of the gluons radiated from the hard parton inside the medium by soft, transversely oriented, turbulent color fields that arise in the presence of plasma instabilities. The magnitude of the effect is found to grow with medium size and density and diminish with increasing energy of the associated hadron.

# Longitudinal Broadening of Quenched Jets in Turbulent Color Fields

## Key claim
The observed rapidity broadening (ridge) of near-side associated hadrons in heavy-ion collisions can be explained by the final-state deflection of medium-induced radiated gluons by soft, transversely polarized turbulent color fields arising from plasma instabilities, rather than by elastic scattering alone. The effect grows with medium size and density but diminishes with increasing energy of the associated hadron.

## Method
The authors model the production of medium-induced gluon radiation using a factorized approach that separates the short-distance hard scattering and radiation process from subsequent soft scattering in the medium. They then evolve the gluon distribution using a Fokker-Planck equation with a diffusion tensor derived from the color Lorentz force generated by turbulent color fields, solving via spherical harmonic decomposition to track the angular broadening in momentum space.

## Key numbers
- Initial transport coefficient (jet quenching parameter) assumed: $\hat{q} = 2.2$ GeV$^2$/fm
- Original jet transverse momenta considered: 10 GeV/c and 20 GeV/c, with gluon carrying forward momentum fraction $z_2 = 0.4$
- Jet vertex formed at a depth of 3 fm within the medium
- Ridge-shaped distribution extends over more than one unit of rapidity in both directions along the beam axis
- Associated hadrons in the ridge are concentrated at transverse momenta $p_T < 2$ GeV/c

## Context
This work extends earlier proposals by Romatschke (2006) that elastic collisions in an expanding anisotropic medium could explain the ridge, but notes that the large momentum anisotropy parameter $\xi \sim 10$ required would be inconsistent with the low shear viscosity $\eta/s \leq 0.3$ extracted from data. The authors instead build on studies of plasma instabilities in anisotropic quark-gluon plasmas (Randrup 2003, Romatschke 2003, Arnold 2003) and the resulting turbulent color fields, which have been proposed as a mechanism for fast thermalization and anomalous shear viscosity (Asakawa 2006). The work enables experimental tests of whether plasma instabilities exist in the quark-gluon plasma and establishes their role in the transport of hard probes, with a predicted enhanced baryon-to-meson ratio in the ridge due to hadronization via parton recombination.
