# Testing charm quark thermalisation within the Statistical Hadronisation Model

> A wealth of data on charmonium production in Pb-Pb collisions from the LHC experiments has provided strong evidence for (re-)generation as a dominant production mechanism at low transverse momentum. We present an important extension of the statistical hadronisation model to describe $\rm{J}/ψ$ transverse momentum distributions based on input parameters from hydrodynamical simulations. Comparison to the data allows the testing of the degree of thermalisation of charm quarks in the quark-gluon plasma. To this end we will report analyses of the $\rm{J}/ψ$ transverse momentum spectra in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ and $5.02$ TeV.

## Key claim

The paper tests the degree of thermalisation of charm quarks in the quark-gluon plasma by extending the statistical hadronisation model (SHM) to describe J/ψ transverse momentum distributions, using input parameters from hydrodynamical simulations; the agreement with data at low transverse momentum supports full thermalisation, but the overshoot at high pT suggests an additional production mechanism is gaining importance.

## Method

The authors extended the statistical hadronisation model to calculate J/ψ transverse momentum spectra by assuming charm quarks thermalise in the quark-gluon plasma and follow the collective expansion of the fireball, modelled using MUSIC(3+1)D hydrodynamical simulations with IP-Glasma initial conditions. The resulting freeze-out hyper surface at T = T_CF = 156.5 MeV was used to constrain a blast-wave function, which models the shape of the thermal part of the pT spectrum, normalised to the core fraction from the SHM; the corona part was modelled using J/ψ measurements in pp collisions. The model was compared to ALICE data for Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 and 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature T_CF = 156.5 MeV with vanishing baryon chemical potential μB.
- At forward rapidity for √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, the SHM calculations for J/ψ and ψ(2S) nuclear modification factor R_AA as a function of centrality show very good agreement with data (Adam 2016, ALICE Tarhini).
- For central collisions (0-10%) at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, the SHM describes the J/ψ R_AA as a function of rapidity very well, following the data trend of decreasing towards larger rapidities (PhysRevLett.109.072301, Abelev 2013).
- At forward rapidity for √s_NN = 2.76 TeV and centrality 0-20%, the SHM transverse momentum spectrum agrees very well with ALICE data at low pT, but shows an overshoot for pT ≳ 5 GeV (ALICE:JPsiForw2760).
- At mid-rapidity for √s_NN = 5.02 TeV and centrality 0-20%, the SHM transverse momentum spectrum agrees very well with preliminary ALICE data at low pT (ALICE:Dennis).

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronisation model of Andronic (2006) by incorporating transverse momentum spectra, building on earlier SHM calculations that described charmonium yields and nuclear modification factors. The results contradict screening-dominated models (e.g., Matsui and Satz 1986) by showing that the rapidity dependence of J/ψ R_AA follows the charm cross section shape rather than screening predictions. The good agreement at low pT provides the most convincing demonstration that the medium consists of deconfined quarks, while the high-pT discrepancy enables future work to identify the additional production mechanism, reminiscent of behaviour observed for open charmed mesons (ALICE:Dmeson2018) and charged particles (ATLAS:Jpsi2018).
