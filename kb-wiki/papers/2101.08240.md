# Renormalization group improved pressure for hot and dense quark matter

> We apply the renormalization group optimized perturbation theory (RGOPT) to evaluate the quark contribution to the QCD pressure at finite temperatures and baryonic densities, at next-to-leading order (NLO). Our results are compared to NLO and state-of-the-art higher orders of standard perturbative QCD (pQCD) and hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt). The RGOPT resummation provides a nonperturbative approximation, exhibiting a drastically better remnant renormalization scale dependence than pQCD, thanks to built-in renormalization group invariance consistency. At NLO, upon simply adding to the RGOPT-resummed quark contributions the purely perturbative NLO glue contribution, our results show a remarkable agreement with ab initio lattice simulation data for temperatures $0.25 \lesssim T \lesssim 1 \, {\rm GeV}$, with a remnant scale dependence drastically reduced as compared to HTLpt.

## Key claim

The authors apply the renormalization group optimized perturbation theory (RGOPT) to compute the quark contribution to the QCD pressure at finite temperature and baryonic density at next-to-leading order (NLO), and claim that upon adding the purely perturbative NLO glue contribution, the results show remarkable agreement with lattice data for temperatures 0.25 ≲ T ≲ 1 GeV with drastically reduced renormalization scale dependence compared to hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt). The hedge is that the RGOPT resummation is applied strictly only to the quark sector, while gluons are treated in standard NLO perturbation theory, and the method is not yet extended to the gluon sector due to specific technical difficulties.

## Method

The authors start from the perturbative expression for the massive quark pressure at NLO (two-loop order) in the MS renormalization scheme, then restore perturbative RG invariance by subtracting finite zero-point contributions with coefficients determined from the QCD beta-function and anomalous mass dimension. They then variationally deform the Lagrangian by rescaling the coupling and modifying quark mass terms with an exponent a = γ₀/(2b₀) determined by requiring the reduced massless RG equation to hold, expand in powers of the bookkeeping parameter δ, set δ → 1, and fix the arbitrary variational mass parameter either by the mass optimization prescription (MOP) ∂P/∂m = 0 or by the reduced RG equation as an independent constraint.

## Key numbers

- The RGOPT results are compared to lattice data for temperatures in the range 0.25 ≲ T ≲ 1 GeV
- The pseudocritical temperature at μ = 0 is T_pc ≈ 155 MeV (from lattice QCD, cited as prior work)
- The QCD scale used is Λ_MSbar = 335 MeV for N_f = 3, corresponding to α_s(N_f = 3, 1.5 GeV) ≈ 0.326
- The one-loop Debye mass is m_PT² = (g/6)T² + O(g²) for μ = 0
- The LO RGOPT dressed mass in the high-T approximation for μ = 0 gives m̄² ≈ (3/8)gT² + O(g²)
- The Stefan-Boltzmann ideal gas limit for quarks is P_SB = T⁴ N_f N_c (7π²/180)(1 + (120/7)μ̂² + (240/7)μ̂⁴) with μ̂ = μ/(2πT)

## Context

This work extends the RGOPT approach previously applied to the scalar φ⁴ model (Kneur et al., 2018) and to cold quark matter at finite density and zero temperature (Kneur et al., 2020) to include thermal effects. It directly compares with standard perturbative QCD (pQCD) up to four-loop order (Kajantie et al., 2003; Kurkela et al., 2010) and with hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) at NNLO (Andersen et al., 2011; Mogliacci et al., 2013), which show substantial remnant renormalization scale dependence that increases at higher orders. The RGOPT approach aims to overcome this limitation by building in RG invariance at all stages, and the present work enables future extension to the gluon sector (work in progress) and application to the full QCD phase diagram at finite baryonic density where lattice QCD suffers from the sign problem.
