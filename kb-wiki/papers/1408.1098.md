# Probing the non-equilibrium dynamics of hot and dense QCD with dileptons

> It is argued that, in heavy ion collisions, thermal dileptons are good probes of the transport properties of the medium created in such events, and also of its early-time dynamics, usually inaccessible to hadronic observables. In this work we show that electromagnetic azimuthal momentum anisotropies do not only display a sensitivity to the shear relaxation time and to the initial shear-stress tensor profile, but also to the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity coefficient.

## Key claim

Thermal dileptons in heavy ion collisions are sensitive not only to the shear relaxation time and initial shear-stress tensor profile, but also to the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity coefficient, which hadronic observables cannot reveal because their memory of early-time dynamics fades by freeze-out.

## Method

The authors used the 3+1D relativistic hydrodynamics code MUSIC to model Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, with a lattice QCD equation of state and Israel-Stewart theory for the shear-stress tensor evolution. Thermal dilepton rates were computed using the Born rate with viscous corrections to the distribution functions obtained by solving the Boltzmann equation for a massless gas with constant 2-to-2 cross sections. The initial energy density profile was determined by the Monte-Carlo Glauber model, and the initial shear-stress tensor, shear relaxation time, and temperature dependence of eta/s were varied systematically.

## Key numbers

- Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, 20-40% centrality class
- Default eta/s = 1/(4pi) (constant); temperature-dependent eta/s(T) = a(T/T_tr - 1) + 1/(4pi) with T_tr = 0.18 GeV and slope parameters a = 0, 0.2427, and 0.5516
- Shear relaxation time tau_pi = b_pi * eta/(epsilon + P) with default b_pi = 5, and tested values b_pi = 10 and 20
- Initial shear-stress tensor parameterization pi_0^{munu} = c * 2*eta*sigma^{munu} with c = 0, 1/2, and 1
- 200 events computed for each parameter configuration
- For thermal dileptons, increasing eta/s(T) in the QGP phase increases v2(M) for invariant masses M < 1.2 GeV, but decreases v2(M) for M > 1.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends studies by Niemi (2011) which showed that charged hadrons have small sensitivity to the temperature dependence of eta/s in the QGP phase at top RHIC energy. The authors demonstrate that thermal dileptons, unlike hadrons, retain memory of the QGP transport properties throughout the collision evolution, making them complementary probes. This enables future extraction of the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity coefficient and initial non-equilibrium conditions from experimental dilepton data at RHIC and LHC, as experimental uncertainties decrease.
