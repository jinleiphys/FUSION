# Symmetry energy in cold dense matter

> We calculate the symmetry energy in cold dense matter both in the normal quark phase and in the 2-color superconductor (2SC) phase. For the normal phase, the thermodynamic potential is calculated by using hard dense loop (HDL) resummation to leading order, where the dominant contribution comes from the longitudinal gluon rest mass. The effect of gluonic interaction to the symmetry energy, obtained from the thermodynamic potential, was found to be small. In the 2SC phase, the non-perturbative BCS paring gives enhanced symmetry energy as the gapped states are forced to be in the common Fermi sea reducing the number of available quarks that can contribute to the asymmetry. We used high density effective field theory to estimate the contribution of gluon interaction to the symmetry energy. Among the gluon rest masses in 2SC phase, only the Meissner mass has iso-spin dependence although the magnitude is much smaller than the Debye mass. As the iso-spin dependence of gluon rest masses is even smaller than the case in the normal phase, we expect that the contribution of gluonic interaction to the symmetry energy in the 2SC phase will be minimal. The different value of symmetry energy in each phase will lead to different prediction for the particle yields in heavy ion collision experiment.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the symmetry energy in cold dense quark matter using QCD-based methods and find that while gluonic interactions have a small effect in the normal phase, non-perturbative BCS pairing in the 2SC phase enhances the symmetry energy because gapped states are forced into a common Fermi sea, reducing the number of quarks that can contribute to asymmetry.

## Method

The thermodynamic potential for the normal quark phase was calculated using hard dense loop (HDL) resummation to leading order, with the dominant contribution coming from the longitudinal gluon rest mass. For the 2SC phase, the authors used high density effective field theory (HDET) to estimate the gluonic contribution to the symmetry energy, finding that only the Meissner mass has isospin dependence among the gluon rest masses, though its magnitude is much smaller than the Debye mass.

## Key numbers

- The density region considered lies between 3 to 5 times nuclear matter density, where $\rho_0 = 0.16~\textrm{fm}^{-3}$
- The quark chemical potential range considered is $0.35~\textrm{GeV} \leq \mu \leq 0.5~\textrm{GeV}$
- The ratio $P(\mu)/P_{\textrm{ideal}}(\mu)$ from gluon resummation alone is approximately 1.1 at $\mu=1~\textrm{GeV}$, while adding quark resummation gives approximately 0.8 at $\mu=1~\textrm{GeV}$
- The constants from the longitudinal integration are $\alpha = 0.321336$ and $\beta = -0.176945$
- The constants from the transverse integration are $\bar{\alpha} = 0.142727$ and $\bar{\beta} = -0.200869$
- The 2SC gap sizes considered are $\Delta=50~\textrm{MeV}$ and $\Delta=150~\textrm{MeV}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy in quark matter that used the MIT bag model (Di Toro, 2006; DiToro, 2009) and NJL-type model calculations (Pagliara, 2010) by incorporating QCD-based methods including HDL resummation and color superconductivity. The results enable predictions for particle yields in heavy ion collision experiments, as the different symmetry energy values in each phase will lead to different predictions for isospin-asymmetric observables.
