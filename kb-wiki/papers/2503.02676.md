# The Electron-Ion Collider as A Prospective Facility for Pentaquark Measurements

> The Electron-Ion Collider provides a groundbreaking opportunity to study heavy pentaquarks with unprecedented precision, leveraging its high collision energy and beam spin polarization capabilities. As a representative case, we analyze electroproduction cross sections of Pc (4312) under different spin-parity hypotheses using the vector meson dominance model. To ensure a parameter-free approach and minimize ambiguity, we incorporate results from the LHCb and GlueX experiments. To characterize the spin and the parity of Pc (4312), we propose measuring the beam spin asymmetry and decay kinematic polarization, quantities that can be accurately determined by the ePIC detector. Our approach can be extended to investigate other heavy pentaquarks produced in electron-proton and electron-deuteron collisions, as well as to study their interactions with nuclear matter in electron-heavy ion collisions. We strongly encourage the EIC community to explore this potential and integrate pentaquark studies as a critical element of the scientific mission.

## Key claim

The Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) can produce and characterize heavy pentaquarks such as Pc(4312) with sufficient statistics for precision measurements, provided that the branching ratio for Pc → p + J/ψ is constrained using LHCb and GlueX data to make the vector meson dominance model parameter-free.

## Method

The authors calculate electroproduction cross sections for Pc(4312) in e+p collisions at the EIC using the vector meson dominance model, where the virtual photon from the electron couples to a J/ψ meson which then interacts with the proton. The coupling constant g_{JpPc} and branching ratio BR for each of six spin-parity hypotheses (J^P = (1/2)^±, (3/2)^±, (5/2)^±) are fixed by requiring consistency with the LHCb measurement of the total decay width (Γ_tot = 9.8 MeV) and with the GlueX photoproduction cross section. The differential cross sections are computed as functions of pseudorapidity η within |η| ≤ 4, matching the ePIC detector coverage, for both unpolarized and polarized beam configurations.

## Key numbers

- Total decay width of Pc(4312): Γ_tot = 9.8 MeV, as measured by LHCb
- Branching ratio for J^P = (3/2)^−: BR = 2.9%, determined from GlueX data
- Coupling constant g_{J/ψ} = 11.2, determined from J/ψ → e+e− decay
- EIC center-of-mass energies: √s = 141 GeV (maximum) and √s = 40 GeV (lowest high-luminosity mode)
- Expected Pc(4312) yields at √s = 141 GeV with 100 fb−1 integrated luminosity: from 350 events for J^P = (1/2)^− to 1.0×10^8 events for J^P = (5/2)^−
- Expected Pc(4312) yields at √s = 40 GeV with 100 fb−1: from 7.7×10^4 events for J^P = (1/2)^− to 1.2×10^7 events for J^P = (5/2)^−
- Photon energy at resonance peak: E_γ = 9.44 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy pentaquarks beyond the LHCb discovery (LHCb, 2015; LHCb, 2019) and the GlueX photoproduction measurements (GlueX, 2019; JPAC, 2016), which could not determine the spin and parity of these states. The proposed measurements at the EIC would enable definitive characterization of Pc(4312) quantum numbers through beam spin asymmetry and decay kinematic polarization, and could be extended to other pentaquarks including Pc(4440), Pc(4457), and the undiscovered Pb (uudb\bar{b}) states, as well as pentaquark interactions with nuclear matter in electron-heavy ion collisions.
