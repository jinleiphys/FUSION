# Nuclear Null Tests for Spacelike Neutrinos

> Recently, a type of null experiment for spacelike neutrinos has been proposed. We examine in detail a class of null tests involving nuclear beta decay or capture in atoms and ions. The most promising candidate systems are identified.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that certain nuclear beta decay or electron capture processes, which are kinematically forbidden for atoms at rest, could become allowed if the parent nucleus is accelerated, provided the neutrino has a spacelike four-momentum (negative squared mass). The most promising candidate systems for such null tests are identified by systematically searching for reactions with the smallest negative Q values, particularly for partially stripped ions.

## Method

The authors systematically computed Q values for two-body final state reactions (beta decay with electron capture by the daughter ion, and inverse beta decay) and three-body final state reactions (standard beta decay and positron emission) for neutral atoms, fully stripped nuclei, and partially stripped ions. They used atomic mass tables and electron binding energy data from the literature, supplemented by GRASP2 code calculations for the dysprosium-holmium system, to identify candidate nuclides with the smallest negative Q values across approximately 1500 nuclides.

## Key numbers

- For two-body reactions with neutral atoms (type 2a), the smallest negative Q value is Q = -2.6 keV for the system $^{163}_{\phantom{1}66}$Dy $\rightarrow$ $^{163}_{\phantom{1}67}$Ho + $\bar\nu_e$.
- For two-body reactions with fully stripped nuclei (type 2a), the smallest negative Q value is Q = -8.5 keV for $^{157}_{\phantom{1}64}$Gd $\rightarrow$ $^{157}_{\phantom{1}65}$Tb.
- For three-body reactions with fully stripped nuclei (type 3a), the smallest negative Q value is Q = -1.6 keV for $^{241}_{\phantom{1}94}$Pu $\rightarrow$ $^{241}_{\phantom{1}95}$Am + e$^-$ + $\bar\nu_e$.
- For the dysprosium-holmium system with partial stripping, the most favorable case found is Q$_{2a}(27) \simeq$ -1.4 keV for the ion with 27 bound electrons.
- The threshold energy per nucleon for a null test scales as $\epsilon_{\rm th} \approx \bar Q / m$, where $\bar Q$ is the modulus of the Q value and $m$ is the neutrino mass parameter; for $\bar Q \approx$ 1 keV and beam energies of order 100 A GeV, the test is sensitive to $m$ on the order of 10 eV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal of null experiments for spacelike neutrinos (Chodos and Kostelecky, 1994) by providing a detailed survey of nuclear systems. It was motivated by several experiments reporting negative squared masses for the electron neutrino (five experiments with mean more than two standard deviations from zero) and muon neutrino (latest at five standard deviations from zero, though a remeasurement of a pionic x-ray intensity later allowed a solution compatible with zero). The identified candidate systems enable future experiments using existing or planned high-energy ion beams at facilities like CERN and RHIC to place lower (negative) bounds on the squared mass of the electron neutrino.
