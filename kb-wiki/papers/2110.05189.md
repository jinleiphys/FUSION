# Hot Neutron Star Matter and Proto-Neutron Stars

> In this chapter, we investigate the structure and composition of hot neutron star matter and proto-neutron stars. Such objects are made of baryonic matter that is several times denser than atomic nuclei and tens of thousands times hotter than the matter in the core of our Sun. The relativistic finite-temperature Green function formalism is used to formulate the expressions that determine the properties of such matter in the framework of the density-dependent mean field approach. Three different sets of nuclear parametrizations are used to solve the many-body equations and to determine the models for the equation of state of ultra-hot and dense stellar matter. The meson-baryon coupling scheme and the role of the \{Delta}(1232) baryon in proto-neutron star matter are investigated in great detail. In addition, using the non-local three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to describe quark matter, the hadron-quark composition of dense baryonic matter at zero temperature is discussed briefly. General relativistic models of non-rotating as well as rotating proto-neutron stars are presented in part two of our study.

## Key claim

The structure and composition of hot proto-neutron star matter are investigated using three different density-dependent relativistic mean-field parametrizations (SWL, DD2, and GM1L), with the claim that the inclusion of hyperons and Delta(1232) baryons, as well as the treatment of finite temperature via the Green function formalism, significantly alters the equation of state and the resulting stellar models, though the specific quantitative impact depends on the chosen parametrization and the evolutionary stage of the proto-neutron star.

## Method

The authors employ the relativistic finite-temperature Green function formalism within the density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) theory to derive expressions for baryon number density, scalar density, energy density, and pressure of hot, dense baryonic matter. Three nuclear parametrization sets (SWL, DD2, and GM1L) are used to solve the many-body equations, accounting for nucleons, hyperons, and Delta(1232) baryons, with leptons treated as free Fermi gases; the hadron-quark composition at zero temperature is also briefly discussed using the non-local three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear saturation density is given as a baryon number density of approximately 0.16 fm^-3, corresponding to a mass density of 2.65 x 10^14 g/cm^3.
* Proto-neutron star radius decreases from over 150 km to less than 20 km within 0.1 to 0.5 seconds after core bounce.
* The Kelvin-Helmholtz phase, during which a hot, lepton-rich proto-neutron star transitions to a cold, deleptonized neutron star, lasts less than about a minute.
* Core cooling via neutrino emission reduces the star's temperature to just a few MeV or less within the first few hundred years.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of proto-neutron star structure and evolution, such as those by Prakash (1997), Pons (1999), and Camelio (2017), by systematically comparing three DDRMF parametrizations and explicitly including Delta(1232) baryons alongside hyperons. The results enable more detailed modeling of the quasi-stationary evolution of proto-neutron stars during the Kelvin-Helmholtz phase, building on numerical simulation efforts by Fischer (2010) and Camelio (2017), and can inform future simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutrino signals.
