# Phase Diagram of Neutron-Proton Condensate in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> We investigate the phase structure of homogeneous and inhomogeneous neutron-proton condensate in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. At extremely low nuclear density the condensed matter is in homogeneous phase at any temperature, while in general case it is in Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde -Ferrell phase at low temperature. In comparison with the homogeneous superfluid, the inhomogeneous superfluid can survive at higher nuclear density and higher isospin asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the strange temperature dependence of the neutron-proton condensate found in earlier homogeneous calculations (where the maximum gap occurs at nonzero temperature or pairing only appears at intermediate temperature) is unrealistic; when the inhomogeneous Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) phase is included, the condensate becomes a regular decreasing function of temperature and the superfluid region extends to higher density and higher isospin asymmetry.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled gap equations for neutron-proton pairing in the \(^3\)S\(_1\)-\(^3\)D\(_1\) channel using a separable form of the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential, with a density-dependent effective nucleon mass from the Gogny D1S force. They compute the free energy for the normal phase, the homogeneous superfluid phase (pair momentum \(\mathbf{q}=0\)), and the LOFF phase (pair momentum \(\mathbf{q}\neq 0\)), and determine the ground state by comparing free energies. The stability of the homogeneous phase against a small pair momentum is analyzed via the superfluid density \(\rho_s\), where a negative \(\rho_s\) indicates the LOFF phase is energetically favored.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap in the \(^3\)S\(_1-^3\)D\(_1\) channel is of the order of 10 MeV at saturation nuclear density.
- The homogeneous phase is stable only for Fermi momentum \(k_F < 0.23\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) at very low temperature (0.1 MeV), where the critical isospin asymmetry approaches 1.
- The separable Paris potential parameters used are \(v_0 = -530\ \text{MeV}\cdot\text{fm}^3\), \(\eta = 0\), and an energy cutoff \(\varepsilon_c = 60\ \text{MeV}\).
- The turning temperature \(T_s\) separating the homogeneous and LOFF phases is determined; at saturation density, the LOFF momentum drops to zero continuously at \(T_s\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron-proton pairing in asymmetric nuclear matter by Sedrakian (2005) and Akhiezer (1997), which assumed a homogeneous condensate and found unusual temperature dependence of the pairing gap. It also builds on LOFF phase studies in nuclear matter at saturation density by LOFFN (2003). The results are analogous to the phase diagram of population-imbalanced two-component ultracold Fermi gases studied by Pao (2005), and the phase diagrams obtained enable future work on neutron star cooling and the role of inhomogeneous pairing in astrophysical contexts.
