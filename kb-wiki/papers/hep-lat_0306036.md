# Nucleons Properties at Finite Lattice Spacing in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Properties of the proton and neutron are studied in partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory at finite lattice spacing. Masses, magnetic moments, the matrix elements of isovector twist-2 operators and axial-vector currents are examined at the one-loop level in a double expansion in the light-quark masses and the lattice spacing. This work will be useful in extrapolating the results of simulations using Wilson valence and sea quarks, as well as simulations using Wilson sea quarks and Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks, to the continuum.

# Nucleon Properties at Finite Lattice Spacing in Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic one-loop calculation of nucleon masses, magnetic moments, axial-vector matrix elements, and twist-2 operator matrix elements in partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQchiPT) including leading O(a) corrections from finite lattice spacing, but only under the assumption of a specific hierarchy where the lattice spacing is treated as small compared to the chiral symmetry breaking scale and the infinite volume limit is taken.

## Method

The authors construct the low-energy effective field theory for nucleons and Delta resonances in partially-quenched QCD at finite lattice spacing by including the Sheikholeslami-Wohlert term in the Symanzik action and treating both the light-quark masses and the lattice spacing as small expansion parameters. They compute one-loop chiral corrections to nucleon observables using dimensional regularization with MS-bar, where the meson masses entering the loop diagrams acquire implicit dependence on the lattice spacing through the O(a) contributions to the meson mass relations. The analysis covers both Wilson fermions (where chiral symmetry is broken at O(a)) and mixed actions with Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks and Wilson sea quarks.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is taken as f ~ 132 MeV throughout the calculations
- The nucleon mass in the QCD and isospin limit at finite lattice spacing is given by M_N = M_0 - 2*overline{m}*(alpha_M^(PQ) + beta_M^(PQ) + 2*sigma_M^(PQ)) - (1/(8*pi*f^2))*[(3/2)*g_A^2*m_pi^3 + (4*g_DeltaN^2/(3*pi))*F_pi] - 2*a*c_sw^(V)*(alpha_A^(PQ) + beta_A^(PQ)) - 4*a*c_sw^(S)*sigma_A^(PQ)
- The proton magnetic moment in the isospin-symmetric QCD limit at finite lattice spacing is mu_p = mu_0 + mu_1 - (M_N/(4*pi*f^2))*[g_A^2*m_pi + (2/9)*g_DeltaN^2*F_pi]
- The neutron magnetic moment in the same limit is mu_n = mu_0 - mu_1 + (M_N/(4*pi*f^2))*[g_A^2*m_pi + (2/9)*g_DeltaN^2*F_pi]
- The proton isovector axial matrix element in the QCD limit includes terms proportional to a*c_sw^(V), a*c_sw^(S), and a*c_A7^(V) at O(a), with explicit expressions given in equation (5.23) of the paper

## Context

This work extends the earlier O(a) analysis of meson properties by Rupak and Shoresh (2003) and Bar, Rupak, and Shoresh (2003) to the baryon sector, and builds upon the partially-quenched baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of Chen and Savage (2002) and Beane and Savage (2002). The results enable extrapolation of lattice QCD simulations using Wilson fermions or mixed actions to the continuum limit, and provide the analytic structure of finite-lattice-spacing corrections that must be accounted for when extracting physical nucleon properties from lattice data at non-zero lattice spacing.
