# Tidal wave in $^{102}$Pd: An extended five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian description

> The five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian based on the covariant density functional theory is applied to investigate the observed tidal wave mode in the yrast band of $^{102}$Pd. The energy spectra, the relations between the spin and the rotational frequency, and the ratios of $B(E2)/\mathcal{J}(I)$ in the yrast band are well reproduced by introducing the empirical $ab$ formula for the moments of inertia. This $ab$ formula is related to the fourth order effect of collective momentum in the collective Hamiltonian. It is also shown that the shape evolution in the tidal wave is determined microscopically by the competition between the rotational kinetic energy and the collective potential in the framework of the collective Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian based on covariant density functional theory (5DCH-CDFT) can reproduce the tidal wave mode in the yrast band of \(^{102}\)Pd only after introducing an empirical \(ab\) formula for the moments of inertia, which the authors relate to a fourth-order expansion of the collective momentum in the Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors performed constrained covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations using the PC-PK1 functional and a density-independent \(\delta\)-force for pairing, solving the Dirac equation in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis to map the potential energy surface in the \(\beta\)-\(\gamma\) plane. They then constructed the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) from these CDFT results, using the Inglis-Belyaev formula for moments of inertia and cranking formulas for mass parameters, and solved the eigenvalue problem by expanding eigenfunctions in a basis of deformation variables and Euler angles. To improve agreement with experiment, they introduced a spin-dependent moment of inertia following the \(ab\) formula \(\mathcal{I}=a\sqrt{1+bI(I+1)}\), which they derived from including a fourth-order term in the collective momentum expansion.

## Key numbers

- The yrast band of \(^{102}\)Pd shows nearly equidistant energies up to spin \(I=14\).
- The experimental reduced transition probabilities \(B(E2)\) display a monotonic increase with spin, interpreted as evidence of seven boson condensation.
- The \(ab\) formula for the moment of inertia is given as \(\mathcal{I}=a\sqrt{1+bI(I+1)}\), with parameters \(a=1/B_2\) and \(b=-B_4/(6B_2)\), where \(B_2\) and \(B_4\) are mass parameters from the fourth-order expansion.

## Context

This work extends the application of the 5DCH-CDFT framework, previously used for phase transitions and shape evolutions (Li 2009, Song 2011), to the novel tidal wave excitation mode first identified experimentally in \(^{102}\)Pd by Ayangeakaa (2013). The tidal wave was previously modeled with the shell correction version of the tilted axis cranking model (SCTAC) by Frauendorf (2011) and a phenomenological phonon model by Macchiavelli (2014), but those approaches treated angular momentum semiclassically; the present work provides a fully quantal treatment of angular momentum and electromagnetic transitions. The introduction of the \(ab\) formula for moments of inertia, originally proposed by Wu and Zeng (1985), enables the 5DCH to reproduce the observed energy spectra and \(B(E2)\) ratios, and the analysis of shape evolution shows that the tidal wave is driven by competition between rotational kinetic energy and collective potential.
