# Decoding the Density Dependence of the Nuclear Symmetry Energy

> The large imbalance in the neutron and proton densities in very neutron rich systems increases the nuclear symmetry energy so that it governs many aspects of neutron stars and their mergers. Extracting the density dependence of the symmetry energy therefore constitutes an important scientific objective. Many analyses have been limited to extracting values for the symmetry energy, $S_0$, and its ``derivative'', $L$, at saturation density $ρ_0 \approx 2.6 \times 10^{14}~\mathrm{g/cm^3}$ $\approx 0.16~\mathrm{nucleons/fm^{3}}$, resulting in constraints that appear contradictory. We show that most experimental observables actually probe the symmetry energy at densities far from $ρ_0$, making the extracted values of $S_0$ or $L$ imprecise. By focusing on the densities these observables actually probe, we obtain a detailed picture of the density dependence of the symmetry energy from $0.25ρ_0$ to $1.5ρ_0$. From this experimentally derived density functional, we extract $L_{01}=53.1\pm6.1 MeV$ at $ρ\approx 0.10~\mathrm{fm^{-3}}$, a neutron skin thickness for $^{208}Pb$ of $R_{np} =$ $0.23\pm0.04$ fm, a symmetry pressure at saturation density of $P_0=3.2\pm1.2 MeV/fm^3$ and suggests a radius for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star of $13.1\pm0.6$ km.

# Decoding the Density Dependence of the Nuclear Symmetry Energy

## Key claim

Most experimental observables that have been used to constrain the symmetry energy $S_0$ and its slope $L$ at saturation density $\rho_0$ actually probe the symmetry energy at densities far from $\rho_0$, making the extracted values of $S_0$ or $L$ imprecise; by instead focusing on the densities these observables actually probe, the authors obtain a detailed picture of the symmetry energy density dependence from $0.25\rho_0$ to $1.5\rho_0$.

## Method

The authors decode published constraints from five observables (nuclear masses, isobaric analog states, isospin diffusion in Sn+Sn collisions, electric dipole polarizability of $^{208}$Pb, and Skyrme fits to doubly magic nuclei) by determining the "sensitive density" $\rho_s$ at which each observable accurately provides $S(\rho_s)$, using both a "cross-over" analysis where symmetry energy functionals intersect and an "inclination" analysis relating the slope $\tau = \Delta S_0 / \Delta L$ of the correlated ($S_0$, $L$) contours to $\rho_s$. They then combine these $S(\rho_s)$ values with additional constraints from neutron-proton spectral ratios, pion spectral ratios from heavy-ion collisions, and the PREXII neutron skin measurement of $^{208}$Pb to construct a symmetry energy density functional $S(\rho)$ over $0.25 < \rho/\rho_0 < 1.5$.

## Key numbers

- $L_{01} = 53.1 \pm 6.1$ MeV at $\rho \approx 0.10$ fm$^{-3}$ (the Taylor expansion coefficient at that density)
- Neutron skin thickness for $^{208}$Pb: $R_{np} = 0.23 \pm 0.04$ fm
- Symmetry pressure at saturation density: $P_0 = 3.2 \pm 1.2$ MeV/fm$^3$
- Radius for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: $13.1 \pm 0.6$ km
- From the Mass(Skyrme) constraint: $S(\rho_s) = 24.7 \pm 0.8$ MeV at $\rho_s/\rho_0 = 0.63 \pm 0.03$
- From the HIC(isodiff) constraint: $S(\rho_s) = 10.3 \pm 1.0$ MeV at $\rho_s/\rho_0 = 0.22 \pm 0.07$
- From the $\alpha_D$ constraint: $S(\rho_s) = 15.9 \pm 1.0$ MeV at $\rho_s/\rho_0 = 0.31 \pm 0.03$
- From the HIC($\pi$) constraint: $S(\rho_s) = 52 \pm 13$ MeV at $\rho_s/\rho_0 = 1.5 \pm 0.2$
- From the PREXII ($^{208}$Pb skin) constraint: $S(\rho_s) = 38.1 \pm 4.7$ MeV at $\rho_s/\rho_0 = 1 \pm 0$
- Extracted $S_0 = 33.3 \pm 1.3$ MeV, $L = 59.6 \pm 22.1$ MeV, and $K_\text{sym} = -180 \pm 96$ MeV

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy from multiple observables including nuclear masses (Kortelainen 2010, 2012), isobaric analog states (Danielewicz 2014, 2017), isospin diffusion in Sn+Sn collisions (Tsang 2004, 2009; Liu 2007), electric dipole polarizability (Tamii 2011; Roca-Maza 2013, 2015), and Skyrme fits to doubly magic nuclei (Brown 2013, 2014). The authors resolve apparent contradictions among these constraints by showing they probe different densities, and combine them with recent results from pion spectral ratios (Estee 2021) and the PREXII neutron skin measurement (Adhikari 2021) to construct a unified symmetry energy density functional. This enables direct calculation of neutron star properties including radii, tidal deformabilities, and crust-core transition properties.
