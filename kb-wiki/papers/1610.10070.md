# Chiral Extrapolation of the Sigma Resonance

> We analyze recent results on isoscalar $ππ$ scattering from a $N_f=2+1$ lattice simulation by the HadronSpectrum collaboration by re-summing the two-flavor chiral scattering amplitude of the next-to-leading order in the so-called inverse amplitude method. The lattice data can be well extrapolated to the physical pion mass. We also find that both $I=0$ and $I=1$ lattice data can be described simultaneously for pion masses up to $M_π=236$ MeV.

# Chiral Extrapolation of the Sigma Resonance

## Key claim

The lattice QCD phase shifts for isoscalar pion-pion scattering at unphysical pion masses (236 MeV and 391 MeV) can be extrapolated to the physical pion mass using the modified inverse amplitude method (mIAM) of unitarized chiral perturbation theory, but only if the low-energy constants (LECs) are refitted to the lattice data; the prediction using LECs from fits to experimental data alone fails dramatically at the heavier pion mass, predicting neither the correct sign of the scattering length nor the presence of a bound state.

## Method

The authors re-sum the two-flavor next-to-leading order chiral scattering amplitude using the modified inverse amplitude method (mIAM), which enforces elastic unitarity and corrects for the Adler zero singularity. They fit the four SU(2) low-energy constants (l1, l2, l3, l4) to lattice QCD phase shifts from the HadronSpectrum collaboration at pion masses of 236 MeV and 391 MeV, using correlated chi-squared minimization that accounts for energy eigenvalue correlations in the rho channel. The fits are performed separately for the isoscalar (sigma) channel alone and simultaneously for both isoscalar and isovector (rho) channels, with either three or four free parameters depending on whether the pion decay constant relation fixes l4.

## Key numbers

- Lattice data at M_pi = 236 MeV and M_pi = 391 MeV from the HadronSpectrum collaboration are used; the four highest-energy data points at M_pi = 391 MeV are omitted from fits due to the onset of K-anti-K channel effects.
- The mIAM with LECs from Hanhart (2008) gives chi^2/d.o.f. = 46.8 for the sigma channel at M_pi = 391 MeV, indicating a strong disagreement with lattice data.
- The best simultaneous fit to sigma and rho data at both pion masses (mIAM^4_sigma(236,391)rho(236,391)) yields l1 = -2.6e-3, l2 = +8.6e-3, l3 = -20.8e-3, l4 = -17.7e-3, with chi^2/d.o.f. = 4.0 for sigma(391) and 1.4 for rho(391).
- The sigma resonance pole at the physical pion mass from the mIAM^3_sigma(236,391) fit has Re W = 434 MeV and -Im W = 197 MeV, with coupling |g_sigma-pi-pi| = 383 MeV.
- At M_pi = 391 MeV, the sigma becomes a bound state on the first Riemann sheet with pole position Re W = 750 MeV and -Im W = 0 MeV, with coupling |g_sigma-pi-pi| = 573 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral extrapolation methodology of Hanhart (2008) and Gomez Nicola (2007), who developed the mIAM for fitting experimental pion-pion scattering data, to the new lattice QCD results of the HadronSpectrum collaboration (Briceno 2016) for the isoscalar channel. The analysis also builds on the three-flavor IAM approach of Hu (2016) and Guo (2016) for the rho channel. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations to be connected to physical resonance properties, but reveal that the mIAM's range of validity may be exhausted at pion masses near 391 MeV, suggesting the need for higher-order chiral contributions or explicit inclusion of strange quark effects via the f0(980) resonance.
