# Differential flow correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> A systematic analysis of correlations between different orders of $p_T$-differential flow is presented, including mode coupling effects in flow vectors, correlations between flow angles (a.k.a. event-plane correlations), and correlations between flow magnitudes, all of which were previously studied with integrated flows. We find that the mode coupling effects among differential flows largely mirror those among the corresponding integrated flows, except at small transverse momenta where mode coupling contributions are small. For the fourth- and fifth-order flow vectors $V_4$ and $V_5$ we argue that the event plane correlations can be understood as the ratio between the mode coupling contributions to these flows and and the flow magnitudes. We also find that for $V_4$ and $V_5$ the linear response contribution scales linearly with the corresponding cumulant-defined eccentricities but not with the standard eccentricities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that correlations between differential (p_T-dependent) anisotropic flow harmonics largely mirror those of integrated flows, except at low transverse momenta where mode coupling contributions are small; for V_4 and V_5, the event-plane correlations can be understood as the ratio of mode coupling contributions to the flow magnitudes, and the linear response part scales with cumulant-defined eccentricities rather than standard moment-based eccentricities.

## Method

The authors used the VISH2+1 hydrodynamic code with MC-Glauber initial conditions to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, evolving 2000 events per centrality class (and up to 42000 events for some analyses) with shear viscosity eta/s=0.08, starting at tau_0=0.6 fm/c and ending at T_dec=120 MeV. They computed p_T-differential flow vectors and correlations using the Cooper-Frye prescription, then analyzed event-plane correlations via scalar products, flow magnitude correlations via event-shape selection with q_n binning, and mode coupling coefficients from decompositions of higher-order flow vectors.

## Key numbers

- Hydrodynamic evolution started at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c and ended at T_dec = 120 MeV
- Shear viscosity set to eta/s = 0.08 as default value
- 2000 events per centrality class used for event-plane correlation analysis; 3000 events per 5% centrality bin for mode coupling and magnitude correlation analyses; 42000 events total for event-shape selection study divided into 14 equal centrality classes
- For p_T-differential event-plane correlations, the correlations become approximately equal to integrated ones around p_T approx 0.5 GeV/c
- For flow magnitude correlations, NSC^v(3,2) and NSC^v(4,3) change sign from negative to positive around p_T approx 3 GeV/c (citing Zhu (2016))
- Mode coupling coefficients for differential flows are generically smaller than those for integrated flows for p_T >= 0.5 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of integrated flow correlations (Teaney (2010), Jia (2012), Aad (2014)) and nonlinear mode coupling effects (Yan (2015), Qian (2016)) to p_T-differential observables, using the same hydrodynamic framework as Song (2007) and Shen (2014). It enables experimental comparisons with LHC data from Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, particularly for understanding how flow angle decorrelation at low p_T (Heinz (2013)) affects higher-order flow harmonics, and tests the cumulant-based eccentricity proposal of Teaney (2012, 2013).
