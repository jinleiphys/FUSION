# Resonances in the quark model

> A discussion is presented of the estimates of the energy and width of resonances in constituent models, with focus on the tetraquark states containing heavy quarks.

## Key claim

The paper argues that resonances in constituent quark models can be reliably estimated using a combination of real scaling (stabilization) and complex scaling or complex absorption methods with a Gaussian expansion basis, but acknowledges that for multiquark systems the situation is "more delicate" and requires very large bases (more than a thousand terms) compared to atomic benchmarks where a few terms suffice.

## Method

The authors apply real scaling (stabilization) and complex scaling methods to benchmark potentials (Bardsley potential, Hazi-Taylor potential), atomic systems (H⁻, He), and tetraquark systems (cc\bar c\bar c) using Gaussian expansion method (GEM) trial wave functions. For the quark-model calculations, they use a standard potential containing spin-independent and spin-spin interactions with color factors, and compare results from real-plus-complex scaling with those from the complex absorption method where an imaginary confining potential is added.

## Key numbers

- Bardsley potential with V₀=7.5: results with N=8 Gaussians agree perfectly with reference calculations (2013JPhA...46h5303K)
- H⁻ ground state variational energy: E_var = -0.527727 a.u., compared to exact E_ex = -0.527751 a.u.
- H⁻ resonance near E = -0.15 a.u. (atomic units)
- Helium ground state: E₀ = -2.90368 a.u., compared to benchmark E₀ = -2.903722 a.u. by Kinoshota
- Helium S-wave resonance: complex energy -0.79484 - i 0.001245 a.u. (from literature, 2018JPhB...51x5001S)
- cc\bar c\bar c J^P=2⁺ resonances from Wang (2022): M - iΓ/2 = 7068.5 - i 41.8 MeV and 7281.3 - i 45.6 MeV
- cc\bar c\bar c with BGS potential: resonance near 6.9 - i 0.04 GeV (with α=1.55) and near 7.1 - i 0.12 GeV (with α=2.55)
- cc\bar c\bar c: accumulation of points near E = 7.0 - i 0.04 GeV (Meng et al., 2023)
- Critical mass ratio for stability of M⁺m⁺M⁻m⁻ in atomic physics: M/m ≈ 2.2 (1997PhRvA..55..200B)

## Context

This work extends the methodology of resonance calculations from atomic physics benchmarks (Bardsley potential from 1974, Hazi-Taylor potential from 1970) to quark-model multiquark systems, following the approach of Hiyama et al. (2005, 2018) who applied real scaling to pentaquark states. The paper contrasts with molecular approaches to XYZ states and addresses the long-standing question of whether the constituent quark model can provide a unified picture of ordinary hadrons and resonances. The results enable future systematic studies of tetraquark resonances such as cc\bar c\bar c, which have been suggested experimentally by CMS (2023).
