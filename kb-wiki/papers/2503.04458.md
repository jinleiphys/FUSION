# Quantum energies of solitons with different topological charges

> The vacuum polarization energy is the leading quantum correction to the classical energy of a soliton. We study this energy for two-component solitons in one space dimension as a function of the soliton's topological charge. We find that both the classical and the vacuum polarization energies are linear functions of the topological charge with a small offset. Because the combination of the classical and quantum offsets determines the binding energies, either all higher charge solitons are energetically bound or they are all unbound, depending on model parameters. This linearity persists even when the field configurations are very different from those of isolated solitons, and would not be apparent from an analysis of their bound state spectra alone.

## Key claim

Both the classical energy and the vacuum polarization energy of two-component solitons in one space dimension are linear functions of the topological charge with a small offset, and this linearity persists even when the field configurations are very different from those of isolated solitons and would not be apparent from an analysis of their bound state spectra alone.

## Method

The authors study a two-field generalization of the sine-Gordon model in 1+1 dimensions, where a second scalar field produces attraction between single charge configurations so that multi-charge solutions can emerge as single bound lumps. They numerically construct static soliton solutions for topological charges up to N=12 using a shooting method with Newton algorithm, then compute the vacuum polarization energy (the leading quantum correction) via the spectral method, which expresses the continuum contribution as a momentum integral weighted by the change in the density of states derived from scattering data, with Born subtractions and counterterms to render the result finite.

- Classical energy for N=1 at μ₁=2.0, μ₂=8.0: 11.14

- Critical loop-counting parameter v² for binding/unbinding transition at μ₂=8.0: v²≈19
- Classical energy of a single sine-Gordon soliton in the present units: 8

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of quantum corrections to BPS vortices in scalar electrodynamics (Graham 2022), where only winding numbers up to four were considered due to numerical complexity. It uses the two-field generalization of the sine-Gordon model proposed by Halcrow (2023), which allows multi-charge solutions as single bound lumps rather than widely separated solitons. The results enable systematic study of binding energies in higher topological charge sectors, showing that the linear dependence of both classical and quantum energies on topological charge determines whether all higher charge solitons are bound or unbound depending on model parameters, with implications for chiral soliton models such as the Skyrme model (Skyrme 1961) where quantum corrections have been suggested to resolve mass disagreements (Meier 1996).
