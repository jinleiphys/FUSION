# Shell-model description of N ~ Z 1f7/2 nuclei

> For natural parity states of several odd-A nuclei a comparison of shell model calculations in the full pf configuration space with the Nilsson diagram and particle-rotor predictions shows that prolate strong coupling applies at low excitation energy, revealing multi-quasiparticle rotational bands and, in some cases, bandcrossings. Moreover, ground state bands experience a change from collective to non-collective regime, approaching the termination. Similar features are observed in the even-even nuclei. In the even-even N=Z nuclei evidence of the vibrational gamma-band is found. A review of non-natural parity structures is furthermore presented.

# Shell-model description of N ~ Z 1f7/2 nuclei

## Key claim
Shell-model calculations in the full pf configuration space reproduce experimental spectroscopic data for nuclei in the middle and second half of the 1f7/2 shell, showing that prolate strong coupling applies at low excitation energy and that ground-state bands undergo a change from collective to non-collective regime approaching termination. The authors qualify that K cannot be considered a good quantum number, with mixings of order ten percent often estimated, and that the rotor formulas should be considered qualitative since K-values are mixed by the Coriolis force.

## Method
Shell-model calculations were performed in the full pf configuration space using the KB3G effective interaction (with comparisons to KB3 and FPD6), assuming isospin conservation and using bare nucleon g-factor values with effective charges of 1.5 for protons and 0.5 for neutrons. The ANTOINE code was used on standard PC computers. For nuclei with A greater than or equal to 42, space truncation was necessary, allowing a limited number of nucleons s to be moved from the 1f7/22p3/2 subspace to the 1f5/2 and 2p1/2 orbitals. Calculated electromagnetic moments were compared with predictions from the axial rotor model and the Nilsson diagram.

## Key numbers
- In 53Fe, the terminating state (I^pi = 19/2-, Ex = 3049 keV, tau = 3.6 min) has a calculated Qs value of 42.9 efm^2 (with s=4 truncation), corresponding to beta* approximately 0.11 from the rotor formula.
- The B(E2) value for the 5/2- to 1/2- transition in 53Fe is 31 Wu (Weisskopf units), indicating fast E2 transition in the K=1/2 sideband.
- In 43Sc, the B(E2) value for the transition from yrast 9/2- to 7/2- is 13.6 efm^2, compared to 217.2 efm^2 for the same transition in 53Fe.
- The experimental B(E2, 6+ to 4+) value in 54Fe is 39.7(5) efm^2; KB3G gives 40.3 efm^2, GXPF1 gives 40.7 efm^2, and FPD6 gives 46.7 efm^2 (all with s=4 truncation), while the 1f7/2^n prediction is 23.1 efm^2.
- In 49Cr, the initial beta* value for the ground-state band is 0.28 from measured lifetimes; the K^pi = 13/2- bandhead has Qs = 51.2 efm^2 (beta* = 0.24), and the K^pi = 23/2- bandhead has Qs = 38.8 efm^2 (beta* = 0.16).
- The calculated g-factor of the 19/2- terminating state in 53Fe is 0.770; the experimental g-factor of the 19/2- level in 43Sc is 0.329(1), and of the 6+ level in 54Fe is 1.37(3).
- The 1f7/2^n configuration contribution to the 53Fe ground-state band ranges from 54% to 64% across spins 7/2 through 19/2, while in 43Sc it ranges from 72% to 96%.

## Context
This work extends the shell-model description of N approximately Z 1f7/2 nuclei previously developed for 48Cr (Caurier 1995), 50Cr (Martinez-Pinedo 1996), and the mirror pair 49Cr-49Mn (Martinez-Pinedo 1997). It complements recent reviews (Caurier 2001, KB3G 2001) by providing further evidence of smooth terminations, multi-quasiparticle bands, and bandcrossings not considered in detail previously. The calculations enable comparison with cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) approaches (Juo 1998) and projected shell model (PSM) calculations (Ha 1994), and provide predictions for electromagnetic moments that can be tested experimentally, particularly for terminating states.
