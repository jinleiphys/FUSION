# Skyrme-RPA study of charged-current neutrino opacity in hot and dense supernova matter

> Neutrino emission and their transport of energy to the supernova shock region are sensitive to the physics of hot and dense nuclear matter, which is a complex problem due to the strong correlations induced by nuclear forces. We derive charged-current opacities for electron neutrinos and antineutrinos in supernova matter using a self-consistent approach based on the Skyrme effective interaction. We include a mean field energy shift due to nuclear interaction and corrections due to RPA correlations. A complete treatment of the full Skyrme interaction, including a spin-orbit term, is given. To test the effect of RPA corrections, neutrino and antineutrino opacities are computed using different Skyrme parametrizations consistent with a number of infinite matter constraints.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, in hot and dense supernova matter, the combined effect of mean-field potential differences and random-phase approximation (RPA) correlations makes the protoneutron star matter more opaque for neutrinos but more transparent for antineutrinos compared to a noninteracting Fermi gas model; however, the magnitude of these effects depends on the specific Skyrme parametrization used, with the KDE0v1 force predicting a substantially larger mean-field potential difference than the other four parametrizations considered.

## Method

The authors computed charged-current opacities for electron neutrinos and antineutrinos in supernova matter using a self-consistent Skyrme-RPA approach, where both the Hartree-Fock mean-field and the residual particle-hole interaction were derived from the same Skyrme effective interaction. They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the particle-hole propagator to obtain the Fermi and Gamow-Teller strength functions, including the full Skyrme interaction with a spin-orbit term, and then integrated the differential absorption cross section per unit volume to obtain opacities. Calculations were performed for five Skyrme parametrizations (KDE0v1, LNS, NRAPR, SKRA, SQMC700) at conditions relevant to the neutrino-sphere region of a protoneutron star.

## Key numbers

- At density ρ = 0.02 fm⁻³, temperature T = 8 MeV, and proton fraction Yp = 0.029 (beta-equilibrium conditions with the LNS force), the mean-field potential difference is ΔU_np = 14.66 MeV.
- For the LNS force at ρ = 0.02 fm⁻³ and Yp = 0.1, the potential difference is ΔU_np ≈ 12 MeV.
- At incoming neutrino energy E_ν = 24 MeV (the mean thermal energy at T = 8 MeV), the Hartree-Fock neutrino opacity is enhanced by about an order of magnitude compared to the noninteracting Fermi gas, while the antineutrino opacity is suppressed by almost a factor of 5.
- For E_ν = 24 MeV neutrinos, the RPA opacity is suppressed by almost a factor of 4 relative to the Hartree-Fock opacity.
- The KDE0v1 force predicts a mean-field potential difference ΔU_np = 20.7 MeV, substantially larger than for the other four parametrizations.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Martinez-Pinedo (2012) and Roberts (2012) that found mean-field potential differences enhance neutrino opacity and suppress antineutrino opacity, and by Reddy (1999) and Burrows and Sawyer (1999) that showed RPA correlations can significantly modify opacities. The paper provides a self-consistent treatment using Skyrme interactions that are also used for the equation of state, addressing the need for consistency between the interaction model used for opacities and that used for the protoneutron star matter composition, as emphasized by Reddy (1999) and Sawyer (2011). The results enable more realistic neutrino transport calculations in core-collapse supernova simulations by providing opacities that include both mean-field and RPA corrections consistently with the nuclear equation of state.
