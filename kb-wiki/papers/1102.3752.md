# Effective range expansion in various scenarios of EFT($\notpi$)

> Using rigorous solutions, we compare the ERE parameters obtained in three different scenarios of EFT($\notpi$) in nonperturbative regime. A scenario with unconventional power counting (like KSW) is shown to be disfavored by the PSA data, while the one with elaborate prescription of renormalization but keeping conventional power counting intact seems more promising.

## Key claim
Using closed-form T-matrices in pionless EFT (EFT($\not\!\pi$)), the paper compares three scenarios for power counting and renormalization. The scenario with unconventional power counting (like KSW) is disfavored by partial-wave analysis (PSA) data, while a scenario that keeps conventional power counting but uses an elaborate renormalization prescription (scenario C) appears more promising, though further fine-tuning is still needed to match data.

## Method
The author derives closed-form on-shell T-matrices for uncoupled and coupled channels in EFT($\not\!\pi$) at truncation order Δ=4, using contact potentials. The T-matrices are expressed in terms of renormalization parameters (J₀, J₂ₘ₊₁) and couplings (C_{L;ij}), with the key observation that the parameter J₀ is renormalization-group invariant in the nonperturbative regime. Three scenarios are defined: A (natural couplings, conventional prescription), B (unnatural couplings like KSW, conventional prescription), and C (natural couplings, but J₀ scaled to the physical upper scale Λ). The scaling of effective range expansion (ERE) parameters (scattering length a, effective range rₑ, shape parameters v₂, v₃, v₄) is computed for each scenario in the ³S₁ and ¹S₀ channels and compared to PSA data.

## Key numbers
- In ³S₁, the PSA scattering length scales as Λ·a ∼ ε⁻¹ (with Λ ≈ m_π± and ε ≈ 1/4), while scenario B gives ε⁻² and scenario C gives ε⁻¹.
- In ¹S₀, the PSA scattering length scales as Λ·a ∼ ε⁻², scenario B gives ε⁻², and scenario C gives ε⁻² (with tuning II).
- The PSA data give Λ³·v₂ in ³S₁ as (4.13)⁻³, scaling as ε³, while scenario B gives ε⁻¹ and scenario C gives O(1).
- The ratio of v₂ between scenario B and PSA in ³S₁ is v₂;B/v₂;P ∼ ε⁻⁴; in scenario C it is v₂;C/v₂;P ∼ ε⁻³.
- In ¹S₀, the ratio v₂;B/v₂;P ∼ ε⁻⁹/⁴, while v₂;C/v₂;P ∼ ε⁻⁵/⁴.
- The effective range rₑ in ³S₁ scales as Λ·rₑ ∼ (0.81)⁻¹ (PSA), O(1) in scenario A and C, and O(1) in scenario B (with 2ε²c̃₀₁).

## Context
This work extends the debate on renormalization and power counting in nuclear EFT, following Weinberg (1990s) and the KSW scheme (Kaplan, Savage, Wise, 1998). It directly addresses criticisms of modified power counting by Epelbaum and Gegelia (2009) and the analysis of Lepage (1997). The paper uses the tractable pionless EFT to gain insights that may apply to chiral EFT with pion exchanges, where closed-form solutions are harder to obtain. It enables future work to determine contact couplings from ERE data and to guide lattice QCD studies of nuclear forces.
