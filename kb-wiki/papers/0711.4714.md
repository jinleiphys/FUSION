# Effects due to Resonant and Continuum States on the Neutrino-Nucleus Cross Section

> Estimates of the neutrino-nucleus cross section, for the charged-current process nu+208Pb-> e+208Bi, are presented. The nuclear structure calculations have been performed by considering bound, resonant, and continuum states in the single-particle basis used to construct correlated proton-particle neutron-hole configurations. The observed features of the spectrum of 208Bi have been reproduced, as accurately as possible, by diagonalizing a phenomenological multipole-multipole interaction. Calculations of the cross section, for values of q 200 $ MeV, were performed, and the dependence of the results upon the choice of the residual proton-neutron interaction was investigated. It is found that the inclusion of resonant states in the calculation of the nuclear wave functions increases the neutrino-nucleus cross section, and that the contribution of the continuum is negligible.

## Key claim

The inclusion of single-particle resonant states in the nuclear structure calculation increases the neutrino-nucleus cross section for the charged-current process on lead, while the contribution from continuum states is negligible, contrary to earlier speculation that the continuum could play a significant role at energies around 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed a direct diagonalization of a phenomenological separable multipole-multipole proton-neutron interaction in a proton-particle neutron-hole basis for 208Bi, using the Berggren representation to include bound, quasi-bound, resonant, and continuum single-particle states. The single-particle basis consisted of 136 proton states (5 bound, 4 quasi-bound, 10 narrow resonant, and 117 continuum states) and 16 bound neutron states. The cross section for the charged-current reaction nu + 208Pb -> e- + 208Bi was calculated by expanding the leptonic wave functions in partial waves and computing the nuclear matrix elements of isospin and spin-isospin multipole operators for momentum transfers up to q = 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section saturates at approximately 6 x 10^-38 cm^2 when only bound and quasi-bound states are included, and at approximately 18 x 10^-38 cm^2 when bound, quasi-bound, and resonant states are included.
- At q = 100 MeV, the calculated cross section using the separable interaction with bound and quasi-bound states is approximately 3.04 x 10^-38 cm^2, compared to 4.16 x 10^-38 cm^2 from Volpe et al.
- The calculated energy difference between the centroids for Gamow-Teller and Fermi transitions is 0.86 MeV, and the calculated position of the Gamow-Teller resonance is 15.6 MeV, yielding an energy difference between the GTR and IAS of approximately 0.4 MeV.
- The ratio between the imaginary and real parts of the calculated Ikeda sum rule is 1.5% for Gamow-Teller transitions and 3.5% for Fermi transitions.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Fuller, Haxton and McLaughlin (2000), Elliot (2000), and Engel, McLaughlin and Volpe (2003) by including resonant and continuum single-particle states in the nuclear structure description, which previous calculations had omitted. The results contradict the speculation by Hayes and Towner that continuum contributions could be significant at energies around 100 MeV. The findings enable improved predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections relevant to supernova neutrino detection and the HALO experiment at SNOLAB, and the authors indicate that similar effects are expected for other targets such as 12C and for single and double beta decay calculations.
