# Spherical configuration of a super-dense hot compact object with particular EoS

> The equation of state (EoS) $P = P (ρ, ...)$ -- pressure as a function of density and other thermodynamical quantities -- is what generates particularities of mass--radius distribution $M (R)$ for super--dense compact stellar bodies, the remnants of cosmic cataclysms. In view of recent nuclear experiments, we propose one particular EoS, which admits the critical state characterized by density $ρ_c$ and temperature $T_c$, and which under certain conditions permits a radial distribution of the super--dense matter in "liquid" phase. We establish such conditions and demonstrate that a stable configuration is indeed possible (only) for temperatures smaller than the critical one. Using Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkoff equations for hydrostatic equilibrium, we derive the mass--radius relation for the super--dense compact objects with masses smaller than the Sun, $M \ll M_{\odot}$. The obtained results are within the constraints established by both heavy--ion collision experiments and theoretical studies of neutron--rich matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a specific equation of state (EoS) that permits a critical state (density ρ_c, temperature T_c) and shows that a stable spherical configuration of super-dense matter in the "liquid" phase is possible **only** for temperatures smaller than the critical temperature, with the resulting mass-radius relation constrained by heavy-ion collision experiments and theoretical studies of neutron-rich matter.

## Method

The authors adopt a polynomial approximation for the nuclear equation of state (EoS) of the form P = (T/m)ρ − A₁ρ² + A₂ρ³, which admits a critical point. They then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for hydrostatic equilibrium in dimensionless form, using a free-energy model per particle to derive pressure and internal energy density. The radial density distribution is obtained via a perturbative expansion in the small parameter G = γ_N ρ_c R_b / c², with boundary conditions P(R)=0 and m(0)=0.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 17.5 MeV is used as a reference value for the model parameters.
- The dimensionless incompressibility factor κ = 16.533, corresponding to K = 289.3 MeV (with T_c = 17.5 MeV).
- The model parameters are a₁ = 1.225, a₂ = 1.841, a₃ = 1.074, with z₁ = 5.5 (where z = ρ/ρ_c at the boundary for θ = 0).
- The spherical configuration for the liquid phase exists only for normalized temperature θ < θ_max = 0.826, corresponding to boundary densities z(1) between z_min(1) = 1.74 and z_max(1) = 5.5.
- The dimensionless parameter T = T_c / m_n c² ≈ 0.0186 when T_c = 17.5 MeV and m_n = 939.76 MeV.
- The critical density ρ_c is of order (0.1–0.4)ρ₀, where ρ₀ ≈ 2.8 × 10¹⁷ kg/m³ is normal nuclear matter density.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Jaqaman (1983) and Goodman (1984) by using a polynomial EoS that incorporates a critical point, and applies it to the relativistic TOV framework for super-dense compact objects with masses much smaller than the Sun. The results are consistent with constraints from heavy-ion collision experiments and theoretical studies of neutron-rich matter (e.g., references to work by Lattimer & Swesty (1991), Haensel, Potekhin & Yakovlev (2007), and others). The model enables future calculations of mass-radius relations for hot, low-mass compact objects and provides a framework for testing the liquid-gas phase transition in dense stellar remnants.
