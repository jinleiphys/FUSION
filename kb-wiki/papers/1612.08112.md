# Triple parton scatterings in proton-nucleus collisions at high energies

> A generic expression to compute triple parton scattering (TPS) cross sections in high-energy proton-nucleus (pA) collisions is derived as a function of the corresponding single-parton cross sections and an effective parameter encoding the transverse parton profile of the proton. The TPS cross sections are enhanced by a factor of $9\,A\approx 2000$ in pPb compared to those in proton-nucleon collisions at the same center-of-mass energy. Estimates for triple charm ($c\overline{c}$) and bottom ($b\overline{b}$) production in pPb collisions at LHC and FCC energies are presented based on next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) calculations for $c\overline{c}, b\overline{b}$ single-parton cross sections. At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.8$ TeV, about 10% of the pPb events have three $c\overline{c}$ pairs produced in separate partonic interactions. At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 63$ TeV, the pPb cross sections for triple-J$/ψ$ and triple-$b\overline{b}$ are ${\cal O}$(1--10 mb). In the most energetic cosmic-ray collisions observed on earth, TPS $c\overline{c}$-pair cross sections equal the total p-Air inelastic cross section.

## Key claim

Triple parton scattering (TPS) cross sections in proton-nucleus collisions can be expressed as a factorized product of single-parton scattering cross sections divided by the square of an effective TPS cross section, and are enhanced by a factor of about 9 relative to naive A-scaling of proton-nucleon TPS cross sections, rather than the factor of 9A ≈ 2000 relative to proton-proton collisions that the abstract emphasizes.

## Method

The authors derive a generic expression for TPS cross sections in proton-nucleus collisions by summing three contributions: interactions where all three partons come from one nucleon, from two different nucleons, or from three different nucleons in the nucleus. They compute the nuclear geometry integrals using a Woods-Saxon Fermi-Dirac distribution for lead (A=208, radius 6.36 fm, surface thickness 0.54 fm) and relate the effective TPS cross section to the experimentally measured DPS effective cross section. Numerical estimates for triple charm and bottom production are obtained using NNLO calculations with the Top++ code, ABMP6 proton PDFs, and EPS09 nuclear PDF modifications.

## Key numbers

- TPS cross sections in pPb are enhanced by a factor of 9.1 compared to A-scaled pN TPS cross sections, with contributions from one, two, and three nucleons in the ratio 1:4.54:3.56
- The effective TPS cross section in pPb is σ_eff,TPS,pA = 0.29 ± 0.05 mb
- At √s_NN = 8.8 TeV, the SPS charm cross section is 960 ± 450_sc ± 100_pdf mb, and the TPS triple-charm cross section is 200 ± 140_tot mb, corresponding to about 10% of pPb events having three ccbar pairs
- At √s_NN = 63 TeV, the TPS triple-bottom cross section is 11 ± 7_tot mb, and the triple-J/ψ cross section is O(1–10 mb)
- The SPS bottom cross section at √s_NN = 8.8 TeV is 72 ± 12_sc ± 5_pdf mb, with TPS triple-bottom at 0.084 ± 0.045_tot mb

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of double parton scatterings in proton-nucleus collisions (d'Enterria and Snigirev, 2014) and triple parton scatterings in proton-proton collisions (d'Enterria and Snigirev, 2016) to the proton-nucleus case. The derived formalism enables quantitative predictions for TPS processes at LHC and FCC energies, and shows that at the highest cosmic-ray energies, TPS charm-pair cross sections become comparable to the total p-Air inelastic cross section. The results indicate that at FCC energies, quadruple and higher-multiplicity parton scatterings will become non-negligible for charm production.
