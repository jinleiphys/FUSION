# Five-flavor pentaquarks and other light- and heavy-flavor symmetry partners of the LHCb hidden-charm pentaquark

> The discovery of three pentaquark peaks -- the $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ -- by the LHCb collaboration has a series of interesting consequences for hadron spectroscopy. If these hidden-charm objects are indeed hadronic molecules, as suspected, they will be constrained by heavy-flavor and SU(3)-flavor symmetries. The combination of these two symmetries will imply the existence of a series of five-flavor pentaquarks with quark content $\bar{b} c s d u$ and $b \bar{c} s d u$, that is, pentaquarks that contain each of the five quark flavors that hadronize. In addition, from SU(3)-flavor symmetry alone we expect the existence of light-flavor partners of the three $P_c$ pentaquarks with strangeness $S=-1$ and $S=-2$. The resulting structure for the molecular pentaquarks is analogous to the light-baryon octet -- we can label the pentaquarks as $P_{Q' \bar{Q}}^N$, $P_{Q' \bar{Q}}^Λ$, $P_{Q' \bar{Q}}^Σ$, $P_{Q' \bar{Q}}^Ξ$ depending on their heavy- and light-quark content (with $N$, $Λ$, $Σ$, $Ξ$ the member of the light-baryon octet to which the light-quark structure resembles and $Q'$, $\bar Q$ the heavy quark-antiquark pair). In total we predict $45$ new pentaquarks from heavy- and light-flavor symmetries alone, which extend up to $109$ undiscovered states if we also consider heavy-quark spin symmetry. If an isoquartet ($I={3/2}$) hidden-charm pentaquark is ever observed, this will in turn imply a second multiplet structure resembling the light-baryon decuplet: $P_{Q' \bar{Q}}^Δ$, $P_{Q' \bar{Q}}^{Σ^*}$, $P_{Q' \bar{Q}}^{Ξ^*}$, $P_{Q' \bar{Q}}^Ω$.

## Key claim

If the LHCb hidden-charm pentaquarks $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$, and $P_c(4457)$ are hadronic molecules, heavy-flavor and SU(3)-flavor symmetries imply the existence of a large family of partner states, including five-flavor pentaquarks containing all five quark flavors that hadronize ($\bar{b} c s d u$ and $b \bar{c} s d u$), but this prediction depends on the molecular interpretation being correct and on the octet configuration being the lowest-energy one.

## Method

The authors model the pentaquarks as non-relativistic bound states of a heavy antimeson and a heavy baryon interacting via a contact-range potential at leading order in an effective field theory (EFT). They impose heavy-flavor symmetry (HFS) and SU(3)-flavor symmetry on the potential, decompose the two-body states into octet and decuplet representations, and solve coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations with a Gaussian regulator and a cutoff $\Lambda = (0.5-1.0)\,\mathrm{GeV}$. The octet coupling constants $C_i^O$ are fixed by reproducing the masses of the three observed $P_c$ pentaquarks, and then used to predict the masses and binding energies of symmetry partners.

## Key numbers

- Masses of the three observed hidden-charm pentaquarks: $m_{P_{c1}} = 4311.9 \pm 0.7^{+6.8}_{-0.6}\,\mathrm{MeV}$, $m_{P_{c2}} = 4440.3 \pm 1.3^{+4.1}_{-4.7}\,\mathrm{MeV}$, $m_{P_{c3}} = 4457.3 \pm 0.6^{+4.1}_{-1.7}\,\mathrm{MeV}$ (LHCb data).
- The $P_{c1}$ is $8.9\,\mathrm{MeV}$ below the $\bar{D} \Sigma_c$ threshold; $P_{c2}$ and $P_{c3}$ are $21.8$ and $4.8\,\mathrm{MeV}$ below the $\bar{D}^* \Sigma_c$ threshold, respectively.
- Predicted masses for the $\bar{c} b$ five-flavor pentaquarks (strange-isoscalar): $m(P^{\Lambda}_{\bar{c} b}) = 7783^{+6}_{-5}\,\mathrm{MeV}$, $7907 \pm 7\,\mathrm{MeV}$, $7930^{+5}_{-4}\,\mathrm{MeV}$ (for partners of $P_{c1}$, $P_{c2}$, $P_{c3}$ respectively) at $\Lambda = 0.75\,\mathrm{GeV}$.
- Predicted masses for the $c \bar{b}$ five-flavor pentaquarks (strange-isoscalar): $m(P^{\Lambda}_{{c} {\bar b}}) = 7829^{+10}_{-9}\,\mathrm{MeV}$, $7858^{+12}_{-10}\,\mathrm{MeV}$, $7883^{+8}_{-7}\,\mathrm{MeV}$ at $\Lambda = 0.75\,\mathrm{GeV}$.
- Total of $45$ new pentaquarks predicted from heavy- and light-flavor symmetries alone, extending up to $109$ undiscovered states if heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) is also considered.
- Octet coupling constants at $\Lambda = 0.75\,\mathrm{GeV}$: $C^O_1 = -1.19\,(-(2.17-0.80))\,\mathrm{fm}^2$, $C^O_2 = -1.44\,(-(2.88-0.93))\,\mathrm{fm}^2$, $C^O_3 = -1.02\,(-(1.80-0.71))\,\mathrm{fm}^2$.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the LHCb pentaquarks, previously applied to hidden-charm states by Liu et al. (2019), Xiao et al. (2019), and Sakai et al. (2019), to include both heavy-flavor and SU(3)-flavor symmetry partners. It builds on earlier predictions of molecular pentaquarks by Wu et al. (2010) and on the application of heavy-quark spin symmetry to the $P_c$ multiplet by Liu et al. (2019) and Du et al. (2019). The predicted strange-isoscalar $\bar{D} \Xi_c'$ molecule mass ($4436.3^{+7.3}_{-10.4}\,\mathrm{MeV}$) closely matches the independent calculation of Xiao et al. (2019) ($4436.7\,\mathrm{MeV}$). The results provide a roadmap for experimental searches for five-flavor pentaquarks, e.g. via $B_c^{+} \Lambda$ and $B_c^{+} \Sigma$ decay channels, and can be tested against the recently observed $P_{cs}(4459)$ pentaquark, though that state involves a different (antitriplet) charmed baryon and is not a direct SU(3)-flavor partner of the $P_c$ states.
