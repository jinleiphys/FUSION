# An Effective Theory of Quarkonia in QCD Matter

> The problem of quarkonium production in heavy ion collisions presents a set of unique theoretical challenges -- from the relevant production mechanism of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ to the relative significance of distinct cold and hot nuclear matter effects in the observed attenuation of quarkonia. Inthese proceedings we summarize recent work on the generalization of non-relativistic Quantum Chromodynamics (NRQCD) to include off-shell gluon (Glauber/Coulomb) interactions in strongly interacting matter. This new effective theory provides for the first time a universal microscopic description of the in-medium interaction of heavy quarkonia, consistently applicable to a range of phases such as cold nuclear matter, dense hadron gas, and quark-gluon plasma. It is an important step forward in understanding the common trends in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus data on quarkonium suppression. We derive explicitly the leading and sub-leading interaction terms in the Lagrangian and show the connection of the leading result to existing phenomenology.

## Key claim

The authors generalize non-relativistic Quantum Chromodynamics (NRQCD) to include off-shell gluon (Glauber/Coulomb) interactions in strongly interacting matter, providing for the first time a universal microscopic description of in-medium heavy quarkonium interactions applicable to cold nuclear matter, dense hadron gas, and quark-gluon plasma. The work is motivated by the finding that traditional energy loss phenomenology overpredicts J/psi suppression by a factor of 2 to 3 and fails to describe the relative suppression of psi(2S) to J/psi, where the energy loss model predicts R_AA[psi(2S)]/R_AA[J/psi] ~ 1.1 while experimental data show the weakly bound psi(2S) is 2 to 3 times more suppressed than J/psi.

## Method

The authors construct an effective theory, NRQCD_G, by adding to the velocity renormalization group NRQCD (vNRQCD) Lagrangian terms describing interactions with medium sources through virtual Glauber/Coulomb gluon exchanges. They derive the leading and sub-leading interaction terms in the Lagrangian using three independent approaches: a background field method, a matching procedure from full QCD, and a hybrid method, all yielding identical results. The scaling of Glauber and Coulomb fields is determined for different sources of in-medium interactions (collinear, static, and soft) and summarized in a table of momentum scalings.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss model overpredicts J/psi suppression in the p_T > 10 GeV range by a factor of 2 to 3 in both minimum bias and central collisions
- The energy loss model predicts R_AA[psi(2S)]/R_AA[J/psi] ~ 1.1, while experimental data show the suppression of psi(2S) is 2 to 3 times larger than that of J/psi
- The coupling between the parton and the medium is varied in the range g = 1.7-1.9 for the energy loss calculations shown in Figure 1

## Context

This work extends the established NRQCD framework of Bodwin (1995) and the velocity renormalization group NRQCD (vNRQCD) of Luke (1999) and potential NRQCD (pNRQCD) of Pineda (1998) to include medium interactions relevant for heavy ion collisions. The authors show that traditional energy loss phenomenology, as applied by Spousta (2016) to high transverse momentum J/psi production at the LHC, fails to describe the relative suppression patterns of ground and excited quarkonium states. The new effective theory enables future studies of medium-induced modifications to the heavy quark-antiquark potential, radial excitations on short time scales, and transitions between quarkonium states, with the lowest order Lagrangian already corresponding to the collisional dissociation rates employed by Sharma (2012) and Aronson (2017).
