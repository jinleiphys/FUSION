# Femtoscopy in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions: Two Decades of Progress

> Analyses of two-particle correlations have provided the chief means for determining spatio-temporal characteristics of relativistic heavy ion collisions. We discuss the theoretical formalism behind these studies and the experimental methods used in carrying them out. Recent results from RHIC are put into context in a systematic review of correlation measurements performed over the past two decades. The current understanding of these results is discussed in terms of model comparisons and overall trends.

## Key claim

Two-particle correlations provide the primary method for determining the spatio-temporal characteristics of relativistic heavy ion collisions, but the extracted source sizes reflect only the "region of homogeneity" (the phase-space cloud of particles with similar velocities) rather than the entire source volume, especially under strong collective expansion.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical formalism connecting two-particle correlation functions to the space-time emission function through convolution with the relative two-particle wave function, including identical-particle interference, Coulomb interactions, and strong interactions. They describe experimental methods for constructing correlation functions as ratios of two-particle to single-particle spectra, and present systematic comparisons of femtoscopic measurements across beam energies spanning two orders of magnitude, particle species, and collision centralities. Gaussian parameterizations in the out-side-long coordinate system are used to extract source radii Rout, Rside, and Rlong from correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- RHIC center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV exceeds previous fixed-target experiments by a factor of 10
- The Bohr radius for pi-pi Coulomb interactions is a0 = 387 fm, while for p-K correlations it is a0 = 84 fm
- Quantum smearing from wave-packet convolution leads to spatial broadening of (Delta R)^2 ~ 0.5 - 1.0 fm^2
- For sources near 1 fm in size, quantum uncertainty effects may be significant; analyses of pi-pi correlations from e+e- collisions yield source sizes less than 1.0 fm
- The curvature of C(q) at q=0 relates to mean-square separation: -d^2C(q)/dq_i' dq_j'|_(q=0) = <r_i r_j>

## Context

This review extends the line of femtoscopic measurements that began with Hanbury-Brown and Twiss (1950s) for stellar sources and Goldhaber, Goldhaber, Lee, and Pais (1960) for proton-antiproton collisions, through Bevalac analyses by Zajc (1984) and Fung (1978) that demonstrated quantitative determination of source dimensions. The work places RHIC results in the context of two decades of correlation measurements and addresses the "HBT puzzle" where dynamical models incorporating a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma over-predict observed source sizes. The review enables future work on determining the equation of state and addressing chiral symmetry restoration through improved spatio-temporal characterization of the collision region.
