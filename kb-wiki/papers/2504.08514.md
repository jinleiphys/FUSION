# Dependence of postmerger properties on the thermal heating efficiency in neutron star mergers

> We systematically perform numerical-relativity simulations for equal-mass binary neutron star mergers for the models varying the thermal index $Γ_{\rm th}$ with three different equations of state (EOSs) of the neutron stars (NSs), which are consistent with current multimessenger observational data and state-of-the-art theoretical calculations, and two different binary total mass ($m_0=2.7\ \text{and}\ 2.9~M_\odot$). By varying the value of $Γ_{\rm th}$ within the hybrid EOS framework, we investigate the thermal effects on the merger dynamics, gravitational waves (GWs), and the dynamical mass ejection process. We find that the choice of the constant $Γ_{\rm th}$ can change the outcome of the remnant for specific EOSs and $m_0$. We also show that the dynamical ejecta mass is affected by the $Γ_{\rm th}$ value in a different way for different EOSs: for a stiff EOS the ejecta mass is high when $Γ_{\rm th}$ is small, while for softer EOSs the largest ejecta is achieved when $Γ_{\rm th} = 1.3$--$1.4$. While the inspiral motion does not depend on the $Γ_{\rm th}$ value, the postmerger phase evolution is highly affected by that. We show that the dominant peak frequency $f_2$ of the postmerger GW spectrum monotonically decreases as the $Γ_{\rm th}$ increases. We find that the universal relations between NS macroscopic properties and postmerger GW frequencies are subject to non-negligible thermal uncertainties, which can obscure the universal relation between the tidal deformability and $f_2$.

## Key claim

The choice of the constant thermal index Gamma_th within the hybrid EOS framework can change the outcome of the remnant for specific equations of state and binary total masses, but the inspiral motion does not depend on the Gamma_th value while the postmerger phase evolution is highly affected by it.

## Method

The authors performed numerical-relativity simulations of equal-mass binary neutron star mergers using the SACRA-MPI code, with three different equations of state (soft, medium, stiff) selected from an ensemble constrained by chiral effective field theory, perturbative QCD, GW170817, and NICER observations. They varied the thermal index Gamma_th from 1.1 to 2.0 within the hybrid EOS framework, where a thermal component modeled as an ideal fluid with constant Gamma_th is added to a zero-temperature cold EOS, and simulated two binary total masses of 2.7 and 2.9 solar masses. Gravitational waveforms were extracted from the Weyl scalar Psi_4 using Nakano's method, and ejecta were identified using the geodesic criterion where fluid elements satisfy -u_t > 1.

## Key numbers

* For the medium EOS with total mass 2.7 solar masses, the dominant postmerger GW peak frequency f_2 decreases monotonically from 3.17 +/- 0.03 kHz at Gamma_th = 1.1 to 2.83 +/- 0.01 kHz at Gamma_th = 2.0
* For the medium EOS with total mass 2.9 solar masses, the remnant type changes from Type II (prompt collapse) for Gamma_th = 1.1-1.3 to Type III (long-lived) for Gamma_th = 1.4-1.5, with the approximate lifetime tau_H increasing from 1.96 +/- 0.04 ms at Gamma_th = 1.1 to > 25 ms at Gamma_th = 1.5
* The dynamical ejecta mass for the medium EOS with total mass 2.7 solar masses peaks at 15 +/- 2 x 10^-3 solar masses for Gamma_th = 1.3, decreasing to 2.1 +/- 0.3 x 10^-3 solar masses at Gamma_th = 2.0
* For the stiff EOS with total mass 2.7 solar masses, the ejecta mass is 7 +/- 1 x 10^-3 solar masses at Gamma_th = 1.1 and 1.0 +/- 0.2 x 10^-3 solar masses at Gamma_th = 2.0
* For the soft EOS with total mass 2.7 solar masses, the ejecta mass is 0.5 +/- 0.1 x 10^-3 solar masses at Gamma_th = 1.1 and 2.0 +/- 0.5 x 10^-3 solar masses at Gamma_th = 2.0

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Shibata (2005) and Bauswein (2010) that showed varying Gamma_th from 1.3 to 2.0 can significantly change postmerger remnants and GW peak frequencies, and by Figura (2020) who suggested Gamma_th approximately 1.7 best approximates fully finite-temperature EOS behavior. The systematic variation of Gamma_th from 1.1 to 2.0 across three EOSs with different stiffness quantifies the thermal uncertainties in universal relations between neutron star macroscopic properties and postmerger GW frequencies, showing that these relations are subject to non-negligible thermal uncertainties that can obscure the relation between tidal deformability and f_2. This enables future multimessenger observations to account for thermal effects when constraining the nuclear equation of state from postmerger gravitational wave signals.
