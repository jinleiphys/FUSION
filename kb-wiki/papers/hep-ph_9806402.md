# On the evaluation of sunset-type Feynman diagrams

> We introduce an efficient configuration space technique which allows one to compute a class of Feynman diagrams which generalize the scalar sunset topology to any number of massive internal lines. General tensor vertex structures and modifications of the propagators due to particle emission with vanishing momenta can be included with only a little change of the basic technique described for the scalar case. We discuss applications to the computation of $n$-body phase space in $D$-dimensional space-time. Substantial simplifications occur for odd space-time dimensions where the final results can be expressed in closed form through rational functions. We present explicit analytical formulas for three-dimensional space-time.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a configuration space technique that reduces the computation of sunset-type ("water melon") Feynman diagrams with any number of massive internal lines to a one-dimensional integral, but the method is most efficient for odd space-time dimensions where closed-form results through elementary functions are obtained; for even dimensions the approach still works but requires numerical integration or series expansions.

## Method

The authors compute the Fourier transform of the polarization function \(\Pi(p)\) by starting from the configuration space representation where the diagram is a product of scalar propagators \(D(x,m_i)\) (modified Bessel functions of the third kind). They perform the angular integration in \(D\)-dimensional Euclidean space-time using the expansion of the plane wave in Gegenbauer polynomials, which reduces the problem to a one-dimensional Hankel transform over the radial variable \(x\). Ultraviolet divergences are handled by momentum subtraction at \(p=0\) using an expansion of the Bessel kernel, with an unorthodox dimensional regularization (adding a factor \(x^{2\omega}\) to the integration measure) as an intermediate step.

## Key numbers

* For a three-loop water melon diagram with two massive lines (mass \(m\)) and two massless lines at vanishing external momentum, the result is \(\Pi(0) = \left(\frac{m^2}{4}\right)^{3\lambda-1} \frac{1}{2^8 \pi^{3\lambda+3}} \frac{\Gamma(\lambda)^2 \Gamma(1-\lambda) \Gamma(1-2\lambda)^2 \Gamma(1-3\lambda)}{\Gamma(\lambda+1) \Gamma(2-4\lambda)}\) where \(D = 2\lambda+2\).
* For a sunset diagram (three massive lines) with masses \(m_1=m_2=m\), \(m_3=2m\) at pseudothreshold \(p=0\), the dimensionally regularized result in \(D=4-2\varepsilon\) dimensions is \(\Pi_D^{ref}(0) = \pi^{4-2\varepsilon} \frac{m^{2-4\varepsilon} \Gamma^2(1+\varepsilon)}{(1-\varepsilon)(1-2\varepsilon)} \left[-\frac{3}{\varepsilon^2} + \frac{8\ln 2}{\varepsilon} - 8\ln^2 2\right] + O(\varepsilon)\).
* The finite part \(F\) of the same sunset diagram at \(p=0\) after subtracting singular terms evaluates numerically to \(F = 16\pi^4 m^2 [-0.306853\ldots] = 16\pi^4 m^2 [-(1-\ln 2) \times 1.0000\ldots]\).

## Context

This work extends the configuration space techniques previously applied to massless diagrams by Chetyrkin et al. (1980) and to general massive diagrams by various authors, but exploits the special topology of water melon diagrams (also called banana or basket ball diagrams) where the angular integrations decouple completely. The method enables efficient computation of two-point functions with any number of internal massive lines, including tensor structures and modifications from particle emission at vanishing momentum, and is particularly relevant for higher-order perturbative calculations in the Standard Model and chiral perturbation theory. The approach provides a practical tool for numerical evaluation in the Euclidean domain and for constructing spectral densities via dispersion relations, complementing earlier work on sunset diagrams by Davydychev and Tausk (1993) and others.
