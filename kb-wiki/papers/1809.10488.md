# The fate of quarkonia in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies: a unified description of the sequential suppression patterns

> Measurements made at the LHC have shown that the production of the ${\rm J}/ψ$, $ψ$(2S), $Υ$(1S) and $Υ$(2S) quarkonia is suppressed in Pb-Pb collisions, with respect to the extrapolation of the pp production yields. The $ψ$(2S) and $Υ$(2S) states are more strongly suppressed than the ground states and the level of the suppression changes with the centrality of the collision. We show that the measured patterns can be reproduced by a simple model, where all quarkonia are treated in a unified way, starting from the recent realisation that, in pp collisions, the probability of quarkonium formation has a universal dependence on the binding-energy of the bound state. The hot-medium suppression effect is parametrized by a penalty factor in the binding energy, identical for all (S- and P-wave) charmonium and bottomonium states, including those that indirectly contribute to the measured results through feed-down decays. This single parameter, computed through a global fit of all available suppression patterns, fully determines the hierarchy of nuclear effects, for all states and centrality bins. The resulting faithful description of the data provides convincing evidence in favour of the conjecture of sequential quarkonium suppression induced by QGP formation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the measured suppression patterns of J/ψ, ψ(2S), Υ(1S), and Υ(2S) in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies can be reproduced by a simple model where a single parameter, a binding-energy penalty factor identical for all charmonium and bottomonium states, fully determines the hierarchy of nuclear effects across all centrality bins, providing evidence for sequential quarkonium suppression induced by quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The authors model quarkonium suppression by modifying the universal binding-energy dependence observed in pp collisions, where the probability of quarkonium formation follows a power law in binding energy (E_b^δ). In Pb-Pb collisions, they introduce a penalty factor ε that shifts the effective binding energy to (E_b − ε), with the same ε distribution applied to all S-wave and P-wave charmonium and bottomonium states, including feed-down contributions. A global fit of 37 R_AA values measured by CMS and ATLAS at √s = 5.02 TeV, across multiple centrality bins (N_part), determines the centrality-dependent average penalty ⟨ε⟩ and a constant width σ_ε, assuming ⟨ε⟩ depends linearly on ln(N_part).

## Key numbers

- The fitted average energy shift ⟨ε⟩ reaches 566 ± 15 MeV for N_part = 400 (most central collisions)
- The fitted width of the ε distribution is σ_ε = 30 ± 5 MeV
- The power-law exponent δ = 0.63 ± 0.02 at 7 TeV and 0.63 ± 0.04 at 13 TeV
- The global fit has χ² = 40 for 34 degrees of freedom (22% probability of higher χ²)
- Binding energies used: ψ(2S) = 44 MeV, J/ψ = 633 MeV, Υ(2S) = 536 MeV, Υ(1S) = 1099 MeV
- The ψ(2S) binding energy (44 MeV) is very small relative to its mass and the open charm threshold 2M(D⁰)

## Context

This work extends the universal scaling patterns observed in pp collisions by Faccioli, Lourenco, Araujo, and Seixas (2018) to nucleus-nucleus collisions, using the same binding-energy power-law parametrization. The model provides a data-driven alternative to theoretical calculations involving multiple competing effects (screening, break-up, energy loss, nuclear parton modifications) and enables future tests with more precise measurements, particularly for the Υ(3S) state and in the forward rapidity region covered by ALICE and LHCb. The results support the sequential suppression conjecture originally proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986) and refined by Digal, Petreczky, and Satz (2001) and Karsch, Kharzeev, and Satz (2001).
