# Two-Photon Exchange and Electromagnetic Proton Form Factors

> The presence of $2γ$-exchange in electron proton elastic scattering is discussed. From C-invariance and crossing symmetry, $2γ$ contribution induces a specific dependence of the reduced cross section on the variable $ε$. No evidence of such dependence exists in the available experimental data.

# Two-Photon Exchange and Electromagnetic Proton Form Factors

## Key claim
The available experimental data on elastic electron-proton scattering show no evidence of a deviation from linearity in the Rosenbluth fit, and therefore no evidence of a two-photon exchange contribution, when the interference term is parametrized with the required symmetry properties from C-invariance and crossing symmetry. However, the possibility of two-photon corrections starting from Q² = 1 GeV² is not excluded, and dedicated experiments are needed.

## Method
The author analyzed four sets of published elastic ep scattering data using a model-independent parametrization of the reduced cross section that includes a three-parameter fit: the electric form factor G_E², the magnetic form factor G_M², and a two-photon exchange parameter C. The two-photon contribution was parametrized with a specific nonlinear dependence on the polarization variable ε, derived from C-invariance and crossing symmetry applied to the annihilation channel e⁺e⁻ → p p̅, rather than assuming a linear ε dependence.

## Key numbers
- The fine structure constant α = e²/4π ≈ 1/137 sets the expected order of the two-photon exchange contribution relative to the one-photon mechanism.
- Data were analyzed up to momentum transfer squared Q² = 6 GeV², referencing the Jefferson Laboratory measurements of the structure function A(Q²) in electron-deuteron elastic scattering.
- The parametrization for the two-photon function f(Q²) used a mass scale m ≈ 1.5 GeV for a tensor or vector meson with positive C-parity.
- The extracted two-photon parameter C was found to be compatible with zero across the analyzed Q² range.

## Context
This work extends the earlier investigation by Rekalo and Tomasi-Gustafsson (1999) of possible two-photon exchange in electron-deuteron scattering data from Jefferson Laboratory (Alexandrou et al., 1999; Abbott et al., 1999). It contradicts the expectation from Gunion and others (1973) that two-photon exchange effects could increase at high momentum transfer due to the steep decrease of form factors. The analysis enables future dedicated experiments comparing electron and positron scattering, or measuring T-odd polarization observables, to definitively test for two-photon exchange contributions that would require reanalysis of all one-photon-based form factor extractions.
