# Doubly virtual Compton scattering and the beam normal spin asymmetry

> We construct an invariant basis for Compton scattering with two virtual photons (VVCS). The basis tensors are chosen to be gauge invariant and orthogonal to each other. The properties of the corresponding 18 invariant amplitudes are studied in detail. We consider the special case of elastic VVCS with the virtualities of the initial and final photons equal. The invariant basis for VVCS in this orthogonal form does not exist in the literatur. We furthermore use this VVCS tensor for a calculation of the beam normal spin asymmetry in the forward kinematics. For this, we relate the invariant amplitudes to the helicity amplitudes of the VVCS reaction. The imaginary parts of these latter are related to the inclusive cross section by means of the optical theorem. We use the phenomenological value of the transverse cross section $σ_T\sim0.1$ mbarn and the Callan-Gross relation which relates the longitudinal cross section $σ_L$ to the transverse one. The result of the calculation agrees with an existing calculation and predicts the negative values of the asymmetry $B_n$ of the order of 4-6 ppm in the energy range from 6 to 45 ppm and for very forward angles.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a new orthogonal, gauge-invariant basis for doubly virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) with 18 invariant amplitudes, which does not exist in the literature; this basis is then used to calculate the beam normal spin asymmetry \(B_n\) in forward kinematics, yielding negative values of order 4-6 ppm for beam energies from 3 to 45 GeV at very forward angles, under the condition that the Callan-Gross relation and a phenomenological transverse cross section \(\sigma_T \sim 0.1\) mbarn are employed.

## Method

The author generalizes the orthogonal basis method of Prange (for real Compton scattering) and Berg-Lindner (for singly virtual Compton scattering) to the doubly virtual case, constructing 18 gauge-invariant and mutually orthogonal basis tensors from four-vectors \(P', n, L', \tilde{K}, \tilde{K}', \tilde{K}''\). The invariant amplitudes \(B_i\) are studied under nucleon crossing and photon crossing symmetries, and the tensor is simplified for the elastic equal-virtuality case. The VVCS tensor is then related to helicity amplitudes in the Breit frame, whose imaginary parts are linked via the optical theorem to the transverse and longitudinal photoabsorption cross sections; the beam normal spin asymmetry is computed by integrating the leptonic and hadronic tensor contraction over the intermediate electron phase space.

## Key numbers

- Transverse cross section: \(\sigma_T \sim 0.1\) mbarn (phenomenological value used as input).
- Beam normal spin asymmetry \(B_n\): predicted negative values of order 4-6 ppm (parts per million) for beam energies from 3 to 45 GeV at very forward angles.
- The asymmetry calculation uses the Callan-Gross relation \(\sigma_L = \frac{Q^2}{q_1^2} \sigma_T\) to relate longitudinal and transverse cross sections.
- The integrals \(I_1^0, I_2, I_3, J_2^{00}, J_{25}\) are evaluated analytically, with expressions involving logarithms of \(\sqrt{-t}/m\), \(E_{thr}/E\), and \(Q/m\).

## Context

This work extends the orthogonal basis method for Compton scattering from real photons (Prange, 1970s) and singly virtual photons (Berg and Lindner, 1960s) to the doubly virtual case, providing a formalism that is more practical for calculations than the earlier non-orthogonal basis of Tarrach (1970s). The calculation of the beam normal spin asymmetry \(B_n\) agrees with an existing calculation and is relevant for interpreting parity-violating electron-proton scattering experiments (e.g., those by the SAMPLE, HAPPEX, and G0 collaborations) where two-photon exchange contributions are accessed. The formalism enables future dispersion-relation calculations of VVCS amplitudes and more precise evaluations of two-photon exchange effects in elastic electron-nucleon scattering.
