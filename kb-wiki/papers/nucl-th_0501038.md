# Shell-model phenomenology of low-momentum interactions

> The first detailed comparison of the low-momentum interaction V_{low k} with G matrices is presented. We use overlaps to measure quantitatively the similarity of shell-model matrix elements for different cutoffs and oscillator frequencies. Over a wide range, all sets of V_{low k} matrix elements can be approximately obtained from a universal set by a simple scaling. In an oscillator mean-field approach, V_{low k} reproduces satisfactorily many features of the single-particle and single-hole spectra on closed-shell nuclei, in particular through remarkably good splittings between spin-orbit partners on top of harmonic oscillator closures. The main deficiencies of pure two-nucleon interactions are associated with binding energies and with the failure to ensure magicity for the extruder-intruder closures. Here, calculations including three-nucleon interactions are most needed. V_{low k} makes it possible to define directly a meaningful unperturbed monopole Hamiltonian, for which the inclusion of three-nucleon forces is tractable.

## Key claim

The low-momentum interaction V_{low k} and traditional G matrices produce quantitatively similar shell-model matrix elements (overlaps > 0.99), but V_{low k} avoids the ill-defined starting-energy dependence of G matrices and can be used directly in many-body calculations without pseudopotential approximations; however, pure two-nucleon interactions fail to ensure magicity for extruder-intruder closures and underbind certain orbits, indicating that three-nucleon forces are necessary.

## Method

The authors compared V_{low k} matrix elements (derived from the Argonne v_{18} potential) with G matrix elements (Idaho A potential, rectangular Pauli operator for 4 major shells, starting energy -80 MeV) using normalized overlaps O_{AB} defined from the two-body matrix elements after subtracting their centroids. They studied the cutoff dependence (Lambda = 1.3 to 3.0 fm^{-1}) and oscillator frequency dependence (hbar-omega = 6.9 to 18.4 MeV) of V_{low k} matrix elements, and extracted the diagonal monopole Hamiltonian H_m^d to calculate binding energies and single-particle/single-hole spectra on closed-shell nuclei in 0 hbar-omega spaces.

## Key numbers

- Overlaps between V_{low k} and G matrix elements: O_{AB} > 0.99
- For V_{low k} at different cutoffs (Lambda = 1.3 to 3.0 fm^{-1}), overlaps O_{AB} are very large after rescaling by width ratios (sigma ratios from 1.003 to 1.111)
- For V_{low k} at different hbar-omega (6.9 to 18.4 MeV) at fixed Lambda = 1.9 fm^{-1}, overlaps range from 0.941 to 1.000
- Binding energy per nucleon in ^{40}Ca at hbar-omega = 12 MeV: BE/A = 8.24 MeV (Lambda = 1.6 fm^{-1}), 5.89 MeV (Lambda = 1.9 fm^{-1}), 4.52 MeV (Lambda = 2.1 fm^{-1})
- Spin-orbit splitting in ^{17}O (d orbits) and ^{41}Ca (f orbits): approximately 6.0 MeV
- Spin-orbit splitting for h orbits at hbar-omega = 8 MeV, Lambda = 1.9 fm^{-1}: 4.6 MeV
- Gaps (magicity measures): g(^{40}Ca,n) = 13 MeV calculated (7 MeV experimental), g(^{48}Ca,p) = 17 MeV calculated (6 MeV experimental), g(^{48}Ca,n) = -0.4 MeV calculated (0.5 MeV experimental), g(^{56}Ni,n) = 0.5 MeV calculated (0.6 MeV experimental)

## Context

This work provides the first detailed comparison of V_{low k} with traditional G matrices (Hjorth-Jensen, Kuo, Osnes 1995), extending the universal scaling behavior previously observed for G matrices (Abzouzi, Caurier, Zuker 1991; Dufour, Zuker 1996) to the low-momentum interaction framework. The results enable direct monopole estimates of binding energies and single-particle spectra without the starting-energy ambiguities of G-matrix approaches, and identify specific anomalies (the "l·l" anomaly and "j_max" anomaly) that must be resolved by three-nucleon forces, building on earlier work by Zuker (2003) on 3N mechanisms and by Duflo and Zuker (1999) on empirical monopole Hamiltonians.
