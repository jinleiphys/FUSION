# Constraining theoretical Corrections to Gamow-Teller Transition Rates

> We propose two novel constraints for the theoretical corrections to Gamow-Teller transition rates. The first, derived from a two-level model, predicts forbidden regions within the plane defined by the isospin-mixing correction, $δ_{C1}$, and the ratio, $η$, of the isospin-symmetry Gamow-Teller matrix elements between the upper and lower admixed states. It serves as a filter for the theoretical calculations, particularly effective for small values of $|η|$. The other employs experimental $ft$ values, incorporating the upper admixed states, and exploits mirror symmetry to eliminate isospin-invariant and nuclear structure-independent quantities. This approach not only offers an alternative mean for collectively testing theoretical corrections but also, as a byproduct, enables the extraction of $η^2$. This provides another sensitive test for the isospin-conserving component of nuclear Hamiltonian. Our investigation reveals a substantial cancellation of among radiative correction contributions in these tests.

## Key claim

The paper proposes two novel constraints for theoretical corrections to Gamow-Teller transition rates, but the first constraint derived from a two-level mixing model is only valid at the weak isospin-mixing limit and yields errors of order O(alpha^2) at eta=0, while the second constraint reveals that the shell-model predictions of eta^2 are likely the largest uncertainty source and may be unreliable.

## Method

The authors derive analytic boundaries for the isospin-mixing correction delta_C1 in the (eta, delta_C1) plane using a two-level mixing model, where eta is the ratio of isospin-symmetry Gamow-Teller matrix elements between upper and lower admixed states. They then construct two new experimental tests: one comparing master formula predictions between transitions to first and second admixed states (Delta_II), and another combining two pairs of mirror GT transitions to eliminate eta^2 (Delta_III). These tests are applied to shell-model calculations for sixteen pairs of mirror GT transitions in the p and sd shells using exact diagonalizations of effective isospin-nonconserving Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- The chi^2/nu value for Delta_I is 16.588, indicating potential underestimate of overall uncertainty
- The weighted average of Delta_I is -0.131 +/- 0.110
- The arithmetic mean of Delta_II is 0.890 +/- 4.028
- The arithmetic mean of Delta_III is 0.154 +/- 0.940
- The largest (delta_C0 - delta_C1) values are approximately 55% for decays of 17Ne(beta+)17F and 26Na(beta-)26Mg
- For 21F(beta-)21Ne and 21Mg(beta+)21Na decaying to second 5/2+ states, delta_C0 - delta_C1 exceeds 100% (428.109% and 262.784% respectively), which would be unphysical if radiative corrections are negligible
- Experimental eta^2 values range from 0.031 +/- 0.010 (26Na/26P) to 444.048 +/- 62.749 (17N/17Ne), with many disagreeing with shell-model predictions even within conservative 50% uncertainty

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of isospin-symmetry breaking in Gamow-Teller transitions by Xayavong (2023) and Smirnova (2003), who assumed zero mirror asymmetry in radiative corrections (delta_NS and delta_R'). The new constraints provide filters for theoretical calculations of delta_C1 and enable experimental extraction of eta^2, which tests the isospin-conserving component of the nuclear Hamiltonian. The results suggest that missing radiative corrections or unreliable shell-model predictions of eta^2 are significant, and that the radiative correction contributions substantially cancel in the mirror-symmetry tests.
