# Elliptic and Hexadecapole flow of charged hadron in viscous hydrodynamics with Glauber and Color Glass Condensate initial conditions for Pb-Pb collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV

> The experimentally measured elliptic ($v_{2}$) and hexadecapole ($v_{4}$) flow of charged particles as a function of transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) at midrapidity in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV are compared with the relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model simulations. The simulations are carried out for two different initial energy density profiles obtained from (i) Glauber model, and (ii) Color Glass Condensate (CGC) model. Comparison to experimental data for 10-20% to 40-50% centrality, shows that a centrality dependent shear viscosity to entropy density ($η/s$) ratio with values ranging between 0.0 to 0.12 are needed to explain the $v_{2}$ data for simulations with the Glauber based initial condition. Whereas for the CGC based initial conditions a slightly higher value of $η/s$ is preferred, around 0.08 to 0.16. From the comparison of the $v_{4}$ simulated results to the corresponding experimental measurements we observe that for the centralities 20-30% to 40-50% the $η/s$ values lies between 0.0 to 0.12 for both the initial conditions studied. The $η/s$ values obtained from our studies for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV are compared to other studies which uses both transport and hydrodynamic approaches.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($\eta/s$) extracted from comparing viscous hydrodynamic simulations to experimental elliptic flow ($v_2$) and hexadecapole flow ($v_4$) data for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV depends on both the choice of initial condition (Glauber versus Color Glass Condensate) and collision centrality, with the extracted values ranging from 0.0 to 0.16, rather than a single universal value.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the Israel-Stewart formalism with the code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA, employing two different initial transverse energy density profiles: one from the optical Glauber model and one from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) approach. The simulations used a lattice QCD + hadron resonance gas equation of state with crossover temperature 175 MeV, a freeze-out temperature of 130 MeV, and constant $\eta/s$ values of 0.0, 0.08, 0.12, and 0.16, with the initial energy density normalized to reproduce the charged particle multiplicity density for 0-5% central collisions. The simulated $v_2(p_T)$ and $v_4(p_T)$ for charged hadrons at midrapidity were compared to ATLAS and ALICE experimental data for four centrality classes (10-20%, 20-30%, 30-40%, and 40-50%).

## Key numbers

- For Glauber initial conditions, the $v_2$ data requires $\eta/s$ ranging from 0.0 to 0.12 depending on centrality (10-20% to 40-50%)
- For CGC initial conditions, the $v_2$ data prefers a slightly higher $\eta/s$ range of 0.08 to 0.16
- For $v_4$ data in centralities 20-30% to 40-50%, the $\eta/s$ values lie between 0.0 and 0.12 for both initial conditions
- The initial time $\tau_i$ was set to 0.6 fm
- The freeze-out temperature was $T_f$ = 130 MeV
- The central energy density $\epsilon_0$ for Glauber initial conditions ranges from 112 GeV/fm$^3$ (for $\eta/s$ = 0.0) to 78 GeV/fm$^3$ (for $\eta/s$ = 0.16)
- The central energy density $\epsilon_0$ for CGC initial conditions ranges from 77.7 GeV/fm$^3$ (for $\eta/s$ = 0.0) to 56.5 GeV/fm$^3$ (for $\eta/s$ = 0.16)
- The experimental charged hadron multiplicity density for 0-5% central collisions is 1601 $\pm$ 60

## Context

This work extends previous viscous hydrodynamic studies at RHIC energies (Roy 2012, Niemi 2011, Shen 2010, Chaudhuri 2009) to LHC energies, and compares results from two different initial condition models (Glauber and CGC) for both $v_2$ and $v_4$ simultaneously. The extracted $\eta/s$ values are compared to estimates from other groups using transport approaches (Xu 2011, Plumari 2011) and hydrodynamic approaches (Bozek 2011, Luzum 2010, Schenke 2011, Song 2012), with most results indicating $\eta/s$ less than 4 times the KSS lower bound of 1/4$\pi$. The study enables further investigation of temperature-dependent $\eta/s$ and the role of initial condition uncertainties in extracting transport coefficients from heavy-ion collision data.
