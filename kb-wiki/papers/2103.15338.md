# Higher order cumulants of transverse momentum and harmonic flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Higher order symmetric cumulants of global collective observables in heavy ion collisions are studied. The symmetric cumulants can be straightforwardly constructed for scalar observables: the average transverse momentum, the multiplicity, and the squares of harmonic flow vectors. Third and fourth order cumulants are calculated in the hydrodynamic model. A linear predictor of the average transverse momentum and harmonic flow coefficients in a collision is used to predict the value of the cumulants from the moments of the initial distribution. The symmetric cumulants divided by the averages (or the standard deviations) of the considered observables can be used as a fine tool to study correlations present in the initial state of the collision.

## Key claim

Higher order symmetric cumulants of the average transverse momentum, multiplicity, and harmonic flow coefficients can serve as a fine tool to study correlations present in the initial state of the collision, but the interpretation of the normalized symmetric cumulants is less obvious than for the correlation coefficient because the average transverse momentum depends on many effects (freeze-out procedure, bulk viscosity, prequilibrium flow, or the experimental transverse momentum range).

## Method

The authors used the 2-dimensional version of the hydrodynamic code MUSIC with shear viscosity η/s = 0.08 to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV and U+U collisions at √sNN = 193 GeV. For Pb+Pb initial conditions they used a two-component Glauber Monte Carlo model to calculate the initial entropy density, while for U+U collisions they used the TRENTO model (with parameter p=0). They constructed third and fourth order symmetric cumulants of scalar observables (average transverse momentum, multiplicity, and squares of harmonic flow vectors) and compared full hydrodynamic results to predictions from a linear predictor based on moments of the initial density distribution (eccentricities, RMS radius, and entropy).

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity η/s = 0.08 used in the hydrodynamic simulations
- Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV and U+U collisions at √sNN = 193 GeV
- Uranium deformation parameter β = 0.265 used for deformed nuclei calculations
- The change from normalization by the average transverse momentum to normalization by its standard deviation yields a factor in the range 20-100 for the scaled symmetric cumulants
- The fourth order normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(pT,v2^2,v3^2,v4^2) is compatible with zero within statistical accuracy for both Pb+Pb and U+U collisions
- The third order cumulants NSC(pT,v2^2,v3^2) and NSC(pT,v2^2,v4^2) show an increase in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions
- The linear predictor describes the full hydrodynamic calculation for centralities 0-50% for cumulants involving v2 and v3

## Context

This work extends the study of correlation coefficients between average transverse momentum and harmonic flow (Bozek 2016) to higher order symmetric cumulants, building on the formalism developed by Bilandzic (2013), Mordasini (2019), and Moravcova (2020). The correlation coefficient ρ(pT,vn^2) has been measured by the ATLAS Collaboration (2019, 2021) and shows sensitivity to nuclear deformation in central U+U collisions (Giacalone 2019, 2020). The proposed higher order cumulants could serve as a more sensitive probe of initial state correlations and nonlinearities in the hydrodynamic response, and the authors present predictions that could be compared to future experimental measurements.
