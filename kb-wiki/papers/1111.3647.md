# Flavor SU(4) breaking between effective couplings

> Using a framework in which all elements are constrained by Dyson-Schwinger equation studies in QCD, and therefore incorporates a consistent, direct and simultaneous description of light- and heavy-quarks and the states they constitute, we analyze the accuracy of SU(4)-flavor symmetry relations between πρπ, KρK and DρD couplings. Such relations are widely used in phenomenological analyses of the interactions between matter and charmed mesons. We find that whilst SU(3)-flavor symmetry is accurate to 20%, SU(4) relations underestimate the DρD coupling by a factor of five.

## Key claim

While SU(3)-flavor symmetry relations between πππ, KρK, and πρπ couplings are accurate to 20%, SU(4) relations underestimate the DρD coupling by a factor of five; the authors find that SU(4) symmetry is violated at a level of 360%–440% when comparing g_{DρD} to g_{KρK} and 320%–540% when comparing g_{DρD} to g_{πρπ}/2 over the domain P² ∈ [-m_ρ², m_ρ²].

## Method

The authors compute the dimensionless couplings g_{πρπ}, g_{KρK}, and g_{DρD} in impulse approximation using a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework that incorporates dressed quark propagators and meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The quark propagators are parametrized to reproduce QCD's gap equation solutions, with light quarks exhibiting dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and the charm quark treated via a confining but momentum-independent mass function. The meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are parametrized with width parameters fixed by fitting to leptonic decay constants and strong decay widths, and the couplings are computed directly as functions of the off-shell ρ-meson's four-momentum squared without extrapolation in current-quark mass or momentum.

## Key numbers

- g_{πρπ}(-m_ρ²) = 4.8, which is 8% smaller than the best numerically-intensive DSE computation value of 5.2 and compared to the experimental value of 6.0
- g_{πρπ}(-m_ρ²)/g_{πρπ}(m_ρ²) = 0.14, just 10% smaller than the result in Mitchell (1996)
- g_{KρK}(0) = 0.92, while exact SU(3) symmetry would give g_{KρK} = g_{πρπ}/2
- g_{DρD}(0) = 5.05, which is 75% larger than a QCD sum rule estimate of 3.0 ± 0.02 from Bracco (2011) and 100% larger than a vector-meson-dominance estimate of 2.52 from Lin (1999)
- The SU(4) symmetry violation at P²=0 is 440% when comparing g_{DρD} to g_{KρK}
- The monopole mass for the DρD coupling at spacelike P² is Λ_{DρD} = 0.69 GeV
- The D-meson width parameter is ω_D = 1.63 ± 0.10 GeV, yielding f_D = 206 ± 9 MeV and g_{D*Dπ} = 18.7^{+2.5}_{-1.4}
- The ρ-meson width parameter is ω_ρ = 0.56 ± 0.01 GeV, yielding f_ρ = 209 MeV compared to the experimental 216 MeV
- The integrated interaction V_0 using the authors' result is roughly 40% greater than that from the Haidenbauer (2007) parametrization with g^H_{DρD} ≈ 2 and Λ^H_{DρD} = 1.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends DSE-based studies of heavy-light meson systems (El-Bennich 2010, Ivanov 1998, Ivanov 2007) and directly tests the SU(4) flavor symmetry assumptions widely used in meson-exchange models for charmed meson interactions with nuclear matter, such as those by Haidenbauer (2007, 2010) and Yamaguchi (2011). The results enable improved parametrizations for DρD couplings in phenomenological studies of charmed nuclear bound states and D-mesic nuclei, and the authors note that using their coupling values inflates the I=1 D̅N cross-section by a factor of approximately 5 in the single-meson exchange model of Haidenbauer (2007), with material consequences for predictions of charmed resonances or bound states in nuclei.
