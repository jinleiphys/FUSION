# Final state interactions in electron scattering at high missing energies and momenta

> Calculations of two-step rescattering and pion emission for the 12C(e,e'p) reaction at very large missing energies and momenta are compared with recent data from TJNAF. For parallel kinematics, final state interactions are strongly reduced by kinematical constraints. A good agreement between calculation and experiment is found for this kinematics when one admits the presence of high momentum components in the nuclear wave function.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for the 12C(e,e'p) reaction at very large missing energies and momenta, final state interactions are strongly reduced by kinematical constraints in parallel kinematics, and a good agreement between calculation and experiment is found only when one admits the presence of high momentum components in the nuclear wave function; however, in perpendicular kinematics the agreement is less favorable, suggesting additional transverse mechanisms are needed.

## Method

The author calculated two-step rescattering contributions using a semiclassical model that accounts for the full one-hole spectral function, and pion emission contributions using a plane wave impulse approximation with MAID response functions for pion production on free nucleons. The calculations were performed for 12C in both parallel and perpendicular kinematics and compared with experimental data from the E97-006 collaboration at Jefferson Lab. The input spectral function combined a Lorentzian fit to the short-range correlation region (for momenta above 230 MeV/c and energies above 19 MeV) with a local density approximation plus Wood-Saxon potential description for the mean-field region.

## Key numbers

- The short-range correlation region represents about 10-20% of the total spectral strength
- The spectral function was parameterized with a Lorentzian energy distribution for momenta above 230 MeV/c and energies above 19 MeV
- For perpendicular kinematics at missing momentum p_m = 570 MeV/c, the predicted cross section from two-step rescattering is 50% larger than the direct process, while the experiment is almost one order of magnitude above
- Single rescattering from mean-field strength gives no appreciable contribution at missing momenta above 500 MeV/c
- The comparison was performed for missing energies up to 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical two-step rescattering model developed by Barbieri and Lapikás (2004) and Barbieri et al. (2005) by including pion emission contributions, and compares with experimental data from the E97-006 collaboration at Jefferson Lab (Daniela, 2004; Frick, 2004). The results confirm the expectation from earlier Monte Carlo simulations and kinematical arguments (discussed in IngoElba and E97proposal) that parallel kinematics provide the best chance for identifying short-range correlations. The poor agreement in perpendicular kinematics suggests that meson exchange currents involving Delta resonance excitation, which are sensitive to transverse degrees of freedom, need to be included in future calculations.
