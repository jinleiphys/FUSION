# Constraining hadron-quark phase transition parameters within the quark-mean-field model using multimessenger observations of neutron stars

> We extend the quark mean-field (QMF) model for nuclear matter and study the possible presence of quark matter inside the cores of neutron stars. A sharp first-order hadron-quark phase transition is implemented combining the QMF for the hadronic phase with "constant-speed-of-sound" parametrization for the high-density quark phase. The interplay of the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter, $L$, and the dimensionless phase transition parameters (the transition density $n_{\rm trans}/n_0$, the transition strength $Δ\varepsilon/\varepsilon_{\rm trans}$, and the sound speed squared in quark matter $c^2_{\rm QM}$) are then systematically explored for the hybrid star proprieties, especially the maximum mass $M_{\rm max}$ and the radius and the tidal deformability of a typical $1.4 \,M_{\odot}$ star. We show the strong correlation between the symmetry energy slope $L$ and the typical stellar radius $R_{1.4}$, similar to that previously found for neutron stars without a phase transition. With the inclusion of phase transition, we obtain robust limits on the maximum mass ($M_{\rm max}< 3.6 \,M_{\odot}$) and the radius of $1.4 \,M_{\odot}$ stars ($R_{1.4}\gtrsim 9.6~\rm km$), and we find that a too-weak ($Δ\varepsilon/\varepsilon_{\rm trans}\lesssim 0.2$) phase transition taking place at low densities $\lesssim 1.3-1.5 \, n_0$ is strongly disfavored. We also demonstrate that future measurements of the radius and tidal deformability of $\sim 1.4 \,M_{\odot}$ stars, as well as the mass measurement of very massive pulsars, can help reveal the presence and amount of quark matter in compact objects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that within the quark mean-field (QMF) model, the presence of a sharp first-order hadron-quark phase transition in neutron star cores imposes robust but model-dependent limits on the maximum mass (below 3.6 solar masses) and the radius of 1.4-solar-mass stars (above 9.6 km), and that a too-weak phase transition (energy density discontinuity less than about 0.2 times the transition energy density) occurring at low densities (below about 1.3-1.5 times nuclear saturation density) is strongly disfavored by current multimessenger observations.

## Method

The authors combine the quark mean-field (QMF) model for the hadronic phase of neutron star matter with the constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) parametrization for the high-density quark phase, implementing a sharp first-order phase transition via Maxwell construction. They systematically vary the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter L (from 30 to 60 MeV) and the three dimensionless CSS phase transition parameters (transition density n_trans/n_0, transition strength Delta-epsilon/epsilon_trans, and squared sound speed in quark matter c^2_QM) to compute mass-radius relations, maximum masses, and tidal deformabilities for hybrid star configurations. The results are then compared against multimessenger constraints from massive pulsar mass measurements, NICER radius estimates, and the GW170817 tidal deformability measurement.

## Key numbers

* Maximum mass of hybrid stars is bounded as M_max < 3.6 solar masses, with the constraint that M_max must be at least 2 solar masses to be compatible with observed massive pulsars.
* Radius of a 1.4-solar-mass hybrid star is bounded as R_1.4 > 9.6 km.
* For hadronic stars without phase transition in the QMF model, R_1.4 = 11.76 km for L=30 MeV and R_1.4 = 12.17 km for L=60 MeV, a relative difference of about 3.5 percent.
* For hybrid stars with n_trans/n_0 = 1.5 and Delta-epsilon/epsilon_trans = 0.2, R_1.4 = 11.83 km (L=30 MeV) and 12.31 km (L=60 MeV) for c^2_QM = 1/3; and R_1.4 = 12.43 km (L=30 MeV) and 12.85 km (L=60 MeV) for c^2_QM = 1.
* A phase transition with Delta-epsilon/epsilon_trans less than about 0.2 occurring at densities below about 1.3-1.5 n_0 is strongly disfavored.
* The maximum-mass star radius R_max is less than 11.5 km for c^2_QM = 1/3 and less than 9 km for c^2_QM = 1 in the region allowed by the 2-solar-mass constraint.
* The tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 for a 1.4-solar-mass star is Lambda_1.4 = 190 (+390, -120) at 90 percent confidence level.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the QMF model for purely hadronic neutron stars (Zhu et al. 2018, 2019) by incorporating a sharp hadron-quark phase transition via the CSS parametrization (Alford et al. 2013). It systematically explores the interplay between the nuclear symmetry energy slope L and phase transition parameters, confirming the strong correlation between L and R_1.4 found in earlier work without phase transitions. The results enable future multimessenger observations of neutron star radii, tidal deformabilities, and very massive pulsar masses to potentially reveal the presence and amount of quark matter in compact objects, and provide constraints that can distinguish between different phase transition scenarios.
