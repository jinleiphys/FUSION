# The Dielectric Skyrme model

> We consider a version of the Skyrme model where both the kinetic term and the Skyrme term are multiplied by field-dependent coupling functions. For suitable choices, this "dielectric Skyrme model" has static solutions saturating the pertinent topological bound in the sector of baryon number (or topological charge) $B=\pm 1$ but not for higher $|B|$. This implies that higher charge field configurations are unbound, and loosely bound higher skyrmions can be achieved by small deformations of this dielectric Skyrme model. We provide a simple and explicit example for this possibility. Further, we show that the $|B|=1$ BPS sector continues to exist for certain generalizations of the model like, for instance, after its coupling to a specific version of the BPS Skyrme model, i.e., the addition of the sextic term and a particular potential.

# The Dielectric Skyrme model

## Key claim

The authors propose a "dielectric Skyrme model" where the kinetic and Skyrme terms are multiplied by field-dependent coupling functions, which allows static BPS solutions saturating the topological bound only in the baryon number B=±1 sector but not for higher |B|, implying that higher-charge skyrmions are unbound. The claim is hedged by the condition that this behavior depends on specific choices of the dielectric functions satisfying the relation ef = (1/2r₀) Tr(𝕀-U), and that loosely bound higher skyrmions can only be achieved by small deformations of this model.

## Method

The authors construct a static energy functional consisting of the Dirichlet term and the Skyrme term, but with field-dependent coupling functions f(ξ) and e(ξ) that depend only on the profile function ξ of the Skyrme field via the trace Tr U. They derive topological bounds and Bogomolny equations using the three eigenvalues λ²ᵢ of the strain tensor, finding that saturation requires λ₁² = λ₂² = λ₃² = e²(ξ)f²(ξ). For the B=1 sector, they solve the Bogomolny equations using a hedgehog ansatz, obtaining the profile ξ = 2 arctan(r₀/r). They then construct a near-BPS model by deforming the dielectric functions to recover the correct small-field limit, using a piecewise function for f(ξ) with a cutoff at ξ* = π/6.

## Key numbers

- The B=1 BPS solution has profile ξ = 2 arctan(r₀/r), interpolating between π at r=0 and 0 at infinity.
- For the extremal case α=0 (constant Skyrme term), the unit BPS Skyrmion energy is E = 12π²/r₀.
- For the extremal case α=1 (dielectric Skyrme term only), the unit BPS Skyrmion energy is E = 24π² r₀.
- In the near-BPS example with r₀=1 and ξ*=π/6, the BPS contribution from the large-field region (ξ∈[π/6,π]) is E^>_BPS = π(10π + 1 + 3√3) ≈ 118.162.
- The BPS contribution from the small-field region (ξ∈[0,π/6]) is E^<_BPS = π f*(2π - 3√3) ≈ 0.4576, where f* ≈ 0.1340.
- The upper bound for the true energy in the small-field region is E_b^< = 0.6918.
- The resulting upper bound on relative binding energies is ΔE(B)/E(B) ≤ 0.00198, i.e., below 0.2%.

## Context

This work extends the BPS submodel discovered by Harland (2014), which consisted of the Skyrme term and a particular potential, by introducing field-dependent coupling functions that generalize the approach. The dielectric Skyrme model provides a systematic method to construct near-BPS models with arbitrarily small binding energies, addressing the longstanding problem of too large binding energies in the standard Skyrme model. The authors note that the BPS Skyrme model (consisting of the sextic term and a potential) already provides zero binding energies at the classical level and describes a perfect fluid, and they show that the dielectric generalization of that model does not change its qualitative properties. The work enables further exploration of in-medium Skyrme models and the construction of realistic nuclear physics effective field theories with small binding energies.
