# Magnetic structure of $A \le 10$ nuclei using the Norfolk nuclear models with quantum Monte Carlo methods

> We present Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments, form factors, and densities of $A\le 10$ nuclei within a chiral effective field theory approach. We use the Norfolk two- and three-body chiral potentials and their consistent electromagnetic one- and two-nucleon current operators. We find that two-body contributions to the magnetic moment can be large (up to $\sim33\%$ in $A=9$ systems). We study the model dependence of these observables and place particular emphasis on investigating their sensitivity to using different cutoffs to regulate the many-nucleon operators. Calculations of elastic magnetic form factors for $A\leq 10$ nuclei show excellent agreement with the data out to momentum transfers $q\approx 3$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Key claim

Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments, form factors, and densities for nuclei with mass number A ≤ 10, using the Norfolk chiral potentials and consistent electromagnetic currents, show that two-body contributions to the magnetic moment can be as large as approximately 33% in A=9 systems, and that the model dependence from different cutoff regulators used for the many-nucleon operators is significant.

## Method

The authors performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations for nuclei up to A=10 using the Norfolk two- and three-body chiral potentials (NV2+3) with consistent one- and two-nucleon electromagnetic current operators derived up to N3LO in chiral effective field theory. They computed magnetic moments by evaluating the matrix element of the current operator at small momentum transfer and fitting to a polynomial, and calculated elastic magnetic form factors by extracting reduced matrix elements of magnetic multipole operators from current matrix elements computed at multiple angles.

## Key numbers

- Two-body contributions to the magnetic moment reach up to approximately 33% in A=9 systems
- For ³H (triton), the total VMC magnetic moment with model Ia* is 2.973 μ_N, compared to the experimental value of 2.979 μ_N
- For ³He, the total VMC magnetic moment with model IIb* is -2.127 μ_N, matching the experimental value of -2.127 μ_N

- For ⁷Be, the total VMC magnetic moment with model Ia* is -1.443 μ_N, compared to the experimental value of -1.465 μ_N
- For ⁸Li, the total VMC magnetic moment with model Ia* is 1.660 μ_N, compared to the experimental value of 1.654 μ_N

- For ⁹Li, the total VMC magnetic moment with model Ia* is 3.494 μ_N, compared to the experimental value of 3.437 μ_N
- For ⁹Be, the total VMC magnetic moment with model IIb* is -1.123 μ_N, compared to the experimental value of -1.178 μ_N

- Elastic magnetic form factors show agreement with data out to momentum transfers q ≈ 3 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends previous Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments and transitions in A ≤ 9 systems by Pastore (2012), which used a "hybrid approach" combining Argonne AV18 and Illinois-7 interactions with chiral effective field theory currents, to now use fully consistent chiral Hamiltonians and currents. The study also extends the hyperspherical harmonics calculations of magnetic form factors for A ≤ 3 nuclei by Gnech (2022) to heavier systems up to A=10 using VMC methods, representing the first Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of form factors in A=7-10 systems. These validated electromagnetic current models are needed for studies of electromagnetic transitions, electron-nucleus scattering, radiative corrections to super-allowed beta decays, and interpretation of searches for new physics in precision beta-decay, neutrinoless double beta decay, and long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments.
