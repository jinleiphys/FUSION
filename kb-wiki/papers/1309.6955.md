# The merging of the islands of inversion at N=20 and N=28

> The N=20 and N=28 "islands of inversion" are described by large scale shell model calculations with an extension of the interaction sdpf-u that makes it possible to mix configurations with different Nhw or equivalently with different number of particles promoted from the sd-shell to the pf-shell. It allows to connect the classical sd-shell calculations below N=18 with the sd(protons)-pf(neutrons) calculations beyond N=24-26, for all the isotopes from Oxygen to Calcium, using the same interaction. For some isotopes this range contains all the nuclei between the proton and the neutron drip lines and includes the N=20 and N=28 islands of inversion. We shall pay particular attention to the properties of the states at fix Nhw which turn out to be the real protagonists of the physics at N=20. The existence of islands of inversion/deformation will be explained as the result of the competition between the spherical mean field which favors the 0hw configurations and the nuclear correlations which favor the deformed Nhw configurations. The Magnesium chain is exceptional because in it, the N=20 and N=28 "islands of inversion" merge, enclosing all the isotopes between N=19 and N=30. Indeed, this would be also the case for the Neon and Sodium chains if their drip lines would reach N=28.

## Key claim

The N=20 and N=28 "islands of inversion" merge into a single continuous region of deformation in the Magnesium isotopic chain, enclosing all isotopes between N=19 and N=30, and this would also occur for Neon and Sodium if their neutron drip lines extended to N=28.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations using the sdpf-u-mix interaction, an extension of the sdpf-u interaction that allows mixing of configurations with different numbers of particles promoted from the sd-shell to the pf-shell (different Nhw). Calculations were carried out using the codes ANTOINE and NATHAN, reaching basis dimensions of order 10^10, covering isotopes from Oxygen to Argon with valence protons in the sd-shell and valence neutrons in the sd-pf shells.

## Key numbers

- In 32Mg, the correlation energy gain is 1.5 MeV for 0p-0h, 12.5 MeV for 2p-2h, and 21 MeV for 4p-4h configurations
- In 32Mg, the lowest 4p-4h 0+ state lies about 250 keV below the lowest 2p-2h 0+ and 1.2 MeV below the 0p-0h 0+ configuration
- The 4p-4h configuration in 32Mg has E(4+)/E(2+) = 3.2, characteristic of a perfect rigid rotor
- In 31Mg, the magnetic moment of the 1/2+ state is -0.85 mu_N (bare g-factors), close to the experimental value of -0.88355(15) mu_N
- In 31Mg, B(M1)(5/2+ -> 3/2+) = 0.35 mu_N^2 (theory) versus 0.1-0.5 mu_N^2 (experiment); B(M1)(3/2+ -> 1/2+) = 0.03 mu_N^2 (theory) versus 0.019(4) mu_N^2 (experiment); B(E2)(5/2+ -> 1/2+) = 84 e^2fm^4 (theory) versus 61(7) e^2fm^4 (experiment)
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0 is approximately 65-70 e fm^2 for the ground state bands in 32Mg through 40Mg
- In 30Ne, the calculated 2+ excitation energy is 0.79 MeV (experimental 0.792 MeV) and B(E2)(2+ -> 0+) is predicted at 66 e^2 fm^4 versus measured 90(54) e^2 fm^4
- In 32Ne, the calculated 2+ excitation energy is 0.67 MeV (experimental 0.72 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the earlier sdpf-u interaction developed by the same group (Nowacki, 2009) which described neutron-rich nuclei around N=28 in a 0hw space but could not describe intruder states. The new sdpf-u-mix interaction connects classical sd-shell calculations below N=18 with sd(protons)-pf(neutrons) calculations beyond N=24-26. The results confirm and extend earlier Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations by the Tokyo group (Otsuka) and other large-scale sd-pf valence space calculations. The paper enables predictions for the structure of very neutron-rich isotopes approaching the neutron drip line, including possible neutron halo formation in 37,39Mg and 40Mg.
