# Quark-meson coupling model for finite nuclei

> A Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model is extended to finite nuclei in the relativistic mean-field or Hartree approximation. The ultra-relativistic quarks are assumed to be bound in non-overlapping nucleon bags, and the interaction between nucleons arises from a coupling of vector and scalar meson fields to the quarks. We develop a perturbative scheme for treating the spatial nonuniformity of the meson fields over the volume of the nucleon as well as the nucleus. Results of calculations for spherical nuclei are given, based on a fit to the equilibrium properties of nuclear matter. Several possible extensions of the model are also considered.

## Key claim

The Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model, previously applied only to nuclear matter, is extended to finite spherical nuclei using a perturbative scheme that treats the spatial nonuniformity of meson fields over the nucleon volume, but the authors note that within the Hartree approximation one cannot simultaneously fit both binding energies and single-particle energies, pointing to the need for additional contributions such as tensor correlations.

## Method

The nucleus is treated as a set of color-singlet nucleon bags containing ultra-relativistic quarks, with interactions between nucleons arising from vector (omega) and scalar (sigma) meson fields coupling directly to the quarks. The authors develop a perturbative scheme where the meson fields are averaged over the nucleon volume using quark density-weighted integrals, then solve the Dirac equation for nucleons in the resulting mean fields self-consistently with the meson field equations. The MIT bag model describes the quark wave functions, and the model parameters are fixed by fitting to equilibrium nuclear matter properties before computing finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation is fitted to a binding energy per nucleon of -15.75 MeV at a Fermi momentum k_F = 1.30 fm^{-1}, corresponding to a saturation density of rho_B = 0.148 fm^{-3}
- The vector meson mass is taken as m_v = 783 MeV; the scalar meson mass m_s is a parameter in the range 400-550 MeV
- Three QMC models are presented with bag radii R_0 of 0.6 fm, 0.8 fm, and 1.0 fm (models A1-A3), with quark mass m_q = 5 MeV for u and d quarks
- The rho-nucleon coupling constant is fixed at g_rho = 2.63
- Binding energies and rms charge radii are computed for ^16O, ^40Ca, ^90Zr, and ^208Pb, with m_s adjusted to fit the experimental rms charge radius of ^40Ca

## Context

This work extends the QMC model of Saito and Thomas (1994) and Guichon (1989) from nuclear matter to finite nuclei, providing a more detailed formalism than the local density approximation used by Guichon et al. (1995). The model is conceptually similar to the QHD model of Walecka and collaborators but incorporates quark degrees of freedom, and in the limit of very large quark mass it reduces to the Walecka model. The results enable future studies of medium-dependent bag constants and alternative confinement models such as the relativistic oscillator description of Tegen et al. (1990).
