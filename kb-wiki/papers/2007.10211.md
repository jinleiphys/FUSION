# Bottomonium suppression and elliptic flow from real-time quantum evolution

> We compute the suppression and elliptic flow of bottomonium using real-time solutions to the Schrödinger equation with a realistic in-medium complex-valued potential. To model the initial production, we assume that, in the limit of heavy quark masses, the wave-function can be described by a lattice-smeared (Gaussian) Dirac delta wave-function. The resulting final-state quantum-mechanical overlaps provide the survival probability of all bottomonium eigenstates. Our results are in good agreement with available data for $R_{AA}$ as a function of $N_{\rm part}$ and $p_T$ collected at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} =$ 5.02 TeV. In the case of $v_2$ for the various states, we find that the path-length dependence of $Υ(1s)$ suppression results in quite small $v_2$ for $Υ(1s)$. Our prediction for the integrated elliptic flow for $Υ(1s)$ in the $10{-}90$% centrality class is $v_2[Υ(1s)] = 0.0026 \pm 0.0007$. We additionally find that, due to their increased suppression, excited bottomonium states have a larger elliptic flow and we make predictions for $v_2[Υ(2s)]$ and $v_2[Υ(3s)]$ as a function of centrality and transverse momentum. Similar to prior studies, we find that it is possible for bottomonium states to have negative $v_2$ at low transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that real-time quantum evolution of bottomonium wave-packets through a complex in-medium potential, without the adiabatic approximation, yields predictions for suppression and elliptic flow that are in reasonable agreement with LHC data at 5.02 TeV, but the authors note that some quantitative differences remain, motivating future inclusion of in-medium thermal noise, octet-state production, and singlet-octet transitions.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation numerically on a discrete lattice using a split-step pseudospectral method, with a complex-valued in-medium potential combining a Karsch-Mehr-Satz real part and a leading-order perturbative QCD imaginary part from Laine et al. (2006). They Monte-Carlo sample 1.2 million bottomonium trajectories through a 3+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics background tuned to reproduce soft hadron observables at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, computing survival probabilities from quantum-mechanical overlaps of the evolved wave-function with vacuum eigenstates, then applying feed-down using vacuum branching ratios from the Particle Data Group.

## Context

This work extends prior studies using the adiabatic approximation by Strickland (2011, 2012), Krouppa (2015, 2016, 2017), and Bhaduri (2018, 2020) by solving the full time-dependent Schrodinger equation, thereby including quantum state mixing effects from the time-dependent potential. The authors compare their results to the adiabatic approximation of Bhaduri (2020) and find good agreement for Upsilon(1s) elliptic flow, but note that the adiabatic approximation predicts less suppression at high p_T. The framework enables future inclusion of in-medium thermal noise, octet-state production, and singlet-octet transitions to address remaining quantitative differences with experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS.
