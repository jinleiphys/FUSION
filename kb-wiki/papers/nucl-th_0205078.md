# Energy dependence of string fragmentation function and $ϕ$ meson production

> The $ϕ$ meson productions in $Au+Au$ and/or $Pb+Pb$ collisions at AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies have been studied systematically with a hadron and string cascade model LUCIAE. After considering the energy dependence of the model parameter $α$ in string fragmentation function and adjusting it to the experimental data of charged multiplicity to a certain extent, the model predictions for $ϕ$ meson yield, rapidity, and/or transverse mass distributions are compatible with the experimental data at AGS, SPS and RHIC energies. A calculation for $Pb+Pb$ collisions at LHC energy is given as well. The obtained fractional variable in string fragmentation function shows a saturation in energy dependence. It is discussed that the saturation of fractional variable in string fragmentation function might be a qualitative representation of the energy dependence of nuclear transparency.

## Key claim

The LUCIAE model, after introducing an energy-dependent parameter α in the string fragmentation function and adjusting it to charged multiplicity data, produces φ meson yields and distributions compatible with experimental data at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies; the fractional variable ⟨z⟩ in the fragmentation function saturates around RHIC energy, which the authors discuss as a possible qualitative representation of the energy dependence of nuclear transparency.

## Method

The authors used the hadron and string cascade model LUCIAE (based on FRITIOF) to simulate Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at AGS (√s_nn = 4.66 GeV), SPS (17.3 GeV), RHIC (130 GeV), and LHC (5500 GeV) energies. They introduced an energy-dependent parameter α(√s_nn) into a simple string fragmentation function f(z) = α × z^(α−1) and tuned α at each energy to reproduce experimental charged multiplicity data; the same α values were then used to calculate φ meson yields, rapidity, and transverse mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- Charged multiplicity N_ch for Au+Au at √s_nn = 130 GeV: experimental 3860 ± 300 (BRAHMS, |η| < 4.7, ≤ 5% centrality), LUCIAE 4114
- Charged multiplicity N_ch for Pb+Pb at √s_nn = 5500 GeV: LUCIAE prediction 15646 (full phase space, ≤ 10% centrality)
- α parameter values: 0.4 (AGS, Au+Au, √s_nn = 4.66 GeV), 1.3 (SPS, Pb+Pb, √s_nn = 17.3 GeV), 12 (RHIC, Au+Au, √s_nn = 130 GeV), 18 (LHC, Pb+Pb, √s_nn = 5500 GeV)
- ⟨z⟩ (fractional variable) values: 0.286 (AGS), 0.565 (SPS), 0.923 (RHIC), 0.947 (LHC)
- φ meson yield ⟨N_φ⟩ for Au+Au at AGS (√s_nn = 4.66 GeV): experimental 0.252 ± 0.107 (dN_φ/dy within 0.9 < y < 1.4, 301 participants), LUCIAE 0.14
- φ meson yield ⟨N_φ⟩ for Pb+Pb at SPS (√s_nn = 17.3 GeV): experimental 7.6 ± 1.1 (≤ 4% centrality), LUCIAE 6.48
- φ meson yield ⟨N_φ⟩ for Au+Au at RHIC (√s_nn = 130 GeV): experimental 5.73 ± 1.06 (dN_φ/dy within -0.5 < y < 0.5, ≤ 11% centrality), LUCIAE 5.10

## Context

This work extends the LUCIAE model studies of strangeness enhancement (previously applied to multi-strange baryons Λ, Ξ, Ω by Sa et al.) to φ meson production across a wide energy range from AGS to LHC, a systematic study the authors note had not been performed before. The energy-dependent string fragmentation function approach parallels the energy-dependent thermal model parameters (temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B) used by Cleymans et al. to fit global hadronic yields from SIS to RHIC energies. The saturation of ⟨z⟩ near RHIC energy is discussed in relation to nuclear transparency, with formation time and hadronic rescattering effects (as considered in the UrQMD model) noted as competing factors. The results enable predictions for φ meson production at LHC energies and suggest a saturation mechanism in particle production at high energies.
