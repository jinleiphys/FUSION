# Excitation of the isomeric ^{229m}Th nuclear state via an electronic bridge process in ^{229}Th^+

> We consider the excitation of the nuclear transition ^{229g}Th - ^{229m}Th near 7.6 eV in singly ionized thorium via an electronic bridge process. The process relies on the excitation of the electron shell by two laser photons whose sum frequency is equal to the nuclear transition frequency. This scheme allows to determine the nuclear transition frequency with high accuracy. Based on calculations of the electronic level structure of Th^+ which combine the configuration-interaction method and many-body perturbation theory, we estimate that a nuclear excitation rate in the range of 10 s^{-1} can be obtained using conventional laser sources.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the nuclear transition from the ground state to the isomeric state in singly ionized thorium-229 can be excited via a two-photon electronic bridge process, and they estimate a nuclear excitation rate of approximately 10 s⁻¹ can be achieved using conventional laser sources, *provided* the nuclear transition energy is close to 7.6 eV and the electronic level structure in the relevant range is as calculated.

## Method

The authors performed *ab initio* calculations of the electronic level structure of Th⁺ using a configuration-interaction method combined with many-body perturbation theory (CI+MBPT) and the relativistic random-phase approximation (RPA). They considered a specific excitation scheme where the first laser photon (at 24874 cm⁻¹) populates the (6d7s7p) J=5/2 state, and a second tunable laser photon excites higher-lying states, with the sum frequency matching the nuclear transition frequency. The probability of the electronic bridge process was calculated using the derived coefficient G₂ and the ratio β_M1 of the electronic bridge rate to the spontaneous nuclear M1 transition rate.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear transition energy is assumed to be in the range 7.4–7.9 eV, corresponding to 60,000–64,000 cm⁻¹, based on the most precise value of 7.6(5) eV from Beck et al. (2007).
- For a nuclear transition frequency ω_N = 7.6 eV, the calculated coefficient G₂ ≈ 2700 and β_M1 ≈ 30.
- The spontaneous nuclear M1 transition rate Γ_N is taken as 6.6 × 10⁻⁴ s⁻¹ from Porsev et al. (2010).
- The estimated nuclear excitation rate W^in_EB ≈ 10 s⁻¹, assuming a laser source with 10 mJ pulses, 3 GHz spectral width, 30 Hz repetition rate, and a beam cross section of 0.1 × 0.1 mm², yielding a time-averaged spectral intensity I ≈ 1.5 × 10⁻³ (W/m²)·s.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the spontaneous electronic bridge process for the decay of the isomeric state in Th⁺ (Porsev et al., 2010) to the case of nuclear excitation. The proposed scheme offers a method to determine the nuclear transition frequency with the accuracy of high-resolution laser spectroscopy, which is needed to realize a nuclear clock based on thorium-229 as proposed by Peik and Tamm (2003). The next step required is the experimental identification of the high-lying electronic energy levels of Th⁺ in the 60,000–64,000 cm⁻¹ range, as the current calculations have an accuracy of several percent, which is insufficient to reliably predict resonance enhancement.
