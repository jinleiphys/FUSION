# Superscaling in charged current neutrino quasielastic scattering in the relativistic impulse approximation

> Superscaling of the quasielastic cross section in charged current neutrino-nucleus reactions at energies of a few GeV is investigated within the framework of the relativistic impulse approximation. Several approaches are used to describe final state interactions and comparisons are made with the plane wave approximation. Superscaling is very successful in all cases. The scaling function obtained using a relativistic mean field for the final states shows an asymmetric shape with a long tail extending towards positive values of the scaling variable, in excellent agreement with the behavior presented by the experimental scaling function.

## Key claim

Superscaling of charged-current neutrino-nucleus quasielastic cross sections is fulfilled to high accuracy within the relativistic impulse approximation for all three descriptions of final-state interactions considered, but only the relativistic mean field (RMF) model reproduces the asymmetric shape and long tail at positive scaling variable observed in experimental electron scattering data.

## Method

The authors computed inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus quasielastic cross sections within the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) using three descriptions of the outgoing nucleon: relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA, no final-state interactions), real relativistic optical potential (rROP, with real potentials from phenomenological parameterizations), and relativistic mean field (RMF, using the same mean field for bound and continuum states). They divided the computed cross sections by the single-nucleon cross section weighted by proton and neutron numbers to obtain scaling functions, which they plotted against the shifted quasielastic scaling variable ψ′ and analyzed for scaling of the first kind (independence of momentum transfer) and second kind (independence of nuclear species).

## Key numbers

- For 12C and 16O at incident neutrino energy ε_ν = 1 GeV and muon scattering angle θ_μ = 45°, the RMF model reduces the cross section by about 20% near the maximum compared to the RPWIA result.
- Fermi momenta used: k_F = 228 MeV/c for 12C, k_F = 216 MeV/c for 16O, and k_F = 241 MeV/c for 40Ca.
- The RMF model breaks scaling of the first kind at roughly the 30% level in the positive ψ′ region for the energy range explored.
- All curves in the scaling function comparison essentially satisfy the Coulomb sum rule, integrating to unity.

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis previously applied to inclusive electron scattering (Donnelly and Sick 1999, Jourdan 1996) to charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions, testing the universality assumption that a single scaling function describes both electron and neutrino scattering. The RMF result contrasts with the symmetric scaling functions from the RPWIA and rROP models, and also differs from the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) of correlation effects presented in earlier work (Antonov et al. 2004), which produced a manifestly symmetrical scaling function around the quasielastic peak. The finding that the asymmetric scaling function can be explained within the RIA using only one-body excitations with strong relativistic potentials opens the door to comparing more elaborate formalisms that include multinucleon excitations with superscaling data.
