# Role of pairing in the description of Giant Monopole Resonances

> We compare the results obtained in the framework of the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) on top of a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) with the most recent experiments on giant monopole resonances in Pb, Sn, Zr, Sm, Mo and Cd. Our calculations are fully self consistent and the density dependence of pairing interactions is, for the first time in this framework, properly taken into account. In the particle-hole (ph) channel we employ different Skyrme functionals (SLy5, SkM* and Skxs20) while in the particle-particle (pp) channel we make use of density dependent contact interactions. We introduce in the pp channel the recently proposed contact interactions which take into account the neutron-proton asymmetry. We find that no single parametrization is able to reproduce with sufficient accuracy all the nuclei. Since about two-thirds of the nuclei under investigation are better explained with a soft parametrization, this tends to suggest that the currently accepted picture for the incompressibility might require modifications.

## Key claim

No single Skyrme functional parametrization (SLy5, SkM*, or Skxs20) combined with any pairing type (volume, mixed, or surface) can reproduce the experimental ISGMR centroids for all the nuclei studied (Pb, Sn, Zr, Sm, Mo, Cd) with sufficient accuracy; because about two-thirds of the nuclei are better explained with a soft parametrization, the currently accepted picture for the nuclear matter incompressibility modulus (K_NM ≈ 230 ± 20 MeV) might require modifications.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) plus quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations using the finite amplitude method (FAM) in coordinate space, with a 20 fm box, 0.05 fm mesh, and 200 MeV quasiparticle cutoff. In the particle-hole channel they employed three Skyrme functionals (SLy5 with K_NM ≈ 230 MeV, SkM* with ≈ 217 MeV, and Skxs20 with ≈ 202 MeV), while in the particle-particle channel they used density-dependent delta interactions (DDDI) of volume, mixed, and surface type, including for the first time in this framework the proper rearrangement terms from the density dependence of the pairing interaction.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the experimental centroid m1/m0 is 13.96 ± 0.20 MeV (TAMU, 2004); SLy5 with volume pairing gives 13.85 MeV, SkM* gives 13.43 MeV, and Skxs20 gives 13.12 MeV (all in the 10-55 MeV energy range).
- For Sn isotopes (RCNP data, 10.5-20.5 MeV range), SLy5 overestimates centroids by about 0.5 MeV; SkM* with surface pairing gives the closest description for 116Sn; Skxs20 with surface pairing reproduces 116Sn, 122Sn, and 124Sn very well.
- For 112Sn, the RCNP centroid is 16.2 ± 0.1 MeV while the TAMU centroid is 15.43 ± 0.1 MeV, a difference of 0.77 MeV.
- For 124Sn, the RCNP centroid is 15.3 ± 0.1 MeV while the TAMU centroid is 14.50 ± 0.14 MeV, a difference of 0.8 MeV.
- For 90Zr, the experimental centroid is 17.88 +0.13/-0.11 MeV; SLy5 with volume pairing gives 18.04 MeV, SkM* gives 17.64 MeV.
- The numerical uncertainty of the calculations is estimated to be less than 0.1 MeV based on convergence tests with mesh size, box size, and cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the fully self-consistent HFB+QRPA approach of Terasaki et al. (2005) and the HF+BCS+QRPA calculations of Cao et al. (2013) by properly including rearrangement terms from the density dependence of pairing interactions, which were neglected in prior studies. The results confirm the puzzle raised by recent experimental data on Sn (Osaka group, 2007) and Cd isotopes, which cannot be reproduced with the same functionals that work for 208Pb, and suggest that the nuclear matter incompressibility modulus may need revision. The calculations enable systematic testing of different Skyrme functionals and pairing parametrizations against the full set of available ISGMR data, and the authors note that the Skxs20 interaction (K_NM ≈ 202 MeV) gives the best overall agreement for Sn isotopes, while SLy5 (K_NM ≈ 230 MeV) works best for Pb.
