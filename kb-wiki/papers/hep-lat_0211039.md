# The Quark-Gluon Mixed Condensate g<\bar{q} sigma G q> in SU(3)c Quenched Lattice QCD

> Using the SU(3)c lattice QCD with the Kogut-Susskind fermion at the quenched level, we study the quark-gluon mixed condensate g<\bar{q} sigma G q>, which is another chiral order parameter. For each current quark mass of m_q=21, 36, 52 MeV, we generate 100 gauge configurations in the 16^4 lattice with β= 6.0, and perform the measurement of the mixed condensate at 16 points in each gauge configuration. Using the 1600 data for each m_q, we find the ratio between the mixed condensate and the quark condensate, m_0^2 = g<\bar{q} sigma G q>/ <\bar{q}q> \simeq 2.5 GeV^2 at the lattice scale in the chiral limit. The large value of the mixed condensate suggests its importance in the operator product expansion in QCD.

# The Quark-Gluon Mixed Condensate g⟨q̄σGq⟩ in SU(3)c Quenched Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The ratio m₀² ≡ g⟨q̄σGq⟩/⟨q̄q⟩ is found to be approximately 2.5 GeV² at the lattice scale (a⁻¹ ≈ 2 GeV) in the chiral limit, which is significantly larger than the standard QCD sum rule value of 0.8 ± 0.2 GeV²; however, this result is obtained at the quenched level using bare condensates without full non-perturbative renormalization, and after perturbative rescaling to μ = 1 GeV the value becomes 3.5–3.7 GeV².

## Method

The authors perform quenched SU(3)c lattice QCD simulations using the standard Wilson gauge action and Kogut-Susskind (staggered) fermions, which preserve explicit chiral symmetry in the massless limit. For each of three β values (5.7, 5.8, 6.0) on lattices of size 8⁴, 12⁴, and 16⁴ respectively, they generate 100 gauge configurations and measure the quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ and the quark-gluon mixed condensate g⟨q̄σGq⟩ at 16 space-time points per configuration, yielding 1600 data points per quark mass. The gluon field strength is defined using a clover-type operator to avoid O(a) discretization errors, and the condensates are chirally extrapolated using a linear fit in the quark mass.

## Key numbers

- m₀² ≡ g⟨q̄σGq⟩/⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ 2.5 GeV² at the lattice scale (β = 6.0, a⁻¹ ≈ 2 GeV) in the chiral limit
- Bare condensates at β = 6.0 in the chiral limit: a³⟨q̄q⟩ = −0.00872(17) and a⁵g⟨q̄σGq⟩ = −0.00565(14)
- After perturbative rescaling to μ = 1 GeV: m₀²|μ ∼ 3.5–3.7 GeV², with ⟨q̄q⟩|μ ∼ −(0.0477–0.0506) GeV³ = −(0.36–0.37 GeV)³ and g⟨q̄σGq⟩|μ ∼ −(0.176–0.177) GeV⁵
- Current quark masses used: m_q = 21, 36, and 52 MeV
- Lattice spacing at β = 6.0: a = 0.10 fm on a 16⁴ lattice, giving physical volume V ≈ (1.6 fm)⁴
- Finite volume effect estimated by comparing periodic vs. anti-periodic boundary conditions: difference at the 1% level

## Context

This work extends the only previous lattice QCD study of the mixed condensate by K&S (about 15 years prior), which used only 5 gauge configurations on an 8⁴ lattice at β = 5.7 and gave m₀² ∼ 1.1 GeV² with insufficient statistics. The present result (m₀² ≈ 2.5 GeV² at the lattice scale) is significantly larger than the phenomenological QCD sum rule value of 0.8 ± 0.2 GeV² from Bel (year), RRY (year), Ovchi (year), and Narison (year), and also larger than the instanton model estimate of m₀² ≈ 1.4 GeV² at μ ≈ 0.6 GeV from Polyakov (year). The large value suggests the mixed condensate plays an important role in the operator product expansion for baryon sum rules, particularly for N-Δ splitting and heavy-light meson systems. Future work should address dynamical quark effects and finite-temperature studies to understand chiral restoration.
