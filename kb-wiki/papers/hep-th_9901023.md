# Fermionic One-Loop Corrections to Soliton Energies in 1+1 Dimensions

> We demonstrate an unambiguous and robust method for computing fermionic corrections to the energies of classical background field configurations. We consider the particular case of a sequence of background field configurations that interpolates continuously between the trivial vacuum and a widely separated soliton/antisoliton pair in 1+1 dimensions. Working in the continuum, we use phase shifts, the Born approximation, and Levinson's theorem to avoid ambiguities of renormalization procedure and boundary conditions. We carry out the calculation analytically at both ends of the interpolation and numerically in between, and show how the relevant physical quantities vary continuously. In the process, we elucidate properties of the fermionic phase shifts and zero modes.

# Fermionic One-Loop Corrections to Soliton Energies in 1+1 Dimensions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate an unambiguous method for computing fermionic one-loop corrections to soliton energies that avoids ambiguities of renormalization procedure and boundary conditions, but only for the specific case of a sequence of background field configurations that interpolates continuously between the trivial vacuum and a widely separated soliton/antisoliton pair in 1+1 dimensions, where the calculation can be carried out analytically at both ends of the interpolation and numerically in between.

## Method

The authors consider a Majorana fermion coupled to a real scalar background field with a specific choice of bosonic and fermionic potentials, using a continuum formalism based on phase shifts, their Born approximations, and Levinson's theorem. They compute the renormalized one-loop fermionic energy as a sum over zero-point energies by solving the Dirac equation for fermionic small oscillations, separating solutions into positive and negative parity channels, and subtracting the first Born approximation to the phase shift to cancel the tadpole graph contribution. The calculation is performed for a continuous sequence of scalar field configurations labeled by a parameter x0 that interpolates from the trivial vacuum at x0=0 to a widely separated soliton/antisoliton pair as x0 approaches infinity.

## Key numbers

- Two bound states approach energy m * sqrt(3)/2 in the limit x0 goes to infinity, corresponding to odd and even parity combinations of the single soliton bound state
- A third positive parity bound state approaches omega = 0 in the x0 goes to infinity limit
- For large but finite x0, delta+(0) = 3*pi/2 and delta-(0) = pi, consistent with two positive parity and one negative parity bound states
- In the x0 goes to infinity limit, the analytic result for the one-loop fermionic correction to the energy is Delta H = 2m * (1/pi - 1/(4*sqrt(3)))
- The bosonic contribution to the energy in the same limit is 2m * (1/(4*sqrt(3)) - 3/(2*pi)), giving a total of -m/pi for the soliton/antisoliton pair in the supersymmetric model

## Context

This work extends the methods of Graham and Jaffe (1998) for bosonic one-loop corrections to the fermionic case, resolving conflicting conclusions in earlier works on supersymmetric theories by d'Adda and di Vecchia (1978), Horsley (1979), and others regarding correct boundary conditions, regularization, and renormalization procedures. The results enable calculation of the energy of localized field configurations in any model of scalars and spinors in 1+1 dimensions, and the authors note that work is underway to extend these techniques to translationally invariant classical field configurations and the nonlocal potentials encountered in that case, as discussed by Bashinsky (to appear).
