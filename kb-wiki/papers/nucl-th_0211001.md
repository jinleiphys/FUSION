# Short-range NN-properties in the processes $pd\to dp$ and $pd\to (pp)n$

> The data from the first measurement, performed at ANKE/COSY, of the unpolarized cross section of the reaction $pd\to (pp)n$ in the kinematics of backward elastic $pd\to dp$ scattering at proton-beam energies between 0.6 and 1.9 GeV are analyzed in a phenomenological approach. The $pd\to (pp)n$ data and the triplet cross section of the reaction $pd\to (pn)p$, calculated from the $pd\to dp$ data on the basis of the Fäldt-Wilkin extrapolation, are used here to derive the ratio $ζ$ of the singlet production matrix element squared to the triplet one. This ratio, defined in our earlier analysis of $pd\to (pn)p$ data in a largely model-independent way, depends on the dynamics of the $pd$ interaction. We find here $ζ\approx 0.02$ and show that the smallness of this value may point toward softness of the deuteron at short NN distances.

## Key claim

The ratio ζ of the singlet production matrix element squared to the triplet one is found to be approximately 0.02, and the smallness of this value may point toward softness of the deuteron at short NN distances, though the analysis is phenomenological and model-dependent.

## Method

The unpolarized cross section of the reaction pd → (pp)n, measured at ANKE/COSY for proton-beam energies between 0.6 and 1.9 GeV in the kinematics of backward elastic pd → dp scattering, was analyzed using the Fäldt-Wilkin extrapolation to obtain the triplet cross section from pd → dp data. The singlet-to-triplet ratio ζ was then derived from the ratio of the measured pd → (pp)n cross section to the pd → dp cross section, incorporating the Migdal-Watson final-state interaction formalism and the Goldberger-Watson factor for the singlet and triplet channels.

## Key numbers

- ζ = (2.3 ± 0.5)% at 0.6 GeV beam energy
- ζ = (1.6 ± 0.3)% at 0.7 GeV beam energy
- ζ = (2.1 ± 1.2)% at 1.9 GeV beam energy
- The FSI parameters used: α_t = 0.232 fm⁻¹, α_s = -0.04 fm⁻¹, β_t = 0.91 fm⁻¹, β_s = 0.79 fm⁻¹
- The integrals Z = 0.101 and R = 2.29 for E_pp^max = 3 MeV
- The ONE calculation with the Reid-Soft-Core NN potential gives ζ_ONE = 5-8% for T_p = 1.4-1.9 GeV, which is 3 to 4 times the experimental value
- For the Δ mechanism, ζ_Δ = 1/27 (approximately 3.7%)

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of pd → (pn)p data by Uzikov et al. (2001) using a largely model-independent approach based on the Migdal-Watson parametrization. The small singlet-to-triplet ratio contradicts predictions from the ONE+SS+Δ model using the Reid-Soft-Core NN potential, which overestimates the pd → (pp)n cross section by a factor of 2 to 4 at beam energies above 1.3 GeV. The results enable further investigation of short-range NN properties and suggest that the deuteron and pp(¹S₀) system may be softer at short distances than modeled by the RSC potential, with detailed analyses of this conjecture in progress.
