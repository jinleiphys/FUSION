# CREX and PREX-II reconciled within energy-density functional theory

> The CREX and PREX-II measurements of the neutron-skin thickness of 48Ca and 208Pb challenge standard nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) descriptions of nuclei and nuclear matter. We show that the apparent tension arises from an implicit constraint in EDF theory, which ties the density dependence of the functional at the dilute nuclear surface to that of uniform matter near saturation. Relaxing this surface-bulk coupling and independently constraining the dilute-density sector, while preserving realistic saturation and high-density behavior, yields EDFs that simultaneously reproduce the neutron skins of both nuclei, their electric dipole polarizabilities, and neutron-star mass-radius relations. The established correlation between the neutron-skin thickness and the symmetry-energy slope parameter L at saturation is retained but becomes substantially broader. The results show that current neutron-skin data do not require extreme values of L and highlight an underconstrained degree of freedom associated with low-density nuclear matter.

# CREX and PREX-II reconciled within energy-density functional theory

## Key claim

The apparent tension between the CREX measurement of the neutron-skin thickness of 48Ca and the PREX-II measurement for 208Pb arises from an implicit constraint in standard energy-density functional (EDF) theory that ties the density dependence at the dilute nuclear surface to that of uniform matter near saturation. By relaxing this surface-bulk coupling and independently constraining the dilute-density sector while preserving realistic saturation and high-density behavior, the author shows that both neutron-skin measurements can be simultaneously reproduced without requiring extreme values of the symmetry-energy slope parameter L.

## Method

The author starts with standard KIDS EDF parameterizations based on three realistic bulk equations of state (APR, QMC, and a stiffer EoS(65)) and adds a minimal Skyrme-like term with an exponential factor that is active only at low densities, vanishing already at densities of order one-tenth of saturation density. The parameter space of the low-density correction term, along with the isoscalar gradient coefficient and isovector spin-orbit coupling, is explored using Metropolis-Hastings Monte Carlo sampling, with a log posterior that includes the CREX and PREX-II neutron-skin values, the average deviation per datum for 19 nuclear binding energies and charge radii, and a loose constraint on the symmetry energy at 0.005 fm^-3. Successful EDFs are then tested against the measured electric dipole polarizabilities of 48Ca and 208Pb calculated within the self-consistent random-phase approximation.

## Key numbers

- PREX-II neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb: 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- CREX neutron-skin thickness of 48Ca: 0.121 ± 0.050 fm
- Dipole polarizability of 208Pb: 20.1 ± 0.6 fm^3/e^2 (Tam 2011)
- Dipole polarizability of 48Ca: 2.07 ± 0.22 fm^3/e^2 (Bir 2017)
- Bulk EoS parameters for APR: (J, L, K_sym, Q_sym) = (32.8, 49.2, -156.3, 583.1) MeV
- Bulk EoS parameters for QMC: (J, L, K_sym, Q_sym) = (34.5, 60.5, -88.9, 751.2) MeV
- Bulk EoS parameters for EoS(65): (J, L, K_sym, Q_sym) = (32.5, 65, -180, 650) MeV
- Saturation density: 0.16 fm^-3; saturation energy: -16 MeV; incompressibility: 240 MeV
- Successful EDFs from QMC EoS: 115 sets satisfying both neutron-skin constraints
- Successful EDFs from EoS(65): over ten thousand sets, but all gave dipole polarizabilities too high (over 2.8 fm^3 for 48Ca and over 24 fm^3 for 208Pb)
- APR(B) and APR(C) EDFs: give a_D(208Pb) between 19.5 and 20.0 fm^3 and a_D(48Ca) below 2.29 fm^3
- QMC(A) EDF: gives a_D(208Pb) up to 19.6 fm^3 and a_D(48Ca) = 2.21-2.32 fm^3
- Neutron-skin thickness of 90Zr from successful EDFs: 0.072 to 0.075 fm

## Context

This work extends efforts to reconcile the CREX and PREX-II measurements that have included Bayesian analyses in meta-models (MoG 2023), explorations of the isovector spin-orbit force (Zha 2025, KPS 2025), studies of alpha clustering (YLX 2023), and investigations of the curvature parameter K_sym (GuN 2025). Previous attempts at reconciliation required free and extreme variations of higher-order parameters like skewness and kurtosis (rila 2022), which affected both dilute and dense matter on an equal footing. The present approach directly addresses the underconstrained degree of freedom associated with low-density nuclear matter, enabling simultaneous description of neutron-skin measurements, dipole polarizabilities, and neutron-star mass-radius relations within a unified framework. The results challenge the narrow correlation between neutron-skin thickness and the symmetry-energy slope parameter L, showing that current data do not require extreme values of L and highlighting the need to incorporate low-density degrees of freedom in nuclear EDF theory.
