# Influence of entrance-channel magicity and isospin on quasi-fission

> The role of spherical quantum shells in the competition between fusion and quasi-fission is studied for reactions forming heavy elements. Measurements of fission fragment mass distributions for different reactions leading to similar compound nuclei have been made near the fusion barrier. In general, more quasi-fission is observed for reactions with non-magic nuclei. However, the $^{40}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb reaction is an exception, showing strong evidence for quasi-fission, though both nuclei are doubly magic. Time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations predict fast equilibration of $N/Z$ in the two fragments early in the collision. This transfer of nucleons breaks the shell effect, causing this reaction to behave more like a non-magic one in the competition between fusion and quasi-fission. Future measurements of fission in reactions with exotic beams should be able to test this idea with larger $N/Z$ asymmetries.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while entrance-channel magicity generally reduces quasi-fission and enhances fusion, the doubly-magic reaction ⁴⁰Ca+²⁰⁸Pb is an exception because fast isospin equilibration (N/Z equilibration) early in the collision breaks the shell effect, causing this reaction to behave like a non-magic system with increased quasi-fission.

## Method

Fission fragment mass-angle distributions (MAD) were measured for seven reactions forming isotopes of Fm, No, and Rf using pulsed and DC beams from the 14UD electrostatic accelerator at the Australian National University, with binary reaction products detected by position-sensitive multiwire proportional counters. Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the SLy4d Skyrme functional were performed to predict the evolution of N/Z asymmetry in the fragments as a function of contact time, and classical trajectory simulations were used to extract quasi-fission time scales from the experimental MAD.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Ca+²⁰⁸Pb (maximal magicity N_m=4, small Δ(N/Z)_i=0.14): σ_MR = 0.068±0.002, consistent with pure fusion-fission (upper limit σ_fiss ≈ 0.07-0.08)
- For ⁴⁰Ca+²⁰⁸Pb (maximal magicity N_m=4 but large Δ(N/Z)_i=0.54): σ_MR = 0.126±0.004, showing strong quasi-fission despite both nuclei being doubly magic
- For ⁴⁸Ca+²⁰⁴Pb (N_m=3, Δ(N/Z)_i=0.09): σ_MR = 0.084±0.008, within the fusion-fission band
- For ⁴⁸Ti+²⁰⁰Hg (N_m=0, Δ(N/Z)_i=0.32): σ_MR = 0.237±0.025, the largest width indicating the most quasi-fission
- TDHF calculations for ⁴⁰Ca+²⁰⁸Pb at contact time ~2.7×10⁻²¹ s give probability of conserving entrance-channel magicity P(ΔZ=0)P(ΔN=0) ≈ 0.083×0.002 ≈ 1.7×10⁻⁴
- For ⁴⁸Ca+²⁰⁸Pb at contact time ~3.5×10⁻²¹ s, the same probability is 0.76×0.57 ≈ 0.43
- Classical trajectory simulations reproduce the ⁴⁰Ca+²⁰⁸Pb experimental MAD best with an average quasi-fission time scale of 14×10⁻²¹ s

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of the competition between fusion and quasi-fission in heavy-element formation, building on earlier findings that closed shells affect fusion probabilities near the barrier (Hinde 2005) and that deformation and orientation influence quasi-fission (Hinde 1995, 1996, 2008; Nishio 2008). The paper resolves an apparent contradiction with the "cold valleys" concept of Sandulescu (1976) and Gupta (1977) by showing that fast isospin equilibration, previously observed in deep-inelastic collisions (Szilner 2005), can destroy entrance-channel magicity on timescales shorter than quasi-fission. The results enable planning of fusion experiments with exotic beams, where large N/Z asymmetries may reduce fusion probability, and explain the different capture cross-section behaviours observed for ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca reactions on ²⁰⁸Pb (Pacquet 1992).
