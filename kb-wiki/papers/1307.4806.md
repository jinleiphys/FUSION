# Electric Dipole Polarizability in ${}^{208}$Pb: insights from the Droplet Model

> We study the electric dipole polarizability $α_D$ in ${}^{208}$Pb based on the predictions of a large and representative set of relativistic and non-relativistic nuclear mean field models. We adopt the droplet model as a guide to better understand the correlations between $α_D$ and other isovector observables. Insights from the droplet model suggest that the product of $α_D$ and the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density $J$ is much better correlated with the neutron skin thickness $Δr_{np}$ of ${}^{208}$Pb than the polarizability alone. Correlations of $α_D J$ with $Δr_{np}$ and with the symmetry energy slope parameter $L$ suggest that $α_D J$ is a strong isovector indicator. Hence, we explore the possibility of constraining the isovector sector of thenuclear energy density functional by comparing our theoretical predictions against measurements of both $α_D$ and the parity-violating asymmetry in ${}^{208}$Pb. We find that the recent experimental determination of $α_D$ in ${}^{208}$Pb in combination with the range for the symmetry energy at saturation density $J=[31\pm (2)_{\rm est.}]$\,MeV suggests $Δr_{np}({}^{208}{\rm Pb}) = 0.165 \pm (0.009)_{\rm exp.} \pm (0.013)_{\rm theo.} \pm (0.021)_{\rm est.} {\rm fm}$ and $L= 43 \pm(6)_{\rm exp.} \pm (8)_{\rm theo.}\pm(12)_{\rm est.}$ MeV.

## Key claim

The product of the electric dipole polarizability α_D and the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density J is a much stronger isovector indicator than α_D alone, enabling tighter constraints on the neutron skin thickness Δr_np of 208Pb and the symmetry energy slope parameter L, provided one adopts a realistic range for J of 31 ± 2 MeV.

## Method

The authors employed a large set of relativistic and non-relativistic nuclear mean-field models (Skyrme, SAMi, SV, NL3/FSU, TF, and DD-ME families) to compute ground-state properties and the electric dipole response of 208Pb via fully self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA). They used the macroscopic droplet model (DM) of Myers and Swiatecki to derive analytical relations linking α_D, J, Δr_np, and L, and then verified these correlations against the microscopic RPA calculations. The experimental value of α_D = 20.1 ± 0.6 fm³ from Tamii et al. (2011) was combined with the adopted J range to extract constraints on Δr_np and L.

## Key numbers

- α_D = 20.1 ± 0.6 fm³ (experimental value for 208Pb from Tamii et al. 2011)
- Δr_np(208Pb) = 0.165 ± (0.009)_exp. ± (0.013)_theo. ± (0.021)_est. fm (extracted using J = 31 ± 2 MeV)
- L = 43 ± (6)_exp. ± (8)_theo. ± (12)_est. MeV (extracted using J = 31 ± 2 MeV)
- Correlation coefficient r = 0.97 between α_D J and Δr_np across the EDF set
- Correlation coefficient r = 0.96 between α_D J and L across the EDF set
- Correlation coefficient r = 0.94 between α_D J and the parity-violating asymmetry A_PV
- A_PV = 0.656 ± (0.060)_stat. ± (0.014)_syst. ppm (PREX measurement at ⟨Q²⟩ = 0.0088 ± 0.0001 GeV²)
- α_D J = (623 ± (19)_exp. ± (40)_est.) MeV fm³ (using J = 31 ± 2 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the correlation between α_D and Δr_np first noted by Tamii et al. (2011) and Piekarewicz et al. (2012) by introducing the droplet model to show that α_D J, rather than α_D alone, is the key isovector indicator. It provides a systematic analysis across multiple EDF families, confirming and refining earlier findings. The results enable tighter constraints on the symmetry energy parameters J and L, and suggest that combining α_D measurements with the PREX parity-violating asymmetry measurement can further constrain the isovector sector of nuclear energy density functionals. The authors encourage similar measurements in other medium and heavy nuclei such as 48Ca and 120Sn.
