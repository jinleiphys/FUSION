# Higher order effects in electromagnetic dissociation of neutron halo nuclei

> We investigate higher order effects in electromagnetic dissociation of neutron halo nuclei using a simple and realistic zero range model for the neutron-core interaction. In the sudden (or Glauber) approximation all orders in the target-core interaction are taken into account. Small deviations from the sudden approximation are readily calculated. We obtain very simple analytical results for the next-to-leading order effects, which have a simple physical interpretation. For intermediate energy electromagnetic dissociation, higher order effects are generally small. We apply our model to Coulomb dissociation of $^{19}$C at 67 A MeV. The analytical results are compared to numerical results from the integration of the time-dependent Schroedinger equation. Good agreement is obtained. We conclude that higher order electromagnetic effects are well under control.

# Higher Order Effects in Electromagnetic Dissociation of Neutron Halo Nuclei

## Key claim

Higher order electromagnetic effects in the Coulomb breakup of neutron halo nuclei at intermediate energies are generally small and well under control, with a reduction of the total cross section of only about 3% for 67 A MeV 19C on a Pb target. This contradicts claims by Tostevin (1999) of substantial higher order effects of 30-40%, which the authors attribute to differences between models rather than genuine higher order electromagnetic contributions.

## Method

The authors employ a semiclassical straight-line trajectory model with a zero-range (Yukawa) neutron-core interaction, expanding the excitation probability analytically up to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the strength parameter. They compare these analytical results to numerical solutions of the time-dependent Schroedinger equation for the 19C system, using a Woods-Saxon potential for the bound state and plane waves for continuum states, with the neutron assumed in a 2s1/2 configuration.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the neutron in 19C: 0.53 MeV, as determined by Nakamura et al. (1999)
- Beam energy: 67 A MeV for 19C on a Pb target
- Reduction of total cross section due to higher order effects: 3.3% in the semiclassical model, 3.2% in the xi-corrected analytical model, and 2.9% from the simple analytical formulas
- Total cross sections: 1.44 b (first order semiclassical), 1.39 b (dynamical semiclassical), 1.49 b (LO analytical), 1.44 b (NLO+NLO analytical), compared to experimental value of 1.34 +/- 0.12 b from Nakamura et al. (1999)
- Normalization factor N = 2.73 to match analytical model to numerical calculations for finite range effects
- E2 contribution is at least three orders of magnitude smaller than E1 excitation and can safely be neglected
- Scattering angles studied: 0.3 degrees (impact parameter 109.7 fm), 0.9 degrees (36.6 fm), and 2.7 degrees (12.2 fm)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-range model (Typel and Baur, 1994) to include next-to-leading order effects and applies it to the specific case of 19C studied experimentally by Nakamura et al. (1999) at RIKEN. The results contradict the claims of Tostevin (1999), who found substantial higher order effects of 30-40% by comparing the adiabatic breakup approach to semiclassical first order theory. The analytical formulas provide a quick estimate of higher order effects for future experiments at RIA facilities, particularly for r-process nuclei where Coulomb dissociation is an important tool.
