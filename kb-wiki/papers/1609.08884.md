# Study of hot and dense nuclear matter in effective QCD model

> In this thesis we use various effective QCD models to investigate hot and dense nuclear matter created in heavy ion collisions. To characterize such matter, we mainly exploit correlation functions and some of the associated spectral properties. We explore the vector meson current-current correlation function with and without the influence of vector interaction in Nambu\textendash Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and also in its Polyakov loop extended version (PNJL). As a spectral property we have computed the dilepton rate which is found to be enhanced in strongly interacting QGP (sQGP) as compared to the Born rate in a weakly coupled QGP. We further consider the idea of entanglement between the chiral and confinement dynamics through the entangled PNJL (EPNJL) model and re-explore the vector spectral function and the spectral property such as the dilepton production rate studied in our earlier effort. Because of the strong entanglement, the coupling strengths run with the temperature and chemical potential. The implications of such running on the dilepton rate have been discussed in details. The Euclidean vector correlator and the response of the conserved density fluctuations related with the temporal vector correlator have also been investigated. We have considered both the scenarios, i.e. presence and absence of the vector interaction. The inclusion of the vector interaction also brings forth some intriguing issues in the fluctuation of conserved density, namely the QNS. It has been discussed in details and we try to address it using EPNJL model. We also assume the QGP to be made of a non-interacting quarks, antiquarks and gluons and construct a partition function which is restricted by the assumption of a color singlet projection to conform with the $SU(3)_c$ symmetry. This type of simple quantum statistical description exhibits very interesting features, which we have discussed in details.

## Key claim

The thesis claims that the dilepton production rate is enhanced in a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) compared to the Born rate in a weakly coupled QGP, but this result depends on the inclusion of the isoscalar-vector interaction and the Polyakov loop dynamics, with the entanglement between chiral and confinement dynamics in the entangled PNJL (EPNJL) model causing the coupling strengths to run with temperature and chemical potential.

## Method

The author computed the vector meson current-current correlation function and its spectral function using the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and its Polyakov loop extended version (PNJL), both with and without the isoscalar-vector interaction. The ring resummation (Dyson-Schwinger equation) was applied to obtain the resummed correlator, and the dilepton production rate was derived from the imaginary part of the resummed temporal correlator. The partition function for a non-interacting quark-antiquark-gluon plasma was constructed with a color singlet projection to respect SU(3)_c symmetry.

- The imaginary part of the temporal correlator in the limit of zero three-momentum and zero chemical potential, with zero constituent quark mass, is given as Im Π_00(ω) = -2π T^2 ω δ(ω) (from equation in section on temporal part).
- The temporal correlator in the limit of vanishing three-momentum is related to the quark number susceptibility as G_00(τ T) = -T χ_q(T) (from appendix on density fluctuation).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of mesonic spectral functions in the NJL model (Kunihiro 1991, Oertel 2000) and scalar/pseudoscalar correlators in the PNJL model (Hansen 2006, Deb 2009) by investigating the vector channel and dilepton production for the first time in the PNJL framework. It also builds on lattice QCD results for the vector correlator at zero chemical potential (Ding 2010, Kaczmarek 2011, Francis 2011) and perturbative HTL calculations (Karsch 2000, Braaten 1990). The inclusion of the vector interaction addresses the finite-density regime relevant for the FAIR experiment, and the color singlet projection in the partition function provides a quantum statistical description of the QGP.
