# Heavy Resonance Production in Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Are heavy quarks produced thermally or only by hard parton collisions? What is the probability that a produced heavy quark or antiquark is observed in a given resonance?

## Key claim

Heavy quarks in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions are produced by both thermal processes and hard parton collisions, with the dominant mechanism depending on quark flavor; the paper argues that for charm quarks the two mechanisms contribute roughly equally, while for bottom quarks hard parton collisions dominate and for strange quarks thermal production dominates.

## Method

The authors model ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions using a simplified Bjorken-like scenario with sequential stages: hard parton collisions, thermal equilibration, gluon plasma expansion, phase transition, resonance gas expansion, and freezeout. They compute heavy quark production cross sections from perturbative QCD and from thermal production including previously neglected thermal gluon decay, then simulate heavy quark freezeout via a random walk in a thermal bath with collision frequencies from Pisarski, and finally apply a statistical recombination model with three chemical potentials (quark, antiquark, and pair) to predict resonance yields.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at RHIC energy (sqrt(s)=200 GeV/nucleon): thermal production gives dN_s/dy ~ 100, dN_c/dy ~ 1, dN_b/dy < 10^-3; perturbative QCD gives dN_c/dy ~ 1, dN_b/dy ~ 0.02
- For S+S collisions at SPS energy (sqrt(s)=20 GeV/nucleon): freezeout collision number n_f = 1 for s, c, and b quarks
- For Au+Au at SPS energy (dN/dy=1000): n_f = 4 for s, 5 for c, 5 for b; freezeout proper time tau_f = 15 fm/c for s, 26 fm/c for c, 56 fm/c for b
- For Au+Au at LHC energy (dN/dy=3500): n_f = 10 for s, 11 for c, 15 for b; freezeout proper time tau_f = 26 fm/c for s, 45 fm/c for c, 97 fm/c for b
- c-cbar meson fractions for Au+Au at dN/dy=2000: range from 0.001 to 0.009 depending on T_c (150 or 200 MeV) and nu (5 or 10)
- b-bbar meson fractions for Au+Au at dN/dy=2000: range from 0.005 to 0.20 depending on T_c and nu

## Context

This work extends earlier thermal production calculations by Altherr and Seibert (1994) and Seibert and Fai (1994) by including heavy quark production through thermal gluon decay, which had been previously neglected. The statistical recombination model is similar to those used by Rafelski, Danielewicz, and others (1993) but adds a pair chemical potential to conserve local quark-antiquark density. The results enable predictions for J/psi and Upsilon suppression in heavy ion collisions, showing that heavy meson suppression depends most strongly on interactions with the resonance gas rather than the quark-gluon plasma, and that theoretical uncertainties remain large due to strong dependence on transition temperature and degrees of freedom.
