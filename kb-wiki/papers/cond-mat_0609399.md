# On the non-Boltzmannian nature of quasi-stationary states in long-range interacting systems

> We discuss the non-Boltzmannian nature of quasi-stationary states in the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model, a paradigmatic model for long-range interacting classical many-body systems. We present a theorem excluding the Boltzmann-Gibbs exponential weight in Gibbs $Γ$-space of microscopic configurations, and comment a paper recently published by Baldovin and Orlandini (2006). On the basis of the points here discussed, the ongoing debate on the possible application, within appropriate limits, of the generalized $q$-statistics to long-range Hamiltonian systems remains open.

## Key claim

The quasi-stationary states (QSS) of the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model are non-Boltzmannian in nature, and the claim by Baldovin and Orlandini (2006) that the energy distribution in the QSS is of the Boltzmann-Gibbs form is not justified; the debate on whether generalized q-statistics can apply to long-range Hamiltonian systems remains open.

## Method

The authors performed microcanonical molecular dynamics simulations of the HMF model at energy U=0.69 with N=5000 rotators, using water-bag initial conditions for three different initial magnetizations (M0=1, 0.8, 0), averaged over 10 events. They constructed probability density functions (pdfs) of stochastic variables y defined as sums of rotator momenta taken at fixed time intervals within the QSS regime, using L=5000 rotators and n=500 time intervals spaced every 10 time units, with an additional average over 10 events yielding 50000 y-values per pdf. They also proved a theorem showing that if the one-momentum marginal distribution is non-Gaussian, the full phase-space distribution cannot be proportional to a Boltzmann-Gibbs exponential weight.

## Key numbers

- Energy U=0.69 with N=5000 rotators used for microcanonical simulations, with initial magnetizations M0=1, 0.8, and 0
- For M0=0.8, the pdf of summed momenta in the QSS regime is well fitted by a q-Gaussian with q=1.75
- The largest Lyapunov exponent in the HMF QSS regime goes to zero as 1/N^(1/9), while it remains finite at equilibrium at this energy
- For the M=1000 example from Baldovin and Orlandini (2006), a q-statistical weight with q=1.5 yields a linear correlation of r=-0.99996 when plotted against the BG exponential weight, nearly identical to the r=-0.99997 reported for the BG fit alone

## Context

This paper extends the ongoing debate on whether generalized q-statistics (Tsallis, 1988) can describe quasi-stationary states in long-range interacting systems, directly responding to claims by Baldovin and Orlandini (2006) that the QSS energy distribution follows Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics. The authors argue that the canonical QSS studied by Baldovin and Orlandini differ fundamentally from microcanonical QSS in lifetimes, relaxation times, and correlations, and that the Baldovin-Orlandini results do not exclude q-statistics because the energy range they examined is too narrow to distinguish between exponential and q-exponential forms. The work builds on prior studies of anomalous dynamics in the HMF model including glassy phases, hierarchical structures, velocity correlations, aging, and vanishing Lyapunov exponents (Rapisarda and Pluchino, 2005; Latora, Rapisarda, and Tsallis, 2002; Chavanis, 2006).
