# Is HBT really puzzling?

> Two-particle correlations from RHIC have provided a surprising snapshot of the final state at RHIC. In this talk I discuss the nature of the HBT puzzle and attempt to delineate several factors which might ultimately resolve the issue.

# Is HBT really puzzling?

## Key claim
The "HBT puzzle", the failure of sophisticated dynamical models to reproduce two-pion correlation data from RHIC, may not be a fundamental problem, because simple parameterizations like the blast-wave model fit the data well; the puzzle instead arises from a conspiracy of several small effects (each at the 10% level or less) that push model interpretations toward more physical but currently unaccounted-for dynamics.

## Method
The authors review blast-wave model fits to two-pion correlation data from RHIC, extracting parameters for breakup temperature, fireball radius, collective surface velocity, and breakup time. They then compare these inferred parameters to predictions from hydrodynamic and microscopic transport models, and examine the consistency of extracted phase-space densities and entropies with lattice-gauge-theory equations of state. They also enumerate five specific physical mechanisms (reduced emissivity, finite longitudinal extent, longitudinal acceleration, shear viscosity, and refraction through mean fields) that could individually or collectively resolve the discrepancies.

## Key numbers
- Blast-wave fit parameters from RHIC data: breakup temperature \(T \sim 110\) MeV, surface collective velocity \(v_\perp \sim 0.7c\), fireball radius \(R \sim 12-13\) fm, breakup time \(\tau \sim 9\) fm/\(c\).
- Emission duration is sudden: less than 5 fm/\(c\), roughly consistent with zero.
- Breakup density is in the range of 0.18 fm\(^{-3}\), higher than nuclear matter density.
- Mean free path estimated from 20 mb cross sections is \(\sim 2.5\) fm, much smaller than the 25 fm fireball diameter.
- Pionic source volumes are a factor of two smaller than hydrodynamic model predictions, representing a deficit of \(\ln(2)\) units of entropy per pion.
- Experimentally extracted entropy per unit rapidity \(dS/dy\) is slightly above 4000, consistent with lattice equation of state expectations.
- Phase space densities saturate near the top SPS energies, at the temperature where chemical freezeout occurs (\(\sim 170\) MeV).

## Context
This work extends the discussion of the "HBT puzzle" first noted after 2001 RHIC data, where models like those of Soff (2000) failed to reproduce correlation radii. The authors contrast the successful blast-wave parameterizations (Retiere, 2003; Kisiel, 2005) and Buda-Lund model (Csanad, 2003) with the failures of full dynamical models. They note that entropy analyses by Pal (2003) show consistency with lattice QCD expectations, and that microscopic simulations (Pratt, 1998) show source sizes vary little with cross section. The paper calls for systematic comparisons of hydrodynamic and Boltzmann approaches using the same equations of state, rather than the current ad-hoc model comparisons, to enable robust conclusions about the equation of state from correlation measurements.
