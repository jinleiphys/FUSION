# Fireball tomography from bottomonia elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We calculate the elliptic flow of bottomonia produced in Pb$\,+\,$Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV. We consider temperature-dependent decay widths for the anisotropic escape of various bottomonium states and observe that the transverse momentum dependence of bottomonia elliptic flow provides a tomographic information about the QGP fireball at different stages of its evolution. For the space-time evolution of the fireball, we employ simulation results from the 3+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamic model. We find that our results for transverse momentum dependence of bottomonia elliptic flow are in reasonable agreement with experimental results from the ALICE and CMS collaborations.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum dependence of bottomonia elliptic flow provides a tomographic probe of the QGP fireball's transverse expansion at different stages of its evolution, with the model predicting a negative elliptic flow at low transverse momentum that arises specifically from the inclusion of transverse expansion of the fireball.

## Method

The authors used the 3+1D quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamic (aHydroQP) framework to simulate the space-time evolution of the QGP fireball in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. Bottomonium production points were distributed according to binary collision density from the optical Glauber model, with initial transverse momentum distributions from PYTHIA simulations. The survival probability of each bottomonium state was calculated by integrating temperature-dependent in-medium decay widths along straight-line trajectories through the evolving medium, accounting for formation time effects and feed-down contributions from excited states.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Initial formation times in bottomonium rest frame: 0.2 fm/c for Upsilon(1S), 0.4 fm/c for Upsilon(2S) and chi_b(1P), 0.6 fm/c for Upsilon(3S) and chi_b(2P)
- Initial central temperature: T_0 = 630 MeV
- Specific shear viscosity: 4 pi eta/s = 2.0
- Initial hydrodynamic starting time: tau_i = 0.25 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 130 MeV
- QGP phase transition temperature: T_c = 160 MeV
- Feed-down fractions: f_{2S→1S}=8.6%, f_{3S→1S}=1%, f_{1P→1S}=17%, f_{2P→1S}=5.1%, f_{3P→1S}=1.5%
- Binary collision fraction: kappa_binary = 0.15
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section: sigma_NN = 67 mb

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of bottomonium suppression within the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework by Krouppa et al. (2016) to include elliptic flow predictions and pre-resonance absorption effects. The results agree with experimental data from the ALICE and CMS collaborations and with recent calculations by Islam et al. (2020) that simulate real-time quantum evolution of bottomonium states. The approach enables tomographic studies of the QGP fireball's transverse geometry evolution, with the prediction of negative elliptic flow at low transverse momentum providing a distinctive signature that can be tested with future higher-statistics LHC data.
