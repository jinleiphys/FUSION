# Chaoticity and Shell Effects in the Nearest-Neighbor Distributions

> Statistics of the single-particle levels in a deformed Woods-Saxon potential is analyzed in terms of the Poisson and Wigner nearest-neighbor distributions for several deformations and multipolarities of its surface distortions. We found the significant differences of all the distributions with a fixed value of the angular momentum projection of the particle, more closely to the Wigner distribution, in contrast to the full spectra with Poisson-like behavior. Important shell effects are observed in the nearest neighbor spacing distributions, the larger the smaller deformations of the surface multipolarities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nearest-neighbor spacing distributions (NNSD) for single-particle levels in a deformed Woods-Saxon potential depend strongly on whether one considers the full spectrum or only levels with a fixed angular momentum projection, with the latter being closer to the Wigner distribution; this result is qualified by the observation that shell effects remain significant, especially at smaller deformations.

## Method

The authors diagonalized a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with axially-symmetric surface shapes defined by Legendre polynomials \(P_2\) through \(P_5\) at deformations \(\alpha = 0.1\) and \(0.4\). They computed the single-particle level densities using the Strutinsky shell-correction method with Gaussian width \(\Gamma = 20-40\) MeV and polynomial order \(M = 4-8\), then constructed the NNSD from the unfolded spectra for both the full set of levels and those with fixed angular momentum projection \(m=0\). The resulting distributions were compared to the standard Poisson and Wigner forms, and to an analytical two-parameter Poisson-Wigner interpolation formula derived from a linear level-density ansatz.

## Key numbers

- The Strutinsky smoothing procedure was applied with Gaussian width \(\Gamma = 20-40\) MeV and correction polynomial degree \(M = 4-8\) to obtain the smooth level density.
- For the full spectra at small deformations (\(\alpha \lesssim 0.1\)), clear shell gaps were observed for all multipolarities from \(P_2\) to \(P_5\).
- At larger deformations (\(\alpha \approx 0.1-0.4\)), shell gaps became less pronounced but remained present for all multipolarities.
- For the fixed \(m=0\) spectra, the NNSD at \(P_5\) shape with deformation \(\alpha = 0.4\) showed the most pronounced Wigner-like behavior among all cases studied.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of order-chaos transitions in nuclear mean-field potentials, particularly those using Poincare sections and Lyapunov exponents by Arvieu (1987) and by Blocki, Swiatecki, and collaborators (1990s). It also builds on the Random Matrix Theory framework of Porter (1965), Brody (1981), and Mehta (1991) by applying NNSD analysis to a realistic deformed Woods-Saxon potential rather than idealized Hamiltonians. The results enable a more nuanced understanding of one-body dissipation in nuclear fission and heavy-ion collisions, where the degree of chaoticity of nucleonic motion affects energy dissipation rates, and suggest that future work should combine microscopic-macroscopic approaches (Strutinsky, 1967; Myers and Swiatecki, 1966) to systematically clarify the multipolarity dependence of shell effects in the NNSD.
