# The fission barriers in Actinides and superheavy nuclei in covariant density functional theory

> The impact of pairing correlations on the fission barriers is investigated in Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) theory and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF)+BCS calculations. It is concluded that the constant gap approximation in the usual RMF+BCS calculations does not provide an adequate description of the barriers. The RHB calculations show that there is a substantial difference in the predicted barrier heights between zero-range and finite range pairing forces even in the case when the pairing strengths of these two forces are adjusted to the same value of the pairing gap at the ground state.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the constant gap approximation in RMF+BCS calculations produces only small changes in fission barrier heights when pairing strength is varied, whereas self-consistent treatments (constant G or RHB) show that pairing correlations vary significantly along the fission path and strongly affect barrier heights; the choice between zero-range and finite-range pairing forces leads to substantial differences in predicted barrier heights even when both are adjusted to the same ground-state pairing gap.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric calculations of inner fission barriers in actinides and superheavy nuclei using both RMF+BCS (with constant gap and constant G approximations) and fully self-consistent RHB theory with the NL3 parameterization of the RMF Lagrangian. They compared two pairing interactions in the RHB framework: the finite-range Gogny D1S force and a zero-range delta-force, introducing a scaling factor g to vary pairing strength systematically. The calculations used a deformed harmonic oscillator basis with N_F = N_B = 20 shells, which reproduces binding energies to within 200 keV and fission barriers to within approximately 100 keV of the exact solution.

## Key numbers

- In 240Pu, the constant gap approximation with scaling factor g varied from 0.8 to 1.2 produces only small changes in the fission barrier height, while constant G calculations reduce the barrier height by nearly a factor of 2 over the same range of g.
- For the Gogny D1S force in RHB calculations on 240Pu, the fission barrier height decreases by roughly 1.00 MeV per 0.4 MeV increase in the average ground-state pairing gap Δ_gs.
- The neutron gap parameters in 240Pu from constant G calculations (G_n = 0.0399 MeV, G_p = 0.0616 MeV) vary along the fission path: Δ_n = 0.912 MeV at the normal-deformed minimum, 1.335 MeV at the saddle point, and 1.033 MeV at the superdeformed minimum; proton gaps vary as Δ_p = 1.056 MeV, 1.535 MeV, and 1.236 MeV respectively.
- The pairing window cut-off energy E_cut-off for zero-range delta-force calculations was tested at 60, 90, and 120 MeV, showing increasing differences in barrier heights for larger pairing gaps.

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF+BCS studies of fission barriers (BMR 1994, RMR 1995, BRR 1998, BBM 2004) by performing the first systematic comparison with fully self-consistent RHB calculations, revealing that the constant gap approximation used in those earlier studies is inadequate. The results contrast with non-relativistic Skyrme HFB calculations (SGP 2005, SDN 2007) which also found strong sensitivity of barriers to pairing strength. The paper enables more reliable predictions of fission barriers for superheavy nuclei and r-process nucleosynthesis by establishing that finite-range Gogny pairing forces are preferable to zero-range forces with arbitrary cut-off choices.
