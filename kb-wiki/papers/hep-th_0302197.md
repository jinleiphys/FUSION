# SO(2,1) conformal anomaly: Beyond contact interactions

> The existence of anomalous symmetry-breaking solutions of the SO(2,1) commutator algebra is explicitly extended beyond the case of scale-invariant contact interactions. In particular, the failure of the conservation laws of the dilation and special conformal charges is displayed for the two-dimensional inverse square potential. As a consequence, this anomaly appears to be a generic feature of conformal quantum mechanics and not merely an artifact of contact interactions. Moreover, a renormalization procedure traces the emergence of this conformal anomaly to the ultraviolet sector of the theory, within which lies the apparent singularity.

## Key claim

The paper explicitly extends the existence of anomalous SO(2,1) commutator algebra solutions beyond the previously studied case of scale-invariant contact interactions, demonstrating that the conformal anomaly is a generic feature of conformal quantum mechanics and not merely an artifact of contact interactions. The authors provide the first conclusive direct computation of the anomaly at the level of the commutator algebra for the two-dimensional inverse square potential, showing that the failure of dilation and special conformal charge conservation arises from the ultraviolet singularity at the origin.

## Method

The authors compute the conformal anomaly for the two-dimensional inverse square potential by evaluating the time derivative of the dilation operator expectation value, expressed as the anomalous term A(r) = (1/2) ∇ · {r V(r)}. They regularize the singular potential using a real-space circular well regulator of radius a, replacing the inverse square potential with a finite square well for r < a, then take the limit a → 0 with a running coupling constant that ensures finite bound-state energy. The renormalization procedure determines the coupling's logarithmic running, and the anomalous commutators [D,H] and [K,H] are extracted from the regularized expectation values.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless coupling constant for the two-dimensional inverse square potential is λ = 2M g/ħ², with critical value zero for d=2.
- The anomalous time derivative of the dilation operator expectation value for a normalized bound state is d⟨D⟩/dt = E, where E is the finite renormalized energy of the state.
- The anomalous time derivative of the special conformal operator expectation value is d⟨K⟩/dt = 2tE.
- The running coupling Θ(a) ∝ -[ln(κa)]⁻¹ → 0 as a → 0, where κ = √(2M|E|)/ħ.
- The renormalized ground-state wave function is Ψ(gs)(r) = (κ/√π) K₀(κr), where K₀ is the modified Bessel function.

## Context

This work extends the conformal anomaly first recognized for the two-dimensional contact interaction by Jackiw (1991) and confirmed by direct commutator calculations in Camblong et al. (2003) and Esteve (2003). The inverse square potential is of fundamental importance due to its applications in black hole physics (Strominger, 1998; Carlip, 1999; Solodukhin, 1999), nuclear physics (Beane, 2000; Bedaque, 1999), and molecular physics (molecular dipole anomaly). The results enable further generalization beyond two dimensions and for more general ultraviolet modifications, with implications for the broader framework of conformal quantum mechanics and its applications to the Calogero model and AdS/CFT correspondence.
