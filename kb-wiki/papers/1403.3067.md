# Ab Initio Description of p-Shell Hypernuclei

> We present the first ab initio calculations for p-shell single-Lambda hypernuclei. For the solution of the many-baryon problem, we develop two variants of the no-core shell model with explicit $Λ$ and $Σ^+$, $Σ^0$, $Σ^-$ hyperons including $Λ$-$Σ$ conversion, optionally supplemented by a similarity renormalization group transformation to accelerate model-space convergence. In addition to state-of-the-art chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions, we use leading-order chiral hyperon-nucleon interactions and a recent meson-exchange hyperon-nucleon interaction. We validate the approach for s-shell hypernuclei and apply it to p-shell hypernuclei, in particular to $^7_Λ$Li, $^9_Λ$Be and $^{13}_Λ$C. We show that the chiral hyperon-nucleon interactions provide ground-state and excitation energies that agree with experiment within the cutoff dependence. At the same time we demonstrate that hypernuclear spectroscopy provides tight constraints on the hyperon-nucleon interactions and we discuss the impact of induced hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first *ab initio* calculations for p-shell single-Lambda hypernuclei, showing that leading-order chiral hyperon-nucleon interactions provide ground-state and excitation energies that agree with experiment within the cutoff dependence, while the Juelich'04 meson-exchange interaction systematically gives wrong ordering and splittings of spin-doublet states.

## Method

The authors developed two variants of the no-core shell model (NCSM), the Jacobi NCSM and the importance-truncated NCSM, that include nucleons and Lambda, Sigma-plus, Sigma-zero, and Sigma-minus hyperons as explicit degrees of freedom, accounting for full Lambda-Sigma coupled-channel conversion. They used chiral two-nucleon interactions at N3LO and three-nucleon interactions at N2LO from chiral effective field theory, combined with either leading-order chiral hyperon-nucleon interactions (with cutoffs of 600 and 700 MeV/c) or the Juelich'04 meson-exchange hyperon-nucleon interaction. A similarity renormalization group transformation was optionally applied to accelerate model-space convergence, with separate flow parameters for nucleonic and hyperonic channels.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of Lambda-3-H: -2.33(1) MeV using the bare Hamiltonian with the LO chiral YN interaction (cutoff 600 MeV/c), in agreement with Faddeev calculations
- Ground-state energies for Lambda-4-H and Lambda-4-He: -11.1(1) MeV and -10.3(1) MeV respectively, corresponding to Lambda separation energies of 2.6(1) MeV for both nuclei
- B(E2) strength for the 5/2+ to 1/2+ transition in Lambda-7-Li: 2.3(1) e^2 fm^4 at Nmax=10 and 2.4(1) e^2 fm^4 at Nmax=12, compared to experimental value 3.6(+0.5/-0.5)(stat)(+0.5/-0.4)(syst) e^2 fm^4
- B(M1) strength for the 3/2+ to 1/2+ spin-flip transition in Lambda-7-Li: 0.31(1) mu_N^2, showing good convergence
- Spin-doublet splitting in Lambda-13-C predicted by LO chiral YN interactions: 650 to 700 keV for the largest model spaces

## Context

This work extends *ab initio* methods beyond the s-shell hypernuclei previously limited to systems of up to 4 nucleons (Nemura 2002, Nogga 2002, Haidenbauer 2007, Nogga 2013), building on recent successes of *ab initio* calculations for p-shell nuclei using chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions (Roth and Langhammer 2011, Roth and Calci 2014). The calculations enable direct confrontation of realistic hyperon-nucleon interactions with precise hypernuclear spectroscopy data, providing tight constraints on the hyperon-nucleon interactions and allowing assessment of induced hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interactions. The results demonstrate the need for next-to-leading order chiral hyperon-nucleon interactions and investigation of three-baryon interactions to resolve remaining deficiencies in higher partial waves.
