# Exact Stochastic Mean-Field dynamics

> The exact evolution of a system coupled to a complex environment can be described by a stochastic mean-field evolution of the reduced system density. The formalism developed in Ref. [D.Lacroix, Phys. Rev. E77, 041126 (2008)] is illustrated in the Caldeira-Leggett model where a harmonic oscillator is coupled to a bath of harmonic oscillators. Similar exact reformulation could be used to extend mean-field transport theories in Many-body systems and incorporate two-body correlations beyond the mean-field one. The connection between open quantum system and closed many-body problem is discussed.

## Key claim

The exact evolution of a system coupled to a complex environment can be described by a stochastic mean-field evolution of the reduced system density, but this reformulation is exact only when the environment correlation functions have "simple" expressions and the initial density is separable (though this assumption can eventually be relaxed).

## Method

The authors derive a stochastic mean-field dynamics by starting from the Liouville von-Neumann equation for a system-plus-environment Hamiltonian with a coupling of the form \(h_I = \mathbf{Q} \otimes \mathbf{B}\). They introduce Gaussian stochastic variables \(du_{S/E}\) and \(dv_{S/E}\) with specific variances to rewrite the exact total density as an average over separable densities, then incorporate a mean-field approximation into the system evolution to reduce numerical effort. The method is illustrated on the Caldeira-Leggett model, where a harmonic oscillator is coupled to a bath of harmonic oscillators initially at thermal equilibrium, and the evolution of first and second moments of the system observables is computed.

## Key numbers

- The spectral density used is \(J(\omega) = m \eta \omega \Delta^2_c /(\Delta^2_c + \omega^2)\) with \(\Delta_c = 5 \omega_0\) and \(\eta = 0.5 \hbar\omega_0\).
- The thermal energy is \(k_B T = \hbar\omega_0\).
- Results for the total fluctuation \(\Sigma_{QQ}\) are obtained with \(7 \times 10^5\) trajectories.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Lacroix (2008) for open quantum systems, providing an exact stochastic reformulation of the reduced system dynamics. It contrasts with earlier approximate stochastic transport theories (e.g., Abe 1996, Lacroix 2004) and connects to the Caldeira-Leggett model (Caldeira and Leggett 1983) for dissipation. The approach enables future extension of mean-field transport theories in many-body systems to incorporate two-body correlations beyond the mean-field, potentially overcoming difficulties encountered with Stochastic Schrodinger Equation methods (Gardiner 2000).
