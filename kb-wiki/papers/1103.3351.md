# Thermodynamics of quark quasi-particles ensemble

> The features of hot and dense gas of quarks which are considered as the quasi-particles of the model Hamiltonian with four-fermion interaction are studied. Being adapted to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model this approach allows us to accommodate a phase transition similar to the nuclear liquid-gas one at the proper scale and to argue an existence of the mixed (inhomogeneous) phase of vacuum and normal baryonic matter as a plausible scenario of chiral symmetry (partial) restoration. Analyzing the transition layer between two phases we estimate the surface tension coefficient and speculate on the possible existence of quark droplet.

## Key claim

The authors argue that within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, a phase transition analogous to nuclear liquid-gas transition can occur for a quark quasi-particle ensemble, with a mixed (inhomogeneous) phase of vacuum and normal baryonic matter representing a plausible scenario for partial chiral symmetry restoration, but they caution that their quantitative estimates should not be directly compared to experimentally measured nuclear matter critical temperatures (15–20 MeV).

## Method

The authors study a hot and dense gas of quarks treated as quasi-particles of a model Hamiltonian with four-fermion interaction, adapted to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. They use the Bogolyubov-Hartree-Fock approximation with a statistical operator quadratic in creation and annihilation operators of quark and anti-quark quasi-particles, minimizing the thermodynamic potential at fixed mean charge and entropy. The gap equation for dynamical quark mass is solved at various temperatures and chemical potentials, and the ensemble pressure is computed to identify phase equilibrium conditions via Van der Waals-type isotherms and the Maxwell construction.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition: \( T_c \sim 45.7 \) MeV, with critical charge density \( \bar Q_4 \sim 0.12 \) ch/fm³.
* At zero temperature, the dynamical quark mass in vacuum is \( M = 335 \) MeV (using standard NJL parameters: cutoff \( \Lambda = 631 \) MeV, current quark mass \( m = 5.5 \) MeV, and coupling \( G\Lambda^2/(2\pi^2) = 1.3 \)).
* The liquid phase in equilibrium with the gas phase at zero temperature has density \( \rho_l = 3 \times 0.157 \) ch/fm³ (corrected from an initial estimate of \( 3 \times 0.185 \) ch/fm³), with dynamical quark mass approximately \( \stackrel{*}{M} \approx 70 \) MeV.
* The vacuum pressure estimate for the NJL model is 40–50 MeV/fm³, compatible with conventional bag model results.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Molodtsov and Zinovjev, earlier works) where quarks were treated as quasi-particles and new solution branches of the gap equation for dynamical quark mass as a function of chemical potential were found. It also builds on the idea of surface degrees of freedom in finite Fermi-liquid systems (Khodel). The approach provides a microscopic substantiation of the bag model and suggests that the filled-up quark states could serve as "construction material" for baryons. The results enable further investigation of quark droplet formation and the transition layer between vacuum and baryonic matter, potentially connecting to the observed degeneracy of excited baryon states (Glozman).
