# Event-by-event generation of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions

> We compute the electromagnetic fields generated in heavy-ion collisions by using the HIJING model. Although after averaging over many events only the magnetic field perpendicular to the reaction plane is sizable, we find very strong magnetic and electric fields both parallel and perpendicular to the reaction plane on the event-by-event basis. We study the time evolution and the spatial distribution of these fields. Especially, the electromagnetic response of the quark-gluon plasma can give non-trivial evolution of the electromagnetic fields. The implications of the strong electromagnetic fields on the hadronic observables are also discussed.

## Key claim

While event-averaged electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions leave only the magnetic field perpendicular to the reaction plane (\(B_y\)) sizable, on an event-by-event basis the fluctuating positions of protons in the colliding nuclei generate strong electric and magnetic fields in all transverse directions, with magnitudes comparable to the average \(B_y\).

## Method

The authors used the HIJING Monte-Carlo event generator to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, sampling nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon distribution and treating collisions via the Glauber model. Electromagnetic fields at any spacetime point were computed from the Liénard-Wiechert potentials summed over all charged particles (spectators, participants, and remnants), neglecting back-reaction on particle motion. The electromagnetic response of the quark-gluon plasma was then modeled separately using magnetohydrodynamic equations with Ohm's law and a Bjorken expansion plus linearized transverse flow.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV) and \(b=10\) fm, the event-averaged absolute magnetic field components at the origin satisfy \(\langle |B_x| \rangle \approx \langle |E_x| \rangle \approx \langle |E_y| \rangle \approx \langle |B_y| \rangle\), all of order several \(m_\pi^2/e\).
- The fields at LHC energy (\(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV) are approximately 14 times larger than those at RHIC energy, following the scaling \(e\cdot\text{Field} \propto \sqrt{s}\).
- For central collisions at RHIC, the probability distribution \(P(B_x,B_y)\) peaks at zero field; for off-central collisions (\(b=10\) fm), the distribution shifts to finite \(B_y\).
- The magnetic diffusion time in the QGP ranges from \(\tau \approx 0.3\) fm (using \(\sigma \approx 0.4 C_{\text{EM}}T\)) to \(\tau \approx 150\) fm (using \(\sigma = 6T/e^2\)), depending on the assumed electric conductivity.
- For \(R_m \gg 1\) and ideal magnetohydrodynamics, the magnetic field at the collision center decays as \(B_{x,y}(t,\mathbf{0}) \propto (t_0/t) \exp[-(c_s^2/2a_{y,x}^2)(t^2 - t_0^2)]\) with \(a_x \sim a_y \sim 3\) fm and \(c_s^2 \sim 1/3\).

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event study of electromagnetic fields initiated by Bzdak and Skokov (2011), who used a Bjorken picture with zero nuclear thickness and found comparable \(B_x\) and \(B_y\) fluctuations. The present results are about three times smaller than those of Bzdak and Skokov (2011) because the finite nuclear thickness is included. The paper also generalizes earlier event-averaged estimates by Kharzeev, McLerran, and Warringa (2008) and by Skokov, Illarionov, and Toneev (2009). The magnetohydrodynamic analysis of QGP response builds on the induction-equation approach used by Tuchin (2010) for the low-Reynolds-number regime. The results enable more realistic modeling of the chiral magnetic effect and other magnetic-field-driven phenomena in heavy-ion collisions.
