# Gluon Saturation Effects in Relativistic U+U Collisions

> We examine entropy production in relativistic U+U collisions on the basis of a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) type picture as implemented in the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi model (KLN). In this framework, we find that the peak entropy density produced in tip-on-tip U+U collisions is about 30% greater than that seen in central Au+Au collisions. Although the resulting difference in the produced charged particle multiplicity between tip-on-tip and side-on-side collisions is smaller than that predicted by previous Glauber model estimates, it is still large enough to allow for experimental discrimination between average orientations of the uranium nuclei. We also point out that in the saturation/CGC approach the collision geometry plays a more important role than previously believed, and that the observed centrality dependence of the produced particle multiplicity per participant in Au+Au collisions can be qualitatively reproduced even without running coupling effects.

## Key claim

The peak entropy density produced in tip-on-tip U+U collisions is about 30% greater than that seen in central Au+Au collisions, but the difference in charged particle multiplicity between tip-on-tip and side-on-side collisions is smaller than predicted by previous Glauber model estimates, though still large enough to allow experimental discrimination between average orientations of the uranium nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) parametrization of the Color Glass Condensate model, implementing the Hirano-Nara form of the kT-factorization formula to compute initial energy density per unit rapidity and transverse area. They converted energy density to entropy density assuming thermalization and an ideal gas equation of state, then normalized to PHOBOS charged particle multiplicity data for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 A GeV. They compared results for three choices of the pT cutoff in the kT-factorization integral: a fixed cutoff of 3 GeV/c, a cutoff scaling with the saturation momentum (2 Qs_max), and no cutoff (infinity).

## Key numbers

- Peak entropy density in tip-on-tip U+U collisions is about 30% greater than in central Au+Au collisions (29% for infinite pT cutoff, 35% for variable pT cutoff)
- Difference in mean multiplicity between tip-on-tip and side-on-side U+U collisions in the Glauber model was approximately 14%
- For the KLN model with high multiplicity cuts, average eccentricity is 0.03-0.04 (vs. 0.014 from Glauber); for low multiplicity cuts, average eccentricity is 0.22-0.23 (vs. 0.24 from Glauber)
- After including trigger inefficiencies (0.5% lowest spectator events), average eccentricities are 0.04 for high-multiplicity and 0.14 for low-multiplicity selections (vs. 0.02 and 0.18 for Glauber)
- KLN eccentricities exceed Glauber ones by about 25-30% over most of the impact parameter range for Au+Au collisions
- Normalization constant Gamma = 0.088 used to convert entropy to charged particle multiplicity
- Woods-Saxon parameters for uranium: radius R(theta) = (6.8 fm)(0.91 + 0.26 cos^2 theta), surface thickness xi = 0.54 fm
- Thermalization time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous Glauber model calculations for U+U collisions by Heinz (2004) and Kuhlman (2005) to include Color Glass Condensate effects via the KLN parametrization, following the approach of Hirano and Nara (2004, 2005) who showed that KLN initial conditions produce flatter entropy profiles and larger eccentricities than Glauber models for Au+Au collisions. The paper demonstrates that despite quantitative differences between KLN and Glauber models, the proposed event selection scheme for U+U collisions remains feasible, enabling tests of ideal hydrodynamic behavior at higher entropy densities than available in Au+Au collisions. The results also show that the observed centrality dependence of particle multiplicity per participant in Au+Au collisions can be qualitatively reproduced by a simple geometrical saturation model without running coupling effects.
