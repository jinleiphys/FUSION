# Thermal photon to dilepton ratio in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The ratio of transverse momentum distribution of thermal photons to dilepton has been evaluated. It is observed that this ratio reaches a plateau beyond a certain value of transverse momentum. We argue that this ratio can be used to estimate the initial temperature of the system by selecting the transverse momentum and invariance mass windows judiciously. It is demonstrated that if the radial flow is large then the plateau disappear and hence a deviation from the plateau can be used as an indicator of large radial flow. The sensitivity of the results on various input parameters has been studied.

## Key claim

The ratio of thermal photon to dilepton transverse momentum spectra reaches a plateau for \(p_T > 1.5\) GeV, and the height of this plateau depends primarily on the initial temperature of the system, provided that radial flow is not too large; if radial flow is large, the plateau disappears, so the flatness of the ratio can serve as an indicator of flow strength.

## Method

The authors compute the ratio \(R_{em} = (d^2N_\gamma/d^2p_Tdy)_{y=0} / (d^2N_{\gamma^*}/d^2p_Tdy)_{y=0}\) for thermal photons and dileptons (electron-positron pairs) produced in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. The space-time evolution of the fireball is modeled with (2+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamics assuming longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry. Photon production rates from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) are taken from the complete order-\(\alpha_s\) calculation of Arnold et al., while hadronic photon rates include an exhaustive set of reactions and radiative decays; dilepton rates from the QGP include processes up to \(O(\alpha^2\alpha_s)\), and hadronic dilepton rates use a parametrization based on vector-meson dominance for \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\phi\).

## Key numbers

* Initial temperatures used: \(T_i = 0.2\) GeV (SPS), \(0.4\) GeV (RHIC), \(0.7\) GeV (LHC), with corresponding thermalization times \(\tau_i = 1\) fm, \(0.2\) fm, and \(0.08\) fm.
* The plateau in \(R_{em}\) appears for \(p_T > 1.5\) GeV for all three collision energies.
* For the mass window \(M = 1.2\)–\(1.3\) GeV at \(p_T = 2.5\) GeV, the change in \(R_{em}\) from SPS to RHIC is about \(40\%\), and from RHIC to LHC about \(20\%\).
* Doubling the \(\rho\) meson width (from \(\sim 150\) MeV to \(\sim 300\) MeV) changes \(R_{em}\) by \(10\%\).
* The transition temperature is taken as \(T_c = 192\) MeV; freeze-out temperature is \(T_f = 120\) MeV for all energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of thermal photon and dilepton spectra (e.g., McLerran and Toimela, Gale and Kapusta, Alam et al.) by focusing on the ratio \(R_{em}\) rather than the individual spectra, thereby canceling many common uncertainties in initial conditions, equation of state, and freeze-out parameters. The plateau behavior is explained analytically from the thermal factor at high \(p_T\), and the sensitivity to radial flow is demonstrated by comparing contributions from the QGP phase (little flow) and the hadronic phase (large flow). The authors note that Renk (2006) previously discussed flow effects on high-mass dileptons, but here the analysis is confined to the mass range \(0.3 < M < 1.2\) GeV. The results suggest that simultaneous measurements of photon and dilepton \(p_T\) spectra in selected mass windows could disentangle the true average temperature from collective flow effects.
