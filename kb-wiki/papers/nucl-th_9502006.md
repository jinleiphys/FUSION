# Isospin Dependence of the Spin-Orbit Force and Effective Nuclear Potentials,

> The isospin dependence of the spin-orbit potential is investigated for an effective Skyrme-like energy functional suitable for density dependent Hartree-Fock calculations. The magnitude of the isospin dependence is obtained from a fit to experimental data on finite spherical nuclei. It is found to be close to that of relativistic Hartree models. Consequently, the anomalous kink in the isotope shifts of Pb nuclei is well reproduced.

## Key claim

The isospin dependence of the spin-orbit potential in a non-relativistic Skyrme-like energy functional, when fitted to experimental data on finite spherical nuclei, yields a ratio of spin-orbit coupling strengths \(W_1/W_2\) close to that derived from relativistic mean-field theory, which allows the modified Skyrme Ansatz to reproduce the anomalous kink in the isotope shifts of Pb nuclei that conventional isospin-independent Skyrme forces cannot.

## Method

The authors constructed a Modified Skyrme Ansatz (MSkA) by neglecting the exchange (Fock) term for the spin-orbit potential and introducing an explicit isospin dependence via a parameter \(x_w\) in the two-body spin-orbit interaction. The 11 parameters of the MSkA were determined by a fit to experimental binding energies and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei (\({}^{16}\)O, \({}^{40}\)Ca, \({}^{90}\)Zr, \({}^{208}\)Pb), Sn isotopes (\({}^{116}\)Sn, \({}^{124}\)Sn, \({}^{132}\)Sn), and \({}^{214}\)Pb. The resulting single-particle spin-orbit field was then compared to that obtained from a non-relativistic reduction of the relativistic mean-field Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.1531\) fm\(^{-3}\) for MSkA, matching the value from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM).
- Binding energy per particle in nuclear matter \(E/A = 16.006\) MeV for MSkA.
- Compression modulus \(K = 319.4\) MeV for MSkA.
- Asymmetry energy \(J = 30.0\) MeV for MSkA.
- Effective mass ratio \(m^*/m = 0.76\) for MSkA.
- Ratio of spin-orbit coupling strengths \(W_1/W_2 = 1.0005\) for MSkA, compared to 1.13 for the relativistic parameter set NL1 and 1.10 for NL-SH.
- Parameter \(x_w = 0.0005\) from the fit, close to zero.

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent Hartree-Fock approach by addressing a known failure of conventional Skyrme forces (e.g., SkM*) and Gogny forces to reproduce the kink in Pb isotope shifts (Tajima et al., 1993) and the shell effects at the neutron drip line in Zr isotopes (Dobaczewski et al., 1994). The authors show that introducing an isospin-dependent spin-orbit term, as motivated by relativistic mean-field theory (Sharma et al., 1993), brings non-relativistic Skyrme calculations into closer agreement with both experimental data and relativistic predictions. The results enable further investigations into density-dependent spin-orbit terms and pairing forces to improve agreement for heavier Pb isotopes.
