# Investigating Roles of Triple Excitations for High-precision Determination of Clock Properties of Alkaline Earth Metal Singly Charged Ions

> High-accuracy calculations of electric dipole polarizabilities and quadrupole moments ($Θ$) of the clock states of the singly charged calcium (Ca$^+$), strontium (Sr$^+$) and barium (Ba$^+$) alkaline-earth ions are estimated by employing relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory. It demonstrates importance of the triple excitations in the RCC method for precise determination of the above quantities. We also observe a different trend of correlations in the $Θ$ values than an earlier study with respect to orbitals from higher angular momenta. Reliability of the results is verified by comparing the calculated energies, magnetic dipole hyperfine structure constants, and lifetimes of the atomic states with the experimental values of the $^{43}$Ca$^+$, $^{87}$Sr$^+$ and $^{137}$Ba$^+$ ions. Nuclear quadrupole moments of these isotopes are also estimated by combining calculations with the measured electric quadrupole hyperfine structure constants, showing large deviations from the literature values.

# Investigating Roles of Triple Excitations for High-precision Determination of Clock Properties of Alkaline Earth Metal Singly Charged Ions

## Key claim

The inclusion of triple excitations in the relativistic coupled-cluster (RCCSDT) method improves the accuracy of calculated electric dipole polarizabilities and quadrupole moments for the clock states of Ca⁺, Sr⁺, and Ba⁺, though the triple contributions to polarizabilities are smaller than 0.5% in ground states and 1.5% in D states. The extracted nuclear quadrupole moments of ⁴³Ca, ⁸⁷Sr, and ¹³⁷Ba show deviations of 4–9% from previously reported literature values, suggesting that earlier determinations may be less accurate.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic coupled-cluster method with singles, doubles, and triples (RCCSDT) to compute electric dipole polarizabilities (scalar and tensor components), quadrupole moments, transition amplitudes, hyperfine structure constants, and lifetimes for the clock states of Ca⁺, Sr⁺, and Ba⁺. Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF) orbitals were constructed using Gaussian-type orbital basis functions (40, 39, 38, 37, 36, 35, and 34 functions for s, p, d, f, g, h, and i symmetries respectively), with contributions from orbitals with l ≥ 7 estimated using third-order many-body perturbation theory. Breit interaction and vacuum polarization corrections were included at the RCCSD level, and nuclear quadrupole moments were extracted by combining calculated B/Qₙ ratios with experimentally measured electric quadrupole hyperfine structure constants.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of polarizabilities and quadrupole moments for Ca⁺, Sr⁺, and Ba⁺ that were performed using lower-order methods such as RCC at the singles and doubles level (Jiang 2008, Sur 2006) and multi-configuration Dirac Hartree-Fock (Itano 2006). The authors find a different trend in contributions from high-angular-momentum orbitals compared to the B-spline basis study of Jiang (2008), where basis extrapolation effects were reported as large as 0.9% but are found to be smaller here. The improved calculations enable more reliable determination of systematic effects (Stark shifts, quadrupole shifts) for high-precision clock measurements and atomic parity violation studies, and the extracted nuclear quadrupole moments resolve discrepancies among earlier values from Sahoo (2009), Tang (arxiv), Yu (2004), and Pyykko (2001).
