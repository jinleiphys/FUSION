# Investigating Roles of Triple Excitations for High-precision Determination of Clock Properties of Alkaline Earth Metal Singly Charged Ions

> High-accuracy calculations of electric dipole polarizabilities and quadrupole moments ($Оҳ$) of the clock states of the singly charged calcium (Ca$^+$), strontium (Sr$^+$) and barium (Ba$^+$) alkaline-earth ions are estimated by employing relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory. It demonstrates importance of the triple excitations in the RCC method for precise determination of the above quantities. We also observe a different trend of correlations in the $Оҳ$ values than an earlier study with respect to orbitals from higher angular momenta. Reliability of the results is verified by comparing the calculated energies, magnetic dipole hyperfine structure constants, and lifetimes of the atomic states with the experimental values of the $^{43}$Ca$^+$, $^{87}$Sr$^+$ and $^{137}$Ba$^+$ ions. Nuclear quadrupole moments of these isotopes are also estimated by combining calculations with the measured electric quadrupole hyperfine structure constants, showing large deviations from the literature values.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of polarizabilities and quadrupole moments for CaвҒә, SrвҒә, and BaвҒә that were performed using lower-order methods such as RCC at the singles and doubles level (Jiang 2008, Sur 2006) and multi-configuration Dirac Hartree-Fock (Itano 2006). The authors find a different trend in contributions from high-angular-momentum orbitals compared to the B-spline basis study of Jiang (2008), where basis extrapolation effects were reported as large as 0.9% but are found to be smaller here. The improved calculations enable more reliable determination of systematic effects (Stark shifts, quadrupole shifts) for high-precision clock measurements and atomic parity violation studies, and the extracted nuclear quadrupole moments resolve discrepancies among earlier values from Sahoo (2009), Tang (arxiv), Yu (2004), and Pyykko (2001).
