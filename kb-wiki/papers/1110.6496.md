# Probing multigluon correlations through single spin asymmetries in pp collisions

> We study the single spin asymmetries for the $D$-meson production, Drell-Yan lepton-pair production and the direct-photon production in the $pp$ collision induced by the twist-3 three-gluon correlation functions in the transversely polarized nucleon. We present a corresponding polarized cross section formula in the leading-order with respect to the QCD coupling and a model calculation for the asymmetries, illustrating the sensitivity to the form of the correlation functions.

## Key claim

The paper derives leading-order twist-3 cross sections for single spin asymmetries (SSAs) in pp collisions induced by three-gluon correlation functions, but the numerical predictions depend on model assumptions about the functional form and relative sign of these correlation functions, with the results being sensitive to the small-x behavior of the functions.

## Method

The authors applied the collinear twist-3 factorization formalism, previously developed for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) by Koike et al. (2010), to compute the single-spin-dependent cross sections for D-meson production, Drell-Yan lepton-pair production, and direct-photon production in pp collisions. They performed model calculations for the asymmetries using two assumed functional forms for the three-gluon correlation functions O(x) and N(x), with constants adjusted to be consistent with RHIC preliminary data for D-meson production.

## Key numbers

- RHIC center-of-mass energy: sqrt(S) = 200 GeV
- Transverse momentum for D-meson calculation: P_T = 2 GeV
- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.3 GeV
- Scale for distribution and fragmentation functions: mu = sqrt(P_T^2 + m_c^2)
- Model 1 constant: K_G = 0.004 (for O(x) = K_G * x * G(x))
- Model 2 constant: K_G' = 0.001 (for O(x) = K_G' * sqrt(x) * G(x))
- QED coupling constant: alpha_em = 1/137
- Number of colors: N = 3
- Transverse momentum for direct-photon calculation: q_T = 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the twist-3 formalism for three-gluon correlation functions from SIDIS (Koike et al., 2010) to pp collisions, completing the leading-order twist-3 cross sections for D-meson production, Drell-Yan, and direct-photon processes when combined with known quark-gluon correlation contributions from earlier work (Ji, 1992; Koike et al., 2008; Kanazawa and Koike, 2011). The results enable experimental tests at RHIC to constrain the form and relative sign of the three-gluon correlation functions, particularly through measurements at negative x_F where the asymmetry is sensitive to small-x behavior.
