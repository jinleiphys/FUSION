# Nuclear physics insights for new-physics searches using nuclei: Neutrinoless $ββ$ decay and dark matter direct detection

> Experiments using nuclei to probe new physics beyond the Standard Model, such as neutrinoless $ββ$ decay searches testing whether neutrinos are their own antiparticle, and direct detection experiments aiming to identify the nature of dark matter, require accurate nuclear physics input for optimizing their discovery potential and for a correct interpretation of their results. This demands a detailed knowledge of the nuclear structure relevant for these processes. For instance, neutrinoless $ββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements are very sensitive to the nuclear correlations in the initial and final nuclei, and the spin-dependent nuclear structure factors of dark matter scattering depend on the subtle distribution of the nuclear spin among all nucleons. In addition, nucleons are composite and strongly interacting, which implies that many-nucleon processes are necessary for a correct description of nuclei and their interactions. It is thus crucial that theoretical studies and experimental analyses consider $β$ decays and dark matter interactions with a coupling to two nucleons, called two-nucleon currents.

## Key claim

Neutrinoless ββ decay nuclear matrix elements disagree between different many-body methods by up to a factor three, but this disagreement reduces to 30% or less when calculations are restricted to uncorrelated states, suggesting that the different treatment of nuclear structure correlations is the primary source of the discrepancy.

## Method

The paper reviews nuclear structure calculations for neutrinoless ββ decay using multiple many-body approaches, energy density functional (EDF), quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA), interacting boson model (IBM), and shell model (SM), and compares their predicted nuclear matrix elements across candidate isotopes. For dark matter direct detection, the work applies chiral effective field theory (EFT) to derive consistent one- and two-nucleon interactions with weakly interacting massive particles (WIMPs), computing nuclear structure factors for coherent and spin-dependent scattering off target nuclei such as xenon.

## Key numbers

- Neutrinoless ββ decay experimental sensitivities reach half-lives longer than T_{1/2}^{0νββ} = 10^{26} years (KamLAND-Zen, 2016)
- Dark matter direct detection excludes scattering cross-sections smaller than σ_{χN} = 10^{-40} cm^2 (LUX, 2015)
- Two-neutrino ββ decay measured half-lives are of order T_{1/2}^{2νββ} ∼ 10^{19}–10^{21} years
- Neutrinoless ββ decay is at least five or six orders of magnitude slower than two-neutrino ββ decay
- Two-neutrino ββ decay involves momentum transfers q ∼ 1 MeV, while neutrinoless ββ decay involves q ∼ 100 MeV
- Shell model calculation of ⁴⁸Ca ββ decay in an extended configuration space (from 1 to 2 major harmonic oscillator shells, core reduced from ⁴⁰Ca to ¹⁶O, dimension over 10⁹) produced a 30% enhancement of the nuclear matrix element due to cross-shell pairing correlations
- Uncorrelated nuclear matrix element disagreement between shell model and EDF was limited to 30% or less
- Two-body current contribution to spin-dependent structure factors is encoded by δ ∼ −0.2 for xenon isotopes
- Expected WIMP masses are M_χ ∼ 1–1000 GeV; lighter masses M_χ ∼ 1 MeV are probed via scattering off electrons
- ¹²⁹Xe and ¹³¹Xe have first excited states at 40 keV and 80 keV respectively, relevant for inelastic spin-dependent scattering

## Context

This review extends the line of work comparing nuclear structure methods for neutrinoless ββ decay matrix elements, as summarized by Engel (2016), and builds on prior calculations by the Jyvaskyla, Tubingen, and Chapel Hill QRPA groups, the IBM calculations of Barea (2015), and shell model results from the Michigan, Strasbourg-Madrid, and Tokyo groups. The discussion of two-body currents in dark matter scattering follows the nonrelativistic EFT framework of Fitzpatrick (2012) and Anand (2013), and the chiral EFT treatment of Hoferichter (2016). The paper enables future ab initio calculations of ββ decay matrix elements in medium-mass nuclei and motivates experimental analyses that include two-nucleon currents in both coherent and spin-dependent dark matter searches.
