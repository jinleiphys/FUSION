# Time advancement in resonance regions of pi-N scattering

> We evaluate the time delay in some of the established resonance regions of $πN$ elastic scattering. In addition to the positive peaks corresponding to resonances, we identify broad regions of negative time delay or time advancement which restrict the energy ranges within which the resonances can be located.

# Time advancement in resonance regions of pi-N scattering

## Key claim

While a positive peak in time delay is a necessary condition for the existence of a resonance, several established baryon resonances identified by conventional partial-wave analyses (such as Speed Plots or pole searches) fall in energy regions where the time delay is actually negative (time advancement), which contradicts the fundamental resonance criterion and restricts the allowed mass range for those resonances.

## Method

The author computes the time delay Δt(E) = 2ħ dδ/dE for πN elastic scattering using both fits to single-energy values of the partial-wave T-matrix extracted from cross-section data and the energy-dependent solutions of the VPI group (Arndt et al., 1995). The time delay is evaluated for several partial waves (S11, S31, D33, F35, P33, D13) by taking the energy derivative of the real phase shifts or equivalently from the T-matrix components, and the resulting energy distributions are compared with resonance masses listed in the Particle Data Group Summary Table and from specific analyses (Manley, Cutkosky, Speed Plots, and Arndt partial-wave analysis).

## Key numbers

- The P33 (Δ(1232)) resonance shows a time-delay peak at 1216 MeV with a width of approximately 116 MeV
- The D13 (N(1520)) resonance shows a time-delay peak at 1512 MeV with a width of approximately 50 MeV
- In the S11 partial wave, a negative dip in time delay occurs around 1535 MeV, coinciding with the peak in inelasticity
- The S11 partial wave shows additional time-delay peaks at 1590, 1630, 1680, and 1700 MeV
- The S31 partial wave shows time-delay peaks at 1624 MeV (width 4.8 MeV) and 1680 MeV (width 12.7 MeV), with negative time-delay regions from 1557 to 1618 MeV and 1630 to 1667 MeV
- The D33 partial wave shows a time-delay peak at 1770 MeV (width 17.2 MeV), with negative time delay from 1550 to 1755 MeV
- The F35 partial wave shows a time-delay peak at 1920 MeV (width 16.7 MeV), with negative time delay from 1766 to 1906 MeV
- The VPI group's pole positions for S31 (1585 MeV), D33 (1655 MeV), and F35 (1832 MeV) all lie in regions of negative time delay

## Context

This work extends the time-delay formalism of Bohm, Eisenbud, and Wigner (1950s) and Smith (1960) to πN elastic scattering, applying it as a resonance criterion distinct from conventional methods such as Speed Plots (Höhler, 1993, 1998) and Breit-Wigner fits used by the Particle Data Group. The finding that several well-established resonances (including those from Manley, Cutkosky, and Arndt analyses) fall in regions of time advancement contradicts the fundamental requirement that a resonance must produce positive time delay. This enables more precise determination of resonance masses by using positive time delay as a constraint in future partial-wave analyses, and suggests that some claimed resonances may need re-evaluation.
