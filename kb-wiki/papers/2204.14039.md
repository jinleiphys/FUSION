# Perturbative QCD and the Neutron Star Equation of State

> We construct a physics-agnostic approach to the neutron star (NS) equation of state (EoS) based on a sound speed model, which connects both low-density information from nuclear theory and high-density constraints from perturbative QCD (pQCD). Using this approach, we study the impact of pQCD calculations on NS EoS that have been constrained by astrophysical observations. We find that pQCD affects the EoS mainly beyond the densities realized in NS. Furthermore, we observe an interesting interplay between pQCD and astrophysical constraints, with pQCD preferring softer EoS for the heaviest NS while recent NICER observations suggest the EoS to be stiffer. We explore the sensitivity of our findings to pQCD uncertainties and study the constraining power of pQCD if future observations of heavy NS were to suggest radii larger than 13 km.

## Key claim
The authors find that perturbative QCD (pQCD) calculations do not significantly constrain the neutron star equation of state when imposed on top of current astrophysical observations, but they are on the brink of becoming constraining; this conclusion depends sensitively on the density at which the pQCD constraints are matched to the EoS.

## Method
The authors construct a physics-agnostic sound-speed model for the neutron star EoS, connecting low-density nuclear theory (using the SLy4 Skyrme EDF up to saturation density) to high-density pQCD constraints via the integral method of Komoltsev and Kurkela (2021). They generate an ensemble of EoSs, compute the corresponding mass-radius-tidal deformability relations, and impose constraints from GW170817 tidal deformability, NICER X-ray observations of pulsars J0740+6620 and J0030+0451, and radio observations of heavy pulsars with M > 2 solar masses. The pQCD constraints are applied by requiring thermodynamic consistency between the EoS at the TOV point and the pQCD EoS at chemical potentials of 2.6 GeV or 2.4 GeV, with the renormalization scale parameter X varied between 1, 2, and 4.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817: Λ̃ = 222^{+420}_{-138} at 90% confidence level
- pQCD matching chemical potential: μ_pQCD = 2.6 GeV (primary) and 2.4 GeV (sensitivity study)
- For μ_pQCD = 2.6 GeV, the minimal value of X at which pQCD becomes constraining is around X ≈ 1.3
- Central chemical potentials in maximally massive neutron stars can reach μ ≈ 2.2 GeV
- When imposing a hypothetical future constraint R_{2.0} > 13 km, pQCD with X=2 and X=4 produces a pronounced peak in sound speed at ε ≳ 500 MeV fm^{-3}

## Context
This work extends the approach of Komoltsev and Kurkela (2021), who showed that pQCD calculations exclude about 65% of the area in the pressure-energy density plane at n = 5 n_sat when neglecting neutron star observations. The present study adds astrophysical constraints and finds less dramatic impact, contrary to the larger effect reported by Gorda et al. (2022) who matched pQCD at a fixed density of 10 n_sat rather than the EoS-dependent TOV density. The results enable future combined analyses of astrophysical and pQCD data to potentially distinguish between nucleonic and exotic degrees of freedom in neutron star cores.
