# Kaon photoproduction on the nucleon with constrained parameters

> The new experimental data of kaon photoproduction on the nucleon, gamma p -> K+ Lambda, have been analyzed by means of a multipoles model. Different from the previous models, in this analysis the resonance decay widths are constrained to the values given by the Particle Data Group (PDG). The result indicates that constraining these parameters to the PDG values could dramatically change the conclusion of the important resonances in this reaction found in the previous studies.

## Key claim

Constraining resonance decay widths to the Particle Data Group (PDG) values in a multipoles model of kaon photoproduction can dramatically change which resonances are identified as important, compared to previous unconstrained analyses.

## Method

The authors constructed a tree-level multipoles model with Breit-Wigner resonant amplitudes for the reaction γp → K⁺Λ, including background terms from s-, u-, and t-channel Born diagrams plus K*(892) and K₁(1270) vector meson exchanges. They fitted the model to SAPHIR and CLAS data sets separately, while forcing the resonance decay widths to match the PDG values, and then compared the resulting resonance contributions to those from earlier unconstrained fits.

## Key numbers

- The largest discrepancy between SAPHIR and CLAS data occurs at W ≈ 1.9 GeV in forward and backward directions.
- The two-star resonance P₁₃(1900) has a total width Γ ≈ 500 MeV, and its extracted mass was found to be much larger than 1900 MeV.
- The S₁₁(2090) resonance showed a Δχ² ≈ 6% in a prior study (Mart 2006), but in the present constrained fit its importance increased.
- The D₁₃ resonance previously identified as "missing" had a total width Γ varying from 165 to 570 MeV in earlier work (Mart 2006).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Mart 2006, Mart 2008) that investigated how using SAPHIR versus CLAS data leads to different resonance parameters and conclusions about "missing resonances." It also relates to the analysis by Julia-Diaz (2006), which found similar results except for the role of P₁₃(1720). By constraining decay widths to PDG values, the study shows that the second peak in the total cross section originates from S₁₁(2090) and P₁₁(2100) rather than a missing D₁₃ resonance, contradicting earlier findings. This enables future single-channel analyses to account for unitarity corrections by incorporating information from leading π and η channels.
