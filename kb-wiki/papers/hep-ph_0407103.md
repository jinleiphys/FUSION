# Equation of State of Gluon Plasma from Fundamental Modular Region

> Numerical studies of the bulk properties of the quark-gluon plasma reveal a pronounced drop in pressure $p$ compared to energy density $\e$ as the deconfining temperature is approached from above. We show by general thermodynamic reasoning that this can be explained only by a term $LT$ in the trace anomaly $þ(T) = \e - 3p$ that is linear in the temperature $T$. Such a term appears in the equation of state of the gluon plasma, which we calculate, described as a gas of quasi-particle gluons with dispersion relation $E(k) = \sqrt{k^2 + {M^4 \over k^2}}$, that follows from the restriction of the configuration space to the fundamental modular region.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the pronounced drop in pressure relative to energy density observed in lattice simulations of the gluon plasma near the deconfining temperature can be explained by a term linear in temperature in the trace anomaly, which arises naturally from a gas of free quasi-particles with a Gribov-type dispersion relation derived from restricting the physical state space to the fundamental modular region; this holds under the condition that the dispersion relation satisfies a power-law deviation from k with exponent greater than 3.

## Method

The author calculates the equation of state of a gas of non-interacting quasi-particles (the "FMR gas") using the Planck distribution, with a temperature-independent dispersion relation of Gribov form E(k) = sqrt(k^2 + M^4/k^2). The partition function, energy density, pressure, and trace anomaly are derived from standard statistical mechanics formulas, and the high-temperature asymptotic behavior is analyzed by expanding the dispersion relation. The single free parameter M is determined by fitting the trace anomaly from the analytic formula to numerical lattice data from Karsch (1996) at high temperature.

## Key numbers

- The trace anomaly at high temperature takes the asymptotic form theta = L T + O(1), where L = (N^2 - 1) (pi sqrt(2))^{-1} M^3 for the Gribov dispersion relation.
- The mass scale M is fitted to M = 2.6 T_c, where T_c is the transition temperature; using T_c = 0.625 sqrt(sigma) from Karsch (1996) and string tension sqrt(sigma) = 440 MeV, this gives M = 1.6 sqrt(sigma) = 705 MeV.
- The minimum energy from the Gribov dispersion relation is E_min = sqrt(2) M = 997 MeV (for M = 705 MeV).
- The Stefan-Boltzmann constant is c_SB = (pi^2 / 45)(N^2 - 1) for SU(N) gauge theory.
- The pressure at high temperature behaves as p = c_SB T^4 - (1/3) L T + O(1), and the energy density as epsilon = 3 c_SB T^4 + O(1).

## Context

This work extends the Gribov scenario (Gribov, 1978) for infrared suppression of gluon modes to the equation of state of the gluon plasma, and it provides a theoretical foundation for earlier phenomenological cut-off models (DeGrand and others, 1987) that used a temperature-independent infrared cut-off. The FMR gas is contrasted with standard finite-temperature perturbation theory, which suffers from infrared divergences discovered by Linde (1980) and which requires a magnetic mass m ~ g^2 T (Pisarski, 1981) to control them; here the effective mass M^2/k controls infrared divergences instead. The paper enables a zeroth-order approximation that allows calculable perturbative-type corrections, for example via Dyson-Schwinger equations, and it is compared with lattice data from Karsch (1996) and phenomenological fits by Engels and others (1989).
