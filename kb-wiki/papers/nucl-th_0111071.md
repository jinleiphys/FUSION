# On the nucleon-nucleon interaction leading to a standing wave instability in symmetric nuclear matter

> We examine a recently proposed nucleon-nucleon interaction, claimed by its authors both realistic and leading to a standing wave instability in symmetric nuclear matter. Contrary to these claims, we find that this interaction leads to a serious overbinding of 4He, 16O and 40Ca nuclei when the Hartree-Fock method is properly applied. The resulting nuclear densities contradict the experimental data and all realistic Hartree-Fock results.

# On the nucleon-nucleon interaction leading to a standing wave instability in symmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim
The interaction proposed by Over (2001) is claimed to be both realistic and to produce a standing wave instability in symmetric nuclear matter, but when the Hartree-Fock method is properly applied (with exact exchange integrals and adequate basis), the interaction leads to serious overbinding of finite nuclei and unphysical central density peaks, making the claimed instability unfounded.

## Method
The author performed exact spherical and three-dimensional Hartree-Fock calculations for the magic nuclei ⁴He, ¹⁶O, and ⁴⁰Ca using the proposed interaction, which consists of a Gaussian-weighted quadratic attractive term plus a contact repulsion term proportional to the square root of the average kinetic energy. The exchange integrals were calculated exactly using the Slater method for spherical symmetry, rather than approximated as a fraction of direct terms as in the original work. The Hartree-Fock equations were solved by imaginary-time evolution on a radial mesh of 100 points (spherical code) and a three-dimensional mesh of about 30 points per direction.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy per nucleon of ⁴He: 8.076 MeV (0.78 MeV more than the value reported in Over (2001), which used the experimental value corrected for Coulomb)
- Binding energy of ¹⁶O (without Coulomb): 173.57 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 127.619 MeV (Wapstra, year not given); after subtracting ~13 MeV for Coulomb, overbinding exceeds 30 MeV
- Binding energy of ⁴⁰Ca (without Coulomb): 565.17 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 342.052 MeV (Wapstra, year not given); after subtracting ~71 MeV for Coulomb, overbinding is about 152 MeV
- For initial configurations from Over (2001), the correct binding energies per nucleon are 8.801 MeV for ¹⁶O and 11.137 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca, versus the reported 8.59 MeV and 10.76 MeV
- Central density of ¹⁶O is more than 1.5 times larger than the experimental value; central density of ⁴⁰Ca is three times larger than the experimental value (Bates, year not given)

## Context
This work directly contradicts the claims of Over (2001) that the proposed interaction is realistic and produces a standing wave instability in symmetric nuclear matter. The author shows that the original work used a very restricted harmonic oscillator basis and approximate treatment of exchange integrals, leading to incorrect binding energies. The exact Hartree-Fock results demonstrate that the interaction fails basic nuclear physics constraints, overbinding ⁴⁰Ca by approximately 152 MeV and producing central density peaks unknown in nuclear physics. This invalidates the basis for the claimed standing wave instability, which was derived from an unrealistic interaction.
