# Preequilibrium particle emissions and in-medium effects on the pion production in heavy-ion collisions

> Within the framework of the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, pion dynamics in heavy-ion collisions near threshold energies and the emission of preequilibrium particles (nucleons and light complex fragments) have been investigated. A density, momentum and isospin dependent pion-nucleon potential based on the $Δ$-hole model is implemented in the transport approach, which slightly leads to the increase of the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio, but reduces the total pion yields. It is found that a bump structure of the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio in the kinetic energy spectra appears at the pion energy close to the $Δ$(1232) resonance region. The yield ratios of neutrons to protons from the squeeze-out particles perpendicular to the reaction plane are sensitive to the stiffness of nuclear symmetry energy, in particular at the high-momentum (kinetic energy) tails.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pion-nucleon potential based on the Δ-hole model slightly increases the π⁻/π⁺ ratio but reduces total pion yields, and that a bump structure in the π⁻/π⁺ kinetic energy spectra appears at the Δ(1232) resonance energy (E_kin = 190 MeV) only when the pion-nucleon potential is included; the isospin effect on these spectra is negligible.

## Method

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate heavy-ion collisions near threshold energies. A density-, momentum-, and isospin-dependent pion-nucleon potential derived from the Δ-hole model was implemented, along with reaction channels for pion production via Δ(1232) and N*(1440, 1535) resonances. Preequilibrium particles (nucleons and light complex fragments) were analyzed in the squeeze-out region perpendicular to the reaction plane, and the stiffness of the nuclear symmetry energy was varied (γ_s = 0.5, 1, 2) to study its effect on neutron-to-proton and π⁻/π⁺ ratios.

## Key numbers

- The threshold energy for π⁺ production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is E_th = 289 MeV.
- In central ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at 400A MeV, the average density of pion production is 1.07ρ₀ with πN absorption and 1.44ρ₀ without it (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³).
- The pion optical potential strengths at the Δ resonance energy (p = 2.1 m_π) are -12 MeV, -19 MeV, and -27 MeV at normal density (ρ₀) for π⁻, π⁰, and π⁺, respectively; at 3ρ₀ they are 7 MeV, -58 MeV, and -124 MeV.
- The bump structure in the π⁻/π⁺ kinetic energy spectra appears at E_kin = 190 MeV, corresponding to the Δ(1232) resonance.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is 31.5 MeV for all stiffness cases.

## Context

This work extends prior transport-model studies of pion dynamics and the high-density symmetry energy, such as those by Li (2002, 2005), Feng (2005, 2013), and the pBUU model of Hong (2014). It contrasts with the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (pBUU) calculations by Hong (2014), which showed a significant effect of symmetry energy stiffness on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio at high kinetic energies (>80 MeV), whereas the present LQMD model finds negligible isospin effects in the kinetic energy spectra. The results enable future experimental tests at facilities such as HIRFL-CSR (Lanzhou) and RIKEN-SAMURAI (Japan) to probe both the high-density symmetry energy and the in-medium pion-nucleon interaction.
