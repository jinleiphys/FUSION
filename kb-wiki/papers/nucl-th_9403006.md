# Covariant Model for Dynamical Quark Confinement

> Based on a recent manifestly covariant time-ordered approach to the relativistic many-body problem, the quark propagator is defined by a nonlinear Dyson--Schwinger-type integral equation, with a one-gluon loop. The resulting energy-dependent quark mass is such that the propagator is singularity-free for real energies, thus ensuring confinement. The self-energy integral converges without regularization, due to the chiral limit of the quark mass itself. Moreover, the integral determines the low-energy limit of the quark-gluon coupling constant, for which a value of $g^2/4π=4.712$ is found.

## Key claim

The paper presents a covariant model for dynamical quark confinement where the quark propagator is singularity-free for real energies, ensuring confinement, but the author notes that the model is the "simplest-possible" one-gluon-loop expansion and that the treatment of gluon confinement via restricting to longitudinal and scalar gluons "can offer no deeper justification" other than that it works for the present purpose.

## Method

The author uses a manifestly covariant time-ordered approach to the relativistic many-body problem to define the quark propagator via a nonlinear Dyson-Schwinger-type integral equation with a one-gluon loop. The self-energy integral is evaluated in the center-of-momentum system, with the gluon treated as an undressed particle restricted to longitudinal and scalar components, and the resulting nonlinear self-consistency equation for the dynamical quark mass is solved by straightforward iteration.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy limit of the quark-gluon coupling constant is found to be $g^2/4\pi = 4.712$, numerically accurate to better than 0.1%.
- The dynamical quark mass at zero energy $m(0)$ is used to fix the energy scale, with the author noting it "should be roughly given by the phenomenological constituent mass of the quarks, around 300 MeV, which in turn is about equal to $\Lambda_{QCD}$."
- The starting function for iteration used $m_0 = 1$ as a constant to fix the energy scale.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Preparata (1974) and Fukada and Kugo (1976) where confinement is achieved by ensuring the quark propagator has no pole, and builds upon the cluster-dynamical approach to the relativistic many-body problem proposed by Haberzettl (1993, 1994). The model enables further investigation of running coupling constant effects, which the author notes may increase the low-energy coupling constant by up to about an order of magnitude, and suggests that the analytic properties of the dynamical quark mass for complex energies could be explored in future work.
