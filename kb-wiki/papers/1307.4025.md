# Mass predictions of superheavy nuclei from the systematics of $α$-Decay Energies

> A recent proposed method for $α$-decay energies ($Q_α$) [J.M. Dong, W. Zuo, and W. Scheid, Phys. Rev. Lett. \textbf{107}, 012501 (2011)] can reproduce experimental data of superheavy nuclei (SHN) with an $rms$-value of less than 100 keV. However, a sinusoid-like periodic deviation from experiments, which limits the accuracy in predictions, is observed when using different reference nuclei. In this paper, we have further extended this hybrid method, i.e., to predict $Q_α$ of the as-yet-unobserved SHN with the help of known nuclei. It is found that the systematic deviation in previous study is rooted in the nuclear mass model employed. By further analyzing the source of errors, different nuclear mass models are evaluated based on the same procedure.

## Key claim

The hybrid method for predicting α-decay energies of superheavy nuclei, which previously showed a sinusoid-like periodic deviation from experimental data with an amplitude of about 0.07 MeV, can be improved by attributing that systematic deviation to the underlying nuclear mass model; after subtracting the periodic pattern, the rms deviation for the test case ²⁹⁴118 drops from 0.196 MeV to 0.038 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended the hybrid method of Dong, Zuo, and Scheid (2011), which derives an unknown Qα value for a target nucleus from an experimental Qα of a nearby reference nucleus plus the theoretical Qα difference between the two nuclei from a mass model. They applied this procedure using 26 reference nuclei with Z ≥ 110 and tested five mass models: liquid-drop model (LD), finite-range droplet model (FRDM), Weizsäcker-Skyrme (WS3.6), Duflo-Zuker (DZ28), and extended Thomas-Fermi plus Strutinsky integral with quenched shell (ETFSI-Q). The systematic deviations between predicted and experimental Qα values were analyzed as a function of reference nucleus mass number.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁹⁴118 using the LD model, the sine-like deviation had an amplitude of 0.0677 MeV and a wavelength of 8.809 mass units.
- After subtracting the periodic systematic error for ²⁹⁴118, the rms deviation improved from 0.196 MeV to 0.038 MeV.
- The rms deviations of the hybrid method for all SHN with Z ≥ 110 were: FRDM 0.56 MeV, WS3.6 0.27 MeV, DZ28 0.47 MeV, LD 0.31 MeV; the corresponding direct model predictions were FRDM 0.65 MeV, WS3.6 0.27 MeV, DZ28 0.77 MeV, LD 0.36 MeV.
- For ETFSI-Q, the hybrid method reduced the rms from 0.53 MeV to 0.27 MeV, a factor of 2 improvement.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid method of Dong, Zuo, and Scheid (2011) by identifying the source of its systematic sine-like deviation as the nuclear mass model rather than the method itself. It evaluates five global mass models (FRDM from Moller 1995, WS3.6 from Liu 2011, DZ28 from Duflo 1995, LD from Dong 2008, and ETFSI-Q from Pearson 1996) on the same procedure, showing that the liquid-drop model optimized for the superheavy region performs best. The approach enables more reliable predictions of Qα values for as-yet-unobserved superheavy nuclei and, combined with direct mass measurements, could extend the measured mass surface into more exotic regions.
