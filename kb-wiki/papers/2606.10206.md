# Charm quark production in heavy-ion collisions as a signature of pre-equilibrium

> The relative abundances and kinematic distributions of hadrons containing (anti)charm quarks are key observables for deconfinement, heavy-quark diffusion and hadronization in heavy-ion collisions. The production of (anti)charm quarks is commonly associated to the initial hard scatterings in hadronic collisions. Based on previous studies on dilepton production, we evaluate the (anti)charm quark production from the pre-equilibrium phase. A non-negligible contribution to the overall charm quark production is found albeit large theoretical uncertainties are limiting factors. We conclude that precise total charm production measurements combined with progress on charm production calculations from the initial hard scatterings can be used to infer information on the pre-equilibrium stage.

## Key claim

The authors evaluate charm quark production from the pre-equilibrium phase of heavy-ion collisions and find a non-negligible contribution to the overall charm yield, but emphasize that large theoretical uncertainties, particularly from the extrapolation of hard-scattering production to nucleus-nucleus collisions and from the current state-of-the-art theory calculations, are limiting factors that must be addressed before precise total charm production measurements can be used to infer information on the pre-equilibrium stage.

## Method

The authors calculate pre-equilibrium charm-anti-charm pair production to leading order in perturbative QCD, employing an effective kinetic theory of QCD to describe the pre-equilibrium dynamics. They compute the production rate using phase-space distributions of gluons, quarks, and anti-quarks, with matrix elements for both quark-antiquark annihilation and gluon-gluon fusion channels. The pre-equilibrium evolution is modeled via conformal Bjorken flow using a universal scaling variable \(\tilde{w} = \tau T_{\text{eff}}/(4\pi \eta/s)\), and the results are compared with a Romatschke-Strickland parametrization model where the phase-space distributions are matched to the QCD kinetic theory evolution of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

* The transverse system size for central Pb+Pb collisions is taken as \(A_T = 104~\text{fm}^2\).
* The entropy per hadron in a hadron resonance gas at freeze-out is taken as \(S/N_{ch} = 6.7\).
* The effective number of degrees of freedom used for the high-temperature equation of state is \(\nu_{\text{eff}} = 40\).
* The charm quark mass range considered is \(1.25~\text{GeV} < m_c < 1.5~\text{GeV}\).
* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratios considered are \(\eta/s = 0.16\) and \(\eta/s = 0.32\).
* QCD kinetic theory simulations are performed at coupling strengths \(\lambda = 5, 10, 20\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies on dilepton production from the pre-equilibrium phase (Coquet 2021, Plaschke 2025) to charm quark production, using the same theoretical framework. The authors note that while thermal charm production has been discussed in the literature (Zhang 2007, Uphoff 2010, Zhou 2016, Song 2024), charm production has not previously been discussed in the context of the pre-equilibrium phase. The results are complementary to dilepton production studies since charm production is also sensitive to the gluon abundance in the pre-equilibrium stage. The work enables future comparisons of precise total charm production measurements from ALICE 2, LHCb U1, ALICE 3, CMS, and LHCb U2 with estimates from initial hard scatterings based on proton-proton data and nuclear parton densities, potentially constraining the pre-equilibrium phase.
