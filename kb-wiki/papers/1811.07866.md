# Error analysis of the parameters of the Skyrme N2LO pseudo-potential

> We perform a detailed analysis of the parameters of the Skyrme SN2LO interaction. By mean of a covariance matrix analysis, we have been able to provide error bars and build the complete covariance matrix. The latter will be used in future applications to properly study the propagation of errors on nuclear observables calculated with this pseudo-potential.

# Error analysis of the parameters of the Skyrme N2LO pseudo-potential

## Key claim
The authors present a covariance analysis of the Skyrme N2LO pseudo-potential parameters, but the original SN2LO1 functional was found to be "sloppy" with poorly constrained higher-order parameters; only after modifying the weights of the penalty function to find a different local minimum could a meaningful covariance matrix and error bars be obtained.

## Method
The 14 parameters of the Skyrme N2LO pseudo-potential were determined by minimizing a chi-squared penalty function containing both infinite nuclear matter properties and finite-nuclei observables, following the Saclay-Lyon fitting protocol with additional asymmetric constraints to avoid finite-size instabilities. The covariance matrix was computed as the inverse of the Hessian matrix of the chi-squared function at the minimum, with numerical derivatives evaluated using an optimal step-size criterion from Roccia (2015). To obtain a well-constrained minimum, the weights of the penalty function components were modified, yielding a new parametrization named SN2LO1_cov.

## Key numbers
- t0 = -2477.48 ± 5.87 MeV fm^3; x0 = 0.766 ± 0.014
- t1 (second order) = 507.80 ± 6.64 MeV fm^5; x1 (second order) = 0.001 ± 0.003
- t2 (second order) = -421.48 ± 23.07 MeV fm^5; x2 (second order) = -0.918 ± 0.012
- t1 (fourth order) = -29.68 ± 4.67 MeV fm^7; x1 (fourth order) = 0.352 ± 0.006
- t2 (fourth order) = 4.69 ± 0.63 MeV fm^7; x2 (fourth order) = 2.14 ± 0.057
- t3 = 13660.67 ± 54.94 MeV fm^(3(1+alpha)); x3 = 1.173 ± 0.018; alpha = 1/6 (fixed)
- W0 = 122.3 ± 6.7 MeV fm^5

## Context
This work extends the UNEDF collaboration studies (2013-2014) on the flexibility of standard Skyrme functionals and the earlier development of the extended Skyrme pseudo-potential by Davesne et al. (2013, 2015, 2016). The SN2LO1 parametrization from Becker et al. (2017) was the first stable parametrization incorporating higher-order gradient terms, but the present analysis reveals that the higher-order parameters are non-zero and non-redundant. The covariance matrix enables future propagation of errors on nuclear observables calculated with this pseudo-potential, addressing the need for rigorous error treatment emphasized by Dobaczewski et al. (2014).
