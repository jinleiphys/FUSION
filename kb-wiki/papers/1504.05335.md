# Well funneled nuclear structure landscape: renormalization

> A complete characterization of the structure of nuclei can be obtained by combining information arising from inelastic scattering, Coulomb excitation and $γ-$decay, together with one- and two-particle transfer reactions. In this way it is possible to probe the single-particle and collective components of the nuclear many-body wavefunction resulting from their mutual coupling and diagonalising the low-energy Hamiltonian. We address the question of how accurately such a description can account for experimental observations. It is concluded that renormalizing empirically and on equal footing bare single-particle and collective motion in terms of self-energy (mass) and vertex corrections (screening), as well as particle-hole and pairing interactions through particle-vibration coupling allows theory to provide an overall, quantitative account of the data.

# Well funneled nuclear structure landscape: renormalization

## Key claim

The paper claims that a theoretical description of nuclear structure based on renormalizing single-particle and collective degrees of freedom through particle-vibration coupling can provide an overall quantitative account of experimental data, but only if the collective modes are themselves empirically renormalized because the bare SLy4 interaction produces insufficient collective density vibrations.

## Method

The authors selected the spherical superfluid nucleus ¹²⁰Sn and its neighboring isotopes ¹¹⁸,¹¹⁹,¹²⁰,¹²¹,¹²²Sn, using HFB with a SLy4 effective interaction and a v₁₄(¹S₀) Argonne bare pairing potential, together with QRPA for collective modes. They calculated dressed single-particle states and the induced pairing interaction through particle-vibration coupling, then solved the Nambu-Gor'kov equation self-consistently using Green's function techniques to obtain renormalized quasiparticle energies and state-dependent pairing gaps. The collective QRPA modes were empirically renormalized by coupling two-quasiparticle solutions to four-quasiparticle doorway states to achieve proper collectivity.

## Key numbers

- Experimental pairing gap Δ_exp ≈ 1.45 MeV for ¹²⁰Sn
- Bare pairing interaction strength v_p^bare consistent with v₁₄(¹S₀) giving G₀ ≈ 0.22 MeV
- Dynamical quadrupole deformation parameter (β₂)₀ ≈ (β₂)_exp ≈ 0.13
- Effective k-mass m_k ≈ 0.7 m
- For the d₅/₂ orbital in ¹²⁰Sn(p,d)¹¹⁹Sn: theoretical summed cross section σ = 6.15 mb compared to experimental 7.93 ± 2 mb
- After shifting the d₅/₂ bare single-particle energy by 600 keV: predicted peak at 1050 keV with 4.4 mb, observed at 1090 keV with 5.35 ± 1.3 mb
- State-dependent k-mass from optimization: ⟨(m_k)ν/m⟩ ≈ 0.74 ± σ, with σ = 0.07

## Context

This work extends the nuclear field theory approach to particle-vibration coupling developed by the authors and collaborators (Idini et al. 2012, Potel et al. 2013) and applies it to a complete characterization of the Sn isotopic chain using multiple complementary observables. It contrasts with approaches that study single nuclear properties across the mass table (Tarpanov et al. 2015) and demonstrates that focusing on a single observable can lead to incorrect conclusions. The results enable quantitative predictions for direct reaction cross sections in spherical superfluid nuclei and are transferable to other mass zones with similar properties, though the authors note the approach is likely circumscribed by phase transition domains.
