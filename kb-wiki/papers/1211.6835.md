# Shearing Black Holes and Scans of the Quark Matter Phase Diagram

> Future facilities such as FAIR and NICA are expected to produce collisions of heavy ions generating quark-gluon plasmas with large values of the quark chemical potential; peripheral collisions in such experiments will also lead to large values of the angular momentum density, associated with the internal shearing motion of the plasma. It is well known that shearing motions in fluids can lead to instabilities which cause a transition from laminar to turbulent flow, and such instabilities in the QGP have recently attracted some attention. We set up a holographic model of this situation by constructing a gravitational dual system exhibiting an instability which is indeed triggered by shearing angular momentum in the bulk. We show that holography indicates that the transition to an unstable fluid happens more quickly as one scans across the quark matter phase diagram towards large values of the chemical potential. This may have negative consequences for the observability of quark polarization effects.

# Shearing Black Holes and Scans of the Quark Matter Phase Diagram

## Key claim
Holographic modeling indicates that shear-induced instabilities in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) develop more quickly as the quark chemical potential increases, which may suppress observable quark polarization effects at future facilities such as FAIR and NICA. The claim is conditional on the holographic model being a valid proxy for QCD, which the author acknowledges is not yet known.

## Method
The author constructs a holographic dual using the QKMV₀ (charged, shearing planar AdS black hole) spacetime, which exhibits frame-dragging that persists at infinity and mimics the shearing motion of the QGP in peripheral heavy-ion collisions. A Seiberg-Witten brane system is placed in this background; the brane action becomes unbounded below when shearing angular momentum is present, signaling an instability. The black hole parameters (mass density M*, charge density Q*, horizon radius r_h) are related to boundary quantities (temperature T, chemical potential μ, shear parameter a) via three equations derived from the horizon condition, Hawking temperature, and regularity of the Euclidean electromagnetic potential.

## Key numbers
- The QGP lifetime is estimated at roughly 5 fm in natural units, used to set the AdS curvature scale L.
- The critical point temperature T_c is approximately 175 MeV (below 1 fm⁻¹ in natural units).
- QGP temperatures in future beam energy scan experiments are estimated in the range 0.5–2 fm⁻¹.
- The shear parameter a for RHIC collisions with optimal impact parameter is estimated at roughly 20 fm; results change by less than 1% if a = 100 fm is used instead when chemical potential is not negligible.
- The angular momentum acquired by the QGP in gold-gold collisions at RHIC is estimated at 7.2 × 10⁴ per collision in natural units (from Becattini et al., cited in the text).
- The quark chemical potential μ around the critical point is estimated similar to the temperature, with experiments expected to probe values between 1 and 3–4 fm⁻¹.

## Context
This work extends holographic studies of the QGP phase diagram, specifically addressing the region of large quark chemical potential to be explored by FAIR and NICA. It builds on the KMV₀ black hole metrics of Klemm, Moretti, and Vanzo (2003) and the Seiberg-Witten brane instability mechanism (1999). The paper contrasts with earlier work by Atmaja and Schalm using AdS-Kerr black holes, which describe rigid rotation rather than shear. The results suggest that polarization effects predicted by Liang and Wang (2005), Becattini et al. (2008), and Huang (2009) may be harder to observe at high chemical potential, potentially explaining the null results from STAR and RHIC searches for vector meson spin alignment.
