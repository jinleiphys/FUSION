# Mass Renormalization for Time Dependent Correlation Functions in Shibata-Hashitsume's Projection Operator Method

> We study the time development of correlation functions at both zero and finite temperature with Shibata-Hashitsume's projection operator method and carry out the renormalization of ultraviolet divergence that appears in a time-dependent frequency shift, using a mass counter term. A harmless divergence of log t-type remains at an initial time t=0 after the lowest order renormalization.

## Key claim

The ultraviolet divergence appearing in the time-dependent frequency shift of correlation functions obtained via Shibata-Hashitsume's projection operator method can be removed by mass renormalization, but a harmless logarithmic divergence of the form log t remains at the initial time t=0 after lowest-order renormalization.

## Method

The authors apply Shibata-Hashitsume's projection operator method to a sigma-2pi model, treating the sigma meson as the system and the pi meson field as the environment. They derive perturbative equations of motion for the sigma meson creation and annihilation operators up to second order in the coupling constant g, then renormalize the ultraviolet divergence in the imaginary part of the 2pi-2pi correlation function A(k,t) by tuning a mass counter term at t0 = infinity, requiring the frequency shift to vanish at zero momentum, zero temperature, and infinite time.

## Key numbers

- Sigma meson mass m_s = 550 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 140 MeV, coupling constant g = 1650 MeV, sigma momentum k = 30 MeV used in numerical calculations at T = 0
- Momentum cutoffs Lambda = 2 GeV, 4 GeV, and 8 GeV used to demonstrate cutoff dependence of Im A_ren(k,t)
- Temperatures T = 0 MeV, 100 MeV, and 200 MeV used to show temperature dependence of Im A_ren(k,t) and Re <c^dagger_k(t)>
- Initial condition for sigma meson creation operator: <c^dagger_k(0)> = 15 MeV^{-3/2}

## Context

This work extends Shibata-Hashitsume's projection operator method (Hashitsume, Shibata, and Shingu 1977; Shibata and Hashitsume 1978; Shibata, Hamano, and Hashitsume 1981) to handle ultraviolet divergences in time-dependent correlation functions, a problem not present in standard S-matrix theory where the time interval is [-infinity, infinity]. The approach contrasts with that of Anastopoulos (1997), who studied a similar problem using Nakajima-Zwanzig's projection operator method with a different projection operator and renormalization procedure. The results enable calculation of time-dependent correlation functions in non-equilibrium quantum field theory as an initial value problem, with the remaining log t divergence at t=0 being harmless for physical quantities.
