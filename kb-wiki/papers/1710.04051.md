# Role of isospin and its conservation in neutron-rich fission fragments

> Following upon our earlier paper [1] containing some initial results, we present detailed discussion and complete results in this paper, which provide the first direct evidence for the validity of isospin as a nearly good quantum number in neutron-rich systems. The evidence comes from the reproduction of the general features of the partition-wise relative yields of neutron-rich fission fragments produced in two heavy-ion induced fusion fission reactions, namely $^{208}$Pb ($^{18}$O, f) and $^{238}$U ($^{18}$O, f), by using the concept of isospin conservation. To fix the isospin values and use the isospin algebra, we invoke what we term as Kelson's conjectures. We present a consistent scheme for isospin assignments based on these considerations. Our calculated results confirm that isospin behaves as an approximately good quantum number in neutron-rich systems, in this case, the fission fragments.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first direct evidence that isospin is a nearly good quantum number in neutron-rich fission fragments, based on the reproduction of partition-wise relative yields from two heavy-ion induced fusion-fission reactions. The authors stress that their work does not represent a model for calculating fission fragment distributions, but rather uses the distribution data to support the goodness of isospin, and that these results may be valid only for compound nuclear fission data.

## Method

The authors assigned total isospin values to fission fragments using Kelson's conjectures, which state that neutron emission during fission enhances the formation of higher excited states with T > T3 and that observed fission fragments are preferably formed in Isobaric Analog States (IAS). For each mass number, three isobars were considered as an isobaric triplet, and the maximum T3 value among them was assigned as the total isospin T. The relative yields of fission fragments were then calculated from the squared Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for the isospin coupling of the two fragments, weighted by experimental neutron multiplicity data from Bogachev et al. (2007) for the 208Pb(18O, f) reaction, and using only the dominant neutron emission channels for the 238U(18O, f) reaction.

## Key numbers

* For the 208Pb(18O, f) reaction, the compound nucleus 226Th has a uniquely fixed total isospin TCN = 23, derived from T3 values of the target (208Pb, T3 = 22) and projectile (18O, T3 = 1).
* For the 238U(18O, f) reaction, the compound nucleus 256Fm has TCN = T3CN = 28.
* In the 208Pb(18O, f) reaction, the TRCN values for different neutron emission channels range from 20 to 25, with the dominant 4n and 6n channels giving TRCN = 22, 23, 24, 25.
* For the Ru-Pd partition in the 12n emission channel of 208Pb(18O, f), the weight factor w12 = 0.0291, relative to the 6n channel normalized to unity.
* The experimental data for 208Pb(18O, f) from Bogachev et al. (2007) includes 65 fragments in six partitions, with neutron multiplicity data extending from zero to 12-14 neutron emission.
* The experimental data for 238U(18O, f) from Danu et al. (2007) includes 65 fragments in seven partitions, with the dominant neutron emission channels being 12n for Sn-Sn, 8n for Ru-Ba, and 10n for the remaining five partitions.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Lane and Soper (1962), who showed that isospin mixing decreases by a factor of 2/(N-Z+2) in neutron-rich nuclei, and builds on the conjectures of Kelson (1969) regarding the role of isospin in fission. The calculations use experimental fission fragment yield data from Bogachev et al. (2007), Banerjee et al. (2007), and Danu et al. (2007), which are the first measurements where both Z and A are precisely identified for even-even fission fragments. The results enable future studies of isospin conservation in neutron-rich systems and suggest that isospin can be used as a tool to understand fission fragment distributions, though the authors note that no shell effects or corrections for isomeric transitions have been included.
