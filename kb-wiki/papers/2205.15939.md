# Effective operators for valence space calculations from the {\itshape ab initio} No-Core Shell Mode

> In recent years, remarkable progress has been achieved in developing novel non-perturbative techniques for constructing valence space shell model Hamiltonians from realistic internucleon interactions. One of these methods is based on the Okubo--Lee--Suzuki (OLS) unitary transformation applied to no-core shell model (NCSM) solutions. In the present work, we implement the corresponding approach to solve for valence space effective electromagnetic operators. To this end, we use the NCSM results for $A=16-18$, obtained at $N_{\rm max}=4$, to derive a charge-dependent version of the effective interaction for the $sd$ shell, which allows us to exactly reproduce selected NCSM spectra of $^{18}$O, $^{18}$F and $^{18}$Ne within the two valence nucleon space. We then deduce effective single-particle matrix elements of electric quadrupole ($E2$) and magnetic dipole ($M1$) operators by matching them to the electromagnetic transitions and moments for $^{17}$O and $^{17}$F from the NCSM at $N_{\rm max}=4$. Thus, effective $E2$ and $M1$ operators are obtained as sets of single-particle matrix elements for the valence space ($sd$ shell) which allow us to reproduce the NCSM results for $A=17$ exactly. Systematic comparison of a large set of $sd$ shell results on quadrupole and magnetic dipole moments and transitions for $A=18$ using effective $E2$ and $M1$ operators that we derive from the full NCSM calculations demonstrates a remarkable agreement.

## Key claim

The authors derive effective one-body electric quadrupole (E2) and magnetic dipole (M1) operators for the sd-shell valence space by matching to no-core shell model (NCSM) results at Nmax=4 for A=17, rather than using a full two-body effective operator derived from the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki (OLS) transformation, and they test these approximate operators against NCSM results for A=18.

## Method

The authors performed NCSM calculations at Nmax=4 with the Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon potential (including two-body Coulomb interaction between protons) for A=16-18 nuclei, using a harmonic-oscillator basis with ℏΩ=14 MeV. They applied the OLS unitary transformation to NCSM solutions to derive a charge-dependent effective interaction for the sd-shell valence space, obtaining separate neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and proton-neutron two-body matrix elements from 18O, 18Ne, and 18F. Effective single-particle matrix elements for E2 and M1 operators were then obtained by matching to electromagnetic transitions and moments from NCSM calculations for 17O and 17F, yielding state-dependent effective charges and g-factors.

## Key numbers

- NCSM calculations performed at Nmax=4 with ℏΩ=14 MeV for A=16-18 nuclei
- 14 eigenpairs selected from 18O, 14 from 18Ne, and 28 from 18F for constructing the effective interaction
- Neutron effective charges range from 0.168e to 0.281e, with average value 0.196e
- Proton effective charges range from 1.060e to 1.297e, with average value 1.202e
- Average effective g-factors: g^s_n = -3.695, g^l_n = 0.026, g^s_p = 5.388, g^l_p = 0.950
- Root-mean-square difference for reduced E2 matrix elements using state-dependent charges: 0.072 e·fm² (18O), 0.114 e·fm² (18F), 0.221 e·fm² (18Ne)
- Root-mean-square difference for reduced M1 matrix elements using state-dependent g-factors: 0.070 μ_N (18O), 0.050 μ_N (18F), 0.108 μ_N (18Ne)
- B(E2; 2^+_1 → 0^+_1) in 18O: NCSM = 0.527 e²·fm⁴, sd-shell with state-dependent charges = 0.535 e²·fm⁴
- B(E2; 2^+_1 → 0^+_1) in 18Ne: NCSM = 21.94 e²·fm⁴, sd-shell with state-dependent charges = 21.76 e²·fm⁴

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative OLS-based method previously applied to effective Hamiltonians by Smirnova and Barrett (2019) and Dikmen et al. (2015) to the construction of effective electromagnetic operators for valence-space calculations. The approach provides an alternative to traditional many-body perturbation theory methods (e.g., Kuo 1973, Brown 1977) and empirical fitting procedures (e.g., USD and USDB interactions by Brown and Richter). The derived effective operators enable valence-space shell model calculations that reproduce NCSM results for A=18 nuclei, and the weak state-dependence of the effective charges supports the conventional use of state-independent effective charges in sd-shell calculations.
