# Open charm and dileptons from relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the dynamics of open charm production and the dilepton radiation of the semi-leptonic decays of correlated $D\bar D$ pairs versus the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) radiation and hadronic sources in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Our study is based on the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach employing a non-perturbative QCD description of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) in terms of dynamical quasiparticles and the EoS based on lattice QCD. We compare the PHSD results for charm observables with the calculations from BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach to Multi-Parton Scatterings) which is based on perturbative QCD with massless partons and interaction cross sections calculated in leading order of the QCD coupling. We compare the $p_T$ dependence of the ratio $R_{AA}$ of $D$-mesons in $A+A$ over $p+p$ collisions scaled by the number of binary collisions $N_{bin}$ as well as the elliptic flow $v_2$ of $D$-mesons calculated within the PHSD and BAMPS at LHC energies. In other study, based on the PHSD calculations we find that the dileptons from correlated $D-$meson semi-leptonic decays dominate the 'thermal' radiation from the QGP in central Pb+Pb collisions at the intermediate masses ($1.2 < M < 3$ GeV) for higher invariant energies However, for invariant energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 40$ GeV the QGP radiation overshines the contribution from $D,{\bar D}$ decays such that one should observe a rather clear signal from the partonic dilepton radiation. This finding provides promising perspectives to measure the QGP radiation in the dilepton experiments at RHIC BES and the future FAIR/NICA facilities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for central Pb+Pb collisions at intermediate dilepton masses (1.2 < M < 3 GeV), the contribution from correlated D-meson semi-leptonic decays dominates over the quark-gluon plasma thermal radiation only for higher collision energies; for invariant energies below 40 GeV, the QGP radiation instead overshines the D-meson decay contribution, providing a cleaner signal of partonic dilepton radiation.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which uses a non-perturbative QCD description of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) with dynamical quasiparticles and a lattice-QCD-based equation of state, to simulate open charm production and dilepton radiation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. They compared PHSD results for D-meson observables with calculations from the BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach to Multi-Parton Scatterings) approach, which uses perturbative QCD with massless partons and leading-order interaction cross sections. The study examined the pT dependence of the nuclear modification factor RAA and elliptic flow v2 of D-mesons at LHC energies, and separately calculated the excitation function of dielectron production from partonic interactions versus D-meson decays across collision energies from 8 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- D-meson RAA and v2 were compared with ALICE data at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV (RAA) and 5.02 TeV (v2) for central Pb+Pb collisions
- BAMPS required a scaling factor K = 3.5 for elastic interactions to describe RAA data for pT < 30 GeV
- The contribution from D-meson decays overshines partonic interactions around sqrt(s_NN) ≈ 40 GeV in the intermediate mass range (1.2 < M < 3 GeV)
- For collision energies sqrt(s_NN) < 40 GeV, the QGP radiation dominates over D-meson decay contributions in the intermediate mass window

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charm dynamics in heavy-ion collisions by comparing two fundamentally different transport approaches (PHSD with non-perturbative QGP and BAMPS with perturbative QCD), building on earlier work by Song (2015) and Uphoff (2012) on charm energy loss. The finding that the QGP dilepton signal is cleanest at collision energies below 40 GeV provides guidance for experimental programs at RHIC Beam Energy Scan and future FAIR/NICA facilities, where the background from heavy-flavor decays is suppressed.
