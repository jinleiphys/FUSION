# $β$-decay properties for neutron-rich Kr-Tc isotopes from deformed pn-QRPA calculations with realistic forces

> In this work we studied $β$-decay properties for deformed neutron-rich nuclei in the region Z=36-43. We use the deformed pn-QRPA methods with the realistic CD-Bonn forces, and include both the Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden types of decays in the calculation. The obtained $β$-decay half-lives and neutron-emission probabilities of deformed isotopes are compared with experiment as well as with previous calculations. The advantages and disadvantages of the method are discussed.

# β-decay properties for neutron-rich Kr-Tc isotopes from deformed pn-QRPA calculations with realistic forces

## Key claim
The deformed pn-QRPA method with realistic CD-Bonn forces and explicit inclusion of first-forbidden decays yields improved agreement with measured β-decay half-lives for even-even nuclei in the Kr-Tc region (Z=36-43), achieving a total error factor of 1.32 for nine even-even isotopes compared to 2.16 in the earlier separable-force approach of Möller et al. (2003), but the method systematically overestimates half-lives for odd-odd nuclei due to neglected collectivity in the daughter even-even final states.

## Method
The authors performed deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) calculations using the realistic CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon force as the residual interaction, with single-particle wave functions from a deformed Woods-Saxon potential and BCS pairing treated via the Brückner G-matrix. Both Gamow-Teller (GT) and first-forbidden (FF) decay channels were included microscopically, with two adjustable parameters: the renormalized particle-hole strength g_ph (fixed from ⁷⁶Ge double-beta decay) and particle-particle strength g_pp (fitted to 0.75 using a quenched axial-vector coupling g_A = 0.95). For odd-mass and odd-odd parent nuclei, the method used single-quasiparticle and spectator approximations without particle-vibration coupling.

## Key numbers
- Total error factor Σ_r¹⁰ = 2.1 for all Kr-Tc nuclei (Z=36-43) compared to 2.3 in Möller et al. (2003)
- For even-even isotopes (9 nuclei with small error bars): total error factor 1.32 versus 2.16 in Möller et al. (2003)
- Quenched axial-vector coupling constant used: g_A = 0.75 × g_A0 = 0.95, where g_A0 = 1.26
- Fitted particle-particle strength: g_pp = 0.75, with uncertainty range 0.6-0.9 giving half-life variations within a factor of two
- Example result: ¹¹⁰Zr calculated half-life 37.9 ms with β-delayed one-neutron emission probability P_n = 10.5%
- First-forbidden branching ratios in this region vary from several percent to at most 30%
- For ¹¹⁴Mo, a larger model space was required due to neutron number 72

## Context
This work extends the deformed pn-QRPA approach of Möller et al. (2003), which used phenomenological separable forces restricted to GT decays and estimated FF contributions by gross theory, by introducing realistic CD-Bonn forces that allow microscopic treatment of all decay channels. The calculations are tested against recent half-life measurements from RIKEN (2011) for neutron-rich Kr-Tc isotopes. The method enables improved predictions for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations, particularly for even-even nuclei that strongly affect abundance peak formation, and the authors plan to extend the calculations to rare-earth deformed nuclei and spherical nuclei near N=82.
