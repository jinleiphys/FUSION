# Comment on "Spin-trap isomers in deformed, odd-odd nuclei in the light rare-earth region near $N=98$"

> A new isomeric $(4^-)$ state at 285.5(32) keV in $^{162}$Tb was reported by R. Orford et al. [Phys. Rev. C 102, 011303(R) (2020)] based on a Penning-trap mass measurement. Here we show that this result is not compatible with existing experimental data. The state identified as $^{162}$Tb$^{m}$ with a mass-excess value of $-65593.9(25)$ keV is actually the $1^-$ ground state. The state identified as the ground state of $^{162}$Tb is most likely a molecular contaminant with the same mass-over-charge ratio.

## Key claim

The isomeric (4⁻) state at 285.5(32) keV in ¹⁶²Tb reported by Orford et al. (2020) is incompatible with existing experimental data; the state identified as the isomer is actually the 1⁻ ground state, while the state identified as the ground state is most likely a molecular contaminant (¹H³²S¹⁶O₃⁺) with the same mass-over-charge ratio.

## Method

The authors compared the mass-excess value reported for the claimed isomer in Orford et al. (2020) (−65593.9(25) keV) with the mass-excess derived from the ¹⁶³Dy(t,α)¹⁶²Tb(1⁻) reaction Q value of 8073(3) keV (Burke 2007) combined with the known mass of stable ¹⁶³Dy. They also examined the frequency ratio reported for the claimed ground state and identified a match with the mass-excess of a singly-charged ¹H³²S¹⁶O₃ molecule, and evaluated the consistency of the reassigned masses with β-decay data and two-neutron separation energy systematics.

## Key numbers

* The mass-excess of the state reported as the (4⁻) isomer in Orford et al. (2020) is −65593.9(25) keV, which agrees within uncertainty with the mass-excess of −65596.9(31) keV derived for the 1⁻ ground state from the ¹⁶³Dy(t,α) reaction Q value of 8073(3) keV (Burke 2007) and the ¹⁶³Dy mass of −66380.9(7) keV.
* The mass-excess reported for the claimed ¹⁶²Tb ground state (−32939.7(10) keV for a singly-charged A=81 ion) is within 2.1(10) keV of the mass-excess of a singly-charged ¹H³²S¹⁶O₃ molecule (−32937.5724(16) keV).
* Reassigning the isomer as the ground state increases the β-decay Q value of ¹⁶²Tb from 2301.8(21) keV to 2587.3(26) keV, resolving the energetic impossibility of β-decay feeding to the 2371-keV state in ¹⁶²Dy.

## Context

This comment contradicts the interpretation of Orford et al. (2020), who claimed discovery of a spin-trap isomer in ¹⁶²Tb based on Penning-trap mass measurements. The work extends earlier β-decay studies (Funke 1966, Schima 1966, Gujrathi 1967, Kaffrell 1968, Chang 1970, Kawade 1977, Gehrke 1982) and transfer-reaction studies (Burke 2007) that firmly established the 1⁻ ground state and 2⁻ first excited state at 39 keV. The reassignment resolves inconsistencies in the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2020 (AME20) and flattens the two-neutron separation energy curve for Tb isotopes near N=98, which previously showed unusual fluctuations not seen in neighboring isotopic chains.
