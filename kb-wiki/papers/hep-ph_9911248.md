# What will the first year of SNO show?

> The ratio of the measured to the predicted standard model CC event rates in SNO will be 0.47 if no oscillations occur. The best-fit active oscillation predictions for the CC ratio are: 0.35-39 (MSW) and 0.38-42 (vacuum) (all for a 5 MeV energy threshold), typically about 20% less than the no-oscillation expectation. We calculate the predicted ratios for six active and sterile neutrino oscillation solutions allowed at 99% CL and determine the dependence of the ratios on energy threshold. If the high-energy anomaly observed by SuperKamiokande is due to an enhanced hep flux, MSW active solutions predict that out of a total of 5000 CC events above 5 MeV in SNO between 49 and 54 events will be observed above 13 MeV whereas only 19 events are expected for no-oscillations and a nominal standard hep flux.

## Key claim

The ratio of the measured to predicted standard model charged-current (CC) event rate in SNO will be 0.47 if no oscillations occur, but the best-fit active oscillation predictions for the CC ratio are 0.35-0.39 (MSW) and 0.38-0.42 (vacuum) for a 5 MeV energy threshold, typically about 20% less than the no-oscillation expectation; these predictions depend on which of the six currently-favored neutrino oscillation solutions (allowed at 99% CL) is correct.

## Method

The authors calculated the predicted CC event rate ratio R_CC,SNO for six neutrino oscillation scenarios (LMA, SMA, LOW, VAC_S, VAC_L, and sterile) using global fits to data from the Homestake, SAGE, GALLEX, and SuperKamiokande experiments, including the SuperKamiokande electron recoil energy spectrum and day-night effect. They determined the best-fit survival probabilities P(E) from the oscillation parameters and the best-fit ratio f_B of observed to standard model 8B flux by minimizing chi-squared fits to the SuperKamiokande recoil energy spectrum at each globally-allowed set of oscillation parameters. The CC absorption cross sections were evaluated using the Bahcall-Lisi subroutines, and the authors used preliminary SNO collaboration estimates for energy resolution, absolute energy scale, and detection efficiencies.

## Key numbers

- No-oscillation prediction: R_CC,SNO = 0.475 (equal to R_SK = 0.475 +/- 0.015 from SuperKamiokande after 825 days with 6.5 MeV threshold)
- LMA best-fit: R_CC,SNO = 0.35 (+0.01/+0.05, -0.05/-0.06) at 5 MeV threshold (90%/99% CL)
- SMA best-fit: R_CC,SNO = 0.39 (+0.03/+0.07, -0.04/-0.07) at 5 MeV threshold (90%/99% CL)
- LOW best-fit: R_CC,SNO = 0.38 (+0.02/+0.02, -0.03/-0.03) at 5 MeV threshold (90%/99% CL)
- VAC_S best-fit: R_CC,SNO = 0.38 (+0.04/+0.08, -0.06/-0.07) at 5 MeV threshold (90%/99% CL)
- VAC_L best-fit: R_CC,SNO = 0.42 (+0.02/+0.03, -0.01/-0.04) at 5 MeV threshold (90%/99% CL)
- Sterile MSW best-fit: R_CC,SNO = 0.48 (+0.005/+0.01, -0.005/-0.01) at 5 MeV threshold (90%/99% CL)
- For an enhanced hep flux scenario, MSW active solutions predict 49-54 CC events above 13 MeV out of 5000 total CC events above 5 MeV, compared to 19 events for no-oscillations with nominal standard hep flux
- Best-fit global oscillation parameters: LMA (Delta m^2 = 2.7e-5 eV^2, sin^2(2theta) = 7.9e-1, 68% CL); SMA (Delta m^2 = 5.0e-6 eV^2, sin^2(2theta) = 7.2e-3, 64% CL); LOW (Delta m^2 = 1.0e-7 eV^2, sin^2(2theta) = 9.1e-1, 83% CL)

## Context

This paper extends the global solar neutrino oscillation analyses of Bahcall, Krastev, and Smirnov (1998) by providing specific predictions for the first year of SNO CC rate measurements, building on earlier discussions of comparing SNO and SuperKamiokande results (SNO collaboration, 1997; SuperKamiokande collaboration, 1998). The work enables SNO to discriminate between active and sterile neutrino oscillation solutions and to test the high-energy anomaly observed by SuperKamiokande, with the key result that a measurement of R_CC,SNO differing from R_SK would provide a "smoking gun" indication of new physics beyond the standard electroweak model.
