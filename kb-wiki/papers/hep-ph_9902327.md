# Hard-thermal-loop Resummation of the Free Energy of a Hot Gluon Plasma

> We calculate the free energy of a hot gluon plasma to leading order in hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory. Effects associated with screening, gluon quasiparticles, and Landau damping are resummed to all orders. The ultraviolet divergences generated by the hard-thermal-loop propagator corrections can be cancelled by a temperature-independent counterterm. The deviation of the hard-thermal-loop free energy from lattice QCD results for T > 2 T_c has the correct sign and roughly the correct magnitude to be accounted for by next-to-leading order corrections.

## Key claim

The leading-order hard-thermal-loop (HTL) free energy of a hot gluon plasma lies significantly below lattice QCD results for T > 2 T_c, but the deviation has the correct sign and roughly the correct magnitude to be accounted for by next-to-leading order corrections, rather than being a complete leading-order match.

## Method

The authors calculate the free energy of a pure gluon plasma to leading order in HTL perturbation theory by resumming the one-loop gluon diagram with HTL-corrected propagators in the imaginary-time formalism. They separate the resulting sum-integrals into quasiparticle contributions (transverse gluons and longitudinal plasmons) and Landau-damping contributions by collapsing contour integrals around branch cuts, then isolate and cancel ultraviolet divergences using dimensional regularization with a temperature-independent counterterm.

## Key numbers

- The ideal gas free energy of massless gluons is F_ideal = -(8π²/45) T⁴.
- The counterterm required to cancel logarithmic divergences is ΔF = -9 m_g⁴/(8π²(d-3)) in dimensional regularization.
- In the high-temperature limit (m_g ≪ T), the HTL free energy expands as F_HTL = F_ideal [1 - (45/4)a + 30 a^(3/2) + (45/8)(2 log(μ₃/(2πT)) - 1.232)a² + O(a³)], where a = 3 m_g²/(4π²T²).
- In the low-temperature limit (T → 0 with m_g fixed), F_HTL → (9/(8π²))(log(m_g/μ₃) - 0.333) m_g⁴.
- The weak-coupling expansion of the QCD free energy through order α_s^(5/2) is F_QCD = F_ideal [1 - (15/4)a + 30 a^(3/2) + (135/2)(log a + 3.51)a² - 799.2 a^(5/2) + O(a³ log a)], where a = α_s(2πT)/π.
- The critical temperature T_c is related to the Λ parameter by T_c = 1.03 Λ_MSbar.

## Context

This work extends screened perturbation theory (Karsch, Patkós, and Petreczky, 1997) to gauge theories by using hard-thermal-loop (HTL) effective propagators (Braaten and Pisarski, 1990) to incorporate plasma effects in a gauge-invariant way. The leading-order HTL free energy lies below lattice QCD results from Boyd et al. (1996) for T > 2 T_c, and the next-to-leading order correction is expected to reduce this discrepancy. The approach enables systematic perturbative calculations of real-time processes that may serve as signatures for the quark-gluon plasma, which cannot be treated by effective-field-theory methods that reduce to three-dimensional lattice gauge theory (Kajantie et al., 1997).
