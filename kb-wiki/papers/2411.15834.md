# Three-nucleon force effects in polarization transfers from the doubly spin-polarized initial neutron-deuteron state to the outgoing neutron in neutron-deuteron scattering

> We discuss new spin observables presently accessible to measurement in the proton-deuteron (pd) system, namely polarization transfer coefficients from doubly spin-polarized initial state to the outgoing nucleon in the elastic nucleon-deuteron (Nd) scattering and in the nucleon-induced deuteron breakup reactions. The sensitivity of these observables to three-nucleon force (3NF) effects is investigated and compared to sensitivities of the constituent standard single polarization transfer coefficients in the neutron-deuteron (nd) system. $K_{y,y}^{y'}$ in elastic nd scattering, for which large 3NF effects, up to 40\%, have been found at higher energies, seems the most promising observable to measure.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the double spin-polarization transfer coefficient \(K_{y,y}^{y'}\) in elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering shows large three-nucleon force effects (up to 40% at higher energies), making it the most promising observable for future measurement, though the authors note that experimental access is currently limited to specific polarization directions (incoming deuteron vector polarization in x, y, z; incoming proton polarization only in y; outgoing proton polarization only in x and y).

## Method

The authors solved rigorous three-nucleon Faddeev equations in momentum-space partial-wave basis for elastic proton-deuteron scattering and exclusive deuteron breakup reactions, using the chiral N\(^4\)LO\(^+\) semilocal momentum-space regularized (SMS) nucleon-nucleon potential (regulator \(\Lambda = 450\) MeV) alone and combined with the chiral N\(^2\)LO three-nucleon force. The three-nucleon force parameters \(c_D = 2.0\) and \(c_E = 0.2866\) were fixed by reproducing the \(^3\)H binding energy and fitting elastic pd cross section data at \(E = 65\) MeV. The proton-proton Coulomb force was omitted, which the authors note provides large effects only at low energies and forward center-of-mass angles.

## Key numbers

- At \(E = 135\) MeV, the double spin-polarization transfer coefficient \(K_{y,y}^{y'}\) shows three-nucleon force effects of about 40%, localized at center-of-mass angles around \(\Theta_{c.m.} = 110^\circ\), with magnitude of order \(-0.3\)
- At \(E = 135\) MeV, the constituent single polarization transfer coefficients \(K_{y}^{y'}\) show three-nucleon force effects of about 10%, localized around \(\Theta_{c.m.} = 90^\circ\)
- At \(E = 70\) MeV, all three polarization transfer coefficients (\(K_{y,y}^{y'}\), \(K_{y}^{y'}\) from proton, \(K_{y}^{y'}\) from deuteron) show three-nucleon force effects of approximately 5%
- At \(E = 10\) MeV, three-nucleon force effects are practically negligible for all polarization transfer coefficients
- The double spin-polarization transfer coefficient \(K_{z,x}^{y'}\) shows three-nucleon force effects of about 8% at \(E = 10\) MeV around \(\Theta_{c.m.} = 120^\circ\), growing to about 50% at \(E = 135\) MeV with magnitude of about 0.2
- The three-nucleon force parameters were determined as \(c_D = 2.10 \pm 0.24\) from fitting pd elastic scattering cross sections at \(E = 65\) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of three-nucleon force effects in nucleon-deuteron scattering by proposing new double spin-polarization transfer observables that are now experimentally accessible, building on the formalism of Ohlsen (1972) and Simonius (1973). The calculations use the chiral SMS potential of Reinert, Krebs, and Epelbaum (2018) combined with the N\(^2\)LO three-nucleon force of Maris et al. (2021), and the results are motivated by planned RIKEN measurements of spin-correlation coefficients at 135 MeV (Kimsek et al.). The paper enables future experimental tests of three-nucleon force effects in both elastic scattering and deuteron breakup reactions, particularly in final-state-interaction configurations where large effects are predicted.
