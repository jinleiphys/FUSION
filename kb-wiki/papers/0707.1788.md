# Searching for the Critical Point of QCD: Theoretical Benchmark Calculations

> We present a comprehensive study of event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in nucleon-nucleon and nucleus-nucleus interactions from AGS/FAIR to RHIC energies within the UrQMD transport approach. The scaled variances of negative, positive, and all charged hadrons are analysed. The scaled variance in central Pb+Pb collisions increases with energy and behaves similar to inelastic p+p interactions. We find a non-trivial dependence of multiplicity fluctuations on the rapidity and transverse momentum interval used for the analysis and on the centrality selection procedure. Quantitative predictions for the NA49 experiment are given, taking into account the acceptance of the detector and the selection procedure of central events.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model predicts that multiplicity fluctuations (scaled variance) in central Pb+Pb collisions increase strongly with beam energy and behave similarly to inelastic p+p interactions, providing a baseline for searches for the QCD critical point; however, the authors caution that the scaled variance shows a non-trivial dependence on the rapidity and transverse momentum interval used for analysis and on the centrality selection procedure, meaning that simple scaling formulas for acceptance corrections are not generally valid.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD v1.3 hadron-string transport model to simulate event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in nucleon-nucleon (p+p, p+n) and central Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies from 6.87A GeV (AGS) through 20A-158A GeV (SPS) up to sqrt(s_NN)=62.4 and 200 GeV (RHIC). They computed the scaled variance omega = Var(n)/<n> for positively, negatively, and all charged hadrons in full 4pi phase space and in various rapidity and transverse momentum intervals. For comparison with the NA49 experiment, they applied the actual detector acceptance tables and the veto calorimeter centrality selection (1% most central events) to the UrQMD output.

## Key numbers

* The scaled variance for all charged hadrons in central Pb+Pb collisions in full 4pi phase space increases from below 1 at low SPS energies to above 1 at higher energies, crossing the Poissonian value (omega=1) in the SPS energy regime.
* For p+p interactions, the mean multiplicity of all charged hadrons is parameterized as <n^pm> approx -4.2 + 4.69 * (sqrt(s_NN)/GeV)^0.31, and the scaled variance as omega(h^pm) approx 0.35 * (<n^pm>-1)^2 / <n^pm>.
* At the highest SPS energy (158A GeV), the scaled variance in central Pb+Pb collisions is much higher near midrapidity than in forward and backward rapidities, even when rapidity bins are constructed to have equal mean multiplicity.
* The veto trigger used by NA49 causes a systematic deviation in the scaled variance at midrapidity on the order of 10% compared to zero impact parameter simulations.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of event-by-event fluctuations within the UrQMD model (Bleicher 2000, Jeon 2005, Konchakovski 2007) and provides a baseline for the NA49 experiment's search for the QCD critical point and the onset of deconfinement. The predictions contrast strongly with hadron gas model calculations (Begun 2006) in the grand-canonical, canonical, and micro-canonical ensembles, which predict a constant scaled variance at high energies rather than the increase seen in UrQMD. The results enable experimental searches at NA49, NA61 (SHINE), critRHIC, CBM at FAIR, and MPD at JINR to distinguish between hadron gas and string-hadronic models and to identify non-monotonous deviations that could signal droplet formation or the critical point.
