# The anomalous transport of axial charge: topological vs non-topological fluctuations

> Axial charge imbalance is an essential ingredient in novel effects associated with chiral anomaly such as chiral magnetic effects (CME). In a non-Abelian plasma with chiral fermions, local axial charge can be generated a) by topological fluctuations which would create domains with non-zero winding number b) by conventional non-topological thermal fluctuations. We provide a holographic evaluations of medium's response to dynamically generated axial charge density in hydrodynamic limit and examine if medium's response depends on the microscopic origins of axial charge imbalance. We show a local domain with non-zero winding number would induce a non-dissipative axial current due to chiral anomaly. We illustrate holographically that a local axial charge imbalance would be damped out with the damping rate related to Chern-Simon diffusive constant. By computing chiral magnetic current in the presence of dynamically generated axial charge density, we found that the ratio of CME current over the axial charge density is independent of the origin of axial charge imbalance in low frequency and momentum limit. Finally, a stochastic hydrodynamic equation of the axial charge is formulated by including both types of fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the medium's response to local axial charge imbalance, specifically the ratio of chiral magnetic current to axial charge density in the low-frequency, low-momentum limit, is independent of whether the axial charge was generated by topological fluctuations (domains with non-zero winding number) or by conventional non-topological thermal fluctuations, even though the axial current itself does depend on the origin.

## Method

The authors use the Sakai-Sugimoto model, a top-down holographic dual of large-Nc QCD with massless chiral quarks, to compute the medium's response to dynamically generated axial charge density in the hydrodynamic (long time, long wavelength) limit. They model topological fluctuations via a boundary source θ(t,x) (the holonomy of the Ramond-Ramond C1 field) and non-topological thermal fluctuations via a boundary axial gauge field aμ(t,x), then solve the coupled equations of motion for the bulk fields Aμ and C7 to extract one-point functions nA, jA, and q.

## Key numbers

* The damping time of axial charge density is given by τ_sph = χT / (2Γ_CS), where χ is the static susceptibility, T is temperature, and Γ_CS is the Chern-Simons diffusive constant (derived analytically from the holographic model).
* The retarded Green's function for topological charge density in the hydrodynamic regime is G_R^qq(ω,k) = (1/2)[-i (Γ_CS/T) ω - κ_CS k^2], where κ_CS is the Chern-Simons diffusive constant and Γ_CS is related to the damping rate.
* The relation between axial charge density and topological charge density in a local topological domain is n_A = (Γ_CS/T) θ(t,x) and j_A^new = κ_CS ∇θ, where θ is the effective field representing the topological domain.
* The dynamical axial susceptibility χ_dyn, defined as the ratio of axial chemical potential to axial charge density in the low-frequency, small-momentum limit, equals the static susceptibility χ and is independent of the origin of axial charge imbalance.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral magnetic effects (CME) that treated axial chemical potential as a static, homogeneous parameter (e.g., Kharzeev 2004, Fukushima 2008) by considering dynamically generated, space-time dependent axial charge density. It provides the first direct holographic verification of the relation τ_sph = χT/(2Γ_CS), previously derived from fluctuation-dissipation arguments by Rubakov (1996), and complements recent numerical work in a bottom-up holographic model by Jimenez-Alba (2015). The results enable the formulation of a stochastic hydrodynamic equation for axial charge that includes both topological and thermal noise sources, which can be applied to simulate anomalous transport in heavy-ion collisions.
