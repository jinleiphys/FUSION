# Apparent and Actual Shifts in Mass and Width of Phi Mesons Produced in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We present a method of analyzing invariant-mass spectra of kaon pairs resulting from decay of $ϕ$ mesons produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. It can be used to extract the shifts in the mass and the width ($ΔM$ and $ΔΓ$) of the $ϕ$ mesons when they are inside the dense matter formed in these collisions. We illustrate our method with the help of available preliminary data. Extracted values of $ΔM$ and $ΔΓ$ are significantly larger than those obtained with an earlier method. Our results are consistent with the experimentally observed $p_T$ dependence of the mass shift. Finally, we present a phenomenological relation between $ΔM$ and $ΔΓ$. It provides a useful constraint on theories which predict the values of these two quantities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard method of fitting a single Breit-Wigner resonance to the background-subtracted invariant-mass spectrum of kaon pairs from phi meson decay is incorrect because most phi mesons decay long after the dense medium has dispersed; a two-component fit (one unshifted and one shifted Breit-Wigner) yields shifts in mass and width that are "several times larger" than those obtained with the earlier single-BW method, though the authors caution that their numerical results are subject to change because the data used are preliminary.

## Method

The authors reanalyze the background-subtracted invariant-mass spectrum of K+K- pairs from the E-802 collaboration (Wang 1995) by fitting it with two relativistic Breit-Wigner terms convoluted with a Gaussian experimental mass resolution (sigma = 2.2 MeV): one unshifted term with free-space mass M0 and width Gamma0 weighted by (1-f), and one shifted term with in-medium mass M1 and width Gamma1 weighted by f, where f is the fraction of phi mesons decaying inside the dense medium. The fraction f is calculated from the mean lifetime, Lorentz factor, and a characteristic medium size d = 5 fm, using the relation f = 1 - exp(-Gamma1 d / v gamma), with input parameters MT = 1100 or 1200 MeV and y_cm = 0.3. A least-squares fit yields the shifts Delta M and Delta Gamma.

## Key numbers

*   For input parameters d = 5 fm, MT = 1100 MeV, y_cm = 0.3: Delta M = 6.0 +/- 1.9 MeV and Delta Gamma = 5.4 +/- 3.9 MeV, with f = 0.37.
*   For input parameters d = 5 fm, MT = 1200 MeV, y_cm = 0.3: Delta M = 6.2 +/- 2.4 MeV and Delta Gamma = 7.7 +/- 5.0 MeV, with f = 0.34.
*   The free-space phi meson mass is 1019.413 +/- 0.008 MeV and the free-space width is 4.43 +/- 0.06 MeV (Particle Data Group 1994).
*   The experimental mass resolution sigma is 2.2 MeV (Wang 1993).
*   For comparison, the earlier single-BW fit to the same data gave Delta M = 2.3 +/- 0.9 +/- 0.1 MeV and an apparent width increase of about 0.78 MeV (Wang 1995).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of phi meson production in heavy-ion collisions by the E-802 collaboration at AGS (BNL) for central Si + Au collisions at 14.6 A GeV/c, where Wang (1995) reported a mass shift of 2.3 MeV for the highest-multiplicity events and a 3.3 MeV shift for low-pT phi mesons. The authors argue that the earlier single-BW fitting method (Wang 1995) is inadequate because the phi meson's long lifetime (about 45 fm/c in its rest frame) means most decay after the dense medium has dissipated, so a two-component fit is more realistic. The method enables future experiments to extract more accurate in-medium modifications of phi meson properties and provides a phenomenological constraint relating Delta M and Delta Gamma that theories (such as those by Asakawa and Ko using QCD sum rules, or Shuryak and Thorsson, Haglin and Gale, Bhattacharyya et al., and Song on finite-temperature effects) must satisfy.
