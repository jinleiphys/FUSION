# GW170817 implications on the frequency and damping time of f-mode oscillations of neutron stars

> Within a minimum model for neutron stars consisting of nucleons, electrons and muons at $β$-equilibrium using about a dozen Equation of States (EOSs) from microscopic nuclear many-body theories and 40,000 EOSs randomly generated using an explicitly isospin-dependent parametric EOS model for high-density neutron-rich nucleonic matter within its currently known uncertainty range, we study correlations among the f-mode frequency, its damping time and the tidal deformability as well as the compactness of neutron stars. Except for quark stars, both the f-mode frequency and damping time of canonical neutron stars are found to scale with the tidal deformability independent of the EOSs used. Applying the constraint on the tidal deformability of canonical neutron stars $Λ_{1.4}=190^{+390}_{-120}$ extracted by the LIGO+VIRGO Collaborations from their improved analyses of the GW170817 event, the f-mode frequency and its damping time of canonical neutron stars are limited to 1.67 kHz - 2.18 kHz and 0.155 s - 0.255 s, respectively, providing a useful guidance for the ongoing search for gravitational waves from the f-mode oscillations of isolated neutron stars. Moreover, assuming either or both the f-mode frequency and its damping time will be measured precisely in future observations with advanced gravitational wave detectors, we discuss how information about the mass and/or radius as well as the still rather elusive nuclear symmetry energies at supra-saturation densities may be extracted.

## Key claim

The authors find that for canonical (1.4 solar mass) neutron stars composed of nucleons, electrons, and muons, both the f-mode frequency and damping time scale universally with tidal deformability independent of the equation of state, but this scaling does not hold for quark stars; applying the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint yields a specific range for f-mode frequency and damping time, though this range depends on the lower limit of tidal deformability being set by terrestrial nuclear experiment constraints rather than the full observational lower bound.

## Method

The authors generated 40,000 parametric equations of state for neutron-rich nucleonic matter by randomly varying high-density nuclear symmetry energy parameters within their known uncertainty ranges, then screened them against causality, mechanical stability, and the requirement of supporting at least a 2.01 solar mass neutron star, leaving over 23,000 viable equations of state. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation and the differential equations governing the complex frequency of the f-mode for each equation of state, and computed the tidal deformability using the second Love number. For comparison, they also used 11 equations of state from microscopic nuclear many-body theories and 2 equations of state from the MIT bag model for quark stars.

## Key numbers

- Tidal deformability of canonical neutron stars from GW170817: Λ₁.₄ = 190⁺³⁹⁰₋₁₂₀ (from LIGO+VIRGO improved analyses)
- f-mode frequency of canonical neutron stars constrained to 1.67 kHz - 2.18 kHz
- f-mode damping time of canonical neutron stars constrained to 0.155 s - 0.255 s
- Lowest tidal deformability allowed by parametric equations of state satisfying terrestrial constraints: Λ = 165
- Revised maximum tidal deformability from GW170817: Λ = 580
- For a hypothetical observed frequency of 1.80 ± 0.01 kHz from a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the damping time would be 0.218 s - 0.223 s and the stellar radius would be 12.23 km - 12.69 km
- For a hypothetical observed frequency of 1.640 ± 0.016 kHz from a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the inferred symmetry energy at twice saturation density is E_sym(2ρ₀) = 54.5 ± 6.5 MeV
- For a hypothetical observed frequency of 1.800 ± 0.018 kHz from a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the inferred symmetry energy at twice saturation density is E_sym(2ρ₀) = 43.0 ± 6.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the well-established universal relations between f-mode frequency and compactness found by Andersson and Kokkotas (1998) and Tsui and collaborators (2005), and the relation between f-mode frequency and damping time found by Blazquez-Salcedo and collaborators (2014). It connects these theoretical relations to the first observational constraint on tidal deformability from the GW170817 event analyzed by the LIGO and VIRGO Collaborations (2018). The results enable future gravitational wave searches for f-mode oscillations of isolated neutron stars to be guided by specific frequency and damping time ranges, and provide a framework for extracting neutron star masses, radii, and the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy from future precise measurements of f-mode gravitational waves.
