# Gamow-Teller and double-beta decays of heavy nuclei within an effective theory

> We study $β$ decays within an effective theory that treats nuclei as a spherical collective core with an even number of neutrons and protons that can couple to an additional neutron and/or proton. First we explore Gamow-Teller $β$ decays of parent odd-odd nuclei into low-lying ground-, one-, and two-phonon states of the daughter even-even system. The low-energy constants of the effective theory are adjusted to data on $β$ decays to ground states or Gamow-Teller strengths. The corresponding theoretical uncertainty is estimated based on the power counting of the effective theory. For a variety of medium-mass and heavy isotopes the theoretical matrix elements are in good agreement with experiment within the theoretical uncertainties. We then study the two-neutrino double-$β$ decay into ground and excited states. The results are remarkably consistent with experiment within theoretical uncertainties, without the necessity to adjust any low-energy constants.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective theory (ET) treating nuclei as a spherical collective core coupled to a few nucleons can describe Gamow-Teller beta decays and two-neutrino double-beta decays of medium-mass and heavy nuclei with quantified theoretical uncertainties, without requiring additional low-energy constants for the double-beta decay predictions beyond those fitted to single-beta decays.

## Method

The authors construct an effective theory where even-even nuclei are described by quadrupole phonon excitations of a spherical core, and odd-odd nuclei are described by coupling a neutron and a proton (or their holes) to this core. The Gamow-Teller transition operator is expanded in terms of the effective degrees of freedom, with low-energy constants fitted to experimental beta-decay data to ground states. Theoretical uncertainties are estimated from the power counting of the effective theory, assuming the breakdown scale is at three-phonon excitations. The same operator is then used without additional fitting to predict two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of matrix elements for decays to one-phonon states relative to ground states is predicted as C_{β1}/C_β ≈ 0.58(^{+42}_{-25})
- The ratio of matrix elements for decays to two-phonon states relative to ground states is predicted as √2 C_{β J_{2ph}1}/C_β ≈ 0.47(^{+35}_{-20})
- The theoretical uncertainty for log(ft) values is estimated as Δlog(ft) ≈ 0.29, based on Λ = 3ω
- The effective theory is tested for nuclei with mass numbers from A = 62 to A = 128
- The breakdown scale is set to Λ = 3ω, where ω is the energy of the first excited 2⁺ state of the even-even core

## Context

This work extends the effective theory framework previously developed for even-even and odd-mass nuclei by Coello Pérez et al. (2015, 2016) to describe beta decays of odd-odd nuclei and double-beta decays. The approach complements traditional many-body methods such as the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) and nuclear shell model calculations, which typically require fitting of nuclear interactions or quenching factors. The ability to provide quantified theoretical uncertainties makes this framework particularly relevant for interpreting neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments, though the present work focuses only on the two-neutrino mode.
