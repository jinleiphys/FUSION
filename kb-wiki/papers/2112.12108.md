# Ensembles of unified crust and core equations of state in a nuclear-multimessenger astrophysics environment

> We present an ensemble of unified neutron star crust and core equations of state, constructed using an extended Skyrme energy density functional through the crust and outer core, and appended by two piecewise polytropes at higher densities. The equations of state are parameterized by the first three coefficients in the density expansion of the symmetry energy $J,L$ and $K_{\rm sym}$, the moment of inertia of a 1.338 M$_{\odot}$ star $I_{1.338}$ and the maximum neutron star mass $M_{\rm max}$. We construct an ensemble with uniform priors on all five parameters, and then apply data filters to the ensemble to explore the effect of combining neutron skin data from PREX with astrophysical measurements of radii and tidal deformabilities from NICER and LIGO/VIRGO. Neutron skins are calculated directly using the EDFs. We demonstrate that both the nuclear data and astrophysical data play a role in constraining crust properties such as the mass, thickness and moment of inertia of the crust and the nuclear pasta layers therein, and that astrophysical data better constrains $K_{\rm sym}$ than PREX data.

## Key claim

The paper presents an ensemble of unified neutron star crust and core equations of state that uniquely combines three degrees of freedom in the symmetry energy (J, L, Ksym) with two polytropic parameters controlling high-density behavior, and demonstrates through simple data filtering (not full Bayesian inference) that nuclear data from PREX and astrophysical data from NICER and LIGO/VIRGO provide complementary constraints on crust properties, with astrophysical data better constraining Ksym than PREX data at current precision levels.

## Method

The authors generated a large ensemble of extended Skyrme energy density functionals (EDFs) parameterized by the symmetry energy coefficients J, L, and Ksym on a 20^3 grid, from which unified crust and outer core equations of state were calculated using a compressible liquid drop model. Two piecewise polytropes were appended at densities of 1.5n0 and 2.7n0, with polytropic parameters tuned to give uniform priors on the moment of inertia of a 1.338 solar mass star (I1.338) and the maximum neutron star mass (Mmax), resulting in approximately 60,000 EOSs characterized by five parameters. Data filters were applied to the ensemble by removing EOSs that did not satisfy 68% confidence limits from PREX neutron skin measurements, LIGO/VIRGO tidal deformability constraints, and NICER mass-radius measurements.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy parameters prior ranges: J from 25 to 42 MeV, L from 5 to 140 MeV, Ksym from -450 to 150 MeV
- PREX filter applied: 0.21 < rnp^208 < 0.35 fm (68% confidence limits)
- LIGO filter applied: 70 < tidal deformability < 580 (68% confidence limits)
- NICER+LV data gives J = 34.9^{+5.4}_{-5.4} MeV, L = 29^{+32}_{-16} MeV, Ksym = -113^{+111}_{-111} MeV
- PREX data alone gives J = 39.4^{+2.7}_{-3.6} MeV, L = 93^{+24}_{-24} MeV, Ksym = -39^{+148}_{-111} MeV
- PREX+NICER+LV combination gives J = 39.4^{+2.6}_{-4.5} MeV, L = 79^{+16}_{-16} MeV, Ksym = -150^{+74}_{-74} MeV
- Radius of 1.4 solar mass star from PREX data: 13.3^{+0.6}_{-1.2} km
- Radius of 1.4 solar mass star from NICER+LV data: 12.2^{+0.5}_{-0.7} km
- Moment of inertia of 1.338 solar mass star from NICER+LV data: 1.48^{+0.10}_{-0.14} x 10^45 g cm^2
- Crust thickness fraction from PREX data: 0.103^{+0.017}_{-0.018}
- Pasta thickness fraction from PREX+NICER+LV data: 0.018^{+0.006}_{-0.007}

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to systematically explore neutron star equation of state space using polytropic models (Read 2009, Ozel 2009, Steiner 2010) and speed-of-sound models (Tews 2018, Annala 2020, Essick 2021) by incorporating a unified crust-core treatment from an extended Skyrme EDF that allows direct calculation of nuclear observables. It addresses a fundamental inconsistency in previous approaches that used universal correlations between L and neutron skin thickness (Roca-Maza 2011) without enforcing the correlations between symmetry energy parameters present in the EDFs used to derive those correlations. The ensemble enables future Bayesian inferences combining nuclear and astrophysical datasets, and can serve as training sets for machine learning or Gaussian process generation of non-parametric EOSs, while also allowing consistent propagation of measurements to crust observables such as crustal oscillation modes, crust cooling timescales, and magnetic field evolution.
