# Novel tools and observables for jet physics in heavy-ion collisions

> Studies of fully-reconstructed jets in heavy-ion collisions aim at extracting thermodynamical and transport properties of hot and dense QCD matter. Recently, a plethora of new jet substructure observables have been theoretically and experimentally developed that provide novel precise insights on the modifications of the parton radiation pattern induced by a QCD medium. This report, summarizing the main lines of discussion at the 5th Heavy Ion Jet Workshop and CERN TH institute "Novel tools and observables for jet physics in heavy-ion collisions" in 2017, presents a first attempt at outlining a strategy for isolating and identifying the relevant physical processes that are responsible for the observed medium-induced jet modifications. These studies combine theory insights, based on the Lund parton splitting map, with sophisticated jet reconstruction techniques, including grooming and background subtraction algorithms.

## Key claim

## Method

The authors compared three grooming settings of the Soft Drop algorithm (SD1: z_cut=0.1, beta=0; SD2: z_cut=0.5, beta=1.5; SD3: z_cut=0.1, beta=-1.0) applied to anti-k_T R=0.4 jets with p_T > 130 GeV/c, using the jet quenching Monte Carlo event generators QPYTHIA and JEWEL (with and without recoil effects) against a PYTHIA8 vacuum baseline. They studied groomed momentum fraction z_g, angular separation Delta R_12, and groomed mass M_g/p_T, and also binned the nuclear modification factor R_AA and photon-jet momentum imbalance x_Jgamma by the angular separation Delta R_12, embedding jets into a thermal background resembling central PbPb collisions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

* The underlying event background was generated with average momentum density <rho> = 250 GeV and total multiplicity of approximately 7000 particles, corresponding to the most central events in the CMS detector.
* Jets were reconstructed using anti-k_T R=0.4 with p_T > 130 GeV/c.
* For SD3 (z_cut=0.1, beta=-1.0), QPYTHIA subjets were enhanced and JEWEL "Recoils off" subjets were suppressed, both by a factor of approximately 1.5-2 relative to vacuum.
* For anti-k_T reclustering with SD1, groomed jets lost on average approximately 20% of their energy.
* For C/A reclustering with SD1, approximately 5 branches were removed by grooming on average, reducing jet p_T by less than 10%.

## Context

This work extends the application of jet substructure techniques, previously developed for flavor-tagging and boosted particle identification in pp collisions (Ellis 2009, Altheimer 2013), to heavy-ion collisions for studying medium-induced modifications of the parton radiation pattern. It builds on recent experimental results from CMS on groomed jet substructure (Sirunyan 2017) and theoretical interpretations (Chien 2016, Mehtar-Tani 2016, Milhano 2017, Chang 2017), and enables more differential studies of jet quenching by dissecting jet samples according to angular separation of subjets, which can help discriminate between different energy-loss and medium-response mechanisms.
