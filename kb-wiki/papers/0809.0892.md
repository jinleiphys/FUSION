# Color Screening by Pions

> Lattice QCD is used to calculate the potential between a static quark and anti-quark in the presence of a finite density of pi^+'s. Correlation functions of multiple pi^+'s are used in conjunction with Wilson-loop correlators to determine the difference between the Q\overline{Q}-potential in free-space and in the presence of a pion condensate. The modifications to the potential are found to have significant dependence on the Q\overline{Q} separation over the range r < 1 fm explored in this work. Our results are consistent with the pion-condensate behaving as a (nonlinear) chromo-dielectric.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD is used to calculate the modification of the static quark-antiquark potential in the presence of a finite density of charged pions, finding that the attractive Q-Qbar interaction is reduced over separations up to about 1 fm, consistent with the pion condensate behaving as a nonlinear chromo-dielectric.

## Method

The authors compute correlation functions of multiple pi+ mesons together with Wilson-loop correlators on mixed-action lattice QCD ensembles (domain-wall valence quarks on rooted-staggered MILC gauge configurations). They extract the in-medium contribution to the Q-Qbar potential from the ratio of three-point to two-point correlation functions at large Euclidean times, using multiple levels of HYP-smearing and APE-smearing to improve signal quality. The calculations are performed on a 28^3 x 96 lattice with spatial dimension ~2.5 fm, at a pion mass of ~320 MeV and lattice spacing b=0.087(1) fm, using 1001 gauge configurations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: b = 0.087(1) fm
- Pion mass: m_pi ~ 320 MeV
- Lattice volume: 28^3 x 96, giving spatial dimension ~2.5 fm
- Single-pion number density: rho_0 ~ 0.064 fm^{-3}
- Five-pion density: 5 rho_0 = 0.32 fm^{-3}
- In-medium potential shift as small as delta V(b,1) ~ 100 keV was determined for a single pion at separation R = b
- Maximum Q-Qbar separation explored: R < 1 fm
- Density range explored: up to ~7 rho_0, where the potential shift was found linear in density within uncertainties

## Context

This work extends prior lattice QCD calculations of J/psi-pion scattering lengths (Liu, poster; Yokokawa et al., 2006) by replacing the J/psi with a Wilson-loop and studying multi-pion backgrounds for the first time. The results are consistent with the pion condensate acting as a chromo-dielectric medium, reducing the Q-Qbar force, and provide a first step toward systematic lattice QCD studies of in-medium effects in hadronic backgrounds, with the ultimate goal of exploring modifications in baryonic backgrounds.
