# Fusion Reactivities with Drift bi-Maxwellian Ion Velocity Distributions

> The calculation of fusion reactivity involves a complex six-dimensional integral that takes into account the fusion cross-section and velocity distributions of two reactants. However, a more simplified one-dimensional integral form can be useful in certain cases, such as for studying fusion yield or diagnosing ion energy spectra. This simpler form has been derived in a few special cases, such as for a combination of two Maxwellian distributions, a beam-Maxwellian combination, and a beam-target combination, and can greatly reduce computational costs. In this study, it is shown that the reactivity for two drift bi-Maxwellian reactants with different drift velocities, temperatures, and anisotropies can also be reduced to a one-dimensional form, unifying existing derivations into a single expression. This result is used to investigate the potential enhancement of fusion reactivity due to the combination of beam and temperature anisotropies. For relevant parameters in fusion energy, the enhancement factor can be larger than 20\%, which is particularly significant for proton-boron (p-B11) fusion, as this factor can have a significant impact on the Lawson fusion gain criteria.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the fusion reactivity for two drift bi-Maxwellian reactants with different drift velocities, temperatures, and anisotropies can be reduced to a one-dimensional integral form, unifying existing derivations into a single expression. The claimed enhancement factor larger than 20% for proton-boron (p-B11) fusion is conditional on specific parameter ranges (e.g., T_r* around 250 keV and T_perp r > 0.75 T_parallel r) and is particularly significant for the Lawson fusion gain criteria due to the close balance between radiation loss and fusion power.

## Method

The authors derived a one-dimensional integral form of the fusion reactivity ⟨σv⟩ for two drift bi-Maxwellian ion velocity distributions by performing coordinate transformations that reduce the six-dimensional velocity integral to a one-dimensional integral over center-of-mass energy E. They validated their analytical result by comparing numerical evaluations of the 1D integral against both a 2D integral form and a 6D Monte Carlo integration, and also benchmarked against prior 3D integral results from Nath (2013). The derived formula was then applied to compute enhancement factors for D-T, D-D, D-He3, and p-B11 fusion reactions under various combinations of temperature anisotropy (R_t = T_perp r / T_parallel r) and drift parameter (R_d = E_d / k_B T_r).

## Key numbers

- For p-B11 fusion at T_r* ≈ 250 keV and T_perp r > 0.75 T_parallel r with R_d = 3, the enhancement factor can reach around 20%.
- Critical temperatures below which enhancement from temperature anisotropy alone occurs: T_c^DT ≈ 15 keV, T_c^DD ≈ 50 keV, T_c^DHe3 ≈ 60 keV, T_c^pB11 ≈ 140 keV.
- For D-T fusion with drift velocity v_d/v_tr up to 3 and T_r = 30 keV, the enhancement factor can decrease below 1 (i.e., reactivity is reduced) at large drift velocities.
- The Lawson criteria for p-B11 fusion shows a decline of an order of magnitude when the enhancement factor f_⟨σv⟩ changes from 1.0 to 1.2 using the cross section from Sikora (2016).

## Context

This work extends prior derivations of simplified fusion reactivity integrals for Maxwellian-Maxwellian (Nevins, 2000), beam-target (Miley, 1974; Morse, 2018), and bi-Maxwellian (Kolmes, 2021; Li, 2022) distributions by providing a unified one-dimensional form for drift bi-Maxwellian reactants. It also generalizes the three-dimensional integral results of Nath (2013) for drift tri-Maxwellian distributions. The results enable efficient computation of fusion yields in numerical codes such as NUBEAM (Pankin, 2004) and provide analytical insight into how non-Maxwellian ion distributions from neutral beam injection or ion cyclotron heating can affect fusion reactivity and Lawson criteria, particularly for the challenging p-B11 fusion reaction.
