# Recent breakthroughs in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mass formulas

> We review our recent achievements in the construction of microscopic mass tables based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with Skyrme effective interactions. In the latest of our series of HFB-mass models, we have obtained our best fit ever to essentially all the available mass data, by treating the pairing more realistically than in any of our earlier models. The rms deviation on the 2149 measured masses of nuclei with N and Z>8 has been reduced for the first time in a mean field approach to 0.581 MeV. With the additional constraint on the neutron-matter equation of state, this new force is thus very well-suited for the study of neutron-rich nuclei and for the description of astrophysical environments like supernova cores and neutron-star crusts.

## Key claim

The authors report that their HFB-17 mass model achieves the best fit ever to measured nuclear masses within a mean-field approach, reducing the rms deviation to 0.581 MeV, but this result depends on imposing an additional constraint that the effective pairing force reproduce microscopic \(^1S_0\) pairing gaps in uniform asymmetric nuclear matter, not just the mass data alone.

## Method

The authors constructed two new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mass models, HFB-16 and HFB-17, using Skyrme effective interactions with contact pairing forces, plus phenomenological Wigner and collective energy corrections. Instead of parametrizing the density dependence of the pairing strength as in earlier models, they solved the HFB equations in uniform matter to determine the pairing strength at each neutron and proton density such that the resulting gap exactly reproduces microscopic \(^1S_0\) pairing gaps calculated with realistic forces (Argonne v14 for HFB-16; Argonne v18 with medium-polarization effects for HFB-17). All model parameters were fitted to 2149 measured masses from the 2003 Atomic Mass Evaluation for nuclei with \(N\) and \(Z \ge 8\).

## Key numbers

- The rms deviation between experimental and calculated masses for the HFB-17 model is 0.581 MeV, based on 2149 measured masses of nuclei with \(N\) and \(Z \ge 8\).
- The mean deviation (data minus theory) for HFB-17 is -0.019 MeV.
- The neutron drip density in the outer crust of neutron stars is predicted to be \(\rho_{\rm ND} \simeq 3.7 \times 10^{11}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) for both HFB-16 and HFB-17.
- The nuclear-matter symmetry coefficient was constrained to \(J = 30\) MeV in the HFB-17 fit.

## Context

This work extends the authors' series of HFB mass models (HFB-1 through HFB-15, including HFB-9 from Goriely et al. 2005) by introducing a microscopically derived effective pairing force constrained to reproduce \(^1S_0\) pairing gaps from realistic calculations, rather than using a simple parametrized density dependence. The new models improve upon earlier mean-field approaches and are compared to the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller et al., showing differences up to 25 MeV for heavy neutron-rich nuclei. The HFB-17 model enables more reliable predictions for neutron-rich nuclei and astrophysical applications such as the r-process of nucleosynthesis, supernova cores, and neutron-star crusts, though the double magicity around \(^{208}\)Pb remains unresolved in all mean-field calculations.
