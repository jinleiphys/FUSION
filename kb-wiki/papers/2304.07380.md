# Quantum fluctuations induce collective multiphonons in finite Fermi liquids

> We show that collective multiphonon states in atomic nuclei emerge at high excitation energies when quantum fluctuations in the collective space are included beyond the independent-particle approximation. The quadrupole response of a nucleus is studied using an extension of the nuclear time-dependent density-functional theory that mixes several many-body trajectories. While a single trajectory can account for the excitation of the first collective quantum, the second and the third quanta emerge due to the interference between trajectories. The collective spectrum, found as nearly harmonic, is in excellent agreement with the experimentally observed three quanta of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance in $^{40}$Ca. This study offers guidance for multiphonon searches in other self-bound systems and demonstrates the resistance to internal excitation of finite Fermi liquids.

## Key claim

Collective multiphonon states in atomic nuclei emerge naturally at high excitation energies when quantum fluctuations in the collective space are included beyond the independent-particle approximation, specifically through interference between multiple time-dependent density-functional theory trajectories rather than from any single trajectory.

## Method

The authors extend the time-dependent density-functional theory (TDDFT) framework into a multiconfigurational TDDFT (MC-TDDFT) approach, where the nuclear many-body wave function is written as a weighted, time-dependent superposition of non-orthogonal Slater determinants, each following its own TDDFT trajectory. The mixing coefficients that account for quantum interference between trajectories are determined through the Dirac-Frenkel variational principle. For the $^{40}$Ca nucleus, a minimal mixed state of three configurations is used: the ground state and two quadrupole-boosted states with excitation energies of about 0.25 and 0.50 MeV, all perturbed with a common isoscalar quadrupole boost of $\lambda = 5.7 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-2}$.

## Key numbers

* The main one-phonon peak of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance in $^{40}$Ca is found at $E_{\rm{ph}} = (18.1 \pm 0.2)$ MeV, in excellent agreement with experimental data.
* The two-phonon state is predicted at $E_{2\rm{ph}} = (35.7 \pm 0.4)$ MeV, close to the experimentally established value of $(34.8 \pm 0.5)$ MeV from Scarpaci (1997).
* The three-phonon state is predicted at $E_{3\rm{ph}} = (53.6 \pm 0.7)$ MeV, within the experimentally observed range of 42-55 MeV from Fallot (2006).
* The anharmonicity of the multiphonon spectrum is estimated to be of the order of $2\%$, based on the reported excitation energies.
* The TDDFT single-trajectory calculation yields a peak at about $E_{\rm{TDDFT}} = 17.8$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the method discussed in Reinhard (1983) and Regnier (2019) to realistic modeling of nuclear dynamics, providing a microscopic foundation for multiphonon states that were previously described only through ad hoc introduction of phonon degrees of freedom via boson mapping methods (Andres 2001, Lanza 2006) or the quasiparticle-phonon model (Soloviev 1992, Bertulani 1999). The results confirm the quasibosonic picture of multiphonon states as essentially harmonic excitations proposed by Chomaz (1995), and demonstrate that the MC-TDDFT approach can reproduce the experimentally observed multiphonon structures in $^{40}$Ca without any harmonic approximation. This study enables future searches for multiphonon states in other self-bound systems such as quantum droplets, and suggests that the MC-TDDFT framework could be extended to study nuclear fission where the lack of quantum fluctuations currently prevents calculation of quantities like mass yields.
