# Isospin Breaking in Heavy-Meson Decay Constants

> Evaluation of Borelized QCD sum rules in the so-called local-duality limit of infinitely large Borel mass parameter provides an alternate route for extraction of the dependence of the decay constants of heavy-light mesons on the mass $m_q$ of the involved light quark $q$: For appropriate choices of the two-point correlation functions of currents interpolating the hadrons under study, the local-duality limit forces all nonperturbative contributions parametrized by vacuum condensates to such kind of correlator to vanish. As a consequence, the sought $m_q$ dependence of the heavy-light meson decay constants proves to be controlled primarily by the correlator contributions from perturbative QCD. Our knowledge of the analytic behaviour of the latter as functions of $m_q$ enables us to derive the $m_q$ dependence of the decay constants of both pseudoscalar and vector heavy-light mesons, for which we estimate strong isospin breaking to be of the order of 1 MeV for both charm and beauty sectors.

## Key claim
The paper estimates strong isospin breaking in heavy-light meson decay constants to be of order 1 MeV for both charm and beauty sectors, using the local-duality limit of Borelized QCD sum rules where nonperturbative vacuum condensate contributions vanish.

## Method
The authors evaluate Borelized QCD sum rules in the local-duality limit (Borel parameter τ → 0) for two-point correlation functions of heavy-light meson currents. In this limit, all nonperturbative power corrections from vacuum condensates vanish, leaving only perturbative QCD spectral densities up to order α_s², with the effective threshold s_eff incorporating all nonperturbative effects. The light-quark mass dependence of the decay constants is extracted by varying m_q continuously between the chiral limit and the strange-quark mass, matching the resulting f_H(m_q) functions to lattice-QCD results at the physical u/d and s quark points.

## Key numbers
- f_D± − f_D0 = (0.96 ± 0.09) MeV
- f_D*± − f_D*0 = (1.18 ± 0.35) MeV
- f_B0 − f_B± = (1.01 ± 0.10) MeV
- f_B*0 − f_B*± = (0.89 ± 0.30) MeV
- The down-up quark mass difference (m_d − m_u)(2 GeV) ≈ 2.5 MeV (from PDG)
- Roughly 70–80% of the isospin breaking arises from the analytically known m_q dependence of the spectral densities

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum-rule analyses using τ-dependent effective thresholds (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2014–2017) to the local-duality limit, providing an alternative route to isospin-breaking predictions. The results are consistent with their earlier findings from conventional Borel sum rules (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2016) and are matched to lattice-QCD results for f_H at the physical u/d and s quark masses. The approach enables precise determination of strong isospin breaking in heavy-meson decay constants, which is relevant for extracting CKM matrix elements and testing the Standard Model.
