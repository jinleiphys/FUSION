# Partial restoration of chiral symmetry in hot and dense neutron matter

> We review efforts to describe the approach to chiral symmetry restoration in neutron matter from the low-energy realization of QCD, chiral effective field theory.

# Partial restoration of chiral symmetry in hot and dense neutron matter

## Key claim

Nuclear interactions from chiral effective field theory systematically delay chiral symmetry restoration in neutron matter at both zero and finite temperature, counteracting the leading linear decrease of the scalar quark condensate with density; however, thermal effects above approximately 50 MeV wash out these interaction contributions, making hot proto-neutron stars more plausible sites for quark-hadron phase transitions than cold neutron stars.

## Method

The authors compute the free energy per particle of pure neutron matter at finite temperature using many-body perturbation theory in the imaginary-time Matsubara formalism, employing a coarse-resolution chiral potential (n3lo414) with a momentum-space cutoff of 414 MeV that includes two- and three-nucleon forces. They then extract the scalar quark condensate ratio via the Hellmann-Feynman theorem and the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation, taking numerical derivatives of the free energy with respect to the pion mass (varied by 1%, 2%, and 5%) to obtain the pion-mass dependence of the interaction contributions.

## Key numbers

- The chiral potential used has a momentum-space cutoff of Λ = 414 MeV.
- At zero temperature and density n = 0.2 fm⁻³, nuclear interactions increase the chiral condensate by about 15% relative to the noninteracting case.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is E_sym = 32.5 MeV and its slope is L = 53.8 MeV, consistent with empirical constraints.
- For temperatures up to T = 50 MeV and densities below n = 0.2 fm⁻³, the second-order interaction contribution to the scalar quark condensate gives less than a 10% correction.
- The leading Hartree-Fock contribution is consistently about three times larger in magnitude than the second-order correction.
- The noninteracting linear term alone would give chiral symmetry restoration at n ≈ 2.5 n₀ (where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³) assuming σ_πN = 45 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature chiral EFT calculations of the quark condensate in neutron matter by Krueger et al. (2013) to finite temperature, and complements finite-temperature studies in symmetric nuclear matter by Fiorilla et al. (2012) using in-medium chiral perturbation theory. The results are qualitatively similar to earlier one-boson-exchange models by Li et al. (1994) and Brockmann et al. (1996), but with systematically improvable theoretical uncertainties. The approach enables more reliable predictions for the possibility of quark-hadron phase transitions in hot proto-neutron stars and neutron star merger remnants.
