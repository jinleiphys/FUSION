# Dynamical Generation of Hyperon Resonances

> In this talk we report on how, using a chiral unitary approach for the meson--baryon interactions, two octets of $J^π=1/2^-$ baryon states and a singlet are generated dynamically, resulting in the case of strangeness $S=-1$ in two poles of the scattering matrix close to the nominal $Λ(1405)$ resonance. We suggest experiments which could show evidence for the existence of these states.

## Key claim
Using a chiral unitary approach for meson–baryon interactions, two octets and a singlet of \(J^\pi = 1/2^-\) baryon states are generated dynamically; for strangeness \(S=-1\) this results in two poles of the scattering matrix close to the nominal \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance, meaning that what experiments observe is a superposition of two distinct states rather than a single resonance.

## Method
The authors start from the lowest-order meson–baryon chiral Lagrangian and construct a coupled-channel scattering matrix that exactly satisfies unitarity via the N/D method. The transition potentials include the Weinberg–Tomozawa (seagull) term, with physical baryon masses used in the potential and in the meson–baryon loop functions \(G_l\), which are regularized via dimensional regularization with subtraction constants chosen to reproduce a cut-off of 630 MeV. Poles of the \(T\)-matrix are then searched for in unphysical Riemann sheets by changing the sign of the on-shell momentum in the loop function for open channels.

## Key numbers
- Two \(I=0\) poles near the \(\Lambda(1405)\): one at \(1390 + 66i\) MeV (coupling more strongly to \(\pi\Sigma\)) and one at \(1426 + 16i\) MeV (coupling more strongly to \(\bar{K}N\)), from the model of Bennhold et al.
- A third \(I=0\) pole at \(1680 + 20i\) MeV, identified with the \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance.
- For \(I=1\), poles at \(1401 + 40i\) MeV and \(1488 + 114i\) MeV from the model of Oller et al.
- The subtraction constants used for the loop functions are: \(a_{\bar{K}N} = -1.84\), \(a_{\pi\Sigma} = -2.00\), \(a_{\pi\Lambda} = -1.83\), \(a_{\eta\Lambda} = -2.25\), \(a_{\eta\Sigma} = -2.38\), \(a_{K\Xi} = -2.67\).
- In the SU(3) symmetric limit, the two octet poles are degenerate and the diagonal transition potentials for irreducible representations are: \(V = \text{diag}(6, 3, 3, 0, 0, -2)\) for the \(1\), \(8_s\), \(8_a\), \(10\), \(\overline{10}\), and \(27\) representations.

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to meson–meson scattering (where a nonet of dynamically generated mesons was found) and to baryon resonances such as the \(N^*(1535)\) and \(\Xi(1620)\). It builds on earlier findings by Oller et al. (2000) and by Bennhold et al. that two poles exist near the \(\Lambda(1405)\), and it clarifies their SU(3) origin as a singlet and two octets. The results enable experimental tests: the reaction \(K^- p \to \Lambda(1405) \gamma\) is suggested to preferentially excite the higher-energy, narrower pole, while hadronic production reactions like \(\pi^- p \to K^0 \pi \Sigma\) probe the superposition of both states. The work also highlights the need to analyze each specific reaction rather than assuming a single resonance shape.
