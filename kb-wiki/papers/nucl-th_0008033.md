# The Past and Future of Coulomb Dissociation in Hadron- and Astrophysics

> Breakup reactions are generally quite complicated, they involve nuclear and electromagnetic forces including interference effects. Coulomb dissociation is an especially simple and important mechanism since the perturbation due to the electric field of the nucleus is exactly known. Therefore firm conclusions can be drawn from such measurements. Electromagnetic matrixelements, radiative capture cross-sections and astrophysical S-factors can be extracted from experiments. We describe the basic theory, give analytical results for higher order effects in the dissociation of neutron halo nuclei and briefly review the experimental results obtained up to now. Some new applications of Coulomb dissociation for nuclear astrophysics and nuclear structure physics are discussed.

# The Past and Future of Coulomb Dissociation in Hadron- and Astrophysics

## Key claim

Coulomb dissociation is a clean and reliable tool for extracting electromagnetic matrix elements, radiative capture cross sections, and astrophysical S-factors because the electromagnetic perturbation is exactly known, but higher-order effects (especially for neutron halo nuclei) can reach corrections of order -35% in some calculations, while the authors' own analytical model finds only -2% for the case of 19C dissociation, indicating that the magnitude of these corrections remains model-dependent and must be carefully assessed for each system.

## Method

The authors develop analytical expressions for first- and second-order electromagnetic excitation amplitudes using a zero-range model for the neutron-core interaction, expanding the breakup probability in the strength parameter eta_eff. They apply the equivalent photon approximation to relate Coulomb dissociation cross sections to photo-induced reactions, and compare their analytical results for higher-order effects to coupled-channels and time-dependent Schrodinger equation approaches. Experimental results from Coulomb dissociation of neutron halo nuclei (11Be, 19C, 11Li) and astrophysically relevant systems (6Li, 8B) are reviewed and compared with direct measurements.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of 19C determined from Coulomb dissociation: 530 +/- 130 keV
- Ratio of next-to-leading-order (NLO) to leading-order (LO) contribution for Coulomb dissociation of 19C: -2% (authors' model) versus approximately -35% (Tostevin 1999 calculation)
- Cross sections for giant dipole resonance excitation in relativistic heavy ion colliders (Au-Au or Pb-Pb): of order 100 barns
- 8B Coulomb dissociation on 208Pb studied at projectile energies of 46.5 A MeV and 250 A MeV, with E1 excitation peaked at very forward angles
- 11Be Coulomb dissociation on Pb target measured at 72 A MeV

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on Coulomb excitation and dissociation dating back to Oppenheimer and Phillips (1935) and the comprehensive review by Baur, Roesel, Trautmann, and Shyam (1984). It builds on the equivalent photon approximation formalism of Bertulani and Baur (1988) and the analytical higher-order calculations of Typel and Baur (1994). The paper enables future applications of Coulomb dissociation to neutron-capture reactions on unstable nuclei relevant to the r-process (as proposed by Gai 1997) and to two-nucleon capture reactions in explosive astrophysical environments such as X-ray bursts and supernova neutrino bubbles, where direct measurements are impossible.
