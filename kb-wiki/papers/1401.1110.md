# $J/ψ$ in a hot baryonic plasma

> We calculate the bound state properties of $J/ψ$ in a hot and dense QCD plasma using phenomenological potentials augmented by inputs from perturbative QCD. The temperature and density region of study will be relevant in future heavy ion collision experiments at FAIR. We find that the effect of baryon density on the dissociation of $J/ψ$ is small in this regime. However we indicate that if there is a critical end point in the QCD phase diagram then strong density fluctuation will dissociate charmonia near hadronization. The measurement of $J/ψ$ suppression can therefore signify the existence of the critical point unambiguously.

## Key claim
The effect of baryon density on the dissociation of J/ψ is small in the temperature and density regime relevant for FAIR experiments (T/Tpc⁰ ~ 1–1.5, μq/Tpc⁰ ~ 1–2), but if a critical end point exists in the QCD phase diagram, strong density fluctuations near that point will dissociate charmonia before hadronization, making J/ψ suppression a clean signal for the critical point.

## Method
The authors solve the Schrödinger equation with a complex potential whose real part is the internal energy derived from a screened Cornell-type free energy (including a relativistic correction) and whose imaginary part is computed in the hard thermal loop approximation. They use a "fuzzy bag" equation of state to extract temperature and chemical potential from URQMD simulations of central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.62 GeV, then evolve the system from τ₀ = 3.5 fm/c to τf = 6.5 fm/c. The binding energy and decay width of J/ψ are computed as functions of time, and the survival probability is obtained by integrating the decay width over the evolution.

## Key numbers
- String tension σ = 0.223 GeV² and α = 0.385 for the potential parameters.
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.3 GeV.
- Pseudo-critical temperature T_pc⁰ = 170 MeV (taken as hadronization temperature).
- System equilibrates at τ₀ = 3.5 fm/c and evolution followed until τ_f = 6.5 fm/c.
- For the critical point scenario, chosen critical values are (T_c, μ_{c,q}) = (159.8, 138.57) MeV.
- Effective screening temperature T_s ≈ T √(1 + 0.076 μ_q²/T²) for N_f = 2, showing density effect is small.
- Critical exponents used: γ = 1.24, δ = 4.815.

## Context
This work extends the potential-model approach to charmonium dissociation (pioneered by Karsch, Mehr, and Satz (1987)) to finite baryon density, using the internal energy prescription of Dumitru (2009) and the imaginary potential from Laine and collaborators (2006–2007). It complements lattice QCD studies, which face a sign problem at finite chemical potential. The results enable predictions for the CBM experiment at FAIR and suggest that a sharp drop in J/ψ yield could serve as an unambiguous signature of the QCD critical end point, whose location remains uncertain from lattice calculations (Fodor, 2004; Datta, 2012).
