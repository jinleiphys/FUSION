# Interference effects in medium-induced gluon radiation

> As a step towards understanding the in-medium evolution of a hard jet, we consider the interference pattern for the medium induced gluon radiation produced by a color singlet quark-antiquark antenna embedded in a QCD medium with size L. We focus on the typical kinematics for medium-induced gluon radiation in the BDMPS-Z regime, that is, short formation times τ_f << L and relatively large emission angles θ>>θ_c = 2/\sqrt{\hat q L^3}, with \hat q the `jet quenching' parameter. We demonstrate that, for a dipole opening angle θ_{q\bar q} larger than θ_c, the interference between the medium-induced gluon emissions by the quark and the antiquark is parametrically suppressed with respect to the corresponding direct emissions. Physically, this is so since the direct emissions can be delocalized anywhere throughout the medium and thus yield contributions proportional to L. On the contrary, the interference occurs only between gluons emitted at very early times, within the characteristic time scales for quantum and color coherence between the two emitters, which in this regime are much smaller than L. This implies that, for θ_{q\bar q} >> θ_c, the medium-induced radiation by the dipole is simply the sum of the two BDMPS-Z spectra individually produced by the quark and the antiquark, without coherence effects like angular ordering. For θ_{q\bar q} << θ_c, the BDMPS-Z radiation by the dipole vanishes.

## Key claim

For a color-singlet quark-antiquark dipole in a QCD medium, interference between medium-induced gluon emissions from the quark and antiquark is parametrically suppressed relative to direct emissions when the dipole opening angle exceeds the critical angle θ_c = 2/√(q̂L³), because direct emissions can occur anywhere throughout the medium (proportional to L) while interference is restricted to early times much smaller than L; only when the dipole angle is smaller than θ_c does the total medium-induced radiation vanish entirely.

## Method

The authors analyze the medium-induced gluon radiation from a color-singlet quark-antiquark dipole using the BDMPS-Z formalism in the multiple soft scattering approximation, focusing on the regime of short formation times τ_f ≪ L and large emission angles θ ≫ θ_c. They compute the direct emission contributions and interference terms separately, using analytic approximations at parametric accuracy, and compare the longitudinal phase-space available for each process. The calculations are performed for a dipole with opening angle θ_{q\bar q} propagating through a medium of length L with jet quenching parameter q̂.

## Key numbers

- Critical angle θ_c = 2/√(q̂ L³), which for typical heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC (L = 6 fm, q̂ = 2-10 GeV²/fm) yields θ_c = 0.005-0.01
- Maximal gluon frequency ω_c = q̂ L²/2
- Saturation momentum Q_s² = q̂ L
- Formation time τ_f = √(2ω/q̂) for a gluon with energy ω
- Formation angle θ_f = θ_c (ω_c/ω)^{3/4} for ω ≪ ω_c
- Saturation angle θ_s = θ_c (ω_c/ω) for ω ≪ ω_c
- Kinematics domain for final gluon: ω between q̂^{1/3} and ω_c, k_⊥ between k_f = Q_s (ω/ω_c)^{1/4} and Q_s

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigné, Schiff, and Zakharov) for medium-induced gluon radiation to the case of a color dipole, building on earlier studies of interference in the single-scattering approximation by Mehtar-Tani (2010) and in a different regime by Mehtar-Tani (2011). The results contradict the expectation of angular ordering for medium-induced radiation and show that for θ_{q\bar q} ≫ θ_c, the dipole radiation is simply the sum of two independent BDMPS-Z spectra. This has implications for understanding jet broadening and di-jet asymmetry in heavy ion collisions at the LHC, as it indicates that large-angle medium-induced radiation is not suppressed by coherence effects.
