# The 3$α$ correlations of ground and excited $0^+$ states of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ within the microscopic cluster model

> The cluster structures of the $0^+$ states in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, including the ground state, the Hoyle state, and the recently identified $0_3^+$ and $0_4^+$ states, are analyzed to explore the cluster configurations and $3α$ correlations without assuming the existence of $^8\mathrm{Be}$. In particular, the key quantity -- two-cluster overlap amplitudes -- is calculated for the $3α$ clustering channels to reveal the essential features of these $0^+$ states. The results clearly show the distinction between the compact structure of the ground state and the gas-like structures of the excited $0^+$ states. The Hoyle state exhibits the expected gas-like dominant ($0S$) configuration, while the $0_3^+$ state shows a more extended $3α$ clustering structure, which can be viewed as a breathing-like excitation of the Hoyle state, with an additional nodal structure. The $0_4^+$ state is found to have a mixed configuration, featuring a bent-arm-like structure in the $S\otimes S$ channel and an enhanced $2α$ correlation in the $D\otimes D$ channel.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $0_3^+$ state of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ is a breathing-like excitation of the Hoyle state with an additional nodal structure, while the $0_4^+$ state has a mixed configuration combining a bent-arm-like structure in the $S\otimes S$ channel and enhanced $2\alpha$ correlation in the $D\otimes D$ channel, without assuming the existence of $^8\mathrm{Be}$ as an intermediate state.

## Method

The authors used the generator coordinate method (GCM) with Brink wave functions to obtain microscopic wave functions for the $0^+$ states of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, employing the modified Volkov No. 2 nucleon-nucleon interaction with parameters identical to Kamimura (1981). They extended the reduced-width amplitude (RWA) approach to calculate the two-cluster overlap amplitude (TCOA) for the $3\alpha$ clustering channels, defined as the overlap between the nuclear wave function and a three-$\alpha$ cluster wave function with specified distances between clusters and orbital angular momenta. The three-cluster spectroscopic factor ($S_{3\alpha}^2$) was computed as the integral of the squared norm of the TCOA over the two relative coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy: 89.65 MeV
- Hoyle state ($0_2^+$) excitation energy: 7.82 MeV, very close to the $3\alpha$ break-up threshold
- $0_3^+$ state excitation energy: 10.43 MeV
- $0_4^+$ state excitation energy: 11.63 MeV
- Ground state TCOA peaks in $S\otimes S$ channel at ($a_{\alpha\text{-}\alpha}=3.3$ fm, $a_{\alpha\text{-}2\alpha}=3.0$ fm); in $D\otimes D$ at ($3.2$ fm, $2.8$ fm); in $G\otimes G$ at ($2.8$ fm, $2.2$ fm)
- Hoyle state highest TCOA peak at ($a_{\alpha\text{-}\alpha}=4.0$ fm, $a_{\alpha\text{-}2\alpha}=5.1$ fm)
- $0_3^+$ state shows additional nodal line at approximately 8 fm separation
- Ground state $S_{3\alpha}^2$ values: 0.303 ($S\otimes S$), 0.252 ($D\otimes D$), 0.140 ($G\otimes G$)

## Context

This work extends the cluster analysis of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ $0^+$ states from the two-body $^8\mathrm{Be}+\alpha$ reduced-width amplitude approach used in earlier studies by Uegaki (1979), Kamimura (1981), and Funaki (2015, 2016) to a full three-body $\alpha+\alpha+\alpha$ description. The results confirm and refine the Bose-Einstein condensation picture of the Hoyle state proposed by Tohsaki (2001) and support the breathing-like excitation interpretation of the $0_3^+$ state from Zhou (2016) and Li (2022). The calculated TCOA can serve as input for reaction calculations, enabling improved predictions for two-cluster knockout reactions and providing microscopic structural information for future studies of rotational band configurations involving higher angular-momentum states.
