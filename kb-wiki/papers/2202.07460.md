# In-medium $Λ$ isospin impurity from charge symmetry breaking in the ${_Λ^4}{\rm H}-{_Λ^4}{\rm He}$ mirror hypernuclei

> The $Λ$ separation energies in the mirror hypernuclei ${_Λ^4}{\rm H}-{_Λ^4}{\rm He}$ exhibit large charge symmetry breaking (CSB). Analyzing this CSB within pionless effective field theory while using partially conserved baryon-baryon SU(3) flavor symmetry, we deduce a $Λ-Σ^0$ induced in-medium admixture amplitude ${\cal A}_{I=1}\approx 1.5\%$ in the dominantly isospin $I=0$ $Λ$ hyperon. Our results confirm the free-space value ${\cal A}^{(0)}_{I=1}$ inferred directly within the SU(3) baryon octet by Dalitz and von-Hippel in 1964 and reaffirmed in a recent QCD+QED lattice calculation. Furthermore, exploring the consequences of SU(3) flavor symmetry on the $Λ$-nucleon interaction, we find that CSB is expected to impact the $S=1$ and $S=0$ spin channels in opposite directions, with the latter dominating by an order of magnitude. These observations explain a recent deduction of $Λ$-nucleon CSB strengths.

# In-medium Λ isospin impurity from charge symmetry breaking in the ⁴_ΛH–⁴_ΛHe mirror hypernuclei

## Key claim

The free-space Λ–Σ⁰ mixing amplitude of about 1.5% (the I=1 isospin impurity in the dominantly I=0 Λ hyperon) is upheld in the A=4 mirror hypernuclei ⁴_ΛH and ⁴_ΛHe, with no significant in-medium renormalization. This conclusion depends on adopting a specific charge symmetry breaking (CSB) ansatz and relating the ΣN↔ΛN transition matrix element to NN and ΛN matrix elements via SU(3) flavor symmetry.

## Method

The authors performed pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) calculations at leading order for the A=4 hypernuclear systems, using only nucleon and Λ hyperon degrees of freedom without explicit Σ hyperons. Two spin-dependent ΛN CSB low-energy constants (LECs) δC^S_ΛN (S=0,1) were fitted perturbatively to the two experimentally measured Λ separation energy differences ΔB_Λ(0⁺_g.s.) and ΔB_Λ(1⁺_exc.) in the ⁴_ΛH–⁴_ΛHe mirror pair. The resulting CSB LECs were then related via the Dalitz–von Hippel ansatz and SU(3) flavor symmetry relations to in-medium I=1 admixture amplitudes A^S_{I=1} for each spin channel.

## Key numbers

- Λ separation energy difference for the ground state: ΔB_Λ(0⁺_g.s.) = 233 ± 92 keV
- Λ separation energy difference for the excited state: ΔB_Λ(1⁺_exc.) = −83 ± 94 keV
- Difference of the two γ-ray energies: ΔE_γ = 316 ± 20 keV
- Free-space I=1 admixture amplitude from Dalitz–von Hippel (1964): −A^{(0)}_{I=1} = 0.0148 ± 0.0006
- Free-space I=1 admixture amplitude from QCD+QED lattice calculation: 0.0168 ± 0.0054
- In-medium I=1 admixture amplitude from EFT(π̸)(LO) with χEFT(NLO) input: −A_{I=1} = 0.0168 ± 0.0014 (extrapolated to λ→∞)
- In-medium I=1 admixture amplitude from EFT(π̸)(LO) with χEFT(LO) input: −A_{I=1} = 0.0139 ± 0.0013 (extrapolated to λ→∞)
- Nuclear CSB splitting in ³H–³He mirror core nuclei: ΔB_CSB = 67 ± 9 keV
- Free-space Λ–Σ⁰ mass-mixing matrix element: M_{Σ⁰Λ} = 1.14 ± 0.05 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Dalitz–von Hippel (1964) SU(3) flavor symmetry analysis of Λ–Σ⁰ mixing in free space to the in-medium environment of A=4 hypernuclei, confirming that the free-space I=1 admixture amplitude of about 1.5% persists in matter. The results are consistent with recent χEFT calculations by Gazda and Gal (2016) and by Hildenbrand et al. (2021) for the ⁴_ΛH–⁴_ΛHe splittings, and provide a theoretical explanation for why CSB acts predominantly on the spin S=0 channel and affects the S=1 and S=0 channels in opposite directions. The approach enables economical evaluation of CSB in strange matter and can be tested in heavier hypernuclei when precise CSB data become available.
