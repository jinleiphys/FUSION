# Low-energy Bremsstrahlung photon in relativistic nucleon+nucleon collisions

> We study the production of Bremsstrahlung photon in relativistic nucleon+nucleon collisions by introducing a deceleration time of electromagnetic currents. It is found that Bremsstrahlung photon spectrum at low energy does not depend on the deceleration time but solely on the amount of reduced electromagnetic current in collision. On the other hand, the photon spectrum becomes soft with increasing deceleration time. We also find that Bremsstrahlung photon spectrum in p+n collisions is considerably different from that in p+p collisions at low energy.

## Key claim

The low-energy Bremsstrahlung photon spectrum in relativistic nucleon+nucleon collisions does not depend on the deceleration time of electromagnetic currents but solely on the amount of reduced electromagnetic current; however, the spectrum becomes soft with increasing deceleration time, and the spectrum in p+n collisions is considerably different from that in p+p collisions at low energy.

## Method

The authors model the production of low-energy Bremsstrahlung photons by introducing a finite deceleration (stopping) time of electromagnetic currents, using a hyperbolic tangent function to describe smooth stopping. They derive analytic expressions for the photon spectrum from a single charged particle and generalize to two colliding particles (p+p and p+n) at mid-rapidity, incorporating the internal quark structure of the nucleon via randomly distributed valence quarks within a sphere of radius R = 1 fm. The amount of reduced electromagnetic current in p+n collisions at √s = 200 GeV is obtained from the PYTHIA event generator.

## Key numbers

* In p+n collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the electromagnetic current after collision is 0.85 e on average, corresponding to Δj = -0.15 e.
* The effective stopping time is defined as 2/a, during which electromagnetic current decreases from 88% to 12%.
* The fine structure constant α = e²/(4π) is taken as 1/137.
* The proton radius R is taken to be 1 fm for the quark structure calculations.
* The correction factor for proton structure at large k_T for a uuduū composition yields a value of 17/9 from the sum of squared quark charges.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of Bremsstrahlung photon production in relativistic nucleon+nucleon collisions, which serves as a reference for interpreting direct photon measurements in heavy-ion collisions. The approach builds on prior work by Koch (1990), Itzykson (1980), and Biro (2014) on the formalism of photon radiation from decelerated charged particles, and uses the PYTHIA event generator (Sjostrand, 2006) to determine charge stopping. The results enable improved modeling of low-energy direct photon backgrounds in heavy-ion collision experiments, particularly for distinguishing thermal photons from Bremsstrahlung contributions.
