# Strangeness content of the pion in the U(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model is considered with flavor-dependent coupling constants $G_{ij} \left[ ( \barψ λ_i ψ) ( \barψ λ_j ψ)+ ( \barψ iγ_5 λ_i ψ) ( \barψ i γ_5λ_j ψ)\right]$ for $i,j=0,1..N_f^2-1$, and $N_f=3$. A self consistent calculation of quark effective masses and coupling constants is performed making the strange quark effective mass to vary considerably. Quantum mechanical mixings between up, down and strange constituent quarks yields a strangeness content of the light u and d quarks constituent and of the pion. Different types of estimates for the strangeness contribution for the pion mass are provided. Mixing type interactions, $G_{i\neq j}$, induce the light mesons mixings and estimates for the $π^0-η$ and $η-η'$ mixings are provided. The $η-π^0$ mixing is argued to be an indication of the strangeness content of the pion.

## Key claim

The paper argues that flavor-dependent coupling constants in the U(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, derived from polarization corrections with effective gluon propagators, produce a strangeness content in the light u and d constituent quarks and in the pion, with the eta-pi0 mixing angle serving as an indicator of this strangeness content, though the results depend on a specific normalization procedure that may overestimate the role of flavor symmetry breaking.

## Method

The author performs a self-consistent calculation of quark effective masses and flavor-dependent coupling constants G_{ij} in the NJL model with N_f=3, using three different effective gluon propagators (one from Schwinger-Dyson equations and two confining parameterizations) to compute the polarization corrections. The gap equations for up, down, and strange quarks are solved iteratively with the flavor-dependent coupling constants, while the Bethe-Salpeter equation at Born level is used to compute pseudoscalar meson masses, with a three-dimensional momentum cutoff regularization scheme and a normalization procedure that sets the flavor-symmetric coupling constant to G_0 = 10 GeV^{-2}.

## Key numbers

- Sets of parameters S and V yield up quark effective masses M_u = 405 MeV (set S) and 422 MeV (set V), down quark masses M_d = 415 MeV (S) and 431 MeV (V), and strange quark masses M_s = 612 MeV (S) and 625 MeV (V), with UV cutoff Lambda = 680 MeV (S) and 685 MeV (V)
- Current quark masses used: m_u = 3 MeV, m_d = 7 MeV, m_s = 133 MeV for both parameter sets
- The strange quark effective mass that produces correct kaon masses is around 0.550-0.580 GeV for the M3 case across all gluon propagator choices
- The coupling constant of reference is G_0 = 10 GeV^{-2}, corresponding to an effective gluon mass M_G ~ 315 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous perturbative investigation of flavor-dependent coupling constants in the NJL model (Braghin 2021) by performing a full self-consistent calculation of effective masses and coupling constants. It addresses the strangeness content of light hadrons, a topic previously studied for the nucleon through experimental and lattice results suggesting a strangeness content of about 5%, and for the pion through meson loop calculations (Cloet and Roberts) that found extremely small contributions. The results enable future investigations of parton structure in pions and kaons at facilities such as AMBERS-CERN, EIC, and JLab, and provide estimates for eta-eta' and pi0-eta mixing angles that can be compared with experimental measurements.
