# Excited-state hadron masses using the stochastic LapH method

> Progress in computing the spectrum of excited baryons and mesons in lattice QCD is described. Large sets of spatially-extended hadron operators are used. The need for multi-hadron operators in addition to single-hadron operators is emphasized, necessitating the use of a new stochastic method of treating the low-lying modes of quark propagation which exploits Laplacian Heaviside quark-field smearing. A new glueball operator is tested and computing the mixing of this glueball operator with a quark-antiquark operator and multiple two-pion operators is shown to be feasible. Some of our initial results show warning signs about extracting high-lying resonance energies using only single-hadron operators.

# Excited-state hadron masses using the stochastic LapH method

## Key claim
The stochastic LapH method enables accurate estimation of temporal correlations for both single-hadron and multi-hadron operators in lattice QCD, but initial results show warning signs that extracting high-lying resonance energies using only single-hadron operators may be unreliable, particularly when multi-hadron thresholds lie below the resonance masses.

## Method
The authors introduce noise vectors only in the LapH subspace defined by the eigenvectors of the three-dimensional covariant Laplacian, using dilution projectors (time, spin, and LapH eigenvector dilution) to reduce variance. Quark lines are estimated stochastically by solving linear systems for diluted noise vectors and combining displaced-smeared-diluted quark source and sink vectors. Temporal correlation matrices of hadron operators are evaluated on anisotropic lattices using Markov-chain Monte Carlo integration of QCD path integrals.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass of about 390 MeV on a 24^3 × 128 anisotropic lattice for the isovector mass spectrum results
- Pion mass of about 240 MeV on a 24^3 × 128 lattice for the ρ-ππ mixing study using 584 configurations
- Pion mass of about 390 MeV on a 16^3 × 128 lattice for the scalar isoscalar 4×4 correlator study using 100 configurations
- Dilution scheme (TF, SF, LI8) used for forward-time quark lines; (TI16, SF, LI8) used for same-sink-time quark lines
- Nearly an order of magnitude reduction in statistical error over previous methods

## Context
This work extends the authors' ongoing program (Bulava et al., 2010; Morningstar et al., 2009) to compute finite-volume stationary-state energies of QCD using lattice methods. The stochastic LapH method improves upon earlier distillation approaches (Peardon et al., 2009) by enabling practical treatment of multi-hadron operators needed as pion masses become lighter. The results enable future full studies of the QCD stationary-state energy spectrum, including mixing between glueball, quark-antiquark, and multi-pion operators, which is necessary for reliable extraction of resonance parameters.
