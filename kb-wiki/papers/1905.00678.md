# Role of crustal physics in the tidal deformation of a neutron star

> In the late inspiral phase, gravitational waves from binary neutron star mergers carry the imprint of the equation of state due to the tidally deformed structure of the components. If the stars contain solid crusts, then their shear modulus can affect the deformability of the star and, thereby, modify the emitted signal. Here, we investigate the effect of realistic equations of state (EOSs) of the crustal matter, with a realistic model for the shear modulus of the stellar crust in a fully general relativistic framework. This allows us to systematically study the deviations that are expected from fluid models. In particular, we use unified EOSs, both relativistic and non-relativistic, in our calculations. We find that realistic EOSs of crusts cause a small correction, of $\sim 1\%$, in the second Love number. This correction will likely be subdominant to the statistical error expected in LIGO-Virgo observations at their respective advanced design sensitivities, but rival that error in third generation detectors. For completeness, we also study the effect of crustal shear on the magnetic-type Love number and find it to be much smaller.

## Key claim

Realistic equations of state for the crust, with a non-zero shear modulus, cause a small correction of approximately 1% in the second tidal Love number of a neutron star; this correction will likely be subdominant to the statistical error expected in LIGO-Virgo observations at their advanced design sensitivities, but could rival that error in third-generation detectors.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to generate equilibrium stellar configurations for six unified equations of state (three non-relativistic Skyrme-type and three relativistic mean-field). They then applied static linear perturbations in a fully general relativistic framework, using the formalism of Finn (1990) and Penner et al. (2011) to incorporate the elastic crust's shear modulus, and integrated the perturbed equations from the center to the surface to compute the electric (k2) and magnetic (j2) tidal Love numbers.

## Key numbers

- The correction to the second electric Love number (k2) due to crustal elasticity is approximately 1% across the considered equations of state.
- For non-relativistic EOSs, the percentage change in k2 ranges from about 0.1-0.4% for KDE0V1, 0.3-0.9% for SLy4, and 0.1-0.2% for SkI4, depending on stellar mass.
- The maximum neutron star masses for the EOSs used range from 1.97 solar masses (KDE0V1) to 2.77 solar masses (NL3).
- Crust-core transition densities range from 0.0359 fm^-3 (SkI4) to 0.0835 fm^-3 (NL3ωρ).
- Shear modulus at the crust-core transition ranges from 2.24 × 10^29 g cm^-1 s^-2 (SkI4) to 3.42 × 10^30 g cm^-1 s^-2 (NL3ωρ).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering general relativistic treatment of elastic crusts by Penner et al. (2011) and Finn (1990) by using unified equations of state where the crust and core are calculated within the same many-body theory, avoiding the uncertainties from thermodynamically inconsistent matching highlighted by Fortin (2016). The results show that crustal elasticity produces a correction too small to be detected by current LIGO-Virgo observations at advanced design sensitivity, but it may become relevant for third-generation detectors, motivating further study of crustal physics for future gravitational-wave astronomy.
