# Angular distribution of medium-induced QCD cascades

> We provide a complete description of the angular distribution of gluons in a medium-induced QCD cascade. We identify two components in the distribution, a soft component dominated by soft multiple scatterings, and a hard component dominated by a few hard scatterings. The typical angle that marks the boundary between these two components is determined analytically as a function of the energy of the observed gluon and the size of the medium. We construct the complete solution (beyond the diffusion approximation) in the regime where multiple branchings dominate the dynamics of the cascade in the form of a power series in the number of collisions with the medium particles. The coefficients of this expansions are related to the moments of the distribution in the diffusion approximation and are determined analytically. The angular distribution may be useful in phenomenological studies of jet shapes in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete analytic description of the angular distribution of gluons in a medium-induced QCD cascade, identifying two distinct components (soft and hard) separated by a characteristic angle that depends on the observed gluon energy and the medium size, but the solution is constructed in the regime where multiple branchings dominate and assumes that the medium length L is smaller than the stopping time t*.

## Method

The authors solve an integro-differential equation for the inclusive gluon distribution D(x,θ,t) that combines collinear branching (governed by the BDMPSZ mechanism with the LPM effect) and transverse momentum broadening from elastic collisions with medium constituents. They transform the transverse momentum distribution into an angular distribution using θ = k/(xE), treat the branching as collinear (angles preserved during splitting), and construct the solution in Fourier space using a Green's function approach. The analysis is performed in three distinct energy regimes: the leading particle regime (x ≈ 1), the primary radiation regime (x_s ≪ x ≪ 1), and the multiple branching regime (x ≪ x_s), with the soft component treated via a diffusion (Fokker-Planck) approximation and the hard component from single hard scatterings.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic energy scale for the onset of multiple branchings is ω_s = ᾱ² q̂ L², where ᾱ ≡ α_s N_c/π and q̂ is the jet-quenching parameter
- The stopping time is given by t* = 1/(ᾱ √(q̂/E)), and the energy E relates to it as E = ᾱ² q̂ t*²
- For the leading particle (x ≈ 1), the mean squared angle is ⟨θ²⟩ = q̂L/E² = θ_s²(1,L)
- For primary gluon radiation (x_s ≪ x ≪ 1), the mean squared angle is ⟨θ²⟩ = (1 + 1/x²) q̂L/(2E²)
- For the multiple branching regime (x ≪ x_s), the mean squared angle is ⟨θ²⟩ = (1/(4ᾱ)) √(q̂/(xE)³) ≡ θ*²(x), which is independent of the medium size L
- The typical branching time for a gluon with energy ω is τ_br(ω) = √(ω/q̂), with associated transverse momentum scale k_br(ω) = (ω q̂)^{1/4} and emission angle θ_br(ω) = (q̂/ω³)^{1/4}
- The maximum energy that can be taken by a single gluon is ω_c = q̂ L²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the first moment of the angular distribution (Blaizot 2014, Kurkela 2014) to provide a complete description of the full angular distribution. It builds on the probabilistic equation for in-medium QCD cascades derived by Blaizot (2013) and the BDMPSZ mechanism (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996). The results are intended for implementation in Monte Carlo event generators such as those developed by Schenke (2009), Zapp (2011), Renk (2008), and Armesto (2009), and may be useful for phenomenological studies of jet shapes in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, particularly for understanding the missing energy observed in unbalanced dijet events (Aad 2010, Chatrchyan 2011).
