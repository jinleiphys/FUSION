# Light-meson masses in an unquenched quark model

> We perform a coupled-channels calculation of the masses of light mesons with the quantum numbers $IJ^{P=-}$, $(I,J)=0,1$, by including $q\bar{q}$ and $(q\bar{q})^2$ components in a nonrelativistic chiral quark model. The coupling between two- and four-quark configurations is realized through a $^3P_0$ quark-pair creation model. With the usual form of this operator, the mass shifts are large and negative, an outcome which raises serious issues of validity for the quenched quark model. Herein, therefore, we introduce some improvements of the $^3P_0$ operator in order to reduce the size of the mass shifts. By introducing two simple factors, physically well motivated, the coupling between $q\bar{q}$ and $(q\bar{q})^2$ components is weakened, producing mass shifts that are around 10-20% of hadron bare masses.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard $^3P_0$ quark-pair creation model, when used to couple $q\bar{q}$ and $(q\bar{q})^2$ components in light mesons, produces mass shifts so large (around -2000 MeV) that they would invalidate the quenched quark model; however, by introducing two physically motivated modifications to the $^3P_0$ operator, the coupling is weakened and mass shifts are reduced to around 10-20% of hadron bare masses, preserving the phenomenological success of the quenched model.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channels calculation of light meson masses ($\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, $\eta$) by solving a SchrĂ¶dinger equation that includes both $q\bar{q}$ and $(q\bar{q})^2$ components within a nonrelativistic chiral quark model, using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) for the spatial wave functions. The coupling between two- and four-quark configurations was realized through a $^3P_0$ quark-pair creation operator, which was then modified in two ways: first, by introducing a Gaussian momentum cutoff factor $e^{-f^2 \mathbf{p}^2}$ to suppress high-momentum intermediate states, and second, by adding a spatial damping factor $e^{-R_{AV}^2/R_0^2}$ to reduce pair creation probability at large distances from the source hadron.

## Key numbers

- With the unmodified $^3P_0$ operator ($f \to 0$), the total mass shifts were: $\pi$: -2672.5 MeV; $\rho$: -1742.4 MeV; $\omega$: -1473.7 MeV; $\eta$: -1741.3 MeV (bare masses: $\pi$ 139.0 MeV, $\rho$ 772.7 MeV, $\omega$ 701.9 MeV, $\eta$ 669.5 MeV)
- With improvement two only ($f=0$, $\gamma=6.95$, $R_0=1$ fm), total mass shifts were reduced by roughly 50%: $\pi$: -927.5 MeV; $\rho$: -581.7 MeV; $\omega$: -504.9 MeV; $\eta$: -548.5 MeV
- With both improvements combined ($\gamma=6.95$, $R_0=1$ fm, $f=0.3$ fm), the $\pi$ mass shift from the $\pi\rho$ channel was only -7.5 MeV (from bare mass 139.0 MeV to 131.5 MeV)
- With both improvements combined ($f=0.5$ fm), the $\rho$ mass shift from the $\pi\pi$ channel was only -0.9 MeV (from bare mass 772.7 MeV to 771.8 MeV)
- The pattern of mass shifts across quark sectors: $b\bar{b}$ ~ -100 MeV (Liu 2011); $c\bar{c}$ ~ -(300-500) MeV (Barnes 2007, Ping 2012); $n\bar{n}$ ~ -2000 MeV with unmodified operator

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels approach previously applied to heavy quarkonia (charmonium and bottomonium) into the light meson sector, where the unquenching effects are found to be dramatically larger. The authors directly address the contradiction between the phenomenological success of the quenched quark model (Godfrey 1985, Vijande 2004) and the alarmingly large mass shifts found in earlier unquenched calculations using the standard $^3P_0$ model (Geiger 1989, Barnes 2007). The proposed modifications to the $^3P_0$ operator, motivated by quantum field theory treatments (Pichowsky 1999), enable a more realistic unquenching procedure that preserves the quenched model's successes while allowing for controlled coupled-channel effects, enabling future studies of light meson spectra and the nature of exotic states like the $X(3872)$.
