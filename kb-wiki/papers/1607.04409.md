# Polarized proton-deuteron scattering as a test of time-reversal invariance

> Scattering of protons with transversal polarization $p_y^p$ on deuterons with tensor polarization $P_{xz}$ provides a null-test signal for time-reversal (T) invariance violating but parity (P) conserving effects. We calculate the corresponding null-test observable at beam energies 100-1000 MeV within the spin-dependent Glauber theory considering T-violating P-conserving nucleon-nucleon interactions. The S-wave component of the deuteron wave function as well as the D-wave are taken into account and the latter is found to play an important role for the magnitude and the energy dependence of the observable in question. Specifically, with inclusion of the D wave the maximum of the obtained signal is shifted to higher beam energies, i.e. to 700-800 MeV.

## Key claim

The null-test observable for time-reversal violating but parity conserving effects in polarized proton-deuteron scattering is strongly modified by the deuteron D-wave component, shifting the maximum signal to higher beam energies of 700–800 MeV, whereas earlier S-wave-only calculations had indicated lower energies around 100 MeV as most favorable.

## Method

The authors used spin-dependent Glauber theory to calculate the forward elastic pd scattering amplitude, applying the generalized optical theorem to obtain the total cross section null-test observable. They considered T-violating P-conserving nucleon-nucleon interactions of the h-type (axial-vector meson h1 exchange) and g-type (constant amplitude), including both S-wave and D-wave components of the deuteron wave function from the CD Bonn and Paris NN potentials. The double-scattering mechanism was evaluated, while the single-scattering mechanism was found to give zero contribution.

## Key numbers

* The null-test signal maximum shifts to beam energies of 700–800 MeV when the D-wave is included, compared to the S-wave-only calculation.
* The calculation covers the proton beam energy range 100–1000 MeV.
* The S-D interference term drastically reduces the signal at energies around 100 MeV, the region of the planned COSY experiment at 135 MeV.
* A zero crossing of the signal occurs below 400 MeV beam energy.
* The pure D-wave contribution (last three terms in the amplitude expression) is negligible, consistent with earlier assumptions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Uzikov and Haidenbauer, 2015) that used only the S-wave component of the deuteron and found lower energies around 100 MeV preferable for a TVPC signal. It also goes beyond the earlier single-scattering approximation by Beyer (1993) and the Faddeev calculations for neutron-deuteron scattering at 100 keV by Lazauskas (2009). The results suggest that experiments at 700–800 MeV could be more promising for detecting a signal, and that measurements at two or more energies are advisable to avoid misinterpretation due to the zero crossing.
