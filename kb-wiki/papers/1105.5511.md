# Gluon spectrum in the glasma from JIMWLK evolution

> The JIMWLK equation with a "daughter dipole" running coupling is solved numerically starting from an initial condition given by the McLerran-Venugopalan model. The resulting Wilson line configurations are then used to compute the spectrum of gluons comprising the glasma inital state of a high energy heavy ion collision. The development of a geometrical scaling region makes the spectrum of produced gluons harder. Thus the ratio of the mean gluon transverse momentum to the saturation scale grows with energy. Also the total gluon multiplicity increases with energy slightly faster than the saturation scale squared.

## Key claim

The JIMWLK evolution with a "daughter dipole" running coupling makes the gluon spectrum in the glasma harder than the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model initial condition, causing the ratio of mean gluon transverse momentum to the saturation scale to grow with energy; however, the numerical results show that the increase in this ratio appears to saturate after about 3 units of rapidity, and the produced gluon spectrum is more sensitive to the lattice ultraviolet cutoff than the JIMWLK evolution itself.

## Method

The JIMWLK equation was solved numerically using a Langevin method for the rapidity evolution of Wilson lines, with a "daughter dipole" running coupling prescription where the coupling depends only on the size of the daughter dipole. The resulting Wilson line configurations from two independent evolutions (representing the two colliding nuclei) were used as initial conditions for solving the Classical Yang-Mills (CYM) equations of motion for the glasma fields. At proper time τ = 12/Qs_adj, the fields were Fourier-decomposed into transverse momentum modes to determine the gluon spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The evolution speed λ = d ln Qs²(y)/dy is shown as a function of Qs/Λ; the phenomenologically preferred evolution speed λ ≲ 0.3 is only reached with an initial saturation scale higher than estimates corresponding to RHIC energy (where Qs_adj ≈ 1.1 GeV, corresponding to Qs/Λ ≈ 7.6)
- The mean transverse momentum of gluons in the glasma, scaled by Qs, increases from approximately 1 for the MV model initial condition to around 1.5 after JIMWLK evolution
- The gluon liberation coefficient c (proportional to total gluon multiplicity) increases with rapidity evolution, with new gluons added for pT ≳ 2 Qs_adj while the shape for pT ≲ Qs_adj changes very little
- The Langevin step size used was dy = 0.0001 π²/α₀, with α₀ = 0.762 for μ₀/Λ = 2.5 and α₀ = 0.434 for μ₀/Λ = 5
- The "base" configuration used N⊥ = 1024 lattice points, Qs0_adj L = 68, μ₀ L = 15, and Λ L = 6

## Context

This work extends previous numerical CYM calculations (Krasnitz 1998, Krasnitz 2001, Krasnitz 2003, Lappi 2003) that used Wilson lines from the MV model, by instead using the full solution of the JIMWLK equation. It also goes beyond kT-factorized calculations of gluon production (Gelis 2006, Albacete 2007, Dusling 2009, Albacete 2010) that used the BK equation but do not correctly give the initial gluon spectrum for collisions of two dense systems (the "AA" case). The results enable future calculations of long-range rapidity correlations (the "ridge") in the glasma, following the work of Dumitru (2008), Dusling (2009), and Dumitru (2010).
