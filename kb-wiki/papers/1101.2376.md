# Three-body structure of low-lying 18Ne states

> We investigate to what extent 18Ne can be descibed as a three-body system made of an inert 16O-core and two protons. We compare to experimental data and occasionally to shell model results. We obtain three-body wave functions with the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method. We study the spectrum of 18Ne, the structure of the different states and the predominant transition strengths. Two 0+, two 2+, and one 4+ bound states are found where they are all known experimentally. Also one 3+ close to threshold is found and several negative parity states, 1-, 3-, 0-, 2-, most of them bound with respect to the 16O excited 3- state. The structures are extracted as partial wave components, as spatial sizes of matter and charge, and as probability distributions. Electromagnetic decay rates are calculated for these states. The dominating decay mode for the bound states is E2 and occasionally also M1.

## Key claim

The paper investigates to what extent the low-lying states of \(^{18}\)Ne can be described as a three-body system of an inert \(^{16}\)O core and two protons, finding that while several bound states are reproduced, the description is limited by the need to include core-excited configurations (specifically the \(3^{-}\) state of \(^{16}\)O) to account for negative parity states, and that the simple three-body picture may not fully capture the structure of all states.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in coordinate space using the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method, with the three-body wave functions expanded in hyperspherical harmonics. Two-body interactions were parametrized to reproduce low-energy scattering properties: a Gaussian nucleon-nucleon potential for the proton-proton interaction, and Gaussian central, spin-orbit, and spin-spin potentials for the proton-\(^{16}\)O interaction, with strengths adjusted to reproduce the bound state energies of \(^{17}\)F (the \(1/2^{+}\) state at \(-0.105\) MeV and the \(5/2^{+}\) state at \(-0.600\) MeV). Four different interaction sets (I–IV) were used, with potentials I–III based on the \(0^{+}\) ground state of \(^{16}\)O and potential IV based on the \(3^{-}\) excited state of \(^{16}\)O at 6.13 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The \(^{17}\)F two-body bound states used to calibrate the proton-core potential are the \(1/2^{+}\) state at \(-0.105\) MeV and the \(5/2^{+}\) state at \(-0.600\) MeV relative to the two-body threshold.
- The \(3^{-}\) excited state of the \(^{16}\)O core lies at 6.13 MeV above the ground state.
- The \(p\)-wave resonances in \(^{17}\)F used for potential II are at 2.504 MeV (\(1/2^{-}\)) and 4.04 MeV (\(3/2^{-}\)) above threshold.
- The \(5/2^{-}\) resonance in the \(^{17}\)F system (for potential IV) lies at 3.257 MeV above threshold.
- The range of the Gaussian proton-core potentials is \(b = 2.60\) fm in all cases.
- The three lowest adiabatic potentials for states built on the \(3^{-}\) core approach \(-3.6\) MeV, \(-2.9\) MeV, and \(-2.1\) MeV at large distances.

## Context

This work extends the three-body cluster model approach previously applied to Borromean systems such as \(^{6}\)He, \(^{6}\)Be, \(^{11}\)Li, and \(^{17}\)Ne (Garrido et al., 2001, 2003, 2006) to the \(^{18}\)Ne nucleus, which is not Borromean. The study is motivated by recent measurements of the three-body decay of the 6.15 MeV \(1^{-}\) state in \(^{18}\)Ne (Gomez del Campo et al., 2001; Raskin et al., 2008) and the astrophysical importance of the \(^{17}\)F\((p,\gamma)^{18}\)Ne reaction (Chipps et al., 2009). The results provide a necessary prerequisite for describing three-body decaying resonances and complement shell model calculations (Brown et al., 2002; Sherr et al., 1998) which cannot accurately describe large-distance cluster structures.
