# Dipole response in deformed halo nuclei $^{42}\mathrm{Mg}$ and $^{44}\mathrm{Mg}$

> The quasiparticle finite amplitude method based on the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum has been developed for the noncharge-exchange multipole response. Taking neutron-rich magnesium isotopes as examples, the isovector electric dipole response, especially in the low-lying region, is studied. It is found that the low-energy dipole strength increases with neutron number and becomes notably enhanced in the predicted deformed halo nuclei $^{42}\mathrm{Mg}$ and $^{44}\mathrm{Mg}$. In these isotopes, the $K^π=1^-$ states below 3 MeV are dominated by transitions from the ``halo" part of the single-neutron orbitals. Their transition densities reveal a low-frequency, out-of-phase oscillation between the neutron halo and the core. These results provide a microscopic picture for the soft dipole resonance in $^{42}\mathrm{Mg}$ and $^{44}\mathrm{Mg}$.

## Key claim

The low-energy dipole strength in the deformed halo nuclei 42Mg and 44Mg is notably enhanced compared to lighter isotopes, and the Kπ=1- states below 3 MeV in these nuclei are dominated by transitions from the "halo" part of single-neutron orbitals, providing a microscopic picture for the soft dipole resonance. The abstract's claim of "notably enhanced" strength is qualified by the finding that this enhancement is specific to the Kπ=1- component, not the Kπ=0- component, and that the enhancement appears as additional peaks around 2.5 MeV in 42Mg and 2.3 MeV in 44Mg.

## Method

The authors developed the quasiparticle finite amplitude method (QFAM) based on the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) by explicitly linearizing the Dirac Hamiltonian and pairing potential. They applied this method to calculate the isovector electric dipole strength functions in neutron-rich even-even magnesium isotopes 28-44Mg, using the PC-PK1 relativistic density functional in the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force with strength V0 = -325 MeV·fm3. The induced densities and potentials were expanded using scalar and vector spherical harmonics without symmetry assumptions, and the linear response equations were solved iteratively at complex frequencies with a Lorentzian smearing width.

## Key numbers

* The pairing strength used in the particle-particle channel is V0 = -325 MeV·fm3 with saturation density ρsat = 0.152 fm-3 and a pairing window of 100 MeV.
* The energy cutoff for the Dirac Woods-Saxon basis in the Fermi sea is E+cut = 150 MeV, with angular momentum cutoff Jcut = 23/2 ℏ and Legendre expansion truncation λmax = 6.
* In 42Mg, the gap between the 5/2- level at -2.45 MeV and the 1/2- level at -0.44 MeV is 2.01 MeV; in 44Mg, the gap between the 5/2- level at -2.85 MeV and the 1/2- level at -0.91 MeV is 1.94 MeV.
* The Kπ=1- peak in 42Mg is contributed by two states at 2.50 MeV and 2.56 MeV; in 44Mg, the peak is contributed by two states at 2.30 MeV and 2.39 MeV.
* The state at 2.50 MeV in 42Mg is 59.7% dominated by transitions from the 1/2- single-neutron level at -0.44 MeV; the state at 2.56 MeV is 65.2% dominated by transitions from the 3/2- level at -0.22 MeV.
* The state at 2.30 MeV in 44Mg is 59.5% dominated by transitions from the 1/2- level at -0.91 MeV; the state at 2.39 MeV is 68.5% dominated by transitions from the 3/2- level at -0.86 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite amplitude method (FAM) framework, previously implemented for nonrelativistic DFTs by Inakura (2009) and Avogadro (2011) and for relativistic DFTs by Liang (2013) and Niksic (2013), to the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) for multipole excitations. The study builds on prior DRHBc predictions of deformed halos in 42Mg and 44Mg by Zhou (2010), Li (2012), and Zhang (2019), and provides the first microscopic description of the soft dipole resonance in these nuclei. The results enable future investigations of collective excitations in exotic deformed nuclei with large spatial extensions, and the comparison with calculations based on SkM* functional by Yoshida (2009) and extended SLy4 functional by Wang (2017) suggests sensitivity to the symmetry energy slope parameter L.
