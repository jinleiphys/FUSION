# Scalar Mesons a0(1450) and sigma(600) from Lattice QCD

> We study the a0 and sigma mesons with the overlap fermion in the chiral regime with the pion mass as low as 182 MeV in the quenched approximation. After the eta'pi ghost states are separated, we find that the a0 mass with q\bar{q} interpolation field to be almost independent of the quark mass in the region below the strange quark mass. The chirally extrapolated results are consistent with a0(1450) being the u\bar{d} meson and K0*(1430) being the u\bar{s} meson with calculated masses at 1.42+_0.13 GeV and 1.41+_ 0.12 GeV respectively. We also calculate the scalar mesonium with a tetraquark interpolation field. In addition to the two pion scattering states, we find a state at around 550 MeV. Through the study of volume dependence, we confirm that this state is a one-particle state, in contrast to the two-pion scattering states. This suggests that the observed state is a tetraquark mesonium which is quite possibly the sigma(600) meson.

# Scalar Mesons a0(1450) and sigma(600) from Lattice QCD

## Key claim
The a0(1450) meson is identified as the u\bar{d} q\bar{q} state, with its mass showing almost no dependence on quark mass below the strange quark mass region; the sigma(600) meson is identified as a tetraquark mesonium state at approximately 550 MeV, distinguished from two-pion scattering states through volume dependence analysis, but these results are obtained in the quenched approximation and rely on a sequential empirical Bayes method to separate ghost states.

## Method
The authors performed quenched lattice QCD calculations using overlap fermions on 16^3 x 28 and 12^3 x 28 lattices with Iwasaki gauge action at beta = 2.264 and lattice spacing a = 0.200(3) fm, with pion masses as low as 182 MeV. For the a0 meson, they used a q\bar{q} interpolation field and applied a sequential empirical Bayes method to fit multiple states including the eta' pi ghost states that contaminate the correlator. For the sigma meson, they used a tetraquark interpolation field (pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar operator) and studied the volume dependence of spectral weights to distinguish one-particle bound states from two-pion scattering states.

## Key numbers
- a0(1450) mass from chiral extrapolation: 1.42 +/- 0.13 GeV
- K0*(1430) mass from chiral extrapolation: 1.41 +/- 0.12 GeV
- Scalar \bar{s}s state mass: 1.46 +/- 0.05 GeV
- Lowest pion mass achieved: 182(8) MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.200(3) fm (from f_pi)
- Lattice sizes: 3.2 fm (16^3) and 2.4 fm (12^3)
- Spectral weight ratio upper limit for a0(980) relative to a0(1450): less than 0.015
- Sigma candidate state found at approximately 550 MeV
- Lowest pi-pi scattering state lies approximately 100(20) to 60(10) MeV below the pi-pi threshold for the pion mass range 182-250 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier quenched lattice calculations of the a0 meson by Lee and Weingarten (2000), Bardeen et al. (2002), and Prelovsek (2003), and dynamical fermion calculations by Burch et al. (2001), by reaching substantially lower pion masses (182 MeV versus previous higher values). The study also builds on the tetraquark mesonium calculation by Alford and Jaffe (2000) for the sigma, using improved fitting methods developed for the Roper resonance and pentaquark studies by Mathur et al. (2005). The results challenge conventional quark model expectations for scalar meson spectroscopy, particularly the near-degeneracy of a0(1450) and K0*(1430) despite the latter containing a strange quark, and enable future lattice studies of the sigma meson width through volume variation and Luscher's method.
