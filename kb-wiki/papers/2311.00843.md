# Compiled Properties of Nucleonic Matter and Nuclear and Neutron Star Models from Non-Relativistic and Relativistic Interactions

> This paper compiles the model parameters and zero-temperature properties of an extensive collection of published theoretical nuclear interactions, including 255 non-relativistic (Skyrme-like) forces, 270 relativistic mean field (RMF) and point-coupling (RMF-PC) forces, and 13 Gogny-like forces. This forms the most exhaustive tabulation of model parameters to date. The properties of uniform symmetric matter and pure neutron matter at the saturation density are determined. Symmetry properties found from the second-order term of a Taylor expansion in neutron excess are compared to the energy difference of pure neutron and symmetric nuclear matter at the saturation density. Selected liquid-droplet model parameters, including the surface tension and surface symmetry energy, are determined for semi-infinite surfaces. Theoretical liquid droplet model neutron skin thicknesses of the neutron-rich closed-shell nuclei $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb are compared to published theoretical Hartree-Fock and experimental results. A similar comparison is made between theoretical liquid-droplet and experimental values of dipole polarizabilities. In addition, radii, binding energies, moments of inertia and tidal deformabilities of 1.2, 1.4 and 1.6 M$_\odot$ neutron stars are computed. An extensive correlation analysis of bulk matter, nuclear structure, and low-mass neutron star properties is performed and compared to nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations.

## Key claim

This paper compiles the model parameters and zero-temperature properties of 255 non-relativistic (Skyrme-like) forces, 270 relativistic mean field (RMF) and point-coupling (RMF-PC) forces, and 13 Gogny-like forces, forming the most exhaustive tabulation of model parameters to date; however, the authors note that some recent RMF forces were omitted because they do not fit into the parameterization schemes of prior compilations, and these will be included in future work.

## Method

The authors collected published theoretical nuclear interactions from the literature, including forces previously tabulated by Dutra et al. (2012, 2014) as well as additional forces, while removing duplicates and correcting typos. For each interaction, they computed the properties of uniform symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter at the saturation density, including energy per particle, pressure, incompressibility, and skewness, using the standard Skyrme and RMF energy density functionals. They also determined symmetry energy parameters from both the energy difference of pure neutron and symmetric matter and from the second-order term of a Taylor expansion in neutron excess, and computed liquid-droplet model parameters for semi-infinite surfaces, neutron skin thicknesses for ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb, dipole polarizabilities, and neutron star properties for masses of 1.2, 1.4, and 1.6 M⊙.

## Key numbers

- 255 non-relativistic (Skyrme-like) forces, 270 relativistic mean field (RMF) and point-coupling (RMF-PC) forces, and 13 Gogny-like forces were compiled.
- Neutron star properties were computed for masses of 1.2, 1.4, and 1.6 M⊙, including radii, binding energies, moments of inertia, and tidal deformabilities.
- The saturation density n₀ is approximately 0.16 fm⁻³, used as the reference density for computing bulk matter properties.
- Theoretical liquid-droplet model neutron skin thicknesses were compared for the closed-shell nuclei ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb against published Hartree-Fock and experimental results.

## Context

This work extends the compilations of Dutra et al. (2012) for 240 Skyrme-like interactions and Dutra et al. (2014) for 263 RMF interactions by adding new forces, removing duplicates, correcting typos, and tabulating interaction parameters that were not included in those earlier works. The database of parameters and computed properties will enable future studies of nuclear structure and neutron star astrophysics without requiring researchers to peruse hundreds of individual publications for this information.
