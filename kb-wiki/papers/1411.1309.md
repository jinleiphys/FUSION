# Analogies between nuclear physics and Dark Matter

> A fermionic description of dark matter using analogies with nuclear physics is developed. At tree level, scalar and vector processes are considered and the two-body potential are explicitly calculated using the Breit approximation. We show that the total cross sections in both cases exhibit Sommerfeld enhancement.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that fermionic dark matter can be described using analogies with nuclear physics, specifically by treating neutrinos as cold dark matter that are the non-relativistic analogue of nucleons. The authors show that total cross sections for both scalar and vector boson exchange exhibit Sommerfeld enhancement, but the hedge is that this is an effective description valid only at very low energies and tree level, where issues of renormalizability and unitarity are circumvented.

## Method

The authors use the Breit approximation to compute two-body potentials for fermionic dark matter interacting via massive scalar or vector boson exchange at tree level, working to order 1/c^2 in the low-energy limit of the Dirac equation. They then solve the radial Schrodinger equation for these potentials to compute the Sommerfeld enhancement factor S_k = |(dχ(0)/dr)/k|^2. For the Yukawa potential with spin-spin coupling, they diagonalize the spin operator to separate singlet and triplet states, while for the exponential potential they transform the radial equation into a Bessel equation.

## Key numbers

- The two-body potential for vector boson exchange contains a central term V_c(r) = [1 + Q_c^2/(8c^2)(1/m_1^2 + 1/m_2^2)] e^{-Q_c r}/(4π r) - (1/(8c^2))(1/m_1^2 + 1/m_2^2) δ(x)
- The spin-spin term in the vector boson potential is V_1(r) O_1 = 1/(4m_1 m_2 c^2) [δ(x)(-1 + 4π/3) + e^{-Q_c r}/(4π r)(Q_c^2 + Q_c/r + 1/r^2)] σ_1 · σ_2
- For the exponential potential V(r) = V_0 e^{-Q_c r}, the Sommerfeld enhancement factor becomes S_k = (2 β^2 α)/(π k^2) e^{π k/β} sin^2(α - π/4), where α = (2/Q_c)√(2μ V_0) and β = Q_c/2
- The Sommerfeld enhancement for vector boson exchange is S = (1/4)S_s + (3/4)S_t, where S_s and S_t correspond to singlet and triplet states with coupling strengths (a - 3b) and (a + b) respectively

## Context

This work extends the Sommerfeld enhancement mechanism proposed by Arkani-Hamed et al. (2009) for dark matter annihilation, which was developed to explain the 511 keV gamma ray line observed from the galactic center. The authors draw analogies with nuclear physics phenomenology, specifically the non-relativistic nucleon-nucleon potential formalism from Bertulani (2007), and apply it to cold dark matter neutrinos. The paper enables future work on using nuclear physics potentials as effective descriptions for dark matter interactions, particularly for modeling enhancement processes in dark matter annihilation.
