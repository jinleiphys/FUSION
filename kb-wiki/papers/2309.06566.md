# Thermodynamics of the parity-doublet model: Symmetric nuclear matter and the chiral transition

> We present a detailed discussion of the thermodynamics of the parity-doublet nucleon-meson model within a mean-field theory, at finite temperature and baryon-chemical potential, with special emphasis on the chiral transition at large baryon densities and vanishing temperature. We consider isospin-symmetric matter. We systematically compare the parity-doublet model to a related singlet model obtained by disregarding the chiral partner of the nucleon. After studying the ground state properties of nuclear matter, the nuclear liquid-gas transition, and the density modifications of the nucleon sigma term which govern the low-density regime, we give new insight into the underlying mechanisms of the zero-temperature chiral transition occurring at several times the nuclear saturation density. We show that the chiral transition is driven by a kind of symmetry energy that tends to equilibrate the populations of opposite parity baryons. This symmetry energy dictates the composition of matter at large baryon densities, once the phase space for the appearance of the negative-parity partner is opened. We furthermore highlight the characteristic role, within the thermodynamics, of the chiral-invariant mass of the parity-doublet model. We include the chiral limit into all of our discussions in order to provide a complete picture of the chiral transition.

## Key claim

The zero-temperature chiral transition in the parity-doublet model is driven by a symmetry energy that equilibrates the populations of positive- and negative-parity baryons, rather than by the vanishing of the scalar field condensate alone; this mechanism only becomes operative once the phase space for the negative-parity partner opens at several times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors study the parity-doublet nucleon-meson model at mean-field level, treating the meson fields classically and computing the fermion determinant at one-loop order, for isospin-symmetric matter at finite temperature and baryon-chemical potential. They systematically compare results with a singlet model obtained by removing the negative-parity baryon, and include the chiral limit by setting the explicit symmetry-breaking term to zero while keeping all other parameters fixed.

## Context

This work extends the parity-doublet model studies of Hatsuda (1989), Zschiesche (2006), and Dexheimer (2008) by providing a detailed thermodynamic analysis of the chiral transition mechanism. It contrasts with the chiral nucleon-meson model of Brandes (2021) and the functional renormalisation group approach of Weyrich (2015), showing that the parity-doublet model predicts chiral symmetry restoration only at densities at least ten times nuclear saturation density. The results enable future extension to isospin-asymmetric neutron matter, announced in a forthcoming publication by the same authors.
