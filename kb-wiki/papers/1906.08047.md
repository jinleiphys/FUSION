# Possible New Phase of Thermal QCD

> Using lattice simulations, we show that there is a phase of thermal QCD, where the spectral density $ρ(λ)$ of Dirac operator changes as $1/λ$ for the infrared eigenvalues $λ<T$. This behavior persists over the entire low energy band we can resolve accurately, over three orders of magnitude on our largest volumes. We propose that in this "IR phase", the well-known non-interacting scale invariance at very short distances (UV, $λ\rightarrow \infty$, asymptotic freedom), coexists with very different interacting type of scale invariance at long distances (IR, $λ<T$). Such dynamics may be responsible for the unusual fluidity properties of the medium observed at RHIC and LHC. We point out its connection to the physics of Banks-Zaks fixed point, leading to the possibility of massless glueballs in the fluid. Our results lead to the classification of thermal QCD phases in terms of IR scale invariance. The ensuing picture naturally subsumes the standard chiral crossover feature at $"\!T_c\!" \,\approx 155$ MeV. Its crucial new aspect is the existence of temperature $T_{IR}$ (200 MeV $< T_{IR} < $ 250 MeV) marking the onset of IR phase and possibly a true phase transition.

## Key claim

Using lattice simulations, the authors propose that thermal QCD possesses a new "IR phase" between approximately 200 MeV and 250 MeV where the Dirac spectral density follows a \(1/\lambda\) power law for infrared eigenvalues \(\lambda < T\), coexisting with the usual ultraviolet scale invariance from asymptotic freedom. The claim is hedged by noting that the IR phase may only be detectable on sufficiently large spatial volumes (\(L > L_{\text{IR}}(T)\)) that are not commonly studied, and that the strict \(1/\lambda\) behavior may actually be \(\rho(\lambda) \propto 1/\lambda^{1-\delta}\) with \(0 < \delta \ll 1\) to avoid integrability issues.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of both pure-glue SU(3) gauge theory (pgQCD) and QCD with \(N_f = 2+1\) quark flavors at physical masses, using the overlap Dirac operator to compute the spectral density \(\rho(\lambda)\) of Dirac eigenvalues. They analyzed the integrated spectral density \(\sigma(\lambda, T) = \int_\lambda^T \rho(\omega) d\omega\) as a function of \(x = \ln(T/\lambda)\) to test for the logarithmic dependence characteristic of \(\rho(\lambda) \propto 1/\lambda\), examining volumes up to \(L = 5.4\) fm in pgQCD and comparing temperatures from below 150 MeV to above 250 MeV.

## Key numbers

- In pgQCD at \(T = 1.12\,T_c\) on the largest volume (\(L = 5.4\) fm), the \(1/\lambda\) behavior persists over 3.5 orders of magnitude from \(T\) down to deep infrared, with fit parameter \(c(1.12\,T_c) = 0.0308(3)\,\text{fm}^{-4}\).
- The IR phase onset temperature in QCD is bounded as \(200\,\text{MeV} < T_{\text{IR}} < 250\,\text{MeV}\).
- The crossover temperature \(T_{\text{cro}} \approx 150\,\text{MeV}\) is associated with the standard chiral crossover at \(``T_c" \approx 155\) MeV.
- In pgQCD at \(T = 0.98\,T_c\) (below the transition), the spectral density shows a minimum at \(\lambda_m \approx 120\) MeV, with no linear segment in \(\sigma(x)\).
- The UV cutoff in the pgQCD simulations was \(a = 0.085\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of chiral polarization transitions in QCD (Alexandru and Horvath, 2012, 2014, 2015) and proposes a unified phase structure for SU(3) gauge theories based on the infrared power \(p\) of the Dirac spectral density (\(\rho(\lambda) \propto \lambda^p\)). It connects the observed IR scale invariance to the physics of the Banks-Zaks fixed point (Banks and Zaks, 1981) and the conformal window, suggesting that the near-perfect fluid observed at RHIC and LHC may be realized by this IR phase rather than by the standard chiral crossover alone. The work enables future large-scale lattice studies to confirm the \(1/\lambda\) behavior in QCD and to explore the proposed contiguous IR phase across the space of temperature, quark mass, and number of flavors.
