# Energy dependence of transverse momentum fluctuations in Au+Au collisions from a multiphase transport model

> Event-by-event mean transverse momentum fluctuations ($\langle p_\mathrm{T}\rangle$) serve as a sensitive probe of initial state overlap geometry and energy density fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We present a systematic investigation of $\langle p_\mathrm{T}\rangle$ fluctuations in \auau collisions at $\mathrm{\sqrt{s_{NN}}} =$3.0-19.6 GeV, examining their centrality and energy dependence with the framework of an improved multiphase transport (AMPT) model. The centrality dependence of the $p_\mathrm{T}$ cumulants up to fourth order deviates significantly from simple powering-law scaling. Scaled cumulants are performed, with variances aligning well with the trends observed in the experimental data. Employing a two-subevent method, short-range correlations are slightly suppressed compared to the standard approach. Furthermore, baryons exhibit more pronounced $\langle p_\mathrm{T}\rangle$ fluctuations than mesons, potentially attributable to the effect of radial flow. These results provide referenced insights into the role of initial state fluctuations across different energies in heavy-ion collisions.

# Energy dependence of transverse momentum fluctuations in Au+Au collisions from a multiphase transport model

## Key claim
The centrality dependence of transverse momentum cumulants up to fourth order deviates significantly from simple power-law scaling in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0–19.6 GeV, and the improved AMPT model with a dynamically tuned Lund fragmentation parameter b_L can reproduce the observed trends, though scaled variances systematically overestimate STAR data in peripheral to mid-central collisions.

## Method
The authors used the improved AMPT model (default version, v1.26t9b-v2.26t9b) with QCD coupling constant alpha_s = 0.33 and screening mass mu = 2.265 fm^-1, corresponding to a parton scattering cross section of 3.0 mb in the ZPC. The Lund string fragmentation parameter b_L was tuned to vary linearly with impact parameter and to increase slightly with collision energy, enabling the model to reproduce the centrality and energy dependence of mean transverse momentum. Transverse momentum cumulants up to fourth order were calculated using both standard and two-subevent methods for charged pions, kaons, and protons within |eta| < 1.0 and 0.5 <= p_T <= 3.0 GeV/c across nine centrality classes from 0–5% to 70–80%.

## Key numbers
- Scaled variance k_2 for 0–5% central collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, and 14.5 GeV shows qualitative agreement with STAR experimental data (STAR, 2019) within |eta| < 0.5
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0 GeV in |eta| < 0.5 with 0.2 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c, k_2 exhibits a significant abnormal increase compared to values at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV
- The two-subevent method with Delta_eta = 0.4 yields slightly suppressed scaled variances compared to the standard method across all energies from 7.7 to 19.6 GeV
- Baryons show more pronounced fluctuations in scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis than mesons at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV

## Context
This work extends prior studies of mean transverse momentum fluctuations from the STAR Beam Energy Scan program (STAR, 2019) and higher-order fluctuations measured at LHC by ALICE (2023) and ATLAS (2024). It improves upon earlier AMPT calculations (Zhang, 2021) that could not reproduce the centrality dependence of mean p_T, and provides model predictions for the RHIC fixed-target program and future FAIR-CBM experiments. The results enable comparison between different hadronization mechanisms (Lund string fragmentation versus quark coalescence) and their effects on fluctuation observables across collision energies.
