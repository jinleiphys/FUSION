# Effective Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction and Fermi Liquid Theory

> We present two novel relations between the quasiparticle interaction in nuclear matter and the unique low momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction in vacuum. These relations provide two independent constraints on the Fermi liquid parameters of nuclear matter. Moreover, the new constraints define two combinations of Fermi liquid parameters, which are invariant under the renormalization group flow in the particle-hole channels. Using empirical values for the spin-independent Fermi liquid parameters, we are able to compute the major spin-dependent ones by imposing the new constraints as well as the Pauli principle sum rules.

# Effective Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction and Fermi Liquid Theory

## Key claim
The authors derive two novel constraints linking the quasiparticle interaction in nuclear matter to the unique low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction in vacuum, but these constraints rely on approximating the driving term of the induced interaction by the square of the quasiparticle strength times the low-momentum potential at a density-dependent cutoff equal to the Fermi momentum, while neglecting self-energy insertions and center-of-mass momentum dependence.

## Method
The authors take the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V_low_k, constructed by integrating out relative momenta above a cutoff in the renormalization group sense, and use it as the driving term in the Babu-Brown induced interaction framework for Fermi liquid theory. They solve the integral equations for the quasiparticle scattering amplitude and induced interaction simultaneously in the limit of equal incoming and outgoing momenta on the Fermi surface, which yields two algebraic constraints relating the Fermi liquid parameters to the s-wave matrix elements of V_low_k at zero momentum and cutoff equal to the Fermi momentum. The spin-independent Fermi liquid parameters are taken from phenomenology, and the spin-dependent ones are computed by imposing the new constraints together with the Pauli principle sum rules.

## Key numbers
- Spin-independent Fermi liquid parameters extracted from phenomenology: F_0 = -0.27, F_1 = -0.85, F_0' = 0.71, F_1' = 0.14, with quasiparticle strength z = 0.8.
- Computed spin-dependent Fermi liquid parameters (without tensor interactions): G_0 = 0.15 ± 0.3, G_1 = 0.45 ± 0.3, G_0' = 1.0 ± 0.2, G_1' = 0 ± 0.2.
- The value G_0' = 1.0 matches the experimental constraint from the Gamow-Teller resonance after correcting for Delta-hole polarization.
- The low-momentum interaction V_low_k is evaluated at cutoff Λ = 2.0 fm^{-1} for the comparison of different bare potentials, and at Λ = k_F for the Fermi liquid constraints.

## Context
This work extends the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction approach of Bogner et al. (2001) by applying it as dynamical input to Fermi liquid theory, building on the induced interaction formalism of Babu and Brown (1973). The constraints provide a microscopic connection between vacuum nucleon-nucleon interactions and in-medium Fermi liquid parameters, enabling the calculation of spin-dependent parameters from empirical spin-independent ones. The results are relevant for computing superfluid gaps and transport processes in neutron star interiors, and the authors note that a full solution of the flow equations as presented by Metzner for the Hubbard model would extend the method to non-forward scattering.
