# Bulk viscous corrections to screening and damping in the deconfined phase at high temperature

> Non-equilibrium corrections in a hot QCD medium modify the "hard thermal loops" (HTL) which determine the resummed propagators for gluons with soft momenta as well as the Debye screening and Landau damping mass scales. We focus on bulk viscous corrections to a thermal fixed point. The screening and damping mass scales are sensitive to the bulk pressure and perhaps to (pseudo-) critical dynamical scaling of the bulk viscosity in the vicinity of a second-order critical point. This would affect the properties of quarkonium bound states in the deconfined phase.

# Bulk viscous corrections to screening and damping in the deconfined phase at high temperature

## Key claim
Bulk viscous corrections to the hard thermal loops (HTL) in a hot QCD medium modify the Debye screening and Landau damping mass scales, which in turn affect the heavy-quark (quarkonium) potential; however, the analysis is a weak-coupling HTL computation that does not apply at temperatures near $T_c$ and only provides baseline expectations for bulk-viscous effects from resummed weakly coupled QCD.

## Method
The author computes the gluon self energy in the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation using a non-equilibrium phase-space distribution that includes an isotropic bulk-viscous correction of the form $\delta_{\rm bulk} f(k) = (k/T)^a \Phi f_{\rm id}(k)(1\pm f_{\rm id}(k))$, where $\Phi$ is proportional to the bulk pressure. The retarded and symmetric gluon self energies are derived from real-time thermal field theory, and the static heavy-quark potential is obtained via the Fourier transform of the Schwinger-Keldysh component of the longitudinal gluon propagator in the static limit.

## Key numbers
- The equilibrium Debye mass squared $m_R^2 = (2N_c+N_f) g^2 T^2 / 6$ shifts to $m_R^2 + \delta m_R^2 = (2N_c(1+c_R^{(g)}(a)\Phi) + N_f(1+c_R^{(q)}(a)\Phi)) g^2 T^2 / 6$, with coefficients $c_R^{(q)}(a) = 2(1-2^{-a}) c_R^{(g)}(a) = (12/\pi^2)(1-2^{-a}) \Gamma(2+a) \zeta(1+a)$.
- The equilibrium symmetric mass squared $m_F^2 = (2N_c+N_f) g^2 T^2 / 6$ shifts to $m_F^2 + \delta m_F^2 = (2N_c(1+c_F^{(g)}(a)\Phi) + N_f(1+c_F^{(q)}(a)\Phi)) g^2 T^2 / 6$, with $c_F^{(q)}(a) = 2(1-2^{-a}) c_F^{(g)}(a) = (6/\pi^2)(1-2^{-a}) \Gamma(3+a) \zeta(1+a)$.
- The real part of the static potential is $\mathrm{Re}\, V(r) = - (g^2 C_F / 4\pi r) e^{-\hat{r}}$, where $\hat{r} \equiv r \sqrt{m_R^2 + \delta m_R^2}$.
- The imaginary part of the static potential is $\mathrm{Im}\, V(r) = - (g^2 C_F T / 4\pi) (m_F^2 + \delta m_F^2)/(m_R^2 + \delta m_R^2) \phi(\hat{r})$, with $\phi(\hat{r}) \sim \hat{r}^2 \log(1/\hat{r})$ when $\hat{r} \ll 1$.

## Context
This work extends the HTL resummed gluon propagator formalism by incorporating bulk-viscous corrections, building on prior shear-viscous corrections to the gluon propagator by Dumitru (2007). The bulk viscosity in QCD at high temperature was computed to leading order by Arnold (2006), who found it proportional to the square of the deviation from conformality. The analysis is motivated by lattice QCD results (Bazavov 2014) showing a large trace anomaly near $T_c$, and by suggestions (Kharzeev and Tuchin) that the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio should increase near the confinement-deconfinement temperature. The results enable qualitative incorporation of non-equilibrium effects into quarkonium potentials derived from lattice QCD (Rothkopf and Strickland).
