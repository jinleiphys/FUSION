# Statistical evolution of isotope composition of nuclear fragments

> Calculations within the statistical multifragmentation model show that the neutron content of intermediate mass fragments can increase in the region of liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei. The model predicts also inhomogeneous distributions of fragments and their isospin in the freeze-out volume caused by an angular momentum and external long-range Coulomb field. These effects can take place in peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions at intermediate energies and lead to neutron-rich isotopes produced in the midrapidity kinematic region.

# Statistical evolution of isotope composition of nuclear fragments

## Key claim

The statistical multifragmentation model predicts that the neutron content of intermediate mass fragments can increase in the region of the liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei, but this effect exhibits a "rise-and-fall" behavior: the N/Z ratio of IMFs rises with excitation energy only up to approximately 8 MeV/nucleon, after which it drops as heavier fragments are destroyed and free neutrons become abundant.

## Method

The authors applied a Markov chain version of the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) that generates partitions via the Metropolis algorithm, allowing explicit calculation of Coulomb interaction energies for each spatial configuration of primary fragments in the freeze-out volume. They performed microcanonical calculations for a gold source (A_s=197, Z_s=79) at freeze-out density ρ_s=ρ_0/3, with fragments of A≤4 treated as a nuclear gas and heavier fragments as heated nuclear liquid drops, followed by secondary deexcitation via evaporation, fission, or Fermi break-up. For peripheral collision simulations, the source was placed at a fixed distance of 20 fm from a second gold nucleus to model the Coulomb field of the target residue.

## Key numbers

- For a Au source (A_s=197, Z_s=79) at freeze-out density ρ_s=ρ_0/3, the N/Z ratio of hot primary intermediate mass fragments increases with excitation energy in the range E_s^*=3–8 MeV/nucleon, then drops at higher energies.
- At the multifragmentation threshold E_s^* ~ 3 MeV/nucleon, the fragment mass distribution shows a U-shape; at high energies it evolves to an exponential fall.
- For the Au source with angular momentum 150 ħ at ρ_s=ρ_0/6 and E_s^*=3 MeV/nucleon, the N/Z ratio of IMFs increases compared to the case without angular momentum.
- In peripheral collision calculations, the projectile-like Au source was placed at a fixed distance of 20 fm from the target-like Au source, corresponding to a break-up time of ~100 fm/c after a 35 A·MeV Au+Au peripheral collision.
- The symmetry energy coefficient used in the grand canonical limit is γ≈25 MeV, and the Coulomb coefficient c=(3/5)(e^2/r_0)(1-(ρ/ρ_0)^(1/3)) with r_0=1.17 fm and normal nuclear density ρ_0≈0.15 fm^(-3).

## Context

This work extends previous SMM calculations (Botvina 1987, PR95) and the Markov chain SMM development (mc_pre, mc_smm) to the isospin degree of freedom in nuclear multifragmentation. The predicted "rise-and-fall" of IMF neutron content with excitation energy is presented as a statistical manifestation of isospin fractionation associated with the liquid-gas phase transition in finite nuclei (Mueller), in contrast to dynamical models (Nebauer 1999, Larionov 1999, Di Toro) which predict decreasing N/Z of larger fragments with increasing excitation energy. The demonstration that spatial asymmetry of fragment emission and isotope composition can arise from statistical processes with an external Coulomb field provides an alternative to dynamical explanations of neck emission, and is consistent with experimental data on neutron-rich IMF production in the midrapidity region (Dempsey, Larochelle).
