# Impacts of nuclear-physics uncertainty in stellar temperatures on the s-process nucleosynthesis

> We evaluated the uncertainty relevant to s-process nucleosynthesis using a Monte-Carlo centred approach. We are based on a realistic and general prescription of temperature dependent uncertainty for the reactions. We considered massive stars for the weak s-process and AGB stars for the main s-process. We found that the adopted uncertainty for (n,$γ$) rates, tens of per cent on average, affect the production of s-process nuclei along the $β$-stability line, while for $β$-decay, for which contributions from excited states enhances the uncertainty, has the strongest impact on branching points.

## Key claim
The uncertainty in (n,γ) reaction rates, tens of percent on average, dominates the total uncertainty in s-process nucleosynthesis yields for both the weak and main s-process, while β-decay uncertainty has the strongest impact only at specific branching points. The authors qualify that their results depend on adopting a temperature-dependent uncertainty prescription and simplified stellar models that reproduce typical s-process patterns.

## Method
The authors performed Monte-Carlo nucleosynthesis calculations using simplified 1D stellar evolution models: a 25 solar mass massive star model for the weak s-process and a 2 solar mass AGB star model computed with the MESA code for the main s-process. They applied temperature-dependent uncertainties to (n,γ) and β-decay rates, using experimental uncertainties for ground-state contributions and a factor of 2 for excited-state uncertainties in (n,γ) rates, and a factor of 5 maximum uncertainty for β-decay rates at high temperature. Three variation cases were run: varying all (n,γ) and β-decay rates, varying only (n,γ) rates, and varying only β-decay rates, using a uniform random distribution between upper and lower limits at each temperature.

## Key numbers
- For the weak s-process with all rates varied, the 90% uncertainty range of abundances is within a factor of 1.5 (0.176 in log10), while some isotopes show a larger uncertainty reaching factor 2.
- For β-decay rates, a factor of 5 is adopted as the maximum uncertainty at high temperature, although uncertainty is about 2 at stellar burning temperatures.
- The stellar models used are 25 solar mass for massive stars and 2 solar mass for AGB stars.
- The uncertainty factor of 2 is applied for excited-state contributions to (n,γ) rates when theoretical calculations are used.

## Context
This work extends the systematic Monte-Carlo approach to s-process uncertainty quantification previously applied by Rauscher (2011, 2012) and by Nishimura (2017) and Rauscher (2016). It uses the temperature-dependent uncertainty prescription from Rauscher (2011, 2012) and applies it to both the weak s-process in massive stars and the main s-process in AGB stars. The method enables identification of key reaction rates that most affect s-process yields, particularly identifying 64Cu(β+)64Zn and 80Br(β+)80Kr for the weak s-process and 122Sb(β+)122Te for the main s-process as dominant β-decay uncertainties at branching points.
