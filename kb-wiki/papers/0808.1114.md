# Holographic quark gluon plasma with flavor

> In this work I explore theoretical and phenomenological implications of chemical potentials and charge densities inside a strongly coupled thermal plasma, using the gauge/gravity correspondence. Strong coupling effects discovered in this model theory are interpreted geometrically and may be taken as qualitative predictions for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. In particular I examine the thermodynamics, spectral functions, transport coefficients and the phase diagram of the strongly coupled plasma. For example stable mesons, which are the analogs of the QCD Rho-mesons, are found to survive beyond the deconfinement transition. This paper is based on partly unpublished work performed in the context of my PhD thesis. New results and ideas extending significantly beyond those published until now are stressed.

## Key claim
Using the gauge/gravity correspondence, finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials or densities significantly affect thermodynamics, spectral functions, and transport properties of a strongly coupled thermal plasma, with the caveat that results are obtained in a supersymmetric model theory (N=4 Super-Yang-Mills coupled to N=2 fundamental hypermultiplets) rather than QCD itself, and are therefore qualitative predictions for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Method
The author considers a gravity setup of N_c D3-branes producing an asymptotically AdS black hole background (dual to finite temperature T) with N_f probe D7-branes introducing fundamental matter (quarks). Chemical potentials are implemented by choosing non-vanishing background gauge fields A_0 on the flavor branes, with the boundary value giving the chemical potential. Thermodynamic quantities, thermal spectral functions, and transport coefficients are computed using holographic methods, including analytic hydrodynamic approximations and numerical evaluation of correlation functions.

## Key numbers
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio bound derived from AdS/CFT: η/s ≥ ħ/(4π)
- The vector meson mass formula for stable mesonic resonances: M = (L_∞/R²) √(2(n+1)(n+2)), where n is the radial gravity excitation number starting at n=0 for the lowest frequency peak
- The black hole horizon relates to temperature as: ϱ_H = π T R², where R is the AdS radius
- The weak force is stated to be 10³² times larger than gravity

## Context
This work extends the original AdS/CFT correspondence of Maldacena (1997) by introducing flavor and fundamental matter following Karch and Katz (2002) and subsequent studies of mesons and phase transitions by Babington et al. (2003), Kruczenski et al. (2003), and Mateos et al. (2006). It builds on the connection between hydrodynamics and black hole physics established by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001) and the viscosity bound derived by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2003). The newly discovered phase transition at equal chemical potentials or densities is noted to be analogous to that found for 2-flavor QCD by Splittorff (2000). The results enable further exploration of the phase diagram of strongly coupled plasmas with multiple chemical potentials and provide qualitative predictions for heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC and the upcoming LHC.
