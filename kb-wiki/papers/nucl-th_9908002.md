# Correlations derived from Modern Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

> Various modern nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials yield a very accurate fit to the nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts. The differences between these interactions in describing properties of nuclear matter are investigated. Various contributions to the total energy are evaluated employing the Hellmann - Feynman theorem. Special attention is paid to the two-nucleon correlation functions derived from these interactions. Differences in the predictions of the various interactions can be traced back to the inclusion of non-local terms.

## Key claim

Four modern nucleon-nucleon potentials that are phase-shift equivalent (CDBonn, ArV18, Nijm1, Nijm2) produce significantly different predictions for nuclear matter properties and two-nucleon correlation functions, with the differences traceable to whether the interactions contain non-local terms.

## Method

The authors employed the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation to calculate nuclear matter properties at various densities, using the Bethe-Goldstone equation to define the effective G-matrix interaction. They applied the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to extract expectation values of the kinetic energy and the one-pion-exchange contribution from the correlated wave functions. The correlated two-nucleon wave functions were constructed from the defect function defined by the BHF approach.

## Key numbers

- BHF energy per nucleon at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.36 fm^-1: -17.11 MeV (CDBonn), -15.85 MeV (ArV18), -15.82 MeV (Nijm1), -13.93 MeV (Nijm2)
- Hartree-Fock energy per nucleon at same density: 4.64 MeV (CDBonn), 30.34 MeV (ArV18), 12.08 MeV (Nijm1), 36.87 MeV (Nijm2)
- Kinetic energy per nucleon from correlated wave function at k_F = 1.36 fm^-1: 36.23 MeV (CDBonn), 47.07 MeV (ArV18), 39.26 MeV (Nijm1), 48.01 MeV (Nijm2), compared to free Fermi gas value of 23 MeV per nucleon
- Expectation value of one-pion-exchange contribution at k_F = 1.36 fm^-1: -22.30 MeV (CDBonn), -40.35 MeV (ArV18), -28.98 MeV (Nijm1), -28.97 MeV (Nijm2)
- Deuteron D-state probability: 4.83% (CDBonn), 5.78% (ArV18), 5.66% (Nijm1), 5.64% (Nijm2)
- Enhancement factor of kinetic energy relative to free Fermi gas at saturation density: 1.57 (CDBonn) to 2.09 (Nijm1)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of deuteron properties from phase-shift equivalent potentials (Polls et al., 1998; Forest, 1999) to the nuclear matter environment, and contrasts with the much harder Reid soft-core potential (Reid, 1968) which gives a Hartree-Fock energy of 176 MeV per nucleon. The results enable experimental discrimination between NN interaction models through exclusive (e,e'NN) reactions, and raise questions about whether the density-dependent enhancement of pionic correlations seen in the Argonne potential (related to pion condensation studies by Akmal and Pandharipande, 1997) is a general feature of realistic interactions.
