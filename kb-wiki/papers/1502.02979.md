# Magnetar superconductivity versus magnetism: neutrino cooling processes

> We describe the microphysics, phenomenology, and astrophysical implication of a $B$-field induced unpairing effect that may occur in magnetars, if the local $B$-field in the core of a magnetar exceeds a critical value $H_{c2}$. Using the Ginzburg-Landau theory of superconductivity, we derive the $H_{c2}$ field for proton condensate taking into the correction ($\le 30\%$) which arises from its coupling to the background neutron condensate. The density dependence of pairing of proton condensate implies that $H_{c2}$ is maximal at the crust-core interface and decreases towards the center of the star. As a consequence, magnetar cores with homogenous constant fields will be partially superconducting for "medium-field" magnetars ($10^{15}\le B\le 5 \times 10^{16}$ G) whereas "strong-field" magnetars ($B>5\times 10^{16}$ G) will be void of superconductivity. The neutrino emissivity of a magnetar's core changes in a twofold manner: (i)~the $B$-field assisted direct Urca process is enhanced by orders of magnitude, because of the unpairing effect in regions where $B\ge H_{c2}$; (ii)~the Cooper-pair breaking processes on protons vanish in these regions and the overall emissivity by the pair-breaking processes is reduced by a factor of only a few.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in magnetars, if the local magnetic field exceeds a density-dependent critical value \(H_{c2}\) (which is maximal at the crust-core interface and decreases toward the center), the proton superconductor is "unpaired" by the field; this effect is conditional on the interior field being approximately homogeneous and constant, and the correction to \(H_{c2}\) from coupling to the neutron condensate is \(\le 30\%\).

## Method

The authors used Ginzburg-Landau (GL) theory to derive the upper critical field \(H_{c2}\) for the proton condensate, including a density-density coupling term between the proton and neutron condensates. They adopted a relativistic density functional with density-dependent couplings to obtain the equation of state and composition of matter (neutrons, protons, electrons, muons in \(\beta\) equilibrium), and used published pairing gaps for neutrons (\(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_2\) channels) and protons (\(^1S_0\) channel). They then computed the neutrino emissivity of the direct Urca process (using a fit to earlier results for the \(B\)-field modification factor \(\mathcal{R}\)) and the pair-breaking processes, applying the unpairing effect by setting the proton suppression factor \(\mathcal{S}_p = 1\) where \(B \ge H_{c2}\).

## Key numbers

- Maximum \(H_{c2}\) is \(7.37 \times 10^{16}\) G at baryon density \(n_b = 0.5 n_0\) (where \(n_0 = 0.152\) fm\(^{-3}\)).
- The correction to \(H_{c2}\) from neutron-proton condensate coupling is \(\le 30\%\).
- "Medium-field" magnetars have interior fields \(10^{15} \le B \le 5 \times 10^{16}\) G; "strong-field" magnetars have \(B > 5 \times 10^{16}\) G.
- At density \(n = n_0\) and \(T = 0.1\) MeV, the Urca emissivity in the forbidden region is enhanced by orders of magnitude for \(B > H_{c2}\) compared to the case where proton pairing is (incorrectly) retained.
- At density \(n = 1.5 n_0\) and \(T = 0.1\) MeV, neglecting the unpairing effect would leave the Urca emissivity about 4–5 orders of magnitude below the \(B=0\) case, whereas with unpairing it is only about one order of magnitude below.
- The GL parameter \(\kappa\) drops below the critical value \(\kappa_c = 0.7071\) at high densities, indicating a transition to type-I superconductivity; at \(n_b/n_0 = 2.1\), \(\kappa = 0.4\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the direct Urca process in strong magnetic fields by Baiko and Yakovlev (1999) and of the density-density coupling between neutron and proton condensates by Alford et al. (2005). It also builds on the standard GL theory of type-II superconductivity in neutron stars (e.g., Baym, Pethick, and Pines 1969; Mendell 1991). The results enable more realistic simulations of magnetar thermal evolution and cooling, because the neutrino emissivity from the Urca and pair-breaking processes is significantly altered by the magnetic unpairing of protons, which had been neglected in prior models.
