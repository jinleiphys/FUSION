# Effective lagrangian approach to structure of selected nuclei far from sta bility

> The convergence of the effective field theory (EFT) approach of Furnstahl, Serot and Tang to the nuclear many-body problem is studied by applying it to selected doubly-magic nuclei far from stability. An independently developed code, which can incorporate various levels of approximation of the chiral effective lagrangi an, is used to solve the self-consistent relativistic Hartree equations. Results are obtained for ground-state properties such as binding energies, single-parti cle level structure, and densities of the selected spherical, doubly-magic nucle i 132-Sn, 100-Sn and 48-Ni, 78-Ni. Calculated spectra of neigh boring nuclei differing by one particle or one hole show agreement with the most recent experimental data. Predictions for nucleon densities are presented.

## Key claim

The chiral effective field theory (EFT) approach of Furnstahl, Serot and Tang converges when applied to doubly-magic nuclei far from stability, with total binding energy deviations below approximately 1% for all tested nuclei, but the convergence does not significantly improve beyond the Q1 parameter set level of approximation.

## Method

The author solved the self-consistent relativistic Hartree equations using an independently developed code that incorporates various levels of approximation of the chiral effective lagrangian. The lagrangian includes nucleon fields, isoscalar-scalar and isoscalar-vector meson fields, an isovector rho-meson field, and pion fields, with interaction terms arranged following naive dimensional analysis (NDA). The code iteratively solves the coupled Dirac equation for nucleons and nonlinear differential equations for the meson fields using a Green's function method, with parameter sets (L2, NLC, W1, Q1, Q2, G1, G2) obtained by fitting to properties of stable doubly-magic nuclei or infinite nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Total binding energy deviations for all four doubly-magic nuclei tested lie below approximately 1% for all parameter sets.
- For the Sn isotopes from 100-Sn to 132-Sn, the percentage deviation for parameter sets Q1, G1 and G2 is in absolute value less than 1%.
- The parameter set L2 was fit to infinite nuclear matter saturation density of 0.1484 fm^-3 and binding energy per nucleon of -15.75 MeV.
- The W1 parameter set uses a scalar meson mass m_s = 566.26 MeV, while Q1 uses m_s = 504.6 MeV.
- The anomalous magnetic moments used are lambda_p = 1.793 and lambda_n = -1.913.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Furnstahl, Serot and Tang (2001) to nuclei far from stability, testing convergence where previous work focused on stable doubly-magic nuclei. The results are compared with experimental data for 132-Sn, 100-Sn, 48-Ni and 78-Ni, and with other theoretical approaches including relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov calculations by Lalazissis and collaborators, density dependent relativistic Hartree (DDRH) by Hofmann and Lenske, and relativistic continuum Hartree Bogoliubov (RCHB) by Meng. The study enables systematic testing of EFT convergence for exotic nuclei relevant to nucleosynthesis processes such as the r-process.
