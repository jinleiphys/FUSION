# Isospin fluctuations in spinodal decomposition

> We study the isospin dynamics in fragment formation within the framework of an analytical model based on the spinodal decomposition scenario. We calculate the probability to obtain fragments with given charge and neutron number, focussing on the derivation of the width of the isotopic distributions. Within our approach this is determined by the dispersion of N/Z among the leading unstable modes, due to the competition between Coulomb and symmetry energy effects, and by isovector-like fluctuations present in the matter that undergoes the spinodal decomposition. Hence the widths exhibit a clear dependence on the properties of the Equation of State. By comparing two systems with different values of the charge asymmetry we find that the isotopic distributions reproduce an isoscaling relationship.

## Key claim

The width of isotopic distributions in multifragmentation is determined by the dispersion of N/Z among the leading unstable modes (due to competition between Coulomb and symmetry energy effects) and by isovector-like fluctuations present in the matter undergoing spinodal decomposition, with the widths exhibiting a clear dependence on the properties of the Equation of State.

## Method

The authors develop an analytical model generalizing the spinodal decomposition framework of Mat00 and Mat03 to include the isospin degree of freedom, treating the growth of density fluctuations via a linearized kinetic equation with a self-consistent stochastic mean field. They calculate the covariance matrix for proton and neutron density fluctuations using the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, separating contributions from unstable isoscalar-like modes (which grow exponentially) and stable isovector-like modes (which contribute quantum fluctuations), then derive the probability distribution for fragment charge and neutron number from correlation lengths and volumes.

## Context

This work extends the spinodal decomposition model of Mat00 and Mat03 (developed for symmetric nuclear matter) to include isospin degrees of freedom, incorporating the Coulomb force following the approach of Fab98. The analytical results are compared with numerical solutions from the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach of Bar02 and Col98, and with the SMF calculations of Liu04 for primary fragment isotopic distributions. The model enables direct analytical predictions of isoscaling parameters and their dependence on the Equation of State, complementing dynamical model calculations based on semiclassical kinetic equations reviewed in Das01, Bor02, and Cho04.
