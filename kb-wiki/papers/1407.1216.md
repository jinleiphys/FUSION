# Magnetic structure of isospin-asymmetric QCD matter in neutron stars

> We study QCD under the influence of background magnetic fields and isospin chemical potentials using lattice simulations. This setup exhibits a sign problem which is circumvented using a Taylor-expansion in the magnetic field. The ground state of the system in the pion condensation phase is found to exhibit a pronounced diamagnetic response. We elaborate on how this diamagnetism may contribute to the pressure balance in the inner core of strongly magnetized neutron stars. In addition we show that the onset of pion condensation shifts to larger chemical potentials due to the enhancement of the charged pion mass for growing magnetic fields. Finally, we sketch the magnetic structure of the QCD phase diagram in the temperature-isospin chemical potential plane.

## Key claim

The paper uses lattice QCD simulations to show that the pion condensation phase of isospin-asymmetric QCD matter exhibits a strong diamagnetic response, with the caveat that the results are obtained on coarse lattices with a larger-than-physical pion mass and should be considered exploratory.

## Method

The author simulates two-flavor QCD on an 8^4 lattice with rooted staggered quarks and a plaquette gauge action at inverse coupling β=5.2, corresponding to a lattice spacing a=0.299(2) fm and pion mass m_π≈260 MeV. To circumvent the sign problem that arises from combining a magnetic field with unequal quark charges, the author performs a leading-order Taylor expansion in the magnetic field around B=0, using a modified magnetic field configuration with zero total flux to allow differentiation with respect to eB. The magnetic susceptibility and other observables are measured as functions of the isospin chemical potential μ_I, with an explicit symmetry breaking parameter λ extrapolated linearly to zero.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: m_π ≈ 260 MeV (from lattice units m_π a = 0.402(5) with a = 0.299(2) fm)
* Lattice size: L ≈ 2.4 fm, corresponding to L^{-1} ≈ 80 MeV
* Onset of pion condensation occurs at μ_I = m_π/2, consistent with chiral perturbation theory
* Renormalized magnetic susceptibility in the pion condensation phase: χ^r ≈ -0.1 (typical value)
* At μ_I=0 and low temperature, the susceptibility is χ^r ≈ -0.002 (from prior work cited as Bali (2014))
* The diamagnetic force density can reach up to 10% of the gravitational pressure gradient in the inner core (r ≲ 3 km) of a magnetar with central field 1.5×10^18 G and radius 10 km

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of magnetic properties from zero isospin chemical potential (Bali 2014) to nonzero μ_I, and builds on earlier lattice simulations of pion condensation at B=0 by Kogut (2002). The results enable first estimates of how the diamagnetic response of pion-condensed matter might affect pressure balance and convective processes in the inner core of strongly magnetized neutron stars (magnetars), where magnetic fields can reach 10^18 G. The paper also sketches a conjectured magnetic phase diagram in the temperature-isospin chemical potential plane, identifying regions of paramagnetic and diamagnetic QCD matter.
