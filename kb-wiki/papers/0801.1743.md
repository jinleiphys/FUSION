# Temperature Effects in a Fermi Gas with Population Imbalance

> We investigate temperature effects in a Fermi gas with imbalanced spin populations. From the general expression of the thermal gap equation we find, in {\it weak coupling limit}, an analytical expression for the transition temperature $T_c$ as a function of various possibilities of chemical potential and mass asymmetries between the two particle species. For a range of asymmetry between certain specific values, this equation always has two solutions for $T_c$ which has been interpreted as a reentrant phenomena or a pairing induced by temperature effect. We show that the lower $T_c$ is never related to a stable solution. The same results are obtained in {\it strong coupling limit}. The thermodynamical potential is carefully analyzed to avoid the consideration of the unstable solutions. We also obtain the tricritical points for the chemical potential and mass imbalanced cases, and beyond these points we properly minimize the thermodynamic potential to find the stable and metastable first order transition lines.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in an imbalanced Fermi gas, the thermal gap equation can yield two solutions for the transition temperature $T_c$ (a reentrant phenomenon), but the lower $T_c$ never corresponds to a stable solution; only the higher $T_c$ represents a true second-order phase transition, and the reentrant behavior could only manifest when BEC-BCS crossover theory with fluctuation contributions (pseudogap) is considered.

## Method

The authors employed mean-field theory to derive the thermodynamic potential and thermal gap equation for a two-species homogeneous Fermi gas with chemical potential and mass asymmetries. In the weak coupling limit, they obtained an analytical expression for the transition temperature $T_c$ as a function of asymmetry parameters using the digamma function. They then numerically solved the gap equation and minimized the thermodynamic potential to distinguish stable, unstable, and metastable solutions, both in weak and strong coupling regimes (parameterized by $k_Fa$).

## Key numbers

- For equal masses ($\delta m=0$), the tricritical point occurs at $(\delta \mu_c, T_c) = (1.2308 \Delta_0, 0.2713 \Delta_0)$ in the weak coupling limit.
- The standard BCS result for the symmetric case gives $T_c/\Delta_0 = e^\gamma/\pi \approx 0.5669$.
- The Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit for equal masses is $\delta\mu_{CC}/\Delta_0 = \sqrt{2}$.
- The two critical temperatures begin to appear at $\delta \mu_0/\Delta_0 = \pm 1$ for equal masses ($\delta m=0$).
- For the strong coupling case $k_Fa = -10$, the critical temperature at the tricritical point is approximately $0.44 \Delta_0$, larger than in the weak coupling limit.
- The argument that minimizes the function $f(T)$ is $a_0 = 1.1343$, and $e^{-\frac{1}{2}{\cal F}(a_0)} \approx 3.4089$.

## Context

This work extends the study of imbalanced Fermi gases beyond the zero-temperature analyses of Sarma (1963) and the phase separation observed by the Rice (Hulet, 2006) and MIT (Ketterle, 2006) groups. It provides analytical expressions for tricritical points in both chemical potential and mass asymmetric cases, which were previously only studied numerically. The results enable the prediction of shell structures in trapped mass-imbalanced systems and identify metastable superfluid states that could be experimentally prepared and studied in ultracold fermionic systems.
