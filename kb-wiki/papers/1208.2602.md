# Dynamical Quarkonia Suppression in a QGP-Brick

> I address the effect that a temperature dependent potential has on the suppression of heavy quarkonia states in deconfined hadronic matter. I focus on a simple medium: a homogenous, fixed temperature and deconfined system with a finite lifetime (QGP-brick). Assuming that all the interactions of a heavy quark anti-quark ($Q-\bar Q$) pair with the medium can be recast into an in-medium potential, I solve the time dependent Schrödinger equation to evolve the density matrix which describes the hard pair production and its connection to the final distribution of hadrons after the medium disappears. For those temperatures in which bound states survive in the medium, I find a non-trivial dependence of the production of excited quarkonia states on the in-medium levels, due to the mixing of vacuum and in-medium wave functions. This mixing leads, in particular, to the enhancement of the relative abundance of 2S to 1S states for those systems in which the in-medium ground state of the $Q-\bar Q$ system is dissolved or close to threshold. I also explore quarkonia production in a non-homogeneous expanding medium and find that the finite formation time effects arising from the low binding energies of in-medium states lead to the insensitivity of the heavy mesons yield to the hottest part of the system evolution.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the relative abundance of 2S to 1S quarkonia states can be enhanced in a QGP-brick when the in-medium ground state is dissolved or close to threshold, due to mixing of vacuum and in-medium wave functions; this effect occurs only for temperatures where bound states survive in the medium, and the author emphasizes that despite the relative enhancement, both states are strongly suppressed compared to vacuum.

## Method

The author solves the time-dependent Schrödinger equation numerically to evolve the density matrix of a heavy quark-antiquark pair produced by a hard process in a homogeneous, fixed-temperature deconfined medium with finite lifetime (QGP-brick). Two toy-model in-medium potentials are used: a real Yukawa potential encoding color screening, and a complex potential with an imaginary part derived from perturbative analyses. The yield of each quarkonia state after the medium disappears is obtained from the modulus squared of the evolved wave function at the origin, and suppression ratios are computed relative to vacuum yields.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement crossover temperature is approximately \(T_c \approx 175\) MeV.
- Bottomonium ground states survive up to \(T = 2\, T_c\), while \(J/\psi\) is totally dissolved at \(T < 1.5\, T_c\).
- For the real Yukawa potential, the double ratio of 2S to 1S suppression factors can exceed 1 for high temperatures (e.g., \(2 \mu_D / \alpha M = 0.9\)), showing relative enhancement of excited states.
- For the complex potential, the late-time suppression of the ground state is dominated by the in-medium width \(\Gamma\), with the absorption rate matching the imaginary part of the in-medium ground state after the formation time \(\mathcal{T} = 2\pi / E_b\).
- The formation time scale for the suppression patterns is of order the ground state period \(\mathcal{T} = 2\pi / E_b\), where \(E_b = \alpha^2 M / 4\) is the vacuum ground state binding energy.

## Context

This work extends potential model studies of in-medium quarkonia properties (Karsch 1987, Digal 2001, Mocsy 2005-2007, Cabrera 2006) by moving beyond adiabatic approximations and solving the full time-dependent Schrödinger equation for a finite-lifetime medium. It contrasts with earlier transport equation approaches (Rapp 2008) and recent studies of \(\Upsilon\) suppression (Sharma 2009, Strickland 2011, Emerick 2011, Song 2011) by focusing on the dynamical effects of wave function mixing rather than sequential dissociation. The results enable more realistic modeling of quarkonia production in expanding heavy-ion collision systems, particularly for understanding the \(\psi(2S)\) enhancement observed in preliminary LHC data (HPtalk).
