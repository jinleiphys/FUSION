# Description of neutron-rich odd-mass krypton isotopes within the interacting boson-fermion model based on the Gogny energy density functional

> The low-lying structure of neutron-rich odd-mass Kr isotopes is studied within the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) based on the Gogny-D1M energy density functional (EDF). The $(β,γ)$-deformation energy surfaces, spherical single-particle energies and occupation probabilities of the odd-mass systems are obtained using the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation. Those quantities are used as a microscopic input to determine most of the parameters of the IBFM Hamiltonian. The remaining parameters are specifically tailored to the experimental spectrum for each of the studied odd-mass nuclei. A gradual structural evolution is predicted for the odd-mass isotopes $^{87-95}$Kr as a function of the nucleon number which, agrees well with the gradual growth of collectivity observed experimentally in the neighboring even-even isotopes.

## Key claim

The low-lying structure of neutron-rich odd-mass Kr isotopes is described using the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) with parameters derived from Gogny-D1M energy density functional calculations, but the coupling constants for the boson-fermion interaction are specifically fitted to reproduce experimental spectra for each odd-mass nucleus, meaning the method is not fully predictive.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny-D1M functional to obtain deformation energy surfaces for even-even core nuclei and spherical single-particle energies and occupation probabilities for odd-mass systems. These quantities were used as microscopic input to determine most parameters of the IBFM-2 Hamiltonian, while the remaining boson-fermion interaction parameters (quadrupole dynamical, exchange, and monopole strengths) were fitted to reproduce the lowest-lying positive-parity levels for each odd-mass nucleus. The IBFM-2 Hamiltonian was then diagonalized to obtain excitation energies, wave functions, and electromagnetic properties for the odd-mass Kr isotopes.

## Key numbers

- For the even-even core nuclei, the experimental $R_{4/2}=E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)$ ratios lie between the U(5) vibrational limit (2.0) and O(6) gamma-soft limit (2.5), confirming a gradual onset of collectivity.
- The ground state spin changes from ${5/2}^+$ in $^{87,91}$Kr to ${1/2}^+$ in $^{93,95}$Kr, with the ${5/2}^+_1$ state in $^{87,91}$Kr being approximately 95% composed of the $2d_{5/2}$ configuration.
- For $^{89}$Kr, the ${5/2}^+$ state lies only 29 keV above the tentatively assigned ${3/2}^+$ ground state.
- The exchange interaction strength $\Lambda_\nu$ for $^{93,95}$Kr is 2.4 MeV, roughly a factor of three larger than the values of 0.87 MeV for $^{89}$Kr and 0.5 MeV for $^{91}$Kr.
- The proton boson effective charge was set to $e_B^\pi = 0.07\,e$b, with the neutron boson charge $e_B^\nu = 0.035\,e$b = $e_B^\pi/2$, and the neutron fermion effective charge was $e_F^\nu = 0.5\,e$b.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous IBM-2 studies of even-even Kr isotopes (Albers 2012, Albers 2013, Nomura 2017) to odd-mass systems using the IBFM framework, which has rarely been applied to the $A\approx 100$ mass region beyond $N=50$. The method enables systematic spectroscopic description of odd-mass nuclei that is computationally more efficient than full HFB blocking calculations, and the results for $^{91}$Kr are compared with large-scale shell-model calculations (Rzaca-Urban 2017). Future work could apply this approach to heavier Kr isotopes beyond $A=96$ where shape coexistence becomes important, requiring intruder configurations in the IBM formalism.
