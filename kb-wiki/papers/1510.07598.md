# Large-N(c) limit reduces the number of independent few-body parity-violating low-energy constants in pionless effective field theory

> The symmetries of the Standard Model dictate that for very low energies, where nucleon dynamics can be described in terms of a pionless effective field theory, the leading-order parity-violating nucleon-nucleon Lagrangian contains five independent unknown low-energy constants (LECs). We find that imposing the approximate symmetry of QCD that appears when the number of colors N(c) becomes large reduces the number of independent LECs to two at leading order in the combined pionless effective field theory and large-N(c) expansions. We also find a relation between the two isoscalar LECs in the large-N(c) limit. This has important implications for the number of experiments and/or lattice calculations necessary to confirm this description of physics.

## Key claim

Imposing the large-\(N_c\) limit of QCD reduces the number of independent leading-order parity-violating low-energy constants in pionless effective field theory from five to two, but this result is only visible after careful analysis of Fierz identities that can hide the large-\(N_c\) scaling of operators in the minimal form of the Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors started from the most general nonrelativistic parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential containing ten operator structures, assigned large-\(N_c\) scaling to each coefficient based on the scaling of spin-isospin operators and momenta, and then applied Fierz identities to reduce the Lagrangian to its minimal five-operator form. They tracked how the large-\(N_c\) scaling of the nonminimal coefficients propagates through the Fierz relations to determine the true leading-order scaling of the minimal low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order parity-violating nucleon-nucleon Lagrangian in pionless effective field theory contains five independent low-energy constants; in the combined large-\(N_c\) and pionless effective field theory expansion, this number reduces to two independent constants at leading order.
- The two isoscalar low-energy constants \(\mathcal{C}^{(^3S_1-^1P_1)}\) and \(\mathcal{C}^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=0)}\) are related by \(\mathcal{C}^{(^3S_1-^1P_1)} = 3 \, \mathcal{C}^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=0)}\) up to corrections of order \(1/N_c^2\), which for \(N_c=3\) corresponds to approximately 10% corrections.
- The existing measurement of the longitudinal asymmetry in \(\vec{p}p\) scattering at \(E=13.6\) MeV gives \(A^{\vec p p}_L = (-0.93 \pm 0.21) \times 10^{-7}\), which constrains the combination \(\frac{4(\mathcal{C}^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=0)} + \mathcal{C}^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=1)} + \mathcal{C}^{(^1S_0-^3P_0)}_{(\Delta I=2)})}{\mathcal{C}^{(^1S_0)}} = (-1.5 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-10}\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- The limit on the induced circular polarization in \(np \rightarrow d\vec{\gamma}\) is \(P_\gamma = (1.8 \pm 1.8) \times 10^{-7}\).

## Context

This work extends the general large-\(N_c\) analysis of parity-violating nucleon-nucleon forces by Phillips et al. (2014) to the pionless effective field theory formalism, which has fewer low-energy constants and a well-understood prescription for higher-order corrections. The results enable a significant simplification: a future measurement of the parity-violating asymmetry in \(\vec{\gamma} d \rightarrow np\) together with the existing \(\vec{p}p\) scattering result would constrain all leading-order low-energy constants in the combined expansion, considerably improving upon the previous understanding of the system.
