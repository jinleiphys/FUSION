# Dipole excitation and geometry of borromean nuclei

> We analyze the Coulomb breakup cross sections of $^{11}$Li and $^6$He nuclei using a three-body model with a density-dependent contact interaction. We show that the concentration of the B(E1) strength near the threshold can be well reproduced with this model. With the help of the calculated B(E1) value, we extract the root-mean-square (rms) distance between the core nucleus and the center of mass of two valence neutrons without resorting to the sum rule, which may suffer from unphysical Pauli forbidden transitions. Together with the empirical rms distance between the neutrons obtained from the matter radius study and also from the three-body correlation study in the break-up reaction, we convert these rms distances to the mean opening angle between the valence neutrons from the core nucleus. We find that the obtained mean opening angles in $^{11}$Li and $^6$He agree with the three-body model predictions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a three-body model with a density-dependent contact interaction can reproduce the concentration of B(E1) strength near the threshold in the borromean nuclei $^{11}$Li and $^6$He, and that the extracted mean opening angles between the valence neutrons agree with the three-body model predictions, though with large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used a three-body model (core plus two valence neutrons) with a density-dependent contact interaction between the valence neutrons, discretizing single-particle continuum states by placing the nucleus in a large box and diagonalizing the three-body Hamiltonian. They calculated B(E1) distributions and Coulomb breakup cross sections for $^6$He+Pb at 240 MeV/nucleon and $^{11}$Li+Pb at 70 MeV/nucleon, then used the ratio of experimental to calculated B(E1) strength to extract the root-mean-square (rms) distance between the core and the center of mass of the two valence neutrons. This rms distance was combined with the rms neutron-neutron distance from matter radii or two-neutron correlation studies to derive the mean opening angle between the valence neutrons.

## Key numbers

* For $^6$He: total B(E1) strength of 0.660 e$^2$fm$^2$ up to $E \leq 5$ MeV and 1.053 e$^2$fm$^2$ up to $E \leq 10$ MeV, compared to experimental values of 0.59 $\pm$ 0.12 e$^2$fm$^2$ and 1.2 $\pm$ 0.2 e$^2$fm$^2$, respectively.
* For $^{11}$Li: total B(E1) strength of 1.405 e$^2$fm$^2$ up to $E_{\rm rel} \leq 3$ MeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.42 $\pm$ 0.18 e$^2$fm$^2$.
* Extracted rms core-dineutron distance $\sqrt{\langle r_{c-2n}^2\rangle_{\rm exp}} = 3.878 \pm 0.324$ fm for $^6$He and $5.15 \pm 0.327$ fm for $^{11}$Li.
* Mean opening angle from matter radii: $\langle\theta_{nn}\rangle = 51.56^{+11.2}_{-12.4}$ degrees for $^6$He and $56.2^{+17.8}_{-21.3}$ degrees for $^{11}$Li.
* Mean opening angle from two-neutron correlation study: $\langle\theta_{nn}\rangle = 74.5^{+11.2}_{-13.1}$ degrees for $^6$He and $65.2^{+11.4}_{-13.0}$ degrees for $^{11}$Li.
* Three-body model predictions for mean opening angle: 66.33 degrees for $^6$He and 65.29 degrees for $^{11}$Li.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of dipole excitations in two-neutron halo nuclei by Nakamura (2006) for $^{11}$Li and Aumann (1999) for $^6$He, using a three-body model previously developed by Hagino and Sagawa (2005). The paper improves upon the earlier sum-rule approach of Bertulani and Hussein (2007) by using a model-calibrated extraction of the core-dineutron distance that avoids unphysical Pauli forbidden transitions. The results enable more precise determination of the geometry of borromean nuclei, particularly the mean opening angle between valence neutrons, though the authors note that higher-precision measurements of the neutron-neutron distance are still needed.
