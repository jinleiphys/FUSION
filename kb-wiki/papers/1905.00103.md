# Point-Particle Catalysis

> We use the point-particle effective field theory (PPEFT) framework to describe particle-conversion mediated by a flavour-changing coupling to a point-particle. We do this for a toy model of two non-relativistic scalars coupled to the same point-particle, on which there is a flavour-violating coupling. It is found that the point-particle couplings all must be renormalized with respect to a radial cut-off near the origin, and it is an invariant of the flow of the flavour-changing coupling that is directly related to particle-changing cross-sections. At the same time, we find an interesting dependence of those cross-sections on the ratio k_out/k_in of the outgoing and incoming momenta, which can lead to a 1/k_in enhancement in certain regimes. We further connect this model to the case of a single-particle non-self-adjoint (absorptive) PPEFT, as well as to a PPEFT of a single particle coupled to a two-state nucleus. These results could be relevant for future calculations of any more complicated reactions, such as nucleus-induced electron-muon conversions, monopole catalysis of baryon number violation, as well as nuclear transfer reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a point-particle effective field theory (PPEFT) with two non-relativistic scalar fields coupled to a common point particle via flavour-violating couplings, the flavour-changing cross-section is directly related to an RG-invariant length scale \(\epsilon_3\) and exhibits a \(1/k_{\text{in}}\) enhancement when the incoming momentum is much smaller than the mass gap between the two species, but only for relativistic particles or when the mass gap is positive.

## Method

The authors construct a PPEFT for two non-relativistic complex scalar fields \(\Psi_1\) and \(\Psi_2\) coupled to a point particle's worldline via a Hermitian coupling matrix \(h_{ab}\), with the bulk action diagonal in flavour. They regulate the theory at a small radial distance \(\epsilon\) from the origin, derive the small-\(r\) boundary conditions from the point-particle action, and solve for the integration constants of the bulk wavefunctions in terms of the couplings. The renormalization-group flow of the couplings \(h_{ij}\) with \(\epsilon\) is then obtained by requiring that the physical integration constants remain regulator-independent, leading to three RG-invariant length scales \(\epsilon_1\), \(\epsilon_2\), and \(\epsilon_3\) that characterize the diagonal and off-diagonal couplings respectively.

## Key numbers

* The \(s\)-wave flavour-violating cross-section for \(\Psi_1 \to \Psi_2\) scattering is \(\sigma^{(1\to 2)}_s = 4\pi (k_2/k_1) \epsilon_3^2\), where \(k_1\) and \(k_2\) are the incoming and outgoing momenta and \(\epsilon_3\) is an RG-invariant length scale.
* For non-relativistic particles, the ratio \(k_2/k_1 = \sqrt{m_2/m_1}\) is constant; for relativistic particles, \(k_2/k_1 = \sqrt{(k_1^2 + m_1^2 - m_2^2)/k_1^2}\), leading to a \(1/k_1\) enhancement when \(k_1^2 \ll m_1^2 - m_2^2\).
* The diagonal couplings \(\widehat{\lambda}_{11}\) and \(\widehat{\lambda}_{22}\) flow to \(-1\) in the ultraviolet (\(\epsilon/\epsilon_i \to 0\)) and to \(+1\) in the infrared (\(\epsilon/\epsilon_i \to \infty\)), while the off-diagonal coupling \(\widehat{\lambda}_{12}\) flows to zero in both limits.

## Context

This work extends the single-species PPEFT framework of Burgess et al. (2017) to multiple interacting channels, generalizing the treatment of point-particle couplings to include flavour mixing. The two-species model and the equivalent single-particle two-state nucleus model provide the building blocks for future calculations of more complicated reactions, such as nucleus-induced electron-muon conversions, monopole catalysis of baryon number violation, and nuclear transfer reactions. The flavour-violating cross-section formula also connects to the absorptive (non-self-adjoint) PPEFT studied by Plestid and Burgess (2018), showing that the two-species model provides a unitary completion of such single-particle absorptive models.
