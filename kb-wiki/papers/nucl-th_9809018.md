# Effect of friction on disoriented chiral condensate formation

> We have investigated the effect of friction on the DCC domain formation. We solve the Newton equation of motion for the O(4) fields, with quenched initial condition. The initial fields are randomly distributed in a Gaussian form. In one dimensional expansion, on the average, large DCC domains can not be formed. However, in some particular orbits, large instabilities may occur. This possibility also greatly diminishes with the introduction of friction. But, if the friction is large, the system may be overdamped and then, there is a possibility of large DCC domain formation in some events.

# Effect of friction on disoriented chiral condensate formation

## Key claim

While on average, one-dimensional expansion with quenched initial conditions does not produce large DCC domains, and introducing friction further suppresses large domain formation, if the friction coefficient is sufficiently large (overdamping the system), large DCC domains can form in some individual events.

## Method

The author solves the Newton equation of motion for O(4) fields in the linear sigma model, augmented with a Bjorken-type scaling expansion and a Rayleigh dissipation term, using quenched initial conditions where the fields are randomly distributed in a Gaussian form. The evolution of the characteristic quantity m_t (related to the effective mass squared) and the amplification coefficient G are computed for 1000 trajectories with different friction coefficients. Hurst coefficient analysis is performed to distinguish persistent from antipersistent trajectories.

## Key numbers

- Friction coefficient values investigated: η = 0, 0.5, 1.1, and 2.2 fm⁻¹
- At T = T_c = 123 MeV, with m/T ≈ 1, the friction η = 2.2 fm⁻¹
- For η = 2.2 fm⁻¹, the most unstable orbit stays negative for approximately 15 fm, indicating possibility of large DCC formation
- Maximum amplification coefficient G = 2.4 in the frictionless case
- Model parameters: f_π = 92 MeV, m_π = 135 MeV, m_σ = 600 MeV, λ = 20.14, v = 86.71 MeV, H = (120.55 MeV)³

## Context

This work extends the dynamical studies of DCC formation based on the linear sigma model, following the initial proposal by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993). It builds on the Langevin equation approach of Biro and Greiner (1997), who investigated the interplay of friction and white noise on order parameter evolution, and on the work of Randrup (1996, 1997) who studied DCC formation in one-dimensional expansion. The results show that the 1/√f probability distribution for neutral-to-total pion ratio may not be a reliable signal for identifying pions from DCC domains, as it appears even when large DCC domains are rare.
