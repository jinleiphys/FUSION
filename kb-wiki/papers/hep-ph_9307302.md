# rho, omega, phi-Nucleon Scattering Lengths from QCD Sum Rules

> The QCD sum rule method is applied to derive a formula for the rho, omega, phi meson-nucleon spin-isospin-averaged scattering lengths $a_{ρ,ω,ϕ}$. We found that the crucial matrix elements are $\langle\bar{q}γ_μD_νq\rangle_N$ ($q=u,d$) (twist-2 nucleon matrix element) for $a_{ρ,ω}$ and $m_s\langle\bar{s}s\rangle_N$ for $a_ϕ$, and obtained $a_ρ=0.14\pm 0.07$ fm, $a_ω=0.11\pm 0.06$ fm and $a_ϕ=0.035\pm 0.020$ fm. These small numbers originate from a common factor $1/(m_N+m_{ρ,ω,ϕ})$. Our result suggests a slight increase ($< 60$ MeV for $ρ$, $ω$, and $<15$ MeV for $ϕ$) of the effective mass of these vector mesons in the nuclear matter (in the {\it dilute} nucleon gas approximation). The origin of the discrepancy with the previous study was clarified.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule method yields small, positive spin-isospin-averaged scattering lengths for rho, omega, and phi mesons on nucleons, corresponding to a repulsive interaction and a slight increase of the vector meson effective mass in dilute nuclear matter, contradicting a previous study that found a decrease.

## Method

The forward scattering amplitude of the vector current off a nucleon target was decomposed via operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-6 operators, including twist-2 and twist-4 nucleon matrix elements. A Borel transform was applied to the OPE expression and to a spectral function saturated by the vector meson-nucleon scattering double pole and a continuum, and the unknown coupling constant was eliminated by taking a ratio with the vacuum QCD sum rule for the vector current correlator. The resulting formulas for the scattering lengths were evaluated numerically using standard vacuum condensates, parton distribution functions from Gluck, Reya and Vogt (1990), and twist-4 matrix elements extracted from deep inelastic scattering data.

## Key numbers

*   \(a_\rho = 0.14 \pm 0.07\) fm for the rho meson-nucleon spin-isospin-averaged scattering length.
*   \(a_\omega = 0.11 \pm 0.06\) fm for the omega meson-nucleon spin-isospin-averaged scattering length.
*   \(a_\phi = 0.035 \pm 0.020\) fm for the phi meson-nucleon spin-isospin-averaged scattering length.
*   The effective mass increase in nuclear matter at density \(\rho_N = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) is less than 60 MeV for rho and omega, and less than 15 MeV for phi.
*   The twist-2 nucleon matrix element \(A_2^{u+d} = 0.90\) (at \(\mu^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\)) dominates the r term in the formula for \(a_{\rho,\omega}\), giving \(r = 0.39\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule method, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering by Koike and collaborators, to vector meson-nucleon scattering. It directly contradicts the earlier QCD sum rule analysis by Hatsuda and Lee (1992), which found a decrease of vector meson masses in nuclear matter; the present paper clarifies that the discrepancy arises from an inadequate choice of spectral function in that earlier work. The results enable a prediction of the in-medium mass shift of vector mesons in the dilute nucleon gas approximation and suggest a possible tendency toward rho-A1 degeneracy in nuclear matter.
