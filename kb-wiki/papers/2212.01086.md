# Heavy Baryons in Compact Stars

> We review the physics of hyperons and $Δ$-resonances in dense matter in compact stars. The covariant density functional approach to the equation of state and composition of dense nuclear matter in the mean-field Hartree and Hartree-Fock approximation is presented, with regimes covering cold $β$-equilibrated matter, hot and dense matter with and without neutrinos relevant for the description of supernovas and binary neutron star mergers, as well as dilute expanding nuclear matter in collision experiments. We discuss the static properties of compact stars with hyperons and $Δ$-resonances in light of constraints placed in recent years by the multimessenger astrophysics of compact stars on the compact stars' masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities. The effects of kaon condensation and strong magnetic fields on the composition of hypernuclear stars are also discussed. The properties of rapidly rotating compact hypernuclear stars are discussed and confronted with the observations of 2.5-2.8 solar mass compact objects in gravitational wave events. We further discuss the cooling of hypernuclear stars, the neutrino emission reactions, hyperonic pairing, and the mass hierarchy in the cooling curves that arises due to the onset of hyperons. The effects of hyperons and $Δ$-resonances on the equation of state of hot nuclear matter in the dense regime, relevant for the transient astrophysical event and in the dilute regime relevant to the collider physics is discussed. The review closes with a discussion of universal relations among the integral parameters of hot and cold hypernuclear stars and their implications for the analysis of binary neutron star merger events.

## Key claim

This review argues that modern covariant density functional (CDF) models for hypernuclear matter can simultaneously satisfy the observational constraints from two-solar-mass pulsars, gravitational wave events, and X-ray measurements, thereby resolving the "hyperon puzzle", the earlier contradiction where hyperon softening of the equation of state prevented compact stars from reaching masses above 2 solar masses.

## Method

The authors present the covariant density functional approach to dense nuclear matter using relativistic mean-field models in the Hartree and Hartree-Fock approximations, with density-dependent coupling constants for the meson-baryon interactions. The Lagrangian includes the spin-1/2 baryon octet (including hyperons) and the spin-3/2 decuplet of Δ-resonances, with mesonic fields (σ, ω, ρ) mediating the interactions, and additional hidden-strangeness mesons (σ*, φ) for strange particle interactions. The formalism covers cold β-equilibrated matter, hot dense matter with and without neutrinos for supernovae and binary neutron star mergers, and dilute expanding matter for collision experiments.

## Key numbers

- The three most massive precisely measured pulsars are PSR J1614-2230 at 1.908(16) M⊙, PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01±0.04 M⊙, and PSR J0740+6620 at 2.08±0.07 M⊙ (68.3% credibility), setting lower limits on the maximum mass of compact stars
- For the gravitational wave event GW170817, the effective tidal deformability was estimated as Λ̃ ≈ 300^{+500}_{-190} (90% confidence), with component masses in the range 1.16-1.6 M⊙ and total binary mass 2.73^{+0.04}_{-0.01} M⊙
- For GW190425, an upper limit Λ̃ ≤ 600 was derived, with component masses 1.46-1.87 M⊙ and total binary mass 3.4^{+0.3}_{-0.1} M⊙
- NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451 gave M = 1.44^{+0.15}_{-0.14} M⊙, R = 13.02^{+1.24}_{-1.06} km (Miller 2019) and M = 1.34^{+0.15}_{-0.16} M⊙, R = 12.71^{+1.14}_{-1.19} km (Riley 2019)
- NICER measurements of PSR J0740+6620 gave R = 13.7^{+2.6}_{-1.5} km (Miller 2021) and R = 12.39^{+1.3}_{-0.98} km (Riley 2021)
- Compact star central densities can reach up to about 10 times nuclear saturation density (ρ_sat ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3})
- The DDME2 parameter set uses meson masses: m_σ = 550.1238 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_ρ = 763 MeV

## Context

This review extends the line of work on hypernuclear matter in compact stars that began with the suggestions of hyperon presence by Ambartsumyan (1960) and Pandharipande (1971), and the early relativistic mean-field treatments by Glendenning (1985, 1991). It directly addresses the "hyperon puzzle" that emerged after the two-solar-mass pulsar discovery by Demorest (2010), where earlier models predicted maximum masses below 2 M⊙. The work enables future analyses of binary neutron star merger events through universal relations among integral parameters of hot and cold hypernuclear stars, and provides a framework for interpreting multimessenger constraints from gravitational wave events (GW170817, GW190425) and X-ray observations (NICER).
