## Key claim

The paper claims that the threefold differential cross sections for the $K^- d \to \pi Y N$ reaction at the J-PARC E31 kinematics can be used to test the existence and pole parameters of low-lying hyperon resonances, but only if the input $\bar K N \to \bar K N$ amplitudes are generated from a model that describes data far above the $\bar K N$ threshold, such as the dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model used here.

## Method

The authors applied a multiple scattering theory to calculate the $\bar K d \to \pi Y N$ reaction, including impulse and $\bar K$-exchange diagrams, using off-shell partial-wave amplitudes from a DCC model that fits over 17,000 data points of $K^- p$ reactions from threshold up to $W = 2.1$ GeV. The deuteron wave function was taken from the Argonne V18 potential, and the calculations were performed at the J-PARC E31 kinematics with incoming $\bar K$ momentum $|\vec p_{\bar K}| = 1$ GeV and outgoing nucleon detected at $\theta_{p_N} = 0$.

## Key numbers

- The incoming $\bar K$ momentum for the J-PARC E31 experiment is $|\vec p_{\bar K}| = 1$ GeV.
- The DCC model fits more than 17,000 data points of $K^- p$ reactions up to $W = 2.1$ GeV.
- A low-lying $J^P = 1/2^+$ $\Sigma$ resonance with pole mass $M_R = 1457 - i39$ MeV was found from analyzing $K^- p$ reaction data within the DCC model.
- Two $S_{01}$ ($J^P = 1/2^-$) $\Lambda$ resonances below the $\bar K N$ threshold were found: Model A gives poles at (1432, 75) MeV and (1372, 56) MeV; Model B gives poles at (1428, 31) MeV and (1397, 98) MeV.
- The product of coupling strengths $g_{\pi\Sigma Y^*}g_{\bar K N Y^*}$ for the A1 pole is $(15.42 \times 10^{-4}, 170^\circ)$ with $|g_{\pi\Sigma Y^*}g_{\bar K N Y^*}/\text{Im}(M_R)|^2 = 4.23 \times 10^{-10}$ MeV$^{-4}$.
- The product of coupling strengths for the B1 pole is $(7.94 \times 10^{-4}, 102^\circ)$ with $|g_{\pi\Sigma Y^*}g_{\bar K N Y^*}/\text{Im}(M_R)|^2 = 6.56 \times 10^{-10}$ MeV$^{-4}$.
- The product of coupling strengths for the A2 pole is $(21.54 \times 10^{-4}, -24^\circ)$ with $|g_{\pi\Sigma Y^*}g_{\bar K N Y^*}/\text{Im}(M_R)|^2 = 14.79 \times 10^{-10}$ MeV$^{-4}$.
- The product of coupling strengths for the B2 pole is $(13.87 \times 10^{-4}, -56^\circ)$ with $|g_{\pi\Sigma Y^*}g_{\bar K N Y^*}/\text{Im}(M_R)|^2 = 2.00 \times 10^{-10}$ MeV$^{-4}$.

## Context

This work extends the DCC analysis of $K^- p$ reactions from Kamano et al. (2014) to the $\bar K d \to \pi Y N$ reaction, aiming to overcome the limitation that $K^- p$ reactions cannot directly access the energy region below the $\bar K N$ threshold. It differs from prior calculations by Ohnishi (2015), Jido (2009), and Yamagata (2006) by using $\bar K N$ amplitudes that describe data far above threshold, which is necessary for the high-momentum J-PARC E31 kinematics. The results enable combined analyses of $K^- p$ and $K^- d$ data to establish low-lying hyperon resonances, particularly the two-pole structure of $\Lambda(1405)$ and a candidate $J^P = 1/2^+$ $\Sigma$ resonance.
