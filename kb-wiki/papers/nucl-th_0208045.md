# Two-Body Dirac Equations for Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

> We investigate the nucleon-nucleon interaction by using the meson exchange model and the two body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics. This approach to the two body problem has been successfully tested for QED and QCD relativistic bound states. An important question we wish to address is whether or not the two body nucleon-nucleon scattering problem can be reasonably described in this approach as well. This test involves a number of related problems. First we must reduce our two body Dirac equations exactly to a Schroedinger-like equation in such a way that allows us to use techniques to solve our two body Dirac equations already developed for Schroedinger-like systems in nonrelativistic quantum mechanics. Related to this we present a new derivation of Calogero's variable phase shift differential equation for coupled Schroedinger-like equations. Then we determine if the use of nine meson exchanges in our two body Dirac equations give a reasonable fit to the experimental scattering phase shifts for n-p scattering. The data involves seven angular momentum states including both singlet and the triplet spin states with orbital angular momentum 0,1,2. Two models that we have tested give us a fairly good fit. The parameters obtained by fitting the n-p experimental scattering phase shift give a fairly good prediction for most of the p-p experimental scattering phase shifts examined (for the spin singlet S and D states and spin triplet P states). Thus the two body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics presents us with a fit that encourages the exploration of a more realistic model. We outline generalizations of the meson exchange model for invariant potentials that may possibly improve the fit.

# Two-Body Dirac Equations for Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

## Key claim

The two-body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics, when combined with a nine-meson exchange model, can provide a "fairly good fit" to experimental n-p scattering phase shifts for seven angular momentum states (singlet and triplet with orbital angular momentum 0, 1, 2) up to 350 MeV, and the parameters from n-p fitting give a "fairly good prediction" for most p-p scattering phase shifts examined, though the authors note this encourages "exploration of a more realistic model" rather than claiming a definitive solution.

## Method

The authors reduce the two-body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics exactly to coupled Schrödinger-like equations, eliminating first-derivative terms to apply Calogero's variable phase shift method for coupled channels. They construct local, covariant potentials from nine meson exchanges (scalar, vector, and pseudoscalar mesons with masses below about 1000 MeV) and fit the resulting phase shifts to experimental n-p scattering data for seven angular momentum states. The fitted parameters are then used to predict p-p scattering phase shifts for selected singlet and triplet states.

## Key numbers

- The scattering data covers laboratory energies up to 350 MeV.
- Seven angular momentum states are fitted: singlet states \(^{1}S_{0}\), \(^{1}P_{1}\), \(^{1}D_{2}\) and triplet states \(^{3}P_{0}\), \(^{3}P_{1}\), \(^{3}S_{1}\), \(^{3}D_{1}\).
- Nine meson exchanges are used, all with masses less than or about 1000 MeV.
- The p-p prediction covers singlet states \(^{1}S_{0}\), \(^{1}D_{2}\) and triplet states \(^{3}P_{0}\), \(^{3}P_{1}\).

## Context

This work extends the constraint dynamics approach previously tested nonperturbatively in QED (positronium and muonium bound states) and QCD (meson spectroscopy) by Crater, Van Alstine, and Long (1990s) to the nucleon-nucleon scattering problem. It contrasts with other relativistic schemes such as those by Gross (1980s) and Stoks (1990s), which use different relativistic frameworks. The results enable future exploration of more realistic meson exchange models with additional interaction types (tensor and pseudovector mesons) that the authors outline as generalizations.
