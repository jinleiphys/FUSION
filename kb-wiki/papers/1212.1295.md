# A new method for extracting poles from single-channel data based on Laurent expansion of T-matrices with Pietarinen power series representing the non-singular part

> We present a new approach to quantifying pole parameters of single-channel processes based on Laurent expansion of partial wave T-matrices. Instead of guessing the analytical form of non-singular part of Laurent expansion as it is usually done, we represent it by the convergent series of Pietarinen functions. As the analytic structure of non-singular term is usually very well known (physical cuts with branhcpoints at inelastic thresholds, and unphysical cuts in the negative energy plane), we show that we need one Pietarinen series per cut, and the number of terms in each Pietarinen series is automatically determined by the quality of the fit. The method is tested on a toy model constructed from two known poles, various background terms, and two physical cuts, and shown to be robust and confident up to three Pietarinen series. We also apply this method to Zagreb CMB amplitudes for the N(1535) 1/2- resonance, and confirm the full success of the method on realistic data. This formalism can also be used for fitting experimental data, and the procedure is very similar as when Breit-Wigner functions are used, but with one modification: Laurent expansion with Pietarinen series is replacing the standard Breit-Wigner T-matrix form.

# A new method for extracting poles from single-channel data based on Laurent expansion of T-matrices with Pietarinen power series representing the non-singular part

## Key claim

The Laurent expansion plus Pietarinen series (L+P) method can extract pole parameters from single-channel T-matrices without guessing the analytic form of the background, but the method is not model-independent; its model dependence is fixed naturally by fitting to real data, and the number of Pietarinen series and their branch points are determined by the quality of the fit rather than imposed a priori.

## Method

The authors represent the partial wave T-matrix as a Laurent expansion separating singular terms (poles) from a regular background, then expand the background as a sum of Pietarinen power series, one series per known cut (physical cuts at inelastic thresholds and an effective negative-energy cut). The fit is performed on the real energy axis using a chi-squared minimization with a penalty function that automatically determines the minimal number of Pietarinen terms needed. The method is tested on a toy model with two known poles, two physical cuts, and various background terms, then applied to the Zagreb CMB amplitude for the N(1535) 1/2- resonance and to GWU single-energy partial wave data.

## Key numbers

- Toy model pole positions were perfectly reproduced: first pole at M1 = 1.650 GeV, Gamma1 = 0.165 GeV (input: 1.65, 0.165); second pole at M2 = 2.250 GeV, Gamma2 = 0.200 GeV (input: 2.25, 0.200), with reduced chi-squared as low as 0.01 x 10^-2 for optimal fits.
- For the Zagreb CMB N(1535) 1/2- amplitude, L+P with three poles and three Pietarinen series gave pole positions: M1 = 1.529 - i 0.073 GeV, M2 = 1.647 - i 0.096 GeV, M3 = 1.801 - i 1.160 GeV, compared to Zagreb CMB poles of M1 = 1.521 - i 0.095 GeV, M2 = 1.646 - i 0.102 GeV, M3 = 1.790 - i 0.210 GeV.
- Physical Pietarinen branch points for the N(1535) fit converged to xQ = 1.057 GeV (near piN elastic threshold) and xR = 1.482 GeV (near eta production threshold).
- For the toy model, fitting only the absolute value (strategy b) reproduced pole positions but gave arbitrary residues, as expected.

## Context

This work extends the Laurent expansion approach previously investigated by others, where the freedom in choosing the analytic form of the background introduced severe ambiguities. The novelty is replacing the arbitrary background function with Pietarinen series, originally introduced by Ciulli and Pietarinen and used successfully in the Karlsruhe-Helsinki partial wave analysis (Hoehler, 1984). The method enables direct pole extraction from experimental data without assuming a final functional form for the scattering matrix regular part, unlike standard methods such as analytic continuation of model functions (Doering, EBAC, CMB, Zagreb, Bonn), speed plot (Hoehler, 1993), time delay (Kelkar), N/D method (Chew and Mandelstam), or regularization procedures (Ceci, 2008). The approach can be used for fitting experimental data similarly to Breit-Wigner functions but with Laurent expansion plus Pietarinen series replacing the standard Breit-Wigner T-matrix form.
