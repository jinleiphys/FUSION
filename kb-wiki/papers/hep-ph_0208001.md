# A Roadmap For Meson Spectroscopy

> An efficient classification of light quark meson states is discussed based on the dominance of angular and radial quark excitation. A synthesis of Regge and quark dynamics allows a natural unification of light quark-antiquark spectroscopies and indicates the states that fall outside this category such as molecules, hybrids, and glueballs.

# A Roadmap For Meson Spectroscopy

## Key claim
Light quark meson states can be efficiently classified by grouping them according to orbital angular momentum and radial excitation quantum numbers, using a synthesis of Regge trajectory and quark dynamics that is relatively model-independent. The classification identifies which observed states fit the ordinary quark-antiquark pattern and which fall outside as candidates for molecules, hybrids, or glueballs, though the author acknowledges that spin splittings are somewhat larger for light quarks than for heavy onia.

## Method
The author plots the 56 meson states assigned to constituent quark configurations by the Particle Data Group on a "fundamental" Regge plot of squared energy E²/(2πa) versus orbital angular momentum L, using a string tension a = 0.16 GeV² to give unit slope. For each strange quark in a meson, 0.1 GeV is subtracted to account for the differing quark mass. The observed states are compared to theoretical trajectories from the QCD flux tube model with linear confinement (Eq. 1: E²/2πa = L + 2n - 1/2) and to trajectories including a short-range Coulomb term V(r) = -k/r with k = 1.

## Key numbers
- String tension a = 0.16 GeV² yields the universal Regge slope of 1 GeV⁻².
- The flux tube spectrum formula is E²/2πa = L + 2n - 1/2, where L = 0, 1, 2, ... and n = 1, 2, ...
- Of the 56 assigned quark model states from the PDG, all 32 expected states of the 1S, 2S, and 1P groups are present.
- The 1D group has 12 of the expected 16 states; the 3S group has 4 of 8 states; the 2P and 1F groups each have 4 of the expected 16 states.
- The short-range Coulomb shift for large L reduces E²/(2πa) by k/2 (with k = 1).
- Two exotic mesons with J^PC = 1⁻⁺ are noted at 1.4 and 1.6 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the approach used for heavy onia (bottomonium and charmonium) to light quark mesons, where spin splittings are larger but the same orbital and radial classification applies. It builds on prior quark model work by Godfrey and others, and on QCD string/flux tube calculations by Collins, Dan, and Ted. The classification provides a tool to identify which observed mesons are ordinary quark-antiquark states and which are candidates for new forms of matter such as the f₀(1.50) as a possible glueball, the f₀(0.98) and a₀(0.98) as four-quark/molecule states, and the 1⁻⁺ exotics as hybrid meson candidates.
