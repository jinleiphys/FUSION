# Consistent analysis of the reaction $γp \to p η^\prime$ and $pp \to ppη^\prime$

> The production of $η'$ mesons in the reactions $γp\to pη'$ and $pp\to ppη'$ is described consistently within a relativistic meson exchange model of hadronic interactions. The photoproduction can be described quite well over the entire energy range of available data by considering an $S_{11}$ and a $P_{11}$ resonance, in addition to the $t$-channel mesonic current. The observed angular distribution is due to the interference between the $t$-channel and the nucleon resonance $s$- and $u$-channel contributions. Our analysis yields positions close to 1650 MeV and 1870 MeV for the $S_{11}$ and $P_{11}$ resonances, respectively. We argue that, at present, identifying these states with the known $S_{11}(1650)$ resonance and the missing $P_{11}$ resonance predicted at 1880 MeV, respectively, would be premature. It is found that the nucleonic current is relatively small and that the $NNη^\prime$ coupling constant cannot be much larger than $g_{NNη^\prime}=3$. As for the $p p \to p p η^\prime$ reaction, different current contributions are constrained by a combined analysis of this and the photoproduction reaction. Difficulties to simultaneously account for the 47-MeV and 144-MeV angular distributions measured by the COSY-11 and DISTO collaborations, respectively, are addressed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the reactions γp → pη′ and pp → ppη′ can be described consistently within a relativistic meson exchange model, but the authors caution that identifying the extracted S11 and P11 resonances with the known S11(1650) and a missing P11 resonance predicted at 1880 MeV would be premature at present.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic meson exchange model for γp → pη′ including t-channel ρ and ω meson exchange, s- and u-channel nucleon and resonance contributions (S11 and P11), and a gauge-invariance-preserving contact current. The photoproduction parameters were fitted to SAPHIR data, then the resulting production current was used as input for the pp → ppη′ reaction within a Distorted-Wave Born Approximation that includes nucleon-nucleon initial-state and final-state interactions, with the Bonn potential generating the final-state interaction.

## Key numbers

- S11 resonance mass extracted at 1646 MeV (column c fit) and 1650 MeV (fixed in column d fits)
- P11 resonance mass extracted at 1873 MeV (column c) and 1849–1870 MeV (column d)
- NNη′ coupling constant constrained to gNNη′ ≤ 3, with best-fit value 2.22 when nucleonic current is included
- Vector meson cutoff parameter Λv = 1400 MeV (column c) and 1225–1286 MeV (column d)
- S11 and P11 resonance widths fixed at ΓN* = 150 MeV
- η′ρ and η′ω radiative decay coupling constants fixed at gη′ργ = 1.25 and gη′ωγ = 0.44
- η′ρρ and η′ωω coupling constants fixed at gη′ρρ = 4.94 and gη′ωω = 4.90
- Mixing parameter λ for NNη′ vertex near 0 (pseudovector) for nucleon and near 1 (pseudoscalar) for resonances

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of η′ photoproduction by Zhang et al., Li, Zhao, Borasoy, Chiang et al., and Sibirtsev et al., and the authors' own previous work on pp → ppη′ (Nakayama, 2003). The combined analysis constrains the NNη′ coupling constant and resonance parameters more tightly than separate analyses of either reaction alone. The results enable future extractions of the flavor-singlet axial charge via the Goldberger-Treiman relation and provide a framework for interpreting new data from the CLAS collaboration at JLab and the Crystal Barrel collaboration at ELSA.
