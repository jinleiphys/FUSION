# Structure of the number projected BCS wave function

> We study the structure of the number projected BCS (PBCS) wave function in the particle-hole basis, displaying its similarities with coupled clusters theory (CCT). The analysis of PBCS together with several modifications suggested by the CCT wave function is carried out for the exactly solvable Richardson model involving a pure pairing hamiltonian acting in a space of equally-spaced doubly-degenerate levels. We point out the limitations of PBCS to describe the non-superconducting regime and suggest possible avenues for improvement.

## Key claim

The number projected BCS (PBCS) wave function, when expanded in the particle-hole basis, has a characteristic inverse factorial-square coefficient structure that limits its ability to describe pairing correlations in the weak-coupling (non-superconducting) regime, even though it gives exact results in the strong-coupling limit.

## Method

The authors expand the PBCS wave function in the particle-hole basis and compare it with coupled cluster theory of doubles (p-CCD) and an exponential form obtained by replacing the factorial-square with a simple factorial. They test these approximations against the exact Richardson solution for the reduced BCS Hamiltonian (picket-fence model) with equally-spaced doubly-degenerate levels at half filling, using 20 levels and 20 particles. A variational interpolation between the exponential and PBCS forms is introduced via a parameter α that controls the factorial exponent.

## Key numbers

- The model uses 20 doubly-degenerate levels at half filling (20 particles), with equally-spaced single-particle energies separated by ε.
- The critical pairing strength Gc (in units of ε) separates the fluctuation-dominated regime (zero gap) from the superconducting regime (finite gap), determined by the BCS equations.
- The variational parameter α interpolates between α=1 (exponential limit) at G=0 and α=2 (PBCS limit) in the extreme superconducting limit.
- The p-CCD approximation fails completely in the superconducting phase, with no solution of the p-CCD equations for large G.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of PBCS with p-CCD and self-consistent RPA (SCRPA) by Hirsch et al. (2002) and Sierra et al. (2002), which showed that those approximations describe the weak-coupling limit well but fail near the transition to superfluidity. The paper also builds on the rediscovery of Richardson's exact solution (1963) for the reduced BCS Hamiltonian, which was exploited by Delft et al. (1996) as a benchmark. The results enable future development of variational theories that include quartet correlations (Sandulescu et al., 2008) or non-variational coupled cluster expansions that interpolate between exponential and PBCS forms (Tom et al., 2016).
