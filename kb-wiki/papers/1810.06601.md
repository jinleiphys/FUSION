# Nuclear-structure corrections to the hyperfine splitting in muonic deuterium

> Nuclear structure corrections of orders $Zα\, E_F$ and $(Zα)^2 E_F$ are calculated for the hyperfine splitting of the muonic deuterium. The obtained results disagree with previous calculations and lead to a $5\,σ$ disagreement with the current experimental value of the $2S$ hyperfine splitting in $μ$D.

## Key claim

The authors calculate nuclear-structure corrections to the hyperfine splitting in muonic deuterium at orders \(Z\alpha\,E_F\) and \((Z\alpha)^2 E_F\), and find that previous calculations by Borie (2014) and Faustov et al. (2014) were incorrect, leading to a \(5\sigma\) disagreement with the experimental value of the \(2S\) hyperfine splitting; the earlier agreement with experiment was likely accidental.

## Method

The two-photon exchange correction was computed by separating the elastic Zemach term, the Low correction, individual nucleon contributions, and the nuclear vector polarizability, using a perturbation expansion in the ratio of nucleon binding energy to nucleon mass. The three-photon exchange elastic correction was derived by splitting the total into low-energy and high-energy parts, employing dimensional regularization to cancel singularities, and verifying the analytic result against a numerical all-order Dirac equation calculation. The inelastic three-photon exchange correction was treated approximately, focusing on the electric dipole polarizability contribution.

## Key numbers

- Fermi energy for the \(2S\) state of muonic deuterium: \(E_F = 6.13594\ \text{meV}\)
- Experimental hyperfine splitting of the \(2S\) state: \(E_{\text{hfs}}(2S)_{\text{exp}} = 6.2747(70)_{\text{stat}}(20)_{\text{syst}}\ \text{meV}\)
- Point-nucleus theoretical result for the \(2S\) state: \(E_{\text{hfs}}(\text{point}) = 6.17815 \pm 0.00020\ \text{meV}\)
- Experimental nuclear-structure correction: \(\delta E_{\text{nucl}} = 0.0966 \pm 0.0073\ \text{meV}\)
- Zemach correction for the \(2S\) state: \(\delta E_{\text{Zem}} = -0.1177(33)\ \text{meV}\)
- Low correction for the \(2S\) state: \(\delta E_{\text{Low}} = 2.566\ \text{meV}\) (scaled from electronic deuterium)
- Elastic three-photon exchange correction for the \(2S\) state of muonic hydrogen: \(\delta^{(2)} E_{\text{fns}}(\mu\text{H}) = -0.00096\ \text{meV}\)
- Mean excitation energy of the deuteron: \(\bar{E} = 7.37(7)\ \text{MeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of nuclear-structure corrections to hyperfine splitting in muonic deuterium, correcting earlier calculations by Borie (2014) and Faustov et al. (2014). The authors show that Borie included only the elastic part, which has the wrong sign, and that Faustov et al. inconsistently included both the Zemach and Low corrections while using an incorrect formula for the nuclear polarizability derived by Khriplovich and Milstein (2004) for electronic atoms. The results enable improved interpretation of the experimental measurement by Pohl et al. (2016) and highlight the need for further detailed investigations of the nuclear vector polarizability, which is the dominant source of theoretical uncertainty.
