# Charmonium dissociation and recombination at RHIC and LHC

> Charmonium production at heavy-ion colliders is considered within the comovers interaction model. The formalism is extended by including possible secondary J/psi production through recombination and an estimate of recombination effects is made with no free parameters involved. The comovers interaction model also includes a comprehensive treatment of initial-state nuclear effects, which are discussed in the context of such high energies. With these tools, the model properly describes the centrality and the rapidity dependence of experimental data at RHIC energy, $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV, for both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions. Predictions for LHC, $\sqrt{s}$ = 5.5 TeV, are presented and the assumptions and extrapolations involved are discussed.

## Key claim

The comovers interaction model (CIM), extended to include secondary J/psi production via recombination of ccbar pairs with no additional free parameters, can reproduce the centrality and rapidity dependence of J/psi suppression at RHIC, provided the recombination term is included; without it the model predicts too strong a suppression.

## Method

The authors extend the CIM by adding a gain term to the rate equation for J/psi density, proportional to the squared density of open charm (ccbar pairs) in the collision, assuming the recombination cross section equals the dissociation cross section (0.65 mb). Initial-state nuclear effects are treated with updated nuclear shadowing from diffractive gluon distributions, nuclear absorption with an energy-dependent parameterization, and energy-momentum conservation. The density of comovers is calculated using the dual parton model with shadowing corrections, and the recombination term is estimated from measured pp cross sections for open charm and J/psi at the same energy, with no free parameters.

## Key numbers

* At RHIC sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the effective dissociation cross section used is sigma_co = 0.65 mb, fixed from SPS data.
* At mid-rapidity in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the open charm cross section is 123 +/- 12 +/- 45 microbarns and the J/psi cross section (times branching ratio) is 44.3 +/- 1.4 +/- 5.1 nb, giving C = 0.59.
* At forward rapidity at RHIC, using PYTHIA for open charm (70.9 +/- 14 microbarns) and measured J/psi (27.61 +/- 0.37 +/- 0.83 nb), the parameter C = 0.32.
* The comover density at mid-rapidity in pp collisions is 2.24 fm^-2 at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV and 4.34 fm^-2 at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV.
* For LHC predictions, the parameter C is estimated to be in the range 2 to 3, about four times larger than at RHIC, based on sigma_cc proportional to s^alpha with alpha = 0.3.

## Context

This work extends the comovers interaction model previously applied to SPS data by Armesto et al. (1999) and Capella et al. (2005), which had predicted stronger suppression at RHIC than observed. The inclusion of recombination resolves the discrepancy with PHENIX data (2007) for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, and contradicts the claim by Linnyk (2007) that the CIM fails at forward rapidity. The predictions for LHC contrast with those of Braun et al. (2000), who predicted strong J/psi enhancement from statistical coalescence, and depend critically on the poorly known total charm cross section in pp collisions at 5.5 TeV.
