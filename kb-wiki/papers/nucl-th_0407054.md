# Energy dependence of transverse quark flow in heavy ion collisions

> Energy dependence of quark transverse flow carries information about dynamical properties (equation of state, initial conditions) of deconfined matter produced in heavy ion collisions. We assume quark-antiquark matter formation in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies and determine quark transverse flow at the critical temperature of the quark-hadron phase transition. Coalescence of massive quarks is calculated in the MICOR hadronization model and hadronic final state effects are considered using the GROMIT cascade program. Comparing theoretical results to data, transverse flow values are determined and energy dependence is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper determines the transverse flow velocity of quark-antiquark matter at the critical temperature of the quark-hadron phase transition by comparing hadronic momentum spectra from the MICOR coalescence model to experimental data, but the extracted flow values depend on whether final-state hadronic interactions are included or neglected, and the authors cannot decide between these two scenarios at RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors assume formation of thermally equilibrated quark-antiquark matter in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, with a critical temperature of 170 MeV. They apply the MICOR hadronization model, which uses coalescence of massive quarks and antiquarks with small relative momentum, to calculate transverse momentum spectra for pions, kaons, and protons at low transverse momentum (p_T < 2 GeV). The quark transverse flow velocity v_T is the single free parameter, and results are compared to experimental data both with and without final-state hadronic interactions simulated by the GROMIT cascade program.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for quark-hadron phase transition: T_c = 170 MeV
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC Au+Au), MICOR reproduces data with v_T = 0.60 (neglecting final state interactions) or v_T = 0.55 (including final state interactions)
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV (RHIC), quark transverse flow is v_T = 0.55 +/- 0.02 (neglecting final state interactions)
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV (SPS Pb+Pb, 158 A GeV), MICOR reproduces data with v_T = 0.49 (neglecting final state interactions) or v_T = 0.44 +/- 0.02 (including final state interactions)
- At SPS energies of 80 A GeV and 40 A GeV, including final state interactions yields v_T = 0.40 and v_T = 0.38, respectively
- The difference between flow values with and without final state interactions is v_T(no FSI) - v_T(FSI) approximately 0.04
- MICOR parameters at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV: eta_max = 1.9, N_q\bar q = 720, f_s = 0.35
- MICOR parameters at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: eta_max = 4, N_q\bar q = 3800, f_s = 0.26
- The number of newly produced quark-antiquark pairs at mid-rapidity follows dN_q\bar q/dy = 130 * ln(sqrt(s_NN)/2.5)

## Context

This work extends coalescence-based hadronization models, previously applied successfully at RHIC energies in the intermediate momentum region (2 < p_T < 6 GeV) by Hwa and Yang (2003), Fries et al. (2003), and others, to the low-p_T region (p_T < 2 GeV) across a wide energy range from sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 to 200 GeV. The results enable future studies of the equation of state of deconfined matter, as a possible plateau in transverse flow between SPS and RHIC energies could indicate modifications in the equation of state; the authors call for new experimental data in the energy range 20 < sqrt(s_NN) < 130 GeV to resolve this question.
