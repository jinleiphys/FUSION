# Model independent extraction of the proton charge radius from electron scattering

> Constraints from analyticity are combined with experimental electron-proton scattering data to determine the proton charge radius. In contrast to previous determinations, we provide a systematic procedure for analyzing arbitrary data without model-dependent assumptions on the form factor shape. We also investigate the impact of including electron-neutron scattering data, and $ππ\to N\bar{N}$ data. Using representative datasets we find r_E^p=0.870 +/- 0.023 +/- 0.012 fm using just proton scattering data; r_E^p=0.880^{+0.017}_{-0.020} +/- 0.007 fm adding neutron data; and r_E^p=0.871 +/- 0.009 +/- 0.002 +/- 0.002 fm adding $ππ$ data. The analysis can be readily extended to other nucleon form factors and derived observables.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic, model-independent procedure for extracting the proton charge radius from electron scattering data by using analyticity constraints (a conformal mapping expansion) and bounded expansion coefficients, without assuming a specific functional form for the form factor. The authors hedge that their results depend on the choice of datasets and the conservative bound \(|a_k|\le 10\) on the expansion coefficients, and that the procedure yields consistent radii across different data combinations but with uncertainties that remain larger than those from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy.

## Method

The authors perform a conformal mapping of the spacelike momentum transfer variable \(t=q^2\) to a variable \(z\) that maps the cut plane (cut beginning at the two-pion threshold \(4m_\pi^2\)) onto the unit disk, then expand the Sachs electric form factor \(G_E^p\) as a power series in \(z\). They fit this expansion to electron-proton scattering data (85 points with \(Q^2\lesssim 0.04\) GeV\(^2\) from Rosenfelder (1999) and higher-\(Q^2\) data from Arrington et al. (2007)), imposing a conservative bound \(|a_k|\le 10\) on the expansion coefficients derived from a vector dominance model and from explicit \(\pi\pi\) continuum contributions. They also perform combined fits including electron-neutron scattering data and \(\pi\pi\to N\bar{N}\) data to raise the effective isovector threshold.

## Key numbers

* Proton charge radius from proton scattering data only: \(r_E^p = 0.870 \pm 0.023 \pm 0.012\) fm.
* Proton charge radius adding neutron data: \(r_E^p = 0.880^{+0.017}_{-0.020} \pm 0.007\) fm.
* Proton charge radius adding \(\pi\pi\) data: \(r_E^p = 0.871 \pm 0.009 \pm 0.002 \pm 0.002\) fm.
* For the Rosenfelder (1999) dataset (\(Q^2\lesssim 0.04\) GeV\(^2\)), the \(z\) expansion with \(k_{\rm max}=5\) and \(|a_k|\le 10\) yields \(r_E^p = 0.880^{+0.039}_{-0.062}\) fm (in units of \(10^{-18}\) m, i.e., \(0.880^{+0.039}_{-0.062}\) fm).
* The conformal mapping parameter \(|z|_{\rm max}\) is 0.062 for \(Q^2_{\rm max}=0.05\) GeV\(^2\) and 0.25 for \(Q^2_{\rm max}=0.5\) GeV\(^2\).
* The neutron charge radius from neutron-electron scattering is \(\langle r^2\rangle_E^n = -0.1161(22)\) fm\(^2\) (Nakamura 2010).

## Context

This work extends the use of analyticity-constrained \(z\)-expansions, previously applied to meson transition form factors (Hill 2006, Bourrely 1980, Boyd 1994, Lellouch 1995, Caprini 1997, Arnesen 2005, Becher 2005, Bharucha 2010), to the nucleon form factor problem. It directly addresses the discrepancy between the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift result \(r_E^p=0.84184(67)\) fm (Pohl 2010) and the CODATA value \(0.8768(69)\) fm (Mohr 2008), as well as the wide spread of 12 previous electron scattering determinations (0.8-0.9 fm) listed in the Review of Particle Physics (Nakamura 2010). The method enables future model-independent extractions of other nucleon form factors and derived observables, and can incorporate additional experimental input (e.g., higher-\(Q^2\) data, neutron data, \(\pi\pi\) data) to reduce uncertainties.
