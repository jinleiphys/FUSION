# The pion form factor from first principles

> We calculate the electromagnetic form factor of the pion in quenched lattice QCD. The non-perturbatively improved Sheikoleslami-Wohlert lattice action is used together with the ${\mathcal O}(a)$ improved current. We calculate form factor for pion masses down to $m_π= 360 MeV$. We compare the mean square radius for the pion extracted from our form factors to the value obtained from the 'Bethe Salpeter amplitude'. Using (quenched) chiral perturbation theory, we extrapolate our results towards the physical pion mass.

# The pion form factor from first principles

## Key claim
The electromagnetic form factor of the pion is calculated in quenched lattice QCD using non-perturbatively improved actions and currents, with results that are consistent with vector meson dominance. However, the mean-square charge radius extracted from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is systematically lower than the value obtained directly from the form factor, and shows almost no dependence on the pion mass.

## Method
The authors compute two- and three-point correlation functions for the pion on a 24³×32 lattice with quenched gluon configurations at β=6.0, using the non-perturbatively improved Sheikoleslami-Wohlert action with c_SW=1.796. Quark propagators are calculated for five values of the hopping parameter κ, yielding pion masses from 360 to 970 MeV. The form factor is extracted from simultaneous fits of all parameters in the two- and three-point functions per configuration, using a single elimination jackknife procedure for error estimation.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses range from 360 to 970 MeV, calculated for five different κ-values on a 24³×32 lattice at β=6.0 with lattice spacing a=0.105 fm.
- The effect of current improvement (comparing the Noether current to the improved current) can be as large as 25% for the highest momenta considered.
- The vector meson mass extracted from monopole fits to the form factor is within 5% of the corresponding rho mass on the lattice.
- The Ward-Takahashi identity is satisfied to 1 ppm at Q²=0.

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice studies of the pion form factor by Martinelli and Sachrajda and by Draper et al., who showed the form factor could be described by a simple monopole form consistent with vector meson dominance. The authors adopt improved lattice techniques from Luscher, Sheikholeslami, Sachrajda, and Sommer to systematically eliminate O(a) discretisation errors. The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude method, previously used by Chu, Hecht, Gupta, Lacock, and Schmidt, is shown to yield radii that are systematically lower and almost mass-independent, a known deficit that this work makes quantitative. The results enable chiral extrapolations toward the physical pion mass using quenched chiral perturbation theory, with comparisons to linear and VMD-based parametrisations.
