# Light-front versus Bethe-Salpeter forms of two nucleon amplitudes

> We discuss the relation between the two nucleon Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and the light front wave functions. Both approaches provide a covariant description for the deuteron bound state and the two nucleon scattering state. A comparison is done for the spin-orbit functions as well explicit integrals are given on the basis of the Nakanishi integral representation method.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a formal mapping between the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and the light-front wave function for the two-nucleon system, showing that the light-front projection reduces the number of independent spin-orbital components (from eight to six for the deuteron channel and from four to two for the \(^1S_0\) channel) because the nucleon momenta are on-shell in the light-front formalism, and that the Nakanishi integral representation provides a direct connection between the two approaches without cancelation of functions.

## Method

The authors construct the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the two-nucleon system in both direct-product and matrix representations using \(\rho\)-spin notation, then project it onto the light front via an integral relation that integrates over the off-shell momentum component. They express the resulting light-front wave functions in terms of the invariant functions \(h_i\) of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and further analyze the connection using the Nakanishi integral representation, which expresses the radial amplitudes as integrals over weight functions \(g_\ell(\alpha,z)\).

## Key numbers

* The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the deuteron (\(J=1\)) channel consists of eight states: \(^3S_1^{++}\), \(^3S_1^{--}\), \(^3D_1^{++}\), \(^3D_1^{--}\), \(^3P_1^{e}\), \(^3P_1^{o}\), \(^1P_1^{e}\), \(^1P_1^{o}\).
* The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the \(J=0\) channel consists of four states: \(^1S_0^{++}\), \(^1S_0^{--}\), \(^3P_0^{e}\), \(^3P_0^{o}\).
* The light-front projection reduces the deuteron channel from eight to six independent functions and the \(^1S_0\) channel from four to two independent functions.
* The Nakanishi integral representation uses an integer \(n \geq 2\) in the denominator \((\alpha+\kappa^2-p^2-z\,P\cdot p-i\epsilon)^n\), where \(\kappa=m^2-M_d^2/4\).
* The light-front variable \(x = \omega\cdot k_1 / \omega\cdot P\) is related to the Nakanishi variable \(z\) by \(z = 1-2x\).

## Context

This work extends the comparison of Bethe-Salpeter and light-front approaches for the deuteron that was partially addressed in Car98, providing explicit integral relations and the use of the Nakanishi representation (Nakanishi) that had not been applied to the spinor case previously. The results enable direct calculation of light-front wave functions from Bethe-Salpeter solutions, which is relevant for relativistic treatments of deuteron electrodisintegration and for three-particle dynamics where light-front constructions of nucleon amplitudes in terms of three valence quarks are being discussed (BKW98, Karm98). The paper also relates specific functions (\(h_5\) and \(h_2\) in the Bethe-Salpeter approach, \(f_5\) and \(g_2\) in the light-front approach) to pair currents, building on earlier work by Arenhoevel.
