# Model independent results for nucleon structure

> I review recent results regarding the nucleon charge and magnetization densities as well as the shape of the nucleon. First, some phenomenolgical considerations that show that the shape of the proton is not round are discussed. Then model independent results regarding the neutron and proton charge density, and the proton magnetization density are presented. Finally, I show how the spin-dependent densities that reveal the shape of the proton can be measured via their relation with transverse momentum distributions. The present work is made possible by the recent tremendous experimental progress made at Bates, Mainz and Jefferson Laboratory.

# Model independent results for nucleon structure

## Key claim
The proton is not round, and this non-spherical shape can be revealed through spin-dependent densities (SDDs) that are experimentally accessible via transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs), specifically the function \(h_{1T}^\perp\); if \(h_{1T}^\perp\) is non-zero, the proton is not round.

## Method
The author reviews model-independent results for nucleon charge and magnetization densities obtained by taking two-dimensional Fourier transforms of the Sachs form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) (or equivalently \(F_1\) and \(F_2\)) using transversely localized nucleon states in the infinite momentum frame. Spin-dependent densities are introduced via matrix elements of operators that combine position and spin, and are linked to TMDs by integrating over the longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\) and the longitudinal coordinate \(\xi^-\), showing that the TMD function \(h_{1T}^\perp\) encodes the non-spherical shape. The analysis uses recent parameterizations of measured form factors from Bates, Mainz, and Jefferson Laboratory.

## Key numbers
- The central up quark density in the proton is larger than that of the down quark by about 30%, determined using charge symmetry from the form factor analysis.
- The neutron charge density is negative at the center, positive in the middle, and negative again at the outer edge, as shown by the quantity \(b\rho(b)\) which integrates to zero.
- The ratio \(G_E/G_M\) was expected to be flat at large momentum transfer based on helicity conservation, but the reverse was observed experimentally (Jones, 1999).

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier relativistic constituent quark model (Frank, 1995) which predicted the qualitative behavior of form factors and showed that quark orbital angular momentum causes the flat nature of \(QF_2/F_1\). It contradicts the early expectation that the proton is spherical and that \(G_E/G_M\) would be constant at high \(Q^2\). The results enable experimental tests of the proton's shape through semi-inclusive leptoproduction reactions such as \(ep^\uparrow \rightarrow e' \pi X\), where the TMD function \(h_{1T}^\perp\) causes a distinctive oscillatory dependence on the angle \(3\phi - \phi_{S_1}\), with planned measurements at Jefferson Laboratory (Harut, 2008).
