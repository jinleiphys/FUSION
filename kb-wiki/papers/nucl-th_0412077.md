# Strange particle production in a single-freeze-out model

> The transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow of strange particles are calculated in the framework of the single-freeze-out model and compared with the recent RHIC data. An overall very good agreement between the model predictions and the data is obtained.

## Key claim

The single-freeze-out model, with parameters fitted exclusively to data for pions, kaons, and (anti)protons, successfully predicts the transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow of strange particles at RHIC, although some discrepancies remain for the normalization of Lambdas and antiprotons.

## Method

The authors use a single-freeze-out model with a parameterized freeze-out hypersurface and flow field that includes azimuthal asymmetry via deformation parameters epsilon (spatial eccentricity) and delta (flow anisotropy). The Cooper-Frye formula is used to compute transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow coefficients, with resonance decays included sequentially. Thermodynamic parameters (temperature T and baryon chemical potential mu_B) are fitted to hadronic abundance ratios, geometric parameters (lifetime tau and transverse size rho_max) to azimuthally averaged spectra, and deformation parameters (epsilon and delta) to HBT radii and v_2 data, all using only the most abundant particles.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV (most central): T = 165 MeV, mu_B = 41 MeV, tau = 7.66 fm, rho_max = 6.69 fm
- For Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (minimum bias): T = 165 MeV, mu_B = 26 MeV, tau = 4.04 fm, rho_max = 3.90 fm, delta = 0.25, epsilon = 0.13
- The model describes data in the soft region p_T < 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the single-freeze-out model of Broniowski et al. (2001-2002) from cylindrically symmetric systems to include azimuthal asymmetry, enabling predictions for elliptic flow. The results agree well with STAR data on hyperon spectra (Adams 2003) and elliptic flow of Lambda and multistrange baryons (Castillo 2004), and are comparable to the full hydrodynamic calculations of Hirano and Tsuda. The approach enables predictions for strange particle production without fitting to strange particle data, using only parameters determined from pions, kaons, and protons.
