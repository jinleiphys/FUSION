# Open charm production in high multiplicity proton-proton events at the LHC

> We present the dependence of $D$ production on the charged particle multiplicity in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies. We show that, in a framework of source coherence, the open charm production exhibits a growth with the multiplicity which is stronger than linear in the high density domain. This departure from linearity was previously observed in the $J/ψ$ inclusive data from proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV and was successfully described in our approach. Our assumption, the existence of coherence effects present in proton-proton collisions at high energy, applies for high multiplicity proton-proton collisions in the central rapidity region and should affect any hard observable.

## Key claim

The authors claim that open charm (D meson) production in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at LHC energies exhibits a growth with charged particle multiplicity that is stronger than linear, specifically transitioning to a quadratic dependence at high multiplicities. This claim is conditional on the assumption that coherence effects among color sources (strings) are present in the high-density domain, and the model applies only for central rapidity and high multiplicity events.

## Method

The authors model high-energy hadronic collisions as exchanges of color sources (strings) between projectile and target, where sources have finite spatial extension and can interact, reducing their effective number. They assume soft sources (driving total multiplicity) are affected by source interactions and scale roughly as the square root of the number of collisions, while hard sources (driving J/psi and D production) scale linearly with the number of collisions. For D mesons, they assume a 50% contribution from soft sources (linear with multiplicity) and 50% from hard sources (following the same quadratic behavior as J/psi), and compare their predictions to ALICE data for D^0 and D^+ production at 7 TeV in the central rapidity region.

## Key numbers

- At relative multiplicity dN/dη / ⟨dN/dη⟩ = 2, the relative D yield n_D/⟨n_D⟩ = 2.16, compared to 2.32 for J/ψ.
- At relative multiplicity dN/dη / ⟨dN/dη⟩ = 5, the relative D yield n_D/⟨n_D⟩ = 8.44, compared to 11.88 for J/ψ.
- At relative multiplicity dN/dη / ⟨dN/dη⟩ = 8, the relative D yield n_D/⟨n_D⟩ = 22.36, compared to 36.73 for J/ψ.
- The mean source density ⟨ρ⟩ ranges from 0.09 at relative multiplicity 1 to 0.58 at relative multiplicity 9.
- The percolation threshold for string percolation is η > 1, which can be achieved in pp collisions at 14 TeV for relative multiplicity dN/dη / ⟨dN/dη⟩ > 8.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Ferreiro and Pajares, 2012) that successfully described the rise of J/ψ production with multiplicity in pp collisions at 7 TeV observed by the ALICE collaboration (Abelev et al., 2012). The model is based on source coherence and string interaction effects, similar to shadowing in pA or AA collisions (Ferreiro et al., 2008) and parton saturation (Kharzeev et al., 2008). The results enable predictions for D meson production at higher LHC energies (14 TeV) and for pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV, though the authors note that cold nuclear matter effects complicate the application to pPb systems.
