# The muon capture rate on hydrogen and the values of $g_A$ and $g_{πNN}$

> Motivated by the recent developments in the determination of the experimental values of the nucleon axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$ and the pion-nucleon coupling constant $g_{πNN}$, we carry out a heavy-baryon chiral perturbation calculation of the hyperfine-singlet $μp$ capture rate $Γ_0$ to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO), with the use of the latest values of $g_A$ and $g_{πNN}$. The calculated N$^2$LO value is $Γ_0^{\rm theor}(μ^- p \to ν_μn) =718 \pm 7 \,s^{-1}$, where the estimated N$^3$LO contribution dominates the error. This value is in excellent agreement with the experimental value reported by the MuCap Collaboration.

# Muon capture rate on hydrogen and the values of \(g_A\) and \(g_{\pi NN}\)

## Key claim

The hyperfine-singlet muon capture rate on the proton, calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory using the latest experimental values of the nucleon axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) and the pion-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{\pi NN}\), gives \(\Gamma_0^{\rm theor} = 718 \pm 7\) s\(^{-1}\), where the dominant error is the estimated N\(^3\)LO contribution rather than uncertainties within the N\(^2\)LO calculation itself.

## Method

The authors performed a heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) calculation of the hyperfine-singlet muon capture rate \(\Gamma_0\) on the proton to N\(^2\)LO, using exactly the same formalism and input parameters as the prior work of Raha et al. (2013), except for the values of \(g_A\) and \(g_{\pi NN}\). They adopted \(g_A = 1.2758 \pm 0.0016\) from Mund et al. (2013) and Mendenhall et al. (2013), and \(g_{\pi NN} = 13.116 \pm 0.092\) from Baru et al. (2011), and evaluated \(\Gamma_0^{\rm theor}\) for four combinations of these constants at their upper and lower error bounds, including radiative corrections and finite proton-size effects as estimated in Raha et al. (2013).

## Key numbers

- \(\Gamma_0^{\rm theor}(\mu^- p \to \nu_\mu n) = 718 \pm 7\) s\(^{-1}\) at N\(^2\)LO, where the error is dominated by estimated N\(^3\)LO contributions
- The experimental value from the MuCap Collaboration is \(\Gamma_0^{\rm exp} = 714.9 \pm 5.4 (\text{stat}) \pm 5.1 (\text{syst})\) s\(^{-1}\)
- With \(g_A = 1.2774\) and \(g_{\pi NN} = 13.116\), \(\Gamma_0^{\rm theor} = 719.7\) s\(^{-1}\)
- With \(g_A = 1.2742\) and \(g_{\pi NN} = 13.116\), \(\Gamma_0^{\rm theor} = 716.9\) s\(^{-1}\)
- With \(g_A = 1.2758\) and \(g_{\pi NN} = 13.208\), \(\Gamma_0^{\rm theor} = 717.4\) s\(^{-1}\)
- With \(g_A = 1.2758\) and \(g_{\pi NN} = 13.024\), \(\Gamma_0^{\rm theor} = 719.2\) s\(^{-1}\)
- The uncertainty in \(g_A\) causes approximately 0.2% variation in \(\Gamma_0^{\rm theor}\); the uncertainty in \(g_{\pi NN}\) causes approximately 0.1% variation
- The new larger value for \(g_A\) increases the central value of \(\Gamma_0^{\rm theor}\) by about 0.8% compared to the result in Raha et al. (2013)

## Context

This work extends the N\(^2\)LO HBChPT calculation of the muon capture rate by Raha et al. (2013) (referred to as RMK), which reported \(\Gamma_0^{\rm theor} = 710 \times (1 \pm 0.007)\) s\(^{-1}\) using older values of \(g_A\) and \(g_{\pi NN}\). The updated calculation uses the recently measured value of \(g_A \simeq 1.276\) from neutron beta decay asymmetry measurements by Mund et al. (2013) and Mendenhall et al. (2013), and the value of \(g_{\pi NN} = 13.116 \pm 0.092\) from the improved Goldberger-Miyazawa-Oehme sum rule analysis by Baru et al. (2011). The improved agreement with the MuCap Collaboration experimental result and the reduced theoretical uncertainty enable more precise tests of the Standard Model description of weak interactions in nuclei, and the authors note that the new values of \(g_A\) and the neutron lifetime may help resolve the reactor antineutrino flux anomaly discussed by Ivanov et al. (2013).
