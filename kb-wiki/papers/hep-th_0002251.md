# The diagonalization of quantum field Hamiltonians

> We introduce a new diagonalization method called quasi-sparse eigenvector diagonalization which finds the most important basis vectors of the low energy eigenstates of a quantum Hamiltonian. It can operate using any basis, either orthogonal or non-orthogonal, and any sparse Hamiltonian, either Hermitian, non-Hermitian, finite-dimensional, or infinite-dimensional. The method is part of a new computational approach which combines both diagonalization and Monte Carlo techniques.

## Key claim

The paper introduces quasi-sparse eigenvector (QSE) diagonalization as a method to find the most important basis vectors of low-energy eigenstates for quantum Hamiltonians, but the method's accuracy depends on the eigenvectors being "quasi-sparse" with respect to the chosen basis, a condition that is not guaranteed and must be checked empirically.

## Method

The QSE algorithm iterates over a subset of basis vectors: it diagonalizes the Hamiltonian restricted to that subspace, identifies the eigenvector components with largest magnitude, discards the least important basis vectors, and replaces them with new basis vectors selected at random from those connected to the old ones via non-vanishing Hamiltonian matrix elements. The process is repeated until the subset converges to an optimal approximation of the exact low-energy eigenvectors. The method works for any sparse Hamiltonian (Hermitian or non-Hermitian, finite or infinite-dimensional) and any basis (orthogonal or non-orthogonal).

## Key numbers

* For a random sparse real symmetric 2000x2000 matrix with diagonal entries log(j) and off-diagonal Gaussian random variables with standard deviation 0.25 and 5% density, the QSE method found the lowest four eigenstates with errors at the 5% to 10% level after about 15 iterations, using 70 basis states per eigenvector and 10 replacement states per iteration.
* For a random sparse complex non-Hermitian matrix derived from the same 2000x2000 matrix multiplied by (1 + i*c_jk) with c_jk uniform in [-1,1], the QSE method found the four eigenstates with lowest real part of eigenvalues with errors at the 5% to 10% level after about 20 iterations.
* For 1+1 dimensional phi^4 theory in a periodic box of length L = 5*pi*mu^{-1} with momentum cutoff N_max = 20 (corresponding to Lambda = 4*mu), the QSE method used 50 new basis vectors per eigenstate per iteration and retained 250 basis vectors, producing energy eigenvalues that showed a phase transition at lambda/4! approximately equal to 2.5*mu^2.

## Context

This work extends the line of computational methods for non-perturbative quantum field theory that includes Tamm-Dancoff truncation (Perry, 1990s), similarity transformations (Wilson, 1960s), density matrix renormalization group (White, 1992), and stochastic diagonalization (Husslein, 1990s). The QSE method is designed as the diagonalization component of a combined diagonalization/Monte Carlo approach, where the Monte Carlo part (stochastic error correction) is described in a companion paper. The method enables treatment of systems with sign or phase oscillations that plague pure Monte Carlo, while avoiding the exponential memory and CPU scaling of pure diagonalization.
