# Theoretical study of the $Ξα$ correlation function

> We study $Ξ$-$^4{\mathrm{He}}$ ($α$) momentum correlation functions in the high-energy nuclear collisions to investigate the nature of the $ΞN$ interactions. We employ the folding $Ξα$ potential based on the lattice QCD $ΞN$ interactions to compute the correlation function. The $Ξα$ potential supports a Coulomb-assisted bound state ${}^5_Ξ\mathrm{H}$ in the $Ξ^-α$ channel, while the $Ξ^0α$ channel is unbound. To examine the sensitivity of the correlation function to the nature of the $Ξα$ interaction, we vary the potential strength simulating stronger and weaker interactions. The result of the correlation function is sensitive to the existence of the bound state in the $Ξ^0 α$ channel, and the characteristic behavior of the bound state remains also in the $Ξ^-α$ correlation with the Coulomb interaction. The effect of the repulsive core of the $Ξα$ potential can be found in the correlation from the small source as the distinctive dip in the intermediate momentum region.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $\Xi\alpha$ momentum correlation function is sensitive to the existence of a bound state in the $\Xi^0\alpha$ channel and to the repulsive core of the $\Xi\alpha$ potential, but the authors hedge that the detailed shape of the potential affects the correlation lineshape, making it difficult to extract such features directly from experimental data.

## Method

The authors compute the $\Xi\alpha$ momentum correlation function using the Koonin-Pratt formula with a static Gaussian source function, employing a folding $\Xi\alpha$ potential derived from HAL QCD $\Xi N$ interactions. They vary the potential strength by factors of two (doubling and halving) to simulate stronger and weaker interactions, and compare results with purely attractive Gaussian potential models and the Lednicky-Lyuboshits (LL) formula to isolate the effects of the repulsive core and bound state existence.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length $a_0 = -522.8$ fm and effective range $r_e = 4.50$ fm for the $\Xi^0\alpha$ system with the folding potential $V_{\rm folding}$.
- Binding energy of the Coulomb-assisted bound state ${}^5_{\Xi}\mathrm{H}$ in the $\Xi^-\alpha$ channel: $B = 0.47$ MeV with $V_{\rm folding}$.
- Binding energy of ${}^5_{\Xi}\mathrm{H}$ with the stronger potential $V_{\rm double} = 2V_{\rm folding}$: $B = 2.08$ MeV; with the weaker potential $V_{\rm half} = V_{\rm folding}/2$: $B = 0.18$ MeV.
- Binding energy of the $\Xi^0\alpha$ bound state with $V_{\rm double}$: $1.15$ MeV; no bound state exists with $V_{\rm folding}$ or $V_{\rm half}$.
- Scattering length for $\Xi^0\alpha$ with $V_{\rm double}$: $a_0 = 6.39$ fm; with $V_{\rm half}$: $a_0 = -3.39$ fm.
- Pure Coulomb binding energy for $\Xi^-\alpha$: $B^{\rm Coulomb} = 0.104$ MeV, with Bohr radius $a^{\rm Bohr} = 13.9$ fm.
- The dip structure in the $\Xi^0\alpha$ correlation function appears in the intermediate momentum region around $q \sim 200$ MeV/$c$ for small source size $R = 1$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the femtoscopic approach to hyperon-nucleus systems, following theoretical studies of $\Lambda\alpha$ correlation functions by Jinno et al. (2024) and earlier $p\Xi^-$ correlation studies by Kamiya et al. (2021) and Haidenbauer et al. (2018, 2022). The $\Xi\alpha$ correlation provides complementary information to $p\Xi^-$ correlations because the $\Xi\alpha$ interaction is dominated by the ${}^{33}S_1$ component of the $\Xi N$ interaction, whereas $p\Xi^-$ is sensitive to the ${}^{11}S_0$ component. The predictions are intended to guide future measurements at facilities such as FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC-HI, and the authors note that a more realistic treatment of the $\alpha$ particle's coalescence formation is left for future work.
