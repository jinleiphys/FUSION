# Coulomb analogy for nonhermitian degeneracies near quantum phase transitions

> Degeneracies near the real axis in a complex-extended parameter space of a hermitian Hamiltonian are studied. We present a method to measure distributions of such degeneracies on the Riemann sheet of a selected level and apply it in classification of quantum phase transitions. The degeneracies are shown to behave similarly as complex zeros of a partition function.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the distribution of nonhermitian degeneracies (branch points) near the real axis in the complex-extended parameter space of a hermitian Hamiltonian can be used to classify quantum phase transitions (QPTs), and that this classification is formally identical to the classification of thermodynamic phase transitions using zeros of the partition function. The method works for Hamiltonians of the form \(H(\lambda)=H_0+\lambda V\) with finite Hilbert space dimension, and the classification relies on the asymptotic behavior of degeneracy densities in the infinite-size limit.

## Method

The authors define a function \(U_k(\lambda)=-\frac{1}{2\Omega}\ln D_k(\lambda)^2\) where \(D_k\) is the partial discriminant whose zeros are the degeneracies on the \(k^{\text{th}}\) Riemann sheet, and \(\Omega=n-1\) is the number of other levels. This function is interpreted as a two-dimensional Coulomb potential generated by point charges at the positions of the degeneracies in the complex plane. From \(U_k\) they derive a force \(F_k=-dU_k/d\lambda\) and its derivative \(C_k=dF_k/d\lambda\), and a jump quantity \(Q_k\) equal to the integral of \(C_k\) across a critical point. They apply this formalism to the interacting boson model (IBM) with Hamiltonian \(H(\lambda)=\lambda\frac{1}{N}n_d-(1-\lambda)\frac{1}{N^2}\sum_m(-)^m Q_m Q_{-m}\), computing \(C_k\) for boson numbers up to \(10^5\) and extracting peak heights and widths.

## Key numbers

* The ground-state QPT in the IBM O(6)-U(5) transition is located at \(\lambda_0^{\text{c}}=\frac{4}{5}\), where the classical potential changes between sombrero-like (\(\lambda<\lambda_0^{\text{c}}\)) and quartic oscillator (\(\lambda>\lambda_0^{\text{c}}\)) forms.
* For the IBM calculations, boson numbers up to \(N=10^5\) were used to study the large-\(N\) evolution of peaks in \(C_x(\lambda)\) for excitation ratios \(x=0\) (ground state) and \(x=0.1\) (excited state at 10% of the spectrum).
* The product \(h_x w_x\) (peak height times width) decreases with increasing \(N\), and its infinite-size limit should coincide with \(Q_x(\lambda_x^{\text{c}})\), the jump in the force at the critical point.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Cejnar (2005) that nonhermitian degeneracies behave like complex zeros of a partition function, providing a formal proof of the analogy. The method builds on prior studies of QPTs in finite many-body systems by Gilmore and Feng (1978), Dieperink, Scholten, and Iachello (1980), and Heiss (1988), and on the analysis of excited-state QPTs in the interacting boson model by Cejnar, Macek, Heinze, Jolie, and Dobes (2006) and Caprio, Cejnar, and Iachello (2007). The results enable classification of QPTs in any finite many-body system described by Hamiltonians linear in a control parameter, including nuclear, molecular, optical, and mesoscopic systems.
