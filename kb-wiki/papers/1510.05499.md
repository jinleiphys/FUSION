# Deformation-induced splitting of the monopole giant resonance in 24Mg

> The strong deformation splitting of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR), recently observed in ($α,α'$) reaction in prolate $^{24}$Mg, is analyzed in the framework of the Skyrme quasiparticle random-phase-approximation (QRPA) approach with the Skyrme forces SkM*, SVbas and SkP$^δ$. The calculations with these forces give close results and confirm that the low-energy E0-peak is caused by the deformation-induced coupling of ISGMR with the K=0 branch of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance.

# Deformation-induced splitting of the monopole giant resonance in 24Mg

## Key claim
The strong deformation splitting of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in prolate 24Mg, observed as a narrow peak at ~16 MeV and a broad structure at ~24 MeV, is confirmed to arise from deformation-induced coupling between the ISGMR and the K=0 branch of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR), with the results being qualitatively similar across three Skyrme forces (SkM*, SVbas, SkPδ) despite their different nuclear incompressibilities.

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme quasiparticle random-phase-approximation (QRPA) calculations on a two-dimensional mesh in cylindrical coordinates, using the experimental quadrupole deformation β=0.605 for 24Mg. Three Skyrme forces with different nuclear incompressibilities were employed: SkM* (K∞=217 MeV), SVbas (K∞=234 MeV), and SkPδ (K∞=202 MeV). The residual interaction included all terms of the Skyrme functional including Coulomb terms, and δ-force volume pairing was treated at the BCS level.

## Key numbers
- The low-energy peak is located at E1 ~ 16 MeV and the high-energy structure at E2 ~ 24 MeV, as observed in the (α,α') reaction at RCNP.
- The experimental quadrupole deformation of 24Mg is β = 0.605 ± 0.008 (Raman).
- The nuclear incompressibilities for the three Skyrme forces are: SkM* K∞=217 MeV, SVbas K∞=234 MeV, and SkPδ K∞=202 MeV.
- The empirical estimation EISGMR = 78 A−1/3 MeV gives ~27 MeV for 24Mg, while the observed high-energy peak at ~24 MeV corresponds to EISGMR = 69 A−1/3 MeV, closer to the ISGQR empirical estimation of 64 A−1/3 MeV.
- The Lorentz smoothing parameter used was Δ = 2.5 MeV (compared to 3 MeV in prior work).
- The two-quasiparticle basis involved about 3700 states with energies up to ~200 MeV, and the energy-weighted sum rule for isoscalar monopole excitations was exhausted by 94%.

## Context
This work extends the initial analysis of Gupta (2015), who first observed the ISGMR splitting in 24Mg using the (α,α') reaction at RCNP and performed QRPA calculations with SkM*. The present study checks the robustness of those results by testing two additional Skyrme forces (SVbas and SkPδ) with different incompressibilities, following earlier work by Kvasil (2015) on ISGMR in deformed nuclei. The calculations confirm the E0-E2 coupling mechanism proposed in the literature (Harakeh and van der Woude, 2001) and suggest that future work should include coupling with complex configurations beyond QRPA for a more precise description of the experimental E0 distribution.
