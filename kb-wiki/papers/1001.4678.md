# Rapidity correlations of protons from a fragmented fireball

> We investigate proton rapidity correlations for a fireball that fragments due to non-equilibrium effects at the phase transition from deconfined to hadronic phase. Such effects include spinodal fragmentation in case of first order phase transition at lower collision energies, and cavitation due to sudden rise of the bulk viscosity at the crossover probed at RHIC and the LHC. Our study is performed on samples of Monte Carlo events. Correlation function in relative rapidity appears to be a sensitive probe of fragmentation. We show that resonance decays make the strength of the correlation even stronger.

## Key claim

Proton rapidity correlations are a sensitive probe of fireball fragmentation at the phase transition, but the correlation strength alone cannot determine both the droplet size and the fraction of hadrons emitted from droplets simultaneously; if one of these parameters is known from other measurements, the correlation can determine the other.

## Method

The authors generated Monte Carlo events using the DRAGON event generator, which places droplets randomly within a fireball with volumes following a Gamma distribution and velocities given by the blast-wave expansion profile. Protons and other hadrons are emitted thermally from either the bulk fireball or from decaying droplets, with resonance decays included. Correlation functions in relative rapidity (both 1D rapidity difference Δy and 3D relative rapidity y₁₂) were constructed from pairs of protons in the same event divided by mixed-event reference distributions, then fitted with a Gaussian form.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC parameter settings (T_ch = 170 MeV, μ_B = 46 MeV, dN/dy = 1000, T_kin = 150 MeV, ρ₀ = 0.7), with b = 50 fm³ and all hadrons from droplets, the correlation amplitude A in y₁₂ is approximately 0.15 (estimated from Fig. 1, red dotted curve)
- At b = 10 fm³ with d = 0.5 at RHIC, the correlation amplitude drops to about 0.01; at b = 5 fm³ the average droplet mass is 1.75 GeV, insufficient for a proton pair, making the correlation trivial
- For FAIR energy (T_ch = 140 MeV, μ_B = 428 MeV, total multiplicity 1250, average 135 protons per event), the correlation amplitude is highest among the three collision energies studied
- For LHC (μ_B = 1 MeV, T_ch = 170 MeV, T_kin = 150 MeV, ρ₀ = 0.8, dN/dy = 2000), the correlation amplitude is lower than at FAIR and RHIC

## Context

This work extends earlier schematic simulations by Randrup (2005) by using the more realistic DRAGON event generator with distributed droplet sizes and resonance decays. The study is motivated by predictions of spinodal fragmentation at first-order phase transitions (Mishustin 1998, 2001) and cavitation from bulk viscosity rise at crossover (Torrieri 2007, 2008; Rajagopal 2009). The results cannot be directly compared to experimental data because final-state interactions (strong and Coulomb) between protons are not included, following the approach of Randrup (2005); the correlation functions should be regarded as estimates of the effect size. The work enables future experimental searches for fragmentation signatures at FAIR, RHIC low-energy runs, and the LHC, though rescattering after emission would weaken the correlations.
