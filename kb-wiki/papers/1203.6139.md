# Inclusive Hadron Productions in pA Collisions

> We calculate inclusive hadron productions in pA collisions in the small-x saturation formalism at one-loop order. The differential cross section is written into a factorization form in the coordinate space at the next-to-leading order, while the naive form of the convolution in the transverse momentum space does not hold. The rapidity divergence with small-x dipole gluon distribution of the nucleus is factorized into the energy evolution of the dipole gluon distribution function, which is known as the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. Furthermore, the collinear divergences associated with the incoming parton distribution of the nucleon and the outgoing fragmentation function of the final state hadron are factorized into the splittings of the associated parton distribution and fragmentation functions, which allows us to reproduce the well-known DGLAP equation. The hard coefficient function, which is finite and free of divergence of any kind, is evaluated at one-loop order.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that inclusive hadron production in pA collisions can be factorized at next-to-leading order in the small-x saturation formalism, but only in coordinate space; the naive transverse-momentum-space convolution factorization does not hold beyond leading order.

## Method

The authors calculated the one-loop perturbative corrections to inclusive hadron production in pA collisions within the color-dipole (color-glass-condensate) formalism, evaluating all four partonic channels: q→qg, g→gg, q→gq, and g→q\(\bar{q}\). They identified and separated three types of divergences: rapidity divergences factorized into the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution of the dipole gluon distribution, and collinear divergences factorized into DGLAP evolution of the incoming parton distribution functions and outgoing fragmentation functions. The remaining finite hard coefficient function was evaluated at one-loop order.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order cross section for quark production in the channel qA→qX is given by \(\frac{d\sigma_{\text{LO}}^{pA\rightarrow qX}}{d^{2}k_{\perp}dy} = \sum_f x q_f(x) \int \frac{d^{2}x_{\perp}d^{2}y_{\perp}}{(2\pi)^2} e^{-ik_{\perp}\cdot(x_{\perp}-y_{\perp})} \frac{1}{N_c} \langle \text{Tr}U(x_{\perp})U^{\dagger}(y_{\perp})\rangle_Y\), with \(x = \frac{k_{\perp}}{\sqrt{s}} e^{y}\) and \(x_g = \frac{k_{\perp}}{\sqrt{s}} e^{-y}\).
* The BK evolution equation for the dipole amplitude is \(\frac{\partial}{\partial Y} S_Y^{(2)}(x_{\perp},y_{\perp}) = -\frac{\alpha_s N_c}{2\pi^2} \int d^2 b_{\perp} \frac{(x_{\perp}-y_{\perp})^2}{(x_{\perp}-b_{\perp})^2(y_{\perp}-b_{\perp})^2} [S_Y^{(2)}(x_{\perp},y_{\perp}) - S_Y^{(4)}(x_{\perp},b_{\perp},y_{\perp})]\).
* The collinear singularity coefficient for the quark channel is \(\mathcal{P}_{qq}(\xi) = \left(\frac{1+\xi^2}{1-\xi}\right)_+ = \frac{1+\xi^2}{(1-\xi)_+} + \frac{3}{2}\delta(1-\xi)\), matching the DGLAP splitting function.

## Context

This work extends earlier partial one-loop calculations by Dumitru et al. (2005) and Altinoluk et al. (2011) to a complete next-to-leading order treatment in the saturation formalism. It parallels similar NLO calculations for DIS structure functions in the saturation formalism by Balitsky (2010), Beuf (2011), and others. The results enable systematic NLO predictions for forward hadron production in pA collisions at RHIC and the LHC, and provide a framework for demonstrating factorization for general hard processes in the small-x saturation formalism.
