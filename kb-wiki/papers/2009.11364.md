# Consistent description of angular correlations in $β$ decay for Beyond Standard Model physics searches

> Measurements of angular correlations between initial and final particles in $β$ decay remain one of the most promising ways of probing the Standard Model and looking for new physics. As experiments reach unprecedented precision well into the per-mille regime, proper extraction of results requires one to take into account a great number of nuclear structure and radiative corrections in a procedure which becomes dependent upon the experimental geometry. We provide here a compilation and update of theoretical results which describe all corrections in the same conceptual framework, point out pitfalls and review the influence of the experimental geometry. Finally, we summarize the potential for new physics reach.

## Key claim

The paper provides a consistent theoretical framework for calculating angular correlation coefficients in beta decay (such as the beta-asymmetry \(A_\beta\) and beta-neutrino correlation \(a_{\beta\nu}\)) that includes nuclear structure corrections, kinematic recoil corrections, and electroweak radiative corrections, all treated within the same formalism. The authors emphasize that as experimental precision reaches the per-mille level, proper extraction of results requires corrections that depend on experimental geometry, and that existing literature treatments are not uniform.

## Method

The authors use the Behrens-Bühring multipole formalism to calculate the general three-body beta decay rate for arbitrary spin-parity transitions, expressing the differential decay rate in terms of Legendre polynomial expansions with spectral functions \(f_k^i\) that encode nuclear structure and kinematic effects. They combine this with the Sirlin separation of electroweak radiative corrections into inner corrections (renormalizing coupling constants) and outer corrections (with kinematic dependence), providing explicit expressions for the functions \(g(W_e, W_0)\) and \(h(W_e, W_0)\) that modify the isotropic and correlation spectral functions respectively. For mirror decays with \(T=1/2\), they discuss the extraction of the Fermi-to-Gamow-Teller mixing ratio \(\rho\) and the role of isospin breaking corrections and axial current quenching.

## Key numbers

- The pseudoscalar form factor \(\widetilde{g}_P \approx -229\) from the Goldberger-Treiman relation, though its influence on beta decay is at the \(10^{-4}\) level
- The anomalous magnetic moment combination \(\widetilde{g}_M = \mu_p - \mu_n = 3.706\) for the neutron, set by CVC
- The ratio of outer radiative corrections \(R \approx 1 + (-1.63 + 4.11W_e^{-1} + 0.236\,W_e) \cdot 10^{-3}\) for the neutron, where \(W_e\) is the beta particle total energy in units of electron rest mass
- The Coulomb function \(\Lambda_1(W_e)\) shows deviations from unity at the few \(10^{-4}\) level for energies below a few MeV
- The anisotropy term \(f_2^{\sigma e}\) has a prefactor \((p_e R) \lesssim 0.1\), making it at most a few \(10^{-3}\) effect
- The shape factor \(C(W_e)\) is \(1 + \mathcal{O}(10^{-2})\) and kinematic recoil corrections are at most \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-3})\)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Behrens and Bühring (1982) and the radiative correction framework of Sirlin (1967) to provide a unified treatment of all corrections needed for precision beta decay correlation measurements. It builds on prior work by Hayen (2018) on the isotropic spectral function and by Shann (1971) and Garcia (1982) on outer radiative corrections for correlations. The paper enables consistent comparison between experimental results from different geometries and provides the theoretical foundation for extracting limits on exotic scalar and tensor currents from measurements of the Fierz interference term \(b_F\) and correlation coefficients in mirror decays, which are prime candidates for new physics searches as discussed by Severijns (2008) and Naviliat-Cuncic (2009).
