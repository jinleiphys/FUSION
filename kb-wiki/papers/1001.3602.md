# Influence of jets and resonance decays on the constituent quark scaling of elliptic flow

> The Monte Carlo HYDJET++ model, that contains both hydrodynamic state and jets, is applied to study the influence of the interplay between soft and hard processes on the formation of the elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. Jets are found to cease the hydro-like increase of the elliptic flow with rising p_T. Since jets are more influential at LHC than at RHIC, the v_2 at LHC should be weaker than that at RHIC. Violation of the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling is predicted. The decays of resonances are found to enhance the low-p_T part of the elliptic flow of pions and light baryons, and work toward the fulfillment of the NCQ scaling.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the elliptic flow at LHC will be smaller than at RHIC for transverse momenta above about 3 GeV/c, and that the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling will be violated at LHC due to the stronger influence of jets; however, resonance decays work toward partial restoration of the scaling, so that only an approximate NCQ scaling is realized at LHC.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo HYDJET++ model, which combines a parametrized hydrodynamic description of soft processes with a hard parton model including radiative and collisional energy loss (jet quenching), to simulate 1,000,000 Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV and 500,000 Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 5.5 ATeV, both at fixed centrality c = 42%. They computed the elliptic flow v₂ as a function of transverse momentum p_T for pions, kaons, protons, and Λ+Σ hyperons, separately for directly produced hadrons and for all hadrons including those from resonance decays (using a table of about 360 baryon and meson resonances). They then examined the NCQ scaling by plotting v₂/n_q versus KE_T/n_q and the ratio (v₂/n_q)/(v₂^Λ/3).

## Key numbers

- Centrality fixed at c = 42% for both Au+Au at √s = 200 AGeV and Pb+Pb at √s = 5.5 ATeV.
- At LHC, resonances contribute to the production of 80% of pions, 70% of protons, 60% of Λ+Σ, and more than 50% of kaons.
- The NCQ scaling holds at 10% up to KE_T/n_q ≥ 1.0 GeV for RHIC energies when resonance decays are included.
- The prediction v₂^LHC(p_T) < v₂^RHIC(p_T) is stated for p_T ≥ 3 GeV/c.
- The flow of the most abundant hadron species at RHIC is stronger than its LHC counterpart (no specific numerical difference given).

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions beyond the purely hydrodynamic models reviewed by Voloshin et al. (2008) and the scaling model of Last (no year given), which predicted v₂(y=0) ≈ 7–8% at LHC. It uses the HYDJET++ model previously applied in Lokhtin et al. (2009) to describe RHIC data, and directly tests the NCQ scaling observed by the PHENIX and STAR collaborations. The results enable predictions for LHC experiments and highlight the need to account for both jet contributions and resonance decay feed-down when interpreting v₂ measurements.
