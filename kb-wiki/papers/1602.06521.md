# Light-Front Dynamics and the 3He Spectral Function

> Two topics are presented. The first one is a novel approach for a Poincare' covariant description of nuclear dynamics based on light-front Hamiltonian dynamics. The key quantity is the light-front spectral function, where both normalization and momentum sum rule can be satisfied at the same time. Preliminary results are discussed for an initial analysis of the role of relativity in the EMC effect in 3He. A second issue, very challenging, is considered in a non-relativistic framework, namely a distorted spin-dependent spectral function for 3He in order to take care of the final state interaction between the observed pion and the remnant in semi-inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering off polarized 3He. The generalization of the analysis within the light-front dynamics is outlined.

## Key claim

The authors propose a Poincare-covariant description of nuclear dynamics for A=3 systems using light-front Hamiltonian dynamics, claiming that the resulting light-front spectral function can simultaneously satisfy both normalization and momentum sum rules. The hedge is that the preliminary results for the EMC effect in 3He use an approximation for the three-body channel (average values for the spectator pair momentum) rather than a full exact calculation.

## Method

The authors construct a light-front spectral function for 3He by embedding non-relativistic wave functions (from the AV18 NN interaction, Pisa group) into a Bakamjian-Thomas construction of Poincare generators, using Melosh rotations to connect instant-form and light-front spin states. For the EMC effect analysis, the two-body channel contribution is calculated exactly while the three-body channel uses average values for the intrinsic momentum of the spectator pair (113.53 MeV for proton, 91.27 MeV for neutron). For the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) analysis, a non-relativistic distorted spin-dependent spectral function is constructed using a generalized eikonal approximation with a Glauber operator to account for final state interactions.

## Key numbers

- Momentum sum rule calculated with the approximation yields MSR_calc = 0.3331 (compared to the exact value of 1/3)
- Average intrinsic momentum for spectator pair in three-body channel: 113.53 MeV (proton), 91.27 MeV (neutron)
- Average intrinsic momentum for two-body channel: 136.37 MeV
- Effective polarizations for 3He in non-relativistic plane wave impulse approximation: p_p = -0.023, p_n = 0.878
- SIDIS analysis performed at beam energy E_i = 8.8 GeV for JLab kinematics

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic spectral function approach of Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1990s) and the Pisa group's few-body wave functions (Kievsky et al., 1990s) into a Poincare-covariant framework. The light-front spectral function enables relativistic treatment of deep inelastic scattering, semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, and deeply virtual Compton scattering off 3He, relevant for the JLab 12 GeV program. The preliminary EMC effect results show improvements over the non-relativistic approach of Sauer (1990s) when compared to experimental data from Seely et al. (2009). The SIDIS analysis demonstrates that the standard extraction formula for neutron asymmetries remains valid even when final state interactions are included via the generalized eikonal approximation, building on prior work by Kaptari et al. (2010s) and Del Dotto et al. (2010s).
