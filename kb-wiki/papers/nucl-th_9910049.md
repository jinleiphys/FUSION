# Deuteron Magnetic and Quadrupole Moments with a Poincaré Covariant Current Operator in the Front-Form Dynamics

> The deuteron magnetic and quadrupole moments are unambiguosly determined within the front-form Hamiltonian dynamics, by using a new current operator which fulfills Poincaré, parity and time reversal covariance, together with hermiticity and the continuity equation. For both quantities the usual disagreement between theoretical and experimental results is largely removed.

## Key claim

Using a new Poincaré-covariant current operator in front-form Hamiltonian dynamics, the deuteron magnetic and quadrupole moments can be unambiguously determined at Q²=0, and the usual disagreement between theoretical and experimental values is largely removed, though the authors note that explicit two-body current contributions (beyond those already contained in their current operator) must still be evaluated separately.

## Method

The authors constructed a current operator in the Breit reference frame (with transverse momentum transfer zero and the three-momentum transfer along the spin quantization axis) that satisfies extended Poincaré covariance, parity and time reversal covariance, hermiticity, and current conservation. They then evaluated the deuteron magnetic and quadrupole moments at Q²=0 using this current operator together with the internal deuteron wave functions from several nucleon-nucleon interactions (including RSC, Av14, Av18, and CD-Bonn), comparing the results to non-relativistic calculations and to experimental values.

## Key numbers

- For the linear behaviour suggested by the asymptotic normalization ratio η^exp = 0.0256, the calculated magnetic moment μ_d differs from the experimental value by only 0.5%, and the quadrupole moment Q_d differs by only 2%.
- The relativistic corrections bring both μ_d and Q_d closer to the experimental values for all interactions except the charge-dependent Bonn interaction.
- The standard non-relativistic results with a one-body current cannot simultaneously reproduce the experimental values for both μ_d and Q_d, while the Poincaré-covariant calculation largely removes this disagreement.

## Context

This work extends previous front-form Hamiltonian dynamics calculations by Kondratyuk (1980), Tjon (1985), Gross (1988), and others, which used the free current in the q⁺=0 frame and suffered from ambiguities related to the "angular condition" that left the quadrupole moment not uniquely determined. The new current operator resolves this ambiguity by yielding only three independent matrix elements of the current, enabling unambiguous extraction of all three electromagnetic form factors. The results suggest that explicit two-body current contributions (meson-exchange, Z-graphs, isobar configurations) are relatively small at Q²=0, contrary to what was needed in non-relativistic frameworks, and enable future evaluation of such explicit two-body contributions within the same Breit-frame formalism.
