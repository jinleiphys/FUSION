# Hybrid model with viscous relativistic hydrodynamics: a role of constraints on the shear-stress tensor

> We present the hybrid hadron string dynamic (HydHSD) model connecting the parton-hadron-string dynamic model (PHSD) and a hydrodynamic model taking into account shear viscosity within the Israel-Stewart approach. The performance of the code is tested on the pion and proton rapidity and transverse mass distributions calculated for Au+Au and Pb+Pb collision at AGS--SPS energies. The influence of the switch time from transport to hydro models, the viscous parameter, and freeze-out time are discussed. Since the applicability of the Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics assumes the perturbative character of the viscous stress tensor, $π^{μν}$, which should not exceed the ideal energy-momentum tensor, $T_{\rm id}^{μν}$, hydrodynamical codes usually rescale the shear stress tensor if the inequality $\|π^{μν}\|\ll \|T_{\rm id}^{μν}\|$ is not fulfilled in some sense. We show that the form of the corresponding condition plays an important role in the sensitivity of hydrodynamic calculations to the viscous parameter -- a ratio of the shear viscosity to the entropy density, $η/s$. It is shown that the constraints used in the vHLLE and MUSIC models give the same results for the observables. With these constraints, the rapidity distributions and transverse momentum spectra are most sensitive to a change of the $η/s$ ratio. As an alternative, a strict condition is used. We performed global fits the rapidity and transverse mass distribution of pion and protons. It was also found that $η/s$ as a function of the collision energy monotonically increases from $E_{\rm lab}=6A$GeV up to $E_{\rm lab}=40A$GeV and saturates for higher SPS energies. We observe that it is difficult to reproduce simultaneously pion and proton rapidity distribution within our model with the present choice of the equation of state without a phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the specific form of the constraint used to enforce the perturbative condition \(|\pi^{\mu\nu}| < C |T_{\rm id}^{\mu\nu}|\) on the shear-stress tensor significantly affects the sensitivity of hydrodynamic calculations to the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\); the constraints used in the vHLLE and MUSIC codes yield the same results for observables, but a stricter condition reduces sensitivity of proton and pion momentum distributions to the viscosity parameter.

## Method

The authors construct the HydHSD hybrid model, which connects the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport model to a 3+1 dimensional viscous relativistic hydrodynamic stage using the Israel-Stewart approach with SHASTA numerical algorithm and Heun's method for time propagation. Initial conditions for hydrodynamics are generated from the kinetic model at a switch time \(t_{\rm start}\), using either an ideal initialization (IIS) that sets all shear-stress components to zero or an exact initialization (EIS) that extracts the full energy-momentum tensor. The particlization from fluid to particles uses the Cooper-Frye formula with a regulated viscous correction \(\delta f^{\rm reg}_a\) to the distribution function, and the freeze-out hypersurface is determined via the CORNELIUS algorithm.

## Key numbers

- \(\eta/s\) as a function of collision energy increases monotonically from \(E_{\rm lab}=6\,A{\rm GeV}\) up to \(E_{\rm lab}=40\,A{\rm GeV}\) and saturates for higher SPS energies.
- Calculations are performed for Au+Au collisions at \(E_{\rm lab}=6\) and \(10.7\,A{\rm GeV}\) and Pb+Pb collisions at \(E_{\rm lab}=40\), \(80\), and \(158\,A{\rm GeV}\), all at impact parameter \(b=1\) fm.
- The switch time \(t_{\rm start}\) is approximately \(4\,{\rm fm}/c\) for Pb+Pb at \(E_{\rm lab}=40\,A{\rm GeV}\), where total energy and wounded baryon number saturate.
- Freeze-out temperature \(\Tfrz=100\) MeV is used for ideal hydro comparison in Fig. 2; \(\Tfrz=150\) MeV is used for viscous calculations in Fig. 4.
- The parameter \(C=4\) is used in the constraint condition for the particlization comparison in Fig. 4.
- The relaxation time is set as \(\tau_\pi = 5\eta/(\varepsilon+P)\) with \(\eta = k_\eta s\).
- Cells with temperature \(T<50\) MeV are excluded from total entropy calculations.

## Context

This work extends the earlier HydHSD hybrid model (Khvorostukhin et al., 2015) from ideal hydrodynamics to include shear viscosity within the Israel-Stewart framework, building on the approach used in the vHLLE code (Karpenko et al., 2013) and the MUSIC code (Schenke et al., 2010). The paper systematically compares different constraints on the shear-stress tensor that are employed in those codes and demonstrates that the choice of constraint affects the sensitivity of observables to \(\eta/s\). The results enable future studies of the temperature and energy dependence of \(\eta/s\) in heavy-ion collisions from AGS to SPS energies, though the authors note difficulty in simultaneously reproducing pion and proton rapidity distributions with the chosen hadronic equation of state without a phase transition.
