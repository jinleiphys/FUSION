# Isovector and isoscalar superfluid phases in rotating nuclei

> The subtle interplay between the two nuclear superfluids, isovector T=1 and isoscalar T=0 phases, are investigated in an exactly soluble model. It is shown that T=1 and T=0 pair-modes decouple in the exact calculations with the T=1 pair-energy being independent of the T=0 pair-strength and vice-versa. In the rotating-field, the isoscalar correlations remain constant in contrast to the well known quenching of isovector pairing. An increase of the isoscalar (J=1, T=0) pair-field results in a delay of the bandcrossing frequency. This behaviour is shown to be present only near the N=Z line and its experimental confirmation would imply a strong signature for isoscalar pairing collectivity. The solutions of the exact model are also discussed in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in an exactly soluble model, isovector (T=1) and isoscalar (T=0) pairing modes are independent and coexist, contrary to mean-field predictions of mutual exclusivity; the isoscalar correlations remain constant under rotation, and a shift in bandcrossing frequency near the N=Z line would be a signature of collective isoscalar (J=1, T=0) pairing, but only if the J=1 component of the T=0 interaction is coherently enhanced in multi-shell nuclei.

## Method

The authors used an exactly soluble deformed single-j shell model with a cranked one-body term and a scalar two-body delta interaction, solving the full shell-model problem for systems of 2+2, 4+2, and 4+4 particles in the f7/2 shell. They compared these exact results to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation, solving the HFB equations self-consistently without symmetry restrictions to allow both T=0 and T=1 pair-fields simultaneously. The pair-energy was extracted from the exact wavefunctions using the coupled pair-field formalism, and the rotational frequency dependence was studied by cranking the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* For a system of 2 protons and 2 neutrons in the f7/2 shell, the T=1 pairing correlations drop at a rotational frequency of approximately hbar-omega = 0.7 G due to particle alignment from the f7/2 shell.
* The T=1 pair-gap is dominated by the J=0 component, while the T=0 mode is dominated by J=1 and J=2j (J=7 for f7/2) components, with intermediate spins J=3 and J=5 also contributing.
* In the HFB calculations, a T=0 solution could be obtained below the critical point by starting from a large T=0 strength (G_T=0 = 2.8) and then using that solution for lower values.
* Increasing the J=1 part of the T=0 interaction by a factor of 2 shifts the bandcrossing frequency to higher values for the N=Z (4+4) system, but the crossing frequency remains essentially unchanged for the N not equal Z (2+4) system.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies by Satula and Wyss (1997) and others that predicted exclusivity between T=0 and T=1 pairing in the BCS approximation, showing that this exclusivity is a mean-field artifact and that both modes coexist in the exact solution. The finding that T=0 correlations remain constant under rotation contrasts with the well-known quenching of isovector pairing, and the predicted shift of bandcrossing frequency due to T=0, J=1 collectivity offers a specific experimental signature for isoscalar pairing in heavy N=Z nuclei, such as the shift reported for 72Kr by de Angelis et al. (1997). The results enable future studies of isoscalar pairing collectivity in realistic multi-shell calculations and experiments near the N=Z line.
