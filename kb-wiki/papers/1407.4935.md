# Electromagnetic moments of the bound system of charged particles

> We consider a bound system of particles interacting via electromagnetic forces in an external electromagnetic field, including leading relativistic corrections. Each particle has a definite mass, charge, spin, and charge radius. We introduce suitable canonical transformations and a perturbation scheme to obtain a Hamiltonian which describes the global dynamics of the system. This enables the derivation of formulas for various electromagnetic moments, such as the magnetic dipole moment, the mean square charge radius, and the electric dipole polarizability.

# Electromagnetic moments of the bound system of charged particles

## Key claim
The paper presents a perturbative approach to derive an effective Hamiltonian for a bound system of charged particles in an external electromagnetic field, including leading relativistic corrections, that enables the derivation of formulas for electromagnetic moments such as the magnetic dipole moment, mean square charge radius, and electric dipole polarizability. The approach assumes that the binding energy is much larger than the characteristic energy scale of the coupling between internal and global degrees of freedom, and that global motion is nonrelativistic.

## Method
The authors decompose the total Hamiltonian into internal, global, and coupling parts, then project the Schrodinger equation onto the internal ground state to obtain an effective equation for global dynamics. They perform canonical transformations to separate center-of-mass motion from internal degrees of freedom, and use a perturbation scheme where the coupling term is treated as small compared to internal excitation energies. The effective Hamiltonian is obtained by taking expectation values of the transformed Hamiltonian on the internal ground state, including second-order corrections from the coupling term.

## Key numbers
- For hydrogen-like ions in the S state, the relativistic correction to the g-factor is given by δg = -(Zα)²/(3n²(1+x)²)[-g_e/2 + 4 - 1/(1+x) + Zx²(g_e + 1/(1+x))], where x = m/m_N
- For the positronium ion Ps⁻ in the ground state, the calculated g-factor is g_{Ps⁻} = 2.002292117(3), with δg = -0.510551028(1)
- The expectation values used for Ps⁻ in atomic units: energy = -0.262005070, ⟨p₃²⟩ = 0.257532962, ⟨1/r₁₃⟩ = 0.339821023, ⟨r₁₃·r₂₃/r₁₃³⟩ = 0.046478421
- The effective kinetic energy includes a factor (1 - ε_S/M) where ε_S is the binding energy and M is the total mass

## Context
This work extends the approach of Hegstrom (1985) and the authors' previous works (Pachucki 2008, 2010) to provide a simpler derivation of electromagnetic moments for bound systems. The paper derives a new term in the mean square charge radius formula not previously included in nuclear charge radius calculations, and identifies a correction to the electric dipole operator δD that was missing in all previous calculations of nuclear electric dipole polarizability. The results for the magnetic moment of hydrogen-like ions agree with Hegstrom (1985) and Eides (2001), while the Ps⁻ result improves upon and disagrees with the recent calculation by Czarnecki (2014). The approach is intended to be extended to nuclear systems using chiral perturbation theory, particularly relevant for interpreting high-precision atomic spectroscopy measurements of isotope shifts.
