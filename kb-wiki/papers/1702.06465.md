# Universal behavior of few-boson systems using potential models

> The universal behavior of a three-boson system close to the unitary limit is encoded in a simple dependence of many observables in terms of few parameters. For example the product of the three-body parameter $κ_*$ and the two-body scattering length $a$, $κ_* a$ depends on the angle $ξ$ defined by $E_3/E_2=\tan^2ξ$. A similar dependence is observed in the ratio $a_{AD}/a$ with $a_{AD}$ the boson-dimer scattering length. We use a two-parameter potential to determine this simple behavior and, as an application, to compute $a_{AD}$ for the case of three $^4$He atoms.

## Key claim

The universal behavior of three-boson systems near the unitary limit can be captured by simple two-parameter gaussian potential models, and the ratio of the boson-dimer scattering length to the two-body scattering length depends only on the angle \(\xi\) defined by \(E_3/E_2 = \tan^2\xi\), even when finite-range corrections are included.

## Method

The authors constructed local and nonlocal gaussian potentials with two parameters (strength and range) tuned to reproduce given values of the two-body scattering length \(a\) and two-body binding energy \(E_2\) for a boson system. They then computed the three-body ground state energy \(E_3\) and the boson-dimer scattering length \(a_{AD}\) for various values of \(\xi\) using these potentials, and compared the resulting ratio \(a_{AD}/a_B\) (where \(a_B\) is the energy length derived from \(E_2\)) to the zero-range universal function and to results from the realistic LM2M2 potential for three \(^4\)He atoms.

## Key numbers

- For the local gaussian potential at unitarity, the effective range is \(r_{\rm eff} = 1.43522\, r_0\) and the strength is \(\lambda V^L_0 = 2.6840\, \hbar^2/m r_0^2\).
- For the nonlocal gaussian potential at unitarity, the effective range is \(r_{\rm eff} = 3.19154 / k_0\) and the strength is \(\lambda V^L_0 = 0.126987\, \hbar^2 k_0^2/m\).
- For three \(^4\)He atoms described by the LM2M2 potential, the two-body energy is \(E_2 = 1.303\) mK, the three-body ground state energy is \(E^0_3 = 126\) mK, giving \(\tan^2\xi = 97.0\).
- The atom-dimer scattering length for three \(^4\)He atoms computed via the gaussian method is \(a_{AD} \approx 212\, a_0\), compared to \(217\, a_0\) from a direct LM2M2 calculation.

## Context

This work extends the zero-range Efimov theory (Efimov, 1970) by incorporating finite-range corrections through simple gaussian potential models, following earlier studies by Kievsky et al. (2015, 2016) on gaussian level functions. The approach enables the calculation of observables like the boson-dimer scattering length for realistic systems such as three \(^4\)He atoms, where the authors reproduce the result of the LM2M2 potential (Aziz, 1987) to within about 2%. The method can be applied to other few-boson systems near the unitary limit to study discrete scaling invariance at finite two-body energies.
