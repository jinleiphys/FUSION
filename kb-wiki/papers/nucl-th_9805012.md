# Strangeness Production in the HSD Transport Approach from SIS to SPS energies

> We study systematically the production of strangeness in nuclear reactions from SIS to SPS energies within the covariant hadronic transport approach HSD. Whereas the proton and pion rapidity distributions as well as pion transverse momentum spectra are well described in the hadronic transport model from 2-200 AGeV, the $K^+$ and $K^-$ spectra are noticeably underestimated at AGS energies while the $K^+$ spectra match well at SIS and SPS energies with the experimental data. We conclude that the failure of the hadronic model at AGS energies points towards a nonhadronic phase during the collision of heavy systems around 10 AGeV.

# Strangeness Production in the HSD Transport Approach from SIS to SPS Energies

## Key claim

The hadronic transport approach HSD systematically underestimates K+ and K- spectra at AGS energies (around 10 AGeV) while matching data at SIS and SPS energies, which the authors interpret as evidence for a nonhadronic phase during heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies. The claim is conditional on the assumption that no string-string interactions or collective effects are included in the model.

## Method

The authors employed the covariant hadronic transport approach HSD (Hadron-String Dynamics) in cascade mode, propagating baryons (p, n, Δ, N(1440), N(1535), Λ, Σ, Σ*, Ξ, Ω), their antibaryons, and mesons (π, K, η, η', ρ, ω, φ, K*, a1) using coupled transport equations with transition rates from experimental cross sections. High-energy inelastic hadron-hadron collisions (above √s = 2.65 GeV for baryon-baryon, above √s = 2.1 GeV for meson-baryon) were described by the FRITIOF string model with a fixed formation time of 0.8 fm/c, while low-energy strangeness production channels (NN → NYK, πN → YK) used explicit parametrizations from experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Strangeness suppression factor γs = 0.3 at SPS energies and γs = 0.4 at AGS energies, with a linear transition as a function of √s between these values
- Formation time tf = 0.8 fm/c in the rest frame of newly produced particles
- String threshold for baryon-baryon collisions: √s = 2.65 GeV; for meson-baryon collisions: √s = 2.1 GeV
- High-energy inelastic baryon-baryon cross sections ≈ 30 mb, meson-baryon ≈ 20 mb
- Diquark production probability P(qq q̄ q̄)/P(u ū) = 0.07 (reduced from standard FRITIOF value of 0.1)
- K+ production threshold: √s0 = mp + mΛ + mK = 2.55 GeV
- K- production threshold in baryon-baryon collisions: √s0 = 2·mp + 2·mK
- Isospin-averaged cross section for meson-meson → K K̄: σ̄mm→K K̄(s) = 2.7·(1 - s0/s)^0.76 mb, with s0 = 4mK²
- Additional πN → KΛ channel added with energy-independent cross section of 1 mb
- Particle multiplicities in p-p at plab = 200 GeV: K+ = 0.274 (data: 0.28 ± 0.06), K- = 0.18 (data: 0.18 ± 0.05), K0s = 0.174 (data: 0.17 ± 0.01), Λ+Σ0 = 0.15 (data: 0.1 ± 0.015)

## Context

This work extends the HSD transport approach, previously validated at SIS energies by Cassing et al. (1990s), to systematically study strangeness production from SIS through AGS to SPS energies (2-200 AGeV) within a single fixed model version. The study contrasts with models that include collective string interactions or color ropes, such as RQMD (Sorge et al.) and VENUS (Werner), which enhance strangeness through string fusion, and with parton models like HIJING that require high momentum transfers (q² > 10 GeV²). The systematic failure at AGS energies motivates future investigation of nonhadronic phases, while the agreement at SIS and SPS energies provides a baseline for distinguishing hadronic from partonic contributions.
