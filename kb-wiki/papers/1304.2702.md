# Photon emission in neutral current interactions with nucleons and nuclei

> We report on our study of photon emission induced by Enu ~ 1 GeV (anti)neutrino neutral current interactions with nucleons and nuclei. This process is an important background for nu_e appearance oscillation experiments. At the relevant energies, the reaction is dominated by the excitation of the Delta (1232) resonance but there are also non-resonant contributions that, close to threshold, are fully determined by the effective chiral Lagrangian of strong interactions. We have obtained differential and integrated cross section for the (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering and compare them with previous results. Furthermore, we have extended the model to nuclear targets taking into account Fermi motion, Pauli blocking and the in-medium modifications of the Delta properties. This study is important in order to reduce systematic effects in neutrino oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

The authors present a microscopic model of photon emission from neutral-current (anti)neutrino interactions on nucleons and nuclei at energies around 1 GeV, finding that the predicted number of electron-like events at MiniBooNE is in good agreement with the experiment's own background estimate, and therefore that this process cannot explain the observed low-energy excess of electron-like events.

## Method

The model is built from hadronic degrees of freedom (nucleon, Delta(1232) resonance, and mesons) with Feynman diagrams including direct and crossed nucleon-pole and Delta-pole terms, plus a pion-pole term. For nuclear targets, the authors adopt a relativistic local Fermi gas description with Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and in-medium modifications of the Delta resonance properties (collisional broadening and Pauli blocking of the decay width). The cross sections are then folded with the MiniBooNE flux, detector composition, and energy-dependent gamma detection efficiencies to predict the number of electron-like events.

## Key numbers

- Axial coupling constant: g_A = 1.267, with axial mass M_A = 1.016 GeV for the nucleon form factor
- Dominant Delta axial form factor: C^A_5(0) = 1.0 ± 0.11, with axial mass M_{AΔ} = 0.93 ± 0.07 GeV, fixed from BNL and ANL neutrino-deuteron data
- At E_ν ~ 1.5 GeV on a single nucleon, the nucleon-pole cross section is only about 2.5 times smaller than the Delta-pole cross section
- On a ¹²C target, including Fermi motion and Pauli blocking reduces the cross section by more than 10%; the full model including Delta modifications gives a reduction of about 30%
- MiniBooNE exposure: 6.46 × 10²⁰ protons on target in neutrino mode, 11.27 × 10²⁰ in antineutrino mode; target mass 806 tons of CH₂

## Context

This work extends the microscopic model of Hill (2009) for neutral-current photon emission on nucleons to nuclear targets, incorporating nuclear-medium corrections that were neglected in that earlier study. The results contrast with Hill (2010), which found twice as many events as the MiniBooNE estimate and suggested a significant contribution to the low-energy excess, but agree with the more recent chiral effective field theory approach of Zhang (2012), which also found that NCγ cannot explain the MiniBooNE anomaly. The model enables more reliable background estimates for future neutrino oscillation experiments such as MicroBooNE and DUNE.
