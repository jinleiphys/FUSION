# Bremsstrahlung emission accompanying ternary fission of \isotope[252]{Cf}

> \textbf{Background} In ternary fission, bremsstrahlung photons are emitted but those have never been studied yet theoretically and experimentally. In other reactions, bremsstrahlung has been studied for a long time. \textbf{Purpose} To clarify which new information about ternary fission can be obtained from study of bremsstrahlung emission accompanying the ternary fission of \isotope[252]{Cf}. \textbf{Methods} A new quantum model of emission of bremsstrahlung photons accompanying ternary fission of heavy nuclei with $α$-particle as light charged particle is developed. The model takes into account geometry and dynamics of ternary fission. \textbf{Results} We present the theoretical results on the bremsstrahlung emission in the ternary fission of the \isotope[252]{Cf} nucleus. High sensitivity of the bremsstrahlung spectra is established by the model concerning to the following aspects of the ternary fission, and the theoretical calcualtions are in agreement with the preliminary experimental data. It is found that: (a) Photons are emitted with highest intensity in case of perpendicular motion of the $α$\,particle concerning to fission axis; (b) Relative motion between heavy fragments reinforces significantly bremsstrahlung, leaving of $α$-particle concerning to system of heavy fragments is less important; (c) Relative motion between two heavy fragments is faster, bremsstrahlung is more intensive. \textbf{Conclusions} Theoretical study of bremsstrahlung in ternary fission of the \isotope[252]{Cf} nucleus shows high sensitivity of bremsstrahlung spectra on the geometry and dynamics of ternary fission process. It is expected that new information can be obtained by the model when new experimental measurements of bremsstrahlung in ternary fission of the \isotope[252]{Cf} nucleus are available.

## Key claim

The bremsstrahlung spectrum in ternary fission of 252Cf is highly sensitive to the geometry and dynamics of the process, with the relative motion between the two heavy fragments reinforcing the emission significantly more than the motion of the alpha particle relative to the heavy-fragment system; however, the theoretical results are only in agreement with preliminary experimental data and the authors expect that new information can be obtained when new experimental measurements become available.

## Method

A new quantum model of bremsstrahlung photon emission accompanying ternary fission was developed, taking into account the geometry and dynamics of the fissioning nuclear system composed of three fragments (two heavy fragments and an alpha particle as the light charged particle). The nuclear surface was defined using a three-fragment geometry with two necks, and the interaction potential between the alpha particle and the binary nucleus was calculated including nuclear and Coulomb components, with the Coulomb potential at far distances approximated by replacing the two fragments with an equivalent fictitious fragment. The bremsstrahlung matrix elements and probabilities were then computed for different geometrical arrangements and relative motions of the fragments.

## Key numbers

- Ternary fission is a rare decay process occurring in less than 1 percent of fission events, with a ratio of approximately 1/260 for 252Cf.
- About 87% of ternary fission events involve emission of an alpha particle.
- The alpha particle energy spectrum in ternary fission is continuous with a mean energy of approximately 16 MeV and a width of approximately 11 MeV.
- Other light charged particles with sizable yields include 3H (approximately 7%), 6He (approximately 3.5%), 8He (approximately 0.2%), 7,8,9Li (approximately 0.5%), and 9,10,11Be (approximately 2%).

## Context

This work extends the study of bremsstrahlung emission, which has been investigated for a long time in other nuclear reactions, to the previously unstudied case of ternary fission. The model builds on a prior quantum mechanical model of bremsstrahlung in spontaneous fission of 252Cf by Maydanyuk (2010) and uses the geometry definition approach of Bolsterli (1972). The results enable future experimental measurements of bremsstrahlung in ternary fission to be interpreted for new information about the geometry and dynamics of the fission process, which has been studied theoretically using liquid drop models (Swiatecki, 1958), three-center shell models (Degheidy, 1979), and other approaches.
