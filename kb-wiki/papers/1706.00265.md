# A complete non-perturbative renormalization prescription for quasi-PDFs

> In this work we present, for the first time, the non-perturbative renormalization for the unpolarized, helicity and transversity quasi-PDFs, in an RI' scheme. The proposed prescription addresses simultaneously all aspects of renormalization: logarithmic divergences, finite renormalization as well as the linear divergence which is present in the matrix elements of fermion operators with Wilson lines. Furthermore, for the case of the unpolarized quasi-PDFs, we describe how to eliminate the unwanted mixing with the twist-3 scalar operator. We utilize perturbation theory for the one-loop conversion factor that brings the renormalization functions to the MS-scheme at a scale of 2 GeV. We also explain how to improve the estimates on the renormalization functions by eliminating lattice artifacts. The latter can be computed in one-loop perturbation theory and to all orders in the lattice spacing. We apply the methodology for the renormalization to an ensemble of twisted mass fermions with Nf=2+1+1 dynamical light quarks, and a pion mass of around 375 MeV.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first fully non-perturbative renormalization prescription for quasi-PDFs that simultaneously removes logarithmic divergences, finite renormalization effects, and the linear divergence associated with the Wilson line, while also addressing operator mixing for the unpolarized case. The method is demonstrated on a single ensemble of twisted mass fermions with a pion mass of around 375 MeV, and the authors note that a proper renormalization of the unpolarized quasi-PDF is still pending because the required scalar nucleon matrix element has not yet been computed on the lattice.

## Method

The authors compute vertex functions of non-local fermion operators with Wilson lines between external quark states in momentum space, applying the RI' renormalization scheme. Renormalization functions Z_O(z) are extracted for each Wilson line length z by imposing a condition on the amputated vertex function at a chosen RI' scale, with the quark field renormalization Z_q determined separately. For the unpolarized quasi-PDF, a 2x2 mixing matrix between the vector and scalar operators is constructed to handle the mixing with the twist-3 scalar operator. The RI' results are converted to the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV using one-loop continuum perturbation theory, and lattice artifacts are studied by comparing "diagonal" and "parallel" choices for the RI' momentum components.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a ≈ 0.082 fm
- Pion mass: around 375 MeV
- Nucleon momentum: P_3 = 6π/L, which is around 1.4 GeV in physical units
- Volume: 32^3 × 64
- Number of gauge configurations: 1000, with 15 forward propagators and 2 stochastic propagators, giving 30000 measurements total
- For the "diagonal" RI' scale, the ratio hat(P) ranges from 0.251 to 0.370 for momenta m1 through m8
- For the "parallel" RI' scale, the ratio hat(P) is 0.539, 0.592, and 0.664 for momenta m9, m10, and m11 respectively
- The one-loop anomalous dimension of the operator: gamma_O = -3 g^2 C_f / (16 pi^2)
- The imaginary part of Z_Delta h in the MSbar scheme is close to zero for |z| up to approximately 10a when using the "diagonal" scale

## Context

This work extends the quasi-PDF approach proposed by Ji (2013) by providing the first concrete non-perturbative renormalization method, which had been identified as a critical missing piece in the quasi-PDF strategy. Prior suggestions for eliminating the linear divergence via the static potential were made by Ma and Qiu (2014) and Ishikawa et al. (2016), while Constantinou et al. (2017) computed the one-loop perturbative divergence and identified the mixing for the unpolarized case. The method enables future lattice calculations of renormalized quasi-PDFs that can be matched to physical light-front PDFs, with the authors noting they are currently computing quasi-PDFs on an ensemble of twisted mass clover improved fermions at the physical pion mass.
