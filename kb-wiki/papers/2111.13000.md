# Electric dipole moments of baryons with bottom quarks

> Triggered by experimental prospects to measure electromagnetic dipole moments of baryons containing a bottom quark, we calculate the CP-odd electric dipole moments (EDMs) of spin-1/2 single-bottom baryons. We consider CP-violating dimension-six operators in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory that involve bottom quarks, and apply heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to compute the EDMs of several baryons. We discuss the expected size of the EDMs for beyond-the-Standard Model physics appearing at the TeV scale.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the electric dipole moments (EDMs) of spin-1/2 bottom baryons using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory matched to CP-violating dimension-six operators in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT), but the results depend on unknown low-energy constants (LECs) that must be determined from nonperturbative information, so the numerical predictions are estimates for the expected size of EDMs assuming beyond-the-Standard-Model physics at the TeV scale.

## Method

The authors start from CP-violating dimension-six SMEFT operators involving bottom quarks: the bottom-quark EDM (qEDM), bottom-quark chromo-EDM (qCEDM), four-quark (4q) operators, and four-quark left-right (4qLR) operators. They match these quark-level operators to hadronic Lagrangians using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) for the SU(3) flavor representations of spin-1/2 bottom baryons (anti-triplet \(B_{\bar{3}}\) and sextet \(B_6\)). The EDMs are then computed at leading chiral order for each CP-violating source, with the chiral Lagrangians constructed by introducing new building blocks (such as \(\beta^+\) for the chiral-singlet qCEDM) and treating the 4q and 4qLR operators as new scalar sources analogous to the quark mass matrix.

## Key numbers

- The bottom-quark EDM and chromo-EDM are expected to scale as \(d_b, \tilde{d}_b \sim m_b/\Lambda^2\), where \(m_b\) is the bottom quark mass and \(\Lambda\) is the scale of BSM physics (e.g., TeV scale).
- The four-quark operator coefficients are expected to scale as \(\mu^{ub,db,sb}_{1,8}\sim 1/\Lambda^2\).
- The 4qLR operator coefficients scale as \(\nu_{1,8}^{ub} \sim v^2/(m_W^2 \Lambda^2) \sim 1/\Lambda^2\), where \(v \simeq 250\) GeV is the electroweak scale and \(m_W\) is the W boson mass.
- The pion decay constant \(F_\pi\) is used in the definition of the Goldstone boson field \(U = \exp(i\phi/F_\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends previous EDM calculations for systems with first-generation valence quarks (nucleons, nuclei, atoms, molecules) by authors such as de Vries (2010, 2012), Bsaisou (2014), and Yamanaka (2020) to the bottom baryon sector, motivated by experimental prospects for measuring EDMs of charm and bottom baryons discussed by Fomin (2017), Aiola (2020), and Bagli (2017). The calculations enable future experimental searches to directly constrain CP-violating SMEFT operators involving bottom quarks, complementing indirect limits from light-quark EDM experiments and helping to disentangle the source of any observed CP violation.
