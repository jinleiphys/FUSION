# Foundations of the proxy-SU(3) symmetry in heavy nuclei

> We show that within the proxy-SU(3) scheme the wave functions of the normal parity orbitals in a given nuclear shell are affected very little as a result of the replacement of the abnormal parity orbitals by their 0[110] proxy-SU(3) counterparts.

## Key claim

The proxy-SU(3) approximation affects the wave functions of normal parity orbitals in a given nuclear shell very little when abnormal parity orbitals are replaced by their 0[110] proxy counterparts, with the 50-82 proton shell being the worst possible example for this substitution and the approximation improving in higher shells.

## Method

The authors compared Nilsson model wave functions from the standard 50-82 proton shell calculation (containing 10 positive parity and 6 negative parity orbitals) with wave functions from the full sdg proxy-SU(3) shell calculation (containing the same 10 positive parity orbitals plus 5 additional positive parity orbitals). Overlaps between the two sets of wave functions were computed for 7 values of the deformation parameter ε, using the asymptotic Nilsson wave functions in the limit of large deformations.

## Key numbers

- Overlaps for the 1/2[400] orbital range from 0.999 at ε=0.0 to 1.000 at ε=0.4, 0.5, and 0.6, with a minimum of 0.853 at ε=0.2
- Overlaps for the 1/2[411] orbital range from 0.981 at ε=0.0 to 0.997 at ε=0.6, with a minimum of 0.670 at ε=0.3
- Overlaps for the 3/2[402] orbital range from 0.977 at ε=0.0 to 0.999 at ε=0.6, with a minimum of 0.846 at ε=0.2
- Overlaps for the 7/2[404] orbital range from 0.959 at ε=0.0 to 0.943 at ε=0.6, with a minimum of 0.759 at ε=0.3
- The 50-82 shell calculation involves 10 Nilsson orbitals of normal parity and 6 orbitals of opposite parity, while the sdg proxy calculation involves the same 10 positive parity orbitals plus 5 more positive parity orbitals

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) symmetry introduced by Bonatsos et al. (2017) by providing quantitative wave-function overlap calculations that justify the approximation beyond the earlier energy-level comparisons. The results enable parameter-free SU(3) calculations for heavy deformed even-even nuclei using algebraic Hamiltonians of the form Q·Q + h, and support applications such as the explanation of prolate over oblate dominance. The authors note that M1 transition probabilities and magnetic moments, as studied by previous pseudo-SU(3) work, provide an appropriate test ground for the scheme.
