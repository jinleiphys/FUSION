# Probing $NNΩ_{ccc}$ three-body systems with the modern QCD $NΩ_{ccc}$ interaction

> Newly, first-principles lattice QCD results at the physical pion mass, $ m_π\backsimeq 137.1 $ MeV, have been reported by the HAL QCD Collaboration for the S-wave interaction between the nucleon ($N$) and the triply charmed Omega baryon ($Ω_{ccc}$). The $NΩ_{ccc}$ potentials in the spin-1 $ \left(^{3}S_{1}\right) $ and spin-2 $ \left(^{5}S_{2}\right) $ channels were derived and found to be attractive, though no two-body bound state was supported in these channels. The present work investigates the $NNΩ_{ccc}$ three-body system using the Malfliet-Tjon $NN$ potential. Analyses of spin-1, spin-averaged, and spin-2 $NΩ_{ccc}$ channels (at Euclidean times 16, 17, 18) reveal a three-body bound state only for the d-$Ω_{ccc}$ configuration with spin $(0)1/2^{+}$ and $t/a=16$. Its binding energy ($B_3 = -2.255$ MeV) lies slightly below the deuteron's ($B_d = -2.23$ MeV). Other parameter sets do not yield a bound state, and complex scaling analysis indicates these configurations correspond to virtual states rather than resonances. The Coulomb potential's role was also examined to differentiate charged states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a three-body bound state exists only for the d-$\Omega_{ccc}$ configuration with spin $(0)1/2^{+}$ at Euclidean time $t/a=16$, with a binding energy of $B_3 = -2.255$ MeV that lies slightly below the deuteron binding energy of $B_d = -2.23$ MeV; however, this result is conditional on the specific lattice QCD potential parametrization and is not observed for other parameter sets or configurations.

## Method

The authors employed the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) within the Faddeev formalism to solve the three-body Schrodinger equation for the $NN\Omega_{ccc}$ system, using the Malfliet-Tjon potential for the $NN$ interaction and lattice QCD-derived $N\Omega_{ccc}$ potentials from the HAL QCD Collaboration at physical pion mass. The $N\Omega_{ccc}$ potentials in the spin-1 ($^{3}S_{1}$), spin-averaged ($^{4}S_{3/2}$), and spin-2 ($^{5}S_{2}$) channels were fitted to a two-range Gaussian form from lattice data at Euclidean times $t/a = 16, 17, 18$. The complex scaling method (CSM) was applied with a rotation angle of $\theta = 30^{\circ}$ to distinguish bound states from resonances and virtual states, and a coupling constant variation technique was used to extrapolate results to the physical point.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: $B_d = -2.23$ MeV, reproduced by the Malfliet-Tjon $NN$ potential
- Three-body binding energy for d-$\Omega_{ccc}$ $(0)1/2^{+}$ at $t/a=16$: $B_3 = -2.255$ MeV (without Coulomb interaction)
- Nucleon mass: $m_N = 938.9$ MeV/$c^{2}$; Omega baryon mass: $m_{\Omega_{ccc}} = 4796.8$ MeV/$c^{2}$
- Lattice spacing: $a \simeq 0.084372$ fm, corresponding to $a^{-1} \simeq 2338.8$ MeV, with spatial extent $L \simeq 8.1$ fm
- Pion mass: $m_{\pi} \simeq 137.1$ MeV (physical pion mass)
- $N\Omega_{ccc}$ threshold: approximately 5740 MeV, about 200 MeV below the $\Lambda_{c}\Xi_{cc}$ threshold (5910 MeV) and $\Sigma_{c}\Xi_{cc}$ threshold (6080 MeV)
- For $pn\Omega_{ccc}$ system at $t/a=16$ without Coulomb: three-body energies of $-2.25$ MeV ($^{3}S_{1}$), $-2.20$ MeV ($^{4}S_{3/2}$), $-2.21$ MeV ($^{5}S_{2}$)
- For $nn\Omega_{ccc}$ system at $t/a=16$: three-body energies of 1.30 MeV ($^{3}S_{1}$), 1.83 MeV ($^{4}S_{3/2}$), 2.59 MeV ($^{5}S_{2}$) (all unbound)
- For $pp\Omega_{ccc}$ system at $t/a=16$ without Coulomb: three-body energies of 2.95 MeV ($^{3}S_{1}$), 4.22 MeV ($^{4}S_{3/2}$), 4.10 MeV ($^{5}S_{2}$) (all unbound)

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD results of the HAL QCD Collaboration (Zhang et al.) for $N\Omega_{ccc}$ potentials at physical pion mass, which found attractive interactions but no two-body bound states in the spin-1 and spin-2 channels. It directly contradicts the recent Faddeev analysis by Filikhin et al. (2025), who found no bound states for the $NN\Omega_{ccc}$ system and instead predicted near-threshold resonances; the present study finds a bound d-$\Omega_{ccc}$ state at the physical point for one parameter set. The results enable future femtoscopic studies in high-energy collisions, such as those by the ALICE collaboration, to search for these predicted three-body states, and may motivate experimental searches for heavy-baryon interactions in the charm sector.
