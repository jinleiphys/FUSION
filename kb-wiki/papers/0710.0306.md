# Unitary Isobar Model - MAID2007

> The unitary isobar model MAID2007 has been developed to analyze the world data of pion photo- and electroproduction. The model contains both a common background and several resonance terms. The background is unitarized according to the K-matrix prescription, and the 13 four-star resonances with masses below 2 GeV are described by appropriately unitarized Breit-Wigner forms. The data have been analyzed by both single-energy and global fits, and the transverse and longitudinal helicity amplitudes have been extracted for the four-star resonances below 2 GeV. Because of its inherent simplicity, MAID2007 is well adopted for predictions and analysis of the observables in pion photo- and electroproduction.

## Key claim

The unitary isobar model MAID2007 provides a global and single-energy partial-wave analysis of world data for pion photo- and electroproduction, extracting transverse and longitudinal helicity amplitudes for all 13 four-star nucleon resonances below 2 GeV, but the authors caution that the fit errors appear unrealistically small due to the large number of data points fitted to a small set of parameters, and that the model uncertainty can be larger by an order of magnitude than the statistical errors.

## Method

The model combines a unitarized background, constructed from Born terms with energy-dependent pseudovector-pseudoscalar mixing and t-channel vector-meson exchange, with resonance contributions described by unitarized Breit-Wigner forms for all 13 four-star resonances below 2 GeV. The background is unitarized according to the K-matrix prescription using pion-nucleon elastic scattering amplitudes from the GWU/SAID analysis, while the resonance amplitudes include a phase factor to satisfy the Fermi-Watson theorem below two-pion threshold. The analysis proceeds in two steps: first a global (energy-dependent) fit to all data in the range 140 MeV ≤ Eγ ≤ 1610 MeV determines the multipole phases, then single-energy fits in bins of 10-20 MeV vary the multipole magnitudes while keeping the phases fixed.

## Key numbers

- Total photoproduction data base contains 19676 data points within the energy range 140 MeV < Eγ < 1610 MeV, including 14700 points from the SAID2000 compilation and 4976 additional points from experiments at MAMI, GRAAL, LEGS, and ELSA
- For the Δ(1232) resonance, the ratio REM = E2/M1 = -2.2% at Q²=0, and RCM = C2/M1 decreases sharply as Q² approaches zero
- The PS-PV mixing parameter Λm = 423 MeV, and the low-energy correction parameter A = 1.9 × 10⁻³/mπ⁺
- For the P33(1232) resonance at Q²=0, the proton helicity amplitudes are A1/2 = -140 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² and A3/2 = -265 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²
- The total χ² for the global fit to proton channels is 76866 for 17434 data points, and for neutron channels is 9262 for 2242 data points

## Context

This work extends the earlier MAID98, MAID2000, MAID2003, and MAID2005 versions of the unitary isobar model developed at Mainz, incorporating all 13 four-star resonances below 2 GeV (compared to only 7 in MAID98) and using helicity amplitudes as input parameters rather than just transverse couplings. The model enables extraction of electromagnetic transition form factors for nucleon resonances from pion photo- and electroproduction data, providing an alternative to the GWU/SAID partial-wave analysis and coupled-channels approaches, and is particularly suited for analyzing the large new datasets from Jefferson Lab, MAMI, and other facilities that cover the resonance region up to W=2 GeV.
