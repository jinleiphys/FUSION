# On the validity of the Wigner-Seitz approximation in neutron star crust

> The inner crust of neutron stars formed of nuclear clusters immersed in a neutron sea has been widely studied in the framework of the Wigner-Seitz approximation since the seminal work of Negele and Vautherin. In this article, the validity of this approximation is discussed in the framework of the band theory of solids. For a typical cell of $^{200}$Zr, present in the external layers of the inner crust, it is shown that the ground state properties of the neutron gas are rather well reproduced by the Wigner-Seitz approximation, while its dynamical properties depend on the energy scale of the process of interest or on the temperature. It is concluded that the Wigner-Seitz approximation is well suited for describing the inner crust of young neutron stars and the collapsing core of massive stars during supernovae explosions. However the band theory is required for low temperature fluid dynamics.

## Key claim

The Wigner-Seitz approximation reproduces ground-state properties of the neutron gas in the outer layers of the inner crust rather well, but its validity for dynamical properties depends on the energy scale of the process or the temperature; the approximation is suitable for hot dense matter (temperatures above about 10^9 K) but band theory is required for low-temperature transport properties such as the effective neutron mass giving rise to entrainment effects.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations with the Skyrme interaction SLy4 for a Wigner-Seitz cell containing a ^200Zr cluster at a baryon density of about 7 × 10^11 g/cm^3. They then constructed an effective Schrödinger equation from the Wigner-Seitz mean fields and solved it with Bloch boundary conditions using the Linearized Augmented Plane Wave method to obtain the full band theory results. The single-particle wavefunctions, energy spectra, and level densities from the two approaches were compared for the unbound neutrons.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density considered: ρ ∼ 7 × 10^11 g/cm^3
- Cell composition: ^200Zr (Z=40, 160 neutrons per lattice site)
- Wigner-Seitz cell radius: R_cell = 49.2 fm
- Asymptotic mean field potential for unbound neutrons: -0.05 MeV
- Number of unbound neutrons per lattice site: 70 out of 160
- Difference in free neutron count inside a radius of 15 fm between band theory and Wigner-Seitz approximation: ΔN = 3
- Quasi band gap in the band theory spectrum: about 30 keV near 90 keV
- Level spacing in the Wigner-Seitz approximation: about 100 keV
- Energy at the bottom of the valence band: E_v ≈ -0.031 MeV
- Characteristic level spacing range across the crust: from about 100 keV to 200 keV, corresponding to temperatures of order 10^9 K

## Context

This work extends the seminal study of Negele and Vautherin (1973), who first applied the Wigner-Seitz approximation to neutron star crust, by quantitatively comparing it to the full band theory of solids. The results confirm that the Wigner-Seitz approximation is adequate for describing hot dense matter in young neutron stars and supernova cores, as previously assumed by Bonche et al. (1981). However, the paper shows that band theory calculations, such as those by Carter et al. (2005) and Chamel (2005, 2006), are necessary for low-temperature fluid dynamics and entrainment effects. The findings also motivate future investigation of the approximation's validity in denser crust layers where pairing effects become important, as studied by Baldo et al. (2005, 2006).
