# Bound state equation for 4 or more relativistic particles

> We apply the 3D reduction method we recently proposed for the N-particle Bethe-Salpeter equation to the 4-particle case. We find that the writing of the Bethe-Salpeter equation is not a straightforward task when N is larger or equal to 4, owing to the presence of mutually unconnected interactions, which could lead to an overcounting of some diagrams in the resulting full propagator. We overcome this difficulty in the N=4 case by including three counterterms in the Bethe-Salpeter kernel. The application of our 3D reduction method to the resulting Bethe-Salpeter equation suggests us a modified 3D reduction method, which gives directly the 3D potential, without the need of writing the Bethe-Salpeter kernel explicitly. The modified reduction method is usable for all N.

# Bound state equation for 4 or more relativistic particles

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter kernel for N ≥ 4 particles cannot be written straightforwardly because mutually unconnected two-body kernels (e.g., K₁₂ and K₃₄) lead to overcounting of diagrams in the expansion of the full propagator; this difficulty is overcome for N=4 by including three counterterms in the kernel, and the resulting 3D reduction method can be modified to bypass explicit kernel construction entirely for any N.

## Method

The author applies an integrating propagator-based 3D reduction method, previously developed for the N-particle Bethe-Salpeter equation, to the four-fermion case. The Bethe-Salpeter kernel for four particles is constructed by adding the six two-body irreducible kernels, the four three-body irreducible kernels, and the four-body irreducible kernel, with three counterterms of the form −K₁₂K₃₄ (and permutations) to remove double-counting of diagrams from commuting kernel pairs. The 3D potential is then computed via a series expansion around a positive-energy instantaneous approximation, and the cancellation of leading terms is verified. A modified reduction method is proposed that writes the 3D potential directly from the irreducible interactions with a single-counting prescription, avoiding explicit Bethe-Salpeter kernel construction.

## Key numbers

* The free propagator for N particles is integrated over relative energies to yield a 3D free propagator with factor (−2iπ)^(N−1)/ω, where ω = 2^b E_{f+1}...E_N for f fermions and b bosons.
* For four fermions in the positive-energy instantaneous approximation, the 3D potential reduces to the sum of six two-fermion potentials: V = V₁₂ + V₃₄ + V₁₃ + V₂₄ + V₁₄ + V₂₃, with V_{ij} = −2iπ β_i β_j K_{ij}.
* The counterterm K₁₂K₃₄ integrates to two terms: V₁₂V₃₄ [1/(P₀−E₁−E₂−E′₃−E′₄) + 1/(P₀−E′₁−E′₂−E₃−E₄)], which cancel against the −⟨K⟩g⁰⟨K⟩ terms in the series expansion.
* The Bethe-Salpeter kernel for N=4 contains 11 subkernels (groups of irreducible graphs) plus 3 counterterms from second order in the irreducible interactions.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous 3D reduction method for the two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation (Bijtebier, 1999) to the four-body problem, and generalizes the approach of Logunov and Tavkhelidze (1963) for two particles and Kvinikhidze and Stoyanov for three particles. The difficulty with mutually unconnected kernels for N ≥ 4 was previously noted by Khvedelidze and Kvinikhidze (1999) and others. The modified reduction method that bypasses explicit Bethe-Salpeter kernel construction enables straightforward treatment of bound states with five or more relativistic particles, where the kernel would otherwise become increasingly complicated due to multiple commuting kernel pairs.
