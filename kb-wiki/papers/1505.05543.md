# Lattice QCD at Non-Zero Temperature

> This is a review of selected recent developments in finite-temperature lattice QCD. The focus is on the properties of the chiral crossover region, deconfinement and fluctuations of conserved charges, the equation of state, properties of heavy quarkonia and reconstruction of spectral functions.

# Lattice QCD at Non-Zero Temperature

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes selected recent developments in finite-temperature lattice QCD, focusing on the chiral crossover region, deconfinement, fluctuations of conserved charges, the equation of state, and heavy quarkonia properties. The author notes that while lattice calculations have established that the transition at physical quark masses is a rapid crossover rather than a genuine phase transition, the location of the critical point and nature of phases at large baryon chemical potential remain open questions.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations using Monte Carlo simulations of the path integral on a space-time lattice, with various fermion discretizations including staggered fermions (stout and highly improved staggered quarks or HISQ), domain-wall fermions (DWF), Wilson fermions, and twisted mass Wilson fermions. For spectral function reconstruction, Bayesian techniques such as the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) are employed to invert the integral relation between Euclidean correlators and spectral functions, and for heavy quarks, effective theories like Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) are used.

## Key numbers

- Chiral crossover temperature from Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration: T_c = 147(2)(3) MeV from the peak in chiral susceptibility, and T_c = 157(3)(3) or 155(3)(3) MeV from the inflection point of the chiral condensate
- Chiral crossover temperature from HotQCD collaboration: T_c = 154(8)(1) MeV from O(4) scaling analysis
- Chiral crossover temperature with domain-wall fermions: T_c = 155(1)(8) MeV at the physical pion mass m_pi = 135 MeV
- The U_A(1) symmetry-breaking difference remains large at T_c, indicating no restoration until at least T > 196 MeV
- For SU(3) pure gauge theory, T_c = 293 MeV determined from the plaquette susceptibility
- The softest point of the equation of state (minimum speed of sound) occurs at T approximately 145-150 MeV
- At T = 214 MeV, the trace anomaly with and without dynamical charm is the same
- The eta_c state survives up to 1.7 T_c in quenched QCD calculations
- No melting of eta_c and J/psi mesons observed up to 1.4 T_c in 2+1 flavor QCD with m_pi = 545 MeV
- The Upsilon ground state survives up to 1.9 T_c in NRQCD calculations
- The chi_b1 P-wave state shows dissociation slightly above T_c in one calculation, but survives up to 1.6 T_c in another calculation using different gauge backgrounds

## Context

This review extends the ongoing lattice QCD effort to understand the QCD phase diagram and properties of the quark-gluon plasma, building on foundational work by McLerran (1980), Kuti (1980), and Engels (1980) on finite-temperature transitions. The results enable comparison with heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, and the equation of state calculations can be used to determine freeze-out parameters as shown by Bazavov (2012) and Borsanyi (2013). The paper highlights tensions between different calculations of bottomonium spectral functions by Aarts (2014) and Kim (2014) that require further study, and notes that the nature of the chiral transition in the limit of vanishing light quark masses remains unsettled due to the role of the axial U_A(1) anomaly.
