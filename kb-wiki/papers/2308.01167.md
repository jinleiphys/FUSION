# Open heavy flavors: Theory

> A brief overview of the theory of open heavy flavor dynamics in QCD matter produced in high energy heavy-ion collisions is presented. First, we will summarise the phenomenological efforts to estimate the heavy quark diffusion coefficients obtained within different models. Then, the recent theoretical developments from different groups to probe the medium properties using heavy quarks will be presented. Heavy quarks are also considered as an ideal probe for the initial stage of heavy-ion collisions. In the end, we present the recent theoretical progress made to probe the early-stage effects, pre-equilibrium phase, and electromagnetic fields.

# Open heavy flavors: Theory

## Key claim

Heavy quarks are not expected to fully thermalize in the quark-gluon plasma because their thermalization time is comparable to the QGP fireball lifetime, preserving a memory of their interaction history; however, the actual thermalization time extracted from current calculations is about 2–6 fm/c at low transverse momentum, and the pre-equilibrium Glasma phase, previously ignored, significantly alters the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow, requiring its inclusion for simultaneous description of both observables.

## Method

The paper reviews phenomenological transport models (Boltzmann transport and Langevin dynamics) that extract the heavy quark spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_x\) by comparing simulated nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of D-mesons to experimental data. The drag coefficient \(\Gamma\) and momentum diffusion coefficient are inputs to these models; \(D_x\) is obtained in the static limit via \(D_x = T/(M\Gamma)\). For the pre-equilibrium Glasma phase, the momentum diffusion coefficient is computed from \(\sigma_p = 2Dt\) at different saturation scales \(Q_s\), and an effective temperature is derived from the energy density at \(t = 0.5\) fm.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass \(M_c \sim 1.5\) GeV, bottom quark mass \(M_b \sim 4.5\) GeV.
- Heavy quark thermalization time in QGP: \(\tau_{th} \cong 1.8 \, (2\pi T D_x)/(T/T_c)^2\) fm/c, with current calculations indicating 2–6 fm/c at low \(p_T\).
- In the pre-equilibrium Glasma phase, two values of the saturation scale are considered: \(Q_s = 1\) GeV and \(Q_s = 2\) GeV.
- Non-equilibrium effects modify charm quark transport coefficients by about 20–30%.
- The \(D^0\) meson \(dv_1/dy\) measured at RHIC by STAR is about 25 times larger than that of charged kaons.
- The ALICE Collaboration at LHC reported a positive slope for \(\Delta v_1\) (directed flow splitting between \(D\) and \(\overline{D}\)) about 3 orders of magnitude larger than that of light-charged hadrons.

## Context

This review summarizes the theoretical status of open heavy flavor dynamics in QCD matter, extending the phenomenological work of Rapp (2018), Aarts (2016), and Cao (2018) on heavy quarks as QGP probes. It contrasts transport model extractions of \(D_x\) with lattice QCD results and highlights recent developments incorporating nonperturbative effects via T-matrix approaches (Liu 2020) and Cornell-type potentials (Xing 2021). The paper enables future work on heavy quark dynamics in small systems (p-Pb collisions), beam energy scan energies, and new observables such as heavy-light event-by-event correlations and \(D-\overline{D}\) correlations to constrain the temperature dependence of transport coefficients.
