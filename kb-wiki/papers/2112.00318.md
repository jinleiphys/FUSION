# Correlated Dirac eigenvalues around the transition temperature on $N_τ=8$ lattices

> We investigate the criticality of chiral phase transition manifested in the first and second order derivatives of Dirac eigenvalue spectrum with respect to light quark mass in (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD. Simulations are performed at temperatures from about 137 MeV to 176 MeV on $N_τ=8$ lattices using the highly improved staggered quarks and the tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action. The strange quark mass is fixed to its physical value $m_s^{\text{phy}}$ and the light quark mass is set to $m_s^{\text{phy}}/40$ which corresponds to a Goldstone pion mass $m_π=110$ MeV. We find that in contrast to the case at $T\simeq 205$ MeV $m_l^{-1} \partial ρ(λ, m_l)/\partial m_l$ is no longer equal to $\partial ^2ρ(λ, m_l)/\partial m_l^2$ and $\partial ^2ρ(λ, m_l)/\partial m_l^2$ even becomes negative at certain low temperatures. This means that as temperature getting closer to $T_c$ $ρ(λ, m_l)$ is no longer proportional to $m_l^2$ and thus dilute instanton gas approximation is not valid for these temperatures. We demonstrate the temperature dependence can be factored out in $\partial ρ(λ, m_l)/ \partial m_l$ and $\partial^2 ρ(λ, m_l)/ \partial m_l^2$ at $T \in [137, 153]$ MeV, and then we propose a feasible method to estimate the power $c$ given $ρ\propto m_l^{c}$.

## Key claim

The paper finds that near the chiral phase transition temperature (around 137–176 MeV), the Dirac eigenvalue spectral density ρ(λ, m_l) is no longer proportional to m_l², contradicting the dilute instanton gas approximation that holds at higher temperatures (≈205 MeV). The authors demonstrate that the temperature dependence can be factored out of the first and second quark mass derivatives of the spectral density in the range 137–153 MeV, enabling a method to estimate the power c in ρ ∝ m_l^c.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations using highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) and tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on N_τ=8 lattices at temperatures from 137 MeV to 176 MeV. They computed the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum ρ_U(λ) and its n-point correlations C_n (n≤3) using the Chebyshev filtering technique combined with stochastic estimation on approximately 10^3 configurations per temperature, with the strange quark mass fixed to its physical value m_s^phy and light quark mass set to m_s^phy/40 (corresponding to m_π=110 MeV). The first and second derivatives of the spectral density with respect to light quark mass were then extracted from the correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 137 MeV to 176 MeV on N_τ=8 lattices
- Light quark mass: m_s^phy/40, corresponding to Goldstone pion mass m_π=110 MeV
- Pseudo-critical temperature for comparison: T_pc ≈ 156 MeV (from HotQCD 2018 and Borsanyi 2020)
- Chiral critical temperature estimate: T_c = 132^{+3}_{-6} MeV (from HotQCD 2019)
- At T≈205 MeV (≈1.6 T_c), the relation m_l^{-1} ∂ρ/∂m_l = ∂²ρ/∂m_l² holds, consistent with ρ ∝ m_l²
- At temperatures from 137 MeV to 171 MeV, m_l^{-1} ∂ρ/∂m_l ≠ ∂²ρ/∂m_l², and ∂²ρ/∂m_l² becomes negative in the infrared region at temperatures below 157 MeV
- For T=140 MeV, the power c in ρ ∝ m_l^c is constrained to the range (0.1, 0.7)
- The temperature factorization works for T ∈ [137, 153] MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of Ding (2020), which examined Dirac eigenvalue derivatives at a single high temperature T≈205 MeV, to a temperature window around the chiral transition. It contradicts the dilute instanton gas approximation (which predicts ρ ∝ m_l²) near T_c, and connects to scaling analyses of the chiral order parameter and its susceptibility from HotQCD (2019) and Ejiri (2009). The proposed factorization method enables future extraction of the critical exponent c from the spectral density, and the authors plan to extend the study to other quark masses.
