# Mass dependence of onset of multifragmentation in low energy heavy-ion collisions

> Based on the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) picture, we calculate the energy and mass dependence of fragment production. For the present study, we simulated the reactions of $^{20}Ne+^{20}Ne$, $^{40}Ar+^{45}Sc$, $^{58}Ni+^{58}Ni$, $^{86}Kr+^{93}Nb$,$^{129}Xe+^{124}Sn$ and $^{197}Au+^{197}Au$ at central geometry. Our findings clearly show a linear mass dependence for the peak center-of-mass energy at which the maximal IMF production occurs. Such linear dependence for peak center-of-mass energy on the system size has also been observed in recent experimental studies. We also predict a similar behavior for the multiplicities of different kinds of fragments. Experiments are called for to verify this prediction.

# Mass dependence of onset of multifragmentation in low energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The peak center-of-mass energy at which maximal intermediate mass fragment (IMF) production occurs scales linearly with the total mass of the colliding system in central heavy-ion collisions, as predicted by quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations; the authors also predict a similar linear (power-law with exponent near unity) mass dependence for the multiplicities of various fragment types, but note that experiments are needed to verify this prediction.

## Method
The authors performed QMD simulations of six symmetric heavy-ion reactions (20Ne+20Ne, 40Ar+45Sc, 58Ni+58Ni, 86Kr+93Nb, 129Xe+124Sn, and 197Au+197Au) at central geometry (impact parameter b = 0 fm) over a range of incident energies. Fragment formation was identified using the minimum spanning tree method with a binding energy check (MSTB method, with E_bind = -4.0 MeV/nucleon for fragments with N_f >= 3). A soft equation of state and Cugnon parametrization of the nucleon-nucleon cross section were employed, and 500 events were simulated per energy-system combination.

## Key numbers
- Peak center-of-mass energy for maximal IMF production scales linearly with total system mass A_tot as: mA_tot + c (explicit fit parameters not given in text).
- Peak mean IMF multiplicity scales as cA_tot^tau with exponent tau close to unity (tau ~ 1) for all fragment types studied.
- The Gaussian width parameter L was fixed at 1.08 fm^2 for density calculations.
- Binding energy threshold for fragment identification was set at -4.0 MeV/nucleon for fragments with N_f >= 3.
- Incident energy ranges studied: 20Ne+20Ne (10 to 55 AMeV), 40Ar+45Sc (35 to 115 AMeV), 58Ni+58Ni (35 to 95 AMeV), 86Kr+93Nb (35 to 95 AMeV), 129Xe+124Sn (45 to 130 AMeV), 197Au+197Au (70 to 130 AMeV).

## Context
This work extends experimental observations by Sisan et al. (2001) using the MSU 4pi-Array, who reported a linear peak energy dependence on system size but found that percolation calculations could not fully explain this dependence. The present QMD-based study reproduces the experimental trend for peak center-of-mass energy and shows that percolation models fail to reproduce the sharp mass dependence, suggesting that dynamical models are necessary. The results enable further theoretical and experimental investigation of finite-size effects in multifragmentation, particularly the predicted power-law scaling of fragment multiplicities with system mass, which the authors call for experiments to verify.
