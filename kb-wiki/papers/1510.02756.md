# BEC-BCS crossover in a cold and magnetized two color NJL model

> The BEC-BCS crossover for a NJL model with diquark interactions is studied in the presence of an external magnetic field. Particular attention is paid to different regularization schemes used in the literature. A thorough comparison of results is performed for the case of a cold and magnetized two-color NJL model. According to our results, the critical chemical potential for the BEC transition exhibits a clear inverse magnetic catalysis effect for magnetic fields in the range $ 1 \lesssim eB/m_π^2 \lesssim 20 $. As for the BEC-BCS crossover, the corresponding critical chemical potential is very weakly sensitive to magnetic fields up to $eB \sim 9\ m_π^2$, showing a much smaller inverse magnetic catalysis as compared to the BEC transition, and displays a strong magnetic catalysis from this point on.

## Key claim

The critical chemical potential for the BEC transition exhibits a clear inverse magnetic catalysis effect for magnetic fields in the range $1 \lesssim eB/m_\pi^2 \lesssim 20$, while the BEC-BCS crossover critical chemical potential is very weakly sensitive to magnetic fields up to $eB \sim 9\ m_\pi^2$ and displays strong magnetic catalysis beyond that point, with the caveat that these results depend on the regularization scheme used.

## Method

The authors studied a two-color, two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar-pseudoscalar and diquark interactions in the presence of a constant external magnetic field, using the mean-field approximation at zero temperature. They solved the coupled gap equations for the dressed quark mass $m$ and the diquark condensate $\Delta$ as functions of baryon chemical potential and magnetic field, and compared three regularization schemes: a Lorentzian form factor with exponent N=5 (Lor5), a Wood-Saxon form factor with $\alpha=0.05$ (WS0.05), and a magnetic field independent regularization (MFIR) scheme. The critical chemical potential for the BEC transition was determined from the vanishing of the quadratic Ginzburg-Landau coefficient $\alpha_2$, while the BEC-BCS crossover point was identified by the condition $\mu_N = \mu_B/2 - m = 0$.

## Key numbers

- The BEC transition at $B=0$ occurs at $\mu_B = m_\pi$, where $m_\pi = 0.14$ GeV, in agreement with chiral effective theories.
- Inverse magnetic catalysis for the BEC transition is clearly seen in the interval $1 \lesssim eB/m_\pi^2 \lesssim 20$ for the MFIR scheme.
- The BEC-BCS crossover critical chemical potential shows little change for $eB/m_\pi^2 \lesssim 9$, with a much smaller inverse magnetic catalysis effect than the BEC transition.
- For the MFIR scheme, the dressed quark mass at $\mu=B=0$ is $m(0) = 305.385$ MeV, with parameters $m_c = 5.400$ MeV, $G = 7.23$ GeV$^{-2}$, and $\Lambda = 657$ MeV.
- For the Lor5 scheme, $m(0) = 337.232$ MeV, $m_c = 5.398$ MeV, $G = 8.00$ GeV$^{-2}$, and $\Lambda = 616$ MeV.
- For the WS0.05 scheme, $m(0) = 311.865$ MeV, $m_c = 5.401$ MeV, $G = 7.39$ GeV$^{-2}$, and $\Lambda = 650$ MeV.
- Magnetic fields of $eB = 10\ m_\pi^2$ and $20\ m_\pi^2$ correspond to approximately 0.2 GeV$^2$ and 0.4 GeV$^2$, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the BEC-BCS crossover in two-color NJL models at zero magnetic field by Sun (2007) and Brauner (2009) to include the effects of an external magnetic field, going beyond the two-channel model approach of Wang (2010) by using a single-channel NJL model. The results are relevant for understanding the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter under strong magnetic fields expected in magnetars (with fields up to $eB/m_\pi^2 \simeq 30$) and in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC (with fields of order $eB \sim 15\ m_\pi^2$). The inverse magnetic catalysis effect observed here was first reported in the NJL model by Klimenko and later found in other models; the MFIR regularization scheme developed by Allen (2015) provides a way to avoid the unphysical oscillations present in smooth regulator schemes like Lor5 and WS0.05.
