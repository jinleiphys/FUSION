# Infrared QCD and the Renormalisation Group

> We study the infrared regime of QCD by means of a Wilsonian renormalisation group. We explain how, in general, the infrared structure of Green functions is deduced in this approach. Our reasoning is put to work in Landau gauge QCD, where the leading infrared terms of the propagators are computed. The results support the Kugo-Ojima scenario of confinement. Possible extensions are indicated.

# Infrared QCD and the Renormalisation Group

## Key claim
The infrared behaviour of gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge QCD, computed via the exact (Wilsonian) renormalisation group, supports the Kugo-Ojima confinement scenario, but the numerical value of the ghost anomalous dimension varies slightly with the choice of cutoff function when corrections beyond leading order are included.

## Method
The authors employed an exact (functional) renormalisation group flow equation for the effective action, integrating out quantum fluctuations with momenta above a sliding infrared cutoff scale k. They truncated the full effective action to retain the full momentum dependence of the gluon and ghost two-point functions in Landau gauge, with vertices constrained by truncated Slavnov-Taylor identities. The leading infrared anomalous dimensions were extracted by solving integral equations for the propagator structure in the deep infrared regime where p² ≪ Λ_QCD² and k² ≪ p², exploiting fixed-point behaviour of the flow.

## Key numbers
- Ghost anomalous dimension κ_C = 0.59535 (leading order, independent of cutoff function)
- Running coupling in the deep infrared α_s = 2.9717 (leading order, independent of cutoff function)
- Ghost anomalous dimension κ_C ranges between 0.539 (sharp cutoff) and 0.59535 (optimised cutoff) when iterating beyond leading order
- QCD scale Λ_QCD ≈ 200 MeV, defining the boundary of the strongly coupled deep infrared regime (p² ≪ Λ_QCD²)

## Context
This work extends the non-perturbative analysis of infrared QCD using renormalisation group methods, complementing earlier studies based on truncated Schwinger-Dyson equations (Alkofer 2000) and stochastic quantisation (Zwanziger 2002), and consistent with lattice simulations (Cucchieri 1997). The results provide independent evidence for the Kugo-Ojima confinement criterion in Landau gauge. The approach enables future extensions to vertex corrections, finite-temperature QCD Green functions, and the inclusion of dynamical quarks.
