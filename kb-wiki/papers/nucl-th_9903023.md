# Superfluidity in a Model of Massless Fermions Coupled to Scalar Bosons

> We study superfluidity in a model of massless fermions coupled to a massive scalar field through a Yukawa interaction. Gap equations for a condensate with total spin J=0 are solved in the mean-field approximation. For the Yukawa interaction, the gaps for right- and left-handed fermions are equal in magnitude and opposite in sign, so that condensation occurs in the J^P = 0^+ channel. At finite scalar mass, there are two different gaps for fermions of a given chirality, corresponding to condensation of particle pairs or of antiparticle pairs. These gaps become degenerate in the limit of infinite scalar mass.

## Key claim

For massless fermions coupled to scalar bosons via a Yukawa interaction, condensation occurs in the \(J^P = 0^+\) channel with gaps for right- and left-handed fermions equal in magnitude and opposite in sign; at finite scalar mass there are two distinct gaps for fermions of a given chirality (corresponding to particle-pair and antiparticle-pair condensation) that become degenerate only in the infinite-scalar-mass limit, meaning NJL-type models are unrealistic because they force equality of quasiparticle and quasi-antiparticle gaps.

## Method

The authors studied a model of massless fermions interacting via Yukawa coupling to a massive scalar field, without a quartic scalar self-interaction. Gap equations for a condensate with total spin \(J=0\) were derived and solved in the mean-field approximation, assuming the gap functions are momentum-independent constants. The full fermion propagator was computed, and the gap equations were projected onto four chirality-helicity channels, with the scalar boson propagator \(D(p,M_s) = 1/(M_s^2 - p^2)\) mediating the interaction.

## Key numbers

* For the Yukawa interaction, the gaps for right- and left-handed fermions are equal in magnitude and opposite in sign, so condensation occurs in the \(J^P = 0^+\) channel.
* At finite scalar mass \(M_s < \infty\), the quasiparticle and quasi-antiparticle gaps are not equal; they become degenerate only in the limit \(M_s \rightarrow \infty\).
* The excitation energy for a quasiparticle at the Fermi surface is \(2\phi\), while for a quasi-antiparticle with zero momentum it is \(2\sqrt{\mu^2 + \phi^2}\) (for a common gap \(\phi = 0.5\mu\) in the figure).
* The gap equations reduce to a single independent gap function for point-like (NJL-type) interactions, where \(D(k-q,M_s) \rightarrow 1/M_s^2\) and terms proportional to \(\hat{\bf k} \cdot \hat{\bf q}\) vanish.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of color superconductivity in QCD, particularly those by Bailin and Love (1984) who neglected quasi-antiparticle branches and found a single gap equation, and by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998) who used NJL-type models. The authors show that for finite-range scalar boson exchange, two independent gap functions appear, which may affect the order of the color-superconducting phase transition: the presence of both particle and antiparticle condensates could drive the transition first order via the Coleman-Weinberg mechanism, rather than allowing a second-order transition as in the single-condensate case.
