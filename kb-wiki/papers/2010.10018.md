# Properties of heaviest nuclei with $98\leq Z \leq 126$ and $134 \leq N \leq 192$

> We systematically determine ground-state and saddle-point shapes and masses for 1305 heavy and superheavy nuclei with $Z=98-126$ and $N=134-192$, including odd-$A$ and odd-odd systems. From these, we derive static fission barrier heights, one- and two-nucleon separation energies, and $Q_α$ values for g.s. to g.s transitions. Our study is performed within the microscopic-macroscopic method with the deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle potential and the Yukawa-plus-exponential macroscopic energy taken as the smooth part. We use parameters of the model that were fitted previously to masses of even-even heavy nuclei. For systems with odd numbers of protons, neutrons, or both, we use a standard BCS method with blocking. Ground-state shapes and energies are found by the minimization over seven axially-symmetric deformations. A search for saddle-points was performed by using the "imaginary water flow" method in three consecutive stages, using five- (for nonaxial shapes) and seven-dimensional (for reflection-asymmetric shapes) deformation spaces. The results are collected in two main tables. Calculated ground-state mass excess, nucleon separation- and $Q_α$ energies, total, macroscopic(normalized to the macroscopic energy at the spherical shape) and shell corrections energies, and deformations are given for each nucleus in \mbox{Table 1}. \mbox{Table 2} contains calculated properties of the saddle-point configurations and the fission barrier heights. In \mbox{Tables 3-7}, are given calculated ground-state, inner and outer saddle-point and superdeformed secondary minima characteristics for 75 actinide nuclei, from Ac to Cf, for which experimental estimates of fission barrier heights are known. These results are an additional test of our model.

## Key claim

The authors systematically calculate ground-state and saddle-point properties for 1305 heavy and superheavy nuclei, including odd-A and odd-odd systems, using the microscopic-macroscopic method with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, but the predictions for fission barriers in superheavy nuclei are markedly different from those of the global m-m model of Moller and coworkers, despite similar agreement with experimental data in actinides.

## Method

The nuclear binding energy is calculated as a sum of a macroscopic Yukawa-plus-exponential liquid-drop energy and a microscopic Strutinsky shell correction plus BCS pairing correction, using the deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle potential with the "universal" parameter set. Ground-state shapes are found by minimization over seven axially-symmetric deformations (β20 through β80) using the conjugate gradient method with at least 30 random starting points per nucleus. Saddle points are located using the Imaginary Water Flow method in three consecutive stages: first in a five-dimensional triaxial deformation space (β20, β22, β40, β60, β80) on a grid of 29,250 points per nucleus, then checking stability against mass-asymmetry via minimization over β30, β50, β70, and finally using a seven-dimensional IWF search for reflection-asymmetric saddles.

## Key numbers

- 1305 heavy and superheavy nuclei with Z=98-126 and N=134-192 are systematically studied
- Ground-state minimization uses 7 axially-symmetric deformation parameters (β20 through β80)
- The primary saddle-point grid contains 29,250 points per nucleus, interpolated to 50,735,286 grid points with step 0.01 in each dimension
- Macroscopic parameters: a_v = 16.0643 MeV, κ_v = 1.9261, a_0 = 17.926 MeV, fitted to binding energies of even-even nuclei with Z ≥ 84
- Pairing strengths: g_0p = 13.40 MeV, g_1p = 44.89 MeV for protons; g_0n = 17.67 MeV, g_1n = -13.11 MeV for neutrons
- Average pairing corrections for odd systems: 0.824 MeV (Z-even, N-odd), 1.013 MeV (Z-odd, N-even), 1.703 MeV (Z-odd, N-odd)
- The smoothed pairing energy varies by about 50 keV over the whole deformation range in actinides
- 75 actinide nuclei from Ac to Cf are additionally calculated for comparison with experimental fission barrier estimates

## Context

This work extends the microscopic-macroscopic calculations of Moller et al. (2016, 2009) by including odd-A and odd-odd nuclei and using parameters fitted specifically to heavy nuclei rather than globally. The results have recently been used to estimate cross sections for 48Ca-induced complete fusion reactions leading to superheavy nuclei (Adamian et al., 2020), showing satisfactory agreement with experimental cross-sections for 3n evaporation channels. The data enable improved estimates of survival probabilities for superheavy element synthesis and provide a consistent set of nuclear properties (masses, separation energies, Qα values, and fission barriers) for 1305 nuclei that can be used in reaction modeling.
