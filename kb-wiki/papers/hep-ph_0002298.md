# Determination of equation of state of quark matter from $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ suppression at RHIC and LHC

> The long life-time of the quark-gluon plasma likely to be created in the relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies renders it sensitive to the details of the equation of state of the quark-matter. We show that the $p_T$ dependence of the survival probability of the directly produced $J/ψ$ at RHIC energies and that of the directly produced $Υ$ at LHC energies is quite sensitive to the speed of sound in the quark matter, which relates the pressure and the energy density of the plasma. The transverse expansion of the plasma is shown to strongly affect the $J/ψ$ suppression at LHC energies.

# Determination of equation of state of quark matter from J/ψ and Υ suppression at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim
The transverse momentum dependence of the survival probability for directly produced J/ψ at RHIC and directly produced Υ at LHC is sensitive to the speed of sound in quark matter, provided the plasma lifetime is sufficiently long; the effect is marginal at SPS and for J/ψ at LHC the transverse expansion of the plasma dominates the suppression pattern.

## Method
The authors model quarkonium suppression in a quark-gluon plasma using a screened interquark potential and Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal hydrodynamics, then extend to include transverse expansion via (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics with cylindrical symmetry. The survival probability is computed by comparing the formation time of each quarkonium state (J/ψ, χ_c, ψ′, Υ, χ_b, Υ′) against the time when the local energy density drops below the threshold required to dissociate that resonance, for varying speed of sound values (c_s² = 1/3 and 1/5). Initial energy densities are taken from self-screened parton cascade calculations for RHIC (60 GeV/fm³ at τ₀=0.25 fm) and LHC (425 GeV/fm³ at τ₀=0.25 fm).

## Key numbers
- Initial average energy density at RHIC: 60 GeV/fm³ (τ₀ = 0.25 fm)
- Initial average energy density at LHC: 425 GeV/fm³ (τ₀ = 0.25 fm)
- Initial average energy density at SPS: 6.3 GeV/fm³ (τ₀ = 0.5 fm)
- For J/ψ at RHIC with c_s² = 1/3, the survival probability at p_T ≈ 15 GeV is identical for longitudinal-only and transverse-expansion cases
- For J/ψ at LHC, the largest p_T for which formation is definitely possible is enhanced by about 10 GeV when transverse expansion is included
- The kink in S(p_T) from χ_c escape occurs at p_T ≈ 10 GeV for c_s² = 1/3 at RHIC, shifting to ≈ 14 GeV for c_s² = 1/5
- At LHC, the χ_c escape kink appears at p_T ≈ 16 GeV for c_s² = 1/3
- For Υ at LHC, the kink from χ_b/Υ′ escape occurs at p_T ≈ 20 GeV for c_s² = 1/3 and at ≈ 26 GeV for c_s² = 1/5
- Only about 58% of observed J/ψ originate directly; 30% from χ_c decay and 12% from ψ′ decay
- Only about 54% of Υ are directly produced; 32% from χ_b decay and 14% from Υ′ decay

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Chu and Matsui (who explored the extremes c_s² = 1/3 and c_s² = 0) by systematically varying the speed of sound and including transverse expansion. It builds on the quarkonium suppression formalism of Matsui and Satz and the screening-mass calculations of Karsch and Satz. The results enable experimental determination of the quark-matter equation of state at RHIC via J/ψ suppression and at LHC via Υ suppression, with the two probes mapping overlapping but different regions of the phase diagram. The authors note that absorption by nucleons and comovers, as well as parton shadowing, must be accounted for before QGP suppression can be isolated, as has been achieved at SPS energies.
