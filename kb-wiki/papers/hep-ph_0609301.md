# The thermal nature of high p_T photons in high energy nuclear collisions

> We discuss the recent status of some penetrating electromagnetic probes of relativistic nuclear collisions, and the information contained in their measurement. We concentrate in turn on sources that produce high p_T photons: those of purely thermal origin, those producing direct photons, those related to jet fragmentation, and those associated with the interaction of jets with the colored plasma. Whenever possible, we compare with RHIC data and in some cases we make predictions for the LHC.

## Key claim
High pT photons from jet-plasma interactions in relativistic nuclear collisions can be detected and distinguished from other sources, but the precise determination of the initial temperature through these measurements may be ambiguous due to normalization uncertainties.

## Method
The author calculates photon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions using perturbative QCD with next-to-leading order corrections, incorporating parton distribution functions (CTEQ5), fragmentation functions from e+e- collisions, and the AMY formalism for jet energy loss via coupled Fokker-Planck equations. The space-time evolution is modeled with a three-dimensional hydrodynamic model, and the strong coupling constant αs is fixed by fitting the nuclear modification factor RAA for neutral pions. Photon sources are decomposed into prompt (Compton, annihilation, fragmentation), jet-thermal conversion, jet-bremsstrahlung, and thermal radiation from quark-gluon plasma and hadronic gas.

## Key numbers
- Kjet = 1.7 for RHIC and 1.6 for LHC, used as higher-order correction factors for jet production
- αs = 0.34, fixed by fitting the π0 nuclear modification factor in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon
- Kγ(10 GeV) = 1.5 for both RHIC and LHC, Kbrem(10 GeV) = 1.8 at RHIC and 1.4 at LHC, for prompt photon production
- Photon v2 becomes negative at moderate pT (around 2-6 GeV/c) for centralities 20-40% and 40-60% in Au+Au at RHIC, due to jet-plasma interactions overcoming the positive v2 from fragmentation

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of electromagnetic probes in heavy ion collisions, building on prior calculations of thermal photon emission by Feinberg (1976) and Shuryak (1978), and on the AMY formalism for jet energy loss developed by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe. It provides predictions for RHIC and LHC photon spectra and azimuthal asymmetries that complement the hadronic suppression measurements from PHENIX, and enables experimental isolation of jet-plasma interaction signals through the characteristic negative v2 signature.
