# Influence of hadronic resonances on the chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions

> Detailed knowledge of the hadronic spectrum is still an open question, which has phenomenological consequences on the study of heavy-ion collisions. A previous lattice QCD study concluded that additional strange resonances are missing in the currently tabulated lists provided by the Particle Data Group (PDG). That study identified the list labeled PDG2016+ as the ideal spectrum to be used as an input in thermal-model-based analyses. In this work, we determine the effect of additional resonances on the freeze-out parameters of systems created in heavy-ion collisions. These parameters are obtained from thermal fits of particle yields and net-particle fluctuations. For a complete picture, we compare several hadron lists including both experimentally discovered and theoretically predicted states. We find that the inclusion of additional resonances mildly influences the extracted parameters -- with a general trend of progressively lowering the temperature -- but is not sufficient to close the gap in temperature between light and strange hadrons previously observed in the literature.

## Key claim

The inclusion of additional hadronic resonances (beyond the standard PDG lists) mildly lowers the extracted chemical freeze-out temperature in thermal model fits to heavy-ion collision data, but this effect is not sufficient to close the observed gap between the freeze-out temperatures of light and strange hadrons, even when using the most complete resonance lists.

## Method

The authors performed thermal fits within the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model to particle yields and yield ratios from ALICE Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and STAR Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV, using four different hadron resonance lists: PDG2005, PDG2016 (only **-**** states), PDG2016+ (*-**** states), and a Quark Model list (PDG2016+ plus theoretically predicted states). For states with incomplete or missing decay information, they assigned branching ratios using phenomenological methods including radiative decays and linear extrapolation from known decays of particles with the same quantum numbers. They also analyzed net-proton, net-charge, and net-kaon fluctuations from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan using the PDG2016+ list, following isentropic trajectories from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- For ALICE Pb-Pb at 5.02 TeV, single freeze-out temperature from yields: PDG2005 gives 158.3 ± 1.8 MeV; PDG2016+ gives 150.4 ± 1.5 MeV; QM gives 147.9 ± 1.4 MeV
- For STAR Au-Au at 200 GeV, single freeze-out temperature from yields: PDG2005 gives 160.2 ± 2.1 MeV; PDG2016+ gives 156.4 ± 2.2 MeV; QM gives 151.5 ± 1.8 MeV
- In the double freeze-out scenario at the LHC with PDG2016+, light hadron temperature from yields is 142.5 ± 1.7 MeV and strange hadron temperature is 165.0 ± 2.4 MeV
- In the double freeze-out scenario at RHIC with PDG2016+, light hadron temperature from yields is 152.5 ± 4.9 MeV and strange hadron temperature is 158.4 ± 3.1 MeV
- The baryon chemical potential at RHIC remains relatively stable across lists, ranging from 20.3 ± 6.6 MeV (PDG2016+) to 23.2 ± 8.1 MeV (PDG2005) for yield fits

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies by Bazavov (2014) and Alba (2017) that identified missing strange resonances in PDG lists, and directly tests the suggestion from Bazavov (2014) that additional states might resolve the tension between proton and multi-strange hadron production at the LHC. The results contradict the hypothesis that missing resonances alone can close the freeze-out temperature gap, instead supporting the flavor hierarchy picture proposed by Bellwied (2013) where strange hadrons freeze-out at higher temperatures than light hadrons. The analysis enables future dynamical studies of the proton-to-pion puzzle, as suggested by Steinheimer (2012) and Noronha-Hostler (2014), by providing the most complete resonance list (PDG2016+) for use in hydrodynamic and transport models.
