# Effect of finite nuclear size on the electric quadrupole hyperfine operator

> We present an expression for the operator of the electric quadrupole hyperfine interaction which takes into account finite nuclear size. We compare the results obtained with the use of this operator with those obtained in the standard approach which ignores finite nuclear size. We found that the effect of changing operators on the hyperfine constant $B$ is small in hydrogen-like systems. There is a very significant enhancement of the effect in many-electron atoms caused by the contribution of the large $s_{1/2}-d_{3/2},d_{5/2}$ and $p_{1/2}-p_{3/2}$ off diagonal matrix elements to the core polarisation, correlation and configuration interaction corrections. Similar enhancement takes place for transition amplitudes induced by the electric quadrupole hyperfine interaction.

## Key claim

The finite nuclear size correction to the electric quadrupole hyperfine operator produces a small effect on the hyperfine constant \(B\) in hydrogen-like systems (less than \(10^{-3}\) for superheavy nuclei with \(Z=120\)), but is significantly enhanced in many-electron atoms due to large off-diagonal matrix elements involving \(s_{1/2}\) and \(p_{1/2}\) states, reaching approximately 1% for \(Z>100\).

## Method

The authors derived an analytical expression for the electric quadrupole operator that replaces the singular \(1/r^3\) form inside the nuclear radius \(R\) with a regularized \(r^2/R^5\) form, based on the vanishing of the quadrupole electric field at the nuclear center. They performed relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) calculations with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock method (equivalent to the random-phase approximation, RPA) to compute matrix elements for hydrogen-like systems and many-electron atoms, using a Fermi distribution for nuclear charge with radius \(R = 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm.

## Key numbers

- For hydrogen-like systems with \(Z=120\), the difference between the standard and corrected operators for diagonal matrix elements is approximately \(0.0128\%\) for \(d_{3/2}-d_{3/2}\) and \(0.0879\%\) for \(p_{3/2}-p_{3/2}\).
- For off-diagonal matrix elements in hydrogen-like systems at \(Z=83\) (Bi), the effect reaches \(0.8075\%\) for \(s_{1/2}-d_{3/2}\) and \(1.0685\%\) for \(s_{1/2}-d_{5/2}\).
- For the many-electron atom E120 II (\(Z=120\)), the effect on diagonal matrix elements ranges from \(-1.373\%\) for the \(7d_{3/2}\) state to \(1.664\%\) for the \(7d_{5/2}\) state.
- For Fr I (\(Z=87\)), the effect is \(0.059\%\) for the \(7p_{3/2}\) state and \(0.216\%\) for the \(6d_{5/2}\) state.

## Context

This work extends the study of finite nuclear size effects from the well-known Bohr-Weisskopf effect for magnetic dipole hyperfine constants (see Bohr and Weisskopf (1950) and subsequent work by Orozco, Roberts, and others) to the electric quadrupole hyperfine constant \(B\), which had not been previously examined. The corrected operator enables more accurate extraction of nuclear electric quadrupole moments \(Q\) from hyperfine structure measurements in heavy and superheavy atoms, particularly for deformed nuclei near the predicted island of stability (see Oganessian (2012)), and improves calculations of transition amplitudes induced by hyperfine interaction for atomic clock transitions (see Dzuba and Flambaum (2022)).
