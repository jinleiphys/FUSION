# Dilepton Production as a Probe of Pion Condensation in Hot and Dense QCD Matter

> We investigate dilepton production from an isospin-asymmetric hot and dense medium in order to explore the role of isospin imbalance in electromagnetic spectral properties. We focus in particular on modifications of the dilepton production rate associated with the onset of pion condensation, which can occur in the presence of a finite isospin chemical potential. We employ the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with isoscalar--vector interaction. We examine the phase structure in the $T-μ_I$ plane and estimate the vector current correlator--resummed dilepton rate for an effective quark chemical potential. We find that the interplay between isospin asymmetry, pion condensation, and vector interactions leads to nontrivial modifications of the dilepton yield. In particular, we observe two key features of the pion condensed phase: an enhancement at lower invariant mass and a prominent plateau-like structure which also help clearly identify the pion condensed phase from a chirally broken/restored phase. These results highlight the potential sensitivity of dilepton observables to pion-condensed phase of QCD matter, with possible implications for future low-energy heavy-ion collision experiments as well as isospin-rich environments such as neutron star matter.

## Key claim

The authors claim that dilepton production rates in hot, dense QCD matter exhibit two distinctive signatures of pion condensation, an enhancement at lower invariant mass and a plateau-like structure, that could distinguish the pion-condensed phase from both chirally broken and chirally restored phases, but only within the specific framework of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with isoscalar-vector interactions and under the condition that the isospin chemical potential exceeds the in-medium pion mass.

## Method

The authors employed the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar, pseudoscalar, and isoscalar-vector four-fermion interactions at finite temperature, baryon chemical potential, and isospin chemical potential. They solved the mean-field gap equations for the chiral condensate, charged pion condensate, and vector condensate, then computed the vector current correlator with random phase approximation (RPA) resummation to account for vector interaction effects. The dilepton production rate was obtained from the resummed vector spectral function using the finite-temperature formula for an isospin-asymmetric medium, summing over u and d quark flavors weighted by their electric charges.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum constituent quark mass: M0 ≈ 303.5 MeV, obtained from model parameters m = 4.76 MeV, Λ = 659 MeV, and GS = 4.78 GeV^{-2}, fitted to lattice QCD pion mass mπ = 131.7 MeV, fπ = 92.4 MeV, and chiral condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩^{1/3} = -250 MeV.
* Pion condensation threshold at T = 0: μI = mπ = 131.7 MeV (Silver-Blaze property).
* Isospin chemical potential values compared: μI = 0 (uncondensed phase) versus μI = 0.25 GeV (pion-condensed phase).
* Baryon chemical potential values studied: μB = 0, 0.5 GeV, and 0.7 GeV.
* Vector coupling strengths studied: GV = 0, 0.5 GS, and GS.
* Temperature for dilepton rate calculations: T = 0.1 GeV.
* Dilepton three-momentum values studied: q = 0, 0.2 GeV, and 0.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies of pion condensation at finite isospin chemical potential by Turko (1993), Abuki (2008), and Andersen (2007), and connects to lattice QCD results at finite isospin density by Brandt (2017, 2018) which are free from the sign problem. The inclusion of isoscalar-vector interactions follows the approach of Fukushima (2008) and Kojo (2014) for dense QCD matter, while the RPA-resummed vector correlator method builds on earlier work by Davidson (1995) and Islam (2014). The results suggest that dilepton observables could serve as signatures for pion condensation in future low-energy heavy-ion collision experiments at FAIR, J-PARC, and NICA, as well as in isospin-rich environments such as neutron star matter.
