# Further evidence for shape coexistence in $^{79}$Zn$^{m}$ near doubly-magic $^{78}$Ni

> Isomers close to doubly-magic $^{78}_{28}$Ni$_{50}$ provide essential information on the shell evolution and shape coexistence near the ${Z=28}$ and ${N=50}$ double shell closure. We report the excitation energy measurement of the $1/2^{+}$ isomer in $^{79}_{30}$Zn$_{49}$ through independent high-precision mass measurements with the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap and with the ISOLTRAP Multi-Reflection Time-of-Flight Mass Spectrometer. We unambiguously place the $1/2^{+}$ isomer at 942(10) keV, slightly below the $5/2^+$ state at 983(3) keV. With the use of state-of-the-art shell-model diagonalizations, complemented with Discrete Non Orthogonal shell-model calculations which are used here the first time to interpret shape coexistence, we find low-lying deformed intruder states, similar to other ${N=49}$ isotones. The $1/2^{+}$ isomer is interpreted as the band-head of a low-lying deformed structure akin to a predicted low-lying deformed band in $^{80}$Zn, and points to shape coexistence in $^{79,80}$Zn similar to the one observed in $^{78}$Ni. The results make a strong case for confirming the claim of shape coexistence in this key region of the nuclear chart.

## Key claim

The 1/2+ isomer in 79Zn is placed at 942(10) keV, slightly below the 5/2+ state at 983(3) keV, confirming shape coexistence near doubly-magic 78Ni through high-precision mass measurements and new Discrete Non-Orthogonal shell-model calculations used for the first time to interpret shape coexistence.

## Method

Independent high-precision mass measurements were performed using the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap at IGISOL (Jyväskylä) via the Time-of-Flight Ion Cyclotron Resonance method and the ISOLTRAP Multi-Reflection Time-of-Flight Mass Spectrometer at ISOLDE/CERN. The excitation energy was extracted from frequency ratios (Penning trap) and time-of-flight differences (MR-ToF MS) between the ground state and isomeric state of 79Zn. Large-scale shell-model diagonalizations with the PFSDG-U interaction in the full proton pf and neutron sdg valence space, complemented by Discrete Non-Orthogonal shell-model calculations, were used to interpret the deformed nature of the states.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the 1/2+ isomer in 79Zn: 942(10) keV (weighted mean of two independent measurements)
- JYFLTRAP measurement: 942(12) keV
- ISOLTRAP MR-ToF MS measurement: 943(14) keV
- 5/2+ state in 79Zn located at 983(3) keV
- Previous NUBASE 2020 value based on transfer reactions: 1100(150) keV
- Mass resolving power of MR-ToF MS: R = 300,000
- Ground state time-of-flight t0 = 4,277,871(2) ns; time-of-flight difference Δt = 274(4) ns
- Theoretical excitation energies from shell model: 1/2+ at 0.83 MeV, 5/2+ at 0.94 MeV
- Correlation energy recovery for excited states: 6.5-6.9 MeV relative to ground state
- Deformation parameters from DNO-SM: β ≈ 0.15 (ground state), β ≈ 0.22 (isomeric state)

## Context

This work extends studies of intruder states in N=49 isotones from transfer-reaction experiments (Ahn 2019 on 81Ge, Montresque 1978 on 83Se, Detorie 1978 on 85Kr, Burks 1986 on 87Sr) and provides the precise excitation energy missing from earlier spectroscopy of 79Zn (Orlandi 2015). The results confirm the shape coexistence scenario predicted for 78Ni (Nowacki 2016) and show striking similarities between the deformed structures in 79,80Zn and the 0+2 intruder state in 78Ni. The new DNO-SM method (Dao 2022) enables direct extraction of deformation amplitudes from shell-model wave functions, providing a tool for future studies of shape coexistence in this region.
