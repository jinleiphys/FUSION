# Vector meson angular distributions in proton-proton collisions

> The resonance model is used to analyze the omega- and phi-meson angular distributions in proton-proton collisions at sqrt{s} = 2.83 and 2.98 GeV. The assumption of dominant contributions from N^*(1720)3/2+ and N^*(1900)3/2+ resonances which both have, according to the pi N scattering multichannel partial-wave analysis and/or quark models predictions, dominant p_{1/2} N-omega decay modes yields the right pattern of the omega angular distribution at sqrt{s} = 2.83 GeV. The angular distribution at sqrt{s} = 2.98 GeV can be reproduced assuming the dominance of N^*(2000)5/2+ and N^*(1900)3/2+. The experimental phi-meson angular distributions do not shown any asymmetry which requires the existence of a massive negative-parity spin-half resonance. This resonance could be identified with the N^*(2090)1/2-.

## Key claim
The resonance model can reproduce the measured ω-meson angular distributions in proton-proton collisions at two different energies by assuming dominance of specific nucleon resonances with known decay modes, but the interpretation is not unique and several competing mechanisms can explain the data; the flat φ-meson distribution requires the existence of a massive negative-parity spin-half resonance, possibly the N*(2090)1/2-.

## Method
The authors used the resonance model, where the reaction proceeds via pp → pN* followed by N* → pω (or pφ). They constructed angular distributions from the polarization matrix of the produced resonance and the partial-wave amplitudes of its decay, using amplitudes extracted from πN multichannel partial-wave analyses (Manley and Saleski, 1992) and quark model predictions (Koniuk, Capstick and Roberts, Stassart and Stancu) and the extended Vector Meson Dominance model. The calculated distributions were compared to experimental data from the COSY-TOF collaboration at √s = 2.83 GeV and the DISTO collaboration at √s = 2.98 GeV.

## Key numbers
- ω-meson angular distribution measured at excess energy ε = 173 MeV (√s = 2.83 GeV) by COSY-TOF; the data show a peak towards beam directions with a ratio between maximal and minimal differential cross section close to a factor of 4.
- ω-meson angular distribution measured at excess energy ε = 320 MeV (√s = 2.98 GeV) by DISTO; the data require a plateau at cosθ*_ω ~ 0.
- φ-meson angular distribution at √s = 2.98 GeV (85 MeV above φ threshold) found consistent with a flat (isotropic) distribution by DISTO.
- N*(1900) total width Γtot = 500 ± 80 MeV (PDG).
- N*(1720) total width Γtot = 150 ± 50 MeV (PDG).
- Maximal φ momentum in c.m. frame p_max_φ = 0.33 GeV, giving maximal φ orbital momentum η_φ = p_max_φ/m_π = 2.4.
- Estimated cross section σ(pp → pN*(1900)) ~ 0.3 mb at √s ~ 2.8 GeV, below the s-wave unitarity limit of 4π/k² ~ 4 mb.
- At ε = 173 MeV, the N*(1720) is produced with velocity v ~ 0.26; the N*(1900) decay produces ω with velocity v ~ 0.82.
- At ε = 320 MeV, the N*(1720) is produced with velocity v ~ 0.35; the N*(1900) decay produces ω with velocity v ~ 0.56.

## Context
This work extends the resonance model previously applied to total cross sections of ρ, ω, and φ production (Faessler et al., J. Phys. G, Yad. Fiz., etc.) to angular distributions, using the same framework that treats matrix elements of higher-mass resonance production as phenomenological constants. It provides input for transport models of dilepton emission in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., DLS and HADES experiments) where the angular dependence of vector mesons affects detector efficiency. The analysis shows that the COSY-TOF and DISTO data can be explained by different combinations of resonances, but a complete partial-wave analysis requires experiments with polarized beams; the φ-meson result specifically motivates further study of the N*(2090)1/2- resonance.
