# Coupled-channel analysis of $KΣ$ production on the nucleon up to 2.0 GeV

> A coupled-channel effective Lagrangian model respecting unitary and gauge invariance is applied to the combined analysis of the $(π,γ) N \to KΣ$ reactions for the center of mass energies up to 2 GeV. The recent photoproduction data obtained by the CLAS, CBELSA, LEPS, and GRAAL groups are included into our calculations with the aim to extract the resonance couplings to the $KΣ$ state. Both resonances and background contributions are found to be important to reproduce a correct shape of the angular distributions and polarization observables. Our description to the data is of good quality. The extracted properties of isospin $I = 3/2$ resonances are discussed in detail while the $I = 1/2$ resonances are largely determined by the non-strangeness channels.

# Coupled-channel analysis of $K\Sigma$ production on the nucleon up to 2.0 GeV

## Key claim

The authors achieve a good-quality description of $K\Sigma$ photoproduction data from CLAS, CBELSA, LEPS, and GRAAL using a coupled-channel K-matrix model, but the extracted properties of isospin $I=1/2$ resonances remain largely determined by non-strangeness channels rather than the $K\Sigma$ data itself, while the $I=3/2$ resonance parameters are discussed in detail with the caveat that the $S_{31}(1900)$ resonance is found to be irrelevant for $K\Sigma$ production and is excluded from the final fit.

## Method

The Giessen coupled-channel K-matrix model, which respects unitarity and gauge invariance, is applied to simultaneously analyze $\pi N \to K\Sigma$ and $\gamma N \to K\Sigma$ reactions up to center-of-mass energies of 2.0 GeV. The interaction potential is constructed from s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel tree-level Feynman diagrams with effective Lagrangians, using form factors with a common cutoff $\Lambda_N = 0.95$ GeV at the $NK\Sigma$ vertex and $\Lambda_t = 0.77$ GeV at t-channel vertices. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in the K-matrix approximation, including 11 isospin $I=1/2$ resonances and 9 isospin $I=3/2$ resonances with spins up to $J=5/2$, fitted to new photoproduction data from CLAS, CBELSA, LEPS, and GRAAL together with older $\pi N \to K\Sigma$ measurements.

## Key numbers

- $\chi^2 = 1.8$ for the fit to $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma^0$ data
- $\chi^2 = 2.0$ for the fit to $\gamma p \to K^0 \Sigma^+$ data
- $\chi^2 = 4.1$ for $\pi^+ p \to K^+ \Sigma^+$, $\chi^2 = 3.2$ for $\pi^- p \to K^0 \Sigma^0$, and $\chi^2 = 2.8$ for $\pi^- p \to K^+ \Sigma^-$ reactions
- Extracted Breit-Wigner mass of $S_{31}(1620)$: 1598 MeV with width 209 MeV
- Branching ratio Br$(P_{31}(1750) \to K\Sigma) \simeq 0.90\%$
- Electromagnetic helicity amplitude $A_{1/2}$ for $S_{31}(1900)$: approximately $-10 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ (small, leading to exclusion of this resonance)
- Cutoff values: $\Lambda_{NK\Sigma} = \Lambda_N = 0.95$ GeV; $\Lambda_t = 0.77$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the Giessen coupled-channel model previously applied to $\omega N$, $\eta N$, and $K\Lambda$ production (Shklyar 2004, 2005, 2007, 2012) to the $K\Sigma$ channel, incorporating new high-precision photoproduction data from CLAS and CBELSA that were not included in earlier analyses such as the Bonn-Gatchina partial wave analysis (BoCh 2005-2012) or the Juelich coupled-channel model (Doring 2011, 2012). The results enable comparison of resonance parameters across different coupled-channel approaches (Usov and Scholten 2005, Shyam 2010) and partial wave analyses (KSU, Pitt-ANL, GWU), and highlight the need for further clarification of the $K^+ \Sigma^+$ production mechanism, where different models disagree on which partial waves dominate.
