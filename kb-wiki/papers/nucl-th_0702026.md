# Early Time Evolution of High Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> We solve the Yang-Mills equations in the framework of the McLerran-Venugopalan model for small times tau after a collision of two nuclei. An analytic expansion around tau=0 leads to explicit results for the field strength and the energy momentum tensor of the gluon field at early times. We then discuss constraints for the energy density, pressure and flow of the plasma phase that emerges after thermalization of the gluon field.

# Early Time Evolution of High Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The Yang-Mills equations in the McLerran-Venugopalan model can be solved analytically via an expansion in powers of proper time τ around τ=0, yielding explicit expressions for the gluon field strength and energy-momentum tensor at early times. However, the initial energy density ε in this model suffers from an ultraviolet divergence, requiring a cutoff Q₀ above which the classical field description is replaced by perturbative QCD.

## Method
The author solves the Yang-Mills equations [D_μ, F^{μν}] = J^ν for currents created by infinitely thin color charge distributions on the light cones of two colliding nuclei. An expansion of the gauge fields A^μ in powers of τ is performed, with explicit solutions computed up to order τ³. The energy-momentum tensor of the gluon field is then constructed from these solutions, and energy-momentum conservation is used to relate the field tensor to the plasma energy-momentum tensor T^{μν}_{pl} = (e+p)u^μ u^ν - p g^{μν} that would emerge after thermalization.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy density ε ≈ 260 GeV/fm³ for a cutoff Q₀ = 2.5 GeV (estimated in an abelianized approximation with color screening at distance R_c = 1/Q_s).
- The saturation scale Q_s = 1/R_c is independent of the cutoff Q₀, as required physically.
- The numerical constant c ≈ 0.42 appears in the expression for ε and ν^i in the abelianized approximation.
- The expansion in τ is valid for τ << 1/Q_s, while thermalization is expected on a time scale τ₀ ≈ 0.5 to 1.0 fm/c.

## Context
This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework of McLerran and Venugopalan (1993) and subsequent developments by Kovchegov (1996) and others, providing analytic early-time solutions that complement numerical lattice calculations by Lappi (2006). The results constrain the initial conditions for relativistic hydrodynamics of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC, particularly the energy density, pressure, and transverse flow (including elliptic flow for non-central collisions). The approach enables matching between the classical field description at early times and hydrodynamic evolution at later times, with the system of equations closed by an equation of state.
