# Gluon condensate, quark propagation, and dilepton production in the quark-gluon plasma

> A calculation of the thermal quark propagator is presented taking the gluon condensate above the critical temperature into account. The quark dispersion relation and the dilepton production following from this propagator are derived.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-perturbative calculation of the thermal quark propagator by including the gluon condensate above the critical temperature, as an alternative to lattice QCD and perturbative QCD methods. The authors find that the resulting quark dispersion relation is qualitatively similar to the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, but with a different effective mass that is approximately constant at \(m_{eff}=1.15\>T\) over the temperature range \(1.1 T_c < T < 4 T_c\).

## Method

The authors compute the quark self-energy using the imaginary time formalism, expanding the quark propagator for small loop momenta and relating the moments of the longitudinal and transverse gluon propagators to the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensates. These condensates are extracted from lattice results for space- and timelike plaquette expectation values, and the quark dispersion relation is obtained numerically from the poles of the full quark propagator. The dilepton production rate is then derived from the imaginary part of the photon self energy in the one-loop approximation, replacing bare quark propagators with the effective propagators containing the gluon condensate.

## Key numbers

- Zero-temperature gluon condensate: \(\langle G^2 \rangle _0 = (2.5 \pm 1.0)\> T_c^4\), where \(T_c\) is the critical temperature of the phase transition to the quark-gluon plasma.
- Effective quark mass: \(m_{eff}=1.15 \> T\) approximately, for the temperature range \(1.1 T_c < T < 4 T_c\).
- The effective mass is given by \(m_{eff}=[(2\pi \alpha _s/3)\> (\langle {\bf E}^2\rangle _T + \langle {\bf B}^2\rangle _T )]^{1/4}\) at zero momentum.
- The dilepton production rate is computed for zero momentum (\(p=0\)) and shows peaks (Van Hove singularities) and gaps, with the collective quark annihilation contribution (\(q_+\> \bar q_+\)) dominating at large invariant mass \(M\) and approaching the Born term.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature calculation of the quark propagator containing the gluon condensate by Reinders et al. (1985) to finite temperature, providing a non-perturbative alternative to the HTL approximation developed by Braaten and Pisarski (1990). The resulting dispersion relation is qualitatively similar to the HTL case but with a different effective mass (\(m_{eff}=gT/\sqrt{6}\) in HTL), and the dilepton production rate exhibits sharp structures (Van Hove singularities and gaps) that are absent in the HTL rate due to the lack of an imaginary part in the gluon condensate self-energy. The authors suggest that these structures might be observable at RHIC, where the quark phase is expected to dominate over hadronic contributions, in contrast to SPS where the hadronic contribution is one to two orders of magnitude larger.
