# Understanding Dilepton Production in Heavy Ion Collisions by Vector Mesons of Different Varieties

> A simple schematic model anchored on the notion of hadronic freedom inferred from hidden local symmetry in the vector manifestation, the infinite tower of vector mesons in holographic QCD and "stickiness" of $ππ$ interactions inferred from dispersion relations is used to describe the dileptons produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions at PHENIX/RHIC. It is shown that due to the near "blindness" of dileptons to Brown-Rho scaling, those dileptons with invariant mass less than $m_ρ=770$ MeV come mostly from pion-composites that we interpret as "transient $ρ$'s."

# Understanding Dilepton Production in Heavy Ion Collisions by Vector Mesons of Different Varieties

## Key claim

The authors argue that dileptons with invariant mass below the rho meson mass of 770 MeV in PHENIX/RHIC heavy ion collisions come predominantly from pion-composites ("transient rho mesons" or rho*) formed by pi+ pi- collisions, not from Brown-Rho scaling vector mesons, because dileptons are nearly "blind" to BR-scaling rho mesons which contribute at most an order of magnitude less than on-shell rho and rho* contributions.

## Method

The authors construct a schematic model using dispersion theory borrowed from the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential, where the attractive p-wave velocity-velocity interaction between pions produces a "stickiness" that holds pi+ pi- pairs together long enough to emit dileptons with free-space parameters. They assume a spherically symmetric distribution of pions at the flash temperature of approximately 120 MeV, compute the invariant mass of colliding pion pairs as a function of their relative solid angle, and estimate the number of rho* mesons produced at different masses by integrating over solid angle bins.

## Key numbers

- The flash temperature where hadrons recover free-space mass is T_flash approximately 120 MeV
- The on-shell rho mass is m_rho = 770 MeV
- The characteristic mass of the transient rho formed from head-on collisions is m_rho* approximately 720 MeV, given by 2 * (3 * T_flash) approximately 720 MeV
- The mass of rho* formed at 90-degree collision angle is approximately 720 / sqrt(2) approximately 509 MeV
- From one SU(4) batch there are 66 pions, among which 18 reconstructed rho mesons (6 rho^0), and 36 to 49 possible pi+ pi- collisions (mean approximately 42)
- The fraction of solid angle for head-on collisions (180 +/- 45 degrees) is Delta Omega / Omega approximately 0.15, yielding approximately 6 near on-shell rho* mesons
- The product of the angular factor f_1 approximately 2.4 and the propagator scaling factor f_2 approximately 2 gives an increase factor of approximately 4.8 in rho* number from 720 MeV to 500 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous scenario in BHHRS (2007) for dilepton production in heavy ion collisions, which is drastically different from the standard scenario. It uses the concept of hadronic freedom inferred from hidden local symmetry in the vector manifestation (Harada and Yamawaki, 2003) and the infinite tower of vector mesons from holographic QCD (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005; Rho, 2007). The "stickiness" mechanism for pi+ pi- collisions is borrowed from dispersion theory used in the Paris potential (Lacombe et al., 1980; Weise, 1972). The analysis suggests that the standard phase diagram for high-temperature QCD may need major revision, with a previously unsuspected "Hadronic Freedom" phase between the critical temperature and the flash temperature.
