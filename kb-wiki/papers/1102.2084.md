# Evolution of energy density fluctuations in A+A collisions

> Two-particle angular correlation for charged particles emitted in Au+Au collisions at the center-of-mass of 200 MeV measured at RHIC energies revealed novel structures commonly referred to as a near-side ridge. The ridge phenomenon in relativistic A+A collisions is rooted probably in the initial conditions of the thermal evolution of the system. In this study we analyze the evolution of the bumping transverse structure of the energy density distribution caused by fluctuations of the initial density distributions that could lead to the ridge structures. We suppose that at very initial stage of collisions the typical one-event structure of the initial energy density profile can be presented as the set of longitudinal tubes, which are boost-invariant in some space-rapidity region and are rather thin. These tubes have very high energy density comparing to smooth background density distribution. The transverse velocity and energy density profiles at different times of the evolution till the chemical freeze-out (at the temperature T=165 MeV) willbe reached by the system are calculated for sundry initial scenarios.

# Evolution of energy density fluctuations in A+A collisions

## Key claim
The ridge phenomenon in relativistic A+A collisions is probably rooted in initial conditions of the thermal evolution, specifically in transversally bumping tube-like fluctuations of the initial energy density that persist through hydrodynamic expansion and do not wash out by the time of chemical freeze-out.

## Method
The authors used a 3D ideal hydrodynamic code with Bjorken-type boost-invariant initial conditions at τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c, comparing a smooth Gaussian energy density profile against configurations with one, four, or ten thin longitudinal tubes (radius a_i = 1 fm) of high energy density superimposed on the smooth background. They evolved the system until chemical freeze-out at T = 165 MeV and calculated the transverse velocity and energy density profiles at different times for each initial scenario.

## Key numbers
- Smooth background maximum energy density E_b = 90 GeV/fm³ with Gaussian radius R = 5.4 fm for the no-fluctuation configuration
- Tube fluctuation maxima: 270 GeV/fm³ for single-tube configurations (center and shifted), 250 GeV/fm³ for four-tube configuration, and E_i = 4E_b exp(-R_i²/R²) for ten-tube configuration
- Tube radius a_i = 1 fm for all fluctuation configurations
- Transverse velocity fluctuations averaged over azimuthal angle and radius: approximately 30% at thermalization time τ = 1 fm/c between central and shifted single-tube cases, dropping to only 2-3% at τ = 10 fm/c
- Chemical freeze-out temperature T = 165 MeV

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic explanation of the near-side ridge phenomenon observed by STAR (Horner), PHENIX (Adare), and PHOBOS (Wosiek) at RHIC, as well as the unexpected ridge in pp collisions at LHC by CMS. It builds on the initial-state correlation approach of Dumitru and the hydrokinetic model (HKM) developed by Akkelin and Sinyukov, testing whether tube-like initial density fluctuations can survive expansion and produce ridge-like correlations. The results enable further correlation studies within the HKM framework that can describe all stages of system evolution including particle momentum formation at decoupling.
