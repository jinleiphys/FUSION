# Coulomb energy of axially deformed nucleus

## Key claim
The authors extend their previously proposed analytical formula for the Coulomb energy of spherical nuclei with Woods-Saxon charge distribution to axially deformed nuclei with β₂ deformation, achieving relative deviations generally smaller than 0.05% for intermediate and heavy nuclei at normal deformations (|β₂| ≤ 0.5, a ≤ 0.7 fm). The formula is not applicable for systems with |β₂| > 0.5, such as fissioning systems, where two-dimensional numerical integration must be used instead.

## Method
The Coulomb potential is obtained by solving the Poisson equation in cylindrical coordinates using a five-point finite difference approximation (the hwscyl Fortran subroutine from FISHPACK) on a grid with step size 0.1 fm within a region x = 0–40 fm and z = −40–40 fm. The Coulomb energy is then calculated via two-dimensional integration over the Coulomb potential, using a Woods-Saxon charge distribution with central density ρ₀ held constant (by varying the half-density radius R₀ to conserve charge number) and including β₂ deformation in the nuclear surface expression. The numerically calculated Coulomb energies for nuclei from A = 16 to 300 are fitted to obtain an analytical formula expressed as a product of a spherical correction factor F(ω) and a deformation factor G(ω, β₂).

## Key numbers
- For ²³⁸U with a = 0.55 fm, the Coulomb energy decreases with increasing β₂ deformation and increasing surface diffuseness a (shown in Fig. 1).
- The coefficients for the spherical correction factor F(ω) are c₃ = 3.005, c₅ = −4.822, c₆ = 2.934, obtained by fitting numerical results for spherical nuclei along the β-stability line.
- The coefficients for the deformation factor G(ω, β₂) are b₁ = 1/(4π), b₂ = 0.188, b₃ = −0.007, b₄ = 0.018, obtained by fitting for nuclei with a ≤ 0.7 fm and |β₂| ≤ 0.5.
- For intermediate and heavy nuclei, ω (defined as πa/(√3 R)) ranges from about 0.1 to 0.25; for these cases, relative deviations of the analytical formula from numerical integration are smaller than 0.05% for a ≤ 0.7 fm and |β₂| ≤ 0.5.
- For ²³⁸U, ω = 0.13 when taking a = 0.55 fm and R = 1.2A¹/³ fm.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analytical formula for spherical nuclei (Wang, Yu, and Liu, 2009) to include β₂ deformation, building on the leptodermous expansion approach of Myers and Swiatecki (1966) and the deformation term from Greiner and Maruhn. The formula enables rapid calculation of Coulomb energies for deformed nuclei in large-scale potential energy surface calculations without time-consuming six-dimensional numerical integration. The code for numerical integration over the Coulomb potential is available from the authors' website.
