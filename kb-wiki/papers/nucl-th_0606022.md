# Toward the energy and the system size dependece of elliptic flow: working on flow fluctuations

> In this talk I concentrate on two topics closely related to the understanding of the energy and the system size dependence of the elliptic flow: determination of the elliptic flow ($v_2$) ``free'' from the effects of non-flow and flow fluctuations, and the role of fluctuations in the initial eccentricity of the overlap region. I introduce a new approach for the analysis of the distribution in flow vector, $dP/dq_n$, namely, I propose to use the Bessel Transform of this ditribution. I show that the Bessel Transform method is similar to the Lee-Yang Zeroes method, and is very transparent in its meaning and applications.

# Toward the energy and the system size dependence of elliptic flow: working on flow fluctuations

## Key claim

The Bessel Transform method for elliptic flow analysis is shown to be very similar to the Lee-Yang Zeroes method and fitting of the q-distribution, but contrary to earlier hopes, its sensitivity to flow fluctuations is comparable to that of four-particle cumulant results, not significantly lower.

## Method

The author introduces the Bessel Transform of the distribution in flow vector magnitude, dP/dq, as a new analysis technique for extracting elliptic flow (v2) from heavy-ion collision data. The method is derived analytically by taking the Fourier transform of the flow vector distribution, showing that the flow contribution factorizes into a Bessel function J0(kvM). The approach is tested on simulated events with and without non-flow correlations, comparing results from two-particle correlations (v2{2}), four-particle cumulants (v2{4}), the Bessel Transform (v2{BT}), and q-distribution fitting (v2{q-dist}). Additionally, Monte-Carlo Glauber calculations are performed to compute participant eccentricity and its fluctuations for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions.

## Key numbers

- For simulated events with 100% v=0.06 and no non-flow: v2{BT} = 6.018 ± 0.004%, v2{q-dist} = 5.999 ± 0.002%, v2{4} = 6.006 ± 0.006%
- For simulated events with 100% v=0.04 and no non-flow: v2{BT} = 4.013 ± 0.008%, v2{q-dist} = 3.997 ± 0.003%, v2{4} = 3.977 ± 0.002%
- For a 50/50 mixture of events with v=0.04 and v=0.06 (no non-flow): v2{BT} = 4.908 ± 0.004%, v2{q-dist} = 4.920 ± 0.004%, v2{4} = 4.895 ± 0.007%, while v2{2} = 5.100 ± 0.004%
- For 100% v=0.06 with non-flow included: v2{BT} = 6.028 ± 0.004%, v2{4} = 6.005 ± 0.007%, v2{2} = 6.495 ± 0.003%
- The first zero of the J0 Bessel function used in the method is j01 ≈ 2.045

## Context

This work extends the Lee-Yang Zeroes method introduced by Bhalerao et al. (2003) for flow analysis, showing that the Bessel Transform approach is mathematically equivalent. The author addresses the problem of disentangling non-flow correlations from flow fluctuations, which was identified as a key difficulty in testing the scaling relation v2/ε ∝ (1/S)(dNch/dy) proposed by Voloshin and Poskanzer (2000). The participant eccentricity calculations extend the Monte-Carlo Glauber work of Snellings and Miller (2004) and the participant plane approach of Manly et al. (2005), showing that using ε{2} and ε{4} from fluctuating initial conditions can improve the scaling of elliptic flow across different collision systems and centralities.
