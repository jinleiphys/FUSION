# Shape transition in the even-even Cerium isotopes

> The deformation energy of the even-even nuclei of the Cerium isotopic chain is investigated by means of the Macroscopic-Microscopic method with a semi-classical shell correction. We consider axially symmetric shapes. Binding energy and two neutron separation energy are also evaluated. For the sake of clarity several important details of the calculations are also given. It turns out that all these nuclei have prolate equilibrium shape. The regions of maximum deformation are obtained around N=64 and N=102. There is no critical-point of quantum phase transition in this isotopic chain.

## Key claim

All even-even Cerium isotopes from N=58 to N=124 have prolate equilibrium shapes, with maximum deformation around N=64 and N=102, and there is no critical-point quantum phase transition in this isotopic chain, contrary to what might be expected from the X(5) critical-point symmetry between U(5) and SU(3) limits of the Interacting Boson Model.

## Method

The authors used the Macroscopic-Microscopic method with a Woods-Saxon deformed mean potential and a semi-classical shell correction (instead of the standard Strutinsky smoothing procedure) to avoid spurious dependence on smoothing parameters. They calculated the potential energy surface for axially symmetric prolate and oblate shapes for 34 even-even Cerium isotopes from the neutron drip lines to the proton drip lines, using a specific set of mean-field parameters for each nucleus rather than a single parameter set as in their prior work (Mohammed-Azizi et al., 2014).

## Key numbers

- Maximum prolate deformation of β ≈ 0.30 occurs around N=64 (e.g., ¹²²Ce with β_pro = 0.31) and N=102 (e.g., ¹⁶⁰Ce with β_pro = 0.30)
- Deformation energy at N=64 is 6.68 MeV for ¹²²Ce and at N=102 is 9.27 MeV for ¹⁶⁰Ce
- Spherical shapes occur at N=82 (¹⁴⁰Ce) and N=128 (not shown in table) with deformation energy 0.00 MeV
- The difference between highest and lowest shell correction values for ¹⁶⁰Ce in the interval β ∈ [0.0, 0.7] is about 11.0 MeV for neutrons and 10.5 MeV for protons
- The difference between highest and lowest pairing correction values for ¹⁶⁰Ce in the same interval is about 5.7 MeV for neutrons and 3.5 MeV for protons
- The deviation between the present model and FRDM model for binding energy variations from one isotope to the next is about ±0.35 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Mohammed-Azizi et al., 2014) on xenon, barium, and cerium isotopes from 13 nuclei to all 34 even-even Cerium isotopes up to the drip lines, and improves them by using nucleus-specific mean-field parameters instead of a single parameter set. The results are compared with experimental deformations from Raman et al. (2001) and with theoretical calculations by Kern et al. (1998), Hilaire and Girod (2002), Gotz et al. (1972), and Nix et al. (1977) using the Macroscopic-Microscopic method, as well as Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with Gogny force. The study enables systematic comparison of deformation systematics across the Cerium chain and tests the validity of the X(5) critical-point symmetry prediction for shape transitions in this region.
