# Generalized Seniority Description of Cold Fermi Gases

> We suggest that the extension of the Racah seniority description of strongly interacting fermions in the nuclear shell model is directly generalizable to describe pairing of atoms in cold Fermi systems. We illustrate this by the fermionic pairing in the much studied cold two-component gas of Li atoms. Our pairing interaction is two orders of magnitude stronger than that used in the usual BCS approach. We also explain why the Racah scheme is less applicable to nuclei, and discuss the similarities of the strongly-coupled matter in cold fermion systems and the new form of matter found in RHIC close to T_c.

# Generalized Seniority Description of Cold Fermi Gases

## Key claim

The Racah seniority scheme from nuclear shell theory can be directly generalized to describe pairing in cold Fermi gases, but only when the harmonic oscillator representation is retained (rather than the Thomas-Fermi plane-wave approximation), which yields a pairing interaction roughly two orders of magnitude stronger than standard BCS treatment.

## Method

The authors applied the Racah seniority formalism, originally developed for degenerate nuclear shell configurations with a delta-function interaction, to a trapped gas of fermionic lithium-6 atoms in a harmonic oscillator potential. They performed a numerical diagonalization of the pairing interaction in the N=15 major shell using harmonic oscillator wave functions, without assuming equal matrix elements, and compared the resulting pairing gap to the Thomas-Fermi approximation used in conventional BCS calculations.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap ratio for the N=15 shell is given by Δ/ħω₀ = 51 |a|/a_osc in the rough estimate, and 58.6 |a|/a_osc from the numerical diagonalization (where a_osc = √(ħ/mω₀)).
- The Thomas-Fermi approximation gives Δ/ħω₀ = 1.18 |a|/a_osc for N_f=15, compared to the shell-model result of 1.14 |a|/a_osc for N_f=12.
- The condition for molecular binding yields a_osc/|a| = 1.75, corresponding to 1/(k_f |a|) = 0.32 for 1632 atoms.
- The pairing gap can be rewritten as Δ/ħω₀ = 11 k_f |a|, where the factor of about 60 enhancement over BCS comes from the degeneracy in the N=15 shell.

## Context

This work extends the Racah seniority description (Racah, 1940s) from nuclear shell theory to cold atomic Fermi gases, directly contradicting the weak-coupling BCS approach that uses the Thomas-Fermi approximation as in Heiselberg (2003). The authors connect their strong-coupling pairing scenario to the experimental observations of large condensate fractions on the BCS side by Regal et al. (2003) and Zwierlein et al. (2004) with potassium-40 and lithium-6 atoms, and draw analogies to the strongly-coupled matter found at RHIC near T_c as discussed by Shuryak and by Brown, Lee, Rho and Shuryak (2004). The work enables a unified description that can smoothly interpolate between weak-coupling BCS and strong-coupling molecular Bose condensation, following the variational ansatz of Leggett (1980).
