# Elliptic flow of multi-strange particles: fragmentation, recombination and hydrodynamics

> We study the elliptic flow $v_2$ of multi-strange hadrons such as the $ϕ$, $Ξ$ and $Ω$ as a function of transverse momentum in the recombination and fragmentation model and compare to a standard hydrodynamic calculation. We find that the measurement of $v_2$ for the $ϕ$ and $Ω$ will allow for the unambiguous distinction between parton recombination and statistical hadro-chemistry to be the dominant process in hadronization at intermediate transverse momenta.

## Key claim

The measurement of elliptic flow $v_2$ for the $\phi$ and $\Omega$ hadrons at intermediate transverse momenta (2-5 GeV/$c$) will allow for an unambiguous distinction between parton recombination and statistical hadro-chemistry (hydrodynamic) as the dominant hadronization mechanism, provided that the strange quark $v_2$ is nearly identical to that of light quarks.

## Method

The authors compute $v_2$ for multi-strange hadrons ($\phi$, $\Xi$, $\Omega$) using a recombination plus fragmentation model, where hadronization occurs on a hypersurface at temperature $T=175$ MeV and proper time $\tau=5$ fm/$c$ with radial flow velocity $v_T=0.55c$. The parton momentum distribution at hadronization is taken as thermal with an elliptic modulation parameterized by $F(p_T)=\alpha/[1+(p_T/p_0)^2]$ where $p_0=1.1$ GeV/$c$ is fit to pion $v_2$ data from PHENIX. The results are compared to a standard hydrodynamic (blast wave) calculation for the same hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization temperature: $T = 175$ MeV; hadronization hypersurface at $\tau = 5$ fm/$c$; radial flow velocity $v_T = 0.55c$
- Constituent quark masses: $m_{u,d}=260$ MeV, $m_s=460$ MeV
- Parameter $p_0 = 1.1$ GeV/$c$ obtained from fit to PHENIX pion $v_2$ data
- Impact parameter $b = 8.0$ fm for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV
- For $P_T > 6$ GeV/$c$, all hadron species merge into a universal $v_2$ curve from fragmentation
- At $P_T$ below 1.5 GeV/$c$, the mass-dependent splitting of $v_2$ curves is observed in data but poorly reproduced by recombination

## Context

This work extends the recombination plus fragmentation approach developed by Fries, Mueller, Nonaka, and Bass (2003) to multi-strange hadrons, building on earlier suggestions by Voloshin (2003) that parton recombination explains the saturation of $v_2$ at intermediate $P_T$. The paper directly contrasts with hydrodynamic models (Huovinen, Kolb, Heinz, 2001) which predict that $\phi$ and $\Lambda$ have nearly identical $v_2$ due to their similar masses, while recombination predicts $\phi$ behaves like other mesons (e.g., kaons) and $\Omega$ like other baryons (e.g., $\Lambda$). The measurement of $\phi$ and $\Omega$ $v_2$ at RHIC would enable discrimination between these two hadronization mechanisms.
