# Beta-decay Studies in N~Z Nuclei Using No-Core Configuration-Interaction Model

> The no-core configuration-interaction model based on the isospin- and angular-momentum projected density functional formalism is introduced. Two applications of the model are presented: (i) determination of spectra of 0+ states in 62Zn and (ii) determination of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed beta-decay between isobaric-analogue 0+ states in 38Ca and 38K. It is shown that, without readjusting a single parameter of the underlying Skyrme interaction, in all three nuclei, the model reproduces the 0+ spectra surprisingly well.

## Key claim

The no-core configuration-interaction model, without adjusting any parameter of the underlying Skyrme interaction, reproduces the 0+ spectra in 62Zn, 38Ca, and 38K surprisingly well, but the calculated isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed beta-decay remain sensitive to the matching of configuration spaces between parent and daughter nuclei and are not yet stable.

## Method

The authors use a no-core configuration-interaction model based on isospin- and angular-momentum projected density functional theory. They first generate a set of low-lying (multi)particle-(multi)hole Hartree-Fock Slater determinants, then apply double projection to obtain states with good isospin and angular momentum, and finally mix these non-orthogonal states by solving the Hill-Wheeler equation. The method is applied to compute 0+ spectra in 62Zn using six reference configurations, and to compute isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections for the 38Ca to 38K superallowed Fermi transition using three to four reference configurations.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies of 0+ states in 62Zn: the model (SVmix) predicts 2953 keV, 3884 keV, 4263 keV, and 4347 keV for the four lowest excited 0+ states, compared to new experimental values of 3045.5(4) keV, 3862(2) keV, 3936(6) keV, and 4552(9) keV.
- The calculated excitation energy of the 0_2+ state in 38Ca is 2871 keV, compared to the experimental value of 3057(18) keV, a difference of 186 keV.
- The isospin-symmetry-breaking correction delta_C for the 38Ca to 38K 0_1+ to 0_1+ transition is 1.7%, and for the 0_2+ to 0_2+ transition is 1.5%, compared to the static DFT result of 8.9% and the shell-model result of Towner and Hardy (2008) of 0.745(70)%.
- The calculated excitation energy of the 0_2+ state in 38Ar is 3161 keV, compared to the experimental value of 3377.45(12) keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous static multi-reference DFT approach (Satula 2011, 2012) by introducing configuration mixing to create a dynamic no-core configuration-interaction model. It addresses the anomalously large isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections that previously excluded the A=38 case from the canonical pool of superallowed data. The results are compared to shell-model calculations using MSDI3 (Koonin 1977), GXPF1 (Honma 2002), and GXPF1A (Honma 2004) interactions for 62Zn, and to the shell-model plus Woods-Saxon mean-field results of Towner and Hardy (2008) for the isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections. The model enables future calculations of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections in heavier nuclei where no-core shell-model approaches are not feasible.
