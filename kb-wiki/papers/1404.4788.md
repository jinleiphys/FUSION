# A real-time thermal field theoretical analysis of Kubo-type shear viscosity : Numerical understanding with simple examples

> A real-time thermal field theoretical calculation of shear viscosity has been described in the Kubo formalism for bosonic and fermionic medium. The two point function of viscous stress tensor in the lowest order provides one-loop skeleton diagram of boson or fermion field for bosonic or fermionic matter. According to the traditional diagrammatic technique of transport coefficients, the finite thermal width of boson or fermion is introduced in their internal lines during the evaluation of boson-boson or fermion-fermion loop diagram. These thermal widths of $ϕ$ boson and $ψ$ fermion are respectively obtained from the imaginary part of self-energy for $ϕΦ$ and $ψΦ$ loops, where interactions of higher mass $Φ$ boson with $ϕ$ and $ψ$ are governed by the simple $ϕϕΦ$ and ${\ovψ}ψΦ$ interaction Lagrangian densities. A two-loop diagram, having same power of coupling constant as in the one-loop diagram, is deduced and its contribution appears much lower than the one-loop values of shear viscosity. Therefore the one-loop results of Kubo-type shear viscosity may be considered as leading order results for this simple $ϕϕΦ$ and ${\ovψ}ψΦ$ interactions. This approximation is valid for any values of coupling constant and at the temperatures greater than the mass of constituent particles of the medium.

## Key claim

The one-loop Kubo-type shear viscosity result can be considered as the leading order result for bosonic and fermionic media with simple $\phi\phi\Phi$ and $\overline{\psi}\psi\Phi$ interactions, because a two-loop diagram with the same coupling constant power contributes much less than the one-loop value; this approximation holds for any coupling constant value and at temperatures greater than the mass of the constituent particles.

## Method

The shear viscosity was calculated in the Kubo formalism using real-time thermal field theory (RTF) for both bosonic ($\phi$) and fermionic ($\psi$) media. The two-point correlator of the viscous stress tensor was evaluated at one-loop order, with a finite thermal width $\Gamma$ introduced into the internal propagators to obtain a non-divergent result; this width was obtained from the imaginary part of the one-loop self-energy for $\phi\Phi$ or $\psi\Phi$ loops. A two-loop ladder-type diagram with the same coupling constant order was also derived in RTF and numerically compared to the one-loop contribution.

## Key numbers

* For the $\phi\phi$ loop, the numerator factor in the shear viscosity expression at zero momentum and frequency is $N^0 = -4\vk^4/3$.
* For the $\psi\psi$ loop, the numerator factor at zero momentum and frequency is $N^0 = -16\vk^4/3$.
* The thermal width $\Gamma_\phi$ for the $\phi$ boson is obtained from the Landau cut contribution of the $\phi\Phi$ loop self-energy, with the threshold condition $m_\Phi = 3m_\phi$ assumed.
* The thermal width $\Gamma_\psi$ for the $\psi$ fermion is obtained from the $\psi\Phi$ loop self-energy, with the same mass ratio $m_\Phi = 3m_\psi$ assumed.
* The two-loop contribution to shear viscosity is substantially suppressed compared to the one-loop value due to additional thermal distribution functions appearing multiplicatively.

## Context

This work extends the traditional diagrammatic technique for transport coefficients (Hosoya, Horsley, Nicola, Weise, and others) by explicitly deriving the Kubo-type shear viscosity for simple $\phi\phi\Phi$ and $\overline{\psi}\psi\Phi$ interactions in real-time thermal field theory. The one-loop expression matches the relaxation-time approximation results from kinetic theory (Gavin, Prakash, Redlich). The analysis shows that for these specific interactions, the infinite class of ladder diagrams does not need to be resummed, as the two-loop contribution is numerically negligible, enabling a simpler leading-order treatment for temperatures above the constituent particle masses.
