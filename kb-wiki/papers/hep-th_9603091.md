# Flux String in Quantum Billiards with Two Particles

> We examine the quantum motion of two particles interacting through a contact force which are confined in a rectangular domain in two and three dimensions. When there is a difference in the mass scale of two particles, adiabatic separation of the fast and slow variables can be performed. Appearance of the Berry phase and magnetic flux is pointed out. The system is reduced to a one-particle Aharonov-Bohm billiard in two-dimensional case. In three dimension, the problem effectively becomes the motion of a particle in the presence of closed flux string in a box billiard.

## Key claim

For two particles in a rectangular domain with a contact interaction and a mass disparity, adiabatic separation of fast and slow variables leads to an effective Hamiltonian for the heavy particle that contains a nontrivial vector potential (Berry connection). This vector potential produces magnetic flux lines in two dimensions and closed flux strings in three dimensions, but the analysis depends on the assumption that the light particle remains in a single adiabatic eigenstate during the heavy particle's motion.

## Method

The authors consider two particles (masses m and M, with M >> m) confined in a d-dimensional rectangular hard-wall domain and interacting via a delta-function contact force. They perform an adiabatic separation: first solving the sub-Hamiltonian for the light particle with the heavy particle's position R fixed (a billiard with a pointlike scatterer, regularized by renormalization of the coupling constant), then constructing an effective Hamiltonian for the heavy particle using the Mead-Berry connection (the gauge potential A_alpha = i <alpha(R)| grad_R |alpha(R)>). The degeneracy points (diabolical points) of the sub-Hamiltonian are identified from the conditions f_n(R*) = 0 and g_n(R*) = 0, and the gauge curvature (magnetic field) is computed from the vector potential.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic flux line in two dimensions has strength pi, or 1/2 in the unit of 2 pi.
- The numerical example uses a rectangular billiard of size [L x 1/L] with L = 1.1557, light particle mass m = 2 pi, heavy particle mass M = 5 m, and renormalized coupling constant bar v = 10.
- The three-dimensional example uses a box of size [L_x, L_y, 1/(L_x L_y)] with L_x = 1.1557 and L_y = 1.0310, with the same masses m = 2 pi and bar v = 10.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum billiards with pointlike scatterers (AG88, SE90, SH94) by introducing a second moving particle and applying the adiabatic approximation. It builds on the identification of Berry phases in one-particle billiards (CS96) and shows that the resulting gauge potential produces magnetic flux structures analogous to U(1) vortices in field theory (NO73) and cosmic strings in cosmology (HA90). The results enable further study of effective magnetic fields in few-body quantum systems and suggest that flux strings may be observable in mesoscopic experiments.
