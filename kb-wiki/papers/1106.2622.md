# The platinum nuclei: concealed configuration mixing and shape coexistence

> The role of configuration mixing in the Pt region is investigated. For this chain of isotopes, the nature of the ground state changes smoothly, being spherical around mass $A\sim 174$ and $A\sim 192$ and deformed around the mid-shell N=104 region. This has a dramatic effect on the systematics of the energy spectra as compared to the systematics in the Pb and Hg nuclei. Interacting Boson Model with configuration mixing calculations are presented for gyromagnetic factors, $α$-decay hindrance factors, and isotope shifts. The necessity of incorporating intruder configurations to obtain an accurate description of the latter properties becomes evident.

## Key claim

The paper argues that configuration mixing between regular (0p-0h) and intruder (2p-2h) proton configurations in platinum isotopes is "concealed" because energy spectra and B(E2) values below approximately 1.5 MeV can be reproduced almost equally well by calculations using only the smaller [N] boson space, even though the wave functions differ substantially; the mixing only becomes evident through observables such as gyromagnetic factors, alpha-decay hindrance factors, and isotope shifts.

## Method

The authors employed the Interacting Boson Model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) in a [N] ⊕ [N+2] boson space, where N corresponds to the regular proton-hole configuration and N+2 to the intruder 2p-2h proton configuration across the Z=82 shell gap. They diagonalized the extended consistent-Q Hamiltonian (ECQF) for each boson space separately to obtain unperturbed bands, then introduced a mixing interaction to produce the fully mixed spectra, fitting parameters to reproduce experimental excitation energies and B(E2) values for even-even Pt isotopes from A=172 to A=194.

## Key numbers

- The mixing strength parameters were fixed at w₀^(N,N+2) = w₂^(N,N+2) = 50 keV for all Pt isotopes
- The energy needed to create an extra particle-hole pair (Δ^(N+2)) was set to 2800 keV
- For ¹⁷²Pt, the regular Hamiltonian parameters include ε_N = 725.0 keV, κ_N = -39.47 keV; for ¹⁹²Pt, ε_N = 314.9 keV, κ_N = -45.32 keV
- The regular component of the yrast 0⁺₁ state varies from approximately 70% in ¹⁷²Pt to about 30% near mid-shell (¹⁸⁴Pt, N=106) and back to predominantly regular character at ¹⁹²Pt
- The yrast 8⁺ state retains its [N+2] intruder character (above 80%) across the entire region 172 ≤ A ≤ 192, except for ¹⁹⁴Pt which is regular

## Context

This work extends the study of shape coexistence from the well-documented Pb (Z=82) and Hg (Z=80) nuclei to the Pt (Z=78) chain, where the characteristic parabolic energy systematics of intruder bands seen in Pb and Hg are lost due to strong configuration mixing. The authors build on their previous study (Garcia-Ramos et al., 2009) and on earlier IBM calculations by Harder et al. (1997) and King et al. (1998), as well as on the conceptual framework of Cohen, Lawson, and Soper (1966, 1967) regarding the similarity of results from truncated versus large model spaces. The findings enable future experimental searches for observables that can discriminate between the [N] and [N]⊕[N+2] descriptions, particularly through measurements of gyromagnetic factors, alpha-decay hindrance factors, and nuclear charge radii.
