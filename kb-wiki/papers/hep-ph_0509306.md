# Hadrons Above T_c

> Invited talk at the International Conference on "QCD and Hadronic Physics", June 16-20, 2005, Beijing, China. 6 pages, 3 fugures, typeset with ws-ijma.cls

## Key claim

Hadronic modes in the $s\bar{s}$ and $c\bar{c}$ channels survive above the critical temperature $T_c$ in quenched lattice QCD simulations, but the relation of these surviving modes to the chiral pseudo-gap or dynamical confinement ideas is not yet clear, and the sharp peaks for charmonia disappear suddenly between $1.6 T_c$ and $1.7 T_c$.

## Method

The author applied the maximum entropy method (MEM) to reconstruct spectral functions from quenched lattice QCD data on anisotropic lattices. For the $s\bar{s}$ mesons, simulations used a $32^3 \times N_\tau$ anisotropic lattice with $\beta=7.0$, renormalized anisotropy $\xi = a_\sigma/a_\tau = 4.0$, and $a_\tau = 9.75 \times 10^{-3}$ fm, with $N_\tau$ values corresponding to $T/T_c$ from 0.78 to 2.33. For charmonia, quark masses were tuned to reproduce the experimental $J/\psi$ and $\eta_c$ masses at $T=0$, and spectral functions were extracted at multiple temperatures.

## Key numbers

- $s\bar{s}$ mesons at $T/T_c = 1.38$ show degenerate peaks around 2.5 GeV (approximately 2.4 times the $\phi$-meson mass at $T=0$) in scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector, and axial-vector channels
- $J/\psi$ and $\eta_c$ sharp peaks survive up to $T/T_c \sim 1.6$, with peak positions almost the same as their $T=0$ values
- The sharp charmonium peaks disappear suddenly between $1.6 T_c$ and $1.7 T_c$
- Quenched QCD critical temperature $T_c^{\rm quench} \sim 270$ MeV versus full QCD ($N_f=2$) critical temperature $T_c^{\rm full} \sim 170$ MeV
- The ratio of plasma constituents between full QCD and quenched QCD at their respective critical temperatures is $n_{\rm q+g}(T_c^{\rm full})/n_{\rm g}(T_c^{\rm quench}) \simeq 0.62$

## Context

This work extends the 20-year-old ideas of hadronic modes above $T_c$ from Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1985) on chiral pseudo-gap precursors and from DeTar (1985) on dynamical confinement of the plasma. It reports on recent quenched lattice QCD results using MEM that show resonance structures above $T_c$ in $s\bar{s}$ and $c\bar{c}$ channels, contradicting earlier expectations from screened Coulomb potential arguments by Hashimoto et al. (1986) and Matsui and Satz (1986) that charmonia would dissolve soon after deconfinement. The results enable future studies of spatial wave functions, spectral functions with finite spatial momentum, and the role of hadronic modes in bulk plasma properties, as well as full QCD simulations with dynamical quarks to make definite conclusions.
