# Lattice quantum chromodynamics at large isospin density: 6144 pions in a box

> We present an algorithm to compute correlation functions for systems with the quantum numbers of many identical mesons from lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The algorithm is numerically stable and allows for the computation of $n$-pion correlation functions for $n \in \{ 1, \dots, N\}$ using a single $N \times N$ matrix decomposition, improving on previous algorithms. We apply the algorithm to calculations of correlation functions with up to 6144 $π^+$s using two ensembles of gauge field configurations generated with quark masses corresponding to a pion mass $m_π= 170$ MeV and spacetime volumes of $(4.4^3\times 8.8)\ {\rm fm}^4$ and $(5.8^3\times 11.6)\ {\rm fm}^4$. We also discuss statistical techniques for the analysis of such systems, in which the correlation functions vary over many orders of magnitude. In particular, we observe that the many-pion correlation functions are well approximated by log-normal distributions, allowing the extraction of the energies of these systems. Using these energies, the large-isospin-density, zero-baryon-density region of the QCD phase diagram is explored. A peak is observed in the energy density at an isospin chemical potential $μ_I\sim 1.5 m_π$, signalling the transition into a Bose-Einstein condensed phase. The isentropic speed of sound in the medium is seen to exceed the ideal-gas (conformal) limit ($c_s^2\leq 1/3$) over a wide range of chemical potential before falling towards the asymptotic expectation at $μ_I\sim 15 m_π$. These, and other thermodynamic observables, indicate that the isospin chemical potential must be large for the system to be well described by an ideal gas or perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The authors present an algorithm for computing correlation functions of many identical mesons in lattice QCD that is numerically stable and efficient enough to study systems of up to 6144 charged pions, but the physical results rely on the empirically-motivated assumption that the correlation functions follow log-normal distributions rather than the central limit theorem, which is inapplicable for these systems.

## Method

The algorithm uses representation theory of the symmetric group to express n-pion correlation functions as elementary symmetric polynomials of the eigenvalues of a pion-block matrix, computed via singular value decomposition of the quark propagator matrix. The authors apply this to two ensembles of Wilson-clover fermion gauge configurations with pion mass mπ = 170 MeV and spacetime volumes of (4.4³ × 8.8) fm⁴ (ensemble A, 201 configurations) and (5.8³ × 11.6) fm⁴ (ensemble B, 322 configurations), using smeared-source propagators from a sparse grid to achieve a spin-color-spatial matrix dimension of N = 6144. Statistical analysis is performed under the assumption of log-normal distributions for the correlation functions, allowing extraction of energies and thermodynamic observables.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass mπ = 170 MeV (from quark masses corresponding to 166(2) MeV on ensemble A and 172(6) MeV on ensemble B)
- Maximum number of pions studied: n = 6144, corresponding to N = 4 × 3 × 512 = 6144 spin-color-spatial degrees of freedom
- Lattice spacing a = 0.091(1) fm for both ensembles
- Spatial extents: L = 48 (ensemble A, 4.4 fm) and L = 64 (ensemble B, 5.8 fm)
- A peak in energy density observed at isospin chemical potential μI ∼ 1.5 mπ, signaling the BEC transition
- Speed of sound exceeds the conformal limit (cs² ≤ 1/3) over a wide range and falls toward asymptotic expectation at μI ∼ 15 mπ
- Required number of configurations for CLT-based analysis grows as Nconf(min)(n) = e^{σn²}/2, already unrealistic for n ∼ 100

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of many-pion systems by Detmold et al. (2012) which reached n ≤ 72, using a new algorithm that avoids the numerical instabilities and O(N⁴) scaling of prior recursive methods. The results probe the large-isospin-density region of the QCD phase diagram beyond the BEC transition observed in earlier work by Kogut et al. (2002) and Endrodi (2014), and demonstrate that perturbative QCD descriptions require μI ≥ 15 mπ. The statistical techniques developed for log-normal distributions may be applicable to other many-body lattice QCD calculations such as nuclei.
