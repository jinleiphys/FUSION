# Heavy ion charge exchange reactions and the link with beta decay processes

> Within the DWBA framework, we develop a theoretical description of single and double heavy ion charge exchange (CE) reactions. We show that absorption effects are particularly important for heavy ion reactions, leading to a noticeable reduction of the CE cross sections. At low momentum transfer, the single CE cross section can be factorised, thus allowing to evaluate corresponding distortion factors and access beta decay strengths. Applications are shown for a system of experimental interest. Preliminary results are discussed also for double CE reactions, modeled as a two-step mechanisms, i.e. a sequence of two charge-changing processes.

## Key claim

Within the DWBA framework, heavy ion single charge exchange (SCE) cross sections can be factorized at low momentum transfer to isolate the transition matrix element and access beta decay strengths, but only when absorption effects, which are particularly strong for heavy ions and cause a noticeable reduction of cross sections, are properly accounted for via distortion factors.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical description of single and double heavy ion charge exchange reactions using the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA), with initial and final state ion-ion interactions described by microscopic optical potentials obtained by folding Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov ground state densities with free-space nucleon-nucleon T-matrices. Nuclear transition densities and response functions are computed using QRPA with a G-Matrix interaction. For double charge exchange (DCE) reactions, the process is modeled as a two-step mechanism, a sequence of two single charge-changing transitions, and calculations are performed using both the HIDEX code (in a double-step approach) and the FRESCO code.

## Key numbers

* For the SCE reaction \(^{18}\text{O}+{}^{40}\text{Ca}\to {}^{18}\text{F}+{}^{40}\text{K}\) at \(T_{\text{lab}}=15\) AMeV, the black disk approximation yields a suppression factor of \(8.14 \times 10^{-4}\) for \(R_{\text{abs}} \approx 8\) fm, in good agreement with the numerical DWBA/PWBA ratio at zero angle of \(f_{\text{BD}}(\text{numerical})|_{E_x=0} = 8.35 \times 10^{-4}\).
* For the same SCE reaction at \(T_{\text{lab}}=270\) MeV, the DWBA cross section integrated over the full angular range is strongly suppressed relative to PWBA, with absorption effects dominated by the imaginary part of the optical potential.
* For the DCE reaction \(^{18}\text{O}+{}^{40}\text{Ca}\to {}^{18}\text{Ne}+{}^{40}\text{Ar}\) at 15 AMeV, DWBA calculations using distorted waves in the intermediate channel underestimate the FRESCO cross section by a factor \(f_{\text{p.w.}} \approx 10\), but scaling by this factor reproduces the FRESCO results.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for heavy ion charge exchange reactions beyond the light-ion studies that have been the workhorse of the field (e.g., \((^3\text{He},{}^3\text{H})\) reactions reviewed by Ichimura (2006), Thies (2012), Frekers (2013, 2015), and Fuji (2011)). It builds on the pilot DCE experiment by Cappuzzello (2015) on \(^{18}\text{O}+{}^{40}\text{Ca}\to {}^{18}\text{Ne}+{}^{40}\text{Ar}\) and the NUMEN project at LNS Catania (Cappuzzello, 2018). The factorization demonstrated here enables future extraction of nuclear matrix elements relevant for beta decay and, for DCE reactions, potentially for neutrinoless double-beta decay calculations, though the conditions for DCE cross section factorization are still under investigation.
