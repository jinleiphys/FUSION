# How much charm can PANDA produce?

> We consider the production of charmed baryons and mesons in the proton-antiproton binary reactions at the energies of the future $\bar{P}$ANDA experiment. To describe these processes in terms of hadronic interaction models, one needs strong couplings of the initial nucleons with the intermediate and final charmed hadrons. Similar couplings enter the models of binary reactions with strange hadrons. For both charmed and strange hadrons we employ the strong couplings and their ratios calculated from QCD light-cone sum rules. In this method finite masses of $c$ and $s$ quarks are taken into account. Employing the Kaidalov's quark-gluon string model with Regge poles and adjusting the normalization of the amplitudes in this model to the calculated strong couplings, we estimate the production cross section of charmed hadrons. For $p\bar{p}\to Λ_c\barΛ_c$ it can reach several tens of $nb$ at $p_{lab}= 15 {GeV}$, whereas the cross sections of $Σ_c$ and $D$ pair production are predicted to be smaller.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that the cross section for $p\bar{p}\to \Lambda_c\bar{\Lambda}_c$ can reach several tens of nb at $p_{lab}=15$ GeV, but this prediction relies on adjusting the normalization of the QGS model amplitudes to strong couplings calculated from QCD light-cone sum rules, and the uncertainties in the strong couplings introduce large uncertainties in the cross sections.

## Method

The authors employ QCD light-cone sum rules (LCSR) to calculate the strong couplings of charmed baryons ($\Lambda_c$, $\Sigma_c$) with nucleons and charmed mesons ($D$, $D^*$), and similarly for strange baryons ($\Lambda$, $\Sigma$) with nucleons and strange mesons ($K$, $K^*$). They then use these couplings as inputs to the Kaidalov quark-gluon string (QGS) model with Regge poles, where the amplitudes for $p\bar{p}$ binary reactions are described by planar diagrams with $t$-channel Regge exchanges, and the helicity amplitudes are matched to elementary meson-exchange Feynman diagrams at the Regge poles.

## Context

This work extends the Kaidalov quark-gluon string model (Kaidalov 1981, Kaidalov 1999) for strange hadron production to charmed hadron production in $p\bar{p}$ collisions at PANDA energies, replacing the $SU(4)_{fl}$ symmetry assumptions used in earlier work (Titov and Kampfer) with strong couplings calculated from QCD light-cone sum rules. The authors use the ratios of charmed to strange couplings from LCSR to reduce uncertainties, and they validate the model by fitting to existing data on $p\bar{p}\to \Lambda\bar{\Lambda}$ and $p\bar{p}\to K^+K^-$ from Becker (1978) and Brabson (1973). The predictions enable assessment of PANDA's capability for charm physics studies such as charm-anticharm mixing, CP-violation in $D$ decays, and $\Lambda_c$ decays, and they can be compared with other models (Haidenbauer 2009, Haidenbauer 2010, Kerbikov 1994) that give predictions differing by several orders of magnitude.
