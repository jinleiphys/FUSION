# Unification of Airy structure in inelastic $α$ +$^{16}$O scattering and $α$-cluster structure with core excitation in $^{20}$Ne

> The Airy structure of the nuclear rainbow and prerainbow in inelastic and elastic $α+^{16}$O scattering is studied with the coupled channel method using a folding potential derived from the microscopic wave functions of $^{16}$O. The theoretical calculations reproduce the characteristic energy evolution of the Airy minimum of the experimental angular distributions. The energy levels with $α$-cluster structure in $^{20}$Ne are reproduced well using the potentials determined from the analysis of scattering. It is shown that the emergence of the $K=0_3^+$ $α$-cluster band with core excitation at 7.19 MeV in $^{20}$Ne is intimately related to the emergence of the prerainbow and rainbow in inelastic scattering to the $^{16}$O($0^+_2$). It is found that the $α$-cluster states with core excitation, the prerainbow and the rainbow in inelastic scattering are understood in a unified way as well as in the case of elastic scattering.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the Airy structure in inelastic α+¹⁶O scattering and the α-cluster structure with core excitation in ²⁰Ne can be understood in a unified way, provided that the interaction potential for the inelastic channel is derived from microscopic cluster wave functions rather than from phenomenological form factors. The claim is conditional on using the double folding potential from the orthogonality condition model (OCM) wave functions of ¹⁶O.

## Method
The authors performed coupled-channel calculations for elastic and inelastic α+¹⁶O scattering using a double folding potential constructed from microscopic OCM wave functions of ¹⁶O with a density-dependent M3Y effective interaction (DDM3Y). They simultaneously included the 0⁺₁ (ground state), 0⁺₂ (6.05 MeV), 3⁻ (6.13 MeV), and 2⁺ (6.92 MeV) states of ¹⁶O, with transition densities from Okabe (1995). The real potential normalization factor N_R was adjusted to fit experimental angular distributions, and a phenomenological volume-type Woods-Saxon imaginary potential accounted for absorption.

## Key numbers
- Volume integral per nucleon pair for the elastic channel at 146 MeV: 304 MeV fm³
- Volume integral per nucleon pair for the elastic channel at 40.5 MeV: 395 MeV fm³
- Airy minimum in elastic scattering observed at approximately 18° at 146 MeV, 36° at 80.7 MeV, and 78° at 48.7 MeV
- Airy minimum for inelastic scattering to the 3⁻ state observed at approximately 27° at 146 MeV and 44° at 80.7 MeV
- Calculated rms radius of matter density distribution: 2.58 fm for the ground state of ¹⁶O and 2.77 fm for the 0⁺₂ state
- Normalization factor N_R = 1.245 adjusted to reproduce the experimental binding energy of the ²⁰Ne ground state (4.73 MeV from α threshold)
- The K=0₃⁺ α-cluster band in ²⁰Ne starts at E_x = 7.19 MeV, close to the 0⁺₂ excitation energy in ¹⁶O (6.05 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the unified description of elastic α+¹⁶O scattering and α-cluster structure in ²⁰Ne (Ohkubo 1977, Michel 1983, Abele 1993) to inelastic channels, which had not been previously attempted for this system. It builds on earlier studies of inelastic rainbow scattering for α+⁴⁰Ca and ⁶Li+¹²C using phenomenological form factors (Michel 2004, Michel 2005), and on the successful application of similar folding methods to α and ³He scattering from ¹²C (Ohkubo 2004, Ohkubo 2007, Hamada 2013). The results enable future experimental measurements of inelastic scattering to the 0⁺₂ state to test the predicted prerainbow and rainbow structure, and provide a framework for studying α-cluster states with core excitation in other nuclei.
