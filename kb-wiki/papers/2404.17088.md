# The Theoretical Study of $p\bar{p}\to\barΛΣη$ Reaction

> We study the production of hyperon resonances in the $p\bar{p}\to\barΛΣη$ reaction within an effective Lagrangian approach. The model includes the production of $Σ(1750)$ and $Λ(1670)$ in the intermediate state excited by the $K$ and $K^*$ meson exchanges between the initial proton and antiproton. Due to the large coupling of $Σ(1750)Ση$ vertex, $Σ(1750)$ is found a significant contribution near the threshold in this reaction. We provide total and differential cross section predictions for the reation and discuss the possible influence of $Σ(1750)Ση$ vertex coupling and model parameters, which will be useful in future experimental studies. This reaction can provide a platform for studying the features of $Σ(1750)$ resonance, especially the coupling to $Ση$ channel.

# Wiki Page: Theoretical Study of \( p\bar{p} \to \bar{\Lambda}\Sigma\eta \) Reaction

## Key claim

The \( p\bar{p} \to \bar{\Lambda}\Sigma\eta \) reaction is predicted to be dominated by the \(\Sigma(1750)\) resonance near threshold, provided the branching ratio \(Br(\Sigma^* \to \Sigma\eta)\) is at least 15% and the cut-off parameter \(\Lambda_{\Sigma^*}\) exceeds 1.25 GeV; if \(\Lambda_{\Sigma^*}\) falls below 1.25 GeV, the \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance becomes the dominant contribution instead.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach with \(t\)-channel and \(u\)-channel Feynman diagrams, where the \(\Sigma(1750)\) and \(\Lambda(1670)\) hyperon resonances are produced via \(K^+\) and \(K^{*+}\) meson exchanges between the initial proton and antiproton. Coupling constants are determined from SU(3) predictions and partial decay widths from the Particle Data Group, with finite-width effects included for the resonances. Form factors with cut-off parameters \(\Lambda_K = 1.1\) GeV and \(\Lambda_{K^*} = 1.5\) GeV are applied at the meson-exchange vertices, and the cut-off for baryon vertices is varied between 1.2 and 1.8 GeV to study parameter dependence.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section is calculated from reaction threshold up to center-of-mass energy \(W = 3.5\) GeV.
- The \(\Sigma(1750)\) resonance has a width of 206 MeV and an adopted branching ratio to \(\Sigma\eta\) of 0.35 (35%), with a range from 15% to 55% considered.
- The \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance has a width of 32 MeV and an adopted branching ratio to \(\Lambda\eta\) of 0.175 (17.5%).
- Coupling constants used: \(g_{\Lambda KN} = -13.99\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(g_{\Sigma KN} = 2.69\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(g_{\Lambda K^*N} = -6.21\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(g_{\Sigma K^*N} = -4.25\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\).
- The squared coupling \(g^2/4\pi\) for \(\Sigma(1750)\Sigma\eta\) is \(4.11 \times 10^{-1}\); for \(\Lambda(1670)\Lambda\eta\) it is \(6.06 \times 10^{-2}\).
- Differential cross sections (invariant mass distributions and angular distributions) are presented at \(W = 3.1\) GeV.
- When \(Br(\Sigma^* \to \Sigma\eta) = 55\%\), the total contribution is about 4 times that for \(Br(\Sigma^* \to \Sigma\eta) = 15\%\).

## Context

This work extends the study of hyperon resonances in the \(\Sigma\eta\) channel, which is a pure \(I=1\) channel only coupled to \(\Sigma\) hyperon resonances. Prior experimental evidence for \(\Sigma(1750)\) coupling to \(\Sigma\eta\) came from \(K^- p \to \Sigma^0 \eta\) reactions (Jones 1974), while chiral \(\bar{K}N\) interaction models (Feijoo 2022, Feijoo 2021) and effective chiral Lagrangian methods (Nogueira-Santos 2023) have analyzed related processes. The reaction proposed here is intended for future experiments at the \(\bar{\mathrm{P}}\)ANDA collaboration and the High-Intensity Heavy-Ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF) in China, enabling studies of \(\Sigma(1750)\) resonance properties and the strong interaction in hyperon spectroscopy.
