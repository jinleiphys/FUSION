# Structure models: from shell model to ab initio methods

> A brief review of models to describe nuclear structure and reactions properties is presented, starting from the historical shell model picture and encompassing modern ab initio approaches. A selection of recent theoretical results on observables for exotic light and medium-mass nuclei is shown. Emphasis is given to the comparison with experiment and to what can be learned about three-body forces and continuum properties.

## Key claim

This review argues that modern ab initio methods, particularly those using chiral effective field theory interactions, can describe light and medium-mass nuclei with accuracy sometimes comparable to experimental data, but this success depends on the systematic inclusion of three-body forces and controlled approximations that allow theoretical error estimation.

## Method

The paper reviews the historical development of nuclear structure models, beginning with the liquid drop model and the non-interacting shell model with spin-orbit coupling, then progressing to the interacting shell model where residual interactions are treated via diagonalization in a valence space. It then describes chiral effective field theory as a systematic framework for constructing nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces, and surveys ab initio methods including the no-core shell model, coupled-cluster methods, and the Lorentz integral transform method for continuum problems.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon is approximately constant at about 8 MeV for mass numbers between 100 and 250
- Magic numbers predicted by the harmonic oscillator mean-field model are 2, 8, 20, 40; only the first three match observation
- Magic numbers predicted by the mean-field model with spin-orbit force are 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, and 126 for neutrons, which correspond to observation
- The chiral expansion parameter is Q/Λχ, where Λχ ∼ 1 GeV represents the chiral-symmetry breaking scale
- Three-nucleon forces first appear at next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) in the chiral expansion
- The USD interaction is used for sd-shell nuclei in the mass range 16 ≤ A ≤ 40
- The GXPF1 or KB3G interactions are used for pf-shell nuclei with a ⁴⁰Ca core

## Context

This review extends the pedagogical tradition of nuclear structure textbooks such as Krane and Suhonen, while incorporating modern developments from chiral effective field theory pioneered by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Epelbaum (2006), Machleidt (2011), and others. The paper enables researchers to understand how ab initio methods like the no-core shell model (Barrett, 2013) and coupled-cluster methods (Hagen, 2010) can now address exotic nuclei studied at rare isotope facilities, building on earlier reviews by Leidemann (2013), Bacca (2014), and Hebeler (2015).
