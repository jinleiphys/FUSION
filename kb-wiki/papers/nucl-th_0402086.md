# S-wave pion nucleon scattering lengths from $πN$, pionic hydrogen and deuteron data

> The isoscalar and isovector scattering lengths (b_0,b_1) are determined using a unitarized coupled channel approach based on chiral Lagrangians. Using experimental values of pionic hydrogen and deuterium as well as low energy pi N scattering data, the free parameters of the model are calculated. Isospin violation is incorporated to a certain extent by working with physical particle masses. For the deuterium scattering length a_{pi-d} new significant corrections concerning real and imaginary parts are evaluated, putting new constraints from pi-d scattering on the values of (b_0,b_1). In particular, dispersion corrections, the influence of the Delta(1232) resonance, crossed terms and multiple scattering in a Faddeev approach are considered.

## Key claim

The isoscalar and isovector pion-nucleon scattering lengths (b0, b1) are determined from a combined analysis of πN scattering data, pionic hydrogen, and pionic deuterium data using a unitarized coupled-channel chiral approach, with the caveat that isospin violation is only partially incorporated through physical particle masses, and the results depend on the treatment of dispersion corrections to the π⁻d scattering length.

## Method

The authors use a unitarized coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach based on the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, extended with isoscalar terms from the second-order chiral Lagrangian (with coefficients c1, c2, c3) and a πN→ππN channel parametrized by a constant γ. The π⁻d scattering length is calculated using the fixed-center approximation to the Faddeev equations, with absorption and dispersion corrections evaluated via Feynman diagrams including Δ(1232) excitation, crossed terms, and multiple scattering, using realistic CD-Bonn and Paris deuteron wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Imaginary part of a_{π⁻d} from absorption: (59.6 ± 5.3) × 10⁻⁴ m_{π⁻}⁻¹ (s+d-wave, average of CD-Bonn and Paris potentials)
- Experimental Im a_{π⁻d}: (63 ± 7) × 10⁻⁴ m_{π⁻}⁻¹ (from Hauser et al.)
- Dispersive real part Δ Re a_{π⁻d} from absorption (s-wave, App3): (2.4 ± 4.3) × 10⁻⁴ m_{π⁻}⁻¹
- Δ Re a_{π⁻d} from d-wave absorption: 0.18 × 10⁻⁴ m_{π⁻}⁻¹
- Δ Re a_{π⁻d} from q'×q term: (0.13 − i 0.38) × 10⁻⁴ m_{π⁻}⁻¹
- Δ Re a_{π⁻d} from Δ(1232) excitation: small (numerical value not explicitly given in the truncated text)
- Previous results for (b0, b1) from Ericson (2000): (−12 ± 2 stat. ± 8 syst., −895 ± 3 stat. ± 13 syst.) × 10⁻⁴ m_{π⁻}⁻¹
- Previous results for (b0, b1) from Beane (2002): (−34 ± 7, −918 ± 13) × 10⁻⁴ m_{π⁻}⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the unitarized chiral approach of Inoue (2001) to low-energy πN scattering and threshold, and provides a more complete treatment of π⁻d scattering corrections than earlier Faddeev calculations by Afnan (1974), Mizutani, and Fayard (1980). The results address the discrepancy between the scattering length determinations of Ericson (2000) and Beane (2002), and enable improved extraction of the πN scattering lengths from pionic atom data, with implications for chiral perturbation theory parameters and the study of isospin violation in the πN system.
