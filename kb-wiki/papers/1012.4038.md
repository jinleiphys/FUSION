# Review on Heavy-Ion Physics

> Collisions of heavy ions (nuclei) at ultra-relativistic energies (sqrt(s_NN) >> 10 GeV per nucleon-nucleon collision in the centre of mass system) are regarded as a unique tool to produce in the laboratory a high energy density and high temperature state of strongly-interacting matter. In this short review, we will discuss the expected features of this hot and dense state, describe indications on its properties emerged from the experimental programs at the CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC accelerators, and finally outlook the perspectives for the forthcoming heavy-ion runs at the CERN-LHC.

## Key claim

Collisions of heavy ions at ultra-relativistic energies (sqrt(s_NN) >> 10 GeV per nucleon-nucleon collision) are regarded as a unique tool to produce a high energy density and high temperature state of strongly-interacting matter in the laboratory, with the review summarizing expected features and experimental indications from the CERN-SPS and BNL-RHIC programs while also outlining perspectives for the forthcoming LHC heavy-ion runs.

## Method

The paper reviews the phenomenology of hot and dense QCD matter by examining the phase diagram of strongly-interacting matter as a function of baryonic chemical potential and temperature, then summarizes experimental evidence from fixed-target experiments at the CERN-SPS (Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) ≈ 17 GeV) and collider experiments at BNL-RHIC (Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV). Key observables discussed include strange baryon enhancement, J/ψ suppression, high-pt hadron suppression (R_AA), and azimuthal correlations, with comparisons to pp and d-Au reference data.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the QCD phase transition: T_c = (175 ± 15) MeV from lattice QCD calculations
- Energy density at the critical temperature: ε_c ≈ 1 GeV/fm^3
- Energy density reached in central Pb-Pb collisions at the SPS: ε ≈ 3 GeV/fm^3, above the critical value
- Strange baryon enhancement in central Pb-Pb collisions at the SPS: up to 20 times for the Ω baryon compared to p-Be collisions
- RHIC Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: high-pt hadron yield reduced by a factor 4-5 (R_AA) compared to expectations from binary nucleon-nucleon collisions
- LHC Pb-Pb energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 2.75 TeV (first run, November 2010), maximum 5.5 TeV (expected 2013)
- Expected initial temperature at LHC: > 600 MeV (compared to 350 MeV at RHIC and 200 MeV at SPS)
- Expected energy density at LHC: 120 GeV/fm^3 (compared to 25 GeV/fm^3 at RHIC and 3 GeV/fm^3 at SPS)
- Expected charged-particle rapidity density at LHC: dN_ch/dy ≈ 2000-3000 (compared to 650 at RHIC and 400 at SPS)
- Expected lifetime of the deconfined medium at LHC: > 10 fm/c (compared to 2-4 fm/c at RHIC and < 2 fm/c at SPS)

## Context

This review extends the experimental findings from the CERN-SPS program (WA97/NA57 strangeness enhancement, NA50/NA60 J/ψ suppression) and the BNL-RHIC program (PHENIX, STAR, BRAHMS, PHOBOS high-pt suppression and azimuthal correlations) to the forthcoming LHC heavy-ion program. The work builds on theoretical predictions by Rafelski and Muller (1982) for strangeness enhancement, Matsui and Satz (1986) for J/ψ suppression, and Bjorken (1982) for parton energy loss, and enables systematic study of Quark-Gluon Plasma properties at higher temperatures and energy densities where the medium is expected to behave more like an ideal gas.
