# Revisiting the $DD^\ast$ chiral interactions with the local momentum-space regularization up to the third order and the nature of $T_{cc}^+$

> We revisit the $DD^\ast$ interactions in chiral effective field theory up to the third order for the first time. We deal with the pion-exchanged interactions via local momentum-space regularization, in which we focus on their long-range behaviors through demanding their contributions vanish at the origin in the coordinate space. The short-range contact interactions and subleading pion-charmed meson couplings are estimated with the phenomenological resonance saturation model. The subleading pion-charmed meson couplings are much weaker than those in the pion-nucleon system, thus the $DD^\ast$ binding mechanism is very different with that of the $NN$ system. We also obtain the analytic structure of the two-pion exchange interactions in the coordinate space, and we find that its asymptotic behavior at long distance is similar to but slightly different with the $NN$ interactions. We get the same asymptotic behavior of the two-pion exchange interaction with that from HAL QCD method but appearing in the longer distance rather than $1 <r<2\text{ fm}$. The binding solution only exists in the isoscalar channel. Our calculation supports the molecular interpretation of $T_{cc}^+$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the binding mechanism of the $DD^\ast$ system is very different from that of the $NN$ system because the subleading pion-charmed meson couplings are much weaker than those in the pion-nucleon system, and that the binding solution only exists in the isoscalar channel, supporting the molecular interpretation of $T_{cc}^+$.

## Method

The authors calculated $DD^\ast$ interactions in chiral effective field theory up to the third order (N$^2$LO) for the first time, using local momentum-space regularization for the pion-exchanged interactions. The short-range contact interactions and subleading pion-charmed meson couplings were estimated using the phenomenological resonance saturation model. The two-pion exchange interactions were obtained via spectral function representation with local momentum-space regularization, and the analytic structure in coordinate space was derived.

## Key numbers

* The LECs for subleading $\pi D^{(\ast)}$ vertices are: $\tilde{c}_1 = -0.21\pm0.07$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\tilde{c}_2 = -0.83\pm0.16$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\tilde{c}_3 = -0.55\pm0.18$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\tilde{c}_4 = 0.61\pm0.10$ GeV$^{-1}$, $\tilde{c}_5 = 0.26$ GeV$^{-1}$ (Table II).
* The contact LECs at LO include $C_1 = 7.5\pm1.3$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $C_2 = 10.9\pm0.2+0.3\,\mathrm{sgn}$ GeV$^{-2}$ (Table I).
* The OPE potential uses $g_\varphi = -0.59$, $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, $m_\pi \simeq 137$ MeV, and $\delta_b = m_{D^\ast} - m_D \simeq 142$ MeV.
* The $T_{cc}^+$ is below the $D^{\ast+}D^0$ threshold by about $300$ keV.

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory from the nucleon-nucleon system to the heavy-light $DD^\ast$ system, following the approach of Xu (2017) who first studied $DD^\ast$ interactions using resonance saturation. The paper addresses the convergence problems noted by Epelbaum (2002) in the $NN$ case and by Chen (2022) for $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$ interactions, where dimensional regularization led to bad convergence and unnaturally deep bound states. The results enable a systematic study of the $T_{cc}^+$ inner structure and properties, and provide a comparison with lattice QCD results from Lyu (2023) using the HAL QCD method.
