# A Comparison of Shell Model Results for Some properties of the Even-Even Ge Isotopes

> In this work we examine two recent effective shell model interactions, JUN45 and JJ4B, that have been proposed for use in the $f_{5/2},p_{3/2}, p_{1/2}, g_{9/2}$ model space for both protons and neutrons. We calculate a number of quantities that did not enter into the fits undertaken to fix the parameters of both interactions. In particular we consider static quadrupole moments (Q's) of excited states of the even-even $^{70-76}$Ge isotopes, as well as the B(E2) values in these nuclei. (We have previously studied $^{70}$Zn isotopes using JJ4B.) Some striking disagreements between the JUN45 prediction and the experimental results had already been noted for the quadrupole moments of the $2_1^+$ states of these nuclei. We investigate whether these discrepancies also occur for the JJ4B interaction. Subsequently, we also apply both interactions to calculate the Q's of some more highly excited states and compare the two sets of predictions regarding the nature of the nuclear states under consideration. In order to gain insight into these more complex large-scale shell-model calculations, we examine the corresponding and much simpler single-j shell model calculations in the $g_{9/2}$ neutron shell.

# A Comparison of Shell Model Results for Some properties of the Even-Even Ge Isotopes

## Key claim

The JUN45 and JJ4B effective shell model interactions, while reproducing excitation energies well because those were included in the parameter fits, yield calculated static quadrupole moments for the 2₁⁺ states of ⁷⁰⁻⁷⁶Ge that disagree substantially with experiment, particularly for ⁷²Ge, ⁷⁴Ge, and ⁷⁶Ge where the sign or magnitude is incorrect; this discrepancy persists even with larger effective charges and suggests the truncated model space itself may be the root cause.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations for ⁷⁰,⁷²,⁷⁴,⁷⁶Ge using two effective interactions (JUN45 and JJ4B) in the f₅/₂, p₃/₂, p₁/₂, g₉/₂ model space above a ⁵⁶Ni core, computing excitation energies, B(E2) transition strengths, and static quadrupole moments for the 2₁⁺, 2₂⁺, and 4₁⁺ states. They compared these results to experimental data from the NNDC database and to simpler single-j shell-model calculations in the g₉/₂ neutron shell using Racah coefficients for (g₉/₂)² and (g₉/₂)⁴ configurations.

## Key numbers

- For excitation energies with JUN45, the average absolute deviation between experiment and calculation is 0.133 MeV; with JJ4B the average deviation is 0.234 MeV (excluding 0₂⁺ states) or 0.349 MeV (including 0₂⁺ states).
- The experimental B(E2; 2₁ → 0₁) values range from 17.8(3) W.u. in ⁷²Ge to 33.0(4) W.u. in ⁷⁴Ge; with standard effective charges (eₚ=1.5, eₙ=0.5), JJ4B gives 19.88 W.u. and JUN45 gives 14.55 W.u. for ⁷²Ge.
- The experimental B(E2; 2₂⁺ → 2₁⁺) for ⁷⁰Ge is 114(5) W.u., while JJ4B gives 26.55 W.u. and JUN45 gives 23.48 W.u.
- For Q(2₁⁺) in ⁷⁴Ge, experiment gives -25(6) fm², JJ4B gives -5.89 fm², and JUN45 gives +12.02 fm² (wrong sign).
- For Q(2₁⁺) in ⁷⁶Ge, experiment gives -19(6) fm², JJ4B gives -14.50 fm², and JUN45 gives +1.77 fm².
- In the single-j g₉/₂ shell, Q_sp for one neutron is -4b² = -17.7 fm²; for (g₉/₂)² I=2 v=2, Q = 11.797 fm².

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of ⁷⁰Zn using JJ4B (Robinson, Zamick, and Sharon, 2009) to the even-even Ge isotopes, and directly follows up on results by Honma et al. (2009) who noted that JUN45 gave poor Q(2₁⁺) predictions for Ge isotopes with N ≥ 38 despite good energy and B(E2) agreement. The paper tests whether JJ4B, an older interaction by Lisetskiy and Brown, performs better for these quadrupole moments, and finds it does only partially. The results highlight the need to include static quadrupole moment data when fitting effective interactions, and suggest that the common f₅/₂pg₉/₂ model space may be too restrictive, motivating future work with larger model spaces or experimental measurements of Q(2₂⁺) and Q(4₁⁺) to clarify the picture.
