# A Heavy Quark Symmetry Approach to Baryons

> We evaluate different properties of baryons with a heavy c or b quark. The use of Heavy Quark Symmetry (HQS) provides with an important simplification of the non relativistic three body problem which can be solved by means of a simple variational approach. This scheme is able to reproduce previous results obtained with more involved Faddeev calculations. The resulting wave functions are parametrized in a simple manner, and can be used to calculate further observables.

## Key claim

Heavy Quark Symmetry (HQS) simplifies the non-relativistic three-body problem for baryons containing a heavy charm or bottom quark, allowing a variational approach that reproduces results from more involved Faddeev calculations, but only up to corrections of order O(q/m_h) where q is a typical light-degree-of-freedom scale and m_h is the heavy quark mass.

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic three-body Schrödinger equation for baryons with one heavy quark (c or b) using a variational ansatz that factorizes the spatial wave function into single-particle solutions for each light quark relative to the heavy quark, multiplied by a Jastrow correlation function in the relative distance between the two light quarks. The variational parameters were optimized for several interquark potentials: the AL1 potential from Silvestre-Brac (1996), the potential from Bhaduri et al. (1981), and a potential from the SU(2) linear sigma model. The method exploits HQS to treat the total spin of the light degrees of freedom as well-defined in the infinite heavy-quark-mass limit.

## Key numbers

- Mass of Λ_c: variational result 2295 MeV, compared to experimental 2285 ± 1 MeV and Faddeev result 2296 MeV
- Mass of Λ_b: variational result 5643 MeV, compared to experimental 5624 ± 9 MeV and Faddeev result 5643 MeV
- Mass of Σ_c: variational result 2469 MeV, compared to experimental 2452 ± 1 MeV and Faddeev result 2466 MeV
- Mass of Σ_b: variational result 5851 MeV, compared to lattice result 5770 ± 70 MeV and Faddeev result 5849 MeV
- Mass mean square radius of Λ_c: 0.106 fm² (variational) versus 0.104 fm² (Faddeev)
- Mass mean square radius of Λ_b: 0.045 fm² (variational) versus 0.045 fm² (Faddeev)
- Charge mean square radius of Λ_c: 0.131 fm² (variational) versus 0.129 fm² (Faddeev)
- Charge mean square radius of Σ_c: -0.261 fm² (variational) versus -0.256 fm² (Faddeev)

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic constituent quark model approach to heavy baryons by systematically incorporating Heavy Quark Symmetry, building on the Faddeev calculations of Silvestre-Brac (1996) and the lattice results of Bowler et al. (1998). The simple parametrized wave functions enable calculation of further observables, and the authors have already applied them to study semileptonic decays of Λ_b and Ξ_b baryons. The method also predicts masses and radii for Σ*, Ξ', Ξ*, and Ω* baryons that were not evaluated in the Faddeev calculations of Silvestre-Brac (1996).
