# Spectra of heavy mesons in the Bethe-Salpeter approach

> We present a calculation of the spectrum of charmonia, bottomonia and B_c-meson states with `ordinary' and exotic quantum numbers. We discuss the merits and limitations of a rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations and explore the effects of different shapes of the effective running coupling on ground and excited states in channels with quantum numbers J <= 3. We furthermore discuss the status of the X(3872) as a potential (excited) quark-antiquark state and give predictions for the masses of charmonia and bottomonia in the tensor channels with J=2,3.

# Spectra of heavy mesons in the Bethe-Salpeter approach

## Key claim
The rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations can reproduce many features of heavy meson spectra, but the X(3872) cannot be described as a pure radially excited quark-antiquark state in this framework; modifications of the effective interaction within rainbow-ladder do not change this conclusion.

## Method
The authors solved the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-antiquark bound states in Euclidean space, using a rainbow-ladder truncation with a generalized Maris-Tandy effective interaction. They varied the shape of the effective running coupling by modifying the polynomial form of the infrared interaction term and the scale parameter, and employed extrapolation techniques for states whose masses required evaluating quark propagators beyond their known analytic structure.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass: m(19 GeV) = 0.870 GeV; bottom quark mass: m(19 GeV) = 3.790 GeV
- Vanilla Maris-Tandy parameters: η = 1.157 for charmonia, η = 1.357 for bottomonia, Λ = 0.72 GeV
- Predicted mass of the 3⁻⁻ charmonium state: m = 3896 MeV, with estimated error on the 3% level
- Regge trajectory slope for charmonia: α = 0.36, intercept α₀ = -2.55 for J = αM² + α₀
- B_c meson pseudoscalar ground state: 6354 MeV (+23.5/-23.5 MeV); vector ground state: 6498 MeV (+64.9/-64.9 MeV); scalar ground state: 6714 MeV (+67.1/-67.1 MeV)
- Excited state in the 1⁺⁺ charmonium channel at m = 3672 MeV, identified as not a radial excitation; second excitation at m = 3912 MeV identified as first radial excitation

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis of light mesons (Fischer, 2014) to heavy quarkonia and B_c mesons, and complements earlier rainbow-ladder studies of quarkonium spectra by Bhagwat (2006), Blank (2011), and Hilger (2014). The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations by Bali (2011) and Liu (2012), and with relativistic quark model predictions by Ebert (2011). The study demonstrates that while rainbow-ladder truncation works well for vector and tensor channels, significant corrections beyond this approximation are needed for scalar and axial-vector channels, motivating future work on beyond-rainbow-ladder interactions and coupled-channel effects.
