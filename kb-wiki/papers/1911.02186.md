# Electromagnetic contribution to $Σ$-$Λ$ mixing using lattice QCD+QED

> Mixing in the $Σ^0$-$Λ^0$ system is a direct consequence of broken isospin symmetry and is a measure of both isospin-symmetry breaking as well as general SU(3)-flavour symmetry breaking. In this work we present a new scheme for calculating the extent of $Σ^0$-$Λ^0$ mixing using simulations in lattice QCD+QED and perform several extrapolations that compare well with various past determinations. Our scheme allows us to easily contrast the QCD-only mixing case with the full QCD+QED mixing.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new lattice QCD+QED scheme for calculating the Σ⁰-Λ⁰ mixing angle that allows direct comparison between QCD-only and full QCD+QED mixing, with the caveat that the QED coupling used in the simulations is roughly 10 times larger than the physical value and must be scaled linearly to match the physical electromagnetic coupling.

## Method
The authors perform dynamical lattice QCD+QED simulations with Nf=1+1+1 flavours using an O(a)-improved stout link non-perturbative clover (SLiNC) action, with tree-level Symanzik improved SU(3) gauge action and noncompact U(1) QED gauge action. They calculate all four elements of the Σ⁰-Λ⁰ correlation matrix on ensembles around the U-spin symmetric point, numerically diagonalize the matrix at each time slice, and fit a constant to the observed plateau region to extract the mixing angle. The mixing angles from multiple ensembles are then used to fit a parametrization derived from a first-order SU(3)-flavour breaking expansion in quark masses and charges, including a renormalization factor Z to account for QED-induced running of quark masses.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a⁻¹ = 2.91(3) GeV
- QCD coupling: β_QCD = 5.5, QED coupling: β_QED = 0.8, giving α_QED ≈ 0.1 (roughly 10× physical value)
- QCD-only mixing angle at physical point: θ_ΣΛ,isospin,QCD-only = -0.55(3)° (from quark masses aδm_u = -0.01140(3), aδm_d = -0.01067(3))
- Best-fit parameters: D_QED/D_QCD = -3.8(7)×10⁻⁵, Z = 0.96(4), with χ²/DOF = 0.84 and parameter correlation coefficient -0.45
- Lightest neutral flavour-singlet meson masses M_uū range from 284(4) MeV to 442(9) MeV across ensembles
- Mixing angles from individual ensembles range from -3.5(4)° to -30° (theoretical) for isospin

## Context
This work extends previous QCD-only lattice calculations of Σ⁰-Λ⁰ mixing by Horsley (2014) and Horsley (2015) to include electromagnetic effects, and provides a new scheme that contrasts with the Dalitz and Von Hippel (1964) approach based on electromagnetic mass-splittings of octet baryons. The results enable future precision determinations of isospin-symmetry breaking in baryon systems and can be compared with leading-order chiral perturbation theory predictions from Gasser and Leutwyler (1982). The method avoids fitting effective masses by extracting the mixing angle directly from diagonalization of the correlation matrix.
