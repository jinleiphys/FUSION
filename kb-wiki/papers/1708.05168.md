# Universality of many-body two-nucleon momentum distributions: the correlated nucleon spectral function of complex nuclei revisited

> Realistic NN interactions and many-body approaches have been used to calculate ground-state properties of nuclei with A=3, 4, 12, 16, 40, with particular emphasis on various kinds of momentum distributions. It is shown that at proper values of the relative (rel) and center-of-mass (c.m.) momenta, the two-nucleon momentum distribution n_A^{N_1N_2} (k_{rel}, K_{c.m.}, θ) exhibits the property of factorization, namely n_A^{N_1N_2} (k_{rel}, K_{c.m.}, θ) \simeq n_{rel}(k_{rel}) n_(c.m.)( K{c.m.}). The factorization of the momentum distributions , bearing a universal character, results from a general property of realistic nuclear wave functions, namely their factorization at short inter-nucleon separations. The factorization of the two-nucleon momentum distribution allows one to develop the correlated part of the nucleon spectral function P(k,E) in terms of a convolution integral involving the product of the many-body, parameter-free relative and c.m. momentum distributions of a given nucleus. It is shown that: (i) the obtained spectral function perfectly satisfies the momentum sum rule, i.e. when it is integrated over the removal energy E, it fully reproduces the momentum distributions obtained from realistic many-body wave functions , (ii) in order to saturate the momentum sum rule at high values of the momentum (k \simeq 5 fm^{-1}) the spectral function has to be integrate up to E \simeq 400 MeV. To sum up a realistic, parameter-free many-body Spectral function has been developed such that : i) a phenomenological convolution spectral function developed in the past is fully justified from a many-body point of view , and (ii) the model dependence which might be present in calculations of inclusive electroweak processes could be reduced by the use of the convolution spectral function developed here.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for nuclei with A=3, 4, 12, 16, and 40, the two-nucleon momentum distribution factorizes into a product of relative and center-of-mass momentum distributions at high relative momenta, and this factorization allows construction of a parameter-free many-body spectral function that satisfies the momentum sum rule without adjustable parameters.

## Method

The authors used realistic Argonne-family nucleon-nucleon interactions within a variational framework employing a normalization-conserving linked cluster expansion (NCLCE) to calculate ground-state wave functions and the associated one- and two-nucleon momentum distributions for nuclei from A=3 to A=40. They analyzed the factorization property of the two-nucleon momentum distributions in the region where the relative momentum is much larger than the center-of-mass momentum, and used this factorization to express the correlated part of the nucleon spectral function as a convolution integral of the relative and center-of-mass momentum distributions.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energies and r.m.s. radii were calculated for A=3, 4, 12, 16, and 40 using NCLCE and compared with Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Cluster Variational Monte Carlo (CVMC) results (Table 1 in the paper).
* The momentum sum rule is saturated up to momenta k approximately 5 fm^{-1} when the spectral function is integrated over removal energies up to E approximately 400 MeV.
* The one-nucleon momentum distributions for 16O and 40Ca from NCLCE show remarkable agreement with CVMC results (Figs. 2-4 in the paper).
* The percent deviation of the NCLCE momentum distribution from CVMC results for 16O is shown in Fig. 4, with the quantity Delta n(k) = 100*(n_x(k)-n_CVMC(k))/n_CVMC(k).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological convolution spectral function model of Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1996) by providing a fully microscopic, parameter-free many-body justification based on realistic wave functions. It builds upon the demonstration of wave function factorization at short inter-nucleon separations in few-nucleon systems by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (2010) and in nuclear matter by Baldo et al. (2012), and the systematic analysis of factorization for complex nuclei by Alvioli et al. (2016). The resulting spectral function can reduce model dependence in calculations of inclusive electroweak processes on nuclear targets, including neutrino scattering and deep inelastic scattering.
