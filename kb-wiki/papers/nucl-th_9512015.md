# Energy Loss, Electron Screening, and the Astrophysical 3He(d,p)4He cross section

> We reanalyze the low-energy 3He(d,p)4He cross section measurements of Engstler et al. using recently measured energy loss data for proton and deuteron beams in a helium gas. Although the new 3He(d,p)4He S-factors are significantly lower than those reported by Engstler et al. they clearly show the presence of electron screening effects. From the new S-factors we find an electron screening energy in agreement with the adiabatic limit.

## Key claim

Reanalyzing the low-energy \(^3\)He(d,p)\(^4\)He cross section data of Engstler et al. using recently measured, lower stopping powers for protons and deuterons in helium gas yields astrophysical S-factors that are significantly lower than previously reported, and an electron screening energy consistent with the adiabatic limit (120 eV), removing the previously claimed excess.

## Method

The authors took the published S(E)-factor data and energies from Engstler et al. (Table 1), converted them to cross sections, and then recalculated effective center-of-mass energies by adding the difference between the tabulated energy loss (Ziegler) and the newly measured energy loss (from stopping power data of a separate study). They then converted the cross sections back to astrophysical S-factors at these new effective energies. The electron screening energy \(U_e\) was determined by fitting the enhancement factor \(f(E) = \exp(\pi \eta(E) U_e / E)\) to the ratio of the new S(E) data to the bare-nuclei S-factor, using both a 3rd-order and 4th-order polynomial parametrization of the bare-nuclei cross section from Chulick et al.

## Key numbers

- At the lowest energy (\(E = 5.88\) keV, 0.2 Torr), the measured energy loss (from stopping power data) is about 80 eV less than the tabulated Ziegler value; at \(E = 10\) keV, the difference is 48 eV.
- Using the new energy loss data and the 3rd-order polynomial bare-nuclei parametrization, the fitted screening energy is \(U_e = 117 \pm 7\) eV (\(\chi^2 = 0.5\) per degree of freedom).
- Using the 4th-order polynomial bare-nuclei parametrization, the fitted screening energy is \(U_e = 134 \pm 8\) eV (\(\chi^2 = 0.4\)).
- When using the exact definition of the enhancement factor (Eq. 2) instead of the approximation, the screening energy becomes \(U_e = 130 \pm 8\) eV (n=3) and \(U_e = 149 \pm 9\) eV (n=4).
- The adiabatic limit for the screening energy is \(U_e = 120\) eV.

## Context

This work reanalyzes the \(^3\)He(d,p)\(^4\)He cross section measurements of Engstler et al. (1992) and the subsequent screening-energy extraction by Prati et al. (1994), which reported a screening energy of \(U_e = 186 \pm 9\) eV, significantly exceeding the adiabatic limit. The reanalysis uses new stopping power measurements (from a separate study) and theoretical calculations by Grande and Schiwietz (1993) that show low-energy stopping power is dominated by electron capture and is much lower than the Ziegler-Andersen tables used previously. The result brings the screening energy into agreement with the adiabatic limit, resolving the discrepancy and validating the strategy of pushing laboratory measurements to lower energies for astrophysical extrapolation, provided improved low-energy stopping power data are used.
