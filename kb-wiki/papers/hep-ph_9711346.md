# Gluon production, cooling and entropy in nuclear collisions

> We study the cooling (heating) of a glue-parton gas due to production (destruction) of particles and determine the associated production of entropy. We incorporate sharing of the system energy among a changing number of particles. We find that the entropy of an evolving glue-parton gas changes in an insignificant range once the initial high temperature state has been formed, despite a great change in particle number and temperature.

## Key claim

The entropy of a hot glue-parton gas changes only insignificantly (by 11-15%) once the initial high-temperature state has been formed, despite a great increase in particle number and a large drop in temperature; this result holds under the condition that the system evolves at fixed energy density toward Hagedorn equilibrium (chemical occupancy \(\gamma \to 1\)).

## Method

The authors maximize the entropy functional for a relativistic Bose gas at fixed total energy, deriving the condition that Hagedorn equilibrium (maximum entropy) occurs when the chemical occupancy factor \(\gamma = e^{-\alpha}\) equals unity. They then compute the entropy density, temperature, particle density, and entropy per particle as functions of \(\gamma\) for a gluon gas at constant energy density, both for massless gluons and for gluons with a thermal mass of 0.200 GeV, with the equilibrium point fixed at \(T = 0.250\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- For massless gluons at fixed energy density 2.66 GeV/fm³: total entropy increases by only 15% as \(\gamma\) evolves from 0.1 to 1, while temperature drops from 0.45 GeV to 0.250 GeV.
- For gluons with thermal mass \(m_{\rm G}^{\rm th} = 0.200\) GeV at fixed energy density 1.89 GeV/fm³: total entropy increases by only 11% as \(\gamma\) evolves from 0.1 to 1, while temperature drops from 0.43 GeV to 0.250 GeV.
- The glue number density more than doubles for massless gluons in the same \(\gamma\) range (0.1 to 1).
- For an initial occupancy \(\gamma_0 = 0.1\) (\(\alpha_0 = 2.3\)), the cooling due to particle production alone gives a temperature reduction factor of 0.63 (exact Bose result including entropy production gives 0.55).

## Context

This work extends the "hot glue" initial state picture proposed by Shuryak (1992) and the kinetic studies of Geiger (1992) and Biró et al. (1993) by quantifying the entropy and temperature evolution during gluon production. The finding that entropy growth is small despite large particle number increase implies that the dominant entropy production in high-energy nuclear collisions must occur in the initial quantum time period before 0.3 fm/c, as argued by Geiger (1992). The result also shows that particle-production cooling is comparable in importance to hydrodynamic expansion cooling, as studied numerically by Biró et al. (1993).
