# On the momentum broadening of in-medium jet evolution using a light-front Hamiltonian approach

> We have developed a non-perturbative light-front Hamiltonian formalism to simulate the real-time evolution of a quark state in a SU(3) colored medium, with a series of works. In this proceeding article, we focus on the transverse momentum broadening of an in-medium quark jet. We perform the numerical simulation of the quark jet evolution in the $\ket{q}+\ket{qg}$ Fock space at various medium densities. By analyzing the resulting jet light-front wavefunction, we extract the gluon emission rate and the non-eikonal quenching parameter. Additionally, we provide the analytical derivation of the eikonal expectation value of the quark-gluon state's transverse momentum for any color configuration and arbitrary spatial distribution. This study can help understand jet momentum broadening beyond the eikonal limit.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-perturbative light-front Hamiltonian formalism (tBLFQ) for simulating real-time quark jet evolution in a SU(3) colored medium, focusing on transverse momentum broadening. The key hedge is that the numerical results are obtained in a truncated Fock space of only |q⟩ + |qg⟩ states, and the non-eikonal corrections to the quenching parameter are found to be sizable and time-dependent, but the formalism is limited to the specific truncation and medium model used.

## Method

The authors use time-dependent Basis Light-Front Quantization (tBLFQ) to simulate a quark jet propagating through a classical color background field described by the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model. They truncate the Fock space to |q⟩ + |qg⟩ components, discretize the system on a lattice with periodic boundary conditions in transverse dimensions and (anti-)periodic boundary conditions in the longitudinal direction, and evolve the quantum state using a time-ordered exponential of the light-front Hamiltonian decomposed into small time steps. The numerical simulation treats kinetic energy, quark-gluon interaction, and medium interaction separately within each time step, using matrix exponentiation in momentum and coordinate space and fourth-order Runge-Kutta for the quark-gluon vertex.

## Key numbers

- Simulation parameters: p⁺ = 17 GeV, N⊥ = 16, L⊥ = 50 GeV⁻¹, Lη = 50 GeV⁻¹, K = 8.5
- The medium-induced gluon emission probability δP|qg⟩ shows a slight dip at early time, then grows linearly after approximately x⁺ = 12 GeV⁻¹
- The final-state medium-induced gluon emission probability δP|qg⟩ is approximately proportional to Qs²
- The eikonal quenching parameter for a quark is given by: q̂_Eik = (C_F g⁴ μ̃²)/(4π) { log[1 + 1/(m_g a⊥/π)²] - 1/[1 + (m_g a⊥/π)²] }
- The gluon eikonal quenching parameter replaces C_F with C_A = N_c in the same formula
- The saturation scale is related to the medium parameter as Qs² = C_F (g²μ̃)² Lη/(2π), with C_F = 4/3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous tBLFQ developments from single-quark states (Li 2020) to include |qg⟩ components (Li 2021, Li 2023), providing a non-perturbative alternative to pQCD-based approaches for jet-medium interactions. The analytical derivation of the eikonal expectation value for the quark-gluon state's transverse momentum for any color configuration and arbitrary spatial distribution is novel, and the numerical results show that non-eikonal contributions to momentum broadening are sizable and associated with the saturation scale. This formalism enables studies of jet momentum broadening beyond the eikonal limit and can be extended to include higher Fock states and more realistic medium models.
