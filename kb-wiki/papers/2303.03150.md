# Neutron star mass-radius constraints using the high-frequency QPOs of GRB 200415A

> Quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) observed in a giant flare of a strongly magnetized neutron star (magnetar), are carrying crucial information for extracting the neutron star properties. The aim of the study is to constrain the mass and radius of the neutron star model for GRB 200415A, by identifying the observed QPOs with the crustal torsional oscillations together with the experimental constraints on the nuclear matter properties. The frequencies of the crustal torsional oscillations are determined by solving the eigenvalue problem with the Cowling approximation, assuming a magnetic field of about $10^{15}$G. We find that the observed QPOs can be identified with several overtones of crustal oscillations, for carefully selected combinations of the nuclear saturation parameters. Thus, we can inversely constrain the neutron star mass and radius for GRB 200415A by comparing them to the values of nuclear saturation parameters obtained from terrestrial experiments. We impose further constraints on the neutron star mass and radius while the candidate neutron star models are consistent with the constraints obtained from other available astronomical and experimental observations.

## Key claim

If the high-frequency QPOs observed in GRB 200415A are due to crustal torsional oscillations (rather than Alfvén waves in the magnetosphere), then the neutron star mass and radius can be constrained by identifying the QPOs with specific overtones, provided the magnetic field is approximately 10^15 G or less so that its effect on frequencies is within observational uncertainty.

## Method

The authors solved the eigenvalue problem for crustal torsional oscillations using the relativistic Cowling approximation, assuming a magnetic field of about 10^15 G, for neutron star models constructed with a family of crust equations of state (OI-EOSs) characterized by nuclear saturation parameters K0 and L. They identified the four observed QPO frequencies (836, 1444, 2132, and 4250 Hz) as the 1st, 2nd, 4th, and 10th overtones of crustal torsional oscillations, then used the dependence of overtone frequencies on the combined parameter ζ = (K0^4 L^5)^(1/9) to constrain the mass and radius.

## Key numbers

- The four QPO frequencies observed in GRB 200415A: 836 Hz (with uncertainties −84.7/+77.3 Hz), 1444 Hz, 2132 Hz, and 4250 Hz
- The fiducial range for the nuclear saturation parameters: K0 = 240 ± 20 MeV and L = 60 ± 20 MeV, corresponding to ζ = 85.3–135.1 MeV
- The tighter range from previous magnetar QPO studies: L_QPO = 58–73 MeV, corresponding to ζ_QPO = 104.9–128.4 MeV
- For the neutron star model with M = 1.6 M⊙ and R = 12 km, the simultaneous identification of all four QPOs yields ζ = 121.7 MeV
- The radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star from GW170817 constraints: R_1.4 ≤ 13.6 km, and from multimessenger analysis: R_1.4 = 11.0^(+0.9)_(−0.6) km and R_1.4 = 11.75^(+0.86)_(−0.81) km
- The upper mass limit from PSR J0952-0607: M = 2.35 ± 0.17 M⊙

## Context

This work extends the approach of identifying magnetar QPOs as crustal torsional oscillations, previously applied to SGR 1806-20 and SGR 1900+14 by Sotani et al. (2018), to the newly detected GRB 200415A. The authors use the same OI-EOS family from Oyamatsu and Iida (2003, 2007) and the fitting formula for overtone frequencies as a function of ζ. The resulting mass-radius constraints are compared with those from NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 (Riley et al. 2019; Miller et al. 2019, 2021), gravitational wave constraints from GW170817 (Annala et al. 2018; Bauswein et al. 2017; Capano et al. 2020), and x-ray burst observations. The analysis enables future joint constraints when combined with the η = (K0 L^2)^(1/3) parameter approach for low-mass neutron stars.
