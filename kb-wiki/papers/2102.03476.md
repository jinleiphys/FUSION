# Fermion propagator in a rotating environment

> We apply the exponential operator method to derive the propagator for a fermion immersed within a rigidly rotating environment with cylindrical geometry. Given that the rotation axis provides a preferred direction, Lorentz symmetry is lost and the general solution is not translationally invariant in the radial coordinate. However, under the approximation that the fermion is completely dragged by the vortical motion, valid for large angular velocities, translation invariance is recovered. The propagator can then be written in momentum space. The result is suited to be used applying ordinary Feynman rules for perturbative calculations in momentum space.

## Key claim

Under the approximation that the fermion is completely dragged by the vortical motion (valid for large angular velocities where the angular velocity Ω is much larger than the expansion rate Γ), the fermion propagator in a rigidly rotating cylindrical environment becomes translationally invariant and can be written in momentum space, enabling the use of ordinary Feynman rules for perturbative calculations.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for a fermion of mass m in a rigidly rotating cylindrical geometry using the exponential operator method (proper-time formalism). They first find the eigenfunctions of the second-order differential equation for the scalar field φ(x) in cylindrical coordinates, expressed in terms of Bessel functions of the first kind. The fermion propagator is then constructed from these eigenfunctions via the closure relation, and the sum over angular momentum quantum numbers ℓ is performed using the Jacobi-Anger expansion. Under the "completely dragged" approximation (φ + Ωt = 0), the propagator becomes translationally invariant and is Fourier-transformed to momentum space.

## Key numbers

- The angular velocity in heavy-ion collisions is estimated as Ω ∼ 10²² s⁻¹, based on STAR Collaboration measurements (2017).
- The causality condition requires RΩ < 1, where R is the cylinder radius.
- The propagator is derived under the condition that Ω is much larger than the expansion rate Γ, making Ω the largest intrinsic energy scale.
- The final propagator S(p) contains terms with denominators (p₀ + Ω/2)² − p⃗² − m² + iε and (p₀ − Ω/2)² − p⃗² − m² + iε.

## Context

This work extends the exponential operator method previously applied to charged bosons, fermions, and gauge bosons in magnetic fields (Iablokov 2019, Iablokov 2020) to the case of fermions in a rotating environment. It provides a first-principles alternative to the phenomenological spin-vorticity coupling used in earlier work by Ayala (2020) for explaining Λ and anti-Λ polarization in heavy-ion collisions. The result enables perturbative calculations of spin-dependent processes in rotating QGP using ordinary Feynman rules, with medium effects (temperature and chemical potential) left for future work.
