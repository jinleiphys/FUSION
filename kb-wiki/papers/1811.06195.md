# Anisotropic flow in the few collisions regime: application to bottomonia

> Considering the kinetic Boltzmann equation in the limit of very few collisions, we study the evolution of the phase space distribution of bottomonia interacting with an expanding gas of massless partons. We investigate the scaling of the anisotropic flow coefficients on the initial eccentricities and the inverse Knudsen number, and compute their transverse momentum dependence.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in the limit of very few collisions, the anisotropic flow coefficients of bottomonia scale linearly with the initial eccentricities and the inverse Knudsen number, but only when the system is far from hydrodynamic equilibrium.

## Method
The authors solved the kinetic Boltzmann equation analytically in the few-collisions regime for bottomonia interacting with an expanding gas of massless partons. They computed the phase-space distribution of bottomonia and extracted the anisotropic flow coefficients v_n as functions of transverse momentum, initial eccentricity, and the inverse Knudsen number.

## Key numbers
- The elliptic flow coefficient v_2 scales linearly with the initial eccentricity epsilon_2 and the inverse Knudsen number K^{-1} in the few-collisions regime.
- The triangular flow coefficient v_3 scales linearly with the initial triangular eccentricity epsilon_3 and the inverse Knudsen number K^{-1}.
- The transverse momentum dependence of v_2 and v_3 was computed for bottomonia with transverse momentum up to approximately 10 GeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the kinetic theory approach to anisotropic flow previously applied to light hadrons (such as in the work of Heiselberg and Levy (1999) and Borghini and Ollitrault (2006)) to the case of heavy quarkonia, specifically bottomonia. It enables quantitative predictions for the flow of bottomonia in small collision systems such as p-Pb and p-p collisions at the LHC, where the few-collisions regime is relevant. The results provide a baseline for distinguishing between hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic contributions to bottomonia flow in future experimental measurements.
