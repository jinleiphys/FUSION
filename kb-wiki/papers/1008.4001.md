# Baryon-Baryon Interactions from Lattice QCD

> We report on new attempt to investigate baryon-baryon interactions in lattice QCD. From the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) wave function, we have successfully extracted the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potentials in quenched QCD simulations, which reproduce qualitative features of modern $NN$ potentials. The method has been extended to obtain the tensor potential as well as the central potential and also applied to the hyperon-nucleon ($YN$) interactions, in both quenched and full QCD.

# Baryon-Baryon Interactions from Lattice QCD

## Key claim
The nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential extracted from the Bethe-Salpeter wave function in quenched lattice QCD reproduces qualitative features of modern NN potentials, but only at a pion mass of 529 MeV and a lattice spacing of 0.137 fm, not yet at the physical pion mass of 140 MeV.

## Method
The equal-time Bethe-Salpeter wave function is computed from lattice QCD for two-nucleon eigenstates, and a non-local potential is defined via the relation [E - H0] phi_E(r) = integral U(r,s) phi_E(s) d^3 s. This non-local potential is expanded in derivatives, and the leading local terms, the central potential V_C(r) and tensor potential V_T(r), are extracted from the wave function at a single energy by solving a system of equations involving projections onto L=0 and L=2 states. The method is applied to both quenched QCD and 2+1 flavor full QCD configurations.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a = 0.137 fm and spatial extension L = 4.4 fm for the quenched QCD simulations at pion mass m_pi = 529 MeV.
- Central potentials compared at three pion masses: m_pi = 380, 529, and 731 MeV in quenched QCD at a = 0.137 fm.
- Energy dependence tested at k approximately 0 MeV and k approximately 250 MeV for the ^1S_0 state at m_pi = 529 MeV.
- Full QCD calculations use 2+1 flavor configurations at a = 0.09 fm and L = 2.9 fm, with m_pi = 702 MeV for the NN potentials and m_pi approximately 400 and 700 MeV for the Lambda-N potentials.
- The tensor potential V_T shows no strong repulsive core, in contrast to the central potential, at m_pi = 529 MeV in quenched QCD.

## Context
This work extends the approach of extracting nuclear potentials from lattice QCD first reported in IAH1 (2007), which was selected as one of 21 papers in Nature Research Highlights 2007. The method builds on the Bethe-Salpeter wave function formalism used in earlier studies of NN scattering (Ishizuka, AHI2). The results contradict the assumption that the repulsive core requires phenomenological modeling, as it emerges directly from QCD. The approach enables first-principles determination of hyperon-nucleon interactions (Nemura, 2009), which cannot be measured precisely in scattering experiments due to hyperon short lifetimes, and opens the path to extracting three-body forces from QCD and understanding the origin of the repulsive core.
