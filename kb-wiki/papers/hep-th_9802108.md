# SU(2) Calorons and Magnetic Monopoles

> We investigate the self-dual Yang-Mills gauge configurations on $R^3\times S^1$ when the gauge symmetry SU(2) is broken to U(1) by the Wilson loop. We construct the explicit field configuration for a single instanton by the Nahm method and show that an instanton is composed of two self-dual monopoles of opposite magnetic charge. We normalize the moduli space metric of an instanton and study various limits of the field configuration and its moduli space metric.

## Key claim

A single SU(2) caloron on R³ × S¹ with nontrivial Wilson loop is explicitly shown to be composed of two distinct fundamental magnetic monopoles of opposite magnetic charge, but only when the asymptotic value of A₄ lies in the interval 0 ≤ u ≤ 2π/β and the gauge symmetry is broken from SU(2) to U(1).

## Method

The authors use the Nahm construction to build the self-dual gauge field configuration for a single instanton on R³ × S¹. They solve the ADHMN equations with Nahm data corresponding to two distinct fundamental monopoles placed on the z-axis, imposing periodic boundary conditions and jumping conditions at the boundaries t = ±u/2. The resulting gauge field is expressed as a combination of ordinary BPS monopole solutions with gauge transformations that encode the superposition of the two constituent monopoles.

## Key numbers

- The topological charges of the two monopole types are k₁ = βu/(2π) and k₂ = 1 − βu/(2π).
- The masses of the two magnetic monopoles are m₁ = 4πu and m₂ = 4π(2π/β − u).
- The distance between the two monopoles D is related to the instanton scale parameter ρ by D = πρ²/β in the massless monopole limit.
- The relative moduli space metric has length parameter r₀ = β/(2πk₁k₂).
- The center-of-mass moduli space metric is ds²_cm = 8π²(dR² + β²/(4π²) dχ²).
- The relative moduli space metric is ds²_rel = 8π² k₁k₂ [(1 + r₀/r) dr² + r₀²(1 + r₀/r)⁻¹ (dψ + w·dr)²].

## Context

This work extends the Nahm construction approach to calorons on R³ × S¹, building on earlier arguments by Piljin (1997) and Kimyeong (1997) that instantons are composite objects made of magnetic monopoles. It differs from earlier caloron solutions by Rossi (1979) and Pisarski (1980) by incorporating a nontrivial Wilson loop, which allows the constituent monopole structure to appear explicitly. The results enable further study of the zero-temperature limit of calorons and may provide insight into chiral symmetry breaking and confinement in QCD through the abelian projection approach of 't Hooft (1981).
