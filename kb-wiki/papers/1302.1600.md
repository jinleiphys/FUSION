# Broken Chiral Symmetry on a Null Plane

> On a null-plane (light-front), all effects of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking are contained in the three Hamiltonians (dynamical Poincaré generators), while the vacuum state is a chiral invariant. This property is used to give a general proof of Goldstone's theorem on a null-plane. Focusing on null-plane QCD with N degenerate flavors of light quarks, the chiral-symmetry breaking Hamiltonians are obtained, and the role of vacuum condensates is clarified. In particular, the null-plane Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner formula is derived, and a general prescription is given for mapping all chiral-symmetry breaking QCD condensates to chiral-symmetry conserving null-plane QCD condensates. The utility of the null-plane description lies in the operator algebra that mixes the null-plane Hamiltonians and the chiral symmetry charges. It is demonstrated that in a certain non-trivial limit, the null-plane operator algebra reduces to the symmetry group SU(2N) of the constituent quark model.

## Key claim

On a null-plane (light-front), spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking is entirely encoded in the three dynamical Poincaré generators (the Hamiltonians), while the vacuum state remains invariant under chiral transformations; this is in contrast to the instant-form picture where symmetry breaking resides in a non-trivial vacuum, and the null-plane axial-vector current cannot be conserved if spontaneous breaking occurs.

## Method

The paper formulates QCD with N degenerate light-quark flavors on a null-plane, expressing the QCD Lagrangian in null-plane coordinates and deriving the chiral-symmetry breaking Hamiltonians. It then uses the commutation relations between the null-plane chiral charges and the reduced Hamiltonians (mass-squared and spin operators) to prove Goldstone's theorem on a null-plane, and recovers the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation by imposing form-invariance of the quark-mass-dependent vacuum energy. A general prescription is given for mapping instant-form chiral-symmetry breaking condensates to chiral-singlet null-plane condensates, and the operator algebra is solved in a non-trivial limit to reveal the SU(2N) symmetry of the constituent quark model.

## Key numbers

- The null-plane dispersion relation for a relativistic particle of mass m is p^- = (p_perp^2 + m^2) / (2 p^+), where p^+ > 0 for massive particles.
- The null-plane Poincaré algebra has seven kinematical generators (the stability group) and three dynamical generators (P^- and F_r), the largest stability group of any form of dynamics (Dirac 1949).
- The reduced Hamiltonians (M^2 and M J_r) together with J_3 satisfy the algebra of U(2): [J_3, M J_r] = i epsilon_{rs} M J_s, [M J_r, M J_s] = i epsilon_{rs} M^2 J_3, and [M^2, M J_r] = 0.
- The null-plane Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner formula is derived, relating the pion mass squared to the product of the quark mass and the chiral condensate, with the explicit form given by the standard relation m_pi^2 = -2 m_q <qbar q> / f_pi^2 (recovered in the front-form).
- In the non-trivial limit where the chiral-symmetry breaking part of the null-plane energy is small (parametrically the case for baryon operators at large N_c), the null-plane operator algebra reduces to the Lie brackets of SU(2N), recovering the group-theoretical structure of the constituent quark model (originally found by Weinberg 1994).

## Context

This work extends the front-form (light-front) approach to chiral symmetry breaking pioneered by Dirac (1949), Weinberg (1966), and Leutwyler (1977), and builds on earlier studies of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking on null-planes by Jersak (1969), Leutwyler (1969), and Weinberg (1969). It provides a model-independent formulation of chiral symmetry breaking in QCD on a null-plane, clarifying the role of vacuum condensates and showing that all instant-form chiral-symmetry breaking condensates can be mapped to chiral-singlet null-plane condensates. The results enable future work on the operator algebra that mixes null-plane Hamiltonians and chiral charges, and demonstrate that the SU(2N) spin-flavor symmetry of the constituent quark model emerges from the null-plane QCD Lie algebraic constraints in a specific limit.
