# Isospin dependence of nucleon effective masses in neutron-rich matter

> In this talk, we first briefly review the isospin dependence of the total nucleon effective mass $M^{\ast}_{J}$ inferred from analyzing nucleon-nucleus scattering data within an isospin dependent non-relativistic optical potential model, and the isospin dependence of the nucleon E-mass $M^{\ast,\rm{E}}_{J}$ obtained from applying the Migdal-Luttinger theorem to a phenomenological single-nucleon momentum distribution in nuclei constrained by recent electron-nucleus scattering experiments. Combining information about the isospin dependence of both the nucleon total effective mass and E-mass, we then infer the isospin dependence of nucleon k-mass using the well-known relation $M^{\ast}_{J}=M^{\ast,\rm{E}}_{J}\cdot M^{\ast,\rm{k}}_{J}$. Implications of the results on the nucleon mean free path (MFP) in neutron-rich matter are discussed.

## Key claim
The authors infer the isospin dependence of the nucleon k-mass at saturation density by combining constraints on the total effective mass from nucleon-nucleus scattering data and on the E-mass from electron-nucleus scattering data via the Migdal-Luttinger theorem, finding that the k-mass splitting is significantly larger than the total effective mass splitting, though the extracted k-mass in symmetric nuclear matter is substantially smaller than earlier empirical estimates.

## Method
The total effective mass splitting was extracted from a global optical model analysis of 2249 data sets of neutron and proton scattering on 234 targets at beam energies from 0.05 to 200 MeV. The E-mass was obtained by applying the Migdal-Luttinger theorem to a phenomenological single-nucleon momentum distribution constrained by electron-nucleus scattering experiments measuring short-range correlations and by Tan's adiabatic sweep theorem applied to pure neutron matter equations of state. The k-mass and its splitting were then inferred using the relation M*_J = M*_J^E * M*_J^k.

## Key numbers
- Isoscalar effective mass from nucleon-nucleus scattering: m*_0/m = 0.65 ± 0.06 at saturation density.
- Neutron-proton total effective mass splitting: m*_{n-p} = (0.41 ± 0.15)δ at saturation density.
- E-mass in symmetric nuclear matter: M_0^{*,E}/M ≈ 2.22 ± 0.35 at saturation density.
- Linear splitting function for E-mass: s_E ≈ -2.22 ± 1.35.
- Linear splitting function for k-mass: s_k ≈ 0.50 ± 0.24.
- k-mass in symmetric nuclear matter: m_0^{*,k} ≈ 0.32 ± 0.07.

## Context
This work extends earlier analyses of nucleon-nucleus scattering (Hama et al., 1990; Koning and Delaroche, 2003) and electron-nucleus scattering experiments at Jefferson Laboratory (Hen et al., 2014, 2015) that constrained short-range correlations. It enables improved predictions of the nucleon mean free path in neutron-rich matter, which is relevant for heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical environments such as neutron stars and supernova neutrino spheres, and it connects the effective mass splitting to the symmetry energy and its slope L(ρ_0) as derived from the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem.
