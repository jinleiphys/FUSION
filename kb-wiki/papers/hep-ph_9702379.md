# A Study of Off-Forward Parton Distributions

> An extensive theoretical analysis of off-forward parton distributions (OFPDs) is presented. The OFPDs and the form factors of the quark energy-momentum tensor are estimated at a low-energy scale using a bag model. Relations among the second moments of OFPDs, the form factors, and the fraction of the nucleon spin carried by quarks are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a first detailed theoretical estimate of off-forward parton distributions (OFPDs) using the MIT bag model, but the authors explicitly note that the model has well-known problems including breaking of chiral symmetry and translational invariance, and that the results should be considered a "reasonable first guess" at a low energy scale of about 0.2 GeV² rather than a definitive prediction.

## Method

The authors calculate the OFPDs by evaluating matrix elements of bilocal quark operators between nucleon states in the Breit frame, using boosted MIT bag model wave functions with spectator quark effects included via a factor Z(t). To handle the momentum mismatch inherent in independent particle models, they introduce a parameter η that modifies the effective momentum transfer through the active quark, with η determined by fitting the calculated electromagnetic form factors to experimental data. The four independent chiral-even distributions H, E, ̃H, and ̃E are extracted by solving two independent combinations from different spin components of the matrix elements for both the unpolarized (γ^μ) and polarized (γ^μγ_5) cases.

## Key numbers

- Bag radius fixed by R = 4ω_0/M, where ω_0 = 2.04 is the lowest frequency solution of the bag eigenequation
- The parameter η takes values 0.35 and 0.55; small-|t| form factor data favor η = 0.55, while large-|t| data favor η = 0.35
- At t = 0, the first moment of the flavor singlet ̃E is G_P(0) ≈ 6 for η = 0.55
- The axial coupling for a quark in the model is g_A = 0.65
- Distributions are shown at t = -1 and t = -2 GeV² for both u and d quarks
- The authors expect H(x,ξ,t) and ̃H(x,ξ,t) to fall like 1/t² as -t → ∞

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for OFPDs introduced by Ji (1997) and independently by Geyer and collaborators (late 1980s) and Jain and Ralston (early 1990s), providing the first model calculation using the MIT bag model which had previously been applied to electromagnetic form factors by Betz and collaborators and to parton distributions by Jaffe and Ji. The results enable future experimental planning for deeply-virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) at facilities such as ELFE, HERMES at HERA, and Jefferson Lab, and provide input for understanding the fraction of nucleon spin carried by quark orbital angular momentum.
