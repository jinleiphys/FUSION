# Convexity of the entanglement energy of SU($2N$)-symmetric fermions with attractive interactions

> The positivity of the probability measure of attractively interacting systems of $2N$-component fermions enables the derivation of an exact convexity property for the ground-state energy of such systems. Using analogous arguments, applied to path-integral expressions for the $n$-th Rényi entanglement entropy $S^{}_n$ derived recently, we prove non-perturbative analytic relations for the entanglement energies $\mathcal E^{}_n$ of those systems defined via $\mathcal{E}^{}_n \equiv \frac{n-1}{n}T S^{}_n + F$ where $β= 1/T$ is the extent of the imaginary time direction and $-βF = \ln \mathcal Z$ where $\mathcal Z$ is the partition sum appropriate to the temperature. These relations are valid for all sub-system sizes, particle numbers and dimensions, and in arbitrary external trapping potentials.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the entanglement energy $\mathcal{E}_n$ (defined via the $n$-th Rényi entropy) of SU($2N$)-symmetric fermions with attractive interactions is a convex function of particle number, for all sub-system sizes, particle numbers, dimensions, and external trapping potentials. The proof relies on the positivity of the probability measure in the auxiliary-field path integral, which holds only for attractive interactions and an even number of fermion components.

## Method

The authors combine a path-integral expression for the Rényi entanglement entropy derived by Grover (2013) with a convexity theorem originally proven by D. Lee (2009) for SU($2N$)-symmetric fermions. They place the system on a $d$-dimensional spatial lattice and use the projection auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) method with a trial Slater-determinant state. By applying Hölder's inequality to the path-integral representation of $\exp((1-n)S_n)$, they derive an inequality showing that the entanglement energy $\mathcal{E}_n$ is convex when the particle number is varied between $2NK$ and $2N(K+1)$ for any integer $K$.

## Key numbers

* The convexity holds for particle numbers between $2NK$ and $2N(K+1)$ for any integer $K$, where $2N$ is the number of fermion species.
* For $K=0$, the convexity holds for all particle numbers $0 \leq N_\text{part} \leq 2N$.
* The result is valid for all Rényi orders $n > 1$.
* The system has $2N$ species of fermions, with $N$ being a positive integer (e.g., SU(6) and SU(10) symmetries are mentioned as experimentally realizable with Yb and Sr isotopes).

## Context

This work extends the convexity property previously proven by D. Lee (2009) for the ground-state energy of SU($2N$)-symmetric fermions to the entanglement energy, using the path-integral formalism for Rényi entropies developed by Grover (2013). The results are applicable to the attractive Hubbard model in arbitrary dimensions and to ultracold atom systems with SU($2N$) symmetry, such as Yb and Sr isotopes. The paper suggests that future work could determine whether real nuclei respect this convexity property, given the approximate SU(4) symmetry in nuclear structure.
