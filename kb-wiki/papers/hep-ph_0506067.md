# The Decay Sigma^+ -> p l^+ l^- within the Standard Model

> The HyperCP collaboration has recently reported the observation of three events for the decay Sigma^+ -> p mu^+ mu^-. They have suggested that new physics may be required to understand the implied decay rate and the observed M_{mumu} distribution. Motivated by this result, we re-examine this mode within the standard model, considering both the short-distance and long-distance contributions. The long-distance part depends on four complex form-factors. We determine their imaginary parts from unitarity, fix two of the real parts from the Sigma^+ -> p gamma measurements, and estimate the other two with vector-meson-dominance models. Taking into account constraints from Sigma^+ -> p e^+ e^-, we find that Sigma^+ -> p mu^+ mu^- is long-distance dominated and its rate falls within the range suggested by the HyperCP measurement.

## Key claim

The standard model can explain the HyperCP observation of \(\Sigma^+ \to p \mu^+ \mu^-\) via long-distance contributions, without requiring new physics, provided the predicted branching ratio range of \(1.6\times10^{-8}\) to \(9.0\times10^{-8}\) is considered; the abstract's claim that the rate "falls within the range suggested by the HyperCP measurement" glosses over the fact that this agreement depends on a specific set of form-factor solutions and that a new-physics interpretation remains possible.

## Method

The authors computed the short-distance contribution using the effective Hamiltonian for \(s \to d \ell^+ \ell^-\) transitions with hadronic matrix elements from chiral perturbation theory. They then calculated the long-distance contribution mediated by a virtual photon, parameterized by four complex form factors \(a, b, c, d\); the imaginary parts were determined from unitarity using \(\Sigma^+ \to N\pi\) decay amplitudes and \(N\pi \to p\gamma^*\) reactions in both relativistic and heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, while the real parts of \(a\) and \(b\) at \(q^2=0\) were extracted from \(\Sigma^+ \to p\gamma\) data and the real parts of \(c\) and \(d\) were estimated using vector-meson-dominance models.

## Key numbers

- Short-distance contribution to \(\Sigma^+ \to p \mu^+ \mu^-\) branching ratio: \(\sim 10^{-12}\), far below the HyperCP central value of \(8.6 \times 10^{-8}\).
- Measured \(\Sigma^+ \to p\gamma\) width: \((10.1 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-15}\) MeV; asymmetry parameter \(\alpha = -0.76 \pm 0.08\).
- From \(\Sigma^+ \to p\gamma\) data: \(|a(0)|^2 + |b(0)|^2 = (15.0 \pm 0.3)^2\) MeV\(^2\); \(\text{Re}(a(0) b^*(0)) = (-85.3 \pm 9.6)\) MeV\(^2\).
- Relativistic \(\chi\)PT imaginary parts at \(q^2=0\): \(\text{Im}\,a(0) = 2.84\) MeV, \(\text{Im}\,b(0) = -1.83\) MeV.
- Heavy-baryon \(\chi\)PT imaginary parts at \(q^2=0\): \(\text{Im}\,a(0) = 6.84\) MeV, \(\text{Im}\,b(0) = -0.54\) MeV.
- Predicted standard-model range for \(\mathcal{B}(\Sigma^+ \to p \mu^+ \mu^-)\): \(1.6 \times 10^{-8}\) to \(9.0 \times 10^{-8}\).
- Predicted standard-model range for \(\mathcal{B}(\Sigma^+ \to p e^+ e^-)\): \(9.1 \times 10^{-6}\) to \(10.1 \times 10^{-6}\).
- HyperCP measured \(\mathcal{B}(\Sigma^+ \to p \mu^+ \mu^-) = (8.6^{+6.6}_{-5.4} \pm 5.5) \times 10^{-8}\).
- Experimental \(\mathcal{B}(\Sigma^+ \to p e^+ e^-)\) inferred from Ref. ang: \((7.7 \pm 4.6) \times 10^{-6}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier standard-model estimates of \(\Sigma^+ \to p \ell^+ \ell^-\) by Bergstrom (1987) and provides an updated calculation motivated by the HyperCP observation (Park, 2005). It uses chiral perturbation theory results from Neufeld (1992) and Jenkins (1992) for the imaginary parts of form factors, and vector-meson-dominance to estimate the real parts of \(c\) and \(d\). The analysis enables future experimental comparisons to distinguish between the standard-model long-distance interpretation and the new-physics hypothesis of a \(\sim 214\) MeV particle suggested by HyperCP.
