# Symplectic dynamics of the nuclear mean-field

> Elements of a restricted variational approach to the nuclear mean-field dynamics, providing the microscopic structure of the low energy collective modes, are summarized. The results are illustrated for the isovector states produced by the proton-neutron interactions in deformed and superfluid nuclei.

# Symplectic dynamics of the nuclear mean-field

## Key claim
The paper argues that the standard random phase approximation (RPA) is a limit case of a more general symplectic variational formalism on trial manifolds, where the collective phase space is constructed by cranking rather than by arbitrary particle-hole operators; the key hedge is that the "requantization" by the Bohr-Wilson-Sommerfeld condition (Eq. 2) is strongly supported by physical applications "though its geometrical meaning is not yet completely clear."

## Method
The author applies a time-dependent variational principle on symplectic trial manifolds constructed from cranked mean-field states, using the gauge-invariant periodic quantization (GIPQ) method to requantize the resulting classical trajectories. For deformed nuclei, the trial manifold is built from rotated cranked Hartree-Fock states for protons and neutrons separately, yielding a two-rotor Hamiltonian; for superfluid nuclei, the trial manifold uses BCS states parameterized by gauge angle and particle number, producing isovector Josephson oscillations. The formalism is shown to reduce to standard RPA when the trial manifold is generated by all possible particle-hole or particle-particle operators.

## Key numbers
- The isovector scissors mode excitation energy is given by ℏΩ_χ = ℏ√(C_χ/I_r), where C_χ ≈ 9δ²A MeV (from the microscopic quadrupole-quadrupole interaction) and I_r is the reduced moment of inertia.
- The B(M1) strength for the scissors mode is B(M1) = (3/4πℏ)(g_p−g_n)² I_r D μ_N², where D = |δ|ω₀.
- The isovector Josephson oscillation excitation energy is E_σ = ℏΩ_σ = 4√(2k_W C_σ), with C_σ = 2σ(Δ/G)² and the symmetry energy coefficient k_W = 28/A MeV.
- The proton-neutron pairing interaction strength σ was estimated as ≈ 2.7/A MeV for 1d₃/₂ shell nuclei, with a suggestion that high-degeneracy j-shells give values about ten times larger.
- The spherical oscillator frequency is ω₀ = 41 A^{-1/3} MeV/ℏ.

## Context
This work extends the cranking method beyond its standard use for calculating moments of inertia, building on the GIPQ requantization approach of Grigorescu (1995) and the symplectic trial manifold construction of Grigorescu (1996). It provides a microscopic foundation for the two-rotor model (TRM) of scissors vibrations proposed by Iudice and Palumbo, and for the phenomenological interacting boson model (IBA-II) of Iachello. The formalism enables the prediction of isovector Josephson oscillations in superfluid nuclei, which may be observable in pion double charge-exchange reactions as suggested by shell-model calculations of Auerbach (1983). The approach also connects to the symplectic sp(3,R) algebra of Rowe and the gl(3,R) excitation operators proposed by Hilton.
