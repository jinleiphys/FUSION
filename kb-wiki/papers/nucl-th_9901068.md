# H-Dihyperon in Quark Cluster Model

> The H dihyperon (DH) is studied in the framework of the SU(3) chiral quark model. It is shown that except the $σ$ chiral field, the overall effect of the other SU(3) chiral fields is destructive in forming a stable DH. The resultant mass of DH in a three coupled channel calculation is ranged from 2225 $MeV$ to 2234 $MeV$.

## Key claim
The H dihyperon mass is predicted to lie in a narrow range of 2225–2234 MeV when all SU(3) chiral fields are included in a three-coupled-channel calculation, but only if the model parameters simultaneously reproduce experimental NN and NY scattering data. The abstract’s range is conditional: the overall effect of the non-σ chiral fields is destructive for forming a stable dihyperon, and the bound state only appears when the σ field alone is included without the other chiral fields.

## Method
The six-quark system with strangeness -2, J^P = 0^+ (S=0, T=0) was studied in two configuration spaces: a pure six-quark cluster with (0s)^6 orbital wave functions including breath modes, and a two-cluster Resonating Group Method (RGM) space with three coupled channels (ΛΛ, NΞ, ΣΣ). The Hamiltonian included confinement, one-gluon-exchange (OGE), pseudoscalar chiral fields (π, K, η_8, η_0), and scalar chiral fields (σ', κ, ε, σ). Variational calculations were performed after eliminating forbidden degrees of freedom via off-shell transformation.

## Key numbers
- In the six-quark cluster case with OGE + confinement + π + σ fields, the H dihyperon mass is 127 MeV above the ΛΛ threshold (i.e., E_H = +127 MeV).
- In the two-cluster case with OGE + confinement + π + σ fields, the three-channel (ΛΛ + NΞ + ΣΣ) calculation gives a binding energy of 65.80 MeV below the ΛΛ threshold (E_H = -65.80 MeV), with a root-mean-squared radius of 0.72 fm.
- In the two-cluster case with all SU(3) chiral fields (OGE + confinement + V^PS + V^S), the three-channel calculation gives E_H = +1.98 MeV (no bound state), with a root-mean-squared radius of 1.41 fm.
- The final predicted mass range for the H dihyperon, when all SU(3) chiral fields are included and parameters fit NN and NY data, is 2225 MeV to 2234 MeV.
- The lower and upper bounds from parameter variation are 2223 MeV and 2237 MeV, respectively.

## Context
This work extends the SU(3) chiral quark model, previously applied to NN scattering phase shifts and NY cross sections by Zhang et al. (1997), to the strangeness -2 sector. It contradicts earlier predictions of a deeply bound H dihyperon from the MIT bag model (Jaffe, 1977) and the Skyrme model (Balachandran et al.), which gave binding energies of ~80 MeV and several hundred MeV, respectively. It is consistent with the experimental lower limit of ~2200 MeV from Imai et al. (1991) and supports the conclusion of Glozman et al. that no bound H dihyperon exists when chiral symmetry is properly treated. The results enable future studies of double-strangeness hypernuclei and the short-range behavior of QCD in multiquark systems.
