# Anisotropic hydrodynamics, holography and the chiral magnetic effect

> We discuss a possible dependence of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) on the elliptic flow coefficient v2. We first study this in a hydrodynamic model for a static anisotropic plasma with multiple anomalous U(1) currents. In the case of two charges, one axial and one vector, the CME formally appears as a first-order transport coefficient in the vector current. We compute this transport coefficient and show its dependence on v2. We also determine the CME-coefficient from first-order corrections to the dual AdS background using the fluid-gravity duality. For small anisotropies, we find numerical agreement with the hydrodynamic result.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) coefficient κ_B depends linearly on the elliptic flow coefficient v₂ for small momentum anisotropies, with the dependence arising from a dynamical term proportional to ρ/(ε+P_T) in the hydrodynamic expression, rather than from the leading anomaly term Cμ₅ alone.

## Method

The authors first derived the CME transport coefficient κ_B from anisotropic hydrodynamics with multiple anomalous U(1) currents, extending the Son-Surowka method to include a spacelike anisotropy vector v^μ and unequal transverse and longitudinal pressures. They then constructed a static anisotropic AdS black hole background with metric functions w_T(r) and w_L(r) using numerical shooting techniques, and computed κ_B from first-order corrections to this background via the fluid-gravity duality, comparing the result to the hydrodynamic prediction for small anisotropies.

## Key numbers

- The CME coefficient in the anisotropic case is κ_B = C μ₅ (1 − μρ/(ε+P_T)), where P_T is the transverse pressure; expanding to linear order in ε_p gives κ_B ≈ C μ₅ (1 − μρ/(ε+P̄)[1 − ε_p/6]), with ε_p = ⟨P_T − P_L⟩/⟨P_T + P_L⟩.
- At freeze-out, the elliptic flow coefficient v₂ ≈ ε_p/2, so for small momentum anisotropies the CME increases linearly in v₂.
- The relation between the momentum anisotropy parameter ζ and ε_p is ζ = 2ε_p/(ε_p+3), and the transverse and longitudinal pressures are P_T = m(1+ζ)/(16πG₅) and P_L = m(1−2ζ)/(16πG₅).
- The charge density is ρ^a = √3 q^a/(16πG₅), and the ratio ρ^a/(ε+P_T) = √3 q^a/[4m(1+ζ/4)].

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic treatment of the CME from isotropic fluids (Son and Surowka 2009; Kirsch et al. 2011) to anisotropic plasmas relevant for heavy-ion collisions, where the STAR collaboration (Wang 2012) observed a proportionality between charge separation and v₂. The hydrodynamic model builds on anisotropic fluid descriptions by Ryblewski and Florkowski (2011), while the holographic computation extends the isotropic STU model analysis of Kirsch et al. (2011) and uses numerical methods similar to those of Erdmenger et al. (2011) for anisotropic AdS backgrounds. The results enable quantitative predictions for the v₂-dependence of CME observables in off-central heavy-ion collisions and provide a testable relation between transport coefficients and flow anisotropy.
