# Third family of compact stars within a nonlocal chiral quark model equation of state

> A class of hybrid compact star equations of state is investigated that joins by a Maxwell construction a low-density phase of hadronic matter, modeled by a relativistic meanfield approach with excluded nucleon volume, with a high-density phase of color superconducting two-flavor quark matter, described within a nonlocal covariant chiral quark model. We find the conditions on the vector meson coupling in the quark model under which a stable branch of hybrid compact stars occurs in the cases with and without diquark condensation. We show that these hybrid stars do not form a third family disconnected from the second family of ordinary neutron stars unless additional (de)confining effects are introduced with a density-dependent bag pressure. A suitably chosen density dependence of the vector meson coupling assures that at the same time the $2~$M$_\odot$ maximum mass constraint is fulfilled on the hybrid star branch. A twofold interpolation method is realized which implements both, the density dependence of a confining bag pressure at the onset of the hadron-to-quark matter transition as well as the stiffening of quark matter at higher densities by a density-dependent vector meson coupling. For three parametrizations of this class of hybrid equation of state the properties of corresponding compact star sequences are presented, including mass twins of neutron and hybrid stars at 2.00, 1.39 and 1.20 $M_\odot$, respectively. The sensitivity of the hybrid equation of state and the corresponding compact star sequences to variations of the interpolation parameters at the 10% level is investigated and it is found that the feature of third family solutions for compact stars is robust against such a variation. This advanced description of hybrid star matter allows to interpret GW170817 as a merger not only of two neutron stars but also of a neutron star with a hybrid star or of two hybrid stars.

## Key claim

Hybrid compact star equations of state constructed via a Maxwell construction between a hadronic phase (relativistic mean-field with excluded nucleon volume) and a color-superconducting quark phase (nonlocal chiral quark model) do **not** produce a disconnected third family of compact stars (and thus no mass twins) unless additional density-dependent (de)confining effects, such as a density-dependent bag pressure, are introduced. The existence of a third family is robust against 10% variations of the interpolation parameters.

## Method

The authors join a hadronic matter equation of state (DD2p40, a relativistic mean-field model with excluded nucleon volume) to a quark matter equation of state from a nonlocal covariant chiral quark model (including scalar, vector, and diquark interactions) using a Maxwell construction at zero temperature. They then introduce a twofold interpolation method that implements both a density-dependent bag pressure (to mimic confinement) and a density-dependent vector meson coupling (to stiffen quark matter at high densities). The resulting hybrid equations of state are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius sequences, and the sensitivity of the third family feature to 10% variations of the interpolation parameters is tested.

## Key numbers

*   Mass twins (neutron star and hybrid star of equal mass) are obtained at 2.00, 1.39, and 1.20 solar masses for three parametrizations of the hybrid equation of state.
*   The maximum mass constraint of 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses (from PSR J0348+0432, Antoniadis 2013) is fulfilled on the hybrid star branch.
*   The sensitivity of the hybrid equation of state and compact star sequences to variations of the interpolation parameters at the 10% level is investigated, and the third family feature is found to be robust.
*   For the case without color superconductivity, hybrid star sequences fulfilling the upper limit for the maximum mass (2.16 ± 0.04 solar masses, Rezzolla 2017) are possible for vector coupling strengths 0.05 < η < 0.07 with either the DD2 or DD2p40 hadronic equation of state.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of third family compact stars that used schematic equations of state like the bag model (Glendenning 1998), the constant-speed-of-sound model (Alford 2013, Alvarez-Castillo 2013), or the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Benic 2014) by employing a more QCD-motivated nonlocal chiral quark model with dynamical confinement. The results enable the interpretation of the GW170817 merger event as not only a binary neutron star merger but also a merger of a neutron star with a hybrid star or two hybrid stars, and they provide a framework for Bayesian analyses of hybrid equations of state constrained by heavy-ion collision data and compact star observations.
