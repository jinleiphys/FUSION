# Beta decays as sensitive probes of lepton flavor universality

> Nuclear $β$ decays as well as the decay of the neutron are well-established low-energy probes of physics beyond the Standard Model (SM). In particular, with the axial-vector coupling of the nucleon $g_A$ determined from lattice QCD, the comparison between experiment and SM prediction is commonly used to derive constraints on right-handed currents. Further, in addition to the CKM element $V_{us}$ from kaon decays, $V_{ud}$ from $β$ decays is a critical input for the test of CKM unitarity. Here, we point out that the available information on $β$ decays can be re-interpreted as a stringent test of lepton flavor universality (LFU). In fact, we find that the ratio of $V_{us}$ from kaon decays over $V_{us}$ from $β$ decays (assuming CKM unitarity) is extremely sensitive to LFU violation (LFUV) in $W$-$μ$-$ν$ couplings thanks to a CKM enhancement by $(V_{ud}/V_{us})^2\sim 20$. From this perspective, recent hints for the violation of CKM unitarity can be viewed as further evidence for LFUV, fitting into the existing picture exhibited by semi-leptonic $B$ decays and the anomalous magnetic moments of muon and electron. Finally, we comment on the future sensitivity that can be reached with this LFU violating observable and discuss complementary probes of LFU that may reach a similar level of precision, such as $Γ(π\toμν)/Γ(π\to eν)$ at the PEN and PiENu experiments or even direct measurements of $W\toμν$ at an FCC-ee.

# Beta decays as sensitive probes of lepton flavor universality

## Key claim
The ratio of $V_{us}$ from kaon decays over $V_{us}$ from beta decays (assuming CKM unitarity) provides a stringent test of lepton flavor universality (LFU) in $W$-$\mu$-$\nu$ couplings, with a CKM enhancement factor of $(V_{ud}/V_{us})^2 \sim 20$. Current data show a deviation of this ratio from unity at the 2-3 sigma level, depending on the choice of radiative corrections, which the authors suggest may be interpreted as further evidence for LFU violation rather than a violation of CKM unitarity.

## Method
The authors re-interpret existing determinations of $V_{ud}$ from superallowed nuclear $0^+ \to 0^+$ beta decays and $V_{us}$ from kaon decays ($K_{\mu 2}$ and $K_{\ell 3}$) as a test of LFU. They parameterize beyond-Standard-Model effects in $W$-$\ell$-$\nu$ couplings via small corrections $\varepsilon_{ij}$, showing that the Fermi constant extracted from muon decay absorbs a factor $(1+\varepsilon_{ee}+\varepsilon_{\mu\mu})$, which propagates into beta decay observables and generates sensitivity to $\varepsilon_{\mu\mu}$ with a CKM enhancement. The observable $R(V_{us}) = V_{us}^{K_{\mu 2}} / V_{us}^{\beta}$ is constructed, where $V_{us}^{\beta}$ is derived from $V_{ud}^{\beta}$ assuming CKM unitarity.

## Context
This work extends the interpretation of beta decays beyond their traditional use for constraining CKM unitarity and right-handed currents (as done by Hardy and Towner, 2018, and Czarnecki et al., 2019) to a probe of lepton flavor universality. The authors connect their analysis to existing hints for LFU violation from semi-leptonic $B$ decays ($R(D^{(*)})$ and $R(K^{(*)})$ deviations) and the anomalous magnetic moments of muon and electron. The proposed observable $R(V_{us})$ is complementary to other LFU tests such as $\Gamma(\pi \to \mu\nu)/\Gamma(\pi \to e\nu)$ (to be improved by PEN and PiENu experiments) and $\tau \to \mu\nu\bar{\nu}/\tau \to e\nu\bar{\nu}$ (at Belle II), and could be further tested by direct $W \to \mu\nu$ measurements at a future FCC-ee. The analysis uses two sets of radiative corrections (SGPR from Seng et al., 2018, and CMS from Czarnecki et al., 2019) to illustrate the current spread in results.
