# Melting of P wave bottomonium states in the quark-gluon plasma from lattice NRQCD

> We study the fate of P wave bottomonium states in the quark-gluon plasma, using a spectral function analysis of euclidean lattice correlators. The correlators are obtained from lattice QCD simulations with two light quark flavours on highly anisotropic lattices, treating the bottom quark nonrelativistically. We find clear indications of melting immediately after the deconfinement transition.

# Melting of P wave bottomonium states in the quark-gluon plasma from lattice NRQCD

## Key claim

P wave bottomonium states (χb and hb) melt in the quark-gluon plasma immediately after the deconfinement transition, in contrast to S wave states (Υ and ηb) which survive up to the highest temperature studied (2.09 Tc). The conclusion is based on spectral function analysis of Euclidean lattice correlators, with the hedge that results were obtained on ensembles with a single lattice spacing and systematic effects related to lattice spacing dependence have not yet been addressed.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two light quark flavours (Nf=2) on highly anisotropic lattices (as/aτ=6, as ≃ 0.162 fm, aτ−1 = 7.35(3) GeV), treating the bottom quark nonrelativistically using NRQCD. They computed Euclidean correlators for P wave channels (3P0, 3P1, 3P2, and 1P1) at seven temperatures from 0.42 Tc to 2.09 Tc, and extracted spectral functions via the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) using a constant default model, with the time interval chosen to exclude the final time slice to avoid thermal lattice artefacts.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: Ns3 × Nτ with Ns = 12, Nτ = 80, 32, 28, 24, 20, 18, 16 corresponding to temperatures T = 90, 230, 263, 306, 368, 408, 458 MeV and T/Tc = 0.42, 1.05, 1.20, 1.40, 1.68, 1.86, 2.09
- Anisotropy as/aτ = 6, spatial lattice spacing as ≃ 0.162 fm, temporal lattice spacing aτ−1 = 7.35(3) GeV
- Maximal deviation of P wave correlator ratios at largest τ is around 20-25%, compared to less than 3% for S waves
- Energy scale fixed by identifying aτω = 0 with ω = 8.57 GeV using the Υ(1S) mass
- Number of configurations per temperature: 250 at T/Tc = 0.42, 1000 at all other temperatures

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of bottomonium at nonzero temperature (Aarts 2010, 2011, 2012) from S waves to P waves, completing the analysis of P wave states that was first presented in Aarts (2010) with limited statistics and no spectral functions. The finding that P wave states melt immediately above Tc while S wave ground states survive up to 2.09 Tc is consistent with experimental observations at the LHC (Chatrchyan 2011, Dahms 2013, ALICE 2013). The results enable comparison with potential model calculations and motivate application of alternative spectral reconstruction techniques (Rothkopf 2011, Burnier 2013) and extension to Nf=2+1 dynamical quark ensembles with finer spatial lattice spacing.
