# Production of high purity $^{52g}$Mn from $^{nat}$V targets with $α$-beams at cyclotrons

> Radioisotope $^{52g}$Mn is of special interest for multimodal imaging. Using state-of-art nuclear reaction codes, we study the alternative nuclear reaction route $^{nat}$V($α$,x)$^{52g}$Mn in comparison with the standard production routes based upon the use of chromium targets. The integral yields of $^{52g}$Mn and contaminants have been evaluated. The main outcome of this investigation is that the production of the main contaminant isotope $^{54}$Mn is expected to be lower than with $^{nat}$Cr. The study also reveals a large spread in the cross-section data set and points out the need of more precise measurements of the reaction $^{nat}$V($α$,x)$^{52g}$Mn as well as the need of a more accurate theoretical description.

## Key claim

The paper proposes the alternative nuclear reaction route $^{nat}$V($\alpha$,x)$^{52g}$Mn for producing the medical radioisotope $^{52g}$Mn, claiming that the main contaminant $^{54}$Mn is expected to be lower than with the standard $^{nat}$Cr(p,x)$^{52g}$Mn route, but the authors hedge that a definitive conclusion requires better experimental cross-section data and more accurate theoretical descriptions due to a large spread in existing data.

## Method

The authors used three nuclear reaction codes (Talys version 1.9, Empire version 3.2.3, and Fluka development version 2018.2) to calculate excitation functions for $^{nat}$V($\alpha$,x)$^{52g}$Mn and contaminant production. For Talys, they introduced a novel "Best Theoretical Evaluation" (BTE) approach using the interquartile range from 24 different model combinations to produce a theoretical uncertainty band. They then calculated integral yields, isotopic purities, and radionuclidic purities for a hypothetical 1-hour irradiation with 1 $\mu$A beam current on a 200 $\mu$m thick $^{nat}$V target, and compared these results with the standard $^{nat}$Cr(p,x)$^{52g}$Mn route and enriched $^{52}$Cr targets with proton and deuteron beams.

## Key numbers

- $^{52g}$Mn half-life: 5.6 days, with $\beta^+$ endpoint energy of about 0.6 MeV
- For $^{nat}$V($\alpha$,x)$^{52g}$Mn at 48 MeV incident energy, 200 $\mu$m target thickness (exit energy 33.9 MeV), 1 $\mu$A, 1 h irradiation: Talys BTE activity at End of Bombardment is $6.23 \pm 0.80$ MBq; Empire gives 3.20 MBq
- $^{52g}$Mn yield rate for the same conditions: between $5.6\times10^8$ and $1.45\times10^9$ nuclei$\cdot$s$^{-1}$ depending on code and model
- Isotopic purity at End of Bombardment: 0.45-0.75
- Radionuclidic purity reaches close to 1 after a few hours and remains close to 1 for about 20 days
- The cross-section ratio $\sigma_{^{52g}\text{Mn}}/(\sigma_{^{52g}\text{Mn}}+\sigma_{^{54}\text{Mn}})$ is nearly 1 above about 30 MeV
- $^{54}$Mn cross section peaks around 13 MeV with about 25% differences among calculations and 30% variability in experimental data at the peak
- Rescaling Empire yield for $^{nat}$V($\alpha$,x)$^{52g}$Mn by factor 1.5/1.7 gives approximately 4.3/4.9 MBq/$\mu$Ah, comparable to the 4.4 MBq/$\mu$Ah from Talys for $^{nat}$Cr(p,x)$^{52g}$Mn
- $^{52}$Cr(d,2n)$^{52g}$Mn produces almost 100% more $^{52g}$Mn than the proton route on enriched $^{52}$Cr

## Context

This work extends the search for alternative production routes for $^{52g}$Mn, which is of interest for multimodal PET/MRI imaging due to its 5.6 day half-life suitable for labeling slow biological compounds. The standard production route uses $^{nat}$Cr(p,x)$^{52g}$Mn as reviewed by Lapy (2019) and Qaim (2020), but this produces long-lived contaminants $^{53}$Mn and $^{54}$Mn. The $^{nat}$V($\alpha$,x)$^{52g}$Mn route was previously studied by Dmitriev (1969), Bowman (1969), and others over the last 50 years, but the authors note the IAEA Livechart website does not mention this route. The paper enables future experimental measurements with better precision to resolve the large spread in existing cross-section data, and supports the METRICS project within the SPES/LARAMED research program at INFN-LNL.
