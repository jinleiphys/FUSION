# Interpretation of the X(3872) as a charmonium state plus an extra component due to the coupling to the meson-meson continuum

> We present a quark model calculation of the charmonium spectrum with self energy corrections due to the coupling to the meson-meson continuum. The bare masses used in the calculation are computed within the relativized quark model by Godfrey and Isgur. The strong decay widths of 3S, 2P, 1D and 2D $c \bar c$ states are also calculated, in order to set the values of the $^3P_0$ pair-creation model's parameters we use to compute the vertex functions of the loop integrals. Finally, the nature of the X(3872) resonance is analyzed and the main possibilities ($c \bar c$ state or $D \bar D^*$ molecule) are discussed. According to our results, the X(3872) is compatible with the meson $χ_{c1}(2P)$, with $J^{PC} = 1^{++}$, and is thus interpreted as a $c \bar c$ core plus higher Fock components due to the coupling to the meson-meson continuum. These $J^{PC} = 1^{++}$ quantum numbers are in agreement with the experimental results found by the LHCb collaboration. In our view, the X(3872)'s mass is lower than the quark model's predictions because of self energy shifts.

# Interpretation of the X(3872) as a charmonium state plus an extra component due to the coupling to the meson-meson continuum

## Key claim
The X(3872) resonance is compatible with the charmonium state χc1(2P) with JPC = 1++ and is interpreted as a c-cbar core plus higher Fock components from coupling to the meson-meson continuum, where the mass is lower than quark model predictions because of self energy shifts. The authors qualify that their arguments cannot on their own completely clarify the nature of the X(3872) and that analysis of other properties such as strong and electromagnetic decays is necessary for a definitive conclusion.

## Method
The authors computed bare masses of charmonium states using the relativized quark model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985), then calculated self energy corrections from coupling to meson-meson continuum channels (including D Dbar, D Dbar*, D* Dbar*, Ds Ds, Ds Ds*, Ds* Ds*) using the 3P0 pair-creation model for vertex functions. Strong decay widths of 3S, 2P, 1D and 2D c-cbar states were calculated to set the 3P0 model parameters, and the physical masses were obtained by solving the transcendental equation M_a = E_a + Sigma(E_a) where E_a is the bare energy and Sigma(E_a) is the self energy.

## Key numbers
- The calculated mass of the χc1(2P) state (2^3P_1, JPC = 1++) is 3908 MeV, compared to the experimental X(3872) mass of 3872 MeV
- The calculated mass of the 1^1D_2 state (JPC = 2-+) is 3741 MeV, which is incompatible with the X(3872)
- The self energy correction for χc1(2P) is -117 MeV, with contributions from D Dbar* (-30 MeV), D* Dbar* (-66 MeV), Ds Ds* (-11 MeV), and Ds* Ds* (-9 MeV)
- The bare energy of χc1(2P) from the relativized quark model is 4025 MeV
- The strong decay width of Ψ(4040)(3^3S_1) is calculated as 80.3 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 80 ± 10 MeV
- The strong decay width of Ψ(3770)(1^3D_1) is calculated as 27.7 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 27.2 ± 1.0 MeV
- The pair-creation strength γ0 is fitted to 0.510, with oscillator parameter α = 0.500 GeV and quark form factor width r_q = 0.335 fm

## Context
This work extends the unquenched quark model approach previously applied to bottomonia by the same group (Ferretti et al., 2012) and to baryons by Santopinto (2010), building on the unitarized quark model of Tornqvist (1980s) and the relativized quark model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985). The calculation addresses the long-standing puzzle of the X(3872) discovered by Belle (Choi, 2003), which has competing interpretations as either a charmonium state or a D Dbar* molecule (Swanson, 2003; Danilkin and Simonov, 2010; Hanhart, 2007). The results favor the charmonium interpretation with JPC = 1++ as confirmed by LHCb (Aaij, 2013), and the methodology enables future calculations of strong and electromagnetic decay rates of the X(3872) within the same framework.
