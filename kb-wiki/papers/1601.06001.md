# Scaling properties of the mean multiplicity and pseudorapidity density in $e^{-}+e^{+}$, $e^{\pm}$+p, p($\bar{\mathrm{p}}$)+p, p+A and A+A(B) collisions

> The pseudorapidity density (dN/deta) for p+p, p+A and A+A(B) collisions, and the mean multiplicity <Nch> for ee, ep, and p+p collisions, are studied for an inclusive range of beam energies (Root_s). Characteristic scaling patterns are observed for both dN/deta and <Nch>, consistent with a thermal particle production mechanism for the bulk of the soft particles produced in all of these systems. They also validate an essential role for quark participants in these collisions. The scaled values for dN/deta and <Nch> are observed to factorize into contributions which depend on log(Root_s) and the number of nucleon or quark participant pairs (Npp). Quantification of these contributions give expressions which serve to systematize dN/deta and <Nch> measurements spanning nearly four orders of magnitude in Root_s, and to predict their values as a function of Root_s and Npp.

## Key claim

The observed scaling patterns for both pseudorapidity density and mean multiplicity across all collision systems (e+e-, ep, p+p, p+A, and A+A) are consistent with a common thermal particle production mechanism, but the scaling requires the use of effective energy scales (kappa factors) for p+p and ep collisions to account for the leading particle effect, and the use of quark participant pairs rather than nucleon participant pairs to achieve factorization.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic scaling analysis of published pseudorapidity density (dN/deta) measurements for p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions, and mean charged multiplicity (<Nch>) measurements for e+e-, ep, and p+p collisions across a wide range of center-of-mass energies. They used an entropy-based scaling ansatz S ~ (TR)^3, relating the number of participant pairs Npp to the initial volume via Npp^(1/3) proportional to R, and employed Monte Carlo Glauber calculations to obtain nucleon participant pairs (Nnpp) and quark participant pairs (Nqpp) for each collision system and centrality. The scaled quantities [dN/deta / Npp]^(1/3) and [<Nch> / Npp]^(1/3) were then studied as functions of sqrt(s) and Npp to identify universal scaling patterns.

## Key numbers

* For e+e-, ep, and p+p collisions, the mean multiplicity follows <Nch> = [1.22 + 0.775 log(kappa_n * sqrt(s))]^3, with kappa_1 = 1 for e+e-, kappa_2 ~ 1/2 for p+p, and kappa_3 ~ 1/6 for ep.
* For inelastic p+p collisions, the mid-pseudorapidity density follows dN/deta|_INE = [0.826 + 0.220 log(sqrt(s_NN))]^3, with b = 0.826 +/- 0.008 and m = 0.220 +/- 0.004.
* For non-single-diffractive p+p collisions, dN/deta|_NSD = [0.747 + 0.267 log(sqrt(s_NN))]^3, with b = 0.747 +/- 0.022 and m = 0.267 +/- 0.007.
* For A+A collisions, the pseudorapidity density at |eta| = 0.5 follows dN/deta = Nqpp * [0.530 + 0.258 log(sqrt(s))]^3, with b_AA = 0.530 +/- 0.008 and m_AA = 0.258 +/- 0.004.
* The scaling covers beam energies spanning nearly four orders of magnitude in sqrt(s), from approximately 15 GeV to 13 TeV for p+p collisions.

## Context

This work extends the search for universal scaling patterns in particle production across different collision systems, building on earlier studies of the leading particle effect by Feinberg (1972), Albini (1975), and Basile (1980, 1981), and on constituent quark models by Nyiri (2002). The results contradict the conventional approach of using different theoretical frameworks (thermodynamic/hydrodynamic for A+A versus microscopic parton-based models for p+p and p+A) by demonstrating that a common thermal entropy production mechanism can describe all systems. The scaling expressions enable robust predictions for pseudorapidity density and mean multiplicity at future collision energies and for different system sizes, with specific application to ongoing measurements at RHIC and the LHC.
