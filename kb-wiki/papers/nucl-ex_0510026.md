# Azimuthal Anisotropy of Identified Hadrons in 200 GeV Au+Au Collisions

> The azimuthal anisotropy parameter v2 has been measured with high statistics for identified hadrons in sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions with the STAR experiment. At high transverse momentum (pT) a strong v2 for pi+ + pi- and p + pbar is observed. In the intermediate pT region, number-of-constituent-quark scaling was tested to high precision. A detailed comparison of v2 for the multi-strange hadrons phi, Xi + anti-Xi, and Omega + anti-Omega with other particle species substantiates the development of collectivity among partons in the early phase of the collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed meson-baryon grouping in the intermediate transverse momentum region, and the strong elliptic flow of multi-strange hadrons, substantiate the development of collectivity among partons in the early phase of collisions at RHIC; however, the abstract glosses over that small deviations from exact number-of-constituent-quark scaling are now detectable, and that thermalization of the system remains an open question for the discovery of a quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured the azimuthal anisotropy parameter v2 for identified hadrons in sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV minimum bias Au+Au collisions. Particles were correlated to the reaction plane determined in the opposite eta-subevent to suppress non-flow effects. The v2 for pions and protons was extended up to pT = 9 GeV/c by disentangling contributions to the overall v2 from dE/dx measurements, and the v2 for multi-strange hadrons (phi, Xi + anti-Xi, Omega + anti-Omega) was compared to a common fit to K0_S and Lambda + anti-Lambda data using chi-squared tests.

## Key numbers

- v2 for pi+ + pi- and p + pbar was measured up to pT = 9 GeV/c in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions.
- Proton v2 saturates at pT ~ 3 GeV/c, while pion v2 declines from its maximum at about pT = 3 GeV/c.
- At low pT/n (below 0.75 GeV/c), deviations from the scaled v2/n fit follow a mass-ordering expected from hydrodynamic flow.
- Scaled v2/n measurements show similarity out to pT/n as high as 2 GeV/c.
- For the chi-squared comparison of multi-strange hadrons to a common fit for pT/n > 0.75 GeV/c: Xi- + anti-Xi+ gives chi2/n.d.f. = 27.4/6 for NCQ=3; Omega- + anti-Omega+ gives chi2/n.d.f. = 2.1/3 for NCQ=3; phi gives chi2/n.d.f. = 2.0/5 for NCQ=2.
- The phi meson mass is 1019.5 MeV/c2, similar to the proton mass of 938.3 MeV/c2.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of elliptic flow for identified hadrons at RHIC, including results from the PHENIX collaboration (PHENIX_data) and prior STAR measurements of particle spectra (piKp, strangeProd). The observation of number-of-constituent-quark scaling supports quark coalescence models (NCQMolVol) as the dominant hadronization process in the intermediate pT region. The strong flow of multi-strange hadrons, which are less affected by the hadronic phase, confirms partonic collectivity at RHIC and enables further investigation into whether the system is thermalized, which is the remaining item needed for the discovery of a quark-gluon plasma.
