# Power Counting of Contact-Range Currents in Effective Field Theory

> We analyze the power counting of two-body currents in nuclear effective field theories (EFTs). We find that the existence of non-perturbative physics at low energies, which is manifest in the existence of the deuteron and the 1S0 NN virtual bound state, combined with the appearance of singular potentials in versions of nuclear EFT that incorporate chiral symmetry, modifies the renormalization-group flow of the couplings associated with contact operators that involve nucleon-nucleon pairs and external fields. The order of these couplings is thereby enhanced with respect to the naive-dimensional-analysis estimate. Consequently, short-range currents enter at a lower order in the chiral EFT than has been appreciated up until now, and their impact on low-energy observables is concomitantly larger. We illustrate the changes in the power counting with a few low-energy processes involving external probes and the few-nucleon systems, including electron-deuteron elastic scattering and radiative neutron capture by protons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that naive dimensional analysis (NDA) underestimates the importance of short-range two-body current operators in chiral effective field theory (χEFT) for nuclei, because it neglects the anomalous dimensions these operators acquire from the singular, non-perturbative nature of nuclear wave functions at short distances. The enhancement means these contact-range currents enter at least one order earlier in the χEFT expansion than previously appreciated, with the exact promotion depending on the specific NN partial wave and transition involved.

## Method

The authors impose renormalization-group (RG) invariance on matrix elements of electroweak current operators between NN wave functions, deriving a differential equation for the cutoff-dependent coefficient of the leading contact-range two-body current. By analyzing the short-distance (UV) behavior of the LO χEFT wave functions, specifically the power-law exponents \(b\) and \(c\) of the reduced radial wave functions \(u(R)/R \sim R^b\) as \(R \to 0\), they compute the anomalous dimension \(-a = b + c\) that determines how the contact coupling \(C_0(\Lambda)\) runs from the hard scale \(M \sim 1\) GeV down to the soft scale \(Q \sim 150\) MeV. This running yields a promotion of the operator by \(a\) powers of \(Q/M\) relative to the NDA estimate.

## Key numbers

- For the deuteron (³S₁–³D₁ channel) in χEFT, the LO wave functions behave as \(u(R)/R \sim R^{-1/4}\) as \(R \to 0\), giving \(b = c = -1/4\) and an anomalous dimension \(-a = -1/2\). This promotes the contact contribution to the deuteron charge radius from NDA's N⁵LO (\(Q^5\)) to N⁹/²LO (\(Q^{9/2}\)), and the magnetic dipole contact operator from N³LO (\(Q^4\)) to N⁵/²LO (\(Q^{7/2}\)).
- For the ¹S₀ NN channel, the LO wave function behaves as \(u(R)/R \sim R^{-1}\) (i.e. \(b = -1\)), because the channel is dominated by a contact interaction. For the \(np \to d\gamma\) M1 transition (¹S₀ → ³S₁–³D₁), this gives \(b = -1\) and \(c = -1/4\), so the contact current enters at N⁷/⁴LO (\(Q^{11/4}\)) relative to the NDA estimate of N³LO (\(Q^4\)).
- For the \(pp \to d e^+ \nu_e\) Gamow-Teller transition, the contact operator \(A(\Lambda)\,\vec{\beta}\) enters at N⁷/⁴LO (\(Q^{7/4}\)) compared to NDA's N³LO (\(Q^3\)).

## Context

This work extends the Wilsonian RG analysis of NN potentials (Birse 2005, Pavon Valderrama 2005) to electroweak current operators, showing that the singular \(1/r^3\) nature of the chiral potential in the ³S₁–³D₁ channel and the contact-dominated ¹S₀ channel both generate anomalous dimensions that enhance short-range currents. It contradicts the standard Weinberg (1990, 1991) power counting for χEFT currents, which assumed NDA applies. The results imply that existing χEFT calculations of electron-deuteron scattering (Phillips 2006, Piarulli 2012), radiative neutron capture, and solar pp fusion (Marcucci 2013) need revision, as they underestimated the role of contact-range currents. The analysis also reproduces the well-known pionless EFT counting (Kaplan 1998, Chen 1999) as a special case where \(b = c = -1\).
