# Resonance model study on $K^+ N \rightarrow K N η$ near threshold

> By using resonance model, we investigate $K^+ N \rightarrow K N η$ reactions with the assumption that these reactions are dominated by the excitation of $N^*(1535)$ near threshold. It is found that the hyperon and $ρ$ exchange diagrams give the most important contributions to these reactions. Thus these reactions may be a good place to study the coupling of $N^*(1535)$ with $KΛ$, $KΣ$ and $Nρ$ channels. We demonstrate that the angular distributions of final particles provide useful information about the different mechanisms of the $N^*(1535)$ excitations, which could be useful for future experimental analysis on these reactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the reactions \(K^+ N \rightarrow K N \eta\) near threshold are dominated by the excitation of the \(N^*(1535)\) resonance, with hyperon (\(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\)) and \(\rho\) meson exchange diagrams providing the most important contributions. However, the claim is conditional on the assumption that contributions from \(K^*\) resonances and other nucleon resonances (such as \(N^*(1650)\)) are negligible near threshold, and the results depend on the choice of cutoff parameters and coupling constants, which are not well constrained by existing data.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach with a resonance model, assuming that the reactions \(K^+ p \rightarrow K^+ p \eta\), \(K^+ n \rightarrow K^0 p \eta\), and \(K^+ n \rightarrow K^+ n \eta\) proceed via intermediate excitation of the \(N^*(1535)\) resonance, which then decays to \(N\eta\). They calculate Feynman diagrams for vector meson exchange (\(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\phi\)) and hyperon exchange (\(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\)), using Breit-Wigner propagators for \(N^*(1535)\) and monopole form factors for the vertices. Total cross sections and angular distributions are computed via Monte Carlo multi-particle phase space integration.

## Key numbers

* Coupling constant for \(N^*(1535)N\rho\): \(g^2_{N^*(1535)N\rho}/4\pi = 0.1\), determined from the partial decay width \(\Gamma_{N^*(1535)\rightarrow N\rho \rightarrow N\pi\pi}\).
* Coupling constant for \(N^*(1535)N\eta\): \(g^2_{N^*(1535)N\eta}/4\pi = 0.28\), obtained from the partial decay width of \(N^*(1535)\) to \(N\eta\).
* Coupling constant for \(N^*(1535)N\omega\): \(g^2_{N^*(1535)N\omega}/4\pi = 0.25\), using a ratio of \(g_{N^*(1535)N\rho}\) to \(g_{N^*(1535)N\omega}\) of 2.
* Coupling constant for \(N^*(1535)N\phi\): \(g^2_{N^*(1535)N\phi}/4\pi = 0.13\), as suggested in Xie (2010).
* Coupling constants for hyperon exchange (Option II): \(g^2_{N^*(1535)K\Lambda}/4\pi = 0.069\) and \(g^2_{N^*(1535)K\Sigma}/4\pi = 0.19\), derived from ratios in Oset (2010).
* Cutoff parameter for vector meson exchange vertices: \(\Lambda_V = 2.0\) GeV.
* Cutoff parameter for \(N^*(1535)N\)-meson vertices: \(\Lambda^* = 1.3\) GeV.
* Cutoff parameter for \(NKY\) vertices: \(\Lambda_u = 1.5\) GeV.
* Cutoff parameter for \(N^*(1535)KY\) vertices: \(\Lambda^*_u = 1.3\) GeV, with uncertainties explored by varying from 1.0 GeV to 2.0 GeV, changing cross sections by factors of 3 (suppression) to 2 (enhancement).
* Angular distributions are shown at excess energy \(Q = 15\) MeV for the reaction \(K^+ n \rightarrow K^0 p \eta\).

## Context

This work extends the study of \(N^*(1535)\) couplings to \(K\Lambda\), \(K\Sigma\), and \(N\rho\) channels, motivated by earlier findings in \(J/\Psi \rightarrow p K^- \bar\Lambda\) decays (BES Collaboration, 2010) and \(\eta\) production in nucleon-nucleon collisions (Batinić et al., 1995; Nakayama et al., 2002; Shyam et al., 2007). It contrasts with models that emphasize \(\pi\) meson exchange (Batinić et al., 1995; Nakayama et al., 2002; Shyam et al., 2007) versus \(\rho\) meson exchange (Gedalin et al., 1998; Santra et al., 2002; Fäldt et al., 2002; Nakayama et al., 2002; Vetter et al., 2002) for \(\eta\) production. The paper enables future experimental analysis of \(K^+ N \rightarrow K N \eta\) reactions to constrain the poorly known coupling constants \(g_{N^*(1535)K\Lambda}\), \(g_{N^*(1535)K\Sigma}\), and \(g_{N^*(1535)N\rho}\), and suggests that combined analysis of all three isospin-related reactions can disentangle interference effects among exchange mechanisms.
