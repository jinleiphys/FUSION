# On the angular momentum dependence of nuclear level densities

> Angular momentum dependence of nuclear level densities at finite temperatures are investigated in the static path approximation(SPA) to the partition function using a cranked quadrupole interaction Hamiltonian in the following three schemes: (i) cranking about x-axis, (ii) cranking about z-axis and (iii) cranking about z-axis but correcting for the orientation fluctuation of the axis. Performing numerical computations for an $sd$ and a $pf$ shell nucleus, we find that the x-axis cranking results are satisfactory for reasonably heavy nuclei and this offers a computationally faster method to include the angular momentum dependence at high temperatures in the SPA approach. It also appears that at high spins inclusion of orientation fluctuation correction would be important.

# On the angular momentum dependence of nuclear level densities

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether cranking about the x-axis within the static path approximation (SPA) can serve as a computationally faster alternative to z-axis cranking for computing angular-momentum-dependent nuclear level densities at finite temperatures. The authors find that x-axis cranking yields satisfactory results for reasonably heavy nuclei, but caution that at high spins the inclusion of orientation fluctuation corrections may be important.

## Method

The authors compute nuclear level densities using the static path approximation (SPA) to the grand canonical partition function with a cranked quadrupole interaction Hamiltonian, applied in three schemes: (i) cranking about the x-axis, (ii) cranking about the z-axis, and (iii) cranking about the z-axis with orientation fluctuation corrections. Numerical calculations are performed for two nuclei: the sd-shell nucleus 24Mg and the pf-shell nucleus 64Zn, using model spaces identical to those in prior work by Miller and by Lauritzen et al. The partition function is evaluated by diagonalizing the one-body Hamiltonian at mesh points in the space of quadrupole deformation variables (beta, gamma, theta, psi) and integrating numerically using eight Gaussian points per variable.

## Key numbers

- For 24Mg, the inverse level density parameter K (= A/a) shows the level density parameter a decreasing from A/6 at T = 1 MeV to A/12 at T = 3.0 MeV on average.
- For 24Mg at E = -40.0 MeV, the difference rho_x - rho_z between x-axis and z-axis cranking level densities is 2.718 for J = 0, 2.459 for J = 2, 2.225 for J = 4, and 1.234 for J = 6.
- For 24Mg, the temperature difference between M = 2 and M = 3 at E = -47.5 MeV is approximately 0.3 MeV.
- The quadrupole interaction strength is taken as chi = 70 A^{-1.4} MeV, and the oscillator frequency as omega_0 = 41/A^{1/3} MeV.
- The maximum deformation parameter beta_max = 0.5 is used in the numerical integration.

## Context

This work extends the static path approximation (SPA) approach developed by Lauritzen, Alhassid, and collaborators (Lauritzen 1991, Alhassid 1990) to include angular momentum dependence via cranking, building on the authors' prior study of 64Zn using J_x-cranking (Agrawal and Ansari 1992). The paper contrasts with the exact angular momentum projection method of Ansari (1993), which is numerically intractable for heavy nuclei due to the need for additional three-dimensional integration. The results enable faster computation of spin-dependent level densities for heavier nuclei at high temperatures, though the authors note that at low spins an exact angular momentum or K projection may still be necessary, and that pairing correlations and RPA corrections (as discussed by Shlomo 1991) should be included at low temperatures.
