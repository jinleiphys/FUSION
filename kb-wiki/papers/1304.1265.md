# QCD magnetic susceptibility at finite temperature beyond the chiral limit

> We investigate the QCD magnetic susceptibility chi_q for flavor SU(2) at finite temperature (T) beyond the chiral limit, using the liquid instanton model, defined in Euclidean space and modified by the T-dependent caloron solution. The background electromagnetic fields are induced to the QCD vacuum, employing the Schwinger method. We first compute the scalar (chiral) and tensor condensates as functions of T as well the current-quark mass m, signaling the correct universal chiral restoration patterns. It turns out that chi_q, given by the ratio of the two condensates, is a smoothly decreasing function of T, showing about 20% reduction of its strength at the chiral transition T = T_0, in comparison to that at T = 0, and decreases almost linearly beyond T_0 for m > 0. We observe that the present numerical results are in qualitatively good agreement with other theoretical results, including the lattice simulations. Finally, we examine the effects of the external magnetic field on the tensor-polarization VEV, resulting in that it plays the role of the chiral order parameter.

# QCD magnetic susceptibility at finite temperature beyond the chiral limit

## Key claim
The QCD magnetic susceptibility χ_q for flavor SU(2) is a smoothly decreasing function of temperature, showing about 20% reduction at the chiral transition temperature T_0 compared to T=0, and decreases almost linearly beyond T_0 for finite current-quark mass m > 0. This behavior is computed using the liquid instanton model with temperature-dependent caloron solutions, and the results are in qualitatively good agreement with lattice simulations, though sizable deviations appear near the chiral transition temperature.

## Method
The authors employed the liquid instanton model (LIM) in Euclidean space, modified by the Harrington-Shepard caloron solution to introduce temperature dependence. They computed the scalar (chiral) and tensor condensates as functions of temperature and current-quark mass using the effective action derived from the instanton vacuum, with the fermionic Matsubara formalism to handle finite temperature. The magnetic susceptibility was obtained as the ratio of the tensor condensate to the chiral condensate, with the background electromagnetic fields induced via the Schwinger method.

## Key numbers
- Chiral condensate at T=0: ⟨iq†q⟩^(1/3) = (258, 260) MeV for m = (0, 5) MeV, compatible with the empirical value (240 ± 10 MeV)^3
- Chiral transition temperature: T_0 = (166, 170) MeV for m = (0, 5) MeV
- Tensor condensate at T=0: ⟨q†Σq⟩ = (52, 49) MeV for m = (0, 5) MeV
- Tensor condensate at T_0 = 170 MeV for m = 5 MeV: 21 MeV
- Magnetic susceptibility at T=0: χ_q = (3.03, 2.77) GeV^(-2) for m = (0, 5) MeV
- At T_0 = 170 MeV, the magnetic susceptibility shows approximately 20% reduction compared to its T=0 value

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of QCD magnetic susceptibility using the liquid instanton model at finite density (Nam 2008) to finite temperature, and complements studies using QCD sum rules (Belyaev 1984, Balitsky 1985, Ball 2002), effective quark models (Kim 2004, Frasca 2011), holographic QCD (Bergman 2008, Gorsky 2009), operator product expansion with pion dominance (Vainshtein 2002), and lattice QCD simulations (Buividovich 2009, Braguta 2010, Bali 2012). The results enable comparison with lattice data at finite temperature and provide a theoretical framework for understanding the temperature dependence of the QCD vacuum's electromagnetic properties relevant to heavy-ion collision experiments.
