# Strangeness and Charm in Nuclear Matter

> The properties of strange ($K$, $\bar K$ and $\bar K^*$) and open-charm ($D$, $\bar D$ and $D^*$) mesons in dense matter are studied using a unitary approach in coupled channels for meson-baryon scattering. In the strangeness sector, the interaction with nucleons always comes through vector-meson exchange, which is evaluated by chiral and hidden gauge Lagrangians. For the interaction of charmed mesons with nucleons we extend the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian to incorporate spin-flavor symmetry and implement a suitable flavor symmetry breaking. The in-medium solution for the scattering amplitude accounts for Pauli blocking effects and meson self-energies. On one hand, we obtain the $K$, $\bar K$ and $\bar K^*$ spectral functions in the nuclear medium and study their behaviour at finite density, temperature and momentum. We also make an estimate of the transparency ratio of the $γA \to K^+ K^{*-} A^\prime$ reaction, which we propose as a tool to detect in-medium modifications of the $\bar K^*$ meson. On the other hand, in the charm sector, several resonances with negative parity are generated dynamically by the s-wave interaction between pseudoscalar and vector meson multiplets with $1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ baryons. The properties of these states in matter are analyzed and their influence on the open-charm meson spectral functions is studied. We finally discuss the possible formation of $D$-mesic nuclei at FAIR energies.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the in-medium properties of strange and open-charm mesons using a unitary coupled-channel approach, but the results for the \(\bar K^*\) spectral function and the transparency ratio are based on a model that does not explicitly incorporate transitions to meson-baryon states with a pseudoscalar meson in the \(\bar K^* N\) unitarized amplitude, which would make the quasiparticle peak less prominent and harder to disentangle from other excitations.

## Method

The authors compute the self-energies and spectral functions of \(K\), \(\bar K\), \(\bar K^*\), \(D\), \(\bar D\), and \(D^*\) mesons in symmetric nuclear matter. For the strangeness sector, the meson-baryon interaction is derived from chiral and hidden gauge Lagrangians, and the in-medium scattering amplitude is solved self-consistently including Pauli blocking, mean-field binding, and meson self-energies. For the charm sector, the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian is extended to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry to incorporate heavy-quark spin symmetry, and the Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in coupled channels to generate dynamically baryon resonances and obtain the in-medium meson self-energies.

## Key numbers

*   The \(\bar K\) feels an attractive potential of about -50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.17 \,{\rm fm^{-3}}\).
*   The \(\bar K^*\) spectral function width at normal nuclear matter density becomes five times bigger than in free space.
*   In the \(C=1, S=0, I=0\) sector, a \(\Lambda_c\) resonance is obtained around 2618.8 MeV, identified with the experimental \(\Lambda_c(2595)\).
*   In the \(C=1, S=0, I=0\) sector, a spin-\(3/2\) resonance is found at \((2666.6 -i 26.7 \ \rm{MeV})\), assigned to the experimental \(\Lambda_c(2625)\).
*   In the \(C=1, S=0, I=1\) sector, three \(\Sigma_c\) resonances are obtained with masses 2571.5, 2622.7, and 2643.4 MeV and widths 0.8, 188.0, and 87.0 MeV, respectively.
*   The transparency ratio for \(\gamma A \to K^+ K^{*-} A'\) is evaluated at center-of-mass energies \(\sqrt{s}=3\) GeV and 3.5 GeV (photon lab energies of 4.3 GeV and 6 GeV, respectively).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strange mesons in matter (Ramos 1999, Tolos 2001, Tolos 2002) and recent calculations of the \(\bar K^*\) in medium (Tolos 2010) by also incorporating a consistent treatment of open-charm mesons using heavy-quark spin symmetry. It builds on the SU(6) spin-flavor model for the \(\bar K^* N\) interaction (Garcia-Recio 2005) and the SU(8) model for charmed baryon resonances (Garcia-Recio 2008, Romanets 2012). The results enable predictions for the transparency ratio in photoproduction experiments and for the possible formation of \(D\)-mesic nuclei at FAIR energies, which could be tested by the PANDA experiment.
