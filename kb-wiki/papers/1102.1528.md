# Connection between effective-range expansion and nuclear vertex constant or asymptotic normalization coefficient

> Explicit relations between the effective-range expansion and the nuclear vertex constant or asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for the virtual decay $B\to A+a$ are derived for an arbitrary orbital momentum together with the corresponding location condition for the ($A+a$) bound-state energy. They are valid both for the charged case and for the neutral case. Combining these relations with the standard effective-range function up to order six makes it possible to reduce to two the number of free effective-range parameters if an ANC value is known from experiment. Values for the scattering length, effective range, and form parameter are determined in this way for the $^{16}$O+$p$, $α+t$ and $α+^3$He collisions in partial waves where a bound state exists by using available ANCs deduced from experiments. The resulting effective-range expansions for these collisions are valid up to energies larger 5 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit relations between the effective-range expansion and the nuclear vertex constant (NVC) or asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for a virtual decay \(B \to A + a\) at arbitrary orbital angular momentum, valid for both charged and neutral particles; these relations allow reducing the number of free effective-range parameters to two (or one if the sixth-order term is set to zero) when an ANC value is known from experiment, but the reliability of the resulting parameters depends on the accuracy of the experimental binding energy and ANC.

## Method

The authors start from the standard effective-range function \(K_{lj}(k^2)\) and the pole-location condition for a bound state at energy \(-\varepsilon\) (momentum \(i\kappa\)). They derive general expressions for the NVC (Eq. 13) and ANC (Eq. 21) in terms of the effective-range expansion truncated to order \(k^6\) (Eq. 16). For three nuclear collisions, \(^{16}\text{O}+p\), \(\alpha+t\), and \(\alpha+^{3}\text{He}\), they use experimental phase shifts and known ANC values to determine the scattering length \(a_{lj}\) by averaging over several energies, then compute the effective range \(r_{lj}\) and form parameter \(P_{lj}\) from Eqs. ## Context

This work extends earlier derivations by Igam1997, Igam2010, Spar2009, and Orlov2010, which gave ANC expressions for the standard effective-range function, by generalizing to an arbitrary orbital momentum and to a form valid for both charged and neutral cases simultaneously. The derived relations enable a consistent description of low-energy scattering phase shifts and bound-state properties (binding energy and ANC), reducing ambiguity in the effective-range coefficients. The results are compared with microscopic cluster-model calculations by KB2007 for the same collisions, showing that the microscopic model often fails to reproduce both the binding energy and phase shifts simultaneously, and that the present method provides a way to test or constrain such models.
