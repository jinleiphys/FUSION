# Baryon masses in large Nc chiral perturbation theory

> We analyse the baryon mass spectrum in a framework which combines the 1/Nc expansion with chiral perturbation theory. Meson loop contributions involving the full SU(3) octet of pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons are evaluated, and the influence of explicit chiral and flavor symmetry breaking by non-zero and unequal quark masses is investigated. We also discuss sigma terms and the strangeness contribution to the nucleon mass.

## Key claim

The paper combines the 1/Nc expansion with chiral perturbation theory to analyze baryon masses, but the one-loop corrections are not suppressed relative to tree-level masses in the 1/Nc expansion (unlike the axial current case), and the η-meson loop corrections require inclusion of n-body operators beyond the leading order for consistency.

## Method

The authors construct baryon states in the large Nc limit using bosonic quark operators, then write an effective chiral Lagrangian with explicit Nc-counting of operators. They evaluate baryon masses at tree level up to O(ε⁵) in a combined expansion where mΠ ∼ O(ε) and 1/Nc ∼ O(ε), and compute one-loop meson self-energy corrections from pion, kaon, and eta loops using dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The Gell-Mann–Okubo relation (M2) gives (27 = 0) MeV empirically, while the improved relation (M2') gives (27 = 11) MeV
- The decuplet equal spacing rule (M3) gives (139 = 149 = 152) MeV, while the improved rule (M3') gives (−3 = −8) MeV
- The hyperfine splitting rule (M1) gives (293 = 308) MeV empirically
- The relation MΞ* − MΞ = MΣ* − MΣ gives (215 = 192) MeV
- The best fit up to O(ε³) already gives a reasonable baryon mass spectrum; O(ε⁵) corrections are not important

## Context

This work extends the tree-level mass relations of Dashen and Manohar (1993) and Jenkins (1993) by computing one-loop corrections in a framework that treats octet and decuplet baryons as degenerate at leading order in 1/Nc. It contrasts with conventional baryon chiral perturbation theory by Borasoy and Meissner (1996, 1997), where decuplet degrees of freedom are integrated out. The results enable systematic inclusion of loop effects while maintaining consistency with the 1/Nc expansion, though the η-meson loop corrections require careful treatment of higher-body operators.
