# Experimental evaluation of the nuclear neutron-proton contact

> The nuclear neutron-proton contact is introduced, generalizing Tan's work, and evaluated from medium energy nuclear photodisintegration experiments. To this end we reformulate the quasi-deuteron model of nuclear photodisintegration and establish the bridge between the Levinger constant and the contact. Using experimental evaluations of Levinger's constant we extract the value of the neutron-proton contact in finite nuclei and in symmetric nuclear matter. Assuming isospin symmetry we propose to evaluate the neutron-neutron contact through measurement of photonuclear spin correlated neutron-proton pairs.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the nuclear neutron-proton contact by generalizing Tan's contact formalism to nuclear systems, and extracts its value from medium-energy photonuclear experiments by establishing a bridge between the Levinger constant and the contact. The extraction relies on the zero-range approximation and assumes that the quasi-deuteron model captures the dominant reaction mechanism, with the caveat that the zero-range approximation is "at best questionable for finite nuclei" and that the quoted error of 0.10 refers only to statistical errors, not systematic errors from model assumptions.

## Method

The authors reformulate the quasi-deuteron model of nuclear photodisintegration using the zero-range approximation, deriving an expression that relates the total photonuclear cross-section to the deuteron cross-section via the sum of spin-singlet and spin-triplet neutron-proton contacts. They then use the experimental evaluation of Levinger's constant from Terranova et al. (1992), which compiled photonuclear data for 14 nuclei from lithium to uranium, to extract the average neutron-proton contact per particle in units of the Fermi momentum. The contact is compared to universal Fermi gas measurements from ultracold atomic experiments with 40K and 6Li.

## Key numbers

- Levinger constant averaged over 14 nuclei: L = 5.50 ± 0.21 (1σ)
- Average neutron-proton contact per particle for symmetric nuclear matter: C̄_pn/(k_F A) = 2.55 ± 0.10, derived using 1/(k_F a_t) ≈ 0.15
- Nuclear two-body scattering lengths: a_t = 5.38 fm (³S₁ state), a_s = -20 fm (¹S₀ state)
- Average interparticle distance in atomic nucleus: approximately 2.4 fm, deduced from R_c ≈ 1.2 A^{1/3} fm
- Photon energy range considered: ħω = 100-200 MeV, corresponding to wave number k ≈ 0.5-1 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends Tan's contact formalism (Tan, 2008) from ultracold atomic Fermi gases to nuclear systems, connecting it to the quasi-deuteron model originally proposed by Levinger (1951). The results enable future separation of the spin-singlet and spin-triplet nuclear contacts through measurement of spin-correlated neutron-proton pairs in photonuclear experiments, and suggest that Tan's relations may be useful in nuclear physics despite nuclei being far from a universal Fermi gas. The experimental data used for Levinger's constant come from compilations by Terranova et al. (1992), which in turn used photonuclear experiments from Ahr et al. (1975), Leprêtre et al. (1978), and Stibius (1984).
