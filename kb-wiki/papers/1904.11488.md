# Bulk quantities in nuclear collisions from running coupling $k_{T}$-factorization and hybrid simulations

> Starting from a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, based on a running-coupling improved $k_T$-factorized formula, we calculate bulk observables in several heavy-ion collision systems. This is done in two ways: first we calculate the particle distribution directly implied from the CGC model, and we compare this to the case where it is instead used as initial conditions for a hybrid hydrodynamic simulation. In this way, we can assess the effects of hydrodynamic and hadronic evolution by quantifying how much they change the results from a pure initial state approach and, therefore, to what extent initial condition models can be directly compared to experimental data. We find that entropy production in subsequent hydrodynamic evolution can increase multiplicity by as much as 50\%. However, disregarding a single overall normalization factor, the centrality, energy, and system size dependence of charged hadron multiplicity is only affected at the $\sim$5\% level. Because of this, the parameter-free prediction for these dependencies gives reasonable agreement with experimental data whether or not hydrodynamic evolution is included. On the other hand, our model results are not compatible with the hypothesis that hydrodynamic evolution is present in large systems, but not small systems like p-Pb, in which case the dependence of multiplicity on system size would be stronger than seen experimentally. Moreover, we find that hydrodynamic evolution significantly changes the distribution of momentum, so that observables such as mean transverse momentum are very different from the initial particle production, and much closer to measured data. Finally, we find that a good agreement to anisotropic flow data cannot be achieved due to the large eccentricity generated by this model.

## Key claim

The paper finds that while hydrodynamic evolution can increase charged hadron multiplicity by up to 50% through entropy production, the centrality, energy, and system-size dependence of multiplicity changes by only about 5% after disregarding a single overall normalization factor, meaning that pure initial-state CGC calculations can reproduce these dependencies reasonably well without including hydrodynamic evolution.

## Method

The authors start from a running-coupling improved $k_T$-factorized formula within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, using unintegrated gluon distributions from solutions of the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation with the AAMQS fit to HERA data. They compute bulk observables in two ways: first directly from the CGC model using local parton-hadron duality (LPHD) for hadronization, and second by using the same CGC calculation as initial conditions for a hybrid model that combines viscous hydrodynamics (solved with the MUSIC code) with a hadronic afterburner (UrQMD), with hydrodynamic parameters taken from a Bayesian analysis by Bernhard (2018).

## Key numbers

- Entropy production from dissipative effects during hydrodynamic evolution can increase multiplicity by as much as 50% for the 0-10% most central heavy-ion collisions in the 0.2 TeV ≤ √s ≤ 5.44 TeV region
- After fixing a single overall normalization, the centrality, energy, and system-size dependence of charged hadron multiplicity is affected at only the ~5% level by hydrodynamic evolution
- Across 2 orders of magnitude in collision energy, hydrodynamic and final-state effects change the predicted multiplicity by slightly more than 6% for A+A systems and 4.5% for p+A collisions
- The ratio of average transverse momentum in Xe+Xe at 5.44 TeV to Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV from the pure CGC calculation is approximately 0.92, based on the scaling $\langle p_T \rangle_{XeXe}/\langle p_T \rangle_{PbPb} \sim (129/208)^{1/6}$
- The viscous hydrodynamic calculation of the Xe+Xe to Pb+Pb average transverse momentum ratio differs from the ideal hydrodynamic result by less than 2%

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Dumitru (2018) and Duraes (2016) that used the same running-coupling $k_T$-factorized formula for bulk observables in the CGC approach, now including predictions for Ar+Ar and O+O collisions and calculations of average transverse momentum. The results are compared to hybrid hydrodynamic simulations with CGC initial conditions, extending earlier comparisons by Hirano (2005, 2007) and Song (2010) that were limited to single collision systems and energies. The finding that the rcBK initial conditions generate eccentricities too large to simultaneously describe anisotropic flow data is consistent with previous studies by Retinskaya (2013) using the leading-order version of the formula and the MC-KLN model by Hirano (2009, 2010). The paper enables future tests of hydrodynamic behavior through system-size comparisons of mean transverse momentum, and suggests that improved knowledge of proton unintegrated gluon distributions from a future Electron-Ion Collider could improve results at RHIC energies.
