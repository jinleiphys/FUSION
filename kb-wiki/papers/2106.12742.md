# Relativistic kinetic approach to light nuclei production in high-energy nuclear collisions

> Understanding the production mechanism of light (anti-)nuclei in high-energy nuclear collisions and cosmic rays has been a long-standing problem in nuclear physics. In the present study, we develop a stochastic method to solve the relativistic kinetic equations for light nuclei production from many-body reactions with the inclusion of their finite sizes. The present approach gives an excellent description of the deuteron and helium-3 data from central Au+Au (Pb+Pb) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ $=$ $200~\rm GeV$ ($2.76~\rm TeV$). It can also naturally explain their suppressed production in $pp$ collisions at 7 TeV as a result of their finite sizes.

## Key claim
The authors develop a stochastic method to solve relativistic kinetic equations for light nuclei production that includes the finite sizes of nuclei, which is essential for describing their suppressed production in small collision systems like pp collisions, whereas in large systems (Au+Au, Pb+Pb) the finite-size effect is negligible and the results agree with data regardless.

## Method
The authors solve relativistic kinetic equations for deuteron and helium-3 production and dissociation via many-body reactions (e.g., πd ↔ πNN, πNNN ↔ πt) using a stochastic test-particle method. The transition amplitudes are evaluated under the impulse approximation, where the pion scatters quasifreely off one nucleon inside the nucleus while the other nucleon(s) act as spectators, with the finite nuclear size encoded via Wigner functions. The hadronic evolution is coupled to initial conditions from the MUSIC hydrodynamic model for Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions and from the AMPT model for pp collisions.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron root-mean-square radius reproduced using σ_d = 3.2 fm, giving a deuteron size of 1.96 fm.
- Renormalization factor F_d ≈ 0.72 for deuteron and F_He3 ≈ 0.51 for helium-3 to match π+d and π+³He cross sections at low pion momenta (e.g., 0.3 GeV).
- Branching ratio for triton dissociation via πt → πNd: 0.56 (Gaussian wave function) or 0.58 (Hulthen wave function); for ³He dissociation: ≈ 0.6.
- In pp collisions at 7 TeV, initial deuteron yields range from 1.3×10⁻⁴ to 5.3×10⁻⁴ and initial helium-3 yields from 7.3×10⁻⁸ to 1.3×10⁻⁶, depending on canonical statistical model assumptions.
- Hadronic evolution in Au+Au and Pb+Pb lasts more than 15 fm/c; in pp collisions the collision time is only a few fm/c.

## Context
This work extends prior transport model studies (Danielewicz 1991, Oh 2008, Oh 2009, Oliinychenko 2019) that treated light nuclei as point particles, by including finite-size effects via Wigner functions. It contrasts with the statistical hadronization model (Andronic 2018) and the coalescence model (Schenke 1999, Bellini 2020), which correspond to equilibrium limits of the present dynamical approach. The method enables future studies of other light nuclei (⁴He, ⁴Li, hypertriton, exotic states like X(3872) and Ω-dibaryon) and has implications for cosmic-ray dark matter searches.
