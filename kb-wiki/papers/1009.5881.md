# Disentangling correlations in Multiple Parton Interactions

> Multiple Parton Interactions are the tool to obtain information on the correlations between partons in the hadron structure. Partons may be correlated in all degrees of freedom and all different correlation terms contribute to the cross section. The contributions due to the different parton flavors can be isolated, at least to some extent, by selecting properly the final state. In the case of high energy proton-proton collisions, the effects of correlations in the transverse coordinates and in fractional momenta are, on the contrary, unavoidably mixed in the final observables. The standard way to quantify the strength of double parton interactions is by the value of the effective cross section and a small value of the effective cross section may be originated both by the relatively short transverse distance between the pairs of partons undergoing the double interaction and by a large dispersion of the distribution in multiplicity of the multi-parton distributions. The aim of the present paper is to show how the effects of longitudinal and transverse correlations may be disentangled by taking into account the additional information provided by double parton interactions in high energy proton-deuteron collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the effects of longitudinal (multiplicity) and transverse (spatial) correlations in multiple parton interactions, which are unavoidably mixed in proton-proton collisions, can be disentangled by measuring double parton scattering in proton-deuteron collisions, provided one separates the contributions where one or both nucleons in the deuteron undergo a hard interaction.

## Method

The authors derive the full expression for the double parton scattering cross section in proton-deuteron collisions using a covariant diagrammatic approach, treating the deuteron with a nonrelativistic wave function in light-cone variables. They separate the cross section into two distinct contributions: one where both nucleons in the deuteron interact with large momentum exchange (σ_double^pD|_2) and one where only a single nucleon interacts while the other is a spectator (σ_double^pD|_1). The double parton distribution is parametrized in terms of two correlation parameters: K (measuring deviations from a Poissonian multiplicity distribution) and λ (measuring correlations in transverse coordinates), and the authors show how comparing the pp and pD cross sections isolates these parameters.

## Key numbers

- The contribution σ_double^pD|_2 to the proton-deuteron double parton scattering cross section is of order σ_eff/(2πR_D^2) ≈ 5% as an order-of-magnitude estimate.
- The effective cross section in pp collisions is related to correlation parameters as σ_eff^pp = 2πR²(2-λ)/K², where R is the nucleon transverse size.
- The correction from the finite longitudinal width of the deuteron wave function is estimated as (1/2)∫(Z-1)²D(Z)dZ ≈ 2/(R²M_D²) ≈ 0.5%, roughly one order of magnitude smaller than the correction due to the transverse structure of the deuteron.
- The double parton scattering cross section in pp collisions with identical interactions is σ_double^pp = (1/2)K²σ_S²/[2πR²(2-λ)], where σ_S is the single scattering cross section.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of multiple parton interactions (MPI) developed by Paver and Treleani (1982, 1984) and addresses the problem of separating correlation effects that Strikman and collaborators (2001, 2010) proposed could be studied using heavy nuclei. The authors instead propose using light nuclei (deuteron) to avoid the complications of rescattering and energy loss in heavy nuclei. The paper enables a model-independent extraction of longitudinal and transverse parton correlations by comparing double parton scattering cross sections in pp and pD collisions, which is relevant for interpreting MPI measurements at the LHC.
