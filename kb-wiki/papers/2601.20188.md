# Baryon Transition Form Factors from Dynamical Coupled-Channel Analyses

> In this talk, the electromagnetic transition form factors from the nucleon ground state to twelve $N^*$ and $Δ$ states are exhibited and discussed. Those results are extracted through a comprehensive coupled-channel approach -- the Juelich-Bonn-Washington model, with the center-of-mass energy ranging from $1.13$ GeV to $1.8$ GeV, and the photon virtuality up to $8$ GeV$^2$. The extraction is based on $10^5$ electroproduction data points of $πN$, $ηN$, and $KΛ$ channels, with additionally about $5\times 10^4$ data points in the hadronic sector as well as photoproductions as boundary conditions. The form factors are defined from the residues at the corresponding resonance poles in the multipole amplitudes. Uncertainties are also estimated by the exploration of the parameter space. The qualitative behavior of the transition form factors of $N(1440)$ and $Δ(1232)$ here are in agreement with the previous studies, while for the other states, there has not been literature results that are also defined at the resonance poles.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic transition form factors (TFFs) for twelve N* and Delta states are extracted from a dynamical coupled-channel analysis, defined at the resonance poles rather than from Breit-Wigner parameters; the results for N(1440) and Delta(1232) agree qualitatively with previous studies, but for the other ten states no pole-defined literature results exist for direct comparison.

## Method

The Juelich-Bonn-Washington dynamical coupled-channel model was used, where hadronic final-state interactions are described by interaction potentials iterated in Lippmann-Schwinger-like equations, and electroproduction amplitudes are constructed via Watson's final state theorem with constraints such as Siegert's theorem. The multipole amplitudes for coupled-channel electroproduction of pi N, eta N, and K Lambda were determined by fitting to 10^5 electroproduction data points plus about 5 x 10^4 hadronic and photoproduction data points as boundary conditions. The TFFs (A_{1/2}, A_{3/2}, S_{1/2}) were then defined from the residues of the Laurent expansion of the multipole amplitudes at the resonance poles on the second Riemann sheet, with uncertainties estimated from four fit solutions exploring the parameter space.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range: 1.13 GeV to 1.8 GeV
- Photon virtuality Q^2 range: 0 to 8 GeV^2
- Electroproduction data points used: 10^5 from pi N, eta N, and K Lambda channels
- Additional hadronic and photoproduction data points: about 5 x 10^4
- Twelve states analyzed: N(1535), N(1650), N(1440), N(1710), N(1720), N(1520), N(1675), N(1680), Delta(1620), Delta(1232), Delta(1600), and Delta(1700)
- Isospin factors in TFF definition: C_{1/2} = -sqrt(3), C_{3/2} = sqrt(2/3)

## Context

This work extends the Juelich-Bonn-Washington model (Mai 2021, Mai 2023) from hadronic scattering and photoproduction into electroproduction, providing pole-defined TFFs that are mathematically rigorous and independent of model parametrization. It contrasts with unitary isobar models such as MAID (Drechsel 2007, Tiator 2016) and the ANL-Osaka model (Kamano 2018), which have provided TFFs but with different definitions; for Delta(1232) the real parts agree well with both MAID and ANL-Osaka results within uncertainties, while some discrepancies appear in Im A_{3/2} at low Q^2. The results enable future extension of the center-of-mass energy to 1.9 GeV to extract TFFs for higher-lying resonances, and provide a foundation for comparing pole-defined TFFs with quark model predictions, Dyson-Schwinger approaches, and lattice QCD calculations.
