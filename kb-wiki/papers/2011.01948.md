# New Developments in Flavor Evolution of a Dense Neutrino Gas

> Neutrino-neutrino refraction dominates the flavor evolution in core-collapse supernovae, neutron-star mergers, and the early universe. Ordinary neutrino flavor conversion develops on timescales determined by the vacuum oscillation frequency. However, when the neutrino density is large enough, collective flavor conversion may arise because of pairwise neutrino scattering. Pairwise conversion is deemed to be fast as it is expected to occur on timescales that depend on the neutrino-neutrino interaction energy (i.e., on the neutrino number density) and is regulated by the angular distributions of electron neutrinos and antineutrinos. The enigmatic phenomenon of fast pairwise conversion has been overlooked for a long time. However, because of the fast conversion rate, pairwise conversion may possibly occur in the proximity of the neutrino decoupling region with yet to be understood implications for the hydrodynamics of astrophysical sources and the synthesis of the heavy elements. We review the physics of this fascinating phenomenon and its implications for neutrino-dense sources.

## Key claim

This review argues that fast pairwise neutrino conversions, which develop on timescales determined by the neutrino-neutrino interaction strength rather than the vacuum oscillation frequency, may occur in the proximity of the neutrino decoupling region in core-collapse supernovae, neutron-star mergers, and the early universe; however, the authors emphasize that the existence of a flavor instability is a necessary but not sufficient condition for significant flavor conversion, and the final flavor outcome is strongly affected by the non-linear regime that is not captured by linear stability analysis.

## Method

The authors review the mean-field equations of motion for neutrino flavor evolution in dense media, including vacuum, matter, and neutrino-neutrino interaction terms. They apply linear stability analysis to the linearized equations of motion to identify conditions for exponential growth of off-diagonal density matrix elements, examining both temporal instabilities (Im(Ω) ≠ 0) and spatial instabilities (Im(k) ≠ 0). The review then explores the phenomenology of fast pairwise conversions through numerical solutions of a simplified homogeneous, mono-energetic, two-flavor neutrino gas with specified angular distributions, varying parameters such as neutrino energy and the neutrino self-interaction strength.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum oscillation frequency is ω = Δm²/2E ≃ 6.3 km⁻¹ / E[MeV], where Δm² is the largest squared mass difference and E ≃ O(15) MeV is the typical neutrino energy.
- The neutrino-neutrino interaction strength μ = √2 G_F (n_ν + n_ν̄) is O(10⁵) km⁻¹ in the proximity of the neutrino decoupling region.
- For the numerical examples, the authors adopt μ = 10³ km⁻¹, Δm² = +2.5 × 10⁻³ eV² (normal ordering), and θ_V = 10⁻⁶, with neutrino energies of E = 1, 10, and 10⁴ MeV.
- The growth rate of fast flavor instabilities is proportional to μ, while slow instabilities have growth rate O(√|ω μ|).

## Context

This review extends the understanding of neutrino-neutrino interactions beyond the classical "slow" conversion picture established by Duan (2006), Fogli (2007), and others, where flavor conversions develop on timescales determined by the vacuum oscillation frequency and produce spectral splits. The work builds on the realization by Sawyer (2005, 2008) that pairwise neutrino scattering can trigger fast conversions even when the vacuum term is zero, and on the linear stability analysis framework introduced by Banerjee (2011) and extended by Chakraborty (2016) and Izaguirre (2016). The review enables future work on the non-linear regime of fast conversions and their implications for supernova dynamics and nucleosynthesis, while noting that a full seven-dimensional transport solution remains computationally intractable with current methods.
