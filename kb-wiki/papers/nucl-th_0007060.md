# Effective Field Theories, Landau-Migdal Fermi-Liquid Theory and Effective Chiral Lagrangians for Nuclear Matter

> We reinterpret Landau-Migdal Fermi-liquid theory of nuclear matter as an effective chiral field theory with a Fermi surface. The effective field theory is formulated in terms of a chiral Lagrangian with its {\it mass and coupling parameters} scaling a la Brown-Rho and with the Landau-Migdal parameters identified as the fixed points of the field theory. We show how this mapping works out for response functions to the EM vector current and then using the same reasoning, make prediction on nuclear axial current, in particular on the enhanced axial-charge transitions in heavy nuclei. We speculate on how to extrapolate the resulting theory which appears to be sound both theoretically and empirically up to normal nuclear matter density $ρ_0$, to hitherto unexplored higher density regime relevant to relativistic heavy-ion processes and to cold compact (neutron) stars.

## Key claim

Landau-Migdal Fermi-liquid theory of nuclear matter can be reinterpreted as an effective chiral field theory with a Fermi surface, where the Landau-Migdal parameters are identified as fixed points of the field theory; this mapping is shown to work for electromagnetic vector current response functions and is then extended to predict nuclear axial current properties, particularly enhanced axial-charge transitions in heavy nuclei, with the caveat that the axial charge result is offered as a "possible" one rather than rigorously proven.

## Method

The author constructs an effective chiral Lagrangian with density-dependent masses and coupling constants that scale according to Brown-Rho (BR) scaling, treating the parameters as functionals of the baryon density operator to maintain thermodynamic consistency. The Lagrangian is then mapped onto Landau-Migdal Fermi-liquid theory by computing the Landau effective mass, the quasiparticle interaction parameters, and the response functions for vector and axial currents in the limit of slowly varying external fields. The particle-hole contributions (back-flow) are calculated using the full quasiparticle interaction expanded in Legendre polynomials, and the results are compared with the predictions from the effective chiral Lagrangian at the mean-field level.

## Key numbers

- Saturation properties from BR scaling fit: nucleon effective mass ratio \( m_N^\star/M = 0.62 \), binding energy \( E/A - M = -16.0 \) MeV, Fermi momentum \( k_F = 257 \) MeV, compression modulus \( K = 296 \) MeV
- Best empirical values for comparison: \( m_N^\star/M = 0.61 \pm 0.03 \), \( E/A - M = -16.0 \pm 0.1 \) MeV, \( k_F = 256 \pm 2 \) MeV, \( K = 250 \pm 50 \) MeV
- Scaling parameter \( y = 0.28 \) giving \( \Phi(\rho_0) \approx 0.78 \) for the BR scaling factor at normal nuclear matter density
- In-medium scalar mass at saturation: \( m_\phi^\star(\rho_0) = 546 \) MeV from BR scaling, compared to \( 500 \pm 20 \) MeV from sophisticated non-linear mean-field models
- Free-space values used: nucleon mass \( M = 938 \) MeV, omega meson mass \( m_\omega = 783 \) MeV, scalar mass \( m_\phi = 700 \) MeV, vector coupling \( g_v = 15.8 \), scalar coupling \( h = 6.62 \)

## Context

This work extends the reformulation of Landau's Fermi-liquid theory as a modern effective field theory (Shankar, 1994) to Migdal's theory of nuclear matter, building on earlier work by the author and collaborators (FRSall, songPR). The approach enables extrapolation of nuclear matter properties known at normal density to higher densities relevant to relativistic heavy-ion collisions and neutron star interiors, which is not possible with standard quantum many-body methods. The paper also connects to the non-topological soliton picture of nuclear matter (Lynn; Lutz, Friman and Appel, 1998) and to the Weinberg program for effective field theories in few-nucleon systems, while the axial charge prediction opens a path for testing the theory against experimental data on enhanced axial-charge transitions in heavy nuclei.
