# Lambda-N scattering length from the reaction gamma d -> K^+ Lambda n

> The perspects of utilizing the strangeness-production reaction gamma d -> K^+ Lambda n for the determination of the Lambda n low-energy scattering parameters are investigated. The spin observables that need to be measured in order to isolate the Lambda n singlet (1S0) and triplet (3S1) states are identified. Possible kinematical regions where the extraction of the Lambda n scattering lengths might be feasible are discussed.

## Key claim

The reaction $\gamma d \to K^+\Lambda n$ can be used to extract the $\Lambda n$ scattering lengths, but only under specific kinematical conditions that avoid the quasi-free production mechanism; the method works for forward kaons at photon energies just below the quasi-free peak (around 850 MeV) or for backward kaons at any energy, though backward rates are predicted to be very small.

## Method

The authors apply a dispersion integral method that factorizes the production amplitude into a part containing the elastic $\Lambda N$ phase shift and a slowly varying function, allowing extraction of the scattering length from the invariant-mass dependence of the differential cross section near the $\Lambda N$ threshold. They derive the spin observables needed to separate the $\Lambda n$ singlet ($^1S_0$) and triplet ($^3S_1$) states, requiring kaon emission in forward or backward direction and, for the singlet case, double polarization measurements. The method is tested on model calculations by Yamamura et al. (2006) that include the $\Lambda n$ final-state interaction using the NSC97f $YN$ force and the Nijmegen93 deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- The required c.m. momentum of the initial photon to produce the $K\Lambda N$ system at threshold is 584 MeV/c.
- For forward kaons at $E_\gamma = 1300$ MeV (excess energy $\epsilon = 349$ MeV), the quasi-free production peak distorts the $\Lambda n$ invariant mass spectrum, making extraction impossible.
- At $E_\gamma = 850$ MeV ($\epsilon = 41.5$ MeV) and forward kaons, applying the dispersion integral method to the model yields a $^3S_1$ scattering length of $-2.06$ fm, compared to the input model value of $-1.70$ fm (difference of 0.4 fm).
- The theoretical uncertainty from the method itself is estimated at 0.3 fm; including possible effects from resonances in the $K\Lambda$ system adds up to 0.2 fm, giving a total theoretical uncertainty of 0.5 fm.
- The quasi-free peak appears when the excess energy in the $\Lambda n K^+$ system is around 90 MeV or more.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dispersion integral method developed for $pp \to K^+\Lambda p$ (Gasparyan 2003, 2005) to the photon-induced reaction $\gamma d \to K^+\Lambda n$, which had been proposed for experiments at CEBAF, ELSA, JLAB, and MAMI-C. The paper identifies that the quasi-free production mechanism, not present in the $pp$ reaction, imposes a lower energy limit for forward-angle measurements, but shows that at $E_\gamma \approx 850$ MeV the method works with similar accuracy to the hadronic reaction. The results enable cross-checking of $\Lambda N$ scattering lengths extracted from $pp \to K^+\Lambda p$ and provide a roadmap for experimental separation of singlet and triplet states using polarization observables.
