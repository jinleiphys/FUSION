# Electric multipole response of the halo nucleus $^6$He

> The role of different continuum components in the weakly-bound nucleus $^6$He is studied by coupling unbound spd-waves of $^5$He by means of simple pairing contact-delta interaction. The results of our previous investigations in a model space containing only p-waves, showed the collective nature of the ground state and allowed the calculation of the electric quadrupole transitions. We extend this simple model by including also sd-continuum neutron states and we investigate the electric monopole, dipole and octupole response of the system for transitions to the continuum, discussing the contribution of different configurations.

## Key claim

The authors extend a simple pairing-contact-delta model of the halo nucleus $^6$He from a previous p-wave-only basis to include sd-continuum neutron states, and they claim that this extended model space allows them to calculate the electric monopole, dipole, and octupole response of the system, with the caveat that for higher multipolarities ($J=1^-$, $2^+$, $3^-$) there is no clear-cut strategy to determine the exact pairing strength, so multiple values are tested.

## Method

The authors construct two-particle states of $^6$He by coupling unbound spd-wave continuum single-particle states of the $^5$He subsystem, generated with a Woods-Saxon potential (depth $V_0=-42.6$ MeV, $r_0=1.2$ fm, $a=0.9$ fm, spin-orbit coefficient $V_{ls}=8.5$ MeV). They use an attractive contact-delta pairing interaction $-g\delta(\vec r_1 - \vec r_2)$ and diagonalize the resulting matrix for $J=0^+$, $1^-$, $2^+$, and $3^-$ states, with basis sizes up to $N=100$ for the ground state and $N=50$ for continuum states. The electric transition probabilities from the ground state to continuum states are then computed for monopole ($\lambda=0$), dipole ($\lambda=1$), and octupole ($\lambda=3$) operators using effective charges.

## Key numbers

- The ground state matter radius $R_m$ is $2.37674$ fm, consistent with the value $2.37$ fm from Myo (2007).
- The mean square distance between valence neutrons $\langle r_{NN}^2\rangle$ is $28.8404$ fm$^2$, compared to $23.2324$ fm$^2$ from Myo (2007) and $21.3$ fm$^2$ from Hagino (2005).
- The mean square distance of the valence neutron center of mass from the core $\langle r_{c-2N}^2\rangle$ is $7.21011$ fm$^2$, compared to $9.9225$ fm$^2$ from Myo (2007) and $13.2$ fm$^2$ from Hagino (2005).
- The ground state wavefunction components are: $(1p_{3/2})^2$ at $0.897$, $(1p_{1/2})^2$ at $0.080$, $(1d_{5/2})^2$ at $0.009$, $(2s_{1/2})^2$ at $0.008$, and $(1d_{3/2})^2$ at $0.005$.
- The total integrated monopole strength is $2682.97$ fm$^4$, compared to a non-energy-weighted sum rule estimate of about $2800$ fm$^4$ using formulas from Meyer (1990).
- The two-particle density shows a di-neutron configuration contribution of approximately $64\%$ and a cigar-like configuration contribution of approximately $36\%$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Fortunato et al., 2016) that only included p-wave continuum states and calculated quadrupole transitions, by adding sd-continuum neutron states to study monopole, dipole, and octupole responses. The calculations are motivated by recent experimental measurements at GANIL (Moug et al., 2008; Povoroznyk et al., 2010) on continuum resonances in $^6$He, and the results are compared with theoretical predictions from Myo (2007), Hagino (2005), and other approaches such as the Gamow Shell Model (Michel et al., 2002; Hagen et al., 2006) and the Complex Scaled Cluster Orbital Shell Model (Myo et al., 2007). The work enables future studies of higher multipole responses in Borromean nuclei and provides a framework for understanding the role of different continuum configurations in halo nuclei.
