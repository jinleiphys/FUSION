# Novel ansatz for charge radii in density functional theories

> Charge radii are one of the most fundamental properties of atomic nuclei characterizing their charge distributions. Though the general trend as a function of the mass number is well described by the $A^{1/3}$ rule, some fine structures, such as the evolution along the calcium isotopic chain and the corresponding odd-even staggerings, are notoriously difficult to describe both in density functional theories and ab initio methods. In this letter, we propose a novel ansatz to describe the charge radii of calcium isotopes, by adding a correction term, proportional to the number of Cooper pairs, and determined by the BCS amplitudes and a single parameter, to the charge radii calculated in the relativistic mean field model with the pairing interaction treated with the BCS method. The new ansatz yields results consistent with data not only for calcium isotopes, but also for ten other isotopic chains, including oxygen, neon, magnesium, chromium, nickel, germanium, zirconium, cadmium, tin, and lead. It is remarkable that this ansatz with a single parameter can describe nuclear charge radii throughout the periodic table, particularly the odd-even staggerings and parabolic behavior. We hope that the present study can stimulate more discussions about its nature and relation with other effects proposed to explain the odd-even staggerings of charge radii.

## Key claim

The authors propose that adding a correction term proportional to the difference in the number of Cooper pairs between neutrons and protons, determined by BCS amplitudes and a single parameter, to charge radii calculated in the relativistic mean field model can describe nuclear charge radii throughout the periodic table, particularly the odd-even staggerings and parabolic behavior that conventional density functional theories fail to reproduce.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the NL3 effective force and a state-dependent BCS method with a delta force for pairing correlations, solving the equations with harmonic oscillator basis expansion (12 shells for fermions, 20 for mesons). They added a correction term proportional to the absolute difference of the summed BCS amplitudes (u_k v_k) between neutrons and protons, scaled by a factor a_0/√A, to the conventional charge radius formula. The single parameter a_0 was fixed by fitting to the charge radius of ⁴⁴Ca, and the ansatz was then tested on ten other isotopic chains.

## Key numbers

- The correction term uses a single parameter a_0 = 0.834, fixed by fitting to the charge radius of ⁴⁴Ca.
- For tin and lead isotopes, a smaller value a_0 = 0.22 was used, fixed by the charge radius of ¹²⁶Sn.
- The conventional charge radius formula includes a proton finite size correction of 0.64 fm².
- Conventional density functional theories describe charge radii with a discrepancy at the level of 5%, while semi-microscopic mass models describe binding energies with an accuracy of less than 3 MeV (discrepancy much less than 1%).

## Context

This work extends efforts to describe charge radii of calcium isotopes, which have remained a challenge for conventional density functional theories such as the relativistic mean field model and the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method, as well as ab initio methods using chiral EFT interactions like NNLO_sat (PhysRevC.91.051301) and SRG1/SRG2 (PhysRevC.83.031301). The Fayans EDF model (PhysRevC.95.064328, nature15) and the DF3-a+ph model with particle-phonon coupling (Saperstein 2017) had partial success but could not fully describe the parabolic behavior and odd-even staggerings. The new ansatz enables future studies of all isotopic chains with experimental data, tests with particle-number-conserving methods like the FBCS method, and application to Skyrme Hartree-Fock models.
