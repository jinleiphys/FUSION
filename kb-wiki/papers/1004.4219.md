# Travels With Tony-Nucleon Structure Through Our Ages

> Nucleon structure is currently understood from a unified light-front, infinite-momentum-frame framework. The specific examples of the neutron transverse charge distribution and the shape of the proton are discussed here.

## Key claim

The neutron's transverse charge density is negative at the center, which appears to contradict previously held notions, but this result is model-independent and can be interpreted as arising from pions that penetrate to the center of the nucleon as in the Cloudy Bag Model when viewed in the infinite momentum frame.

## Method

The author uses light-front coordinates (infinite-momentum-frame) to define a true transverse charge density as a two-dimensional Fourier transform of the Dirac form factor \(F_1(Q^2)\). For the neutron, the negative-definite nature of \(F_1\) directly yields a negative central transverse charge density. The author also computes spin-dependent densities in both coordinate and momentum space using a relativistic constituent quark model with Dirac spinors, and relates these to transverse momentum distributions (TMDs) and generalized parton distributions (GPDs) accessible in experiment and lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- The neutron transverse charge density at the center \(\rho(b=0)\) is negative, derived from the negative-definite Dirac form factor \(F_1(Q^2)\) for all \(Q^2\).
- The spin-dependent density for a non-relativistic \(p_{1/2}\) state yields \(\rho({\bf r},{\bf n}) \sim \cos^2\theta\) when the polarization direction \({\bf n}\) is parallel to \({\bf r}\), and \(\sim \sin^2\theta\) for the perpendicular case.
- For quarks of high momentum in the relativistic model, peanut and donut shapes are obtained for quark spins parallel and antiparallel to the direction of nucleon polarization.

## Context

This work extends the Cloudy Bag Model approach of Thomas and collaborators (1979-1982) by reinterpreting neutron form factors in the light-front framework. The author's earlier model (Miller, 1995) predicted the rapid decrease of \(G_E/G_M\) and the flat nature of \(QF_2/F_1\). The paper enables experimental tests of proton non-sphericity through measurement of the TMD \(h_{1T}^\perp\) in semi-inclusive electroproduction or Drell-Yan reactions, and cites lattice QCD calculations showing a non-vanishing spin-dependent density that indicates the proton is not round.
