# Source Chaoticity from Two- and Three-Pion Correlations in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV?

> We consistently analyze two-pion correlation functions and three-pion correlation functions of 130$A$ GeV Au+Au collisions measured at RHIC, applying three models of partially coherent pion sources. The effect of long-lived resonances on the chaoticity in the two-pion correlation function is estimated on the basis of a statistical model. We find that the chaoticity extracted from the three-pion correlation function is consistent with that extracted from the two-pion correlation function at vanishing relative momenta after the subtraction of the contribution of the long-lived resonance decays. All three models indicate that pions are emitted from the mixture of a chaotic source and coherent sources in the 130$A$ GeV Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

After correcting for the contribution of long-lived resonance decays, the chaoticity extracted from the two-pion correlation function is consistent with that extracted from the three-pion correlation function at vanishing relative momenta, and all three partially coherent source models indicate that pions are emitted from a mixture of a chaotic source and coherent sources in 130A GeV Au+Au collisions.

## Method

The authors analyzed two-pion and three-pion correlation data from STAR for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV using three partially coherent source models (Model I: single chaotic plus single coherent source; Model II: multiple independent coherent sources; Model III: mixture of both). They estimated the reduction of the two-pion chaoticity due to long-lived resonance decays using a statistical model fit to particle ratios, obtaining temperature T=158±9 MeV and baryon chemical potential μ_B=36±6 MeV. The two- and three-pion correlation functions were simultaneously fitted with three different source function shapes (Gaussian, exponential, and cosh) to extract the chaoticity λ and the three-pion weight factor ω.

## Key numbers

- Temperature from statistical model fit: T = 158 ± 9 MeV, with μ_B = 36 ± 6 MeV (χ²/dof = 2.4/5)
- Average experimental two-pion chaoticity after resonance correction: λ̅_exp = 0.57 ± 0.06
- True chaoticity after resonance correction: λ_true = 0.93 ± 0.08
- Weight factor from cosh source function fit: ω = 0.872 ± 0.097
- Best fit parameters for Model III: ε = 0.75, α = 0.77
- For Model I: ε_pc = 0.73 ± 0.14 from λ, ε_pc = 0.65 ± 0.11 from ω
- For Model II: α_m = 12.6 ± 14 from λ, α_m = 7.6 ± 9.7 from ω

## Context

This work extends the STAR collaboration analysis (STAR_3pi) which only used three-pion correlations and found about 80% chaotic fraction, by consistently incorporating two-pion correlation data after correcting for long-lived resonance decay effects using the method of Csorgo (ZPHYS). The analysis uses three models previously developed by Heinz (PRC56) and Nakamura (PRC60, PRC61) to describe partially coherent sources. The results enable future studies of multiplicity and collision energy dependence of source chaoticity, and suggest that a "partial" Coulomb correction treating long-lived resonance pions separately (as proposed by Bowler and Sinyukov) should be incorporated in future analyses.
