# Calculation of heavy ion e+ e- pair production to all orders in Z alpha

> The heavy ion total cross section for continuum e+ e- pair production has been calculated to all orders in Z alpha. The formula resulting from an exact solution of the semiclassical Dirac equation in the ultrarelativistic limit is evaluated numerically. An energy dependent spatial cutoff of the heavy ion potential is utilized, leading to an exact formula agreeing with the known perturbative formula in the ultrarelativistic, perturbative limit. Cross sections and sample momentum distributions are evaluated for heavy ion beams at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. e+ e- pair production probabilities are found to be reduced from perturbation theory with increasing charge of the colliding heavy ions and for all energy and momentum regions investigated.

# Calculation of heavy ion e+ e- pair production to all orders in Z alpha

## Key claim
The heavy ion total cross section for continuum e+ e- pair production has been calculated to all orders in Z alpha using an exact solution of the semiclassical Dirac equation, but only for inclusive observables (the average number of pairs) and not for exclusive amplitudes of correlated electron-positron pairs. The calculation shows reductions from perturbation theory that persist across all momentum regions, contrary to earlier suggestions that Coulomb corrections would vanish at large or small transverse momenta.

## Method
The author numerically evaluates a cross section formula derived from the exact solution of the semiclassical Dirac equation in the ultrarelativistic limit, using an energy-dependent spatial cutoff of the heavy ion potential via a modified Bessel function K0(rho omega/gamma) rather than the previously used unregularized logarithmic potential. The seven-dimensional integral over electron and positron momenta and virtual photon transverse momentum is evaluated directly on uniform logarithmic meshes in each radial momentum dimension without Monte Carlo methods. Calculations labeled "exact" use the numerically evaluated expression for the transverse potential integrals F_{A,B}(k), while perturbative calculations use the analytical form 4 pi Z alpha / (k^2 + omega^2/gamma^2).

## Key numbers
- For Pb + Au at SPS (gamma = 9.2): exact total cross section 2670 barns, perturbative 3720 barns, reduction of 28%
- For Au + Au at RHIC (gamma = 100): exact total cross section 28,600 barns, perturbative 34,600 barns, reduction of 17%
- For Pb + Pb at LHC (gamma = 2960): exact total cross section 201,000 barns, perturbative 227,000 barns, reduction of 11%
- For S + Au at SPS (gamma = 9.2): exact total cross section 119.7 barns, perturbative 141.6 barns, reduction of 15%
- The positive contribution from multiple photon emission from both ions to the difference between exact and perturbative results is +80 barns for Pb+Au at SPS, +220 barns for Au+Au at RHIC, and +600 barns for Pb+Pb at LHC
- In the STAR acceptance at RHIC, the exact result is 17% lower than perturbation theory, giving an estimated Delta sigma = -0.17 sigma_QED

## Context
This work extends the earlier analytical solutions of the Dirac equation for continuum e+ e- pair production obtained independently by Segev and Wells (2002) and Baltz and McLerran (2002), and the approximate total cross section calculations of Lee and Milstein (2000) that included Coulomb corrections. The author previously calculated bound-electron pair production with a predicted reduction of less than 10% for Au+Au at RHIC. The present numerical results disagree with the earlier argument by Ivanov, Schiller, and Serbo (1998) that Coulomb corrections contribute mostly at q_perp = m_e and disappear for larger and smaller transverse momenta. The CERN SPS data from Vane et al. (1997) showed perturbative scaling with Z^2, which appears inconsistent with the predicted Coulomb corrections; the author calls for more precise data at RHIC or LHC to resolve this puzzle.
