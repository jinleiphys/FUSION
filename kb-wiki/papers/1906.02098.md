# Superfluid Phase Transitions and Effects of Thermal Pairing Fluctuations in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> We investigate superfluid phase transitions of asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature ($T$) and density ($ρ$) with a low proton fraction ($Y_{\rm p} \le 0.2$) which is relevant to the inner crust and outer core of neutron stars. A strong-coupling theory developed for two-component atomic Fermi gases is generalized to the four-component case and is applied to the system of spin-$1/2$ neutrons and protons. The empirical phase shifts of neutron-neutron (nn), proton-proton (pp) and neutron-proton (np) interactions up to $k = 2$ ${\rm fm}^{-1}$ are described by multi-rank separable potentials. We show that (i) the critical temperature of the neutron superfluidity $T_{\rm c}^{\rm nn}$ at $Y_{\rm p}=0$ agrees well with Monte Carlo data at low densities and takes a maximum value $T_{\rm c}^{\rm nn}=1.68$ MeV at $ρ/ρ_0 = 0.14$ with $ρ_0=0.17$ fm$^{-3}$, (ii) the critical temperature of the proton superconductivity $T_{\rm c}^{\rm pp}$ for $Y_{\rm p} \le 0.2$ is substantially suppressed at low densities due to np-pairing fluctuations and starts to dominate over $T_{\rm c}^{\rm nn}$ only above $ρ/ρ_0 = 0.70$ $(0.77)$ for $Y_p =0.1$ $(0.2)$, and (iii) the deuteron condensation temperature $T_{\rm c}^{\rm d}$ is suppressed at $Y_{\rm p}\le 0.2$ due to the large mismatch of the two Fermi surfaces.

## Key claim

The authors show that in asymmetric nuclear matter with low proton fraction (Y_p ≤ 0.2), the critical temperature of proton superconductivity is substantially suppressed at low densities due to neutron-proton pairing fluctuations, and only dominates over neutron superfluidity above ρ/ρ_0 = 0.70 (for Y_p = 0.1) or 0.77 (for Y_p = 0.2), a condition that depends on the specific separable potential used to describe nuclear interactions.

## Method

The authors generalize a strong-coupling theory (Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink or NSR theory) originally developed for two-component atomic Fermi gases to a four-component system of spin-1/2 neutrons and protons. They describe the empirical phase shifts of neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton interactions up to k = 2 fm^{-1} using multi-rank separable potentials (rank-one SEP1 and rank-three SEP3 and SEP3'), and compute the critical temperatures for neutron superfluidity, proton superconductivity, and deuteron condensation via the Thouless criterion applied to the pair-correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron superfluidity critical temperature T_c^{nn} = 1.68 MeV at ρ/ρ_0 = 0.14, where ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- For Y_p = 0.1, proton superconductivity T_c^{pp} dominates over T_c^{nn} only above ρ/ρ_0 = 0.70
- For Y_p = 0.2, proton superconductivity T_c^{pp} dominates over T_c^{nn} only above ρ/ρ_0 = 0.77
- Neutron-neutron interaction on the Fermi surface turns repulsive for k_F,n > 1.39 fm^{-1}
- The neutron drip density is ρ_drip/ρ_0 ≃ 1.5 × 10^{-3}
- The crust-core transition density is approximately ρ/ρ_0 ∼ 0.5

## Context

This work extends the NSR strong-coupling theory previously applied to pure neutron matter by three of the authors (Pieter van Wyk and collaborators, 2018) to the more realistic case of asymmetric nuclear matter with protons. It also builds on prior NSR calculations using renormalization-group based low-momentum interactions (Ramanan and collaborators) and lattice Monte Carlo simulations for pionless effective field theory (Abe and collaborators). The results are relevant for understanding superfluid phases in the inner crust and outer core of neutron stars, and the framework enables future studies of pairing fluctuations in neutron star matter including the effects of charge neutrality and beta equilibrium.
