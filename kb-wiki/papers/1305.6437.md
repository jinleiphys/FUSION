# Shape evolution of giant resonances in Nd and Sm isotopes

> Giant multipole resonances in Nd and Sm isotopes are studied by employing the quasiparticle-random-phase approximation on the basis of the Skyrme energy-density-functional method. Deformation effects on giant resonances are investigated in these isotopes which manifest a typical nuclear shape change from spherical to prolate shapes. The peak energy, the broadening, and the deformation splitting of the isoscalar giant monopole (ISGMR) and quadrupole (ISGQR) resonances agree well with measurements. The magnitude of the peak splitting and the fraction of the energy-weighted strength in the lower peak of the ISGMR reflect the nuclear deformation. The experimental data on ISGMR, ISGDR, and ISGQR are consistent with the nuclear-matter incompressibility $K \simeq 210-230$ MeV and the effective mass $m^*_0/m \simeq 0.8-0.9$. However, the high-energy octupole resonance (HEOR) in $^{144}$Sm seems to indicates a smaller effective mass, $m^*_0/m \simeq 0.7-0.8$. A further precise measurement of HEOR is desired to determine the effective mass.

## Key claim

The experimental data on ISGMR, ISGDR, and ISGQR in Nd and Sm isotopes are consistent with nuclear-matter incompressibility K around 210-230 MeV and effective mass m*_0/m around 0.8-0.9, but the high-energy octupole resonance (HEOR) in 144Sm seems to indicate a smaller effective mass of m*_0/m around 0.7-0.8, suggesting that further precise measurement of HEOR is needed to determine the effective mass.

## Method

The authors performed deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in cylindrical coordinates using the SkM* Skyrme energy-density functional, then solved the quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (QRPA) in the quasiparticle basis to compute transition strength distributions for giant resonances in Nd and Sm isotopes. The calculations used a lattice mesh size of 0.6 fm with box boundary conditions, a quasiparticle energy cutoff of 60 MeV, and a smearing width of 2 MeV to simulate spreading effects. The parallelized code employed 512 processors for matrix element computation and the ScaLAPACK pdsyev subroutine for diagonalization.

## Key numbers

- ISGMR peak energy in 144Sm: Ex = 14.8 MeV with width Gamma = 2.61 MeV (calculated), compared to experimental Ex = 15.40 ± 0.30 MeV and Gamma = 3.40 ± 0.20 MeV
- In 154Sm, the lower-energy peak (8-13.5 MeV) of ISGMR exhausts 31.4% of the IS monopole EWSR value (3.38 × 10^5 fm^4 MeV), while the higher-energy peak (13.5-19 MeV) exhausts 60.6%
- K-splitting for ISGQR in 154Sm: E(K=2) - E(K=0) = 2.8 MeV
- ISGQR centroid energy follows 65.6 × A^(-1/3) line, consistent with empirical (64.0 ± 1.7) × A^(-1/3)
- IVGQR energy follows approximately 129.5 × A^(-1/3) for Nd and 130.3 × A^(-1/3) for Sm isotopes
- HEOR centroid energy follows 124.8 × A^(-1/3) line
- In 154Sm, low-lying octupole states carry 6.5% of IS octupole EWSR in 0-3 MeV and 24% in 0-7 MeV
- Calculated octupole strength carries 51-53% of EWSR in HEOR energy region (17-33 MeV)
- Spurious states in 154Sm appear at 0.35 MeV (K^π=0^+), 0.34 MeV (1^+), 1.46i MeV (0^-), and 1.60i MeV (1^-)

## Context

This work extends the deformed Skyrme-QRPA approach developed by Yoshida et al. (2008) from light systems to heavy deformed nuclei using massively parallel computing, enabling systematic study of giant resonances across the shape phase transition from spherical to prolate nuclei in Nd and Sm isotopes. The results are compared with experimental data from Texas A&M University (Youngblood et al., 2004) and RCNP Osaka (Itoh et al., 2002, 2003), as well as with earlier theoretical approaches including the fluiddynamical model and scaling model of Nix and Sierk (1985), the cranking model of Abgrall et al. (1980), and the doubly-stretched pairing-plus-quadrupole model of Kishimoto et al. (1975). The work enables future determination of nuclear-matter incompressibility and effective mass from giant resonance measurements, particularly calling for more precise HEOR measurements.
