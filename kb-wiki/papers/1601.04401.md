# The correlation between quarter point angle and nuclear radius

> The main objective of the present work is to correlate quarter-point angle and nuclear radius or nuclear matter distribution. Various phenomenological formulae with parameters for strong absorption radius Rs are obtained and compared by fitting the experimental data of quarter point angle extracted from nuclear elastic scattering reaction systems. The parameterized formula of Rs related to the isospin and binding energy is recommended, that gives a good reproduction of nuclear matter radii of halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The quarter-point angle of elastic scattering is correlated with nuclear matter radius, and a phenomenological formula (PF4) that includes binding energy per nucleon and isospin dependence can reproduce nuclear matter radii of halo nuclei, reducing the deviation between experimental data and the theoretical quarter-point angle function better than simpler radius formulas.

## Method

The authors extracted experimental quarter-point angles from elastic scattering angular distributions for tightly bound, weakly bound, and halo projectiles. They fitted four phenomenological formulas (PF1 to PF4) for the strong absorption radius R_int to the experimental data, with PF4 incorporating binding energy per nucleon and the symmetry parameter I = (A-2Z)/A. The goodness of fit was quantified using the deviation parameter eta = (x - x_PF)/x_PF and the differences between mean eta values for halo and tightly bound systems (Delta-eta) and between two subgroups of tightly bound systems (Delta-eta_TB).

## Key numbers

* Fitted parameter a for PF2 (R_int = a*A_p^(1/3) + b*A_t^(1/3), with b=1 fixed): 1.123 fm for tightly bound, 1.187 fm for weakly bound, and 1.333 fm for halo projectiles.
* Fitted parameters for PF3 (isospin-dependent formula): r0 = 1.0152 fm, r1 = 0.6383 fm, r2 = -1.2781 fm, a_z = -0.2981 fm.
* Fitted parameters for PF4 (binding-energy-dependent formula): lambda0 = 0.9776 fm, lambda1 = 0.2475 fm, lambda2 = -0.1492 fm/MeV^(1/2), lambda3 = 10.7186 fm/MeV.
* R-Square values: 0.998585 for PF3 and 0.9986632 for PF4.
* Delta-eta (difference between mean eta for halo and tightly bound nuclei): 0.0374 (PF1), 0.0077 (PF2), 0.0342 (PF3), 0.0059 (PF4).
* Delta-eta_TB (difference between two subgroups of tightly bound nuclei): 0.056 (PF1), 0.040 (PF2), 0.039 (PF3), 0.033 (PF4).
* Experimental rms matter radii for halo nuclei: e.g., ^6He 2.30 +/- 0.07 fm, ^11Li 3.34_(+0.04)(-0.08) fm, ^14Be 3.10 +/- 0.15 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that used the simple formula R_int = A_p^(1/3) + A_t^(1/3) (PF1, as employed by L. Jin et al.) to compare quarter-point angles of different reaction systems. It builds on the observation that nuclear size is correlated to the quarter-point angle, and on prior work by Wang et al. relating nuclear rms radius to binding energy per nucleon. The recommended PF4 formula enables a new experimental method to extract nuclear matter radii of unstable and halo nuclei from elastic scattering quarter-point angles, complementing existing techniques such as electron scattering, isotope shift, and interaction cross section measurements.
