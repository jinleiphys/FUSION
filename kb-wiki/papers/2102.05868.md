# Spectrum of the doubly charmed molecular pentaquarks in chiral effective field theory

> We perform a systematic study on the interactions of the $Σ_c^{(*)}D^{(*)}$ systems within the framework of chiral effective field theory. We introduce the contact term, one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange contributions to describe the short-, long-, and intermediate-range interactions. The low energy constants of the $Σ_c^{(*)}D^{(*)}$ systems are estimated from the $N\bar{N}$ scattering data by introducing a quark level Lagrangian. With three solutions of LECs, all the $Σ_c^{(*)}D^{(*)}$ systems with isospin $I=1/2$ can form bound states, in which different inputs of LECs may lead to distinguishable mass spectra. In addition, we also investigate the interactions of the charmed-bottom $Σ_c^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$, $Σ_b^{(*)}D^{(*)}$, and $Σ_b^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ systems. Among the obtained bound states, the bindings become deeper when the reduced masses of the corresponding systems are heavier.

## Key claim

All $\Sigma_c^{(*)}D^{(*)}$ systems with isospin $I=1/2$ can form bound states under three different sets of low-energy constants (LECs) estimated from $N\bar{N}$ scattering data via a quark-level Lagrangian, but the resulting mass spectra are distinguishable depending on which LEC input is used.

## Method

The authors constructed effective potentials for the $\Sigma_c^{(*)}D^{(*)}$ systems within chiral effective field theory, including contact terms (short-range), one-pion-exchange (long-range), and two-pion-exchange (intermediate-range) contributions. The low-energy constants for the contact terms were estimated by relating hadron-level contact potentials to quark-level contact potentials, with the quark-level couplings determined from fits to $N\bar{N}$ scattering data from Kang (2013). The Schrödinger equation was then solved to search for bound states.

## Key numbers

- Mass splittings used: $\delta_a = M_{\Sigma_c^*} - M_{\Sigma_c} \simeq 65$ MeV, $\delta_b = m_{D^*} - m_D = 142.0$ MeV, $\delta_c = M_{\Sigma_c} - M_{\Lambda_c} \simeq 168.5$ MeV, $\delta_d = M_{\Sigma_c^*} - M_{\Lambda_c} \simeq 233.5$ MeV
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV
- Axial couplings: $g = -0.59$ for charmed mesons, $g_2 = -0.60$, $g_4 = 1.04$ for charmed baryons
- Three sets of LECs used: Case 1 ($c_s = -5.84$ GeV$^{-2}$, $c_t = 2.50$ GeV$^{-2}$), Case 2 ($c_s = -8.10$ GeV$^{-2}$, $c_t = 0.65$ GeV$^{-2}$), Case 3 ($c_s = -7.46$ GeV$^{-2}$, $c_t = 1.02$ GeV$^{-2}$)
- Cutoff parameter: $\Lambda = 0.4$ GeV with $n=3$ in the Gaussian regulator

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach previously applied to $\Sigma_c^{(*)}\bar{D}^{(*)}$ systems by Wang (2019) to the doubly charmed $\Sigma_c^{(*)}D^{(*)}$ systems, which had lacked a systematic study. The method of relating LECs via a quark-level Lagrangian was developed in earlier work by Wang (2020) and Meng (2019) for other heavy-flavor systems. The results provide predictions for doubly charmed molecular pentaquark states that could be searched for in future experiments, complementing compact pentaquark studies using the color-magnetic interaction model by Zhou (2018) and the chiral quark model by Yang (2020).
