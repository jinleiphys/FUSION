# Influence of finite baryon density on hadronization in nucleus-nucleus collisions via recombination

> In this paper is investigated the influence of net baryon density on baryon and meson yields in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, based on the recombination model for hadronization. Unitarity condition is used as a constraint on the model. Three cases with different assumptions on the expansion of partonic system are considered and the baryon to meson ratio is calculated for those situations.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how a nonzero net baryon density (due to nuclear stopping) affects baryon and meson yields in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, using a recombination model with a unitarity constraint that conserves constituent quark numbers. The authors show that the baryon-to-meson ratio depends strongly on the initial antiquark-to-quark density ratio and on the expansion dynamics of the partonic system, but they only consider simplified cases without full hydrodynamic calculations.

## Method

The authors formulate rate equations for meson, baryon, and antibaryon production based on the recombination model, where meson production is proportional to the product of quark and antiquark densities and baryon (antibaryon) production is proportional to the cube of the quark (antiquark) density. They impose conservation of constituent quark numbers to enforce unitarity, and consider three scenarios: (1) fixed system volume, (2) fixed quark density, and (3) a system expanding with a constant expansion rate (ν = 0.1). The equations are solved numerically for two values of the initial antiquark-to-quark density ratio (κ = 0.8 and 0.6) as a function of the parameter u, which combines the initial quark density and the relative probability of baryon versus meson production.

## Key numbers

- For the fixed-volume case with κ = 0.6, the baryon-to-meson ratio can exceed 0.8 at u = 2.
- For the fixed-volume case, the baryon-to-meson ratio increases almost linearly with u and is larger when κ is smaller (κ = 0.6 gives a larger ratio than κ = 0.8 at the same u).
- For the fixed-quark-density case, the baryon yield can exceed the meson yield when u is approximately equal to κ.
- For the hydrodynamic expansion case, the expansion rate is set to ν = 0.1; the meson yield is slightly less than in the fixed-volume case because expansion reduces the parton density.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier recombination model (Yang, 2007) which considered only zero net baryon density, to include the finite net baryon density present in real Au+Au collisions due to nuclear stopping. The paper addresses the unitarity violation in standard recombination models (where meson yield scales as density squared and baryon yield as density cubed) by introducing a dynamical hadronization process with conserved constituent quark numbers. The results enable future work to constrain the hadronization dynamics from experimental measurements of baryon and meson yields, but the authors note that more realistic investigations with physical hydrodynamic expansion are needed for reliable conclusions.
