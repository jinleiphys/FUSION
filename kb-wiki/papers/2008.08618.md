# From Chiral Kinetic Theory To Relativistic Viscous Spin Hydrodynamics

> In this work, we start with chiral kinetic theory and construct the spin hydrodynamic framework for a chiral spinor system. Using the 14-moment expansion formalism, we obtain the equations of motion of second-order dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics with non-trivial spin polarization density. In a chiral spinor system, the spin alignment effect could be treated in the same framework as the Chiral Vortical Effect (CVE). However, the quantum corrections due to fluid vorticity induce not only CVE terms in the vector/axial charge currents but also corrections to the stress tensor. In this framework, viscous corrections to the hadron spin polarization are self-consistently obtained, which will be important for the precise prediction of the polarization rate for the observed hadrons, e.g. $Λ$-hyperon.

## Key claim

The authors derive a second-order dissipative relativistic spin hydrodynamics from chiral kinetic theory, showing that quantum corrections from fluid vorticity modify not only the vector/axial currents (Chiral Vortical Effect) but also the stress tensor, and that viscous corrections to hadron spin polarization can be obtained self-consistently within this framework.

## Method

Starting from the Wigner function formalism for massless Dirac spinors, the authors perform a semiclassical (ℏ) expansion to obtain the chiral kinetic equation. They derive the equilibrium distribution function including vorticity corrections, then use the 14-moment expansion method to obtain non-equilibrium distribution functions truncated at second order in momentum. From these, they compute the dissipative currents, stress tensor, and relaxation equations for shear stress tensor and particle diffusion currents.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium axial current quantum correction in the neutral case (μ_V = μ_A = 0) is ℏ(T²ω^μ/6), leading to spin density u_λ S_eq^(λμν) = ℏ T³ ϖ^(μν)/12
- The energy density in equilibrium is ε = 7π²T⁴/60 + T²(μ_V²+μ_A²)/2 + (μ_V⁴ + 6μ_V²μ_A² + μ_A⁴)/(4π²)
- The particle number densities are n_± = (μ_±/6)(T² + μ_±²/π²)
- The shear stress relaxation equation contains a term (8/5)Pσ^(αβ) as the leading-order driving term

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory framework of Hidaka (2016) and Huang (2018) to construct a full hydrodynamic theory with spin degrees of freedom. It addresses discrepancies between theoretical predictions and STAR Collaboration measurements of Λ-hyperon polarization (2017, 2018, 2019), particularly the longitudinal polarization at different azimuthal angles where calculations by Becattini (2015, 2017) and Xia (2018) disagree with data. The framework enables self-consistent inclusion of viscous corrections to spin polarization, which were neglected in prior approaches by Becattini (2016), Xie (2015, 2019), Shi (2017), Guo (2019), Li (2017), and Karpenko (2016).
