# Superdeformation and alpha - cluster structure in 35Cl

> A superdeformed (SD) band has been identified in a non - alpha - conjugate nucleus 35Cl. It crosses the negative parity ground band above 11/2- and becomes the yrast at 15/2-. Lifetimes of all relevant states have been measured to follow the evolution of collectivity. Enhanced B(E2), B(E1) values as well as energetics provide evidences for superdeformation and existence of parity doublet cluster structure in an odd-A nucleus for the first time in A = 40 region. Large scale shell model calculations assign (sd)16(pf)3 as the origin of these states. Calculated spectroscopic factors correlate the SD states in 35Cl to those in 36Ar.

# Superdeformation and alpha-cluster structure in 35Cl

## Key claim
A superdeformed (SD) band has been identified in the non-alpha-conjugate odd-A nucleus 35Cl for the first time in the A ≈ 40 region, with evidence for a parity doublet cluster structure. The claim is hedged by the fact that the SD band is identified through a combination of enhanced B(E2) and B(E1) values and energetic systematics, with the band crossing the negative parity ground band above 11/2- and becoming yrast at 15/2-, and the interpretation of cluster structure relies on the observed alternating parity sequence and strong E1 transitions.

## Method
High-spin states in 35Cl were populated via the 12C(28Si,αp)35Cl reaction in inverse kinematics using a 110 MeV 28Si beam on a 12C target with Au backing, with γ-γ coincidence measurements performed using the INGA array of 13 Compton-suppressed clover detectors. Lifetimes of all relevant states were measured via Doppler-shift attenuation lineshape analysis using a modified version of the LINESHAPE code, with the initial recoil momentum distribution obtained from the statistical model code PACE4. Large basis shell model (LBSM) calculations using the SDPFMW Hamiltonian and SDPF.SM Hamiltonian were performed with full sd-pf orbitals above a 16O inert core, including configuration mixing between 1p-1h and 3p-3h excitations.

## Key numbers
- Average kinematic moment of inertia (KMOI) for the SD band: approximately 8 ℏ²/MeV, compared with approximately 6 ℏ²/MeV for the SD band in 28Si
- Average transition quadrupole moment Qt: approximately 0.75 eb
- Quadrupole deformation β₂ for the SD states: approximately 0.37 (for the 19/2- state)
- B(E2) values for SD states: 224(31) e²fm⁴ for the 19/2- → 15/2- transition (1862 keV), 172(22) e²fm⁴ for the 23/2- → 19/2- transition (2391 keV), and >87 e²fm⁴ for the 27/2- → 23/2- transition (3734 keV)
- B(E1) values for decay-out transitions: 1.98(2) × 10⁻³ e²fm² for the 1336 keV transition and 1.8(2) × 10⁻³ e²fm² for the 1113 keV transition
- Lifetime of the 19/2- state: 61(4) fs; lifetime of the 23/2- state: 48(6) fs; lifetime of the 15/2- state: 18(1) fs
- Band terminates at 27/2- with B(E2) approximately 13 W.u. (moderate deformation)

## Context
This work extends the study of superdeformed bands from even-even nuclei in the upper sd shell (such as 36Ar and 40Ca) to a non-alpha-conjugate odd-A nucleus, where no such band had previously been observed in the A ≈ 40 region. The SD band in 35Cl is interpreted via coupling a proton hole to the alpha-cluster SD states in 36Ar, following the Ikeda threshold energy picture (Ikeda, 1970s) where cluster configurations appear near breakup thresholds (here, the 31P + α threshold at approximately 6.5 MeV). The observed 17/2+ and 21/2+ states provide indirect experimental evidence for the existence of a negative-parity partner band of the SD band in 36Ar, which had been predicted by cluster model calculations (cluster model references from the 1970s and later) but not previously verified experimentally. This enables future searches for similar parity doublet cluster structures in other odd-A nuclei in the A ≈ 40 region.
