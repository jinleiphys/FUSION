# The electric dipole moment of the deuteron from the QCD $θ$-term

> The two-nucleon contributions to the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the deuteron, induced by the QCD $θ$-term, are calculated in the framework of effective field theory up-to-and-including next-to-next-to-leading order. In particular we find for the difference of the deuteron EDM and the sum of proton and neutron EDM induced by the QCD $θ$-term a value of $(-5.4\pm 3.9)\,\barθ\times 10^{-4}\, e$ fm. The by far dominant uncertainty comes from the CP- and isospin-violating $πNN$ coupling constant.

## Key claim

The two-nucleon contribution to the deuteron electric dipole moment (EDM) from the QCD θ-term is calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order in effective field theory, finding that the difference between the deuteron EDM and the sum of the proton and neutron EDMs is dominated by the CP- and isospin-violating πNN coupling constant g₁, whose uncertainty is the dominant source of error in the final result.

## Method

The authors used chiral effective field theory to compute the CP-violating two-nucleon potential and irreducible transition currents induced by the QCD θ-term, working up to next-to-next-to-leading order. The CP-violating πNN couplings g₀ and g₁ were derived from the θ-term via the chiral Lagrangian, with g₁ obtained from vacuum alignment and η-π⁰ mixing, and the deuteron EDM was evaluated in the Breit frame using the deuteron wave function parametrization of Machleidt (2000) with a ³D₁-state probability of 4.8%. The power counting was modified from Weinberg's original scheme to treat two-nucleon intermediate states as off-shell, with nucleon momenta identified with the pion mass Mπ.

## Key numbers

* Difference of deuteron EDM and sum of proton and neutron EDM induced by the QCD θ-term: (−5.4 ± 3.9) × 10⁻⁴ e fm × θ̄
* CP-violating πNN coupling constant g₀: (−0.018 ± 0.007) θ̄
* CP-violating πNN coupling constant g₁: (0.003 ± 0.002) θ̄
* Ratio g₁/g₀: −0.2 ± 0.1
* Quark-mass-induced part of proton-neutron mass difference δmₙₚ^(str): (2.6 ± 0.5) MeV
* Low-energy constant c₁: (−1.0 ± 0.3) GeV⁻¹
* Pion decay constant Fπ: 92.2 MeV
* Quark mass ratio ε = (mᵤ − m_d)/(mᵤ + m_d): −0.35 ± 0.10

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for nuclear EDMs from the QCD θ-term, building on earlier calculations by Khriplovich (1999), Lebedev and Olive (1999), and de Vries (2011). The calculation enables a combined analysis of future measurements of proton, neutron, and deuteron EDMs together with lattice QCD results to determine whether the θ-term is the source of observed CP violation, since direct lattice calculations for nuclear EDMs are much more challenging. The dominant uncertainty comes from the CP- and isospin-violating πNN coupling constant g₁, which is significantly enhanced compared to naive dimensional analysis estimates due to the unusually small value of g₀.
