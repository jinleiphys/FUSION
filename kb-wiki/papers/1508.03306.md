# Anisotropic distributions in a multi-phase transport model

> With A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model we investigate the relation between the magnitude, fluctuations and correlations of the initial state spatial anisotropy $\varepsilon_{n}$ and the final state anisotropic flow coefficients $v_{n}$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=$ 200 GeV. It is found that the relative eccentricity fluctuations in AMPT account for the observed elliptic flow fluctuations, in agreement with measurements of the STAR collaboration. In addition, the studies based on 2- and multi-particle correlations and event-by-event distributions of the anisotropies suggest that the Elliptic-Power function is a promising candidate of the underlying probability density function of the event-by-event distributions of $\varepsilon_{n}$ as well as $v_{n}$. Furthermore, the correlations between different order symmetry planes and harmonics in the initial coordinate space and final state momentum space are presented. Non-zero values of these correlations have been observed. The comparison between our calculations and data will, in the future, shed new insight into the nature of the fluctuations of the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The AMPT model reproduces the centrality dependence of elliptic flow fluctuations measured by STAR, and the Elliptic-Power function provides a better description of the event-by-event distributions of both initial spatial anisotropy \(\varepsilon_n\) and final anisotropic flow \(v_n\) than the Bessel-Gaussian or Power-Law functions, especially for peripheral collisions and for triangular flow.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200\) GeV, calculating anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_n\) via 2- and multi-particle Q-cumulant methods with and without pseudorapidity gaps. Initial state spatial anisotropies \(\varepsilon_n\) were computed from the parton spatial distributions, and event-by-event distributions of \(\varepsilon_n\) were fitted with Bessel-Gaussian, Power-Law, and Elliptic-Power functions. Correlations between different order symmetry planes and flow harmonics were also examined.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow fluctuations \(\sigma_{v_2}\) estimated from \(v_2\{2,|\Delta\eta|>1\}\) and \(v_2\{4\}\) increase toward peripheral collisions; relative flow fluctuations \(R_{v_{2,4}}\) range from 0.4 to 0.6 in central to mid-peripheral collisions.
- Relative eccentricity fluctuations \(R_{\varepsilon_{2,4}}\) are compatible with \(R_{v_{2,4}}\) for central and mid-central collisions; this consistency holds when the partonic cross section is increased to 10 mb.
- The true relative eccentricity fluctuations \(\sigma_{\varepsilon_2}/\overline{\varepsilon}_2\) from AMPT agree with the estimated relative eccentricity fluctuations in the 20-50% centrality percentile, but deviate for central and peripheral collisions.
- \(v_3\{\Psi_{\rm RP}\}\) is consistent with zero across the presented centrality range, while \(v_3\{2\}\) and \(v_3\{4\}\) are nonzero with weak centrality dependence.
- \(\varepsilon_3\{4\}\) is nonzero in the 5-40% centrality percentile range, contrary to the Bessel-Gaussian expectation of zero.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anisotropic flow fluctuations using the AMPT model and hydrodynamic calculations (Alver 2010, Niemi 2012) by systematically comparing initial state eccentricity fluctuations to final state flow fluctuations. The finding that the Elliptic-Power function (proposed by Yan 2014) describes both \(\varepsilon_2\) and \(\varepsilon_3\) distributions better than the Bessel-Gaussian (Voloshin 2007) or Power-Law (Yan 2013) functions enables more precise extraction of initial state properties and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the Quark-Gluon Plasma from future comparisons to experimental data.
