# Effects of the Symmetry Energy and its Slope on Neutron Star Properties

> In this work we study the influence of the symmetry energy and its slope on three major properties of neutron stars: the maximum mass, the radii of the canonical 1.4$M_\odot$ and the minimum mass that enables the direct URCA effect. We utilize four parametrizations of the relativistic quantum hadrodynamics and vary the symmetry energy within accepted values. We see that although the maximum mass is almost independent of it, the radius of the canonical $1.4M_\odot$ and the mass that enables the direct URCA effect is strongly correlated with the symmetry energy and its slope. Also, since we expect that the radius grows with the slope, a theoretical limit arises when we increase this quantity above certain values.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while the maximum mass of neutron stars is almost independent of the symmetry energy and its slope, the radius of the canonical 1.4 solar mass star and the minimum mass that enables the direct URCA process are strongly correlated with these quantities; however, when the slope is fixed using the delta meson, the correlation between symmetry energy and radius can invert, meaning the slope alone is not sufficient to determine the neutron star radius.

## Method

The authors used four parametrizations of relativistic quantum hadrodynamics (GM1, GM3, NL3, and NLrho) within the mean field approximation, first varying the symmetry energy in the standard sigma-omega-rho model without the delta meson, then including the scalar-isovector delta meson to independently fix either the slope L or the symmetry energy S0 at saturation. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and computed the proton fraction to determine the minimum mass for direct URCA cooling.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses vary less than 0.02 solar masses across all parametrizations without the delta meson, ranging from 2.04 to 2.81 solar masses depending on the model
- Radii of the canonical 1.4 solar mass star vary by about 0.2 km within each parametrization without the delta meson, always increasing with symmetry energy and slope
- The minimum mass enabling direct URCA process varies up to 25 percent (0.3 solar masses) without the delta meson; for GM1 at S0=30.49 MeV it is 1.26 solar masses, dropping to 0.98 solar masses at S0=34.49 MeV
- With fixed slope L, different symmetry energy values cause radius variations of up to 1.7 km for the same L value (e.g., GM1 at L=99.9 MeV gives radii from 13.91 km to 15.56 km)
- The slope L values range from 83.7 MeV (GM3 at S0=30.49) to 118.4 MeV (NL3 original) without the delta meson

## Context

This work extends studies of symmetry energy effects on neutron star properties by Rafa2011 and others, using the QHD framework of Serot and the parametrization survey of Dutra. It enables future observational constraints on the symmetry energy slope by linking it to neutron star radii measurements, and shows that the delta meson must be included to disentangle the effects of S0 and L, contradicting simpler models where the slope is uniquely determined by the symmetry energy.
