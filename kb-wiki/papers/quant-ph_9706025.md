# Quantum Chaos in a Yang-Mills-Higgs System

> We study the energy fluctuations of a spatially homogeneous SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs system. In particular, we analyze the nearest-neighbour spacing distribution which shows a Wigner-Poisson transition by increasing the value of the Higgs field in the vacuum. This transition is a clear quantum signature of the classical chaos-order transition of the system.

# Quantum Chaos in a Yang-Mills-Higgs System

## Key claim
The nearest-neighbour spacing distribution of the quantum SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs system shows a transition from Wigner (chaotic) to Poisson (integrable) statistics as the value of the Higgs field in the vacuum is increased, providing a quantum signature of the classical chaos-order transition. This transition is regulated by the single parameter \(E/(g^2 v^4)\), meaning the system exhibits either an order-chaos transition by increasing energy or a chaos-order transition by increasing the Higgs vacuum value.

## Method
The spatially homogeneous SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs system was reduced to a two-degree-of-freedom Hamiltonian in the Higgs vacuum. Classical chaos was analyzed using the Gaussian curvature criterion of the potential energy surface and Poincaré sections computed via fourth-order Runge-Kutta integration. Quantum energy levels were obtained by numerical diagonalization of the truncated Hamiltonian matrix (up to dimension \(1156 \times 1156\)) in the harmonic oscillator basis, with the first 100 converged levels used to compute the nearest-neighbour spacing distribution \(P(s)\) after unfolding each parity sub-matrix separately.

## Key numbers
- Critical Higgs vacuum value for the onset of chaos at \(E = 10\) and \(g = 1\): \(v_c = (E / 6g^2)^{1/4} \simeq 1.14\), derived from the zero-curvature condition \(E_c = 6 g^2 v^4\).
- Brody parameter \(\omega\) values from the \(P(s)\) distribution fit: \(\omega = 0.92\) at \(v = 1\) (near-Wigner, chaotic), \(\omega = 0.34\) at \(v = 1.1\) (intermediate), and \(\omega = 0.01\) at \(v = 1.2\) (near-Poisson, integrable), using the first 100 energy levels with \(g = 1\).
- Matrix dimension required for convergence of the first 100 energy levels to 8 digits: \(1156 \times 1156\).

## Context
This work extends classical chaos studies of the Yang-Mills-Higgs system by Savvidy (1984), Kawabe and Ohta (1991), and Salasnich (1995) into the quantum domain, using spectral statistics methods previously applied to two-dimensional anharmonic oscillators by Seligman, Verbaarschot and Zirnbauer (1984). The Brody distribution interpolation between Poisson and Wigner statistics provides a quantitative measure of chaoticity. The results demonstrate that the spatially homogeneous Yang-Mills-Higgs model serves as a tractable toy model for comparing classical and quantum chaos in field theories, and the author suggests future work should extend such studies to more realistic field theories.
