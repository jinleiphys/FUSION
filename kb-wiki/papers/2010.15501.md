# Higher order cumulants of electric charge and strangeness fluctuations on the crossover line

> We present lattice QCD calculations of higher order cumulants of electric charge distributions for small baryon chemical potentials $μ_B$ by using up to NNNLO Taylor expansions. Ratios of these cumulants are evaluated on the pseudo-critical line, $T_{pc}(μ_B)$, of the chiral transition and compared to corresponding measurements in heavy ion collision experiments by the STAR and PHENIX Collaborations. We demonstrate that these comparisons give strong constraints on freeze-out parameters. Furthermore, we use strangeness fluctuation observables to compute the ratio $μ_S/μ_B$ on the crossover line and compare it to $μ_S/μ_B$ at freeze-out stemming from fits to strange baryon yields measured by the STAR Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice QCD calculations of electric charge cumulant ratios on the pseudo-critical line can be used to extract freeze-out chemical potentials from heavy-ion collision data, but only under the assumption that freeze-out occurs on the crossover line; the extracted values are consistent with STAR and PHENIX measurements within the current precision.

## Method

The authors compute up to eighth-order generalized susceptibilities of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness using (2+1)-flavor HISQ gauge field configurations with physical light and strange quark masses (m_l/m_s=1/27). They construct Taylor series for charge and strangeness cumulants up to NNNLO in μ_B, imposing the constraints n_Q/n_B=0.4 and n_S=0 to match heavy-ion collision conditions, and evaluate cumulant ratios R^X_nm on the pseudo-critical line T_pc(μ_B) for nine temperatures from 135 MeV to 175 MeV and μ_B/T from 0 to 2.

## Key numbers

- T_pc,0 = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV and κ^B,f_2 = 0.012 ± 0.004 for the pseudo-critical line parametrization
- Extracted freeze-out chemical potentials from STAR data: μ_B,f = 19.4(1) MeV at √s_NN = 200 GeV; 58(1) MeV at 62.4 GeV; 92(2) MeV at 39 GeV; 131(3) MeV at 27 GeV
- R^Q_31(T_pc(μ_B), μ_B) = 1.07(9) for μ_B < 150 MeV
- R^Q_42(T_pc) = 0.73(5) for μ_B < 150 MeV
- s_1(T_pc,0) = 0.251(6) for the leading coefficient of μ_S/μ_B on the crossover line

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of net-baryon number cumulants by Bazavov et al. (2020) to electric charge and strangeness fluctuations, providing thermal QCD baselines for comparison with heavy-ion experiments. The results enable mapping between beam energies and freeze-out chemical potentials through comparison with STAR and PHENIX data, and the μ_S/μ_B ratio from lattice QCD on the crossover line is consistent with values extracted from fits to STAR strange baryon yields (Λ, Ξ, Ω) using hadron resonance gas relations, with the QM-HRG (including predicted but unobserved states) showing better agreement than the PDG-HRG.
