# Quantum design using a multiple internal reflections method in a study of fusion processes in the capture of alpha-particles by nuclei

> A high precision method to determine fusion in the capture of $öÝ$-particles by nuclei is presented. For $öÝ$-capture by $^{40}{\rm Ca}$ and $^{44}{\rm Ca}$, such an approach gives (1) the parameters of the $öÝ$--nucleus potential and (2) fusion probabilities. This method found new parametrization and fusion probabilities and decreased the error by $41.72$ times for $öÝ+ ^{40}{\rm Ca}$ and $34.06$ times for $öÝ+ ^{44}{\rm Ca}$ in a description of experimental data in comparison with existing results. We show that the sharp angular momentum cutoff proposed by Glas and Mosel is a rough approximation, Wong's formula and the Hill-Wheeler approach determine the penetrability of the barrier without a correct consideration of the barrier shape, and the WKB approach gives reduced fusion probabilities. Based on our fusion probability formula, we explain the difference between experimental cross-sections for $öÝ+ ^{40}{\rm Ca}$ and $öÝ+ ^{44}{\rm Ca}$, which is connected with the theory of coexistence of the spherical and deformed shapes in the ground state for nuclei near the neutron magic shell $N=20$. To provide deeper insight into the physics of nuclei with the new magic number $N=26$, the cross-section for $öÝ+ ^{46}{\rm Ca}$ is predicted for future experimental tests. The role of nuclear deformations in calculations of the fusion probabilities is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a high-precision method for determining fusion probabilities in alpha-particle capture by nuclei, claiming that the multiple internal reflections (MIR) approach reduces the error in describing experimental data by 41.72 times for alpha + Ca-40 and 34.06 times for alpha + Ca-44 compared to existing results, but this improvement depends on the specific choice of fusion probability parametrization and the rejection of approximations such as the sharp angular momentum cutoff, Wong's formula, and the Hill-Wheeler approach.

## Method

The authors apply the multiple internal reflections (MIR) method to calculate barrier penetrability for alpha-particle capture by nuclei, approximating the radial potential barrier of arbitrary shape by a large number of rectangular steps. They then extract fusion probabilities by minimizing error functions that compare theoretical cross-sections (computed from penetrability and fusion probability) with experimental data for alpha + Ca-40 and alpha + Ca-44, using a spherically symmetric alpha-nucleus potential with Coulomb, nuclear, and centrifugal components based on the parametrization of Denisov (2005).

## Key numbers

- Error decreased by 41.72 times for alpha + Ca-40 and 34.06 times for alpha + Ca-44 in description of experimental data compared to existing results (from minimization of error functions defined in the paper).
- Critical angular momenta from the sharp angular momentum cutoff approach: l_cr = 9.9 for alpha + Ca-40 and l_cr = 10.9 for alpha + Ca-44 (from Eberhard et al., 1979).
- The MIR method uses step widths of 0.01 fm for approximating the barrier, with stability demonstrated for thousands of steps.
- Cross-section for alpha + Ca-46 is predicted for future experimental tests (no numerical value given in the provided text).

## Context

This work extends the MIR method previously developed by Maydanyuk (2000-2011) to the study of fusion in alpha-capture, directly challenging the sharp angular momentum cutoff approach of Glas and Mosel (1974, 1975) and the WKB, Wong (1973), and Hill-Wheeler (1953) methods for calculating penetrability. The authors explain the difference between experimental cross-sections for alpha + Ca-40 and alpha + Ca-44 using their fusion probability formula in connection with the theory of coexistence of spherical and deformed shapes near the neutron magic shell N=20, and they predict cross-sections for alpha + Ca-46 to test the new magic number N=26 proposed by Penionzhkevich (2006).
