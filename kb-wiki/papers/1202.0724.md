# Chiral restoration effects on the shear viscosity of a pion gas

> We investigate the shear viscosity of a pion gas in relativistic kinetic theory, using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to construct the pion mass and the pi-pi interaction at finite temperature. Whereas at low temperatures the scattering properties and, hence, the viscosity are in agreement with lowest-order chiral perturbation theory, we find strong medium modifications in the crossover region. Here the system is strongly coupled and the scattering lengths diverge, similarly as for ultra-cold Fermi gases at a Feshbach resonance. As a consequence, the ratio eta/s is found to be strongly reduced as compared to calculations without medium-modified masses and scattering amplitudes. However, the quantitative results are very sensitive to the details of the applied approximations.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of a pion gas is strongly reduced near the chiral crossover region due to medium modifications of the pion mass and pi-pi scattering amplitudes, but the quantitative results are very sensitive to the details of the applied approximations.

## Method

The authors used relativistic kinetic theory with the Chapman-Enskog expansion to compute the shear viscosity of a pion gas, constructing the in-medium pion mass and pi-pi scattering amplitudes from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at finite temperature. They employed four increasingly sophisticated approximations for the scattering amplitude: momentum-independent Weinberg amplitudes, medium-modified momentum-independent amplitudes using temperature-dependent scattering lengths, momentum-dependent sigma-meson exchange, and inclusion of the pi-pi decay width of the sigma-meson via a dressed propagator.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum pion mass: m_pi = 135 MeV (from parameter fit to NJL model)
- Vacuum scattering lengths: a^0 = 0.164 m_pi^{-1}, a^2 = -0.041 m_pi^{-1} (NJL results), compared to Weinberg values a_W^0 = 0.149 m_pi^{-1}, a_W^2 = -0.0425 m_pi^{-1}
- Sigma dissociation temperature: T_diss = 179.9 MeV
- Mott temperature: T_Mott = 198.55 MeV
- NJL model parameters: m_0 = 5.0 MeV, g*Lambda^2 = 2.06, Lambda = 664.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transport coefficients in pion gases that used vacuum values for masses and scattering amplitudes, such as Prakash et al. (1993), Dobado and Pelaez (1993, 1998), and Chen (2006, 2007). It contrasts with the linear sigma model approach of D. L. Ll. E. T. R. (2009) by using the NJL model, which naturally describes the dissolution of pions into quark-antiquark resonances near chiral restoration. The finding of divergent scattering lengths analogous to Feshbach resonances in ultra-cold Fermi gases enables connections between strongly coupled QCD matter and cold atom systems, and the results are relevant for understanding elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
