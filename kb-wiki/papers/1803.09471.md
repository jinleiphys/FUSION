# Low-lying states in even Gd isotopes studied with five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian based on covariant density functional theory

> Five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian based on the covariant density functional theory has been applied to study the the low-lying states of even-even $^{148-162}$Gd isotopes. The shape evolution from $^{148}$Gd to $^{162}$Gd is presented. The experimental energy spectra and intraband $B(E2)$ transition probabilities for the $^{148-162}$Gd isotopes are reproduced by the present calculations. The relative $B(E2)$ ratios in present calculations are also compared with the available interacting boson model results and experimental data. It is found that the occupations of neutron $1i_{13/2}$ orbital result in the well-deformed prolate shape, and are essential for Gd isotopes.

## Key claim

The five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian based on covariant density functional theory (5DCH-CDFT) with the PC-PK1 density functional reproduces the experimental energy spectra and intraband B(E2) transition probabilities for even-even 148-162Gd isotopes, but the agreement is conditional: the calculations systematically overestimate β-band head energies due to the absence of Thouless-Valatin dynamical rearrangement contributions and pairing-vibration coupling, and the theoretical 2+1 state energy is normalized to experiment by renormalizing the Inglis-Belyaev moments of inertia by a constant factor that varies from 0.818 to 1.889 across the isotopic chain.

## Method

The authors performed constrained triaxial relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) calculations with the point-coupling density functional PC-PK1 and a density-independent delta-force pairing interaction (strength 349.5 MeV fm3 for neutrons, 330.0 MeV fm3 for protons) to generate the collective potential, mass parameters, and moments of inertia for the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian. The moments of inertia were computed with the Inglis-Belyaev formula and the mass parameters from the cranking approximation; the collective potential was obtained by subtracting zero-point energy corrections from the constrained triaxial CDFT total energy. The resulting 5DCH was diagonalized to obtain excitation energies, collective wave functions, and electric quadrupole transition probabilities for the ground-state, γ, and β bands.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state minimum of 148Gd is at β = 0.1 and γ = 60° (slight oblate); 150Gd has a prolate minimum at β = 0.15 with an extremely flat γ-direction potential; 152Gd has β = 0.25; 154Gd has β = 0.3; 156-162Gd all have β = 0.35 and γ = 0° (well-deformed prolate).
- The renormalization factors applied to the Inglis-Belyaev moments of inertia to match the experimental 2+1 energy are: 1.889 (148Gd), 0.863 (150Gd), 0.818 (152Gd), 1.379 (154Gd), 1.500 (156Gd), 1.500 (158Gd), 1.455 (160Gd), and 1.411 (162Gd).
- For the γ-band head energies, the difference between theory and experiment is about 350 keV for 152Gd and smaller than 150 keV for all other nuclei.
- The staggering parameter S(I) for 148Gd oscillates with magnitude about 1 (spherical vibrator behavior); for 156-162Gd, S(I) is close to a constant value of 0.33 (axial rotor character).

## Context

This work extends previous 5DCH-CDFT studies of even 152-160Gd isotopes using the PC-F1 parameter (Nikšić et al., 2009) by applying the PC-PK1 functional to the full 148-162Gd chain, revealing a shape evolution from weakly deformed (148,150Gd) through soft prolate (152,154Gd) to well-deformed prolate (156-162Gd). The results are compared with interacting boson model (IBM) calculations for relative B(E2) ratios (Casten et al., 2014) and with prior mean-field calculations using NL3 (Fossion et al., 2006), Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS (Guzman et al., 2007), and Gogny interactions (Robledo et al., 2008); the present PES for 154Gd does not exhibit the flat behavior expected for X(5) critical-point symmetry, consistent with those earlier works. The study enables systematic tests of SU(3) partial dynamical symmetry in the well-deformed isotopes 156-162Gd and provides a microscopic foundation for interpreting relative B(E2) ratios in transitional and deformed rare-earth nuclei.
