# Thermal pairing and giant dipole resonance in highly excited nuclei

> Recent results are reported showing the effects of thermal pairing in highly excited nuclei. It is demonstrated that thermal pairing included in the phonon damping model (PDM) is responsible for the nearly constant width of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) at low temperature $T <$ 1 MeV. It is also shown that the enhancement observed in the recent experimentally extracted nuclear level densities in $^{104}$Pd at low excitation energy and various angular momenta is the first experimental evidence of the pairing reentrance in finite (hot rotating) nuclei. In the study of GDR in highly excited nuclei, the PDM has been extended to include finite angular momentum. The results of calculations within the PDM are found in excellent agreement with the latest experimental data of GDR in the compound nucleus $^{88}$Mo. Finally, an exact expression is derived to calculate the shear viscosity $η$ as a function of $T$ in finite nuclei directly from the GDR width and energy at zero and finite $T$. Based on this result, the values $η/s$ of specific shear viscosity in several medium and heavy nuclei were calculated and found to decrease with increasing $T$ to reach $(1.3 - 4)\times\hbar/(4πk_B)$ at $T =$ 5 MeV, that is almost the same value obtained for quark-gluon-plasma at $T >$ 170 MeV.

# Thermal pairing and giant dipole resonance in highly excited nuclei

## Key claim
Thermal pairing included in the phonon damping model (PDM) is responsible for the nearly constant width of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) at low temperature T < 1 MeV, and the enhancement observed in experimentally extracted nuclear level densities in 104Pd at low excitation energy and various angular momenta constitutes the first experimental evidence of pairing reentrance in finite hot rotating nuclei.

## Method
The phonon damping model (PDM) was extended to include finite angular momentum via a rotation frequency term in the Hamiltonian, and the finite-temperature BCS equations were corrected by quasiparticle-number fluctuations (FTBCS1) to compute pairing gaps and level densities. The GDR width was calculated as the sum of quantal and thermal widths using quasiparticle occupation numbers and delta-function representations, with parameters chosen to match zero-temperature GDR width and maintain stable GDR energy. Shear viscosity was derived from GDR widths and energies at zero and finite temperature using a Green-Kubo relation.

## Key numbers
- GDR width remains nearly constant at T < 1 MeV in heavy nuclei
- Pairing reentrance in 104Pd appears at J >= 20 hbar for neutrons and J >= 30 hbar for protons
- FTBCS1 level densities in 104Pd show local enhancement at 2 < E* < 4.5 MeV for all J values considered
- GDR line shapes in 88Mo were measured at average temperatures of 2.04 MeV and 3.06 MeV with average angular momentum 38 hbar
- Specific shear viscosity eta/s reaches (1.3 - 4) x hbar/(4 pi k_B) at T = 5 MeV for medium and heavy nuclei
- PDM predicts 1.3 <= eta/s <= 4 KSS units for medium and heavy nuclei at T = 5 MeV
- FLDM predicts eta/s between (3.7 - 6.5) KSS units at T = 5 MeV for 120Sn and 208Pb
- FLDM predicts eta/s between (4 - 19) KSS units for heavy nuclei using eta(0) = 0.6u

## Context
This work extends the phonon damping model (PDM) previously developed by the author to include finite angular momentum, and applies it to interpret experimental data from the Bhabha Atomic Research Center (BARC) on 104Pd level densities and from Ciemala on 88Mo GDR line shapes. The pairing reentrance phenomenon was predicted 50 years ago by Kammuri and physically interpreted by Moretto; this work provides the first experimental evidence for it in finite nuclei. The specific shear viscosity results are compared with predictions from the Fermi-liquid drop model (FLDM) by Auerbach and the thermal shape fluctuation model (TSFM), and are found to approach values similar to those obtained for quark-gluon plasma at T > 170 MeV.
