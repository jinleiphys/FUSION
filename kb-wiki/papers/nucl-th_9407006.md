# A Relativistic Approach to Deep Inelastic Scattering on the Deuteron

> A covariant field theoretical approach to deep inelastic scattering on the deuteron is presented. The deuteron structure function is calculated in terms of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. Numerical calculations for the nucleon contribution are made with a realistic model of the $NN$-interaction, including $ПЂ$-, $ПЃ$-, $П‰$-, $О·$-, $Оґ$- and $Пѓ$-mesons, and results are compared with previous non-relativistic calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a covariant field-theoretical approach to deep inelastic scattering on the deuteron using the Bethe-Salpeter equation, finding that the magnitude of nuclear structure effects (binding and off-mass-shell effects) is larger than in previous non-relativistic calculations, though the qualitative agreement with those calculations is maintained.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the deuteron in the ladder approximation with a realistic meson-exchange potential including ПЂ-, ПЃ-, П‰-, О·-, Оґ-, and Пѓ-mesons. They then compute the deuteron structure function Fв‚‚^D using the Operator Product Expansion method within the same meson-nucleon theory, expressing the moments of the structure function in terms of matrix elements of twist-2 operators on the deuteron state via the Mandelstam method. The structure function is obtained through an inverse Mellin transform, yielding a convolution form that separates nucleon and meson exchange contributions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deep inelastic scattering on the deuteron that used various relativistic equations and approximations to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, such as those by Frankfurt and Strikman (year not specified) and Melnitchouk et al. (year not specified). The authors compare their results with non-relativistic calculations (the x-rescaling model and the OPE-OBE model) and light-cone kinematics calculations, showing that the relativistic off-mass-shell effects produce an "EMC-effect" on the deuteron similar to that observed experimentally for heavy nuclei. This approach enables more accurate extraction of the neutron structure function from combined proton-deuteron data and improved estimates of the EMC-effect on the deuteron for experiments at SLAC, CERN, DESY, and CEBAF.
