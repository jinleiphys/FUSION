# Pion Polarizabilities and Volume Effects in Lattice QCD

> We use chiral perturbation theory to study the extraction of pion electromagnetic polarizabilities from lattice QCD. Chiral extrapolation formulae are derived for partially quenched QCD, and quenched QCD simulations. On a torus, volume dependence of electromagnetic observables is complicated by SO(4) breaking, as well as photon zero-mode interactions. We determine finite volume corrections to the Compton scattering tensor of pions. We argue, however, that such results cannot be used to ascertain volume corrections to polarizabilities determined in lattice QCD with background field methods. Connection is lacking because momentum expansions are not permitted in finite volume. Our argument also applies to form factors. Volume effects for electromagnetic moments cannot be deduced from finite volume form factors.

## Key claim

The authors argue that finite volume corrections to pion polarizabilities cannot be determined from finite volume Compton scattering tensors, because momentum expansions are not permitted in finite volume due to momentum quantization; this argument also applies to electromagnetic form factors and moments.

## Method

The authors use chiral perturbation theory (χPT) at one-loop order to compute the Compton scattering amplitude for pions in infinite volume, partially quenched QCD (PQχPT), and quenched QCD (QχPT). They then compute the finite volume corrections to the Compton tensor on a torus with periodic boundary conditions, replacing momentum integrals with sums over quantized spatial modes, and match the finite and infinite volume results to isolate the volume-dependent terms.

## Key numbers

- For the neutral pion: α_E^{π^0} - β_M^{π^0} = - (2 α_fs Q_π^2) / (3 (4π f)^2 m_π), where α_fs = e^2 / 4π is the fine structure constant and f ≈ 132 MeV
- For the charged pion: α_E^{π^±} - β_M^{π^±} = (16 α_fs Q_π^2 (α_9 + α_10)) / (f^2 m_π), where α_9 + α_10 is a scale-independent combination of low-energy constants
- At one-loop order, α_E + β_M = 0 for both charged and neutral pions in χPT, PQχPT, and QχPT
- The finite volume regime requires m_π L ≳ 2π and 2 f L ≫ 1 for the p-regime of chiral perturbation theory

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations of pion polarizabilities by Gasser (1983) and Holstein (1990) to include partially quenched and quenched QCD, as well as finite volume effects relevant for lattice QCD simulations. The authors show that the cancellation of loop contributions in infinite volume persists in PQχPT and QχPT, and that the neutral pion polarizabilities survive quenching entirely from quark-line connected topology. The paper enables future lattice QCD calculations of pion polarizabilities using background field methods, but warns that finite volume corrections to these polarizabilities cannot be extracted from finite volume Compton scattering tensors, contrary to naive expectation.
