# The chiral effective pion-nucleon Lagrangian of order p^4

> We construct the minimal effective chiral pion-nucleon SU(2) Lagrangian at fourth order in the chiral expansion. The Lagrangian contains 118 in principle measurable terms. We develop both the relativistic as well as the heavy baryon formulation of the effective field theory. For the latter, we also work out explicitly all 1/m corrections at fourth order. We display all relevant relations needed to find the linearly independent terms.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the minimal effective chiral pion-nucleon SU(2) Lagrangian at fourth order in the chiral expansion, containing 118 in principle measurable terms, but notes that most physical processes are only sensitive to a small number of low-energy constants (e.g., elastic pion-nucleon scattering involves only five independent combinations of LECs at fourth order).

## Method

The authors built all possible invariant monomials from the basic chiral fields (pion fields, nucleon fields, external vector/axial-vector/scalar/pseudoscalar sources, and their covariant derivatives) consistent with chiral symmetry, parity, charge conjugation, and hermiticity. They eliminated linearly dependent terms using identities including the Cayley-Hamilton theorem, curvature relations, Bianchi identities, and the equations of motion from the lowest-order Lagrangians. They then performed the heavy baryon projection to obtain both the relativistic and heavy baryon formulations, including all 1/m corrections at fourth order.

## Key numbers

* The Lagrangian contains 118 independent terms at fourth chiral order.
* The paper provides the complete fourth-order Lagrangian for both the relativistic and heavy baryon formulations, including strong isospin breaking and external fields.
* For elastic pion-nucleon scattering, there are four independent (combinations of) LECs at second order, four at third order, and five at fourth order.

## Context

This work extends the known chiral pion-nucleon Lagrangian, which was previously constructed only up to third order by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988), Krause (1990), and others. The divergent part of the fourth-order heavy baryon Lagrangian was already known from Müller, Meißner, and Steininger (1998), and a non-minimal fourth-order Lagrangian with virtual photons was worked out by Müller and Meißner (1999). The complete fourth-order Lagrangian provided here enables full one-loop calculations in pion-nucleon chiral perturbation theory, which were previously impossible without this missing ingredient.
