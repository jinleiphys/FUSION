# QCD Sum Rule for S_{11}(1535)

> We propose a new interpolating field for S$_{11}$(1535) to determine its mass from QCD sum rules. In the nonrelativistic limit, this interpolating field dominantly reduces to two quarks in the s-wave state and one quark in the p-wave state. An optimization procedure, which makes use of a duality relation, yields the interpolating field which overlaps strongly with the negative-parity baryon and at the same time does not couple at all to the low lying positive-parity baryon. Using this interpolating field and applying the conventional QCD sum rule analysis, we find that the mass of S$_{11}$ is reasonably close to the experimentally known value, even though the precise determination depends on the poorly known quark-gluon condensate. Hence our interpolating field can be used to investigate the spectral properties of S$_{11}$(1535).

## Key claim

The authors propose a new interpolating field for the S$_{11}$(1535) resonance that, after optimization, couples strongly to the negative-parity baryon and does not couple at all to the low-lying positive-parity nucleon, enabling a conventional QCD sum rule analysis. The mass prediction is reasonably close to the experimental value, but the precise determination depends on the poorly known quark-gluon condensate parameter $\lambda_q^2$.

## Method

The authors constructed an interpolating field for the S$_{11}$(1535) by inserting a covariant derivative $z \cdot D$ acting on the d-quark in the standard nucleon interpolating fields, so that in the nonrelativistic limit the field corresponds to two quarks in the s-wave state and one quark in the p-wave state. They calculated the two-point correlation function in the operator product expansion up to dimension-8 operators, applied Borel transformation, and matched the OPE side to the phenomenological side assuming two separate continuum thresholds for positive- and negative-parity resonances. An optimization procedure using a finite energy sum rule (the "old-fashioned" sum rule of Jido, Kodama, and Oka (1996)) was employed to find the linear combination parameter $t$ that makes the coupling to the positive-parity nucleon vanish while maximizing the coupling to the negative-parity S$_{11}$.

## Key numbers

* The quark condensate used: $\langle {\bar q} q \rangle = -(230 \pm 20\ {\rm MeV})^3$
* The gluon condensate used: $\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} {\cal G}^2 \rangle = (350 \pm 20\ {\rm MeV})^4$
* For $\lambda_q^2 = 0.6$ GeV$^2$ with optimal $t = -4.59$, the predicted S$_{11}$ mass from the stable Borel plateau is $M_{N^-} = 1.59$ GeV
* For $\lambda_q^2 = 1.0$ GeV$^2$ with optimal $t = -2.04$, the predicted S$_{11}$ mass is $M_{N^-} = 1.42$ GeV
* For $\lambda_q^2 = 0.4$ GeV$^2$ with optimal $t = 4.97$, the predicted S$_{11}$ mass is $M_{N^-} = 1.49$ GeV
* The best predictions for the S$_{11}$ mass (experimental value 1.535 GeV) are obtained for $\lambda_q^2 = 0.435$ GeV$^2$ or $\lambda_q^2 = 0.69$ GeV$^2$
* The positive-parity continuum threshold was fixed to $s_+ = (1.44\ {\rm GeV})^2$ and the negative-parity threshold to $s_- = (1.65\ {\rm GeV})^2$

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to negative-parity baryons, specifically the S$_{11}$(1535) resonance, which is important for understanding $\eta$-production experiments at MAMI (Mainz), ELSA (Bonn), and CEBAF. The authors improve upon the technique of Jido, Kodama, and Oka (1996), who used the usual nucleon interpolating fields and had to take differences between sum rules to subtract the nucleon contribution, making it difficult to study form factors or couplings. The new interpolating field with a covariant derivative enables conventional sum rule analysis for other properties of the S$_{11}$, such as couplings and transition form factors, which the authors indicate will be the subject of future work (Kim and Lee, in preparation).
