# Collective multipole excitations in a microscopic relativistic approach

> A relativistic mean field description of collective excitations of atomic nuclei is studied in the framework of a fully self-consistent relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA). In particular, results of RRPA calculations of multipole giant resonances and of low-lying collective states in spherical nuclei are analyzed. By using effective Lagrangians which, in the mean-field approximation, provide an accurate description of ground-state properties, an excellent agreement with experimental data is also found for the excitation energies of low-lying collective states and of giant resonances. Two points are essential for the successful application of the RRPA in the description of dynamical properties of finite nuclei: (i) the use of effective Lagrangians with non-linear terms in the meson sector, and (ii) the fully consistent treatment of the Dirac sea of negative energy states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully self-consistent relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA), using effective Lagrangians with non-linear meson terms and including the Dirac sea of negative energy states, achieves excellent agreement with experimental excitation energies of both low-lying collective states and giant resonances in spherical nuclei. The key qualifier is that this agreement holds only when both (i) non-linear terms in the meson sector and (ii) the fully consistent treatment of the Dirac sea are included, which had not been simultaneously achieved in prior RRPA work.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation and meson field equations for spherical nuclei by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in a harmonic oscillator basis, then built the RRPA basis from particle-hole (ph) and antiparticle-hole (ah) pairs. They calculated multipole strength distributions either by computing the linear response function via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in momentum space, or by diagonalizing the full non-Hermitian RRPA matrix in configuration space, using the NL3 effective interaction (and comparing with NL1, NL-SH, TM1, and the linear HS parameter set). The calculations included both isoscalar and isovector multipole operators for monopole, dipole, quadrupole, and octupole modes, with an energy averaging width of 2 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in $^{208}$Pb, the experimental centroid energy is 13.5 ± 0.2 MeV; all calculated peak energies with non-linear interactions are very close to this value.
- For the isovector giant quadrupole resonance (IVGQR) in $^{208}$Pb, the calculated peak is at 23 MeV, close to the experimental centroid energy of 22 MeV.
- The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) calculations indicate that experimental GMR energies are best reproduced by an effective force with nuclear matter compression modulus $K_{\rm nm} \approx 250 - 270$ MeV.
- The isovector giant monopole resonance (IVGMR) in heavy nuclei is found at excitation energies of 25 - 30 MeV, with widths around 10 MeV.
- For the isoscalar giant octupole resonance (ISGOR) in $^{208}$Pb, the low-energy octupole resonance (LEOR) is found between 6 and 10 MeV, and the high-energy octupole resonance (HEOR) is concentrated around 23 MeV.
- The inclusion of $ah$-configurations shifts the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) main peaks to higher energy by 1-2 MeV in nuclei heavier than $^{16}$O.
- The symmetry energy values for the parameter sets used are 35 MeV for HS and 43.7 MeV for NL1.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic RPA calculations that were based on the linear Walecka model (Furnstahl 1985, Hirata and Giai 1989) and could only achieve qualitative agreement with experiment, by incorporating non-linear meson self-interactions and the Dirac sea of negative energy states. It also builds on recent developments showing that $ah$-configurations strongly affect isoscalar giant resonances (Ma et al. 2001, Vretenar et al. 2000) and that their inclusion is essential for current conservation and decoupling of the spurious state (Dawson and Furnstahl 1990). The results enable systematic RRPA studies of collective excitations across the periodic table using the same effective Lagrangians that successfully describe ground-state properties, and they highlight the need to resolve the 10-15% difference between relativistic and non-relativistic predictions for the nuclear matter compression modulus.
