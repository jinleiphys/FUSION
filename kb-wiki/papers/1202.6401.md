# Parity-violating DIS and the flavour dependence of the EMC effect

> Isospin-dependent nuclear forces play a fundamental role in nuclear structure. In relativistic models of nuclear structure constructed at the quark level these isovector nuclear forces affect the u and d quarks differently, leading to non-trivial flavour dependent modifications of the nuclear parton distributions. We explore the effect of isospin dependent forces for parity-violating deep inelastic scattering on nuclear targets and demonstrate that the cross-sections for nuclei with N /= Z are sensitive to the flavour dependence of the EMC effect. Indeed, for nuclei like lead and gold we find that these flavour dependent effects are large.

## Key claim

Parity-violating deep inelastic scattering (PVDIS) on nuclei with neutron excess (N > Z) is sensitive to the flavour dependence of the EMC effect, and for heavy nuclei like lead and gold these flavour-dependent effects are predicted to be large within a relativistic quark-level model of nuclear structure.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, a QCD-motivated chiral effective theory, to self-consistently calculate medium-modified quark distributions in nuclear matter. The model incorporates isoscalar-scalar, isoscalar-vector (ω), and isovector-vector (ρ) mean fields, with the ρ-field coupling strength fixed by the empirical symmetry energy of nuclear matter (a₄ = 32 MeV). The parity-violating asymmetry A_PV and the EMC ratios for both electromagnetic (γ) and γ-Z interference structure functions are then computed for asymmetric nuclear matter with proton-neutron ratios matching those of iron and lead.

## Key numbers

- The empirical symmetry energy used to constrain the isovector-vector coupling is a₄ = 32 MeV, giving G_ρ = 14.2 GeV⁻².
- For an isoscalar target in the valence region, the parity-violating asymmetry parameter a₂(x_A) reduces to the constant value a₂ = 9/5 − 4 sin²θ_W, where sin²θ_W = 0.2227 (on-shell renormalization scheme value).
- The isovector correction term in a₂(x_A) is proportional to (u⁺_A − d⁺_A)/(u⁺_A + d⁺_A), with a prefactor of −12/25.
- For nuclei with N > Z, the electromagnetic EMC ratio R^γ is found to be smaller than the parity-violating EMC ratio R^{γZ} for x_A ≳ 0.2, with the difference increasing as the neutron excess grows.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies of the isovector EMC effect (Cloët 2009, Bentz 2009) and its role in interpreting the NuTeV measurement of sin²θ_W. It proposes PVDIS at Jefferson Lab after the 12 GeV upgrade as a timely alternative to charged-current reactions at a future electron-ion collider (Thomas 2009) or Drell-Yan measurements (Dutta 2010, Chang 2011), which would not be possible for ten to twenty years. The results directly test whether the isovector mean-field mechanism that resolves 1–1.5σ of the NuTeV anomaly also produces measurable flavour-dependent modifications of nuclear parton distributions in heavy nuclei.
