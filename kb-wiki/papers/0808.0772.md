# Low-Density Instability of Multi-Component Matter with Trapped Neutrinos

> The effect of neutrino trapping on the longitudinal dielectric function at low densities has been investigated by using different relativistic mean field models. Parameter sets G2 of Furnstahl-Serot-Tang and Z271 of Horowitz-Piekarewicz, along with the adjusted parameter sets of both models, have been used in this study. The role of the isovector adjustment and the effect of the Coulomb interaction have been also studied. The effect of the isovector adjustment is found to be more significant in the Horowitz-Piekarewicz model, not only in the neutrinoless matter, but also in the matter with neutrino trapping. Although almost independent to the variation of the leptonic fraction, the instability region of matter with neutrino trapping is found to be larger. The presence of more protons and electrons compared to the neutrinoless case is the reason behind this finding. For parameter sets with soft equation of states at low density, the appearance of a large and negative epsilon_L (q, q0 = 0) in some parts of the edge of the instability region in matter with neutrino trapping is understood as a consequence of the fact that the Coulomb interaction produced by electrons and protons interaction is larger than the repulsive isovector interaction created by the asymmetry between the proton and neutron numbers.

## Key claim

Neutrino trapping enlarges the instability region of low-density multi-component matter compared to neutrinoless matter, and this effect is almost independent of the leptonic fraction. The enlargement is driven by the presence of more protons and electrons in the trapped-neutrino case, which enhances the attractive Coulomb interaction beyond the repulsive isovector interaction from proton-neutron asymmetry.

## Method

The authors calculated the longitudinal dielectric function at zero frequency (epsilon_L(q, q0=0)) for uniform matter composed of protons, neutrons, electrons, and trapped electron neutrinos, using four relativistic mean field (RMF) parameter sets: G2 and G2* from the Furnstahl-Serot-Tang model, and Z271 and Z271* from the Horowitz-Piekarewicz model. They mapped the onset of instability (where epsilon_L <= 0) in the plane of baryon density versus perturbation momentum q, and separately examined the behavior at very small q (0.01 fm^-1) to isolate the role of the Coulomb interaction and electron contributions by switching them off in selected calculations.

## Key numbers

- The critical density (rho_c) for matter without neutrino trapping is higher for the adjusted parameter sets (G2*, Z271*) than for the standard sets (G2, Z271), consistent with the authors' prior work (Sulaksono and Mart, 2006).
- For matter with neutrino trapping, the instability boundaries expand for all parameter sets, with no substantial difference in the onset due to isovector adjustment except in the region with q close to zero.
- At q = 0.01 fm^-1, the Z271* parameter set yields epsilon_L > 0 in the range 0.1 <= rho/rho_0 <= 0.3, unlike the other three parameter sets which show negative epsilon_L in that region.
- The Coulomb interaction stabilizes matter with neutrino trapping in the region with q < 0.6 fm^-1, in the range 0.2 <= rho/rho_0 <= 0.6.
- For matter without neutrino trapping, the repulsive Coulomb interaction from protons moderately enlarges the stability region in the range 0.05 <= rho/rho_0 <= 0.4.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Sulaksono and Mart, 2006) on critical densities of uniform matter by calculating the full longitudinal dielectric function to analyze every point along the onset of instability, not just the critical density. It builds on the dynamical stability analysis of Carriere, Horowitz, and Piekarewicz (2003), who found a strong correlation between the critical density and the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The results show that the driving mechanism for instability differs between parameter sets with stiff versus soft equations of state at low density when neutrinos are trapped, which may affect neutrino transport in supernova cores and neutron star crusts.
