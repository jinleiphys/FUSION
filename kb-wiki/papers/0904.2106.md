# Formation of hypernuclei in high energy reactions within a covariant transport model

> We investigate the formation of fragments with strangeness degrees of freedom in proton- and heavy-ion-induced reactions at high relativistic energies. The model used is a combination of a dynamical transport model and a statistical approach of fragment formation. We discuss in detail the applicability and limitations of such a hybrid model by comparing data on spectator fragmentation at relativistic $SIS/GSI$-energies. The theoretical results are analyzed in terms of spectator fragmentation with strangeness degrees of freedom such as the production of single-$Λ-{}^{3,4,5}He$ hypernuclei. We provide theoretical estimates on the spectra and on inclusive cross sections of light hypernuclei, which could be helpful for future experiments on hypernuclear physics at the new GSI- and J-PARC-facilities.

# Formation of hypernuclei in high energy reactions within a covariant transport model

## Key claim
The hybrid GiBUU+SMM transport model, combined with a phase-space coalescence approach, provides theoretical estimates for light hypernucleus production cross sections in both heavy-ion collisions at 2 AGeV and proton-induced reactions at 50 GeV, but the coalescence velocity parameter R is phenomenological and cannot be precisely calibrated due to the absence of direct hypernuclear formation data.

## Method
The authors combine the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport equation (GiBUU code) for the dynamical pre-equilibrium stage with the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) for spectator decay, and model hypernucleus formation via phase-space coalescence between spectator fragments and hyperons from the fireball. The transport model propagates nucleons, hyperons, resonances up to 2 GeV mass, and mesons including pions and kaons, with the baryonic mean-field described by the nonlinear Walecka model (NL2 parametrization). For the 50 GeV proton case, the PYTHIA string fragmentation model is included for high-energy interactions.

## Key numbers
- Compression modulus of the NL2 parametrization: K = 210 MeV, with nuclear saturation density ρ_sat = 0.16 fm⁻³
- For ¹²C+¹²C at 2 AGeV: total inclusive cross sections of 2.2 μb for ⁴_ΛH, 4 μb for ⁴_ΛHe, and 1.4 μb for ⁵_ΛHe
- For p+¹²C at 50 GeV: total inclusive cross sections of 43 μb for ³_ΛH, 28 μb for ⁴_ΛH, 9.5 μb for ⁵_ΛH, 0.8 μb for ⁶_ΛH, 0.9 μb for ³_ΛHe, 35 μb for ⁴_ΛHe, 9.8 μb for ⁵_ΛHe, and 16 μb for ⁶_ΛHe
- Pionic contribution (πN → YK) to hypernucleus production at 50 GeV: 33 μb for ³_ΛH, 14 μb for ⁴_ΛH, 2.6 μb for ⁵_ΛH, 0.2 μb for ⁶_ΛH, 0.8 μb for ³_ΛHe, 16 μb for ⁴_ΛHe, 4.3 μb for ⁵_ΛHe, and 1.3 μb for ⁶_ΛHe
- Freeze-out time for spectator matter in heavy-ion collisions: t_fr ∼ 50-70 fm/c
- Thermal equilibration time for p+¹²C at 50 GeV: t > 15-20 fm/c

## Context
This work extends previous fragmentation studies by Gaitanos et al. (2008) to include strangeness degrees of freedom, building on earlier theoretical proposals by Kerman and Weiss for hypernuclear production in high-energy reactions. The results are intended to guide upcoming experiments at GSI (HypHI collaboration) and J-PARC, and the authors note that a future combination with the statistical multifragmentation model including strangeness (SMMH, still in development) would be a helpful extension. The approach contrasts with fully quantum-mechanical treatments for coherent reactions by Shyam and with non-relativistic transport theory applied by Cassing et al. to COSY data.
