# Relativistic corrections to the nuclear Schiff moment

> Parity and time invariance violating ($P,T$-odd) atomic electric dipole moments (EDM) are induced by interaction between atomic electrons and nuclear $P,T$-odd moments which are produced by $P,T$-odd nuclear forces. The nuclear EDM is screened by atomic electrons. The EDM of a non-relativistic atom with closed electron subshells is induced by the nuclear Schiff moment. For heavy relativistic atoms EDM is induced by the nuclear local dipole moments which differ by 10-50% from the Schiff moments calculated previously. We calculate the local dipole moments for ${^{199}{\rm Hg}}$ and ${^{205}{\rm Tl}}$ where the most accurate atomic and molecular EDM measurements have been performed.

## Key claim

For heavy relativistic atoms, the atomic electric dipole moment (EDM) is induced by the nuclear local dipole moment rather than the Schiff moment; these two quantities differ by 10-50%, and the correction can reduce the result by a factor of two when the Schiff moment is anomalously small.

## Method

The authors calculated the local dipole moment \(L\) for the nuclei \({}^{199}\)Hg (odd neutron) and \({}^{205}\)Tl (odd proton) using a finite-range \(P,T\)-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction with pion exchange. They included both single-particle contributions (for the odd proton in \({}^{205}\)Tl) and core-polarization contributions from the residual strong interaction, solving the renormalization equations for the local dipole moment operator. The results are presented for three coupling constants \(g_0\), \(g_1\), and \(g_2\), with and without core-polarization effects.

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{205}\)Tl with coupling \(g_2\), the bare Schiff moment \(S_0 = 0.237\) (units not specified in text) and the ratio of the relativistic correction to the Schiff moment for the \(s-p_{1/2}\) transition is \(L'_0/S_0 = -0.08\); after core polarization, \(S_{tot} = -0.007\) and \(L'_{tot}/S_{tot} = -0.51\).
- For \({}^{199}\)Hg with coupling \(g_0\), the bare Schiff moment \(S_0 = -0.085\) and \(L'_0/S_0 = -0.1\); after core polarization, \(S_{tot} = -0.006\) and \(L'_{tot}/S_{tot} = -0.05\).
- For \({}^{199}\)Hg with coupling \(g_1\), the bare Schiff moment \(S_0 = -0.085\) and \(L'_0/S_0 = -0.1\); after core polarization, \(S_{tot} = -0.036\) and \(L'_{tot}/S_{tot} = -0.15\).
- The ratio \(L'/S\) for the \(s-p_{3/2}\) transition differs from the \(s-p_{1/2}\) value by a factor of 3/4.
- For \({}^{199}\)Hg, the relativistic parameter \(Z^2\alpha^2 = 0.34\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier formalism of Flambaum and Ginges (2002) for the local dipole moment, which corrects the Schiff moment for heavy atoms where relativistic effects are significant (scaling as \(Z^2\alpha^2\)). The calculations provide the specific local dipole moments for \({}^{199}\)Hg and \({}^{205}\)Tl, the nuclei used in the most accurate atomic EDM measurements by Fortson (for Hg) and Hinds (for Tl). The results enable more precise interpretation of experimental EDM limits in terms of \(P,T\)-odd nuclear forces.
