# Equation of state and speed of sound of isospin-asymmetric QCD on the lattice

> We determine the QCD equation of state at nonzero temperature in the presence of an isospin asymmetry between the light quark chemical potentials on the lattice. Our simulations employ $N_f=2+1$ flavors of dynamical staggered quarks at physical masses, using three different lattice spacings. The main results are based on a two-dimensional spline interpolation of the isospin density, from which all relevant quantities can be obtained analytically. In particular, we present results for the pressure, the interaction measure, the energy and entropy densities, as well as the speed of sound. Remarkably, the latter is found to exceed its ideal gas limit deep in the pion condensed phase, the first account of the violation of this limit in first principles QCD. Finally, we also compute the phase diagram in the temperature -- isospin density plane for the first time. The data for all observables will be useful for the benchmarking of effective theories and low-energy models of QCD and are provided in ancillary files for simple reuse.

## Key claim

The speed of sound in QCD matter with finite isospin asymmetry is found to exceed the conformal bound of \(c_s^2 = 1/3\) deep in the pion condensed phase, marking the first first-principles lattice QCD observation of this violation; however, this result is obtained from a two-dimensional spline interpolation of the isospin density and is subject to the lattice spacings and volumes used in the simulation.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with \(N_f = 2+1\) flavors of dynamical staggered quarks at physical masses, using three different lattice spacings corresponding to \(a \approx 0.22\) fm, \(a \approx 0.15\) fm, and a finer spacing. They computed the isospin density as a function of temperature \(T\) and isospin chemical potential \(\mu_I\), then constructed a two-dimensional spline interpolation from which the pressure, interaction measure, energy density, entropy density, and speed of sound were derived analytically.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound squared \(c_s^2\) exceeds the conformal bound of \(1/3\) for \(\mu_I/m_\pi \gtrsim 0.6\) at low temperatures, reaching values above 0.4 in the BEC phase.
- The phase diagram in the temperature-isospin density plane is mapped for the first time, with the BEC phase boundary located near \(\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 0.5\) (the pion mass threshold) at low \(T\).
- Lattice spacings used: \(a \approx 0.22\) fm and \(a \approx 0.15\) fm, with additional finer lattices (\(N_t = 8, 10, 12\)) for continuum extrapolation.
- The isospin density \(n_I\) rises from zero in the vacuum phase to values up to \(n_I/m_\pi^3 \approx 2.0\) at \(\mu_I/m_\pi \approx 1.0\) on the coarser lattice.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the equation of state at nonzero isospin chemical potential, previously explored in effective models and at zero temperature, to finite temperature for the first time. The violation of the conformal speed-of-sound bound contradicts expectations from simple ideal-gas or conformal limits and provides a benchmark for effective theories such as chiral perturbation theory and low-energy QCD models (e.g., Son and Stephanov 2001). The results enable future comparisons with neutron-star merger simulations and heavy-ion collision phenomenology where isospin asymmetry is relevant.
