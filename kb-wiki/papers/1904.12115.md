# Direct capture cross section of $^{9}$Be(n,$γ$)$^{10}$Be

> The cross section of the $^{9}$Be(n,$γ$)$^{10}$Be reaction was calculated in the direct capture model. All parameters of the calculations were adjusted to properties of the $^{9}$Be + n system at thermal energies. The calculated cross section at thermonuclear energies shows the expected $1/v$ behavior of $s$-wave capture at low energies, but increases towards higher energies as typical $p$-wave capture. Excellent agreement between new experimental data in the astrophysically relevant energy region and the present calculation is found.

## Key claim

The direct capture model, with all parameters adjusted solely to the thermal properties of the ⁹Be + n system (without any adjustment to the new experimental data in the keV region), reproduces the recently measured ⁹Be(n,γ)¹⁰Be cross section in the astrophysically relevant energy region with excellent agreement (χ² ≈ 1.25 per point).

## Method

The direct capture (DC) cross section was calculated using a folding potential with a single adjustable strength parameter λ, which was fixed for each bound state by the binding energy and for the scattering s-wave by the thermal scattering lengths. Spectroscopic factors C²S were derived from the thermal neutron capture cross section (8.299 ± 0.119 mb) and branching ratios from Firestone and Revay (2016), and the p-wave potential strength was determined by averaging over all L=1 bound and quasi-bound states in ¹⁰Be. The DC calculation was complemented by the contributions of four low-lying resonances with properties taken from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Thermal neutron capture cross section adopted: σ_γ = 8.299 ± 0.119 mb (weighted average of 8.27 ± 0.13 mb from Firestone (2016), 8.49 ± 0.34 mb from Conneely (1986), and 8.31 ± 0.52 mb from Wallner (2019)).
- Calculated cross section at E_n,lab = 473 keV: σ_calc = 6.97 μb; after averaging over the experimental neutron energy distribution: σ_calc^av = 7.18 μb, deviating by 1.2σ from the experimental average of 8.4 ± 1.0 μb.
- χ² per point = 1.25 for the Wallner (2019) data; including the Shibata (1992) points reduces χ² to approximately 0.6 per point.
- Radiation width of the lowest 3⁻ resonance at E_R = 0.559 MeV: Γ_γ = 0.73 eV (experimental value from Kitazawa (1994)).
- Radiation width of the 2⁺ resonance at E_R = 0.730 MeV: estimated Γ_γ = 0.28 eV (significantly lower than the 0.73 eV assumed by Wallner (2019)).
- Fit parameters for the astrophysical reaction rate: a_0 = 1164.44, b_0 = 6.64777 (full set of eight parameters given in Table III).

## Context

This work extends the direct capture analysis of the ⁹Be(n,γ)¹⁰Be reaction first presented in Wallner (2019), providing a more detailed calculation that requires no adjustment to the new keV-region data. The calculated astrophysical reaction rate confirms the KADoNiS database rate (derived from preliminary Wallner data) and the REACLIB recommendation, but disagrees significantly with the STARLIB rate (Sallaska, 2013), which is based on an inappropriate statistical model calculation for such a light nucleus. The results enable reliable reaction rate calculations for the α-process in core-collapse supernovae, where ⁹Be(n,γ)¹⁰Be may detract from ¹²C formation via the ⁹Be(α,n)¹²C reaction.
