# Systematic effects in the low-energy behavior of the current SAID solution for the pion-nucleon system

> We investigate the description of the pion-nucleon experimental data at low energy (i.e., below pion laboratory kinetic energy of $100$ MeV) on the basis of the current SAID solution (WI08). We demonstrate that, in a self-consistent analysis scheme, the scale factors of the fits based on the Arndt-Roper formula come out independent of the beam energy and `cluster' around the expectation value of $1$. We report systematic effects in the low-energy behavior of the WI08 solution. These effects indicate that at least one of the three following assumptions, underlying the WI08 analysis, does \emph{not} hold, namely that: a) the bulk of the data is reliable, b) the electromagnetic effects are correctly accounted for, and c) the isospin invariance in the strong interactions of the $πN$ system is valid.

# Systematic effects in the low-energy behavior of the current SAID solution for the pion-nucleon system

## Key claim

The current SAID solution (WI08) exhibits systematic effects in its low-energy behavior (below 100 MeV pion laboratory kinetic energy), indicating that at least one of three underlying assumptions does not hold: that the bulk of the data is reliable, that electromagnetic effects are correctly accounted for, or that isospin invariance in the strong interactions of the pion-nucleon system is valid. The authors demonstrate that when a self-consistent analysis scheme is used, the scale factors from the Arndt-Roper formula cluster around the expectation value of 1, unlike the SAID results.

## Method

The authors performed partial-wave analyses of low-energy pion-nucleon data (T ≤ 100 MeV) using K-matrix parameterizations of s- and p-wave amplitudes, retaining terms up to epsilon^2 in the center-of-mass pion kinetic energy. They fitted the Arndt-Roper minimization function separately to their own curated databases for pi+ p elastic scattering, pi- p elastic scattering, and pi- p charge-exchange reactions, iteratively removing outliers based on p-value thresholds. The scale factors z_j from these fits were then compared to those obtained from the WI08 solution using the SAID databases.

## Key numbers

- The initial low-energy pi+ p database contained 57 data sets with 389 data points; after removing 24 degrees of freedom, the truncated database had 55 data sets with 365 degrees of freedom, and chi^2_min decreased from 706.7 to approximately 459.5
- For pi+ p, the weighted linear fit to scale factors yielded an intercept of 1.023 ± 0.033 and a slope of (-0.38 ± 0.40) × 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}
- For pi- p elastic scattering, the weighted linear fit to scale factors yielded an intercept of 1.007 ± 0.019 and a slope of (-0.08 ± 0.24) × 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}
- For pi- p charge exchange, the weighted linear fit to scale factors yielded an intercept of 1.002 ± 0.015 and a slope of (0.05 ± 0.27) × 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}
- The SAID pi+ p database for T ≤ 100 MeV contains 721 data points with overall chi^2_min = 1595.2, yielding a p-value of a few × 10^{-68}
- The SAID pi- p elastic scattering database for T ≤ 100 MeV contains 634 data points with overall chi^2_min = 1102.1, yielding a p-value of about 10^{-27}
- The SAID pi- p charge-exchange database for T ≤ 100 MeV contains 353 data points with overall chi^2_min = 401.3, yielding a p-value of about 3.9 × 10^{-2}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous critiques of the SAID phase-shift solutions (Matsinos and Rasche, multiple references) and supports the earlier conclusion by Gibbs, Ai, and Kaufmann (mid-1990s) that isospin invariance may be violated in the pion-nucleon interaction at low energy. The paper demonstrates that the WI08 solution provides a poor description of low-energy elastic scattering data (p-values of 10^{-68} and 10^{-27}) while the charge-exchange description is acceptable (p-value 0.039), and that the scale factors from the SAID analysis show energy-dependent systematic effects that disappear when the three reactions are analyzed jointly under the assumption of isospin invariance. This enables future work to test whether the violation of isospin invariance, incorrect electromagnetic corrections, or unreliable data is the source of the discrepancy.
