# Photoproduction of relativistic QED bound states in hadronic collisions

> In this paper we investigate the photoproduction of QED bound states in proton -- proton, proton -- nucleus and nucleus -- nucleus collisions at RHIC, LHC and FCC energies considering an accurate treatment of the absorptive corrections and for the nuclear form factor. The total cross sections for the production of singlet and triplet $(l^+ l^-)$ states, with $l = e,\, μ, \, τ$, are estimated and a detailed analysis of the photoproduction of singlet QED bound states is performed considering the rapidity ranges covered by central and forward detectors. The impact of the Coulomb corrections on the parapositronium production in heavy ion collisions is estimated. We predict a large number of events associated to the production of ($e^+ e^-$) and ($μ^+ μ^-$) states in hadronic collisions.

# Photoproduction of relativistic QED bound states in hadronic collisions

## Key claim
The photoproduction of singlet and triplet QED bound states (positronium, muonium, tauonium) in hadronic collisions at RHIC, LHC, and FCC energies yields large event rates for electron and muon bound states, but the tauonium cross sections are nine (three) orders of magnitude smaller than for positronium (muonium), making experimental reconstruction of tauonium a hard task. The Coulomb corrections reduce the parapositronium cross section by approximately 18-22% depending on energy, but the event rates remain large enough for future experimental constraints.

## Method
The authors computed total cross sections using the equivalent photon approximation with realistic nuclear form factors (Wood-Saxon for nuclei, dipole for protons) and an absorptive factor excluding hadronic overlap. Singlet state cross sections were derived from the Low formula using two-photon decay widths, while triplet state cross sections used the formalism of Serbo et al. (1998) incorporating the Gauss hypergeometric function. Rapidity distributions were calculated for central (-2.5 ≤ Y ≤ 2.5) and forward (2.0 ≤ Y ≤ 4.5) detector ranges, and Coulomb corrections from multiphoton exchange were included for parapositronium production in heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers
- Parapositronium total cross section in PbPb at LHC (√s_NN = 5.02 TeV): 333.2 × 10^12 fb (33.3 × 10^8 events/year)
- Paramuonium total cross section in PbPb at LHC (√s_NN = 5.02 TeV): 1297.0 × 10^6 fb (12.9 × 10^3 events/year)
- Paratauonium total cross section in PbPb at LHC (√s_NN = 5.02 TeV): 832.7 × 10^3 fb (8.3 events/year)
- Orthopositronium total cross section in PbPb at LHC (√s_NN = 5.02 TeV): 23.3 × 10^12 fb (2.3 × 10^8 events/year)
- Parapositronium cross section in pp at LHC (√s_NN = 14 TeV), central rapidity: 3.6 × 10^6 fb
- Parapositronium cross section in pPb at LHC (√s_NN = 8.8 TeV), central rapidity: 19.4 × 10^9 fb
- Coulomb-corrected parapositronium cross section in AuAu at RHIC (√s_NN = 0.2 TeV), central rapidity: 36.8 × 10^12 fb (3.6 × 10^8 events/year), a reduction of approximately 22% from the Born-level value of 46.6 × 10^12 fb
- Coulomb-corrected parapositronium cross section in PbPb at FCC (√s_NN = 39 TeV), central rapidity: 117.3 × 10^12 fb (13.0 × 10^9 events/year), a reduction of approximately 18% from the Born-level value of 142.5 × 10^12 fb

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Serbo et al. (1998, 2001) on positronium and muonium photoproduction in heavy ion collisions by updating predictions for RHIC and LHC energies and presenting first results for HE-LHC and FCC energies, as well as for pp and pA collisions. The authors also provide the first predictions for tauonium production. The results enable future experimental searches for QED bound states at current and future colliders, and the Coulomb correction analysis shows that future data can constrain the treatment of higher-order corrections, which have been debated in the context of QED pair production by Baltz et al. (2001-2009) and Zha et al. (2021). The authors note that generalization of Monte Carlo generators such as StarLight and SuperChic is needed to include experimental cuts for realistic detector simulations.
