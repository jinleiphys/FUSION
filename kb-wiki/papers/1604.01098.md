# Influence of coalescence parameters on the production of protons and Helium-3 fragments

> The time evolution of protons and $^3$He fragments from Au+Au/Pb+Pb reactions at 0.25, 2, and 20 GeV$/$nucleon is investigated with the potential version of the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model combined with the traditional coalescence afterburner. In the coalescence process, the relative distance $R_0$ and relative momentum $P_0$ are surveyed in the range of 3-4 fm and 0.25-0.35 GeV$/$c, respectively. For both clusters, a strong reversed correlation between $R_0$ and $P_0$ is seen and it is time-dependent as well. For protons, the accepted ($R_0$, $P_0$) bands lie in the time interval 30-60 fm$/$c, while for $^3$He, a longer time evolution (at about 60-90 fm$/$c) is needed. Otherwise, much smaller $R_0$ and $P_0$ values should be chosen. If we further look at the rapidity distributions from both central and semi-central collisions, it is found that the accepted [$t_{\rm cut}, (R_0, P_0$)] assemble can provide consistent results for proton yield and collective flows especially at mid-rapdities, while for $^3$He, the consistency is destroyed at both middle and projectile-target rapidities.

## Key claim

The accepted coalescence parameter sets (relative distance R0 and relative momentum P0) that reproduce proton yields are time-dependent and show a strong reversed correlation, with protons requiring a stopping time of 30-60 fm/c while Helium-3 needs a longer evolution of 60-90 fm/c; however, for Helium-3 the consistency of rapidity distributions and collective flows is destroyed at both mid-rapidities and projectile-target rapidities even when the total yield is matched.

## Method

The potential version of the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD/M) transport model was combined with a traditional phase-space coalescence afterburner to simulate Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies of 0.25, 2, and 20 GeV/nucleon. The coalescence parameters R0 (relative distance) and P0 (relative momentum) were scanned in the ranges 3-4 fm and 0.25-0.35 GeV/c respectively, with the stopping time t_cut varied from 10 to 100 fm/c in steps of 10 fm/c, to identify parameter sets that reproduce experimental yields of free protons and Helium-3 fragments.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au at 0.25 GeV/nucleon, the total free proton yield is about 34 ± 8.
- For central Au+Au at 2 GeV/nucleon, the total free proton yield is about 116 ± 2.
- For central Pb+Pb at 20 GeV/nucleon, the total free proton yield is about 145 ± 1, and the number of Helium-3 fragments within rapidity |y| < 1.7 is 0.22 ± 0.02.
- The accepted time interval for proton production is 30-60 fm/c, while for Helium-3 it is 60-90 fm/c.
- The maximum central baryon density reaches less than 2 times normal density at 0.25 GeV/nucleon and more than 5 times normal density at 20 GeV/nucleon.
- The time interval for reduced baryon density above unity shortens from about 25 fm/c at 0.25 GeV/nucleon to about 9 fm/c at 20 GeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Li 2016) that used a single parameter set (R0=3.8 fm, P0=0.3 GeV/c) with different stopping times to describe proton and Helium-3 yields at AGS and SPS energies. It systematically scans the coalescence parameter space across three beam energies spanning SIS, AGS, and SPS regimes, revealing that the strong R0-P0 correlation is time-dependent and that Helium-3 production requires longer evolution times than protons. The results provide a benchmark for future improvements such as incorporating binding-energy checks, as examined by Goyal (2011) for Au+Au at ALADIN energies, and for constraining isospin-sensitive observables related to light clusters.
