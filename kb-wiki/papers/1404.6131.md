# Electric dipole moments of the nucleon and light nuclei

> The electric dipole moments of the nucleon and light ions are discussed and strategies for disentangling the underlying sources of CP violation beyond the Kobayashi-Maskawa quark-mixing mechanism of the Standard Model are indicated. Contribution to "45 years of nuclear theory at Stony Brook: a tribute to Gerald E. Brown".

# Electric dipole moments of the nucleon and light nuclei

## Key claim
The electric dipole moments (EDMs) of the nucleon and light ions can serve as probes for CP violation beyond the Kobayashi-Maskawa mechanism of the Standard Model, but there is no theorem that directly links the baryon asymmetry of the universe (BAU) to EDMs; sizable EDMs could exist in the QCD theta-term scenario without providing enough CP violation to explain the observed BAU.

## Method
The paper reviews the effective field theory (EFT) framework for connecting beyond-Standard-Model (BSM) CP-violating sources to hadronic and nuclear EDMs. This involves a four-step procedure: (1) integrating out BSM degrees of freedom above the electroweak scale to obtain dimension-six operators with Standard Model fields; (2) integrating out electroweak-scale degrees of freedom to retain only gluon and five lightest quark fields; (3) running the operators down to the chiral symmetry breaking scale (~1 GeV) via QCD renormalization-group equations; (4) using chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) or lattice QCD to translate the quark/gluon operators into hadronic observables. The paper specifically applies this to the deuteron, helion, and triton EDMs induced by the QCD theta term.

## Key numbers
- The baryon-to-photon ratio from CMB measurements: (6.08 ± 0.09) × 10⁻¹⁰, compared to the Standard Model prediction of ~10⁻¹⁸
- The experimental bound on the neutron EDM: |d_n| < 2.9 × 10⁻²⁶ e·cm (Sussex/RAL/ILL group, Baker 2006)
- The experimental bound on the proton EDM: |d_p| < 7.9 × 10⁻²⁵ e·cm (inferred from ¹⁹⁷Hg atom data)
- The experimental bound on the electron EDM: |d_e| < 8.7 × 10⁻²⁹ e·cm (from ThO molecule, Baron 2013)
- The QCD theta parameter bound from neutron EDM: |θ̄| ≲ 10⁻¹⁰
- The naive dimensional analysis estimate for the nucleon EDM from the KM mechanism: |d_N^SM| ~ 10⁻³¹ e·cm
- The "new physics window" for neutron EDM: 10⁻²⁴ e·cm > |d_N| ≳ 10⁻³⁰ e·cm
- The g₁ contribution to the deuteron EDM: (1.43–1.53) × 10⁻² g₁ g_{πNN} e·fm across multiple calculations (Liu and Timmermans with Av₁₈ potential; Afnan and Gibson with Reid93; Song et al. with Av₁₈; Bsaisou et al. with CD Bonn)

## Context
This paper extends the line of work connecting CP violation to baryogenesis, originally formulated by Sakharov (1967), and addresses the insufficiency of the Kobayashi-Maskawa mechanism (Kobayashi and Maskawa, 1973) to explain the matter-antimatter asymmetry. The review builds on EFT approaches to hadronic EDMs developed by Pospelov and collaborators, and on chiral perturbation theory calculations by de Vries (2011), Guo and Meißner (2012), and Bsaisou (2013). The work enables experimental strategies by the srEDM Collaboration (Farley 2003, Semertzidis 2003) and the JEDI Collaboration (Lehrach, Pretz 2013) to measure proton, deuteron, and helion EDMs in storage rings, with a precursor experiment at COSY in Jülich aiming for ~10⁻²⁴ e·cm sensitivity.
