# Parton dynamics and hadronization from the sQGP

> The hadronization of an expanding partonic fireball is studied within the Parton-Hadron-Strings Dynamics (PHSD) approach which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic equilibrium. Apart from strong parton interactions the expansion and development of collective flow is found to be driven by strong gradients in the parton mean-fields. An analysis of the elliptic flow $v_2$ demonstrates a linear correlation with the spatial eccentricity $ε$ as in case of ideal hydrodynamics. The hadronization occurs by quark-antiquark fusion or 3 quark/3 antiquark recombination which is described by covariant transition rates. Since the dynamical quarks become very massive, the formed resonant 'pre-hadronic' color-dipole states ($q\bar{q}$ or $qqq$) are of high invariant mass, too, and sequentially decay to the groundstate meson and baryon octets increasing the total entropy. This solves the entropy problem in hadronization in a natural way. Hadronic particle ratios turn out to be in line with those from a grandcanonical partition function at temperature $T \approx 170$ MeV.

## Key claim

The hadronization process in the Parton-Hadron-Strings Dynamics (PHSD) approach conserves four-momentum and flavor currents while slightly increasing total entropy, solving the entropy problem that plagues simple coalescence models; this is achieved because the fusion of rather massive dynamical quarks leads to high-invariant-mass pre-hadronic states that decay microcanonically to lower-mass hadrons.

## Method

The authors simulate an expanding partonic fireball using the PHSD approach, which combines the Dynamical Quasiparticle Model (DQPM) for partonic degrees of freedom with off-shell transport equations. The partonic system is initialized at temperature T = 1.7 Tc (Tc = 0.185 GeV) with a Gaussian ellipsoidal spatial distribution and a strangeness suppression factor of 1/3 relative to u and d quarks. Hadronization proceeds via covariant transition rates for q+qbar fusion to mesons and 3q/3qbar fusion to baryons, with the formed pre-hadronic states decaying through resonance or string decays (using the JETSET model for the high-mass continuum).

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature: T = 1.7 Tc, where Tc = 0.185 GeV
- Total energy conserved within 3% throughout the partonic expansion and hadronization phase
- Hadronization proceeds mainly during the time interval 1 fm/c < t < 7 fm/c
- Width of the pre-hadron phase-space distribution: sqrt(<r^2>) = 0.66 fm (in the rest frame)
- Ratio v2/epsilon is practically constant at approximately 0.2, as in ideal hydrodynamics
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio in the DQPM: eta/s approximately 0.2
- Particle ratios from PHSD: p/pi+ = 0.086, Lambda/K+ = 0.28, K+/pi+ = 0.157
- Statistical model at T = 170 MeV gives: p/pi+ = 0.086, Lambda/K+ = 0.26, K+/pi+ = 0.180
- K+/pi+ ratio from PHSD deviates from the statistical model by about 13%, indicating incomplete strangeness equilibration

## Context

This work extends the DQPM framework (Cassing 2006, Cassing 2007) and the PHSD approach (CasBrat) to study the hadronization dynamics of an expanding partonic fireball. It directly addresses the entropy problem identified in earlier coalescence models (Koal1, Koal2) by showing that the formation of high-mass pre-hadronic states and their subsequent decay naturally increases entropy. The results demonstrate that PHSD reproduces the constituent quark number scaling of elliptic flow observed experimentally at RHIC (STARS, Star08) and yields particle ratios compatible with the statistical hadronization model (PBM, PBM2) at a freeze-out temperature of about 170 MeV, while also showing that parton mean-field gradients drive collective flow similarly to ideal hydrodynamics.
