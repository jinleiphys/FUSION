# Hydrodynamic response in simulations within a multiphase transport model

> We carry out simulations using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model to describe the observed flow signatures in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions. Especially, we calculate the flow fluctuations of $v_2$ in terms of cumulant ratios and the standardized skewness. Based on event-by-event AMPT simulations, we study the linear and cubic response relation between $v_2$ and $\varepsilon_2$. We found that the observed response relation is compatible to what has been noticed in hydrodynamic modelings, with similar dependence on shear viscosity. Besides, this response relation is not sensitive to nonflow effects.

## Key claim

The AMPT model reproduces the linear and cubic hydrodynamic response relation between elliptic flow \(v_2\) and initial eccentricity \(\varepsilon_2\) that has been established in hydrodynamic simulations, and this response relation is not sensitive to nonflow effects even though the AMPT model contains nonhydrodynamic event-by-event fluctuations.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations using the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string melting for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV. They extracted the initial-state energy density profile before parton scatterings to compute \(\varepsilon_2\), and calculated \(v_2\) from the final-state particle distributions using both event-plane and multiparticle cumulant methods. The linear response coefficient \(\kappa_2\) and cubic response coefficient \(\kappa_2'\) were solved by minimizing event-by-event fluctuations, and the effect of shear viscosity was studied by adjusting the parton cascade parameters to vary \(\eta/s\) from 0.08 to 0.273.

## Key numbers

* For Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV in the 45-50% centrality class, the cubic-order response coefficient remains approximately constant at \(\kappa_2' \approx 0.1\) across all four viscosity settings.
* The effective specific shear viscosity values studied at temperature \(T=468\) MeV are \(\eta/s = 0.08\), 0.10, 0.14, and 0.273, corresponding to parameter sets A through D respectively.
* The Pearson correlation coefficient \(C(2,2)\) between \(V_2\) and \(\E_2\) decreases linearly as \(\eta/s\) increases from set A to set D.
* For the 40-50% centrality class at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, the AMPT simulation with \(\eta/s=0.273\) reproduces the CMS differential elliptic flow \(v_2\{EP\}\) as a function of \(p_T\) and pseudorapidity.

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic response relations, previously established in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations by Noronha-Hostler (2015) and Niemi (2015), to the AMPT transport model which includes nonflow effects beyond pure hydrodynamics. The results confirm that the linear and cubic response between \(v_2\) and \(\varepsilon_2\) is a robust feature of medium evolution in heavy-ion collisions, and the finding that nonflow effects do not alter the response coefficients enables more reliable extraction of medium properties from experimental flow data.
