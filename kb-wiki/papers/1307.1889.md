# Symmetric nuclear matter with chiral three-nucleon forces in the self-consistent Green's functions approach

> We present calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using chiral nuclear interactions within the Self-Consistent Green's Functions approach in the ladder approximation. Three-body forces are included via effective one-body and two-body interactions, computed from an uncorrelated average over a third particle. We discuss the effect of the three-body forces on the total energy, computed with an extended Galitskii-Migdal-Koltun sum-rule, as well as on single-particle properties. Saturation properties are substantially improved when three-body forces are included, but there is still some underlying dependence on the SRG evolution scale.

## Key claim

The inclusion of chiral three-nucleon forces via a density-dependent effective two-body interaction substantially improves saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter within the self-consistent Green's functions approach, but a residual dependence on the similarity renormalization group evolution scale remains, indicating that scale invariance is not fully achieved at this level of approximation.

## Method

The authors perform self-consistent Green's functions calculations in the ladder approximation for symmetric nuclear matter, using the Entem-Machleidt N3LO chiral two-nucleon force and a N2LO chiral three-nucleon force. The three-nucleon force is reduced to effective one-body and two-body interactions via an uncorrelated average over a third particle, following the prescription of Holt et al. (2010). The total energy is computed using an extended Galitskii-Migdal-Koltun sum-rule that includes a pure three-body term, and results are compared with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the same underlying interactions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density with two-nucleon forces only: approximately 0.42 fm⁻³, with saturation energy approximately -22 MeV (beyond the limit of the figure shown)
- Saturation density with two- and three-nucleon forces: approximately 0.14 fm⁻³, with saturation energy approximately 10 MeV (slightly repulsive relative to the empirical value)
- At density ρ = 0.32 fm⁻³, self-consistent Green's functions results are approximately 5 MeV more repulsive than Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results when three-nucleon forces are included
- Thermal effects at saturation are approximately 1 MeV, with differences between T = 0 and T = 5 MeV energies of approximately 3 MeV at the lowest densities considered
- The contact term contribution to the total energy in Hartree-Fock approximation is given by E_cE/A = -5.5 c_E (ρ/ρ₀)² MeV

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent Green's functions calculations that used only two-nucleon forces (Ramos 1989, Frick 2003, Rios 2008) by including chiral three-nucleon forces. It addresses the long-standing failure of realistic two-nucleon forces to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties, which places calculations in the Coester band (Muther 2000, Dewulf 2003). The results are relevant for recent ab initio calculations of finite nuclei using the same chiral interactions (Hagen 2012, Roth 2012, Soma 2013), and the approach enables systematic testing of scale invariance in non-perturbative many-body calculations with evolved interactions.
