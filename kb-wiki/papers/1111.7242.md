# Shear viscosity and damping of collective modes in a two-dimensional Fermi gas

> We compute the shear viscosity of a two dimensional Fermi gas interacting via a short range potential with scattering length $a_{2d}$ in kinetic theory. We find that kinetic theory predicts that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of a strongly interacting two dimensional gas is comparable to that of the three dimensional unitary gas. We use our results to compute the damping of collective modes in a trapped Fermi gas, and compare to experimental data recently obtained in E. Vogt et al., arXiv:1111.1173.

## Key claim

Kinetic theory predicts that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of a strongly interacting two-dimensional Fermi gas is comparable to that of the three-dimensional unitary gas, but this prediction is only formally reliable in the high-temperature limit (T >> T_F) or weak-interaction limit (k_F a_2d >> 1), and the classical calculation breaks down at T/T_F ≲ 0.5 where quantum statistics, correlations, and fluctuations become important.

## Method

The shear viscosity was computed by solving the linearized Boltzmann equation for fermions with a quadratic dispersion relation and elastic two-body scattering, using a variational ansatz with the trial function χ_ij = p_ij. The two-dimensional scattering T-matrix for a short-range potential was used to evaluate the collision integral, which was reduced to a one-dimensional integral and solved via saddle-point approximation. The resulting shear viscosity was then used to compute the damping of the quadrupole collective mode in a trapped gas by spatially averaging the viscosity over the cloud density profile and incorporating the frequency dependence of the viscosity via the relaxation time τ_R = η/(nT).

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity in two dimensions: η_2d = (mT)/(2π²) ( [log(5T/(2T_a,2d))]² + π² ), where T_a,2d = 1/(m a_2d²)
- Shear viscosity to density ratio: η_2d/n = (π/2)(T/T_F_loc) ( 1 + (1/π²)[log(5T/(2T_a,2d))]² )
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio reaches η_2d/s ≃ 0.5 for T/T_F_loc ≲ 0.5 at (k_F a_2d)² = 2
- Shear viscosity at unitarity in three dimensions: η_3d = (15/(32√π)) (mT)^(3/2)
- Three-dimensional shear viscosity to density ratio: η_3d/n = (45π^(3/2))/(64√2) (T/T_F_loc)^(3/2)
- Trap average ⟨α_n⟩ grows approximately as T² for small log(k_F a_2d) and T/T_F
- Theoretical predictions are in quantitative agreement with experimental data for log(k_F a_2d) = 5.3 and 9.7

## Context

This work extends kinetic theory calculations of shear viscosity from three-dimensional Fermi gases at unitarity (Bruun 2006, Schaefer 2009) to two-dimensional Fermi gases, motivated by recent experimental measurements of collective mode damping by Vogt et al. (2011). The results enable extraction of the shear viscosity from damping measurements in two-dimensional trapped Fermi gases, though the author notes that for log(k_F a_2d) ≳ 5 the measured mode frequencies deviate from hydrodynamic predictions, requiring a full kinetic theory treatment of the collective mode. The paper also identifies open questions about the role of the pseudo-gap phenomenon (observed by Feld 2011 in two dimensions), the Berezinsky-Kosterlitz-Thouless transition, and the logarithmic divergence of shear viscosity from hydrodynamic fluctuations (Kadanoff 2009).
