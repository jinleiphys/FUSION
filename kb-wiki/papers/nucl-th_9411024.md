# Subthreshold kaon production and the nuclear equation of state

> We reexamine in the relativistic transport model the dependence of kaon yield on the nuclear equation of state in heavy ion collisions at energies that are below the threshold for kaon production from the nucleon-nucleon interaction in free space. For Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon, we find that the kaon yield measured by the Kaos collaboration at GSI can be accounted for if a soft nuclear equation of state is used. We also confirm the results obtained in the non-relativistic transport model that the dependence of kaon yield on the nuclear equation of state is more appreciable in heavy ion collisions at lower incident energies. We further clarify the difference between the predictions from the relativistic transport model and the non-relativistic transport model with a momentum-dependent potential.

## Key claim

The kaon yield measured by the Kaos collaboration in Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon can be accounted for if a soft nuclear equation of state is used, but this conclusion should be taken with caution due to the lack of empirical information on the elementary kaon production cross section from nucleon-nucleon interactions near threshold.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model with a perturbative calculation of kaon production in head-on Au+Au collisions, employing two sets of relativistic equations of state based on the nonlinear sigma-omega model. One set used a nucleon effective mass of m* = 0.83 m with compressibilities K=200 MeV and K=380 MeV, while a second set used a smaller effective mass m* = 0.68 m with K=380 MeV to match the stiffness of the Skyrme-type equations of state used in non-relativistic models. Kaon production from baryon-baryon interactions was included using the linear parameterization of Randrup and Ko, and kaon rescattering with nucleons was treated by the perturbative test particle method.

## Key numbers

- For head-on Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon with the soft EOS, the total number of baryon-baryon collisions above kaon production threshold was about 95; with the stiff EOS it was reduced to about 54.
- The maximum central density reached was about 2.9 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0) with the soft EOS and about 2.4 rho_0 with the stiff EOS.
- The average density at which kaons were produced was about 2.5 rho_0 for the soft EOS and about 2.1 rho_0 for the stiff EOS.
- The average maximum kaon momentum (p_max) was about 0.272 GeV/c for the soft EOS and about 0.245 GeV/c for the stiff EOS.
- At 3 rho_0, the two relativistic EOS's (with m* = 0.68 m and K=380 MeV versus the soft EOS) differed by about 56 MeV in energy per nucleon, matching the difference in the Skyrme parameterization.
- The ratio of kaon production probabilities between soft and stiff EOS increased from 1.9 at 1.2 GeV/nucleon to 4.2 at 0.6 GeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Aichelin and Ko (1985) that subthreshold kaon production is sensitive to the nuclear equation of state at high densities, and it reconciles the discrepancy between relativistic transport model calculations by Fang et al. (1994) and non-relativistic transport model calculations by Huang et al. (1993), Hartnack et al. (1994), and Li (1994). The results show that when similar nuclear equations of state are used, both relativistic and non-relativistic transport models give consistent kaon yields, and the Kaos collaboration data favor a soft EOS. The paper enables future experiments at lower incident energies (around 0.6 GeV/nucleon) to more definitively determine the nuclear equation of state at high densities.
