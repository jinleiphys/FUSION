# Asymmetric nuclear matter in a parity doublet model with hidden local symmetry

> We construct a model to describe dense hadronic matter at zero and finite temperature, based on the parity doublet model of DeTar and Kunihiro, with including the iso-singlet scalar meson $σ$ as well as $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons. We show that, by including a six-point interaction of $σ$ meson, the model reasonably reproduces the properties of the normal nuclear matter with the chiral invariant nucleon mass $m_0$ in the range from $500~{\rm MeV}$ to $900~{\rm MeV}$. Furthermore, we study the phase diagram based on the model, which shows that the value of the chiral condensate drops at the liquid-gas phase transition point and at the chiral phase transition point. We also study asymmetric nuclear matter and find that the first order phase transition for the liquid-gas phase transition disappears in asymmetric matter and that the critical density for the chiral phase transition at non-zero density becomes smaller for larger asymmetry.

## Key claim

The parity doublet model extended with hidden local symmetry and a six-point scalar interaction can reproduce normal nuclear matter properties for a chiral invariant nucleon mass \(m_0\) in the range from 500 MeV to 900 MeV, but only when the six-point interaction is included; without it, the incompressibility cannot be matched.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral effective Lagrangian based on the parity doublet model of DeTar and Kunihiro (1988), adding \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) vector mesons via hidden local symmetry and a six-point interaction of the \(\sigma\) meson. They performed a global fit of ten model parameters to vacuum masses (nucleon, pion, \(\rho\), \(\omega\)), the pion decay constant, and four saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (density, binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy), for five fixed values of \(m_0\) (500, 600, 700, 800, 900 MeV). The equation of state and phase diagram were then computed in the mean-field approximation at zero and finite temperature for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and binding energy \(E/A - m_+ = -16\ \text{MeV}\) are used as inputs for all \(m_0\) values.
- Incompressibility \(K = 240\ \text{MeV}\) and symmetry energy \(E_{\text{sym}} = 31\ \text{MeV}\) are used as inputs.
- Predicted slope parameter \(L\) ranges from 74 MeV (for \(m_0 = 800\ \text{MeV}\)) to 78 MeV (for \(m_0 = 600, 700\ \text{MeV}\)), all within the allowed region from heavy-ion and neutron-star constraints.
- For symmetric matter at \(T=0\), the critical baryon chemical potential for chiral phase transition ranges from \(\mu_B^c = 1305\ \text{MeV}\) (for \(m_0 = 500\ \text{MeV}\)) to \(1540\ \text{MeV}\) (for \(m_0 = 900\ \text{MeV}\)), corresponding to critical densities from \(0.44\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) to \(2.98\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- For asymmetric matter at \(\mu_I = 100\ \text{MeV}\) and \(T=0\), the critical density for chiral transition is slightly smaller, e.g., \(0.43\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) for \(m_0 = 500\ \text{MeV}\) and \(2.98\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) for \(m_0 = 900\ \text{MeV}\).
- The liquid-gas phase transition becomes second order in asymmetric matter; the critical baryon chemical potential for this transition at \(\mu_I = 100\ \text{MeV}\) is about 881–891 MeV depending on \(m_0\).

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model studies of Zschiesche (2006) and Sasaki (2010) by including vector mesons via hidden local symmetry and a six-point scalar interaction, which allows reproduction of the incompressibility for a wider range of the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\) (500–900 MeV) than previously possible. The results for symmetric matter are consistent with the earlier parity doublet study by Sasaki (2010) for \(m_0 = 800\ \text{MeV}\). The finding that the first-order liquid-gas transition disappears in asymmetric matter agrees with earlier work by Muller (1995). The predicted slope parameters satisfy constraints from heavy-ion experiments and neutron-star observations compiled by Lattimer (2012) and others. The study enables future work on hyperonic matter, charged pion condensation, and further narrowing of the chiral invariant mass value.
