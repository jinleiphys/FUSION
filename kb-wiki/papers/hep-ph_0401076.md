# Skyrmion formation in 1+1 dimensions with chemical potential

> Formation of topological objects during phase transitions has been discussed extensively in literature. In all these discussions defects and anti-defects form with equal probabilities. In contrast, many physical situations, such as formation of baryons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at present energies, flux tube formation in superconductors in the presence of external magnetic field, and formation of superfluid vortices in a rotating vessel, require a mechanism which can bias (say) defects over anti-defects. Such a bias can crucially affect defect-anti-defect correlations, apart from its effects on defect density. In this paper we initiate an investigation for the basic mechanism of biased formation of defects. For Skyrmions in 1+1 dimensions, we show that incorporation of a chemical potential term in the effective potential leads to a domain structure where order parameter is spatially varying. We show that this leads to biased formation of Skyrmions.

## Key claim

The authors show that incorporating a chemical potential term into the effective potential for Skyrmions in 1+1 dimensions leads to a spatially varying order parameter even in equilibrium, which in turn biases the formation of Skyrmions over anti-Skyrmions. This bias is derived under the assumption that the chemical potential term is linear in the derivative of the order parameter and therefore not positive definite, making constant field configurations no longer the lowest free energy state.

## Method

The authors add a chemical potential term \(-\mu n(x)\) to the free energy of the linear chiral sigma model in 1+1 dimensions, where \(n(x) = (1/2\pi) \partial_x \theta\) is the Skyrmion number density. They minimize the free energy density with respect to \(\partial_x \theta\) to find the equilibrium spatial variation \(\partial_x \theta = \mu / (4\pi \phi_0^2)\). The physical space is divided into domains of size \(\xi\) with this prescribed gradient, and inter-domain regions of size \(\chi\) where the phase interpolates between domains; the path on the vacuum manifold \(S^1\) that minimizes the free energy (including the chemical potential term) is selected, modifying the conventional geodesic rule. Analytical calculations for a two-domain compact space \(S^1\) and numerical simulations for a three-domain case are performed to determine the probability of Skyrmion formation.

## Key numbers

* The equilibrium gradient of the phase is \(\partial_x \theta = \mu / (4\pi \phi_0^2)\), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential and \(\phi_0\) is the constant magnitude of the field.
* The net phase variation within a single domain of size \(\xi\) is \(\Delta \theta = \mu \xi / (4\pi \phi_0^2)\).
* For the two-domain case, no anti-Skyrmions form when \(\Delta \theta < \pi / (\chi/\xi + 2)\), where \(\chi\) is the inter-domain region size.
* For the two-domain case, when \(\theta_2\) lies between \(\Delta \theta\) and \(2\pi - \Delta \theta\), case (4) (both junctions anti-clockwise) gives formation of a winding-one defect, while case (3) (both junctions clockwise) gives an anti-defect; the conditions for case (3) are mutually inconsistent, so no anti-defects form.

## Context

This work extends the Kibble mechanism (Kibble, 1976; Kibble, 1980) and the Kibble-Zurek mechanism (Zurek, 1985) for topological defect formation, which conventionally predict equal probabilities for defects and anti-defects. The authors address physical situations requiring a bias, such as baryon formation in heavy-ion collisions with non-zero baryon chemical potential (referenced as rhic, 2004), flux tube formation in superconductors with external magnetic fields, and superfluid vortices in rotating vessels. The results enable predictions for baryon-anti-baryon correlations in heavy-ion collisions and provide a framework for biased defect formation that can be extended to higher dimensions.
