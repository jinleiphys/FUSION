# Chiral phase transition in (2 + 1)-flavor QCD

> The chiral phase transition temperature $T_{c}^{0}$ is a fundamental quantity of QCD. To determine this quantity we have performed simulations of (2 + 1)-flavor QCD using the Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ/tree) action on $N_τ=6, 8$ and 12 lattices with aspect ratios $N_σ/N_τ$ ranging from 4 to 8. In our simulations the strange quark mass is fixed to its physical value $m_{s}^{\rm{phy}}$, and the values of two degenerate light quark masses $m_{l}$ are varied from $m_{s}^{\rm{phy}}/20$ to $m_{s}^{\rm{phy}}/160$ which correspond to a Goldstone pion mass $m_π$ ranging from 160 MeV to 55 MeV in the continuum limit. By investigating the light quark mass dependence and the volume dependence of various chiral observables, e.g. chiral susceptibilities and Binder cumulants, no evidence for a first order phase transition in our current quark mass window is found. Two estimators $T_{60}$ and $T_δ$ are proposed to extract the chiral phase transition temperature $T_{c}^{0}$ in the chiral and continuum limit and our current estimate for $T_{c}^{0}$ is $132_{-6}^{+3}$ MeV.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition temperature in the chiral and continuum limits is estimated to be \(132_{-6}^{+3}\) MeV, but this result carries the caveat that no evidence for a first-order phase transition was found in the quark mass window studied (\(m_\pi\) from 160 MeV down to 55 MeV), and the analysis relies on extrapolations from finite quark masses and finite lattice spacings using two different estimators (\(T_{60}\) and \(T_\delta\)).

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of (2+1)-flavor QCD using the Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ/tree) action on \(N_\tau = 6, 8, 12\) lattices with aspect ratios \(N_\sigma/N_\tau\) from 4 to 8. The strange quark mass was fixed to its physical value, while the degenerate light quark masses were varied from \(m_s^{\rm phy}/20\) to \(m_s^{\rm phy}/160\), corresponding to Goldstone pion masses from 160 MeV to 55 MeV in the continuum limit. Chiral susceptibilities, Binder cumulants, and the ratio \(M/\chi_M\) were analyzed to extract the chiral phase transition temperature via two estimators: \(T_{60}\) (where the susceptibility falls to 60% of its peak value) and \(T_\delta\) (where \(H\chi_M/M = 1/\delta\)), with finite-volume and finite-quark-mass extrapolations.

## Key numbers

* The chiral phase transition temperature in the continuum limit: \(T_c^0 = 132_{-6}^{+3}\) MeV.
* On \(N_\tau = 12\) lattices, the infinite-volume extrapolation gave \(T_\delta(V\to\infty, m_l=m_s/80) \approx 138\) MeV and \(T_\delta(V\to\infty, m_l=m_s/40) \approx 141\) MeV; the chiral extrapolation gave \(T_c^0(N_\tau=12) \simeq 134(2)\) MeV.
* On \(N_\tau = 8\) lattices, \(T_c^0 = 142(2)\) MeV; on \(N_\tau = 6\) lattices, \(T_c^0 = 147(2)\) MeV.
* The light quark mass range studied corresponds to pion masses from 160 MeV down to 55 MeV in the continuum limit.
* The strange quark mass was fixed to its physical value; light quark masses were varied from \(m_s^{\rm phy}/20\) to \(m_s^{\rm phy}/160\).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the chiral phase transition by the HotQCD collaboration (Ding 2015, Li 2017, Ding 2018) using finer lattices and lighter quark masses. The result \(T_c^0 = 132_{-6}^{+3}\) MeV updates earlier estimates and constrains the location of the tricritical point in the Columbia plot, which separates the first-order and crossover regions. The finding that the chiral transition is consistent with a second-order \(O(N)\) transition rather than a first-order one for quark masses down to \(m_s/160\) informs the search for a possible critical point in the QCD phase diagram at non-zero baryon chemical potential, as discussed in the context of the Columbia plot scenarios from Pisarski and Wilczek (1984).
