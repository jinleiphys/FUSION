# A Holographic Dual of Hydrodynamics

> We consider a gravity dual description of time dependent, strongly interacting large-Nc N=4 SYM. We regard the gauge theory system as a fluid with shear viscosity. Our fluid is expanding in one direction following the Bjorken's picture that is relevant to RHIC experiments. We obtain the dual geometry at the late time that is consistent with dissipative hydrodynamics. We show that the integration constants that cannot be determined by hydrodynamics are given by looking at the horizon of the dual geometry. Relationship between time dependence of the energy density and bulk singularity is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a holographic dual (via AdS/CFT) of a strongly coupled large-Nc N=4 SYM fluid undergoing one-dimensional Bjorken expansion with shear viscosity, showing that the dual geometry at late times is consistent with dissipative hydrodynamics and that integration constants undetermined by hydrodynamics alone can be fixed by requiring regularity at the black hole horizon.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a boost-invariant fluid with shear viscosity, using the Bjorken picture of one-dimensional expansion. They then solve the five-dimensional Einstein equations with negative cosmological constant in Fefferman-Graham coordinates, using the boundary energy-momentum tensor from hydrodynamics as input. The metric is obtained recursively and resummed into an analytic form valid at late times, with the shear viscosity assumed to scale as eta ~ 1/tau (beta=1) based on the static strong-coupling result eta ~ T^3.

## Key numbers

- The energy density for the viscous case with beta=1 is given by rho = rho_0 / tau^(4/3) - 2 eta_0 / tau^2.
- The temperature behaves as T = T_0 ( 1/tau^(1/3) - (eta_0/(2 rho_0)) (1/tau) + ... ).
- The entropy per unit rapidity and unit transverse area at infinite time is S_infinity = (N_c^2/(2 pi))^(1/4) (pi/3)^(3/4) * 2 sqrt(2) * rho_0^(3/4).
- The normalized entropy creation rate from the gravity dual is (1/S) dS/dtau = eta_0/(rho_0 tau^(5/3)) + O(tau^(-7/3)).
- The regularity analysis shows the geometry is regular and viscosity effects are visible for 1/3 <= beta < 5/3, with beta=1 lying in this range.

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach of Janik and Peschanski (2006), who constructed the dual geometry for a non-viscous (perfect fluid) Bjorken expansion, by including shear viscosity effects. It builds on the calculation of shear viscosity at strong coupling for N=4 SYM by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001). The results enable further study of non-equilibrium strongly coupled gauge theories via holography, including computation of observables relevant to RHIC physics, and open the door to extensions with chemical potential, bulk viscosity, or three-dimensional expansion.
