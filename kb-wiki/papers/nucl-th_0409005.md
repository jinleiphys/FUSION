# Empirical Estimates of the Neutron-Nucleus Scattering Cross Sections

> Theoretical study of systematics of neutron scattering cross sections on various materials for neutron energies up to several hundred MeV are of practical importance. In this paper, we analysed various cross sections of neutron-nucleus (n-N) scattering for several systems in the energy range of 50-250 MeV, predicted by the optical model using Koning-Delaroche potentials. We perform the Ramsauer model parameterization of total scattering cross sections and derive the systematics of the Ramsauer model parameters. We propose an empirical approach to successfully predict the energy dependence of total, shape elastic, reaction cross sections and zero degree scattering angular distributions. We demonstrate that owing to two conditions, only two out of these four cross sections need to be fitted empirically. Further, we modified the Wick's approximation for the zero degree angular distributions and use this approach for estimating various cross sections for any nucleus from Aluminum to Lead.

# Empirical Estimates of the Neutron-Nucleus Scattering Cross Sections

## Key claim
The authors propose that only two of the four neutron-nucleus scattering cross sections (total, shape elastic, reaction, and zero-degree angular distributions) need to be fitted empirically, with the remaining two derived from relations between them, provided the fits are performed for neutron energies between 50-250 MeV and for nuclei from Aluminum to Lead. The claim is conditional on using the Koning-Delaroche optical model potentials as the reference "data" rather than experimental measurements.

## Method
The authors used the optical model code SCAT2 with Koning-Delaroche potentials to generate total, shape elastic, reaction, and zero-degree angular distribution cross sections for seven nuclei (Al, Ca, Cu, Y, Sn, W, Pb) over the center-of-mass energy range 50-250 MeV. They performed Ramsauer model parameterizations of the total cross sections using six global parameters, and separately fitted reaction cross sections with three parameters. They also parameterized the deviation of zero-degree angular distributions from the Wick's limit using a Woods-Saxon function of energy with nine parameters. The shape elastic cross sections were either derived as the difference of total and reaction cross sections, or fitted directly using a modified Ramsauer form with seven parameters.

## Key numbers
- Energy range studied: 50-250 MeV center-of-mass neutron energy
- Nuclei studied: 7 targets from Al (A=27) to Pb (A=208)
- Ramsauer model fit to total cross sections: chi-squared/N = 653.762 with 6 free parameters
- Reaction cross section fit: chi-squared/N = 323.641 with 3 parameters
- Shape elastic cross section fit: chi-squared/N = 1222.97 with 7 parameters
- Ramsauer radius parameter for total cross sections: r0 = 1.25519 fm
- Ramsauer radius parameter for reaction cross sections: r0 = 1.38413 fm
- Ramsauer radius parameter for shape elastic cross sections: r0 = 1.20815 fm
- Potential depth parameter in global beta function: b0 = 5.84633
- Non-local parameter in global beta function: c0 = 0.97676
- Woods-Saxon deviation function energy midpoint parameter: E0 = 61.557 + 0.47825*A - 9.90931e-4*A^2 (in MeV)

## Context
This work extends the Ramsauer model parameterization approach previously applied to neutron total cross sections by Grimes et al. (1998, 2000) and Anderson and Grimes (1990), and the deviation function analysis of Dietrich et al. (2003). It builds on the comprehensive optical model potential parameterization of Koning and Delaroche (2003) by providing empirical formulas that reproduce their SCAT2 code predictions. The empirical method enables rapid estimation of neutron scattering cross sections for applications such as accelerator-driven sub-critical (ADS) systems, where neutron cross sections up to several hundred MeV are needed for materials from Aluminum to Lead.
