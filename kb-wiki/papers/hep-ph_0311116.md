# How to extract the $ΛN$ scattering length from production reactions

> A dispersion integral is derived that allows one to relate directly (spin dependent) $ΛN$ invariant mass spectra, measured in a large-momentum transfer reaction such as $pp\to K^+pΛ$ or $γd\to K^+nΛ$, to the scattering length for elastic $ΛN$ scattering. The involved systematic uncertainties are estimated to be smaller than 0.3 fm. This estimate is confirmed by comparing results of the proposed formalism with those of microscopic model calculations. We also show, for the specific reaction $pp\to K^+Λp$, how polarization observables can be used to separate the two spin states of the $ΛN$ system.

# How to extract the ΛN scattering length from production reactions

## Key claim

The paper derives a dispersion integral that directly relates spin-dependent ΛN invariant mass spectra from large-momentum transfer reactions (such as pp → K⁺pΛ or γd → K⁺nΛ) to the elastic ΛN scattering length, with a theoretical uncertainty estimated to be smaller than 0.3 fm, but only for observables dominated by a single partial wave, which requires polarization measurements to separate the spin-singlet and spin-triplet contributions.

## Method

The authors derive a dispersion relation for the squared production amplitude |A(m²)|², using Cauchy's theorem to relate the ΛN scattering phase shift δ(m²) to the logarithm of the measured invariant mass distribution. They truncate the integration to a finite range up to m_max², chosen such that the ΛN system remains in an S-wave and below the ΣN threshold (corresponding to a maximum kinetic energy ε_max ≈ 40 MeV). The slowly varying background function Φ is omitted from the integral because the principal value vanishes for a constant argument, allowing direct extraction of the scattering length from the differential cross section data. The method is tested against microscopic model calculations for pp → K⁺pΛ using several YN interaction models (NSC97a, NSC97f, and the Jülich model).

## Key numbers

- Theoretical uncertainty of the extracted scattering length: δa ≤ 0.3 fm, estimated from left-hand cut contributions (δa^(lhc) ~ 0.05 fm) and high-energy phase shift contributions (δa^(m_max) ~ 0.2 fm, using δ_max ≤ 0.4 rad for all considered YN models).
- Recommended maximum kinetic energy for integration: ε_max ≈ 40 MeV, derived from the condition a p' ~ 1 for a ~ 1 fm, corresponding to m_max² ≈ m₀²(1 + 1/(μ m₀ a²)).
- For the reaction pp → K⁺X at T_p = 2.3 GeV and θ_K^L = 10±2°, the extracted effective scattering length is (-1.5 ± 0.15 ± 0.3) fm, where the first error is experimental (from data with ~2 MeV resolution) and the second is theoretical.
- Prior ΛN scattering lengths from elastic data (Ref. data1b): a_s = -1.8^{+2.3}_{-4.2} fm and a_t = -1.6^{+1.1}_{-0.8} fm, with effective ranges between 0 and 16 fm allowed.
- Six different YN models (Rijken, 1999) give S-wave scattering lengths ranging from -0.7 to -2.6 fm (singlet) and -1.7 to -2.15 fm (triplet).

## Context

This work extends the approach of using production reactions to determine low-energy hyperon-nucleon parameters, previously attempted for K⁻d → nΛγ (Ben, 1998; Workman, 1992; Akh, 1998) and K⁻d → π⁻pΛ (Tan, 1998). It directly addresses limitations of the effective range expansion used in the analysis of pp → K⁺pΛ data (Jan, 2002), which gave -2 ± 0.2 fm but with only statistical errors and strong correlations between a and r. The proposed dispersion method avoids these correlations and provides a controlled theoretical uncertainty. The formalism enables future experiments with polarized beam and/or target to separately determine the spin-singlet and spin-triplet ΛN scattering lengths, which would test SU(3) flavor symmetry in the baryon-baryon interaction.
