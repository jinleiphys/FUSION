# Bottomonium transport in p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

> We employ the Boltzmann transport model to study the sequential suppression pattern of $Υ(1S,2S,3S)$ states in both small (p-Pb) and the large (Pb-Pb) collision systems at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. The cold nuclear matter effects happen before the formation of bottomonium, which is the same for different bottomonium states $Υ(1S,2S,3S)$. The sequential suppression pattern of bottomonium states is treated as a signal of the hot medium effects, where bottomonium states suffer different magnitudes of the color screening and parton inelastic scatterings due to their different geometry sizes and binding energies. Bottomonium excited states are more easily dissociated with smaller binding energy via the final-state interactions. Including both cold and hot medium effects, transport model consistently explains the experimental data about bottomonium in both small and large collision systems.

## Key claim
The Boltzmann transport model, including both cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing, Cronin) and hot medium effects (color screening, gluon-dissociation), consistently explains the sequential suppression pattern of bottomonium states in both small (p-Pb) and large (Pb-Pb) collision systems at the LHC, with the caveat that the in-medium binding energy of Υ(1S) is approximated as 40% of its vacuum value and its temperature dependence is neglected.

## Method
The authors solve the Boltzmann transport equation for bottomonium phase-space distributions in a (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic medium. Cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing via EPS09, Cronin effect via Gaussian smearing) are included in the initial conditions, while hot medium effects are encoded in a temperature-dependent decay rate for Υ(1S) from gluon-dissociation; decay rates for excited states are obtained by geometric scaling with the ground state. Bottomonium regeneration from bottom-anti-bottom coalescence is neglected due to the small bottom quark yield.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: √s_NN = 5.02 TeV for both p-Pb and Pb-Pb systems.
- Direct production cross-sections before feed-down at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: dσ_direct(1S,1P,2S,2P,3S)/dy = (37.97, 44.2, 18.27, 37.68, 8.21) nb.
- Bottomonium masses taken from PDG: m_Υ(1S,1P,2S,2P,3S) = (9.46, 9.89, 10.02, 10.25, 10.35) GeV.
- Maximum initial QGP temperatures in central collisions: 248 MeV (forward) and 289 MeV (backward) in p-Pb; 510 MeV in central Pb-Pb.
- QGP lifetime in central collisions: ~3 fm/c (p-Pb) and ~12 fm/c (Pb-Pb).
- Critical temperature for QGP-hadron transition: T_c = 165 MeV.
- Hydrodynamic start time: τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c.
- In-medium binding energy of Υ(1S) taken as 40% of its vacuum value.
- Bottom quark mass: m_b = 5.28 GeV.
- Mean transverse momentum square in pp: ⟨p_T²⟩_pp = 80 (GeV/c)²; n = 2.5.
- Cronin parameter: a_gN = 0.15 (GeV/c)².

## Context
This work extends the Boltzmann transport approach previously applied to charmonium (Chen 2016) to bottomonium in both small and large systems, building on the rate-equation model of Grandchamp (2003) and Du (2017) and the complex potential Schrödinger equation approach of Wen (2022). It provides a unified description of CMS (2022) p-Pb data and CMS (2017) and ALICE (2018) Pb-Pb data, enabling extraction of reliable in-medium decay rates for bottomonium states in the quark-gluon plasma. The neglect of regeneration is justified by the small bottom quark yield compared to charm, contrasting with the statistical hadronization model of Andronic (2007).
