# Spin-1 Particle in the Light-Front Approach

> The electromagnetic current of spin-1 composite particles does not transform properly under rotations if only the valence contribution is considered in the light-front model. In particular, the plus component of the current, evaluated only for the valence component of the wave function, in the Drell-Yan frame violates rotational symmetry, which does not allow a unique calculation of the electromagnetic form-factors. The prescription suggested by Grach and Kondratyuk [Sov. J. Nucl. Phys. 38, 198 (1984)] to extract the form factors from the plus component of the current, eliminates contributions from pair diagrams or zero modes, which if not evaluated properly cause the violation of the rotational symmetry. We address this problem in an analytical and covariant model of a spin-1 composite particle.

## Key claim
The paper shows analytically that the Grach-Kondratyuk prescription for extracting electromagnetic form factors from the plus component of the current eliminates pair-term (zero-mode) contributions in a light-front model of a spin-1 composite particle, even when instant-form polarization states in the cartesian representation are used, generalizing a previous result that had been demonstrated only for light-cone polarization states.

## Method
The authors construct an analytical light-front model of a spin-1 composite particle as a two-fermion bound state with a simple Dirac vertex structure (gamma-mu). They evaluate the plus component of the electromagnetic current in the Breit frame with momentum transfer along the transverse direction (Drell-Yan condition q+=0), using the pole dislocation method to isolate the pair-term contributions that survive in the limit q+ → 0+. They then substitute the pair-term matrix elements into the Grach-Kondratyuk prescription for the charge (G0), magnetic (G1), and quadrupole (G2) form factors and demonstrate the cancellation algebraically.

## Key numbers
- The angular condition for the matrix elements of J+ in the Breit-frame with q+=0 is given by Δ(q²) = (1+2η)I⁺₁₁ + I⁺₁₋₁ − √(8η) I⁺₁₀ − I⁺₀₀ = 0, where η = q²/(4m_v²).
- The pair-term contributions to the matrix elements satisfy J⁺Z_xx = −η J⁺Z_zz and J⁺Z_zx = −√η J⁺Z_zz, with J⁺Z_yy = 0.
- Substituting these into the Grach-Kondratyuk prescription yields G₀^(GK,Z) = 0, G₁^(GK,Z) = 0, and G₂^(GK,Z) = 0, demonstrating complete cancellation of pair terms.

## Context
This work extends previous numerical studies by Pacheco et al. (1997, 1998) on the ρ-meson electromagnetic form factors, which found that the Grach-Kondratyuk prescription (Grach and Kondratyuk, 1984) reproduced covariant results when only valence contributions were included. The paper generalizes the analytical demonstration of pair-term cancellation by Jaus (2001) from light-cone polarization states to instant-form polarization states in the cartesian representation. The results enable future application of this prescription to more realistic vertex structures for the ρ-meson or deuteron elastic photo-absorption processes.
