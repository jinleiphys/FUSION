# Relaxation time ansatz and shear and bulk viscosities of gluon matter

> Shear and bulk viscosity-to-entropy density ratios are calculated for the pure gluon matter in a non-equilibrium mean-field quasiparticle approach within the relaxation time approximation. We study how different approximations used in the literature affect the results for the shear and bulk viscosities. Though the results for the shear viscosity turned out to be quite robust, all evaluations of the shear and bulk viscosities obtained in the framework of the relaxation time approximation can be considered only as rough estimations.

## Key claim

The shear and bulk viscosity-to-entropy density ratios for pure gluon matter are calculated within a non-equilibrium mean-field quasiparticle approach using the relaxation time approximation, but all such evaluations can be considered only as rough estimations because different choices of the relaxation time ansatz and the variation operation lead to significantly different expressions for the bulk viscosity, even though the shear viscosity results are robust.

## Method

The authors model pure SU(3) gluon matter at temperatures above the critical temperature T_c using a quasiparticle model with a temperature-dependent gluon pole mass m^2(T) = (N_c/6) g^2(T) T^2, where the running coupling g^2(T) includes a next-to-leading-order double-logarithmic correction. They derive expressions for shear and bulk viscosities by linearizing the kinetic equation around local equilibrium and using three distinct relaxation time ansatze: ansatz I uses the collision term St f = -δf/τ with the Landau-Lifshitz condition δT^00=0; ansatz II uses St f = -δ̃f/τ̃ with the condition δ̃T^00=0; ansatz III uses St f = -δ̃f/τ̃ but imposes the Landau-Lifshitz condition δT^00=0, which requires an additional variable shift.

## Key numbers

* The gluon degeneracy factor is d_g = 2(N_c^2 - 1) = 16 for N_c = 3.
* The critical temperature is T_c = 265 MeV for both the leading-order fit (T_s/T_c = 0.5853, λ = 3.3) and the new subleading-order fit (T_s/T_c = 0.015, λ = 1.53).
* The difference between bulk viscosities from ansatz I and ansatz III is bounded by 0 ≤ (ζ_I - ζ_III)/ζ_III < 0.23 for the gluon model where 0 < T dm/dT < m.
* For the case dm/dT < 0 (as in relativistic mean-field hadronic models), the inequality becomes 0.15 (T dm)/(m dT) ≤ (ζ_I - ζ_III)/ζ_III.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle model approach previously applied to gluon and glueball matter by the same authors (Khvorostukhin, Toneev, Voskresensky 2010) and compares with lattice data from Sakai and Nakamura (2007), Meyer (2008), and Borsanyi et al. (2009). The paper demonstrates that different relaxation time ansatze used in the literature, such as those by Chakraborty and Kapusta (2010) and Bluhm et al. (2010), yield different expressions for bulk viscosity, highlighting ambiguities in the relaxation time approximation that must be resolved before reliable predictions can be made for heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
