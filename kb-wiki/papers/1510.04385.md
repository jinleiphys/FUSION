# Pairing reentrance in warm rotating $^{104}$Pd nucleus

> Pairing reentrance phenomenon in the warm rotating $^{104}$Pd nucleus is studied within the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS)-based approach (the FTBCS1). The theory takes into account the effect of quasiparticle number fluctuations on the pairing field at finite temperature and angular momentum within the pairing model plus noncollective rotation along the symmetry axis. The numerical calculations for the pairing gaps and nuclear level densities (NLD), of which an anomalous enhancement has been experimentally observed at low excitation energy $E^*$ and high angular momentum $J$, show that the pairing reentrance is seen in the behavior of pairing gap obtained within the FTBCS1 at low $E$ and high $J$. This leads to the enhancement of the FTBCS1 level densities, in good agreement with the experimental observation. This agreement indicates that the observed enhancement of the NLD might be the first experimental detection of the pairing reentrance in a finite nucleus.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of nuclear level densities (NLD) in $^{104}$Pd at low excitation energy and high angular momentum might be the first experimental detection of pairing reentrance in a finite nucleus, but only when quasiparticle number fluctuations are included via the FTBCS1 theory; the standard FTBCS theory shows no such effect.

## Method

The authors applied the finite-temperature BCS theory including quasiparticle number fluctuations (FTBCS1) to calculate pairing gaps and nuclear level densities for $^{104}$Pd, using single-particle spectra from an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit and Coulomb interactions. The pairing interaction parameters were adjusted so that zero-temperature gaps matched empirical odd-even mass differences, and quadrupole deformation parameters were varied with angular momentum to fit empirical NLD used in CASCADE code fits to experimental proton spectra from the reaction $^{12}$C + $^{93}$Nb $\rightarrow$ $^{105}$Ag$^*$ $\rightarrow$ $^{104}$Pd$^* + p$.

## Key numbers

- Neutron pairing gap at $J = 30\hbar$ shows reentrance: zero at $E^* = 0$, increases with excitation energy, then decreases to vanish at $E^* \approx 7$ MeV (proton gap at $J = 20\hbar$ shows similar behavior, peaking at $E^* \approx 3$ MeV)
- Shape transition from prolate ($\beta_2 > 0$) to oblate ($\beta_2 < 0$) occurs at around $J = 20\hbar$
- Pairing gaps within FTBCS1 remain finite even at $E^* = 20$ MeV, except the proton gap at $J = 20\hbar$
- Local NLD enhancements appear at exactly $J = 20\hbar$ and $J = 30\hbar$, where pairing reentrance occurs

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction of pairing reentrance by Kammuri and the explanation by Moretto (1970s), which used standard FTBCS, by incorporating quasiparticle number fluctuations that smooth out the superfluid-normal phase transition. It directly addresses the experimental NLD enhancement observed by Mitra et al. (BARC experiments) in $^{104}$Pd, which was qualitatively similar to shell model quantum Monte Carlo predictions for $^{72}$Ge by Dean et al. The results suggest that the FTBCS1 theory can explain this enhancement as the first experimental signature of pairing reentrance in a finite nucleus, enabling future experimental searches for this phenomenon in other warm rotating nuclei.
