# Nonperturbative determination of the QCD potential at O(1/m)

> The relativistic correction to the QCD static inter-quark potential at O(1/m) is investigated nonperturbatively for the first time by using lattice Monte Carlo QCD simulations. The correction is found to be comparable with the Coulombic term of the static potential when applied to charmonium, and amounts to one-fourth of the Coulombic term for bottomonium.

## Key claim
The relativistic correction to the static inter-quark potential at O(1/m) is determined nonperturbatively for the first time using lattice QCD simulations; the correction is comparable to the Coulombic term of the static potential for charmonium and amounts to one-fourth of the Coulombic term for bottomonium, but the functional form of the potential at O(1/m) is not yet conclusively established and may deviate from perturbative expectations at distances beyond r/a ≈ 5.

## Method
The authors computed the electric field strength correlator on Polyakov loop correlation functions (PLCF) using SU(3) lattice gauge theory with the Wilson gauge action at β=6.0 on a 20^4 lattice (a ≈ 0.093 fm). They applied a multi-level algorithm to overcome signal-to-noise problems at intermediate distances, extracted the spectral decomposition of the correlator by fitting up to three exponentials, and obtained V^(1)(r) from the spectral representation. The lattice field strength operator was defined via plaquette variables with a Huntley-Michael factor Z_E ≈ 1.62 to remove self-energy contributions.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a ≈ 0.093 fm, determined from the Sommer scale r_0 = 0.5 fm at β = 6.0
- For charmonium (m_c = 1.3 GeV): the dimensionless coefficient 2c''/m_c = 0.26(1), which is 93(5)% of the Coulombic coefficient c = 0.281(5) of the static potential
- For bottomonium (m_b = 4.7 GeV): the dimensionless coefficient 2c''/m_b = 0.073(4), which is 26(2)% of the Coulombic coefficient c
- Fit to V^(1)(r) = -c''/r + μ'' over r/a ∈ [2,6] gave a c'' = 0.081(4) and a^2 μ'' = 0.417(1) with χ²_min/N_df = 2.3
- Fit to perturbative form V^(1)(r) = -c'/r² + μ' over r/a ∈ [2,5] gave c' = 0.099(5) and a^2 μ' = 0.401(1) with χ²_min/N_df = 6.6

## Context
This work extends the nonperturbative lattice QCD approach to heavy quark potentials beyond the static potential V^(0)(r), which was previously well parameterized by a Coulomb plus linear term (with string tension σ and Lüscher term c = π/12). The method builds on techniques developed for spin-dependent potentials at O(1/m²) by Koma et al. (2005, 2006) and uses the multi-level algorithm of Lüscher (2001). The results enable future determination of velocity-dependent potentials at O(1/m²), particularly the V_d(r) potential, and can be used in Schrödinger equation studies of heavy quarkonium spectroscopy.
