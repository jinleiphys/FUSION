# Proxy-SU(3) symmetry in heavy nuclei: Prolate dominance and prolate-oblate shape transition

> Using a new approximate analytic parameter-free proxy-SU(3) scheme, simple predictions for the global feature of prolate dominance and for the locus of the prolate-oblate shape transition have been made and compared with empirical data. Emphasis is placed on the mechanism leading to the breaking of the particle-hole symmetry, which is instrumental in shaping up these predictions. It turns out that this mechanism is based on the SU(3) symmetry and the Pauli principle alone, without reference to any specific Hamiltonian.

# Proxy-SU(3) symmetry in heavy nuclei: Prolate dominance and prolate-oblate shape transition

## Key claim

The particle-hole symmetry breaking that causes prolate dominance and defines the prolate-oblate shape transition in heavy nuclei arises solely from the SU(3) symmetry and the Pauli principle, without any reference to a specific Hamiltonian. This mechanism is demonstrated through the proxy-SU(3) scheme, which provides parameter-free predictions for these global nuclear structure features.

## Method

The authors extend the proxy-SU(3) scheme to actinides and superheavy elements by determining the highest-weight SU(3) irreducible representations for valence protons and neutrons in the 82-126 and 126-184 shells, approximated by U(21) and U(28) proxy-SU(3) shells respectively. They compare the order of appearance and highest-weight irreps for systems with different numbers of fermions in harmonic oscillator shells (U(6), U(10), U(15), U(21), U(28), U(36)) using the UNTOU3 code, and contrast these with the irreps selected by the highest eigenvalue of the second-order Casimir operator of SU(3).

## Key numbers

- For $^{260}_{100}$Fm$_{160}$: 18 valence protons (82-126 shell) give highest-weight irrep (36,6); 34 valence neutrons (126-184 shell) give highest-weight irrep (46,16); the stretched irrep for the whole nucleus is (82,22).
- In the pf shell (U(10) symmetry): for 6 particles the highest-weight irrep is (12,0); for 14 particles the highest-weight irrep is (6,6), demonstrating particle-hole asymmetry.
- In the pf shell: for 8 particles the highest-weight irrep is (10,4); for 12 particles the highest-weight irrep is (12,0), while the irrep with highest C₂ eigenvalue is (4,10).
- Percentage of unique irreps qualifying as highest-weight candidates in the pf shell: 100% for 2 particles (2 unique out of 2), 81.2% for 4 particles (9 unique out of 11), 37.5% for 6 particles (9 unique out of 24), 25% for 8 particles (8 unique out of 32).

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) scheme previously introduced by Bonatsos et al. (2017) for rare earth nuclei to actinides and superheavy elements, providing parameter-independent predictions of deformation parameters and shape transitions. The particle-hole symmetry breaking mechanism is discussed in more detail than in earlier work by the same group (Bonatsos et al., 2017), and contrasts with the traditional approach of selecting the irrep with the highest eigenvalue of the second-order Casimir operator of SU(3), which preserves particle-hole symmetry but depends on a specific Hamiltonian choice. The results enable parameter-free predictions for the prolate-oblate shape transition border across the nuclear chart, including superheavy elements where experimental data are scarce.
