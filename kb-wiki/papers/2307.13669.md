# Order parameters for gauge invariant condensation far from equilibrium

> Nuclear collisions at sufficiently high energies are expected to produce far-from-equilibrium matter with a high density of gluons at early times. We show gauge condensation, which occurs as a consequence of the large density of gluons. To identify this condensation phenomenon, we construct two local gauge-invariant observables that carry the macroscopic zero mode of the gauge condensate. The first order parameter for gauge condensation investigated here is the correlator of the spatial Polyakov loop. We also consider, for the first time, the correlator of the gauge invariant scalar field, associated to the exponent of the Polyakov loop. Using real-time lattice simulations of classical-statistical $SU(2)$ gauge theory, we find gauge condensation on a system-size dependent time scale $t_{\text{cond}} \sim L^{1/ζ}$ with a universal scaling exponent $ζ$. Furthermore, we suggest an effective theory formulation describing the dynamics using one of the order parameters identified. The formation of a condensate at early times may have intriguing implications for the early stages in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Gauge condensation occurs in far-from-equilibrium SU(2) gauge theory as a consequence of high gluon density, and can be identified using local gauge-invariant order parameters (the spatial Polyakov loop correlator and the algebra-valued scalar field correlator) rather than the non-local Wilson loop used previously. The condensation time scales as \(t_{\text{cond}} \sim L^{1/\zeta}\) with a universal scaling exponent \(\zeta\), but this is a far-from-equilibrium phenomenon that does not occur in thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on cubic lattices with sizes \(N_s = 48, 64, 96, 128\) and lattice spacing \(Q a_s = 0.5\), initialized with overoccupied gluon fields parameterized by a saturation scale \(Q\). They computed two-point connected correlation functions of the traced spatial Polyakov loop \(P\) and of the gauge-invariant scalar field \(\varphi\) (defined via \(\langle P \rangle = \langle \cos \varphi \rangle\)), and extracted scaling exponents by analyzing the volume-dependent time rescaling of these observables.

## Key numbers

- Condensation time scaling: \(t_{\text{cond}} \sim L^{1/\zeta}\) with \(\zeta = 0.36 \pm 0.04\) for the connected Polyakov loop correlator at separation \(\Delta x = L/2\), and \(\zeta = 0.37 \pm 0.03\) for the connected algebra field correlator at \(\Delta x = L/2\)
- For the connected Polyakov loop correlator at \(\Delta x = L/4\): \(\zeta = 0.31 \pm 0.09\)
- For the connected algebra field correlator at \(\Delta x = L/4\): \(\zeta = 0.34 \pm 0.03\)
- One-point function scaling exponents: \(\zeta = 0.24 \pm 0.03\) for \(\langle P \rangle\) and \(\zeta = 0.25 \pm 0.03\) for \(\langle \varphi \rangle\)
- Wilson loop scaling exponents: \(\zeta = 0.27 \pm 0.04\) at \(\Delta x = L/2\) and \(\zeta = 0.24 \pm 0.06\) at \(\Delta x = L/4\)
- Simulations used lattice spacing \(Q a_s = 0.5\) and averaged over 200 configurations

## Context

This work extends the study of gauge condensation in far-from-equilibrium Yang-Mills theory previously investigated using non-local spatial Wilson loops by Berges et al. (2019). The authors introduce local order parameters (Polyakov loop correlator and algebra-valued scalar field correlator) that capture the same condensation phenomenon, enabling future formulation of an effective kinetic theory or low-energy effective field theory for QCD. The approach connects to studies of far-from-equilibrium Bose condensation in scalar systems by Berges et al. (2012), Orioli et al. (2015), and others, and builds on the self-similar scaling regime of highly occupied plasmas characterized by Kurkela and Moore (2011), Berges et al. (2013), and Schlichting (2012).
