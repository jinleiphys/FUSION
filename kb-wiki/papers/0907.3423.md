# The Giant Monopole Resonance in Pb isotopes

> The extraction of the nuclear incompressibility from the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) measurements is analysed. Both pairing and mutually enhanced magicity (MEM) effects play a role in the shift of the GMR energy between the doubly closed shell $^{208}$Pb nucleus and other Pb isotopes. Pairing effects are microscopically predicted whereas the MEM effect is phenomenologically evaluated. Accurate measurements of the GMR in open-shell Pb isotopes are called for.

## Key claim
Pairing effects and mutually enhanced magicity (MEM) both contribute to the shift of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (GMR) energy between doubly magic 208Pb and open-shell Pb isotopes, but the MEM effect is evaluated only phenomenologically because no microscopic model yet exists to treat it for the GMR.

## Method
Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations were performed in coordinate space using Skyrme functionals (SLy4 and SkM*) with a zero-range surface pairing interaction. The GMR centroid energy was obtained from the m-1 sum rule via the derivative of the constrained monopole operator expectation value, combined with the m1 sum rule from the Thouless theorem. Pairing effects were treated microscopically, while the MEM effect was evaluated phenomenologically by using SLy4 (which describes 208Pb GMR data) for 208Pb and SkM* (which describes open-shell GMR data) for the other Pb isotopes.

## Key numbers
- The SLy4 functional has K∞ = 230 MeV and describes the 208Pb GMR data; the SkM* functional has K∞ = 215 MeV and describes open-shell GMR data such as in Tin isotopes.
- An existing measurement of the GMR in 206Pb gave a centroid of 14.0 ± 0.3 MeV, but this measurement was performed above 12 degrees where the GMR cross section is weak relative to the giant quadrupole resonance.
- A separate measurement of the 208Pb GMR centroid at RCNP gave 13.5 ± 0.2 MeV, compared to the Texas A&M value.
- Pairing effects on the GMR centroid are around 10% of the centroid energy, which is the level of accuracy of present K∞ extractions.
- The difference in K∞ between 208Pb and open-shell nuclei like Sn is about 20 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of pairing effects on the GMR from the Tin isotopic chain (Li 2008, Khan 2009) to the Pb isotopic chain, and addresses the puzzle of why 208Pb appears stiffer than Sn, Zr, Sm, and Cd nuclei. The MEM effect was previously observed in nuclear masses (Zeldes 1983, Lunney 2003) and attributed to quadrupole correlations from flat mean-field potentials in open-shell nuclei (Bender 2005). Accurate measurements of the GMR in 204Pb and 206Pb at 0 degrees are called for to test whether both pairing and MEM effects resolve the apparent softness of open-shell nuclei, and to determine whether K∞ should be extracted from isotopic chains rather than single nuclei.
