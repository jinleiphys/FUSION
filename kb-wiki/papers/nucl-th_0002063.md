# Single-Particle and Collective Motion for Proton-Rich Nuclei on the Astrophysical rp-Process Path

> Based on available experimental data, a new set of Nilsson parameters is proposed for proton-rich nuclei with proton or neutron numbers $28\leq N\leq 40$. The resulting single-particle spectra are compared with those from relativistic and non-relativistic mean field theories. Collective excitations in some even--even proton-rich nuclei in the upper $pf$ shell are investigated using the Projected Shell Model with the new Nilsson basis. It is found that the regular bands are sharply disturbed by band crossings involving $1g_{9/2}$ neutrons and protons. Physical quantities for exploring the nature of the band disturbance and the role of the $1g_{9/2}$ single-particle are predicted, which may be tested by new experiments with radioactive beams.

# Single-Particle and Collective Motion for Proton-Rich Nuclei on the Astrophysical rp-Process Path

## Key claim
A new set of Nilsson parameters for proton-rich nuclei with proton or neutron numbers 28 ≤ N ≤ 40 reproduces experimental single-particle states and collective yrast bands better than the standard Nilsson parameterization, but the improvement is conditional on the specific mass region and the new parameters have not been tested beyond the upper pf shell.

## Method
The authors adjusted the Nilsson model spin-orbit strength κ and the μ parameters separately for the N=3 and N=4 oscillator shells to match experimental single-particle states in 57Ni, 57Cu, 59Cu, and 61Cu. They then used these new Nilsson states as a basis in Projected Shell Model (PSM) calculations of yrast bands for 60Zn, 62Zn, 64Ge, and 66Ge, employing a quadrupole-quadrupole plus monopole and quadrupole pairing Hamiltonian with deformation parameters taken from Möller et al. (1995).

## Key numbers
- Neutron 1g9/2 state lies 3.7 MeV above the 2p3/2 orbital in 57Ni
- Proton 1g9/2 state estimated at 3.15 MeV above the 2p3/2 orbital from 59,61Cu data
- Deformation parameters: ε2=0.167, ε4=-0.020 for 60Zn; ε2=0.192, ε4=0.013 for 62Zn; ε2=0.200, ε4=0.047 for 64Ge; ε2=0.208, ε4=0.067 for 66Ge
- Ratio of quadrupole pairing to monopole pairing strength fixed at 0.30
- Effective charges: 0.5e for neutrons and 1.5e for protons
- B(E2; 2+ → 0+) = 0.050 e²b² and B(E2; 4+ → 2+) = 0.065 e²b² for 64Ge from Quantum Monte Carlo Diagonalization Method (QMCD) comparison

## Context
This work extends the standard Nilsson parameterization of Bengtsson and Ragnarsson (1985) to proton-rich nuclei beyond 56Ni, where the standard parameters produce spin-orbit splittings that are too large. The new parameters enable Projected Shell Model calculations for deformed nuclei (Z=36-40) on the rp-process path that are not feasible with full pf-shell diagonalizations. The results predict distinct band crossings involving 1g9/2 neutrons and protons, with g-factors jumping to near 1 for N=Z nuclei and dropping to zero for N=Z+2 nuclei, which can be tested with radioactive beam experiments.
