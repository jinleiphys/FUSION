# Target Polarization for $^2 \vec H(e,e'p)n$ at GeV energies

> We perform a fully relativistic calculation of the $^2 \vec H(e,e'p)n$ reaction in the impulse approximation employing the Gross equation to describe the deuteron ground state, and we use the SAID parametrization of the full NN scattering amplitude to describe the final state interactions (FSIs). The formalism for treating target polarization with arbitrary polarization axes is discussed, and general properties of some asymmetries are derived from it. We show results for momentum distributions and angular distributions of various asymmetries that can only be accessed with polarized targets.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully relativistic calculation of the $^2 \vec H(e,e'p)n$ reaction at GeV energies, showing that the vector target asymmetry $A^V_d$ and the tensor-beam asymmetry $A^T_{ed}$ vanish in the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and become nonzero only when final state interactions (FSIs) are included, while the tensor asymmetry $A^T_d$ and the double spin asymmetry $A^V_{ed}$ are nonzero already in PWIA.

## Method

The authors performed a fully relativistic calculation in the impulse approximation using the Gross equation for the deuteron ground state wave function and the SAID parametrization of the full NN scattering amplitude to describe final state interactions. They developed a formalism for treating target polarization with arbitrary polarization axes, rotating the density matrix from the hadron-plane coordinate system to the experimental beam-axis coordinate system. The response functions were evaluated in the $x'y'z'$ coordinate system (hadron plane) and then transformed to the $xyz$ system (electron plane) to obtain the $\phi_p$ dependence, with asymmetries calculated for both vector ($\widetilde{T}_{10}$) and tensor ($\widetilde{T}_{20}$) polarization.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy of 5.5 GeV, $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$, $x = 1$ (quasi-elastic kinematics): $A^V_d$ and $A^T_{ed}$ vanish in PWIA and become nonzero only with FSIs; the difference between on-shell FSIs and full (on-shell plus off-shell) FSIs is very small.
- Beam energy of 5.5 GeV, $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$, $x = 1.3$ (away from quasi-elastic peak): off-shell FSI effects are large for $A^V_d$ and $A^T_{ed}$, with differences apparent already at low missing momentum; for $A^V_{ed}$ and $A^T_d$, off-shell effects are less pronounced and most significant at the largest missing momenta considered.
- For angular distributions at $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$, $p_m = 0.4$ GeV, $\phi_p = 35^\circ$: the non-relativistic factorized PWIA prediction for $A^T_d$ gives zeros at $\theta = 54.7^\circ$ and $\theta = 125.3^\circ$; the $np$ scattering amplitude used is available only for $pn$ energies up to 1.3 GeV, limiting the accessible polar angle range.
- For angular distributions at $Q^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$, $p_m = 0.2$ GeV, $\phi_p = 35^\circ$: the influence of FSIs is not large; the maximum kinematically possible $x$ value is 1.3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fully relativistic calculation of the unpolarized $D(e,e'p)n$ reaction (Jeschonnek and Van Orden, 2008) by incorporating target polarization observables. It contrasts with non-relativistic approaches such as those by Arenhovel (1980s) and the Glauber theory treatment of tensor asymmetries at higher energies by Jeschonnek (2001) which used only central FSIs. The formalism enables analysis of data from Jefferson Lab Hall B polarized deuteron target experiments currently under analysis (referenced as "sebastian" in the text), and provides predictions for asymmetries that are sensitive to spin-dependent parts of the NN scattering amplitude, which can help distinguish between different reaction mechanism components at GeV energies.
