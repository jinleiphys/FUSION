# Systematics of E2 strength in the sd-shell with the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group

> Background: Recent developments in {\it ab initio} nuclear theory demonstrate promising results in medium- to heavy-mass nuclei. A particular challenge for many of the many-body methodologies, however, is an accurate treatment of the electric-quadrupole, $E2$, strength associated with collectivity. Purpose: The valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) is a particularly powerful method for accessing medium- and high-mass nuclei but has been found to underpredict $E2$ strengths. The purpose of this work is to evaluate the isospin dependence of this underprediction. Methods: We perform a systematic comparison of valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations with available literature. We make use of isoscalar and isovector contributions to the $E2$ matrix elements to assess isoscalar and isovector contributions to the missing strength. Results: It is found that the $E2$ strength is consistent throughout $T_z=\left|\frac{1}{2}\right|$, $T_z=\left|1\right|$, $T_z=\left|\frac{3}{2}\right|$ and $T_z=2$ pairs within the $sd$-shell. Furthermore, no isovector contribution to the deficiency is identified. Conclusions: A comparison with toy-models and coupled-cluster calculations is used to discuss potential origins of the missing strength, which arises from missing many-particle, many-hole excitations out of the model space. The absence of any significant isovector contribution to the missing $E2$ strength indicates that the $E2$ strength discrepancy, and therefore any correction, is largely independent of the isospin of the nuclei in question.

## Key claim

The valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) systematically underpredicts E2 strengths in sd-shell nuclei, but this deficiency is found to be isoscalar in nature with no significant isovector component, meaning the missing strength is largely independent of the isospin of the nuclei in question.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic comparison of VS-IMSRG calculations with experimental B(E2) values for mirror pairs across the sd-shell, covering Tz = |1/2|, |1|, |3/2|, and |2| nuclei. They employed the isoscalar (M0) and isovector (M1) matrix element formalism of Brown et al. (1982) to decompose the E2 strength and assess the isospin dependence of the underprediction. Calculations used the EM1.8/2.0 chiral interaction with a harmonic oscillator basis of hbar-omega=20 MeV, emax=12, and E3max=16, with all operators truncated at the normal-ordered two-body level.

## Key numbers

- VS-IMSRG calculations yield M0(IMSRG)/M0(Expt.) approximately 75% on average across sd-shell nuclei
- USDB shell-model calculations with effective charges ep=1.36 and en=0.45 yield M0(USDB)/M0(Expt.) = 1.032(26), consistent with experiment on average
- Without effective charges, USDB calculations yield M0(USDB)/M0(Expt.) approximately 55%
- For the schematic Q.Q interaction in a d5/2 nucleon above 16O, the VS-IMSRG effective charge for neutrons is 0.43 and for protons is 1.49, comparable to RPA values of 0.42 and 1.44 respectively
- For the realistic NN+3N interaction, the VS-IMSRG effective charges (en=0.26, ep=1.02) are smaller than RPA values (en=0.33, ep=1.19)
- The charge radius underprediction of approximately 7% would correspond to only 13% underprediction in E2 matrix elements, smaller than the observed deficiency

## Context

This work extends the findings of Henderson et al. (2018) and Ruotsalainen et al. (2019), who previously reported that VS-IMSRG calculations underpredict absolute B(E2) strength. The present systematic study across the full sd-shell demonstrates that the missing strength is isoscalar, contradicting a potential isovector component suggested by the earlier limited study of |Tz|=1 nuclei. The analysis uses the isoscalar/isovector matrix element formalism of Brown et al. (1982) and compares with coupled-cluster calculations and toy models to identify the origin of the deficiency as missing many-particle, many-hole excitations out of the model space, particularly 6p6h excitations from the p to sd shell. This understanding is critical for applying VS-IMSRG to neutron-rich nuclei where experimental data is absent.
