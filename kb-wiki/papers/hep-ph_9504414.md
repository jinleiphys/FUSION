# Isospin breaking corrections to nucleon form factors in the constituent quark model

> We examine isospin breaking in the nucleon wave functions due to the $u - d$ quark mass difference and the Coulomb interaction among the quarks, and their consequences on the nucleon electroweak form factors in a nonrelativistic constituent quark model. The mechanically induced isospin breaking in the nucleon wave functions and electroweak form factors are exactly evaluated in this model. We calculate the electromagnetically induced isospin admixtures by using first-order perturbation theory, including the lowest-lying resonance with nucleon quantum numbers but isospin 3/2. We find a small ($\leq 1\%$), but finite correction to the anomalous magnetic moments of the nucleon stemming almost entirely from the quark mass difference, while the static nucleon axial coupling remains uncorrected. Corrections of the same order of magnitude appear in charge, magnetic, and axial radii of the nucleon. The correction to the charge radius in this model is primarily isoscalar, and may be of some significance for the extraction of the strangeness radius from e.g. elastic forward angle parity violating electron-proton asymmetries, or elastic ${}^4He({\vec e},e')$ experiments.

## Key claim

The paper finds that isospin-breaking corrections to nucleon electroweak form factors are small (≤1%) but finite, with the correction to the anomalous magnetic moments stemming almost entirely from the quark mass difference, while the static nucleon axial coupling remains uncorrected; the correction to the charge radius is primarily isoscalar and may be significant for extracting the strangeness radius from parity-violating electron scattering experiments.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator confining interactions to calculate isospin-breaking effects from two sources: the u-d quark mass difference (mechanical corrections, evaluated exactly) and the Coulomb interaction among quarks (electromagnetic corrections, evaluated using first-order perturbation theory including the lowest-lying isospin 3/2 resonance with nucleon quantum numbers). The isospin-breaking admixtures in the nucleon wave functions were then used to compute corrections to the charge, magnetic, and axial form factors and their radii.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass difference used: Δm_q ≡ m_u - m_d = -4 MeV (from Weinberg (1977)), giving Δm_Q/m_Q ≈ -1/85
- Isospin-breaking correction to the anomalous magnetic moments: ≤ 1%
- Isospin-breaking correction to the neutron weak magnetic form factor at zero momentum transfer: approximately 0.3%
- Isospin-breaking magnetic form factor values at zero momentum transfer: ^{u+d}G_M(0)^{p+n} ≈ 1.0, ^{u-d}G_M(0)^{p-n} ≈ 5.0, ^{u-d}G_M(0)^{p+n} ≈ 0.008, ^{u+d}G_M(0)^{p-n} ≈ 0.008

## Context

This work extends the analysis of isospin breaking in nucleon structure beyond earlier studies of hadron mass differences by Isgur (1980) and others, and addresses assumption (vi) of good isospin in nucleon states that underlies standard analyses of parity-violating electron-nucleon scattering. The results are immediately relevant to the interpretation of isoscalar axial couplings measured in elastic neutrino-nucleon scattering (BNL experiment, 1992) and to future parity violation experiments, because intrinsic isospin breaking modifies observables in much the same way as nonzero strangeness content of the nucleon does, and the two effects cannot be separated by experiment.
