# The Pauli principle in the soft-photon approach to proton-proton bremstrahlung

> A relativistic and manifestly gauge-invariant soft-photon amplitude, which is consistent with the soft-photon theorem and satisfies the Pauli Principle, is derived for the proton-proton bremsstrahlung process. This soft-photon amplitude is the first two-u-two-t special amplitude to satisfy all theoretical constraints. The conventional Low amplitude can be obtained as a special case. It is demonstrated that previously proposed amplitudes for this process, both the (u,t) and (s,t) classes, violate the Pauli principle at some level. The origin of the Pauli principle violation is shown to come from two sources: (i) For the (s,t) class, the two-s-two-t amplitude transforms into the two-s-two-u amplitude under the interchange of two initial-state (or final-state) protons. (ii) For the (u,t) class, the use of an internal emission amplitude determined from the gauge-invariance constraint alone, without imposition of the Pauli principle, causes a problem. The resulting internal emission amplitude can depend upon an electromagnetic factor which is not invariant under the interchange of the two protons.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic, manifestly gauge-invariant soft-photon amplitude for proton-proton bremsstrahlung that is the first two-u-two-t special amplitude to satisfy all theoretical constraints including the Pauli principle, whereas previously proposed amplitudes of both the (u,t) and (s,t) classes violate the Pauli principle at some level.

## Method

The authors construct the external bremsstrahlung amplitude by summing photon emission from all four proton legs, using the Goldberger-Grisaru-MacDowell-Wong (GGMW) form of the proton-proton elastic scattering amplitude without Fierz transformation. They then determine the internal emission amplitude by imposing both gauge invariance and the Pauli principle, using a mean-value theorem expansion to express the internal amplitude in terms of finite differences of the elastic amplitude functions. The resulting two-u-two-t special amplitude is verified to be consistent with the soft-photon theorem and to change sign under interchange of the two initial-state or two final-state protons.

## Key numbers

- The internal amplitude M^{TuTts}_mu(I) is shown to be of order K (photon momentum), specifically proportional to [2(p_i-p_f)·K][2(q_i-p_f)·K] times second derivatives of the elastic amplitude, plus terms of order K^2
- For any one-boson-exchange model, the internal amplitude M^{TuTts}_mu(I) vanishes exactly at tree level
- The Mandelstam variables satisfy s + t + u = 4m^2, where m is the proton mass

## Context

This work extends the soft-photon approach to proton-proton bremsstrahlung originally developed by Low (1958) and later pursued by Nyman (1968) and Fearing (1980), who constructed amplitudes in the (s,t) class. It corrects and extends the two-u-two-t special amplitude prescription introduced by Liou (1993) for spinless bosons and the more realistic amplitude proposed by Liou (1995) for pp-gamma, which violated the Pauli principle at order K^1 due to an ambiguous internal amplitude. The new amplitude enables consistent treatment of identical fermions in soft-photon bremsstrahlung calculations and resolves the ambiguity in determining internal emission amplitudes from gauge invariance alone.
