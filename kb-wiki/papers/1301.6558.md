# Chiral magnetic effect and holography

> The chiral magnetic effect (CME) is a highly discussed effect in heavy-ion collisions stating that, in the presence of a magnetic field B, an electric current is generated in the background of topologically nontrivial gluon fields. We present a holographic (AdS/CFT) description of the CME in terms of a fluid-gravity model which is dual to a strongly-coupled plasma with multiple anomalous U(1) currents. In the case of two U(1) charges, one axial and one vector, the CME formally appears as a first-order transport coefficient in the vector current. We will holographically compute this coefficient at strong coupling and compare it with the hydrodynamic result. Finally, we will discuss an anisotropic variant of the model and study a possible dependence of the CME on the elliptic flow coefficient v_2.

## Key claim

The paper presents a holographic (AdS/CFT) derivation of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) transport coefficient at strong coupling, showing that the holographic result agrees with the hydrodynamic result; however, the authors note that the CME's dependence on the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) in an anisotropic fluid model is very mild, meaning that separating the CME signal from the \(v_2\)-dependent background in heavy-ion collision data remains challenging.

## Method

The authors construct a five-dimensional \(U(1)^n\) Einstein-Maxwell theory with a Chern-Simons term in an asymptotic AdS space, dual to a strongly-coupled plasma with multiple anomalous \(U(1)\) currents. They use the fluid-gravity correspondence to compute first-order corrections to the AdS black hole geometry (a Reissner-Nordström black hole in \(AdS_5\)) under perturbations by rotation and external magnetic fields, extracting the chiral magnetic and vortical conductivities from the near-boundary behavior of the bulk gauge fields via holographic renormalization. For the anisotropic case, they introduce different transverse and longitudinal pressures and compute the modified chiral magnetic conductivity to first order in the anisotropy parameter.

## Key numbers

* The chiral magnetic conductivity at strong coupling is \(\kappa_B = C \mu_5 \left( 1 - \frac{\mu \rho}{\epsilon + P} \right)\), where \(C\) is the anomaly coefficient, \(\mu_5\) the axial chemical potential, \(\mu\) the vector chemical potential, \(\rho\) the charge density, and \(\epsilon+P\) the enthalpy density.
* The chiral vortical conductivity is \(\kappa_\omega = 2 C \mu_5 \left( \mu - \frac{\rho}{\epsilon + P} \left[\mu^2 + \frac{\mu_5^2}{3}\right] \right)\).
* For an anisotropic fluid with small anisotropy \(\varepsilon_p = (P_T - P_L)/(P_T + P_L)\), the chiral magnetic conductivity becomes \(\kappa_B \approx C \mu_5 \left( 1 - \frac{\mu \rho}{\epsilon + \bar P} \left[ 1 - \frac{\varepsilon_p}{6}\right] \right)\), where \(\bar P = (2P_T + P_L)/3\) is the average pressure.
* The anisotropy parameter \(\varepsilon_p\) is related to the elliptic flow coefficient by \(\varepsilon_p \approx 2 v_2\) for pions.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic description of the chiral magnetic effect by Son and Surowka (2009) and the fluid-gravity framework of Hubeny, Minwalla, and Rangamani (2010) to the case of multiple anomalous \(U(1)\) charges, providing a holographic derivation that reproduces the hydrodynamic results at strong coupling. The anisotropic variant builds on the authors' earlier work (Kirsch and Kalaydzhyan, 2011) and addresses the ongoing experimental debate (Liao, 2012) about whether current measurements of charge-dependent correlations in heavy-ion collisions are sensitive enough to isolate the CME from the \(v_2\)-dependent background. The results enable future attempts to separate the CME signal from background effects in experimental data by exploiting the weak anisotropy dependence of the transport coefficient.
