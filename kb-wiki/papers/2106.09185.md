# Dispersion relation analysis of the radiative corrections to $g_A$ in the neutron $β$-decay

> We present the first and complete dispersion relation analysis of the inner radiative corrections to the axial coupling constant $g_A$ in the neutron $β$-decay. Using experimental inputs from the elastic form factors and the spin-dependent structure function $g_1$, we determine the contribution from the $γW$-box diagram to a precision better than $10^{-4}$. Our calculation indicates that the inner radiative corrections to the Fermi and the Gamow-Teller matrix element in the neutron $β$-decay are almost identical, i.e. the ratio $λ=g_A/g_V$ is almost unrenormalized. With this result, we predict the bare axial coupling constant to be {$\mathring{g}_A=-1.2754(13)_\mathrm{exp}(2)_\mathrm{RC}$} based on the PDG average $λ=-1.2756(13)$

## Key claim

The inner radiative corrections to the axial coupling constant \(g_A\) in neutron \(\beta\)-decay are almost identical to those for the vector coupling constant \(g_V\), so the ratio \(\lambda = g_A/g_V\) is nearly unrenormalized by these corrections, with the residual difference at the \(2 \times 10^{-4}\) level.

## Method

The authors performed a dispersion relation analysis of the \(\gamma W\)-box diagram contribution to the axial coupling constant, expressing the correction as a two-fold integral over the momentum transfer \(Q^2\) and the Bjorken variable \(x\) of the isoscalar spin-dependent structure functions \(g_1^{(0)}\) and \(g_2^{(0)}\). They used experimental data on the elastic nucleon form factors to compute the Born (elastic) contribution, and world data on the spin-dependent structure functions \(g_1^p\) and \(g_1^n\) from deep inelastic scattering experiments (including JLab EG1b data) to evaluate the inelastic contribution from \(g_1^{(0)}\), with perturbative QCD applied above a separation scale \(Q_0^2 = 2\ \text{GeV}^2\). The contribution from \(g_2^{(0)}\) was treated using the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule.

## Key numbers

*   Born (elastic) contribution to the inner radiative correction parameter \(d\): \(d_B = 1.22(1)_\text{FF}\), with the uncertainty from form factor parametrizations.
*   Predicted bare axial coupling constant: \(\mathring{g}_A = -1.2754(13)_\text{exp}(2)_\text{RC}\), based on the PDG average \(\lambda = -1.2756(13)\).
*   The precision of the \(\gamma W\)-box diagram contribution to \(g_A\) is determined to better than \(10^{-4}\).
*   The difference between the inner radiative corrections to \(g_V\) and \(g_A\) cancels in the ratio \(\lambda\) down to \(2 \times 10^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach previously applied to the vector current radiative corrections in neutron and superallowed \(\beta\)-decays (Seng 2018, Gorchtein 2018) to the axial current case. It corrects algebraic mistakes in earlier attempts by Hayen (2020, 2021) to compute the elastic contribution and replaces the model-dependent holographic QCD approach used in those works for the inelastic part with a fully data-driven analysis. The result enables a precise comparison between lattice QCD calculations of the bare axial coupling \(\mathring{g}_A\) and experimental determinations of \(\lambda\), which can be used to constrain beyond-standard-model physics such as right-handed currents.
