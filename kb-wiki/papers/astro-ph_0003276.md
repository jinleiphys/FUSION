# Recent Progress in Neutron Star Theory

> This review contains chapters discussing: Energy density fluctionals of nuclear matter, Many-body theory of nucleon matter, Hadronic and quark matter, Mixtures of phases in dense matter, Neutron star observations and predictions.

## Key claim

This review argues that while simple mean-field energy functionals (Skyrme and relativistic) are widely used to model neutron star matter, they are inadequate for extrapolating from known nuclear properties to the high-density, high-asymmetry regime of neutron stars; the authors emphasize that more sophisticated many-body approaches with realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions are necessary for reliable predictions.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for neutron star structure, starting from the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for gravitational equilibrium and the energy-density functional approach for nuclear matter. It then examines two main theoretical approaches: phenomenological energy-density functionals (Skyrme-type and relativistic mean-field Lagrangians) and many-body theory based on realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions fitted to scattering data, including modern potentials such as Argonne v18, Nijmegen I/II, Reid 93, and CD-Bonn, along with three-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 0.16 \pm 0.01\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Binding energy per nucleon at equilibrium: \(E_0 = -16 \pm 0.5\) MeV
- Symmetry energy at equilibrium: \(E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 34 \pm 6\) MeV
- Incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: \(K_0 = 240 \pm 30\) MeV
- Maximum mass of Oppenheimer-Volkoff neutron stars (pure neutron Fermi gas): \(\sim 0.7 M_\odot\)
- Observed neutron star masses in double neutron star binaries: \(1.36 \pm 0.08 M_\odot\)
- Mass of Vela X-1: \(M = 1.87^{+0.23}_{-0.17} M_\odot\)
- Mass of Cygnus X-2: \(M = 1.8 \pm 0.4 M_\odot\)
- Surface temperature of thermally radiating neutron star: \(T \simeq 6 \times 10^5\) K \(\simeq 50\) eV
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: \(f^2_{\pi NN}/4\pi = 0.075 \pm 0.002\)
- Proton charge form factor dipole parameter: \(q_0 = 840\) MeV/c

## Context

This review extends the line of work from Oppenheimer and Volkoff (1939) on neutron star structure and from Tsuruta and Cameron (1960s) on nuclear forces in neutron stars, while incorporating modern NN interaction models from the Nijmegen group (1990s), Argonne v18 (Wiringa, Stoks, Schiavilla, 1995), and CD-Bonn (Machleidt, Sammarruca, Song, 1996). It enables future work by identifying the need for improved energy functionals that can reproduce realistic many-body calculations of neutron matter, and by connecting theoretical predictions to observations from binary pulsars, X-ray pulsars, and quasi-periodic oscillations that may constrain neutron star masses up to \(\simeq 2.3 M_\odot\).
