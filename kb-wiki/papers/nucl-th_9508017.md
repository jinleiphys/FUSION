# Fusion reactions in molecules via nuclear threshold resonances

> It is widely accepted that in molecular systems the nuclear interaction plays a negligible role, because of the strong Coulomb repulsion of the nuclei at small distances. We are going to show that this is not always true. The existence of an extended nuclear resonance may lead to considerably enhanced nuclear reaction rates in appropriately prepared molecules. Especially we point out that p+p+ ^{16}O, i.e., the constituents of water, can form a ^{18}Ne(1^-) threshold resonance which decays under energy release into ^{17}F and a proton.

# Fusion reactions in molecules via nuclear threshold resonances

## Key claim

The authors argue that near-threshold nuclear resonances with long-ranged wave function tails can produce exponentially enhanced fusion probabilities in molecules, contrary to the usual assumption that nuclear interactions in molecular systems are negligible due to Coulomb repulsion. The enhancement is specifically conditional on the existence of a nuclear resonance whose energy coincides with the threshold energy of the molecular constituents, as in the case of rotationally excited water molecules (p+p+¹⁶O) forming a ¹⁸Ne(1⁻) resonance.

## Method

The authors construct a model using outgoing Coulomb s-waves for the nuclear resonance state and a product of the regular Coulomb solution with an exponentially decreasing function for the molecular wave function. They compute the overlap integral between these wave functions analytically using an integral representation of the regular Coulomb function, then evaluate the resulting integral via the saddle point method under the condition that the Sommerfeld parameter η is much larger than unity near threshold.

## Key numbers

- The ⁸B ground state proton separation energy is 0.13 MeV, requiring integrations up to 300 fm for the p+⁷Be → ⁸B+γ process
- The ⁵He(3/2⁺) resonance in muon catalyzed dtμ fusion has a resonance energy of 50 keV and width of 60–70 keV, enhancing fusion probability by at least four orders of magnitude compared to pdμ or ddμ molecules
- The ¹⁸Ne(1⁻) excited state has an experimental energy of 4.522 MeV, coinciding with the p+p+¹⁶O three-body threshold energy
- The ¹⁸Ne(1⁻) resonance decay into ¹⁷F+p releases 0.6 MeV of energy
- The ⁸Be(2⁺) resonance energy is (22.2 + i 0.8) MeV, while the d+⁶Li threshold lies at 22.2798 MeV
- The transition amplitude I scales as η^(3/2) exp[(0.614 − 1/2)π η], yielding an increasing exponential factor instead of the usual Coulomb barrier factor exp(−π η)

## Context

This work extends the mechanism known from muon catalyzed fusion in dtμ molecules, where the ⁵He(3/2⁺) near-threshold resonance (50 keV, 60–70 keV width) dramatically enhances fusion rates (Breunlich et al., 1989; Bogdanova and Markushin, 1990/1991). The authors propose that analogous enhancement can occur in normal electronic molecules, specifically identifying rotationally excited water molecules as candidates for fusion via the ¹⁸Ne(1⁻) threshold resonance. The formalism developed here enables quantitative estimates of transition probabilities and reaction rates for systems such as d+⁶Li and p+p+¹⁶O, with practical applications to be pursued in subsequent work (Belyaev, Motovilov, and Sandhas, 1995).
