# Distinguishing (Dirac or Majorana) neutrinos in purely leptonic decays of leptons

> We investigate purely leptonic decays of leptons $ЕӮ^{\prime-}\to l^-\barОҪ_lОҪ_{l^{\prime}}$ to distinguish Dirac or Majorana neutrinos. We derive the differences of the decay width (and associated quantities) between the two neutrino hypotheses by the effect of identical particles. Evidence from experimental data makes the hypothesis of Majorana neutrinos very unlikely and our results strongly favor the hypothesis of Dirac neutrinos (in the standard three-neutrino theory). Moreover, our exploration for decay widths can be extended to the polarization angles of leptons, which will inspire new information for experimental and theoretical studies.

## Key claim
The paper argues that purely leptonic decays of leptons (e.g., П„вҒ» вҶ’ ОјвҒ» ОҪМ…_Ој ОҪ_П„) can distinguish Dirac from Majorana neutrinos via the effect of identical particles, and that experimental data disfavor the Majorana hypothesis at more than 3Пғ, but this conclusion is explicitly conditional on the standard three-neutrino theory.

## Method
The authors compute the decay width for lвҖІвҒ» вҶ’ lвҒ» ОҪМ…_l ОҪ_{lвҖІ} under both Dirac and Majorana neutrino hypotheses, using the standard four-fermion interaction and the PMNS matrix. For Majorana neutrinos, the case i=j (identical ОҪМ…_i and ОҪ_i) introduces an extra interference term from Fermi-Dirac statistics, which reduces the partial width. The ratio R = О“(П„вҒ» вҶ’ ОјвҒ» ОҪМ…_Ој ОҪ_П„) / О“(П„вҒ» вҶ’ eвҒ» ОҪМ…_e ОҪ_П„) is then compared to experimental data from PDG.

## Context
This work extends the idea of using identical-particle statistics to distinguish neutrino types, analogous to the treatment of identical pions in DвҒә вҶ’ ПҖвҒәПҖвҒәПҖвҒ» decays by Cheng (2007, 2022). It contrasts with the traditional approach of neutrinoless double beta decay (Schechter 1981), which has only set increasingly stringent limits (Adams 2022). The results enable future studies of polarization angles in П„ decays, which could be tested at BESIII (Ablikim 2019).
