# Distinguishing (Dirac or Majorana) neutrinos in purely leptonic decays of leptons

> We investigate purely leptonic decays of leptons $ł^{\prime-}\to l^-\barν_lν_{l^{\prime}}$ to distinguish Dirac or Majorana neutrinos. We derive the differences of the decay width (and associated quantities) between the two neutrino hypotheses by the effect of identical particles. Evidence from experimental data makes the hypothesis of Majorana neutrinos very unlikely and our results strongly favor the hypothesis of Dirac neutrinos (in the standard three-neutrino theory). Moreover, our exploration for decay widths can be extended to the polarization angles of leptons, which will inspire new information for experimental and theoretical studies.

## Key claim
The paper argues that purely leptonic decays of leptons (e.g., τ⁻ → μ⁻ ν̅_μ ν_τ) can distinguish Dirac from Majorana neutrinos via the effect of identical particles, and that experimental data disfavor the Majorana hypothesis at more than 3σ, but this conclusion is explicitly conditional on the standard three-neutrino theory.

## Method
The authors compute the decay width for l′⁻ → l⁻ ν̅_l ν_{l′} under both Dirac and Majorana neutrino hypotheses, using the standard four-fermion interaction and the PMNS matrix. For Majorana neutrinos, the case i=j (identical ν̅_i and ν_i) introduces an extra interference term from Fermi-Dirac statistics, which reduces the partial width. The ratio R = Γ(τ⁻ → μ⁻ ν̅_μ ν_τ) / Γ(τ⁻ → e⁻ ν̅_e ν_τ) is then compared to experimental data from PDG.

## Key numbers
- For Dirac neutrinos: B(τ⁻ → μ⁻ ν̅_μ ν_τ) = (17.36 ± 0.03) × 10⁻², B(τ⁻ → e⁻ ν̅_e ν_τ) = (17.85 ± 0.03) × 10⁻², R_D = 0.9726 ± 0.0000.
- For Majorana neutrinos: B(τ⁻ → μ⁻ ν̅_μ ν_τ) = (16.53 ± 0.08) × 10⁻², B(τ⁻ → e⁻ ν̅_e ν_τ) = (17.51 ± 0.19) × 10⁻², R_M = 0.9440 ± 0.0091.
- Experimental values (PDG 2020): B(τ⁻ → μ⁻ ν̅_μ ν_τ) = (17.39 ± 0.04) × 10⁻², B(τ⁻ → e⁻ ν̅_e ν_τ) = (17.82 ± 0.04) × 10⁻², R_exp = 0.9762 ± 0.0028.
- The Majorana hypothesis deviates from experiment for R by more than 3σ; the Dirac hypothesis agrees within 1.3σ.
- Fermi constant for Majorana hypothesis: G_F^M = (1.1948 ± 0.0025) × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻², compared to G_F^D = 1.1664 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻² from PDG.

## Context
This work extends the idea of using identical-particle statistics to distinguish neutrino types, analogous to the treatment of identical pions in D⁺ → π⁺π⁺π⁻ decays by Cheng (2007, 2022). It contrasts with the traditional approach of neutrinoless double beta decay (Schechter 1981), which has only set increasingly stringent limits (Adams 2022). The results enable future studies of polarization angles in τ decays, which could be tested at BESIII (Ablikim 2019).
