# Extraction of N* information from the limited p(gamma,K+)Lambda data set

> Nucleon resonance information is often obtained from fitting hadrodynamical model calculations to data, where model parameters such as resonance coupling constants are the free parameters in the fitting procedure. For reactions with a limited data set, such as p(gamma, K+)Lambda, complications in the extraction of reliable N* information occur not only through theoretical uncertainties, but also due to technical difficulties in the fitting procedure. In this article we outline a fitting strategy based on a genetic algorithm and illustrate the kind of ambiguities which can arise.

# Extraction of N* information from the limited p(gamma,K+)Lambda data set

## Key claim

Even when a genetic algorithm fitting strategy finds multiple solutions with similarly good chi-squared values (2.45 to 2.93) that all describe the available SAPHIR data well, the extracted nucleon resonance coupling constants can differ dramatically between solutions, meaning that reliable N* parameters cannot be uniquely determined from the limited p(gamma,K+)Lambda data set alone.

## Method

The authors employed a hadrodynamical effective Lagrangian model at tree level for the p(gamma,K+)Lambda reaction, including Born terms, t-channel K*(892) and K1(1270) mesons, u-channel hyperon resonances S01(1800) and P01(1810), and s-channel nucleon resonances S11(1650), P11(1710), P13(1720), and D13(1895). They performed 50 independent minimizations using a genetic algorithm to broadly search the 22-dimensional parameter space, followed by local refinement with the Minuit package using the Davidon-Fletcher-Powell variable-metric algorithm. The fits were made to the SAPHIR data set containing 24 total cross section points, 90 differential cross section points, and 12 recoil polarization asymmetry points from threshold up to 2 GeV photon energy.

## Key numbers

- 38 out of 50 GA-plus-Minuit calculations converged with chi-squared below 3.00, with a range of 2.45 to 2.93
- The model had 22 free parameters (coupling constants and cutoff masses) to be determined from the data
- The SAPHIR data set contained 24 total cross section, 90 differential cross section, and 12 recoil polarization asymmetry points
- Subset 1 (9 solutions) had average chi-squared 2.56 with standard deviation 0.13; Subset 2 (29 solutions) had average chi-squared 2.69 with standard deviation 0.12
- The best chi-squared values were 2.45 for Subset 1 and 2.50 for Subset 2

## Context

This work extends the single-channel analysis of p(gamma,K+)Lambda by Janssen et al. (previous work) which had shown difficulties in parameterizing background diagrams, and complements coupled-channel analyses by Manley, Vrana, and Penner that aim to incorporate all possible initial and final states. The paper demonstrates that even with a sophisticated global optimization strategy, the limited data set for kaon photoproduction cannot uniquely determine resonance parameters, and it identifies specific polarization observables (photon beam asymmetry Sigma, beam-recoil asymmetry Ox, and beam-target asymmetry E) that upcoming measurements by Zegers, D'Angelo, and Klein will need to measure to resolve the ambiguities.
