# A note on causal hydrodynamics for M-theory branes

> We obtain new transport coefficients of causal hydrodynamics for the M2 and the M5-brane using a Kubo-like formula proposed by Baier, Romatschke, Son, Starinets, and Stephanov (arXiv:0712.2451 [hep-th]). The relaxation times agree with the ones obtained from the "sound mode" in our paper (arXiv:0712.2916 [hep-th]).

## Key claim

The relaxation times for causal hydrodynamics of M2 and M5-branes, obtained via a Kubo-like formula from the tensor mode, agree with those previously extracted from the sound mode, confirming that the shear mode result is unreliable due to third-order hydrodynamic corrections.

## Method

The authors compute the retarded Green's function for tensor-mode metric perturbations of Schwarzschild-AdS black holes in 4 and 7 dimensions (dual to M2 and M5-branes, respectively) by solving the minimally-coupled scalar field equation in the bulk. They extract the second-order transport coefficients τπ and κ by matching the low-frequency, low-momentum expansion of the Green's function to the Kubo-like formula proposed by Baier, Romatschke, Son, Starinets, and Stephanov (2007).

## Key numbers

* For the M5-brane (SAdS₇): τπ = [36 − (9 ln 3 + √3 π)] / (24π T), and κ = 3η / (4π T), where η = (π²/2)(2/3)⁶ N_c³ T⁵.
* For the M2-brane (SAdS₄): τπ = [18 − (9 ln 3 − √3 π)] / (24π T), with η = (π/(16√2))(4/3)³ N_c^(3/2) T².
* For the M5-brane, the ratio κ/η = 3/(4π T), which differs from the value κ/η = 1/(π T) found for the N=4 SYM plasma (SAdS₅) by Baier et al. (2007).

## Context

This work extends the causal hydrodynamics program for gauge theory plasmas using AdS/CFT, previously applied to the N=4 SYM plasma (SAdS₅) by Baier et al. (2007) and to the shear and sound modes of M2 and M5-branes by Natsuume and Okamura (2007). The tensor-mode computation fills the missing channel for SAdS₄ and SAdS₇, confirming that the shear-mode extraction of τπ is contaminated by third-order effects, as argued by Baier et al. (2007) for SAdS₅. The non-universal ratio κ/η across different theories motivates future evaluation of κ in perturbative QCD.
