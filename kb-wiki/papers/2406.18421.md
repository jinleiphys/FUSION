# Neural Network Emulation of Flow in Heavy-Ion Collisions at Intermediate Energies

> Applications of new techniques in machine learning are speeding up progress in research in various fields. In this work, we construct and evaluate a deep neural network (DNN) to be used within a Bayesian statistical framework as a faster and more reliable alternative to the Gaussian Process (GP) emulator of an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model simulator of heavy-ion reactions at intermediate beam energies. We found strong evidence of DNN being able to emulate the IBUU simulator's prediction on the strengths of protons' directed and elliptical flow very efficiently even with small training datasets and with accuracy about ten times higher than the GP. Limitations of our present work and future improvements are also discussed.

## Key claim

A deep neural network (DNN) can emulate the IBUU transport model simulator's predictions for proton directed flow slope and elliptical flow with accuracy about ten times higher than a Gaussian Process emulator, but only when used for forward emulation (predicting observables from parameters) rather than direct inversion (inferring parameters from observables), and the DNN does not provide variance estimates for its predictions.

## Method

The authors constructed DNNs using both Scikit-Learn and TensorFlow/Keras packages, each with four hidden layers using hyperbolic tangent activation functions, trained on 89 sets of IBUU simulation results for mid-central Au+Au collisions at 1.23 GeV/nucleon beam energy. The DNNs were trained to emulate the IBUU simulator's predictions of the proton directed flow slope F1 and elliptical flow v2 from the two input parameters (incompressibility K and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section modification factor X), using 75% of the data for training and 25% for validation with mean squared error as the loss function.

## Key numbers

- DNN total MSE for emulating F1 and v2: 1.19e-4 (Scikit-Learn) and 2.17e-4 (TensorFlow), compared to GP total MSE of 9.20e-4 from the same training dataset
- Largest DNN error for F1 prediction: 2.64e-2 (Scikit-Learn) versus 8.22e-2 (TensorFlow)
- Largest DNN error for v2 prediction: 6.30e-2 (Scikit-Learn) versus 1.31e-1 (TensorFlow)
- Scikit-Learn DNN training time: 5.44e-2 seconds; prediction time: 3.86e-6 seconds per prediction
- TensorFlow DNN training time: 3.87 seconds; prediction time: 2.29e-3 seconds per prediction
- Linear regression R2 values: F1 vs X and K gave R2=0.927; v2 vs X and K gave R2=0.959; inversion to X from F1 and v2 gave R2=0.782; inversion to K gave R2=0.587

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian+GP framework of Li and Xie (2023) who used a Gaussian Process emulator trained on 89 IBUU simulation parameter sets to infer the incompressibility K and in-medium cross section modification factor X from HADES Collaboration measurements of proton flow in Au+Au collisions at 1.23 GeV/nucleon. The DNN approach achieves approximately tenfold improvement in emulation accuracy over the GP while requiring comparable or smaller training datasets, enabling more efficient Bayesian inference of nuclear equation of state parameters. Future work should address the DNN's inability to provide prediction variances and explore its integration into a Bayesian+DNN framework for solving inverse problems in heavy-ion collisions.
