# Fine Structure in the Decay of Deformed Proton Emitters: Non-adiabatic Approach

> The coupled-channel Schrodinger equation with outgoing wave boundary conditions is employed to study the fine structure seen in the proton decay of deformed even-N, odd-Z rare earth nuclei 131-Eu and 141-Ho. Experimental lifetimes and proton-decay branching ratios are reproduced. The comparison with the standard adiabatic theory is made.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a non-adiabatic coupled-channel formalism, which accounts for the finite moment of inertia of the daughter nucleus and includes Coriolis coupling, can reproduce experimental lifetimes and proton-decay branching ratios for deformed proton emitters, whereas the standard adiabatic (strong-coupling) approximation systematically underestimates branching ratios by factors of 2-3.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation with outgoing wave boundary conditions for the proton-daughter system, using a Woods-Saxon potential with Chepurnov parameterization and an axially deformed average potential. The cluster radial functions are expanded in channels labeled by daughter spin, proton orbital angular momentum, and total proton spin, and the equations are solved in the complex energy plane using extended precision arithmetic with a piece-wise perturbation method. The daughter nucleus is treated as a rotor with finite moment of inertia, and the depth of the Woods-Saxon potential is adjusted to reproduce the experimental Q-value for each decay.

## Key numbers

- For 131Eu ground state assigned as [411]3/2: calculated half-life 34.0 ms, branching ratio to 2+ state 39% (non-adiabatic) vs 37% (adiabatic); experimental values: half-life 17.8(19) ms, branching ratio 24(5)%
- For 131Eu [413]5/2 orbital: calculated half-life 184 ms, branching ratio 7% (non-adiabatic) vs 2% (adiabatic)
- For 131Eu [532]5/2 orbital: calculated half-life 3.90 s, branching ratio 52% (non-adiabatic) vs 38% (adiabatic)
- For 141Ho ground state assigned as [523]7/2: calculated half-life 19.1 ms, branching ratio 6% (non-adiabatic) vs 3% (adiabatic); experimental half-life 3.9(5) ms
- For 141mHo isomeric state assigned as [411]1/2: calculated half-life 3.3 microseconds, branching ratio 1% (both non-adiabatic and adiabatic); experimental half-life 8(3) microseconds
- Energy of 2+ state in 130Sm assumed as 120 keV; in 140Dy assumed as 160 keV
- Deformation parameters: beta2=0.32 for 131Eu; beta2=0.29, beta4=-0.06 for 141Ho

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of deformed proton emitters beyond the strong-coupling (adiabatic) approximation used in prior calculations by Davids (1998), Sonzogni (1999), Bugrov (1989), and the Gamow-state approaches of Rykaczewski (1999), Ferreira (1997), Maglione (1998, 1999). The non-adiabatic formalism automatically includes Coriolis coupling, which was neglected in all previous strong-coupling calculations, and enables the study of fine structure in proton decay to excited rotational states of the daughter nucleus. The results enable future experimental searches for fine structure in 141Ho and provide a framework for interpreting proton decay from rotational bands in deformed parent nuclei.
