# Valence quark distribution of the pion inside a medium with finite baryon density: A Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model approach

> We calculate the in-medium valence quark distribution of the pion immersed in a finite baryon density using the light-cone quark model. The medium-modified pion properties are obtained by using the constituent quark mass-dependent light cone wave functions. To obtain the constituent quark masses at finite baryon density, we employ the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. We primarily focus on the in-medium electromagnetic form factor, distribution amplitude, and the parton distribution function of the pion. The parton distribution functions are also evolved from the model scale to a perturbative scale using next to leading order Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi evolution equations. Furthermore, our calculated form factors are compared with available experimental measurements and lattice quantum chromodynamics studies. We also examine the Mellin moments derived from our parton distribution functions in comparison with existing extractions and theoretical model predictions.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the in-medium valence quark distribution of the pion at finite baryon density using a hybrid light-cone quark model (LCQM) combined with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, finding that the pion's distribution amplitude flattens and its decay constant decreases monotonically with increasing density, but the results depend on the assumption that the harmonic scale parameter beta remains unchanged in the nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors first solve the two-flavor NJL model gap equations for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature to obtain the density-dependent constituent quark mass, using proper-time regularization with infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs. They then input these in-medium quark masses into the light-cone wave functions of the pion's valence Fock state, using the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage prescription for the momentum-space wave function and Melosh-Wigner rotation for the spin part. The parton distribution functions are evolved from the model scale (0.20 GeV^2) to a perturbative scale (25 GeV^2) using next-to-leading order DGLAP evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum constituent quark mass: m*(rho_B=0) = 0.4 GeV
- Harmonic scale parameter: beta = 0.2775 GeV (fixed from vacuum pion decay constant)
- NJL model parameters: G_pi = 19.60 GeV^{-2}, G_omega = 7.24 GeV^{-2}, G_s = 9.96 GeV^{-2}, Lambda_UV = 0.6385 GeV, Lambda_IR = 0.200 GeV, bare quark mass m = 0.01693 GeV
- Vacuum pion mass reproduced: m_pi = 140 MeV; vacuum pion decay constant (NJL convention): f_pi' = 93 MeV
- At nuclear saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}), the in-medium pion decay constant is approximately 95% of the vacuum value
- Vacuum inverse moment <x^{-1}> = 2.385, increasing to 2.496 at rho_B/rho_0 = 1, 2.619 at rho_B/rho_0 = 2, 2.713 at rho_B/rho_0 = 3, and 2.778 at rho_B/rho_0 = 4
- <xi^2> moment increases from 0.1275 (vacuum) to 0.1910 (rho_B/rho_0 = 4) at model scale

## Context

This work extends the hybrid LCQM approach previously applied by Puhan et al. (2025) and Kaur et al. (2024) using the chiral SU(3) quark mean-field model, now replacing it with the two-flavor NJL model for symmetric nuclear matter as developed by Bentz and Thomas (2001). The results are compared with in-medium pion studies using Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes by de Melo et al. (2014, 2016), Gaussian wave functions in the quark-meson coupling model by Arifi et al. (2023, 2024), and the NJL-Bethe-Salpeter equation framework by Hutauruk et al. (2021) and Gifari et al. (2024). The approach enables future studies of in-medium modifications of pion properties relevant for understanding heavy-ion collision dynamics and the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in dense baryonic matter.
