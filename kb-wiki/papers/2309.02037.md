# Nuclear charge radii of silicon isotopes

> The nuclear charge radius of $^{32}$Si was determined using collinear laser spectroscopy. The experimental result was confronted with ab initio nuclear lattice effective field theory, valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group, and mean field calculations, highlighting important achievements and challenges of modern many-body methods. The charge radius of $^{32}$Si completes the radii of the mirror pair $^{32}$Ar - $^{32}$Si, whose difference was correlated to the slope $L$ of the symmetry energy in the nuclear equation of state. Our result suggests $L \leq 60$\,MeV, which agrees with complementary observables.

## Key claim

The measured charge radius of 32Si, combined with the known radius of its mirror partner 32Ar, yields a mirror charge-radius difference that constrains the slope L of the symmetry energy to L ≤ 60 MeV, but this constraint depends on the specific density-functional framework used and the treatment of the spin-orbit potential.

## Method

Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed on 28,29,30,32Si at the BECOLA setup at FRIB, using a continuous ion beam neutralized by charge exchange with Na vapor. The 391-nm transition from the metastable 3s2 3p2 1S0 state to the 3s2 3p 4s 1P1o state was excited, and fluorescence at 288 nm was detected background-free. The laser beam was chopped with an AOM to avoid optical depopulation of the metastable state along the 70-cm flight path. Atomic field-shift and mass-shift constants were determined from a King fit constrained by Fock-Space coupled cluster calculations (method A) and independently validated by a second set of atomic calculations (method B) using FS-CCSDT and CI+MBPT approaches.

## Key numbers

* Charge radius of 32Si: Rch = 3.153(12) fm, extracted from the measured isotope shift δν32,28 = -1505.3(3.1)(2.1) MHz relative to 28Si.
* Differential mean square charge radius of 32Si relative to 28Si: δ⟨r2⟩32,28 = 0.195(76) fm2.
* Mirror charge-radius difference for the 32Ar-32Si pair: ΔRch = Rch(32Si) - Rch(32Ar) = 0.194(14) fm.
* Mass-shift constant from constrained King fit: K(MS) = -340.8(1.4) GHz u; field-shift constant: F(el) = 97.0(8) MHz/fm2.
* Normal and specific mass shift constants from method B: 418(17) GHz u and -791(17) GHz u, respectively.
* Magnetic hyperfine parameter for 29Si: Aupper = -252.5(6) MHz.

## Context

This work extends the mirror-charge-radius method previously applied to the A=36 pair 36Ca-36S (Brown 2020) and the A=54 pair 54Ni-54Fe (Pineda 2021) to the A=32 mirror pair 32Ar-32Si, which has a larger isospin asymmetry of |N-Z|=4. The experimental results are compared with three complementary many-body methods: nuclear lattice effective field theory (Elhatisari 2022), valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations using EM1.8_2.0 and ΔN2LOGO interactions, and density functional theory (DFT) with NL3* and SVmin functionals. The lattice calculations and SVmin DFT show good agreement with experiment, while VS-IMSRG with EM1.8_2.0 deviates from the experimental trend, highlighting ongoing challenges in ab initio descriptions of nuclear radii. The L ≤ 60 MeV constraint from the mirror pair agrees with complementary observables and provides input for the nuclear equation of state relevant to neutron star properties.
