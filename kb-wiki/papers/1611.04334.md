# Charming quasi-exotic open-flavor mesons

> We discuss charmed mesons in the covariant Dyson-Schwinger-Bethe-Salpeter-equation approach. In particular we computed masses, leptonic decay constants, and an orbital-angular-momentum decomposition for a basic set of states. We also report an efficient way to treat the two coupled quark propagator dressing functions via a single function.

## Key claim

The authors argue that open-flavor mesons with one charm quark can realize "quasi-exotic" quantum numbers (states absent in the quark model) within a Poincaré-covariant Dyson-Schwinger–Bethe-Salpeter approach, even though such states are not exotic in the sense of having more than two constituents. The claim is hedged by the assumption that these open-flavor states are absent from the quark model both conceptually and in any solution set, and the analysis is limited to states below the "pole threshold" where the quark propagator's complex poles do not spoil the numerical solution.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for the dressed quark propagator and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for meson bound states, both in rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation. They used a well-known effective interaction with parameters ω = 0.5 GeV and D = 1/ω⁴ GeV⁻², and adjusted the charm quark mass in two ways: to reproduce the experimental D-meson mass and to reproduce the experimental J/ψ mass. The full hyperpolar angle integration was performed to preserve Poincaré covariance, and the momentum partitioning parameter was exploited to maximize the pole threshold. An orbital-angular-momentum decomposition (OAMD) of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes was computed by projecting onto eigenstates of the Pauli-Lubanski operator.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters: ω = 0.5 GeV, D = 1/ω⁴ GeV⁻².
- Results are presented for four flavor combinations: \(\bar q c\), \(\bar s c\), \(\bar c b\), and \(\bar c c\) (Fig. 1), with meson masses (GeV, blue axis) and leptonic decay constants (GeV, red axis) for ground states, first excitations, and second excitations below the pole threshold.
- For the pseudoscalar D_s meson, the OAMD analysis (Fig. 2) shows that the ground state is a predominantly mixed S–P-wave configuration with negligible pure S- and P-wave contributions, while the first and second excitations (the first being quasi-exotic) are predominantly S-wave states.

## Context

This work extends the study of Hilger (2016) to a wider range of quark masses, applying the Dyson-Schwinger–Bethe-Salpeter approach in rainbow-ladder truncation to charmed open-flavor mesons. It builds on prior demonstrations (Hilger, 2009; Hilger, 2010; Buchheim, 2014–2015) that open-flavor mesons are sensitive probes of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) due to the enhanced chiral condensate contribution. The results enable further investigation of quasi-exotic states and their OAMD, though the authors note that the unusual ground-state OAMD for the D_s is not robust and requires further study.
