# Kaon Condensation, Black Holes and Cosmological Natural Selection

> It is argued that a well measured double neutron star binary in which the two neutron stars are more than 4% different from each other in mass or a massive neutron star with mass M > 2 M_sun would put in serious doubt or simply falsify the following chain of predictions: (1) nearly vanishing vector meson mass at chiral restoration, (2) kaon condensation at a density n ~ 3 n_0, (3) the Brown-Bethe maximum neutron star mass M_max ~ 1.5 M_sun and (4) Smolin's `Cosmological Natural Selection' hypothesis.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a confirmed observation of a neutron star with mass greater than about 2 solar masses, or a well-measured double neutron star binary where the two masses differ by more than 4%, would falsify a linked chain of predictions: nearly vanishing vector meson mass at chiral restoration, kaon condensation at density near 3 times nuclear saturation density, the Brown-Bethe maximum neutron star mass of about 1.5 solar masses, and Smolin's Cosmological Natural Selection hypothesis. The hedge is that these predictions are falsifiable by future observations, and the authors note that no "smoking-gun" evidence currently exists against their scenario.

## Method

The authors connect effective field theory predictions for dense matter, specifically hidden local symmetry (HLS) theory with vector manifestation (VM) of chiral symmetry, to the equation of state (EOS) of compact-star matter via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation. They then link the resulting maximum neutron star mass to stellar evolution calculations of carbon abundance and iron core mass, and finally to Smolin's cosmological natural selection scenario which requires maximized black hole formation. The argument is theoretical and synthetic, drawing on prior calculations of kaon condensation, the Brown-Bethe scenario for black hole formation, and the entropy argument for black hole maximization.

## Key numbers

- Kaon condensation predicted at density \(n \sim 3 n_0\), where \(n_0\) is ordinary nuclear matter density.
- Brown-Bethe maximum neutron star mass: \(M^{BB}_{max} \approx 1.5 M_\odot\).
- A neutron star with mass \(M \gsim 2 M_\odot\) would falsify the vector manifestation of HLS theory and kaon condensation at \(\sim 3 n_0\).
- A double neutron star binary with masses differing by more than 4% would falsify the Brown-Bethe theory.
- The Hulse-Taylor pulsar has mass \(1.44 M_\odot\).
- The Schwarzschild black hole entropy formula: \(S_H = 1.05 \times 10^{77} k_B (M/M_\odot)^2\).
- The minimum ZAMS mass for black hole production is \(\sim 18 M_\odot\), based on carbon abundance dropping below 15%.
- The reconstructed upper limit on gravitational mass of the SN1987A progenitor was \(1.56 M_\odot\).
- The \(^{12}\)C(\(\alpha,\gamma\))\(^{16}\)O reaction rate is \(165 \pm 50\) keV barns (Kunz et al. 2001).
- PSRJ0751+1807 was initially reported as \(2.1^{+0.20}_{-0.20} M_\odot\) but later revised to \(1.26^{+0.14}_{-0.12} M_\odot\).

## Context

This paper extends the Brown-Bethe scenario (Brown and Bethe, 1994) for black hole formation via kaon condensation, connecting it to Smolin's cosmological natural selection hypothesis (Smolin, 1997). It contrasts with the anthropic principle with eternal inflation and with models that allow color-superconducting quark stars at high density. The work enables future falsification tests via neutron star mass measurements in binaries, gravitational wave signals from crystalline color-superconducting phases (which would require a stiff EOS and rule out kaon condensation below \(\gsim 7 n_0\)), and LIGO searches for solid structures in neutron star cores.
