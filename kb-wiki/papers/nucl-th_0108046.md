# Pair Creation and an X-ray Free Electron Laser

> Using a quantum kinetic equation coupled to Maxwell's equation we study the possibility that focused beams at proposed X-ray free electron laser facilities can generate electric field strengths large enough to cause spontaneous electron-positron pair production from the QED vacuum. Our approach yields the time and momentum dependence of the single particle distribution function. Under conditions reckoned achievable at planned facilities, repeated cycles of particle creation and annihilation take place in tune with the laser frequency. However, the peak particle number density is insensitive to this frequency and one can anticipate the production of a few hundred particle pairs per laser period. Field-current feedback and quantum statistical effects are small and can be neglected in this application of non-equilibrium quantum mean field theory.

## Key claim

Using a quantum kinetic equation coupled to Maxwell's equation, the authors predict that under conditions achievable at proposed X-ray free electron laser (XFEL) facilities, repeated cycles of electron-positron pair creation and annihilation will occur in tune with the laser frequency, but the peak particle number density is insensitive to this frequency. The authors explicitly state that their idealized experiment (assuming a volume where the electric field is nonzero but the magnetic field vanishes) is impossible to achieve in practice, meaning their estimate of the production rate is an upper bound.

## Method

The authors solved a quantum Vlasov equation (a non-Markovian kinetic equation for the single-particle momentum distribution function) self-consistently coupled to Maxwell's equation for the internal electric field, using a 4th order Runge-Kutta method. They modeled an alternating laser field as a spatially homogeneous, time-dependent electric field with no magnetic component, representing an idealized standing wave formed by focusing two coherent X-ray laser beams. The calculation was performed for two field strengths: a weak field of 0.1 times the critical Schwinger field (XFEL-like) and a strong field equal to the critical field.

## Key numbers

- Critical Schwinger field: \(E_{cr} = 1.3 \times 10^{18}\) V/m.
- For XFEL-like fields (Set Ia: \(\lambda = 0.15\) nm, \(E_0 = 1.3 \times 10^{17}\) V/m): peak number density \(n_{max}(t_>) = 4.6 \times 10^{-13}\) fm\(^{-3}\), yielding approximately \(10^3\) particle pairs per laser period in the spot volume.
- For XFEL-like fields (Set Ib: \(\lambda = 0.075\) nm, \(E_0 = 1.3 \times 10^{17}\) V/m): peak number density \(n_{max}(t_>) = 4.6 \times 10^{-13}\) fm\(^{-3}\), yielding approximately \(10^2\) particle pairs per laser period.
- For strong fields (Set IIa: \(\lambda = 0.15\) nm, \(E_0 = 1.3 \times 10^{18}\) V/m): peak number density \(n_{max}(t_>) = 7.2 \times 10^{-10}\) fm\(^{-3}\), yielding approximately \(10^6\) particle pairs.
- For strong fields (Set IIb: \(\lambda = 0.075\) nm, \(E_0 = 1.3 \times 10^{18}\) V/m): peak number density \(n_{max}(t_>) = 6.4 \times 10^{-10}\) fm\(^{-3}\), yielding approximately \(10^5\) particle pairs.
- Laser frequencies: \(\Omega_{\rm Ia} = 2.0 \times 10^{18} \times 2\pi\) s\(^{-1}\); \(\Omega_{\rm Ib} = 4.0 \times 10^{18} \times 2\pi\) s\(^{-1}\).
- Estimated plasma oscillation period (ultrarelativistic formula): \(\tau_{\rm pl}^{UR} \approx 6.5 \times 10^{-19}\) s, compared to laser periods of \(2.5 \times 10^{-19}\) s and \(5.0 \times 10^{-19}\) s.
- For XFEL-like fields, the induced current is a factor of \(10^3\) smaller than the external current.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Schwinger pair production (Sauter, 1931) from static fields to time-dependent alternating fields relevant to planned XFEL facilities at SLAC and DESY. It builds on prior kinetic theory approaches to pair production in strong fields (Bastianelli, 1995; KME, 1998) and on earlier estimates of pair production by laser beams (Brezin, 1970; Popov, 1972; Chen, 1999; Ringwald, 2001). The authors demonstrate that for XFEL-like fields, the low-density approximation to the distribution function is accurate, non-Markovian memory effects are negligible, and field-current feedback can be ignored, enabling future experimental searches for this QED vacuum decay phenomenon.
