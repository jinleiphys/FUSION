# Low mass dimuons within a hybrid approach

> We analyse dilepton emission from hot and dense hadronic matter using a hybrid approach based on the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model with an intermediate hydrodynamic stage for the description of heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies. Focusing on the enhancement with respect to the contribution from long-lived hadron decays after freeze-out observed at the SPS in the low mass region of the dilepton spectra (often referred to as "the excess"), the relative importance of the emission from the equilibrium and the non-equilibrium stages is discussed.

## Key claim

The hybrid model combining UrQMD transport with an intermediate hydrodynamic stage shows that non-thermal cascade emission dominates the dimuon invariant mass region around the vector meson peak at both low and intermediate transverse pair momenta, while thermal radiation with in-medium spectral function fills the very low invariant mass region (M < 0.5 GeV) only at low pT; however, the sum of both contributions overestimates the vector meson peak region at low transverse momenta, indicating that the specific spectral function used or neglected residual in-medium modifications during the cascade stage may be responsible.

## Method

The authors simulate In+In collisions at 158 A GeV using a hybrid approach that couples the UrQMD hadronic cascade to a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic evolution (SHASTA algorithm), with a gradual transition from hydrodynamics back to cascade when the energy density drops below five times the ground state energy density. During the hydrodynamic stage, thermal dilepton emission is computed using in-medium ρ meson spectral functions with self-energies from interactions with pions and nucleons (based on empirical scattering amplitudes), while during the cascade stages dimuon emission from ρ mesons is calculated via the time integration method.

## Key numbers

- In-In collisions at 158 A GeV (SPS energy) are simulated
- Initial temperatures in central cells reach up to T ∼ 240 MeV during the hydrodynamic phase
- The low invariant mass region studied is M < 1 GeV, with very low masses M < 0.5 GeV filled by thermal radiation at low pT
- For pT > 1 GeV, thermal emission had previously shown discrepancies, motivating the hybrid approach
- Calculations with a quark-gluon plasma equation of state suggest a reduction of hadronic emission by about 30%–40% across the entire dimuon invariant mass region considered

## Context

This work extends previous thermal fireball and hydrodynamic calculations that successfully described NA60 data (Arnaldi 2008) by incorporating both equilibrium and non-equilibrium stages of the collision evolution. The hybrid approach builds on the UrQMD transport model with hydrodynamic coupling developed by Petersen (2008) and earlier applications to electromagnetic probes by Bäuchle (2009) and Santini (2009). The results enable future discrimination between in-medium ρ meson broadening and mass dropping scenarios, and extension to the intermediate mass region (1.0 < M < 1.5 GeV) is under development.
