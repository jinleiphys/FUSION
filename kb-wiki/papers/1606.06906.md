# Limiting Fragmentation in a Thermal Model with Flow

> The property of limiting fragmentation of various observables such as rapidity distributions ($dN/dy$), elliptic flow ($v_{2}$), average transverse momentum ($\langle p_{T} \rangle$) etc. of charged particles is observed when they are plotted as a function of rapidity ($y$) shifted by the beam rapidity ($y_{beam}$) for a wide range of energies from AGS to RHIC. Limiting fragmentation (LF) is a well studied phenomenon as observed in various collision energies and colliding systems experimentally. It is very interesting to verify this phenomenon theoretically. We study such a phenomenon for pion rapidity spectra using our hydrodynamic-like model where the collective flow is incorporated in a thermal model in the longitudinal direction. Our findings advocate the observation of extended longitudinal scaling in the rapidity spectra of pions from AGS to lower RHIC energies, while it is observed to be violated at top RHIC and LHC energies. Prediction of LF hypothesis for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=5.02 TeV is given.

# Limiting Fragmentation in a Thermal Model with Flow

## Key claim

The authors find that extended longitudinal scaling (limiting fragmentation) in pion rapidity spectra holds from AGS energies up to below top RHIC energy, but is violated at top RHIC (200 GeV) and LHC energies when modeled using a thermal model with longitudinal flow. The violation at higher energies is attributed to the increasing longitudinal flow velocity, which approaches the speed of light and breaks the scaling behavior.

## Method

The authors use an excluded-volume statistical hadron gas model with a hard-core radius of 0.8 fm for baryons (mesons treated as point-like) and incorporate collective flow in the longitudinal direction by integrating the stationary thermal rapidity distribution over a boost angle interval up to a maximum value η_max. The chemical freeze-out parameters (temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B) are extracted using the model's freeze-out criteria, and the longitudinal flow velocity is obtained from β_L = tanh(η_max/2). Pion rapidity distributions are calculated over a center-of-mass energy range from 8.7 GeV to 5.02 TeV and plotted as a function of shifted rapidity y - y_beam to test limiting fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 163.5 MeV and baryon chemical potential μ_B = 1.525 MeV at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Longitudinal flow velocity β_L = 0.993c at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (η_max = 5.8)
- Longitudinal flow velocity β_L = 0.996c at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (η_max = 6.5)
- Hard-core radius r = 0.8 fm used for baryon excluded-volume correction
- Energy range studied: from 8.7 GeV to 5.02 TeV for π+ rapidity distributions

## Context

This work extends the statistical thermal model approach of Cleymans et al. (2008), who studied extended longitudinal scaling for pion rapidity distributions using a Gaussian fireball model and found consistency up to highest RHIC energies with violation at LHC. The authors' model incorporates longitudinal flow, which was absent in the earlier thermal model, making the formalism more realistic. The results are compared with AMPT model calculations with string melting (Ma 2016) and with experimental data from the ALICE collaboration at LHC. The work enables future experimental verification of limiting fragmentation at LHC energies using forward rapidity pion data, which would help fine-tune theoretical models of particle production in heavy-ion collisions.
