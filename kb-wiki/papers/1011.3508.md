# Entropy production and equilibration in Yang-Mills quantum mechanics

> The Husimi distribution provides for a coarse grained representation of the phase space distribution of a quantum system, which may be used to track the growth of entropy of the system. We present a general and systematic method of solving the Husimi equation of motion for an isolated quantum system, and we construct a coarse grained Hamiltonian whose expectation value is exactly conserved. As an application, we numerically solve the Husimi equation of motion for two-dimensional Yang-Mills quantum mechanics (the x-y model) and calculate the time evolution of the coarse grained entropy of a highly excited state. We show that the coarse grained entropy saturates to a value that coincides with the microcanonical entropy corresponding to the energy of the system.

## Key claim

The coarse-grained (Wehrl-Husimi) entropy of a highly excited state in two-dimensional Yang-Mills quantum mechanics saturates to a value that coincides with the microcanonical entropy corresponding to the energy of the system, but only after a characteristic time scale after which the complexity of the system no longer increases.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Husimi equation of motion for the two-dimensional Yang-Mills quantum mechanics (the x-y model) using a test-particle method that respects the superposition principle. They represented the time-dependent Husimi distribution as a superposition of Gaussian test functions, derived equations of motion for the test-particle centers and widths from the moments of the Husimi equation, and integrated these equations using the Runge-Kutta method. They also constructed a conserved coarse-grained Hamiltonian for the Husimi representation by analytic continuation of the Fourier transform between the Wigner and Husimi distributions.

## Key numbers

- The final entropy per unit rapidity produced in central Au+Au collisions at the top RHIC energy of 200 GeV per nucleon pair in the center-of-mass frame is 5600 ± 500 at mid-rapidity (cited from Müller (2005)).
- The thermalization time for the initial phase of nuclear collisions is about 1.5 fm/c or less (cited from Fries (2009) and Kunihiro (2010)).
- The Wehrl-Husimi entropy for a two-dimensional system satisfies the bound S_H(t) ≥ 2, where equality holds for a minimum-uncertainty Husimi distribution (generalized from Wehrl's conjecture proved by Lieb (1978)).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Kunihiro (2008) for entropy production in isolated quantum systems using the Husimi representation, applying it to a specific non-integrable quantum system with well-known chaotic properties studied by Matinyan (1981) and Biro (1994). The results enable quantitative study of entropy production and thermalization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and inflationary cosmology, where the formalism can describe the transition from a color-glass condensate to a thermally equilibrated quark-gluon plasma, as discussed by McLerran (2005) and Müller (2006).
