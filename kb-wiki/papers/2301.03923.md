# $ΛNN$ content of $Λ$-nucleus potential

> A minimally constructed $Λ$-nucleus density-dependent optical potential is used to calculate binding energies of observed $1s_Λ$, $1p_Λ$ states across the periodic table, leading to a repulsive $ΛNN$ contribution $D_Λ^{(3)}\approx 14$ MeV to the phenomenological $Λ$-nucleus potential depth $D_Λ\approx -30$ MeV. This value is significant in connection with the so-called 'hyperon puzzle'.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a repulsive three-body ΛNN contribution to the Λ-nucleus potential depth is approximately 14 MeV, but this value is conditional on including Pauli correlations in the two-body term and using a modified density ansatz that discards the bilinear core-excess nucleon term in the three-body potential.

## Method
The authors constructed a density-dependent optical potential for Λ-nucleus interactions, parameterized by a two-body ΛN term proportional to ρ and a three-body ΛNN term proportional to ρ². They fitted the two strength parameters b₀ and B₀ to the experimental 1sΛ and 1pΛ binding energies of ¹⁶ΛN, then used these parameters to calculate binding energies for eight other hypernuclei across the periodic table from ¹²ΛB to ²⁰⁸ΛPb. Nuclear densities were constrained by experimental charge radii, and Pauli correlations were included via a density-dependent factor C_Pauli(ρ) in the two-body term.

## Key numbers
- DΛ⁽³⁾ = 13.9 ± 1.4 MeV (repulsive three-body ΛNN contribution to the Λ-nucleus potential depth at nuclear-matter density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³)
- DΛ⁽²⁾ = -40.6 ± 1.0 MeV (attractive two-body ΛN contribution)
- DΛ = -26.7 ± 1.7 MeV (total Λ-nucleus potential depth)
- b₀ = 1.85 fm (fitted strength parameter for the two-body term, agreeing with the Λp scattering length of 1.7 ± 0.1 fm)
- B₀ = 0.170 fm (fitted strength parameter for the three-body term)
- The 1sΛ binding energy in ¹⁶ΛN is 13.76 ± 0.16 MeV; the 1pΛ binding energy is 2.84 ± 0.18 MeV

## Context
This work extends the optical potential approach of Dover-Hüfner-Lemmer (1971) for pions to the Λ-nucleus system, and updates earlier density-dependent potential studies by Millener, Dover, and Gal (1988) that found a larger DΛ⁽³⁾ ≈ 30 MeV. The derived DΛ⁽³⁾ value is considerably smaller than the 53 MeV inferred from Quantum Monte Carlo calculations by Lonardoni et al. (2013, 2014), a discrepancy explained by the QMC use of overly compact nuclear densities. The result suggests that the repulsive three-body ΛNN interaction is strong enough to prevent Λ hyperons from appearing in neutron-star matter, supporting a stiff equation of state capable of sustaining two-solar-mass neutron stars, which addresses the hyperon puzzle.
