# Testing the Inverted Neutrino Mass Ordering with Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay

> We quantify the extent to which future experiments will test the existence of neutrinoless double-beta decay mediated by light neutrinos with inverted-ordered masses. While it remains difficult to compare measurements performed with different isotopes, we find that future searches will fully test the inverted ordering scenario, as a global, multi-isotope endeavor. They will also test other possible mechanisms driving the decay, including a large uncharted region of the allowed parameter space assuming that neutrino masses follow the normal ordering.

## Key claim

Future neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments, considered as a global multi-isotope endeavor, will fully test the inverted neutrino mass ordering scenario, but the authors advocate refraining from quantitatively ranking experiments' reach across different isotopes due to the lack of objective criteria and unknown nuclear matrix element uncertainties.

## Method

The authors convert the half-life sensitivities quoted by the LEGEND, CUPID, and nEXO collaborations into effective Majorana mass values using nuclear matrix elements from four many-body methods: the nuclear shell model, quasiparticle random-phase approximation, energy-density functional theory, and the interacting boson model. They compare discovery and exclusion sensitivities for the isotopes ⁷⁶Ge, ¹⁰⁰Mo, and ¹³⁶Xe, grouping calculations by many-body method to aid consistent comparison.

## Key numbers

- The minimum effective Majorana mass for the inverted ordering, minimized with respect to unknown Majorana phases and m₃, is 18.4 ± 1.3 meV using Particle Data Group values, with uncertainty dominated by the solar mixing angle θ₁₂.
- Using NuFIT results, the minimum effective Majorana mass is 18.6 ± 1.2 meV.
- The lower bound on m_ββ corresponds to an upper bound on the half-life at the scale of 10²⁷ to 10²⁸ years.
- Nuclear matrix elements for ⁷⁶Ge range from 2.66 to 3.57 (NSM), 3.12 to 5.26 (QRPA), 4.60 to 6.04 (EDF), and 5.14 to 6.34 (IBM).
- Nuclear matrix elements for ¹³⁶Xe range from 1.63 to 2.45 (NSM), 1.11 to 3.38 (QRPA), 4.20 to 4.77 (EDF), and 3.25 to 3.40 (IBM).
- Nuclear matrix elements for ¹⁰⁰Mo range from 3.90 to 5.87 (QRPA), 5.08 to 6.59 (EDF), and 3.84 to 5.08 (IBM); no NSM calculations are available for ¹⁰⁰Mo.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of experimental sensitivity to the inverted ordering scenario by Agostini et al. (2017) and Caldwell et al. (2017), providing a quantitative framework for comparing proposed ton-scale experiments (LEGEND, CUPID, nEXO) that use different isotopes and detection technologies. The paper enables the community to set a concrete discovery sensitivity goal of 18.4 meV for fully testing the inverted ordering, while highlighting that ongoing ab initio nuclear calculations and the recently identified contact term in nuclear matrix elements (Cirigliano et al., 2017, 2018) may modify the expected decay rates but are unlikely to change the conclusion that the proposed experimental program will fully probe the inverted ordering parameter space.
