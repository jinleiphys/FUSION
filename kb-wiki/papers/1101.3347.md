# Extracting Scattering Phase-Shifts in Higher Partial-Waves from Lattice QCD Calculations

> Lüscher's method is routinely used to determine meson-meson, meson-baryon and baryon-baryon s-wave scattering amplitudes below inelastic thresholds from Lattice QCD calculations - presently at unphysical light-quark masses. In this work we review the formalism and develop the requisite expressions to extract phase-shifts describing meson-meson scattering in partial-waves with angular-momentum l<=6 and l=9. The implications of the underlying cubic symmetry, and strategies for extracting the phase-shifts from Lattice QCD calculations, are presented, along with a discussion of the signal-to-noise problem that afflicts the higher partial-waves.

## Key claim

This work develops the explicit formalism and expressions needed to extract scattering phase-shifts for meson-meson interactions in partial-waves with angular momentum up to l=6 and l=9 from Lattice QCD calculations, but the authors note that the underlying cubic symmetry of the lattice causes mixing of angular momentum in cubic irreducible representations, which fundamentally limits the precision with which phase-shifts in any given partial-wave can be extracted.

## Method

The authors review and extend Luscher's finite-volume formalism by deriving the explicit relations between two-meson energy eigenvalues in a cubic volume and the scattering phase-shifts for higher partial-waves, specifically for angular momentum l <= 6 and l = 9. They analyze how the irreducible representations of the cubic group determine the degeneracy patterns of eigenstates and present the required expressions for the zeta-functions that appear in the quantization condition, along with strategies for extracting phase-shifts from Lattice QCD calculations.

## Key numbers

- The formalism covers partial-waves with angular momentum l <= 6 and l = 9 for meson-meson scattering
- The paper presents explicit calculations of the zeta-functions Z_{lm}(1; q̃²) for various combinations including (l=12, m=0), (l=12, m=4), (l=14, m=0), (l=16, m=0), (l=16, m=4), (l=18, m=0), (l=18, m=4), (l=4, m=0), (l=6, m=0), (l=8, m=0), and (l=10, m=0)
- The zeta-function values shown in the figures span ranges from approximately -3e+09 to +3e+09 for the highest partial-waves (l=16, l=18) and from approximately -200 to +200 for lower partial-waves (l=4)
- The authors reference exploratory quenched calculations of the s-d mixing parameter epsilon at pion masses of m_pi ~ 730, 530, and 380 MeV performed in a small number of lattice volumes (Aoki 2009)

## Context

This work extends the foundational formalism of Luscher (1986, 1990) for extracting scattering phase-shifts from Lattice QCD calculations, which had been routinely applied only to s-wave scattering. It builds upon the earlier work of Mandula, Zweig, and Govaerts (1983) on cubic symmetry implications, and the extension to systems with non-zero center-of-mass momentum by Rummukainen and Gottlieb (1995) and later by Kim, Sharpe, and Sachrajda (2005). The paper enables future Lattice QCD calculations of higher partial-wave phase-shifts in meson-meson scattering, which have direct implications for understanding the rho-meson resonance and for nuclear structure calculations where partial-waves with l <= 4 are phenomenologically required.
