# Neutron-proton differential transverse flow in $^{132}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn collisions at 270 MeV/nucleon

> Within a transport model, we study the neutron-proton differential transverse flow and its excitation function in central $^{132}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn collisions at 270 MeV/nucleon. To more accurately evaluate effects of the high-density behavior of symmetry energy \esym on this observable, we also consider the uncertainties of \esym around the saturation density $ρ_{0}$. It is shown that the neutron-proton differential transverse flow and its excitation function are mainly sensitive to the slope $L$ of \esym at $ρ_{0}$. However, the effects of low-density behavior of \esym on this observable should also be considered. Therefore, it is suggested that measurements of the neutron-proton differential transverse flow and its excitation function may provide useful complements to the constraints on $L$ extracted from the spectral pion ratio in S$π$RIT experiments.

## Key claim

The neutron-proton differential transverse flow and its excitation function in central $^{132}$Sn + $124$Sn collisions at 270 MeV/nucleon are mainly sensitive to the slope $L$ of the symmetry energy at saturation density $\rho_0$, but the uncertainties of the symmetry energy around $\rho_0$ (low-density behavior) must also be considered when using this observable to probe the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The study uses an isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with an improved momentum-dependent interaction (IMDI). The authors simulate central collisions ($b=3$ fm) of $^{132}$Sn + $124$Sn at 270 MeV/nucleon, varying the slope parameter $L$ of the symmetry energy from 30.3 to 125.6 MeV under two different cases: Case I fixes the symmetry energy at $2\rho_0/3$ to 25.5 MeV, while Case II fixes the symmetry energy at $\rho_0$ to 32.5 MeV. The free neutron-proton differential transverse flow is computed as a function of center-of-mass rapidity, and its excitation function (slope at mid-rapidity) is evaluated as a function of $L$.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter $L$ is varied over four values: 30.3, 62.0, 93.8, and 125.6 MeV for both Case I and Case II.
- The symmetry energy at $2\rho_0/3$ is fixed to 25.5 MeV in Case I; the symmetry energy at $\rho_0$ is fixed to 32.5 MeV in Case II.
- The saturation density $\rho_0$ is taken as 0.16 fm$^{-3}$; the binding energy is -16 MeV; the incompressibility $K_0$ is 230 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter; the isoscalar effective mass $m^*_s$ is 0.7$m$.
- The symmetry potential at infinitely large nucleon momentum $U_{sym}^{\infty}(\rho_0)$ is constrained to approximately -160 MeV.
- The excitation function of the neutron-proton differential transverse flow is evaluated at mid-rapidity $|y_{c.m.}| \le 0.1$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron-proton differential transverse flow as a probe of the high-density symmetry energy (LiBA00, LiBA02, Yong05, Yong06) and complements recent S$\pi$RIT experimental results on the spectral pion ratio, which constrained $L$ to the range 42-117 MeV (Estee21). The authors use an IBUU model that incorporates constraints on the momentum dependence of the isovector potential from S$\pi$RIT pion data (Wei21b). The results suggest that measurements of the neutron-proton differential transverse flow and its excitation function can provide complementary constraints on $L$ to those from pion observables, but the uncertainties of the symmetry energy around saturation density must be accounted for, as previously discussed by Stone (2017).
