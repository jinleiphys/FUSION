# Non-perturbative structure of the nucleon

> Chiral perturbation theory is the effective field theory of the standard model. In this talk, I discuss some applications of this framework to the pion--nucleon system. These are chiral corrections to the S--wave pion--nucleon scattering lengths, the reaction $πN \to ππN$ at threshold and low--energy theorems in $π^0$ photoproduction.

# Non-perturbative structure of the nucleon

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) provides a systematic framework for calculating corrections to current-algebra results in the pion–nucleon system, but the convergence of the chiral expansion varies strongly between processes: it is well-behaved for the isovector scattering length and the amplitude D₁ in πN → ππN, yet slowly converging for the electric dipole amplitude E₀⁺ in π⁰ photoproduction.

## Method
The author applies chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) to the two-flavor sector of QCD, expanding the effective Lagrangian in powers of derivatives and meson mass insertions. Calculations are performed at one-loop order (chiral dimension D = 2L + …) for threshold πN scattering, the reaction πN → ππN, and π⁰ photoproduction, with low-energy constants (LECs) determined from data or estimated from resonance exchange (Δ(1232), N*(1440), ρ-meson).

## Key numbers
- Current-algebra prediction for the isovector S-wave πN scattering length: a⁻_CA = 0.079 M_π⁻¹ (with M_π = 139.57 MeV, F_π = 92.5 MeV, m_p = 938.27 MeV).
- CHPT band for a⁻: 0.088 M_π⁺⁻¹ ≤ a⁻ ≤ 0.096 M_π⁺⁻¹, 10–20% larger than the current-algebra value.
- Extracted isospin-2 S-wave ππ scattering length from πN → ππN: a₀² = –0.052 ± 0.013, compatible with the one-loop CHPT prediction of –0.042 ± 0.008.
- Extracted isospin-0 S-wave ππ scattering length: a₀⁰ = 0.16 ± 0.05, compatible with the current-algebra value of 0.16 and the one-loop CHPT prediction of 0.20 ± 0.01.
- CHPT prediction for the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant in muon capture: g_P = 8.44 ± 0.23.
- Empirically believed value of the S-wave multipole E₀⁺ in π⁰ photoproduction: (–2.0 ± 0.2) × 10⁻³ M_π⁻¹ (magnitude), with new data suggesting a smaller magnitude.

## Context
This talk extends the program of systematic chiral perturbation theory calculations for the single-nucleon sector, building on the foundational work of Weinberg (1979) and Gasser & Leutwyler (1984) for the meson sector, and the pion–nucleon framework of Gasser, Sainio & Švarc (1988). The results for πN scattering lengths refine the classic current-algebra prediction of Weinberg (1966) and reconcile it with empirical values from the Karlsruhe–Helsinki phase shift analysis and the PSI-ETH pionic atom measurements (Sigg, 1995). The πN → ππN analysis supersedes the Olsson–Turner model and provides the first systematic extraction of a₀² and a₀⁰ from threshold data, while the π⁰ photoproduction results correct an incomplete low-energy theorem from the 1970s and are being tested by new MAMI and SAL experiments. The work enables sharper tests of chiral dynamics in the nucleon sector and points to the need for three-flavor extensions and consistent inclusion of isospin-breaking effects.
