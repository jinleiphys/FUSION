# Estimates of absolute branching fractions for the $f_0(1710)$ decays and radiative transitions $ψ(2S)\toγf_0(1710)$ and $Υ(1S)\toγf_0(1710)$

> Using the result of the VES Collaboration for $Br(J/ψ\toγf_0(1710))$, we estimate the absolute branching fractions for the $f_0(1710)$ decays into $ππ$, $K\bar K$, $ηη$, $ωω$, and $ωϕ$. In addition, we estimate $Br(ψ(2S)\toγf_0(1710))\approx3.5\times10^{-5}$ and $Br(Υ(1S)\toγf_0(1710))\approx1\times10^{-5}$.

## Key claim

Using the VES Collaboration's measurement of the total branching fraction for \(J/\psi \to \gamma f_0(1710)\) as \((4.46 \pm 0.82) \times 10^{-3}\), the authors derive absolute branching fractions for the \(f_0(1710)\) decays into \(\pi\pi\), \(K\bar K\), \(\eta\eta\), \(\omega\omega\), and \(\omega\phi\) for the first time, and also estimate the radiative transition branching fractions for \(\psi(2S) \to \gamma f_0(1710)\) and \(\Upsilon(1S) \to \gamma f_0(1710)\) under the assumption of factorization of the production and decay couplings.

## Method

The authors take the product \(Br(f_0(1710)\to\pi\pi)Br(f_0(1710)\to\omega\phi) = (4.8\pm1.2)\times10^{-3}\) from the VES charge-exchange reaction \(\pi^- p \to \omega\phi n\) and the product of \(J/\psi \to \gamma f_0(1710)\) branching fractions from the Review of Particle Physics (RPP). They divide the latter product by the former, take the square root, and obtain \(Br(J/\psi \to \gamma f_0(1710))\). They then divide the RPP values for \(J/\psi \to \gamma f_0(1710) \to \gamma X\) decays by this total to extract absolute branching fractions for the \(f_0(1710)\) decays. Using factorization, they scale the known \(J/\psi\) radiative branching fractions to \(\psi(2S)\) and \(\Upsilon(1S)\) via the ratios of their respective \(\pi\pi\) and \(K\bar K\) channels.

## Key numbers

*   \(Br(J/\psi \to \gamma f_0(1710)) = (4.46 \pm 0.82) \times 10^{-3}\) from the VES Collaboration.
*   Absolute branching fractions for \(f_0(1710)\) decays: \(\pi\pi = 0.0852 \pm 0.0193\), \(K\bar K = 0.213 \pm 0.045\), \(\eta\eta = 0.054 \pm 0.029\), \(\omega\omega = 0.070 \pm 0.026\), \(\omega\phi = 0.056 \pm 0.017\), total = \(0.478 \pm 0.065\).
*   \(Br(\psi(2S) \to \gamma f_0(1710)) \approx 3.5 \times 10^{-5}\) (average of \((4.1 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-5}\) from \(\pi\pi\) and \((3.1 \pm 0.7) \times 10^{-5}\) from \(K\bar K\)).
*   \(Br(\Upsilon(1S) \to \gamma f_0(1710)) \approx 1 \times 10^{-5}\) (average of \((0.93 \pm 0.27) \times 10^{-5}\) from \(\pi\pi\) and \((1.03 \pm 0.19) \times 10^{-5}\) from \(K\bar K\)).
*   Ratio \(Br(f_0(1710)\to\pi\pi)/Br(f_0(1710)\to K\bar K) = 0.40 \pm 0.07\) from \(J/\psi\) data, \(0.58 \pm 0.11\) from \(\psi(2S)\) data, and \(0.36 \pm 0.17\) from \(\Upsilon(1S)\) data.

## Context

This work extends the VES Collaboration (2023) measurement of the \(f_0(1710)\) production in \(\pi^- p \to \omega\phi n\) to provide absolute branching fractions for the resonance, which the Particle Data Group (2023) lists only as "seen" without averages or fits. The results enable comparison with the properties of the \(a_0(1700/1800)\) meson discussed by Achasov and Shestakov (2023) as a possible \(q^2 \bar q^2\) state from the MIT bag model (Jaffe, 1977), and the method can be applied to other heavy scalar resonances such as \(f_0(1370)\), \(f_0(1500)\), \(f_0(1770)\), and \(f_0(2020)\).
