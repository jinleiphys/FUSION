# $J$-pairing interaction, number of states, and nine-$j$ sum rules of four identical particles

> In this paper we study $J$-pairing Hamiltonian and find that the sum of eigenvalues of spin $I$ states equals sum of norm matrix elements within the pair basis for four identical particles such as four fermions in a single-$j$ shell or four bosons with spin $l$. We relate number of states to sum rules of nine-$j$ coefficients. We obtained sum rules for nine-$j$ coefficients $<(jj)J,(jj)K:I| (jj)J, (jj)K:I>$ and $<(ll)J,(ll)K:I| (ll)J, (ll)K:I>$ summing over (1) even $J$ and $K$, (2) even $J$ and odd $K$, (3) odd $J$ and odd $K$, and (4) both even and odd $J,K$, where $j$ is a half integer and $l$ is an integer.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the sum of eigenvalues of spin \(I\) states for a \(J\)-pairing Hamiltonian of four identical particles (fermions in a single-\(j\) shell or bosons with spin \(l\)) equals the sum of norm matrix elements within the pair basis, and this relation, combined with previously derived formulas for the number of spin \(I\) states \(D_I\), yields new sum rules for nine-\(j\) coefficients. The abstract glosses over the condition that the sum rules are derived specifically for the case of four identical particles and that the results for \(I \le 2j-1\) are less transparent and require more complicated formulas.

## Method

The authors study the \(J\)-pairing Hamiltonian \(H_J\) for four identical fermions in a single-\(j\) shell (and analogously for bosons with spin \(l\)). They compute the trace of the \(H_J\) matrix for a given total spin \(I\) by summing diagonal matrix elements of the norm of the pair basis states, which involves a nine-\(j\) symbol. This trace is equated to \(6 D_I\), where \(D_I\) is the number of spin \(I\) states for four particles, previously obtained analytically by the authors (Zhao and Arima, 2005) and others. By substituting the known \(D_I\) formulas into this equality, they derive explicit sum rules for the nine-\(j\) coefficients summed over different parity conditions on the intermediate angular momenta \(J\) and \(K\).

## Key numbers

* For four fermions with half-integer \(j\), the sum rule for even \(J\) and even \(K\) at \(I = I_{\text{max}} = 4j-6\) yields \(S_I = 2\).
* For four fermions with half-integer \(j\), the sum rule for even \(J\) and even \(K\) at \(I = I_{\text{max}} - 2\) yields \(S_I = 6\).
* For four fermions with half-integer \(j\) and \(I \le 2j-1\), the sum rule for even \(J\) and even \(K\) at \(I = 0\) is \(2m - 2\), where \(m = (j+3/2) \bmod 3\).
* For four bosons with integer \(l\), the sum rule for even \(J\) and even \(K\) at \(I = 0\) is \(4 - 2m\), where \(m = l \bmod 3\).
* For four fermions with half-integer \(j\), the sum rule for both even and odd \(J\) and \(K\) when \(I \le 2j+1\) is \(4 \left[ (1+I)/2 \right]\).
* For four fermions with half-integer \(j\), the sum rule for both even and odd \(J\) and \(K\) when \(I \ge 2j\) is \(4 + 4 \left[ (4j - I)/2 \right]\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier results on the number of spin states for three and four identical particles (Zhao and Arima, 2004; Zhao and Arima, 2005) and on sum rules for six-\(j\) symbols derived from three-particle systems (Zhao and Arima, 2004). It also generalizes specific nine-\(j\) symbol formulas found by Zamick and Escuderos (2004) for particular values of \(J\), \(K\), and \(I\). The derived sum rules provide a systematic, algebraic method for evaluating sums of nine-\(j\) coefficients, which are widely used in nuclear structure theory and angular momentum recoupling. The approach is limited to four-particle systems; extending it to \(n \ge 5\) is noted to be difficult except for special cases near maximal spin.
