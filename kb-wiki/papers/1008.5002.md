# Short-range interaction in three-dimensional quantum mechanics

> We show that it is possible to define shape-independent three-dimensional short-range quantum interactions in two parameter form for non-spherical angular momentum channels through double rescaling of potential strength. Unlike the special case of $l=0$, where the zero range limit of the system is renormalizable, the effective ranges diverge for $l /ne 0$ channels, and the system becomes trivial at zero-size limit. It is also shown that the two-parameter representation with finite interaction range is useful in analyzing phase shifts with accuracy and describing resonances in non-spherical scatterings.

## Key claim

The paper shows that shape-independent three-dimensional short-range quantum interactions can be defined for non-spherical angular momentum channels (l > 0) using a two-parameter form with finite interaction range, but the strict zero-range limit is trivial for all l > 0 channels because the effective range diverges as the cutoff approaches zero.

## Method

The author considers quantum scattering off a small spherical obstacle of radius Λ in three dimensions, imposing a general Robin boundary condition ψ'_l(Λ) - C_l ψ_l(Λ) = 0 at the surface. By rescaling the boundary parameter C_l(Λ) as C_l(Λ) = l/Λ + χ_l Λ^(2l), the scattering phase shift is derived in a low-energy expansion that yields a generalized shape-independent formula -k^(2l+1)/((2l-1)!!^2) cot δ_l = χ_l + k^2/((2l-1)Λ^(2l-1)). Numerical calculations for p-wave (l=1) scattering with Λ=0.1 are performed to test the formula against exact solutions.

## Key numbers

- For l=1 scattering with Λ=0.1, three boundary parameters C_1 are examined: C_1=9.75 (weakly attractive, X_1=-25), C_1=9.999 (strongly attractive, X_1=-0.1), and C_1=10.25 (weakly repulsive, X_1=25)
- For χ_1=-25, the scattering matrix pole is at k_pole = 1.5 - 0.12i
- For χ_1=-0.1, the scattering matrix pole is at k_pole = 0.10 - 0.0005i
- The two-parameter shape-independent formula agrees with exact calculations up to kΛ ≈ 0.5

## Context

This work extends the well-known shape-independent scattering formula for s-waves (l=0) with scattering length and effective range, as developed by Newton (1966), to general angular momentum channels. The paper provides a physical explanation for why zero-range interactions in three dimensions are trivial for l > 0, in contrast to the rich four-parameter family of one-dimensional point interactions described by Albeverio and Gesztesy (2005). The results enable accurate analysis of low-energy non-spherical scatterings and resonances in nuclear and atomic physics using a finite-range two-parameter description.
