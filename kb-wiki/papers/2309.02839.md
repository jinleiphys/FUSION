# Surprising charge-radius kink in the Sc isotopes at N=20

> Charge radii of neutron deficient 40Sc and 41Sc nuclei were determined using collinear laser spectroscopy. With the new data, the chain of Sc charge radii extends below the neutron magic number N=20 and shows a pronounced kink, generally taken as a signature of a shell closure, but one notably absent in the neighboring Ca, K and Ar isotopic chains. Theoretical models that explain the trend at N=20 for the Ca isotopes cannot reproduce this puzzling behavior.

## Key claim
The charge radii of neutron-deficient Sc isotopes exhibit a pronounced kink at the neutron magic number N=20, which is absent in the neighboring Ca, K, and Ar isotopic chains. Theoretical models that successfully explain the smooth trend at N=20 for Ca isotopes cannot reproduce this kink in Sc.

## Method
Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed on the 363.2 nm transition in Sc+ ions at the BECOLA facility at Michigan State University. The 40Sc and 41Sc isotopes were produced at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory via nucleon pick-up of a 40Ca beam at 140 MeV/nucleon, separated using the A1900 fragment separator, thermalized in a gas stopper, and transported to the BECOLA facility at 30 keV. The isotope shifts were measured relative to stable 45Sc, and differential mean square charge radii were extracted using multi-configuration Dirac-Hartree-Fock (MCDHF) atomic calculations for the mass-shift and field-shift parameters.

## Key numbers
- Charge radius of 40Sc: 3.514(3)(25) fm (statistical and systematic uncertainties in parentheses)
- Charge radius of 41Sc: 3.503(1)(20) fm
- Isotope shift δν(40,45): -1594(8) MHz
- Isotope shift δν(41,45): -1199(4) MHz
- Weighted mean mass-shift parameter K(MS) = 604 ± 22 GHz·u
- Weighted mean field-shift parameter F(el) = -352 ± 12 MHz/fm²

## Context
This work extends charge radius measurements of Sc isotopes below N=20, where previous measurements by Avgoulea (2011) and Koszorus (2021) covered only N≥22. The observed kink at N=20 contrasts with the smooth charge radius trends seen in Ca (Miller 2019), K (Touchard 1982, Behr 1997, Rossi 2015), and Ar (Klein 1996) isotopic chains. The failure of both density functional theory (using the Fy functional) and ab-initio methods (coupled-cluster and VS-IMSRG) to reproduce the kink motivates further measurements in the Ti chain and theoretical studies of core polarization effects in this mass region.
