# Neutron specific heat in the crust of neutron stars from the nuclear band theory

> The inner crust of neutron stars, formed of a crystal lattice of uclear clusters immersed in a sea of unbound neutrons, may be the nique example of periodic nuclear systems. We have calculated the neutron specific heat in the shallow part of the crust using the band theory of solids with Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interactions. We have also tested the validity of various approximations. We have found that the neutron specific heat is well described by that of a Fermi gas, while the motion of the unbound neutrons is strongly affected by the nuclear lattice. These apparently contradictory results are explained by the particular properties of the neutron Fermi surface.

## Key claim

The neutron specific heat in the shallow inner crust of neutron stars is well described by that of a free Fermi gas, even though the motion of the unbound neutrons is strongly affected by the nuclear lattice; this apparent contradiction is resolved by the fact that the nuclear lattice reduces both the Fermi surface area and the neutron group velocity by approximately the same factor, leaving the density of states and specific heat nearly unchanged.

## Method

The authors applied the nuclear band theory of solids to a body-centered cubic crystal lattice of nuclear clusters, using the Skyrme SLy4 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. They solved the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock equations in the Wigner-Seitz approximation with mixed Neumann-Dirichlet boundary conditions to determine nucleon distributions and mean fields, then solved the Schrödinger equation with Bloch boundary conditions using the Linearized Augmented Plane Wave method. The neutron specific heat was computed from the entropy density by numerical differentiation for temperatures between 10 keV and 100 keV in three shallow layers of the inner crust.

## Key numbers

- For the three layers studied, total densities are \(6.69 \times 10^{11}\) g.cm\(^{-3}\), \(1.00 \times 10^{12}\) g.cm\(^{-3}\), and \(1.47 \times 10^{12}\) g.cm\(^{-3}\).
- The neutron gas densities at \(T=0\) are \(1.3 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(2.6 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\), and \(4.9 \times 10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-3}\) for the three layers respectively.
- The dynamical effective mass ratios \(m_n^\star/m_n\) are 4.0, 3.6, and 3.2 for the three layers in order of increasing density.
- The Fermi surface area reduction factors \(\xi_F\) are 0.44, 0.49, and 0.52 for the three layers.
- The Fermi wavelengths are approximately 40.09 fm, 31.82 fm, and 25.76 fm for the three layers, comparable to the cell radii of 49.24 fm, 46.33 fm, and 44.3 fm.
- The de Gennes approximation yields specific heats a factor of 2-3 smaller than the band theory results.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear band theory previously developed by the same authors (Chamel 2005, 2007) to thermodynamic properties, specifically the neutron specific heat in neutron star crusts. It contradicts the implicit assumption in many cooling simulations that the neutron specific heat can be approximated by that of uniform neutron matter without accounting for lattice effects, while also showing that the Wigner-Seitz approximation (used by Pizzochero 2002, Monrozeau 2007) and the de Gennes approximation (applied by Monrozeau 2007) are less accurate than the simple Fermi gas model for this quantity. The results enable more reliable modeling of the thermal relaxation of neutron star crusts observed in systems like KS 1731-260 and MXB 1659-29, and provide a foundation for understanding how the same lattice that strongly affects neutron dynamics (relevant for pulsar glitch mechanisms) leaves the specific heat largely unchanged.
