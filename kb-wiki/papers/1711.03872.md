# Anisotropic-hydrodynamics approach to a quark-gluon fluid mixture

> Anisotropic-hydrodynamics framework is used to describe a mixture of quark and gluon fluids. The effects of quantum statistics, finite quark mass, and finite baryon number density are taken into account. The results of anisotropic hydrodynamics are compared with exact solutions of the coupled kinetic equations for quarks and gluons in the relaxation time approximation. The overall very good agreement between the hydrodynamic and kinetic-theory results is found.

## Key claim

Anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) reproduces the exact solutions of coupled kinetic equations for a quark-gluon fluid mixture with very good agreement, provided the framework uses an appropriate set of moments (including second moments and special combinations of zeroth moments), but only when the mixing parameter \(\alpha\) is chosen as either 0 or 1 to avoid direct quark-gluon coupling beyond energy-momentum conservation.

## Method

The authors solved seven coupled ordinary differential equations for the quark anisotropy parameter \(\xi_Q\), gluon anisotropy parameter \(\xi_G\), quark transverse-momentum scale \(\Lambda_Q\), gluon transverse-momentum scale \(\Lambda_G\), non-equilibrium quark chemical potential \(\lambda\), effective temperature \(T\), and effective baryon chemical potential \(\mu\) as functions of proper time \(\tau\). These equations were derived from the zeroth, first, and second moments of the relativistic Boltzmann equations in the relaxation time approximation, using Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic distribution functions for massive quarks (mass 300 MeV) obeying Fermi-Dirac statistics and massless gluons obeying Bose-Einstein statistics, under the assumption of boost-invariant (0+1)-dimensional expansion. The aHydro results were compared to exact solutions of the coupled kinetic equations from Florkowski (2017).

## Key numbers

* Initial quark anisotropy \(\xi_Q^0 = 1\) and gluon anisotropy \(\xi_G^0 = 10\) (oblate-oblate initial momentum distributions), with initial transverse momentum scales \(\Lambda_Q^0 = \Lambda_G^0 = 1\) GeV.
* Initial quark anisotropy \(\xi_Q^0 = -0.5\) and gluon anisotropy \(\xi_G^0 = -0.25\) (prolate-prolate initial momentum distributions), with initial transverse momentum scales \(\Lambda_Q^0 = \Lambda_G^0 = 1\) GeV.
* Quark mass set to 300 MeV; gluons treated as massless.
* Initial baryon number density \({\cal B}_0 = 0.001\) fm\(^{-3}\) or \({\cal B}_0 = 1\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* Internal degeneracy ratio \(r = g_G / g_Q = 4/3\) for gluons to quarks (with \(N_f = 2\) quark flavours).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of anisotropic hydrodynamics for quark-gluon mixtures by Florkowski (2012, 2013, 2014, 2015) and Tinti (2013), which were restricted to massless particles obeying classical statistics, by incorporating quantum statistics (Fermi-Dirac for quarks, Bose-Einstein for gluons), finite quark mass, and finite baryon number density. The comparisons with exact kinetic-theory solutions from Florkowski (2017) validate aHydro as a reliable approximation for phenomenological modeling of heavy-ion collisions, particularly for reproducing the time dependence of the ratio of longitudinal to transverse pressures.
