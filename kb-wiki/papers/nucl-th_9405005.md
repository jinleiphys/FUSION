# Thermal properties of Fe-54

> We study the thermal properties of Fe-54 with the Brown-Richter interaction in the complete 1p0f model space. Monte Carlo calculations show a peak in the heat capacity and rapid increases in both the moment of inertia and M1 strength near a temperature of 1.1 MeV that are associated with the vanishing of proton-proton and neutron-neutron monopole pair correlations; neutron-proton correlations persist to higher temperatures. Our results are consistent with a Fermi gas level density whose back-shift vanishes with increasing temperature.

# Thermal properties of Fe-54

## Key claim

Monte Carlo shell model calculations of Fe-54 in the complete 1p0f model space show a peak in the heat capacity and rapid increases in both the moment of inertia and M1 strength near a temperature of 1.1 MeV, which are associated with the vanishing of proton-proton and neutron-neutron monopole pair correlations; however, neutron-proton correlations persist to higher temperatures, and the results are only quantitatively reliable for temperatures below about 1.5 MeV due to the finite model space.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo shell model calculations for Fe-54 using the Brown-Richter interaction in the complete 1p3/2,1/2 0f7/2,5/2 model space, involving approximately 5x10^9 configurations of 8 valence neutrons and 6 valence protons in 20 orbitals. To circumvent the sign problem, they employed an extrapolation procedure from a family of sign-problem-free Hamiltonians to the physical Hamiltonian, using a linear extrapolation for most observables and a quadratic extrapolation for quadrupole moments, with a time step of Delta-beta = 1/32 MeV^-1 and 5000-9000 independent Monte Carlo samples at seven values of the coupling constant g.

## Key numbers

- Heat capacity peak and pairing phase transition occur near a temperature of 1.1 MeV
- Moment of inertia reaches the rigid rotor value of 10.7 hbar^2/MeV at temperatures above 1.5 MeV
- Level density parameter a = 7.2 MeV^-1 was adopted for the backshifted Fermi gas model comparison
- Empirical level density parameter A/8 MeV = 6.8 MeV^-1 agrees with results for T approximately 1.1-1.5 MeV
- Single-particle estimate for B(M1) strength is 41 mu_N^2, but calculated values remain significantly lower for T = 1.3-2 MeV
- Low-temperature GT beta+ strength is 4.7 +/- 0.2
- Ground state energy after Delta-beta extrapolation is -57.3 +/- 0.3 MeV
- Backshift parameter P_0 = 1.45 MeV was used in the constant-backshift Fermi gas model
- Temperature-dependent backshift parameters: T_0 = 1.05 MeV, alpha = 0.25 MeV

## Context

This work extends finite-temperature nuclear structure calculations beyond the sd-shell nuclei (such as Ne-20 and Mg-24) studied by Miller and collaborators (1990) using conventional shell model diagonalization, and beyond mean-field approaches such as those by Goodman (1981) and Egido and Ring (1993). The Monte Carlo technique, building on the extrapolation method of Alhassid et al. (1994), enables fully microscopic calculations in model spaces far larger than those accessible to conventional methods. The results enable improved modeling of nuclear properties at high excitation energies relevant to astrophysical scenarios, particularly the late stages of supernova collapse, and demonstrate that the common assumption of temperature-independent Gamow-Teller strength is valid for temperatures below 2 MeV.
