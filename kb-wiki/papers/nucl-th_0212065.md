# The extended, relativistic hyperon star model

> In this paper an equation of state of neutron star matter which includes strange baryons in the framework of Zimanyi and Moszkowski (ZM) model has been obtained. We concentrate on the effects of the isospin dependence of the equation of state constructing for the appropriate choices of parameters the hyperons star model. Numerous neutron star models show that the appearance of hyperons is connected with the increasing density in neutron star interiors. Various studies have indicated that the inclusion of delta meson mainly affects the symmetry energy and through this the chemical composition of a neutron star. As the effective nucleon mass contributes to hadron chemical potentials it alters the chemical composition of the star. In the result the obtained model of the star not only excludes large population of hadrons but also does not reduce significantly lepton contents in the star interior.

# The extended, relativistic hyperon star model

## Key claim

The Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) derivative coupling model, extended with the isovector-scalar delta meson and nonlinear vector meson interactions, produces an equation of state for neutron star matter that avoids the negative effective nucleon masses seen in the standard TM1 parameter set when hyperons are included, while also shifting the onset of hyperon populations toward higher densities and reducing the proton fraction compared to models without the delta meson.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model based on the Zimanyi-Moszkowski derivative coupling scheme, including the full baryon octet (n, p, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) and an extended meson sector comprising sigma, omega, rho, delta, sigma*, and phi mesons. They solve the mean-field field equations for three parameter sets: set I (no delta meson), set II (with delta meson), and set III (with delta meson plus nonlinear vector meson interactions), imposing charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions to determine the equilibrium composition of neutron star matter.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy is fixed to the experimental value of 32 ± 4 MeV.
- The single Lambda potential in nuclear matter is taken as -U^N_Lambda(rho_0) = 27-28 MeV.
- The Sigma potential in nuclear matter is taken as repulsive: U^N_Sigma(rho_0) = 30 MeV.
- The Xi potential well depth is -U^N_Xi(rho_0) = 18 MeV.
- The hyperon-hyperon potential well depth is -U_h(rho_0) = 40 MeV.
- The first strange baryon (Lambda) appears at a baryon number density of rho = 2.5 x rho_0.
- The critical proton fraction for the direct URCA process is about Y_p ~ 0.11 for nucleons and electrons, increasing to ~0.14 when muons are included.
- The quark constituent masses used in the QMF comparison model are m_c,u = m_c,d = 367.61 MeV and m_c,s = 504.1 MeV, with bag constant B^{1/4} = 154.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) model (Zimanyi and Moszkowski, 1990) to include hyperons and the delta meson, building on the relativistic hadrodynamics framework of Walecka (1974) and the TM1 parameter set of Sugahara and Toki (1994). The inclusion of the delta meson follows studies showing its effect on symmetry energy and chemical composition (delta meson references), while the hyperon-hyperon interactions are simulated via hidden strange meson exchange (sigma* and phi). The results enable comparison with quark star models constructed in the quark mean field (QMF) framework (R. Manka and I. Bednarek, 2001) and inform predictions for neutron star cooling via the direct URCA process, which requires a proton fraction above the critical threshold.
