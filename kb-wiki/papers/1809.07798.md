# Role of charged particle emission on the evaporation residue formation in the $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba reaction leading to the $^{220}$Th compound nucleus

> We present detailed results of a theoretical investigation on the production of evaporation residue nuclei obtained in a heavy ion reaction when charged particles (proton and $α$-particle) are also emitted with the neutron evaporation along the deexcitation cascade of the formed compound nucleus. The almost mass symmetric $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba reaction has been studied since there are many experimental results on individual evaporation residue (ER) cross sections after few light particle emissions along the cascade of the $^{220}$Th compound nucleus (CN) covering the wide 12--70 MeV excitation energy range. Our specific theoretical results on the ER cross sections for the $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba are in good agreement with the available experimental measurements, but our overall theoretical results concerning all possible relevant contributions of evaporation residues are several times greater than the ERs measured in experiment. The discrepancy could be due to the experimental difficulties in the identification of ER nuclei after the emission of multiple neutral and charged particles, nevertheless the analysis of ER data is very important to test the reliability of the model and to stress the importance on the investigation of ER nuclei also obtained after charged particle emissions.

## Key claim

The theoretical model reproduces the measured evaporation residue cross sections for the $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba reaction when only the experimentally accessible channels (neutron, proton+neutron, and alpha+neutron emissions) are considered, but the total calculated ER cross section including all possible charged-particle channels (such as 2alpha+xn and alpha+p+xn) is several times larger than the measured values, suggesting that experimental limitations in detecting multiple charged-particle emissions are responsible for the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors used a non-adiabatic approach based on the dinuclear system (DNS) model to calculate capture, complete fusion, quasifission, and fast fission cross sections. The deexcitation cascade of the $^{220}$Th compound nucleus was modeled with an advanced statistical code that computes fission and particle-decay widths using level densities that include collective effects, with the effective fission barrier incorporating shell-correction damping as functions of temperature and angular momentum. Evaporation residue cross sections were calculated for each step of the cascade after emission of neutrons, protons, alpha particles, and gamma quanta.

## Key numbers

- The mass asymmetry parameter for the $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba reaction is $\eta = 0.255$.
- The excitation energy range of the $^{220}$Th compound nucleus studied is 12 MeV to 70 MeV.
- The ratio of the total calculated ER cross section (all channels) to the measured ER cross section is factors of 2-3 on average, with peaks of 10 at $E^*_{\rm CN} = 28$ MeV and 4 at $E^*_{\rm CN} = 50$ MeV.
- The ratio of the total calculated ER cross section (all channels) to the calculated ER cross section from neutron emission only ranges from 17 to 53 over the 12-72 MeV excitation energy interval.
- The damping function parameters for shell corrections are: $T_0 = 1.16$ MeV, $d = 0.3$ MeV, $\ell_{1/2} = 20\hbar$, $\Delta\ell = 3\hbar$.
- The asymptotic level density parameter is $\tilde{a} = 0.094 \times A$ MeV$^{-1}$ and the shell-washing parameter for particle emission is $\gamma = 0.0064$ MeV$^{-1}$; for fission the parameter is $\gamma_{\rm fis} = 0.024$ MeV$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework previously applied by the same group (Mandaglio et al., 2015; Nasirov et al., various) to the nearly mass-symmetric $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba reaction, for which experimental data were reported by the authors of Physical Review C 65 (2002). The results highlight that charged-particle emission channels (especially 2alpha+xn and alpha+p+xn) contribute significantly to the total evaporation residue cross section, which is important for planning future experiments aimed at synthesizing exotic nuclei that cannot be produced by neutron emission alone. The analysis also demonstrates the need for improved experimental detection capabilities to fully test theoretical models of compound nucleus deexcitation.
