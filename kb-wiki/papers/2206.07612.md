# Quasideuterons in Light Nuclei

> The role of pairing correlations for nucleon pairs with isospin $T=1$ and $T=0$ is investigated for nuclei in the mass region $12 \leq A \leq 42$. For that purpose the two-nucleon densities resulting from nuclear shell-model calculations in one and two major shells are analyzed. Significant effects on the resulting energies are observed due to the formation of $T=0$ pairs. The formation of quasi-deuterons is maximal for symmetric nuclei with $N=Z$. The formation of $T=0$ pairs is less sensitive to the density of single-particle states close to the Fermi energy than the $T=1$ pairing and is relevant also for excitations across shell-closures. This robustness also explains why $T=0$ pairing does not lead to such a clear evidence in comparing energies of neighbored nuclei as the "odd-even mass staggering" due to the formation of $T=1$ pairing.

# Quasi-Deuterons in Light Nuclei

## Key claim

The formation of isoscalar (T=0) proton-neutron pairs, quasi-deuterons, is maximal in symmetric N=Z nuclei and is less sensitive to the density of single-particle states near the Fermi energy than T=1 pairing, which explains why T=0 pairing does not produce a clear odd-even mass staggering signal comparable to that of T=1 pairing. The authors argue that T=0 pairing correlations are relevant even for excitations across shell-closures, but the effect is more subtle and distributed than the clear signature of T=1 pairing in binding-energy systematics.

## Method

The authors performed configuration-mixing shell-model calculations for ground states of light nuclei in the mass region 12 ≤ A ≤ 42, using a realistic G-matrix derived from the One-Boson-Exchange Potential (OBEP) A and, for comparison, the phenomenological USD interaction of Brown et al. They analyzed two-nucleon densities via a partial-wave decomposition, computing enhancement factors S^T_ij for the ^1S_0 (T=1) and ^3S_1 (T=0) partial waves relative to a filled-shell reference, and compared shell-model results to Hartree-Fock calculations with angular-momentum projection. Calculations were performed in one major shell (sd-shell) and in two major shells (0p+1s0d for nuclei around ^16O, and 1s0d+1p0f for nuclei around ^40Ca).

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of T=0 versus T=1 pairing correlations in finite nuclei, following earlier theoretical efforts by Bertsch and Zuo (2010) who argued that the spin-orbit field in light nuclei suppresses isoscalar pairing, and by Artur et al. (2019) who studied the transition from nuclear matter to finite nuclei using realistic NN interactions. The present analysis uses shell-model two-body densities to quantify quasi-deuteron formation, complementing earlier work by Langanke et al. (1996) on sd-shell wave functions and by Engels et al. on isovector pairing in pf-shell nuclei. The results enable future experimental tests via isoscalar and isovector M1 transitions, as suggested by Matsubara et al., and provide a benchmark for beyond-mean-field calculations including symmetry-projected HFB approaches such as the VAMPIR method.
