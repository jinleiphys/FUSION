# Estimating initial state and quark-gluon plasma medium properties using a hybrid model with nucleon substructure calibrated to $p$-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ TeV

> We posit a unified hydrodynamic and microscopic description of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in ultrarelativistic $p$-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ TeV and evaluate our assertion using Bayesian inference. Specifically, we model the dynamics of both collision systems using initial conditions with parametric nucleon substructure, a pre-equilibrium free-streaming stage, event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics, and a microscopic hadronic afterburner. Free parameters of the model which describe the initial state and QGP medium are then simultaneously calibrated to fit charged particle yields, mean $p_T$, and flow cumulants. We argue that the global agreement of the calibrated model with the experimental data strongly supports the existence of hydrodynamic flow in small collision systems at ultrarelativistic energies, and that the flow produced develops at length scales smaller than a single proton. Posterior estimates for the model's input parameters are obtained, and new insights into the temperature dependence of the QGP transport coefficients and event-by-event structure of the proton are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a unified hydrodynamic and microscopic description of both p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, calibrated simultaneously using Bayesian inference, strongly supports the existence of hydrodynamic flow in small collision systems, with the caveat that this conclusion depends on the global agreement of the calibrated model with experimental data across both system sizes.

## Method

The authors construct a multi-stage hybrid model using the TRENTO initial condition framework with parametric nucleon substructure (varying the number and width of Gaussian constituents per nucleon), a pre-equilibrium free-streaming stage of variable duration, event-by-event (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics solved with VISH2+1, and the UrQMD microscopic hadronic afterburner. Free parameters describing the initial state (entropy deposition exponent, nucleon substructure parameters, fluctuation magnitude) and QGP medium (temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosities, free-streaming time, switching temperature) are simultaneously calibrated to charged particle yields, mean transverse momentum, and anisotropic flow cumulants from both p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV using Bayesian parameter estimation.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for both p-Pb and Pb-Pb systems
- Free-streaming time parameter tau_fs: varied in the approximate window 0 < tau_fs < 1 fm/c
- Pseudocritical transition temperature T_c = 0.154 GeV used in the shear viscosity parametrization
- Temperature interval for connecting hadron resonance gas to lattice QCD equation of state: 165 <= T <= 200 MeV
- Nucleon constituent width v and radial distribution width r are free parameters (no specific numerical values given in the truncated text)
- Number of constituents per nucleon n_c is a free parameter (illustrated with examples of 3 and 9 constituents in a schematic figure)

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian calibration studies of heavy-ion collisions (Bernhard 2016, Bernhard 2018) by simultaneously fitting p-Pb and Pb-Pb data to test the universality of hydrodynamic descriptions across system sizes. It builds on earlier substructure sensitivity studies (Welsh 2016, Bozek 2017) that identified cause-effect relationships but could not systematically constrain parameters, and on IP-Glasma calculations (Schenke 2018) that calibrated color charge fluctuations to HERA diffraction data. The results enable future global analyses that could incorporate additional collision systems and observables to further constrain the temperature dependence of QGP transport coefficients and the event-by-event structure of the proton.
