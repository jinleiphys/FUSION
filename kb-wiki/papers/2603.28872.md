# Quark-Mass Dependence of Light-Nuclei Masses from Lattice QCD and Trace-Anomaly Contributions to Nuclear Bindings

> We present lattice QCD calculations of the masses of the deuteron, dineutron, Helium-3 and Helium-4 with physical sea quarks and valence quark masses corresponding to pion masses between 140 and 700 MeV. At the physical point, the lowest finite-volume two-nucleon energy levels exhibit the qualitative pattern of a bound deuteron and an unbound dineutron within uncertainties, while at heavier quark masses they indicate the presence of deeply bound states. Compared with expectations from low-energy effective field theories, the observed mass dependence of the binding energies provides first-principles constraints on the quark-mass dependence of two- and three-nucleon interactions. From the quark-mass variation of the nuclear energies, we determine nuclear sigma terms and quantify the response of light-nuclear masses to changes in the light-quark mass. Using the QCD trace anomaly relation, we decompose the nuclear binding energy into quark-mass and gluonic contributions around the deuteron mass scale of $μ=2$ GeV. We find that the quark-mass contribution to the binding energy is small and approximately additive in nucleon number within current precision, whereas the gluonic component provides the dominant contribution and show milder increases with mass number.

## Key claim
The paper presents lattice QCD calculations of light-nuclei masses that, at the physical point, show the qualitative pattern of a bound deuteron and an unbound dineutron within uncertainties, while at heavier quark masses the systems become more deeply bound. The quark-mass contribution to nuclear binding energy is found to be small and approximately additive in nucleon number, whereas the gluonic component from the QCD trace anomaly provides the dominant contribution.

## Method
The authors used a 2+1-flavor HISQ gauge ensemble with physical sea quarks (140 MeV pion mass) on a 64^4 lattice with spacing a ≈ 0.076 fm, corresponding to a (5 fm)^3 volume. They employed a tree-level tadpole-improved Wilson-clover action for valence quarks at three kappa values corresponding to valence pion masses of 140, 295, and 695 MeV, and extracted finite-volume energy levels for the deuteron, dineutron, Helium-3, and Helium-4 using both standard multi-state exponential fits and the transfer-matrix generalized eigenvalue problem (TGEVP) procedure.

## Key numbers
- Valence pion masses used: 140(1) MeV, 295(1) MeV, and 695(1) MeV
- Nucleon masses at these pion masses: 932(5) MeV, 1045(3) MeV, and 1478(2) MeV respectively
- Lattice spacing: a ≈ 0.076 fm on a 64^4 lattice, giving a physical volume of approximately (5 fm)^3
- The lowest energy levels for two-nucleon systems at heavier pion masses lie within ~10 MeV of the two-nucleon threshold
- The nuclear sigma term ratio sigma_A/sigma_N was determined from linear fits of E_A(m_N) as a function of m_N, using a lattice-QCD based sigma_N value of 43.7 MeV
- The trace-anomaly decomposition was performed at the MS-bar scale μ = 2 GeV

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of multi-nucleon systems (Beane 2010, NPLQCD 2013, CalLat 2017, Detmold 2015) by using physical-mass sea quarks rather than heavy sea quarks matched to the valence mass, which was the standard approach in earlier studies. The results provide first-principles constraints on the quark-mass dependence of two- and three-nucleon interactions that can be compared with low-energy effective field theories such as pionless EFT (Kaplan 1996, Bedaque 1999) and chiral EFT (Weinberg-type). The trace-anomaly decomposition of nuclear binding into quark-mass and gluonic components (Hatta 2018, Tanaka 2018) enables a direct connection between nuclear binding and its QCD origin, and the observed A-dependence suggests that the increase of nuclear binding with mass number is driven primarily by gluonic many-body dynamics.
