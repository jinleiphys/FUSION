# An estimate of the prehadron production time

> A semi-quantitative estimate of the prehadron production time based on recent preliminary HERMES data on hadron transverse momentum broadening in nuclear DIS is presented. The obtained production time can well explain the data except for their dependence on the photon virtuality, which remains a challenge to current theoretical models. A few mechanisms that may contribute to its explanation are suggested, along with possible experimental tests. The average time scale at HERMES is found to be of the order of 4-5 fm, comparable to heavy nuclei radii, so that by suitable kinematic cuts it will be possible to study both the case in which hadronization starts inside and the case in which it starts outside the nucleus. A comparison with recent CLAS preliminary data, which qualitatively but not quantitatively agree with HERMES, is performed.

## Key claim

The prehadron production time extracted from HERMES data can explain the dependence of transverse momentum broadening on atomic number, hadron fractional energy, and virtual photon energy, but the observed linear increase of broadening with photon virtuality Q^2 contradicts current theoretical models and remains unexplained.

## Method

The author analyzed preliminary HERMES data on hadron transverse momentum broadening in deep inelastic scattering on nuclear targets, using a scaling variable tau = C * z^lambda * (1-z) * nu derived from the Lund string model, where the scaling exponent lambda = 0.5 was determined from a previous analysis of hadron multiplicity ratios. The production time was estimated by matching the scaling variable to the average in-medium path length of the hadronizing system in xenon, yielding a proportionality constant C = 0.8 fm/GeV. The resulting production times were computed for each experimental bin and compared to the observed dependences on A, z, nu, and Q^2.

## Key numbers

- Average prehadron production time at HERMES: 4-5 fm, comparable to heavy nuclei radii
- Scaling exponent lambda = 0.5 from pion multiplicity ratio analysis
- Proportionality constant C = 0.8 fm/GeV, estimated from xenon average path length L_Xe = 4.3 fm at z = 0.4 and nu = 14 GeV
- Production time formula: t_p = 0.8 * z^0.5 * (1-z) * nu fm/GeV (nu in GeV)
- HERMES beam energy: 27 GeV; CLAS beam energy: 5 GeV
- HERMES Q^2 range: 2-10 GeV^2; CLAS Q^2 range: 1-4 GeV^2
- CLAS saturation of p_T broadening appears at nu > 4 GeV
- CLAS prehadron production time t_p > 4.3 fm (from iron target saturation)
- Ratio t_p(HERMES) / t_p(CLAS) approximately 0.2

## Context

This work extends the scaling analysis of hadron multiplicity ratios by Accardi (2006) to directly estimate the prehadron production time from transverse momentum broadening data. The results contradict the color dipole model prediction by Kopeliovich (2003) that broadening decreases as 1/Q^2, and also disagree with string-based models that predict flat Q^2 dependence. The analysis enables future experimental tests at the proposed Electron-Ion Collider to distinguish between three proposed mechanisms: medium-enhanced DGLAP evolution, next-to-leading order photon-gluon fusion processes, and colored prehadrons with short formation times.
