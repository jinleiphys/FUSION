# Nuclear Physics and the European Particle Physics Strategy Update 2026

> This document provides input to the update of the European Strategy for Particle Physics in fields that are related to Nuclear Physics as described in the NuPECC Long Range Plan 2024 (arXiv:2503.15575).

## Key claim

This document provides input to the 2026 European Strategy for Particle Physics Update from the nuclear physics perspective, as described in the NuPECC Long Range Plan 2024, but it does not present new experimental results or original research; rather, it summarizes existing priorities and recommends continued support for a diverse, multi-site approach to nuclear physics facilities across Europe.

## Method

The NuPECC Working Group compiled a summary of nuclear physics priorities from the NuPECC Long Range Plan 2024, organized into six topical sections: hadron physics, strongly interacting matter, nuclear structure and reaction dynamics, nuclear astrophysics, symmetries and fundamental interactions, and applications and societal benefits. The document identifies existing and planned European facilities relevant to each topic and formulates recommendations for the European Strategy for Particle Physics Update, emphasizing the complementarity between collider and non-collider experiments.

## Key numbers

* Approximately 2000 users currently work on ultrarelativistic heavy-ion physics at CERN, a community that would face a gap of several decades from the shutdown of HiLumi-LHC (year 2041) until FCC-hh could become operational (around 2065) if FCC-ee is chosen as the first phase.
* The proposed NA60+ experiment at CERN's SPS aims to achieve 3-5% accuracy on rare lepton decays utilizing its high-rate capabilities.
* The ALICE-3 detector upgrade is proposed for an extended Run 5 of 6 years, exploiting super-thin, flexible, stitched Si detectors in a new superconducting toroidal magnet.

## Context

This paper serves as the nuclear physics community's input to the European Strategy for Particle Physics Update 2026, extending the NuPECC Long Range Plan 2024 (arXiv:2503.15575) into the particle physics strategy process. It emphasizes the interconnectedness of nuclear, particle, and astroparticle physics communities represented by NuPECC, ECFA, and APPEC respectively, and argues that a diverse physics program beyond the HL-LHC is crucial for addressing fundamental questions. The document enables future strategy discussions by identifying specific facility priorities (FAIR, SPIRAL2, SPES, ELI-NP, ISOL@MYRRHA, ISOLDE upgrades) and highlighting the potential impact of the FCC decision on the heavy-ion physics community.
