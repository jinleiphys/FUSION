# Stability of parton distributions at high $x$: impact of nuclear and power corrections

> We present a comprehensive new global QCD analysis of unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) based upon proton, deuteron and $A\!=\!3$ data, including the latest inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) measurements from Jefferson Lab at high Bjorken-$x$. Using the JAM Bayesian Monte Carlo framework, we systematically explore the stability of the PDFs with respect to variations in the cuts on the invariant mass $W$ of the DIS final state, the implementation of target mass and higher twist corrections, as well as on the nuclear wave functions for the $A\!=\!2$ and 3 data. We find the $u$ and $d$ quark PDFs (and the $d/u$ ratio) are relatively stable up to $x \approx 0.8$, and able to describe DIS data down to $W^2=3.5$ GeV$^2$ and $Q^2=m_c^2$. Within the collinear factorization framework, the fitted higher twist corrections to DIS are found to be positive, and largely isospin independent. The description of the nuclear data also requires nonzero isoscalar and isovector nucleon off-shell PDF contributions, which gives specific predictions for the ratio, $R_D$, of deuteron to isoscalar nucleon structure functions.

## Key claim

The u and d quark PDFs and the d/u ratio are relatively stable up to x ≈ 0.8 when varying the invariant mass cut, target mass corrections, higher twist treatments, and nuclear wave functions, but this stability is conditional on including both isoscalar and isovector nucleon off-shell PDF contributions and using an additive higher twist parameterization within the JAM Bayesian Monte Carlo framework.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis at next-to-leading order using the JAM Bayesian Monte Carlo framework, fitting unpolarized PDFs to inclusive deep-inelastic scattering data from proton, deuteron, and A=3 (³He and ³H) targets, including recent Jefferson Lab high-x measurements. They systematically varied the invariant mass cut on DIS data down to W² = 3.5 GeV², implemented target mass corrections using the collinear factorization approach, and fitted additive higher twist corrections parameterized as H(x)/Q². Nuclear effects were treated via the impulse approximation with on-shell and off-shell nucleon smearing functions, using multiple deuteron wave functions (Paris, AV18, CD-Bonn, WJC1, WJC2) and A=3 spectral functions (KPSV, SS), with off-shell PDFs decomposed into isoscalar and isovector contributions.

## Key numbers

- The analysis describes DIS data down to W² = 3.5 GeV² and Q² = m_c² (1.3 GeV²), extending the kinematic reach compared to the standard W² > 15 GeV² cut
- For the deuteron, the ratio of off-shell to on-shell smearing functions integrated over y ranges from -3.7% to -6.1% depending on the wave function, with an average of -4.7%
- For ³He, the off-shell smearing function contributions average -6.2% (KPSV: -6.8%, SS: -5.6%)
- For ³H, the off-shell smearing function contributions average -8.8% (KPSV: -9.5%, SS: -8.0%)
- The PDFs are found to be stable up to x ≈ 0.8

## Context

This work extends previous JAM analyses (Cocuzza et al., 2021-2026) by systematically studying PDF stability under variations of kinematic cuts and theoretical corrections, addressing conflicting conclusions between Cerutti et al. (2025) and Alekhin et al. (2022-2024) regarding whether additive or multiplicative higher twist parameterizations bias the neutron-to-proton structure function ratio. The analysis also builds on the MSHT group's study (Harland-Lang et al., 2025) of target mass corrections and higher twists with approximate N³LO corrections. The results enable reliable PDF extraction at high x for applications such as interpreting the CDF W-boson mass anomaly and planning future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider.
