# Extraction of the D13(1520) photon-decay couplings from pion- and eta-photoproduction data

> We compare results for the D13(1520) photon-decay amplitudes determined in analyses of eta- and pion-photoproduction data. The ratio of helicity amplitudes (A_3/2 / A_1/2), determined from eta-photoproduction data, is quite different from that determined in previous analyses of pion-photoproduction data. We consider how strongly the existing pion-photoproduction data constrain both this ratio and the individual photon-decay amplitudes.

# Extraction of the D13(1520) photon-decay couplings from pion- and eta-photoproduction data

## Key claim
The ratio of helicity amplitudes A₃/₂ / A₁/₂ for the D₁₃(1520) resonance, as extracted from eta-photoproduction data (approximately -2.5), is several standard deviations smaller than the PDG estimate of -6.9 ± 2.1 from pion-photoproduction analyses; however, the existing pion-photoproduction data do not entirely exclude the eta-photoproduction result, provided only a qualitative (not a χ²) description is required.

## Method
The authors performed a single-energy analysis of pion-photoproduction data centered at a lab photon energy of 760 MeV (near the D₁₃(1520) resonance position) and tested the effect of shifting the E₂₋¹/² and M₂₋¹/² multipoles to reproduce the multipole ratio implied by the eta-photoproduction result (E₂₋¹/² / M₂₋¹/² ≈ 1.4, corresponding to A₃/₂ / A₁/₂ ≈ -2.5). They assumed resonance dominance in both eta- and pion-photoproduction multipoles and examined the sensitivity of cross sections and polarization observables to these changes, without performing a full fit.

## Key numbers
- The ratio A₃/₂ / A₁/₂ from eta-photoproduction data is reported as -2.5 ± 0.2 ± 0.4 (Mukhopadhyay et al.) or -2.1 ± 0.2 (Mainz group).
- The PDG estimate for the same ratio from pion-photoproduction analyses is -6.9 ± 2.1.
- The ratio E₂₋¹/² / M₂₋¹/₂ corresponding to A₃/₂ / A₁/₂ = -2.5 is approximately 1.4.
- A representative analysis of pion-photoproduction data (SM99) gives a ratio of multipoles (imaginary parts) of about 2.1 at the resonance energy.
- Increasing M₂₋¹/² by 15% while fixing the E₂₋¹/² / M₂₋¹/² ratio at 1.4 was tested against the pion-photoproduction data.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Mukhopadhyay et al. and the Mainz group, which found a much smaller A₃/₂ / A₁/₂ ratio from eta-photoproduction than from pion-photoproduction analyses. It also relates to coupled-channel approaches by Dytman et al. and Feuster et al., which found intermediate values. The results suggest that precise measurements of cross sections and polarization observables at backward angles, particularly for pion photoproduction, are needed to resolve the discrepancy and better constrain the D₁₃(1520) photon-decay amplitudes.
