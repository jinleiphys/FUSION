# Evolution from few- to many-body physics in one-dimensional Fermi systems: One- and two-body density matrices, and particle-partition entanglement

> We study the evolution from few- to many-body physics of fermionic systems in one spatial dimension with attractive pairwise interactions. We determine the detailed form of the momentum distribution, the structure of the one-body density matrix, and the pairing properties encoded in the two-body density matrix. From the low- and high-momentum scaling behavior of the single-particle momentum distribution we estimate the speed of sound and Tan's contact, respectively. Both quantities are found to be in agreement with previous calculations. Based on our calculations of the one-body density matrices, we also present results for the particle-partition entanglement entropy, for which we find a logarithmic dependence on the total particle number.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods can accurately compute one- and two-body density matrices and particle-partition entanglement entropy for one-dimensional Fermi gases across the crossover from few- to many-body physics, with the caveat that translation invariance is broken for systems with an even number of fermions per spin component in a periodic box, though this violation weakens as particle number increases.

## Method

The authors use a lattice QMC approach with an auxiliary field transformation to decouple spin species in the density channel, applied to the Gaudin-Yang model of two-component fermions in one dimension with attractive contact interactions. They perform ground-state calculations on a discretized Euclidean spacetime with \(N_x = 80\) spatial lattice sites, using Hybrid Monte Carlo to project out the ground state from a guess wave function of fixed particle number, averaging approximately 5000 decorrelated samples. From the computed one-body density matrices, they extract the momentum distribution, estimate the speed of sound from low-momentum scaling behavior and Tan's contact from high-momentum scaling behavior, and compute the particle-partition entanglement entropy.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy per pair in the strong-coupling limit: \(E/N_{\text{pairs}} = -g^2/4\), corresponding to \(E_{1+1}/E_{\text{F}} = -3\gamma^2/\pi^2\) for the two-body problem
* Sound velocity ratio \(v_s/v_F\) varies from 1 at \(\gamma=0\) (free gas) to 0.5 in the strong-coupling limit \(1/\gamma \to 0\), with \(\eta\) varying from 2 to 4
* Tan's contact density \(C/(L k_F^4)\) for \(N=2+2\) fermions: 0.003(1) at \(\gamma=0.2\), 0.36(2) at \(\gamma=2.0\), 2.3(3) at \(\gamma=4.0\)
* Tan's contact density \(C/(L k_F^4)\) for \(N=4+4\) fermions: 0.0013(2) at \(\gamma=0.2\), 0.330(5) at \(\gamma=2.0\), 2.17(9) at \(\gamma=4.0\)
* Tan's contact density \(C/(L k_F^4)\) for \(N=6+6\) fermions: 0.0012(4) at \(\gamma=0.2\), 0.33(1) at \(\gamma=2.0\), 2.20(8) at \(\gamma=4.0\)
* The crossover from Cooper pairing to tightly bound bosonic molecules occurs at \(\gamma \sim 2\), where the bound state diameter \(d_0 n = 2/\gamma\) becomes comparable to interparticle spacing

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QMC studies of one-dimensional Fermi gases by Rammelmuller et al. (GCS1D) and Loheac (2015) by computing the full one- and two-body density matrices and particle-partition entanglement entropy, rather than just the equation of state. The results agree with Bethe ansatz solutions for the ground-state energy and with prior calculations of Tan's contact from GCS1D using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem. The study enables future quantitative investigations of one- and two-body density matrices for Fermi gases in higher dimensions, where exact analytic approaches like the Bethe ansatz do not generalize straightforwardly, and provides benchmarks for studying inhomogeneous ground states in spin- and mass-imbalanced systems.
