# Cooper quartet correlations in infinite symmetric nuclear matter

> We investigate the quartet correlations in four-component fermionic systems at the thermodynamic limit within a variational many-body theory. The Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS)-type variational wave function is extended to the systems with the coexistence of pair and quartet correlations at zero temperature. Special attention is paid to the application of the present framework to an alpha-particle condensation in symmetric nuclear matter, where the coexistence of deuteron and alpha condensations is anticipated. We also discuss how physical properties, such as quasiparticle dispersion, can be modified by the pair and quartet correlations and show a hierarchical structure of in-medium cluster formations in infinite nuclear matter. The present results may also contribute to the interdisciplinary understanding of fermionic condensations beyond the BCS paradigm in many-body systems.

## Key claim

The paper extends the BCS-type variational wave function to include both pair and quartet (alpha-like) correlations in symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, showing that quartet correlations modify the quasiparticle dispersion and produce a hierarchical structure of in-medium cluster formations, but the analysis is limited to a simplified case where the quartet creation operator is approximated by taking equal relative momenta for the four nucleons.

## Method

The authors construct a variational many-body wave function in momentum space that coherently superposes neutron-proton Cooper pairs (isoscalar and isovector channels) and alpha-like Cooper quartets, with all clusters having zero center-of-mass momentum. They derive the expectation value of the Hamiltonian, define energy gaps for isoscalar and isovector pairing, and obtain coupled variational equations for the parameters describing pair and quartet correlations. The number densities and condensate fractions for both pairing and quartetting are computed from the variational parameters.

## Key numbers

* Alpha particle binding energy: \(E_\alpha = 28.29\) MeV, given as context for the stability of four-body clusters
* The total nucleon density is given by \(n = \sum_{\bm{q}} \left(2|\bm{v}_{\bm{q}}|^2 + 2|\bm{x}_{\bm{q}}|^2 + 4|w_{\bm{q}}|^2\right)\), where \(\bm{v}_{\bm{q}}\), \(\bm{x}_{\bm{q}}\), and \(w_{\bm{q}}\) are variational parameters for isoscalar pairs, isovector pairs, and quartets respectively
* The quartet condensate fraction is \(f_{\rm quartet} = \sum_{\bm{q}} u_{\bm{q}}^2 w_{\bm{q}}^2\), where \(u_{\bm{q}}\) and \(w_{\bm{q}}\) are variational parameters
* The pair condensate fraction is \(f_{\rm pair} = \sum_{\bm{q}}\sum_{T_3} |u^\ast_{\bm{q}}x_{\bm{q},T_3} + \delta_{T_3,0}x^\ast_{\bm{q},T_3}w_{\bm{q}}|^2 + \sum_{\bm{q}}\sum_{S_z} |u^\ast_{\bm{q}}v_{\bm{q},S_z} - (-1)^{S_z}v^\ast_{\bm{q},S_z}w_{\bm{q}}|^2\)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of alpha-particle condensation in infinite nuclear matter by Ropke, Schuck, Funaki, and Sogo (various years) from finite-temperature treatments to a zero-temperature variational framework. It also connects to the BCS-BEC crossover studies for neutron-proton pairing in symmetric nuclear matter by Lombardo, Huang, Jin, and Stein (various years), and to quartet correlation studies in finite nuclei by Senkov, Sandulescu, and Sambataro (various years). The framework enables future investigation of condensation fractions with more realistic nuclear interactions and may apply to other four-component fermionic systems such as biexciton condensation and SU(4) Fermi atomic gases.
