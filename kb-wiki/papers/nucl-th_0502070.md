# Microscopic models for exotic nuclei

> Starting from successful self-consistent mean-field models, this paper discusses why and how to go beyond the mean field approximation. To include long-range correlations from fluctuations in collective degrees of freedom, one has to consider symmetry restoration and configuration mixing, which give access to ground-state correlations and spectroscopy.

## Key claim

Starting from self-consistent mean-field models, the paper argues that to include long-range correlations from fluctuations in collective degrees of freedom one must go beyond the mean field by restoring broken symmetries and mixing configurations, but the authors hedge that on a quantitative level neither masses nor excitation spectra are yet described with the desired precision, likely due to imperfections in the effective interactions and remaining deficiencies in the modeling of configuration mixing.

## Method

The authors perform particle-number and angular-momentum projection on HF+BCS or HFB states with axial, time-reversal, and parity symmetry, then mix these projected states with different axial quadrupole deformations using the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) to solve the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation. For mass systematics, they approximate the full method using the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA) to estimate integrals and construct the Hill-Wheeler matrices from diagonal and nearest-neighbor matrix elements. The Skyrme interactions SLy4 or SLy6 are used for the particle-hole channel with a density-dependent delta pairing interaction for the particle-particle channel.

## Key numbers

- For $^{240}$Pu, projection gives about 3 MeV additional binding for the ground state and about 4 MeV for the fission isomer, with the outer barrier lowered by as much as 2 MeV.
- For $^{240}$Pu, the mean-field ground state deformation is $\beta_2 = 0.29$, the transitional quadrupole deformation $\beta_2^{(t)}(J+2 \to J) = 0.30$ for all $E2$ transitions within the ground-state band, and the spectroscopic quadrupole moments give $\beta_2^{(s)}(J) = 0.30$, in agreement with the experimental value of 0.29 deduced from $B(E2; 0^+_1 \to 2^+_1)$.
- For $^{188}$Pb, the difference between mean-field and projected $J=0$ states (rotational energy) increases rapidly to about 3 MeV at small $\beta_2$, then grows at a slower rate with deformation.
- The accuracy of the Gaussian overlap approximation is better than 300 keV, sufficient for studying quadrupole correlation energies which are an order of magnitude larger.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent mean-field models (reviewed in a 2003 review by Bender, Heenen, and Reinhard) by adding symmetry restoration and configuration mixing to describe ground-state correlations and spectroscopy in exotic nuclei. The method is applied to neutron-deficient Pb isotopes (building on work by Duguet (2003) and Bender (2004)) and to well-deformed nuclei like $^{240}$Pu (Bender 2004), and the mass systematics improve on mean-field mass formulas (such as those by Tondeur (2000)) by consistently calculating quadrupole correlation energies. The approach enables future work including additional collective modes (pairing vibrations, triaxial deformations, octupole vibrations) and generalization to cranked states for describing odd-mass nuclei.
