# On the kinematic limit of the charm production in fixed-target experiments with the intrinsic charm from the target

> In this note we provide a detailed derivation of the kinematic limit of the charm production in fixed-target experiments with the intrinsic charm coming from the target. In addition, we discuss the first measurement of charm quark production in the fixed-target configuration at the LHC.

# On the kinematic limit of the charm production in fixed-target experiments with the intrinsic charm from the target

## Key claim
The LHCb/SMOG measurement of charm production in fixed-target configuration, which reported no evidence for substantial intrinsic charm content of the nucleon, is based on a misunderstanding of the kinematics of intrinsic charm from the target. The LHCb analysis was performed outside the signal region, because the kinematic limit for charm produced by the intrinsic charm mechanism from the target in the laboratory frame is rapidity y < 0.6 and longitudinal momentum p_L ~ 1.2-1.3 GeV/c, whereas the LHCb acceptance covers rapidity 2 < y < 4.6.

## Method
The authors derive the kinematic limit for charm production from intrinsic charm in the target by calculating the center-of-mass energy from the laboratory frame quantities, then applying Lorentz transformations to obtain the four-momentum of a charmed particle (here J/psi) in the laboratory frame when it takes a fraction x of the three-momentum from the target. Expanding for large three-momenta relative to masses and taking x < 0 (target side) yields maximum energy and momentum expressions that depend only on the two masses involved, not on the beam energy.

## Key numbers
- The kinematic limit for charm produced by intrinsic charm from the target in the laboratory frame: rapidity y < 0.6 and longitudinal momentum p_L ~ 1.2-1.3 GeV/c.
- LHCb/SMOG measured J/psi and D0 production in pHe collisions at nucleon-nucleon centre-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 86.6 GeV and in pAr collisions at sqrt(s) = 110.4 GeV.
- The J/psi production cross-section in pHe collisions in rapidity range [2,4.6] is sigma(J/psi) = 652 +/- 33 +/- 42 nb per nucleon (statistical and systematic uncertainties).
- The D0 production cross-section in pHe collisions in rapidity range [2,4.6] is sigma(D0) = 80.8 +/- 2.4 +/- 6.3 microbarn per nucleon (statistical and systematic uncertainties).
- The LHCb acceptance gives access to Bjorken-x up to x ~ 0.37 for D0 mesons.
- The c quark mass used in the LHCb analysis is m_c = 1.28 GeV/c^2.

## Context
This work extends the intrinsic charm model proposed by Brodsky (2016) and Brodsky (2017), which predicted that charm hadrons produced from intrinsic charm in the target have maximum momentum of only a few GeV/c and rapidity y < 2. The authors previously investigated production of double charmed baryons and J/psi mesons from the target at STAR and LHCb/SMOG fixed-target programs (Groote 2017, Koshkarev 2017). The present note shows that the LHCb/SMOG result (Aaij 2018) contradicts the properties of the intrinsic charm model because the measurement was performed outside the kinematic region where intrinsic charm from the target can appear. This implies that investigating intrinsic charm from the target at the LHCb/SMOG fixed-target program is fundamentally unfeasible.
