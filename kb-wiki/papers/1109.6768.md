# Tuning of the GEANT4 FRITIOF (FTF) Model Using NA61/SHINE Experimental Data

> The NA61/SHINE collaboration measured inclusive cross sections of π^+ and π^- meson production in the interactions of 31 GeV/c protons with carbon nuclei at forward emission angles (0 - 420 mrad). The collaboration also presented predictions of Monte Carlo models - FLUKA, VENUS and UrQMD, in comparison with the data. A careful analysis shows that deviations of the FLUKA and VENUS predictions from the data have different tendencies. The worst description of the data was observed for the UrQMD model results. All the models assume the creation of quark-gluon strings in the interactions, but it is complicated to analyze the models in order to find the source of the deviations. Thus, the quark-gluon string model - FRITIOF (FTF) - was implemented in the GEANT4 toolkit and is used to understand the deviations mentioned above. It was found that the most important factor influencing the FTF calculations is the sampling of quark-gluon string masses. The other factors/parameters are not essential for a description of the data. Also, a good description of the data is achieved by the FTF model.

## Key claim

The GEANT4 FRITIOF (FTF) model can achieve a good description of NA61/SHINE data on pion production in 31 GeV/c proton-carbon interactions, but only after replacing the default 1/P light-cone momentum distribution with a flat distribution in double diffraction dissociation processes; the most important factor influencing the FTF calculations is the sampling of quark-gluon string masses.

## Method

The author implemented the quark-gluon string model FRITIOF (FTF) in the GEANT4 toolkit and compared its predictions to NA61/SHINE inclusive cross sections of π⁺ and π⁻ meson production from 31 GeV/c protons on carbon at forward angles (0–420 mrad). The model was tuned by varying the transferred transverse momentum, the single diffraction probability, and the form of the light-cone momentum distribution used to sample string masses in double diffraction dissociation. The best agreement was found using a flat P distribution (dW ∝ dP) instead of the original 1/P distribution for double diffraction processes.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum: 31 GeV/c protons on carbon nuclei.
- Forward emission angles covered: 0–420 mrad.
- Mean squared transferred transverse momentum: ⟨P_T²⟩ = 0.15 (GeV/c)².
- String mass sampling threshold: m_th = m₀ + 280 MeV.
- Single diffraction probability formula parameters: D = 0.805, d = 0.35, with W_sd = D exp(-d Y_lab).
- Double diffraction probability for nucleon-nucleon interactions: W_dd = 1 – 2 W_sd.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of Monte Carlo models (FLUKA, VENUS, UrQMD) to NA61/SHINE data by implementing and tuning the FTF model within GEANT4. The author finds that FLUKA likely overestimates string masses while VENUS underestimates them, and that the NA61/SHINE data are uniquely suited to constrain string mass distributions. The tuned FTF model enables improved simulations of hadron-nucleus interactions for nuclear physics and particle transport applications, and the author recommends extending measurements to higher statistics and to K-meson and proton spectra.
