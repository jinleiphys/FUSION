# Spectator charge splitting of directed flow in heavy ion collisions

> We estimate the effect of the spectator charge on distortion of single charged pion distributions as well as on azimuthal anisotropies in heavy ion collisions. A large electromagnetic effect on directed flow $v_1$ is predicted in good agreement with existing WA98 as well as RHIC data. This effect results in a splitting of $v_1$ for positive and negative pions. Detailed analysis of this phenomenon may provide new information on the collision dynamics.

# Spectator charge splitting of directed flow in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The electromagnetic fields generated by charged, fast-moving spectator remnants in noncentral heavy ion collisions produce a measurable splitting of directed flow $v_1$ between positive and negative pions, and this electromagnetic contribution can be isolated by subtracting the average directed flow of both charges; the effect is sensitive to the pion emission time and can be used to extract this timescale from experimental data.

## Method

The authors model a peripheral heavy ion collision by treating the two spectator systems as point charges moving at constant velocity along their initial trajectories, while the participant region evolves until pions are produced at a specified emission time $t_E$. Charged pion trajectories are then evolved under the electromagnetic fields of both spectators, and the resulting azimuthal distributions are Fourier-analyzed to obtain the electromagnetic component of directed flow $v_1^{\pi,EM}$, which is added to the strong-interaction flow component $v_1^{flow}$ to obtain the final observed $v_1$.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3$ GeV, the electromagnetic effect on directed flow is shown for pion transverse momenta $p_T = 75$, 125, and 175 MeV/c, with emission times $t_E = 0$ and 1 fm/c.
- The best fit to STAR data for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV yields an average pion production distance of about 3 fm from the spectator system.
- The $\pi^+/\pi^-$ ratio in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions shows a dip in $\pi^+$ density and an accumulation of $\pi^-$ density at pion $x_F \approx 0.15$, demonstrated for $p_T$ values of 25, 75, 125, 175, and 325 MeV/c.
- WA98 experimental data on directed flow of positive pions are compared to simulations assuming three values of pion emission time: $t_E = 0$, 0.5, and 1 fm/c, with results integrated over $p_T$ from 0 to 1 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier finding (Szczurek and Rybicki, 2007) that electromagnetic fields from spectators distort charged pion spectra, which was confirmed by NA49 data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3$ GeV (Rybicki, 2011). The present study adds the directed flow observable and demonstrates that the electromagnetic splitting of $v_1$ for $\pi^+$ and $\pi^-$ is consistent with WA98 and STAR data, providing a new tool to extract the pion emission time and complement information from HBT measurements. The approach contrasts with purely hydrodynamic descriptions of directed flow (such as the tilted initial condition model of Bozek and Wyskiel) by isolating an electromagnetic contribution that is opposite for opposite charges.
