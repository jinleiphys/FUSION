# Mechanisms of multiparticle production in heavy ion collisions at high energy

> In the framework of a microscopic string model inclusive charged particle distribution and baryon and antibaryon production are described. The emphasis is put on high energies (RHIC) where shadowing corrections play a crucial role. Some recent developments on $J/ψ$ suppression at CERN-SPS are also discussed. Possible consequences for the crucial issue of thermal equilibration of the produced system are considered.

## Key claim

Within the Dual Parton Model (DPM) framework, inclusive charged particle distributions and baryon/antibaryon production at RHIC energies can be described using the same mechanisms and parameter values as in pp interactions, provided that shadowing corrections (computed from high-mass diffraction with essentially no new free parameters) are included; the author argues that contrary to some claims, there is no evidence for "anomalous" stopping in heavy ion data, and that thermal equilibration of the produced system appears improbable given the short interaction times and small required cross-sections.

## Method

The author applies the Dual Parton Model (DPM) and Quark Gluon String Model (QGSM), which are based on the large-N expansion of non-perturbative QCD and Gribov's Reggeon Field Theory, to compute inclusive particle spectra in nucleus-nucleus collisions. Shadowing corrections are incorporated via triple-Pomeron contributions computed from measured diffractive cross-sections, and final-state interactions between comoving hadrons are treated using gain-and-loss differential equations with cylindrical space-time variables and boost invariance. The model uses universal momentum distribution and fragmentation functions determined from known Regge intercepts, with weights for different graphs obtained from perturbative Reggeon Field Theory fitted to total and elastic cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- Average number of inelastic collisions k = 1.4 at SPS energies, k = 2 at sqrt(s) = 130 GeV, and k = 2.2 at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV at RHIC
- For central Au Au collisions at y* ~ 0, R_AA = A^{1.13-4/3} = 0.34 when numerator and denominator are integrated over p_T
- The A-dependence of dN^{AA}/dy at y* ~ 0 gives alpha ~ 1.08 between 2 and 370 participants at SPS, alpha ~ 1.13 at RHIC, and alpha ~ 1.1 predicted at LHC
- Final-state interaction cross-sections required to describe the data are sigma = 0.2 mb for all reactions, with interaction time of 5 to 7 fm
- Net baryon production uses a = 0.4, c = 1, alpha_q = 1/2, and alpha_qq = -1 as parameters from pp fits
- Saturation scale for most central collisions is 260 MeV with Lambda_QCD = 200 MeV in the saturation model comparison

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis at CERN-SPS energies (Capella, 2003) to higher RHIC energies where shadowing corrections become crucial, and provides a comparison with the saturation model approach of Kharzeev et al. (2003). The paper demonstrates that DPM can describe RHIC data on charged particle multiplicities, net baryon production, and hyperon yields without invoking anomalous stopping or thermal equilibrium, contrary to claims by several authors. The results enable predictions for LHC energies and for Omega and anti-Omega production at RHIC, while the analysis of J/psi suppression at CERN-SPS using the comovers approach provides expectations for RHIC measurements.
