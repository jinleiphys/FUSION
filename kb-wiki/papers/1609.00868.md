# Advances in solving the two-fermion homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space

> Actual solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for a two-fermion bound system are becoming available directly in Minkowski space, by virtue of a novel technique, based on the so-called Nakanishi integral representation of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and improved by expressing the relevant momenta through light-front components, i.e. $k^\pm=k^0 \pm k^3$. We solve a crucial problem that widens the applicability of the method to real situations by providing an analytically exact treatment of the singularities plaguing the two-fermion problem in Minkowski space, irrespective of the complexity of the irreducible Bethe-Salpeter kernel. This paves the way for feasible numerical investigations of relativistic composite systems, with any spin degrees of freedom. We present a thorough comparison with existing numerical results, evaluated in both Minkowski and Euclidean space, fully corroborating our analytical treatment, as well as fresh light-front amplitudes illustrating the potentiality of non perturbative calculations performed directly in Minkowski space.

# Advances in solving the two-fermion homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space

## Key claim

The authors provide an analytically exact treatment of the singularities that arise when solving the two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space using the Nakanishi integral representation, overcoming a limitation that previously required either Euclidean-space calculations or numerical smoothing of singularities. The method works irrespective of the complexity of the irreducible Bethe-Salpeter kernel, and the highest power of the singular term depends only on the external propagators and the structure of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, not on the kernel complexity.

## Method

The authors decompose the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for a two-fermion system in a J^pi = 0^+ state into four scalar functions, each expressed via the Nakanishi integral representation with n=3. They then project the coupled integral equations onto light-front coordinates, integrate over k^- analytically, and isolate the singular contributions that arise when k^+_D = 0. The singular integrals are evaluated using a known identity for integrals of the form 1/(beta x - y +/- i epsilon)^2, yielding terms proportional to delta(z' - z) and its derivative, which are handled by expanding the Nakanishi weight functions on a basis of Laguerre and Gegenbauer polynomials and integrating the derivative of the delta function by parts.

## Key numbers

* For scalar coupling with mu/m = 0.15 and binding energy B/m = 0.01, the squared coupling constant g^2 = 7.844 (full analytical treatment) compared to 7.813 from Carbonell-Karmanov (2010).
* For scalar coupling with mu/m = 0.50 and B/m = 0.04, g^2 = 36.183 (full analytical treatment) compared to 36.19 from Carbonell-Karmanov (2010) and 36.19 from Euclidean-space results (Dork).
* For pseudoscalar coupling with mu/m = 0.15 and B/m = 0.01, g^2 = 225.7 (full analytical treatment) compared to 224.8 from Carbonell-Karmanov (2010).
* For pseudoscalar coupling with mu/m = 0.50 and B/m = 0.50, g^2 = 525.8 (full analytical treatment) compared to 525.9 from Carbonell-Karmanov (2010).
* The numerical basis used up to 44 Laguerre polynomials and 44 Gegenbauer polynomials, with 120 Gaussian points (180 for B/m = 0.01) and epsilon = 10^{-7}.
* Calculations were extended up to B/m ~ 2, where the critical behavior of a phi^3 theory manifests itself (partial derivative B / partial derivative g^2 approaches infinity).

## Context

This work extends the Nakanishi integral representation method previously applied to two-scalar systems (Kusaka; Carbonell-Karmanov 2006; Frederico-Salme-Viviani; Tomio 2016) to the two-fermion case, solving the singularity problem that Carbonell and Karmanov (2010) had addressed only through a numerical smoothing function. The results agree with both the Minkowski-space calculations of Carbonell-Karmanov (2010) and Euclidean-space results (Dork), validating the analytical treatment. The method enables non-perturbative calculations of light-front amplitudes and momentum distributions directly in Minkowski space for relativistic composite systems with any spin degrees of freedom, and can be extended to more complex kernels beyond the ladder approximation.
