# Partial wave contributions to the antikaon potential at finite momentum

> The momentum dependence of the antikaon optical potential in nuclear matter is obtained from a microscopic and self-consistent calculation using the meson-exchange Jülich $\bar{K}N$ interaction. Two self-consistent schemes are discussed, which would lead to substantially different predictions for the width of ${\bar K}$ nuclear bound states. The effect of higher partial waves of the $\bar{K}N$ interaction, beyond the L=0 component, is studied and found to have moderate but non-negligible effects on the ${\bar K}$ nuclear potential at zero momentum. At momenta as large as 500 MeV/c, relevant in the analysis of heavy-ion collisions, the higher partial partial waves modify the ${\bar K}$ optical potential by nearly a factor of two.

## Key claim

The momentum dependence of the antikaon optical potential in nuclear matter is obtained from a microscopic and self-consistent calculation using the meson-exchange Julich $\bar{K}N$ interaction, and the effect of higher partial waves beyond $L=0$ is found to be moderate but non-negligible at zero momentum, while at momenta as large as 500 MeV/c the higher partial waves modify the optical potential by nearly a factor of two.

## Method

The authors performed a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation of the $\bar{K}$ single-particle potential in symmetric nuclear matter, solving a coupled-channel $G$-matrix equation using the meson-exchange Julich $\bar{K}N$ interaction. Two self-consistent schemes were compared: one retaining only the real part of the $\bar{K}$ potential in the $G$-matrix intermediate states, and one using the full complex $\bar{K}$ self-energy. The $G$-matrix was decomposed into partial waves ($L$, $J$, $I$) to isolate the contributions from $L=0$ and higher partial waves.

## Key numbers

- At zero $\bar{K}$ momentum and normal nuclear matter density ($\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$), the real part of the $\bar{K}$ optical potential is about $-100$ MeV in the simplified self-consistent scheme, and about $-87$ MeV in the full complex self-consistent scheme.
- The imaginary part of the $\bar{K}$ optical potential at zero momentum changes from about $-3$ MeV in the simplified scheme to about $-25$ MeV in the full complex scheme.
- At a $\bar{K}$ momentum of 500 MeV/c, including all partial waves changes the complex optical potential from $(-28, -39)$ MeV (for $L=0$ only) to $(-52, -61)$ MeV (all partial waves included).
- The nucleon single-particle potential at $\rho=\rho_0$ is taken as $-70$ MeV.
- The $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ hyperon single-particle potentials are taken as $-30$ MeV at $\rho=\rho_0$.

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic self-consistent calculations of the antikaon optical potential using chiral models by Ramos and Oset (2000) and Lutz (1998), and addresses the momentum dependence relevant for heavy-ion collisions studied by Schaffner-Bielich, Koch, and Effenberg (2000) and Sibirtsev and Cassing (1998). The results show that the self-consistent treatment of the complex $\bar{K}$ self-energy dramatically increases the imaginary part of the potential compared to simplified schemes, making narrow deeply-bound $\bar{K}$ nuclear states less likely, and that higher partial waves become important at momenta above 200 MeV/c, relevant for interpreting the $K^-/K^+$ ratio measured at GSI.
