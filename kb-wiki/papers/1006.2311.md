# Resonance parameters of the first 1/2+ state in 9Be and astrophysical implications

> Spectra of the 9Be(e,e') reaction have been measured at the S-DALINAC at an electron energy E_0 = 73 MeV and scattering angles of 93° and 141° with high energy resolution up to excitation energies E_x = 8 MeV. The astrophysically relevant resonance parameters of the first excited 1/2+ state of 9Be have been extracted in a one-level approximation of R-matrix theory resulting in a resonance energy E_R = 1.748(6) MeV and width Gamma_R = 274(8) keV in good agreement with the latest 9Be(gamma,n) experiment but with considerably improved uncertainties. However, the reduced B(E1) transition strength deduced from an extrapolation of the (e,e') data to the photon point is a factor of two smaller. Implications of the new results for a possible production of 12C in neutron-rich astrophysical scenarios are discussed.

## Key claim

The resonance parameters of the first excited 1/2+ state in 9Be extracted from high-resolution (e,e') data agree with the latest direct (gamma,n) experiment (Utsunomiya 2000) but with considerably improved uncertainties; however, the reduced B(E1) transition strength deduced from extrapolation to the photon point is a factor of two smaller than the real-photon result, indicating a possible violation of Siegert's theorem.

## Method

High-resolution 9Be(e,e') spectra were measured at the S-DALINAC at an incident electron energy of 73 MeV and scattering angles of 93° and 141° up to excitation energies of 8 MeV. The resonance parameters of the 1/2+ state were extracted in a one-level approximation of R-matrix theory, and the equivalent (gamma,n) cross sections were obtained by extrapolating the B(E1,q) transition strength measured at finite momentum transfer to the photon point using both shell-model and no-core shell model calculations as well as a plane-wave Born approximation analysis.

## Key numbers

- Resonance energy E_R = 1.748(6) MeV for the first 1/2+ state in 9Be, extracted from averaged (e,e') data
- Resonance width Gamma_R = 274(8) keV for the same state
- Reduced transition strength B(E1,k)up = 0.027(2) e^2 fm^2 from the present (e,e') data, compared to 0.0535(35) e^2 fm^2 from the real-photon experiment of Utsunomiya (2000)
- Radiative width Gamma_gamma = 0.302(45) eV for the 1/2+ state
- Neutron threshold S_n = 1.6654 MeV for 9Be
- Energy resolution Delta E = 30 keV (FWHM) achieved in the measurements
- Target areal density of 5.55 mg/cm^2 used for the 9Be target

## Context

This work extends previous electron scattering measurements on 9Be by Kuechler (1987) and Glickman (1991) and resolves discrepancies in the resonance parameters through a reanalysis showing that earlier work likely quoted the maximum energy and FWHM rather than true resonance parameters. The results confirm the reanalysis by Barker (2000) and provide improved uncertainties compared to the direct photoneutron experiment of Utsunomiya (2000). The new reaction rates for the 8Be(n,gamma)9Be process, which may provide an alternative route for 12C production in explosive nucleosynthesis, deviate from the NACRE compilation (1999) by +20% to -60% depending on temperature, and the factor-of-two discrepancy in B(E1) strength between electron and photon experiments poses an open problem that may require investigation of isospin mixing or meson-exchange current effects.
