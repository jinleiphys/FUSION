# Central and tensor components of three-nucleon forces in low-energy proton-deuteron scattering

> Contributions of three-nucleon forces (3NF) to proton-deuteron scattering observables at energies below the deuteron breakup threshold are studied by solving the Faddeev equation that includes the Coulomb interaction. At E_p=3.0 MeV, we find that the central part of a two-pion exchange 3NF removes the discrepancy between measured cross sections and the calculated ones by two-nucleon forces, and improves the agreement with T_{22} experimental data. However, the tensor part of the 3NF fails in reproducing data of the analyzing power T_{21} by giving worse agreement between the measured and the calculated. Detailed examinations of scattering amplitudes suggest that a P-wave contribution in spin quartet tensor amplitudes has unsuitable sign for reproducing the T_{21} data.

## Key claim

At proton energies below the deuteron breakup threshold, the central part of a two-pion exchange three-nucleon force (3NF) resolves the long-standing discrepancy between measured and calculated differential cross sections and improves agreement for the tensor analyzing power T22, but the tensor part of the same 3NF systematically worsens agreement with the T21 data, especially above 2 MeV, due to an unsuitable sign in the P-wave spin-quartet tensor amplitude.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev integral equation including the Coulomb interaction for proton-deuteron scattering at energies below the deuteron breakup threshold (0.65, 1.0, 2.5, and 3.0 MeV). They used the Argonne V18 two-nucleon force (AV18) and the Brazil model (BR) for the two-pion exchange 3NF, and introduced a spin-independent Gaussian (GS) 3NF to simulate the central part of the BR-3NF and a spin-orbit (SO) 3NF to simulate spin-vector type 3NFs. By comparing calculations with AV18 alone, AV18+BR, AV18+GS, and AV18+BR+SO, they isolated the contributions of the central and tensor components of the 3NF to the scattering observables.

## Key numbers

- At Ep=3.0 MeV, the AV18+GS calculation reduced the chi-squared per datum for the differential cross section from 25 (AV18 alone) to 2.3; the AV18+BR calculation gave 2.2.
- At Ep=3.0 MeV, the chi-squared per datum for T21 increased from 7.1 (AV18 alone) to 20 (AV18+BR), while AV18+GS gave 8.3 and AV18+BR+SO gave 18.
- At Ep=3.0 MeV, the chi-squared per datum for T22 improved from 18.0 (AV18 alone) to 3.6 (AV18+GS), 4.3 (AV18+BR), and 5.3 (AV18+BR+SO).
- The calculated 3He binding energies were 7.79 MeV (AV18+BR), 7.74 MeV (AV18+GS), and 7.74 MeV (AV18+BR+SO), compared with the empirical value of 7.72 MeV.
- The GS-3NF used V0G = -40 MeV and rG = 1.0 fm; the SO-3NF used W0 = -20 MeV and alpha = 1.5 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev calculations of Ishikawa (2003) that first included the Coulomb interaction in low-energy proton-deuteron scattering, and builds on the earlier finding that three-nucleon forces resolve the three-nucleon underbinding problem for realistic two-nucleon forces (Sasakawa, 1986). The results contradict the expectation that the full two-pion exchange 3NF would improve all polarization observables; instead, the tensor part fails for T21, pointing to a need for improved tensor components in 3NF models. The analysis enables future studies of the energy dependence of the tensor 3NF effect above 2 MeV, where precise measurements of proton-deuteron observables are needed, and connects to reported tensor analyzing power problems at higher energies (Kievsky, 2001; Sekiguchi, 2002).
