# Nucleon-nucleon effective potential in dense matter including rho-meson exchange

> We obtain the RPA summed one-meson exchange potential between nucleons in symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, from a model which includes $ρ$, $σ$, $ω$ and $π$ mesons. The behavior of rho mesons inside the medium is first discussed using different schemes to extract a finite contribution from the vacuum polarization. These schemes give qualitatively different results for the in-medium rho mass. The results are discussed in connection with the non-renormalizability of the model. We next study the modified potential as density increases. In the intermediate distance range, it is qualitatively modified by matter and vacuum effects. In the long-distance range ($r>2$ fm), one observes the presence of oscillations, which are not present in free-space. Features on this distance range are insensitive to the renormalization scheme.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) summed one-meson exchange potential in symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, including rho-meson exchange, shows qualitatively different in-medium rho mass behavior depending on the renormalization scheme used to handle the non-renormalizable derivative coupling; however, the long-distance oscillations (beyond 2 fm) are insensitive to the choice of renormalization scheme.

## Method

The authors start from a Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) Lagrangian with nucleons coupled to sigma, omega, pi, and rho mesons, using Yukawa couplings for the first three and a derivative (tensor) coupling for the rho. They compute the in-medium meson propagators in the RPA by linear response theory around a Hartree ground state, obtaining the polarization tensor from nucleon particle-hole loops. The vacuum polarization for the rho meson is regularized via dimensional regularization and counterterms, and four different renormalization schemes are compared. The one-boson exchange potential in the center-of-mass frame is then obtained by replacing free meson propagators with the in-medium RPA propagators and expanding to order (p/M)^2.

## Key numbers

- The long-distance range where oscillations appear is given as r > 2 fm.
- The expansion of the potential is kept to order (p/M)^2, with the next term being of order (p/M)^4 ~ (0.3/0.7)^4 at saturation density.
- The rho meson mass in vacuum is given as mu_rho (the bare mass parameter in the Lagrangian), with the specific numerical value not stated in the truncated text.
- The nucleon effective mass is M = m - g_sigma <sigma>, with m the bare nucleon mass (numerical value not given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends previous RPA studies of the one-pion exchange potential (DPS89) and the Walecka sigma-omega model (GDP94, GDP94L) by including rho-meson exchange, which is necessary for the proper isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon potential. The in-medium rho mass behavior is discussed in connection with dilepton excess observations in heavy ion collisions (ChR96). The paper notes that RPA corrections to the NN interaction are of the same order as Brueckner corrections (DM98) and that a full treatment would require parquet resummation (JW94), but the present work focuses on the RPA piece as a necessary component.
