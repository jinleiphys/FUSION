# Light-Ion Collisions: Bridging Small and Large QCD Systems

> Light-ion collisions at the LHC bridge the gap between small proton-proton and large heavy-ion collision systems, providing a unique laboratory to study the onset of QCD collective phenomena. The first light-ion run at the LHC took place July~1--9, 2025, with proton-oxygen (pO), oxygen-oxygen (OO), and neon-neon (NeNe) collisions. Early experimental results provide strong evidence of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation in these small systems. I review the motivation for the light-ion collisions and the first experimental results, connecting perturbative QCD, hot QCD, and low-energy nuclear structure physics.

# Light-Ion Collisions: Bridging Small and Large QCD Systems

## Key claim
Light-ion collisions at the LHC provide strong evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation in systems with as few as ~10 participating nucleons, but the residual uncertainty from nuclear parton distribution functions prevents an unambiguous ≥5σ attribution of hadron suppression to final-state energy loss in oxygen-oxygen and neon-neon collisions.

## Method
The author reviews the first light-ion run at the LHC (July 1–9, 2025) which collected proton-oxygen (pO) at √sNN = 9.62 TeV, oxygen-oxygen (OO) and neon-neon (NeNe) at √sNN = 5.36 TeV in collider mode, plus NeNe and OH2 data in fixed-target mode by LHCb SMOG2. The analysis connects perturbative QCD baseline computations for nuclear modification factors with hydrodynamic predictions for elliptic flow, comparing early experimental results from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS to theoretical models.

## Key numbers
- Oxygen-oxygen collisions: the nuclear modification factor R_OO^min.bias = (1/A^2) × (dσ_OO/dp_T)/(dσ_pp/dp_T) with A=16, free of binary collision modeling
- A potential discovery window for charged-hadron R_AA in OO collisions was identified in the range 20 GeV < p_T < 100 GeV, where energy-loss signal separates from nPDF uncertainty
- Proton-oxygen collisions were recorded at √sNN = 9.62 TeV; OO and NeNe at √sNN = 5.36 TeV
- RHIC OO collision data were taken at √sNN = 200 GeV in 2021 and 2026 campaigns
- In central Pb-Pb collisions, jets are suppressed by up to 40%, with suppression decreasing in more peripheral collisions

## Context
This work extends the study of QCD collective phenomena from large heavy-ion systems (Pb-Pb) down to small systems, addressing the "small system puzzle" where collective flow coexists with inconclusive jet-quenching signatures in p-Pb collisions. The author's earlier baseline computations (Huss 2020) identified nPDF uncertainties as the dominant theoretical limitation, and recent updates (Gebhard 2024, Mazeliauskas 2025, Jonas 2026) incorporate new nPDF extractions. The flow observable predictions (Giacalone 2024) leverage the sensitivity of elliptic flow to nuclear ground-state structure, with 16O having a compact four-alpha-cluster shape and 20Ne showing large intrinsic deformation from an additional alpha cluster. The results enable a proposed "New Ions @ LHC Run 4" initiative to produce a white paper by 2029 documenting science cases for additional ion species.
