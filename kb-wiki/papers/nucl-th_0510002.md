# Interferometry search for new forms of matter in A+A collisions

> A method allowing studies of the hadronic matter at the early evolution stage in A+A collisions is developed. It is based on an interferometry analysis of approximately conserved values such as the averaged phase-space density (APSD) and the specific entropy of thermal pions. The plateau found in the APSD behavior vs collision energy at SPS is associated, apparently, with the deconfinement phase transition at low SPS energies; a saturation of this quantity at the RHIC energies indicates the limiting Hagedorn temperature for hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method to study hadronic matter at the early evolution stage in A+A collisions using interferometry analysis of approximately conserved quantities, and associates a plateau in the averaged phase-space density (APSD) versus collision energy at SPS with the deconfinement phase transition, while a saturation at RHIC energies indicates the limiting Hagedorn temperature for hadronic matter. The authors note that the plateau is "apparently" associated with the deconfinement phase transition, and the saturation "can be treated as" an existence of the limiting Hagedorn temperature, reflecting the interpretive nature of these associations.

## Method

The method extracts the averaged phase-space density (APSD) and specific entropy of thermal pions from experimental data on yields, transverse momentum spectra, and interferometry (HBT) radii of negative pions at mid-rapidity in central heavy ion collisions. The APSD is obtained via an equation relating the phase-space density to the measured spectra and HBT radii, assuming a uniform freeze-out temperature of 120 MeV and accounting for resonance decay contributions through a parameter kappa (0.65 for SPS, 0.7 for RHIC). The specific entropy is then calculated from the extracted pion chemical potential and the assumed freeze-out temperature.

## Key numbers

- The freeze-out temperature used for analysis is T = 120 MeV, described as a typical "average value" for SPS and RHIC experiments.
- The parameter kappa accounting for resonance decay contributions is kappa = 0.65 for SPS and kappa = 0.7 for RHIC.
- For PHENIX data at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the interferometry volume for 0-30% centrality events was increased by a factor of 1.215 to correspond to the most central 0-5% centrality bin.
- The rapidity density of all negative pions follows a logarithmic law: a * log10(sqrt(s_NN)/b), with a = 160 (230) and b = 1.91 GeV (3 GeV) for solid (dashed) lines respectively.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering interferometry idea of Bertsch (Bertsch) and builds on the authors' prior development of conserved observables in hydrodynamics (Akkelin and Sinyukov). The method enables studies of the hadronization stage in A+A collisions across a range of energies from AGS to RHIC, and the observed anomalous increase in pion entropy/multiplicities at SPS energies is interpreted as a possible signature of the QCD critical end point, with connections to dissipative effects discussed by Gyulassy and to sigma meson decays near the critical point as studied by Stephanov.
