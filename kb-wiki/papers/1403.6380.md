# Light Meson Dynamics Workshop. Mini proceedings

> The mini-proceedings of the Light Meson Dynamics Workshop held in Mainz from February 10th to 12th, 2014, are presented. The web page of the conference, which contains all talks, can be found at https://indico.cern.ch/event/287442/overview .

## Key claim

The mini-proceedings summarize the talks and discussions of the Light Meson Dynamics Workshop held in Mainz from February 10th to 12th, 2014, covering the current status and open questions in light meson physics rather than presenting a single new result or unified claim.

## Method

The workshop brought together theorists and experimentalists working on light meson dynamics, with presentations covering chiral perturbation theory, unitarized chiral dynamics, dispersion relations, and experimental results from BESIII, Crystal Ball, KLOE, NA48/62, and WASA. The proceedings compile written summaries of individual talks, each addressing specific topics such as the odd-intrinsic parity sector of chiral perturbation theory, eta-eta-prime mixing, and experimental results from BESIII.

## Key numbers

- The decay width for pi0 to gamma gamma is predicted to be 8.1 +/- 0.1 eV, in agreement with the experimental measurement of 7.82 +/- 0.22 eV, testing the chiral anomaly to about 3%
- The branching fraction for eta-prime to pi+ pi- e+ e- is measured as (2.11 +/- 0.12 +/- 0.14) x 10^{-3} by BESIII
- The upper limit for eta-prime to pi+ pi- mu+ mu- is set at less than 2.9 x 10^{-5} at 90% confidence level by BESIII
- Upper limits for CP-violating decays from BESIII: B(eta to pi+ pi-) < 3.9 x 10^{-4}, B(eta to pi0 pi0) < 6.9 x 10^{-4}, B(eta-prime to pi+ pi-) < 5.5 x 10^{-5}
- The eta-eta-prime mixing angle in the quark-flavour basis from V to P gamma decays is phi_P = (41.5 +/- 1.2) degrees (Escribano and Nadal, 2007)
- From eta and eta-prime transition form factors, the mixing parameters are F_q/F_pi = 1.06 +/- 0.01, F_s/F_pi = 1.56 +/- 0.24, and phi_P = (40.3 +/- 1.8) degrees (Escribano, Masjuan, and Sanchez-Puertas, 2014)

## Context

This workshop proceedings extends the ongoing theoretical and experimental effort to understand light meson structure and dynamics, building on the chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Weinberg, Gasser, and Leutwyler, and the Wess-Zumino-Witten anomaly term. The contributions address open questions such as the gluonic content of the eta-prime meson (where KLOE results suggest a nonzero gluonium component while phenomenological analyses by Escribano and Nadal (2007) find no evidence for it), the need for precision measurements of rare and forbidden decays, and the extraction of mixing parameters from transition form factors. The workshop enables future progress by identifying key experimental datasets expected from BESIII, KLOE-2, and other facilities, and by highlighting the need for improved theoretical calculations including isospin-breaking and electromagnetic corrections.
