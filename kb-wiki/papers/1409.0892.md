# A Predictive Theory for Elastic Scattering and Recoil of Protons from $^4$He

> Low-energy cross sections for elastic scattering and recoil of protons from $^4$He nuclei (also known as $α$ particles) are calculated directly by solving the Schrödinger equation for five nucleons interacting through accurate two- and three-nucleon forces derived within the framework of chiral effective field theory. Precise knowledge of these processes at various proton backscattering/recoil angles and energies is needed for the ion-beam analysis of numerous materials, from the surface layers of solids, to thin films, to fusion-reactor materials. Indeed, the same elastic scattering process, in two different kinematic configurations, can be used to probe concentrations and depth profiles of either hydrogen or helium. We compare our results to available experimental data and show that direct calculations with modern nuclear potentials can help to resolve remaining inconsistencies among different data sets and can be used to predict these cross sections when measurements are not available.

# A Predictive Theory for Elastic Scattering and Recoil of Protons from $^4$He

## Key claim

The authors present the most complete *ab initio* calculation of proton-$^4$He elastic scattering to date, claiming that direct solution of the five-nucleon Schrödinger equation with chiral two- and three-nucleon forces can resolve inconsistencies among experimental data sets and predict cross sections where measurements are unavailable. However, the calculation overestimates the width of the $^5$Li ground-state resonance by about 24% compared to R-matrix analysis, and the agreement with experiment deteriorates at intermediate energies near the resonances, indicating remaining deficiencies in the nuclear Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for five nucleons using the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC), which expands the wave function on an overcomplete basis combining square-integrable eigenstates of the $^5$Li compound system and continuous states of a proton and a $^4$He nucleus separated by a relative coordinate. The Hamiltonian employs the chiral N$^3$LO nucleon-nucleon interaction of Entem and Machleidt (2003) and the N$^2$LO three-nucleon force of Navrátil et al. (2007), unitarily softened via the similarity-renormalization-group method to a momentum scale of 2 fm$^{-1}$. The scattering matrix is obtained by matching the NCSMC solutions to asymptotic wave functions using the microscopic R-matrix method.

## Key numbers

- The $^5$Li $3/2^-$ resonance centroid is calculated at $E_R = 1.77(1)$ MeV with width $\Gamma = 1.70(5)$ MeV, compared to R-matrix values of $E_R = 1.67$ MeV and $\Gamma = 1.37$ MeV.
- The $^5$Li $1/2^-$ resonance centroid is calculated at $E_R = 3.11(2)$ MeV with width $\Gamma = 7.90(50)$ MeV, compared to R-matrix values of $E_R = 3.35$ MeV and $\Gamma = 9.40$ MeV.
- The statistical error due to finite model space size is within 9% for the elastic recoil cross section, based on comparison between $N_{\rm max}=13$ and $N_{\rm max}=11$ calculations.
- The theoretical uncertainty associated with the treatment of helium excitations is within 5% when comparing calculations with 6 versus 7 $^4$He states at backscattering angle $\theta_p=169^\circ$.
- Discrepancies as large as 10% exist among R-matrix fits based on different data sets in the critical region near the $3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$ resonances of $^5$Li, as reported by Pusa (2004).

## Context

This work extends previous *ab initio* calculations of $p$-$^4$He scattering by Hupin et al. (2013), which only included the continuous sector of the model space and achieved accurate convergence only for energies above the $^5$Li resonances. The present calculation adds the first fourteen square-integrable eigenstates of the $^5$Li compound nucleus, substantially improving the description of the low-energy resonance region. The results enable more reliable reference cross sections for ion-beam analysis techniques such as non-Rutherford backscattering spectroscopy and elastic recoil detection analysis, where existing experimental data sets show inconsistencies of up to 40% in the dip near $E_\alpha=3$ MeV for the $^1$H$(\alpha,p)^4$He reaction at $\varphi_p=30^\circ$.
