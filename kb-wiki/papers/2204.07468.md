# Nuclear matter calculations with the phenomenological three-nucleon interaction

> Employing the concept of three-body radial distribution function and using the two-body correlation functions, calculated based on the lowest order constrained variational method, we investigated the effect of the three-body force (TBF) on the nuclear matter properties, for Argonne and Urbana $\it{v_{14}}$ potentials. As such, the results for nuclear matter density, incompressibility, energy per nucleon, and symmetry energy are presented at the saturation point. The inclusion of a phenomenological TBF resulted in closer values of the saturation density, incompressibility, and symmetry energy to the empirical ones for the symmetric nuclear matter. This is especially the case for the Urbana $\it{v_{14}}$ potential. In addition, an empirically-verified parabolic approximation of the interaction energy was utilized to perform an approximate study of the nuclear matter with neutron excess. Hence, at densities higher than about 0.3~fm$^{-3}$ and for proton-to-neutron density ratios close to the symmetric nuclear matter, the inclusion of TBF resulted in an extra attraction for the Argonne as compared to the Urbana $\it{v_{14}}$ potential.

## Key claim
The inclusion of a phenomenological three-body force (TBF) within the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method brings the saturation density, incompressibility, and symmetry energy of symmetric nuclear matter closer to empirical values, particularly for the Urbana v14 potential; however, at densities above about 0.3 fm^-3 and for proton-to-neutron ratios near symmetric matter, the Argonne v14 potential with TBF shows extra attraction compared to Urbana v14 with TBF.

## Method
The authors used the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method to calculate two-body correlation functions, then employed the concept of the three-body radial distribution function to compute the expectation value of a phenomenological three-nucleon interaction (TBF_UA) based on the Urbana-Argonne model. The TBF_UA consists of a two-pion-exchange attractive term and a repulsive term, with strength parameters A_2π and U adjusted to reproduce the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter. An empirically-verified parabolic approximation of the interaction energy was used to extend the study to asymmetric nuclear matter with neutron excess.

## Key numbers
- Saturation energy per nucleon for UV14 alone: about -20.8 MeV; for UV14+TBF_UA: about -11.2 MeV.
- Saturation energy per nucleon for AV14 alone: about -15.7 MeV; for AV14+TBF_UA: about -10.3 MeV.
- Saturation density for UV14 alone: about 0.364 fm^-3; for UV14+TBF_UA: about 0.178 fm^-3.
- Saturation density for AV14 alone: about 0.288 fm^-3; for AV14+TBF_UA: about 0.178 fm^-3.
- Symmetry energy at saturation density for UV14 alone: about 44.8 MeV; for UV14+TBF_UA: about 29.2 MeV.
- Symmetry energy at saturation density for AV14 alone: about 35.5 MeV; for AV14+TBF_UA: about 27.6 MeV.
- Saturation incompressibility for UV14 alone: about 302 MeV; for UV14+TBF_UA: about 193 MeV.
- Saturation incompressibility for AV14 alone: about 240 MeV; for AV14+TBF_UA: about 167 MeV.
- TBF strength parameters: A_2π^(AV14) = -0.0329, U^(AV14) = 0.0064, A_2π^(UV14) = -0.0331, U^(UV14) = 0.0045 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior LOCV calculations by Bordbar and Modarres (1997, 1998) that used only two-body forces and could not reproduce empirical saturation properties. It follows the procedure of Baldo et al. (1999) to constrain the TBF strength parameters to the saturation point alone, rather than fitting light nuclei simultaneously. The results enable future studies of more realistic potentials such as AV18 and modern chiral potentials, and are relevant for neutron star equation of state modeling, particularly in light of observed neutron star masses around 2 solar masses (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013, Linares 2018).
