# Quark-Meson Coupling Model, Nuclear Matter Constraints and Neutron Star Properties

> We explore the equation of state for nuclear matter in the quark-meson coupling model, including full Fock terms. The comparison with phenomenological constraints can be used to restrict the few additional parameters appearing in the Fock terms which are not present at Hartree level. Because the model is based upon the in-medium modification of the quark structure of the bound hadrons, it can be applied without additional parameters to include hyperons and to calculate the equation of state of dense matter in beta-equilibrium. This leads naturally to a study of the properties of neutron stars, including their maximum mass, their radii and density profiles.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling model, when extended to include full Fock terms (including both Dirac vector and Pauli tensor contributions), can reproduce phenomenological nuclear matter constraints, but the additional repulsion from the Fock terms causes the model to lose its previously successful prediction of bound Lambda hyperons unless the sigma-hyperon couplings are artificially adjusted.

## Method

The authors start from the quark-meson coupling model, which describes baryons as MIT bags containing three quarks coupled to sigma, omega, rho, and pion mesons. They compute the equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and beta-equilibrium matter (including the full baryon octet) at zero temperature, adding the full exchange (Fock) contributions to the Hartree mean-field energy density. The three quark-meson coupling constants are fixed to reproduce standard saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter, while the tensor couplings are determined from the baryons' anomalous magnetic moments, and the form factor cutoff Lambda is varied between 0.9 and 2.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E(rho = rho_0) = -15.865 MeV
- Asymmetry energy coefficient at saturation: a_asym = S_0 = 32.5 MeV
- Sigma meson mass set to 700 MeV (value giving best agreement with finite nuclei in prior QMC calculations)
- Free nucleon bag radius chosen as R_N^{free} = 1.0 fm
- Form factor cutoff mass range explored: 0.9 GeV <= Lambda <= 2.0 GeV, with preferred value 0.9 GeV
- Standard ratio of tensor to vector coupling for rho-nucleon: f_{rho N}/g_{rho N} = 3.70; alternative scenario uses 5.68 from Nijmegen potentials

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1987) and Guichon et al. (1995, 2008) by including the full Fock terms (previously treated only at Hartree level or with partial exchange contributions). It complements the work of Miyatsu et al. (2011), who also considered tensor interactions in a QMC variant, by investigating a broader set of nuclear matter properties and comparing to heavy-ion collision data. The model enables parameter-free predictions of three-body forces between all combinations of baryons (nucleons and hyperons) and allows calculation of neutron star properties including maximum mass, radii, and density profiles.
