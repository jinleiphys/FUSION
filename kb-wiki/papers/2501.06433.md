# Quark flavor equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma

> The early stage of a heavy-ion collision is marked by rapid entropy production and the transition from a gluon saturated initial condition to a plasma of quarks and gluons that evolves hydrodynamically. However, during the early times of the hydrodynamic evolution, the chemical composition of the QCD medium is still largely unknown. We present a study of quark chemical equilibration in the (Q)GP using a novel model of viscous hydrodynamic evolution in partial chemical equilibrium. Motivated by the success of gluon saturated initial condition models, we initialize the QCD medium as a completely gluon dominated state. Local quark production during the hydrodynamic phase is then simulated through the evolution of time-dependent fugacities for each independent quark flavor, with the timescales set as free parameters to compare different rates of equilibration. We present the results of complete heavy-ion collision simulations using this partial chemical equilibrium model, and show the effects on hadronic and electromagnetic observables. In particular, we show that the development of flow is sensitive to the equilibration timescale, providing an empirical way to probe the chemical equilibration of the QCD medium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quark chemical equilibration in the quark-gluon plasma can be studied using a novel hydrodynamic model with time-dependent quark fugacities, and that the development of transverse flow is sensitive to the equilibration timescale, providing an empirical way to probe chemical equilibration. However, the authors note that the charged particle multiplicities agree within approximately 2% across all equilibration timescales from 0 to 10 fm/c, indicating a close cancellation between higher temperatures and lower fugacities at particlization.

## Method

The authors simulate Pb+Pb collisions using a multi-stage model that generates initial energy density profiles with T_RENTo at τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c, then evolve the QGP using (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) with a partial chemical equilibrium equation of state. The equation of state interpolates between (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD and pure SU(3) gauge theory using a quark fugacity γ_q that evolves according to γ_q(τ_p) = 1 - exp((τ₀ - τ_p)/τ_eq), where τ_eq is a free parameter ranging from 0 to 10 fm/c. Particlization is performed using iS3D at a fugacity-dependent critical temperature T_c(γ_q), followed by hadronic transport with SMASH.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for pure glue (N_f = 0) is T₀ ≈ 260 MeV, while for (2+1)-flavor QCD it is T₃ ≈ 158 MeV
- The initial time for hydrodynamic evolution is τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c
- Equilibration timescales τ_eq are varied between 0 and 10 fm/c
- Charged particle multiplicities agree within approximately 2% when varying τ_eq from 0 to 10 fm/c
- For τ_eq = 5 fm/c, the entire particlization surface has γ_q < 0.9, with much of it falling within γ_q < 0.5
- For τ_eq = 10 fm/c, the medium can particlize as low as γ_q < 0.7
- For equilibration times up to τ_eq = 2 fm/c, variation in pion and proton multiplicities is negligible
- For τ_eq ≥ 5 fm/c, the proton-to-pion ratio shows a ≲ 20% effect
- The energy density at T_c(γ_q) can vary by as much as approximately 50% across fugacities, though in practice variation is typically approximately 20%

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hydrodynamic evolution in partial chemical equilibrium by Vovchenko (2015) and Vovchenko (2016), which examined effects on photonic and dileptonic observables but did not extend to precision studies of hadronic observables. The paper builds on the concept of gluon-saturated initial conditions from models like IP-Glasma (Schenke, 2012) and EKRT (Eskola, 1999), and on two-stage equilibration models showing quark-antiquark chemical equilibration may follow on timescales over 3 fm/c (Xu, 2004; Kurkela, 2018). The results enable future empirical constraints on the chemical equilibration timescale of the QGP through measurements of hadronic observables, particularly the proton-to-pion ratio and mean transverse momentum, at both LHC and RHIC energies.
