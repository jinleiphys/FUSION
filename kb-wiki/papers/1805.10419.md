# Chiral magnetohydrodynamic turbulence in core-collapse supernovae

> Macroscopic evolution of relativistic charged matter with chirality imbalance is described by the chiral magnetohydrodynamics (chiral MHD). One such astrophysical system is high-density lepton matter in core-collapse supernovae where the chirality imbalance of leptons is generated by the parity-violating weak processes. After developing the chiral MHD equations for this system, we perform numerical simulations for the real-time evolutions of magnetic and flow fields, and study the properties of the chiral MHD turbulence. In particular, we observe the inverse cascade of the magnetic energy and the fluid kinetic energy. Our results suggest that the chiral effects that have been neglected so far can reverse the turbulent cascade direction from direct to inverse cascade, which would impact the magnetohydrodynamics evolution in the supernova core toward explosion.

## Key claim

The authors show that chiral effects (the chiral magnetic effect and chiral anomaly), which have been neglected in conventional supernova modeling, can reverse the turbulent cascade direction from direct to inverse cascade in three-dimensional core-collapse supernova cores, potentially impacting explosion dynamics. This result is demonstrated under simplified conditions that ignore the chiral vortical effect, fluid helicity, and cross-helicity, and the authors caution that their computations "should not be taken as a quantitative prediction."

## Method

The authors derive one-fluid chiral magnetohydrodynamic (chiral MHD) equations for protons and electrons with a chirality imbalance in the supernova core, incorporating the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral anomaly while neglecting the chiral vortical effect, chiral separation effect, and neutrino chiral effects. They perform three-dimensional numerical simulations using a local Cartesian model with periodic boundary conditions, solving the MHD equations with a second-order Godunov-type finite-difference scheme and the Consistent Method of Characteristics-Constrained Transport scheme, treating the axial charge density as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Fiducial model (Model 1): initial axial charge density \(n_{\rm A} = 0.1\) (in units of \(100\ {\rm MeV} = 1\)), corresponding to initial chiral magnetic conductivity \(\xi_{B,{\rm ini}} = 4.2 \times 10^{-3}\); resistivity \(\eta = 100.0\); viscosity \(\nu = 0.1\); resolution \(256^3\) grid points; box size \(L = 2\times 10^4\)
- Critical wavelength of the chiral plasma instability: \(\lambda_{\rm crit} = 1.5\times 10^3\) for the fiducial parameters
- Maximum growth rate of the chiral plasma instability: \(\sigma_{\rm max} = \eta\xi_B^2/4\); growth time \(\tau_{\rm CPI} = 4(\eta\xi_{B,{\rm ini}}^2)^{-1} = 2.3\times 10^3\) for the fiducial model
- Saturation magnetic field strength \(B_{\rm sat} = 2.5\times 10^{-2}\) for the fiducial model; ratio \(\xi_{B,{\rm sat}}/\xi_{B,{\rm ini}} = 0.077\)
- Initial density \(\rho = 5.0\) and pressure \(P = 1.0\) in code units, corresponding to \(\rho \simeq 10^{13}\ {\rm g/cm^3}\) and \(P \simeq 10^{34}\ {\rm erg/cm^3}\) of a typical proto-neutron star
- Seed magnetic field amplitude \(|\delta {\bm B}| < 0.01\), giving a maximum value \(\simeq 10^{12}\ {\rm G}\) in cgs units

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral MHD turbulence from high-temperature electroweak plasmas in the early Universe (studied by Schober et al. 2017, Brandenburg et al. 2017, Schober et al. 2018) to the high-density lepton matter in core-collapse supernova cores. It contrasts with conventional three-dimensional neutrino radiation-hydrodynamic simulations (reviewed by Janka 2016, Radice et al. 2017) where direct cascade dominates, and with axisymmetric two-dimensional models where inverse cascade is often observed and may account for vigorous explosions. The results suggest that chiral effects could provide a new mechanism for large-scale magnetic field generation and turbulent energy transfer in supernova cores, potentially relevant to the explosion mechanism, though the authors note that incorporating the chiral vortical effect and chiral separation effect is necessary to ensure conservation of total helicity.
