# The in-medium few-body problem

> We are concerned with few-particle correlations in a fermionic system at finite temperature and density. Within the many-body Green functions formalism the description of correlations is provided by the Dyson equation approach that leads to effective few-body equations. They contain the dominant medium effects, which are self energy corrections and the Pauli blocking. Hence the effective two-body interactions between quasiparticles are momentum/energy-dependent and therefore they can be usesed in the medium modified, momentum space, integral AGS equations for three- and four-body systems. To investigate correlations and clusters beyond four-body, we employ, instead, the configuration space two-variable integro-differential equations (IDEA) for $A$-body bound systems which are based on Hyperspherical Harmonics and the Faddeev decomposition of the wave function in two-body amplitudes. This requires the transformation of the energy dependent two-body interactions to equivalent local, energy independent, ones. To achieve this we use inverse scattering techniques the resulting interactions being, on-- and (to all practical purposes) off--shell equivalent to the energy dependent potentials. In this way we obtain binding energy results for the 2--, 3--, 4--, and 16--particle in a medium at a finite temperature and various densities. Several aspects of the problem are discussed and the behavior of the potential surfaces obtained in the extreme adiabatic approximation, below and above the Mott transition, is investigated.

# The in-medium few-body problem

## Key claim

The authors propose a method to study few-body correlations in a fermionic system at finite temperature and density by transforming energy-dependent in-medium two-body interactions into equivalent local, energy-independent potentials via inverse scattering techniques, enabling configuration-space calculations for systems with up to 16 particles. The approach is demonstrated for densities below and above the Mott transition, but the results are limited to boson-like systems (neglecting spin-isospin dependence) and use the quasiparticle approximation, neglecting retardation effects in the mass matrix.

## Method

The authors start from the many-body Green functions formalism and the Dyson equation approach to derive effective in-medium few-body equations that include self-energy corrections and Pauli blocking. For two-body systems, they obtain an energy-dependent effective potential V(E,r) = (1-2f(E)) V₂(r) using an effective mass approximation and a local bare nucleon-nucleon potential (the Volkov potential). They then apply the Marchenko inverse scattering method to construct energy-independent equivalent local potentials (ELPs) from the phase shifts generated by V(E,r). These ELPs are used as input to the integro-differential equation approach (IDEA) in configuration space, which is based on the Faddeev decomposition of the wave function and hyperspherical harmonics, to compute binding energies for 2-, 3-, 4-, and 16-body systems in the extreme adiabatic approximation.

## Key numbers

- Bare two-body binding energy with the Volkov potential: E₂ = 0.5462 MeV
- At density ρ = 0.003 fm⁻³ and T = 10 MeV, the in-medium two-body binding energy is E₂ = 0.0282 MeV
- At density ρ = 0.034 fm⁻³ and T = 10 MeV, the two-body system has no bound state (phase shifts start from zero, consistent with Levinson's theorem)
- Three-body binding energy at zero density: E₃ = 8.6704 MeV; at ρ = 0.003 fm⁻³: E₃ = 4.7202 MeV; at ρ = 0.034 fm⁻³: no bound state
- Four-body binding energy at zero density: E₄ = 30.7250 MeV; at ρ = 0.003 fm⁻³: E₄ = 22.2798 MeV; at ρ = 0.034 fm⁻³: E₄ = 5.4093 MeV
- Sixteen-body binding energy at zero density: E₁₆ = 1630.1462 MeV; at ρ = 0.003 fm⁻³: E₁₆ = 1554.0695 MeV; at ρ = 0.034 fm⁻³: E₁₆ = 1410.5321 MeV
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³; the Mott transition occurs near ρ₀/10

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous momentum-space studies of in-medium few-body correlations (Beyer et al., 1996-2003) to configuration space, where the IDEA method (developed by Sofianos and collaborators) can handle systems with more than four particles. The approach builds on the Dyson equation framework for correlations in nuclear matter developed by Schuck and collaborators (1971-1996) and reviewed by Dukelsky et al. (1998). The use of inverse scattering to construct equivalent local potentials follows earlier work by Sofianos and Beyer (2001) with the Malfliet-Tjon potential. The results enable future investigations of cluster formation and dissolution in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies, particularly for larger clusters such as alpha-particle systems in oxygen-16, and open questions about the behavior of the two-body spectral function and the lifetime of clusters when self-consistency and three-body collisions are included.
