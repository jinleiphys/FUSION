# 12C(p,n)12N reaction at 135 MeV

> We report observations from the (p,n) reaction on 12C at 135 MeV. The experiment was performed with the beam-swinger neutron time-of-flight system at the Indiana University Cyclotron Facility. Neutrons were detected in large-volume plastic scintillation detectors located in three detector stations at 0 deg, 24 deg, and 45 deg with respect to the undeflected beam line; the flight paths were 91 m, 91 m, and 74 m, respectively. Overall time resolutions of about 825 ps provided energy resolutions of about 350 keV in the first two stations and about 425 keV in the third station. The angular distributions for states with excitation energies up to 10 MeV are presented and comparisons are made with DWIA calculations that use one-body density matrices from 0hw and 1hw shell-model calculations. New information is deduced on the excitation energies, widths and spin-parity assignments for several energy levels of 12N.

## Key claim
The paper reports new spectroscopic information on energy levels of \(^{12}\)N from the \(^{12}\)C(p,n) reaction at 135 MeV, but the authors note that cross sections for states above 6 MeV represent lower limits because contributions from the underlying continuum are not included.

## Method
Neutron time-of-flight spectra were measured at 12 angles between 0° and 63° using the beam-swinger system at the Indiana University Cyclotron Facility, with three detector stations at 0°, 24°, and 45° and flight paths of 91 m, 91 m, and 74 m, respectively. Excitation energies and cross sections were extracted by fitting peaks with Lorentzian line shapes folded with a Gaussian resolution function, and angular distributions were compared with distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations using one-body density matrices from \(0\hbar\omega\) and \(1\hbar\omega\) shell-model calculations with the Cohen-Kurath and Millener-Kurath interactions.

## Key numbers
- Energy resolution: about 350 keV in the first two detector stations (0° and 24°) and about 425 keV in the third station (45°), from overall time resolutions of about 825 ps.
- Flight paths: 90.9 m (0° station), 90.8 m (24° station), and 74.4 m (45° station), each with ±0.2 m uncertainty.
- Target thickness: 31.4 mg/cm² natural carbon (98.9% \(^{12}\)C).
- Cross section uncertainty dominated by detector efficiency uncertainty, estimated at 12%.
- For the 1⁺ ground state transition, DWIA calculations using the 140-MeV \(t\)-matrix agree with experimental angular distributions to within 10% at small angles.
- The 2⁺ state at 0.96 MeV in \(^{12}\)N has analogs at 0.95 MeV in \(^{12}\)B and 16.11 MeV in \(^{12}\)C; the 2⁻ state at 1.19 MeV in \(^{12}\)N has analogs at 1.67 MeV and 16.58 MeV in \(^{12}\)B and \(^{12}\)C, respectively.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the (p,n) reaction at 135 MeV on closed-shell nuclei such as \(^{16}\)O and \(^{40}\)Ca (Anderson et al., 1985; Fazely et al., 1982; Chittrakarn et al., 1986) and on self-conjugate nuclei including \(^{20}\)Ne, \(^{24}\)Mg, \(^{28}\)Si, and \(^{32}\)S (Anderson et al., 1991; Tamimi et al., 1992). The new data on \(^{12}\)N provide a test case for extended-basis shell-model calculations comparable to those performed for \(A=16\) (Warburton and Brown, 1992; Millener, 1992), and the results enable improved spin-parity assignments and width determinations for states in \(^{12}\)N that were previously unresolved in lower-resolution experiments.
