# Imprints of fluctuating proton shapes on flow in proton-lead collisions at the LHC

> Results for particle production in root-s = 5.02 TeV p+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider within a combined classical Yang-Mills and relativistic viscous hydrodynamic calculation are presented. We emphasize the importance of sub-nucleon scale fluctuations in the proton projectile to describe the experimentally observed azimuthal harmonic coefficients, demonstrating their sensitivity to the proton shape. We stress that the proton shape and its fluctuations are not free parameters in our calculations. Instead, they have been constrained using experimental data from HERA on exclusive vector meson production. Including temperature dependent shear and bulk viscosities, as well as UrQMD for the low temperature regime, we present results for mean transverse momenta, harmonic flow coefficients for charged hadrons and identified particles, as well as Hanbury-Brown-Twiss radii.

## Key claim

Including subnucleonic fluctuations in the proton's gluon distribution, constrained by HERA data on exclusive vector meson production, increases the calculated harmonic flow coefficients in p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV by up to a factor of 5 compared to calculations using round protons, enabling a quantitative description of experimental data for multiplicities above approximately twice the average multiplicity.

## Method

The authors combine the IP-Glasma initial state model (with three gluonic hot spots per nucleon whose positions and widths are constrained by HERA diffractive J/Psi production data) with the relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulation Music and the hadronic cascade UrQMD. The full energy-momentum tensor from the classical Yang-Mills simulation is used to initialize hydrodynamics at switching times tau_0 = 0.2 fm and 0.4 fm, with temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosities applied throughout the evolution.

## Key numbers

- Flow harmonics increase by approximately a factor of 5 compared to previous calculations using round protons (Schenke 2014)
- Hot spot width parameter B_q = 0.3 GeV^{-2}, hot spot position spread B_qc = 3 GeV^{-2}, infrared regulator m = 0.4 GeV (from HERA data analysis)
- Minimum shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s)_min = 0.08 at T = 166 MeV, with slopes a = 15 GeV^{-1} in hadronic phase and b = 1 GeV^{-1} in QGP phase
- Switching temperature T_switch = 155 MeV for transition from hydrodynamics to UrQMD
- Without bulk viscosity, pion mean transverse momentum is overestimated by approximately 50%
- For pions at p_T ~ 0.45 GeV, 25% of freeze-out surface cells have shear corrections |delta f|/f >= 100%; at p_T ~ 1 GeV this fraction is 45%
- When not including the initial viscous stress tensor, integrated v_n increase by up to 50% (for tau_0 = 0.4 fm, eta/s = 0.2)

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma framework (Schenke 2012) to include subnucleonic fluctuations, addressing the significant underestimation of flow harmonics in p+Pb collisions reported by Schenke (2014) when using round protons. The proton shape parameters are independently constrained by HERA data on diffractive J/Psi production (Mantysaari 2016). The results enable future studies of the energy dependence of the proton's fluctuating shape via proton-nucleus collisions, and suggest that higher-order cumulants and multi-particle correlators could further constrain subnucleonic structure.
