# Analytic, Non-Perturbative, Gauge-invariant QCD: Nucleon Scattering and Binding Potentials

> Removal of the quenched approximation in the mechanism which produced an analytic estimate of quark-binding potentials, along with a reasonable conjecture of the color structure of the nucleon formed by such a binding potential, is shown to generate an effective, nucleon scattering and binding potential. The mass-scale factor on the order of the pion mass, previously introduced to define transverse imprecision of quark coordinates, is again used, while the strength of the potential is proportional to the square of a renormalized QCD coupling constant. The potential so derived does not include corrections due to spin, angular momentum, nucleon structure, and electroweak interactions; rather, it is qualitative in nature, showing how Nuclear Physics can arise from fundamental QCD.

## Key claim
Removing the quenched approximation and introducing a closed-quark-loop (vacuum functional) generates an effective nucleon-nucleon scattering and binding potential from fundamental QCD, but the result is qualitative only and does not include corrections due to spin, angular momentum, nucleon structure, or electroweak interactions.

## Method
The authors start from a non-perturbative, gauge-invariant QCD generating functional and replace each nucleon's three bound quarks by a single effective nucleon Green's function with a color-singlet constraint, reducing a six-body problem to a two-body problem. They use eikonal approximations for high-energy scattering, introduce transverse coordinate imprecision via a smearing function, and evaluate the vacuum functional L[A] at lowest order (single closed quark loop) to obtain the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The Halpern functional integral over gluon fields is approximated by a Gaussian evaluation.

## Key numbers
- The quark-binding potential from prior work is given as \(V(r) \simeq \xi \, \mu \, (r \, \mu)^{(1+\xi)}\), where \(\xi \ll 1\) is a small positive parameter and \(\mu\) is a mass scale on the order of the pion mass \(m_{\pi}\).
- The nucleon-nucleon eikonal scattering amplitude takes the form \(\mathcal{T}(s,t) = \frac{is}{2M^{2}} \int{\mathrm{d}^{2} \, e^{i \vec{q} \cdot \vec{b}} \, \left[ 1 - e^{i \mathbb{X}(b,s)} \right] }\), where \(s = - (p_{\mathrm{I}}^{} + p_{\mathrm{I\!I}}^{})^{2}\) and \(t = - (p_{\mathrm{I}}^{} - p'_{\mathrm{I\!I}})^{2} = - \vec{q}^{\, 2}\).
- The interaction strength is proportional to the square of a renormalized QCD coupling constant \(g^{2}\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analytic, non-perturbative QCD approach (Fried et al., 2009-2011) that produced quark-binding potentials, by removing the quenched approximation and including vacuum loop effects. The closed quark loop acts as an effective pion exchange mechanism, transmitting gluon interactions across impact parameters larger than \(1/\mu\). This provides a qualitative derivation of nuclear forces from fundamental QCD, in contrast to phenomenological nuclear potentials, and enables future inclusion of spin, angular momentum, and nucleon structure corrections.
