# Prediction of two-neutron halos in the $N=28$ isotones $^{40}$Mg and $^{39}$Na

> The ground states of the nuclei $^{40}$Mg and $^{39}$Na are investigated using the hyperspherical formalism. Since they are located at the edge of the "big island of inversion", we concentrate on whether we are likely to find two-neutron Borromean halos in these nuclei. A three-body model with effective $n$-$n$ and $^{38}$Mg$+n$ interactions is built for $^{40}$Mg based on the available data. We also give predictions for the low-lying spectrum of $^{38}$Na$=^{37}$Na$+n$ and two-neutron separation energy of the $^{39}$Na nucleus. Depending on parameter choice, we report an increase in the matter radii in the range $0.1$-$0.5$ fm relative to those of the core nuclei. The results suggest a two-neutron halo structure in $^{40}$Mg for a subset of parameters, reinforcing the prediction of a Borromean halo nucleus. The calculations indicate that a two-neutron halo is even more likely for $^{39}$Na. As expected, the halo is linked to the disappearance of the shell gap in these nuclei due to the inversion of the $2p_{3/2}$ and $1f_{7/2}$ orbitals. We study the total cross section for scattering of these nuclei from a carbon target using a Glauber model and show that these provide a clear signal to assess the halo structure.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the nuclei $^{40}$Mg and $^{39}$Na may exhibit two-neutron Borromean halo structures, but only for a subset of parameter choices; the halo formation is conditional on the two-neutron separation energy being sufficiently small and on the inversion of the $2p_{3/2}$ and $1f_{7/2}$ orbitals, which leads to increased $p$-wave occupancy in the ground state.

## Method

The authors employ a three-body model in the hyperspherical formalism, treating $^{40}$Mg as a $^{38}$Mg core plus two valence neutrons and $^{39}$Na as a $^{37}$Na core plus two valence neutrons. Effective Woods-Saxon potentials for the core+neutron subsystems are constructed based on available data and three scenarios (normal, degenerate, and inverted single-particle ordering), while the neutron-neutron interaction uses the Gogny-Pires-Tourreil potential. The three-body Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a transformed harmonic oscillator basis, with a phenomenological three-body force adjusted to reproduce different values of the two-neutron separation energy, and the resulting wave functions are used to compute matter radii and partial wave occupancies.

## Key numbers

- The evaluated two-neutron separation energy of $^{40}$Mg is $s_{2n} = 0.670 \pm 0.710$ MeV (from Wang 2021).
- The increase in matter radii relative to the core nuclei ranges from $0.1$ to $0.5$ fm depending on parameter choice and two-neutron separation energy.
- For $^{39}$Na, the predicted two-neutron separation energy ranges between $0.010$ and $0.824$ MeV (without the three-body force, up to $1.828$ MeV).
- The core radii used are $R_c(^{38}\text{Mg}) = 3.600$ fm and $R_c(^{37}\text{Na}) = 3.637$ fm.
- The maximum variation in calculated total radii between different scenarios is up to $5\%$ for both systems.
- The $^{38}$Mg+$n$ subsystem has a predicted low-lying resonance at $0.129$ MeV with $J^\pi$ either $7/2^-$ or $3/2^-$ (from Fossez 2016).

## Context

This work extends the search for two-neutron Borromean halos beyond the heaviest known case, $^{29}$F (studied by Singh 2020, Fortunato 2020, Casal 2020, and Bagachi 2020), to the $N=28$ isotones $^{40}$Mg and $^{39}$Na at the edge of the "big island of inversion" identified by Caurier (2014) and Doornenbel (2013). The study builds on the observation that the $N=28$ shell gap melts in neutron-rich nuclei, similar to the $N=20$ case, and provides Glauber-model predictions for total reaction cross sections on a carbon target that could be tested experimentally to confirm halo formation.
