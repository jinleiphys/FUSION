# Exploring the neutron-star matter properties via the deformed nuclear reactions

> Within the framework of Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics transport model, the correlation of initial deformation and isospin diffusion is systematically investigated in collisions of $^{238}$U + $^{238}$U. The impacts of the collision centrality, symmetry energy and initial configuration on the collective flows, neutron/proton and $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratios have been systematically investigated. It is found that the broader neutron-rich region is formed in the body-body collisions in comparison with the ones in the tip-tip collisions. The neutron-star matter might be created in the density region of 0.2-0.5 $ρ_{0}$ (the normal nuclear density $ρ_{0}$=0.165 fm$^{-3}$) formed in the $^{238}$U + $^{238}$U reaction at the incident energy of 500 MeV/nucleon. The elliptic flows of protons are related to the incident energy, collision centrality, symmetry energy and collision orientation. The hard symmetry energy enables the larger free neutron/proton ratio at the beam energy of 500 MeV/nucleon. However, the neutron/proton and $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratios in the density regime of $1.2\leq ρ/ρ_{0}\leq1.8$ are enhanced by the soft symmetry energy with the slope parameter of L=42 MeV.

# Exploring the neutron-star matter properties via the deformed nuclear reactions

## Key claim

Neutron-star-like matter with isospin asymmetry close to that of neutron-star crust may be created in the density region of 0.2-0.5 ρ₀ during ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U collisions at 500 MeV/nucleon, but only in body-body collisions which produce a broader neutron-rich region than tip-tip collisions. The claim is conditional on the collision orientation and the specific density regime formed in the reaction.

## Method

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U collisions at 500 MeV/nucleon with different initial quadrupole deformation orientations (tip-tip and body-body) and collision centralities. The symmetry energy stiffness was varied by adjusting the parameter γₛ to produce soft (L=42 MeV), linear (L=82 MeV), and hard (L=139 MeV) symmetry energies, and the resulting collective flows, neutron/proton ratios, and π⁻/π⁺ ratios were computed as functions of transverse momentum, kinetic energy, and nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- The normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.165 fm⁻³
- The quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ = 0.215 for ²³⁸U
- The soft symmetry energy has slope parameter L = 42 MeV; the hard symmetry energy has L = 139 MeV
- Neutron-star-like matter may be created in the density region of 0.2-0.5 ρ₀ (and up to 0.6 ρ₀ in the broader discussion)
- The neutron/proton and π⁻/π⁺ ratios in the density regime 1.2 ≤ ρ/ρ₀ ≤ 1.8 are enhanced by the soft symmetry energy with L = 42 MeV
- The hard symmetry energy (L = 139 MeV) enables larger free neutron/proton ratio at 500 MeV/nucleon, particularly at kinetic energies above 300 MeV in semi-central collisions (centrality 3-4 fm)
- The direct NN → NNπ process contributes roughly 15% of pion yields
- The isovector coefficient Cπ = 36 MeV and isospin splitting parameter γπ = 2 for the pion energy in nuclear medium

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy constraints from heavy-ion collisions using the LQMD model (Feng 2018, 2011, 2012) and connects to the experimental programs at HIRFL-CSR in Lanzhou, particularly the Cooling-Storage-Ring External target Experiment (CEE). The use of deformed ²³⁸U projectiles builds on earlier investigations of nuclear shape effects in relativistic collisions (Jia 2022, Zhang 2022, STAR Collaboration 2024) and on studies of isospin diffusion and collective flows in deformed nuclear reactions (Li 2000, Luo 2007, Cao 2010, Xu 2012, Das 2017, Fan 2023, Liu 2023). The results enable future experimental tests at CSR, HIAF, RIBF, RAON, and FRIB to constrain the high-density symmetry energy and neutron-star matter properties through measurements of n/p and π⁻/π⁺ ratios.
