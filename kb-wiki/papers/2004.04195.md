# Thermodynamically consistent equation of state for an accreted neutron star crust

> We study equation of state (EOS) of an accreting neutron star crust. Usually, such EOS is obtained assuming (implicitly) that the free (unbound) neutrons and nuclei in the inner crust move together. We argue, that this assumption violates the condition $μ_n^\infty={\rm const}$, required for hydrostatic (and diffusion) equilibrium of unbound neutrons ($μ^\infty_n$ is the redshifted neutron chemical potential). We construct a new EOS respecting this condition, working in the compressible liquid-drop approximation. We demonstrate that it is close to the catalyzed EOS in most part of the inner crust, being very different from EOSs of accreted crust discussed in the literature. In particular, the pressure at the outer-inner crust interface does not coincide with the neutron drip pressure, usually calculated in the literature, and is determined by hydrostatic (and diffusion) equilibrium conditions within the star. We also find an instability at the bottom of fully accreted crust that transforms nuclei into homogeneous nuclear matter. It guarantees that the structure of fully accreted crust remains self-similar during accretion.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard assumption in models of accreting neutron star crusts, that unbound neutrons and nuclei move together, violates the condition μ_n^∞ = const required for hydrostatic and diffusion equilibrium of free neutrons. The authors construct a new equation of state (nHD EOS) that respects this condition and find it is close to the catalyzed (ground state) EOS, not the traditional accreted crust EOSs.

## Method

The authors work in the compressible liquid-drop approximation (CLDM) using the SLy4 energy density functional, with surface energy and tension from Ref. DHM00. They minimize the energy density of a Wigner-Seitz cell with respect to independent variables to obtain beta-equilibrium, mechanical, and local neutron diffusion equilibrium conditions, yielding a two-parameter EOS ε = ε(n_b, n_N). For the fully accreted crust, the nHD condition μ_n^∞ = const leads to the requirement μ_N/μ_n = C, where C is determined by matching μ_n at the crust-core boundary.

## Key numbers

- Pressure at the outer-inner crust interface P_oi: 8.0 × 10^29 dyn cm^−2 (catalyzed), 8.1 × 10^29 dyn cm^−2 (nHD), and 9.1 × 10^29 dyn cm^−2 (Trad).
- Pressure at the crust-core boundary: 4.93 × 10^32 dyn cm^−2 (catalyzed), 5.20 × 10^32 dyn cm^−2 (nHD), and 5.14 × 10^32 dyn cm^−2 (Trad).
- The constant C for nHD EOS is approximately 0.0025.
- The flat region in the Trad EOS at ρ ∼ 1.1 × 10^12 g cm^−3 corresponds to a pycnonuclear reaction.
- The narrow nonsuperfluid layer near the outer-inner crust interface has width L ≲ 5 m for T = 5 × 10^8 K, with diffusion time τ^D ≲ 3 × 10^6 s and replacement timescale ≳ 2 × 10^9 s (at ρ ∼ 4.3 × 10^11 g cm^−3 and Eddington accretion rate ∼ 10^5 g cm^−2 s^−1).
- Preliminary estimate of net heat release for nHD EOS is ∼ 0.5–0.7 MeV/nucleon, 2–3 times smaller than in the traditional approach.

## Context

This work extends the series of studies on accreted neutron star crust evolution starting with Sato (1979) and including Haensel and Zdunik (1990, 1990b, 2003, 2008), Steiner (2012), Lau et al. (2018), and Fantina et al. (2018). It contradicts the traditional approach used in those works by showing that allowing unbound neutrons to move independently of nuclei dramatically changes the crust composition and EOS. The new nHD EOS is close to the catalyzed crust EOS, suggesting accretion has a less pronounced effect on crust thickness, tidal deformability, and heat release than previously thought, which may affect interpretation of transiently accreting neutron stars and problems like shallow heating and superburst ignition.
