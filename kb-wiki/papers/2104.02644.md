# Empirical determination of the energy loss of heavy quarks in nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies

> Heavy quarks produced in the heavy ion collisions loose energy while propagting in the hot partonic matter which finally fragment to heavy ($D$ or $B$) mesons. The energy loss suffered by the heavy quarks is imprinted in the nuclear modification factor as a function of transverse momenta ($p_T$) of these heavy mesons. An alternate measure of in-medium energy loss comes through the effective shift in transverse momentum spectra of hadrons recorded in nucleus-nucleus collisions when it is compared to the same in proton-proton collisions. We start by parametrizing invariant momentum yields of heavy mesons in p+p collisions. The fit function from p+p collisions and measured nuclear modification factor in heavy ion collisions are then utilised to obtain the shift in the transeverse mass $Δm_T$ of heavy mesons produced at the RHIC and LHC experiments.The energy loss $Δm_T$ so obtained is found to scale with the transverse mass ($m_T$) of heavy mesons through a power law at different energies and centralities of collisions. We have also calculated using theoretical formalism, the total energy loss suffered by a charm quark in quark-gluon plasma produced in Pb~+~Pb collisions at the LHC energies. The evolution of the plasma is described by (2+1) dimensional longitudinal boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamics. It is found that the total energy loss of charm quarks scales with the transverse mass of charm quarks through a similar power law which supports our empirical analysis of energy loss.

## Key claim

The energy loss of heavy quarks in nuclear collisions, quantified as the shift in transverse mass (Δm_T) of heavy mesons, scales with the transverse mass (m_T) through a power law at different collision energies and centralities, and this empirical scaling is supported by theoretical calculations of charm quark energy loss in a hydrodynamically evolving quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors parameterized the invariant momentum yields of D^0 and B^+ mesons in proton-proton collisions using a Hagedorn function. They then used the measured nuclear modification factor (R_AA) in heavy ion collisions together with the Hagedorn fit parameters to extract the shift in transverse mass (Δm_T) of heavy mesons at RHIC (200 GeV) and LHC (2.76 and 5.02 TeV) energies. Separately, they calculated the total energy loss of a charm quark in Pb+Pb collisions using collisional and radiative energy loss formalisms, with the plasma evolution described by (2+1)-dimensional longitudinal boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

* The power n of the Hagedorn fit for D^0 mesons in p+p collisions is 11.77 ± 0.70 at √s = 200 GeV (STAR), 6.96 ± 0.69 at √s = 2.76 TeV (CMS), and 6.32 ± 0.32 at √s = 5.02 TeV (CMS).
* The power α in the Δm_T versus m_T power-law fit for D^0 mesons in 0-10% central collisions is 0.24 ± 0.14 (CMS, 2.76 TeV, fit range m_T = [5.0,20.0] GeV) and 0.17 ± 0.08 (CMS, 5.02 TeV, fit range m_T = [5.0,40.0] GeV).
* For D^0 mesons in 0-10% central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV (STAR), the power α is 0.73 ± 0.14 (fit range m_T = [3.0,16.0] GeV).
* The normalization parameter a_1 for D^0 mesons in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV increases from 0.45 ± 0.07 (60-80% centrality) to 1.64 ± 0.01 (0-10% centrality), with the power α remaining nearly constant around 0.38-0.45.

## Context

This work extends the empirical approach to energy loss previously applied to light hadrons by Wang (2001), Horowitz (2010), and the PHENIX Collaboration (2012) to heavy mesons (D and B) at both RHIC and LHC energies. The authors compare their empirical Δm_T scaling with theoretical calculations using the DGLV, XDZR, and corrected AJMS formalisms for radiative energy loss and the Peigne-Peshier formalism for collisional energy loss. The results enable systematic comparison of heavy quark energy loss mechanisms across collision energies and centralities, and support the interpretation that the energy loss mechanism depends chiefly on collision energy with weak centrality dependence.
