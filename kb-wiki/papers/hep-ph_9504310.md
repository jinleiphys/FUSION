# The Chiral Phase Transition in QCD: Critical Phenomena and Long Wavelength Pion Oscillations

> In QCD with two massless quarks, the chiral phase transition is plausibly in the same universality class as the classical O(4) magnet. To test this hypothesis, critical exponents characterizing the behaviour of universal quantities near the 2nd order critical point can be calculated and compared to results from lattice simulations. Present simulations already allow many qualitative tests; quantitative tests await future simulations with longer correlation lengths. In a heavy ion collision, a long correlation length would lead to large fluctuations in the number ratio of neutral to charged pions. Unfortunately, no equilibrium correlation length gets long enough for this to occur. Modelling the dynamics of the chiral order parameter in a far from equilibrium transition by quenching in the linear sigma model suggests that long wavelength modes of the pion field can be amplified. This could have dramatic phenomenological consequences. Theoretical advances include attempts to relax the quench approximation and to include expansion and quantum effects. Long wavelength pion oscillations arise in a number of theoretical treatments; however, all involve idealizations and are at best qualitative guides. It is up to experimentalists to determine whether such phenomena occur; detection in a heavy ion collision would imply an out of equilibrium chiral transition.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition in QCD with two massless quarks is plausibly in the same universality class as the classical O(4) magnet, but quantitative tests of this hypothesis await future lattice simulations with longer correlation lengths, and the equilibrium correlation length does not get long enough for dramatic pion fluctuations to occur in heavy ion collisions due to explicit chiral symmetry breaking from finite up and down quark masses.

## Method

The author applies renormalization group techniques from condensed matter physics to calculate critical exponents characterizing the scaling behavior of universal quantities near the critical point of the chiral phase transition, assuming the transition is second order and in the O(4) universality class. For the out-of-equilibrium case, the dynamics of the chiral order parameter are modeled by quenching in the O(4) linear sigma model, with numerical simulations of the period immediately following the quench. The analysis also reviews attempts to relax the quench approximation and to include expansion and quantum mechanical effects.

## Key numbers

- Mean transverse momentum of secondary particles in high energy proton-proton collisions: p_T ~ 0.35 GeV
- At RHIC, Au-Au collisions with sqrt(s) = 200 GeV/A are expected to achieve multiplicities of at least 1400 per unit rapidity
- At the LHC, Pb-Pb collisions with sqrt(s) = 6300 GeV/A are expected to achieve multiplicities of at least 2600 per unit rapidity
- At RHIC, y_max ~ 5; at the LHC, y_max ~ 9
- The meson-to-baryon number ratio in the central region at RHIC is estimated to be ~ 10^2; at the LHC, this ratio is expected to be much greater than 10^5
- At the latest times considered by Geiger (approximately 3 fm/c), the baryon number is in fragmentation regions of high rapidity

## Context

This work extends the universality arguments first advanced by Pisarski and Wilczek for the chiral phase transition, and builds on original work done with Wilczek to develop quantitative predictions for equilibrium critical phenomena. The analysis of out-of-equilibrium pion oscillations follows from quenching studies in the O(4) linear sigma model. The paper enables future lattice simulations to make quantitative measurements of critical exponents and the equation of state, and provides a qualitative guide for experimental searches for long wavelength pion oscillations in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, where detection would imply an out-of-equilibrium chiral transition.
