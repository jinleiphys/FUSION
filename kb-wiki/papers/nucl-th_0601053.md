# Dynamical coupled-channel approach to hadronic and electromagnetic production of kaon-hyperon on the proton

> A dynamical coupled-channel formalism for processes $πN \to KY$ and $γN \to KY$ is presented which provides a comprehensive investigation of recent data on the $γp \to K^+ Λ$ reaction. The non-resonant interactions within the subspace $KY\oplusπN$ are derived from effective Lagrangians, using a unitary transformation method. The calculations of photoproduction amplitudes are simplified by casting the coupled-channel equations into a form such that the empirical $γN \to πN$ amplitudes are input and only the parameters associated with the $KY$ channel are determined by performing $χ^2$-fits to all of the available data for $π^- p \to K^\circΛ, K^\circΣ^\circ$ and $γp \to K^+Λ$. Good agreement between our models and those data are obtained. In the fits to $πN \to KY$ channels, most of the parameters are constrained within $\pm 20%$ of the values given by the Particle Data Group and/or quark model predictions, while for $γp \to K^+ Λ$ parameters, ranges compatible with broken $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ symmetry are imposed. The main reaction mechanisms in $K^+ Λ$ photoproduction are singled out and issues related to newly suggested resonances $S_{11}$, $P_{13}$, and $D_{13}$ are studied. Results illustrating the importance of using a coupled-channel treatment are reported. Meson cloud effects on the $γN \to N^*$ transitions are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dynamical coupled-channel formalism for kaon-hyperon production that achieves good agreement with available data, but the authors acknowledge that the model parameters are constrained within ±20% of values from the Particle Data Group and/or quark model predictions, and that the approach is more in line with SAPHIR differential cross-section data than with CLAS results.

## Method

The authors developed a coupled-channel formalism restricted to the γN, πN, and KY subspaces, deriving non-resonant interactions from effective Lagrangians using a unitary transformation method. They simplified the photoproduction amplitude calculations by casting the coupled-channel equations so that empirical γN → πN amplitudes from the SAID analysis are used as input, and only parameters associated with the KY channel are determined via χ²-minimization fits to π⁻p → K°Λ, π⁻p → K°Σ°, and γp → K⁺Λ data. For the direct γN → KY channel, they employed a chiral constituent quark model (CQM) with SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry breaking coefficients as adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- Reduced χ² = 1.86 for the πN → KY fits (Table 1)
- For γp → K⁺Λ, reduced χ² values were 2.1 for CLAS data and 1.3 for SAPHIR data when fitted separately
- The KYN coupling constant g_{KNΛ} was determined to be 8.02 (Model M₁) and 8.00 (Model M₂)
- Cut-off parameters: Λ_s = 623.0 MeV, Λ_u = 1468.0 MeV, Λ_i = 930.0 MeV, Λ_{πN} = 1491.0 MeV
- The off-shell cutoff Λ for the γN → πN amplitude was found to be 1.5 GeV
- Data sets included: 720 differential cross-section points from ELSA/SAPHIR, 1068 from JLab/CLAS, 233 recoil polarization asymmetry points from JLab/CLAS, 44 polarized beam asymmetry points from SPring-8/LEPS, and 3 polarized target asymmetry points from Bonn synchrotron

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel formalism developed in earlier papers by the same group (CC-01, CC-04, CC-05a, CC-05b) by incorporating proper treatment of off-shell effects and refining the treatment of πN channel distortion effects that were neglected in previous calculations. The approach contrasts with isobar models in tree approximation used by earlier phenomenological works (ELA-SL, ELA-MB, ELA-SK, ELA-BE, Ireland 2004, Bydzovsky 2004) and K-matrix based approaches (K-matrix-1, K-matrix-2). The results serve as a starting point for future investigations including additional meson-baryon channels and all KY channels, with the authors noting that upcoming data from EPECUR and J-PARC projects will afford deeper insights into meson-baryon interactions.
