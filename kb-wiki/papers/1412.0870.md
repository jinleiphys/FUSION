# Ward identities, $\bm{ B\to V}$ transition form factors and applications

> Long distance effects are studied in the rare exclusive semileptonic $B_{(d,s)}\to V \ell^+ \ell^-$ decays, where $V$ denotes a $K^*$ or $ϕ$ meson. The form factors, which describe the meson transition amplitudes in the effective Hamiltonian approach, are calculated by means of Ward identities, experimental constraints and extrapolated within a general vector meson dominance framework. These form factors are then compared to the ones obtained in Lattice QCD simulations, with Light Cone Sum Rules and a Dyson-Schwinger equation approach. Additionally, the $B_d\to K^* \ell^+ \ell^-$ and $B_s\to ϕ\ell^+ \ell^- $ branching ratios are computed and the differential branching fractions are given as a function of the squared-momentum transfer.

# Ward identities, B→V transition form factors and applications

## Key claim
The authors use Ward identities to relate B→V transition form factors (V = K*, φ) in a model-independent way at q²=0, then extrapolate to the full kinematic range using a vector meson dominance framework. The extracted form factors yield branching ratios for B_d→K*μ⁺μ⁻ and B_s→φμ⁺μ⁻ that agree with experimental PDG averages within uncertainties, though the central values are higher than the experimental central values.

## Method
The authors derive relations between vector (V), axial-vector (A₁, A₂, A₀), and tensor (F₁, F₂, F₃) form factors using Ward identities in the heavy quark limit. They extract the universal parameter g₊(0) from experimental B→Vγ branching ratios, then extrapolate form factors to the full q² range using a double-pole parametrization with B* and B_A* masses and their radial excitations. The resulting form factors are used to compute differential and total branching ratios for B_d→K*μ⁺μ⁻ and B_s→φμ⁺μ⁻ decays.

## Key numbers
- g₊(0)^{B_d→K*} = 0.365^{+0.025}_{-0.025}, extracted from Br(B_d→K*γ) = (4.33 ± 0.15)×10⁻⁵
- g₊(0)^{B_s→φ} = 0.335^{+0.02}_{-0.02}, extracted from Br(B_s→φγ) = (3.6 ± 0.4)×10⁻⁵
- Present work Br(B_d→K*μ⁺μ⁻) = (1.37 ± 0.2)×10⁻⁶, compared to PDG average (1.06 ± 0.09)×10⁻⁶
- Present work Br(B_s→φμ⁺μ⁻) = (1.21 ± 0.15)×10⁻⁶, compared to PDG average (7.6 ± 1.5)×10⁻⁷
- Form factor V(0) for B_d→K* = 0.456, with ratio V(q²_max)/V(0) = 6.267
- Form factor A₁(0) for B_d→K* = 0.365, with ratio A₁(q²_max)/A₁(0) = 1.226
- Form factor A₂(0) for B_d→K* = 0.316, with ratio A₂(q²_max)/A₂(0) = 3.186
- Form factor V(0) for B_s→φ = 0.433, with ratio V(q²_max)/V(0) = 5.926
- Form factor A₁(0) for B_s→φ = 0.335, with ratio A₁(q²_max)/A₁(0) = 1.301
- Form factor A₂(0) for B_s→φ = 0.282, with ratio A₂(q²_max)/A₂(0) = 2.936

## Context
This work extends the vector meson dominance approach previously applied to B→ρ (Ref. RD1), B→γ (Ref. RD2), B→K₁ (Ref. AP), and B_c→D_s* (Ref. AP1) decays to the B→K* and B→φ transitions. The form factors are compared with those from Lattice QCD simulations (Ref. lat), Light Cone Sum Rules (Ref. AAli), and a Dyson-Schwinger equation approach (Ref. DSE). The results enable improved predictions for rare semileptonic B decays under experimental scrutiny at LHCb, and the method can be extended to other heavy-to-light transitions where Ward identities provide model-independent constraints at zero momentum transfer.
