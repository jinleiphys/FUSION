# A harmonic oscillator in nonadditive statistics and a novel transverse momentum spectrum in high-energy collisions

> It is widely observed that particles produced in high-energy collisions follow a power-law distribution. One such power-law distribution used extensively in the phenomenological studies owes its origin to nonadditive statistics proposed by C. Tsallis. In this article, we derive a novel nonadditive generalization of the conventional Bose-Einstein distribution using a single-mode harmonic oscillator. The approach taken in this paper eliminates the need of a regularization procedure proposed in previous works. We observe that the spectra of the bosonic particles like the pions and kaons produced in high-energy collisions are well-described by the nonadditive bosonic distribution derived in this paper.

# A harmonic oscillator in nonadditive statistics and a novel transverse momentum spectrum in high-energy collisions

## Key claim

The authors derive a novel nonadditive generalization of the Bose-Einstein distribution from a single-mode harmonic oscillator, which eliminates the need for a regularization procedure required in previous derivations. The resulting distribution describes pion and kaon transverse momentum spectra from ALICE data across the full measured range, whereas the conventional Bose-Einstein distribution deviates from experimental data at transverse momenta around 0.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors extremize the Tsallis nonadditive entropy (with the normalized averaging scheme) for a grand canonical ensemble, then apply the Hilhorst integral transformation to express nonadditive single-particle distributions in terms of Boltzmann-Gibbs averages. They evaluate this integral analytically for a single-mode harmonic oscillator (with chemical potential set to zero), obtaining a closed form involving the Hurwitz zeta function. The resulting single-particle distribution is then integrated over azimuthal angle and parameterized in terms of transverse mass and rapidity to produce the transverse momentum spectrum used for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

* For $\pi^+$ spectra in p-p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=900$ GeV (ALICE data): $q = 0.8795 \pm 0.004391$, $T = 0.0883 \pm 0.002957$ GeV, $R = 3.873 \pm 0.1063$ fm, $\chi^2$/NDF = 2.667/30.
* For $K^+$ spectra in p-p collisions at $\sqrt{s}=900$ GeV (ALICE data): $q = 0.853 \pm 0.01213$, $T = 0.0657 \pm 0.01433$ GeV, $R = 3.994 \pm 1.083$ fm, $\chi^2$/NDF = 5.357/24.
* The conventional Bose-Einstein distribution deviates from experimental data at transverse momentum around 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior derivations of nonadditive quantum distributions by Parvan and Bhattacharyya (2020) that required a regularization scheme to handle divergences. The single-mode harmonic oscillator approach avoids this regularization entirely. The result is a bosonic distribution that, in the high-energy limit ($\varepsilon_p \gg T, \Lambda$), reduces to the classical nonadditive Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution that appears as the stationary solution of the nonadditive Boltzmann transport equation proposed by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2000). The work enables future extensions to fermionic systems via a fermionic oscillator, estimation of thermal mass and strong coupling $\alpha_s$ in terms of temperature and $q$, and formulation of a nonadditive equation of state for studies of Quark-Gluon Plasma or stellar matter.
