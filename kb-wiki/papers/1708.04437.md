# From quark and nucleon correlations to discrete symmetry and clustering in nuclei

> Starting with a quark model of nucleon structure in which the valence quarks are strongly correlated within a nucleon, the light nuclei are constructed by assuming similar correlations of the quarks of neighboring nucleons. Applying the model to larger collections of nucleons reveals the emergence of the face-centered cubic (FCC) symmetry at the nuclear level. Nuclei with closed shells possess octahedral symmetry. Binding of nucleons are provided by quark loops formed by three and four nucleon correlations. Quark loops are responsible for formation of exotic (borromean) nuclei, as well. The model unifies independent particle (shell) model, liquid-drop and cluster models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nucleons within nuclei are bound via quark-quark interactions that lead to strong nucleon-nucleon correlations, and that the resulting nuclear geometry corresponds to a face-centered cubic (FCC) lattice, unifying the shell, liquid-drop, and cluster models. The claim is hedged by the statement that the model is qualitative and must include a quantitative consideration of nuclear forces based on quark-quark interactions as a task for further development.

## Method

The author proposes a Strongly Correlated Quark Model (SCQM) in which valence quarks inside a nucleon oscillate under a soliton-antisoliton potential derived from the Sine-Gordon equation, with parameters adjusted to reproduce pp and pbar-p scattering data. Light nuclei are then constructed by assuming that quarks of neighboring nucleons overlap their color fields, forming quark loops that bind nucleons according to rules of SU(3) color antisymmetry, SU(2) isospin antisymmetry, and SU(2) spin symmetry. The resulting nucleon arrangements are shown to correspond to an FCC lattice with alternating spin and isospin layers, and the model is applied to describe shell structure, clustering, pairing effects, and nuclear deformation.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass: M_Q(x_max) = 360 MeV, taken as one-third of the average of the delta isobar and nucleon masses.
- Maximum quark displacement: x_max = 0.64 fm.
- Gaussian parameters for hadronic matter distribution around valence quarks: sigma_x,y = 0.24 fm, sigma_z = 0.12 fm.
- Binding energy per nucleon for deuteron (2H): 1.11 MeV, with 0 quark loops and 4 unbound quark ends.
- Binding energy per nucleon for 3H: 2.83 MeV, with 1 quark loop and 3 unbound quark ends.
- Binding energy per nucleon for 3He: 2.57 MeV, with 1 quark loop and 3 unbound quark ends.
- Binding energy per nucleon for 4He: 7.07 MeV, with 4 quark loops and 0 unbound quark ends.
- The additional potential well depth from quark-quark interactions between neighboring nucleons is approximately 2-8 MeV.
- In the FCC lattice, the maximal number of nearest neighbors to any nucleon is 12.

## Context

This work extends the FCC lattice model of nuclear structure developed by N.D. Cook (Cook1, Cook2, Cook3) over more than 30 years, and the SCQM previously introduced by Musulmanbekov (Mus1, Mus2, Mus3). The model aims to resolve the long-standing dilemma that the shell model, liquid-drop model, and cluster model each work well for limited data sets but are mutually exclusive. The approach enables a unified description of nuclear properties including shell structure, clustering, pairing effects, halo nuclei, and collective motion, while also predicting that nuclei with closed shells possess octahedral symmetry and that all nuclei are deformed. The author notes that model-independent analysis of electron scattering data by Sick (1987) found depressions in nuclear matter distributions for 3- and 4-nucleon systems, consistent with the model's predictions.
