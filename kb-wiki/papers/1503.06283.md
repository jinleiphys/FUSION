# Strange behavior of rapidity dependent strangeness enhancement of particles containing and not containing leading quarks

> Rapidity dependent strangeness enhancement factors for the identified particles have been studied with the help of a string based hadronic transport model UrQMD-3.3 (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) at FAIR energies. A strong rapidity dependent strangeness enhancement could be observed with our generated data for $Au + Au$ collisions at the beam energy of 30\textit{A} GeV. The strangeness enhancement is found to be maximum at mid-rapidity for the particles containing leading quarks while for particles consisting of produced quarks only, the situation is seen to be otherwise. Such rapidity dependent strangeness enhancement could be traced back to the dependence of rapidity width on centrality or otherwise on the distribution of net-baryon density.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the rapidity dependence of strangeness enhancement in Au+Au collisions at 30A GeV follows two distinct patterns depending on quark content: maximum at mid-rapidity for particles containing leading quarks (u or d from the incoming nuclei) and minimum at mid-rapidity for particles composed only of produced quarks, with the caveat that this behavior is traced to the centrality dependence of the rapidity width and the distribution of net-baryon density.

## Method

The authors used the string-based hadronic transport model UrQMD-3.3 in default mode to generate 93 million minimum-bias events for Au+Au collisions at a beam energy of 30A GeV. They calculated the strangeness enhancement factor E_S as the ratio of the yield in central collisions (impact parameter 0-4.1 fm) to that in peripheral collisions (impact parameter 9-13 fm), normalized by the average number of produced negative pions, and plotted this factor as a function of rapidity for various identified strange hadrons. Additional event samples were generated to test the influence of specific reaction channels (switching off Xi K to Omega pi channels and switching off baryon-antibaryon annihilation) and to test the Lund fragmentation function as an alternative to the default Field-Feynman scheme.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 30A GeV for Au+Au collisions.
- Central collision impact parameter window: 0-4.1 fm; peripheral window: 9-13 fm.
- Total events used for the main analysis: 93 million minimum-bias UrQMD events.
- For the test switching off Xi K to Omega pi channels: 60 million additional minimum-bias events.
- For the test switching off baryon-antibaryon annihilation: 20 million additional events at 30A GeV.
- For the test using Lund fragmentation: 45 million additional events.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same authors (Dey, 2015) on the rapidity width of Lambda and anti-Lambda as a function of beam rapidity, and is motivated by predictions from Steinheimer (2013) that strangeness is not uniformly distributed over rapidity space, leading to local violation of strangeness conservation. The study is designed to anticipate measurements possible with the large-acceptance detectors of the upcoming FAIR-CBM experiment. The observed centrality dependence of rapidity width for kaons and lambdas is consistent with results reported by the NA49 collaboration for 40A GeV Pb+Pb collisions.
