# Fragments in Gaussian Wave-Packet Dynamics with and without Correlations

> Generalization of Gaussian trial wave functions in quantum molecular dynamics models is introduced, which allows for long-range correlations characteristic for composite nuclear fragments. We demonstrate a significant improvement in the description of light fragments with the correlations. Utilizing either type of Gaussian wave functions, with or without correlations, however, we find that we cannot describe fragment formation in a dynamic situation. Composite fragments are only produced in simulations if these fragments are present as clusters in the substructure of original nuclei. The difficulty is traced to the delocalization of wave functions during emission. Composite fragments are produced abundantly in the Gaussian molecular dynamics in the limit hbar -> 0.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that introducing long-range correlations into Gaussian trial wave functions significantly improves the description of light nuclear fragments like deuterons and alpha particles, but even with these correlations, fragment formation cannot be described in dynamic reaction simulations unless the fragments are already present as clusters in the substructure of the original nuclei.

## Method

The authors generalized the product of single-particle Gaussian wave functions used in quantum molecular dynamics models to a correlated Gaussian with a width matrix A that allows full separation of center-of-mass and intrinsic coordinates for any nucleon cluster. They derived equations of motion from the time-dependent variational principle and performed simulations of excited nuclear systems (A=80) and nuclear collisions, comparing dynamics with static wave-packet widths, dynamic uncorrelated widths, and dynamic correlated widths.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass localization energy for light clusters: ΔE ~ (10-20) MeV, leading to suppression factors e^(-ΔE/T) < (1/10 - 1/3) for temperatures T < 10 MeV
- In uncorrelated dynamics, a free deuteron becomes unbound at t ~ 12 fm/c due to energy exchange between intrinsic and center-of-mass motion
- Decay simulations used systems of mass A = 80 with initial radius R = 5 fm, temperatures T = 5-12 MeV, and radial flow energies E_col = 0-25 MeV/nucleon
- Initial Gaussian width parameter Δx = 1.9 fm for static width calculations
- In collision simulations of two ¹²C nuclei, pre-existing alpha clusters could break up into three alpha particles, but collisions of two ⁴⁰Ca nuclei with no internal substructure produced no intermediate-mass fragments

## Context

This work extends the family of quantum molecular dynamics approaches including QMD (Aichelin 1986, Maruyama 1990), AMD (Ono 1992), EQMD (Maruyama 1996), and FMD (Feldmeier 1990, 1995). The authors identify a fundamental limitation: the Gaussian parametrization cannot describe fragment formation from spreading wave packets at low density, even with correlations. This contradicts FMD results (Feldmeier 1995) reporting multifragmentation events, which the authors attribute to pre-existing clusters surviving the reaction rather than new clusters forming. The work suggests that successful fragment description requires wave functions flexible enough to change over distances comparable to the interaction range at the time of fragment formation.
