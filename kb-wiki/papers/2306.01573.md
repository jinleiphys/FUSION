# Equation Generator for Equation-of-Motion Coupled Cluster Assisted by Computer Algebra System

> We present an equation generator algorithm that utilizes second-quantized operators in normal order with respect to a correlated or non-correlated reference and the corresponding Wick theorem. The algorithm proposed here, written with \textsc{mathematica}, enables the generation of non-redundant strings of second-quantized operators that, after classification, are directly assigned to intermediate quantities used to construct the coupled-cluster (CC) effective Hamiltonian. We demonstrate the capabilities of the algorithm by computing the CC amplitude equations and various blocks of the equation-of-motion CC effective Hamiltonian. A comprehensive description of this four-step algorithm is provided alongside concrete examples.

## Key claim

The paper presents a four-step algorithm implemented in Mathematica that automatically generates the working equations for equation-of-motion coupled cluster (EOM-CC) methods, including neutral excitations (EE-EOM-CC), ionization potentials (IP-EOM-CC), and electron affinities (EA-EOM-CC), by using second-quantized operators in normal order and Wick's theorem to avoid redundant terms through the introduction of many-body intermediate quantities.

## Method

The algorithm uses second-quantized operators in normal order with respect to a Fermi vacuum (typically the Hartree-Fock determinant) and applies Wick's theorem to evaluate products of operator strings. It generates non-redundant strings of second-quantized operators, classifies them, and assigns them to many-body intermediate quantities that are used to construct the coupled-cluster effective Hamiltonian. The method relies on the Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff expansion of the similarity-transformed Hamiltonian, which terminates at the four-fold commutator due to the two-body nature of the electronic Hamiltonian, and expresses the EOM-CC matrix elements in terms of connected contractions between the Hamiltonian and cluster operators.

## Context

This work extends the line of automated equation generators for coupled-cluster methods pioneered by Janssen and Schaefer (1991) and later developed by Hirata through the Tensor Contraction Engine (TCE) (2003-2006), as well as the SMITH3 interpreter by Matthew and coworkers (2015) and the Wick and D approach by Evangelista (2022). The algorithm enables the systematic derivation of EOM-CC working equations for neutral excitations, ionization potentials, and electron affinities, and facilitates comparison between many-body perturbation theory and coupled-cluster methods as discussed by Monino and Loos (2022) and Berkelbach (2018).
