# Extended RPA with ground-state correlations in a solvable model

> The ground states and excited states of the Lipkin model hamiltonian are calculated using a new theoretical approach which has been derived from an extended time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory known as the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM). TDDM enables us to calculate correlated ground states, and its small amplitude limit (STDDM), which is a version of extended RPA theories based on a correlated ground state, can be used to calculate excited states. It is found that this TDDM plus STDDM approach gives much better results for both the ground states and the excited states than the Hartree-Fock ground state plus RPA approach.

## Key claim

The time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) plus its small-amplitude limit (STDDM) gives much better results for both ground states and excited states of the Lipkin model than the Hartree-Fock (HF) ground state plus RPA approach, but the two-phonon state calculated in STDDM is not as good as the one-phonon state due to missing three-body effects, which can be partially remedied by a modified STDDM (mSTDDM).

## Method

The authors solve the TDDM equations for the correlated ground state of the N=4 Lipkin model using an iterative gradient method, starting from the HF ground state and gradually increasing the interaction strength. Excited states are obtained by solving the STDDM equations in matrix form for one-body and two-body transition amplitudes, and strength functions are computed for one-phonon and two-phonon excitation operators. A modified version (mSTDDM) adds three-body effects to the hamiltonian matrix acting on two-body amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Total energy deviation from exact in TDDM is only 1.1% at |V|/epsilon = 0.3 for N=4.
- Energy-weighted sum m1 of transition strength for the one-phonon state is 14.2 in STDDM, compared to exact value 14.6 and RPA value 15.2.
- The interaction strength used for excited state calculations is V = -0.6, slightly smaller than the critical value V = -2/3.
- Strength functions are smoothed with an artificial width Gamma/epsilon = 0.025.

## Context

This work extends the TDDM plus STDDM approach previously applied to unstable oxygen isotopes (Tohyama and Schuck, 2004; Takahara et al., 2004) by testing it on the solvable Lipkin model (Lipkin, 1965). The approach improves upon standard RPA (Ring and Schuck, 1980) and second RPA (SRPA) by including ground-state correlations and two-body amplitudes. The results enable further development of extended RPA theories that incorporate three-body effects for better description of multi-phonon states.
