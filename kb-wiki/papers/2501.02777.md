# Extracting the Speed of Sound in Heavy-Ion Collisions: A Study of Quantum-Initiated Fluctuations and Thermalization

> The thermalization of quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions is crucial for understanding its behavior as a relativistic fluid and the thermodynamic properties of the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). This study investigates the role of fluctuations in the relationship between transverse momentum and particle multiplicity, with a particular focus on their impact on extracting the QCD speed of sound. In a thermalized quark-gluon plasma, these fluctuations mostly originate from quantum fluctuations in the colliding nuclei, and exhibit a Gaussian distribution as a consequence of their independence from thermodynamic response. In contrast, non-thermalized systems display non-Gaussian fluctuations, reflecting the breakdown of thermalization. By leveraging the Gaussianity condition of quantum-initiated fluctuations, the physical value of the speed of sound can be extracted statistically, even in the presence of significant event-by-event fluctuations. This framework provides a robust diagnostic tool for probing thermalization and extracting thermodynamic properties in both large and small collision systems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the QCD speed of sound can be extracted statistically from the relationship between mean transverse momentum and charged particle multiplicity in ultracentral heavy-ion collisions, even in the presence of large event-by-event fluctuations, provided the system is locally thermalized. The key condition is that quantum-initiated fluctuations in a thermalized quark-gluon plasma follow a Gaussian distribution, which allows the thermodynamic response to be isolated from stochastic noise.

## Method

The authors generate two-dimensional joint probability distributions of mean transverse momentum variation and multiplicity variation from hybrid hydrodynamic simulations (using T$_{\rm R}$ENTo initial conditions, MUSIC viscous hydrodynamics with lattice QCD equation of state, and UrQMD for hadronization) for Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and p-Pb collisions at 5.02 and 8.16 TeV. For comparison, they also simulate non-thermal systems using HIJING and PYTHIA event generators. They extract the speed of sound by solving the zero skewness condition of the fluctuation distribution, and verify Gaussianity by examining higher-order cumulants such as kurtosis.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV from hydrodynamic simulations, the speed of sound squared extracted via zero skewness condition is $c_s^2 = 0.217 \pm 0.032$, compared to the lattice QCD equation of state (LEOS) expectation of 0.222-0.242
- For p-Pb collisions at 8.16 TeV from hydrodynamics, $c_s^2 = 0.292 \pm 0.013$ via zero skewness, compared to LEOS expectation of 0.282-0.309
- For p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV from hydrodynamics, $c_s^2 = 0.318 \pm 0.011$ via zero skewness, compared to LEOS expectation of 0.269-0.304
- For p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV from PYTHIA (non-thermal), $c_s^2 = 1.178 \pm 0.006$ via zero skewness, exceeding the causal limit of unity
- For p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV from HIJING (non-thermal), $c_s^2 = 1.104 \pm 0.019$ via zero skewness, also exceeding unity
- The standardized kurtosis of the fluctuation distribution for Pb-Pb hydrodynamics is $\kappa_4 = -0.064 \pm 0.096$, for p-Pb hydrodynamics at 5.02 TeV is $\kappa_4 = 0.16 \pm 0.05$, while for HIJING it is $\kappa_4 = 2.15 \pm 0.05$ and for PYTHIA it is $\kappa_4 = 1.91 \pm 0.11$

## Context

This work extends the recent approach of Gardim et al. (2019) for extracting the speed of sound from the correlation between mean transverse momentum and multiplicity in ultracentral collisions, which was applied experimentally by CMS (2024) and ALICE (2024). The paper addresses the previously unaccounted role of quantum-initiated fluctuations, which the authors show systematically bias the simpler sub-bin slope method. The proposed Gaussianity-based extraction method provides a direct diagnostic for local thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma, applicable to both large (Pb-Pb) and small (p-Pb) collision systems, and is expected to extend to lower collision energies relevant for the search of the QCD critical point.
