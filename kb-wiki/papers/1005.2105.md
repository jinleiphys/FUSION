# Is the nonmonotonic behavior in the cross section of phi photoproduction near threshold a signature of a resonance?

> We study whether the nonmonotonic behavior found in the differential cross section of the phi-meson photoproduction near threshold can be described by a resonance. Namely, we add a resonance to a model consisting of Pomeron and (pi,eta) exchange by fiat and see if, with a suitable assignment of spin and parity, mass and width, as well as the coupling constants, one would be able to obtain a good description to all the data reported by the LEPS collaboration in the low-energy region. The resonant contribution is evaluated by using an effective Lagrangian approach. We find that, with the assumption of a J^P=3/2^- resonance with mass of 2.10 +- 0.03 GeV and width of 0.465 +- 0.141 GeV, LEPS data can indeed be well described. The ratio of the helicity amplitudes A_(1/2)/A_(3/2) calculated from the resulting coupling constants differs in sign from that of the known D13(2080). We further find that the addition of this postulated resonance can substantially improve the agreement between the existing theoretical predictions and the recent omega photoproduction data if a large value of the OZI evading parameter x_OZI = 12 is assumed for the resonance.

## Key claim

The nonmonotonic behavior observed by LEPS in the forward differential cross section of phi photoproduction near threshold can be described by adding a single resonance with J^P = 3/2^- to a model of Pomeron plus (pi, eta) exchange, but the parity assignment is preferred only because the extracted properties are more stable against variations in the poorly-constrained low-energy Pomeron parameters, not because the data uniquely require negative parity.

## Method

The authors constructed a model for gamma p -> phi p consisting of t-channel Pomeron exchange, t-channel (pi, eta) exchange, and s- and u-channel nucleon-resonance exchange amplitudes evaluated with effective Lagrangians. They performed a chi-squared fit using MINUIT to the LEPS data (differential cross section at forward angle as a function of photon energy, t-dependence at eight energies, and five decay angular distributions in the Gottfried-Jackson frame) for resonance spin-parity assignments of 1/2^+, 1/2^-, 3/2^+, and 3/2^-, with the resonance mass, width, and coupling constants as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Resonance mass: 2.10 +/- 0.03 GeV (for J^P = 3/2^-)
- Resonance width: 0.465 +/- 0.141 GeV (for J^P = 3/2^-)
- Chi-squared per data point: 0.983 (for J^P = 3/2^-); 1.066 (for J^P = 3/2^+)
- Alternative fit (J^P = 3/2^+): mass 2.05 +/- 0.06 GeV, width 0.450 +/- 0.111 GeV
- Ratio of helicity amplitudes A_(1/2)/A_(3/2) = 1.16 for the J^P = 3/2^- resonance; this ratio is -1.18 for the known D13(2080) resonance
- OZI-evading parameter x_OZI = 12 assumed when applying the resonance to omega photoproduction

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of phi photoproduction that used only t-channel exchanges (Titov et al., various years) and previous resonance-inclusive models (Titov 2007; Zhao 1999) which included known resonances below 2 GeV but failed to reproduce the LEPS local maximum near E_gamma ~ 2.0 GeV. The analysis contrasts with a coupled-channel study by Ozaki (2009) that the authors claim was marred by a phase confusion in the Pomeron amplitude. The results enable predictions for polarization observables that could determine the resonance parity if confirmed, and suggest the resonance would have significant strangeness content if it also improves agreement with omega photoproduction data from M. Williams.
