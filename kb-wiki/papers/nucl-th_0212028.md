# Structure of Neutron-Rich Nuclei in the 132Sn Region

> We report on a study of neutron-rich nuclei around 132Sn in terms of the shell model employing a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. We present results for some Sb and Te isotopes. Comparison shows that our results are in very good agreement with the available experimental data supporting confidence in the predictions of our calculations. This may stimulate experimental efforts to gain more information on these nuclei lying well away from the valley of stability.

# Structure of Neutron-Rich Nuclei in the 132Sn Region

## Key claim
Shell-model calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, via the low-momentum Vlow-k approach, reproduce experimental spectra of neutron-rich Sb and Te isotopes around 132Sn with discrepancies typically below 100-170 keV, supporting the predictive power of the method for nuclei far from stability.

## Method
The authors assumed 132Sn as a closed core and performed shell-model calculations using the OXBASH code, with valence protons and neutron holes occupying the five single-particle levels of the 50-82 shell and valence neutrons occupying the six single-particle levels of the 82-126 shell. The effective interaction was derived from the CD-Bonn free NN potential by first constructing a low-momentum potential Vlow-k with a cutoff momentum of 2.1 fm^-1, then calculating matrix elements via a folded-diagram method; for 130,132Sb and 133Te the neutron-proton effective interaction was derived explicitly in the particle-hole formalism.

## Key numbers
- For 132Sb, discrepancies between calculated and experimental multiplet energies are "in the order of tens of keV", except for the 1+ state of the πd5/2 νd-13/2 multiplet which lies 300 keV above the experimental value.
- For 130Sb, the calculated ground state has Jπ = 4+, with the 8- state lying at 92 keV excitation energy.
- For 133Te, discrepancies between calculated and experimental excited levels are "well below 100 keV for most of them".
- For 135Te, the largest discrepancy between calculated and experimental spectra is about 170 keV.
- For 137Te, the largest discrepancy between calculated and experimental spectra is about 170 keV.

## Context
This work extends earlier shell-model studies of nuclei around 132Sn by Covello et al. (2001) and Coraggio et al. (2002), using a modern NN potential rather than older potentials like the Hamada-Johnston potential used by Kuo (1968) for the 208Pb region. The results revive theoretical interest in proton particle-neutron hole multiplets near doubly magic nuclei, following early work on 208Bi by Erskine (1964), Alford (1968), Kuo (1968), and Moinester (1969). The calculations may stimulate further experimental studies of neutron-rich nuclei using radioactive ion beams.
