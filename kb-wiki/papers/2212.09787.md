# Towards an effective action for chiral magnetohydrodynamics

> We consider chiral magnetohydrodynamics, i.e. a finite-temperature system where an axial $U(1)$ current is not conserved due to an Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly saturated by the dynamical operator $F_{μν} \tilde{F}^{μν}$. We express this anomaly in terms of the 1-form symmetry associated with magnetic flux conservation and study its realization at finite temperature. We present Euclidean generating functional and dissipative action approaches to the dynamics and reproduce some aspects of chiral MHD phenomenology from an effective theory viewpoint, including the chiral separation and magnetic effects. We also discuss the construction of non-invertible axial symmetry defect operators in our formalism.

## Key claim

The paper constructs effective actions for chiral magnetohydrodynamics that realize the non-invertible axial symmetry arising from the Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly at finite temperature, but only in the simplified limit where fluid velocity and temperature are frozen, and the construction is exploratory rather than fully general.

## Method

The authors place the theory on a thermal circle \(S^1 \times \mathbb{R}^3\) and construct a Euclidean equilibrium generating functional using background sources \(a_\mu\) (for the axial 0-form current) and \(b_{\mu\nu}\) (for the magnetic 1-form current). They impose the anomaly Ward identity as a constraint on the action, solving it order by order in the anomaly coefficient \(k\) to find gauge non-invariant terms. For the dissipative sector, they build a real-time Schwinger-Keldysh effective action by combining independent 0-form and 1-form sectors while preserving the anomaly structure.

## Key numbers

* The anomaly coefficient for massless QED is \(k = 1/(16\pi^2)\) (stated in the text as the value for a single species of Dirac fermions).
* The equilibrium action is constructed up to \(\mathcal{O}(\partial^2)\) in derivatives, with coefficients \(\chi_A\), \(\chi_B\), \(\chi_O\) treated as constants.
* The solution for the gauge non-invariant vector \(V^i\) is obtained to order \(k^2\), with explicit forms: \(V^i_{(0)} = \chi_B \chi_O B^i |h|\) and \(V^i_{(1)} = \frac{\alpha}{2} a^i + \frac{\gamma}{2} (a \cdot B) B^i\), where \(\alpha = \chi_O^2 \chi_B |h|^2\) and \(\gamma = \chi_B^2 \chi_O\).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic treatment of systems with 't Hooft anomalies (reviewed by Landsteiner (2016)) to the case of an Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly with dynamical gauge fields, building on the non-invertible symmetry framework of Choi (2022) and Cordova (2022). It contrasts with earlier chiral MHD constructions by Boyarsky (2015) and Rogachevskii (2017) that introduced a dynamical axion field, and with the entropy-current approach of Hattori (2017) that required the anomaly coefficient to be treated as a small parameter. The paper enables a systematic effective field theory description of chiral MHD without assuming a weakly coupled Maxwell sector, though it leaves open the construction of a fully general action with dynamical fluid velocity and temperature.
