# Parity doubling of baryons in a chiral approach with three flavors

> We formulate a set of mass relations for the baryon octet and decuplet with positive and negative parity in terms of the order parameter of QCD chiral symmetry. The Gell-Mann--Okubo mass formula and Gell-Mann's equal spacing rule hold manifestly in this approach. Thermal masses of the baryons are calculated in the mean field approximation for various pion masses, and the results are compared with the recent lattice studies. A general trend of the nucleon, $Δ$ and $Ω$ parity-doublers seen in the available lattice data can be understood qualitatively. Expected mass modifications of other strange baryons are also given with the physical and heavier pion masses.

## Key claim

The paper formulates mass relations for positive- and negative-parity baryon octet and decuplet states that are manifestly consistent with the Gell-Mann–Okubo mass formula and Gell-Mann’s equal spacing rule, but only under the condition that the quark condensates are much smaller than the chiral-invariant mass \(m_0\) (i.e., \(\sigma_{q,s} \ll m_0\)), which the author argues is well justified even at low temperature.

## Method

The author starts from a general SU(3) interaction Lagrangian for baryons and mesons and extends it to a parity-doublet picture by introducing a chiral-invariant mass \(m_0\) for two fermion fields. Linearized mass relations for the octet and decuplet parity doublers are derived under the assumption \(\sigma_{q,s} \ll m_0\), with additional parameters \(m_{1,2,3}\) and \(m_s\) to account for explicit SU(3) breaking while preserving the Gell-Mann–Okubo formula and equal spacing rule. Thermal masses are then computed by substituting in-medium quark condensates \(\sigma_q\) and \(\sigma_s\) obtained from a three-flavor linear sigma model in the mean-field approximation at zero chemical potential, for both physical and heavier pion masses.

## Key numbers

- Chiral-invariant mass \(m_0 = 0.889\) GeV (common for octet and decuplet in this work).
- Nucleon positive-parity mass \(m_{N_+} = 0.939\) GeV; negative-parity mass \(m_{N_-} = 1.535\) GeV (input).
- Delta positive-parity mass \(m_{\Delta_+} = 1.232\) GeV; negative-parity mass \(m_{\Delta_-} = 1.710\) GeV (input).
- Pion decay constant \(f_\pi = \sigma_q = 92.4\) MeV; kaon decay constant \(f_K = 113\) MeV (input).
- Current strange-quark mass \(m_s = 0.1\) GeV (used in decuplet mass relations).
- Predicted masses: \(m_{\Lambda_+} = 1.11\) GeV, \(m_{\Lambda_-} = 1.79\) GeV; \(m_{\Sigma_-} = 1.88\) GeV; \(m_{\Xi_-} = 2.09\) GeV; \(m_{\Sigma^\ast_-} = 1.93\) GeV; \(m_{\Xi^\ast_+} = 1.53\) GeV, \(m_{\Xi^\ast_-} = 2.15\) GeV; \(m_{\Omega_+} = 1.69\) GeV, \(m_{\Omega_-} = 2.38\) GeV.
- Pseudo-critical temperatures from the chiral model: \(T_c = 151\) MeV for physical \(r = (m_\pi/m_K)^2 = 0.077\); \(T_c = 203\) MeV for \(r = 0.64\); \(T_c = 272\) MeV for \(r = 0.8\).

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor parity-doublet model (Jido et al., 2000; Hatsuda, 1990) to three flavors in a way that respects the Gell-Mann–Okubo mass formula and equal spacing rule, which earlier three-flavor parameterizations (e.g., Sasaki & Redlich, 2010; Weyrich, 2015) did not satisfy. The thermal mass calculations are compared directly with the first systematic lattice QCD study of baryon parity doublers by Aarts et al. (2017) at \(m_\pi \sim 400\) MeV. The results enable future comparisons with lattice data for strange baryons (\(\Sigma, \Lambda, \Xi, \Sigma^\ast, \Xi^\ast\)) and motivate studies linking mass modifications to resonance widths and bulk thermodynamics in heavy-ion collisions.
