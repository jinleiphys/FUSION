# Charged charmonium-like structures and the initial single chiral particle emission mechanism

> This paper summarizes what we have done so far to explain charged charmonium/bottomonium-like structures using a hadronic triangle diagram which we call the initial single chiral particle emission mechanism. We discuss processes like $A\to A'+P+P'$ in which two chiral particles $P$ and $P'$ are emitted from $A$. In the intermediate we consider a hadronic one-loop diagram in which $D^{(*)}/D_s^{(*)}/B^{(*)}/B_s^{(*)}$ are included to explain some enhancements experimentally observed. Using this mechanism we explain some of the exotic enhancements and predict a couple of enhancement structures.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the charged charmonium-like and bottomonium-like structures observed experimentally (such as Z_b and Z_c states) can be explained by a hadronic triangle diagram mechanism called the initial single chiral particle emission (ISChE) mechanism, rather than requiring interpretation as genuine exotic hadrons; the mechanism specifically applies to processes where two chiral particles (pions or kaons) are emitted from a heavy quarkonium initial state.

## Method

The authors calculate hidden-bottom and hidden-charm dipion and dikaon decays of higher bottomonia and charmonia using a hadronic one-loop triangle diagram, where an initial heavy quarkonium (such as Upsilon(5S) or psi(4160)) emits a single chiral particle (pion or kaon), making the intermediate heavy mesons (B(*)/B_s(*) or D(*)/D_s(*)) have low momenta so they can rescatter into the final state. The mechanism is applied to processes like Upsilon(5S) -> Upsilon(nS) pi+ pi- and psi(4160) -> h_c(1P) pi+ pi-, with the intermediate loops containing pairs of heavy mesons near their thresholds.

## Key numbers

- Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) are observed near the B Bbar* and B* Bbar* thresholds respectively in Upsilon(5S) -> h_b(mP) pi+ pi- (m=1,2) and Upsilon(5S) -> Upsilon(nS) pi+ pi- (n=1,2,3) decays
- The branching ratios of Upsilon(5S) -> Upsilon(nS) pi+ pi- (n=1,2,3) are larger than dipion transition rates between lower Upsilon family members by two orders of magnitude
- Z_c(3900) was observed by BESIII in the J/psi pi+/- invariant mass spectrum of Y(4260) -> J/psi pi+ pi- at a mass near the D Dbar* threshold
- CLEO-c measured the h_c(1P) pi+/- mass distribution from e+ e- -> h_c(1P) pi+ pi- at center-of-mass energy E_CM = 4170 MeV
- Predicted charged charmonium-like structures near the D Dbar* threshold (around 3860 MeV) and D* Dbar* threshold (around 4016 MeV) in various decay channels
- Predicted charged charmonium-like structures with open-strange near 3976/3977 MeV in Y(4660) -> J/psi K+ K- and psi(4790) -> J/psi K+ K- decays

## Context

This work extends the initial single pion emission (ISPE) mechanism first proposed by Chen and Liu (2011) for Upsilon(5S) decays to the broader initial single chiral particle emission (ISChE) mechanism applicable to both pion and kaon emission. It contrasts with alternative explanations for peak structures such as threshold cusp effects considered by Wigner (1948), Cabibbo (2004), and Bugg (2008, 2011), and with molecular state interpretations proposed by Sun (2011) and Bondar (2011). The mechanism successfully predicted the Z_c(3900) structure later confirmed by BESIII and Belle, and provides a framework for predicting additional charged charmonium-like and bottomonium-like structures that can be tested by BESIII and BelleII experiments.
