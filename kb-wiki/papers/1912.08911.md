# The Frame-Independent Spatial Coordinate $\tilde{z}$: Implications for Light-Front Wave Functions, Deep Inelastic Scattering, Light-Front Holography, and Lattice QCD Calculations

> A general procedure for obtaining frame-independent, three-dimensional light-front coordinate-space wave functions is introduced. The third spatial coordinate, $\tilde z$ , is the frame independent coordinate conjugate to the light-front momentum coordinate $x={k^+\over P^+}$ which appears in the momentum-space light-front wave functions underlying generalized parton distributions, structure functions, distribution amplitudes, form factors, and other hadronic observables. These causal light-front coordinate-space wave functions are used to derive a general expression for the quark distribution function of hadrons as an integral over the frame-independent longitudinal distance (the Ioffe time) between virtual-photon absorption and emission appearing in the forward virtual photon-hadron Compton scattering amplitude. Specific examples using models derived from light-front holographic QCD show that the spatial extent of the proton eigenfunction in the longitudinal direction can have very large extent in $\tilde z$.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a frame-independent longitudinal spatial coordinate \(\tilde{z} = P^+ x^-\) that allows three-dimensional light-front coordinate-space wave functions, and shows through models that the spatial extent of the proton eigenfunction in this longitudinal direction can have very large extent, with the caveat that this is demonstrated using specific models derived from light-front holographic QCD rather than from first-principles QCD.

## Method

The authors define the boost-invariant longitudinal coordinate \(\tilde{z} = P^+ x^-\) as the canonical conjugate to the light-front momentum fraction \(x = k^+/P^+\), then construct coordinate-space light-front wave functions \(\psi_n(\tilde{z}, \mathbf{b})\) via Fourier transform of the momentum-space wave functions. They use these wave functions to derive a general expression for the quark distribution function \(q(x)\) as an integral over the Ioffe time \(\tilde{z}\), and compute the function \(g(\tilde{z}, x)\) that measures the contribution to quark distributions at a given longitudinal separation. The analysis is applied to two models: a simple valence model for a pseudoscalar meson and the universal light-front wave functions from holographic QCD of de Teramond et al. (2018).

## Key numbers

- The universal scale \(\sqrt{\lambda} = \kappa = m_\rho / \sqrt{2} = 0.548\) GeV, fixed from the \(\rho\) mass.
- The flavor-independent parameter \(a = 0.531 \pm 0.037\) in the universal model.
- For the valence model with massless quarks, the density \(\rho_M(\tilde{z}, b)\) vanishes at values of \(\tilde{z} \approx 7.6\) for each value of \(\kappa b\).
- In the universal model, the density \(\rho(\tilde{z}, b)\) falls roughly as \(1/\tilde{z}^{1.6}\) for \(b = 0\).
- The mean-square transverse size \(b^2(x)\) ranges from about 1.1 fm\(^2\) at \(x = 0\) to 0.23 fm\(^2\) at \(x = 1\) in the universal model.
- In the lab frame, \(x^- = \sqrt{2}/M \tilde{z} = 0.3 \tilde{z}\) fm, so the proton radius corresponds to about \(\tilde{z} = 3\).

## Context

This work extends the light-front Fock state formalism of Brodsky et al. (1973, 1997, 2000) by introducing a frame-independent longitudinal spatial coordinate, complementing the transverse impact parameter approach of Soper (1976) and Burkardt (2002). The derived expression for \(g(\tilde{z}, x)\) provides a new way to interpret the matrix elements computed in lattice QCD calculations of quasi-pdfs by Ji (2013), Lin (2018), and Alexandrou (2018), and the slow falloff with \(\tilde{z}\) explains the large Ioffe time underlying deep inelastic scattering at small \(x_{bj}\) as discussed by Ioffe (1969) and Braun (1994). The results enable future lattice QCD calculations to directly compare their computed matrix elements with predictions from light-front holographic QCD models.
