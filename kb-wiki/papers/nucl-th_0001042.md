# Proton decay of high-lying states in odd nuclei

> In the framework of the quasiparticle-phonon model, we study the non-statistical proton decay of excited states in odd nuclei towards low-lying collective states. Partial cross sections and branching ratios for the proton decay of the high angular momentum states in $^{41}$Sc, $^{59}$Cu and $^{91}$Nb %{\it excited by means of the ($^{7}$Li,$^{6}$He) reaction} are evaluated. The calculated branching ratios predict strong direct proton decays to the low-lying vibrational states in $^{41}$Sc and $^{91}$Nb. A general agreement with existing experimental data is found.

# Proton decay of high-lying states in odd nuclei

## Key claim
Within the quasiparticle-phonon model, the calculated branching ratios predict strong direct proton decays to low-lying vibrational states in ⁴¹Sc and ⁹¹Nb, but the agreement with existing experimental data is only general and qualitative, with some calculated partial widths (e.g., for the 3⁻₁ and ground-state channels in ⁹¹Nb) being much smaller than measured values.

## Method
The authors applied the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) with a projection operator method to treat single-particle continua, using a Woods-Saxon mean field and a separable multipole-multipole residual interaction. They calculated partial cross sections and branching ratios for non-statistical proton decay of high angular momentum states in ⁴¹Sc, ⁵⁹Cu, and ⁹¹Nb by solving for complex eigenstates of an effective Hamiltonian that includes coupling to the continuum. The strength function method with a Breit-Wigner averaging width of 0.1 MeV was used to evaluate the one-quasiparticle strength distributions and partial escape widths.

## Key numbers
- For the 1g₉/₂ proton strength distribution in ⁴¹Sc, the calculated B(E3) for the 3⁻₁ state in ⁴⁰Ca is 1.36 e²fm⁶ (experimental: 1.24 e²fm⁶), and B(E5) for the 5⁻₁ state is 2.67 e²fm¹⁰ (experimental: 2.97 e²fm¹⁰).
- For the energy interval 2.0–12.4 MeV in ⁴¹Sc, the proton decay to the 5⁻₁ state contributes about 54% and the 3⁻₁ channel contributes about 37% of the total sum of partial widths.
- For the excitation energy interval 2–8.9 MeV in ⁵⁹Cu, the summed ground-state width is 10.3 eV, and the 2⁺ channel summed width is six times smaller than the ground-state channel.
- For the 11/2⁻ isobaric analog state in ⁹¹Nb at approximately 12 MeV, the calculated partial width to the 5⁻₁ state is 3.3 keV, in agreement with the measured value of 2.9 keV.
- For the three isobaric analog states in ⁹¹Nb summed, the 4⁺₁ channel exhausts almost 60% of the total cross section, the 5⁻₁ channel contributes about 26%, the 2⁺₁ channel gives about 10%, and the ground-state channel contributes only 4%.

## Context
This work extends the method previously applied to neutron decay of high-lying states in ²⁰⁹Pb by Giai et al. (1991) and by Stoyanov et al. (1996) to the case of proton decay, using the QPM framework of Soloviev (1992). The calculations are compared with experimental data from Yoo et al. (1998) and Molen et al. for proton decays measured via (⁷Li,⁶He) and (α,t) reactions. The results enable interpretation of the relative roles of direct versus statistical decay components in the damping of single-particle modes, and the predicted branching ratios can guide future exclusive measurements of proton decay from high-lying states.
