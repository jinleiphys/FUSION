# A Correspondence between Phenomenological and IBM-1 Models of Even Isotopes of Yb

> This paper studies the nonterminal complexity of weakly conditional grammars, and it proves that every recursively enumerable language can be generated by a weak conditional grammar with no more than \textit{seven} nonterminals. Moreover, it is shown that the number of nonterminals in weakly conditional grammars without erasing rules leads to an infinite hierarchy of families of languages generated by weakly conditional grammars. Energy levels and the reduced probability of $E2$-- transitions for Ytterbium isotopes with proton number $Z=70$ and neutron numbers between 100 and 106 have been calculated through phenomenological ($PhM$) and interacting boson ($IBM-1$) models. The predicted low-lying levels (energies, spins and parities) and the reduced probability for $E2$-- transitions results are reasonably consistent with the available experimental data. The predicted low-lying levels ($gr$--, $β_1$-- and $γ_1$-- band) by produced in the $PhM$ are in good agreement with the experimental data comparison with those by $IBM-1$ for all nuclei of interest. In addition, the phenomenological model was successful in predicted the $β_2$--, $β_3$--, $β_4$--, $γ_2$-- and $1^+$-- band while it was a failure with $IBM-1$. Also, the $3^+$-- band is predicted by the $IBM-1$ model for $^{172}Yb$ and $^{174}Yb$ nuclei. All calculations are compared with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The phenomenological model (PhM) and the interacting boson model (IBM-1) both produce low-lying energy levels and E2 transition probabilities for Ytterbium isotopes (Z=70, neutron numbers 100-106) that are reasonably consistent with experimental data, but the PhM generally shows better agreement than IBM-1, except for a few specific transitions where IBM-1 performs better.

## Method

Energy levels and reduced E2 transition probabilities for the positive-parity states of even-even Ytterbium isotopes (170, 172, 174, 176) were calculated using two theoretical approaches: the phenomenological model (PhM), which includes mixing of ground, beta, gamma, and K-pi=1+ bands with Coriolis coupling, and the interacting boson model (IBM-1) in its SU(3) rotational limit, treating the nuclei as systems of s and d bosons. The PhM parameters were fitted by least-squares minimization against experimental ground-band energies up to spin I ≤ 10 h-bar, while IBM-1 calculations used a Hamiltonian with only L·L and Q·Q terms, with boson numbers determined from valence nucleons relative to closed shells. Both models' results were compared with experimental data from published nuclear data compilations.

## Key numbers

- The ratio E(4+)/E(2+) for 170-176Yb isotopes indicates rotational (deformed) character, consistent with SU(3) symmetry in IBM-1.
- For 170Yb, 174Yb, and 176Yb, the IBM-1 calculation of B(E2; 2_gr+ → 0_gr+) agrees better with experiment than the PhM calculation.
- For 172Yb, the IBM-1 calculation of B(E2; 6_gr+ → 4_gr+) agrees better with experiment than the PhM calculation.
- For 174Yb, the IBM-1 calculations of B(E2; 4_gr+ → 2_gr+) and B(E2; 14_gr+ → 12_gr+) agree better with experiment than the PhM calculations.
- For 170Yb, the IBM-1 calculation of B(E2; 12_gr+ → 10_gr+) agrees better with experiment than the PhM calculation.
- The 3+ band is predicted by IBM-1 for 172Yb and 174Yb nuclei, a feature not reproduced by PhM.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of Ytterbium isotopes using the projected shell model (Hara 1980; Archer 1998) and experimental investigations including deep inelastic reactions with Gammasphere (Lee 1997) and decay, Coulomb excitation, and transfer reactions (Gelletly 1987). The PhM approach follows the formalism of Usmanov (1997, 2010), while the IBM-1 calculations follow the standard SU(3) limit of Arima (1976, 1978, 1979) and Casten (1988). The results enable future studies to incorporate pairing parameters into IBM-1 to resolve the observed discrepancies with experimental data, and to extend the PhM predictions for higher-lying beta and gamma bands to other rare-earth nuclei.
