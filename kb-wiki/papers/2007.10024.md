# Complexified quasinormal modes and the pole-skipping in a holographic system at finite chemical potential

> We develop a method to study coupled dynamics of gauge-invariant variables, constructed out of metric and gauge field fluctuations on the background of a AdS$_5$ Reissner-Nordström black brane. Using this method, we compute the numerical spectrum of quasinormal modes associated with fluctuations of spin 0, 1 and 2, non-perturbatively in $μ/T$. We also analytically compute the spectrum of hydrodynamic excitations in the small chemical potential limit. Then, by studying the spectral curve at complex momenta in every spin channel, we numerically find points at which hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic poles collide. We discuss the relation between such collision points and the convergence radius of the hydrodynamic derivative expansion. Specifically in the spin 0 channel, we find that within the range $1.1\lesssim μ/T\lesssim 2$, the radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic sound mode is set by the absolute value of the complex momentum corresponding to the point at which the sound pole collides with the hydrodynamic diffusion pole. It shows that in holographic systems at finite chemical potential, the convergence of the hydrodynamic derivative expansion in the mentioned range is fully controlled by hydrodynamic information. As the last result, we explicitly show that the relevant information about quantum chaos in our system can be extracted from the pole-skipping points of energy density response function. We find a threshold value for $μ/T$, lower than which the pole-skipping points can be computed perturbatively in a derivative expansion.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a holographic system at finite chemical potential, the radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic derivative expansion for the sound mode is set by the collision of the sound pole with the hydrodynamic diffusion pole (rather than with a non-hydrodynamic mode) within the range \(1.1 \lesssim \mu/T \lesssim 2\), meaning convergence is fully controlled by hydrodynamic information in that range.

## Method

The authors study linear perturbations of metric and gauge field fluctuations on an AdS\(_5\) Reissner-Nordström black brane background, constructing gauge-invariant variables classified by spin (0, 1, 2). They develop a generalized Frobenius method to solve the coupled differential equations for the gauge-invariant variables numerically, computing quasinormal mode spectra non-perturbatively in \(\mu/T\). They also analytically compute hydrodynamic mode spectra in the small chemical potential limit using a derivative expansion, and study spectral curves at complex momenta to find collision points between hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic poles.

## Key numbers

- The numerical method works well in the range \(0 \le Q \lesssim 0.88\), equivalently \(0 \le \mu/T \lesssim 4\); results are shown for \(0 \le Q \le 0.85\).
- In the spin 0 channel, within the range \(1.1 \lesssim \mu/T \lesssim 2\), the radius of convergence of the hydrodynamic sound mode is set by the absolute value of the complex momentum at which the sound pole collides with the hydrodynamic diffusion pole.
- A threshold value for \(\mu/T\) is found, below which the pole-skipping points can be computed perturbatively in a derivative expansion; a critical value \(Q_c\) is identified beyond which chaos points lie outside the domain of convergence of the hydrodynamic derivative expansion.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Withers (2018), who studied the convergence radius of the shear mode in a 2+1 dimensional holographic model at finite chemical potential, to a 3+1 dimensional AdS\(_5\) Reissner-Nordström model. It also builds on the general theory of spectral curves and level-crossing developed by Grozdanov et al. (2019) for neutral fluids. The results enable a systematic understanding of how finite chemical potential affects the convergence of hydrodynamic derivative expansions and the relationship between pole-skipping points and quantum chaos in holographic systems.
