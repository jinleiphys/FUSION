# Quarkonium dissociation in a thermal medium

> We investigate the Mott effect for heavy quarkonia due to Debye screening of the heavy quark potential in a plasma of massless quarks and antiquarks. The influence of residual color correlation is investigated by coupling the light quark sector to a temporal gauge field driven by the Polyakov loop potential. This leads to an increase of the Mott dissociation temperatures for quarkonia states which stabilizes in particular the excited states, but has marginal effect on the ground states.

## Key claim

The inclusion of residual color correlations via coupling to the Polyakov loop potential increases the Mott dissociation temperatures for quarkonium states, stabilizing excited states more than ground states, but the effect remains marginal for the ground states.

## Method

The authors compute the Debye screening mass in a plasma of massless quarks using a one-loop polarization function within the Polyakov loop Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, which couples the light quark sector to a temporal gauge field. They then apply a variational method with a trial wave function to the nonrelativistic two-body Hamiltonian with a screened Coulomb potential, solving simultaneously for the ground state energy minimum and the condition of vanishing binding energy (Mott effect) to obtain analytic expressions for the dissociation temperature.

## Key numbers

- For the free quark gas (Φ=1), the Mott temperature for J/ψ is T_Mott/T_c = 1.25; with the PNJL model it increases to T_Mott,Φ/T_c = 1.37.
- For χ_c, T_Mott/T_c = 0.83 (free gas) and T_Mott,Φ/T_c = 1.11 (PNJL).
- For ψ', T_Mott/T_c = 0.66 (free gas) and T_Mott,Φ/T_c = 0.99 (PNJL).
- For Υ, T_Mott/T_c = 2.50 (free gas) and T_Mott,Φ/T_c = 2.50 (PNJL, essentially unchanged).
- For χ_b, T_Mott/T_c = 1.72 (free gas) and T_Mott,Φ/T_c = 1.73 (PNJL).
- For Υ', T_Mott/T_c = 1.28 (free gas) and T_Mott,Φ/T_c = 1.39 (PNJL).
- The critical temperature T_c is 202 MeV, taken from Blaschke (2007).
- The heavy quark masses used are m_c = 1.94 GeV and m_b = 5.1 GeV; ground state binding energies are 0.78 GeV (charmonium) and 0.75 GeV (bottomonium), from Arleo (2001).

## Context

This work extends earlier potential model calculations of quarkonium dissociation in a quark-gluon plasma, which had yielded Mott temperatures around 1.2 T_c for J/ψ, in disagreement with lattice QCD spectral function analyses suggesting approximately 1.9 T_c (Asakawa, 2000). The authors use the PNJL model to incorporate residual confining correlations, building on the approach of Ratti (2005) and Hansen (2006), and obtain results that remain closer to the nonrelativistic potential model values than to the lattice spectral function estimates. The work enables further study of sequential quarkonium dissociation as a diagnostic tool for the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions.
