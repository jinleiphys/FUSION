# Confronting particle emission scenarios with strangeness data

> We show that a hadron gas model with continuous particle emission instead of freeze out may solve some of the problems (high values of the freeze out density and specific net charge) that one encounters in the latter case when studying strange particle ratios such as those by WA85. This underlines the necessity to understand better particle emission in hydrodynamics to be able to analyze data. It also re-opens the possibility of a quark-hadron transition occuring with phase equilibrium instead of explosively.

# Confronting particle emission scenarios with strangeness data

## Key claim
A hadron gas model using continuous particle emission (rather than a sharp freeze-out surface) can resolve the problems of high freeze-out density and excessive specific net charge that arise when fitting WA85 strange particle ratios with standard freeze-out models. The authors show this is possible provided the strangeness saturation factor gamma_s is treated carefully, but they do not claim to have fully solved all discrepancies.

## Method
The authors compare two particle emission mechanisms for a hadron gas in local thermal and chemical equilibrium: the standard Cooper-Frye freeze-out formula and a continuous emission model where particles escape at different space-time points when their escape probability reaches 0.5. They solve conservation equations for a resonance gas (207 known lowest-mass particles) with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, imposing strangeness neutrality, and compute particle spectra using both emission scenarios. The continuous emission spectra are integrated over the fluid history, with heavy particle spectra dominated by initial conditions and light particles reflecting the full evolution.

## Key numbers
- WA85 data for strange baryons at 2.3 < y < 2.8 and 1.0 < p_T < 3.0 GeV: bar-Lambda/Lambda = 0.20 +/- 0.01, bar-Xi-/Xi- = 0.41 +/- 0.05, Xi-/Lambda = 0.09 +/- 0.01
- In the continuous emission scenario, the overlap region reproducing all WA85 ratios requires gamma_s(tau_0) = 0.58 (without hadronic volume corrections) or gamma_s(tau_0) = 0.49 (with hadronic volume corrections)
- For freeze-out models, the overlap region requires gamma_s = 0.7
- The freeze-out temperature needed is T_f.out ~ 200 MeV with baryonic potential mu_b_f.out ~ few 100 MeV
- In the continuous emission scenario, initial conditions near T_0 ~ 190 MeV and mu_b_0 ~ 180 MeV are considered reasonable (bottom of overlap region)
- The apparent temperature extracted from experimental p_T spectra for Lambda, bar-Lambda, Xi- and bar-Xi- is ~ 230 MeV
- For the ratio (Omega- + bar-Omega-)/(Xi- + bar-Xi-) at m_T >= 2.3 GeV, the model predicts ~ 0.7, compared to the experimental value 1.7 +/- 0.9

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Davidson et al. (1991), von Oertzen et al. (1992), and Cleymans and Satz (1993) who used freeze-out models to fit WA85 strangeness data, and by Letessier et al. (1995) who identified problems with high freeze-out densities and specific net charge. The continuous emission approach was developed earlier by Grassi, Hama, and Kodama (1995, 1996) for massless pion gases. The paper suggests that the quark-hadron transition might occur with phase equilibrium rather than explosively, and calls for better understanding of particle emission in hydrodynamics. A concurrent solution using excluded volume corrections was proposed by Yen, Gorenstein, Greiner, and Yang (1997).
