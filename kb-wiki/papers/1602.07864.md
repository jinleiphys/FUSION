# Decay $B_s\to ϕ\ell^+ \ell^-$ in covariant quark model

> Our article is devoted to the study of the rare $B_s\to ϕ\ell^+\ell^-$ decay where $\ell=μ,τ$. We compute the relevant form factors in the framework of the covariant quark model with infrared confinement in the full kinematical momentum transfer region. The calculated form factors are used to evaluate branching fractions and polarization observables in the cascade decay $B\to ϕ(\to K^+K^-)\ell^+\ell^-$. We compare the obtained results with available experimental data and the results from other theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The covariant quark model with infrared confinement can reproduce the form factors for the $B_s\to\phi$ transition across the full kinematical region, and the resulting branching fractions and angular observables for $B_s\to\phi\mu^+\mu^-$ are consistent with LHCb data within uncertainties, though the model predictions tend to be slightly higher than the experimental central values.

## Method

The authors compute the $B_s\to\phi$ transition form factors using the covariant confined quark model, an effective quantum field approach where hadrons interact with constituent quarks via a Gaussian vertex function and quark confinement is implemented through an infrared cutoff parameter $\lambda$. The form factors are obtained from three-fold loop integrals evaluated numerically, then used with Standard Model Wilson coefficients (including two-loop corrections from Asatryan (2001) and Greub (2008)) to calculate differential branching fractions, forward-backward asymmetry, and optimized angular observables for the cascade decay $B_s\to\phi(\to K^+K^-)\ell^+\ell^-$.

## Key numbers

- Form factors at $q^2=0$: $V^c(0)=0.31\pm0.03$, $A_0^c(0)=0.28\pm0.03$, $A_1^c(0)=0.27\pm0.03$, $A_2^c(0)=0.27\pm0.03$, $T_1^c(0)=0.27\pm0.03$, $T_3^c(0)=0.18\pm0.02$
- Branching fraction ${\cal B}(B_s\to\phi\mu^+\mu^-) = (9.11\pm1.82)\times10^{-7}$, compared to experimental value $(7.97\pm0.77)\times10^{-7}$ from LHCb (2015)
- Branching fraction ${\cal B}(B_s\to\phi\tau^+\tau^-) = (1.03\pm0.20)\times10^{-7}$
- Branching fraction ${\cal B}(B_s\to\phi\gamma) = (2.39\pm0.48)\times10^{-5}$, compared to experimental value $(3.52\pm0.34)\times10^{-5}$
- Branching fraction ${\cal B}(B_s\to\phi\nu\bar\nu) = (0.84\pm0.16)\times10^{-5}$
- Branching fraction ${\cal B}(B_s\to\phi J/\psi) = (0.16\pm0.03)\times10^{-2}$, compared to experimental value $(0.108\pm0.009)\times10^{-2}$
- Model parameters: constituent quark masses $m_{u/d}=0.241$ GeV, $m_s=0.428$ GeV, $m_c=1.67$ GeV, $m_b=5.05$ GeV; infrared cutoff $\lambda=0.181$ GeV; size parameters $\Lambda_{B_s}=2.05$ GeV, $\Lambda_\phi=0.88$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the covariant confined quark model framework previously applied to other B-meson decays by Ivanov (2011) and Dubnicka (2015), providing form factors for the $B_s\to\phi$ transition that are compared with results from light-cone sum rules (Ball 2004), relativistic quark models (Faustov 2013), and lattice QCD (Horgan 2013). The calculated observables address the $b\to s\ell\ell$ anomalies reported by LHCb (2013, 2015), where differential branching fractions in certain $q^2$ bins showed deviations above $3\sigma$ from Standard Model predictions; the results enable comparison with global analyses such as Descotes-Genon (2015) and can be used to constrain new physics scenarios.
