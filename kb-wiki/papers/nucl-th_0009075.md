# Two-pion decay modes of the N*(1440) in n p -> d pi pi

> A simple model for the n p -> d pi pi reaction has been developed. It is shown that the deuteron momentum spectra measured at T_n = 795 MeV can be understood in terms of the Roper excitation and its N pi pi decay modes. A similar pattern, recently observed in p p -> p p pi+ pi-, can be explained in the same way.

## Key claim

The deuteron momentum spectra measured in the \(n p \rightarrow d \pi \pi\) reaction at a neutron kinetic energy of 795 MeV can be explained by the interference of two Roper resonance (\(N^*(1440)\)) decay mechanisms, specifically the \(N^* \rightarrow N (\pi \pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\) and \(N^* \rightarrow \Delta \pi\) channels, rather than by the ABC effect or other models available at the time.

## Method

The author developed a reduced version of a prior model for \(NN \rightarrow NN \pi \pi\) (Ref. Yo1) and applied it to the \(n p \rightarrow d \pi \pi\) reaction. The calculation included the excitation of the Roper resonance \(N^*(1440)\) and its subsequent decay via two modes: \(N^* \rightarrow N (\pi \pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\) and \(N^* \rightarrow \Delta \pi\). The Paris deuteron wave function was used, and the deuteron momentum distribution was computed for different laboratory angles at a neutron beam energy of \(T_n = 795\) MeV.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron momentum spectra were measured at a neutron kinetic energy of \(T_n = 795\) MeV.
* The double-\(\Delta\) mechanism contribution was calculated at \(T_n = 830\) MeV and \(\theta_{lab} = 4.5^\circ\) using the Hulthen wave function.
* For the \(pp \rightarrow p p \pi^+ \pi^-\) reaction, the calculation was performed at a proton kinetic energy of \(T_p = 750\) MeV, with experimental kinematic constraints of \(3^\circ \leq \theta_{lab} \leq 24^\circ\) for detected particles.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the \(n p \rightarrow d \pi \pi\) reaction that were connected to the ABC effect, but the data at \(T_n = 795\) MeV (Hollas et al.) showed no ABC peaks and instead exhibited a bump at high \(\pi\pi\) missing masses that previous models could not explain. The model is a reduced version of a prior approach by Yo1 for \(NN \rightarrow NN \pi \pi\) and uses the same interference mechanism to explain a similar pattern observed in \(pp \rightarrow p p \pi^+ \pi^-\) at WASA (Ref. Wasa). The results enable further study of Roper resonance decay properties and the role of interference in double-pion production near threshold.
