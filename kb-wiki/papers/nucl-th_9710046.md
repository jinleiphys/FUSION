# Nucleon-Antinucleon Interaction from the Skyrme Model: II. Beyond the Product Ansatz

> We calculate the full static interaction of a Skyrmion and an anti-Skyrmion as a function of separation and relative grooming. From this, using projection methods and Born-Oppenheimer mixing, we obtain the nucleon antinucleon interaction. We find agreement with the major features of the empirical interaction including the strong central attraction and the sharp onset of annihilation at about 1 fm.

## Key claim

The full dynamical Skyrme calculation of the Skyrmion-antiSkyrmion interaction, combined with Born-Oppenheimer mixing, reproduces the strong central attraction and sharp onset of annihilation at about 1 fm seen in the empirical nucleon-antinucleon interaction, which the earlier product ansatz approach (Lu and Amado, 1996) had failed to capture.

## Method

The authors solved the static Skyrme equations of motion for a Skyrmion-antiSkyrmion system using a relaxation algorithm on a variable-grid lattice in the first octant, imposing reflection symmetries as boundary conditions and holding the chiral angle field fixed at its maximum value at two locations on the separation axis to prevent total annihilation. They computed the total energy for three essential relative groomings (no grooming, pi around the x-axis, and pi around the z-axis) as a function of separation distance. From these Skyrmion-antiSkyrmion interaction energies, they extracted the nucleon-antinucleon potentials using standard Skyrmion-to-baryon projection methods and Born-Oppenheimer mixing to account for coupling to Delta resonances, including finite N_C corrections for N_C=3.

## Key numbers

- The total energy of two free Skyrmions at large separation is 2.92 GeV for the chosen parameters (f_pi = 93 MeV, e = 4.76).
- The calculation covers separation distances from 0.8 fm to 2.2 fm; below 0.8 fm the adiabatic interaction concept ceases to be meaningful.
- The baryon number in the half-space containing the Skyrmion plunges abruptly from near one to near zero at about 1.2 fm in the attractive grooming channel.
- The central T=0 potential from the full Born-Oppenheimer diagonalization is about as strong as that seen in the Bryan-Phillips (1970s) and Nijmegen group (1970s) phenomenological analyses.
- The central T=1 potential after diagonalization is comparable to or more attractive than phenomenology, with about half the scale of the T=0 case.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to the nucleon-antinucleon interaction beyond the product ansatz calculation of Lu and Amado (1996), which reproduced some features but failed to give the observed strong central attraction. The full dynamical calculation follows the same two-step method used by Walhout and Wambach (1990s) for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, where a full dynamical Skyrmion-Skyrmion calculation combined with Born-Oppenheimer Delta mixing yielded the empirical nucleon-nucleon attraction. The results enable a complete QCD-based (via Skyrme) description of low-energy nucleon-antinucleon annihilation from the initial state through to the final annihilation products, building on the earlier annihilation channel work of Sommermann et al. (1990s) and the authors' own previous studies.
