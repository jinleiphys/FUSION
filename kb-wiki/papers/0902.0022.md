# Phonon and multi-phonon excitations in rotational nuclei by exact diagonalization of the Bohr Hamiltonian

> Exact numerical diagonalization of the Bohr Hamiltonian by SU(1,1)xSO(5) methods is used to obtain detailed quantitative predictions for single-phonon and multi-phonon excitations in well-deformed rotor nuclei. Dynamical gamma deformation is found to significantly influence the predictions through its coupling to the rotational motion. Basic signatures for the onset of rigid triaxial deformation are obtained.

## Key claim

Exact numerical diagonalization of the Bohr Hamiltonian using SU(1,1)xSO(5) algebraic methods reveals that dynamical gamma deformation significantly influences predictions for single-phonon and multi-phonon excitations in well-deformed rotor nuclei, and that the Bohr description quantitatively overpredicts and even qualitatively mis-predicts deviations from adiabatic rotational structure.

## Method

The Bohr Hamiltonian is diagonalized numerically using the algebraic collective model (ACM) approach, which employs a direct product basis of SU(1,1) beta wave functions and SO(5) spherical harmonics Psi_{v alpha L M}(gamma, Omega). The angular degrees of freedom are treated in full, including dynamical gamma deformation and its coupling to rotational motion, using newly calculated SO(5) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. Calculations are performed for both an angular-only Hamiltonian (with beta held fixed) and the full Bohr Hamiltonian including beta dynamics with a Davidson potential.

## Key numbers

- For the angular Hamiltonian with xi=0 and chi=50, the energy ratio E(2+_gamma)/E(2+_1) is approximately 10, appropriate for well-deformed rare earth nuclei
- The ground state band energy ratio E(4+_1)/E(2+_1) varies from 2.5 for gamma-soft rotation to 3.33 for rigid axial rotation as gamma stiffness increases
- For the full Bohr Hamiltonian calculation with beta_0=3 and chi=5, fewer than five SU(1,1) basis functions are required for convergence
- Seniority quantum numbers v <= 50 suffice for convergence of the angular calculations
- For 162Dy, the experimental gamma->g intrinsic matrix element is 0.241(3) in units of B(E2;2+_1->0+_1)^(1/2), about a factor of two lower than the Bohr Hamiltonian prediction of 0.51 for xi=0 (chi=50)
- The experimental K=4 gamma-gamma->gamma intrinsic matrix element ratio is 0.99(3), compared to the Bohr Hamiltonian prediction of approximately 1.9

## Context

This work extends the algebraic collective model (ACM) approach developed by Rowe (2004, 2005) and Caprio, which provides a tractable scheme for numerical diagonalization of the Bohr Hamiltonian. It contrasts with earlier approximate treatments such as the rotation-vibration model (Faessler, 1965), the rigid triaxial rotor (Davydov, 1958), and the Y(5) critical point model (Iachello, 2003). The results enable direct comparison of exact Bohr Hamiltonian predictions with experimental data for rotational nuclei, as demonstrated for 162Dy studied by Aprahamian (2006), and raise fundamental questions about the limitations of the collective quadrupole degree of freedom description.
