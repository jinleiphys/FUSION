# Analytical Analysis and Numerical Solution of Two Flavours Skyrmion

> Two flavours Skyrmion will be analyzed analytically, in case of static and rotational Skyrme equations. Numerical solution of a nonlinear scalar field equation, i.e. the Skyrme equation, will be worked with finite difference method. This article is a more comprehensive version of \textit{SU(2) Skyrme Model for Hadron} which have been published at Journal of Theoretical and Computational Studies, Volume \textbf{3} (2004) 0407.

# Analytical Analysis and Numerical Solution of Two Flavours Skyrmion

## Key claim

The SU(2) Skyrme model, despite being a very simple two-flavour model, can reproduce nucleon and delta masses to within approximately 2% and 6% of experimental values respectively, but the authors explicitly note that the discrepancies arise because the model omits vector mesons, the third flavour family, chiral and flavour symmetry breaking effects, and N_C (colour) corrections.

## Method

The authors analytically derive the static and rotational Skyrme equations from the SU(2) Skyrme Lagrangian density, which includes the chiral model term, the Skyrme stabilizing term, and a pion mass term. They then solve the second-order nonlinear ordinary differential equation for the profile function g(r) numerically using a finite difference method with central and backward differences, applying the shooting method to satisfy the boundary conditions g(0)=π and g(∞)=0. The static energy and moment of inertia are computed numerically from the profile function using the trapezoid method, and hadron masses are obtained by adding the quantized rotational energy.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: F ≅ 123 MeV
- Dimensionless constant a = 4.95
- Static mass M_static ≅ 905.08 MeV
- Skyrmion moment of inertia I ≅ 7.49 × 10^{-3} (in natural units)
- Calculated nucleon mass m_N ≅ 955.15 MeV (experimental value ~939 MeV, difference ~16.15 MeV)
- Calculated delta mass m_Δ ≅ 1155.41 MeV (experimental value ~1232 MeV, difference ~76.59 MeV)
- Static energy numerical result: 23.2154 π f_π / e (from trapezoid integration)
- Static energy in terms of F and a: E_static = (π F / a) × (23.2 / 2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier SU(2) Skyrme model paper published in the Journal of Theoretical and Computational Studies (2004) by providing a more comprehensive analytical and numerical treatment. The Skyrme model was originally proposed by Tony Hilton Royle Skyrme (1962) as a way to describe hadrons as topological solitons from a nonlinear sigma model field theory with SU(2) internal symmetry. The numerical results enable calculation of nucleon and delta masses, Skyrmion moment of inertia, and provide a foundation for extending the model to include three flavours, vector mesons, and symmetry breaking effects as noted by the authors.
