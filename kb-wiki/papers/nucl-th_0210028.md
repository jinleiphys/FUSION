# Nucleon-nucleon interaction models and non-locality

> The effect of non-locality in the NN interaction models is examined. It is shown that this feature can explain differences in predictions made from models evidencing a difference with this respect. This is done for both static and dynamical observables, taking into account that a non-local term can be transformed away by performing a unitary transformation. Some results for the deuteron form factors, the A(Q^2) structure function and the T_{20}(Q^2) tensor polarization are given as an example. A few cases where discrepancies cannot be explained are also considered. They point to differences in the models as for the deuteron asymptotic normalizations, A_S and A_D, which are not affected by the present analysis.

# Nucleon-nucleon interaction models and non-locality

## Key claim
Non-locality in NN interaction models can explain most, but not all, differences in predictions for deuteron electromagnetic observables; discrepancies that remain are tied to the asymptotic normalizations \(A_S\) and \(A_D\), which are not affected by unitary transformations that remove non-local terms.

## Method
The authors examine how non-local terms in the NN interaction (written as anticommutators and commutators of \(p^2/M\) with scalar and tensor functions) can be transformed away by a first-order unitary transformation, generating additional local potentials and many-body currents. They then compare predictions for deuteron static properties, form factors, the \(A(Q^2)\) structure function, and the \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) tensor polarization across several models (Nij1, Nij2, Nij93, Paris, Argonne V18, Reid93, Bonn-QB, Bonn-CD), with and without corrections for non-locality.

## Key numbers
- The difference in deuteron D-state probability between the Paris and Bonn-QB models is 0.78%; the non-local term explains 0.74% (0.60% from the commutator part and 0.14% from the anticommutator part).
- The difference in the observable ratio \(Q_D/(A_S A_D)\) between the same two models is 0.23 fm\(^3\); the non-local term explains 0.20 fm\(^3\).
- The squared charge radius differs by 0.004 fm\(^2\) between Nij1 and Nij2; the non-locality effect is estimated at 0.001 fm\(^2\).
- Around \(Q^2 = 20\) fm\(^{-2}\), the ratio of predictions for \(A(Q^2)\) and \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) between models becomes very close to 1, whereas without corrections departures up to 10% and 20% respectively could be observed.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of non-locality by Doleschall and Borbely, and by Friar on the difference between pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector \(\pi NN\) couplings. It builds on the observation that the Nijmegen group's Nij1 (non-local, 41 parameters) and Nij2 (local, 47 parameters) models both fit scattering phase shifts equally well (\(\chi^2\) per datum = 1.03), suggesting non-locality may be beneficial. The analysis shows that accounting for non-locality reduces model dependence in deuteron electromagnetic observables, which motivated Schiavella and Sick to use the quadrupole form factor to extract the neutron charge form factor. Remaining discrepancies point to the need for more accurate determinations of the asymptotic normalizations \(A_S\) and the triplet scattering length \(a_t\).
