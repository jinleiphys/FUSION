# The multilevel pairing Hamiltonian versus the degenerate case

> We study the pairing Hamiltonian in a set of non degenerate levels. First, we review in the path integral framework the spontaneous breaking of the U(1) symmetry occurring in such a system for the degenerate situation. Then the behaviors with the coupling constant of the ground state energy in the multilevel and in the degenerate case are compared. Next we discuss, in the multilevel case, an exact strong coupling expansion for the ground state energy which introduces the moments of the single particle level distribution. The domain of validity of the expansion, which is known in the macroscopic limit, is explored for finite systems and its implications for the energy of the latter is discussed. Finally the seniority and Gaudin excitations of the pairing Hamiltonian are addressed and shown to display the same gap in leading order.

## Key claim

The multilevel pairing Hamiltonian, unlike the degenerate case, does not exhibit spontaneous U(1) symmetry breaking at the microscopic scale; instead, the system can live in different regimes (normal fermionic, mixed, or superfluid) depending on the coupling strength relative to critical values determined by the single-particle level distribution.

## Method

The authors study the pairing Hamiltonian for fermions in L non-degenerate single-particle levels using Richardson's exact algebraic solution, which yields the ground state energy as a sum of pair energies E_ν satisfying the Richardson equations. They derive an exact strong-coupling expansion of the ground state energy in inverse powers of the coupling constant g, expressing the coefficients in terms of the moments (mean, variance, skewness, kurtosis) of the single-particle level distribution. They compare this expansion with exact numerical solutions for equispaced levels with unit pair degeneracy and analyze the radius of convergence of the expansion.

## Key numbers

- For a system of n=2 pairs in L=12 equispaced levels (Ω_k=1, e_k=k for k=0,...,11), the critical coupling g_crit^min = 0.66 d (where d=1 is the level spacing) and g_crit^max does not exist (only one critical point).
- For n=6 pairs (half filling) in the same system, g_crit^min = 0.40 d and g_crit^max = 0.90 d.
- For n=12 pairs (full filling) in the same system, g_crit^min = 0.35 d and g_crit^max = 0.94 d.
- In the macroscopic limit (n→∞, half filling), the expansion converges for g/d > 1/π ≈ 0.318, while the BCS energy gives (g/d)_crit^max = 1/arcsinh(1) ≈ 1.1345.
- For one pair in two levels with unit degeneracy and spacing d, the expansion converges for g/d > 1, and the exact ground state energy is E = -g - sqrt(d^2 + g^2).
- For two levels with equal pair degeneracy Ω_1=Ω_2=100 and n=100 (half filling), the radius of convergence is ḡ/d = 0.0068.
- For L=200 levels with unit degeneracy (Ω_Tot=200, d=1/199) and n=100, the radius of convergence is ḡ/d = 0.65.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the pairing Hamiltonian from the well-studied degenerate single-level case (where the U(1) symmetry is spontaneously broken and the ground state energy is given by E = 2ēn - gn(Ω-n+1)) to the more realistic multilevel situation relevant for finite nuclei. The authors build on the Richardson exact solution (Richardson, 1963) and the path integral treatment of Barbaro et al. (2004), and they compare with the strong-coupling expansion of Yuzbashyan et al. (2005). The results enable a systematic understanding of how the single-particle level distribution affects the ground state energy and the transition between normal and superfluid regimes in finite fermionic systems, with implications for the Anderson criterion for superconductivity in small systems.
