# Proton and neutron polarized structure functions from low to high Q**2

> Phenomenological parameterizations of proton and neutron polarized structure functions, g1p and g1n, are developed for x > 0.02 using deep inelastic data up to ~ 50 (GeV/c)**2 as well as available experimental results on photo- and electro-production of nucleon resonances. The generalized Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rules are predicted from low to high values of Q**2 and compared with proton and neutron data. Furthermore, the main results of the power correction analysis carried out on the Q**2-behavior of the polarized proton Nachtmann moments, evaluated using our parameterization of g1p, are briefly summarized.

# Proton and neutron polarized structure functions from low to high Q²

## Key claim
The authors develop phenomenological parameterizations of the proton and neutron polarized structure functions g₁ᵖ and g₁ⁿ that cover the range x ≥ 0.02 from the photon point up to Q² ≈ 50 (GeV/c)², but the parameterization is explicitly stated to be a simple approximation valid only in a limited x-range, and the Bjorken sum rule cannot be checked because the parameterization does not extend below x ≈ 0.02.

## Method
The polarized structure functions are decomposed into elastic, resonant, and non-resonant contributions. The non-resonant part uses an interpolation formula for the transverse asymmetry contribution g^Δσ with parameters fitted to deep inelastic scattering data on the asymmetry A₁, while the resonant part uses Breit-Wigner shapes for four-star nucleon resonances with masses below 2 GeV. The neutron parameterization additionally incorporates the theoretical value of the Bjorken sum rule as a constraint in the fitting procedure.

## Key numbers
- The parameterization of g₁ᵖ uses 14 parameters fitted to 209 experimental points, yielding χ²/d.o.f. = 0.66
- The parameterization of g₁ⁿ uses 245 experimental points and yields χ²/d.o.f. = 0.90
- The parameter W_T (describing the transition to the partonic regime) is fixed through the DHG sum rule to W_T = 0.475 GeV for proton and W_T = 0.451 GeV for neutron
- The zero-crossing point of the proton first moment Γ₁(Q²) is predicted at Q² = 0.16 ± 0.04 (GeV/c)², while JLab data show the crossing at Q² ≈ 0.25 (GeV/c)²
- The singlet axial charge (in the AB scheme) extracted from the twist analysis is a₀(10 GeV²) = 0.16 ± 0.09
- The non-singlet moment Δq^NS is fixed at 1.095 from experimental axial coupling constants

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous phenomenological fits of unpolarized structure functions (Ricco, Simula 2000) and the polarized proton parameterization (Simula 2002) to the neutron case. The parameterizations enable predictions of the generalized Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rules from low to high Q², which are compared with JLab data from Burkert (2002) and with Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions from Ji (2001) and Bernard, Hemmert, and Meissner (2002). The twist analysis of the proton Nachtmann moments reveals that spin-dependent multiparton correlations may have more impact than spin-independent ones, and that soft gluon resummation beyond next-to-leading order is necessary for reliable extraction of higher twists at Q² of a few (GeV/c)².
