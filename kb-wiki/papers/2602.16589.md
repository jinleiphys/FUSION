# Isospin dependence of nuclear EMC effect from global QCD analysis

> We perform a new global QCD analysis of unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) in the nucleon from proton, deuteron and $A=3$ data, including recent measurements of $^3$He/$D$ and $^3$H/$D$ cross section ratios from the MARATHON experiment at Jefferson Lab. Simultaneously inferring the PDFs and nucleon off-shell corrections allows both to be determined consistently, without theoretical assumptions about the isospin dependence of nuclear effects. The analysis provides strong evidence for the need of nucleon off-shell corrections to describe the $A=3$ data, and suggests the presence of both isoscalar and isovector nuclear effects in $A \leq 3$ nuclei. We find that the extracted EMC ratios of nuclear to nucleon structure functions for $A=2$ and 3 differ from those naively extrapolated from heavy nuclei down to low $A$.

## Key claim

The analysis provides strong evidence that nucleon off-shell corrections are necessary to describe the $A=3$ data, and suggests the presence of both isoscalar and isovector nuclear effects in $A \leq 3$ nuclei, contradicting the assumption that nuclear effects in these light nuclei mostly cancel or can be extrapolated from heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis of unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) using the JAM Bayesian Monte Carlo framework, simultaneously fitting free nucleon PDFs and nucleon off-shell corrections from proton, deuteron, and $A=3$ data. The analysis included recent MARATHON experiment measurements of $^3$He/D and $^3$H/D cross section ratios, along with other high-energy scattering data, parametrizing the off-shell functions in terms of isoscalar and isovector components using a template function with sum rule constraints.

## Key numbers

- The $W^2$ cut for unpolarized DIS data was increased from 3.0 GeV$^2$ to 3.5 GeV$^2$, which significantly improved the $\chi^2_{\rm red}$ for some datasets
- 21 of the 22 original data points for both the $^3$He/D and $^3$H/D ratios were included after the $W^2$ restriction
- The analysis involved a total of 74 free parameters: 33 for onshell PDFs, 9 for off-shell functions, 10 for higher twist corrections, and 22 fitted normalizations
- The MARATHON $^3$He/D and $^3$H/D ratios were previously normalized by factors of 1.021 and 0.996 respectively in the KP model analysis, but no such model-dependent normalization was imposed here

## Context

This work extends the previous JAM analysis by Cocuzza et al. (2021) that included the earlier MARATHON $^3$He/$^3$H ratio data, now incorporating the new individual $^3$He/D and $^3$H/D cross section ratio measurements. It contradicts the approach of Kulagin and Petti (2004, 2010) which assumed the same universal shape for nuclear to nucleon cross section ratios across all nuclei from Pb down to deuterium, despite binding energies per nucleon for $^2$H, $^3$He and $^3$H being considerably smaller ($\lesssim 3$ MeV) than for heavier nuclei (7-8 MeV). The analysis enables a more robust determination of the isospin dependence of nuclear off-shell corrections and the neutron to proton structure function ratio without theoretical bias from heavy-nucleus extrapolations.
