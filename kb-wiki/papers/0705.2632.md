# Dynamics of Baryons from String Theory and Vector Dominance

> We consider a holographic model of QCD from string theory, a la Sakai and Sugimoto, and study baryons. In this model, mesons are collectively realized as a five-dimensional \$U(N_F)=U(1)\times SU(N_F)$ Yang-Mills field and baryons are classically identified as $SU(N_F)$ solitons with a unit Pontryagin number and $N_c$ electric charges. The soliton is shown to be very small in the large 't Hooft coupling limit, allowing us to introduce an effective field ${\cal B}$. Its coupling to the mesons are dictated by the soliton structure, and consists of a direct magnetic coupling to the $SU(N_F)$ field strength as well as a minimal coupling to the $U(N_F)$ gauge field. Upon the dimensional reduction, this effective action reproduces all interaction terms between nucleons and an infinite tower of mesons in a manner consistent with the large $N_c$ expansion. We further find that all electromagnetic interactions, as inferred from the same effective action via a holographic prescription, are mediated by an infinite tower of vector mesons, rendering the baryon electromagnetic form factors completely vector-dominated as well. We estimate nucleon-meson couplings and also the anomalous magnetic moments, which compare well with nature.

## Key claim

The authors show that in the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model, baryons emerge as small instantons in five dimensions whose size is stabilized by competition between the instanton mass and Coulomb repulsion from \(N_c\) electric charges, and that this picture yields a complete vector dominance of baryon electromagnetic form factors involving the entire tower of vector mesons, not just the lightest ones.

## Method

The authors start from the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic model, where mesons are described by a five-dimensional \(U(N_F) = U(1) \times SU(N_F)\) Yang-Mills field on D8 branes in a curved D4 brane background. Baryons are identified as \(SU(N_F)\) instanton solitons with unit Pontryagin number and \(N_c\) electric charges, arising from D4 branes wrapped on \(S^4\) that dissolve into D8 branes. In the large 't Hooft coupling limit, the instanton size becomes very small, allowing the authors to introduce an effective five-dimensional Dirac field \(\mathcal{B}\) for the baryon, with couplings to the gauge field consisting of a minimal coupling to the \(U(N_F)\) gauge field and a magnetic coupling to the \(SU(N_F)\) field strength. This five-dimensional effective action is then dimensionally reduced to four dimensions, yielding interaction terms between nucleons and the infinite tower of mesons, and electromagnetic interactions are extracted via the holographic prescription.

## Key numbers

* Baryon mass at leading order: \(m_B^{(0)} = \frac{(g_{YM}^2 N_c) N_c}{27\pi} M_{KK}\), where \(M_{KK}\) is the Kaluza-Klein mass scale
* Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi^2 = \frac{1}{54\pi^4} (g_{YM}^2 N_c) N_c M_{KK}^2\)
* For realistic QCD with \(N_c = 3\) and \(f_\pi \sim 93\) MeV, the 't Hooft coupling is \(g_{YM}^2 N_c \sim 17\)
* The instanton size-dependent energy contribution scales as \(\sim \frac16 m_B^{(0)} M_{KK}^2 \rho^2\) for small instanton size \(\rho\)
* The five-dimensional Coulomb energy scales as \(\sim \frac12 \times \frac{e(0)^2 N_c^2}{10\pi^2 \rho^2}\)
* The fifth coordinate range is finite: \(w_{max} \simeq \frac{3.64}{M_{KK}}\)
* The Skyrme term coefficient: \(e^2_{Skyrme} \simeq \frac{54\pi^7}{61} \frac{1}{(g_{YM}^2 N_c) N_c}\)

## Context

This work extends the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) from the meson sector to baryons, building on the observation that baryons in this framework are instantons related to the Skyrmion picture of Atiyah and Manton (1989). The authors show that the hidden local symmetry approach of Bando et al. (1985), which previously incorporated only the lightest vector mesons (\(\rho\) and \(\omega\)), emerges naturally from the five-dimensional theory with the entire tower of vector mesons. This enables computation of nucleon-meson couplings and electromagnetic form factors that are completely vector-dominated, generalizing the vector dominance principle that was already established for mesons in the same model.
