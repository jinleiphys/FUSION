# Asymmetric nuclear matter based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions

> We calculate the properties of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter based on chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions. To this end, we develop an improved normal-ordering framework that allows to include general 3N interactions starting from a plane-wave partial-wave-decomposed form. We present results for the energy per particle for general isospin asymmetries based on a set of different Hamiltonians, study their saturation properties, the incompressibility, symmetry energy, and also provide an analytic parametrization for the energy per particle as a function of density and isospin asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper develops an improved normal-ordering framework that allows inclusion of general three-nucleon (3N) interactions in calculations of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, extending previous work that was limited to small proton fractions. The results show that the commonly used approximation of setting the center-of-mass momentum P=0 for the effective density-dependent NN interaction becomes unreliable at higher densities for asymmetric matter, while a P-averaged approximation reproduces exact Hartree-Fock results over the entire density range.

## Method

The authors compute the energy per particle of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter using many-body perturbation theory up to second order, based on chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at N3LO evolved via similarity renormalization group (SRG) to different resolution scales λ, combined with leading three-nucleon (3N) interactions at N2LO. They develop a framework that evaluates the normal-ordered 3N contribution as an effective density-dependent NN interaction by performing the integration over the Fermi sphere in a partial-wave-decomposed Jacobi momentum basis, including a P-averaged approximation for the center-of-mass momentum dependence. Calculations are performed for eleven proton fractions using seven different Hamiltonians with varying SRG resolution scales, 3N cutoffs, and low-energy couplings, with both free and Hartree-Fock single-particle spectra.

## Key numbers

- The set of seven Hamiltonians uses SRG resolution scales λ = 1.8, 2.0, 2.2, and 2.8 fm^{-1} with 3N cutoffs Λ_3N = 2.0 or 2.5 fm^{-1}
- For neutron matter (x=0), the uncertainty band width is about 4 MeV at saturation density
- For symmetric nuclear matter (x=0.5), the uncertainty band width grows to about 5-6 MeV at the highest density shown
- The P-average approximation shifts results by approximately 1 MeV at saturation density in symmetric matter compared to the P=0 approximation
- The partial-wave convergence test shows deviation of less than 100 keV at saturation density for both neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter when including 3N matrix elements up to J = 9/2

## Context

This work extends the study of Drischler et al. (2014) that was limited to small proton fractions, enabling calculations at arbitrary isospin asymmetries. The framework builds on previous normal-ordering approaches for chiral 3N interactions at N2LO by Holt et al. (2010) and Hebeler et al. (2010), which used the P=0 approximation. The results enable microscopic extraction of the nuclear symmetry energy without empirical parametrizations, and provide input for astrophysical applications such as core-collapse supernovae and neutron star studies. The framework is designed to accommodate subleading 3N interactions at N3LO, whose partial-wave decomposition was completed by Hebeler et al. (2015).
