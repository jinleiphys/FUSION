# Evaluation of theoretical uncertainties in parity-violating electron scattering from nucleons and nuclei

> Parity-violating polarized electron scattering from nucleons and nuclei provides an excellent tool to extract valuable information on nuclear and nucleon structure, as well as to determine Standard Model couplings and higher-order radiative corrections. As measurements become more precise, theoretical models should improve accordingly in order to exploit the experimental data fully in extracting meaningful information. At the same time, it is crucial that theoretical evaluations come with realistic estimations of the corresponding theoretical uncertainties to establish that the precision reached in the measurements is not compromised. Here we consider isospin mixing and the charge distribution in nuclei, and strangeness content of the nucleon together with its axial form factor.

# Evaluation of theoretical uncertainties in parity-violating electron scattering from nucleons and nuclei

## Key claim
The paper systematically evaluates theoretical uncertainties in parity-violating (PV) electron scattering asymmetries for both proton and nuclear targets, showing that while Coulomb distortion effects in $^{12}$C can be controlled below the 0.3% target precision, the uncertainty from nucleon strangeness content in nuclear targets can reach 0.5–1.5%, and the uncertainty from isospin mixing in $^{12}$C can approach 0.3%, the maximum acceptable level, depending on the Skyrme parametrization used.

## Method
The authors compute the PV asymmetry for elastic scattering from protons and from $^{12}$C (a spin-0, $N=Z$ nucleus) using the plane-wave Born approximation with a reference asymmetry, then evaluate deviations from this reference due to Coulomb distortion (via distorted-wave Born approximation with Fermi charge distributions), isospin mixing (via axially-deformed Hartree-Fock with Skyrme interactions and BCS pairing), and nucleon strangeness content (via electric and magnetic strangeness form factors with parameters $\rho_s$ and $\mu_s$). For the proton case, they perform a statistical analysis of existing PV asymmetry data (including Qweak, G0, SAMPLE, PVA4, HAPPEX) to extract correlated constraints on the axial form factor at $Q^2=0$, the strangeness parameters, and the weak neutral current effective couplings.

## Key numbers
- The reference PV asymmetry scale is ${\cal A}_0 = G_F|Q^2|/(2\sqrt{2}\pi\alpha) \approx 1.8 \times 10^{-4} |Q^2|$ with $|Q^2|$ in (GeV/c)$^2$.
- For $ep$ scattering at $|Q^2|=1$ (GeV/c)$^2$, the EM form factor uncertainty in the asymmetry is $\sim$2–3% at $\theta_e=5^\circ$ and $\sim$0.7% at $\theta_e=170^\circ$.
- The axial form factor at $Q^2=0$ is $G_A^{ep}(0) = -1.04 \pm 0.44$ (tree-level value $-g_A = -1.27$ lies within this range).
- For $ep$ backward angles ($110^\circ$), the uncertainty from radiative corrections in the axial form factor is $\sim$12% at $|Q^2|=0.75$ (GeV/c)$^2$ and $\sim$9% at $|Q^2|=0.25$ (GeV/c)$^2$.
- The uncertainty in the $ep$ PV asymmetry from magnetic strangeness ($\mu_s$ in $[-0.3, 0.3]$) at backward angles is $\sim$24–25%.
- For forward-angle $ep$ scattering ($\theta_e < 21^\circ$), the uncertainty from magnetic strangeness is $\sim$30% and from electric strangeness ($\rho_s$ in $[-1.0, 2.0]$) is $\sim$53% at $|Q^2|=0.25$ (GeV/c)$^2$.
- For the Qweak kinematics ($\theta_e \approx 8^\circ$, $|Q^2|=0.025$ (GeV/c)$^2$), the sensitivity is $\sim$12% for $\mu_s$ and $\sim$20% for $\rho_s$.
- For $^{12}$C at 150 MeV incident electrons, Coulomb distortion deviations are around 3% with a spread below 0.01%.
- Isospin mixing deviations in $^{12}$C lie between 0.1 and 0.7% in the region of interest ($q \approx 0.04$–0.12 GeV/c), with a spread between 0.05 and 0.3%.
- Strangeness content deviations in $^{12}$C range from 0.2 to 1% with a spread of 0.5–1.5% in the region of interest.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of PV electron scattering from the proton by Gonzalez-Jimenez et al. (2013, 2014) and from $^{12}$C by Moreno et al. (2014), providing a unified treatment of theoretical uncertainties for both targets. The paper addresses the precision goals set by the MIT workshop (2012) and future facilities such as the MESA accelerator at Mainz (Aulenbacher, 2011) and an upgraded FEL at Jefferson Lab (Neil, 2000). The results enable improved extraction of Standard Model couplings and higher-order radiative corrections from upcoming high-precision PV experiments, while identifying nucleon strangeness content as the dominant source of theoretical uncertainty that requires additional measurements (e.g., the HAPPEX-He experiment on $^4$He by Acha et al., 2007) to be reduced below the target 0.3% level.
