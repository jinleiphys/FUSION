# Relations Between Isoscalar Charge Form Factors of Two- and Three-Nucleon Systems

> Theoretical predictions for the deuteron and the isoscalar trinucleon charge form factors are compared. Correlations between them are found. Linear relations hold for the position of the diffraction minimum and for the position and height of the secondary maximum. The linear relation for the diffraction minimum misses the experimental data.

# Relations Between Isoscalar Charge Form Factors of Two- and Three-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim

Linear correlations exist between the deuteron and isoscalar trinucleon charge monopole form factors for the position of the diffraction minimum, the position of the secondary maximum, and the height of the secondary maximum, but the linear relation for the diffraction minimum misses the experimental data, indicating that the present understanding of meson-exchange currents cannot simultaneously account for the electromagnetic properties of the two- and three-nucleon systems.

## Method

The authors calculated deuteron and isoscalar trinucleon charge form factors using six realistic two-nucleon potentials (Paris, Reid soft-core RSC, and Bonn OBEPQ, A, B, C) in momentum space, employing a charge operator expanded to order (1/M)^2 that includes one-nucleon and two-nucleon corrections of relativistic order based on meson exchange. The calculations were performed both in nonrelativistic impulse approximation and with full charge operator corrections, using trinucleon wave functions obtained from momentum-space techniques without Delta-isobar degrees of freedom. The characteristic properties of the form factors (diffraction minimum position, secondary maximum position and height) were then compared across potentials to identify linear relationships.

## Key numbers

- Common slope a = 0.60 for the linear relation between deuteron and isoscalar trinucleon positions of both the diffraction minimum and the secondary maximum
- Intercept b = 2.43 for the linear relation of the diffraction minimum and secondary maximum positions
- Slope c = 0.78 for the linear relation between the heights of the secondary maximum
- The parameters determined individually for the considered potentials cluster around a = 0.60 (0.75), b = 2.43 (0.00), and c = 0.78 (1.40), where values in brackets are predicted by a strict quasi-deuteron model
- The deviation between estimated and calculated deuteron charge form factors falls below 30% for momentum transfers Q^2 >= 9 fm^{-2}
- The parameters a, b, and c coincide within 10% for a and within 20% for c across potentials

## Context

This work extends comparisons of electromagnetic form factors between the deuteron and the isoscalar trinucleon system, made possible by experimental separations of the deuteron charge form factor into monopole and quadrupole parts (The, 1991) and of trinucleon form factors into isoscalar and isovector components (Amroun, 1992). The linear relations are conceptually related to the quasi-deuteron model of Gangopadhyay and Levinger (1992), though the authors note that realistic trinucleon wave functions do not precisely satisfy the quasi-deuteron assumption (Hajduk, 1980). The finding that existing nonrelativistic potentials and meson-exchange current models cannot simultaneously describe two- and three-nucleon electromagnetic properties motivates further experimental measurements of the deuteron charge form factor with reduced error bars and at larger momentum transfers, as well as fully covariant treatments of the three-nucleon system.
