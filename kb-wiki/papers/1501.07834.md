# States generated in the $K$-multi-$ρ$ interactions

> In the present work we use three-body interaction formalism to investigate the $K$-multi-$ρ$ interactions. First, we reproduced the resonances $f_2(1270)$ and $K_1(1270)$ in the $ρρ$ and $ρK$ two-body interactions respectively, as the clusters of the fixed center approximation. Then, we study the three-body $K -ρρ(f_2)$ and $ρ- ρK (K_1)$ interactions with the fixed center approximation of the Faddeev equations. Furthermore, we extrapolate the formalism to study the four-body, five-body and six-body systems, containing one $K$ meson and multi-$ρ$ mesons. In our research, without introducing any free parameters, we generate the $K_2 (1770)$ state in the three-body interaction with the mass of 1707 MeV and a width about 113 MeV, which are consistent with the experiments. We also find a clear resonant structure in our results of the five-body interaction, with a mass 2505 MeV and a width about 32 MeV or more, which is associated to the $K_4 (2500)$ state, where we obtain consistent results with the experimental findings. Furthermore, we predict some new states in the other many body interactions, $K_3 (2080)$, $K_5 (2670)$ (isospin $I=1/2$), and $K_4 (2640)$ (isospin $I=3/2$), with uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $K_2(1770)$ resonance can be dynamically generated as a $\rho$-$K_1(1270)$ molecular state in the three-body $K$-multi-$\rho$ interaction, and that the $K_4(2500)$ state appears as a clear resonant structure in the five-body interaction, both without introducing any free parameters; however, the $K_2(1770)$ result is extracted from the scattering amplitude on the first Riemann sheet rather than from a pole search on the second sheet, and the width of the $K_4(2500)$ is given as "about 32 MeV or more," indicating significant uncertainty.

## Method

The author uses the fixed center approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations, treating a two-body resonance (either $f_2(1270)$ from $\rho\rho$ or $K_1(1270)$ from $\rho K$) as a fixed cluster and then computing the multiple scattering of a third particle (either $K$ or $\rho$) with the constituents of that cluster. The two-body scattering amplitudes for the $\rho\rho$ and $\rho K$ subsystems are obtained from the chiral unitary approach (ChUA) using local hidden gauge Lagrangians and the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the loop functions regularized by a cutoff method. The formalism is then extrapolated to four-body, five-body, and six-body systems by iteratively treating the newly generated resonances as clusters for the next level of interaction.

## Key numbers

* $K_2(1770)$ state: mass of 1707 MeV and width about 113 MeV, generated in the three-body $\rho$-$K_1(1270)$ interaction with isospin $I=1/2$.
* $K_4(2500)$ state: mass of 2505 MeV and width about 32 MeV or more, found in the five-body interaction.
* $f_2(1270)$ cluster: reproduced in the $\rho\rho$ two-body interaction with a mass of about 1275.1 MeV (threshold at 1551.0 MeV).
* $K_1(1270)$ cluster: reproduced in the $\rho K$ two-body interaction with a mass of about 1270.0 MeV (threshold at 1271.0 MeV).
* Cutoff values used: $q_{max}=875$ MeV for the $f_2(1270)$ cluster and $q_{max}=1035$ MeV for the $K_1(1270)$ cluster, determined by matching the dimensional regularization loop function at threshold.

## Context

This work extends the fixed center approximation studies of multi-$\rho$ systems by Roca (2010) and of $K^*$-multi-$\rho$ systems by Yamagata-Sekihara (2010) to the $K$-multi-$\rho$ sector, where the $K$ meson replaces the $K^*$ meson. It also parallels the charmed $D^*$-multi-$\rho$ predictions by Xiao (2012). The results predict new states $K_3(2080)$, $K_5(2670)$ (isospin $I=1/2$), and $K_4(2640)$ (isospin $I=3/2$) with uncertainties, which could guide future experimental searches for strange meson resonances with high spin.
