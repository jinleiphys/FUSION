# Application of the inverse Hamiltonian method to Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations

> We solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations for a spherical mean field and a pairing potential with the inverse Hamiltonian method, which we have developed for the solution of the Dirac equation. This method is based on the variational principle for the inverse Hamiltonian, and is applicable to Hamiltonians that are bound neither from above nor below. We demonstrate that the method works well not only for the Dirac but also for the HFB equations.

## Key claim

The inverse Hamiltonian method, previously developed for solving the Dirac equation, can be applied to Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in coordinate space without variational collapse, provided the energy shift parameter \(W\) is placed between the positive and negative quasi-particle spectra.

## Method

The authors solve the radial HFB equation for a spherical mean field and a pairing potential by iteratively applying the operator \(\exp(\Delta T/(H-W))\) to an initial trial wave function, where \(W\) is a real constant set between the positive and negative quasi-particle energy spectra. At each iteration step, the linear system \((H-W)|\phi^{(n)}\rangle = |\psi^{(n)}\rangle\) is solved using the conjugate gradient normal residual (CGNR) method, a Krylov subspace technique for sparse linear systems. The radial coordinate is discretized on a mesh with box boundary conditions, and the second derivative is approximated by a three-point finite difference.

## Key numbers

* The lowest \(s_{1/2}\) quasi-particle energy converges to \(E = 0.424\) MeV, matching the exact diagonalization result.
* The three lowest \(s_{1/2}\) quasi-particle energies from the inverse Hamiltonian method are 0.42414 MeV, 1.0383 MeV, and 2.3063 MeV, identical to the exact values to the digits shown.
* The corresponding occupation probabilities \(v_k^2\) are 0.5574, \(3.972 \times 10^{-2}\), and \(9.689 \times 10^{-3}\), again matching the exact values exactly.
* The calculation uses a Woods-Saxon mean field with parameters \(v_0 = -38.5\) MeV, \(v_{\ell s} = 14\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^2\), \(R_0 = 5.63\) fm, and \(a = 0.66\) fm, with an average pairing gap \(\bar\Delta = 1.0\) MeV and chemical potential \(\lambda = -0.5\) MeV.
* The method parameters are set to \(W = 0.1\) MeV and \(\Delta T = 10\) MeV, with a radial mesh up to \(r_{\rm max} = 30\) fm and mesh size \(\Delta r = 0.1\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the inverse Hamiltonian method from the Dirac equation (Hagino and Tanimura, 2010) to the HFB equations, which share the problem of spectra unbounded from below. It provides an alternative to the two-basis method (Bonche et al., 1985; Terasaki et al., 1996) and the gradient method (Mang et al., 1976) for solving HFB equations in coordinate space, avoiding the variational collapse that would occur with the imaginary time method (Davies et al., 1980). The results enable future application to self-consistent HFB calculations on a three-dimensional mesh, which the authors state is in progress.
