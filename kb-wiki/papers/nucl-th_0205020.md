# Pseudovector vs. pseudoscalar coupling in one-boson exchange NN potentials

> We examine the effects of pseudoscalar and pseudovector coupling of the pi and eta mesons in one-boson exchange models of the NN interaction using two approaches: time-ordered perturbation theory unitarized with the relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and a reduced Bethe-Salpeter equation approach using the Thompson equation. Contact terms in the one-boson exchange amplitudes in time-ordered perturbation theory lead naturally to the introduction of s-channel nucleonic cutoffs for the interaction, which strongly suppresses the far off-shell behavior of the amplitudes in both approaches. Differences between the resulting NN predictions of the various models are found to be small, and particularly so when coupling constants of the other mesons are readjusted within reasonable limits.

## Key claim

Differences between pseudoscalar and pseudovector coupling of the π and η mesons in one-boson exchange NN potentials are small in both time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) and Thompson equation approaches, particularly when coupling constants of other mesons are readjusted within reasonable limits; the most notable exception is the mixing parameter ε₁, which is sensitive to the coupling scheme.

## Method

The authors constructed four one-boson exchange NN models: two using TOPT unitarized with the relativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation (one with pseudovector and one with pseudoscalar coupling for π and η), and two using the Thompson equation (a reduced Bethe-Salpeter approach) with the same coupling alternatives. All models included π, η, ρ, ω, σ, and a₀ mesons, with both t-channel multipole form factors and s-channel nucleonic cutoffs (Λ_N fixed at 700 MeV). A constrained least-squares fit of coupling constants and cutoff masses was performed to NN phase shifts from 20 to 300 MeV laboratory kinetic energy (SP40 analysis), with parameters restricted to ranges consistent with literature values.

## Key numbers

- πN coupling constant fixed at g_π²/4π = 13.8 in all models.
- ηN coupling constant allowed to vary from 0 to 6.
- Nucleonic cutoff mass Λ_N fixed at 700 MeV in all models.
- ω coupling in the TOPT pseudoscalar model (PS) was about 25 percent larger than in the pseudovector model (PV).
- σ meson t-channel cutoff Λ_σ reached 10 GeV in the Thompson equation models (effectively infinite).
- Deuteron binding energy fitted accurately in all four models; d-state probability was lower for pseudoscalar coupling than pseudovector coupling in both TOPT and Thompson approaches.
- Phase shifts fitted in the range 20 to 300 MeV laboratory kinetic energy.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing comparison of pseudoscalar versus pseudovector πN coupling in NN interactions, previously examined in early meson-exchange models (e.g., Erkelenz 1974, Holinde 1975, Machleidt 1989) and in chiral perturbation theory approaches (Epelbaum 1998, Entem 2001). The authors specifically address the omission of contact terms in earlier TOPT-based Bonn potentials (Machleidt 1987) and show that including them with s-channel cutoffs yields small differences between coupling schemes. The results enable future refinements of OBE models by clarifying that either coupling form can be accommodated without major reworking, provided other meson parameters are adjusted.
