# Azimuthally-sensitive femtoscopy from RHIC to LHC in hydrodynamics with statistical hadronization

> Azimuthally-sensitive femtoscopy for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC is explored within the approach consisting of the hydrodynamics of perfect fluid followed by statistical hadronization. It is found that for the RHIC initial conditions the very same framework that reproduces the standard soft observables (including the transverse-momentum spectra, the elliptic flow, and the azimuthally-averaged HBT radii) leads to a proper description of the azimuthally-sensitive femtoscopic observables - we find that the azimuthal variation of the side and out HBT radii is very well reproduced for all centralities, while the out-side correlation is somewhat too large for non-central events. Concerning the dependence of the femtoscopic parameters on kT we find that it is very well reproduced for the out and side radii, and fairly well for the long radius. The model is then extrapolated for the LHC energy. We predict the overall moderate growth of the HBT radii and the decrease of their azimuthal oscillations. Such effects are naturally caused by longer evolution times. In addition, we discuss in detail the space-time patterns of particle emission. We show that they are quite complex and argue that the overall shape seen by the femtoscopic methods cannot be easily disentangled on the basis of simple-minded arguments.

## Key claim

The same hydrodynamic plus statistical hadronization framework that successfully reproduces standard soft observables (transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and azimuthally-averaged HBT radii) at RHIC also reproduces azimuthally-sensitive femtoscopic observables, with the qualification that the out-side correlation is somewhat too large for non-central events.

## Method

The authors use ideal-fluid, boost-invariant hydrodynamics with Gaussian initial conditions matched to Glauber Monte Carlo simulations, followed by statistical hadronization via THERMINATOR at a universal freeze-out temperature of 145 MeV. They compute azimuthally-sensitive HBT radii by constructing 144 separate correlation functions (6 centrality bins × 4 kT bins × 6 bins for the azimuthal angle) and fitting the ϕ dependence with second-order Fourier coefficients. The model is then extrapolated to LHC energy by adjusting the initial central temperature to 768 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 145 MeV (universal, for both RHIC and LHC)
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamics: τ₀ = 0.25 fm (for all centralities, both RHIC and LHC)
- For RHIC central 0-5% collisions: a = 2.70 fm, b = 2.93 fm, initial central temperature T_i = 500 MeV
- For LHC central 0-5% collisions: a = 2.65 fm, b = 2.89 fm, initial central temperature T_i = 768 MeV
- RHIC freeze-out lifetime: about 9 fm for central (0-5%) and 7 fm for mid-peripheral (20-30%) collisions
- R_out/R_side ratio decreases from 1.1 at RHIC to 0.95 at LHC for top 5% centrality
- For RHIC, R_long,0^2 overshoots data by up to 10% in the lowest kT bin (0.15-0.25 GeV)
- R_out-side oscillations are about 50% above the data for non-central events
- Less than 0.5% of particles are emitted back into the hydrodynamic region (non-causal surface emission negligible)
- Average number of trajectory crossings after freeze-out: about 1.5-1.7 per pion

## Context

This work extends the successful uniform description of soft observables at RHIC reported in Broniowski (2008) to azimuthally-sensitive femtoscopy, addressing the "RHIC HBT puzzle" where previous hydrodynamic calculations (Heinz 2002, Hirano 2004, Huovinen 2006) struggled to simultaneously describe elliptic flow and HBT radii. The Gaussian initial condition improves over the standard Glauber initial condition used in Chojnacki (2007), where the R_out/R_side ratio was reproduced only at the 20% level. The LHC predictions enable future experimental tests of the transition from "outside-in" to "inside-out" freeze-out patterns, which would provide strong evidence for hydrodynamically behaving medium in heavy-ion collisions.
