# Exploring the effects of $α$-clustered structure of $^{16}O$ nuclei in anisotropic flow fluctuations in $^{16}O$-$^{16}O$ collisions at the LHC within a CGC+Hydro framework

> In this paper, we explore the effects of the presence of clustered nuclear structure of $^{16}O$ in the final state elliptic flow fluctuations through $^{16}O$-$^{16}O$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7$ TeV within a hybrid model, IPGlasma+MUSIC+iSS+ UrQMD. We compare the results of elliptic flow fluctuations using $α$-clustered nuclear structure to the Woods-Saxon nuclear profile having no clustered structure. We observe a significant difference in the elliptic flow fluctuations, which arise due to the consideration of a clustered nuclear structure of $^{16}O$.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the presence of an alpha-clustered nuclear structure in 16O nuclei produces a significant difference in elliptic flow fluctuations compared to a Woods-Saxon profile in 16O-16O collisions at the LHC, but this effect is most pronounced in the (10-30)% multiplicity class where both initial-state eccentricity fluctuations and final-state elliptic flow fluctuations are higher for the Woods-Saxon case.

## Method
The authors used a hybrid IPGlasma+MUSIC+iSS+UrQMD model to simulate 16O-16O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV, comparing two nuclear density profiles: a Woods-Saxon profile with no clustered structure and an alpha-clustered profile where alpha particles sit at the corners of a regular tetrahedron. Elliptic flow fluctuations were estimated using the multi-particle Q-cumulant method.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN)=7 TeV for 16O-16O collisions
- For the (10-30)% multiplicity class, both F(epsilon_2) and F(v_2) are higher for the Woods-Saxon case compared to the alpha-clustered case
- From 0 to 30% multiplicity class, <epsilon_2> increases for both nuclear profiles while <v_2> shows a slightly decreasing trend
- F(epsilon_2) decreases from 0 to 30% multiplicity class for both profiles, while F(v_2) shows an increasing feature for the Woods-Saxon case

## Context
This work extends studies of small collision systems at the LHC and RHIC, which bridge the multiplicity gap between pp, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions as discussed by Brewer (2021). The observation of QGP-like signatures in small systems, noted by ALICE (2016), motivates this investigation. The results suggest that elliptic flow fluctuations can serve as an experimental observable to distinguish between clustered and uniform nuclear density profiles in oxygen-oxygen collisions at the LHC.
