# The interaction of glueball and heavy-light flavoured meson in holographic QCD

> We construct the D4/D8 brane configuration in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model by introducing a pair of heavy flavour brane with a heavy-light open string. The multiplets created by the heavy-light string acquire mass due to the finite separation of the heavy and light flavour branes thus they could be identified as the heavy-light meson fields in this model. On the other hand the glueball field is identified as the gravitational fluctuations carried by the close string in the bulk, so this model is able to describe the interaction of glueball and heavy-light meson through the open-close string interaction in gauge-gravity duality. We explicitly derive the effective action for the various glueballs and heavy-light mesons then numerically evaluate the associated coupling constants. Afterwards the decay widths of various glueballs to the lowest heavy-light meson, which is identified as $D^{0}$ meson, are calculated by using our effective action. This work extends the previous investigations of glueball in holographic QCD and it is also a further prediction of glueball-meson interaction.

## Key claim

This work extends the holographic investigation of glueball-meson interactions in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model by including heavy flavour, specifically deriving the effective action for the interaction of various glueballs and heavy-light mesons and numerically evaluating the decay widths of glueballs into the lowest heavy-light meson identified as the \(D^{0}\) meson, with the caveat that the mass terms \(M_V, M_S\) for the heavy-light meson fields may be theoretically divergent and require holographic renormalization.

## Method

The authors construct a D4/D8 brane configuration in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model by introducing an extra pair of heavy flavour D8/anti-D8 branes separated from the light flavour branes, with a heavy-light open string stretched between them. The glueball field is identified as gravitational fluctuations of the 11d supergravity background, and the heavy-light meson fields arise from the multiplets created by the heavy-light string. The effective action for the interaction of various glueballs (exotic scalar \(0^{++}\), dilatonic scalar \(0^{++}\), and tensor \(2^{++}\)) with heavy-light mesons is derived by combining the gravitational perturbations in the bulk with the heavy-light meson fields on the flavour branes, and the decay widths are computed numerically.

## Key numbers

* The lightest scalar glueball mass is predicted to be around 1600-1800 MeV by lattice QCD simulations (cited from prior work).
* The Kaluza-Klein mass scale \(M_{KK}\) is related to the geometry via \(\delta X^4 = 1/M_{KK} = L^2/(3 r_{KK})\).
* The normalization constant for the exotic scalar glueball kinetic term is \(\mathcal{C}_E = \int_{r_{KK}}^{\infty} dr \frac{r^3}{L^3} \frac{5}{8} H_E^2(r)\).
* The normalization constants for the dilatonic and tensor glueball are \(\mathcal{C}_D = 6 \int_{r_{KK}}^{\infty} dr \frac{r^3}{L^3} H_D^2(r)\) and \(\mathcal{C}_T = 2 \int_{r_{KK}}^{\infty} dr \frac{r^3}{L^3} H_T^2(r)\).
* The Yang-Mills action prefactor is \(\kappa = \lambda N_c / (216 \pi^3)\).
* The mass terms for the heavy-light vector and scalar mesons are given by \(M_V^2 = v_V^2 \kappa \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} dZ K(Z)^{1/6} \phi_m(Z) \phi_n(Z)\) and \(M_S^2 = v_S^2 \kappa \int_{-\infty}^{+\infty} dZ K(Z)^{3/2} \tilde{\phi}_m(Z) \tilde{\phi}_n(Z)\), with \(v_V^2 = \frac{4}{27} \frac{(1+N_f)^2}{N_f^2} v^2 M_{KK}^3 R^3\) and \(v_S^2 = \frac{64}{2187} \frac{(1+N_f)^2}{N_f^2} v^2 M_{KK}^9 R^9\).

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of glueball interactions in holographic QCD using the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, specifically building on the approaches of prior studies (key-25, key-26) that examined glueball-light meson interactions. The mechanism for introducing heavy flavour via separated D8-branes with a heavy-light string follows the Higgs mechanism in string theory as proposed in earlier work (key-28, key-29, key-30, key-31). The results enable further predictions of glueball-meson interactions and decay widths that are not explicitly provided by lattice QCD, which becomes extremely complex when including dynamical quarks with real-time quantities.
