# Energy dependence of the proton geometry in exclusive vector meson production

> The gluon radius of the proton is expected to increase at small gluon momentum fractions $x$, an effect which has hitherto not been considered in the dipole model framework. We investigate the energy dependence of exclusive $J/ψ$, $ϕ$, and $ρ$ production by introducing three models for $x$ dependence of the gluon thickness function. We allow the transverse width of the proton to increase as $x$ decreases, using novel parametrisations in the spherical proton and the hotspot model. We compare these with a model where the number of hotspots increases as $x$ decreases and confront the models with HERA data. The models exhibit clear differences in the slope of the $t$-spectra and in the cross section ratios between coherent and incoherent events. Comparisons to $t$-slopes and $W_{γp}$ measurements show a preference for models where the proton's size increases as $x$ decreases.

# Energy dependence of the proton geometry in exclusive vector meson production

## Key claim
The gluonic radius of the proton increases at small gluon momentum fractions x, and models where the proton's transverse size grows with decreasing x are preferred by comparisons to HERA t-slope and Wγp measurements, though the current data cannot definitively distinguish between different implementations of this energy dependence.

## Method
The authors introduce three models for the x-dependence of the gluon thickness function within the dipole model framework: (1) a spherical proton with x-dependent width, (2) a varying hotspot width (VHW) model where the transverse size of individual gluon hotspots increases as x decreases, and (3) a varying hotspot number (VHN) model where the number of hotspots increases at small x. These are implemented in both the saturated bSat (IP-Sat) and linearised bNonSat dipole models, and the parameters are determined through simultaneous fits to the t-dependence of coherent and incoherent vector meson production data from HERA for J/ψ, ρ, and φ mesons.

## Key numbers
- For the spherical proton with x-dependent width: Bp = 2.3 ± 0.03 GeV⁻² and λp = -0.062 ± 0.002, with χ²/ndf = 48.06/48
- For the VHW model with bSat for J/ψ: Bhs = 0.245 ± 0.010 GeV⁻², λhs = -0.213 ± 0.007, with χ²/ndf = 72.23/54
- For the VHW model with bNonSat for J/ψ: Bhs = 0.256 ± 0.009 GeV⁻², λhs = -0.1980 ± 0.0045, with χ²/ndf = 83.87/54
- For the logarithmic model for J/ψ: b0 = 0.075 ± 0.004 GeV⁻², x0 = 6.7 ± 1.2, with χ²/ndf = 72.23/54
- For the logarithmic model for ρ and φ: b0 = 0.117 ± 0.004 GeV⁻², x0 = 20 ± 6, with χ²/ndf = 458.53/65
- For the FH model with bSat for J/ψ: Bq = 0.985 ± 0.050 GeV⁻², with χ²/ndf = 71.95/55
- For the FH model with bNonSat for J/ψ: Bq = 0.935 ± 0.030 GeV⁻², with χ²/ndf = 66.76/55
- The extracted coherent slope BD grows with Wγp in the VHW and logarithmic models, while remaining constant in the FH and VHN models
- The ratio of incoherent to coherent cross section decreases with x in the VHW and logarithmic models, while remaining nearly constant for the FH model

## Context
This work extends the dipole model framework of Kowalski (2003) and the hotspot model of Mantysaari (2016) by introducing energy-dependent proton geometry, an effect previously not considered in these models. The study is motivated by Regge theory predictions of Gribov diffusion (Gribov 1973) and by JIMWLK evolution studies (Mantysaari 2018, Schlichting 2014) that indicate increasing gluonic radius at small x. The VHN model follows the approach of Cepila (2016) where the number of hotspots increases with decreasing x. The results enable predictions for future collider experiments such as the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the Large Hadron-Electron Collider (LHeC), and provide a framework for distinguishing between different mechanisms of energy-dependent proton structure through measurements of the incoherent-to-coherent cross section ratio at high energies.
