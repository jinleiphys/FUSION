# A T-odd Momentum Correlation in Radiative β-Decay

> We consider neutron radiative β-decay, n\to p e^- \barν_eγ, and compute the T-odd momentum correlation in the decay rate characterized by the kinematical variable ξ= {\bf l}_ν\cdot ({\bf l}_e\times{\bf k}) arising from electromagnetic final-state interactions in the Standard Model. Our expression for the corresponding T-odd asymmetry A_ξ^{SM} is exact in O(α) up to terms of recoil order, and we evaluate it numerically under various kinematic conditions. Noting the universality of the V-A law in the absence of recoil order terms, we retain the parametric dependence on masses and coupling constants throughout, so that our results serve as a template for the computation of A_ξ^{SM} in allowed nuclear radiative β-decays and hyperon radiative β-decays as well. We consider the pattern of A_ξ^{SM} which appears in nuclear decays and show that A_ξ^{SM} can be suppressed in particular cases, facilitating searches for new sources of CP-violation in such processes.

## Key claim

The Standard Model produces a T-odd triple momentum correlation in neutron radiative β-decay via electromagnetic final-state interactions, yielding an asymmetry A_ξ^SM that is exact in O(α) up to recoil-order corrections; this asymmetry can be suppressed in particular nuclear decays, which facilitates searches for new CP-violation sources beyond the Standard Model.

## Method

The authors computed the T-odd asymmetry A_ξ from the kinematical variable ξ = l_ν · (l_e × k) by evaluating the interference between the tree-level amplitude and the anti-Hermitian (imaginary) parts of the O(α) one-loop corrections, using Cutkosky cuts to identify all physical two-particle intermediate states. They categorized the cut diagrams into gauge-invariant families (γ-e Compton, γ-p Compton, e-p scattering with bremsstrahlung, and e-p scattering) and performed the intermediate phase-space integrals using Passarino-Veltman reduction, working to leading order in the recoil expansion (ε/M). The asymmetry was then obtained by numerically integrating the spin-averaged T-odd squared matrix element over the allowed phase space in the neutron rest frame, with a restricted angular range for the γ-e opening angle.

## Key numbers

- A_ξ = 1.76 × 10^{-5} for neutron radiative β-decay with minimum photon energy ω_min = 0.01 MeV
- A_ξ = 6.07 × 10^{-5} for ω_min = 0.1 MeV
- A_ξ = 1.31 × 10^{-4} for ω_min = 0.3 MeV
- A_ξ = 1.89 × 10^{-4} for ω_min = 0.7 MeV
- The asymmetry scales as (1-λ²)/(1+3λ²) with λ = g_A/g_V = -1.2701 ± 0.0025
- Input parameters: m_e = 0.510999 MeV, M_n = 939.565 MeV, M_p = 938.272 MeV, α^{-1} = 137.0360

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of T-odd correlations in β-decay, particularly the D-term correlation D S·(l_e × l_ν) studied by Mumm (2011) and Calaprice, and the triple momentum correlation first considered in K⁺_{l3γ} decay by Braguta. The authors note that their results serve as a template for computing A_ξ^SM in allowed nuclear radiative β-decays and hyperon radiative β-decays, and that the (1-λ²) dependence means the asymmetry can be suppressed when the effective axial-vector coupling is near unity, as in ¹⁹Ne decay (g_A^eff = 0.928), enabling cleaner searches for CP-violation beyond the Standard Model.
