# Chiral perturbation theory for heavy hadrons and chiral effective field theory for heavy hadronic molecules

> Chiral symmetry and its spontaneous breaking play an important role both in the light hadron and heavy hadron systems. The chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT) is the low energy effective field theory of the QCD. In this work, we shall review the investigations on the chiral corrections to the properties of the heavy mesons and baryons within the framework of $χ$PT. We will also review the scatterings of the light pseudoscalar mesons and heavy hadrons, through which many new resonances such as the $D_{s0}^\ast(2317)$ could be understood. Moreover, many new hadron states were observed experimentally in the past decades. A large group of these states is near-threshold resonances, such as the charged charmoniumlike $Z_c$ and $Z_{cs}$ states, bottomoniumlike $Z_b$ states, hidden-charm pentaquark $P_c$ and $P_{cs}$ states and the doubly charmed $T_{cc}$ state, etc. They are very good candidates of the loosely bound molecular states composed of a pair of charmed (bottom) hadrons, which are very similar to the loosely bound deuteron. The modern nuclear force was built upon the chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT), which is the extension of the $χ$PT to the systems with two matter fields. The long-range and medium-long-range interactions between two nucleons arise from the single- and double-pion exchange respectively, which are well constrained by the chiral symmetry and its spontaneous breaking. The short-distance interactions can be described by the low energy constants. Such a framework works very well for the nucleon-nucleon scattering and nuclei. In this work, we will perform an extensive review of the progress on the heavy hadronic molecular states within the framework of $χ$EFT. We shall emphasize that the same chiral dynamics not only govern the nuclei and forms the deuteron, but also dictates the shallow bound states or resonances composed of two heavy hadrons.

# Chiral perturbation theory for heavy hadrons and chiral effective field theory for heavy hadronic molecules

## Key claim
The same chiral dynamics that govern nuclear forces and form the deuteron also dictate the shallow bound states or resonances composed of two heavy hadrons, making heavy hadronic molecular states natural extensions of the nuclear force framework to the heavy flavor sector.

## Method
The authors review the construction of chiral Lagrangians for heavy hadrons using superfields that embed heavy quark spin symmetry, building blocks for Goldstone bosons with SU(3) flavor symmetry, and the heavy field expansion formalism. They then extend chiral perturbation theory to chiral effective field theory for systems with two heavy matter fields, where long-range and medium-long-range interactions arise from single- and double-pion exchange constrained by chiral symmetry, with short-distance interactions described by low energy constants.

## Key numbers
- The scalar external field is set as $s = \mathrm{diag}(m_u, m_d, m_s)$ to introduce quark mass effects, with $\chi_+ = 2B_0\mathrm{diag}(m_u, m_d, m_s) = \mathrm{diag}(2m_\pi^2, 2m_\pi^2, 4m_K^2 - 2m_\pi^2)$ at leading order
- The electric charge matrix for charmed meson SU(3) multiplets is $\mathscr{Q} = \mathrm{diag}(0, -1, -1)$ for $(\bar{D}^{(\ast)0}, D^{(\ast)-}, D_s^{(\ast)-})$ and $\mathscr{Q} = \mathrm{diag}(1, 0, 0)$ for $(B^{(\ast)+}, B^{(\ast)0}, B_s^{(\ast)0})$
- The light quark electric charge matrix is $\mathscr{Q}_l = \mathrm{diag}(2/3, -1/3, -1/3)$
- The fine structure constant used in radiative decay widths is $\alpha = 1/137$
- The radiative decay width for $V \to P\gamma$ is $\Gamma[V \to P\gamma] = \frac{\alpha}{3} \frac{m_P}{m_V} |\mu'(0)|^2 |\boldsymbol{q}|^3$

## Context
This review extends the chiral perturbation theory framework developed for light hadrons to heavy hadron systems, building on the foundational work of Falk (1991) on superfield constructions for heavy quark symmetry. The paper systematizes the building block approach for chiral Lagrangians (following Fettes 1998, Fettes 2000, Jiang 2019, Qiu 2020) and applies it to understand near-threshold resonances including the $D_{s0}^\ast(2317)$, charged charmoniumlike $Z_c$ and $Z_{cs}$ states, bottomoniumlike $Z_b$ states, hidden-charm pentaquark $P_c$ and $P_{cs}$ states, and the doubly charmed $T_{cc}$ state as molecular candidates. The work enables systematic calculations of electromagnetic form factors and radiative decay widths for heavy hadronic molecules using the same chiral dynamics that govern nuclear forces.
