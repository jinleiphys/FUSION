# A Model for the 3He(\vec d, p)4He Reaction at Intermediate Energies

> Polarization correlation coefficients have been measured atRIKEN for the \vec 3He(\vec d,p)4He reaction at intermediate energies. We propose a model for the (\vec d, p) reaction mechanism using the pd elastic scattering amplitude which is rigorously determined by a Faddeev calculation and using modern NN forces. Our theoretical predictions for deuteron polarization observables A_y, A_{yy}, A_{xx} and A_{xz} at E_d=140, 200 and 270 MeV agree qualitatively in shape with the experimental data for the reaction 3He(\vec d,p)4He.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the \(^3\text{He}(\vec d, p)^4\text{He}\) reaction at forward angles is mainly driven by elastic pd scattering, and that the resulting spin observables from a full Faddeev solution for the pd amplitude agree qualitatively in shape with experimental data, though the absolute values are too high.

## Method

The reaction mechanism is modeled as a three-body process where the deuteron picks up a proton from \(^3\text{He}\), scatters elastically, and then combines with the spectator nucleons to form an \(\alpha\) particle. The elastic pd scattering amplitude is rigorously determined by solving the 3N Faddeev equation using the AV18 NN potential. The kinematics are fixed by assuming the momentum of the scattered deuteron equals the spectator deuteron momentum in \(^3\text{He}\), and the Faddeev results are evaluated at effective 3-body center-of-mass energies \(E_{3CM} = 66.7\), 100, and 133 MeV corresponding to deuteron lab energies \(E_d = 140\), 200, and 270 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The polarization correlation coefficient \(C_\parallel\) from a simple PWIA model ranges from 0.645 (CD-Bonn potential) to 0.726 (Nijmegen II potential), while the experimental value is \(0.223 \pm 0.044 (\text{statistical}) \pm 0.037 (\text{systematic})\).
- The deuteron D-state probabilities for the modern NN potentials used are: CD-Bonn 4.86%, AV18 5.78%, Nijmegen 93 5.76%, Nijmegen I 5.68%, Nijmegen II 5.65%.
- The effective 3-body center-of-mass energies used in the Faddeev calculations are \(E_{3CM} = 66.7\), 100, and 133 MeV, corresponding to \(E_d = 140\), 200, and 270 MeV respectively.
- The model predictions for \(A_{yy}\) and \(A_{xx}\) at the highest energy (\(E_d = 270\) MeV) show qualitative agreement with a single data point from the RIKEN experiment (Uesaka et al., 2000).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the \(^3\text{He}(\vec d, p)^4\text{He}\) reaction beyond the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA), which was shown to be very poor in describing the experimental data from RIKEN (Uesaka et al., 2000). The model contrasts with the three-cluster model of the SUT group (Oryu et al.) which gave only tiny deviations from PWIA, and the Hosei group's analysis (Hosei et al.) which concluded PWIA describes global features. The approach enables future improvements by including neutron pickup from \(^3\text{He}\) (since the polarization of \(^3\text{He}\) is carried by more than 90% by the neutron) and proper rotation of the deuteron spin for polarization observables.
