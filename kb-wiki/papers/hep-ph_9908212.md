# The effective potential of composite diquark fields and the spectrum of resonances in dense QCD

> The effective potential of composite diquark fields responsible for color symmetry breaking in cold very dense QCD, in which long-range interactions dominate, is derived. The spectrum of excitations and the universality class of this dynamics are described.

## Key claim

The effective potential for the diquark condensate in cold dense QCD is multibranched with an infinite number of local minima, implying an infinite tower of massive radial excitations of Nambu-Goldstone bosons, but only under the assumption that baryon density is high enough that long-range magnetic gluon interactions dominate and fermion pairing in the diquark channel dominates over chiral pairing.

## Method

The authors derive the one-particle-irreducible effective potential for the composite diquark field by introducing a constant external source coupled to the diquark operator, solving the Schwinger-Dyson gap equation (modified for nonzero source) analytically in two momentum regions, matching the solutions at the scale of the gap, and then performing a Legendre transform. The gap equation is reduced to a differential equation whose solution involves Bessel functions, and the effective potential is obtained in parametric form as a function of the zero-momentum gap parameter.

## Key numbers

- The normal nuclear density is \(n_0 \simeq 0.17~\text{fm}^{-3}\), cited as the threshold above which color superconducting quark matter may exist in neutron stars.
- The effective cutoff in the gap equation is \(\Lambda = (4\pi)^{3/2}\mu/\alpha_s^{5/2}\), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential and \(\alpha_s\) is the QCD running coupling at scale \(\mu\).
- The parameter \(\nu = \sqrt{8\alpha_s/(9\pi)}\) appears in the differential equation for the gap.
- The global minimum gap is \(\Delta_0^{(0)} \simeq \Lambda e \exp\left[-\frac{3\pi^{3/2}}{2^{3/2}\sqrt{\alpha_s}}\right]\), with higher minima at \(\Delta_0^{(n)} \simeq \Lambda e \exp\left[-\frac{3\pi^{3/2}(1+2n)}{2^{3/2}\sqrt{\alpha_s}}\right]\) for \(n=0,1,2,\dots\).
- The vacuum expectation value of the diquark field at the \(n\)-th minimum is \(\phi^{(n)} = (-1)^n \frac{8\mu^2 \Delta_0^{(n)}}{\nu\pi^2} \sqrt{J_0^2(\nu) + J_1^2(\nu)}\).
- The second derivative of the potential at each minimum is \(d^2V/d\phi^2|_{\phi^{(n)}} = (\nu\pi/4\mu)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau approach to color superconductivity by deriving the effective potential directly from QCD, following the method of Gorkov for ordinary superconductivity, rather than postulating it from symmetry arguments as done by Pisarski and Rischke (1999) and Evans, Hsu, and Schwetz (1999). The analysis builds on Schwinger-Dyson equation studies of color superconductivity by Hong, Miransky, Shovkovy, and Wijewardhana (1999) and by Schaefer and Wilczek (1999). The universality class is compared to that of quenched QED\(_4\) in a constant magnetic field studied by Gusynin, Miransky, and Shovkovy (1995) and by Leung, Ng, and Ackley (1996), with the key difference being the explicit Lorentz symmetry breaking by the chemical potential in dense QCD. The predicted infinite tower of resonances with masses of order the fermion gap could serve as a signature of long-range forces in heavy ion collision experiments.
