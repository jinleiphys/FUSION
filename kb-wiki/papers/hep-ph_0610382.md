# Evolution of Critical Correlations at the QCD Phase Transition

> We investigate the evolution of the density-density correlations in the isoscalar critical condensate formed at the QCD critical point. The initial equilibrium state of the system is characterized by a fractal measure determining the distribution of isoscalar particles (sigmas) in configuration space. Non-equilibrium dynamics is induced through a sudden symmetry breaking leading gradually to the deformation of the initial fractal geometry. After constructing an ensemble of configurations describing the initial state of the isoscalar field we solve the equations of motion and show that remnants of the critical state and the associated fractal geometry survive for time scales larger than the time needed for the mass of the isoscalar particles to reach the two-pion threshold. This result is more transparent in an event-by-event analysis of the phenomenon. Thus, we conclude that the initial fractal properties can eventually be transferred to the observable pion-sector through the decay of the sigmas even in the case of a quench.

# Evolution of Critical Correlations at the QCD Phase Transition

## Key claim
The initial fractal geometry of the sigma-field condensate at the QCD critical point can survive for time scales longer than the time needed for the sigma mass to reach the two-pion threshold, provided the quench duration is sufficiently short (approximately 2.1 fm or less for lambda^2 = 20). This survival is most clearly visible in an event-by-event analysis, where a percentage of individual configurations reacquire the critical fractal dimension even after the system has been driven out of equilibrium.

## Method
The authors model the chiral order parameter (sigma field) and pion fields using a linear sigma-model Lagrangian with explicit symmetry breaking, discretized on a 25x25x25 lattice. An ensemble of 10^4 initial sigma-field configurations is generated with fractal mass dimension D_f = 5/2, corresponding to the 3D Ising universality class at the QCD critical point; the pion fields are initialized as an ideal thermal gas at T_0 = 140 MeV. Non-equilibrium dynamics is induced by a linear quench in the potential minimum v(t), and the equations of motion are solved numerically using a leap-frog scheme for various quench durations tau and coupling strengths lambda^2.

## Key numbers
- Fractal mass dimension of the initial critical sigma-field ensemble: D_f = 5/2 (theoretically expected for 3D Ising universality class with delta ≈ 5), reproduced numerically within less than 1% error.
- For quench duration tau = 1 fm and lambda^2 = 20, the relaxation time tau_rel (time after which the time-averaged percentage of configurations with critical slope drops below 0.5%) is approximately 40 fm, significantly larger than the freeze-out time in heavy-ion collisions.
- For tau = 2 fm and lambda^2 = 20, tau_rel decreases to approximately 1.6 fm.
- For tau = 3 fm and lambda^2 = 20, tau_rel = 0 (the time-averaged percentage is always below 0.5%).
- The ratio tau_rel / tau_th reaches approximately 10 for tau ≈ 1.6 fm and lambda^2 = 20, and decreases almost exponentially with increasing tau.
- The standard deviation of the fractal dimension d_f across individual configurations at t=0 is approximately 0.05.
- The initial thermal pion ensemble is generated at T_0 ≈ 140 MeV, with negligible effect when varied between 100 MeV and 180 MeV.
- Lattice sizes from 15x15x15 to 25x25x25 sites were tested; results converge for ensembles larger than 7x10^3 configurations.

## Context
This work extends the study of critical fluctuations at the QCD critical point, building on earlier results by Antoniou (2001) on the fractal dimension of the order parameter and by Hatta (2003) on sigma-field correlators. It addresses the dynamical question of whether critical correlations survive the rapid expansion (quench) expected in heavy-ion collisions, complementing studies of dissipative effects by Biro (1997) and Randrup (2000). The event-by-event analysis proposed here enables a direct experimental signature of the QCD critical point through intermittent fluctuations in transverse momentum space, as previously suggested by Antoniou (2001) and Bialas (1986).
