# Prospectives for A Fixed-Target ExpeRiment at the LHC: AFTER@LHC

> We argue that the concept of a multi-purpose fixed-target experiment with the proton or lead-ion LHC beams extracted by a bent crystal would offer a number of ground-breaking precision-physics opportunities. The multi-TeV LHC beams will allow for the most energetic fixed-target experiments ever performed. The fixed-target mode has the advantage of allowing for high luminosities, spin measurements with a polarised target, and access over the full backward rapidity domain --uncharted until now-- up to x_F ~ -1.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a fixed-target experiment using LHC beams extracted by a bent crystal can achieve luminosities competitive with LHC collider mode and enable access to the backward rapidity region (x_F up to -1), but this depends on the successful demonstration of bent-crystal extraction technology that does not degrade LHC performance, with crystal degradation of about 6% per 10^20 particles/cm^2 requiring the crystal to be moved less than a millimeter after one year of operation.

## Method

The authors propose extracting a small fraction of the LHC proton or lead-ion beam halo using a bent crystal, which deflects particles into a fixed-target station. The extracted beam intensity of 5 × 10^8 protons per second would impinge on various solid or liquid targets (hydrogen, deuterium, beryllium, copper, tungsten, lead) of 1-100 cm thickness. The fixed-target mode yields center-of-mass energies of √s_NN ≈ 115 GeV for proton beams and √s_NN ≈ 72 GeV for lead beams, with a rapidity shift of Δy ≈ 4.8 between the laboratory and center-of-mass frames.

## Key numbers

- Extracted beam intensity: 5 × 10^8 protons per second
- Crystal degradation: 6% per 10^20 particles/cm^2
- Center-of-mass energy for proton fixed-target: √s_NN ≈ 115 GeV (using 7 TeV proton beam)
- Center-of-mass energy for lead fixed-target: √s_NN ≈ 72 GeV (using 2.76 TeV per nucleon lead beam)
- Rapidity shift for proton beam: Δy ≈ 4.8; boost factor γ ≈ 60
- Rapidity shift for lead beam: Δy ≈ 4.3; boost factor γ ≈ 38
- Integrated luminosity for 100 cm liquid hydrogen target: 20 fb^-1 yr^-1
- Integrated luminosity for 1 cm lead target: 0.16 fb^-1 yr^-1
- Target heating power from beam: 50 μW for a typical 1 cm thick target
- Target irradiation damage threshold: 10^15 protons per cm^2 (approximately one month of beam)
- Maximum photon energy from ultra-peripheral collisions: E_γ ≈ 200 GeV

## Context

This proposal extends the concept of fixed-target experiments using bent-crystal extraction, which was first considered for LHC flavor physics in the early 1990s but rejected due to concerns about crystal radiation damage. The technique has since been demonstrated at SPS (Arduini 1997), Fermilab (Asseev 1997), and Protvino (Afonin 2012), and was used in the NA48 kaon experiment at CERN (Doble 1995). The project would complement existing LHC experiments (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb) and the proposed LHeC (Abelleira Fernandez 2012), enabling precision QCD measurements at energies between SPS and RHIC for heavy-ion collisions, and providing access to parton distributions at large x and x_F approaching -1 that are inaccessible to collider experiments.
