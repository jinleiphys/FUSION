# Domain Walls in QCD

> QCD was shown to have a nontrivial vacuum structure due to the topology of the theta = theta+2*pi*n parameter. As a result of this nontrivial topology, in the large N_c limit, quasi-stable QCD domain walls appear, characterized by a transition in the singlet eta' field. We discuss the physics of these QCD domain walls as well as related axion domain walls and we present a new type of axion wall which also contains an eta' transition. We argue that these domain walls are topologically stable in the limit N_c->infinity and classically stable for large but finite N_c, however, they can decay through a tunneling process. We argue that the qualitative features of these QCD domain walls -- namely their classical stability -- persist to the realistic case of N_c=3 and that it is at least possible that their lifetime could be macroscopically large. If it is, then QCD domain walls could play an important role in the evolution of early universe and may be detectable in energetic collisions such as those at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

The authors argue that QCD domain walls, which are topologically stable in the large \(N_c\) limit and classically stable for large but finite \(N_c\), can decay through a tunneling process; they claim that the qualitative features of classical stability persist for the realistic case \(N_c=3\) and that the lifetime could be macroscopically large, but this is presented as a possibility rather than a certainty.

## Method

The authors construct an effective low-energy QCD Lagrangian that generalizes the Di Vecchia-Veneziano-Witten (VVW) Lagrangian to include terms subleading in \(1/N_c\) and a constraint from topological charge quantization. They analyze the vacuum structure of this effective potential, find classical domain wall solutions involving the singlet \(\eta'\) field, and estimate the lifetime of these walls by considering tunneling through gluon degrees of freedom that are integrated out in the effective theory.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 133\) MeV.
* The quark condensate is \(\langle \bar{\Psi}^{i} \Psi^{i} \rangle \simeq -(240\; {\rm MeV})^3\).
* The gluon condensate is \(\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} G^2 \rangle \simeq 1.2\times 10^{-2}{\rm GeV}^4\).
* In the chiral limit, the \(\eta'\) mass scales as \(m_{\eta'} \sim N_c^{-1/2}\) in the large \(N_c\) limit.
* The \(\eta'\) mass relation \(f_{\pi}^2 m_{\eta'}^2 = \frac{4N_f}{N_c^2}E + \frac{4}{N_f}\sum_i m_i |\langle 0|\bar{\Psi}^i \Psi^i|0\rangle|\) agrees with phenomenology at the 20% level.

## Context

This work extends the study of domain walls from supersymmetric gauge theories (Shifman 1997) and D-brane constructions (Witten 1998) to non-supersymmetric QCD, motivated by the observation that the number of vacuum states in both approaches is \(N_c\). The authors build on the effective Lagrangian approach of Halperin (1998) and the VVW Lagrangian (Witten 1980, Di Vecchia 1980) to analyze QCD domain walls and their possible decay. The results enable future study of primordial magnetic field generation from short-lived QCD domain walls after the QCD phase transition in the early universe, and suggest possible detection at RHIC.
