# The x-Dependent Helicity Distributions for Valence Quarks in Nucleons

> We derive simple relations between the polarized and unpolarized valence quark distributions in a light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator model for nucleons. The explicit x-dependent Wigner rotation effect for the light-flavor quarks is calculated. It is shown that the mass difference between the scalar and vector spectators could reproduce the up and down valence quark asymmetry that accounts for the observed ratio $F_2^{n}/F_2^{p}$. The proton, neutron, and deuteron polarization asymmetries, $A_1^p$, $A_1^n$, and $A_1^d$, are in agreement with the available data by taking into account the Wigner rotation effect. The calculated $x$-dependent helicity distributions of the up and down valence quarks are compared with the recent results from semi-inclusive hadron asymmetries in polarized deep inelastic scattering by the Spin Muon Collaboration.

# The x-Dependent Helicity Distributions for Valence Quarks in Nucleons

## Key claim

The paper derives simple relations between polarized and unpolarized valence quark distributions within a light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator model, showing that the mass difference between scalar and vector spectators can reproduce the up/down valence quark asymmetry that accounts for the observed ratio F2^n/F2^p, but only when the Wigner rotation effect is included. The model reproduces polarization asymmetries A1^p, A1^n, and A1^d for x >= 0.1, but the author notes that valence-only contributions cannot fit the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule data without introducing additional asymmetry between the Wigner rotation factors for scalar and vector spectators.

## Method

The author constructs a light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator model where the proton wave function is a superposition of quark-scalar-diquark and quark-vector-diquark Fock states, with the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage prescription for the light-cone momentum space wave function. The unpolarized quark distributions u_v(x) and d_v(x) are expressed in terms of amplitudes a_S(x) and a_V(x) for scattering off a quark with a scalar or vector spectator diquark, with the mass difference between scalar (m_S=600 MeV) and vector (m_V=900 MeV) spectators breaking SU(6) symmetry. The polarized distributions Delta u_v(x) and Delta d_v(x) are then obtained by applying a Wigner rotation factor W_D(x) to the spin-dependent probabilities from the wave function, yielding simple relations connecting polarized and unpolarized distributions.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass m_q = 330 MeV and harmonic oscillator scale parameter beta_D = 330 MeV adopted for the light-cone wave function
- Scalar spectator mass m_S = 600 MeV and vector spectator mass m_V = 900 MeV, chosen to explain the N-Delta mass difference
- Predicted ratio F2^n(x)/F2^p(x) = 1/4 for x -> 1 from the quark model (compared to perturbative QCD expectation of 3/7)
- To reproduce the observed Ellis-Jaffe sums Gamma_1^p = 0.136 and Gamma_1^n = -0.03, the model requires average Wigner rotation factors <W_S> = 0.56 and <W_V> = 0.92
- Quark model prediction for the d quark: Delta d(x)/d(x) -> -1/3 near x -> 1 (compared to perturbative QCD prediction of Delta d(x)/d(x) -> 1)

## Context

This work extends the light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator approach previously developed by Ma (1993) and others (Brodsky, 1994; Weber, 1992) to calculate explicit x-dependent helicity distributions for valence quarks. The model addresses the violation of the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule observed by the European Muon Collaboration and Spin Muon Collaboration at CERN and experiments E142/E143 at SLAC, and provides predictions that can be tested against semi-inclusive hadron asymmetry measurements from the Spin Muon Collaboration. The results enable comparison between conventional quark model predictions and perturbative QCD expectations for the x -> 1 and x -> 0 endpoint behaviors of F2^n/F2^p, d(x)/u(x), and Delta d(x)/d(x), with the author noting that improved precision in these endpoint regions is needed to distinguish between the two frameworks.
