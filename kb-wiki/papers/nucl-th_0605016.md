# Tensor electric polarizability of the deuteron in storage-ring experiments

> The tensor electric polarizability of the deuteron gives important information about spin-dependent nuclear forces. If a resonant horizontal electric field acts on a deuteron beam circulating into a storage ring, the tensor electric polarizability stimulates the buildup of the vertical polarization of the deuteron (the Baryshevsky effect). General formulas describing this effect have been derived. Calculated formulas agree with the earlier obtained results. The problem of the influence of tensor electric polarizability on spin dynamics in such a deuteron electric-dipole-moment experiment in storage rings has been investigated. Doubling the resonant frequency used in this experiment dramatically amplifies the Baryshevsky effect and provides the opportunity to make high-precision measurements of the deuteron's tensor electric polarizability.

## Key claim

The tensor electric polarizability of the deuteron can be measured via the buildup of vertical polarization (Baryshevsky effect) in a storage ring, and doubling the resonant frequency from the usual g-2 frequency to twice that value dramatically amplifies this effect while making the electric dipole moment (EDM) effect nonresonant, thereby enabling high-precision measurements.

## Method

The author derived general relativistic formulas for the correction to the Hamilton operator due to tensor electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the deuteron in a storage ring, using the method of spin amplitudes with three-component spinors and 3×3 matrices. The spin dynamics were solved analytically for two resonant conditions: when the driving frequency ω equals the spin precession frequency ω₀, and when ω equals 2ω₀, with the latter case analyzed in detail for its experimental advantages. The calculation considered a constant vertical magnetic field and an oscillating longitudinal electric field that induces coherent longitudinal oscillations of the beam particles.

## Key numbers

- The planned Lorentz factor for the deuteron EDM experiment is γ = 1.28.
- The angular frequency of spin rotation (g-2 frequency) is given by ω₀ = -(e a / m) B_z, where a = (g-2)/2 for the deuteron.
- The amplitude of the oscillating electric field is E₀ = (γ₀³ m ω / e) Δβ₀, where Δβ₀ is the amplitude of velocity modulation.
- For resonance at ω ≈ ω₀, the vertical polarization growth rate is dP_z/dt = (2/3) a₂ [P_{ρφ}(0) cos(2φ) - (P_{ρρ}(0) - P_{φφ}(0))/2 sin(2φ)], where a₂ is proportional to α_T (Δβ₀)².
- For resonance at ω ≈ 2ω₀, the vertical polarization growth rate is dP_z/dt = (2/3) a₁ [P_{ρφ}(0) cos(φ) - (P_{ρρ}(0) - P_{φφ}(0))/2 sin(φ)], where a₁ is proportional to α_T Δβ₀ (linear in the modulation amplitude).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Baryshevsky et al. (Bar1, Bar3, Bar4), who first proposed using the tensor electric polarizability to induce vertical polarization buildup in storage rings, by providing exact relativistic formulas rather than first-order-in-β approximations. The results are directly applicable to the planned deuteron EDM experiment in storage rings described by YOr, YS, and OMS, where the Baryshevsky effect must be distinguished from a true EDM signal. The key advance is the identification that operating at twice the resonant frequency (2ω₀ instead of ω₀) amplifies the tensor polarizability effect while suppressing the EDM contribution, enabling separate high-precision measurements of the deuteron's tensor electric polarizability.
