# Electromagnetic Properties of the Baryon Decuplet in Quenched and Partially Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We calculate electromagnetic properties of the decuplet baryons in quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory. We work at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, leading order in the heavy baryon expansion, and obtain expressions for the magnetic moments, charge radii, and electric quadrupole moments. The quenched calculation is shown to be pathological since only quenched chiral singularities are present at this order. We present the partially quenched results for both the SU(2) and SU(3) flavor groups and use the isospin limit in the latter. These results are necessary for proper extrapolation of lattice calculations of decuplet electromagnetic properties.

## Key claim

The paper calculates electromagnetic properties of decuplet baryons in quenched and partially quenched chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, finding that the quenched calculation is pathological because only quenched chiral singularities appear at this order and the charge radii have no quark mass dependence at all, whereas the partially quenched results contain the same low-energy constants as full QCD and are therefore suitable for extrapolating lattice data.

## Method

The authors work in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at leading order in the heavy baryon expansion and next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, using the graded symmetry group SU(6|3) for partially quenched QCD. They compute one-loop diagrams contributing to the magnetic moments, charge radii, and electric quadrupole moments of the decuplet baryons, with the loop meson sums including valence, sea, and ghost quark contributions. For the quenched case, they additionally evaluate hairpin diagram contributions involving the singlet meson propagator.

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of decuplet electromagnetic properties by Butler (1994) and Banerjee (1996) to the quenched and partially quenched frameworks needed for lattice QCD extrapolation. It builds on the partially quenched chiral perturbation theory formalism developed by Sharpe (1997, 2000, 2001) and Golterman (1998), and on the octet baryon calculations by Chen (2001) and Beane (2002). The results enable proper extrapolation of upcoming partially quenched lattice calculations of decuplet electromagnetic form factors to physical quark masses, and provide the first calculation of decuplet charge radii in chiral perturbation theory.
