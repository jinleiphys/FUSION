# Dynamics of quarks and gauge fields in the lowest-energy states in QCD and QED

> We examine the dynamics of quarks and gauge fields in the lowest energy states in the QED and QCD interactions by combining Schwinger's longitudinal confinement in (1+1)D with Polyakov's transverse confinement in (2+1)D in a ``stretch (2+1)D'' flux tube model in (3+1)D. For such QED and QED systems in the flux tube configuration with cylindrical symmetry, we separate out the transverse and longitudinal degrees of freedom, approximate the non-Abelian QCD in the quasi-Abelian approximation, and solve the derived equations to study the collective excitations. We find stable collective QED and QCD excitations showing up as confined QED and QCD mesons, in support of previous theoretical studies and recent observations of the anomalous hypothetical X17 and E38 particles. Future theoretical lattice gauge calculations of QED in (3+1)D with the inclusion of the Schwinger longitudinal confinement mechanism and experimental confirmation of the hypothetical X17 and E38 particles will shed definitive light on quark confinement in the QED interaction in (3+1)D.

## Key claim

By combining Schwinger's longitudinal confinement in (1+1)D with Polyakov's transverse confinement in (2+1)D within a "stretch (2+1)D" flux tube model in (3+1)D, the authors find stable collective QED and QCD excitations that appear as confined mesons, supporting the interpretation that the hypothetical X17 and E38 particles may be QED-confined quark-antiquark states, but only if the Schwinger longitudinal confinement mechanism, omitted in prior static lattice gauge calculations, is included.

## Method

The authors construct a "stretch (2+1)D" flux tube model with cylindrical symmetry, separating transverse and longitudinal degrees of freedom of the Dirac equation. They treat the non-Abelian QCD interaction in the quasi-Abelian approximation, apply Polyakov's result for transverse QED confinement in (2+1)D, and solve the resulting equations to study collective excitations of the quark and gauge field system. The Maxwell equations are then used with the quark currents to obtain bound meson masses as functions of the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling constant for confinement in compact QED in (3+1)D from lattice gauge calculations is $\beta_c = 1/e_c^2 = 1.0111331(21)$, or $e_c^2 = 0.988989481$, while the physical QED coupling $e_{\rm QED}^2 = 1/137 \ll e_c^2$, placing QED in the weak-coupling deconfined regime according to static lattice calculations.
- The hypothetical X17 particle mass is reported as $16.70 \pm 0.35(\text{stat}) \pm 0.5(\text{syst})$ MeV from ATOMKI measurements, and $16.7 \pm 0.4$ MeV from the HUS Collaboration.
- The hypothetical E38 particle appears as a resonance structure at approximately 38 MeV in diphoton invariant mass spectra from Dubna experiments.
- The isoscalar QED meson mass was predicted to be 12.8 MeV and the isovector QED meson mass 38.6 MeV in the limit of massless quarks (Table I of Wong 2010).
- The anomalous soft photon excess occurs for $e^+e^-$ pairs with $p_T \lesssim 60$ MeV/c, exceeding Standard Model QED bremsstrahlung predictions by a factor of about 4.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological open-string QCD and QED meson model developed by Wong and collaborators (2010–2022), which successfully described $\pi^0$, $\eta$, and $\eta'$ in the QCD sector and predicted QED meson masses near 17 and 38 MeV. It directly addresses the contradiction between these phenomenological successes and the well-established lattice gauge theory results from Wilson (1974), Polyakov (1977, 1987), and others showing that quarks are deconfined in compact QED in (3+1)D. The authors argue that future lattice gauge calculations including the Schwinger longitudinal confinement mechanism, and experimental confirmation of the X17 and E38 particles, will definitively resolve whether quarks can be confined by QED interactions alone in (3+1)D.
