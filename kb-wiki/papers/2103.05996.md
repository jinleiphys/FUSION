# Application of an ab-initio-inspired energy density functional to nuclei: impact of the effective mass and the slope of the symmetry energy on bulk and surface properties

> The YGLO (Yang-Grasso-Lacroix-Orsay) functional is applied for the first time to investigate ground-state properties of different isotopic chains, from Oxygen to Lead. Mean-field Hartree-Fock calculations are carried out to analyze global trends for separation energies, binding energies, radii, neutron skins, and density profiles. We have three objectives: i) we study whether this functional leads to a reasonable description of ground-state properties (despite the fact that it was not adjusted on nuclei) and we discuss the associated limitations; ii) we investigate whether the correct description of the low-density nuclear gas, which is the peculiarity of this functional, has any relevant impact on predictions for nuclei; iii) we connect nuclear energies, radii and density profiles with properties of the corresponding equations of state of infinite matter. In particular, we identify a link existing between the isoscalar effective mass and spatial properties in neutron-deficient nuclei, namely proton radii and tails of proton densities. On the other side, we show that the slope of the symmetry energy is connected with spatial properties in neutron-rich nuclei: the slope computed at saturation density is related to neutron skin thicknesses, as already well known, whereas the slope calculated at lower densities is linked to the tails of neutron densities. The YGLO effective mass turns out to be quite low. Directions to improve this aspect are explored and suggested at the end of the manuscript.

## Key claim

The YGLO functional, which was not adjusted on nuclei but incorporates a correct description of the low-density nuclear gas, provides a reasonable description of ground-state properties for isotopic chains from Oxygen to Lead, although with systematic deviations in binding energies that increase for heavier and more neutron-rich systems, and with a notably low effective mass that requires improvement.

## Method

Mean-field Hartree-Fock calculations in spherical symmetry and coordinate space were performed for even-even nuclei across Oxygen, Calcium, Nickel, Zirconium, Tin, and Lead isotopic chains, using two versions of the YGLO functional (YGLO(FP) and YGLO(Akmal)) benchmarked on different neutron-matter equations of state. The functional parameters were first adjusted on neutron drops trapped in a harmonic potential by fitting to the average of several ab-initio results, then applied to finite nuclei without further tuning. Pairing correlations were neglected, and results were compared to the Skyrme interaction SLy5 and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The YGLO isoscalar effective mass at saturation density (0.168 fm^-3) is 0.445 for YGLO(FP) (original), 0.468 for YGLO(FP) (this work), and 0.497 for YGLO(Akmal); the neutron effective mass is 0.476, 0.500, and 0.554 respectively.
- The two-neutron drip line is predicted at 60Ca and 122Zr for all YGLO versions and SLy5; for Tin, YGLO functionals predict 162Sn while SLy5 predicts 176Sn.
- Root mean square deviations of binding energies from experiment for Oxygen, Calcium, Nickel, and Zirconium isotopes are smaller for YGLO(Akmal) than for YGLO(FP), with closed-shell deviations (sigma_cs) smaller than all-nuclei deviations (sigma_all) in both cases.
- The neutron-neutron s-wave scattering length used in the functional is a0 = -18.9 fm; the average spin-singlet s-wave scattering length for symmetric matter is a1 = -20.0 fm.

## Context

This work extends the YGLO functional, previously applied only to infinite nuclear matter (Yang et al., 2016) and neutron drops (Bonnard et al., 2018), to finite nuclei for the first time. It connects nuclear properties such as proton radii and neutron skin thicknesses to equation-of-state parameters (isoscalar effective mass and slope of the symmetry energy at different densities), and identifies that the low effective mass leads to overly pronounced shell closures compared to standard Skyrme functionals like SLy5 (Chabanat et al., 1998). The results enable future improvements by suggesting directions to increase the effective mass, and provide a baseline for comparing ab-initio-inspired functionals against phenomenological ones across the nuclear chart.
