# Correlation studies of fission fragment neutron multiplicities

> We calculate neutron multiplicities from fission fragments with specified mass numbers for events having a specified total fragment kinetic energy. The shape evolution from the initial compound nucleus to the scission configurations is obtained with the Metropolis walk method on the five-dimensional potential-energy landscape, calculated with the macroscopic-microscopic method for the three-quadratic-surface shape family. Shape-dependent microscopic level densities are used to guide the random walk, to partition the intrinsic excitation energy between the two proto-fragments at scission, and to determine the spectrum of the neutrons evaporated from the fragments. The contributions to the total excitation energy of the resulting fragments from statistical excitation and shape distortion at scission is studied. Good agreement is obtained with available experimental data on neutron multiplicities in correlation with fission fragments from $^{235}$U(n$_{\rm th}$,f). At higher neutron energies a superlong fission mode appears which affects the dependence of the observables on the total fragment kinetic energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fission model combining microscopic level densities with a five-dimensional potential-energy surface can reproduce measured neutron multiplicities gated on total fragment kinetic energy (TKE) for 235U(nth,f), but only for asymmetric fission events (AL ≤ 104 and AH ≥ 132) because the model overestimates TKE in the symmetric region due to insufficient elongation of calculated scission configurations.

## Method

The authors performed Metropolis random walks on a five-dimensional potential-energy landscape calculated with the macroscopic-microscopic method for the three-quadratic-surface shape family, using shape-dependent microscopic level densities computed by the combinatorial method. For each of 10^6 fission events per reaction case, they recorded the mass numbers of the two primary fission fragments, their total kinetic energy, and the number of neutrons evaporated from each fragment, using microcanonical partitioning of intrinsic excitation energy at scission and microscopic level densities for neutron evaporation spectra.

## Key numbers

- 10^6 fission events were generated for each reaction case considered
- The neck radius at which asymmetry is frozen in is c0 = 2.5 fm, and scission occurs at csc = 1.5 fm
- For the mass division AL:AH = 104:132 at En = 0, scission shapes span a quadrupole moment range of 8 < q2 < 18, with TKE values varying by about 40 MeV between most compact and most elongated shapes
- For fragment 160Sm, a deformation change from ε = 0.25 to ε = 0.0 yields a distortion energy of 11 MeV, while the same deformation change for 94Kr yields only 3 MeV
- At Esc* = 10 MeV for the 104:132 division, about 6 MeV goes to the light fragment 104Zr and about 4 MeV to the heavy fragment 132Te
- For the 88:148 division at Esc* = 5 MeV, the heavy fragment acquires all the energy with approximately 14% probability

## Context

This work extends the Brownian shape evolution method developed by Randrup et al. (2011, 2012, 2013) and the microscopic level density approach of Uhrenholt et al. (2013), which were previously applied to fission-fragment mass distributions by Ward et al. (2017) and to neutron multiplicities by Albertsson et al. (2020). The study enables comparison with recent correlation measurements by Gook et al. (2018) of nubar(A;TKE) for 235U(nth,f), and complements a phenomenological deterministic model applied to the same problem by Tudora (2019). At higher neutron energies (En = 5.55 MeV), a superlong fission mode appears that affects the dependence of observables on TKE.
