# Scattering and gluon emission of physical quarks in a SU(3) colored field

> We study the scattering of the gluon-dressed physical quarks, defined as the eigenstates of the vacuum QCD Hamiltonian, off a colored medium. We solve the wavefunction of the physical quark state by diagonalizing the QCD Hamiltonian in vacuum in a $\ket{q}+\ket{qg}$ Fock space, with implementing the sector-dependent mass renormalization scheme. We then perform numerical simulations of the real-time quantum state evolution of the initially dressed quark state at various medium densities. The results are compared with those of an initially bare or off-shell quark states. With the obtained light-front wavefunction of the evolved state, we extract the quark jet transverse momentum broadening, the quenching parameter, the cross section, the gluon emission rate, and the evolution of the invariant mass. The scenario considered is relevant for high energy scattering processes, where the quark originates from far outside the color field describing the scattering target. This investigation on dressed quarks complements our earlier studies of the single quark scattering in the $\ket{q}$ Fock space, and of the bare quark scattering in the $\ket{q}+\ket{qg}$ Fock space, providing a novel systematic description of quark scattering process using a non-perturbative formalism.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using a "dressed" quark (an eigenstate of the vacuum QCD Hamiltonian in the |q⟩+|qg⟩ Fock space) as the initial state for in-medium scattering yields different jet evolution compared to using a bare or off-shell quark, but the authors hedge that this difference depends on the specific observable and medium density, and the dressed quark formalism provides a more physically motivated initial condition for quarks originating far outside the medium.

## Method

The authors solve for the light-front wavefunction of the physical (dressed) quark by diagonalizing the QCD Hamiltonian in the |q⟩+|qg⟩ Fock space using a sector-dependent mass renormalization scheme, exploiting symmetries (longitudinal boost, transverse Galilean boost, color rotation, spin rotation, and discrete rotational invariance) to reduce the matrix dimension. They then perform real-time numerical simulations of the quantum state evolution of this dressed quark as it traverses a SU(3) colored background field using the time-dependent Basis Light-Front Quantization (tBLFQ) approach, extracting observables such as transverse momentum broadening, the quenching parameter, cross sections, gluon emission rates, and invariant mass evolution.

## Key numbers

- The dimension of the Hamiltonian matrix to be diagonalized is reduced from dim_tot = N_c × 2 × dim_⊥ + floor(K) × N_c(N_c²-1) × 4 × dim_⊥² to dim B_q = 1 + floor(K) × dim_⊥, where N_c=3, K is the total longitudinal momentum quantum number, and dim_⊥ = (2N_⊥)² with N_⊥ the number of transverse lattice sites per dimension.
- The mass counterterm δm is determined numerically by requiring the ground state eigenvalue of the light-cone Hamiltonian H_LC to satisfy H_LC(δm)|φ₀⟩ = m_q²|φ₀⟩, where m_q is the physical quark mass.
- The coupling coefficient in the gluon emission vertex is C_Γ = g √((N_c²-1)/(2N_c)) × 1/(2L_⊥√(2πK)), where g is the QCD coupling, N_c=3, L_⊥ is the transverse box size, and K is the total longitudinal momentum quantum number.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies of single quark scattering in the |q⟩ Fock space (Li 2023) and bare quark scattering in the |q⟩+|qg⟩ Fock space (Li 2021, Li 2023), providing a non-perturbative formalism for dressed quark scattering that complements pQCD-based approaches such as the opacity expansion (Gyulassy 1999, Gyulassy 2000, Wiedemann 2000) and the BDMPS formalism (Baier 1996, Baier 1997, Zakharov 1997). The results enable systematic studies of jet quenching where the quark originates far outside the medium, relevant for high-energy collisions at RHIC, LHC, and the future EIC, and can be extended to include higher Fock sectors or more realistic medium profiles.
