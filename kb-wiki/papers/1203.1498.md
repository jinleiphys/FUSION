# Model of the bremsstrahlung emission accompanying interactions between protons and nuclei from low up to intermediate energies: role of magnetic emission

> A new model of the bremsstrahlung emission which accompanies proton decay and collisions of protons off nuclei in the energy region from the lowest up to intermediate, has been developed. This model includes spin formalism, potential approach for description of interaction between protons and nuclei, and operator of emission includes component of the magnetic emission (defined on the basis of Pauli equation). In the problem of the bremsstrahlung during the proton decay in the first time a role of the magnetic emission is studied using such a model. For the studied $^{146}{\rm Tm}$ nucleus it has been studied the following: (1) How much does the magnetic emission change the full bremsstrahlung spectrum? (2) At which angle is the magnetic emission the most intensive relatively electric one? (3) Is there some space region where the magnetic emission increases strongly relatively electric one? (4) How intensive is the magnetic emission in the tunneling region? (5) Which values has the probability at its maximum and at zero energy limit of the emitted photons? It is demonstrated that the model is able to describe enough well experimental data of the bremsstrahlung emission which accompanies collisions of protons off the $^{9}{\rm Be}$, $^{12}{\rm C}$ and $^{208}{\rm Pb}$ nuclei at the incident energy $T_{\rm lab}=140$ MeV (at the photon energy up to 120 MeV).

## Key claim
The paper develops a new model of bremsstrahlung emission accompanying proton decay and proton-nucleus collisions that includes, for the first time in the proton-decay problem, a magnetic emission component derived from the Pauli equation; the model can describe experimental data for proton-nucleus collisions at incident energies up to 140 MeV, but the magnetic emission contribution is studied only for the specific case of ¹⁴⁶Tm decay.

## Method
The model starts from the Pauli equation for the A+1 nucleon system, passes to the center-of-mass frame, and separates the Hamiltonian into a free part and an emission operator that contains both electric and magnetic terms. The magnetic emission operator is constructed from the spin-dependent term of the Pauli equation, and the wave function of the proton-nucleus system is built as a bilinear combination of orbital and spinor eigenfunctions using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. Matrix elements for photon emission are then computed by expanding the vector potential in multipoles and summing over spinor states.

## Key numbers
- The model is tested against experimental bremsstrahlung data for proton collisions on ⁹Be, ¹²C, and ²⁰⁸Pb at incident energy T_lab = 140 MeV (photon energy up to 120 MeV).
- The abstract also mentions data for ⁹C, ⁶⁴Cu, and ¹⁰⁷Ag at T_lab = 72 MeV (photon energy up to 60 MeV), though these are not discussed in the truncated text.
- The high-energy limit for validity of the Pauli-equation approach is given as ε_p ≪ 2m_p ≈ 1.86 GeV, which is "essentially higher" than the intermediate energies studied.

## Context
This work extends earlier potential-based models of bremsstrahlung in proton decay (Maydanyuk 2011) that did not include magnetic emission, and contrasts with relativistic two-nucleon approaches (Nakayama 1989, Herrmann 1991, Liou 1987-2011) that use momentum-space Dirac formalism. The model enables future study of how magnetic emission affects the full bremsstrahlung spectrum, its angular dependence, and its behavior in the tunneling region, particularly for proton decay of ¹⁴⁶Tm.
