# Benchmarking ANN extrapolations of the ground-state energies and radii of Li isotopes

> We present a comparison of model-space extrapolation methods for No-Core Shell Model calculations of ground-state energies and root-mean-square radii in Li isotopes. In particular, we benchmark the latest machine learning tools against widely used exponential and infrared extrapolations for energies and crossing point estimates for radii. Our findings demonstrate that machine learning-based approaches provide reliable predictions with robust statistical uncertainties for both observables even in small model spaces. These predictions are compatible with established exponential and IR extrapolations of energies and mark a notable improvement over conventional radius estimates.

## Key claim

Machine learning-based extrapolations (specifically the ISU and TUDa Artificial Neural Networks) provide reliable predictions for ground-state energies and point-proton radii of Li isotopes from No-Core Shell Model calculations, with the TUDa ANNs showing remarkably robust results even in small model spaces, though the ISU ANNs produce rather large uncertainties in small model spaces and sometimes large jumps in predicted values or uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations for 6Li, 7Li, and 8Li using the Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon potential over a range of harmonic oscillator frequencies from 10 to 30 MeV in steps of 2.5 MeV, with basis truncation parameters Nmax from 2 up to 18 for 6Li, 16 for 7Li, and 14 for 8Li. Two distinct Artificial Neural Network (ANN) approaches were benchmarked: the ISU ANNs use feed-forward networks with one hidden layer of eight nodes trained on NCSM data at multiple hbarOmega and Nmax values to predict observables at large Nmax, while the TUDa ANNs use a five-layer network (12,48,48,24,1 nodes) trained on fully converged A<=4 systems to directly predict converged values from sequences of four NCSM data points at consecutive Nmax for three hbarOmega values. These were compared against empirical exponential extrapolations, infrared (IR) extrapolations, and crossing point estimates for radii.

## Key numbers

- For 6Li ground-state energy (experiment: -31.993987 +/- 0.000002 MeV): TUDa ANNs with selected hbarOmega=10-20 MeV predict -32.011_{-0.014}^{+0.006} MeV at Nmax=18; ISU ANNs predict -32.036 +/- 0.020 MeV at Nmax=18; exponential extrapolation gives -31.977 MeV (variational minimum at Nmax=18)
- For 6Li point-proton rms radius (experiment: 2.465 +/- 0.041 fm): TUDa ANNs with selected hbarOmega=15-25 MeV predict 2.496_{-0.023}^{+0.013} fm at Nmax=18; ISU ANNs predict 2.546 +/- 0.011 fm at Nmax=18
- For 7Li ground-state energy (experiment: -39.245081 +/- 0.000004 MeV): TUDa ANNs with selected hbarOmega=10-20 MeV predict -39.419_{-0.018}^{+0.009} MeV at Nmax=16; ISU ANNs predict -39.494 +/- 0.059 MeV at Nmax=16
- For 7Li point-proton rms radius (experiment: 2.321 +/- 0.044 fm): TUDa ANNs with selected hbarOmega=15-25 MeV predict 2.340_{-0.009}^{+0.011} fm at Nmax=16; ISU ANNs predict 2.330 +/- 0.012 fm at Nmax=16
- For 8Li ground-state energy (experiment: -41.277699 +/- 0.000047 MeV): TUDa ANNs with selected hbarOmega=10-20 MeV predict -41.403_{-0.051}^{+0.023} MeV at Nmax=14; ISU ANNs predict -41.570 +/- 0.065 MeV at Nmax=14
- For 8Li point-proton rms radius (experiment: 2.219 +/- 0.046 fm): TUDa ANNs with selected hbarOmega=15-25 MeV predict 2.250_{-0.007}^{+0.010} fm at Nmax=14; ISU ANNs predict 2.226 +/- 0.002 fm at Nmax=14
- The standard IR extrapolation for 6Li ground-state energy gives -31.888 MeV at Nmax=18, which violates variational boundaries (variational minimum at Nmax=18 is -31.977 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the pioneering ANN extrapolation work of Negoita et al. (2018) from Iowa State University and the TUDa ANN approach of Knoll and Wolfgruber (2023) by providing a detailed benchmark against established exponential extrapolations (Maris, 2008) and infrared extrapolations (Furnstahl, 2012; Wendt, 2015) for Li isotopes. The results enable application of machine learning extrapolation methods across the p-shell for any realistic Hamiltonian, with the TUDa ANNs being computationally efficient as they require training only once, while the ISU ANNs have been demonstrated for magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments (McCarty, 2024) and the TUDa framework has recently been extended to electric quadrupole moments (Knoll, 2025).
