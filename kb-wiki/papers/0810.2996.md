# Nuclear Structure of the even-even Argon isotopes with a focus on magnetic moments

> We study the role of configuration mixing in the heavier even-even isotopes of Argon. We begin by limiting the configurations of the even-even Ar isotopes to $(d_{3/2}^2)_π$ $(f_{7/2}^n)_ν$. There, due to the particular location in this shell model space of $^{40}$Ar and $^{44}$Ar, we find that the spectra, B(E2)'s and magnetic moments of these two nuclei are identical. Any deviation from this equality is direct evidence of configuration mixing. In a larger shell model space there are significant differences between these two nuclei, with $^{44}$Ar being more collective. We also consider other even-even isotopes of Argon and study how their nuclear structure effects evolve with N. We compare in the full 0$\hbar ω$ space $(sd)_π$ $(fp)_ν$ the results of calculations with the WBT interaction and with the newer SDPF, denoted SDPF-U, interaction.

# Nuclear Structure of the even-even Argon isotopes with a focus on magnetic moments

## Key claim
In a restricted shell model space with only $(d_{3/2}^2)_\pi$ $(f_{7/2}^n)_\nu$ configurations, the spectra, B(E2) values, and magnetic moments of $^{40}$Ar and $^{44}$Ar are identical; any experimental or large-space deviation from this equality constitutes direct evidence of configuration mixing.

## Method
The authors performed shell model calculations for the even-even Argon isotopes $^{38,40,42,44,46}$Ar in two model spaces: a small space with protons restricted to the $d_{3/2}$ orbital and neutrons to the $f_{7/2}$ orbital, and a full $0\hbar\omega$ space with protons in the $sd$ shell and neutrons in the $fp$ shell. Calculations used the WBT interaction with the OXBASH code and the SDPF-U interaction with the ANTOINE code, employing effective charges $e_p=1.5$ and $e_n=0.5$ and free nucleon g factors.

## Key numbers
- In the small space, $^{40}$Ar and $^{44}$Ar have identical g($2^+_1$) = -0.441
- Experimental g($2^+_1$) for $^{40}$Ar is -0.02(2); for $^{44}$Ar no experimental value is reported
- In the large space with WBT, g($2^+_1$) for $^{40}$Ar is -0.197 and for $^{44}$Ar is -0.022
- For $^{46}$Ar, the large space g($2^+_1$) differs dramatically between interactions: +0.100 (WBT) versus +0.513 (SDPF-U)
- Experimental B(E2; $0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1$) for $^{46}$Ar is 196(39) $e^2fm^4$, while large space calculations give 541 $e^2fm^4$ (WBT) and 525 $e^2fm^4$ (SDPF-U)
- The $2^+_1$ excitation energy of $^{46}$Ar is 1.550(10) MeV experimentally; large space WBT gives 1.143 MeV and SDPF-U gives 1.592 MeV
- In the $2^+_1$ wavefunction of $^{46}$Ar, the $d_{3/2}^3$ $s_{1/2}$ proton configuration has 21.77% occupancy with SDPF-U versus 2.51% with WBT
- The N=28 shell gap (p3/2-f7/2 neutron single particle energy difference) calculated with SDPF-U is 4.74 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 4.80 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous experimental and theoretical studies of magnetic dipole g factors in $^{36,38,40}$Ar and sulfur isotopes by Speidel et al. (2008) and others. The authors identify a symmetry between $^{40}$Ar and $^{44}$Ar in a restricted space that provides a clean test of configuration mixing. The results highlight significant divergences between the older WBT interaction and the newer SDPF-U interaction for $^{46}$Ar, particularly in magnetic moments, motivating future experimental measurements of g factors in neutron-rich argon isotopes. The paper also connects to earlier work by Johnstone on $J=3/2^+$ - $J=1/2^+$ splittings in potassium isotopes and to self-consistent mean-field calculations by Werner et al. that better reproduce the B(E2) in $^{46}$Ar.
