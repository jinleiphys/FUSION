# Nuclear medium effects in structure functions of nucleon at moderate $Q^2$

> Recent experiments performed on inclusive electron scattering from nuclear targets have measured the nucleon electromagnetic structure functions $F_1(x,Q^2)$, $F_2(x,Q^2)$ and $F_L(x,Q^2)$ in $^{12}C$, $^{27}Al$, $^{56}Fe$ and $^{64}Cu$ nuclei. The measurements have been done in the energy region of $1 GeV^2 < W^2 < 4 GeV^2$ and $Q^2$ region of $0.5 GeV^2 < Q^2 < 4.5 GeV^2$. We have calculated nuclear medium effects in these structure functions arising due to the Fermi motion, binding energy, nucleon correlations, mesonic contributions from pion and rho mesons and shadowing effects. The calculations are performed in a local density approximation using relativistic nucleon spectral function which include nucleon correlations. The numerical results are compared with the recent experimental data from JLab and also with some earlier experiments.

## Key claim

The authors calculate nuclear medium effects in the nucleon electromagnetic structure functions \(F_1(x,Q^2)\), \(F_2(x,Q^2)\), and \(F_L(x,Q^2)\) for several nuclear targets at moderate \(Q^2\) (0.5–4.5 GeV\(^2\)) and \(W^2\) (1–4 GeV\(^2\)), but the abstract does not emphasize that the calculations are performed at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in QCD, and that the results explicitly avoid assuming the Bjorken limit by including target mass corrections (TMC).

## Method

The authors use a local density approximation with a relativistic nucleon spectral function derived from nuclear many-body theory to incorporate Fermi motion, binding energy, and nucleon correlations. They also include mesonic contributions from pion and rho meson clouds via a many-body field theoretical approach, and add shadowing effects following Petti (no year given). The nucleon parton distribution functions (PDFs) are taken from CTEQ6.6, and the NLO evolution of structure functions follows Vermaseren et al. (no year given) and van Neerven and Vogt (no year given); target mass corrections are applied following Schienbein et al. (2007).

## Key numbers

- The kinematic region covered is \(1~\mathrm{GeV}^2 < W^2 < 4~\mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(0.5~\mathrm{GeV}^2 < Q^2 < 4.5~\mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- Calculations are performed for nuclear targets \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\), \(^{27}\mathrm{Al}\), \(^{56}\mathrm{Fe}\), \(^{64}\mathrm{Cu}\), \(^{119}\mathrm{Sn}\), \(^{197}\mathrm{Au}\), and \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\).
- The change between NLO and NNLO results is found to be less than half a percent.
- The pion-nucleon form factor uses a cutoff \(\Lambda = 1~\mathrm{GeV}\) and coupling \(f = 1.01\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nuclear medium effects in electromagnetic and weak structure functions (Sajjad Athar et al., no year given) and in the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation (Haider et al., no year given). It directly compares with recent JLab data from Mamyan (2012) and earlier NMC and SLAC experiments, and is motivated by the need for theoretical understanding of nuclear modifications in the moderate-\(Q^2\) region relevant for future 12 GeV JLab measurements of quark-hadron duality in nuclei.
