# Coupling of pairing and triaxial shape vibrations in collective states of $γ$-soft nuclei

> In addition to shape oscillations, low-energy excitation spectra of deformed nuclei are also influenced by pairing vibrations. The simultaneous description of these collective modes and their coupling has been a long-standing problem in nuclear structure theory. Here we address the problem in terms of self-consistent mean-field calculations of collective deformation energy surfaces, and the framework of the interacting boson approximation. In addition to quadrupole shape vibrations and rotations, the explicit coupling to pairing vibrations is taken into account by a boson-number non-conserving Hamiltonian, specified by a choice of a universal density functional and pairing interaction. An illustrative calculation for $^{128}$Xe and $^{130}$Xe shows the importance of dynamical pairing degrees of freedom, especially for structures built on low-energy $0^+$ excited states, in $γ$-soft and triaxial nuclei.

# Coupling of pairing and triaxial shape vibrations in collective states of γ-soft nuclei

## Key claim
The coupling between pairing and triaxial shape vibrations is essential for describing low-energy excitation spectra of γ-soft nuclei, particularly for structures built on excited 0⁺ states; however, the effect is demonstrated only for two illustrative cases (¹²⁸Xe and ¹³⁰Xe) and the model space is restricted to collective monopole and quadrupole bosons, excluding two- or four-quasiparticle states that become relevant above approximately 3 MeV.

## Method
Constrained self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculations using the relativistic mean-field plus BCS (RMF+BCS) model with the PC-PK1 density functional and a separable pairing interaction were performed to generate three-dimensional potential energy surfaces in the (α, β, γ) deformation space, where α is the intrinsic pairing deformation parameter. These SCMF results were mapped onto an interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian with a boson-number non-conserving term, using a Hilbert space that is a direct sum of three subspaces comprising n₀−1, n₀, and n₀+1 bosons. The boson Hamiltonian parameters were determined by fitting the SCMF energy surfaces: the (β, γ) surface at fixed α to the diagonal matrix element of the boson-number conserving part, and the (α, β) surface at fixed γ to the lowest eigenvalue of the 3×3 energy matrix.

## Key numbers
- For ¹²⁸Xe, the SCMF-(β,γ) potential energy surface displays a shallow triaxial minimum at γ = 18°; for ¹³⁰Xe, the corresponding minimum is at γ = 0°.
- The SCMF-(α,β) surfaces show shallow minima at α = 10 for ¹²⁸Xe and α = 12 for ¹³⁰Xe, at the respective γ minima.
- The moment of inertia of the yrast band for both ¹²⁸Xe and ¹³⁰Xe required an adjustment approximately 40% larger than the Inglis-Belyaev value computed from SCMF single-nucleon quasiparticle states at the equilibrium minimum.
- For the γ-band, the calculated ratio E(2⁺_γ)/E(2⁺_g) from the full calculation is closer to the Wilets-Jean (γ-unstable rotor) limit of 2.50, while the ratio B(E2;3⁺_γ→2⁺_γ)/B(E2;2⁺_g→0⁺_g) trends toward the Davydov-Filippov (rigid triaxial rotor) limit of 1.78.
- For ¹²⁸Xe, the 0⁺₂ wave function has more than 90% of its components distributed between the [n₀−1] and [n₀+1] subspaces, corresponding to a pairing vibrational state.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies that included pairing vibrations only for axially symmetric shapes (Xiang 2020, Nomura 2020) to the triaxial (β, γ) case, addressing a long-standing problem in nuclear structure theory dating back to Bes (1963, 1966) and Casten (1972). The method combines the self-consistent mean-field framework with the interacting boson model, building on the mapping technique of Nomura (2008) and the O(6) dynamical symmetry of Arima and Iachello (1979). The results enable future systematic studies of pairing vibrations across regions of γ-soft and triaxial nuclei, particularly where shape coexistence is expected (Heyde 2011).
