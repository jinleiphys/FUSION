# Bohr space in six dimensions

> A conformal factor in the Bohr model embeds Bohr space in six dimensions, revealing the $O(6)$ symmetry and its contraction to the $E(5)$ at infinity. Phenomenological consequences are discussed after the re-formulation of the Bohr Hamiltonian in six dimensions on a five sphere.

## Key claim

A conformal transformation embeds the five-dimensional Bohr space as the boundary at infinity of a six-dimensional five-sphere, revealing an \(O(6)\) symmetry that contracts to \(E(5)\) at the infinite-radius limit; the paper does not claim that this embedding is unique or that the resulting Hamiltonian exactly reproduces experimental data without additional parameters.

## Method

The author starts from the Bohr line element \(ds^2 = d\beta^2 + \beta^2 d\Omega_4^2\) and applies a conformal factor \(1/(1+a\beta^2)^2\) to obtain a metric that matches the stereographic projection of a five-sphere \(S_5\) of radius \(1/\sqrt{4a}\) onto a five-dimensional tangent plane. The Bohr Hamiltonian is then re-formulated on the \(S_5\) and on the projected plane, and the generators of \(O(6)\) are constructed in terms of the six-dimensional coordinates and their conjugate momenta. Group contraction of \(O(6)\) to \(E(5)\) is examined at the north pole of the sphere in the limit \(a \to 0\).

## Key numbers

* The five-sphere radius is \(R = 1/\sqrt{4a}\), with \(a\) a free parameter controlling the conformal factor.
* The moments of inertia in the projected plane become \(J_k = 4B \beta^2 / (1+a\beta^2)^2 \sin^2(\gamma - k 2\pi/3)\), replacing the original Bohr model’s \(J_k \propto \beta^2\).
* For \(a=0\) the Hamiltonian on the projected plane coincides with the \(E(5)\) Hamiltonian of Iachello (2001).
* The parameter \(a\) is zero for the series of isotopes \(^{128}\)Xe, \(^{130}\)Xe, \(^{132}\)Xe, \(^{134}\)Xe and for \(^{134}\)Ba, \(^{136}\)Ba, based on rms fitting to experimental energy spectra from DDM (2012).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing comparison between the Bohr collective model and the geometrical limit of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) by Dieperink (1980). It provides a geometric embedding that makes explicit the relation between the \(O(6)\) symmetry of the IBM and the five-dimensional Bohr space, and it shows that the \(E(5)\) critical-point symmetry of Iachello (2001) emerges naturally at the boundary. The re-formulated Hamiltonian introduces a deformation-dependent mass parameter, previously proposed phenomenologically by DDM (2012), and suggests that the relation between moments of inertia and intrinsic quadrupole moment is symmetry-dependent, potentially resolving known discrepancies with experiment noted by Ring (1980) and Scharff (1960).
