# Neutron electric dipole moment: Constituent-dressing and compositeness

> Contributions to the neutron's EDM, are calculated using a well-constrained Ansatz for the nucleon's Poincare' covariant Fadde'ev amplitude. The momentum-dependent quark dressing amplifies the contribution from the current-quarks' EDMs; and dressed-quark confinement and binding make distinguishable the effect of the two CP and T violating interactions: i gamma_5 sigma_{mu nu} (p_1-p_2)_nu and gamma_5 (p_1+p_2)_mu, where p_{1,2} are the current-quarks' momenta. The value of |d_n| obtained using the current-quark EDMs generated by a minimal three Higgs doublet model of spontaneous CP violation is close to the current experimental upper bound.

## Key claim

The neutron's electric dipole moment (EDM) is calculated using a Poincaré covariant Fadde'ev amplitude for the nucleon, and the momentum-dependent dressing of quarks amplifies the contribution from current-quark EDMs; the value obtained using current-quark EDMs from a minimal three Higgs doublet model of spontaneous CP violation is close to the current experimental upper bound, though the calculation neglects a possible dressed-quark-gluon CP-violating coupling and is therefore not a complete calculation of \(d_n\).

## Method

The neutron is described as a relativistic three-quark bound state using a product Ansatz for the Poincaré covariant Fadde'ev amplitude, incorporating a scalar diquark correlation with a pseudoparticle propagator and a Bethe-Salpeter-like amplitude for the relative momentum correlation between the dormant quark and the diquark. The dressed-quark propagator is parametrized as an entire function that exhibits dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and confinement, and the dressed-quark-photon vertex includes both the CP-conserving Ball-Chiu form and two CP- and T-violating structures: \(i\gamma_5\sigma_{\mu\nu}q_\nu\) and \(\gamma_5(\ell_1+\ell_2)_\mu\). The nucleon-photon vertex is computed in impulse approximation using Monte-Carlo integration with statistical accuracy below 1%.

## Key numbers

* Experimental upper bound on neutron EDM: \(|d_n| < 6.3 \times 10^{-26}\,e\,{\rm cm}\) (90% C.L.)
* Standard model prediction for quark EDMs: \(d_u < d_d \lesssim 10^{-34}\,e\,{\rm cm}\)
* Current-quark mass used: \(m = 5.1\,{\rm MeV}\) for \(u=d\) quarks
* Euclidean constituent-quark mass: \(M_{u,d}^E = 0.33\,{\rm GeV}\)
* Vacuum quark condensate: \(-\langle \bar qq \rangle_0^{1\,{\rm GeV}^2} = (0.221\,{\rm GeV})^3\)
* Nucleon mass from Fadde'ev equation: \(M_N = 0.94\,{\rm GeV}\) (with axial-vector diquark correlations included)
* Delta mass from Fadde'ev equation: \(M_\Delta = 1.23\,{\rm GeV}\)
* Scalar diquark parameters: \(m_{0^+}=0.64\,{\rm GeV}\), \(\omega_{0^+}=0.42\,{\rm GeV}\) (from Fadde'ev equation study)
* Axial-vector diquark parameters: \(m_{1^+}=0.86\,{\rm GeV}\), \(\omega_{1^+}=1.09\,{\rm GeV}\)
* Calculated proton magnetic moment: \(\mu_p = 2.83\,\mu_N\) (Set 1)
* Calculated neutron magnetic moment: \(\mu_n = -1.61\,\mu_N\) (Set 1)
* Calculated proton charge radius squared: \((r_p)^2 = (0.78\,{\rm fm})^2\) (Set 1)
* Calculated neutron charge radius squared: \((r_n)^2 = -(0.40\,{\rm fm})^2\) (Set 1)
* Calculated axial coupling: \(g_A = 0.98\) (Set 1), \(g_A = 1.27\) (Set 2)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dipole moments in bound states, particularly the \(\rho\)-meson (Martina, Martinb), where direct calculations showed that the naive valence-quark sum rule underestimates the EDM of a bound state by up to two orders of magnitude. It complements QCD Sum Rule approaches to the neutron EDM (Meissner, Bfhda, Bfhdb) by using a Poincaré covariant Fadde'ev equation framework that incorporates dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, quark confinement, and hadron compositeness. The results enable more reliable constraints on extensions of the standard model, such as the minimal three Higgs doublet model of spontaneous CP violation, by accounting for the momentum-dependent dressing of quarks and the distinguishability of the two CP- and T-violating operator structures in the bound state.
