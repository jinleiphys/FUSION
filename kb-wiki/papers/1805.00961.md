# Effective kinetic description of event-by-event pre-equilibrium dynamics in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We develop a macroscopic description of the space-time evolution of the energy-momentum tensor during the pre-equilibrium stage of a high-energy heavy-ion collision. Based on a weak coupling effective kinetic description of the microscopic equilibration process (à la "bottom-up"), we calculate the non-equilibrium evolution of the local background energy-momentum tensor as well as the non-equilibrium linear response to transverse energy and momentum perturbations for realistic boost-invariant initial conditions for heavy ion collisions. We demonstrate how this framework can be used on an event-by-event basis to propagate the energy momentum tensor from far-from-equilibrium initial state models, e.g. IP-Glasma, to the time $τ_\text{hydro}$ when the system is well described by relativistic viscous hydrodynamics. The subsequent hydrodynamic evolution becomes essentially independent of the hydrodynamic initialization time $τ_\text{hydro}$ as long as $τ_\text{hydro}$ is chosen in an appropriate range where both kinetic and hydrodynamic descriptions overlap. We find that for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\,\text{TeV}$ central Pb-Pb collisions, the typical time scale when viscous hydrodynamics with shear viscosity over entropy ratio $η/s=0.16$ becomes applicable is $τ_\text{hydro}\sim 1\,\text{fm/c}$ after the collision.

## Key claim

The paper develops a macroscopic description of the pre-equilibrium energy-momentum tensor evolution that smoothly matches onto viscous hydrodynamics, but the claim of universality is conditional: the scaling of background and response functions with scaled time \(\tau T/(\eta/s)\) holds only for the range of couplings \(\lambda = 10\text{--}25\) (corresponding to \(\eta/s \approx 0.62\text{--}0.16\)) and fails at very weak coupling where the bottom-up thermalization scenario predicts parametrically different hydrodynamization times.

## Method

The authors use QCD effective kinetic theory (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe 2002) to solve the Boltzmann equation for a boost-invariant gluonic plasma, including elastic \(2\leftrightarrow 2\) scattering and inelastic \(1\leftrightarrow 2\) bremsstrahlung with Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression. They compute the non-equilibrium evolution of a homogeneous background energy-momentum tensor and the linear response to transverse energy and momentum perturbations, then propagate these via a convolution formula (Eq. 1) from an initial time \(\tau_\text{EKT} \sim 0.1\,\text{fm}\) to a hydrodynamic initialization time \(\tau_\text{hydro} \sim 1\,\text{fm}\).

## Key numbers

- For \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\,\text{TeV}\) central Pb-Pb collisions, the typical time when viscous hydrodynamics with \(\eta/s = 0.16\) becomes applicable is \(\tau_\text{hydro} \sim 1\,\text{fm/c}\) after the collision.
- The coupling constant \(\lambda = N_c g^2\) is extrapolated to values \(\lambda = 10\text{--}25\) to match realistic \(\eta/s \sim 0.16\).
- The hydrodynamization time for a boost-invariant homogeneous plasma is \(\tau_\text{hydro} \approx 0.8\,\text{fm} \left( \frac{4\pi(\eta/s)}{2} \right)^{3/2} \left( \frac{\langle \tau e^{3/4} \rangle}{1.6\,\text{GeV}^2} \right)^{-1/2} \left( \frac{\nu_{\text{eff}}}{16} \right)^{3/8}\).
- For central Pb-Pb events at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\,\text{TeV}\), the average value of \(\langle \tau e^{3/4} \rangle \approx 1.6\,\text{GeV}^2\).
- Changing the number of degrees of freedom from \(\nu_{\text{eff}} = 16\) (gluon gas) to \(\nu_{\text{eff}} = 40\) (realistic QCD) increases the hydrodynamization time to \(1.1\,\text{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up thermalization scenario (Baier et al. 2000) and the linear response approach of Keegan et al. (2016) by providing a practical event-by-event framework to propagate far-from-equilibrium initial conditions (e.g., IP-Glasma) to a time when viscous hydrodynamics applies. It enables consistent matching between pre-equilibrium kinetic theory and hydrodynamic simulations, reducing dependence on the ad hoc hydrodynamic initialization time \(\tau_\text{hydro}\). The scaling property observed here is related to the "hydrodynamic attractor" behavior reported by Heller et al. (2016) and Romatschke (2017).
