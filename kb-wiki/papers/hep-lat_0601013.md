# Lattice QCD at High Temperature and the QGP

> We review recent progress in studies of bulk thermodynamics of strongly interacting matter, present results on the QCD equation of state and discuss the status of studies of the phase diagram at non-vanishing quark chemical potential.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations at non-zero quark chemical potential are still limited to high temperature and small chemical potential (T ≳ 0.9Tc, μq/T ≲ 1), but they suggest that the transition from low to high temperature is a continuous crossover rather than a phase transition, with the possible existence of a second-order critical point at higher baryon density that remains unconfirmed.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of bulk thermodynamics using staggered fermion actions on lattices with temporal extent Nτ = 4 and 6, employing Taylor expansion of the partition function around μq = 0 to compute pressure and energy density up to sixth order in μq/T, and analyzing Lee-Yang zeroes via reweighting techniques to search for a chiral critical point in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature Tc ≃ 170 MeV, with systematic errors of about 10%
- Energy density at the transition: εc/T0^4 = 6 ± 2
- For RHIC conditions (μq/T ≃ 0.1), higher-order contributions beyond leading (μq/T)^2 are negligible; at SPS energies (μq/T ≲ 0.6) they are still only a few percent
- Lee-Yang zero analysis suggests a critical point at μB = 3μq ≃ 360 MeV (about a factor two smaller than earlier estimates on smaller lattices)
- Radius of convergence estimated from Taylor series of quark number susceptibility gives μB ≃ 180 MeV
- Isentropic equation of state for T > T0 (ε ≳ 0.8 GeV/fm^3) described by p/ε = (1/3)(1 - 1.2/(1 + 0.5 ε fm^3/GeV)), corresponding to bag model with B^1/4 ≃ 260 MeV

## Context

This review extends earlier lattice QCD thermodynamics work by the MILC collaboration (Bernard 2004) and pure gauge theory calculations (Boyd), and discusses the search for a chiral critical point originally proposed by Stephanov. The results enable quantitative comparison with heavy ion experiments at RHIC, SPS, and AGS, and provide a framework for determining the equation of state along isentropic expansion trajectories relevant for freeze-out conditions.
