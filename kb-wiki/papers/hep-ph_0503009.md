# The effect of the in-medium $Θ^+$ pentaquark on the kaon optical potential

> The kaon nuclear optical potential is studied including the effect of the $Θ^+$ pentaquark. The one-nucleon contribution is obtained using an extension of the Jülich meson-exchange potential as bare kaon-nucleon interaction. Significant differences between a fully self-consistent calculation and the usually employed low-density $Tρ$ approach are observed. The influence of the one-nucleon absorption process, $K N \to Θ^+$, on the kaon optical potential is negligible due to the small width of the pentaquark. In contrast, the two-nucleon mechanism, $K N N \to Θ^+ N$, estimated from the coupling of the pentaquark to a two-meson cloud, provides the required amount of additional kaon absorption to reconcile with data the systematically low $K^+$-nucleus reaction cross sections found by the theoretical models.

## Key claim

The two-nucleon absorption mechanism \(K N N \to \Theta^+ N\), estimated from the coupling of the pentaquark to a two-meson cloud, provides the required amount of additional kaon absorption to reconcile with data the systematically low \(K^+\)-nucleus reaction cross sections found by theoretical models, while the one-nucleon process \(K N \to \Theta^+\) has a negligible effect due to the small width of the pentaquark.

## Method

The kaon optical potential in symmetric nuclear matter was calculated using a self-consistent \(G\)-matrix approach, starting from an extension of the Jülich meson-exchange potential for the \(KN\) interaction that includes the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark as an additional pole term. The one-nucleon contribution was obtained from the in-medium \(KN\) effective interaction, while the two-nucleon mechanism \(K N N \to \Theta^+ N\) was evaluated microscopically using a model for the \(\Theta^+ K \pi N\) vertex, allowing the pion to couple to particle-hole and Delta-hole excitations in the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

- The bare pentaquark mass used was \(M_{\Theta^+}^0 = 1545\) MeV, with a bare coupling constant \(g^0_{KN\Theta^+}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 0.03\), chosen to reproduce a physical width of 5 MeV in free space.
- At normal nuclear matter density (\(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\)) and zero kaon momentum, the self-consistent calculation gives a repulsive kaon optical potential of 39 MeV, while the \(T\rho\) approximation gives 29 MeV.
- At a kaon momentum of 400 MeV/c and normal density, the imaginary part of the kaon optical potential from the one-nucleon process is \(-9\) MeV (corresponding to a width of 18 MeV).
- At a kaon momentum of 500 MeV/c and normal density, the two-nucleon contribution to the imaginary part of the kaon optical potential is almost half of that from one-nucleon reactions.
- The two-nucleon mechanism produces \(K^+\) nuclear absorption cross sections per nucleon of about 2–3 mb.
- The reaction cross sections per nucleon are increased by 10–15% with respect to the \(G\rho\) values, bringing them into agreement with experimental data.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent calculations of the kaon optical potential (Korpa, 2004) and addresses a long-standing anomaly where theoretical models based on the \(T\rho\) approximation systematically underestimated \(K^+\)-nucleus reaction cross sections by about 10–15% (Friedman, 1999). The results confirm the expectations of a recent phenomenological analysis (Gal, 2004) that two-nucleon absorption mechanisms could resolve this discrepancy, and they enable more accurate predictions for kaon interactions in dense nuclear matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions.
