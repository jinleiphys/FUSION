# Magnetic catalysis (and inverse catalysis) at finite temperature in two-color lattice QCD

> Two-color lattice QCD with N_f=4 staggered fermion degrees of freedom (no rooting trick is applied) with equal electric charge q is studied in a homogeneous magnetic background field B and at non-zero temperature T. In order to circumvent renormalization as a function of the bare coupling we apply a fixed-scale approach. We study the influence of the magnetic field on the critical temperature. At rather small pseudo-scalar meson mass ($m_π \approx 175 \mathrm{MeV} \approx T_c(B=0)$) we confirm a monotonic rise of the quark condensate $<\barψ ψ>$ with increasing magnetic field strength, i.e. magnetic catalysis, as long as one is staying within the confinement or deconfinement phase. In the transition region we find indications for a non-monotonic behavior of $T_c(B)$ at low magnetic field strength ($qB<0.8 \mathrm{GeV}^2$) and a clear rise at stronger magnetic field. The conjectured existence of a minimum value $T_c(B^{*}) < T_c(B=0)$ would leave a temperature window for a decrease of $<\barψ ψ>$ with rising $B$ (inverse magnetic catalysis) also in the present model.

## Key claim

The paper finds indications for a non-monotonic behavior of the critical temperature \(T_c(B)\) at low magnetic field strength (\(qB < 0.8\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)) and a clear rise at stronger magnetic field, suggesting a possible minimum \(T_c(B^{*}) < T_c(B=0)\) that would leave a temperature window for inverse magnetic catalysis (decrease of the quark condensate with rising \(B\)) in two-color QCD with four flavors.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of two-color QCD with \(N_f=4\) staggered fermions (no rooting) carrying equal electric charge \(q\) in a homogeneous magnetic background field \(B\) at finite temperature \(T\). They employed a fixed-scale approach at \(\beta=1.80\) with lattice size \(32^3 \times N_\tau\) for \(N_\tau = 4,6,8,10\), varying the temperature by changing \(N_\tau\) while keeping the lattice spacing fixed at \(a = 0.168(4)\ \mathrm{fm}\). The bare quark mass was set to \(ma = 0.0025\), and magnetic flux values \(N_b = 0, 80, 200\) were used, corresponding to \(qB = 0, 0.67, 1.69\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 175(4)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at bare quark mass \(ma = 0.0025\), determined from a zero-temperature simulation on a \(32^3 \times 48\) lattice.
* Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.168(4)\ \mathrm{fm}\) from the Sommer parameter \(r_0 = 0.468(4)\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* Critical temperature at zero magnetic field: \(T_c(B=0) \approx 160-190\ \mathrm{MeV}\), comparable to the pion mass.
* Magnetic field strengths studied: \(qB = 0.67\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (intermediate) and \(qB = 1.69\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (strong), with the latter corresponding to \(\sqrt{qB}/T \approx 4.5\) at \(T = 195\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* At \(T = 195\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (\(N_\tau = 6\)), a sharp change in the Polyakov loop and chiral condensate occurs in the range \(0.7\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 < qB < 1.0\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ilgenfritz et al., 2012) by using a fixed-scale approach to avoid renormalization issues and by employing a smaller quark mass yielding \(m_\pi \approx T_c(B=0)\), similar to physical QCD. The results are compared with full QCD lattice studies by D'Elia et al. (2010, 2011) and Bali et al. (2011, 2012, 2013), where the latter group observed inverse magnetic catalysis near the phase transition. The findings suggest that two-color QCD with four flavors may exhibit a non-monotonic \(T_c(B)\) similar to that seen in QCD, enabling future studies of the phase diagram at finite chemical potential (where two-color QCD has no sign problem) and investigations of topological structure.
