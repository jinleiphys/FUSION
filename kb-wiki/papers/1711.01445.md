# Remarks on the hidden color components in multi-quark study

> Problems related to the application of hidden color components in multi-quark systems are discussed in this report.

# Remarks on the hidden color components in multi-quark study

## Key claim

Hidden color components in multi-quark systems are physically meaningful and not spurious, but their interpretation depends critically on the choice of basis (symmetry bases vs. physical cluster bases) and on the separation distance between clusters; at short distances where clusters merge, the distinction between hidden color and colorless channels collapses, and the transformation coefficients between bases no longer represent probability amplitudes.

## Method

The authors analyze the group-theoretic transformation between symmetry bases (classified by group chains such as SU(12) or SU(18)) and physical cluster bases (resonating group method bases) for six-quark systems, extending prior work by Harvey and Chen. They examine the color magnetic interaction (CMI) matrix elements for different six-quark orbital configurations and compare the hidden color content of NN, ΔΔ, and other baryon-baryon channels. They also compute adiabatic energies for the YIJ=203 system using Harvey's symmetry bases at varying cluster separations.

## Key numbers

- The CMI matrix element for a single nucleon (octet baryon) is −8C, while for a single Δ (decuplet baryon) it is +8C.
- For the six-quark state with deuteron quantum numbers IJ=01 in a totally symmetric orbital configuration, the CMI matrix element is 8/3 C.
- The difference ⟨CMI⟩_d − 2⟨CMI⟩_N = 56/3 C (repulsive for NN), while ⟨CMI⟩_d − 2⟨CMI⟩_Δ = −40/3 C (attractive for ΔΔ).
- For the YIJ=203 system, the measured d* dibaryon resonance mass is 2.37 GeV with a width of about 70 MeV; the partial width of resonating scattering is about 12 MeV.
- The quark model calculated rms radius of d* is about 0.9 fm.
- In the adiabatic calculation for YIJ=203, at separation s=0.9 fm the ΔΔ channel energy is 2604 MeV and the hidden color channel energy is 2739 MeV, with the eigenstate at 2347 MeV.
- The overlap of ΔΔ and hidden color physical bases at s=0.1 fm is 0.99996, decreasing to 0.007 at s=2.0 fm.

## Context

This work extends the group-theoretic transformation methods of Harvey (1981) and Chen (1980s) for six-quark systems from SU(4) to SU(6) flavor-spin symmetry and from non-relativistic to relativistic cases. It contradicts the claim by Brodsky, Ji and others that the NN repulsive core arises from hidden color components, showing instead that the repulsion comes from color magnetic interactions and that hidden color channels couple equally to octet and decuplet channels. The paper also addresses the interpretation of the d* dibaryon observed by the WASA-at-COSY collaboration (Clement, 2014), arguing that the dominant component remains the ΔΔ channel rather than hidden color configurations as proposed by Bashkanov, Brodsky and Clement. The work enables more careful dynamical calculations of multi-quark states using proper translational-invariant bases rather than Harvey's adiabatic approximation.
