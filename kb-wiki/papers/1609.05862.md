# The physics mechanisms of light and heavy flavor $v_{2}$ and mass ordering in AMPT

> A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model has been shown to describe experimental data well, such as the bulk properties of particle spectra and elliptic anisotropy ($v_{2}$) in heavy ion collisions. Recent studies have shown that AMPT describes the $v_{2}$ data in small system collisions as well. In these proceedings, we first investigate the origin of the mass ordering of identified hadrons $v_{2}$ in heavy ion as well as small system collisions. We then study the production mechanism of the charm $v_{2}$ in light of the escape mechanism for the light quark $v_{2}$.

# The physics mechanisms of light and heavy flavor \(v_{2}\) and mass ordering in AMPT

## Key claim
The mass ordering of identified hadron elliptic flow (\(v_{2}\)) at low transverse momentum is not a unique signature of hydrodynamics; it arises primarily from kinematic effects in the coalescence hadronization process and from hadronic rescattering, not from collective anisotropic flow. The charm quark \(v_{2}\) in small systems is generated mainly by the same escape mechanism that dominates light quark \(v_{2}\), rather than by thermalization.

## Method
The authors used the string melting version of the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, which includes fluctuating initial conditions, parton elastic scatterings via Zhang's parton cascade (ZPC) with a Debye-screened cross-section, quark coalescence for hadronization, and hadronic interactions via the ART model. They simulated Au+Au collisions at impact parameter \(b = 6.6\)–8.1 fm at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, \(d\)+Au collisions at \(b = 0\) fm at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, and \(p\)+Pb collisions at \(b = 0\) fm at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) TeV. They analyzed \(v_{2}\) at different evolution stages: right after coalescence hadronization (primordial hadrons), after resonance decays, and after hadronic rescattering at freezeout, and also compared normal AMPT with a \(\phi\)-randomized version that removes hydrodynamic flow.

## Key numbers
- Strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.33\) and Debye screening mass \(\mu_D = 2.265\)/fm, giving a total parton cross-section \(\sigma = 3\) mb.
- Au+Au collisions simulated at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV with impact parameter \(b = 6.6\)–8.1 fm; \(d\)+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV with \(b = 0\) fm; \(p\)+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) TeV with \(b = 0\) fm.
- Hadron \(v_{2}\) values are shown for transverse momentum bin \(0.7 < p_{\perp} < 0.8\) GeV/\(c\) at mid-rapidity (\(|\eta| < 1\)) across different evolution stages.
- Charm quark \(v_{2}\) is systematically lower than light quark \(v_{2}\) in normal AMPT; in \(\phi\)-randomized AMPT, charm and light quark \(v_{2}\) become similar, indicating a common escape mechanism.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by He et al. (2015) and Lin et al. (2015) which showed that light parton \(v_{2}\) in AMPT is mainly generated by anisotropic parton escape rather than hydrodynamics. The authors apply this escape mechanism framework to charm quarks in small systems (\(p\)+Pb) and clarify that the mass ordering of hadron \(v_{2}\), commonly attributed to hydrodynamics as in the work of Heinz (2013) and Song (multistrange), actually arises from coalescence kinematics and hadronic rescattering. This enables a reinterpretation of experimental \(v_{2}\) mass ordering data in both heavy ion and small system collisions, and suggests that charm quark flow in small systems does not require thermalization.
