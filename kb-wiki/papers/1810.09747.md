# Simulation studies of $\rm{R_{2}}(Δη, Δφ)$ and $\rm{P_{2}}(Δη, Δφ)$ correlation functions in pp collisions with the PYTHIA and HERWIG models

> We report studies of charge-independent (CI) and charge-dependent (CD) two-particle differential-number correlation functions, $\rm{R_{2}}(Δη, Δφ)$, and transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) correlation functions, $\rm{P_{2}}(Δη, Δφ)$, of charged particles in $\sqrt{\textit{s}}$ = 2.76 TeV pp collisions with the PYTHIA and HERWIG models. Model predictions are presented for inclusive charged hadrons ($h^\pm$), as well as pions ($π^\pm$), kaons (K$^\pm$), and (anti-)protons ($\rm \bar{p}$/p) in the ranges $0.2 < \textit{p}_{\rm T} \le 2.0~\rm{GeV}/\textit{c}$, $2.0 < \textit{p}_{\rm T} \le 5.0~\rm{GeV}/\textit{c}$, and $5.0 < \textit{p}_{\rm T} \le 30.0~\rm{GeV}/\textit{c}$, with full azimuthal coverage in the range $|η|< 1.0$. We compare the model predictions for the strength and shape of the $\rm{R_{2}}$ and $\rm{P_{2}}$ correlators as these pertain to recent measurements by the ALICE collaboration. The $\rm{R_{2}}$ and $\rm{P_{2}}$ correlation functions estimated with PYTHIA and HERWIG exhibit qualitatively similar near-side and away-side correlation structures but feature important differences. Our analysis indicates that comparative studies of $\rm{R_{2}}$ and $\rm{P_{2}}$ correlation functions would provide valuable insight towards the understanding of particle production in pp collisions, and by extension, should also be useful in studies of heavy-ion collisions. Comparison of the $Δη$ dependence of $\rm{R_{2}}$ and $\rm{P_{2}}$ could contribute, in particular, to a better understanding and modeling of the angular ordering of particles produced by hadronization in jets, as well as a better description of jet fragmentation functions of identified species at low momentum fraction $(z)$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the near-side peak of the $\Ptwo$ correlation function is systematically narrower than that of the $\Rtwo$ correlation function in PYTHIA and HERWIG simulations of pp collisions, and that this narrowing arises from the angular ordering of transverse momentum within jets, a feature that the abstract presents as a qualitative finding rather than a definitive measurement.

## Method

The authors generated $2\times 10^8$ pp collision events at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV with PYTHIA 6.425 (Perugia-0 tune) and $2\times 10^8$ events with HERWIG 6.5, using three hard QCD $\hat{p}_T$ bins (5.0–10.0, 10.0–20.0, and 20.0–30.0 GeV/$c$). They computed charge-independent (CI) and charge-dependent (CD) versions of the $\R_2(\Delta\eta,\Delta\phi)$ and $\P_2(\Delta\eta,\Delta\phi)$ correlation functions for inclusive charged hadrons, pions, kaons, and (anti-)protons in three $p_T$ ranges (0.2–2.0, 2.0–5.0, and 5.0–30.0 GeV/$c$) within $|\eta|<1.0$, and compared the model predictions for the strength and shape of the correlators.

## Key numbers

* PYTHIA and HERWIG each generated $2\times 10^8$ events in each of three $\hat{p}_T$ bins: 5.0–10.0 GeV/$c$, 10.0–20.0 GeV/$c$, and 20.0–30.0 GeV/$c$.
* Correlation functions were computed for charged hadrons in three $p_T$ ranges: $0.2 < p_T \le 2.0$ GeV/$c$, $2.0 < p_T \le 5.0$ GeV/$c$, and $5.0 < p_T \le 30.0$ GeV/$c$.
* The pseudorapidity acceptance was $|\eta| < 1.0$ with full azimuthal coverage.
* The ratio of cross-sections for positively and negatively charged hadrons was found to be of order unity in the $p_T$ range of interest.

## Context

This work extends the experimental measurements of $\R_2$ and $\P_2$ correlation functions reported by the ALICE collaboration (Adam 2017, AliceDptDptLongPaper) by providing model predictions from PYTHIA and HERWIG that can be directly compared to those data. The study builds on the observation by Sharma (2008) that comparative measurements of $\P_2$ and $\R_2$ may provide additional sensitivity to particle production mechanisms. The results enable future comparisons with pp, p–Pb, and Pb–Pb collision data to discriminate between different parton splitting and hadronization mechanisms, particularly regarding the angular ordering of jet constituents and the description of jet fragmentation functions at low momentum fraction $z$.
