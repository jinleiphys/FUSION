# Temperature dependent Nucleon Mass and entropy bound inequality

> Mass of a baryon as a function of temperature is calculated using colour-singlet partition function for massless quarks (with two flavours) and abelian gluons confined in a bag with a temperature dependent bag pressure constant $B(T)$. The non-perturbative aspect of QCD interaction is included through colour-singlet restriction on quark-gluon partition function in a phenomenological way. The entropy bound inequality $ S/E \ \leq \ 2πR/\hbar c $, where $S, \ E $ and $R$ are entropy, energy and radius, respectively of the enclosed system with $\hbar c \ = \ 197.331 $ MeVfm, is found to be consistent with the equilibrium solutions of the baryon mass upto a temperature $T_E$. There is a region of temperature $T_E < T < T_C$ ($T_C$ is critical temperature for quark-gluon plasma formation) in which no admissible equilibrium states exist for the bag. We say that the system expriences a phase jump from hadron to quark-gluon plasma through thermodynamic non-equlibrium processes.

# Temperature Dependent Nucleon Mass and Entropy Bound Inequality

## Key claim
The entropy bound inequality S/E ≤ 2πR/ħc is consistent with equilibrium solutions of the baryon mass only up to a temperature T_E ≈ 145 MeV; for temperatures between T_E and T_C (the critical temperature for quark-gluon plasma formation), no admissible equilibrium states exist for the bag, implying a non-equilibrium phase jump from hadron to quark-gluon plasma.

## Method
The authors calculate the temperature-dependent mass of a baryon using a colour-singlet grand canonical partition function for massless quarks (two flavours) and abelian gluons confined in a spherical bag, with a temperature-dependent bag pressure constant B(T) = B(0)[1 − (T/T_C)^4]. The chemical potential is adjusted to enforce N = 3 quarks, and the free energy is minimized with respect to the bag radius at each temperature to find stable and metastable equilibrium solutions, against which the entropy bound inequality is tested.

## Key numbers
- T_E = 144.3 MeV: the maximum temperature at which equilibrium solutions exist for the bag (with B^(1/4)(0) = 200 MeV and T_C = 165 MeV).
- T_S = 130 MeV: the temperature at which a metastable solution first appears (colour-singlet case).
- At T = T_S = 130 MeV, two mass values exist: M(T) = 1107 MeV (stable) and M(T) = 33349 MeV (metastable).
- At T = T_E = 144.3 MeV, the two mass values coincide at M(T) = 1919 MeV.
- For the colour unprojected case with T_C = 145 MeV, T_S = 121 MeV and T_E = 125 MeV, giving T_E − T_S = 4 MeV.
- For the colour-singlet case, T_E − T_S ≈ 15 MeV.
- The baryon mass around T_E scales roughly as 4πT.
- ħc = 197.331 MeV·fm.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Dey et al. (1991) on meson formation limits and by Mustafa et al. (1993) on temperature-dependent meson masses in spherical and deformed bags, now applied to nucleons. The entropy bound inequality was originally proposed by Bekenstein (1981) based on black hole thermodynamics and has been tested in systems with strong gravitational fields, quartic field interactions, and superstring systems. The present results show that the inequality can serve as a criterion for bound systems and that the colour-singlet restriction (a non-perturbative QCD effect) significantly widens the temperature window of metastable states compared to colour-unprojected calculations. The temperature-dependent baryon mass obtained is qualitatively consistent with screening masses from lattice calculations by Gottlieb et al. (1987).
