# A unified approach for nucleon knock-out, coherent and incoherent pion production in neutrino interactions with nuclei

> We present a theory of neutrino interactions with nuclei aimed at the description of the partial cross-sections, namely quasi-elastic and multi-nucleon emission, coherent and incoherent single pion production. For this purpose, we use the theory of nuclear responses treated in the random phase approximation, which allows a unified description of these channels. It is particularly suited for the coherent pion production where collective effects are important whereas they are moderate in the other channels. We also study the evolution of the neutrino cross-sections with the mass number from carbon to calcium. We compare our approach to the available neutrino experimental data on carbon. We put a particular emphasis on the multi-nucleon channel, which at present is not easily distinguishable from the quasi-elastic events. This component turns out to be quite relevant for the interpretation of experiments (K2K, MiniBooNE, SciBooNE). It can account in particular for the unexpected behavior of the quasi-elastic cross-section.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unified treatment of neutrino-nucleus interactions using random phase approximation (RPA) nuclear response functions can simultaneously describe quasi-elastic scattering, multi-nucleon emission, and coherent and incoherent single pion production, but the approach is limited to single-pion production via the Delta resonance and ignores two-pion production, meson exchange effects (except in the time component of the axial current), and final-state interactions for the incoherent channels.

## Method

The authors compute nuclear response functions in the spin-isospin channel using the random phase approximation (RPA) in the ring approximation, starting from bare polarization propagators that include nucleon-nucleon (quasi-elastic and 2p-2h), nucleon-Delta (2p-2h), and Delta-Delta (pion emission, 2p-2h, and 3p-3h) contributions. The in-medium Delta width is parameterized following Oset and Salcedo (1987), and the RPA equations are solved with an effective interaction containing pion, rho-meson, and contact (Landau-Migdal) terms. The coherent pion production channel is isolated from the RPA response using a separation method introduced by Marteau (1999), while the incoherent channels are obtained from the remaining part of the response, though final-state interactions for pions are not included.

## Key numbers

* Axial form factor cutoff parameter: M_A = 1.032 GeV/c^2, with axial coupling G_A(0) = 1.255
* Vector form factor cutoff parameter: M_V = 0.84 GeV/c^2, with magnetic coupling G_M(0) = 4.71
* Landau-Migdal parameters: g'_NN = 0.7, g'_NΔ = g'_ΔΔ = 0.5, and f' = 0.6
* Scaling factor between nucleon and Delta form factors: f*/f = 2.2
* The coherent longitudinal response per nucleon decreases from carbon to calcium and vanishes in nuclear matter (fictitious lead density profile)
* Suppression of the meson exchange correction in the axial current (G_A* → G_A) produces a moderate ~10% increase of the coherent cross-section
* For 12C at E_ν = 1 GeV, the estimated overall correction for the incoherent production cross-section due to pion final-state interactions is a moderate reduction of ~15%

## Context

This work extends the RPA-based approach previously applied to ν-16O interactions by Marteau (1999) and builds on the spin-isospin phenomenology of Delorme and Guichon for 2p-2h contributions. The authors compare their results to experimental data from K2K, MiniBooNE, and SciBooNE, and emphasize that the multi-nucleon channel can account for the unexpected behavior of the quasi-elastic cross-section observed in those experiments. The study is motivated by the planned liquid argon chamber for T2K, and the authors explore the evolution of cross-sections from carbon to calcium to assess whether control of neutrino-nucleus interactions can be maintained for medium-weight nuclei.
