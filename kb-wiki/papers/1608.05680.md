# Formation of hypernuclei in heavy-ion collisions around the threshold energies

> In relativistic ion collisions there are excellent opportunities to produce and investigate hyper-nuclei. We have systematically studied the formation of hypernuclear spectator residues in peripheral heavy-ion collisions with the transport DCM and UrQMD models. The hyperon capture was calculated within the potential and coalescence approaches. We demonstrate that even at the beam energies around and lower than the threshold for producing Lambda hyperons in binary nucleon-nucleon interactions a considerable amount of hypernuclei, including multi-strange ones, can be produced. This is important for preparation of new experiments on hypernuclei in the wide energy range. The uncertainties of the predictions are investigated within the models, and the comparison with the strangeness production measured in experiments is also performed.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a considerable amount of hypernuclei, including multi-strange ones, can be produced in peripheral heavy-ion collisions even at beam energies around and below the threshold for producing Lambda hyperons in binary nucleon-nucleon interactions (approximately 1.6 A GeV), though the model predictions show deviations of up to one order of magnitude at the very lowest subthreshold energies.

## Method

The authors used the Dubna Cascade Model (DCM) and the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport models to simulate peripheral heavy-ion collisions, then applied both potential and coalescence criteria for hyperon capture by nuclear spectator residues. The calculations were performed on an event-by-event basis for various projectile-target combinations (carbon, nickel, lead) across beam energies from 1 to 10 A GeV, with the resulting primary hyper-residue yields reported before statistical de-excitation.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for Lambda production in binary nucleon-nucleon collisions is approximately 1.6 A GeV
- For Pb+Pb collisions at 2 A GeV, UrQMD+CB calculations with coalescence parameter v_c = 0.22 predict single hyper-residue cross sections of 9.0 mb, double hyper-residue cross sections of 0.045 mb, and triple hyper-residue cross sections of approximately 0.0013 mb at 1.0 A GeV
- For Pb+Pb collisions at 1 A GeV, DCM predicts nearly 4 times larger hyperon yields than UrQMD
- For carbon collisions at approximately 2 A GeV, double light hyper-residues can be produced with cross sections of approximately 1 nanobarn
- Reducing the hyperon capture potential from 30 MeV to 15 MeV decreases hyper-residue yields by approximately 20 percent
- The probability for forming residues with one additional captured hyperon decreases by two orders of magnitude in heavy nucleus collisions and up to four orders of magnitude for very light nuclei

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Botvina et al. (2013, 2015) that demonstrated saturation of single hypernuclei yields above 3-5 A GeV, now systematically investigating the subthreshold energy region down to 1 A GeV. The results provide predictions for upcoming experiments at facilities including FAIR (with Super-FRS, CBM), NICA, and GSI, where the detection of hypernuclei at energies below 2 A GeV can become very effective due to detector acceptance advantages. The authors note that the lack of experimental data on low-energy particles in subthreshold heavy-ion collisions is the main obstacle for adequate model adjustment, and suggest that experimental determination of spectator heavy hypernuclei yields could provide important constraints on hyperon interactions in nuclear matter.
