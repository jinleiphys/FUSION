# The Hyperon-Nucleon Interaction Potential in the Bound State Soliton Model: The $ΛN$ Case

> We develop the formalism to study the hyperon-nucleon interaction potential within the bound state approach to the SU(3) Skyrme model. The general framework is illustrated by applying it to the diagonal $ΛN$ potential. The central, spin-spin and tensor components of this interaction are obtained and compared with those derived using alternative schemes.

# The Hyperon-Nucleon Interaction Potential in the Bound State Soliton Model: The ΛN Case

## Key claim

The diagonal ΛN potential is derived within the bound state approach to the SU(3) Skyrme model, yielding central, spin-spin, and tensor components that are compared with alternative schemes; however, the central potential is repulsive at all distances due to the well-known missing intermediate-range central attraction inherited from the SU(2) Skyrme model, and the spin-spin and tensor components are suppressed by an order of magnitude relative to the central component.

## Method

The authors use the product ansatz for the B=2 soliton field, approximating the two-baryon configuration as a product of two B=1 solutions centered at different positions. The interaction Lagrangian is derived from the SU(3) chiral action including quadratic, quartic, Wess-Zumino, and symmetry-breaking terms, expanded to second order in the kaon field. Collective coordinates are introduced via independent SU(2) rotations of each soliton, and the potential is obtained by taking matrix elements between two-baryon wavefunctions and integrating over the center-of-mass coordinate, working within the adiabatic approximation (neglecting non-adiabatic terms that would give spin-orbit contributions).

## Key numbers

- Meson parameters used: m_π = 138 MeV, m_K = 495 MeV, f_π = 93 MeV, f_K/f_π = 1.22, and ε = 4.26 (fitted to the nucleon-Δ mass difference).
- The central potential V_C is repulsive at all distances, dominated by the quartic term contribution; spin-spin and tensor components are suppressed by an order of magnitude relative to the central component.
- At r ≈ 0.9 fm, the tensor potential from OBE model D is approximately -14 MeV while OBE model F gives approximately +9 MeV; the bound state approach results favor model D in this region.
- For r > 1.2 fm, the tensor component agrees well with OBE model predictions.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to hyperon-nucleon interactions, complementing earlier collective coordinate approach (CCA) calculations by KE (1990) and SSG (1995) for the SU(3) Skyrme model. Unlike the CCA where strangeness appears as a collective rotational excitation, the bound state approach describes hyperons as bound states of kaons in the background field of an SU(2) soliton. The formalism enables future investigations of coupling to vibrations (which could provide missing central attraction) and coupling to rotationally excited states (which may change the sign of the spin-spin interaction), as suggested by KE (1990). The results are compared with phenomenological one-boson exchange (OBE) models, specifically the Nijmegen models D and F from NRS (1979).
