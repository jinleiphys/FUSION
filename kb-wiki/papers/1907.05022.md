# Charmed baryon$-$nucleon interaction

> We present a comparative study of the charmed baryon$-$nucleon interaction based on different theoretical approaches. For this purpose, we make use of i) a constituent quark model tuned in the light-flavor baryon$-$baryon interaction and the hadron spectra, ii) existing results in the literature based both on hadronic and quark-level descriptions, iii) (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD results of the HAL QCD Collaboration at unphysical pion masses and their effective field theory extrapolation to the physical pion mass. There is a general qualitative agreement among the different available approaches to the charmed baryon$-$nucleon interaction. Different from hadronic models based on one-boson exchange potentials, quark$-$model based results point to soft interactions without two-body bound states. They also support a negligible channel coupling, due either to tensor forces or to transitions between different physical channels, $Λ_c N - Σ_c N$. Short-range gluon and quark-exchange dynamics generate a slightly larger repulsion in the $^1S_0$ than in the $^3S_1$ $Λ_c N$ partial wave. A similar asymmetry between the attraction in the two $S$ waves of the $Λ_c N$ interaction also appears in hadronic approaches. A comparative detailed study of Pauli suppressed partial waves, as the $^1S_0 (I=1/2)$ and $^3S_1 (I=3/2)$ $Σ_c N$ channels, would help to disentangle the short-range dynamics of two-baryon systems containing heavy flavors. The possible existence of charmed hypernuclei is discussed.

## Key claim

Quark-model-based approaches to the charmed baryon-nucleon interaction predict soft interactions without two-body bound states, in contrast to hadronic models based on one-boson exchange potentials which do produce bound states; the quark-model results also support a negligible channel coupling between the Lambda_c N and Sigma_c N systems, unlike the significant tensor-force effects seen in some hadronic descriptions.

## Method

The authors employed a constituent quark model (CQM) previously tuned to light-flavor baryon-baryon interactions and hadron spectra, deriving the charmed baryon-nucleon interaction via a Born-Oppenheimer approximation that integrates out quark coordinates at fixed two-baryon separation. They solved coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the Lambda_c N and Sigma_c N systems in S-waves, including the coupling to D-waves via tensor forces and the Lambda_c N - Sigma_c N channel coupling for isospin 1/2. The CQM results were compared to existing hadronic models (one-boson exchange potentials), the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM), hybrid models, SU(4) meson-exchange potentials, and lattice QCD results from the HAL QCD Collaboration at unphysical pion masses with chiral effective field theory extrapolations.

- CQM scattering length for Lambda_c N ^3S_1: a_t = -2.31 fm; effective range r_t = 2.97 fm
- CQM scattering length for Sigma_c N (I=1/2) ^1S_0: a_s = 0.74 - i 0.18 fm
- CQM scattering length for Sigma_c N (I=1/2) ^3S_1: a_t = -5.21 - i 1.96 fm
- CQM scattering length for Sigma_c N (I=3/2) ^1S_0: a_s = -1.25 fm; effective range r_s = 8.70 fm
- CQM scattering length for Sigma_c N (I=3/2) ^3S_1: a_t = 0.95 fm; effective range r_t = 4.89 fm
- HAL QCD EFT extrapolation to physical pion mass gives ^1S_0 Lambda_c N phase shift maximum of 17-21 degrees
- HAL QCD EFT extrapolation gives ^3S_1 Lambda_c N scattering length a_t in range [-0.81, -0.98] fm
- Hard-core radii from color-magnetic part of OGE: 0.35 fm for spin-triplet state, 0.44 fm for spin-singlet state
- Mass difference between Lambda_c and Sigma_c: 168 MeV (compared to 73 MeV in the strange sector)

## Context

This work provides the first comparative study of the charmed baryon-nucleon interaction across multiple theoretical approaches, extending earlier constituent quark model studies of light baryon-baryon systems (Valcarce et al., 2005; Vijande et al., 2005) and nucleon-nucleon interactions (Garcilazo et al., 1999). It contrasts with hadronic one-boson exchange models (Liu et al., 2012; Oka et al., 2013) that predict bound Lambda_c N states, and with the hybrid model of Maeda et al. (2016) that supplements boson exchange with short-range repulsion. The results are compared to lattice QCD simulations by the HAL QCD Collaboration (Miyagawa et al., 2018) at pion masses of 410 and 570 MeV and their chiral EFT extrapolation to the physical pion mass (Haidenbauer et al., 2018). The study enables future experimental searches for charmed hypernuclei at J-PARC and FAIR by clarifying which theoretical scenarios predict binding and which do not.
