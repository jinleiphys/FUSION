# Bose-Einstein condensate of alpha particles in the ground state of nuclear matter?

> The phase diagram of isospin-symmetric chemically equilibrated mixture of alpha particles and nucleons is studied in the mean-field approximation. Skyrme-like parametrization is used for the mean-field potentials as functions of partial densities of nucleons and alphas. We find that there is a threshold value a* of the parameter a(N-alpha) which describes the attractive interaction between alpha-particles and nucleons. At a(N-alpha)<a* the ground state of nuclear matter at zero temperature behaves as a pure system of interacting nucleons, whereas at a(N-alpha)>a* the nuclear ground state includes also a nonzero fraction of alphas. We demonstrate that the equation of state of such alpha-N system includes both the first-order liquid-gas phase transitions and the Bose-Einstein condensation of alpha particles.

## Key claim

For sufficiently strong attractive interaction between alpha particles and nucleons (parameter \(a_{N\alpha} > a_* \approx 2.12\ \mathrm{GeV\,fm}^3\)), the ground state of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature contains a nonzero fraction of alpha particles forming a Bose-Einstein condensate, coexisting with a Fermi distribution of nucleons; this contrasts with the conventional picture where the ground state contains only nucleons.

## Method

The authors use a mean-field model with Skyrme-like parametrizations for the interaction potentials of an isospin-symmetric mixture of nucleons and alpha particles in chemical equilibrium (\(\alpha \leftrightarrow 4N\)). The pressure is written as a sum of ideal-gas terms for nucleons and alphas plus a mean-field correction \(\Delta p\) that depends on partial densities \(n_N\) and \(n_\alpha\). The model parameters for pure nucleon matter and pure alpha matter are fixed by fitting known ground-state properties, leaving the cross-term coefficient \(a_{N\alpha}\) (describing \(\alpha N\) attraction) as the only free parameter; its threshold value \(a_*\) is derived analytically.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of pure nucleon matter: baryon density \(n_0 \simeq 0.15\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), binding energy per baryon \(W_0 \simeq -15.9\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Threshold value of the \(\alpha N\) attraction parameter: \(a_* \simeq 2.12\ \mathrm{GeV\,fm}^3\).
- For \(a_{N\alpha} = 2.13\ \mathrm{GeV\,fm}^3 > a_*\), the ground state has an alpha fraction \(\chi \equiv 4n_\alpha/n_B \simeq 0.12\) (without readjusting nucleon parameters) or \(\chi \simeq 0.04\) (after readjusting \(a_N, b_N\) to preserve empirical ground-state properties).
- Critical point of the liquid-gas phase transition for \(a_{N\alpha} = 2.13\ \mathrm{GeV\,fm}^3\): \(T_{\mathrm{CP}} \simeq 14.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(\mu_{\mathrm{CP}} \simeq 907\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(n_{\mathrm{B\,CP}} \simeq 0.048\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(\chi_{\mathrm{CP}} \simeq 0.19\).
- Triple point temperature: \(T_{\mathrm{TP}} \simeq 0.38\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(a_{N\alpha} = 2.13\ \mathrm{GeV\,fm}^3\).
- Pure nucleon matter critical temperature: \(T_{\mathrm{CP}} \simeq 15.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\); pure alpha matter critical temperature: \(T_{\mathrm{CP}} \simeq 10.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous mean-field model (Satarov et al., 2019) which assumed that the ground state of \(\alpha\)-\(N\) matter contains only nucleons and no alphas. The present paper shows that for sufficiently strong \(\alpha N\) attraction (\(a_{N\alpha} > a_*\)), the ground state includes a Bose-Einstein condensate of alpha particles, and the phase diagram exhibits both a liquid-gas phase transition and BEC. The results differ from those of Zhang et al. (2019), who considered alpha condensation at low temperatures and densities assuming a homogeneous system; the present calculation finds that under such conditions the matter exists in a highly inhomogeneous liquid-gas phase where BEC does not occur in the low-density gas domain. The model also contrasts with the virial expansion approach (Horowitz and Schwenk, 2006) which is valid only at small densities; the authors compare their results to that approach to constrain \(a_{N\alpha}\).
