# Improved nuclear-structure corrections to the hyperfine splitting of electronic and muonic deuterium

> We calculate the nuclear-structure correction to the hyperfine splitting in both electronic and muonic deuterium using interactions from chiral effective field theory. We explore the sensitivity to different parameterizations of the nucleon-nucleon force, study the convergence pattern in the order-by-order chiral expansion, and estimate remaining uncertainties. Our results are consistent with earlier calculations from pionless effective field theory, offering new insights for a robust uncertainty quantification. Thanks to the order-of-magnitude reduction in uncertainty achieved with chiral effective field theory, the two-photon exchange contribution in electronic deuterium agrees with experimental extractions within $0.7σ$, in contrast to the $2.7σ$ discrepancy observed in muonic deuterium. This study lays the groundwork for extending TPE calculations to HFS in heavier atomic systems.

## Key claim

The nuclear-structure correction to the hyperfine splitting in both electronic and muonic deuterium, calculated using chiral effective field theory interactions, achieves an order-of-magnitude reduction in uncertainty compared to earlier pionless EFT calculations, but the agreement with experiment depends on the lepton species: the two-photon exchange contribution agrees within 0.7 sigma for electronic deuterium while a 2.7 sigma discrepancy persists for muonic deuterium.

## Method

The authors extended the formalism of Ji et al. (2024) to compute the two-photon exchange (TPE) contribution to the hyperfine splitting in deuterium using nucleon-nucleon interactions from chiral effective field theory at NLO, NNLO, and N3LO, employing the Bochum RS450 and Idaho potentials. They calculated the nuclear polarizability contributions using one-body charge and vector current operators, the elastic contributions using deuteron form factors from Piarulli et al. (2012) fitted to a dipole parameterization, and the single-nucleon TPE contributions using effective nucleon Zemach radii from Antognini et al. (2022) and Tomalak et al. (2019). Uncertainties from the chiral expansion truncation and neglected two-body currents were estimated using a Q-expansion with the pion mass scale of 140 MeV and breakdown scale of 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Ji et al. (2024), who calculated TPE effects in deuterium using pionless effective field theory at NNLO with 5-6% uncertainty, by employing chiral effective field theory which incorporates explicit pion exchanges and achieves an order-of-magnitude reduction in uncertainty. The results are consistent with earlier pionless EFT calculations and improve upon the closure approximation approaches of Friar (2005) and Kalinowski et al. (2018), which did not capture the correct energy-dependent weight in TPE calculations. This study lays the groundwork for extending TPE calculations to hyperfine splitting in heavier atomic systems, where the higher breakdown scale of chiral EFT (approximately 600 MeV versus about 1.4 times the pion mass for pionless EFT) makes it particularly well-suited.
