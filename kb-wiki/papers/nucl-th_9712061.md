# Dilepton from Disoriented Chiral Condensates

> Disoriented chiral condensates are manifested as long wavelength pionic oscillations and their interaction with the thermal environment can be a significant source of dileptons. We calculate the yield of such dilepton production within the linear sigma model and illustrate the basic features of the dilepton spectrum in a schematic model. We find that the dilepton yield with invariant mass near and below $2m_π$ due to the soft pion modes can be up to two orders of magnitude larger than the corresponding equilibrium yield. We conclude with a discussion on how this enhancement can be detected by present dilepton experiments.

## Key claim

Disoriented chiral condensates (DCCs), manifested as long-wavelength pionic oscillations, can produce dilepton yields with invariant mass near and below twice the pion mass that are up to two orders of magnitude larger than the corresponding equilibrium (thermal) yield, but this enhancement is confined to small dilepton momenta (below about 300-500 MeV) and is only observable if experimental acceptance cuts on lepton transverse momentum are relaxed sufficiently.

## Method

The authors calculated dilepton production from DCC fields using two distinct dynamical treatments of the linear sigma model: a quantum 1/N mean-field approximation and a semi-classical treatment. They compared the dilepton spectra from "quench" initial conditions (which produce a DCC state with enhanced low-momentum pion modes) against thermal initial conditions at the same energy density. They also developed a schematic model that factorizes the system into a thermal gas of hard pions and a dynamically evolving DCC field under Bjorken expansion, allowing separation of coherent and incoherent emission channels.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement factor of approximately 100 (two orders of magnitude) for dilepton yield from quench initial conditions compared to thermal initial conditions at invariant masses near 2m_pi (about 280 MeV)
- The enhancement is confined to dilepton momenta q <= 300-500 MeV
- In the schematic model, the initial temperature of the thermal environment was T_0 = 145 MeV, with characteristic DCC field energy density epsilon_0 = 58 MeV/fm^3
- For experimental observability, the CERES collaboration's transverse momentum cut of p_t > 200 MeV per lepton would hide the signal; reducing the cut to p_t > 60 MeV would make the signal visible
- The unstable modes grow exponentially for momenta k < |m_pi*|, where m_pi* is approximately 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of DCC formation via rapid cooling (quenching) by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993), and by Gavin, Gocksch, and Pisarski (1994), among others. It builds on prior predictions of enhanced photon emission from DCCs by Boyanovsky et al. (1995) and on dilepton production from collective isospin oscillations by Zhang, He, and Wang (1996). The paper enables experimental searches for DCC signals in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies, specifically suggesting that the CERES experiment could detect the enhancement if its lepton transverse momentum cuts are lowered. The authors note that the signal would compete primarily with eta-meson Dalitz decay background, as modeled in transport calculations by Koch and Soff (1996).
