# Path Integrals for Potential Scattering

> Two path integral representations for the $T$-matrix in nonrelativistic potential scattering are derived and proved to produce the complete Born series when expanded to all orders. They are obtained with the help of "phantom" degrees of freedom which take away explicit phases that diverge for asymptotic times. In addition, energy conservation is enforced by imposing a Faddeev-Popov-like constraint in the velocity path integral. These expressions may be useful for attempts to evaluate the path integral in real time and for alternative multiple scattering expansions. Standard and novel eikonal-type high-energy approximations and systematic expansions immediately follow.

## Key claim

The paper derives two exact path integral representations for the $T$-matrix in nonrelativistic potential scattering that produce the complete Born series when expanded to all orders, but the derivation relies on the assumption that the action is invariant under a time-translation transformation in the limit of infinite scattering times, which the author notes is nontrivial and requires more rigorous investigation.

## Method

Starting from the standard velocity path integral for the time-evolution operator, the author expresses the $S$-matrix as a functional integral over velocities and then eliminates a "dangerous phase" proportional to $T$ (the asymptotic time cutoff) by introducing "phantom" degrees of freedom with a wrong-sign kinetic term. Energy conservation is enforced by inserting a Faddeev-Popov-like constraint into the velocity path integral, which extracts the energy-conserving delta function and yields the $T$-matrix. Two versions are given: one with a three-dimensional antivelocity (the "3-3" representation) and one with only a longitudinal antivelocity (the "3-1" or "ray" representation), the latter obtained by shifting the impact parameter and velocities to produce a reference path that follows the initial momentum for negative times and the final momentum for positive times.

## Key numbers

* The dangerous phase that must be eliminated is $\mathbf{q}^2 T/(4m)$, where $\mathbf{q} = \mathbf{k}_f - \mathbf{k}_i$ is the momentum transfer and $T$ is the asymptotic time cutoff.
* The momentum transfer and mean momentum are defined as $\mathbf{q} = \mathbf{k}_f - \mathbf{k}_i$ and $\mathbf{K} = (\mathbf{k}_i + \mathbf{k}_f)/2$, with magnitudes $q = 2k \sin(\theta/2)$ and $K = k \cos(\theta/2)$ for scattering angle $\theta$.
* The Faddeev-Popov constraint factor is $|\mathbf{K}|/m$ times an integral over the gauge parameter $\tau$, producing the energy-conserving delta function $2\pi \, \delta(\mathbf{q} \cdot \mathbf{K}/m) = 2\pi \, \delta(k_f^2/(2m) - k_i^2/(2m))$.
* In the "ray" representation, the reference momentum is $\mathbf{p}_{\text{ray}}(t) = \mathbf{K} + (\mathbf{q}/2) \, \text{sgn}(t) = \mathbf{k}_i \, \Theta(-t) + \mathbf{k}_f \, \Theta(t)$, which has magnitude $k$ for all $t$.

## Context

This work extends earlier path integral approaches to potential scattering by Campbell et al. (CFJM) and others (BoDu, VaKu), which used phase-space path integrals, by developing a velocity path integral that reduces complexity. The author notes that previous attempts (Rosenfelder 2008, Kleinert) suffered from ambiguities in the order of eliminating dangerous phases and enforcing energy conservation. The new representations may enable real-time evaluation of path integrals by stochastic methods and provide alternative multiple scattering expansions, with immediate applications to eikonal-type high-energy approximations and systematic expansions beyond the standard Glauber approach.
