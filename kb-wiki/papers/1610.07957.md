# Analytical On-shell Calculation of Higher Order Scattering: Massive Particles

> We demonstrate that the use of on-shell methods, involving calculation of the discontinuity across the t-channel cut associated with the exchange of a pair of massless particles, can be used to evaluate loop contributions to both the electromagnetic and gravitational scattering of massive systems. In the gravitational case, the use of factorization permits a straightforward and algebraic calculation of higher order scattering results, which were obtained previously by much more arduous Feynman diagram techniques.

## Key claim

On-shell methods, using the discontinuity across the t-channel two-photon or two-graviton cut, can evaluate loop contributions to electromagnetic and gravitational scattering of massive systems more simply than conventional Feynman diagram techniques, but the resulting classical potential depends on the specific form of the lowest-order potential and propagator used for the Born subtraction, with the quantum piece remaining unchanged.

## Method

The discontinuity across the t-channel two-particle cut is computed using unitarity, which requires the product of tree-level Compton annihilation amplitudes for massive scalar particles. For the gravitational case, the graviton Compton amplitudes are obtained via factorization as a product of two electromagnetic Compton amplitudes multiplied by a kinematic factor, avoiding the need to evaluate bubble, triangle, box, cross-box, triple-graviton vertex, and vacuum polarization diagrams. The angular integrals resulting from the phase-space integration are evaluated analytically, and the imaginary (Born iteration) component is subtracted to define the higher-order potential.

## Key numbers

- Electromagnetic second-order potential: \( V_2^{em}(r) = -\frac{\alpha_{em}^2(m_A+m_B)}{2 m_A m_B r^2} - \frac{7\alpha_{em}^2\hbar}{6\pi m_A m_B r^3} \)
- Charged-neutral polarizable potential: \( V_N(r) = -\frac{\alpha_{em}\alpha_E^A}{2 r^4} + \alpha_{em}\frac{(11\alpha_E^A+5\beta_M^A)\hbar}{4\pi m_B r^5} \)
- Neutral-neutral (Casimir-Polder) potential: \( V_{NN}(r) = \frac{-23(\alpha_E^A\alpha_E^B+\beta_M^A\beta_M^B)+7(\alpha_E^A\beta_M^B+\alpha_E^B\beta_M^A)}{4\pi r^7} \)
- Gravitational second-order amplitude: \( \text{Amp}_2^{grav}(q) = -\frac{\kappa^4 m_A m_B}{1024\pi^2}\left[\frac{41}{5}L - 6S(m_A+m_B) + 4\pi i\frac{m_A m_B m_r}{p_0 t}L + \ldots\right] \), where \( L = \log(-t) \) and \( S = \pi^2/\sqrt{-t} \)

## Context

This work extends the on-shell dispersive method of Feinberg and Sucher (1988) for electromagnetic scattering to the gravitational case, where previous calculations by Donoghue (1994), Muzinich and Vokos (1995), Hamber and Liu (1995), Akhoury et al. (1997), Khriplovich and Kirilin (2003), and Bjerrum-Bohr et al. (2003) required arduous Feynman diagram techniques and contained numerical errors in early attempts. The factorization property used for graviton amplitudes was noted by Choudhury (1995), Bern (2002), and Holstein (2006). The method enables straightforward algebraic calculation of higher-order gravitational scattering results and can be extended to polarizable systems and to higher orders.
