# The reactions p + n --> d + omega and p + n --> d + phi near threshold

> The reactions p+n-->d+omega and p+n-->d+phi are studied within a relativistic meson-exchange model of hadronic interactions. Predictions for the total cross sections and for the angular distributions of the vector mesons are presented. The resulting cross sections near threshold are around 10 - 30 microb for p+n-->d+omega$ and 200 - 250 nb for p+n-->d+phi. A moderate deviation of the cross section ratio sigma_{p+n-->d+phi} / sigma_{p+n-->d+omega} from that of the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka rule is predicted.

## Key claim

The paper presents genuine predictions for the total cross sections and angular distributions of the reactions p + n → d + ω and p + n → d + φ near threshold, based on a relativistic meson-exchange model whose parameters were fixed in a previous study of pp → ppω and pp → ppφ. The authors predict a moderate deviation of the cross section ratio σ(pn→dφ)/σ(pn→dω) from the Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka (OZI) rule, but emphasize that this deviation arises from conventional dynamical effects (interferences, energy dependence) rather than requiring an exotic mechanism such as intrinsic strangeness in the nucleon.

## Method

The authors employ a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) approach where the transition amplitude is given by the matrix element of a current operator between the initial unperturbed NN wave function and the final deuteron wave function (Bonn B model). The current operator consists of two mechanisms: a nucleonic current (involving NNv vertices with vector and tensor couplings) and a vρπ meson-exchange current, with all parameters fixed from a combined analysis of near-threshold data on pp → ppω and pp → ppφ. The initial-state interaction is not treated explicitly but accounted for effectively via adjustment of phenomenological form factors at hadronic vertices.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections near threshold: 10–30 μb for p + n → d + ω; 200–250 nb for p + n → d + φ.
- For the reaction p + n → d + φ, the meson-exchange current dominates; the nucleonic current contributes only about 3% of the total cross section.
- For p + n → d + ω, predictions from the four parameter sets vary by up to a factor of roughly 3 in total cross section.
- Cross section ratio R(φ/ω) = σ(pn→dφ)/σ(pn→dω) at excess energy Q ≈ 10 MeV: between 9 × 10⁻³ (set 3) and 32 × 10⁻³ (set 2), compared to the naive OZI estimate of 4.2 × 10⁻³.
- At higher excess energies Q = 80–100 MeV, the enhancement over the OZI estimate decreases to a factor of around 3.
- The enhancement over the OZI estimate for the ratio in pp → ppv at Q = 80 MeV is also around a factor of 3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous meson-exchange model for pp → ppω and pp → ppφ (Nakayama, Haidenbauer, Speth, 1999) to the reactions pn → dω and pn → dφ, providing genuine predictions for experiments planned at the COSY facility in Jülich. The results contrast with the qualitative model of Ellis et al. (1995, 1999), which assumes an intrinsic s̄s component in the nucleon and predicts that φ production in pn → dφ should be OZI-suppressed (since it proceeds from a ¹P₁ initial state), whereas the present model finds comparable cross section ratios for pp → ppv and pn → dv reactions. The paper cautions against drawing direct conclusions on the strangeness content of the nucleon from such cross section ratios, as dynamical effects within conventional hadronic models can produce deviations from the OZI rule of up to a factor of 8.
