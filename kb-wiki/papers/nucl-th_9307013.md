# Parity-Violating Electron Scattering from the Pion-Correlated Relativistic Fermi Gas

> Parity-violating quasielastic electron scattering is studied within the context of the relativistic Fermi gas and its extensions to include the effects of pionic correlations and meson-exchange currents. The work builds on previous studies using the same model; here the part of the parity-violating asymmetry that contains axial-vector hadronic currents is developed in detail using those previous studies and a link is provided to the transverse vector-isovector response. Various integrated observables are constructed from the differential asymmetry. These include an asymmetry averaged over the quasielastic peak, as well as the difference of the asymmetry integrated to the left and right of the peak -- the latter is shown to be optimal for bringing out the nature of the pionic correlations. Special weighted integrals involving the differential asymmetry and electromagnetic cross section, based on the concepts of y-scaling and sum rules, are constructed and shown to be suited to studies of the single-nucleon form factor content in the problem, in particular, to determinations of the isovector/axial-vector and electric strangeness form factors. Comparisons are also made with recent predictions made on the basis of relativistic mean-field theory.

## Key claim

The parity-violating asymmetry in quasielastic electron scattering can be used to isolate either nuclear pionic correlation effects or single-nucleon form factor information, depending on how the energy integration of the differential asymmetry is performed; the authors show that the difference of the asymmetry integrated to the left and right of the quasielastic peak (Δ𝒜) is optimal for revealing pionic correlations, while the energy-averaged asymmetry (𝒜̅) and the weighted integrals ℛ₁ and ℛ₂ suppress nuclear structure uncertainties and are suited to extracting the axial-vector and strangeness form factors of the nucleon.

## Method

The authors calculate the parity-violating helicity asymmetry within the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model extended to include pionic correlations and meson-exchange currents (MECs) via selected perturbative diagrams, respecting gauge invariance. They derive the axial-vector nuclear response R^{T'}_{VA} using a non-relativistic link to the isovector transverse electromagnetic response that holds to better than 2% in the momentum range 300 MeV/c < q < 1 GeV/c, and then compute the full asymmetry from the five electroweak response functions. Various integrated observables are constructed: Δ𝒜 (difference of left/right integrals about the quasielastic peak), 𝒜̅ (energy-averaged asymmetry), and ℛ₁, ℛ₂ (weighted integrals based on y-scaling and sum rules).

## Key numbers

- The non-relativistic link R^{T'}_{VA} ≈ a_V (G_A^{(1)}/G_M^{(1)}) √((τ+1)/τ) R^{T(1)} holds to better than 2% for momentum transfers 300 MeV/c < q < 1 GeV/c.
- The fractional precision in Δ𝒜 is |2𝒜̅/Δ𝒜| times the fractional precision in 𝒜̅, where |2𝒜̅/Δ𝒜| ranges from about 1–7 in the forward direction and 10–20 in the backward direction as q goes from 300 MeV/c to 1 GeV/c.
- The Fermi momentum is set to p_F = 225 MeV/c, corresponding roughly to ¹²C.
- The vector dipole form factor parameter λ_D^V ≈ 4.97; the axial-vector dipole parameter λ_D^A ≈ 3.53; the axial coupling constant g_A^{(1)} = 1.26; sin²θ_W ≈ 0.227.
- The electric strangeness form factor strength ρ_s is varied in the range 0 to −3, and the magnetic strangeness strength μ_s in the range 0 to −1.
- The Galster parameterization for G_En uses λ_n = 5.6.
- The quasielastic peak energy is ω_QEP = |Q²|/2m_N.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of electromagnetic and weak neutral current vector responses in the pion-correlated RFG (Barbaro et al., 1993) by now including the axial-vector response in detail and combining all responses to predict the parity-violating asymmetry. It provides a theoretical framework for interpreting planned PV electron scattering experiments at MIT/Bates, Mainz, and CEBAF, and compares with predictions from relativistic mean-field theory (Horowitz et al., 1993). The integrated observables Δ𝒜, 𝒜̅, ℛ₁, and ℛ₂ are designed to separately access nuclear dynamics (pionic correlations) and nucleonic physics (axial-vector and strangeness form factors), enabling future extractions of the latter with controlled nuclear-structure uncertainties.
