# Rotating compact star with superconducting quark matter

> A compact star with superconducting quark core, the hadron crust and the mixed phase between the two is considered. The quark meson coupling model for hadron matter and the color flavor locked quark model for quark matter is used in order to construct the equation of state for the compact star. The effect of pairing of quarks in the color flavor locked phase and the mixed phase on the mass, radius, and period of the rotating star is studied.

# Rotating compact star with superconducting quark matter

## Key claim

The paper claims that hybrid compact stars with a mixed phase of hadronic and color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter favor low pairing gaps and high bag pressures, and that no pure CFL quark matter core can exist on stability grounds, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific equation of state (QMC for hadrons, CFL for quarks) and the parameter ranges considered (gap parameter Δ = 0–150 MeV, bag pressure B^{1/4} = 210 MeV fixed for most results).

## Method

The authors construct an equation of state for hybrid compact stars using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for the hadron phase (including hyperons) and the color-flavor locked (CFL) model for the quark phase, with a mixed phase determined by Gibbs criteria and global charge neutrality. They then solve the Einstein field equations for rapidly rotating relativistic stars using the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu (KEH) method, computing mass, radius, angular velocity, and period as functions of the pairing gap Δ and bag pressure B.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for Δ = 150 MeV: 1.61 M⊙, with corresponding radius 18.54 km
- Maximum mass for Δ = 0 MeV: 2.35 M⊙, with corresponding radius 16.47 km
- Compactness ratio M_max/R_max: 0.142 M⊙/km for Δ = 0 MeV; 0.086 M⊙/km for Δ = 150 MeV
- For fixed Δ = 100 MeV and B^{1/4} = 190 MeV: central energy density 7.95 × 10^14 g cm^{-3}, M_max = 1.32 M⊙, R_max = 18.8 km
- For fixed Δ = 100 MeV and B^{1/4} = 210 MeV: central energy density 1.29 × 10^15 g cm^{-3}, M_max = 2.17 M⊙, R_max = 17.4 km
- Angular velocity at M_max for Δ = 150 MeV: 5.5 × 10^3 s^{-1}
- Angular velocity at M_max for Δ = 0 MeV: 7.7 × 10^3 s^{-1}
- Period range for Δ = 0–150 MeV: 0.8 to 1.2 milliseconds
- Nuclear matter saturation properties used: binding energy −15.75 MeV at ρ₀ = 0.15 fm^{-3}, symmetry energy 32.5 MeV, incompressibility 257 MeV, effective nucleon mass 0.774 M

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hybrid stars by combining the QMC model for hadronic matter (developed by Guichon and collaborators) with the CFL phase of superconducting quark matter (studied by Alford, Rajagopal, and others). It contrasts with earlier results by Lugones and collaborators who found increasing maximum mass with pairing gap for pure CFL stars; the authors note their results for hybrid stars show the opposite trend. The paper enables further comparison with observations of fast pulsars such as PSR B1937+21 (period 1.55 ms, angular velocity 4033 s^{-1}) and PSR B1957+20 (period 1.6 ms, angular velocity 3908 s^{-1}), and with the mass measurement of Vela X-1 (1.86 ± 0.32 M⊙) by van Kerkwijk et al.
