# Electromagnetic Nucleon-to-Delta Transition in Holographic QCD

> We study nucleon-to-delta electromagnetic transition form factors and relations between them within the framework of holographic dual model of QCD proposed by Sakai and Sugimoto. In this setup, baryons appear as topological solitons of the five dimensional holographic gauge theory that describes a tower of mesons and their interactions. We find a relativistic extension of the nucleon-delta-vector meson interaction vertices and use these to calculate transition form factors from holographic QCD. We observe that at low momentum transfer, magnetic dipole, electric and Coulomb quadrupole form factors and their ratios follow the patterns expected in the large N_c limit. Our results at this approximation are in reasonable agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper finds that within the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model, the nucleon-to-delta electromagnetic transition form factors (magnetic dipole, electric quadrupole, and Coulomb quadrupole) all depend on a single holographic form factor, leading to ratios that follow the patterns expected in the large \(N_c\) limit, with results in reasonable agreement with experiment only at low momentum transfer and within the approximation of the model.

## Method

The authors start from the nonrelativistic nucleon-delta-vector meson vertices derived by Park and Yi (2008) within the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model, where baryons appear as topological solitons of the five-dimensional gauge theory. They then propose a relativistic generalization of these vertices consistent with four-dimensional Rarita-Schwinger formalism for spin-3/2 particles, and use the holographic vector meson dominance feature to compute the transition form factors via tree-level exchanges of a tower of vector mesons. The calculation integrates over the holographic fifth dimension \(Z\) and uses the mode expansion of the five-dimensional gauge field to extract the four-dimensional form factors.

## Key numbers

- The model uses the scale \(M_{KK} = 0.949\) GeV, chosen to reproduce the experimental \(\rho\)-meson mass.
- The coupling constant for the nonrelativistic \(N\Delta\) vertex is \(k_{1/2} = \sqrt{15}/(4 M_{KK})\) after replacing \(N_c\) by \(N_c+2\).
- The \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance decays via \(\Delta \to N\pi\) with 99% branching ratio, and less than 1% of the total decay width comes from the electromagnetic channel \(\Delta \to N\gamma\).
- The ratios \(R_{EM} = E2/M1\) and \(R_{SM} = C2/M1\) are at the level of a few percent at moderate momentum transfers \(Q^2\), with the \(E2/M1\) ratio negative and very close to zero for energies up to \(Q^2 \sim 4\) GeV\(^2\) experimentally.
- In the large \(N_c\) limit, the \(E2/M1\) ratio is of order \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach of Sakai and Sugimoto (2005) to the nucleon-to-delta electromagnetic transition, building on the nonrelativistic baryon treatment of Park and Yi (2008) and the related approach by Hashimoto, Sakai, and Sugimoto (2008). It provides a holographic counterpart to studies using the nonrelativistic SU(6) quark model (Becchi, 1965), relativistic quark models (Aznaurian, 1993), large \(N_c\) QCD (Jenkins, 2002), QCD sum rules (Belyaev, 1995; Braun, 2005; Wang, 2009), and lattice calculations (Alexandrou, 2004). The results enable further tests of holographic QCD predictions for baryon structure and can be extended to study photoproduction processes and other observables.
