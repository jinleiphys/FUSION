# Stochastic approach to correlations beyond the mean field with the Skyrme interaction

> Large-scale calculation based on the multi-configuration Skyrme density functional theory is performed for the light N=Z even-even nucleus, 12C. Stochastic procedures and the imaginary-time evolution are utilized to prepare many Slater determinants. Each state is projected on eigenstates of parity and angular momentum. Then, performing the configuration mixing calculation with the Skyrme Hamiltonian, we obtain low-lying energy-eigenstates and their explicit wave functions. The generated wave functions are completely free from any assumption and symmetry restriction. Excitation spectra and transition probabilities are well reproduced, not only for the ground-state band, but for negative-parity excited states and the Hoyle state.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a stochastic multi-configuration approach using the Skyrme density functional theory, without any pre-assumed cluster configurations or symmetry restrictions, can reproduce low-lying excitation spectra and transition probabilities in 12C, including the Hoyle state, though negative-parity excitation energies come out too high by a few MeV.

## Method

The authors stochastically generate initial Slater determinants using random numbers, then apply imaginary-time evolution to remove high-momentum components inconsistent with the Skyrme interaction (SLy4 parameter set). Each Slater determinant is projected onto eigenstates of parity and angular momentum via full 3D rotation, and the generalized eigenvalue problem is solved in each parity and angular momentum sector to obtain energy eigenstates. The calculation uses a 3D Cartesian mesh representation with a sphere of radius 8 fm and mesh spacing of 0.8 fm.

## Key numbers

- B(E2;2_1+ → 0_1+) = 8.6 e²fm⁴ (calculated) versus 7.6 ± 0.4 e²fm⁴ (experimental)
- B(E2;4_1+ → 2_1+) = 13.4 e²fm⁴ (calculated)
- B(E2;0_2+ → 2_1+) = 13.6 e²fm⁴ (calculated) versus 13 ± 2 e²fm⁴ (experimental)
- M(E0) transition matrix element between ground state and Hoyle state: 4.5 fm² (calculated) versus 5.4 ± 0.2 fm² (experimental)
- Third 0+ state at excitation energy approximately 15 MeV
- Angular momentum states calculated only for J < 6 due to numerical difficulty

## Context

This work extends the stochastic approach previously applied to the simple BKN energy functional (Fukuoka et al. 2006) to the realistic Skyrme interaction with the SLy4 parameter set. It contrasts with traditional cluster-model approaches such as the resonating group method (RGM), generator coordinate method (GCM), and orthogonal condition model (OCM), which assume cluster configurations from the beginning. The method enables fully unconstrained description of cluster correlations, shape fluctuations, and shape mixing in light nuclei without pre-selecting a generator coordinate space, though it requires heavy computational resources.
