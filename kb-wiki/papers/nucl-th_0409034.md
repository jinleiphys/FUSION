# PCAC Relation and Pion Production-Absorption in Nuclei

> Nuclear PCAC relation is studied in the framework of the effective theory of nuclear interaction, in which the interaction of real pion production-absorption is expressed by many-body operators, and does not include the one-nucleon operator as was assumed in the conventional works, while the effective axial-vector current includes the one-nucleon current in contrast to the former interaction. This problem is investigated under the simple linear $σ$-model. Results are as folows: 1) The theory describes consistently the PCAC relation and the pion production-absorption process. 2) The conventional interpretation of the effective pion source function as the interaction Hamiltonian of pion production-absorption does not hold. 3) The effective pion source function still includes the one-nucleon operator for the pion production-absorption at threshold effectively, which may justify the conventional theory.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the absence of a one-nucleon operator for pion production-absorption in the effective nuclear Hamiltonian does not contradict the PCAC relation, provided the nuclear system is described with explicit meson degrees of freedom and a consistent treatment of the nuclear force, axial-vector current, and pion production operator. The effective pion source function still effectively includes a one-nucleon operator for pion production-absorption at threshold, which may justify the conventional theory.

## Method

The authors adopt the linear σ-model, which satisfies the PCAC relation, and derive the effective nuclear Hamiltonian, pion production-absorption operator, and nuclear axial-vector current by applying a unitary transformation that eliminates "virtual" processes while keeping "real" processes. The analysis is restricted to the one-boson-exchange approximation, and the PCAC relation is examined in the two-nucleon system by connecting the matrix element of the divergence of the axial-vector current to the pion production T-matrix via the reduction formula.

## Key numbers

- The Goldberger-Treiman relation is given as \(gf_\pi = M_N\) with \(g_A = 1\).
- The pion production operator at threshold in the center-of-mass system is expressed as \((W_\pi + W_\sigma)_+ = \frac{1}{m_\pi}[H_{\pi NN}, V_{NN}]\), where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass.
- The energy conservation condition at threshold is \(E_i - E_f = m_\pi\).
- The meson-exchange current includes a new pair-current term \(D_{3-}\) expressed as a commutator of the nucleon-current and the operator \(S'\) of the unitary transformation.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kobayashi et al. (1997) and Shebeko and Shirokov (2000), who argued that the one-nucleon operator for pion production-absorption does not exist in the effective nuclear interaction Hamiltonian when the one-pion-exchange potential is introduced. The paper contradicts the earlier assumption by Blin-Stoyle and Tint (1967) that the two-nucleon axial-vector current is simply connected to pion production-absorption by two-nucleons, showing instead that the PCAC relation for the two-nucleon axial-vector current is not a simple relation. The results enable a consistent framework for constructing nuclear forces, pion production operators, and exchange currents that satisfy the PCAC relation, and point toward incorporating short-range mechanisms based on chiral symmetry.
