# What is the structure of the Roper resonance?

> We investigate the structure of the nucleon resonance N^*(1440) (Roper) within a coupled-channel meson exchange model for pion-nucleon scattering. The coupling to pipiN states is realized effectively by the coupling to the sigmaN, piDelta and rhoN channels. The interaction within and between these channels is derived from an effective Lagrangian based on a chirally symmetric Lagrangian, which is supplemented by well known terms for the coupling of the Delta isobar, the omega meson and the 'sigma', which is the name given here to the strong correlation of two pions in the scalar-isoscalar channel. In this model the Roper resonance can be described by meson-baryon dynamics alone; no genuine N^*(1440) (3 quark) resonance is needed in order to fit piN phase shifts and inelasticities.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance N*(1440) can be described entirely by meson-baryon dynamics within a coupled-channel meson exchange model; no genuine three-quark resonance state is required to fit pion-nucleon phase shifts and inelasticities, provided the full Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved rather than using a K-matrix approximation.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel meson exchange model solving the full Lippmann-Schwinger equation for pion-nucleon scattering up to 1.9 GeV, coupling the πN, σN, πΔ, ρN, and ηN channels. The interaction potentials were derived from an effective Lagrangian based on chiral symmetry, supplemented by terms for the Δ isobar, ω meson, and σ (the strong correlation of two pions in the scalar-isoscalar channel). The double counting problem from including both correlated ππ exchange and π exchange diagrams was avoided by subtracting the box diagram from the N̄N → ππ amplitudes using a microscopic model for those amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The Roper resonance pole position from the model lies approximately 100 MeV below the Breit-Wigner mass parameter of 1440 MeV, with the ratio -Γ/Im(Pole) ≈ 5 instead of the expected value of 2 for an undistorted resonance
- The σ channel contribution to the N̄N → ππ amplitudes was reduced by approximately 20% after subtracting the box diagram to avoid double counting
- The ρ meson self-energy parameters used were g²ρππ/4π = 2.9, Λρ = 1.8 GeV, and mρ⁰ = 911 MeV
- The model describes πN data up to energies of about 1.9 GeV

## Context

This work extends the model of Schütz et al. (1998), which described πN partial waves up to 1.6 GeV by coupling πN, σN, πΔ, and ηN channels, by adding the ρN channel and including previously omitted π exchange diagrams with proper double-counting subtraction. The results contradict the assumption in quark models such as those of Isgur and Karl (1978) and Capstick (1986) that the Roper requires a three-quark radial excitation, and instead support the interpretation that the resonance is dynamically generated by meson-baryon interactions. The model enables future investigation of whether other nucleon resonances can similarly be described without intrinsic quark degrees of freedom.
