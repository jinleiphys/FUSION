# From Stopping to Viscosity in Nuclear Reactions

> Data on stopping in intermediate-energy central heavy-ion collisions are analyzed following transport theory based on the Boltzmann equation. In consequence, values of nuclear shear viscosity are inferred. The inferred values are significantly larger than obtained for free nucleon dispersion relations and free nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of nuclear matter inferred from stopping data in central heavy-ion collisions is significantly larger than values obtained using free nucleon-nucleon cross sections and free dispersion relations, but the stopping data alone do not uniquely constrain the in-medium cross-section reduction mechanism.

## Method

The authors analyzed data on stopping in central heavy-ion collisions using a Boltzmann-equation transport model for nucleon Wigner functions. They compared model predictions for the isotropization observable $vartl$ and linear momentum transfer (LMT) to experimental data from the FOPI, INDRA, and other collaborations, adjusting the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections using a parameterization with a density-dependent saturation limit $\sigma_0 = \nu n^{-2/3}$. The shear viscosity was then extracted from the Boltzmann equation using the Chapman-Enskog method, with cross sections weighted by $p_{12}^4 \sin^2\theta$ to emphasize collisions relevant for momentum dissipation.

## Key numbers

- For Au + Au collisions, the $vartl$ observable reaches values $\gtrsim 0.85$ at beam energies of 400-800 MeV/nucleon, indicating proximity to isotropy.
- The in-medium cross-section parameter $\nu = 0.6$ in the saturation formula $\sigma_0 = \nu n^{-2/3}$ best reproduces the Au + Au stopping data in the energy region 250-800 MeV/nucleon.
- A simple mean-free-path estimate yields a shear viscosity of approximately 30 MeV/fm$^2c$ using representative values of $n = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, $m = 939$ MeV/$c^2$, $v_\text{kin} = 0.3c$, and $\lambda = 2$ fm.
- For the Ar + Ag reaction at 90 MeV/nucleon, the net collision number drops by 25% for Rostock in-medium cross sections and by a factor of 4 for $\nu = 1$ cross sections, compared to free cross sections.

## Context

This work extends prior transport analyses of heavy-ion collisions by Danielewicz (1999) and others that extracted equilibrium properties such as energy, pressure, and optical potential from reaction data. The authors directly compare their Boltzmann-equation results to stopping data from the FOPI Collaboration (Reisdorf, 2004) and linear momentum transfer measurements (PhysRevC.57.R1032, 1998). The in-medium cross-section calculations from the Rostock group (PhysRevC.55.3006, 1997; PhysRevC.57.806, 1998) are tested but found to still overestimate stopping. The extracted viscosity values are relevant for assessing whether nuclear matter approaches the limit of a near-perfect liquid with increasing temperature, as discussed by Lacey (2006).
