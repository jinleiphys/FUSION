# Nucleon mean free path in nuclear matter based on nuclear Schwinger-Dyson formalism

> A mean free path of nucleon moving through nuclear matter with kinetic energy of more than 100MeV is formulated based on the bare vertex nuclear Schwinger-Dyson (BNSD) method in the Walecka model. The self-energy which is derived from the higher order diagrams more than the forth order includes the Feynman part of propagator of energetic nucleon and grows up rapidly as an increase of kinetic energy. To avoid too large growth of these diagrams, meson propagators are modified by introducing some form factors to take account of a internal structure of hadron. It is confirmed that the mean free path calculated by the BNSD method agrees good with experimental data if a reasonable form factor is chosen, i.e., a dipole (quadrupole) type of form factor with a cut-off parameter about 750 MeV $\sim$ 1000 MeV (1200 MeV $\sim$ 1500 MeV).

## Key claim

The nucleon mean free path calculated using the bare vertex nuclear Schwinger-Dyson (BNSD) method in the Walecka model agrees with experimental data only after meson propagators are modified by introducing form factors (dipole or quadrupole type) to suppress the unphysically large growth of higher-order self-energy diagrams, with the specific cut-off parameters chosen to match the data.

## Method

The authors formulated the nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter using the BNSD method within the sigma-omega (Walecka) model, including Hartree, Fock, and higher-order diagrams beyond fourth order. They computed the real and imaginary parts of the scalar and vector self-energy components for nucleons with kinetic energy above 100 MeV, then converted these into Schrodinger-equivalent optical potentials. To tame the unphysical growth of the Feynman part of the self-energy, they introduced monopole, dipole, or quadrupole form factors at the meson-nucleon vertices, modifying the meson propagators as D(q^2) -> [F(q^2)]^2 * D(q^2) with F(q^2) = 1/[1+q^2/Lambda^2]^n. The mean free path was then obtained from the imaginary part of the nucleon momentum via the relation lambda = 1/(2k_I).

## Key numbers

* Normal nuclear matter density used: rho = 0.170 fm^{-3} (k_F = 1.36 fm^{-1}) and rho = 0.193 fm^{-3} (k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1}).
* Coupling constants at rho = 0.170 fm^{-3}: g_s = 9.59, g_v = 11.67, with sigma meson mass m_s = 550 MeV and omega meson mass m_v = 783 MeV.
* Dipole-type form factor with cut-off Lambda = 750 MeV yields mean free path values in good agreement with experimental data.
* Reasonable form factor range for dipole type: Lambda = 750 MeV to 1000 MeV; for quadrupole type: Lambda = 1200 MeV to 1500 MeV.
* The imaginary part of the scalar self-energy changes sign from positive to negative at kinetic energy E-M approximately 350 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BNSD studies of nuclear matter saturation properties (Nakano et al., 1994) and optical potentials near the Fermi surface (Hasegawa et al., 1995) to the regime of energetic nucleons far above the Fermi surface. It addresses the unphysical growth of higher-order Feynman diagrams previously noted in calculations of the energy density (Bedau and Beck, 1993; Ji, 1988; Furnstahl, Perry, and Serot, 1990) and follows the approach of Prakash, Ellis, and Kapusta (1992) and MacNeil, Price, and Shepard (1993) in introducing form factors to account for hadron internal structure. The results enable improved relativistic calculations of nucleon-nucleus scattering at intermediate energies and highlight the need to further study the role of vacuum effects in the meson self-energy at high momentum transfer.
