# Application of the constituent quark exchange model to the parton distributions and the EMC ratios of $^{12}C$ and $^{14}N$ nuclei

> The quark exchange model (QEM) is reformulated for the $A=12$ and $14$ systems to obtain the constituent quark distributions of $^{12}C$ and $^{14}N$ nuclei, respectively. Afterwards, the different types of the point-like parton distribution functions (PDFs), i.e., the valence quarks, the sea quarks and the gluons, are extracted from the constituent quark model (CQM), at the hadronic scale $Q_0^2=0.34$ $GeV^2$ to generate the constituent quark exchange model (CQEM = QEM $\oplus$ CQM) PDFs. From the resulted PDFs, the structure functions and the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) ratios of the above-mentioned nuclei at the appropriate hadronic scale are calculated. To make our results more comparable with the available experimental data, we evolve the PDFs, by using the standard DGALP evolution equations, to the higher hard scales. Subsequently, the EMC ratios of $^{12}C$ and $^{14}N$ nuclei at the some higher energy scales, corresponding to the scales of available data, are calculated, at the leading (LO) and the next-to-leading (NLO) orders of pQCD. By doing so, it is observed that the EMC ratios do not significantly depend on the hard scale $Q^2$, and the outcomes are consistent with the various experimental data such as HERMES, BCDMS, JLab, SLAC, NMC, and EMC. Especially, in the mid-range of Bjorken $x$ values, the results are so desirable. Like our previous works for the $^4He$ and $^6Li$ nuclei, we again observe that at a fixed scale $Q^2$, the LO and the NLO EMC ratios with high precision are approximately the same. Therefore, one can conclude that at a given hard scale, the LO approximation is good enough for calculating the EMC ratios of light nuclei.

# Application of the constituent quark exchange model to the parton distributions and the EMC ratios of $^{12}C$ and $^{14}N$ nuclei

## Key claim
The constituent quark exchange model (CQEM), when applied to $^{12}C$ and $^{14}N$ nuclei, produces EMC ratios that are consistent with experimental data from HERMES, BCDMS, JLab, SLAC, NMC, and EMC, particularly in the mid-range of Bjorken $x$ values. However, the results are not valid at large $x$ ($x \geq 0.8$) because Fermi motion is excluded, and at small $x$ ($x \leq 0.1$) the agreement is incomplete due to omission of shadowing and unintegrated parton distribution function (UPDF) contributions.

## Method
The quark exchange model (QEM) was reformulated for A=12 and A=14 systems to obtain constituent quark momentum distributions for $^{12}C$ and $^{14}N$ nuclei, using a Gaussian nucleon wave function with nucleon radius $b$ and assuming uniform nuclear matter density. Point-like parton distribution functions (PDFs) for valence quarks, sea quarks, and gluons were then extracted at the hadronic scale $Q_0^2 = 0.34$ GeV$^2$ by combining the QEM with the constituent quark model (CQM), using the convolution of constituent quark distributions with splitting functions $\phi_{q_1 q_2}(x)$. The PDFs were evolved to higher hard scales using DGLAP evolution equations via the QCDNUM code, and the structure functions and EMC ratios were calculated at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) of pQCD.

## Key numbers
- Hadronic scale used: $Q_0^2 = 0.34$ GeV$^2$
- Nucleon radius values tested: $b = 0.7$, $0.8$, and $0.9$ fm
- Constituent quark mass and binding energy for $^{12}C$: $(m_a, \epsilon_0^a) = (325, 140$ MeV$)$
- Constituent quark mass and binding energy for $^{14}N$: $(m_a, \epsilon_0^a) = (330, 140$ MeV$)$
- CQM parameters at hadronic scale: $A = 0.435$, $B = 0.378$, $C = 0.05$, $D = 2.778$, $G = 0.135$
- Momentum fractions at hadronic scale: 53.5% carried by valence quarks, 35.7% by gluons, remainder by sea quarks
- QCD scale parameter: $\Lambda = 0.248$ GeV
- EMC ratios calculated for $x \leq 0.75$ due to exclusion of Fermi motion
- Quark number conservation verified: $\int \rho(k) d\vec{k} = n/2$ for $n = 12$ or $14$

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous applications of the constituent quark exchange model to $^4He$ (Hadian and Modarres, 2020) and $^6Li$ (Hadian and Modarres, 2019-2020) nuclei, now reformulating the QEM for the heavier A=12 and A=14 systems. The approach builds on the original quark exchange model established by Hoodbhoy and Jaffe for three-nucleon systems. The results enable comparison of EMC ratios across light nuclei from $^4He$ through $^{14}N$, showing that increasing nucleon number leads to greater deviation of the EMC ratio from unity due to increased quark exchange probability. The authors note that future work should incorporate Fermi motion effects for large $x$ and UPDF contributions for small $x$ to improve agreement with experimental data.
