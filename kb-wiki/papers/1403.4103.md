# A Laplace-like formula for the energy dependence of the nuclear level density parameter

> Collective effects in the level density are not well understood, and including these effects as enhancement factors to the level density does not produce sufficiently consistent predictions of observables. Therefore, collective effects are investigated in the level density parameter instead of treating them as a final factor in the level density. A new Laplace-like formula is proposed for the energy dependence of the level density parameter, including collective effects. A significant improvement has been achieved in agreement between observed and predicted energy levels. This new model can also be used in both structure and reaction calculations of the nuclei far from stability, especially near the drip lines.

# A Laplace-like formula for the energy dependence of the nuclear level density parameter

## Key claim

The authors propose that collective effects in nuclear level density should be incorporated directly into the level density parameter rather than treated as multiplicative enhancement factors to the level density, and they introduce a new Laplace-like formula for the energy dependence of this parameter. The claim is hedged by the observation that the collective amplitude depends strongly on the shape-dependent mass formula and experimental mass data, and that the model's success is demonstrated primarily for nuclei with sufficient discrete level scheme information (1136 nuclei) and mean resonance spacing data (289 stable nuclei).

## Method

The authors compute the asymptotic level density parameter semi-classically from single-particle level densities at corrected Fermi energies, using a potential that includes harmonic oscillator and Coulomb terms. They then fit the energy-dependent level density parameter to experimental data for each nucleus, revealing a Laplacian (double-exponential) shape rather than the exponential decay of Ignatyuk's formula. A new formula is constructed with a Laplace distribution centered at the energy of the first 2+ state (0.2 ℏω), with a scale parameter derived from the spin cut-off parameter at a critical temperature defined by the neutron separation energy, and a collective amplitude given by the shape-dependent shell correction energy at that critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- The peak of the level density parameter occurs around 2 MeV excitation energy, corresponding to the first phonon state from vibrational motion.
- The location of the peak is given by E₀ = 0.2 ℏω, where ℏω = 41/A^(1/3) MeV.
- The charge radius is given by R_C = 1.169 A^(0.291) fm, obtained from a fit to nuclear charge radii data.
- For the local calculation, the rms deviation factor f_rms = 1.34 for 289 nuclei and the average goodness-of-fit estimator for discrete levels f_lev = 0.98 for 1136 nuclei.
- For the global calculation, f_rms = 1.53 and f_lev = 1.32.
- The ratio of predicted to observed mean resonance spacings lies in the range 0.8 to 2.0 for most nuclei.
- The pairing correction is Δ = n × 12/√A MeV, where n = -1 for odd-odd, 1 for even-even, and 0 for odd nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical approach of Canbula (2011) by including shell and pairing corrections in the asymptotic level density parameter calculation. It directly addresses the long-standing problem of collective enhancement in nuclear level density, which previous treatments by Bjørnholm (1973), Hansen (1983), and Junghans (1998) handled through multiplicative enhancement factors (rotational and vibrational) that produced less consistent predictions. The new model enables reliable extrapolation of level density parameters to nuclei far from stability, particularly near the drip lines, and may help resolve unresolved quasi-elastic cross-section issues for light exotic nuclei as noted by Keeley (2009).
