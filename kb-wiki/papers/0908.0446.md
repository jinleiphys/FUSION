# Regge-plus-resonance predictions for kaon photoproduction from the neutron

> We present predictions for n(gamma,K+)Sigma- differential cross sections and photon-beam asymmetries and compare them to recent LEPS data. We adapt a Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) model developed to describe photoinduced and electroinduced kaon production off protons. The non-resonant contributions to the amplitude are modelled in terms of K+(494) and K*+(892) Regge-trajectory exchange. This amplitude is supplemented with a selection of s-channel resonance diagrams. The three Regge-model parameters of the n(gamma,K+)Sigma- amplitude are derived from the ones fitted to proton data through SU(2) isospin considerations. A fair description of the n(gamma,K+)Sigma- data is realized, which demonstrates the Regge model's robustness and predictive power. Conversion of the resonances' couplings from the proton to the neutron is more challenging, as it requires knowledge of the photocoupling helicity amplitudes. We illustrate how the uncertainties of the helicity amplitudes propagate and heavily restrain the predictive power of the RPR and isobar models for kaon production off neutron targets.

## Key claim

The Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) model, previously fitted to proton-target data, can be extended to predict neutron-target observables using SU(2) isospin symmetry for the non-resonant background, but the conversion of resonance couplings requires poorly known photocoupling helicity amplitudes, which severely limits the predictive power of the RPR and isobar models for the n(gamma,K+)Sigma- channel.

## Method

The authors adapted a Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) model originally developed for p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 to predict n(gamma,K+)Sigma- differential cross sections and photon-beam asymmetries. Non-resonant contributions were modeled via K+(494) and K*+(892) Regge-trajectory exchange in the t-channel, with the three Regge parameters derived from proton fits through SU(2) isospin considerations. Resonant contributions from eight s-channel nucleon and delta resonances were included, with their couplings converted from proton to neutron targets using photocoupling helicity amplitudes from the SAID SM95 analysis, the Review of Particle Physics, and the Bonn constituent-quark model.

## Key numbers

- The Regge model predictions for n(gamma,K+)Sigma- differential cross sections settle between 0.1 and 0.2 microbarns, with the Regge-3 variant providing a better description than Regge-4, which overpredicts by roughly a factor of two.
- The LEPS data cover forward angles (cos theta*_K >= 0.65) in the energy range 1.5 GeV <= omega_lab <= 2.4 GeV, with systematic uncertainties of about 20% for differential cross sections and |Delta Sigma| approx 0.2 for photon-beam asymmetries.
- The RPR-3 and RPR-4 models for p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 achieved a goodness-of-fit of chi^2/d.o.f. = 2.0 using 57 high-energy data points (omega_lab >= 4 GeV) and a large body of resonance-region data.
- The uncertainties in the helicity amplitudes for the six nucleon resonances produce deviations up to 100% for the differential cross section and up to Delta Sigma approx 0.8 for the photon-beam asymmetry in the RPR model.
- The Regge trajectories use slopes alpha'_K+ = 0.70 GeV^-2 and alpha'_K*+ = 0.85 GeV^-2, with a scale s0 = 1 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the Regge-plus-resonance formalism previously applied to p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 (Vancraeyveld et al., 2009) and kaon electroproduction (Vancraeyveld et al., 2009) to the neutron channel, enabling tests against LEPS data (2006) for n(gamma,K+)Sigma-. The paper demonstrates that the pure Regge model, relying only on isospin symmetry, has robust predictive power, while the inclusion of resonances is severely hampered by the poor experimental knowledge of neutron photocoupling helicity amplitudes, a limitation that also affects the Kaon-MAID isobar model (Mart and Bennhold, 2002). This work enables future constraints on resonance photocouplings from neutron-target kaon production data.
