# Lepton Double Charge Exchange Reactions as Probes for Lepton Number Violation

> Lepton number violating (LNV) $A(e^-,e^+)X$ double charge exchange (LDCE) reactions on nuclei at accelerator facilities with multi-GeV beams are proposed as a probe for physics beyond the Standard Model (BSM). A second order formalism is presented including LNV dynamics by the left-right symmetric model (LRSM). For practical studies a phenomenological model is used to estimate LDCE cross sections numerically. Sizable cross sections are predicted for multi-GeV beam energies. LDCE reactions proceed preferentially by the energy-momentum dependent left-right mixing terms. While Majorana mass terms are negligible for light neutrinos, they may become sizable for heavy neutral leptons at higher beam energies. In the ~10 GeV region inclusive total LDCE cross sections of about $100\times|Γ_{BSM}|^2$~fb in units of the BSM vertices are predicted, increasing strongly with energy and target mass. LDCE experiments seem to be feasible with existing equipment under full laboratory control and free of the constraints imposed on decay or capture experiments of nuclear and hadronic systems.

## Key claim

Lepton double charge exchange (LDCE) reactions on nuclei with multi-GeV electron beams can serve as a probe for lepton number violation, with cross sections dominated by energy-momentum dependent left-right mixing terms rather than Majorana mass terms, which are negligible for light neutrinos but may become sizable for heavy neutral leptons at higher beam energies.

## Method

The authors develop a second-order formalism for LDCE reactions within the left-right symmetric model (LRSM), treating the process as a double charged current interaction mediated by t-channel exchange of massive Majorana neutrinos. They derive the reaction amplitude in terms of helicity-projected lepton states and decompose it into vector and scalar components. For numerical estimates, they parameterize available neutrino-nucleus charged current cross section data from the literature using Lorentzian and Gaussian distributions, derive mean matrix elements by dividing out phase space factors, and use these empirical matrix elements as input for schematic LDCE calculations in the no-recoil limit.

## Key numbers

* Inclusive total LDCE cross section on 208Pb at approximately 10 GeV beam energy: about 10^(-37) x |Gamma_BSM|^2 cm^2, equivalent to 100 x |Gamma_BSM|^2 fb
* Total cross sections depend on beam energy by a power law approximately proportional to T^3_lab
* For a light neutral lepton of mass 1 eV at 10 GeV beam energy, the quenching factor relative to four-momentum mixing parts is approximately 1.3 x 10^(-13) in the average for targets from 12C to 208Pb
* For heavy neutral leptons of mass approximately 100 GeV, the quenching factor is of order 1 up to A approximately 40
* At JLab, luminosities should allow sensitivities down to |Gamma_BSM|^2 approximately 10^(-6)
* At the EIC, beam energies up to 140 GeV in the center-of-mass frame are reachable, with luminosity approximately 10^(31-32) cm^(-2) s^(-1) for a 208Pb target

## Context

This work proposes a new experimental approach to lepton number violation research that bridges the gap between nuclear-scale neutrinoless double beta decay (DBD) studies and multi-TeV collider searches for same-charge lepton pairs plus hadron di-jets (LLjj events). The authors extend the Black Box Theorem originally formulated by Schechter and Valle (1982) to include LDCE reactions, arguing that observation of any of the three processes would prove neutrinos are Majorana fermions. The approach is complementary to DBD experiments, which are constrained by nuclear structure and cannot be manipulated experimentally, while LDCE reactions offer full laboratory control at accelerator facilities like JLab and the EIC.
