# Impact of pi N --> pi pi N data on determining high-mass nucleon resonances

> Motivated by an experimental proposal for the measurement of the pi N --> pi pi N reactions at J-PARC, we examine a potential impact of the pi N --> pi pi N cross section data on the determination of the resonance parameters of the high-mass N* states. For this purpose, we make use of the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels model, which has been developed recently by a combined analysis of the unpolarized as well as polarized observables of pion- and photon-induced pi N, eta N, K Lambda, and K Sigma production reactions off a proton target. We present predictions for the pi N --> pi pi N total cross sections and invariant mass distributions, and demonstrate that the pi N --> pi pi N differential cross section data can indeed be a crucial source of information for understanding N* --> pi Delta, rho N, sigma N --> pi pi N decay of the high-mass N* states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that including precise $\pi N \to \pi \pi N$ differential cross section data in partial wave analyses is crucial for resolving significant ambiguities in the decay branching ratios of high-mass nucleon resonances ($N^\ast$), even though the pole masses of clear resonances like the $F_{37}$ may be well determined from existing data without double-pion production channels.

## Method

The author uses the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model, which solves unitary coupled-channels integral equations for meson production reactions off a nucleon, incorporating $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $\pi \pi N$ (via $\pi\Delta$, $\rho N$, $\sigma N$), $K\Lambda$, and $K\Sigma$ channels. To test the impact of $\pi N \to \pi \pi N$ data, the author constructs a "refitted DCC" model by setting the bare $N^\ast \to \pi\Delta$ coupling constants for the $F_{37}$ partial wave to zero and refitting the remaining $F_{37}$ parameters to $\pi N \to \pi N, K\Sigma$ and $\gamma N \to \pi N, K\Sigma$ data, then comparing the predicted $\pi N \to \pi \pi N$ observables between the original and refitted models.

## Key numbers

- The original DCC model reproduces available $\pi N \to \pi \pi N$ total cross section data to better than $\sim 20\%$ accuracy at $W < 1.6$ GeV and $\sim 60\%$ accuracy at $W > 1.6$ GeV.
- The $F_{37}$ resonance pole mass from the original DCC model is $1872 - i103$ MeV; from the refitted DCC model it is $1867 - i85$ MeV, giving a difference of $|M_R^{\text{orig.}} - M_R^{\text{refit}}| \sim 19$ MeV.
- For the $F_{37}$ resonance, the branching ratio to $\pi\Delta$ (with $L=3, S=3/2$) is $46.7\%$ in the original DCC but only $0.2\%$ in the refitted DCC; the branching ratio to $\rho N$ (with $L=3, S=3/2$) is $0.1\%$ in the original DCC but $34.2\%$ in the refitted DCC.
- The $\pi N$ branching ratio for the $F_{37}$ resonance is $51.5\%$ in the original DCC and $53.5\%$ in the refitted DCC.
- The analysis covers center-of-mass energies up to $W = 2.3$ GeV for $\pi N \to \pi N$ data and up to $W = 2.1$ GeV for other reactions.

## Context

This work extends the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels analysis of Kamano et al. (2013) by specifically examining how the lack of $\pi N \to \pi \pi N$ differential cross section data above $W \sim 1.6$ GeV leaves large uncertainties in the decay dynamics of high-mass $N^\ast$ states, particularly for the $F_{37}$, $D_{15}$, and $F_{15}$ partial waves. The paper is motivated by the J-PARC E45 experimental proposal (Hicks, proposed) to measure $\pi^\pm p \to \pi \pi N$ reactions, and it demonstrates that such data would enable quantitative determination of $N^\ast \to \pi\Delta, \rho N, \sigma N$ decay branching ratios, which are essential for establishing the poorly understood high-mass nucleon resonance spectrum.
