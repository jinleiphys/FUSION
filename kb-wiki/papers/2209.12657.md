# Role of vector self-interaction in Neutron Star properties

> Previous studies have claimed that there exist correlations among certain nuclear saturation parameters and neutron star observables, such as the slope of the symmetry energy and the radius of a $1.4M_{\odot}$ neutron star. However, it is not clear whether such correlations are physical or spurious, as they are not observed universally for all equation of state models. In this work, we probe the role of vector self-interaction within the framework of the Relativistic Mean Field model and its role in governing the observable stellar properties and their correlations with nuclear parameters. We confirm that the effect of this term is not only to control the high density properties of the equation of state but also to govern such correlations. We also impose a limit on the maximum strength of the vector self-interaction using recent astrophysical data.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the vector self-interaction term (ζ) in the Relativistic Mean Field model controls not only the high-density behavior of the equation of state but also governs the correlations between nuclear saturation parameters and neutron star observables, though the authors note that such correlations are not observed universally for all equation of state models and may be spurious rather than physical.

## Method

The authors employed the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with nonlinear meson self-couplings, including the quartic vector self-interaction term ζ, to describe β-equilibrated charge-neutral hadronic matter composed of nucleons and leptons. They varied the nuclear saturation parameters (saturation density n₀, energy per particle E_sat, incompressibility K_sat, effective mass m*/m_N, symmetry energy J_sym, and slope L) within ranges from Hornick et al., while also varying ζ from 0.00 to 0.10. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, and weighted Pearson correlation coefficients were computed using Gaussian likelihood weighting from astrophysical observations of PSR J0740+6620 and GW170817.

## Key numbers

- The maximum strength of the vector self-interaction is constrained to ζ ≤ 0.033 to satisfy astrophysical constraints (producing a stable 2 M⊙ neutron star and tidal deformability ≤ 800 for a 1.4 M⊙ star)
- For ζ = 0.00, the canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star radius is 13.487 km with dimensionless tidal deformability 707.904 and maximum mass 2.53 M⊙
- For ζ = 0.01, the 1.4 M⊙ radius is 13.262 km with tidal deformability 623.650 and maximum mass 2.19 M⊙
- For ζ = 0.02, the 1.4 M⊙ radius is 13.051 km with tidal deformability 553.791 and maximum mass 2.02 M⊙
- For ζ = 0.03, the 1.4 M⊙ radius is 12.852 km with tidal deformability 494.164 and maximum mass 1.89 M⊙ (fails to reach 2 M⊙)
- The correlation between the slope of symmetry energy L and radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star increases from a weak value at ζ = 0 to a moderate value of 0.60 at ζ = 0.03
- The correlation between effective mass m*/m_N and maximum mass decreases from 0.93 at ζ = 0 to 0.49 at ζ = 0.03
- The correlation between m*/m_N and tidal deformability of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star decreases from 0.69 at ζ = 0 to 0.14 at ζ = 0.03

## Context

This work extends the studies of Hornick et al. and others who found correlations between nuclear saturation parameters and neutron star observables, but questions whether such correlations are physical or spurious since they are not observed universally across all equation of state models. The paper demonstrates that the vector self-interaction term, often ignored or fixed to a constant value in works such as Hornick et al., Ghosh et al., and Pradhan et al., significantly affects these correlations and the high-density behavior of dense matter. The results enable future studies to incorporate ζ as a free parameter when using astrophysical data to constrain nuclear matter properties, and the provided equation of state tables in a public GitHub repository can be used for subsequent investigations of neutron star structure and mergers.
