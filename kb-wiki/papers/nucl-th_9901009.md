# Anti-Hyperon Enhancement through Baryon Junction Loops

> The baryon junction exchange mechanism recently proposed to explain valence baryon number transport in nuclear collisions is extended to study midrapidity anti-hyperon production. Baryon junction-anti-junction (J anti-J) loops are shown to enhance anti-Lambda, anti-Xi, anti-Omega yields as well as lead to long range rapidity correlations. Results are compared to recent WA97 Pb + Pb -> Y + anti-Y + X data.

# Anti-Hyperon Enhancement through Baryon Junction Loops

## Key claim
The baryon junction-antijunction (J-anti-J) loop mechanism, with parameters fixed by pp and pA phenomenology, can enhance midrapidity anti-hyperon yields in nuclear collisions, but the absolute yields of multi-strange baryons like Omega are still underestimated without additional mechanisms such as ropes or more complex multibaryon junction diagrams.

## Method
The authors extended the HIJING/B event generator to include J-anti-J loop configurations, creating a new version called HIJING/B-anti-B. They fitted the J-anti-J exchange cross section and soft transverse momentum kick parameters to pp and p-S data at 200-400 GeV/c, then compared the computed yields of anti-Lambda, anti-Xi, and anti-Omega to WA97 Pb+Pb data at 158A GeV.

## Key numbers
- The J-anti-J exchange cross section fitted to pp and p-S data is sigma_B-anti-B = 6 mb, with an effective cross section reduced to approximately 3 mb at SPS energies due to kinematic constraints.
- The threshold cutoff mass for J-anti-J loop configurations is m_c = 6 GeV.
- The soft transverse momentum kick width is sigma = 0.6 GeV/c, fitted to p-S data at 200 GeV/c.
- The predicted rapidity correlation length for baryon-antibaryon pairs is |y_B - y_anti-B| approximately 2, based on the assumed intercept alpha_MJ0(0) approximately 1/2.
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200A GeV with impact parameter b less than or equal to 3 fm, the HIJING/B-anti-B prediction for anti-protons is a factor of 3 lower than the original HIJING prediction, and for anti-Lambda a factor of 2.5 lower.

## Context
This work extends the baryon junction exchange mechanism proposed by Kharzeev (1996) and implemented in HIJING/B by Vance (1998) to explain valence baryon number transport, now addressing anti-hyperon production that previous models could not explain. The diquark-quark string models HIJING, FRITIOF, and DPM underestimate midrapidity Lambda yields by a factor of 6 and Omega yields by a factor of 10^3. The J-anti-J loop mechanism provides a non-equilibrium alternative to quark-gluon plasma interpretations (Rafelski 1982, Koch 1986) and string rope models (Biro 1984), with the advantage that its parameters are constrained by pp and pA data rather than being free. The results enable future tests of baryon number transport and B-anti-B correlations at RHIC.
