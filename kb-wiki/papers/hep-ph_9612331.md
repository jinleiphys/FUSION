# Strangeness Correlation: A Clue to Hadron Modification in Dense Matter?

> 8 pages, Latex, uses art10.sty,hip-100ps.sty,twoside.sty and epsf.sty invited talk by M. Asakawa at the Strangeness'96 conference (15-17 May 1996, Budapest, Hungary), to appear in Heavy Ion Physics 4 (1996) 233-240

## Key claim

Hadron modification in dense matter (mass shifts of mesons) generates a new type of two-particle correlation, back-to-back correlation \(C_2(\mathbf{k}, -\mathbf{k}) \neq 1\), which is not present for unmodified hadrons; however, for pions the effect is smaller than current experimental error bars, while for kaons it may be observable at large momenta.

## Method

The authors model freeze-out as a sudden transition from a thermalized system of dressed quasi-particles (b-quanta) in medium to observable vacuum particles (a-quanta), using a scalar field theory with a mean-field approximation. They diagonalize the in-medium Hamiltonian via a Bogoliubov transformation that mixes \(\mathbf{k}\) and \(-\mathbf{k}\) modes, then compute the one- and two-particle distribution functions for the vacuum quanta using a Glauber-Sudarshan representation of the thermal density matrix in the b-basis. The two-particle correlation function \(C_2(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}')\) is evaluated for the cases \(\mathbf{k} = \mathbf{k}'\) and \(\mathbf{k} = -\mathbf{k}'\), with finite-size effects neglected.

## Key numbers

* For pions with an in-medium mass shift of \(-10\) MeV and freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 120\) MeV, the deviation of \(C_2(\mathbf{k}, -\mathbf{k})\) from unity is very small and below current experimental error bars.
* For kaons with an in-medium mass shift of \(-14\) MeV, the back-to-back correlation shows a strongly increasing enhancement at large particle momenta in the center-of-mass frame of the fireball.
* The intercept parameter \(C_2(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k})\) remains at the canonical thermal value of 2, independent of the mass shift.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) correlations in heavy ion collisions by incorporating hadron modification in the medium, which had previously been treated as separate topics. It contradicts the earlier result of Asakawa and Csorgo (1995) which predicted over-bunching (\(C_2(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}) > 2\)) for identical pions at small relative momenta; the present model finds the intercept remains 2. The proposed back-to-back correlation for kaons offers a new observable that is not contaminated by resonance decays, unlike standard Bose-Einstein correlations at small relative momenta, and motivates future studies with finite-size and expanding-source effects.
