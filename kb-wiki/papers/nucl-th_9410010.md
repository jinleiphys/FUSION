# Staggering of the Nuclear Charge Radii in a Superfluid Model with Good Particle Number

> A simple superfluid model with an effective four body interaction of monopole pairing type is used to explain the staggering of the charge radii in the isotope chains. The contribution of deformation and of the particle number projection are analyzed for the Sn isotopes. Good results are obtained for the staggering parameters and neutron pairing energies.

# Staggering of the Nuclear Charge Radii in a Superfluid Model with Good Particle Number

## Key claim

A simple superfluid model with an effective four-body interaction of monopole pairing type can explain the odd-even staggering of nuclear charge radii in Sn isotopes, provided that the deformation and particle number projection are taken into account. The model reproduces the staggering parameter well in a spherical approximation, but the absolute charge radii deviate from experiment due to an incorrect slope of the radii versus neutron number, which is partially improved by including deformation.

## Method

The author uses a Hamiltonian with a canonical mean field, standard monopole pairing interactions for protons and neutrons, and a four-body monopole pairing interaction between proton and neutron pairs. Variational BCS solutions yield coupled gap equations where the proton gap depends on the neutron pairing tensor and vice versa. For the Sn isotopes, single-particle wave functions are computed using a Woods-Saxon potential with small deformation (β₂ = 0.005, β₄ = 0.0) for the spherical case, and with β₂ values extracted from experimental B(E2) values for the deformed case. Particle number projection is applied using the projection operator method with recursive relations for matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strengths used in spherical calculation: Cₚ = 33.5 MeV, Cₙ = 20.0 MeV, C₄ = 2.0 MeV
- Pairing strengths used in deformed calculation: Cₚ = 30.0 MeV, Cₙ = 18.0 MeV, C₄ = 10.0 MeV
- β₂ values for Sn isotopes extracted from B(E2) are around 0.1, with total variation around 0.04 across the chain
- A deviation of 0.01 in β₂ gives a deviation of about 0.01 in δ⟨r²⟩ for nuclei with A ∼ 120
- The staggering parameter γ_A (defined as 2[⟨r²⟩_{A+1}−⟨r²⟩_{A}]/[⟨r²⟩_{A+2}−⟨r²⟩_{A}]) is in very good accord with experiment for the spherical calculation
- Experimental proton pairing energies P_Z for Sn are larger than 1 MeV, indicating some proton superfluidity

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of the four-body interaction in Pb isotopes (Horoi, 1994) and builds on the mechanism proposed by Zawischa (1990) that neutron pairing properties couple to proton properties through collective effects. Earlier attempts by Uher and Sorensen (1966) using the pairing-plus-quadrupole model could not describe the odd-even staggering, while Reehal and Sorensen (1970) found agreement only in some cases using a mixed microscopic and liquid drop model. The present model offers a simpler alternative to the HFB calculations with three-body forces by Zawischa and coworkers (1992-1993), and can be extended to include quantum fluctuations such as RPA pairing and quadrupole vibrations.
