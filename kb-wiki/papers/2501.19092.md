# Exploring total fission cross sections of neutron- and proton-induced reactions of exotic nuclei at relativistic energies using the INCL-ABLA++ models

> Spallation-induced fission reactions, in combination with state-of-the-art dynamical calculations, provide a robust framework for studying the nuclear dissipation mechanism in nuclear matter over a wide range of deformations. Experimental datasets of neutron- and proton-induced fission, accumulated over the last decades, cover a wide range of nuclei from the pre-actinide to the actinide region. In this work, these datasets are used to benchmark the dynamical Liège intranuclear cascade model INCL++ integrated with the ABLA++ de-excitation code, which has been improved by updating the calculation of particle separation energies to the atomic mass evaluation (AME2020) and incorporating phenomenological corrections to enhance the description of fission-barrier heights. The results show good agreement with the available experimental data of total fission cross sections induced by protons and neutrons, thereby confirming the applicability and predictive capability of these models. Moreover, the improved framework is used to investigate the fission cross sections of exotic heavy fissile nuclei, far from the valley of stability, with masses $A > 155$.

## Key claim

The improved INCL-ABLA++ models, updated with AME2020 separation energies and phenomenological corrections to fission-barrier heights, show good agreement with available experimental total fission cross sections for both neutron- and proton-induced reactions, but the paper hedges that this confirms "applicability and predictive capability" specifically for nuclei with masses A > 155, and the validation is limited to the energy range and datasets used for benchmarking.

## Method

The authors coupled the Liège intranuclear cascade model INCL++ (v6.33.8) with the de-excitation model ABLA++ (v24.12) to simulate spallation-induced fission reactions. They updated particle separation energies to the atomic mass evaluation AME2020 and incorporated phenomenological corrections to fission-barrier heights using the finite-range liquid-drop model of Sierk with ground-state shell effects from Möller et al. (1995). The dynamical fission width was computed using the Fokker-Planck equation with a dissipation coefficient β = 4.5 × 10⁻²¹ s⁻¹, including time-dependent transient effects and Kramers' reduction factor.

## Key numbers

- Dissipation coefficient β = 4.5 × 10⁻²¹ s⁻¹, constrained by multiple studies and used in the Fokker-Planck treatment of fission dynamics.
- The asymptotic level-density parameter coefficients used are α_v = 0.095 MeV⁻¹, α_s = 0.073 MeV⁻¹, and α_k = 0 MeV⁻¹, from Ignatyuk (1975).
- The critical energy for the superfluid phase transition is set to E_crit = 10 MeV.
- The damping parameters for collective enhancement are E_c = 40 MeV and d_c = 10 MeV.
- The cascade stopping time is parametrized as t_stop = 29.8 × A_T^0.16 fm/c, where A_T is the target mass number.

## Context

This work extends the benchmarking of the INCL-ABLA++ framework against decades of experimental fission cross-section data for neutron- and proton-induced reactions on nuclei from pre-actinides (e.g., natPb, 209Bi) to actinides (e.g., 232Th, 235,238U, 237Np, 239Pu), building on prior measurements by Kotov et al. (2006) and the n_TOF collaboration (Tagliente 2024). The improved model enables predictions of fission cross sections for exotic heavy fissile nuclei far from stability, which is relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis studies and for applications at facilities such as RIBF, FRIB, and GSI/FAIR. The paper also compares its fission-barrier heights with those from the GEF model (2016), ABLA07, and Möller et al. (2015).
