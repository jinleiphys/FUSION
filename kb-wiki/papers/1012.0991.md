# Beam-energy and system-size dependence of the CME

> The energy dependence of the local ${\cal P}$ and ${\cal CP}$ violation in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions in a large energy range is estimated within a simple phenomenological model. It is expected that at LHC the Chiral Magnetic effect (CME) will be about 20 times weaker than at RHIC. In the lower energy range this effect should vanish sharply at energy somewhere above the top SPS one. To elucidate CME background effects a transport model including magnetic field evolution is put forward.

## Key claim

The Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) is expected to be about 20 times weaker at LHC than at RHIC, and to vanish sharply at an energy somewhere above the top SPS energy; however, this energy dependence is model-dependent and can be reconciled with experiment only by a detailed treatment of the lifetime of the magnetic field and the deconfined phase, including a finite initial time for equilibration.

## Method

The authors estimate the CME signal via a phenomenological model where the average charge-separation correlator \(a\) is related to the topological winding number \(n_w\) and the hadron multiplicity, with the energy dependence entering through the saturation momentum \(Q_s\) and the lifetime \(\tau_B\) of the magnetic field. The magnetic field evolution is computed using the UrQMD model with retarded Lienard-Wiechert potentials, and the energy density of created particles is obtained from the same transport framework. The lifetime \(\tau_B\) is taken as the minimum of the magnetic-field lifetime \(\tilde{\tau}_B\) (time above a critical field \(eB_{crit}\)) and the lifetime of deconfined matter \(\tau_\epsilon\) (time above \(\epsilon > 1\) GeV/fm\(^3\)), and the model is calibrated to STAR data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

*   At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV for Au+Au at impact parameter \(b = 10\) fm (40-50% centrality), the measured same-charge correlator \(\langle \cos(\psi_\alpha+\psi_\beta-2\Psi_{RP})\rangle = -(0.312 \pm 0.027) \cdot 10^{-3}\).
*   For Au+Au at \(b = 10\) fm with \(eB_{crit} = 0.2\ m_\pi^2\), the predicted correlator \(a^2\) is \(0.31 \cdot 10^{-3}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, rising to \(3.10 \cdot 10^{-3}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11\) GeV, and falling to \(0.016 \cdot 10^{-4}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.5 \cdot 10^3\) GeV (LHC).
*   The magnetic field strength \(eB_y\) at \(b = 10\) fm decreases by a factor of about 10 from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV to \(E_{lab} = 11\) GeV, while the energy density \(\varepsilon\) decreases by a factor of 250.
*   The CME at LHC is suppressed by a factor of about 20 relative to RHIC, estimated as \(\sim 1/\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) (ratio \(4.5/0.2 \approx 22\)).
*   For Cu+Cu collisions at the same centrality (40-50%), the impact parameter is \(b = 4.2\) fm, and the transverse overlap area ratio \(S_{\rm Cu}(b=4.2)/S_{\rm Au}(b=10) \approx 1.65\).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological CME estimates of Kharzeev (2004) and others by incorporating a dynamical treatment of the magnetic field lifetime from UrQMD (Bass 1998) and comparing to STAR data (Voloshin 2009). It predicts a sharp disappearance of the CME near top SPS energies and a strong suppression at LHC, which can be tested by the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and LHC experiments. The authors also introduce a kinetic approach based on the HSD transport model (including magnetic field evolution) to study CME background effects, which is a step beyond the static estimates of Skokov (2009).
