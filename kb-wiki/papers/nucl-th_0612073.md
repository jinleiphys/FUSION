# Microscopic Cluster Model for Exotic Nuclei

> For a better understanding of the dynamics of exotic nuclei it is of crucial importance to develop a practical microscopic theory easy to be applied to a wide range of masses. Theoretically the basic task consists in formulating an easy solvable theory able to reproduce structures and transitions of known nuclei which should be then used to calculate the sparely known properties of proton- or neutron-rich nuclei. In this paper we start by calculating energies and distributions of A\leq4 nuclei withing a unitary correlation model restricted to include only two-body correlations. The structure of complex nuclei is then calculated extending the model to include correlation effects of higher order.

## Key claim

The paper presents a microscopic cluster model based on a unitary correlation operator (e\(^{iS}\)) that, when restricted to only two-body correlations, can reproduce energies and distributions of A \(\leq\) 4 nuclei, and can then be extended to include higher-order correlation effects for complex nuclei; the hedge is that the short-range correlation is considered only up to two-body, while the long-range component requires three- and four-body operators, especially for open-shell exotic nuclei.

## Method

The authors start from a Hamiltonian with a singular nucleon-nucleon potential and apply the e\(^{iS}\) unitary correlation method, initially truncating the correlation operator to the two-body part \(S_2\) to define an effective Hamiltonian. For A=3 and A=4 nuclei, they solve eigenvalue equations derived from commutator equations (equations of motion) using the Cluster Factorization Theory (CFT) to factorize three- and four-particle states into pairs. For heavier nuclei (Carbon and Oxygen isotopes), they extend the model to include higher-order correlation operators \(S_3\) through \(S_n\), linearize the commutator chain via generalized linearization approximations, and diagonalize the resulting eigenvalue equations within a correlated model space that includes mixed valence modes and core-excited states.

## Context

This work extends the unitary-model operator (UMO) approach originally introduced by Villars (1963) and implemented by Shakin (1966) for G-matrix calculations from hard-core interactions, and builds upon the Dynamic Correlation Model and Boson Dynamic Correlation Model (BDCM) previously applied to odd and even nuclei. The paper enables systematic calculations of charge distributions and electromagnetic transitions for neutron-rich Carbon and Oxygen isotopes using a microscopic correlation framework that treats short-range correlations via the \(S_2\) operator and long-range correlations via higher-order \(S_n\) operators, with the authors reporting ongoing work on 5- and 6-neutron systems.
