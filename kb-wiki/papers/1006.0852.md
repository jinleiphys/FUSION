# Chiral Symmetry and Axial Anomaly in Hadron and Nuclear Physics --- a review --

> The important role played by chiral symmetry and axial anomaly in QCD in nuclear physics is reviewed. Some recent topics on possible chiral restoration in hot and/or dense matter are picked up. We also discuss so called {\em effective} restoration of chiral anomaly hot and/or dense matter, as may be seen in a character change of $η'$ meson.

## Key claim

This review argues that chiral symmetry and its dynamical breaking in QCD are ultimately responsible for the saturation property of nuclear matter, and that the QCD vacuum can change with increasing temperature and/or baryon density, leading to partial chiral restoration in finite nuclei and effective restoration of the axial U(1) anomaly in hot and/or dense matter as may be seen in a character change of the eta' meson.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical developments connecting chiral symmetry, the axial anomaly, and nuclear phenomena, focusing on the Nambu-Goldstone nature of the pion and its consequences for the nuclear force. It discusses the derivation of sum rules relating the in-medium quark condensate to the temporal pion decay constant and pion coupling, and examines how the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinantal interaction in effective models (generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models) predicts temperature-dependent changes in the eta-eta' mixing angle and masses.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant in vacuum: f_pi approximately 93 MeV, from the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation.
* Coefficient gamma = 0.184 in the expression Z_pi*^(1/2) = 1 - gamma (rho/rho_0), where beta = 2.17 +/- 0.04 fm^3.
* Eta' meson mass: 958 MeV, cited as a reflection of the U(1)_A anomaly.
* The s-wave pi- nucleus optical potential parameter b_1* relates to the in-medium temporal pion decay constant as (b_1 / b_1*) = (F_pi^t / F_pi)^2.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on chiral symmetry in nuclear physics reviewed by Hatsuda (1994) and Hatsuda (2001), and discusses experimental evidence from deeply bound pionic atoms (Suzuki, 2002), low-energy pion-nucleus scattering (Friedman, 2004), and di-pion production (Bonutti, 2000; Starostin, 2000; Messchendorp, 2002). The theoretical framework builds on the sum rule approach of Jido, Hatsuda, and Kunihiro (2008) connecting the quark condensate to the temporal pion decay constant, and on earlier work by Pisarski and Wilczek (1983) on effective U(1)_A restoration. The review enables future experimental searches for chiral restoration signals in finite nuclei and heavy-ion collisions, particularly through studies of the eta' meson mass modification and the properties of negative-parity baryons such as N*(1535).
