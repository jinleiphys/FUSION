# Implications of chiral symmetry for scalar isoscalar channel and multi- nucleon forces

> Chiral perturbation theory has to be substantially modified for the problem of nuclear forces. Loop effects dominate over tree level effects. The dominant behaviours in the scalar isoscalar channel and multi-nucleon forces are obtained in the chiral limit and for a small explicit breaking.

# Implications of chiral symmetry for scalar isoscalar channel and multi-nucleon forces

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory must be substantially modified for nuclear forces in the scalar isoscalar (sigma) channel because loop effects dominate over tree-level effects; the intermediate range attraction in nucleon-nucleon interactions is not a consequence of chiral symmetry itself but arises entirely from the *small explicit breaking* of chiral symmetry, and in the chiral limit the sigma channel decouples from nucleons at low momenta despite having an infrared-divergent propagator.

## Method

The authors analyze the infrared behavior of the sigma propagator in both linear and nonlinear sigma models, showing that in the chiral limit sigma-prime behaves as a composite of two pions with a logarithmic infrared singularity. They then compute the dominant two-pion exchange contribution to nucleon-nucleon scattering in the scalar isoscalar channel by including an explicit chiral symmetry breaking term (the pion-nucleon sigma term) via a Sigma vertex, and evaluate the resulting loop integral for small pion mass and momentum transfer. The analysis is extended to three-nucleon forces by classifying contributions from Pi, Rho, and Sigma vertices in the nonlinear sigma model.

## Key numbers

- The sigma propagator in the chiral limit behaves as `<sigma'(-q) sigma'(q)> ~ ln(Lambda^2 / q^2)` as q approaches 0, where Lambda is the microscopic scale or cutoff.
- The leading non-analytic contribution to the scalar isoscalar amplitude from two-pion exchange with explicit breaking is `i * (3 Sigma_N^2) / (32 pi^2 f^4) * integral_0^1 dx ln( Lambda^2 / (m_pi^2 - x(1-x)q^2) )`.
- For three-nucleon forces, the three contributions scale as: Fig.3a ~ q^3/m_pi^4, Fig.3b ~ Sigma_N * q^2/m_pi^4, and Fig.3c ~ Sigma_N^3/m_pi^2.
- In QCD, the authors expect Sigma_N ~ m_pi^2 ~ m_q (quark mass).

## Context

This work extends the chiral symmetry analysis of nuclear forces initiated by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and contrasts with standard chiral perturbation theory approaches (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1982) that order graphs by powers of momenta without counting logarithms. The authors show that the logarithmic infrared behavior of the sigma channel, previously noted in renormalization group studies (Brezin, Wallace and Wilson, 1973) and in soft pion unitarity corrections (Lehmann, 1972), survives to all orders and must be treated explicitly. The result enables systematic computation of dominant pion-exchange contributions to nucleon-nucleon and multi-nucleon forces for momentum transfers q much less than m_pi, with corrections of O(q^2) and higher accessible via a modified chiral perturbation theory (Anishetty, Hari Dass and Sharatchandra, in preparation). The analysis resolves the puzzle of why the intermediate range attraction is not predicted by chiral symmetry alone, and identifies the pion-nucleon sigma term (Delorme, Chanfray and Erickson, 1996) as the key driver of this attraction.
