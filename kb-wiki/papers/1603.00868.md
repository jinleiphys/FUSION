# Amplitude analysis of $\bar{K}N$ scattering

> We present the results of a coupled-channel model for $\bar{K}N$ scattering in the resonance region. The model fulfills unitarity, has the correct analytical properties for the amplitudes and the partial waves have the right threshold behavior. The parameters of the model have been established by fitting single-energy partial waves up to $J=7/2$ and up to 2.15 GeV of center-of-mass energy. The $Λ^*$ and $Σ^*$ spectra has been obtained, providing a comprehensive picture of the $S=-1$ hyperon spectrum. We use the structure of the hyperon spectrum and Regge phenomenology to gain insight on the nature of the $Λ(1405)$ resonances.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the two poles associated with the Lambda(1405) resonance have different natures: the higher-mass pole (around 1429 MeV) is mostly a three-quark state, while the lower-mass pole (around 1325 MeV) is either a molecule or a pentaquark, based on how they fit onto Regge trajectories. This conclusion is conditional on the specific Regge trajectory analysis and the pole positions taken from a prior chiral amplitude analysis by Mai (2015).

## Method

A coupled-channel K-matrix model for antikaon-nucleon scattering was constructed, incorporating up to 13 channels per partial wave, analyticity, unitarity, and correct angular momentum barrier behavior. The model parameters were fitted to single-energy partial waves up to total angular momentum J=7/2 and center-of-mass energy up to 2.15 GeV, using the MINUIT package and a genetic algorithm, with uncertainties computed via bootstrap. The resulting partial waves were analytically continued to unphysical Riemann sheets to extract hyperon resonance pole positions, and Regge trajectories were constructed from the Lambda* spectrum to assess the nature of the two Lambda(1405) poles.

## Key numbers

- The model fitted single-energy partial waves up to J=7/2 and up to 2.15 GeV center-of-mass energy.
- The experimental database covered the range 2.19 < s < 4.70 GeV^2, containing approximately 17500 data points across six reaction channels.
- The two Lambda(1405) poles from Mai (2015) used in the Regge analysis are located at 1429(+8/-7) - i 12(+2/-3) MeV and 1325(+15/-15) - i 90(+12/-18) MeV.
- Quark-diquark model calculations cited obtain Lambda(1405) masses of 1431 MeV (Santopinto, 2014) and 1406 MeV (Faustov, 2015).

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel analysis of antikaon-nucleon scattering by Fernandez-Ramirez (2015) to provide a comprehensive picture of the S=-1 hyperon spectrum, and applies Regge phenomenology to address the long-standing problem of the Lambda(1405) nature. The analysis builds on prior amplitude studies by Mai (2015) and the two-pole interpretation of the Lambda(1405), as well as lattice QCD calculations by Hall (2015) and Engel (2013) that reached different conclusions about its structure. The resulting model and codes are made available for use in three-body decay analyses for pentaquark searches and two-kaon photoproduction experiments at Jefferson Lab.
