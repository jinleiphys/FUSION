# Nuclear lattice simulations: Status and perspectives

> Plenary talk sponsored by The European Physical Journal A -- Hadrons and Nuclei, INPC 2013, Florence, Italy, 8 pages, 3 figures, uses webofc.cls

## Key claim

Nuclear lattice simulations, combining chiral effective field theory with Monte Carlo techniques, can now compute the spectrum and structure of light nuclei up to \(^{12}\)C from first principles, but the method currently shows overbinding for heavier \(\alpha\)-cluster nuclei like \(^{16}\)O, which requires the introduction of a smeared four-nucleon operator whose strength is fitted to the \(^{16}\)O ground state.

## Method

The authors formulate chiral effective field theory on a Euclidean space-time lattice with spacing \(a \simeq 2\) fm, which serves as an ultraviolet regulator. They treat the leading-order (LO) interaction as a smeared Gaussian potential summed non-perturbatively, while all higher-order contributions (up to next-to-next-to-leading order, NNLO) are included in perturbation theory. Parameters (low-energy constants) are fixed from \(np\) scattering S- and P-waves, the deuteron quadrupole moment, isospin-breaking scattering lengths, the \(nd\) doublet scattering length, and the triton binding energy. Ground and excited states are extracted using a nuclear lattice projection Monte Carlo technique that employs multiple initial and final states, including delocalized and cluster-type wave functions, to probe different nuclear geometries.

## Key numbers

*   Ground state energy of \(^{4}\)He at NNLO: \(-28.3(6)\) MeV, matching the empirical value of \(-28.3\) MeV.
*   Ground state energy of \(^{12}\)C at NNLO: \(-92(3)\) MeV, compared to the empirical value of \(-92.2\) MeV.
*   Energy of the Hoyle state (\(0_2^+\)) in \(^{12}\)C at NNLO: \(-85(3)\) MeV, compared to the empirical value of \(-84.51\) MeV.
*   Energy difference between \(^{3}\)He and \(^{3}\)H: \(0.78(5)\) MeV, in agreement with the empirical value of \(0.76\) MeV.
*   Preliminary ground state energy of \(^{16}\)O at NNLO: \(-141.4(9)\) MeV, overbinding the empirical value of \(-127.6\) MeV by about 14 MeV.
*   The Hoyle state lies 379 keV above the \(3\alpha\) threshold.
*   Variations of the light quark mass at the \(2-3\%\) level are unlikely to be detrimental to carbon-oxygen based life; variations of the fine-structure constant \(\alpha_{\rm QED}\) up to \(\pm 2.5\%\) are consistent with sufficient carbon and oxygen production.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces, initiated by Weinberg (1990), from few-nucleon systems to nuclei with \(A>4\) using lattice Monte Carlo methods introduced by Borasoy et al. (2006) and reviewed by Lee (2008). It reports the first ab initio calculation of the Hoyle state in \(^{12}\)C, a long-standing challenge for nuclear theory, and uses the framework to study the sensitivity of stellar carbon production to the fundamental parameters of QCD and QED. The results enable future investigations of capture reactions on the lattice, topological volume corrections in scattering, and the equation of state of neutron matter.
