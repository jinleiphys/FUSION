# Disentangling flow and signals of Chiral Magnetic Effect in U+U, Au+Au and p+Au collisions

> We present STAR measurements of the charge-dependent three-particle correlator $γ^{a,b}=\langle \cos(ϕ_1^{a}+ϕ_2^{b}-2ϕ_3)\rangle/v_{2}\{2\}$ and elliptic flow $v_{2}\{2\}$ in U+U, Au+Au and p+Au collisions at RHIC. The difference $Δγ= γ(\rm{opposite\!-\!sign})\!-\!γ(\rm{same\!-\!sign})$ measures charge separation across the reaction plane, a predicted signal of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME). Although charge separation has been observed, it has been argued that the measured separation can also be explained by elliptic flow related backgrounds. In order to separate the two effects, we perform measurements of the $γ$-correlator where background expectations differ from magnetic field driven effects. A differential measurement of $γ$ with the relative pseudorapidity ($Δη$) between the first and second particles indicate that $Δγ$ in peripheral A+A and p+A collisions are dominated by short-range correlations in $Δη$. However, a relatively wider component of the correlation in $Δη$ tends to vanish the same way as projected magnetic field as predicted by MC-Glauber simulations.

## Key claim

The charge-dependent three-particle correlator difference Δγ, a proposed signal of the Chiral Magnetic Effect, can be decomposed into a short-range positive component and a wider residual component; the residual component vanishes in central A+A, peripheral A+A, and minimum-bias p+Au collisions where MC-Glauber simulations predict strong decorrelation of the magnetic field with the second harmonic event plane, while the short-range component persists as a significant background source.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured the three-particle correlator γ^{a,b} = ⟨cos(φ₁^{a}+φ₂^{b}-2φ₃)⟩/v₂{2} and elliptic flow v₂{2} in U+U collisions at √s_{NN}=193 GeV and Au+Au and p+Au collisions at √s_{NN}=200 GeV using charged particles within |η|<1 and pT>0.2 GeV/c detected by the Time Projection Chamber. The Δη dependence of the correlator numerator C₁₁₂ was fitted with a three-component function comprising a short-range positive Gaussian, an intermediate-range negative Gaussian, and a constant pedestal, allowing extraction of charge-dependent short-range and residual components separately.

## Key numbers

- U+U collisions at √s_{NN}=193 GeV and Au+Au, p+Au collisions at √s_{NN}=200 GeV were analyzed.
- The short-range positive Gaussian width was σ_{SR+} ≤ 0.46 ± 0.03 in pseudorapidity units.
- The intermediate-range negative Gaussian width was σ_{IR} ≥ 0.66 ± 0.04 in pseudorapidity units.
- Centrality bins used: Au+Au 0-20%, U+U 0-10%, and U+U 30-40% for the Δη dependence measurement.
- The short-range positive component of Δγ multiplied by Npart increased with decreasing Npart (toward peripheral events), while the residual component showed a non-monotonic trend.

## Context

This work extends STAR's previous measurements of charge-dependent correlations in heavy-ion collisions (Abelev 2009, Adamczyk 2014) by introducing a Δη decomposition technique similar to that used by STAR for two-particle correlations (Adamczyk 2016). The analysis addresses the long-standing ambiguity between CME signals and flow-driven backgrounds (Voloshin 2004, Schlichting 2010, Bzdak 2010) by exploiting scenarios where MC-Glauber simulations (Chatterjee 2014, Belmont 2016) predict the magnetic field projection on the event plane vanishes while v₂/N remains non-zero. The results motivate future isobar collision measurements at RHIC to further disentangle signal from background.
