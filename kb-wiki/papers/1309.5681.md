# Energy Dependence of Moments of Net-proton Multiplicity Distributions at RHIC

> We report the beam energy (\sqrt s_{NN} = 7.7 - 200 GeV) and collision centrality dependence of the mean (M), standard deviation (σ), skewness (S), and kurtosis (κ) of the net-proton multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions. The measurements are carried out by the STAR experiment at midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) and within the transverse momentum range 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c in the first phase of the Beam Energy Scan program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. These measurements are important for understanding the Quantum Chromodynamic (QCD) phase diagram. The products of the moments, Sσand κσ^{2}, are sensitive to the correlation length of the hot and dense medium created in the collisions and are related to the ratios of baryon number susceptibilities of corresponding orders. The products of moments are found to have values significantly below the Skellam expectation and close to expectations based on independent proton and anti-proton production. The measurements are compared to a transport model calculation to understand the effect of acceptance and baryon number conservation, and also to a hadron resonance gas model.

## Key claim

The products of moments of net-proton multiplicity distributions (Sσ and κσ²) are found to be significantly below the Skellam expectation and close to expectations based on independent proton and anti-proton production, but the paper does not claim observation of a critical point; rather, these measurements are presented as sensitive probes of the correlation length and baryon number susceptibilities that can help map the QCD phase diagram.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured the mean (M), standard deviation (σ), skewness (S), and kurtosis (κ) of net-proton multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) and transverse momentum 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c, across beam energies √sNN = 7.7 to 200 GeV and various collision centralities. The moment products Sσ and κσ² were computed and compared to Skellam expectations, independent proton/anti-proton production expectations, a transport model (to study acceptance and baryon number conservation effects), and a hadron resonance gas model.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: √sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- Transverse momentum range: 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c
- Rapidity acceptance: |y| < 0.5
- The products of moments (Sσ and κσ²) are found to be significantly below the Skellam expectation across the measured energy range
- The measured values are close to expectations based on independent proton and anti-proton production

## Context

This work extends the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, which aims to map the QCD phase diagram (temperature versus baryonic chemical potential) by studying fluctuations in net-proton distributions. The measurements are compared to transport model calculations to understand acceptance and baryon number conservation effects, and to a hadron resonance gas model. The results provide experimental constraints on baryon number susceptibilities and correlation lengths, enabling future searches for the QCD critical point and comparisons with lattice QCD calculations at finite baryon chemical potential.
