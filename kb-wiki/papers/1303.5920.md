# Effects of the density-dependent weak form factors on the neutrino reaction via neutral current for the nucleon in nuclear medium and $^{12}$C

> The nucleon form factors in free space are usually thought to be modified when a nucleon is bound in a nucleus or immersed in a nuclear medium. We investigate effects of the density-dependent axial and weak-vector form factors on the electro-neutrino ($ν_e$) and anti-electro-neutrino $({\bar ν_e})$ reactions via neutral current (NC) for a nucleon in nuclear medium or $^{12}$C. For the density-dependent form factors, we exploit the quark-meson-coupling (QMC) model, and apply them to the $ν_e$ and ${\bar ν_e}$ induced reactions by NC. About 12% decrease of the total cross section by $ν_e$ reaction on the nucleon is obtained at normal density, $ρ= ρ_0 \sim 0.15 {fm}^{-3} $, as well as about 18% reduction of total $ν_e$ cross section on $^{12}$C, by the modification of the weak form factors of the bound nucleon. However, similarly to the charged current reaction, effects of the nucleon property change in the ${\bar ν}_e$ reaction reduce significantly the cross sections about 30% for the nucleon in matter and $^{12}$C cases. Such a large asymmetry in the ${\bar ν}_e$ cross sections is addressed to originate from the different helicities of ${\bar ν}_e$ and $ν_e$.

## Key claim

The modification of nucleon weak form factors in a nuclear medium, calculated using the quark-meson-coupling (QMC) model, reduces neutral-current neutrino and antineutrino cross sections on both free nucleons and bound nucleons in $^{12}$C, but the reduction is significantly larger for antineutrinos (about 30%) than for neutrinos (about 12–18%) due to the different helicities of the two particles.

## Method

The authors calculated differential and total cross sections for $\nu_e$ and $\bar{\nu}_e$ neutral-current reactions on a nucleon in nuclear matter and on $^{12}$C, using density-dependent weak form factors derived from the quark-meson-coupling (QMC) model. For the nucleon in matter, they used the standard differential cross section formula with Sachs form factors and the axial form factor $G_A^{NC}$. For $^{12}$C, they applied a quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) framework to describe the nuclear transition from the $0^+$ ground state to the $1^+$ excited state, using a general differential cross section formula without the extremely relativistic limit.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear density $\rho = \rho_0 \sim 0.15 \text{ fm}^{-3}$, the total $\nu_e$ cross section on a nucleon decreases by about 12% due to the modified weak form factors.
- At $\rho = \rho_0$, the total $\nu_e$ cross section on $^{12}$C decreases by about 18% due to the modified weak form factors.
- At $\rho = \rho_0$, the total $\bar{\nu}_e$ cross section on both the nucleon in matter and $^{12}$C decreases by about 30% due to the modified weak form factors.
- For the $\nu_e + n \rightarrow \nu_e' + n$ reaction on a nucleon, the cross section variation below $E_\nu \sim 30$ MeV is less than 3%, and the maximum decrease at $E_\nu \sim 80$ MeV is about 12% at $\rho = \rho_0$.
- The axial form factor $g_A(\rho, Q^2)$ is reduced by 11% at $\rho = \rho_0$ in the small momentum transfer region.
- For $^{12}$C at an average Fermi momentum $k_F = 225$ MeV ($\rho = 0.668 \rho_0$), the maximum change from in-medium effects is less than 7%.
- The flux-averaged cross section for $(\nu_e, \nu_e') + (\nu_\mu, \nu_\mu')$ on $^{12}$C, calculated at $\rho = 0.6 \rho_0$, is $9.52 \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2$, consistent with the experimental value of $10.4 \pm 1.0 \pm 0.9 \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2$ from the LSND Collaboration (1997).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of medium effects on charged-current neutrino reactions (Cheoun et al., 2013) to neutral-current reactions, using the same QMC model for density-dependent form factors. The results show that the large asymmetry between neutrino and antineutrino cross sections in dense matter, previously found for charged currents, also appears for neutral currents irrespective of the target. This asymmetry could significantly affect antineutrino propagation inside proto-neutron stars and is relevant for understanding neutrino-driven supernova explosions and nucleosynthesis in the $\nu$-process, as well as for interpreting data from neutrino facilities like MiniBooNE (2010) in the quasi-elastic region.
