# Production of charmed baryon $Λ_c(2940)$ by kaon-induced reaction on a proton target

> We investigate the possibility to study the charmed baryon $Λ_c(2940)$ by kaon-induced reaction on a proton target. By assuming the $Λ_c(2940)$ as a $pD^{*0}$ molecular state with spin-parity $J^{p}=1/2^{\pm}$, an effective Lagrangian approach was adopted to calculate the cross section, the $D^0p$ invariant mass spectrum and Dalitz plot of the $Λ(2940)$ production. The total cross section of the $K^{-}p\toΛ_c(2940)D_s^{-}$ reaction is found at an order of magnitude about 10 $μ$b. By considering the sub sequential decay $Λ_c(2940)\to{}D^0p$ with contributions from the $Λ_c(2286)$ and the $Σ_c(2455)$ as background, the $K^{-}p\to{}D_{s}^{-}D^{0}p$ reaction are studied. It is found that the $Λ_c(2940)$ is produced mainly at forward angles. The $Λ_c(2940)$ signal is predicted to be significant in the $D^0p$ invariant mass spectrum and the Dalitz plot of the $K^{-}p\to{}D_{s}^{-}D^{0}p$ reaction. The results suggest that it is promising to study the $Λ_c(2940)$ with high-energy kaon beam on a proton target in experiment.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the charmed baryon Λc(2940) can be produced with a total cross section on the order of 10 μb in the kaon-induced reaction K−p → Ds−Λc(2940), assuming the Λc(2940) is a pD*0 molecular state with spin-parity Jp = 1/2±; the signal is expected to be significant in the D0p invariant mass spectrum and Dalitz plot, but the cross section depends strongly on the assumed spin-parity assignment and on the cutoff parameter used in the form factors.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach to calculate the t-channel D*0 exchange mechanism for the K−p → Ds−Λc(2940) reaction, using coupling constants determined from SU(4) symmetry and from previous molecular-state studies. They computed the total cross section as a function of kaon beam momentum, and then included the subsequent decay Λc(2940) → D0p along with background contributions from Λc(2286) and Σc(2455) to predict the D0p invariant mass spectrum, angular distributions, and Dalitz plot for the K−p → Ds−D0p reaction. A Monte Carlo multiparticle phase space integration (FOWL program) was used for the three-body final state calculations.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section of K−p → Ds−Λc(2940) reaction: order of magnitude about 10 μb at a beam momentum around 15 GeV.
- Mass of Λc(2940) from BaBar: 2939.8 ± 1.3 ± 1.0 MeV; width: 17.5 ± 5.2 ± 5.9 MeV.
- Mass of Λc(2940) from Belle: 2938.0 ± 1.3 +2.0/−4.0 MeV; width: 13 +8+27/−5−7 MeV.
- Coupling constants used: gΛc*pD = −0.54, gΛc*pD* = 6.64 for Jp = 1/2−; fΛc*pD = −0.97, fΛc*pD* = 3.75 for Jp = 1/2+.
- Cutoff parameter ΛD* = ΛB = 3 GeV used for form factors.
- Total cross section for K−p → Ds−D0p reaction: about 1 μb for Jp = 1/2+ and about 10 μb for Jp = 1/2− at beam momentum 16 GeV.
- Width of Λc(2940) used in resonance propagator: Γ = 17 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of Λc(2940) production in antiproton-proton annihilation (He 2011, Dong 2014), pion-induced reactions (Xie 2015), and electromagnetic production (Wang 2015) to the kaon-induced channel, motivated by the availability of high-energy kaon beams at OKA@U-70, SPS@CERN, and potentially J-PARC. The results suggest that kaon-induced production offers a promising alternative to e+e− collisions (BaBar 2006, Belle 2006) for studying the Λc(2940) and could help distinguish between the Jp = 1/2+ and Jp = 1/2− molecular-state assignments. Future experiments with kaon beams could test these predictions and clarify the internal structure of the Λc(2940).
