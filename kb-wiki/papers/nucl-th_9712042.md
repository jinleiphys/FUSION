# Nucleon resonances in the constituent quark model with chiral symmetry

> The mass spectra of nucleon resonances with spin 1/2, 3/2, and 5/2 are systematically studied in the constituent quark model with meson-quark coupling, which is inspired by the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry of QCD. The meson-quark coupling gives rise not only to the one-meson-exchange potential between quarks but also to the self-energy of baryon resonances due to the existence of meson-baryon decay channels. The two contributions are consistently taken into account in the calculation. The gross properties of the nucleon resonance spectra are reproduced fairly well although the predicted mass of N(1440) is too high.

# Nucleon resonances in the constituent quark model with chiral symmetry

## Key claim
The constituent quark model with meson-quark coupling, incorporating both the one-meson-exchange potential (OMEP) and the self-energy from meson-baryon decay channels with a subtraction term to avoid double counting, reproduces the gross features of nucleon resonance spectra fairly well, although the predicted mass of N(1440) remains too high.

## Method
The authors constructed a non-relativistic constituent quark model with pseudovector meson-quark coupling inspired by spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, using a linear confinement potential and harmonic-oscillator basis functions up to the 8ħω-shell. The mass operator was decomposed into the OMEP, the self-energy from πN and ηN intermediate states, and a subtraction term to eliminate double counting, with resonance masses determined by solving Re(E_R - H_eff(E_R)) = 0. The six model parameters (confinement strength c, form factor parameter a, bare mass M_0, and coupling constants g_πqq, g_ηqq, g_η'qq) were fitted to the spin-1/2 nucleon resonance spectrum, with g_πqq derived from the πN coupling constant G_πNN = 14.3 via SU(6) relations.

## Key numbers
- Quark mass m = 340 MeV (conventional value used)
- Harmonic-oscillator range parameter β = 3.7 fm⁻² (obtained variationally)
- Ground-state nucleon mass calculated as 1017 MeV, about 10% above the observed value
- Diagonal OMEP element for ground-state nucleon: -175 MeV
- Self-energy Σ(E ≃ m_N) for ground-state nucleon: about -400 MeV
- Subtraction term Σ̄ for ground-state nucleon: -266 MeV
- Probability Z of three-quark component in nucleon: Z ≃ 0.6
- Excitation energies of first negative-parity states: about 500 MeV
- g_ηqq = 3.52 (from fitting), compared to 4.59 from η-nucleon coupling constant
- Without form factor ρ(k), N(1520) mass becomes smaller by about 60 MeV, while N(1535) changes by a few MeV only

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Glozman et al. (1990s), who introduced the one-meson-exchange potential based on spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, by consistently including the self-energy from meson-baryon decay channels that was previously studied by Arima and collaborators using the one-gluon-exchange potential. The model enables future calculations of πN scattering quantities and extension to strange baryons, while highlighting the need for relativistic corrections and inclusion of additional channels such as πΔ and ρN for detailed spectroscopy.
