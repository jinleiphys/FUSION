# 1D inelastic relativistic-collision

> In this paper, we discuss one dimensional inelastic relativistic-collisions in the framework of the relativistic kinetic theory. In particular, we focus on the relativistic effects on time evolutions of the temperature and flow velocity under the spatially homogeneous state. As a result of the relativistic effects, we obtain the characteristic time evolutions of the temperature and flow velocity, which are never obtained under the nonrelativistic limit.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in one-dimensional inelastic relativistic collisions under a spatially homogeneous state, the absolute value of the flow velocity increases as the temperature decreases, and the cooling rate depends on both the thermally relativistic measure \(\chi\) and the initial flow velocity via the constant \(\mathcal{C}\); these effects are never obtained under the nonrelativistic limit.

## Method

The authors formulate a 1D inelastic relativistic-Boltzmann equation for the reaction \(qq \rightarrow qq+g\) (quark + quark → quark + quark + gluon) under the spatially homogeneous state, assuming no reverse reaction and neglecting spin. They derive analytic expressions for the time evolution of the temperature and flow velocity by assuming the distribution function is the 1D Maxwell–Jüttner function, using Eckart's decomposition and Grad's 14 moments. Numerical solutions are obtained via the direct simulation Monte Carlo (DSMC) method for two initially nonequilibrium test cases (Test 1 and Test 2) with \(\Lambda = 0\) and \(A = 1\).

## Key numbers

* The cooling rate parameter \(\psi_1(\chi,0)\) for zero initial flow velocity has a peak value of \(0.295\) at \(\chi \simeq 4.14\).
* In the nonrelativistic limit (\(\chi \rightarrow 0\)), the temperature evolves as \(\chi(t) = \chi(0) \exp\left(\frac{A}{8} t\right)\).
* In the ultrarelativistic limit (\(\chi \rightarrow \infty\)), the temperature evolves as \(\chi(t) = \left(A\frac{t}{\sqrt{\pi}} + \sqrt{\chi(0)}\right)^2\).
* The limiting values of \(\psi_1(\chi,0)\) are \(\lim_{\chi \rightarrow 0} \psi_1(\chi,0) = 1/8\) and \(\lim_{\chi \rightarrow \infty} \psi_1(\chi,0) = 2/\sqrt{\pi \chi}\).
* The flow velocity in Test 2 approximates to \(0.97\) as time increases.

## Context

This work extends the study of inelastic collisions from granular gases (Ben-Naim, 1999) to the relativistic regime, using the 1D inelastic relativistic-Boltzmann equation as a first step toward understanding inelastic collisions in quark-gluon plasma (QGP) dynamics at RHIC and LHC. The paper contrasts with the nonrelativistic case where flow velocity is conserved in inelastic collisions, and it provides analytic results that are not tractable in 3D. The findings enable future studies of nonequilibrium moments (dynamic pressure, heat flux, pressure deviator) and their effects on cooling rates in relativistic kinetic theory, and they highlight differences from the nonrelativistic homogeneous cooling state where overpopulation at high velocity tails occurs on both sides of the flow velocity.
