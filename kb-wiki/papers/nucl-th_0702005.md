# First Order Relativistic Three-Body Scattering

> Relativistic Faddeev equations for three-body scattering at arbitrary energies are formulated in momentum space and in first order in the two-body transition-operator directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. Relativistic invariance is incorporated within the framework of Poincare invariant quantum mechanics, and presented in some detail. Based on a Malfliet-Tjon type interaction, observables for elastic and break-up scattering are calculated up to projectile energies of 1 GeV. The influence of kinematic and dynamic relativistic effects on those observables is systematically studied. Approximations to the two-body interaction embedded in the three-particle space are compared to the exact treatment.

# First Order Relativistic Three-Body Scattering

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that relativistic three-body scattering calculations can be performed at energies up to 1 GeV laboratory kinetic energy by using direct integration methods that avoid partial wave decomposition, but the results are limited to the first order term of the Faddeev multiple scattering series and use spin-independent (three-boson) interactions rather than full nucleon-nucleon interactions with spin.

## Method

The authors formulate relativistic Faddeev equations in momentum space using Poincare invariant quantum mechanics, solving them directly in terms of vector variables without partial wave decomposition. They employ a Malfliet-Tjon type Yukawa interaction and use the Kamada-Glockle approach of unitary rescaling of momentum variables to ensure the nonrelativistic and relativistic interactions are phase-shift equivalent. The calculations are performed for the first order term of the Faddeev multiple scattering series, comparing different approximations to the two-body interaction embedded in the three-particle space against the exact treatment.

## Key numbers

- Projectile energies up to 1 GeV laboratory kinetic energy are considered for elastic and break-up scattering observables
- The calculations cover the intermediate energy regime from 0.2 to 1 GeV
- Below about 200 MeV projectile energy, non-relativistic momentum-space Faddeev equations can be solved with high accuracy for realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic momentum-space Faddeev calculations (Witala et al., various years; Glockle et al., 1996) that were limited to below 200 MeV, where relativistic effects were found to be quantitatively small except for some breakup cross sections in certain phase space regions (Skibin). The approach builds on earlier bound state calculations using vector variables (Bound3d, 2005) and scattering at intermediate energies (Scatter3d, 2005), and contrasts with the covariant spectator approach of Stadler (1999). The paper enables future full relativistic three-nucleon scattering calculations at higher energies, though the present work is restricted to spinless interactions and first-order terms.
