# Regge phenomenology of photoproduction of $ŽĆ^-╬ö^{++}$ and scaling with saturation of trajectory

> In this work we investigate the reaction $╬│p\toŽĆ^-╬ö^{++}$ in the Reggeized model for $ŽĆ(138)+Žü(775)+a_2(1320)$ exchanges in the $t$-channel. For a convergence of the reaction cross section at high energies the minimal forms of proton and $╬ö^{++}$ exchanges are introduced in the direct and crossed channels for gauge invariance of $ŽĆ$ Regge-pole exchange. The role of spin-2 tensor meson $a_2$ is found to be crucial to agree with existing data at high energies. Electromagnetic multipoles of $╬ö^{++}$ baryon are analyzed in the $╬ö$ resonance region. Based on the constituents counting rule the scaled differential cross section at $E_╬│=4$ GeV is reproduced with the Regge trajectory saturated at large momentum transfer $-t$.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the reaction $\gamma p\to\pi^-\Delta^{++}$ can be described by a Reggeized model with $\pi(138)+\rho(775)+a_2(1320)$ exchanges, but only if a minimal gauge prescription is applied to remove divergent terms from proton and $\Delta^{++}$ pole exchanges, and only if the tensor meson $a_2$ exchange is included to reproduce high-energy data.

## Method

The authors constructed a Reggeized model for $t$-channel exchanges of pion, rho, and $a_2$ mesons in the reaction $\gamma p\to\pi^-\Delta^{++}$. They enforced gauge invariance of the pion Regge-pole exchange by introducing minimal forms of proton and $\Delta^{++}$ exchanges in the direct and crossed channels, removing divergent transverse components. A new Lagrangian for the $a_2 N\Delta$ coupling was constructed based on spin-parity conservation, with its coupling constant determined from the identity $f_{a_2 N\Delta}/m_{a_2} = -3 f_{\rho N\Delta}/m_{\rho}$. For wide-angle scattering, the linear Regge trajectories were saturated using a square-root parameterization, and a monopole form factor with cutoff mass 1.6 GeV was applied to the production amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section calculated up to photon energy $E_\gamma = 16$ GeV, with convergence achieved via minimal gauge prescription.
- Coupling constants used: $f_{\pi N\Delta} = 2.0$ (high-energy fit) and $f_{\pi N\Delta} = 1.7$ (low-energy fit); $f_{\rho N\Delta} = 8.57$ (high-energy) and $f_{\rho N\Delta} = 5.5$ (low-energy).
- Electromagnetic multipole moments of $\Delta^{++}$: $e_{\Delta^{++}} = 2$, $\kappa_{\Delta^{++}} = 4.34$, $\chi_{\Delta^{++}} = 12.34$, $\lambda_{\Delta^{++}} = 6.8$.
- Saturation points for trajectories: $\pi$ at $t_0 = -0.5$ GeV$^2$, $\rho$ at $t_0 = -1$ GeV$^2$, $a_2$ at $t_0 = -0.9$ GeV$^2$, all saturating to $\alpha(t) \to -1$ up to $-t = 20$ GeV$^2$.
- Scaled differential cross section at $E_\gamma = 4$ GeV reproduced with monopole form factor cutoff mass $\Lambda = 1.6$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous Regge model analyses of $\gamma p\to\pi^-\Delta^{++}$ that used only $\pi+\rho$ exchanges (Wu, Ballam, Anderson, various years) but suffered from divergence at high energies. The authors previously addressed the divergence issue with a minimal gauge prescription (Yu, 2015), but found that data at $E_\gamma \approx 16$ GeV required the additional $a_2$ tensor meson exchange. The study also applies the constituent counting rule for scaling at wide angles, following the pQCD dimensional analysis approach, and uses trajectory saturation as described by Collins (1977) and Sergeenko (various years). This enables future studies of hadron reactions at both high energy and wide angles within a unified Regge framework.
