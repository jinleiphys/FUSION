# Selection of projectiles for producing trans-uranium nuclei in transfer reactions within an improved dinuclear system model

> The multinucleon transfer process is one promising approach for producing neutron-rich heavy nuclei. The favorable projectile-target combinations play a key role in experiments. As one important approach to investigate the multinucleon transfer process, one new version of the dinuclear system (DNS) model is developed. The improved version of the DNS model is shown in detail. Based on deformation degree of freedom in the improved DNS model, one way of calculating excitation energies of primary fragments is proposed. And the remarkable improvements for describing experimental data of producing trans-uranium nuclei is noticed. To produce trans-uranium nuclei, the collisions of 48Ca, 136Xe, and 238U projectiles with the 238U target are investigated within the improved DNS model. Based on the potential energy surface, the influence of projectiles on the probabilities of transferring neutrons and protons from the projectiles to the target are studied. One behavior is found that the transfer probabilities strongly depend on the projectiles in the neutron stripping process, while relatively weak projectile dependence is noticed in the proton transferring process. The 136Xe and 238U projectiles show great advantages of cross sections for producing neutron-rich trans-uranium nuclei, although the fission probabilities are large.

## Key claim

The improved dinuclear system (DNS) model, which includes deformation degrees of freedom in the master equation and a new method for calculating excitation energies of primary fragments, shows that the transfer probabilities for neutrons strongly depend on the projectile choice while proton transfer probabilities show relatively weak projectile dependence, with the caveat that these conclusions are drawn from calculations for collisions with a 238U target only.

## Method

The authors developed an improved version of the DNS model (called "DNS-sysu") that incorporates deformation evolution self-consistently in the master equation, temperature-dependent shell corrections, and a new method for calculating excitation energies of primary fragments based on deformation. They applied this model to investigate multinucleon transfer reactions of 48Ca, 136Xe, and 238U projectiles with a 238U target, calculating potential energy surfaces, nucleon transfer probabilities, and production cross sections for trans-uranium nuclei. The cooling process of excited primary fragments was simulated using a Monte Carlo statistical model including neutron, proton, alpha, gamma emission, and fission decay channels.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 48Ca + 238U, the favorable incident energy used was E_c.m. = 220 MeV (1.3 times the contact potential V_cont = 169 MeV)
- For the reaction 136Xe + 238U, the favorable incident energy used was E_c.m. = 627 MeV (1.4 times V_cont = 448 MeV)
- For the reaction 238U + 238U, the favorable incident energy used was E_c.m. = 902 MeV (1.3 times V_cont = 694 MeV)
- The calculated excitation energy of 261Md at central collisions in 48Ca + 238U was 8.9 MeV, compared to 126 MeV in 238U + 238U
- The N/Z ratio of 48Ca is 1.4, which is smaller than 1.59 for 238U
- The characteristic relaxation time of radial energy tau_R = 2 x 10^(-22) s; the relaxation time for angular momentum tau_J = 12 x 10^(-22) s

## Context

This work extends the DNS model approach previously applied to heavy nuclei around N=126 (Zhu 2015, Zhu 2016) and exotic nuclei near the neutron drip line (Zhu 2017) by adding deformation evolution and improved excitation energy calculations. The study directly compares with experimental data for 136Xe + 238U from Vogt (2001) and 238U + 238U from Schadel (1982), showing good agreement. The results are also compared with Langevin-type approach calculations from Saiko (2019) for Md isotope production. This work enables selection of optimal projectile-target combinations for future experiments at facilities like HIAF, and suggests that heavier targets than 238U would be needed for producing transactinide nuclei with Z > 100.
