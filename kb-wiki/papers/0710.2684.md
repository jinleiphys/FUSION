# Open charm in nuclear matter at finite temperature

> We study the properties of open-charm mesons ($D$ and $\bar {D}$) in nuclear matter at finite temperature within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach. The meson-baryon interactions are adopted from a type of broken SU(4) s-wave Tomozawa-Weinberg terms supplemented by an attractive scalar-isoscalar attraction. The in-medium solution at finite temperature incorporates Pauli blocking effects, mean-field binding on all the baryons involved, and $π$ and open-charm meson self-energies in a self-consistent manner. In the $DN$ sector, the $Λ_c$ and $Σ_c$ resonances, generated dynamically at 2593 MeV and 2770 MeV in free space, remain close to their free-space position while acquiring a remarkable width due to the thermal smearing of Pauli blocking as well as from the nuclear matter density effects. As a result, the $D$ meson spectral density shows a single pronounced peak for energies close to the $D$ meson free-space mass that broadens with increasing matter density with an extended tail particularly towards lower energies. The $\bar D$ potential shows a moderate repulsive behavior coming from the dominant I=1 contribution of the $\bar D N$ interaction. The low-density theorem is, however, not a good approximation for the $\bar D$ self-energy in spite of the absence of resonance-hole contributions close to threshold in this case. We speculate the possibility of $D$-mesic nuclei as well as discuss some consequences for the $J/Ψ$ suppression in heavy-ion collisions, in particular for the future CBM experiment at FAIR.

## Key claim

The authors show that the $\Lambda_c$ and $\Sigma_c$ resonances, dynamically generated at 2593 MeV and 2770 MeV in free space, remain close to their free-space positions in hot nuclear matter but acquire a remarkable width due to thermal smearing of Pauli blocking and nuclear matter density effects, contradicting earlier mean-field approaches that predicted simple mass shifts.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for the $DN$ and $\bar D N$ systems using an $s$-wave Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction derived from broken SU(4) symmetry, supplemented by a scalar-isoscalar attraction. The in-medium calculation at finite temperature incorporates Pauli blocking on nucleons, mean-field binding on all baryons (nucleons, hyperons, and charmed baryons) via a Walecka-type $\sigma$-$\omega$ model, and self-consistent dressing of pions and open-charm mesons through their self-energies, iterating until convergence.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda_c$ resonance position in free space: 2593 MeV; $\Sigma_c$ resonance position in free space: 2770 MeV
- At saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$ and $T=0$ MeV, the in-medium $\tilde\Lambda_c$ lies 45 MeV lower than its free-space value for model A (with scalar attraction)
- At saturation density and $T=0$ MeV, the in-medium $\tilde\Sigma_c$ lies 40 MeV lower than its free-space value for model A
- The scalar-isoscalar interaction strength is set to $\Sigma_{DN} \approx 2000$ MeV, with $f_D \sim 200$ MeV giving $\Sigma = \Sigma_{DN}/f_D^2 = 0.05$ MeV$^{-1}$
- The momentum cutoff parameters are $\Lambda = 727$ MeV (model A) and $\Lambda = 787$ MeV (model B)
- The $I=1$ $\tilde\Sigma_c$ resonance develops a width of order 200 MeV in medium at $\rho_0$ and $T=0$
- The nucleon scalar and vector potentials at $\rho_0$ and $T=0$ are $\Sigma^s = 356$ MeV and $\Sigma^v = 278$ MeV
- The $\bar D N$ coupling strength for $I=1$ is $\tilde C_{\bar D N \rightarrow \bar D N}^{I=1} = -2$; for $I=0$ it is zero

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature coupled-channel study of Tolos et al. (2004) and the SU(4)-based approach of Mizutani et al. (2006) to finite temperatures up to 150 MeV and densities up to twice normal nuclear matter density. It goes beyond earlier mean-field approaches by Tsu et al. (1999), Sibirtsev et al. (1999), Hayashigaki (2000), and Mishra et al. (2004) by computing the full spectral function of $D$ and $\bar D$ mesons rather than assuming a simple delta-function mass shift. The results enable more realistic calculations of $J/\Psi$ suppression in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the future CBM experiment at FAIR, and provide a theoretical basis for investigating possible $D$-mesic nuclei.
