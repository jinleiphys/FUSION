# Energy loss in a strongly coupled thermal medium and the gauge-string duality

> We review methods developed in the gauge-string duality to treat energy loss by energetic probes of a strongly coupled thermal medium. After introducing the black hole description of the thermal medium, we discuss the trailing string behind a heavy quark and the drag force that it implies. We then explain how to solve the linearized Einstein equations in the presence of the trailing string and extract from the solutions the energy density and the Poynting vector of the dual gauge theory. We summarize some efforts to compare these calculations to heavy ion phenomenology.

## Key claim

The gauge-string duality provides a framework for computing energy loss of energetic probes in a strongly coupled thermal medium, but the translation of these results from \( \mathcal{N}=4 \) super-Yang-Mills theory to QCD requires a scheme-dependent identification of parameters (temperature and 't Hooft coupling) that introduces substantial uncertainty, with heavy quark relaxation times uncertain by a factor of about 1.5 even within a given scheme.

## Method

The authors review the dual description of a thermal state of \( \mathcal{N}=4 \) SYM as an AdS\(_5\)-Schwarzschild black hole, then model a heavy quark as a string hanging from the boundary into the bulk. For a quark moving at constant velocity \( v \), the string trails behind it (the "trailing string" solution), and the drag force is extracted from the conserved worldsheet momentum current. The linearized Einstein equations are solved in the presence of the trailing string to obtain the energy density and Poynting vector of the dual gauge theory, and the results are compared to heavy ion phenomenology via Langevin dynamics.

## Key numbers

* Drag force on an infinitely massive quark: \( F_{\text{drag}} = -\frac{\pi\sqrt{\lambda}}{2} T^2 \frac{v}{\sqrt{1-v^2}} \), where \( \lambda \) is the 't Hooft coupling and \( T \) is the temperature.
* Heavy quark relaxation time: \( t_{\text{quark}} = \frac{2}{\pi\sqrt{\lambda}} \frac{m}{T^2} \); for charm (\( m_c = 1.5 \) GeV) in the obvious scheme (\( T_{\text{SYM}} = 250 \) MeV, \( \lambda = 6\pi \)), \( t_c = 0.69 \) fm; in the alternative scheme (\( T_{\text{SYM}} = 195 \) MeV, \( \lambda = 5.5 \)), \( t_c = 2.1 \) fm.
* For bottom (\( m_b = 4.8 \) GeV): \( t_b = 2.2 \) fm (obvious scheme) and \( t_b = 6.8 \) fm (alternative scheme).
* Dimensionless drag coefficient \( \gamma = \pi\sqrt{\lambda}/2 \times (T_{\text{SYM}}/T_{\text{QCD}})^2 \): \( \gamma = 6.8 \) (obvious scheme) and \( \gamma = 2.2 \) (alternative scheme).
* Ratio of degrees of freedom: SYM has about 2.7 times as many degrees of freedom as QCD above the confinement transition.
* Correlation time for stochastic forces: \( t_{\text{cor}} \approx 1/(\pi T (1-v^2)^{1/4}) \).
* Bound on Lorentz boost factor for validity of Langevin dynamics: \( 1/\sqrt{1-v^2} \lesssim 30 \) for a scale \( \mu = 1.2 \) GeV and \( T_{\text{QCD}} = 250 \) MeV.

## Context

This review extends the gauge-string duality methods developed by Maldacena (1997), Gubser, Klebanov, Polyakov (1998), and Witten (1998) to the problem of energy loss in a thermal medium, building on the trailing string solution independently found by Herzog, Karch, Kovtun, Kozcaz, and Yaffe (2006) and by Gubser, Pufu, and Rocha (2006). The drag force calculation is compared to heavy ion phenomenology via Langevin dynamics, as studied by Akamatsu, Hatsuda, and Hirano (2008), and the results are contrasted with the model of van Hees, Greco, and Rapp (2005) which favors a longer charm relaxation time of roughly 4.5 fm. The work enables further comparison of strongly coupled plasma predictions to RHIC data on the nuclear modification factor \( R_{AA} \) for non-photonic electrons from heavy quark decays.
