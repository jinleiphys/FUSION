# Probing spin and pseudospin symmetries in deformed nuclei by the Green's function method

> (Pseudo)spin symmetries play vital roles in nuclear physics and have been studied extensively in spherical nuclei. In this work, possible spin and pseudospin symmetries in deformed nuclei are examined by solving a coupled-channel Dirac equation with quadruple deformation. The Green's function method is taken which provides a novel way to exactly determine the single-particle levels and properly describe the spacial density distributions. Taking axially-deformed nucleus $^{154}$Dy as an example, the spin doublets with a combination of Nilsson levels $Λ\pm 1/2[\mathcal{N},n_z, Λ]$ and pseudospin doublets with a combination of $\widetildeΛ\pm 1/2[\widetilde{\mathcal{N},n_z, Λ}]$ are determined. Different behaviors are displayed for the spin and pseudospin doublets. For the spin partners, those with smaller angular momentum $l$ and the third component $Λ$ owns better symmetry such as the $1p$ doublet while good pseudospin symmetry appears in partners locating close to the continuum threshold. By examining the single-particle Nilsson levels $Ω[\mathcal{N},n_z,Λ]$ and the energy splittings between the partners, the conservation and breaking of SS and PSS are examined at different deformations. In the prolate side, the Nilsson levels for the spin and pseudospin doublets are almost parallel and the energy splittings are stable against varying deformations. By examining the density distributions, great similarities have been observed in the upper components for the spin doublets while great similarities in the lower component for the pseudospin doublets. Besides, these similarities maintain well at different deformations.

# Probing spin and pseudospin symmetries in deformed nuclei by the Green's function method

## Key claim

Spin symmetry (SS) and pseudospin symmetry (PSS) in deformed nuclei are approximately conserved for bound single-particle states, with the quality of conservation depending on specific quantum numbers and deformation, but the symmetries are never exact due to the nonzero sum of scalar and vector potentials; the Green's function method provides a novel way to determine single-particle levels exactly and describe spatial density distributions properly in deformed systems.

## Method

The authors solved a coupled-channel Dirac equation with axially quadrupole-deformed Woods-Saxon potentials using the Green's function method, expanding the single-particle wave functions in spherical Dirac spinors with quantum numbers Ω (z-component of total angular momentum) and parity π. The density of states was calculated by integrating the imaginary part of the Green's function in coordinate space, and single-particle bound state energies were determined by locating the extremes of the density of states. Taking the axially deformed nucleus ¹⁵⁴Dy as an example, spin doublets (Λ ± 1/2[𝒩, n_z, Λ]) and pseudospin doublets (Λ̃ ± 1/2[𝒩̃, n_z, Λ̃]) were identified from the Nilsson level scheme.

## Key numbers

- Energy differences for spin doublets with Λ=1: 0.73 MeV (1p), 1.23 MeV (1d), 2.14 MeV (1f), 3.59 MeV (1g), and 5.14 MeV (1h) in ¹⁵⁴Dy at deformation β=0.24.
- Energy differences for the 1g spin doublets with different Λ: 3.59 MeV (Λ=1, doublet 1/2[431], 3/2[431]), 3.85 MeV (Λ=2, doublet 3/2[422], 5/2[422]), 4.43 MeV (Λ=3, doublet 5/2[413], 7/2[413]), and 5.14 MeV (Λ=4, doublet 7/2[404], 9/2[404]).
- Energy differences for pseudospin doublets with Λ̃=1: 1.75 MeV (1p̃), 1.73 MeV (1d̃), 1.62 MeV (1f̃), and 0.86 MeV (1g̃) in ¹⁵⁴Dy at β=0.24.
- Single-particle energies for the 1/2[000] state: -47.681 MeV (positive parity); for the 1/2[110] state: -42.268 MeV (negative parity) in ¹⁵⁴Dy.
- Deformation parameter β=0.24 for ¹⁵⁴Dy; Woods-Saxon potential depths S_WS = -405.0 MeV and V_WS = 350.0 MeV; radius R = 6.81 fm; diffuseness a = 0.67 fm.
- Accuracy of single-particle energies: up to 1.0 keV with energy step dε = 1.0 × 10⁻³ MeV and infinitesimal parameter ε = 1.0 × 10⁻⁶ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin and pseudospin symmetries from spherical nuclei to deformed systems, building on the relativistic symmetry origin of PSS established by Ginocchio (1997) and the condition dΣ(r)/dr = 0 for PSS proposed by Meng et al. (1998). It applies the Green's function method, previously used by Sun et al. (2014, 2020) for spherical and deformed nuclei, to examine SS and PSS in bound states of ¹⁵⁴Dy, complementing earlier deformed RMF studies by Sugawara-Tanabe et al. (2000, 2002) and Lalazissis et al. (1998). The results enable systematic investigation of how deformation affects symmetry conservation and provide a foundation for studying PSS in resonant states of deformed nuclei using the same Green's function framework.
