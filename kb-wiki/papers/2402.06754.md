# Detecting anomalous CP violation in heavy ion collisions through baryon-electric charge correlations

> The chiral magnetic effect (CME) and the chiral vortical effect (CVE) induce a correlation between baryon and electric currents. We show that this correlation can be detected using a new observable: a mixed baryon-electric charge correlator. This correlator is proportional to the baryon asymmetry, suggesting a novel way to separate the chiral effects from the background in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the recently observed baryon separation in PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV can be explained by the chiral vortical effect using a chiral chemical potential consistent with other estimates, but only if the CVE signal fraction is assumed to be suppressed relative to the CME fraction by a factor of about 30 due to lower baryon multiplicity.

## Method

The authors relate the measured \(\Delta\gamma\) correlators for baryons (CVE) and charged hadrons (CME) to the underlying anomalous currents by assuming Bjorken flow with a time-independent chiral chemical potential, using a fit for vorticity from the AMPT model, and modeling the magnetic field decay with constant electric conductivity. They extract the chiral chemical potential from both the CVE and CME data separately, then propose a new mixed baryon-electric charge correlator \(\Delta\Gamma_{QB}\) that is predicted to scale linearly with the event-by-event baryon asymmetry.

## Key numbers

* Chiral chemical potential from CVE data: \(\mu_5/T_0\) ranges from 2.3 (0-10% centrality) to 9.2 (40-50% centrality) for PbPb at 5.02 TeV
* Chiral chemical potential from CME data: \(\mu_5/T_0\) ranges from 7.3 (10-20% centrality) to 10.3 (0-10% centrality) for the same system
* CVE fraction estimate: \(f_{CVE} \approx 0.3\%\), derived from \(f_{CME} \approx 10\%\) suppressed by factor \(\sqrt{N_\pi^2/N_pN_\Lambda} \approx 30\)
* Baryon separation \(\Delta N_B^{\uparrow\downarrow}\) from CVE: ranges from 0.4 (0-10% centrality) to 0.1 (40-50% centrality)
* Initial magnetic field \(eB_0\): ranges from \(1.8 \times 10^{-2} m_\pi^2\) (0-10%) to \(7.4 \times 10^{-2} m_\pi^2\) (40-50%)
* Observed \(\Delta\gamma_{CVE}\) values: from \(8 \times 10^{-5}\) (0-10%) to \(12 \times 10^{-4}\) (40-50%)
* Observed \(\Delta\gamma_{CME}\) values: from \(2 \times 10^{-5}\) (0-10%) to \(25 \times 10^{-5}\) (40-50%)

## Context

This work extends the experimental search for the chiral magnetic effect and chiral vortical effect in heavy ion collisions, following the recent ALICE measurement of baryon separation in PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV (Wang 2023). The authors provide a quantitative framework connecting the measured \(\Delta\gamma\) correlators to the chiral chemical potential, building on earlier estimates by Yuan (2023) and Yu (2014). The proposed mixed baryon-electric charge correlator offers a new method to separate anomalous transport from background, analogous to how the chiral magnetic wave was studied by STAR (Wang 2012) using charge asymmetry selection.
