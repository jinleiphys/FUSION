# Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics for Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The method of smoothed particle hydrodynamics (SPH) is developped appropriately for the study of relativistic heavy ion collision processes. In order to describe the flow of a high energy but low baryon number density fluid, the entropy is taken as the SPH base. We formulate the method in terms of the variational principle. Several examples show that the method is very promising for the study of hadronic flow in RHIC physics.

## Key claim

The paper develops a smoothed particle hydrodynamics (SPH) method for relativistic heavy-ion collisions that uses entropy as the base quantity carried by SPH particles, rather than baryon number, because in the ultrarelativistic regime most energy is carried by produced pions and baryon density becomes very small; the method is formulated via a variational principle and is intended to provide robust but not overly precise solutions suitable for exploring different equations of state and initial conditions.

## Method

The authors parametrize the entropy density in the calculational frame as a sum over kernel functions centered on SPH particles, each carrying a fixed entropy portion. They derive the equations of motion from a variational principle applied to the action \(I = -\int d^4x\,\varepsilon\), with the entropy current as a constraint, and obtain a system of first-order differential equations for particle positions, momenta, and entropy weights. The formulation is extended to generalized coordinate systems, including hyperbolic (Bjorken) coordinates \((\tau, \eta, \vec{r}_T)\), and includes a bulk-viscosity term \(Q\) to handle shock waves via artificial viscosity.

## Key numbers

* One-dimensional Landau model: 100 SPH particles produced "quite satisfactory" agreement with the exact solution (Fig. 1-a, 1-b).
* Three-dimensional scaling solution: \(50 \times 50 \times 50\) particles reproduced the analytical solution "fairly well" (Fig. 2); computation took less than 2 minutes per time step on a Pentium-II PC.
* Transverse expansion on longitudinal scaling expansion: \(50 \times 50 \times 50\) particles gave results "quite satisfactory" compared to (2+1)-dimensional method-of-characteristics results (Fig. 3); reducing accuracy by 10% allows reducing particle number by almost one order of magnitude.
* Shock wave test (massless pion gas): without artificial viscosity, rapid oscillations appear behind the shock front (Fig. 4); the paper demonstrates that including a bulk-viscosity term \(Q\) (von Neumann–Richtmyer type) suppresses these oscillations.

## Context

This work extends the variational SPH approach of Ref. (Variation) to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, addressing the limitation that conventional baryon-number-based SPH fails in the pion-dominated mid-rapidity region. It builds on prior hydrodynamic treatments of nuclear collisions (Landau, Bjorken, Strottman, Csernai, Stöcker) and on earlier relativistic SPH studies (Chow and Monaghan, Zurek, Siegler, Thacker). The entropy-based formulation enables direct tracking of entropy production in shocks and first-order phase transitions, and the use of hyperbolic coordinates facilitates setting initial conditions on a constant proper-time hypersurface, as expected for RHIC physics.
