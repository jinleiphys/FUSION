# SU(2) Chiral sigma model and the properties of neutron stars

> We discuss the {\it SU}(2) chiral sigma model in the context of nuclear matter using a mean field approach at high density. In this model we include a dynamically generated isoscalar vector field and higher-order terms in the scalar field. With the inclusion of these, we reproduce the empirical values of the nuclear matter saturation density, binding energy, and nuclear incompressibility. The value of the incompressibility is chosen according to recently obtained heavy-ion collision data. We then apply the same dynamical model to neutron-rich matter in beta equilibrium, related to neutron star structure. The maximum mass and corresponding radius of stable non-rotating neutron stars are found to be in the observational limit.

## Key claim

The modified SU(2) chiral sigma model, which includes a dynamically generated isoscalar vector field and higher-order scalar field self-interactions, reproduces empirical nuclear matter saturation properties and yields neutron star maximum masses and radii that fall within observational limits, provided the nuclear incompressibility is chosen between 250 and 350 MeV (approximately 300 MeV) in accordance with heavy-ion collision data.

## Method

The authors construct a mean-field Lagrangian for the SU(2) chiral sigma model that includes a dynamically generated omega vector field and higher-order scalar potential terms with parameters B and C. They fit the four free parameters (c_sigma, c_omega, B, C) to reproduce the empirical saturation density, binding energy, and effective nucleon mass of symmetric nuclear matter, with the incompressibility set to approximately 300 MeV. They then extend the model to neutron-rich matter in beta equilibrium by including the rho meson, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.153 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -16.3 MeV
- Effective nucleon Landau mass at saturation: 0.85 M (where M is the free nucleon mass)
- Fitted coupling constants: c_omega = 1.9989 fm^2, c_sigma = 6.8158 fm^2
- Fitted scalar potential parameters: B = -100, C = -133.6
- Symmetry energy coefficient: 32 MeV (empirical value used to fix c_rho = 4.66 fm^2)
- Maximum neutron star mass for MCH model: 2.12 M_sun (at central density 2.0 x 10^15 g cm^-3)
- Corresponding radius at maximum mass for MCH model: 12.1 km
- Central density at maximum mass for MCH model: 2.0 x 10^15 g cm^-3 (greater than 7 times nuclear matter density, where nuclear matter density is 2.8 x 10^14 g cm^-3)
- Maximum neutron star mass for NL3 model: 2.18 M_sun (radius 11.6 km)
- Maximum neutron star mass for original CH model: 2.51 M_sun (radius 13.8 km)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier SU(2) chiral sigma model (Sahu 1993) by adding higher-order scalar field terms to reduce the previously too-large nuclear incompressibility. The resulting equation of state is softer than the original chiral model and comparable to the NL3 model derived from heavy-ion collision flow data (Sahu 2000). The neutron star masses obtained (up to 2.12 M_sun) are consistent with observations of binary pulsars (Thorsett 1999), X-ray binaries such as Vela X-1 (Barziv 1999) and Cygnus X-2 (Orosz 1999), and masses inferred from quasi-periodic oscillations in low-mass X-ray binaries (Miller 1998). The work enables future investigations of hyperonic matter in an SU(3) chiral sigma model and studies of phase transitions to quark matter in heavy-ion collisions.
