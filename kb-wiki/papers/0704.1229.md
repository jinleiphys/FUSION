# High-precision covariant one-boson-exchange potentials for np scattering below 350 MeV

> All realistic potential models for the two-nucleon interaction are to some extent based on boson exchange. However, in order to achieve an essentially perfect fit to the scattering data, characterized by a chi2/Ndata ~ 1, previous potentials have abandoned a pure one boson-exchange mechanism (OBE). Using a covariant theory, we have found a OBE potential that fits the 2006 world np data below 350 MeV with a chi2/Ndata = 1.06 for 3788 data. Our potential has fewer adjustable parameters than previous high-precision potentials, and also reproduces the experimental triton binding energy without introducing additional irreducible three-nucleon forces.

## Key claim
Using the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST), the authors demonstrate that a pure one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential can fit the 2006 world np scattering data below 350 MeV with a chi2/Ndata of 1.06 for 3788 data, contradicting the previous belief that OBE mechanisms alone are insufficient for high-precision fits. The potential reproduces the experimental triton binding energy without introducing additional irreducible three-nucleon forces.

## Method
The authors solve the CST (Gross) equation for np scattering, a covariant three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with a kernel consisting of sums of one-boson-exchange diagrams for pseudoscalar, scalar, vector, and axial vector bosons. The potential parameters (coupling constants, boson masses, cutoff masses, and off-shell coupling parameters) are fitted directly to the 2006 world np scattering data below 350 MeV using a minimization program that constrains the deuteron binding energy and the 1S0 scattering length. Two models are presented: WJC-1 with 27 parameters including axial vector contact interactions, and WJC-2 with 15 parameters and no axial vectors.

## Key numbers
- WJC-1 achieves chi2/Ndata = 1.06 for 3788 np data below 350 MeV (27 parameters)
- WJC-2 achieves chi2/Ndata = 1.12 for the same data set (15 parameters)
- Deuteron binding energy: Ed = -2.2246 MeV (used as constraint)
- 1S0 scattering length: a0 = -23.749 fm (used as constraint)
- Triton binding energy from WJC-1: Et = -8.48 MeV (experimental: -8.48 MeV)
- Triton binding energy from WJC-2: Et = -8.50 MeV (experimental: -8.48 MeV)
- Deuteron D/S ratio for WJC-1: eta_D = 0.0256(1) (experimental: 0.0256(4))
- Deuteron D/S ratio for WJC-2: eta_D = 0.0257(1) (experimental: 0.0256(4))
- Pion coupling constants in WJC-1: G_pi0 = 14.608, G_pi+/- = 13.703
- Pion coupling constant in WJC-2: G_pi = 14.038 (charge symmetric)

## Context
This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory approach to NN scattering previously developed by Gross (1992) and applied to three-nucleon systems by Stadler (1997). It directly contradicts the conclusion of the Nijmegen group (Stoks, 1994) that pure OBE potentials could not achieve chi2/Ndata below 1.87, which led to the construction of partial-wave-dependent boson parameters in models like Nijm93, CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 2001), and the phenomenological AV18 (Wiringa, 1995). The results enable a consistent covariant treatment of electromagnetic currents in few-nucleon systems and provide a new phase shift analysis updated to 2006 data.
