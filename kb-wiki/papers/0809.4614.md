# Realistic shell model; 132Sn region; 208Pb region

> We report on a study of exotic nuclei around doubly magic 132Sn in terms of the shell model employing a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. The short-range repulsion of the latter is renormalized by constructing a smooth low-momentum potential, V-low-k, that is used directly as input for the calculation of the effective interaction. In this paper, we focus attention on proton-neutron multiplets in the odd-odd nuclei 134Sb, 136Sb. We show that the behavior of these multiplets is quite similar to that of the analogous multiplets in the counterpart nuclei in the 208Pb region, 210Bi and 212Bi.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the proton-neutron multiplets in the odd-odd nuclei 134Sb and 136Sb, calculated using a realistic shell-model effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn potential via the V-low-k renormalization method, behave quite similarly to the analogous multiplets in the counterpart nuclei 210Bi and 212Bi in the 208Pb region, with the caveat that the comparison is based on the similarity of multiplet patterns rather than exact numerical agreement.

## Method

The authors derived a shell-model effective interaction by first constructing a smooth low-momentum potential V-low-k from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, using a cutoff momentum of 2.2 fm^-1. They then calculated the effective interaction via a folded-diagram method, including diagrams up to second order in the two-body interaction within the Q-box, and summed the series using the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. Shell-model calculations for 134Sb and 136Sb were performed using the NushellX code, with valence neutrons occupying the 82-126 shell and valence protons the 50-82 shell, while for the Bi isotopes the valence spaces were the 82-126 shell for protons and the 126-184 shell for neutrons.

## Key numbers

* The cutoff momentum Lambda used in the V-low-k construction was 2.2 fm^-1.
* The oscillator parameter was 7.88 MeV for the A=132 region and 6.88 MeV for the A=208 region, obtained from the expression hbar omega = 45 A^{-1/3} - 25 A^{-2/3}.
* For the 134Sb pi g7/2 nu f7/2 multiplet, the leading component of the wave functions ranged from 88% (for the J^pi = 2^- state) to 100% for other states, indicating very little configuration mixing.
* The discrepancies between theory and experiment for the calculated states in 134Sb and 136Sb were on the order of a few tens of keV for most states, with the largest discrepancy occurring for the 7^- state.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous realistic shell-model studies of nuclei beyond doubly magic 132Sn (Coraggio 2005, Coraggio 2006, Covello 2007, Simpson 2007) by focusing on proton-neutron multiplets in odd-odd Sb isotopes. The V-low-k approach provides an alternative to the traditional Brueckner G-matrix method (Bogner 2002, Covello 2003) for renormalizing the short-range repulsion of the bare nucleon-nucleon potential. The results enable quantitative comparison between the spectroscopy of the 132Sn region and the well-studied 208Pb region, building on earlier observations of resemblance between these regions (Fornal 2001, Isakov 2006, Korgul 2007), and the new experimental data on 136Sb (Simpson 2007) provide a test for the model at an extreme N/Z ratio of 1.67.
