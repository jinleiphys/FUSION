# Time-odd mean fields in covariant density functional theory I. Non-rotating systems

> Time-odd mean fields (nuclear magnetism) are analyzed in the framework of covariant density functional theory (CDFT). It is shown that they always provide additional binding to the binding energies of odd-mass nuclei. This additional binding only weakly depends on the RMF parametrization reflecting good localization of the properties of time-odd mean fields in CDFT. The underlying microscopic mechanism is discussed in detail. Time-odd mean fields affect odd-even mass differences. However, our analysis suggests that the modifications of the strength of pairing correlations required to compensate for their effects are modest. In contrast, time-odd mean fields have profound effect on the properties of odd-proton nuclei in the vicinity of proton-drip line. Their presence can modify the half-lives of proton-emitters (by many orders of magnitude in light nuclei) and affect considerably the possibilities of their experimental observation.

## Key claim

Time-odd mean fields (nuclear magnetism) always provide additional binding to the binding energies of odd-mass nuclei in covariant density functional theory, but the magnitude of this additional binding is typically below 200 keV and only reaches 300 keV in some lower mass nuclei, with the effect being largest near the proton-drip line and smallest near the neutron-drip line.

## Method

The authors used Cranked Relativistic Mean Field (CRMF) theory without pairing correlations to isolate the effects of time-odd mean fields in non-rotating systems. They performed fully self-consistent calculations with and without nuclear magnetism (NM and WNM schemes) for fixed nuclear configurations, as well as perturbative calculations to isolate pure time-odd mean field effects without polarization of time-even fields. The calculations were performed in a basis of anisotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator states, primarily using the NL3 parametrization of the RMF Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- Additional binding due to nuclear magnetism is typically below 200 keV, reaching up to 300 keV in some lower mass nuclei
- The correction for spurious center-of-mass motion is given by E_cm = -(3/4) * 41 * A^(-1/3) MeV
- The fermionic basis truncation uses shells up to N_F=12 for light and medium-mass nuclei, and N_F=14 for actinides; bosonic basis uses N_B=16
- The oscillator frequency is set to hbar*omega_0 = 41 * A^(-1/3) MeV
- The deformation parameters used are beta_0=0.3 (beta_0=0.4 for superdeformed states) and gamma=0 degrees

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of time-odd mean fields from the Skyrme energy density functional framework (where they were studied by Dobaczewski and Dudek (1995) and Satula (1999)) to covariant density functional theory, where much less attention had been paid to these fields. The analysis shows that unlike in Skyrme EDF where time-odd mean fields can be either attractive or repulsive depending on parametrization, in CDFT they are always attractive and show very weak dependence on the RMF parametrization. This work enables better understanding of binding energies for mass table fits and nuclear astrophysics applications, and provides a foundation for studying time-odd mean fields in rotating systems in a forthcoming article.
