# Peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering at fifth order of chiral perturbation theory

> We present the two- and three-pion exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction which occur at next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N4LO, fifth order) of chiral effective field theory, and calculate nucleon-nucleon scattering in peripheral partial waves with L>=3 using low-energy constants that were extracted from pi-N analysis at fourth order. While the net three-pion exchange contribution is moderate, the two-pion exchanges turn out to be sizeable and prevailingly repulsive, thus, compensating the excessive attraction characteristic for NNLO and N3LO. As a result, the N4LO predictions for the phase shifts of peripheral partial waves are in very good agreement with the data (with the only exception of the 1F3 wave). We also discuss the issue of the order-by-order convergence of the chiral expansion for the NN interaction.

# Peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering at fifth order of chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The two-pion exchange contributions at N4LO (fifth order) are sizeable and prevailingly repulsive, compensating the excessive attraction characteristic of NNLO and N3LO predictions; as a result, the N4LO phase shift predictions for peripheral partial waves with L>=3 are in very good agreement with data, with the only exception of the 1F3 wave.

## Method

The authors derived the two-pion exchange (2PE) and three-pion exchange (3PE) contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction at N4LO using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with the most general fourth-order Lagrangian for pions and nucleons. They calculated perturbative K-matrices for neutron-proton scattering in peripheral partial waves (L>=3) using low-energy constants (LECs) extracted from a fourth-order pi-N analysis, with spectral-function regularization applied via a cutoff tilde-Lambda in the range 0.7 to 1.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The N4LO 2PE contributions are prevailingly repulsive and compensate the excessive attraction of lower orders, bringing phase shift predictions into agreement with data for all F and G waves except 1F3.
- The SFR cutoff tilde-Lambda was varied between 0.7 and 1.5 GeV to produce uncertainty bands; at N3LO the variations are very large and always too attractive, while at N4LO the variations are small and predictions are close to data.
- The LECs used include c1 = -0.75 GeV^-1, c2 = 3.49 GeV^-1, c3 = -4.77 GeV^-1, c4 = 3.34 GeV^-1, with g_A = 1.29 and f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
- The average pion mass used in multi-pion exchanges is m_pi = 138.039 MeV, with the nucleon mass M_N = 938.9182 MeV.
- Phase shifts were calculated for T_lab up to 300 MeV for partial waves with L>=3.

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT treatment of the NN interaction from N3LO (fourth order) to N4LO (fifth order), addressing the well-known problem of excessive attraction at NNLO and N3LO documented in prior work by Entem and Machleidt (2002, 2003) and Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (2005). The results enable future inclusion of N4LO three-nucleon forces in nuclear structure calculations, which may resolve persistent problems such as the Ay puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering. The paper also raises the open question of whether the chiral expansion of the NN amplitude converges, since NNLO, N3LO, and N4LO contributions are all about the same magnitude, motivating future investigations at N5LO.
