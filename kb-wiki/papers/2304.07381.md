# Properties of First-Order Hadron-Quark Phase Transition from Inverting Neutron Star Observables

> By inverting the observational data of several neutron star observables in the three dimensional parameter space of the constant speed of sound (CSS) model while fixing all hadronic Equation of State parameters at their currently known most probable values, we constrain the three parameters of the CSS model and their correlations. Using two lower radius limits of $R_{2.01}=11.41$ km and $R_{2.01}=12.2$ km for PSR J0740+6620 obtained from two independent analyses using different approaches by the Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) Collaboration, the speed of sound squared $c_{\rm QM}^2$ in quark matter is found to have a lower limit of $0.35$ and $0.43$ in unit of $c^2$, respectively, above its conformal limit of $c_{\rm QM}^2<1/3$. An approximately linear correlation between the first-order hadron-quark transition density $ρ_t$ and its strength $Δ\varepsilon$ is found. Moreover, the presence of twin star is deemed improbable by the present work.

## Key claim

By inverting neutron star observables within the constant speed of sound (CSS) model while fixing hadronic equation of state parameters at their most probable values, the authors find that the speed of sound squared in quark matter has a lower limit of 0.35 or 0.43 (depending on the adopted radius constraint), which is above the conformal limit of 1/3; this violation of the conformal limit is conditional on the assumption of a first-order hadron-quark phase transition in hybrid stars.

## Method

The authors used the constant speed of sound (CSS) model, which assumes a first-order hadron-quark phase transition with three free parameters: transition density ρ_t, energy density discontinuity Δε, and constant speed of sound squared c²_QM in quark matter. They fixed all hadronic equation of state parameters for npeμ matter at β-equilibrium to their currently known most probable values, then performed a brute-force inversion of neutron star observables (maximum mass, radius of a 2.01 M⊙ star, and tidal deformability of a 1.4 M⊙ star) by scanning the three-dimensional CSS parameter space through nested loops to find all parameter combinations satisfying the observational constraints.

## Key numbers

- Lower limit of speed of sound squared in quark matter: c²_QM > 0.35 (using R₂.₀₁ = 11.41 km constraint) and c²_QM > 0.43 (using R₂.₀₁ = 12.2 km constraint), both above the conformal limit of c²_QM < 1/3.
- Upper limit for transition density: ρ_t/ρ₀ < 3.03 (with R₂.₀₁ = 11.41 km) and ρ_t/ρ₀ < 2.45 (with R₂.₀₁ = 12.2 km).
- Upper limit for energy density discontinuity: Δε < 231 MeV fm⁻³ (with R₂.₀₁ = 11.41 km) and Δε < 175 MeV fm⁻³ (with R₂.₀₁ = 12.2 km).
- Linear correlation fits on the causality surface (c²_QM = 1): Δε = -112.91(ρ_t/ρ₀) + 345.86 MeV fm⁻³ (r=0.9994) for R₂.₀₁ = 11.41 km; Δε = -119.89(ρ_t/ρ₀) + 296.33 MeV fm⁻³ (r=0.9994) for R₂.₀₁ = 12.2 km.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the CSS model (Alford, Han, and Prakash, 2013) by simultaneously constraining all three CSS parameters from neutron star observations rather than assuming representative speed of sound values. The finding that the conformal limit c²_QM < 1/3 is violated in hybrid stars with a first-order phase transition is consistent with earlier analyses (Kurkela et al., 2014; Bedaque and Steiner, 2015; Alsing et al., 2018; Godzieba et al., 2021), and the upper limits on transition density (ρ_t/ρ₀ < 2.5) agree with results from Miao et al. (2020), Xie et al. (2021), Liang et al. (2021), and Somasundaram et al. (2022). The work enables future studies to use more precise radius measurements from NICER and other observatories to further constrain the correlation between transition density and phase transition strength, and to test the possibility of twin stars.
