# Photon and nucleon induced production of Theta^+

> We investigate Theta^+ production via photon and nucleon induced reactions. We observe that the positive parity Theta^+ production provides about ten times larger total cross sections than those of the negative parity one in both photon and nucleon induced reactions due to P-wave enhancement of the K-N-Theta vertex. We also consider the model independent method in the nucleon induced reaction to determine the parity of Theta^+ and show clearly distinguishable signals for the two parities.

## Key claim
The total cross sections for positive parity Theta^+ production are about ten times larger than those for negative parity Theta^+ in both photon and nucleon induced reactions, due to P-wave enhancement of the K-N-Theta vertex; however, this factor is a model-dependent result from Born diagram calculations with specific form factors and coupling constants, and the authors note that theoretical ambiguities remain.

## Method
The authors performed Born diagram calculations for Theta^+ production via photon induced reactions (gamma N to anti-K Theta^+) and nucleon induced reactions (np to Y Theta^+ and polarized pp to Sigma^+ Theta^+), including pseudoscalar K-exchange and vector K*-exchange contributions. For the photon induced reactions, they employed both pseudo-scalar (PS) and pseudo-vector (PV) coupling schemes for the K-N-Theta vertex, used a gauge invariant form factor, and set the anomalous magnetic moment of Theta^+ to -0.8. For the nucleon induced reactions, they used the Nijmegen potential for K-N-Y coupling constants and a monopole form factor with a cutoff mass of 1.0 GeV, and considered only the Y = Lambda channel since sigma_Lambda is about 5 times sigma_Sigma0.

## Key numbers
- Positive parity Theta^+ production provides total cross sections about ten times larger than negative parity Theta^+ in both photon and nucleon induced reactions.
- The decay width Gamma_{Theta to K N} = 15 MeV is used to obtain the K-N-Theta coupling constant.
- The anomalous magnetic moment of Theta^+, kappa_Theta, is set to -0.8.
- For the nucleon induced reaction np to Lambda Theta^+, sigma_Lambda is approximately 5 times sigma_Sigma0.
- A monopole type form factor with a cutoff mass of 1.0 GeV is employed for the nucleon induced reactions.
- If initial state interaction is considered, the order of magnitudes of the total cross sections will be reduced by about a factor of three.
- The threshold energy for the polarized pp reaction is approximately 2730 MeV.
- Up to about 100 MeV above the threshold, the spin observable A_XX shows clear difference between the two parities of Theta^+.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical investigation of Theta^+ production following the observation of evidence for the pentaquark by the LEPS collaboration (Nakano, 2003), motivated by predictions from Diakonov et al. (1997). The authors build on the model-independent parity determination method suggested by Thomas et al. (2004) for polarized pp scattering, and also reference the spin observable A_XX proposed by Hanhart et al. (2004). The results enable future experimental tests by the COSY-TOF collaboration to determine the parity of Theta^+ through polarized pp scattering.
