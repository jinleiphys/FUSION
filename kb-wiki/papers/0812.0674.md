# BCS-BEC Crossover and Thermodynamics in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter with Pairings in Isospin I=0 and I=1 Channels

> The BCS-BEC crossover and phase diagram for asymmetric nuclear superfluid with pairings in isospin I = 0 and I = 1 channels are investigated at mean field level, by using a density dependent nucleon-nucleon potential. Induced by the in-medium nucleon mass and density dependent coupling constants, neutron-proton Cooper pairs could be in BEC state at sufficiently low density, but there is no chance for the BEC formation of neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairs at any density and asymmetry. We calculate the phase diagram in asymmetry-temperature plane for weakly interacting nuclear superfluid, and find that including the I = 1 channel changes significantly the phase structure at low temperature. There appears a new phase with both I = 0 and I = 1 pairings at low temperature and low asymmetry, and the gapless state in any phase with I = 1 pairing is washed out and all excited nucleons are fully gapped.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutron-proton Cooper pairs in asymmetric nuclear matter can undergo a BCS-BEC crossover at sufficiently low density, but neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairs cannot form a BEC state at any density or asymmetry, and that including the isospin I=1 pairing channel introduces a new mixed phase at low temperature and low asymmetry while eliminating gapless excitations in any I=1 pairing phase.

## Method

The authors employed a mean-field formalism using a density-dependent contact interaction (DDCI) potential to model the nucleon-nucleon interaction, with a density-dependent effective nucleon mass derived from the Gogny force D1. They solved coupled gap equations and number equations for the condensates in the isospin I=0 (neutron-proton) and I=1 (neutron-neutron and proton-proton) channels, including the possibility of a single-plane-wave FFLO state, and determined the ground state by minimizing the free energy. The BCS-BEC crossover was characterized by calculating the scaled root-mean-square pair radius, scaled chemical potential, scaled gap parameter, effective scattering length, and probability of finding a pair within the average inter-nucleon distance.

## Key numbers

- The BCS-BEC crossover for neutron-proton pairs in symmetric nuclear matter occurs between nuclear densities of approximately 0.003 rho_0 and 0.4 rho_0, where rho_0 is normal nuclear density; the BEC region is at rho/rho_0 less than 0.003 and the BCS region at rho/rho_0 greater than 0.4.
- The density-dependent coupling constants used are v = -481 MeV fm^3 for the I=1 channel and v = -530 MeV fm^3 for the I=0 channel, with an energy cutoff of 60 MeV in both channels.
- The effective nucleon mass is calculated from the Gogny force D1 with parameters including two ranges: mu_1 = 0.7 fm and mu_2 = 1.2 fm, with corresponding W, B, H, and M coefficients listed in Table 1.
- The parameters for the DDCI potential are w = 0.45 and gamma = 0.47 for the I=1 channel, and w = 0 for the I=0 channel.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter that considered only isospin I=0 pairing (Baldo 1995, Lombardo 2001) or only I=1 pairing (Matsuo 2006, Margueron 2007, Isayev 2008) by including both channels simultaneously. It also builds on studies of phase transitions induced by Fermi surface mismatch in asymmetric nuclear matter (Sedrakian 2000, Muther 2003, Alford 2005). The results enable future investigations of the phase diagram of nuclear superfluid in neutron stars and heavy nuclei, particularly the competition between different pairing channels and the nature of the mixed phase at low temperature and low asymmetry.
