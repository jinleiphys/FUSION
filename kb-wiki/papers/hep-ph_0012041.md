# Domain walls of high-density QCD

> We show that in very dense quark matter there must exist metastable domain walls where the axial U(1) phase of the color-superconducting condensate changes by 2pi. The decay rate of the domain walls is exponentially suppressed and we compute it semiclassically. We give an estimate of the critical chemical potential above which our analysis is under theoretical control.

## Key claim

In very dense quark matter, metastable domain walls must exist where the axial U(1) phase of the color-superconducting condensate changes by 2π, but this conclusion is only under theoretical control above a critical chemical potential estimated to be about 6ΛQCD ∼ 1 GeV, below which the effective description breaks down.

## Method

The authors construct an effective low-energy Lagrangian for the Goldstone mode η of the spontaneously broken axial U(1) symmetry in two-flavor color-superconducting QCD, including an instanton-induced potential term. They then solve the classical equations of motion to find domain-wall solutions and compute the semiclassical decay rate of these walls via nucleation of holes bounded by vortex lines, using perturbative results for the decay constant f and velocity u of the η boson from Beane, Bedaque and Savage.

## Key numbers

- The decay constant of the η boson is f² = μ²/(8π²) and its velocity is u² = 1/3, for large chemical potential μ ≫ ΛQCD.
- The critical chemical potential for domain walls to appear is estimated as μcrit ∼ 6ΛQCD ∼ 1 GeV.
- The domain-wall tension is σ = 8√(2a) u f μ Δ, where a is a dimensionless function of μ.
- The η boson mass is m = 2π√a Δ, which becomes much smaller than the gap Δ at large μ.
- The decay rate of the domain wall is Γ ∼ exp[−(π⁴/3)(u³/a)(f⁴/(μ²Δ²)) ln³(1/√a)], which is exponentially suppressed at high μ.
- The dimensionless function a(μ) = 5 × 10⁴ (ln(μ/ΛQCD))⁷ (ΛQCD/μ)^(29/3).

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Zhitnitsky for domain walls in QCD at zero temperature and baryon density, but now in the theoretically controllable regime of high baryon densities where weak-coupling calculations are reliable. The domain walls are analogous to N=1 axion domain walls from axion theory. The results enable future investigation of possible astrophysical consequences in neutron stars and can be generalized to the color-flavor-locking state of three-flavor QCD and to QCD with large isospin density, where lattice simulations could test the predictions.
