# Dirac Sea Contribution in Relativistic Random Phase Approximation

> In the hadrodynamics (QHD) there are two methods to take account of the contribution of negative-energy states in the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA). Dawson and Furnstahl made the ansatz that the Dirac sea were empty, while according to the Dirac hole theory the sea should be fully occupied. The two methods seem contradictory. Their close relationship and compatibility are explored and in particular the question of the ground-state (GS) instability resulting from Dawson-Furnstanhl's ansatz is discussed.

# Wiki Page: Dirac Sea Contribution in Relativistic Random Phase Approximation

## Key claim

The Dawson-Furnstahl (D-F) ansatz of an empty Dirac sea and Dirac's original hole theory of a fully occupied sea are compatible in the relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) for nuclear matter, provided the vacuum contribution from the filled sea can be neglected. The two methods yield identical results for excitation energies below the nucleon-antinucleon threshold (approximately 1.37 GeV), and the D-F ansatz does not cause ground-state instability if the vacuum contribution is already encoded in the effective Lagrangian parameters.

## Method

The authors compare the two approaches by calculating the correlation function and polarization tensor in symmetric nuclear matter using the Walecka sigma-omega model. They derive the nucleon propagator under both the Dirac filled-sea and D-F empty-sea prescriptions, then compute the lowest-order correlation function and the full RRPA response via the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the scalar-vector meson propagator. Numerical calculations are performed for the longitudinal response function with bare vertices and with phenomenological vertex form factors to study the effects of nucleon compositeness.

## Context

This work extends the earlier RRPA formulation of Dawson and Furnstahl (1990) and the detailed study of alpha-h pairs by Ring et al. (2001), while addressing the ground-state instability problem noted in the literature (references 7 and 8 in the paper). The authors show that Dirac's filled-sea approach is preferable for both conventional quantum hadrodynamics (CQHD) and modern effective field theory (MQHD) as developed by Furnstahl, Pickarewicz, and Serot (1997). The work enables a consistent treatment of vacuum contributions in RRPA calculations of nuclear giant resonances, building on the improvements demonstrated by Ma et al. (2001).
