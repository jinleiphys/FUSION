# QCD Phase Structure and Interactions at High Baryon Density: Continuation of BES Physics Program with CBM at FAIR

> We advocate for an active US participation in the international collaboration of the CBM experiment that will allow the US nuclear physics program to build on its successful exploration of the QCD phase diagram, use the expertise gained at RHIC to make complementary measurements at FAIR, and contribute to achieving the scientific goals of the beam energy scan (BES) program.

## Key claim

The paper advocates for active US participation in the CBM experiment at FAIR, arguing that the CBM program is essential to complement and extend the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) program's search for the QCD critical point and phase structure at high baryon density, particularly because the RHIC BES fixed-target measurements above 3.0 GeV lack the rapidity coverage and event statistics needed for definitive cumulant analyses.

## Method

The paper reviews results from the RHIC BES program across nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energies from 3.0 to 200 GeV, covering baryon chemical potentials from 760 to 25 MeV, and compares these with the capabilities of the planned CBM experiment at FAIR, which will operate at beam energies corresponding to 2.9 to 4.9 GeV with event rates up to 10 MHz. The authors examine multiple observables including net-proton cumulants, collective flow, hyperon and hypernuclei production, and dilepton spectra, evaluating how CBM's high-luminosity, full mid-rapidity coverage, and ability to collide various nuclear species will address limitations in the existing BES data.

## Key numbers

- The RHIC BES program covered collision energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0 to 200 GeV, with baryon chemical potentials ranging from mu_B = 760 MeV to 25 MeV
- Among BES fixed-target energies, only the sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0 GeV data have full mid-rapidity coverage for protons (|y_p - y_beam| <= 0.5) and very high statistics (2,000 million events collected)
- The CBM experiment will operate at beam energies corresponding to sqrt(s_NN) = 2.9 to 4.9 GeV, probing baryon chemical potentials mu_B = 500 to 800 MeV
- The CBM experiment can take data at event rates up to 10 MHz
- Collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 to 4 GeV and 4 to 5 GeV are most sensitive to the equation of state at densities 2-3 and 3-4 times nuclear saturation density, respectively
- The fourth-order net-proton cumulant data show intriguing non-monotonic behavior in the energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 27 GeV, deviating from non-critical baselines from UrQMD and thermal models
- Hyperon and hypernuclei production peaks around sqrt(s_NN) = 3 to 10 GeV, with the peak around 4.0 to 4.5 GeV
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0 GeV, 2,000 million events have been collected, with initial analysis using 240 million events from 2018
- The ratio S_3 = (^3_Lambda H/^3He)/(Lambda/p) shows a gradual increase with collision energy, approaching the equilibrium limit at LHC energies, with the limiting value in canonical calculations about 2/3

## Context

This white paper extends the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program initiated in 2008, which searched for the QCD critical point and phase transition onset at STAR, and addresses limitations identified in BES-I and BES-II data. The paper builds on measurements from STAR (BES program), HADES, and NA60, and argues that CBM will enable decisive measurements of net-proton fluctuations, collective flow, hyperon interactions, and dilepton spectra that cannot be achieved with existing RHIC fixed-target data above 3.0 GeV due to kinematic and statistical limitations. The proposed measurements would provide crucial constraints for understanding the equation of state of dense nuclear matter relevant to neutron star structure and mergers, complementing theoretical work by Stephanov (2011) on critical fluctuations, Rapp (2021) on dilepton signatures of phase transitions, and Oliinychenko (2022) on flow constraints on the equation of state.
