# Isospin effects in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the density dependence of the symmetry energy in a relativistic description by decomposing the iso-vector mean field into contributions with different Lorentz properties. We find important effects of the iso-vector, scalar $δ$ channel on the density behavior of the symmetry energy. Finite nuclei studies show only moderate effects originating from the virtual $δ$ meson. In heavy ion collisions from Fermi to relativistic energies up to $1-2 AGeV$ one finds important contributions on the dynamics arising from the different treatment of the microscopic Lorentz structure of the symmetry energy. We discuss a variety of possible signals which could set constraints on the still unknown density dependence of the symmetry energy, when experimental data will be available. Examples of such observables are isospin collective flow, threshold production of pions and kaons, isospin equilibration and stopping in asymmetric systems like $Au+Au$, $Sn+Sn$ and $Ru(Zr)+Zr(Ru)$.

## Key claim

The density dependence of the symmetry energy at supra-normal densities is significantly influenced by the Lorentz structure of the iso-vector mean field, specifically the inclusion of the iso-vector, scalar δ meson channel, which leads to a stiffer symmetry energy at high densities; however, the authors note that "more heavy ion data with radioactive beams are needed to make a final definite statement."

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean field (RMF) theory within a Hartree approximation, solving the Dirac equation for baryons coupled to σ, ω, ρ, and δ meson fields self-consistently. They compared two non-linear Walecka model parameter sets (NLρ with only the ρ meson, and NLρδ with both ρ and δ mesons) and three Density Dependent Hadronic (DDH) model variants, applying these to both finite nuclei calculations and relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport simulations of heavy ion collisions at SIS energies (0.1–2 AGeV) for asymmetric systems such as Au+Au, Sn+Sn, and Ru(Zr)+Zr(Ru).

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation fixed to a4 = 30.5 MeV for all models.
- NLρδ model uses fρ = 3.15 fm² and fδ = 2.5 fm²; NLρ model uses fρ = 0.95 fm² and fδ = 0.0 fm².
- Beam energies studied: 0.4 AGeV and 1.528 AGeV for mixed Ru(Zr)+Zr(Ru) reactions.
- Impact parameters: semi-central at b = 6 fm for Sn+Sn flow calculations; central at b < 2 fm for Au+Au pion ratios; central at b = 1.5 fm for mixed reactions.
- For the imbalance ratio R(p) at target rapidity, the initial value for full transparency is Z^Zr/Z^Ru = 40/44 = 0.91 for protons and N^Zr/N^Ru = 56/52 = 1.077 for neutrons.
- The (π⁻/π⁺)-ratio shows a crossover at p_t^(0) ≈ 0.35, with opposite trends for high transverse momenta (p_t^(0) >> 0.35) between NLρ and NLρδ models.

## Context

This work extends relativistic mean field studies of asymmetric nuclear matter by explicitly decomposing the iso-vector mean field into vector (ρ meson) and scalar (δ meson) contributions, building on earlier non-relativistic studies by Bao (year not specified) and Greco (year not specified) that required additional momentum-dependent parameters. The findings are compared with microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations by Jong (year not specified), which show that the ρ meson coupling decreases with density while the δ meson coupling increases above saturation. The results enable future experimental constraints on the high-density symmetry energy using observables such as isospin collective flow, pion and kaon production ratios, and the imbalance ratio of light clusters (t/³He) from FOPI collaboration data.
