# Statistical analysis of beta decays and the effective value of g_A in the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation framework

> We perform a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) statistical analysis of a number of measured ground-state-to-ground-state single $β^+$/electron-capture and $β^-$ decays in the nuclear mass range A = 62 - 142. The corresponding experimental comparative half-lives (log ft values) are compared with the theoretical ones obtained by the use of the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with G-matrix based effective interactions. The MCMC analysis is performed separately for 47 isobaric triplets and 28 more extended isobaric chains of nuclei to extract values and uncertainties for the effective axial-vector coupling constant g_A in nuclear-structure calculations performed in the pnQRPA framework. As far as available, measured half-lives for two-neutrino double beta-minus decays occurring in the studied isobaric chains are analyzed as well.

## Key claim

The effective axial-vector coupling constant \(g_\text{A}\) in the pnQRPA framework is extracted from single beta-decay and electron-capture data across a wide mass range, but the extracted values show strong variation depending on the specific isobaric triplet or chain, and the results are not directly transferable to neutrinoless double beta decay because the latter involves much larger momentum exchange and higher multipole contributions.

## Method

The authors performed a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) statistical analysis comparing experimental comparative half-lives (\(\log ft\) values) of ground-state-to-ground-state single \(\beta^+\)/electron-capture and \(\beta^-\) decays with theoretical values computed using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with G-matrix based effective interactions. The analysis was conducted separately for 47 isobaric triplets and 28 more extended isobaric chains of nuclei in the mass range \(A = 62-142\), treating the particle-hole (\(g_\text{ph}\)) and particle-particle (\(g_\text{pp}\)) coupling parameters as independent for each decay transition, and using the Gamow-Teller giant resonance centroid to fix \(g_\text{ph}\) with a 15% relative uncertainty.

## Key numbers

* The free value of the axial-vector coupling constant is \(g_\text{A} = 1.269\).
* For the triplet \(^{100}\text{Mo}\), \(^{100}\text{Tc}\), \(^{100}\text{Ru}\), the fitted effective \(g_\text{A}\) is \(0.56^{+0.09}_{-0.12}\).
* For the triplet \(^{106}\text{Rh}\), \(^{106}\text{Pd}\), \(^{106}\text{Ag}\), the fitted effective \(g_\text{A}\) is \(0.40^{+0.02}_{-0.02}\).
* For the triplet \(^{108}\text{Ru}\), \(^{108}\text{Rh}\), \(^{108}\text{Pd}\), the fitted effective \(g_\text{A}\) is \(0.27^{+0.01}_{-0.02}\).
* For the triplet \(^{128}\text{Te}\), \(^{128}\text{I}\), \(^{128}\text{Xe}\), the fitted effective \(g_\text{A}\) is \(0.55^{+0.08}_{-0.03}\).
* The experimental \(2\nu\beta\beta\) half-life of \(^{100}\text{Mo}\) is \((0.71\pm 0.04)\times 10^{19}\) yr, and the triplet-based prediction is \((1.1\pm 0.6)\times 10^{19}\) yr.
* The experimental \(2\nu\beta\beta\) half-life of \(^{116}\text{Cd}\) is \((2.85\pm 0.15)\times 10^{19}\) yr, and the triplet-based prediction is \((1.3\pm 0.3)\times 10^{19}\) yr.
* The experimental \(2\nu\beta\beta\) half-life of \(^{128}\text{Te}\) is \((2.00\pm 0.30)\times 10^{24}\) yr, and the triplet-based prediction is \((0.8\pm 0.2)\times 10^{24}\) yr.

## Context

This work extends earlier pnQRPA analyses by Faessler (2008) and Suhonen (2005, 2013, 2014) to a wider mass region and introduces a refined MCMC statistical method that treats \(g_\text{pp}\) and \(g_\text{ph}\) as independent parameters for each decay transition, a freedom not explored before. The extracted effective \(g_\text{A}\) values (ranging from about 0.27 to 1.30 across different triplets) are consistent with the strong quenching reported in shell-model studies by Juodagalvis (2005) and Caurier (2012) and in the IBA-2 model by Barea (2013), but the authors caution that these results apply only to the \(1^+\) multipole at low momentum transfer and cannot be directly used for \(0\nu\beta\beta\) decay, which involves momentum exchanges of order 100 MeV and significant contributions from higher multipoles.
