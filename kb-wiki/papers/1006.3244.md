# Statistical Significance of the Gallium Anomaly

> We calculate the statistical significance of the anomalous deficit of electron neutrinos measured in the radioactive source experiments of the GALLEX and SAGE solar neutrino detectors taking into account the uncertainty of the detection cross section. We found that the statistical significance of the anomaly is about 3.0 sigma. A fit of the data in terms of neutrino oscillations favors at about 2.7 sigma short-baseline electron neutrino disappearance with respect to the null hypothesis of no oscillations.

# Statistical Significance of the Gallium Anomaly

## Key claim

After properly accounting for the uncertainty of the detection cross section (using Haxton's cross section estimates rather than Bahcall's best-fit values alone), the Gallium anomaly remains statistically significant at about 3.0 sigma, and a fit favoring short-baseline electron neutrino disappearance over the null hypothesis of no oscillations is favored at about 2.7 sigma.

## Method

The authors combined the measured ratios of observed to predicted event rates from the four GALLEX and SAGE radioactive source experiments (two with Cr-51 sources, one with Cr-51, one with Ar-37) and rescaled them using Haxton's cross section estimates, which include uncertainties for transitions to excited states of Ge-71. They then computed the probability distribution of the combined ratio R_Ga by integrating over the truncated Gaussian distribution of the cross section rescaling factor, and performed a maximum likelihood analysis of the data in terms of a two-neutrino short-baseline oscillation model with survival probability P = 1 - sin^2(2theta) sin^2(Delta m^2 L / 4E).

## Key numbers

- Combined ratio of measured to predicted event rates (using Bahcall cross sections): R_Ga_B = 0.86 +0.05/-0.05 (1 sigma), +0.10/-0.10 (2 sigma), +0.15/-0.15 (3 sigma)
- After rescaling by Haxton cross section uncertainty: R_Ga = 0.76 +0.09/-0.08 (1 sigma), +0.17/-0.15 (2 sigma), +0.24/-0.21 (3 sigma)
- Probability of R_Ga < 1: 99.86% (3.0 sigma anomaly)
- Best-fit oscillation parameters: sin^2(2theta)_bf = 0.50, Delta m^2_bf = 2.24 eV^2
- Likelihood ratio between null hypothesis and oscillation hypothesis: 8 x 10^-3, corresponding to Delta chi^2 = 9.7 (2.7 sigma favoring oscillations)
- Lower bounds at 99% C.L.: sin^2(2theta) > 0.07, Delta m^2 > 0.35 eV^2

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of the Gallium anomaly (e.g., Bahcall 1997, Haxton 1998) by explicitly incorporating the uncertainty of the detection cross section for transitions to excited states of Ge-71, which previous simple approaches either ignored or arbitrarily suppressed. The results are consistent with the possibility of short-baseline electron neutrino disappearance in four-neutrino mixing schemes, and the authors note compatibility with the reactor antineutrino anomaly reported by Mention et al. (2011). The findings motivate future experimental tests such as the proposed two-zone SAGE measurement and Borexino radioactive source experiments, and have implications for cosmology regarding the possible existence of a fourth massive neutrino with mass above about 0.6 eV.
