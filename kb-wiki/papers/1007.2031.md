# Structure of the sigma meson and the softening

> We study the structure of the sigma meson, the lowest-lying resonance of the pi pi scattering in the scalar-isoscalar channel, through the softening phenomena associated with the partial restoration of chiral symmetry. We build dynamical chiral models to describe the pi pi scattering amplitude, in which the sigma meson is described either as the chiral partner of the pion or as the dynamically generated resonance through the pi pi attraction. It is shown that the internal structure is reflected in the softening phenomena; the softening pattern of the dynamically generated sigma meson is qualitatively different from the behavior of the chiral partner of the pion. On the other hand, in the symmetry restoration limit, the dynamically generated sigma meson behaves similarly to the chiral partner.

## Key claim

The softening pattern of the sigma meson under partial chiral symmetry restoration is qualitatively different depending on whether the sigma is the chiral partner of the pion or a dynamically generated resonance from pi pi attraction; however, in the full restoration limit with vanishing pion mass, both descriptions yield the same asymptotic behavior.

## Method

The authors construct two unitary chiral models for pi pi scattering in the scalar-isoscalar channel using the N/D method, with a tree-level amplitude derived from the linear sigma model Lagrangian. A parameter x interpolates between the chiral partner description (x=1, bare sigma pole present) and the dynamically generated resonance (x=0, no bare sigma pole, only leading-order chiral interaction). Chiral symmetry restoration is implemented by scaling the chiral condensate from its vacuum value toward zero while keeping the pion mass fixed, and the sigma pole trajectory and scattering amplitude are computed as functions of the condensate.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length in vacuum: model A (chiral partner) gives a = 0.244 m_pi^{-1}; model B (dynamically generated) gives a = 0.174 m_pi^{-1}; experimental value is approximately 0.216 m_pi^{-1}
- Pole position in vacuum: model A at 423 - 126i MeV; model B at 364 - 356i MeV; experimental reference value is 441 - 272i MeV
- Vacuum parameters: sigma condensate = 93 MeV, pion mass = 140 MeV, bare sigma mass = 550 MeV
- In the restoration limit for the dynamically generated sigma, the coupling constant squared is g^2 = (4 pi)^2 (pi/(3 sqrt(3)) - 1/2)^{-1} m_pi^2

## Context

This work extends the study of sigma meson structure beyond conventional mass spectrum and decay comparisons (e.g., Jaffe (1976) for tetraquark, Oller (1997) for dynamical generation) by using the response to chiral symmetry restoration as a diagnostic tool. It builds on prior softening studies by Hatsuda (1994) and Jido (2000) that showed threshold enhancement occurs in both linear and nonlinear realizations, but demonstrates that the pole trajectory differs qualitatively due to the appearance of a virtual state in the dynamically generated case. The results enable future experimental searches for softening signatures in nuclear medium and motivate further analysis including the sigma as a CDD pole.
