# The piNNN--NNN problem. Connectedness, transition amplitudes and quasi-particle approximation

> In this paper we review the present status of the piNNN--NNN problem. In particular, we re-consider the chain-labelled approach recently proposed by us, and identify a class of graphs, previously overlooked, which prevents the kernel of the corresponding piNNN--NNN equations from being connected. We propose some pproximate schemes, yielding connected-kernel equations. A generalization of the residue method allows to relate the transition amplitudes for the coupled piNNN--NNN system to the chain-labelled formalism. The quasi-particle approach is extended to the present situation, where emission/absorption of particles is allowed. The open problems for the piNNN--NNN system in the light of the present and of previous approaches are finally discussed.

## Key claim

The chain-labelled approach previously proposed by the authors for the coupled πNNN–NNN system does not yield connected-kernel equations, contrary to their earlier belief; a class of previously overlooked self-energy-type graphs, arising when 3+1 and 2+2 partitions are treated on equal footing, prevents the kernel from being connected, but connectedness can be restored by switching off the coupling between the πNNN and NNN sectors in the 2+2 partitions or by projecting the exact sub-system amplitudes onto the sole πNNN sector.

## Method

The authors start from the Afnan–Blankleider (AB) transition operators for the πNNN–NNN system and embed them into a Faddeev–Yakubovskiĭ-type (FY) scheme via a generalization of the Grassberger–Sandhas (GS) matrix method, introducing chain-labelled operators for two-cluster partitions of three types (Type-I: πNN plus spectator nucleon; Type-II: πN and NN pairs; Type-III: π plus three nucleons). They then analyze the connectedness of the resulting integral equations by examining the kernel for disconnected graphs, and propose approximate schemes that yield connected-kernel equations. A generalization of the residue method is employed to relate physical transition amplitudes for two-fragment→two-fragment processes (including pion absorption/emission) to the chain-labelled formalism, and the quasi-particle approximation (QPA) is extended to allow particle emission/absorption.

## Key numbers

- The standard Yakubovskiĭ–Grassberger–Sandhas (YGS) scheme for four nucleons involves 18 coupled equations; the present πNNN–NNN extension yields a set of 24 coupled equations.
- In the approximation where coupling between πNNN and NNN sectors is switched off for Type-II partitions, the equations reduce to a set of 21 coupled equations, with a kernel that becomes connected after two iterations.
- The disconnectedness problem arises from self-energy-type contributions to the transition from a (πN)(NN) to a N(πNN) configuration with an intermediate zero-pion state; these disconnected graphs appear in any iteration of the kernel, with a typical disconnected term exhibited in the fourth iteration (K⁴).

## Context

This work extends the Grassberger–Sandhas n-body method (Grassberger and Sandhas, 1967; Sandhas, 1980) to systems where particle number is not conserved, specifically the πNNN–NNN problem. It corrects the authors' own previous claims (Cattapan and Canton, 1995, 1996) about connectedness of their chain-labelled equations, and identifies the same disconnectedness issue noted earlier by Avishai and Mizutani (1983) in the context of mass renormalization. The paper enables future connected-kernel formulations of pion production/absorption in few-nucleon systems, and compares the rigorous FY-type approaches to the phenomenological coupled-channel formalism of Ueda (various years), finding that the latter assumes ad hoc couplings excluded in microscopic FY-type formulations.
