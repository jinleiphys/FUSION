# What Can Possibly Go Wrong?

> 20 pages LaTeX2e (pdflatex) including 4 figures as .pdf files using includegraphics and svjour3. Text-identical to publication in Few-Body Systems, Special Issue "Celebrating 30 years of the Steven Weinberg's paper Nuclear Forces from Chiral Lagrangians", but correcting journal's erroneous placing of motto

## Key claim

The paper argues that while Effective Field Theories (EFTs) are powerful tools for nuclear physics, their application involves numerous assumptions and choices (power counting, degrees of freedom, naturalness) that can lead to errors if not carefully verified; the "Folk Theorem" that EFTs are automatically correct is insufficient, and practitioners must remain vigilant against convenience, prejudice, and hidden assumptions.

## Method

The author reviews several conceptual pitfalls in nuclear EFTs, focusing on chiral EFT (χEFT) and pion-less EFT (EFT(π̸)). He examines the expansion parameter Q = p_typ/Λ_EFT, the role of Naïve Dimensional Analysis for estimating theory uncertainties, the choice of degrees of freedom (pions, nucleons, Delta resonance, f0(500) meson), the importance of conservation laws and approximate symmetries (including the unitarity limit), and the consistency conditions for power-counting when shallow bound states require non-perturbative treatment at leading order.

## Key numbers

- Q ≈ 0.4 in χEFT with a dynamical Delta resonance, at best 1/2 without Deltas, and 1/3 in EFT(π̸) (pion-less EFT)
- The Delta(1232) resonance peak energy is about 300 MeV ≈ 2m_π above the nucleon mass, with width Γ/2 ≈ 70 MeV ≈ m_π/2
- The breakdown scale Λ_χ of χEFT with dynamical Delta is Λ_χ ≈ [700...1000] MeV, consistent with ω and ρ meson masses
- The expansion parameter at p_typ ∼ m_π is about 1/6 for pion physics and about 0.4 for the perturbative Delta; convergence reaches ≤ ±3% around m_π at N⁴LO
- The NN scattering length fine-tuning gives 1/(m_π a) ≤ 0.3
- Typical binding momentum in heavy nuclei is γ_A ∼ √(2M B_A/A) → 120 MeV ≤ m_π for B_A/A ≈ 8 MeV
- The scale Λ_NN = 16π f_π²/(g_A² M) ≈ 300 MeV lies between typical low scales and the breakdown scale

## Context

This paper extends the critical examination of Weinberg's approach to nuclear EFTs (Weinberg 1990, 1991, 1992) by cataloging common mistakes and hidden assumptions. It discusses the KSW power-counting variant (Kaplan 1998) as a consistent alternative to the standard Weinberg power-counting, and references the unitarity-limit expansion (Kievsky 2015, König 2016, van Kolck 2017) as a paradigm shift toward understanding nuclear structure from approximate symmetries. The work enables more rigorous uncertainty quantification and self-consistency checks in future nuclear EFT calculations.
