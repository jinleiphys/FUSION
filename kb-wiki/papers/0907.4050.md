# Large scale shell model calculations for even-even $^{62-66}$Fe isotopes

> The recently measured experimental data of Legnaro National Laboratories on neutron rich even isotopes of $^{62-66}$Fe with A=62,64,66 have been interpreted in the framework of large scale shell model. Calculations have been performed with a newly derived effective interaction GXPF1A in full $\it{fp}$ space without truncation. The experimental data is very well explained for $^{62}$Fe, satisfactorily reproduced for $^{64}$Fe and poorly fitted for $^{66}$Fe. The increasing collectivity reflected in experimental data when approaching N=40 is not reproduced in calculated values. This indicates that whereas the considered valence space is adequate for $^{62}$Fe, inclusion of higher orbits from $\it{sdg}$ shell is required for describing $^{66}$Fe.

# Large scale shell model calculations for even-even $^{62-66}$Fe isotopes

## Key claim

Large-scale shell model calculations using the GXPF1A effective interaction in the full $fp$ valence space without truncation reproduce experimental data very well for $^{62}$Fe, satisfactorily for $^{64}$Fe, but poorly for $^{66}$Fe; the increasing collectivity observed experimentally when approaching N=40 is not captured, indicating that the $fp$ space is adequate for $^{62}$Fe but inclusion of higher $sdg$ shell orbits is required for $^{66}$Fe.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations for $^{62}$Fe, $^{64}$Fe, and $^{66}$Fe using the GXPF1A effective interaction in the full $fp$ configuration space (0f$_{7/2}$, 1p$_{3/2}$, 0f$_{5/2}$, 1p$_{1/2}$ orbitals) with $^{40}$Ca as an inert core, without any truncation on particle excitations. Calculations were carried out using the ANTOINE code on the SGI-cluster computer at GANIL, with matrix dimensions in the m-scheme reaching up to 14,625,240 for the 0$^+$ state of $^{62}$Fe. Results were compared to experimental data from Legnaro National Laboratories and to previous calculations using the $fpg$ interaction with truncation from Lunardi et al. (2007).

## Key numbers

- For $^{62}$Fe, discrepancies between calculated and experimental excitation energies are 70 keV for the 2$^+$ state, 5 keV for the 4$^+$ state, and 138 keV for the 6$^+$ state; with the $fpg$ interaction the corresponding discrepancies are 6 keV, 219 keV, and 494 keV.
- The calculated 2$^+$ excitation energies are 0.807 MeV for $^{62}$Fe, 0.899 MeV for $^{64}$Fe, and 1.160 MeV for $^{66}$Fe.
- The calculated B(E2; 2$_1^+$ $\rightarrow$ 0$^+$) values are 14.8 W.u. for $^{62}$Fe, 10.1 W.u. for $^{64}$Fe, and 5.4 W.u. for $^{66}$Fe, using effective charges e$_\pi$ = 0.5 and e$_\nu$ = 1.5.
- The calculated quadrupole moments Q(2$^+$) are -26 e fm$^2$ for $^{62}$Fe, -19 e fm$^2$ for $^{64}$Fe, and -17 e fm$^2$ for $^{66}$Fe.
- Computing time was three days for $^{62}$Fe compared to two hours for $^{66}$Fe, reflecting matrix dimensions of 14,625,240 and 3,952 for the 0$^+$ state, respectively.

## Context

This work extends shell model studies of neutron-rich $fp$-shell nuclei, following the derivation of the GXPF1A interaction by Honma et al. (2005) which modified five two-body matrix elements from the earlier GXPF1 interaction to improve agreement for unstable isotopes. The calculations directly test the adequacy of the full $fp$ space against experimental data from Lunardi et al. (2007), who used a truncated $fpg$ space including the g$_{9/2}$ orbital. The results show that while the $fp$ space suffices for $^{62}$Fe, the failure for $^{66}$Fe near N=40 demonstrates the need to include the $sdg$ shell, enabling future calculations that incorporate the g$_{9/2}$ orbital to capture the increasing collectivity observed experimentally.
