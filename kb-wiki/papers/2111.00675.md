# Local two- and three-nucleon chiral interactions

> Understanding the structure and reactions of nuclei from first principles has been a long-standing goal of nuclear physics. In this respect, few- and many-body systems provide a unique laboratory for studying nuclear interactions. In the past couple of decades, the modeling of nuclear interactions has progressed significantly owing, in particular, to the development of chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT), a low-energy effective representation of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Within $χ$EFT, many studies have dealt with the construction of both two- and three-nucleon interactions. The aim of the present article is to provide a concise account of chiral interaction models that are local in configuration space, and to report on a selection of recent results for nuclear systems obtained with these interactions.

## Key claim

The paper provides a concise account of chiral interaction models that are local in configuration space, suitable for quantum Monte Carlo methods, and reports on a selection of recent results for nuclear systems obtained with these interactions; the authors note that no existing interactions and electroweak currents can simultaneously predict different nuclear few- and many-body observables over a wide range of mass number within quantified theoretical uncertainties.

## Method

The authors review the construction of local chiral two-nucleon (2N) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions within both Delta-less and Delta-full chiral effective field theory formulations, describing the regularization schemes, the low-energy constants (LECs) determined by fits to scattering data and nuclear observables, and the application of these interactions in quantum Monte Carlo (Green's function Monte Carlo and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo) and hyperspherical-harmonics expansion methods for few- and many-body systems up to A=16.

## Key numbers

- The Delta-less interactions use a cutoff scale R0 in the range of 1.0–1.2 fm, with 11 LECs fixed by fits to 2N phase shifts up to 150 MeV laboratory energy.
- The Delta-full (Norfolk) interactions use cutoff radii (R_L, R_S) of (1.2, 0.8) fm, (1.0, 0.7) fm, and (0.8, 0.6) fm for models a, b, and c respectively, corresponding to momentum-space cutoffs from about 660 MeV down to 500 MeV.
- The Delta-full interactions have 26 LECs; fits to the Granada database achieved chi-squared per datum less than or equal to 1.1 for class I (0–125 MeV) and less than or equal to 1.4 for class II (0–200 MeV), using about 2700 and 3700 data points respectively.
- Preliminary AFDMC calculations of pure neutron matter with NV2+3-Ia/b and NV2+3-IIa/b models are not compatible with the existence of two solar mass neutron stars, while models with smaller c_E values (NV2+3-Ia*/b* and NV2+3-IIa*/b*) mitigate this problem and predict low-lying state energies in light nuclei within 4% of experimental values.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on chiral effective field theory interactions initiated by Weinberg (1990, 1991, 1992) and Ordonez et al. (1995), and addresses the need for local chiral interactions suitable for quantum Monte Carlo methods, which cannot use the strongly non-local momentum-space interactions common in the literature. The paper discusses how these local interactions enable calculations with Green's function Monte Carlo and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo methods for nuclei up to A=16 and neutron matter, and identifies the open challenge of simultaneously describing light nuclei and dense neutron star matter within a single interaction model.
