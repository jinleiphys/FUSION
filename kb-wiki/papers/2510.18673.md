# Charm-strange meson production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study charm-strange mesons, or $D_s$, $D_s^*$, $D_{s0}^*(2317)$, and $D_{s1}(2460)$ mesons by focusing on their production by coalescence from a quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. Starting from the investigation of the transverse momentum distribution of both charm and strange quarks through transverse momentum distributions of $Ď•$ and $D^0$ mesons, we calculate the transverse momentum distributions and yields of $D_s$, $D_s^*$, $D_{s0}^*(2317)$, and $D_{s1}(2460)$ mesons based on the coalescence model. We find that the yield and transverse momentum distribution of the $D_s$ meson agree well with the experimental measurements at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV at LHC. We further evaluate the transverse momentum ratio between $D_s$ and $D^0$ mesons, and investigate the role of light and strange quarks in the production of charmed mesons in heavy ion collisions. Finally, we calculate the transverse momentum distribution and yield of the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ meson in a four-quark state, and compare to those of $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ meson in a two-quark state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transverse momentum distribution and yield of the $D_s$ meson calculated using a coalescence model with quark distributions extracted from $\phi$ and $D^0$ meson data agree well with ALICE measurements at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, but this agreement depends on the specific parameterization of strange and charm quark transverse momentum distributions and the choice of oscillator frequency (0.103 GeV) that ensures full conversion of charm quarks at zero transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors extracted strange quark transverse momentum distributions by fitting the coalescence model prediction for $\phi$ meson production to ALICE data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, using a thermal form at low $p_T$ and a power-law form at high $p_T$ with a matching point at $p_{sT}'=1.5$ GeV. They similarly extracted charm quark distributions by fitting the $D^0$ meson transverse momentum spectrum from ALICE, combining coalescence production at low $p_T$ with Peterson fragmentation ($\epsilon_c=0.022$) at high $p_T$, and determined the oscillator frequency $\omega=0.103$ GeV by requiring all charm quarks at zero $p_T$ to be converted into charmed hadrons. They then applied the coalescence model with $s$-wave Wigner functions for $D_s$ and $D_s^*$ mesons and $p$-wave Wigner functions for $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ mesons to compute yields and transverse momentum distributions.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence volume at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV: 3360 fm$^3$, compared to 3530 fm$^3$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV
- Effective temperature for strange quarks: $T_{eff}=173$ MeV
- Total number of strange quarks at mid-rapidity: $dN_s/dy|_{|y|<0.5} \approx 784$
- Total number of charm quarks at mid-rapidity: $dN_c/dy = 17.4$
- Total number of light quarks at mid-rapidity: $dN_q/dy = 1040$
- Oscillator frequency for charmed hadrons: $\omega = 0.103$ GeV, corresponding to root-mean-square radius of $D^0$ meson: $\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle}=1.51$ fm
- Effective temperature for light quarks: $T_{eff}=187$ MeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: $T_c=156.5$ MeV with volume $V_c=4997$ fm$^3$
- Critical temperature for coalescence volume: $T_C=166$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the coalescence model approach previously applied at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV (Cho 2017) to the higher energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV at the LHC, using new ALICE measurements of $\phi$ mesons (ALICE 2019) and $D^0$ mesons (ALICE 2021) to extract quark distributions. The study enables comparison of $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ production in two-quark versus four-quark (tetraquark) configurations, building on earlier work by Cho (2010, 2011, 2017) that showed strong dependence of exotic hadron yields on their internal structure. The results also allow investigation of strangeness enhancement relative to light quarks through the $D_s/D^0$ ratio at low transverse momentum.
