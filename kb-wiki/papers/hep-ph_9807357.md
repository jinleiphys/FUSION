# On the instanton-induced portion of the nucleon strangeness

> We calculate the instanton contribution to the proton strangeness in the MIT bag enriched by the presence of a dilute instanton liquid. The evaluation is based on expressing the nucleon matrix elements of bilinear strange quark operators in terms of a model valence nucleon state and interactions producing quark-antiquark fluctuations on top of that valence state. Our method combines the usage of the evolution operator containing a strangeness source, and the Feynman-Hellmann theorem. It enables one to evaluate the strangeness in different Lorentz channels in, essentially, the same way. Only the scalar channel is found to be affected by the interaction induced by the random instanton liquid.

## Key claim

The instanton-induced interaction in a dilute instanton liquid contributes to nucleon strangeness only in the scalar channel, while vector, axial-vector, and tensor channels remain unaffected at the order considered, contrary to the expectation that QCD-vacuum fluctuations might select pseudoscalar and scalar channels via anomalies.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon matrix elements of bilinear strange quark operators by starting from a valence nucleon state in the MIT bag model and adding instanton-induced interactions that produce strange quark-antiquark fluctuations. They combine the evolution operator containing a strangeness source with the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to evaluate the response of the valence state to the strange probe, treating the instanton-induced interaction perturbatively in the random instanton liquid model (RILM) with the interaction of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1980) as reformulated by Nowak, Verbaarschot, and Zahed (1989).

## Key numbers

* Scalar strangeness from pion-nucleon scattering: ⟨N|¯ss|N⟩ ≈ 2.8 (dimensionless nucleon matrix element)
* QCD sum-rules result for scalar strangeness: ⟨N|¯ss|N⟩ ≈ 2.4 (dimensionless)
* Axial strangeness from polarized lepton-nucleon scattering: Δs = −0.11 ± 0.06, compared to Δu = 0.82 ± 0.06 and Δd = −0.44 ± 0.06
* Average instanton size: ρ ≈ 1/3 fm = (600 MeV)^(−1)
* Instanton density: n ≈ 1.6 × 10^9 MeV^4 = 1 fm^(−4), corresponding to dimensionless density ˜n ≈ 12.4 × 10^(−3)
* Quark condensate: ⟨0|¯ss|0⟩ ≈ ⟨0|¯uu|0⟩ ≈ (−200 MeV)^3
* Upper bound on strange radius from CCFR data: |⟨r^2⟩_s| ≤ 0.005 fm^2
* Cut-off on quark energies: Λ ∼ 0.6–1 GeV, with specific cut-off of 1.1 GeV on single strange quark and antiquark energies

## Context

This work extends the investigation of nucleon strangeness beyond the vacuum condensate contribution of Donoghue and Nussinov (1986) and the QCD sum-rules result of Khatsimovsky, Khriplovich, and Zhitnitsky (1987), by explicitly calculating the instanton-induced portion using the random instanton liquid model. The finding that only the scalar channel is affected contrasts with the conjecture by Zhitnitsky (1997) that non-trivial QCD-vacuum structure selects pseudoscalar and scalar channels, and it enables future calculations of the strange magnetic moment and strangeness radius within the same framework, provided the vector channel contributions are identified.
