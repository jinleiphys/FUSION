# Polarization and Vorticity in the Quark Gluon Plasma

> The quark-gluon plasma produced by collisions between ultra-relativistic heavy nuclei is well described in the language of hydrodynamics. Non-central collisions are characterized by very large angular momentum, which in a fluid system manifests as flow vorticity. This rotational structure can lead to a spin polarization of the hadrons that eventually emerge from the plasma, providing experimental access to flow substructure at unprecedented detail. Recently, first observations of $Λ$ hyperon polarization along the direction of collisional angular momentum have been reported. These measurements are in broad agreement with hydrodynamic and transport-based calculations and reveal that the QGP is the most vortical fluid ever observed. However, there remain important tensions between theory and observation which might be fundamental in nature. In the relatively mature field of heavy ion physics, the discovery of global hyperon polarization and three-dimensional simulations of the collision have opened an entirely new direction of research. We discuss the current status of this rapidly developing area and directions for future research.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon plasma produced in non-central heavy ion collisions exhibits flow vorticity that induces spin polarization of emitted hadrons, and while first measurements of Λ hyperon polarization broadly agree with hydrodynamic and transport-based calculations, important tensions between theory and observation remain that may be fundamental in nature.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework linking spin polarization to thermal vorticity, defined as the antisymmetric derivative of the four-temperature field β(x) = (1/T)u(x). They derive the mean spin vector for spin-1/2 particles at leading order in thermal vorticity using a local thermodynamic equilibrium density operator integrated over the freeze-out hypersurface, and compare these predictions with experimental measurements of Λ and anti-Λ polarization from the STAR collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Angular momentum densities in heavy ion collisions reach 10^(3-4) ħ over volumes ~250 fm^3
- The QGP freeze-out temperature is close to the pseudo-critical QCD temperature of 160 MeV
- First hydrodynamic calculations predicted global Λ polarization of a few percent at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- The conjectured universal lower bound for viscosity-to-entropy ratio is ħ/4π
- Low-energy heavy ion reactions discussed have kinetic energy E_kin ~ 30 MeV per nucleon (√s_NN - 2m_p ≈ 15 MeV)
- The QGP exists for a transient of around 10^(-22) seconds

## Context

This review extends the hydrodynamic description of the QGP, which previously focused on momentum anisotropies encoded in Fourier coefficients v_n, to include spin degrees of freedom through vorticity. The work builds on the Barnett effect (1915) and Einstein-de Haas effect (1915) as historical precedents for spin-rotation coupling, and on the first observation of global polarization in nonrelativistic heavy ion reactions by correlating γ-ray circular polarization with fragment deflection angles (PhysRevLett.57.559, Lemmon 1999). The theoretical framework originates from the equilibrium statistical operator approach of Becattini (2013) and the perturbative-QCD model of Liang (2004), and enables a new experimental probe of flow substructure in the QGP.
