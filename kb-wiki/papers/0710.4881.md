# Inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions calculated with the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation

> Inclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections are calculated using a consistent relativistic mean-field theoretical framework. The weak lepton-hadron interaction is expressed in the standard current-current form, the nuclear ground state is described with the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model, and the relevant transitions to excited nuclear states are calculated in the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation. Illustrative test calculations are performed for charged-current neutrino reactions on $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{56}$Fe, and $^{208}$Pb, and results compared with previous studies and available data. Using the experimental neutrino fluxes, the averaged cross sections are evaluated for nuclei of interest for neutrino detectors. We analyze the total neutrino-nucleus cross sections, and the evolution of the contribution of the different multipole excitations as a function of neutrino energy. The cross sections for reactions of supernova neutrinos on $^{16}$O and $^{208}$Pb target nuclei are analyzed as functions of the temperature and chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully consistent relativistic mean-field framework (RHB+PN-RQRPA) can describe inclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections across a wide mass range, but the calculated flux-averaged cross section for $^{56}$Fe differs considerably from another self-consistent QRPA calculation (140 vs 352 $\times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$), indicating that theoretical predictions remain model-dependent and require further nuclear structure studies.

## Method

The nuclear ground state is described using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the density-dependent meson-exchange effective interaction DD-ME2 in the particle-hole channel and the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction for pairing. Excited states are calculated using the proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (PN-RQRPA), which includes both $T=1$ and $T=0$ pairing channels and configurations built from positive-energy states and empty negative-energy states from the Dirac sea. The weak interaction is expressed in standard current-current form, and the cross section is evaluated using multipole-expanded transition operators with Coulomb corrections applied via a combined Fermi function and effective momentum approximation prescription.

## Key numbers

- For $^{12}$C($\nu_e,e^-$)$^{12}$N, the PN-RQRPA flux-averaged cross section agrees well with experiment, while for $^{12}$C($\nu_\mu,\mu^-$)$^{12}$N it agrees with previous (Q)RPA results but is somewhat larger than the experimental cross section.
- For $^{56}$Fe($\nu_e,e^-$)$^{56}$Co, the PN-RQRPA gives a flux-averaged cross section of 140 $\times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$, compared to 352 $\times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$ from QRPA with Skyrme SIII, both within the experimental uncertainty of (256 $\pm$ 108 $\pm$ 43) $\times 10^{-42}$ cm$^2$ from KARMEN.
- The Landau-Migdal parameter $g^\prime$ = 0.52 is adjusted to reproduce the Gamow-Teller resonance excitation energy in $^{208}$Pb using the DD-ME2 interaction.
- For $^{208}$Pb($\nu_e,e^-$)$^{208}$Bi, the cross sections corrected with the Fermi function and effective momentum approximation are compared, with the combined prescription used for all calculations.
- The inclusive cross sections for reactions on $^{16}$O, $^{56}$Fe, and $^{208}$Pb increase by more than two orders of magnitude from the lightest to the heaviest target.
- For $^{56}$Fe($\nu_e,e^-$)$^{56}$Co, below $E_{\nu_e} \approx$ 30 MeV the dominant contribution comes from the isobaric analog state and Gamow-Teller resonance, while at higher energies other multipole transitions become more important.

## Context

This work extends the consistent relativistic mean-field approach previously applied to charge-exchange excitations (Paar, Vretenar, Ring 2004) and beta-decay rates (Nikšić et al. 2005, Marketin et al. 2007) to the calculation of inclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections. It provides a fully consistent alternative to nonrelativistic QRPA calculations using Skyrme interactions (Volpe et al. 2000) and hybrid CRPA+shell-model approaches (Kolbe et al. 1999, 2003), with the advantage of using a single universal effective interaction across the nuclide chart. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutrino detector response, supernova neutrino interactions, and feasibility studies for low-energy beta-beam facilities.
