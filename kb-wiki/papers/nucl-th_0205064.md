# Nuclear modification of heavy quark fragmentation function and J/psi production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> In ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, charm quark fragmentation is modified due to the melting of strings inside the high density partonic matter. D mesons produced in hadronization can further produce J/psi particles. Using a multiphase transport model, we investigate the effect on the rapidity distribution of the produced J/psi particles with two different charm quark fragmentation functions. It is shown that the J/psi rapidity distribution is sensitive to the nuclear modification of charm quark fragmentation and thus is a good indicator of the onset of string melting and the production of deconfined partonic matter.

# Nuclear modification of heavy quark fragmentation function and J/psi production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The J/psi rapidity distribution is sensitive to whether charm quark fragmentation follows a delta-function (string-pulling) or Peterson (independent) form, and this sensitivity can serve as an indicator of string melting and deconfined partonic matter formation at RHIC energies. The claim is conditional on the assumption that string melting occurs when the gluon density is high enough to screen interactions between charm quarks and receding nuclei.

## Method
The authors used a multiphase transport model (a hybrid of HIJING for initial parton production, ZPC for parton cascade, PYTHIA/JETSET for Lund fragmentation, and ART for hadron evolution) to simulate central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200A GeV. Charm quarks were generated with PYTHIA using a charm production cross section of 350 microbarns per nucleon-nucleon collision, and their evolution was treated perturbatively by allowing charm quarks from several independent events to evolve with a common hadron environment. Two fragmentation functions were compared: a delta function fragmentation (representing string-pulling in hadron-induced reactions) and the Peterson fragmentation function (representing independent fragmentation in e+e- annihilation).

## Key numbers
- Charm production cross section per nucleon-nucleon collision: 350 microbarns
- Charm quark mass: 1.50 GeV; D meson mass: 2.01 GeV
- Gluon rapidity density at midrapidity in Au+Au at 200A GeV: about 300; charm and anticharm rapidity density: about 3
- For delta function fragmentation: initial charm quark rapidity distribution plateau value about 3.6 at y=0; J/psi rapidity distribution plateau value about 0.7/300 at y=0 after parton stage; final J/psi value about 1.9/300 at y=0
- For Peterson fragmentation: D and anti-D rapidity distribution narrower by about 0.6 units of rapidity with peak value about 6.4 at y=0; final J/psi distribution peak value about 5.6/300 at y=0
- Cross section for elastic collisions between gluons and charm quarks and for J/psi dissociation by gluons and light mesons: 3 mb

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of J/psi suppression as a QGP probe (Matsui 1986, Shuryak 1988) by focusing on the modification of charm quark fragmentation due to string melting at RHIC energies, building on calculations by Biro (1993) and Lin (1999) that demonstrated the possibility of string melting. The multiphase transport model used here had previously been applied to particle production at RHIC and successfully described global features of RHIC data (Zhang 1999, Lin 2000, Lin 2001). The results suggest that measuring J/psi rapidity distributions together with charmed meson distributions can distinguish between string-dominated and deconfined scenarios, enabling experimental tests of QGP formation at RHIC.
