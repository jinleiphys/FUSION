# Thermodynamics of strongly magnetized dense quark matter from hard dense loop perturbation theory

> We discuss the hard dense loop perturbation theory approach for studying the thermodynamics of strongly magnetized dense quark matter. The free energy of quarks and gluons have been calculated for one-loop quark and gluon self-energies, respectively. The longitudinal and transverse components of pressure, magnetization, second-order quark number susceptibility, and speed of sound have been computed, and their behavior with chemical potential and magnetic field has been analyzed. Our numerical results show that the longitudinal pressure increases with chemical potential and magnetic field, while for the transverse component, it is diminished. We also analyze the longitudinal component of the speed of sound at high chemical potentials, which approaches the speed of light in the asymptotic limit. The obtained results may be helpful in studying magnetized quark matter in the core of neutron stars and magnetars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that hard dense loop perturbation theory (HDLpt) can be extended to compute the thermodynamics of strongly magnetized cold quark matter at zero temperature and high chemical potential, but only under the condition that the magnetic field is the largest scale such that all quarks are confined to the lowest Landau level (LLL), which reduces the dynamics from (3+1) to (1+1) dimensions.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop quark self-energy in a strong magnetic field at finite chemical potential and zero temperature using the HDL approximation, where external momenta are taken as soft (~gμ) and loop momenta as hard (~μ). They derived the form factors of the quark self-energy in the LLL approximation, then used these to calculate the quark free energy and the gluon free energy from one-loop gluon self-energy. From the free energy, they extracted the longitudinal and transverse components of pressure, magnetization, second-order quark number susceptibility, and speed of sound as functions of chemical potential and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal pressure increases with chemical potential and magnetic field, while the transverse component is diminished (no specific numerical values provided for pressure).
- The longitudinal component of the speed of sound approaches the speed of light in the asymptotic limit of high chemical potentials.
- The maximum number of Landau levels is given by l_max = floor(μ²/(2|q_f B|)), and the LLL condition requires μ² < 2|q_f B| so that l_max → 0.
- The magnetic field magnitude in compact stars can be of order 10¹⁵ Gauss at the surface and possibly up to 10²⁰ Gauss in the core.

## Context

This work extends the hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) approach of Karmakar et al. (2019) from finite temperature to zero temperature and high density in a strong magnetic field. It is the first application of a first-principles perturbative approach to the equation of state of cold quark matter in a magnetic field, which was previously studied at finite temperature by Fraga et al. (2023). The results may be used as input for the mass-radius relation of magnetars and for modeling the core of neutron stars, building on the work of Andersen et al. (2002) who used HDLpt to study dense quark matter thermodynamics without magnetic fields.
