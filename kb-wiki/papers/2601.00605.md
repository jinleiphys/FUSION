# Effective field theory for dissipative photons from higher-form symmetries

> Recent developments in generalized symmetries have provided new insights into quantum field theories. Within this framework, photons can be understood as Nambu-Goldstone modes associated with a spontaneously broken higher-form symmetry. In this work, we develop an effective field theory that builds on this symmetry structure to describe the real-time dynamics of photons in insulating media at finite temperature. Combining the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism with the generalized coset construction, we formulate a symmetry-based effective action that incorporates both conservative and dissipative effects. The effective theory implements the dynamical Kubo-Martin-Schwinger symmetry, ensuring consistency with the fluctuation-dissipation relation and Onsager's reciprocal relations. Within this framework, we derive the entropy current associated with dissipative photon dynamics and demonstrate the non-negativity of its divergence, in accordance with the second law of thermodynamics. We also clarify the symmetry origin of the gauge redundancy in the unbroken phase within the Schwinger-Keldysh framework, relating it to strong and weak realizations of higher-form symmetries. Our results provide a model-independent effective description of photon dynamics in insulating media at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory for dissipative photon dynamics in insulating media at finite temperature by combining the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism with the generalized coset construction for higher-form symmetries, claiming that this provides a model-independent description that respects the fluctuation-dissipation relation and Onsager's reciprocal relations, though the construction is limited to the classical limit and relies on a derivative expansion truncated at finite order.

## Method

The authors construct a Schwinger-Keldysh effective action for photons by treating them as Nambu-Goldstone modes associated with a spontaneously broken U(1) 1-form symmetry, using the generalized coset construction for higher-form symmetries extended to the closed-time-path formalism. They impose the dynamical Kubo-Martin-Schwinger symmetry, which encodes equilibrium constraints such as the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, and enforce unitarity conditions including the requirement that the effective action vanishes when all "a-type" fields are set to zero. The effective Lagrangian is organized in a derivative expansion with a power-counting scheme where the number of a-type fields and time derivatives are counted equally, and they derive the most general terms up to a given order consistent with rotational symmetry, internal symmetry, and the dynamical KMS condition.

## Key numbers

- The dispersion relation for photon modes in the long-wavelength limit is given by ω(k) = ±c_s k - (i/2)(τ c_s² + σ)k² + O(k³), where τ ≥ 0 and σ ≥ 0 are dissipative coefficients constrained by unitarity.
- The effective Lagrangian is constructed in D = 3+1 dimensions (d = 3 spatial dimensions) with Minkowski metric η_μν = diag(-1, +1, +1, +1).
- The totally antisymmetric tensor is normalized as ε_{01⋯d} = +1 and ε^{01⋯d} = -1.

## Context

This work extends the generalized coset construction for higher-form symmetries proposed by Hidaka et al. (2020) to finite-temperature dissipative systems by incorporating the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism developed by Crossley et al. (2015), Glorioso et al. (2016, 2017), and others. It builds on the reinterpretation of photons as Nambu-Goldstone modes of spontaneously broken U(1) 1-form symmetry introduced by Gaiotto et al. (2014) and further developed by Lake (2018) and Hofman (2018). The framework enables a systematic treatment of dissipative photon dynamics in insulating media and clarifies the relation between strong and weak symmetries in open quantum systems, as discussed by Lessa et al. (2024), Gu et al. (2024), and Huang et al. (2024).
