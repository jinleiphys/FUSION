# Pre-equilibrium Neutron Emission in Fission or Fragmentation

> Fissioning nuclei and fission fragments, nuclear fragments emerging from energetic collisions, or nuclei probed with various external fields can emit one or more pre-equilibrium neutrons, protons, and potentially other heavier nuclear fragments. I describe a formalism which can be used to evaluate the pre-equilibrium neutron emission probabilities and the excitation energies of the remnant fragments.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism for calculating pre-equilibrium neutron emission probabilities and remnant excitation energies in fission or fragmentation, but the author explicitly states that the results obtained without accounting for in-medium collisions (spreading width) should be interpreted as "upper bound estimates" and that the approach neglects the role of fluctuations, which are expected to lead to wider distributions.

## Method

The author develops a time-dependent mean-field formalism using Slater determinants (or Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov quasiparticle wave functions) to describe the time evolution of nucleons in fission fragments. Projectors onto bound and unbound single-particle states of the final fragments are constructed, and the probability to emit exactly n pre-equilibrium neutrons is computed from the overlaps between time-evolved wave functions and the stationary bound-state wave functions of the remnant fragments. The formalism is extended to treat two fragments simultaneously and to estimate the excitation energy of the remnant fragments using a BCS approximation with occupation probabilities constrained by the remaining neutron number.

## Key numbers

- For 240Pu induced fission, the fragments carry on average about 0.7 MeV kinetic energy per nucleon, corresponding to a wave vector k ≈ 0.2 fm⁻¹
- Two example α²-distributions yield average pre-equilibrium neutron multiplicities ν = 1.61 and 6.44, with variances ⟨⟨n²⟩⟩ of 1.52 and 5.06 respectively
- For the same two distributions, the conditional average multiplicities ν_PE (excluding zero-neutron events) are 1.99 and 6.44
- The Poisson distribution mean rates λ for these two cases are 1.66 and 7.28
- The average fission fragment diameter is d = 2R ≈ 12 fm

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Carjan (2010, 2012, 2015, 2018, 2019), Rizea (2013), and Capote (2016) on pre-equilibrium neutron emission in fission, and builds on the time-dependent projector method developed by Bulgac (2019) and Simenel (2010). The approach clarifies the role of various approximations used in previous studies and extends the range of observables considered. The formalism enables calculation of full probability distributions for neutron emission (not just averages), correlations between neutrons emitted from heavy and light fragments, and excitation energies of remnant fragments for subsequent statistical decay calculations.
