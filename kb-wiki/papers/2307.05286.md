# Heavy-flavor hadronization mechanism from pp to AA collisions: a theoretical perspective

> The interest in studying heavy-flavor hadronization in high-energy nuclear collisions is twofold. On one hand hadronization represents a source of systematic uncertainties in phenomenological attempts of extracting heavy-flavor transport coefficients in the Quark Gluon Plasma which one assumes to be produced in the collision. Hence, developing the most possible reliable model for this process is important to get a precise and accurate estimate of a fundamental property of hot QCD. On the other hand studying how hadronization changes in the presence of a dense medium of colored partons can be considered an issue of interest by itself. In particular, the observation of modifications of heavy-flavor hadronization in proton-proton collisions strongly suggests that also in this case a small droplet of Quark-Gluon Plasma can be formed. Here we try to provide a general overview on heavy-flavor hadronization, from pp to AA collisions, stressing the aspects and challenges common to all mechanisms proposed in the literature. Then, focusing on a particular model, we show how a consistent description of several observables involving heavy-flavor hadrons can be obtained

# Heavy-flavor hadronization mechanism from pp to AA collisions: a theoretical perspective

## Key claim
Heavy-flavor hadronization modifications observed in proton-proton collisions may indicate that a small droplet of quark-gluon plasma is also produced in these smaller systems, and a unified description from pp to AA collisions can be obtained by assuming local color neutralization via recombination of heavy quarks with nearby thermal partons. The claim is conditional on the specific model presented, which treats hadronization via recombination on an isothermal hypersurface at T_H = 155 MeV, and the author notes that color-reconnection models in PYTHIA 8 can also provide a satisfactory description of the data without invoking a deconfined medium.

## Method
The author presents a minimal model of heavy-flavor hadronization in which color neutralization occurs locally on an isothermal hypersurface at the critical temperature T_H = 155 MeV, recombining a heavy quark with a light antiquark or diquark from the same fluid cell, with species and momentum sampled from a thermal distribution in the local rest frame. Light color-singlet clusters undergo two-body decay into a charmed hadron plus a soft pion, while heavier clusters above approximately 4 GeV invariant mass are treated as Lund strings fragmented via PYTHIA 6.4. For pp collisions, event-by-event simulations are performed using TrENTo initial conditions with hydrodynamic evolution, stochastic heavy-quark propagation, and recombination at hadronization.

## Key numbers
- Hadronization temperature: T_H = 155 MeV, used as the isothermal hypersurface for color neutralization
- Heavy clusters treated as Lund strings: threshold above approximately 4 GeV invariant mass
- Fraction of heavy quarks initially found in fireball corona below T_H in minimum-bias pp events: approximately 5%
- High-multiplicity pp events defined as the 1% of events with the highest deposited entropy
- String transverse thickness radius: approximately 0.5 fm (from Baker 2018)
- Collision energy for comparisons: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for pp, pPb, and PbPb systems

## Context
This work extends the approach of Beraudo (2022) on heavy-flavor hadronization via parton recombination in heavy-ion collisions to include proton-proton collisions, addressing the surprising observation by ALICE (2020) of enhanced Lambda_c+/D0 ratios in pp collisions. The model provides a unified description from minimum-bias pp to central PbPb collisions, in contrast to standard vacuum fragmentation approaches like POWHEG+PYTHIA standalone which undershoot charmed-baryon production. The results enable improved extraction of heavy-quark transport coefficients (spatial diffusion coefficient D_s and momentum diffusion coefficient kappa) by reducing systematic uncertainties from hadronization modeling, and the author notes ongoing extensions to the bottom sector by He (2022) and to multi-charmed hadrons by Minissale (2023).
