# Elliptic Flow arising from Ridges due to Semi-hard Scattering

> Azimuthal anisotropy in heavy-ion collisions is studied by taking into account the ridges generated by semi-hard scattering of intermediate-momentum partons, which can be sensitive to the initial spatial configuration of the medium in non-central collisions. In a simple treatment of the problem where the recombination of only thermal partons is considered, analytical formulas can be derived that yield results in accord with the data on v_2 for p_T <1-2 GeV/c. Centrality dependence is described by a geometrical factor. Ridge phenomenology is used to determine the initial slopes of v_2 at low p_T for both pion and proton. For higher p_T, shower partons from high-p_T jets must be included, but they are not considered here.

# Elliptic Flow arising from Ridges due to Semi-hard Scattering

## Key claim
Azimuthal anisotropy (elliptic flow, v₂) at low transverse momentum (p_T < 2 GeV/c) can be explained by ridges generated from semi-hard scattering of partons near the surface of the overlap region, without requiring rapid thermalization or early-time hydrodynamics. The model is restricted to thermal parton recombination only and does not include shower partons from high-p_T jets.

## Method
The author models the almond-shaped overlap region in non-central collisions with sharp nuclear boundaries, defining an angular window Φ = cos⁻¹(b/2R_A) for the initial spatial configuration. Semi-hard partons scattered near the surface produce ridges of enhanced thermal partons, whose hadronization is treated by recombination of thermal partons only. The ridge parton distribution is taken as exponential in q_T with a temperature parameter T', while the bulk medium has temperature T; the difference ΔT = T' − T is taken from ridge phenomenology. Analytical formulas for v₂(p_T, b) are derived from the second harmonic of the φ distribution of hadrons.

## Key numbers
- ΔT = 45 MeV, taken from ridge data in central Au+Au collisions (range 40–50 MeV)
- T = 0.287 GeV, from fitting π⁺ distribution at 40–50% centrality in the range 0.5 < p_T < 2.7 GeV/c
- T'' = 2.12 GeV, derived from 1/T'' = 1/T − 1/T' with ΔT = 45 MeV
- v₂^π(p_T, b)/p_T = 0.15 (GeV/c)⁻¹ at maximum (50% centrality), giving v₂ = 0.075 at p_T = 0.5 GeV/c
- The centrality dependence factor is f(β) = sin 2(cos⁻¹ β) with β = b/2R_A, peaking at Φ = π/4 (b = √2 R_A, ~50% centrality)

## Context
This work extends the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2004) for p_T > 2 GeV/c to lower p_T, and applies ridge phenomenology from triggered-event studies (J. Adams et al. 2005; J. Putschke et al. 2006; J. Bielcikova et al. 2006) to explain elliptic flow without early-time hydrodynamics. It provides an alternative to the conventional hydrodynamical description that assumes rapid thermalization. The model enables a direct connection between ridge properties and v₂, and predicts universal scaling of the v₂ slope versus transverse kinetic energy for pions and protons at low p_T, consistent with quark number scaling observed by Molnar and Voloshin (2003) and Adams et al. (2004).
