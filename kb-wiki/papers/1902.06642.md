# Ultra-peripheral-collision studies in the fixed-target mode with the proton and lead LHC beams

> We address the physics case related to the studies of ultra-peripheral pH, pPb, PbH, and PbPb collisions in the fixed-target mode at the LHC. In particular, we discuss how one can measure the gluon generalized parton distribution E_g(x,xi,t) in exclusive J/psi photoproduction with a transversely polarized hydrogen target.

## Key claim

The paper argues that ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) in the fixed-target mode at the LHC can provide a first-ever measurement of the gluon generalized parton distribution \(E_g(x,\xi,t)\) via exclusive \(J/\psi\) photoproduction off a transversely polarized hydrogen target, but the feasibility depends on achieving a target polarization of 80% and sufficient integrated luminosity (e.g., \(10^4\) pb\(^{-1}\)yr\(^{-1}\) for \(p\)H collisions).

## Method

The authors performed simulations using the STARLIGHT event generator with LHCb kinematic cuts (muon pseudorapidity \(2 < \eta_\mu < 5\) and muon transverse momentum \(p_T^\mu > 0.4\) GeV) to compute cross sections and event rates for exclusive \(J/\psi\) production via UPCs in fixed-target \(p\)H and PbH collisions at the LHC. They then projected the statistical sensitivity of the single transverse spin asymmetry (STSA) \(A_N^{\gamma p^\uparrow \to J/\psi p}\) assuming an 80% polarized hydrogen target, using the relation \(A_N \propto (1+\xi) \, \text{Im} ({\cal E}^{g*}{\cal H}^{g})\) to connect the asymmetry to the gluon GPDs.

## Key numbers

* For \(p\)H collisions at a beam energy of 7000 GeV per nucleon, the maximal photon-proton center-of-mass energy is \(\sqrt{s_{\gamma p}^{\rm max}} = 44\) GeV.
* For PbH collisions at a beam energy of 2760 GeV per nucleon, the maximal photon-proton center-of-mass energy is \(\sqrt{s_{\gamma p}^{\rm max}} = 12\) GeV.
* The total cross section for exclusive \(J/\psi\) production in \(p\)H collisions is \(1.2 \times 10^3\) pb, yielding \(2 \times 10^5\) events per year at a luminosity of \(1.0 \times 10^4\) pb\(^{-1}\)yr\(^{-1}\).
* The total cross section for exclusive \(J/\psi\) production in PbH collisions is \(2.8 \times 10^5\) pb, yielding \(1 \times 10^3\) events per year at a luminosity of \(0.12\) pb\(^{-1}\)yr\(^{-1}\).
* The statistical uncertainty projection for the STSA \(A_N\) in \(p\)H collisions is within a few percent; for PbH collisions, the uncertainty is about 10%.

## Context

This work extends the physics case for fixed-target experiments at the LHC, previously outlined by Brodsky (2012) and Hadjidakis (2018), by specifically focusing on UPCs as a probe of gluon GPDs. It builds on the theoretical framework of Koempel (2011) and Lansberg (2018) for accessing the gluon orbital angular momentum via exclusive \(J/\psi\) photoproduction, and it complements recent measurements of the gluon Sivers function by COMPASS (Adolph, 2017). The results enable future feasibility studies for measuring quark GPDs via timelike Compton scattering, light-by-light scattering, and axion-like particle searches in the MeV-GeV mass range.
