# The Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

> The hypercentral constituent quark model contains a spin independent three-quark interaction inspired by lattice QCD calculations which reproduces the average energy of SU(6) multiplets. The splittings are obtained with a residual generalized SU(6)-breaking interaction including an isospin dependent term. The long standing problem of the Roper is absent and all the 3- and 4-star states are well reproduced. The model has been used in a systematic way for transverse and longitudinal electromagnetic transition form factor of the 3- and 4- star and also for the missing resonances. The prediction of the electromagnetic helicity amplitudes agrees quite well with the data except for low $Q^2$, showing that it can supply a realistic set of quark wave functions. In particular we report the calculated helicity amplitude $A_{1/2}$ for the $S11(1535)$, which is in agreement with the TJNAF data.

# The Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim

The hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM), using a spin-independent three-quark interaction inspired by lattice QCD, reproduces the average energy of SU(6) multiplets and eliminates the long-standing Roper resonance problem, but the predicted electromagnetic helicity amplitudes agree with data only except at low $Q^2$, where discrepancies remain.

## Method

The authors employed a hypercentral quark interaction consisting of a linear plus Coulomb-like term in the hyperradius $x$, with parameters fitted to the baryon spectrum ($\alpha = 1.61\ \text{fm}^{-2}$, $\tau = 4.59$, hyperfine strength set by the $\Delta$-nucleon mass difference). A residual generalized SU(6)-breaking interaction including an isospin-dependent term was added perturbatively. Helicity amplitudes $A_{1/2}$, $A_{3/2}$, and $S_{1/2}$ for electroexcitation of baryon resonances were calculated using non-relativistic point-quark currents.

## Key numbers

- Potential parameters: $\alpha = 1.61\ \text{fm}^{-2}$ (linear confinement strength), $\tau = 4.59$ (Coulomb-like strength)
- Hyperfine interaction strength determined by the $\Delta$-Nucleon mass difference
- The calculated helicity amplitude $A_{1/2}$ for the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance agrees with TJNAF data
- Discrepancies remain at low $Q^2$, particularly in the $A^{p}_{3/2}$ amplitude for the $D_{13}(1520)$ transition

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral constituent quark model approach previously developed by the Genova group (Giannini, Santopinto, and collaborators) to systematically calculate electromagnetic transition form factors for all 3- and 4-star baryon resonances and missing resonances. The model contrasts with other constituent quark models (such as those by Isgur and collaborators, or the chiral constituent quark model) that use different potentials and SU(6)-breaking schemes. The results provide a realistic set of quark wave functions that can serve as input for ongoing experimental data analysis at facilities like TJNAF, though the low-$Q^2$ discrepancies suggest the need for relativistic corrections and inclusion of quark-antiquark configurations.
