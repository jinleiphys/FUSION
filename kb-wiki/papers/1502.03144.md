# Constraints on string percolation model from anomalous centrality evolution data in Au-Au collisions at $\mathbf{\sqrt{s_{NN}}=}$ 62 and 200 GeV

> Anomalous centrality evolution of two-particle angular correlations observed in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62$ and 200 GeV and the onset of ridge structures are considered in the model of interacting quark-gluon strings. We assume that at the given energy of nucleus-nucleus collisions the critical energy density may be reached at the specific centrality. In a string percolation model this might be treated equivalently to a formation of a large cluster of strings characterized by the critical string density, with a size comparable to the whole area of interaction of two nuclei. This hypothesis allows to define some constraints on the string percolation model using data on transitional centralities in Au-Au collisions at these two energies. Results are extrapolated to the LHC energy where high string densities (exceeding the critical value) are confirmed for all classes of centralities in Pb-Pb collisions. Interaction between strings inside large clusters formed in nucleus-nucleus collisions is considered in a simplified Monte Carlo model. This model is applied to the qualitative analysis of the onset of collectivity and the ridge formation in Pb-Pb collisions. It is shown that the approach of the repulsive string-string interaction is capable to explain the appearance of elliptic and triangular flow observed in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper hypothesizes that the anomalous centrality evolution of two-particle angular correlations and the onset of the ridge structure in Au-Au collisions at RHIC are related to reaching a critical quark-gluon string density, which in the string percolation model corresponds to the formation of a large cluster of strings comparable in size to the whole nuclear overlap area. The authors use this hypothesis to place constraints on the string percolation model parameters, but note that the uncertainties in the extracted critical number of participants ($N_{part}^{crit}$) from RHIC data produce systematic errors that are taken into account in their calculations.

## Method

The authors use the string percolation model to calculate the dimensionless percolation parameter $\tilde{\eta}$ and the transverse particle density $\tilde{\rho}$ for Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62$ and 200 GeV. They extract the critical string density from the ratio $\tilde{\rho}^{crit}/\tilde{\eta}^{crit}$ at the transitional centralities where the ridge onset is observed, and use this to determine the total number of strings $N_{str}$ and the sea-string parameter $a$ in a parametrization $N_{str}=N_{part}+a\cdot N_{coll}$. A simplified Monte Carlo toy model with repulsive string-string interaction (characterized by an interaction radius $R_{\rm int}$) is then applied to simulate the azimuthal anisotropy in two-particle correlation functions for various centralities.

## Key numbers

- The critical transverse particle density at the ridge onset is $\tilde{\rho}^{crit} = 2.6 \pm 0.2$ fm$^{-2}$ for both 62 and 200 GeV Au-Au collisions.
- The critical percolation parameter is taken as $\tilde{\eta}^{crit} \approx 1.15 \pm 0.03$, with string radius $r_0 = 0.25$ fm.
- The ratio $\tilde{\rho}^{crit}/\tilde{\eta}^{crit} = 2.3 \pm 0.2$ fm$^{-2}$ at the critical point.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, the critical number of participants is $N_{part}^{crit} \approx 40$, with $N_{str} = 194 \pm 25$ strings and sea-string parameter $a = 2.6 \pm 0.4$.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62$ GeV, the critical number of participants is $N_{part}^{crit} \approx 90$, with $N_{str} = 352 \pm 28$ strings and $a = 1.6 \pm 0.2$.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3$ GeV (SPS), the critical point gives $N_{part} = 110$, $N_{str} = 302 \pm 45$, and $a = 1.2 \pm 0.2$.
- The efficient string-string interaction radius that provides qualitative description of elliptic and triangular flows is $R_{\rm int} \sim 2$ fm, larger than the string radius $r_0 = 0.25$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the string percolation model (Armesto 1996, Nardi, Braun 2000) to explain the anomalous centrality evolution of two-particle correlations observed by STAR (Daugherity) in Au-Au collisions at 62 and 200 GeV, where the ridge onset occurs at different centralities for the two energies. The approach builds on earlier ideas of interacting color flux tubes (Abramovsky 1980, 1988) and provides a dynamical origin for elliptic and triangular flow observed at RHIC and LHC, complementing the Color Glass Condensate model (ridge-cgc) and the string fusion approach (Amelin 1994, Braun). The results enable future quantitative estimates for proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, and suggest that the increase of string density with centrality is mainly due to sea-strings.
