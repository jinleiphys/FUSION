# Self-consistent approach to deformation of intruder states in neutron-deficient Pb and Po

> We present systematic calculations of the properties of 11- isomers in neutron-deficient 184-198Pb and 188-200Po. These states are based on the proton (h9/2 i13/2)_{K = 11-} configuration. They are calculated in the framework of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with a Skyrme interaction and density-dependent pairing force. The energies and deformations of the 11- states are compared to those of the intruder 0+ states in both Pb and Po isotopes. In the most neutron-deficient Po isotopes, the calculations predict, below a weakly oblate 11- state, another oblate 11- state which is even more deformed than the intruder oblate 11- state in their Pb isotones. The energies and quadrupole moments of the 11- isomers, corresponding to a weakly oblate nuclear shape, are in fair agreement with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method with a Skyrme interaction and density-dependent pairing force provides a fully self-consistent description of 11- isomers in neutron-deficient Pb and Po isotopes, with the calculated energies and quadrupole moments showing fair agreement with available experimental data, though the excitation energies in Pb are overestimated by 600 to 800 keV.

## Method

The authors performed systematic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Skyrme parameterization SLy4 for the mean-field channel and a density-dependent zero-range force for the pairing channel, solving the equations on a three-dimensional cubic mesh with approximate particle-number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami prescription. The 11- isomeric states were constructed fully self-consistently as two-quasiparticle (2qp) excitations, selecting quasiparticles whose dominant components in the Hartree-Fock basis are the 9/2- and 13/2+ particle states. For Po isotopes, additional configuration-mixing calculations using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with respect to the axial quadrupole moment were performed to validate the construction of isomers on oblate configurations.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies of 11- states in Pb isotopes are overestimated by 600 to 800 keV compared to experimental data.
- Deformation parameters (beta2, beta4) for 11- states in Pb isotopes vary from (-0.15, -0.014) for 198Pb to (-0.19, -0.018) for 184Pb.
- Deformation parameters (beta2, beta4) for 11- states in heavier Po isotopes vary from (-0.114, -0.007) for 200Po to (-0.117, -0.007) for 196Po.
- The oblate minimum in Pb isotopes occurs around quadrupole moment q = -10 b.
- The barrier between symmetric minima at q ~ +/- 8 b (beta ~ +/- 0.1) in Po isotopes is about 100 keV in the gamma plane.
- In 186Pb, the excitation energy of the prolate minimum significantly overestimates experimental data.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of intruder states in neutron-deficient Pb and Po, which had been mainly devoted to 0+ states using schematic spherical shell models (e.g., Van Duppen et al., Heyde and Jolie, 1987), Strutinsky approaches with Woods-Saxon potentials (Bengtsson and Nazarewicz, 1989; Nazarewicz, 1993), and various self-consistent mean-field methods (Tajima et al., 1993; Chasman and Egido, 2001; Heenen et al., 2001; Duguet et al., 2002). The only previous systematic study of isomers in these nuclei was based on cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations with a schematic Woods-Saxon potential (Bengtsson and Nazarewicz, 1989; Clark and Wadsworth, 1993). This work provides the first fully microscopic (self-consistent) investigation of these isomeric configurations, enabling more reliable predictions for the deformation and energy systematics of 11- states in lighter isotopes where experimental data are not yet available.
