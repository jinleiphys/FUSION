# Three-body system in leading order Effective Field Theory without three-body forces

> The use of leading order effective field theory (EFT) to describe neutron-deuteron scattering leads to integral equations that have unusual behaviour: when only two-body interactions are included, the scattering amplitude does not approach a limit when the cutoff used to solve the equations is removed. It has recently been shown that this cutoff dependence can be eliminated by the careful inclusion of a three-body force. In this paper we show that the cutoff dependence is just a reflection of the fact that the aforementioned integral equations admit an infinite number of solutions amongst which only one corresponds to the physical scattering amplitude. We show how to numerically extract the physical scattering amplitude from the general solution and in this way explicitly demonstrate that the amplitude for a particle scattering off a two-body bound state, in leading order EFT, is in fact determined entirely by two-body forces.

## Key claim

The cutoff dependence observed in leading-order effective field theory calculations of neutron-deuteron scattering arises not from a missing three-body force, but because the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equation admits an infinite number of solutions; the physical scattering amplitude can be extracted from this family by requiring that it vanish asymptotically at large momentum, without introducing any three-body counterterm.

## Method

The authors solved the regularized K-matrix version of the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation for three bosons (λ=1) by introducing an ultraviolet cutoff Λ via an additive term 1/(Λ+q²) in the kernel. They generated a sequence of solutions with progressively smaller inhomogeneous terms (e.g., a₂/(10ⁱ(p a₂)⁶+1)) to isolate the homogeneous solution that shares the same large-momentum oscillatory tail as the regularized solution. By subtracting this homogeneous component, they extracted the physical K-matrix, which vanishes as p→∞, and then reconstructed the physical scattering amplitude through the unitarity relation.

## Key numbers

- The exponent s₀ ≈ 1.00624, which governs the asymptotic oscillatory behavior p^(±i s₀) of solutions to the homogeneous equation for λ=1.
- Cutoff values used in numerical solutions: Λ₁ = 1.6×10⁷ a₂⁻¹, Λ₂ = 3.2×10⁷ a₂⁻¹, Λ₃ = 6.4×10⁷ a₂⁻¹, where a₂ is the two-body scattering length.
- On-shell momentum k = 0.82 a₂⁻¹ was used for the numerical examples shown.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bedaque et al. (1999), who argued that a three-body force counterterm is necessary to remove cutoff dependence in leading-order EFT for neutron-deuteron scattering. The authors instead follow the approach of Danilov (1961), who showed that the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation for zero-range potentials has homogeneous solutions at scattering energies. By demonstrating that the physical amplitude can be selected purely by its asymptotic behavior, the paper enables leading-order EFT calculations of three-body scattering without introducing three-body forces, and promises extension to the specific case of J=1/2 neutron-deuteron scattering in a separate publication.
