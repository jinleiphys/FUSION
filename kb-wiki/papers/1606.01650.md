# Axial charges of hyperons and charmed baryons using $N_f=2+1+1$ twisted mass fermions

> The axial couplings of the low lying baryons are evaluated using a total of five ensembles of dynamical twisted mass fermion gauge configurations. The simulations are performed using the Iwasaki gauge action and two degenerate flavors of light quarks, and a strange and a charm quark fixed to approximately their physical values at two values of the coupling constant. The lattice spacings, determined using the nucleon mass, are $a=0.082$ fm and $a=0.065$ fm and the simulations cover a pion mass in the range of about 210 MeV to 430 MeV. We study the dependence of the axial couplings on the pion mass in the range of about 210 MeV to 430 MeV as well as the $SU(3)$ breaking effects as we decrease the light quark mass towards its physical value.

## Key claim

The axial charges of hyperons and charmed baryons are computed using lattice QCD with five ensembles of twisted mass fermions, finding that the pion mass dependence of these charges is similar to that observed for the nucleon axial charge within the pion mass range of about 210 MeV to 430 MeV, though disconnected contributions are neglected in this first study.

## Method

The authors analyze five ensembles of \(N_f=2+1+1\) twisted mass fermion gauge configurations produced by the European Twisted Mass Collaboration, using the Iwasaki gauge action at two lattice spacings (\(a=0.082\) fm and \(a=0.065\) fm). They compute the connected contributions to the axial charges of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 baryons from the 20-plet and 20-prime-plet of SU(4) by evaluating two-point and three-point correlation functions at zero momentum transfer, extracting the axial charges via the plateau method after renormalization with \(Z_A\).

## Key numbers

*   Lattice spacings: \(a=0.082\) fm (at \(\beta=1.95\)) and \(a=0.065\) fm (at \(\beta=2.10\)), determined using the nucleon mass.
*   Pion mass range covered: approximately 210 MeV to 430 MeV across the five ensembles.
*   Renormalization constants: \(Z_A^{\beta=1.95}=0.7556(5)(85)\) and \(Z_A^{\beta=2.10}=0.7744(7)(31)\), taken from Abdel-Rehim (2015).
*   For the B55.32 ensemble (pion mass 373 MeV), the disconnected contribution to the isoscalar axial charge of the nucleon was previously found to be about 10% of the connected isoscalar axial charge.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the nucleon axial charge, which have been extensively performed by collaborations such as those using simulations with a physical pion mass (Abdel-Rehim 2015, Bali 2014), to the less-studied hyperons and charmed baryons. The results enable tests of SU(3) relations among axial charges as a function of pion mass and provide input for chiral expansions of baryonic quantities, with future work needed to include the neglected disconnected contributions for a complete determination.
