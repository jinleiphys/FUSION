# Low-Energy Magnetic Radiation

> A pronounced spike at low energy in the strength function for magnetic radiation (LEMAR) is found by means of Shell Model calculations, which explains the experimentally observed enhancement of the dipole strength. LEMAR originates from statistical low-energy M1-transitions between many excited complex states. Re-coupling of the proton and neutron high-j orbitals generates the strong magnetic radiation. LEMAR is predicted for nuclides with $A\approx 132$ participating in the r-process of element synthesis. It increases the reaction rates by a factor of 2.5. The spectral function of LEMAR follows Planck's Law. A power law for the size distribution of the $B(M1)$ values are found.

## Key claim

Shell Model calculations reveal a pronounced low-energy spike in the magnetic dipole (M1) strength function (LEMAR) that explains the experimentally observed enhancement of dipole strength below 3 MeV; the enhancement originates from statistical low-energy M1 transitions between many excited complex states, not from electric dipole transitions.

## Method

Shell Model calculations were performed for the nuclides ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁵Mo, ⁹⁶Mo, and ⁹⁰Zr using active proton orbits π(0f₅/₂, 1p₃/₂, 1p₁/₂, 0g₉/₂) and neutron orbits ν(0g₉/₂, 1d₅/₂, 0g₇/₂) relative to a ⁶⁸Ni core, with effective g-factors of g_s^eff = 0.7 g_s^free. For each spin, the lowest 40 states were calculated, and reduced transition probabilities B(M1) were computed for all transitions with E_f < E_i and spins J_f = J_i, J_i ± 1, resulting in more than 14000 M1 transitions per parity sorted into 100 keV bins.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹⁴Mo, the average B(M1) values follow an exponential function with T_B = 0.67 MeV and a Black Body distribution with T_P = 0.9 MeV
- For ⁹⁰Zr, ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁵Mo, and ⁹⁶Mo, the M1 strength function parameters are f_0 = (34, 37, 39, 55) × 10⁻⁹ MeV⁻³ and T_f = (0.50, 0.50, 0.51, 0.48) MeV respectively
- Including LEMAR increases the ¹³⁰Cd(n,γ) reaction rate by a factor of 2.5 over a wide temperature range
- The size distribution of B(M1) values follows a power law with exponent ν = 1.2
- For ⁹⁴Mo, the constant-temperature level density parameter is T_ρ = 0.66 MeV (Shell Model) and T_ρ = 0.63 MeV (experimental from RIPL3 database)
- With half-strength interaction, the Black Body temperature becomes T_P = 0.45 MeV

## Context

This work extends the experimental observations of enhanced dipole strength below 3 MeV in nuclides from A ≈ 40 to 100, particularly from (³He,³He') reactions on Mo isotopes by Guttormsen et al. (2005), by identifying the mechanism as M1 transitions rather than E1. The LEMAR mechanism is closely related to Magnetic Rotation (Frauendorf, Reviews of Modern Physics) but involves highly mixed configurations rather than pure near-yrast states. The results enable improved calculations of (n,γ) reaction rates for r-process nucleosynthesis, particularly for nuclei with A ≈ 132, and suggest that LEMAR could serve as a new thermometer of nuclear temperature if measured with sufficient accuracy.
