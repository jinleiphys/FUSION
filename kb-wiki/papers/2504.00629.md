# Selected topics on the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and density

> We will report recent progress on the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and density. In particular, we discuss the universal scaling of the chiral transition in the limit of two massless quarks and one strange quark. We also discuss influence of other control parameter as chemical potentials, external magnetic field strength and number of quark flavors on the chiral transition. From calculations of Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure w.r.t the baryon chemical potential and at imaginary chemical potential, we discuss estimates of the QCD critical point. Those estimates make use of the universal scaling ansatz of the Lee-Yang edge singularity.

## Key claim

The paper reports that universal scaling of the chiral transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD is consistent with O(2) universality class (which corresponds to O(4) in the continuum), but the scaling region is controversial and the analysis is restricted to a narrow temperature interval of T in [140 MeV, 148 MeV] and quark mass ratios H in [1/80, 1/40] to achieve a good fit quality.

## Method

The authors analyze lattice QCD calculations using highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) on Nτ=8 lattices, extracting the improved chiral order parameter and chiral susceptibility from simulations at finite temperature and varying light quark masses. They perform scaling fits to the equation of state using O(2) scaling functions, and also determine the critical exponent δ from ratios of the improved order parameter at different quark masses. For the QCD critical point search, they use Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure with respect to baryon chemical potential and multi-point Padé approximations from calculations at imaginary chemical potential to locate Lee-Yang edge singularities.

## Key numbers

- Chiral critical temperature from scaling fits: Tc = 143.7(2) MeV (Nτ=8, HISQ fermions)
- Chiral critical temperature from HotQCD (2019): Tc = 132+3-6 MeV
- Crossover temperature at physical quark masses: Tpc = 156.5(1.5) MeV (HotQCD 2018)
- Curvature coefficient κ2B = 0.012(4) (continuum extrapolated, nS=0 constraint)
- Curvature coefficient κ2B = 0.0153(18) (Borsanyi et al. 2020)
- Chiral transition temperature for Nf=3: Tc = 98+3-6 MeV
- Tricritical point located at Nftric > 7
- Charmed quarks generate half of total charm pressure already at T > 1.1 Tpc (approximately 175 MeV)
- Cumulants become perturbative for T > 450-700 MeV
- Lee-Yang edge singularity method tested on Ising Model, Roberge-Weiss transition, 3-state Potts model, and heavy-quark QCD

## Context

This proceedings paper from LATTICE2024 reviews recent progress on the QCD phase diagram, extending the work of HotQCD (2019, 2018) on chiral transition temperature and crossover temperature determinations. The universal scaling analysis builds on the framework of Pisarski and Wilczek (1983) and uses scaling functions from Karsch (2023). The critical point search via Lee-Yang edge singularities extends the approach of Dimopoulos (2021) and Clarke (2024), and has been validated on the Ising model by Singh (2023). The analysis of conserved charge fluctuations and hadron melting extends the HRG comparison work of Bazavov (2013, 2014) to include charmed degrees of freedom. The paper enables future continuum extrapolated scaling analyses with improved statistics and alternative fermion discretizations (Mobius domain wall fermions, twisted mass Wilson fermions) to resolve the universality class and scaling region controversies.
