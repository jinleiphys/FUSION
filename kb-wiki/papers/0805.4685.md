# Low-density limit as a test for the models of complicated processes in the nuclei

> We propose to use the branching ratio of channels of free-space hadron-nucleon interaction as a test in the construction and verification of the models of complicated processes in the nuclei. The particle self-energy and amplitudes of subprocesses are studied as well.

# Low-density limit as a test for the models of complicated processes in the nuclei

## Key claim
The branching ratio of channels in nuclear-medium decays and reactions should reproduce the corresponding free-space hadron-nucleon branching ratio in the low-density limit, and this condition serves as a necessary test for any model of multistep nuclear processes. The paper argues that models using dressed propagators with a separated real potential fail this test because they cannot reproduce the free-space subprocess when the medium density goes to zero.

## Method
The author calculates the decay widths for the process a → π⁰ + n̄ → π⁰ + M (annihilation channel) and the total decay width a → π⁰ + n̄ in nuclear matter using a diagrammatic approach with a bare antineutron propagator. The amplitude for antineutron-medium annihilation is defined through a T-exponential expansion analogous to the free-space case, and the low-density approximation is applied to express the annihilation rate as Γ = vρσ_a. The resulting branching ratios are compared with those obtained from a model where the antineutron propagator is dressed by a real optical potential, showing that the dressed-propagator model fails to reproduce the free-space limit.

## Key numbers
- For low-energy antineutron-nucleon interactions, σ_a/σ_t > 0.7, where σ_a is the free-space annihilation cross section and σ_t = σ_a + σ_s is the total cross section.
- The differential branching ratio for the decay in the medium satisfies dΓ_a/dq divided by dΓ_t/dq = σ_a/σ_t, and similarly dΓ_s/dq divided by dΓ_t/dq = σ_s/σ_t.
- For reactions a + N → b + c in the medium, the differential cross-section ratio satisfies dσ_r^m/dq divided by dσ_s^m/dq = σ_r/σ_s.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous analysis of n̄n transitions in the medium followed by annihilation (Nazaruk, 2008) to more general processes involving decays a → b + c and reactions a + N → b + c with intermediate-state interactions. It directly addresses the failure of phenomenological optical-potential models (shown in prior work by the author) to describe annihilation channels in decays, because the relation Σ|T_fi|² ≈ 2 Im T_ii cannot be used for non-unitary S-matrices. The results provide a necessary condition that any future model of multistep nuclear processes must satisfy, and the approach enables verification of model correctness through comparison with experimentally measured free-space hadron-nucleon branching ratios.
