# Multiplicities and Transverse Energies in Central AA Collisions at RHIC and LHC from pQCD, Saturation and Hydrodynamics

> We compute the particle multiplicities and transverse energies at central and nearly central AA collisions at RHIC and LHC. The initial state is computed from perturbative QCD supplemented by the conjecture of saturation of produced partons. The expansion stage is described in terms of hydrodynamics assuming longitudinal boost invariance and azimuthal symmetry. Transverse flow effects, a realistic list of hadrons and resonance decays are included. Comparison with the data of the multiplicities at $\sqrt s=56$ AGeV and 130 AGeV from RHIC is done and predictions for the full RHIC energy and LHC energy are made for the multiplicities and transverse energies. The reduction from the initially released minijet transverse energy to the $E_T$ in the final state is less than in the one-dimensional case but still dramatic: a factor of 2.7 at RHIC, and 3.6 at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper computes particle multiplicities and transverse energies in central AA collisions at RHIC and LHC using perturbative QCD with a saturation condition for produced partons, followed by hydrodynamic expansion including transverse flow; the reduction from initial minijet transverse energy to final-state \(E_T\) is a factor of 2.7 at RHIC and 3.6 at LHC, which is smaller than the factor of 3 (RHIC) and 6 (LHC) estimated in the earlier one-dimensional expansion model of Eskola et al. (2000).

## Method

The initial state is computed from leading-order pQCD minijet production with a \(K\)-factor from NLO calculations, using the GRV94 parton distributions and nuclear shadowing from the EKS98 analysis. A saturation criterion equates the total transverse area of minijets above a scale \(p_0\) to the nuclear transverse area, yielding a saturation scale \(p_{\rm sat}\) and formation time \(\tau_i = 1/p_{\rm sat}\); the initial energy density profile is then obtained from the minijet transverse energy. The expansion is modeled with (1+1)-dimensional boost-invariant hydrodynamics including transverse flow, using an equation of state with a QGP phase (massless gluons and two quark flavors), a mixed phase at \(T_c = 167\) MeV, and a hadron gas with resonances up to \(\Sigma(1385)\) and a mean-field repulsion. Final particle spectra are computed via the Cooper-Frye decoupling procedure at \(T_{\rm dec} \approx 120\) MeV, including resonance decays, and the results are averaged over a 6% centrality selection using effective nuclei \(A_{\rm eff}\).

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale \(p_{\rm sat}\) for central Au-Au at \(\sqrt s = 200\) GeV: 1.16 GeV (1.15 GeV with 6% centrality cut); for central Pb-Pb at \(\sqrt s = 5500\) GeV: 2.03 GeV (2.01 GeV with 6% cut).
- Formation time \(\tau_i = 1/p_{\rm sat}\) for central Au-Au at \(\sqrt s = 200\) GeV: 0.17 fm; for central Pb-Pb at \(\sqrt s = 5500\) GeV: 0.10 fm.
- Reduction factor from initial minijet \(E_T\) to final-state \(dE_T/dy\) at \(y=0\): 2.7 at full RHIC energy (\(\sqrt s = 200\) GeV) and 3.6 at LHC energy (\(\sqrt s = 5500\) GeV).
- Predicted charged particle multiplicity \(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta|_{|\eta|\le1}\) for central Pb-Pb at \(\sqrt s = 5.5\) TeV with 6% centrality cut: about 2560.
- Average entropy per particle at freeze-out: \(S_f/N_f \approx 4.68\) with the hadron list used.
- Phase transition temperature: \(T_c = 167\) MeV; decoupling temperature: \(T_{\rm dec} \approx 120\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the saturation-based pQCD approach of Eskola et al. (2000, EKRT) by including transverse flow in hydrodynamics and a realistic hadron resonance gas with decays, improving predictions for final-state transverse energies and multiplicities. The results agree well with the first PHOBOS data at \(\sqrt s = 56\) and 130 GeV without fitting, and the predicted \(\sqrt s\) scaling of multiplicity is compatible with those data. The approach enables more precise tests of transverse dynamics via particle spectra and can be extended to non-central collisions and elliptic flow, as noted for future work by Huovinen et al.
