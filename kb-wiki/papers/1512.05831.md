# The pole structure of the Lambda(1405) in a recent QCD simulation

> The $Λ(1405)$ baryon is difficult to detect in experiment, absent in many quark model calculations, and supposedly manifested through a two-pole structure. Its uncommon properties made it subject to numerous experimental and theoretical studies in recent years. Lattice-QCD eigenvalues for different quark masses were recently reported by the Adelaide group. We compare these eigenvalues to predictions of a model based on Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory. The UCHPT calculation predicts the quark mass dependence remarkably well. It also explains the overlap pattern with different meson-baryon components, mainly $πΣ$ and $\bar KN$, at different quark masses. More accurate lattice QCD data are required to draw definite conclusions on the nature of the $Λ(1405)$.

## Key claim

The U$\chi$PT (Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory) model predicts the quark mass dependence of the $\Lambda(1405)$ finite-volume eigenvalues from lattice QCD remarkably well, but the authors state that more accurate lattice QCD data are required to draw definite conclusions on the nature of the $\Lambda(1405)$.

## Method

The authors compare lattice QCD eigenvalues reported by the Adelaide group (Hall et al.) for different quark masses to predictions from a coupled-channel U$\chi$PT model. The model uses the lowest-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction in a finite-volume formalism, with the Bethe-Salpeter equation solved in a cubic box to generate energy levels. The quark mass dependence of meson decay constants $f_\pi$, $f_K$, and $f_\eta$ is included via an SU(3) chiral extrapolation, while subtraction constants are varied to estimate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Two poles are found in the infinite-volume amplitude at $W_0 = (1379 - 71i)$ MeV and $(1412 - 20i)$ MeV, both on the same Riemann sheet.
- The lighter pole couples predominantly to $\pi\Sigma$ with coupling $|g_{\pi\Sigma}| = 3.1$, while the heavier pole couples more strongly to $\bar{K}N$ with $|g_{\bar{K}N}| = 3.1$.
- The $\Lambda(1670)$ pole is found at $W_0 = (1672 - 18i)$ MeV, appearing as a quasi-bound $K\Xi$ state with $|g_{K\Xi}| = 3.4$.
- Lattice data from Hall et al. cover pion masses between 170 MeV and 620 MeV, with a box size of approximately $L \simeq 3$ fm.
- The subtraction constants used are $a_{\bar{K}N} = -2.2$, $a_{\pi\Sigma} = -1.6$, $a_{\eta\Lambda} = -2.5$, $a_{K\Xi} = -2.9$, with the dimensional regularization scale $\mu = 630$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume predictions for the $\Lambda(1405)$ from Doring (2011) and provides the first comparison between lattice QCD data from the Adelaide group (Hall et al.) and U$\chi$PT predictions. The study tests the two-pole hypothesis of the $\Lambda(1405)$ originally proposed by Oller (2000) and Jido (2003), and enables future lattice simulations to use meson-baryon operators for improved level detection. The results also connect to the compositeness analysis of Sekihara (2014) and the finite-volume formalism developed by Doring (2011) and Martinez Torres (2011).
