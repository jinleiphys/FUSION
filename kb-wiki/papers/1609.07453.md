# Shear viscosity of nuclear matter

> Shear viscosity $η$ is calculated for the nuclear matter described as a system of interacting nucleons with the van der Waals (VDW) equation of state. The Boltzmann-Vlasov kinetic equation is solved in terms of the plane waves of the collective overdamped motion. In the frequent-collision regime, the shear viscosity depends on the particle-number density $n$ through the mean-field parameter $a$, which describes attractive forces in the VDW equation. In the temperature region $T=15 - 40$~MeV, a ratio of the shear viscosity to the entropy density $s$ is smaller than 1 at the nucleon number density $n =(0.5 - 1.5)\,n^{}_0$, where $n^{}_0=0.16\,$fm$^{-3}$ is the particle density of equilibrium nuclear matter at zero temperature. A minimum of the $η/s$ ratio takes place somewhere in a vicinity of the critical point of the VDW system. Large values of $η/s\gg 1$ are, however, found in both the low-density, $n\ll n^{}_0$, and high-density, $n>2n^{}_0$, regions. This makes the ideal hydrodynamic approach inapplicable for these densities.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of nuclear matter, calculated using the Boltzmann-Vlasov kinetic equation with a van der Waals equation of state, acquires a dependence on particle-number density through the mean-field attractive interaction parameter, a result that the abstract presents as a general finding but which is derived specifically within the frequent-collision (overdamped) regime and for temperatures above the critical point (T > 14 MeV).

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Boltzmann-Vlasov kinetic equation for a system of nucleons interacting via the van der Waals equation of state, using damping plane wave solutions for the dynamical variations of the distribution function. The collision term was treated in the frequent-collision regime using a perturbation expansion in the small parameter ω/ν (frequency over collision frequency), with the multipole expansion of the angular dependence truncated at quadrupole order (ℓ ≤ 2). The shear viscosity was extracted from the stress tensor via the relationship between the traceless stress tensor and the velocity gradient tensor, using the leading-order solution of the cubic dispersion relation for the complex sound velocity.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity in the frequent-collision regime is given by η = (9/(20√(2π))) (1 - (5/9)(an/T)) √(mT)/d², which equals 1.018 (1 - (5/9)(an/T)) η_CE, where η_CE is the Chapman-Enskog result.
- The critical temperature of the van der Waals system is T_c = 14 MeV, with critical density n_c = 0.16 fm⁻³ (equal to the normal nuclear matter density n₀).
- The van der Waals parameters used are d = 1 fm (giving b ≈ 2.1 fm³) and a = 100 MeV·fm³.
- The ratio η/s is smaller than 1 in the temperature region T = 15-40 MeV at nucleon number densities n = (0.5-1.5)n₀.
- Large values η/s ≫ 1 are found at low densities n ≪ n₀ and high densities n > 2n₀.
- The collision frequency is ν ≈ (3/2) n v_T σ, where σ = πd² is the hard-sphere cross section.

## Context

This work extends the Chapman-Enskog approach (Chapman, 1939) for shear viscosity by incorporating the self-consistent mean field from attractive van der Waals interactions, which the standard CE method neglects at leading order. The results contrast with the density-independent CE viscosity formula and show that the η/s ratio can fall below 1 near the critical point, similar to findings in relativistic heavy-ion collision studies (Csernai, 2006; Shaefer, 2009; Wiranata, 2012; Kapusta, 2012). The approach enables future studies of the transition between frequent-collision and rare-collision regimes, which is relevant for nuclear matter at low and high densities where ideal hydrodynamics becomes inapplicable.
