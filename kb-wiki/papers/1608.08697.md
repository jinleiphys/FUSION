# Proton distribution radii of $^{12-19}$C illuminate features of neutron halos

> Proton radii of $^{12-19}$C densities derived from first accurate charge changing cross section measurements at 900$A$ MeV with a carbon target are reported. A thick neutron surface evolves from $\sim$ 0.5 fm in $^{15}$C to $\sim$ 1 fm in $^{19}$C. The halo radius in $^{19}$C is found to be 6.4$\pm$0.7 fm as large as $^{11}$Li. Ab initio calculations based on chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces reproduce well the radii.

## Key claim

The first accurate proton radii for neutron-rich carbon isotopes \(^{15-19}\)C are derived from charge-changing cross section measurements, revealing a thick neutron surface that grows from about 0.5 fm in \(^{15}\)C to about 1 fm in \(^{19}\)C, with the halo radius of \(^{19}\)C found to be 6.4\(\pm\)0.7 fm, comparable to that of \(^{11}\)Li. The abstract's claim that ab initio calculations reproduce the radii well is qualified by the fact that for \(^{19}\)C, which is weakly bound, the coupled-cluster effective interaction method with the NNLO\(_{\rm sat}\) interaction yields an unbound nucleus, so its radius is not defined and not shown in the comparison.

## Method

Charge-changing cross sections (\(\sigma_{cc}\)) were measured for \(^{12-19}\)C at approximately 900\(A\) MeV using a carbon target at the FRS fragment separator at GSI, Darmstadt, using the transmission technique with event-by-event particle identification via magnetic rigidity, time-of-flight, and energy loss. The point-proton radii (\(R_p\)) were extracted from the measured \(\sigma_{cc}\) using a finite-range Glauber model with harmonic oscillator densities, and the method was validated by comparing the derived \(R_p\) for \(^{12-14}\)C with known values from electron scattering and muonic X-ray measurements. Coupled-cluster calculations using the chiral NNLO\(_{\rm sat}\) interaction (including nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces) were performed for \(^{13-19}\)C and compared with the experimental radii.

## Key numbers

*   Proton radius \(R_p\) of \(^{12}\)C: 2.32\(\pm\)0.02 fm, derived from \(\sigma_{cc}\) = 733\(\pm\)7 mb at 937\(A\) MeV.
*   Proton radius \(R_p\) of \(^{15}\)C: 2.37\(\pm\)0.03 fm, derived from \(\sigma_{cc}\) = 743\(\pm\)7 mb at 907\(A\) MeV.
*   Proton radius \(R_p\) of \(^{19}\)C: 2.40\(\pm\)0.03 fm, derived from \(\sigma_{cc}\) = 749\(\pm\)9 mb at 895\(A\) MeV.
*   Matter radius \(R_m\) of \(^{19}\)C: 3.16\(\pm\)0.07 fm.
*   Halo radius of \(^{19}\)C: 6.4\(\pm\)0.7 fm, derived from a core-plus-neutron model.
*   Halo radius of \(^{15}\)C: 4.2\(\pm\)0.5 fm.
*   Neutron skin thickness evolves from about 0.5 fm in \(^{15}\)C to about 1 fm in \(^{19}\)C.
*   Charge radius of \(^{12}\)C from electron scattering: 2.478\(\pm\)0.009 fm (from Of91).
*   Charge radius of \(^{12}\)C from muonic X-ray studies: 2.472\(\pm\)0.015 fm (from Sc82).

## Context

This work extends the technique of extracting proton radii from charge-changing cross sections, previously applied by Es15 and Te, to the neutron-rich carbon isotopes up to the drip line, providing the first such data for \(^{15-19}\)C. The results contradict the earlier \(\sigma_{cc}\) measurements for \(^{12-20}\)C by Ch00, which had large uncertainties and were inconsistent with electron-scattering radii, and also disagree with the report by Ya11 that \(\sigma_{cc}\) for \(^{16}\)C is smaller than for \(^{15}\)C. The data enable stringent tests of ab initio coupled-cluster calculations with chiral interactions, showing that the NNLO\(_{\rm sat}\) interaction (Ek15), which simultaneously optimizes nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon force parameters, provides much better agreement with the measured radii than other chiral interactions such as NNLO\(_{\rm opt}\) (Ekstrom 2013) or the EM family of interactions (He11).
