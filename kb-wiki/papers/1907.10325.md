# $s$-wave quasiparticle resonance in neutron-rich drip-line nuclei

> We investigate unbound single-particle states in pair-correlated drip-line nuclei by describing a low-energy elastic scattering of a neutron in the $s$-wave within the framework of the coordinate space Hartree-Fock-Bogolibov (Bogoliubov-de Genne) equation. Numerical study is performed for a neutron drip-line carbon isotope where the neutron $2s_{1/2}$ orbit is located close to zero energy. Analyzing the S-matrix poles of the elastic scattering, we discuss properties of the $s$-wave quasiparticle resonance and, in particular, behaviors characteristic to drip-line nuclei. It is found that the S-matrix has two pairs of poles; one pair appears as either a weakly bound state, a virtual state or a resonance while the other pair gives contribution analogous to a bound single-particle state. The $s$-wave quasiparticle resonance emerges with a large variation depending on the pairing gap and the single-particle energy of the $s$-orbit.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pairing correlation in neutron-rich drip-line nuclei can produce an \(s\)-wave quasiparticle resonance, which is otherwise nearly impossible in ordinary potential scattering. The authors show that the S-matrix contains two pairs of poles, and that the resonance emerges with a large variation depending on the pairing gap and the single-particle energy of the \(s\)-orbit, rather than being a fixed feature.

## Method

The authors solve the coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Bogoliubov–de Gennes) equation for low-energy elastic neutron scattering on a spherical, even-\(N\) target nucleus, using a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential and a local Woods-Saxon pair potential. They compute the elastic cross section, phase shift, and S-matrix poles on the four-sheeted Riemann surface of the complex energy plane, varying the average pairing gap \(\bar{\Delta}\) from 0 to 3 MeV and the depth of the mean-field potential to shift the \(2s_{1/2}\) single-particle energy near zero.

## Key numbers

* The average pairing gap \(\bar{\Delta}\) is varied from 0 to 3 MeV; the empirical systematics is \(\Delta^{(\mathrm{exp})} \approx 12/\sqrt{A} \approx 2.7\) MeV for carbon isotopes.
* For the representative case with single-particle energy \(e_{2s_{1/2}} = -0.242\) MeV and Fermi energy \(\lambda = -0.230\) MeV, the scattering length \(a_{\mathrm{s}}\) changes from 12.658 fm at \(\bar{\Delta}=0.0\) MeV to \(-4.823\) fm at \(\bar{\Delta}=1.5\) MeV and to 3.165 fm at \(\bar{\Delta}=3.0\) MeV.
* The effective range \(r_{\mathrm{eff}}\) becomes negative for \(\bar{\Delta} \ge 1.0\) MeV, reaching \(-156.011\) fm at \(\bar{\Delta}=2.5\) MeV, compared to 5.373 fm at \(\bar{\Delta}=0.0\) MeV.
* In the limit \(\bar{\Delta}\rightarrow 0\), the bound quasiparticle state lies at \(E_a = |e_{2s_{1/2}}-\lambda| = 0.012\) MeV, with wave numbers \(k_1 = i\kappa_{+} = i0.108\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(k_2 = i\kappa_{-} = i0.103\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous study of \(p\)-wave quasiparticle resonance in neutron-rich Si-region nuclei (Kobayashi, 2016) to the \(s\)-wave case, which is more interesting because an \(s\)-wave resonance hardly exists in potential scattering without many-body correlation. It contrasts with a pioneering analysis by Belyaev (1987) that focused on stable nuclei, by emphasizing the role of a shallow Fermi energy in weakly bound nuclei. The results enable future experimental searches for low-lying \(s\)-wave resonances in unbound isotopes such as \({}^{21}\)C and \({}^{23}\)C, where current experimental evidence is conflicting (Orr, 2016; Nakamura, 2016; Mosby, 2013) and theoretical models (Hamamoto, 2016; Lu, 2013; Yuan, 2012; Jansen, 2014) have not clarified the resonance mechanism.
