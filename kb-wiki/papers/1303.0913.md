# Universal hadronization condition in heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}= 62$ GeV and at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.76$ TeV

> We obtain a detailed description of all available hadron multiplicity yields in central Pb--Pb collisions at LHC measured in the rapidity interval $|y|<0.5$. We find that the hadronization of the fireball at LHC occurs at nearly identical intensive physical bulk conditions for all centralities similar to those seen already at RHIC.

## Key claim

The hadronization of the fireball in Pb–Pb collisions at LHC occurs at nearly identical intensive physical bulk conditions (temperature, energy density, pressure, entropy density, strangeness per entropy) for all centralities, and these conditions are similar to those seen at RHIC at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4\) GeV, despite a factor of 45 difference in reaction energy. This universality is demonstrated using a chemical non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model (SHM) with light quark phase space occupancy \(\gamma_q > 1\), which is necessary to describe the proton-to-pion ratio alongside multi-strange baryon yields.

## Method

The authors fit hadron multiplicity yields in central Pb–Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV (LHC) using the chemical non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model (SHM) implemented in the SHARE v2.2 program. The fit parameters are the hadronization volume \(dV/dy\), temperature \(T\), light quark phase space occupancy \(\gamma_q\), strange quark phase space occupancy \(\gamma_s\), and light quark fugacity \(\lambda_q\), with isospin fugacity \(\lambda_{I3}\) constrained by charge per baryon ratio and strangeness fugacity \(\lambda_s\) constrained by strangeness conservation. For more peripheral centralities (20–40%, 40–60%, 60–80%), the fit uses a reduced data set of particle ratios (K*\(^0\)/K\(^-\), \(\Lambda/\pi\), \(\phi\)/K\(^-\)) and charged particle rapidity density \(dN_{\mathrm{ch}}/dy\), complemented by yields of \(\pi^\pm\), K\(^\pm\), and p\(^\pm\).

## Key numbers

- Temperature \(T = 138.6 \pm 1.1\) MeV for 0–20% centrality at LHC; \(T = 139.5\) MeV for 0–5% centrality at RHIC.
- Light quark phase space occupancy \(\gamma_q = 1.627 \pm 0.007\) for 0–20% centrality at LHC; \(\gamma_q = 1.58\) for 0–5% centrality at RHIC.
- Strange quark phase space occupancy \(\gamma_s = 2.04 \pm 0.04\) for 0–20% centrality at LHC; \(\gamma_s = 2.24\) for 0–5% centrality at RHIC.
- Energy density \(\varepsilon = 0.466 \pm 0.018\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for 0–20% centrality at LHC; \(\varepsilon = 0.493\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for 0–5% centrality at RHIC.
- Pressure \(P = 79.1 \pm 2.8\) MeV/fm\(^3\) for 0–20% centrality at LHC; \(P = 82.0\) MeV/fm\(^3\) for 0–5% centrality at RHIC.
- Entropy density \(\sigma = 3.23 \pm 0.11\) fm\(^{-3}\) for 0–20% centrality at LHC; \(\sigma = 3.40\) fm\(^{-3}\) for 0–5% centrality at RHIC.
- Strangeness per entropy \(s/S = 0.0296 \pm 0.0002\) for 0–20% centrality at LHC; \(s/S = 0.0322\) for 0–5% centrality at RHIC.
- Hadronization volume \(dV/dy = 2455 \pm 146\) fm\(^3\) for 0–20% centrality at LHC; \(dV/dy = 853\) fm\(^3\) for 0–5% centrality at RHIC.
- \(\chi^2/\mathrm{ndf} = 7.40/8\) for 0–20% centrality at LHC (non-equilibrium SHM), compared to \(\chi^2 = 34.8\) for the equilibrium SHM fit to the same data.
- Entropy ratio \(S_{\mathrm{LHC}}/S_{\mathrm{RHIC}} = 3.05\) for 0–20% centrality, decreasing to 1.52 for 60–80% centrality.

## Context

This work extends the chemical non-equilibrium SHM approach previously applied to SPS data (Letessier 1998, Letessier 2000) and RHIC data (Rafelski 2004, Letessier 2005, Petran 2011) to LHC energies, demonstrating that the same hadronization conditions (energy density \(\varepsilon \approx 0.50 \pm 0.05\) GeV/fm\(^3\), pressure \(P \approx 82 \pm 8\) MeV/fm\(^3\)) hold across a wide energy range. The results contradict the equilibrium SHM fits (Abelev 2012) which yield large \(\chi^2\) and fail to describe the proton-to-pion ratio alongside multi-strange baryons, and also argue against post-hadronization interaction models (Steinheimer 2012, Karpenko 2012) that attempt to explain the proton anomaly through hadronic rescattering. The universal hadronization condition enables a consistent picture of sudden QGP hadronization across SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, with the entropy content \(dS/dy\) serving as a measure of pre-hadronization fireball properties.
