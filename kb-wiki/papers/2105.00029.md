# Constraining free parameters of a color superconducting non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model using Bayesian analysis of neutron stars mass and radius measurements

> We provide a systematic study of hybrid neutron star equations of state (EoS) consisting of a relativistic density functional for the hadronic phase and a covariant nonlocal Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (nlNJL) model to describe the color superconducting quark matter phase. Changing the values of the two free parameters, the dimensionless vector and diquark coupling strengths $η_V$ and $η_D$ results in a set of EoS with varying stiffness and deconfinement onset. The favorable parameters are obtained from a systematic Bayesian analysis for which the multi-messenger constraint on the neutron star radius at $1.4~$M$_\odot$ and the combined mass-radius constraint for PSR J0740+6620 from NICER experiment are used as the constraints. Additionally, the transition from hadronic matter to deconfined quark matter is constrained to occur above nuclear saturation density. Hybrid stars modeled with these favorable parameters are compatible with the NICER results for the radius of the highest known mass neutron star, PSR J0740+6620. Three new observations interesting for neutron star phenomenology are reported: 1) We show that the constant sound speed (CSS) EoS provides an excellent fit to that of the nlNJL model which implies the squared speed of sound at high densities to be about $0.5$ for the optimized parameters; 2) we give a simple functional form for the mapping between the parameter spaces of these two models valid for the whole range of relevant chemical potentials and 3) we observe that the special point property of hybrid EoS based on CSS quark matter generalizes to a set of lines consisting of special points when two EoS parameters are varied instead of one. A lower limit for the maximum mass of hybrid stars as a function of the vector coupling strength is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a covariant non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nlNJL) model with two free parameters (vector coupling strength η_V and diquark coupling strength η_D) can produce hybrid neutron star equations of state that are compatible with NICER mass-radius constraints for PSR J0740+6620, but only when the parameters are constrained by Bayesian analysis using multi-messenger data and the condition that the hadron-to-quark phase transition occurs above nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid neutron star equations of state by combining a relativistic density functional for the hadronic phase (DD2p40 model) with a covariant non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (nlNJL) model for the color superconducting quark matter phase, using a Maxwell construction for the first-order phase transition. They varied the two free parameters η_V (vector coupling strength) and η_D (diquark coupling strength) over ranges 0.10 < η_V < 0.20 and 0.70 < η_D < 0.80, then performed a Bayesian analysis using multi-messenger constraints including the neutron star radius at 1.4 solar masses and the combined mass-radius constraint for PSR J0740+6620 from NICER. The nlNJL equation of state was also fitted to a constant sound speed (CSS) parameterization to enable simpler extrapolation to higher densities.

## Key numbers

- The squared speed of sound in the quark matter phase is found to be in the range 0.45 < c_s^2 < 0.54 for the whole domain of parameters studied
- The squared speed of sound for the optimized parameters is about 0.5
- The Gaussian form factor parameters are fixed: current quark mass m_c = 5.4869 MeV, effective momentum scale p_0 = 782.16 MeV, and coupling constant G_S p_0^2 = 19.804
- The maximum neutron star mass must fulfill the observational lower bound of 2.01 solar masses from PSR J0740+6620 at the 1-sigma level
- The CSS model parameters A, B, and c_s^2 are free parameters defining the quark matter equation of state behavior

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid neutron stars using non-local NJL models, particularly building on the approach of Blaschke (2007) which showed that including both vector and diquark interactions in the covariant nlNJL model produces early deconfinement transitions to stiff color superconducting quark matter. The paper also connects to the constant sound speed (CSS) parameterization widely used in the literature, as in Alford (2013) and Zdunik (2012), and demonstrates that the nlNJL model can be accurately approximated by the CSS model. The Bayesian analysis methodology follows the approach of Ayriyan (2021) and Blaschke (2020), and the results enable future phenomenological studies of hybrid stars using the simpler CSS parameterization while retaining microphysical justification from the nlNJL model.
