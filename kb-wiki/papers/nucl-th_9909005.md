# Meson mass modification in strange hadronic matter

> We investigate in stable strange hadronic matter (SHM) the modification of the masses of the scalar ($σ$, $σ^*$) and the vector ($ω$, $ϕ$) mesons. The baryon ground state is treated in the relativistic Hartree approximation in the nonlinear $σ$-$ω$ and linear $σ^*$-$ϕ$ model. In stable SHM, the masses of all the mesons reveal considerable reduction due to large vacuum polarization contribution from the hyperons and small density dependent effects caused by larger binding.

# Meson mass modification in strange hadronic matter

## Key claim

In stable strange hadronic matter (SHM) composed of nucleons, Λ, and Ξ baryons, the masses of scalar (σ, σ*) and vector (ω, φ) mesons are considerably reduced relative to their vacuum values, primarily due to large vacuum polarization contributions from hyperons rather than density-dependent effects from the baryon Fermi seas.

## Method

The baryon ground state was treated in the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) within a nonlinear σ-ω and linear σ*-φ quantum hadrodynamic (QHD) model, including vacuum fluctuations from all baryon species. The meson propagators in the medium were computed by summing over one-loop polarization insertions in the relativistic random phase approximation (RPA), with scalar-vector mixing included, and the effective meson masses were determined from the zeros of the dielectric function for given three-momentum transfer q.

## Key numbers

- Maximum binding energy of stable SHM: E/B = -38.46 MeV at strangeness fraction f_S ≈ 1.35 and baryon density n_B ≈ 3n_0 (where n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is nuclear matter saturation density).
- Nuclear matter saturation properties used to fix nucleon couplings: n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, binding energy E/B = -16 MeV, effective nucleon mass m_N*/m_N = 0.78, compression modulus K = 300 MeV.
- Well-depths used for hyperon couplings: U_Λ^(N) = 30 MeV, U_Ξ^(N) = 28 MeV in nuclear matter; U_(Λ,Ξ)^(Ξ) ≈ 40 MeV in a Ξ "bath" at n_Ξ ≈ n_0.
- Free-space widths used for scalar mesons: Γ_σ^0 = 300 MeV, Γ_σ*^0 = 70 MeV.
- Bare hadronic masses: m_N = 939 MeV, m_Λ = 1116 MeV, m_Ξ = 1313 MeV, m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_σ* = 975 MeV, m_φ = 1020 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of in-medium meson mass modification, which had been performed only in nuclear matter (with nucleons alone), to the environment of strange hadronic matter containing hyperons. Prior studies in the QHD model by Jea (1994), Shi (1995), and Sai (1996) had shown that including particle-antiparticle excitations from the Dirac sea leads to decreasing meson masses, in contrast to the increase found when only particle-hole excitations from the Fermi sea were considered. The present calculation also incorporates the σ*-φ model of Schaffner (1994) to reproduce the strongly attractive ΛΛ interaction, and uses SU(6) relations for hyperon-meson couplings. The results suggest that meson mass modification could serve as a signal for the formation of strange hadronic matter in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
