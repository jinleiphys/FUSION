# Charmonium spectral functions in $\bar p A$ collision

> We study the in-medium propagation of low-lying charmonium states: $J/Ψ$, $Ψ$(3686), and $Ψ$(3770) in a $\bar p$ Au $10$ GeV collision. This energy regime will be available for the PANDA experiment. The time evolution of the spectral functions of the charmonium states is studied with a BUU type transport model. We observe a substantial effect of the medium in the dilepton spectrum.

## Key claim

The in-medium modification of higher charmonium states (Ψ(3686) and Ψ(3770)) produces a two-peak structure in the dilepton spectrum that should be observable in a 10 GeV antiproton-gold collision at PANDA, but the J/Ψ effect is negligible due to its very small mass shift.

## Method

The authors used a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) type transport model with off-shell propagation of test particles, solving the Kadanoff-Baym equations via a first-order gradient expansion after Wigner transformation. The self-energy of each vector meson was parameterized with a linear density dependence for both the real part (mass shift) and imaginary part (collision broadening), using parameters from Lee (2015). The time evolution of the spectral functions for J/Ψ, Ψ(3686), and Ψ(3770) was computed in a 10 GeV antiproton-gold collision.

## Key numbers

- Mass shift parameters at normal nuclear density: J/Ψ Δm = -15 MeV, Ψ(3686) Δm = -100 MeV, Ψ(3770) Δm = -140 MeV
- Collision broadening parameter ΔΓ = 20 MeV for all three charmonium states
- Transition time from dense nuclear matter to vacuum is approximately 4 fm/c
- In the dilepton spectrum figure, Ψ(3770) yields are multiplied by 5000 and Ψ(3686) yields are multiplied by 10 relative to J/Ψ for visibility

## Context

This work extends the off-shell transport method previously applied to ω and ρ mesons at HADES energies by Kampfer and Wolf (2010) to the charmonium sector. It uses self-energy parameters from Lee (2015) and contrasts with Golubeva et al. (2015), who did not include a mass shift for charmonium states and therefore did not observe the two-peak structure. The results enable experimental searches at the PANDA experiment at FAIR, though the authors note that background from Drell-Yan processes and open charm decay must still be evaluated.
