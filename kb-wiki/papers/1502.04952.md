# Effect of the symmetry energy and hyperon interaction on neutron stars

> The joint effect of the density dependence of the symmetry energy and strangeness content on the structure of cold neutron stars is studied within the framework of a relativistic mean field theory. It is shown that 2$M_\odot$ are obtained for repulsive $YY$ interaction and preferably for a small or a large slope $L$. An attractive $Σ$ potential in nuclear matter will favor the appearance of strangeness in stars with a mass as small as $\sim 1 M_\odot$, if, however it is repulsive only stars with a mass $\gtrsim 1.4 M_\odot$ will contain strangeness. The joint effect of reducing the symmetry energy slope and including hyperons is to farther reduce the radius. Neutron star maximum mass evolve non-monotonically with the symmetry energy slope, and the smallest masses are obtained for values $L\sim 80$ MeV. Other neutron star variables evolve nonlinearly with the slope of the symmetry energy and depend on the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon couplings. The radius of a neutron star is linearly correlated with the neutron star total strangeness fraction and the slope is independent of the slope of the symmetry energy and the mass of the star.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neutron stars can reach 2 solar masses with repulsive hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions, and this occurs preferably for either a small or a large slope L of the symmetry energy, not for intermediate values. The abstract's claim that "2 solar masses are obtained for repulsive YY interaction and preferably for a small or a large slope L" glosses over the non-monotonic behavior where the smallest maximum masses occur at L values around 80 MeV.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach with the TM1-2 parameter set, incorporating the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ⁺, Σ⁰, Σ⁻, Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻) and leptons (e⁻, μ⁻) in β-equilibrium. They varied the symmetry energy slope L from 50 to 111 MeV by including nonlinear ω-ρ and σ-ρ coupling terms, and explored nine different hyperon optical potential sets for Σ and Ξ hyperons, with hyperon-hyperon interactions mediated by σ* and φ mesons with hidden strangeness. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations were solved to obtain neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass for nucleonic (no strangeness) matter: 2.27 M⊙ at L=111 MeV, 2.22 M⊙ (Λω) and 2.24 M⊙ (Λσ) at L=50 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope L range explored: 50 to 111 MeV
- Symmetry energy J range: 31.32 to 36.84 MeV
- Symmetry energy fixed at 25.52 MeV at ρ=0.1 fm⁻³
- Λ hyperon potential in nuclear matter: U^N_Λ = -28 MeV (fixed)
- Σ hyperon potentials explored: U^N_Σ = +30, 0, -30 MeV
- Ξ hyperon potentials explored: U^N_Ξ = +18, 0, -18 MeV
- Smallest maximum masses obtained for L ∼ 80 MeV
- An attractive Σ potential (U^N_Σ = -30 MeV) allows strangeness appearance in stars as small as ∼1 M⊙
- A repulsive Σ potential (U^N_Σ = +30 MeV) restricts strangeness to stars with mass ≳ 1.4 M⊙

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies by Providência et al. (2013) and Rabhi et al. (2011) on the nonlinear dependence of neutron star properties on the symmetry energy slope, now incorporating hyperonic degrees of freedom. It addresses the tension between the 2 M⊙ pulsar observations (PSR J1614-2230 by Demorest et al. 2010 and PSR J0348+0432 by Antoniadis et al. 2013) and earlier Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Balberg and Gal (1997) that could barely reach 1.4-1.5 M⊙ with hyperons. The results enable future studies to constrain hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions by combining neutron star mass and radius measurements with the observed linear correlation between radius and total strangeness fraction.
