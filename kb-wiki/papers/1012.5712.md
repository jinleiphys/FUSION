# Graphical Method for Effective Interaction with a New Vertex Function

> Introducing a new vertex function, Z(E), of an energy variable E, we derive a new equation for the effective interaction. The equation is obtained by replacing the Q-box in the Krenciglowa-Kuo (KK) method by Z(E). This new approach can be viewed as an extension of the KK method. We show that this equation can be solved both in iterative and non-iterative ways. We observe that the iteration procedure with Z(E) brings about fast convergence compared to the usual KK method. It is shown that, as in the KK approach, the procedure of calculating the effective interaction can be reduced to determining the true eigenvalues of the original Hamiltonian H and they can be obtained as the positions of intersections of graphs generated from Z(E). We find that this graphical method yields always precise results and reproduces any of the true eigenvalues of H. The calculation in the present approach can be made regardless of overlaps with the model space and energy differences between unperturbed energies and the eigenvalues of H. We find also that Z(E) is a well-behaved function of E and has no singularity. These characteristics of the present approach ensure stability in actual calculations and would be helpful to resolve some difficulties due to the presence of poles in the Q-box. Performing test calculations we verify numerically theoretical predictions made in the present approach.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new vertex function, the Z-box, which replaces the Q-box in the Krenciglowa-Kuo (KK) method to derive an extended Krenciglowa-Kuo (EKK) equation for the effective interaction; the authors claim this approach yields fast convergence and can reproduce any of the true eigenvalues of the original Hamiltonian, regardless of overlaps with the model space or energy differences, and that the Z-box has no singularity even at the pole energies of the Q-box.

## Method

The authors define the Z-box operator as a function of energy E in terms of the Q-box and its first energy derivative, then derive an eigenvalue equation in the model space (P space) using the Z-box. They show that the true eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian H can be obtained as the intersections of graphs of the eigenvalues F_m(E) of the Z-box operator with the line y = E, and that the energy derivative of F_m(E) distinguishes true eigenvalues (derivative zero) from pole-energy solutions (derivative two). The authors implement a combined secant and Newton-Raphson method to solve the graphical equations, and test the approach on an exactly solvable model Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* For the model Hamiltonian with strength x = 0.05, the exact lowest eigenvalue is E_1 = 0.8904504858869942 and the second lowest is E_2 = 2.2156808150096040.
* In the iteration E^{(n+1)} = F_m(E^{(n)}) (EKK method), the lowest eigenvalue converges to more than fifteen decimal places after 4 iterations, while the KK method requires 7 iterations to reach 0.8904504858...
* For the second lowest eigenvalue, the EKK method converges to more than fifteen decimal places after 4 iterations, while the KK method requires 9 iterations to reach 2.215680815...
* The energy derivative of F_m(E) at a true eigenvalue E_k is zero: dF_m(E)/dE|_{E=E_k} = 0.
* The energy derivative of F_m(E) at a pole energy epsilon_q is two: dF_m(E)/dE|_{E=epsilon_q} = 2.

## Context

This work extends the Krenciglowa-Kuo (KK) method (Krenciglowa and Kuo, 1974) by introducing the Z-box to resolve difficulties with poles in the Q-box and to allow reproduction of any d true eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian, not only those with largest P-space overlaps as in the standard KK method. The approach also relates to the Lee-Suzuki (LS) method (Lee and Suzuki, 1980), which requires higher derivatives of the Q-box for accurate solutions. The graphical method with the Z-box enables stable calculations and has been followed by Dong, Kuo, and Holt (2010) for shell-model effective interaction calculations.
