# The electromagnetic Sigma-to-Lambda transition form factors with coupled-channel effects in the space-like region

> Using dispersion theory, the electromagnetic Sigma-to-Lambda transition form factors are expressed as the product of the pion electromagnetic form factor and the $Σ\barΛ\toππ$ scattering amplitudes with the latter estimated from SU(3) chiral perturbation theory including the baryon decuplet as explicit degrees of freedom. The contribution of the $K\bar{K}$ channel is also taken into account and the $ππ$-$K\bar{K}$ coupled-channel effect is included by means of a two-channel Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation. It is found that the electric transition form factor shows a significant shift after the inclusion of the $K\bar{K}$ channel, while the magnetic transition form factor is only weakly affected. However, the $K\bar{K}$ effect on the electric form factor is obscured by the undetermined coupling $h_A$ in the three-flavor chiral Lagrangian. The error bands of the Sigma-to-Lambda transition form factors from the uncertainties of the couplings and low-energy constant in three-flavor chiral perturbation theory are estimated by a bootstrap sampling method.

## Key claim

Using dispersion theory with a two-channel Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation, the inclusion of the $K\bar{K}$ channel produces a significant shift in the electric Sigma-to-Lambda transition form factor but only weakly affects the magnetic transition form factor; however, the $K\bar{K}$ effect on the electric form factor is obscured by the undetermined coupling $h_A$ in the three-flavor chiral Lagrangian.

## Method

The electromagnetic Sigma-to-Lambda transition form factors were expressed via once-subtracted dispersion relations as the product of the pion electromagnetic form factor and the $\Sigma\bar{\Lambda}\to\pi\pi$ scattering amplitudes, with the latter estimated from SU(3) chiral perturbation theory including the baryon decuplet as explicit degrees of freedom. The $K\bar{K}$ channel contribution was added, and the $\pi\pi$-$K\bar{K}$ coupled-channel effect was included using a two-channel Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation. The reduced amplitudes $K_{\pi/K}$ and $P_{0,\pi/K}$ for the left-hand cut and polynomial parts were calculated up to next-to-leading order in three-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory, and the $P$-wave isovector $\pi\pi$-$K\bar{K}$ Omnès matrix was solved numerically using an iterative procedure with input phase shifts from Roy-type analyses and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous threshold for the electric transition form factor is located at $t_- = 0.935$ GeV in the unphysical time-like region.
- With the radius-adjusted parameters ($F_\Phi = 100$ MeV, $b_{10} = 1.06$ GeV$^{-1}$, $h_A = 2.22$) and cutoff $\Lambda = 1.5$ GeV, the $K\bar{K}$ channel produces a $52\%$ decrease in $G_E$ at $t = -1$ GeV$^2$, while only a $3\%$ decrease occurs for $G_M$.
- The normalizations are $F_1(0) = G_E(0) = 0$ and $F_2(0) = G_M(0) = \kappa \approx 1.98$, where $\kappa$ is estimated from the experimental width of the decay $\Sigma^0 \to \Lambda\gamma$.
- The couplings used are $D = 0.80$, $F = 0.46$, $F_\Phi = 100 \pm 10$ MeV, $h_A = 2.3 \pm 0.3$, and $b_{10} = (1.0 \pm 0.3)$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- The energy cutoff for the dispersion integrals was taken as $\Lambda = 1.5$ GeV and $2.0$ GeV to check sensitivity.

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-theoretical framework of Granados (2017) for the electromagnetic Sigma-to-Lambda transition form factors by including the $K\bar{K}$ inelasticity via a two-channel Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation, whereas the earlier work considered only the single $\pi\pi$ channel. The approach parallels the state-of-the-art dispersive analyses of nucleon electromagnetic form factors reviewed in Lin (2021) and applied in Lin (2021) to consistently describe all current measurements. The results enable precise predictions of the Sigma-to-Lambda transition form factors up to 1 GeV and provide a foundation for future inclusion of higher inelasticities such as the four-pion channel, which is expected to become important above 1.4 GeV.
