# $K^-$-Nucleus Scattering at Low and Intermediate Energies

> We calculate $K^-$-nucleus elastic differential, reaction and total cross sections for different nuclei ($^{12}$C,$^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb) at several laboratory antikaon momenta, ranging from 127 MeV to 800 MeV. We use different antikaon-nucleus optical potentials, some of them fitted to kaonic atom data, and study the sensitivity of the cross sections to the considered antikaon-nucleus dynamics.

## Key claim
The paper explores whether different antikaon-nucleus optical potentials, which all fit kaonic atom data equally well, can be distinguished by their predictions for low- and intermediate-energy scattering cross sections. The authors find that at 127 MeV laboratory momentum, the chirally-inspired self-consistent potential (THPH) produces clearly distinguishable elastic differential cross sections compared to other models, but caution that at 300 MeV the theoretical basis for the THPH model becomes unreliable.

## Method
The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation for K- scattering from 12C, 40Ca, and 208Pb using five different optical potentials: a chirally-inspired self-consistent potential (THPH), a modified version with an added phenomenological term (THPH), two potentials fitted to kaonic atom data (2DD and IAPH), and an impulse approximation (IA) potential. They compute elastic differential, reaction, and total cross sections at laboratory momenta of 127, 300, and 800 MeV, comparing results among models and against existing experimental data at 800 MeV.

## Key numbers
- At q_lab = 127 MeV for 12C: THPH gives elastic cross section 444 mb, reaction 501 mb, total 945 mb; 2DD gives 370, 415, 785 mb; IAPH gives 411, 568, 979 mb; IA gives 380, 420, 800 mb
- At q_lab = 127 MeV for 40Ca: THPH gives elastic 1190 mb, reaction 1201 mb, total 2391 mb; 2DD gives 1064, 1024, 2088 mb; IAPH gives 1029, 1313, 2342 mb; IA gives 1040, 1043, 2083 mb
- At q_lab = 127 MeV for 208Pb: THPH gives elastic 4345 mb, reaction 4219 mb, total 8564 mb; 2DD gives 4287, 3667, 7954 mb; IAPH gives 4094, 4363, 8457 mb; IA gives 4264, 3699, 7963 mb
- At q_lab = 127 MeV for 12C around theta = 60 degrees: 2DD, IA, and IAPH give similar elastic cross sections of about 33 mb/sr, while THPH gives about 52 mb/sr
- At q_lab = 800 MeV for 12C: IA model (including up to f-waves) gives total cross section 345 mb, compared to experimental value 338 +/- 8 mb
- At q_lab = 655 MeV for 12C: IA model gives 304 mb, compared to experimental value 306 +/- 8 mb
- The THPH model has a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 2.9 when fitting 63 kaonic atom shifts and widths

## Context
This work extends the chirally-inspired antikaon-nucleus optical potential developed by Ramos and Oset (2000) and its p-wave extension by the same group, which had previously been tested only against kaonic atom data. The paper contrasts these potentials with empirical models from Friedman and Gal (1993, 1999) that also fit kaonic atom data. The results suggest that low-energy scattering experiments at facilities like DAΦNE, KEK, or the future Japanese Hadron Collider could distinguish between different theoretical approaches to the K- nucleus interaction, particularly at the 127 MeV momentum characteristic of phi-meson decay.
