# Exploring sensitivity of charge-exchange ($p, n$) reactions to the neutron density distribution

> $Background:$ The determination of the nuclear neutron properties suffers from uncontrolled uncertainties, which attracted considerable attention recently, such as in the context of the PREX experiment. $Purpose:$ Our aim is to analyze the sensitivity of charge-exchange ($p, n$) reactions to the neutron density distribution $ρ_{n}$ and constrain the neutron characteristics in the nuclear structure models. $Method:$ By combing the folding and the mean-field models, the nucleon-nucleus ($NA$) potential can be obtained from the nuclear density distribution. Further, the ($p, p$) and ($p, n$) cross sections for $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb are calculated following the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) method. $Results:$ Compared with the ($p, p$) cross section, the effects of $ρ_{n}$ variation on the ($p, n$) cross section are significant, which is due to the impact of isovector properties. Based on the global folding model analyses of data, it is found that $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb have relatively large neutron skin thickness $ΔR_{n p}$. $Conclusions:$ Results illustrate that the charge-exchange ($p, n$) reaction is a sensitive probe of $ρ_{n}$. The results in this paper can offer useful guides for future experiments of neutron characteristics.

## Key claim

The charge-exchange (p, n) reaction is a sensitive probe of the neutron density distribution because its cross section is directly driven by the isovector potential U1, whereas elastic (p, p) scattering is dominated by the isoscalar potential and shows little sensitivity to variations in neutron skin thickness. The authors hedge that this sensitivity is demonstrated through folding-model calculations that must first be calibrated against the PREX-II central value of the neutron skin thickness for 208Pb.

## Method

The authors combined Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) and relativistic mean-field (RMF) models to generate proton and neutron density distributions for 48Ca and 208Pb. They then used the complex folding model with the CDM3Y6 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to construct the isoscalar and isovector parts of the nucleon-nucleus optical potential, and calculated (p, p) and (p, n) cross sections at 35 MeV and 45 MeV within the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA). The renormalization coefficients of the folded potential were calibrated against experimental (p, p) and (p, n) cross sections for 208Pb assuming the central value of the PREX-II neutron skin thickness, and then applied to 48Ca.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the PREX-II neutron skin thickness is ΔRnp = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm; the NL3* parameter set gives ΔRnp = 0.284 fm, matching the central value.
- For 208Pb, the NL1 parameter set gives ΔRnp = 0.313 fm (upper limit of PREX-II), SkO gives 0.218 fm (lower limit), and SLy4 gives 0.160 fm (below PREX-II range).
- For 48Ca, the calculated neutron skin thicknesses range from 0.153 fm (SLy4) to 0.271 fm (NL1).
- The renormalization coefficients for the complex folded potential at 35 MeV are NV = 0.849, NW = 0.591, NV1(48Ca) = 0.992, and NV1(208Pb) = 1.749; at 45 MeV they are NV = 0.840, NW = 0.619, NV1(48Ca) = 1.136, and NV1(208Pb) = 1.452.
- The binding energies per nucleon and charge radii for 208Pb agree with experimental data at the level of 0.5%.

## Context

This work extends the use of charge-exchange (p, n) reactions as a probe of neutron density distributions, building on prior folding-model studies by Khoa (2007) and the DWBA framework of Danielewicz (2017). It directly addresses the uncertainty in neutron skin thickness highlighted by the PREX experiment (Adhikari 2021) and provides guidance for the upcoming CREX experiment on 48Ca (Horowitz 2014). The results enable future experiments to use quasielastic (p, n) reactions to constrain neutron properties in nuclear structure models, complementing parity-violating electron scattering measurements.
