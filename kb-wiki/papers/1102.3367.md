# Does nuclear matter bind at large $N_c$?

> The existence of nuclear matter at large $N_{c}$ is investigated in the framework of effective hadronic models of the Walecka type. This issue is strongly related to the nucleon-nucleon attraction in the scalar channel, and thus to the nature of the light scalar mesons. Different scenarios for the light scalar sector correspond to different large $N_{c}$ scaling properties of the parameters of the hadronic models. In all realistic phenomenological scenarios for the light scalar field(s) responsible for the attraction in the scalar channel it is found that nuclear matter does not bind in the large $N_{c}$ world. We thus conclude that $N_{c}=3$ is in this respect special: 3 is fortunately not large at all and allows for nuclear matter, while large $N_{c}$ would not.

## Key claim
The paper argues that nuclear matter does not bind in the large \(N_c\) limit, based on effective hadronic models of the Walecka type, but only under the condition that the light scalar meson responsible for nucleon-nucleon attraction is not a pure quark-antiquark state. The abstract's conclusion that "\(N_c=3\) is special" is hedged by the finding that if the scalar meson were a naive quarkonium state, nuclear matter would bind at all \(N_c\); the negative result depends on adopting realistic phenomenological scenarios for the scalar sector.

## Method
The authors use the Walecka model, a mean-field effective Lagrangian with a scalar field (attraction) and a vector field (repulsion), to describe cold, isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. They fix the couplings at \(N_c=3\) to reproduce saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and binding energy per nucleon \(E/A = -16\ \text{MeV}\), then rescale the masses and couplings with powers of \(N_c\) according to the large-\(N_c\) scaling rules appropriate for each assumed nature of the scalar meson (quark-antiquark, tetraquark, two-scalar, two-pion-exchange effective field, glueball, or dynamically generated resonance). They solve the mean-field equations for each \(N_c\) and check whether a saturation minimum exists in the energy per nucleon versus density curve.

## Key numbers
- At \(N_c=3\), the Walecka model parameters are fixed to give saturation at \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and \(E/A = -16\ \text{MeV}\).
- In the naive quark-antiquark scenario, the saturation density asymptotes to \(\sim 2.3\,\rho_0\) as \(N_c \to \infty\).
- In the tetraquark scenario, nuclear matter becomes unbound already at \(N_c = 4\).
- In the two-scalar scenario (tetraquark plus quarkonium), the light scalar mass is \(m_\chi = 470\ \text{MeV}\) and the heavy scalar mass is \(m_\sigma = 1225\ \text{MeV}\); nuclear matter again does not bind for \(N_c > 3\).
- For the two-pion-exchange effective scalar scenario, stable nuclear matter at \(N_c=4\) would require an axial coupling \(g_A^{\exp} \gtrsim 5\), whereas the experimental value is \(g_A^{\exp} = 1.267 \pm 0.004\).
- The glueball scenario uses a mass \(m_G \sim 1.5\ \text{GeV}\) (from lattice QCD) and yields no bound nuclear matter at large \(N_c\).

## Context
This work extends the large-\(N_c\) analysis of nuclear matter beyond the naive quarkonium picture, which had been assumed in earlier Walecka-model studies. It directly contradicts the expectation that nuclear matter would bind at all \(N_c\) if the scalar meson were a simple \(\bar{q}q\) state (as in the linear sigma model of Lee (1972) or the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model). The paper builds on the tetraquark interpretation of Jaffe (1977) and on the two-scalar fit to nucleon-nucleon scattering by the authors of Ref. [ml] (2009). It also engages with the large-\(N_c\) scaling of two-pion-exchange potentials studied by Cohen (1995). The result enables future work beyond mean-field approximations and suggests that the smallness of the nuclear binding energy relative to \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}\) is naturally explained only if \(N_c\) is close to 3.
