# An extension of the generator coordinate method with basis optimization

> The generator coordinate method (GCM) has been a well-known method to describe nuclear collective motions. In this method, one specifies {\it a priori} the relevant collective degrees of freedom as input of the method, based on empirical and/or phenomenological assumptions. We here propose a new extension of the GCM, in which both the basis Slater determinants and weight factors are optimized according to the variational principle. Applying this method to $^{16}$O and $^{28}$Si nuclei with the Skyrme functional, we demonstrate that the optimized bases correspond to excited states along a collective path, unlike the conventional GCM which superposes only the local ground states. This implies that a collective coordinate for large amplitude collective motions is determined in a much more complex way than what has been assumed so far.

# Extension of the generator coordinate method with basis optimization

## Key claim
The authors propose a new extension of the generator coordinate method (GCM) in which both the basis Slater determinants and the weight factors are optimized variationally, and demonstrate for ¹⁶O and ²⁸Si that the optimized bases correspond to excited states along a collective path rather than the local ground states used in conventional GCM. This implies that a collective coordinate for large amplitude collective motions is determined in a much more complex way than previously assumed, and that the quadrupole moment Q₂ alone may not constitute an appropriate collective coordinate.

## Method
The trial wave function is a superposition of Slater determinants as in standard GCM, but both the weight coefficients fₐ and the single-particle orbitals φᵢ⁽ᵃ⁾ within each determinant are optimized by minimizing the total energy using the conjugate gradient method. The method (called Optimized-basis GCM, OptGCM) is applied to ¹⁶O and ²⁸Si using the Skyrme functional with the SIII parameter set, with single-particle states expanded on an axial harmonic-oscillator basis and the weight factors restricted to real numbers.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁶O: HF ground state energy = −142.024 MeV; GCM energy = −142.407 MeV; OptGCM energy = −143.109 MeV (with 8 Slater determinants and 14 harmonic-oscillator major shells)
- The OptGCM lowers the ¹⁶O ground state energy by 0.7 MeV relative to the conventional GCM using Q₂-constrained Hartree-Fock bases
- For ²⁸Si: HF energy = −272.63 MeV; GCM energy = −273.95 MeV; OptGCM energy = −275.00 MeV, with an energy difference of 1.05 MeV between GCM and OptGCM
- In ¹⁶O, the GCM quadrupole moment Q₂ = 0.179 fm² and hexadecupole moment Q₄ = 4.662 fm⁴; OptGCM gives Q₂ = 0.165 fm² and Q₄ = 0.563 fm⁴
- The eigenvalues of the norm kernel for OptGCM bases are all larger than approximately 10⁻¹, whereas for constrained-HF bases they range from ∼1 to 10⁻¹⁰
- The beyond-mean-field correlations lower the ¹⁶O ground state energies by about 1 MeV compared to the HF approximation

## Context
This work extends the generator coordinate method by removing the need to pre-specify collective coordinates, a limitation noted in prior GCM applications (Ring and Schuck, 1980; Reinhard, 1987). The approach is similar in spirit to the Monte Carlo shell model (Shimizu, 2012) and to multi-configuration self-consistent field methods in quantum chemistry (Schmidt, 1998), but is formulated within multi-reference density functional theory. The results challenge the conventional GCM assumption that superposing only local ground states along a pre-set collective coordinate is sufficient, and suggest that future work should compare OptGCM with the self-consistent collective coordinate method (Marumori, 1980; Matsuo, 1986; Hinohara, 2008) and the dynamical GCM with collective momenta (Hizawa, 2021; Hizawa, 2022).
