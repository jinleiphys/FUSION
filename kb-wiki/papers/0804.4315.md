# Dispelling the curse of the neutron skin in atomic parity violation

> We perform calculations for the neutron skin of nuclei and its contribution to atomic parity non-conservation (PNC) in many isotopes of Cs, Ba, Sm, Dy, Yb, Tl, Pb, Bi, Fr, Ra. Three problems are addressed: i) Neutron-skin induced errors to single-isotope PNC, ii) Possibility to measure neutron skin using atomic PNC, iii) Neutron-skin induced errors for ratios of PNC effects in different isotopes. In the latter case the correlations in the neutron skin values for different isotopes lead to cancelations of the errors; this makes the isotopic ratio method a competitive tool in a search for new physics beyond the standard model.

## Key claim

The authors show that neutron-skin-induced uncertainties in atomic parity non-conservation (PNC) ratios for different isotopes of the same element are substantially reduced because the neutron skin values for different isotopes are correlated, making the isotopic ratio method a competitive tool for searching for new physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear-structure calculations using Skyrme energy-density functional (EDF) models constrained by antiprotonic-atom data, specifically the Skxs20 interaction with nuclear matter incompressibility K ≈ 200-230 MeV. They computed neutron skin values ΔR_np and proton rms radii R_p for isotopes of Cs, Ba, Sm, Dy, Yb, Tl, Pb, Bi, Fr, and Ra, then used these to evaluate the relative corrections to atomic PNC amplitudes and the sensitivity of isotopic ratios to new physics couplings.

## Key numbers

- For 133Cs, the relative correction to the PNC amplitude due to neutron skin is -0.0023(5)
- For 213Fr, the relative correction is -0.0063(16)
- The neutron skin for 208Pb is ΔR_np = 0.20(±0.04)(±0.05) fm, where ±0.04 fm is experimental error from antiprotonic line width and ±0.05 fm is theoretical error
- For 132Sn, the neutron skin is ΔR_np = 0.24(4) fm from pygmy dipole resonance data, consistent with 0.27(5) fm from Skxs20 interactions
- The nuclear-structure uncertainty δF for new physics constraints ranges from 2.1×10^-3 for Cs (Z=55, masses 129-137) to 10.2×10^-3 for Yb (Z=70, masses 168-176)
- For Fr (Z=87, masses 209-221), δF = 8.8×10^-3; for Ra (Z=88, masses 210-222), δF = 8.9×10^-3
- The correlated treatment of neutron-skin errors reduces error bars by four to ten times compared to treating errors independently

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Derevianko (2002), which used empirical antiprotonic-atom data fits from Trzcinska et al. (2001) to reduce neutron-skin uncertainties in single-isotope PNC for Cs. The authors address the conceptual limitation pointed out by Fortson, Pang, and Wilets (1990) that neutron-skin uncertainties could mask new physics in isotopic ratio measurements. The results enable competitive constraints on new physics couplings to protons from atomic PNC isotopic ratios, comparable to bounds from parity-violating electron scattering experiments analyzed by Young et al. (2007).
