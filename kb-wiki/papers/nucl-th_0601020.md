# Analysis of full-QCD and quenched-QCD lattice propagators

> Recent lattice-QCD results for the dressed-gluon propagator are used within the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation to determine the gluon-quark vertex dressing necessary to reproduce the lattice-QCD results for the dressed-quark propagator. Both quenched and full QCD lattice simulations, for a range of low quark current masses, are analyzed. The chiral extrapolation is made through this continuum DSE form. Resulting chiral and physical pion observables are investigated.

# Analysis of full-QCD and quenched-QCD lattice propagators

## Key claim

The dressed quark-gluon vertex inferred from lattice-QCD propagators, when used in a continuum Dyson-Schwinger equation kernel, reproduces the lattice-QCD dressed-quark propagator for both quenched and full QCD; however, the resulting pion decay constant remains about 15% too low compared to experiment, indicating that the simple one-amplitude vertex ansatz is insufficient.

## Method

The authors take lattice-QCD results for the dressed-gluon propagator and insert them into the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation, replacing the full gluon-quark vertex with a phenomenological single-amplitude vertex dressing \(\Gamma_1(k^2)\). They then adjust the parameters of this vertex so that the DSE solution for the dressed-quark propagator \(S(p)\) fits the lattice-QCD quark propagator data for four different current quark masses, separately for quenched and full QCD in Landau gauge with Euclidean metric.

## Key numbers

- Full-QCD chiral condensate: \(\langle \bar{q} q \rangle_{\mu=1\ \mathrm{GeV}} = -(0.23\ \mathrm{GeV})^3\), compared to the experimental value \(-(0.24\ \mathrm{GeV})^3\)
- Quenched-QCD chiral condensate: \(\langle \bar{q} q \rangle_{\mu=1\ \mathrm{GeV}} = -(0.19\ \mathrm{GeV})^3\)
- Full-QCD chiral pion decay constant: \(f_\pi^0 = 0.072\ \mathrm{GeV}\), compared to the experimental value \(0.090\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Full-QCD physical pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 0.075\ \mathrm{GeV}\), compared to the experimental value \(0.092\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Quenched-QCD chiral pion decay constant: \(f_\pi^0 = 0.063\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Quenched-QCD physical pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 0.066\ \mathrm{GeV}\)
- Full-QCD gluon propagator fit parameters (model A, \(N_f=3\)): \(A = 3.25\), \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{g}} = 0.54\), \(\alpha = 1.15\), \(Z_{\mathrm{g}} = 1.22\)
- Full-QCD vertex dressing parameters: \(a_1 = 4.5\), \(a_2 = 2.1\), \(a_3 = 18.1\), \(b = 0.31\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of quenched-QCD lattice propagators (Bhagwat and Tandy, 2003) to full QCD using lattice data for gluon propagators from Bowman et al. (2004) and quark propagators from Bowman et al. (2005). The resulting DSE kernel enables a chiral symmetry-preserving Bethe-Salpeter kernel (following Bender et al., 1996) for calculating meson observables. The full-QCD results improve the chiral condensate compared to the quenched approximation, which underestimates it by a factor of two, but the pion decay constant remains about 15% below experiment, motivating future work with more complete vertex structures.
