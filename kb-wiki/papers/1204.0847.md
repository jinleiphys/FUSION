# Transition from subbarrier to deep subbarrier regimes in heavy-ion fusion reactions

> We analyze the recent experimental data of heavy-ion fusion cross sections available up to deep subbarrier energies in order to discuss the threshold incident energy for a deep subbarrier fusion hindrance phenomenon. To this end, we employ a one-dimensional potential model with a Woods-Saxon internuclear potential. Fitting the experimental data in two different energy regions with different Woods-Saxon potentials, we define the threshold energy as an intersect of the two fusion excitation functions. We show that the threshold energies so extracted are in good agreement with the empirical systematics as well as with the values of the Krappe-Nix-Sierk (KNS) potential at the touching point. We also discuss the asymptotic energy shift of fusion cross sections with respect to the potential model calculations, and show that it decreases with decreasing energies in the deep subbarrier region although it takes a constant value at subbarrier energies.

## Key claim

The threshold energy for deep subbarrier fusion hindrance can be defined as the intersection of two fusion excitation functions fitted separately to subbarrier and deep subbarrier data using a one-dimensional Woods-Saxon potential model, and this definition yields values in good agreement with both the empirical systematics based on the astrophysical S-factor maximum and the Krappe-Nix-Sierk (KNS) potential at the touching point.

## Method

The authors fitted experimental fusion cross sections for seven heavy-ion systems in two distinct energy regions using a one-dimensional potential model with a Woods-Saxon internuclear potential, treating the depth, radius, and surface diffuseness as adjustable parameters. The subbarrier region was defined as cross sections between 10^{-2} mb and 10^0 mb, and the deep subbarrier region as cross sections below 10^{-3} mb. The threshold energy for deep subbarrier hindrance was then taken as the energy at which the two fitted excitation functions intersect. The asymptotic energy shift, defined as the extra energy needed to match experimental cross sections relative to a single-channel potential model calculation, was also extracted as a function of fusion cross section.

## Key numbers

* For the $^{28}$Si+$^{64}$Ni system ($\zeta = 1730.05$), the extracted threshold energy $E_s = 46.2$ MeV, compared to the empirical value $E_s^{\rm (emp)} = 51.3$ MeV and the KNS potential at touching $V_{\rm KNS} = 43.9$ MeV.
* For the $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb system ($\zeta = 2528.55$), $E_s = 68.8$ MeV, with $E_s^{\rm (emp)} = 66.1$ MeV and $V_{\rm KNS} = 70.5$ MeV.
* For the $^{64}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni system ($\zeta = 4434.97$), $E_s = 88.92$ MeV, with $E_s^{\rm (emp)} = 96.1$ MeV and $V_{\rm KNS} = 89.0$ MeV.
* For the $^{60}$Ni+$^{89}$Y system ($\zeta = 6537.33$), $E_s = 124.5$ MeV, with $E_s^{\rm (emp)} = 124.5$ MeV and $V_{\rm KNS} = 125.4$ MeV.
* For the $^{90}$Zr+$^{89}$Y system ($\zeta = 10435.5$), $E_s = 171.8$ MeV, with $E_s^{\rm (emp)} = 170.3$ MeV and $V_{\rm KNS} = 175.2$ MeV.
* For the $^{90}$Zr+$^{90}$Zr system ($\zeta = 10733.1$), $E_s = 176.1$ MeV, with $E_s^{\rm (emp)} = 173.2$ MeV and $V_{\rm KNS} = 179.9$ MeV.
* For the $^{90}$Zr+$^{92}$Zr system ($\zeta = 10791.9$), $E_s = 171.7$ MeV, with $E_s^{\rm (emp)} = 173.9$ MeV and $V_{\rm KNS} = 179.1$ MeV.
* The surface diffuseness parameter $a_>$ (subbarrier fit) ranged from 0.53 fm to 0.87 fm, while $a_<$ (deep subbarrier fit) ranged from 0.76 fm to 0.99 fm across the systems studied.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of deep subbarrier fusion hindrance previously studied using the astrophysical S-factor maximum by Jiang et al. (2006) and others, providing a physically more transparent definition of the threshold energy. The results confirm the earlier empirical systematics of Esbensen and Jiang (2009) and support the conclusion that dynamics after the colliding nuclei touch, as described by the KNS potential, is responsible for the hindrance phenomenon. The study enables future theoretical work to understand why and how post-touching dynamics leads to both the change in exponential slope of fusion cross sections and the maximum in the astrophysical S-factor.
