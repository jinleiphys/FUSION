# Investigation of the one-neutron transfer in $^{13}$C + $^{28}$Si at E$_{lab}$ = 30 and 34 MeV

> Background: Neutron transfer measurements for the $^{18}$O + $^{28}$Si system have shown that the experimental one-neutron and two-neutron transfer cross sections are well reproduced with spectroscopic amplitudes from two different shell model interactions for the Si isotopes: \textit{psdmod} for the two-neutron transfer, and \textit{psdmwkpn} for the one-neutron transfer. Purpose: The origin of this ambiguity can be related to a more complex mechanism in the one-neutron transfer that requires the unpairing of neutrons prior to its transfer in the ($^{18}$O,$^{17}$O) reaction. Studying a nucleus where this characteristic is absent ($^{13}$C) should help to elucidate this question. Method: One-neutron transfer cross sections were measured for the $^{13}$C + $^{28}$Si at E$_{lab}$ = 30, and 34 MeV, and compared with coupled reaction channel calculations using spectroscopic amplitudes derived from the \textit{psdmod} and \textit{psdmwkpn} shell model interactions. Results: The spectroscopic amplitudes from the \textit{psdmod} interaction for the relevant states in $^{29}$Si provide a good description of the experimental data and the corresponding values agree with previous estimates obtained from the (d,p) reaction. Conclusions: The experimental data for the one-neutron transfer to $^{28}$Si induced by ($^{13}$C,$^{12}$C) reaction is well reproduced using spectroscopic amplitudes from the \textit{psdmod}.

## Key claim

The one-neutron transfer cross sections for the $^{13}$C + $^{28}$Si system are well reproduced using spectroscopic amplitudes from the *psdmod* shell model interaction, in contrast to the $^{18}$O + $^{28}$Si system where the one-neutron transfer required the *psdmwkpn* interaction; this difference suggests that the earlier need for *psdmwkpn* was not due to the nuclear structure of $^{29}$Si but rather to the more complex transfer mechanism involving the unpairing of the dineutron in $^{18}$O.

## Method

One-neutron transfer cross sections were measured for the $^{13}$C + $^{28}$Si system at laboratory energies of 30 and 34 MeV using the SATURN detector array at the University of São Paulo tandem accelerator. The experimental angular distributions were compared with coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations using the FRESCO code, with spectroscopic amplitudes for $^{29}$Si derived from both the *psdmod* and *psdmwkpn* shell model interactions; the São Paulo double folding potential was used for the optical potential, with the imaginary strength factor adjusted to reproduce elastic and inelastic scattering data.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron transfer Q-value for $^{13}$C + $^{28}$Si ground state to ground state: +3.53 MeV.
- Coulomb barrier height for $^{13}$C + $^{28}$Si: V$_B$ = 18.9 MeV (laboratory frame).
- Spectroscopic factor ($\mathcal{S}^2$) for $^{28}$Si$_{g.s.}$ to $^{29}$Si$_{g.s.}$ from *psdmod*: 0.51; from *psdmwkpn*: 0.32; from (d,p) reaction (Mermaz 1971): 0.53; from (d,p) reaction (Peterson 1983): 0.37; from (d,p) with systematic uncertainty (Timofeyuk 2005): 0.42 $\pm$ 0.13.
- Deformation parameter for $^{28}$Si: $\beta_2 = 0.407$; for $^{13}$C: $\beta_1 = 0.143$.
- Neutron separation energies: S$_n$ = 4.95 MeV for $^{13}$C; S$_n$ = 8.45 MeV for $^{29}$Si.
- Imaginary strength factor N$_i$ = 0.1 in the entrance partition provided best fit to elastic and inelastic data; in the exit partition N$_i$ = 0.78.

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron transfer reactions on $^{28}$Si, previously investigated with $^{18}$O projectiles by Lépine-Szily (2018) for one-neutron transfer and by Chamon (2018) for two-neutron transfer. In those studies, the one-neutron transfer required the *psdmwkpn* interaction while the two-neutron transfer used *psdmod*, creating an ambiguity. By using $^{13}$C, which has a single valence neutron rather than a paired dineutron, the present work shows that *psdmod* describes the one-neutron transfer data well, resolving the ambiguity in favor of *psdmod* for the $^{29}$Si structure. This enables future studies to focus on the pairing dynamics in the transfer mechanism for projectiles like $^{18}$O, rather than attributing discrepancies to nuclear structure uncertainties.
