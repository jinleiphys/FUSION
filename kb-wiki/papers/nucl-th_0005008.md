# First order optical potentials and 25 to 40 MeV proton elastic scattering

> The differential cross sections and analyzing powers from the elastic scattering of 25 and 40 MeV protons from many nuclei have been studied. Analyses have been made using a fully microscopic model of proton-nucleus scattering seeking to establish a means appropriate for use in analyses of radioactive beam scattering from hydrogen with ion energies 25A and 40A MeV.

## Key claim

First-order microscopic optical potentials formed by folding effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions with shell-model ground state densities can produce reasonable agreement with proton elastic scattering data at 25 and 40 MeV, though the agreement is noticeably worse than at higher energies (65 and 200 MeV) and the predicted cross-section minima are too sharp, suggesting that additional medium modifications to the effective NN interaction may be needed.

## Method

The authors constructed coordinate-space optical potentials by folding complex, energy- and density-dependent effective NN interactions (derived from Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone g-matrices of the Paris and Bonn-B potentials) with one-body density matrices (OBDME) from shell-model descriptions of target nuclei ranging from A=6 to 208. The resulting non-local optical potentials were used without approximation in DWBA98 calculations to predict differential cross sections and analyzing powers for proton elastic scattering at 25, 30, and 40 MeV, with no a posteriori adjustments to the input interactions or structure models.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections and analyzing powers calculated for 40 MeV proton scattering on targets from ⁶He to ²⁰⁸Pb, with data from Blumberg (1966) and radioactive beam experiments
- For 25 MeV scattering, calculations compared with data at 25.9 MeV (⁶Li), 24.0 MeV (¹²C), 25.0 MeV (²⁸Si and ⁴⁰Ca), 24.5 MeV (¹⁵²Sm), and 30.3 MeV (²⁰⁸Pb)
- Analyzing power data used at 24.1 MeV (¹²C), 25.0 MeV (²⁸Si), 29.0 MeV (⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb), and 24.5 MeV (¹⁵²Sm)
- Virtual excitation of giant resonances estimated to contribute at most 1 mb/sr to elastic cross sections at large momentum transfers
- For ⁶He, calculations used both 4ħω and 6ħω shell model spaces, with results indistinguishable between the two models

## Context

This work extends the g-folding approach previously applied successfully at 65 and 200 MeV by Karataglidis, Dortmans, and Amos (1995-1997) to the lower energy range of 25-40 MeV, where collective effects from giant resonance excitations (von Geramb et al., 1975) may become important. The results establish that first-order microscopic optical potentials are a reasonable starting point for analyzing radioactive beam scattering from hydrogen targets at 25A and 40A MeV, and provide the distorted waves and transition operators needed for distorted wave approximation analyses of inelastic excitations such as the 2⁺ (1.8 MeV) state in ⁶He.
