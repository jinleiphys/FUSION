# Rho-Nucleon Tensor Coupling and Charge-Exchange Resonances

> The Gamow-Teller resonances are discussed in the context of a self-consistent RPA, based on the relativistic mean field theory. We inquire on the possibility of substituting the phenomenological Landau-Migdal force by a microscopic nucleon-nucleon interaction generated from the rho-nucleon tensor coupling. The effect of this coupling turns out to be very small when the short range correlations are not taken into account, but too large when these correlations are simulated by the simple extraction of the contact terms from the resulting nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

The tensor coupling of the rho meson to the nucleon cannot substitute for the phenomenological Landau-Migdal force in reproducing the Gamow-Teller resonance energy in a relativistic RPA calculation: its effect is very small when short-range correlations are omitted, but produces too much repulsion when correlations are simulated by simply extracting contact terms from the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent relativistic random-phase-approximation (RRPA) calculations for charge-exchange resonances in 208Pb, using a Lagrangian that includes sigma, omega, rho, and pion mesons plus a rho-nucleon tensor coupling term. The mean field was computed in the relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) with the NL1 and TM1 parameter sets, and the residual interaction was derived from the same Lagrangian in the instantaneous approximation, including only direct matrix elements. The Gamow-Teller strength distribution was computed in a discretized spectral RRPA with a model space of 0 and 2 hbar-omega excitations, omitting negative-energy states.

## Key numbers

- For the NL1 parameter set with K_rho=3.7 and g'=0.7, the Gamow-Teller resonance is at 19.5 MeV and the isobaric analogue resonance at 18.6 MeV; experimental values are 19.2 MeV and 18.8 MeV respectively.
- For the TM1 parameter set with the same conditions, the Gamow-Teller resonance is at 20.3 MeV and the isobaric analogue resonance at 18.7 MeV.
- With K_rho=3.7, the spin-orbit splitting is modified by less than 150 keV compared to the case without tensor coupling.
- The effective Landau-Migdal parameter g' derived from the contact-term extraction procedure is g'_pi+rho = 1.6 in the relativistic treatment, compared to the phenomenological value g' = 0.7.
- In the nonrelativistic approximation the same procedure yields g'_pi+rho = 2.3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RRPA calculation of Gamow-Teller and isobaric analogue resonances in 48Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb (De Conti et al., 1998), which required a phenomenological Landau-Migdal force with g'=0.7 to reproduce experimental energies. The paper tests whether the rho-nucleon tensor coupling, motivated by effective field theory arguments (Furnstahl et al., 1995) and known to contribute to the spin-isospin component of the nucleon-nucleon interaction (Machleidt, 1989), can replace this phenomenological force. The results show that a simple contact-term extraction is inadequate for heavy mesons, and the authors conclude that realistic short-range correlations and finite-nucleon-size effects must be implemented in the relativistic RPA framework.
