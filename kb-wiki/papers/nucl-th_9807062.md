# Size of Fireballs Created in High Energy Lead-Lead Collisions as Inferred from Coulomb Distortions of Pion Spectra

> We compute the Coulomb effects produced by an expanding, highly charged fireball on the momentum distribution of pions. We compare our results to data on Au+Au at 11.6 A GeV from E866 at the BNL AGS and to data on Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV from NA44 at the CERN SPS. We conclude that the distortion of the spectra at low transverse momentum and mid-rapidity can be explained in both experiments by the effect of the large amount of participating charge in the central rapidity region. By adjusting the fireball expansion velocity to match the average transverse momentum of protons, we find a best fit when the fireball radius is about 10 fm, as determined by the moment when the pions undergo their last scattering. This value is common to both the AGS and CERN experiments.

## Key claim

The suppression of the $\pi^+/\pi^-$ ratio at low transverse momentum in central heavy-ion collisions can be quantitatively explained as a Coulomb distortion effect from the expanding fireball, but the extracted fireball radius depends on the assumed surface expansion velocity; the authors break this degeneracy by matching the model's average proton transverse momentum to data, yielding a common freezeout radius of about 10 fm for both AGS and SPS energies.

## Method

The authors model the fireball as a uniformly charged sphere whose radius expands linearly with time at constant surface speed $v_s$, producing a time-dependent electric field. They solve the relativistic Vlasov equation by the method of characteristics, computing classical trajectories for $3\times 10^6$ initial phase-space points per parameter set using a 6th-order Runge-Kutta method. The initial pion distribution is taken as a relativistic Boltzmann distribution with effective temperature 110 MeV (SPS) or 111 MeV (AGS), and the final momentum distributions are obtained by evolving these trajectories forward in time from an initial radius $R_0$ at time $t_0$.

## Key numbers

- Fireball radius at pion freezeout: about 10 fm, common to both AGS (Au+Au at 11.6 A GeV) and SPS (Pb+Pb at 158 A GeV) experiments
- Surface expansion velocity $v_s = 0.916$ (AGS) and $v_s = 0.916$ (SPS), determined by matching average proton transverse momentum of 820 MeV (AGS) and 825 MeV (SPS)
- Effective fireball charge: $Z = 116$ for Au+Au at AGS; $Z = 120$ for Pb+Pb at SPS
- Effective temperature of initial pion distribution: 110 MeV (SPS) and 111 MeV (AGS)
- Chi-squared minimum for AGS data: 0.53 per degree of freedom at $R_0 = 10$ fm
- For SPS data, chi-squared minima at $R_0 = 13$ fm (for $v_s = 0.8$) and $R_0 = 8.3$ fm (for $v_s = 1$), with no statistically significant difference between the two fits
- Average density in fireball reference frame: 0.60/fm$^3$ (4 times nuclear density); local density after relativistic correction: 0.42/fm$^3$

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic Coulomb distortion analysis of Ayala and Kapusta (1998) for kaons to relativistic pions, and applies it to data from the E866 collaboration at the BNL AGS and the NA44 collaboration at the CERN SPS. The results are consistent with pion interferometry measurements by Barrette (AGS) and Wiedemann (SPS) showing transverse and longitudinal radii about twice the cold nuclear radius. The analysis enables extraction of the fireball size at the moment pions undergo their last scattering, and suggests that at freezeout the hadronic density remains interestingly high (0.42/fm$^3$ locally), motivating more accurate data and inclusion of Coulomb forces in event simulators.
