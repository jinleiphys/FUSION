# Dependence of two-nucleon momentum densities on total pair momentum

> Two-nucleon momentum distributions are calculated for the ground states of 3He and 4He as a function of the nucleons' relative and total momenta. We use variational Monte Carlo wave functions derived from a realistic Hamiltonian with two- and three-nucleon potentials. The momentum distribution of pp pairs is found to be much smaller than that of pn pairs for values of the relative momentum in the range (300--500) MeV/c and vanishing total momentum. However, as the total momentum increases to 400 MeV/c, the ratio of pp to pn pairs in this relative momentum range grows and approaches the limit 1/2 for 3He and 1/4 for 4He, corresponding to the ratio of pp to pn pairs in these nuclei. This behavior should be easily observable in two-nucleon knock-out processes, such as A(e,e'pN).

## Key claim

The ratio of pp to pn pair momentum distributions in 3He and 4He, which is very small at zero total pair momentum due to tensor-force-induced nodes in the pp distribution, grows rapidly and approaches the nuclear ratio of 1/2 for 3He and 1/4 for 4He as the total pair momentum increases to 400 MeV/c, because higher orbital angular momentum states become accessible.

## Method

The authors used variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wave functions derived from the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions (AV18/UIX) to compute two-nucleon momentum densities for the ground states of 3He and 4He. The double Fourier transform of the density was computed by Monte Carlo integration, using a Metropolis walk guided by the squared wave function and symmetric displacement of both the relative and center-of-mass coordinates on Gauss-Legendre grids of up to 96 and 80 points, respectively.

## Key numbers

- For 3He at total momentum Q=0, integrating relative momentum q over 1.5–2.5 fm⁻¹ gives a ratio of pp to pn pairs R_pp/pn = 0.014 ± 0.004, compared to 1/2 for the whole nucleus integrated over all q and Q.
- For 4He at Q=0, the same integration gives R_pp/pn = 0.023 ± 0.006, compared to 1/4 for the whole nucleus.
- As total momentum Q increases to 400 MeV/c (approximately 2 fm⁻¹), the ratio R_pp/pn approaches 1/2 for 3He and 1/4 for 4He.
- The relative momentum range studied is 300–500 MeV/c (1.5–2.5 fm⁻¹).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Schiavilla 2007) of relative momentum distributions at vanishing total momentum, which predicted large differences between pp and pn pairs due to tensor forces. The present calculations are motivated by preliminary CLAS data on 3He (Weinstein 2008) and are directly relevant to two-nucleon knockout experiments such as A(e,e'pN), including a recent JLab measurement on 12C (Subedi 2008) that found a pn to pp cross-section ratio of approximately 10. The results enable quantitative predictions for future knockout experiments on light nuclei and help disentangle tensor-correlation effects from final-state interactions.
