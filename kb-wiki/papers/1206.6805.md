# Event-by-event gluon multiplicity, energy density and eccentricities at RHIC and LHC

> The event-by-event multiplicity distribution, the energy densities and energy density weighted eccentricity moments epsilon_n (up to n=6) at early times in heavy-ion collisions at both RHIC (root-s=200 GeV) and LHC (root-s=2.76 TeV) are computed in the IP-Glasma model. This framework combines the impact parameter dependent saturation model (IP-Sat) for nucleon parton distributions (constrained by HERA deeply inelastic scattering data) with an event-by-event classical Yang-Mills description of early-time gluon fields in heavy-ion collisions. The model produces multiplicity distributions that are convolutions of negative binomial distributions without further assumptions or parameters. The eccentricity moments are compared to the MC-KLN model; a noteworthy feature is that fluctuation dominated odd moments are consistently larger than in the MC-KLN model.

# Event-by-event gluon multiplicity, energy density and eccentricities at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

The IP-Glasma model, which combines IP-Sat nucleon parton distributions constrained by HERA data with event-by-event classical Yang-Mills evolution, produces multiplicity distributions that are convolutions of negative binomial distributions without additional parameters; however, the overall normalization of the initial gluon multiplicity is adjusted by varying the ratio between the saturation scale Q_s and the color charge squared g^2 mu, and by multiplying by a factor of 2/3 to convert to charged particle multiplicity for comparison to experimental data.

## Method

The authors sample nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon (Fermi) distribution for gold and lead nuclei, then sum the color charge squared per unit area from individual nucleons using the IP-Sat dipole cross-section. Color charges are sampled from a Gaussian distribution with variance proportional to the saturation scale, and classical Yang-Mills equations are solved on a 2+1 dimensional lattice to determine the gluon fields and their time evolution from proper time tau=0 to tau=0.4 fm/c. The gluon multiplicity per unit rapidity is computed in transverse Coulomb gauge, and eccentricity moments epsilon_n up to n=6 are calculated from the energy density weighted spatial distributions.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the total inelastic cross section used to determine N_part is sigma_NN=42 mb; for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV, sigma_NN=64 mb.
- The nuclear radius for Au is R=6.38 fm with skin depth a=0.535 fm; for Pb, R=6.62 fm with a=0.546 fm.
- The infrared regulator m is set to Lambda_QCD approximately 0.2 GeV, and the relation Q_s approximately 0.75 g^2 mu is used.
- The gluon multiplicity is computed at tau=0.4 fm/c and multiplied by 2/3 to compare to charged particle data.
- The evolution time shown for energy density smoothing is Delta tau=0.2 fm, of order 1/Q_s.
- Eccentricity moments epsilon_n up to n=6 are computed; odd moments are consistently larger than in the MC-KLN model.

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma model introduced in Schenke (2012) by including time evolution with classical Yang-Mills equations in 2+1 dimensions, building on the IP-Sat model of Kowalski (2003) and the Glasma flux tube picture of Gelis (2009) and Lappi (2009). The results are compared to the MC-KLN model, which uses k_perp factorization approximations and does not include negative binomial fluctuations on the scale 1/Q_s, and to the MC-Glauber model of Schenke (2010, 2011) where a Gaussian energy density with fixed width 0.4 fm is added per participant nucleon. The IP-Glasma model enables future event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations to determine flow coefficients v_n from the computed initial eccentricity moments, potentially allowing extraction of shear and bulk viscosities of hot QCD matter.
