# Leading-order decuplet contributions to the baryon magnetic moments in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We extend an earlier study of the baryon magnetic moments in chiral perturbation theory by the explicit inclusion of the spin-3/2 decuplet resonances. We find that the corrections induced by these heavier degrees of freedom are relatively small in a covariant framework where unphysical spin-1/2 modes are removed. Consequently, implementing the leading SU(3)-breaking corrections given by both the baryon and decuplet contributions, we obtain a description of the baryon-octet magnetic moments that is better than the Coleman-Glashow relations. Finally, we discuss the uncertainties and compare between heavy baryon and covariant approaches.

# Leading-order decuplet contributions to the baryon magnetic moments in Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim

The corrections to baryon-octet magnetic moments from explicit inclusion of spin-3/2 decuplet resonances are relatively small in a covariant chiral perturbation theory framework where unphysical spin-1/2 modes are removed via consistent couplings; consequently, the improvement over the Coleman-Glashow relations obtained with only octet-baryons remains essentially unchanged after proper inclusion of the decuplet.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop contributions of the baryon decuplet (spin-3/2 Rarita-Schwinger fields) to the anomalous magnetic moments of the baryon octet at leading chiral order O(p^3), using both consistent (spin-3/2 gauge-invariant) and conventional (z = -1) couplings. They apply the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme to restore chiral power counting, and compare results with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and with covariant calculations using infrared regularization.

## Key numbers

- Decuplet-octet mass splitting: delta = M_D - M_B = 0.231 GeV (using average masses M_B = 1.151 GeV, M_D = 1.382 GeV)
- phi B D coupling constant: C = 1.0 (obtained from Delta -> N pi decay width)
- Pseudoscalar meson masses used: m_pi = 0.13957 GeV, m_K = 0.49368 GeV, m_eta = 0.5475 GeV
- For the covariant EOMS calculation with consistent couplings, the chi-squared (sum of squared deviations from experiment) is 0.22, compared to 0.46 for the SU(3)-symmetric tree-level description
- The proton magnetic moment in the covariant EOMS with consistent couplings is 2.61 mu_N (experiment: 2.793 mu_N); the neutron is -2.23 mu_N (experiment: -1.913 mu_N)
- The Lambda magnetic moment is -0.60 mu_N (experiment: -0.613 mu_N); the Lambda-Sigma^0 transition moment is predicted as 1.65 mu_N (experiment: +/- 1.61 mu_N)
- The decuplet contributions to the proton magnetic moment are +3.5% of the tree-level value; for the neutron they are -6%; for the Sigma^- they are +70%

## Context

This work extends the earlier covariant EOMS study of baryon magnetic moments by Geng, Camalich, and Vacas (2008) by adding explicit decuplet resonances, which were omitted in that prior calculation. It contrasts with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Jenkins (1992), Durand (1997), and Puglia (1999) that included decuplets but required next-to-next-to-leading order for good agreement, and with the infrared regularization approach by Kubis (2000) that gave worse results at next-to-leading order. The results enable future next-to-next-to-leading order studies and applications to the electromagnetic structure of decuplet resonances and decuplet-octet transitions.
