# Hadron production and QGP Hadronization in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV

> We show that all central rapidity hadron yields measured in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV are well described by the chemical non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model (SHM), where the chemically equilibrated QGP source breaks up directly into hadrons. SHM parameters are obtained as a function of centrality of colliding ions, and we compare CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) with Brookhaven National Laboratory Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) results. We predict yields of unobserved hadrons and address anti-matter production. The physical properties of the quark--gluon plasma fireball particle source show universality of hadronization conditions at LHC and RHIC.

## Key claim

The chemical non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model (SHM), where the quark-gluon plasma source breaks up directly into hadrons, describes all central rapidity hadron yields in Pb--Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, contradicting claims that SHM alone does not describe the particle multiplicity data at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors applied the chemical non-equilibrium SHM implemented within the SHARE program to fit experimental hadron yields measured by the ALICE experiment at mid-rapidity (|y|<0.5) across nine centrality bins. They allowed the light quark phase space occupancy parameter gamma_q and strangeness occupancy gamma_s to vary freely, while imposing strangeness conservation, charge per baryon ratio, and a constant baryon number stopping per participating nucleon as constraints. The fits were performed for three variants: chemical non-equilibrium (gamma_q != 1, gamma_s != 1), chemical semi-equilibrium (gamma_q = 1, gamma_s != 1), and chemical equilibrium (gamma_q = gamma_s = 1).

## Key numbers

- For the 0-20% centrality bin, the chemical non-equilibrium SHM fit yields chi^2/ndf = 9.5/9 ~ 1, while the chemical equilibrium SHM gives chi^2/ndf = 64/11 ~ 6
- The p/pi ratio at LHC is 0.046 +/- 0.003
- The critical gamma_q value for pion condensate formation ranges from 1.63 at T=138 MeV to 1.525 at T=160 MeV
- The net baryon number per participating nucleon in mid-rapidity is constrained to 0.0054 +/- 1%
- About 80% of pion and proton yields originate from decaying resonances, while about 50% of all charged hadrons are produced by strong decays of heavier resonances

## Context

This work extends the chemical non-equilibrium SHM approach previously applied to SPS and RHIC data (Letessier 2005, Petran 2011) to the new LHC energy regime, demonstrating universality of hadronization conditions across collision energies from RHIC at sqrt(s_NN)=62.4 GeV to LHC at 2.76 TeV. The results contradict claims by the ALICE collaboration (Abelev 2012) that SHM alone cannot describe LHC data, and invalidate the chemical equilibrium SHM approach (Becattini 2012, Braun-Munzinger 2001) at LHC energies. The findings enable predictions of unobserved hadron yields and anti-matter production, and provide physical properties of the QGP fireball source that can be compared with lattice QCD calculations.
