# Relativistic chiral nuclear forces: status and prospects

> Understanding nuclear structure, reactions, and the properties of neutron stars from \textit{ab initio} calculations from the nucleon degrees of freedom has always been a primary goal of nuclear physics, in which the microscopic nuclear force serves as the fundamental input. So far, the Weinberg chiral nuclear force, first proposed by the Nobel laureate Weinberg, has become the \textit{de facto} standard input for nuclear \textit{ab initio} studies. However, compared to their non-relativistic counterparts, relativistic \textit{ab initio} calculations, which describe better nuclear observables, have only begun. The lack of modern relativistic nucleon-nucleon interactions is an important issue restricting their development. In this work, we briefly review the development and status of the Weinberg chiral nuclear force, as well as its limitations. We further present a concise introduction to the relativistic chiral nuclear force, show its description of the scattering phase shifts and observables such as differential cross sections, and demonstrate its unique features. Additionally, we show that the relativistic framework could be naturally extended to the antinucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that relativistic chiral nuclear forces, constructed within a covariant baryon chiral effective field theory framework, can overcome fundamental and empirical limitations of the standard non-relativistic Weinberg chiral nuclear force, such as slow convergence and renormalization group issues, while already at leading order achieving a description of nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts comparable to the non-relativistic next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors construct relativistic chiral nuclear forces using covariant chiral effective Lagrangians up to \(\mathcal{O}(p^4)\), retaining full Dirac spinors and Clifford algebra. Short-range contact interactions are derived from covariant power counting, while long-range one-pion and medium-range two-pion exchanges are included via the \(\pi N\) Lagrangian. The non-perturbative nucleon-nucleon scattering is solved using the relativistic Blankenbecler-Sugar equation, with low-energy constants fitted to Nijmegen partial-wave phase shifts for angular momentum \(J \leq 2\) at laboratory kinetic energies of 1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 150, and 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order relativistic chiral nuclear force describes the \(^1S_0\) partial-wave phase shift comparably to the non-relativistic next-to-leading order (NLO), demonstrating faster convergence.
* Low-energy constants are fitted to eight experimental points per partial wave at laboratory kinetic energies \(T_{\rm lab} = 1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 150, 200\) MeV.
* The regulator used is a sharp cutoff: \(f_R(p) = \theta(\Lambda^2 - p^2)\).
* The relativistic Blankenbecler-Sugar equation uses the nucleon momentum in the center-of-mass frame \(|\bm{q}_{\rm cm}| = \sqrt{s/4 - m^2}\) with nucleon mass \(m\).

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg chiral nuclear force (Weinberg, 1990–1992) by addressing its known issues: slow convergence, failure to satisfy renormalization group invariance, and inconsistency in treating three-body forces (Epelbaum, 2019; Machleidt, 2023). The relativistic framework builds on covariant baryon chiral effective field theory (Ren, 2016; Xiao, 2018) and the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme. It enables future development of consistent relativistic three-body forces and applications to antinucleon-nucleon interactions, as well as improved ab initio calculations of nuclear structure and neutron star properties.
