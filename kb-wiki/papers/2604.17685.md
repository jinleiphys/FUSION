# Constraining neutron skin impurity in $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ and its relevance for the CREX-PREX puzzle

> The impact of Coulomb core polarization on the neutron density distribution of $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ is investigated, revealing that Coulomb-driven proton shifts toward the nuclear surface lead to neutron skin impurity, defined as the presence of protons in the neutron skin region. The Coulomb boundary radius, where protons begin to be driven outward, is located before the neutron skin region, leading to proton admixture and establishing impurity as an intrinsic feature of the density profile in $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$. Using the $(^3\mathrm{He},t)$ isobaric analog state (IAS) reaction at 420 MeV, we quantify impurity-induced modifications of the probed neutron distribution. For a neutron skin thickness of 0.144 fm, consistent with the thin-skin scenario suggested by CREX ($0.121 \pm 0.035$ fm), Coulomb core polarization effectively enhances the neutron distribution by approximately 0.052 fm via the symmetry potential response to proton admixture. Our analysis shows that neutron skin impurity is thickness-dependent and becomes suppressed in large-skin scenarios as the symmetry potential neutralizes core polarization, thereby motivating IAS charge-exchange measurements as a complementary probe of neutron skin observables.

# Constraining neutron skin impurity in $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ and its relevance for the CREX-PREX puzzle

## Key claim

Coulomb core polarization in $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ produces a neutron skin impurity, proton admixture in the neutron skin region, that is thickness-dependent and becomes suppressed in large-skin scenarios, but for a thin neutron skin of 0.144 fm (consistent with the CREX value of 0.121 ± 0.035 fm) it effectively enhances the neutron distribution probed by hadronic reactions by approximately 0.052 fm via the symmetry potential response to proton admixture.

## Method

Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations using the SAMi-J energy density functional family were performed for $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$, systematically varying the symmetry energy to generate neutron skin thicknesses covering the experimental ranges from CREX and RCNP. The $(^3\mathrm{He},t)$ isobaric analog state (IAS) reaction at 420 MeV was described within the Lane model and distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA), using a double-folding model with Chiral three-nucleon forces G-matrix effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, comparing transition densities constructed from the full isovector density $(\rho_n - \rho_p)$ versus the neutron excess density $\rho_{n\mathrm{exc}}$ alone.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness $\Delta R_{np} = 0.144$ fm for SAMi-J27, consistent with the CREX value of $0.121 \pm 0.035$ fm
- Coulomb boundary radius shifts systematically inward from 3.28 fm (SAMi-J27) to 3.18 fm (SAMi-J29) to 3.07 fm (SAMi-J30) as neutron skin increases
- For SAMi-J27, the impurity-affected region spans from the Coulomb boundary at 3.28 fm to the convergence point at 3.64 fm, giving an impurity region of approximately 0.36 fm
- Impurity-induced outward extension of the neutron distribution $\Delta R_{\mathrm{imp}} \approx 0.052$ fm for SAMi-J27, effectively increasing the neutron skin from 0.144 fm to approximately 0.196 fm as probed by the incident particle
- Number of protons driven into the surface region $S$ decreases from 1.017 (SAMi-J27) to 0.971 (SAMi-J28) to 0.691 (SAMi-J29) to 0.541 (SAMi-J30)
- Relative strength $\alpha = S/(N-Z)$ decreases from 0.127 (SAMi-J27) to 0.068 (SAMi-J30)
- For SAMi-J29 (0.184 fm skin), impurity-affected region is approximately 0.33 fm; for SAMi-J30 (0.203 fm skin), approximately 0.32 fm
- For $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ with SAMi-J35 (0.266 fm skin), $S = 1.297$ and $\alpha = 0.029$

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of Coulomb core polarization in $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ (Huan 2024) to the $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ system, addressing the CREX-PREX puzzle, the apparent inconsistency between the thick neutron skin of $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ reported by PREX-II (0.283 ± 0.071 fm) and the thin skin of $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ reported by CREX (0.121 ± 0.035 fm). Prior theoretical attempts to resolve this tension, such as enhanced isovector spin-orbit interactions (Kunjipurayil 2025), can lead to inconsistencies with established nuclear structure features. The results demonstrate that neutron skin impurity is an intrinsic feature of $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$ that must be accounted for in reaction-based analyses, and motivate a dedicated measurement of the $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}(^3\mathrm{He},t)$ reaction at 420 MeV at facilities like FRIB as a complementary probe of neutron skin observables.
