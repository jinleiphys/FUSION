# Unitarity cutting rules for the nucleus excitation and topological cross sections in hard production off nuclei from nonlinear k_t-factorization

> At the partonic level, a typical final state in small-x deep inelastic scattering off nuclei and hard proton-nucleus collisions can be characterized by the multiplicity of color-excited nucleons. Within reggeon field theory, each color-excited nucleon is associated with the unitarity cut of the pomeron exchanged between the projectile and nucleus. In this communication we derive the unitarity rules for the multiplicity of excited nucleons, alias cut pomerons, alias topological cross sections, for typical hard dijet production processes. We demonstrate how the coupled-channel non-Abelian intranuclear evolution of color dipoles, inherent to pQCD, gives rise to the reggeon field theory diagrams for final states in terms of the uncut, and two kinds of cut, pomerons. Upon the proper identification of the uncut and cut pomeron exchanges, the topological cross sections for dijet production follow in a straightforward way from the earlier derived nonlinear k_t - factorization quadratures for the inclusive dijet cross sections. The concept of a coherent (collective) nuclear glue proves extremely useful for the formulation of reggeon field theory vertices of multipomeron - cut and uncut - couplings to particles and between themselves. A departure of our unitarity cutting rules from the ones suggested by the pre-QCD Abramovsky-Kancheli-Gribov rules, stems from the coupled-channel features of intranuclear pQCD. We propose a multiplicity re-summation as a tool for the isolation of topological cross sections for single-jet production.

## Key claim

The paper derives pQCD-based unitarity cutting rules for topological cross sections in hard dijet production off nuclei, showing that the non-Abelian coupled-channel intranuclear evolution of color dipoles necessitates two distinct kinds of cut pomerons (rotation-type and excitation-type), which depart from the pre-QCD Abramovsky-Kancheli-Gribov (AGK) rules.

## Method

The authors start from the color-dipole S-matrix approach to high-energy hard processes, treating the nucleus as an uncorrelated dilute gas of color-singlet nucleons. They evaluate the color traces that emerge from the differential cross section for a specific final state of the target nucleus, separating color-diagonal (elastic) and color-excitation interactions of color dipoles. The topological cross sections for dijet production are then obtained by reinterpreting the previously derived nonlinear k_perp-factorization quadratures for inclusive dijet cross sections, with the identification of uncut pomerons (Pom) and two kinds of cut pomerons (CutPom_r and CutPom_e).

## Key numbers

* The coherence condition for coherent interaction over the whole nucleus is x < x_A = 1/(2 R_A m_p) approximately 0.1 A^{-1/3}, where R_A is the nuclear radius and m_p the proton mass.
* The rapidity threshold for independent string hadronization is eta_A approximately log(1/(2 R_A m_p)).
* The dipole cross section for a color-singlet q-bar-q dipole is sigma(x, r) = C_F integral d^2 b_q [chi(b_q) - chi(b_q - r)]^2.
* The dipole cross section for a parton in representation R is sigma_{R-bar-R}(x, r) = (C_R / C_F) sigma(x, r), where C_R is the quadratic Casimir operator for multiplet R.
* The total inelastic dipole-nucleon cross section is described by the two-gluon exchange approximation, with the unintegrated gluon density f(x, kappa) = (4 pi alpha_S(r) / N_c) * (1/kappa^4) * F(x, kappa^2).

## Context

This work extends the pre-QCD unitarity cutting rules of Abramovsky, Gribov, and Kancheli (1972) to pQCD, finding that the coupled-channel features of non-Abelian intranuclear evolution require two kinds of cut pomerons rather than one. The approach builds on the color-dipole S-matrix formalism developed in earlier work by Nikolaev, Zakharov, and collaborators (1991-1994) and the nonlinear k_perp-factorization framework for hard processes in nuclear environments. The results enable the calculation of topological cross sections for dijet production in deep inelastic scattering off nuclei and hard proton-nucleus collisions, with applications to long-range rapidity correlations between forward jets and target fragmentation, as well as the nonperturbative quenching of leading jets.
