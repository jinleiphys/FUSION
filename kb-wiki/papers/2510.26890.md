# Baryon-antibaryon photoproduction cross sections off the proton

> The GlueX experiment at Jefferson Lab has observed $p\bar{p}$ and, for the first time, $Λ\barΛ$ and $p\barΛ$ photoproduction from a proton target at photon energies up to 11.6 GeV. The angular distributions are forward peaked for all produced pairs, consistent with Regge-like $t$-channel exchange. Asymmetric wide-angle anti-baryon distributions show the presence of additional processes. In a phenomenological model, we find consistency with a double $t$-channel exchange process where anti-baryons are created only at the middle vertex. The model matches all observed distributions with a small number of free parameters. In the hyperon channels, we observe a clear distinction between photoproduction of the $Λ\barΛ$ and $p\barΛ$ systems but general similarity to the $p\bar{p}$ system. We report both total cross sections and cross sections differential with respect to momentum transfer and the invariant masses of the created particle pairs. No narrow resonant structures were found in these reaction channels. The suppression of $s\bar{s}$ quark pairs relative to $d\bar{d}$ quark pairs is similar to what has been seen in other reactions.

## Key claim

The GlueX experiment reports the first observation of Lambda anti-Lambda and proton anti-Lambda photoproduction from a proton target, and finds that a phenomenological model with double t-channel exchange, where antibaryons are created only at the middle vertex, matches all observed angular distributions, though the model is not uniquely determined and represents consistency rather than proof.

## Method

The GlueX experiment at Jefferson Lab measured exclusive photoproduction of p anti-p, Lambda anti-Lambda, and p anti-Lambda pairs from a proton target using photon beams with energies up to 11.6 GeV. The data were analyzed by reconstructing the final-state particles in the GlueX detector and extracting differential cross sections with respect to momentum transfer and invariant masses of the produced pairs, then fitting the angular distributions with a phenomenological model incorporating single and double Regge-like t-channel exchange diagrams.

## Key numbers

* Photon beam energies up to 11.6 GeV were used for all three reaction channels.
* The angular distributions are forward peaked for all produced baryon-antibaryon pairs, consistent with Regge-like t-channel exchange.
* No narrow resonant structures were found in any of the reaction channels.
* The suppression of s anti-s quark pairs relative to d anti-d quark pairs is similar to what has been seen in other reactions (no specific numerical suppression factor provided in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of exclusive p anti-p photoproduction from SLAC (1973), Daresbury, CERN, and DESY (up to 1984), which were mostly below 6 GeV beam energy, and includes unpublished work from CLAS at Jefferson Lab (Stokes 2006, Phelps 2017). The first observation of Lambda anti-Lambda and p anti-Lambda photoproduction opens the door to systematic comparisons of strange and nonstrange baryon-antibaryon pair creation mechanisms, and the phenomenological double-exchange model provides a framework for future theoretical treatments of these reactions.
