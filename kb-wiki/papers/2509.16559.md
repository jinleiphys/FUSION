# Equation of state for hyperonic neutron-star matter in SU(3) flavor symmetry

> Using a relativistic mean-field model calibrated to finite-nucleus observables and bulk properties of dense nuclear matter, we investigate hyperonic neutron-star matter within an SU(3) flavor-symmetry scheme. To retain SU(6)-based couplings within SU(3) flavor symmetry, we add a quartic $ϕ$ self-interaction and $ϕ$-$ρ$ mixing. We demonstrate the roles of $α_{v}$ ($F/(F+D)$ ratio), $θ_{v}$ (mixing angle), and $z_{v}$ (singlet-to-octet coupling ratio) in SU(3)-invariant vector-meson couplings. It is found that $z_{v}$ predominantly controls the maximum mass of a neutron star, and $2M_{\odot}$ neutron stars can be supported for $z_{v}\le0.15$. The $α_{v}$ also helps sustain large masses, whereas $θ_{v}$ has a smaller effect on neutron-star properties. This SU(3) framework reconciles nuclear and astrophysical constraints, and offers a plausible resolution to the hyperon puzzle.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an SU(3) flavor-symmetry extension of a relativistic mean-field model, calibrated to finite-nucleus data and dense-matter constraints, can support neutron stars with masses above 2 solar masses even when hyperons are present, provided the singlet-to-octet coupling ratio \(z_v\) is kept at or below 0.15. The resolution of the hyperon puzzle is conditional on this specific parameter range, with the \(F/(F+D)\) ratio \(\alpha_v\) also contributing but the mixing angle \(\theta_v\) having a smaller effect.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model including \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), \(\delta\), \(\rho\), \(\sigma^\ast\), and \(\phi\) mesons, with nonlinear self-interactions and mixing terms (quartic \(\phi\) self-interaction and \(\phi\)-\(\rho\) mixing) to preserve SU(6)-based couplings within the SU(3) flavor-symmetry scheme. The model is first calibrated to ground-state properties of closed-shell nuclei (binding energies and charge radii) and saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter in SU(6) symmetry, then extended to SU(3) symmetry by adjusting vector-meson couplings via three parameters: \(\alpha_v\) (\(F/(F+D)\) ratio), \(\theta_v\) (mixing angle), and \(z_v\) (singlet-to-octet coupling ratio). Hyperon coupling constants for scalar mesons are fixed to empirical single-baryon potential depths in symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, while vector-meson couplings to hyperons are determined by the SU(3) relations.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density \(n_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), with effective nucleon mass \(M_N^\ast/M_N = 0.70\), binding energy \(E_0(n_0) = -16.35\) MeV, incompressibility \(K_0 = 240\) MeV, symmetry energy \(E_{\text{sym}}(n_0) = 32.5\) MeV, slope parameter \(L = 50\) MeV, and curvature parameter \(K_{\text{sym}} = -210.67\) MeV.
* Neutron stars with masses of \(2M_\odot\) can be supported for \(z_v \le 0.15\) in the SU(3) framework.
* The crust-core phase transition occurs at total baryon density \(n_B = 0.089\) fm\(^{-3}\) in all cases.
* Single-baryon potential depths used: \(U_\Lambda^{(\text{SNM})}(n_0) = -27.7\) MeV, \(U_\Sigma^{(\text{SNM})}(n_0) = +30\) MeV, \(U_\Xi^{(\text{SNM})}(n_0) = -21\) MeV, and \(U_{\Xi^-}^{(\text{PNM})} = +6\) MeV from lattice QCD.
* Proton-neutron effective mass splitting at saturation: \(M_p^\ast - M_n^\ast \simeq 100\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMF study (Miyatsu 2013) by incorporating updated constraints from NICER radius measurements, GW170817 tidal deformability, and heavy-ion collision data, while also including quartic \(\phi\) self-interactions and \(\phi\)-\(\rho\) mixing to maintain SU(6) saturation properties within SU(3) symmetry. It addresses the hyperon puzzle, which arises because hyperon onset typically softens the equation of state and reduces maximum neutron-star masses below the observed \(2M_\odot\) threshold (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013). The approach contrasts with nonrelativistic methods using three-body forces (Schulze 2006, Vidana 2010) and other relativistic extensions with additional strange mesons (Schaffner 1993, Schaffner 1995) or explicit three-baryon couplings (Tsubakihara 2012, Muto 2021). The results enable future studies to systematically explore the interplay between \(\alpha_v\), \(\theta_v\), and \(z_v\) in reconciling nuclear experimental data with astrophysical observations of neutron-star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities.
