# Coupled-channels calculations of nonelastic cross sections using a density-functional structure model

> A microscopic calculation of the reaction cross-section for nucleon-nucleus scattering has been performed by explicitly coupling the elastic channel to all particle-hole (p-h) excitation states in the target and to all one-nucleon pickup channels. The p-h states may be regarded as doorway states through which the flux flows to more complicated configurations, and subsequently to long-lived compound nucleus resonances. Target excitations for 40,48Ca, 58Ni, 90Zr and 144Sm were described in a QRPA framework using a Skyrme functional. Reaction cross sections calculated in this approach were compared to predictions of a fitted optical potential and to experimental data, reaching very good agreement. Couplings between inelastic states were found to be negligible, while the couplings to pickup channels contribute significantly. For the first time observed reaction cross-sections are completely accounted for by explicit channel coupling, for incident energies between 10 and 40 MeV.

## Key claim
For the first time, observed reaction cross-sections for nucleon-nucleus scattering are completely accounted for by explicit channel coupling, but only within a doorway approximation that treats the total flux leaving the elastic channel to all first-order channels as independent of subsequent processes. The agreement with experiment and phenomenological optical potentials is achieved for incident energies between 10 and 40 MeV.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale coupled-channels calculations coupling the elastic channel to all particle-hole (p-h) excitation states in the target and to all one-nucleon pickup channels. Target excitations for 40,48Ca, 58Ni, 90Zr and 144Sm were described using a QRPA framework with the Skyrme SLy4 energy-density functional, and the scattering effective nucleon-nucleon interaction was a Gaussian matched to the M3Y interaction at 40 MeV. Coupled reaction channels (CRC) calculations were performed for pickup channels using the Johnson-Soper prescription for the deuteron potential, and non-orthogonality corrections were included.

## Key numbers
- Inelastic couplings alone accounted for only approximately 23% of the phenomenological optical-model reaction cross-section for p + 40Ca at Elab = 30 MeV.
- The isoscalar Gaussian interaction parameters were V0^0 = -24.1921 MeV and μ0 = 0.7180 fm^-1; the isovector parameters were V0^1 = 11.3221 MeV and μ1 = 0.7036 fm^-1.
- Calculations were performed only at Elab = 10, 20, 30 and 40 MeV for each system.
- The proton pairing gap of 90Zr was 1.2 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier nuclear-structure approaches that calculated second-order diagrams using RPA particle-hole propagators but could only account for approximately 44% of absorption (Bernard 1979) or approximately 71% including charge exchange (Osterfeld 1981). It also contrasts with the nuclear matter approach (Jeukenne 1977) which is accurate above approximately 50 MeV, and with recent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov-based calculations (Pilipenko 2010) that were limited to neutron scattering below 15 MeV. The results enable future fully-consistent ab initio reaction calculations for unstable nuclei relevant to rare-isotope facilities, the r-process, and advanced reactor applications.
