# Intrinsic transverse momentum and the polarized Drell-Yan process

> In this paper we study the cross section at leading order in $1/Q$ for polarized Drell-Yan scattering at measured lepton-pair transverse momentum $Q_T$. We find that for a hadron with spin $1/2$ the quark content at leading order is described by six distribution functions for each flavor, which depend on both the lightcone momentum fraction $x$, and the quark transverse momentum $\bbox{k}_T^2$. These functions are illustrated for a free-quark ensemble. The cross sections for both longitudinal and transverse polarizations are expressed in terms of convolution integrals over the distribution functions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Ralston and Soper (1979) analysis of polarized Drell-Yan scattering by identifying a sixth transverse-momentum-dependent distribution function that was previously omitted, and by calculating the polarized Drell-Yan cross section without constraining the lepton-pair transverse momentum \(Q_T\) to zero, thereby becoming sensitive to all six distribution functions at leading order in \(1/Q\).

## Method

The authors analyze the quark-quark correlation function for a spin-1/2 hadron using Dirac-structure expansion constrained by hermiticity, parity, and time-reversal invariance, identifying six leading distribution functions that depend on the lightcone momentum fraction \(x\) and the quark transverse momentum \(\mathbf{k}_T^2\). They then compute the hadron tensor for the Drell-Yan process \(A+B \rightarrow \ell + \bar{\ell} + X\) at leading order in \(1/Q\) using the diagrammatic expansion of Ellis, Furmanski, and Petronzio (1983), expressing the cross sections for longitudinal and transverse polarizations as convolution integrals over these distribution functions. A free-quark ensemble model is used to illustrate the six distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- The six distribution functions per flavor are: \(f_1(x,\mathbf{k}_T^2)\), \(g_{1L}(x,\mathbf{k}_T^2)\), \(g_{1T}(x,\mathbf{k}_T^2)\), \(h_{1T}(x,\mathbf{k}_T^2)\), \(h_{1L}^\perp(x,\mathbf{k}_T^2)\), and \(h_{1T}^\perp(x,\mathbf{k}_T^2)\).
- The analysis applies at leading order in \(1/Q\), with \(Q_T \lesssim \Lambda\) where \(\Lambda^2 \sim 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\) is the hadronic scale.
- The cross section is given by \(d\sigma/(d^4 q d\Omega) = (\alpha^2)/(2s Q^4) L_{\mu\nu} W^{\mu\nu}\), with \(Q^2 \equiv q^2\) and \(s = (P_A + P_B)^2\).
- For unpolarized Drell-Yan, the cross section contains four structure functions: \(W_{0,0}\), \(W_{2,0}\), \(W_{2,1}\), and \(W_{2,2}\).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering analysis of Ralston and Soper (1979) on polarized Drell-Yan scattering, who found five distribution functions and calculated the cross section only at \(Q_T = 0\). The authors identify a sixth distribution function \(h_{1T}^\perp\) that Ralston and Soper omitted, and compute the cross section for general \(Q_T\), making all six functions accessible. This enables future experimental extraction of transverse-momentum-dependent quark distributions from polarized Drell-Yan data, complementing semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering measurements such as those discussed by Levelt (1994).
