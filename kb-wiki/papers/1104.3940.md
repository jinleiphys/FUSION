# Deformation of hypernuclei studied with antisymmetirzed molecular dynamics

> An extended version of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics to study structure of $p$-$sd$ shell hypernuclei is developed. By using an effective $ΛN$ interaction, we investigate energy curves of $^9_Λ$Be, $^{13}_Λ$C and $^{20,21}_Λ$Ne as function of nuclear quadrupole deformation. Change of nuclear deformation caused by $Λ$ particle is discussed. It is found that the $Λ$ in p-wave enhances nuclear deformation, while that in s-wave reduces it. This effect is most prominent in $^{13}_Λ$C. The possibility of the parity inversion in $^{20}_Λ$Ne is also examined.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Lambda hyperon in a p-wave orbital enhances nuclear quadrupole deformation, while a Lambda in an s-wave reduces it, with the magnitude of this effect depending strongly on the softness of the core nucleus against quadrupole deformation. The most prominent change is found in 13_Lambda C, but the authors note that this drastic change may depend on the choice of effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and will be investigated in future work.

## Method

The authors developed an extended version of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (HyperAMD) to describe single-Lambda hypernuclei, representing the Lambda single-particle wave function as a superposition of Gaussian wave packets. They calculated energy curves as functions of nuclear quadrupole deformation beta for 9_Lambda Be, 13_Lambda C, 20_Lambda Ne, and 21_Lambda Ne using the Gogny D1S nucleon-nucleon interaction and the YNG-ND effective Lambda N interaction, with constraints on the nuclear quadrupole deformation and on the Lambda orbital (s-wave or p-wave).

## Key numbers

- 9_Lambda Be: Lambda_s binding energy B_Lambda = 5.75 MeV (observed 6.71 MeV), quadrupole deformation beta = 0.65 (core 8_Be beta = 0.68); Lambda_p B_Lambda = -0.77 MeV (observed 0.19 MeV), beta = 0.71
- 13_Lambda C: Lambda_s B_Lambda = 11.6 MeV (observed 11.69 MeV), beta = 0.00 (core 12_C positive-parity beta = 0.27); Lambda_p B_Lambda = 0.46 MeV (observed 1.65 MeV), beta = 0.30
- 20_Lambda Ne: Lambda_s B_Lambda = 16.8 MeV, beta = 0.25 (core 19_Ne positive-parity beta = 0.27); Lambda_p B_Lambda = 5.74 MeV, beta = 0.30
- 21_Lambda Ne: Lambda_s B_Lambda = 17.2 MeV, beta = 0.37 (core 20_Ne positive-parity beta = 0.38); Lambda_p B_Lambda = 6.75 MeV, beta = 0.38
- The variation of Lambda single particle energy as function of quadrupole deformation is within a range of 1 to 2 MeV
- The reduction of nuclear radius by the Lambda particle is less than 5% in all cases

## Context

This work extends earlier cluster model calculations by Motoba (1983, 1985) and Yamada (1984) and mean-field calculations by Zhou (2007), Win (2008), and Schulze (2010) that demonstrated deformation changes from s-wave Lambda hyperons. The results contradict the cluster model prediction by Sakuda (1987) of parity inversion in 20_Lambda Ne, where the current calculation finds the positive-parity state coupled to Lambda_s is more deeply bound than the negative-parity state. The HyperAMD framework enables systematic study of sd-shell and neutron-rich hypernuclei, with future work planned to include angular momentum projection and generator coordinate method calculations to resolve discrepancies with experimental binding energies and the parity inversion question.
