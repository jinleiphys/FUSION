# The phase transition in hot $Λ$ hypernuclei within relativistic Thomas-Fermi approximation

> A self-consistent description for hot $Λ$ hypernuclei in hypothetical big boxes is developed within the relativistic Thomas-Fermi approximation in order to investigate directly the liquid-gas phase coexistence in strangeness finite nuclear systems. We use the relativistic mean-field model for nuclear interactions. The temperature dependence of $Λ$ hyperon density, $Λ$ hyperon radius, excitation energies, specific heat, and the binding energies of $Λ$ hypernuclei from $^{16}_Λ$O to $^{208}_Λ$Pb in phase transition region are calculated by using the subtraction procedure in order to separate the hypernucleus from the surrounding baryon gas. The $Λ$ central density is very sensitive to the temperature. The radii of $Λ$ hyperon at high temperature become very large. In the relativistic Thomas-Fermi approximation with the subtraction procedure, the properties of hypernuclei are independent of the size of the box in which the calculation is performed. The level density parameters of hypernuclei in the present work are confirmed to be almost constant at low temperature. It is also found that the single-$Λ$ binding energies of $Λ$ hypernuclei are largely reduced with increasing temperature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the liquid-gas phase coexistence in strangeness finite nuclear systems can be directly investigated using a relativistic Thomas-Fermi approximation with a subtraction procedure, but this approach is limited to temperatures up to about 8 MeV because a limiting temperature exists above which the hypernucleus becomes unstable.

## Method

The authors solve coupled relativistic mean-field equations for the sigma, omega, and rho meson fields together with the Poisson equation for the Coulomb potential, using the TM1 parametrization for nuclear interactions and fixed ratios of meson-Lambda couplings (R_omega = 2/3 from quark counting, R_sigma = 0.621 from fitting experimental binding energies). They compute two sets of solutions: one for the liquid-plus-gas phase (NG) and one for the gas phase alone (G), then subtract the gas contribution from the liquid-plus-gas contribution to isolate the properties of the hot Lambda hypernucleus. The calculations are performed in a spherical box with radius 20 fm, and the independence of results on box size is verified by comparing radii of 15, 16, 18, and 20 fm.

## Key numbers

- The Lambda central density at T=8 MeV is about 30% of its value at T=0 MeV for both Ca-40 and Pb-208 hypernuclei.
- The Lambda rms radius for Ca-40 increases from 2.43 fm at T=0 MeV to 3.84 fm at T=8 MeV.
- The Lambda rms radius for Pb-208 increases from 3.98 fm at T=0 MeV to 6.10 fm at T=8 MeV.
- The single-Lambda binding energy for Pb-208 at T=0 MeV is 27.41 MeV, consistent with the experimental value of 26.3 +/- 0.8 MeV.
- The single-Lambda binding energy for Ca-40 at T=0 MeV is 22.19 MeV, decreasing to 9.15 MeV at T=8 MeV.
- The level density parameter for Ca-40 is approximately 3.8 (from S/2T definition) at T=2-4 MeV, and for Pb-208 is approximately 16.1 at T=2 MeV.
- The excitation energy per particle for Ca-40 at T=8 MeV is 5.33 MeV, and for Pb-208 at T=8 MeV is 4.52 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the subtraction procedure previously applied to normal nuclei in the Hartree-Fock framework by Bonche, Levit, and Vautherin (1985) and in the non-relativistic Thomas-Fermi approach by Suraud (1987), now incorporating Lambda hyperons within a relativistic Thomas-Fermi approximation. The authors had previously developed a relativistic Thomas-Fermi model with subtraction for hot nuclei (Zhang et al., 2014), and here they extend that formalism to strangeness-carrying systems. This enables future studies of hypernuclei produced in heavy ion collisions, where the statistical multifragmentation model of Bondorf et al. (1995) and its extension to hypernuclei by Botvina et al. (2007) have been used, and provides a self-consistent description of the liquid-gas phase transition in hypermatter that is independent of the assumed box size.
