# Quark Condensates in Nuclear Matter in the Global Color Symmetry Model of QCD

> With the global color symmetry model being extended to finite chemical potential, we study the density dependence of the local and nonlocal scalar quark condensates in nuclear matter. The calculated results indicate that the quark condensates increase smoothly with the increasing of nuclear matter density before the critical value (about 12$ρ_0$) is reached. It also manifests that the chiral symmetry is restored suddenly as the density of nuclear matter reaches its critical value. Meanwhile, the nonlocal quark condensate in nuclear matter changes nonmonotonously against the space-time distance among the quarks.

## Key claim

The local and nonlocal scalar quark condensates in nuclear matter increase smoothly with density up to a critical value (about 11.8 times normal nuclear density), then vanish suddenly, indicating a first-order chiral symmetry restoration. This behavior contradicts the conventional expectation that condensates should decrease monotonically with density, but the authors argue it is consistent with the competing effects of decreasing nucleon mass and increasing nucleon number.

## Method

The authors extended the Global Color Symmetry Model (GCM) of QCD to finite chemical potential by transforming the quark field as q(x) → e^{μx₄}q(x) and deriving the corresponding Dyson-Schwinger gap equations for the quark self-energy components A(p,μ), B(p,μ), and C(p,μ). Using an infrared-dominant gluon propagator g²D(p-q) = (3π²η²/q²)δ(p-q) with η = 1.37 GeV fixed by fitting π and ρ meson properties, they solved the gap equations in the Nanbu-Goldstone (confined) and Wigner (deconfined) phases. The bag constant was computed from the action difference between hadron and vacuum configurations, and the chemical potential was converted to nuclear matter density via the baryon number density relation ρ_n = (2/(2π)⁴) ∂/∂μ ∫ d⁴p [B(̃p, p₄+iμ)]²/p².

## Key numbers

* Critical nuclear matter density for deconfinement: ρ_c ≈ 11.8 ρ₀, corresponding to critical chemical potential μ_c ≈ 0.316 GeV.
* Quark condensate in free space (Nanbu-Goldstone phase): ⟨:q̄q:⟩₀ = -(148 MeV)³.
* Nucleon radius in free space: R₀ = 0.69 fm, obtained after fitting nucleon mass M₀ = 939 MeV with center-of-mass correction parameter Z₀ = 3.707.
* Energy-scale parameter η = 1.37 GeV, fixed by fitting π and ρ meson properties.
* Nonlocal condensate ratio reaches its maximum at space-time distance about 0.2–0.24 fm (approximately one quarter of the nucleon radius) for densities from 3ρ₀ to 11ρ₀.

## Context

This work extends the Global Color Symmetry Model (GCM) of QCD to finite density, following earlier GCM studies of hadron properties in free space by Cahill and Roberts (1985) and Tandy (1997). The results are consistent with Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations by Maris, Roberts, and Schmidt (1998) and by Bender, Roberts, and von Smekal (1998), which also found increasing condensates with density, and with the sudden chiral restoration feature from the composite-operator formalism of Bardeen (1990). However, the behavior contradicts the conventional monotonic decrease seen in the linear Walecka model (Malheiro, 1997), Dirac-Brueckner method (Li and Ko, 1994), and most approaches reviewed by Drukarev, Ryskin, and Sadovnikova (2001). The results suggest that the pion production threshold in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions may increase with density, potentially explaining the small pion-to-proton ratio observed at RHIC (Seifert, 2002).
