# Predictions for 5.023 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> We compute predictions for various low-transverse-momentum bulk observables in $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.023$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC from the event-by-event next-to-leading-order perturbative-QCD + saturation + viscous hydrodynamics ("EKRT") model. In particular, we consider the centrality dependence of charged hadron multiplicity, flow coefficients of the azimuth-angle asymmetries and correlations of event-plane angles. The centrality dependencies of the studied observables are predicted to be very similar to those at 2.76 TeV, and the magnitudes of the flow coefficients and event-plane angle correlations are predicted to be close to those at 2.76 TeV. The flow coefficients may, however, offer slightly more discriminating power on the temperature dependence of QCD matter viscosity than the 2.76 TeV measurements. Our prediction for the multiplicity in the 0-5\% centrality class, obtained using the two temperature-dependent shear-viscosity-to-entropy ratios that give the best overall fit to RHIC and LHC data is $dN_{\rm ch}/dη\big|_{|η|\le 0.5} =1876\dots2046$. We also predict a power-law increase from 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC to 2.76 and 5.023 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, $dN_{\rm ch}/dη\big|_{|η|\le 0.5} \propto s^{0.164\dots0.174}$.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the centrality dependencies of charged hadron multiplicity, flow coefficients, and event-plane angle correlations in 5.023 TeV Pb+Pb collisions will be very similar to those at 2.76 TeV, with flow coefficients potentially offering slightly more discriminating power on the temperature dependence of QCD matter viscosity than the lower-energy measurements.

## Method

The authors used the event-by-event next-to-leading-order perturbative-QCD + saturation + viscous hydrodynamics ("EKRT") model, with parameters fixed exactly as in their previous work (Niemi 2015). They computed initial conditions from NLO pQCD minijet production with a local saturation criterion, evolved the system with 2+1 dimensional dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics using five different temperature-dependent shear-viscosity-to-entropy ratio parametrizations, and calculated final-state observables for charged hadrons at mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Predicted charged hadron multiplicity in the 0-5% centrality class at 5.023 TeV: dN_ch/dη (|η| ≤ 0.5) = 1876 to 2046
- Predicted power-law increase from 200 GeV Au+Au to 5.023 TeV Pb+Pb: dN_ch/dη ∝ s^(0.164 to 0.174)
- For η/s=0.2: multiplicity at 200 GeV = 662.1, at 2.76 TeV = 1564, at 5.023 TeV = 1908, with power p = 0.1643
- For param1: multiplicity at 200 GeV = 643.2, at 2.76 TeV = 1599, at 5.023 TeV = 1973, with power p = 0.1739
- For param2: multiplicity at 200 GeV = 679.6, at 2.76 TeV = 1591, at 5.023 TeV = 1941, with power p = 0.1630
- For param3: multiplicity at 200 GeV = 619.7, at 2.76 TeV = 1583, at 5.023 TeV = 1965, with power p = 0.1791
- For param4: multiplicity at 200 GeV = 655.6, at 2.76 TeV = 1583, at 5.023 TeV = 1945, with power p = 0.1689
- Predicted increase of v2{2} from 2.76 to 5.023 TeV: at most about 5% from central to mid-peripheral collisions
- Predicted increase of v3{2} and v4{2}: up to about 10% for best η/s parametrizations, up to 30% across all parametrizations

## Context

This work extends the NLO EKRT event-by-event model previously applied to 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Niemi 2015) to predict observables for the higher-energy 5.023 TeV LHC heavy-ion run. The predictions enable testing whether the power-law growth of multiplicity with center-of-mass energy continues as predicted, and whether the higher-energy data can provide additional constraints on the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio of QCD matter, particularly in the high-temperature regime of the quark-gluon plasma.
