# Towards testing the magnetic moment of the tau at one part per million

> If physics beyond the Standard Model (BSM) explains the $4.2σ$ difference between the Standard-Model and measured muon anomalous magnetic moment, $a_μ$, minimal flavor violation predicts a shift in the analog quantity for the $τ$ lepton, $a_τ$, at the $10^{-6}$ level, and even larger effects are possible in generic BSM scenarios such as leptoquarks. We show that this produces equivalent BSM deviations in the Pauli form factor, $F_2(s)$, at $s=(10\,\text{GeV})^2$, and report the first complete two-loop prediction of $\text{Re}\, F_2^\text{eff}(100\,\text{GeV}^2)=-268.77(50)\times 10^{-6}$ for resonant $τ$-pair production in $e^+e^-\rightarrow Υ(nS) \rightarrow$ $τ^+τ^-$, $n=1,2,3$. $\text{Re}\, F_2^\text{eff}$ can be measured from $e^-$-helicity-dependent transverse and longitudinal asymmetries in $τ$-pair events, which requires a longitudinally polarized $e^-$ beam. We discuss how Belle II asymmetry measurements could probe $a_τ^\text{BSM}$ at $10^{-6}$, assuming such a polarization upgrade of the SuperKEKB $e^+e^-$ collider, and conclude by outlining the next steps to be taken in theory and experiment along this new avenue for exploring realistic BSM effects in $a_τ$.

# Towards testing the magnetic moment of the tau at one part per million

## Key claim

If new physics explains the 4.2σ discrepancy in the muon anomalous magnetic moment, minimal flavor violation predicts a shift in the tau magnetic moment at the 10⁻⁶ level, and larger effects are possible in generic beyond-Standard-Model scenarios such as leptoquarks; this shift can be probed via the Pauli form factor at s = (10 GeV)² using electron-helicity-dependent asymmetries in e⁺e⁻ → τ⁺τ⁻ at the Υ resonances, provided a longitudinally polarized electron beam is available at SuperKEKB.

## Method

The authors computed the complete two-loop Standard-Model prediction for the real part of the effective Pauli form factor Re F₂ᵉᶠᶠ at s = 100 GeV² for resonant τ-pair production via e⁺e⁻ → Υ(nS) → τ⁺τ⁻ (n = 1,2,3). They derived the relation between Re F₂ᵉᶠᶠ and the transverse and longitudinal asymmetries A_T± and A_L±, which require a longitudinally polarized electron beam, and showed that these asymmetries isolate the F₂ contribution from the dominant F₁ term. They then demonstrated that beyond-Standard-Model contributions to a_τ at the 10⁻⁶ level produce equivalent deviations in Re F₂ᵉᶠᶠ, using the scalar leptoquark S₁ as an example model.

## Key numbers

- Re F₂ᵉᶠᶠ(100 GeV²) = −268.77(50) × 10⁻⁶ (two-loop Standard-Model prediction for resonant τ-pair production)
- a_μᵉˣᵖ − a_μˢᴹ = 2.51(59) × 10⁻⁹ (current muon g−2 discrepancy)
- a_τᴹᶠⱽ = 7.1(1.7) × 10⁻⁷ (minimal flavor violation prediction from scaling the muon discrepancy by (m_τ/m_μ)²)
- Experimental bound on a_τ: −0.052 < a_τᵉˣᵖ < 0.013 at 2σ (from LEP2 e⁺e⁻ → e⁺e⁻τ⁺τ⁻)
- Effective field theory bound: −0.007 < a_τᴮˢᴹ < 0.005 at 2σ (from global LEP/SLD analysis)
- Resonance enhancement factor |H(M_Υ)|² ≈ 100 for Υ(nS), n = 1,2,3
- Statistical error on Re F₂ᵉᶠᶠ projected at 1 × 10⁻⁵ with 40 ab⁻¹ of data and 60% selection efficiency
- Higgs branching ratio constraint: Br[h → τ⁺τ⁻]/Br[h → τ⁺τ⁻]|_SM = 1.15⁺⁰·¹⁶₋₀.₁₅
- Z axial-vector coupling constraint: g_A^τ/g_A^τ|_SM = 1.00154(128) from LEP

## Context

This work extends the asymmetry-based approach of Bernabeu et al. (2007, 2008) for measuring the tau magnetic moment at Υ resonances by providing the first complete two-loop Standard-Model prediction for the relevant effective Pauli form factor. It addresses the need for theoretical precision matching the experimental goal of 10⁻⁶, which is motivated by the muon g−2 discrepancy (Fermilab E989, 2021) and by leptoquark models that can explain flavor anomalies in B decays such as R(D(*)). The paper enables a concrete experimental program at a potential SuperKEKB polarization upgrade, while also motivating improved measurements of m_τ and M_Υ(1S) and further theoretical work on nonresonant τ-pair production including box diagrams at two-loop order.
