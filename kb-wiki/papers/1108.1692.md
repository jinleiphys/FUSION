# Nuclear Effects in Generators: the Path Forward

> The extraction of neutrino oscillation parameters requires the determination of the neutrino energy from observations of the hadronic final state. The use of nuclear targets then requires the use of event generators to isolate the interesting elementary processes and to take experimental acceptances into account. In this talk I briefly summarize the history of event generators and their use in nuclear physics, talk briefly about the generators used in the neutrino community and then discuss future necessary developments.

# Nuclear Effects in Generators: the Path Forward

## Key claim

The extraction of neutrino oscillation parameters depends critically on the reliability of event generators used to correct for nuclear effects, but current neutrino event generators have been developed with little coupling to nuclear transport theory and have not been adequately validated against electron- or photon-induced nuclear data. The widely discussed extraction of a higher axial mass from MiniBooNE data (M_A ≈ 1.2–1.3 GeV) may be an artifact of forcing a 1p-1h model to describe 2p-2h contributions that are not yet implemented in any standard neutrino event generator.

## Method

The paper reviews the history of event generators in nuclear physics, tracing development from Serber (1947) through Metropolis (1958), Bertini (1963), and Cugnon (1980), to modern Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport theory codes such as GiBUU. It contrasts these nuclear-physics-based transport codes with specialized neutrino event generators (NUANCE, NEUT, GENIE, NUWRO) that evolved independently, and identifies specific deficiencies in the latter: lack of 2p-2h multi-nucleon interactions, inadequate treatment of off-shell particle transport, and insufficient validation against electron/photonuclear data.

## Key numbers

- The MiniBooNE quasielastic cross section surplus over theoretical calculations is approximately 25%, attributed to 2p-2h events rather than true QE scattering.
- The axial mass extracted from experiments ranges from M_A ≈ 1.2–1.3 GeV (AguilarArevalo 2010, Gran 2006) up to M_A = 1.6 GeV (Benhar 2010), compared to the world-average value of M_A ≈ 1 GeV.
- In GiBUU calculations for a 12C target at 1 GeV neutrino energy, the probability to observe 2 nucleons in the final state from primary QE events alone amounts to 20% of the 1-nucleon contribution, even though the initial interaction always occurred on one nucleon only.
- The 2p-2h contributions that could increase this multi-nucleon knockout fraction are estimated to amount to about 25% of the total cross section.

## Context

This talk extends the line of work comparing neutrino event generators that began at the NUINT09 workshop, which revealed major differences between generators for both QE scattering and pion production (Boyd 2009). It argues that neutrino generators should follow the path of transport-theoretical codes like GiBUU, which have been validated across heavy-ion, hadron, electron, photon, and neutrino reactions (Buss 2011). The paper specifically criticizes the approach of Bodek (2011) to include 2p-2h effects simply by increasing the single-particle transverse response, as this produces incorrect knockout nucleon numbers. It calls for validation of neutrino generators against electron- and photon-induced nuclear data, noting that only NEUT has performed such checks (Hayato 2002) and that these were not published. The open question is whether 2p-2h effects are unambiguously observable and how their inclusion will affect neutrino energy reconstruction for oscillation parameter extraction.
