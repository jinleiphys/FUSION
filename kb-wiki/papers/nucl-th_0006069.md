# Photonuclear Reactions of Three-Nucleon Systems

> We discuss the available data for the differential and the total cross section for the photodisintegration of $^3$He and $^3$H and the corresponding inverse reactions below $E_γ= 100$ MeV by comparing with our calculations using realistic $NN$ interactions. The theoretical results agree within the errorbars with the data for the total cross sections. Excellent agreement is achieved for the angular distribution in case of $^3$He, whereas for $^3$H a discrepancy between theory and experiment is found.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their theoretical calculations using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions agree within error bars with experimental total cross section data for photodisintegration of 3He and 3H below 100 MeV, but this agreement is qualified by noting that for 3H a discrepancy between theory and experiment is found, and that for 3He above 60 MeV the measured points lie slightly above the computed curves.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev-type Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations in momentum space for the three-nucleon system, using separable Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) expansions of the Bonn A, Bonn B, and Paris NN potentials. The electromagnetic operator included electric dipole (E1) and electric quadrupole (E2) contributions via Siegert's theorem to incorporate meson exchange currents, and the final-state scattering amplitudes were obtained by solving an integral equation with cubic B-spline expansion and subtraction techniques for singularities.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Schadow 1998, Sandhas 1998, Schadow 1999) from photon energies below 20 MeV up to 100 MeV, using the same integral equation approach with realistic NN interactions. It addresses inconsistencies of up to 30% between existing experimental data sets and provides the first full final-state amplitude calculations in this energy range using realistic interactions with E1 and E2 contributions. The results enable future work on polarization observables for p-d capture, which the authors state will be published in a subsequent paper, and build upon earlier theoretical efforts by Gibson and Lehman (1975) who used Yamaguchi interactions with only E1 contributions, and by Aufleger and Drechsel (1981) and Craver et al. (1977) who used realistic interactions but without exact treatment of the three-body scattering state.
