# Photon electroproduction off nuclei in the $Δ$-resonance region

> The cross section for the $A(e,e'γ)A$ reaction is calculated, investigating the contribution from the nuclear target with respect to the radiative corrections from the electron. The reaction mechanism is studied for photon emission in the $Δ$-resonance region, varying the scattering geometry and analyzing the most favourable kinematical conditions to extract information on the nuclear system.

## Key claim

The cross section for the $A(e,e'\gamma)A$ reaction in the $\Delta$-resonance region is calculated, showing that the nuclear (full virtual Compton scattering) contribution can be separated from the electron bremsstrahlung (Bethe-Heitler) contribution only under specific kinematic conditions, particularly at backward photon scattering angles and high incoming electron energy.

## Method

The differential cross section for photon electroproduction off nuclei is computed as the coherent sum of the Bethe-Heitler (BH) amplitude, evaluated in the external field approximation using the nuclear charge form factor, and the full virtual Compton scattering (FVCS) amplitude. The FVCS amplitude is modeled within a local-density approximation to the $\Delta$-hole model for the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance, including a non-resonant background from seagull diagrams and s-wave pion production. The cross section is separated into BH, FVCS, and interference terms, and expressed in terms of four nuclear structure functions ($W_{\rm L}$, $W_{\rm T}$, $W_{\rm LT}$, $W_{\rm TT}$) that depend on the virtual photon energy $\omega$, momentum $q$, and the photon scattering angle $\theta_{\gamma}$.

## Key numbers

- Calculations performed for $^4$He at fixed $\omega = 310$ MeV, with virtual photon momentum $q$ between 330 and 480 MeV
- Three incoming electron energies studied: $E = 500$, $885$, and $2000$ MeV
- At $E = 500$ MeV, the nuclear (FVCS) contribution becomes the leading term for $\theta_{\gamma} \geq 60^{\circ}$ at small $q$
- At $E = 2000$ MeV and $q = 330$ MeV, the FVCS contribution dominates for $\theta_{\gamma} \geq 40^{\circ}$
- The $q$ range explored is between 330 and 480 MeV
- At the highest $q = 480$ MeV, the BH contribution dominates even at backward angles

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of virtual Compton scattering in the $\Delta$-resonance region (Pasquini, 1995) by including the full Bethe-Heitler bremsstrahlung contribution from the electron. It builds on earlier proposals for photon electroproduction experiments in the giant resonance region (Hubbard, Acker) and on the formulation of nuclear generalized polarizabilities (Arenhövel and Drechsel). The results identify kinematic conditions suitable for experiments at MAMI-B and CEBAF, where the pure transverse structure function $W_{\rm T}$ can be extracted at backward angles, while the longitudinal response remains difficult to isolate.
