# Application of the variational principle to a coherent-pair condensate: The HFB case

> Recently we proposed a scheme that applies the variational principle to a coherent-pair condensate in the BCS case [Phys. Rev. C 99, 014302 (2019)]. This work extends the scheme to the HFB case by allowing variation of the canonical single-particle basis. The result is equivalent to that of the so-called variation after particle-number projection in the HFB case, but now the particle number is always conserved and the time-consuming projection is avoided. Specifically, we derive the analytical expression for the gradient of the average energy with respect to changes of the canonical basis, which is then used in the family of gradient minimizers to iteratively minimize energy. In practice, we find the so-called ADAM minimizer (adaptive moments of gradient), borrowed from the machine-learning field, is very effective. The new algorithm is demonstrated in a semi-realistic example using the realistic $V_{\rm{low}{\textrm{-}}k}$ interaction and large model spaces (up to $15$ harmonic-oscillator major shells). It easily runs on a laptop, and practically the computer time cost (in the HFB case) is several times that of solving HF by the gradient minimizers. We hope the new algorithm could become a common practice because of its simplicity.

## Key claim

This work extends the variational principle applied directly to a coherent-pair condensate (VDPC) from the BCS case to the HFB case by allowing variation of the canonical single-particle basis, achieving equivalence to variation after particle-number projection (VAP) in HFB while conserving particle number exactly and avoiding the time-consuming numerical gauge-angle integration.

## Method

The authors derive an analytical expression for the gradient of the average energy with respect to changes of the canonical single-particle basis at fixed pair structure v_alpha, parameterizing basis changes as orthogonal transformations mixing pairs of canonical states. This gradient is then used in iterative gradient minimizers (steepest descent, preconditioned gradient, and ADAM from machine learning) to minimize the energy of the coherent-pair condensate. The full VDPC+HFB algorithm combines variation of v_alpha (VDPC+BCS) with variation of the canonical basis (VDPC+CB), inserting v_alpha updates every 20 iterations of the basis variation.

## Key numbers

* The computer time cost for VDPC+HFB is about 4 to 6 times that of solving HF by gradient minimizers, and for VDPC+CB alone it is about 2 to 3 times that of HF.
* The demonstration uses model spaces up to 15 harmonic-oscillator major shells with the realistic V_low-k interaction.
* The algorithm runs on a laptop with practical computational cost.

## Context

This work extends the VDPC scheme previously proposed for the BCS case by Jia (2019) to the HFB case, providing an alternative to traditional VAP methods that require numerical gauge-angle integration as used by Sheikh (2000), Anguiano (2001, 2002), Stoitsov (2007), and Hupin (2012). The new algorithm avoids the computational expense of numerical projection noted by Wang (2014) and offers a simpler alternative to the Lipkin-Nogami prescription for approximate particle-number restoration developed by Lipkin (1960) and Nogami (1964). This enables practical VAP-equivalent calculations in large model spaces on modest computing resources.
