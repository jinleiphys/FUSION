# On the nature of light scalar mesons from their large $N_c$ behavior

> We show how to obtain information about the states of an effective field theory in terms of the underlying fundamental theory. In particular we analyze the spectroscopic nature of meson resonances from the meson-meson scattering amplitudes of the QCD low energy effective theory, combined with the expansion in the large number of colors. The vectors follow a qqbar behavior, whereas the sigma, kappa and f_0(980) scalars disappear for large N_c, in support of a qqqbarqbar-like nature. The a_0 shows a similar pattern, but the uncertainties are large enough to accommodate both interpretations.

# On the nature of light scalar mesons from their large \(N_c\) behavior

## Key claim

The large \(N_c\) scaling of unitarized chiral perturbation theory amplitudes shows that the \(\sigma\), \(\kappa\), and \(f_0(980)\) scalar mesons disappear for large \(N_c\), supporting a \(\bar q \bar q qq\)-like (tetraquark or two-meson) nature rather than a \(\bar q q\) interpretation; however, the \(a_0(980)\) shows a similar pattern but with uncertainties large enough to accommodate both interpretations, especially when the renormalization scale is taken at the lower end of its estimated range (0.5 GeV).

## Method

The author uses one-loop \(O(p^4)\) meson-meson scattering amplitudes from Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT), unitarized via the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) to extend the description up to 1.2 GeV while preserving unitarity and the low-energy chiral expansion. The large \(N_c\) scaling of QCD is then applied to the chiral parameters \(L_i(\mu)\) and the pion decay constant \(f_0\), with the scaling applied at a renormalization scale \(\mu\) estimated between 0.5 and 1 GeV, and the pole positions of resonances are tracked as \(N_c\) is increased from 3 to 10 or 25.

## Key numbers

- The \(\rho\) and \(K^*(892)\) vector mesons show a \(\bar q q\) behavior: their masses remain constant with \(N_c\) and their widths scale as \(\Gamma_{N_c}/\Gamma_3 \sim 3/N_c\), with the agreement holding quantitatively within the uncertainty band \(\mu = 0.5-1\) GeV.
- The \(\sigma\) and \(\kappa\) poles show a width that grows with \(N_c\), in conflict with a \(\bar q q\) interpretation; their imaginary parts scale as \(\mbox{Im}\, t \sim O(1/N_c^2)\) and real parts as \(\mbox{Re}\, t \sim O(1/N_c)\) across the whole resonant region.
- For the \(f_0(980)\), the sharp dip in the amplitude vanishes at large \(N_c\), and for \(N_c > 5\) the amplitude follows a \(1/N_c^2\) scaling compatible with \(\bar q \bar q qq\) states or glueballs.
- The \(a_0(980)\) peak disappears at large \(N_c\) when scaling is applied at \(\mu = 0.55-1\) GeV, but the peak does not vanish if \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV is used, leaving the \(\bar q q\) interpretation possible in an extreme corner of the uncertainty band.

## Context

This work extends the longstanding controversy over the nature of the lightest scalar mesons by combining Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) with the large \(N_c\) expansion of QCD, a technique pioneered by 't Hooft (1974). It builds on prior unitarization methods such as the IAM developed by Dobado et al. (1996) and Oller et al. (1997), and on earlier large \(N_c\) arguments using leading-order unitarized amplitudes with a regularization scale by Oller et al. (1997) and Harada et al. (2003). The results confirm and extend the finding that the \(\sigma\) and \(\kappa\) are not \(\bar q q\) states, and suggest a dominant tetraquark or two-meson component for the \(\sigma\), possibly with glueball mixing, while leaving the \(a_0(980)\) nature ambiguous. The techniques presented could be applied to other frameworks such as Heavy Baryon ChPT or the strongly interacting symmetry breaking sector of the Standard Model.
