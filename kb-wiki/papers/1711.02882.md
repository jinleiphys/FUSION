# The Physics Case for the $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 10$ GeV Energy Region

> There are indications that the beam energy region $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 10$ GeV for heavy-ion collisions is an interesting one. The final state has the highest net baryon density at this beam energy. A transition from a baryon dominated to a meson dominated final state takes place around this beam energy. Ratios of strange particles to mesons show clear and pronounced maxima around this beam energy. The theoretical interpretation can be clarified by covering fully this energy region. In particular the strangeness content needs to be determined, data covering the full phase space ($4 π$) would be helpful to establish the properties of this energy region.

# The Physics Case for the \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 10\) GeV Energy Region

## Key claim

The beam energy region around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 10\) GeV for heavy-ion collisions is marked by several coincident phenomena, maximum net baryon density, a transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated final states, and pronounced maxima in strange particle-to-pion ratios, but the theoretical interpretation remains incomplete without full phase space (\(4\pi\)) data, particularly to determine the strangeness content and clarify the nature of a possible phase transition or quarkyonic phase.

## Method

The paper uses a statistical-thermal model analysis of particle yields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, assuming all hadronic resonances listed in the Particle Data Group tables are in thermal and chemical equilibrium at chemical freeze-out. The freeze-out parameters (temperature \(T\), baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\), and volume \(V\)) are extracted from experimental data across beam energies from 1 GeV to LHC energies, and compared to constant-ratio curves for entropy density over \(T^3\) and energy per hadron. The model calculates particle yields including resonance decay contributions, which can account for over 80% of observed pions.

## Key numbers

- The transition from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated final state occurs at \(T = 151\) MeV, \(\mu_B = 327\) MeV, and \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11\) GeV.
- The maximum net baryon density along the freeze-out curve occurs just below \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 10\) GeV.
- In Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy of 5.02 TeV, approximately 30,000 particles are produced on average (including neutral particles).
- Over 80% of observed pions can originate from resonance decays depending on temperature.

## Context

This work extends the statistical-thermal model analysis of chemical freeze-out previously applied by Cleymans (1999), Broniowski (2001), and Akkelin (2001) to the Bjorken scaling expansion, and builds on the freeze-out parameterization comparisons by Wheaton. The paper highlights that the pronounced "horn" in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio and strong variation in the \(\Lambda/\pi\) ratio observed by the NA49 collaboration around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 10\) GeV are not seen in \(p-p\) collisions, and connects these to the baryon-to-meson transition within the thermal model framework. The results motivate full \(4\pi\) coverage at proposed new facilities to determine thermal parameters and investigate the possible existence of a quarkyonic phase as discussed by McLerran.
