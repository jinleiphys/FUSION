# Quantum simulations of strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma

> A strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) of heavy constituent quasiparticles is studied by a path-integral Monte-Carlo method, which improves the corresponding classical simulations by extending them to the quantum regime. It is shown that this method is able to reproduce the lattice equation of state and also yields valuable insight into the internal structure of the QGP. The results indicate that the QGP reveals liquid-like rather than gas-like properties. At temperatures just above the critical one it was found that bound quark-antiquark states still survive. These states are bound by effective string-like forces. Quantum effects turned out to be of prime importance in these simulations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a path-integral Monte Carlo (PIMC) method, which extends classical molecular dynamics simulations of a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) to the quantum regime, can reproduce the lattice equation of state and reveal liquid-like rather than gas-like properties, but only after fitting the quasiparticle density as a free parameter to achieve agreement with lattice pressure data.

## Method

The authors model the QGP as a nonrelativistic system of heavy dressed quarks, antiquarks, and gluons interacting via a color Coulomb potential, with color degrees of freedom treated classically via Wong's dynamics. They use a path-integral Monte Carlo approach that represents each quasiparticle by a chain of "beads" and employs the color Kelbg potential to incorporate quantum corrections to the pair interaction, while Fermi and Bose statistics are handled through determinants and permanents in the density matrix. The simulations are performed in a canonical ensemble at zero baryon density with SU(2) color group, using quasiparticle masses parametrized from lattice data and a fitted density of \( n(T) = 0.24 T^3 \) to match the lattice equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Quasiparticle mass parametrization: \( m(T)/T_c = 0.9/(T/T_c - 1) + 3.45 + 0.4 T/T_c \) with critical temperature \( T_c = 175 \) MeV.
- Quasiparticle density fitted to \( n(T) = 0.24 T^3 \), compared to \( n(T)/T^3 = 6.3 \) used in classical simulations of Shuryak (2004) and \( n(T)/T^3 \approx 1.4 \) in quasiparticle models of Peshier (1996) and Ivanov (2005).
- Degeneracy parameter \( \chi_a = n_a \lambda_a^3 \) varies from 0.1 to 2 over the temperature range \( 1 < T/T_c < 3 \).
- Plasma coupling parameter \( \Gamma \) is of order unity, indicating strongly coupled Coulomb liquid behavior.
- Mean interparticle distance satisfies \( T r_s(T) = 1 \), with \( \sigma = 1/T_c = 1.1 \) fm.

## Context

This work extends the classical molecular dynamics simulations of Shuryak (2004) for a color Coulomb interacting QGP by incorporating quantum effects through path-integral Monte Carlo, a method previously applied to strongly coupled electrodynamic plasmas by Filinov (2001) and Bonitz (2003). The approach enables first-principles study of bound quark-antiquark states and liquid-like structure in the QGP, and it opens the possibility of extending such simulations to finite baryon chemical potential and out-of-equilibrium dynamics where lattice QCD methods fail.
