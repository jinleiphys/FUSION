# Back-to-back isolated photon-quarkonium production at the LHC and the transverse-momentum-dependent distributions of the gluons in the proton

> The study of a quasi back-to-back isolated pair made of a heavy quarkonium, such as a J/psi or a Upsilon, and a photon produced in proton-proton collisions at the LHC, is probably the optimal way to get right away a first experimental determination of two gluon transverse-momentum-dependent distributions (TMDs) in an unpolarized proton, f1^g and h1^perp,g the latter giving the distribution of linearly polarized gluons. To substantiate this, we calculate the transverse-momentum-dependent effects that arise in the process under study and discuss the feasibility of their measurements.

## Key claim

The study of quasi back-to-back isolated photon plus heavy quarkonium (J/ψ or Υ) production in proton-proton collisions at the LHC is probably the optimal way to obtain a first experimental determination of two gluon transverse-momentum-dependent distributions (TMDs) in an unpolarized proton, f1^g and h1^perp,g, but the feasibility depends on the dominance of color-singlet contributions, which is well established for Υ+γ but requires more precise knowledge of color-octet NRQCD matrix elements for J/ψ+γ.

## Method

The author calculates the TMD-factorized cross section for the process p(P_A)+p(P_B) → Q(P_Q) + γ(P_γ) + X, where Q is a C=-1 quarkonium (J/ψ or Υ) produced nearly back-to-back with a photon, such that the momentum imbalance q_T = P_{Q,T} + P_{γ,T} is small. The cross section is expressed in terms of convolutions of gluon TMDs with the hard-scattering amplitude for g g → Q γ, yielding terms proportional to cos(0φ), cos(2φ), and cos(4φ) that isolate contributions from f1^g f1^g, f1^g h1^perp,g, and h1^perp,g h1^perp,g respectively. The author then defines q_T-dependent observables S^{(n)}_{q_T} for n=0,2,4 to extract these TMDs from azimuthal modulations of the cross section.

## Key numbers

- The process is studied at LHC center-of-mass energies √s = 7 and 8 TeV, with yields large enough to perform analyses using already existing data.
- For the Υ+γ process, predictions are shown for Q = 20 GeV, Y = 0, θ = π/2, and √s = 14 TeV.
- The gluon TMDs are probed at x values on the order of 10^{-3}.
- The cross section formula contains the factor 4α_s^2 α^2 e_Q^2 |R_0(0)|^2 / (3 M_Q^2), where R_0(0) is the quarkonium radial wave function at the origin and e_Q is the heavy-quark charge in units of proton charge.
- The bound on h1^perp,g is given by (p_T^2 / (2 M_p^2)) |h1^perp,g(x, p_T^2)| ≤ f1^g(x, p_T^2).

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions to measure gluon TMDs through dijet production in pp collisions (Boer, 2009), which suffers from TMD-factorization breaking effects (Rogers, 2010), and through heavy-quark or dijet electroproduction at a future EIC facility. It also builds on proposals for diphoton production (Qiu, 2011) and C=+1 quarkonium production (Boer, 2012; Ma, 2014), which face background or kinematic challenges at the LHC. The photon-quarkonium channel avoids factorization-breaking issues because one final-state particle is colorless, and the process is dominated by gluon-gluon fusion, enabling extraction of gluon TMDs from existing LHC data at √s = 7 and 8 TeV. Future work could extend this to J/ψ+Z production (studied by ATLAS, 2014) to probe TMD evolution at higher scales Q ≃ m_Z.
