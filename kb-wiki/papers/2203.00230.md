# Chandrasekhar-Kendall-Woltjer-Taylor state in a resistive plasma

> We give a criterion for the Chandrasekhar-Kendall-Woltjer-Taylor (CKWT) state in a resistive plasma. We find that the lowest momentum (longest wavelength) of the initial helicity amplitudes of magnetic fields are the key to the CKWT state which can be reached if one helicity is favored over the other. This indicates that the imbalance between two helicities at the lowest momentum or longest wavelength in the initial conditions is essential to the CKWT state. A few examples of initial conditions for helicity amplitudes are taken to support the above statement both analytically and numerically.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Chandrasekhar-Kendall-Woltjer-Taylor (CKWT) state in a resistive plasma can be reached if and only if one helicity is favored over the other at the lowest momentum (longest wavelength) in the initial helicity amplitudes of the magnetic field; this condition is necessary and sufficient for analytic initial helicity amplitudes with a positive lower momentum bound, but the CKWT state cannot be reached if the integration range includes zero momentum.

## Method

The authors expand the magnetic field and vector potential in vector spherical harmonics (VSH), which are eigenfunctions of the curl operator, and solve the diffusion equation for the expansion coefficients. They then compute the time evolution of the observables magnetic energy W, the squared vector potential Q, and magnetic helicity H, and analyze the long-time limit of the quantity WQ/H^2 - 1 = tan^2(theta), where theta is the average angle between the magnetic field and vector potential. The analysis considers initial helicity amplitudes g_+(0,k) and g_-(0,k) as delta functions, step functions (two-band and multi-band), analytic functions, and a special non-analytic function.

## Context

This work extends the relaxation theory of Taylor (1974, 1986), who proposed that magnetic relaxation in resistive plasmas is caused by small-scale turbulence, a hypothesis that lacked conclusive experimental and theoretical support. The authors build on the VSH method used by Hirono (2015) and Xia (2016) for chiral plasmas, and on the work of Qin (2012) who studied general conditions for the CKWT state using inequalities. The paper provides a criterion that resolves the role of long-wavelength structure in reaching the CKWT state, enabling future studies of magnetic relaxation without relying on Taylor's conjecture or the chiral magnetic effect.
