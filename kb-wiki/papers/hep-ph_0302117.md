# Renormalization of relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory and power counting

> We discuss a renormalization scheme for relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory which provides a simple and consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams. The method involves finite subtractions of dimensionally regularized diagrams beyond the standard $\bar{\rm MS}$ scheme of chiral perturbation theory to remove contributions violating the power counting. This is achieved by a suitable renormalization of the parameters of the most general effective Lagrangian. In addition to simplicity our method has the benefit that it can be easily applied to multiloop diagrams. As an application we discuss the mass and the scalar form factor of the nucleon and compare the results with the expressions of the infrared regularization of Becher and Leutwyler.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a renormalization scheme for relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory that restores a simple power counting for renormalized diagrams by performing finite subtractions beyond the standard modified minimal subtraction scheme, with the condition that the subtracted terms are local polynomials in external momenta and can therefore be absorbed into counterterms of the most general effective Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors apply an "extended on-mass-shell" (EOMS) renormalization scheme to dimensionally regularized loop integrals in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory. They subtract from the integrand those terms in a series expansion around the on-shell point that violate the naive power counting, where the subtraction terms are analytic in small parameters and free of infrared singularities. The method is illustrated on a one-loop integral of the type appearing in the nucleon self-energy and then applied to calculate the nucleon mass at order \(q^4\).

## Key numbers

- The pion-decay constant in the chiral limit is \(F_\pi = F[1 + \mathcal{O}(\hat{m})] = 92.4\) MeV.
- The renormalized one-loop integral \(H_R\) for the case of finite pion mass is given by \(H_R = -\frac{1}{8\pi^2} \frac{\alpha \sqrt{1-\Omega^2}}{1+2\alpha\Omega+\alpha^2} \arccos(-\Omega) - \frac{1}{8\pi^2} \frac{\alpha(\alpha+\Omega)}{1+2\alpha\Omega+\alpha^2} \ln(\alpha)\), where \(\alpha = M/m\) and \(\Omega = (p^2 - m^2 - M^2)/(2mM)\).
- The subtracted term needed to restore power counting for the one-loop integral is \(H_{\text{subtr}} = -2\bar{\lambda} + 1/(16\pi^2) + O(n-4)\), where \(\bar{\lambda} = m^{n-4}/(4\pi)^2 \{ 1/(n-4) - \frac{1}{2}[\ln(4\pi) + \Gamma'(1) + 1] \}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory framework of Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988) by providing a renormalization scheme that restores power counting, which was problematic in the original relativistic formulation due to the nonvanishing nucleon mass in the chiral limit. The method is compared to the infrared regularization of Becher and Leutwyler (1999), which achieves power counting by splitting loop integrals into an infrared part and a remainder, but the EOMS scheme is simpler and can be easily applied to multiloop diagrams. This enables systematic calculations of nucleon properties such as the nucleon mass and scalar form factor within a fully relativistic framework with consistent power counting.
