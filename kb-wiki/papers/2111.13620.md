# Heavy-Light Susceptibilities in a Strongly Coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Quark number susceptibilities as computed in lattice QCD are commonly believed to provide insights into the microscopic structure of QCD matter, in particular its degrees of freedom. We generalize a previously constructed partonic $T$-matrix approach to finite chemical potential to calculate various susceptibilities, in particular for configurations containing a heavy charm quark. At vanishing chemical potential and moderate temperatures, this approach predicts large collisional widths of partons generated by dynamically formed hadronic resonance states which lead to transport parameters characteristic for a strongly coupled system. The quark chemical potential dependence is implemented into the propagators and the in-medium color potential, where two newly introduced parameters for the thermal and screening masses are fixed through a fit to the baryon number susceptibility, $χ^B_2$. With this setup, we calculate heavy-light susceptibilities without further tuning; the results qualitatively agree with the lattice-QCD (lQCD) data for both $χ^{uc}_{11}$ and $χ^{uc}_{22}$. This implies that the lQCD results are compatible with a significant content of broad $D$-meson and charm-light diquark bound states in a moderately hot QGP.

## Key claim

The paper claims that lattice QCD results for heavy-light susceptibilities are compatible with a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma containing significant broad D-meson and charm-light diquark bound states near the pseudocritical temperature, but this compatibility depends on the specific strongly coupled scenario (SCS) of the T-matrix approach and the imposition of a constraint (χ^{uc}_{13} = χ^{uc}_{11}) motivated by lattice data to resolve numerical degeneracy.

## Method

The authors extended a previously constructed thermodynamic T-matrix approach, which self-consistently solves Dyson-Schwinger equations for parton propagators and two-body T-matrices, to finite light-quark and charm-quark chemical potentials. Two new parameters (b_m for thermal parton masses and b_s for screening mass) were introduced to capture the leading-order chemical potential dependence and were fixed by fitting to lattice QCD data for the baryon number susceptibility χ^B_2. The pressure was then computed on a grid of chemical potential values using the Luttinger-Ward functional with Matrix-Log resummation, and susceptibilities were extracted as coefficients of a polynomial fit to the pressure.

## Key numbers

* The charm-quark widths in the T-matrix approach are Γ_c ≈ 0.5 GeV, which are essential for agreement with lattice data.
* At μ_q/T = 1, the increase in the Debye screening mass m_d amounts to only ∼10-20% relative to zero chemical potential.
* The long-range part of the in-medium potential is suppressed by only ∼5-10% at finite chemical potential.
* The T-matrix results for χ^c_2 show good agreement with Hot-QCD lattice results, while Wuppertal-Bielefeld results (computed with N_f=2+1+1 flavors including dynamical charm) are somewhat lower.
* The heavy-light susceptibilities χ^{uc}_{11}/χ^c_2 and χ^{uc}_{22}/χ^c_2 show fair semi-quantitative agreement with lattice QCD data from Mukherjee (2015).
* The strongly coupled scenario produces a heavy-quark diffusion coefficient 2π T D_s ≈ 2-5.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic T-matrix approach of Liu (2016, 2017) to finite chemical potential, which previously had been constrained by lattice QCD data for heavy-quark free energies, Euclidean quarkonium correlator ratios, and the equation of state at zero chemical potential. The results contradict perturbative hard-thermal loop calculations by Haque (2014) where off-diagonal susceptibilities vanish, and differ from mean-field hadron-quark crossover model predictions by Miyahara (2017) which found positive χ^{uc}_{11}. The approach enables future deployment of the T-matrix formalism into the finite-μ_q plane of the QCD phase diagram for studying transport and spectral properties relevant to heavy-ion collisions at lower energies and neutron star mergers.
