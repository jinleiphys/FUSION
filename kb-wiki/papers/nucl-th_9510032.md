# First Order Variational Calculation of Form Factor in a Scalar Nucleon--Meson Theory

> We investigate a relativistic quantum field theory in the particle representation using a non-perturbative variational technique. The theory is that of two massive scalar particles, `nucleons' and `mesons', interacting via a Yukawa coupling. We calculate the general Euclidean Green function involving two external nucleons and an arbitrary number of external mesons in the quenched approximation for the nucleons. The non-perturbative renormalization and truncation is done in a consistent manner and results in the same variational functional independent of the number of external mesons. We check that the calculation agrees with one-loop perturbation theory for small couplings. As an illustration the special case of meson absorption on the nucleon is considered in detail. We derive the radius of the dressed particle and numerically investigate the vertex function after analytic continuation to Minkowski space.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a first-order cumulant (variational) calculation of the $(2+n)$-point Green function in a scalar Yukawa theory yields a consistent, truncatable result where the same variational parameters determined from the nucleon propagator ($n=0$) suffice for all $n$, and the resulting vertex function agrees with one-loop perturbation theory for small couplings.

## Method

The authors use Feynman's variational method in the particle representation of a scalar nucleon-meson theory with Yukawa coupling, working in Euclidean space and the quenched approximation for nucleons. They expand the nucleon path in Fourier components, choose a quadratic trial action with variational parameters $A_k$ and $\rho_k = \lambda A_k$, and evaluate the path integrals analytically to first order in the cumulant expansion (Jensen's inequality). The external nucleon legs are truncated by taking the limit of infinite proper time, using Poisson summation to handle the infinite sums, and the mass renormalization condition from the nucleon propagator is applied to obtain the final truncated $(2+n)$-point function.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon mass renormalization relation is given by $M_0^2 + 2\Omega = M^2[1 - (1-\lambda)^2] + \frac{g^2}{4\pi^2} \int_0^\infty \frac{d\sigma}{\mu^2(\sigma)} \int_0^1 du \, e(m\mu(\sigma), \frac{\lambda\sigma M}{\mu(\sigma)}, u)$, where $M_0$ is the bare mass and $M$ the physical mass.
* The truncated $(2+n)$-point function contains a factor $N_0 = \frac{1}{A(0)} \exp(1 - \frac{1}{A(0)})$ from the zero-mode contribution.
* The tree-level result is recovered in the zero-coupling limit as $G_{2,n}^{\rm tree}(p,p';\{q\}) = \frac{1}{2g} \sum_{\mathcal{P}(\{q\})} \prod_{i=1}^{n+1} \frac{2g}{p_i^2 + M^2_{\rm phys}}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous variational calculations of the nucleon propagator (Schreiber, Rosenfelder 1994-1995) and zeroth-order cumulant results for $(2+n)$-point functions (Schreiber, Rosenfelder, Alexandrou 1994) to first order in the cumulant expansion, improving upon the gaussian form factors obtained at zeroth order. The method adapts Feynman's variational treatment of the polaron (Feynman 1955) to relativistic field theory, specifically the Wick-Cutkosky model (Wick 1954, Cutkosky 1954). The approach enables non-perturbative calculation of vertex functions and form factors in a scalar nucleon-meson theory, with the specific example of meson absorption on the nucleon worked out in detail, including numerical investigation after analytic continuation to Minkowski space.
