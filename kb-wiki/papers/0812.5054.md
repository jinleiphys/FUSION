# Renormalization Group Equation and QCD Coupling Constant in the Presence of SU(3) Chromo-Electric Field

> We solve renormalization group equation in QCD in the presence of SU(3) constant chromo-electric field E^a with arbitrary color index a=1,2,...8 and find that the QCD coupling constant α_s depends on two independent casimir/gauge invariants C_1=[E^aE^a] and C_2=[d_{abc}E^aE^bE^c]^2 instead of one gauge invariant C_1=[E^aE^a]. The βfunction is derived from the one-loop effective action. This coupling constant may be useful to study hadron formation from color flux tubes/strings at high energy colliders and to study quark-gluon plasma formation at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The QCD coupling constant \(\alpha_s\) in a constant SU(3) chromo-electric field depends on two independent gauge invariants, \(C_1 = [E^a E^a]\) and \(C_2 = [d_{abc} E^a E^b E^c]^2\), rather than only on \(C_1\); however, the one-loop result is under control only for asymptotically large values of \(C_1\) or for specific ranges of the angular parameter \(\theta\) that are close to the maximum allowed range.

## Method

The renormalization group equation for the effective action in the background field method was solved in the presence of a constant SU(3) chromo-electric field \(E^a\). The \(\beta\) function was derived from the one-loop effective action for gluons and massless quarks, using the eigenvalues \(\lambda_j\) of the matrices \(f^{abc}E^c\) (adjoint representation) and \(T^a_{ij}E^a\) (fundamental representation). The resulting running coupling \(\alpha_s(\lambda_j)\) was expressed in terms of the two Casimir invariants \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) via the angular parameter \(\theta\) defined by \(\cos 3\theta = -1 + 6C_2/C_1^3\) for gluons and \(\cos^2 3\theta = 3C_2/C_1^3\) for quarks.

## Key numbers

* For gluons, the one-loop \(\beta\) function coefficient is \(\bar{\beta}_0^g = 11/(32\pi^2)\), giving \(\bar{\beta}(g) = -\bar{\beta}_0^g g^3\).
* For quarks, the one-loop \(\beta\) function coefficient is \(\bar{\beta}_0^q = -1/(48\pi^2)\).
* The maximum allowed range for the angular parameter \(\theta\) in the adjoint representation is \(0 \le \theta \le 2\pi/3\).
* The maximum allowed range for \(\theta\) in the fundamental representation is \(\pi/6 \le \theta \le \pi/2\).
* For a realistic example with \(\Lambda = 0.2\) GeV, \(g = 3\), and \(C_1 = 1000\) GeV\(^4\) (LHC scale), the allowed \(\theta\) range for gluons is \(0.0021 \le \theta \le 2.092\), very close to the maximum range \(0 \le \theta \le 2.094\).
* For the same parameters in the fundamental representation, the allowed \(\theta\) range is \(0.526 \le \theta \le 1.569\), very close to the maximum range \(0.5236 \le \theta \le 1.571\).

## Context

This work extends the standard perturbative QCD running coupling to the case of a non-Abelian background field, going beyond the usual dependence on a single gauge invariant \(C_1\). It builds on the background field method of 't Hooft (1970s) and Abbott (1981), and on the Schwinger mechanism for particle production from strong fields (Schwinger, 1951; Heisenberg and Euler, 1936). The result is intended for use in modeling hadron formation from color flux tubes in PYTHIA (Sjostrand et al.) and quark-gluon plasma formation at RHIC and LHC via the color glass condensate framework (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994).
