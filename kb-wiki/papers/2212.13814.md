# Neutral pion mass in warm magnetized medium within Linear Sigma Model coupled to Quarks $\left(\text{LSM}_q\right)$ framework

> We study the neutral pion mass in the presence of an external arbitrary magnetic field in the framework of the linear sigma model coupled to quark (LSMq) at finite temperature. In doing so, we have calculated the pion self-energy, constructed the dispersion equation via re-summation, and solved the dispersion relation at zero three momentum limit. In calculating the pion mass, we have included meson self-coupling's thermal and magnetic contribution and approximate chiral order parameter $v_0$. We report that the $π^0$ mass decreases with the magnetic field and increases with temperature.

## Key claim

The neutral pion mass decreases monotonically with increasing magnetic field strength and increases with temperature, but this behavior depends on whether bare couplings, one-loop corrected meson couplings, or one-loop corrected couplings plus quantum-corrected effective potential are used in the calculation.

## Method

The authors computed the neutral pion self-energy within the Linear Sigma Model coupled to quarks (LSMq) at finite temperature and arbitrary external magnetic field, including contributions from charged pion loops, neutral pion loops, sigma meson loops, and quark-antiquark loops. They constructed the dispersion relation via re-summation and solved it in the zero three-momentum limit to extract the pion mass, using the imaginary time formalism for thermal effects and Landau level summation for magnetic field effects.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum neutral pion mass set to \(m_{\pi} = 0.14\) GeV as input parameter.
- The sigma meson mass is given by \(M^2_{\sigma}(v^{\prime}_0) = 2a^2 + 3m^2_{\pi}\), with \(a = \sqrt{(m^2_{\sigma} - 3m^2_{\pi})/2}\).
- The quark mass is \(M_f = g v_0^{\prime}\) with \(v_0^{\prime} = \sqrt{(a^2 + m^2_{\pi})/\lambda}\).
- The charged pion mass in magnetic field is \(m_{\ell,b} = \sqrt{(2\ell + 1)|eB| + m_b^2}\) for Landau level \(\ell\).

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature calculations of neutral pion mass in a magnetic field using the LSMq model by the same authors, now incorporating finite temperature effects. It builds on lattice QCD results from Ding (2020) showing monotonic decrease of \(\pi^0\) mass with magnetic field, and earlier LSMq work by Ayala (2020) in the strong field limit \(|eB| \gg m_{\pi^0}^2\). The study also relates to NJL model calculations using magnetic field independent regularization by Avancini (2015, 2016) and chiral perturbation theory work by Andersen (2012) at finite temperature and magnetic field.
