# Nuclear force with LapH smearing

> The nuclear forces are determined by combining the HAL QCD method and a new type of source smearing technique. The new smearing is a projection to a space spanned by the lowest-lying eigenvectors of the free Laplacian operator on a lattice, which enables efficient calculation of hadron correlators at an affordable cost by utilizing the hadron-level momentum conservations. We find that this new approach reduces the statistical and systematic errors in the resultant nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The free Laplacian Heaviside (fLapH) smearing technique, when combined with the HAL QCD method, reduces both statistical and systematic errors in the determination of nuclear forces from lattice QCD, but the improvement is demonstrated only at a heavy pion mass of approximately 702 MeV and on a relatively small lattice (32^3 x 64).

## Method

The authors compute nucleon four-point correlators on (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD gauge configurations using the HAL QCD method, which extracts the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function and defines an energy-independent non-local potential. They introduce a new source smearing operator that projects quark fields onto the lowest-lying eigenvectors of the free Laplacian operator, effectively imposing a momentum cutoff, and apply it to determine the central and tensor potentials in the ^1S_0 and ^3S_1+^3D_1 channels at leading order in the derivative expansion.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0907(13) fm (a^-1 ≈ 2176 MeV)
- Pion mass: m_π ≈ 702 MeV; nucleon mass: m_N ≈ 1581 MeV
- Number of eigenmodes tested: N_l = 1, 7, 19, corresponding to momentum cutoffs Λ = 0 MeV, 2π/L = 427 MeV, and 2√2π/L = 604 MeV respectively
- Computational cost scaling: fLapH method scales as O(N_l^3), while covariant LapH scales as O(N_l^4)
- Number of measurements: 399 configurations times 2 (forward/backward time averaging)

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method previously developed by Ishii (2006), Aoki (2009), and Ishii (2012) for determining nuclear forces from lattice QCD, which had difficulty achieving precision for nucleon-nucleon systems due to light quark masses. The fLapH smearing is a variant of the covariant LapH smearing introduced by the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (2009), but uses the free Laplacian instead of the gauge-covariant Laplacian, enabling momentum conservation that reduces computational cost. The results enable future high-precision determinations of S-wave nuclear forces and can be extended to P-wave baryon interactions including spin-orbit forces.
