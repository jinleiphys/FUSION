# On the intersection of the shell, collective and cluster models of atomic nuclei II: Symmetry-breaking and large deformations

> We discuss the role of the broken symmetries in the connection of the shell, collective and cluster models. The cluster-shell competition is described in terms of cold quantum phases. Stable quasi-dynamical U(3) symmetry is found for specific large deformations for a Nilsson-type Hamiltonian.

# On the intersection of the shell, collective and cluster models of atomic nuclei II: Symmetry-breaking and large deformations

## Key claim

The quasi-dynamical (effective) U(3) symmetry survives for specific large deformations even under realistic Nilsson-type Hamiltonians that include spin-orbit and l² terms, provided the deformation is large enough that the L-S coupling recovers and the quadrupole interaction dominates. The authors find stable quasi-dynamical U(3) symmetry for shape isomers with commensurable axis ratios, but only when the deformation is sufficiently large; for smaller deformations the effective quantum numbers must be calculated via expansion of Nilsson states in the asymptotic basis.

## Method

The authors combine the Nilsson model with the concept of quasi-dynamical SU(3) symmetry: they systematically vary the input quadrupole deformation parameters (β_in, γ_in) for a Nilsson-model Hamiltonian, compute the resulting effective U(3) quantum numbers, translate these into output quadrupole deformations (β_out, γ_out), and check for self-consistency and stability against small changes in input parameters. Shape isomers are identified as horizontal plateaus in the output deformation versus input deformation curves, rather than energy minima. The method is applied to the ²⁰Ne nucleus as an illustrative case.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of ²⁰Ne: effective U(3) quantum numbers [11,5,4], β = 0.43, γ = 7.6°, axis ratio 1.5:1.1:1
- Superdeformed state: effective U(3) [14,10,0], β = 0.75, γ = 43.9°, axis ratio 2.4:2.0:1
- Hyperdeformed state: effective U(3) [24,3,1], β = 1.24, γ = 4.5°, axis ratio 3.1:1.2:1
- Linear α-chain state: effective U(3) [40,0,0], β = 1.85, γ = 0.0°, axis ratio 5.0:1.0:1
- For the ground band of ²⁰Ne, shell model calculation by Vargas et al gave control parameters y = 0.99, z = 0.77; cluster model calculation by Yepez-Martinez et al gave v = 1.00, x = 0.78
- Sugawara-Tanabe et al found L-S coupled spherical wavefunction components exceed 85% of total wavefunction for superdeformed shapes

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) connection between shell, collective, and cluster models first established by Elliott (1958) and Wildermuth and Bacher (1958) from single-major-shell spherical basis to multi-major-shell and large-deformation regimes. It builds on the authors' previous paper (Cseh and Darai, 2014) that addressed vertical extension via major shell excitations. The quasi-dynamical symmetry concept follows the work of Rowe (2004) and Jarrio et al (2001). The phase diagram approach connects to the semimicroscopic algebraic cluster model (SACM) and to earlier phase-transition studies by Fraser et al (2008) and Rowe and Turner (2005). The results enable systematic classification of superdeformed and hyperdeformed states in terms of effective U(3) quantum numbers and provide a unified framework for cluster-shell competition across the nuclear chart.
