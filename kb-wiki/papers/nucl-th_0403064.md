# Spin-5/2 resonance contributions to the pion-induced reactions for energies $\sqrt{s}\leqslant$2.0 GeV

> The spin-$\ffh$ resonance effects are studied within the coupled channel effective Lagrangian model for baryon resonance analysis. We extend our previous hadronic calculations to incorporate the $D_{15}$, $F_{15}$, $D_{35}$, $F_{35}$ states. While the effect of the spin-$\ffh$ resonances to the $ηN$, $K Λ$, and $K Σ$ reactions are small, the contribution to the $ωN$ is found to be important. The results for the 'conventional' and Pascalutsa-like spin-$\ffh$ descriptions are discussed. \PACS{{11.80.-m},{13.75.Gx},{14.20.Gk},{13.30.Gk}}

## Key claim

The inclusion of spin-5/2 resonances ($D_{15}$, $F_{15}$, $D_{35}$, $F_{35}$) in the coupled-channel effective Lagrangian model yields small contributions to $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, and $K\Sigma$ reactions, but important contributions to the $\omega N$ final state, with the conventional coupling prescription providing a better overall description of the data than the Pascalutsa-like prescription.

## Method

The authors extended their previous hadronic coupled-channel model (the Giessen model) by incorporating spin-5/2 resonances and solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the K-matrix approximation for partial-wave amplitudes up to total spin $J=5/2$, isospin $I=1/2$ and $3/2$, and parity $P=(-1)^{J\pm 1/2}$. The interaction potential was approximated by tree-level Feynman diagrams from effective Lagrangians, with spin-5/2 couplings implemented in both a conventional Rarita-Schwinger form and a Pascalutsa gauge-invariant form, and all available pion-induced reaction data up to $\sqrt{s}=2.0$ GeV were simultaneously fitted.

## Key numbers

- Total $\chi^2$ of 2.60 for the conventional ($C$) fit and 3.65 for the Pascalutsa ($P$) fit across all channels
- $\chi^2_{\pi\pi}=2.60$ ($C$) and 3.80 ($P$) for elastic $\pi N$ partial waves (excluding $D_{35}$)
- $\chi^2_{\pi 2\pi}=7.63$ ($C$) and 10.06 ($P$) for the $2\pi N$ channel
- $\chi^2_{\pi\omega}=1.23$ ($C$) and 1.83 ($P$) for the $\omega N$ channel
- $D_{15}(1675)$ resonance mass: 1665 MeV ($C$) and 1662 MeV ($P$), total width 144 MeV ($C$) and 138 MeV ($P$)
- $F_{15}(1680)$ resonance mass: 1674 MeV ($C$) and 1669 MeV ($P$), total width 120 MeV ($C$) and 122 MeV ($P$)
- $F_{15}(2000)$ resonance mass: 1981 MeV ($C$) and 1986 MeV ($P$), total width 361 MeV ($C$) and 488 MeV ($P$)
- $F_{35}(1905)$ resonance mass: 1859 MeV ($C$) and 1830 MeV ($P$), total width 400 MeV ($C$) and 457 MeV ($P$)
- $D_{15}(1675)$ $\pi N$ branching ratio: 40.2% ($C$) and 41.2% ($P$)
- $F_{15}(1680)$ $\pi N$ branching ratio: 68.5% ($C$) and 65.8% ($P$)
- $F_{15}(2000)$ $\omega N$ branching ratio: 2.2% ($C$) and 1.7% ($P$)
- Nucleon cutoff $\Lambda_{1/2}=2.79$ for the $C$-coupling

## Context

This work extends the Giessen model calculations of Penner (2002) and Penner and Mosel (2002) by adding spin-5/2 resonances to the existing framework that previously included only spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 states. The study addresses the problem of "missing" nucleon resonances predicted by quark models (Capstick, 2002) that may be weakly coupled to pion channels, and enables future investigation of vector meson production mechanisms and higher-spin baryon spectra. The results also provide a basis for extending Compton scattering analyses beyond $\sqrt{s}=1.6$ GeV, which was previously limited by the absence of spin-5/2 states with their large electromagnetic couplings.
