# Isospin QCD as a laboratory for dense QCD

> QCD with the isospin chemical potential, $μ_I$, is a useful laboratory to delineate the microphysics in dense QCD. To study the quark-hadron-continuity we use a quark-meson model that interpolates hadronic and quark matter physics at microscopic level. The equation of state is dominated by mesons at low density but taken over by quarks at high density. We extend our previous studies with two-flavors to the three-flavors case to study the impact of the strangeness which may be brought by kaons $(K_+, K_0) = (u\bar{s}, s\bar{d})$ and the U$_A$(1) anomaly. In the normal phase the excitation energies of kaons are reduced by $μ_I$ in the same way as hyperons in nuclear matter at finite baryon chemical potential. Once pions condense, kaon excitation energies increases as $μ_I$ does. Moreover, strange quarks become more massive through the U$_A$(1) coupling to the condensed pions. Hence at zero and low temperature the strange hadrons and quarks are highly suppressed. The previous findings in two-flavor models, sound speed peak, negative trace anomaly, gaps insensitve to $μ_I$, persist in our three-flavor model and remain consistent with the lattice results to $μ_I \sim 1$ GeV. We discuss the non-perturbative power corrections and quark saturation effects as important ingredients to understand the crossover equations of state measured on the lattice.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous two-flavor quark-meson model to three flavors to study how strangeness and the U$_A$(1) anomaly affect dense QCD matter at finite isospin chemical potential, finding that strange hadrons and quarks are highly suppressed at zero and low temperature due to increased kaon excitation energies after pion condensation and enhanced strange quark masses from the anomaly coupling.

## Method

The authors use a renormalizable three-flavor quark-meson model that interpolates between hadronic and quark matter descriptions, including scalar and pseudoscalar flavor nonets, the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) interaction for U$_A$(1) breaking, and explicit symmetry breaking terms for current quark masses. They analyze the effective potential and meson spectra at tree level, then include quark loop corrections to study the equation of state at zero temperature, comparing their results to lattice QCD simulations up to $\mu_I \sim 1$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- The onset of pion condensation occurs at $\mu_I = m_\pi/2$, where $m_\pi$ is the pion mass in vacuum
- The quark-meson coupling $g$ scales as $\sim \Nc^{-1/2}$ in the large-$\Nc$ counting
- The gap from soft gluon exchange is $\Delta \sim \Lambda \exp(-2\pi^2/c)$ where $\Lambda$ is the soft momentum cutoff and $c \sim O(1)$
- The model remains consistent with lattice results up to $\mu_I \sim 1$ GeV
- Nuclear saturation density is $n_0 \simeq 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- The rapid stiffening of matter occurs around $n_B = 2$-$4n_0$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two-flavor studies (Chiba 2023, Chiba 2024) to three flavors, building on the quark-hadron continuity framework developed by Schafer (1998), Masuda (2012, 2015), and others. The results are consistent with lattice QCD simulations of isospin QCD (Abbott 2023, 2024; Brandt 2022) and provide a microscopic understanding of the rapid stiffening in neutron star matter observed in nuclear many-body calculations (Drischler 2020, Han 2022). The work enables further study of non-perturbative power corrections and quark saturation effects in dense QCD, concepts previously explored in two-color QCD and quarkyonic matter models.
