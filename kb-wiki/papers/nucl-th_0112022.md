# Electromagnetic, weak, and strong interactions of light mesons

> The ladder-rainbow truncation of the set of Dyson-Schwinger equations is used to study a variety of electroweak and strong processes involving light mesons. The parameters in the effective interaction are constrained by the chiral condensate and f_π; the current quark masses are fitted to m_πand m_K. The obtained electromagnetic form factors are in good agreement with the data. Also the weak K_{l3} decay and the radiative and strong decays of the vector mesons agree reasonably well with the data. Finally, we indicate how processes such as π-πscattering can be described within this framework as well.

## Key claim

The ladder-rainbow truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations, with parameters fixed by the chiral condensate and pion decay constant, can simultaneously describe electromagnetic form factors, weak kaon decays, and radiative and strong decays of vector mesons to within about 10-15% of experimental data, though the charged kaon radiative decay and the phi meson strong decay width show larger discrepancies.

## Method

The dressed-quark propagator is obtained from its Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) using a rainbow truncation, while meson bound states are described by solutions of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in ladder truncation. The effective quark-antiquark interaction is chosen to reproduce perturbative QCD in the ultraviolet and a chiral condensate of about (240 MeV)^3 in the infrared. Electroweak processes are computed in impulse approximation using the same dressed propagators, meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and dressed quark-photon and quark-W vertices obtained from inhomogeneous BSEs, without adjusting any additional parameters.

## Context

This work extends the ladder-rainbow Dyson-Schwinger approach previously applied to meson masses and decay constants by Maris and Roberts (1997) and Maris and Tandy (1999) to a comprehensive set of electroweak and strong interaction processes. The calculations demonstrate that the same framework can describe electromagnetic form factors, weak K_{l3} decays, and vector meson decays without additional parameter tuning. The paper also shows that for four-point processes like pi-pi scattering, impulse approximation is insufficient and ladder kernel insertions must be included to satisfy chiral symmetry constraints, as demonstrated by Bicudo, Cotanch, Llanes-Estrada, Maris, Ribeiro, and Szczepaniak (2001). This approach enables a unified description of meson interactions from QCD-based Dyson-Schwinger equations that can underpin effective meson Lagrangian approaches.
