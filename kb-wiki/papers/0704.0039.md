# Scalar radius of the pion and zeros in the form factor

> The quadratic pion scalar radius, \la r^2\ra^π_s, plays an important role for present precise determinations of ππscattering. Recently, Ynduráin, using an Omnès representation of the null isospin(I) non-strange pion scalar form factor, obtains \la r^2\ra^π_s=0.75\pm 0.07 fm^2. This value is larger than the one calculated by solving the corresponding Muskhelishvili-Omnès equations, \la r^2\ra^π_s=0.61\pm 0.04 fm^2. A large discrepancy between both values, given the precision, then results. We reanalyze Ynduráin's method and show that by imposing continuity of the resulting pion scalar form factor under tiny changes in the input ππphase shifts, a zero in the form factor for some S-wave I=0 T-matrices is then required. Once this is accounted for, the resulting value is \la r^2\ra_s^π=0.65\pm 0.05 fm^2. The main source of error in our determination is present experimental uncertainties in low energy S-wave I=0 ππphase shifts. Another important contribution to our error is the not yet settled asymptotic behaviour of the phase of the scalar form factor from QCD.

## Key claim

The authors show that Ynduráin's larger value for the quadratic pion scalar radius arises from neglecting a zero in the scalar form factor when the S-wave I=0 ππ phase shift at the K̅K threshold is greater than or equal to π; once this zero is properly included, the resulting value agrees with the Muskhelishvili-Omnès equation approach.

## Method

The authors reanalyze Ynduráin's Omnès representation of the I=0 non-strange pion scalar form factor, imposing continuity of the form factor under small changes in the input ππ phase shifts. They derive a modified sum rule for the quadratic scalar radius that includes a term −6/s₁ when the phase shift at the K̅K threshold exceeds π, where s₁ is the zero position determined by δπ(s₁)=π. They evaluate the sum rule using two parameterizations for the low-energy S-wave I=0 ππ phase shifts (CGL up to 0.8 GeV and PY up to 0.9 GeV) combined with the K-matrix parameterization of Hyams et al. for higher energies, and estimate uncertainties from experimental phase shift errors, inelasticity effects, and the asymptotic behavior of the form factor phase.

## Key numbers

- Quadratic pion scalar radius: ⟨r²⟩_s^π = 0.65 ± 0.05 fm² (central result after including zero correction)
- For comparison, Ynduráin's value: ⟨r²⟩_s^π = 0.75 ± 0.07 fm² (from Omnès representation without zero)
- For comparison, Muskhelishvili-Omnès solution: ⟨r²⟩_s^π = 0.61 ± 0.04 fm² (from coupled-channel analysis)
- The zero position s₁ is determined by δπ(s₁)=π, occurring slightly below the K̅K threshold at s_K = 4m_K²
- The low-energy constant ℓ̅₄ from the authors' result is ℓ̅₄ = 4.4 ± 0.2 (from pipiscat) versus ℓ̅₄ = 5.4 ± 0.5 (from Ynduráin)
- The K-matrix parameters from Hyams et al. include α₁ = 2.28 ± 0.08, with the central value of δπ(s_K) = 3.05 rad (slightly below π)

## Context

This work resolves a discrepancy between Ynduráin (2004, 2005, 2006) and the earlier coupled-channel Muskhelishvili-Omnès calculations of Gasser, Leutwyler, and Donoghue (1990) and the update by pipiscat (2001). The authors show that Ynduráin's approach, when corrected to include a zero in the form factor for phase shifts at the K̅K threshold above π, yields results consistent with the Muskhelishvili-Omnès approach and with Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory (Oller and Oset, 2000). The corrected value enables more precise determinations of ππ scattering lengths via Roy equations and resolves the tension in the low-energy constant ℓ̅₄ between the two approaches.
