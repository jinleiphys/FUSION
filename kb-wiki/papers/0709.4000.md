# Strangeness -2 and -3 Baryons in a Constituent Quark Model

> We apply a quark model developed in earlier work to the spectrum of baryons with strangeness -2 and -3. The model describes a number of well-established baryons successfully, and application to cascade baryons allows the quantum numbers of some known states to be deduced.

# Strangeness -2 and -3 Baryons in a Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim

The model reproduces the masses of known cascade and omega baryons to within about 20 MeV for most states, but the authors caution that the model systematically underpredicts masses of negative-parity states (as it does for the S11(1535) nucleon resonance, predicted about 90 MeV too light), so assignments for excited states remain tentative. The authors deduce that the Xi(1690) likely has J^P = 1/2^- based on a model prediction of 1725 MeV, and that the Xi(1823) is probably the second 3/2^- state rather than the lowest one.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic constituent quark model Hamiltonian with linear confinement, Coulomb, contact, tensor, and spin-orbit interactions, with parameters fit to a selection of known baryon masses. Wave functions are constructed in a harmonic oscillator basis up to N=2 (where N = 2(n_rho + n_lambda) + l_rho + l_lambda), using SU(6) symmetric wave functions for states with three identical quarks and a mixed-symmetry basis for states with two identical quarks. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized variationally to obtain masses and spin-parity assignments for cascade (Xi) and omega (Omega) baryons.

## Key numbers

- Ground state Xi(1/2^+): model mass 1.325 GeV, experiment 1.317 ± 0.001 GeV
- Xi(3/2^+): model mass 1.520 GeV, experiment 1.532 ± 0.001 GeV
- Xi(3/2^-) lowest: model mass 1.759 GeV; second state at 1.826 GeV matches experimental Xi(1823) at 1.823 ± 0.005 GeV
- Xi(1690) assigned as 1/2^-: model mass 1.725 GeV, 35 MeV above nominal mass
- Xi(1950) assigned as 5/2^+: model mass 1.936 GeV, experiment 1.950 ± 0.015 GeV
- Xi(2030) assigned as 7/2^+: model mass 2.035 GeV, experiment 2.025 ± 0.005 GeV
- Omega(3/2^+) ground state: model mass 1.656 GeV, experiment 1.672 GeV
- Omega excited states: model predicts multiple states near 2.17-2.21 GeV, including 1/2^+ at 2.175 and 2.191 GeV, 3/2^+ at 2.170, 2.182, and 2.194 GeV, 5/2^+ at 2.178 and 2.210 GeV, and 7/2^+ at 2.183 GeV

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach of Isgur and Karl (1978) and Capstick and Isgur (1986) to multistrange baryons, using a nonrelativistic Hamiltonian with parameters refit to include tensor interactions and SU(6) wave functions. The authors note that previous predictions for the lowest Xi(1/2^-) mass range from 1550 to 1869 MeV across different approaches including the Skyrme model (Oh, 2007), QCD sum rules, and the collective model of Bijker, Iachello, and Leviathan. The results provide specific spin-parity assignments for poorly known cascade and omega states that can be tested by ongoing experiments at BaBar, Jefferson Lab, and elsewhere, particularly given the recent BaBar determination of Xi(1530) as 3/2^+ and the spin of Omega(1672) as consistent with 3/2.
