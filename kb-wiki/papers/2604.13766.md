# Spectroscopy of $^{11}$Be from the $^{10}$Be($d,p$) reaction measured in inverse kinematics by the AT-TPC in SOLARIS

> The spectroscopy of $^{11}$Be is explored using the $^{10}$Be$(d,p)$$^{11}$Be transfer reaction performed in inverse kinematics at $9.6\,\MeV/u$ using the Active Target Time Projection Chamber (AT-TPC) inside the SOLARIS solenoid. This experiment is the first attempt at coupling the AT-TPC with SOLARIS to perform a high luminosity transfer reaction measurement without compromising excitation energy and scattering angle resolutions. The angular momentum transfer for states up to $3.40\,\MeV$ are determined from distorted-wave Born approximation analysis of the measured angular distributions, from which the corresponding spectroscopic factors are deduced. These factors are compared with those from various shell model interactions, and those for the $3.40\,\MeV$ state are consistent with a positive parity assignment. Recent \textit{ab initio} no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations with various nucleon-nucleon interactions are presented for the low-lying positive parity states of $^{11}$Be. The excitation energies produced using the Daejeon16 interaction are in good agreement with those found from both this experiment and the literature, thus supporting a positive parity assignment. The $3.40\,\MeV$ state, if assigned a tentative $J^π=3/2^+$, would then correspond to the second excited state of the $K^P=1/2^+$ one-neutron halo ground state rotational band also predicted from such NCCI calculations.

## Key claim

The 3.40 MeV state in ¹¹Be is tentatively assigned a positive parity (J^π = 3/2^+), based on spectroscopic factors extracted from DWBA analysis and comparison with shell model and *ab initio* NCCI calculations, though the angular distribution alone could not definitively differentiate between ℓ=1 and ℓ=2 transfer due to limited angular coverage.

## Method

The ¹⁰Be(d,p)¹¹Be transfer reaction was performed in inverse kinematics at 9.6 MeV/u using the Active Target Time Projection Chamber (AT-TPC) inside the SOLARIS solenoid. Event-by-event analysis was carried out with the Python package *spyral* to extract particle tracks and kinematic parameters, and Monte Carlo simulations using *attpc_engine* provided efficiency corrections. Distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations using the finite-range code *ptolemy* were performed with the AV18 potential for the deuteron bound state and Woods-Saxon potentials for neutron bound states, using two different optical model potentials (DA1p and An Cai) in the incoming channel and the Koning-Delaroche OMP in the exit channel.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 9.6 MeV/u (inverse kinematics), with energy ranging from 9.30 to 8.07 MeV/u across the 1 m active volume filled with 600 Torr deuterium gas (13 mg/cm² thickness)
- Excitation energy resolution: approximately 350 keV FWHM
- Spectroscopic factor for the 1.78 MeV (5/2^+) state: 0.72 ± 0.03
- Spectroscopic factor for the 3.40 MeV state assuming 3/2^+ (ℓ=2, 0d₃/₂): 0.08 ± 0.02
- Spectroscopic factor for the 3.40 MeV state assuming 3/2^- (ℓ=1, 0p₃/₂): 0.18 ± 0.05
- Spectroscopic factor for the ground state (1/2^+): 0.99 ± 0.20
- Spectroscopic factor for the 0.32 MeV state (1/2^-): 0.72 ± 0.09
- Spectroscopic factor for the 2.65 MeV state (3/2^-): 0.13 ± 0.04
- Unbound state correction factors for single-particle cross sections: 1.08 (1.78 MeV), 2.09 (2.65 MeV), and 2.49 (3.40 MeV)

## Context

This work extends prior transfer reaction studies of ¹¹Be, such as those by Zwieglinksi et al. (1979) and the passive-target inverse kinematics measurement by Schmitt et al. (2013), achieving comparable statistics with a beam exposure approximately three orders of magnitude smaller. The results are compared with shell model calculations using WBP, WBT, YSOX, and FSU interactions, and with *ab initio* no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations using the NNLOopt and Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon interactions. The Daejeon16 interaction yields excitation energies for the 5/2^+ and 3/2^+ states (≈1.7–1.8 MeV and ≈3.2–3.4 MeV, respectively) in remarkable agreement with experiment, supporting the interpretation of a K^P = 1/2^+ rotational band built on the parity-inverted ground state of ¹¹Be. This measurement demonstrates the first coupling of the AT-TPC with SOLARIS as a solenoidal spectrometer, enabling high-luminosity transfer reaction measurements with rare isotope beams at intensities of a few hundred particles per second.
