# Directed flow at mid-rapidity in event-by-event hydrodynamics

> Fluctuations in the initial geometry of a nucleus-nucleus collision have been recently shown to result in a new type of directed flow (v_1) that, unlike the usual directed flow, is also present at midrapidity. We compute this new v_1 versus transverse momentum and centrality for Au-Au collisions at RHIC using the hydrodynamic code NeXSPheRIO. We find that the event plane of v_1 is correlated with the angle of the initial dipole of the distribution, as predicted, though with a large dispersion. It is uncorrelated with the reaction plane. Our results are in excellent agreement with results inferred from STAR correlation data.

# Directed flow at mid-rapidity in event-by-event hydrodynamics

## Key claim

Fluctuations in the initial geometry of nucleus-nucleus collisions produce a new type of directed flow (v₁) at midrapidity that is an even function of rapidity, but the correlation between the final flow angle and the initial dipole asymmetry angle has a large dispersion, meaning the initial dipole asymmetry is only a rough predictor of the final directed flow direction on an event-by-event basis.

## Method

The authors used the event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic code NeXSPheRIO to simulate Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon at RHIC, with initial conditions provided by the event generator NeXus. They computed v₁ for hadrons in the pseudorapidity interval |η| < 1 using an event-plane method with a weight function w(p_t) = p_t - ⟨p_t²⟩/⟨p_t⟩ that eliminates the rapidity-odd component of directed flow and nonflow correlations from momentum conservation. Results were averaged over 120 events uniformly distributed in the 0-60% centrality range, with each event generating 7×10⁵ particles to ensure high event-plane resolution.

## Key numbers

- v₁(p_t) crosses zero at p_t ≃ 1.1 GeV/c for 0-40% centrality Au-Au collisions at RHIC
- The minimum value of v₁ lies between -0.02 and -0.01 in the 0-40% centrality range
- The weight function w(p_t) changes sign at p_t = ⟨p_t²⟩/⟨p_t⟩ ≃ 0.85 GeV/c
- 75% of events used have a resolution parameter χ > 3, corresponding to an event-plane resolution ⟨cosΔφ⟩ > 0.97
- The rms dipole asymmetry ε₁ʳᵐˢ scales approximately with number of participants as N_part^{-1/2}, similar to triangular flow ε₃
- v₁ averaged over hadrons with p_t < 1 GeV/c, |η| < 1 increases mildly with centrality (more peripheral collisions give larger v₁)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction by Teaney and Yan (2010) that fluctuations in the initial geometry create a rapidity-even directed flow component at midrapidity, and provides the first quantitative hydrodynamic predictions for this v₁. The results are compared with values inferred from STAR correlation data by Luzum (2010) and show excellent agreement in the range 0 < p_t < 2 GeV/c for 20-60% centrality. The study establishes directed flow at midrapidity as a probe of hydrodynamic behavior that appears less sensitive to shear viscosity than elliptic flow v₂ or triangular flow v₃, and enables future dedicated experimental analyses at RHIC and LHC as well as viscous hydrodynamic calculations.
