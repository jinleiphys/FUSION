# Similarity Renormalization Group Evolution of Three-Nucleon Forces in a Hyperspherical Momentum Representation

> A new framework for computing the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution of three-nucleon forces (3NF) in momentum representation is presented. The use of antisymmetric three-particle hyperspherical momentum states ensures unitary evolutions within certain basis truncations, much like antisymmetric harmonic oscillator SRG evolutions. Additionally, in each partial wave the $T_{\mbox{rel}}$-SRG regulator is exactly represented, similar to recent 3NF momentum representation evolutions. Unitary equivalence is demonstrated for the triton using several chiral two- plus three-nucleon interactions. This method allows for a clean visualization of the evolution of the three-nucleon forces, which manifests the SRG decoupling pattern and low-momentum universality.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new hybrid momentum-space framework for SRG evolution of three-nucleon forces that maintains exact unitarity within basis truncations, unlike previous momentum-representation approaches that incur unitary-violating errors (approximately 0.5 keV for the triton) from non-block-diagonal antisymmetrization.

## Method
The three-body Hamiltonian is represented in fully antisymmetric hyperspherical harmonic momentum states, built by projecting Jacobi momentum states onto hyperspherical harmonics with grand angular momentum quantum number G. The antisymmetrizer is constructed in blocks of fixed G, diagonalized, and used to build linearly independent antisymmetric states. The SRG flow equation is then solved in this basis using the Trel-SRG generator, with the evolved three-nucleon force isolated by subtracting the embedded evolved two-nucleon force from the total evolved interaction.

## Key numbers
- Triton binding energy from the NNLO 550/600 MeV interaction is reproduced within approximately 3 keV of the expected -8.482 MeV, using 60 momentum states per partial wave and Gmax = 20
- The unitary violation from the finite basis truncation is approximately 1 eV, determined by the precision of the ODE solver (Sundials CVODE)
- The unitary-violating error in the previous momentum-representation approach (Hebeler 2012) is at the approximately 0.5 keV level for the triton

## Context
This work extends the SRG evolution of three-nucleon forces from the harmonic oscillator basis approach (Jurgenson 2009, 2011, 2013; Roth 2012; Binder 2013) and the recent momentum-representation approach (Hebeler 2012) by combining their relative strengths. The method enables clean visualization of the SRG decoupling pattern and low-momentum universality in three-nucleon forces, and provides evolved interaction matrix elements applicable directly in infinite systems while being easily projectable to a harmonic oscillator basis for finite-system calculations. Extension to four-nucleon forces and alternative SRG generators is noted as straightforward.
