# Conserved charge susceptibilities in the relativistic mean-field hadron resonance gas model: constraints on hadronic repulsive interactions

> We investigate the effect of repulsive interaction between hadrons on the susceptibilities of conserved charges, namely baryon number (B), electric charge (Q) and strangeness (S). We estimate second fourth and sixth-order susceptibilities of conserved charges, their differences, ratios, and correlations within the ambit of the mean-field hadron resonance gas (MFHRG) model. We consider repulsive mean-field interaction among meson pairs, baryon pairs and anti-baryon pairs separately and constrain them by confronting the results of various susceptibilities with the recent lattice QCD (LQCD) data. We find that the repulsive interactions between baryon-baryon pairs and antibaryon-antibaryon pairs are sufficient to describe the baryon susceptibilities of hadronic matter at temperatures below the QCD transition temperature. However, small but finite mesonic repulsive interaction is needed to describe electric charge and strangeness susceptibilities. We finally conclude that the repulsive interaction between hadrons plays a very important role in describing the thermodynamic properties of hadronic matter, especially near the quark-hadron phase transition temperature ($T_c$). The mean-field parameter for baryons ($K_B$) should be constrained in the range $0.40\le K_B\le 0.450$ $\text{GeV.fm}^{3}$ to get a good agreement of baryon susceptibilities with the LQCD results, whereas meson mean-field parameter $K_M\sim 0.05$ $\text{GeV.fm}^{3}$ must be included with $K_B$ to get a reasonable agreement of the MFHRG model with the LQCD results for electric charge and strangeness susceptibilities.

## Key claim

The authors find that repulsive interactions between baryon-baryon and antibaryon-antibaryon pairs alone are sufficient to describe baryon number susceptibilities from lattice QCD, but a small but finite mesonic repulsive interaction (mean-field parameter \(K_M \sim 0.05\) GeV.fm\(^3\)) must be added to also describe electric charge and strangeness susceptibilities, with the baryon mean-field parameter constrained to \(0.40 \le K_B \le 0.450\) GeV.fm\(^3\).

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean-field hadron resonance gas (MFHRG) model, where repulsive interactions are introduced via density-dependent mean fields that shift single-particle energies by \(U = K n\), with separate parameters \(K_B\) for baryons (and antibaryons) and \(K_M\) for mesons. They calculated second, fourth, and sixth-order susceptibilities of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness, as well as their differences, ratios, and correlations, using the QMHRG2020 hadron list. The model results were compared with recent lattice QCD data from Bollweg (2021) and Borsanyi (2018, 2023) to constrain the mean-field parameters.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mean-field parameter constrained to \(0.40 \le K_B \le 0.450\) GeV.fm\(^3\) for good agreement of baryon susceptibilities with lattice QCD.
- Meson mean-field parameter \(K_M \sim 0.05\) GeV.fm\(^3\) required alongside \(K_B\) to describe electric charge and strangeness susceptibilities.
- Best fit for \(\chi_B^2\) observed at \(K_B = 0.45\) GeV.fm\(^3\) with \(K_M = 0\).
- With \(K_B = 0.45\) GeV.fm\(^3\) and \(K_M = 0.05\) GeV.fm\(^3\), good agreement with lattice data for \(\chi_Q^2\) and \(\chi_S^2\) is obtained up to \(T \sim 160\) MeV.
- The ratio \(\chi_B^4 / \chi_B^2\) deviates from the ideal HRG value of 1 due to repulsive interactions, in agreement with lattice results near \(T_c\).
- The difference \(\chi_B^2 - \chi_B^4\) is approximately \(12 (K_B T^2 / 2) (\beta^3 n_B^{\text{id}})\) and vanishes for ideal HRG; MFHRG with \(K_B = 0.45\) GeV.fm\(^3\) reproduces lattice data up to \(T = 170\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the ideal hadron resonance gas model by incorporating repulsive interactions via a mean-field approach, building on prior studies that used excluded-volume HRG (EVHRG) by Rischke (1992) and van der Waals approaches by Vovchenko (2020). It directly confronts the MFHRG model with recent lattice QCD results from Bollweg (2021) and Borsanyi (2018, 2023), providing constraints on the repulsive interaction parameters. The findings enable improved modeling of the hadronic equation of state near the QCD transition temperature and can be used to refine the interpretation of fluctuations measured in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC.
