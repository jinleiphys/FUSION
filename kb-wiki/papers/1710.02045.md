# Antibaryon interactions with the nuclear medium

> This contribution deals with our recent study of antibaryon interactions with the nuclear medium within the relativistic mean-field approach using antibaryon coupling constants consistent with available experimental data. We performed calculations of $\bar{B}$ ($\bar{B}=\bar{p}, \barΛ, \barΣ, \barΞ$) bound states in selected nuclei. Due to the lack of information on the in-medium antihyperon annihilation near threshold only the $\bar{p}$ absorption was considered. It was described by the imaginary part of a phenomenological optical potential fitted to $\bar{p}$-atom data. The annihilation was treated dynamically, taking into account explicitly the reduced phase space for annihilation products in the nuclear medium, as well as the compressed nuclear density due to the antiproton. The energy available for the annihilation products was evaluated self-consistently, considering additional energy shift due to particle momenta in the ${\bar p}$-nucleus system. Corresponding $\bar{p}$ widths were significantly reduced, however, they still remain sizable. Next, the $\bar{p}$-nucleus interaction was constructed using the latest version of the Paris $\bar{N}N$ potential. Related scattering amplitudes were used to define the complex $\bar{p}$ optical potential in the nuclear medium. The resulting $\bar{p}$ $1s$ binding energies are about 10% smaller and widths about 20% larger than those obtained with the phenomenological approach.

# Antibaryon interactions with the nuclear medium

## Key claim
Antibaryon-nucleus bound states exist with deep attractive potentials due to G-parity transformation, but the actual binding energies and widths depend critically on the treatment of annihilation and the choice of reference frame for the energy available to annihilation products. The authors find that the Paris $\bar{N}N$ S-wave potential yields $\bar{p}$ 1s binding energies about 10% smaller and widths about 20% larger than those obtained with a phenomenological optical potential fitted to $\bar{p}$-atom data.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations of $\bar{B}$ ($\bar{B}=\bar{p}, \bar{\Lambda}, \bar{\Sigma}, \bar{\Xi}$) bound states in selected nuclei ($^{6}$Li, $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, $^{208}$Pb), using G-parity motivated coupling constants scaled by a factor $\xi=0.2-0.3$ to match experimental data. For $\bar{p}$ only, absorption was included via an imaginary optical potential: first using a phenomenological $t\rho$ form fitted to $\bar{p}$-atom data with self-consistent phase-space suppression factors, then using S-wave $\bar{p}N$ scattering amplitudes from the Paris $\bar{N}N$ potential modified for Pauli correlations in the medium. The energy available for annihilation products was evaluated both in the two-body center-of-mass frame (M) and in the $\bar{p}$-nucleus laboratory frame (J), with the latter including momentum-dependent shifts.

## Key numbers
- The $\bar{p}$ potential depth inside a nucleus is about 750 MeV from G-parity, but experimental data suggest the real part is 100-300 MeV deep in the nuclear interior.
- The scaling factor $\xi$ for antibaryon-meson coupling constants is taken as $\xi=0.2-0.3$, consistent with experimental data fits.
- The imaginary part of the phenomenological optical potential uses parameter Im$b_0=1.9$ fm, fitted to $\bar{p}$-atom data.
- The Paris $\bar{N}N$ S-wave potential yields $\bar{p}$ 1s binding energies about 10% smaller and widths about 20% larger than the phenomenological approach.
- The phase space suppression factor $f_s$ is evaluated as a function of center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}$ for all annihilation channels considered.

## Context
This work extends prior RMF studies of antibaryon-nucleus interactions (Mishustin, 2000s) by incorporating self-consistent treatment of $\bar{p}$ annihilation with phase-space suppression and comparing phenomenological and microscopic (Paris $\bar{N}N$) potentials. The G-parity approach follows earlier work on antiprotonic atoms (Mares, 1990s) and $\bar{p}$ scattering at low energies. The results enable future comparisons with experiments planned at FAIR and provide a framework for including antihyperon annihilation once experimental data become available. The authors note that the Paris $\bar{N}N$ potential contains sizable P-wave interaction not included here, with those calculations performed recently and to be published separately.
