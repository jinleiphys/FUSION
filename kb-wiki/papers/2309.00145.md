# Semi-inclusive direct photon+jet and $π^{0}$+jet correlations measured in $p+p$ and central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200$ GeV

> The STAR experiment at RHIC reports new measurements of jet quenching based on the semi-inclusive distribution of charged-particle jets recoiling from direct photon ($γ_{\rm dir}$) and neutral pion ($π^{0}$) triggers in pp and central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV, for triggers in the range $9<E_{\rm T}^{\rm trig}<20$ GeV. The datasets have integrated luminosities of 3.9$ {\rm nb}^{-1}$ for Au+Au and 23$ {\rm pb}^{-1}$ for pp collisions. Jets are reconstructed using the anti-$k_{\rm T}$ algorithm with resolution parameters $R$=0.2 and 0.5. The large uncorrelated jet background in central Au+Au collisions is corrected using a mixed-event approach, which enables precise charged-particle jet measurements at low transverse momentum $p_{\rm T,jet}^{\rm ch}$ and large $R$. Recoil-jet distributions are reported in the range $p_{\rm T,jet}^{\rm ch}<25$ \gev. Comparison of the distributions measured in pp and Au+Au collisions reveals strong medium-induced jet yield suppression for $R=0.2$, with markedly less suppression for $R=0.5$. Comparison is also made to theoretical models incorporating jet quenching. These data provide new insight into the mechanisms underlying jet quenching and the angular dependence of medium-induced jet-energy transport, and provide new constraints on modelling such effects.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports the first direct photon+jet and neutral pion+jet coincidence measurements in pp and central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, finding strong medium-induced jet yield suppression for jet resolution parameter R=0.2 but markedly less suppression for R=0.5, with the caveat that the direct-photon trigger yields are determined by a statistical subtraction of decay and fragmentation photons rather than event-wise identification.

## Method

Charged-particle jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with resolution parameters R=0.2 and 0.5 from primary tracks in the Time Projection Chamber, with acceptance |eta|<1.0 and full azimuth. The large uncorrelated jet background in central Au+Au collisions was corrected using a mixed-event approach, enabling precise measurements at low jet transverse momentum and large R. Direct photon and neutral pion triggers in the range 9 < ETtrig < 20 GeV were identified using the Barrel Electromagnetic Calorimeter and Barrel Shower Maximum Detector, with direct-photon purity determined by a statistical subtraction assuming direct photons have no near-side associated hadrons above a specified pT threshold.

## Key numbers

- Integrated luminosities: 3.9 nb^-1 for Au+Au collisions and 23 pb^-1 for pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Trigger energy range: 9 < ETtrig < 20 GeV
- Recoil-jet distributions reported for pT,jet^ch < 25 GeV/c
- Primary-track efficiency: 55% for pTtrack = 0.2 GeV/c, 60% for pTtrack = 0.5 GeV/c, and 72% for pTtrack > 2 GeV/c in central Au+Au collisions; 70% for pTtrack = 0.25 GeV/c and 82% for pTtrack > 1 GeV/c in pp collisions
- Primary-track momentum resolution approximately sigma_pT/pT = 0.01 * pT (GeV/c) for both collision systems

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements of direct photon+hadron and neutral pion+hadron correlations (STAR, 2016) and semi-inclusive charged-particle jet distributions from high-pT charged-hadron triggers in Au+Au collisions (STAR, 2017), combining both approaches for the first time at RHIC. The measurements provide new constraints on theoretical models incorporating jet quenching, including the Jet-fluid model, Linear Boltzmann Transport model, CoLBT-hydro model, Soft Collinear Effective Theory model, and the Hybrid model, enabling discrimination between different mechanisms of medium-induced jet-energy transport and its angular dependence.
