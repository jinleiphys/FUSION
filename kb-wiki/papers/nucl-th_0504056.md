# Medium modifications of antikaons in dense matter

> We investigate the modification of antikaons in isospin symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. Using the leading s-wave couplings of the SU(3) chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian we solve the coupled channel kaon-nucleon scattering equation selfconsistently. The in-medium antikaon propagator is calculated for different densities and different proton/neutron ratios. The spectral function of the antikaon is found to be broadened strongly, and its mass is shifted downward significantly. However, comparing the effective in-medium mass of the antikaon to the relevant charge chemical potential of neutron star matter, we find that kaon condensation is unlikely to occur in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors find that, although the antikaon mass is shifted downward significantly and its spectral function is broadened strongly in dense nuclear matter, kaon condensation is unlikely to occur in neutron stars when the effective in-medium mass is compared to the relevant charge chemical potential of neutron star matter.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel kaon-nucleon scattering equation self-consistently using the leading s-wave couplings of the SU(3) chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian, with the Bethe-Salpeter equation factorized via on-shell approximation. The in-medium antikaon propagator is calculated for different densities and proton/neutron ratios by iterating the T-matrix and the kaon self-energy to self-consistency, including the dynamical generation of the Lambda(1405) resonance and the coupling to pion-baryon channels.

## Key numbers

- The empirical s-wave K- nucleon scattering length is repulsive at threshold: Re a_{K-p} = -0.78 fm.
- The Lambda(1405) resonance appears about 30 MeV below the Kbar N threshold.
- The self-consistency procedure converges after 3-4 iterations.
- At normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} and three-momentum 100 MeV, the antikaon spectral function has a full width at half maximum of roughly 130 MeV.
- The vacuum antikaon energy at the peak position is approximately 505 MeV (sqrt(m_K^2 + k^2) for k=100 MeV).
- Calculations are performed for densities up to 5 rho_0.
- The pion-nucleon pseudovector coupling constant is f_N = 1.01, and the monopole form factor cutoff is Lambda = 550 MeV.
- Migdal parameters used: g'_NN = 0.8, g'_NDelta = g'_DeltaDelta = 0.5; for p-wave kaon selfenergy, g' = 1/3.

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channel calculations of antikaon-nucleon scattering in matter (Koch, 1995; Waas et al., 1996; Weise, 1998; Schaffner-Bielich et al., 2000) by implementing full self-consistency between the in-medium T-matrix and the kaon propagator, following the approach of Lutz (1998) and Lutz and Korpa (2002). The results contradict earlier expectations that kaon condensation would soften the neutron star equation of state (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986), and the finding that the self-consistent Lambda(1405) does not move very much (Gal et al., 2001; Lutz, 1998) renders the argument for kaonic atoms questionable. The momentum dependence of all in-medium properties is emphasized, in contrast to many earlier calculations that assumed the attractive potential is momentum-independent (Waas et al., 1996; Oset et al., 1999).
