# Developments in Chiral effective Field Theory for Nuclear Matter

> We review on a chiral power counting scheme for in-medium chiral perturbation theory with nucleons and pions as degrees of freedom \cite{ref}. It allows for a systematic expansion taking into account local as well as pion-mediated inter-nucleon interactions. Based on this power counting, one can identify classes of non-perturbative diagrams that require a resummation. We then calculate the nuclear matter energy density for the symmetric and purely neutron matter cases up-to-and-including next-to-leading order (NLO), in good agreement with sophisticated many-body calculations. Next, the neutron matter equation of state is applied to calculate the upper limit for neutron stars, with an upper bound around 2.3 solar masses, large enough to accommodate the most massive neutron star observed until now. We also apply our equation state to constraint $G_N$ in exceptionally large gravitational fields.

## Key claim

The chiral power counting scheme for in-medium chiral perturbation theory, which treats nucleon propagators as order p^{-2} (non-standard counting) from the start, yields nuclear matter energy densities at next-to-leading order that agree with sophisticated many-body calculations, but the agreement depends on tuning a single free constant (g̃₀) to values around -1 m_π² for symmetric matter and -0.5 m_π² for neutron matter.

## Method

The author derived a chiral power counting formula (Eq. fff) for in-medium chiral perturbation theory with nucleons and pions, where nucleon propagators are counted as O(p^{-2}) and the Fermi momentum k_F is taken to be of the same chiral order as the pion mass m_π. The nuclear matter energy density was calculated up to next-to-leading order (O(p^6)) by evaluating diagrams for kinetic energy, nucleon self-energy, and NN interactions in the nuclear medium, with the NN partial-wave expansion used to handle interactions. The neutron matter equation of state was then applied to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star masses and to constrain variations of the gravitational constant G_N.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility K = 259 MeV from the chiral EFT calculation, compared to the experimental value K = 250 ± 25 MeV (Blaizot).
- Upper bound for neutron star mass around 2.3 solar masses from the neutron matter equation of state.
- The observed neutron star PSR J1614-2230 has mass (1.97 ± 0.04) M_⊙ (Demorest).
- G_N cannot exceed its value on Earth by more than 12% at 95% confidence level, based on constraints from a 2 solar mass neutron star with surface acceleration around 2 × 10^12 m/s^2.
- The constant g̃₀ was set to approximately -1 m_π² for symmetric nuclear matter and -0.5 m_π² for pure neutron matter.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach of Weinberg (1990, 1991) and the in-medium power counting of previous studies (Lutz, 1998; Kaiser, 2001) by incorporating multi-nucleon interactions and heavy meson exchanges within a unified counting scheme. The results enable first-principles calculations of neutron star properties and provide constraints on the constancy of the gravitational constant in extreme gravitational fields, complementing laboratory measurements and binary pulsar observations.
