# Exploring the QCD phase diagram through correlations and fluctuations

> The exploration of the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) phase diagram is a central goal of relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments. This review focuses on the role of fluctuations and correlations as sensitive probes of the phase structure. We discuss theoretical advancements and experimental methodologies employed to map the QCD phase diagram, highlighting constraints derived from both lattice QCD calculations and existing experimental data. Key observables such as cumulants and factorial cumulants of conserved charges (e.g., net-proton, net-charge) are explored as promising signatures of phase transitions and the QCD critical point. We discuss how these quantities are measured experimentally and compared with theoretical predictions, addressing challenges and best practices for meaningful comparisons. Special attention is given to predictions and current experimental results at high baryon density, including recent findings from the STAR collaboration at RHIC. Finally, we identify open issues and future directions for fluctuation and correlation studies at lower collision energies, relevant for future measurements, for example by the CBM experiment.

## Key claim

This review argues that cumulants and factorial cumulants of conserved charges (especially net-proton and net-baryon number) are sensitive probes of the QCD phase structure, but that direct comparison between experimental measurements and lattice QCD predictions requires careful correction for global charge conservation, thermal smearing, the proton-vs-baryon distinction, and volume fluctuations, effects that can substantially alter the sign and magnitude of higher-order cumulants such as the hyper-kurtosis.

## Method

The authors review theoretical predictions for the QCD critical point location from lattice QCD extrapolations (constant entropy density contours, Yang-Lee edge singularities), functional methods (Dyson-Schwinger equations, functional renormalization group), and holographic models (Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton, V-QCD). They then examine how cumulants of conserved charges are defined, measured experimentally in heavy-ion collisions, and related to lattice QCD susceptibilities, emphasizing the corrections needed for meaningful comparison: global charge conservation corrections (Eqs. 21-23), binomial folding for protons vs baryons, thermal smearing effects, and volume fluctuation corrections via centrality bin width corrections or mixed-event methods.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD calculations have extracted baryon number susceptibilities up to 8th order at vanishing chemical potential (Borsanyi 2018, Bazavov 2017)
- The QCD critical point is predicted by multiple approaches in the region: critical temperature 100 MeV ≤ T_C ≤ 120 MeV and critical baryon chemical potential 550 MeV ≤ μ_C ≤ 650 MeV, corresponding roughly to collision energy √s_NN ≈ 5 GeV
- The constant entropy density extrapolation (Shah 2024) predicts a CP at T ≈ 114 MeV and μ_B ≈ 602 MeV under μ_S = μ_Q = 0 conditions
- The Budapest-Wuppertal-Houston group (Borsanyi 2025) rules out a CP at μ_B < 450 MeV at the 2σ level using imaginary chemical potential extrapolation
- Holographic models (Hippert 2023) place the CP in the range T_C ≈ 101–108 MeV and μ_C ≈ 560–625 MeV; V-QCD (Ecker 2025) favors T_C ≈ 119 MeV and μ_C ≈ 626 MeV
- Functional methods (Fu 2019, Gao 2020, Gunkel 2021) predict μ_C ≈ 600–650 MeV and T_C ≈ 110–120 MeV
- The CBM experiment at FAIR will study Au-Au collisions in the energy range √s_NN = 2.8–4.9 GeV
- Lattice QCD expansions are valid for μ_B/T ≲ 3; the cluster expansion in fugacity space has comparable range of validity

## Context

This review synthesizes and extends the line of work on QCD phase diagram exploration through fluctuations, building on the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program (reviewed by Bzdak 2019) and lattice QCD calculations at vanishing chemical potential (Aoki 2006). It directly addresses the challenges raised by Kitazawa (2011, 2012) regarding proton vs baryon cumulants, the global charge conservation corrections developed by Vovchenko (2020, 2021), and the volume fluctuation corrections studied by Skokov (2012) and Friman (2025). The review enables future measurements by the CBM experiment at FAIR and continued analysis by STAR at RHIC, providing a framework for comparing experimental cumulant measurements with lattice QCD predictions after applying the necessary corrections.
