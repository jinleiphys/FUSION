# Heavy-quark mass effects in off-light-cone distributions

> We compute the one-loop correction to the forward matrix element of an off-light-cone bi-local quark correlator characterised by a space-like separation $z^2$ in the presence of heavy quarks with mass $m$. This calculation allows us to extract the one-loop matching kernel, necessary to connect quasi and pseudo-distributions to collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs), accounting for heavy-quark mass effects. Our result is exact in that it includes all powers of $z^2m^2$ at one loop in $α_s$. In the limit $z^2m^2\rightarrow 0$, it consistently reduces to the known massless result. We also carry out an implementation of our expression, which allows us to compute the charm pseudo-distribution of the proton given its PDF. We finally comment on the quantitative impact of heavy-quark mass corrections.

## Key claim

The paper computes the one-loop matching kernel connecting quasi- and pseudo-distributions to collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs) including heavy-quark mass effects exactly in all powers of \(z^2 m^2\), not just the leading-power approximation. The authors show that at leading power in \(z^2 m^2 \rightarrow 0\) the massive matching kernel reduces to the known massless result, but the full expression resums higher-power corrections proportional to powers of \(z^2 m^2\) that are absent in the massless case.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop correction to the forward matrix element of an off-light-cone bi-local quark correlator with space-like separation \(z^2\) for a heavy quark of mass \(m\), working in Feynman gauge with dimensional regularization. They evaluate four contributions separately: the Wilson-line self-energy, the quark-line self-energy, the box-type diagram, and the vertex-type diagram, then combine them to obtain the complete massive Ioffe-time distribution. The matching kernel is extracted by relating this distribution to the massive light-cone Ioffe-time distribution in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme.

## Key numbers

- The Wilson-line self-energy contribution yields \(\Gamma_{\rm Wils.}(z) = -\frac{g^2 C_F}{(4\pi)^{D/2}} \frac{4 \Gamma(D/2-1)}{(D-3)(D-4)} \left(\frac{-z^2}{4}\right)^{2-D/2}\), which vanishes on the light-cone where \(z^2 = 0\).
- The quark self-energy contribution contains an IR divergence \(\frac{2 g^2 C_F}{(4\pi)^{D/2}} (m^2)^{D/2-2} \frac{\Gamma(3-D/2)}{\epsilon_{\rm IR}}\) that cancels against an identical IR divergence in the box-type contribution.
- The box-type contribution includes terms proportional to Bessel functions \(K_0(\sqrt{-z^2 (1-\beta)^2 m^2})\) and \(K_1(\sqrt{-z^2 (1-\beta)^2 m^2})\), with the latter giving a term \(\sqrt{-z^2 (1-\beta)^2 m^2} K_1(\sqrt{-z^2 (1-\beta)^2 m^2})\) that combines finite-mass and off-light-cone effects.
- In the limit \(z^2 m^2 \rightarrow 0\), the massive light-cone Ioffe-time distribution contains the term \(\left[ \frac{1+\beta^2}{1-\beta} \left( -\ln(m^2/\mu^2) - 2\ln(1-\beta) - 1 \right) \right]_+\), which is a known result for heavy-quark threshold matching in variable-flavour-number schemes.

## Context

This work extends the short-distance factorization approach of Radyushkin (2017) and the LaMET formalism of Ji (2013) by incorporating heavy-quark mass effects into the matching kernels that connect lattice QCD simulations to collinear PDFs. Prior calculations of these kernels were performed in the massless limit up to next-to-next-to-leading order for LaMET (Xiong 2013, Ji 2014, Ji 2015, Ji 2017, Radyushkin 2016, Izubuchi 2018, Radyushkin 2018, Li 2020, Chen 2020) and up to one loop for pseudo-distributions (Radyushkin 2017, Radyushkin 2018, Balitsky 2019, Balitsky 2021, Yao 2022). The result enables lattice extractions of heavy-meson distribution amplitudes and PDFs, as recently pursued by Wang (2019), Zhao (2020), Blossier (2024), and the LatticeParton collaboration (2024), by providing the correct matching for heavy quarks where mass corrections proportional to \(z^2 m^2\) are non-negligible.
