# Collective rotation from ab initio theory

> Through ab initio approaches in nuclear theory, we may now seek to quantitatively understand the wealth of nuclear collective phenomena starting from the underlying internucleon interactions. No-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for p-shell nuclei give rise to rotational bands, as evidenced by rotational patterns for excitation energies, electromagnetic moments, and electromagnetic transitions. In this review, NCCI calculations of 7-9Be are used to illustrate and explore ab initio rotational structure, and the resulting predictions for rotational band properties are compared with experiment. We highlight the robustness of ab initio rotational predictions across different choices for the internucleon interaction.

## Key claim

Through no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for p-shell nuclei, rotational bands emerge in ab initio theory, and these rotational predictions are robust across different choices for the internucleon interaction, though the calculations are carried out in a finite truncated space and convergence of observables varies significantly.

## Method

NCCI calculations were performed for ⁷⁻⁹Be isotopes using the MFDn code in a proton-neutron M-scheme basis, with harmonic oscillator single-particle states truncated to a maximum number Nmax of oscillator excitations. The Hamiltonian matrix eigenproblem was solved using two different realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions: the JISP16 interaction (charge-independent two-body interaction from scattering data) and the NNLOopt interaction (from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order). Calculations were carried out for Nmax values from 2 to 10 at various oscillator energies ħω, with states of different parity solved separately.

## Key numbers

- For ⁸Be, the calculated E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) ratio is approximately 3.42 with JISP16 and 3.46 with NNLOopt, compared to the experimental value of approximately 3.75(5) and the rotational expectation of 10/3 ≈ 3.33
- Nmax=10 calculations for ⁸Be involve a Hamiltonian matrix dimension of ~2×10⁸
- The optimal oscillator energy for Be isotopes is typically in the range ħω ≈ 20–25 MeV
- Even at Nmax=10, changes in the variational minimum energy for ⁸Be are still at the MeV scale
- The experimental E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) for ⁸Be is approximately 3.75(5)

## Context

This work extends previous systematic studies of rotational bands in NCCI calculations of ⁷⁻¹²Be using the JISP16 interaction (Caprio 2013, Maris 2015, Caprio 2015) and investigations of spin and orbital angular momentum structure in ⁷Li and ⁹Be using a chiral N³LO interaction (Johnson 2015). The review enables quantitative comparison of ab initio rotational predictions with experimental band properties and provides a framework for understanding how collective rotation emerges from underlying internucleon interactions, with implications for future studies of heavier nuclei and more complex rotational structures.
