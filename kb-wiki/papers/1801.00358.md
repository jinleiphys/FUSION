# Chiral phase transition at finite chemical potential in 2+1-flavor soft-wall AdS/QCD

> The phase transition from hadronic matter to chirally-symmetric quark-gluon plasma is expected to be a rapid crossover at zero quark chemical potential ($μ$), becoming first order at some finite value of $μ$, indicating the presence of a critical point. Using a three-flavor soft-wall model of AdS/QCD, we investigate the effect of varying the light and strange quark masses on the order of the chiral phase transition. At zero quark chemical potential, we reproduce the Columbia Plot, which summarizes the results of lattice QCD and other holographic models. We then extend this holographic model to examine the effects of finite quark chemical potential. We find that the the chemical potential does not affect the critical line that separates first-order from rapid crossover transitions. This excludes the possibility of a critical point in this model, suggesting that a different setup is necessary to reproduce all the features of the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in this 2+1-flavor soft-wall AdS/QCD model, the quark chemical potential does not affect the critical line separating first-order from rapid crossover transitions, which excludes the possibility of a critical point in the T-mu plane. This contradicts the expected QCD phase diagram where a critical point is anticipated at finite chemical potential.

## Method

The authors use a 2+1-flavor soft-wall AdS/QCD model with an AdS-Reissner-Nordstrom black hole metric to incorporate finite temperature and chemical potential. They numerically solve the equations of motion for the chiral fields (light and strange) using a shooting method, imposing UV boundary conditions that relate the chiral fields to quark masses and condensates, and requiring regularity at the black hole horizon. The scalar potential includes a mass term, a quartic term for independent explicit and spontaneous symmetry breaking, and a cubic t'Hooft determinant term for flavor mixing.

## Key numbers

- Critical quark mass separating first-order from crossover transitions in the flavor-symmetric case (m_l = m_s): m_q = 35 MeV
- Tri-critical point (end of first-order region) occurs at m_s = 200 MeV
- Physical point (m_l = 5 MeV, m_s = 95 MeV) lies within the first-order region at mu = 0, contradicting lattice QCD expectations
- Representative calculations shown at mu = 0 MeV and mu = 300 MeV, with no change in transition order
- For m_l = 10 MeV, m_s = 10 MeV at mu = 10 MeV, the critical temperature T_c is approximately 0.185 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor holographic studies by Bartz (2016) and the zero-chemical-potential Columbia Plot analysis by Li (2017) to finite chemical potential in a 2+1-flavor model. The results contradict lattice QCD findings by deForcrand (2006), Bonati (2014), and Bellwied (2016) which place limits on a critical point, and differ from other holographic models by Critelli (2017) and Knaute (2017) that show evidence of a critical point through baryon susceptibilities. The paper suggests that a different setup is necessary to reproduce all features of the QCD phase diagram, such as coupling chemical potential to light quarks only or solving the dilaton and metric dynamically.
