# Spectral-shift and scattering-equivalent Hamiltonians on a coarse momentum grid

> The solution of the scattering problem based on the Lippmann-Schwinger equation requires in many cases a discretization of the spectrum in the continuum which does not respect the unitary equivalence of the S-matrix on the finite grid. We present a new prescription for the calculation of phase shifts based on the shift that is produced in the spectrum of a Chebyshev-angle variable. This is analogous to the energy shift that is produced in the energy levels of a scattering process in a box, when an interaction is introduced. Our formulation holds for any momentum grid and preserves the unitary equivalence of the scattering problem on the finite momentum grid. We illustrate this procedure numerically considering the non-relativistic NN case for $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ channels. Our spectral shift formula provides much more accurate results than the previous ones and turns out to be at least as competitive as the standard procedures for calculating phase shifts.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new prescription for calculating phase shifts from a discretized momentum grid that preserves unitary equivalence of the S-matrix on the finite grid, unlike the standard Lippmann-Schwinger equation solution which does not remain invariant under unitary transformations on the grid. The method is claimed to be "much more accurate" than previous spectral-shift formulas and "at least as competitive" as standard procedures, with the hedge that this holds specifically for the Gauss-Chebyshev grid where the Chebyshev angle is the equidistant variable.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the Hamiltonian on a finite Gauss-Chebyshev momentum grid of N points and compute the phase shift from the shift in the Chebyshev-angle variable (phi) between the free and interacting spectra, analogous to the energy shift in a spherical box. They compare this phi-shift prescription against the standard Lippmann-Schwinger equation solution on the grid and against two older spectral-shift formulas (momentum-shift and energy-shift), using a separable Gaussian potential model for the NN system in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels.

## Key numbers

- For the 1S0 channel, the separable Gaussian potential parameters are C = -1.92 fm and L = 1.20 fm^{-1}; for the 3S1 channel, C = -2.30 fm and L = 1.55 fm^{-1}
- The phi-shift formula reproduces the exact continuum solution even with as few as N = 10 grid points, while the standard LS equation solution requires a "comparatively larger number of grid points" to converge
- The phi-shift formula is given explicitly as: delta_n = (2N/Lambda) * [sqrt(p_n(Lambda-P_n)) - sqrt(P_n(Lambda-p_n))], where Lambda is the UV cutoff, p_n are free momenta, and P_n are interacting momenta

## Context

This work extends the spectral-shift approach originally proposed by Fukuda and Newton (1956) for the momentum-shift prescription and by DeWitt (1956) for the energy-shift prescription, which assumed uniform grids. The authors had previously shown (Arriola, 2014; Arriola, 2016) that the standard LS equation on a finite grid violates phase-equivalence under unitary transformations. The new phi-shift method enables accurate phase-shift calculations on coarse momentum grids, which is relevant for non-local interactions common in low- and intermediate-energy hadronic physics, and the authors note extensions to coupled channels and relativistic systems are forthcoming (Gomez-Rocha, 2019).
