# Bulk Viscosity in Neutron Stars from Hyperons

> The contribution from hyperons to the bulk viscosity of neutron star matter is calculated. Compared to previous works we use for the weak interaction the one-pion exchange model rather than a current-current interaction, and include the neutral current $nn \leftrightarrow nΛ$ process. Also the sensitivity to details of the equation of state is examined. Compared to previous works we find that the contribution from hyperons to the bulk viscosity is about two orders of magnitude smaller.

## Key claim

The contribution from hyperons to the bulk viscosity of neutron star matter is about two orders of magnitude smaller than found in previous works, when using the one-pion exchange model for the weak interaction instead of the contact W exchange model and including the neutral current \(nn \leftrightarrow n\Lambda\) process.

## Method

The authors calculated the bulk viscosity from weak nonleptonic hyperon processes (\(nn \leftrightarrow p\Sigma^-\), \(pn \leftrightarrow p\Lambda\), and \(nn \leftrightarrow n\Lambda\)) using a finite temperature Green's function formalism in the quasi-particle approximation, with the weak interaction described by one-pion exchange in Born approximation rather than the contact W exchange used previously. The collision rates were evaluated assuming strong degeneracy of the participating baryons, and the sensitivity to the equation of state was examined using two parametrizations from Balberg and Gal (1997) with different density dependences (\(\gamma=\delta=2\) and \(\gamma=\delta=5/3\)).

## Key numbers

- The bulk viscosity from hyperons is about two orders of magnitude smaller than previous calculations using the contact W exchange interaction.
- The inverse relaxation time for the weak nonleptonic hyperon processes scales as \(\tau^{-1} \propto T^2\), compared to \(\tau^{-1} \propto T^4\) for the direct Urca process and \(\tau^{-1} \propto T^6\) for the modified Urca process.
- The neutron star pulsation frequency considered is \(\omega \sim 10^{3}-10^{4} \ \textrm{s}^{-1}\).
- Typical neutron star temperatures considered are \(T \sim 10^9-10^{10} \ \textrm{K}\).
- The saturation density is \(n_0 \approx 0.15 \ {\rm fm}^{-3}\).
- The Urca limit for the proton fraction is \(x_p \approx 0.11\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of bulk viscosity in neutron stars from hyperons by Haensel et al. (2002) and Lindblom and Owen (2002), who used a contact W exchange interaction and did not include the neutral current \(nn \leftrightarrow n\Lambda\) process. The authors adopt a more realistic one-pion exchange model motivated by weak hyperon decay experiments in hypernuclei, as described by Alberico and Garbarino (2002). The results enable more accurate modeling of damping of neutron star pulsations and gravitational radiation driven instabilities, particularly r-mode damping.
