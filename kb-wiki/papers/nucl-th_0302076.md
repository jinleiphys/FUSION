# Alpha-particle condensation in nuclei

> A round up of the present status of the conjecture that n alpha nuclei form an alpha-particle condensate in excited states close to the n alpha threshold is given. Experiments which could demonstrate the condensate character are proposed. Possible lines of further theoretical developments are discussed.

## Key claim
Light nα nuclei (such as ⁸Be, ¹²C, ¹⁶O) may exhibit excited states near the nα disintegration threshold that are dilute, self-bound gases of almost unperturbed α-particles all occupying the same 0s orbital, forming a Bose condensate analogous to atomic Bose-Einstein condensates; the claim is hedged by the fact that the condensate character remains conjectural and requires experimental confirmation.

## Method
The authors constructed a variational wavefunction for nα nuclei in which each α-cluster occupies the same 0s harmonic oscillator orbit with a width parameter B, while the internal α-cluster wavefunction has a separate width b. They treated B and b as Hill-Wheeler coordinates and solved the Hill-Wheeler equation for spherical 0⁺ states of ⁸Be, ¹²C, and ¹⁶O, using the Tohsaki effective nucleon-nucleon force (a sum of two-body and three-body Gaussians fitted to α-α scattering and α-particle properties, including the Coulomb force) with no adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers
- The Hill-Wheeler calculation yields one 0⁺ state for ⁸Be, two for ¹²C, and three for ¹⁶O, with energies within "some hundred keV" of experiment for the ground state of ⁸Be, the second 0⁺ in ¹²C (relative to the 3α threshold), and the fifth 0⁺ in ¹⁶O (relative to the 4α threshold).
- The threshold states in ¹²C and ¹⁶O have approximately three times the volume of their respective ground states.
- The spin-orbit splitting in ¹³C corresponding to the 0₂⁺ state in ¹²C is only half of that corresponding to the 0₁⁺ state.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier conjecture (Schuck et al., 2002) that nα nuclei can form α-particle condensates, analogous to atomic Bose-Einstein condensates in magnetic traps. It contrasts with standard resonating group calculations, which have achieved the correct position of the second 0⁺ state in ¹²C, and with AMD calculations (recent at the time) that miss that state's energy by several MeV. The paper proposes experimental tests (e.g., multiparticle detection of α decay from excited condensate states, reduced moment of inertia in rotating condensates) and outlines future theoretical developments including a four-body gap equation and effective Gross-Pitaevskii-type treatments for larger α numbers.
