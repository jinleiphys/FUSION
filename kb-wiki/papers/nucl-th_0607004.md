# Configuration mixing calculation for complete low-lying spectra with the mean-field Hamiltonian

> We propose a new theoretical approach to ground and low-energy excited states of nuclei extending the nuclear mean-field theory. It consists of three steps: stochastic preparation of many Slater determinants, the parity and angular momentum projection, and diagonalization of the generalized eigenvalue problems. The Slater determinants are constructed in the three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate representation capable of describing arbitrary shape of nuclei. We examine feasibility and usefulness of the method by applying the method with the BKN interaction to light 4N-nuclei, 12C, 16O, and 20Ne. We discuss difficulties of keeping linear independence for basis states projected on good parity and angular momentum and present a possible prescription.

## Key claim

The authors propose a method to obtain ground and low-lying excited states of nuclei by stochastically generating many Slater determinants, projecting them onto good parity and angular momentum, and then solving a generalized eigenvalue problem, with the caveat that the approach is designed to capture only long-range correlations because the short-range correlation is already renormalized into the effective interaction.

## Method

The method consists of three steps: (1) stochastic generation of many Slater determinants using imaginary-time evolution from randomly initialized Gaussian orbitals, followed by selection based on energy (below Hartree-Fock ground state plus 30 MeV) and overlap (maximum overlap less than 0.7 after parity projection and axis permutations); (2) parity and angular momentum projection onto good quantum numbers using three-dimensional Euler angle integration with finite-difference rotation operators; (3) diagonalization of the generalized eigenvalue problem after screening basis states to remove those with small norm eigenvalues (below 10^-3) to avoid numerical instability from overcompleteness. The method is applied to light 4N-nuclei (12C, 16O, 20Ne) using the BKN interaction with a three-dimensional Cartesian grid representation (grid spacing 0.8 fm, sphere radius 8 fm).

## Key numbers

- For 16O, the cut-off energy for selecting Slater determinants is 30 MeV above the Hartree-Fock ground-state energy.
- Overlap threshold for adding a new Slater determinant to the basis set is 0.7 (condition b).
- For the angular momentum projection, discretization uses 15 grid points for alpha and gamma angles and 20 points for beta angle, requiring evaluation of 4,500 matrix elements per pair of Slater determinants.
- Norm eigenvalues smaller than 10^-2 are discarded after K-diagonalization; for J^pi=3^- in 16O, about 100 out of 350 configurations are discarded by this criterion.
- The smallest eigenvalue of the normalized norm matrix must be greater than 10^-3; for J^pi=3^- in 16O, the final number of basis states after screening is approximately 50.
- For 16O, there are 19 basis states for J^pi=0^+ and 53 for J^pi=3^- after all screening.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field approach by going beyond a single Slater determinant to include long-range correlations, building on the generator coordinate method (GCM) and the Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM). It is similar in concept to the MCSM of Otsuka et al. but uses a mean-field Hamiltonian with a much wider model space than the shell model, and differs from the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) approach by not restricting orbitals to Gaussian form. The method enables systematic calculation of complete low-lying spectra for nuclei across a wide mass region, addressing limitations of the shell model (which fails for states like the second 0^+ state in 16O) and cluster models (which are limited to light nuclei).
