# A proof of quark confinement in QCD

> I propose to reformulate the gauge field theory as the perturbative deformation of a novel topological quantum field theory. It is shown that this reformulation leads to quark confinement in QCD$_4$. Similarly, the fractional charge confinement is also derived in the strong coupling phase of QED$_4$. As a confinement criterion, we use the area decay of the expectation value of the Wilson loop.

# A proof of quark confinement in QCD

## Key claim

The paper claims that quark confinement in 4D QCD can be proven by reformulating the theory as a perturbative deformation of a topological quantum field theory (TQFT), which then reduces to a 2D nonlinear sigma model (NLSM) where the area law for the Wilson loop can be derived from instanton calculus. The proof is exact except for the instanton calculus step, which relies on a dilute gas approximation.

## Method

The author reformulates QCD in the maximal Abelian gauge (MAG) as a perturbative deformation of a TQFT that describes 4D magnetic monopoles. Using Parisi-Sourlas dimensional reduction, the TQFT is shown to be equivalent to a G/H nonlinear sigma model in (D-2) dimensions, reducing 4D QCD to a 2D NLSM. The Wilson loop expectation value in the 2D NLSM is then evaluated using instanton calculus (dilute gas approximation), yielding an area law for the diagonal string tension, which saturates the full non-Abelian string tension for large Wilson loops via the non-Abelian Stokes theorem.

## Key numbers

- The string tension is defined as $\sigma := - \lim_{A(C) \rightarrow \infty} (1/A(C)) \ln \langle W^C[\mathcal{A}] \rangle_{YM_4}$, where $A(C)$ is the area of the Wilson loop.
- The diagonal string tension $\sigma_{Abel}$ is defined analogously using the diagonal Wilson loop, and the relation $|\sigma - \sigma_{Abel}| \downarrow 0$ holds as $A(C) \uparrow \infty$.
- The instanton number in the 2D NLSM is given by $Q_S = (1/8\pi) \int_S d^2z \, \epsilon_{\mu\nu} \mathbf{n} \cdot (\partial_\mu \mathbf{n} \times \partial_\nu \mathbf{n})$, and the diagonal Wilson loop becomes $\langle e^{i (q/g) 8\pi Q_S} \rangle_{NLSM_2}$.
- For the gauge group $G=SU(2)$, the G/H NLSM is equivalent to the O(3) NLSM, and $\Pi_2(SU(N)/U(1)^{N-1}) = \Pi_1(U(1)^{N-1}) = \mathbf{Z}^{N-1}$, guaranteeing instanton existence for any $N$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Polyakov (1975, 1977) for 3D compact U(1) and the Georgi-Glashow model, and the Seiberg-Witten (1994) solution for 4D N=2 supersymmetric Yang-Mills, to non-supersymmetric 4D QCD. It provides an analytic framework to explain the "monopole dominance" observed in numerical simulations under the Abelian projection of 't Hooft (1981). The same strategy is applied to QED$_4$ to prove the existence of a strong coupling confinement phase, following from the Berezinski-Kosterlitz-Thouless transition of the O(2) NLSM$_2$.
