# Comment on "Influence of protons on the capture of electrons by 7Be in the Sun"

> This paper suffers from conceptual difficulties and unjustified approximations that render its conclusions dubious.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the claims in Belyaev et al. (2007) regarding a new three-body electron capture process for 7Be in the Sun are incorrect because the influence of nearby protons is already included in standard plasma screening corrections, and the ternary reaction concept is a mischaracterization of a binary reaction occurring in a plasma environment.

## Method

The authors identify conceptual difficulties in the target paper by analyzing its treatment of the electron capture reaction as a three-body process (7Be + e- + p) rather than a binary reaction (7Be + e-) occurring in a plasma. They compare the claimed approach against established equilibrium statistical mechanics methods for computing plasma screening corrections, as developed by Salpeter (1954) and DeWitt et al. (1973), and against detailed density matrix calculations by Gruzinov and Bahcall (1997) that included effects of nearby ions and thermal fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The nearest proton remains on average some 30,000 fm away from the electron when captured by 7Be at zero range, which is more than 10^7 times the Compton wavelength of the W boson (the range of the weak interaction mediating electron capture)
- The small parameter epsilon used in the target paper's expansion is approximately 0.0218, not 0.0233 as stated in the target paper
- Density matrix calculations by Gruzinov and Bahcall (1997) agree with the standard mean field approach within 1%
- The effects of aspherical fluctuations in the ion distribution were found to be smaller than 1%
- A recent review by Adelberger et al. (1998) concluded the 7Be electron capture rate in the Sun is known within 2%

## Context

This comment extends the standard treatment of plasma screening corrections to nuclear reaction rates first discovered by Salpeter (1954) and later refined by DeWitt et al. (1973). It directly contradicts the claims of Belyaev et al. (2007) by showing that the effects of nearby ions are already comprehensively included in existing calculations, particularly the density matrix calculations of Gruzinov and Bahcall (1997) and the review by Adelberger et al. (1998). The comment enables continued use of the standard Salpeter screening factor for solar model calculations, concluding that the arguments in the target paper should be ignored.
