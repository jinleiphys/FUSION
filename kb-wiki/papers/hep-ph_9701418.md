# Low-Energy QCD and Ultraviolet Renormalons

> We discuss the contribution of ultraviolet (UV) renormalons in QCD to two-point functions of quark current operators. This explicitly includes effects due to the exchange of one renormalon chain as well as two chains. It is shown that, when the external euclidean momentum of the two-point functions becomes smaller than the scale $Λ_L$ associated with the Landau singularity of the QCD one-loop running coupling constant, the positions of the UV renormalons in the Borel plane become true singularities in the integration range of the Borel transform. This introduces ambiguities in the evaluation of the corresponding two-point functions. The ambiguities associated with the leading UV renormalon singularity are of the same type as the contribution due to the inclusion of dimension d=6 local operators in a low-energy effective Lagrangian valid at scales smaller than $Λ_L$. We then discuss the inclusion of an infinite number of renormalon chains and argue that the previous ambiguity hints at a plausible approximation scheme for low-energy QCD, resulting in an effective Lagrangian similar to the one of the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model of QCD at large N_c.

## Key claim
When the external Euclidean momentum of two-point functions falls below the Landau pole scale Λ_L, the leading ultraviolet renormalon singularity in the Borel plane produces ambiguities that are of the same type as contributions from dimension d=6 local operators in a low-energy effective Lagrangian, hinting at a connection to the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model of QCD at large N_c.

## Method
The authors calculate the contribution of ultraviolet renormalons to two-point functions of colour-singlet vector and pseudoscalar quark currents, using an "amputated" generating functional that replaces gluon propagators with an effective charge derived from the pinch technique at one-loop level. They evaluate the exchange of one and two renormalon chains (gluon self-energy-like insertions) via both Gegenbauer expansion of Feynman diagrams and operator product expansion techniques, working in the chiral limit for light u, d, s quarks. The analysis focuses on the regime where the external Euclidean momentum Q^2 is smaller than Λ_L^2, the Landau pole scale.

## Key numbers
- The Landau pole scale is defined as Λ_L = c × Λ_MS, with c(N_c=3, n_f=3) = 2.87, and Λ_MS ~ 300-400 MeV, giving Λ_L ~ 1 GeV, numerically similar to the chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ.
- The leading infrared renormalon ambiguity in the Adler function scales as (Λ_L/Q)^4, with the form δA(Q^2) ~ (N_c/16π^2) × (C_F/(-β_1)) × (Λ_L/Q)^4.
- The first coefficient of the QCD β-function at one loop is β_1 = (-11/6)N_c + (1/3)n_f.
- The function Ξ(z) at z=1 evaluates to Ξ(1) = 14/3 - (4/9)π^2 = 0.2802.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of ultraviolet renormalons in QCD, following the observation by Vainshtein and Zakharov (1994) that multi-chain contributions to the Adler function in an Abelian-like model could produce 1/Q^2-like ambiguities. The paper connects these renormalon ambiguities to the appearance of dimension-six four-fermion operators, similar to those in the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model used successfully by Bijnens, Bruno, and de Rafael (1993) to predict low-energy chiral coupling constants. This suggests a possible bridge between perturbative QCD and low-energy effective Lagrangians, with the Landau pole scale Λ_L naturally identified with the chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ.
