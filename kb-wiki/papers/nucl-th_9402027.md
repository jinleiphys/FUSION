# A Fully Isotopic Model of Fragmentation

> A general model for the fragmentation of a two-component system (e.g. protons and neutrons) is proposed and solved exactly. The extension of this model to any number of components is also shown to be exactly solvable. A connection between this models and the permutation group is discussed. The notion of isotopic equivalence is defined in order to evaluate the equivalence of these models to earlier one-component models. All the one-component models considered in earlier papers are shown to be equivalent to a particular class of two-component models. A simplified model applicable to the case of nuclear fragmentation is introduced and analyzed. Modifications to this model to include effects such as pairing and Coulomb interactions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes and exactly solves a general statistical model for the fragmentation of a two-component system (protons and neutrons), showing that a particular class of such models is exactly solvable by recursion, which obviates the need for Monte Carlo methods and allows exact evaluation of all expectation values. The authors hedge that realistic models with symmetry or Coulomb terms are not isotopically equivalent to earlier one-component models, and that interfragment interactions must be handled in a mean-field manner.

## Method

The authors define a canonical weight for two-component partitions using Gibbs factors and a parameter vector x_{jk}, then derive recursive relations for the partition function Z_{ZN}(x) from the constraints on total proton and neutron numbers. They connect this model to the permutation group S_A via cycle decomposition, deriving Cauchy-type counting formulas that map permutations to partitions. Using this permutation representation, they prove that certain two-component models are isotopically equivalent to one-component models when the parameters satisfy a non-interference condition, and they construct explicit two-component extensions of known one-component models.

## Context

This work extends the one-component fragmentation models of Mekjian (1991), Chase (1993), and Sneppen (1993) to a fully isotopic two-component framework, providing exact solutions where previous work relied on Monte Carlo methods. The paper establishes a connection to Kingman's non-interference condition (Kingman, 1978) for partition models, and shows that the chain model of Gross et al. (1990) can be extended to two components. The results enable exact calculation of isotopic distributions in nuclear fragmentation, including effects of symmetry energy, pairing, and Coulomb interactions, which were previously difficult or impossible to treat in one-component models.
