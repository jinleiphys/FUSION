# Proton induced $K^+$ production and the $s\bar{s}$ content of the proton

> We investigate the exclusive proton-induced $K^+$ production near the kaon threshold. Compared are two models: a meson-exchange model, which includes $π$, ρ$, $K$ and $K^\ast$ exchange, together with the dominant baryon resonances, and a quark-gluon model, where momentum sharing is provided via the exchange of two gluons. Based on covariant bound state wave functions for the meson and the baryons - which we treat as quark-diquark objects - first results for the $pp\to pΛK^+$ total cross section are presented for both models.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary results for the exclusive proton-induced kaon production near threshold, comparing a meson-exchange model and a quark-gluon model; however, a detailed conclusion on the s̄s content of the proton requires further numerical study and consistency tests for other meson channels.

## Method

The authors compare two theoretical approaches for the reaction pp → pΛK⁺: a meson-exchange model incorporating π, ρ, K, and K* exchange with dominant baryon resonances in the 1.6 to 1.8 GeV region, and a quark-gluon model where momentum sharing occurs via two-gluon exchange between constituent quarks. Both models use covariant bound state wave functions derived from a Bethe-Salpeter equation reduced to a three-dimensional quasi-potential equation, with baryons treated as quark-diquark objects. The strangeness content in the proton is defined via an impulse approximation relation involving the 3q and 3q,s̄s Fock components.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: m_u,d = 330 MeV, m_s = 505 MeV
- QCD scale parameter: Λ_QCD = 250 MeV
- The intrinsic short-range scale of proton-induced K production is λ ∼ 0.2–0.5 fm
- Baryon resonances in the meson-exchange model are included in the region of 1.6 to 1.8 GeV
- The large momentum transfer in the reaction is typically ≥ 1 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD-inspired quark model calculations that described mesons and baryons as qq̄ and 3q valence quark objects, motivated by the "spin crisis of the nucleon" which raised questions about sea quark admixtures in hadrons (Alvarez-Gaume, 1986). The study is connected to experimental activities at hadron factories such as COSY and CELSIUS, and the authors note that the meson-exchange model reproduces existing data (Flaminio, 1984) only qualitatively, with serious shortcomings due to its nonrelativistic nature and sensitivity to off-shell parametrizations. The work enables future studies of the s̄s content of the proton through exclusive kaon production, with further consistency tests needed for other meson channels.
