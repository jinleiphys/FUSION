# Spin polarization of fermions at local equilibrium: Second-order gradient expansion

> We present a calculation of the spin polarization of spin-1/2 fermions in a relativistic fluid at local thermodynamic equilibrium at the second order in the gradient expansion, including second-order derivatives. The second-order derivative terms vanish if the local equilibrium hypersurface is the hyperplane $t=const$ in the collision center-of-mass frame. However, since the freeze-out hypersurface has a non-trivial space-time structure, these terms may result in a non-vanishing contribution to the spin polarization, whose magnitude needs to be assessed with numerical computations.

## Key claim

The authors calculate spin polarization of spin-1/2 fermions at local thermodynamic equilibrium including second-order gradient terms, but note that these second-order derivative contributions vanish if the freeze-out hypersurface is the hyperplane \(t=const\) in the collision center-of-mass frame; their magnitude can only be assessed through numerical computations because the freeze-out hypersurface has a non-trivial space-time structure.

## Method

The authors expand the local equilibrium density operator in a Taylor series of thermodynamic fields around the point where the operator is evaluated, then use the Kubo identity to compute the mean value of the Wigner function for free spin-1/2 fermions up to quadratic order in the gradient expansion. They express the spin polarization vector via the trace of the Wigner function with \(\gamma^\mu\gamma^5\) and separate contributions into first-order terms (thermal vorticity, thermal shear tensor, gradient of chemical potential) and second-order terms (second derivatives of \(\beta\) and \(\zeta\), products of first derivatives, and derivatives of the spin potential).

## Key numbers

* The component \(\partial_x \varpi_{tz}\) can be as large as \(0.3\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1} \approx 0.05\,\mathrm{GeV}\) at some points on the freeze-out hypersurface, based on a naive estimation from Karpenko (2016).
* Assuming a typical energy scale \(E\approx 1\,\mathrm{GeV}\), the ratio \(\partial_x \varpi_{tz}/E\approx 0.05\), which is the same order as \(\varpi_{tz}\) itself.
* The Knudsen number \(K_n = \lambda/|\beta/\partial \beta|\) must satisfy \(K_n \ll 1\) for the hydrodynamic limit to hold, where \(\lambda\) is the interaction length and \(\beta\) the four-temperature field.

## Context

This work extends the first-order gradient expansion of spin polarization in relativistic fluids, previously developed by Becattini (2021) and Liu (2021, 2020), which included thermal vorticity, thermal shear tensor, and chemical potential gradients. The first-order models successfully reproduced global \(\Lambda\) hyperon polarization data from STAR (2017, 2018, 2019) and ALICE (2019, 2021) but failed to describe the momentum dependence, motivating the need for second-order terms. The present calculation provides the analytic framework for second-order non-dissipative contributions, enabling future numerical simulations to assess whether these terms resolve the discrepancy between theory and experiment.
