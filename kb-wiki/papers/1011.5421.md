# The competition between superconductivity and ferromagnetism in small metallic grains: thermodynamic properties

> We study the thermodynamic properties of a small superconducting metallic grain using a quantum Monte Carlo method. The grain is described by the universal Hamiltonian, containing pairing and ferromagnetic exchange correlations. In particular, we study how the thermodynamic signatures of pairing correlations are affected by the spin exchange interaction. We find the exchange interaction effects to be qualitatively different in the BCS and fluctuation-dominated regimes of pairing correlations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the thermodynamic signatures of pairing correlations in small metallic grains are qualitatively different in the BCS regime and the fluctuation-dominated regime when a ferromagnetic exchange interaction is present; in the fluctuation-dominated regime the exchange interaction can destroy the odd-even effects in heat capacity and canonical pair gap, while in the BCS regime these signatures survive even for strong exchange.

## Method

The authors used a quantum Monte Carlo method based on canonical loop updates to simulate the universal Hamiltonian of a metallic grain, which includes both a BCS-like pairing interaction and a ferromagnetic exchange term proportional to the square of the total spin. They computed thermodynamic properties for even and odd grains with a half-filled picket-fence single-particle spectrum at three values of the BCS gap ratio (Delta/delta = 0.5, 1, and 5) and for exchange couplings J_s ranging from 0 to 0.91 delta. The exchange term was incorporated by adding it to the diagonal part of the Hamiltonian and sampling the total spin of each configuration according to the degeneracy of blocked levels.

## Key numbers

*   The ground-state spin of an even grain with no pairing (Delta/delta = 0) changes from S=0 to S=1 at J_s = 0.5 delta, and to S=4 at J_s = 0.875 delta.
*   For an even grain with Delta/delta = 0.5, the ground-state spin changes from S=0 to S=1 at J_s = 0.8379 delta, and to S=4 at J_s = 0.9147 delta.
*   For an even grain with Delta/delta = 1, the ground-state spin makes a direct transition from S=0 to S=4 at J_s = 0.9295 delta.
*   For a strong pairing interaction (Delta/delta = 5), the even grain remains fully paired up to J_s/delta approximately 1, at which it makes a transition to a fully polarized state via a Stoner instability at J_s = 1.0029 delta.
*   The temperature at which pairs start to break up for Delta/delta = 5 and J_s = 0 is roughly 1.5 delta, while for Delta/delta = 0.5 it is about 0.4 delta.
*   For Delta/delta = 1 and J_s = 0.6 delta, about 84% of electrons are still paired to S=0 at T = 3.5 delta.

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing correlations in small metallic grains, previously investigated using the reduced BCS Hamiltonian by Muhlschlegel (1972), von Delft (1996), and others, to include the ferromagnetic exchange interaction that appears in the universal Hamiltonian derived by Kurland (2000) and Aleiner (2002) for chaotic or weakly diffusive grains. The authors had previously studied the ground-state phase diagram of this model in Schmidt (2007) and mesoscopic fluctuations in conductance in Schmidt (2008). The present results show how the exchange interaction competes with pairing in thermodynamic observables, and they enable future experimental tests of pairing signatures in grains made of materials with strong exchange, such as palladium, for which J_s/delta values of 0.84-0.89 were reported by Gorokhov (2004).
