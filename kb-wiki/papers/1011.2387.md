# Analysis of the new Crystal Ball data on $K^-p\toπ^0Λ$ reaction with beam momenta of $514\sim 750$ MeV/$c$

> The Crystal Ball Collaboration has recently reported the differential cross sections and $Λ$ polarization for the reaction $K^-p\rightarrow π^0Λ$ using an incident $K^-$ beam with momenta between 514 and 750 MeV/$c$. We make a partial wave analysis for this process with an effective Lagrangian approach and study the properties of some $Σ$ resonances around this energy range. With the inclusion of the 4-star resonances $Σ(1189)$, $Σ^*(1385)$, $Σ(1670){3\over 2}^-$, $Σ(1775){5\over 2}^-$, as well as a $Σ(1635){1\over 2}^+$, which is compatible with the 3-star $Σ(1660){1\over 2}^+$ in PDG, our results can well reproduce the experimental data. The parameters on the $Σ$ resonances and related couplings are studied.

# Analysis of the new Crystal Ball data on $K^-p\to\pi^0\Lambda$ reaction with beam momenta of $514\sim 750$ MeV/$c$

## Key claim

A partial wave analysis of the Crystal Ball data for $K^-p\to\pi^0\Lambda$ using an effective Lagrangian approach finds that the four established 4-star $\Sigma$ resonances alone are insufficient to describe the data ($\chi^2 \sim 763$ for 248 data points), but the inclusion of an additional $\Sigma(1635){1\over 2}^+$ resonance, compatible with the 3-star $\Sigma(1660){1\over 2}^+$ in the Particle Data Group, reduces the $\chi^2$ to 223, providing further support for the existence of this resonance.

## Method

The authors performed a partial wave analysis of the reaction $K^-p\to\pi^0\Lambda$ using an effective Lagrangian approach with Feynman diagrams including t-channel $K^*$ meson exchange, u-channel proton exchange, and s-channel $\Sigma$ resonance exchanges. The amplitudes included couplings up to D-wave for intermediate states with $J^P = {1\over 2}^\pm$, ${3\over 2}^\pm$, and ${5\over 2}^-$, with form factors of the form $\Lambda^4/(\Lambda^4 + (q_{ex}^2 - M_{ex}^2)^2)$ and cutoff parameters constrained between 0.8 and 1.5 GeV. The model was fitted simultaneously to differential cross sections and $\Lambda$ polarization data at beam momenta from 514 to 750 MeV/$c$ (center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=1569-1676$ MeV).

## Key numbers

- The best fit including only the four 4-star $\Sigma$ resonances ($\Sigma(1189)$, $\Sigma^*(1385)$, $\Sigma(1670){3\over 2}^-$, $\Sigma(1775){5\over 2}^-$) gave $\chi^2 = 763$ for 248 data points.
- Including an additional $\Sigma({1\over 2}^+)$ resonance with mass $1635^{+3}_{-4}$ MeV and width $121^{+12}_{-10}$ MeV reduced $\chi^2$ to 223 for 248 data points.
- The fitted mass of $\Sigma(1670){3\over 2}^-$ was $1673.4^{+1.1}_{-0.8}$ MeV with width $54\pm 5$ MeV, and $(\Gamma_{\pi\Lambda}\Gamma_{KN})^{1\over 2}/\Gamma_{\rm tot} = 0.08^{+0.02}_{-0.015}$.
- The t-channel $K^*$ coupling $g_{K^*N\Lambda}$ was fitted to $-6.11^{+0.15}_{-0}$ (constrained between $-6.11$ and $-4.26$ from the NSC97a/f potential models).
- The product $f_{KN\Sigma^*}f_{\Sigma^*\Lambda\pi}$ for $\Sigma^*(1385)$ was fitted to $-3.95^{+0.35}_{-0.38}$ (SU(3) prediction: $-4.1$).
- The product $g_{KN\Sigma}g_{\Sigma\Lambda\pi}$ for $\Sigma(1189)$ was fitted to $49.1^{+0}_{-1}$ (SU(3) prediction: $34.8$).
- The product $g_{\pi NN}g_{KN\Lambda}$ was fitted to $-177^{+9}_{-7}$ (SU(3) prediction: $-176$).

## Context

This work extends the earlier energy-dependent partial wave analysis of $\bar K N \to \pi\Lambda$ by Van Horn (1975) and the energy-independent analysis by Baillon (1975), which both used Breit-Wigner resonance terms with polynomial background parametrizations. The present analysis replaces the ad hoc background with physically motivated t-channel $K^*$ exchange and u-channel proton exchange amplitudes, constrained by potential model predictions from Stoks and Rijken (1999). The results provide further support for the 3-star $\Sigma(1660){1\over 2}^+$ resonance listed in the Particle Data Group (2008) and enable future combined-channel analyses with wider energy ranges to improve knowledge of $\Sigma$ resonance properties and interactions.
