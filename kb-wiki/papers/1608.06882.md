# On the strength of the $U_A(1)$ anomaly at the chiral phase transition in $N_f=2$ QCD

> We study the thermal transition of QCD with two degenerate light flavours by lattice simulations using $O(a)$-improved Wilson quarks. Temperature scans are performed at a fixed value of $N_t = (aT)^{-1}=16$, where $a$ is the lattice spacing and $T$ the temperature, at three fixed zero-temperature pion masses between 200 MeV and 540 MeV. In this range we find that the transition is consistent with a broad crossover. As a probe of the restoration of chiral symmetry, we study the static screening spectrum. We observe a degeneracy between the transverse isovector vector and axial-vector channels starting from the transition temperature. Particularly striking is the strong reduction of the splitting between isovector scalar and pseudoscalar screening masses around the chiral phase transition by at least a factor of three compared to its value at zero temperature. In fact, the splitting is consistent with zero within our uncertainties. This disfavours a chiral phase transition in the $O(4)$ universality class.

## Key claim

The observed strong reduction of the splitting between isovector scalar and pseudoscalar screening masses around the chiral phase transition, by at least a factor of three compared to zero temperature and consistent with zero within uncertainties, disfavours a chiral phase transition in the O(4) universality class for two-flavour QCD.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using O(a)-improved Wilson quarks with two degenerate light flavours, conducting temperature scans at fixed N_t = 16 (where N_t is the temporal lattice extent in lattice spacing units) at three fixed zero-temperature pion masses between 200 MeV and 540 MeV. They computed static screening masses in the pseudoscalar (P), scalar (S), vector (V), and axial-vector (A) channels from correlation functions, using the bootstrap procedure with 1000 bins for error analysis and neglecting non-diagonal terms of correlation matrices in fits.

## Key numbers

* The splitting between isovector scalar and pseudoscalar screening masses around the chiral phase transition is reduced by at least a factor of three compared to its value at zero temperature
* Temperature scans were performed at three fixed zero-temperature pion masses: between 200 MeV and 540 MeV
* The temporal lattice extent was fixed at N_t = 16
* For scan C1 at T = 227 MeV, the screening mass difference aΔM_PS = -0.014(5) and aΔM_VA = 0.01(3) × 10^(-2)
* For scan D1 at T = 245 MeV, the screening mass difference aΔM_PS = -0.012(6) and aΔM_VA = 0.1(1) × 10^(-2)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of the N_f=2 chiral transition using staggered fermions (Karsch 1993, Karsch 1994, Aoki 1998, Bernard 1999, D'Elia 2005, Cossu 2007, Bonati 2009), O(a)-improved Wilson fermions (Ali Khan 2000, Bornyakov 2009, Bornyakov 2011), and domain wall/overlap fermions (Chiu 2013, Cossu 2013, Tomiya 2014, Cossu 2015), which had not provided a conclusive answer in the chiral limit. The results enable discrimination between the two possible scenarios for the QCD phase diagram: whether the chiral critical line reaches the m_ud = 0 axis (second order transition for N_f=2) or never reaches it (first order transition for all strange quark masses), with the outcome depending on the strength of the U_A(1) anomaly at the critical temperature.
