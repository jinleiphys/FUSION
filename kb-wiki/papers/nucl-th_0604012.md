# Evidence for the Strong Dominance of Proton-Neutron Correlations in Nuclei

> We analyze recent data from high-momentum-transfer $(p,pp)$ and $(p,ppn)$ reactions on Carbon. For this analysis, the two-nucleon short-range correlation (NN-SRC) model for backward nucleon emission is extended to include the motion of the NN-pair in the mean field. The model is found to describe major characteristics of the data. Our analysis demonstrates that the removal of a proton from the nucleus with initial momentum 275-550 MeV/c is $92^{+8}_{-18}%$ of the time accompanied by the emission of a correlated neutron that carries momentum roughly equal and opposite to the initial proton momentum. Within the NN-SRC dominance assumption the data indicate that the probabilities of $pp$ or $nn$ SRCs in the nucleus are at least a factor of six smaller than that of $pn$ SRCs. Our result is the first estimate of the isospin structure of NN-SRCs in nuclei, and may have important implication for modeling the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter.

# Evidence for the Strong Dominance of Proton-Neutron Correlations in Nuclei

## Key claim

The removal of a proton from a carbon nucleus with initial momentum 275–550 MeV/c is accompanied by the emission of a correlated neutron carrying roughly equal and opposite momentum in 92% of cases, with an asymmetric uncertainty of +8% and −18%. This result depends on the assumption that two-nucleon short-range correlations (NN-SRCs) dominate the nuclear wave function in this momentum range, and the analysis uses a specific model for the center-of-mass motion of the correlated pair.

## Method

The authors extended the NN-SRC model for backward nucleon emission in high-momentum-transfer (p,pp) and (p,ppn) reactions on ¹²C to include the center-of-mass motion of the NN-pair in the mean field. They used a light-cone distorted-wave impulse approximation (LC-DWIA) to factorize the hard pp→pp scattering cross section from the nuclear decay function, and extracted the relative probability of proton-neutron SRCs from the measured ratio of (p,ppn) to (p,pp) events. The analysis incorporated corrections for neutron attenuation (T_n = 0.85) and used the experimentally determined width of the SRC center-of-mass momentum distribution (σ_exp = 143 ± 17 MeV/c).

## Key numbers

- The ratio F = (number of (p,ppn) events with p₂,p_n > k_F) / (number of (p,pp) events with p₂ > k_F) was measured as F = 0.43⁺⁰·¹¹₋₀·₀₇ for p_min = 275 MeV/c.
- The probability that a removed proton originates from a pn-SRC is P_pn/pX = 0.92⁺⁰·⁰⁸₋₀·₁₈, corresponding to at least 74% of such events.
- The upper limit on the ratio of pp-SRC to pn-SRC probabilities is P_pp/P_pn ≤ 0.04⁺⁰·⁰⁹₋₀·₀₄, indicating pp and nn SRCs are at least a factor of six less probable than pn SRCs.
- The total probability of NN-SRCs in ¹²C is P_NN(¹²C) = 0.20 ± 0.042, from which P_pn(¹²C) = 0.184 ± 0.045 and P_pp(¹²C) = P_nn(¹²C) ≤ 0.03 are derived.
- The center-of-mass momentum width of the SRC was extracted as σ_exp = 143 ± 17 MeV/c, in agreement with the theoretical expectation of 139 MeV/c from Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1996).

## Context

This work extends the NN-SRC model used in earlier analyses of A(p,pp)X data by Yaron (2004) to incorporate center-of-mass motion of the correlated pair, and provides the first experimental estimate of the isospin structure of NN-SRCs in nuclei. The results confirm and quantify the dominance of proton-neutron correlations over proton-proton and neutron-neutron correlations, which had been suggested by earlier inclusive (e,e') scaling studies by Frankfurt et al. (1993), Day (1987), and Kim et al. (2004). The finding has implications for modeling the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron stars, and motivates future high-Q² (e,e'pp) and (e,e'pn) experiments on medium-to-heavy nuclei to improve the accuracy of these results.
