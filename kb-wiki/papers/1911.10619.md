# Transitions between hyperfine structure states of antiprotonic $^4 \mathrm{He}$ at collisions with medium atoms: interaction \emph{ab initio}

> Collisions of metastable antiprotonic helium atoms with atoms of the medium induce, among other processes, transitions between hyperfine structure (HFS) states, as well as shifts and broadening of microwave M1 spectral lines. In order to obtain matrix potential of interaction between $(\mathrm{\bar{p} He} ^+)$ and $\mathrm{He}$, we have calculated the potential energy surface (PES) in the framework of unrestricted Hartree-Fock method taking into account electron correlations in the second-order perturbation theory (MP2). With this potential, the system of close-coupling equations for HFS channels is solved numerically. Cross sections and transition rates, shifts and broadening of M1 spectral lines are calculated. They are used to obtain a numerical solution of the master equation that determines the time evolution of the HFS-states density matrix. The results are compared with the experimental data and with the results of model calculation.

# Transitions between hyperfine structure states of antiprotonic $^4 \mathrm{He}$ at collisions with medium atoms: interaction *ab initio*

## Key claim

The *ab initio* potential energy surface (PES) calculated using unrestricted Hartree-Fock with MP2 corrections yields collisional transition rates, line shifts, and broadenings that are several times smaller than those from the earlier model potential of Korenman et al. (2015), yet the resulting time-dependent annihilation signals agree fairly well with experimental data from the ASACUSA collaboration, though the model calculations are somewhat closer to the measured values due to free fitting of model parameters.

## Method

The potential energy surface for the interaction between antiprotonic helium ($\bar{p}^4\mathrm{He}^+$) and ordinary $^4\mathrm{He}$ was computed using the unrestricted Hartree-Fock method with electron correlations treated in second-order perturbation theory (MP2) and an aug-cc-pV5Z basis set. The resulting PES was expanded in Legendre polynomials up to $k=2$, averaged over the antiproton radial wavefunction for the $(n,L)=(37,35)$ state, and used as the matrix potential in close-coupling equations for the four hyperfine structure channels. Cross sections, transition rates, and M1 line shifts and broadenings were calculated from the S-matrix, then fed into a master equation for the time evolution of the HFS-state density matrix under microwave irradiation, with results compared to experimental data from the ASACUSA collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Transition rate constant $\langle\sigma v\rangle$ for the $2 \rightarrow 4$ transition ($n,L=37,35$) at $T=6$ K: $1.11 \times 10^{-7}$ a.u. with *ab initio* potential, compared to $4.46 \times 10^{-7}$ a.u. with model potential.
- Per-atom shift $\Delta/N$ for the $2 \rightarrow 4$ transition at $T=6$ K: $6.64 \times 10^{-9}$ a.u. with *ab initio* potential, compared to $40.53 \times 10^{-9}$ a.u. with model potential.
- Per-atom broadening $\gamma/N$ for the $2 \rightarrow 4$ transition at $T=6$ K: $2.03 \times 10^{-7}$ a.u. with *ab initio* potential, compared to $12.03 \times 10^{-7}$ a.u. with model potential.
- Radiative decay rate $\lambda_r$ for the $(n,L)=(37,35)$ state: $7.15 \times 10^5$ s$^{-1}$.
- Depopulation parameter $\varepsilon$ used in master equation: 0.65 for model potential, 0.5 for *ab initio* potential, chosen to match experimental annihilation signal at delay time $t=350$ ns.

## Context

This work extends the earlier model-potential approach of Korenman et al. (2015) for collisional effects on antiprotonic helium hyperfine structure by replacing the phenomenological interaction with a first-principles potential energy surface calculated using quantum chemistry methods. The results enable more reliable predictions for collisional Stark transitions with change of $n,L$ quantum numbers, and the authors state that results for different $(n,L)$ states and for $^3\mathrm{He}$ targets will be published separately. The approach also opens the possibility of treating transitions with change of antiproton quantum numbers without introducing model parameters.
