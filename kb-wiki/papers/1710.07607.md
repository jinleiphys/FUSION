# Novel subjet observables for jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions

> Using a novel observable that relies on the momentum difference of the two most energetic subjets within a jet $ΔS_{12}$ we study the internal structure of high-energy jets simulated by several Monte Carlo event generators that implement the partonic energy-loss in a dense partonic medium. Based on inclusive jet and di-jet production we demonstrate that $ΔS_{12}$ is an effective tool to discriminate between different models of jet modifications over a broad kinematic range. The new quantity, while preserving the colinear and infrared safety of modern jet algorithms, it is experimentally attractive because of its inherent resiliance against backgrounds of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the new observable ΔS₁₂, defined as the difference between the transverse momentum fractions carried by the two hardest subjets within a jet, is an effective tool to discriminate between different Monte Carlo models of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, while being experimentally attractive due to its inherent resilience against the heavy-ion background. The claim is conditional on the observable's performance being demonstrated only through simulations with specific event generators (Q-Pythia, Jewel, and PyQuen) for central PbPb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV.

## Method

The authors reconstruct jets with the anti-kt algorithm (R=0.5) and then recluster the jet constituents into subjets using a smaller radius parameter (rsj=0.15, varied between 0.1-0.2). They compute ΔS₁₂ = z₁ - z₂, where zᵢ = pT,i/pT,jet for the leading and subleading subjets, and compare distributions from several Monte Carlo event generators (Q-Pythia with q̂=1 and 4 GeV²/fm, Jewel v2.0.0, and PyQuen v1.5.1 with different angular parameterizations) against their respective vacuum references for central (0-10% most central) PbPb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Jets with pT > 150 GeV/c were used for the main ΔS₁₂ distribution analysis, with subjet radius rsj = 0.15
- Five jet pT bins were considered: 10-50, 50-120, 120-300, 300-500, and 500-1000 GeV/c
- For Q-Pythia, two average transport coefficient values were used: q̂ = 1 GeV²/fm and q̂ = 4 GeV²/fm
- The subjet radius parameter was varied in the range 0.1 < rsj < 0.2 for dependence studies
- For dijet analysis, leading jets required pT > 120 GeV/c and recoil jets pT > 50 GeV/c, with azimuthal separation at least 5π/6
- Hadronization effects on the median ΔS₁₂ (Q₂) were smaller than 1% for jets with pT > 100 GeV/c and subjet radius 0.15
- For low momentum jets (pT < 100 GeV/c), hadronization differences in Q₂ reached 2% for Rsubjet = 0.15 and 10% for Rsubjet = 0.1
- The interquartile range (IQR) showed relative changes of ~25% between hadronization models for anti-kt subjets with Rsubjet = 0.15 for jets with pT > 100 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet substructure in heavy-ion collisions (Zhang 2015) by proposing an observable specifically designed to minimize background contributions from the quark-gluon plasma. The ΔS₁₂ observable is complementary to the zg observable measured by the CMS collaboration (Sirunyan 2017) using the Soft Drop algorithm, and the authors show that while the two are strongly correlated in vacuum, they become complementary in the presence of a QGP, potentially allowing discrimination between QGP backreaction effects (Milhano 2017) and other dynamical processes. The study enables future experimental measurements at the LHC that can distinguish between different jet quenching mechanisms such as jet collimation (Jewel, PyQuen Coll) versus jet broadening (Q-Pythia, PyQuen Small).
