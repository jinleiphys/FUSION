# Residual Confinement in High-Temperature Yang-Mills Theory

> The infrared behavior of Landau gauge gluon and ghost propagators are investigated in Yang-Mills theory at non-vanishing temperatures. Self-consistent solutions are presented for temperatures below the presumed phase transition and in the infinite temperature limit. Gluon confinement is manifest in the infrared behavior of these propagators. As expected confinement prevails below the phase transition. In the infinite-temperature limit a qualitative change is observed: the chromoelectric sector exhibits a near-perturbative behavior while long-range chromomagnetic interactions, mediated by soft ghost modes, are still present. The latter behavior is in agreement with corresponding lattice results. It furthermore implies that part of the gluons are still confined.

## Key claim

The paper claims that even in the infinite-temperature limit of Yang-Mills theory, residual confinement persists for part of the gluon sector: the chromomagnetic (transverse) gluons remain confined while the chromoelectric (longitudinal) sector becomes near-perturbative, contradicting the naive expectation of a fully deconfined gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors solved a truncated set of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the Landau gauge gluon and ghost propagators at finite temperature, using a toroidal discretized space for temperatures below the critical temperature and continuum methods for the infinite-temperature limit. The truncation used a bare ghost-gluon vertex and slightly modified bare gluon vertices, with gauge invariance issues addressed qualitatively. The propagators were decomposed into transverse and longitudinal components with respect to the heat bath, and their infrared behavior was analyzed for confinement signatures.

## Key numbers

- The ghost dressing function shows a divergence at zero momentum at all temperatures, including the infinite-temperature limit, indicating long-range forces mediated by soft ghost modes.
- The transverse gluon propagator at T < T_c exhibits confinement via the condition lim_{q→0} D(q^2) = 0, and becomes steeper in the infrared with increasing temperature.
- In the infinite-temperature limit, the longitudinal gluon propagator shows screening behavior similar to a massive particle, in contrast to the transverse sector which retains its confining infrared behavior.
- The results for the transverse gluon at T → ∞ agree with corresponding lattice data from Cucchieri et al. (2001) and Cucchieri et al. (2003).

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach of Alkofer (2000) to finite temperatures and the infinite-temperature limit, and addresses doubts raised by lattice calculations of Cucchieri et al. (2001) about the simple deconfined plasma picture. The results confirm that the dimensionally reduced theory (3d Yang-Mills plus adjoint Higgs) remains confining in the chromomagnetic sector, consistent with lattice data from Langfeld (2002) and Bowman (2004). This enables further study of non-perturbative effects in high-temperature QCD and the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenario at all temperatures.
