# Four-body resonances of 7B using the complex scaling method

> We study the resonance spectroscopy of the proton-rich nucleus 7B in the 4He+p+p+p cluster model. Many-body resonances are treated on the correct boundary condition as the Gamow states using the complex scaling method. We predict five resonances of 7B and evaluate the spectroscopic factors of the 6Be-p components. The importance of the 6Be(2+)-p component is shown in several states of 7B, which is a common feature of 7He, a mirror nucleus of 7B. For only the ground state of 7B, the mixing of 6Be(2+) state is larger than that of 6He(2+) in 7He, which indicates the breaking of the mirror symmetry. This is caused by the small energy difference between 7B and the excited 6Be(2+) state, whose origin is the Coulomb repulsion.

## Key claim

The paper predicts five resonances of the proton-rich nucleus 7B using a four-body cluster model, but the ground state decay width (0.49 MeV) is slightly smaller than the experimental value (0.80(2) MeV), and the mirror symmetry between 7B and 7He is broken only for the ground state due to the larger mixing of the 6Be(2+) component compared to the 6He(2+) component in 7He.

## Method

The authors employed the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) for the 4He+p+p+p four-body system, treating many-body resonances as Gamow states using the complex scaling method (CSM). The Hamiltonian included the KKNN potential for the 4He-p interaction and the Minnesota potential for the p-p interaction, with the Coulomb interaction included via a folded Coulomb potential using the 4He density. The wave functions were expanded in Gaussian basis functions with angular momenta ℓ ≤ 2, and the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation was solved to obtain resonance energies, decay widths, and spectroscopic factors of the 6Be-p components.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of 7B: energy 3.35 MeV (experimental 3.38(3) MeV), decay width 0.49 MeV (experimental 0.80(2) MeV), measured from the 4He+3p threshold
- Excited states of 7B: 3/2−_2 at 6.92 MeV (width 5.422 MeV), 3/2−_3 at 8.39 MeV (width 9.86 MeV), 1/2− at 5.93 MeV (width 4.73 MeV), 5/2− at 4.63 MeV (width 3.91 MeV), all measured from the 4He+3p threshold
- 6Be ground state: energy 1.383 MeV (experimental 1.370 MeV), width 0.041 MeV (experimental 0.092 MeV), measured from the 4He+p+p threshold
- 6Be(2+_1) state: energy 2.90 MeV (experimental 3.04 MeV), width 1.05 MeV (experimental 1.16 MeV), measured from the 4He+p+p threshold
- 6Be ground state matter radius: 2.80 + i0.17 fm; 6He ground state matter radius: 2.37 fm (experimental 2.33(4) fm from Tanihata (1992))
- 6Be ground state: (p3/2)^2 configuration amplitude squared 0.918−i0.006; 6He ground state: (p3/2)^2 configuration amplitude squared 0.917

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful description of neutron-rich He isotopes using the 4He+4n model with CSM (Myo 2009, Myo 2010) to the proton-rich side, specifically 7B as a four-body resonance. The study contradicts the naive expectation of mirror symmetry between 7B and 7He by finding that the 6Be(2+)-p component mixing in the 7B ground state is larger than the 6He(2+)-n component in 7He, attributed to the Coulomb repulsion reducing the energy difference between 7B and the 6Be(2+) state. The predictions of five 7B resonances and their spectroscopic factors enable future experimental searches for excited states of 7B, which have never been observed, and provide a theoretical framework for understanding proton-rich unbound nuclei beyond the drip line.
