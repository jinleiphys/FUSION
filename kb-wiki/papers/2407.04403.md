# Constraining the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy with direct Urca processes

> The density dependence of the symmetry energy in relativistic mean-field models with density dependent couplings is discussed in terms of the possible opening of nucleonic direct Urca processes inside neutron stars, which induce a very rapid cooling of the star. The modification of the parametrization of the isospin channel of two models, DD2 and DDMEX, keeping the same isoscalar properties is considered and the implications are discussed. Within the models discussed it is not possible the onset of nucleonic direct Urca processes in stars with a mass below $\sim1.6\,M_\odot$ if chiral effective field theory constraints for neutron matter are imposed. A Bayesian inference calculation confirms the low probability that nucleonic direct Urca processes occur inside stars with masses below 1.8$M_\odot$, considering the isoscalar channel of the equation of state described by DD2 or DDMEX and the same symmetry energy at saturation. The lowest masses allowing direct Urca processes are associated with a slope of the symmetry energy above $60$ MeV and most likely a positive symmetry energy incompressibility. It is shown that the parametrization of the isospin channel proposed destroys the correlation between symmetry energy slope and incompressibility previously identified in several works.

# Constraining the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy with direct Urca processes

## Key claim

Within the relativistic mean-field models DD2 and DDMEX with density-dependent couplings, nucleonic direct Urca processes cannot occur in neutron stars with masses below approximately 1.6 solar masses if chiral effective field theory constraints for neutron matter are imposed; the lowest masses that allow direct Urca processes are associated with a symmetry energy slope above 60 MeV and most likely a positive symmetry energy incompressibility.

## Method

The authors modified the isovector channel parametrization of two relativistic mean-field models (DD2 and DDMEX) by introducing a new parameter y that controls the high-density behavior of the rho-meson coupling, while keeping the same isoscalar properties and symmetry energy at saturation. They constructed sets of equations of state that satisfy chiral effective field theory neutron matter constraints within 2 sigma and performed Bayesian inference calculations to study the complete isovector channel and identify correlations between symmetry energy parameters and the minimum mass for direct Urca processes.

## Key numbers

- Direct Urca processes cannot occur in stars with mass below approximately 1.6 solar masses when chiral effective field theory constraints are imposed
- Bayesian inference confirms low probability of nucleonic direct Urca processes inside stars with masses below 1.8 solar masses
- The lowest masses allowing direct Urca processes are associated with a symmetry energy slope above 60 MeV
- For the DD2 family, the slope L(2.5 rho_0) varies in the range 43-55 MeV; for the DDMEX family, it varies in the range 48-58 MeV
- The original DD2 model has L_0 = 55.12 MeV and K_sym,0 = -93.21 MeV; the original DDMEX model has L_0 = 49.66 MeV and K_sym,0 = -71.57 MeV
- The modified DD2-1.6b model has L_0 = 64.50 MeV and K_sym,0 = -5.078 MeV; the modified DDMEX-1.6 model has L_0 = 70.86 MeV and K_sym,0 = 10.78 MeV
- Direct Urca onset densities occur at approximately 2.5 rho_0, 2.75 rho_0, and 3.1 rho_0 for M_DU values of 1.6, 1.8, and 2.0 solar masses respectively
- The maximum masses of neutron stars calculated using DD2 and DDMEX models are not very sensitive to the isovector channel, suffering a small increase not larger than 0.02 solar masses
- DDMEX models can predict neutron stars exceeding 2.5 solar masses, consistent with the GW190814 secondary compact object mass range of 2.50-2.67 solar masses

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Malik et al. (2022) that introduced modifications to density-dependent models to predict nucleon direct Urca processes inside neutron stars, and by Beznogov et al. (2015) which indicated that neutron star cooling curves suggest direct Urca processes should be possible in stars with mass greater than approximately 1.6 solar masses. The study contradicts the behavior of standard density-dependent models such as DD2 and DDMEX, which typically do not predict nucleonic direct Urca processes because the rho-meson coupling tends to zero at high densities. The authors show that their proposed parametrization destroys the previously identified correlation between symmetry energy slope and incompressibility found in works by Providencia et al. (2014) and Ducoin et al. (2010, 2011) for a large set of RMF and Skyrme force models. This work enables future studies to incorporate direct Urca cooling constraints when modeling the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy.
