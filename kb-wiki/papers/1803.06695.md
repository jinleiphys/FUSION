# Vortex-like solutions and internal structures of covariant ideal magnetohydrodynamics

> We discuss a manifestly covariant formulation of ideal relativistic magnetohydrodynamics, which has been recently used in astrophysical and heavy-ion contexts, and compare it to other similar frameworks. We show that the covariant equations allow for stationary vortex-like solutions that represent generalizations of the perfect-fluid solutions describing systems in global equilibrium with rotation. Such solutions are further used to demonstrate that inhomogeneous Maxwell equations, implicitly included in the covariant framework, may generate very large electric charge densities. This suggests that solutions of the covariant formulation may violate in some cases the assumptions of standard ideal magnetohydrodynamics. Furthermore, we show that the flow four-vector and conserved currents obtained in the covariant approach are usually not related to each other, which hinders kinetic-theory interpretation of the obtained results.

## Key claim

The covariant formulation of ideal relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (cvMHD) allows stationary vortex-like solutions that generalize perfect-fluid solutions in global equilibrium with rotation, but these solutions can generate very large electric charge densities that may violate the quasi-neutrality assumptions of standard ideal magnetohydrodynamics, and the flow four-vector and conserved currents obtained from the covariant approach are usually not related to each other, hindering kinetic-theory interpretation.

## Method

The authors compare three formulations of ideal relativistic MHD: the manifestly covariant version (cvMHD) from Pu (2016), an ideal version (idMHD) that is equivalent but not manifestly covariant, and a standard version (stMHD) that neglects charge density and displacement current. They construct stationary vortex-like solutions of the cvMHD equations by assuming a rigidly rotating flow four-vector with constant angular velocity Omega and a magnetic field perpendicular to the vortex plane, then derive the radial force balance equation and examine its solutions under global equilibrium conditions following the Killing vector approach of Becattini (2009).

## Key numbers

* The flow profile is constrained to a cylinder of radius R less than 1/Omega, where Omega is the constant angular velocity and gamma = (1 - Omega^2 r^2)^(-1/2)
* In global equilibrium with rotation, the temperature follows T(r) = T_c * gamma(r) where T_c is the central temperature, the chemical potential follows mu_B(r) = mu_B^c * gamma(r), and the magnetic field follows B(r) = B_c * gamma(r) where B_c is the central magnetic field
* The electric charge density in the global equilibrium solution is rho = 2 * Omega_m * gamma^3 * B(r), where Omega_m = -(1 - chi_m) * Omega and chi_m is the magnetic susceptibility
* For the massless equation of state (EOS II), the baryon number density is n_B = (2g/pi^2) * sinh(mu_B^c/T_c) * T_c^3 * gamma^3, making baryon and electric charge densities proportional

## Context

This work extends the covariant MHD framework introduced by Pu (2016) and compares it to standard formulations from Krall and Trivelpiece. The vortex solutions generalize the global equilibrium with rotation solutions of Becattini (2009), which have become important for connecting fluid thermal vorticity to spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions. The results challenge the applicability of cvMHD in cases where large charge densities arise, and suggest that cvMHD solutions should be checked a posteriori against standard MHD assumptions of quasi-neutrality. The findings also relate to recent work by Florkowski (2017) questioning the direct connection between vorticity and spin polarization.
