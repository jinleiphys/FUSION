# Nucleon axial, tensor, and scalar charges and $σ$-terms in lattice QCD

> We determine the nucleon axial, scalar and tensor charges at the continuum limit by analyzing three $N_f=2+1+1$ twisted mass fermion ensembles with all quark masses tuned to approximately their physical values. We include all contributions from valence and sea quarks. We use the Akaike Information Criterion to evaluate systematic errors due to excited states and the continuum extrapolation. For the nucleon isovector axial charge we find $g_A^{u-d}=1.250(24)$, in agreement with the experimental value. We compute the axial, tensor and scalar charges for each quark flavor. The axial charge provides crucial information on the intrinsic spin carried by quark in the nucleon and the the latter two provide input for experimental searches of physics beyond the standard model. Moreover, we extract the nucleon $σ$-terms and find $σ_{πN}=41.9(8.1)$ MeV, for the strange $σ_{s}=30(17)$ MeV and for the charm $σ_{c}=82(29)$ MeV. We also present preliminary results on the isovector quantities using a fourth ensemble at smaller lattice spacing.

# Nucleon axial, tensor, and scalar charges and σ-terms in lattice QCD

## Key claim
The authors determine nucleon axial, scalar, and tensor charges at the continuum limit using three ensembles with all quark masses tuned to approximately physical values, finding that the isovector axial charge g_A^{u-d}=1.250(24) agrees with the experimental value. This is the first determination of these charges and σ-terms at the continuum limit using only physical-point ensembles, avoiding chiral extrapolations, though the results carry systematic uncertainties evaluated via the Akaike Information Criterion for excited-state and continuum extrapolation modeling.

## Method
The authors analyze three N_f=2+1+1 twisted mass clover-improved fermion ensembles (B64, C80, D96) at lattice spacings from 0.07957 fm to 0.05692 fm with pion masses near 140 MeV, extracting nucleon charges from two- and three-point correlation functions at zero momentum transfer. They perform two-state fits to control excited-state contamination, varying the minimum source-sink separation and the number of insertion time slices, then average over fit models using the Akaike Information Criterion. Continuum extrapolation uses a model average over linear fits in a^2 and constant fits with and without the coarsest ensemble, with renormalization performed nonperturbatively via the RI'/MOM scheme and conversion to the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Isovector axial charge: g_A^{u-d}=1.250(24) at the continuum limit
- Isovector scalar charge: g_S^{u-d}=1.08(31) at the continuum limit
- Isovector tensor charge: g_T^{u-d}=0.955(29) at the continuum limit
- Pion-nucleon sigma term: σ_{πN}=41.9(8.1) MeV at the continuum limit
- Strange sigma term: σ_s=30(17) MeV at the continuum limit
- Charm sigma term: σ_c=82(29) MeV at the continuum limit
- Single flavor axial charges: g_A^u=0.832(28), g_A^d=-0.417(22), g_A^s=-0.037(18), g_A^c=0.003(13)
- Single flavor tensor charges: g_T^u=0.756(29), g_T^d=-0.196(12), g_T^s=-0.0009(11), g_T^c=-0.0028(26)
- Single flavor scalar charges: g_S^u=6.4(1.1), g_S^d=5.30(98), g_S^s=0.16(37), g_S^c=0.09(26)
- Lattice spacings: 0.07957(13) fm (B64), 0.06821(13) fm (C80), 0.05692(12) fm (D96), 0.04892(11) fm (E112, preliminary)

## Context
This work extends previous ETMC results (Alexandrou 2023, Alexandrou 2022, Alexandrou 2019) by providing the first continuum limit determination of nucleon charges and σ-terms using only ensembles simulated directly at the physical pion mass, unlike other collaborations (Mainz group, PNDME, RQCD, QCDSF/UKQCD/CSSM, NME, CalLat, χQCD) that combine physical-point with heavier-than-physical ensembles. The results enable improved constraints on beyond-Standard-Model interactions for experiments such as DUNE, COHERENT, and GEMMA, and provide input for understanding nucleon spin structure and transversity parton distributions, with future inclusion of a fourth finer ensemble (E112 at a≈0.05 fm) expected to refine the continuum extrapolation accuracy.
