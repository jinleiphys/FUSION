# Hybrid isentropic twin stars

> We present a study of hybrid neutron stars with color superconducting quark matter cores at finite temperature that results in sequences of stars with constant entropy per baryon, $s/n_B={\rm const}$. For the quark matter equation of state, we employ a recently developed nonlocal chiral quark model while nuclear matter is described with a relativistic density functional model of the DD2 class. The phase transition is obtained by a Maxwell construction under isothermal conditions. We find that traversing the mixed phase on a trajectory at low $s/n_B\lesssim 2$ in the phase diagram shows a heating effect while at larger $s/n_B$ the temperature drops. This behavior may be attributed to the presence of a color superconducting quark matter phase at low temperatures and the melting of the diquark condensate which restores the normal quark matter phase at higher temperatures. While the isentropic hybrid star branch at low $s/n_B\lesssim 2$ is connected to the neutron star branch, it gets disconnected at higher entropy per baryon so that the "thermal twin" phenomenon is observed. We find that the transition from connected to disconnected hybrid star sequences may be estimated with the Seidov criterion for the difference in energy densities. The radii and masses at the onset of deconfinement exhibit a linear relationship and thus define a critical compactness of the isentropic star configuration for which the transition occurs which for large enough $s/n_B\gtrsim 2$ is accompanied by an instability. The results of this study may be of relevance for uncovering the conditions for the supernova explodability of massive blue supergiant stars by the quark deconfinement mechanism. The accretion-induced deconfinement transition with thermal twin formation may contribute to explaining the origin of eccentric orbits in some binary systems and the origin of isolated millisecond pulsars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that sequences of hybrid neutron stars with constant entropy per baryon can exhibit either connected or disconnected "thermal twin" branches depending on the entropy value, with the transition between these regimes occurring near s/n_B ~ 2 and estimable via the Seidov criterion for the energy density jump.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid neutron star sequences by combining a nonlocal chiral quark model for color-superconducting quark matter with a DD2-class relativistic density functional model for nuclear matter, using a Maxwell construction for the isothermal phase transition. They then impose the condition of constant entropy per baryon (s/n_B = const) to generate isentropic stellar sequences, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for each fixed entropy value. The temperature evolution through the mixed phase is tracked, and the Seidov criterion is applied to estimate the critical energy density difference that separates connected from disconnected hybrid star branches.

## Key numbers

* For low entropy per baryon (s/n_B less than or approximately 2), the isentropic hybrid star branch remains connected to the neutron star branch, while at higher s/n_B the branch becomes disconnected, producing the "thermal twin" phenomenon.
* The transition from connected to disconnected hybrid star sequences can be estimated using the Seidov criterion for the difference in energy densities at the phase transition.
* The radii and masses at the onset of deconfinement exhibit a linear relationship, defining a critical compactness of the isentropic star configuration; for s/n_B greater than or approximately 2 this onset is accompanied by an instability.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid neutron stars with color-superconducting quark matter cores (e.g., work by Blaschke and collaborators) by incorporating finite temperature and isentropic conditions relevant to proto-neutron star evolution. The results enable future investigations of the quark deconfinement mechanism in supernova explosions of massive blue supergiant stars and the formation of eccentric binary systems or isolated millisecond pulsars through accretion-induced deconfinement transitions. The linear relationship between radius and mass at deconfinement onset provides a new diagnostic for identifying critical compactness in isentropic stellar configurations.
