# Hydrodynamics of nuclear matter in the chiral limit

> Using the Poisson bracket method, we construct the hydrodynamics of nuclear matter in the chiral limit, which describes the dynamics of all low-energy degrees of freedom, including the fluid-dynamical and pionic ones. The hydrodynamic equations contain, beside five Euler equations of relativistic fluid dynamics, Nf^2-1 second order equations describing propagating pions and Nf^2-1 first order equations describing the advection of the baryonic vector isospin charges. We present hydrodynamic arguments showing that the pion velocity vanishes at the second order phase transition at Nf=2.

# Hydrodynamics of Nuclear Matter in the Chiral Limit

## Key claim

Using the Poisson bracket method, the paper constructs a complete hydrodynamic theory of nuclear matter in the chiral limit that unifies fluid-dynamical variables with pionic degrees of freedom; the pion velocity is shown to vanish at the second order chiral phase transition for Nf=2, but only under the assumption that the isovector response coefficient f_v remains nonzero at the critical temperature.

## Method

The author employs the Poisson bracket technique from Hamiltonian fluid dynamics, writing down the Poisson brackets between hydrodynamic variables (energy-momentum density, baryon density, left and right isospin charge densities, and the SU(2) chiral condensate phase Sigma) based on the canonical commutators of the microscopic theory and the transformation laws of Sigma under chiral rotations. The Hamiltonian is constructed to leading order in derivatives of Sigma and in powers of the isospin charge densities, with coefficients f_s, f_t, and f_v that are functions of local temperature and chemical potential. The equations of motion are then derived by taking Poisson brackets of the variables with the Hamiltonian, yielding a set of second-order equations for the pion field and first-order equations for the advection of the baryonic vector isospin charge.

## Key numbers

- The hydrodynamic equations contain 5 Euler equations of relativistic fluid dynamics, Nf^2-1 second order equations for propagating pions, and Nf^2-1 first order equations for the advection of baryonic vector isospin charges.
- For Nf=2, the system involves 3(Nf^2-1)=9 variables in the reduced hydrodynamics of isospin charges and the condensate.
- The pion velocity in the nonlinear sigma model limit is v_pi = f_s/f_t, where f_s and f_t are the spatial and temporal pion decay constants.
- At the critical temperature T_c, the Hamiltonian becomes independent of Sigma only if f_s=0 and f_t=f_v, leading to v_pi approaching zero as T approaches T_c.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic treatment of nuclear matter beyond previous approaches that treated fluid-dynamical and chiral variables separately, such as the phenomenological framework of Mishustin, Pedersen, and Scavenius (1997, 1999). The formalism unifies relativistic fluid dynamics and the nonlinear sigma model as special cases, making it applicable to the study of disoriented chiral condensates and the coupling between sound waves and pions in heavy-ion collisions. The method is noted to be extendable to the color-flavor-locking phase of finite-density QCD with Nf=3, as developed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999).
