# Relativistic fluctuations in stochastic fluid dynamics

## Key claim
The authors present a covariant formalism for the non-equilibrium evolution of non-Gaussian fluctuations in relativistic fluids, extending previous work that was limited to Gaussian fluctuations or lacked covariance. The formalism is kept general enough to apply to arbitrary n-point correlation functions without relying on separation of relaxation time scales.

## Method
The authors start from a covariant Langevin equation for stochastic fields (such as charge density and energy-momentum density) and derive evolution equations for connected n-point correlation functions using a double expansion in two small parameters: epsilon (controlling loop expansion) and epsilon_q (controlling gradient expansion). They then implement a confluent formalism to make the equations Lorentz covariant by boosting fluctuations at different points into the local rest frame of the midpoint, and introduce a generalized multi-point Wigner transform. Finally, they apply a rotating-wave approximation that diagonalizes the linear operator in the ideal hydrodynamic equations, allowing fast-oscillating modes to be averaged out and reducing the number of independent correlation functions.

## Key numbers
- The power counting scheme specifies: G^c_n ~ epsilon^(n-1), L ~ epsilon_q + O(epsilon_q^2), Q ~ epsilon_q^2 * epsilon, and u·∂ ~ epsilon_q^2
- The evolution equations for n-point connected correlation functions are derived at leading order epsilon_q^2 * epsilon^(n-1)
- The confluent derivative includes connections bar-omega and epsilon-con that account for frame changes as the midpoint moves

## Context
This work extends the covariant dynamical description developed in An (2019) and An (2019) which was limited to Gaussian fluctuations, and also extends subsequent work in An (2021) where a generic formalism for non-Gaussian fluctuation dynamics was established but not implemented covariantly. The formalism is necessary for interpreting observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions that are sensitive to fluctuations and criticality, particularly for the search for the hypothetical QCD critical point. The explicit full equations for correlation functions relevant for heavy-ion collisions will be presented in a forthcoming paper by An (2022).
