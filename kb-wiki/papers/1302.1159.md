# Halo Modification of a Supernova Neutronization Neutrino Burst

> We give the first self-consistent calculation of the effect of the scattered neutrino halo on flavor evolution in supernovae. Our example case is an O-Ne-Mg core collapse supernova neutronization neutrino burst. We find that the addition of the halo neutrinos produces qualitative and quantitative changes in the final flavor states of neutrinos. We also find that the halo neutrinos produce a novel distortion of the neutrino flavor swap. Our results provide strong motivation for tackling the full multidimensional and composition-dependent aspects of this problem in the future.

## Key claim

The scattered neutrino halo produces qualitative and quantitative changes in the final flavor states of neutrinos during an O-Ne-Mg core collapse supernova neutronization burst, including a novel distortion of the neutrino flavor swap; however, these results are obtained under the condition that the calculation is limited to the region where inwardly-propagating halo neutrinos contribute less than 1% to the neutrino self-coupling potential, and the authors note that the halo converts flavor evolution from an initial value problem into a boundary value problem in the general case.

## Method

The authors performed spherically symmetric, multi-angle flavor transformation calculations in the coherent forward-scattering limit, using neutrino emission parameters from Fischer (2010) for two time slices (7 ms and 15 ms post-bounce) of an O-Ne-Mg core collapse supernova. They defined a "halosphere" at radius R_H = 850 km, outside which halo neutrinos on inward trajectories contribute negligibly to the forward-scattering potential, and computed the neutral-current scattering of neutrinos off nucleons and nuclei using energy-dependent cross sections from Tubbs (1975) to populate the halo population. The initial flavor states of all neutrinos at the halosphere surface were uniquely determined because multi-angle suppression prevents collective oscillations inside 1100 km, ensuring all neutrinos within the halosphere remain in their original flavor states.

## Key numbers

- Halosphere radius defined at R_H = 850 km, where inwardly-scattered neutrinos contribute less than 1% to the neutrino self-coupling potential
- Neutrinosphere radius for average-energy neutrinos: R_nu = 60 km (at optical depth tau = 1)
- Neutrino luminosity at 7 ms post-bounce: L_nu_e = 3.3 x 10^53 erg/s; L_antinu_e = 2.6 x 10^51 erg/s; L_nu_mu/tau = 1.6 x 10^52 erg/s
- Neutrino luminosity at 15 ms post-bounce: L_nu_e = 1.3 x 10^53 erg/s; L_antinu_e = 9.1 x 10^51 erg/s; L_nu_mu/tau = 2.6 x 10^52 erg/s
- Mean energies at 7 ms: <E_nu_e> = 13.0 MeV, <E_antinu_e> = 9.8 MeV, <E_nu_mu/tau> = 16.7 MeV
- Mean energies at 15 ms: <E_nu_e> = 11.3 MeV, <E_antinu_e> = 10.6 MeV, <E_nu_mu/tau> = 15.4 MeV
- Multi-angle suppression prevents collective oscillations out to radius r = 1100 km
- The halo produces a clear swap signature between 20-30 MeV, yielding approximately 15 additional nu_e events in a 17 kt liquid argon detector over a 20 ms window
- Neutrino mixing parameters used: Delta m^2_solar = 7.6 x 10^-5 eV^2, Delta m^2_atm = 2.4 x 10^-3 eV^2, theta_13 = 0.152

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the neutrino halo effect (Cherry 2012) by providing the first self-consistent calculation that includes halo neutrinos in flavor evolution, using the O-Ne-Mg core collapse supernova as a tractable case due to its centrally condensed matter envelope. The results contradict the standard assumption that only outwardly-directed neutrinos need be followed in flavor evolution calculations, and demonstrate that the halo converts the problem from an initial value problem to a boundary value problem. The findings motivate future work on full multidimensional and composition-dependent calculations, which are currently precluded in accretion-phase supernovae by hydrodynamics-generated matter inhomogeneity and inwardly-propagating neutrinos (Sarikas 2012).
