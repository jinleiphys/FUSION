# Distribution of Magnetic Dipole Strength -Binning

> In previous works we examined the systematics of magnetic dipole transitions in a single j shell. We here extend the study to large space calculations. We consider Ti and Cr isotopes.. In this work we focus on the B(M1) strength as a function of excitation of energy. The initial state is the lowest J= state. T=1 state in a specified nucleus. The final states are J=0 T=2 , all in one plot, and J=2 T=2 in another. The initial figures have points all over the map although there is a suggestion of an exponential trend. To reduce clutter we perform binning operations in which the summed strength in a given energy interval is represented by a single point. The new binning curves show more clearly the exponential fall of B(M1)'s with energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that after applying a binning procedure to reduce clutter, the B(M1) strength from the lowest J=1+ T=1 state to J=0+ T=2 and J=2+ T=2 states in Ti and Cr isotopes shows a near exponential decrease with excitation energy, though the authors note the precise fit is less important than the qualitative exponential trend.

## Method

The authors performed large-space shell model calculations in the full f-p shell using the GX1A interaction for 44Ti, 46Ti, and 48Cr. They computed B(M1) transition strengths from the lowest J=1+ T=1 state to final states with J=0+ T=2 and J=2+ T=2, then applied a binning procedure where summed strength in energy intervals of 0.25 MeV (for J=0 T=2) or 0.2 MeV (for J=2 T=2) was represented by a single point on log-scale plots.

## Key numbers

- For 46Ti transitions from lowest J=1 T=1 to J=0 T=2, a rough fit gives log(B(M1)) = -0.69897 - (E-15) x 0.20264, or equivalently B(M1) = 0.2 x 10^{-(E-15) x 0.20264} for E greater than 15 MeV
- For 44Ti transitions from lowest J=0 T=0 to J=1 T=1, the slope is log(B(M1)) = -0.2068E + 0.526
- For 44Ti transitions from lowest J=1 T=1 to J=1 T=0, the slope is log(B(M1)) = -0.2091E + 0.05206
- For 44Ti transitions from lowest J=1 T=1 to J=1 T=1, the slope is log(B(M1)) = -0.1984E - 1.534
- For 46Ti from J=1 T=1 to J=0 T=2, the slope is log(B(M1)) = -0.166E - 2.094
- For 46Ti from J=1 T=1 to J=2 T=2, the slope is log(B(M1)) = -0.152E - 0.861
- For 46Ti from ground state J=0 T=1 to J=1 T=1, the slope is log(B(M1)) = -0.167E - 0.124
- Selected single-j shell results for 46Ti include B(M1) = 2.474 μN^2 to a (0,1) state at 4.625 MeV and B(M1) = 1.622 μN^2 to a (2,2) state at 8.255 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous single-j shell studies of magnetic dipole transitions in the f7/2 shell by Kingan, Ma, and Zamick (2018) to full f-p shell calculations, and contrasts with the traditional scissors mode analysis starting from J=0 ground states as done by Beller et al. (2015). The observed exponential falloff of B(M1) strength with energy is related to similar exponential behavior found by Schwengner et al. (2017) in photonuclear reactions and by Brown et al. (2018), supporting the claim that such behavior is widespread. The work enables further study of double scissors mode states and connects to earlier work on double Gamow-Teller states by Zheng, Zamick, and Auerbach (1996).
