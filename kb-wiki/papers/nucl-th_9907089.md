# Back-to-Back Correlations for Bosons Modified by Medium

> Novel back-to-back correlations are shown to arise for thermal ensembles of squeezed bosonic states associated with medium-modified mass-shifts. The strength of these correlations could become unexpectedly large in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Novel back-to-back correlations (BBC) arise for thermal ensembles of squeezed bosonic states when medium-modified mass-shifts are present, and these correlations could become unexpectedly large in heavy ion collisions, though the magnitude is reduced if the freeze-out is not completely sudden.

## Method

The authors model a scalar field with a momentum-dependent effective mass \(m_*^2(|\mathbf{k}|) = m_0^2 - \delta M^2(|\mathbf{k}|)\) and relate in-medium quasi-particle operators \(b_{\mathbf{k}}\) to asymptotic operators \(a_{\mathbf{k}}\) via a Bogoliubov transformation with squeezing parameter \(r_{\mathbf{k}} = \frac{1}{2}\log(\omega_{\mathbf{k}}/\Omega_{\mathbf{k}})\). They compute the two-particle correlation function \(C_2(\mathbf{k}_1,\mathbf{k}_2)\) for a thermal ensemble of \(b\)-quanta, first for a homogeneous system and then for an inhomogeneous system by dividing the fluid into cells with local Bogoliubov transformations. The time evolution of the decay is modeled with a probability distribution \(F(t)\) to account for gradual freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- For \(\phi\) mesons at \(T \simeq 140\) MeV, \(|\mathbf{k}| = 0\)–500 MeV, back-to-back correlations as big as 100–1000 for reasonable values of \(m^*_{\phi}\)
- For an exponential decay with \(\delta t = \hbar/\Gamma = 2\) fm/c, the BBC for \(\phi\) mesons survives suppression with a strength as large as 2–3
- The HBT correlation intercept remains 2 for identical momenta
- For \(\pi^0\) pairs, a momentum-dependent mass-shift \(m^*_{\pi} = m_{\pi}[1 + \exp(-\mathbf{k}^2/\Lambda_s^2)]\) with \(\Lambda_s = 325\) MeV is used in the schematic illustration

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding of back-to-back correlations by Asakawa and Csorgo (1999) to include finite-time suppression effects and inhomogeneous systems with local hydrodynamical flow. It contradicts the naive expectation that medium modifications of hadron masses vanish on the freeze-out surface and therefore have little effect on two-boson correlations, which previously were described solely by the Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) effect. The results enable experimental searches for these BBC in \(\phi\phi\), \(K^+K^-\), \(\pi^0\pi^0\), and \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pairs at CERN SPS and RHIC BNL heavy ion experiments.
