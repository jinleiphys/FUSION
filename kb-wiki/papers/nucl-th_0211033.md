# Energy Level Statistics of the U(5) and O(6) Symmetries in the Interacting Boson Model

> We study the energy level statistics of the states in U(5) and O(6) dynamical symmetries of the interacting boson model and the high spin states with backbending in U(5) symmetry. In the calculations, the degeneracy resulting from the additional quantum number is eliminated manually. The calculated results indicate that the finite boson number $N$ effect is prominent. When $N$ has a value close to a realistic one, increasing the interaction strength of subgroup O(5) makes the statistics vary from Poisson-type to GOE-type and further recover to Poisson-type. However, in the case of $N \to \infty$, they all tend to be Poisson-type. The fluctuation property of the energy levels with backbending in high spin states in U(5) symmetry involves a signal of shape phase transition between spherical vibration and axial rotation.

# Energy Level Statistics of the U(5) and O(6) Symmetries in the Interacting Boson Model

## Key claim

For finite boson numbers close to realistic values (e.g., N=25), increasing the relative strength of the O(5) subgroup interaction in the U(5) and O(6) dynamical symmetries of the interacting boson model causes the energy level statistics to vary from Poisson-type to GOE-type and then back to Poisson-type; however, in the limit N → ∞, all cases tend to Poisson-type regardless of interaction parameters, contradicting the naive symmetry paradigm that dynamical symmetries should always yield Poisson statistics.

## Method

The authors diagonalized the IBM Hamiltonians for the U(5) and O(6) dynamical symmetries (Eqs. 1-2) for systems with boson numbers N=25, 70, and 200, using several sets of parameters with varying β (the O(5) subgroup coupling strength). They performed an unfolding procedure on the resulting energy spectra using a sixth-degree polynomial fit to the staircase function, then computed the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution P(S) and the spectral rigidity Δ₃(L) as statistical measures. Degenerate states differing only by the additional quantum number K were manually reduced to a single level per degenerate set to avoid over-Poisson type distributions.

## Key numbers

- Boson numbers studied: N = 25, 70, and 200, with N=25 described as "close to a realistic one in nuclei"
- For N=25 in U(5) symmetry, varying the ratio β/α caused the Brody parameter ω to change from near 0 (Poisson-type) through near 1 (GOE-type) and back to near 0
- For the collective backbending analysis in U(5) with N=25, parameters ε=3.53 and α=-0.101 (arbitrary units) gave a critical angular momentum L_c = 12 from Eq. (20)
- In the backbending case, the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution P(S) exhibited two distinct maxima, unlike the single-peak Poisson or Wigner distributions
- For N=70 and N=200 with the same ε and α values, L_c became negative, and the two-maximum feature disappeared, yielding Poisson-type statistics

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of energy level statistics in the IBM, particularly the SU(3) symmetry analysis by Paar and collaborators (1990, 1992), which showed that SU(3) statistics depended strongly on boson number and could approach GOE-type for realistic N. The authors also connect their results to the hydrogen atom in a uniform magnetic field (Friedrich and Wintgen, 1989) as an analogous case of symmetry breaking leading to chaos. The finding that the O(5) subgroup interaction strength controls the onset of chaos supports the view of Bohigas (1988) that residual interactions, rather than the static mean field, cause chaotic behavior in nuclei. The two-maximum structure in P(S) for the backbending case is compared to the X(5) symmetry critical point spectra (Iachello, 2001), suggesting that collective backbending signals a first-order shape phase transition between spherical vibration and axial rotation.
