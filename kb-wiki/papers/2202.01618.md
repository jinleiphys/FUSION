# NCQ scaling of $f_{0}(980)$ elliptic flow in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions by STAR and its constituent quark content

> Searching for exotic state particles and studying their properties have furthered our understanding of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The $f_{0}$(980) resonance is an exotic state with relatively high production rate in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, decaying primarily into $ππ$. Currently, the structure and quark content of the $f_{0}$(980) are unknown with several predictions from theory being a $q\overline{q}$ state, a $qq\overline{q}\overline{q}$ state, a $K\overline{K}$ molecule state, or a gluonium state. We report the first $f_{0}$(980) elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) measurement from 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at STAR. The transverse momentum dependence of $v_{2}$ is examined and compared to those of other hadrons (baryons and mesons). The empirical number of constituent quark (NCQ) scaling is used to investigate the constituent quark content of $f_{0}$(980), which may potentially address an important question in QCD.

## Key claim

The first measurement of the $f_{0}(980)$ elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at STAR is reported, but with current uncertainties the result cannot determine whether the $f_{0}(980)$ is a $q\overline{q}$ state, $qq\overline{q}\overline{q}$ state, $K\overline{K}$ molecule, gluonium state, or produced through $\pi\pi$ coalescence.

## Method

The $f_{0}(980)$ resonance was reconstructed via $\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$ pairs using the STAR Time Projection Chamber (TPC) in 2.4 billion minimum-bias Au+Au events at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. Combinatorial background was subtracted using the mixed-event technique and like-sign method, and the resonance peaks were parametrized with a relativistic Breit-Wigner function. The elliptic flow was measured using the event-plane method, and the number of constituent quark (NCQ) scaling was applied by fitting the $v_{2}$ as a function of $(m_T-m_0)/n_q$ with the quark number $n_q$ as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Total of 2.4 billion minimum-bias events from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV were analyzed.
- The extracted quark content of $f_{0}(980)$ from NCQ scaling fit is $n_q = 3.0 \pm 0.7 \text{ (stat)} \pm 0.5 \text{ (syst)}$.
- The kinetic freeze-out temperature was set to 120 MeV in the phase space correction.
- The $\rho^{0}$ width $\Gamma_{\rho}$ was fixed to 160 MeV in the fit.
- The $f_{0}(980)$ $v_{2}$ was measured in 30-80% centrality Au+Au collisions over the $p_T$ range $0 < p_T < 5.0$ GeV/$c$.

## Context

This work extends previous preliminary measurements of $f_{0}(980)$ yield at RHIC (Fachini, 2004) and theoretical calculations suggesting it could be a $K\overline{K}$ state (ExHIC, 2010). The NCQ scaling approach follows the method established by Molnar (2003), PHENIX (2003), and STAR (2003) for identifying hadron quark content. The results are compared with other identified particles ($\pi$, $K$, $p$, $K_{s}^{0}$, $\Lambda$, $\Xi$, $\Omega$, $\phi$) from STAR (2015). Future measurements using isobar data from 2018 and the expected 8-fold increase in Au+Au data in 2023-2025 could provide more insights into the $f_{0}(980)$ structure.
