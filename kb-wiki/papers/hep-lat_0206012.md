# Exotic mesons from quantum chromodynamics with improved gluon and quark actions on the anisotropic lattice

> Hybrid (exotic) mesons, which are important predictions of quantum chromodynamics (QCD), are states of quarks and anti-quarks bound by excited gluons. First principle lattice study of such states would help us understand the role of ``dynamical'' color in low energy QCD and provide valuable information for experimental search for these new particles. In this paper, we apply both improved gluon and quark actions to the hybrid mesons, which might be much more efficient than the previous works in reducing lattice spacing error and finite volume effect. Quenched simulations were done at $β=2.6$ and on a $ξ=3$ anisotropic $12^3\times36$ lattice using our PC cluster. We obtain $2013 \pm 26 \pm 71$ MeV for the mass of the $1^{-+}$ hybrid meson ${\bar q}qg$ in the light quark sector, and $4369 \pm 37 \pm 99$Mev in the charm quark sector; the mass splitting between the $1^{-+}$ hybrid meson ${\bar c}c g$ in the charm quark sector and the spin averaged S-wave charmonium mass is estimated to be $1302 \pm 37 \pm 99$ MeV. As a byproduct, we obtain $1438 \pm 32 \pm 57$ MeV for the mass of a P-wave $1^{++}$ ${\bar u}u$ or ${\bar d}d$ meson and $1499 \pm 28 \pm 65$ MeV for the mass of a P-wave $1^{++}$ ${\bar s}s$ meson, which are comparable to their experimental value 1426 MeV for the $f_1(1420)$ meson. The first error is statistical, and the second one is systematical. The mixing of the hybrid meson with a four quark state is also discussed.

# Exotic mesons from quantum chromodynamics with improved gluon and quark actions on the anisotropic lattice

## Key claim

Using both improved gluon and quark actions on an anisotropic lattice in quenched QCD, the authors obtain masses for the $1^{-+}$ hybrid meson that are consistent with previous lattice calculations (e.g., MILC97, UKQCD97, MILC99) but claim their approach is more efficient in reducing systematic errors from finite lattice spacing and finite volume, even on coarser and smaller lattices.

## Method

Quenched lattice QCD simulations were performed at $\beta=2.6$ on a $12^3 \times 36$ anisotropic lattice with aspect ratio $\xi = a_s/a_t = 3$, using a tadpole-improved gluon action containing both $1\times1$ plaquette and $2\times1$ rectangle terms, together with a tadpole-improved clover (Sheikholeslami-Wohlert) quark action. The quark propagators were computed via the BICGStab algorithm at four values of the Wilson hopping parameter $\kappa_t$, and correlation functions for ordinary mesons, hybrid mesons, and a four-quark state were fitted to extract effective masses, which were then extrapolated to the chiral limit or tuned to the charm quark mass.

## Key numbers

- Light $1^{-+}$ hybrid meson mass: $2013 \pm 26 \pm 71$ MeV (statistical and systematic errors, respectively), from chiral extrapolation using $a_t^{-1} = 1.679(2)(7)$ GeV set by the $\rho$ meson mass.
- Charm $1^{-+}$ hybrid meson mass: $4369 \pm 37 \pm 99$ MeV, obtained by tuning $\kappa_t^{\rm charm}=0.1806(5)(18)$ to the spin-averaged S-wave charmonium mass of 3067.6 MeV.
- Mass splitting between the $1^{-+}$ charm hybrid and spin-averaged S-wave charmonium: $1302 \pm 37 \pm 99$ MeV.
- P-wave $1^{++}$ light meson mass (chiral limit, ${\bar u}u$ or ${\bar d}d$): $1438 \pm 32 \pm 57$ MeV.
- P-wave $1^{++}$ ${\bar s}s$ meson mass: $1499 \pm 28 \pm 65$ MeV, compared to the experimental $f_1(1420)$ mass of 1426 MeV.
- Lattice spacings: $a_t \approx 0.11$ fm, $a_s \approx 0.33$ fm, giving a physical spatial volume of $3.96^3$ fm$^3$.

## Context

This work extends previous quenched lattice calculations of hybrid mesons by the MILC collaboration (MILC97, MILC99) and UKQCD collaboration (UKQCD97), which used Wilson gauge actions or clover quark actions on isotropic lattices, as well as CP-PACS (1999) which used NRQCD for heavy quarks on anisotropic lattices. The use of both improved gluon and quark actions on an anisotropic lattice allows coarser lattices and smaller volumes while maintaining control over systematic errors, enabling future studies of dynamical quark effects, continuum extrapolations, and the mixing of hybrid mesons with four-quark states.
