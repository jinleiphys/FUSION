# Nuclear symmetry energy with mesonic cross-couplings in the effective chiral model

> The effective chiral model is extended by introducing the contributions from the cross-couplings between isovector and isoscalar mesons. These cross-couplings are found to be instrumental in improving the density content of the nuclear symmetry energy. The nuclear symmetry energy as well as its slope and curvature parameters at the saturation density are in harmony with those deduced from a diverse set of experimental data. The equation of state for pure neutron matter at sub-saturation densities is also in accordance with the ones obtained from different microscopic models. The maximum mass of neutron star is consistent with the measurement and the radius at the canonical mass of the neutron star is within the empirical bounds.

## Key claim

The effective chiral model, when extended with cross-couplings between isovector and isoscalar mesons (specifically sigma-rho and omega-rho couplings), can simultaneously satisfy constraints on the symmetry energy, its slope and curvature at saturation density, the equation of state for pure neutron matter at sub-saturation densities, and neutron star maximum mass and radius, but only the sigma-rho coupling model (SR) satisfies all constraints, while the omega-rho coupling model (WR) fails to reach the observed maximum neutron star mass of 2.01 solar masses.

## Method

The authors extended the effective chiral model Lagrangian by adding cross-coupling terms between the rho meson and the sigma meson (eta_1 term) and between the rho meson and the omega meson (eta_2 term). They constructed three model variants: no cross-coupling (NCC), sigma-rho coupling (SR), and omega-rho coupling (WR), with coupling parameters adjusted to yield symmetry energy J_0 = 32.5 MeV and slope L = 65 MeV at saturation density rho_0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}. The equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter were computed self-consistently, and neutron star mass-radius relations were obtained using both a polytropic crust matching approach and the Zdunik-Fortin-Haensel (ZFH) method.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.153 fm^{-3}
- Nuclear incompressibility: K = 247 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass at saturation: m*/m = 0.864
- Symmetry energy at saturation: J_0 = 32.5 MeV for all three models (NCC, SR, WR)
- Symmetry energy slope: L = 87 MeV (NCC), 65 MeV (SR and WR)
- Symmetry energy at rho = 0.1 fm^{-3}: J_1 = 22.30 MeV (NCC), 24.49 MeV (SR), 23.68 MeV (WR)
- Symmetry energy curvature: K_sym = -20.09 MeV (NCC), -59.16 MeV (SR), -204.78 MeV (WR)
- Asymmetry term of nuclear incompressibility: K_tau = -434 MeV (NCC), -368 MeV (SR), -513 MeV (WR)
- Maximum neutron star mass: M_max = 1.97 M_sun (NCC and SR), 1.84 M_sun (WR)
- Radius at 1.4 M_sun (ZFH method, appropriate transition density): R_1.4 = 13.12 km (NCC), 12.72 km (SR), 12.03 km (WR)

## Context

This work extends the effective chiral model of Jha (2006) by introducing mesonic cross-couplings that were previously shown to be important in relativistic mean field models such as those by Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005), Agrawal (2010), and Agrawal (2012). The results are compared with several RMF models including NL3 (Lalazissis 1996), IUFSU (Fattoyev 2010), BSP (Agrawal 2012), and BKA22 (Agrawal 2010). The SR model satisfies constraints from isobaric analog states (Danielewicz 2013), heavy ion collisions (Tsang 2008, Russotto 2016), microscopic calculations for pure neutron matter (Gezerlis 2009, Hebeler 2013), and the neutron star mass measurement of PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013). The WR model is disfavored because it cannot reach the 2.01 M_sun maximum mass constraint.
