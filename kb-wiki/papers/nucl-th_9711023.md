# Heavy- to light-meson transition form factors

> Semileptonic heavy -> heavy and heavy -> light meson transitions are studied as a phenomenological application of a heavy-quark limit of Dyson-Schwinger equations. Employing two parameters: E, the difference between the mass of the heavy meson and the effective-mass of the heavy quark; and Lambda, the width of the heavy-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, we calculate f_+(t) for all decays on their entire kinematically accessible t-domain. Our study favours f_B in the range 0.135-0.17 GeV and with E=0.44 GeV and 1/Lambda = 0.14 fm we obtain f_+^{B pi}(0) = 0.46. As a result of neglecting 1/m_c-corrections, we estimate that our calculated values of ρ^2 = 0.87 and f_+^{DK}(0)=0.62 are too low by approximately 15%. However, the bulk of these corrections should cancel in our calculated values of Br(D -> πl nu)/Br(D -> K l nu)=0.13 and f_+^{D pi}(0)/f_+^{DK}(0) = 1.16.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework with only two free parameters (the heavy-meson binding energy \(E\) and the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude width \(\Lambda\)) can provide a unified description of both heavy-to-heavy and heavy-to-light semileptonic meson transitions, but the authors caution that their calculated values for charm-quark processes are systematically too low by approximately 15% due to the neglect of \(1/m_c\) corrections.

## Method

The authors employ a heavy-quark limit of Dyson-Schwinger equations, approximating the \(b\)- and \(c\)-quark propagators as free-particle forms with constant effective masses, while using algebraic parametrisations of light-quark propagators and light-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes fixed from earlier DSE studies. The heavy-meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is modelled by a Gaussian or dipole Ansatz with width \(\Lambda\), and the dressed quark-W-boson vertex is taken as the bare \(\gamma_\mu\) vertex. The two free parameters \(E\) and \(\Lambda\) are fitted to lattice data for \(f_+^{B\pi}\) and the experimental branching ratio for \(B^0 \to \pi^- \ell^+ \nu\), while excluding \(D\)-meson observables from the fit to expose the size of \(1/m_c\) corrections.

## Key numbers

- \(f_B\) favoured in the range 0.135–0.17 GeV
- With \(E = 0.44\) GeV and \(1/\Lambda = 0.14\) fm, \(f_+^{B\pi}(0) = 0.46\)
- Calculated \(\rho^2 = 0.87\) for the heavy-to-heavy universal form factor \(\xi(w)\)
- Calculated \(f_+^{DK}(0) = 0.62\), estimated to be too low by approximately 15%
- Calculated branching ratio ratio \(Br(D \to \pi \ell \nu)/Br(D \to K \ell \nu) = 0.13\)
- Calculated form factor ratio \(f_+^{D\pi}(0)/f_+^{DK}(0) = 1.16\)
- Monopole fit for \(f_+^{B\pi}(t)\): \(m_{\rm mon} = 5.67\) GeV
- Monopole fit for \(f_+^{D\pi}(t)\): \(m_{\rm mon} = 2.15\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based heavy-quark limit developed by Ivanov, Kalinovsky, Maris, and Roberts (1997) for heavy-to-heavy transitions to include heavy-to-light decays such as \(B \to \pi\), \(D \to K\), and \(D \to \pi\). It contrasts with Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET), which cannot calculate the \(q^2\)-dependence of form factors, and with lattice QCD simulations (e.g., the lattice data used for fitting). The results enable improved extraction of CKM matrix elements \(V_{cb}\) and \(V_{ub}\) from experimental semileptonic decay rates, and the calculated ratio \(Br(D \to \pi \ell \nu)/Br(D \to K \ell \nu)\) provides a testable prediction where \(1/m_c\) corrections largely cancel.
