# Isospin Coherence and Final-State Scattering of Disoriented Chiral Condensate

> We examine the validity of the notion of the coherent state for pions and the quantum scattering effect in the final state of pion emission. When the number of particles is large, the effect caused by the small but finite mass difference between the neutral and charged pions can add up substantially in the quantum evolution of an initially coherent state. As a result, the states with quite different numbers of neutral or charged pions are essentially incoherent. The importance of the quantum scattering in the final-state isospin charge distribution of a disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) is investigated. We find that the scattering effect significantly reduces the spectacular Centauro and anti-Centauro events. The deformation of a charge distribution $dP/df$ predicted by the classical field theory is significant only for a DCC with the size of 10 fm or more.

# Isospin Coherence and Final-State Scattering of Disoriented Chiral Condensate

## Key claim

The classical field theory prediction of the neutral pion fraction distribution $dP/df = 1/(2\sqrt{f})$ for disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) is not altered by the isospin-breaking phase shift from the pion mass difference, but quantum rescattering in the final state significantly reduces the spectacular Centauro and anti-Centauro events. The deformation of the charge distribution is significant only for a DCC with size of 10 fm or more.

## Method

The authors examine the validity of the coherent state description for pions emitted from a DCC by considering the effect of the finite mass difference between neutral and charged pions ($\Delta m_\pi = 4.6$ MeV) on the quantum evolution of an initially coherent isosinglet state. They then model the final-state quantum scattering using a Boltzmann transport equation for the three pion charge states, considering only two-body $\pi\pi$ scattering processes $\pi^0\pi^0 \leftrightarrow \pi^+\pi^-$, and solve analytically for the time evolution of the neutral pion fraction $f(t)$ under the approximation of free-streaming diffusion.

## Key numbers

- The electromagnetic mass difference between charged and neutral pions is $\Delta m_\pi = m(\pi^\pm) - m(\pi^0) = 4.6$ MeV.
- For a DCC with $N=50$ pions and $\Delta f = 0.2$, the isospin-breaking coherence loss occurs on a time scale $\tau \simeq 4$ fm/c.
- The mean free path for the conversion $\pi^+\pi^- \rightarrow \pi^0\pi^0$ at threshold is $l_0 \simeq 27.7$ fm, using $f_\pi = 93$ MeV.
- For a DCC with radius $R_D = 5$ fm, the dilution factor ${\cal F}(\infty) \simeq 0.24$, leading to $f(\infty) \simeq 80\%$ when starting from all neutral pions ($f_0=1$), meaning 20% of original $\pi^0$'s are converted into $\pi^\pm$.
- The deformation of the charge distribution is significant for $R_D = 10$ fm but not too severe for $R_D = 4$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the DCC literature following the "Baked Alaska" scenario of Bjorken, Kowalski, and Taylor (1992) and the classical field theory predictions of Anselm and Ryskin (1991) and Blaizot and Krzywcki (1992). It addresses the validity of the coherent state formalism used by Horn and Silver (1971) and Kowalski and Taylor (1992), and the isospin-breaking effect studied by Amado and Kogan (1995). The results show that for DCC sizes up to about 5 fm, the classical prediction remains testable, but for larger DCCs the final-state scattering makes experimental distinction from non-DCC events more difficult, motivating further study of the diffusion dynamics in the strongly interacting regime.
