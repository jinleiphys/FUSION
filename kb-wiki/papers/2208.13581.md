# Multiquark-Oriented QCD Sum Rules

> We propose to increase the factual reliability of descriptions of exotic multiquark hadrons utilizing the approach to bound states of strongly interacting constituents known as QCD sum rules, by allowing exclusively all contributions that potentially bear some relevance for multiquark states to enter the correlation functions that form the main ingredient of this framework. The route to this goal is illustrated for the (presumably least involved) special case of tetraquark states.

# Multiquark-Oriented QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim
The authors propose that standard QCD sum rule analyses of multiquark hadrons can be made more reliable by systematically retaining only those contributions to correlation functions that are capable of bearing information about multiquark states, while discarding all contributions that relate exclusively to ordinary mesons. This "multiquark-adequate" approach is illustrated for the specific case of tetraquarks, with the condition that at least two gluon exchanges of appropriate topology are required for a contribution to be tetraquark-phile.

## Method
The authors analyze four-point correlation functions of quark-antiquark bilinear currents, which describe scattering amplitudes of two conventional mesons to two conventional mesons, and derive from them two-point and three-point correlation functions for tetraquark-interpolating operators. They apply a rigorous criterion based on the Landau equations to identify "tetraquark-phile" contributions, which depend nonpolynomially on the Mandelstam variable s and exhibit branch cuts starting at the four-quark threshold. For both flavour-retaining and flavour-rearranging correlation functions, they decompose the relations into separate QCD sum rules for ordinary mesons and tetraquark-adequate QCD sum rules, incorporating perturbative spectral densities, Borel-transformed power corrections, and effective thresholds.

## Key numbers
- The branch point for true intermediate four-quark states is at $\hat s = (m_a + m_b + m_c + m_d)^2$, where $m_a, m_b, m_c, m_d$ are the masses of the four constituent quarks.
- The tetraquark-adequate QCD sum rules involve spectral densities $\rho_{\rm p}$, $\rho_{\rm r}$, $\Delta_{\rm p}$, and $\Delta_{\rm r}$ integrated from $\hat s$ up to an effective threshold $s_{\rm eff}(\tau)$ that depends on the Borel parameter $\tau$.
- The sum rules yield expressions for tetraquark masses $M$, decay constants $f_{\overline ab\overline cd}$ and $f_{\overline ad\overline cb}$, and amplitudes $A(T \to j_{\overline ab} j_{\overline cd})$ and $A(T \to j_{\overline ad} j_{\overline cb})$.

## Context
This work extends the systematic scrutiny of QCD sum rules for exotic hadrons by Lucha, Melikhov, and Sazdjian (2017-2022) and earlier studies on effective thresholds (Ioffe and others, 1980s). It addresses the problem that traditional QCD sum rules for multiquark states often mix contributions from ordinary mesons with those genuinely related to tetraquarks, as identified in prior work on exotic state sum rules (ESRp, ESRr) and tetraquark-meson amplitude analyses (TMA1, TMA2, TMA3). The framework enables reliable extraction of tetraquark properties from two- and three-point correlation functions, and explicitly accounts for the cluster reducibility of multiquarks, which requires treating them as both compact and molecular-type hadrons.
