# Tensor optimized Fermi sphere method for nuclear matter -- power series correlated wave function and a cluster expansion

> A new formalism, called "tensor optimized Fermi sphere (TOFS) method", is developed to treat the nuclear matter using a bare interaction among nucleons. In this method, the correlated nuclear matter wave function is taken to be a power series type, $Ψ_{N}=[\sum_{n=0}^{N} {(1/n!)F^n}]Φ_0$ and an exponential type, $Ψ_{\rm ex}=\exp(F) Φ_0$, with the uncorrelated Fermi-gas wave function $Φ_0$, where the correlation operator $F$ can induce central, spin-isospin, tensor, etc.~correlations, and $Ψ_{\rm ex}$ corresponds to a limiting case of $Ψ_{N}$ ($N \rightarrow \infty$). In the TOFS formalism based on Hermitian form, it is shown that the energy per particle in nuclear matter with $Ψ_{\rm ex}$ can be expressed in terms of a linked-cluster expansion. On the basis of these results, we present the formula of the energy per particle in nuclear matter with $Ψ_{N}$. We call the $N$th-order TOFS calculation for evaluating the energy with $Ψ_N$, where the correlation functions are optimally determined in the variation of the energy. The TOFS theory is applied for the study of symmetric nuclear matter using a central NN potential with short-range repulsion. The calculated results are fairly consistent to those of other theories such as the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach etc.

## Key claim

The paper develops a new formalism called the "tensor optimized Fermi sphere (TOFS) method" for nuclear matter, which uses a power-series correlated wave function and proves that the energy per particle can be expressed as a linked-cluster expansion only when the wave function takes the exponential form \(\Psi_{\rm ex} = \exp(F)\Phi_0\) (the \(N \to \infty\) limit), while arbitrary power-series forms without this limit do not fully cancel unlinked diagrams.

## Method

The correlated nuclear matter wave function is taken as either a power series type \(\Psi_N = [\sum_{n=0}^N (1/n!)F^n]\Phi_0\) or an exponential type \(\Psi_{\rm ex} = \exp(F)\Phi_0\), where \(\Phi_0\) is the uncorrelated Fermi-gas wave function and the correlation operator \(F = F_S + F_D\) includes central and tensor correlations. The energy per particle is evaluated via a cluster expansion in Hermitian form, with the correlation functions expanded in Gaussian functions whose coefficients and ranges are treated as variational parameters. The formalism is applied to symmetric nuclear matter using the Argonne V4' central NN potential with short-range repulsion, and the results are compared with other many-body approaches such as the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon density of symmetric nuclear matter is given by \(\rho = 2k_F^3/(3\pi^2)\), where \(k_F\) is the Fermi wave number.
- The Slater function \(h(k_F R) = 3j_1(k_F R)/(k_F R)\) appears in the evaluation of matrix elements, where \(j_1\) is the spherical Bessel function.
- The calculated results for symmetric nuclear matter using the Argonne V4' potential are "fairly consistent" with those of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach (no specific numerical energy values are provided in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD) framework, previously developed for finite nuclei by Myo et al. (2015-2017), to infinite nuclear matter. It contrasts with the Jastrow-type correlated wave function used in the Fermi hypernetted chain (FHNC) method developed by Fantoni and Rosati (1972-1978) and Pandharipande (1979), and with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach (Brueckner, 1955-1958). The TOFS method enables systematic treatment of tensor correlations in nuclear matter via a power-series expansion, and is applicable to other infinite fermionic systems such as neutron matter and electron gas.
