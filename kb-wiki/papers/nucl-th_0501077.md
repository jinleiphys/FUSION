# Evidence for psi' Regeneration in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The production of the psi' meson in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS is analyzed. It provides an evidence for charmonium regeneration via charm quark-antiquark coalescence at hadronization.

# Evidence for $\psi'$ Regeneration in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The observed $\psi'$ yield in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS is too high to be explained by standard suppression models alone, providing evidence that $\psi'$ mesons are regenerated via charm quark-antiquark coalescence at hadronization, rather than being produced only in initial hard parton collisions.

## Method
The authors analyzed $\psi'$ production data from the NA50 collaboration in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, comparing the measured $\psi'$ to $J/\psi$ ratio with predictions from three models: the threshold suppression model, the comover model, and the statistical coalescence model (SCM). They extracted fit parameters for each model and examined whether the inferred suppression thresholds and cross sections were physically reasonable given the weaker binding of $\psi'$ compared to $\chi$ states.

## Key numbers
- Threshold density for $\psi'$ suppression: $n_{\psi'} = 2.17^{+0.33}_{-0.43}$ fm$^{-2}$, which is approximately the same as or larger than the threshold for $\chi$ states ($n_{\chi} = 1.95^{+0.35}_{-0.45}$ fm$^{-2}$), despite $\psi'$ being much more weakly bound.
- Comover dissociation cross section for $\psi'$: $\sigma^{co}_{\psi'} = 2.84^{+1.56}_{-0.79}$ mb, about a factor of 3 larger than for $J/\psi$ ($\sigma^{co}_{J/\psi} = 1.01 \pm 0.05$ mb).
- The $\psi'$ to $J/\psi$ ratio is nearly constant for events with participant nucleon number $N_p > 100-150$, and equals the thermal value corresponding to freeze-out temperatures $T = 150-170$ MeV, consistent with the SCM.

## Context
This work extends the statistical coalescence model (SCM) of Gorenstein and Gazdzicki (2001) to excited charmonium states, challenging the standard approach of Matsui and Satz (1986) which assumes charmonia are created only in initial hard collisions and then suppressed. The standard threshold suppression model (Blaizot, 1996) and comover model (Capella, 1996; Spieles, 1998) can fit the $\psi'$ data but require physically implausible parameters, whereas the SCM naturally explains the observed yield. The results suggest that excited charmonium states are likely formed at hadronization rather than in the initial stage, and the authors propose that measurements at lower energies (e.g., at the new GSI facility) could help distinguish between production mechanisms.
