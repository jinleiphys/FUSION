# Nuclear matter superfluidity in an effective hadronic field model with excluded volume corrections

> Properties of the 1S0 superfluid phase are studied for symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature. It is described within a covariant hadronic field model, of the sigma-omega type, with addition of density dependent correlations simulating effects due to finite extension of nucleons. The model is solved in a selfconsistent Hartree-Bogoliubov approach, assuming instantaneous interactions in the superfluid phase. A comparison with the results obtained from several hadronic field models is done. Main characteristics of our description of the superfluid gap are in qualitative agreement with some studies using microscopic potentials, although further refinements could improve its performance.

## Key claim

The paper claims that introducing a finite-volume (excluded volume) correction to the nucleon fields in a simple sigma-omega QHD model reduces the excessively large 1S0 superfluid gap in symmetric nuclear matter by 30-40%, bringing it closer to non-relativistic estimates, but the model still overestimates the gap compared to microscopic calculations (e.g., by about 2.5 MeV at the maximum).

## Method

The author uses a covariant hadronic field model of the sigma-omega type, solved in a self-consistent Hartree-Bogoliubov approach with instantaneous interactions, to study the 1S0 superfluid phase in symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature. Density-dependent correlations simulating the finite spatial extension of nucleons are introduced via an excluded volume correction factor Theta = 1 - sum(n_b v_b), where v_b is the effective volume of a nucleon, which modifies the normalization of nucleon fields and the gap equation. The coupling constants are fixed to reproduce saturation properties (binding energy -16 MeV, Fermi momentum 1.42 fm^-1), and the nucleon size R is treated as an adjustable parameter between 0.5 and 0.9 fm.

## Key numbers

* Maximum gap without finite extension corrections (NFEC) for the density-dependent coupling model (Hofmann, 2001): Delta_max(p_F) ≈ 15 MeV at n/n0 = 0.25.
* Maximum gap without finite extension corrections (NFEC) for the non-linear model (Zimanyi, 1988): Delta_max(p_F) = 10.6 MeV at n/n0 ≈ 0.1.
* With finite extension corrections (FEC) and R = 0.7 fm, the lowest maximum gap is Delta_max = 5.5 MeV at p_F = 0.8 fm^-1.
* The ratio Delta_max(FEC)/Delta_max(NFEC) ranges from 0.68 (R = 0.6 fm) to 0.56 (R = 0.7 fm), corresponding to a 32% to 44% reduction.
* The upper density for non-zero pairing gap in the FEC case is p_F = 1.6 fm^-1, a reduction of more than 20% compared to NFEC.
* The pairing potential v(p_F, q) at the density of maximum gap ranges from -5 MeV to +1 MeV in the FEC case.

## Context

This work extends the QHD-based study of nuclear superfluidity initiated by Kucharek (1990), which found an excessively large gap (Delta_max ≈ 10 MeV) that disagreed with non-relativistic expectations. The author compares results to microscopic Brueckner theory calculations by Cao (2008), which found Delta_max ≈ 5 MeV at p_F = 0.9 fm^-1, and to the form-factor approach of Matsuzaki (2002), which also aimed to produce physically acceptable gaps within QHD. The excluded volume correction provides a simpler alternative to form factors or meson self-energy corrections (Chen, 2004) and enables a unified description of particle-particle and particle-hole channels, though further refinements (Fock terms, additional mesons, non-linear sigma terms) are needed to match microscopic calculations.
