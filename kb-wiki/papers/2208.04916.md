# SU(3)_f Constraints on Hypernuclear Energy Density Functionals

> A covariant hyper-nuclear energy density functional (EDF) is derived from in medium nucleon meson vertex functionals, assuring the proper description of nuclear mean-field dynamics. The fundamental SU(3) coupling constants for the mean-field relevant vector (m=V) and scalar (m=S) interactions as functionals of the total baryon density are determined. Scalar and vector potentials and the resulting hyperon mean-fields in asymmetric nuclear matter are constructed and discussed, addressing also effects from three-body interactions. Λ- Σ^0 mixing in asymmetric nuclear through the coupling to the background isovector mean-field is addressed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a covariant hypernuclear energy density functional (EDF) from in-medium nucleon-meson vertex functionals using SU(3) symmetry relations, but the numerical values of the resulting SU(3) density-dependent coupling functionals depend on the chosen in-medium NN-interaction model and are affected by the inclusion of three-body interactions at the level of about 5% or less.

## Method

The authors start from Density Dependent Relativistic Hadron (DDRH) theory, where effective density-dependent NN-meson coupling functionals are derived from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) G-matrix calculations by projecting the DBHF self-energies onto an effective Hartree theory. Using the four available NN-meson vertex functionals (isoscalar sigma and omega, isovector delta and rho), they apply SU(3) symmetry relations to determine the fundamental SU(3) coupling constants g_D, g_F, and g_S for scalar and vector interactions as functionals of total baryon density. The approach assumes ideal mixing (mixing angle theta = 35.26 degrees) and that nucleons do not couple to physical singlet mesons, fixing g_NN_eta' = g_NN_f0 = g_NN_phi = 0.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: 0.180 fm^-3 (2-body only) and 0.160 fm^-3 (with 3-body interactions)
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -15.603 MeV (2-body only) and -16.000 MeV (with 3-body interactions)
- Incompressibility K_infinity: 281.945 MeV (2-body only) and 283.136 MeV (with 3-body interactions)
- Symmetry energy E_sym: 31.154 MeV (2-body only) and 32.000 MeV (with 3-body interactions)
- Slope parameter L_sym: 88.627 MeV (2-body only) and 90.000 MeV (with 3-body interactions)
- Symmetry energy curvature K_sym: 201.399 MeV (2-body only) and 133.502 MeV (with 3-body interactions)
- Three-body interactions modify scalar and vector NN-vertices at a level of about 5% or less

## Context

This work extends the DDRH approach developed by Lenske (2004, 2018) from pure nucleon dynamics to the full octet of baryons using SU(3) symmetry, building on the SU(3) rules introduced by de Swart (1963). The approach differs from earlier hypernuclear EDF work by Glendenning (1991), Lanskoy (1997), and Schulze (2014) by exploiting SU(3) relations not on the Born level but on the level of fully resummed in-medium interactions. The results enable construction of well-constrained hypernuclear EDFs for investigations of medium- and heavy-mass hypernuclei and extrapolations to neutron star matter, with applications to physical systems in preparation.
