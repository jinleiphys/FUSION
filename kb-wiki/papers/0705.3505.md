# Thermal photons to dileptons ratio at LHC

> It is shown that the ratio of transverse momentum (p_T) distribution of thermal photons to dileptons produced in heavy ion collisions reaches a plateau above p_T=1 GeV. We argue that the value of the ratio in the plateau region can be used to estimate the initial temperature.

## Key claim

The ratio of transverse momentum distributions of thermal photons to thermal dileptons reaches a plateau above \(p_T = 1\) GeV, and the value of this ratio in the plateau region can be used to estimate the initial temperature of the system produced in heavy ion collisions. The authors hedge that this holds only because model dependences in the individual spectra cancel in the ratio, and that the plateau is a thermal effect distinct from the corresponding ratio from hard processes.

## Method

The authors computed the invariant yield of thermal photons and dileptons by convolving static production rates over a (2+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic expansion with longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry. Photon rates from QGP were included up to \(O(\alpha \alpha_s)\), and from hadronic matter via an exhaustive set of reactions including strange mesons and radiative decays of higher resonances with form factors. Dilepton rates from QGP were included up to \(O(\alpha^2 \alpha_s)\), and from hadronic matter via decays of light vector mesons, with the invariant mass integrated over \(2m_\pi < M < 1.05\) GeV. The ratio \(R_{em}\) was then evaluated for SPS, RHIC, and LHC initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- For LHC, two initial temperatures were used: \(T_i = 0.85\) GeV (with \(dN/dy = 4000\) and \(\tau_i = 0.08\) fm) and \(T_i = 0.905\) GeV (with \(dN/dy = 4730\) and \(\tau_i = 0.08\) fm).
- For RHIC: \(T_i = 0.4\) GeV, \(\tau_i = 0.2\) fm, \(dN/dy = 1100\).
- For SPS: \(T_i = 0.2\) GeV, \(\tau_i = 1\) fm, \(dN/dy = 700\).
- The freeze-out temperature for all cases was \(T_f = 120\) MeV.
- The ratio \(R_{em}\) reaches a plateau for \(p_T > 1\) GeV for SPS, RHIC, and LHC.
- The plateau values satisfy \(R_{em}^{LHC} > R_{em}^{RHIC} > R_{em}^{SPS}\).
- The ratio from hard processes, \(R_{em}^{pQCD}\), differs from the thermal ratio for \(p_T\) up to \(\sim 3\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Nayak and Alam (2005) by showing that the ratio of thermal photon to dilepton spectra cancels model-dependent parameters that affect the individual spectra, such as the equation of state, medium effects on hadrons, radial flow, transition temperature, and freeze-out temperature. The result enables a more robust extraction of the initial temperature at LHC energies than from either spectrum alone. The authors note that at SPS the hadronic matter contribution dominates the total, making QGP conclusions difficult, but at RHIC and LHC the quark matter contribution is significant.
