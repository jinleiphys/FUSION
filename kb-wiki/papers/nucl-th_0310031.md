# Integrability and Quantum Phase Transitions in Interacting Boson Models

> The exact solution of the boson pairing hamiltonian given by Richardson in the sixties is used to study the phenomena of level crossings and quantum phase transitions in the integrable regions of the sd and sdg interacting boson models.

## Key claim

The paper uses Richardson's exact solution of the boson pairing Hamiltonian to show that the transition from O(6) to U(5) in the sd interacting boson model is quantum integrable everywhere along the path, even though there is no global dynamical symmetry, and that this integrability precludes first-order phase transitions, allowing only phase transitions of order greater than one.

## Method

The authors construct integrable boson pairing Hamiltonians using the rational class of SU(1,1) pair algebras, where the constants of motion are given by operators R_l that mutually commute. They diagonalize these Hamiltonians via Richardson's method, solving a set of coupled nonlinear equations for the pair energies e_alpha, and apply this to the sd and sdg interacting boson models to study level crossings and quantum phase transitions as functions of the control parameters x and chi.

## Key numbers

- For N=10 bosons in the sd model, the SU(3)-O(6)-SU(3) transition (as a function of chi) shows level repulsion everywhere except at chi=0, where the O(6) dynamical symmetry is realized and level crossings are allowed.
- For N=10 bosons in the sd model, the SU(3)-U(5) transition (as a function of x) shows level repulsion preventing crossings, with the inset amplifying one of the closest two-level approaches.
- For N=10 bosons in the sdg model, the O(15)-U(14) transition shows level crossings in the 0+, tau=0 states but no crossings with the ground state.
- For N=1000 bosons in the O(6)-U(5) transition, the order parameter and 2+ energy indicate a second-order phase transition.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of quantum integrability beyond dynamical symmetry limits in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) of nuclei, building on the three dynamical symmetry limits (U(5), SU(3), O(6)) identified in the IBM framework. The authors use Richardson's exact solution from the 1960s to analyze the O(6)-U(5) leg of the Casten triangle, confirming earlier results by Arias and collaborators (2000) and by Casten and collaborators (1999) that this region is integrable, and reconciling Landau theory with the Ehrenfest classification of phase transitions as previously studied by Jolie and collaborators (2002). The work enables exact finite-size studies of quantum phase transitions in larger boson spaces such as the sdg model.
