# Tracing pT-differential radial flow from blast-wave analytics to quark coalescence

> The observable $v_0(\pt)$, which quantifies event-by-event fluctuations in the differential transverse-momentum spectrum, is proposed as a direct and penetrating probe of radial flow in heavy-ion collisions. Recent measurements at the LHC exhibit a clear mass ordering for pions, kaons and protons at low \pt and a baryon-meson splitting at intermediate \pt, resembling to the well-known features of elliptic flow ($v_2$). In this letter, we first derive an analytic expression of $v_0(\pt)$ within a Blast-Wave framework incorporating fluctuations of freeze-out temperature and radial expansion velocity, which can naturally explains the experimentally observed mass ordering. The distinct dynamical origins of the mass ordering in $v_0(\pt)$ and $v_2(\pt)$ are discussed. Furthermore, using the AMPT model, we demonstrate that the baryon-meson splitting emerges spontaneously from the quark coalescence. This study provides deeper insight into the $v_0(\pt)$ observable and the collective dynamics of the QGP.

# Tracing pT-differential radial flow from blast-wave analytics to quark coalescence

## Key claim

The observable \(v_0(p_T)\) is proposed as a direct probe of radial flow fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, with the claim that its mass ordering at low \(p_T\) originates from thermal spectral response to radial flow fluctuations rather than from azimuthal anisotropy, and that its baryon-meson splitting at intermediate \(p_T\) emerges spontaneously from quark coalescence. The hedge is that the analytic blast-wave expression for \(v_0(p_T)\) assumes independent fluctuations of freeze-out temperature and radial expansion velocity with zero cross-correlation, and that the coalescence derivation neglects multiplicity fluctuations.

## Method

The authors first derive an analytic expression for \(v_0(p_T)\) within a blast-wave model extended to include event-by-event fluctuations of the kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T\) and the surface flow velocity \(\beta_s\), performing a simultaneous fit to ALICE data for pions, kaons, and protons in 30–40% centrality Pb–Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV. They then use the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model with string melting, which includes partonic evolution via ZPC, quark coalescence for hadronization, and hadronic rescatterings via ART, to simulate the same collision system and extract \(v_0(p_T)\) for both partons and hadrons. A generalized coalescence relation \(v_0^h(p_T^h) = n_q \, v_0^q(p_T^h / n_q)\) is derived and applied to quark-level \(v_0\) from AMPT to reproduce the baryon-meson splitting.

## Key numbers

- Blast-wave fit parameters for 30–40% Pb–Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV: freeze-out temperature \(T = 0.108 \pm 0.002\) GeV, surface velocity \(\beta_s = 0.837 \pm 0.002\), \(\rho_2 = 0.085 \pm 0.002\), \(R_x = 8.30 \pm 0.013\) fm.
- Extracted fluctuation amplitudes from simultaneous fit to ALICE \(v_0(p_T)\) data: \(\sigma_T = 0.0012 \pm 0.0006\), \(\sigma_\beta = 0.0098 \pm 0.0021\), corresponding to approximately 1.1–1.2% of the mean parameter values.
- In the blast-wave model with \(\rho_2\) variation, at \(p_T \approx 3\) GeV/\(c\), a 50% variation in \(v_2\) induces less than a 3% change in \(v_0(p_T)\).
- The coalescence calculation yields a crossing point between meson and baryon \(v_0(p_T)\) near \(3\) GeV/\(c\), consistent with experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the study of the \(v_0(p_T)\) observable first proposed by Shen et al. (2023) and measured by the ATLAS and ALICE Collaborations, which previously demonstrated sensitivity to bulk viscosity and revealed mass ordering and baryon-meson splitting. The authors provide the first analytic blast-wave derivation for \(v_0(p_T)\), complementing hydrodynamic simulations by V0_2024 and the Bayesian blast-wave analysis by Swati Saha et al. (2024). The coalescence relation derived here parallels the well-established number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling for elliptic flow \(v_2\), enabling future studies of NCQ scaling and event shape engineering for \(v_0(p_T)\).
