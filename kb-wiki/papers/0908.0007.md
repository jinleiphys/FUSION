# Chiral and deconfinement transition from Dyson-Schwinger equations

> We determine the quark condensate and the dressed Polyakov loop from the finite temperature Landau gauge quark propagator evaluated with U(1)-valued boundary conditions in an approximation to quenched QCD. These gauge invariant quantities allow for an investigation of the chiral and deconfinement transition. We compare results from Dyson-Schwinger equations on a lattice with infinite volume continuum results and study the temperature and quark mass dependence of both quantities. In particular we investigate the chiral condensate and the dressed Polyakov loop in the chiral limit. We also consider an alternative order parameter for the deconfinement transition, the dual scalar quark dressing, and compare with the dressed Polyakov loop. As a result we find only slightly different transition temperatures for the chiral and the deconfinement transition at finite quark masses; in the chiral limit both transitions coincide.

## Key claim

The chiral and deconfinement transitions in quenched SU(2) QCD have only slightly different transition temperatures at finite quark masses (roughly corresponding to an up-quark), and in the chiral limit both transitions coincide exactly; this result depends on the use of a modified renormalization condition that eliminates temperature effects in the bare quark mass.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator in Landau gauge at finite temperature using the Matsubara formalism, with input from quenched SU(2) lattice results for the temperature-dependent gluon propagator (Cucchieri, 2007) and a temperature-dependent ansatz for the quark-gluon vertex. They computed the quark condensate and the dressed Polyakov loop (dual condensate) as functions of temperature and boundary angle, using U(1)-valued boundary conditions, and compared results from infinite volume continuum DSEs with DSEs formulated on a torus.

## Key numbers

- Chiral transition temperature from the peak of the temperature derivative of the ordinary chiral condensate: approximately 300 MeV (for quark mass corresponding to an up-quark).
- Deconfinement transition temperature from the dressed Polyakov loop: approximately 308 MeV (infinite volume continuum result, for the same quark mass).
- The ultraviolet cutoff used for regularization: Lambda = sqrt(5) x 10^2 GeV (approximately 224 GeV).
- Renormalization point for the infinite volume calculation: mu^2 = 10545 GeV^2.
- Renormalized quark mass: m(mu) = 1.85 MeV, matching the conditions of Fischer (2009) for mass 10 MeV at T = 200 MeV and mu^2 = 20 GeV^2.
- Fit parameters for the gluon propagator: Lambda = 1.4 GeV, c = 9.8 GeV^2; temperature-dependent parameters a_T and a_L given for T = 0, 119, 298, 597 MeV (e.g., a_T = 1.34 at 298 MeV, a_L = 0.8 at 298 MeV).
- Fit parameters for the quark-gluon vertex: d_1 = 7.6 GeV^2, d_2 = 0.5 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the study of the dressed Polyakov loop from Dyson-Schwinger equations on a torus (Fischer, 2009) to the infinite volume and continuum limit, and to the chiral limit. It also introduces the dual scalar quark dressing function as an alternative order parameter for deconfinement. The results are compared with lattice studies of the dual condensate (Bilgici, 2008) and with the general framework connecting chiral symmetry breaking and confinement via spectral sums of the Dirac operator (Gattringer, 2006; Synatschke, 2008). The finding that chiral and deconfinement transitions coincide in the chiral limit supports the idea of a common underlying mechanism, while the slight separation at finite quark masses enables future studies of the crossover region in real-world QCD.
