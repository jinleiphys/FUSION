# Weakening of $N=28$ shell gap and the nature of $0_2^+$ states

> The work reports a novel application of the interacting boson model in light-mass region around $^{48}$Ca, that takes into account intruder states and configuration mixing. The model is shown to provide a reasonable description of the observed low-lying yrast and yrare states of the $N=28$ even-even isotones from Si to Fe, and even-even Ca isotopes. The nature of $0_2^+$ states is addressed in terms of the competition between spherical and deformed intruder configurations, and the rigidity of the $N=28$ shell gap is tested. The $0_2^+$ isomer in $^{44}$S is shown to arise from a weak mixing between the two configurations. The unresolved low-lying spectra in $^{46}$Ar is also approached using the core-excitations across $N=28$ shell. The SU(3) structure in $^{42}$Si is supported by the present calculation. The nearly spherical nature of $^{50}$Ti, $^{52}$Cr, $^{54}$Fe and $^{42,44,46}$Ca isotopes is found, while the core excitations are found to be essential for the description of yrare states. Shell model calculation is also performed for comparison.

## Key claim
The interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) provides a reasonable description of low-lying yrast and yrare states in N=28 isotones from Si to Fe and Ca isotopes, with the 0_2^+ isomer in 44S arising from weak mixing between spherical U(5) and deformed SU(3) configurations, though the model's applicability to light-mass nuclei where seniority-type configurations dominate was previously considered beyond its conventional scope.

## Method
The authors applied the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) to even-even N=28 isotones (42Si, 44S, 46Ar, 50Ti, 52Cr, 54Fe) and even-even Ca isotopes (42,44,46Ca), using up to two configurations: normal [n] (0p-0h) and intruder [n+2] (2p-2h) boson spaces. Parameters were fitted to reproduce experimental excitation energies and transition rates, with specific symmetry assumptions including U(5) for spherical and SU(3) or overline{SU(3)} for deformed configurations. Nuclear shell model (NSM) calculations using the SDPF-MU interaction for lighter isotones and GXPF1A/KB3G interactions for heavier isotones were performed for comparison using the NushellX code.

## Key numbers
- 44S: 0_2^+ isomer at 2.6 microseconds; mixing parameters alpha=beta=0.2 MeV and energy offset Delta=0.55 MeV; calculated B(E2; 0_2^+ -> 2_1^+) reproduces experimental value of 42 +/- 13 e^2 fm^4 (NSM with SDPF-MU gives 25.5 e^2 fm^4)
- 44S: energy ratio R_{4/2}=E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+) < 2, consistent with experiment; calculated B(E2; 4_1^+ -> 2_1^+) about 50 e^2 fm^4
- 42Si: experimental 2_1^+ energy about 0.7 MeV; epsilon=0.4 MeV for [n] and 0.5 MeV for [n+2] configurations; oblate overline{SU(3)} symmetry assumed with chi=1.33 for normal configuration
- 46Ar: experimental 2_1^+ energy about 1.4-1.5 MeV; epsilon=1.2 MeV for [n] and 1.1 MeV for [n+2] configurations; bosonic effective charges e_{2,n}=e_{2,n+2}=6 e fm^2
- 50Ti, 52Cr, 54Fe: experimental 2_1^+ excitation energy approximately 1.4-1.5 MeV; epsilon values in that range for all three nuclei
- Ca isotopes: 0_2^+ states at 1.8 MeV in 42,44Ca and at 2.4 MeV in 46Ca; epsilon relatively small compared to Z>20 N=28 isotones

## Context
This work extends the IBM-CM framework, previously applied in heavy nuclei such as the Pb-Hg region (Heyde 2011, Duval 1981, Nomura 2013, Nomura 2016), neutron-rich N≈60 region (Sambataro 1982, Nomura 2016), Z≈50 region (Leviatan 2018), and N≈Z≈40 region (Nomura 2022), to the light-mass region around 48Ca where shape coexistence is empirically observed. The study addresses controversial cases of 42Si, 44S, and 46Ar that have been challenging for shell model calculations (Bastin 2007, Gade 2019) and previous theoretical approaches including NSM (Force 2010) and symmetry conserving configuration mixing (Rodriguez 2011). The results enable further experimental investigation of 0_2^+ states in 42Si and 46Ar using radioactive-ion beams, and suggest that configuration mixing plays a role in lighter mass nuclei similar to heavier regions where shape coexistence occurs.
