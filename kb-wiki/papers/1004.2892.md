# Effective field theory and electro-weak processes

> Talk at the conference on "Achievements and New Directions in Subatomic Physics: Workshop in Honour of Tony Thomas' 60th Birthday", 15-19 February 2010

## Key claim

Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) provides a systematic, model-independent framework for evaluating one- and two-nucleon electroweak processes at low momentum transfer, but the two-nucleon calculations rely on a hybrid approach (EFT*) that introduces a Gaussian cut-off and an axial two-nucleon low-energy constant (LEC) that must be determined from three-nucleon data until the MuSun experiment provides a direct two-nucleon determination.

## Method

The author applies Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) to one-nucleon processes (ordinary muon capture, radiative muon capture, and radiative corrections to neutron beta-decay and the CHOOZ process) and to two-nucleon processes (muon capture on deuteron, SNO neutrino-deuteron reactions, and radiative pion capture on deuteron). For two-nucleon processes, a hybrid approach (EFT*) is used where one- and two-nucleon transition operators come from ChPT but nuclear wave functions are evaluated using modern high-precision NN potentials (Argonne V18, CD-Bonn, Vlow-k) with a Gaussian cut-off to limit high-momentum components.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic scale in ChPT is Lambda_ch ~ 1 GeV, much larger than the pion mass m_pi ~ sqrt(m_quark) << Lambda_ch
- The expansion parameter is Q/Lambda_ch << 1, where Q is the typical 4-momentum or m_pi
- The nucleon axial coupling constant g_A ~ 1.27 is an example of a LEC
- The muon-proton spin-singlet capture rate expansion: Gamma = (957 - 245 GeV/m_N + [30.4 GeV^2/m_N^2 - 43.17]) s^{-1}
- The Gaussian cut-off Lambda_G is varied between 500 MeV and 800 MeV, giving less than 1% variation in observables
- The MuSun collaboration projects a 1.5% error on the muon-deuteron capture rate measurement (2009-2011)
- The TRIUMF RMC measurement was for photon energies E_gamma > 60 MeV

## Context

This talk extends the systematic HBChPT framework developed in reviews by Bernard (1995, 2008) and applies it to electroweak processes of astrophysical importance. The work builds on prior calculations of the pseudoscalar form factor by Adler and Dothan (1966) and Wolfenstein (1970), with Kaiser (2003) showing next-order corrections are very small. The MuCap experiment at PSI (Andreev, 2007) has confirmed the ChPT prediction for muon-proton capture, while the TRIUMF RMC measurement (Jonkmans, 1996; Wright, 1998) disagreed with HBChPT predictions. The MuSun experiment at PSI (Kammel, 2010) will determine the axial two-nucleon LEC d^R, enabling model-independent predictions for solar pp fusion, SNO neutrino-deuteron reactions, and the neutron-neutron scattering length determination, while also fixing one of the two unknown LECs in the ChPT three-nucleon potential (related to c_D in Nogga, 2004).
