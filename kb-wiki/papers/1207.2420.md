# Weinberg's approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering revisited

> We propose a new, renormalizable approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering in chiral effective field theory based on the manifestly Lorentz invariant form of the effective Lagrangian without employing the heavy-baryon expansion. For the pion-less case and for the formulation based on perturbative pions, the new approach reproduces the known results obtained by Kaplan, Savage and Wise. Contrary to the standard formulation utilizing the heavy-baryon expansion, the nonperturbatively resummed one-pion exchange potential can be renormalized by absorbing all ultraviolet divergences into the leading S-wave contact interactions. We explain in detail the differences to the heavy-baryon formulation and present numerical results for two-nucleon phase shifts at leading order in the low-momentum expansion.

## Key claim

The authors propose a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of chiral effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon scattering that avoids the non-renormalizability problem of the standard heavy-baryon Weinberg approach, provided one uses a relativistic three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation rather than the nonrelativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Method

The authors start from the manifestly Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian without employing the heavy-baryon expansion, then use time-ordered perturbation theory and decompose the nucleon propagator to isolate the leading-order piece. They solve a three-dimensional integral equation (Eq. 13) for the NN amplitude that has a milder ultraviolet behavior than the standard Lippmann-Schwinger equation, using a momentum-space cutoff regularization and taking the limit \(\Lambda \to \infty\) after fitting the leading-order contact interactions to Nijmegen partial wave analysis data.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass \(M_\pi = 138\) MeV, pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, axial-vector coupling \(g_A = 1.267\) used for the one-pion exchange potential
- Leading-order contact interactions \(C_S\), \(C_T\) and an additional counter term \(C_{3P0}\) fitted to Nijmegen \(^1S_0\), \(^3S_1\) and \(^3P_0\) phase shifts at laboratory energies \(E_{\rm lab} < 25\) MeV
- In the \(^3P_0\) partial wave, a limit-cycle-like behavior of the phase shift at \(E_{\rm lab} = 100\) MeV is observed as the cutoff \(\Lambda\) is varied, requiring an additional counter term of the form \(C_{3P0} \, p \, p' / \Lambda^2\)
- For the \(^1S_0\) effective range expansion at LO Weinberg: scattering length \(a\) fitted, effective range \(r = 1.50\) fm, shape parameters \(v_2 = -1.9\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 8.6(8)\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -37(10)\) fm\(^7\) (Nijmegen PWA: \(a = -23.7\) fm, \(r = 2.67\) fm, \(v_2 = -0.5\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 4.0\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -20\) fm\(^7\))
- For the \(^3S_1\) effective range expansion at LO Weinberg: scattering length \(a\) fitted, effective range \(r = 1.60\) fm, shape parameters \(v_2 = -0.05\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 0.8(1)\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -4(1)\) fm\(^7\) (Nijmegen PWA: \(a = 5.42\) fm, \(r = 1.75\) fm, \(v_2 = 0.04\) fm\(^3\), \(v_3 = 0.67\) fm\(^5\), \(v_4 = -4.0\) fm\(^7\))

## Context

This work extends the original Weinberg approach (Weinberg, 1990) to nucleon-nucleon scattering in chiral effective field theory, which suffered from non-renormalizability when iterating the one-pion exchange potential in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The authors show that using a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation resolves the inconsistency that required an infinite number of counter terms in spin-triplet partial waves, as previously discussed by Savage (1998). The new approach reproduces the results of the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) framework (Kaplan, Savage, Wise, 1998) for perturbative pions, while for non-perturbative pions it provides cutoff-independent phase shifts at leading order that agree reasonably with Nijmegen partial wave analysis (Stoks, 1993) and the Virginia Tech SAID database, enabling a consistent renormalization program for chiral nuclear forces.
