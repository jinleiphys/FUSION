# Initial-state splitting kernels in cold nuclear matter

> We derive medium-induced splitting kernels for energetic partons that undergo interactions in dense QCD matter before a hard-scattering event at large momentum transfer $Q^2$. Working in the framework of the effective theory ${\rm SCET}_{\rm G}\,$, we compute the splitting kernels beyond the soft gluon approximation. We present numerical studies that compare our new results with previous findings. We expect the full medium-induced splitting kernels to be most relevant for the extension of initial-state cold nuclear matter energy loss phenomenology in both p+A and A+A collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives medium-induced splitting kernels for energetic partons undergoing interactions in dense QCD matter *before* a hard-scattering event, going beyond the traditional soft-gluon approximation (small-\(x\) limit). The authors find that the initial-state in-medium splitting kernels are not symmetric under \(x \to 1-x\) at the differential level, unlike their vacuum and final-state counterparts, and that the off-diagonal splitting processes (\(q\to gq\) and \(g\to q\bar q\)) vanish in the small-\(x\) limit.

## Method

The authors work within the effective theory SCET\(_{\rm G}\) (Soft Collinear Effective Theory with Glauber gluons) to compute the splitting kernels for \(q\to qg\), \(q\to gq\), \(g\to gg\), and \(g\to q\bar q\) to first order in opacity. They evaluate single- and double-Born Feynman diagrams in the hybrid gauge (light-cone gauge for collinear gluons, covariant gauge for Glauber gluons) and integrate over the transverse momenta \(\mathbf{k}_\perp\) and \(\mathbf{q}_\perp\) and the medium path length \(\Delta z\). The results are presented as differential number densities \(\mathrm{d}N/(\mathrm{d}x\,\mathrm{d}^2\mathbf{k}_\perp)\), where \(x\) is the light-cone momentum fraction carried by the emitted parton.

## Key numbers

- Parent parton energy \(E_0 = 100\) GeV used for numerical comparisons of intensity spectra \(x(\mathrm{d}N/\mathrm{d}x)\) for three values of momentum transfer \(\mu = 0.1,\ 0.3,\ 0.5\) GeV (Fig. 1).
- Effective mass for initial-state partons in the medium taken as \(m_{\text{eff}} = m_N = 0.94\) GeV, following Vitev (2007).
- Medium parameters: strong coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), gluon mean free path \(\lambda_g = 1\) fm, medium size \(L = 5\) fm.
- For phase-space cut studies (Fig. 2): \(k_{\text{max}} = E_0\sqrt{x(1-x)}\) and two choices \(q_{\text{max}} = \sqrt{2E_0\mu}\) and \(q_{\text{max}} = \sqrt{E_0\mu/2}\) at \(E_0 = 100\) GeV, \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV.
- Energy dependence shown for \(E_0 = 0.1\) TeV, 1 TeV, and 10 TeV at \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV (Fig. 3).

## Context

This work extends the traditional cold nuclear matter (CNM) energy loss approach of Vitev (2007), which was limited to the soft-gluon approximation, by providing finite-\(x\) splitting kernels derived within SCET\(_{\rm G}\). It parallels the final-state in-medium splitting kernels derived by Ovanesyan, Ringer, and Vitev (2011, 2013) and enables future DGLAP evolution treatments for parton distribution functions in nuclear matter, analogous to the in-medium DGLAP evolution for fragmentation functions applied by Kang et al. (2014, 2015) in A+A collisions. The results are most relevant for extending CNM energy loss phenomenology in both p+A and A+A collisions, particularly for understanding jet suppression observed at LHC and RHIC.
