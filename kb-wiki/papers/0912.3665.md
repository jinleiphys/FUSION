# Nonperturbative Renormalisation Group: Applications to the few and many-body systems

> We consider the applications of functional renormalisation group to few and many-body systems. As an application to the few-body dynamics we study the ratio between the fermion-fermion scattering length and the dimer-dimer scattering length for systems of few nonrelativistic fermions. We find a strong dependence on the cutoff function used in the renormalisation flow for a two-body truncation of the action. Adding a simple three-body term substantially reduces this dependence. In the context of many-body physics we study the dynamics of both symmetric and asymmetric many-fermion systems using the same functional renormalisation technique. It is demonstrated that functional renormalisation group gives sensible and reliable results and provides a solid theoretical ground for the future studies. Open questions as well as lines of further developments are discussed.

## Key claim

The functional renormalisation group (ERG) approach yields sensible results for few- and many-fermion systems, but the ratio of dimer-dimer to fermion-fermion scattering length shows a strong dependence on the cutoff function when only two-body interactions are included; adding a simple three-body term substantially reduces this cutoff dependence, though not completely eliminating it.

## Method

The author applied the functional renormalisation group to nonrelativistic fermions by parametrising the scale-dependent effective action with a finite set of coefficients, turning the evolution into coupled ordinary differential equations solved numerically. For the few-body case, a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation introduced an auxiliary boson field, and the evolution of the boson self-energy and boson-boson scattering amplitude was computed using a sharp cutoff function, first with only two-body interactions and then with an additional three-body term. For the many-body case, the effective action was expanded about its minimum to study pairing in symmetric and asymmetric fermion systems, with the evolution of the effective potential computed including both fermionic and bosonic loop contributions.

## Key numbers

- Mean-field result for the dimer-dimer scattering length ratio: \(a_B = 2a\) (far from the exact value \(a_B = 0.6a\) from Shl (year not given))
- With two-body truncation and boson scale parameter \(c_B = 1\): \(a_B/a = 1.13\); with \(c_B = \sqrt{2}\): \(a_B/a = 0.75\)
- With three-body term included and \(c_B = 1\): \(a_B/a = 0.74\); with \(c_B = \sqrt{2}\): \(a_B/a = 0.69\)
- Universal coefficients for the unitary regime (infinite scattering length): \(\xi = 0.62\), \(\eta = 0.96\), \(\epsilon = 1.11\) for ground state energy, chemical potential, and pairing gap respectively
- Critical scale where the system enters the broken phase: \(k_{crit} \simeq 1.2 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\)
- Starting scale independence achieved for \(K > 5 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\) in the many-body case

## Context

This work extends the ERG approach previously applied to nonrelativistic two-body systems by Bir (year not given) and many-body systems by Kri (year not given), and specifically follows the framework of the Heidelberg group (Die2, year not given). The few-body results are compared to the exact solution of the Schrodinger equation by Shl (year not given) giving \(a_B = 0.6a\), and to the mean-field result by Ha (year not given). The many-body results for universal coefficients in the unitary regime are compared to experimental measurements by Ge (year not given), Bo (year not given), Ba (year not given), Pa (year not given), and Ki (year not given), as well as to theoretical calculations by Ca (year not given), Lee (year not given), As (year not given), Bu2 (year not given), Ch (year not given), So (year not given), Se (year not given), and Di3 (year not given). The work enables future studies of the BCS-BEC crossover and asymmetric fermion systems, with open questions including the role of screening effects (Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov correction) and the need for more complete truncations of the effective action.
