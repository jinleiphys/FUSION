# QCD and the Chiral Critical Point

> As an extension of $QCD$, consider a theory with ``$2+1$'' flavors, where the current quark masses are held in a fixed ratio as the overall scale of the quark masses is varied. At nonzero temperature and baryon density it is expected that in the chiral limit the chiral phase transition is of first order. Increasing the quark mass from zero, the chiral transition becomes more weakly first order, and can end in a chiral critical point. We show that the only massless field at the chiral critical point is a sigma meson, with the universality class that of the Ising model. Present day lattice simulations indicate that $QCD$ is (relatively) near to the chiral critical point.

## Key claim

The authors show that for QCD with "2+1" flavors (up, down, and strange quarks with masses held in fixed ratio), the chiral critical point where the first-order chiral phase transition ends has only one massless field, a sigma meson, placing it in the Ising model universality class; however, mean-field theory predicts the critical quark masses are about two orders of magnitude smaller than lattice simulations indicate, suggesting the first-order transition arises from a Coleman-Weinberg mechanism rather than instanton effects.

## Method

The authors construct a linear sigma model for three quark flavors with a complex 3x3 matrix field Phi, fit its parameters to zero-temperature meson masses (pion, eta, eta-prime) and the pion decay constant, then extend the model to nonzero temperature by varying the mass parameter mu^2 in mean-field theory. They analyze the potential for the chiral condensate in the SU(3) symmetric case and then with explicit symmetry breaking to determine the conditions for a critical point where the sigma meson becomes massless.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass fitted to m_pi = 137 MeV, eta mass to m_eta = 547 MeV, eta-prime mass to m_eta' = 958 MeV, pion decay constant to f_pi = 93 MeV
- Sigma_0 = 127 MeV, Sigma_8 = 13 MeV, M^2 = +(642 MeV)^2, c = 1920 MeV, g_2 = 30 from the zero-temperature fit
- Predicted scalar masses: m_sigma_pi = 1177 MeV, m_sigma_K = 1322 MeV
- Ratio of strange to up quark masses from the fit: m_strange/m_up = 32
- At the chiral critical point in mean-field theory: h_0^crit = (62 MeV)^3, h_8^crit = (60.4 MeV)^3, mu^2_crit = (183 MeV)^2, Sigma_0^crit = 14.5 MeV, Sigma_8^crit = 2.7 MeV
- Ratio of critical to actual quark masses from mean-field theory: m_up^crit/m_up = 0.01, while lattice simulations give approximately 0.5

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Pisarski and Wilczek (1984) on the chiral phase transition for massless quarks, and the lattice results of the Columbia group (Brown et al., 1990) showing a gap between chiral and deconfining first-order transition lines. The authors show that the chiral critical point for 2+1 flavors belongs to the Ising universality class, contradicting earlier expectations of O(4) critical behavior, and they connect the zero-temperature scalar meson spectrum to the finite-temperature phase diagram. The results enable predictions for disoriented chiral condensates in heavy-ion collisions and motivate further lattice studies to determine how close QCD is to the chiral critical point.
