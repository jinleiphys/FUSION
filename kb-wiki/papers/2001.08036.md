# Relativistic hypernuclear compact stars with calibrated equations of state

> Within the covariant density functional theory of hypernuclear matter we build a series of equations of state for hypernuclear compact stars, by calibrating the coupling constants of the $Ξ$-hyperon to the experimental binding energy of the single-$Ξ$ hypernuclei $^{15}_{Ξ^-}$C and $^{12}_{Ξ^-}$Be. Coupling constants of the $Λ$-hyperon to nucleons have been calibrated on a vast collection of experimental data on single $Λ$-hypernuclei and we employ those values. Uncertainties on the couplings of the $Σ$-hyperon to nuclear matter, due to lack of experimental data, are accounted for by allowing for a wide variation of the well depth of $Σ$ at rest in symmetric saturated nuclear matter. To account for uncertainties in the nucleonic sector at densities much larger than the saturation density, a rich collection of parametrizations is employed, some of them in agreement with existing constraints from nuclear physics and astrophysics. Neutron star properties are investigated with all these calibrated equations of state. The effects of the presence of hyperons on the radius, on the tidal deformability, on the moment of inertia, and on the nucleonic direct Urca process are discussed. The sensitivity of the hyperonic direct Urca processes to uncertainties in the nucleonic and hyperonic sectors is also addressed. It is shown that the relative variations of the radius, tidal deformability and moment of inertia from the values that characterize purely nucleonic stars are linearly correlated with the strangeness fraction. The maximum radius deviation, obtained for most massive neutron stars, is $\approx 10\%$. The reduction of the maximum mass, triggered by nucleation of strangeness, is estimated at $\approx 15 - 20\%$, out of which 5\% comes from insufficient information on the $Σ$-hyperon interactions.

## Key claim

The paper calibrates hyperon-nucleon coupling constants for $\Xi$-hyperons using experimental binding energies of single-$\Xi$ hypernuclei, and shows that the maximum radius deviation of hypernuclear stars from purely nucleonic stars is approximately 10%, while the reduction of maximum mass due to strangeness nucleation is estimated at 15-20%, with 5% of that reduction coming from insufficient information on $\Sigma$-hyperon interactions.

## Method

The authors employ covariant density functional theory within a relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach, using 11 different nucleonic equation of state parametrizations (both constant-coupling NL models and density-dependent DD models). They calibrate the $\Xi$-meson coupling constants by solving Dirac equations for nucleons and hyperons to match the experimental binding energies of $^{15}_{\Xi^-}$C (the Kiso event) and $^{12}_{\Xi^-}$Be, while $\Lambda$ couplings are taken from prior calibrations on single-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei. For $\Sigma$-hyperons, lacking experimental data, the well depth $U_{\Sigma}^{(N)}$ is varied across a wide range from -10 to 50 MeV to explore uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Maximum radius deviation of hypernuclear stars from purely nucleonic stars: approximately 10%, obtained for most massive neutron stars
- Reduction of maximum mass triggered by strangeness nucleation: approximately 15-20%, out of which 5% comes from insufficient information on $\Sigma$-hyperon interactions
- For the $^{15}_{\Xi 1s}$C scenario (binding energy 4.4 MeV), the $\Xi$ well depth $U_{\Xi}^{(N)}$ ranges from -12.5 to -8.7 MeV across models, with coupling fraction $x_{s\Xi} = g_{\sigma\Xi}/g_{\sigma N}$ between 0.295 and 0.306
- For the $^{15}_{\Xi 1p}$C scenario (binding energy 1.1 MeV), $U_{\Xi}^{(N)}$ ranges from -18.8 to -14.6 MeV, with $x_{s\Xi}$ between 0.31 and 0.32
- The binding energy of $\Xi$ in $^{12}_{\Xi 1s}$Be ranges from 2.35 to 2.84 MeV for the 1s scenario and from 5.34 to 7.31 MeV for the 1p scenario
- The slope of symmetry energy at saturation $L$ for models with moderate values ranges 44-55 MeV, while models with high values have $L$ in the range 108-119 MeV
- The incompressibility parameter $K_{\infty}$ spans values between 238 MeV (FSU, FSU2R, FSU2H) and 281.2 MeV (TM1, TM1$\omega\rho$, TM1-2, TM1-2$\omega\rho$)

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Fortin et al. (2017, 2018) and Providencia et al. (2019), where $\Lambda$-nucleon and $\Lambda-\Lambda$ interaction potentials were constrained on experimental hypernuclei data, by now calibrating $\Xi$-nucleon potentials on single-$\Xi$ hypernuclei data from the KEK-E373 experiment (Kiso event). The study addresses the "hyperon puzzle", whether two-solar-mass neutron stars can accommodate hyperons, previously discussed by Chatterjee and Vidaña (2015), and enables more realistic modeling of neutron star properties including radius, tidal deformability, moment of inertia, and direct Urca processes for future comparison with observations from NICER, LIGO/Virgo, and the Square Kilometre Array.
