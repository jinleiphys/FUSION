# Explicit Delta(1232) Degrees of Freedom in Compton Scattering off the Deuteron

> We examine elastic Compton scattering off the deuteron for photon energies between 50 MeV and 100 MeV in the framework of chiral effective field theories to next-to-leading order. We compare one theoretical scheme with only pions and nucleons as explicit degrees of freedom to another in which the Delta(1232) resonance is treated as an explicit degree of freedom. Whereas pion degrees of freedom suffice to describe the experimental data measured at about 70 MeV, the explicit Delta(1232) gives important contributions that help to reproduce the angular dependence at higher energies. The static isoscalar dipole polarizabilities alpha_E^s and beta_M^s are fitted to the available data, giving results for the neutron polarizabilities alpha_E^n=(14.2+-2.0(stat)+-1.9(syst))*10^(-4)fm^3, beta_M^n=(1.8+-2.2(stat)+-0.3(syst))*10^(-4)fm^3. These values are in good agreement with previous experimental analyses. Comparing them to the well-known proton values we conclude that there is currently no evidence for significant differences between the proton and neutron electromagnetic dipole polarizabilities.

## Key claim
The paper shows that while a chiral effective field theory with only pions and nucleons suffices to describe deuteron Compton scattering data at around 70 MeV, including the Delta(1232) resonance as an explicit degree of freedom provides important contributions that improve agreement with the angular dependence at higher energies (up to 100 MeV). The extracted neutron polarizabilities are consistent with small isovector components, indicating no significant difference between proton and neutron electromagnetic dipole polarizabilities.

## Method
The authors calculated elastic Compton scattering off the deuteron in the Small Scale Expansion (SSE) framework of chiral effective field theory to next-to-leading order (O(epsilon^3)), treating pions, nucleons, and the Delta(1232) resonance as explicit degrees of freedom. They used a hybrid approach where the interaction kernel was power-counted according to SSE rules while deuteron wave functions were obtained from realistic NN potentials (Nijm93, CD-Bonn, AV18, and NNLO chiral potential). The static isoscalar dipole polarizabilities were fitted to available experimental data on elastic gamma-d scattering at photon energies between 50 and 100 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Neutron electric dipole polarizability: alpha_E^n = (14.2 +/- 2.0 (stat) +/- 1.9 (syst)) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- Neutron magnetic dipole polarizability: beta_M^n = (1.8 +/- 2.2 (stat) +/- 0.3 (syst)) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- Photon energy range studied: 50 MeV to 100 MeV
- The Delta(1232)-nucleon mass splitting used: Delta_0 = 271.1 MeV
- Deuteron binding energy: B approximately 2.225 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of deuteron Compton scattering by Phillips et al. (2000) and McGrath et al. (2003) that used only pions and nucleons in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory, by adding explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom. It contrasts with earlier analyses by Lvov et al. (1997) and Miller et al. (1998) that gave contradictory results for neutron polarizabilities. The approach enables more reliable extraction of neutron polarizabilities from elastic gamma-d scattering data and provides a framework for future experiments at TUNL/HIgammaS and MAXlab.
