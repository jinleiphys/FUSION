# Machine learning Hadron Spectral Functions in Lattice QCD

> Hadron spectral functions carry all the information of hadrons and are encoded in the Euclidean two-point correlation functions. The extraction of hadron spectral functions from the correlator is a typical ill-posed inverse problem and infinite number of solutions to this problem exists. We propose a novel neural network (sVAE) based on the Variation Auto-Encoder (VAE) and Bayesian theorem. Inspired by the maximum entropy method (MEM) we construct the loss function of the neural work such that it includes a Shannon-Jaynes entropy term and a likelihood term. The sVAE is then trained to provide the most probable spectral functions. For the training samples of spectral function we used general spectral functions produced from the Gaussian Mixture Model. After the training is done we performed the mock data tests with input spectral functions consisting 1) only a free continuum, 2) only a resonance peak, 3) a resonance peak plus a free continuum and 4) a NRQCD motivated spectral function. From the mock data test we find that the sVAE in most cases is comparable to the maximum entropy method in the quality of reconstructing spectral functions and even outperforms the MEM in the case where the spectral function has sharp peaks with insufficient number of data points in the correlator. By applying to temporal correlation functions of charmonium in the pseudoscalar channel obtained in the quenched lattice QCD at 0.75 $T_c$ on $128^3\times96$ lattices and $1.5$ $T_c$ on $128^3\times48$ lattices, we find that the resonance peak of $η_c$ extracted from both the sVAE and MEM has a substantial dependence on the number of points in the temporal direction ($N_τ$) adopted in the lattice simulation and $N_τ$ larger than 48 is needed to resolve the fate of $η_c$ at 1.5 $T_c$.

## Key claim

The proposed sVAE neural network, which incorporates a Shannon-Jaynes entropy term and a likelihood term in its loss function inspired by the maximum entropy method (MEM), reconstructs hadron spectral functions from Euclidean correlators with quality comparable to MEM in most cases, and outperforms MEM specifically when the spectral function has sharp peaks and the correlator has an insufficient number of data points.

## Method

The authors constructed a variational auto-encoder (sVAE) with two encoders and one decoder: Encoder 1 maps ground-truth spectral function and correlator pairs into a latent space, while Encoder 2 maps stochastic correlators into the same latent space. The loss function combines a Shannon-Jaynes entropy term (encoding prior information about the spectral function) and a likelihood term (encoding the fit to correlator data), plus a Kullback-Leibler divergence term between the two encoder distributions. The network was trained on general spectral functions generated from a Gaussian Mixture Model, then applied to mock data tests and to quenched lattice QCD correlator data for charmonium in the pseudoscalar channel.

## Key numbers

* At 0.75 T_c on 128^3 x 96 lattices, the MEM reconstructed peak location for eta_c lies at omega approximately 3.34 GeV; the sVAE peak location is about 5% larger than that from MEM.
* At 1.5 T_c on 128^3 x 48 lattices, the MEM results using default models DM2 and DM3 show a peak location shifted to approximately 4.26 GeV, while sVAE shows no remarkable peak structure.
* The training spectral functions used N_omega = 10000 points in frequency space, with Gaussian noise added to correlators at a typical level of 1.5% at tau = N_tau/2.
* Mock data tests used N_tau = 96 and N_tau = 48; the reconstruction quality degraded for peak widths Gamma = 0.03 and improved for Gamma >= 0.06.

## Context

This work extends the application of machine learning to the ill-posed inverse problem of extracting hadron spectral functions from Euclidean correlators, a problem previously addressed by the maximum entropy method (MEM) as developed by Asakawa (2003) and others, as well as by stochastic approaches (Ding 2017) and Tikhonov regularization. The sVAE approach is compared directly to MEM on both mock data and quenched lattice QCD data from Ding (2012), and the results indicate that N_tau larger than 48 is needed to resolve the fate of eta_c at 1.5 T_c, highlighting a limitation of current lattice data that both methods share.
