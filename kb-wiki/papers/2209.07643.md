# Equation of State and Energy Loss of Hot and Dense Quark-Gluon matter from Holographic Black Holes

> By using gravity/gauge correspondence, we construct a holographic model, constrained to mimic the lattice QCD equation of state at zero density, to investigate the temperature and baryon chemical potential dependence of the equation of state. We also obtained the energy loss of light and heavy partons within the hot and dense plasma represented by the heavy quark drag force, Langevin diffusion coefficients and jet quenching parameter at the critical point and across the first-order transition line predicted by the model.

## Key claim

The holographic Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton (EMD) model, constrained to reproduce lattice QCD results at zero baryon chemical potential, predicts a critical end point (CEP) at T = 89 MeV and μB = 724 MeV, and the transport coefficients (drag force, Langevin diffusion coefficients, jet quenching parameter) remain finite at the CEP as expected for holographic/large-Nc approaches (type-B dynamical universality class), rather than diverging.

## Method

The authors constructed a five-dimensional holographic EMD model with a real scalar dilaton field and a Maxwell field, where the scalar potential V(ϕ) and gauge coupling f(ϕ) were dynamically fixed by matching the holographic equation of state and second-order baryon susceptibility to lattice QCD results with 2+1 flavours and physical quark masses at μB = 0 from Borsanyi (2013) and Bellwied (2015). The equations of motion were numerically solved for charged isotropic black hole backgrounds parameterized by the dilaton field at the horizon and the electric field at the horizon, with thermodynamic quantities and transport coefficients (heavy quark drag force via trailing string approach, Langevin diffusion coefficients, and jet quenching parameter) computed via the holographic dictionary.

## Key numbers

- Critical end point located at T = 89 MeV and μB = 724 MeV in the model.
- Holographic pressure compared with lattice QCD data up to μB/T = 3.5 from Borsanyi (2021).
- Heavy quark drag force computed for quark velocities v = 0.5 and v = 0.99 as a function of temperature for several μB values.
- Parallel Langevin diffusion coefficient shown at v = 0.99 as a function of temperature for several μB values.
- Normalized jet quenching parameter q̂/T³ shown as a function of temperature for several μB values, displaying a peak around the crossover that becomes sharper and increases in magnitude with increasing baryon chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the holographic EMD approach of Critelli (2017) and Grefa (2021, 2022) to compute both equilibrium and dynamical properties of the quark-gluon plasma at finite baryon density, complementing lattice QCD which is limited by the sign problem at finite density. The model predicts a critical end point and first-order transition line, and the transport coefficients (drag force, Langevin coefficients, jet quenching parameter) show sensitivity to the crossover-to-first-order transition, with inflection points that can define pseudo-transition temperatures; this enables phenomenological predictions for heavy ion collision experiments searching for the QCD critical point.
