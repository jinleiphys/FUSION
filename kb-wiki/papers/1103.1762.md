# Neutron skin of 208Pb, nuclear symmetry energy, and the parity radius experiment

> A precise determination of the neutron skin thickness of a heavy nucleus sets a basic constraint on the nuclear symmetry energy (the neutron skin thickness is the difference of the neutron and proton rms radii of the nucleus). The parity radius experiment (PREX) may achieve it by electroweak parity-violating electron scattering (PVES) on 208Pb. We investigate PVES in nuclear mean field approach to allow the accurate extraction of the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb from the parity-violating asymmetry probed in the experiment. We demonstrate a high linear correlation between the parity-violating asymmetry and the neutron skin thickness in successful mean field forces as the best means to constrain the neutron skin of 208Pb from PREX, without assumptions on the neutron density shape. Continuation of the experiment with higher precision in the parity-violating asymmetry is motivated since the present method can support it to constrain the density slope of the nuclear symmetry energy to new accuracy.

## Key claim

The parity-violating asymmetry measured by PREX exhibits a high linear correlation with the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb across a wide range of successful mean field models, enabling extraction of the neutron skin without assuming a specific shape for the neutron density; however, a 3% measurement of the asymmetry provides only modest constraints on the density slope of the nuclear symmetry energy, and a 1% measurement would be needed to achieve new accuracy.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations of the parity-violating asymmetry for 208Pb at the PREX kinematics (1.06 GeV beam energy, 5° scattering angle, momentum transfer ~0.47 fm⁻¹) using 47 nuclear mean field interactions spanning nonrelativistic and relativistic models. They computed the asymmetry from self-consistently calculated pointlike proton and neutron densities folded with electromagnetic and weak form factors, then established linear regressions between the asymmetry and both the neutron radius and neutron skin thickness.

## Key numbers

- The charge radius of 208Pb is accurately known as r_ch = 5.5010(9) fm
- The predicted neutron radius r_n of 208Pb varies from 5.55 to 5.8 fm across the 47 mean field models
- The parity-violating asymmetry A_pv at PREX kinematics ranges from about 0.67 to 0.75 ppm across the models
- The linear fit between A_pv and r_n gives 10^7 A_pv = 25.83 - 3.31 r_n, with correlation coefficient r = 0.974
- The linear fit between A_pv and the neutron skin Δr_np gives 10^7 A_pv = 7.88 - 3.75 Δr_np, with correlation coefficient r = 0.995
- A test value A_pv = 0.715 ppm with 3% accuracy yields r_n = 5.644 ± 0.065 fm and Δr_np = 0.195 ± 0.057 fm
- The linear fit between Δr_np and the symmetry energy slope L gives Δr_np = 0.101 + 0.00147 L
- A 3% measurement of A_pv yields L = 64 ± 39 MeV; a 1% measurement could achieve δΔr_np ~ 0.02 fm and δL ~ 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the seminal study of PREX (prex1, prex2) by providing a method to extract the neutron skin from the parity-violating asymmetry without assuming a specific functional form for the neutron density, unlike earlier analyses that used a two-parameter Fermi function. The correlation between neutron skin and symmetry energy slope builds on prior work by Brown (2000), Furnstahl (2002), and Steiner (2005) establishing the connection between neutron skin thickness and the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy. The results enable a model-independent determination of the neutron skin of 208Pb from electroweak scattering, which can then constrain the symmetry energy slope L that is relevant for neutron-rich nuclei, heavy-ion collisions, and neutron star physics.
