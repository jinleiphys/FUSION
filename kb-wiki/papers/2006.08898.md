# Generating Giant Vortex in a Fermi Superfluid via Spin-Orbital-Angular-Momentum Coupling

> Spin-orbital-angular-momentum (SOAM) coupling has been realized in recent experiments of Bose-Einstein condensates [Chen et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 121, 113204 (2018) and Zhang et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 122, 110402 (2019)], where the orbital angular momentum imprinted upon bosons leads to quantized vortices. For fermions, such an exotic synthetic gauge field can provide fertile ground for fascinating pairing schemes and rich superfluid phases, which are yet to be explored. Here we demonstrate how SOAM coupling stabilizes vortices in Fermi superfluids through a unique mechanism that can be viewed as the angular analog to that of the spin-orbit-coupling-induced Fulde-Ferrell state under a Fermi surface deformation. Remarkably, the vortex size is comparable with the beam waist of Raman lasers generating the SOAM coupling, which is typically much larger than previously observed vortices in Fermi superfluids. With tunable size and core structure, these giant vortex states provide unprecedented experimental access to topological defects in Fermi superfluids.

## Key claim

Spin-orbital-angular-momentum (SOAM) coupling can stabilize vortices in a Fermi superfluid, but only when a moderate two-photon detuning is introduced; without detuning, SOAM coupling alone does not induce vortices because fermions in a Cooper pair acquire opposite orbital angular momenta that cancel.

## Method

The authors model a two-component Fermi gas confined in a two-dimensional plane with SOAM coupling generated by copropagating Raman beams carrying different orbital angular momenta. They solve the Bogoliubov–de Gennes (BdG) equations self-consistently for the superfluid order parameter and density profiles, comparing free energies of states with different vorticities to determine the ground state phase diagram in the Raman-coupling–detuning plane.

## Key numbers

- Vortex states with vorticity \(\kappa = -1\) (or \(\kappa = +1\) for negative detuning) are stabilized for detuning \(\delta / E_F = 0.4\) and above, with parameters \(l=3\), \(\Omega_0/E_F=0.2\), \(k_F w = 15\), and \(k_F R = 15\).
- For a fixed \(\Omega_0/E_F = 1.5\), the ground state transitions from a usual superfluid to a fully-gapped vortex state (V1) at a critical detuning, and then to a gapless vortex state (V2) at larger detuning.
- With \(\Omega_0 = 0.5 E_F\), no vortex state appears for any finite detuning, in contrast to the SOC-induced Fulde-Ferrell state.
- For a quasi-two-dimensional Fermi gas of \(^6\)Li atoms with \(E_F \sim 2\pi \hbar \times 3.4\) kHz and \(w \sim 7.5\ \mu\)m, the stable vortex parameter window is \(\Omega_0 \sim 2\pi \hbar \times (3.4, 8.5)\) kHz and \(\delta \sim 2\pi \hbar \times (1.4, 4.1)\) kHz.

## Context

This work extends the experimental realization of SOAM coupling in Bose-Einstein condensates (Chen et al., 2018; Zhang et al., 2019) to fermionic systems, where it was previously unexplored. The vortex formation mechanism is the angular analog of the spin-orbit-coupling-induced Fulde-Ferrell state (Wu et al., 2013; chuanwei-13; Wei-13), but with quantized angular momentum producing topological defects. The giant vortex cores, tunable via the laser waist, enable experimental access to vortex-core structure and Caroli–de Gennes–Matricon (CdGM) states that are much larger than those in previously studied atomic Fermi superfluids (type2-vortex; Ketterle-vortex).
