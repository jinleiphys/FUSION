# The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei

> The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of the deuteron suggested by Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (Phys. Rev. 124 (1961) 246) is formulated from the first principles of QCD. The deuteron appears as a neutron-proton collective excitation, i.e. a Cooper np-pair, induced by a phenomenological local four-nucleon interaction in the nuclear phase of QCD. The model describes the deuteron coupled to itself, nucleons and other particles through one-nucleon loop exchanges providing a minimal transfer of nucleon flavours from initial to final nuclear states and accounting for contributions of nucleon-loop anomalies which are completely determined by one-nucleon loop diagrams. The dominance of contributions of nucleon-loop anomalies to effective Lagrangians of low-energy nuclear interactions is justified in the large N_C expansion, where N_C is the number of quark colours.

## Key claim

The deuteron can be described as a neutron-proton Cooper pair induced by a phenomenological local four-nucleon interaction, and this Nambu-Jona-Lasinio approach is fully motivated by low-energy QCD when the dominance of nucleon-loop anomalies is justified in the large \(N_C\) expansion, where \(N_C\) is the number of quark colours.

## Method

The authors formulate the nuclear Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NNJL) model by starting from QCD and integrating out high-energy quark-gluon fluctuations, then heavy hadron degrees of freedom, to arrive at a nuclear phase with local multi-nucleon interactions. They linearize a phenomenological local four-nucleon interaction to introduce an interpolating deuteron field, then integrate over nucleon fields in the one-nucleon loop approximation to derive the effective Lagrangian of the free deuteron field, with the anomalous contributions from one-nucleon loop diagrams evaluated using the Gertsein-Jackiw procedure.

## Key numbers

*   Deuteron binding energy: \(\varepsilon_{\rm D} = 2.225\,{\rm MeV}\) (experimental value cited).
*   Deuteron magnetic dipole moment: \(\mu_{\rm D} = 0.857\,{\mu}_{\rm N}\), where \({\mu}_{\rm N}\) is a nuclear magneton (experimental value cited).
*   Deuteron electric quadrupole moment: \(Q_{\rm D} = 0.286\,{\rm fm}^2\) (experimental value cited).
*   Nucleon mass: \(M_{\rm N} = 940\,{\rm MeV}\).
*   Constituent quark mass: \(M_q \sim 300\,{\rm MeV}\).
*   Spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry scale: \(\Lambda_{\chi} \simeq 1\,{\rm GeV}\).
*   Coupling constant \(g_{\rm V} = 11.319\) calculated at \(N_C = 3\).
*   The relation \(g^2_{\rm V}= 2\pi^2 Q_{\rm D}M^2_{\rm N}\) is used.
*   The binding energy expression derived: \(\varepsilon_{\rm D}= \frac{17}{48}\,\frac{g^2_{\rm V}}{\pi^2}\,\frac{J_1(M_{\rm N})}{M_{\rm N}}= \frac{17}{18}\,Q_{\rm D}\,\Lambda^3_{\rm D} \sim O(1/N_C)\).

## Context

This work extends the original Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (1961) suggestion to treat the deuteron as a Cooper np-pair by providing a derivation from QCD first principles, addressing the earlier poor relation to QCD noted in the Relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD) of Ivanov et al. (Refs. [14,15]). It also connects to the treatment of nuclei as elementary particles by Sakita and Goebal (1966) and Kim and Primakoff, and complements the dispersion relation approach of Anisovich and Sadovnikova. The paper enables a quantum field theoretic description of light nuclei as collective excitations in the nuclear phase of QCD, with effective Lagrangians determined by nucleon-loop anomalies, and naturally incorporates chiral perturbation theory.
