# Time-evolution of parity-odd cascades in homogeneous Abelian and non-Abelian media with chiral imbalance

> We study radiation by a fast particle in a medium with a finite chiral chemical potential. The medium's anomalous response encompasses the chiral magnetic effect, enabling novel chiral Cherenkov and pair production processes. The kinematics of the corresponding cascades is fundamentally different from conventional cascades. Notably, it is not dominated by the strong ordering of momenta. Employing the effective Chern-Simons extensions of QED and QCD to incorporate the chiral magnetic effect, we derive and solve the evolution equations describing the cascades in a chiral medium, presuming that the anomalous contributions are dominant.

# Time-evolution of parity-odd cascades in homogeneous Abelian and non-Abelian media with chiral imbalance

## Key claim

The paper claims that cascades initiated by a fast particle in a medium with a finite chiral chemical potential develop 100% chirality and are not dominated by strong ordering of momenta, unlike conventional cascades; however, this result holds only under the assumption that anomalous contributions from the chiral magnetic effect dominate over conventional processes, which the authors explicitly neglect to obtain a clear physical picture.

## Method

The authors employ effective Chern-Simons extensions of QED and QCD (termed chiQED and chiQCD) to incorporate the chiral magnetic effect, deriving inclusive single-particle rates for chiral Cherenkov radiation (e to e gamma) and pair production (gamma to e ebar) in chiQED, and analogous processes in chiQCD including gluon self-interactions (g to g g). They prove factorization of multiparticle production rates into convolutions of single-particle rates using the resonance approximation for intermediate fermions, then derive and numerically solve evolution equations for the energy-fraction distributions of electrons, positrons, right-handed photons, and left-handed photons (or quarks, antiquarks, right-handed gluons, and left-handed gluons in chiQCD) as functions of time.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic time scale of the cascade is tau = 4/(alpha b_0), where b_0 is the chiral magnetic conductivity and alpha is the fine structure constant (Eq. p12).
- The dimensionless mass parameter is nu = m^2/(b_0 E_0), where E_0 is the total cascade energy (Eq. p14).
- In chiQED, the maximum energy fraction for chiral Cherenkov photon emission is x_0 = 1/(1 + m^2/(b_0 E)) (Eq. p2).
- In chiQED, the pair production kinematic limits are x_{1,2} = (1/2)(1 +/- sqrt(1 - 4m^2/(b_0 omega))) (Eq. p7).
- In chiQCD, the quark-gluon splitting function carries a color factor C_F = 4/3 (Eq. QCD1).
- The degree of cascade polarization Lambda(t) reaches near-complete right-handed polarization by time t ~ tau for cascades initiated by left-handed photons (Fig. fig:lambda_e, right panel).

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral Cherenkov radiation previously developed by Tuchin (2018) and Huang and Hong (2018) and the non-Abelian chiral Cherenkov decays computed by Hansen and Tuchin (2024). It contrasts with conventional QED and QCD cascades studied by Arnold (2002), Baier (2000), Blaizot (2015), and Mehtar-Tani (2022), which are dominated by strong ordering of transverse momenta and logarithmic evolution scales. The results enable inclusion of chiral Cherenkov decays into existing models of cascades and jets in chiral media, such as those occurring in heavy-ion collisions or neutron star environments, and provide a framework for studying how anomalous transport effects modify parton shower development.
