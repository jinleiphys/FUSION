# Far-from-equilibrium attractor in non-conformal plasmas

> We study the far-off-equilibrium dynamics of a Bjorken expanding non-conformal system within kinetic theory and hydrodynamics. We show that, in contrast to the conformal case, neither shear nor bulk viscous pressure relax quickly to a non-equilibrium attractor. In kinetic theory an early-time, far-from-equilibrium attractor exists for the scaled longitudinal pressure, driven by the rapid longitudinal expansion of the medium. Second-order dissipative hydrodynamics fails to accurately describe this attractor, but a modified anisotropic hydrodynamic formulation reproduces it and provides excellent agreement with kinetic theory.

# Far-from-equilibrium attractor in non-conformal plasmas

## Key claim

In non-conformal plasmas undergoing Bjorken expansion, neither shear nor bulk viscous pressure relax quickly to a non-equilibrium attractor, contrary to the conformal case; however, an early-time far-from-equilibrium attractor does exist for the scaled longitudinal pressure, driven by the rapid longitudinal expansion of the medium. Second-order dissipative hydrodynamics fails to accurately describe this attractor, but a modified anisotropic hydrodynamic formulation reproduces it and provides excellent agreement with kinetic theory.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for a system with Bjorken symmetry, using a parametrization of the relaxation time as tau_R = 5 C / T where C is a unitless constant and T is the temperature. They compare the exact kinetic theory solutions to second-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics and to a modified anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) approach that includes an additional non-equilibrium fugacity factor in the leading-order anisotropic distribution function.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions use mass m = 50 MeV and temperature T_0 = 500 MeV at initial time tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c for the free-streaming trajectories shown in Fig. 1a.
- For finite Knudsen number dynamics, the microscopic interaction strength is set to C = 10/4 pi (Fig. 2) and C = 3/4 pi (shorter relaxation times, Fig. 3a).
- Shear stress trajectories in Fig. 2b converge only at scaled time bar-tau >= 2, contrasting with conformal systems where convergence occurs on a much shorter time scale controlled by the initialization time tau_0.
- The scaled longitudinal pressure P_L/P from kinetic theory joins a universal attractor at bar-tau < 1, starting from P_L/P approximately 0 at bar-tau_0 approaching 0, and approaches unity at bar-tau >= 4 where it joins the first-order Navier-Stokes solution.
- Second-order non-conformal hydrodynamics is not an accurate approximation of kinetic theory before bar-tau approximately 3.
- Modified aHydro reproduces the rapid convergence onto the early-time attractor at bar-tau < 1, before merging with the late-time Navier-Stokes attractor at bar-tau >= 3.

## Context

This work extends studies of far-from-equilibrium attractors in conformal systems by Heller (2015), Jaiswal (2019), and Kurkela (2019) to non-conformal plasmas, showing that the attractor structure is fundamentally different when bulk viscosity is present. The paper demonstrates that standard second-order dissipative hydrodynamics fails for non-conformal systems at early times, but a modified version of anisotropic hydrodynamics introduced by Jaiswal (2021) and Chattopadhyay (2021) successfully reproduces the kinetic theory results. This enables more reliable modeling of the early-time dynamics in heavy-ion collisions where the quark-gluon plasma is non-conformal due to finite quark masses.
