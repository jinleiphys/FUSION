# Light Fermion Finite Mass Effects in Non-relativistic Bound States

> We present analytic expressions for the vacuum polarization effects due to a light fermion with finite mass in the binding energy and in the wave function at the origin of QED and (weak coupling) QCD non-relativistic bound states. Applications to exotic atoms, Υ(1s) and t\bar{t} production near threshold are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents fully analytic expressions for the vacuum polarization corrections due to a light fermion with finite mass to the binding energy and wave function at the origin of non-relativistic bound states, covering the full range of the mass parameter \(\xi = n m_l / (\mu \alpha)\) rather than only the limiting cases of very small or very large light-fermion mass.

## Method

The authors match the non-relativistic effective theory (NRQED or NRQCD) to the potential non-relativistic theory (pNRQED or pNRQCD) by integrating out the light fermion while keeping its mass dependence exact. This produces a light-fermion-mass-dependent correction to the static Coulomb potential. Using this potential, the energy shift for arbitrary quantum numbers \((n,l)\) is obtained by evaluating the expectation value \(\langle nl | V_{vpc} | nl \rangle\) with Coulomb wave functions, and the correction to the ground-state wave function at the origin is obtained from a representation of the Coulomb propagator combined with the vacuum-polarization potential.

## Key numbers

* For the exotic atom \(pK^{-}\), the vacuum-polarization-induced energy splitting ratio \(\frac{\delta E_{21} - \delta E_{10}}{\alpha (E_2 - E_1)}\) is 0.44593.
* For the exotic atom \(p\pi^{-}\), the same ratio is 0.18103.
* For the exotic atom \(p\mu^{-}\), the same ratio is 0.13616.
* For the ground-state wave function at the origin in \(K^{-}p\) (exotic atom), the total correction \(\frac{\delta \Psi({\bf 0})}{\alpha \Psi({\bf 0})}\) is 0.59394, with \(\xi = 0.21648\).
* For the ground-state wave function at the origin in \(\pi^{-}p\) (exotic atom), the total correction is 0.33064, with \(\xi = 0.57635\).
* For the ground-state wave function at the origin in \(\mu^{-}p\) (exotic atom), the total correction is 0.27552, with \(\xi = 0.73738\).
* For \(\bar{b}b\) (bottomonium), the vacuum polarization correction to the wave function at the origin \(\frac{\delta \Psi({\bf 0})_{\xi \neq 0} - \delta \Psi({\bf 0})_{\xi=0}}{\alpha_s \Psi({\bf 0})}\) is 0.088, with \(\xi = m_c / (C_F \alpha_s \mu) = 1.4\).
* For \(\bar{t}t\) (toponium) with bottom mass effects, the same correction is 0.011, with \(\xi = m_b / (C_F \alpha_s \mu) = 0.28\).
* For \(\bar{t}t\) with charm mass effects, the same correction is 0.0019, with \(\xi = m_c / (C_F \alpha_s \mu) = 0.10\).

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical and partial analytical results for vacuum polarization in non-relativistic bound states, such as those by DiGiacomo, Borie, and Savely, by providing fully analytic formulas valid for any light-fermion mass. It also fills the gap left by Labelle and others in the calculation of the pionium decay width, where only a numerical result for the electron-mass contribution to vacuum polarization was available. The results enable systematic inclusion of finite light-quark mass effects in heavy quarkonium systems like \(\Upsilon(1s)\) and \(t\bar{t}\) production near threshold, where prior calculations by Hoang and Manohar (for binding energy) and by the authors of the top-quark NNLO cross-section calculations assumed massless light quarks.
