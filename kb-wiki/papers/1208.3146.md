# The consequences of SU(3) colorsingletness, Polyakov Loop and Z(3) symmetry on a quark-gluon gas

> Based on quantum statistical mechanics we show that the $SU(3)$ color singlet ensemble of a quark-gluon gas exhibits a $Z(3)$ symmetry through the normaized character in fundamental representation and also becomes equivalent, within a stationary point approximation, to the ensemble given by Polyakov Loop. Also Polyakov Loop gauge potential is obtained by considering spatial gluons along with the invariant Haar measure at each space point. The probability of the normalized character in $SU(3)$ vis-a-vis Polyakov Loop is found to be maximum at a particular value exhibiting a strong color correlation. This clearly indicates a transition from a color correlated to uncorrelated phase or vise-versa. When quarks are included to the gauge fields, a metastable state appears in the temperature range $145\le T({\rm{MeV}}) \le 170$ due to the explicit $Z(3)$ symmetry breaking in the quark-gluon system. Beyond $T\ge 170$ MeV the metastable state disappears and stable domains appear. At low temperature a dynamical recombination of ionized $Z(3)$ color charges to a color singlet $Z(3)$ confined phase is evident along with a confining background that originates due to circulation of two virtual spatial gluons but with conjugate $Z(3)$ phases in a closed loop. We also discuss other possible consequences of the center domains in the color deconfined phase at high temperature.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an SU(3) color singlet ensemble of a quark-gluon gas is equivalent, within a stationary point approximation, to the ensemble described by the Polyakov Loop, and that the normalized character in the fundamental representation of SU(3) exhibits a Z(3) center symmetry. The authors qualify that the equivalence relies on a stationary point approximation and that the local Haar measure approach faces known issues from perturbative calculations where the Vandermonde contribution is canceled by integration over spatially longitudinal gluon fields.

## Method

The authors start from a quantum statistical mechanics description of a non-interacting quark-gluon gas and impose SU(3) color singletness using a projection operator with the invariant Haar measure of SU(3). They express the color singlet partition function in terms of the normalized characters of the fundamental and adjoint representations, then perform a stationary point approximation to obtain a thermodynamic potential that is identical to the Polyakov Loop model potential. The resulting potential is analyzed numerically for pure gauge sector and for the full system including quarks, examining the behavior of the Z(3) center symmetry and the formation of center domains as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

- The metastable state appears in the temperature range 145 ≤ T (MeV) ≤ 170 due to explicit Z(3) symmetry breaking when quarks are included.
- Beyond T ≥ 170 MeV the metastable state disappears and stable domains appear.
- The Vandermonde term constant κ is found as κ ∼ 0.0075 GeV³ in the literature.
- For pure gauge sector, T < 270 MeV is the color confining phase, while T ≥ 270 MeV shows spontaneously broken Z(3) phase with three minima.
- T ∼ (265-270) MeV indicates the phase transition for pure gauge, in agreement with lattice results.
- The maximum probability for |Φ| to occur is when |Φ| = 1/3, indicating a phase transition from color confining to color deconfining phase.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of color neutrality effects in a free quark-gluon gas by Auberson et al. and Redlich et al., moving from global to local color neutrality by using the Haar measure at every spatial point. The derived Polyakov Loop potential matches the phenomenological form used in the PNJL model studies by Ratti et al. and Schaefer et al., and the authors connect their findings to lattice QCD results by Boyd et al. The paper enables further study of center domain formation in quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, with implications for jet quenching and hadronization via recombination.
