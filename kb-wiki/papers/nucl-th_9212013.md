# Microscopic approach to pion-nucleus dynamics

> Elastic scattering of pions from finite nuclei is investigated utilizing a contemporary, momentum--space first--order optical potential combined with microscopic estimates of second--order corrections. The calculation of the first--order potential includes:\ \ (1)~full Fermi--averaging integration including both the delta propagation and the intrinsic nonlocalities in the $π$-$N$ amplitude, (2)~fully covariant kinematics, (3)~use of invariant amplitudes which do not contain kinematic singularities, and (4)~a finite--range off--shell pion--nucleon model which contains the nucleon pole term. The effect of the delta--nucleus interaction is included via the mean spectral--energy approximation. It is demonstrated that this produces a convergent perturbation theory in which the Pauli corrections (here treated as a second--order term) cancel remarkably against the pion true absorption terms. Parameter--free results, including the delta--nucleus shell--model potential, Pauli corrections, pion true absorption, and short--range correlations are presented. (2 figures available from authors)

# Microscopic approach to pion-nucleus dynamics

## Key claim

A parameter-free microscopic calculation of pion-nucleus elastic scattering, combining a momentum-space first-order optical potential with second-order corrections, can achieve remarkably good agreement with data across the resonance region (80 to 226 MeV) provided that the delta-nucleus interaction is included via the mean spectral energy approximation and that the Pauli corrections cancel against the pion true absorption terms. The authors hedge that firm conclusions about the short-range correlation parameter should await a more thorough, internally consistent treatment of all higher-order terms.

## Method

The authors constructed a momentum-space first-order optical potential incorporating full Fermi-averaging integration with delta propagation and intrinsic nonlocalities, fully covariant kinematics, invariant amplitudes free of kinematic singularities, and a finite-range off-shell pion-nucleon model containing the nucleon pole term. The delta-nucleus interaction was included via the mean spectral energy approximation, which collects the delta-nucleus shell-model potential together with binding corrections into the first-order potential. Second-order corrections for Pauli blocking, pion true absorption (taken from the delta-hole model spreading potential), and short-range correlations (Lorentz-Lorenz-Ericson-Ericson effect) were added using a local density approximation, with the interaction radius determined from the distorted pion waves.

## Key numbers

- The delta resonance half-width is 55 MeV, comparable to typical nuclear energies, making the choice of the energy variable W_alpha critical.
- For pi+ elastic scattering from 12C, the interaction radius R_2 ranges from 2.40 fm at 80 MeV to 2.90 fm at 230 MeV, corresponding to densities between 0.289 rho_0 and 0.151 rho_0 (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3).
- The Pauli correction coefficient lambda_0^(2) (Pauli) at 80 MeV is -0.40 to -1.46 fm^3; the spreading (true absorption) coefficient lambda_0^(2) (spread) is -0.93 to 2.02 fm^3; their sum is -1.33 to 0.56 fm^3, demonstrating large cancellation.
- At 148 MeV, the Pauli coefficient is 2.50 to -1.74 fm^3, the spreading coefficient is -3.29 to 1.18 fm^3, and their sum is -0.80 to -0.56 fm^3.
- The Lorentz-Lorenz parameter xi/3 ranges from a minimum of 0.08 (pion-nucleon monopole cutoff of 800 MeV/c, no delta-nucleon interaction) to a maximum of 0.23 (cutoff of 990 MeV/c including delta-nucleon interaction), corresponding to delta E_ms = 23 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the isobar-hole model of Hirata, Lenz, and Yasaki (1977) and the phenomenological optical model of Siciliano, Cooper, Johnson, and Leitch (1986) by providing a fully microscopic, parameter-free calculation that avoids the factorization approximations and neglect of the nucleon pole term present in those coordinate-space approaches. The results enable future work toward a definitive determination of the short-range correlation parameter xi and the delta-nucleus interaction U_Delta, and support the development of a more thorough, internally consistent treatment of higher-order terms that could be extended to higher nuclear densities and temperatures relevant to heavy-ion reactions and astrophysics.
