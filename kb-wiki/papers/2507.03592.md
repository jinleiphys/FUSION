# Nuclear $β^{-}$-decay with statistical de-excitation

> The accurate description of nuclear $β^{-}$-decay has far-reaching consequences for applications spanning nuclear reactors to the creation of heavy elements in astrophysical environments. We present the nuclear particle spectra associated with the $β$-decay of neutron-rich nuclei calculated with the well benchmarked coupled Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation and Hauser-Feshbach (QRPA+HF) model. This approach begins with the population of the daughter nucleus via semi-microscopic Gamow-Teller or First-Forbidden strength distributions (QRPA) and follows the statistical de-excitation (HF) until the initial available excitation energy is exhausted. At each stage of de-excitation the emission by neutrons and $γ$-rays is considered obeying quantum mechanical selection rules. For completeness we also provide parsed Auger and Internal Conversion (IC) electron spectra from Evaluated Nuclear Data Files (ENDF). Our results are tabulated and provided in parsable ASCII formatted tables that are suitable for inclusion in various applications.

## Key claim

The paper provides tabulated nuclear particle spectra (gamma-rays, neutrons, electrons, and neutrinos) from beta-minus decay of neutron-rich nuclei using the coupled Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation and Hauser-Feshbach (QRPA+HF) model, but the results are limited to the specific theoretical framework and do not include experimental validation for most of the listed nuclei (as indicated by the "Exp mass" column where many entries are 0).

## Method

The authors calculate beta-decay strength distributions for Gamow-Teller and First-Forbidden transitions using the semi-microscopic QRPA approach, then feed the resulting population of the daughter nucleus into a Hauser-Feshbach statistical de-excitation model that follows the cascade of neutron and gamma-ray emissions obeying quantum mechanical selection rules until the available excitation energy is exhausted. Additionally, Auger and Internal Conversion (IC) electron spectra are parsed from Evaluated Nuclear Data Files (ENDF) and included in the final tables. The results are provided as parsable ASCII formatted tables.

- For Argon-39 (Z=18, N=21, A=39): all average particle spectra quantities are zero (0.00000e+00)

## Context

This work extends the QRPA+HF approach previously benchmarked by the authors to provide comprehensive beta-decay particle spectra for neutron-rich nuclei across multiple isotopic chains from oxygen through argon. The tabulated results are intended for inclusion in applications ranging from nuclear reactor modeling to astrophysical nucleosynthesis simulations, particularly for the r-process where beta-decay of neutron-rich nuclei plays a crucial role. The paper enables future studies to incorporate these calculated spectra into simulations of nuclear reactors and astrophysical environments without needing to repeat the QRPA+HF calculations.
