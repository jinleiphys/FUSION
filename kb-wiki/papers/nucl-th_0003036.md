# Statistical analysis of light fragment production from medium energy proton-induced reactions

> The light fragment production reactions for 10 MeV to 3 GeV protons incident on O-16, Al-27, Fe, Nb-93, and Ag are analyzed by a combination of an intranuclear cascade model and a generalized evaporation model which includes light nuclei up to Mg as ejectiles. It is concluded that evaporation is the dominant process by which particles lighter than or equal to Be are produced from targets heavier than O.

## Key claim

Evaporation is the dominant process for producing particles lighter than or equal to Be from targets heavier than O, but the accuracy of the generalized evaporation model (GEVAP) predictions depends on the choice of parameter set, with the precise parameter set achieving 50% accuracy on average and the simple parameter set yielding results within a factor of two.

## Method

The author combined the Bertini intranuclear cascade model (INC) from the LAHET code with a generalized evaporation model (GEVAP) based on the Weisskopf-Ewing formalism. GEVAP includes 66 nuclides up to Mg as ejectiles, considers both ground and excited states, and uses the Gilbert-Cameron-Cook-Ignatyuk (GCCI) level density parameter instead of the simpler form used in Dostrovsky's evaporation models. The INC model provided the ensemble of hot residual nuclei (mass, charge, excitation energy, recoil) after the non-equilibrium stage, and GEVAP then calculated the de-excitation process for these nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the O target, differences between INC/GEVAP with r0=1.5 and with r0=1.2 or 1.5 are 20%, except at threshold energy
- For the Al target, differences between parameter sets are within a factor of two in the whole energy region (10 MeV to 3 GeV)
- For the Fe(p,X)7Be reaction, INC/GEVAP with r0=2.0 produces almost the same cross sections as with the precise parameter set, with differences within 20%
- For Fe and Nb targets, INC/GEVAP with the precise parameter set agrees with most experimental data within 50%
- INC/GEVAP reproduces isotopic distributions of H, He, Li, and Be from O and Ag targets with 50% accuracy
- LAHET severely underestimates 7Be production from Al below 300 MeV, and from Fe and Nb below 3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the evaporation model approach beyond the standard treatment in codes like LAHET, which only considered nucleons and helium nuclei as ejectiles in Dostrovsky's evaporation models. The study was motivated by the 1997 OECD/NEA benchmark calculations, which showed that most computer codes failed to reproduce light fragment production reactions such as Fe(p,X)7Be. The INC/GEVAP combination enables more accurate predictions of light fragment production cross sections for applications in spallation neutron sources, accelerator-based transmutation systems, and radiation safety calculations.
