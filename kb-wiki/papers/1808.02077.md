# Gluon Quasi-PDF From Lattice QCD

> We present the first attempt to access the $x$-dependence of the gluon unpolarized parton distribution function (PDF), based on lattice simulations using the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) approach. The lattice calculation is carried out with pion masses of 340 and 678 MeV on a 2+1-flavor DWF configuration with lattice spacing $a=0.111$~fm, for the gluon quasi-PDF matrix element with the nucleon momentum up to 0.93 GeV. Taking the normalization from similar matrix elements in the rest frame of the nucleon and pion, our results for these matrix elements are consistent with the Fourier transform of the global fit CT14 and PDF4LHC15 NNLO of the gluon PDF, within statistical uncertainty and the systematic one up to power corrections, perturbative ${\cal O}(α_s)$ matching and the mixing from the quark PDFs.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the gluon quasi-PDF matrix element, finding consistency with the Fourier transform of global-fit gluon PDFs (CT14 and PDF4LHC15 NNLO) within statistical uncertainty, but only up to power corrections, perturbative O(alpha_s) matching, and mixing from quark PDFs; the authors explicitly state that the Fourier transform of their current results cannot provide any meaningful constraint on the gluon PDF g(x).

## Method

The calculation uses the large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) approach on a 2+1-flavor domain-wall fermion gauge ensemble with lattice spacing a=0.111 fm, employing valence overlap fermions with pion masses of 340 and 678 MeV. The gluon quasi-PDF matrix element is computed for nucleon momenta up to 0.93 GeV using a "ratio renormalization" scheme to remove the linear divergence from the Wilson link, and results are compared with the Fourier transform of global-fit gluon PDFs.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.111 fm
- Nucleon boost momenta: P_z = 0.0, 0.46, 0.92 GeV
- Pion masses: 340 and 678 MeV
- Bare gluon momentum fraction: xg^bare = 0.55(8) from the matrix element at z=0, P_z=0
- MS-bar renormalized gluon momentum fraction at 2 GeV: xg^MSbar(2 GeV) = 0.50(7)(5), compared to phenomenological determination 0.42(2) from Dulat (2015)
- Statistics: 203 configurations x 64 timeslices x 8 sources x 2 (forward/backward) = 207,872 measurements
- Lattice volume: 24^3 x 64

## Context

This work extends the LaMET approach from quark PDFs to gluon PDFs, following the theoretical framework of Ji (2013, 2014) and the non-perturbative renormalization strategy for gluon operators introduced by Yang (2018). The results are compared with global fits from CT14 (Dulat 2015) and PDF4LHC15 NNLO (Butterworth 2015). The authors note that future calculations with nucleon momenta of 2-3 GeV are needed to meaningfully constrain the x-dependence of the gluon PDF, and that momentum smearing techniques (Bali 2016) and cluster decomposition error reduction (Liu 2017) should be helpful for achieving such large momenta.
