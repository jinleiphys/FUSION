# Electromagnetic form factors for nucleons in short-range correlations

> Recent experimental studies have led to the suggestion that short-range correlations may be a major contributor to the nuclear EMC effect. This hypothesis requires that the structure function for nucleons involved in short-range correlations should be heavily suppressed compared to that of a free nucleon. Based on calculations performed within an AdS/QCD motivated, light-front quark-diquark model, we find that this large suppression of the nucleon structure function leads to a strong suppression of the nucleon elastic form factors.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if short-range correlations (SRCs) are the primary driver of the nuclear EMC effect, the resulting suppression of the nucleon structure function in the valence region (x ~ 0.5 to 0.7) necessarily leads to a strong suppression of the nucleon elastic form factors, which would imply that the number of SRC pairs in nuclei may have been dramatically underestimated by prior quasi-elastic scattering analyses.

## Method

The authors use a light-front quark-diquark model with AdS/QCD-motivated wavefunctions, originally developed by Maji et al. (2016), to compute the electromagnetic form factors of nucleons. They first reproduce the free nucleon structure function and form factors using the model's parameters, then modify the parameters to match the suppressed structure function shape predicted by the SRC-driven EMC model (a sharp drop-off between x ~ 0.5 and x ~ 0.7). The modified parameters are used to calculate the Dirac, Pauli, electric, and magnetic form factors for nucleons in SRC pairs, and these are compared to the free nucleon results.

## Key numbers

- The suppression of the longitudinal quasi-elastic cross section for a proton in an SRC pair is by a factor of 4 at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2
- The model uses a nucleon mass M = 0.94 GeV and a scale parameter kappa = 0.4 GeV
- The initial scale for the parton distribution functions is mu_0^2 = 0.098 GeV^2
- The SRC model assumes n^d_SRC = 0.04 for the deuteron (the number of SRC pairs)
- The free parameters for the u quark in the SRC case are: a1^u = 0.12, b1^u = 1.0, a2^u = 0.92, b2^u = 0.80, delta^u = 1.0
- The free parameters for the d quark in the SRC case are: a1^d = 0.20, b1^d = 0.75, a2^d = 0.90, b2^d = 0.50, delta^d = 1.0

## Context

This work extends the debate between two competing explanations of the nuclear EMC effect: the mean-field approach (Thomas 1989, Saito 1992, Cloet 2006) and the SRC-driven model (Frankfurt 1988, Weinstein 2010). The authors note that a prior study by Kim and Miller (2022) using a two-component holographic model found relatively small effects on form factors from SRC, whereas the present work finds large suppression. The results suggest that if SRCs drive the EMC effect, the number of SRC pairs deduced from quasi-elastic scattering experiments (CLAS 2018) would need to increase dramatically, potentially implying that almost all nucleons in heavy nuclei are in SRC pairs rather than the 20-30% previously reported. The paper calls for further investigation into the relationship between off-shell nucleon structure functions and elastic form factors.
