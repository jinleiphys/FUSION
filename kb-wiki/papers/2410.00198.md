# Unlocking higher-order moments of parton distribution functions from lattice QCD

> We present a new method to calculate moments of parton distribution functions of any order with lattice QCD computations. This method leverages the gradient flow for fermion and gauge fields. The flowed matrix elements of twist-2 operators renormalize multiplicatively, and the matching with physical matrix elements is achieved through the use of continuum symmetries. We derive the matching coefficients at one-loop in perturbation theory for moments of any order in the flavor non-singlet case and provide specific examples of operators suitable for lattice QCD computations. The multiplicative renormalization and matching are independent of the choice of Lorentz indices, allowing the use of temporal indices for twist-2 operators of any dimension. This approach should then also significantly enhance the signal-to-noise ratio in the computation of moments.

# Unlocking higher-order moments of parton distribution functions from lattice QCD

## Key claim
The method uses gradient-flowed fermion and gauge fields to compute moments of parton distribution functions of any order from lattice QCD, with multiplicative renormalization and matching performed via continuum perturbation theory. The approach claims to remove the theoretical limitation on the order of moments that can be calculated, but the matching coefficients are derived only at one-loop order in perturbation theory for the flavor non-singlet case, and the method's practical applicability depends on perturbative uncertainties estimated at approximately 10% or less at next-to-leading log (NLL) order.

## Method
The method constructs flowed twist-2 operators using ringed fermion fields and flowed gauge fields, which renormalize multiplicatively and have a finite continuum limit. The short flow time expansion (SFTX) relates matrix elements of these flowed operators to renormalized matrix elements at vanishing flow time through matching coefficients calculated in perturbation theory. The matching coefficients are derived by imposing matching equations between flowed and unflowed Green's functions, yielding a one-loop expression proportional to the anomalous dimension of the twist-2 operators plus a finite part. The matrix elements are computed with zero external spatial momentum by choosing temporal Lorentz indices for the operators, and ratios of matrix elements at different moments can be formed to avoid explicit renormalization of the flowed fermion fields.

## Key numbers
- The one-loop matching coefficient is given by \( c_n(t,\mu) = 1 + \frac{\bar{g}^2(\mu)}{(4\pi)^2} c_n^{(1)}(t,\mu) + O(\bar{g}^4) \) with \( c_n^{(1)}(t,\mu) = C_F [ \gamma_n \log(8\pi \mu^2 t) + B_n ] \), where \( C_F = 4/3 \).
- The anomalous dimension for twist-2 operators is \( \gamma_n = 1 + 4 \sum_{j=2}^n \frac{1}{j} - \frac{2}{n(n+1)} \).
- The finite part \( B_n \) includes terms such as \( \frac{4}{n(n+1)} \), \( 4\frac{n-1}{n}\log 2 \), and \( -\log(432) \), among others involving the digamma function \( \psi \) and the Lerch transcendent \( \phi \).
- For \( n=2 \), the result reproduces the matching coefficient for the energy-momentum tensor from Makino (2014).
- The ratio of matching coefficients \( \frac{c_2(t,\mu)}{c_n(t,\mu)} \) introduces perturbative corrections typically up to around 10%.
- The ringed fermion field condition is \( \langle \bar{\chi}^r(x,t) \overset{\leftrightarrow}{\slashed{D}} \chi^r(x,t) \rangle = -\frac{N_c}{(4\pi)^2 t^2} \).

## Context
This work extends the gradient flow methodology developed by Luscher (2010, 2013) and the ringed fermion scheme of Makino (2014) to the calculation of parton distribution function moments of arbitrary order, addressing renormalization mixing problems that have limited standard lattice QCD approaches to moments up to \( \langle x^3 \rangle \) (n=4). The method overcomes the power-divergent mixing with lower-dimensional operators that occurs for operators with identical Lorentz indices, as described by Kronfeld (1984) and Martinelli (1987), and avoids the signal-to-noise degradation from requiring non-zero spatial momenta. The approach enables future lattice QCD computations of arbitrarily high moments, with initial numerical results reported by Kim (2024) supporting the expected improvements in statistical uncertainties.
