# Application of Multiple Scattering Theory to Lower Energy Elastic Nucleon-Nucleus Reactions

> The optical model potentials for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering at $65$~MeV are calculated for $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{28}$Si, $^{40}$Ca, $^{56}$Fe, $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb in first order multiple scattering theory, following the prescription of the spectator expansion, where the only inputs are the free NN potentials, the nuclear densities and the nuclear mean field as derived from microscopic nuclear structure calculations. These potentials are used to predict differential cross sections, analyzing powers and spin rotation functions for neutron and proton scattering at 65 MeV projectile energy and compared with available experimental data.

## Key claim

First-order multiple scattering theory, parameter-free and using only free NN potentials, nuclear densities, and a nuclear mean field, provides a surprisingly good description of elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering observables at 65 MeV for angles below about 60 degrees, despite the expectation that higher-order terms would be required at such low energies.

## Method

The optical potential was constructed in momentum space using the first-order spectator expansion, with the NN t-matrix from the full Bonn potential as input. The propagator was modified to account for the coupling of the struck nucleon to the residual nucleus via a one-body mean field, taken either from Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations or from a Dirac-Hartree model. The optical potential was evaluated in the optimum factorized (off-shell tau-rho) form, and used to solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for elastic scattering observables.

## Key numbers

- Projectile energy: 65 MeV for all calculations.
- Targets studied: 12C, 16O, 28Si, 40Ca, 56Fe, 90Zr, and 208Pb.
- First-order multiple scattering predictions for neutron total cross sections on 40Ca at 65 MeV fall short of measured values by about 10%.
- Good agreement with data is obtained for angles below approximately 60 degrees.
- The momentum transfer at 65 MeV for 180 degrees is roughly equivalent to that at 60 degrees for 260 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful applications of first-order multiple scattering theory at higher energies (Chinn, Elster, Thaler 1991; Chinn, Elster, Thaler, Weppner 1994) down to 65 MeV, a regime where phenomenological optical potentials or effective NN interactions had previously been assumed necessary. The availability of both neutron and proton data at the same energy (Hjort et al. 1994) allows the authors to isolate Coulomb effects and test the isospin dependence of the theory. The results suggest that higher-order terms in the spectator expansion, while likely needed for full quantitative agreement, will not be strongly structured and will mainly raise the forward cross section and fill in diffraction minima.
