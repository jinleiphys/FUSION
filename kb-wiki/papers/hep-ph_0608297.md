# Can the X(3872) be a 1^{++} four-quark state?

> We use QCD spectral sum rules to test the nature of the meson X(3872), assumed to be an exotic four-quark (c\bar{c}q\bar{q}) state with J^{PC}=1^{++}. For definiteness, we work with the current proposed recently by Maiani et al [1], at leading order in α_s, consider the contributions of higher dimension condensates and keep terms which are linear in the light quark mass m_q. We find M_X=(3925+- 127) MeV which is compatible, within the errors, with he experimental candidate X(3872), while the SU(3) breaking-terms lead to an unusual mass-splitting M_{X^{s}}-M_X=- (61+-30) MeV. The mass-difference between the neutral states due to isospin violation of about (2.6-3.9) MeV is much smaller than the value (8+-3) MeV proposed in [1]. For the b-quark, we predict M_{X_b}= (10144+-106) MeV for the X_b(b\bar{b}q \bar{q}), which is much below the {\bar B}B* threshold in contrast to the {\bar B}B* molecule prediction [2], and for the X_b^s(b\bar{b}s \bar{s}), a mass-splitting M_{X^s_{b}}-M_{X_b}=-(121+-182) MeV. Our analysis also indicates that the mass-splitting between the ground state and the radial excitation of about (225~250) MeV is much smaller than in the case of ordinary mesons and is (within the errors) flavour-independent. We also extract the decay constants, analogous to f_π, of such mesons, which are useful for further studies of their leptonic and hadronic decay widths. The uncertainties of our estimates are mainly due to the ones from the c and b quark masses.

## Key claim

The X(3872) can be described as a 1++ four-quark (c-cbar-q-qbar) state using QCD spectral sum rules, with a predicted mass of (3925 ± 127) MeV that is compatible with the experimental candidate within the large errors, though the central value is about 53 MeV higher than the observed 3872 MeV.

## Method

The authors used QCD spectral sum rules (both Laplace/Borel sum rules and finite energy sum rules) to study the two-point correlation function of an axial vector current representing a tetraquark state with the symmetric spin distribution [cq]_{S=1}[cbar-qbar]_{S=0} + [cq]_{S=0}[cbar-qbar]_{S=1}. They worked at leading order in α_s, included contributions of condensates up to dimension eight, and kept terms linear in the light quark mass m_q. The analysis used the interpolating current proposed by Maiani et al. (2005) and extracted the mass from the ratio of moments to reduce sensitivity to radiative corrections.

## Key numbers

- M_X = (3925 ± 127) MeV for the c-cbar-q-qbar tetraquark, compatible with the experimental X(3872) within errors
- M_{X^s} - M_X = -(61 ± 30) MeV for the SU(3) breaking mass splitting between the strange and non-strange tetraquark states
- Isospin violation mass difference |M(X_h) - M(X_l)| ≈ (2.6 - 3.9) MeV, smaller than the (8 ± 3) MeV value proposed by Maiani et al. (2005)
- M_{X_b} = (10144 ± 106) MeV for the b-bbar-q-qbar tetraquark, much below the B-B* threshold
- M_{X^s_b} - M_{X_b} = -(121 ± 182) MeV for the bottom-sector SU(3) mass splitting
- Ground state to radial excitation mass splitting ≈ (225 - 250) MeV, much smaller than in ordinary mesons
- Decay constant f_X = (4.66 ± 0.16 ± 0.29 ± 0.68) × 10^{-5} GeV

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach previously applied to light scalar mesons and the D_{sJ}(2317) meson as four-quark states by Latorre and Pascual (1984), Narison (1989), and others. It directly tests the tetraquark interpretation proposed by Maiani et al. (2005) for the X(3872), which was discovered by Belle (2003) and has anomalous properties including isospin violation and a mass too low for conventional charmonium. The prediction of a bottom-sector tetraquark X_b well below the B-B* threshold contrasts with the molecular prediction by Swanson (2004), and the results enable future studies of leptonic and hadronic decay widths using the extracted decay constants.
