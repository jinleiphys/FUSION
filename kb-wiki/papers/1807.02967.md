# Asymptotic behavior of Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for scalar systems with a bound state

> We study the asymptotic behaviors of the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions, which are important for the HAL QCD potential method to extract hadron interactions, in the case that a bound state exists in the system. We consider the complex scalar particles, two of which lead to the formation of a bound state. In the case of the two-body system, we show that the NBS wave functions for the bound state as well as scattering states in the asymptotic region behave like the wave functions in quantum mechanics, which carry the information of the binding energy as well as the scattering phase shift. This analysis theoretically establishes under some conditions that the HAL QCD potential can correctly reproduce not only the scattering phase shift but also the binding energy. As an extension of the analysis, we also study the asymptotic behaviors of all possible NBS wave functions in the case of the three-body systems, two of which can form a bound states.

## Key claim

The paper shows that Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions for both bound and scattering states in scalar systems with a bound state asymptotically behave like quantum mechanical wave functions, provided the binding energy is smaller than other mass scales such as the scattering particle mass. This establishes that the HAL QCD potential method can correctly reproduce both scattering phase shifts and binding energy from correlation functions that couple to bound states.

## Method

The authors apply the Lippmann-Schwinger formalism to two-body and three-body complex scalar systems, splitting the Hamiltonian into a free part that includes both scattering states and bound states, plus an interaction part. For the two-body system, they derive the asymptotic forms of NBS wave functions in the center-of-mass frame by evaluating integrals over half off-shell T-matrices at large separation distances. For the three-body system, they extend the analysis using nonrelativistic approximations and hyperspherical expansions to handle the coupled channels of three-particle states and one-particle-plus-bound-state configurations.

## Key numbers

- Bound state mass \(m_B < 2m\) (where \(m\) is the mass of each scalar particle)
- Binding momentum \(\kappa_B = \sqrt{m^2 - m_B^2/4}\) characterizes the exponential decay of the bound state NBS wave function as \(e^{-\kappa_B r}/r\)
- The asymptotic form of the scattering state NBS wave function behaves as \(\frac{4\pi}{(2\pi)^3E_{k}Z_k}\frac{e^{i\delta_l^{2-2}(k)}}{kr}\sin\left(kr -l\pi/2 +\delta_l^{2-2}(k)\right)\)
- For the three-body system, the asymptotic form involves \(K_2 = \sqrt{2m_{\rm red}(E - m_B - m)}\) and \(K_3 = \sqrt{2m (E - 3m)}\) where \(1/m_{\rm red} = 1/m + 1/m_B\)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of NBS wave function asymptotics by Aoki (2013) and others, which considered systems without bound states, to include the case where a bound state exists in the spectrum. The results theoretically justify the time-dependent HAL QCD method introduced by the HAL QCD Collaboration (2012) for extracting hadron interactions from lattice QCD correlation functions. The analysis enables reliable extraction of both scattering phase shifts and binding energies from lattice QCD calculations of hadron interactions, particularly for systems like the two-baryon system where previous numerical results showed discrepancies between different methods.
