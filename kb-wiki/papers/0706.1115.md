# Continuum quasiparticle linear response theory using the Skyrme functional for multipole responses of exotic nuclei

> We develop a new formulation of the continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) in which the residual interaction is derived directly from the Skyrme energy functional, keeping all the velocity dependent terms of the Skyrme effective interaction. Numerical analysis using the SkM$^*$ parameter set is performed for the isovector dipole and the isovector/isoscalar quadrupole responses in $^{20}$O and $^{54}$Ca. It is shown that the energy-weighted sum rule including the enhancement factors for the isovector responses is satisfied with good accuracy. We investigate also how the velocity dependent terms influence the strength distribution and the transition densities of the low-lying surface modes and the giant resonances.

## Key claim

The authors develop a continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) that derives the residual interaction directly from the Skyrme energy functional including velocity-dependent terms, but they explicitly neglect spin-dependent densities and the Coulomb interaction, so full self-consistency is not achieved.

## Method

The authors formulate the continuum QRPA using the Green's function method with outgoing-wave boundary conditions, deriving the residual interaction from the Skyrme Hartree-Fock energy functional by including fluctuations in the current density, spatial derivatives of the density, and kinetic energy density alongside the usual local density and pair density fluctuations. They solve the linear response equation for eighteen radial functions representing these density fluctuations, using the SkM* parameter set for the Skyrme interaction and a density-dependent delta interaction for pairing. The particle-hole residual interaction is renormalized by an overall factor to force the spurious mode to zero energy.

## Key numbers

- The renormalization factor for the particle-hole residual interaction is f=1.0470 for 20O and f=1.0142 for 54Ca
- The pairing force strength V0 is -280 MeV fm^-3 for 20O and -285 MeV fm^-3 for 54Ca, producing average neutron pairing gaps of 1.91 MeV and 1.29 MeV respectively
- The cutoff for quasiparticle states in the HFB calculation is set at Emax = 60 MeV
- The radial coordinate space is discretized up to rmax = 15 fm with interval Δr = 0.2 fm
- The smearing parameter used is ε = 1.0 MeV with strength functions evaluated at excitation energy intervals of 0.5 MeV
- Angular momentum quantum numbers are summed up to lmax = 7ħ for 20O and lmax = 8ħ for 54Ca

## Context

This work extends the continuum QRPA methods developed by Matsuo (2001, 2002, 2006) and Khan (2002, 2004) that used simplified contact or Landau-Migdal residual interactions, by incorporating the full velocity-dependent terms of the Skyrme interaction to satisfy the energy-weighted sum rule. The approach is similar to that of Yamagami (2005, 2006) but uses outgoing-wave boundary conditions instead of finite-box discretization for continuum quasiparticle states. This enables improved description of multipole responses in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, where coupling to continuum states is essential.
