# Three dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical model for QGP gas

> We numerically solve fully (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical equation coupled with the baryon number conservation law without spatial symmetry. We discuss the effect of transverse expansion based on the deviation our numerical result from Bjorken's scaling solution. We analyze the space-time evolution of the QGP gas in the case of non cylindrical initial conditions.

# Three dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical model for QGP gas

## Key claim

The authors numerically solve fully (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical equations without cylindrical symmetry, and show that Bjorken's scaling solution remains a valid approximation for large systems up to a characteristic proper time τ_B that scales with the initial transverse size. The transverse flow causes deviations from Bjorken's scaling solution only after τ ≈ 3.0 fm for the chosen initial conditions.

## Method

The authors solve the (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical equation for a perfect fluid coupled with baryon number conservation, using a Lagrangian algorithm where lattice points move with the local fluid velocity and entropy conservation is enforced explicitly. They adopt a QGP gas equation of state for massless three-flavor quarks with finite chemical potential, and compare their numerical solutions to Bjorken's (1+1)-dimensional scaling solution by fixing the longitudinal velocity to v_z = z/t. Particle emission from the freeze-out hypersurface is computed using the Cooper-Frye formula.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T₀ = 200 MeV and initial chemical potential μ₀ = 210 MeV are used for the Gaussian initial distributions centered at x₀ = y₀ = z₀ = 1.0 fm with widths σ_x = σ_y = σ_z = 1.0 fm
- The numerical solution coincides with Bjorken's scaling solution up to proper time τ = 3.0 fm; beyond this point deviations due to transverse flow increase with proper time
- The characteristic time τ_B (when the difference between the numerical and Bjorken solutions exceeds 0.1%) is approximately proportional to the initial transverse size x₀, y₀ over the range 1.0 fm to 6.0 fm
- The freeze-out temperature is set to 140 MeV at vanishing chemical potential, based on chemical and thermal freeze-out models
- Non-central collisions are studied with impact parameter b = 0.8 fm and nuclear radius R = 1.0 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier (1+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical models following Bjorken's scaling solution (Bjorken, 1983) and previous (3+1)-dimensional Eulerian hydrodynamical calculations by Rischke et al. (1995). It also builds on prior hydrodynamical analyses that assumed cylindrical symmetry (Ishii, 1992; Sollfrank, 1997; Hung, 1995; Schlei, 1996). The authors enable future studies of anisotropic collective flow (directed flow, elliptic flow, radial flow) observed at AGS and SPS experiments, and plan to incorporate more realistic initial conditions from event generators and equations of state including the QGP-to-hadron phase transition.
