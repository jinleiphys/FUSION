# Finite particle number description of neutron matter using the unitary correlation operator and high-momentum pair methods

> By using bare Argonne V4' (AV4'), V6' (AV6'), and V8' (AV8') nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions respectively, the nuclear equations of state (EOSs) for neutron matter are calculated with the unitary correlation operator and high-momentum pair methods. The neutron matter is described under a finite particle number approach with magic number $N=66$ under a periodic boundary condition. The central short-range correlation coming from the short-range repulsion in the NN interaction is treated by the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) and the tensor correlation and spin-orbit effects are described by the two-particle two-hole (2p2h) excitations of nucleon pairs, in which the two nucleons with a large relative momentum are regarded as a high-momentum pair (HM). With the 2p2h configurations increasing, the total energy per particle of neutron matter is well converged under this UCOM+HM framework. By comparing the results calculated with AV4', AV6', and AV8' NN interactions, the effects of the short-range correlation, the tensor correlation, and the spin-orbit coupling on the density dependence of the total energy per particle of neutron matter are demonstrated. Moreover, the contribution of each Hamiltonian component to the total energy per particle is discussed. The EOSs of neutron matter calculated within the present UCOM+HM framework agree with the calculations of six different microscopic many-body theories, especially in agreement with the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key claim

The UCOM+HM framework, using a finite particle number approach with neutron number N=66 under periodic boundary conditions, produces equations of state for neutron matter that agree with six different microscopic many-body theories, particularly the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations, but only when the maximum transfer momentum mode is set to n_q^max=6 to balance computational cost and convergence.

## Method

The authors describe neutron matter in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions using a finite particle number approach with magic number N=66. The short-range repulsion in the nucleon-nucleon interaction is treated by the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM), while the tensor correlation and spin-orbit effects are described by two-particle two-hole (2p2h) excitations of nucleon pairs, where the two nucleons with large relative momentum are regarded as a high-momentum pair (HM). The total wave function is a superposition of the 0p0h state and 2p2h configurations, with configuration amplitudes variationally determined by minimizing the total energy. Calculations are performed with bare Argonne AV4', AV6', and AV8' nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

* Total energy per particle of neutron matter at normal density ρ=0.17 fm⁻³ with AV4' interaction: 41.873 MeV (0p0h state), converging to approximately 12.5 MeV (UCOM+HM with n_q^max=6, N=66)
* The short-range correlation contributes about 9 MeV per particle to the kinetic energy for AV4', AV6', and AV8' potentials
* With n_q^max=6, the number of 2p2h configurations is 1.8×10⁶; with n_q^max=8, it increases to 4.0×10⁶, but the energy difference between these two modes is only tens of keV (about 0.3% for AV4' and AV6', 0.4% for AV8')
* The relative error for kinetic energy with N=66 is about 0.5% for 0p0h and 0.4% for 0p0h+UCOM; for potential energy, the relative error decreases from 2.7% (0p0h) to 0.4% (0p0h+UCOM)
* The transfer momentum magnitude at normal density is about |q|=5.2 fm⁻¹ with integer mode |n_q|=6, compared to the empirical Fermi momentum k_F=1.4 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous UCOM+HM framework (Wan et al., 2020) from symmetric nuclear matter to neutron matter, specifically investigating the effects of tensor correlation and spin-orbit coupling using AV6' and AV8' interactions. The results are compared with benchmark calculations from Baldo et al. using six different microscopic many-body theories including Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, self-consistent Green's function, Fermi hypernetted chain, auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo, Green's function Monte Carlo, and coupled cluster theory. The finite particle number approach with periodic boundary conditions follows the methodology used in AFDMC, GFMC, and CC calculations for nuclear matter.
