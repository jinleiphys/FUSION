# Scalar susceptibility and chiral symmetry restoration in nuclei

> We study the nuclear modification of the scalar QCD susceptibility, calculated as the derivative of the quark condensate with respect to the quark mass. We show that it has two origins. One is the low lying nuclear excitations. At normal nuclear density this part is constrained by the nuclear incompressibility. The other part arises from the individual nucleon response and it is dominated by the pion cloud contribution. Numerically the first contribution dominates. The resulting increase in magnitude of the scalar susceptibility at normal density is such that it becomes close to the pseudoscalar susceptibility, while it is quite different in the vacuum. We interpret it as a consequence of chiral symmetry restoration in nuclei.

## Key claim

The scalar QCD susceptibility in nuclear matter increases at normal density to become nearly equal to the pseudoscalar susceptibility, which the authors interpret as a consequence of chiral symmetry restoration in nuclei; however, this convergence should be taken "with a grain of salt" due to possible medium renormalisation of the nucleon sigma term, which was not taken into account.

## Method

The scalar susceptibility was computed as the derivative of the quark condensate with respect to the quark mass, decomposed into a vacuum contribution, a nucleonic contribution from individual nucleon response (dominated by the pion cloud), and a nuclear contribution from low-lying nuclear excitations. The nuclear contribution was evaluated using a free Fermi gas model and related to the nuclear incompressibility, while the nucleonic contribution was calculated from the pion cloud using the sigma term and its derivative with respect to the quark mass, including contributions from πN and πΔ intermediate states.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear excitation contribution to the scalar susceptibility at normal density: χ_Nuclear_S(ρ₀) = -8.2 × 10⁵ MeV²
- The nucleonic contribution at normal density (including Pauli blocking reduction of about 25%): ρ₀ × χ̃_S^N ≈ -6.8 × 10⁴ MeV²
- Total scalar susceptibility modification at normal density: χ_S(ρ₀) = χ_S(vac.) - 8.9 × 10⁵ MeV²
- Vacuum pseudoscalar susceptibility: χ_PS(vac) ≈ -1.3 × 10⁶ MeV²
- Nucleon sigma term used: Σ_N ≈ 45 MeV
- Nuclear incompressibility used: K ≈ 230 MeV
- Bag model valence quark contribution to nucleon scalar susceptibility: χ_S^N(Bag) ≈ 2.5 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹ (for bag radius R = 0.8 fm)
- Pion cloud contribution to nucleon scalar susceptibility (with monopole form factor Λ = 5m_π): χ_S^N(π) ≈ -4 × 10⁻² MeV⁻¹
- Pion cloud contribution including πΔ states: χ_S^N(πΔ) ≈ -1.4 × 10⁻² MeV⁻¹
- Total nucleon scalar susceptibility from pion cloud: ≈ -5.4 × 10⁻² MeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dispersive approach to in-medium QCD susceptibilities (Chanfray and Ericson, 2002) which used the linear sigma model and ignored coupling to pion density fluctuations. The new derivation is free from the specific features of the linear sigma model and incorporates interference effects between sigma exchange and two-pion exchange contributions to the nucleon sigma term. The result that scalar and pseudoscalar susceptibilities become nearly equal at normal nuclear density is a spectacular consequence of partial chiral restoration that may have consequences in processes involving higher densities, and the authors note that systematic investigation of medium renormalisation effects and extension to larger densities deserves further work.
