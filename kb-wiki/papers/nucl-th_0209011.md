# Equation of State for the Two-component Van der Waals Gas with Relativistic Excluded Volumes

> A canonical partition function for the two-component excluded volume model is derived, leading to two different van der Waals approximations. The one is known as the Lorentz-Berthelot mixture and the other has been proposed recently. Both models are analysed in the canonical and grand canonical ensemble. In comparison with the one-component van der Waals excluded volume model the suppression of particle densities is reduced in these two-component formulations, but in two essentially different ways. Presently used multi-component models have no such reduction. They are shown to be not correct when used for components with different hard-core radii. For high temperatures the excluded volume interaction is refined by accounting for the Lorentz contraction of the spherical excluded volumes, which leads to a distinct enhancement of lighter particles. The resulting effects on pion yield ratios are studied for AGS and SPS data.

## Key claim

The paper derives a canonical partition function for a two-component van der Waals excluded volume model and shows that the commonly used multi-component excluded volume models are incorrect when components have different hard-core radii, because they correspond to a system where components are separated by a mobile wall and cannot mix.

## Method

The authors start from the canonical partition function for hard-core particles and generalize the one-component van der Waals excluded volume approximation to two components with different radii. They derive two distinct van der Waals approximations: the non-linear approximation (Lorentz-Berthelot mixture) and the linear approximation proposed by Gorbar (1999). Both models are analyzed in the canonical and grand canonical ensembles, and the excluded volume interaction is refined at high temperatures by accounting for Lorentz contraction of the spherical excluded volumes.

- The excluded volume terms are defined as 2b_pq = (4π/3)(R_p+R_q)^3 for particles of components p and q

## Context

This work extends the one-component van der Waals excluded volume model used in thermal model analyses of heavy-ion collisions, specifically addressing the problem of different hard-core radii for different hadron species. The authors show that the multi-component models used by Yen (1997) and Braun-Munzinger (1999) correspond to a separated system with a mobile wall and are therefore incorrect for mixed systems. The relativistic excluded volume treatment builds on the approach of Bugaev (2000, 1999). The results enable more realistic modeling of pion yield ratios at AGS and SPS energies by properly accounting for different hard-core radii and Lorentz contraction effects.
