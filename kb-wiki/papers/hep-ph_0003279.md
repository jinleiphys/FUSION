# Parametric amplification with a friction in heavy ion collisions

> We study the effects of the expansion of the system and the friction on the parametric amplification of mesonic fields in high energy heavy ion collisions within the linear $σ$ model . The equation of motion which is similar to Mathieu equation is derived to describe the time development of classical fields in the last stage of a heavy ion collision after the freezeout time. The enhanced mode is extracted analytically by comparison with Mathieu equation and the equation of motion is solved numerically to examine whether soft modes will be enhanced or not. It is found that the strong peak appears around 267 MeV in the pion transverse momentum distribution in cases with weak friction and high maximum temperature. This enhancement can be extracted by taking the ratio between different modes in the pion transverse momentum distribution.

## Key claim

Parametric amplification of pion fields can produce a strong peak around 267 MeV in the transverse momentum distribution, but only in cases with weak friction and high maximum temperature; the amplification saturates as maximum temperature increases because the time interval for amplification converges to a finite constant even at infinite temperature.

## Method

The author derived an equation of motion for mesonic fields in the linear σ model, incorporating a friction term phenomenologically and using scaling variables (τ, η) for one-dimensional expansion after freezeout. The equation was reduced to a Mathieu-like form with a time-dependent coefficient q(ξ), and solved numerically for the pion field π(1) with initial conditions mimicking a thermalized system at freezeout temperature Tf = 100 MeV, using parameters fπ = 92.5 MeV, v = 87.4 MeV, λ = 20, and friction coefficients ησ = 0.25, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0 fm−1 with ηπ = 0.

## Key numbers

* The amplified mode for pion fields at Aπ = 1 corresponds to transverse momentum kT ≈ 267 MeV (for ησ = 0.5 fm−1, ηπ = 0).
* The amplified mode for pion fields at Aπ = 4 corresponds to kT ≈ 583 MeV.
* The amplified mode for sigma fields at Aσ = 9 corresponds to kT ≈ 669 MeV.
* The friction coefficient for sigma field from Rischke is about 3 fm−1 at zero temperature; the author uses smaller values: ησ = 0.25, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0 fm−1.
* The ratio ησ²/(4mσ²) is about 0.1 for ησ = 2 fm−1 and mσ = 600 MeV.
* The initial condensate amplitude used is σini = −30 MeV.
* The freezeout temperature used is Tf = 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of parametric amplification of mesonic fields in heavy ion collisions by Müller and by Hiro-Oka, who considered amplification on the normal vacuum without damping, and by Biró and Greiner (who derived friction on the chirally symmetric vacuum) and Rischke (who gave friction on the chirally broken vacuum). The paper shows that including both expansion and friction reduces the amplification strength, but a detectable peak near 267 MeV in the pion transverse momentum distribution survives for weak friction and high maximum temperature, and can be extracted by taking ratios between different momentum modes.
