# Neutral 3-body system in a strong magnetic field: factorization and exact solutions

> Neutral systems containing two identical particles, in homogeneous magnetic field are shown to obey exact factorizable solutions both in nonrelativistic and relativistic formalism, similarly to the neutral two-body systems. Concrete examples of the helium atom and the neutron as a (ddu) system are considered.

## Key claim

Neutral three-body systems containing two identical particles in a homogeneous magnetic field admit exact factorizable solutions for the center-of-mass and internal motion, analogous to the known two-body case, but only under the specific charge condition \(e_1 = e_2 = -e/2, e_3 = e\) and mass condition \(m_1 = m_2\).

## Method

The author starts from the nonrelativistic Pauli Hamiltonian for three particles in a magnetic field, introduces Jacobi coordinates for the case of two identical particles, and applies a phase factor transformation to separate the center-of-mass motion from the internal motion. The resulting Hamiltonian is shown to be fully separable into independent transverse and longitudinal degrees of freedom, yielding exact analytic eigenfunctions in terms of Landau-like states. The same factorization procedure is then extended to the relativistic case by replacing masses with dynamical energies \(\omega_i\) and adding gluon exchange and confinement terms for baryon systems.

## Key numbers

- For the helium atom in a strong magnetic field, a lower bound for the binding energy is estimated as twice the limiting binding energy of the hydrogen atom in a strong magnetic field: \(2 \cdot 1.74\ \text{keV} = 3.48\ \text{keV}\).
- The energy eigenvalues for the \(\xi\)-motion are given by \(E_{n_\xi} = \frac{k^2_\xi}{2m} + \frac{eB m_+}{2 m m_3} \left( n_\xi + \frac{1+|l_\xi|}{2} + \frac{m_3-2m}{2m_3} l_\xi \right)\), where \(m_+ = 2m + m_3\).
- The energy eigenvalues for the \(\eta\)-motion are \(E_{n_\eta} = \frac{k^2_\eta}{2m} + \frac{eB}{2m} \left( n_\eta + \frac{1+|l_\eta|+l_\eta}{2} \right)\).
- The scaling variable for the \(\xi\)-motion Landau wavefunctions is \(x = \frac{eB m_+}{4 m_3} \xi_\perp^2\), and for the \(\eta\)-motion \(y = \frac{eB}{4} \eta_\perp^2\).

## Context

This work extends the factorization theorem for neutral two-body systems in a magnetic field, previously established by the author and others (Simonov, earlier works; see also references to Avron et al. and Herold et al.), to the three-body case with two identical particles. The paper provides the theoretical foundation for accurate calculations of the helium atom spectrum in strong magnetic fields, building on prior studies of the hydrogen atom in strong fields (Herold et al., 1981; Shabad et al., 2007) which showed stabilization due to \(e^+e^-\) loop corrections. The formalism also opens the door to computing neutral baryon masses (neutron, \(\Delta^0\), \(\Xi^0\)) as functions of magnetic field strength, analogous to the meson calculations performed in the author's earlier relativistic two-body work (Simonov, 2012).
