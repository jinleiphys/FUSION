# Production characteristics of light (anti-)nuclei from (anti-)nucleon coalescence in heavy ion collisions at energies employed at the RHIC beam energy scan

> With the kinetic freeze-out nucleons and antinucleons obtained from the quark combination model, we study the production of light nuclei and antinuclei in the (anti-)nucleon coalescence mechanism in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We derive analytic formulas of the momentum distributions of different light nuclei and apply them to compute transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra of (anti-)deuterons ($d$, $\bar d$) and (anti-)tritons ($t$, $\bar t$) in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 54.4 GeV. We find that the experimental data available for these $p_T$ spectra can be well reproduced. We further study the yields and yield ratios of different light (anti-)nuclei and naturally explain their interesting behaviors as a function of the collision energy. We especially point out that the multi-particle yield ratio $tp/d^2$ should be carefully corrected from hyperon weak decays for protons to probe the production characteristics of light nuclei. All of our results show that the coalescence mechanism for (anti-)nucleons plays a dominant role for the production of light nuclei and antinuclei at the RHIC beam energy scan energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nucleon coalescence mechanism plays a dominant role for light (anti-)nuclei production at RHIC beam energy scan energies, but this conclusion is conditional on using kinetic freeze-out nucleons from the quark combination model and on properly correcting the multi-particle yield ratio \(tp/d^2\) for hyperon weak decay contaminations to protons.

## Method

The authors start with kinetic freeze-out (anti-)nucleons obtained from the Shandong quark combination model (SDQCM) and then apply analytic formulas for deuteron and triton momentum distributions derived from a nucleon coalescence framework with a spherical harmonic oscillator wave function and a factorized coordinate-momentum joint distribution. They compute transverse momentum spectra and yield densities of (anti-)deuterons and (anti-)tritons in Au-Au collisions at six beam energies, using the effective source radius \(R_f = a (dN_{ch}/dy)^{1/3}\) with a free parameter \(a\) fitted to data.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron and antideuteron \(p_T\) spectra are reproduced at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 54.4\) GeV in centralities \(0-10\%\), \(10-20\%\), \(20-40\%\), \(40-60\%\), and \(60-80\%\).
* Triton \(p_T\) spectra are reproduced at the same six energies in centralities \(0-10\%\), \(10-20\%\), \(20-40\%\), and \(40-80\%\).
* The effective radius parameter is \(a = 0.58\) for deuterons and antideuterons, and \(a = 0.55\) for tritons and antitritons.
* Weak decay contamination (WDC) for protons ranges from \(12.9\%\) (60-80% centrality at 7.7 GeV) to \(35.2\%\) (0-10% centrality at 54.4 GeV); for antiprotons, WDC ranges from \(32.2\%\) (60-80% at 39 GeV) to \(52.5\%\) (0-10% at 7.7 GeV).
* Yield density \(dN/dy\) of deuterons in 0-10% centrality decreases from \(50.2 \times 10^{-2}\) (data) at 7.7 GeV to \(23.6 \times 10^{-2}\) (theory) at 54.4 GeV; antideuteron yield increases from \(0.36 \times 10^{-5}\) (data) at 7.7 GeV to \(9.9 \times 10^{-5}\) (theory) at 54.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon coalescence approach previously applied at LHC energies by Wang et al. (2021) to the lower RHIC beam energy scan range, and it uses the same Shandong quark combination model as Song et al. (2021) and Feng et al. (2021) for the nucleon input. The results enable systematic studies of energy- and centrality-dependent light nuclei yields and yield ratios, and the authors specifically caution that the ratio \(tp/d^2\) must be corrected for hyperon weak decays before it can be used to probe critical end point signals, as suggested by Liu et al. (2020) and Sun et al. (2021).
