# Dilepton production and elliptic flow from an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma

> We calculate the yield and elliptic flow of mid-rapidity dileptons emitted from the quark-gluon plasma generated in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC. We use relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics for the 3+1 dimensional evolution of the quark-gluon plasma and convolve this with the momentum-anisotropic local rest frame production rate for dileptons. The effects of momentum anisotropy of the quark distribution functions, viscosity to entropy density ratio, centrality of the collisions, and initial momentum anisotropy on the results are investigated and discussed.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that including momentum-anisotropic local rest frame dilepton production rates, rather than isotropic rates, in a 3+1 dimensional relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma reduces the predicted elliptic flow of intermediate-mass dileptons, even below the near-ideal fluid case, while hardening the transverse momentum spectrum.

## Method

The authors used 3+1 dimensional relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) with a quasiparticle equation of state extracted from lattice QCD to evolve the quark-gluon plasma produced in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC. They convolved this space-time evolution with the leading-order momentum-anisotropic local rest frame dilepton production rate, which uses an ellipsoidally deformed Fermi-Dirac distribution parameterized by anisotropy parameters ξ₁ and ξ₂, performing the rate integration numerically without linearization. The hydrodynamic parameters were tuned to reproduce soft hadron observables, and only dileptons from the QGP phase above the critical temperature (0.155 GeV) were included, focusing on invariant mass M ≥ 1 GeV and transverse momentum p_T ≥ 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The initial proper time for the hydrodynamic evolution was taken as τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c.
* The freeze-out temperature was 0.130 GeV; the critical temperature for the QGP phase was 0.155 GeV.
* For the reference setting (4πη/s = 2, no initial momentum anisotropy), the initial central temperature was T₀ = 0.6 GeV, which gave the best fit to hadron spectra.
* For 4πη/s values of 1, 2, and 3 (with no initial momentum anisotropy), the initial central temperatures were T₀ = 0.63, 0.6, and 0.58 GeV respectively, adjusted to fit soft hadron spectra.
* For the case with initial momentum anisotropy (α_z(τ₀) = 0.5) and 4πη/s = 2, the re-tuned initial central temperature was T₀ = 0.58 GeV.
* The integration regions for dilepton mass and transverse momentum were 1 < M < 20 GeV and 1 < p_T < 20 GeV.
* The elliptic flow v₂(p_T) showed a reversal of ordering with η/s: for p_T ≲ 4 GeV, smaller η/s gave larger v₂, but for p_T ≳ 4 GeV, larger η/s gave higher v₂.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of QGP dilepton yields using 1+1 dimensional models (Martinez and Strickland, 2008) and 3+1 dimensional dissipative aHydro with spheroidal anisotropy (Ryblewski and Strickland, 2015) by incorporating ellipsoidal momentum anisotropy in both the hydrodynamic evolution and the local rest frame production rates. It also extends studies of dilepton elliptic flow using ideal hydrodynamics (Chatterjee et al., 2007) and viscous hydrodynamics (Vujanovic et al., 2013, 2016) by using aHydro with a realistic equation of state. The results enable extraction of information about the effective temperature and momentum anisotropy of the early QGP stages by comparing model predictions for both differential yield and elliptic flow to experimental data.
