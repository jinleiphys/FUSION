# Microscopic calculation of inelastic proton scattering off $^{18}$O, $^{10}$Be, $^{12}$Be, and $^{16}$C for study of neutron excitation in neutron-rich nuclei

> The microscopic coupled-channel calculation of inelastic proton scattering is performed for the study of neutron excitations in $2^+_1$ states of $^{18}$O, $^{10}$Be, $^{12}$Be, and $^{16}$C. Proton-nucleus potentials in the coupled-channel calculation are microscopically derived by folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction with matter and transition densities of target nuclei obtained by the structure model calculation of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The calculated result reasonably reproduces the elastic and inelastic proton scattering cross sections, and supports the dominant contribution of neutron in the $2^+_1$ excitation of $^{12}$Be and $^{16}$C as well as $^{18}$O. Sensitivity of the inelastic scattering cross sections to the neutron transition density is discussed. The exotic feature of the neutron transition density with the amplitude in the outer region in $^{12}$Be and $^{16}$C is focused.

## Key claim

The microscopic coupled-channel calculation supports the dominant contribution of neutrons in the $2^+_1$ excitation of $^{12}$Be and $^{16}$C as well as $^{18}$O, but the agreement with experimental data depends on the quality of the structure model densities and the neutron transition density used, with the calculation for $^{18}$O showing that the default AMD neutron transition density may need adjustment to better reproduce proton scattering data.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic coupled-channel calculations for inelastic proton scattering by folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction with matter and transition densities of target nuclei obtained from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) structure calculations. The diagonal and coupling potentials for the nucleon-nucleus system were microscopically calculated using the Brieva and Rook prescription to localize exchange terms, with only the central part of the proton-nucleus potential taken into account. The cross sections were calculated by coupled-channel calculations using the AMD densities for $^{18}$O, $^{10}$Be, $^{12}$Be, and $^{16}$C, and AMD+GCM densities for $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O.

## Key numbers

- For $^{18}$O, the AMD calculation gives $M_n/M_p = 5.4$ for the $2^+_1 \to 0^+_1$ transition, while the experimental value from mirror transitions is $2.3(2)$
- For $^{12}$Be, the AMD calculation gives $M_n/M_p = 1.9$ for the $2^+_1 \to 0^+_1$ transition, with $B^{(p)}_{\lambda=2} = 14.4$ fm$^4$ (experiment: $14.2(2.8)$ fm$^4$) and $B^{(n)}_{\lambda=2} = 51.1$ fm$^4$
- For $^{16}$C, the AMD calculation gives $M_n/M_p = 3.2$ for the $2^+_1 \to 0^+_1$ transition, with $B^{(p)}_{\lambda=2} = 2.7$ fm$^4$ (experiment: $2.6(9)$ fm$^4$ from Ong (2007)) and $B^{(n)}_{\lambda=2} = 27.0$ fm$^4$
- For $^{10}$Be, the AMD calculation gives $M_n/M_p = 0.9$ for the $2^+_1 \to 0^+_1$ transition, with $B^{(p)}_{\lambda=2} = 11.6$ fm$^4$ (experiment: $10.2(1.0)$ fm$^4$) and $B^{(n)}_{\lambda=2} = 8.9$ fm$^4$
- For $^{18}$O, the proton transition density was scaled by factor $M^{\rm exp}_p/M^{\rm cal}_p = 3.88$ to fit experimental $B(E2)$, and the neutron transition density was scaled by $1.72$ using mirror symmetry with $^{18}$Ne

## Context

This work extends the microscopic folding model approach previously developed by the Melbourne group (Amos, 2000) and applied to elastic scattering by Minomo (2010) and Toyokawa (2015), now applied to inelastic proton scattering off neutron-rich nuclei. The results for $^{12}$Be and $^{16}$C confirm the neutron dominance in $2^+_1$ excitations suggested by earlier AMD structure calculations (Kanada-En'yo, 1999, 2003, 2004) and experimental studies (Iwasaki, 2000; Ong, 2006). The approach enables future extraction of neutron transition densities from inelastic proton scattering data on exotic nuclei, complementing the earlier work by Takashina (2008) who used the JLM interaction for $^{10}$Be and $^{12}$Be.
