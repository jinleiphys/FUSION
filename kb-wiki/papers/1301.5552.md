# Microscopic analysis of sub-barrier fusion enhancement in $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca vs. $^{132}$Sn+$^{48}$Ca}

> We provide a theoretical analysis of recently measured fusion cross sections which show a surprising enhancement at low $E_\mathrm{c.m.}$ energies for the system $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca as compared to the more neutron-rich system $^{132}$Sn+$^{48}$Ca. Dynamic microscopic calculations are carried out on a three-dimensional lattice with a time-dependent density-constrained density functional theory. There are no adjustable parameters, the only input is the Skyrme effective NN interaction. Heavy-ion potentials $V(R)$, coordinate-dependent mass parameters $M(R)$, and total fusion cross sections $σ(E_\mathrm{c.m.})$ are calculated for both systems. We are able to explain the measured fusion enhancement in terms of the {\it narrower width} of the ion-ion potential for $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca, while the barrier heights and positions are approximately the same in both systems.

## Key claim

The observed sub-barrier fusion enhancement for the neutron-deficient system $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca relative to the more neutron-rich $^{132}$Sn+$^{48}$Ca is explained by the narrower width of the ion-ion potential for the $^{40}$Ca system, while the barrier heights and positions are approximately the same for both systems.

## Method

The authors used the Density Constrained Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) method on a three-dimensional Cartesian lattice to extract heavy-ion interaction potentials $V(R)$ and coordinate-dependent mass parameters $M(R)$ from the TDHF time-evolution of the dinuclear system. The only input was the Skyrme SLy4 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, with no adjustable parameters. Total fusion cross sections $\sigma(E_\mathrm{c.m.})$ were then obtained by numerically integrating the Schrodinger equation with the transformed potentials $U(\bar{R})$ (corresponding to constant reduced mass $\mu$) using the Incoming Wave Boundary Condition method.

## Key numbers

- At $E_\mathrm{c.m.}=110$ MeV, the experimental fusion cross section for $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca is approximately 6 mb, compared to 0.8 mb for $^{132}$Sn+$^{48}$Ca.
- The empirical Wong model fit yields a barrier curvature $\hbar \omega = 13.13 \pm 1.09$ MeV for $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca, compared to $\hbar \omega = 5.77 \pm 0.52$ MeV for $^{132}$Sn+$^{48}$Ca.
- For $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca, the DC-TDHF potential calculated at $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=120$ MeV has a barrier height $E_B=112.3$ MeV located at $\bar{R}=12.4$ fm; at $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=180$ MeV the barrier is $E_B=115.3$ MeV located at $\bar{R}=11.8$ fm.
- The lowest-energy DC-TDHF potential calculated for $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca (at $E_\mathrm{TDHF}=115$ MeV) has a barrier $E_B=111.5$ MeV located at $\bar{R}=12.4$ fm.
- The 3D Cartesian lattice spans 50 fm along the collision axis and 30-42 fm in the other two directions; the time step is $\Delta t = 0.4$ fm/c.
- Total CPU time for the $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca calculations was 192 days on a single Intel Xeon processor, reduced to about 16 days on a 12-processor workstation.

## Context

This work provides a microscopic explanation for the puzzling experimental observation at HRIBF (Kohley and Radha, 2012) that $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca shows enhanced sub-barrier fusion compared to $^{132}$Sn+$^{48}$Ca, contrary to geometric expectations. The DC-TDHF method extends prior applications to systems such as $^{132,124}$Sn+$^{96}$Zr (Oberacker and Umar, 2010), $^{132}$Sn+$^{64}$Ni (Oberacker and Umar, 2006, 2007), and calcium+calcium isotopes (Keser and Umar, 2012). The results demonstrate that a fully microscopic approach with no adjustable parameters can reproduce the data where phenomenological coupled-channels calculations with Woods-Saxon potentials (Kohley and Radha, 2012) underpredict cross sections by a factor of 10 or more. This enables future microscopic studies of sub-barrier fusion for heavy neutron-rich systems where empirical models fail.
