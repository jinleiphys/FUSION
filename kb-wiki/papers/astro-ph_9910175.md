# Sterile neutrinos and supernova nucleosynthesis

> A light sterile neutrino species has been introduced to explain simultaneously the solar and atmospheric neutrino puzzles and the results of the LSND experiment, while providing for a hot component of dark matter. Employing this scheme of neutrino masses and mixings, we show how matter-enhanced active-sterile neutrino transformation followed by active-active neutrino transformation can solve robustly the neutron deficit problem encountered by models of r-process nucleosynthesis associated with neutrino-heated supernova ejecta.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a specific four-neutrino mass scheme with a light sterile neutrino can solve the neutron deficit problem for r-process nucleosynthesis in neutrino-heated supernova ejecta, but only if the active-sterile and active-active neutrino transformations occur in the correct sequence and at the appropriate radii. The authors hedge that they "cannot definitively proclaim at this point that new neutrino physics is required" because it must first be established that r-process material originates in conventional neutrino-heated ejecta and that the late-stage neutrino-driven wind hydrodynamics is understood.

## Method

The authors adopt a simple exponential wind model for neutrino-driven outflow above a proto-neutron star and analyze how matter-enhanced active-sterile (νμ,τ ⇌ νs) neutrino transformation followed by active-active (νμ,τ ⇌ νe) neutrino transformation can alter the electron fraction Ye in the ejecta. They treat the neutrino flavor evolution through two separate mass-level crossings using a Schroedinger-like equation with vacuum masses, forward scattering on electrons and nucleons, and forward scattering on background neutrinos, requiring adiabatic conversion (γ > 3) for efficient flavor transformation. The analysis uses a specific four-neutrino mass scheme with two nearly degenerate doublets separated by a mass-squared difference compatible with LSND data, maximal νμ-ντ mixing for atmospheric neutrinos, and small vacuum mixing between the doublets.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-to-seed ratio R must exceed 100 for a good r-process yield of heavy nuclear species, but conventional models give smaller values.
- The gravitational binding energy per nucleon near the neutron star surface is approximately 100 MeV, while average νe and ν̄e energies are about 10 MeV, requiring a nucleon to interact with neutrinos about 10 times for ejection.
- For the νμ* ⇌ νs resonance to occur below the weak freeze-out radius (T9WFO ≈ 10), the required mass-squared difference is δm2μ*s ≳ 3.8 eV2 scaled to (T9WFO/10)3 (Eν/25 MeV) [(1-Ye)/2 / 0.3] S100-1.
- For the νμ* ⇌ νe resonance below weak freeze-out, δm2μ*e ≳ 5.1 eV2 scaled to (T9WFO/10)3 (Eν/25 MeV) (Ye/0.4) S100-1.
- A value of δm2μ*s ≈ δm2μ*e ≈ 6 eV2 is consistent with LSND data and fulfills the requirements.
- The resonance temperature for νμ* ⇌ νs is T9,μ*s ≈ 11.6 (δm2μ*s/6 eV2)1/3 (25 MeV/Eν)1/3 [0.3/((1-Ye)/2)]1/3 S1001/3.
- The resonance temperature for νμ* ⇌ νe is T9,μ*e ≈ 10.6 (δm2μ*e/6 eV2)1/3 (25 MeV/Eν)1/3 (0.4/Ye)1/3 S1001/3.
- The radial separation between the two resonances is typically 2 km, much larger than the resonance region widths of approximately 0.2 km each.
- Adiabatic conversion requires sin22θ ∼ 10-3 for the relevant mixing angles, corresponding to γ > 3 with γ ≈ 4.6 (δm2/6 eV2) (25 MeV/Eν) (sin22θ/10-3) (H/7.5 km).

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Fuller and Meyer (1995) and McLaughlin, Fuller, and Meyer (1998) regarding the alpha effect in neutrino-heated supernova ejecta, which causes a neutron deficit that prevents successful r-process nucleosynthesis. The paper introduces a specific sterile neutrino solution that builds on the four-neutrino mass scheme of Caldwell and Mohapatra (1993) and others, which simultaneously addresses solar and atmospheric neutrino puzzles and the LSND result. The scheme enables next steps in understanding whether neutrino-heated supernova ejecta can be the site of r-process nucleosynthesis, contingent on establishing that r-process material originates from such environments and that late-stage neutrino-driven wind hydrodynamics can be accurately modeled.
