# Modelling Hybrid Stars in Quark-Hadron Approaches

> The density in the core of neutron stars can reach values of about 5 to 10 times nuclear matter saturation density. It is, therefore, a natural assumption that hadrons may have dissolved into quarks under such conditions, forming a hybrid star. This star will have an outer region of hadronic matter and a core of quark matter or even a mixed state of hadrons and quarks. In order to investigate such phases, we discuss different model approaches that can be used in the study of compact stars as well as being applicable to a wider range of temperatures and densities. One major model ingredient, the role of quark interactions in the stability of massive hybrid stars is discussed. In this context, possible conflicts with lattice QCD simulations are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hybrid stars (neutron stars with a quark matter core) can be modeled using a unified approach that includes both hadronic and quark degrees of freedom, but the stability of such stars depends critically on the strength of quark interactions and the treatment of the phase transition; strong repulsive quark interactions can stabilize hybrid stars, while strong first-order phase transitions with large latent energies can lead to stellar instability.

## Method

The authors combine a chiral mean field (CMF) hadronic model based on a SU(3) extension of a sigma-omega chiral model with quark degrees of freedom, introducing an effective Polyakov loop field to describe the deconfinement transition. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations for static spherically symmetric stars using equations of state generated from these models, comparing Maxwell (local charge neutrality) and Gibbs (global charge neutrality) constructions for the phase transition. Additionally, they incorporate excluded volume corrections for hadrons and explore a parity-doublet extension that includes both positive and negative parity baryon states.

## Key numbers

- Maximum central densities in neutron stars reach about 5 to 10 times nuclear matter saturation density
- The hadronic star including hyperons has a maximum mass reduced by about 0.06 solar masses compared to nucleonic stars
- The maximum central density shifts from about 6 rho_0 to 4 rho_0 (in the Maxwell case) due to instability from the quark phase
- The reduction in maximum mass from the quark phase is only about 0.1 solar masses
- The mixed phase region from Gibbs construction can extend up to 2 km in the center of the star
- Excluded volume for baryons is v = 0.64 fm^3, with meson volume smaller by a factor a = 8
- Twin star solutions exist for specific quark interaction strengths, with equal mass stars of M = 1.68 solar masses showing distinctly different cooling curves
- The parity-doublet model exhibits a second first-order phase transition ending in a critical end-point around a temperature of 60 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral mean field model developed by Papazoglou (1998, 1999) and applies it to hybrid star calculations, building on earlier hybrid star studies by Glendenning (1991, 1992) and Alford (2005, 2013). The paper discusses potential conflicts with lattice QCD simulations by Borsanyi (2014) and Bazavov (2014), which show a smooth crossover at vanishing net baryon density, motivating the need for unified models rather than simple connections of separate hadronic and quark equations of state. The results enable future investigations of twin star solutions as a possible resolution to the "hyperon puzzle" and provide a framework for studying neutron star merger calculations that might sample conditions near the critical end-point.
