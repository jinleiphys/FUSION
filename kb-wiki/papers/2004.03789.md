# Do short-range correlations cause the nuclear EMC effect in the deuteron?

> The relative contributions to the valence nuclear EMC effect in the deuteron arising from off-shell effects and Fermi motion are examined in models which include nuclear binding and off-shell effects. Contrary to expectations, the effect of Fermi motion overwhelms the off-shell effects for nucleons in short-range correlations (SRCs), calling into question the hypothesized causal connection between SRCs and the EMC effect.

## Key claim

Contrary to the hypothesis that short-range correlations (SRCs) are the underlying cause of the nuclear EMC effect in the deuteron, the authors show that in all three microscopic models examined, the effect of Fermi motion overwhelms off-shell effects for nucleons in SRCs, and the high-momentum (p > p_F) region never contributes more than half of the total valence EMC effect.

## Method

The authors decompose the deuteron structure function into contributions from low-momentum (p < p_F) and high-momentum (p > p_F) regions using three established models: the relativistic spectator model (MST), the CJ15 global QCD analysis, and the Kulagin-Petti (KP) phenomenological analysis. They compute the ratio R = (F2^d - (F2^p + F2^n))/F2^d separately for each momentum region, using both the Paris and WJC-2 deuteron wave functions, and compare the relative contributions from SRC-associated high-momentum nucleons versus low-momentum nucleons.

## Key numbers

- For the Paris deuteron wave function with p_F = 300 MeV, the number of np pairs in SRCs is n_SRC^d = 3.4%; for the WJC-2 wave function it is 3.8%.
- The average nucleon virtuality is ⟨v⟩ = -4.3% for the Paris wave function and -5.0% for the WJC-2 wave function.
- When p_F is raised to 400 MeV, n_SRC^d decreases to 2.0% for the Paris wave function and 2.6% for the WJC-2 wave function.
- In the MST model, less than half of the EMC effect strength in the valence region (0.3 ≲ x ≲ 0.7) comes from the high-momentum p > p_F region.
- In the CJ15 model, essentially 100% of the total effect comes from the p < p_F region for x ≲ 0.5.
- In the KP model, the deviation from zero is greatest at x ≈ 0.6-0.7, where it is completely dominated by the low-momentum p < p_F contributions.

## Context

This work directly tests the hypothesis proposed by Schmookler et al. (2019) that SRCs may be the underlying source of the valence EMC effect, which was based on a correlation between the size of the EMC effect and the number of nucleons in SRCs. The authors use three established theoretical frameworks (MST from Melnitchouk et al. 1994, CJ15 from the CTEQ-JLab collaboration, and KP from Kulagin and Petti 2006) to show that the causal connection does not hold for the deuteron. The results enable future experimental tests using semi-inclusive DIS with tagged recoil protons and neutrons, as well as studies of asymmetric nuclei like 3He to probe isospin dependence of off-shell effects.
