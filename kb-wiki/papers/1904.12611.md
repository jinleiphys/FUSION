# Probing chiral interactions up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in medium-mass nuclei

> We study ground-state energies and charge radii of closed-shell medium-mass nuclei based on novel chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) interactions, with a focus on exploring the connections between finite nuclei and nuclear matter. To this end, we perform in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) calculations based on chiral interactions at next-to-leading order (NLO), N$^2$LO, and N$^3$LO, where the 3N interactions at N$^2$LO and N$^3$LO are fit to the empirical saturation point of nuclear matter and to the triton binding energy. Our results for energies and radii at N$^2$LO and N$^3$LO overlap within uncertainties, and the cutoff variation of the interactions is within the EFT uncertainty band. We find underbound ground-state energies, as expected from the comparison to the empirical saturation point. The radii are systematically too large, but the agreement with experiment is better. We further explore variations of the 3N couplings to test their sensitivity in nuclei. While nuclear matter at saturation density is quite sensitive to the 3N couplings, we find a considerably weaker dependence in medium-mass nuclei. In addition, we explore a consistent momentum-space SRG evolution of these NN and 3N interactions, exhibiting improved many-body convergence. For the SRG-evolved interactions, the sensitivity to the 3N couplings is found to be stronger in medium-mass nuclei.

# Probing chiral interactions up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in medium-mass nuclei

## Key claim

The first N3LO calculations of medium-mass nuclei using chiral NN+3N interactions show that ground-state energies are underbound and charge radii are systematically too large compared to experiment, with N2LO and N3LO results overlapping within uncertainties; however, the correlation between finite-nuclei observables and nuclear matter saturation properties is less direct than expected, as varying the 3N couplings produces much weaker sensitivity in medium-mass nuclei than in nuclear matter at saturation density.

## Method

The authors performed in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) calculations at the IM-SRG(2) truncation level for closed-shell oxygen, calcium, and nickel isotopes, using nonlocal chiral NN interactions from Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (EMN) at NLO, N2LO, and N3LO with cutoffs of 420, 450, and 500 MeV. The 3N interactions at N2LO and N3LO were fitted to the triton binding energy and the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter, with the 3N low-energy couplings cD and cE determined via many-body perturbation theory; additionally, consistent momentum-space SRG evolution of the NN and 3N interactions was explored for resolution scales of 1.8, 2.0, and 2.2 fm^-1.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 16O is underbound by about 30 MeV relative to experiment for the N3LO EMN 450 MeV interaction with consistent 3N forces.
- Ground-state energy of 56Ni is underbound by about 200 MeV relative to experiment for the same interaction.
- Charge radius of 16O is too large by about 0.2 fm for the N3LO EMN 450 MeV interaction.
- For 40Ca, the difference between calculated and experimental ground-state energy is about 2 MeV per nucleon for the N3LO EMN 450 and 500 MeV interactions.
- Varying the short-range 3N coupling cE by +0.7 changes the 40Ca ground-state energy by approximately 260 MeV and the charge radius by about 0.6 fm.
- The uncertainty due to the 3N cut E3Max is less than or equal to 0.1 MeV/A through 40Ca and increases up to about 0.5 MeV/A for 68Ni.
- The N3LO EMN 450 MeV interaction with cD = 5.0 and SRG evolution to lambda = 1.8 fm^-1 reproduces ground-state energies and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei in good agreement with experiment while still reproducing the experimental triton binding energy of -8.482 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral interactions in medium-mass nuclei to N3LO for the first time, building on the nuclear matter saturation studies of Drischer et al. (2017) using the EMN potentials of Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017). The results are compared to coupled-cluster calculations by Ekstrom et al. (2017) using Delta-full interactions at N2LO, which show better agreement with experiment. The findings highlight that fitting only to the empirical saturation point of nuclear matter may not be sufficient for accurate predictions in medium-mass nuclei, and that the connection between few-body systems, medium-mass nuclei, and nuclear matter requires further investigation for the development of next-generation chiral interactions.
