# Pions in Proton Structure and Everywhere Else

> The pion cloud is important in nuclear physics and in a variety of low-energy hadronic phenomena. We argue that it is natural to expect it to also be important in lepton-proton deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan studies of proton structure. We compute the necessary consequences of the pion cloud in connection with the recent SeaQuest data. The effects are detailed by using the exact kinematics of the experiment. Good agreement with the measurements is obtained. Thus the universality of pionic effects is understood.

## Key claim

The pion cloud of the nucleon provides a natural explanation for the flavor asymmetry of the proton sea, and the authors argue that pionic effects are universal across nuclear physics, low-energy hadronic phenomena, and high-energy probes of proton structure such as deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes. The claim is hedged by the assumption that pionic fluctuations are the only source of flavor asymmetry, with all quark-gluon processes treated as flavor independent.

## Method

The authors used a light-front perturbation theory approach with a Fock-state expansion of the proton wave function into bare nucleon, pion-nucleon, and pion-Delta components, derived from the leading-order chiral Lagrangian. They computed pion splitting functions using a four-dimensional formalism with vertex functions constrained by the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation and experimental axial form factor data, then convolved these with pion and baryon parton distribution functions evolved to the SeaQuest scale of Q² = 25.5 GeV². The calculations used two sets of pion PDFs (ASV and xFitter) and a bare proton sea from Holtmann et al. (1997), with coupling constants and cutoff parameters varied within physically motivated ranges.

## Key numbers

- The integrated asymmetry prediction: 0.098 ≤ D̄ − Ū ≤ 0.131, compared to the E866 experimental result of D̄ − Ū = 0.118 ± 0.012
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant range: lower limit 12.8 from the Goldberger-Treiman relation (g_A = 1.267 ± 0.04, M = 0.939 GeV, f_π = 92.6 MeV), upper limit 13.2 from scattering data
- The axial form factor dipole mass parameter: M_A = 1.03 ± 0.04 GeV, with the cutoff Λ = √3/2 M_A
- The πNΔ coupling constant range: g_{πΔ} = 1.5 g_{πN} (large N_C limit) to g_{πΔ} = 1.7 g_{πN} (upper limit), corresponding to (g_{πΔ}/g_{πN})² = 72/25
- The SeaQuest scale used for PDF evolution: Q² = 25.5 GeV²
- A 15% uncertainty in d̄,ū at x = 0.3 translates to 7% uncertainty in the ratio d̄/ū

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous pion cloud model (Alberg, 2017) by updating calculations to the exact kinematics of the recent SeaQuest experiment (Dove, 2021), which measured the d̄/ū ratio. The paper addresses long-standing ambiguities in earlier meson cloud calculations regarding vertex function dependence and relativistic invariance, resolving them through a four-dimensional formalism constrained by experimental data. The results enable future tests of whether the downturn in d̄/ū at high x, hinted at by SeaQuest, signals the increasing influence of the πΔ contribution, and provide a framework for understanding the non-perturbative origin of the proton sea as distinct from perturbative QCD evolution.
