# Isovector Effects in Neutron Stars, Radii and the GW170817 Constraint

> An isovector-scalar meson is incorporated self-consistently into the quark-meson coupling description of nuclear matter and its most prominent effects on the structure of neutron stars are investigated. The recent measurement of GW170817 is used to constrain the strength of the isovector-scalar channel. With the imminent measurements of the neutron star radii in the NICER mission it is particularly notable that the inclusion of the isovector-scalar force has a significant impact. Indeed, the effect of this interaction on the neutron star radii and masses is larger than the uncertainties introduced by variations in the parameters of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation, namely the density, binding energy per nucleon and the symmetry energy. In addition, since the analysis of GW170817 has provided constraints on the binary tidal deformability of merging neutron stars, the predictions for this parameter within the quark-meson coupling model are explored, as well as the moment of inertia and the quadrupole moment of slowly rotating neutron stars.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the isovector-scalar meson (δ) in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model has a larger effect on neutron star radii and masses than the uncertainties introduced by varying the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (density, binding energy per nucleon, and symmetry energy), and the GW170817 constraint suggests that within the QMC model the δ coupling strength is most likely \(G_\delta \lessapprox 6.0\ \text{fm}^2\).

## Method

The authors extended the QMC model, which describes baryons as MIT bags containing three confined valence quarks interacting via meson exchange, by adding the isovector-scalar meson \(a_0(980)\) (labeled δ) to the existing σ, ω, ρ, and π mesons. They solved the mean-field equations including full Fock terms from single-pion exchange, minimizing the energy density under constraints of charge neutrality, β-equilibrium, and baryon number conservation, then used the resulting equations of state to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- For neutron stars with gravitational masses around \(1.5\ M_\odot\), the inclusion of the isovector-scalar channel results in radius changes of the order of \(0.5\ \text{km}\).
- The maximum neutron star mass changes by less than \(0.15\ M_\odot\) when varying the δ coupling strength.
- The standard nuclear matter parameters used are: saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), binding energy per nucleon \(\mathcal{E} = -15.8\ \text{MeV}\), and symmetry energy \(S = 30\ \text{MeV}\).
- The central value of the δ-nucleon coupling is \(G_\delta = 3.0\ \text{fm}^2\), taken from the one-boson-exchange potential of Haidenbauer.
- The GW170817 constraint on binary tidal deformability is \(80 < \tilde{\Lambda} < 640\).
- For the standard fit, the slope of the symmetry energy is \(L_0 = 63.7\ \text{MeV}\) and the incompressibility is \(K_0 = 282\ \text{MeV}\).
- For stars with masses \(\geq 1.4\ M_\odot\), the crust contributes less than \(5\%\) of the total moment of inertia.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model of Rikovska Stone (2006) by adding the isovector-scalar δ meson with full Fock terms for the first time to predict properties of high-density matter in neutron stars. It builds on earlier QMC calculations by Santos (low-density instabilities) and Wang (finite nuclei), and on relativistic-mean-field studies by Kubis, Liu, Menezes, Roca-Maza, and Singh that explored the δ meson in the Hartree approximation. The results are consistent with the GW170817 constraints from Abbott (2018) and the moment-of-inertia analysis of Zhao and Lattimer (2018), and the model's predictions for radii can be tested by the upcoming NICER mission.
