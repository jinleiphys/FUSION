# Amplitude analysis of the $\overline{N}N \to K^-K^+$ reaction

> A simple partial wave amplitude analysis of $\overline{p}p \rightarrow K^-K^+$ has been performed for data in the range $p_{\rm lab}$ = 360 -- 1000 MeV/$c$. In this low momentum interval only partial wave amplitudes with $J$ equal to 0, 1 and 2 are needed to obtain a good fit to the experimental data. This maximal $J$ = 2 value is smaller than what is required for the data of the reaction $\overline{p}p \rightarrow π^-π^+$ in the same momentum interval.

## Key claim

The partial wave analysis of $\overline{p}p \rightarrow K^-K^+$ data in the range $p_{\rm lab}$ = 360–1000 MeV/$c$ requires only amplitudes with $J \leq 2$ for a good fit, which is fewer partial waves than needed for the $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$ reaction in the same momentum interval, consistent with the expectation that the $K^-K^+$ channel involves a smaller interaction volume due to the need to annihilate additional quark pairs and create strange quarks.

## Method

The authors performed a $\chi^2$ fit of partial wave amplitudes to the measured differential cross section $d\sigma/d\Omega$ and analyzing power $A_{on}$ for $\overline{p}p \rightarrow K^-K^+$ at ten antiproton laboratory momenta between 360 and 988 MeV/$c$. The two independent helicity amplitudes $f_{++}$ and $f_{+-}$ were expanded in spin-triplet partial wave amplitudes with total angular momentum $J$, parameterized by a magnitude $R_{LJ}$ and phase $\delta_{LJ}$ for each $LJ$ combination, with $J_{max}$ treated as a free parameter. Fits were performed separately at each energy without energy-smoothing, using the amplitude set from one energy as starting values for the neighboring energy.

## Key numbers

- The data span $p_{\rm lab}$ = 360–988 MeV/$c$ (ten momenta between 360 and 988 MeV/$c$).
- Fits with $J_{max} = 2$ are sufficient for all momenta; including $J = 3$ amplitudes improves $\chi^2$ only above $p_{\rm lab}$ = 886 MeV/$c$, and the improvement is marginal except at 988 MeV/$c$.
- For $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$, the preferred maximum angular momentum is $J_{max} = 3$ at all momenta in the same range.
- At the lowest antiproton momentum of 360 MeV/$c$, the kaon momentum is a factor 0.86 of the pion momentum in the corresponding $\pi^-\pi^+$ reaction.
- The $J = 2$ partial wave amplitudes give very significant contributions to the cross section at all momenta.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous partial wave analysis of $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$ (Kloet, 1994) to the $K^-K^+$ channel, using the same methodology. The finding that $J_{max} = 2$ suffices for $K^-K^+$ while $J_{max} = 3$ is needed for $\pi^-\pi^+$ is consistent with a simple diffractive scattering model from a black or grey sphere used at higher energies (Timmermans, Myhrer, and Kloet, 1994). The results suggest that future measurements closer to threshold and of spin-correlation observables $A_{ss}$ or $A_{ls}$ using polarized antiprotons would help resolve amplitude ambiguities and constrain models of the annihilation and hadronization process.
