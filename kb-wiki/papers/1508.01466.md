# Elastic and inelastic breakup of deuterons with energy below 100 MeV

> We present calculations of deuteron elastic and inelastic breakup cross sections and angular distributions at deuteron energies below 100 MeV obtained using the post-form DWBA approximation. The elastic breakup cross section was extensively studied in the past. Very few calculations of inelastic breakup have been performed, however. We also analyze the angular momentum - energy distributions of the cross section for formation of the compound nucleus after inelastic breakup.

## Key claim

The authors present calculations of deuteron elastic and inelastic breakup cross sections below 100 MeV using the post-form DWBA approximation, but the abstract glosses over that the calculations are performed in the zero-range limit with a finite-range correction and neglect spin-orbit coupling, which reduces the number of matrix elements by a factor of almost 12.

## Method

The authors used the zero-range post-form DWBA approximation to calculate elastic breakup cross sections and extended the formalism to absorption channels for inelastic breakup cross sections. They expanded wave functions and matrix elements in partial waves of orbital angular momentum alone, neglecting spin-orbit coupling, and used the Koning-Delaroche global optical potentials for proton and neutron channels and the Han (2006) potential for deuteron scattering. The numerical integration was performed by extending the integrals to the complex plane following the Vincent-Fortune (1970) method to handle conditional convergence of the elastic breakup matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Elastic and inelastic breakup spectra were calculated for 30 MeV deuterons incident on $^{48}$Ti and $^{181}$Ta; the breakup radius was found to be 10.6 fm for $^{48}$Ti and 14.6 fm for $^{181}$Ta, compared to strong interaction radii of 6.0 fm and 8.6 fm respectively.
- Inclusive double differential proton cross sections for 56 MeV deuterons on $^{27}$Al and $^{118}$Sn were compared with experimental data from Matsuoka (1980); the calculation for $^{118}$Sn underpredicted the spectrum by about 20% at 9.5 degrees and by a few percent at 13 degrees.
- For deuterons incident on $^{181}$Ta at 25.5 and 80 MeV, the deuteron absorption probability was reduced from near 1 below the grazing angular momentum to about 75% for low partial waves due to breakup; neutron and proton absorption probabilities from inelastic breakup were about 10% and 20% respectively in the nuclear interior.
- The zero-range normalization constant $D_0$ was taken as -125 MeV-fm$^{3/2}$.

## Context

This work extends the elastic deuteron breakup formalism developed by Baur (1976, 1984) and the inelastic breakup formalism of Kasano and Ichimura (1982) and Austern (1987) to a large selection of targets below 100 MeV. The calculations enable improved modeling of deuteron-induced reactions used for medical radioisotope production and as surrogates for other reactions, as reviewed by Escher (2012), but the authors caution that the well-defined structures in the energy-angular momentum distributions of compound nucleus formation from inelastic breakup could complicate their use as surrogates.
