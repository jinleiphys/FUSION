# Numerical modelling of bulk viscosity in neutron stars

> The early post-merger phase of a binary neutron-star coalescence is shaped by characteristic rotational velocities as well as violent density oscillations and offers the possibility to constrain the properties of neutron star matter by observing the gravitational wave emission. One possibility to do so is the investigation of gravitational wave damping through the bulk viscosity which originates from violations of weak chemical equilibrium. Motivated by these prospects, we present a comprehensive report about the implementation of the self-consistent and second-order formulation of the equations of relativistic hydrodynamics for dissipative fluids proposed by Müller, Israel and Stewart. Furthermore, we report on the results of two test problems, namely the viscous damping of linear density oscillations of isolated nonrotating neutron stars and the viscous migration test, both of which confirm our implementation and can be used for future code tests. Finally, we present fully general-relativistic simulations of viscous binary neutron-star mergers. We explore the structural and thermal properties of binary neutron-star mergers with a constant bulk-viscosity prescription and investigate the impact of bulk viscosity on dynamical mass ejection. We find that inverse Reynolds numbers $\sim 1\%$ can be achieved for the highest employed viscosity thereby suppressing the dynamically ejected mass by a factor of $\sim 5$ compared to the inviscid case.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that constant bulk viscosity in binary neutron-star merger simulations can suppress dynamically ejected mass by a factor of approximately 5 compared to the inviscid case, but only when inverse Reynolds numbers reach about 1% for the highest employed viscosity; the differences between viscous and inviscid binaries become significant only for the most extreme configurations.

## Method

The authors implement the second-order Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) formulation of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics for bulk viscosity in the FIL code, solving the Einstein equations alongside the viscous general-relativistic hydrodynamics equations. They use a constant bulk-viscosity prescription with a smooth cubic-polynomial transition zone between high and low densities, and test the implementation on two isolated neutron-star problems: viscous damping of linear density oscillations of perturbed Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) solutions and a viscous migration test. Finally, they perform fully general-relativistic simulations of binary neutron-star mergers with this constant bulk-viscosity model.

## Key numbers

* Inverse Reynolds numbers of approximately 1% are achieved for the highest employed viscosity in binary merger simulations.
* Dynamically ejected mass is suppressed by a factor of approximately 5 compared to the inviscid case for the highest viscosity.
* Four bulk-viscosity values are tested in the oscillating neutron-star test: zero, approximately 9.42 × 10^25 g s⁻¹ cm⁻¹ (low), approximately 1.98 × 10^26 g s⁻¹ cm⁻¹ (medium), and approximately 8.20 × 10^26 g s⁻¹ cm⁻¹ (high).
* The TOV star used in the oscillating test has mass 1.4 M⊙, radius 14.2 km, and central density 1.28 × 10⁻³ M⊙⁻² (approximately 7.91 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³).
* Four grid resolutions are used on the finest refinement level: approximately 207 m, 281 m, 369 m, and 487 m.
* The high-density threshold for bulk viscosity is set at ρ_h = 1.28 × 10⁻⁴ M⊙⁻² (approximately 7.91 × 10¹³ g cm⁻³) and the low-density threshold at ρ_l = 1.28 × 10⁻⁷ M⊙⁻² (approximately 7.91 × 10¹⁰ g cm⁻³).
* The relaxation time τ_h is set to approximately 1.1 times the minimum timestep Δt_min, and τ_l to approximately 1.1 times the maximum timestep Δt_max.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Chabanov 2023) by providing a comprehensive numerical methods report using a simplified constant bulk-viscosity model rather than the microphysically derived transport coefficients used in that earlier work. The paper builds on prior studies of bulk viscosity in neutron-star mergers by Alford (2010, 2018, 2020, 2021, 2022) and Most (2022), who estimated post-merger kHz-density oscillations to be damped over timescales less than 100 ms via URCA processes. The results enable future code tests and comparisons, as the two isolated neutron-star test problems (viscous damping of oscillations and viscous migration) can serve as standard benchmarks for other implementations of bulk viscosity in general-relativistic hydrodynamics codes.
