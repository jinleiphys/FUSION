# Systematic study of Charmonium production in pp collisions at the LHC energies

> We have performed a systematic study of $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ production in $p-p$ collisions at different LHC energies and at different rapidities using the leading order (LO) non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) model of heavy quarkonium production. We have included the contributions from $χ_{cJ}$ ($J$ = 0, 1, 2) and $ψ(2S)$ decays to $J/ψ$. The calculated values have been compared with the available data from the four experiments at LHC namely, ALICE, ATLAS, CMS and LHCb. In case of ALICE, inclusive $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ cross-sections have been calculated by including the feed-down from $B$ meson using Fixed-Order Next-to-Leading Logarithm (FONLL) formalism. It is found that all the experimental cross-sections are well reproduced for $p_T >$ 4 GeV within the theoretical uncertainties arising due to the choice of the factorization scale. We also predict the transverse momentum distributions of $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ both for the direct and feed-down processes at the upcoming LHC energies of $\sqrt{s} =$ 5.1 TeV and 13 TeV for the year 2015.

## Key claim

The leading-order NRQCD model, including feed-down from chi_cJ and psi(2S) decays and B-meson decays via FONLL, reproduces the measured J/psi and psi(2S) transverse momentum distributions from all four LHC experiments only for p_T > 4 GeV, with the dominant uncertainty coming from the choice of factorization scale.

## Method

The authors computed the differential cross-sections for J/psi and psi(2S) production in pp collisions using the leading-order NRQCD factorization formalism, summing contributions from color-singlet and color-octet channels with long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) taken from the literature. For ALICE data, inclusive cross-sections included B-meson feed-down calculated with the FONLL formalism. The calculations used CTEQ6M parton distribution functions, a charm quark mass of 1.4 GeV, and factorization/renormalization scales set to sqrt(p_T^2 + 4m_c^2), with uncertainty bands derived from varying this scale by factors of 0.5 and 2.

## Key numbers

* J/psi differential cross-sections at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV are well reproduced for p_T > 4 GeV across all rapidity ranges (|y| < 0.9, 0.9 < |y| < 2.4, |y| < 0.75, 0.75 < |y| < 2.4, 2 < y < 4.5, 2.5 < y < 4)
* psi(2S) differential cross-sections at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV are also well described for p_T > 4 GeV
* Inclusive J/psi at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV shows good agreement for p_T > 3 GeV
* The inclusive psi(2S)-to-J/psi ratio at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV is reproduced for p_T > 4 GeV, showing an increasing trend with p_T
* The dominant uncertainty from factorization scale variation is 45% when reduced by a factor of 2 and 30% when enhanced by a factor of 2
* Uncertainty from charm quark mass variation (1.2 to 1.6 GeV) is about 12%
* Uncertainty from PDF choice (CTEQ6M, CTEQ6L, CTEQ6L1) is within 10%
* Color-singlet LDME for J/psi: 1.2 GeV^3; for psi(2S): 0.76 GeV^3
* Color-octet LDME for J/psi (^3S_1^[8]): 0.0013 +/- 0.0013 GeV^3; for psi(2S) (^3S_1^[8]): 0.0033 +/- 0.00021 GeV^3

## Context

This work extends the LO NRQCD approach to charmonium production by systematically comparing with data from all four LHC experiments (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb) at multiple energies and rapidities, including feed-down from chi_cJ states and B mesons. The authors note that the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) predicts a p_T-independent psi(2S)-to-J/psi ratio, which contradicts the observed increasing trend, while their NRQCD calculation reproduces this behavior. The predictions at sqrt(s) = 5.1 TeV and 13 TeV are intended for future LHC runs and for normalization of Pb-Pb collision data. The authors acknowledge that low-p_T data (p_T < 4 GeV) require inclusion of Color Glass Condensate effects as demonstrated by Ma and Venugopalan (2014), and that fragmentation processes at high p_T may further improve the calculations.
