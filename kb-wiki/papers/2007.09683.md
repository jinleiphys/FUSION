# Confronting GW190814 with hyperonization in dense matter and hypernuclear compact stars

> We examine the possibility that the light companion in the highly asymmetric binary compact object coalescence event GW190814 is a hypernuclear star. We use density functional theory with functionals that have been tuned to the properties of $Λ$ hypernuclei as well as astrophysical constraints placed by the masses of the most massive millisecond pulsars, the mass-radius range inferred from the NICER experiment, and the binary neutron star merger event GW170817. We compute general-relativistic static and maximally rotating Keplerian configurations of purely nucleonic and hypernuclear stars. We find that while nucleonic stars are broadly consistent with a neutron star being involved in GW190814, this would imply no new degrees of freedom in the dense matter up to 6.5 times the nuclear saturation density. Allowing for hyperonization of dense matter, we find that the maximal masses of hypernuclear stars, even for maximal rapidly rotating configurations, are inconsistent with a stellar nature interpretation of the light companion in GW190814, implying that this event involved two black holes rather than a neutron star and a black hole.

## Key claim

While purely nucleonic stars are broadly consistent with the light companion in GW190814 being a neutron star (implying no new degrees of freedom up to 6.5 times nuclear saturation density), hypernuclear stars, even in maximally rotating Keplerian configurations, have maximum masses incompatible with the 2.50–2.67 solar mass range of the companion, implying the event involved two black holes rather than a neutron star and a black hole.

## Method

The authors used covariant density functional theory with two parametrizations (DDME2 and NL3) extended to the hypernuclear sector, with couplings tuned to Λ hypernuclear data and astrophysical constraints from massive pulsars, NICER, and GW170817. They solved the general-relativistic Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static configurations and used the RNS code to compute maximally rotating Keplerian configurations of both purely nucleonic and hypernuclear stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of static nucleonic stars: 2.48 solar masses (radius 12.1 km), marginally compatible with GW190814's light companion mass range of 2.50–2.67 solar masses
- Maximum mass of maximally rotating nucleonic stars: up to 3 solar masses, compatible with a neutron star in GW190814
- Maximum mass of static hypernuclear stars: approximately 2.0 solar masses, consistent with massive pulsar MSP J0740+6620 but inconsistent with GW190814
- Maximum mass of maximally rotating hypernuclear stars: less than or equal to 2.3 solar masses, insufficient to reach the 2.50 solar mass lower bound of GW190814
- Radius of a 1.33 solar mass nucleonic star: 13.3 km (consistent with NICER constraints)
- Nucleonic interpretation would require matter to maintain purely nucleonic degrees of freedom up to 6.5 times nuclear saturation density

## Context

This work extends studies of hyperonization in dense matter (Ambartsumyan 1960, Glendenning 1985) and recent covariant density functional models compatible with hypernuclear data and astrophysical constraints (Oertel 2015, Fortin 2016, Providencia 2019, Li 2018, Li 2019, Li 2020). It directly confronts the GW190814 event (Abbott 2020) with hypernuclear star models, contradicting proposals that the light companion could be a very massive neutron star composed of nucleonic matter (Most 2020, Fattoyev 2020, Tsokaros 2020, Zhang 2020, Tews 2020). The results enable future work on whether phase transitions to quark matter or Δ-resonance admixture could alter the conclusion, though the authors note Δ-resonances do not significantly increase maximum masses.
