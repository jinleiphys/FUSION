# Effects of the Symmetry energy slope on the exotic content of the neutron stars

> By varying the symmetry energy slope ($L$), I investigate how the exotic content within the interiors of neutron stars changes and how it affects both macroscopic and microscopic quantities. Using two different parametrizations (L3$ωρ$ and BigApple), and three different possibilities about the neutron star core (nucleons+hyperons, nucleons+deltas, nucleons+hyperons+deltas), I show that, for the models analyzed in this work, changing the slope barely changes the amount of hyperons, but it can strongly suppress the $Δ$ resonances for large values of $L$. I also show that, in general, the presence of exotic content will be more evident for lower values of $L$ than for large ones. Differences and similarities between the two parametrizations are also analyzed.

## Key claim

By varying the symmetry energy slope ($L$) from 44 MeV to 92 MeV within two parametrizations (L3ωρ and BigApple), the author shows that changing the slope barely changes the amount of hyperons but can strongly suppress Δ resonances for large values of $L$, with the caveat that these results are model-dependent and the BigApple parametrization is in tension with some astronomical constraints, particularly the dimensionless tidal parameter of the canonical star.

## Method

The author uses relativistic mean-field (quantum hadrodynamics) models with non-linear ω-ρ coupling to fix five saturation properties while varying the symmetry energy slope $L$ across four values (44, 60, 76, 92 MeV). Three compositions for the neutron star core are considered: nucleons+hyperons, nucleons+Δ resonances, and nucleons+hyperons+Δ resonances, with coupling constants fixed via SU(3) symmetry arguments and known potential depths ($U_Λ$ = -28 MeV, $U_Δ$ = -90 MeV). The equation of state is solved in mean-field approximation, and the TOV equations yield mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- For L3ωρ with $L$ = 44 MeV, the Λ⁰ threshold occurs around 0.43 fm⁻³, reaching a fraction $Y_Λ$ = 0.19 at 1.0 fm⁻³; for BigApple with the same $L$, Λ⁰ appears around 0.36 fm⁻³, reaching $Y_Λ$ = 0.22 at 1.0 fm⁻³
- For $L$ = 44 MeV, the Δ⁻ reaches a fraction of 0.01 at 0.27 fm⁻³ for BigApple and 0.32 fm⁻³ for L3ωρ
- The PSR J0740+6620 mass constraint ($M = 2.08 ± 0.07 M_⊙$) is satisfied for all models except pure nucleonic BigApple with $L$ = 92 MeV
- The strong NICER constraint (11.80 km < $R_{1.4}$ < 13.10 km) is satisfied for L3ωρ except at $L$ = 92 MeV; for BigApple it is satisfied only for $L$ = 44 and 60 MeV, and only with Δ resonances present
- The weak constraint ($R_{1.4}$ < 13.6 km) is satisfied for all models except BigApple with $L$ = 92 MeV

## Context

This work extends studies of the symmetry energy slope's influence on neutron star properties (Rafa 2011, Lopes 2024) by focusing specifically on exotic baryonic content. It builds on prior constraints from nuclear physics (Dutra 2014, Micaela 2017) that fixed five saturation properties while leaving $L$ debated, with PREX II suggesting 76 MeV < $L$ < 165 MeV and CREX pointing to 0 < $L$ < 51 MeV (Tagami 2022). The results enable future work to use neutron star observations, particularly NICER radius constraints and tidal deformability from GW170817, to narrow the allowed range of $L$ and determine which exotic degrees of freedom are viable in dense matter.
