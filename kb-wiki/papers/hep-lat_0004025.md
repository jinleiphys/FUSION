# Visualizations of the QCD Vacuum

> Action and topological charge densities of the Euclidean-space QCD vacuum are visualized in three-dimensional slices of a 24^3 x 36 space-time lattice and an order-a^2 improved 16^3 x 32 lattice. Features include instanton anti-instanton annihilation and a comparison of standard and over-improved actions used in the smoothing of the gauge fields.

# Visualizations of the QCD Vacuum

## Key claim
The visualizations reveal that the QCD vacuum contains rich, long-range non-perturbative correlations between strongly interacting instantons and anti-instantons, but the apparent density and size of these topological objects depends critically on the choice of cooling algorithm used to suppress quantum fluctuations.

## Method
The author generated pure-gauge QCD vacuum configurations on a 24³ × 36 lattice using the standard Wilson action at β = 6.0 (lattice spacing 0.1 fm) and on an O(a²)-improved 16³ × 32 lattice at β = 4.38 (lattice spacing 0.17 fm). Short-range perturbative fluctuations were removed via cooling, a local algorithm that minimizes the gauge action at each update, applied for 30 sweeps using both the standard one-loop Wilson action and a five-loop over-improved action designed to stabilize instantons over hundreds of sweeps. Three-dimensional slices of action density and topological charge density were visualized using isosurfaces and volume rendering.

## Key numbers
- 24³ × 36 space-time lattice with standard Wilson action at β = 6.0, providing a lattice spacing of 0.1 fm
- 16³ × 32 lattice with O(a²)-improved action at β = 4.38, providing a lattice spacing of 0.17 fm
- 30 cooling sweeps applied for the primary visualizations of action and topological charge density
- 100 cooling iterations after which instanton anti-instanton pairs in the process of annihilation had largely vanished from the region

## Context
This work extends the established cooling method (first cited as "cooling" in the paper) for removing quantum fluctuations from lattice QCD configurations while preserving topologically nontrivial field configurations. It directly compares the standard Wilson action with the five-loop over-improved action of De Forcrand et al., designed to eliminate O(a²) and O(a⁴) discretization errors and stabilize instantons. The visualizations address questions raised by Smith (1998) regarding polarization phenomena where large instantons tend to have the same sign and are over-screened by smaller instantons of opposite topological charge, and complement the work of Bonnet (2000) on microscopic effects of smoothing algorithms. The results enable future investigations into the true density of the QCD vacuum and the dynamics of instanton anti-instanton annihilation.
