# Role of $σ$ exchange in the $γp\to ϕp$ process and scaling with the $f_1$ axial vector meson from a Reggeized model

> We investigate the role driven by the scalar meson $σ$ exchange in the photoproduction of the vector meson $ϕ$(1020) off a proton by using a Reggeized model. Based on the $π^0(135)+σ(500)+f_2(1270)$+Pomeron exchanges, we demonstrate that the $σ$ exchange plays the role to reproduce the bump structure at the forward angle in the differential cross section as well as the peaking behavior in the total cross section observed in the CLAS Collaboration. We also discuss the possible observation of the scaled cross section $s^7dσ/dt$ at the production angle $θ=90^{\circ}$ from the CLAS data. It is found that the axial vector meson $f_1(1285)$ exchange with the trajectory $α_{f_1}(t)=0.028\,t+0.9\pm0.2$ arising from the axial anomaly of the QCD vacuum plays the role to clarify the scaling up to 5 GeV.

# Role of σ exchange in the γp → φp process and scaling with the f₁ axial vector meson from a Reggeized model

## Key claim
The scalar σ(500) meson exchange, usually excluded due to uncertainty in its coupling strength, is essential to reproduce the bump structure in the forward-angle differential cross section and the peaking behavior in the total cross section near threshold observed by the CLAS Collaboration. The axial vector meson f₁(1285) exchange, with a trajectory arising from the axial anomaly of the QCD vacuum, clarifies the scaling behavior of the scaled cross section s⁷dσ/dt at θ = 90° up to 5 GeV, but only when the σ exchange is also included to form the bump below √s ≈ 3 GeV.

## Method
The authors construct a Reggeized amplitude for γp → φp consisting of Pomeron, f₂(1270) tensor meson, σ(500) scalar meson, and π⁰(135) exchanges, with coupling constants determined from decay widths and the QCD sum rule. They then add the f₁(1285) axial vector meson exchange with a trajectory α_{f₁}(t) = 0.028t + 0.9 to the model and compute differential cross sections, total cross sections, and the scaled cross section s⁷dσ/dt at the production angle θ = 90° in the center-of-mass frame, comparing with CLAS and LEPS data from threshold up to √s = 100 GeV.

## Key numbers
- σ meson mass: m_σ = 500 MeV; σNN coupling constant: g_{σNN} = 14.6 (from QCD sum rule); γσφ coupling: g_{γσφ} = 0.085 (intermediate value between 0.031 from Γ_{φ→ππγ} ≈ 0.48 keV and 0.146 from chiral effective Lagrangian prediction)
- π⁰ mass: m_π = 134.9766 MeV; πNN coupling: g_{πNN} = 13.4; γπφ coupling: g_{γπφ} = 0.065 (from Γ_{φ→πγ} = 5.42 keV)
- f₂ mass: m_{f₂} = 1275.1 MeV; γf₂φ coupling: g_{γf₂φ} = 0.0173 (from Γ_{f₂→φγ} = 1.3 keV); f₂NN couplings: g^{(1)}_{f₂NN} = 6.45, g^{(2)}_{f₂NN} = 0
- Pomeron trajectory: α_ℙ(t) = 0.25t + 1.08; quark couplings: β_u = β_d = 2.07 GeV⁻¹, β_s = 1.60 GeV⁻¹; decay constant f_φ = -13.4; μ₀² = 1.1 GeV²
- f₁(1285) trajectory: α_{f₁}(t) = 0.028t + 0.9 (intercept within 0.9 ± 0.2 from Kochelev); γf₁φ coupling: g_{γf₁φ} = 0.18 (from Γ_{f₁→φγ} = 0.019 MeV); f₁NN coupling: g_{f₁NN} = 2.5; nucleon axial cutoff mass: m_A = 1.08 GeV
- Scaled cross section value: s⁷dσ/dt ≈ 0.1 [10⁷ GeV¹² nb] at the scaling plateau
- The σ exchange produces nodes in the scaled cross section at √s ≈ 2.3 and 3.3 GeV from the vanishing of the canonical phase condition α_σ(t) = -1, -3

## Context
This work extends previous Reggeized model studies of φ photoproduction that used π, η, and Pomeron exchanges (Titov, 2007; Kiswandhi, 2011; Ozaki, 2010; Ryu, 2012) by adding the σ exchange, which was previously shown to be crucial for the γp → ρ⁰p process (Yu, 2012). The authors contrast their n = 9 constituent counting rule scaling (Brodsky, 1973) with the s¹²-like scaling (n = 14) proposed by Dey (2014) for the same process, where the latter predicts scaling below √s ≈ 3 GeV while the present model predicts scaling above 3 GeV. The f₁ exchange trajectory is taken from Kochelev (1999) who related it to the axial anomaly of the QCD vacuum via two-gluon exchange. The results enable future experimental tests at LEPS2 at SPring-8 and CLAS12 at JLab to explore quark-gluon dynamics in the scaling region above 2.8 GeV.
