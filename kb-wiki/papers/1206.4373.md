# Hadron Yield Correlation in Combination Models in High Energy AA Collisions

> We study the hadron yield correlation in the combination models in high energy heavy ion collisions. We derive the relationship between the average yields of different hadrons produced in the combination of a system consisting of equal number of quarks and antiquarks. We present the results for the directly produced hadrons as well as those for the final hadrons including the strong and electromagnetic decay contributions. We also study the net quark influence by considering the case when the number of quarks is larger than that of the antiquarks. We make comparison with the data wherever possible.

## Key claim

The paper derives general, model-independent relationships between average hadron yields in quark combination models for high-energy heavy-ion collisions, showing that under the assumptions of factorized flavor and momentum dependence and flavor-independent joint momentum distributions, the ratios of directly produced mesons, baryons, and antibaryons become constants depending only on the strangeness suppression factors and the vector/pseudoscalar and octet/decuplet ratios, independent of the total number of quarks and antiquarks when net quark contributions are negligible.

## Method

The authors start from the general formalism for hadron yields in combination models, where mesons form from quark-antiquark pairs and baryons from three-quark systems, and then impose two key simplifications: factorization of flavor and momentum dependences in the combination kernel functions, and flavor independence of the normalized joint momentum distributions of quarks and antiquarks. They average over multinomial flavor distributions for u, d, s quarks and antiquarks, and include strong and electromagnetic decay contributions from short-lived hadrons to obtain final-state yields, using parameters R_V/P (vector-to-pseudoscalar meson ratio) and R_O/D (octet-to-decuplet baryon ratio) taken from prior data.

## Key numbers

* R_V/P = 3 is taken according to spin counting, and R_O/D = 2 is used based on observed suppression of decuplet baryon production (Anisovich 1973, Shao 2005).
* For final-state protons with no net quarks: ⟨N_p^f⟩ = 4/(2+λ)^3 ⟨N_B⟩, where λ is the strangeness suppression factor for antiquarks.
* For final-state Λ with R_V/P=3 and R_O/D=2: ⟨N_Λ^f⟩ = 7.74 p_u^2 p_s ⟨N_B⟩.
* For final-state K⁺ with R_V/P=3: ⟨N_K⁺^f⟩ = (p_u p_¯s + 0.37 p_s p_¯s) ⟨N_M⟩.
* For Ω⁻: directly produced yield is p_s³ ⟨N_B⟩, unchanged by decays, giving λ³/(2+λ)³ ⟨N_B⟩ when net quarks are zero.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of hadron yield correlations from combination models, building on earlier coalescence models (Zimanyi 2000, Greco 2003), recombination models (Hwa 2003, Fries 2003), and the quark combination model (Shao 2005, Xie 1988) by providing a framework that is as independent of specific model assumptions as possible. The derived constant ratios for the net-quark-free case provide testable predictions for LHC central rapidity data (e.g., from ALICE), while the inclusion of net quark effects allows comparison with RHIC and SPS data where the number of quarks exceeds that of antiquarks, enabling a probe of whether the combination mechanism signals the formation of a deconfined quark matter system before hadronization.
