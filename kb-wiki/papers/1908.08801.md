# Semirelativistic Bound States: (Pseudo-) Spinless-Salpeter Approaches Reassessed

> Relativistic quantum field theory offers, in form of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter framework, a (Poincaré-covariant) description of bound states in terms of their underlying theory's fundamental degrees of freedom. In view of the intrinsic complexity of this approach, simplifications have been sought and abundantly found. The significance of these latter approximations may be estimated by comparing their predictions with (easily inferable) rigorous constraints on the bound-state spectra, such as existence, number and location of discrete eigenstates. The application of these techniques to selected proposed bound-state equations is exemplified for a large class of generalizations of the Hellmann potential frequently employed in several areas of science such as physics and chemistry.

## Key claim
The paper reassesses the validity of various semirelativistic bound-state approximations derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation, showing that many "pseudo-spinless-Salpeter" approaches fail rigorous scrutiny, while rigorous constraints on the spectrum (boundedness, number, and location of eigenvalues) can be derived for generalized Hellmann potentials.

## Method
The author applies rigorous spectral analysis to the spinless Salpeter Hamiltonian \(H = 2\sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2 + m^2} + V(r)\) for generalized Hellmann potentials \(V_{\rm H}(r) = -\kappa/r - \ups \exp(-b r)/r\). Boundedness from below is established via comparison with relativistic Coulomb bounds and trial-state expectation values. Variational upper bounds on discrete eigenvalues are computed using a basis of generalized Laguerre polynomials and spherical harmonics, and the relativistic virial theorem is used to assess the quality of approximate eigenstates.

## Key numbers
- For the relativistic Coulomb problem, the spectrum is bounded from below by \(2m \sqrt{1 - (\pi \alpha / 4)^2}\) if and only if \(\alpha \le 4/\pi = 1.273239\dots\), and by \(2m \sqrt{(1 + \sqrt{1 - \alpha^2})/2}\) for \(\alpha \le 1\).
- The coupling condition for boundedness of the Hellmann Hamiltonian is \(\kappa + \ups \le 16/(3\pi) = 1.69765\dots\) for \(\ups > 0\), and \(\kappa \le 16/(3\pi) + |\ups|\) for \(-\kappa < \ups < 0\).
- Upper bounds on binding energies \(B_k = E_k - 2m\) (in units of \(m\)) for the ground state (\(n_r=0, \ell=0\)) are: \(-0.11673\) for \(\kappa = \ups = 1/2\); \(-0.17951\) for \(\kappa=1, \ups=-1\); \(-0.14410\) for \(\kappa=1, \ups=-2\), all with Yukawa range \(b=m\).
- Lower bounds on binding energies for the same three cases are \(-0.58578\dots\), \(-1\), and \(-0.37336\dots\) (in units of \(m\)), respectively.

## Context
This work extends rigorous spectral methods previously applied to Woods-Saxon (Lucha 2018), Hulthen (Lucha 2018), Yukawa (Lucha 2017), and kink-like potentials (Lucha 2018) to the class of generalized Hellmann potentials, which combine Coulomb and Yukawa terms. The results enable validation of approximate semirelativistic bound-state calculations and provide benchmarks for testing new variational or numerical methods. The paper also notes that the number of bound states for generalized Hellmann potentials is infinite due to their Coulombic tail, unlike potentials in \(L^{3/2}(\mathbb{R}^3) \cap L^3(\mathbb{R}^3)\) which have finite bound-state counts.
