# Photoproduction of $η$-mesons off protons and deuterons

> An effective field theory with hadrons and photons is constructed, where the hadronic parameters are determined by fitting elastic $π$N scattering data. The predicted pion-induced $η$-production on the nucleon agrees well with the data. The hadronic model is then used to describe the final-state interaction in photoinduced processes. We present a consistent description of pion photoproduction in the E$_0^+$-channel and of the total cross sections for photoinduced $η$-production on the proton as well as on the deuteron.

# Photoproduction of $\eta$-mesons off protons and deuterons

## Key claim
The authors present a unitary and gauge-invariant effective field theory for photoproduction of $\eta$-mesons where the hadronic parameters are fixed by elastic $\pi$N scattering data, and they claim a consistent description of total cross sections for both proton and deuteron targets. However, the agreement for the deuteron target shows a deviation near threshold, and the model requires inclusion of the S$_{11}$(1650) resonance and vector-meson exchange contributions to reproduce the data, contrary to models that ignore the second resonance.

## Method
The authors construct an effective field theory with hadrons and photons, determining hadronic coupling constants and resonance masses by fitting elastic $\pi$N scattering data. They use a K-matrix approach including three coupled channels ($\pi$N, $\eta$N, and an effective $\pi\pi$N channel parametrized by a scalar field $\zeta$) with two S$_{11}$ resonances (1535 and 1650). For photoproduction, they add non-resonant Born terms from the linear sigma model, vector-meson exchange contributions, and a contact interaction proportional to the nucleon anomalous magnetic moment, then compute total cross sections for $\gamma$p$\rightarrow\eta$p and $\gamma$d$\rightarrow\eta$d in the impulse approximation.

## Key numbers
- Helicity amplitudes for S$_{11}$(1535): $A_{1/2}^p = 102 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$, $A_{1/2}^n = -82 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$
- Helicity amplitudes for S$_{11}$(1650): $A_{1/2}^p = 83 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$, $A_{1/2}^n = -24 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$
- The total cross section for $\gamma$p$\rightarrow\eta$p is shown as a function of energy (figure 2, left panel), with data from Krusche (1995) and Wilhelm (1995)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous hadronic model for $\pi$N scattering and pion-induced $\eta$-production (Sauermann, Friman, and Nörenberg, 1995) to electromagnetic processes. It contrasts with models that ignore the S$_{11}$(1650) resonance, such as those by Tiator (1995), and with tree-level calculations by Mukhopadhyay (1995) that cannot capture the coupled-channel effects. The model enables future extension to include P$_{11}$ and D$_{13}$ channels for describing differential cross sections, as noted by Tiator (1995) and Krusche (1995).
