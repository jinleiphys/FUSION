# Neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb, $^{116,120,124}$Sn, and $^{40}$Ca determined from reaction cross sections of $^{4}$He scattering

> [Background] We constructed the Kyushu chiral $g$-matrix and confirmed its reliability at $30 \lesssim E_{\rm in} \lesssim 100 $~MeV and $250 \lesssim E_{\rm in} \lesssim 400$~MeV for $^{12}$C scattering. Reaction cross section data of $^{4}$He scattering are available for some nuclides including $^{208}$Pb. PREX II collaboration reported a thick neutron skin for $^{208}$Pb. [Purpose] Our purpose is to deduce neutron skin thicknesses of $^{208}$Pb and some other nuclides from reaction cross sections calculated in terms of the double folding model with the $g$-matrix. [Methods] We fold the $g$-matrix and densities given by mean field calculations. In order to remedy less-constrainedness of the neutron sector, we renormalize densities so as to reproduce the observed cross sections. [Results] We found that a 3.4~$\%$ renormalization is necessary for $^{208}$Pb. The neutron density obtained from renormalization results in $R_{\rm skin}=$ 0.416$\pm$0.146 fm by confronting the precision proton radius. [Conclusions] Our result is consistent with PREX II and therefore supports larger slope parameter $L$. Results for $^{40}$Ca and $^{124}$Sn are also consistent with $R_{\rm skin}$ deduced from other experiments. For $^{116,120}$Sn the present method gives thicker skins.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb deduced from reaction cross sections of $^{4}$He scattering using the double folding model with the Kyushu chiral $g$-matrix and renormalized mean-field densities is $0.416\pm0.146$ fm, which is consistent with the PREX II result of $0.283\pm0.071$ fm within the large uncertainty, supporting a stiffer equation of state with a larger slope parameter $L$.

## Method

The authors performed double folding model calculations of reaction cross sections for $^{4}$He scattering on $^{208}$Pb, $^{116,120,124}$Sn, and $^{40}$Ca using the Kyushu chiral $g$-matrix as the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. Target densities were obtained from Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov with angular-momentum projection and Skyrme-HFB (SLy7 parameter set) mean-field calculations. To remedy the less-constrained neutron sector, the authors renormalized the densities by scaling the proton density to match the experimental proton radius from electron scattering and scaling the neutron density to reproduce the observed reaction cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}$Pb: calculated reaction cross sections are 96.6% of the experimental data in average (29.3 to 85 MeV incident energy range), requiring a 3.4% renormalization
- $^{208}$Pb neutron skin thickness from $^{4}$He scattering: $R_{\rm skin}=0.416\pm0.146$ fm (compared to PREX II: $0.283\pm0.071$ fm)
- $^{208}$Pb neutron radius from renormalized densities: $R_{\rm n}=5.860\pm0.146$ fm; matter radius: $R_{\rm m}=5.700\pm0.146$ fm (proton radius fixed at $R_{\rm p}=5.444$ fm)
- $^{120}$Sn neutron skin thickness from renormalization: $R_{\rm skin}=0.377\pm0.140$ fm (compared to Krasznahorkay: $0.18\pm0.07$ fm and Hashimoto: $0.148\pm0.034$ fm)
- $^{116}$Sn neutron skin thickness from renormalization: $R_{\rm skin}=0.242\pm0.140$ fm (compared to Krasznahorkay: $0.12\pm0.06$ fm)
- $^{124}$Sn neutron skin thickness from renormalization: $R_{\rm skin}=0.180\pm0.142$ fm (compared to Krasznahorkay: $0.19\pm0.07$ fm)
- $^{40}$Ca neutron skin thickness from renormalization: $R_{\rm skin}=-0.035\pm0.075$ fm (compared to Zenihiro: $-0.010^{+0.022}_{-0.024}$ fm)
- Using the relation $R_{\rm skin}^{208}=0.101+0.00147L$, the present result gives $L=115-313.6$ MeV, while PREX II gives $L=75.5-172.1$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous single-folding model analysis of p + $^{208}$Pb scattering (Tagami, 2020) to $^{4}$He scattering and lighter nuclei, using the same Kyushu chiral $g$-matrix approach. The results are consistent with the PREX II parity-violating electron scattering measurement (Adhikari, 2021) for $^{208}$Pb, supporting a stiffer equation of state and excluding the APR equation of state (Akmal, 1998) which predicts $R_{\rm skin}^{208}=0.16$ fm. For $^{116,120}$Sn the method gives thicker skins than those deduced from dipole resonance measurements (Krasznahorkay, 1999; Hashimoto, 2015), suggesting possible errors in the reaction cross section data for these isotopes. The method works well for $^{40}$Ca where mean-field parameters are better determined and the skin thickness is near zero.
