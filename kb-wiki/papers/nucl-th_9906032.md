# Effective Theory of the Triton

> We apply the effective field theory approach to the three-nucleon system. In particular, we consider S=1/2 neutron-deuteron scattering and the triton. We show that in this channel a unique nonperturbative renormalization takes place which requires the introduction of a single three-body force at leading order. With one fitted parameter we find a good description of low-energy data. Invariance under the renormalization group explains some universal features of the three-nucleon system ---such as the Thomas and Efimov effects and the Phillips line--- and the origin of SU(4) symmetry in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the spin-1/2 channel of neutron-deuteron scattering, a nonperturbative renormalization occurs that requires a single three-body force at leading order, contrary to naive dimensional analysis which would suggest three-body forces appear only in higher orders. With one fitted parameter, the effective field theory describes low-energy data, but the authors note that range corrections are expected to improve agreement and that definite conclusions require their inclusion.

## Method

The authors apply a pionless effective field theory to the three-nucleon system, using dibaryon fields to represent two-nucleon interactions in the \(^3S_1\) and \(^1S_0\) channels. They solve coupled integral equations for S-wave neutron-deuteron scattering in the spin-1/2 channel, introducing a momentum cutoff and a three-body force counterterm \(H(\Lambda)/\Lambda^2\) that runs with the cutoff to achieve renormalization. The three-body force parameter \(\Lambda_*\) is fixed by fitting to the experimental spin-1/2 neutron-deuteron scattering length.

## Key numbers

* Neutron-deuteron scattering length in the spin-1/2 channel: \(a_3^{(1/2)} = (0.65 \pm 0.04)\ \text{fm}\) (experimental value used to fix the three-body force parameter)
* Three-body force parameter determined from fit: \(\Lambda_* = 0.9\ \text{fm}^{-1}\)
* Triton binding energy predicted by the leading-order theory: \(B_3 = 8.0\ \text{MeV}\), compared to the experimental value \(B_3^{exp} = 8.48\ \text{MeV}\)
* Universal constant \(s_0 \approx 1.0064\) appearing in the renormalization group running of the three-body force
* Cutoff values used in calculations: \(\Lambda = 1.9,\ 6.0,\ 11.6\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (for cutoff-independent results) and \(\Lambda = 1.0,\ 2.0,\ 3.0 \times 10^4 a_2^{-1}\) (for demonstrating cutoff dependence)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach from the two-nucleon system to the three-nucleon system, building on the KSW counting scheme for nucleon-nucleon scattering (Weinberg, 1990s) and the authors' previous work on the spin-3/2 channel (Bedaque, Hammer, van Kolck, 1998). The paper provides a unified explanation for several universal features of three-body systems previously studied in potential models: the Thomas effect (Thomas, 1935), the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970), and the Phillips line (Phillips, 1968). The results enable future applications to polarization observables in neutron-deuteron scattering, triton charge form factors, and extension to proton-deuteron scattering and \(^3\)He physics through incorporation of the Coulomb force.
