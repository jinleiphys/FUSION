# NNLO Calculation of Two-Nucleon Scattering in EFT for a Two Yukawa Toy Model

> The effective field theory (EFT) for a toy model of nucleons interacting via a short range and a long range Yukawa potential is presented. The scattering amplitude in the 1S0 channel is calculated up to NNLO using KSW power counting scheme. A particular expansion of the amplitude about the pole at low (imaginary) momentum is used to derive matching conditions for the EFT couplings. In addition, after imposing constraints from a Renormalization Group analysis, there are no new free parameters in the amplitude at NNLO. Comparing the NNLO phase shifts to the full model we show that the EFT expansion is converging.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a toy model with two Yukawa potentials (representing pion and rho meson exchange), the KSW power counting scheme for effective field theory can reproduce the exact phase shifts up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) with no new free parameters at NNLO after imposing constraints from matching conditions and renormalization group flow analysis, though the authors note this toy model omits retardation effects from pion exchange and relativistic corrections that would appear in real nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for non-relativistic nucleons interacting via two instantaneous Yukawa potentials with masses m_pi = 140 MeV and m_rho = 770 MeV, using the KSW power counting scheme with power divergence subtraction (PDS) regularization. They calculate the 1S0 scattering amplitude up to NNLO by expanding about the pole at low imaginary momentum (p = i*gamma) rather than about p = 0, and derive matching conditions that fix sub-leading couplings by requiring the pole position and effective range parameters remain unchanged at higher orders. The renormalization group flow equations are then used to determine the remaining couplings, leaving no free parameters at NNLO.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass M = 940 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 140 MeV, rho mass m_rho = 770 MeV, pion decay constant f_pi = 132 MeV, axial coupling g_A = 1.25
- g_rho = 13.5 tuned to give scattering length a = -24.7 fm
- The effective theory without pions describes the exact phase shift well up to p ~ 50 MeV, with errors from different orders becoming comparable at p ~ 200 MeV
- The loop integral L in PDS scheme gives X_sub = mu, with mu ~ p as the subtraction scale

## Context

This work extends the KSW power counting scheme introduced by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for nucleon-nucleon scattering to NNLO in a simplified toy model, building on their earlier LO and NLO calculations. The expansion about the pole at p = i*gamma (following Bethe's approach) avoids the breakdown of the naive Taylor expansion near the pole that occurs at p ~ 1/a. The paper enables future NNLO calculations in real nucleon-nucleon scattering by demonstrating that matching conditions and RG analysis can eliminate free parameters, though the authors note that the real case would additionally require including radiation pion effects and relativistic corrections.
