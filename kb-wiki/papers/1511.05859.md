# Strangeness S=-2 baryon-baryon interaction at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory

> The strangeness $S=-2$ baryon-baryon interaction is studied in chiral effective field theory up to next-to-leading order. The potential at this order consists of contributions from one- and two-pseudoscalar-meson exchange diagrams and from four-baryon contact terms without and with two derivatives. SU(3) flavor symmetry is imposed for constructing the interaction in the $S=-2$ sector. Specifically, the couplings of the pseudoscalar mesons to the baryons are fixed by SU(3) symmetry and, in general, also the contact terms are related via SU(3) symmetry to those determined in a previous study of the $S=-1$ hyperon-nucleon interaction. The explicit SU(3) symmetry breaking due to the physical masses of the pseudoscalar mesons ($π$, $K$, $η$) is taken into account. It is argued that the $ΞN$ interaction has to be relatively weak to be in accordance with available experimental constraints. In particular, the published values and upper bounds for the $Ξ^- p$ elastic and inelastic cross sections apparently rule out a somewhat stronger attractive $ΞN$ force and, specifically, disfavor any near-threshold deuteron-like bound states in that system.

## Key claim

The strangeness S=-2 baryon-baryon interaction, constructed at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory with SU(3) flavor symmetry, yields a relatively weak ΞN interaction that is consistent with available experimental upper bounds for cross sections and disfavors any near-threshold deuteron-like bound states in that system.

## Method

The chiral potential at next-to-leading order was constructed from one- and two-pseudoscalar-meson exchange diagrams and four-baryon contact terms without derivatives and with two derivatives, with SU(3) flavor symmetry imposed for the couplings and contact terms. The low-energy constants for the S=-2 sector were taken from a previous study of the S=-1 hyperon-nucleon interaction (Haidenbauer 2013), with some readjustments to satisfy experimental constraints, and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation was solved in the particle basis with a regulator cutoff between 500 and 650 MeV.

## Key numbers

- ΛΛ ¹S₀ scattering length: a = -0.62 to -0.70 fm (NLO, cutoff 500-650 MeV), within the range of -1.3 to -0.5 fm implied by studies of double-Λ hypernuclei
- ΛΛ ¹S₀ effective range: r = 4.56 to 6.95 fm (NLO, cutoff 500-650 MeV)
- Σ⁺Σ⁺ ¹S₀ scattering length: a = -1.82 to -2.19 fm (NLO, cutoff 500-650 MeV)
- Σ⁺Σ⁺ ¹S₀ effective range: r = 5.67 to 6.05 fm (NLO, cutoff 500-650 MeV)
- Upper limit for Ξ⁻p elastic cross section: 24 mb at p_Ξ⁻ = 0.2-0.8 GeV/c (from Ahn 2006)
- Upper limit for Ξ⁻p → ΛΛ cross section: 12 mb at p_Ξ⁻ = 0.2-0.8 GeV/c (from Ahn 2006)
- Measured cross section for Ξ⁻p → ΛΛ: 4.3^{+6.3}_{-2.7} mb (from Ahn 2006) and 4.19 ± 1.9 mb (from Kim 2015)
- Inelastic cross section for Ξ⁻N in nucleus: 12.7^{+3.5}_{-3.1} mb at p_Ξ⁻ = 0.4-0.6 GeV/c (from Aoki 1998)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous leading-order study (Polinder 2007) of the S=-2 baryon-baryon interaction to next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, following the same framework successfully applied to the nucleon-nucleon system by Epelbaum and by Entem and Machleidt, and to the hyperon-nucleon sector by Haidenbauer (2013). The results confirm the weak ΞN interaction already predicted by the leading-order interaction and by several phenomenological models (Stoks 1999, Fujiwara 2006, Gasparyan 2011), and provide a chiral EFT framework for analyzing future scattering data once available. The interaction is relevant for understanding the hyperon puzzle in neutron star physics and for interpreting evidence for Ξ hypernuclei.
