# Charge density of light exotic nuclei and $ρNN$ tensor coupling

> We use a relativistic mean field model to study the charge density distributions of exotic oxygen isotopes. Nonlinear isoscalar-isovector terms which are not constrained by the present data are considered and the $ρNN$ tensor coupling that does not affect the symmetry energy in the mean field model is included. Strong correlations between the neutron radius of $^{208}$Pb and the charge radius of $^{12,13,23,24}$O are found. The $ρNN$ tensor coupling explicitly enhances the radius correlations between $^{23,24}$O and $^{208}$Pb. This enhancement is due to the neutron occupation of the orbital $2s_{1/2}$. The charge radius is sensitive to the change of the density dependence of the symmetry energy as the isotope goes towards the proton drip line.

## Key claim

The paper finds that strong correlations exist between the neutron radius of $^{208}$Pb and the charge radius of $^{12,13,23,24}$O, and that including the $\rho NN$ tensor coupling explicitly enhances the radius correlations for $^{23,24}$O due to neutron occupation of the $2s_{1/2}$ orbital; however, this enhancement is conditional on the specific structure of these isotopes and does not occur for isotopes with more neutrons or for proton-rich isotopes.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the NL3 parameter set, including nonlinear isoscalar-isovector coupling terms (parameter $\Lambda_v$) and the $\rho NN$ tensor coupling (with $f_\rho/g_\rho = 6.1$). They calculate ground-state properties of oxygen isotopes ($^{12,13,22,23,24}$O) and $^{208}$Pb, readjusting the $\rho NN$ coupling constant $g_\rho$ for each $\Lambda_v$ to keep the average symmetry energy fixed at 25.68 at $k_F = 1.15$ fm$^{-1}$. Pairing correlations are included using BCS theory with constant pairing gaps from the Möller-Nix prescription.

## Key numbers

- The experimental binding energy of $^{24}$O is 168.48 MeV; calculated values range from 167.72 MeV (with $\Lambda_v = 0.030$, $f_\rho = 0$) to 172.64 MeV (with $\Lambda_v = 0.000$, $f_\rho = 6.1 g_\rho$).
- For $^{24}$O without tensor coupling, the proton radius $r_p$ varies from 2.633 fm ($\Lambda_v = 0.000$) to 2.649 fm ($\Lambda_v = 0.030$); with tensor coupling, $r_p$ varies from 2.639 fm to 2.667 fm over the same $\Lambda_v$ range.
- The neutron thickness ($r_n - r_p$) for $^{24}$O without tensor coupling ranges from 0.651 fm ($\Lambda_v = 0.000$) to 0.616 fm ($\Lambda_v = 0.030$); with tensor coupling it ranges from 0.619 fm to 0.557 fm.
- The binding energy of $^{12}$O changes by 1.5 MeV as $\Lambda_v$ increases from 0 to 0.03.
- The uncertainty of the $^{12}$O charge radius is estimated to be about 0.3 fm based on linear extrapolation.
- The ratio $f_\rho/g_\rho = 6.1$ is used, based on Bonn potentials, and is within $8.0 \pm 2.0$ from light cone sum rules.

## Context

This work extends the study of correlations between the neutron radius of $^{208}$Pb and properties of neutron-rich nuclei, previously established by Horowitz and collaborators (2001) and Todd-Rutel and Piekarewicz (2005), to charge radii of light exotic oxygen isotopes. The inclusion of the $\rho NN$ tensor coupling, which does not affect nuclear matter symmetry energy, is a novel addition that enhances correlations for isotopes with $2s_{1/2}$ neutron occupation. The results suggest that combined measurements of the $^{208}$Pb neutron radius (via parity-violating electron scattering at Jefferson Laboratory) and electron scattering on exotic nuclei (at RIKEN and GSI) can jointly constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
