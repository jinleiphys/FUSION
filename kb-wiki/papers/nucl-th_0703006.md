# The Unitary Correlation Operator Method from a Similarity Renormalization Group Perspective

> We investigate how the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), developed to explicitly describe the strong short-range central and tensor correlations present in the nuclear many-body system, relates to the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG), a method to band-diagonalize Hamiltonians by continuous unitary transformations. We demonstrate how the structure of the UCOM transformation, originally motivated from the physically intuitive picture of correlations in coordinate space, arises naturally from the SRG flow equation. Apart from formal considerations we show that the momentum space matrix elements of the effective interactions obtained in both schemes agree extremely well.

## Key claim

The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) and the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) are closely related: the structure of the UCOM generators arises naturally from the SRG flow equation, and the momentum-space matrix elements of the effective interactions produced by both schemes agree extremely well, despite the UCOM using a static generator and the SRG using a dynamically adapting generator.

## Method

The authors compare the UCOM and SRG approaches for constructing effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions. For UCOM, they use a unitary transformation with separate central and tensor generators, whose correlation functions are determined by energy minimization in the two-nucleon system. For SRG, they solve the flow equation with the generator η(α) = [T_int, H_α] in a two-body space, using the Argonne V18 potential as the starting interaction. They evaluate the initial SRG generator analytically and compare the resulting momentum-space matrix elements of the correlated interactions in partial-wave bases.

## Key numbers

- The UCOM interaction with tensor correlation range parameter I_ϑ = 0.09 fm^3 (optimized for calculations without three-nucleon forces) is compared to the SRG interaction with flow parameter α̅ = 0.0215 fm^4, fixed by matching matrix elements at q = q' = 0 fm^{-1}.
- The repulsive core of the Argonne V18 potential has a very steep slope at about 0.5 fm.
- The tensor interaction has considerable strength up to 2-3 fm.
- Off-diagonal matrix elements are compared for UCOM with I_ϑ values of 0.03 fm^3, 0.06 fm^3, and 0.09 fm^3, and for SRG with α̅ values of 0.0055 fm^4, 0.0125 fm^4, and 0.0215 fm^4.

## Context

This work extends the formal understanding of the UCOM approach, originally developed by Feldmeier (1997) and Neff (2002), by connecting it to the SRG framework recently applied to NN interactions by Bogner (2006, 2007). The UCOM has been successfully used in no-core shell model calculations by Roth (2004, 2005) and others, showing improved convergence due to band-diagonal matrix elements. The SRG approach, based on Wegner's (1994) flow equation formulation, provides a systematic way to construct correlation functions without parametrizations, and this connection suggests future work on three-nucleon correlations and operator representations of SRG interactions.
