# Heavy Quark Production and Energy Loss

> Heavy flavor research is a vigorous and active topic in high-energy QCD physics. Comparing theoretical predictions to data as a function of flavor provides a unique opportunity to tease out properties of quark-gluon plasma. We explicitly demonstrate this utility with energy loss predictions based on the assumption of 1) a weakly-coupled plasma weakly coupled to a high-pT probe using pQCD and 2) a strongly-coupled plasma strongly coupled to a high-pT probe using AdS/CFT; we find that while the former enjoys broad qualitative agreement with data, it is difficult to reconcile the latter with experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that comparing heavy flavor energy loss predictions to data across collision energies and parton species can discriminate between two extreme assumptions about quark-gluon plasma: while a weakly-coupled plasma weakly coupled to a high-pT probe (using pQCD) shows broad qualitative agreement with experimental data, a strongly-coupled plasma strongly coupled to a high-pT probe (using AdS/CFT) is difficult to reconcile with measurements, though momentum fluctuations neglected in the AdS/CFT calculations may play a significant numerical role.

## Method

The author compares two theoretical frameworks for heavy quark energy loss in quark-gluon plasma: (1) pQCD-based calculations using the WHDG model, which convolved elastic and radiative energy loss with thermal field theory methods and the DGLV derivation for radiative loss, and (2) AdS/CFT calculations using a drag force model where dpT/dt = -μ pT with μ = π λ^(1/2) T^2 / (2 M_Q). Both frameworks are compared to experimental data on nuclear suppression factors R_AA(pT) for pions, D mesons, and B mesons at RHIC and LHC energies, with the pQCD model having one free parameter (the rapidity density of gluons) constrained by PHENIX π^0 data.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC (√s = 200 AGeV), the best fit rapidity density of gluons is dN_g/dy = 1400^(-200)_(+375) from PHENIX π^0 R_AA measurements in most central collisions
- For T_RHIC = 350 MeV: 1/μ ≈ 0.3 fm and λ_mfp^g ≈ 0.8 fm; for T_LHC = 450 MeV: 1/μ ≈ 0.2 fm and λ_mfp^g ≈ 0.7 fm
- The AdS/CFT drag coefficient is μ = π λ^(1/2) T^2 / (2 M_Q) for heavy quarks
- For light quarks in AdS/CFT, the thermalization time is approximately 3 fm, increasing to about 4 fm when 1D Hubble flow is included
- At T ≈ 250 MeV, the running coupling g ≈ 2 and λ = g^2 N_c ≈ 12 ≫ 1
- At T ≈ 250 MeV, α_s(2π T) = 0.3
- For heavy quarks in pQCD, L/λ_mfp ≈ 4

## Context

This work extends the program of using heavy flavor as a probe of quark-gluon plasma properties, building on earlier pQCD energy loss calculations by Wicks (2005) and Djordjevic (2005) and AdS/CFT drag calculations by Herzog (2006) and Gubser (2006). The paper directly compares the WHDG model (Wicks, 2005) and AdS/CFT predictions (Horowitz, 2007; Horowitz, 2010) to RHIC and LHC data, finding that pQCD provides a surprisingly good qualitative description across multiple observables while AdS/CFT overpredicts D meson suppression at LHC. The results enable future work on improving pQCD calculations through parton cascades (Uphoff, 2012), running coupling treatments (Buzzatti, 2012), and in-medium fragmentation (Sharma, 2012), while raising the question of how strong-coupling low-pT physics transitions to weak-coupling high-pT physics in the QGP.
