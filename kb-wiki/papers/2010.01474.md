# Local spin alignment of vector mesons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the spin alignment of vector mesons arising from locally polarized quarks and anti-quarks (local spin alignment) in heavy-ion collisions. We find that $ρ_{00}\neq 1/3$ does not necessarily signal the global polarization of quarks and anti-quarks along the orbital angular momentum of the system, but may also originate from their local spin polarization. Such local spin polarization could be induced by local vorticity arising from the anisotropic expansion of the fireball. We further propose experimental observables that can distinguish between the local and the global spin alignments. Measurements of these observables in heavy-ion collisions can probe the vorticity pattern and shed light on the puzzles of $ϕ$ and $K^{*0}$ spin alignments.

# Local spin alignment of vector mesons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The observed deviation of the spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ from 1/3 for φ and K*⁰ mesons does not necessarily signal global polarization of quarks and anti-quarks along the orbital angular momentum direction; it may instead originate from local spin polarization induced by local vorticity arising from the anisotropic expansion of the fireball, which can produce ρ₀₀ ≠ 1/3 even when global polarization is zero.

## Method

The authors derived general expressions for the 3×3 spin density matrix ρ^V of a vector meson formed by quark recombination, allowing arbitrary spin polarization vectors for the constituent quark and anti-quark (not restricted to the y-axis component as in prior global alignment studies). They then applied specific parametrizations of local polarization patterns, longitudinal vorticity producing a quadrupole pattern P_z ∝ sin(2Δψ) and transverse vorticity producing a circular pattern (P_x ∝ sin Δψ, P_y ∝ -cos Δψ), and computed the resulting ρ₀₀ and off-diagonal matrix elements as functions of the azimuthal angle Δψ relative to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- The centrality-averaged deviation |Δρ₀₀| = |ρ₀₀ - 1/3| observed by STAR and ALICE is of order 10⁻², which is two orders of magnitude larger than the ∼10⁻⁴ expected from global polarization alone based on Λ polarization data at √s_NN ∼ O(10) GeV.
- For central collisions with transverse local polarization amplitude |F_⊥| = 0.1 (estimated from prior theoretical calculations of local Λ polarization at √s_NN = 200 GeV in Au+Au collisions for |Y| < 1), the average ⟨ρ₀₀⟩ ≈ 1/3 - F_⊥²/9 = 1/3 - 0.0011.
- The amplitudes of the off-diagonal element modulations (≈ 2F_⊥²/3) are twice that of ρ₀₀ (≈ F_⊥²/3) in the local spin alignment scenario.
- The global polarization P_G decreases from a few percent at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV to a few permille at 200 GeV, and is consistent with zero at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Liang (2004) and Yang (2017) for global spin alignment of vector mesons, which assumed only the y-component of quark polarization (along the orbital angular momentum). The authors show that the observed ρ₀₀ ≠ 1/3 for φ and K*⁰ mesons reported by STAR (Zhou 2019, Singha 2020) and ALICE (Acharya 2019) cannot be explained by global polarization alone, as the magnitude is too large by ∼10². The local polarization patterns considered are based on prior work on local Λ polarization by Becattini (2017), Voloshin (2017), Xia (2018), and Wei (2018). The proposed experimental observables, the Δψ dependence of ρ₀₀, nonzero off-diagonal matrix elements, and the behavior of ⟨ρ₀₀⟩ under different event-plane choices, can distinguish local from global spin alignment and probe the vorticity structure of the quark-gluon plasma.
