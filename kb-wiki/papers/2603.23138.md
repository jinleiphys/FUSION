# Unified description of Sivers and Boer-Mulders asymmetries from twist-3 correlations

> We present the first calculation of the Efremov-Teryaev-Qiu-Sterman functions and associated twist-3 quark-gluon correlation functions for both the proton and pion. These functions are determined using the light-front wave functions obtained by diagonalizing a light-front effective Hamiltonian within a Fock space truncated to include a dynamical gluon. We compute the twist-3 correlations in the hard-pole region and extrapolate them to the soft-gluon pole limit. After the scale evolutions, our predictions demonstrate quantitative consistency with recent experimental extractions, providing a unified description of the Sivers and Boer-Mulders asymmetries from a light-front Hamiltonian approach.

## Key claim

The authors present the first calculation of the Efremov-Teryaev-Qiu-Sterman (ETQS) functions and associated twist-3 quark-gluon correlation functions for both the proton and pion, using light-front wave functions from a Hamiltonian diagonalized in a Fock space truncated to include one dynamical gluon. The quantitative consistency with experimental extractions is achieved only after applying QCD scale evolution and is limited to the hard-pole region, with extrapolation to the soft-gluon pole limit.

## Method

The authors diagonalize a light-front effective QCD Hamiltonian within the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) framework, truncating the Fock space to include valence quarks plus one dynamical gluon (|qqqg⟩ for the proton and |q\bar{q}g⟩ for the pion). They compute the multiparton correlation matrix elements from the resulting light-front wave functions, extract the twist-3 functions in the hard-pole region (x1 ≠ x2), and extrapolate to the soft-gluon pole limit (x1 = x2 = x). The results are evolved from initial model scales (μ²₀,ₚ = 0.079 GeV² for proton, μ²₀,π = 0.222 GeV² for pion) to experimental scales using leading-order DGLAP evolution with approximate splitting kernels.

## Key numbers

- Proton ground-state mass: M_p = 0.956 GeV; pion ground-state mass: M_π = 0.139 GeV
- Initial scale for proton: μ²₀,ₚ = 0.079 GeV²; for pion: μ²₀,π = 0.222 GeV²
- Proton valence quark moment matched at μ² = 10 GeV²: ⟨x⟩_u + ⟨x⟩_d = 0.3742
- Pion valence distribution first moment matched at μ² = 5 GeV²: ⟨x⟩_valence = 0.480
- Results evolved to μ² = 4 GeV² and μ² = 100 GeV² for comparison with experimental extractions
- Uncertainty bands estimated by varying initial scale μ₀² and coupling constant g² by ±10%

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ framework previously applied to hadron structure observables (Lan 2021, Xu 2021) to the calculation of twist-3 quark-gluon correlations, which are fundamental to understanding Sivers and Boer-Mulders asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes. The results are compared with phenomenological extractions from JAM20 (Cammarota 2020), EKT20 (Echevarria 2020), PV20 (Bacchetta 2020), JAM22 (Gamberg 2022), EIKV (Echevarria 2014), TC (Boglione 2018), and PV11 (Bacchetta 2011). This establishes a direct connection between light-front wave functions and spin-dependent observables, enabling first-principles calculations of higher-twist effects that were previously accessible only through model-dependent extractions or lattice QCD at an early stage.
