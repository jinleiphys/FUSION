# Anomalous viscosity of an expanding quark-gluon plasma

> We argue that an expanding quark-gluon plasma has an anomalous viscosity, which arises from interactions with dynamically generated colour fields. The anomalous viscosity dominates over the collisional viscosity for large velocity gradients or weak coupling. This effect may provide an explanation for the apparent near perfect liquidity of the matter produced in nuclear collisions at RHIC without the assumption that it is a strongly coupled state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that an expanding quark-gluon plasma has an anomalous viscosity arising from interactions with dynamically generated colour fields, which dominates over collisional viscosity for large velocity gradients or weak coupling. This effect may explain the near perfect liquidity observed at RHIC without assuming the plasma is a strongly coupled state.

## Method

The authors derive a heuristic expression for the anomalous shear viscosity η_A by treating the mean free path as determined by the number of colour field domains a thermal parton must traverse to randomize its direction, rather than by collisional cross sections. They estimate the average field intensity ⟨ℬ²⟩ and domain size r_m from the characteristic wavelength of unstable field modes near thermal equilibrium, using the colour-electric screening mass m_D ∼ gT and a dimensionless anisotropy parameter ξ. The analysis combines classical transport theory with the theory of particle transport in turbulent plasmas, following the approach of Abe and Niu (1980).

## Key numbers

- The collisional shear viscosity η_C is compared to the postulated lower bound η_min = s/4π (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2004).
- For a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma, particle density n ≈ 5T³ and thermal momentum p̄ ≈ 3T.
- The wave vector domain of unstable modes is k² ≤ ξ m_D², giving domain size r_m ∼ ξ^{-1/2}(gT)^{-1}.
- At saturation, the denominator in the anomalous viscosity expression scales as g²Q²⟨ℬ²⟩r_m ∼ ξ^{3/2}(gT)³.
- The anomalous viscosity relation is η_A ∼ s/(g³ξ^{3/2}).
- For boost-invariant longitudinal flow, the anisotropy parameter ξ = 10(η/s)(|∇u|/T), where |∇u| = 1/τ.
- The resulting anomalous viscosity is η_A/s = c̄₀ (T/(g²|∇u|))^{3/5}.
- The anomalous viscous stress grows like |∇u|^{2/5}, while collisional viscous stress grows linearly with |∇u|.

## Context

This work extends the theory of particle transport in turbulent plasmas (Dupree, 1966; Dupree, 1968) and the treatment of anisotropic momentum distributions in quark-gluon plasmas (Mrowczynski, 1988; Romatschke, 2003) to explain the small viscosity observed at RHIC. It offers an alternative to the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) interpretation (Shuryak, 2004; Koch, 2005) by showing that a weakly coupled but expanding plasma can exhibit small shear viscosity through this anomalous mechanism. The authors note that the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2004) does not apply to the anomalous viscosity, and they call for confirmation by numerical simulations.
