# Infrared extrapolations of quadrupole moments and transitions

> We study the convergence of bound-state quadrupole moments in finite harmonic oscillator spaces. We derive an expression for the infrared extrapolation for the quadrupole moment of a nucleus and benchmark our results using different model interactions for the deuteron. We find good agreement between the analytically derived and numerically obtained convergence behavior. We also derive an extrapolation formula for electric quadrupole transitions and find good agreement with the numerical calculation of a simple system.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic infrared (IR) extrapolation formulas for bound-state quadrupole moments and electric quadrupole (E2) transition matrix elements in finite harmonic oscillator spaces, showing that the leading-order correction scales as \(e^{-2k_\infty L}\) with a polynomial prefactor that depends on whether the observable is an expectation value or a transition amplitude.

## Method

The authors start from the deuteron wave function as a superposition of S- and D-states and compute the quadrupole moment expectation value in a finite oscillator basis with IR length scale \(L\). They split the radial integration into an interior region (dependent on the interaction) and an exterior region where the potential vanishes, using spherical Hankel functions to describe the asymptotic wave functions with Dirichlet boundary conditions at \(r = L\). The resulting expressions are expanded in powers of \(e^{-2kL}\) to obtain leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) correction terms, and the analysis is generalized to arbitrary mixtures of partial waves and to E2 transition amplitudes between bound states with different angular momenta.

## Key numbers

- For quadrupole moments of bound states with partial-wave mixing or finite orbital angular momentum \(l>0\), the leading-order extrapolation formula is \(Q_L = Q_\infty - a(k_\infty L)^3 e^{-2k_\infty L}\) (Eq. master_simp).
- For the deuteron specifically, the NLO formula includes an additional term: \(Q_L = Q_\infty - a(k_\infty L)^3\left(1+{\frac{d}{k_\infty L}}\right) e^{-2k_\infty L}\) (Eq. master).
- For E2 transitions between bound states with momenta \(k_1\) and \(k_2\), the leading-order extrapolation is \(\mathcal{A}_L = \mathcal{A}_{\infty} + a_0 e^{-2 k_< L}\), where \(k_< \equiv \min{(k_1,k_2)}\) (Eq. trans).
- For the specific case of an S-wave to D-wave transition, the E2 amplitude extrapolates as \(\mathcal{A}_L = \mathcal{A}_{\infty} + a_0 \left[1 + \frac{a_1}{k_2 L} + \mathcal{O}\left(\frac{1}{(k_2 L)^2}\right)\right]e^{-2 k_2 L}\) (Eq. E2convergence).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for IR extrapolations in harmonic oscillator bases previously developed for ground-state energies by Coon et al. (2012) and for radii by Furnstahl et al. (2014), which showed that the finite oscillator basis imposes a Dirichlet boundary condition at an effective hard-wall radius \(L\). The derived formulas enable extrapolation of quadrupole moments and E2 transitions to the infinite-basis limit for nuclei where full convergence is computationally prohibitive, such as \(p\)-shell nuclei studied by Cockrell et al. (2012), Forssen et al. (2013), and Maris et al. (2013). The results also connect to Luscher's (1985) finite-volume corrections in lattice QCD, providing analogous formulas for nuclear structure calculations in harmonic oscillator spaces.
