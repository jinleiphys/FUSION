# Role of Three-Nucleon Forces in Neutron-Rich Nuclei Beyond 132Sn

> The role of three-nucleon forces (3NF) in the description of nuclear structure properties is nowadays a main topic in the field of microscopic many-nucleon calculations. We investigate the relative weight between effective two- and three-nucleon forces in neutron-rich nuclei beyond the doubly-closed 132Sn core within the realistic shell-model framework, studying the evolution of the spectroscopic properties of N=82 isotopes and heavy tin isotopes. This problem is tackled indirectly without explicitly taking into account effective 3NF through the comparison of the results of shell-model calculations obtained from realistic on-shell-equivalent low-momentum potentials.

## Key claim

The paper indirectly investigates the role of three-nucleon forces (3NF) in neutron-rich nuclei beyond 132Sn by comparing shell-model calculations derived from two on-shell-equivalent low-momentum potentials with different cutoffs, without explicitly including 3NF in the calculations.

## Method

Starting from the CD-Bonn high-precision nucleon-nucleon potential, the authors derived two Vlow-k potentials with cutoffs Lambda = 2.1 and 2.6 fm^-1 using the Vlow-k approach. They then constructed effective shell-model Hamiltonians via time-dependent degenerate linked-diagram perturbation theory, including one- and two-body Goldstone diagrams through third order, and performed shell-model calculations for N=82 isotones and heavy tin isotopes. The sensitivity of calculated spectroscopic properties to the cutoff value was used to infer the role of missing three-nucleon forces.

## Key numbers

- Two Vlow-k potentials were derived from the CD-Bonn potential with cutoffs Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1 and 2.6 fm^-1
- Convergence of single-particle energies and two-body matrix elements was achieved at Nmax = 16 for the cutoff Lambda = 2.6 fm^-1
- The neutron 0i13/2 energy was estimated from the observed 10+ state at 2434 keV in 134Sb
- Calculations for heavy tin isotopes could not be carried out beyond N = 90 due to computational complexity

## Context

This work extends the investigation of three-nucleon forces in nuclear structure calculations, following studies of cutoff dependence in neutron and nuclear infinite matter by Coraggio (2013, 2014). The authors build on the Vlow-k approach developed by Bogner (2001, 2002) and the folded-diagram expansion method of Kuo (1971). The results enable future studies of shell evolution in neutron-rich nuclei at next-generation radioactive ion beam facilities, particularly regarding the role of 3NF in determining shell closures at Z=70 and N=102.
