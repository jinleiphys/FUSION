# Lattice QCD study of the $H$ dibaryon using hexaquark and two-baryon interpolators

> We present a lattice QCD spectroscopy study in the isospin singlet, strangeness $-2$ sectors relevant for the conjectured $H$ dibaryon. We employ both local and bilocal interpolating operators to isolate the ground state in the rest frame and in moving frames. Calculations are performed using two flavors of O($a$)-improved Wilson fermions and a quenched strange quark. Our initial point-source method for constructing correlators does not allow for bilocal operators at the source; nevertheless, results from using these operators at the sink indicate that they provide an improved overlap onto the ground state in comparison with the local operators. We also present results, in the rest frame, using a second method based on distillation to compute a hermitian matrix of correlators with bilocal operators at both the source and the sink. This method yields a much more precise and reliable determination of the ground-state energy. In the flavor-SU(3) symmetric case, we apply Lüscher's finite-volume quantization condition to the rest-frame and moving-frame energy levels to determine the $S$-wave scattering phase shift, near and below the two-particle threshold. For a pion mass of 960 MeV, we find that there exists a bound $H$ dibaryon with binding energy $ΔE=(19\pm10)$ MeV. In the 27-plet (dineutron) sector, the finite-volume analysis suggests that the existence of a bound state is unlikely.

## Key claim
Using lattice QCD with two dynamical flavors and a quenched strange quark at a pion mass of 960 MeV, the authors find evidence for a bound H dibaryon with binding energy ΔE = (19 ± 10) MeV, but only in the flavor-SU(3) symmetric case. The result is conditional on the use of Lüscher's finite-volume quantization condition applied to both rest-frame and moving-frame energy levels, and the binding is significantly smaller than earlier lattice claims.

## Method
The authors computed correlation matrices using both local hexaquark operators (six quarks at the same point) and bilocal two-baryon operators (spatially displaced, momentum-projected baryon pairs) on ensembles with O(a)-improved Wilson fermions. For point-source propagators, only hexaquark operators could be placed at the source, yielding a non-hermitian correlator matrix that was diagonalized via a generalized eigenvalue problem; a separate distillation-based calculation allowed a hermitian matrix with bilocal operators at both source and sink. Lüscher's finite-volume quantization condition was applied to energy levels in the rest frame and moving frames to extract S-wave scattering phase shifts near and below the two-particle threshold.

## Key numbers
- H dibaryon binding energy ΔE = (19 ± 10) MeV at a pion mass of 960 MeV on ensemble E1 (lattice spacing a = 0.0658(10) fm, spatial extent L = 2.11 fm)
- Pion mass values: 858 MeV (ensemble N1), 436 MeV (ensemble E5), 960 MeV (ensemble E1), 744 MeV (ensemble A1)
- Lattice spacings: 0.0486(6) fm (N1), 0.0658(10) fm (E5 and E1), 0.0755(11) fm (A1)
- Spatial extents: 2.33 fm (N1), 2.11 fm (E5 and E1), 2.42 fm (A1)
- mπL values: 10.0 (N1), 4.7 (E5), 10.2 (E1), 9.9 (A1)

## Context
This work extends the long-standing search for the H dibaryon predicted by Jaffe (1976) using the MIT bag model, which predicted a binding of about 80 MeV. Previous lattice calculations by NPLQCD (Beane et al., 2010-2012) and HAL QCD (Inoue et al., 2010-2011) both found a bound state but disagreed significantly on the binding energy (74.6 ± 4.7 MeV versus 37.8 ± 5.1 MeV at similar quark masses). The present result of 19 ± 10 MeV is substantially smaller than both earlier claims, and the authors note that the 27-plet (dineutron) sector shows no evidence for a bound state. The use of distillation for a hermitian correlator matrix in the two-baryon sector is a methodological advance that enables more precise ground-state determination, and the work enables future studies with dynamical strange quarks and lighter pion masses to resolve the remaining discrepancies.
