# Dressed-quark anomalous magnetic moments

> Perturbation theory predicts that a massless fermion cannot possess a measurable magnetic moment. We explain, however, that the nonperturbative phenomenon of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking generates a momentum-dependent anomalous chromomagnetic moment for dressed light-quarks, which is large at infrared momenta; and demonstrate that consequently these same quarks also possess an anomalous electromagnetic moment with similar magnitude and opposite sign.

## Key claim

Nonperturbative dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) generates a momentum-dependent anomalous chromomagnetic moment for dressed light-quarks that is large at infrared momenta, and this in turn produces an anomalous electromagnetic moment of similar magnitude and opposite sign; the effect is completely nonperturbative and vanishes at any finite order in perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) for the dressed-quark propagator using a simplified interaction model, and then solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the quark-photon vertex using a vertex Ansatz that includes both the longitudinal (Ball-Chiu) vertex and transverse anomalous chromomagnetic terms (τ₄ and τ₅). The anomalous electromagnetic moment distribution κ(ς) is extracted from the dressed quark-photon vertex by defining off-shell spinors with mass ς = M(p²), the momentum-dependent quark mass function.

## Key numbers

- The Euclidean constituent-quark mass from the full calculation is M_full^E = 0.44 GeV, compared to M_RL^E = 0.35 GeV in rainbow-ladder truncation.
- The anomalous chromomagnetic moment at the Euclidean mass-shell is κ_full^acm = -0.22.
- The anomalous electromagnetic moment at the Euclidean mass-shell is κ_full^aem = 0.45.
- In rainbow-ladder truncation, the anomalous electromagnetic moment is only κ_RL^aem = 0.048, an order of magnitude smaller.
- The interaction model parameters used are ω = 0.5 GeV, D = (0.72 GeV)², current-quark mass m = 5 MeV, and η = -7/4.
- The lattice result shows |τ₅(p,-p;2p)| ≳ |λ₃(p,p;0)| for all p² > 0, with τ₅ roughly two orders of magnitude larger than the perturbative form.

## Context

This work extends DSE studies of the quark-gluon vertex (Bhagwat 2004) and lattice simulations (Skullerud 2003) that revealed DCSB-generated components in the vertex, particularly the λ₃ term. It builds on the method of Chang (2009) for symmetry-preserving treatment of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The results contradict the perturbative expectation that massless fermions have no anomalous magnetic moment (Schwinger 1948) and show that rainbow-ladder truncation (Bender 1996) underestimates these effects by an order of magnitude. The findings enable reanalysis of hadron spectra and electromagnetic form factors, and may contribute to understanding the muon g-2 anomaly via hadronic contributions.
