# The Shear Viscosity to Entropy Density Ratio of Trapped Fermions in the Unitarity Limit

> We extract the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s of cold fermionic atoms in the unitarity limit from experimental data on the damping of collective excitations. We find that near the critical temperature η/s is roughly equal to 1/2 in units of \hbar/k_B. With the possible exception of the quark gluon plasma, this value is closer to the conjectured lower bound 1/(4π) than any other known liquid.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s of cold fermionic atoms in the unitarity limit near the critical temperature is roughly 0.5 in units of ħ/k_B, which is about six times larger than the conjectured lower bound of 1/(4π) and not at the bound itself.

## Method

The author extracted η/s from experimental data on the damping of the radial breathing mode of trapped ⁶Li gas near a Feshbach resonance at 840 G, using viscous corrections to ideal (Eulerian) hydrodynamics. The damping rate was related to the volume integral of the shear viscosity, and the entropy density was obtained from experimental calorimetric measurements and quantum Monte Carlo thermodynamic data using the local density approximation. A surface defined by the condition that a particle incident from outside has a collision probability of no more than 1/e was used to estimate systematic uncertainty from the breakdown of hydrodynamics near the cloud edge.

## Key numbers

- η/s near the critical temperature Tc is roughly 0.5 in units of ħ/k_B
- The conjectured lower bound is η/s ≥ 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08 in units of ħ/k_B
- The extracted value η/s ∼ 0.5 is smaller than the previously known minimum for all other liquids, η/s ∼ 0.7 for liquid Helium near the lambda point
- The critical temperature for the trapped system from the Duke group calorimetry is Tc/TF = 0.29(2)
- The critical entropy per particle from the Duke group is S/N = 2.7
- Burovski et al. find Tc/TF = 0.152(7) and critical entropy per particle S/N = 0.16(2) for the bulk system
- Bulgac et al. quote Tc/TF = 0.23(2) and S/N ≃ 1.1 for the bulk system, corresponding to S/N ≃ 2.1 for the trapped system
- The universal parameter ξ at zero temperature is ξ ≃ 0.44 from quantum Monte Carlo

## Context

This work tests the viscosity bound conjecture of Kovtun et al. (2004) by extracting η/s from cold atomic gas experiments, extending prior studies of damping near the unitarity limit by Massignan (2004), Gelman (2004), and Bruun (2006). The result that η/s ∼ 0.5 near Tc is consistent with the picture of a very strongly correlated liquid and, with the possible exception of the quark gluon plasma produced at RHIC, is closer to the conjectured lower bound than any other known liquid. The analysis enables future work on establishing shear viscosity as the dominant damping mechanism through studies of different collective modes or system size dependence, and motivates more detailed understanding of damping below Tc using superfluid two-fluid hydrodynamics.
