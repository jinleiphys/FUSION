# Spatial symmetries of the local densities

> Spatial symmetries of the densities appearing in the nuclear Density Functional Theory are discussed. General forms of the local densities are derived by using methods of construction of isotropic tensor fields. The spherical and axial cases are considered.

## Key claim

The paper derives the general forms of local nuclear densities under spherical and axial self-consistent symmetries using isotropic tensor field methods, showing that the parity of the field uniquely determines which local densities vanish (e.g., pseudoscalar and pseudovector densities vanish under full O(3) symmetry) and that the axial case introduces a material vector fixed by the symmetry axis direction.

## Method

The authors treat nonlocal and local densities as isotropic tensor fields depending on position vectors, using the Generalized Cayley-Hamilton theorem to construct general expressions for scalar, vector, and symmetric traceless tensor fields. They apply this formalism to the particle-hole and particle-particle density matrices from nuclear Density Functional Theory, considering both spherical symmetry (SO(3) and O(3) groups) and axial symmetry (SO^⊥(2) and O^z⊥(2) groups), and derive the resulting forms for zero-order, first-order, and second-order local densities including particle density, spin density, current density, spin-current density, kinetic density, and spin-kinetic density.

## Key numbers

- Under O(3) symmetry: local spin density s(r) = 0, pseudoscalar J(r) = 0, pseudotensor J̲(r) = 0, and pseudovector densities T(r) = 0 and F(r) = 0
- Under O^z⊥(2) symmetry: local spin z-component s_z(r) = 0, pseudoscalar J(r) = 0, while s_⊥(r) takes the form φ_⊥(z², r²_⊥)(r_z × r_⊥)
- Under SO^⊥(2) symmetry: local spin density s_z(r) = ρ_z(z, r_⊥)r_z and s_⊥(r) = ρ_⊥(z, r_⊥)r_⊥ + φ_⊥(z, r_⊥)(r_z × r_⊥)
- Under spherical SO(3) symmetry: all local scalar densities take form q_0(r²), vectors take form q_1(r²)r, and traceless symmetric tensors take form q_2(r²)(r⊗r − ⅓r²1)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of self-consistent symmetries in nuclear DFT presented in Rohozinski et al. (2009) (referred to as I) by detailing the consequences of spatial symmetries with simplified notation. The paper builds on earlier investigations of point symmetries and symmetry-breaking schemes of particle-hole densities by Dobaczewski et al. (2000a, 2000b). The results enable practitioners to know the general form of density matrices possessing a given self-consistent symmetry, which is crucial for choosing initial density matrices in self-consistent calculations and avoiding incorrect predictions from ad hoc symmetry assumptions.
