# Dissipative Charged Fluid in a Magnetic Field

> We study the collective excitations in a dissipative charged fluid at zero chemical potential when an external magnetic field is present. While in the absence of magnetic field, four collective excitations appear in the fluid, we find five hydrodynamic modes in presence of magnetic field. This implies that the magnetic field splits the degeneracy between the transverse shear modes. Using linear response theory, we then compute the retarded response functions. In particular, it turns out that the correlation between charge and the energy fluctuations will no longer vanish, even at zero chemical potential. By use of the response functions, we also derive the relevant Kubo formulas for the transport coefficients.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a dissipative charged fluid at zero chemical potential, an external magnetic field splits the degeneracy of the two transverse shear modes, resulting in five hydrodynamic modes instead of four, and that the magnetic field introduces new dissipative contributions to all modes. This result is conditional on the magnetic field strength being of order \(B \sim \sqrt{\eta/\sigma} \, k\) for consistency with the hydrodynamic derivative expansion.

## Method

The authors linearize the relativistic hydrodynamic equations (energy-momentum and charge conservation) around an equilibrium state with constant temperature, zero chemical potential, and a constant external magnetic field, using the Landau-Lifshitz frame for the constitutive relations. They solve the resulting eigenvalue problem for the matrix \(M_{ab}(\mathbf{k})\) to obtain the dispersion relations of the collective modes. Using linear response theory, they compute the retarded response functions \(G^R_{ab}(\omega, \mathbf{k})\) for the hydrodynamic fields (charge density, momentum density, energy density) and derive Kubo formulas for the transport coefficients (conductivity, shear viscosity, bulk viscosity) in the presence of the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

*   The five hydrodynamic modes are given by: \(\omega_{1,2}(\mathbf{k}) = \pm v_s k - \frac{i}{2}\left(\mathbf{k}^2 \gamma_s + \frac{\sigma}{\bar{w}} \mathbf{B}^2 \sin^2 \theta\right)\); \(\omega_{3,4}(\mathbf{k}) = -\frac{i}{2}\left(\mathbf{k}^2 (D+\gamma_\eta) + \frac{\sigma}{\bar{w}} \mathbf{B}^2 \pm \sqrt{(\mathbf{k}^2(D-\gamma_\eta) - \frac{\sigma}{\bar{w}} \mathbf{B}^2)^2 + \frac{4 D \sigma}{\bar{w}} \mathbf{B}^2 \mathbf{k}^2 \sin^2 \theta}\right)\); \(\omega_5(\mathbf{k}) = -i\left(\mathbf{k}^2 \gamma_\eta + \frac{\sigma}{\bar{w}} \mathbf{B}^2 \cos^2 \theta\right)\), where \(\theta\) is the angle between \(\mathbf{k}\) and \(\mathbf{B}\).
*   When \(\mathbf{B} \parallel \mathbf{k}\), the two shear modes become degenerate: \(\omega_4(\mathbf{k}) = \omega_5(\mathbf{k}) = -i\left(\gamma_\eta \mathbf{k}^2 + \frac{\sigma}{\bar{w}} \mathbf{B}^2\right)\).
*   The retarded response function \(G^R_{\pi_i n}(\omega, \mathbf{k})\) is anti-symmetric and proportional to \((\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{k})_i\), with the explicit form \(G^R_{\pi_i n}(\omega, \mathbf{k}) = \frac{\sigma \omega}{\Delta_{34}} (\mathbf{B} \times \mathbf{k})_i\).

## Context

This work extends the study of relativistic hydrodynamic fluctuations, previously analyzed for neutral fluids and charged fluids without external fields (e.g., Kovtun (2012)), to the case of a charged fluid in a constant magnetic field at zero chemical potential. The results show that the magnetic field breaks time-reversal symmetry, leading to non-vanishing correlations between charge and momentum density fluctuations and modifying the Kubo formulas for transport coefficients. The paper enables future investigations into dissipative effects in chiral fluids, such as the Chiral Alfven wave predicted by Yamamoto (2015), by providing a framework for including dissipation in the presence of anomalies and magnetic fields.
