# Testing Chiral Dynamics in Pionic Atoms

> The energy dependence of chirally expanded pi N isoscalar and isovector amplitudes b_0(E) and b_1(E) respectively, for zero-momentum off shell pions near threshold, is used to impose the minimal substitution requirement E -> E - V_c on the properly constructed pion optical potential within a large-scale fit to 100 pionic-atom data across the periodic table which also include the recently established `deeply bound' pionic atoms of Pb and Sn. This fit cannot be reconciled with the well known free-space values of the pi N threshold amplitudes. In contrast, introducing the empirically known energy dependence for on-shell pions leads to a better fit and to satisfactory values for the pi N threshold amplitudes. The difference between these two approaches is briefly discussed.

# Testing Chiral Dynamics in Pionic Atoms

## Key claim

The energy dependence of chirally expanded πN amplitudes for zero-momentum off-shell pions, when imposed via minimal substitution on the pion optical potential, fails to reproduce the bulk of pionic-atom data across the periodic table; in contrast, using the empirically known on-shell energy dependence yields a better fit and recovers the free-space πN threshold amplitudes. The chiral off-shell approach works for the limited set of deeply bound states but lacks statistical significance to be validated by the full data set.

## Method

The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation for pionic-atom eigenenergies using the standard Ericson-Ericson form of the pion-nuclear polarization operator, with s-wave amplitudes b₀(E) and b₁(E) made energy-dependent via minimal substitution E → E − V_c. They performed large-scale χ² fits to 100 strong-interaction shifts and widths across nuclei from ²⁰Ne to ²³⁸U, comparing three models: a conventional energy-independent potential, a potential using empirical on-shell πN amplitudes from the SAID database, and a potential using chirally expanded off-shell (q=0) amplitudes at the two-loop level. Neutron density distributions were parameterized using a linear relation between r_n−r_p and (N−Z)/A, with the slope α varied over a wide range.

## Key numbers

- The free-space πN isovector threshold amplitude b₁(m_π) = −0.0885^{+0.0010}_{−0.0021} m_π⁻¹ (from SAID analysis)
- The conventional energy-independent fit yields b₁ = −0.108 ± 0.007 m_π⁻¹, more repulsive than the free-space value
- For the conventional potential: Im B₀ = 0.053 ± 0.002 m_π⁻⁴ and Re B₀ = −0.10 ± 0.03 m_π⁻⁴
- For the empirical energy-dependent potential: Re B₀ = −0.07 ± 0.03 m_π⁻⁴
- The slope of the chiral q=0 b₀(E) amplitude is larger than the on-shell empirical slope by about 60%
- The empirical b₁(E) slope is essentially zero, in contrast to the fairly large slope of the chiral q=0 amplitude
- The neutron-excess parameter α from RMF calculations is 1.51 ± 0.07 fm; from antiprotonic atoms α = 1.0 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Friedman and Gal, 2003) which showed that deeply bound pionic states alone are insufficient to determine whether b₁ is renormalized in dense matter, and that a larger data set yields b₁ = −0.108 ± 0.007 m_π⁻¹. It directly tests the suggestion by Kolomeitsev, Kaiser, and Weise (2003) that chirally expanded off-shell amplitudes with minimal substitution could reproduce pionic atom data, finding that this approach fails for the bulk data while the on-shell prescription of Ericson and Tauscher (1982) succeeds. The results imply that chiral dynamics is not yet at a stage of being testable in pionic atoms, and that the renormalization of the pion decay constant f_π in dense matter need not be invoked if the full energy dependence of the polarization operator is incorporated.
