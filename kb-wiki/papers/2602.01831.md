# Helium-3 relativistic wave function in light-front dynamics

> The relativistic wave function of $^3$He nucleus is calculated in the framework of Light-Front Dynamics. It is determined by 32 spin-isospin components, each of which depends on five scalar variables. For NN interaction, the one-boson exchange model is assumed, but without a potential approximation. The relativistic effects manifest themselves in deviation of the relativistic components from the non-relativistic input, in the appearance of the components absent in the non-relativistic limit, and in dependence of solutions on specific variables that don't exist in the non-relativistic wave function.

## Key claim

The relativistic wave function of $^3$He is calculated in Light-Front Dynamics using a one-boson exchange model for the NN interaction without a potential approximation, producing 32 spin-isospin components that show relativistic effects through deviations from non-relativistic input and the appearance of components absent in the non-relativistic limit.

## Method

The three-fermion wave function is decomposed into Faddeev components, each expanded in an orthonormalized basis of 16 spin structures constructed from Dirac spinors and the light-front four-vector ω, with separate bases for pair isospin t=0 and t=1 (32 components total). The invariant coefficients g^{(t)}_{ij} depending on five scalar variables are obtained by solving a system of integral equations derived from the light-front dynamics framework, using the one-boson exchange interaction kernel without a potential approximation. A second basis χ_n is constructed that connects to the non-relativistic wave function components in the non-relativistic limit, allowing comparison between relativistic and non-relativistic results.

## Key numbers

- The $^3$He wave function is determined by **32 spin-isospin components** (16 for pair isospin t=0 and 16 for t=1), each depending on **five scalar variables**
- The deuteron LF wave function has **6 spin components** (compared to 2 non-relativistic components), each depending on **two variables** (|k_⊥| and x)
- The non-relativistic deuteron wave function has **2 spin components** (L=0 and L=2 S- and D-waves), each depending on **one variable** (relative proton-neutron momentum)
- The normalization factors for the spin structures are: N_{S_j} = 2√(x_3 R_{3⊥}^2) for j=1,3 and N_{S_j} = 2√(x_3 m^2) for j=2,4; N_{T_i} = √(8x_1 x_2 R_{12⊥}^2) for i=1,3 and N_{T_i} = √(8x_1 x_2 m^2) for i=2,4

## Context

This work extends the explicitly covariant Light-Front Dynamics approach previously applied to the deuteron by Carbonell and Karmanov (1995), where the deuteron LF wave function was calculated and successfully used to predict electromagnetic form factors later confirmed by experiment (Abbott et al.). The general spin structure of the three-fermion LF wave function was established earlier by Karmanov (1976), but the technical difficulties from the 32 components and five variables made the solution impractical until recent advances in computing power. This calculation enables future predictions of $^3$He electromagnetic form factors at large momentum transfer and studies of high-momentum tails of nuclear wave functions beyond the non-relativistic regime.
