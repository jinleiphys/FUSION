# Elliptic and triangular flow in p+Pb and peripheral Pb+Pb collisions from parton scatterings

> Using a multiphase transport model (AMPT) we calculate the elliptic, $v_2$, and triangular, $v_3$, Fourier coefficients of the two-particle azimuthal correlation function in proton-nucleus (p+Pb) and peripheral nucleus-nucleus (Pb+Pb) collisions. Our results for $v_3$ are in a good agreement with the CMS data collected at the Large Hadron Collider. The $v_2$ coefficient is very well described in p+Pb collisions and is underestimated for higher transverse momenta in Pb+Pb interactions. The characteristic mass ordering of $v_2$ in p+Pb is reproduced whereas for $v_3$ this effect is not observed. We further predict the pseudorapidity dependence of $v_2$ and $v_3$ in p+Pb and observe that both are increasing when going from a proton side to a Pb-nucleus side. Predictions for the higher order Fourier coefficients, $v_4$ and $v_5$, in p+Pb are also presented.

## Key claim

The AMPT model with string melting and a modest parton-parton cross-section of 3 mb reproduces the elliptic flow v2 and triangular flow v3 in p+Pb collisions across a broad range of transverse momentum and centrality, but in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions the integrated v2 is underestimated by roughly 20% while v3 remains consistent with CMS data.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with the string melting mechanism, in which all initial minijets and soft strings are converted into quarks and antiquarks that undergo elastic scatterings with a partonic cross-section of 3 mb, controlled by the strong coupling constant and Debye screening mass. They simulated p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV and peripheral Pb+Pb collisions (50-100%) at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV, then extracted the Fourier coefficients v2 and v3 from two-particle azimuthal correlation functions following the CMS analysis procedure with a pseudorapidity gap |Delta eta| > 2. Hadronization was performed via a simple coalescence model followed by hadronic scatterings.

## Key numbers

- Parton-parton cross-section used: 3 mb, consistent with the authors' previous study (Ma 2014).
- In peripheral Pb+Pb collisions, the integrated v2 is underestimated by roughly 20% compared to CMS data.
- The average number of elastic scatterings per parton is approximately 2 for N_track = 200 in p+Pb collisions.
- The lifetime of the partonic matter is roughly 1 fm/c.
- For N_track = 200, the effective area per parton is approximately 0.1 fm^2 (assuming a p+Pb radius of 2 fm).
- The higher-order Fourier coefficients v4 and v5 in p+Pb collisions are roughly a factor of 2 smaller than the v3 coefficient.
- The analysis used charged particles with p_T > 0.4 GeV/c in |eta| < 2.4 for centrality definition, and integrated v2 and v3 were computed in the range 0.3 < p_T < 3 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ma 2014) which showed that incoherent parton scatterings in AMPT could reproduce long-range two-particle azimuthal correlations in p+Pb and high-multiplicity p+p collisions. The results support the interpretation that collective dynamics, specifically the transformation of initial coordinate-space anisotropy into final momentum anisotropy via parton scatterings, is present in small systems such as p+Pb collisions. The mass ordering of v2 for pions, kaons, and protons is reproduced, consistent with hydrodynamic calculations by Bozek (2013) and Werner (2013), while the absence of mass ordering for v3 also agrees with those calculations. The pseudorapidity dependence predictions for v2 and v3 increasing toward the Pb fragmentation region are presented as a probe to distinguish between initial-state models (such as the color glass condensate/glasma approach by Dusling 2013) and final-state interaction models.
