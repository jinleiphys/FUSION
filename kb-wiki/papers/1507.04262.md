# Temperature-dependent cross sections for charmonium dissociation in collisions with kaons and eta mesons in hadronic matter

> We study kaon-charmonium and eta-charmonium dissociation reactions. The K-charmonium dissociation and the eta-charmonium dissociation include 27 reactions. Cross sections for the reactions are calculated in the Born approximation, in the quark-interchange mechanism and with a temperature-dependent quark potential. The temperature dependence of peak cross sections of endothermic reactions is linked to the temperature dependence of quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions, meson masses and the quark potential. Although the eta meson and kaon have similar masses, the energy and temperature dependence of the eta-charmonium dissociation cross sections are quite different from those of the K-charmonium dissociation cross sections. Using the eta-charmonium and pion-charmonium dissociation cross sections, we calculate the ratio of the corresponding dissociation rates in hadronic matter and we find that such rates are comparable at low J/psi momenta.

## Key claim

The paper calculates temperature-dependent cross sections for kaon-charmonium and eta-charmonium dissociation reactions in hadronic matter using the quark-interchange mechanism, finding that although the eta meson and kaon have similar masses, the energy and temperature dependence of their dissociation cross sections are quite different, and that eta-charmonium dissociation rates are comparable to pion-charmonium dissociation rates only at low J/psi momenta.

## Method

Cross sections for 27 dissociation reactions (9 K-charmonium and 18 eta-charmonium) were calculated in the Born approximation using the quark-interchange mechanism with a temperature-dependent quark potential derived from lattice QCD results. The potential includes a spin-independent part with temperature-dependent confinement and a spin-spin interaction, and the Schrodinger equation was solved to obtain temperature-dependent meson masses and quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions. Unpolarised cross sections were computed as the average of prior-form and post-form transition amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The peak cross section for K + J/psi -> anti-D* + D+_s at T=0 is about 0.27 mb, much smaller than the 8 mb magnitude obtained in the meson-exchange approach of Haglin (2000)
- The peak cross section for K + J/psi -> anti-D* D+_s + anti-D D*+_s + anti-D* D*+_s is about 0.6 mb at kinetic energy of order 0.44 GeV
- The peak cross section for K + chi_c -> anti-D* D+_s + anti-D D*+_s + anti-D* D*+_s is about 3.4 mb at kinetic energy of order 0.08 GeV
- At T/T_c=0, the peak cross section of eta + J/psi -> anti-D* + D is 1.64 times the peak cross section of K + J/psi -> anti-D* + D+_s
- At T/T_c=0.85, the peak cross section of eta + J/psi -> anti-D* + D is 12.11 times the peak cross section of K + J/psi -> anti-D* + D+_s
- For eta + J/psi -> anti-D* + D* and pi + J/psi -> anti-D* + D*, the ratio of dissociation rates at T/T_c=0.85 is larger than 1 when J/psi momentum is smaller than 3.3 GeV/c
- For eta + J/psi -> anti-D* + D and pi + J/psi -> anti-D* + D, the ratio at T/T_c=0.85 is larger than 1 when J/psi momentum is smaller than 0.7 GeV/c
- The ratio for eta + psi' -> anti-D* + D and pi + psi' -> anti-D* + D is smaller than 0.018
- The ratio for eta + chi_c -> anti-D* + D and pi + chi_c -> anti-D* + D is smaller than 0.08

## Context

This work extends previous quark-interchange calculations of pion-charmonium and rho-charmonium dissociation cross sections by Xu (2012) to include kaon and eta meson projectiles, which are experimentally observed to be abundant in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The results differ significantly from the meson-exchange approach of Haglin (2000) which gave much larger cross sections, and are comparable to the vacuum calculations of Wong (2004) for K-charmonium dissociation. The finding that eta + J/psi dissociation rates are comparable to pi + J/psi dissociation rates at low J/psi momenta indicates that eta-charmonium reactions must be included in studies of J/psi suppression in hadronic matter, complementing previous work that focused primarily on pion and rho meson dissociation.
