# Two-neutron transfer reaction mechanisms in $^{12}$C($^6$He,$^{4}$He)$^{14}$C using a realistic three-body $^{6}$He model

> The reaction mechanisms of the two-neutron transfer reaction $^{12}$C($^6$He,$^4$He) have been studied at 30 MeV at the TRIUMF ISAC-II facility using the SHARC charged-particle detector array. Optical potential parameters have been extracted from the analysis of the elastic scattering angular distribution. The new potential has been applied to the study of the transfer angular distribution to the 2$^+_2$ 8.32 MeV state in $^{14}$C, using a realistic 3-body $^6$He model and advanced shell model calculations for the carbon structure, allowing to calculate the relative contributions of the simultaneous and sequential two-neutron transfer. The reaction model provides a good description of the 30 MeV data set and shows that the simultaneous process is the dominant transfer mechanism. Sensitivity tests of optical potential parameters show that the final results can be considerably affected by the choice of optical potentials. A reanalysis of data measured previously at 18 MeV however, is not as well described by the same reaction model, suggesting that one needs to include higher order effects in the reaction mechanism.

## Key claim

The simultaneous (one-step) two-neutron transfer mechanism dominates the $^{12}$C($^6$He,$^{4}$He)$^{14}$C reaction at 30 MeV, but the same reaction model fails to describe data previously measured at 18 MeV, suggesting that higher-order effects in the reaction mechanism must be included at lower energies.

## Method

The experiment used a 30 MeV $^6$He beam from the TRIUMF ISAC-II facility on a 217 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ $^{12}$C target, with charged particles detected by the SHARC array covering approximately 2$\pi$ solid angle. Elastic scattering angular distributions were extracted to constrain optical potential parameters, and the transfer angular distribution to the $2^+_2$ 8.32 MeV state in $^{14}$C was analyzed using finite-range Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations. The calculations employed a realistic three-body ($^4$He+n+n) model for $^6$He and advanced shell-model calculations for the carbon structure, allowing coherent summation of simultaneous and sequential (through intermediate $^{13}$C states) transfer pathways using the code fresco.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 30 MeV $^6$He on $^{12}$C target
- Target thickness: 217 $\mu$g/cm$^2$ $^{12}$C
- Average beam intensity: 8 $\times$ 10$^5$ particles per second
- Total integrated beam current on target: 77 nC
- Q-value of the $^{12}$C($^6$He,$^4$He)$^{14}$C reaction: 12.15 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy of $^6$He: $S_{2n}=0.97$ MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy of triton: $S_{2n}=6.25$ MeV
- Excitation energy of the studied state in $^{14}$C: 8.32 MeV ($2^+_2$)
- Neutron separation energy of $^{14}$C: $S_n=8.1$ MeV
- Deformation length for $^{12}$C inelastic scattering: $\delta_l=-1.34$ fm (taken from Ker96)
- Optical potential for $^6$He+$^{12}$C: real depth V=240.3 MeV, radius r$_R$=1.18 fm, diffuseness a$_R$=0.74 fm; imaginary depth W=10.0 MeV, radius r$_I$=2.10 fm, diffuseness a$_I$=1.18 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the $^{12}$C($^6$He,$^4$He)$^{14}$C reaction performed at 5.9 MeV by Ostrowski et al. (1998) and at 18 MeV by Milin et al. (2004), where the latter used a simple one-step di-neutron model. The present analysis uses a more sophisticated three-body $^6$He model and full shell-model calculations for carbon, enabling the first quantitative separation of simultaneous and sequential transfer contributions. The results enable future studies of two-neutron pairing correlations using ($^6$He,$^4$He) reactions, particularly for states in heavier nuclei where the higher Q-value (12.15 MeV) provides better matching than traditional $(t,p)$ reactions.
