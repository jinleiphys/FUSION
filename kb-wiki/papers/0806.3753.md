# Neutron Capture Rates near A=130 which Effect a Global Change to the r-Process Abundance Distribution

> We investigate the impact of neutron capture rates near the A=130 peak on the $r$-process abundance pattern. We show that these capture rates can alter the abundances of individual nuclear species, not only in the region of A=130 peak, but also throughout the abundance pattern. We discuss the nonequilibrium processes that produce these abundance changes and determine which capture rates have the most significant impact.

## Key claim

Neutron capture rates for nuclei near the A=130 peak can alter the final r-process abundance pattern not only locally but globally, with an average abundance change of 43% when all rates in the region 128<A<138 are varied by a factor of 100, comparable to the 52% change from switching between the ETFSI and FRDM mass models.

## Method

The authors performed dynamical nuclear network calculations using a parameterized neutrino-driven wind model with fixed entropy per baryon s/k=100, initial electron fraction Ye=0.26, and outflow timescales tau=0.1 s and 0.3 s. They varied individual neutron capture rates for 50 nuclei near the A=130 peak by factors of 10, 50, 100, 500, or 1000 across twelve baseline simulations that combined different mass models (ETFSI or FRDM), beta-decay rate sets (Moller 1997 or 2003), and inclusion or exclusion of experimental data. The impact was quantified by the average fractional change F, summing absolute differences in mass fractions across all nuclear species between the varied and baseline simulations.

## Key numbers

- Average abundance change of 43% when neutron capture rates for nuclei with 128<A<138 were changed by a factor of 100, compared to 52% change from switching mass models from ETFSI to FRDM
- Neutron capture rates for most nuclei vary within a factor of 100 across theoretical sets, with a handful varying up to a factor of 1000 or more
- A factor of 10 increase in the neutron capture rate of certain nuclei (darkest colored in the study) produced a 5-20% change in the overall abundance pattern F in at least one simulation
- A factor of 50 increase in other nuclei (medium shade) produced at least a 5% change in the abundance pattern
- The study used two outflow timescales: tau = 0.1 s and tau = 0.3 s

## Context

This work extends the finding of Beun et al. (2008) that a single neutron capture rate, that of Sn-130, can affect changes throughout the r-process abundance pattern. It also builds on Surman and Engel (2001), who showed that neutron-capture rates in the A=195 region influence abundances at late times, and on Rauscher (2005), who demonstrated that simultaneous modifications of all neutron capture rates can alter the time until freeze-out onset. The results enable identification of specific neutron capture rates most in need of experimental measurement to reduce nuclear physics uncertainties in r-process modeling, complementing the known importance of beta decay rates and nuclear masses reviewed by Arnould et al. (2007).
