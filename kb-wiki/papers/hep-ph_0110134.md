# Effective Lagrangians Induced by the Anomalous Wess-Zumino Action and $I^G(J^{PC})=1^-(1^{-+})$ Exotic States

> A simple dynamical model for the exotic waves with $I^G(J^{PC})=1^-(1^{-+})$ in the reactions $ρπ\toρπ$, $ρπ\toηπ$, $ρπ\toη'π$, $ ρπ\to(K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K)$, and in the related ones, is constructed beyond the scope of the quark-gluon approach. The model satisfies unitarity and analyticity and uses as a "priming" the anomalous non-diagonal $VPPP$ interaction which couples together the four channels $ρπ$, $ηπ$, $ η'π$, and $K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K$. The possibility of the resonance-like behavior of the $I^G(J^{PC})=1^-(1^{-+})$ amplitudes belonging to the $\{10\}- \{\bar{10}\}$ and $\{8\}$ representations of SU(3) as well as their mixing is demonstrated explicitly in the 1.3--1.6 GeV mass range which, according to the current experiments, is really rich in exotics.

# Effective Lagrangians Induced by the Anomalous Wess-Zumino Action and $I^G(J^{PC})=1^-(1^{-+})$ Exotic States

## Key claim
The authors construct a unitarized coupled-channel model for exotic $I^G(J^{PC})=1^-(1^{-+})$ waves that can produce resonance-like behavior in the 1.3–1.6 GeV mass range, but only under specific choices of subtraction constants and coupling strengths; the model demonstrates the *possibility* of such resonances rather than proving their existence.

## Method
The model starts from an anomalous effective Lagrangian for $VPPP$ interactions derived from the Wess-Zumino action, which generates tree-level exotic amplitudes for reactions such as $\rho\pi\to\eta\pi$, $\rho\pi\to\eta'\pi$, and $\rho\pi\to K^*\bar K+\bar K^*K$. These tree amplitudes are unitarized by summing $s$-channel loop diagrams in a $4\times4$ coupled-channel matrix equation $\tilde T_{ij}=[(\hat1-\hat h\hat\Pi)^{-1}]_{im}h_{mj}$, where the loop functions $\Pi_i$ are defined by doubly subtracted dispersion integrals with subtraction constants $C_{1i}$ and $C_{2i}$. The authors consider three SU(3) symmetry cases: (i) all amplitudes in the $\{10\}-\{\bar{10}\}$ representation ($h'=0$), (ii) all amplitudes in the $\{8\}$ representation ($h=0$), and (iii) nonet symmetry ($h'=h$).

## Key numbers
- The coupling constant constraint: $|\tilde h = F_\pi^3 h| \leq 0.4$, where $F_\pi \approx 130$ MeV, with typical values used near $\tilde h = 0.10746$.
- Subtraction constants used: in case (i) $C_{11}=0.17$ GeV$^2$, $C_{12}=1.25$ GeV$^2$; in case (ii) $C_{11}=0.34$ GeV$^2$, $C_{12}=0.67$ GeV$^2$; in case (iii) $C_{11}=0.49$ GeV$^2$, $C_{12}=0.5$ GeV$^2$.
- Reference cross sections for conventional $a_2(1320)$ production: $\sigma(\rho^0\pi^-\to a_2\to\rho^0\pi^-)\approx 5.7$ mb and $\sigma(\rho^0\pi^-\to a_2\to\eta\pi^-)\approx 2.36$ mb at $\sqrt{s}=m_{a_2}=1.32$ GeV.
- Resonance effects are demonstrated in the invariant mass region 1.3–1.4 GeV, with a similar picture obtained for the 1.5–1.6 GeV region.

## Context
This work extends the approach of using effective chiral Lagrangians with unitarization, previously applied to $\pi\pi$ scattering with $\sigma$ and $\rho$ resonances (Ref. [5] in the paper), to the exotic $1^{-+}$ channel. It builds on the bootstrap prediction by Schechter and Okubo (1964) for an exotic $1^{-+}$ state in the icosuplet representation, and responds to experimental evidence from GAMS, KEK, VES, CB, and BNL for $\pi_1$ candidates in the 1.3–1.6 GeV range. The model enables future comparison with experimental data on $\rho\pi$, $\eta\pi$, $\eta'\pi$, and $K^*K$ final states to test whether the predicted resonance-like behavior corresponds to actual exotic $\pi_1$ resonances.
