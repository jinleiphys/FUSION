# Gamow shell model description of radiative capture reactions $^6$Li$(p,γ)$$^7$Be and $^6$Li$(n,γ)$$^7$Li

> According to standard stellar evolution, lithium abundance is believed to be a useful indicator of the stellar age. However, many evolved stars like red giants show huge fluctuations around expected theoretical abundances that are not yet fully understood. The better knowledge of nuclear reactions that contribute to the creation and destruction of lithium can help to solve this puzzle. In this work we apply the Gamow shell model (GSM) formulated in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) to investigate the mirror radiative capture reactions $^6$Li$(p,γ)$$^7$Be and $^6$Li$(n,γ)$$^7$Li. The cross-sections are calculated using a translationally invariant Hamiltonian with the finite-range interaction which is adjusted to reproduce spectra, binding energies and one-nucleon separation energies in $^{6-7}$Li, $^7$Be. All relevant $E1$, $M1$, and $E2$ transitions from the initial continuum states to the final bound states $J={3/2}_1^-$ and $J={1/2}^-$ of $^7$Li and $^7$Be are included. We demonstrate that the $s$-wave radiative capture of proton (neutron) to the first excited state $J^π=1/2_1^+$ of $^7$Be ($^7$Li) is crucial and increases the total astrophysical $S$-factor by about 40 \%.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that including the $s$-wave radiative capture of a proton (neutron) to the first excited state $J^\pi=1/2_1^+$ of $^7$Be ($^7$Li) is crucial and increases the total astrophysical $S$-factor by about 40%, a detail the abstract presents as a conclusion rather than a conditional finding dependent on the specific Hamiltonian and interaction parameters used.

## Method

The authors applied the Gamow shell model formulated in the coupled-channel representation (GSM-CC) to calculate cross-sections for the mirror reactions $^6$Li$(p,\gamma)$$^7$Be and $^6$Li$(n,\gamma)$$^7$Li. They used a translationally invariant Hamiltonian with a finite-range Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) two-body interaction, adjusted to reproduce spectra, binding energies, and one-nucleon separation energies in $^{6-7}$Li and $^7$Be, with $^4$He as the inert core. Reaction channels were built by coupling the ground state $1_1^+$ and excited states $3^+_1$, $0^+_1$, $2^+_1$ of $^6$Li with the projectile wave function in different partial waves, and all relevant $E1$, $M1$, and $E2$ transitions from initial continuum states to final bound states $J={3/2}_1^-$ and $J={1/2}^-$ of $^7$Li and $^7$Be were included.

## Key numbers

- The calculated proton separation energy in $^7$Be is $S_{\rm p}^{\rm (th)} = 5.613$ MeV, agreeing well with the experimental value $S_{\rm p}^{\rm (exp)} = 5.606$ MeV.
- The $s$-wave radiative capture to the first excited state $J^\pi=1/2_1^+$ increases the total astrophysical $S$-factor by about 40%.
- The ${5/2}_1^-$ and ${5/2}_2^-$ resonances of $^7$Be appear at calculated center-of-mass energies $E_{\rm c.m.} = 1.126$ MeV and $E_{\rm c.m.} = 1.543$ MeV, respectively, compared to experimental values of $E_{\rm c.m.} = 1.1242$ MeV and $E_{\rm c.m.} = 1.604$ MeV.
- The $E2$ contribution to the total $S$-factor is approximately $10^3$ smaller than the $E1$ contribution.
- The ground state of $^6$Li is bound with respect to $^4$He by 3.658 MeV in GSM calculations, close to the experimental value of 3.698 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the GSM-CC approach, previously applied to low-energy elastic and inelastic proton scattering (Jaganathen 2014) and proton or neutron radiative capture reactions (Fossez 2015), to the specific mirror reactions $^6$Li$(p,\gamma)$$^7$Be and $^6$Li$(n,\gamma)$$^7$Li. The study addresses the long-standing puzzle of lithium abundance fluctuations in evolved stars like red giants, where better knowledge of nuclear reactions that create and destroy lithium is needed. The results enable more accurate modeling of stellar nucleosynthesis, particularly for the pp-II and pp-III reaction chains in solar neutrino production, and complement previous theoretical studies using the potential model (Barker 1980, Angulo 1999) and cluster model approaches (Arai 2002, Dubovichenko 2011).
