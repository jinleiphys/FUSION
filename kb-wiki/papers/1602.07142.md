# Impact of momentum anisotropy and turbulent chromo-fields on thermal particle production in quark-gluon plasma medium

> Momentum anisotropy present during the hydrodynamic evolution of Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) in RHIC may lead to chromo-Weibel instability and turbulent chromo-fields.The dynamics of the quark and gluon momentum distributions in this case is governed by an effective diffusive Vlasov equation (linearized). The solution of this linearized transport equation for the modified momentum distribution functions lead to the mathematical form of non-equilibrium momentum distribution functions of quarks/antiquarks and gluons. The modification to these distributions encode the physics of turbulent color fields and momentum anisotropy. In the present manuscript, we employ these distribution functions to to estimate thermal dilepton production rate in the QGP medium. The production rate is seen to have appreciable sensitivity to the strength of the anisotropy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that thermal dilepton production rates in a quark-gluon plasma are appreciably sensitive to the strength of momentum anisotropy, but this sensitivity is conditional on the presence of turbulent chromo-fields induced by chromo-Weibel instability, and the analysis is performed within a linearized (near-equilibrium) approximation where the anisotropic modification to the distribution functions is small.

## Method

The authors model the non-equilibrium momentum distribution functions of quarks/antiquarks and gluons by solving a linearized diffusive Vlasov-Boltzmann equation (the Dupree-Vlasov equation) that incorporates turbulent chromo-fields via ensemble-averaged Gaussian correlators. The isotropic (equilibrium) baseline is described by a quasi-particle model with effective fugacities that reproduce the (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD equation of state. The unknown strength of the turbulent fields is related to the phenomenologically known jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\), and the resulting modified distribution functions are then used in the kinetic theory expression for dilepton production via \(q\bar{q}\) annihilation at leading order, assuming a (1+1)-d boost-invariant longitudinal expansion.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio of QGP is cited as \(\eta/S \sim 1/(4\pi)\), described as the smallest among almost all known fluids.
- The jet quenching parameter for quarks is given in the range \(\hat{q}_q / T^3 = 4.6 \pm 1.2\) at RHIC and \(\hat{q}_q / T^3 = 3.7 \pm 1.4\) at LHC, based on phenomenological estimates from multiple approaches.
- The gluon jet quenching parameter is related to the quark value by \(\hat{q}_g \equiv (9/4) \hat{q}_q\) via Casimir invariants of SU(3).
- The dilepton production cross section for \(N_f=2\) and \(N_c=3\) is given by \(M^2 g^2 \sigma(M^2) = \frac{80\pi}{9} \alpha^2\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Asakawa et al. (2006) for turbulent chromo-fields and anomalous transport in QGP, which was originally developed for an ultra-relativistic ideal gas, to a more realistic hot QCD medium described by a lattice-based quasi-particle equation of state. The same non-equilibrium distribution functions were previously used by Chandra (2015) to study heavy-quark dynamics in anisotropic QGP. The paper enables future investigation of how momentum anisotropy and turbulent fields affect other thermal particle observables (e.g., photons) and may help resolve simultaneous descriptions of elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor for heavy quarks, as noted by Das et al. (2014).
