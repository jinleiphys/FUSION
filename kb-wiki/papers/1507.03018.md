# Analytic Boosted Boson Discrimination

> Observables which discriminate boosted topologies from massive QCD jets are of great importance for the success of the jet substructure program at the Large Hadron Collider. Such observables, while both widely and successfully used, have been studied almost exclusively with Monte Carlo simulations. In this paper we present the first all-orders factorization theorem for a two-prong discriminant based on a jet shape variable, $D_2$, valid for both signal and background jets. Our factorization theorem simultaneously describes the production of both collinear and soft subjets, and we introduce a novel zero-bin procedure to correctly describe the transition region between these limits. By proving an all orders factorization theorem, we enable a systematically improvable description, and allow for precision comparisons between data, Monte Carlo, and first principles QCD calculations for jet substructure observables. Using our factorization theorem, we present numerical results for the discrimination of a boosted $Z$ boson from massive QCD background jets. We compare our results with Monte Carlo predictions which allows for a detailed understanding of the extent to which these generators accurately describe the formation of two-prong QCD jets, and informs their usage in substructure analyses. Our calculation also provides considerable insight into the discrimination power and calculability of jet substructure observables in general.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first all-orders factorization theorem for the two-prong discriminant $D_2$, valid for both signal and background jets, but this theorem is proven at leading power in each parametrically separated phase space region and requires a novel zero-bin procedure to correctly describe the transition region between collinear and soft subjet limits.

## Method

The authors used soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive factorization theorems for three distinct subjet configurations: the soft haze region (no resolved subjet), the collinear subjets region (two hard subjets with comparable energy and small opening angle), and the soft subjet region (hierarchical energies with wide angle). They measured three energy correlation functions ($e_2^{(\alpha)}$, $e_2^{(\beta)}$, $e_3^{(\alpha)}$) to parametrically separate these regions, then marginalized the multi-differential cross section to obtain the $D_2$ distribution at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy, including non-perturbative effects through a shape function.

## Key numbers

* The parametric condition for non-overlap of one- and two-prong regions is $3\alpha \geq 2\beta$, derived from power counting of the energy correlation functions.
* For the collinear subjets configuration, the collinear-soft energy fraction scales as $z_{cs} \sim e_3^{(\alpha)} / (e_2^{(\beta)})^{3\alpha/\beta} \ll 1$, defining the two-prong region boundary.
* The one-prong region upper boundary scales as $e_3^{(\alpha)} \sim (e_2^{(\beta)})^{2}$ for $\alpha \geq \beta$, and the lower boundary scales as $e_3^{(\alpha)} \sim (e_2^{(\beta)})^{3\alpha/\beta}$ for $\alpha \geq \beta$.
* The paper shows comparison of analytic calculation with Vincia Monte Carlo predictions for boosted $Z$ bosons and massive QCD jets at a 1 TeV $e^+e^-$ collider, with non-perturbative effects included via a shape function.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory description of two-prong jets initiated by Bauer et al. (2011) for collinear subjets and Larkoski et al. (2015) for soft subjets, and applies it to the ratio-type observable $D_2$ formed from energy correlation functions. It enables systematically improvable precision comparisons between data, Monte Carlo generators, and first-principles QCD calculations for jet substructure observables, and provides analytic understanding of the discrimination power of two-prong discriminants at the LHC.
