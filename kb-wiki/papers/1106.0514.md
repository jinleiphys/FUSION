# The QCD trace anomaly

> In this brief report we compare the predictions of a recent next-to-next-to-leading order hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) calculation of the QCD trace anomaly to available lattice data. We focus on the trace anomaly scaled by T^2 in two cases: N_f=0 and N_f=3. When using the canonical value of mu = 2 pi T for the renormalization scale, we find that for Yang-Mills theory (N_f=0) agreement between HTLpt and lattice data for the T^2-scaled trace anomaly begins at temperatures on the order of 8 T_c while when including quarks (N_f=3) agreement begins already at temperatures above 2 T_c. In both cases we find that at very high temperatures the T^2-scaled trace anomaly increases with temperature in accordance with the predictions of HTLpt.

## Key claim

The paper claims that next-to-next-to-leading order hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) predictions for the QCD trace anomaly scaled by T^2 agree with lattice data at temperatures on the order of 8 T_c for pure Yang-Mills (N_f=0) and already above 2 T_c for QCD with three quark flavors (N_f=3), but only when using the canonical renormalization scale mu = 2 pi T.

## Method

The authors compared NNLO HTLpt calculations of the trace anomaly (E - 3P)/T^2 to available lattice data from the Wuppertal-Budapest, HotQCD, RBC-Bielefeld, and WHOT-QCD collaborations for both N_f=0 and N_f=3. They used both one-loop and three-loop running of the strong coupling constant, with the renormalization scale set to mu = 2 pi T as the central value, and varied mu by a factor of two in either direction to estimate theoretical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- For pure Yang-Mills (N_f=0) with mu = 2 pi T, agreement between HTLpt and lattice data for the T^2-scaled trace anomaly begins at temperatures on the order of 8 T_c.
- For N_f=3 QCD with mu = 2 pi T, agreement begins already at temperatures above 2 T_c.
- The one-loop running coupling was fixed to alpha_s(5 GeV) = 0.140553 for the pure Yang-Mills case.
- For N_f=3, the coupling was fixed to alpha_s(5 GeV) = 0.2034 based on high precision lattice measurements.
- In the pure Yang-Mills case, the lattice determination of T_c / Lambda_MSbar = 1.26 was used to fix the scale for three-loop running.

## Context

This work extends previous NNLO HTLpt calculations of the QCD equation of state (Andersen, Strickland, Su, 2010-2011) by focusing specifically on the T^2-scaled trace anomaly, which is a more sensitive observable than pressure or energy density alone. The comparison with lattice data from multiple collaborations (Wuppertal-Budapest, HotQCD, RBC-Bielefeld, WHOT-QCD) tests whether HTLpt can describe the nonperturbative regime near the phase transition, where previous weak-coupling perturbation theory fails unless temperatures are astronomically high (T ~ 10^5 GeV). The results enable future applications of HTLpt to real-time properties of the quark-gluon plasma at LHC temperatures, since the framework is formulated in Minkowski space.
