# QCD at finite chemical potential in and out-of equilibrium

> We review the transport properties of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in heavy-ion collisions at ultrarelativistic energies, i.e. out-of equilibrium, and compare them to the equilibrium properties. The description of the strongly interacting (non-perturbative) QGP in equilibrium is based on the effective propagators and couplings from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) that is matched to reproduce the equation-of-state of the partonic system above the deconfinement temperature $T_c$ from lattice QCD. We study the transport coefficients such as the ratio of shear viscosity and bulk viscosity over entropy density, diffusion coefficients, electric conductivity etc. versus temperature and baryon chemical potential. Based on a microscopic transport description of heavy-ion collisions we, furthermore, discuss which observables are sensitive to the QGP formation and its properties.

## Key claim

The paper reviews transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) both in equilibrium (using the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model, DQPM) and out-of-equilibrium (using the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics, PHSD transport approach), finding that the DQPM results for shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, electric conductivity, and baryon diffusion coefficient at vanishing baryon chemical potential agree with available lattice QCD calculations, while at finite baryon chemical potential the transport coefficients show only a weak dependence on mu_B.

## Method

The equilibrium description uses the DQPM, which defines dressed parton propagators with complex self-energies (thermal masses and widths) matched to reproduce the lattice QCD equation of state above the deconfinement temperature T_c. The coupling g^2(T, mu_B) is determined from the entropy density at mu_B=0 and extended to finite mu_B via a scaling hypothesis using an effective temperature T* and a mu_B-dependent critical temperature T_c(mu_B). Transport coefficients (shear viscosity eta, bulk viscosity zeta, electric conductivity sigma_0, baryon diffusion coefficient kappa_B) are computed using the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) from parton interaction rates, which are obtained from on-shell scattering cross sections for binary processes (q-q, q-g, g-g) evaluated with DQPM masses and couplings. The out-of-equilibrium dynamics is studied using the PHSD off-shell transport approach, which simulates heavy-ion collisions with partonic interactions based on DQPM cross sections evaluated at local temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature at vanishing chemical potential is T_c = 0.158 GeV, with the mu_B-dependent critical line given by T_c(mu_B) = T_c (1 - alpha mu_B^2)^(1/2) where alpha = 0.974 GeV^(-2).
* The DQPM coupling parameters are d = 169.934, e = -0.178434, f = 1.14631, with the Stefan-Boltzmann entropy density s_SB^QCD = 19/9 pi^2 T^3.
* The strange quark dynamical mass is m_s(T,mu_B) = m_u/d(T,mu_B) + Delta m with Delta m = 30 MeV.
* The width parameter c_m = 14.4 is used for the parton widths gamma_i(T,mu_B).
* The total parton cross sections scale as sigma_ij ~ 1/T^3 or ~ 1/T^4 depending on the channel, with sigma_ij ~ c_3/T^3 + c_4/T^4 for the g-g channel.
* The specific shear viscosity eta/s at mu_B=0 shows a minimum near T_c and increases with temperature, consistent with the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound (eta/s)_KSS = 1/(4 pi).
* The scaled electric conductivity sigma_0/T and baryon diffusion coefficient kappa_B/T^2 rise quadratically with temperature above T_c at mu_B=0.
* For the Chapman-Enskog method using massless parton cross sections, the total cross section is sigma_tot = 0.72/T^2, and the baryon conductivity is approximately constant at sigma_B^CE/T ~ 0.048 for all temperatures.
* The DQPM results are compared to lattice QCD data for N_f=2+1 from Aarts (2014) and Astrakhantsev (2019), and for pure SU(3) gauge theory from Astrakhantsev (2017, 2018), Nakamura (2004), and Meyer (2007).

## Context

This work extends the DQPM framework, originally developed by Peshier (2005) and Cassing (2007), to finite baryon chemical potential and compares the resulting transport coefficients with lattice QCD results at mu_B=0 from Borsanyi (2012, 2013) and with estimates from non-conformal holographic models by Attems (2016) and Rougemont (2017). The results enable the use of mu_B-dependent transport coefficients in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon density, particularly for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and future experiments at FAIR and NICA, and provide input for the PHSD transport approach to study observables sensitive to QGP formation at non-zero baryon chemical potential.
