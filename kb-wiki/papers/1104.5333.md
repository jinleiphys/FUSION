# Multi-neutron transfer coupling in sub-barrier 32S+90,96Zr fusion reactions

> The role of neutron transfers is investigated in the fusion process below the Coulomb barrier by analyzing 32S+90Zr and 32S+96Zr as benchmark reactions. A full coupled-channel calculation of the fusion excitation functions has been performed for both systems by using multi-neutron transfer coupling for the more neutron-rich reaction. The enhancement of fusion cross sections for 32S+96Zr is well reproduced at sub-barrier energies by NTFus code calculations including the coupling of the neutron-transfer channels following the Zagrebaev semiclassical model. We found similar effects for 40Ca+90Zr and 40Ca+96Zr fusion excitation functions.

# Multi-neutron transfer coupling in sub-barrier 32S+90,96Zr fusion reactions

## Key claim

The enhancement of sub-barrier fusion cross sections for the neutron-rich system 32S+96Zr is well reproduced by coupled-channel calculations that include multi-neutron transfer coupling via the Zagrebaev semiclassical model, but only for the more neutron-rich reaction where neutron-transfer Q-values are positive; the 32S+90Zr system, with negative Q-values, shows no such enhancement and is well described without transfer coupling.

## Method

Full coupled-channel (CC) calculations of fusion excitation functions were performed using the newly developed NTFus code, which implements the Zagrebaev semiclassical model to couple multi-neutron transfer channels (up to +4n, and tested up to +6n) into the fusion cross-section calculation. The code uses a normalized barrier distribution function and modifies the Hill-Wheeler transmission probability to account for neutron transfers, while standard CC calculations without transfer coupling were also performed for comparison. Experimental fusion-evaporation residue cross sections and barrier distributions (derived via the three-point difference method) for both 32S+90Zr and 32S+96Zr were taken from Zhang (2010) and quasi-elastic scattering data from Yang (2008).

## Key numbers

- Q-values for neutron pickup in 32S+96Zr: +0.788 MeV (+1n), +5.737 MeV (+2n), +4.508 MeV (+3n), +7.655 MeV (+4n); all positive.
- Q-values for neutron pickup in 32S+90Zr: -3.33 MeV (+1n), -1.229 MeV (+2n), -6.59 MeV (+3n), -6.319 MeV (+4n); all negative.
- Separation energies for 96Zr: 7.854 MeV (1st neutron), 6.463 MeV (2nd), 8.230 MeV (3rd), 6.733 MeV (4th).
- Deformation parameters: 32S 2+ state at 2.230 MeV with beta=0.32; 96Zr 2+ state at 1.751 MeV with beta=0.08; 96Zr 3- state at 1.897 MeV with beta=0.27 and B(E3)=51 W.u.
- Barrier distributions for both systems are nearly identical up to E_cm approximately 85 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier coupled-channel analyses of Timmers (1998) for 40Ca+90,96Zr and the quasi-elastic barrier distribution study of Yang (2008) for the same 32S+Zr systems, confirming that multi-neutron transfer with positive Q-values is the key mechanism for sub-barrier fusion enhancement in neutron-rich systems. The NTFus code calculations reproduce the 32S+96Zr data and also agree with the earlier 40Ca+96Zr results of Zagrebaev (2003) and the fragment-gamma coincidence measurements of Szilner (2011). The work enables future studies where direct measurement of neutron transfer cross sections is needed to fully understand the coupling strength between transfer and fusion channels.
