# Ab initio nuclear response functions for dark matter searches

> We study the process of dark matter particles scattering off $^{3,4}$He with nuclear wave functions computed using an ab initio many-body framework. We employ realistic nuclear interactions from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and develop an ab initio scheme to compute a general set of different nuclear response functions. In particular, we then perform an accompanying uncertainty quantification on these quantities and study error propagation to physical observables. We find a rich structure of allowed nuclear responses with significant uncertainties for certain spin-dependent interactions. The approach and results that are presented in this Paper establish a new framework for nuclear structure calculations and uncertainty quantification in the context of direct and (certain) indirect searches for dark matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that ab initio nuclear response functions for dark matter scattering off 3,4He can be computed with quantified uncertainties, finding a rich structure of allowed nuclear responses with significant uncertainties for certain spin-dependent interactions, but the results are limited to light nuclei where convergence of the no-core shell model is achievable.

## Method

The authors employ the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) in a Jacobi-coordinate harmonic oscillator basis to compute nuclear wave functions for 3,4He. They use 42 different chiral effective field theory interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLOsim) with regulator cutoffs from 450 to 600 MeV and varying maximum scattering energies in the fit data. The nuclear response functions are evaluated from one-body transition densities and single-particle matrix elements of the six independent response operators (M, Sigma', Sigma'', Delta, Phi'', and tilde-Phi') that arise from the multipole expansion of nuclear charges and currents in dark matter-nucleus scattering.

## Key numbers

- Seven different regulator cutoffs Lambda_EFT = 450, 475, 500, 525, 550, 575, 600 MeV are used for the NNLOsim potentials
- Six different maximum scattering energies T_Lab^max = 125 to 290 MeV are used to truncate the experimental NN scattering cross section database
- The EFT breakdown scale Lambda is of the order of 1 GeV
- The basis truncation is defined by restricting the total number of harmonic oscillator quanta: sum_i (2n_i + l_i) <= N_max

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic effective theory (NREFT) framework for dark matter-nucleon interactions developed by Fitzpatrick (2012) and Anand (2013) by replacing phenomenological shell model calculations with ab initio nuclear structure methods. It builds on the uncertainty quantification framework for chiral EFT interactions developed by Carlsson (2016) and applies it to dark matter detection. The approach enables future calculations of nuclear response functions with quantified theoretical uncertainties for direct detection experiments, particularly for directional detectors using helium targets as considered by Ahlen (2010) and Mayet (2016).
