# Double parton interactions initiated by direct photons in $γp$ and $γA$ collisions revisited

> We consider the double parton scattering (DPS) initiated by direct photons in $γp$ and $γA$ collisions. We extend the previously known results for photoproduction to the case of electroproduction with nonzero photon virtuality $Q^2$, and then consider the DPS for both photo and electroproduction for HERA and IEC ( electron ion collider) kinematics. The number of DPS events at IEC is shown to be of the same order as in HERA and is even larger, with increase in luminocity compensating the decrease of the center of mass energy.

## Key claim
The number of double parton scattering (DPS) events initiated by direct photons at the future Electron-Ion Collider (IEC) is of the same order as at HERA and can be even larger, because the increase in luminosity compensates for the decrease in center-of-mass energy. This conclusion depends on assuming luminosities 100–1000 times larger than HERA's for the IEC scenarios considered.

## Method
The authors extended previous results for photoproduction (Q²=0) to electroproduction with nonzero photon virtuality Q², calculating the DPS cross section for direct photons splitting into a quark-antiquark pair that each scatter off a parton from the target. They used the parton model with Sudakov variables, incorporated gauge invariance by including additional diagrams, and accounted for DGLAP evolution in leading logarithmic approximation. The cross section was then convolved with photon fluxes and integrated over phase space for HERA and IEC kinematics, using gluon PDFs from GRV and a two-gluon form factor for the proton.

## Key numbers
- For photoproduction at HERA (W=90000 GeV², L=1.7×10³¹ cm⁻²s⁻¹) with p_t=3 GeV: 3.6×10⁵ events per year for light quark jets, 1.2×10⁵ per year for charmed jets.
- For photoproduction at IEC (W=10000 GeV², L=10³⁴ cm⁻²s⁻¹) with p_t=3 GeV: 2×10⁶ events per year for light quark jets, ~10⁶ per year for charmed jets.
- For photoproduction at IEC (W=22500 GeV², L=10³³ cm⁻²s⁻¹) with p_t=3 GeV: event numbers are between the HERA and lower-energy IEC values.
- For photon-nucleus collisions with A=208 (Pb), the number of DPS events is approximately 3 times larger than for photon-proton collisions at the same luminosity, with the nuclear enhancement factor G(A)=11.5 and AU(x₃,x₄) ranging from 4.5 to 5.7.
- The longitudinal photon contribution to electroproduction is only 5–10% of the transverse photon contribution.

## Context
This work extends the earlier photoproduction results of Blok and Segev (previous paper) to electroproduction with nonzero Q², correcting several arithmetic mistakes from that prior work. It builds on the "1⊗2" mechanism for DPS developed by Blok, Dokshitzer, Frankfurt, Strikman, and others (2010–2014), which describes a perturbative gluon splitting into two partons that interact with two independent partons from the target. The results enable experimental study of short-range parton-parton correlations in nucleons and nuclei at the future Electron-Ion Collider, and contribute to understanding the underlying event in DIS collisions as studied by B. Blok (2020) and by Ceccopieri and Rinaldi (2020).
