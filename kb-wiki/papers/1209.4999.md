# Investigation of the triple-alpha reaction in a full three-body approach

> Background: The triple-alpha reaction is the key to our understanding about the nucleosynthesis and the observed abundance of $^{12}$C in stars. The theory of this process is well established at high temperatures but rather ambiguous in the low temperature regime where measurements are impossible. Purpose: Develop a new three-body method, which tackles properly the scattering boundary condition for three charged particles and takes into account both the resonant and the non-resonant reaction mechanisms on the same footing, to compute the triple-alpha reaction rate at low temperatures. Methods: We combine the R-matrix expansion, the R-matrix propagation method, and the screening technique in the hyperspherical harmonics basis. Results: Both the $2^+_1$ bound state and the $0^+_2$ resonant state in $^{12}$C are well reproduced. We also study the cluster structure of these states. We calculate the triple-alpha reaction rate for $\mathrm{T}=0.01-0.1$ GK. Conclusions: We obtain the same rate as NACRE for temperatures above 0.07 GK, but the new rate is largely enhanced at lower temperatures ($\approx 10^{12}$ at 0.02 GK). The differences are caused by the direct capture contribution to the reaction when three alpha particles can not reach the resonant energies.

# Investigation of the triple-alpha reaction in a full three-body approach

## Key claim
The authors develop a new three-body method (HHR) that treats both resonant and non-resonant reaction mechanisms on equal footing, and find that the triple-alpha reaction rate at low temperatures (below 0.07 GK) is dramatically enhanced compared to the standard NACRE rate, by approximately 12 orders of magnitude at 0.02 GK, due to direct capture contributions when three alpha particles cannot reach resonant energies.

## Method
The authors combine the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) expansion with the R-matrix expansion and R-matrix propagation technique, together with a screening method for off-diagonal Coulomb couplings, to solve the three-body scattering problem for three charged alpha particles. They use the modified Ali-Bodmer alpha-alpha potential and adjust a three-body Gaussian force to reproduce the experimental binding energy of the 2⁺₁ bound state and the 0⁺₂ Hoyle resonant state of ¹²C. The photo-dissociation cross section is computed from the electromagnetic transition strength, and the energy-averaged reaction rate is obtained by integrating over the three-body Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution.

## Key numbers
- The 2⁺₁ bound state of ¹²C is reproduced at E = -2.875 MeV (experimental value) with a three-body force strength V₀ = -15.94 MeV and range ρ₀ = 6 fm.
- The 0⁺₂ Hoyle resonant state is reproduced at E = 0.380 MeV with V₀ = -19.46 MeV and ρ₀ = 6 fm.
- The rms radius of the 2⁺₁ bound state converges to 2.459 fm, close to the microscopic result of 2.50 fm from Chernykh et al. (2007).
- The triple-alpha reaction rate is calculated for temperatures T = 0.01–0.1 GK.
- Above 0.07 GK, the new rate agrees with the NACRE rate; at 0.02 GK, the new rate is enhanced by approximately 10¹² relative to NACRE.

## Context
This work extends the efforts of Ogata et al. (2009) using the CDCC method, which gave a rate 20 orders of magnitude above NACRE at 0.02 GK, and the Madrid-Aarhus collaboration (Alvarez et al. 2007, Diego et al. 2010, Garrido et al. 2011) using hyperspherical adiabatic expansion with a three-body Breit-Wigner extrapolation (BW(3B)), which gave a rate 7 orders of magnitude above NACRE at 0.02 GK. The present HHR method resolves the large discrepancies among these prior approaches by providing a fully quantum-mechanical treatment of the three-body continuum with correct Coulomb asymptotic behavior, enabling reliable calculation of the non-resonant direct capture contribution at low temperatures without extrapolation. This new rate has significant implications for astrophysical modeling of helium burning in stars, particularly for white dwarfs and neutron stars.
