# Nuclear effects in the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process

> We compute the nuclear corrections to the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross section for inclusive dilepton production, which, when combined with the proton-proton cross section, is used to determine the flavor asymmetry in the proton sea, dbar - ubar. In addition to nuclear smearing corrections that are known to be important at large values of the nucleon's parton momentum fraction x_N, we also consider dynamical off-shell nucleon corrections associated with the modifications of the bound nucleon structure inside the deuteron, which we find to be significant at intermediate and large x_N values. We also provide estimates of the nuclear corrections at kinematics corresponding to existing and planned Drell-Yan experiments at Fermilab and J-PARC which aim to determine the dbar/ubar ratio for x < 0.6.

## Key claim

Nuclear corrections to the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross section, including both Fermi smearing and dynamical off-shell nucleon modifications, are significant at intermediate and large values of the nucleon's parton momentum fraction \(x_N\), contrary to the common assumption that such effects are negligible at the energies of existing experiments.

## Method

The authors compute the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan cross section within the weak binding approximation, factorizing the deuteron hadronic tensor into a nucleon-level tensor and a deuteron spectral function derived from nonrelativistic wave functions (AV18, CD-Bonn, WJC-1). The off-shell nucleon cross section is expanded around its on-shell limit, and the off-shell parton distribution functions (PDFs) are modeled using a relativistic spectator quark model with a single-pole approximation, extending the Kulagin-Petti approach to include sea quarks. The resulting convolution is expressed in terms of on-shell and off-shell smearing functions \(f(z)\) and \(f^{(\text{off})}(z)\), which are then applied to kinematics of existing and planned experiments.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: \(\varepsilon_d = -2.2\) MeV.
- Average transverse momentum of the bound nucleon: \(\langle {\bm p}_\perp^2 \rangle \approx p_F^2 \sim 0.1\) GeV\(^2\), with \(p_F\) the deuteron Fermi momentum.
- The off-shell smearing function \(f^{(\text{off})}(z)\) is suppressed relative to the on-shell function \(f(z)\) by about an order of magnitude, due to the small deuteron binding energy and average three-momentum distribution.
- Nuclear corrections are estimated for kinematics of the Fermilab E866 experiment and the planned E906 (SeaQuest) experiment, with effects becoming potentially larger at \(x \gtrsim 0.5\) for the lower-energy E906 experiment.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of nuclear corrections in the deuteron from deep-inelastic scattering (DIS), where smearing and off-shell effects have been included in global PDF analyses such as CJ10, CJ11, CJ12, and ABKM09, to the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process. It builds on the weak binding approximation formalism of Kulagin and Petti (2006) and the spectator model of KPW94, and addresses the need for consistent nuclear corrections across all data sets involving deuterium. The results enable more precise extraction of the \(\bar d/\bar u\) flavor asymmetry from Drell-Yan experiments at Fermilab and J-PARC, and highlight the importance of including these corrections in future global QCD fits.
