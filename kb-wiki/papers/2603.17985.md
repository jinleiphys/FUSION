# Asymptotics of superfluid Bjorken flow

> We consider the dynamics of an expanding superfluid modeled by Mueller-Israel-Stewart theory coupled to a complex scalar field with a $U(1)$ symmetry that is spontaneously broken. This is a manageable theoretical setting for explorations of the chiral phase transition of expanding quark-gluon plasma. We study the late proper-time behavior of Bjorken flow in this physical system and find that asymptotic solutions can be expressed as a transseries of a novel form, which contains factors like $τ^{-a\ln τ}$. This transseries describes how the information encoded in the initial data is diluted in the course of dissipative evolution. These solutions retain memory of the symmetry-breaking transition and describe two qualitatively different late-time behaviors of the dynamical variables, depending on condensate relaxation rate: either a purely damped fall-off or damped oscillations. The possibility that such oscillations could be imprinted in the observed outcomes of heavy ion collision experiments is the main physical insight that follows from our analysis.

# Asymptotics of superfluid Bjorken flow

## Key claim

The late-time behavior of an expanding superfluid undergoing Bjorken flow can be expressed as a transseries containing novel logarithmic factors of the form τ^{-a ln τ}, and depending on the condensate relaxation rate C_κ, the system exhibits either purely damped fall-off or damped oscillations that could potentially be imprinted in heavy ion collision observables.

## Method

The authors model an expanding superfluid using Mueller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory coupled to a complex scalar field with a spontaneously broken U(1) symmetry, undergoing Bjorken flow in Milne coordinates. They derive a system of three ordinary differential equations for the condensate ρ, effective temperature T, and shear stress parameter χ, then apply asymptotic methods to determine the late proper-time behavior as a transseries expansion. The analysis is complemented by numerical solutions of the equations of motion for various values of the condensate relaxation rate C_κ.

## Key numbers

- The critical value of the condensate relaxation rate for the chosen parameters (λ = 1/(6 T_c), m_0 = T_c/3, T_c = 1) is C_κ^(crit) = 8/3, above which oscillations disappear and the system becomes overdamped.
- The leading asymptotic temperature at late times approaches t_{0,0} = 1/2 (in units where T_c = 1).
- The purely damped transseries sector has exponential decay parameter γ_0 = 1/(2 C_τ) and logarithmic correction coefficient γ'_0 = C_η/(6 C_τ).
- The oscillatory transseries sectors have complex exponential parameters γ_± = C_κ/4 ± (1/12)√(9 C_κ² - 64) and logarithmic correction coefficients γ'_± = (C_η/12)(C_κ ± (3 C_κ² + 16)/√(9 C_κ² - 64)).
- The transport coefficients used in numerical calculations are C_η = 1/(4π) and C_τ = (2 - ln 2)/(2π), appropriate for N = 4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory.

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies of superfluid Bjorken flow by Mitra (2020) and Buza (2024) by providing an analytic asymptotic description using transseries methods. The analysis builds on the framework developed by Heller (2015) for the asymptotic expansion of Bjorken flow in MIS theory, where exponentially suppressed terms correspond to quasinormal modes. The novel logarithmic structure in the transseries resembles that previously observed in the Painlevé I equation by Aniceto (2011). The results enable future investigation of resurgence relations connecting different transseries sectors and suggest that condensate oscillations could affect particle spectra in heavy ion collisions, complementing studies of phase oscillations by Florio (2025).
