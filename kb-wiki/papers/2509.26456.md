# Experimental overview of electromagnetic radiation in heavy-ion collisions

> Electromagnetic (EM) probes such as photons and dileptons provide direct insight into the space-time evolution of the hot and dense matter formed in heavy-ion collisions. Being unaffected by strong interactions, they serve as penetrating messengers from all collision stages, from pre-equilibrium dynamics to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadronic phases. This contribution summarises recent experimental results on direct-photon and dilepton production from RHIC and LHC experiments, as well as at lower energies. Particular emphasis is given to the ongoing 'direct-photon puzzle', the study of universal scaling of direct-photon production over a large range of collision systems and energies. Recent dielectron measurements from ALICE, STAR, and HADES, as well as new experimental developments at the LHC, are presented, along with perspectives for future facilities.

# Experimental overview of electromagnetic radiation in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The longstanding "direct-photon puzzle", the simultaneous description of direct-photon yield and elliptic flow, remains unresolved, with new PHENIX data confirming the tension; however, exotic sources such as early electromagnetic fields and radiative processes in hadronisation may offer a resolution, though constraining these sources is highly non-trivial.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental results on real direct photons and dileptons (dielectrons and dimuons) from RHIC (PHENIX, STAR), LHC (ALICE, LHCb), and lower-energy facilities (HADES, NA60). Direct-photon yields and elliptic flow are measured as functions of transverse momentum and charged-particle multiplicity; dilepton excess spectra are analysed as functions of invariant mass to extract fireball temperatures via exponential fits, and results are placed on the QCD phase diagram as a function of baryochemical potential.

## Key numbers
- PHENIX Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: direct-photon yield scaling exponent alpha ≈ 1.1 as a function of charged-particle density dN_ch/deta.
- STAR Au+Au: direct-photon scaling exponent alpha ≈ 1.43, differing from the PHENIX value.
- HADES Ag+Ag at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.4 and 2.55 GeV: extracted average fireball temperature T = 82.2 ± 0.6 (stat) ± 2.9 (syst) MeV from dielectron excess spectrum.
- STAR Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 54.4 and 27 GeV: temperatures extracted in low-mass region (0.4 < m_ll < 1.2 GeV/c^2) and intermediate-mass region (1 < m_ll < 2.9 GeV/c^2), systematically above the critical temperature in the IMR.
- ALICE pp at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV: direct-photon fraction r ≈ 1–2% in both minimum-bias and high-multiplicity collisions.

## Context
This review extends the ongoing discussion of the direct-photon puzzle first highlighted by PHENIX (2000s) and compares new PHENIX (2025) and STAR (2025) results on universal scaling, which disagree on the scaling exponent. Dilepton measurements from HADES (2019, 2025), STAR (2024), and NA60 (2008) are combined to map the QCD phase diagram over a baryochemical potential range from 2.4 to 200 GeV collision energies. The results enable future temperature measurements at LHC energies (ALICE Run 3, ALICE 3) and at lower energies (NA60+ at SPS, CBM at FAIR), with particular interest in the in-medium spectral function and chiral symmetry restoration.
