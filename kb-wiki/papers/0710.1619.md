# Meissner screening masses in gluonic phase

> A numerical analysis for the Meissner mass in the simplest gluonic phase (the minimal cylindrical gluonic phase II) is performed in the framework of the gauged Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with cold two-flavor quark matter. We derive Meissner mass formulae without using the numerical second derivative. It is revealed that the gapless mode yields a characterized contribution to the Meissner mass. We also find that there are large and positive contributions from the tree gluon potential term to the transverse modes of gluons. It is shown that the simplest gluonic phase resolves the chromomagnetic instability in a rather wide region.

## Key claim

The simplest gluonic phase (minimal cylindrical gluonic phase II) resolves the chromomagnetic instability in a rather wide region of parameter space, specifically for 2SC gap parameter values 65.4 MeV < Δ₀ < 130 MeV, but the instability persists in the intermediate and strong coupling region 130 MeV < Δ₀ < 160 MeV for the transverse modes of the 4th and 5th gluons.

## Method

The authors perform a numerical analysis of Meissner screening masses in the minimal cylindrical gluonic phase II within the gauged Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with cold two-flavor quark matter, using a self-consistent solution of the gap equations and neutrality conditions. They derive Meissner mass formulae that avoid numerical second derivatives by diagonalizing the propagator inverse in the Nambu-Gor'kov space and performing the p₀ integral analytically. The analysis uses the dynamical solutions for the gluonic, single plane-wave LOFF, and 2SC/g2SC phases obtained previously in Hashimoto (2007).

## Key numbers

- The gluonic phase exists for 65.4 MeV < Δ₀ < 160 MeV, where Δ₀ is the 2SC gap parameter defined at δμ = 0, with μ = 400 MeV and Λ = 653.3 MeV.
- The chromomagnetic instability in the transverse modes of the 4th and 5th gluons is resolved for 65.4 MeV < Δ₀ < 130 MeV but reappears for 130 MeV < Δ₀ < 160 MeV.
- The squared Meissner mass for the transverse mode of the 8th gluon becomes negative in a small region around Δ₀ ≈ 150 MeV.
- The single plane-wave LOFF phase resolves the chromomagnetic instability only in the narrow region 64.9 MeV < Δ₀ < 80 MeV.
- The 2SC phase suffers from chromomagnetic instability for 134.6 MeV < Δ₀ < 160 MeV, while the g2SC phase does for Δ₀ < 134.6 MeV.
- The tree gluon potential term contributes positive contributions of order B² to the transverse Meissner masses, where the gluon condensate B is of order 100-250 MeV in the almost whole region where the gluonic phase exists.

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau approach of Gorbar (2005, 2007) by performing a self-consistent numerical analysis outside the scaling region around δμ ≈ Δ/√2, and builds on the self-consistent solutions of Hashimoto (2007) which showed the gluonic phase is energetically more favorable than the normal, 2SC/g2SC, and single plane-wave LOFF phases. The results demonstrate that the simplest gluonic phase resolves the chromomagnetic instability previously identified in the g2SC phase by Huang (2004) and in the 2SC phase, though not completely; the authors suggest that more complicated gluonic phases such as the cylindrical gluonic phase I or the gluonic color-spin locked phase might completely remove the instability.
