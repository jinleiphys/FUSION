# Transport Theories for Heavy Ion Collisions in the 1 AGeV Regime

> We compare multiplicities as well as rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of protons, pions and kaons calculated within presently available transport approaches for heavy ion collisions around 1 AGeV. For this purpose, three reactions have been selected: Au+Au at 1 and 1.48 AGeV and Ni+Ni at 1.93 AGeV.

## Key claim

The paper compares multiplicities and distributions of protons, pions, and kaons from multiple transport simulation codes for heavy ion collisions around 1 AGeV, finding that while the codes agree qualitatively for heavy systems, significant quantitative differences remain even after enforcing common inputs such as a constant Delta lifetime; the authors caution that the predictive power of these programs is limited by uncertainties in resonance lifetimes, elementary cross sections, and in-medium potentials.

## Method

The authors compared eight different transport simulation programs (BUU/VUU and QMD-based approaches) for three selected reactions: Au+Au at 1 and 1.48 AGeV and Ni+Ni at 1.93 AGeV, all at an impact parameter of b = 1 fm. They enforced a common constant Delta resonance lifetime of 1/120 MeV for most comparisons and, for kaon production, used the same cross sections for the NDelta and DeltaDelta channels to isolate differences arising from other physics inputs. The codes were run without experimental filters, and results for proton, pion, and kaon rapidity and transverse momentum distributions were compared directly.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au at 1 AGeV: average squared longitudinal proton momentum ranges from 0.131 to 0.165 GeV^2/c^2 across programs; average squared out-of-plane momentum ranges from 0.102 to 0.124 GeV^2/c^2; the ratio <p_y^2>/<p_z^2> ranges from 0.63 to 0.89
- Au+Au at 1.48 AGeV: average squared longitudinal proton momentum ranges from 0.219 to 0.262 GeV^2/c^2; out-of-plane ratio ranges from 0.49 to 0.72
- Ni+Ni at 1.93 AGeV: average squared longitudinal proton momentum ranges from 0.300 to 0.416 GeV^2/c^2; out-of-plane ratio ranges from 0.26 to 0.42
- The choice of Delta lifetime (tau_iso versus tau_wig) can yield up to 60% more pions with tau_iso, almost independent of energy and system size
- Experimental total pion yield per participant for central Au+Au at 1 AGeV is approximately 0.075, corresponding to about 30 pions in the most central collisions
- The experimental ratio pi-/pi+ at 1 AGeV is close to 1.95, consistent with exclusive production via Delta resonances
- Switching on the kaon-nucleon repulsive potential decreases the total K+ yield by about a factor of 2 for heavy systems and less for the light system
- The compressibility of nuclear matter extracted from K+ yields points to kappa less than or equal to 280 MeV (soft equation of state)

## Context

This work extends the earlier cascade approaches of Yariv and Frankel and Cugnon, and the BUU/VUU approaches of Aichelin and Stoecker, by providing a systematic intercomparison of the major transport codes that have matured over two decades. The comparison identifies that the Delta resonance lifetime treatment (tau_iso versus tau_wig from Wigner time delay) is a major source of pion yield differences, and that subthreshold K+ production is sensitive to the nuclear equation of state, building on proposals by the KaoS collaboration (Sturm) to use the ratio of K+ yields from heavy versus light systems as a compressibility probe. The results enable future work to focus on improving the treatment of resonance lifetimes in matter, the extrapolation of elementary cross sections to subthreshold energies, and the implementation of in-medium spectral functions from Kadanoff-Baym approaches (Cassing, Leupold, Knoell).
