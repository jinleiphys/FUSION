# Benchmarking Faddeev and transfer-to-the-continuum calculations for (p, pN ) reactions

> Nucleon-knockout reactions on proton targets (p, pN ) have experienced a renewed interest due to the availability of inverse-kinematics experiment with exotic nuclei. Various theoretical descriptions have been used to describe these reactions, such as the Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA), the Faddeev-type formalism and the Transfer to the Continuum method. Our goal is to benchmark the observables computed with the Faddeev and Transfer to the Continuum formalisms in the intermediate energy regime relevant for the experimental (p, pn) and (p, 2p) studies. In this paper, we analyze the 11 Be(p,pn)10Be reaction for different beam energies, binding energies and orbital quantum numbers with both formalisms to assess their agreement for different observables. We obtain a good agreement in all cases considered, within 10%, when the input potentials are taken consistently and realistically.

## Key claim
The Faddeev/AGS and Transfer-to-the-Continuum formalisms produce observables that agree within approximately 10% for the 11Be(p,pn)10Be reaction across different beam energies, binding energies, and orbital quantum numbers, provided the input potentials are taken consistently and realistically.

## Method
The authors benchmarked the Faddeev/AGS and Transfer-to-the-Continuum (TC) formalisms by calculating observables for the 11Be(p,pn)10Be reaction. The TC method reduces the reaction to an effective three-body problem using the prior form of the transition amplitude and expands the final three-body wave function in discretized p+N eigenstates. The Faddeev/AGS method solves exact three-body integral equations in momentum-space partial-wave representation for transition operators. Both calculations used consistent input potentials for the same reaction at multiple beam energies.

## Key numbers
- Agreement between Faddeev/AGS and TC formalisms is within approximately 10% for all considered cases.
- The benchmark was performed for the 11Be(p,pn)10Be reaction at intermediate energies relevant for experimental (p,pn) and (p,2p) studies.
- The Faddeev/AGS method in its present implementation is restricted to nuclei not heavier than nickel due to convergence limitations with Coulomb screening.

## Context
This work extends a previous benchmark between the Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) and the Transfer-to-the-Continuum formalism by Yoshida et al. (2018) to include a systematic comparison between Faddeev/AGS and TC methods. The results establish the consistency and accuracy of both approaches for (p,pN) reactions, enabling more reliable extraction of spectroscopic information from nucleon-knockout experiments on exotic nuclei at radioactive isotope beam facilities. The benchmark addresses the long-standing puzzle of asymmetry dependence of reduction factors (Rs) in one-nucleon removal reactions, which was highlighted by systematic studies of knockout reactions on 9Be and 12C targets by Gade et al. (2008) and Tostevin et al. (2014).
