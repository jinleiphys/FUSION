# Toward the application of large-$N$ deconfinement to SU(3) QCD

> It is generally known for $\mathrm{U}(N)$ gauge theory at finite temperature that phase transitions are manifested by taking the large-$N$ limit. Since the large-$N$ theory undergoes two thermodynamic phase transitions, a nontrivial intermediate phase can be realized in addition to the phases classified as the conventional confined and deconfined phases. In this talk, we discuss that a similar picture can be applied to QCD with $N=3$. In particular, we analyze the gauge configurations of lattice QCD calculations involving dynamical quarks and show the results of an analysis of the deviation due to finite-temperature effects from the Haar randomness expected at zero temperature in $\mathrm{SU}(N)$ gauge theory, using physical pictures suggested by the large-$N$ theory.

## Key claim

The large-\(N\) deconfinement picture, which features an intermediate partially-deconfined phase between confined and fully-deconfined phases, can be applied to SU(3) QCD with dynamical quarks by using the infinite spatial volume to mimic the large-\(N\) limit, provided that the eigenphase distribution of the Polyakov line deviates from the Haar-random distribution expected at zero temperature.

## Method

The author analyzed lattice QCD gauge configurations generated by the WHOT-QCD collaboration for \(N_f = 2+1\) QCD with \(O(a)\)-improved Wilson quarks and RG-improved Iwasaki gauge action at lattice spacing \(a \simeq 0.07\) fm. The eigenphase distribution of the spatially-averaged Polyakov line was computed at several temperatures and compared to the SU(3) Haar-random distribution \(\rho_\mathrm{Haar}(\theta) = \frac{1}{2\pi}(1 + \frac{2}{3}\cos(3\theta))\). Multiply-wound Polyakov loops and characters in the fundamental, adjoint, rank-2 symmetric, and rank-3 symmetric representations were evaluated to characterize the thermal phases.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing \(a \simeq 0.07\) fm; up- and down-quark mass heavier than physical mass with \(m_\pi/m_\rho \simeq 0.63\); strange-quark mass almost physical.
* Temperatures analyzed: \(T = 174, 232, 279, 348\) MeV, corresponding to temporal lattice extents \(N_t = 16, 12, 10, 8\).
* At low temperatures, the multiply-wound Polyakov loops satisfy \(\tilde{\rho}_{n \ne 3} = 0\) and \(\tilde{\rho}_3 = \frac{1}{3}\), consistent with Haar randomness.
* \(\langle\chi_{\rm fund.}\rangle\) becomes nonzero at around \(174\) MeV; \(\langle\chi_{\rm adj.}\rangle\), \(\langle\chi_{\rm 2\mathchar`-sym.}\rangle\), and \(\langle\chi_{\rm 3\mathchar`-sym.}\rangle\) depart from zero at higher temperatures (\(T \gtrsim 348\) MeV).
* The author speculates that \(T \ge 199\) MeV should be the partially deconfined phase.

## Context

This work extends the large-\(N\) deconfinement framework developed by Sundborg (1999), Aharony et al. (2003), and Hanada et al. (2016, 2018) to finite-\(N\) SU(3) QCD with dynamical fermions. It builds on the concept of partial deconfinement, where the Polyakov line phase distribution exhibits two-phase coexistence in color space, and applies it to lattice QCD configurations from the WHOT-QCD collaboration (Umeda et al., 2012). The results enable future studies of the relationship between the GWW transition analog and instanton condensation (Taniguchi et al., 2016), and may help connect finite-\(N\) deconfinement to black hole physics via gauge/gravity duality.
