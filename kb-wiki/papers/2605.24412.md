# Low-lying $Λ$ and $Σ$ resonances studied with the forward $K^*$ productions off the proton induced by high-momentum $π$ beam

> We develop a novel model utilizing the forward $K^*$ production reaction off the nucleon, $πN \to K^* MB$, induced by a high-momentum $π$ beam, as a tool to study low-lying $Y^*$ resonances below and just above the $\bar{K}N$ threshold. Because conventional $K^- p$ scattering experiments face difficulties in directly accessing this kinematic region, the proposed reaction offers a valuable complementary approach for $Y^*$ spectroscopy. The constructed model is based on the one-meson exchange mechanism, which is known to dominate forward-angle production at high energies, and the half-off-shell scattering amplitudes from the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) models (Model~A and Model~B). We predict various observables, including differential cross sections and angular distributions. Our results demonstrate significant enhancements in the sub-threshold region of the invariant mass spectra. Notably, we show that overlapping resonances, such as a potential new $3/2^+$ $Σ$ state and the well-established $Σ(1385)3/2^+$, can constitute a single peak in the $πΛ$ mass spectrum, indicating that the existence of previously unconfirmed sub-threshold states cannot be ruled out by analyzing only the existing mass spectrum data. Furthermore, we find that angular distributions provide strong discriminatory power to disentangle such overlapping states through partial-wave interference effects, while the $t'$ and $ϕ_M^*$ dependencies provide crucial constraints on the high-energy production mechanisms.Our predictions for these highly sensitive observables can facilitate high-statistics measurements, which are accessible at modern hadron facilities such as J-PARC, to unravel the $S=-1$ $Y^*$ mass spectrum.

# Low-lying Λ and Σ resonances studied with the forward K* productions off the proton induced by high-momentum π beam

## Key claim

The forward πN → K* MB reaction, dominated by one-meson exchange at high beam momenta, can serve as a complementary tool to access low-lying Y* resonances below and just above the K̅N threshold, a kinematic region difficult to probe via conventional K⁻p scattering. The authors show that overlapping resonances such as a potential new 3/2⁺ Σ state and the well-established Σ(1385)3/2⁺ can produce a single peak in the πΛ mass spectrum, meaning that the existence of previously unconfirmed sub-threshold states cannot be ruled out by analyzing only existing mass spectrum data.

## Method

The authors constructed a model for the πN → K* MB reaction based on t-channel one-meson exchange (K̅ and K̅* exchange), which dominates forward-angle production at high energies. The half-off-shell scattering amplitudes for the exchanged-meson-nucleon to meson-baryon subprocesses were taken from the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) models (Model A and Model B) developed in 2014, which were fit to over 17,000 data points for K⁻p reactions. The finite width of the outgoing K* meson (decaying to πK) was incorporated via a weight function integrated over the K* invariant mass range 0.85–0.95 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The 2014 ANL-Osaka DCC analysis identified 18 Y* resonances in Model A and 20 in Model B within the energy region from the K̅N threshold up to W = 2.1 GeV
- In the low-lying region (pole mass real part below 1.5 GeV), 5 Y* resonances were found in Model A and 7 in Model B
- A new 3/2⁺ Σ resonance was found at pole mass 1303 − i13 MeV in Model A and 1360 − i38 MeV in Model B, with the Model A state lying approximately 80 MeV below the Σ(1385)3/2⁺
- The well-established Σ(1385)3/2⁺ was fixed at pole mass 1381 − i20 MeV as a constraint in the χ² fitting
- A 1/2⁻ Σ resonance at 1381 − i150 MeV was found in Model A, and a 1/2⁺ Λ resonance at 1401 − i155 MeV was found in Model B
- The weight function for K* decay shows a clear resonance peak within the range 0.85–0.95 GeV, setting the integration limits for the K* invariant mass

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the ANL-Osaka DCC model (Kamano 2014, Kamano 2015) for S = −1 meson-baryon reactions, which successfully reproduced K⁻p scattering data but were limited to energies above the K̅N threshold. The authors had previously investigated the K⁻d → πYN reaction (Kamano 2016) as an alternative probe, successfully reproducing J-PARC E31 data published in 2023, but noted that combined fits including deuteron reactions are computationally impractical. The present πN → K* MB reaction offers a more tractable alternative that can access sub-threshold Y* resonances, with predictions that can be tested at modern hadron facilities such as J-PARC. The possible existence of a low-lying 1/2⁻ Σ resonance has been widely investigated both experimentally and theoretically (Wang 2024, Belle 2022, He 2025), and this reaction could help clarify its nature.
