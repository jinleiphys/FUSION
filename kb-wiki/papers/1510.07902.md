# Charmonium Production with QGP and Hadron Gas Effects at SPS and FAIR

> The production of charmonium in heavy-ion collisions is investigated based on Boltzmann-type transport model for charmonium evolution and langevin equation for charm quark evolution. Charmonium suppression and regeneration in both quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadron phase are considered. Charm quarks are far from thermalization, and regeneration of charmonium in QGP and hadron gas is neglectable at SPS and FAIR. At peripheral collisions, charmonium suppression with hadron gas explains the experimental data well. But at central collisions, additional suppression from deconfined matter (QGP) is necessary for the data. This means there should be QGP produced at central collisions, and no QGP produced at peripheral collisions at SPS energy. Predictions are also made at FAIR $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

At SPS and FAIR energies, charmonium regeneration from charm quark recombination is negligible because charm quarks are far from thermalization and retain strong back-to-back momentum correlations; the data can be explained by suppression from hadron gas alone in peripheral collisions, but require additional suppression from deconfined matter (QGP) in central collisions, implying that QGP is produced only in central collisions at SPS energy.

## Method

The paper uses a (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model with net baryon density conservation to evolve the medium, coupled with a Boltzmann-type transport equation for charmonium phase-space distributions in both QGP and hadron gas phases. Charm quark evolution is treated via a Langevin equation with a drag coefficient proportional to \(T^2\), and the probability of \(c\bar{c}\) recombination into charmonium is evaluated from the distance and relative momentum of the pair during their stochastic evolution. Cold nuclear matter effects (nucleon absorption and Cronin effect) are included in the initial charmonium distributions.

## Key numbers

- At SPS \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV Pb+Pb collisions, the maximum temperature at the center of the medium is about 215 MeV at FAIR \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV Au+Au collisions.
- The formation time of \(J/\psi\) in its local rest frame is \(\tau_{J/\psi}\approx0.35\ \mathrm{fm/c}\), and in the lab frame \(\tau_{J/\psi}^\mathrm{Lab}\approx0.37\ \mathrm{fm/c}\).
- The dissociation temperatures for charmonium states are \(T_d(J/\psi, \chi_c, \psi^\prime)=(2.3, 1.1, 1.1)T_c\).
- The inelastic cross-section for nucleon absorption \(\sigma^{abs}\) is taken as \(4.3\ \mathrm{mb}\) for \(J/\psi\) and \(\chi_c\), and \(7.9\ \mathrm{mb}\) for \(\psi^\prime\) at SPS; at FAIR, \(\sigma^{abs}\) is \(4.5\ \mathrm{mb}\) for \(J/\psi\) and \(\chi_c\), and \(7.9\ \mathrm{mb}\) for \(\psi^\prime\).
- The Cronin effect parameter \(a_{gN}=0.076\ \mathrm{GeV^2/fm}\).
- The average transverse momentum square of \(J/\psi\) in pp collisions at FAIR is \(\langle p_t^2\rangle_{pp}=1.13\ \mathrm{(GeV/c)^2}\).
- The total cross-section ratio \(\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar c}/\sigma_{pp}^{J/\psi}=150\).
- The freeze-out temperature of the medium is \(T_{\mathrm{freeze-out}}=0.12\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the transport-model approach of Yan (2006) and Liu (2010) to low colliding energies at SPS and FAIR, where hadron gas effects become comparable to QGP effects. It contrasts with higher-energy studies at RHIC and LHC (e.g., Adare 2006, Abelev 2009) where charmonium regeneration is significant. The results provide predictions for the upcoming FAIR experiment at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV Au+Au collisions, and the two limits (with and without QGP) serve as benchmarks to identify the onset of deconfinement at low energies.
