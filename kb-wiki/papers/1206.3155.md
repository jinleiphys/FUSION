# Different Methods for the Two-Nucleon T-Matrix in the Operator Form

> We compare three methods to calculate the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix based on the three-dimensional formulation of J. Golak et al., Phys. Rev. C 81, 034006, (2010). In the first place we solve a system of complex linear inhomogeneous equations directly for the t-matrix. Our second method is based on iterations and a variant of the Lanczos algorithm. In the third case we obtain the t-matrix in two steps, solving a system of real linear equations for the k-matrix expansion coefficients and then solving an on-shell equation, which connects the scalar coefficients of the k- and t-matrices. A very good agreement among the three methods is demonstrated for selected nucleon-nucleon scattering observables using a chiral next-to-next-to-leading-order neutron-proton potential. We also apply our three-dimensional framework to the demanding problem of proton-proton scattering, using a corresponding version of the nucleon-nucleon potential and supplementing it with the (screened) Coulomb force, taken also in the three-dimensional form. We show converged results for two different screening functions and find a very good agreement with other methods dealing with proton-proton scattering.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three different methods for calculating the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix in a three-dimensional operator formalism (direct matrix inversion, an iterative Lanczos-based scheme, and a two-step k-matrix approach) yield very good agreement for neutron-proton and neutron-neutron scattering observables, but the k-matrix method is numerically unstable for certain choices of which operator is eliminated on-shell, specifically when the w6 operator is removed at 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix using a three-dimensional operator expansion into six spin-momentum operators, avoiding partial wave decomposition. They compare three numerical approaches: (1) direct solution of a system of complex linear inhomogeneous equations via matrix inversion using SCALAPACK; (2) an iterative method using a variant of the Lanczos algorithm to sum the Neumann series; (3) a two-step method solving a real linear system for the k-matrix coefficients, then solving an on-shell equation connecting k- and t-matrix scalar coefficients after eliminating one of the five linearly dependent on-shell operators. For proton-proton scattering, the screened Coulomb force is added in three-dimensional form using two different screening functions.

## Key numbers

- Calculations performed at projectile laboratory kinetic energies of 13 MeV and 300 MeV using a chiral next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) neutron-proton potential
- For the iterative method at 13 MeV: 10 iterations are fully sufficient for neutron-neutron; 12 iterations needed for neutron-proton
- At 300 MeV: 8 iterations bring results very close to convergence for neutron-neutron; 10 iterations still show slight deviations for neutron-proton
- For proton-proton scattering at 13 MeV with exponential screening (n=4): convergence achieved at R = 120 fm
- For proton-proton scattering with localized sine-function screening: convergence achieved at R = 55 fm
- Discrepancies between fully 3D calculations and other methods (Vincent-Phatak method and combined PWD/3D approach) for proton-proton observables do not exceed 1-2%
- Typical numerical grid: n_x = 36, n_p = 36, n_phi = 60 Gaussian points

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional operator formalism for nucleon-nucleon scattering introduced by Golak et al. (2010) in Physical Review C 81, 034006, which avoided partial wave decomposition. The iterative method developed here has already been successfully applied to three-nucleon bound state calculations by the same group. The proton-proton scattering results with screened Coulomb force are compared to the Vincent-Phatak method and a combined partial-wave/3D approach from the authors' prior work (referred to as romek in the text), showing good agreement. The methods enable efficient calculation of the full off-shell t-matrix needed for three-nucleon scattering calculations without partial wave decomposition.
