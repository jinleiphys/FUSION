# Interplay between bulk medium evolution and (D)GLV energy loss

> We study the consistency between high-pT nuclear suppression (R_AA) and elliptic flow (v2) using Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) energy loss or a simpler power-law dE/dL formula, for a variety of bulk evolution models. The results generally confirm our earlier work [arXiv:1305.1046] that found suppressed elliptic flow for transversely expanding media. One exception is the set of hydrodynamic solutions used recently [arXiv:1305.6458] by Betz and Gyulassy, which give significantly higher v2 but unfortunately assume unrealistic bag-model equation of state. On the other hand, we show that covariant treatment of energy loss introduces an interplay between jet direction and hydrodynamic flow of the medium, which largely counteracts elliptic flow suppression caused by transverse expansion.

## Key claim
The paper confirms the authors' earlier finding that transverse expansion of the bulk medium suppresses high-\(p_T\) elliptic flow, but shows that a covariant treatment of energy loss introduces a jet-medium flow coupling that largely counteracts this suppression. The exception is the hydrodynamic solutions used by Betz and Gyulassy (2013), which give significantly higher \(v_2\) but assume an unrealistic bag-model equation of state.

## Method
The authors computed neutral pion nuclear suppression (\(R_{AA}\)) and differential elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) at midrapidity in mid-peripheral Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, using either the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) radiative energy loss formula or a simpler power-law \(dE/dL = \kappa E^{1/3} L^1 T^{8/3}\) parameterization. They tested five different bulk medium evolution models: four boost-invariant 2+1D hydrodynamic solutions from the TECHQM Collaboration (including ideal hydrodynamics with a bag-model equation of state and viscous hydrodynamics with lattice QCD equation of state) and one covariant parton transport evolution using Molnar's Parton Cascade (MPC). They then implemented a covariant formulation of energy loss by requiring that energy loss contributions be computed in the local rest frame of the fluid.

## Key numbers
- Neutral pion \(R_{AA}\) was fixed to \(\sim 0.4\) at \(p_T \sim 15-20\) GeV for all scenarios.
- Elliptic flow \(v_2\) at high \(p_T\) was reduced to \(\sim 4-5\%\) for all bulk evolution models except the ideal-fKLN (bag-model) evolution used by Betz and Gyulassy.
- The impact parameter for the Au+Au collisions was \(b \approx 7.5\) fm.
- With covariant energy loss, the jet-medium flow coupling produced a significant enhancement of \(v_2\) that largely canceled the suppression from transverse expansion.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study (Molnar and Sun, 2013) that found suppressed elliptic flow for transversely expanding media using GLV energy loss. It directly addresses a contradiction with Betz and Gyulassy (2013), who claimed simultaneous reproduction of \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) using simple pQCD-motivated energy loss formulas. The paper shows that Betz and Gyulassy's result is largely due to their use of an unrealistic bag-model equation of state, and that realistic lattice QCD equations of state give lower \(v_2\). The covariant treatment of energy loss introduces a new interplay between jet direction and hydrodynamic flow that was not present in earlier calculations, and this finding enables future work to constrain initial geometry through the modest \(\sim 15\%\) difference between fKLN and Glauber initial profiles with viscous hydrodynamics.
