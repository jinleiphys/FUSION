# Investigating the Impact of Higher-Order Phase Transitions in Binary Neutron-Star Mergers

> In this paper we investigate quark deconfinement in neutrons stars and their mergers, focusing on the effects of higher orders for the phase transition between hadronic and quark matter. The different descriptions we use to describe matter microscopically contain varying particle degrees of freedom, including nucleons, hyperons, Delta baryons, and light and strange quarks. We use tabulated equations of state from the CompOSE database in which the quark deconfinement phase transition is described as being first-order, and then smooth it out by introducing a percolation, replacing the single first-order phase transition with two transitions of second or third order. We then perform binary neutron-star merger simulations using these new equations of st ate, focusing on groups of binaries with the same single-star mass, radius, and tidal deformability, but different equations of state. We go on to discuss differences in their evolution, and the ramifications for interpreting future gravitational wave observations and the potential to learn about dense matter.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether replacing a single first-order hadron-to-quark deconfinement phase transition with two higher-order (second or third order) transitions, via a percolation procedure, produces observable differences in binary neutron-star merger gravitational-wave signals, specifically for stars that have the same mass, radius, and tidal deformability but different underlying equations of state.

## Method

The authors start with three tabulated zero-temperature equations of state from the CompOSE database (two from the Chiral Mean Field model, CMF-2 and CMF-7, and one from a Relativistic Density Functional model, RDF 1.7) that each contain a first-order phase transition between hadronic and quark matter. They replace the first-order transition with a percolation region using a fifth-order polynomial in baryon chemical potential, matching pressure and its first or second derivatives at the boundaries to create second-order or third-order phase transitions. They then perform binary neutron-star merger simulations using the GR-Athena++ general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamics code, focusing on groups of binaries that share the same single-star mass, radius, and tidal deformability but use different percolated equations of state.

## Key numbers

* CMF-2 recurring region: stellar mass 1.675 solar masses, radius 12.7 km, tidal deformability approximately 159.8-159.9.
* CMF-7 recurring region: stellar mass 1.500 solar masses, radius 12.8 km, tidal deformability between 328.1 and 338.6.
* RDF 1.7 recurring region (pair 1): stellar mass 1.600 solar masses, radius 11.8 km, tidal deformability approximately 123.8-124.4.
* RDF 1.7 recurring region (pair 2): stellar mass 1.675 solar masses, radius 11.9 km, tidal deformability approximately 97.7-98.0.
* Simulation grid resolution: 184 meters on the finest grid, with a Courant-Friedrichs-Lewy number of 0.25.
* Maximum masses supported by the percolated EoSs range from 2.00 to 2.15 solar masses, consistent with observations of heavy neutron stars.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of quark deconfinement in neutron-star mergers, particularly those using mixed-phase equations of state (Blacker 2020, Prakash 2021, Haque 2022) and earlier percolation studies (Huang 2022, Most 2022, Kedia 2022). The key novelty is the use of "recurring regions" in mass-radius and mass-tidal deformability diagrams (detailed in Clevinger 2025), which allow the authors to construct groups of binaries with identical inspiral properties but different equations of state, isolating the post-merger signal for comparison. The results enable future work on whether current and third-generation gravitational-wave detectors (such as the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer) can distinguish between first-order and higher-order phase transitions in dense matter.
