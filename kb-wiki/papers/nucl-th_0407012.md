# Transverse momentum versus multiplicity fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We discuss recently measured event-by-event fluctuations of transverse momentum and of multiplicity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It is shown that the non-monotonic behavior of the p_T-fluctuations as a function of collision centrality can be fully explained by the observed non-monotonic multiplicity fluctuations. A possible mechanism responsible for the multiplicity fluctuations is also considered.

## Key claim

The non-monotonic behavior of transverse momentum fluctuations as a function of collision centrality can be fully explained by the observed non-monotonic multiplicity fluctuations, assuming the correlation between mean transverse momentum and multiplicity in nucleus-nucleus collisions is the same as in proton-proton interactions. This explanation excludes various exotic interpretations of the transverse momentum fluctuations, but the mechanism behind the multiplicity fluctuations themselves remains unresolved.

## Method

The authors derive an analytic expression for the Φ-measure of transverse momentum fluctuations in terms of the scaled variance Var(N)/⟨N⟩ of the multiplicity distribution, assuming a weak correlation between event multiplicity and the slope parameter of the transverse momentum distribution. They fit the parameters T = 137 MeV and δT = 15.5 MeV to NA49 p-p data on average pT versus multiplicity, then compute Φ(pT) from Eq. (5) using the experimental multiplicity fluctuations from NA49. A Monte Carlo simulation is also performed, generating multiplicities from a Negative Binomial Distribution with the experimental mean and variance, assigning transverse momenta from the thermal distribution with the fitted parameters, and computing Φ(pT) for each centrality bin.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient relating Φ(pT) to Var(N)/⟨N⟩ is √2 (δT)²/T ≈ 2.48 MeV, derived from the fitted parameters T = 137 MeV and δT = 15.5 MeV for p-p collisions at 158-A-GeV.
- The multiplicity fluctuations Var(N)/⟨N⟩ show a peak at Nw ≈ 70 wounded nucleons, with values close to unity at Nw ≤ 10 and Nw ≥ 250.
- The correlation function ν(r) changes sign at r ≈ 4 fm, corresponding to Nw ≈ 70, and vanishes for r ≳ 7 fm.
- The p-p data used for fitting were measured in the transverse momentum interval (0.005, 1.5) GeV and rapidity interval (4.0, 5.5) at 158-A-GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of transverse momentum fluctuations measured by the NA49 Collaboration (Anticic 2003) and multiplicity fluctuations (Gazdzicki 2004, Rybczynski 2004) at SPS energies, showing that the Φ-measure of pT fluctuations is dominated by the multiplicity fluctuations through the pT-vs.-multiplicity correlation. The result contradicts an earlier conclusion by Korus (2001) that this correlation produces too small a Φ value, because the earlier estimate used p-p data at 205 GeV in full phase-space rather than the forward-rapidity NA49 data. The paper enables future studies to focus on the physical origin of the multiplicity fluctuations themselves, which remain unexplained, and suggests that other fluctuation measures (F from PHENIX, Δσ and σdyn from STAR) are similarly influenced by multiplicity fluctuations.
