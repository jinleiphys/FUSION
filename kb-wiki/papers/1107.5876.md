# Effect of isospin dependent cross-section on nuclear stopping

> Nuclear stopping in heavy ion collisions (HIC) has been studied by means of rapidity distribution and asymmetry of nucleon momentum distribution. It is an important quantity in determining the outcome of a reaction. Fen Fu {\it et al.,} \cite{2} calculate both the radial flow and the degree of nuclear stopping in Pb + Pb and Ni + Ni at 0.4, 0.8 and 1.2 GeV/neucleon. They found that the expansion velocity as well as the degree of nuclear stopping are higher in the heavier system at all energies.

## Key claim
Nuclear stopping in heavy ion collisions is influenced by the isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon cross-section, with the effect being most pronounced in systems with less neutron content, where the isospin-dependent cross-section plays a dominant role alongside the symmetry energy.

## Method
The authors performed simulations using the Isospin dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model for several thousand events at 100 MeV/nucleon for eight systems of mass 124 with varying proton number (Z from 47 to 59). They compared three different cross-section treatments: isospin-dependent cross-section (σ_iso), isospin-independent cross-section (σ_noiso), and an enhanced cross-section where neutron-neutron and proton-proton cross-sections were increased.

## Key numbers
- Incident energy: 100 MeV/nucleon for all simulated systems.
- Symmetry energy parameter: 32(ρ/ρ₀)^γ MeV with γ = 0.66.
- For the Sn+Sn system, the stopping parameter R = 0.7022.
- In the isospin-dependent cross-section treatment, neutron-neutron and proton-proton cross-sections are a factor of three lower than the neutron-proton cross-section.

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear stopping by Fen Fu et al. (2011), who examined radial flow and stopping in Pb+Pb and Ni+Ni at 0.4, 0.8, and 1.2 GeV/nucleon, finding higher stopping in heavier systems. The present study focuses on the lower energy of 100 MeV/nucleon and systematically varies the isospin content of the colliding nuclei to isolate the effect of isospin-dependent cross-sections. The results enable a more nuanced understanding of how the isospin dependence of nucleon-nucleon collisions affects nuclear stopping, which is relevant for constraining transport models used to extract the nuclear equation of state.
