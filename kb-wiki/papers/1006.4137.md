# Augmented Lagrangian Method for Constrained Nuclear Density Functional Theory

> The augmented Lagrangiam method (ALM), widely used in quantum chemistry constrained optimization problems, is applied in the context of the nuclear Density Functional Theory (DFT) in the self-consistent constrained Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) variant. The ALM allows precise calculations of multidimensional energy surfaces in the space of collective coordinates that are needed to, e.g., determine fission pathways and saddle points; it improves accuracy of computed derivatives with respect to collective variables that are used to determine collective inertia; and is well adapted to supercomputer applications.

# Augmented Lagrangian Method for Constrained Nuclear Density Functional Theory

## Key claim
The augmented Lagrangian method (ALM) enables precise constrained self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations that always converge to the requested constraint values, regardless of the chosen Lagrange multiplier, thereby accessing regions of the multidimensional collective energy surface that are inaccessible to the standard quadratic penalty method (QPM) when the primal function is locally concave.

## Method
The authors implement the ALM algorithm within the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework using the diagonalization-based HFB solvers HFODD v.2.43c and HFBTHO. The ALM replaces the fixed requested constraint value \(q^0\) in the QPM penalty term with an effective value \(q^0(\lambda) = q^0 - \lambda/2c\), where the Lagrange multiplier \(\lambda\) is iteratively updated according to \(\lambda^{k+1} = \lambda^k + 2c(q - q^0)\) until the self-consistent solution yields the originally requested constraint. The method is tested on a two-dimensional grid of quadrupole (\(Q_{20}\)) and octupole (\(Q_{30}\)) moments for the spontaneous fission of \(^{252}\)Fm using the SkM* energy density functional and a seniority pairing force.

## Key numbers
- The single-particle basis consisted of the lowest 1,140 stretched states originating from the lowest 31 major oscillator shells.
- The difference between contour lines on the two-dimensional total energy surface for \(^{252}\)Fm is 5 MeV.
- The ALM algorithm was tested with the HFB solvers HFODD v.2.43c and HFBTHO.

## Context
This work extends the constrained optimization techniques used in nuclear density functional theory, specifically addressing the well-known failure of the linear constraint method (LCM) in concave regions of the energy surface (discussed by Giraud and Flocard in 1970 and 1973) and the systematic inaccuracy of the quadratic penalty method (QPM), which never delivers exactly the requested constraint values. The ALM approach, originally developed by Hestenes (1969) and Powell (1969) for quantum chemistry, is here adapted for nuclear CHFB calculations; it enables precise mapping of multidimensional fission pathways and saddle points, including the discovery of a second (fusion) valley in \(^{252}\)Fm that was inaccessible to QPM. This method is intended for large-scale calculations of fission pathways and collective inertia, and is well-suited for supercomputer implementations.
