# Differential Hanbury-Brown-Twiss for an exact hydrodynamic model with rotation

> We study an exact rotating and expanding solution of the fluid dynamical model of heavy ion reactions, that take into account the rate of slowing down of the rotation due to the longitudinal and transverse expansion of the system. The parameters of the model are set on the basis of realistic 3+1D fluid dynamical calculation at TeV energies, where the rotation is enhanced by the build up of the Kelvin Helmholtz Instability in the flow.

## Key claim

The Differential Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (DHBT) method can detect rotation in an exact, rotating, and expanding hydrodynamic model of heavy ion collisions, but the differential correlation function vanishes if either rotation or radial expansion is eliminated, and the effect is only observable at the level of 2-3% for estimated rotation velocities of ω = 0.01–0.15 c/fm.

## Method

The authors numerically evaluate the two-pion correlation function C(k,q) and the differential correlation function ΔC(k,q) = C(k⁺,q) – C(k⁻,q) for an exact analytic solution of relativistic hydrodynamics with cylindrical symmetry, where the source has a flat temperature profile of 250 MeV and a velocity field consisting of radial expansion, longitudinal expansion, and rotation in the reaction plane. The correlation functions are computed as a function of q_out for three different times (t = 0, 3, and 8 fm/c) using parameters taken from a realistic 3+1D fluid dynamical calculation at TeV energies, with detectors placed at two different azimuthal angles in the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- Temperature profile is flat at 250 MeV throughout the source.
- At t = 0 fm/c: R = 2.500 fm, Ṙ = 0.250 c, Y = 4.000 fm, Ẏ = 0.300 c, ω = 0.150 c/fm.
- At t = 3.0 fm/c: R = 3.970 fm, Ṙ = 0.646 c, Y = 5.258 fm, Ẏ = 0.503 c, ω = 0.059 c/fm.
- At t = 8.0 fm/c: R = 7.629 fm, Ṙ = 0.779 c, Y = 8.049 fm, Ẏ = 0.591 c, ω = 0.016 c/fm.
- The differential correlation function amplitude reaches 2-3% for rotation velocities ω = 0.01–0.15 c/fm.
- Correlation functions are shown for k values of 0.2 fm⁻¹, 1 fm⁻¹, and 5 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the DHBT method, previously introduced by Csernai et al. (2015) and applied to a Particle-in-Cell Relativistic (PICR) fluid dynamical model, to an exact analytic hydrodynamic solution with rotation originally developed by Csörgő et al. (2014) and others. The results show that the DHBT signal is sensitive to the combined presence of rotation and expansion, and that the effect can be maximized by optimal detector placement, enabling experimental searches for rotation in heavy ion collisions at TeV energies where the Kelvin-Helmholtz instability enhances vorticity.
