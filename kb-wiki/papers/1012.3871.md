# Neutrino interactions: challenges in the current theoretical picture

> The present theoretical status of neutrino interactions in the few-GeV region is reviewed. Quasielastic scattering, pion production, photon emission and their importance for neutrino oscillation studies are discussed, making emphasis on the open questions that arise in the comparison with new experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that current theoretical models of neutrino-nucleus interactions in the few-GeV region systematically underestimate several measured cross sections, and that simply fitting new data by adjusting parameters like the axial mass \(M_A\) is a dangerous long-term strategy; instead, the discrepancies point to missing nuclear effects such as two-particle-two-hole (\(2p-2h\)) contributions.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical status of quasielastic scattering, incoherent and coherent pion production, and single photon emission in neutrino-nucleus interactions at energies of 1–3 GeV. The review compares predictions from models including the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG), spectral functions (SF), relativistic mean field (RMF), local Fermi gas (LFG) with random phase approximation (RPA) correlations, and transport models (GiBUU, NuWRO) against experimental data from MiniBooNE, NOMAD, SciBooNE, and ANL/BNL bubble chamber experiments.

## Key numbers

- The axial mass from early CCQE measurements on deuterium is \(M_A = 1.016 \pm 0.026\) GeV (Bodek, 2007).
- From chiral perturbation theory and pion electroproduction, \(\tilde{M}_A = 1.069 \pm 0.016\) GeV, and after hadronic correction \(M_A = 1.014 \pm 0.016\) GeV (Liesenfeld, 1999; Bernard, 1992).
- MiniBooNE CCQE data at \(E_\nu = 0.8\) GeV: theoretical cross sections \(\sigma_{th} \sim 4.5-5 \times 10^{-38}\) cm\(^2\) versus experimental \(\sigma_{exp} \sim 7 \times 10^{-38}\) cm\(^2\).
- Fitting MiniBooNE CCQE data with the RFG model yields \(M_A = 1.35 \pm 0.17\) GeV (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2010); NOMAD at high energies finds \(M_A = 1.05 \pm 0.02\) (stat) \(\pm 0.06\) (syst) GeV (Lyubushkin, 2008).
- For the N-\(\Delta\) axial transition, \(C_5^A(0) = 1.22 \pm 0.06\) from BNL data (Alvarez-Ruso, 1998); a fit including nonresonant backgrounds gives \(C_5^A(0) = 1.00 \pm 0.11\) with \(M_{A\Delta} = 0.93 \pm 0.07\) GeV (Hernandez, 2010).
- NOMAD NC\(\pi^0\) coherent cross section at 3–100 GeV: \(\sigma = [72.6 \pm 8.1(\text{stat}) \pm 6.9(\text{syst})] \times 10^{-40}\) cm\(^2\), consistent with the Rein-Sehgal prediction of \(78 \times 10^{-40}\) cm\(^2\) (Kullenberg, 2009).
- SciBooNE CC/NC coherent pion production ratio at \(\langle E_\nu \rangle \sim 0.8\) GeV: \(\sigma(\text{CCcoh}\pi^+)/\sigma(\text{NCcoh}\pi^0) = 0.14^{+0.30}_{-0.28}\) (Kurimoto, 2010); theoretical models predict ratios in the range 1–2.

## Context

This review extends the discussion of neutrino-nucleus interactions beyond the standard RFG treatment (Smith, 1972) and the Rein-Sehgal model for pion production (Rein, 1980; Rein, 1982), which have been the basis of most event generators used in oscillation experiments. The paper highlights the "CCQE puzzle" where all known calculations underestimate MiniBooNE data, and the "coherent pion production puzzle" where the SciBooNE CC/NC ratio contradicts all model predictions. It enables future work by identifying the need to incorporate \(2p-2h\) contributions (Martini, 2009), meson-exchange currents (Amaro, 2010), and improved axial form factors into event generators for precision oscillation measurements.
