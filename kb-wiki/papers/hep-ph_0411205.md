# $g_{s}$-factor quenching with quantum statistics

> We discuss the effect of quantum statistics on the $g_{s}$-factor quenching in nuclear medium. We have found that the two nucleon correlation function can be described by a Gaussian with correlation length $σ\sim1.2$ fm. An estimate of the M1 quenching strength is given. The effect of quantum statistics on the retardation of the paramagnetic susceptibility is also discussed.

## Key claim

Quantum statistics (the indistinguishability of nucleons) produces spin correlations that contribute to the quenching of the spin-M1 transition strength and the \(g_s\)-factor in nuclei, beyond the mechanisms of core polarization and \(\Delta\)-isobar excitation that have been studied previously. The effect is quantified by a mixing parameter \(p(r)\) that can be approximated by a Gaussian with correlation length \(\sigma = 1.2\) fm, and the resulting \(g_s\) quenching follows a phenomenological form \(g_s^{\mathrm{qs}} = 1 - \gamma A \exp(\beta A^{2/3})\).

## Method

The authors use a Fermi gas model to compute the two-nucleon space-spin density matrix, from which they extract the spin correlation function \(P(\theta) = p \cos(\theta)\) and the mixing parameter \(p = f^2/(2-f^2)\) with \(f(r) = 3j_1(k_f r)/k_f r\). They approximate \(p(r)\) as a Gaussian \(p(r) = e^{-r^2/2\sigma^2}\) with \(\sigma = 1.2\) fm, and then estimate the suppression of M1 transition strength by assuming that the fraction of nucleon pairs in a singlet state (controlled by \(p(r)\)) leads to a quenched effective \(g_s\) factor. The model parameters \(\gamma\) and \(\beta\) are fitted to experimental M1 quenching data.

## Key numbers

* Correlation length of the Gaussian approximation for the mixing parameter: \(\sigma = 1.2\) fm.
* Fermi momentum used for nuclear matter: \(k_f \sim 1.27\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
* Typical nucleon separation used: \(r \sim 1.2\) fm.
* Fitted parameters for the quenching formula \(g_s^{\mathrm{qs}} = 1 - \gamma A \exp(\beta A^{2/3})\): \(\gamma = 0.008 \pm 0.002\) and \(\beta = -0.04 \pm 0.008\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} \sim 2.6\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of spin-M1 quenching beyond the standard explanations involving \(\Delta\)-isobar excitation (which the Sakai group found insufficient for Gamow-Teller transitions) and core polarization (which fails for heavy nuclei according to Akito). The authors explicitly note that quantum statistics effects on nucleon spin correlations have been rarely studied in nuclear physics, citing Bertulani's earlier work on weakly bound nucleons. The results enable experimental tests via two-proton radioactivity and Coulomb dissociation of nuclei such as \({}^{11}\mathrm{Li}\), and provide a framework for incorporating spin correlations into calculations of M1 strength and paramagnetic susceptibility.
