# Electromagnetic signal of the QCD phase transition in neutron star mergers

> Mergers of binary neutron stars create conditions of supranuclear density $n\gtrsim n_{\rm nuc}\simeq 0.17 {\rm fm}^{-3}$ and moderate temperature $50\lesssim T \lesssim 90 {\rm MeV}$. These events thus probe a sensitive region of the density-temperature phase diagram of QCD matter. We study photon production by the QCD conformal anomaly for a signal of a possible transition to quark degrees of freedom during the merger. We discuss energy loss due to photon radiation as a cooling mechanism that is sensitive to the bulk viscosity and thermal conductivity of the quark matter.

## Key claim

Photon production via the QCD conformal anomaly could serve as an electromagnetic signal of a hadron-to-quark phase transition during binary neutron star mergers, but the effect depends on the enhancement of the conformal anomaly and bulk viscosity near the phase transition, which are not precisely known for hot, high-density quark matter.

## Method

The authors analytically estimate the temperature (50–90 MeV) and density (0.3–2.4 times nuclear saturation density) achieved in the surface layers of merging neutron stars using post-Newtonian kinematics and baryon number conservation. They then compute the photon production rate from the QCD conformal anomaly using an effective low-energy Lagrangian coupling a scalar dilaton field (representing the trace of the energy-momentum tensor) to two photons, with the dilaton spectral function related to bulk viscosity via the Kubo formula. The energy loss rate is evaluated for two channels: diphoton emission and single-photon emission assisted by an external magnetic field, accounting for photon dispersion in the dense plasma and considering only emission from a boundary layer of thickness equal to the photon mean-free path (~100 fm).

## Key numbers

- Temperature range achieved in the collision: 50–90 MeV, with a conservative estimate of 48 ± 1 MeV and an upper estimate of 85 ± 2 MeV from post-Newtonian kinematics for a total mass of 3 solar masses and symmetric mass ratio 1/4.
- Density achieved in the overlapping surface layers: 0.3–2.4 times nuclear saturation density (n_nuc ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³), corresponding to baryon chemical potentials 944–1240 MeV at zero temperature.
- Photon mean-free path in the dense plasma: ~100 fm, much smaller than the 10 m–10 km scale of the merging stars.
- Effective coupling constant for the dilaton–two-photon interaction: g_σγγ ≈ (50 GeV)⁻¹, with dilaton mass m_σ ≈ 550 MeV and decay constant f_σ ≈ 100 MeV.
- Plasma mass scale at twice nuclear saturation density: m_pl ≈ 15–20 MeV.
- Energy loss rate from the diphoton channel: 7.13 × 10³ GeV/(fm³·s) × (ζ/s) × (s/s₀) × (m_pl/15 MeV)¹⁰ × I_2γ(β m_pl), where ζ is bulk viscosity, s is entropy density, and I_2γ is a dimensionless integral.
- Cooling time scale τ_E at T = 50 MeV: on the order of (ζ/s)⁻¹ × 10⁻¹⁵ s, much shorter than the weak interaction time scale of 10⁻⁷–10⁻⁶ s.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of photon production from the QCD conformal anomaly, previously studied for quark-gluon plasma in collider experiments by Basar et al. (2012), to the astrophysical context of binary neutron star mergers. The authors build on numerical simulations of neutron star mergers by Bauswein et al. (2009, 2011, 2012) and Sekiguchi et al. (2011), and on lattice QCD studies showing enhancement of the trace anomaly near the phase transition by Bazavov et al. (2009) and Basak et al. (2009). The proposed mechanism could provide an ultraprompt electromagnetic signal (tens of MeV photons) that escapes within the first tens of nanoseconds of the merger, potentially distinguishable from bremsstrahlung and Compton backgrounds, and could complement gravitational wave observations by LIGO and Virgo in probing the dense matter equation of state.
