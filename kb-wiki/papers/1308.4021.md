# Barrier penetration effects in the triple-alpha reaction at low energies

> We investigate the triple-alpha reaction at low energies, by assuming a direct process. The Coulomb potential of three $α$ particles is examined carefully. The three-body continuum wave functions are generated by calculating an adiabatic potential barrier. We discuss the influence of the $αα$ potential, and compare our reaction rates with the literature. The reaction rate at $T=0.01$\,GK is about 10$^3$ times larger than that of NACRE.

## Key claim

The triple-alpha reaction rate at very low temperatures (T=0.01 GK) is about 10³ times larger than the NACRE rate, but this enhancement is many orders of magnitude smaller than the 20-order-of-magnitude increase claimed by Ogata et al. (2009) using the CDCC method, which the authors argue is incompatible with observation.

## Method

The authors model the triple-alpha reaction as a direct three-body capture process using hyperspherical coordinates and an adiabatic potential barrier constructed from the Coulomb and nuclear interactions of three alpha particles. They diagonalize the charge factor operator q(Ω) to find the minimum Coulomb barrier, then compute the adiabatic potential by averaging over generator-coordinate wave functions with the constraint of fixed hyperradius ρ, using both Ali-Bodmer A′ and D αα potentials. The E2 photoabsorption cross section from a simple 2⁺ bound state of ¹²C to the 0⁺ three-alpha continuum is calculated from the radial integral of the continuum wave function obtained by solving the radial Schrödinger equation with the adiabatic potential.

## Key numbers

- The minimum eigenvalue of the charge factor q(Ω) approaches 3.002 at Kmax=70, corresponding to a regular triangle configuration of three alpha particles
- The adiabatic potential barrier peak is 1.1 MeV at ρ=12.4 fm for the AB(A′) potential and 1.5 MeV at ρ=9.3 fm for the AB(D) potential
- The resonance in the AB(A′) adiabatic potential has energy 0.702 MeV and width 6 keV, compared to the Hoyle state values of 0.379 MeV and 8.5 eV
- The photoabsorption cross section at E=0.05 MeV is 4×10⁴⁰ times the NACRE value for AB(A′) and 9×10⁴⁰ for AB(D), compared to 6×10⁴⁴ for the BW model and 1×10³⁸ for the Faddeev calculation
- The reaction rate at T=0.01 GK is about 10³ times larger than the NACRE rate of 2.9×10⁻⁷¹ cm⁶ s⁻¹ mol⁻²
- The non-symmetrized Coulomb barrier is smaller by about 7% for any ρ compared to the symmetrized one, approaching 4e²×3.35/ρ instead of 4e²×3.60/ρ

## Context

This work addresses the controversy following Ogata et al. (2009), who used the CDCC method to find reaction rates 20 orders of magnitude above NACRE at low temperatures, which was incompatible with stellar models. The authors extend the direct three-body capture approach of Garrido et al. (2011) and the hyperspherical R-matrix method of Nguyen et al. (2012), and compare with the Faddeev calculations of Ishikawa (2013). The results confirm that above T≈0.1 GK all models agree with NACRE, but at lower temperatures the present results lie between the BW upper limit and the Faddeev lower limit, suggesting that the Hoyle state's direct three-alpha decay width Γ₃α is considerably smaller than its total width, as noted by Kirsebom (2012).
