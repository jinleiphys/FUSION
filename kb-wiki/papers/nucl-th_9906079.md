# Multifragmentation of charge asymmetric nuclear systems

> The multifragmentation of excited spherical nuclear sources with various N/Z ratios and fixed mass number is studied within dynamical and statistical models. The dynamical model treats the multifragmentation process as a final stage of the growth of density fluctuations in unstable expanding nuclear matter. The statistical model makes a choice of the final multifragment configuration according to its statistical weight at a global thermal equilibrium. Similarities and differences in the predictions of the two models on the isotopic composition of the produced fragments are presented and the most sensitive observable characteristics are discussed.

## Key claim

For neutron-rich spherical sources, the dynamical (Stochastic Mean Field) and statistical (Statistical Multifragmentation Model) approaches predict opposite trends in the average N/Z ratio of hot primary fragments as a function of fragment charge, and the statistical model yields a factor of 2 to 5 larger dispersion in that ratio; however, these differences are largely washed out in the cold fragments seen by detectors, though they may survive in the isotopic ratios of light charged particles emitted from primary fragments.

## Method

The authors performed Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) dynamical simulations of the multifragment breakup of A=197 isobars with Z=95, 79, and 63, starting at an initial density of 0.5 times the equilibrium density, with a temperature of 3 MeV and a radial flow energy of 3 MeV/nucleon, and followed the evolution for 150 fm/c. In parallel, they applied the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) to the same sources with the same excitation energy of 8 MeV/nucleon and a freeze-out volume of 3 times the ground-state volume. For each source, 100 SMF runs were performed, and the hot fragments from both models were then subjected to the same secondary deexcitation procedure to produce cold fragments.

## Key numbers

- Source mass number: A=197 isobars with Z=95, 79, and 63.
- Initial source temperature: T=3 MeV.
- Initial density for SMF: 0.5 times the equilibrium density (rho_eq = 0.17 fm^-3).
- Radial flow energy added: 3 MeV/nucleon.
- Total excitation energy per nucleon: E*/A = 8 MeV.
- Freeze-out volume for SMM: V_f.o. = 3 V_0, where V_0 = 4 pi R_0^3 / 3 and R_0 = 1.2 A^(1/3).
- SMF evolution time: 150 fm/c.
- For the most neutron-rich source (Z=63, N/Z=2.13), the dynamical calculation gives a decrease of the average N/Z ratio with charge number Z for 3 ≤ Z ≤ 10, while the statistical calculation shows an increase.
- The dispersion of the N/Z ratio for hot fragments in the statistical calculation is a factor of 2 to 5 larger than in the dynamical calculation.
- The difference in fragment excitation energies between SMF and SMM is no more than 0.5 MeV/nucleon.
- The yield ratios of ^4He to ^3He and ^3H to ^3He emitted from primary IMFs are always larger in the dynamical calculation than in the statistical one, with the difference increasing for the high-asymmetric source (Z=63).

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of statistical and dynamical multifragmentation models (Dur98, Riv98, Bou99) by focusing on isospin-sensitive observables, and builds on prior studies of spinodal decomposition in charge-asymmetric nuclear matter (CDL98, BCDL98) and the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (BBIMS95). The results suggest that comparing fragmentation data for extremely neutron-rich sources (N/Z ~ 2) could help disentangle the two pictures and, if dynamical signatures are confirmed, provide a new method to study the symmetry energy of the nuclear equation of state at subnuclear densities, relevant for unstable nuclei and astrophysics (PR93).
