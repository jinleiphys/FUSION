# The imprint of the equation of state on the axial w-modes of oscillating neutron stars

> We discuss the dependence of the pulsation frequencies of the axial quasi-normal modes of a nonrotating neutron star upon the equation of state describing the star interior. The continued fraction method has been used to compute the complex frequencies for a set of equations of state based on different physical assumptions and spanning a wide range of stiffness. The numerical results show that the detection of axial gravitational waves would allow to discriminate between the models underlying the different equation of states, thus providing relevant information on both the structure of neutron star matter and the nature of the hadronic interactions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the complex frequencies of axial w-modes of nonrotating neutron stars depend sensitively on the equation of state (EOS) of the star interior, and that the range of pulsation frequencies is more distinctive for each EOS than in the polar case, but only for stars that are not compact enough to support the slowly damped s-modes.

## Method

The authors computed the axial quasi-normal mode frequencies for six different EOSs (models A, B, WFF, APR1, APR2, and L) by solving the Regge-Wheeler equation outside the star and the Schrodinger-like axial perturbation equation inside the star. They used the continued fraction method, originally developed by Leaver for black hole quasi-normal modes, adapted to the stellar case by matching the interior numerical solution to an exterior power-series expansion at a matching point outside the star.

## Key numbers

- The EOSs are ordered by increasing stiffness as: B, A, APR2, APR1, WFF, L.
- The supernuclear density threshold for the EOS models is epsilon > 2.8 x 10^14 g/cm^3.
- The matching point a in the continued fraction method must satisfy a/2 < R < a and a > 2 (in units where c=G=2M=1).
- The asymptotic behavior of the expansion coefficients is a_n ~ n^{-3/4 + i omega (a+1)} exp( +/- 2 sqrt(2 i omega a n) ) for large n.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron star quasi-normal modes, such as the determination of f-mode frequencies by Lindblom and Detweiler (1983) and the computation of polar w-mode frequencies by Andersson and Kokkotas (1998). The authors use EOS models A, B, L, and WFF that have been widely used in previous neutron star studies (Arnett and Bowers 1977; Pandharipande, Pines and Smith 1976; Wiringa, Fiks and Fabrocini 1988), and introduce the newer APR1 and APR2 models (Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall 1998) that had not previously been applied to nonstatic neutron star properties. The results enable future gravitational wave detections to discriminate between different EOS models and thus constrain the structure of neutron star matter and hadronic interactions.
