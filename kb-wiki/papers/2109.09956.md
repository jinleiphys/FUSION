# Lambda polarization in $^{108}$Ag +$^{108}$Ag and $^{197}$Au +$^{197}$Au collisions around a few GeV

> Within the framework of Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, we extract the global spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperon in $^{108}$Ag + $^{108}$Ag and $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.42 - 62.4$ GeV. We use two different approaches to calculate the $Λ$ polarization $P_y$: approach I is based on equilibrium assumption so that $P_y$ is determined by thermal vorticity and approach II assumes a proportional relation between $P_y$ and the system's angular momentum in $Λ$'s rest frame. We find that both approaches can describe the experimental data at low energies around a few GeV but only approach I describes well also higher-energy data. This suggests that at higher energies the relativistic effect plays an important role. After taking into such effect properly, the relativity-improved approach II can describe the higher-energy data as well.

## Key claim

Both a thermal-equilibrium approach (approach I, based on thermal vorticity) and a non-relativistic angular-momentum approach (approach II) can describe the experimental global Lambda polarization data at low collision energies around a few GeV, but only approach I also describes higher-energy data; after a relativistic correction is applied, the modified approach II can describe higher-energy data as well.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate Ag+Ag and Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies per nucleon pair from 2.42 to 62.4 GeV. They extracted the global spin polarization of Lambda hyperons using two methods: approach I assumes spin equilibrium and computes polarization from the thermal vorticity tensor at the Lambda freeze-out point; approach II assumes a proportional relation between the Lambda spin vector and the relative orbital angular momentum of surrounding particles in the Lambda rest frame, with a constant susceptibility chi = 1/20 fitted to data.

## Key numbers

- Global Lambda polarization in 108Ag+108Ag collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.55 GeV, for 0.2 < p_T [GeV/c] < 1.5 and |Y| < 0.3, as a function of centrality (10-40%): both approaches reproduce HADES data.
- Global Lambda polarization in 197Au+197Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV, for p_T > 0.7 GeV/c and -0.2 < Y < 1, as a function of centrality (10-40%): both approaches reproduce STAR data.
- Global Lambda polarization as a function of collision energy from sqrt(s_NN) = 2.5 GeV to 62.4 GeV for 197Au+197Au collisions at 0.4 < p_T [GeV/c] < 3.0 and |Y| < 0.3: approach I describes data across the full range; approach II deviates above about 15 GeV; the relativity-improved approach II' describes the full range.
- The susceptibility chi in approach II is set to a constant value of 1/20.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of kinematic and thermal vorticities by Deng et al. (2020) to directly extract Lambda spin polarization at low collision energies (2.42-62.4 GeV), a region less explored than the higher-energy RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. It compares with recent measurements from the HADES Collaboration (2021) at 2.4-2.55 GeV and the STAR Collaboration (2021) at 3 GeV, and with calculations by Y. B. Ivanov et al. (2021), Guo et al. (2021), and Ayala et al. (2021). The results enable predictions for the existence and location of a maximum in global Lambda polarization as a function of collision energy, which could be tested by future experiments at NICA, FAIR, HIAF, and the STAR fixed-target program.
