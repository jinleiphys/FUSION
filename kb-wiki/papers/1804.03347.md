# Low-Energy Gamow-Teller Transitions in deformed $N = Z$ odd-odd Nuclei

> We have investigated the Gamow-Teller transitions from ${}^{22}\textrm{Ne}$ to ${}^{22}\textrm{Na}$ by applying the isospin projected antisymmetrized molecular dynamics combined with generator coordinate method. We have found that the GT strength from $^AZ(J^πT)={}^{22}\textrm{Ne}(0_1^+1)$ is fragmented into two final states ${}^{22}\textrm{Na}(1_{1,2}^+0)$, which belong to $K=0$ and $K=1$ bands constructed by a prolately deformed $^{20}$Ne core with a $S=1$ proton-neutron ($pn$) pair. Coupling of the intrinsic-spin of the $pn$ pair with the core deformation play an important role in the GT fragmentation. The symmetry breaking in the intrinsic-spin rotation leads to the $SU(4)$-symmetry breaking of the $NN$ pair and causes the GT fragmentation. We have compared the features of the GT transitions with those for ${}^{10}\textrm{Be}\to{}^{10}\textrm{B}$ and discuss the link of the $SU(4)$-symmetry and the GT fragmentation in the deformed systems.

## Key claim

The Gamow-Teller (GT) strength from the initial state \(^{22}\mathrm{Ne}(0_1^+1)\) is fragmented into two final states \(^{22}\mathrm{Na}(1_{1,2}^+0)\) belonging to \(K=0\) and \(K=1\) bands, rather than being concentrated into a single final state as in lighter systems; this fragmentation arises from the coupling of the intrinsic spin of a proton-neutron (\(pn\)) pair with the prolate deformation of the \(^{20}\mathrm{Ne}\) core, which breaks the \(SU(4)\) symmetry of the \(NN\) pair.

## Method

The authors applied the isospin-projected antisymmetrized molecular dynamics combined with the generator coordinate method (\(T\beta\gamma\)-AMD+GCM) to calculate the low-lying states and GT transitions of \(^{22}\mathrm{Ne}\to{}^{22}\mathrm{Na}\). Wave functions were constructed as Slater determinants of Gaussian wave packets, with parity, isospin, and angular momentum projections performed before energy variation under constraints on the deformation parameters \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\). The Volkov No. 2 central force with Majorana exchange parameter \(m=0.6\) and the G3RS spin-orbit force were used as the effective interactions.

## Key numbers

* \(B(\mathrm{GT};0_1^+1\to 1_1^+0) = 1.98\) (calculated) compared to the experimental value 0.949(28) for the mirror transition \(^{22}\mathrm{Mg}\to{}^{22}\mathrm{Na}\).
* \(B(\mathrm{GT};0_1^+1\to 1_2^+0) = 1.55\) (calculated) compared to the experimental value 1.43(8) for the mirror transition.
* The ground state \(^{22}\mathrm{Na}(3_1^+0)\) has a quadrupole moment \(Q = 17.66\) \(e\,\mathrm{fm}^2\) (calculated) versus the experimental value 18.0(11) \(e\,\mathrm{fm}^2\).
* The magnetic moment of \(^{22}\mathrm{Na}(3_1^+0)\) is \(\mu = 1.784\) \(\mu_N\) (calculated) versus the experimental value 1.746(3) \(\mu_N\).
* The magnetic moment of \(^{22}\mathrm{Na}(1_1^+0)\) is \(\mu = 0.622\) \(\mu_N\) (calculated) versus the experimental value 0.535(10) \(\mu_N\).
* The ground state binding energy of \(^{22}\mathrm{Na}\) is 173.041 MeV (calculated) versus the experimental value 174.1456 MeV.
* The \(K=3\) band head \(3_1^+0\) has a 89.7% overlap with the \(K=3\) projected basis at deformation \(\beta=0.29\) and \(\gamma=0.19\).
* The \(K=0\) band head \(1_1^+0\) has an 82.7% overlap with the \(K=0\) projected basis at \(\beta=0.31\) and \(\gamma=0.11\).
* The \(K=1\) band head \(1_2^+0\) has a 76.6% overlap with the \(K=1\) projected basis at \(\beta=0.29\) and \(\gamma=0.19\).

## Context

This work extends the study of GT transitions in deformed \(N=Z\) odd-odd nuclei beyond the \(p\)-shell system \(^{10}\mathrm{Be}\to{}^{10}\mathrm{B}\) (previously studied by the same authors with the same method) into the \(sd\)-shell region. The fragmentation of GT strength into two final states in \(^{22}\mathrm{Ne}\to{}^{22}\mathrm{Na}\) contrasts with the concentration observed in lighter systems such as \(^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) and \(^{10}\mathrm{B}\), where the \(pn\) pair couples weakly to the core and approximately maintains \(SU(4)\) symmetry. The results enable further investigation of the competition between deformation and \(pn\) pairing in medium-mass \(N=Z\) odd-odd nuclei, and provide a framework for interpreting GT strength distributions measured via charge-exchange reactions such as \((^{3}\mathrm{He},t)\) experiments.
