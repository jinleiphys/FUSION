# Tensor glueball-meson mixing phenomenology

> The overpopulated isoscalar tensor states are sifted using Schwinger-type mass relations. Two solutions are found: one where the glueball is the fJ(2220), and one where the glueball is more distributed, with f2(1820) having the largest component. The f2(1565) and fJ(1710) cannot be accommodated as glueball-(hybrid) meson mixtures in the absense of significant coupling to decay channels. f2'(1525) -> pi pi is in agreement with experiment. The fJ(2220) decays neither flavour democratically nor is narrow.

## Key claim

The paper uses Schwinger-type mass relations to sift the overpopulated isoscalar tensor states, finding two possible solutions: one where the glueball is the fJ(2220), and one where the glueball is more distributed with f2(1820) having the largest component; the f2(1565) and fJ(1710) cannot be accommodated as glueball-(hybrid) meson mixtures without significant coupling to decay channels.

## Method

The authors constructed a 9x9 mass matrix describing the mixing of a primitive tensor glueball with four meson nonets (1P, 2P, 3P, and 1F), using Schwinger-type mass formulae derived from the matrix diagonalization. They first solved two 5x5 sub-matrix simulations to obtain all solutions, then reconstructed the full 9x9 matrix by solving the Schwinger equations exactly using the sub-matrix solutions as initial values. The analysis was extended to 13x13 matrices by adding 2F and 4P nonets, and decay widths were calculated using the 3P0 model with relativistic phase space.

## Key numbers

- Primitive glueball mass: 2.10 GeV for solution 1, 2.05 GeV for solution 2
- Couplings range: 30-120 MeV for the various nonets
- Physical masses for solution 1: 2.29, 2.23, 2.14, 2.04, 1.93, 1.82, 1.64, 1.52, 1.28 GeV
- Physical masses for solution 2: 2.38, 2.23, 2.12, 2.01, 1.95, 1.82, 1.63, 1.52, 1.28 GeV
- Gamma(f2'(1525) -> pi pi) for solution 2b: 0.7 MeV (relativistic phase space), in best agreement with experimental value 0.60 +/- 0.12 MeV
- Primitive 2P nbar n mass: 1.66 GeV, fixed from a2(1660)
- Primitive 1F nbar n mass: 1.94 GeV, primitive 1F sbar s mass: 2.15 GeV
- Primitive 3P nbar n mass: 2.05 GeV
- Primitive 1P nbar n mass: 1.318 GeV
- Primitive 1P sbar s mass: 1.55 GeV
- sbar s - nbar n mass splitting for 2P: ~180 MeV (solution 1), ~290 MeV (solution 2)
- Lattice QCD tensor glueball mass ratio: M(2++)/M(0++) = 1.39 +/- 0.04 or 1.42 +/- 0.06
- Lower limit on tensor glueball mass: >~ 2.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger-type mass formula approach previously applied to scalar mesons, providing a model-independent tool for sorting the overpopulated isoscalar tensor states that have accumulated experimentally. The analysis contradicts naive expectations that physical mesons have small glueball content, instead finding substantial glueball components in most states, which explains observations of these states in OZI-forbidden processes like pi p -> phi phi n. The results enable future decay studies of tensor states and provide the first quantitative understanding of f2'(1525) -> pi pi, demonstrating that at least four different primitive components must be included to reproduce the experimental width.
