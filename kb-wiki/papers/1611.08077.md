# Gauge cooling for the singular-drift problem in the complex Langevin method --- an application to finite density QCD

> We study full QCD at finite density and low temperature with light quark mass using the complex Langevin method. Since the singular drift problem turns out to be mild on a $4^3 \times 8$ lattice we use, the gauge cooling is performed only to control the unitarity norm in this exploratory study. We report on our preliminary data obtained from the complex Langevin simulation up to certain Langevin time. While the data are still noisy due to lack of statistics, the onset of the baryon number density seems to occur at larger $μ$ than half the pion mass, which is the value for the phase quenched QCD. The validity of our simulation is tested by the recently proposed criterion based on the probability distribution of the drift term.

## Key claim

The complex Langevin method with gauge cooling can be applied to finite-density QCD in the confined phase with light quarks on a small lattice, but the authors present this as an exploratory study where the singular drift problem is mild only on the specific $4^3 \times 8$ lattice used, and the data remain noisy due to limited statistics.

## Method

The authors performed complex Langevin simulations of full QCD with four flavors of staggered fermions on a $4^3 \times 8$ lattice at $\beta = 5.7$ and quark mass $m a = 0.05$, extending the link variables from SU(3) to SL(3, C). Gauge cooling was applied after each Langevin step to minimize the unitarity norm, which measures deviation from SU(3), but was not used to address the singular drift problem. The validity of the simulations was tested using the recently proposed criterion that the probability distribution of the drift term must fall off exponentially or faster at large values.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: $4^3 \times 8$, with $\beta = 5.7$ and $m a = 0.05$, corresponding to the confined phase at $\mu = 0$.
- Chemical potential range: $\mu a \in [0, 0.65]$, corresponding to $\mu/T \in [0, 5.2]$.
- Langevin stepsize: $\epsilon = 10^{-4}$ for QCD simulations, with total Langevin time $t = 30$.
- The onset of baryon number density in QCD occurs at $\mu a \approx 0.4$ to $0.5$, where the density reaches approximately $0.01$, corresponding to roughly one baryon per lattice volume ($1/4^3 \approx 0.016$).
- The mean field analysis predicts the onset at $\mu a = 0.55$ for QCD and $\mu a = 0.265$ (half the pion mass) for phase quenched QCD.
- The criterion for correct convergence is satisfied for $\mu a \lesssim 0.65$.

## Context

This work extends the complex Langevin method, previously successful in the deconfined phase (Sexty 2013, Fodor 2015) and the heavy dense limit (Aarts 2016), to the confined phase with light quarks where the singular drift problem from near-zero eigenvalues of the fermion matrix was anticipated. The results enable further study of the onset of baryon density in QCD, but the authors note that on larger lattices the gap in the eigenvalue distribution of the fermion matrix will disappear, causing a serious singular drift problem that requires new techniques to overcome.
