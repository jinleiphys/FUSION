# Chiral Superfluidity for QCD

> We argue that the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma formed at LHC and RHIC can be considered as a chiral superfluid. The "normal" component of the fluid is the thermalized matter in common sense, while the "superfluid" part consists of long wavelength (chiral) fermionic states moving independently. We use the bosonization procedure with a finite cut-off and obtain a dynamical axion-like field out of the chiral fermionic modes. Then we use relativistic hydrodynamics for macroscopic description of the effective theory obtained after the bosonization. Finally, solving the hydrodynamic equations in gradient expansion, we find that in the presence of external electromagnetic fields or rotation the motion of the "superfluid" component gives rise to the chiral magnetic, chiral vortical, chiral electric and dipole wave effects. Latter two effects are specific for a two-component fluid, which provides us with crucial experimental tests of the model.

# Chiral Superfluidity for QCD

## Key claim
The strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) formed at LHC and RHIC can be modeled as a two-component chiral superfluid, but only in the temperature range \(T_c < T \lesssim 2\,T_c\); the "superfluid" component consists of long-wavelength chiral fermionic modes, while the "normal" component is the thermalized medium, and the model does not involve spontaneously broken symmetry, unlike conventional superfluidity.

## Method
The author bosonizes QCD quarks with Dirac eigenvalues below a finite cut-off \(\Lambda\), obtaining an effective Lagrangian with a dynamical axion-like field \(\theta\) from the infrared fermionic modes. Relativistic hydrodynamic equations are then written for the two-component fluid, with the superfluid velocity derived from gradients of \(\theta\) and a Josephson equation linking \(\theta\) to the axial chemical potential. The hydrodynamic equations are solved in gradient expansion to extract anomalous currents, and singularities in \(\theta\) (vortex lines) are introduced to describe the chiral vortical effect under rotation.

## Key numbers
- The cut-off \(\Lambda\) is given by \(\Lambda = \pi\sqrt{2\, T^2/3 + {2\mu^2}/{\pi^2}}\) at high temperatures; \(\Lambda = 2\sqrt{|eB|}\) at strong magnetic fields; \(\Lambda \simeq 3\, \mathrm{GeV}\) at small temperatures and weak or absent magnetic fields.
- The axial anomaly coefficient is \(C = {N_c}/{2\pi^2}\).
- The "axion decay constant" is \(f = 2 \Lambda \sqrt{N_c}/\pi\).
- The quantized vorticity per vortex is \(\Omega_{quant}= - \pi /\mu_5\).
- The chiral vortical effect current along the rotation axis is \(J^z_{CVE} = - C \mu \mu_5 \Omega^z_{tot}\).

## Context
This work extends the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007) and chiral electric effect by embedding them in a hydrodynamic two-fluid model that avoids the unitarity and stability problems of constant axial chemical potentials (Adam 2001, Khaidukov 2013, Avdoshkin 2014). The model builds on the bosonization approach of Damgaard (1993) and the relativistic superfluid formalism of Son (2000). It enables experimental tests via the predicted chiral electric and dipole wave effects, which are specific to a two-component fluid, and connects to holographic axion models (Jimenez-Alba 2014) and lattice studies of near-zero fermionic modes (Sharma 2013, Alexandru 2014).
