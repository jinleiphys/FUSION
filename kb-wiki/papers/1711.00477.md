# Imprints of neutrino-pair flavor conversions on nucleosynthesis in ejecta from neutron-star merger remnants

> The remnant of neutron star mergers is dense in neutrinos. By employing inputs from one hydrodynamical simulation of a binary neutron star merger remnant with a black hole of $3\ M_\odot$ in the center, dimensionless spin parameter $0.8$ and an accretion torus of $0.3\ M_\odot$, the neutrino emission properties are investigated as the merger remnant evolves. Initially, the local number density of $\barν_e$ is larger than that of $ν_e$ everywhere above the remnant. Then, as the torus approaches self-regulated equilibrium, the local abundance of neutrinos overcomes that of antineutrinos in a funnel around the polar region. The region where the fast pairwise flavor conversions can occur shrinks accordingly as time evolves. Still, we find that fast flavor conversions do affect most of the neutrino-driven ejecta. Assuming that fast flavor conversions lead to flavor equilibration, a significant enhancement of nuclei with mass numbers $A>130$ is found as well as a change of the lanthanide mass fraction by more than a factor of a thousand. Our findings hint towards a potentially relevant role of neutrino flavor oscillations for the prediction of the kilonova (macronova) lightcurves and motivate further work in this direction.

## Key claim

Fast pairwise neutrino flavor conversions can affect most of the neutrino-driven ejecta from neutron-star merger remnants, but only if the electron lepton number angular distribution has crossings; the region where such crossings occur shrinks over time as the torus evolves from a configuration with polar antineutrino excess to one with polar neutrino excess, meaning the flavor instability is time-dependent and not guaranteed at all stages.

## Method

The authors used inputs from one hydrodynamical simulation of a post-merger black hole accretion disk (model M3A8m3a5 from Just (2014)) with a central black hole of 3 solar masses, dimensionless spin parameter 0.8, and an accretion torus of 0.3 solar masses. They defined neutrino emitting surfaces using the flux factor criterion (j = 1/3), then performed a linearized flavor stability analysis by solving the dispersion relation for temporal instabilities at multiple time snapshots (20, 35, and 50 ms), assuming a linear angular distribution of neutrinos at the emission surfaces consistent with the flux factor. They then computed nucleosynthesis yields assuming fast flavor conversions lead to flavor equilibration.

## Key numbers

- Central black hole mass: 3 solar masses, dimensionless spin parameter 0.8, accretion torus mass 0.3 solar masses
- Total mass of neutrino-driven ejecta: approximately 1.47 x 10^{-3} solar masses, compared to total outflow mass of about 66.2 x 10^{-3} solar masses
- Transition from polar antineutrino excess to polar neutrino excess occurs at about 25 ms in this model
- Assuming flavor equilibration, a significant enhancement of nuclei with mass numbers A > 130 is found, with a change of the lanthanide mass fraction by more than a factor of a thousand
- Fast flavor conversion growth timescale: approximately 10 cm at the vicinity of the neutrinosphere, given by (G_F |n_nu_e - n_nu_bar_e|)^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Wu (2017) which used a simplified two-disk model with uniform neutrino properties and flat disk geometry, finding favorable conditions for flavor instabilities everywhere above the neutrino emission surfaces. By using a realistic time-evolving torus configuration from Just (2014), the authors show that the flavor instability region shrinks over time and can vanish in the inner polar region at later times, contradicting the earlier stationary model's conclusions. The findings motivate further work on neutrino flavor oscillations for predicting kilonova light curves and suggest that fast flavor conversions could significantly affect nucleosynthesis in the neutrino-driven ejecta component of merger remnants.
