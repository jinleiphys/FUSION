# Neutrinos from pre-supernova in the framework of TQRPA method

> We propose a new method for calculating spectra and luminosities for (anti)neutrinos produced in the pre-supernova environment by weak processes with hot nuclei. It is based on the thermal quasiparticle random phase approximation (TQRPA), that allows microscopic thermodynamically consistent calculations of the weak-interaction response of nuclei at finite temperatures. For realistic representative pre-supernova conditions from the stellar evolution code MESA, we compute (anti)neutrino luminosities and spectra arising from neutral- and charged-current weak reactions with hot $^{56}$Fe and compare them with the contribution of thermal processes. We find that the TQRPA approach produces not only a higher total luminosity of electron neutrinos (mainly born in the electron capture reaction), compared to the standard technique based on the large-scale shell model (LSSM) weak-interaction rates, but also a harder neutrino spectrum. Besides, applying the TQRPA and LSSM, we find that in the context of electron antineutrino generation, the neutral-current nuclear de-excitation (ND) process via neutrino-antineutrino pair emission is at least as important as the electron-positron pair annihilation process. We also show that flavor oscillations enhance the high-energy contribution of the ND process to the electron antineutrino flux. This could potentially be important for pre-supernova antineutrino registration by the Earth's detectors.

## Key claim

The TQRPA method predicts not only a higher total luminosity of electron neutrinos from electron capture on hot \(^{56}\)Fe compared to the standard large-scale shell model (LSSM) weak-interaction rates, but also a harder neutrino spectrum; however, this conclusion is conditional on the specific pre-supernova conditions from the MESA stellar evolution code and on the use of the SkM* parametrization of the Skyrme energy density functional with a quenched axial coupling constant \(g^*_A = 0.56 g_A\).

## Method

The authors computed (anti)neutrino spectra and luminosities from charged-current (electron capture, positron capture, \(\beta^\pm\)-decay) and neutral-current (nuclear de-excitation via \(\nu\bar\nu\)-pair emission) weak processes on hot \(^{56}\)Fe using the thermal quasiparticle random phase approximation (TQRPA), which provides temperature-dependent Gamow-Teller strength functions without invoking the Brink hypothesis. These nuclear-process results were combined with thermal neutrino production (pair annihilation, plasmon decay, photo-neutrino, bremsstrahlung) using the subroutines of Timmes based on Itoh (1996), and integrated over the volume of a realistic pre-supernova model (25_79_0p005_ml) from the MESA code at the onset of core collapse. Flavor oscillation effects were also included in the final flux calculations.

## Key numbers

- Central temperature of the pre-supernova model is \(T_9 \approx 9.0\)–\(10.0\) (i.e., \(T \approx 0.86\)–\(0.86\) MeV, where \(10^{10}\,\text{K} = 0.86\) MeV).
- The neutral-current nuclear de-excitation (ND) process produces electron antineutrinos with a spectral peak at \(E_\nu \approx 6.0\) MeV, and its luminosity exceeds the pair annihilation (PA) luminosity in the energy range \(E_\nu \approx 6\)–\(9\) MeV.
- Electron capture on \(^{56}\)Fe is the dominant energy loss process, with a luminosity several orders of magnitude higher than that from other processes for \(\nu_e\).
- The ND process produces the same spectra for all (anti)neutrino flavors, and for \(\mu,\tau\) (anti)neutrinos the final (at \(m=2.0\,M_\odot\)) PA and ND luminosities are almost the same, but the spectra differ qualitatively.
- The GT\(_0\) resonance in \(^{56}\)Fe is located at about 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Dzhioev et al., 2023, Particles) by integrating TQRPA-based nuclear weak-process spectra and luminosities over the volume of a realistic pre-supernova model and including thermal processes and oscillation effects. It contrasts with the standard approach used in MESA, which relies on LSSM weak-interaction rates (Langanke et al., 2000) combined with the effective \(q\)-value approximation (Langanke et al., 2001; Patton et al., 2017), and confirms the finding of Fischer et al. (2013) that electron capture dominates energy loss during collapse. The results enable more accurate predictions for pre-supernova antineutrino detection on Earth, particularly by highlighting the role of the ND process in producing high-energy electron antineutrinos and heavy-lepton flavor (anti)neutrinos.
