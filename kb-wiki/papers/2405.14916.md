# Static and dynamic properties of atomic nuclei with high-resolution potentials

> We compute ground-state and dynamical properties of $^4$He and $^{16}$O nuclei using as input high-resolution, phenomenological nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces that are local in coordinate space. The nuclear Schrödinger equation for both nuclei is accurately solved employing the auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo approach. For the $^4$He nucleus, detailed benchmarks are carried out with the hyperspherical harmonics method. In addition to presenting results for the binding energies and radii, we also analyze the momentum distributions of these nuclei and their Euclidean response function corresponding to the isoscalar density transition. The latter quantity is particularly relevant for lepton-nucleus scattering experiments, as it paves the way to quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electroweak response functions of $^{16}$O.

# Static and dynamic properties of atomic nuclei with high-resolution potentials

## Key claim
High-resolution phenomenological potentials (AV8P, AV6P) combined with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force can be used with auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo to compute ground-state and dynamical properties of $^4$He and $^{16}$O, but the UIX interaction simultaneously overbinds $^4$He by about 2-3 MeV while underbinding $^{16}$O by about 15-18 MeV relative to experiment, indicating that simply refitting the UIX parameters is unlikely to resolve this discrepancy.

## Method
The authors solved the nuclear Schrödinger equation using auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) with constrained-path and unconstrained diffusion propagation, employing variational wave functions with two- and three-body correlations including linearized spin-isospin operators. For $^4$He, they benchmarked AFDMC results against the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) method, which uses a basis of hyperspherical functions expanded in Jacobi coordinates with a Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle. The Hamiltonians used were the Argonne $v_8^\prime$ (AV8P) and Argonne $v_6^\prime$ (AV6P) nucleon-nucleon forces supplemented by the Urbana IX (UIX) three-nucleon interaction, with approximations made for the isospin-dependent spin-orbit and cubic spin-isospin operators in the imaginary-time propagator.

## Key numbers
- $^4$He ground-state energy with AV6P: HH = -26.13(1) MeV, DMC-UC = -26.17(10) MeV
- $^4$He ground-state energy with AV8P+UIX: HH = -29.92(1) MeV, DMC-UC = -29.4(9) MeV (experimental value: -28.30 MeV)
- $^{16}$O ground-state energy with AV6P+UIX: DMC-UC = -113(9) MeV (experimental value: -127.62 MeV)
- $^{16}$O ground-state energy with AV8P+UIX: DMC-UC = -112(9) MeV
- $^4$He charge radius with AV8P: 1.69(4) fm; with AV8P+UIX: 1.64(6) fm (experimental: 1.676(3) fm)
- AV8P overbinds the deuteron by 18 keV due to omission of electromagnetic terms

## Context
This work extends previous quantum Monte Carlo calculations of light nuclei using phenomenological Argonne potentials, which have been shown to reproduce nucleon-nucleon scattering data with high accuracy up to 350 MeV laboratory energy. The authors benchmark AFDMC against the HH method for $^4$He, providing validation for the many-body method. The results are consistent with earlier cluster variational Monte Carlo calculations by Lonardoni (2017) using AV18+UIX, which also found underbinding of nuclei with more than four nucleons. The work enables future quantum Monte Carlo calculations of electroweak response functions for $^{16}$O, which are relevant for lepton-nucleus scattering experiments, and suggests that modifying the range of the repulsive three-nucleon force term, as proposed by Gnech (2023), may improve agreement with experimental binding energies across the nuclear chart.
