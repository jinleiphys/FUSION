# Spin polarisability of the nucleon at NLO in the chiral expansion

> We present a calculation of the fourth-order (NLO) contribution to spin-dependent forward Compton scattering in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. No low-energy constants, except for the anomalous magnetic moments of the nucleon, enter at this order. The fourth-order piece of the spin polarisability of the proton turns out to be almost twice the size of the leading piece, with the opposite sign. This leads to the conclusion that no prediction can currently be made for this quantity.

# Spin polarisability of the nucleon at NLO in the chiral expansion

## Key claim
The fourth-order (NLO) contribution to the spin polarisability of the proton is almost twice the size of the leading-order piece and has the opposite sign, meaning that no prediction for this quantity can currently be made due to poor convergence of the chiral expansion.

## Method
The authors calculated the fourth-order (NLO) contribution to spin-dependent forward Compton scattering in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCPT) using the gauge condition v·ε = 0. They evaluated all one-loop diagrams at NLO, including insertions on the nucleon propagator, and extracted the spin polarisability from the ω³ term in the forward scattering amplitude. No undetermined low-energy constants enter at this order except for the anomalous magnetic moments of the nucleon.

## Key numbers
- Leading-order (third-order) spin polarisability: γ = α_em g_A²/(24 π² f_π² m_π²) = 4.54 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ for both proton and neutron
- NLO contribution to the spin polarisability: γ = 4.5 − (6.9 + 1.6 τ₃) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, where τ₃ = +1 for proton and τ₃ = −1 for neutron
- The NLO piece for the proton is −8.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, giving a total of −4.0 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ (compared to the leading-order +4.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴)
- The Δ-pole contribution estimated by Hemmert et al. is −2.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, with πΔ loops contributing −0.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴
- Experimental extraction by Sandorfi et al. gives γ_p = −1.3 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ and γ_n = −0.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴; Drechsel et al. find γ_p = −0.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴

## Context
This work extends the leading-order HBCPT calculation of Bernard et al. (1992) for the nucleon spin polarisability by computing the next-to-leading-order πN loop contributions. The results contradict the expectation that the chiral expansion converges rapidly, as the NLO term is larger than the LO term with opposite sign. The calculation agrees with the independent NLO result of Ji et al. (1999) after subtraction of pole terms, and it highlights the need for explicit Δ-resonance degrees of freedom or higher-order calculations to obtain a reliable prediction. The work also relates to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule and ongoing experimental measurements at MAMI (Mainz) and future experiments at Bonn.
