# Chiral spiral induced by a strong magnetic field

> We study the modification of the chiral phase structure of QCD due to an external magnetic field. We first demonstrate how the effect of magnetic field can systematically be incorporated into a generalized Ginzburg-Landau framework. We then analyze the phase structure in the vicinity of the chiral critical point. In the chiral limit, the effect is found to be so drastic that it totally washes the tricritical point out of the phase diagram, bringing the continent for the chiral spiral. This is the case no matter how small is the intensity of the magnetic field. On the other hand, the current quark mass protects the chiral critical point from a weak magnetic field. However the critical point will eventually be covered by the chiral spiral phase as the magnetic field grows.

## Key claim

In the chiral limit, an external magnetic field of arbitrarily small intensity completely eliminates the tricritical point from the QCD phase diagram and stabilizes a chiral spiral phase; however, a finite current quark mass protects the chiral critical point and the real-kink crystal phase from weak magnetic fields, with the chiral spiral phase only taking over once the magnetic field becomes sufficiently strong.

## Method

The author derives a generalized Ginzburg-Landau (gGL) effective action for QCD by expanding the quark loop contribution to the effective action in powers of the quark self-energy and performing a derivative expansion up to sixth order in the condensates and spatial derivatives. The effects of a finite current quark mass (h-term) and an external magnetic field (b-term) are incorporated as explicit symmetry-breaking terms, with the b-term obtained by expanding the quark propagator to first order in the magnetic field. The phase diagrams are then analyzed by minimizing the gGL potential for various ratios of the magnetic field strength b to the quark mass parameter h, using the scaled couplings α₂, α₄, and α₆.

## Key numbers

- For a magnetic field corresponding to √(eB) ≈ 20 MeV (B ≈ 7×10¹² T), the phase diagram is "not much modified" and the Lifshitz critical point remains intact.
- For √(eB) ≈ 40 MeV (B ≈ 3×10¹³ T), a sizable region for the χ-spiral appears and the Lifshitz critical point is replaced by a new critical point.
- For √(eB) ≈ 90 MeV (B ≈ 10¹⁴ T), the χ-spiral region is "significantly magnified" and the original Lifshitz critical point is completely covered by the spiral phase.
- For √(eB) ≈ 150 MeV (B ≈ 4×10¹⁴ T), the magnetic field completely dominates over the quark mass term and the real-kink crystal phase is replaced by the χ-spiral over a wide region.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of inhomogeneous chiral condensates in QCD at finite density, such as the real-kink crystal (RKC) and dual chiral density wave (DCDW) phases studied by Nickel (2009) and Buballa (2014), and the effect of magnetic fields on critical points investigated by Ruggieri (2014) and Costa (2015). It also builds on the finding by Nishiyama (2015) that the chiral spiral is favored by a magnetic field, and on the work of Yoshiike (2015) showing that the massive dual chiral density wave survives with a skewed phase. The results enable future studies of the QCD phase diagram under realistic magnetic field strengths relevant to magnetars (B ∼ 10¹⁰ T) and heavy-ion collisions (B ∼ 10¹⁴ T), and provide a systematic framework for analyzing the competition between explicit symmetry breaking from quark masses and magnetic fields in inhomogeneous phases.
