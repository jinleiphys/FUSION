# Coarse Graining in Hydrodynamics and Effects of Fluctuations

> We address the physical meaning of hydrodynamic approach related with the coarse graining scale in the frame work of variational formulation. We point out that the local thermal equilibrium does not necessarily play a critical role in the description of the collective flow patterns. We further show that the effect of viscosity is also formulated in the form of the variational method including fluctuations.

# Coarse Graining in Hydrodynamics and Effects of Fluctuations

## Key claim
The hydrodynamic description of collective flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions does not necessarily require local thermal equilibrium (LTE) in a strict sense; rather, the observed hydrodynamic behavior can emerge from coarse-graining of microscopic configurations, where the existence of an effective equation of state for the coarse-grained variables is sufficient.

## Method
The authors introduce an explicit coarse-graining procedure using a four-dimensional smoothing kernel (Gaussian in time with width tau and in space with width h) applied to the microscopic energy-momentum tensor and conserved currents, producing smoothed hydrodynamic variables. They then formulate the dynamics of these coarse-grained variables through a variational principle, and extend this to include fluctuations via the stochastic variational method (SVM), which introduces forward and backward stochastic differential equations to derive viscous terms.

## Key numbers
- The smoothing kernel uses a time resolution width tau and a space resolution width h, both characterized by Gaussian distributions, though no specific numerical values for these scales are given in the text.
- The stochastic variational method introduces a noise strength parameter nu, with the viscosity coefficient given by eta = alpha_1 (1 + 2 alpha_2) nu rho^m, where alpha_1 and alpha_2 are arbitrary constants from the stochastic representation of the kinetic term.
- The term kappa = 2 alpha_2 nu^2 appears in the resulting equation, and setting alpha_2 = 0 reduces the equation to the Navier-Stokes equation.

## Context
This work extends the variational formulation of hydrodynamics (Hydro-action, cited in the paper) by explicitly incorporating coarse-graining and fluctuations, addressing questions raised by the success of event-by-event hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions (ebeHydro, cited). The paper directly responds to the puzzle that pA collisions at ALICE show collective flow similar to AA collisions (pA, cited), which challenges the assumption of LTE. The authors note that the non-necessity of strict LTE was previously noticed (Larry, cited), and suggest that analysis using a microscopic model such as PHSD (Elena, cited) could quantify the coarse-graining scales. The work enables future experimental searches for genuine hydrodynamic signatures, such as shock wave remnants or event-plane dependence on transverse momentum, to determine the actual coarse-graining scale in heavy-ion collisions.
