# $γW$-box Inside-Out: Nuclear Polarizabilities Distort the Beta Decay Spectrum

> I consider the $γW$-box correction to superallowed nuclear $β$-decays in the framework of dispersion relations. I address a novel effect of a distortion of the emitted electron energy spectrum by nuclear polarizabilities and show that this effect, while neglected in the literature, is sizable. I estimate its size in the approximation of a linear energy dependence, and using two models that are expected to give the lower and the upper bound. The respective correction to the $β^+$ spectrum is estimated to be $Δ_R(E)=(1.6\pm1.6)\times10^{-4}{E}/{\rm MeV}$ assuming a conservative 100\% uncertainty. The effect is positive-definite and can be observed if a high-precision measurement of the positron spectrum is viable. If only the full rate is observed, it should be included in the calculated ${\cal F}t$-values of nuclear decays. I argue that this novel effect should be evaluated in state-of-the-art nuclear models and included in the analyses of nuclear beta decay experiments to ensure the correct extraction of $V_{ud}$ from decay rates, and of the Fierz interference term from precision measurements of decay spectra.

## Key claim

The conventional splitting of the γW-box radiative correction into energy-independent "inner" and energy-dependent "outer" parts is not warranted for nuclear beta decays because nuclear excitation energies (few MeV) provide a much smaller scale than the pion mass (~140 MeV), leading to a previously neglected energy-dependent nuclear structure correction δ_NS(E) that is sizable at current precision levels.

## Method

The author uses forward dispersion relations to compute the γW-box correction to superallowed nuclear β-decays, separating the amplitude into even and odd energy components. The energy-dependent (odd) part is estimated using two complementary models: a dimensional analysis based on the nuclear electric dipole polarizability and charge radius, and a free Fermi gas (FFG) model with quasielastic knockout of a single active nucleon, using effective removal energies and Fermi momentum parameters.

## Key numbers

- The energy-dependent correction to the β⁺ spectrum is estimated as δ_NS(E) = (1.6 ± 1.6) × 10⁻⁴ × (E/MeV), assuming a conservative 100% uncertainty
- The FFG model gives δ_NS(E) = (2.8 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁴ × (E/MeV), using effective separation energy ε̄ = 7.5 ± 1.5 MeV and Fermi momentum k_F = 235 ± 10 MeV
- The dimensional analysis with dipole polarizability gives δ_NS(E) = 2 × 10⁻⁵ × (E/MeV) × (A/N), with A/N ≈ 2 for superallowed decays
- The integrated correction to the ℱt value is δ^E_NS ≈ (8 ± 8) × 10⁻⁵ × (Q/MeV)
- The shift in the average ℱt value from the energy-dependent correction is [δℱt]^(E-dep.) = (1.4 ± 1.4) s
- The energy-independent correction from Seng (2018) gives [δℱt]^(E-indep.) = -(1.8 ± 1.2) s
- The combined average ℱt value becomes 3072(2) s
- The CKM first-row unitarity deficit becomes |V_ud|² + |V_us|² + |V_ub|² - 1 = -0.0016(6)

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation framework applied to the γW-box by Seng (2018) and Seng (2018b), who reexamined hadronic contributions to the universal correction Δ_R^V and the nuclear-specific δ_NS. The author argues that the conventional argument that energy-dependent strong interaction effects scale as (α/π)(E/M_π) ~ 10⁻⁵ is invalid for nuclear decays where the relevant scale is nuclear excitation energies (few MeV), not the pion mass. The effect is comparable in size to the uncertainties of the seven most precise ℱt values from Hardy (2014) and must be included in future extractions of V_ud and searches for Fierz interference in beta decay spectra.
