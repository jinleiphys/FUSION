# The Skyrmion in the nucleus

> The energy levels of a skyrmion in nucleus are calculated in a field theory of skyrmions coupled to the dilaton field and the $ω$ meson . The central potential fits well with expectations. The nucleon spin-orbit interaction derived from the omega meson in a rotating frame gives the correct level splittings. The same interaction originating from the Thomas precession effect is negligible. Energy levels are calculated for closed shell nuclei. The meson fields are obtained from a Thomas-Fermi mean field approximation to the nucleus.

## Key claim

The skyrmion model, when coupled to the dilaton field and the omega meson in a mean-field Thomas-Fermi approximation, can reproduce the central and spin-orbit single-particle energy levels of closed-shell nuclei, with the spin-orbit interaction arising specifically from the omega meson in a rotating frame rather than from Thomas precession or Dirac relativistic effects.

## Method

The authors used a field theory of skyrmions coupled to the dilaton field and the omega meson, with measured nuclear densities input into a Thomas-Fermi mean-field approximation to generate the meson fields. They solved the Euler-Lagrange equations for the mean fields, then quantized the skyrmion center coordinate to obtain an effective Schrodinger equation for the radial wave function. The spin-orbit interaction was derived from three possible sources: a Lorentz boost of the skyrmion, Thomas precession, and the transformation of the static omega meson field to a rotating frame; only the last gave a significant contribution.

## Key numbers

- For C12: calculated 1s1/2 binding energy 36.3 MeV (experimental 35.2 MeV); calculated 1p3/2 binding energy 15.7 MeV (experimental 16.9 MeV)
- For O16: calculated 1s1/2 binding energy 37.1 MeV (experimental 43 +/- 5 MeV); calculated 1p3/2 binding energy 20.5 MeV (experimental 20.1 MeV); calculated 1p1/2 binding energy 15.6 MeV (experimental 13.9 MeV)
- For Ca40: calculated 1s1/2 binding energy 48 MeV (experimental 50 +/- 10 MeV); calculated 1p3/2 binding energy 35 MeV (experimental 34 +/- 6 MeV); calculated 1p1/2 binding energy 30.7 MeV (experimental 34 +/- 6 MeV); calculated 1d5/2 binding energy 21 MeV (experimental 18.5 MeV); calculated 2s1/2 binding energy 15.7 MeV (experimental 14.5 MeV); calculated 1d3/2 binding energy 14.8 MeV (experimental 12 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous dilute-fluid skyrmion model (Kalbermann, 1995-1996) from nuclear matter to finite closed-shell nuclei, using measured densities to avoid detailed parametrization of the dilaton potential. It contrasts with Walecka-type relativistic mean-field models (Serot and Walecka, 1986) where the spin-orbit force arises from the addition of large scalar and vector potentials in the Dirac equation, whereas here it originates from the omega meson coupling to the rotating skyrmion's moment of inertia. The results enable further application of the Skyrme model to single-particle nuclear structure without requiring a Dirac formalism.
