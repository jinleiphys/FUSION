# Study of correlation of production and decay planes in $π\to3π$ diffractive dissociation process on nuclei

> A large correlation of production and decay planes of (π^-π^+π^-)-system in dissociation of $π^-$-beam 40 GeV/c on nuclear targets was observed. The dependence of the correlation on atomic number, Feynmann variable and transversal momentum as well as on invariant mass of the pion triple and neutral pion pair were investigated. It was shown that the phenomenon has a clear dynamic origin and resembles the single spin asymmetry behavior.

## Key claim

A large correlation (transversal handedness) between the production and decay planes of the \((\pi^-\pi^+\pi^-)\) system in diffractive dissociation of a 40 GeV/\(c\) \(\pi^-\) beam on nuclear targets is observed, with the effect having a clear dynamical origin and resembling single spin asymmetry behavior; the abstract glosses over the fact that the effect systematically decreases with increasing atomic number \(A\), approximately as the inverse nuclear radius, suggesting a depolarization effect from multiple scattering.

## Method

The transversal handedness \(H_{T1}\) was measured as the asymmetry of event probability with respect to the sign of the scalar product of the normal to the triple production plane (\(\mathbf{N}\)) and the normal to the triple decay plane in its center of mass (\(\mathbf{n}\)), using data from the Bologna–Dubna–Milan Collaboration for the reaction \(\pi^- + A \to (\pi^- \pi^+ \pi^-) + A\) at 40 GeV/\(c\) on eight nuclear targets (Be, \(^{12}\)C, \(^{28}\)Si, \(^{48}\)Ti, \(^{63}\)Cu, \(^{107}\)Ag, \(^{181}\)Ta, \(^{207}\)Pb). The dependence of \(H_{T1}\) on atomic number, Feynman variable \(X_F\) of the leading \(\pi^-\), triple invariant mass \(m_{3\pi}\), neutral pair invariant mass \(m_{\pi^+\pi^-}\), and triple transverse momentum \(k_T\) was studied using about 250,000 selected events. Monte-Carlo events generated with a constant mass spectrum and exponential \(t'\) slope were traced through apparatus simulation to check for kinematic or acceptance biases.

## Key numbers

- Transversal handedness averaged over all nuclei: \(H_{T1} = (5.96 \pm 0.21)\%\).
- Longitudinal handedness (parity-forbidden): \(H_L = (0.25 \pm 0.21)\%\).
- Second transverse handedness (parity-forbidden): \(H_{T2} = (0.43 \pm 0.21)\%\).
- Monte-Carlo simulation result for transversal handedness: \(H_{T1}^{\rm MC} = (0.20 \pm 0.28)\%\), consistent with zero.
- Beam momentum: 40 GeV/\(c\).
- Number of selected events: about 250,000.
- Admixture of non-diffractive events: less than 1\%.
- Slope for exponential \(t'\) dependence (beryllium): \(40\, ({\rm GeV}/c)^{-2}\).
- Mass range for Monte-Carlo generation: 0.6–2.5 GeV/\(c^2\).
- Resonance region used for exclusion: 1.05–1.80 GeV (covering \(a_1(1260)\) and \(\pi_2(1670)\)).
- Sharp dip in \(k_T\) dependence at \(k_T = 0.05\)–\(0.07\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation of transversal handedness in diffractive triple-pion production reported by the same collaboration (Efremov et al., previous publication) with increased statistics and systematic study of dependence on atomic number, kinematic variables, and invariant masses. The observed decrease of the effect with atomic number resembles a depolarization effect in multiple scattering, and the growth with \(X_F\) and \(k_T\) resembles the behavior of single spin asymmetry (e.g., pion asymmetry or \(\Lambda\)-polarization) as studied in prior polarization literature. The results contradict the Regge pole exchange model description of the same reaction at 4.5 GeV on a proton target (earlier hydrogen bubble chamber experiment), which found no angular dependence of the decay-plane normal. The work enables future partial-wave analysis to determine the contribution of different spin states to the effect.
