# Electromagnetic Radiation from Baryon-Rich Matter in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We perform a study of electromagnetic radiation in heavy-ion collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) Beam Energy Scan (BES) and SPS energies using the iEBE-MUSIC framework, which includes 3D dynamical Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions, MUSIC (3+1)D viscous relativistic hydrodynamics, and the UrQMD hadronic afterburner. The multistage modeling has been calibrated to hadronic data at RHIC-BES energies using a Bayesian analysis. Integrating the thermal photon emission rates with the medium evolution, we study the direct photon yield and elliptic flow and how they vary with collision energy and emission source. We compare with results obtained by the STAR and PHENIX Collaborations. We employ next-to-leading order thermal QCD dilepton emission rates to compute dilepton invariant mass spectra and extract the effective temperature of the quark-gluon plasma at different collision energies.

## Key claim

The study demonstrates that a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic framework with Bayesian-calibrated parameters can simultaneously describe hadronic observables and electromagnetic radiation across RHIC Beam Energy Scan and SPS energies, but the direct photon calculations remain limited by theoretical uncertainties in the prompt photon component, particularly at lower collision energies where pQCD-based calculations become less reliable.

## Method

The authors employed the iEBE-MUSIC framework with 3D dynamical Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions, (3+1)D MUSIC viscous relativistic hydrodynamics with the NEOS-BQS crossover equation of state, and the UrQMD hadronic afterburner. Model parameters were calibrated using Bayesian inference on hadron multiplicity, mean transverse momentum, and anisotropic flow coefficients from STAR data at 7.7, 19.6, and 200 GeV. Thermal photon emission rates included leading-order contributions with shear and bulk viscosity corrections, while thermal dilepton rates were calculated up to next-to-leading order in alpha_s without viscosity corrections.

## Key numbers

- Direct photon yield scaling exponent alpha = 1.40 +/- 0.01 (without uncertainty bands) and 1.43 +/- 0.05 (with uncertainty) for the sum of prompt and thermal photons
- Prompt photon scaling exponent alpha = 1.24 +/- 0.02; thermal photon scaling exponent alpha = 1.53 +/- 0.01
- Transition temperature T_tr = 0.16 GeV used to separate QGP and hadronic phase contributions for photon and dilepton emission
- Hydrodynamic evolution run down to temperature of 0.12 GeV for hadronic photon emission
- Constant time interval tau_hydro = 0.5 fm/c for string deceleration in parton collision rest frame
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, prompt photons dominate direct photon yield for p_T > 2.5 GeV; thermal photons dominate for p_T < 2.5 GeV
- Below sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV, thermal photon contribution overtakes prompt photons in the p_T range of 1-4 GeV
- QGP lifetime remains about 10 fm/c even at 14.6 GeV, while overlap time between nuclei reaches about 2 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electromagnetic radiation in heavy-ion collisions by applying a dynamically initialized (3+1)D framework to baryon-rich matter at RHIC BES and SPS energies, building on the Bayesian calibration approach of Jahan (2024) and Shen (2023). The results address the long-standing discrepancy between STAR and PHENIX direct photon measurements, showing that theoretical uncertainties from the prompt photon component make the model compatible with both datasets. The study also confronts the "direct photon v2 puzzle" identified by David (2019), finding that dynamical initialization alone is insufficient to resolve the discrepancy with PHENIX elliptic flow data. This work enables future multimessenger studies combining hadronic, photon, and dilepton observables to constrain QGP properties at finite baryon chemical potential.
