# QCD at small x and nucleus-nucleus collisions

> At large collision energy sqrt(s) and relatively low momentum transfer Q, one expects a new regime of Quantum Chromo-Dynamics (QCD) known as "saturation". This kinematical range is characterized by a very large occupation number for gluons inside hadrons and nuclei; this is the region where higher twist contributions are as large as the leading twist contributions incorporated in collinear factorization. In this talk, I discuss the onset of and dynamics in the saturation regime, some of its experimental signatures, and its implications for the early stages of Heavy Ion Collisions.

## Key claim

At large collision energy and relatively low momentum transfer, QCD enters a "saturation" regime where higher twist contributions become as large as the leading twist contributions incorporated in collinear factorization, characterized by a very large occupation number for gluons inside hadrons and nuclei.

## Method

The paper reviews the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, where fast partons (large x) are treated as static color sources and slow partons (low x) as dynamical gauge fields, with the distribution of hard sources evolving via the JIMWLK equation. It discusses the leading order calculation of gluon production from classical solutions of Yang-Mills equations with retarded boundary conditions, and the next-to-leading order corrections expressed as perturbations of the initial value problem on the light-cone.

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum varies like $A^{1/3} x^{-0.3}$ with nucleus atomic number $A$ and momentum fraction $x$.
- In a typical central heavy ion collision at high energy, 99% of produced particles have transverse momentum below 2 GeV, which is below the saturation momentum.
- Geometrical scaling in $\gamma^*p$ cross-section at HERA is observed for $x \le 0.01$, with $Q_s^2 \propto x^{-0.3}$.

## Context

This talk extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) and the JIMWLK/Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equations that incorporate gluon recombination beyond the BFKL equation. It discusses experimental evidence from HERA (geometrical scaling), RHIC (limiting fragmentation), and BRAHMS (forward suppression in deuteron-gold collisions), and outlines the path toward proving factorization of rapidity divergences in the CGC framework for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
