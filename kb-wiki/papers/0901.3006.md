# Theoretical investigations of isoscalar, isovector and mixed symmetry states in skin nuclei

> We present recent investigations on dipole and quadrupole excitations in spherical skin nuclei, particular exploring their connection to the thickness of the neutron skin. Our theoretical method relies on density functional theory, which provides us with a proper link between nuclear many-body theory of the nuclear ground state and its phenomenological description. For the calculation of the nuclear excited states we apply QPM theory. A new quadrupole mode related to pygmy quadrupole resonance (PQR) in tin isotopes is suggested.

## Key claim
The paper suggests that a new low-energy quadrupole mode, the Pygmy Quadrupole Resonance (PQR), exists in neutron-rich tin isotopes, with properties analogous to the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR), but this is a theoretical prediction based on Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) calculations rather than an experimentally confirmed observation.

## Method
The authors combine a Hartree-Fock-Bogoljubov (HFB) description of the nuclear ground state, using a phenomenological energy-density functional (EDF) fitted to experimental binding energies, radii, and separation energies, with the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) for excited states. The QPM Hamiltonian includes separable multipole-multipole and spin-multipole interactions in the particle-hole channel and multipole pairing in the particle-particle channel, with coupling constants fitted to low-lying vibrational states and giant dipole resonances (GDR). Excited states are described by QRPA phonons, and transition densities are analyzed to separate isoscalar, isovector, and mixed-symmetry modes.

## Key numbers
- The total PDR strength in tin isotopes increases with neutron skin thickness δr, where δr = √⟨r²_n⟩ − √⟨r²_p⟩, and for A ≥ 106 the neutron distribution begins to extend beyond the proton density, up to ¹³²Sn.
- In ¹²⁰Sn, the calculated positions of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) and isovector giant quadrupole resonance (IVGQR) are E* = 12.2 MeV and E* = 28.1 MeV, respectively.
- Low-lying dipole states in ¹¹²–¹³²Sn and N=50, 82 nuclei exhaust less than 1% of the total Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) energy-weighted dipole sum rule.
- In ¹⁰⁰–¹⁰²Sn, a tiny proton skin appears, reversing the neutron-skin trend seen in heavier tin isotopes.

## Context
This work extends previous investigations of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) in tin isotopes and N=50, 82 nuclei by Tsoneva and Lenske (2004, 2008) and by Volz (2006) and Tsoneva and Schwengner (2008), which established the connection between low-energy dipole strength and neutron skin thickness. The new prediction of a Pygmy Quadrupole Resonance (PQR) in ¹²⁰Sn, identified through analysis of quadrupole transition densities, opens the possibility of experimentally searching for this mode in neutron-rich nuclei, complementing existing studies of the PDR and giant resonances. The results are compared with experimental data on total PDR strengths in ¹¹⁶Sn and ¹²⁴Sn from Govaert (1998) and in ¹¹²Sn from Ozel, and with skin thickness derived from charge exchange reactions by Krasznahorkay et al.
