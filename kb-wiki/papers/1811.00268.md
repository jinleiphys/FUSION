# Old and New in Strangeness Nuclear Physics

> Several persistent problems in strangeness nuclear physics are discussed in this opening talk at HYP2018, Portsmouth-Norfolk VA, June 2018: (i) the $^3_Λ$H and $^3_Λ$n (if existing) lifetimes; (ii) charge symmetry breaking in $Λ$ hypernuclei; (iii) the overbinding of $^5_Λ$He which might be related to the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars; and (iv) does $Λ^\ast$(1405) survive in strange hadronic matter?

## Key claim

Several persistent problems in strangeness nuclear physics are discussed, including the lifetimes of \(^3_\Lambda\)H and \(^3_\Lambda\)n (if it exists), charge symmetry breaking in \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei, the overbinding of \(^5_\Lambda\)He (which may relate to the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars), and whether the \(\Lambda^\ast(1405)\) survives in strange hadronic matter; the abstract presents these as open questions rather than resolved issues.

## Method

The paper reviews and analyzes four topics using experimental data from emulsion, counter, and relativistic heavy-ion experiments, combined with theoretical calculations including quantum Monte Carlo (QMC), leading-order chiral effective field theory (\(\chi\)EFT), pionless effective field theory (\(\nopieft\)) with stochastic variational method (SVM) few-body calculations, and no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations for \(A=4\) hypernuclei. For the hypertriton lifetime, the author compares calculations using the Rayet-Dalitz approach and Faddeev equations against recent measurements from STAR, HypHI, and ALICE.

## Key numbers

- \(D_\Lambda\) (depth of \(\Lambda\) nuclear potential well) from a Woods-Saxon fit: 30 MeV; earlier values: 27.8\(\pm\)0.3 MeV (1988 theoretical analysis), 27\(\pm\)3 MeV (1964 emulsion), 27.2\(\pm\)1.3 MeV (1965 emulsion)
- \(B_\Lambda^{\rm exp}(^5_\Lambda\)He) = 3.12\(\pm\)0.02 MeV; LO \(\chi\)EFT calculations give values as large as 6 MeV (overbinding)
- Charge symmetry breaking in \(A=4\) mirror hypernuclei: \(\Delta B_\Lambda^{J=0} = 233\pm 92\) keV (experimental); NCSM calculations give \(\Delta B_\Lambda^{J=0} = 177^{+119}_{-147}\) keV and \(\Delta B_\Lambda^{J=1} = -215^{+43}_{-41}\) keV
- \(\Lambda-\Sigma^0\) mixing CSB splitting \(\Delta E_x^{\rm exp} = 320\pm 20\) keV for \(A=4\) hypernuclei
- Free \(\Lambda\) lifetime \(\tau_\Lambda = 263\pm 2\) ps; recent heavy-ion measurements of \(\tau(^3_\Lambda\)H) are shorter by (30\(\pm\)8)% than \(\tau_\Lambda\)
- Calculated \(^3_\Lambda\)H decay rate (total, including pion FSI): 1.05 (1966), 1.14 (1992), 1.03 (1998), 1.23 (2019) in units of \(\Gamma_\Lambda\)
- Estimated \(^3_\Lambda\)n lifetime: \(\tau(^3_\Lambda\)n) \(\approx 368\) ps

## Context

This opening talk at HYP2018 reviews long-standing puzzles in strangeness nuclear physics, extending work by Dalitz et al. (1972) on the overbinding of \(^5_\Lambda\)He and by Dalitz and Von Hippel (1964) on charge symmetry breaking via \(\Lambda-\Sigma^0\) mixing. The discussion of the hypertriton lifetime puzzle updates earlier calculations by Rayet and Dalitz (1966) and Congleton (1992), and addresses recent measurements from STAR, HypHI, and ALICE that suggest a lifetime significantly shorter than the free \(\Lambda\) lifetime. The paper enables future work on applying \(\Lambda NN\) interaction terms from pionless EFT to neutron-star matter calculations to test resolutions of the hyperon puzzle, and on extending CSB studies to heavier hypernuclei.
